समिधानाः सहस्रजिद अग्ने धर्माणि पुष्यसि |देवानां दूत उक्त्यः ||
samidhānaḥ sahasrajid agne dharmāṇi puṣyasi | devānāṁ dūta ukthyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samidhānaḥ: This term refers to the act of offering or presenting something, often in the context of a fire sacrifice.
    • sahasrajid: This means "one who is a thousand-fold" or "one who has a thousand forms," often referring to the vastness and omnipresence of the divine.
    • agne: This is a direct address to Agni, the fire god, who symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine messenger.
    • dharmāṇi: This word translates to "righteousness" or "moral duties," indicating the importance of ethical conduct.
    • puṣyasi: This means "you nourish" or "you promote growth," suggesting the nurturing aspect of the divine.
    • devānāṁ: This translates to "of the gods," indicating a connection to the divine realm.
    • dūta: This means "messenger," referring to the role of Agni as a communicator between humans and the divine.
    • ukthyaḥ: This refers to "sacred hymns" or "invocations," emphasizing the importance of prayer and ritual in connecting with the divine.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of Agni as a divine force that nourishes righteousness and serves as a messenger to the gods.
    • It emphasizes the importance of ethical living and the role of rituals in maintaining a connection with the divine.
    • In real life, this can be seen in the practice of offering prayers and performing rituals to seek blessings and guidance from higher powers.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, lighting a fire during rituals symbolizes the presence of the divine and the purification of intentions.
    • People often engage in charitable acts and ethical behavior, believing that such actions align them with dharma and attract positive energy.
    • In modern contexts, individuals may seek guidance through meditation and prayer, reflecting the desire to connect with a higher purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine, ethical living, and the importance of rituals in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate righteousness and to recognize the divine presence in their actions and intentions.
    • By embodying these principles, one can foster a deeper connection with the universe and contribute positively to the world around them.
