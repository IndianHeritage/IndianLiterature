यथा देवाः असुरेषु श्रद्धां उग्रेषु चक्रिरे |एवं भोजेषु यज्वस्व अस्माकं उदितं कृधि ||
yathā devā asureṣu śraddhām ugreṣu cakrire | evam bhojeṣu yajvasv asmākam uditaṁ kṛdhi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yathā: as, like
    • devā: the gods
    • asureṣu: among the demons
    • śraddhām: faith, trust
    • ugreṣu: in the fierce, powerful
    • cakrire: they established, they created
    • evam: thus, in this way
    • bhojeṣu: in the offerings, in the sacrifices
    • yajvasv: those who perform sacrifices
    • asmākam: our
    • uditaṁ: arising, emerging
    • kṛdhi: do, create
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of faith and devotion in both divine and earthly realms.
    • It draws a parallel between the actions of the gods and the expectations from humans in their rituals and offerings.
    • The phrase "yathā devā asureṣu" suggests that just as the gods have established faith among the powerful beings (demons), humans too should cultivate a similar faith in their offerings.
    • The term "bhojeṣu yajvasv" indicates that those who perform sacrifices should do so with sincerity and dedication, reflecting the same intensity of faith as the gods.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the concept of commitment in various fields, such as business or personal relationships. Just as the gods are committed to their roles, individuals should be dedicated to their responsibilities.
    • For instance, a leader in a company can draw inspiration from this mantra by establishing trust and faith among their team, similar to how the gods inspire faith among their followers.
    • In personal life, when one engages in rituals or traditions, doing so with genuine faith and intention can lead to a more fulfilling experience, akin to the offerings made to the gods.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of faith and dedication in both spiritual and worldly pursuits.
    • It teaches that just as the gods have established trust and faith, humans too should embody these qualities in their actions and relationships.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger connections, greater commitment, and a more profound sense of purpose in both personal and professional realms.
