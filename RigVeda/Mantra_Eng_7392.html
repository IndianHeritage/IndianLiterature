यो नः शाश्वत पुराविथामृतध्रो वाजसातये |स त्वं न इन्द्र मृळय ||
yo naḥ śaśvat purāvithāmṛdhro vājasātaye | sa tvaṁ na indra mṛḻaya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • naḥ: our
    • śaśvat: eternal
    • purā: ancient
    • vithāmṛdhro: possessor of wealth
    • vājasātaye: for the purpose of strength or power
    • sa: he
    • tvaṁ: you
    • na: not
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • mṛḻaya: please protect or save
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who is often associated with strength and power.
    • The speaker is invoking Indra, asking him to protect and bless them with eternal wealth and strength.
    • The phrase "yo naḥ śaśvat purāvithāmṛdhro" emphasizes the desire for lasting prosperity and abundance, which is a common aspiration in human life.
    • The term "vājasātaye" indicates the pursuit of strength, which can be interpreted as both physical and mental resilience.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, individuals often seek protection and guidance from higher powers or principles, whether through spirituality, community support, or personal development.
    • Businesses may invoke this mantra metaphorically by striving for sustainable growth and resilience in the face of challenges, seeking to create a lasting impact.
    • In personal relationships, the desire for eternal wealth can translate to emotional richness, where individuals seek to cultivate deep, meaningful connections with others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the universal human desire for protection, strength, and lasting prosperity.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of higher powers and the importance of seeking support in our endeavors.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to cultivate resilience and to strive for abundance, not just materially but also in relationships and personal growth.
