को वेद जानम् एषां को वा पुरा सुम्नेष्व आस मरुताम् |यद युयुज्रे किलास्यः ||
ko veda jānam eṣāṁ ko vā purā sumneṣv āsa marutām | yad yuyujre kilāsyaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ko veda jānam eṣāṁ ko vā purā sumneṣv āsa marutām | yad yuyujre kilāsyaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Who knows this? Who can tell us about it? Who among the Maruts (the storm deities) has the knowledge of this? What did they unite for?"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ko: Who
    • veda: knows or has knowledge
    • jānam: this (referring to a specific knowledge or truth)
    • eṣāṁ: of these (referring to the Maruts)
    • ko vā: who else
    • purā: in the past
    • sumneṣv: in auspicious or favorable things
    • āsa: has or possesses
    • marutām: of the Maruts (the storm gods)
    • yad: what
    • yuyujre: they united or came together
    • kilāsyaḥ: for this purpose or for this reason
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra reflects a deep inquiry into the nature of knowledge and the divine forces that govern the universe.
    • The Maruts symbolize the forces of nature, particularly storms and winds, which can be seen as both destructive and life-giving.
    • In real life, this can be related to the quest for understanding the natural phenomena around us, such as climate change, where we seek knowledge to mitigate its effects.
    • It also emphasizes the importance of collaboration and unity in achieving a common goal, much like how communities come together to address environmental issues.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encourages us to seek knowledge and understanding of the forces that shape our lives.
    • It highlights the importance of asking questions and seeking answers, which is fundamental in both spiritual and scientific pursuits.
    • In a broader sense, it serves as a reminder of our interconnectedness with nature and the need for collective action in addressing global challenges.
