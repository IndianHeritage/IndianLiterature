ते गव्यतां मनसा दृढं उभ्दं गा येमाणं परि शन्तम अद्रिम् |दृढं नरो वचसा दैव्यम् व्रजं गोमन्तं उशिजो वि वव्रुः ||
te gavyatā manasā dṛdhram ubdhaṁ gā yemānam pari ṣantam adrim | dṛḻhaṁ naro vacasā daivyena vrajaṁ gomantam uśijo vi vavruḥ ||
  • te gavyatā: This phrase can be interpreted as "you who are endowed with the essence of cows." In Vedic culture, cows symbolize wealth, nourishment, and abundance. They are revered as sacred animals, representing the sustenance of life.
  • manasā dṛdhram: This translates to "with a steadfast mind." It emphasizes the importance of mental strength and focus in achieving one's goals. In real life, this can be likened to the determination shown by athletes who train rigorously to achieve their dreams.
  • ubdhaṁ gā yemānam: This can be understood as "the one who is invoked or called upon." It suggests a connection to divine or higher powers. For instance, in times of crisis, people often seek guidance through prayer or meditation, invoking a sense of peace and clarity.
  • pari ṣantam adrim: This phrase means "surrounding the mountain." Mountains often symbolize challenges or obstacles in life. The idea of surrounding a mountain can represent the various strategies we employ to overcome difficulties, much like how climbers prepare and strategize to reach the summit.
  • dṛḻhaṁ naro vacasā: This translates to "with firm words of a man." It highlights the power of speech and communication. In real life, effective communication can lead to successful negotiations, relationships, and leadership. For example, a leader who speaks with conviction can inspire and motivate their team.
  • daivyena vrajaṁ: This means "by divine grace." It suggests that success is not solely dependent on human effort but also on the blessings of the divine. Many people attribute their achievements to a higher power, reflecting the belief that faith plays a crucial role in success.
  • gomantam uśijo vi vavruḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is full of strength and vitality." It signifies the importance of health and well-being. In modern life, maintaining physical and mental health is essential for productivity and happiness, akin to how athletes prioritize their fitness to excel in their sports.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, determination, and the interplay between human effort and divine grace. It serves as a reminder that while we must strive with a steadfast mind and firm words, we should also seek the blessings of the divine in our endeavors. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal challenges, professional pursuits, and spiritual growth, encouraging individuals to harness their inner strength while remaining open to the guidance of higher powers.
