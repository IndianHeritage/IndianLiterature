मन्त्र: सोम इद वः सुतो अस्तु कलयो मा बिभीतन |अपेद एष ध्वस्मायति स्वयम् घैषो अपायति ||
soma id vaḥ suto astu kalayo mā bibhītana | aped eṣa dhvasmāyati svayaṁ ghaiṣo apāyati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • soma: Refers to the sacred drink or elixir, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • id: This word can be translated as "this" or "here."
    • vaḥ: Means "to you" or "for you."
    • suto: Translates to "son" or "offspring," often implying something that is produced or generated.
    • astu: Means "let it be" or "may it be."
    • kalayo: Refers to "the powers" or "the energies."
    • mā: Means "not" or "do not."
    • bibhītana: Translates to "be afraid" or "fear."
    • aped: Means "if" or "in case."
    • eṣa: Refers to "this" or "he."
    • dhvasmāyati: Means "to destroy" or "to dissolve."
    • svayaṁ: Translates to "by itself" or "on its own."
    • ghaiṣo: Refers to "the one who is strong" or "powerful."
    • apāyati: Means "to lead away" or "to take away."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of Soma, a symbol of divine energy and inspiration.
    • It emphasizes the importance of not fearing the powers that come from this divine source.
    • The phrase "if this destroys" suggests a contemplation of the potential consequences of divine energy, urging one to embrace it rather than fear it.
    • In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and creativity, where individuals may fear failure or the unknown.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the context of personal growth, individuals often hesitate to take risks due to fear of failure. This mantra encourages embracing challenges as a source of strength.
    • In creative fields, artists and innovators may fear criticism or rejection. The mantra serves as a reminder to harness their inner power and express themselves without fear.
    • In leadership, the mantra can be applied to inspire confidence in teams, encouraging them to take bold steps without the fear of potential setbacks.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its encouragement to embrace divine energy and creativity without fear.
    • It teaches that the powers we possess should not intimidate us but rather empower us to act and create.
    • In various aspects of life, from personal development to professional endeavors, this mantra serves as a guiding principle to overcome fear and harness one's true potential.
