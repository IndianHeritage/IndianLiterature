सं यन् मही मिथती स्पर्धमाने तनूरुचां शूरसाता यतैते |अदेवयुं विदथे देवयुभिः सत्रा हतम् सोमसुतां जनेन ||
saṁ yan mahī mithatī spardhamāne tanūrucā śūrasātā yataite | adevayuṁ vidathe devayubhiḥ satrā hataṁ somasutā janena ||
  • saṁ yan mahī mithatī: This phrase can be translated as "when the great earth unites." Here, "mahī" refers to the earth, and "mithatī" implies coming together or uniting. In real life, this can be seen in the context of environmental conservation efforts where communities come together to protect the earth.
  • spardhamāne tanūrucā: This translates to "competing with great energy." "Spardhamāne" indicates competition, while "tanūrucā" refers to the energy or vigor. An example of this can be found in sports, where athletes compete with intense energy and determination to achieve their goals.
  • śūrasātā yataite: This means "those who are courageous." "Śūrasātā" refers to bravery or valor. In real life, this can be exemplified by individuals who stand up for justice, such as activists fighting for human rights, showcasing courage in the face of adversity.
  • adevayuṁ vidathe devayubhiḥ: This translates to "the divine powers in the assembly." "Adevayuṁ" refers to divine or spiritual forces, while "vidathe" means assembly or gathering. This can be related to community gatherings or spiritual congregations where people come together to share wisdom and support one another.
  • satrā hataṁ somasutā janena: This means "the offerings made by the people." "Satrā" refers to offerings or sacrifices, "hataṁ" means made or performed, and "janena" refers to the people. In modern contexts, this can be seen in charitable acts where individuals contribute to the welfare of society, such as food drives or fundraising events.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, competition, courage, divine support, and communal offerings. Its significance lies in the reminder that when individuals come together with energy and bravery, supported by divine forces, they can achieve great things. This mantra is applicable in real-life scenarios such as community service, environmental activism, and social justice movements, where collective efforts lead to positive change and upliftment of society.
