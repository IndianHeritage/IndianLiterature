मन्त्र: इमे तुरं मरुतो रामयन्तीमे सहः सहसा ā नमन्ति |इमे शंसाṁ वनुष्यतो नि पान्ति गुरु द्वेषो अररुषे दधन्ति ||
ime turam maruto rāmayantīme sahaḥ sahasa ā namanti | ime śaṁsaṁ vanuṣyato ni pānti guru dveṣo araruṣe dadhanti ||
  • ime turam maruto rāmayantī: This phrase refers to the Maruts, the storm deities in Vedic literature, who are known for their strength and power. The term "turam" signifies speed or swiftness, indicating the dynamic nature of these deities.
  • ime sahaḥ sahasa ā namanti: Here, "sahaḥ" means together or in unity, while "sahasa" refers to strength or might. This line emphasizes the collective power of the Maruts as they come together, bowing down in reverence or humility.
  • ime śaṁsaṁ vanuṣyato ni pānti: "śaṁsaṁ" can be interpreted as blessings or good fortune, and "vanuṣyato" refers to humanity. This line suggests that the Maruts protect and bestow blessings upon humanity, ensuring their well-being.
  • guru dveṣo araruṣe dadhanti: In this context, "guru" means heavy or significant, while "dveṣo" refers to enmity or hostility. "Araruṣe" implies a sense of conflict or struggle. This line indicates that the Maruts can also bring forth challenges or adversities, which are essential for growth and resilience.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the dual nature of the Maruts as both protectors and challengers. In real life, this can be seen in the way we face difficulties and adversities that ultimately lead to personal growth and strength. Just as the Maruts bring storms that can be destructive yet necessary for renewal, we too encounter challenges that shape our character and resilience. The significance of this mantra lies in recognizing the balance between receiving blessings and facing hardships, understanding that both are integral to the human experience. It encourages us to embrace life's challenges with the knowledge that they contribute to our overall well-being and development.
