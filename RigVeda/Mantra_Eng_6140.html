विचिद्वृत्रस्य डोधतो वज्रेण शतपर्वणा |शिरो बिभेद वृष्णिना ||
vi cid vṛtrasya dodhato vajreṇa śataparvaṇā | śiro bibheda vṛṣṇinā ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vi: a prefix meaning "apart" or "asunder."
    • cid: means "to cut" or "to separate."
    • vṛtrasya: refers to "Vritra," a serpent or dragon-like demon in Vedic mythology, symbolizing obstacles or challenges.
    • dodhato: means "striking" or "hitting."
    • vajreṇa: translates to "with the thunderbolt," symbolizing strength and power.
    • śataparvaṇā: means "with a hundred folds," indicating something that is multifaceted or complex.
    • śiro: means "head" or "top."
    • bibheda: means "to pierce" or "to break through."
    • vṛṣṇinā: refers to "the one who is strong" or "the powerful," often associated with the divine or heroic figures.
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra describes a powerful act of overcoming obstacles represented by Vritra.
    • The use of "vajreṇa" signifies the use of immense strength or divine power to break through challenges.
    • The phrase "śataparvaṇā" suggests that the challenges are complex and multifaceted, requiring a strong and decisive action to overcome.
    • Ultimately, the mantra conveys the idea of breaking through difficulties with strength and determination.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal life, when facing a significant challenge such as a health issue, one might draw strength from this mantra, symbolizing the need to confront and overcome the problem with determination.
    • In a professional context, an individual might encounter complex projects that seem insurmountable. The mantra serves as a reminder to tackle these challenges head-on with the necessary strength and strategy.
    • In societal issues, communities facing adversity, such as poverty or discrimination, can find inspiration in this mantra to unite and confront their challenges collectively.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its powerful imagery of overcoming obstacles through strength and determination.
    • It serves as a reminder that challenges, no matter how complex, can be faced and conquered with the right mindset and approach.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, encouraging individuals and communities to harness their inner strength to break through barriers and achieve their goals.
