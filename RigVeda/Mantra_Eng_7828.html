एतं मृजन्ति मार्ज्यम उप द्रोणेष्व आयवः |प्रचक्राणम् महीर इषः ||
etam mṛjanti marjyam upa droṇeṣv āyavaḥ | pracakrāṇam mahīr iṣaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • etam: this
    • mṛjanti: they cleanse or purify
    • marjyam: the cleansed or purified
    • upa: near or towards
    • dronēṣv: in the containers or vessels
    • āyavaḥ: the beings or entities
    • pracakrāṇam: the one who is manifesting or creating
    • mahīr: of the great or the earth
    • iṣaḥ: the will or desire
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the process of cleansing and purification.
    • It suggests that there are entities or beings that are involved in the act of purification, bringing forth a sense of renewal.
    • The mention of "containers" can symbolize the various aspects of life or the universe that hold potential and energy.
    • The phrase "the will of the great" indicates a higher power or universal force that drives creation and manifestation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we often engage in acts of cleansing, whether it be through physical cleaning of our surroundings or through mental and emotional practices like meditation and mindfulness.
    • For instance, when we declutter our homes, we create a more harmonious environment, which can lead to improved mental clarity and well-being.
    • Similarly, in nature, the cycle of seasons represents a form of cleansing, where winter purifies the earth, making way for new growth in spring.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of purification in various forms, whether it be physical, mental, or spiritual.
    • Its significance lies in the understanding that cleansing is essential for renewal and growth, both in personal life and in the broader context of the universe.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more balanced and harmonious existence, encouraging individuals to engage in practices that promote purification and renewal.
