यदाद्य स्थः परावती यदर्वावत्य अश्विनाः |यद्वा पुरू पुरुभुजाऽ यदन्तरिक्ष आगतम् ||
yad adya sthaḥ parāvati yad arvāvaty aśvinā | yad vā purū purubhujā yad antarikṣa ā gatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • adya: today
    • sthaḥ: stands or exists
    • parāvati: the highest or supreme
    • yad: whatever
    • arvāvaty: flows or moves
    • aśvinā: by the Ashvins (the twin gods of medicine and health)
    • yad: whatever
    • vā: or
    • purū: many or abundant
    • purubhujā: those who enjoy or partake
    • yad: whatever
    • antarikṣa: the space or atmosphere
    • ā: to
    • gatam: has gone or reached
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the various manifestations of existence and the divine.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the earthly and the celestial, highlighting how different aspects of life and the universe are interlinked.
    • The mention of the Ashvins signifies the importance of health, healing, and the divine assistance in our lives.
    • It reflects the idea that everything that exists today, whether it is the supreme, the flowing, or the abundant, is part of a larger cosmic order.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In our daily lives, we often encounter situations where we seek help or guidance, much like invoking the Ashvins for health and well-being.
    • For instance, when facing a health crisis, people may turn to doctors (symbolizing the Ashvins) for healing, reflecting the divine intervention in human affairs.
    • The interconnectedness of life can be seen in community efforts, where individuals come together to support one another, embodying the idea of abundance and shared resources.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives and the importance of seeking help and guidance from higher powers.
    • It encourages us to recognize the interconnectedness of all things and to appreciate the abundance that surrounds us.
    • In practical terms, it can inspire us to foster community, seek healing, and acknowledge the divine in our everyday experiences.
