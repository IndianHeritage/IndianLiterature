अग्निरात्रिम्भरद्वाजं गविष्टिरं प्रावन नः कण्वं त्रसदस्युमाहवे |अग्निं वसिष्ठो हवते पुरोहितो मृलीकाय पुरोहितः ||
agnir atrim bharadvājaṁ gaviṣṭhiram prāvan naḥ kaṇvaṁ trasadasyum āhave | agniṁ vasiṣṭho havate purohito mṛḻīkāya purohitaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • agnir: Fire
    • atrim: Atri (a sage)
    • bharadvājaṁ: Bharadvaja (another sage)
    • gaviṣṭhiram: The one who is most excellent in the cow (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • prāvan: May he (Agni) purify
    • naḥ: Our
    • kaṇvaṁ: Kanva (a sage)
    • trasadasyum: The one who is swift
    • āhave: In the invocation
    • vasiṣṭho: Vasistha (a sage)
    • havate: He who is invoked
    • purohito: The priest or the one who performs rituals
    • mṛḻīkāya: For the purpose of prosperity
    • purohitaḥ: The one who is the chief priest
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • Agni is associated with purity and is often seen as a symbol of transformation and energy.
    • The mention of sages like Atri, Bharadvaja, and Vasistha highlights the importance of wisdom and guidance in rituals.
    • The phrase "gaviṣṭhiram" signifies the importance of wealth and nourishment, often represented by cows in Vedic literature.
    • The invocation of Agni is a call for purification and blessings, emphasizing the need for spiritual and material prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of invoking Agni can be seen in various rituals and ceremonies where fire is used as a purifying element, such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies.
    • The emphasis on wisdom from sages can be related to seeking guidance from mentors or elders in our personal and professional lives.
    • The idea of nourishment and wealth can be connected to sustainable living practices, where we seek to maintain balance with nature while ensuring prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine energies for purification and prosperity.
    • It teaches us to seek wisdom and guidance from those who are knowledgeable, emphasizing the role of community and mentorship.
    • In real life, the principles of this mantra can be applied in various aspects, from personal growth to community well-being, reminding us of the interconnectedness of spiritual and material realms.
