को नु वाम मित्रास्तुतो वरुणो वा तनूनाम् |तत्सु वामेषते मतिर्अत्रिभ्य एषते मतिः ||
ko nu vām mitrāstuto varuṇo vā tanūnām | tat su vām eṣate matir atribhya eṣate matiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ko nu: Who indeed
    • vām: You (plural)
    • mitrāstuto: Praised by friends
    • varuṇo: Varuna (the god of cosmic order)
    • vā: Or
    • tanūnām: Of the bodies
    • tat: That
    • su: Good
    • vām: You (plural)
    • eṣate: Is present
    • matir: Intellect or understanding
    • atribha: Without any hindrance
    • eṣate: Is present
    • matiḥ: Intellect or understanding
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a rhetorical question about who among the divine beings, particularly Varuna, is praised by friends.
    • It emphasizes the importance of good understanding and intellect that is free from obstacles.
    • The invocation of Varuna signifies the cosmic order and moral authority, suggesting that true understanding comes from aligning oneself with universal principles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, when team members support each other and share knowledge, they create an environment where collective understanding flourishes, akin to the praise of friends.
    • In personal relationships, when individuals communicate openly and honestly, they build a strong foundation of trust and understanding, reflecting the essence of the mantra.
    • In education, when students collaborate and help each other, they enhance their learning experience, embodying the idea of shared intellect and support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of friendship, support, and the pursuit of understanding in both personal and communal contexts.
    • It teaches us that true wisdom and intellect are achieved when we are free from hindrances and are in harmony with the cosmic order.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger relationships, better teamwork, and a more profound understanding of our place in the universe.
