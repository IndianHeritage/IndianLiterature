न ते वार्तास्ति राधसा इन्द्र देवो न मर्त्यः |यद दित्ससि स्तुतो मगहम् ||
na te vartāsti rādhasa indra devo na martyaḥ | yad ditsasi stuto magham ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na: not
    • te: your
    • vartāsti: existence or presence
    • rādhasa: in the realm of the mortal
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • devo: divine
    • na: not
    • martyaḥ: mortal
    • yat: that which
    • ditsasi: you desire
    • stuto: praised
    • magham: wealth or abundance
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, the king of the gods, emphasizing that he is beyond the mortal realm.
    • It suggests that the desires of mortals do not affect the divine presence of Indra.
    • The phrase "yad ditsasi" indicates that whatever Indra desires or wishes for is significant and worthy of praise.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader (akin to Indra) may have a vision that transcends the immediate concerns of employees (the mortals). Their ability to see beyond the present challenges can lead to greater success.
    • In personal life, individuals often have aspirations that seem unattainable. This mantra can inspire one to pursue their goals, understanding that divine or universal forces may support their journey.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the relationship between the divine and the mortal, highlighting that while mortals may have desires, the divine operates on a different level.
    • It teaches us to recognize the greater forces at play in our lives and encourages us to align our desires with a higher purpose.
    • In real-life scenarios, this understanding can lead to a more profound sense of purpose and fulfillment, as we strive to connect our aspirations with the divine will.
