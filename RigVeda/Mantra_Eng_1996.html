द्यौः वः पिता पृथिवी माता सोमो भ्राता इतिḥ स्वसा |अदृष्टा विश्वदृष्टास तिष्ठतेलयता सु कम ||
dyaur vaḥ pitā pṛthivī mātā somo bhrātāditiḥ svasā | adṛṣṭā viśvadṛṣṭās tiṣṭhatelayatā su kam ||
  • Translation: "Heaven is the father, Earth is the mother; the Moon is the brother, and the unseen is the sister. All that is seen and unseen exists in harmony."
  • Meaning of Words:
    • Dyaur: Heaven or sky, representing the divine and the expansive nature of existence.
    • Vaḥ: Is or are, indicating a state of being.
    • Pitā: Father, symbolizing authority, protection, and guidance.
    • Pṛthivī: Earth, representing nurturing, sustenance, and the physical realm.
    • Mātā: Mother, embodying care, love, and the source of life.
    • Somā: Moon, often associated with calmness, reflection, and the cyclical nature of life.
    • Bhrātā: Brother, indicating companionship and support.
    • Adṛṣṭā: Unseen, referring to the spiritual or intangible aspects of existence.
    • Viśvadṛṣṭās: All that is seen, encompassing the material world.
    • Tiṣṭhatelayatā: Existing in harmony, suggesting balance and coexistence.
    • Su kam: Goodness or well-being, indicating a positive state of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The relationship between nature and humanity can be seen in agriculture, where the Earth (Pṛthivī) provides resources, and the sky (Dyaur) influences weather patterns essential for growth.
    • In family dynamics, the roles of father (Pitā) and mother (Mātā) illustrate the balance of authority and nurturing, which is crucial for a child's development.
    • The Moon (Somā) serves as a reminder of the cycles in life, such as the phases of the moon reflecting the ups and downs we experience.
    • In community settings, the brother (Bhrātā) symbolizes the support systems we build, emphasizing the importance of relationships in overcoming challenges.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the interconnectedness of all elements of existence, highlighting the roles of the divine, nature, and relationships in our lives. It teaches us to appreciate the balance between the seen and unseen, urging us to live in harmony with our environment and each other. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire us to foster relationships, respect nature, and seek balance in our lives, ultimately leading to a state of well-being (Su kam).
