उद अग्नि तिष्ठ प्रत्य आ तनुष्व न्य अमित्रां ओषतात तिग्महेते |यो नो अरातिं समिधाना चक्रे नीचां तं धक्ष्य अतसं न शुष्कम् ||
ud agne tiṣṭha praty ā tanuṣva ny a1mitrām̐ oṣatāt tigmahete | yo no arātiṁ samidhāna cakre nīcā taṁ dhakṣy atasaṁ na śuṣkam ||
  • ud agne tiṣṭha: This phrase translates to "O Agni, arise!" Agni is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, light, and transformation. In real life, this can be seen as a call to awaken one's inner potential and drive.
  • praty ā tanuṣva: This means "direct your energy." It emphasizes the importance of focusing one's efforts and intentions towards a specific goal. For example, in a professional setting, this could relate to setting clear objectives for a project.
  • ny a1mitrām̐ oṣatāt tigmahete: This can be interpreted as "may you dispel our enemies with your fierce energy." In a modern context, this could refer to overcoming challenges or adversities in life, such as competition in business or personal struggles.
  • yo no arātiṁ samidhāna cakre: This translates to "whoever has kindled our enmity." It suggests the idea of addressing conflicts or negative influences in our lives. For instance, in relationships, it may refer to resolving misunderstandings or disputes.
  • nīcā taṁ dhakṣy atasaṁ na śuṣkam: This means "may we burn away the lowly and not let it dry up." This can be seen as a metaphor for eliminating negativity or unproductive habits. In practical terms, it could relate to personal development, such as letting go of toxic relationships or habits that hinder growth.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of awakening one's inner strength, focusing energy towards positive goals, and overcoming adversities. It serves as a reminder to confront conflicts and eliminate negativity in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in personal development, professional endeavors, and interpersonal relationships, guiding individuals towards a more empowered and fulfilling existence.
