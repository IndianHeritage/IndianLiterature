सत्यं तद् तुर्वशे यदौ विदानो अह्नवाय्यम् |व्य आनट तुर्वणे शमि ||
satyaṁ tat turvaśe yadau vidāno ahnavāyyam | vy ānaṭ turvaṇe śami ||
  • Meaning of the Mantra:
    • Satyaṁ: Truth or reality. In many cultures, truth is considered a fundamental virtue.
    • Tat: That. This word often refers to something beyond the immediate, a higher truth or reality.
    • Turvaśe: To the one who is powerful or strong. This can refer to a deity or a higher power.
    • Yadau: When or in which. This indicates a condition or a time frame.
    • Vidāno: Knowing or understanding. It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom.
    • Ahnavāyyam: The one who is worthy of worship or reverence. This can refer to a divine being or a person of great virtue.
    • Vy: A prefix that can denote a sense of completeness or fullness.
    • Ānaṭ: To be free or liberated. This suggests a state of being unbound or unrestricted.
    • Turvaṇe: In the powerful or strong. This reinforces the idea of strength and capability.
    • Śami: A tree, often associated with shelter and protection. In many cultures, trees symbolize life and stability.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The pursuit of truth in personal relationships, where honesty fosters trust and understanding.
    • In leadership, a leader who embodies truth and strength can inspire their team to achieve greater goals.
    • In education, the quest for knowledge is paramount; understanding the truth leads to personal and societal growth.
    • Environmental conservation efforts can be seen as a way to honor the strength of nature, represented by trees like the śami, which provide shelter and sustenance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of truth, knowledge, and strength in our lives.
    • It teaches us that understanding and revering the truth leads to liberation and empowerment.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying these principles can lead to healthier relationships, effective leadership, and a deeper connection with nature.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to seek truth and wisdom, which are essential for personal and collective growth.
