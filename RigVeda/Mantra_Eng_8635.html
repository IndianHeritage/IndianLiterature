सुत इन्दो पवित्रा नृभिर यतो विनीयसे |इन्द्राय मत्सरिन्तमश्चमूष्वानि शीदसि ||
suta indo pavitra ā nṛbhir yato vi nīyase | indrāya matsarintamaś camūṣv ā ni ṣīdasi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • suta: son or offspring
    • indo: of Indra, the king of gods
    • pavitra: pure or sacred
    • ā: from
    • nṛbhir: by men or humans
    • yato: where or from which
    • vi: apart or separately
    • nīyase: you are led or guided
    • indrāya: for Indra
    • matsarintama: the most envious or competitive
    • ścamūṣv: among the armies or hosts
    • ā: to
    • ni: down or below
    • ṣīdasi: you sit or reside
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, emphasizing the purity and sacredness of the offerings made by humans. It highlights the idea that from pure intentions and actions, one can gain the favor of the divine, particularly in competitive situations, where Indra is seen as a protector and guide.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive environment, such as a workplace or academic setting, individuals often strive to achieve their goals while maintaining integrity and purity of purpose. This mantra serves as a reminder that success should be pursued with a pure heart and intentions.
    • During rituals or ceremonies, offerings made with sincerity and purity are believed to attract divine blessings. This can be seen in various cultural practices where individuals seek guidance and support from higher powers.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on purity and the divine guidance that comes from sincere actions. It teaches that in our pursuits, whether in competition or in daily life, maintaining a pure heart and intentions can lead to divine support and success. This principle is applicable in various real-life scenarios, encouraging individuals to act with integrity and seek blessings in their endeavors.
