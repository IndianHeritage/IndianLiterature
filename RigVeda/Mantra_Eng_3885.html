त्यस्य चिन् महतो निर मृगस्य वधार जग्हान तविषीभिर इन्द्रः |य एक इद अप्रतिर मन्यमान आद अस्माद अन्यो जनिषṭ तव्यān ||
tyasya cin mahato nir mṛgasya vadhar jaghāna taviṣībhir indraḥ | ya eka id apratir manyamāna ād asmād anyo ajaniṣṭa tavyān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tyasya: of that
    • cin: indeed
    • mahato: of the great
    • nir: without
    • mṛgasya: of the deer (or creature)
    • vadhar: the killer
    • jaghāna: has struck down
    • taviṣībhir: with strength
    • indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • ya: who
    • eka: alone
    • id: this
    • apratir: without a rival
    • manyamāna: considering
    • ād: from
    • asmād: this
    • anyo: another
    • ajaniṣṭa: has not been born
    • tavyān: worthy of worship
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the great Indra, who is depicted as a powerful deity capable of overcoming challenges and adversaries.
    • The phrase "tyasya cin mahato" emphasizes the greatness of Indra, suggesting that he is beyond comparison.
    • Indra is portrayed as a protector, striking down the "mṛgasya" (the creature or enemy) with strength, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil.
    • The mantra also highlights the uniqueness of Indra, stating that he is "apratir" (without a rival), which signifies his unparalleled power and authority.
    • In a broader sense, this can be interpreted as a call to recognize and honor the strength and protection that comes from divine forces in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal struggle or conflict, individuals may seek strength and guidance from a higher power, much like the invocation of Indra in this mantra.
    • In leadership, the qualities of decisiveness and strength, akin to Indra's character, are essential for overcoming challenges and guiding others.
    • In nature, the cycle of life and death can be seen as a reflection of this mantra, where the strong prevail and maintain balance in ecosystems.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the strength and protection that can be found in divine forces, encouraging individuals to seek guidance and support in their lives.
    • It emphasizes the importance of recognizing one's own strength and the power of unity in overcoming adversities.
    • In a world filled with challenges, invoking the qualities of Indra can inspire resilience and courage, making this mantra relevant in various aspects of life, from personal struggles to leadership roles.
