इमं महे विदथ्याय शूषं शाश्वत कृत्वा ईड्याय प्र जभ्रुः |श्रिणोतु नो दाम्येभिर अनीकैः श्रिणोत्व अग्निर दिव्यैर अजस्रः ||
imam mahe vidathyāya śūṣaṁ śaśvat kṛtva īḍyāya pra jabhruḥ | śṛṇotu no damyebhir anīkaiḥ śṛṇotv agnir divyair ajasraḥ ||
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "May we, through our great wisdom, create a lasting bond with the divine. Let us be heard by the powerful forces, and may the divine fire listen to us always."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • imam: This refers to "this" or "here," indicating the present moment or the current situation.
    • mahe: Meaning "great" or "mighty," it signifies the importance of wisdom and knowledge.
    • vidathyāya: This word relates to "wisdom" or "intellect," emphasizing the need for understanding in our actions.
    • śūṣaṁ: This can be interpreted as "lasting" or "eternal," suggesting the desire for enduring connections.
    • śaśvat: Meaning "everlasting" or "perpetual," it reinforces the idea of continuity in our relationships with the divine.
    • kṛtva: This means "to create" or "to make," indicating an active role in forming connections.
    • īḍyāya: Referring to "worship" or "adoration," it highlights the importance of reverence in our interactions.
    • pra jabhruḥ: This phrase means "let us be heard," emphasizing the need for communication with the divine.
    • śṛṇotu: Meaning "may he listen," it is a call for attention from the divine forces.
    • no: This means "us," indicating a collective approach.
    • damyebhir: Referring to "powerful forces," it acknowledges the strength of the elements around us.
    • anīkaiḥ: This means "with our groups" or "with our communities," emphasizing the importance of unity.
    • śṛṇotv: Again, a call for listening, reinforcing the desire for divine attention.
    • agnir: Referring to "fire," which symbolizes transformation and energy.
    • divyair: Meaning "divine," it indicates the sacred nature of the forces we are addressing.
    • ajasraḥ: This means "always" or "constantly," suggesting a continuous relationship with the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, people often come together to share their wisdom and experiences, creating a lasting bond that strengthens their relationships.
    • In personal development, individuals seek knowledge and understanding to create a meaningful life, much like the wisdom sought in this mantra.
    • During rituals or prayers, people call upon divine forces for guidance and support, reflecting the essence of being heard by the divine.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for wisdom, unity, and divine connection. It emphasizes the importance of creating lasting relationships with the divine and the community. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as community gatherings, personal growth, and spiritual practices. By fostering wisdom and unity, individuals can enhance their lives and the lives of those around them, creating a harmonious existence that resonates with the eternal truths of the universe.
