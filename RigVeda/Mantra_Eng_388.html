परā चिच्छीर्षा ववृजुस त इन्द्रायज्वानो यज्वभिः स्पर्धमानाः |प्र यद दिवो हरिवः स्थातार उग्र निर अव्रताम् अधमो रोधस्योः ||
parā cic chīrṣā vavṛjus ta indrāyajvāno yajvabhiḥ spardhamānāḥ | pra yad divo harivaḥ sthātar ugra nir avratām̐ adhamo rodasyoḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • parā: supreme, beyond
    • cic: consciousness, awareness
    • chīrṣā: the highest, the peak
    • vavṛjus: they have attained, they have achieved
    • ta: that
    • indrāyajvāno: those who worship Indra, the deity of rain and thunder
    • yajvabhiḥ: with offerings, through sacrifices
    • spardhamānāḥ: competing, striving
    • pra: towards, for
    • yad: when, that which
    • divo: of the sky, celestial
    • harivaḥ: the swift, the powerful
    • sthātar: the one who stands firm
    • ugra: fierce, strong
    • nir: without
    • avratām̐: transgression, deviation
    • adhamo: the lowest, inferior
    • rodasyoḥ: of the two worlds, the earth and the sky
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the supreme consciousness that has achieved the highest state of awareness.
    • It emphasizes the importance of worship and offerings to Indra, who represents strength and power.
    • The phrase "spardhamānāḥ" indicates a sense of competition and striving for excellence, which can be seen in various aspects of life, such as sports, academics, and personal development.
    • The mantra also highlights the significance of standing firm and strong in one's beliefs and actions, akin to the steadfastness required in leadership roles.
    • The reference to "the lowest" suggests a warning against falling into mediocrity or complacency, urging individuals to rise above challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In sports, athletes often strive for excellence, competing against each other while also pushing their own limits, much like the "spardhamānāḥ" in the mantra.
    • In the corporate world, leaders who embody the qualities of Indra—strength, decisiveness, and the ability to inspire—are often the most successful.
    • In personal development, individuals who seek higher consciousness and awareness through meditation or self-reflection can relate to the idea of achieving the "parā" state.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of striving for excellence, maintaining strong values, and the power of conscious awareness.
    • It encourages individuals to engage in practices that elevate their consciousness and to remain steadfast in their pursuits, whether in personal, professional, or spiritual realms.
    • Ultimately, the mantra signifies the journey towards achieving one's highest potential while remaining grounded and connected to the divine.
