पिबन्ति मित्रो आर्यमा तनā पूतस्य वरुणः |त्रिषदस्थस्य जावतः ||
pibanti mitro aryamā tanā pūtasya varuṇaḥ | triṣadhasthasya jāvataḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pibanti mitro aryamā tanā pūtasya varuṇaḥ | triṣadhasthasya jāvataḥ ||"
  • Translation: "The friends (mitra) drink (pibanti) the pure (pūtasya) essence (tanā) of Aryama, while Varuna watches over the threefold (triṣadhasthasya) existence (jāvataḥ)."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pibanti: to drink, to consume
    • mitro: friends, allies
    • aryamā: a divine figure associated with truth and order
    • tanā: essence, body, or form
    • pūtasya: pure, clean, sacred
    • varuṇaḥ: a deity representing cosmic order and moral authority
    • triṣadhasthasya: threefold, indicating the three realms of existence (physical, mental, spiritual)
    • jāvataḥ: living, existing
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of friendship and community, suggesting that the essence of purity and truth is shared among allies.
    • Varuna, as a deity, represents the moral and cosmic order, indicating that there is a higher power overseeing the actions and relationships of individuals.
    • The mention of "threefold existence" can be interpreted as a reminder to maintain balance in our physical, mental, and spiritual lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be reflected in how friends support each other in times of need, sharing wisdom and resources to uplift one another.
    • In a workplace, collaboration among colleagues can be seen as a manifestation of this mantra, where the collective effort leads to a pure and productive environment.
    • On a personal level, individuals can strive to maintain purity in their thoughts and actions, ensuring that their relationships are built on trust and mutual respect.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of friendship, purity, and moral order in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to foster strong, supportive relationships while being mindful of the higher principles that govern our existence.
    • In real life, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to harmonious relationships, a balanced life, and a deeper understanding of our interconnectedness with others and the universe.
