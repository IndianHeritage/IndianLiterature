अनु पूर्वाण्य ओक्यā साम्राज्यस्य सश्चिम |मित्रस्य व्रता वरुणस्य दीर्घश्रुत ||
anu pūrvāṇy okyā sāmrājyasya saścima | mitrasya vratā varuṇasya dīrghaśrut ||
  • anu pūrvāṇy okyā sāmrājyasya saścima
    • anu - This word means "following" or "after." It signifies continuity and the importance of what comes after.
    • pūrvāṇy - This translates to "the former" or "the previous." It emphasizes the significance of past actions and their influence on the present.
    • okyā - This can be interpreted as "the spoken" or "the declared." It refers to the importance of words and promises made.
    • sāmrājyasya - This means "of the kingdom" or "of sovereignty." It highlights the concept of governance and authority.
    • saścima - This translates to "and the last" or "the ultimate." It signifies the culmination of efforts and the final outcomes.
  • mitrasya vratā varuṇasya dīrghaśrut
    • mitrasya - This means "of Mitra," who is often associated with friendship, harmony, and the sun. It represents the importance of relationships and alliances.
    • vratā - This translates to "vows" or "commitments." It signifies the promises made in relationships and the importance of keeping them.
    • varuṇasya - This refers to "of Varuna," the god of cosmic order and water. It symbolizes the moral and ethical dimensions of life.
    • dīrghaśrut - This means "long-heard" or "well-known." It emphasizes the wisdom that comes from tradition and the teachings passed down through generations.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of past actions, spoken words, and the commitments we make in our relationships. It highlights the importance of maintaining harmony and ethical conduct in governance and personal interactions. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In leadership roles, understanding the impact of past decisions on current governance.
  • In personal relationships, valuing the promises made and nurturing friendships.
  • In community building, fostering a sense of unity and shared values based on mutual respect and ethical conduct.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of our actions, words, and commitments in shaping our lives and the world around us.
