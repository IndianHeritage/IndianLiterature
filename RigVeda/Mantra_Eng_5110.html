बृहस्पतिः सम अजयद् वसूनि महो व्रजान गोमतो देव एषः |अपः सिषासन स्वार अप्रतीतो बृहस्पतिर हन्त्य अमित्रं अर्कैः ||
bṛhaspatiḥ sam ajayad vasūni maho vrajān gomato deva eṣaḥ | apaḥ siṣāsan sva1r apratīto bṛhaspatir hanty amitram arkaiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "bṛhaspatiḥ sam ajayad vasūni maho vrajān gomato deva eṣaḥ | apaḥ siṣāsan sva1r apratīto bṛhaspatir hanty amitram arkaiḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Bṛhaspati, the lord of wisdom, has conquered the great wealth and the powerful forces. This divine being, who is the protector of the righteous, drives away the enemies with his radiant light."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Bṛhaspatiḥ: The deity representing wisdom and knowledge.
    • sam ajayad: has conquered or achieved.
    • vasūni: wealth or riches.
    • maho: great or powerful.
    • vrajān: forces or groups.
    • gomato: protector or guardian.
    • deva: divine being or god.
    • eṣaḥ: this.
    • apaḥ: waters or life-giving elements.
    • siṣāsan: to sit or reside.
    • sva1r: in one's own realm or domain.
    • apratīto: unchallenged or unparalleled.
    • bṛhaspatir: Bṛhaspati, the lord of wisdom.
    • hanty: destroys or eliminates.
    • amitram: enemies or adversaries.
    • arkaiḥ: with light or radiance.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the power of wisdom and knowledge in overcoming challenges and adversities in life.
    • Bṛhaspati symbolizes the importance of intellect and strategic thinking in achieving success and prosperity.
    • The reference to driving away enemies with light signifies the triumph of good over evil, and the protective nature of wisdom.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In personal life, individuals can invoke the essence of this mantra by seeking knowledge and wisdom to navigate through difficult situations.
    • In professional settings, it can be applied by making informed decisions and using strategic thinking to overcome competition.
    • In a broader societal context, the mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of education and enlightenment in fostering peace and harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra encapsulates the idea that wisdom is a powerful tool that can lead to victory over challenges and adversaries.
    • By embracing the teachings of Bṛhaspati, individuals and societies can strive for a more prosperous and harmonious existence.
