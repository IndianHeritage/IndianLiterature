स मानविषु दूलभो विक्षु प्रावीः अमर्त्यः |दूतो विश्वेषाम्भुवत ||
sa mānuṣīṣu dūḻabho vikṣu prāvīr amartyaḥ | dūto viśveṣām bhuvat ||
  • sa mānuṣīṣu: This phrase translates to "among humans." It signifies the connection and interaction between individuals in society.
  • dūḻabho: Meaning "rare" or "difficult to find," this word emphasizes the uniqueness of certain qualities or individuals.
  • vikṣu: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is vigilant" or "the one who observes." It suggests the importance of awareness and mindfulness in our actions.
  • prāvīr: This word means "brave" or "valiant." It highlights the qualities of courage and strength in facing challenges.
  • amartyaḥ: Translated as "immortal," this term refers to the eternal aspect of the soul or spirit, transcending physical existence.
  • dūto: Meaning "messenger," this word signifies the role of communication and the sharing of knowledge or wisdom.
  • viśveṣām: This term translates to "of all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • bhuvat: Meaning "exists" or "is present," this word emphasizes the reality of existence and the importance of being aware of our surroundings.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of human existence, emphasizing the rarity of true bravery and awareness among people. It highlights the role of messengers in sharing wisdom and the importance of recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as leadership, where a brave and vigilant leader can inspire others, or in community building, where effective communication fosters unity and understanding. The mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate these qualities within ourselves and to appreciate the unique contributions of others in our lives.
