इदं वपुर निवचनं जनासश्चरन्ति यन्नद्यस्तस्थुरापः |द्वे यदīm बिभृतो मातुर अन्ये इहेह जाते यम्याः सबन्धू ||
idaṁ vapur nivacanaṁ janāsaś caranti yan nadyas tasthur āpaḥ | dve yad īm bibhṛto mātur anye iheha jāte yamyā3 sabandhū ||
  • idaṁ vapur nivacanaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "this body is a manifestation of speech." It emphasizes the connection between our physical existence and the words we speak. In real life, this can be seen in how our communication shapes our identity and relationships. For example, a person's reputation is often built on their words and how they express themselves.
  • janāsaś caranti: This means "the people move or act." It highlights the dynamic nature of human life and the actions of individuals within society. In a practical sense, this can be observed in community activities, where people come together to achieve common goals, such as organizing a local event or participating in social movements.
  • yan nadyas tasthur āpaḥ: This translates to "like the rivers that flow." Rivers are often seen as symbols of life, continuity, and nourishment. In real life, rivers provide water for drinking, agriculture, and transportation, illustrating the importance of natural resources in sustaining human life and communities.
  • dve yad īm bibhṛto: This phrase means "two that are held." It can refer to the duality of existence, such as the balance between material and spiritual life. In everyday life, this can be seen in the choices people make between pursuing career ambitions and nurturing personal relationships.
  • mātur anye iheha jāte: This translates to "others are born from the mother here." It signifies the nurturing aspect of life and the importance of maternal figures in our existence. In real life, this can be reflected in the roles of mothers and caregivers who provide support and guidance to individuals as they grow and develop.
  • yamyā3 sabandhū: This means "those who are related." It emphasizes the significance of relationships and connections among people. In practical terms, this can be seen in family ties, friendships, and community bonds that provide support and a sense of belonging.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the interconnectedness of life, speech, action, and relationships. It serves as a reminder of the importance of communication in shaping our identities and the roles we play in our communities. In real life, it encourages us to recognize the value of our words, the significance of our actions, and the nurturing relationships that sustain us. By understanding and applying these principles, we can foster a more harmonious and supportive environment in our personal and social lives.
