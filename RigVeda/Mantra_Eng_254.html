कस्य नूनं कतमस्यामृतानाम् मनामहे चारु देवस्य नाम |को नो मह्या अदितये पुनर दात पिताṁ च दृष्टेयं मातराṁ च ||
kasya nūnaṁ katamasyāmṛtānām manāmahe cāru devasya nāma | ko no mahyā aditaye punar dāt pitaraṁ ca dṛśeyam mātaraṁ ca ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kasya: Whose
    • nūnaṁ: Indeed
    • katamasyām: Of which
    • amṛtānām: Of the immortal
    • manāmahe: We meditate
    • cāru: Beautiful
    • devasya: Of the deity
    • nāma: Name
    • ko: Who
    • no: For us
    • mahyā: To me
    • aditaye: To the infinite
    • punar: Again
    • dāt: Give
    • pitaraṁ: Father
    • ca: And
    • dṛśeyam: May be seen
    • mātaraṁ: Mother
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep inquiry into the nature of divinity and existence.
    • It reflects a yearning to understand the essence of the immortal beings and their connection to the divine.
    • The phrase "Whose indeed is the name of the beautiful deity" suggests a quest for knowledge and understanding of the divine attributes.
    • The invocation of "father" and "mother" signifies the importance of parental figures in our lives, representing guidance and nurturing.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, individuals often seek guidance from their parents or mentors when faced with challenges, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In a broader societal context, communities may come together to honor and seek blessings from deities, recognizing the importance of divine guidance in their lives.
    • In moments of uncertainty, people may meditate or pray, similar to the mantra's call for meditation on the divine name, seeking clarity and strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of understanding our roots, both in terms of divine connection and familial bonds.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and guidance from both the divine and their immediate family, fostering a sense of belonging and purpose.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in moments of reflection, meditation, and when seeking guidance in personal or communal challenges.
