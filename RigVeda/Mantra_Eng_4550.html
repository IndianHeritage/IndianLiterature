अध द्यौश्चित ते अप सानु वज्राद द्वितानमद भियस स्वस्य मन्योः |अहिं यद इन्द्रो अभ्य ओहसानाṁ निचिद विश्वायुः शयथे जग्हान ||
adha dyauś cit te apa sā nu vajrād dvitānamad bhiyasā svasya manyoḥ | ahiṁ yad indro abhy ohasānaṁ ni cid viśvāyuḥ śayathe jaghāna ||
  • Adha dyauś cit te apa sā nu vajrād dvitānamad bhiyasā svasya manyoḥ
    • Adha dyauś: This phrase translates to "down to the sky," indicating a connection between the heavens and the earth.
    • cit te: This means "your consciousness" or "your awareness," suggesting a divine presence or higher intelligence.
    • apa sā: This can be interpreted as "from the waters," which often symbolizes life and nourishment.
    • nu vajrād: "Now from the thunderbolt," referring to strength and power, often associated with the deity Indra.
    • dvitānamad: This means "twofold," indicating duality or the balance of opposites.
    • bhiyasā svasya manyoḥ: This translates to "by the fear of your own mind," suggesting that our thoughts and perceptions shape our reality.
  • Ahiṁ yad indro abhy ohasānaṁ ni cid viśvāyuḥ śayathe jaghāna
    • Ahiṁ: This word means "non-violence" or "peace," emphasizing the importance of harmony.
    • yad indro: "What Indra," referring to the king of gods, who represents strength and protection.
    • abhy ohasānaṁ: This means "by the power of light," indicating enlightenment and clarity.
    • ni cid viśvāyuḥ: "In all directions," suggesting a universal presence or influence.
    • śayathe jaghāna: This translates to "lies down in the world," indicating the manifestation of divine energy in the physical realm.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of the universe, the power of consciousness, and the importance of non-violence. It teaches us that our thoughts and perceptions shape our reality, and that by aligning ourselves with higher consciousness, we can manifest peace and strength in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways:

  • In personal development, understanding that our mindset influences our experiences can lead to more positive outcomes.
  • In conflict resolution, promoting non-violence and harmony can help in creating peaceful environments.
  • In leadership, embodying the qualities of strength and protection, like Indra, can inspire and uplift others.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of our thoughts and the importance of living in harmony with ourselves and the world around us.
