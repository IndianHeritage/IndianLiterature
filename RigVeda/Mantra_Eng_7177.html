सहस्रे पृषतिनाम् अधि श्चन्द्रम् बृहत् पृथु |शुक्रं हिरण्यं आ ददे ||
sahasre pṛṣatīnām adhi ścandram bṛhat pṛthu | śukraṁ hiraṇyam ā dade ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahasre: thousand
    • pṛṣatīnām: of the horses
    • adhi: upon
    • ścandram: the moon
    • bṛhat: great
    • pṛthu: vast
    • śukraṁ: bright or pure
    • hiraṇyam: gold
    • ā: to
    • dade: give
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the vastness and brilliance of the universe, symbolized by the moon and gold.
    • The phrase "sahasre pṛṣatīnām" suggests a multitude, indicating abundance and prosperity, often associated with horses in ancient texts, symbolizing strength and wealth.
    • The mention of "ścandram bṛhat pṛthu" refers to the great moon, which is often seen as a source of nourishment and guidance in the night sky.
    • "śukraṁ hiraṇyam" signifies purity and wealth, suggesting that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses spiritual richness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the moon's phases are often used to determine the best times for planting and harvesting, reflecting the connection between celestial bodies and earthly prosperity.
    • In modern finance, the idea of "wealth" extends beyond mere money to include investments in education, health, and relationships, echoing the mantra's emphasis on holistic abundance.
    • In personal development, the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom can be seen as a way to achieve the "gold" of life, aligning with the mantra's call for purity and greatness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of abundance, both materially and spiritually, urging individuals to seek a balance between wealth and wisdom.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that true prosperity comes from a harmonious relationship with the universe, nature, and oneself.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue holistic growth, emphasizing the importance of nurturing both physical and spiritual well-being.
