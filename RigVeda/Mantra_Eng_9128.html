प्र मा युयुज्रे प्रयुजो जनानां वहामि स्म पूषणम अन्तरेंण |विश्वे देवासो अध माम अरक्षन् दुःशासुर आगाद इति घोष आसीत ||
pra mā yuyujre prayujo janānāṁ vahāmi sma pūṣaṇam antareṇa | viśve devāso adha mām arakṣan duḥśāsur āgād iti ghoṣa āsīt ||
  • pra mā yuyujre prayujo janānāṁ vahāmi sma pūṣaṇam antareṇa
    • pra mā: This can be interpreted as "I offer" or "I invoke." It signifies the act of calling upon divine forces.
    • yuyujre: This means "to unite" or "to bring together." It emphasizes the importance of harmony and cooperation among people.
    • prayujo: This refers to "those who are engaged" or "those who are devoted." It highlights the role of dedicated individuals in society.
    • janānāṁ: This translates to "of the people." It signifies the collective aspect of humanity.
    • vahāmi: This means "I carry" or "I bear." It indicates the responsibility of the speaker to support and uplift others.
    • sma: This is an affirmation, meaning "indeed" or "certainly." It adds emphasis to the statement.
    • pūṣaṇam: This refers to "the nourisher" or "the sustainer," often associated with the Sun or divine energy that provides life.
    • antareṇa: This means "in the midst" or "between." It suggests a connection or presence among individuals.
  • viśve devāso adha mām arakṣan duḥśāsur āgād iti ghoṣa āsīt
    • viśve devāso: This translates to "all the gods." It signifies the universal divine presence and the interconnectedness of all spiritual forces.
    • adha: This means "below" or "under." It indicates a grounding or foundational aspect.
    • mām: This means "me" or "myself," referring to the speaker's personal experience or plea.
    • arakṣan: This translates to "protection" or "safeguarding." It emphasizes the need for divine protection in life.
    • duḥśāsur: This refers to "the evil one" or "the malevolent force." It represents challenges or adversities faced in life.
    • āgād: This means "came" or "approached." It indicates the arrival of difficulties or negative influences.
    • iti ghoṣa āsīt: This translates to "thus was the proclamation." It signifies the importance of declaring one's intentions and the reality of the situation.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, support, and divine protection in the face of challenges. It calls upon the collective strength of humanity and the divine to overcome adversities. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community building, teamwork in workplaces, or even in personal relationships where mutual support is essential. The invocation of divine energy serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our struggles and that there is a higher power that can guide and protect us. This mantra encourages individuals to come together, support one another, and seek divine assistance in navigating life's challenges.
