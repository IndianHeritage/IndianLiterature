आविरभून्महि माग्होनमेषां विश्वं जीवंतमसो निरमोचि। महि ज्योतिः पितृभिर dattमागादुरुः पन्थादक्षिणायादर्शि॥
āvir abhūn mahi māghonam eṣāṁ viśvaṁ jīvaṁ tamaso nir amoci | mahi jyotiḥ pitṛbhir dattam āgād uruḥ panthā dakṣiṇāyā adarśi ||
  • āvir abhūn mahi māghonam: This phrase can be translated as "Let the great and powerful ones manifest." Here, "āvir" means to manifest or appear, "abhūn" refers to the great or powerful, and "mahi māghonam" signifies the abundance or greatness of the divine forces.
  • eṣāṁ viśvaṁ jīvaṁ: This translates to "this entire universe is alive." "Eṣāṁ" means this, "viśvaṁ" refers to the universe, and "jīvaṁ" means living or life. This part emphasizes the interconnectedness of all life forms and the universe.
  • tamaso nir amoci: This means "free from darkness." "Tamasa" refers to darkness or ignorance, "nir" means without, and "amoci" signifies liberation or freedom. This suggests a call for enlightenment and the removal of ignorance.
  • mahi jyotiḥ: This translates to "great light." "Mahi" means great, and "jyotiḥ" refers to light. This symbolizes knowledge, wisdom, and divine illumination.
  • pitṛbhir dattam: This means "given by the ancestors." "Pitṛbhir" refers to ancestors or forefathers, and "dattam" means given or bestowed. This highlights the importance of ancestral wisdom and guidance in our lives.
  • āgād uruḥ panthā: This translates to "the vast path has been revealed." "Āgād" means revealed or shown, "uruḥ" refers to vastness, and "panthā" means path. This indicates that a broad and enlightened path is available for us to follow.
  • dakṣiṇāyā adarśi: This means "towards the south, it has been seen." "Dakṣiṇāyā" refers to the south, and "adarśi" means seen or perceived. In many cultures, the south is associated with prosperity and growth, suggesting that following this path leads to abundance.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of enlightenment, the interconnectedness of life, and the wisdom passed down from ancestors. It calls for the manifestation of divine forces, the pursuit of knowledge, and the liberation from ignorance. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various ways, such as in personal development, community building, and spiritual practices. By seeking knowledge and wisdom, we can navigate our lives more effectively, fostering a sense of unity and purpose. The significance of this mantra lies in its timeless message of striving for enlightenment and recognizing the guidance of those who came before us, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
