अद्रिभिः सुतः पवते गभस्त्योर्वृषायते नभसां वेपते मती |स मोदते नासते साधते गिरा नेनिक्ते अप्सु यजते परीमणि ||
adribhiḥ sutaḥ pavate gabhastyor vṛṣāyate nabhasā vepate matī | sa modate nasate sādhate girā nenikte apsu yajate parīmaṇi ||
  • adribhiḥ sutaḥ: This phrase translates to "born from the mountains." In a real-life context, this can symbolize strength and stability, much like the mountains that stand firm against the elements.
  • pavate: This means "to blow" or "to purify." In our lives, this can be likened to the cleansing power of the wind, which can remove impurities and bring freshness, similar to how we seek to cleanse our minds and spirits through meditation or nature walks.
  • gabhastyor: This refers to "the light of the heavens." It signifies enlightenment and knowledge. In practical terms, this can relate to the pursuit of education and wisdom, guiding us through life's challenges.
  • vṛṣāyate: This means "to be strong" or "to be powerful." In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who exhibit resilience in the face of adversity, drawing strength from their experiences.
  • nabhasā: This translates to "in the sky" or "in the atmosphere." It can represent the vast possibilities that life offers, encouraging us to reach for our dreams and aspirations.
  • vepate: This means "to tremble" or "to vibrate." In a real-life scenario, this can symbolize the excitement and anticipation we feel when embarking on new ventures or experiences.
  • matī: This translates to "intellect" or "understanding." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in decision-making, akin to how we rely on our knowledge and experiences to navigate life's complexities.
  • sa modate: This means "he rejoices." This can be related to moments of happiness and fulfillment in our lives, such as celebrating achievements or cherishing relationships.
  • nasate: This translates to "he nourishes." In practical terms, this can refer to the nurturing aspects of life, such as caring for family, friends, or even our own well-being.
  • sādhate: This means "to accomplish" or "to achieve." It highlights the importance of setting goals and working diligently towards them, much like how we strive for personal and professional success.
  • girā: This translates to "speech" or "words." It signifies the power of communication, which is essential in building relationships and expressing our thoughts and feelings.
  • nenikte: This means "to flow" or "to move." In real life, this can symbolize the natural flow of life and the importance of adapting to changes and challenges.
  • apsu: This translates to "in the waters." It can represent the source of life and the importance of emotional well-being, akin to how water sustains all living beings.
  • yajate: This means "to worship" or "to offer." In a practical sense, this can relate to the acts of gratitude and reverence we show towards nature, our community, or a higher power.
  • parīmaṇi: This translates to "the measure" or "the limit." It signifies the importance of balance in life, reminding us to find harmony between our desires and responsibilities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of strength, knowledge, joy, and nurturing. It serves as a reminder to seek enlightenment, embrace challenges, and celebrate achievements while maintaining balance. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with resilience and wisdom, fostering a sense of community and gratitude in their daily lives.
