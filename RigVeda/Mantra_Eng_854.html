प्रजावतā वचसā वह्निर आसā च हुवे नि च सत्त्सीह देवैः |वेṣi होत्रम् उता पोत्रं यजत्र बोधि प्रयन्तर जनितर वसूनाम् ||
prajāvatā vacasā vahnir āsā ca huve ni ca satsīha devaiḥ | veṣi hotram uta potraṁ yajatra bodhi prayantar janitar vasūnām ||
  • prajāvatā vacasā: This phrase can be translated as "with the speech of the one who brings forth life." It emphasizes the power of words and communication in creating and nurturing life. In real life, this can be seen in the way educators, parents, and leaders use their words to inspire and motivate others.
  • vahnir āsā ca huve: Here, "vahnir" refers to fire, which symbolizes transformation and energy. "āsā" means "hope" or "aspiration," and "huve" translates to "I invoke." This part of the mantra suggests invoking the transformative power of fire and hope. In practical terms, this can relate to how individuals harness their passions and aspirations to bring about change in their lives or communities.
  • ni ca satsīha devaiḥ: "ni" means "and," "ca" means "also," "satsīha" refers to "truth" or "goodness," and "devaiḥ" means "by the gods." This indicates a connection between truth and divine support. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as the importance of aligning one's actions with truth and goodness to receive support and blessings from higher powers or the universe.
  • veṣi hotram uta potraṁ: "veṣi" means "to protect," "hotram" refers to "the priest" or "the one who offers," and "uta potraṁ" translates to "and the offering." This suggests the role of protection and offerings in spiritual practices. In real life, this can be seen in rituals and ceremonies where individuals seek protection and blessings through offerings, reflecting the importance of gratitude and reverence.
  • yajatra bodhi prayantar: "yajatra" means "sacrifice" or "worship," "bodhi" refers to "wisdom," and "prayantar" means "to reach out." This part emphasizes the connection between sacrifice, wisdom, and outreach. In practical terms, it can be related to how individuals engage in acts of service and sacrifice for the greater good, seeking wisdom in their actions.
  • janitar vasūnām: "janitar" means "the creator" or "the one who gives birth," and "vasūnām" refers to "the wealth" or "prosperity." This final phrase highlights the role of the creator in bestowing wealth and prosperity. In real life, this can be interpreted as recognizing the interconnectedness of creation and abundance, where nurturing relationships and environments lead to prosperity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the significance of communication, transformation, truth, protection, sacrifice, and creation in our lives. It serves as a reminder of the power of our words and actions in shaping our reality and the importance of aligning ourselves with higher principles for personal and communal growth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in meaningful conversations, pursue their aspirations, act with integrity, and contribute positively to their communities, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
