त्वं अग्नि तवष्टा विधते सुवीर्यं तव ज्ञावो मित्रमहः सजात्यम् |त्वं आशुहेमा ररिषे स्वश्व्यं त्वं नरां शर्धोऽसि पुरूवसुः ||
tvam agne tvaṣṭā vidhate suvīryaṁ tava gnāvo mitramahaḥ sajātyam | tvam āśuhemā rariṣe svaśvyaṁ tvaṁ narāṁ śardho asi purūvasuḥ ||
  • tvam agne - "You, O Agni" - This refers to Agni, the fire god, who is a central figure in Vedic rituals and symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification.
  • tvaṣṭā - "the creator" - Tvaṣṭā is the divine craftsman, representing the creative force in the universe, akin to an artist shaping their masterpiece.
  • vidhate - "who establishes" - This word indicates the act of creation and establishment, emphasizing the role of Agni in the cosmic order.
  • suvīryaṁ - "good strength" - This signifies the positive and beneficial aspects of strength, suggesting that true power is constructive and life-giving.
  • tava gnāvo - "your knowledgeable ones" - This refers to those who are wise and enlightened, who understand the deeper truths of existence.
  • mitramahaḥ - "the great friend" - This highlights the importance of friendship and alliances, suggesting that Agni fosters relationships and community.
  • sajātyam - "in harmony" - This term emphasizes unity and the importance of working together for a common purpose.
  • tvam āśuhemā - "you, O swift one" - This refers to the speed and agility of Agni, symbolizing the rapid transformation and movement of energy.
  • rariṣe - "you who are fierce" - This acknowledges the powerful and sometimes destructive nature of fire, which can both create and consume.
  • svaśvyaṁ - "your own" - This indicates possession and the personal connection individuals have with the divine.
  • tvaṁ narāṁ - "you, O man" - This phrase connects the divine with humanity, suggesting that humans are a reflection of divine qualities.
  • śardho - "in the autumn" - This signifies a time of harvest and abundance, representing the fruits of labor and the cyclical nature of life.
  • asi purūvasuḥ - "you are the great wealth" - This concludes the mantra by affirming that Agni embodies prosperity and abundance.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the multifaceted nature of Agni as a divine force that embodies creation, strength, knowledge, and community. In real life, it can be applied to the importance of harnessing our inner strength and creativity to foster relationships and build a harmonious society. Just as fire can purify and transform, individuals can use their talents and wisdom to contribute positively to their communities. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine energy that flows through us, urging us to act with purpose and unity for the greater good.
