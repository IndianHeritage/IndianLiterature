त्वं धुनिर इन्द्र धुनिमतीर ऋणोर अपः सीरा न श्रवन्तीः | प्र यत समुद्रम अति शूर परषि पारयाऽ तुर्वशं यदुं स्वस्ति ||
tvaṁ dhunir indra dhunimatīr ṛṇor apaḥ sīrā na sravantīḥ | pra yat samudram ati śūra parṣi pārayā turvaśaṁ yaduṁ svasti ||
  • tvaṁ dhunir indra dhunimatīr: This phrase can be translated as "You are the mover, Indra, the one who moves with strength." Here, "tvaṁ" means "you," "dhunir" refers to "the mover" or "the one who shakes," and "Indra" is the name of the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power and authority.
  • ṛṇor apaḥ sīrā na sravantīḥ: This part translates to "the waters of the debt do not flow." "ṛṇor" means "of debt," "apaḥ" refers to "waters," "sīrā" means "the flow," and "na sravantīḥ" means "do not flow." This suggests a state where the burdens of debt are not overwhelming or flowing over one’s life.
  • pra yat samudram ati śūra: This translates to "when the ocean is crossed by the brave." "pra" means "when," "yat" means "that," "samudram" refers to "ocean," "ati" means "beyond," and "śūra" means "brave" or "courageous." This indicates the importance of courage in overcoming great challenges.
  • parṣi pārayā turvaśaṁ yaduṁ svasti: This can be interpreted as "may you, O Turvaśa, grant prosperity." "parṣi" means "may you grant," "pārayā" refers to "prosperity," "turvaśaṁ" is a name that can refer to a group or a deity, and "yaduṁ svasti" means "may there be well-being." This emphasizes the desire for blessings and prosperity in life.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates themes of strength, courage, and the quest for prosperity. It highlights the importance of overcoming obstacles and the role of divine support in achieving success.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:
    • In personal challenges, where one must summon courage to face difficulties, akin to crossing an ocean.
    • In financial matters, where the idea of "debt" can be overwhelming, and one seeks to find a way to manage or overcome it.
    • In professional settings, where teamwork and collaboration (symbolized by divine support) are essential for achieving common goals.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the strength within us to overcome challenges and the importance of seeking blessings for prosperity. It encourages individuals to be brave in the face of adversity and to strive for well-being, both personally and collectively.
