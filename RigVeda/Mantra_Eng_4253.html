व्रतेन स्थो ध्रुवक्षेमā धर्मणा यातयजनā |नि बर्हिषि सदा तं सोमपीतये ||
vratena stho dhruvakṣemā dharmaṇā yātayajjanā | ni barhiṣi sadataṁ somapītaye ||
  • Vratena: This word translates to "by vow" or "by commitment." It signifies the importance of dedication and promises made in one's life.
  • Stho: Meaning "to stand firm" or "to be stable," this term emphasizes the need for resilience and steadfastness in one's beliefs and actions.
  • Dhruvakṣemā: This can be interpreted as "permanent well-being" or "lasting prosperity." It suggests a state of enduring peace and security.
  • Dharmaṇā: Referring to "by righteousness" or "by moral duty," this word highlights the significance of living a life aligned with ethical principles.
  • Yātayajjanā: This term means "those who perform sacrifices" or "those who engage in selfless acts." It points to the importance of altruism and community service.
  • Ni barhiṣi: This phrase can be understood as "in the sacred place" or "in the holy context," indicating the significance of performing actions in a spiritually uplifting environment.
  • Sadataṁ: Meaning "always" or "continuously," this word stresses the need for consistency in one's actions and commitments.
  • Somapītaye: This translates to "for the enjoyment of the soma," which symbolizes the pursuit of spiritual and material nourishment, often associated with divine blessings.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of living a life grounded in commitment, stability, righteousness, and selfless service. It teaches us that by adhering to our vows and ethical principles, we can achieve lasting well-being and prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal relationships, professional commitments, and community involvement. For instance, a person who remains dedicated to their family values (vratena) and stands firm in their beliefs (stho) can create a nurturing environment that fosters lasting happiness (dhruvakṣemā). Similarly, engaging in community service (yātayajjanā) while maintaining moral integrity (dharmaṇā) can lead to a more harmonious society. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for individuals seeking to lead a fulfilling and meaningful life.
