यानि स्थानानि अश्विनाः दधाथे दिवो यह्वीष्वोषधिषु विक्षु |नि पर्वतस्य मूर्धनि सदन्तेषं जनाय दाशुषे वहन्ता ||
yāni sthānāny aśvinā dadhāthe divo yahvīṣv oṣadhīṣu vikṣu | ni parvatasya mūrdhani sadanteṣaṁ janāya dāśuṣe vahantā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yāni: those which
    • sthānāny: places or positions
    • aśvinā: by the Ashvins (the twin deities)
    • dadhāthe: you establish or place
    • divo: of the sky
    • yahvīṣv: in the herbs or plants
    • oṣadhīṣu: among the medicinal plants
    • vikṣu: in the waters
    • ni: in
    • parvatasya: of the mountain
    • mūrdhani: on the summit
    • sadanteṣaṁ: in the depths
    • janāya: for the people
    • dāśuṣe: for the one who serves
    • vahantā: carrying or bringing
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the Ashvins, who are revered as divine physicians and bringers of health and well-being.
    • It highlights their role in establishing beneficial places in the sky, in plants, and in waters, which can be interpreted as the natural elements that sustain life.
    • The mention of mountains and depths suggests a connection to the earth and its resources, emphasizing the importance of nature in providing for humanity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The Ashvins can be likened to modern-day doctors and healers who utilize natural resources, such as herbs and plants, to promote health.
    • In agriculture, the mantra can be applied to sustainable farming practices that respect the earth and utilize its resources wisely.
    • Environmental conservation efforts reflect the essence of this mantra, as they aim to protect the natural elements that provide for human life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of nature and humanity, emphasizing the importance of respecting and utilizing natural resources for the well-being of all.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek harmony with the environment, recognizing that our health and survival depend on the balance of natural elements.
    • In contemporary life, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to engage in practices that promote health, sustainability, and respect for the earth.
