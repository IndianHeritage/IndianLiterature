एव वसिष्ठ इन्द्रं ऊतये नृण कृष्टीनां वृषभं सुतं गृणाति |सहस्रिण उप नो माहि वाजान यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
evā vasiṣṭha indram ūtaye nṝn kṛṣṭīnāṁ vṛṣabhaṁ sute gṛṇāti | sahasriṇa upa no māhi vājān yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • evā vasiṣṭha indram: This phrase can be translated as "Thus, we invoke Indra, the most excellent." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. In real life, this can be seen as a call for strength and support in challenging situations, much like how people seek guidance or help from mentors or leaders in their lives.
  • ūtaye nṝn kṛṣṭīnāṁ: This translates to "for the benefit of mankind." It emphasizes the importance of actions and prayers that are aimed at the welfare of humanity. In modern contexts, this can relate to community service or humanitarian efforts where individuals work for the greater good, such as volunteering at shelters or participating in environmental conservation.
  • vṛṣabhaṁ sute gṛṇāti: This means "the bull, who is the giver of strength." The bull symbolizes power and fertility, representing the strength that is necessary for growth and sustenance. In everyday life, this can be likened to the hard work and dedication of farmers who cultivate the land, providing food and resources for society.
  • sahasriṇa upa no māhi: This translates to "with a thousand (strengths), may we be protected." It signifies the desire for abundant protection and support. In real life, this can be seen in the context of teamwork and collaboration, where individuals come together to support one another, much like a sports team working together to achieve victory.
  • vājān yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ: This means "may you protect us with auspiciousness." It reflects a plea for safety and well-being. In contemporary life, this can be related to the importance of mental health and well-being, where individuals seek positive environments and relationships that foster growth and happiness.
  • sadā naḥ: This translates to "always for us." It emphasizes the continuous nature of the blessings and support sought. In real life, this can be interpreted as the ongoing need for guidance and support throughout life’s journey, akin to the lifelong relationships we build with family, friends, and mentors.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for strength, protection, and the well-being of humanity. Its significance lies in the universal themes of seeking support, working for the greater good, and fostering community. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to engage in acts of kindness, support one another, and strive for collective growth and prosperity. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of working together for a harmonious existence.
