तवाहं शूर रातिभिः प्रत्यायं सिन्धुम आवदन |उपातिष्ठन्त गिरवणो विदुष्टे तस्य कारवः ||
tavāhaṁ śūra rātibhiḥ praty āyaṁ sindhum āvadan | upātiṣṭhanta girvaṇo viduṣ ṭe tasya kāravaḥ ||
  • tavāhaṁ: This word translates to "I am yours" or "I belong to you." It signifies a sense of devotion and surrender to a higher power or divine entity.
  • śūra: This means "brave" or "valiant." It refers to the qualities of courage and strength that one should embody.
  • rātibhiḥ: This term can be understood as "with the warriors" or "in the company of the brave." It emphasizes the importance of community and support from others who share similar values.
  • praty: This means "towards" or "in the direction of." It indicates a movement or intention towards a goal or purpose.
  • āyaṁ: This word translates to "the ocean" or "the vastness." It symbolizes the challenges and vast experiences one encounters in life.
  • sindhum: This refers specifically to "the sea" or "the river," often representing the flow of life and the journey one undertakes.
  • āvadan: This means "to speak" or "to declare." It signifies the act of expressing one's intentions or desires.
  • upātiṣṭhanta: This translates to "they rise up" or "they stand up." It indicates action and the readiness to confront challenges.
  • girvaṇo: This term refers to "the wise" or "the knowledgeable." It highlights the importance of wisdom in navigating life's journey.
  • viduṣ: This means "of the learned" or "of the wise." It emphasizes the value of knowledge and understanding.
  • ṭe: This is a possessive form, meaning "of you" or "your." It indicates a connection to the divine or the higher power being addressed.
  • tasya: This means "of that" or "of him." It refers to the divine or the higher power that is being invoked.
  • kāravaḥ: This translates to "the doers" or "the creators." It signifies action and the importance of taking initiative in life.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of courage, community, and wisdom in facing life's challenges. It encourages individuals to surrender to a higher power while actively participating in their journey. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in professional settings, where collaboration and support from colleagues lead to success. It also resonates in personal growth, where one must confront fears and challenges with bravery and the guidance of wise mentors. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to embrace both the journey and the support of others while remaining connected to a greater purpose.
