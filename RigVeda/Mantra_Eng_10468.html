अन्तरिक्षे पथिभिर ईयमानो न निःविशते कतमच् चनाहः |अपां सखा प्रथमजा ऋतावा क्व स्विज जातः कुत आ बभूव ||
antarikṣe pathibhir īyamāno na ni viśate katamac canāhaḥ | apāṁ sakhā prathamajā ṛtāvā kva svij jātaḥ kuta ā babhūva ||
  • antarikṣe: This word refers to the "interspace" or "sky," symbolizing the vastness of the universe.
  • pathibhir: This means "by the paths" or "by the ways," indicating the various routes or methods through which one can navigate life.
  • īyamāno: This translates to "moving" or "progressing," suggesting a journey or evolution.
  • na ni viśate: This phrase means "does not enter" or "does not penetrate," implying barriers or limitations that exist.
  • katamac: This means "which" or "what," posing a question about the nature of existence or reality.
  • canāhaḥ: This translates to "is it" or "is there," further emphasizing the inquiry into existence.
  • apāṁ: This word means "of the waters," symbolizing life, nourishment, and the essential elements of existence.
  • sakhā: This translates to "friend" or "ally," indicating companionship and support in life's journey.
  • prathamajā: This means "firstborn" or "origin," referring to the fundamental aspects of creation.
  • ṛtāvā: This translates to "truth" or "order," signifying the underlying principles that govern the universe.
  • kva: This means "where," questioning the location or origin of something.
  • svij: This translates to "born" or "created," indicating the emergence of existence.
  • jātaḥ: This means "born" or "produced," reinforcing the idea of creation.
  • kuta: This translates to "from where," further probing the origins of existence.
  • ā babhūva: This means "has come into being" or "has arisen," indicating the manifestation of reality.

The mantra poses profound questions about existence, creation, and the nature of reality. It reflects on the journey of life, the paths we take, and the barriers we encounter. The reference to "waters" symbolizes the essential elements that sustain life, while the notion of "truth" suggests that there is an order to the universe that we must seek to understand.


In real life, this mantra can be applied to various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to reflect on their life paths and the choices they make, understanding that obstacles may arise but can be navigated with wisdom.
  • In relationships, the concept of "sakhā" emphasizes the importance of companionship and support, reminding us that we are not alone in our journeys.
  • In environmental discussions, the reference to "waters" can be a reminder of the importance of preserving our natural resources, as they are vital for life.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek understanding in our existence, to navigate our paths with awareness, and to appreciate the interconnectedness of life. It encourages us to question our origins and the nature of reality, fostering a deeper connection with ourselves and the universe.
