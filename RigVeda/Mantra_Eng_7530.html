आदु मे निवरो भुवद वृत्रहादिष्ट पौंस्यम् |अजातशत्रुर अस्तृतः ||
ād u me nivaro bhuvad vṛtrahādiṣṭa pauṁsyam | ajātaśatrur astṛtaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ād: "from the beginning" or "first"
    • u: "to" or "towards"
    • me: "my" or "of me"
    • nivaro: "to be freed" or "to be liberated"
    • bhuvad: "becoming" or "existing"
    • vṛtrahādiṣṭa: "the one who has conquered Vritra" (Vritra being a dragon or serpent representing obstacles)
    • pauṁsyam: "of manhood" or "masculine strength"
    • ajātaśatrur: "one who has no enemies" or "unborn enemy"
    • astṛtaḥ: "is established" or "is firm"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of liberation and strength, emphasizing the importance of overcoming obstacles (symbolized by Vritra).
    • The phrase "ajātaśatrur" suggests a state of peace and security, where one is free from conflict and strife.
    • The mantra can be seen as a call for inner strength and resilience, encouraging individuals to rise above challenges and adversities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual facing challenges at work can embody the spirit of this mantra by seeking solutions and overcoming obstacles, much like a leader who navigates through crises.
    • In personal life, someone dealing with emotional turmoil can find solace in the idea of being "ajātaśatrur," focusing on self-empowerment and inner peace.
    • In community settings, this mantra can inspire collective action against social injustices, promoting unity and strength among individuals to overcome societal challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for resilience and the pursuit of inner strength.
    • It serves as a reminder that, despite the obstacles we face, we have the power to liberate ourselves and establish a state of peace and security.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, from personal struggles to collective efforts in overcoming societal issues, encouraging individuals to embody strength and unity.
