त्रिर अस्मै सप्त धेनवो दुदुहरे सत्याम आशिरम् पूर्व्ये व्योमनि |चत्वार्य अन्याः bhuvanानि निर्णिजे चारुणि चक्रे यद ऋतैर अवर्धत ||
trir asmai sapta dhenavo duduhre satyām āśiram pūrvye vyomani | catvāry anyā bhuvanāni nirṇije cārūṇi cakre yad ṛtair avardhata ||
  • Trir asmai: This phrase can be interpreted as "three times to him." It signifies the importance of repetition and dedication in spiritual practices. In real life, this can be related to the idea of persistence in achieving goals, such as studying for an exam or training for a marathon.
  • Sapta dhenavo: "Seven cows" symbolizes abundance and prosperity. Cows are often seen as sacred in many cultures, representing nourishment and wealth. In a modern context, this could relate to the idea of having multiple sources of income or resources that provide sustenance, such as investments or a diverse skill set.
  • Duduhre: This term means "to give milk" or "to nourish." It emphasizes the act of providing sustenance and support. In everyday life, this can be seen in the role of caregivers, teachers, or mentors who nurture and guide others towards growth and success.
  • Satyām āśiram: "The abode of truth" suggests a place or state where truth prevails. This can be related to the importance of honesty and integrity in personal and professional relationships. For instance, a workplace that values transparency fosters trust among employees.
  • Pūrvye vyomani: "In the eastern sky" can symbolize new beginnings or the dawn of a new day. This reflects the idea of hope and renewal. In real life, this can be likened to starting a new project or embarking on a new journey, such as moving to a new city or beginning a new career.
  • Catvāry anyā bhuvanāni: "Four other worlds" indicates the existence of multiple realms or dimensions. This can be interpreted as the various aspects of life we navigate, such as personal, professional, social, and spiritual. Each realm requires attention and balance, much like maintaining a work-life balance.
  • Nirṇije: This term means "to be free" or "to be liberated." It signifies the importance of freedom in thought and action. In a contemporary context, this can relate to the pursuit of personal freedom, such as advocating for human rights or striving for self-expression in art and culture.
  • Cārūṇi cakre: "Beautiful circles" can symbolize harmony and interconnectedness. This reflects the idea that all aspects of life are interrelated, much like the cycles of nature. In real life, this can be seen in community building, where individuals come together to support one another, creating a harmonious environment.
  • Yad ṛtair avardhata: "That which is upheld by truth" emphasizes the foundation of truth in all endeavors. This highlights the significance of ethical practices in achieving success. In real life, businesses that prioritize ethical standards often build a loyal customer base and a positive reputation.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of persistence, abundance, nourishment, truth, new beginnings, freedom, harmony, and ethical practices. Its significance lies in the reminder to uphold these values in our daily lives. By embodying these principles, individuals can create a balanced and fulfilling existence, contributing positively to their communities and the world at large. Whether in personal relationships, professional endeavors, or spiritual journeys, the teachings of this mantra encourage a holistic approach to life, fostering growth and interconnectedness.
