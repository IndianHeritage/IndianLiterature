त्वां अग्न ऋतायवः सम ईधिरे प्रत्नम् प्रत्नास ūtaye सहस्कृत |पुरुश्चन्द्रं यजतं विश्वधायसं दमूनसं गृहपतिं वरेण्यम् ||
tvām agna ṛtāyavaḥ sam īdhire pratnam pratnāsa ūtaye sahaskṛta | puruścandraṁ yajataṁ viśvadhāyasaṁ damūnasaṁ gṛhapatiṁ vareṇyam ||
  • tvām - This word translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity, often interpreted as Agni, the fire god.
  • agna - Refers to Agni, symbolizing fire, energy, and transformation. In real life, fire is essential for cooking, warmth, and rituals.
  • ṛtāyavaḥ - This term means "those who uphold truth and order." It signifies the importance of righteousness and moral integrity in life.
  • sam īdhire - Translates to "they come together" or "they unite." This emphasizes the power of unity and collaboration in achieving goals.
  • pratnam - Means "offering" or "sacrifice." In a practical sense, this can relate to the sacrifices we make for our families or communities.
  • pratnāsa - This can be interpreted as "the one who is invoked." It highlights the importance of calling upon divine forces in times of need.
  • ūtaye - Means "for prosperity" or "for well-being." This reflects the human desire for growth and success in various aspects of life.
  • sahaskṛta - Translates to "with strength" or "with effort." It underscores the necessity of hard work and determination in achieving one's aspirations.
  • puruścandraṁ - Refers to "the moon among men," symbolizing a person of great wisdom and virtue. This can be likened to a leader or mentor in society.
  • yajataṁ - Means "those who perform sacrifices." This can relate to the various forms of dedication and commitment we show in our personal and professional lives.
  • viśvadhāyasaṁ - Translates to "the one who supports all." This signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of community support.
  • damūnasaṁ - Refers to "the master of the house." This can symbolize the role of a caretaker or provider in a family or community setting.
  • gṛhapatiṁ - Means "the lord of the home," emphasizing the significance of family and domestic harmony.
  • vareṇyam - Translates to "worthy of worship." This highlights the reverence we should have for those who embody virtues and contribute positively to society.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of unity, righteousness, and the divine presence in our lives. It calls upon Agni, the fire god, as a symbol of transformation and energy, urging individuals to come together in truth and sacrifice for the greater good. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to community efforts, family values, and personal growth, reminding us that through collaboration, hard work, and reverence for the divine, we can achieve prosperity and harmony in our lives. The mantra serves as a guiding principle for ethical living and the importance of supporting one another in our journeys.
