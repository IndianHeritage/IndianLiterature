देवं नरः सवितारं विप्राः यज्ञैः सुवृक्तिभिः |नमस्यन्ति धियेषिताः ||
devaṁ naraḥ savitāraṁ viprā yajñaiḥ suvṛktibhiḥ | namasyanti dhiyeṣitāḥ ||
  • devaṁ: This word translates to "the divine" or "the god." In the context of the mantra, it refers to the supreme being or the divine essence that governs the universe.
  • naraḥ: This means "humans" or "men." It signifies the human aspect of existence, highlighting the relationship between the divine and humanity.
  • savitāraṁ: This term refers to "the sun" or "the solar deity." In Vedic literature, Savitar is often associated with the life-giving energy of the sun, symbolizing illumination and enlightenment.
  • viprā: This word means "the wise" or "the learned." It denotes those who possess knowledge and understanding, often referring to sages or scholars who seek truth and wisdom.
  • yajñaiḥ: This translates to "through sacrifices" or "by offerings." In Vedic tradition, yajna (sacrifice) is a ritual performed to honor the gods and seek their blessings.
  • suvṛktibhiḥ: This means "with well-spoken words" or "with good speech." It emphasizes the importance of communication and the power of words in rituals and prayers.
  • namasyanti: This translates to "they bow down" or "they offer respect." It signifies the act of reverence and humility towards the divine.
  • dhiyeṣitāḥ: This term means "those who are wise" or "the enlightened ones." It refers to individuals who possess deep understanding and insight, often guiding others in their spiritual journey.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for humans to recognize and honor the divine essence represented by the sun, Savitar, through wise actions and well-spoken words. It emphasizes the importance of knowledge, respect, and the practice of rituals in connecting with the divine.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various ways:

  • Spiritual Practice: Individuals can incorporate this mantra into their daily prayers or meditations, fostering a sense of connection with the divine and seeking guidance in their lives.
  • Community Rituals: In communal settings, such as festivals or religious gatherings, this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings and unity among participants, emphasizing the importance of collective worship.
  • Personal Development: The emphasis on wisdom and well-spoken words encourages individuals to pursue knowledge and communicate effectively, fostering personal growth and better relationships.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness between the divine and humanity. It highlights the significance of reverence, knowledge, and the power of words in our spiritual and everyday lives. By embodying these principles, individuals can cultivate a deeper understanding of themselves and their place in the universe.
