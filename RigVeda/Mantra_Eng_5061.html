विष्वेषां वः सतां ज्येष्ठतमā गीरभिर मित्रावरुणा वावृद्धध्यै |सं यā रश्मेवा यमतुर यमिष्टा द्वा जनाम् असमा बाहुभिः स्वैः ||
viśveṣāṁ vaḥ satāṁ jyeṣṭhatamā gīrbhir mitrāvaruṇā vāvṛdhadhyai | saṁ yā raśmeva yamatur yamiṣṭhā dvā janām̐ asamā bāhubhiḥ svaiḥ ||
  • viśveṣāṁ: This word refers to "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity and the collective nature of existence.
  • vaḥ: This translates to "of you," suggesting a personal connection or address to the audience or the divine.
  • satāṁ: Meaning "of the good" or "of the virtuous," it emphasizes the qualities of righteousness and morality.
  • jyeṣṭhatamā: This means "the greatest" or "the eldest," indicating a position of respect and authority.
  • gīrbhir: Translated as "by hymns" or "by words," it highlights the power of speech and expression in invoking the divine.
  • mitrāvaruṇā: Referring to the deities Mitra and Varuna, who symbolize friendship and cosmic order, respectively. They represent the balance of nature and social harmony.
  • vāvṛdhadhyai: This means "may they increase" or "may they grow," indicating a desire for prosperity and abundance.
  • saṁ: This word means "together" or "with," suggesting unity and collaboration.
  • yā: Meaning "who" or "which," it introduces a relative clause that describes the subject.
  • raśmeva: This translates to "like a ray," symbolizing light, knowledge, and enlightenment.
  • yamatur: Meaning "the one who is wise," it refers to the pursuit of wisdom and understanding.
  • yamiṣṭhā: This means "the one who is desired" or "the one who is sought after," indicating the importance of aspiration and goals.
  • dvā: Translated as "two," it signifies duality, which can represent various pairs in life, such as body and mind, or individual and community.
  • janām̐: Meaning "people" or "beings," it emphasizes the social aspect of existence.
  • asamā: This means "unequal" or "different," highlighting diversity in society.
  • bāhubhiḥ: Translated as "by arms" or "by strength," it refers to the power and capability of individuals.
  • svaiḥ: Meaning "their own," it emphasizes personal responsibility and agency.

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of unity, virtue, and the pursuit of wisdom in society. It calls upon the deities Mitra and Varuna to bless the collective with prosperity and growth, highlighting the significance of collaboration and mutual support among individuals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Community Building: Encouraging collaboration among community members to achieve common goals, fostering a sense of belonging and support.
  • Leadership: Leaders can embody the qualities of righteousness and wisdom, guiding their teams towards success while respecting diversity.
  • Conflict Resolution: The principles of friendship and cosmic order can be applied to resolve disputes, promoting harmony and understanding.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for collective growth, virtue, and wisdom. It serves as a reminder that by working together and respecting each other's strengths, we can create a harmonious and prosperous society. The teachings of this mantra are applicable in everyday life, encouraging individuals to strive for personal and communal betterment.
