इन्द्राय नूनमर्चतोक्तानि च ब्रवीतानि |सुताऽमात्सुरिन्दवो ज्येष्ठं नमस्यतां सहः ||
indrāya nūnam arcatokthāni ca bravītana | sutā amatsur indavo jyeṣṭhaṁ namasyatā sahaḥ ||
  • Indrāya: This word refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunder, and war. Indra symbolizes strength and power.
  • Nūnam: This term can be translated as "indeed" or "certainly," indicating a strong affirmation of the statement that follows.
  • Arcatokthāni: This word combines "arcati," meaning to worship or praise, and "ukthāni," which refers to hymns or mantras. Together, it suggests the act of offering praises or hymns to Indra.
  • Bravītana: This term means "speaking" or "declaring," indicating the act of vocalizing or expressing something significant.
  • Sutā: This word means "the one who is poured" or "libation," often referring to offerings made to the gods, particularly in the context of rituals.
  • Amatsur: This term can be interpreted as "immortal" or "eternal," suggesting the divine nature of the offerings or the gods themselves.
  • Indavo: This word refers to "the Indras" or "the divine beings," indicating a collective reference to the deities associated with Indra.
  • Jyeṣṭhaṁ: This term means "the eldest" or "the most revered," highlighting the importance of the subject being addressed, in this case, Indra.
  • Namasyatā: This word means "with reverence" or "with respect," indicating the attitude of devotion and honor towards the divine.
  • Sahaḥ: This term means "together" or "along with," suggesting a sense of unity or collective action in the worship or offering.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of worship and reverence towards Indra, the king of the gods, through the offering of hymns and libations. It signifies the acknowledgment of divine power and the need for unity in worship. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, ceremonies, or personal prayers where individuals seek strength, guidance, and blessings from higher powers. It serves as a reminder of the significance of collective devotion and the importance of honoring the divine in our lives.
