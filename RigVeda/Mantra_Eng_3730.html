मन्त्र: उता स्म दुर्गृभीयसे पुत्रो न ह्वार्याणाम् |पुरू यो दग्धासि वनाग्ने पशुर न यवसे ||
uta sma durgṛbhīyase putro na hvāryāṇām | purū yo dagdhāsi vanāgne paśur na yavase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and
    • sma: indeed
    • durgṛbhīyase: to the one who is difficult to grasp
    • putro: son
    • na: not
    • hvāryāṇām: of those who are worthy
    • purū: many
    • yo: who
    • dagdhāsi: you have burned
    • vanāgne: O fire of the forest
    • paśur: the animal
    • na: not
    • yavase: to be sacrificed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine fire, often associated with Agni, the Vedic god of fire, who is both a physical and spiritual presence.
    • The phrase "durgṛbhīyase" suggests that the divine or spiritual essence is often elusive and difficult to comprehend.
    • The mention of "putro" indicates a connection to lineage and the importance of the next generation, emphasizing the role of the son in carrying forward traditions.
    • The line "purū yo dagdhāsi vanāgne" acknowledges the transformative power of fire, which can consume and purify, symbolizing both destruction and renewal.
    • Finally, the phrase "paśur na yavase" implies that not all beings are meant for sacrifice, hinting at the sanctity of life and the need for discernment in our actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern contexts, the mantra can be related to the challenges of understanding complex issues in life, such as mental health or societal problems, which can be "difficult to grasp."
    • The importance of nurturing the next generation can be seen in educational initiatives where parents and communities invest in the future of children, ensuring they are equipped to face challenges.
    • The transformative power of fire can be likened to personal growth experiences, where individuals face hardships that ultimately lead to renewal and strength.
    • The idea of discernment in actions can be applied in ethical decision-making, where individuals must consider the implications of their choices on others and the environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of understanding the complexities of life, the importance of legacy, and the transformative power of experiences.
    • Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to seek deeper understanding, to value the next generation, and to approach life with discernment and respect for all beings.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to embrace challenges, invest in future generations, and make ethical choices that honor the sanctity of life.
