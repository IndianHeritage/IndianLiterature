द्यौर्मे पिता जनिता नाभिरात्र बन्धुरमे माता पृथिवी महीयम् |उत्तानयोश्चम्वोऽर्योनिरन्तरात्रा पिता दुधितुरगर्भमाधात ||
dyaur me pitā janitā nābhir atra bandhur me mātā pṛthivī mahīyam | uttānayoś camvo3r yonir antar atrā pitā duhitur garbham ādhāt ||
  • Translation: "Heaven is my father, the Earth is my mother; from the union of these two, I have been born. The two worlds, the sky and the earth, are my parents, and from their union, I have come into existence."
  • Meaning of Words:
    • Dyaur: Heaven or sky, representing the divine or spiritual aspect of existence.
    • Pitā: Father, symbolizing authority, protection, and guidance.
    • Janitā: Born or created, indicating the act of coming into existence.
    • Nābhir: Navel or center, often symbolizing the source of life.
    • Bandhur: Relative or connection, emphasizing the bond between beings.
    • Mātā: Mother, representing nurturing, care, and the physical world.
    • Pṛthivī: Earth, symbolizing the material aspect of existence.
    • Mahīyam: Greatness or vastness, indicating the expansive nature of the Earth.
    • Uttānayoś: Of the two worlds, referring to the duality of existence.
    • Camvo3r: Union or connection, highlighting the interdependence of elements.
    • Yonir: Womb or source, indicating the origin of life.
    • Antar: Within, suggesting the inner connection between beings.
    • Duhitur: Daughter, representing the outcome of the union.
    • Garbham: Embryo or fetus, symbolizing potential and growth.
    • Ādhāt: Established or placed, indicating the grounding of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The relationship between nature and humanity can be seen in environmental conservation efforts. Just as the mantra emphasizes the connection between heaven and earth, individuals and communities work to protect the environment, recognizing it as a nurturing mother.
    • In family structures, the roles of parents can be reflected in this mantra. A father provides guidance and authority, while a mother offers care and nurturing, illustrating the balance of roles in raising children.
    • In spiritual practices, many cultures emphasize the connection between the divine (heaven) and the earthly (earth), encouraging individuals to seek harmony between their spiritual beliefs and their physical existence.
  • Conclusion: The mantra highlights the profound connection between the cosmos and human existence. It signifies the importance of recognizing our origins and the interdependence of all life. In real-life scenarios, this understanding can lead to greater respect for nature, stronger family bonds, and a holistic approach to spirituality. By acknowledging that we are born from the union of heaven and earth, we can cultivate a sense of responsibility towards our environment and relationships, fostering a more harmonious existence.
