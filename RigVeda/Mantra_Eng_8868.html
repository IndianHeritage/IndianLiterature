उशान्ति घा ते अमृतासा एतद एकस्य चित त्यजसम मार्त्यस्य |नि ते मनो मनसि धाय्य अस्मे जन्युः पतिस तन्बम् आविविश्यः ||
uśanti ghā te amṛtāsa etad ekasya cit tyajasam martyasya | ni te mano manasi dhāyy asme janyuḥ patis tanva1m ā viviśyāḥ ||
  • uśanti: This word can be interpreted as "they are peaceful" or "they are calm." It signifies a state of tranquility and harmony.
  • ghā: This term means "to shine" or "to illuminate." It represents the idea of enlightenment or clarity.
  • te: This is a pronoun meaning "you" or "those." It refers to the divine or higher powers being addressed in the mantra.
  • amṛtāsa: This word translates to "immortal" or "eternal." It signifies the essence of life that transcends physical existence.
  • etad: Meaning "this," it points to the subject or the essence being discussed in the mantra.
  • ekasya: This means "of one" or "of the singular." It emphasizes unity and oneness in existence.
  • cit: This term refers to "consciousness" or "awareness." It highlights the importance of being aware and present.
  • tyajasam: This word means "of the mortal" or "of the transient." It refers to the physical body or the temporary nature of life.
  • ni te: This phrase translates to "not you," indicating a separation or distinction from the divine or eternal.
  • mano: Meaning "mind," it refers to the mental faculties and thoughts.
  • manasi: This is the locative form of "mind," indicating a state of being within the mind.
  • dhāyy: This means "to meditate" or "to contemplate." It emphasizes the practice of reflection and deep thought.
  • asme: This translates to "to us," indicating a collective or communal aspect.
  • janyuḥ: This word means "born" or "origin." It signifies the source of life or creation.
  • patis: This term means "lord" or "master," referring to a higher power or divine entity.
  • tanva1m: This translates to "body" or "form," emphasizing the physical aspect of existence.
  • ā viviśyāḥ: This phrase means "having entered" or "having pervaded," indicating a sense of immersion or involvement.

In summary, this mantra speaks to the relationship between the eternal and the transient, the divine and the mortal. It emphasizes the importance of meditation and awareness in understanding our existence. The mantra encourages individuals to seek peace and enlightenment while recognizing the temporary nature of life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in practices such as mindfulness meditation, where individuals strive to achieve a state of calm and clarity, allowing them to navigate the complexities of life with a deeper understanding of their purpose and existence.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for introspection and the pursuit of eternal truths amidst the transient nature of life. It serves as a reminder to cultivate peace and awareness, which can lead to a more fulfilling and meaningful existence.
