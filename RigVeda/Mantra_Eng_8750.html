मन्तर: नृभिर येमानो जजñानः पूतः क्षरद विश्वानि मन्द्रः स्वरवित ||
nṛbhir yemāno jajñānaḥ pūtaḥ kṣarad viśvāni mandraḥ svarvit ||
  • nṛbhir: This word refers to "men" or "humans." It signifies the collective human experience and the importance of community.
  • yemāno: This term can be translated as "those who are engaged" or "those who are involved." It emphasizes active participation in life and the world around us.
  • jajñānaḥ: This word means "born" or "arising." It indicates the emergence of knowledge, wisdom, or consciousness.
  • pūtaḥ: Translated as "pure" or "sacred," this term highlights the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions.
  • kṣarad: This word means "falling" or "perishing." It suggests the transient nature of life and the inevitability of change.
  • viśvāni: This term translates to "all" or "universal." It signifies inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings and elements in the universe.
  • mandraḥ: This word can mean "sweet" or "pleasant." It refers to the harmonious and joyful aspects of life.
  • svarvit: This term means "to be invoked" or "to be called upon." It indicates the act of seeking divine guidance or inspiration.

The mantra "nṛbhir yemāno jajñānaḥ pūtaḥ kṣarad viśvāni mandraḥ svarvit" encapsulates profound themes of human existence, knowledge, purity, and the transient nature of life. It emphasizes the importance of being actively engaged in the world while maintaining purity of thought and action. The mantra suggests that through this engagement, individuals can attain wisdom and a deeper understanding of the universe.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Community Engagement: Just as the mantra speaks of humans being involved, it encourages individuals to participate in community service, fostering a sense of belonging and collective growth.
  • Personal Development: The emphasis on purity and knowledge can inspire individuals to pursue education and self-improvement, leading to a more enlightened and fulfilling life.
  • Mindfulness: Recognizing the transient nature of life (kṣarad) encourages mindfulness and appreciation of the present moment, helping individuals to live more fully and authentically.
  • Spiritual Practices: The invocation of divine guidance (svarvit) can be seen in various spiritual practices, where individuals seek connection with a higher power for wisdom and support.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of living a life of purity, engagement, and wisdom. It encourages individuals to embrace their roles in the community, seek knowledge, and remain mindful of the transient nature of existence, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
