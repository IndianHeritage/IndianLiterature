प्र ये दिवः पृथिव्या न बर्हणात्तमना रिरिच्रे अभ्रान्न सूर्यः | पाजस्वन्तो न वीराः पनस्यवो रिषादसो न मर्याः अभिद्यवः ||
pra ye divaḥ pṛthivyā na barhaṇā tmanā riricre abhrān na sūryaḥ | pājasvanto na vīrāḥ panasyavo riśādaso na maryā abhidyavaḥ ||
  • pra ye divaḥ pṛthivyā: This phrase translates to "those who are in the heavens and on the earth." It signifies the connection between the celestial and terrestrial realms, emphasizing the unity of the universe.
  • na barhaṇā tmanā: This means "not by the strength of the mind." It suggests that true power and influence do not come merely from intellectual prowess or mental strength.
  • riricre abhrān na sūryaḥ: This can be interpreted as "they who are not obstructed by the clouds, nor by the sun." It indicates a state of clarity and unobstructed vision, where one can see beyond superficial barriers.
  • pājasvanto na vīrāḥ: This translates to "the powerful ones are not the heroes." It implies that true strength lies not in physical might or heroism but in wisdom and understanding.
  • panasyavo riśādaso: This phrase means "those who are not defeated by obstacles." It highlights resilience and the ability to overcome challenges in life.
  • na maryā abhidyavaḥ: This translates to "they who do not desire limitations." It speaks to the idea of transcending boundaries and striving for greater achievements without being confined by societal norms or expectations.

  • Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of unity between the celestial and terrestrial, the value of wisdom over mere strength, and the significance of resilience in the face of challenges. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as leadership, where a leader must look beyond immediate challenges and inspire others through wisdom rather than force. It also encourages individuals to pursue their goals without being limited by societal expectations, promoting a mindset of growth and exploration.
