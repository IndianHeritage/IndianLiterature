इन्द्र आशाभ्यस् परि सर्वाभ्यो अभयं करत् |जेताः शत्रून् विचर्षणिः ||
indra āśābhyas pari sarvābhyo abhayaṁ karat | jetā śatrūn vicarṣaṇiḥ ||
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with thunder, rain, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • Āśābhyas: This term can be interpreted as "from hopes" or "from aspirations." It signifies the desires and expectations that individuals hold.
  • Pari: Meaning "around" or "in all directions," this word indicates a comprehensive or all-encompassing approach.
  • Sarvābhyo: This translates to "from all" or "to all," suggesting inclusivity and universality.
  • Abhayaṁ: This means "fearlessness" or "safety." It conveys a sense of security and protection.
  • Karat: This word means "to do" or "to make." It implies action and the manifestation of intentions.
  • Jetā: This term means "victor" or "conqueror," indicating success and triumph over challenges.
  • Śatrūn: This translates to "enemies" or "opponents," representing obstacles or adversities one may face.
  • Vicarṣaṇiḥ: This word can be interpreted as "one who moves" or "one who travels," suggesting a dynamic and proactive approach to overcoming challenges.

The mantra "indra āśābhyas pari sarvābhyo abhayaṁ karat | jetā śatrūn vicarṣaṇiḥ ||" can be understood as a prayer for strength and fearlessness in the face of challenges. It invokes Indra to grant protection and courage, enabling one to overcome adversities and emerge victorious.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Facing Challenges: When individuals encounter difficulties in their personal or professional lives, invoking the essence of this mantra can inspire them to act fearlessly and confidently.
  • Leadership: Leaders can embody the qualities of Indra by fostering an environment of safety and support, encouraging their teams to pursue their aspirations without fear of failure.
  • Conflict Resolution: In situations of conflict, this mantra serves as a reminder to approach adversaries with courage and a desire for resolution, rather than fear.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the importance of courage, protection, and the pursuit of aspirations. It serves as a powerful reminder that with the right mindset and divine support, individuals can conquer their fears and challenges, leading to personal growth and success.
