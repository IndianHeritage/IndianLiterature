मध्वो वो नाम मारुतं यजत्राः प्र यज्ञेषु शवसां मदन्ति |ये रेजयन्ति रोडसी चिद उर्वी पिन्वन्त्य उत्सं यद आयासुर उग्राः ||
madhvo vo nāma mārutaṁ yajatrāḥ pra yajñeṣu śavasā madanti | ye rejayanti rodasī cid urvī pinvanty utsaṁ yad ayāsur ugrāḥ ||
  • madhvo vo nāma mārutaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "O Maruts, we invoke your sweet name." The Maruts are a group of storm deities in the Rigveda, often associated with the power of wind and storms. The term "madhvo" suggests sweetness or pleasantness, indicating a reverence for their benevolent aspects.
  • yajatrāḥ pra yajñeṣu: This translates to "those who perform sacrifices." In Vedic culture, yajnas (sacrifices) were essential rituals performed to appease the gods and ensure prosperity. This line emphasizes the importance of rituals in connecting with divine forces.
  • śavasā madanti: This means "they who are filled with strength." It refers to the Maruts as powerful beings who embody vigor and vitality. In real life, this can be likened to individuals or groups who exhibit strength in overcoming challenges, such as athletes or leaders in times of crisis.
  • ye rejayanti rodasī: This translates to "those who make the heavens rejoice." It signifies the Maruts' role in bringing joy and abundance to the world. In a contemporary context, this can be seen in how natural phenomena, like rain brought by storms, lead to agricultural prosperity and sustenance for communities.
  • cid urvī pinvanty: This means "they who fill the earth with consciousness." It suggests that the Maruts have a role in awakening awareness and life on Earth. This can be related to the idea of environmental consciousness today, where awareness of ecological balance is crucial for sustainability.
  • utsaṁ yad ayāsur ugrāḥ: This translates to "they who are fierce and powerful." This line acknowledges the dual nature of the Maruts, embodying both benevolence and ferocity. In real life, this can be seen in the balance of nature, where storms can be destructive yet are essential for ecological cycles.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the reverence for the Maruts, highlighting their roles as powerful deities who bring both joy and strength to the world. It signifies the importance of rituals in connecting with divine forces and acknowledges the dual nature of these forces in our lives. In contemporary scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to recognize the balance of power and gentleness in nature, encouraging a harmonious relationship with the environment and a deeper understanding of the forces that shape our existence.
