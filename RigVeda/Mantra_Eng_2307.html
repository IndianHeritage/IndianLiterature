न मां तमन न श्रमन नोत तन्द्रन न वोचाम मां सुनोतेति सोमम् |यो मे पृणाद यो ददद योNibodhād यो मां सुन्वन्तम उप गोभिर आयात ||
na mā taman na śraman nota tandran na vocāma mā sunoteti somam | yo me pṛṇād yo dadad yo nibodhād yo mā sunvantam upa gobhir āyat ||
  • na mā taman: This phrase can be interpreted as "do not let darkness come to me." It signifies a plea for enlightenment and clarity in life, urging individuals to seek knowledge and understanding rather than ignorance.
  • na śraman: This translates to "do not let me be weary." It reflects the human desire for strength and resilience, emphasizing the importance of perseverance in the face of challenges.
  • nota tandran: This means "do not let me be troubled." It highlights the need for peace and tranquility in one's life, suggesting that one should strive to maintain inner calm amidst external chaos.
  • na vocāma mā sunoteti somam: This can be understood as "do not let me speak without being heard." It expresses the importance of communication and being acknowledged, indicating that one's voice and thoughts should be valued and respected.
  • yo me pṛṇād: This translates to "whoever nourishes me." It signifies gratitude towards those who provide support and sustenance, whether physically, emotionally, or spiritually.
  • yo dadad: This means "whoever gives." It emphasizes the value of generosity and the act of giving, which is essential for building relationships and community.
  • yo nibodhād: This translates to "whoever teaches me." It highlights the importance of learning and education, recognizing those who impart wisdom and knowledge.
  • yo mā sunvantam upa gobhir āyat: This can be interpreted as "whoever listens to me and comes close." It signifies the importance of connection and understanding, suggesting that relationships are built on mutual respect and attentiveness.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound understanding of human needs and relationships. It emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge, maintaining resilience, and fostering connections with others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in educational settings where the pursuit of knowledge is paramount, in personal relationships where communication and support are vital, and in community building where generosity and understanding play crucial roles. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to cultivate a life filled with light, strength, peace, and meaningful connections.
