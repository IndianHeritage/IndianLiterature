यः सप्तर्षिम् वृषभस्तुविष्मान् अवासृजत् सार्तवे सप्त सिन्धून् |यो रौहिणम् अस्फुरद् वज्रबाहुः द्यां आरोहन्तं स जनासा इन्द्रः ||
yaḥ saptaraśmir vṛṣabhas tuviṣmān avāsṛjat sartave sapta sindhūn | yo rauhiṇam asphurad vajrabāhur dyām ārohantaṁ sa janāsa indraḥ ||
  • yaḥ saptaraśmir: This phrase refers to "he who is the seven rays" or "the one who has seven rays of light." In a real-life context, this can symbolize the sun, which is often associated with life, energy, and enlightenment.
  • vṛṣabhas tuviṣmān: This translates to "the powerful bull." The bull is a symbol of strength and fertility in many cultures. For example, in agriculture, a bull is essential for plowing fields, representing hard work and productivity.
  • avāsṛjat sartave sapta sindhūn: This means "he who has created the seven rivers." The seven rivers can be interpreted as the major rivers of the Indian subcontinent, which are vital for agriculture, trade, and sustenance. In modern times, rivers are crucial for irrigation and drinking water, highlighting their importance in sustaining life.
  • yo rauhiṇam asphurad vajrabāhur: This translates to "he who shines with the strength of a thunderbolt." The imagery of a thunderbolt signifies immense power and the ability to overcome obstacles. In real life, this can be likened to individuals or leaders who exhibit resilience and strength in the face of challenges.
  • dyām ārohantaṁ: This means "who ascends to the sky." This can symbolize aspirations and the pursuit of higher goals. In contemporary life, this can relate to personal growth, ambition, and the quest for knowledge and enlightenment.
  • sa janāsa indraḥ: This translates to "that Indra, the leader of the people." Indra is often seen as a king or leader in Vedic literature, representing authority and guidance. In today's context, this can refer to leaders in society who inspire and guide others towards a common goal.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of creation, strength, leadership, and the importance of natural resources. It signifies the interconnectedness of life, the environment, and the role of powerful leaders in guiding society. In real-life scenarios, these themes are applicable in various fields such as environmental conservation, leadership in communities, and the pursuit of knowledge and personal growth. The mantra serves as a reminder of the vital forces that sustain life and the importance of strong leadership in navigating challenges.
