महाम् इन्द्रः परश्च नु महित्वम् अस्तु वज्रीणि |द्योः न प्रथिना शवः ||
mahām̐ indraḥ paraś ca nu mahitvam astu vajriṇe | dyaur na prathinā śavaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mahām̐ - Great or mighty
    • indraḥ - Indra, the king of gods
    • paraś - Also, or in addition
    • ca - And
    • nu - Indeed, or surely
    • mahitvam - Greatness or glory
    • astu - Let there be
    • vajriṇe - To the one who wields the thunderbolt (Indra)
    • dyaur - The sky or heaven
    • na - Not
    • prathinā - By the one who is not moving or inactive
    • śavaḥ - Dead or lifeless
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes the mighty Indra, asking for his greatness to be present and recognized.
    • It emphasizes the importance of action and vitality, contrasting the active nature of Indra with the lifelessness of inaction.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, a great leader (like Indra) inspires action and vitality in their team, fostering an environment where greatness can flourish.
    • In personal development, individuals are encouraged to embrace challenges and take action rather than remain passive, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and invoking greatness in our lives, whether through leadership, personal growth, or community engagement.
    • It serves as a reminder that inaction leads to stagnation, while embracing the qualities of strength and vitality can lead to success and fulfillment.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as motivating teams in a workplace, encouraging students in an educational setting, or inspiring individuals to pursue their goals with vigor.
