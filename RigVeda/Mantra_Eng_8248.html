पवमानस्य ते कव वाजिन्सर्गा असृक्षत |अर्वन्तो न श्रवस्यवः ||
pavamānasya te kave vājin sargā asṛkṣata | arvanto na śravasyavaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pavamānasya te kave vājin sargā asṛkṣata | arvanto na śravasyavaḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "The one who is pure (pavamāna), O poet (kave), may you protect the vital energy (vājin) and the creations (sargā) from the flow of life (asṛkṣata). Those who are strong (arvanto) do not lose their glory (na śravasyavaḥ)."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • Pavamāna: Refers to purity or the pure essence, often associated with the vital breath or life force.
    • Kave: Means poet or seer, someone who has insight and can express profound truths.
    • Vājin: Represents strength or vital energy, often linked to the power of life and sustenance.
    • Sargā: Refers to creation or the act of bringing forth, encompassing all forms of life and existence.
    • Asṛkṣata: Means to protect or preserve, indicating a safeguarding of life and its essence.
    • Arvanto: Signifies strength or those who are powerful, often referring to individuals or entities that possess resilience.
    • Śravasyavaḥ: Relates to glory or fame, indicating the recognition and honor that comes from strength and purity.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, the mantra emphasizes the importance of maintaining purity in thoughts and actions. For instance, a person striving for a healthy lifestyle embodies the essence of pavamāna by choosing nutritious foods and engaging in physical activity, thus protecting their vital energy.
    • In a community setting, a leader who acts with integrity and inspires others can be seen as a kave, guiding their community towards collective strength and creation, much like a poet who weaves words into a powerful narrative.
    • In environmental conservation, the idea of protecting creations (sargā) resonates with efforts to preserve nature. Activists working to safeguard endangered species embody the essence of asṛkṣata, ensuring that life continues to thrive.
    • In the context of personal development, individuals who cultivate their inner strength (arvanto) and maintain their integrity (na śravasyavaḥ) often find themselves recognized and respected in their fields, illustrating the mantra's relevance in achieving success.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for purity, strength, and protection of life. It serves as a reminder that by nurturing our inner vitality and acting with integrity, we can create a positive impact in our lives and the lives of others. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal health, community leadership, environmental stewardship, or personal growth, the principles encapsulated in this mantra guide us towards a harmonious existence, fostering resilience and recognition in our endeavors.
