ये पायवो मातेत्यं ते अग्ने पश्यन्तो अन्धं दुरिताद् अरक्षन् | ररक्ष तान सुखृतो विश्ववेदाः दिप्सन्त इद् रिपवो नाह देभुः ||
ye pāyavo māmateyaṁ te agne paśyanto andhaṁ duritād arakṣan | rarakṣa tān sukṛto viśvavedā dipsanta id ripavo nāha debhuḥ ||
  • ye pāyavo māmateyaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "those who protect my thoughts." Here, "pāyavo" refers to protectors or guardians, and "māmateyaṁ" signifies one's thoughts or intellect. In real life, this can relate to the importance of surrounding oneself with positive influences and mentors who guide our thinking.
  • te agne paśyanto: This translates to "O Agni, those who see." Agni, the fire god, symbolizes knowledge, purity, and transformation. This line emphasizes the need for clarity and vision in our pursuits. For example, in a business context, having a clear vision can lead to successful outcomes.
  • andhaṁ duritād arakṣan: This means "to protect from darkness and misfortune." "Andhaṁ" refers to darkness, while "duritād" signifies misfortune or obstacles. This highlights the importance of seeking guidance and wisdom to navigate through life's challenges. In personal life, this could mean seeking advice from experienced individuals when facing tough decisions.
  • rarakṣa tān sukṛto viśvavedā: This translates to "those who perform good deeds are protected by all-knowing." "Sukṛto" refers to good deeds, and "viśvavedā" signifies universal knowledge. This line underscores the idea that righteous actions lead to protection and support from the universe. For instance, in community service, helping others often brings about a sense of fulfillment and support from the community.
  • dipsanta id ripavo nāha debhuḥ: This means "may we not be harmed by our enemies." "Dipsanta" refers to desires or aspirations, while "ripavo" signifies enemies. This line reflects the universal desire for safety and success in the face of adversity. In real life, this can be seen in competitive environments, where individuals strive to achieve their goals while facing challenges from rivals.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of protection, clarity of thought, and the pursuit of good deeds. It teaches us to seek guidance from wise sources, to act righteously, and to remain resilient against adversities. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal development, professional growth, and community engagement, reminding us that our thoughts and actions shape our experiences and outcomes. By embodying these principles, we can navigate life's challenges with wisdom and integrity.
