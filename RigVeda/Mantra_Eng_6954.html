यस् ते मदो वरेण्यो य इन्द्र वृत्रहन्तमः |य आददिः स्वर्ण नृभिर यः पृतनासु दुष्टरः ||
yas te mado vareṇyo ya indra vṛtrahantamaḥ | ya ādadiḥ sva1r nṛbhir yaḥ pṛtanāsu duṣṭaraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • mado: intoxication or delight
    • vareṇyo: the most excellent or worthy
    • ya: who
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • vṛtrahantamaḥ: the slayer of Vritra (a demon representing chaos)
    • ya: who
    • ādadiḥ: who takes or seizes
    • sva1r: the heavenly realm or bliss
    • nṛbhir: by men or humans
    • yaḥ: who
    • pṛtanāsu: in battles or struggles
    • duṣṭaraḥ: difficult or hard to overcome
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the qualities of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who is often associated with strength and victory.
    • The phrase "yas te mado vareṇyo" suggests a celebration of the divine intoxication or joy that comes from the blessings of the gods.
    • Indra is recognized as the slayer of Vritra, symbolizing the triumph of order over chaos, which can be related to overcoming obstacles in our lives.
    • The mantra also emphasizes the importance of human effort ("nṛbhir") in achieving success and overcoming challenges ("pṛtanāsu duṣṭaraḥ").
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied to the pursuit of success. Just as Indra overcomes Vritra, individuals can face and conquer their own challenges in the workplace.
    • In personal life, the idea of seeking joy and divine blessings can be reflected in practices such as gratitude and mindfulness, which help individuals find happiness amidst struggles.
    • In sports, athletes often invoke the spirit of victory and strength, akin to Indra, to overcome their opponents and achieve greatness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine support and human effort in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It encourages individuals to seek joy and strength from higher powers while actively engaging in their struggles.
    • In essence, it serves as a reminder that with determination and the right mindset, one can triumph over adversity, much like Indra's victory over chaos.
