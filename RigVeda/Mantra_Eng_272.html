पराः हि मे विमन्यवः पतन्ति वस्यैष्टये |वयो न वसतीर उप ||
parā hi me vimanyavaḥ patanti vasyaïṣṭaye | vayo na vasatīr upa ||
  • Mantra: "parā hi me vimanyavaḥ patanti vasyaïṣṭaye | vayo na vasatīr upa ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "Those who are beyond my understanding, they descend for my benefit; may they not be far from me."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • parā: Beyond, transcendent, or superior.
    • hi: Indeed, emphasizing the statement.
    • me: My, indicating possession or personal connection.
    • vimanyavaḥ: Those who are beyond comprehension or understanding.
    • patanti: They descend or come down.
    • vasyaïṣṭaye: For my benefit or welfare.
    • vayo: Age or time.
    • na: Not.
    • vasatīr: To reside or dwell.
    • upa: Near or close.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra reflects a deep yearning for guidance and support from higher powers or forces that are beyond human understanding.
    • It acknowledges the existence of transcendent beings or energies that can influence our lives positively.
    • The phrase "they descend for my benefit" suggests a belief in divine intervention or assistance in times of need.
    • The latter part, "may they not be far from me," expresses a desire for closeness to these higher powers, indicating a need for their presence in one's life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In moments of crisis, individuals often seek help from a higher power, whether through prayer, meditation, or other spiritual practices, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • People may feel a connection to their ancestors or spiritual guides, believing that their wisdom and support are available to them, which aligns with the idea of transcendent beings descending for one's benefit.
    • In various cultures, rituals are performed to invite blessings and guidance from divine entities, showcasing the universal desire for support from the unseen forces.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance from higher powers and recognizing the support that may come from beyond our understanding.
    • It serves as a reminder to remain open to the influences of the universe and to cultivate a relationship with the transcendent, which can lead to personal growth and well-being.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in daily life through practices such as mindfulness, meditation, and prayer, fostering a sense of connection to something greater than oneself.
