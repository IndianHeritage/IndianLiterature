यथाहान्य अनुपूर्वं भवन्ति यथा ऋतव ऋतुभिर यान्ति साधु |यथा न पूर्वं अपरो जहात्य एव धातर आयूंषि कल्पयैषाम ||
yathāhāny anupūrvam bhavanti yatha ṛtava ṛtubhir yanti sādhu | yathā na pūrvam aparo jahāty evā dhātar āyūṁṣi kalpayaiṣām ||
  • Mantra: "yathāhāny anupūrvam bhavanti yatha ṛtava ṛtubhir yanti sādhu | yathā na pūrvam aparo jahāty evā dhātar āyūṁṣi kalpayaiṣām ||"
  • Translation: "Just as the seasons follow one another in order, and as the good ones go forth, so too does the Creator not abandon the life spans of these beings."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yathā: as, just as
    • hāny: to follow, to come after
    • anupūrvam: in succession, in order
    • bhavanti: they become, they exist
    • ṛtava: seasons, cycles
    • ṛtubhir: by the seasons
    • yanti: they go, they proceed
    • sādhu: good, virtuous
    • na: not
    • pūrvam: before, previously
    • aparo: another, different
    • jahāty: abandons, lets go
    • evā: indeed, certainly
    • dhātar: the Creator, the sustainer
    • āyūṁṣi: life spans
    • kalpayaiṣām: of these beings, of these creatures
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra reflects the cyclical nature of life and existence, much like the changing seasons.
    • It emphasizes that just as seasons come and go in a predictable order, life also unfolds in a sequence that is governed by a higher power.
    • The Creator, or the divine force, ensures that life spans are not abandoned, indicating a sense of continuity and purpose in existence.
    • For example, in nature, we see how spring follows winter, bringing new life and growth, which can be likened to the renewal of life in human experiences.
    • In human life, this can be observed in the stages of life: childhood, adolescence, adulthood, and old age, each phase following the other in a natural order.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra can be applied in understanding the importance of patience and acceptance in life. Just as seasons change, so do our circumstances.
    • It encourages individuals to embrace change and recognize that every phase of life has its significance and beauty.
    • In times of hardship, remembering this mantra can provide comfort, reminding us that challenges are temporary and will eventually lead to new beginnings.
    • In a broader sense, it can inspire communities to work together for a sustainable future, acknowledging that every action has a consequence in the cycle of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of all life and the divine order that governs it.
    • It teaches us to respect the cycles of nature and life, fostering a sense of harmony and balance.
    • By understanding and applying the wisdom of this mantra, individuals can cultivate resilience, patience, and a deeper appreciation for the journey of life.
