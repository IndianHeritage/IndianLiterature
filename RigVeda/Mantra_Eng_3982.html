तं उष्ठुहि यः स्विषुः सुदन्वा यो विश्वस्य क्षयति भेषजस्य |यक्ष्वा महे सुमनसाया रुद्रं नमोभिर देवम् असुरं दुवस्य ||
tam u ṣṭuhi yaḥ sviṣuḥ sudhanvā yo viśvasya kṣayati bheṣajasya | yakṣvā mahe saumanasāya rudraṁ namobhir devam asuraṁ duvasya ||
  • tam u ṣṭuhi: This phrase can be translated as "to that one, I offer my praise." It signifies the act of devotion and recognition of a higher power or divine entity.
  • yaḥ sviṣuḥ sudhanvā: This means "who is endowed with great strength." It refers to a powerful being, often associated with the divine, who possesses the ability to protect and provide.
  • yo viśvasya kṣayati bheṣajasya: This translates to "who removes the afflictions of the universe." It highlights the role of the divine in alleviating suffering and providing healing to all beings.
  • yakṣvā mahe saumanasāya: This can be interpreted as "may he grant us great happiness." It expresses a desire for blessings and well-being from the divine.
  • rudraṁ namobhir devam asuraṁ duvasya: This phrase means "to Rudra, the divine being, I bow with reverence." Rudra is often associated with storms and healing, representing both destruction and regeneration.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of devotion, strength, and healing in our lives. It serves as a reminder to seek divine assistance in overcoming challenges and afflictions. In real-life scenarios, individuals can apply the teachings of this mantra by practicing gratitude, seeking inner strength during difficult times, and fostering a sense of community to support one another in healing. The mantra encourages us to recognize the divine presence in our lives and to approach it with humility and reverence, ultimately leading to personal and collective well-being.
