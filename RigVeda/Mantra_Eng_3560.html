आ न इन्द्राबृहस्पति गृहम इन्द्रश्च गच्छताम् |सोमपा सोमपीतये ||
ā na indrābṛhaspatī gṛham indraś ca gacchatam | somapā somapītaye ||
  • Mantra: "ā na indrābṛhaspatī gṛham indraś ca gacchatam | somapā somapītaye ||"
  • Translation: "May Indra and Brihaspati come to our home; may the drinkers of Soma come for the enjoyment of Soma."
  • Key Terms:
    • Indra: The king of the gods, associated with rain, thunder, and war. In real life, Indra symbolizes strength and leadership.
    • Brihaspati: The guru of the gods, representing wisdom and knowledge. He can be seen as a mentor or teacher in our lives.
    • Soma: A sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. In modern terms, it can represent any source of inspiration or creativity.
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra is a call for divine presence in one's home, inviting strength and wisdom into daily life.
    • In a real-life scenario, this can be likened to inviting positive influences, such as mentors or supportive friends, into our lives to help us grow.
    • The mention of Soma suggests the importance of creativity and inspiration, which can be found in various forms, such as art, music, or even nature.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra emphasizes the significance of inviting positive energies and influences into our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek wisdom and strength from those around us, fostering a supportive environment.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied by surrounding ourselves with inspiring individuals and engaging in activities that promote creativity and growth.
