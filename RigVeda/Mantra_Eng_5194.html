त्वां अग्ने हरितो वावशानां गिरः साचन्ते धुनयो घृताचिः | पतिं कृष्टीनां रथ्यं रयीणां वैश्वानरं उषसां केतुम अह्नाम् ||
tvām agne harito vāvaśānā giraḥ sacante dhunayo ghṛtācīḥ | patiṁ kṛṣṭīnāṁ rathyaṁ rayīṇāṁ vaiśvānaram uṣasāṁ ketum ahnām ||
  • tvām agne - "to you, O Agni" - This phrase addresses Agni, the fire god, who is considered a vital force in Vedic rituals and symbolizes energy and transformation.
  • harito vāvaśānā - "who is the one who brings forth the green" - This refers to Agni's role in promoting growth and fertility, akin to how fire can clear land for new growth.
  • giraḥ sacante - "the words that are spoken" - This highlights the importance of speech and communication, suggesting that words have power and can invoke divine presence.
  • dhunayo ghṛtācīḥ - "the one who is worshipped with ghee" - Ghee is a sacred offering in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and nourishment, indicating that Agni is nourished through devotion.
  • patiṁ kṛṣṭīnāṁ - "the lord of the cultivated fields" - This signifies Agni's connection to agriculture and sustenance, representing the importance of fire in cooking and farming.
  • rathyaṁ rayīṇāṁ - "the charioteer of wealth" - This portrays Agni as a guide or facilitator of prosperity, suggesting that through proper worship, one can attain wealth and abundance.
  • vaiśvānaram - "the universal" - This term indicates Agni's omnipresence and universal significance, representing the fire that exists in all beings and elements.
  • uṣasāṁ ketum - "the light of dawn" - This symbolizes new beginnings and enlightenment, suggesting that Agni brings clarity and illumination to life.
  • ahnām - "of the days" - This refers to the cyclical nature of time and existence, emphasizing the continuity and eternal presence of Agni in our lives.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the multifaceted role of Agni in life, symbolizing energy, growth, communication, nourishment, prosperity, and enlightenment. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to various aspects such as agriculture, personal growth, and the importance of communication in relationships. By recognizing the significance of Agni, individuals can cultivate a deeper connection with nature and the divine, leading to a more harmonious and prosperous life. The mantra serves as a reminder to honor the forces that sustain us and to seek enlightenment in our daily endeavors.
