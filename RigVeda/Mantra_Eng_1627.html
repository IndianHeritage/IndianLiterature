मन्त्र: उता वां विक्षु मद्याश्व अन्धो गावा आपश्च पीपयन्त देविः |उतो नो अस्य पूर्व्यः पतिर्दान वीतम पातं पयसा उष्रियायाः ||
uta vāṁ vikṣu madyāsv andho gāva āpaś ca pīpayanta devīḥ | uto no asya pūrvyaḥ patir dan vītam pātam payasa usriyāyāḥ ||
  • uta vāṁ vikṣu madyāsv: This phrase can be translated as "May we be filled with the essence of joy and happiness." The word "madyāsv" refers to a state of intoxication, not necessarily in the literal sense of alcohol, but rather a metaphorical intoxication with joy and bliss. In real life, this can be likened to moments of celebration, such as weddings or festivals, where people come together to share happiness.
  • andho gāva āpaś ca pīpayanta devīḥ: Here, "andho" means "blind," and "gāva" refers to "cows," which are often seen as symbols of wealth and nourishment in Vedic culture. "Āpaś" means "water," and "pīpayanta" suggests the act of nourishing or providing sustenance. "Devīḥ" refers to the divine or goddesses. This line can be interpreted as a call for divine nourishment and abundance, akin to how water nourishes crops and cows provide sustenance. In modern life, this can be seen in the importance of sustainable practices that ensure the nourishment of both people and the environment.
  • uto no asya pūrvyaḥ patir: This translates to "May the previous lord of this place be favorable to us." The term "pūrvyaḥ" indicates something that has come before, suggesting respect for tradition and the wisdom of ancestors. In contemporary society, this can be related to honoring our heritage and learning from the past to make informed decisions for the future, such as in community gatherings or cultural festivals.
  • dan vītam pātam payasa usriyāyāḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "May we receive the wealth and nourishment that flows like milk." "Dan" refers to wealth or gifts, "vītam" means "to be free from," and "payasa" refers to milk, a symbol of purity and nourishment. This line emphasizes the importance of receiving blessings and abundance in life. In real life, this can be seen in the context of community support systems, where individuals come together to share resources and uplift one another.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for joy, nourishment, and the blessings of the divine. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of happiness, sustenance, and respect for tradition. In today's world, this mantra can serve as a reminder to cultivate joy in our lives, honor our roots, and support one another in our communities. By embracing these values, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous society, reflecting the essence of this ancient wisdom in our daily lives.
