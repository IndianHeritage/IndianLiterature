प्र व एको मिमय भूर्य आगो यन् मा पितेव किटवं शशास |आरे पाशा आरे अघानि देवां मा माधि पुत्रे विम इव ग्रभिष्ट ||
pra va eko mimaya bhūry āgo yan mā piteva kitavaṁ śaśāsa | āre pāśā āre aghāni devā mā mādhi putre vim iva grabhīṣṭa ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May the one who is the source of all, who is the great protector, grant us strength. Just as a father protects his son, may the divine beings remove our obstacles and grant us the strength to overcome challenges."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra va: A call for attention or invocation.
    • eko: One, indicating unity or singularity.
    • mimaya: Source or essence, referring to the fundamental nature of existence.
    • bhūry: Abundance or plenty, suggesting a request for prosperity.
    • āgo: Protector or guardian, emphasizing the role of divine beings in safeguarding us.
    • yan: That which, indicating a connection to the previous words.
    • mā: Not or may not, a negation that implies a request for protection.
    • piteva: Like a father, drawing a parallel to paternal care.
    • kitavaṁ: Deceit or obstacles, referring to challenges we face.
    • śaśāsa: To protect or to guard, reinforcing the theme of safety.
    • āre: Remove or take away, a plea for the removal of difficulties.
    • pāśā: Bonds or ties, symbolizing the constraints that hold us back.
    • āre aghāni: Remove the obstacles, a direct request for divine intervention.
    • devā: Divine beings or gods, representing higher powers.
    • mādhi: May not, again a plea for protection.
    • putre: Son, indicating familial bonds and care.
    • vim iva: Just as, drawing a comparison.
    • grabhīṣṭa: To be protected or embraced, suggesting a nurturing aspect.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, such as losing a job or facing health issues, individuals often seek strength and support from their family and friends, akin to the protective nature of a father.
    • In a community setting, when facing natural disasters, people come together, invoking divine protection and support, much like the plea in the mantra for divine beings to remove obstacles.
    • In educational settings, students often rely on mentors and teachers for guidance and protection against the challenges of learning, paralleling the fatherly protection mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for protection, strength, and the removal of obstacles. It emphasizes the importance of unity and divine support in overcoming life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal struggles, community crises, or educational endeavors, invoking such protective energies can foster resilience and a sense of security. This mantra serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our struggles and that there are higher powers and supportive relationships that can help us navigate through difficulties.
