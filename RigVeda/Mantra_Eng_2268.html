त आदित्यास उरवो गभीराः अदब्धासो दिप्सन्तो भूयक्षाः | अन्तः पश्यन्ति वृजिनोता साधु सर्वं राजभ्यः परमाऽचिदन्ति ||
ta ādityāsa uravo gabhīrā adabdhāso dipsanto bhūryakṣāḥ | antaḥ paśyanti vṛjinota sādhu sarvaṁ rājabhyaḥ paramā cid anti ||
  • ta ādityāsa uravo gabhīrā: This phrase refers to the "Adityas," the solar deities, who are often associated with light, truth, and cosmic order. The term "uravo" suggests depth, indicating the profound nature of these deities.
  • adabdhāso dipsanto bhūryakṣāḥ: "Adabdhāso" means unblemished or pure, while "dipsanto" implies a desire or longing. "Bhūryakṣāḥ" refers to those who are watchful or vigilant. Together, this suggests that the pure and vigilant beings are always in pursuit of truth and righteousness.
  • antaḥ paśyanti vṛjinota sādhu: "Antaḥ paśyanti" means they see within, indicating introspection or inner vision. "Vṛjinota" refers to the removal of obstacles or impurities, while "sādhu" signifies goodness or virtue. This line emphasizes the importance of inner clarity and the pursuit of virtue in overcoming challenges.
  • sarvaṁ rājabhyaḥ paramā cid anti: "Sarvaṁ" means all, and "rājabhyaḥ" refers to kings or rulers. "Paramā cid anti" suggests the ultimate truth or essence. This line conveys that the ultimate truth is accessible to all, including those in positions of power.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of purity, vigilance, and inner vision in the pursuit of truth and virtue. It highlights that true understanding and clarity come from within and that all individuals, regardless of their status, can access the ultimate truth. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to leadership, where leaders are encouraged to be introspective and virtuous, ensuring that their decisions are guided by a deeper understanding of truth and righteousness. It serves as a reminder that the pursuit of knowledge and virtue is a universal endeavor, essential for personal growth and societal harmony.
