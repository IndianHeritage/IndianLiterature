यौ ते श्वानौ यम रक्षितारौ चतुरक्षौ पथिरक्षी नृचक्षसौ |ताभ्याम एनं परि देहि राजन स्वस्ति चास्मा अनमीवम् च देहि ||
yau te śvānau yama rakṣitārau caturakṣau pathirakṣī nṛcakṣasau | tābhyām enam pari dehi rājan svasti cāsmā anamīvaṁ ca dhehi ||
  • Mantra: "yau te śvānau yama rakṣitārau caturakṣau pathirakṣī nṛcakṣasau | tābhyām enam pari dehi rājan svasti cāsmā anamīvaṁ ca dhehi ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "May the two dogs of Yama, the guardians of the path, protect us. O King, grant us safety and well-being, and may we be free from harm."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Yau: "Those who" or "who are."
    • Te: "Your."
    • Śvānau: "Dogs," referring to the two dogs of Yama, the god of death.
    • Yama: The god of death and the underworld.
    • Rakṣitārau: "Protectors" or "guardians."
    • Caturakṣau: "Four-eyed," symbolizing vigilance and awareness.
    • Pathirakṣī: "Guardian of the path," indicating protection on the journey of life.
    • Nṛcakṣasau: "Human protectors," emphasizing the need for safety among humans.
    • Tābhyām: "By them" or "through them."
    • Enam: "This" or "him," referring to the speaker or the one seeking protection.
    • Pari dehi: "Grant us" or "give us."
    • Rājan: "O King," a respectful address to a higher power or authority.
    • Svasti: "Safety" or "well-being."
    • Cāsmā: "And from us."
    • Anamīvaṁ: "Free from harm" or "without injury."
    • Dhehi: "Bestow" or "give."
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be recited before embarking on a journey, whether it be a physical trip or a new venture, to invoke protection and safety.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders may use this mantra to seek guidance and protection for their teams, ensuring a safe and productive work environment.
    • Parents may recite this mantra for their children, wishing for their safety and well-being as they navigate through life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of protection and safety in our lives, acknowledging the guardianship of higher powers.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek guidance and support from both divine and human protectors in our journeys.
    • In a world filled with uncertainties, this mantra can be a source of comfort and reassurance, encouraging individuals to move forward with confidence and faith.
