अस्मे सोम श्रीयम् अधि नि धेहि शतस्य नृणाम् |महि श्रवस् तुविनृम्णम् ||
asme soma śriyam adhi ni dhehi śatasya nṛṇām | mahi śravas tuvinṛmṇam ||
  • asme: This word translates to "to us" or "for us." It signifies a request or a plea directed towards a higher power.
  • soma: Soma refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes the essence of life and spiritual awakening.
  • śriyam: This term means "prosperity" or "wealth." It encompasses not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional richness.
  • adhi: This word means "upon" or "over." It indicates a sense of elevation or enhancement.
  • ni dhehi: This phrase translates to "bestow" or "grant." It is a request for the divine to provide something valuable.
  • śatasya: This means "of a hundred" or "of many." It signifies abundance and multiplicity.
  • nṛṇām: This word means "of men" or "of humans." It emphasizes the human experience and the collective nature of the request.
  • mahi: This translates to "great" or "mighty." It denotes strength and significance.
  • śravas: This term means "fame" or "glory." It represents the recognition and honor that comes from virtuous actions.
  • tuvinṛmṇam: This word can be interpreted as "to those who are worthy" or "to the deserving." It highlights the importance of merit and righteousness.

The mantra can be understood as a heartfelt plea to the divine for the bestowal of prosperity, glory, and abundance upon humanity. It emphasizes the collective nature of human existence and the desire for a life filled with richness in all aspects—material, spiritual, and emotional.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or prayer, seeking guidance and blessings for their personal and professional endeavors.
  • Community Welfare: It can be used in community gatherings or rituals, invoking prosperity and well-being for the entire community.
  • Business Ventures: Entrepreneurs may chant this mantra before starting a new business, hoping to attract success and recognition.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for prosperity and recognition. It serves as a reminder that true wealth encompasses not just material possessions but also the richness of experiences and relationships. By invoking this mantra, individuals and communities can align themselves with the divine energies that promote abundance and glory, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in their lives.
