मन्त्र: स्याम ते त इन्द्र ये त ऊती अवश्यव उर्जं वर्धयन्तः | शुष्मिन्तमं यं चाकनाम देवास्मे रयिं राशि वीरवन्तम् ||
syāma te ta indra ye ta ūtī avasyava ūrjaṁ vardhayantaḥ | śuṣmintamaṁ yaṁ cākanāma devāsme rayiṁ rāsi vīravantam ||
  • syāma te ta indra: This phrase can be translated as "O Indra, may your dark (or powerful) form be present." Indra is a significant deity in the Rigveda, often associated with strength, rain, and thunder. The term "syāma" suggests a deep, powerful presence, akin to the strength one might find in nature, such as a storm.
  • ye ta ūtī avasyava ūrjaṁ vardhayantaḥ: This part means "those who increase the energy that is necessary." Here, "ūrja" refers to energy or strength, and "vardhayantaḥ" indicates the act of increasing or enhancing. In real life, this can be likened to individuals or communities that work together to enhance their collective strength, such as a sports team training together to improve their performance.
  • śuṣmintamaṁ yaṁ cākanāma: This translates to "the one who is the most powerful among the dry." "Śuṣmintamaṁ" refers to something that is dry or barren, which can symbolize challenges or hardships. This line suggests invoking the strength to overcome difficulties, much like a farmer who perseveres through drought to cultivate crops.
  • devāsme rayiṁ rāsi vīravantam: This means "O gods, grant us wealth and abundance." "Rayiṁ" refers to wealth or prosperity, and "vīravantam" indicates bravery or valor. This line emphasizes the importance of divine blessings in achieving success and prosperity, similar to how a business seeks favorable conditions and support to thrive.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a plea for strength, energy, and divine support in overcoming challenges and achieving prosperity. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal endeavors, community efforts, or even in professional settings where teamwork and resilience are essential. The invocation of Indra symbolizes the need for powerful forces, both divine and human, to work together to foster growth and success. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, support, and the pursuit of abundance in our lives.
