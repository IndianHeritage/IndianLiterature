सुवीरस् ते जनिता मन्यत द्यौर इन्द्रस्य कर्ता स्वपस्तमो भूत्व् |य इङ् जजाना स्वर्ण्यं सुवज्रम अनपच्युतं सदसो न भूमा ||
suvīras te janitā manyata dyaur indrasya kartā svapastamo bhūt | ya īṁ jajāna svaryaṁ suvajram anapacyutaṁ sadaso na bhūma ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • suvīras: Good heroes or strong individuals
    • te: Your
    • janitā: Creator or originator
    • manyata: Considered or thought of
    • dyaur: Sky or heaven
    • indrasya: Of Indra, the king of gods
    • kartā: Doer or maker
    • svapastamo: The best or the highest
    • bhūt: Being or existence
    • ya: Who
    • īṁ: This or that
    • jajāna: Born or created
    • svaryaṁ: Divine or heavenly
    • suvajram: Good or auspicious weapon
    • anapacyutaṁ: Unfailing or eternal
    • sadaso: Of the assembly or gathering
    • na: Not
    • bhūma: Earth or ground
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the qualities of strong individuals who are considered the creators of the heavens and the earth.
    • It emphasizes the importance of good deeds and the role of divine forces in shaping existence.
    • The reference to Indra suggests a connection to leadership and power, indicating that true strength comes from virtuous actions.
    • The mantra also highlights the idea of eternal truth and the significance of community and assembly in achieving greatness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, leaders who embody the qualities of strength and virtue, such as Mahatma Gandhi or Nelson Mandela, can be seen as 'suvīras' who inspire others.
    • In a corporate setting, a manager who fosters teamwork and encourages ethical practices can be viewed as a 'kartā' who shapes the organizational culture positively.
    • Community gatherings, such as town hall meetings, reflect the 'sadaso' aspect, where collective decision-making leads to better outcomes for all.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of virtuous leadership and the collective strength of communities.
    • It encourages individuals to strive for excellence and to contribute positively to society, reinforcing the idea that true power lies in good deeds and collaboration.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, stronger relationships, and a more harmonious society.
