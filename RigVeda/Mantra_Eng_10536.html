यः परस्याः परावतस तिरो धन्वातिरोचते |स नः पर्वद अतिद्विषः ||
yaḥ parasyāḥ parāvatas tiro dhanvātirocate | sa naḥ parṣad ati dviṣaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaḥ: who
    • parasyāḥ: of the other (or external)
    • parāvatas: of the highest (or supreme)
    • tiro: beyond
    • dhanvāti: the bow (or weapon)
    • rocate: shines or radiates
    • sa: he or that
    • naḥ: for us
    • parṣad: assembly or group
    • ati: exceedingly
    • dviṣaḥ: of the enemies
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a supreme force or entity that transcends the ordinary and shines brightly beyond the reach of adversaries.
    • The phrase "who of the other" suggests a connection to something external, possibly a divine or higher power that influences our lives.
    • The mention of "the bow" can symbolize strength, protection, and the ability to defend oneself against challenges or enemies.
    • Overall, the mantra invokes this supreme force to aid us in overcoming our adversaries and to shine in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive work environment, individuals often face challenges from colleagues or external factors. This mantra can be a reminder to seek guidance from a higher power or inner strength to navigate these challenges effectively.
    • In personal relationships, conflicts may arise that seem insurmountable. The essence of this mantra encourages individuals to look beyond the immediate issues and seek a higher understanding or resolution.
    • In sports, athletes often face fierce competition. The mantra can serve as a motivational reminder to focus on their strengths and seek support from their team or a higher purpose to overcome their rivals.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength and guidance from a higher power when facing adversities.
    • It emphasizes the importance of looking beyond immediate challenges and seeking support, whether through spiritual means, community, or personal resilience.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal struggles, professional challenges, and competitive situations, reminding us that we are not alone in our battles and that there is a greater force that can aid us in overcoming obstacles.
