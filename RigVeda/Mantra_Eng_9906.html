शतं वो अम्ब धामानि सहस्रं उता वो रुहः |अधा शतक्रत्वो युयम इमं मे अगदं कृत ||
śataṁ vo amba dhāmāni sahasram uta vo ruhaḥ | adhā śatakratvo yūyam imam me agadaṁ kṛta ||
  • śataṁ - This word translates to "hundred" or "many." It signifies abundance and prosperity.
  • vo - This means "to you" or "for you," indicating a direction of blessing or offering.
  • amba - This term refers to "mother," often used to denote the nurturing aspect of nature or the divine feminine.
  • dhāmāni - This translates to "abodes" or "places," suggesting a connection to various realms or states of existence.
  • sahasram - Meaning "thousand," it emphasizes the vastness and multiplicity of blessings or offerings.
  • uta - This word means "or," indicating an alternative or additional aspect to what is being discussed.
  • ruhaḥ - This translates to "growth" or "expansion," often associated with prosperity and flourishing.
  • adhā - Meaning "below" or "under," it can imply a foundation or something that supports growth.
  • śatakratvo - This term refers to "the one who has performed a hundred sacrifices," symbolizing dedication and commitment to spiritual practices.
  • yūyam - This means "you all," addressing a collective group, often the community or society.
  • imam - This translates to "this," indicating something specific that is being referred to.
  • me - Meaning "my," it denotes a personal connection or ownership.
  • agadaṁ - This term means "medicine" or "healing," suggesting a remedy or solution to problems.
  • kṛta - This translates to "done" or "made," indicating the action of creating or manifesting something.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for blessings and growth. It expresses a desire for the divine mother to provide abundant resources and healing, emphasizing the importance of community and collective effort in achieving prosperity.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Community Development: When a community comes together to support each other, they can create a nurturing environment that fosters growth and prosperity.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to seek abundance in their lives, whether through education, career, or personal relationships.
  • Health and Well-being: The reference to "medicine" can be seen as a call to prioritize health, both physically and mentally, by seeking holistic solutions and support from others.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the interconnectedness of individuals within a community and the importance of seeking blessings for growth and healing. It serves as a reminder that through collective effort and a nurturing spirit, one can achieve abundance and well-being in various aspects of life.
