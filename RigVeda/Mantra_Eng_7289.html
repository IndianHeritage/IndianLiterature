आ दशभिर विवस्वत इन्द्रः कोशम् अचुच्यवीत् |खेदयाऽ त्रिवृतः दिवः ||
ā daśabhir vivasvata indraḥ kośam acucyavīt | khedayā trivṛtā divaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • daśabhir: ten
    • vivasvata: of the sun
    • indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • kośam: treasure or wealth
    • acucyavīt: has been revealed or uncovered
    • khedayā: by the sky or atmosphere
    • trivṛtā: threefold
    • dvaḥ: of heaven
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine Indra, who is associated with strength, rain, and the heavens.
    • The phrase "ā daśabhir vivasvata" suggests that from the ten aspects or forms of the sun, a treasure is revealed.
    • Indra is depicted as the one who uncovers this wealth, which can be interpreted as spiritual or material abundance.
    • The mention of "khedayā trivṛtā divaḥ" indicates a connection to the three realms of existence: the physical, the mental, and the spiritual.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a practical sense, this mantra can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment. Just as Indra reveals treasures, individuals can uncover their potential through education and self-discovery.
    • In a business context, the mantra can symbolize the importance of collaboration (the ten aspects) in achieving success. When different talents come together, they can create something valuable.
    • On a personal level, it can inspire individuals to seek balance in their lives, recognizing the importance of nurturing both material and spiritual wealth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking and uncovering the treasures of life, whether they be knowledge, wealth, or spiritual fulfillment.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize the interconnectedness of different aspects of existence and to strive for a harmonious balance.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various fields such as education, business, and personal development, reminding us that true wealth comes from a holistic approach to life.
