प्र नूनं धावतां पृथङ् नेह यो वो अवावरित् |नि शीं वृत्रस्य मर्मणि वज्रम इन्द्रो अपीपतत् ||
pra nūnaṁ dhāvatā pṛthaṅ neha yo vo avāvarīt | ni ṣīṁ vṛtrasya marmaṇi vajram indro apīpatat ||
  • pra nūnaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "indeed, surely" or "certainly." It emphasizes certainty and assurance.
  • dhāvatā: This word means "running" or "moving." It signifies action and dynamism.
  • pṛthaṅ: This translates to "separately" or "distinctly." It indicates individuality or separation from the whole.
  • neha: This means "here" or "in this place." It refers to the present context or situation.
  • yo: This word means "who." It is used to refer to a person or entity.
  • vo: This translates to "your" or "to you." It indicates possession or relation.
  • avāvarīt: This means "has covered" or "has enveloped." It suggests a sense of protection or concealment.
  • ni ṣīṁ: This translates to "to sit" or "to dwell." It indicates a state of being or existence.
  • vṛtrasya: This word refers to "Vritra," a serpent or dragon in Vedic mythology, symbolizing obstacles or challenges.
  • marmaṇi: This means "in the heart" or "in the essence." It signifies the core or the most important part of something.
  • vajram: This translates to "thunderbolt" or "weapon." It symbolizes strength, power, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • indro: This refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic mythology, representing authority and leadership.
  • apīpatat: This means "has struck" or "has hit." It indicates action taken to overcome an obstacle.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to action, emphasizing the importance of overcoming challenges and obstacles in life. It suggests that while one may face difficulties (symbolized by Vritra), there is a certainty that through strength (vajra) and the right actions (dhāvatā), one can achieve success and clarity (pṛthaṅ) in their endeavors.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing personal difficulties, such as health issues or emotional struggles, this mantra serves as a reminder to take decisive action and seek strength within oneself to overcome these challenges.
  • Professional Obstacles: In a work environment, when encountering obstacles in projects or career advancement, the mantra encourages individuals to confront these challenges head-on with determination and resilience.
  • Spiritual Growth: On a spiritual journey, the mantra can inspire individuals to recognize and confront their inner fears and doubts, using their inner strength to achieve enlightenment and clarity.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of recognizing challenges, taking action, and harnessing inner strength to overcome obstacles. It serves as a powerful reminder that with determination and the right mindset, one can navigate through life's difficulties and emerge victorious.
