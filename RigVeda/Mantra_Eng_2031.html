एव नो अग्नि अमृतेषु पूर्व्य धिष पीपाय बृहद्दिवेषु मानवः |दुहानां धेनुर वृजनेषु कारवे तमनā शतिनं पुरुरूपं इषणि ||
evā no agne amṛteṣu pūrvya dhīṣ pīpāya bṛhaddiveṣu mānuṣā | duhānā dhenur vṛjaneṣu kārave tmanā śatinam pururūpam iṣaṇi ||
  • evā no agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni, may you be with us." Agni is the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, this can be likened to the presence of a guiding force or inspiration in our lives, such as a mentor or a source of motivation.
  • amṛteṣu pūrvya: This means "in the immortal (or eternal) realms." It suggests a connection to the divine or the eternal truths of existence. In practical terms, this can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom that transcends the temporary nature of life, much like the quest for truth in philosophy or spirituality.
  • dhīṣ pīpāya: This translates to "may you inspire us." It emphasizes the importance of inspiration and intellect. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way artists, scientists, and thinkers draw inspiration from their surroundings or from historical figures to create or innovate.
  • bṛhaddiveṣu mānuṣā: This means "in the vastness of the heavens, among humans." It highlights the connection between the divine and humanity. This can be reflected in how people often seek guidance from higher powers or the universe when making significant life decisions, such as during moments of crisis or when seeking purpose.
  • duhānā dhenur: This translates to "the cow that gives milk." In Vedic culture, the cow is a symbol of nourishment and abundance. In modern life, this can be related to the sources of sustenance and support we rely on, such as family, community, or even nature itself.
  • vṛjaneṣu kārave: This means "may we be free from obstacles." It speaks to the desire for a life free from hindrances. In real life, this can be seen in the efforts individuals make to overcome challenges, whether they be personal, professional, or societal, such as pursuing education or advocating for social justice.
  • tmanā śatinam: This translates to "with a strong mind." It emphasizes the importance of mental strength and resilience. In practical terms, this can be related to how individuals cultivate mental fortitude to face life's challenges, such as through mindfulness practices or therapy.
  • pururūpam iṣaṇi: This means "with many forms." It suggests adaptability and versatility. In real life, this can be reflected in how people adapt to changing circumstances, such as in careers that require continuous learning and flexibility in the face of new technologies or societal shifts.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about seeking divine guidance, inspiration, and strength while navigating the complexities of life. Its significance lies in the reminder to connect with higher ideals, draw nourishment from our surroundings, and cultivate resilience. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can serve as a source of motivation for individuals facing challenges, encouraging them to seek inspiration, remain adaptable, and strive for a life of purpose and fulfillment.
