प्र वर्तय दिवो अश्मानम् इन्द्र सोमशितम् मगवन् सं शिशाधि | प्राक्ताद अपाक्ताद अधराद उदक्ताद अभि जहि रक्षसः पर्वतेन ||
pra vartaya divo aśmānam indra somaśitam maghavan saṁ śiśādhi | prāktād apāktād adharād udaktād abhi jahi rakṣasaḥ parvatena ||
  • pra vartaya: This phrase can be translated as "bring forth" or "initiate." It signifies the act of starting or activating something, often associated with divine intervention or guidance.
  • divo: Meaning "of the heavens" or "from the sky," this word connects the mantra to celestial or divine realms, suggesting a higher power at work.
  • aśmānam: This term refers to "stone" or "rock," symbolizing strength, stability, and permanence. In a metaphorical sense, it can represent the foundation of life or the earth itself.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, storms, and strength. He represents power and authority, embodying the qualities of a leader.
  • somaśitam: This word relates to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes nourishment and spiritual awakening.
  • maghavan: This term means "the generous one" or "the bountiful," referring to a deity who bestows blessings and abundance upon devotees.
  • saṁ śiśādhi: This phrase translates to "may you establish" or "may you set in place," indicating the desire for stability and order in the world.
  • prāktād: Meaning "from the past," this word emphasizes the continuity of tradition and the importance of ancestral wisdom.
  • apāktād: This term means "from the unripe" or "not yet matured," suggesting the potential for growth and development.
  • adharād: This word translates to "from below," indicating a grounding or foundational aspect, often associated with the earth or lower realms.
  • udaktād: Meaning "from above," this term signifies the influence of higher powers or celestial forces.
  • abhi jahi: This phrase means "overcome" or "conquer," suggesting a call to triumph over obstacles or adversities.
  • rakṣasaḥ: Referring to "demons" or "evil forces," this term represents challenges or negative influences that one may face in life.
  • parvatena: This word means "by the mountain," symbolizing strength, stability, and the ability to rise above challenges.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a powerful invocation for divine assistance in overcoming obstacles and establishing stability in life. It emphasizes the importance of drawing from both past wisdom and present potential, while also recognizing the influence of higher powers. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of challenge, such as during personal struggles, community issues, or even in leadership roles where guidance and strength are needed. By invoking the qualities of Indra and the blessings of abundance, individuals can find the courage to face adversities and create a solid foundation for growth and prosperity.
