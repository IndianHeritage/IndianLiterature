कस तं इन्द्र त्वावसुम आ मर्त्यो दधर्षति |श्रद्धा इत ते मगवन पार्ये दिवि वाजी वाजं सिषासति ||
kas tam indra tvāvasum ā martyo dadharṣati | śraddhā it te maghavan pārye divi vājī vājaṁ siṣāsati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kas: Who
    • tam: That
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • tvā: You
    • avasum: Wealth or prosperity
    • ā: From
    • martyo: Mortal or human
    • dadharṣati: Can dare or challenge
    • śraddhā: Faith or trust
    • it: This
    • te: Your
    • maghavan: O bounteous one
    • pārye: In the assembly
    • divi: In the heavens
    • vājī: The one who bestows strength
    • vājaṁ: Wealth or resources
    • siṣāsati: Bestows or grants
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, who represents strength, power, and prosperity.
    • The speaker questions who among mortals can challenge the divine authority of Indra, emphasizing the greatness of the deity.
    • The mantra highlights the importance of faith (śraddhā) in receiving blessings and wealth from Indra.
    • It suggests that through trust and devotion, one can attain divine favor and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, employees may invoke the spirit of Indra by working together with faith in their leadership, believing that their efforts will lead to prosperity for the company.
    • In personal life, individuals may practice gratitude and trust in a higher power, believing that their faith will attract positive outcomes and opportunities.
    • During community gatherings or festivals, people may come together to celebrate and express their faith, reinforcing the idea that collective trust can lead to shared prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the relationship between faith, divine authority, and the pursuit of prosperity.
    • It teaches that while mortals may feel insignificant in the face of divine power, faith and trust can lead to blessings and abundance.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages individuals and communities to cultivate faith, work together, and trust in the process of achieving their goals, ultimately leading to a more prosperous existence.
