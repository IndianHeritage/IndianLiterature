यो भोजने च दयसे च वर्धनम् आर्द्राद् आ शुष्कम् मधुमद् दुदोहिथा |स शेवधिं न दधिषे विवस्वति विश्वस्यैक ईशिषे सास्य उक्त्यः ||
yo bhojanaṁ ca dayase ca vardhanam ārdrād ā śuṣkam madhumad dudohitha | sa śevadhiṁ ni dadhiṣe vivasvati viśvasyaika īśiṣe sāsy ukthyaḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "He who nourishes and increases the food, who is both moist and dry, who is the source of sweetness, you who are the giver of nourishment, grant us the wealth of the earth, the one who rules over all, the lord of all beings."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yo: He who
    • bhojanaṁ: nourishment or food
    • dayase: to the one who gives
    • vardhanam: increases or enhances
    • ārdrād: moist or wet
    • ā śuṣkam: and dry
    • madhumad: sweet or delightful
    • dudohitha: who is the source or giver
    • sa: he or that one
    • śevadhiṁ: grant us wealth or prosperity
    • ni dadhiṣe: you who bestow
    • vivasvati: the one who rules over all
    • viśvasya: of all beings
    • ika: one
    • īśiṣe: lord or ruler
    • sāsy: of the earth or crops
    • ukthyaḥ: through our prayers or hymns
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of nourishment, which can be seen in our daily lives through the act of cooking and sharing meals with family and friends. For instance, a family gathering around a table filled with food symbolizes the essence of nourishment and togetherness.
    • The duality of moist and dry can be related to the balance needed in agriculture. Farmers must understand the importance of both water (moist) and sunlight (dry) for healthy crop growth. For example, during the monsoon season, farmers rely on rain (moist) while also needing sunny days for the crops to thrive.
    • The concept of sweetness can be linked to the joy and satisfaction derived from sharing food. Celebrations often involve sweet dishes, symbolizing happiness and prosperity, such as during festivals like Diwali or Eid.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of nourishment, prosperity, and the natural elements that sustain life. It serves as a reminder to appreciate the resources we have and to seek balance in our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in agriculture, family gatherings, and community celebrations, highlighting the importance of sharing and gratitude for the sustenance we receive.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to recognize the divine presence in our daily sustenance and to honor the sources of our nourishment, fostering a sense of responsibility towards nature and each other.
