स जायतामः परम व्योमनि आवीर अग्निर अभवन मातरिश्वने |अस्य क्रत्वा समिधानस्य मजनना प्र द्यावा शोचिः पृथिवी अरोचयत ||
sa jāyamānaḥ parame vyomany āvir agnir abhavan mātariśvane | asya kratvā samidhānasya majmanā pra dyāvā śociḥ pṛthivī arocayat ||
  • sa jāyamānaḥ: This phrase translates to "as it is being born" or "as it is coming into existence." It signifies the process of creation and the emergence of new life or ideas.
  • parame vyomany: This means "in the supreme sky" or "in the highest realm." It refers to the ultimate reality or the divine space where creation takes place.
  • āvir agnir abhavan: This translates to "the fire has manifested." Fire here symbolizes energy, transformation, and the spark of life. It can be seen as a metaphor for knowledge and enlightenment.
  • mātariśvane: This term refers to the wind or the divine force that connects the heavens and the earth. It represents the movement and flow of energy in the universe.
  • asya kratvā: This means "by this sacrifice" or "through this effort." It emphasizes the importance of action and intention in the process of creation.
  • samidhānasya: This translates to "of the one who is being kindled." It refers to the nurturing aspect of creation, where energy is harnessed and directed towards growth.
  • majmanā: This means "of the mind" or "of the intellect." It signifies the role of consciousness and thought in shaping reality.
  • pra dyāvā: This translates to "the heavens" or "the sky." It represents the spiritual realm and the connection to the divine.
  • śociḥ: This means "shining" or "illuminated." It signifies clarity, wisdom, and the light of knowledge.
  • pṛthivī: This translates to "the earth." It represents the physical realm and the material world we inhabit.
  • arocayat: This means "has illuminated" or "has made bright." It signifies the act of bringing forth light and understanding into the world.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, the interplay between the divine and the material, and the importance of intention and action in manifesting reality. It highlights how the birth of new ideas and life is a sacred process that requires energy, thought, and connection to both the spiritual and physical realms. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in creative endeavors, personal growth, and spiritual practices. It serves as a reminder that through our actions and intentions, we can illuminate our lives and the world around us, fostering a deeper understanding of our existence and purpose.
