किम् स्विन नो राजा जगृहे कद् अस्याती व्रतम् चक्रिमा को विबेद |मित्रश्चिद् हि ष्मा जुहुराणो देवाञ्छ्लोको न याताम् अपि वाजो अस्ति ||
kiṁ svin no rājā jagṛhe kad asyāti vrataṁ cakṛmā ko vi veda | mitraś cid dhi ṣmā juhurāṇo devāñ chloko na yātām api vājo asti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kiṁ: What
    • svin: In the night or in the dark
    • no: Our
    • rājā: King or ruler
    • jagṛhe: Has taken or grasped
    • kad: When
    • asyāti: This vow or promise
    • vrataṁ: Vow or commitment
    • cakṛmā: We have made
    • ko: Who
    • vi: Indeed
    • veda: Knows or understands
    • mitraś: Friend or ally
    • cid: Indeed
    • dhi: Mind or intellect
    • ṣmā: We are
    • juhurāṇo: Offering or sacrifice
    • devāñ: To the gods
    • chloko: The sacred verses
    • na: Not
    • yātām: Let it go or depart
    • api: Even
    • vājo: Strength or power
    • asti: Is
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a series of questions about the nature of authority and knowledge.
    • It reflects on the relationship between the ruler (rājā) and the people (no), emphasizing the importance of understanding and commitment (vrataṁ).
    • The inquiry into who truly knows (ko vi veda) suggests a quest for wisdom and insight, which is essential for effective leadership.
    • The mention of friends (mitraś) and offerings to the gods (devāñ) indicates the importance of community and spirituality in governance.
    • It also highlights the significance of maintaining one's vows and commitments, which is crucial in both personal and societal contexts.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a CEO must understand the needs of their employees and stakeholders, akin to the rājā's role in the mantra.
    • Community leaders often make promises to their constituents, reflecting the concept of vrataṁ, and must strive to fulfill these commitments.
    • In personal relationships, the importance of trust and understanding mirrors the mantra's emphasis on friendship and knowledge.
    • Spiritual practices, such as meditation or prayer, can be seen as offerings to the divine, similar to the juhurāṇo aspect of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the responsibilities of leadership and the importance of wisdom, commitment, and community.
    • Its teachings are applicable in various aspects of life, from personal relationships to professional environments, emphasizing the need for integrity and understanding.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to reflect on their roles within their communities and the impact of their actions on others.
