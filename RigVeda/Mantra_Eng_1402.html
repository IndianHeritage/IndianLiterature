उपो अदर्शि शुन्ध्युवो न वक्षो नोधा इवाविर अकृत प्रियाणि |अद्मसन न ससतो बोधयन्ती शाश्वत्तमागात पुनर एयुषीणाम् ||
upo adarśi śundhyuvo na vakṣo nodhā ivāvir akṛta priyāṇi | admasan na sasato bodhayantī śaśvattamāgāt punar eyuṣīṇām ||
  • Upo adarśi: This phrase can be translated as "He/She who is near and visible." It signifies the presence of a divine or guiding force that is close to us.
  • Śundhyuvo: This term refers to the "twilight" or the transitional phase between day and night. It symbolizes change and the potential for new beginnings.
  • Na vakṣo nodhā: This means "not hidden, nor concealed." It emphasizes transparency and the idea that truth and guidance are always available to those who seek them.
  • Ivāvir akṛta priyāṇi: This can be interpreted as "like the beloved who has not been created." It suggests that the divine presence is eternal and uncreated, always existing in our lives.
  • Admasan: This word means "in the abode" or "in the home." It indicates a sense of belonging and comfort in the presence of the divine.
  • Na sasato bodhayantī: This translates to "not awakening the sleeping." It implies that the divine does not force enlightenment upon us; rather, it is a gentle guidance that we must choose to accept.
  • Śaśvattamāgāt: This means "the eternal one has come." It signifies the arrival of a timeless truth or wisdom that is always present.
  • Punar eyuṣīṇām: This phrase translates to "for those who are returning." It suggests a cycle of life, death, and rebirth, emphasizing the continuous journey of the soul.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine guidance and the importance of seeking truth in our lives. It reminds us that the divine presence is always near, especially during times of transition, like twilight. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to moments of change, such as starting a new job, moving to a new place, or undergoing personal transformation. The mantra encourages us to be open to the guidance that is available and to recognize that enlightenment is a personal journey that requires our willingness to awaken. Ultimately, it signifies the eternal nature of truth and the cyclical journey of existence, urging us to embrace change and seek the divine in our everyday lives.
