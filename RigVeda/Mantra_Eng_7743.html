अभि त्यं मध्यं मदं इन्दव इन्द्र इति क्षरः |अभि वाजिनो अरवतः ||
abhi tyam madyam madam indav indra iti kṣara | abhi vājino arvataḥ ||
  • Mantra: "abhi tyam madyam madam indav indra iti kṣara | abhi vājino arvataḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, for, or in favor of
    • tyam: that, that which
    • madyam: the middle, the essence, or the core
    • madam: intoxication, joy, or bliss
    • indav: of the gods, divine
    • indra: the king of gods, a deity associated with strength and power
    • iti: thus, so, or in this manner
    • kṣara: perishable, transient, or mortal
    • vājino: of the strong, powerful, or victorious
    • arvataḥ: of the chariots, or those who are swift
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the essence of joy and intoxication that comes from divine sources.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking strength and power, represented by Indra, in our pursuits.
    • The mention of "kṣara" reminds us of the transient nature of life and the importance of making the most of our time.
    • The phrase "abhi vājino arvataḥ" suggests that we should strive for victory and strength in our endeavors, akin to a chariot racing towards its goal.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive environment, such as a workplace, individuals often seek inspiration from leaders (like Indra) to motivate themselves and their teams.
    • During celebrations, people often indulge in joyous activities that can be seen as a form of 'madam' or intoxication, bringing communities together.
    • In sports, athletes embody the spirit of "vājino" as they strive for victory, pushing their limits much like a chariot racing towards the finish line.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder to embrace joy and strength in our lives while being aware of the transient nature of existence.
    • It encourages us to seek divine inspiration and strive for excellence in our pursuits, whether personal or professional.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the importance of balance between enjoying life and recognizing the impermanence of our experiences.
