तिष्ठा हरि रथ आ युज्यमाना याहि वायुर न नीयुतो नो अच् छ |पिबास्य अन्धो अभिषृष्टो अस्मे इन्द्र स्वाहा ररिमा ते मदाय ||
tiṣṭhā harī ratha ā yujyamānā yāhi vāyur na niyuto no accha | pibāsy andho abhisṛṣṭo asme indra svāhā rarimā te madāya ||
  • tiṣṭhā: This word translates to "stand" or "be firm." It signifies stability and strength, which are essential qualities in both personal and communal life.
  • harī: Often associated with the divine, "harī" can mean "the one who removes sins" or "the Lord." This term emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in our endeavors.
  • ratha: This means "chariot," symbolizing the vehicle of life or the means through which we navigate our journey. In a real-life context, it can represent our goals and aspirations.
  • ā yujyamānā: This phrase means "being yoked" or "joined together." It suggests the importance of collaboration and unity in achieving our objectives.
  • yāhi: This translates to "come" or "go," indicating a call to action or movement towards a goal.
  • vāyur: Meaning "wind," it symbolizes the force of nature and the unseen energies that influence our lives. It can represent change and the need to adapt.
  • na niyuto: This means "not restrained" or "not held back," suggesting the importance of freedom and the ability to pursue one's path without obstacles.
  • no accha: This translates to "not bad" or "not evil," emphasizing the pursuit of goodness and righteousness in our actions.
  • pibāsy: Meaning "drink," it symbolizes the act of receiving or taking in nourishment, both physically and spiritually.
  • andho: This word means "blind," which can represent ignorance or lack of awareness. It serves as a reminder to seek knowledge and understanding.
  • abhisṛṣṭo: This translates to "released" or "set free," indicating liberation from constraints or limitations.
  • asme: Meaning "to us," it signifies a collective experience or shared journey.
  • indra: Referring to the king of gods, Indra symbolizes strength, power, and leadership. This highlights the importance of seeking strength in our endeavors.
  • svāhā: A term often used in Vedic rituals, it signifies offering or surrendering to the divine.
  • rarimā: This means "to be strong" or "to be powerful," emphasizing the need for resilience in the face of challenges.
  • te madāya: This translates to "for you, for the sake of joy," indicating that our actions should be aimed at achieving happiness and fulfillment.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, unity, and divine guidance in our lives. It encourages us to stand firm in our pursuits, seek collaboration, and embrace change while remaining free from negativity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork in professional settings, personal growth journeys, and spiritual practices. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can navigate life's challenges with resilience and purpose, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
