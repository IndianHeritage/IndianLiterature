इन्द्रो अश्रायि सुध्यो निरके पज्रेणु स्तोमो दुर्यो न यूपः | अश्वयुर गव्यूर रथयुर वसूयुर इन्द्र इद् रायः क्षयति प्रयन्ता ||
indro aśrāyi sudhyo nireke pajreṣu stomo duryo na yūpaḥ | aśvayur gavyū rathayur vasūyur indra id rāyaḥ kṣayati prayantā ||
  • Indro: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Aśrāyi: Means to support or uphold, indicating the role of Indra as a protector and sustainer.
  • Sudhyo: Translates to 'of the good' or 'pure', suggesting a connection to positive qualities and virtues.
  • Nireke: Means 'without any hindrance', emphasizing the idea of unobstructed progress or success.
  • Pajreṣu: Refers to 'in the battles' or 'in the conflicts', indicating the challenges faced in life.
  • Stomo: Means 'praise' or 'hymn', highlighting the importance of expressing gratitude and reverence.
  • Duryo: Translates to 'difficult' or 'hard', representing the struggles and adversities one may encounter.
  • Na: Means 'not', indicating negation or the absence of something.
  • Yūpaḥ: Refers to 'the sacrificial post', symbolizing the importance of rituals and offerings in Vedic culture.
  • Aśvayur: Means 'the one who has horses', often associated with wealth and prosperity.
  • Gavyū: Refers to 'the one who has cows', symbolizing nourishment and sustenance.
  • Rathayur: Means 'the one who has chariots', representing mobility and progress.
  • Vasūyur: Refers to 'the one who has wealth', indicating abundance and prosperity.
  • Indra: Reiterating the presence of Indra, emphasizing his significance in the mantra.
  • Id: Means 'this', pointing to the immediate context or situation.
  • Rāyaḥ: Refers to 'wealth' or 'prosperity', highlighting the goal of achieving abundance.
  • Kṣayati: Means 'to destroy' or 'to remove', indicating the removal of obstacles or negativity.
  • Prayantā: Means 'those who strive' or 'those who move forward', emphasizing the importance of effort and action.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, support, and prosperity in the face of challenges. It highlights the importance of divine assistance in overcoming difficulties and achieving success. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as pursuing career goals, facing personal challenges, or striving for spiritual growth. By invoking the qualities of Indra, individuals can find the courage and resources needed to navigate through life's adversities, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and prosperous existence.
