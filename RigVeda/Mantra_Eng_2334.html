प्र बभ्रवे वृषभाय श्वितीचे महो महीं सुष्टुतिम ईरयामि |नमस्यā कल्मलीकिनं नमोभिर गृणीमसि त्वेषं रुद्रस्य नाम ||
pra babhrave vṛṣabhāya śvitīce maho mahīṁ suṣṭutim īrayāmi | namasyā kalmalīkinaṁ namobhir gṛṇīmasi tveṣaṁ rudrasya nāma ||
  • pra babhrave: This phrase can be interpreted as "I invoke" or "I call upon." It signifies the act of reaching out to a higher power or divine entity.
  • vṛṣabhāya: This term refers to "the bull," which is often a symbol of strength and power in Vedic literature. The bull can represent the force of nature or the divine masculine energy.
  • śvitīce: This word means "in the bright light" or "radiant." It suggests a connection to purity, enlightenment, and the divine presence that illuminates the path.
  • maho mahīṁ: Translated as "great earth," this phrase emphasizes the vastness and significance of the earth, which is often seen as a nurturing mother in many cultures.
  • suṣṭutim: This means "praise" or "hymn." It indicates the act of expressing gratitude and reverence towards the divine or the forces of nature.
  • īrayāmi: This translates to "I offer" or "I present." It signifies the act of giving or dedicating something to the divine, often in the form of prayers or rituals.
  • namasyā: This word means "with reverence" or "with respect." It indicates a humble approach towards the divine, acknowledging its greatness.
  • kalmalīkinaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who removes impurities." It refers to a divine force that cleanses and purifies, often associated with spiritual growth and enlightenment.
  • namobhir: This means "with salutations" or "with bowing." It emphasizes the importance of humility and respect in spiritual practices.
  • gṛṇīmasi: This translates to "we sing" or "we chant." It signifies the collective act of worship and the power of community in spiritual practices.
  • tveṣaṁ: This means "your" or "of you," indicating a personal connection to the divine being addressed.
  • rudrasya: This refers to "Rudra," a form of the divine often associated with storms, wind, and the fierce aspects of nature. Rudra embodies both destruction and regeneration.
  • nāma: This means "name." In many spiritual traditions, the name of the divine holds significant power and is often used in prayers and chants.

Conclusion: The mantra "pra babhrave vṛṣabhāya śvitīce maho mahīṁ suṣṭutim īrayāmi | namasyā kalmalīkinaṁ namobhir gṛṇīmasi tveṣaṁ rudrasya nāma ||" is a powerful invocation that emphasizes the importance of reverence, strength, and purification in our spiritual journey. It calls upon the divine forces of nature, particularly Rudra, to cleanse and guide us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during rituals, meditation, or moments of reflection when seeking strength and clarity. It reminds us of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of humility and gratitude in our lives. By acknowledging the divine and the natural world, we cultivate a deeper understanding of our place within it and foster a sense of community and respect for all living things.
