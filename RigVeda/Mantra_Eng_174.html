इन्द्रावरुण वाम अहं हुवे चित्राय राधसे |अस्मान सु जिग्युषस कृतम् ||
indrāvaruṇa vām ahaṁ huve citrāya rādhase | asmān su jigyuṣas kṛtam ||
  • Indrāvaruṇa: This term refers to the combined deities Indra and Varuna. Indra is the god of rain and thunderstorms, symbolizing strength and power, while Varuna is associated with the cosmic order and water. Together, they represent the balance of nature and the forces that govern the universe.
  • Vām: This word translates to "beautiful" or "pleasant." It signifies the aesthetic and harmonious aspects of life, suggesting that beauty and goodness are essential components of existence.
  • Ahaṁ: Meaning "I" or "myself," this word emphasizes the personal connection and the speaker's identity in the invocation. It indicates a sense of self-awareness and the importance of individual intention in spiritual practices.
  • Huve: This term means "I invoke" or "I call upon." It reflects the act of prayer or supplication, highlighting the importance of communication with the divine and the desire for connection with higher powers.
  • Citrāya: This word means "colorful" or "variegated." It symbolizes diversity and richness in life experiences, suggesting that one should appreciate the various aspects of existence.
  • Rādhase: This term can be interpreted as "for the purpose of prosperity" or "for the benefit." It indicates a desire for abundance and well-being, both materially and spiritually.
  • Asmān: Meaning "us" or "we," this word emphasizes community and collective identity. It suggests that the invocation is not just for the individual but for the benefit of all.
  • Su: This word translates to "well" or "good." It signifies positive outcomes and the aspiration for favorable conditions in life.
  • Jigyuṣas: This term means "to achieve" or "to attain." It reflects the desire for success and accomplishment in various endeavors.
  • Kṛtam: Meaning "done" or "performed," this word indicates the actions taken and the efforts made towards achieving goals.

Conclusion: The mantra "indrāvaruṇa vām ahaṁ huve citrāya rādhase | asmān su jigyuṣas kṛtam ||" is a powerful invocation that emphasizes the importance of seeking divine assistance for prosperity and success. It highlights the interconnectedness of individual and collective aspirations, urging us to appreciate the beauty and diversity of life while striving for positive outcomes. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal challenges, community endeavors, or when seeking guidance in professional pursuits. By invoking the blessings of Indra and Varuna, individuals can cultivate a sense of harmony, strength, and abundance in their lives.
