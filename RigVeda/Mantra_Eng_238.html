मन्त्र: हत वृत्रं सुदानव इन्द्रेण सहसा युजा |मा नो दुष्हंस īशत ||
hata vṛtraṁ sudānava indreṇa sahasā yujā | mā no duḥśaṁsa īśata ||
  • Mantra: "hata vṛtraṁ sudānava indreṇa sahasā yujā | mā no duḥśaṁsa īśata ||"
  • Translation: "O Indra, with your powerful might, you have defeated Vritra, the great serpent. May you protect us from all harm."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Hata: Defeated or vanquished.
    • Vṛtraṁ: Vritra, often depicted as a serpent or dragon, symbolizes obstacles or challenges.
    • Sudānava: A term that can refer to the one who is well-versed or skilled, often associated with Indra.
    • Indreṇa: Referring to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, known for his strength and valor.
    • Sahasā: With great power or might.
    • Yujā: To join or unite, often implying a connection or alliance.
    • Mā: May not or do not.
    • Duḥśaṁsa: Harmful or evil deeds.
    • Īśata: To rule or control.

  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra invokes the power of Indra, who is often seen as a protector and a warrior against chaos and evil.
    • Vritra represents the obstacles and challenges that one faces in life, akin to personal struggles or societal issues.
    • The plea for protection from "duḥśaṁsa" reflects a universal desire for safety and well-being in the face of adversity.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, someone facing a significant challenge, such as a health issue or financial crisis, may recite this mantra to seek strength and support.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be relevant during times of natural disasters or social unrest, where collective strength and protection are sought.
    • In a corporate environment, leaders may draw inspiration from this mantra when overcoming market challenges or competition, emphasizing teamwork and resilience.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity and strength in overcoming obstacles.
    • It highlights the importance of seeking divine support in times of trouble, reinforcing the belief that with courage and collective effort, challenges can be vanquished.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals and communities to remain steadfast and resilient, trusting in their ability to overcome difficulties with the help of higher powers.
