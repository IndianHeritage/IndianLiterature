अर्वावतो न आगहि परावतश्च वृत्रहन् |इमा जुषस्व नो गिरः ||
arvāvato na ā gahi parāvataś ca vṛtrahan | imā juṣasva no giraḥ ||
  • Mantra: "arvāvato na ā gahi parāvataś ca vṛtrahan | imā juṣasva no giraḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • arvāvato: Of the swift or the quick.
    • na: Not.
    • ā gahi: Hold or grasp.
    • parāvataś: Of the one who is beyond or superior.
    • ca: And.
    • vṛtrahan: Slayer of Vritra (a demon representing obstacles or drought).
    • imā: These.
    • juṣasva: Enjoy or accept.
    • no: Our.
    • giraḥ: Words or utterances.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the swift forces of nature and the divine, asking them not to be held back or grasped by obstacles.
    • The reference to "vṛtrahan" signifies the removal of barriers and challenges, akin to overcoming difficulties in life.
    • The phrase "imā juṣasva no giraḥ" suggests a plea for the acceptance of our words, indicating the importance of communication and expression in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when facing challenges in a project, one might invoke the spirit of this mantra to seek clarity and swift resolution, much like a team rallying together to overcome obstacles.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra can be a reminder to communicate openly and effectively, ensuring that our words are received and understood, thus fostering harmony.
    • During times of crisis, such as natural disasters or personal hardships, the essence of this mantra can inspire individuals to seek strength and support from their community, much like the collective effort to rebuild and recover.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of swift action and clear communication in overcoming life's challenges.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that we should not let obstacles hinder our progress and that our words have the power to influence and inspire.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal, professional, or communal contexts, invoking the spirit of this mantra can lead to resilience, understanding, and collective strength.
