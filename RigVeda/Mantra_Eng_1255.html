यच चित्रं अप्ना उषसो वहन्तीजानाय शशमानाय भद्रम् |तन नो मित्रो वरुणो मामहन्ताम् अदितिः सिन्धुः पृथिवी उता द्यौः ||
yac citram apna uṣaso vahantījānāya śaśamānāya bhadram | tan no mitro varuṇo māmahantām aditiḥ sindhuḥ pṛthivī uta dyauḥ ||
  • Translation: "That which is colorful, bringing forth the dawn, for the wise, for the auspicious moon; may that which is Mitra and Varuna, the great Aditi, the ocean, the earth, and the sky, protect us."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • yac: That which
    • citram: Colorful or diverse
    • apna: Bringing forth
    • uṣaso: Dawn
    • vahantī: Carrying or leading
    • jānāya: For the wise or knowledgeable
    • śaśamānāya: For the auspicious moon
    • bhadram: Auspiciousness or well-being
    • tan: May that
    • no: Us
    • mitro: Mitra (the god of friendship and harmony)
    • varuṇo: Varuna (the god of cosmic order)
    • māmahantām: The great Aditi (the mother of gods)
    • aditiḥ: Aditi (the infinite or boundless)
    • sindhuḥ: Ocean
    • pṛthivī: Earth
    • uta: And
    • dyauḥ: Sky
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The dawn symbolizes new beginnings and opportunities. Just as the sun rises each day, we too can embrace new chances in our lives.
    • The moon represents calmness and reflection. In times of stress, looking at the moon can remind us to find peace and tranquility.
    • Mitra and Varuna symbolize friendship and cosmic order. In our relationships, fostering harmony and understanding can lead to a more peaceful existence.
    • Aditi, as the mother of gods, signifies nurturing and protection. In our families, we can embody these qualities by supporting and caring for one another.
    • The elements of nature—ocean, earth, and sky—remind us of our connection to the environment. We can take action to protect our planet, ensuring a sustainable future.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of auspiciousness, wisdom, and the interconnectedness of all elements of life. It serves as a reminder to seek harmony in our relationships, embrace new beginnings, and protect our environment. In real life, we can apply these teachings by fostering positive relationships, reflecting on our actions, and being mindful of our impact on the world around us. This mantra encourages us to live with intention and gratitude, recognizing the beauty and diversity of life.
