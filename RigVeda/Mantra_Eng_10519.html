ते 'विन्दन् मनसा दीध्याना यजुः ष्कन्नम् प्रथमं देवयानम् |धातुर द्युतानात् सवितुः च विष्णोः आ सूर्याद् अभरण घर्मम् एते ||
te 'vindan manasā dīdhyānā yajuḥ ṣkannam prathamaṁ devayānam | dhātur dyutānāt savituś ca viṣṇor ā sūryād abharan gharmam ete ||
  • te 'vindan manasā dīdhyānā: This phrase can be translated as "They who seek with their minds." It emphasizes the importance of intention and focus in one's pursuits. In real life, this can be seen in the context of students preparing for exams or professionals striving for career advancement. The clarity of thought and determination to achieve a goal is crucial.
  • yajuḥ ṣkannam prathamaṁ devayānam: This translates to "the first offering to the divine." It signifies the act of devotion and the importance of prioritizing spiritual practices. For instance, many people start their day with prayers or meditation, dedicating their first thoughts to a higher power, which can set a positive tone for the rest of the day.
  • dhātur dyutānāt: This means "from the brilliance of the elements." It refers to the natural forces and energies that govern the universe. In everyday life, this can be related to how we harness natural resources, such as solar energy, to power our homes and lives, reflecting our connection to the environment.
  • savituh ca viṣṇor ā sūryād abharan: This translates to "from the sun and Vishnu, they bring warmth." Here, the sun symbolizes life and energy, while Vishnu represents preservation and protection. In practical terms, this can be seen in how sunlight is essential for agriculture, providing the warmth and energy needed for crops to grow, which sustains life.
  • gharmam ete: This means "they bring warmth." It signifies the nurturing aspect of warmth, both physically and emotionally. In real life, this can be reflected in the warmth of human relationships, such as family bonds and friendships, which provide comfort and support in times of need.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of intention, devotion, and the interconnectedness of natural forces in our lives. It teaches us to prioritize our spiritual practices, harness the energy of nature, and appreciate the warmth of our relationships. In a world that often feels disconnected, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek clarity in our thoughts, dedicate our actions to a higher purpose, and cultivate warmth in our interactions with others. By applying these principles, we can lead more fulfilling and harmonious lives.
