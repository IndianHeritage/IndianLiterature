एष वाजी हितो नृभिर विश्वविन मनसस पतिः | अव्यो वारं वि धावति ||
eṣa vājī hito nṛbhir viśvavin manasas patiḥ | avyo vāraṁ vi dhāvati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣa: this
    • vājī: the one who is strong or powerful
    • hita: beneficial or good
    • nṛbhir: by men or among people
    • viśvavin: the one who knows everything or universal knowledge
    • manasas: of the mind
    • patiḥ: lord or master
    • avyō: without obstacles
    • vāraṁ: protection or shelter
    • vi: indeed
    • dhāvati: runs or moves
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful entity that is beneficial to humanity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of universal knowledge and the mastery of the mind.
    • The phrase "avyō vāraṁ vi dhāvati" suggests that this entity moves without obstacles, indicating a sense of freedom and protection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of strength and benevolence can inspire their team to achieve great things.
    • In education, a teacher who possesses deep knowledge and understanding can guide students effectively, helping them overcome challenges.
    • In personal relationships, a supportive friend who provides protection and encouragement can help one navigate life's obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of strength, knowledge, and benevolence in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek out and embody these qualities in our interactions with others.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in leadership, education, or personal relationships, the principles outlined in this mantra can lead to positive outcomes and a harmonious existence.
