तं गाथया पुराण्या पुनानम अभ्य अनूषत | उतो कृपन्त धीतयो देवानां नाम बिभ्रतीः ||
taṁ gāthayā purāṇyā punānam abhy anūṣata | uto kṛpanta dhītayo devānāṁ nāma bibhratīḥ ||
  • taṁ gāthayā purāṇyā: This phrase can be translated as "that which is recited in ancient verses." It refers to the wisdom and knowledge that has been passed down through generations in the form of hymns and mantras.
  • punānam abhy anūṣata: This means "is renewed through recitation." It emphasizes the idea that the ancient wisdom is not static; it is revitalized and made relevant through continuous practice and recitation.
  • uto kṛpanta dhītayo: This translates to "and those who are wise and learned." It highlights the importance of wisdom and understanding in interpreting and applying the teachings of the ancient texts.
  • devānāṁ nāma bibhratīḥ: This means "who carry the names of the gods." It signifies the connection between the divine and the learned individuals who embody and express this ancient wisdom.

  • Significance: This mantra underscores the importance of ancient wisdom and its relevance in contemporary life. It suggests that by reciting and reflecting on these teachings, individuals can renew their understanding and connection to the divine.
  • Real-life Application: In modern contexts, this mantra can be applied in various ways, such as:
    • Education: Just as ancient texts are studied and recited, modern education systems can benefit from revisiting foundational knowledge and integrating it with contemporary learning.
    • Spiritual Practices: Many individuals engage in daily recitations of mantras or prayers, which serve to connect them with their spiritual heritage and provide a sense of renewal and purpose.
    • Cultural Preservation: By keeping ancient traditions alive through recitation and practice, communities can maintain their cultural identity and pass on valuable lessons to future generations.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the cyclical nature of knowledge and wisdom, where ancient teachings are continuously renewed through practice and understanding. It serves as a reminder of the importance of learning from the past while applying that knowledge to navigate the present and future. In a world that often feels disconnected from its roots, this mantra encourages individuals to seek wisdom, engage with their heritage, and embody the values that have been cherished through time.
