त्वं इन्द्र बलाद अधि सहसो जात ओजसः |त्वं वृषन् वृषेद असि ||
tvam indra balād adhi sahaso jāta ojasaḥ | tvaṁ vṛṣan vṛṣed asi ||
  • tvam - This word translates to "you" or "thou," indicating a direct address to a powerful entity.
  • indra - Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and strength. He represents power and authority.
  • balād - This means "by strength" or "from strength," emphasizing the source of power.
  • adhi - This translates to "above" or "over," suggesting superiority or dominance.
  • sahaso - This word means "of might" or "of force," reinforcing the theme of strength.
  • jāta - This means "born" or "arisen," indicating the emergence of power or strength.
  • ojasaḥ - This translates to "of energy" or "of vigor," highlighting the dynamic aspect of strength.
  • tvaṁ - Again, this means "you," reiterating the address to Indra.
  • vṛṣan - This word means "the one who brings rain" or "the rain-giver," symbolizing fertility and abundance.
  • vṛṣed - This translates to "you are the one who brings forth," indicating a nurturing aspect.
  • asi - This means "you are," affirming the identity of the addressed entity.

This mantra can be interpreted as a powerful invocation to Indra, recognizing his strength, might, and nurturing qualities. It emphasizes the idea that true power comes from a combination of strength and the ability to nurture and provide for others.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra is a leader among the gods, effective leaders in our society must balance strength with the ability to support and nurture their teams.
  • Community Support: The nurturing aspect of Indra can be seen in community leaders who provide resources and support to those in need, fostering a sense of abundance and care.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can draw inspiration from this mantra to cultivate their own strengths while also being mindful of how they can support and uplift others.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its dual emphasis on strength and nurturing. It serves as a reminder that true power is not just about dominance but also about the ability to care for and uplift others. In various aspects of life, whether in leadership, community engagement, or personal development, embodying the qualities of strength and nurturing can lead to a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
