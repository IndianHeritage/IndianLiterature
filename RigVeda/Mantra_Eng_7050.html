यस्मै त्वं मगवन्न इन्द्र गिरवणः शिक्षो शिक्षसि दाशुषे |अस्माकं गिर उता सुष्टुतिं वसो कण्ववच छृणुधी हवम् ||
yasmai tvam maghavann indra girvaṇaḥ śikṣo śikṣasi dāśuṣe | asmākaṁ gira uta suṣṭutiṁ vaso kaṇvavac chṛṇudhī havam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasmai: To whom
    • tvam: You
    • maghavann: O Mighty One
    • indra: Indra (the king of gods)
    • girvaṇaḥ: Of the singers or poets
    • śikṣo: You teach
    • śikṣasi: You are learning
    • dāśuṣe: For the servant or devotee
    • asmākaṁ: Our
    • gira: Speech or words
    • uta: And
    • suṣṭutiṁ: Good praise or commendation
    • vaso: Of the dwelling or abode
    • kaṇvavac: Like the sage Kanva
    • chṛṇudhī: Listen
    • havam: Our invocation or offering
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, the king of gods, and emphasizes the relationship between the divine and the devotee.
    • It highlights the importance of learning and teaching, suggesting that even the mighty Indra is involved in the process of education and guidance.
    • The mention of "dāśuṣe" indicates a connection to service, implying that the devotee is seeking knowledge and blessings for their service to the divine.
    • The phrase "asmākaṁ gira uta suṣṭutiṁ" suggests that the speaker is asking for their words and praises to be heard and acknowledged, indicating the significance of communication in spiritual practice.
    • By referencing the sage Kanva, the mantra connects the speaker to a lineage of wisdom and knowledge, reinforcing the idea that learning is a continuous process.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In educational settings, teachers often embody the role of Indra, guiding students (the devotees) in their quest for knowledge.
    • In workplaces, leaders who mentor their employees reflect the essence of this mantra, as they teach and empower their teams.
    • In spiritual communities, the act of sharing wisdom and praising the divine can be seen as a modern-day invocation, similar to the offerings made in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of the relationship between the divine and the devotee, emphasizing learning, teaching, and the power of words.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as education, mentorship, and spiritual practices, where communication and guidance play crucial roles.
    • Ultimately, it reminds us that seeking knowledge and offering praise are vital components of our spiritual and personal growth.
