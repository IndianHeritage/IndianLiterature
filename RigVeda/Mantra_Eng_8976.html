पुनर एनā निवर्तय पुनर एनā न्य आ कुरु |इन्द्र एनā निव यच्छत्व अग्निर एनā उपाजतु ||
punar enā ni vartaya punar enā ny ā kuru | indra eṇā ni yacchatv agnir enā upājatu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • punar: again, anew
    • enā: this, here
    • ni: down, towards
    • vartaya: lead, guide
    • ny: not
    • ā: to, towards
    • kuru: do, act
    • indra: the king of gods, a symbol of strength and power
    • yacchatv: may grant, bestow
    • agnir: fire, representing energy and transformation
    • upājatu: may approach, may come near
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a call for renewal and guidance.
    • It seeks the divine intervention of Indra, the king of gods, to lead one back to the right path.
    • It emphasizes the importance of fire (Agni) as a transformative force that brings energy and clarity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of confusion or moral dilemmas, individuals often seek guidance through prayer or meditation, similar to invoking Indra for direction.
    • In personal development, the concept of renewal can be seen in practices like New Year resolutions, where individuals aim to transform their lives positively.
    • Fire, as a symbol of energy, can be related to the passion and drive one needs to pursue their goals, akin to how Agni represents the transformative power in life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking guidance and renewal in our lives.
    • It teaches us to acknowledge the divine forces that can help us navigate challenges and transform our circumstances.
    • In a broader context, it encourages individuals to embrace change and seek clarity, making it applicable in various life situations, from personal growth to professional challenges.
