पुत्रो न जातो रण्वो दुरोणे वाजी न प्रीतो विश्वो वि तारीत ||
putro na jāto raṇvo duroṇe vājī na prīto viśo vi tārīt ||
  • Putro na jāto: This phrase translates to "a son is not born." It reflects the idea of lineage and the importance of progeny in Vedic culture. In real life, this can be seen in families that place a high value on having children, particularly sons, to carry on the family name and legacy.
  • Raṇvo duroṇe: This means "not born in battle." It suggests that the individual is not born into conflict or strife. In contemporary terms, this can relate to the desire for children to grow up in peaceful environments, away from violence and turmoil, which is a common aspiration for many parents today.
  • Vājī na prīto: This translates to "not pleased with the strong." It implies a preference for qualities beyond mere strength or power. In modern contexts, this can be seen in the appreciation for intelligence, kindness, and emotional strength over physical prowess, as many people value character and integrity in relationships and leadership.
  • Viśo vi tārīt: This phrase can be interpreted as "may the community be protected." It emphasizes the importance of community and collective well-being. In today's world, this resonates with the idea of social responsibility and the need for individuals to contribute positively to their communities, whether through volunteer work, activism, or simply being a good neighbor.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the values of lineage, peace, character, and community. Its significance lies in the reminder that the essence of life is not just about physical existence or strength, but about nurturing a peaceful environment, fostering strong moral values, and contributing to the greater good of society. In real life, these principles can guide individuals in their personal lives, relationships, and community interactions, promoting a holistic approach to living that values peace, integrity, and social responsibility.
