मन्त्र: हिरण्यैभिः पविभिः पयोवृद्ध उज्जिघ्नन्त आपथ्यो न पर्वतान् |मखाऽयासः स्वसृतो ध्रुवच्युतो दुध्रकृतो मरुतो भ्राजदृष्टयः ||
hiraṇyayebhiḥ pavibhiḥ payovṛdha ujjighnanta āpathyo3 na parvatān | makhā ayāsaḥ svasṛto dhruvacyuto dudhrakṛto maruto bhrājadṛṣṭayaḥ ||
  • hiraṇyayebhiḥ: This term refers to "golden" or "wealthy." In a broader sense, it symbolizes prosperity and abundance.
  • pavibhiḥ: This word means "by the winds" or "by the forces of nature." It signifies the dynamic and ever-changing aspects of life.
  • payovṛdha: This translates to "the increase of nourishment" or "the growth of sustenance." It emphasizes the importance of nourishment for both body and spirit.
  • ujjighnanta: This means "to strive" or "to reach out." It indicates the pursuit of goals and aspirations.
  • āpathyo3: This term can be interpreted as "the path" or "the way." It suggests the journey one undertakes in life.
  • na parvatān: This translates to "not from the mountains," indicating that the source of wealth and nourishment may not always come from traditional or expected places.
  • makhā: This refers to "sacrifice" or "offering." It highlights the importance of giving and sharing in the pursuit of prosperity.
  • ayāsaḥ: This means "without effort" or "effortlessly." It suggests that some achievements can come naturally without excessive struggle.
  • svasṛto: This translates to "born of oneself" or "self-generated." It emphasizes self-reliance and personal growth.
  • dhruvacyuto: This means "steadfast" or "unwavering." It signifies the importance of being resolute in one's pursuits.
  • dudhrakṛto: This translates to "well-formed" or "well-made." It indicates the value of quality and craftsmanship in endeavors.
  • maruto: This refers to "the winds" or "the storm gods." It symbolizes the forces of nature that can aid or challenge us.
  • bhrājadṛṣṭayaḥ: This means "with shining vision" or "with clarity of sight." It emphasizes the importance of insight and understanding in one's journey.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of striving for prosperity and nourishment through self-reliance, effort, and clarity of vision. It teaches us that wealth and sustenance can come from unexpected sources and that the journey is as important as the destination. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, career pursuits, and community service. By embracing the principles of sacrifice, self-reliance, and unwavering determination, individuals can navigate their paths effectively, leading to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
