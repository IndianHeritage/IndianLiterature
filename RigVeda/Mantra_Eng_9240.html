विषूवृद इन्द्रो अमतेर उता क्षुधः स इद रायो मगवाः वस्व ईशते |तस्येद इमे प्रवणेषु सप्त सिन्धवो वयो वर्धन्ति वृषभस्य शुष्मिणः ||
viṣūvṛd indro amater uta kṣudhaḥ sa id rāyo maghavā vasva īśate | tasyed ime pravaṇe sapta sindhavo vayo vardhanti vṛṣabhasya śuṣmiṇaḥ ||
  • viṣūvṛd indro amater uta kṣudhaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "Indra, the one who nourishes, is the master of abundance and hunger." Here, Indra represents a deity associated with strength and prosperity. The term "amater" signifies nourishment, indicating that true abundance comes from a source that sustains life.
  • sa id rāyo maghavā vasva īśate: This translates to "He who possesses wealth and is the great lord of all." This line emphasizes the importance of wealth not just in material terms but as a means to achieve greater goals and fulfill one's duties in life.
  • tasyed ime pravaṇe sapta sindhavo: This means "These seven rivers flow forth from him." The seven rivers symbolize the flow of prosperity and abundance in life. In many cultures, rivers are seen as life-giving forces, providing water for agriculture, drinking, and transportation.
  • vayo vardhanti vṛṣabhasya śuṣmiṇaḥ: This translates to "The strength of the bull increases with the flow of these rivers." The bull is often a symbol of strength and fertility. This line suggests that true strength and vitality come from the abundance provided by nature and the divine.

  • Significance: This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of nourishment, wealth, and strength. It teaches that true prosperity is not just about material wealth but also about the sustenance that comes from nature and the divine.
  • Real-life Application: In today's world, this mantra can be applied in various contexts. For instance, in agriculture, understanding the importance of water sources (rivers) for crop growth is crucial. Similarly, in business, recognizing that wealth should be used to nourish and support the community can lead to sustainable practices.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder that abundance and strength are gifts that should be respected and utilized wisely. It encourages individuals to seek nourishment not just for themselves but for the greater good, fostering a sense of responsibility towards nature and society.
