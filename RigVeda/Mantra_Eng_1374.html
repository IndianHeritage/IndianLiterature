उत त्या मे यशसा श्वेतनायै व्यन्ता पान्तौशिजो हुवध्यै |प्र वो नपातम अपां कृत्नुध्वं प्र मातराः रास्पिनस्यायोः ||
uta tyā me yaśasā śvetanāyai vyantā pāntauśijo huvadhyai | pra vo napātam apāṁ kṛṇudhvam pra mātarā rāspinasyāyoḥ ||
  • uta - This word can be translated as "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition.
  • tyā - This means "by that" or "through that," suggesting a means or method.
  • me - Translates to "my," indicating possession or personal connection.
  • yaśasā - This word means "with glory" or "with fame," referring to the concept of honor and reputation.
  • śvetanāyai - This can be interpreted as "to the white" or "to purity," symbolizing clarity, truth, or auspiciousness.
  • vyantā - This word means "to be free" or "to be liberated," indicating a state of release or enlightenment.
  • pāntauśijo - This can be understood as "the one who protects" or "the guardian," referring to a protective force or deity.
  • huvadhyai - This means "to invoke" or "to call upon," suggesting a plea or request for assistance.
  • pra - This word means "forth" or "forward," indicating direction or movement.
  • vo - Translates to "your," indicating a connection to the audience or the divine.
  • napātam - This means "descendant" or "offspring," referring to lineage or heritage.
  • apāṁ - This translates to "of waters," symbolizing life, nourishment, and purity.
  • kṛṇudhvam - This means "make" or "create," indicating an action of bringing forth or manifesting.
  • pra mātarā - This translates to "to the mothers," referring to nurturing forces or divine feminine energies.
  • rāspinasyāyoḥ - This can be interpreted as "of the radiant" or "of the shining," symbolizing brightness, clarity, and enlightenment.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer for glory, protection, and the nurturing of life. It invokes the divine forces to assist in achieving fame and purity while seeking liberation and guidance. The mention of "waters" signifies the importance of sustenance and life, while "mothers" emphasizes the nurturing aspect of the divine.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra when seeking to enhance their reputation or achieve personal goals, invoking the protective and nurturing energies to support their journey.
  • Community Well-being: Communities can use this mantra during rituals or gatherings to seek blessings for collective prosperity and harmony, emphasizing the importance of unity and support.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to "waters" can inspire individuals to engage in environmental conservation efforts, recognizing the significance of nature in sustaining life.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of glory, protection, and nurturing forces in our lives. It encourages individuals and communities to seek divine assistance in their endeavors while fostering a sense of responsibility towards nature and each other.
