अग्निर ददाति सत्पतिं सासाह यो युधा नृभिः |अग्निर अत्यं रघुष्यदं जेतारं अपराजितम् ||
agnir dadāti satpatiṁ sāsāha yo yudhā nṛbhiḥ | agnir atyaṁ raghuṣyadaṁ jetāram aparājitam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • agnir: Fire, representing energy and transformation.
    • dadāti: Gives or bestows.
    • satpatiṁ: The lord of truth or righteousness.
    • sāsāha: With strength or power.
    • yo: Who.
    • yudhā: In battle or conflict.
    • nṛbhiḥ: By men or people.
    • atyaṁ: Beyond or surpassing.
    • raghuṣyadaṁ: The one who is swift or powerful.
    • jetāram: The conqueror or victor.
    • aparājitam: Unconquered or undefeated.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine aspect of fire, Agni, as a source of strength and righteousness.
    • It emphasizes the importance of truth and moral integrity in achieving success in life.
    • The reference to battles signifies the struggles and challenges one faces in life, and how righteousness can lead to victory.
    • Agni is portrayed as a powerful force that not only gives strength but also ensures that one remains undefeated in their endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies integrity and truthfulness can inspire their team to overcome challenges and achieve success.
    • In personal relationships, being honest and righteous can help resolve conflicts and strengthen bonds.
    • In sports, athletes who train hard and maintain a strong moral compass often find themselves victorious, as their dedication and integrity shine through.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of Agni as a symbol of strength, truth, and victory.
    • It teaches us that by embodying righteousness and integrity, we can overcome life's challenges and emerge victorious.
    • In various aspects of life, whether in personal, professional, or social contexts, the principles of truth and strength are essential for success and fulfillment.
