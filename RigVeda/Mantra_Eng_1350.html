युवं ह्यास्तं महो रण युवं वा यन् निरततंसतम् |तां नो वसू सुगोपā स्यातं पातं नो वृकादघायोः ||
yuvaṁ hy āstam maho ran yuvaṁ vā yan niratataṁsatam | tā no vasū sugopā syātam pātaṁ no vṛkād aghāyoḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yuvaṁ hy āstam maho ran yuvaṁ vā yan niratataṁsatam | tā no vasū sugopā syātam pātaṁ no vṛkād aghāyoḥ ||"
  • Translation: "May the young (yuvaṁ) be victorious (maho ran) in their endeavors, and may they be protected (pātaṁ) from harm (vṛkād aghāyoḥ) as they strive (niratataṁsatam) for success (vasū sugopā)."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yuvaṁ: young, youthful, representing energy and potential.
    • hy: indeed, emphasizing the importance of the statement.
    • āstam: to be victorious or to achieve success.
    • maho: great, significant, or important.
    • ran: battle or endeavor, symbolizing challenges faced in life.
    • vā: or, indicating alternatives or choices.
    • yan: that which, referring to the actions taken.
    • niratataṁsatam: striving or working diligently towards a goal.
    • tā: those, referring to the blessings or outcomes desired.
    • no: our, indicating possession or connection.
    • vasū: wealth or prosperity, representing the rewards of hard work.
    • sugopā: well-protected, indicating safety and security.
    • syātam: may it be, a wish or prayer for the desired outcome.
    • pātaṁ: protection, safety, or preservation.
    • vṛkād: from wolves, symbolizing dangers or threats.
    • aghāyoḥ: of harm or evil, representing negative influences.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive environment, such as a school or workplace, the mantra encourages young individuals to strive for excellence while being mindful of the challenges they face.
    • In sports, athletes embody the spirit of this mantra as they train hard (niratataṁsatam) to achieve victory (āstam) while seeking protection from injuries (pātaṁ).
    • In personal development, young people are encouraged to pursue their goals (maho ran) with determination, while also being aware of the potential pitfalls (vṛkād aghāyoḥ) that could hinder their progress.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its encouragement for the youth to pursue their ambitions with vigor while being aware of the challenges and dangers that may arise. It serves as a reminder that success is not just about striving hard but also about ensuring one's safety and well-being. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as education, career development, and personal growth, where the balance between ambition and caution is essential for achieving lasting success.
