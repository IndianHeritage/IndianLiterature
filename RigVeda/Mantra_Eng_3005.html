मन्त्र: सुयुग्भिर अश्वैः सुवृतā रथेन दसराव इमं श्रिणुतं श्लोकम् अद्रेः | किम अङ्ग वाम प्रत्य अवर्तिं गमिष्टाहुर विप्रासो अश्विना पुराजाः ||
suyugbhir aśvaiḥ suvṛtā rathena dasrāv imaṁ śṛṇutaṁ ślokam adreḥ | kim aṅga vām praty avartiṁ gamiṣṭhāhur viprāso aśvinā purājāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "suyugbhir aśvaiḥ suvṛtā rathena dasrāv imaṁ śṛṇutaṁ ślokam adreḥ | kim aṅga vām praty avartiṁ gamiṣṭhāhur viprāso aśvinā purājāḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • suyugbhir: with good pairs
    • aśvaiḥ: with horses
    • suvṛtā: well-formed or well-constructed
    • rathena: by the chariot
    • dasrāv: of the ten (or the ten directions)
    • imaṁ: this
    • śṛṇutaṁ: listen
    • ślokam: verse or hymn
    • adreḥ: of the mountain
    • kim: what
    • aṅga: indeed
    • vām: your
    • praty: in return
    • avartiṁ: will turn
    • gamiṣṭhāhur: they will say
    • viprāso: the wise ones
    • aśvinā: by the Ashvins (the twin gods)
    • purājāḥ: ancient or primordial
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence of the Ashvins, who are known as the twin gods of health and medicine in Vedic literature.
    • The phrase "suyugbhir aśvaiḥ" suggests a well-prepared chariot, symbolizing readiness and strength in life’s journey.
    • The call to "listen" emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge, urging individuals to pay attention to the teachings and guidance of the wise.
    • The mention of "viprāso" indicates that the wise or learned individuals have insights that can guide us in our actions and decisions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of a "well-prepared chariot" can be likened to being well-equipped for challenges, such as having the right education and skills for a career.
    • Listening to the advice of mentors or experienced individuals can lead to better decision-making, much like how the wise in the mantra guide the actions of others.
    • The Ashvins symbolize health and well-being; thus, this mantra can be relevant in contexts such as seeking medical advice or holistic health practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of preparation, wisdom, and the divine guidance of the Ashvins in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to be attentive to the teachings of the wise and to approach life’s challenges with readiness and strength.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge, be well-prepared, and value the insights of those who have experience, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and successful life.
