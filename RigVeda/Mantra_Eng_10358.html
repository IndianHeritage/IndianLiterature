आञ्जनगन्धिं सुरभिम् बह्वन्नाम अकृषीवलाम् |प्राहम् मृगाणाम् मातरम् अरण्याणिम् अशंसिषम् ||
āñjanagandhiṁ surabhim bahvannām akṛṣīvalām | prāham mṛgāṇām mātaram araṇyānim aśaṁsiṣam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āñjanagandhiṁ: One who has the fragrance of collyrium (kajal), symbolizing beauty and allure.
    • surabhim: The one who is fragrant, often associated with divine qualities.
    • bahvannām: Having many names, indicating the multifaceted nature of the divine.
    • akṛṣīvalām: One who is not bound by worldly attachments, representing freedom and liberation.
    • prāham: I speak or I declare.
    • mṛgāṇām: Of the animals, often referring to the natural world.
    • mātaram: Mother, indicating nurturing and care.
    • araṇyānim: In the wilderness or forest, symbolizing the untamed aspects of nature.
    • aśaṁsiṣam: I praise or I extol, indicating reverence and admiration.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine feminine presence that embodies beauty, fragrance, and nurturing qualities.
    • The reference to "mātaram" (mother) signifies the protective and caring aspects of nature, akin to how a mother nurtures her children.
    • The mention of "araṇyānim" (in the wilderness) highlights the connection between the divine and the natural world, suggesting that divinity is present in all aspects of life, including the wild and untamed.
    • The phrase "bahvannām" (having many names) reflects the idea that the divine can be understood and approached in various forms, much like how different cultures have different names and representations for the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we often find beauty and nurturing qualities, such as a mother bird caring for her chicks, which can be seen as a reflection of the divine feminine energy described in the mantra.
    • In various cultures, the concept of the Earth as a mother (Mother Nature) is prevalent, emphasizing the nurturing aspect of the environment and our responsibility to care for it.
    • The idea of freedom from worldly attachments can be related to modern mindfulness practices, where individuals seek to connect with their inner selves and the natural world without distractions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing the divine in nature and the nurturing qualities that surround us.
    • It encourages us to appreciate the beauty and freedom found in the natural world, reminding us of our connection to it.
    • In real life, this can inspire us to cultivate a deeper respect for nature, practice mindfulness, and acknowledge the various forms of divinity that exist in our lives.
