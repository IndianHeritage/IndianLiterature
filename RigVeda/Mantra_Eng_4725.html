मन्त्र: मन्द्रस्य कवेर दिव्यस्य वह्नेर विप्रमनमनो वचनस्य मध्वः |अपानस तस्य सचनस्य देवेशो युवस्व गृणते गोAGRाः ||
mandrasya kaver divyasya vahner vipramanmano vacanasya madhvaḥ | apā nas tasya sacanasya deveṣo yuvasva gṛṇate goagrāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mandrasya: of the mantra
    • kaver: of the poet or sage
    • divyasya: divine
    • vahner: of the fire
    • vipramanmano: the wise or learned
    • vacanasya: of the speech or words
    • madhvaḥ: sweet or pleasant
    • apā: may we not
    • nas: us
    • tasya: of that
    • sacanasya: of the sacred
    • deveṣo: O divine lord
    • yuvasva: may you be youthful
    • gṛṇate: may you sing or praise
    • goagrāḥ: the best of the cows or wealth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine essence of the mantra itself, emphasizing the importance of wisdom and the power of words.
    • The invocation of fire symbolizes transformation and purity, suggesting that the words we speak can have a profound impact on our lives and the world around us.
    • The plea for the divine lord to remain youthful signifies the desire for eternal wisdom and vitality, which is essential for growth and understanding.
    • The reference to "the best of the cows" can be interpreted as a metaphor for prosperity and abundance, highlighting the importance of nurturing our resources and relationships.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, the mantra can be applied to the importance of effective communication. Just as the mantra emphasizes the power of words, in business, clear and impactful communication can lead to successful negotiations and collaborations.
    • In personal relationships, the idea of nurturing wisdom and maintaining a youthful spirit can be seen in how we approach conflicts. By valuing open dialogue and understanding, we can foster stronger connections with others.
    • In environmental contexts, the reference to abundance can relate to sustainable practices. By treating our resources with respect and care, we can ensure a prosperous future for generations to come.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of our words and actions. It encourages us to seek wisdom, communicate effectively, and nurture our resources.
    • In a world where communication is key, the teachings of this mantra can guide us in our personal and professional lives, promoting harmony and prosperity.
    • Ultimately, the mantra highlights the interconnectedness of wisdom, speech, and abundance, urging us to live mindfully and purposefully.
