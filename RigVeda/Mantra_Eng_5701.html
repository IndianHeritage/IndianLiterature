अविष्टं धीष्व अश्विनां न आसु प्रजावद रेतो अह्रयं नो अस्तु |आ वां टोके तनये तूतुजानाः सुरत्नासो देववीतिं गमेमा ||
aviṣṭaṁ dhīṣv aśvinā na āsu prajāvad reto ahrayaṁ no astu | ā vāṁ toke tanaye tūtujānāḥ suratnāso devavītiṁ gamema ||
  • Aviṣṭaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "not obstructed" or "unhindered." It signifies a state of clarity and openness, which is essential for progress and understanding.
  • Dhīṣv: This word refers to "intellect" or "understanding." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and discernment in our actions and decisions.
  • Aśvinā: The Aśvins are twin deities in Vedic mythology, often associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the dual aspects of life, such as light and darkness, and the balance between them.
  • Na āsu: This phrase means "may there not be." It expresses a wish or a prayer for the absence of negativity or obstacles.
  • Prajāvad: This term relates to "progeny" or "offspring." It highlights the significance of family and the continuation of life through generations.
  • Retā: This word refers to "semen" or "seed," symbolizing fertility and the potential for new life.
  • Ahrayaṁ: This can be interpreted as "may it not be lost" or "may it be preserved." It signifies the importance of safeguarding what is valuable, such as knowledge and relationships.
  • No astu: This phrase translates to "may there not be." It reinforces the desire for a positive outcome and the avoidance of misfortune.
  • Ā vāṁ: This means "to you" or "for you," indicating a focus on the recipient of the prayer or mantra.
  • Toke: This term refers to "children" or "descendants," emphasizing the importance of nurturing the next generation.
  • Tanaye: Similar to "toke," it also means "offspring" or "children," reinforcing the theme of family and legacy.
  • Tūtujānāḥ: This word can be interpreted as "those who are well-versed" or "knowledgeable." It highlights the value of wisdom and expertise in guiding others.
  • Suratnāso: This term refers to "those who are dear" or "beloved." It emphasizes the importance of love and connection in relationships.
  • Devavītiṁ: This phrase means "the path of the divine" or "the way of the gods." It signifies a spiritual journey or quest for higher understanding.
  • Gamema: This term translates to "may we reach" or "may we attain." It expresses a desire for progress and achievement in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a prayer for clarity, wisdom, and the preservation of family and relationships. It emphasizes the importance of nurturing the next generation while seeking a divine path in life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in family gatherings, educational settings, or any situation where guidance and support are needed. It serves as a reminder to foster love, understanding, and wisdom in our interactions, ensuring a positive legacy for future generations.
