नाहम इन्द्राणि रारण सख्युर वृषाकपेर ऋते |यस्येदम अप्यं हविः प्रियं देवेषु गच्छति विश्वस्माद इन्द्र उत्तरः ||
nāham indrāṇi rāraṇa sakhyur vṛṣākaper ṛte | yasyedam apyaṁ haviḥ priyaṁ deveṣu gacchati viśvasmād indra uttaraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nāham: I am not
    • indrāṇi: Indra's (the king of gods)
    • rāraṇa: the one who is powerful
    • sakhyur: friends or allies
    • vṛṣākaper: the one who is strong like a bull
    • ṛte: except
    • yasyedam: of whom this
    • apyaṁ: offering or sacrifice
    • haviḥ: the oblation
    • priyaṁ: dear or beloved
    • deveṣu: among the gods
    • gacchati: goes or reaches
    • viśvasmād: from all
    • indra: Indra
    • uttaraḥ: the best or supreme
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a profound understanding of the relationship between the individual and the divine.
    • The speaker asserts that they are not like Indra, the powerful king of the gods, nor do they possess the strength of a bull without the divine support.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine blessings in achieving success and the futility of relying solely on one's strength or alliances.
    • In real life, this can be likened to individuals who recognize their limitations and seek help from a higher power or community to achieve their goals.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights humility and the acknowledgment of one's dependence on divine grace.
    • It teaches that true strength comes not from oneself but from the support of the divine and the community.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in teamwork, where individuals contribute their strengths while relying on the collective effort for success.
    • It also serves as a reminder to remain grounded and grateful for the support one receives in life.
