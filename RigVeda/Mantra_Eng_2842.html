इमा ब्रह्म ब्रह्मवाहः क्रियन्त आ बरहिः सीद |वीहि शूर पुरोळाशम् ||
imā brahma brahmavāhaḥ kriyanta ā barhiḥ sīda | vīhi śūra puroḻāśam ||
  • imā - This word translates to "these" or "this." It indicates a focus on the present or the immediate context.
  • brahma - Refers to the ultimate reality or the cosmic spirit. In a broader sense, it signifies the source of all creation and existence.
  • brahmavāhaḥ - This term can be understood as "those who carry or uphold Brahman." It implies individuals or forces that embody or promote the principles of the ultimate reality.
  • kriyanta - Meaning "they perform" or "they act." This suggests an active engagement in the world, emphasizing the importance of action in accordance with higher principles.
  • ā - A preposition that can mean "towards" or "in the direction of," indicating movement or intention.
  • barhiḥ - Refers to "sacred grass" or "the seat of the divine." In Vedic rituals, barhiḥ is often used as a symbol of purity and sanctity.
  • sīda - This word means "to sit" or "to reside." It implies a state of being or presence, often associated with meditation or contemplation.
  • vīhi - Translates to "O you who are strong" or "O you who are powerful." It addresses a force or entity that possesses strength and capability.
  • śūra - Means "brave" or "valiant." It signifies courage and the qualities of a warrior, emphasizing the importance of bravery in the face of challenges.
  • puroḻāśam - This term can be interpreted as "the one who is the best among men" or "the noble one." It highlights the qualities of excellence and virtue in individuals.

This mantra encapsulates a profound philosophical and spiritual message. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and embodying the ultimate reality (Brahman) in our actions and interactions. The call to "perform" and "act" suggests that mere knowledge of the divine is not enough; one must actively engage with it in daily life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Development: Individuals can strive to embody the qualities of bravery and excellence in their personal and professional lives, acting with integrity and purpose.
  • Community Engagement: The mantra encourages collective action towards a higher good, inspiring communities to work together for positive change.
  • Spiritual Practice: It serves as a reminder to engage in spiritual practices that connect one with the divine, such as meditation, prayer, or rituals that honor the sacred.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call to action and the embodiment of higher principles in everyday life. It encourages individuals to recognize their connection to the ultimate reality and to act with courage and virtue. By doing so, one can contribute to a more harmonious and meaningful existence, both personally and within the larger community.
