ऋतेन देवीर अमृताऽमृतक्ता अर्णोभिर आपो मधुमद्भिर अग्ने |वाजी न सर्गेषु प्रस्तुभानः प्र सदम इति श्रवितवे दधन्युः ||
ṛtena devīr amṛtā amṛktā arṇobhir āpo madhumadbhir agne | vājī na sargeṣu prastubhānaḥ pra sadam it sravitave dadhanyuḥ ||
  • ṛtena: This word translates to "by truth" or "in accordance with the cosmic order." It signifies the importance of truth and righteousness in the universe.
  • devīr: This means "goddesses" or "divine beings." It refers to the celestial powers that govern various aspects of life and nature.
  • amṛtā: This translates to "immortal" or "eternal." It signifies the everlasting nature of the divine and the soul.
  • amṛktā: Similar to amṛtā, it also means "not subject to death" or "imperishable." It emphasizes the idea of eternal existence.
  • arṇobhir: This means "from the waters" or "of the oceans." It highlights the significance of water as a source of life and sustenance.
  • āpo: This translates to "waters." In Vedic literature, water is often associated with purity, nourishment, and the flow of life.
  • madhumadbhir: This means "with sweetness" or "with pleasantness." It signifies the joy and richness that life can offer.
  • agne: This refers to "Agni," the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine spark within all beings.
  • vājī: This translates to "the one who brings strength" or "the powerful." It signifies the importance of strength and vitality in life.
  • na sargeṣu: This means "in the creations" or "in the manifestations." It refers to the various forms of life and existence in the universe.
  • prastubhānaḥ: This translates to "those who are well-prepared" or "those who are ready." It emphasizes the importance of readiness and preparation in life.
  • pra sadam: This means "to the abode" or "to the dwelling." It signifies the importance of finding a place of peace and stability.
  • it sravitave: This translates to "to those who flow" or "to those who radiate." It refers to the idea of sharing and spreading positivity.
  • dadhanyuḥ: This means "may they bestow" or "may they grant." It signifies the act of giving and sharing blessings.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of truth, divinity, and the natural elements of life. It highlights the importance of living in harmony with the cosmic order, being prepared for life's challenges, and sharing blessings with others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek truth in their actions, appreciate the beauty of nature, and cultivate a sense of community by sharing their strengths and resources. It serves as a reminder that by aligning ourselves with the divine and the natural world, we can lead fulfilling and meaningful lives.
