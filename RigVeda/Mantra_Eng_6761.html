गिरो जुषेथाम् अध्वरं जुषेथां विश्वेह देवौ सवनावा गच्छताम् |सजोषसां उषसा सूर्येण चेषं नो वोळ्हम् अश्विनाः ||
giro juṣethām adhvaraṁ juṣethāṁ viśveha devau savanāva gacchatam | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa ceṣaṁ no voḻham aśvinā ||
  • giro juṣethām: This phrase can be translated as "May the songs (or hymns) be pleasing." It emphasizes the importance of sound and expression in spiritual practices. In real life, this can relate to how music and poetry can uplift our spirits and create a sense of community.
  • adhvaraṁ juṣethāṁ: Here, "adhvaraṁ" refers to the ritual or the sacred act. The phrase suggests that the rituals should be enjoyable and fulfilling. In modern contexts, this can be seen in how people engage in various cultural or religious ceremonies that bring joy and a sense of belonging.
  • viśveha devau: This translates to "all the gods." It signifies the universality of divine presence and the acknowledgment of various deities. In everyday life, this can be reflected in the respect for different beliefs and the idea that there are many paths to the divine.
  • savanāva gacchatam: This means "may the offerings reach (the gods)." It highlights the importance of offerings and intentions in spiritual practices. In real life, this can be related to the idea of giving back to the community or the environment, ensuring that our actions have positive impacts.
  • sajoṣasā uṣasā: This phrase can be interpreted as "with the dawn." It symbolizes new beginnings and the hope that comes with each day. In practical terms, this can be seen in how people set intentions or goals at the start of a new day or a new year.
  • sūryeṇa ceṣaṁ: This translates to "with the sun's energy." It emphasizes the life-giving force of the sun and its role in sustaining life. In real life, this can be related to the importance of nature and how sunlight affects our mood and well-being.
  • no voḻham aśvinā: This phrase means "may we not be deprived (of blessings) by the Ashvins." The Ashvins are twin deities associated with health and medicine. This can be interpreted as a prayer for health and well-being, which is universally relevant in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of joy in rituals, the universality of divine presence, the importance of offerings, and the hope that comes with new beginnings. It serves as a reminder to appreciate the beauty of life, engage in meaningful practices, and respect the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate positivity, engage in community service, and maintain a connection with nature, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
