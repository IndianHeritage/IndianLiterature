अध्वर्यवो य उरणं जग्हाना नव चख्वांसं नवतिं च बाहून् |यो अर्बुदम अव निचा बबाधे तं इन्द्रं सोमस्य भ्रते हिनोत ||
adhvaryavo ya uraṇaṁ jaghāna nava cakhvāṁsaṁ navatiṁ ca bāhūn | yo arbudam ava nīcā babādhe tam indraṁ somasya bhṛthe hinota ||
  • adhvaryavo: This term refers to the priests who perform the rituals and sacrifices in the Vedic tradition. They are responsible for the proper execution of the rites.
  • ya: This is a relative pronoun meaning "who" or "that," indicating a connection to the subject that follows.
  • uraṇaṁ: This word can be interpreted as "the great" or "the vast," often referring to the expansive nature of the universe or the divine.
  • jaghāna: This means "to strike" or "to conquer," suggesting a sense of overcoming obstacles or challenges.
  • nava: This translates to "new" or "fresh," indicating renewal or the emergence of new possibilities.
  • cakhvāṁsaṁ: This term refers to "the one who has been created" or "the one who has come into being," emphasizing the act of creation.
  • navatiṁ: This means "ninety," which can symbolize completeness or a significant number in the context of Vedic rituals.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking the ideas presented in the mantra.
  • bāhūn: This translates to "arms," symbolizing strength and the ability to act or perform tasks.
  • yo: Another relative pronoun meaning "who," connecting to the subject of the following action.
  • arbudam: This term can mean "the multitude" or "the many," indicating a vast number of entities or challenges.
  • ava: This means "down" or "away," suggesting a sense of removal or overcoming difficulties.
  • nīcā: This translates to "low" or "inferior," referring to obstacles or challenges that may seem daunting.
  • babādhe: This means "to strike" or "to defeat," indicating the act of overcoming challenges.
  • tam: This means "that," referring to the subject being discussed, in this case, Indra.
  • indraṁ: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, representing power, strength, and the ability to overcome adversities.
  • somasya: This refers to "of Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals that symbolizes immortality and divine inspiration.
  • bhṛthe: This means "the bearer" or "the one who supports," indicating a source of strength or sustenance.
  • hinota: This translates to "to bring forth" or "to produce," suggesting the act of creation or manifestation.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of ritual, strength, and the overcoming of challenges through divine support. It highlights the role of the priest (adhvaryava) in invoking the powers of the universe (uraṇaṁ) to conquer obstacles (jaghāna) and bring forth new possibilities (nava cakhvāṁsaṁ). The mention of Indra signifies the need for strength and leadership in facing life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to situations where individuals seek to overcome difficulties, whether in personal struggles, professional challenges, or societal issues. It serves as a reminder that with the right support and strength, one can navigate through adversities and emerge renewed and empowered.
