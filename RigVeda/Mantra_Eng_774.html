विओ वीरुत्सु रोधन महित्वोता प्रजा उता प्रसूष्व अन्तः ||
vi yo vīrutsu rodhan mahitvota prajā uta prasūṣv antaḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vi: A prefix that can denote separation or distinction.
    • yo: Refers to "who" or "that" in a relative sense.
    • vīrutsu: Derived from "vīra," meaning "hero" or "brave." This term can refer to those who are courageous or valiant.
    • rodhan: Means "to restrain" or "to hold back." It can imply control or governance.
    • mahitvota: A combination of "mahitva," meaning "greatness" or "majesty," and "uta," which can mean "and" or "also." This suggests a connection to greatness.
    • prajā: Refers to "offspring" or "creatures," often used in the context of humanity or living beings.
    • uta: Again, meaning "and" or "also," indicating a continuation of thought.
    • prasūṣv: Derived from "prasū," meaning "to produce" or "to give birth." This can refer to creation or generation.
    • antaḥ: Means "inside" or "within," suggesting an internal aspect.
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks to the qualities of bravery and greatness, emphasizing the importance of restraint and governance in the context of creation and existence.
    • It highlights the relationship between the courageous (vīrutsu) and the act of creation (prasūṣv), suggesting that true greatness comes from the ability to manage and control one's inner self and external actions.
    • The mantra can be interpreted as a call to recognize the strength within oneself and to use that strength wisely for the benefit of all beings (prajā).
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, a great leader (vīra) must often restrain their impulses (rodhan) to make decisions that benefit their community (prajā). For instance, a CEO may need to hold back on aggressive expansion plans to ensure the long-term sustainability of the company.
    • In personal development, individuals often face challenges that require bravery (vīrutsu) and the ability to manage their emotions (rodhan) to achieve their goals. For example, someone overcoming addiction must show great courage and self-control to create a healthier life (prasūṣv).
    • In parenting, the act of nurturing children (prajā) requires both love and the ability to set boundaries (rodhan), ensuring that they grow up to be responsible and courageous individuals (vīra).
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra "vi yo vīrutsu rodhan mahitvota prajā uta prasūṣv antaḥ" serves as a reminder of the importance of courage, restraint, and the nurturing of life.
    • Its significance lies in the balance between strength and wisdom, urging individuals to harness their inner power for the greater good.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, personal growth, and parenting, emphasizing that true greatness is achieved through self-control and the responsible creation of a better world.
