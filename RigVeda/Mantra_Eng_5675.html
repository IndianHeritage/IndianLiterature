आ नो मित्रावरुणा हव्यजुष्टिं घृतैर गव्यूतिम उक्षतम इळाभिः |प्रतिवाम अत्र वरम् आ जनाय पृणीतम उद्नो दिवस्य चारोः ||
ā no mitrāvaruṇā havyajuṣṭiṁ ghṛtair gavyūtim ukṣatam iḻābhiḥ | prati vām atra varam ā janāya pṛṇītam udno divyasya cāroḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā no: May we be blessed
    • mitrāvaruṇā: by Mitra and Varuna (the deities representing friendship and cosmic order)
    • havyajuṣṭiṁ: with offerings
    • ghṛtair: with clarified butter
    • gavyūtim: with the wealth of cows
    • ukṣatam: may we be nourished
    • iḻābhiḥ: with the essence of the offerings
    • prati: towards
    • vām: you (plural)
    • atra: here
    • varam: the best
    • ā: for
    • janāya: for the people
    • pṛṇītam: may it be fulfilled
    • udno: of the divine
    • divyasya: of the celestial
    • cāroḥ: of the one who is worthy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for blessings and prosperity, invoking the deities Mitra and Varuna, who symbolize friendship, harmony, and the moral order of the universe.
    • The mention of offerings, particularly clarified butter and the wealth of cows, signifies the importance of generosity and sharing in achieving spiritual and material abundance.
    • The phrase "may we be nourished" emphasizes the need for sustenance, both physical and spiritual, which can be interpreted as a call for community support and cooperation.
    • The mantra concludes with a request for the best blessings to be fulfilled for the people, highlighting the collective well-being over individual desires.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, this mantra can be recited to invoke unity and cooperation among members, encouraging them to work together for common goals.
    • During festivals, offerings made to deities can be seen as a way to express gratitude and seek blessings for prosperity and harmony in the community.
    • In personal life, individuals can reflect on the values of friendship and moral order in their relationships, striving to create a supportive environment for themselves and others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals within a community and the importance of collective well-being.
    • Its significance lies in promoting values of generosity, harmony, and moral responsibility, which are essential for a prosperous and peaceful society.
    • In modern life, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide individuals and communities in fostering relationships based on trust, support, and shared goals.
