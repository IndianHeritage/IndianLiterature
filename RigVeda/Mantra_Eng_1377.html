स्तुषे स वां वरुण मित्र रात्रि गवां शतां प्रक्षयामेषु पज्रे |श्रुतरथे प्रियरथे दधानाः सद्यः पुष्टिं निरुन्धानासो अग्मन ||
stuṣe sā vāṁ varuṇa mitra rātir gavāṁ śatā pṛkṣayāmeṣu pajre | śrutarathe priyarathe dadhānāḥ sadyaḥ puṣṭiṁ nirundhānāso agman ||
  • stuṣe: This word can be interpreted as "to praise" or "to extol." It signifies the act of offering respect and admiration.
  • sā: This means "that" or "she," referring to a divine presence or energy.
  • vāṁ: This translates to "your," indicating a personal connection or address to the divine.
  • varuṇa: Varuṇa is a deity associated with cosmic order and truth, often seen as a guardian of moral law.
  • mitra: Mitra represents friendship and harmony, emphasizing the importance of relationships and community.
  • rātir: This word means "night," symbolizing the time of rest, reflection, and the mysteries of life.
  • gavāṁ: This translates to "of the cows," which in Vedic culture symbolizes wealth, nourishment, and prosperity.
  • śatā: Meaning "hundred," it signifies abundance and the idea of many blessings.
  • pṛkṣayāmeṣu: This can be interpreted as "we observe" or "we witness," indicating an active engagement with the divine and the world around us.
  • pajre: This word can mean "to be free" or "to be liberated," suggesting a desire for spiritual freedom and enlightenment.
  • śrutarathe: This refers to "the one who hears," indicating the importance of listening and understanding in spiritual practice.
  • priyarathe: Meaning "beloved charioteer," it symbolizes guidance and support in one's journey through life.
  • dadhānāḥ: This translates to "holding" or "possessing," indicating the act of embracing or nurturing something valuable.
  • sadyaḥ: Meaning "immediately" or "right away," it emphasizes the urgency and immediacy of spiritual connection.
  • puṣṭiṁ: This word means "nourishment" or "prosperity," highlighting the importance of sustenance in both physical and spiritual realms.
  • nirundhānāso: This can be interpreted as "removing obstacles," indicating the desire to clear the path for growth and success.
  • agman: Meaning "they have come," it signifies the arrival of blessings or divine presence in one's life.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of praise, connection, and the nurturing of relationships with the divine and the community. It highlights the significance of abundance, both materially and spiritually, and the need for guidance and support in our journeys. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as fostering friendships, seeking spiritual growth, and striving for prosperity while removing obstacles in our paths. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a harmonious and fulfilling life, enriched by the blessings of the divine and the support of their community.
