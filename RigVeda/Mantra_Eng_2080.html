या उ श्रीया दमेष्वादोषोऽसि प्रशस्यते। यस्य व्रतम् न मीयते॥
ya u śriyā dameṣv ā doṣoṣasi praśasyate | yasya vrataṁ na mīyate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who
    • u: indeed
    • śriyā: with prosperity or wealth
    • dameṣv: in the household or in the home
    • ā: towards
    • doṣoṣasi: faults or defects
    • praśasyate: is praised or is considered good
    • yasya: whose
    • vrataṁ: vow or commitment
    • na: not
    • mīyate: is diminished or is broken
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about a person who, despite being in a prosperous household, does not let faults or shortcomings overshadow their virtues.
    • It emphasizes the importance of maintaining one's commitments and vows, suggesting that true integrity lies in not wavering from one's principles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A successful business leader who, despite facing challenges and criticisms, remains committed to ethical practices and does not compromise on their values.
    • A family member who, while living in a comfortable environment, continues to support and uplift others, ensuring that their actions reflect kindness and integrity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of integrity and commitment in our lives. It teaches us that true prosperity is not just about material wealth but also about maintaining our values and principles.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal relationships, professional ethics, and community service, where one’s actions should reflect their commitments and virtues, regardless of external circumstances.
