अक्षेत्रवित् क्षेत्रविदं ह्य अप्रात् स प्रैति क्षेत्रविदानुशिष्टः |एतद् वै भद्रम अनुशासनस्योता श्रुतिं विन्दत्य अञ्जसीनाम् ||
akṣetravit kṣetravidaṁ hy aprāṭ sa praiti kṣetravidānuśiṣṭaḥ | etad vai bhadram anuśāsanasyota srutiṁ vindaty añjasīnām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • akṣetravit: One who knows the field (the body or the material world).
    • kṣetravidaṁ: The knowledge of the field.
    • hy: Indeed.
    • aprāṭ: Not covered or hidden.
    • sa: He.
    • praiti: Goes or departs.
    • kṣetravidānuśiṣṭaḥ: One who is instructed by the knower of the field.
    • etad: This.
    • vai: Certainly.
    • bhadram: Auspiciousness or good fortune.
    • anuśāsanasyota: Of the instruction or teaching.
    • srutiṁ: The hearing or knowledge.
    • vindaty: Attains or finds.
    • añjasīnām: Of those who are straightforward or sincere.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of understanding the material world (the field) and the knowledge that comes from it.
    • It emphasizes that one who is knowledgeable about the field and is guided by that knowledge will attain auspiciousness and clarity.
    • The phrase "kṣetravidānuśiṣṭaḥ" suggests that guidance from a knowledgeable source is essential for true understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, a student who seeks guidance from a knowledgeable teacher (kṣetravidā) will have a better understanding of the subject matter and achieve better results.
    • In a professional setting, an employee who learns from an experienced mentor will navigate challenges more effectively and contribute positively to the organization.
    • In personal development, individuals who seek wisdom from spiritual leaders or philosophical texts often find greater clarity and purpose in their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of knowledge and guidance in achieving success and auspiciousness in life.
    • It teaches us that understanding the world around us, along with the wisdom imparted by knowledgeable individuals, leads to a more fulfilling and meaningful existence.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to seek knowledge, remain open to learning, and value the teachings of those who have greater experience and insight.
