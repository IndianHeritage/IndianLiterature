अस्मै तिस्रो व्यथ्याय नारीर देवाय देवीर दिधिषन्त्य अन्नम् |कृताऽइवोप हि प्रसर्ष्रे अप्सु स पीयूषं धयति पूर्वसूनाम् ||
asmai tisro avyathyāya nārīr devāya devīr didhiṣanty annam | kṛtā ivopa hi prasarsre apsu sa pīyūṣaṁ dhayati pūrvasūnām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmai: for him
    • tisro: three
    • avyathyāya: without distress
    • nārīr: women
    • devāya: to the deity
    • devīr: goddesses
    • didhiṣanty: who desire
    • annam: food
    • kṛtā: made
    • ivopa: as if
    • hi: indeed
    • prasarsre: flowing
    • apsu: in the waters
    • sa: he
    • pīyūṣaṁ: nectar
    • dhayati: nourishes
    • pūrvasūnām: of the ancient ones
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine connection between nourishment and the divine feminine.
    • The phrase "for him" suggests a prayer or offering directed towards a deity, emphasizing the importance of divine blessings in our sustenance.
    • The mention of "three" could symbolize various triads in Hindu philosophy, such as the three gunas (qualities) or the three stages of life.
    • The term "without distress" indicates a desire for peace and well-being, which is essential for a fulfilling life.
    • The reference to "women" and "goddesses" highlights the reverence for feminine energy and its role in creation and sustenance.
    • "Who desire food" signifies the basic human need for nourishment, linking it to divine blessings.
    • The imagery of "flowing waters" and "nectar" evokes a sense of abundance and the life-giving properties of nature.
    • The phrase "of the ancient ones" connects the present with the wisdom of the past, suggesting that the teachings and blessings of ancestors are still relevant today.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals are performed to honor the earth and feminine deities to ensure a good harvest, reflecting the connection between spirituality and sustenance.
    • Modern practices of mindfulness and gratitude before meals can be seen as a way to acknowledge the divine in our daily nourishment.
    • Women in various societies often play a crucial role in food production and preparation, symbolizing the nurturing aspect of the feminine divine.
    • Environmental movements that emphasize the importance of water and nature resonate with the imagery of "flowing waters" in the mantra, highlighting the need for sustainable practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nourishment, the divine feminine, and the wisdom of the past.
    • It serves as a reminder to honor the sources of our sustenance and the spiritual dimensions of our lives.
    • In real life, it encourages us to cultivate gratitude, respect for nature, and recognition of the roles women play in nurturing and sustaining life.
    • Ultimately, it calls for a harmonious relationship with the divine, nature, and each other, fostering a sense of community and well-being.
