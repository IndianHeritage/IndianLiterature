स पूर्वया निविदा कव्यतायोर इमाः प्रजा जनयन मनूनाम् |विवस्वता चक्षसा dyām अपश च देवाः अग्निं धारयन द्रविणोदाम् ||
sa pūrvayā nividā kavyatāyor imāḥ prajā ajanayan manūnām | vivasvatā cakṣasā dyām apaś ca devā agniṁ dhārayan draviṇodām ||
  • sa pūrvayā nividā kavyatāyor imāḥ prajā ajanayan manūnām
    • sa - This word translates to "he" or "that," indicating a subject or a divine entity.
    • pūrvayā - Meaning "from the past" or "ancient," it suggests a connection to traditions or ancient wisdom.
    • nividā - This can be interpreted as "knowledge" or "insight," referring to the understanding that comes from experience.
    • kavyatāyor - This term relates to "poetry" or "creative expression," indicating the importance of art and literature in understanding life.
    • imāḥ prajā - Translates to "these beings" or "creatures," referring to all living entities.
    • ajanayan - Meaning "to give birth" or "to create," it signifies the act of creation or generation.
    • manūnām - Refers to "of mankind" or "of humans," emphasizing the human aspect of creation.
  • vivasvatā cakṣasā dyām apaś ca devā agniṁ dhārayan draviṇodām
    • vivasvatā - This word means "of the sun" or "radiant," symbolizing light, knowledge, and enlightenment.
    • cakṣasā - Translates to "with vision" or "with sight," indicating the importance of perception and awareness.
    • dyām - Refers to "the sky" or "heaven," representing the vastness of existence and the divine.
    • apaś - Meaning "to see" or "to perceive," it emphasizes the act of observation and understanding.
    • ca - Simply means "and," connecting the ideas presented.
    • devā - Refers to "gods" or "divine beings," indicating the presence of higher powers in creation.
    • agniṁ - This means "fire," which is often a symbol of transformation, energy, and purification.
    • dhārayan - Meaning "holding" or "sustaining," it suggests the act of maintaining or nurturing.
    • draviṇodām - Refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating the abundance that comes from creation and sustenance.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, the interplay between divine forces, and the importance of knowledge and perception in human existence. It highlights how ancient wisdom and creative expression contribute to the birth of life and the sustenance of prosperity. In real life, this can be seen in various contexts, such as:

  • The role of education and knowledge in shaping individuals and societies.
  • The importance of creativity in arts and literature as a means of understanding and expressing human experiences.
  • The recognition of natural elements, like fire, as vital forces that transform and sustain life.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine forces that guide creation and existence, encouraging us to seek knowledge, embrace creativity, and appreciate the abundance of life.
