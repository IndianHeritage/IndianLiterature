वयं शूरैर्भिरस्तृभिरिन्द्र त्वया युजा वयम् |सासह्याम पृतन्यतः ||
vayaṁ śūrebhir astṛbhir indra tvayā yujā vayam | sāsahyāma pṛtanyataḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vayaṁ: We
    • śūrebhir: With the strong or powerful
    • astṛbhir: With the weapons or armaments
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • tvayā: By you
    • yujā: United or connected
    • sāsahyāma: We shall be able
    • pṛtanyataḥ: To overcome or to conquer
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra expresses a plea for strength and unity. It invokes Indra, the god of thunder and rain, asking for his support in overcoming challenges. The mantra emphasizes the importance of being united with powerful forces and having the right tools to face adversities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Teamwork in Sports: Just as a sports team relies on the strength and skills of each member to win a game, this mantra highlights the importance of unity and collective strength in achieving goals.
    • Community Support: In times of crisis, communities come together to support one another, much like the invocation of Indra for strength. For instance, during natural disasters, people unite to help those affected, showcasing the power of collective action.
    • Workplace Collaboration: In a professional setting, teams that collaborate effectively can overcome challenges more efficiently. This mantra can be seen as a reminder of the strength found in unity and shared resources.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and strength in the face of challenges. It serves as a reminder that by coming together and leveraging our collective abilities, we can overcome obstacles. In real life, whether in sports, community efforts, or workplace dynamics, the principles of collaboration and support are essential for success. This mantra encourages individuals to seek strength in unity and to recognize the power of working together towards a common goal.
