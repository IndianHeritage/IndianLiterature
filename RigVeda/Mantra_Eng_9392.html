अव द्वके अव त्रिकां दिवश्चरण्ति भेषजाः |क्षमा चरिष्ण्व एककम् भारताम् अप यद् रपो द्यौः पृथिवी क्षमा रपो मोषु ते किं चनामतम् ||
ava dvake ava trikā divaś caranti bheṣajā | kṣamā cariṣṇv ekakam bharatām apa yad rapo dyauḥ pṛthivi kṣamā rapo mo ṣu te kiṁ canāmamat ||
  • Mantra: "ava dvake ava trikā divaś caranti bheṣajā |
  • Meaning: This line can be interpreted as a call for healing and protection. The term "ava dvake" suggests a plea for safety and refuge, while "trikā" refers to the three realms: heaven, earth, and the atmosphere. "Divaś caranti bheṣajā" indicates that the divine beings or healers traverse these realms, bringing health and well-being.
  • Real-life Example: In modern life, this can be likened to seeking medical help or spiritual guidance during times of distress. For instance, when someone is ill, they may turn to doctors (the healers) who work tirelessly across various fields (trikā) to restore health.
  • Mantra: "kṣamā cariṣṇv ekakam bharatām apa yad rapo dyauḥ pṛthivi kṣamā rapo mo ṣu te kiṁ canāmamat ||"
  • Meaning: This part emphasizes the importance of forgiveness and unity. "Kṣamā" means forgiveness, while "cariṣṇv ekakam" suggests coming together as one. The phrase "bharatām apa" can be interpreted as a call to the people of Bharat (India) to unite. "Rapo dyauḥ pṛthivi" refers to the waters of heaven and earth, symbolizing the interconnectedness of all life. "Kṣamā rapo mo ṣu te kiṁ canāmamat" implies that through forgiveness and unity, we can overcome obstacles and achieve harmony.
  • Real-life Example: This can be seen in community efforts where individuals come together to support each other during crises, such as natural disasters. For example, during floods, communities unite to help those affected, showcasing the power of forgiveness and collaboration.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for healing, unity, and forgiveness. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to seek help, support one another, and work together towards common goals. By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can foster a sense of belonging and resilience in our lives, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
