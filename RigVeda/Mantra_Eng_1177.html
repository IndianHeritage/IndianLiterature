यावद इदं भुवनं विश्वम् अस्त्य उर्व्यचā वरिमता गभीरम् |तावाम् अयं पातवे सोमो अस्त्व अरम् इन्द्राग्नी मनसे युवभ्यां ||
yāvad idam bhuvanaṁ viśvam asty uruvyacā varimatā gabhīram | tāvām̐ ayam pātave somo astv aram indrāgnī manase yuvabhyām ||
  • yāvad idam bhuvanaṁ viśvam asty: This phrase translates to "As long as this universe exists." It emphasizes the eternal nature of the universe and the interconnectedness of all beings within it.
  • uruvyacā varimatā gabhīram: This can be interpreted as "with vastness and depth." It suggests that the universe is not only expansive but also profound, filled with mysteries and wisdom that are yet to be discovered.
  • tāvām̐ ayam pātave somo astv: This part means "may this soma (the divine nectar) be powerful." Soma is often associated with immortality and divine inspiration in Vedic literature, symbolizing the essence of life and vitality.
  • aram indrāgnī manase yuvabhyām: This translates to "may Indra and Agni, the deities of strength and fire, be with us." Indra represents power and rain, while Agni symbolizes fire and transformation. Together, they signify the forces that sustain life and energy in the universe.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of existence, the vastness of the universe, and the divine forces that govern life. It serves as a reminder of our connection to the cosmos and the importance of seeking wisdom and strength in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of reflection, meditation, or during rituals that honor the natural world and the divine energies that support us. It encourages individuals to recognize their place in the universe and to draw upon the strength of the elements and deities for guidance and inspiration.
