वाचस पतिं विश्वकर्माणम् ऊतये मनोजुवं वाजे अद्याहुवेमा |स नो विश्वानि हवनानि जोषद विश्वशम्भूर अवसे साधुकर्मा ||
vācas patiṁ viśvakarmāṇam ūtaye manojuvaṁ vāje adyā huvema | sa no viśvāni havanāni joṣad viśvaśambhūr avase sādhukarmā ||
  • vācas patiṁ: This phrase translates to "the lord of speech." In a real-life context, this can refer to the power of communication and expression. For example, a skilled orator or a writer who can influence and inspire others through their words embodies this concept.
  • viśvakarmāṇam: This means "the all-doer" or "the universal creator." In practical terms, this can be seen in individuals who are versatile and capable of handling multiple tasks or projects, such as a project manager who coordinates various teams to achieve a common goal.
  • ūtaye: This word signifies "for the purpose of nourishment" or "for sustenance." In everyday life, this can relate to the importance of providing for oneself and others, such as a parent working hard to ensure their family has food and shelter.
  • manojuvaṁ: This translates to "swift of mind." It emphasizes the value of quick thinking and adaptability. For instance, a student who can rapidly grasp complex concepts during an exam demonstrates this quality.
  • vāje: This means "in the context of strength or power." In real life, this can be seen in athletes who train rigorously to build their physical strength and endurance, showcasing the importance of power in achieving success.
  • adyā huvema: This phrase means "today we invoke." It signifies the act of calling upon divine forces or energies. In a modern context, this can be likened to setting intentions or goals at the beginning of a new project or venture.
  • sa no viśvāni havanāni: This translates to "may all our offerings be accepted." It reflects the desire for recognition and acceptance of one's efforts. In real life, this can be seen in the workplace, where employees seek acknowledgment for their hard work and contributions.
  • joṣad: This means "may it be pleasing." It emphasizes the importance of harmony and satisfaction in our endeavors. For example, a well-executed event that brings joy to its participants embodies this idea.
  • viśvaśambhūr: This translates to "the universal benefactor." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of contributing positively to society. In real life, this can be seen in community service initiatives where individuals come together to help those in need.
  • avase sādhukarmā: This means "may we accomplish good deeds." It highlights the significance of ethical actions and integrity. In practical terms, this can be observed in businesses that prioritize corporate social responsibility, ensuring their operations benefit society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of communication, creativity, nourishment, quick thinking, strength, intention, acceptance, harmony, universal benefaction, and ethical actions. Its significance lies in the reminder to harness these qualities in our daily lives, whether in personal relationships, professional endeavors, or community involvement. By embodying the principles expressed in this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and productive society, fostering growth and well-being for all.
