पीपाय स श्रवसा मार्त्येषु यो अग्नये ददाश विप्र उक्थैः |चित्राभिस तं ऊतिभिः चित्रशोचिर व्रजस्य साता गोमतो दधाति ||
pīpāya sa śravasā martyeṣu yo agnaye dadāśa vipra ukthaiḥ | citrābhis tam ūtibhiś citraśocir vrajasya sātā gomato dadhāti ||
  • pīpāya: This term can be understood as "to nourish" or "to sustain." It signifies the act of providing sustenance or support.
  • sa: This is a pronoun meaning "he" or "that," referring to a divine entity or force.
  • śravasā: This word translates to "with fame" or "with glory," indicating the importance of reputation and recognition.
  • martyeṣu: This means "among mortals" or "in the human realm," highlighting the connection between the divine and human experiences.
  • yo: This is a relative pronoun meaning "who," referring to the divine force being addressed.
  • agnaye: This term refers to "Agni," the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • dadāśa: This means "has given" or "has bestowed," indicating a gift or offering.
  • vipra: This term refers to a "sage" or "wise person," often associated with knowledge and spiritual insight.
  • ukthaiḥ: This translates to "with hymns" or "with praises," indicating the act of worship or reverence through vocal expression.
  • citrābhis: This means "with various" or "with colorful," suggesting diversity and richness in offerings or experiences.
  • tam: This means "that" or "him," referring again to the divine force or Agni.
  • ūtibhiś: This translates to "with powers" or "with strengths," indicating the capabilities or attributes of the divine.
  • citraśocir: This means "brilliantly shining" or "radiantly bright," symbolizing enlightenment and clarity.
  • vrajasya: This refers to "of the herd" or "of the group," often associated with community and collective well-being.
  • sātā: This means "truth" or "reality," emphasizing the importance of authenticity and integrity.
  • gomato: This translates to "of the cow," symbolizing nourishment, wealth, and prosperity in Vedic culture.
  • dadhāti: This means "gives" or "provides," indicating the act of offering or bestowing blessings.

  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine force, Agni, who nourishes and sustains mortals with fame and glory.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wise individuals (vipra) who, through their hymns and praises, connect the human realm with the divine.
    • The mantra highlights the diversity of offerings and the radiant qualities of the divine, suggesting that through collective efforts and truth, one can achieve prosperity and well-being.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, leaders who embody wisdom and integrity can uplift the entire group, much like the divine Agni in the mantra.
    • In personal life, individuals can seek to nourish their relationships and endeavors with truth and diverse experiences, leading to a fulfilling existence.
    • In a professional context, organizations that value collective contributions and recognize the efforts of their members often achieve greater success and harmony.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness between the divine and human realms, emphasizing the importance of wisdom, community, and authenticity.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement to cultivate a life filled with diverse experiences, truth, and collective support, leading to prosperity and well-being.
    • In modern life, the principles derived from this mantra can be applied in various contexts, including personal relationships, community building, and professional environments, fostering a holistic approach to living.
