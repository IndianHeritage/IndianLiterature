श्रिये सुदृशीः उपरस्य याः स्वर विरोचमानः ककुभां अचोदते |सुगोपाऽसि न दभाय सुक्रतो परो मायाभिर ऋत आस नामा ते ||
śriye sudṛśīr uparasya yāḥ svar virocamānaḥ kakubhām acodate | sugopā asi na dabhāya sukrato paro māyābhir ṛta āsa nāma te ||
  • śriye sudṛśīr: This phrase can be translated as "for the sake of prosperity and good vision." Here, "śriye" refers to prosperity or wealth, while "sudṛśīr" implies having a good or clear vision. In real life, this can be related to the importance of having a clear vision or goal in life to achieve prosperity.
  • uparasya yāḥ svar: This translates to "those who are above, shining like the sky." The term "uparasya" indicates something that is elevated or above, and "svar" refers to light or the sky. This can be likened to individuals who rise above challenges and shine in their endeavors, much like successful leaders or innovators who inspire others.
  • virocamānaḥ kakubhām acodate: This means "the shining ones who inspire the directions." "Virocamānaḥ" refers to those who shine or illuminate, while "kakubhām" relates to the directions or realms. This can be seen in real life through mentors or guiding figures who illuminate the path for others, helping them navigate through life's complexities.
  • sugopā asi na dabhāya: This translates to "you are the good protector, not to be harmed." "Sugopā" means a good protector, and "na dabhāya" implies invulnerability or safety. In practical terms, this can refer to the role of guardians or protectors in society, such as parents, teachers, or law enforcement, who ensure the safety and well-being of others.
  • sukrato paro māyābhir: This means "by the good deeds, beyond illusions." "Sukrato" refers to good actions or deeds, while "māyābhir" indicates the influence of illusions or distractions. This can be applied in real life by emphasizing the importance of integrity and ethical behavior, which can help individuals rise above the distractions and challenges of life.
  • ṛta āsa nāma te: This translates to "truth is your name." "ṛta" signifies truth or righteousness, and "nāma te" means "your name." This highlights the significance of truthfulness and integrity in one's character. In everyday life, this can be seen in the value placed on honesty in personal relationships and professional settings.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of clarity of vision, guidance, protection, good deeds, and truthfulness in achieving prosperity and navigating life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership, and community service. By embodying these values, individuals can contribute positively to society and inspire others to do the same, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
