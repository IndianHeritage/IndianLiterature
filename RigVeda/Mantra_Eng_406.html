त्रिर अश्विनाऽ सिंधुभिः सप्तमातृभिस् त्रय आहावास त्रीधā हविष् कृतम् |तिस्रः पृथिवीर उपरि प्रवा दिवो नाकं रक्षेते द्युभिर अक्तुभिर हितम् ||
trir aśvinā sindhubhiḥ saptamātṛbhis traya āhāvās tredhā haviṣ kṛtam | tisraḥ pṛthivīr upari pravā divo nākaṁ rakṣethe dyubhir aktubhir hitam ||
  • Mantra: "trir aśvinā sindhubhiḥ saptamātṛbhis traya āhāvās tredhā haviṣ kṛtam | tisraḥ pṛthivīr upari pravā divo nākaṁ rakṣethe dyubhir aktubhir hitam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • trir: three times
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin deities associated with health and medicine)
    • sindhubhiḥ: with the rivers
    • saptamātṛbhis: with the seven mothers (representing various aspects of nature)
    • traya: three
    • āhāvās: offerings or invocations
    • tredhā: in three ways
    • haviṣ: offerings
    • kṛtam: made or performed
    • tisraḥ: three
    • pṛthivīr: of the earth
    • upari: above
    • pravā: flowing
    • divo: of the sky
    • nākaṁ: the heavens
    • rakṣethe: may protect
    • dyubhir: from the heavens
    • aktubhir: from the stars
    • hitam: beneficial or auspicious
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the Ashvins, who are revered as divine physicians and bringers of health and well-being.
    • The mention of "sindhubhiḥ" (with the rivers) signifies the importance of water and rivers in sustaining life, much like how rivers nourish the land and provide for communities.
    • The "saptamātṛbhis" (with the seven mothers) symbolizes the nurturing aspects of nature, akin to how mothers provide care and sustenance to their children.
    • The phrase "traya āhāvās" (three offerings) suggests a holistic approach to life, where one should offer gratitude and respect to the elements of nature.
    • "Tisraḥ pṛthivīr" (three of the earth) indicates the connection between the earth and the sky, emphasizing the balance between terrestrial and celestial forces.
    • The invocation for protection from "dyubhir aktubhir" (from the heavens and stars) reflects a desire for divine safeguarding in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, the importance of clean water and rivers can be seen in environmental movements advocating for the protection of water bodies, which are essential for life.
    • The nurturing aspect of nature can be paralleled with community support systems, where individuals come together to care for one another, much like a family.
    • Incorporating gratitude practices in daily life, such as acknowledging the resources we have, can lead to a more fulfilling and balanced existence.
    • Environmental conservation efforts reflect the mantra's call for respect towards nature, ensuring that future generations can thrive.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, nature, and the divine, urging us to recognize and honor these relationships.
    • Its significance lies in promoting a holistic approach to living, where we are mindful of our environment and the support systems around us.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to healthier communities, sustainable practices, and a deeper appreciation for the natural world.
