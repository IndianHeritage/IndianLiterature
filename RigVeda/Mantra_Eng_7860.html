प्र कविर देववीतये 'व्यो वारेभिर अर्षति |साह्वान विश्वा अभि स्पृधः ||
pra kavir devavītaye 'vyo vārebhir arṣati | sāhvān viśvā abhi spṛdhaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: before, in front of
    • kavir: the wise one, the poet, the seer
    • devavītaye: for the divine purpose, for the gods
    • 'vyo: with, by means of
    • vārebhir: the best, the most excellent
    • arṣati: to rise, to ascend
    • sāhvān: with strength, with power
    • viśvā: all, the universe
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • spṛdhaḥ: to desire, to aspire
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the wise seer who, with divine purpose, rises with the best of intentions.
    • It emphasizes the strength and aspiration towards the universal good.
    • The invocation of the divine suggests a connection between human efforts and higher powers.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, a wise leader (kavir) aims to uplift their community (devavītaye) by making decisions that benefit everyone (viśvā).
    • In personal development, individuals aspire (spṛdhaḥ) to rise above challenges (arṣati) by harnessing their inner strength (sāhvān).
    • In creative endeavors, artists and poets channel their wisdom (kavir) to create works that resonate with universal themes (viśvā).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of wisdom and aspiration in achieving divine and universal goals.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as leadership, personal growth, and creative expression.
    • By embodying the qualities of the wise seer, individuals can contribute positively to society and strive for a higher purpose.
