त्यं चिद अश्वं न वाजिनम् अरेणवो यम् अतनत | दृढं granthiं न वि ष्यतम अत्रिं यविष्टम् आ रजः ||
tyaṁ cid aśvaṁ na vājinam areṇavo yam atnata | dṛḻhaṁ granthiṁ na vi ṣyatam atriṁ yaviṣṭham ā rajaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tyaṁ: that
    • cid: indeed
    • aśvaṁ: horse
    • na: not
    • vājinam: charioteer
    • areṇavo: of the sand
    • yam: whom
    • atnata: the one who is alive
    • dṛḻhaṁ: firm
    • granthiṁ: knot
    • na: not
    • viṣyatam: to be loosened
    • atriṁ: the seer
    • yaviṣṭham: the one who is well-versed
    • ā: from
    • rajaḥ: dust or matter
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the essence of life and the importance of being grounded.
    • The "horse" symbolizes strength and vitality, while the "charioteer" represents guidance and control.
    • The phrase "not loosened" suggests the importance of maintaining focus and stability in life.
    • The "seer" or "atri" indicates wisdom and insight, emphasizing the need for knowledge in navigating life's challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, the "horse" can represent one's career ambitions, while the "charioteer" symbolizes a mentor or leader guiding you.
    • In personal relationships, maintaining a "firm knot" can refer to the importance of trust and commitment.
    • In education, the "seer" represents teachers or guides who help students navigate their learning journey.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of strength, guidance, and wisdom in our lives.
    • It teaches us to remain focused and grounded, ensuring that we do not lose sight of our goals.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying these principles can lead to personal growth, successful relationships, and professional achievements.
