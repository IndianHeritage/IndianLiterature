प्र सुवानो अक्षाः सहस्रधारस तिरः पवित्रं वि वारम अव्यम् ||
pra suvāno akṣāḥ sahasradhāras tiraḥ pavitraṁ vi vāram avyam ||
  • pra suvāno akṣāḥ: This phrase can be translated to "the well-formed, auspicious eyes." In a real-life context, this can refer to the ability to perceive the world positively and with clarity. For example, someone who approaches challenges with optimism and a clear vision can be seen as having "auspicious eyes."
  • sahasradhāras: This term means "thousands of streams" or "many sources." It symbolizes abundance and the flow of resources or knowledge. In practical terms, this can relate to the various opportunities and pathways available to individuals in their careers or personal lives. For instance, a person may have multiple job offers or avenues for growth, representing the "thousands of streams."
  • tiraḥ pavitraṁ: This translates to "pure beyond the veil." It suggests a state of purity and clarity that transcends superficial appearances. In everyday life, this can be seen in the pursuit of truth and integrity. For example, a person who acts with honesty and maintains their values, even in difficult situations, embodies this purity.
  • vi vāram avyam: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we not be hindered." It expresses a desire for freedom from obstacles and challenges. In real life, this can be applied to anyone striving to achieve their goals, whether in education, career, or personal development. For instance, a student seeking to excel in their studies may pray for the removal of distractions and difficulties.

  • Conclusion: The mantra "pra suvāno akṣāḥ sahasradhāras tiraḥ pavitraṁ vi vāram avyam" emphasizes the importance of having a positive outlook, recognizing the abundance of opportunities, maintaining purity of intention, and seeking freedom from obstacles. In real life, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional journeys, encouraging them to approach challenges with optimism, embrace the resources available to them, uphold their values, and strive for success without hindrance. This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of perspective and intention in shaping one's life experiences.
