आ दधिक्राह् शवसा पञ्च कृष्टिः सूर्य इव ज्योतिषापस ततान | सहस्रसाह् शतसां वाज्य अर्वा पृणक्तु मध्वा सम इमा वचांसि ||
ā dadhikrāḥ śavasā pañca kṛṣṭīḥ sūrya iva jyotiṣāpas tatāna | sahasrasāḥ śatasā vājy arvā pṛṇaktu madhvā sam imā vacāṁsi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā dadhikrāḥ: "May they (the divine forces) bestow" - a call for blessings and support.
    • śavasā: "with strength" - referring to the power and vitality of life.
    • pañca: "five" - often symbolizes completeness or the five elements in Vedic philosophy.
    • kṛṣṭīḥ: "the fields" - representing the physical world and our efforts in it.
    • sūrya iva: "like the sun" - a metaphor for brightness, energy, and life-giving force.
    • jyotiṣā: "of lights" - referring to knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment.
    • apas tatāna: "they have spread" - indicating the dissemination of blessings and knowledge.
    • sahasrasāḥ: "thousands" - symbolizing abundance and prosperity.
    • śatasā: "hundreds" - reinforcing the idea of plenty and richness.
    • vājy: "of the horses" - often symbolizes strength, speed, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • arvā: "the swift" - indicating agility and quickness in achieving goals.
    • pṛṇaktu: "may they fill" - a request for fulfillment and satisfaction.
    • madhvā: "with sweetness" - representing joy, happiness, and positive experiences.
    • sam imā: "these" - referring to the blessings and words being invoked.
    • vacāṁsi: "words" - emphasizing the power of speech and communication.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra invokes divine blessings for strength, prosperity, and enlightenment.
    • It emphasizes the importance of communication and the power of words in manifesting our desires.
    • The imagery of the sun and light symbolizes the pursuit of knowledge and the illumination of our paths.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be recited to seek guidance and strength in challenging situations, such as during exams or important decisions.
    • It can be used in rituals or prayers to invoke abundance and prosperity in personal and professional endeavors.
    • The emphasis on communication can remind us to use our words wisely, fostering positive relationships and understanding in our interactions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, knowledge, and communication in achieving our goals.
    • By invoking these divine qualities, we can enhance our lives and the lives of those around us, creating a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
