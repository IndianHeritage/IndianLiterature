इह त्या सदहमाद्याः युजानः सोमपीतये |हरि इन्द्र प्रतद्वसू अभि स्वर ||
iha tyā sadhamādyā yujānaḥ somapītaye | harī indra pratadvasū abhi svara ||
  • iha - Here, in this world.
  • tyā - By that, through that.
  • sadhamādyā - The best of the best, the most excellent.
  • yujānaḥ - Those who are united, connected, or joined together.
  • somapītaye - For the sake of drinking Soma, a sacred drink associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
  • harī - The divine, often referring to the god Indra or the divine forces.
  • indra - The king of gods, a deity associated with thunder, rain, and war.
  • pratadvasū - The one who brings forth wealth, prosperity, and abundance.
  • abhi - Towards, in the direction of.
  • svara - Sound, voice, or resonance, often referring to the divine sound or the essence of creation.

This mantra can be interpreted as a call for unity and excellence in the pursuit of divine inspiration and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of coming together in harmony for a higher purpose, which is symbolized by the drinking of Soma, a metaphor for spiritual enlightenment and the quest for immortality.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Community Building: Just as the mantra speaks of unity, it can inspire communities to come together for common goals, such as environmental conservation or social justice.
  • Teamwork in Organizations: In a workplace, the essence of this mantra can encourage collaboration among team members to achieve excellence in projects, fostering a culture of support and shared success.
  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can use this mantra in meditation or prayer, focusing on the pursuit of higher knowledge and connection with the divine, enhancing their spiritual journey.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and the pursuit of excellence for a higher purpose. It serves as a reminder that when individuals come together with a shared vision, they can achieve great things, whether in personal growth, community development, or organizational success. The mantra encourages us to seek divine inspiration and prosperity through collective efforts, making it relevant in various aspects of life today.
