या उदृचिंद्र देवगोपाः सखायस ते शिवतमाऽसाम् |त्वां स्तोषाम तवया सुवीराः द्राघीय आयुः प्रतरं दधानाः ||
ya udṛcīndra devagopāḥ sakhāyas te śivatamā asāma | tvāṁ stoṣāma tvayā suvīrā drāghīya āyuḥ prataraṁ dadhānāḥ ||
  • ya udṛcīndra devagopāḥ: This phrase can be translated as "O Indra, the protector of the gods." Indra is often regarded as the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and power. In real life, this can be likened to a leader or protector in a community who safeguards the interests of the people.
  • sakhāyas te śivatamā asāma: This means "your friends are the most auspicious." It emphasizes the importance of companionship and support from those around us. In everyday life, this can be seen in the value of friendships and relationships that uplift and inspire us to be better individuals.
  • tvāṁ stoṣāma: This translates to "we praise you." It signifies the act of acknowledging and honoring the divine or the forces that guide us. In a practical sense, this can be related to gratitude practices, where individuals express appreciation for the positive aspects of their lives.
  • tvayā suvīrā drāghīya āyuḥ prataraṁ dadhānāḥ: This phrase means "with your strength, may we have long life." It reflects a desire for longevity and vitality, which is a common aspiration in human life. This can be connected to health and wellness practices, where individuals seek to maintain their physical and mental well-being through various means such as diet, exercise, and mindfulness.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of protection, friendship, gratitude, and the pursuit of longevity. Its significance lies in the recognition of the interconnectedness of these elements in our lives. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek strength from their community, appreciate their relationships, and strive for a healthy and fulfilling life. By embodying these principles, one can cultivate a harmonious existence that aligns with the values expressed in the mantra.
