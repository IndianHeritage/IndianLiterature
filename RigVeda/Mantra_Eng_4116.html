अभ्राजी शर्धो मरुतो यद अर्णसम मोषथा वृक्षं कपनेव वेधसः |अध स्मा नो अरमातिं सजोषसश चक्षुर इव यन्तम अनु नेषथा सुगम ||
abhrāji śardho maruto yad arṇasam moṣathā vṛkṣaṁ kapaneva vedhasaḥ | adha smā no aramatiṁ sajoṣasaś cakṣur iva yantam anu neṣathā sugam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhrāji: O clouds
    • śardho: in the autumn
    • maruto: O winds
    • yad: that
    • arṇasam: which is in the ocean
    • moṣathā: you may release
    • vṛkṣaṁ: the tree
    • kapaneva: like a bird
    • vedhasaḥ: of the wise
    • adha: below
    • smā: may we
    • no: our
    • aramatiṁ: prosperity
    • sajoṣasaś: with joy
    • cakṣur: like the eye
    • iva: as if
    • yantam: moving
    • anu: after
    • neṣathā: you lead
    • sugam: to good fortune
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the clouds and winds, asking them to release the blessings of prosperity and abundance.
    • The imagery of the tree and the bird symbolizes growth and freedom, suggesting that just as a bird takes flight, so too can prosperity be attained.
    • The reference to the ocean indicates a vast source of potential and richness, from which blessings can be drawn.
    • The phrase "like the eye" suggests clarity and vision, emphasizing the importance of perception in recognizing and seizing opportunities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain (symbolized by clouds) to ensure a good harvest. This mantra can be seen as a call for the natural elements to cooperate in providing the necessary conditions for growth.
    • In personal development, individuals may seek clarity and direction in their lives, akin to the "eye" that perceives opportunities. This mantra encourages one to remain open to the guidance of external forces (like the winds) that can lead to success.
    • In community settings, this mantra can be applied to collective efforts where the community seeks to uplift each other, much like the tree providing shelter and sustenance to various forms of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for harmony between nature and human endeavors. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all elements in the pursuit of prosperity.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as agriculture, personal growth, and community development, reminding us to seek balance and cooperation with the natural world.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that by aligning ourselves with the forces of nature and maintaining a clear vision, we can navigate towards a path of abundance and fulfillment.
