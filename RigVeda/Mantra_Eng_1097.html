स सव्येन यमति व्राधतश्चित स दक्षिणे संगृभीताकृतानि |स कीरिणा चित सनीत धनानि मरुत्वान नो भवतु इन्द्र ऊती ||
sa savyena yamati vrādhataś cit sa dakṣiṇe saṁgṛbhītā kṛtāni | sa kīriṇā cit sanitā dhanāni marutvān no bhavatv indra ūtī ||
  • sa savyena yamati vrādhataś cit: This phrase can be interpreted as "He who is skilled in the art of governance and leadership." In real life, this can relate to effective leaders in various fields, such as politics, business, or community organizations, who guide their teams with wisdom and foresight.
  • sa dakṣiṇe saṁgṛbhītā kṛtāni: This translates to "He who gathers and organizes resources wisely." This can be seen in successful entrepreneurs who manage their resources efficiently to create sustainable businesses, or in environmentalists who work to conserve natural resources for future generations.
  • sa kīriṇā cit sanitā dhanāni: This means "He who is known for his wealth and prosperity." This can be exemplified by philanthropists who use their wealth to support charitable causes, thereby enhancing their reputation and contributing positively to society.
  • marutvān no bhavatv indra ūtī: This phrase can be understood as "May the powerful Indra bless us with strength and abundance." In a modern context, this can relate to the collective prayers or aspirations of communities seeking strength in unity and abundance in resources, such as during times of crisis or natural disasters.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the qualities of effective leadership, resource management, prosperity, and the importance of divine blessings for strength and abundance. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership roles, community building, and personal development. By embodying these qualities, individuals and communities can strive for success and harmony in their endeavors, ultimately leading to a more prosperous and united society.
