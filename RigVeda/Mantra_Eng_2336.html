अर्हन् बिभर्षि सायकानि धन्वार्हन् निष्कं यजतं विश्वरूपम् |अर्हन्न इदं दयसे विश्वम् अभ्वं न वा ओजीयो रुद्र त्वद अस्ति ||
arhan bibharṣi sāyakāni dhanvārhan niṣkaṁ yajataṁ viśvarūpam | arhann idaṁ dayase viśvam abhvaṁ na vā ojīyo rudra tvad asti ||
  • arhan bibharṣi: This phrase can be interpreted as "You who are worthy of reverence." It acknowledges the divine qualities of the deity being addressed, suggesting that they possess attributes that command respect and admiration.
  • sāyakāni dhanvārhan: This translates to "the arrows of the bow." Here, the bow symbolizes strength and protection, while the arrows represent the power to overcome obstacles. In real life, this can be likened to the tools and skills we possess to face challenges.
  • niṣkaṁ yajataṁ viśvarūpam: This means "offering the unblemished (or pure) sacrifice to the universal form." It emphasizes the importance of purity in our actions and intentions. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way we approach our duties and responsibilities, ensuring that our efforts are sincere and selfless.
  • arhann idaṁ dayase: This phrase translates to "You are worthy of this, O compassionate one." It highlights the importance of compassion and kindness in our interactions with others. For example, in a workplace, showing empathy towards colleagues can foster a positive environment.
  • viśvam abhvaṁ: This means "the whole universe." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of recognizing our place within the larger cosmos. In real life, this can be reflected in our environmental responsibilities and the need to care for our planet.
  • na vā ojīyo rudra tvad asti: This translates to "there is no greater strength than you, O Rudra." It acknowledges the supreme power of the divine. In practical terms, this can be understood as recognizing the sources of strength in our lives, whether they be spiritual, familial, or communal.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes reverence, strength, purity, compassion, interconnectedness, and the recognition of divine power. Its significance lies in guiding individuals towards a life of integrity, empathy, and responsibility. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as personal relationships, professional environments, and our interactions with the world around us. By embodying these values, we contribute to a more harmonious and compassionate society.
