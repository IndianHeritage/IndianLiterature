यः शक्रो मृक्षो अश्व्यो यो वा कीजो हिरण्ययः |स उर्वस्य रेजयत्य अपावृतिम इन्द्रो गव्यस्य वृत्रहा ||
yaḥ śakro mṛkṣo aśvyo yo vā kījo hiraṇyayaḥ | sa ūrvasya rejayaty apāvṛtim indro gavyasya vṛtrahā ||
  • yaḥ - "who" or "that which"
  • śakro - "the powerful" or "the strong one\ often referring to Indra, the king of gods
  • mṛkṣo - "of the deer" or "the one who hunts"
  • aśvyo - "of the horses" or "the one who is swift"
  • yo - "who" or "that which"
  • vā - "or"
  • kījo - "the one who is powerful" or "the one who creates"
  • hiraṇyayaḥ - "of gold" or "the one who brings wealth"
  • sa - "he" or "that one"
  • ūrvasya - "of the earth" or "the one who is grounded"
  • rejayaty - "shines" or "radiates"
  • apāvṛtim - "without obstruction" or "unhindered"
  • indro - "Indra\ the deity associated with rain, storms, and strength
  • gavyasya - "of the cows" or "the one who nourishes"
  • vṛtrahā - "the slayer of Vritra\ a demon representing drought and obstacles

This mantra can be interpreted as a call to the powerful deity Indra, who is associated with strength, wealth, and the ability to overcome obstacles. It highlights the qualities of Indra as a protector and provider, emphasizing his role in ensuring prosperity and abundance.


In real life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of strength and resilience in facing challenges. For example, in a business context, a leader may embody the qualities of Indra by being decisive and strong in the face of competition, ensuring the growth and success of their organization. Similarly, in personal life, individuals may draw upon their inner strength to overcome personal struggles, much like Indra overcoming the demon Vritra to bring rain and fertility to the land.


In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of strength, resilience, and the ability to overcome obstacles in both personal and communal contexts. It serves as a reminder that, like Indra, we can harness our inner power to create abundance and prosperity in our lives and the lives of those around us.
