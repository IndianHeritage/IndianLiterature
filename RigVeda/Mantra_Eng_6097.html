नृवद दसरā मनोयुजā रथेन पृथुपाजसā | सचेतः अश्विनोषसम ||
nṛvad dasrā manoyujā rathena pṛthupājasā | sacethe aśvinoṣasam ||
  • nṛvad: This term can be interpreted as "human" or "mankind." It signifies the collective human experience and the importance of human actions and intentions.
  • dasrā: This word translates to "the one who is swift" or "quick." It emphasizes the need for speed and efficiency in our endeavors.
  • manoyujā: This means "with the mind directed" or "focused thought." It highlights the significance of mental clarity and intention in achieving our goals.
  • rathena: This translates to "by the chariot." The chariot symbolizes progress, movement, and the journey of life. It represents the means by which we navigate through challenges.
  • pṛthupājasā: This term means "with great offerings" or "with abundant worship." It signifies the importance of gratitude and recognition of the forces that support us.
  • sacethe: This can be interpreted as "if one is able" or "if one can." It suggests the potential for achievement and the importance of self-belief.
  • aśvinoṣasam: This term refers to "the two Ashvins," who are divine twin horsemen in Vedic mythology, symbolizing health, vitality, and the duality of life.

The mantra can be understood as a call for humans to harness their mental focus and swift actions, guided by the journey of life (the chariot), while offering gratitude for the support they receive from the divine forces represented by the Ashvins. It emphasizes the importance of intention, speed, and recognition of the interconnectedness of life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Goals: When pursuing personal ambitions, one should focus their mind (manoyujā) and act swiftly (dasrā) to achieve their objectives, much like a charioteer navigating through a race.
  • Professional Endeavors: In a work environment, being efficient and focused can lead to success. Recognizing the support of colleagues and mentors (pṛthupājasā) can foster a positive work culture.
  • Health and Well-being: The reference to the Ashvins can remind individuals to prioritize their health and vitality, seeking balance in life.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of focused intention, swift action, and gratitude in our lives. It encourages us to recognize the divine support we receive and to navigate our journeys with purpose and clarity. By embodying these principles, we can enhance our personal and professional lives, leading to a more fulfilling existence.
