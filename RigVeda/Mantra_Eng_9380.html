यत् ते पर्वतान् बृहतो मनो जगाम दूरकम् |तत् त आ वर्तयामसिह क्षयाय जीवसे ||
yat te parvatān bṛhato mano jagāma dūrakam | tat ta ā vartayāmasīha kṣayāya jīvase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yat: that which
    • te: of you
    • parvatān: mountains
    • bṛhato: of the great
    • mano: mind
    • jagāma: has gone
    • dūrakam: far away
    • tat: that
    • ta: you
    • ā: indeed
    • vartayāmasi: we are moving
    • ha: indeed
    • kṣayāya: for destruction
    • jīvase: for life
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the connection between the mind and the vastness of nature, represented by the mountains.
    • It suggests that the thoughts and aspirations of an individual can reach far and wide, akin to the heights of great mountains.
    • The phrase "we are moving for destruction for life" indicates a transformative journey where one must confront challenges to achieve growth and vitality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often face significant challenges (the mountains) that seem daunting. However, by focusing their minds and aspirations, they can overcome these obstacles.
    • In environmental conservation, the mantra can be interpreted as a call to protect nature (the mountains) for the sake of life on Earth, emphasizing the importance of sustainable practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the power of the mind and the importance of perseverance in the face of challenges.
    • It encourages individuals to harness their thoughts and aspirations to navigate through life's difficulties, ultimately leading to personal growth and a deeper connection with the world around them.
    • This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, including personal development, environmental awareness, and any situation where overcoming obstacles is essential for progress.
