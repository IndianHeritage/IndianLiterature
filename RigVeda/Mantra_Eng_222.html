तयोरिद्घृतवत्पयो विप्राः रिहन्ति धीतिभिः |गन्धर्वस्य ध्रुवे पदे ||
tayor id ghṛtavat payo viprā rihanti dhītibhiḥ | gandharvasya dhruve pade ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tayor: of those (referring to the divine or celestial beings)
    • id: this
    • ghṛtavat: like ghee (clarified butter), symbolizing richness and nourishment
    • payo: milk, representing purity and sustenance
    • viprā: the wise or learned ones (often referring to sages or priests)
    • rihanti: they enjoy or relish
    • dhītibhiḥ: with wisdom or intellect
    • gandharvasya: of the Gandharva (celestial musicians or beings)
    • dhruve: stable or eternal
    • pade: place or position
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine nourishment that is enjoyed by the wise, akin to the richness of ghee and the purity of milk.
    • The reference to Gandharvas suggests a connection to the celestial realm, where music and harmony prevail.
    • The wise individuals, or viprā, are depicted as those who can appreciate and relish this divine sustenance through their intellect and wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be related to the nourishment we receive from knowledge and wisdom. Just as ghee and milk provide physical sustenance, education and learning provide intellectual nourishment.
    • For instance, a teacher (viprā) who imparts knowledge to students is akin to a source of nourishment, helping them grow intellectually and spiritually.
    • The idea of enjoying wisdom can be seen in practices like meditation or mindfulness, where individuals seek to connect with deeper truths and enjoy the richness of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge as essential forms of nourishment in our lives.
    • It signifies that true enjoyment and fulfillment come from understanding and appreciating the deeper aspects of existence, much like the enjoyment derived from rich and pure foods.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as education, personal growth, and spiritual practices, where the pursuit of knowledge leads to a more enriched and harmonious life.
