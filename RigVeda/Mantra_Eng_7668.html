यस्य त्रिधात्व अवृतं बर्हिस तस्थाव असंदिनम् |आपश्चिन्नि दधापदं ||
yasya tridhātv avṛtam barhis tasthāv asaṁdinam | āpaś cin ni dadhā padam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • tridhātv: threefold nature
    • avṛtam: covered or concealed
    • barhis: sacred grass or the essence of life
    • tasthāv: stood or established
    • asaṁdinam: unshaken or stable
    • āpaś: waters or the flow of life
    • cin: to gather or collect
    • ni: down or within
    • dadhā: to place or establish
    • padam: a place or position
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence that is threefold in nature, suggesting a holistic view of existence that encompasses physical, mental, and spiritual realms.
    • The term "barhis" refers to the sacred grass used in rituals, symbolizing purity and the essence of life. It indicates the importance of grounding oneself in nature and spirituality.
    • The phrase "asaṁdinam" emphasizes stability and resilience, suggesting that true strength lies in being unshaken by external circumstances.
    • The waters mentioned in the mantra symbolize the flow of life and consciousness, indicating that one should seek to gather wisdom and understanding from the depths of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often strive for a balance between their physical, emotional, and spiritual well-being, reflecting the threefold nature mentioned in the mantra.
    • In environmental practices, the reverence for nature and the use of natural elements in rituals can be seen in various cultures, emphasizing the connection between humanity and the earth.
    • In times of crisis, people often draw upon their inner strength and resilience, embodying the idea of being "unshaken" and stable amidst chaos.
    • Seeking knowledge and wisdom from various sources, much like gathering waters, is a common practice in education and personal growth, highlighting the importance of continuous learning.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of stability, balance, and the pursuit of wisdom in life. It serves as a reminder to remain grounded in our values and to seek knowledge from the depths of our experiences.
    • Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, including personal growth, environmental consciousness, and resilience in the face of challenges.
    • By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can cultivate a harmonious existence that honors both the material and spiritual dimensions of life.
