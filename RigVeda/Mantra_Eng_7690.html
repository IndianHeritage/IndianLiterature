आग्ने याहि मरुत्सखाः रुद्रैभिः सोमपीतये |सोभार्या उप सुश्तुतिम् मादयस्व स्वर्णरे ||
āgne yāhi marutsakhā rudrebhiḥ somapītaye | sobharyā upa suṣṭutim mādayasva svarṇare ||
  • Āgne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, light, and transformation.
  • Yāhi: This means "come" or "approach," indicating a call or invitation.
  • Marutsakhā: This term refers to the Maruts, the storm deities, who are often associated with strength and power.
  • Rudrebhiḥ: This refers to Rudra, a fierce form of Shiva, representing destruction and regeneration.
  • Somapītaye: This means "for the sake of drinking soma," where soma is a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight.
  • Sobharyā: This term can be interpreted as "beautiful" or "auspicious," indicating a positive quality or state.
  • Upa: This means "near" or "towards," suggesting closeness or approach.
  • Suṣṭutim: This refers to "good praise" or "excellent commendation," indicating the importance of expressing gratitude and reverence.
  • Mādayasva: This means "may you be delighted" or "may you rejoice," suggesting a state of joy and fulfillment.
  • Svarṇare: This term refers to "golden" or "radiant," symbolizing purity, wealth, and prosperity.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Agni, asking him to come along with the Maruts and Rudra for the purpose of enjoying the divine soma. It emphasizes the importance of beauty, praise, and joy, while also invoking the qualities of strength and transformation.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can recite this mantra during rituals or meditation to invoke positive energies and divine blessings.
  • Celebrations: It can be used during festivals or gatherings to create an atmosphere of joy and reverence.
  • Personal Growth: The themes of transformation and strength can inspire individuals to embrace change and seek personal development.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine presence and blessings in our lives. It encourages us to appreciate beauty, express gratitude, and seek joy, while also recognizing the transformative power of the divine. By incorporating these principles into our daily lives, we can foster a deeper connection with the spiritual realm and enhance our overall well-being.
