दूतं वो विश्ववेदसं हव्यवाहम् अमर्त्यम् | यजिष्टम् ऋञ्जसे गिरा ||
dūtaṁ vo viśvavedasaṁ havyavāham amartyam | yajiṣṭham ṛñjase girā ||
  • Mantra: "dūtaṁ vo viśvavedasaṁ havyavāham amartyam | yajiṣṭham ṛñjase girā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dūtaṁ: Messenger or envoy
    • vo: to you
    • viśvavedasaṁ: all-knowing or omniscient
    • havyavāham: one who carries offerings
    • amartyam: immortal or divine
    • yajiṣṭham: most worthy of worship
    • ṛñjase: to be praised or glorified
    • girā: through speech or words
  • Detailed Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes a divine messenger who is all-knowing and capable of carrying offerings to the gods. In a real-life context, this can be likened to the role of a mediator or a spiritual guide who helps individuals connect with higher powers or truths.
    • The term "havyavāham" suggests the importance of offerings in spiritual practices. Just as one might offer gratitude or seek blessings in daily life, this mantra emphasizes the significance of sincere offerings in establishing a connection with the divine.
    • The phrase "amartyam" highlights the concept of immortality, which can be interpreted as the pursuit of eternal truths or values that transcend the physical realm. For example, acts of kindness and love can be seen as immortal contributions to society.
    • "Yajiṣṭham ṛñjase girā" indicates that the messenger is worthy of worship and praise through words. This can be related to the importance of communication in our lives, where expressing gratitude and reverence can strengthen relationships and foster a sense of community.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of connecting with the divine through sincere offerings and communication. In real life, it encourages individuals to seek guidance from knowledgeable sources and to express gratitude and reverence in their interactions.
    • Its significance lies in the understanding that spiritual practices are not just rituals but are deeply connected to our daily lives, influencing our actions, relationships, and the values we uphold.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a more meaningful existence, fostering connections that are both divine and human.
