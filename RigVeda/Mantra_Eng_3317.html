को अस्य वीरः सदहमादम आप सम आनंश सुमतिभिः को अस्य |कद अस्य चित्रं चिकीते कद ऊती वृधे भुवच छशमानस्य यज्ञोः ||
ko asya vīraḥ sadhamādam āpa sam ānaṁśa sumatibhiḥ ko asya | kad asya citraṁ cikite kad ūtī vṛdhe bhuvac chaśamānasya yajyoḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ko asya vīraḥ sadhamādam āpa sam ānaṁśa sumatibhiḥ ko asya | kad asya citraṁ cikite kad ūtī vṛdhe bhuvac chaśamānasya yajyoḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ko: who
    • asya: of this
    • vīraḥ: hero or brave one
    • sadhamādam: in harmony or together
    • āpa: water or flow
    • sam: together
    • ānaṁśa: portion or share
    • sumatibhiḥ: with good thoughts or intentions
    • kad: when
    • asya: of this
    • citraṁ: wonderful or diverse
    • cikite: has been perceived or understood
    • kad: when
    • ūtī: strength or power
    • vṛdhe: in growth or increase
    • bhuvac: of the earth or worldly
    • chaśamānasya: of the one who is engaged in sacrifice
    • yajyoḥ: of the sacrifices
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses questions about the nature of bravery and unity.
    • It reflects on the importance of collaboration and shared intentions in achieving greatness.
    • The mention of "water" symbolizes the flow of life and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It emphasizes the significance of understanding and perceiving the diverse aspects of life.
    • The reference to "sacrifice" highlights the value of selflessness and contribution to the greater good.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team project, the success often depends on how well team members collaborate and share their ideas, much like the "vīraḥ" working together in harmony.
    • In community service, individuals come together with "sumatibhiḥ" to address social issues, showcasing the power of collective action.
    • In nature, ecosystems thrive when different species coexist and support each other, reflecting the mantra's theme of interconnectedness.
    • In personal growth, understanding diverse perspectives can lead to a more enriched life experience, akin to the "citraṁ" mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of unity, collaboration, and selflessness in our lives.
    • It encourages us to seek understanding and to appreciate the diverse experiences that life offers.
    • In a world that often emphasizes individualism, this mantra calls for a return to collective values and shared goals.
    • Ultimately, it signifies that true strength lies in our ability to work together for a common purpose, enriching both our lives and the lives of others.
