तदिद्ध्यस्य सवनाṁ विवेर्व अपो यथा पुरा मनवे गातुम अश्रेत् |गोर्णसी त्वाष्ट्रे अश्वनिर्णिजि प्रेम अध्वरिषु अध्वराम् अशिश्रयुः ||
tad id dhy asya savanaṁ viver apo yathā purā manave gātum aśret | goarṇasi tvāṣṭre aśvanirṇiji prem adhvareṣv adhvarām̐ aśiśrayuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • id: indeed
    • dhy: meditate
    • asya: of this
    • savanaṁ: offering
    • viver: to be revealed
    • apo: waters
    • yathā: as
    • purā: in the past
    • manave: to Manu (the progenitor of mankind)
    • gātum: to go
    • aśret: to dwell
    • goarṇasi: in the cow
    • tvāṣṭre: O Tvashtar (the divine carpenter)
    • aśvanirṇiji: O horse-born
    • prem: love
    • adhvareṣv: in the sacrifices
    • adhvarām̐: of the rituals
    • aśiśrayuḥ: they have taken refuge
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the importance of meditation and offerings, emphasizing the connection between the divine and the earthly.
    • The reference to "waters" symbolizes purity and the life-giving aspects of nature, suggesting that through meditation and offerings, one can connect with the divine essence that sustains life.
    • The mention of Manu indicates the foundational role of humanity in the cosmic order, highlighting the responsibility of humans to engage in rituals and offerings to maintain harmony.
    • The invocation of Tvashtar and the horse-born signifies the creative and transformative powers of the divine, suggesting that through love and devotion in rituals, one can invoke these powers for personal and communal benefit.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in practices such as meditation, yoga, and mindfulness, where individuals seek to connect with their inner selves and the universe.
    • Rituals and offerings, whether in a religious context or personal practice, can be seen as a way to express gratitude and seek blessings, similar to how people celebrate festivals or perform ceremonies to honor nature and the divine.
    • The emphasis on community and shared rituals can be reflected in how people come together for events like weddings, festivals, or communal prayers, reinforcing social bonds and collective well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of connecting with the divine through meditation, offerings, and rituals, emphasizing the importance of love and community in these practices.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that through our actions and intentions, we can foster a deeper connection with the universe and contribute to the harmony of life.
    • In a world often filled with chaos, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for individuals seeking peace, purpose, and a sense of belonging through spiritual practices.
