मन्त्र: बळ इत्त्था पर्वतानां खिद्रम बिभर्षि पृथिवि | प्र याऽभूमिम प्रवत्वति मह्ना जिनोषि महिनी ||
baḻ itthā parvatānāṁ khidram bibharṣi pṛthivi | pra yā bhūmim pravatvati mahnā jinoṣi mahini ||
  • baḻ itthā parvatānāṁ: This phrase can be translated as "Here, the mountains." It refers to the majestic mountains that are often seen as symbols of strength and stability in various cultures.
  • khidram bibharṣi pṛthivi: This translates to "You bear the burden of the earth." It signifies the responsibility that the earth holds, as well as the nurturing aspect of nature that supports life.
  • pra yā bhūmim pravatvati: This means "As you flow towards the earth." It suggests a movement or a connection to the earth, indicating the importance of grounding and being connected to our environment.
  • mahnā jinoṣi mahini: This can be interpreted as "You conquer the great one." It speaks to the idea of overcoming challenges and achieving greatness, possibly through the support of nature and the earth.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the relationship between nature, particularly the mountains and the earth, and the responsibilities that come with it. In real life, this can be seen in the way we interact with our environment. For example, the mountains can symbolize the challenges we face, while the earth represents the foundation of our existence. The mantra encourages us to respect and nurture our surroundings, recognizing that they play a crucial role in our lives. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all things and the importance of overcoming obstacles with the support of nature. This can be applicable in various scenarios, such as environmental conservation efforts, personal growth, and community building, where we draw strength from our surroundings to achieve our goals.
