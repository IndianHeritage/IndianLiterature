यदाद्य त्वा पुरुष्टुत ब्रवाम दश्र मन्तुमः |तत्सु नो मनमा साधय ||
yad adya tvā puruṣṭuta bravāma dasra mantumaḥ | tat su no manma sādhaya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • adya: today
    • tvā: you
    • puruṣṭuta: praised or glorified
    • bravāma: we speak or declare
    • dasra: the one who is capable or powerful
    • mantumaḥ: with thought or intention
    • tat: that
    • su: good or auspicious
    • no: our
    • manma: mind or thoughts
    • sādhaya: fulfill or accomplish
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer or invocation to a divine entity, asking for the fulfillment of our thoughts and intentions.
    • It emphasizes the importance of speaking or declaring what is good and powerful, and seeking the divine's assistance in realizing our aspirations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team may gather to discuss their goals for a project. They might invoke this mantra to align their intentions and seek success in their endeavors.
    • During personal challenges, individuals may recite this mantra to focus their thoughts on positive outcomes and to seek guidance in overcoming obstacles.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the power of intention and the spoken word in manifesting our desires.
    • It serves as a reminder to align our thoughts and actions with our higher goals, encouraging mindfulness in our daily lives.
    • In spiritual practices, it can be used as a meditative tool to cultivate clarity and purpose.
