मृत्यमानः सुहस्त्य समुद्रे वाचं इन्बसि |रयिम पीषङ्गं बहुलं पुरुष्पृहं पवमानाभ्य अर्षसि ||
mṛjyamānaḥ suhastya samudre vācam invasi | rayim piśaṅgam bahulam puruspṛham pavamānābhy arṣasi ||
  • mṛjyamānaḥ: This term can be translated as "being purified" or "being cleansed." In a real-life context, this can refer to the process of self-improvement or personal growth, where individuals strive to cleanse themselves of negative thoughts and behaviors.
  • suhastya: This word means "good health" or "well-being." It emphasizes the importance of physical and mental health in our lives. For example, maintaining a balanced diet and regular exercise can lead to better health outcomes.
  • samudre: Translated as "in the ocean," this term symbolizes vastness and depth. In life, it can represent the vast experiences and emotions we encounter, much like the ocean's depth. It reminds us to navigate through life's challenges with resilience.
  • vācam: This means "speech" or "voice." It highlights the power of communication. In real life, effective communication can lead to better relationships and understanding among individuals.
  • invasi: This term can be interpreted as "invoking" or "calling upon." It suggests the act of seeking help or guidance, which is essential in times of need. For instance, people often seek support from friends or mentors during difficult times.
  • rayim: This word translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the importance of abundance in various forms, not just material wealth but also emotional and spiritual richness. For example, cultivating gratitude can lead to a sense of abundance in life.
  • piśaṅgam: This term means "yellow" or "golden," often associated with auspiciousness and positivity. In real life, it can symbolize optimism and hope, encouraging individuals to maintain a positive outlook even in challenging situations.
  • bahulam: This translates to "abundant" or "plentiful." It emphasizes the idea of having enough resources and support in life. For instance, a supportive community can provide the necessary resources for personal and professional growth.
  • puruspṛham: This term can be understood as "the one who is full of vigor" or "energetic." It encourages individuals to embrace their vitality and pursue their passions with enthusiasm. For example, engaging in hobbies can enhance one's energy and zest for life.
  • pavamānābhy: This can be interpreted as "through the purifying winds." It symbolizes the cleansing and refreshing aspects of nature. In real life, spending time in nature can rejuvenate the mind and body, promoting overall well-being.
  • arṣasi: This term means "to rise" or "to ascend." It signifies growth and elevation in life. For instance, overcoming challenges can lead to personal development and a higher sense of purpose.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of personal growth, well-being, and the pursuit of abundance in life. It emphasizes the importance of communication, support, and resilience in navigating life's challenges. By embodying the principles of purification, health, and positivity, individuals can enhance their quality of life and contribute positively to their communities. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek balance, embrace growth, and maintain a hopeful outlook, making it applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal development to community building.
