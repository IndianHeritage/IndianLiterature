पुष्यात क्षेमे अभि योगे भवात्य उभे वृत्तौ संयती सं जयाति |प्रियः सूर्ये प्रियः अग्ना भवाति य इन्द्राय सुतसोमो ददाशत ||
puṣyāt kṣeme abhi yoge bhavāty ubhe vṛtau saṁyatī saṁ jayāti | priyaḥ sūrye priyo agnā bhavāti ya indrāya sutasomo dadāśat ||
  • puṣyāt: This word can be translated as "from the nourishing" or "from the fruitful." It signifies abundance and prosperity.
  • kṣeme: This means "in safety" or "in peace." It emphasizes the importance of security and well-being in life.
  • abhi yoge: This phrase translates to "in the union" or "in the connection." It highlights the significance of relationships and collaborations.
  • bhavāty: This means "becomes" or "is." It indicates a transformation or a state of being.
  • ubhe: This word means "both" or "the two." It suggests duality or the balance between two aspects.
  • vṛtau: This translates to "in the seasons" or "in the cycles." It reflects the natural rhythms of life.
  • saṁyatī: This means "is restrained" or "is controlled." It signifies the importance of self-discipline and moderation.
  • saṁ jayāti: This translates to "conquers" or "overcomes." It emphasizes victory and success through effort.
  • priyaḥ: This means "beloved" or "dear." It signifies affection and the importance of love in relationships.
  • sūrye: This translates to "to the sun." The sun symbolizes energy, vitality, and life-giving force.
  • priyo: This means "dear" or "beloved." It reinforces the theme of love and connection.
  • agnā: This translates to "fire" or "sacred fire." Fire represents transformation, purification, and the divine.
  • bhavāti: This means "becomes" or "is." It indicates a state of existence or realization.
  • ya: This means "who" or "that." It introduces a relative clause.
  • indrāya: This translates to "to Indra," the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power and authority.
  • sutasomo: This means "the son of Soma," referring to the moon and its qualities of nourishment and tranquility.
  • dadāśat: This translates to "has given" or "has bestowed." It signifies generosity and the act of giving.

The mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of life, emphasizing the importance of nourishment, safety, relationships, and the cycles of nature. It suggests that through self-discipline and effort, one can achieve success and harmony in life. The invocation of deities like Indra and the reference to the sun and fire highlight the spiritual dimensions of these concepts.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In personal relationships, it encourages nurturing connections and valuing loved ones.
  • In professional settings, it emphasizes collaboration and the importance of working together for mutual benefit.
  • In times of challenge, it serves as a reminder to maintain self-discipline and strive for victory through perseverance.
  • In the context of nature, it highlights the significance of respecting natural cycles and seeking balance in life.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of living a balanced and harmonious life, where love, effort, and respect for nature play crucial roles. It serves as a guiding principle for personal growth, relationship building, and achieving success in various aspects of life.
