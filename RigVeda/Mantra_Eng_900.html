मदेमदे हि नो ददिर यूथा गवाम ऋजुकृतुः |सङ्गृहाय पुरू शतobhयाहस्त्या वसु शिशीहि राया आ भर ||
made-made hi no dadir yūthā gavām ṛjukratuḥ | saṁ gṛbhāya purū śatobhayāhastyā vasu śiśīhi rāya ā bhara ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • made-made: "made" refers to the act of creation or manifestation.
    • hi: "indeed" or "certainly," emphasizing the statement.
    • no: "our," indicating possession or belonging.
    • dadir: "to give" or "to bestow."
    • yūthā: "herds" or "groups," often referring to cattle.
    • gavām: "of cows," highlighting the importance of cattle in Vedic culture.
    • ṛjukratuḥ: "of straight-mindedness" or "of righteous intent."
    • saṁ: "together" or "with."
    • gṛbhāya: "to grasp" or "to hold."
    • purū: "many" or "abundant."
    • śatobhayāhastyā: "with a hundredfold strength" or "with great power."
    • vasu: "wealth" or "prosperity."
    • śiśīhi: "may you bestow" or "may you grant."
    • rāya: "wealth" or "prosperity."
    • ā: "to" or "towards."
    • bhara: "bring" or "carry."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer for abundance and prosperity, particularly in the context of cattle, which were a symbol of wealth in Vedic society.
    • The invocation of "straight-mindedness" suggests a desire for clarity and righteousness in one's actions and intentions.
    • The phrase "may you bestow wealth" indicates a request for divine blessings to achieve material and spiritual prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, cattle are often seen as a source of livelihood. This mantra can be recited during rituals to invoke blessings for a good harvest and healthy livestock.
    • In business, individuals may use this mantra to seek clarity and ethical practices in their dealings, ensuring that their intentions align with righteousness.
    • During family gatherings or festivals, this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings for prosperity and unity among family members.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in achieving prosperity and maintaining ethical integrity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from agriculture to business and family life, emphasizing the importance of righteousness and abundance.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can align their intentions with their actions, fostering a sense of community and shared prosperity.
