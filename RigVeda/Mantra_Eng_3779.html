नू न इद धि वारण āsā सचन्त सूरयः |ऊर्जो नपाद अभिष्टये पाहि शग्धि स्वस्तय उतैधि पृत्सु नो वृधे ||
nū na id dhi vāryam āsā sacanta sūrayaḥ | ūrjo napād abhiṣṭaye pāhi śagdhi svastaya utaidhi pṛtsu no vṛdhe ||
  • nū na id dhi vāryam: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, there is no doubt about the strength." It emphasizes the certainty of power and strength in the universe.
  • āsā sacanta sūrayaḥ: This means "the brave ones who are full of hope." It refers to those who possess courage and optimism, suggesting that hope is a vital force in overcoming challenges.
  • ūrjo napād abhiṣṭaye: This translates to "for the attainment of energy and strength." It highlights the importance of energy in achieving goals and aspirations.
  • pāhi śagdhi svastaya: This can be interpreted as "protect us with auspiciousness." It calls for divine protection and blessings for well-being and prosperity.
  • utaidhi pṛtsu no vṛdhe: This means "may we grow and thrive in the world." It expresses a desire for growth, prosperity, and success in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of strength, hope, and the pursuit of well-being. It serves as a reminder that in the face of challenges, one must harness inner strength and maintain hope. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal struggles, professional challenges, or even in community efforts. For instance, when facing a difficult project at work, invoking the spirit of this mantra can inspire individuals to remain hopeful and resilient, ultimately leading to success and growth. The call for divine protection and auspiciousness also resonates in daily prayers and rituals, reinforcing the belief that with strength and hope, one can navigate through life's complexities and emerge victorious.
