मित्रावरुणवन्ता उता धर्मवन्ता मरुत्वन्ता जारितुर गच्छथो हवम् |सजोषसा उषसा सूर्येण चादित्यैः यातम अश्विनाः ||
mitrāvaruṇavantā uta dharmavantā marutvantā jaritur gacchatho havam | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa cādityair yātam aśvinā ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May the Mitra and Varuna, the guardians of truth and order, along with the Maruts, the storm deities, come to us. May we be guided by the dawn and the sun, and may we journey with the Ashvins, the divine horsemen."
  • Key Terms:
    • Mitra: Represents friendship, harmony, and the social order.
    • Varuna: Symbolizes cosmic order, truth, and moral authority.
    • Maruts: The storm gods, representing strength and the forces of nature.
    • Uṣas: The dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and enlightenment.
    • Sūrya: The sun, representing life, energy, and clarity.
    • Ashvins: Twin deities associated with health, medicine, and the transition between night and day.
  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine forces of Mitra and Varuna, emphasizing the importance of truth and order in our lives. For example, in a community, when people uphold honesty and integrity, it fosters trust and cooperation.
    • The Maruts represent the dynamic and sometimes chaotic forces of nature. In real life, this can be likened to the challenges we face, such as natural disasters or personal struggles, which require resilience and strength to overcome.
    • The mention of Uṣas and Sūrya signifies the hope and clarity that comes with new beginnings. For instance, starting a new job or moving to a new city can be daunting, but it also brings opportunities for growth and learning.
    • The Ashvins symbolize healing and the journey towards wellness. In a practical sense, this can relate to seeking medical help or engaging in self-care practices to improve one’s health.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of truth, strength, new beginnings, and healing in our lives. It serves as a reminder to seek harmony and balance, to embrace challenges as opportunities for growth, and to pursue health and well-being.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, community building, and health practices. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can navigate life's complexities with grace and purpose.
