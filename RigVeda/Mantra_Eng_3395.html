अभी न आ ववृत्स्व चक्रं न वृत्तम् अरवतः |नियुद्भिः चर्षणीनाम् ||
abhī na ā vavṛtsva cakraṁ na vṛttam arvataḥ | niyudbhiś carṣaṇīnām ||
  • Mantra: "abhī na ā vavṛtsva cakraṁ na vṛttam arvataḥ | niyudbhiś carṣaṇīnām ||"
  • Translation: "Do not let the wheel of the chariot turn away from us; may it not roll away from the path of the righteous, guided by the reins of the wise."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • abhī: towards us, in our direction
    • na: not
    • ā: away, from
    • vavṛtsva: let it turn, let it roll
    • cakraṁ: wheel, chariot wheel
    • na: not
    • vṛttam: rolling, turning
    • arvataḥ: of the righteous, of the virtuous
    • niyudbhiś: by the reins, by the control
    • carṣaṇīnām: of the wise, of the knowledgeable
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of guidance and control in our lives.
    • The "wheel" symbolizes the journey of life, which can often veer off course without proper direction.
    • The "reins" represent wisdom and knowledge, which are essential for steering our lives in the right direction.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, having a mentor can be likened to the "reins" that guide one's career path, ensuring that one does not stray from their goals.
    • In personal relationships, effective communication serves as the "wheel" that keeps the relationship on track, preventing misunderstandings and conflicts.
    • In education, a good teacher provides the necessary guidance to students, helping them navigate their learning journey effectively.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of guidance and wisdom in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and support to ensure they remain on the right path.
    • In a broader sense, it highlights the collective responsibility of society to provide direction and support to its members, fostering a harmonious and righteous community.
