अगन्म महा नमसां यविष्टं यो दीदाय समिद्धः स्वे दुरोणे |चित्रभानुं rodasī अन्तर उर्वी स्वाहुतं विश्वतः प्रत्यञ्चम् ||
aganma mahā namasā yaviṣṭhaṁ yo dīdāya samiddhaḥ sve duroṇe | citrabhānuṁ rodasī antar urvī svāhutaṁ viśvataḥ pratyañcam ||
  • aganma: This term can be interpreted as "we have come" or "we have arrived." It signifies the act of approaching or invoking a divine presence.
  • mahā: Meaning "great" or "mighty," this word emphasizes the grandeur and significance of the entity being addressed.
  • namasā: This translates to "with reverence" or "with respect," indicating a humble approach towards the divine.
  • yaviṣṭhaṁ: This word means "the most excellent" or "the best," referring to the supreme quality of the divine being invoked.
  • yo: Meaning "who," this word introduces the subject of the mantra, the divine entity being praised.
  • dīdāya: This term can be understood as "who shines" or "who illuminates," suggesting the enlightening nature of the divine.
  • samiddhaḥ: This means "kindled" or "ignited," often used in the context of fire, symbolizing purity and transformation.
  • sve: This translates to "in one's own," indicating a personal connection or ownership.
  • duroṇe: Meaning "in the abode" or "in the dwelling," it refers to the place where the divine presence resides.
  • citrabhānuṁ: This term means "the colorful sun" or "the radiant sun," symbolizing vitality, energy, and life-giving force.
  • rodasī: This translates to "the heavens" or "the sky," representing the vastness and the divine realm.
  • antar: Meaning "within," it indicates an inner dimension or the essence of existence.
  • urvī: This word means "the earth," symbolizing stability, grounding, and the physical realm.
  • svāhutaṁ: This translates to "offered" or "sacrificed," indicating a gesture of giving or devotion.
  • viśvataḥ: Meaning "from all sides" or "universally," it emphasizes the omnipresence of the divine.
  • pratyañcam: This term means "the opposite" or "the other side," suggesting a duality or balance in existence.

  • Significance: This mantra is a profound invocation that emphasizes the connection between the divine and the earthly realms. It highlights the importance of reverence, illumination, and the duality of existence.
  • Real-life Application: In daily life, this mantra can be applied in moments of reflection, meditation, or prayer. It serves as a reminder to approach life with humility and respect, acknowledging the greater forces at play. For instance, when facing challenges, one might recite this mantra to invoke strength and clarity, much like seeking guidance from a mentor or a wise figure.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and recognizing the interconnectedness of all existence. It encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of reverence and to acknowledge the light within themselves and others. By integrating this understanding into daily life, one can foster a deeper connection with the world and the divine, leading to a more harmonious existence.
