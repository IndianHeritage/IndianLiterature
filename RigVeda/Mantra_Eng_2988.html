मन्त्र: निष्सिध्वरिस त ओषधीर उतापो रयिं त इन्द्र पृथिवी बिभर्ति | सखायस ते वामभाजः स्याम महद देवानाम असुरत्वम् एकम् ||
niṣṣidhvarīs ta oṣadhīr utāpo rayiṁ ta indra pṛthivī bibharti | sakhāyas te vāmabhājaḥ syāma mahad devānām asuratvam ekam ||
  • niṣṣidhvarīs: This term can be broken down into "niṣṣidh" meaning "to be free from" or "to be liberated" and "varīs" which refers to "the best" or "the most excellent." Together, it suggests a state of liberation or excellence.
  • ta oṣadhīr: "oṣadhīr" refers to "herbs" or "plants," which are often associated with healing and nourishment. This phrase can imply the importance of nature and its healing properties.
  • utāpo rayiṁ: "utāpo" means "to rise" or "to elevate," while "rayiṁ" refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." This part of the mantra suggests the elevation of wealth or prosperity, indicating a positive growth in material or spiritual wealth.
  • ta indra pṛthivī bibharti: "indra" is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength and power. "pṛthivī" means "the earth," and "bibharti" means "to bear" or "to support." This phrase indicates that the earth, under the influence of Indra, supports and nurtures life.
  • sakhāyas te vāmabhājaḥ syāma: "sakhāyas" refers to "friends" or "companions," "te" means "your," and "vāmabhājaḥ" can be interpreted as "those who share in your fortune." This suggests the importance of companionship and shared prosperity.
  • mahad devānām asuratvam ekam: "mahad" means "great," "devānām" refers to "of the gods," "asuratvam" can be interpreted as "the quality of being divine," and "ekam" means "one." This part emphasizes the unity and greatness of divine qualities.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of liberation, prosperity, and the nurturing aspect of nature, while also highlighting the importance of companionship and divine unity. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Promoting environmental awareness and the healing properties of nature, encouraging individuals to connect with and protect their natural surroundings.
  • Fostering community and friendship, emphasizing the significance of shared experiences and mutual support in achieving prosperity.
  • Encouraging individuals to seek spiritual growth and unity with the divine, recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings.

This mantra serves as a reminder of the holistic approach to life, where liberation, prosperity, and companionship are intertwined, leading to a fulfilling existence.
