वृषा ते वज्र उता ते वृषा रथो वृषाणां हरि वृषभाण्य आयुधा |वृष्णो मदस्य वृषभ त्वम ईशिष इन्द्र सोमस्य वृषभस्य तृप्णुहि ||
vṛṣā te vajra uta te vṛṣā ratho vṛṣaṇā harī vṛṣabhāṇy āyudhā | vṛṣṇo madasya vṛṣabha tvam īśiṣa indra somasya vṛṣabhasya tṛpṇuhi ||
  • vṛṣā te vajra uta te vṛṣā ratho: This phrase can be translated as "Your strength is like a thunderbolt, and your strength is like a chariot." Here, "vṛṣā" signifies strength or power, while "vajra" refers to a thunderbolt, symbolizing indomitable force. The chariot represents the ability to move forward and conquer challenges.
  • vṛṣaṇā harī vṛṣabhāṇy āyudhā: This translates to "The powerful one, the divine horse, and the one who wields weapons." "Harī" often refers to a divine being or god, while "vṛṣabhāṇy" indicates a strong protector. This line emphasizes the importance of divine support and strength in overcoming obstacles.
  • vṛṣṇo madasya vṛṣabha tvam īśiṣa: This means "You are the powerful one of the Vṛṣṇi clan, the lord of intoxication." "Vṛṣṇo" refers to the Vṛṣṇi clan, which is associated with strength and valor. "Madasya" implies a state of bliss or intoxication, suggesting that true power comes from a state of joy and fulfillment.
  • indra somasya vṛṣabhasya tṛpṇuhi: This translates to "Indra, satisfy the desires of the powerful one." "Indra" is the king of gods in Vedic literature, representing authority and strength. The call to satisfy desires indicates a plea for abundance and fulfillment in life.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, divine support, and the pursuit of fulfillment. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Facing challenges in personal or professional life, where one can draw strength from the metaphorical "thunderbolt" and "chariot" to overcome obstacles.
  • Seeking divine guidance and support in times of need, much like the invocation of Indra for satisfaction of desires.
  • Emphasizing the importance of joy and fulfillment in achieving true power and success, reminding individuals to find happiness in their pursuits.

This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, divine support, and the pursuit of happiness, encouraging individuals to harness these elements in their daily lives.
