अम्यक् साता इन्द्र ऋष्टिर अस्मे सनेम्य अभ्वम् मरुतो जुन्थि | अग्निश्चिद् धि ष्मातसे शुशुक्वान् आपो न द्वीपं दधति प्रयांसी ||
amyak sā ta indra ṛṣṭir asme sanemy abhvam maruto junanti | agniś cid dhi ṣmātase śuśukvān āpo na dvīpaṁ dadhati prayāṁsi ||
  • Mantra: "amyak sā ta indra ṛṣṭir asme sanemy abhvam maruto junanti | agniś cid dhi ṣmātase śuśukvān āpo na dvīpaṁ dadhati prayāṁsi ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "May that Indra, who is the bestower of riches, grant us prosperity and strength. The Maruts (storm deities) support us, and Agni (the fire god) is our witness. The waters do not withhold their bounty from us."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • amyak: "May that" - a call for divine intervention.
    • sā: "that" - referring to the divine presence or energy.
    • ta: "that" - emphasizing the importance of the divine.
    • indra: "Indra" - the king of gods, associated with rain, storms, and strength.
    • ṛṣṭir: "riches" - signifies wealth, prosperity, and abundance.
    • asme: "to us" - a plea directed towards the divine for blessings.
    • sanemy: "grant" - a request for bestowing blessings.
    • abhvam: "strength" - a desire for physical and spiritual strength.
    • maruto: "Maruts" - storm deities, representing power and energy.
    • junanti: "support" - indicating the assistance and backing from the divine forces.
    • agniś: "Agni" - the fire god, symbolizing purity and transformation.
    • cid: "is" - affirming the presence of Agni.
    • dhi: "witness" - Agni as a witness to our actions and intentions.
    • ṣmātase: "from the earth" - grounding the blessings in reality.
    • śuśukvān: "bounty" - abundance and generosity.
    • āpo: "waters" - representing nourishment and life.
    • na: "not" - negation, indicating the absence of withholding.
    • dvīpaṁ: "island" - metaphorically referring to isolation or lack.
    • dadhati: "bestow" - to give or provide.
    • prayāṁsi: "bounty" - the blessings and resources available to us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain and favorable weather conditions, similar to invoking Indra for prosperity.
    • In business, entrepreneurs seek strength and support from their networks, akin to the Maruts providing assistance.
    • During rituals, fire (Agni) is used as a witness to vows and commitments, symbolizing purity and transformation in personal and professional relationships.
    • Communities often come together to share resources, reflecting the idea that the waters (āpo) do not withhold their bounty, promoting collaboration and support.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine forces for prosperity, strength, and support. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, community, and spirituality. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as agriculture, business, personal growth, and community building. By recognizing the blessings of nature and the divine, individuals and communities can foster a sense of gratitude and responsibility towards their resources and relationships.
