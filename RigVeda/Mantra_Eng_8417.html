इन्द्राय सोम सुषुतः परि श्रवापामिवा भवतु रक्षसा सह |मा ते रसस्य मत्सत द्वयाविनो द्रविणस्वन्त इह सन्त्व इन्दवः ||
indrāya soma suṣutaḥ pari sravāpāmīvā bhavatu rakṣasā saha | mā te rasasya matsata dvayāvino draviṇasvanta iha santv indavaḥ ||
  • Indrāya: This refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunder, and war. Indra symbolizes strength and power.
  • Soma: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. It represents the essence of life and vitality.
  • Suṣutaḥ: This word means "well-prepared" or "well-made," indicating the importance of quality and readiness in offerings.
  • Pari sravāpāmīvā: This phrase can be interpreted as "may the streams flow abundantly," suggesting a desire for prosperity and abundance in life.
  • Bhavatu: This means "may it be" or "let it happen," expressing a wish or intention.
  • Rakṣasā saha: This translates to "with protection," indicating a plea for safety and security in the face of challenges.
  • Mā te: This means "may you not," suggesting a negation or a wish to avoid something undesirable.
  • Rasasya: This refers to "essence" or "juice," often symbolizing the richness of life and experiences.
  • Matsata: This word means "to be satisfied" or "to be content," highlighting the importance of fulfillment in life.
  • Dvayāvino: This can be interpreted as "twofold" or "dual," possibly referring to the dual nature of existence.
  • Draviṇasvanta: This means "wealthy" or "prosperous," emphasizing the desire for material and spiritual abundance.
  • Iha: This means "here" or "in this world," grounding the mantra in the present reality.
  • Indavaḥ: This refers to "the moon" or "moon-like," symbolizing calmness, serenity, and the nurturing aspects of life.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a prayer for abundance, protection, and fulfillment in life. It emphasizes the importance of divine blessings, quality offerings, and the desire for prosperity both materially and spiritually. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable during rituals, personal prayers, or moments of reflection when one seeks guidance and support from higher powers. It serves as a reminder to cultivate gratitude for the richness of life and to remain grounded in the present while aspiring for growth and security.
