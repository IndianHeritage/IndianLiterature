शुष्मिन्तमं न ऊतये द्युम्निनं पाहि जागृविम् |इन्द्र सोमं शतक्रतो ||
śuṣmintamaṁ na ūtaye dyumninam pāhi jāgṛvim | indra somaṁ śatakrato ||
  • śuṣmintamaṁ: This term can be broken down into "śuṣmin," which refers to something that is auspicious or beneficial, and "tamaṁ," meaning darkness or ignorance. Together, it suggests the removal of ignorance and the promotion of auspiciousness.
  • na ūtaye: "na" means "not," and "ūtaye" refers to the act of rising or elevating. This phrase implies a plea for the absence of negativity or obstacles that hinder progress.
  • dyumninam: This word is derived from "dyumna," which means glory or brilliance. "Dyumninam" can be interpreted as those who are glorious or who possess brilliance, suggesting a focus on the positive attributes of individuals or deities.
  • pāhi: This is a plea for protection or safeguarding. It indicates a desire for divine intervention to shield from harm.
  • jāgṛvim: This term means "awakened" or "alert." It signifies a state of awareness and readiness, both spiritually and mentally.
  • indra: Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and the warrior spirit. He represents strength, power, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • somaṁ: Soma refers to a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. It is often associated with the moon and represents nourishment for the soul.
  • śatakrato: This term means "one who has a hundred powers" or "the one with a hundred sacrifices." It emphasizes the multifaceted nature of Indra's strength and capabilities.

Conclusion: The mantra "śuṣmintamaṁ na ūtaye dyumninam pāhi jāgṛvim | indra somaṁ śatakrato ||" is a powerful invocation for protection and enlightenment. It calls upon Indra, the deity of strength and power, to safeguard the individual from ignorance and negativity while promoting awareness and brilliance. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during times of personal struggle or when facing challenges that require clarity and strength. It serves as a reminder to seek divine guidance and protection, fostering a sense of resilience and positivity in the face of adversity. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset that embraces auspiciousness and strives for enlightenment, ultimately leading to personal growth and empowerment.
