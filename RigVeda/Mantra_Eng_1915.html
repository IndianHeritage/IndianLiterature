युवम् एतं चक्रतुः सिन्धुषु प्लवम् आत्मन्वन्तम् पक्षिणं तौग्र्याय कम् |येन देवत्रा मनसा निरूहतुः सुपप्तनी पेततुः क्षोदसो महः ||
yuvam etaṁ cakrathuḥ sindhuṣu plavam ātmanvantam pakṣiṇaṁ taugryāya kam | yena devatrā manasā nirūhathuḥ supaptanī petathuḥ kṣodaso mahaḥ ||
  • yuvam etaṁ cakrathuḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "you both (the divine entities) are the ones who revolve or move in a circle." In a real-life context, this can symbolize the cyclical nature of life, where beginnings and endings are interconnected, much like the seasons or the cycle of day and night.
  • sindhuṣu plavam: Translated, this means "the boat in the rivers." This can represent the journey of life, where we navigate through various challenges and experiences, much like a boat navigating through rivers. For example, in our careers, we often face obstacles that we must navigate to reach our goals.
  • ātmanvantam pakṣiṇaṁ: This phrase refers to "the bird that is self-governed." It symbolizes freedom and self-determination. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who pursue their passions and dreams, much like an artist or entrepreneur who takes charge of their own destiny.
  • taugryāya kam: This can be interpreted as "for the purpose of strength." It emphasizes the importance of resilience and fortitude in our endeavors. For instance, athletes train rigorously to build strength and endurance, demonstrating the value of perseverance.
  • yena devatrā manasā nirūhathuḥ: This translates to "by which the divine beings are established in the mind." It suggests the importance of mental clarity and focus. In practical terms, this can relate to mindfulness practices, where individuals cultivate a clear and focused mind to achieve their goals.
  • supaptanī petathuḥ: This phrase means "may the well-structured (or well-formed) ones descend." It signifies the arrival of positive influences or blessings in our lives. For example, when we surround ourselves with supportive friends and mentors, we create an environment conducive to growth and success.
  • kṣodaso mahaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the great one who is free from sorrow." It highlights the pursuit of happiness and the importance of overcoming challenges. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who, despite facing hardships, maintain a positive outlook and strive for joy and fulfillment.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of cyclical existence, self-determination, resilience, mental clarity, and the pursuit of happiness. Its significance lies in its encouragement to navigate life's challenges with strength and purpose. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various aspects, such as personal growth, career development, and fostering positive relationships. By embodying the teachings of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a fulfilling and meaningful life, embracing both the struggles and joys that come their way.
