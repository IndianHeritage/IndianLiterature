एता अर्षन्त्य अललाभवन्तीर ऋतावरीर इव संक्रोशमानाः |एता वि पृच्छ किम इदं भनन्ति कं आपो अद्रिम परिधिं रुजन्ति ||
etā arṣanty alalābhavantīr ṛtāvarīr iva saṁkrośamānāḥ | etā vi pṛccha kim idam bhananti kam āpo adrim paridhiṁ rujanti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • etā: these
    • arṣanty: are moving or flowing
    • alalābhavantīr: not being restrained or held back
    • ṛtāvarīr: like the truth or righteousness
    • iva: like
    • saṁkrośamānāḥ: crying out or calling out
    • etā: these
    • vi: indeed
    • pṛccha: ask or inquire
    • kim idam: what is this
    • bhananti: they speak or declare
    • kam: whom
    • āpo: the waters
    • adrim: the mountain or rock
    • paridhiṁ: the boundary or limit
    • rujanti: they cause pain or distress
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of forces that are dynamic and unrestrained, akin to the flow of water or the essence of truth.
    • The imagery of "crying out" suggests a call for attention or inquiry into deeper truths.
    • The mention of "waters" and "mountains" symbolizes the natural elements and their interactions, reflecting the balance and harmony in nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, rivers flow freely, carving their paths through mountains, symbolizing the unstoppable force of truth and righteousness.
    • In human life, individuals often face challenges that require them to seek the truth, much like asking questions to understand their circumstances better.
    • Environmental movements can be seen as a response to the "crying out" of nature, urging humanity to recognize and address the pain caused by pollution and climate change.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing the dynamic forces of nature and truth in our lives.
    • It encourages us to inquire and seek understanding, reminding us that our actions can have significant impacts on the world around us.
    • In a broader sense, it serves as a call to action for individuals and societies to align with the principles of truth and righteousness, fostering harmony with nature and each other.
