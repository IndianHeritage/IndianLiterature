आद इं हंसो यथा गणं विश्वस्याविवशान मतिम् |अत्यो न गोभिर अज्यते ||
ād īṁ haṁso yathā gaṇaṁ viśvasyāvīvaśan matim | atyo na gobhir ajyate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ād: as, like
    • īṁ: the sound or vibration
    • haṁso: the swan, symbolizing purity and wisdom
    • yathā: as, in the manner of
    • gaṇaṁ: a group or assembly
    • viśvasyā: of the universe, all-encompassing
    • āvīvaśan: to be filled with, to be imbued with
    • matim: intellect, understanding, or thought
    • atyō: beyond, surpassing
    • na: not
    • gobhir: by the cows, symbolizing nourishment and wealth
    • ajyate: is smeared or anointed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the swan (haṁsa) as a metaphor for the pure and wise aspect of the self.
    • It suggests that just as the swan is part of a larger assembly (gaṇaṁ), so too is our intellect (matim) part of the universal consciousness (viśvasyā).
    • The phrase "atyō na gobhir ajyate" implies that true understanding and wisdom cannot be tainted or limited by material possessions or worldly concerns (gobhir).
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of the swan—wisdom, purity of intention, and the ability to unite a team—can inspire others to work towards a common goal, transcending individual interests.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who approach conflicts with a mindset of understanding and empathy, akin to the swan's purity, can foster deeper connections and resolve issues harmoniously.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of cultivating a pure and wise intellect that is connected to the greater whole of existence.
    • It serves as a reminder that true wisdom and understanding go beyond material concerns and are rooted in a deeper connection to the universe.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more meaningful relationships, effective leadership, and a greater sense of purpose.
