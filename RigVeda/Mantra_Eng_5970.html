इन्द्रो यातूनाम् अभवत् पराशरः हविर्मथीनाम् अभ्य आविवासतम् |अभीदु शक्रः परशुर्व यथा वनं पात्रेव भिन्दन् सता एति राक्षसः ||
indro yātūnām abhavat parāśaro havirmathīnām abhy ā3vivāsatām | abhīd u śakraḥ paraśur yathā vanam pātreva bhindan sata eti rakṣasaḥ ||
  • Indro yātūnām abhavat: This phrase refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, who is often associated with strength and power. The term "yātūnām" can be interpreted as "of the beings" or "of the creatures." This suggests that Indra is a protector or a leader among all beings.
  • Parāśaro havirmathīnām abhy ā3vivāsatām: "Parāśaro" refers to a sage or a wise person, while "havirmathīnām" relates to those who are engaged in rituals or offerings. The phrase indicates that the wise ones, through their actions and offerings, invoke the presence of Indra, suggesting a connection between divine favor and human efforts.
  • Abhīd u śakraḥ paraśur: Here, "abhīd" means "to strike" or "to hit," and "śakraḥ" refers to Indra again, emphasizing his might. "Paraśur" can be translated as "the axe," symbolizing strength and the ability to overcome obstacles. This indicates that Indra, with his powerful axe, can remove barriers and challenges.
  • Yathā vanam pātreva bhindan: This phrase translates to "just as one splits a tree with an axe." It draws a parallel between Indra's strength and the act of cutting through difficulties, much like how one would cut through a tree. This metaphor illustrates the idea of overcoming challenges in life.
  • Sata eti rakṣasaḥ: "Sata" means "hundreds" or "many," and "rakṣasaḥ" refers to "demons" or "evil forces." This suggests that Indra, with his strength, can vanquish numerous adversaries or negative influences, reinforcing the idea of protection and victory over evil.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the themes of strength, protection, and the importance of wise actions in invoking divine favor. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios where individuals face challenges or adversities. For instance, in times of personal struggle, one might seek guidance and strength through meditation or prayer, much like the rituals performed by the sages. The mantra serves as a reminder that with determination and the right intentions, one can overcome obstacles and emerge victorious against negative forces, whether they be external challenges or internal doubts. It highlights the significance of faith, wisdom, and the power of divine support in navigating life's difficulties.
