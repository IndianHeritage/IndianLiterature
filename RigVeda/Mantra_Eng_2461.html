मन्त्र: क्रत्वा दाक्षस्य तरुषो विधर्मणि देवासो अग्निं जनयन्त चित्तिभिः |रुरुचानं भानुना ज्योतीषा महाम अत्यं न वाजं सनीष्यन्न उप ब्रुवे ||
kratvā dakṣasya taruṣo vidharmaṇi devāso agniṁ janayanta cittibhiḥ | rurucānam bhānunā jyotiṣā mahām atyaṁ na vājaṁ saniṣyann upa bruve ||
  • kratvā - This term refers to the act of doing or performing. It signifies the importance of action in achieving results.
  • dakṣasya - This word means 'of the capable' or 'skilled'. It highlights the value of competence and expertise in any endeavor.
  • taruṣo - This translates to 'of the trees' or 'of the plants', symbolizing growth, nature, and the sustenance provided by the earth.
  • vidharmaṇi - This term means 'in accordance with righteousness' or 'following the path of dharma', emphasizing the importance of ethical conduct.
  • devāso - This refers to 'the gods', indicating divine presence and support in human actions.
  • agniṁ - This means 'fire', which is a symbol of energy, transformation, and purification in many cultures.
  • janayanta - This translates to 'they generate' or 'they create', indicating the act of bringing something into existence.
  • cittibhiḥ - This word means 'with consciousness' or 'with awareness', highlighting the role of mindfulness in actions.
  • rurucānam - This term refers to 'the shining' or 'the radiant', often associated with light and enlightenment.
  • bhānunā - This means 'by the sun', symbolizing vitality, life, and the source of energy.
  • jyotiṣā - This translates to 'by light', further emphasizing illumination and knowledge.
  • mahām - This means 'great', indicating the significance and magnitude of the subject being discussed.
  • atyam - This term means 'beyond' or 'transcending', suggesting a higher state of being or understanding.
  • na vājaṁ - This translates to 'not the prize' or 'not the reward', indicating a focus on the process rather than the outcome.
  • saniṣyann - This means 'by restraining' or 'by controlling', highlighting the importance of discipline.
  • upa bruve - This translates to 'let it be spoken' or 'let it be declared', indicating the importance of communication and sharing knowledge.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of action, skill, and ethical conduct in achieving one's goals. It highlights the role of divine support and the importance of mindfulness and discipline in our endeavors. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, professional growth, and community service. By focusing on righteous actions and being aware of our intentions, we can create a positive impact in our lives and the lives of others. The mantra serves as a reminder that the journey and the process are as important as the destination, encouraging us to act with purpose and integrity.
