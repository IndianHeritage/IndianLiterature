उशनā यत् परावतोऽजगन्नूतये कवे |सुम्नानि विश्वा मनुषेवा तुर्वणिर अहा विश्वेवा तुर्वणिः ||
uśanā yat parāvato 'jagann ūtaye kave | sumnāni viśvā manuṣeva turvaṇir ahā viśveva turvaṇiḥ ||
  • uśanā: This term refers to the dawn or the light of the morning. In a metaphorical sense, it symbolizes knowledge, enlightenment, and the dispelling of ignorance.
  • yat: This word means "that" or "which," indicating a connection to the following concepts.
  • parāvato: This term can be interpreted as "of the highest" or "supreme," suggesting something that transcends ordinary understanding.
  • 'jagann: This word means "the universe" or "the world," encompassing all that exists.
  • ūtaye: This term translates to "for the benefit" or "for the welfare," indicating a purpose or intention behind the actions or phenomena described.
  • kave: This word means "the poet" or "the seer," referring to those who possess insight and wisdom.
  • sumnāni: This term means "auspicious" or "beneficial," suggesting positive outcomes or blessings.
  • viśvā: This word translates to "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity and comprehensiveness.
  • manuṣeva: This term means "like a human," suggesting a connection to human experience and understanding.
  • turvaṇir: This word can be interpreted as "the one who brings forth" or "the creator," indicating a source of creation or inspiration.
  • ahā: This term means "day" or "light," reinforcing the theme of illumination and clarity.
  • viśveva: This word means "like all" or "as if all," suggesting a universal application or relevance.
  • turvaṇiḥ: This term again refers to "the creator" or "the one who brings forth," emphasizing the role of creation in the context of the mantra.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of enlightenment, creativity, and the universal nature of knowledge. It suggests that the dawn of understanding brings forth auspiciousness and benefits for all, much like the light of day dispels darkness. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as education, where the pursuit of knowledge leads to personal and societal growth. It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and share it with others, fostering a community that thrives on enlightenment and creativity. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the potential for collective upliftment through shared knowledge and understanding.
