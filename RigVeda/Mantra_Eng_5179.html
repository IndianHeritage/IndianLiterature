एता नो अग्नि सौभगा दिदीहि अपि क्रतुं सुचेतसं वतेम |विश्वा स्तोत्रिभ्यो गृणते च सन्तु यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
etā no agne saubhagā didīhy api kratuṁ sucetasaṁ vatema | viśvā stotṛbhyo gṛṇate ca santu yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • etā no agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni (the divine fire), this is for us." Agni represents the sacred fire and is a symbol of purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • saubhagā didīhy api: This means "may you bestow upon us auspiciousness." It reflects the desire for blessings and good fortune in life.
  • kratuṁ sucetasaṁ vatema: This translates to "we invoke the wise and thoughtful intellect." Here, 'kratu' refers to the mental power or intellect that guides actions and decisions.
  • viśvā stotṛbhyo gṛṇate: This means "may all the singers of praise (the stotṛs) sing." It emphasizes the importance of collective praise and gratitude towards the divine.
  • ca santu yūyam pāta: This translates to "may you all protect us." It is a plea for protection and guidance from the divine forces.
  • svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: This means "may you always grant us well-being." It signifies a continuous wish for health, prosperity, and peace in life.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for divine blessings, protection, and wisdom. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • During rituals and ceremonies, invoking Agni for auspicious beginnings.
  • In personal prayers, seeking guidance and clarity in decision-making.
  • In community gatherings, fostering a sense of unity and collective gratitude.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine support in our endeavors, promoting well-being, and nurturing a wise and thoughtful approach to life.
