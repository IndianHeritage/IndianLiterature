अग्नि शुक्रम् शोचिषा विश्वाभिर देवहूतिभिः |इमं स्तोमं जुषस्व नः ||
agne śukreṇa śociṣā viśvābhir devahūtibhiḥ | imaṁ stomaṁ juṣasva naḥ ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic tradition, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Śukreṇa: Meaning "with brightness" or "with purity," it emphasizes the importance of clarity and righteousness.
  • Śociṣā: This term translates to "with radiance" or "with brilliance," indicating the illuminating nature of knowledge and wisdom.
  • Viśvābhir: This word means "with all" or "universal," suggesting inclusivity and the collective nature of divine blessings.
  • Devahūtibhiḥ: Referring to "the divine beings" or "gods," it highlights the connection between humans and the divine forces that govern the universe.
  • Imam: This means "this," indicating a specific offering or prayer being made.
  • Stomaṁ: Refers to "hymn" or "praise," emphasizing the act of worship and reverence.
  • Juṣasva: This translates to "may you enjoy" or "may you accept," expressing a desire for the divine to receive the offering.
  • Naḥ: Meaning "for us," it signifies a personal appeal for blessings and support.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer to Agni, asking for his blessings and radiance to illuminate our lives. It emphasizes the importance of purity, collective divine support, and the act of worship. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:


  • Personal Growth: Just as Agni represents transformation, individuals can invoke this mantra during times of change, seeking clarity and guidance in their personal journeys.
  • Community and Unity: The emphasis on "with all" encourages collective efforts in communities, promoting harmony and shared goals.
  • Spiritual Practices: This mantra can be recited during rituals or meditation to invite divine energy and wisdom into one’s life.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine illumination and support in our lives. It serves as a reminder of the importance of purity, collective effort, and the pursuit of knowledge. By incorporating this mantra into daily practices, individuals can foster a deeper connection with the divine and enhance their spiritual journey.
