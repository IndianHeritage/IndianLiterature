तद इनु ते करणं दसम विप्राहिं यद घ्नन्न ओजो अत्रामिमीताः |शुष्णस्य चित परि मायाऽगृभ्णाः प्रपित्वं यन्न अप दस्यूम् असेधः ||
tad in nu te karaṇaṁ dasma viprāhiṁ yad ghnann ojo atrāmimīthāḥ | śuṣṇasya cit pari māyā agṛbhṇāḥ prapitvaṁ yann apa dasyūm̐r asedhaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • in: in
    • nu: indeed
    • te: your
    • karaṇaṁ: action or deed
    • dasma: from here
    • viprāhiṁ: wise or learned
    • yad: which
    • ghnann: striking or hitting
    • ojo: energy or strength
    • atrāmimīthāḥ: here, in this place
    • śuṣṇasya: of the one who is powerful
    • cit: consciousness or awareness
    • pari: around or about
    • māyā: illusion or magic
    • agṛbhṇāḥ: not grasping or holding
    • prapitvaṁ: the great power
    • yann: which
    • apa: away from
    • dasyūm̐r: the wicked or evil
    • asedhaḥ: do not disturb or harm
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the importance of wise actions and the energy that comes from them.
    • It emphasizes the need to avoid being caught in illusions and distractions that can lead one away from their true purpose.
    • The reference to "the wicked" suggests a call to protect oneself from negative influences and harmful actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied to the importance of making wise decisions that are grounded in knowledge and experience, rather than being swayed by superficial or illusory factors.
    • In personal relationships, it serves as a reminder to be aware of the influences around us and to avoid engaging with negativity or harmful behaviors.
    • In spiritual practices, it encourages individuals to seek clarity and understanding, avoiding distractions that may lead them away from their spiritual goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for conscious action and awareness in all aspects of life.
    • It teaches us to harness our inner strength and wisdom while remaining vigilant against negative influences.
    • By applying the principles of this mantra, individuals can lead more purposeful and fulfilling lives, grounded in awareness and positive action.
