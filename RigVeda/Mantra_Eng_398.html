आवः शमनं वृषभं तुग्र्यासु क्षेत्रजेषे मगवञ्च छ्वित्र्यं गाम् |ज्योक चिदात्र तस्थिवांसो अकर्ण चत्रूयताम् अधरा वेदनाकः ||
āvaḥ śamaṁ vṛṣabhaṁ tugryāsu kṣetrajeṣe maghavañ chvitryaṁ gām | jyok cid atra tasthivāṁso akrañ chatrūyatām adharā vedanākaḥ ||
  • āvaḥ: This word translates to "may we invoke" or "may we call upon." It signifies the act of inviting or summoning divine energies or blessings into our lives.
  • śamaṁ: This means "peace" or "calmness." It emphasizes the importance of tranquility in our lives, suggesting that peace is essential for harmony and well-being.
  • vṛṣabhaṁ: This term refers to "the bull," which is often a symbol of strength, power, and fertility. In a broader sense, it can represent the vital forces of nature and the universe.
  • tugryāsu: This word can be interpreted as "in the fields" or "in the realms of agriculture." It highlights the connection between spirituality and the sustenance provided by nature.
  • kṣetrajeṣe: This translates to "the one who is born in the field," indicating the importance of the earth and its resources in our lives.
  • maghavañ: This term refers to "the mighty" or "the powerful," often associated with divine beings or forces that can bestow blessings and prosperity.
  • chvitryaṁ: This can be understood as "the one who brings forth" or "the creator," emphasizing the role of divine forces in creation and sustenance.
  • gām: This means "the village" or "the community," highlighting the importance of social structures and relationships in human life.
  • jyok: This word signifies "light" or "illumination," representing knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment.
  • cid: This translates to "consciousness" or "awareness," emphasizing the significance of being aware and mindful in our actions and thoughts.
  • atra: This means "here" or "in this place," grounding the mantra in the present moment and our immediate surroundings.
  • tasthivāṁso: This can be interpreted as "those who stand firm," suggesting stability and resilience in the face of challenges.
  • akrañ: This term refers to "the one who is unyielding" or "the steadfast," emphasizing the importance of determination and strength.
  • chatrūyatām: This means "may we be protected" or "may we be shielded," highlighting the desire for safety and security in our lives.
  • adharā: This translates to "the foundation" or "the base," indicating the importance of having a strong foundation in life.
  • vedanākaḥ: This term can be understood as "the one who knows" or "the wise," emphasizing the value of knowledge and wisdom in guiding our actions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound invocation for peace, strength, and protection in our lives. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, community, and divine forces, urging us to seek harmony and stability. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during agricultural practices, community gatherings, or personal reflections. By invoking these qualities, we can foster a sense of unity, resilience, and awareness in our daily lives, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
