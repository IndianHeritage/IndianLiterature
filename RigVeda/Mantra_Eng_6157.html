आ न इन्द्र महीमिषं पुरं न दर्षि गोमतीं |उत प्रजां सुवीर्यम् ||
ā na indra mahīm iṣam puraṁ na darṣi gomatīm | uta prajāṁ suvīryam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: O (a call or invocation)
    • na: not
    • indra: Indra (the king of gods, representing strength and power)
    • mahīm: greatness or vastness
    • iṣam: the essence or the best
    • puraṁ: city or abode
    • na: not
    • darṣi: to see or perceive
    • gomatīm: the one who possesses wealth or resources
    • uta: and or also
    • prajāṁ: progeny or offspring
    • suvīryam: possessing great strength or valor
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer invoking Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, who is often associated with strength and valor.
    • The speaker is expressing a desire to not only witness the greatness of Indra but also to seek his blessings for prosperity and strength in their progeny.
    • The mention of "gomatīm" signifies a wish for wealth and resources, indicating that the speaker seeks a life of abundance and success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be related to individuals seeking success in their careers. For instance, a businessperson may recite this mantra to invoke strength and prosperity in their ventures.
    • Parents may use this mantra to bless their children, wishing for them to grow strong and successful, embodying the qualities of valor and resourcefulness.
    • In community gatherings, this mantra can be recited to seek collective strength and prosperity, fostering a sense of unity and shared goals among members.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine strength and prosperity. It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking blessings for both personal and communal growth.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as personal aspirations, family blessings, and community endeavors, emphasizing the universal desire for strength, success, and abundance.
