अभि सिध्मो अजिगाद अस्य शत्रून् वि तिग्मेन वृषभेण पूरोऽभेत् |सं वज्रेणासृजद वृत्रम इन्द्रः प्र स्वाम् मतीम् अतिरच् छाशदाणः ||
abhi sidhmo ajigād asya śatrūn vi tigmena vṛṣabheṇā puro 'bhet | saṁ vajreṇāsṛjad vṛtram indraḥ pra svām matim atirac chāśadānaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards
    • sidhmo: success or accomplishment
    • ajigād: he spoke or declared
    • asya: of this
    • śatrūn: enemies
    • vi: with
    • tigmena: powerful or strong
    • vṛṣabheṇā: with the bull (symbolizing strength)
    • puro: in front or before
    • 'bhet: fear or dread
    • saṁ: together
    • vajreṇ: with the thunderbolt
    • āsṛjad: he created or produced
    • vṛtram: the obstacle or the demon
    • indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • pra: forth or out
    • svām: his own
    • matim: mind or thought
    • atirac: exceedingly or very much
    • chāśadānaḥ: the one who gives or bestows
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of Indra, the king of gods, who declares his intent to overcome enemies with great strength.
    • The phrase "abhi sidhmo" indicates a proactive approach towards achieving success.
    • Indra's use of "vajreṇ" (thunderbolt) symbolizes the power and authority he wields to eliminate obstacles (vṛtram) in his path.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of mental strength and clarity ("matim") in overcoming challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive work environment, one may face various challenges (enemies) such as tight deadlines or difficult projects. By adopting a proactive mindset and utilizing one's skills (strength), success can be achieved.
    • In sports, athletes often face formidable opponents. By focusing on their training (mental clarity) and employing strategies (thunderbolt), they can overcome these challenges and achieve victory.
    • In personal life, individuals may encounter obstacles such as financial difficulties or health issues. By maintaining a strong will and seeking solutions (Indra's approach), they can navigate through tough times.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of strength, both mental and physical, in overcoming adversities.
    • It teaches us to face challenges head-on with confidence and clarity of thought.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, success in endeavors, and the ability to conquer obstacles that may arise.
