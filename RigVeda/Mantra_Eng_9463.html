नरा वाऽशंसं पूषणं अगोऽह्यं अग्निं देवेद्धमाभ्यर्चसे गिरा |सूर्यामासा चन्द्रमस यमं दिवि तृतं वातं उषसमक्तुम् अश्विनाः ||
narā vā śaṁsam pūṣaṇam agohyam agniṁ deveddham abhy arcase girā | sūryāmāsā candramasā yamaṁ divi tritaṁ vātam uṣasam aktum aśvinā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • narā: men or humans
    • vā: or
    • śaṁsam: praise or worship
    • pūṣaṇam: the nourisher or provider
    • agohyam: the one who is beyond perception
    • agniṁ: fire
    • deveddham: divine or godly
    • abhy: towards
    • arcase: I offer or I worship
    • girā: with words or through speech
    • sūryāmāsā: the sun
    • candramasā: the moon
    • yamaṁ: the god of death or the lord of the underworld
    • divi: in the heavens
    • tritaṁ: the third
    • vātam: the wind
    • uṣasam: the dawn
    • aktum: to be invoked or to be called
    • aśvinā: the twin gods, often associated with health and medicine
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer that acknowledges and praises various divine entities and natural forces.
    • It emphasizes the importance of worshipping the nourishing aspects of life, represented by Pūṣaṇa, and the divine qualities of Agni (fire) and the celestial bodies like the sun and moon.
    • The invocation of Yama signifies the recognition of mortality and the importance of living a righteous life.
    • The mention of the wind and dawn highlights the significance of nature in our lives and the need to respect and honor these elements.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, people often perform rituals or prayers to seek blessings from nature and the divine, similar to the worship described in this mantra.
    • For instance, during festivals, offerings are made to fire (Agni) and the sun (Sūrya) to express gratitude for their life-sustaining properties.
    • Health practices, such as yoga and meditation, can be seen as modern interpretations of invoking the energies of the aśvinā, promoting well-being and balance.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, nature, and the divine.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of gratitude and reverence for the forces that sustain them.
    • In a contemporary context, it can inspire mindfulness and a holistic approach to living, where one acknowledges the importance of both spiritual and physical nourishment.
    • Ultimately, the mantra teaches us to live harmoniously with the world around us, recognizing the divine in every aspect of life.
