परिष्कृत्वन्न अनिष्कृतं जनाय यातयन्न इषः |वृष्टिं दिवः परि श्रव ||
pariṣkṛṇvann aniṣkṛtaṁ janāya yātayann iṣaḥ | vṛṣṭiṁ divaḥ pari srava ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pariṣkṛṇvann: Making complete or perfect.
    • aniṣkṛtaṁ: That which is not accomplished or fulfilled.
    • janāya: For the people or for humanity.
    • yātayann: Striving or working towards.
    • iṣaḥ: The divine power or energy.
    • vṛṣṭiṁ: Rain or abundance.
    • dvaḥ: Of the heavens or celestial.
    • pari srava: Flowing forth completely.
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra speaks to the divine energy that works towards the fulfillment of human needs. It emphasizes the importance of striving for completeness and abundance, particularly in the context of rain, which is essential for life and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Agriculture: Farmers pray for rain to ensure a good harvest. This mantra can be seen as a call for divine intervention to provide the necessary rainfall for crops to thrive.
    • Community Efforts: In times of drought, communities come together to pray or perform rituals for rain, reflecting the collective striving for abundance and fulfillment.
    • Personal Growth: Individuals may interpret this mantra as a reminder to strive for personal goals and to seek the support of a higher power in achieving their aspirations.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in achieving completeness and abundance in life. It highlights the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine grace. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to seek balance and fulfillment, whether in agriculture, community welfare, or personal endeavors. By invoking this mantra, individuals and communities can align their efforts with a higher purpose, fostering a sense of unity and shared aspiration for prosperity and well-being.
