इन्द्राग्नि नवतिम् पुरो दासपत्नीः अधूनुतम् |साकं एकेन कर्मणा ||
indrāgnī navatim puro dāsapatnīr adhūnutam | sākam ekena karmaṇā ||
  • Indrāgnī: This term refers to the combined powers of Indra, the king of gods, and Agni, the fire god. Indra symbolizes strength, power, and rain, while Agni represents energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Navatim: This word means 'ninety' and can symbolize abundance or a large quantity. In a spiritual context, it can represent the vastness of divine blessings.
  • Puro: This term translates to 'in the front' or 'before.' It signifies the importance of leading or being at the forefront in actions and intentions.
  • Dāsapatnīr: This word can be broken down into 'dāsa' (servant) and 'patnī' (wife), suggesting a relationship of service and devotion. It can also imply the idea of nurturing and supporting roles in relationships.
  • Adhūnutam: This term means 'shaken off' or 'removed.' It indicates the act of cleansing or purifying oneself from negativity or obstacles.
  • Sākam: This word means 'together' or 'with.' It emphasizes the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving goals.
  • Ekena: This translates to 'one' or 'single,' highlighting the power of singular focus and intention in actions.
  • Karmaṇā: This term means 'by action' or 'through deeds.' It underscores the significance of actions in manifesting desires and achieving results.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to harness the combined energies of Indra and Agni, emphasizing the importance of unity and focused action in overcoming challenges and achieving abundance. It suggests that by working together with a singular purpose, one can shake off negativity and obstacles, leading to success and prosperity.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Teamwork: In a workplace setting, this mantra encourages collaboration among team members to achieve common goals. For example, a project team can embody the spirit of 'sākam ekena karmaṇā' by working together with a unified vision to complete a project successfully.
  • Personal Growth: On an individual level, one can reflect on the importance of cleansing oneself from negative thoughts and habits (adhūnutam) to embrace a more positive and productive mindset. This can be seen in practices like meditation or therapy.
  • Community Building: In community initiatives, the mantra highlights the significance of collective action (sākam) to address social issues. For instance, a group of volunteers working together to clean up a local park embodies the essence of this mantra.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of unity, focused action, and the cleansing of negativity in our lives. By embodying these principles, individuals and communities can achieve greater success and harmony in their endeavors.
