प्र धारा अस्य शुष्मिणो वृथा पवित्रे अक्षरन् | पुनानो वाचम् इष्यति ||
pra dhārā asya śuṣmiṇo vṛthā pavitre akṣaran | punāno vācam iṣyati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: forward, towards
    • dhārā: flow, stream
    • asya: of this
    • śuṣmiṇo: of the one who is pure or clean
    • vṛthā: in vain, without purpose
    • pavitre: pure, sacred
    • akṣaran: imperishable, eternal
    • punāno: renewing, refreshing
    • vācam: speech, word
    • iṣyati: is desired, is sought
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the flow of pure and eternal speech.
    • It emphasizes the importance of purity in communication and expression.
    • The phrase "in vain" suggests that without purity, words may lack true meaning or impact.
    • The idea of renewing speech indicates the need for constant reflection and improvement in how we communicate.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal relationships, clear and honest communication is vital. When we speak with purity of intention, our words can strengthen bonds.
    • In professional settings, effective communication can lead to better teamwork and project outcomes. Words that are thoughtful and respectful can foster a positive work environment.
    • In public speaking or leadership, the ability to convey messages with clarity and integrity can inspire and motivate others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of purity in our words and thoughts.
    • It serves as a reminder to reflect on our communication styles and strive for clarity and sincerity.
    • In a world filled with noise and distractions, the call for pure and meaningful speech is more relevant than ever, encouraging us to engage in conversations that uplift and inspire.
