हविमभिर हवते यो हविर्भिर अव स्तोमेभि रुद्रं दिषीय |ऋदूदरः सुहवो मा नो अस्यै बभ्रुः सुशिप्रो रीरधन मनायै ||
havīmabhir havate yo havirbhir ava stomebhī rudraṁ diṣīya | ṛdūdaraḥ suhavo mā no asyai babhruḥ suśipro rīradhan manāyai ||
  • Mantra: "havīmabhir havate yo havirbhir ava stomebhī rudraṁ diṣīya | ṛdūdaraḥ suhavo mā no asyai babhruḥ suśipro rīradhan manāyai ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as a prayer to Rudra, invoking him with offerings and praises. It expresses a desire for protection and blessings, asking for the removal of obstacles and the bestowal of prosperity.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • havīmabhir: With offerings
    • havate: To the one who is invoked
    • yo: Who
    • havirbhir: With offerings
    • ava: Downward or to remove
    • stomebhī: With praises
    • rudraṁ: Rudra, a form of Shiva
    • diṣīya: To be seen or manifested
    • ṛdūdaraḥ: The one who is kind
    • suhavo: Auspicious
    • mā: May not
    • no: Us
    • asyai: To this
    • babhruḥ: The one who is strong
    • suśipro: Well-disposed
    • rīradhan: Bestowing wealth
    • manāyai: For our minds
  • Context and Significance:
    • This mantra is a call for divine intervention and support from Rudra, who represents transformation and healing.
    • In real life, individuals often face challenges and uncertainties. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek help from higher powers or the universe when in need.
    • For example, during times of personal crisis, such as loss of a job or health issues, reciting this mantra can provide comfort and a sense of connection to something greater.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and collective effort in seeking blessings, as it is often recited in group settings during rituals.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be used in meditation or prayer sessions to focus on positive intentions and the removal of negativity.
    • It can also be applied in ceremonies for new beginnings, such as weddings or housewarming events, where blessings for prosperity and harmony are sought.
    • Additionally, it can inspire individuals to cultivate a mindset of gratitude and openness to receiving help from others, fostering a supportive community.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support and blessings in our lives. It highlights the significance of community, intention, and the transformative power of faith. By invoking Rudra, we align ourselves with the energies of protection, prosperity, and positivity, making it a valuable practice in both personal and communal settings.
