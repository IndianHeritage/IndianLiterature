अर्वावतो न आगह्य अथो शक्र पर्वतः |उ लोको यस् ते अद्रिव इन्द्रेह तता आगहि ||
arvāvato na ā gahy atho śakra parāvataḥ | u loko yas te adriva indreha tata ā gahi ||
  • Mantra: "arvāvato na ā gahy atho śakra parāvataḥ | u loko yas te adriva indreha tata ā gahi ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • arvāvato: of the swift, or the quick
    • na: not
    • ā gahy: to be grasped or held
    • atho: then, or therefore
    • śakra: powerful, often referring to Indra, the king of gods
    • parāvataḥ: of the highest, or supreme
    • u: and
    • loko: world, or realm
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • adriva: without obstacles
    • indreha: in Indra
    • tata: then, or thus
    • ā gahi: grasp or hold
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the swift and powerful nature of the divine, particularly referring to Indra, who is often seen as a protector and a force against obstacles.
    • The phrase "arvāvato na ā gahy" suggests that the swift cannot be held back, indicating the unstoppable nature of divine energy.
    • In a real-life context, this can be likened to the relentless pursuit of goals or dreams, where one must not let obstacles deter them.
    • The mention of "adriva" implies a state of being without obstacles, which can be interpreted as achieving clarity and focus in one's life.
    • In practical terms, this could relate to overcoming challenges in personal or professional life, where one seeks the strength and guidance of a higher power or inner resolve.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of resilience and the power of divine support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that, like Indra, we can harness our inner strength to navigate through difficulties.
    • In daily life, this can be applied by fostering a mindset of determination and seeking support from community or spirituality when faced with obstacles.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to embrace their potential and strive for success, knowing that they are not alone in their journey.
