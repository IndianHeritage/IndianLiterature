युवं नराः स्तुवते पज्रियाय कक्षीवते अरदतम पुरंढिम् |कारोतराच्छफाद अश्वस्य वृष्णः शतम् कुम्भांऽं असीञ्चतं सुरायाः ||
yuvaṁ narā stuvate pajriyāya kakṣīvate aradatam puraṁdhim | kārotarāc chaphād aśvasya vṛṣṇaḥ śataṁ kumbhām̐ asiñcataṁ surāyāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yuvaṁ narā stuvate pajriyāya kakṣīvate aradatam puraṁdhim | kārotarāc chaphād aśvasya vṛṣṇaḥ śataṁ kumbhām̐ asiñcataṁ surāyāḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yuvaṁ: young, youthful
    • narā: men, people
    • stuvate: praise, worship
    • pajriyāya: for prosperity, for well-being
    • kakṣīvate: who is strong, powerful
    • aradatam: the best, the most excellent
    • puraṁdhim: the creator, the architect
    • kārotarāc: from the great, the mighty
    • chaphād: from the abundance, the plenty
    • aśvasya: of the horse, symbolizing strength and speed
    • vṛṣṇaḥ: the powerful, the strong
    • śataṁ: a hundred, many
    • kumbhām̐: pot, vessel
    • asiñcataṁ: pouring, sprinkling
    • surāyāḥ: of the divine, the celestial
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the youth and strength of men who praise and worship for prosperity and well-being.
    • The term "puraṁdhim" refers to a creator or architect, suggesting that there is a divine force behind creation and prosperity.
    • The mention of "aśvasya" symbolizes the qualities of a horse, which is often associated with speed, strength, and nobility in various cultures.
    • The act of pouring from a vessel ("kumbhām̐ asiñcataṁ") signifies the sharing of abundance and blessings, which can be interpreted as a call for generosity and the distribution of wealth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, this mantra can be related to the importance of youth empowerment and the role of young people in driving change and prosperity.
    • Organizations often conduct rituals or ceremonies to invoke blessings for success and abundance, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In community gatherings, the act of sharing food and resources can be seen as a practical application of "pouring blessings" for collective well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of youth, strength, and the divine in achieving prosperity and well-being.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of community, generosity, and the acknowledgment of divine forces in our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to work together towards common goals, fostering a spirit of collaboration and abundance.
