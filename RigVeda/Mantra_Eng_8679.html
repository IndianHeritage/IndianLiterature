स नो मदानां पत इन्दो देवाप्सरा असि |सखेव सख्ये गातुवित्तमो भव ||
sa no madānām pata indo devapsarā asi | sakheva sakhye gātuvittamo bhava ||
  • sa no madānām pata indo devapsarā asi
    • sa - This word translates to "may he" or "let him." It is an invocation for divine presence.
    • no - This means "us" or "our," indicating a collective appeal.
    • madānām - This refers to "intoxication" or "joy," often associated with divine ecstasy or bliss.
    • pata - This means "path" or "way," suggesting guidance or direction.
    • indo - This refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and power.
    • devapsarā - This translates to "divine beings" or "celestial nymphs," representing beauty and grace.
    • asi - This means "you are," indicating the presence of the divine in the invocation.
  • sakheva sakhye gātuvittamo bhava
    • sakheva - This means "like a friend," emphasizing camaraderie and support.
    • sakhye - This translates to "in friendship," reinforcing the theme of unity and companionship.
    • gātuvittamo - This means "the best of singers" or "the most excellent in song," suggesting the importance of expression and creativity.
    • bhava - This means "become" or "may you become," indicating a wish for transformation or realization.

Conclusion:

This mantra is a beautiful invocation that seeks the blessings of divine beings, particularly Indra, to guide and inspire us towards joy and creativity. It emphasizes the importance of friendship and support in our lives, suggesting that we should strive to be the best versions of ourselves, much like the best singers who express their emotions through music. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal relationships, fostering a sense of camaraderie and support among friends and family.
  • In creative endeavors, encouraging individuals to express themselves fully and authentically.
  • In spiritual practices, invoking divine guidance and joy in our daily lives.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of joy, creativity, and friendship, urging us to seek divine inspiration in our pursuits and relationships.
