यदाद्य भागं विभजासि नृभ्य उषो देवी मार्त्यत्रा सुजाते |देवो नो अत्र सविता दामूनान अनागसो वाचति सूर्याय ||
yad adya bhāgaṁ vibhajāsi nṛbhya uṣo devi martyatrā sujāte | devo no atra savitā damūnā anāgaso vocati sūryāya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • adya: today
    • bhāgaṁ: portion or share
    • vibhajāsi: you distribute
    • nṛbhya: to humans
    • uṣo: O Dawn
    • devi: goddess
    • martyatrā: in the mortal realm
    • sujāte: well-born or auspicious
    • devo: the divine
    • no: for us
    • atra: here
    • savitā: the Sun
    • damūnā: with restraint or control
    • anāgaso: free from sin
    • vocati: calls or speaks
    • sūryāya: to the Sun
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses the divine forces, particularly the goddess of dawn, Uṣas, and the Sun, Savitā.
    • It speaks of the distribution of blessings and portions of life that are given to humanity.
    • The invocation of Uṣas signifies the arrival of new beginnings, hope, and enlightenment.
    • The mention of Savitā emphasizes the importance of the Sun as a source of life, energy, and sustenance.
    • The phrase "damūnā anāgaso" suggests that these blessings are bestowed upon those who are free from sin and act with restraint.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to seek new opportunities and embrace change, much like the dawn brings a new day.
    • Individuals may reflect on their actions and strive to live a life of integrity and virtue, aligning with the idea of being "anāgaso" or free from sin.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can inspire collective efforts towards growth and harmony, as everyone seeks to share in the blessings of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine blessings in our lives and the importance of living virtuously.
    • It serves as a reminder to appreciate the new beginnings each day brings and to act with integrity in our pursuits.
    • In a broader context, it encourages a sense of community and shared responsibility in seeking and distributing blessings among all.
