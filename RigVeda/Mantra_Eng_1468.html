यद गव्यन्ता द्वा जनā स्वर्ग यन्ता समूहसि |आविष्करिक्रद वृषणं सचाभुवं वज्रम इन्द्र सचाभुवम् ||
yad gavyantā dvā janā sva1r yantā samūhasi | āviṣ karikrad vṛṣaṇaṁ sacābhuvaṁ vajram indra sacābhuvam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • gavyantā: the cows (gavyā refers to cows, symbolizing nourishment and wealth)
    • dvā: two
    • janā: people or beings
    • sva1r: their own
    • yantā: who are moving or going
    • samūhasi: you gather or assemble
    • āviṣ: manifest or reveal
    • karikrad: the powerful or strong
    • vṛṣaṇaṁ: the bull (symbolizing strength and virility)
    • sacābhuvaṁ: that which is auspicious or beneficial
    • vajram: the thunderbolt (symbolizing power and protection)
    • indra: the king of gods, representing authority and strength
    • sacābhuvam: that which is auspicious or beneficial
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the gathering of people and the importance of community.
    • The mention of cows symbolizes wealth and nourishment, indicating that prosperity comes from collective effort.
    • The two beings moving together suggest collaboration and partnership, essential for achieving common goals.
    • The invocation of Indra, the king of gods, emphasizes the need for strength and protection in our endeavors.
    • The bull represents virility and power, suggesting that strength is necessary to overcome challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to teamwork. Just as the mantra emphasizes gathering and collaboration, successful businesses thrive on the collective efforts of their employees.
    • In community service, the idea of coming together for a common cause reflects the essence of this mantra. For instance, local organizations often unite to address social issues, showcasing the power of collective action.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra highlights the importance of partnership. Couples who work together towards shared goals often find greater success and fulfillment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity, strength, and collaboration in achieving prosperity and overcoming challenges.
    • It teaches us that by coming together, we can manifest our goals and protect our interests, much like the gathering of people and the invocation of divine strength.
    • In real life, whether in personal relationships, community efforts, or professional settings, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide us towards success and fulfillment.
