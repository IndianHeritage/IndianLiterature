यज्ञैर इषूः संनममानो अग्ने वाचा शल्यां अशनिभिर दिहानः | ताभिर विध्य हृदये यातुधानान प्रतीको बाहून प्रति भन्ध्य एषाम् ||
yajñair iṣūḥ saṁnamamāno agne vācā śalyām̐ aśanibhir dihānaḥ | tābhir vidhya hṛdaye yātudhānān pratīco bāhūn prati bhaṅdhy eṣām ||
  • yajñair: This word refers to sacrifices or offerings made to the deities. In real life, this can be likened to acts of charity or service that individuals perform for the betterment of society.
  • iṣūḥ: This translates to arrows, symbolizing the focus and direction of one's efforts. In a modern context, it can represent goals or aspirations that one aims to achieve.
  • saṁnamamāno: This means to be devoted or dedicated. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who commit themselves to their work, family, or community.
  • agne: Referring to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, this can be related to the passion and drive that fuels our endeavors.
  • vācā: This means speech or words. It emphasizes the power of communication in influencing and guiding others, akin to how leaders inspire their followers.
  • śalyām̐: This word means obstacles or challenges. In life, everyone faces difficulties that must be overcome to achieve success.
  • aśanibhir: This refers to the swift or powerful. It can be associated with the urgency and determination needed to tackle life's challenges.
  • dihānaḥ: This means to be nourished or sustained. It highlights the importance of support systems, such as family and friends, in our lives.
  • tābhir: This translates to 'by them', referring to the tools or means we have at our disposal to achieve our goals.
  • vidhya: This means to know or to understand. Knowledge is crucial in making informed decisions in life.
  • hṛdaye: This translates to the heart, symbolizing emotions and intentions. It reminds us that our motivations should come from a place of sincerity.
  • yātudhānān: This refers to malevolent beings or obstacles. In life, this can represent negative influences or distractions that hinder our progress.
  • pratīco: This means towards the west, symbolizing the direction of challenges or adversities we face.
  • bāhūn: This translates to arms or strength, representing the power we possess to confront and overcome difficulties.
  • prati bhaṅdhy: This means to break or overcome. It signifies the act of overcoming obstacles in our path.
  • eṣām: This means 'of these', referring to the challenges and adversities mentioned earlier.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of dedication, knowledge, and the power of communication in overcoming life's challenges. It teaches us that through our efforts (yajñair) and focus (iṣūḥ), we can navigate obstacles (śalyām̐) with the support of our community (dihānaḥ) and the strength we possess (bāhūn). In real life, this mantra serves as a reminder to stay committed to our goals, seek knowledge, and communicate effectively to inspire and lead others, ultimately helping us to break through the barriers we encounter on our journey. Its significance lies in its universal applicability, encouraging individuals to harness their inner strength and work collaboratively towards a common purpose.
