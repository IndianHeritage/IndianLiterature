स्वध्वराऽकरति जातवेदाऽयक्षद् देवांऽमृतान् पिप्रयाच् च ||
svadhvarā karati jātavedā yakṣad devām̐ amṛtān piprayac ca ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Svadhvarā: This term can be interpreted as "self-formed" or "self-created." It signifies the inherent nature of something that arises from within.
    • Karati: This means "to do" or "to make." It indicates action or creation.
    • Jātavedā: Referring to the "one who knows the origin" or "the one who is born of knowledge." This term is often associated with Agni, the fire god, who is seen as a source of wisdom and enlightenment.
    • Yakṣad: This can be understood as "to grant" or "to bestow." It implies a sense of giving or providing.
    • Devām̐: This translates to "gods" or "divine beings." It represents the higher powers or deities in the Vedic tradition.
    • Amṛtān: Meaning "immortal" or "nectar," this word signifies something that is eternal and beyond the cycle of life and death.
    • Piprayac: This means "to nourish" or "to sustain." It indicates the act of providing sustenance or support.
  • Overall Meaning: The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine, invoking the self-created energy (Agni) to bestow immortality and nourishment upon the gods and the worshippers. It emphasizes the importance of inner strength and the divine connection that sustains life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, the concept of "svadhvarā" can be seen in individuals who cultivate their inner strengths and talents, leading to self-actualization.
    • In community service, "yakṣad devām̐" can be reflected in acts of charity where individuals or organizations provide support to those in need, embodying the spirit of giving.
    • The idea of "amṛtān" can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, which is often seen as a path to achieving a lasting legacy or impact in the world.
  • Conclusion: This mantra highlights the significance of self-creation, divine connection, and the nourishment of both the body and spirit. It serves as a reminder of the importance of inner strength and the role of divine forces in our lives. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek their inner potential, contribute positively to society, and strive for wisdom that transcends the temporal nature of existence.
