विष्वे हि त्वा सजोषसो देवासो दूतम अकृत || श्रुष्टि देव प्रथमो यज्ञियो भुवः ||
viśve hi tvā sajoṣaso devāso dūtam akrata | śruṣṭī deva prathamo yajñiyo bhuvaḥ ||
  • Translation: "Indeed, all the gods have made you their messenger; the creation is the first offering of the gods."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • viśve: all, universal
    • hi: indeed, surely
    • tvā: you
    • sajoṣaso: of the united, harmonious
    • devāso: gods, divine beings
    • dūtam: messenger, envoy
    • akrata: have made, created
    • śruṣṭī: creation, manifestation
    • deva: god, divine
    • prathamo: first, primary
    • yajñiyo: offering, sacrifice
    • bhuvaḥ: of the earth, worldly
  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the role of a messenger or envoy in the divine order. It suggests that the gods have chosen a particular entity (which can be interpreted as a person, a community, or even nature) to convey their messages and intentions.
    • The phrase "all the gods" indicates a universal consensus among divine beings, highlighting the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving a common goal.
    • The term "creation" signifies the foundational act of existence, suggesting that the act of creation itself is an offering to the divine. This can be likened to how in many cultures, the act of creating art, music, or even nurturing relationships is seen as a sacred offering to the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader can be seen as a messenger of the company's vision and values, uniting the team towards a common goal. Just as the gods have a messenger, organizations often rely on individuals to communicate and embody their mission.
    • In personal relationships, individuals often act as messengers of love and support, fostering harmony and understanding among family and friends. This reflects the idea of being a conduit for positive energy and intentions.
    • In environmental stewardship, individuals and communities can be seen as messengers of nature, advocating for the protection and preservation of the earth. Their actions can be viewed as offerings to the planet, promoting sustainability and harmony with the environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of acting as a messenger of goodwill and harmony in our daily lives.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize their role in the larger tapestry of existence, where every action can be seen as an offering to the divine.
    • By embodying the principles of unity, communication, and creation, we can contribute positively to our communities and the world at large, fulfilling our purpose as messengers of peace and understanding.
