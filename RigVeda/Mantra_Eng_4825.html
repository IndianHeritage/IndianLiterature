इन्द्र प्राणः पुरैतिव पश्य प्र नो नय प्रतरं वस्यो अच्छ | भवासुपारो अतिपारयो नो भवासुनीतिर उता वामनीतिः ||
indra pra ṇaḥ puraeteva paśya pra no naya prataraṁ vasyo accha | bhavā supāro atipārayo no bhavā sunītir uta vāmanītiḥ ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Pra ṇaḥ: This term can be interpreted as 'life force' or 'vital energy,' indicating the essence of life and vitality.
  • Puraeteva: Means 'as if dwelling in a fortified place,' suggesting security and stability.
  • Paśya: Translates to 'see' or 'perceive,' urging one to observe and understand the world around them.
  • Pra no naya: This phrase means 'lead us,' indicating a desire for guidance and direction in life.
  • Prataraṁ: Refers to 'crossing over' or 'transcending,' symbolizing overcoming obstacles.
  • Vasyo: Means 'to be controlled' or 'to be subdued,' indicating mastery over one's desires and challenges.
  • Accha: Translates to 'good' or 'well,' suggesting positivity and well-being.
  • Bhavā: Refers to 'existence' or 'being,' emphasizing the importance of life and its experiences.
  • Supāro: Means 'well-prepared' or 'well-structured,' indicating readiness for challenges.
  • Atipārayo: Translates to 'beyond measure,' suggesting abundance and limitless potential.
  • Sunītir: Means 'good guidance' or 'wise counsel,' emphasizing the need for wisdom in decision-making.
  • Uta: Translates to 'or' or 'also,' indicating alternatives or additional considerations.
  • Vāmanītiḥ: Refers to 'the one who is small' or 'diminutive,' which can symbolize humility and the importance of modesty.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance and strength from higher powers, like Indra, while navigating life's challenges. It encourages individuals to observe their surroundings, overcome obstacles, and strive for mastery over their desires. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership roles, personal development, and decision-making processes. By embodying the qualities of preparedness, wisdom, and humility, one can achieve a balanced and fulfilling life. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek support and guidance while remaining grounded and aware of one's surroundings.
