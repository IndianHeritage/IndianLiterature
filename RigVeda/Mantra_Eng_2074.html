मन्त्र: विश्वा उता त्वया वयं धाराऽउदन्या इव | अतिगाहेमहि द्विषः ||
viśvā uta tvayā vayaṁ dhārā udanyā iva | ati gāhemahi dviṣaḥ ||
  • viśvā uta tvayā vayaṁ: This phrase can be translated to "We are all connected through you." Here, "viśvā" refers to the universe or all beings, "tvayā" means "through you," and "vayaṁ" means "we." This emphasizes the interconnectedness of all life and the divine presence that binds us together.
  • dhārā udanyā iva: This translates to "like the flowing waters." "Dhārā" means "flow" or "stream," and "udanyā" refers to "uplifting" or "nourishing." This imagery evokes the idea of life-giving forces that sustain us, much like water sustains all living beings.
  • ati gāhemahi dviṣaḥ: This can be interpreted as "we strive to overcome our enemies." "Ati" means "beyond" or "over," "gāhemahi" translates to "we strive" or "we seek," and "dviṣaḥ" means "enemies" or "hatred." This line encourages us to rise above negativity and conflict, seeking harmony instead.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of unity, nourishment, and the pursuit of peace. It reminds us that we are all part of a larger whole and that our actions should reflect this interconnectedness.
  • Real-life Application: In today's world, where division and conflict are prevalent, this mantra serves as a reminder to foster connections with others. For example, in community service, individuals come together to support those in need, embodying the idea of being "connected through you." Similarly, in environmental efforts, recognizing our shared responsibility to care for the planet reflects the "flowing waters" that nourish all life.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encourages us to embrace our interconnectedness, seek nourishment from one another, and strive for peace over conflict. By applying these principles in our daily lives, we can create a more harmonious and supportive world.
