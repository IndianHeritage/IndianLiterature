त्वं नो अग्नि पितरोर उपस्थ आ देवो देवेष्व अनवद्य जागृविः |तनूकृत् बोधि प्रमतिश्च कारवे त्वं कल्याण वसु विश्वम् ओपिषे ||
tvaṁ no agne pitror upastha ā devo deveṣv anavadya jāgṛviḥ | tanūkṛd bodhi pramatiś ca kārave tvaṁ kalyāṇa vasu viśvam opiṣe ||
  • tvaṁ no agne: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni." Agni is the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, fire is essential for cooking, warmth, and rituals, representing the transformative power of energy in our lives.
  • pitror upastha: This means "in the presence of our ancestors." It emphasizes the importance of honoring and remembering our forebears. In many cultures, including modern society, rituals and ceremonies are conducted to pay respect to ancestors, highlighting the continuity of family and heritage.
  • ā devo deveṣv anavadya jāgṛviḥ: This translates to "O divine one, who is beyond reproach among the gods." It signifies the purity and sanctity of Agni. In our lives, we often seek purity in our actions and thoughts, striving to be virtuous and beyond reproach in our dealings with others.
  • tanūkṛd bodhi pramatiś ca kārave: This means "may you grant us understanding and wisdom." It reflects the human quest for knowledge and insight. In today's world, education and self-improvement are paramount, as individuals seek to gain wisdom to navigate complex life situations.
  • tvaṁ kalyāṇa vasu viśvam opiṣe: This translates to "You, who are the source of all auspiciousness and wealth." It highlights the belief that divine blessings lead to prosperity and well-being. In real life, people often seek blessings for success in their endeavors, whether in business, relationships, or personal growth.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, wisdom, and prosperity while honoring our ancestors. Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of purity in our actions, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during rituals, personal reflections, or moments of decision-making, reminding us to seek higher wisdom and blessings in our daily lives.
