यां वां सन्ति पुरुष्पृहो नियुतो दाशुषे नराः | इन्द्राग्नī ताभिर आगतम् ||
yā vāṁ santi puruspṛho niyuto dāśuṣe narā | indrāgnī tābhir ā gatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yā: who
    • vāṁ: your
    • santi: are
    • puruspṛho: desires of men
    • niyuto: directed
    • dāśuṣe: to the giver
    • narā: men
    • indrāgnī: Indra and Agni (the deities)
    • tābhir: by these
    • ā: to
    • gatam: come
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the desires of men and how they are directed towards the divine, specifically to Indra and Agni.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking blessings from these deities for the fulfillment of human desires.
    • Indra represents strength, power, and rain, while Agni symbolizes fire, transformation, and energy.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be related to individuals seeking success in their careers. Just as men direct their desires towards Indra and Agni, people often seek guidance and blessings from mentors or higher powers to achieve their goals.
    • For instance, a student may pray for success in exams, directing their aspirations towards a higher power, akin to how the mantra directs desires to the deities.
    • In a community setting, people may come together to perform rituals or prayers for prosperity and well-being, invoking the blessings of Indra and Agni for collective success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the connection between human desires and divine blessings, highlighting the importance of seeking higher guidance in fulfilling one's aspirations.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal ambitions to community welfare, reminding individuals to align their desires with a higher purpose.
    • Ultimately, it teaches that while human effort is essential, seeking divine support can lead to greater fulfillment and success.
