मन्त्र: एते सोमाऽति वाराण्य अव्यā दिव्यā न कोशासो अभ्रवर्षाः |वृथा समुद्रं सिन्धवो न नीचिः सुतासो अभि कलशां अषृग्न ||
ete somā ati vārāṇy avyā divyā na kośāso abhravarṣāḥ | vṛthā samudraṁ sindhavo na nīcīḥ sutāso abhi kalaśām̐ asṛgran ||
  • Translation: "These are the divine, powerful, and intoxicating (soma) drinks; they do not fall short like the clouds that do not rain. The rivers do not flow downwards; they rise up to the vessels (kalasha) filled with the essence of life."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ete: these
    • somā: soma, a sacred drink in Vedic tradition
    • ati: very, extremely
    • vārāṇy: powerful, strong
    • avyā: divine, celestial
    • na: not
    • kośāso: like the clouds
    • abhravarṣāḥ: that do not rain
    • vṛthā: in vain, futile
    • samudraṁ: ocean
    • sindhavo: rivers
    • na: not
    • nīcīḥ: flow downwards
    • sutāso: those who are born from the essence
    • abhi: towards
    • kalaśām̐: vessels, pots
    • asṛgran: filled with, overflowing
  • Explanation:
    • The mantra speaks of the divine nature of soma, which is not just a drink but symbolizes the essence of life and spiritual enlightenment.
    • The phrase "do not fall short like the clouds that do not rain" suggests that true spiritual experiences and knowledge are abundant and fulfilling, unlike empty promises or unfulfilled desires.
    • The rivers rising up to the vessels indicate the flow of knowledge and wisdom that nourishes the soul, rather than being a mere physical journey.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the pursuit of knowledge and spiritual growth can be likened to the quest for soma. Just as soma nourishes the body and spirit, education and self-awareness enrich our lives.
    • Consider a person who seeks fulfillment through material possessions. This mantra reminds us that true satisfaction comes from within and through meaningful experiences, not from external sources.
    • In a community setting, sharing knowledge and wisdom can be seen as the rivers flowing into the vessels, where collective growth and enlightenment occur.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine knowledge and spiritual fulfillment rather than being distracted by superficial desires.
    • It serves as a reminder that true richness in life comes from wisdom and understanding, which can elevate our existence and connect us with the divine.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to engage in lifelong learning, self-reflection, and community sharing, fostering a culture of growth and enlightenment.
