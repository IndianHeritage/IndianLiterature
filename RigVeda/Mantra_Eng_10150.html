इति वाऽइति मे मनो गाम अश्वं सनुयाम इति |कुवित सोमस्यापाम इति ||
iti vā iti me mano gām aśvaṁ sanuyām iti | kuvit somasyāpām iti ||
  • iti vā iti me mano: This phrase can be translated as "Thus, indeed, my mind." It signifies the importance of directing one's thoughts and intentions. In real life, this can be related to the practice of mindfulness, where individuals focus their thoughts on the present moment, enhancing clarity and purpose in their actions.
  • gām aśvaṁ sanuyām iti: This translates to "I lead the horse to the village." The horse symbolizes strength, energy, and the ability to achieve goals. Leading the horse to the village can be seen as guiding one's efforts towards a productive outcome. For example, in a business context, this could represent the journey of a startup founder leading their team towards a successful launch.
  • kuvit somasyāpām iti: This phrase can be interpreted as "What is the essence of the soma?" Soma is often associated with vitality and the elixir of life. This part of the mantra encourages individuals to seek the deeper meaning and essence of life experiences. In everyday life, this could relate to the pursuit of knowledge and understanding, such as a student striving to grasp the core concepts of their studies rather than just memorizing facts.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of focused intention, the journey towards achieving goals, and the quest for deeper understanding. In real life, it serves as a reminder to be mindful of our thoughts, to lead our efforts towards meaningful outcomes, and to seek the essence of our experiences. This can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, professional endeavors, and educational pursuits, guiding individuals to live with purpose and clarity.
