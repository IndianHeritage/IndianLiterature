स पितृयाण्य आयुधानि विद्वान इन्द्रेषित आप्त्यो अभ्य आयुध्यत |त्रिशीर्षाणं सप्तरश्मिं जगहन्वान त्वाष्ट्रस्य चिन् निः ससृजे तृतो गाः ||
sa pitryāṇy āyudhāni vidvān indreṣita āptyo abhy ayudhyat | triśīrṣāṇaṁ saptaraśmiṁ jaghanvān tvāṣṭrasya cin niḥ sasṛje trito gāḥ ||
  • sa pitryāṇy āyudhāni vidvān: This phrase can be translated as "the wise one, who knows the weapons of the ancestors." Here, "pitryāṇy" refers to the ancestors or forefathers, and "āyudhāni" means weapons or tools. In a real-life context, this can be understood as the knowledge and skills passed down through generations, which can be seen in various professions where traditional techniques are valued, such as in crafts, martial arts, or even in family businesses.
  • indreṣita āptyo abhy ayudhyat: This translates to "having been directed by Indra, he fought for the purpose." Indra is often seen as a deity representing strength and power. This line emphasizes the importance of divine guidance in one's endeavors. In modern life, this can be related to seeking mentorship or guidance from experienced individuals before undertaking significant challenges, such as starting a new project or entering a competitive field.
  • triśīrṣāṇaṁ saptaraśmiṁ: This can be interpreted as "the one with three heads and seven rays." The imagery here can symbolize multifaceted wisdom and the ability to see things from different perspectives. In practical terms, this can be likened to leaders or innovators who approach problems with a holistic view, considering various angles before making decisions, much like how successful entrepreneurs analyze market trends.
  • jaghanvān tvāṣṭrasya cin niḥ sasṛje trito gāḥ: This translates to "the one who created the third cow of Tvaṣṭra, the divine craftsman." Tvaṣṭra is associated with creation and craftsmanship. The "third cow" can symbolize abundance and prosperity. In real life, this can be seen in the context of innovation and creativity, where new ideas or products (the 'cows') are developed to meet the needs of society, leading to economic growth and sustainability.

  • Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, guidance, creativity, and the importance of ancestral knowledge. Its significance lies in the reminder that success is often a combination of inherited skills, divine guidance, and innovative thinking. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge from their predecessors, look for mentorship, and approach challenges with a multifaceted perspective. This holistic approach can lead to personal and professional growth, fostering a sense of community and continuity through generations.
