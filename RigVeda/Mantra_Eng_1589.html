दिदृक्षेण्यः परि काष्ठासु जेन्य ईळेन्यो महो अर्भाय जीवसे |पुरुत्रा यद अभवत सूर अहैभ्यो गर्भेभ्यो मगवाऽविश्वदर्शतः ||
didṛkṣeṇyaḥ pari kāṣṭhāsu jenya īḻenyo maho arbhāya jīvase | purutrā yad abhavat sūr ahaibhyo garbhebhyo maghavā viśvadarśataḥ ||
  • Didṛkṣeṇyaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "with the desire to see" or "with the intention of perceiving." It emphasizes the importance of awareness and observation in our lives.
  • Pari kāṣṭhāsu: This phrase translates to "around the wood" or "in the surroundings." It suggests a connection to nature and the environment, highlighting the significance of our surroundings in shaping our experiences.
  • Jenya: Meaning "born" or "produced," this word signifies creation and the emergence of new life or ideas.
  • Īḻenyo: This term can be understood as "to be nourished" or "to be sustained," indicating the necessity of support and sustenance in life.
  • Mahā arbhāya: Translated as "great offspring" or "significant progeny," this phrase points to the importance of legacy and the impact of our actions on future generations.
  • Jīvase: Meaning "for life" or "to live," it emphasizes the essence of existence and the vitality of living beings.
  • Purutrā: This term means "from all sides" or "in every direction," suggesting a holistic approach to life and the interconnectedness of all things.
  • Yad abhavat: Translated as "that which has happened," it reflects on the past and the lessons learned from experiences.
  • Sūr: Meaning "hero" or "brave one," this word signifies strength and courage in facing challenges.
  • Ahaibhyo: This term can be interpreted as "from the strong" or "from the powerful," indicating the influence of strength in our lives.
  • Garbhebhyō: Meaning "from the wombs" or "from the sources," it highlights the origins of life and the nurturing aspect of creation.
  • Maghavā: This term refers to "the mighty" or "the powerful," emphasizing the importance of strength and capability in achieving goals.
  • Viśvadarśataḥ: Translated as "from the universal vision," it suggests a broader perspective and understanding of life, encouraging us to see beyond our immediate surroundings.

Conclusion: This mantra from the RigVeda encapsulates the essence of life, creation, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It encourages us to observe our surroundings, learn from our experiences, and recognize the strength within ourselves and others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as environmental awareness, personal growth, and the importance of nurturing relationships. By embracing the teachings of this mantra, we can foster a deeper understanding of our place in the world and the impact of our actions on future generations.
