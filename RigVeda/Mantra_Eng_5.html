अग्निः होतार् कविक्रतुः सत्यश् चित्रश्रवस्तमः |देवो देवैभिर् आगमत् ||
agnir hotā kavikratuḥ satyaś citraśravastamaḥ | devo devebhir ā gamat ||
  • Agnir: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • Hotā: This term means "the one who offers" or "the priest." It signifies the role of a mediator between the divine and the human realms.
  • Kavikratuḥ: "Kavi" means "poet" or "wise one," and "kratu" refers to "intellect" or "will." Together, it implies a being of great wisdom and creative power.
  • Satyaś: This translates to "truth" or "truthful." It emphasizes the importance of honesty and integrity in one's actions and thoughts.
  • Citraśravastamaḥ: This compound word means "of diverse fame" or "renowned for various qualities." It highlights the multifaceted nature of divinity and excellence.
  • Devo: This means "god" or "deity," indicating the divine aspect of the mantra.
  • Devebhir: This term refers to "by the gods" or "among the deities," suggesting a collective divine presence.
  • Ā gamat: This means "has come" or "arrived," indicating the manifestation of divine energy or blessings.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Agni, the fire god, who is called upon to bring wisdom, truth, and divine blessings. It emphasizes the role of the priest as a mediator who offers prayers and sacrifices to the divine, seeking the presence of the gods in the rituals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: The invocation of Agni is significant in Hindu rituals, where fire is considered a purifying element. For example, during weddings, the couple circles the sacred fire, symbolizing their commitment and the presence of divine blessings.
  • Personal Growth: The qualities of truthfulness (Satyaś) and wisdom (Kavikratuḥ) can be applied in daily life. Individuals can strive to be honest in their dealings and seek knowledge to make informed decisions.
  • Community and Collaboration: The collective aspect of the mantra (Devebhir) can inspire teamwork and collaboration in communities, emphasizing the importance of working together for a common goal.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine energy through rituals, the pursuit of truth and wisdom, and the recognition of the multifaceted nature of divinity. It serves as a reminder to integrate these values into our daily lives, fostering personal growth and community harmony.
