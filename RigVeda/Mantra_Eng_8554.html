पवस्वेन्दो पवमानो महोभिः कणिक्रदत परि वाराण्य अर्षा |क्रीळञ्ज चंवोऽर आ विश पूयमान इन्द्रं ते रसो मदिरो ममत्तु ||
pavasvendo pavamāno mahobhiḥ kanikradat pari vārāṇy arṣa | krīḻañ camvo3r ā viśa pūyamāna indraṁ te raso madiro mamattu ||
  • pavasvendo: This term can be broken down into two parts: "pava" meaning "pure" or "sacred," and "svendo," which refers to "the one who moves" or "the wind." Together, it signifies the divine essence of purity and movement, akin to the refreshing breeze that brings clarity and rejuvenation in our lives.
  • pavamāno: This word translates to "the one who is being purified" or "the one who is in the process of becoming pure." It emphasizes the ongoing journey of purification, much like how individuals strive for personal growth and moral integrity in their lives.
  • mahobhiḥ: This term means "with great strength" or "with might." It highlights the power and energy that accompany the process of purification, similar to how determination and resilience are essential in overcoming life's challenges.
  • kanikradat: This word can be interpreted as "the one who roars" or "the one who makes a sound." It symbolizes the expression of strength and vitality, akin to how individuals assert themselves in various situations, making their presence known.
  • pari vārāṇy arṣa: This phrase translates to "surrounding the sacred places." It signifies the importance of protecting and honoring sacred spaces, much like how communities come together to preserve cultural heritage and natural environments.
  • krīḻañ: This term means "to play" or "to engage in joyful activities." It reflects the essence of joy and celebration in life, similar to how people gather for festivals and communal events to foster connections and happiness.
  • camvo3r: This word can be understood as "the one who is full of vigor" or "the one who is energetic." It represents the vitality that drives individuals to pursue their passions and ambitions, much like athletes who train tirelessly to achieve their goals.
  • ā viśa pūyamāna: This phrase translates to "to the one who is being honored." It signifies respect and reverence towards those who have achieved greatness or contributed positively to society, akin to how we celebrate leaders and role models in our communities.
  • indraṁ: This term refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing power, authority, and protection. It represents the aspiration for strength and guidance in our lives, much like how we look up to leaders for direction and support.
  • te raso madiro: This phrase means "your essence is intoxicating." It signifies the allure and charm of divine qualities, similar to how people are drawn to inspiring figures who embody wisdom and compassion.
  • mamattu: This term translates to "may it be mine" or "let it be my possession." It expresses a desire for the qualities and blessings being invoked, much like how individuals seek to embody positive traits and experiences in their lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of purification, strength, joy, and reverence towards the divine. It serves as a reminder of the importance of personal growth, community, and the pursuit of higher ideals. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during personal reflection, community gatherings, or rituals that honor the sacred. By embodying the qualities represented in this mantra, individuals can strive for a more fulfilling and meaningful existence, fostering connections with themselves, others, and the divine.
