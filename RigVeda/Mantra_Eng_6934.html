यः कृतदिद् वि योन्यं त्रिशोकाय गिरिम पृथुम् |गोभ्यो गातुं निरेतवे ||
yaḥ kṛntad id vi yonyaṁ triśokāya girim pṛthum | gobhyo gātuṁ niretave ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaḥ: who
    • kṛntat: has cut or severed
    • id: this
    • vi: apart or asunder
    • yonyaṁ: the womb or source
    • triśokāya: for the three sorrows
    • girim: the mountain
    • pṛthum: vast or great
    • gobhyo: from the cows or the wealth
    • gātuṁ: to go or to move
    • niretave: to the one who is unrestrained or free
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine entity or force that has the power to sever ties or connections, particularly those that bind us to sorrow.
    • The reference to "the three sorrows" can be interpreted as the sorrows of the past, present, and future, which often plague human existence.
    • The "mountain" symbolizes strength and stability, suggesting that this divine force provides a solid foundation amidst life's challenges.
    • The mention of "cows" can be seen as a metaphor for wealth, nourishment, and prosperity, indicating that this divine presence also brings abundance.
    • Finally, the phrase "to the one who is unrestrained" suggests a connection to freedom and liberation from the burdens of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In moments of personal crisis, such as the loss of a loved one, individuals often seek ways to sever their emotional ties to the pain. This mantra can serve as a reminder of the possibility of healing and moving forward.
    • In a professional context, when facing overwhelming stress or challenges, one might reflect on the strength of the "mountain" to find resilience and stability in their work environment.
    • During times of financial hardship, the invocation of abundance through the metaphor of "cows" can inspire individuals to seek new opportunities and pathways to prosperity.
    • In spiritual practices, this mantra can be used as a meditative focus to cultivate a sense of freedom from the constraints of worldly sorrows.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of liberation from sorrow and the pursuit of abundance and strength.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals to seek freedom from their burdens and to embrace the strength that comes from within.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a powerful reminder that, despite the challenges we face, there is always a path to healing, stability, and prosperity.
