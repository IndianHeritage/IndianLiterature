इन्द्रस्य सोम राधसे पुनानो हार्दि चोदय |ऋतस्य योनिम आसदं ||
indrasya soma rādhase punāno hārdi codaya | ṛtasya yonim āsadam ||
  • Indrasya: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Soma: A sacred drink in Vedic rituals, representing divine inspiration and the essence of life.
  • Rādhase: This term can be interpreted as 'to the one who is dear' or 'beloved', indicating a connection to love and devotion.
  • Punāno: Means 'purifying' or 'refreshing', suggesting a renewal or cleansing process.
  • Hārdi: Translates to 'in the heart', indicating the emotional or spiritual center of a person.
  • Codaya: Means 'inspire' or 'urge', suggesting a call to action or motivation.
  • Ṛtasya: Refers to 'truth' or 'cosmic order', emphasizing the importance of righteousness and harmony in the universe.
  • Yonim: Means 'womb' or 'source', indicating the origin or foundation of something.
  • Āsadam: Means 'to sit' or 'to dwell', suggesting a state of being or existence.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for inspiration and purification from the divine source, urging the heart to align with truth and righteousness. It emphasizes the importance of connecting with the divine essence (Soma) to gain strength and clarity in life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use this mantra during meditation to seek inspiration and clarity in their personal and professional lives.
  • Leadership: Leaders can reflect on the qualities of Indra, seeking to embody strength and righteousness in their decision-making processes.
  • Emotional Healing: The emphasis on the heart suggests that this mantra can be used for emotional healing, encouraging individuals to purify their thoughts and feelings.
  • Community Building: In a community setting, this mantra can inspire collective action towards truth and harmony, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine inspiration and the pursuit of truth. It serves as a reminder to align our hearts with righteousness and to seek purification in our thoughts and actions. By incorporating this mantra into daily life, individuals can cultivate a deeper connection with their inner selves and the universe, leading to a more harmonious existence.
