आ याहि शश्वद उषता ययाथेन्द्र महा मनसा सोमपेयम् |उप ब्रह्माणि श्रिणव इमा नो 'था ते यज्ञस तन्वे3 वयो धात ||
ā yāhi śaśvad uśatā yayāthendra mahā manasā somapeyam | upa brahmāṇi śṛṇava imā no 'thā te yajñas tanve3 vayo dhāt ||
  • Transliteration: ā yāhi śaśvad uśatā yayāthendra mahā manasā somapeyam | upa brahmāṇi śṛṇava imā no 'thā te yajñas tanve3 vayo dhāt ||
  • Meaning:
    • ā yāhi: "Come here" or "approach us."
    • śaśvad uśatā: "with everlasting well-being" or "with eternal prosperity."
    • yayāthendra: "by which, O Indra," referring to the deity Indra, who is often associated with strength and power.
    • mahā manasā: "with great intellect" or "with profound understanding."
    • somapeyam: "may we drink the soma," where soma is a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight.
    • upa brahmāṇi: "approaching the divine" or "to the ultimate reality."
    • śṛṇava: "we listen" or "we hear."
    • imā no 'thā: "these offerings of ours" or "these rituals of ours."
    • te yajñas: "your sacrifices" or "your worship."
    • tanve3 vayo dhāt: "may they be established in our bodies" or "may they reside within us."
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a call for divine presence and blessings, inviting the deities, particularly Indra, to come and bestow prosperity and wisdom upon the worshippers.
    • The phrase "Come here with everlasting well-being" emphasizes the desire for continuous prosperity and health, which is a universal aspiration in human life.
    • By invoking Indra, the mantra acknowledges the importance of strength and intellect in achieving one's goals, suggesting that divine support is essential for success.
    • The reference to drinking soma symbolizes the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, akin to how people today seek education and experiences that enrich their lives.
    • Listening to the divine and participating in rituals reflects the human need for connection with something greater than oneself, a theme prevalent in various cultures and religions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and blessings in our lives, particularly in our endeavors for prosperity and wisdom.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to moments of decision-making, where individuals seek clarity and strength, such as during significant life changes, career choices, or personal challenges.
    • Moreover, it highlights the value of community and collective rituals, reminding us that we are not alone in our pursuits and that support from others, as well as from the divine, is crucial.
    • Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate a mindset of openness to divine influence and to actively engage in practices that foster well-being and understanding in our lives.
