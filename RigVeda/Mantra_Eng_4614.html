तं पृच्छन्ती वज्रहस्तं रथेष्टाम इन्द्रं वेपी वक्वरी यस्य नू गीः |तुविग्राभं तुविकूर्मिं रभोदां गातुम इषे नक्षते तुम्रम accha ||
tam pṛcchantī vajrahastaṁ ratheṣṭhām indraṁ vepī vakvarī yasya nū gīḥ | tuvigrābhaṁ tuvikūrmiṁ rabhodāṁ gātum iṣe nakṣate tumram accha ||
  • tam pṛcchantī: This phrase can be translated as "they who inquire" or "those who seek." It emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and understanding.
  • vajrahastaṁ: This means "one with a thunderbolt in hand," referring to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and power.
  • ratheṣṭhām: This translates to "seated in the chariot," indicating a position of control and guidance, often associated with the journey of life.
  • indraṁ: Refers to Indra himself, representing not just a deity but also the qualities of leadership, bravery, and protection.
  • vepī: This word means "trembling" or "shaking," which can symbolize the dynamic nature of life and the challenges one faces.
  • vakvarī: This can be interpreted as "the one who speaks," highlighting the importance of communication and expression in human interactions.
  • yasya nū gīḥ: Translated as "whose song is indeed," this phrase suggests the power of words and music, which can inspire and uplift.
  • tuvigrābhaṁ: This means "with great strength," indicating the need for resilience and fortitude in the face of adversity.
  • tuvikūrmiṁ: This can be understood as "with great effort," emphasizing the importance of hard work and determination.
  • rabhodāṁ: This translates to "to seize," suggesting the act of taking control of one's destiny.
  • gātum: Meaning "to go" or "to move," it signifies progress and the journey of life.
  • iṣe: This word means "to the one who is powerful," referring to the divine or higher forces that guide us.
  • nakṣate: This translates to "the stars," symbolizing destiny and the cosmic order.
  • tumram accha: This phrase can be interpreted as "may you be well," a blessing for health and prosperity.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking knowledge, strength, and guidance in life. It emphasizes the importance of resilience, communication, and the pursuit of one's goals. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, leadership, and overcoming challenges. For instance, in a professional setting, one might embody the qualities of Indra by leading a team with strength and clarity, while also encouraging open communication and collaboration. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to harness our inner strength and seek guidance from higher powers as we navigate the complexities of life.
