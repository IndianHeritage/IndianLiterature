देवानां युगे प्रथमेषटः सद अजायत |तद आशा अन्व अजायन्त तद उत्तानपदस परी ||
devānāṁ yuge prathame 'sataḥ sad ajāyata | tad āśā anv ajāyanta tad uttānapadas pari ||
  • devānāṁ: This word translates to "of the gods." It signifies the divine beings or deities that are revered in Vedic literature.
  • yuge: This means "in the age" or "in the era." It refers to a specific period in time, often associated with cosmic cycles.
  • prathame: This translates to "first" or "initial." It indicates the beginning of something significant.
  • sataḥ: This means "of a hundred" or "of many." It suggests abundance or a multitude.
  • sad: This word means "truth" or "reality." It emphasizes the essence of existence.
  • ajāyata: This translates to "was born" or "came into being." It signifies the emergence or creation of something.
  • tad: This means "that." It refers to something previously mentioned or significant.
  • āśā: This translates to "hope" or "desire." It signifies aspirations or expectations.
  • anv: This means "following" or "after." It indicates a sequence or continuation.
  • ajāyanta: This translates to "were born" or "came into being." It signifies the emergence of new entities or ideas.
  • tad: Again, this means "that," referring to the previously mentioned concepts.
  • uttānapadas: This translates to "the elevated paths" or "the higher realms." It signifies a state of elevation or transcendence.
  • pari: This means "around" or "surrounding." It indicates a sense of encompassing or inclusion.

The mantra speaks about the birth of divine beings in the first age, highlighting the significance of truth and the emergence of hope and aspirations. It suggests that in the beginning, a multitude of divine entities came into existence, leading to the development of higher paths and aspirations.


In real life, this mantra can be applied to the concept of new beginnings and the importance of hope and truth in our lives. For instance, when starting a new project or venture, one can draw inspiration from the idea that every significant creation is rooted in truth and hope. Just as the gods emerged in the first age, individuals can also manifest their aspirations by staying true to their values and nurturing their hopes.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the foundational aspects of existence—truth, hope, and the divine. It serves as a reminder that every new beginning is an opportunity to align with these principles, guiding us toward elevated paths in our personal and collective journeys.
