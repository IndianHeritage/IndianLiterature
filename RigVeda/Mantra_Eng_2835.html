दधिष्वा जटहरे सुतं सोमं इन्द्र वरेण्यं |तव द्युक्षास इन्दवः ||
dadhiṣvā jaṭhare sutaṁ somam indra vareṇyam | tava dyukṣāsa indavaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dadhiṣvā: "O Indra, please listen" or "O Indra, pay attention."
    • jaṭhare: "in the belly" or "in the womb."
    • sutaṁ: "the son" or "the offspring."
    • somam: "the soma," which is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals.
    • indra: "Indra," the king of gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms.
    • vareṇyam: "worthy of praise" or "to be chosen."
    • tava: "your" or "of you."
    • dyukṣāsa: "the shining" or "the bright."
    • indavaḥ: "the moon" or "the lunar."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, asking him to pay attention to the offerings made, particularly the soma, which symbolizes vitality and divine inspiration.
    • The reference to "in the belly" suggests a nurturing aspect, indicating that the offerings are meant to foster growth and prosperity.
    • Indra is invoked as a powerful deity who can bestow blessings, and the mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring divine forces in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the importance of seeking guidance and blessings from higher powers or mentors when embarking on new ventures, such as starting a business or a family.
    • The concept of nurturing, as indicated by "in the belly," can be seen in parenting, where the care and attention given to a child can lead to their successful development.
    • In rituals or ceremonies, invoking divine presence, similar to how this mantra calls upon Indra, can be a way to seek blessings for health, prosperity, and success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine connection and the role of nurturing in achieving success and prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder to honor and seek the blessings of higher powers in our endeavors, whether they are personal, familial, or professional.
    • In essence, it teaches us the value of respect, gratitude, and the importance of fostering growth in ourselves and others.
