सहस्रधारेऽव तासश्चतस्तृतीये सन्तु रजसि प्रजावतिः |चतस्रो नाभो निहिता अवो दिवो हविः भरन्त्य अमृतं घृतश्चुतः ||
sahasradhāre 'va tā asaścatas tṛtīye santu rajasi prajāvatīḥ | catasro nābho nihitā avo divo havir bharanty amṛtaṁ ghṛtaścutaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahasradhāre: One who has a thousand streams or channels.
    • va: or.
    • tā: that.
    • asaścatas: in the third (or third part).
    • tṛtīye: in the third.
    • santu: may they be.
    • rajasi: in the realm of activity or action.
    • prajāvatīḥ: may they be fruitful or productive.
    • catasro: four.
    • nābho: centers or sources.
    • nihitā: placed or established.
    • avo: from or of.
    • divo: of the heavens.
    • havir: offerings or oblations.
    • bharanty: they carry or bring.
    • amṛtaṁ: immortality or eternal essence.
    • ghṛtaścutaḥ: clarified butter or ghee that flows.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of abundance and the flow of life-giving resources.
    • The phrase "sahasradhāre" suggests a source of infinite nourishment, akin to rivers that provide water to sustain life.
    • The mention of "rajasi prajāvatīḥ" indicates the importance of action and productivity in our lives, emphasizing that our efforts should lead to fruitful outcomes.
    • The "catasro nābho" refers to the four centers or sources of energy, which can be interpreted as the four cardinal directions or the four elements that sustain life.
    • The idea of "amṛtaṁ" signifies the pursuit of immortality, not just in a physical sense but also in terms of legacy and impact on the world.
    • "ghṛtaścutaḥ" symbolizes the richness and purity of offerings, suggesting that what we give back to the universe should be of the highest quality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the concept of "sahasradhāre" can be seen in sustainable farming practices that utilize multiple water sources to ensure crops thrive.
    • In business, "rajasi prajāvatīḥ" can be related to the importance of innovation and productivity, where companies strive to create products that meet consumer needs effectively.
    • The "catasro nābho" can be reflected in holistic approaches to health, where individuals consider physical, mental, emotional, and spiritual well-being as interconnected sources of energy.
    • In personal development, the pursuit of "amṛtaṁ" can be understood as striving for a meaningful life, where one's actions contribute positively to society and leave a lasting impact.
    • In community service, "ghṛtaścutaḥ" emphasizes the importance of giving back to the community with genuine intentions and high-quality contributions, such as volunteering time or resources to help those in need.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of abundance, productivity, and the pursuit of a meaningful existence.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us to harness our resources wisely, engage in fruitful actions, and contribute positively to the world around us.
    • In real life, it encourages us to seek balance in our endeavors, ensuring that we not only strive for personal success but also uplift others and the environment.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a guiding principle for living a life that is rich in purpose, connection, and legacy.
