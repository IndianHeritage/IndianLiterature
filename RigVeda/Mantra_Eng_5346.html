युजे रथं गवेक्षणं हरिभ्यां उप ब्रह्माणि जुजुषाणम अस्तुḥ |वि बादिष्टस्य रोडसी महित्वेन्द्रो वृत्राण्य अप्रती जगहन्वान ||
yuje rathaṁ gaveṣaṇaṁ haribhyām upa brahmāṇi jujuṣāṇam asthuḥ | vi bādhiṣṭa sya rodasī mahitvendro vṛtrāṇy apratī jaghanvān ||
  • yuje rathaṁ gaveṣaṇaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "unite the chariot and the seeker." Here, the chariot symbolizes the journey of life, while the seeker represents an individual striving for knowledge and truth. In real life, this can be seen in the context of education and personal growth, where one must align their efforts (the chariot) with their goals (the seeker).
  • haribhyām upa brahmāṇi jujuṣāṇam asthuḥ: This part means "may the divine energies assist in the pursuit of the ultimate reality." The term "haribhyām" refers to divine beings or energies, while "brahmāṇi" signifies the ultimate truth or reality. In practical terms, this can be related to seeking guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders who help individuals navigate their paths toward enlightenment.
  • vi bādhiṣṭa sya rodasī mahitvendro: This translates to "may the great Indra, who is beyond obstacles, protect us." Indra is often seen as a protector and a deity of strength. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as the need for resilience and support in overcoming challenges, whether they be personal, professional, or spiritual.
  • vṛtrāṇy apratī jaghanvān: This phrase means "he who has conquered the obstacles." Vṛtra represents obstacles or challenges in life. This can be related to individuals who have faced significant hardships but have emerged victorious, serving as inspirations for others. For example, someone overcoming a serious illness or achieving success after numerous failures embodies this concept.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of aligning one's efforts with their goals, seeking divine guidance, and having the strength to overcome obstacles. In real life, it serves as a reminder that the journey toward truth and fulfillment requires both personal commitment and external support. It encourages individuals to be resilient in the face of challenges and to seek wisdom from those who have successfully navigated similar paths. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during times of personal struggle, in educational pursuits, or when embarking on new ventures, reminding us that we are not alone in our journeys.
