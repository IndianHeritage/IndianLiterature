अच्छा कविं नृमणो गा अभिष्टौ स्वरषाटां मगवन नाधमानम् |ऊतिभिस तं इषणो द्युम्नहूतौ न मायावान अब्रह्मा दस्युर अर्त ||
acchā kaviṁ nṛmaṇo gā abhiṣṭau svarṣātā maghavan nādhamānam | ūtibhis tam iṣaṇo dyumnahūtau ni māyāvān abrahmā dasyur arta ||
  • acchā kaviṁ: This phrase can be translated as "the excellent poet" or "the wise seer." In real life, this can refer to individuals who possess great wisdom and insight, such as philosophers, leaders, or artists who inspire others through their words and actions.
  • nṛmaṇo gā: This means "the song of the human." It signifies the collective expression of humanity, reflecting our shared experiences, struggles, and aspirations. For example, songs and literature that resonate with people's emotions can unite communities and foster understanding.
  • abhiṣṭau: This term refers to "the desired" or "the goal." It emphasizes the importance of having clear objectives in life. In a practical sense, setting personal or professional goals can guide individuals toward success and fulfillment.
  • svarṣātā: This word can be interpreted as "from the heavens" or "divine." It suggests that inspiration and guidance can come from higher powers or the universe. Many people find solace and direction through spirituality or meditation, seeking a connection with something greater than themselves.
  • maghavan: This term refers to "the generous one" or "the benefactor." In real life, this can be seen in acts of kindness and generosity, where individuals or organizations contribute to the well-being of others, such as charitable foundations or community service initiatives.
  • nādhamānam: This means "the one who does not diminish." It signifies resilience and the ability to maintain one's essence despite challenges. For instance, individuals who overcome adversity and continue to thrive exemplify this quality.
  • ūtibhis: This term can be translated as "with strength" or "with vigor." It highlights the importance of perseverance and determination in achieving one's goals. Athletes, for example, demonstrate this through their rigorous training and commitment to excellence.
  • tam iṣaṇo: This phrase means "that which is desired." It emphasizes the pursuit of aspirations and dreams. In everyday life, this can relate to pursuing education, career advancement, or personal growth.
  • dyumnahūtau: This can be interpreted as "the one who is praised" or "the one who brings glory." It signifies the recognition of achievements and contributions. Celebrating successes, whether personal or communal, fosters motivation and encourages others to strive for greatness.
  • ni māyāvān: This phrase means "not deluded" or "free from illusion." It emphasizes the importance of clarity and truth in one's life. In a practical sense, critical thinking and self-awareness can help individuals navigate complex situations and make informed decisions.
  • abrahmā: This term refers to "the one who is not bound by limitations." It signifies the potential for growth and expansion beyond conventional boundaries. In real life, this can be seen in innovators and visionaries who challenge the status quo and create new possibilities.
  • dasyur arta: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who brings forth wealth" or "the one who provides." It highlights the importance of abundance and prosperity in life. In practical terms, this can relate to economic growth, entrepreneurship, and the creation of opportunities for others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of wisdom, aspiration, resilience, and the pursuit of goals. Its significance lies in the encouragement to seek knowledge, set clear objectives, and act with generosity and strength. In real life, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, community service, and professional endeavors. By embodying the qualities expressed in this mantra, individuals can contribute positively to their own lives and the lives of others, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in the world.
