अतीहि मन्युṣाविणं सुषुवांसम उपारणे |इमं रातं सुतं पिब ||
atīhi manyuṣāviṇaṁ suṣuvāṁsam upāraṇe | imaṁ rātaṁ sutam piba ||
  • atīhi: This word can be interpreted as "come" or "approach." It signifies an invitation or a call to action.
  • manyuṣāviṇaṁ: This term can be broken down into "manyu" meaning "anger" or "wrath," and "ṣāviṇaṁ" which can imply "to be pacified" or "to be controlled." Together, it suggests the idea of controlling or pacifying anger.
  • suṣuvāṁsam: This word refers to "well-being" or "prosperity." It indicates a state of happiness and success.
  • upāraṇe: This can be understood as "in the gathering" or "in the assembly." It denotes a communal or collective aspect.
  • imaṁ: This word means "this," pointing to something specific that is being referred to.
  • rātaṁ: This term translates to "joy" or "delight." It signifies a state of happiness or celebration.
  • sutam: This word means "the drink" or "the essence." It can refer to something nourishing or fulfilling.
  • piba: This is a command form meaning "drink" or "consume." It encourages action towards the essence being offered.

  • Meaning of the Mantra: The mantra invites individuals to come together in a gathering to pacify anger and promote well-being. It emphasizes the importance of joy and the act of consuming the essence of happiness.
  • Real-Life Example: In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied during team-building activities. When conflicts arise, it is essential to address and pacify any anger among team members. By fostering a joyful environment and encouraging open communication, the team can work together harmoniously, leading to increased productivity and satisfaction.
  • Significance: The mantra highlights the importance of community and collective well-being. It serves as a reminder that in times of conflict or anger, coming together to share joy and positivity can lead to healing and prosperity.
  • Application in Real Life: This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as family gatherings, community events, or even in personal relationships. By focusing on joy and the essence of togetherness, individuals can create a supportive environment that nurtures growth and happiness.

  • Conclusion: The mantra "atīhi manyuṣāviṇaṁ suṣuvāṁsam upāraṇe imaṁ rātaṁ sutam piba" serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of unity, joy, and the need to pacify anger in our lives. By embracing these principles, we can foster a sense of community and well-being that enriches our personal and collective experiences.
