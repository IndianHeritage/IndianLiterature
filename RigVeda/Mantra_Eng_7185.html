वयं एनं इदाः योऽपिपेमेह वज्रीणम् |तस्मादु अद्य समना सुतं भर नूनं भूषत श्रुते ||
vayam enam idā hyo 'pīpemeha vajriṇam | tasmā u adya samanā sutam bharā nūnam bhūṣata śrute ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vayam: We
    • enam: this
    • idā: here
    • hyo: from the past
    • 'pīpemeha: we have desired
    • vajriṇam: the one who is powerful or strong
    • tasmā: therefore
    • u: and
    • adya: today
    • samanā: equal or similar
    • sutam: the son or offspring
    • bharā: may you fill or bestow
    • nūnam: certainly
    • bhūṣata: may you adorn or bless
    • śrute: with hearing or knowledge
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a collective aspiration for strength and power.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and shared desires among individuals.
    • The invocation of a powerful entity suggests a plea for guidance and support in achieving goals.
    • The phrase "may you fill" indicates a request for abundance and blessings in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, such as a workplace or sports team, members often come together to support one another in achieving a common goal, reflecting the unity expressed in the mantra.
    • During community events or gatherings, people collectively seek blessings for prosperity and strength, similar to the invocation in the mantra.
    • In personal development, individuals may meditate on their aspirations for strength and guidance, aligning with the desire for empowerment expressed in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for collective strength and the importance of unity in achieving goals.
    • It serves as a reminder that seeking blessings and support from a higher power can enhance our endeavors.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork, community support, and personal growth, emphasizing the value of collaboration and shared aspirations.
