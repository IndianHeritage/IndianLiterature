यथा नो मित्रो आर्यमाः वरुणः सन्ति गोपाः |सुगाः ऋतस्य पन्थाः ||
yathā no mitro aryamā varuṇaḥ santi gopāḥ | sugā ṛtasya panthāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yathā no mitro aryamā varuṇaḥ santi gopāḥ | sugā ṛtasya panthāḥ ||"
  • Translation: "May the friends, Aryama and Varuna, who are the protectors, lead us on the path of truth."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • yathā: as, in the way that
    • no: for us
    • mitro: friends, allies
    • aryamā: a deity associated with friendship and protection
    • varuṇaḥ: the god of cosmic order and water, representing moral authority
    • santi: may they be, exist
    • gopāḥ: protectors, guardians
    • sugā: lead us well
    • ṛtasya: of truth, order, or righteousness
    • panthāḥ: path, way

  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the deities Aryama and Varuna, asking for their guidance and protection.
    • Aryama symbolizes friendship and community, while Varuna represents the moral and cosmic order.
    • The phrase "sugā ṛtasya panthāḥ" emphasizes the importance of following a righteous path, suggesting that true success and fulfillment come from living in accordance with truth and morality.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal relationships, embodying the qualities of Aryama can lead to stronger bonds and mutual support among friends.
    • In a professional setting, adhering to the principles of Varuna can foster an ethical workplace where integrity is valued.
    • In community service, following the "path of truth" can inspire individuals to engage in altruistic actions that benefit society.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of friendship, protection, and moral integrity in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek guidance from higher principles and to walk the path of righteousness.
    • In a world often filled with challenges, invoking the support of divine protectors can provide strength and direction in our personal and communal journeys.
