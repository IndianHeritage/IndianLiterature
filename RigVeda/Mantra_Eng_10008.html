सप्तापो देविः सुरणा अमृत्का याभिः सिन्धुम अतरा इन्द्र पूर्बित् |नवतीं स्रोत्यां नव च श्रवन्तीर देवभ्यो गातुम मनुषे च विन्दः ||
saptāpo devīḥ suraṇā amṛktā yābhiḥ sindhum atara indra pūrbhit | navatiṁ srotyā nava ca sravantīr devebhyo gātum manuṣe ca vindaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "saptāpo devīḥ suraṇā amṛktā yābhiḥ sindhum atara indra pūrbhit | navatiṁ srotyā nava ca sravantīr devebhyo gātum manuṣe ca vindaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • saptāpo: Seven streams or rivers
    • devīḥ: Goddesses or divine beings
    • suraṇā: Of the celestial or divine
    • amṛktā: Immortal or eternal
    • yābhiḥ: By whom
    • sindhum: The ocean or water
    • atara: To cross or to go beyond
    • indra: The king of gods, representing strength and power
    • pūrbhit: Filled or enriched
    • navatiṁ: Ninety
    • srotyā: Streams or channels
    • nava: New or fresh
    • ca: And
    • sravantīr: Flowing or streaming
    • devebhyo: To the gods
    • gātum: To sing or to praise
    • manuṣe: To humans
    • ca: And
    • vindaḥ: To find or to attain
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the seven divine streams that are eternal and are to be crossed with the help of Indra, the king of gods.
    • The mention of "ninety streams" suggests a vast network of divine blessings and resources available to humanity.
    • The rivers symbolize the flow of knowledge, wisdom, and spiritual energy that can be accessed by both divine beings and humans.
    • In real life, this can be likened to the various paths of knowledge and wisdom that individuals can pursue, such as education, spirituality, and personal development.
    • Just as rivers nourish the land, the divine streams nourish the soul, providing sustenance for growth and enlightenment.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of divine and human realms.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom, to cross the metaphorical rivers of challenges with the strength of divine support.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can inspire people to pursue their goals with determination, knowing that they are supported by greater forces.
    • It serves as a call to recognize and appreciate the abundance of resources available to us, both seen and unseen, in our journey through life.
