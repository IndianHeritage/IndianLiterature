तं इद गर्भं प्रथमं दध्र आपो यत्र देवाः समागच्छन्त विश्वे |अजस्य नाभाव अध्य एकं अर्पितं यस्मिन विश्वानि भुवनानि तस्थुः ||
tam id garbham prathamaṁ dadhra āpo yatra devāḥ samagacchanta viśve | ajasya nābhāv adhy ekam arpitaṁ yasmin viśvāni bhuvanāni tasthuḥ ||
  • tam id garbham prathamaṁ dadhra: This phrase translates to "that which holds the first embryo." In a real-life context, this can be related to the concept of creation and the nurturing of life. For example, a mother nurturing her unborn child in her womb symbolizes this idea of holding the potential of life.
  • āpo yatra devāḥ samagacchanta viśve: This means "where all the gods come together." This can be interpreted as a place of unity and harmony. In real life, this could refer to a community gathering where people come together for a common purpose, such as a festival or a spiritual retreat, emphasizing the importance of collective effort and collaboration.
  • ajasy nābhāv adhy ekam arpitaṁ: This translates to "the one who is not born, yet is the source of all." This can be related to the concept of a universal consciousness or a higher power that exists beyond physical birth. In practical terms, this could be seen in the way people seek wisdom and guidance from philosophical teachings or spiritual leaders, recognizing that true knowledge transcends physical existence.
  • yasmin viśvāni bhuvanāni tasthuḥ: This means "in which all worlds reside." This can be understood as the interconnectedness of all existence. In real life, this can be observed in the way ecosystems function, where every organism plays a role in maintaining the balance of nature, highlighting the significance of every individual in the larger scheme of life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the themes of creation, unity, and the interconnectedness of all existence. It signifies the importance of nurturing life, coming together for a common cause, recognizing a higher source of wisdom, and understanding our place within the larger universe. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as family dynamics, community building, environmental conservation, and spiritual growth. By embodying these values, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and balanced world.
