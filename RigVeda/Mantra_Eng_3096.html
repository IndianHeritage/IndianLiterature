त्वं चिन् नः शम्या अग्ने अस्याः ऋतस्य बोध्य ऋतचित् स्वाधीः |कदā त उक्ताः सदहमाद्यानी कदā भवन्ति सख्या गृहे ते ||
tvaṁ cin naḥ śamyā agne asyā ṛtasya bodhy ṛtacit svādhīḥ | kadā ta ukthā sadhamādyāni kadā bhavanti sakhyā gṛhe te ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "you," referring to the divine or the sacred fire, Agni, which is a significant deity in Vedic literature.
  • cin - This means "to know" or "to perceive," indicating a quest for understanding or enlightenment.
  • naḥ - This translates to "our," suggesting a collective identity or community seeking guidance.
  • śamyā - This word can mean "peaceful" or "calm," indicating a desire for tranquility and harmony.
  • agne - Referring to "Agni," the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine spark within.
  • asyā - This means "of this," pointing to the context or the situation at hand.
  • ṛtasya - This translates to "of truth" or "of order," emphasizing the importance of righteousness and cosmic law.
  • bodhy - Meaning "to awaken" or "to enlighten," it signifies the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom.
  • ṛtacit - This can be interpreted as "one who knows the truth," reinforcing the idea of wisdom and understanding.
  • svādhīḥ - This word means "self-controlled" or "self-possessed," highlighting the importance of self-discipline and mastery over oneself.
  • kadā - This translates to "when," indicating a moment of inquiry or reflection.
  • ta - Meaning "that," it refers back to the divine or the situation being addressed.
  • ukthā - This means "utterances" or "sacred words," suggesting the power of speech and intention.
  • sadhamādyāni - This can be interpreted as "the best of the best," indicating a pursuit of excellence.
  • kadā - Repeated for emphasis, asking again about the timing of certain events.
  • bhavanti - This means "become" or "manifest," indicating the process of realization or coming into being.
  • sakhyā - This translates to "friendship" or "companionship," emphasizing the importance of relationships.
  • gṛhe - Meaning "in the home," it signifies the domestic sphere and the importance of harmony within it.
  • te - This means "your," referring back to the divine or the sacred fire.

In summary, this mantra is a prayer to Agni, seeking enlightenment, peace, and the realization of truth within the home and community. It emphasizes the importance of self-control, wisdom, and the power of words in creating a harmonious environment. The repeated inquiries about timing suggest a deep yearning for understanding when these qualities will manifest in life.


Significance: This mantra is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals seek guidance in their personal and communal lives. It encourages self-reflection, the pursuit of knowledge, and the cultivation of peaceful relationships. In modern contexts, it can be used in family settings, community gatherings, or personal meditative practices to foster a sense of unity and purpose.
