इन्द्र प्राणो रथम अव पश्याच् चित् सन्तम् अद्रिवः |पुरस्ताद् एनम् मे कृधि ||
indra pra ṇo ratham ava paścāc cit santam adrivaḥ | purastād enam me kṛdhi ||
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • Pra ṇo: This term refers to life force or vital energy. It signifies the essence of life that sustains all beings.
  • Ratham: Ratham means chariot. In this context, it symbolizes the journey of life and the means through which one navigates challenges.
  • Ava: This word means 'down' or 'from below,' indicating a grounding or foundational aspect.
  • Paścāc: This means 'after' or 'behind,' suggesting a sense of reflection or looking back.
  • Cit: This term refers to consciousness or awareness, highlighting the importance of being mindful and aware of one's surroundings.
  • Santam: This means 'peaceful' or 'calm,' indicating a state of tranquility that one should strive for.
  • Adrivaḥ: This term can be interpreted as 'without obstacles' or 'unhindered,' suggesting a smooth path in life.
  • Purastād: This means 'in front' or 'before,' indicating the importance of foresight and planning.
  • Enam: This refers to 'this' or 'him,' pointing to the subject of the mantra, which is the chariot or the journey of life.
  • Kṛdhi: This means 'to make' or 'to create,' emphasizing the active role one must take in shaping their destiny.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Indra, asking for guidance and support in navigating the journey of life. It emphasizes the importance of being aware and mindful (cit) while moving forward (purastād) and reflecting on past experiences (paścāc) to create a peaceful and unhindered path (adrivaḥ, santam).


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: When facing challenges, one can reflect on past experiences to gain insights and make informed decisions for the future.
  • Leadership: Leaders can use this mantra to remind themselves of the importance of foresight and planning while being aware of their team's needs.
  • Meditation and Mindfulness: Practicing mindfulness can help individuals achieve a state of calmness and clarity, allowing them to navigate life's challenges more effectively.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of reflection, and the need for conscious action. By invoking the qualities of Indra, one can seek strength and guidance in their journey, ultimately leading to a more peaceful and fulfilling life.
