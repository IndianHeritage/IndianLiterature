आधिषमानायः पतिः शुचायाश च शुचस्य च |वासोवायो 'वीनाम आ वासांसि मर्मृतjat ||
ādhīṣamāṇāyāḥ patiḥ śucāyāś ca śucasya ca | vāsovāyo 'vīnām ā vāsāṁsi marmṛjat ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ādhīṣamāṇāyāḥ: Refers to the one who is in control or the ruler.
    • patiḥ: Means lord or master.
    • śucāyāś: Pertains to purity or cleanliness.
    • ca: Means 'and'.
    • śucasya: Again refers to purity, emphasizing the aspect of cleanliness.
    • vāsovāyo: Relates to the essence or the spirit of the atmosphere.
    • 'vīnām: Refers to the winds or the forces of nature.
    • ā: A preposition meaning 'towards' or 'to'.
    • vāsāṁsi: Refers to the garments or coverings.
    • marmṛjat: Means to cleanse or purify.
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine ruler who embodies purity and is associated with the natural elements, particularly the winds.
    • It emphasizes the importance of cleanliness and purity, both in a physical and spiritual sense.
    • The reference to the winds suggests a connection to the environment and the need for harmony with nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a practical sense, this mantra can be related to the importance of maintaining a clean environment. For instance, community clean-up drives reflect the essence of this mantra, where individuals come together to purify their surroundings.
    • In personal life, practicing mindfulness and maintaining mental clarity can be seen as a form of spiritual cleanliness, aligning with the purity mentioned in the mantra.
    • In leadership, a ruler or leader who embodies purity and integrity can inspire their followers, much like the divine ruler mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for purity and harmony with nature, which is essential in today's world where environmental issues are prevalent.
    • It serves as a reminder to individuals and leaders alike to uphold values of cleanliness, integrity, and respect for the natural world.
    • By embodying these principles, one can contribute to a more harmonious and balanced existence, both personally and collectively.
