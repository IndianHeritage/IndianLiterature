त आदित्याऽ आगता सर्वतातये वृधे नो यज्ञं अवतास सजोषसः |बृहस्पतिं पूषणं अश्विनां भागं स्वस्त्यग्निं समिधानं ईमहे ||
ta ādityā ā gatā sarvatātaye vṛdhe no yajñam avatā sajoṣasaḥ | bṛhaspatim pūṣaṇam aśvinā bhagaṁ svasty a1gniṁ samidhānam īmahe ||
  • ta ādityā: This phrase refers to the Adityas, a group of solar deities in Vedic literature. They symbolize light, truth, and the life-giving energy of the sun.
  • ā gatā: This means "having come" or "arrived." It signifies the presence of divine energy or blessings in our lives.
  • sarvatātaye: This term translates to "for all purposes" or "in all aspects." It emphasizes the universality of the divine presence and its relevance in every facet of life.
  • vṛdhe: This word means "to increase" or "to grow." It indicates the idea of prosperity and abundance that comes from divine blessings.
  • no yajñam: "Our sacrifice" or "our offering." In Vedic tradition, yajna (sacrifice) is a means of connecting with the divine and seeking blessings.
  • avatā: This means "to protect" or "to preserve." It signifies the safeguarding of our efforts and intentions by divine forces.
  • sajoṣasaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "with joy" or "with happiness." It highlights the importance of positivity and joy in our actions and offerings.
  • bṛhaspatim: Referring to Brihaspati, the guru of the gods and the deity of wisdom and eloquence. This invokes the need for guidance and wisdom in our endeavors.
  • pūṣaṇam: This refers to Pushan, the deity associated with nourishment and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of sustenance and growth in our lives.
  • aśvinā: The Ashvins are twin deities representing health, medicine, and vitality. They symbolize the importance of well-being and healing.
  • bhagaṁ: This term means "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the abundance that comes from divine blessings.
  • svasty: This translates to "well-being" or "good fortune." It emphasizes the desire for a harmonious and prosperous life.
  • agniṁ: Referring to Agni, the fire god, who represents transformation and purification. Fire is a central element in Vedic rituals, symbolizing the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • samidhānam: This means "the act of offering" or "the act of kindling." It signifies the importance of intention and action in our spiritual practices.
  • īmahe: This translates to "we meditate" or "we contemplate." It emphasizes the importance of reflection and mindfulness in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for prosperity, wisdom, and well-being in all aspects of life. It highlights the importance of joy in our actions and the need for guidance from higher powers. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during rituals, personal meditations, or even in daily affirmations to invoke positivity and abundance. By recognizing the interconnectedness of our actions with the divine, we can cultivate a life filled with purpose, growth, and harmony.
