वाजे-वाजेऽवता वाजिनो नो धनेषु विप्राः अमृताः ऋतज्ञाः |अस्य मध्वः पिबत माध्यध्वं तृप्ताः यात पथिभिर देवयानैः ||
vāje-vāje 'vata vājino no dhaneṣu viprā amṛtā ṛtajñāḥ | asya madhvaḥ pibata mādayadhvaṁ tṛptā yāta pathibhir devayānaiḥ ||
  • vāje-vāje: This term can be translated as "in every endeavor" or "in every effort." It emphasizes the importance of striving in all aspects of life.
  • 'vata: This word can mean "to be strong" or "to be powerful." It suggests the need for strength and resilience in our pursuits.
  • vājino: This refers to "those who are strong" or "the powerful." It highlights the qualities of individuals who are capable and determined.
  • no dhaneṣu: This phrase translates to "in our wealth" or "in our resources." It indicates the importance of prosperity and abundance in life.
  • viprā: This term means "the wise" or "the learned." It signifies the value of knowledge and wisdom in achieving success.
  • amṛtā: This word translates to "immortal" or "eternal." It suggests the pursuit of lasting values and truths.
  • ṛtajñāḥ: This means "those who know the truth" or "the righteous." It emphasizes the importance of living in accordance with truth and righteousness.
  • asya madhvaḥ: This phrase translates to "of this sweetness" or "of this joy." It indicates the enjoyment and fulfillment that comes from our efforts.
  • pibata: This means "drink" or "consume." It suggests the act of taking in or embracing the joys and rewards of life.
  • mādayadhvaṁ: This translates to "become joyful" or "be happy." It encourages a positive and joyful approach to life.
  • tṛptā: This word means "satisfied" or "content." It signifies the importance of finding contentment in our achievements.
  • yāta pathibhir: This phrase translates to "go along the paths" or "follow the ways." It suggests the importance of following the right path in life.
  • devayānaiḥ: This means "by the divine beings" or "by the gods." It indicates the role of divine guidance and support in our endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of striving for success in all endeavors while embodying strength, wisdom, and righteousness. It encourages individuals to seek prosperity and joy in their pursuits, while also recognizing the importance of divine guidance. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, career growth, and spiritual journeys. By embracing these principles, individuals can lead fulfilling lives that are aligned with truth and joy, ultimately contributing to their overall well-being and success.
