तं ऊषु समना गिरा पितृणां च मनमभिः |नाभाकस्य प्रशस्तिभिर यः सिंधूनाम उपोदय साप्तस्वसा स मध्यमो नभन्ताम अन्यके समे ||
tam ū ṣu samanā girā pitṝṇāṁ ca manmabhiḥ | nābhākasya praśastibhir yaḥ sindhūnām upodaye saptasvasā sa madhyamo nabhantām anyake same ||
  • tam ū ṣu samanā girā: This phrase can be translated as "that which is the same in all utterances." It emphasizes the unity and harmony found in diverse expressions. In real life, this can be seen in how different cultures express similar emotions through music, art, or language, showcasing a universal human experience.
  • pitṝṇāṁ ca manmabhiḥ: This translates to "with the thoughts of the ancestors." It highlights the importance of remembering and honoring our forebears. In contemporary society, this can be reflected in family traditions, rituals, and the values passed down through generations, reminding us of our roots and guiding our actions.
  • nābhākasya praśastibhir: This means "by the praises of the one who is the center." It suggests that there is a central figure or principle that is worthy of praise. In a modern context, this could refer to leaders, mentors, or even core values that guide organizations and communities, emphasizing the need for a strong foundation in any endeavor.
  • yaḥ sindhūnām upodaye: This translates to "who rises in the rivers." It symbolizes growth and the flow of life. In real life, this can be likened to the way rivers nourish the land and support ecosystems, representing the interconnectedness of life and the importance of nurturing our environment.
  • saptasvasā sa madhyamo nabhantām: This phrase means "the one who is in the middle of the seven streams." It signifies balance and the importance of being centered amidst various influences. In everyday life, this can be seen in the need for balance between work and personal life, or between different responsibilities, highlighting the importance of maintaining equilibrium.
  • anyake same: This translates to "in the same way as others." It emphasizes the idea of equality and shared experiences. In a societal context, this can be related to the principles of justice and fairness, advocating for equal treatment and opportunities for all individuals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of unity, remembrance, balance, and interconnectedness. Its significance lies in the reminder to honor our roots while striving for harmony in our expressions and actions. In real life, these principles can guide us in fostering relationships, respecting traditions, and maintaining balance in our lives. By applying these teachings, we can create a more harmonious and equitable society, where the wisdom of the past informs our present and future actions.
