वितिष्ठध्वम् मरुतो विक्ष्व इच्छत गृभायत रक्षसः सम पिनष्टन |वयो ये भूत्वी पतयन्ति नक्तभिर ये वा रिपो दधिरे देव अध्वरे ||
vi tiṣṭhadhvam maruto vikṣv i1cchata gṛbhāyata rakṣasaḥ sam pinaṣṭana | vayo ye bhūtvī patayanti naktabhir ye vā ripo dadhire deve adhvare ||
  • vi tiṣṭhadhvam: This phrase translates to "stand firm" or "be steadfast." It emphasizes the importance of stability and resilience in the face of challenges.
  • maruto: Referring to the Maruts, the storm deities in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and the forces of nature. They represent the dynamic aspects of life.
  • vikṣv i1cchata: This means "desiring to see" or "wishing to observe." It suggests a quest for knowledge and understanding, urging individuals to seek clarity and insight.
  • gṛbhāyata: This translates to "may you grasp" or "may you hold." It indicates the importance of seizing opportunities and embracing what life offers.
  • rakṣasaḥ: Referring to the demons or negative forces, this term highlights the presence of obstacles and challenges in life that one must confront.
  • sam pinaṣṭana: This phrase can be interpreted as "may they be united" or "may they come together." It signifies the importance of unity and collaboration in overcoming difficulties.
  • vayo ye bhūtvī: This means "those who have become" or "those who have manifested." It refers to the essence of existence and the transformation that occurs in life.
  • patayanti naktabhir: This translates to "who fly through the nights." It symbolizes the journey through darkness and uncertainty, emphasizing perseverance.
  • ye vā ripo dadhire: This means "those who have faced enemies." It acknowledges the struggles and adversities one encounters in life.
  • deve adhvare: This translates to "in the divine sacrifice." It signifies the importance of devotion and the spiritual aspect of life, suggesting that challenges can lead to growth and enlightenment.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of resilience, unity, and the pursuit of knowledge in the face of adversity. It serves as a reminder that life is filled with challenges (rakṣasaḥ) and that one must stand firm (vi tiṣṭhadhvam) while seeking understanding (vikṣv i1cchata). The call for unity (sam pinaṣṭana) emphasizes the importance of collaboration in overcoming obstacles. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork in professional settings, community support during crises, or personal growth through challenges. Ultimately, it encourages individuals to embrace their journey, confront their fears, and strive for enlightenment through perseverance and unity.
