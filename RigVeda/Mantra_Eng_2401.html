हस्तेवा शक्तिम अभि संददी नः क्षामेवा नः सम अजतं राजांसि |इमा गिरो अश्विनाऽ युष्मयन्तीः क्ष्णोत्रेणेव स्वधितिं सं शिशीतम् ||
hasteva śaktim abhi saṁdadī naḥ kṣāmeva naḥ sam ajataṁ rajāṁsi | imā giro aśvinā yuṣmayantīḥ kṣṇotreṇeva svadhitiṁ saṁ śiśītam ||
  • Translation: "May we be endowed with strength, O Ashvins, and may we be protected by your grace. May our words be pleasing and may we attain the highest wisdom."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • hasteva: endowed with strength
    • śaktim: power or energy
    • abhi: towards or in the direction of
    • saṁdadī: may we be granted or endowed
    • naḥ: our
    • kṣāmeva: may we be protected
    • sam: together or in harmony
    • ajataṁ: born or created
    • rajāṁsi: wisdom or knowledge
    • imā: these
    • giro: words or speech
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin deities)
    • yuṣmayantīḥ: may they be pleasing
    • kṣṇotreṇeva: like the black (or dark) one
    • svadhitiṁ: wisdom or understanding
    • saṁ: together
    • śiśītam: may it be attained or achieved
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be invoked during team meetings to foster collaboration and strength among team members, ensuring that everyone contributes positively and harmoniously.
    • In personal life, individuals can recite this mantra during challenging times to seek strength and wisdom, reminding themselves to stay resilient and focused on their goals.
    • In educational contexts, students can use this mantra to encourage themselves to speak clearly and effectively, aiming for their words to be impactful and well-received.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, protection, and wisdom. It emphasizes the importance of harmonious communication and the pursuit of knowledge. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork, personal challenges, and educational endeavors, serving as a reminder to seek support from higher powers and to strive for excellence in our words and actions.
