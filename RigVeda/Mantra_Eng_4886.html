नु नो रयिं रथ्यं चर्षणिप्रां पुरवीरम् मह ऋतस्य गोपाम् |क्षयं दाताजरं येन जनान् स्पृधो अदेवीर अभि च क्रमाम विषा आदेवीर अभ्य अ1श्नवाम ||
nu no rayiṁ rathyaṁ carṣaṇiprām puruvīram maha ṛtasya gopām | kṣayaṁ dātājaraṁ yena janān spṛdho adevīr abhi ca kramāma viśa ādevīr abhy a1śnavāma ||
  • nu no rayiṁ rathyaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as a call for prosperity and abundance. "Rayiṁ" refers to wealth or resources, while "rathyaṁ" suggests a vehicle or means of transport. In real life, this can be likened to the pursuit of financial stability and the means to achieve one's goals.
  • carṣaṇiprām puruvīram: "Carṣaṇiprām" refers to the earth or the ground, and "puruvīram" signifies the valor of the past. This can be understood as a recognition of the strength and resilience derived from one's roots and heritage. For example, individuals often draw inspiration from their ancestors' struggles and achievements to overcome their own challenges.
  • maha ṛtasya gopām: "Maha ṛtasya" translates to the great truth or cosmic order, while "gopām" refers to the protector or guardian. This suggests a plea for guidance and protection in alignment with universal truths. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way people seek ethical guidance in their decisions, striving to live in harmony with the world around them.
  • kṣayaṁ dātājaraṁ: "Kṣayaṁ" means decline or destruction, and "dātājaraṁ" refers to the giver of life. This part of the mantra acknowledges the transient nature of life and the importance of nurturing what is given to us. For instance, in environmental conservation, recognizing the fragility of ecosystems encourages individuals to take action to protect and sustain them.
  • yena janān spṛdho: This translates to "by whom the people are inspired." It emphasizes the role of a leader or guiding force in motivating others. In a real-world context, this can be seen in effective leadership, where a leader inspires their team to achieve common goals through vision and encouragement.
  • adevīr abhi ca kramāma: "Adevīr" refers to divine beings or forces, and "kramāma" means to progress or move forward. This suggests a journey towards higher consciousness or spiritual growth, often aided by divine guidance. In practice, this can be reflected in personal development practices such as meditation or mindfulness, where individuals seek to elevate their awareness and understanding.
  • viśa ādevīr abhy a1śnavāma: "Viśa" means community or society, while "ādevīr" refers to the divine. This part of the mantra calls for unity and collective progress under divine influence. In real life, this can be observed in community service initiatives where individuals come together to support one another and work towards common goals, fostering a sense of belonging and shared purpose.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound understanding of the interconnectedness of prosperity, heritage, truth, and community. Its significance lies in the reminder that individuals are not isolated but part of a larger cosmic order. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages us to seek abundance while honoring our roots, to strive for ethical living, and to inspire and uplift our communities. By embodying these principles, we can navigate life's challenges with resilience and purpose, ultimately contributing to a harmonious society.
