यद इम एनां उषतो अभ्य अवर्षीत तृष्यावतः प्रावृश्य आगतायाम् |अख्खलीकृत्य पितरं न पुत्रो अन्यो अन्यं उप वदन्तम् एति ||
yad īm enām̐ uśato abhy avarṣīt tṛṣyāvataḥ prāvṛṣy āgatāyām | akhkhalīkṛtyā pitaraṁ na putro anyo anyam upa vadantam eti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • īm: we desire
    • enām̐: this (feminine form)
    • uśato: of the auspicious
    • abhy: towards
    • avarṣīt: has rained down
    • tṛṣyāvataḥ: for the thirsty
    • prāvṛṣy: during the rainy season
    • āgatāyām: having come
    • akhkhalīkṛtyā: with a pure heart
    • pitaraṁ: father
    • na: not
    • putro: son
    • anyo: another
    • anyam: another
    • upa: towards
    • vadantam: speaking
    • eti: goes
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the blessings that rain brings, especially during the rainy season, which is crucial for sustenance and growth.
    • The phrase "for the thirsty" emphasizes the need for nourishment and fulfillment, both physically and spiritually.
    • The mention of "with a pure heart" suggests that intentions matter; one should approach life and relationships with sincerity and purity.
    • The latter part of the mantra reflects on familial relationships, particularly the bond between a father and son, highlighting the importance of communication and understanding within families.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the arrival of monsoon rains is celebrated as it signifies the beginning of a fruitful season. Farmers often perform rituals to invoke rain, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on auspiciousness.
    • In personal relationships, approaching conflicts with a "pure heart" can lead to better understanding and resolution, much like the father-son relationship mentioned in the mantra.
    • During community gatherings, sharing resources and supporting each other during times of need can be seen as a practical application of the mantra's teachings on nourishment and familial bonds.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking blessings for sustenance, the importance of pure intentions, and the value of familial relationships.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us to appreciate the natural cycles of life, nurture our relationships, and approach challenges with sincerity.
    • In real life, it encourages us to cultivate a sense of community, support one another, and maintain purity in our hearts and actions.
