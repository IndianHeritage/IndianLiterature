एष विश्वानि वार्याः शूरो यन्न इव सत्वभिः |पवमानः सिषासति ||
eṣa viśvāni vāryā śūro yann iva satvabhiḥ | pavamānaḥ siṣāsati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣa: this
    • viśvāni: all, universal
    • vāryā: powerful, strong
    • śūro: hero, brave one
    • yann: like, as if
    • iva: like, as
    • satvabhiḥ: with strength, with vigor
    • pavamānaḥ: the purifying one, the one that moves swiftly
    • siṣāsati: achieves, accomplishes
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a universal power that embodies strength and heroism.
    • The term "eṣa viśvāni vāryā" suggests that this power is not limited to one individual but is accessible to all who seek it.
    • The phrase "yann iva satvabhiḥ" indicates that this power can be harnessed through strength and determination.
    • "Pavamānaḥ siṣāsati" implies that this purifying force can help individuals achieve their goals and aspirations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive environment, such as a workplace, individuals can draw upon their inner strength and courage to overcome challenges, much like the "śūro" or hero mentioned in the mantra.
    • In sports, athletes often tap into their universal potential ("viśvāni vāryā") to perform at their best, demonstrating the power of teamwork and collective strength.
    • In personal development, individuals may use the mantra's essence to motivate themselves to pursue their goals with vigor and determination, embodying the qualities of "pavamānaḥ" to achieve success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of universal strength and the heroic qualities within each individual.
    • It serves as a reminder that by harnessing our inner power and determination, we can achieve our aspirations and overcome obstacles.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal growth, teamwork, or competitive settings, the principles outlined in this mantra can inspire individuals to strive for excellence and embrace their potential.
