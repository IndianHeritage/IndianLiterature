गावश्चिद्घा समान्यवः सजात्येन मरुतः स्बन्धवः |रिहते ककुभो मिथः ||
gāvaś cid ghā samanyavaḥ sajātyena marutaḥ sabandhavaḥ | rihate kakubho mithaḥ ||
  • gāvaś cid ghā samanyavaḥ: This phrase can be translated to "the cows (gāva) are indeed the essence (cid) of the common (samanyavaḥ)." In this context, cows symbolize nourishment, wealth, and sustenance in Vedic culture. They are often seen as a source of life and prosperity.
  • sajātyena marutaḥ sabandhavaḥ: This part translates to "the Maruts (the storm deities) are united (sajātyena) with their kin (sabandhavaḥ)." The Maruts represent the forces of nature, particularly storms and winds, and their unity signifies the importance of collaboration and harmony in nature.
  • rihate kakubho mithaḥ: This can be interpreted as "they (the Maruts) reside (rihate) together (mithaḥ) in the heights (kakubho)." This suggests a sense of community and shared purpose among the forces of nature, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe.

  • Real-Life Example: In agriculture, the significance of cows is evident as they provide milk, dung for fertilizer, and are integral to the rural economy. The unity of the Maruts can be likened to teamwork in a business environment, where collaboration leads to success.
  • Conclusion: This mantra highlights the importance of harmony between nature and humanity. It teaches us to respect and nurture our resources, like cows, which are vital for sustenance. In a broader sense, it reminds us of the significance of working together, whether in families, communities, or workplaces, to achieve common goals. The interconnectedness of all beings and elements is a fundamental principle that can guide us in our daily lives, promoting sustainability and cooperation.
