प्रोत्ये वरुणं मित्रं इन्द्रं मरुतः कृष्वावसे नो अद्य। प्र पूषणं विष्णुम अग्निम पुरन्धिं सवितारं ओषधिः पर्वतांश च॥
protaye varuṇam mitram indram marutaḥ kṛṣvāvase no adya | pra pūṣaṇaṁ viṣṇum agnim puraṁdhiṁ savitāram oṣadhīḥ parvatām̐ś ca ||
  • Protaye: This word can be understood as "to invoke" or "to call upon." It signifies the act of reaching out to divine forces.
  • Varuṇam: Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water. He represents the moral and natural laws that govern the universe.
  • Mitram: Mitra is the god of friendship and contracts, symbolizing harmony and cooperation among people.
  • Indram: Indra is the king of the gods, associated with rain, storms, and warfare. He embodies strength and power.
  • Marutaḥ: The Maruts are storm deities, representing the forces of nature and the power of the wind.
  • Kṛṣvāvase: This term can be interpreted as "to be present" or "to dwell." It indicates a desire for divine presence in our lives.
  • No adya: "Today" signifies the immediacy of the invocation, emphasizing the need for divine assistance in the present moment.
  • Pra pūṣaṇaṁ: Pra Pushaṇa refers to the nourishing aspect of the divine, often associated with sustenance and growth.
  • Viṣṇum: Vishnu is the preserver and protector of the universe, representing stability and balance.
  • Agnim: Agni is the fire god, symbolizing transformation, energy, and the sacred fire used in rituals.
  • Puraṁdhiṁ: Puraṁdhi refers to the divine architect or creator, representing the creative forces in the universe.
  • Savitāram: Savitar is the sun god, symbolizing illumination, life, and the source of energy.
  • Oṣadhīḥ: Oṣadhī refers to herbs and plants, symbolizing healing and nourishment from nature.
  • Parvatām̐ś ca: Parvatām̐ś refers to the mountains, representing stability, strength, and the eternal nature of the earth.

Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation that calls upon various deities to provide support, nourishment, and strength in our lives. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature and the divine, reminding us of the importance of harmony, cooperation, and respect for the natural world. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in moments of need, such as during challenges in personal or professional life, where invoking divine support can provide guidance and strength. It encourages us to seek balance and nourishment from both the spiritual and natural realms, fostering a holistic approach to life.
