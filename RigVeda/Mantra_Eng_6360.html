तं व अभि प्र गायत पुरुहूतं पुरुष्टुतम् | इन्द्रं गीरभिः तविषं आ विवासत ||
tam v abhi pra gāyata puruhūtam puruṣṭutam | indraṁ gīrbhis taviṣam ā vivāsata ||
  • tam v abhi pra gāyata: This phrase can be translated as "Let us sing praises to Him." The word "tam" refers to the divine or the supreme being, while "gāyata" means to sing or chant. This indicates a call to worship and acknowledge the greatness of the divine.
  • puruhūtam puruṣṭutam: "Puruhūtam" means "one who is invoked frequently" and "puruṣṭutam" translates to "the one who is praised by many." This emphasizes the idea that the divine is not only worthy of praise but is also a central figure in the lives of many devotees.
  • indraṁ gīrbhis taviṣam: "Indraṁ" refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength and power. "Gīrbhis" means "with hymns" and "taviṣam" translates to "the one who is powerful." This line suggests that through hymns and praises, one can connect with the powerful forces of the universe.
  • ā vivāsata: This phrase means "may He be invoked" or "may He be brought forth." It signifies the act of calling upon the divine presence to manifest in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of devotion and the power of collective worship. It serves as a reminder that through singing praises and invoking the divine, individuals can connect with higher energies and seek guidance in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applicable in various forms of worship, community gatherings, and rituals where people come together to celebrate and honor the divine. The significance lies in fostering a sense of unity, strength, and purpose among individuals, encouraging them to seek the divine presence in their daily lives and challenges.
