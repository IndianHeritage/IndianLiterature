मन्त्र: हव त्वा सूर उदिते हव मध्यंदिन दिवः | जुषाण इन्द्र सप्तिभिर न आगहि ||
have tvā sūra udite have madhyaṁdine divaḥ | juṣāṇa indra saptibhir na ā gahi ||
  • Mantra: "have tvā sūra udite have madhyaṁdine divaḥ | juṣāṇa indra saptibhir na ā gahi ||"
  • Translation: "O Indra, as you rise, may you be invoked at midday; may you, who are the strong one, be pleased with our offerings and not be far from us."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • have: A call or invocation, expressing a desire for presence.
    • tvā: You (referring to Indra).
    • sūra: Strong or powerful, indicating Indra's might.
    • udite: Rising or emerging, often associated with the sun or a new beginning.
    • madhyaṁdine: At midday, a time of strength and clarity.
    • divaḥ: Of the heavens or sky, indicating a divine context.
    • juṣāṇa: Being pleased or satisfied, indicating a desire for favor.
    • indra: The king of gods, representing strength and power.
    • saptibhir: With seven, possibly referring to the seven rays of the sun or seven aspects of strength.
    • na ā gahi: Do not be far or distant, expressing a wish for closeness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek strength and guidance during challenging times, much like invoking Indra for support.
    • During important events, such as weddings or festivals, people may call upon divine forces to bless the occasion, similar to how this mantra seeks Indra's presence.
    • In moments of decision-making, one might reflect on the clarity and strength represented by midday, using it as a metaphor for making wise choices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine strength and guidance in our lives.
    • It highlights the significance of seeking support during pivotal moments, reminding us that we are not alone in our struggles.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to connect with their inner strength and the divine, fostering a sense of community and support.
