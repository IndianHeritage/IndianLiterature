अध्वर्यवश चक्रवांसो मधूनी प्र वायवे भरत चारु शुक्रम् |होतेव नः प्रथमः पाह्य अस्य देव मध्वो ररिमा ते मदाया ||
adhvaryavaś cakṛvāṁso madhūni pra vāyave bharata cāru śukram | hoteva naḥ prathamaḥ pāhy asya deva madhvo rarimā te madāya ||
  • adhvaryavaś: This term refers to the priests who perform the rituals, particularly the Adhvaryu, who is responsible for the physical aspects of the sacrifice.
  • cakṛvāṁso: This word can be interpreted as "those who are endowed with strength" or "those who are capable." It signifies the power and capability of the priests and participants in the ritual.
  • madhūni: This term means "sweet" or "honey-like," often symbolizing the sweetness of offerings and the positive outcomes of the rituals.
  • pra vāyave: This phrase translates to "to the wind" or "to the atmosphere," indicating the importance of the natural elements in the rituals.
  • bharata: This term can refer to the people or the community, emphasizing the collective aspect of the ritual.
  • cāru: Meaning "beautiful" or "pleasant," this word highlights the aesthetic and harmonious aspects of the offerings and the ritual itself.
  • śukram: This word means "pure" or "bright," often associated with the divine and the purity of the offerings made to the deities.
  • hoteva: This term refers to the priest who invokes the deities, emphasizing the role of the priest in connecting the human and divine realms.
  • naḥ: This means "our," indicating a collective identity and shared purpose among the participants in the ritual.
  • prathamaḥ: This word means "first," suggesting the importance of prioritizing the divine and the rituals in one's life.
  • pāhy: This term translates to "protect" or "guard," indicating a plea for divine protection and guidance.
  • asya: Meaning "this," it refers to the offerings or the ritual being performed.
  • deva: This word means "god" or "deity," emphasizing the divine presence in the ritual.
  • madhvo: This term can be interpreted as "sweetness" or "honey," reinforcing the idea of positive outcomes and blessings.
  • rarimā: This word means "to be strong" or "to be powerful," indicating a desire for strength and resilience.
  • te: This means "your," referring to the deities being invoked.
  • madāya: This term translates to "for the sake of joy" or "for the sake of happiness," indicating the ultimate goal of the rituals.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of rituals, community, and the connection between the human and divine realms. It highlights the roles of the priests and the collective identity of the participants, invoking divine protection and blessings for a harmonious and prosperous life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various communal gatherings, ceremonies, and rituals where individuals seek to connect with the divine, celebrate together, and seek guidance and protection in their endeavors. It serves as a reminder of the significance of community, the beauty of shared experiences, and the pursuit of purity and joy in life.
