इहि तिस्रः परावत इहि पञ्च जनाम् अति |धेनाऽ इन्द्रावचाकशत ||
ihi tisraḥ parāvata ihi pañca janām̐ ati | dhenā indrāvacākaśat ||
  • Mantra: "ihi tisraḥ parāvata ihi pañca janām̐ ati | dhenā indrāvacākaśat ||"
  • Translation: "Come here, O threefold (or three), come here, O five beings; with the hundredfold (or abundant) wealth of Indra."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ihi: Here, indicating a call or invitation.
    • tisraḥ: Referring to three, which can symbolize various triads in life, such as mind, body, and spirit.
    • parāvata: Often interpreted as 'the highest' or 'the supreme', suggesting a call to higher consciousness or awareness.
    • pañca: Referring to five, which can represent the five elements (earth, water, fire, air, and space) that constitute the universe.
    • janām̐: Beings or people, indicating a collective or community.
    • ati: Beyond or transcending, suggesting a movement towards something greater.
    • dhenā: Wealth or abundance, often associated with nourishment and prosperity.
    • indrāvacākaśat: The hundredfold wealth of Indra, symbolizing divine abundance and blessings.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra can be seen as a call to gather resources and support from both the physical and spiritual realms. For instance, in a community project, individuals may come together (the three and five) to create something greater than themselves, pooling their talents and resources.
    • The concept of 'three' and 'five' can also be reflected in teamwork dynamics, where diverse skills (like the five elements) contribute to a successful outcome, much like how different departments in a company work together to achieve a common goal.
    • The invocation of Indra's wealth can be likened to seeking divine guidance or inspiration in times of need, such as during a challenging business venture or personal crisis, where one looks for abundance in creativity and solutions.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of community, collaboration, and the pursuit of higher consciousness. It serves as a reminder that by coming together and transcending individual limitations, we can access greater abundance and prosperity.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as community service, teamwork in professional settings, or personal growth endeavors, where collective effort leads to shared success and fulfillment.
    • Ultimately, the mantra encourages us to recognize the interconnectedness of all beings and the potential for collective abundance when we unite our strengths and aspirations.
