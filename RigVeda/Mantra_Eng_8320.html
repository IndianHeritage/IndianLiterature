इन्दव इन्द्राय बृहत् पवस्व सुमृळीको अनवद्यो ऋषादाः |भरā चन्द्राणि गृणते वसूनि देवैर द्यावापृथिवी प्रावतम् नः ||
indav indrāya bṛhate pavasva sumṛḻīko anavadyo riśādāḥ | bharā candrāṇi gṛṇate vasūni devair dyāvāpṛthivī prāvataṁ naḥ ||
  • Indav: This term refers to the divine or celestial beings, often associated with the gods in Vedic literature.
  • Indrāya: This is a reference to Indra, the king of the gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms, symbolizing strength and power.
  • Bṛhate: Meaning "great" or "vast," this word signifies the expansive nature of the universe and the divine.
  • Pavasva: This term means "to purify" or "to cleanse," indicating a desire for spiritual and physical purification.
  • Sumṛḻīko: This word can be interpreted as "well-being" or "prosperity," suggesting a wish for abundance and health.
  • Anavadyo: Meaning "without fault" or "perfect," this term emphasizes the importance of purity and righteousness.
  • Riśādāḥ: This refers to the "sacred offerings" or "sacrifices," which are essential in Vedic rituals to please the deities.
  • Bharā: This means "to bear" or "to carry," indicating the act of receiving blessings or abundance.
  • Candrāṇi: Referring to the "moon" or "lunar," this symbolizes calmness, nourishment, and the cyclical nature of life.
  • Gṛṇate: This means "to sing" or "to praise," highlighting the importance of devotion and gratitude towards the divine.
  • Vasūni: This term refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating a desire for material and spiritual abundance.
  • Devair: Meaning "by the gods," this emphasizes the divine influence in the material world.
  • Dyāvāpṛthivī: This refers to "heaven and earth," symbolizing the connection between the spiritual and physical realms.
  • Prāvataṁ: Meaning "to be favorable" or "to be auspicious," indicating a wish for positive outcomes.
  • Naḥ: This means "for us," indicating a personal appeal for blessings and guidance.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a profound appeal for divine blessings, purification, and prosperity. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of the spiritual and material worlds, urging individuals to seek the favor of the gods for well-being and abundance. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as during rituals, prayers for health and prosperity, or even in moments of reflection when seeking guidance and strength in challenging times. By invoking these divine qualities, individuals can cultivate a sense of gratitude and connection to the greater universe, fostering a harmonious balance in their lives.
