अस्य पिबताम अश्विनौ युवं मदस्य चारुणः |मध्वो रातस्य धिष्ण्या ||
asya pibatam aśvinā yuvam madasya cāruṇaḥ | madhvo rātasya dhiṣṇyā ||
  • Mantra: "asya pibatam aśvinā yuvam madasya cāruṇaḥ | madhvo rātasya dhiṣṇyā ||"
  • Translation: "May you, O Ashvins, drink this (soma) with your youthful vigor, and may you be the source of delight in the rituals."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Asya: This (referring to the soma or offering)
    • Pibatam: Drink (imperative form, inviting the deities to partake)
    • Aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin deities associated with health and medicine)
    • Yuvam: You (referring to the youthful and energetic nature of the Ashvins)
    • Madasya: Of delight or intoxication (often associated with the soma drink)
    • Cāruṇaḥ: Beautiful or charming (describing the nature of the offering)
    • Madhvo: Of sweetness (referring to the essence of the offering)
    • Rātasya: Of the ritual or sacrifice (indicating the context of the offering)
    • Dhiṣṇyā: In the dwelling or place (referring to the sacred space of the ritual)
  • Context and Significance:
    • The mantra is a prayer to the Ashvins, who are revered as divine physicians and bringers of health and prosperity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of offerings in rituals, particularly the soma, which is considered sacred and is believed to bestow vitality and joy.
    • The invocation of the Ashvins highlights the connection between divine blessings and human well-being, suggesting that health and happiness are gifts from the gods.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra can be recited during health-related rituals or ceremonies, invoking divine support for physical and mental well-being.
    • In modern contexts, it can serve as a reminder of the importance of seeking balance and vitality in life, encouraging individuals to engage in practices that promote health, such as yoga, meditation, and a balanced diet.
    • The mantra also reflects the significance of community and shared rituals, emphasizing that collective well-being is enhanced through spiritual practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine intervention for health and happiness, reminding us of the interconnectedness of life, spirituality, and well-being.
    • By invoking the Ashvins, we acknowledge the role of higher powers in our lives and the importance of rituals in fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
    • Ultimately, this mantra encourages us to cultivate a life filled with joy, health, and spiritual awareness, making it relevant in both ancient and contemporary contexts.
