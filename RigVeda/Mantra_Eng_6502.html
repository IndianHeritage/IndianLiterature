मन्त्र: चित्र इद राजा राजका इद अन्यके यके सरस्वतीं अनु |पर्जन्य इव ततानद हि वर्ष्ट्या सहस्रं अयुतां ददत ||
citra id rājā rājakā id anyake yake sarasvatīm anu | parjanya iva tatanad dhi vṛṣṭyā sahasram ayutā dadat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • citra: colorful, diverse, or splendid
    • id: this
    • rājā: king
    • rājakā: of kings or royal
    • id: this
    • anyake: others
    • yake: those who are
    • sarasvatīm: associated with Sarasvati, the goddess of knowledge and wisdom
    • anu: following or in accordance with
    • parjanya: rain or the rain-giver
    • iva: like or as
    • tatanad: spreads or extends
    • hi: indeed
    • vṛṣṭyā: with rain or by rain
    • sahasram: thousand
    • ayutā: ten thousand
    • dadat: gives or bestows
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a splendid king who is aligned with the divine wisdom of Sarasvati.
    • It emphasizes the importance of leadership that is not only powerful but also wise and benevolent.
    • The comparison to Parjanya, the rain-giver, signifies the nurturing aspect of a good ruler, who provides abundance and prosperity to his subjects.
    • The mention of "sahasram ayutā" suggests that the blessings and resources provided are vast and plentiful, akin to the bountiful rains that nourish the earth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern governance, leaders who prioritize education, wisdom, and the welfare of their citizens embody the qualities of the king described in this mantra.
    • For instance, a community leader who implements educational programs and sustainable practices can be seen as a modern-day "rājā" who follows the path of Sarasvati.
    • Similarly, businesses that focus on corporate social responsibility and contribute to the community reflect the nurturing aspect of leadership, akin to the rain-giver Parjanya.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wise and benevolent leadership in society.
    • It serves as a reminder that true power lies in the ability to nurture and provide for others, much like the rains that sustain life.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals in positions of authority to act with wisdom and compassion, ensuring the well-being of their communities.
