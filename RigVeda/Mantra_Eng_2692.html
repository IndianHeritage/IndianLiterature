अमित्रायुधो मरुतामिव प्रयाह् प्रथमजा ब्रह्मणो विश्वम् इद् विदुः |द्युम्नवत् ब्रह्म कुशिकास एरिर एक-एको दमे अग्निं सम ईधिरे ||
amitrāyudho marutām iva prayāḥ prathamajā brahmaṇo viśvam id viduḥ | dyumnavad brahma kuśikāsa erira eka-eko dame agniṁ sam īdhire ||
  • amitrāyudho: This term can be broken down into "a" (not) and "mitrāyudho" (one who has friends or allies). It signifies a state of being without allies, suggesting strength in solitude or independence.
  • marutām iva prayāḥ: "Marutām" refers to the Maruts, the storm deities in Vedic literature, known for their power and energy. "Iva" means "like" or "as," and "prayāḥ" means "the way" or "path." This phrase suggests a powerful and dynamic approach to life, akin to the force of storms.
  • prathamajā brahmaṇo: "Prathamajā" means "firstborn" or "primary," and "brahmaṇo" refers to the divine or the ultimate reality. This indicates the importance of the foundational principles of existence and creation.
  • viśvam id viduḥ: "Viśvam" means "the universe" or "all," and "id viduḥ" translates to "they know this." This implies a universal understanding or awareness of the divine principles that govern existence.
  • dyumnavad: This term means "like glory" or "radiant." It suggests a state of brilliance or excellence, often associated with success and achievement.
  • brahma kuśikāsa: "Brahma" refers to the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit, while "kuśikāsa" can be interpreted as "those who are wise" or "the learned." This emphasizes the connection between wisdom and the divine.
  • erira eka-eko dame: "Erira" means "to approach" or "to come near," "eka-eko" translates to "one by one," and "dame" refers to "control" or "restraint." This suggests a methodical approach to achieving control or mastery over oneself and one's environment.
  • agniṁ sam īdhire: "Agniṁ" refers to fire, a symbol of transformation and purification, while "sam īdhire" means "they have approached" or "they have invoked." This indicates the importance of invoking the transformative power of fire in one's life.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of independence, strength, and wisdom in navigating the complexities of life. It encourages individuals to embrace their unique paths, much like the Maruts, while recognizing the foundational principles of existence. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal growth, leadership, and the pursuit of knowledge. By invoking the transformative power of fire, individuals can seek to purify their intentions and actions, leading to a more radiant and successful life. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of wisdom, strength, and the divine in our journey through life.
