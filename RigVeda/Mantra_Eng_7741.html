विष्वे देवाः स्वाहाकृतिम पवमानस्य गत |वायुर बृहस्पतिः सूर्यः अग्निः इन्द्रः सजोषसः ||
viśve devāḥ svāhākṛtim pavamānasyā gata | vāyur bṛhaspatiḥ sūryo 'gnir indraḥ sajoṣasaḥ ||
  • viśve devāḥ: This phrase translates to "all the gods" or "universal deities." It signifies the collective presence of divine forces in the universe.
  • svāhākṛtim: This term can be understood as "the form of offering" or "the act of sacrifice." It emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine.
  • pavamānasyā gata: This means "the one who is purified" or "the one who has been made pure." It suggests the idea of purification, both physical and spiritual, as a prerequisite for divine communion.
  • vāyur: Referring to "the wind" or "the breath," it symbolizes the vital force and the movement of life. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of air and breath in sustaining life.
  • bṛhaspatiḥ: This is the name of the deity associated with wisdom and knowledge. In practical terms, it represents the pursuit of knowledge and the importance of learning in our lives.
  • sūryo: Meaning "the sun," it symbolizes light, energy, and life. The sun is essential for growth and sustenance, reflecting the importance of natural elements in our existence.
  • 'gnir: This translates to "fire," which represents transformation, energy, and purification. Fire is a crucial element in many cultures, often used in rituals and as a source of warmth and cooking.
  • indraḥ: Referring to the king of gods, Indra symbolizes strength, power, and leadership. In real life, this can be related to the qualities of a good leader who protects and guides their community.
  • sajoṣasaḥ: This term means "with joy" or "with happiness." It emphasizes the importance of joy and positivity in our interactions with the divine and with each other.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings through purification, knowledge, and joy. It highlights the interconnectedness of various elements of life—air, fire, sun, and the divine. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during rituals, personal reflections, or community gatherings, where individuals seek to connect with the divine and enhance their spiritual journey. It serves as a reminder of the importance of maintaining purity in thoughts and actions, pursuing knowledge, and fostering joy in our lives.
