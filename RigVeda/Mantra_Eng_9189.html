अस्माकं देवाः उभयाय जनमने शर्म यच्छत द्विपद चतुष्पदः |अदत पिबद ऊर्ज्यमानम् आशितं तद अस्मे शं योर अरपो दधातन ||
asmākaṁ devā ubhayāya janmane śarma yacchata dvipade catuṣpade | adat pibad ūrjayamānam āśitaṁ tad asme śaṁ yor arapo dadhātana ||
  • Translation: "May the gods grant us happiness in both our births; may they provide us with shelter, both for the two-legged and the four-legged. May they give us strength and nourishment, and may that which we have consumed bring us peace and prosperity."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • asmākaṁ: "our" - indicating a collective identity.
    • devā: "gods" - representing divine forces or deities.
    • ubhayāya: "both" - referring to dual aspects, in this case, life and existence.
    • janmane: "births" - signifies the importance of life and rebirth.
    • śarma: "happiness" or "shelter" - a state of well-being and protection.
    • yacchata: "may grant" - a plea for blessings.
    • dvipade: "two-legged" - referring to humans.
    • catuṣpade: "four-legged" - referring to animals.
    • adat: "may give" - another plea for divine provision.
    • pibad: "to drink" - signifies nourishment.
    • ūrjayamānam: "strength" - the essence of vitality and energy.
    • āśitaṁ: "that which is consumed" - referring to food or sustenance.
    • tad: "that" - pointing to the blessings received.
    • asme: "to us" - emphasizing the collective need.
    • śaṁ: "peace" - a state of tranquility.
    • yor: "of both" - referring to the dual aspects of life.
    • arapo: "prosperity" - abundance and well-being.
    • dadhātana: "may grant" - a final plea for divine favor.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, people often pray for the well-being of both humans and animals, reflecting the mantra's call for universal happiness.
    • Farmers may invoke blessings for a bountiful harvest, ensuring that both their families and livestock thrive, embodying the essence of nourishment and strength.
    • In modern times, environmental movements emphasize the interconnectedness of all living beings, aligning with the mantra's plea for shelter and protection for all creatures.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking blessings for happiness, strength, and nourishment for all beings. It highlights the interconnectedness of life and the need for divine support in achieving peace and prosperity. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community prayers, environmental conservation efforts, and personal reflections on gratitude and well-being. By recognizing the significance of both human and animal welfare, we foster a harmonious existence that benefits all.
