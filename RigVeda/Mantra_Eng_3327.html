कां सुष्टुतिः शवसः सूनुम इन्द्रं अरवाचीनं राधसा आ ववर्तत |ददिर हि वीरो गृणते वसूनि स गोपतीर निष्सिधां नो जनासः ||
kā suṣṭutiḥ śavasaḥ sūnum indram arvācīnaṁ rādhasa ā vavartat | dadir hi vīro gṛṇate vasūni sa gopatir niṣṣidhāṁ no janāsaḥ ||
  • kā suṣṭutiḥ: This phrase translates to "What is the excellent praise?" It raises a question about the nature of praise and what constitutes true commendation.
  • śavasaḥ sūnum: This means "the son of the dead" or "the son of the body." It can refer to the physical aspect of existence, emphasizing the connection between life and death.
  • indram arvācīnaṁ: "Indra, the ancient one." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, rain, and the heavens. The term "arvācīnaṁ" suggests a timeless quality, indicating that Indra's influence transcends time.
  • rādhasa ā vavartat: This translates to "he who moves in the realm of the radiant." It implies a connection to light, energy, and the divine, suggesting that the subject of the mantra is engaged in a higher purpose.
  • dadir hi vīro: "For the brave one gives." This emphasizes the idea that true bravery is rewarded, and those who act with courage will receive blessings or gifts.
  • gṛṇate vasūni: "He who sings the treasures." This suggests that those who express gratitude and appreciation for the blessings in life will attract more abundance.
  • sa gopatir niṣṣidhāṁ: "He is the protector of the unrestrained." This indicates a guardian role, suggesting that there is a divine force that protects those who are free-spirited and unbound.
  • no janāsaḥ: "Of the people." This phrase connects the previous ideas to the community, emphasizing the collective aspect of praise and protection.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of praise, bravery, and divine protection. It highlights the importance of recognizing and appreciating the blessings in life, as well as the role of community in fostering a supportive environment. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in leadership, where a leader who praises their team fosters a positive atmosphere, or in personal relationships, where expressing gratitude strengthens bonds. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of courage, and the blessings that come from a grateful heart.
