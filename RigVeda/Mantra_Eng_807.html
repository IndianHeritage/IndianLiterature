उप प्र जिवन्न उषतीर उषन्तं पतिं न नित्यं जनयः सनीलाः |स्वसारः श्याविम अरुषीम अजुष्रञ् चित्रम् उचन्तीम् उषसं न गावाḥ ||
upa pra jinvann uśatīr uśantam patiṁ na nityaṁ janayaḥ sanīḻāḥ | svasāraḥ śyāvīm aruṣīm ajuṣrañ citram ucchantīm uṣasaṁ na gāvaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "upa pra jinvann uśatīr uśantam patiṁ na nityaṁ janayaḥ sanīḻāḥ | svasāraḥ śyāvīm aruṣīm ajuṣrañ citram ucchantīm uṣasaṁ na gāvaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • upa: towards, near
    • pra: forth, forward
    • jinvann: winning, achieving
    • uśatīr: auspicious, beneficial
    • uśantam: the one who is prosperous
    • patiṁ: lord, master
    • na: not
    • nityaṁ: eternal, everlasting
    • janayaḥ: those who generate, create
    • sanīḻāḥ: those who are endowed with wealth
    • svasāraḥ: the one who is related, sister
    • śyāvīm: dark, black
    • aruṣīm: red, bright
    • ajuṣrañ: not to be neglected
    • citram: colorful, diverse
    • ucchantīm: the one who is called, invoked
    • uṣasaṁ: dawn, morning
    • na: not
    • gāvaḥ: cows, wealth
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the auspiciousness of the dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and opportunities.
    • The phrase "upa pra jinvann" suggests a movement towards achieving success and prosperity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of generating wealth and abundance, as indicated by "janayaḥ sanīḻāḥ".
    • The mention of "svasāraḥ" and the colors "śyāvīm" and "aruṣīm" can be interpreted as the duality of life, where both dark and light aspects coexist.
    • The mantra encourages us to recognize and embrace the diversity of life, represented by "citram".
    • In real life, this can be seen in the way we approach challenges and opportunities, understanding that both success and failure are part of the journey.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of striving for prosperity while acknowledging the dualities of life.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios such as personal growth, business ventures, and relationships, where one must navigate through various challenges to achieve success.
    • By invoking the essence of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset that embraces both the light and dark aspects of their experiences, leading to a more balanced and fulfilling life.
