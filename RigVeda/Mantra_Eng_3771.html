स हि द्युिभिर जनानां होतā दाक्षस्य बाह्वोः |विः हव्यं अग्निर अनुषग भगो न वारम् ऋण्वति ||
sa hi dyubhir janānāṁ hotā dakṣasya bāhvoḥ | vi havyam agnir ānuṣag bhago na vāram ṛṇvati ||
  • sa hi dyubhir janānāṁ: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, the one who is the hota (priest) of the people in the heavens." Here, "hota" refers to the priest or the one who performs rituals, and "dyubhir" signifies the celestial or heavenly aspect.
  • hotā dakṣasya bāhvoḥ: This means "the priest of the capable (dakṣa) arms." "Dakṣa" implies skill or capability, and "bāhvoḥ" refers to arms or strength. This suggests that the priest is associated with those who possess strength and skill.
  • vi havyam agnir ānuṣag: Translated, this means "the fire (Agni) that is invoked (anuṣag) for offerings." "Havyam" refers to offerings made to the divine, and "Agni" is the fire god, symbolizing purity and the medium through which offerings are made to the deities.
  • bhago na vāram ṛṇvati: This translates to "the divine wealth (bhago) does not decline (vāram ṛṇvati)." This indicates that the blessings or wealth bestowed by the divine are everlasting and do not diminish.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of the priestly role in connecting the earthly realm with the divine, highlighting the significance of skill and strength in performing rituals. It also underscores the idea that divine blessings, represented by Agni, are eternal and do not fade away. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to the practice of rituals and offerings in various cultures, where the act of giving and invoking divine presence is believed to bring prosperity and strength to individuals and communities. It serves as a reminder of the importance of spiritual practices in fostering a connection with the divine and the enduring nature of blessings received through such acts.
