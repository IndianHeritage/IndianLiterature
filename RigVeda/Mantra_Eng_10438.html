यस्तौ ऊरू विहरत्यन्तरां दम्पती शये |योऽन्तरारेषि तं इतो नाशयामसि ||
yas ta ūrū viharaty antarā dampatī śaye | yoniṁ yo antar āreḻhi tam ito nāśayāmasi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • ta: that
    • ūrū: thighs
    • viharaty: dwells or resides
    • antarā: between
    • dampatī: husband and wife
    • śaye: in the bed
    • yoniṁ: womb or source
    • yo: who
    • antar: within
    • āreḻhi: has entered
    • tam: him
    • ito: from here
    • nāśayāmasi: we do not destroy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the sacred union between a husband and wife, emphasizing the intimacy and connection that exists between them.
    • The phrase "who dwells between the thighs" symbolizes the physical and emotional closeness that is integral to a marital relationship.
    • The mention of "womb" signifies the creative and nurturing aspect of this union, highlighting the importance of family and progeny.
    • The mantra concludes with a commitment to not destroy this sacred bond, indicating the importance of preserving love and harmony in relationships.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern marriages, couples often face challenges that can strain their relationship. This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing the bond they share, much like how partners in a relationship should prioritize communication and understanding.
    • In family planning, the significance of the "womb" can be seen in the decisions couples make regarding having children, emphasizing the joy and responsibility that comes with parenthood.
    • In a broader context, this mantra can be applied to any close relationship, where the essence of connection, respect, and commitment is vital for its longevity.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra encapsulates the essence of marital harmony and the sacredness of the union between partners.
    • It serves as a reminder to cherish and protect the intimate bonds we share with our loved ones, reinforcing the idea that love and commitment are foundational to a fulfilling relationship.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate deeper connections, prioritize their relationships, and approach challenges with a mindset of preservation and respect.
