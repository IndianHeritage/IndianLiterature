स त्वं अग्नि सौभगत्वस्य विद्वान् अस्माकं आयुः प्र तिरेह देव |तन नो मित्रो वरुणो मामहन्ताम् अदितिः सिन्धुः पृथिवी उता द्यौः ||
sa tvam agne saubhagatvasya vidvān asmākam āyuḥ pra tireha deva | tan no mitro varuṇo māmahantām aditiḥ sindhuḥ pṛthivī uta dyauḥ ||
  • sa tvam agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni," referring to the fire god, who is considered a messenger between humans and the divine. In real life, fire symbolizes transformation and purification, much like how we use fire in rituals and cooking to transform raw ingredients into nourishing meals.
  • saubhagatvasya vidvān: This means "the wise one of auspiciousness." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in achieving good fortune. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way educated decisions lead to better outcomes, such as choosing a career path or making financial investments.
  • asmākam āyuḥ pra tireha deva: This translates to "may you grant us longevity, O deity." It reflects the human desire for a long and healthy life. In modern contexts, this can relate to health practices, such as balanced diets and regular exercise, which are essential for longevity.
  • tan no mitro varuṇo: This means "may Mitra and Varuna be favorable to us." Mitra and Varuna are deities associated with friendship and cosmic order, respectively. In real life, this can be interpreted as the importance of maintaining good relationships and harmony in society, which can lead to collective well-being.
  • māmahantām aditiḥ: This translates to "may the great Aditi protect us." Aditi is the mother of the gods and symbolizes boundless space and protection. In practical terms, this can be seen in the way communities come together to support one another during crises, reflecting the protective nature of Aditi.
  • sindhuḥ pṛthivī uta dyauḥ: This means "the ocean, the earth, and the sky." These elements represent the vastness of nature and the interconnectedness of all life. In real life, this can be related to environmental awareness and the need to protect our planet, as all living beings are part of a larger ecosystem.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking wisdom, longevity, and divine protection while emphasizing the importance of relationships and the natural world. Its significance lies in its holistic approach to life, encouraging individuals to strive for personal growth, community harmony, and environmental stewardship. In today's context, it serves as a reminder to cultivate wisdom in our decisions, nurture our relationships, and respect the natural world, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
