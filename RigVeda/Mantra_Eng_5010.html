सां नो विश्वा अतिद्विषः स्वसृर अन्याऽऋतावरि |अतन्न अहेव सूर्यः ||
sā no viśvā ati dviṣaḥ svasṝr anyā ṛtāvarī | atann aheva sūryaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sā: that
    • no: for us
    • viśvā: all, universal
    • ati: beyond, surpassing
    • dviṣaḥ: enemies, those who hate
    • svasṝr: our own, related
    • anyā: other, different
    • ṛtāvarī: truth, righteousness
    • atann: may not be, do not
    • aheva: indeed, certainly
    • sūryaḥ: the sun
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the universal forces for protection and harmony.
    • It emphasizes the importance of truth and righteousness in overcoming negativity and hostility.
    • The mention of the sun symbolizes enlightenment, clarity, and the dispelling of darkness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, fostering a culture of openness and collaboration can help overcome conflicts and negativity.
    • In personal relationships, practicing honesty and understanding can lead to stronger bonds and resolution of misunderstandings.
    • In community settings, promoting shared values and mutual respect can help in addressing social issues and conflicts.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of truth and righteousness in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals and communities to strive for harmony and understanding, transcending hatred and division.
    • By embodying these principles, we can create a more peaceful and enlightened society, much like the sun dispels darkness.
