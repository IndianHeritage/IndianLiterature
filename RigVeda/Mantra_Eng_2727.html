पित्रे चिच्चक्रुः सदनं सम अस्मै महि त्विषीमत सुखृतो विहि ख्यन |विष्कभ्नन्तः स्कम्भनेन जनित्री आसीनाः ऊर्ध्वं रभसं विं मिन्वन ||
pitre cic cakruḥ sadanaṁ sam asmai mahi tviṣīmat sukṛto vi hi khyan | viṣkabhnantaḥ skambhanenā janitrī āsīnā ūrdhvaṁ rabhasaṁ vi minvan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pitre: to the father
    • cic: indeed
    • cakruḥ: they have made
    • sadanaṁ: a dwelling or abode
    • sam: together
    • asmai: to him
    • mahi: great
    • tviṣīmat: shining or radiant
    • sukṛto: good deeds
    • vi: indeed
    • hi: for
    • khyan: the one who is nourished
    • viṣkabhnantaḥ: those who are separated
    • skambhanenā: by the support or foundation
    • janitrī: the mother
    • āsīnā: seated
    • ūrdhvaṁ: upwards
    • rabhasaṁ: with force or energy
    • vi: indeed
    • minvan: to be made manifest
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the relationship between the father and the mother, emphasizing the importance of their roles in creating a nurturing environment.
    • The phrase "they have made a dwelling" suggests the creation of a home filled with love and support, which is essential for the growth of individuals.
    • The mention of "good deeds" highlights the significance of virtuous actions in maintaining harmony within the family unit.
    • The reference to the mother being "seated upwards" indicates her elevated status and the respect she commands in the family structure.
    • Overall, the mantra reflects the interconnectedness of familial relationships and the foundational role they play in society.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the family unit is considered the cornerstone of society. For instance, in Indian culture, the respect for parents is deeply ingrained, and children are taught to honor their mothers and fathers.
    • In modern times, creating a nurturing home environment can be seen in practices such as family dinners, where members come together to share their experiences and strengthen their bonds.
    • Community service and good deeds, as mentioned in the mantra, can be observed in families that engage in charitable activities, teaching children the value of giving back to society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of familial relationships and the values that bind them together.
    • It emphasizes the need for a supportive home environment, where good deeds and mutual respect flourish.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger family ties, a sense of community, and a more compassionate society.
