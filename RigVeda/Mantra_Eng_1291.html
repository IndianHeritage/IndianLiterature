रयिं सुखात्रं स्वपत्यम् आयुः सुवीर्यं नासत्याः वहन्ता |आ जहनावीं समनसोपा वाजैः त्रिरह्नो भागं दधतीं अयातम् ||
rayiṁ sukṣatraṁ svapatyam āyuḥ suvīryaṁ nāsatyā vahantā | ā jahnāvīṁ samanasopa vājais trir ahno bhāgaṁ dadhatīm ayātam ||
  • Rayiṁ: This word refers to wealth or prosperity. In real life, it can be associated with financial stability or abundance in resources.
  • Sukṣatraṁ: This term means auspicious or favorable. It signifies the importance of good fortune in our endeavors, such as starting a new business or project.
  • Svapatyam: This translates to offspring or progeny. It highlights the value of family and the continuation of lineage, which can be seen in the importance placed on raising children and nurturing future generations.
  • Āyuḥ: Meaning life or longevity, this word emphasizes the desire for a long and healthy life, which is a common aspiration for individuals and families.
  • Suvīryaṁ: This means strength or valor. It can be related to personal strength in overcoming challenges, such as facing adversity in one's career or personal life.
  • Nāsatyā: Referring to the twin deities, the Aśvins, who symbolize health and well-being. This can be connected to the importance of good health in our daily lives.
  • Vahantā: This means to carry or bear. It signifies the act of bringing forth or nurturing, which can be seen in the responsibilities of parents or leaders in society.
  • Ā Jahnāvīṁ: This refers to the Ganges River, symbolizing purity and the flow of life. It can be related to the importance of clean water and environmental conservation in our lives.
  • Samanasopa: This means to be in harmony or balance. It emphasizes the need for balance in life, such as work-life balance or emotional stability.
  • Vājais: This translates to strength or power, often associated with horses in Vedic literature. It can symbolize the power of knowledge and education in achieving success.
  • Trir ahno: Meaning threefold or thrice, it can represent the importance of repetition and consistency in achieving goals, such as practicing a skill regularly.
  • Bhāgaṁ: This means share or portion. It signifies the importance of sharing resources and wealth with others, promoting community and social responsibility.
  • Dadathīm: This translates to giving or bestowing. It emphasizes the act of generosity and kindness in our interactions with others.
  • Ayātam: This means to be unyielding or steadfast. It signifies the importance of resilience in the face of challenges, such as persevering through difficult times.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of prosperity, health, strength, and harmony in life. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of wealth, family, and well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects, such as striving for financial stability, nurturing relationships, maintaining good health, and fostering a sense of community. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can lead a balanced and fulfilling life, contributing positively to society and future generations.
