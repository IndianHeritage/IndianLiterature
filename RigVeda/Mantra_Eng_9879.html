यā सुजūr्णिः श्रेणीः सुम्नापिर ह्रदेकक्षुर न ग्रन्थिनी चराण्युः |तā अञ्जयो 'रुणयो न सस्रुः श्रीये गāवो न धेनवो 'नवन्त ||
yā sujūrṇiḥ śreṇiḥ sumnaāpir hradecakṣur na granthinī caraṇyuḥ | tā añjayo 'ruṇayo na sasruḥ śriye gāvo na dhenavo 'navanta ||
  • yā sujūrṇiḥ: This phrase can be translated as "that which is well-formed" or "that which is auspicious." In real life, this can refer to anything that is organized and beneficial, such as a well-structured community or a harmonious family.
  • śreṇiḥ: This word means "group" or "assembly." It signifies the importance of community and collaboration. For example, in a workplace, a team that works well together can achieve greater success than individuals working in isolation.
  • sumnaāpir: This term translates to "with good intentions" or "auspiciously." It emphasizes the need for positive motives in our actions. In daily life, this can be seen in acts of kindness, where individuals help others with genuine intentions.
  • hradecakṣur: This phrase means "with the eye of the heart." It suggests seeing beyond the surface and understanding deeper emotions and connections. For instance, a good friend who empathizes with your struggles is someone who sees with the "eye of the heart."
  • na granthinī: This means "not bound" or "not tied." It implies freedom and liberation from constraints. In a practical sense, this can relate to breaking free from societal norms that limit personal growth.
  • caraṇyuḥ: This word can be interpreted as "those who move" or "those who progress." It signifies the importance of action and movement in life. For example, individuals who pursue their dreams actively rather than waiting for opportunities are embodying this principle.
  • tā añjayo: This translates to "they who are swift" or "they who are quick." It highlights the value of being proactive and responsive in life. In a business context, companies that adapt quickly to market changes often thrive.
  • 'ruṇayo: This term means "with strength" or "with vigor." It emphasizes the importance of resilience and determination. For instance, athletes who train rigorously to overcome challenges exemplify this strength.
  • na sasruḥ: This means "not falling" or "not failing." It suggests stability and success. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who maintain their integrity and values despite facing difficulties.
  • śriye: This word translates to "prosperity" or "wealth." It signifies the importance of abundance in various forms, not just material wealth but also emotional and spiritual richness.
  • gāvo: This means "cows," which in Vedic culture symbolize nourishment and wealth. In modern terms, this can relate to sustainable practices that ensure the well-being of both people and the environment.
  • na dhenavo: This translates to "not the milch cows," indicating a focus on the essence of wealth rather than just the means of obtaining it. It suggests that true prosperity comes from sustainable practices rather than exploitation.
  • 'navanta: This means "not diminishing" or "ever-increasing." It signifies the idea of growth and abundance that is sustainable over time.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of community, positive intentions, emotional understanding, freedom, proactivity, strength, stability, and sustainable prosperity. In real life, these principles can guide individuals and communities towards harmonious living, fostering environments where everyone can thrive. By embodying these values, we can create a society that is not only prosperous but also compassionate and resilient.
