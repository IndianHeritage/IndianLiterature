त्वं हि विश्वतोमुख विश्वतः परिभूर् असि |अप नः शोशुचदाघम् ||
tvaṁ hi viśvatomukha viśvataḥ paribhūr asi | apa naḥ śośucad agham ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • hi: indeed
    • viśvatomukha: having faces in all directions
    • viśvataḥ: from all sides
    • paribhūr: encompassing or pervading
    • asi: are
    • apa: away from
    • naḥ: our
    • śośucad: may you remove
    • agham: sin or impurity
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses a divine entity, acknowledging its omnipresence and all-encompassing nature.
    • The phrase "having faces in all directions" suggests that the divine is aware of everything happening in the universe, symbolizing omniscience.
    • The request to "remove our sin" indicates a plea for purification and protection from negative influences or actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek guidance and support from a higher power during challenging times, reflecting the desire for divine intervention to cleanse their lives of negativity.
    • In a community setting, people may come together in prayer or meditation, invoking this mantra to foster unity and seek collective strength against adversity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing a higher power that is all-encompassing and aware of our actions.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking forgiveness and striving for purity in thoughts and deeds.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of reflection, meditation, or communal gatherings, promoting a sense of connection to the divine and to one another.
