उत त्ये मां ध्वन्यस्य जुष्टा लक्ष्मण्यस्य सुरुचो यतानाः |मह्ना रायः संवरणस्य ऋषेर व्रजं न गावाḥ प्रयता अपि गमन ||
uta tye mā dhvanyasya juṣṭā lakṣmaṇyasya suruco yatānāḥ | mahnā rāyaḥ saṁvaraṇasya ṛṣer vrajaṁ na gāvaḥ prayatā api gman ||
  • uta tye: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we attain" or "let us achieve." It signifies a desire for success and fulfillment in our endeavors.
  • mā dhvanyasya: "mā" means "not," and "dhvanyasya" refers to "the sound" or "the call." This suggests a caution against distractions or negative influences that may hinder our progress.
  • juṣṭā lakṣmaṇyasya: "juṣṭā" means "filled" or "endowed," while "lakṣmaṇyasya" refers to "prosperity" or "wealth." Together, this implies a state of being enriched with good fortune and abundance.
  • surucō yatānāḥ: "surucō" means "beautiful" or "pleasant," and "yatānāḥ" refers to "efforts" or "endeavors." This part emphasizes the importance of pursuing noble and beautiful efforts in life.
  • mahnā rāyaḥ: "mahnā" means "great" or "vast," and "rāyaḥ" refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." This indicates a call for immense prosperity and abundance in our lives.
  • saṁvaraṇasya ṛṣer: "saṁvaraṇasya" means "of protection" or "of safeguarding," and "ṛṣer" refers to "the sage" or "the wise one." This suggests the need for guidance and protection from wise individuals in our pursuits.
  • vrajaṁ na gāvaḥ: "vrajaṁ" means "to the place" or "to the goal," while "na gāvaḥ" translates to "not the cows." This metaphorically indicates that we should not be distracted by trivial matters (represented by cows) but focus on our ultimate goals.
  • prayatā api gman: "prayatā" means "with effort" or "with diligence," and "gman" refers to "to achieve" or "to reach." This emphasizes the importance of hard work and dedication in achieving our aspirations.

Conclusion: This mantra from the RigVeda encapsulates the essence of striving for prosperity and success while remaining focused on noble pursuits. It serves as a reminder to seek guidance from wise individuals and to avoid distractions that may lead us astray. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in professional settings where one aims for career advancement, in personal development endeavors, or in community service where the focus is on contributing positively to society. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can cultivate a life of purpose, abundance, and fulfillment.
