उप नः सूनवो गिरः श्रिण्वन्तु अमृतस्य ये |सुमृळीकाऽ भवन्तु नः ||
upa naḥ sūnavo giraḥ śṛṇvantv amṛtasya ye | sumṛḻīkā bhavantu naḥ ||
  • Mantra: "upa naḥ sūnavo giraḥ śṛṇvantv amṛtasya ye | sumṛḻīkā bhavantu naḥ ||"
  • Translation: "May the divine words of the wise reach us, those who are the bearers of immortality; may they bring us prosperity."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • upa: towards, near
    • naḥ: our
    • sūnavo: wise, learned
    • giraḥ: words, speech
    • śṛṇvantv: may they listen
    • amṛtasya: of immortality
    • ye: who
    • sumṛḻīkā: may they bring prosperity
    • bhavantu: may they become
    • naḥ: our
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for wisdom and guidance. It emphasizes the importance of listening to the teachings of the wise.
    • The phrase "upa naḥ sūnavo giraḥ" suggests a call to the wise to share their knowledge with us, highlighting the value of learning from those who have experience and insight.
    • "amṛtasya ye" refers to the teachings that lead to immortality, which can be interpreted as spiritual wisdom or knowledge that transcends the physical realm.
    • The concluding part, "sumṛḻīkā bhavantu naḥ," expresses a desire for these teachings to bring prosperity and well-being into our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied when seeking mentorship from experienced leaders. By listening to their guidance, employees can gain insights that lead to career growth and success.
    • In education, students benefit from the wisdom of their teachers. This mantra encourages students to be receptive to the knowledge imparted by their educators, which can lead to a deeper understanding of subjects and personal development.
    • In personal relationships, seeking advice from wise friends or family members can help individuals navigate challenges and make informed decisions, leading to a more fulfilling life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of wisdom and the pursuit of knowledge in our lives. It encourages us to be open to learning from others and to seek guidance from those who possess greater understanding.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and overall prosperity. It serves as a reminder that wisdom is a valuable asset that can guide us through life's challenges.
