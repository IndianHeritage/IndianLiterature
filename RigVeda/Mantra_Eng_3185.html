मन्त्र: भद्रं ते अग्ने सहसिन्न अनीकम् उपाक आ रोचते सूर्यस्य |रुशद दृष्टे ददृशे नक्तया चिद अरूक्षितं दृष्ट आ रूपे अन्नम् ||
bhadraṁ te agne sahasinn anīkam upāka ā rocate sūryasya | ruśad dṛśe dadṛśe naktayā cid arūkṣitaṁ dṛśa ā rūpe annam ||
  • bhadraṁ te agne: This phrase translates to "May auspiciousness be upon you, O Agni (the fire god)." Agni is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine, representing purity and transformation.
  • sahasinn anīkam: This means "with a thousand forms." It signifies the multifaceted nature of Agni, who manifests in various forms and aspects in our lives, such as warmth, light, and energy.
  • upāka ā rocate sūryasya: This translates to "may it shine forth like the sun." Here, the sun symbolizes vitality, life, and enlightenment. Just as the sun illuminates the world, Agni is invoked to bring clarity and positivity into our lives.
  • ruśad dṛśe: This means "may it be seen with joy." It emphasizes the importance of perceiving the blessings and positivity that Agni brings into our lives.
  • dadṛśe naktayā: This translates to "may it be visible even in the night." This suggests that the light of Agni can guide us through dark times, providing hope and direction when we face challenges.
  • cid arūkṣitaṁ dṛśa: This means "may it not be obstructed." It indicates the desire for the blessings of Agni to flow freely and unobstructed in our lives.
  • ā rūpe annam: This translates to "in the form of food." Food is essential for sustenance and life, and here it symbolizes nourishment not just for the body but also for the mind and spirit.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of Agni as a source of auspiciousness, guidance, and nourishment in our lives. It highlights the importance of recognizing and appreciating the blessings that come our way, even in challenging times. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during rituals, prayers, or moments of reflection, reminding us to seek positivity and clarity in our actions and thoughts. By invoking Agni, we can cultivate an environment of warmth, light, and sustenance, both physically and spiritually, fostering a sense of community and connection with the divine.
