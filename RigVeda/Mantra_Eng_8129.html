एष वृषा वृषव्रतः पवमानो अशस्तिहा |करद वसूनि दाशुषे ||
eṣa vṛṣā vṛṣavrataḥ pavamāno aśastihā | karad vasūni dāśuṣe ||
  • eṣa: This word translates to "this" or "he," indicating a specific entity or presence that is being referred to.
  • vṛṣā: This term means "the one who is strong" or "the bull," symbolizing power, strength, and vitality.
  • vṛṣavrataḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is committed to strength" or "the vow of strength," suggesting a dedication to maintaining power and integrity.
  • pavamāno: This word means "purifying" or "the one who purifies," indicating a cleansing or sanctifying force.
  • aśastihā: This term can be understood as "the one who is established in goodness" or "the one who is virtuous," emphasizing moral integrity and righteousness.
  • karad: This translates to "with hands" or "by action," highlighting the importance of active participation and effort.
  • vasūni: This word means "wealth" or "prosperity," referring to the abundance of resources and blessings.
  • dāśuṣe: This term translates to "for the one who gives" or "to the generous," indicating a focus on sharing and generosity.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to recognize and embody strength and purity, while actively engaging in virtuous actions that lead to prosperity and generosity. It emphasizes the importance of being strong and committed to goodness, while also purifying oneself and contributing positively to the world.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In personal development, individuals can strive to embody strength and integrity in their actions, ensuring that they remain committed to their values.
  • In leadership, this mantra serves as a reminder for leaders to act with strength and virtue, promoting a culture of generosity and support within their teams.
  • In community service, the emphasis on giving and sharing wealth can inspire individuals to engage in charitable activities, helping those in need and fostering a sense of community.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to strength, purity, and generosity. It encourages individuals to cultivate their inner strength while actively participating in the betterment of society. By embodying these principles, one can lead a fulfilling life that not only benefits themselves but also uplifts others around them.
