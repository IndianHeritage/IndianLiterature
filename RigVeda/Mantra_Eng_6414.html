पाकत्रा स्थान देवाः हृत्सु जानीत मर्त्यम् |उप द्वयं चाद्वयं च वसवः ||
pākatrā sthana devā hṛtsu jānītha martyam | upa dvayuṁ cādvayuṁ ca vasavaḥ ||
  • pākatrā: This word can be interpreted as "pure" or "sacred." In real life, purity is often associated with cleanliness, both physical and mental. For example, maintaining a clean environment can lead to better health and well-being.
  • sthāna: This means "place" or "position." In a broader sense, it can refer to one's place in society or the universe. For instance, understanding one's role in a family or community can lead to harmony and cooperation.
  • devā: This translates to "gods" or "divine beings." In many cultures, people look to divine figures for guidance and inspiration. For example, individuals may pray to deities for wisdom in making important life decisions.
  • hṛtsu: This word means "in the hearts." It signifies the emotional and spiritual aspects of human existence. Real-life examples include the importance of empathy and compassion in relationships, where feelings are deeply felt in the heart.
  • jānītha: This translates to "you know" or "you understand." Knowledge and awareness are crucial in everyday life, such as being informed about social issues or understanding personal responsibilities.
  • martyam: This word means "mortal" or "human." It reminds us of our human limitations and the transient nature of life. For example, recognizing our mortality can inspire us to live more fully and appreciate each moment.
  • upa: This means "near" or "towards." It suggests a movement towards something, such as striving for personal goals or seeking closeness in relationships.
  • dvayuṁ: This can be interpreted as "two" or "duality." In life, we often encounter dualities, such as joy and sorrow, success and failure. Understanding these dualities can help us navigate life's challenges.
  • cādvayuṁ: This means "and the non-dual." It refers to the idea of unity beyond dualities. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of peace and understanding among diverse groups, recognizing our shared humanity.
  • vasavaḥ: This translates to "the powerful" or "the strong." It signifies strength and resilience. In everyday life, this can be exemplified by individuals who overcome adversity through determination and courage.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of purity, understanding, and the divine presence in our hearts. It encourages us to recognize our place in the world and the dualities of life while striving for unity and strength. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek knowledge, foster compassion, and embrace their humanity, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine guidance available to us in our journey through life.
