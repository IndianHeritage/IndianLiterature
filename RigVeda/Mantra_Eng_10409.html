इमा नु कम bhuvanā सिषधामेन्द्रश च विश्वे च देवाः ||
imā nu kam bhuvanā sīṣadhāmendraś ca viśve ca devāḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • imā: This means "these" or "this" in the context of the mantra, referring to the present moment or the current situation.
    • nu: This is an emphatic particle, often used to emphasize the statement that follows.
    • kam: This translates to "who" or "what," indicating a question or inquiry about existence or reality.
    • bhuvanā: This means "worlds" or "realms," referring to the various dimensions of existence.
    • sīṣadhām: This can be interpreted as "the one who holds" or "the one who supports," often referring to a divine or powerful entity.
    • indraś: This refers to "Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
    • ca: This means "and," connecting different elements of the mantra.
    • viśve: This translates to "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity of all beings or entities.
    • ca: Again, this means "and," reinforcing the connection between the elements.
    • devāḥ: This means "gods" or "divine beings," representing the spiritual and divine aspects of existence.
  • Meaning:
    • The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine, asking, "Who are these beings that hold the worlds?"
    • It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all realms and the divine forces that govern them.
    • By invoking Indra and the universal gods, it acknowledges the power and presence of divine energy in the world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a moment of uncertainty, one might reflect on this mantra to seek guidance and strength from the divine, recognizing that there are greater forces at play in their lives.
    • During a community gathering or a celebration, invoking the essence of this mantra can foster a sense of unity and connection among individuals, reminding them of their shared existence and the divine support surrounding them.
    • In times of crisis, individuals may turn to this mantra as a source of comfort, understanding that they are part of a larger cosmic order that is upheld by divine beings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It encourages individuals to seek strength and support from the divine, especially in challenging times.
    • By recognizing the power of the gods and the worlds they hold, one can cultivate a deeper sense of purpose and connection in their daily life.
