मन्त्र: इन्द्र याहि मत्वा चित्रेण देव राधसः |सरो न प्रास्य उदरं सपीतिभिरा सोमैभिरुरु स्फिरम् ||
endra yāhi matsva citreṇa deva rādhasā | saro na prāsy udaraṁ sapītibhir ā somebhir uru sphiram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • endra: O Indra, the king of gods
    • yāhi: come
    • matsva: to my place
    • citreṇa: with brilliance or splendor
    • deva: O divine one
    • rādhasā: with abundance or wealth
    • saro: with the waters
    • na: not
    • prāsy: to be taken away
    • udaraṁ: the belly or the womb
    • sapītibhir: with the drinkers (of soma)
    • ā: with
    • somebhir: with the soma (sacred drink)
    • uru: wide or vast
    • sphiram: to be filled or to swell
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, inviting him to come with his brilliance and abundance.
    • The mention of "soma" refers to a sacred drink that was believed to confer immortality and divine insight, symbolizing spiritual nourishment.
    • The phrase "saro na prāsy udaraṁ" suggests a plea for sustenance and abundance, emphasizing the importance of nourishment in both physical and spiritual realms.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a metaphor for seeking guidance and support from higher powers or mentors when facing challenges.
    • Just as the ancient Vedic people sought the blessings of Indra for prosperity, individuals today often seek inspiration and abundance in their personal and professional lives.
    • The concept of "soma" can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and experiences that enrich our lives, much like how we seek education and personal growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine guidance and the pursuit of abundance in life.
    • It teaches us to seek support from higher powers, whether through spirituality, community, or mentorship, to achieve our goals.
    • In a broader sense, it reminds us of the interconnectedness of physical and spiritual nourishment, encouraging a holistic approach to well-being.
