हवयामसि तवेन्द्र याह्य अर्वाङ् अरं ते सोमस तन्वे भवाति |शतक्रतो मादयस्वा सुतेषु प्रास्मां अव पृतनासु प्र विक्षु ||
hvayāmasi tvendra yāhy arvāṅ araṁ te somas tanve bhavāti | śatakrato mādayasvā suteṣu prāsmām̐ ava pṛtanāsu pra vikṣu ||
  • hvayāmasi: This term translates to "we invoke" or "we call upon." It signifies the act of seeking divine presence or assistance.
  • tvendra: This means "O Indra," referring to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, who is associated with strength, rain, and thunder.
  • yāhy: This word can be interpreted as "come" or "approach," indicating a request for the deity to be present.
  • arvāṅ: This term refers to "the one who is strong" or "the powerful one," emphasizing Indra's might.
  • araṁ: This means "to you," indicating a direction towards Indra, reinforcing the invocation.
  • te: This translates to "your," indicating possession or connection to Indra.
  • somas: Referring to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration.
  • tanve: This means "in the body" or "in the form," suggesting a physical manifestation of divine energy.
  • bhavāti: This translates to "may it become" or "may it manifest," indicating a desire for transformation or realization.
  • śatakrato: This term refers to "the one with a hundred powers," another epithet for Indra, highlighting his multifaceted strength.
  • mādayasvā: This means "be joyful" or "be pleased," expressing a wish for Indra's happiness and favor.
  • suteṣu: This translates to "in the offerings," referring to the rituals and sacrifices made to the gods.
  • prāsmām̐: This means "we have invoked" or "we have called upon," indicating a past action of seeking divine presence.
  • ava: This translates to "down" or "from above," suggesting a descent of divine grace or blessings.
  • pṛtanāsu: This means "in the praises" or "in the hymns," indicating the context of worship and reverence.
  • pra vikṣu: This translates to "may you be pleased" or "may you grant us," expressing a desire for divine favor and blessings.

Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation to Indra, seeking his presence and blessings in the context of rituals and offerings. It emphasizes the importance of divine connection in achieving strength, joy, and fulfillment in life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of seeking guidance, strength in challenges, or during rituals that require divine intervention. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness between the divine and human efforts, encouraging individuals to invoke higher powers for support and inspiration in their endeavors.
