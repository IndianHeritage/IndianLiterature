अस्मभ्यं तद वसो दानाय राधः सम अर्थयस्व बहु ते वसव्यं |इन्द्र यच चित्रं श्रवस्याऽनु dyūn बृहद वदेम विदथे सुवीराः ||
asmabhyaṁ tad vaso dānāya rādhaḥ sam arthayasva bahu te vasavyam | indra yac citraṁ śravasyā anu dyūn bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmabhyaṁ: for us
    • tad: that
    • vaso: of wealth
    • dānāya: for giving
    • rādhaḥ: prosperity
    • sam: together
    • arthayasva: may you bestow
    • bahu: many
    • te: your
    • vasavyam: wealth
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • yac: whatever
    • citraṁ: wonderful
    • śravasyā: of fame
    • anu: after
    • dyūn: the heavens
    • bṛhad: great
    • vadema: we may speak
    • vidathe: in the assembly
    • suvīrāḥ: of the brave
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for abundance and prosperity, invoking the blessings of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who is often associated with strength and wealth.
    • The phrase "asmabhyaṁ tad vaso dānāya" emphasizes the desire for wealth and resources to be shared among the community, highlighting the importance of generosity and collective prosperity.
    • In real life, this can be seen in community initiatives where resources are pooled together for the benefit of all, such as cooperative businesses or community gardens.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies the need for divine support in achieving these goals, suggesting that human efforts should be complemented by spiritual blessings.
    • The mention of "citraṁ śravasyā" reflects the aspiration for fame and recognition that comes from noble deeds, encouraging individuals to act with valor and integrity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals within a community and the importance of working together for mutual benefit.
    • It highlights the significance of seeking divine guidance in our endeavors, reinforcing the belief that success is not solely a result of individual effort but also of collective support and spiritual favor.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can inspire social responsibility, encouraging individuals and organizations to contribute positively to society, thereby creating a more prosperous and harmonious world.
