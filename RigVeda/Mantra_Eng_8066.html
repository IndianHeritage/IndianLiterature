मन्त्र: इन्दो यथा तव स्तवो यथा ते जातमन्दसः |नि बरहिषि प्रिय सदः ||
indo yathā tava stavo yathā te jātam andhasaḥ | ni barhiṣi priye sadaḥ ||
  • Indo: This term refers to the deity Indra, who is often associated with rain, thunder, and war in Vedic literature. Indra symbolizes strength and power.
  • Yathā: This word means "as" or "like," indicating a comparison or a manner in which something is done.
  • Tava: This translates to "your," indicating possession or belonging to the deity being addressed.
  • Stavo: This means "praise" or "hymn," referring to the act of worship or adoration directed towards the divine.
  • Yathā te: This phrase means "as you," continuing the comparison to the actions or qualities of the deity.
  • Jātam: This word means "born" or "created," suggesting the origin or emergence of something.
  • Andhasaḥ: This term refers to "darkness" or "ignorance," often symbolizing the challenges or obstacles in life.
  • Ni: This is a prefix that can mean "down" or "into," indicating a direction or focus.
  • Barhiṣi: This word refers to "sacred grass" or "offerings," which are often used in rituals and symbolize purity and devotion.
  • Priye: This means "beloved" or "dear," indicating affection or a close relationship with the divine.
  • Sadaḥ: This translates to "always" or "forever," suggesting continuity and permanence in the relationship with the divine.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to Indra, praising him for his strength and power, and seeking his guidance to overcome darkness and ignorance. It emphasizes the importance of devotion and the need for divine support in navigating life's challenges.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Overcoming Challenges: Just as the mantra seeks help from Indra to dispel darkness, individuals can invoke this spirit when facing difficulties, whether in personal relationships, career challenges, or health issues.
  • Seeking Guidance: The act of praising a higher power can serve as a reminder to seek wisdom and guidance in decision-making processes, encouraging individuals to reflect on their choices.
  • Fostering Community: The communal aspect of worship and praise can strengthen bonds within a community, promoting unity and collective strength in overcoming obstacles.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of divine support in overcoming ignorance and challenges in life. It serves as a reminder to seek strength and guidance from higher powers, fostering a sense of community and devotion that can lead to personal and collective growth.
