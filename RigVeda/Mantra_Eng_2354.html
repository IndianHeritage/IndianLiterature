ते क्षोणीभिर अरुणेभिर नाञ्जिभी रुद्राः ऋतस्य सदनेषु वावृधुः |निमेघमानाः अत्येन पाजसा सुचन्द्रं वर्णं दधिरे सुपेशसम् ||
te kṣoṇībhir aruṇebhir nāñjibhī rudrā ṛtasya sadaneṣu vāvṛdhuḥ | nimeghamānā atyena pājasā suścandraṁ varṇaṁ dadhire supeśasam ||
  • te kṣoṇībhir aruṇebhir: This phrase can be translated as "with the red rays of the dawn." Here, "kṣoṇī" refers to the earth or the ground, and "aruṇebhir" signifies the reddish hues of the early morning light. This imagery evokes the beauty of a new day, symbolizing hope and renewal.
  • nāñjibhī rudrā ṛtasya sadaneṣu vāvṛdhuḥ: This part translates to "the Rudras, who are not to be disturbed, dwell in the abode of truth." The term "Rudra" refers to a form of the divine, often associated with storm and wind, but also with healing and transformation. "Ṛtasya sadaneṣu" indicates the realm of truth and order, suggesting that the divine presence is stable and unshakeable.
  • nimeghamānā atyena pājasā: This translates to "the clouds that bring forth rain." "Nimeghamānā" refers to the clouds that are about to rain, while "atyena pājasā" suggests the abundance and richness that comes with the rain. This can be seen as a metaphor for nourishment and sustenance, both physically and spiritually.
  • suścandraṁ varṇaṁ dadhire supeśasam: This phrase means "they hold the beautiful color of the moon." "Suścandraṁ" refers to the bright and auspicious moonlight, while "varṇaṁ" signifies color or hue. "Supeśasam" indicates something that is well-formed or beautiful. This imagery connects to the idea of beauty and harmony in nature, reflecting the balance that exists in the universe.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates themes of renewal, divine presence, nourishment, and beauty. It emphasizes the connection between nature and the divine, illustrating how the elements of the earth and sky work together to create a harmonious existence.
  • Real-life Application: In our daily lives, we can draw inspiration from this mantra by recognizing the importance of balance and harmony in our surroundings. Just as the dawn brings new beginnings, we can embrace change and renewal in our own lives. The idea of divine presence can encourage us to seek truth and stability in our actions and decisions.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all things. By appreciating the beauty of nature and the divine forces at play, we can cultivate a sense of gratitude and mindfulness. This awareness can lead to a more fulfilling and harmonious life, where we honor both the natural world and the spiritual dimensions of our existence.
