द्विताय मृक्तवाहसे स्वस्य दक्षितस्य मंहनाः |इन्दुं स धत्त आanusak स्तोता चित्ते अमर्त्य ||
dvitāya mṛktavāhase svasya dakṣasya maṁhanā | induṁ sa dhatta ānuṣak stotā cit te amartya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dvitāya: Twofold or dual.
    • mṛktavāhase: One who carries or brings forth (mṛkta means 'to bring forth').
    • svasya: Of one's own or belonging to oneself.
    • dakṣasya: Skillful or capable.
    • maṁhanā: The act of nourishing or sustaining.
    • induṁ: The moon or a celestial body, often symbolizing nourishment and prosperity.
    • sa: He or that (referring to the divine or the celestial).
    • dhatta: May he hold or sustain.
    • ānuṣak: In accordance with or following the path.
    • stotā: The one who praises or worships.
    • cit: Consciousness or awareness.
    • te: Your or of you (referring to the divine).
    • amartya: Immortal or divine.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the dual nature of existence, emphasizing the importance of both the material and spiritual realms.
    • The phrase "mṛktavāhase svasya dakṣasya maṁhanā" suggests that one should skillfully nourish their own essence, indicating self-care and personal growth.
    • The reference to the moon ("induṁ") symbolizes the nurturing aspect of life, representing emotions, intuition, and the cyclical nature of existence.
    • By invoking the divine ("sa dhatta"), the mantra encourages individuals to seek support from higher powers while following their own path ("ānuṣak").
    • The act of praising or worshiping ("stotā") is a reminder of the importance of gratitude and recognition of the divine in our lives.
    • Finally, the mention of "amartya" highlights the aspiration for immortality, not in a physical sense, but through the legacy of our actions and the impact we have on others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often strive to balance their material needs with spiritual growth, reflecting the duality mentioned in the mantra.
    • Practices such as meditation and mindfulness can be seen as ways to nourish one's inner self, aligning with the idea of "dakṣasya maṁhanā."
    • In relationships, the nurturing aspect of the moon can be likened to the emotional support we provide to loved ones, emphasizing the importance of care and compassion.
    • Seeking guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders can be viewed as "sa dhatta," where individuals look for wisdom while navigating their own paths.
    • Acts of gratitude, such as keeping a gratitude journal, embody the essence of "stotā," fostering a positive mindset and appreciation for life's blessings.
    • Leaving a positive legacy through acts of kindness and service to others reflects the pursuit of "amartya," aiming for a lasting impact beyond one's lifetime.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of balancing material and spiritual aspects of life, encouraging self-nourishment and gratitude.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to seek divine support while actively participating in our own growth and the well-being of others.
    • In real-life scenarios, the teachings of this mantra can guide individuals in their personal development, relationships, and contributions to society, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and meaningful existence.
