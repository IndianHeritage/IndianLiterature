या ऋज्रां मह्यम् मामहे सह त्वचā हिरण्यया |एष विश्वानि अभ्य अस्तु सौभगासङ्गस्य स्वनद्रथः ||
ya ṛjrā mahyam māmahe saha tvacā hiraṇyayā | eṣa viśvāny abhy astu saubhagāsaṅgasya svanadrathaḥ ||
  • ya ṛjrā mahyam māmahe saha tvacā hiraṇyayā
    • ya - who
    • ṛjrā - is auspicious or brings good fortune
    • mahyam - to me
    • māmahe - we invoke or we call upon
    • saha - together with
    • tvacā - your skin or body
    • hiraṇyayā - golden or radiant
  • eṣa viśvāny abhy astu saubhagāsaṅgasya svanadrathaḥ
    • eṣa - this
    • viśvāny - all or universal
    • abhy - may come to us
    • astu - let it be
    • saubhagāsaṅgasya - of good fortune and auspiciousness
    • svanadrathaḥ - the one who is endowed with wealth or prosperity

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for auspiciousness and prosperity. The speaker is invoking a divine presence, asking for blessings that are radiant and golden, symbolizing wealth and abundance. The phrase "who brings good fortune" emphasizes the importance of seeking positive influences in our lives.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or reflection to attract positive energy and good fortune into their lives.
  • Business Ventures: Entrepreneurs may use this mantra before starting a new business or project, seeking blessings for success and prosperity.
  • Family Well-being: Families can chant this mantra together during auspicious occasions, such as weddings or festivals, to invoke blessings for happiness and abundance.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with the divine source of prosperity and auspiciousness. By invoking these blessings, one can cultivate a positive mindset and attract favorable circumstances in various aspects of life. It serves as a reminder to seek and appreciate the good fortune that surrounds us, fostering a sense of gratitude and abundance.
