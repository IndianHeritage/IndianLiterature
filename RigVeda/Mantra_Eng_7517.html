नव यो नवातिम पुरो बिभेद बाह्वोजसा |अहिं च वृत्रहावधीत ||
nava yo navatim puro bibheda bāhvojasā | ahiṁ ca vṛtrahāvadhīt ||
  • Mantra: "nava yo navatim puro bibheda bāhvojasā | ahiṁ ca vṛtrahāvadhīt ||"
  • Translation: "The one who has nine (nava) and who has attained the ninetieth (navatim) in the forefront (puro) has split (bibheda) with strength (bāhvojasā); and he has also slain (vṛtrahāvadhīt) the serpent (ahiṁ)."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Nava: Nine, representing completeness or a cycle.
    • Navatim: Ninetieth, indicating a higher state or achievement.
    • Puro: Forefront or front, suggesting leadership or prominence.
    • Bibheda: Split or divided, indicating a decisive action or separation.
    • Bāhvojasā: With strength or power, emphasizing the force behind the action.
    • Ahiṁ: Serpent, often symbolizing obstacles or challenges.
    • Vṛtrahāvadhīt: Slain or defeated, indicating overcoming challenges.

  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of overcoming challenges and achieving greatness through strength and determination.
    • The number nine can symbolize the completion of a cycle, while the ninetieth signifies reaching a new level of achievement.
    • The serpent (ahiṁ) often represents obstacles or fears that one must confront and overcome in life.
    • In real life, this can be seen in various scenarios such as personal growth, career advancements, or overcoming fears. For example, a student who faces challenges in their studies may find the strength to overcome these obstacles and achieve academic success.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength and determination in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that with perseverance, one can achieve greatness and conquer obstacles that may seem insurmountable.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, including personal development, professional endeavors, and spiritual growth.
    • By embodying the qualities of strength and resilience, individuals can navigate through difficulties and emerge victorious, much like the hero who slays the serpent in mythology.
