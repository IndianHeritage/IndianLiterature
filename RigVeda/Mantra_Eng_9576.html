द्रुहो निषत्तā पृशनी चिदेवैः पुरू शंसेन वावृधुषṭा इन्द्रम् |अभिवृतेव ता महापदेन ध्वान्तात प्रपित्वाद उद अरन्त गर्भाः ||
druho niṣattā pṛśanī cid evaiḥ purū śaṁsena vāvṛdhuṣ ṭa indram | abhīvṛteva tā mahāpadena dhvāntāt prapitvād ud aranta garbhāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "druho niṣattā pṛśanī cid evaiḥ purū śaṁsena vāvṛdhuṣ ṭa indram | abhīvṛteva tā mahāpadena dhvāntāt prapitvād ud aranta garbhāḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • druho: hostility or enmity
    • niṣattā: strength or power
    • pṛśanī: the one who is praised or revered
    • cid: consciousness or awareness
    • evaiḥ: indeed or truly
    • purū: many or abundant
    • śaṁsena: through praise or commendation
    • vāvṛdhuṣ: may they grow or increase
    • ṭa: a particle often used for emphasis
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods
    • abhīvṛteva: as if to expand or grow
    • tā: those
    • mahāpadena: with great strength or power
    • dhvāntāt: from darkness or ignorance
    • prapitvād: having overcome or transcended
    • ud: up or out
    • aranta: limitless or boundless
    • garbhāḥ: wombs or sources
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about overcoming enmity and darkness through the power of consciousness and praise.
    • It emphasizes the importance of strength and awareness in transcending negativity and ignorance.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, symbolizes the ultimate power and authority that can help in overcoming challenges.
    • The phrase "may they grow" suggests a desire for positive qualities to flourish in abundance.
    • In real life, this can be related to personal growth, where individuals strive to overcome their inner conflicts and negativity through self-awareness and positive reinforcement.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength and awareness to combat hostility and ignorance.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as conflict resolution, personal development, and fostering positive relationships.
    • By invoking the qualities of Indra, individuals can aspire to rise above challenges and cultivate a life filled with positivity and growth.
    • This mantra serves as a reminder that through consciousness and commendation, one can transcend darkness and emerge stronger.
