त्वं मायाभिर अनवद्य मायिनं श्रवस्यता मनसा वृत्म अरदयः |त्वां इन नरो वृणते गविष्टिषु त्वां विश्वासु हव्यास्व इष्टिषु ||
tvam māyābhir anavadya māyinaṁ śravasyatā manasā vṛtram ardayaḥ | tvām in naro vṛṇate gaviṣṭiṣu tvāṁ viśvāsu havyāsv iṣṭiṣu ||
  • tvam - This word translates to "you" or "thou," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or force.
  • māyābhir - This term refers to "by illusions" or "through the power of illusion," suggesting the presence of a transformative or deceptive force in the universe.
  • anavadya - Meaning "without fault" or "immaculate," this word highlights the purity and perfection of the addressed entity.
  • māyinaṁ - This translates to "the one who creates illusions," referring to a divine power that shapes reality.
  • śravasyatā - This word means "by fame" or "through renown," indicating the importance of reputation and recognition in the context of the divine.
  • manasā - Meaning "with the mind," this term emphasizes the role of thought and intention in understanding and connecting with the divine.
  • vṛtram - This refers to "the obstacle" or "the demon," symbolizing challenges or adversities that one may face in life.
  • ardayaḥ - This means "destroying" or "overcoming," indicating the power to conquer obstacles.
  • naro - This translates to "man" or "human," emphasizing the human experience and the quest for understanding.
  • vṛṇate - Meaning "chooses" or "selects," this word highlights the act of making conscious decisions.
  • gaviṣṭiṣu - This term refers to "in the offerings," suggesting the importance of rituals and sacrifices in connecting with the divine.
  • viśvāsu - Meaning "in all," this word indicates universality and the omnipresence of the divine.
  • havyāsv - This translates to "in the offerings," again emphasizing the significance of rituals in spiritual practice.
  • iṣṭiṣu - Meaning "in the sacrifices," this term reinforces the idea of devotion and the importance of offerings in worship.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine interaction with the world. It acknowledges the presence of a higher power that is both immaculate and capable of overcoming obstacles through the power of thought and intention.
  • Real-Life Application: In daily life, this mantra can be applied to personal challenges. For instance, when facing difficulties at work or in relationships, one can invoke the idea of overcoming these "vṛtrams" (obstacles) through focused intention and positive action. It encourages individuals to recognize their own power in choosing their paths and making offerings of effort and dedication to achieve their goals.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the divine's role in our lives and the importance of our own agency in navigating challenges. By understanding and embracing the concepts of illusion, intention, and sacrifice, individuals can cultivate a deeper connection with the divine and empower themselves to overcome life's obstacles.
