उप त्वा रण्वसंदृशं प्रयस्वन्तः सहस्कृत |अग्नि ससृज्महे गिरः ||
upa tvā raṇvasaṁdṛśam prayasvantaḥ sahaskṛta | agne sasṛjmahe giraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • upa: towards, near
    • tvā: you (Agni, the fire god)
    • raṇvasaṁdṛśam: shining brightly, radiant
    • prayasvantaḥ: those who are strong, vigorous
    • sahaskṛta: accomplished, skilled
    • agne: O Agni, the fire god
    • sasṛjmahe: we create, we invoke
    • giraḥ: words, hymns
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a call to Agni, the fire god, inviting him to come closer and bless the invokers with his radiant energy. It emphasizes the importance of strength and skill in the pursuit of knowledge and creation through words and hymns.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be likened to the way we seek inspiration and guidance in our endeavors. For instance, when starting a new project, individuals often look for motivation and support from mentors or leaders who embody the qualities of strength and skill.
    • In educational settings, students may invoke the spirit of knowledge and wisdom, akin to calling upon Agni, to enhance their learning experience and achieve academic success.
    • In personal development, individuals may use affirmations or motivational speeches to invoke their inner strength and capabilities, similar to how the mantra calls upon Agni for support.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine energy and strength, encouraging individuals to harness their skills and vigor in their pursuits. It serves as a reminder of the power of words and intentions in shaping our reality. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal growth, education, or professional endeavors, the essence of this mantra can inspire individuals to seek guidance, cultivate strength, and create meaningful outcomes through their actions and words.
