स नो बोधि श्रुधी हवम् उरुष्याणो अग्हायतः समस्मात् ||
sa no bodhi śrudhī havam uruṣyā ṇo aghāyataḥ samasmāt ||
  • sa - This word translates to "that" or "he," indicating a connection to a higher power or divine entity.
  • no - This means "our," suggesting a personal relationship or connection with the divine.
  • bodhi - Refers to "wisdom" or "enlightenment," emphasizing the importance of knowledge and understanding.
  • śrudhī - This term can be interpreted as "hearing" or "listening," highlighting the significance of being receptive to wisdom.
  • havam - This word means "sacrifice" or "offering," indicating the act of giving or devotion.
  • uruṣyā - Translates to "greatness" or "vastness," often associated with the divine or the universe.
  • ṇo - Again means "our," reinforcing the personal connection to the divine.
  • aghāyataḥ - This term means "free from sin" or "without obstacles," suggesting purity and the removal of hindrances.
  • samasmāt - This translates to "from all sides" or "from everywhere," indicating a holistic approach to wisdom and understanding.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for wisdom and enlightenment, asking for guidance from a higher power to help us navigate through life without obstacles. It emphasizes the importance of being open to receiving knowledge and understanding, as well as the need for personal devotion and sacrifice in the pursuit of wisdom.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Education: Students can recite this mantra before exams or important learning sessions to seek clarity and understanding.
  • Decision Making: Individuals facing tough choices can use this mantra to invoke wisdom and guidance, helping them to make informed decisions.
  • Meditation: Practicing mindfulness and meditation with this mantra can enhance one's ability to listen and absorb knowledge from the universe.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for wisdom and purity in our thoughts and actions. It serves as a reminder to seek knowledge, remain open to learning, and strive for a life free from obstacles. By incorporating this mantra into daily practices, individuals can cultivate a deeper understanding of themselves and the world around them, ultimately leading to a more enlightened and fulfilling life.
