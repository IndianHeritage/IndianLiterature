त्वां उ ते स्वाभुवः शुम्भन्त्य अश्वराद्हसः |वेति त्वां उपसेचनī वि वो मद ऋजीतिर अग्न आहुतिर विवक्षसे ||
tvām u te svābhuvaḥ śumbhanty aśvarādhasaḥ | veti tvām upasecanī vi vo mada ṛjītir agna āhutir vivakṣase ||
  • tvām - This word translates to "you" or "thy," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or force.
  • u - This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking thoughts or ideas together.
  • te - This means "your," further emphasizing the personal connection to the divine.
  • svābhuvaḥ - This term can be interpreted as "self-existent" or "of your own nature," suggesting a sense of independence and intrinsic value.
  • śumbhanty - This word means "to praise" or "to glorify," indicating an act of reverence towards the divine.
  • aśvarādhasaḥ - This can be broken down into "aśva" (horse) and "rādhasaḥ" (to descend), which may symbolize strength and power, often associated with divine forces.
  • veti - This means "knows" or "understands," indicating awareness or recognition of the divine.
  • tvām - Reiterating the address to the divine, emphasizing the importance of this connection.
  • upasecanī - This term refers to "offering" or "worship," suggesting a ritualistic approach to connecting with the divine.
  • vi - This means "you" or "your," again emphasizing the personal aspect of the divine relationship.
  • mada - This translates to "intoxication" or "joy," indicating a state of bliss or ecstasy that comes from divine connection.
  • ṛjītir - This can mean "truth" or "righteousness," highlighting the importance of moral integrity in the relationship with the divine.
  • agna - This refers to "fire," often symbolizing purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • āhutir - This means "offering" or "sacrifice," indicating the act of giving to the divine as a form of worship.
  • vivakṣase - This translates to "you wish" or "you desire," suggesting a call to the divine to manifest intentions or blessings.

In summary, this mantra is a profound invocation that emphasizes the relationship between the devotee and the divine. It highlights themes of praise, understanding, and the importance of offerings in establishing a connection with the divine. The repeated references to "you" and "your" create a personal bond, while the elements of joy and righteousness suggest that this relationship is not only about worship but also about living a life aligned with truth and integrity.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as during personal meditation, rituals, or community gatherings. It serves as a reminder to cultivate a personal relationship with the divine, to engage in acts of worship and offering, and to live a life grounded in truth and righteousness. By embodying these principles, individuals can experience a sense of joy and fulfillment, fostering a deeper connection with the spiritual aspects of life.
