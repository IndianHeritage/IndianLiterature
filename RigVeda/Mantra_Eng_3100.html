कथा शर्धाय मरुताम ऋताय कथा सूरे बृहते पृच्छ्यमानः |प्रति ब्रह्व 'दितये तुराय साधा दिवो जातवेदश चिकीत्वान ||
kathā śardhāya marutām ṛtāya kathā sūre bṛhate pṛcchyamānaḥ | prati bravo 'ditaye turāya sādhā divo jātavedaś cikitvān ||
  • kathā: This word translates to "how" or "in what manner." It signifies the inquiry into the nature of existence and the universe.
  • śardhāya: This term refers to "with faith" or "with devotion." It emphasizes the importance of belief and trust in the divine or the cosmic order.
  • marutām: This word denotes "of the Maruts," who are the storm deities in Vedic literature. They represent the forces of nature and the power of the wind.
  • ṛtāya: This translates to "truth" or "cosmic order." It signifies the underlying principle that governs the universe, emphasizing the importance of living in harmony with this order.
  • sūre: This means "to the divine" or "to the gods." It indicates a connection to the higher powers and the spiritual realm.
  • bṛhate: This translates to "great" or "vast," referring to the expansive nature of the universe and the divine.
  • pṛcchyamānaḥ: This term means "being questioned" or "inquired." It suggests a dialogue or a quest for knowledge and understanding.
  • prati: This means "towards" or "in response to," indicating a direction or purpose in the inquiry.
  • bravo: This word can be interpreted as "speak" or "declare," urging the divine to respond to the inquiries of the seeker.
  • 'ditaye: This term refers to "the one who is born" or "the one who has come into being," highlighting the connection between existence and the divine.
  • turāya: This translates to "swift" or "quick," suggesting the urgency of the inquiry and the need for immediate understanding.
  • sādhā: This means "to achieve" or "to accomplish," emphasizing the goal of the inquiry.
  • divo: This word means "of the sky" or "heavenly," indicating a connection to the higher realms.
  • jātavedaś: This translates to "the one who knows all," referring to the divine knowledge and wisdom that governs the universe.
  • cikitvān: This means "having understood" or "having realized," indicating the culmination of the inquiry into knowledge and truth.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a profound inquiry into the nature of existence, the divine, and the cosmic order. It emphasizes the importance of faith, dialogue, and the pursuit of truth. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as seeking knowledge in education, understanding the complexities of relationships, or exploring one's spiritual path. The mantra encourages individuals to ask questions, seek answers, and strive for a deeper understanding of themselves and the universe around them. It serves as a reminder that the quest for knowledge and truth is a sacred journey, one that connects us to the divine and the greater cosmic order.
