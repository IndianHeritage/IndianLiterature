आ मण्येथाम आ गतं कचिदेवैर विश्वे जनासो अश्विनाः हवन्ते |इमा हि वां गोर्झीकां मधूनी प्र मित्रासो न ददुर उष्रो अग्रे ||
ā manyethām ā gataṁ kac cid evair viśve janāso aśvinā havante | imā hi vāṁ goṛjīkā madhūni pra mitrāso na dadur usro agre ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā manyethām: May we think of you.
    • ā gataṁ: Having come from afar.
    • kac cid: Perhaps.
    • evair: Indeed.
    • viśve janāso: All people.
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the divine twin horsemen).
    • havante: Invoke or call upon.
    • imā hi: These indeed.
    • vāṁ: Your.
    • goṛjīkā: The offerings or gifts.
    • madhūni: Sweet or pleasant things.
    • pra mitrāso: For the friends.
    • na dadur: They do not give.
    • usro agre: In the forefront or ahead.
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the Ashvins, who are considered the divine physicians and bringers of health and prosperity.
    • The invocation suggests a desire for the blessings of the Ashvins, asking them to consider the offerings made by the devotees.
    • The phrase "may we think of you" emphasizes the importance of mindfulness and intention in spiritual practices.
    • The mention of "all people" indicates a universal appeal for health and well-being, transcending individual desires.
    • The reference to "sweet offerings" symbolizes the good deeds and positive intentions that one should cultivate in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community health initiative, people come together to support each other’s well-being, reflecting the mantra's call for collective health.
    • During festivals, offerings are made to deities, symbolizing gratitude and the hope for blessings, akin to the "sweet offerings" mentioned in the mantra.
    • In personal development, individuals may focus on positive thoughts and intentions, aligning with the mantra's emphasis on mindfulness and invoking divine support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of community, intention, and the divine in achieving health and prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder to cultivate positive thoughts and actions, not just for oneself but for the collective good.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek support from the divine and from each other, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.
