त्रिदिलā अतृदिलासो अद्रयो 'श्रमानā अशृथितā अमृत्यवः | अनातुरā अजराḥ स्थामविष्णवः सुपीवसो अतृषितā अतृष्णजः ||
tṛdilā atṛdilāso adrayo 'śramaṇā aśṛthitā amṛtyavaḥ | anāturā ajarāḥ sthāmaviṣṇavaḥ supīvaso atṛṣitā atṛṣṇajaḥ ||
  • tṛdilā atṛdilāso: This phrase can be interpreted as "threefold" or "triple" and "not threefold." It suggests a duality or contrast between different states of existence or consciousness.
  • adrayo 'śramaṇā: This translates to "the mountains are not weary." Mountains symbolize stability and permanence, indicating that true strength does not tire or diminish over time.
  • aśṛthitā amṛtyavaḥ: This can be understood as "those who are not bound by death." It speaks to the idea of immortality or transcendence beyond physical existence.
  • anāturā ajarāḥ: This means "free from illness and ageless." It emphasizes the state of being beyond physical ailments and the passage of time.
  • sthāmaviṣṇavaḥ: This phrase refers to "the stable, the abode of Vishnu." Vishnu represents preservation and stability in the universe, suggesting a connection to divine protection and support.
  • supīvaso: This translates to "well-fed" or "satisfied." It indicates a state of abundance and contentment, both physically and spiritually.
  • atṛṣitā atṛṣṇajaḥ: This means "not thirsty, free from desire." It reflects a state of fulfillment and detachment from worldly cravings.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound philosophical concepts regarding existence, immortality, and spiritual fulfillment. It emphasizes the importance of transcending physical limitations and desires to achieve a state of eternal peace and contentment. In real life, this can be applied through practices such as meditation, mindfulness, and a focus on spiritual growth, encouraging individuals to seek stability and satisfaction beyond material pursuits. By embodying these principles, one can cultivate a life that is resilient, fulfilling, and aligned with higher truths.
