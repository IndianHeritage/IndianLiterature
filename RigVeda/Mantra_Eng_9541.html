पितेव पुत्रम् अभिभर उपस्थे त्वां अग्नि वध्र्यश्वः सपर्णन |जुषाणो अस्य समिधं यविष्टोत पूर्वाम् अवनोर्व्राधतश्चित् ||
piteva putram abibhar upasthe tvām agne vadhryaśvaḥ saparyan | juṣāṇo asya samidhaṁ yaviṣṭhota pūrvām̐ avanor vrādhataś cit ||
  • Translation: "As a father carries his son in his lap, O Agni, may you, the strong one, be worshipped. Accept this offering of ours, as we invoke you, the one who is the first to be honored."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • piteva: like a father
    • putram: son
    • abibhar: carries or supports
    • upasthe: in the lap
    • tvām: you
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • vadhryaśvaḥ: the strong one
    • saparyan: worshipped or honored
    • juṣāṇo: accepting or enjoying
    • asya: this
    • samidhaṁ: offering or fuel
    • yaviṣṭhota: invoking or calling
    • pūrvām̐: the first
    • avanor: of the earth
    • vrādhataś: with strength
    • cit: may it be so
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the relationship between a father and son, symbolizing care, support, and nurturing. In real life, this can be seen in the way parents provide for their children, both emotionally and materially.
    • Agni, the fire god, represents purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals. Just as a father supports his son, Agni is invoked to accept the offerings made by devotees, signifying the importance of divine blessings in our lives.
    • The act of worshipping Agni can be compared to how individuals seek guidance and support from mentors or leaders in their lives, reflecting the need for a strong foundation and direction.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of familial bonds and the role of divine forces in our lives. It teaches us the importance of nurturing relationships and seeking blessings from higher powers.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in family dynamics, where the support of parents is crucial for the growth and development of children. It also reflects the need for community support and the importance of honoring those who guide us.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to appreciate the strength of our relationships and the divine presence that aids us in our journey through life.
