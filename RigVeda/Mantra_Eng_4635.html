मन्त्र: अन्यदाद्य कर्वारम् अन्यदु श्वोऽसच्च सन्मुहुराचक्रिर इन्द्रः। मित्रो नोऽत्र वरुणश्च पूषार्यो वशस्य पर्येतास्ति॥
anyad adya karvaram anyad u śvo 'sac ca san muhur ācakrir indraḥ | mitro no atra varuṇaś ca pūṣāryo vaśasya paryetāsti ||
  • Mantra: "anyad adya karvaram anyad u śvo 'sac ca san muhur ācakrir indraḥ | mitro no atra varuṇaś ca pūṣāryo vaśasya paryetāsti ||"
  • Translation: "Today, we perform different actions; yesterday, we did something else. Indra, the powerful one, is always present. May Mitra, Varuna, and Pūṣan, the divine protectors, be with us."

  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • anyad: different, another
    • adya: today
    • karvaram: actions, deeds
    • u: or
    • śvo: yesterday
    • 'sac: that which is true, real
    • ca: and
    • san: being, existence
    • muhur: repeatedly, often
    • ācakrir: has spoken, has declared
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods
    • mitro: Mitra, the god of friendship and contracts
    • no: our
    • atra: here, in this place
    • varuṇaś: Varuna, the god of cosmic order
    • ca: and
    • pūṣāryo: Pūṣan, the god of nourishment
    • vaśasya: of control, of governance
    • paryetāsti: may be present, may exist

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra reflects the dynamic nature of life, emphasizing that our actions and experiences are constantly changing.
    • The mention of Indra signifies strength and power, suggesting that divine support is always available to us.
    • Mitra, Varuna, and Pūṣan represent different aspects of life: friendship, cosmic order, and nourishment, respectively, indicating that we are supported in various dimensions of our existence.
    • For example, in a business context, one might face different challenges each day (anyad adya karvaram) and rely on the support of colleagues (Mitra) and ethical practices (Varuna) to navigate these challenges successfully.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra teaches us the importance of adaptability and the recognition of divine support in our daily lives.
    • It encourages us to acknowledge the changes we face and to seek guidance and strength from higher powers.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in personal growth, decision-making, and fostering relationships, reminding us that we are not alone in our endeavors.
