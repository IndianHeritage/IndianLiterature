मन्त्र: स्थिरौ गावौ भवतां वीलुर अक्षो मेषा वि वरहि मा युगं वि शारी | इन्द्रः पातल्ये ददतां शरीरतो अरिष्टनेमे अभि नः सच्चस्व ||
sthirau gāvau bhavatāṁ vīḻur akṣo meṣā vi varhi mā yugaṁ vi śāri | indraḥ pātalye dadatāṁ śarītor ariṣṭaneme abhi naḥ sacasva ||
  • sthirau gāvau bhavatāṁ: This phrase can be translated as "May the two cows be stable." In Vedic culture, cows symbolize wealth, nourishment, and prosperity. The stability of cows represents a stable source of sustenance and abundance in life.
  • vīḻur akṣo meṣā: This translates to "May the bull be strong." The bull is often associated with strength and virility. In real life, this can be seen as a metaphor for having strong foundations in our endeavors, whether in business, relationships, or personal growth.
  • vi varhi mā yugaṁ vi śāri: This means "May the two be united." This can refer to the unity of different aspects of life, such as work and family, or mind and body. In practice, this emphasizes the importance of balance and harmony in our lives.
  • indraḥ pātalye dadatāṁ: This translates to "May Indra grant us protection." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain and thunderstorms, symbolizing power and protection. In real life, this can be interpreted as seeking divine guidance and support in our challenges.
  • śarītor ariṣṭaneme: This means "May we be free from obstacles." It highlights the desire for a life free from hindrances. In everyday life, this can relate to overcoming challenges in our careers, health, or personal relationships.
  • abhi naḥ sacasva: This translates to "May we be blessed with good fortune." This is a prayer for prosperity and success in our endeavors. In real life, this can be seen as a call to action to work hard and remain optimistic about the future.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a prayer for stability, strength, unity, protection, freedom from obstacles, and good fortune. Its significance lies in its holistic approach to life, encouraging individuals to seek balance and harmony in various aspects. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal development, business ventures, and relationships, reminding us to strive for stability and seek divine support in our journeys. By embodying these principles, we can cultivate a fulfilling and prosperous life.
