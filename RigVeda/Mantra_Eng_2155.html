अध्वर्यवो यो दिव्यस्य वस्वो यः पार्थिवस्य क्षम्यस्य राजा |तम ऊर्धरं न पृणतā यवेन्द्रं सोमैभिः तद आपो वो अस्तु ||
adhvaryavo yo divyasya vasvo yaḥ pārthivasya kṣamyasya rājā | tam ūrdaraṁ na pṛṇatā yavenendraṁ somebhis tad apo vo astu ||
  • adhvaryavo: This term refers to the priests who perform the rituals and sacrifices in Vedic traditions. They are responsible for the proper execution of the rites.
  • yo divyasya: "Who is divine" - This phrase indicates a connection to the celestial or divine realm, suggesting that the subject of the mantra is of a higher spiritual nature.
  • vasvo: This word can be interpreted as "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies abundance and the material blessings that one seeks in life.
  • yaḥ pārthivasya: "Who is earthly" - This part contrasts the divine with the earthly, indicating a balance between spiritual and material aspects of life.
  • kṣamyasya: This term means "forgiveness" or "tolerance." It emphasizes the importance of compassion and understanding in human interactions.
  • rājā: This means "king" or "ruler." It symbolizes authority, leadership, and governance, highlighting the role of a leader in society.
  • tam ūrdaraṁ: "That which is exalted" - This phrase refers to the elevated state of being or consciousness that one aspires to achieve.
  • na pṛṇatā: "May not be diminished" - This indicates a desire for the exalted state to remain intact and not to be reduced or lost.
  • yavenendraṁ: "By the strength of the lord" - This suggests invoking divine strength or power to achieve one's goals.
  • somebhis: "With the moon" - The moon is often associated with calmness, reflection, and the nurturing aspects of life.
  • tad apo vo astu: "May that be for you" - This is a blessing, wishing for the recipient to receive the benefits and abundance mentioned earlier.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of balancing the divine and earthly aspects of life. It emphasizes the importance of leadership, compassion, and the pursuit of both spiritual and material wealth. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as leadership roles in organizations, where a leader must balance authority with empathy, or in personal life, where one seeks to achieve success while remaining grounded and compassionate. The mantra serves as a reminder to strive for an exalted state of being, supported by divine strength, while ensuring that one's blessings and prosperity are shared with others.
