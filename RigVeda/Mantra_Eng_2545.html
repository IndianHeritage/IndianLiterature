तं त्वा मर्ता अगृभ्णत देवभ्यो हव्यवाहन |विश्वान यद यज्ञाम् अभिपासी मानवष तव क्रत्वा यविष्ठ्य ||
taṁ tvā martā agṛbhṇata devebhyo havyavāhana | viśvān yad yajñām̐ abhipāsi mānuṣa tava kratvā yaviṣṭhya ||
  • taṁ tvā martā agṛbhṇata: This phrase can be translated as "the mortals (martā) have grasped (agṛbhṇata) you (tvā)." It signifies the connection between humans and the divine, indicating that mortals seek the presence and blessings of the divine in their lives.
  • devebhyo havyavāhana: This translates to "to the gods (devebhyo) who are the carriers of offerings (havyavāhana)." It emphasizes the importance of offerings and rituals in connecting with the divine, suggesting that the gods are the recipients of human devotion and sacrifices.
  • viśvān yad yajñām̐ abhipāsi: This means "all (viśvān) the sacrifices (yajñām̐) that you (tava) protect (abhipāsi)." It highlights the idea that the divine oversees and safeguards all forms of worship and offerings made by humans, ensuring that they are accepted and fruitful.
  • mānuṣa tava kratvā yaviṣṭhya: This translates to "by the power (kratvā) of humans (mānuṣa), may you (tava) be invoked (yaviṣṭhya)." It suggests that human effort and intention play a crucial role in invoking the divine, indicating that the relationship between humans and the divine is reciprocal.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of the relationship between humans and the divine. It emphasizes the importance of rituals, offerings, and the active participation of humans in their spiritual journey. The mantra serves as a reminder that while the divine is powerful and protective, it is the human effort and devotion that invoke divine blessings.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:
    • Rituals and Offerings: Engaging in rituals, whether in personal or communal settings, can help individuals feel connected to a higher power. For example, lighting a diya (lamp) during festivals or offering food to deities can be seen as a way to invoke blessings.
    • Community and Cooperation: Just as the mantra emphasizes collective offerings, it can inspire individuals to work together in community service or charitable activities, recognizing that collective human effort can lead to greater good.
    • Personal Growth: The mantra encourages individuals to take responsibility for their spiritual journey, reminding them that their intentions and actions are vital in shaping their relationship with the divine.
