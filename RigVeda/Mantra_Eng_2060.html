तां अस्य वर्णम् आयुवो नेष्टुः साचन्त धेनवः |कुवित तिसृभ्य ā वरं स्वसारो या इदं ययुः ||
tā asya varṇam āyuvo neṣṭuḥ sacanta dhenavaḥ | kuvit tisṛbhya ā varaṁ svasāro yā idaṁ yayuḥ ||
  • tā asya varṇam āyuvo neṣṭuḥ sacanta dhenavaḥ: This phrase can be translated to "May those who are endowed with the qualities of life and prosperity, like cows, come to us." Here, "varṇam" refers to qualities or attributes, "āyuvo" signifies life or longevity, and "sacanta dhenavaḥ" refers to the cows, which are often seen as symbols of wealth and nourishment in Vedic literature.
  • kuvit tisṛbhya ā varaṁ svasāro yā idaṁ yayuḥ: This part translates to "May we receive the best from those who are wise and knowledgeable." "kuvit" refers to wisdom or knowledge, "tisṛbhya" implies the threefold aspects of wisdom, and "svasāro" indicates those who are close or related, suggesting a community or family context.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking qualities that lead to a prosperous and fulfilling life. It highlights the value of wisdom and the nurturing aspects of life, represented by cows, which are essential for sustenance and growth.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:
    • Community Building: Just as the mantra calls for the qualities of life and wisdom, it encourages individuals to foster strong community ties where knowledge and resources are shared for mutual benefit.
    • Personal Growth: Individuals can reflect on their own qualities and strive to cultivate wisdom and prosperity in their lives, much like nurturing a garden for growth.
    • Environmental Awareness: The reference to cows can also be seen as a call for sustainable living and respect for nature, reminding us of the importance of nurturing our environment for future generations.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, wisdom, and prosperity. It encourages individuals and communities to seek out and cultivate these qualities, leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling existence. By applying the principles of this mantra in daily life, one can contribute to personal well-being and the welfare of society as a whole.
