अस्ताव्य अग्निः शिमीवद्भिर अर्कैः साम्राज्याय प्रतरं दधानः |अमी च ये मगवाणो वयं च मिहं न सूरो अति निष्टतन्युः ||
astāvy agniḥ śimīvadbhir arkaiḥ sāmrājyāya prataraṁ dadhānaḥ | amī ca ye maghavāno vayaṁ ca mihaṁ na sūro ati niṣ ṭatanyuḥ ||
  • Mantra: "astāvy agniḥ śimīvadbhir arkaiḥ sāmrājyāya prataraṁ dadhānaḥ | amī ca ye maghavāno vayaṁ ca mihaṁ na sūro ati niṣ ṭatanyuḥ ||"
  • Translation: "May the fire, like a shining light, bring forth the wealth of the kingdom, and may those who are powerful and prosperous, along with us, not be defeated by any adversary."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • astāvy: a form of the word "ashta," meaning "eight," often symbolizing completeness or fullness.
    • agniḥ: means "fire," representing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • śimīvadbhir: derived from "śimī," meaning "shining" or "brilliant," indicating brightness and clarity.
    • arkaiḥ: refers to "light" or "radiance," often associated with divine blessings.
    • sāmrājyāya: means "for the kingdom," indicating a focus on prosperity and governance.
    • prataraṁ: means "to bring forth" or "to spread," suggesting the dissemination of wealth and resources.
    • dadhānaḥ: means "holding" or "possessing," indicating the act of maintaining or acquiring wealth.
    • amī: means "those" or "they," referring to powerful beings or deities.
    • maghavāno: means "the powerful" or "the mighty," often referring to divine forces or influential leaders.
    • vayaṁ: means "we," indicating a collective identity or community.
    • mihaṁ: means "here" or "in this place," suggesting a focus on the present moment.
    • na: means "not," indicating negation or absence.
    • sūro: means "hero" or "warrior," representing strength and valor.
    • ati: means "beyond" or "exceeding," indicating surpassing limits.
    • niṣṭatanyuḥ: means "defeated" or "overcome," suggesting the idea of resilience against challenges.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, the mantra can be applied to a team striving for success. The "fire" symbolizes the passion and energy of the team, while the "wealth of the kingdom" represents the company's goals and achievements.
    • In personal life, individuals can invoke this mantra during challenging times, seeking strength and resilience to overcome obstacles, much like a warrior facing adversity.
    • In community gatherings, this mantra can serve as a reminder of collective strength and unity, encouraging members to support one another in achieving common goals.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and the pursuit of prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of collective effort in overcoming challenges and achieving success. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal endeavors, professional settings, or community initiatives, the principles encapsulated in this mantra can inspire individuals and groups to harness their inner strength and work together towards a common goal, ensuring that they remain resilient against adversities and thrive in their pursuits.
