एक एवाग्निर बहुधा समिद्ध एकः सूर्यो विश्वम् अनु प्रभूतः |एकैवोषाः सर्वम् इदं वि भात्य एकं वा इदं वि बभूव सर्वम् ||
eka evāgnir bahudhā samiddha ekaḥ sūryo viśvam anu prabhūtaḥ | ekaivoṣāḥ sarvam idaṁ vi bhāty ekaṁ vā idaṁ vi babhūva sarvam ||
  • Mantra: "eka evāgnir bahudhā samiddha ekaḥ sūryo viśvam anu prabhūtaḥ | ekaivoṣāḥ sarvam idaṁ vi bhāty ekaṁ vā idaṁ vi babhūva sarvam ||"
  • Translation: "There is one fire, which is kindled in many ways; there is one sun, which shines upon the whole universe. That one energy illuminates everything; it is one that has become all this."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • eka: One
    • evāgnir: indeed, fire
    • bahudhā: in many ways
    • samiddha: kindled or ignited
    • sūryo: sun
    • viśvam: the whole universe
    • anu: following or in accordance with
    • prabhūtaḥ: abundant or powerful
    • eko: one
    • viṣāḥ: energy or essence
    • sarvam: everything
    • idaṁ: this
    • bhāty: shines or illuminates
    • vā: or
    • babhūva: has become

  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the unity of existence, suggesting that despite the apparent diversity in the universe, there is a singular essence that connects all things.
    • It highlights the idea that different forms of energy, like fire and sunlight, are manifestations of the same underlying reality.
    • In practical terms, this can be seen in how various cultures and religions interpret the concept of a singular divine force or energy that permeates all aspects of life.

  • Real-Life Applications:
    • In environmental science, the interconnectedness of ecosystems can be understood through this mantra. Just as fire can be kindled in many ways, various elements of nature work together to sustain life.
    • In personal development, recognizing that we are all part of a larger whole can foster empathy and compassion, encouraging individuals to act for the greater good.
    • In conflict resolution, understanding that different perspectives stem from the same source can help bridge divides and promote harmony.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the fundamental unity of all existence, encouraging individuals to look beyond superficial differences and recognize the shared essence that binds us all.
    • Its teachings are applicable in various fields, from science to spirituality, promoting a holistic understanding of life and our place within it.
    • Ultimately, it inspires a sense of responsibility towards each other and the environment, urging us to act in ways that honor our interconnectedness.
