स वायुम इन्द्रं अश्विनā साकं मदेन गच्छति |रणा यो अस्य धर्मभिः ||
sa vāyum indram aśvinā sākam madena gacchati | raṇā yo asya dharmabhiḥ ||
  • sa vāyum: This phrase translates to "that wind" or "that air." In a broader sense, it can refer to the vital breath or life force that sustains all living beings.
  • indram: This refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and strength. Indra symbolizes power and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • aśvinā: The Aśvins are twin deities in Vedic tradition, representing health, medicine, and the dawn. They are often invoked for their healing powers and the ability to bring light and clarity.
  • sākam: This word means "together" or "in unison." It emphasizes the importance of collaboration and unity in achieving goals.
  • madena: This translates to "with joy" or "with intoxication." It signifies a state of bliss or ecstasy that can come from achieving harmony and balance in life.
  • gacchati: This means "goes" or "moves." It indicates progress or movement towards a goal or destination.
  • raṇā: This word means "battle" or "war." It can symbolize the struggles and challenges one faces in life.
  • yo asya: This translates to "whoever" or "who." It refers to individuals who engage in the actions described in the mantra.
  • dharmabhiḥ: This means "by virtue" or "by righteousness." It emphasizes the importance of ethical conduct and moral principles in one's actions.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for the vital forces of life (represented by wind) to unite with the strength of Indra and the healing powers of the Aśvins, moving forward joyfully in the face of life's battles, guided by righteousness.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra emphasizes unity and strength, individuals can seek to align their personal goals with ethical values, fostering a sense of purpose and direction in their lives.
  • Teamwork: In professional settings, the idea of working together (sākam) can be crucial for success. Teams that collaborate effectively, drawing on each member's strengths, can overcome challenges (raṇā) more efficiently.
  • Health and Well-being: The invocation of the Aśvins highlights the importance of health. In daily life, this can translate to seeking balance through physical activity, mental well-being, and community support.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of ethical conduct, and the power of unity in overcoming challenges. It encourages individuals to embrace joy and righteousness as they navigate the complexities of life, ultimately leading to personal and collective growth.
