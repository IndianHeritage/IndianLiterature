क्व त्या वल्गू पुरुहूताद्य दूतो न स्तोमो 'विदन नमस्वान् |आ यो अरवाङ नासत्यā ववर्त प्रेष्ठा ह्य असथो अस्य मनमन ||
kva1 tyā valgū puruhūtādya dūto na stomo 'vidan namasvān | ā yo arvāṅ nāsatyā vavarta preṣṭhā hy asatho asya manman ||
  • kva1 tyā valgū puruhūtādya dūto na stomo 'vidan namasvān
    • kva1: This word can be interpreted as "where" or "in what place." It sets the context for the inquiry or the search for understanding.
    • tyā: This means "by that" or "through that," indicating a means or method of achieving something.
    • valgū: This term refers to "the one who is swift" or "the messenger," often associated with divine beings or deities who convey messages.
    • puruhūtādya: This can be broken down into "puruhūta," which refers to a deity or divine figure, and "ādya," meaning "the first" or "the original." Together, it suggests a connection to a primary divine force.
    • dūto: This translates to "messenger," emphasizing the role of communication and the importance of conveying messages.
    • na stomo 'vidan namasvān: This phrase can be interpreted as "not knowing the praise" or "not being aware of the offerings." It highlights a lack of recognition or understanding of the divine or the messages being conveyed.
  • ā yo arvāṅ nāsatyā vavarta preṣṭhā hy asatho asya manman
    • ā: This word means "from" or "out of," indicating a source or origin.
    • yo: This translates to "who" or "that which," referring to a subject or entity.
    • arvāṅ: This term can mean "the one who is swift" or "the one who moves quickly," often associated with the divine or spiritual forces.
    • nāsatyā: This refers to "the Asvins," twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing and protective qualities.
    • vavarta: This means "to turn" or "to revolve," suggesting a dynamic process or change.
    • preṣṭhā: This translates to "the best" or "the foremost," indicating a position of importance or priority.
    • hy asatho asya manman: This phrase can be interpreted as "for the one who is not false" or "for the one who is true," emphasizing the importance of truth and authenticity in communication and understanding.

Conclusion:

The mantra reflects the significance of communication, the role of divine messengers, and the importance of recognizing and understanding the messages conveyed by the divine. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to the importance of clear communication in relationships, the need for authenticity in our interactions, and the recognition of the deeper meanings behind our experiences. It encourages individuals to seek truth and clarity in their lives, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and with one another.
