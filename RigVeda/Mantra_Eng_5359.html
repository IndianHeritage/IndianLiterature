त्वावतो हीन्द्र क्रत्वे अस्मि त्वावतो 'वितुः शूर रातौ | विश्वेद अहानि तविषीव उग्रं ओकः कृत्नुष्व हरिवो न मर्धीः ||
tvāvato hīndra kratve asmi tvāvato 'vituḥ śūra rātau | viśved ahāni taviṣīva ugram̐ okaḥ kṛṇuṣva harivo na mardhīḥ ||
  • tvāvato: This word translates to "by your power" or "through your strength." It emphasizes the importance of divine strength in achieving goals.
  • hīndra: Referring to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing leadership, courage, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • kratve: This term means "with determination" or "with effort." It highlights the necessity of willpower and perseverance in any endeavor.
  • asmi: Meaning "I am," it signifies self-awareness and the recognition of one's own identity and purpose.
  • tvāvato 'vituḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "by your strength, O protector," indicating a plea for guidance and protection from higher powers.
  • śūra: Translated as "brave" or "valiant," it underscores the qualities of bravery and valor that are essential in facing life's challenges.
  • rātau: Meaning "in the night," it symbolizes the times of darkness or uncertainty in life.
  • viśved: This word means "all" or "universal," suggesting inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • ahāni: Translated as "days," it refers to the passage of time and the importance of each moment in our lives.
  • taviṣīva: This term means "with great strength," emphasizing the need for robust energy and determination.
  • ugram̐: Meaning "fierce" or "powerful," it denotes the intensity required to overcome obstacles.
  • okaḥ: This word translates to "home" or "shelter," symbolizing safety and security.
  • kṛṇuṣva: Meaning "make" or "create," it is a call to action, urging one to take initiative.
  • harivo: This term can be interpreted as "O divine beings," invoking the presence of higher powers.
  • na mardhīḥ: Translated as "do not harm," it is a plea for protection and non-violence.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a profound message about the importance of divine strength, courage, and determination in overcoming life's challenges. It serves as a reminder that, even in times of darkness and uncertainty, one can seek guidance and protection from higher powers. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied when facing difficult situations, such as during personal struggles, professional challenges, or moments of self-doubt. By invoking the qualities of bravery and resilience, individuals can find the strength to persevere and create a safe and secure environment for themselves and others. Ultimately, it emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the collective strength that can be harnessed to navigate through life's trials.
