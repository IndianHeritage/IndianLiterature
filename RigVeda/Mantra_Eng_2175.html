नूनं सा ते प्रति वरं जरीत्रे दूहीयद इन्द्र दक्षिणा मघोनि |शिक्षा स्तोत्रिभ्यो माती धग भगो नो बृहद वदेम विदथे सुवीराः ||
nūnaṁ sā te prati varaṁ jaritre duhīyad indra dakṣiṇā maghonī | śikṣā stotṛbhyo māti dhag bhago no bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ ||
  • nūnaṁ sā te prati varaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "Indeed, that is your excellent gift." It suggests a recognition of the divine blessings that one receives.
  • jaritre duhīyad indra: This translates to "O Indra, the one who supports and nourishes." Indra is often seen as a deity who provides strength and sustenance, symbolizing the importance of support in our lives.
  • dakṣiṇā maghonī: This means "the generous offering." It emphasizes the value of giving and sharing, which is crucial in building relationships and communities.
  • śikṣā stotṛbhyo: This translates to "the teachings to the worshippers." It highlights the importance of learning and guidance in spiritual and everyday life.
  • māti dhag bhago: This can be interpreted as "may our intellect shine." It signifies the importance of wisdom and understanding in making decisions.
  • no bṛhad vadema: This means "let us speak of the great." It encourages discussions about significant and meaningful topics, fostering deeper connections among individuals.
  • vidathe suvīrāḥ: This translates to "in the assembly of the brave." It emphasizes the value of courage and strength in facing challenges together.

Conclusion: This mantra from the RigVeda encapsulates the essence of community, support, and wisdom. It highlights the significance of divine blessings, the importance of generosity, and the need for guidance in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as family gatherings, community service, or team collaborations where support, sharing, and collective wisdom are essential for success. By embodying these principles, individuals can foster a more harmonious and productive environment, ultimately leading to personal and communal growth.
