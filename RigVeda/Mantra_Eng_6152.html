या इन्द्र यतयस् त्वा भृगवो ये च तुष्टुवुः |ममेद उग्र श्रुधी हवम् ||
ya indra yatayas tvā bhṛgavo ye ca tuṣṭuvuḥ | mamed ugra śrudhī havam ||
  • Mantra: "ya indra yatayas tvā bhṛgavo ye ca tuṣṭuvuḥ | mamed ugra śrudhī havam ||"
  • Translation: "Those who strive towards you, O Indra, and those who praise you, O Bhrigus, listen to my powerful invocation."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Indra: The king of the gods, representing strength, power, and leadership.
    • Yatayas: Those who strive or exert effort, symbolizing determination and hard work.
    • Bhrigavo: Referring to the Bhrigus, a group of sages known for their wisdom and spiritual insight.
    • Tuṣṭuvuḥ: Those who praise or worship, indicating the importance of reverence and gratitude.
    • Mamed: My, indicating a personal connection to the invocation.
    • Ugra: Powerful or fierce, suggesting the intensity of the invocation.
    • Śrudhī: Listen or hear, emphasizing the need for attention and receptiveness.
    • Havam: Invocation or offering, representing the act of calling upon the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader may invoke the spirit of teamwork and collaboration, similar to how the mantra calls upon Indra for strength and support.
    • In personal development, individuals striving for their goals can relate to the concept of "yatayas," as they work hard and seek guidance from mentors or spiritual figures.
    • During community gatherings, people often express gratitude and praise for those who contribute positively, akin to the "tuṣṭuvuḥ" aspect of the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support and recognition of the efforts made by individuals. It emphasizes the importance of striving towards goals, seeking wisdom, and expressing gratitude. In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios such as personal growth, leadership, and community engagement, reminding us that invoking higher powers and acknowledging our efforts can lead to greater success and fulfillment.
