त्वष्टा मायाऽवेद अपसाम् अपस्तमो बिभ्रत् पात्रा देवपानानि शान्तमा |शिशीते नूनं परशुं स्वायसं येन वृश्चाद एतशो ब्रह्मणस् पतिḥ ||
tvaṣṭā māyā ved apasām apastamo bibhrat pātrā devapānāni śaṁtamā | śiśīte nūnam paraśuṁ svāyasaṁ yena vṛścād etaśo brahmaṇas patiḥ ||
  • tvaṣṭā: This term refers to the divine craftsman or the creator, often associated with the deity Tvashtar in Vedic literature. In real life, this can be likened to skilled artisans or engineers who create and innovate.
  • māyā: This word signifies illusion or the creative power of the divine. In everyday life, it can represent the distractions and illusions that prevent individuals from seeing the truth or reality clearly.
  • ved: This refers to knowledge or wisdom, particularly the sacred knowledge contained in the Vedas. In a modern context, it can be compared to the pursuit of education and understanding in various fields.
  • apasām: This term means 'from the depths' or 'from the lower realms.' It can symbolize the challenges and struggles one faces in life, akin to overcoming obstacles in personal or professional journeys.
  • apastamo: This translates to 'the darkness' or 'the lowest point.' It represents ignorance or confusion that can cloud judgment. In real life, this can be seen in moments of despair or lack of clarity in decision-making.
  • bibhrat: This means 'holding' or 'bearing.' It signifies the act of carrying or sustaining something important, much like how individuals bear responsibilities in their lives.
  • pātrā: This word means 'vessel' or 'container.' It can symbolize the human body or mind as a vessel for knowledge and experiences, similar to how we learn and grow through various life experiences.
  • devapānāni: This term refers to 'divine drinks' or 'nectar of the gods.' In a practical sense, it can represent the pursuit of spiritual nourishment or enlightenment, akin to seeking wisdom and truth in life.
  • śaṁtamā: This means 'the most peaceful' or 'tranquil.' It signifies the state of inner peace that individuals strive for, much like the calmness one seeks in meditation or mindfulness practices.
  • śiśīte: This translates to 'nourishes' or 'sustains.' It reflects the idea of nurturing and supporting growth, similar to how mentors or educators guide individuals in their development.
  • nūnam: This word means 'indeed' or 'certainly.' It emphasizes certainty and assurance in the context of the mantra, akin to having faith in one's path or decisions.
  • paraśuṁ: This refers to 'the axe' or 'tool.' It symbolizes the tools or means by which one can cut through ignorance or obstacles, much like how education and skills empower individuals to overcome challenges.
  • svāyasaṁ: This means 'of one's own' or 'self-made.' It signifies self-reliance and personal effort, akin to the importance of hard work and determination in achieving success.
  • yena: This translates to 'by which' or 'through which.' It indicates the means or methods employed to achieve a goal, similar to the strategies one uses in life to reach their aspirations.
  • vṛścād: This term means 'from the scorpion' or 'of the scorpion.' It can symbolize the challenges that sting or hurt, representing the trials one faces in life.
  • etaśo: This means 'from this' or 'from here.' It signifies the source or origin of something, much like how experiences shape an individual's identity.
  • brahmaṇas: This refers to 'of the Brahman' or 'the ultimate reality.' It signifies the pursuit of spiritual truth and understanding, akin to the quest for meaning in life.
  • patiḥ: This means 'lord' or 'master.' It represents authority and guidance, similar to how leaders or mentors guide individuals in their journeys.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, knowledge, and the journey towards enlightenment. It emphasizes the importance of overcoming ignorance and distractions (māyā) to attain inner peace (śaṁtamā) and spiritual nourishment (devapānāni).
