सप्तानां सप्त ऋष्टयः सप्त द्युम्नान्य एषाम् |सप्तोऽधि श्रीयो धिरे ||
saptānāṁ sapta ṛṣṭayaḥ sapta dyumnāny eṣām | sapto adhi śriyo dhire ||
  • Mantra: "saptānāṁ sapta ṛṣṭayaḥ sapta dyumnāny eṣām | sapto adhi śriyo dhire ||"
  • Translation: "Of the seven, the seven sages; of the seven, the glories; may these seven grant us strength."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • saptānāṁ: Of the seven
    • sapta: Seven
    • ṛṣṭayaḥ: Sages or seers
    • dyumnāny: Glories or fame
    • eṣām: Of these
    • adhi: Above or upon
    • śriyo: Prosperity or wealth
    • dhire: With strength or wisdom
  • Explanation:
    • The mantra speaks of the number seven, which holds significant importance in various cultures and philosophies. In this context, it refers to the seven sages (ṛṣis) who are revered for their wisdom and spiritual insight.
    • The mention of "dyumnāny" indicates the glories or achievements that come from the wisdom of these sages. It suggests that their teachings and insights lead to fame and recognition.
    • The phrase "sapto adhi śriyo dhire" emphasizes the need for strength and wisdom to attain prosperity. It implies that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses spiritual and intellectual growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, we often seek guidance from mentors or wise individuals who can help us navigate challenges. Just as the sages in the mantra provide wisdom, we can look to teachers, leaders, or even books that offer profound insights.
    • The concept of seven can be seen in various aspects of life, such as the seven continents, seven wonders of the world, or even the seven days of the week, all of which symbolize completeness and wholeness.
    • In personal development, the idea of gaining strength through wisdom can be applied when individuals pursue education, self-improvement, or spiritual practices that enhance their understanding and capabilities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of wisdom, guidance, and strength in achieving prosperity and success in life. It serves as a reminder to seek knowledge and support from those who have walked the path before us.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to cultivate relationships with mentors, engage in lifelong learning, and strive for a balanced approach to both material and spiritual wealth.
    • Ultimately, the mantra inspires us to recognize the interconnectedness of wisdom, strength, and prosperity, urging us to pursue a holistic approach to life.
