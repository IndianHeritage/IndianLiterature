अस्येदेव प्र रिरिचे महित्वं दिवस पृथिव्याः पर्य अन्तरिक्षात् |स्वराल् इन्द्रो दमा आ विश्वगूर्तः स्वरिर अमात्रो ववक्षे रणाय ||
asyed eva pra ririce mahitvaṁ divas pṛthivyāḥ pary antarikṣāt | svarāḻ indro dama ā viśvagūrtaḥ svarir amatro vavakṣe raṇāya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asyed: this (of this)
    • eva: indeed
    • pra: forth
    • ririce: to rise or to grow
    • mahitvaṁ: greatness or glory
    • divas: of the sky
    • pṛthivyāḥ: of the earth
    • pary: around
    • antarikṣāt: from the space or atmosphere
    • svarāḻ: the one who is bright or shining
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • dama: control or restraint
    • ā: towards
    • viśvagūrtaḥ: the one who is the master of all
    • svarir: sound or voice
    • amatro: without fear
    • vavakṣe: will declare or proclaim
    • raṇāya: for battle or for war
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the greatness that arises from the unity of the sky (divas) and the earth (pṛthivyāḥ), emphasizing the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe.
    • It highlights the role of Indra, the king of gods, who embodies strength and control, suggesting that true power comes from restraint and wisdom.
    • The phrase "without fear" indicates a call to courage, encouraging individuals to face challenges boldly, especially in the context of conflict or competition.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, a great leader often embodies the qualities of Indra, balancing strength with wisdom and control. For instance, a CEO who leads a company through a crisis by making tough decisions while maintaining transparency and calmness.
    • In personal life, individuals may face battles such as health issues or career challenges. The mantra encourages them to rise with courage and seek greatness through their struggles, much like athletes who train rigorously to overcome obstacles.
    • In environmental efforts, the interconnectedness of earth and sky can be seen in movements advocating for sustainability, where the health of the planet is viewed as a collective responsibility.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of unity, strength, and courage in the face of challenges. It teaches that greatness is not just about power but also about the wisdom to use that power responsibly.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to embrace their roles as leaders, whether in their personal lives, communities, or workplaces, and to act with integrity and purpose.
    • Ultimately, the mantra inspires a holistic view of existence, urging us to recognize our place within the larger cosmos and to act in harmony with it.
