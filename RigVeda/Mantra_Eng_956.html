गूहतā गुह्यं तमो वि यात विश्वम् अत्रिणम् |ज्योतिष कर्ता यद उष्मसि ||
gūhatā guhyaṁ tamo vi yāta viśvam atriṇam | jyotiṣ kartā yad uśmasi ||
  • gūhatā: This word translates to "hidden" or "concealed." It signifies the aspects of life and existence that are not immediately visible or understood.
  • guhyaṁ: Meaning "secret" or "mystery," this term emphasizes the enigmatic nature of certain truths and knowledge that require deeper exploration.
  • tamo: This word means "darkness" or "ignorance." It represents the lack of knowledge or awareness that can cloud judgment and understanding.
  • vi yāta: Translated as "may it go away," this phrase expresses a desire for the removal of darkness or ignorance from one's life.
  • viśvam: This term means "the universe" or "all that exists." It encompasses everything in creation, highlighting the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • atriṇam: This word can be interpreted as "to the one who is worthy" or "to the enlightened." It refers to those who possess knowledge and wisdom.
  • jyotiṣ: Meaning "light" or "illumination," this term symbolizes knowledge, clarity, and enlightenment that dispels darkness.
  • kartā: This word translates to "creator" or "doer." It signifies the active role of individuals in shaping their reality through knowledge and action.
  • yad: This means "that" or "which," referring to the light or knowledge that is being invoked.
  • uśmasi: This term means "in the warmth" or "in the light," suggesting a state of being where one is surrounded by knowledge and understanding.

In essence, this mantra speaks to the journey from ignorance to knowledge, urging individuals to seek enlightenment and clarity in their lives. It acknowledges the hidden and mysterious aspects of existence while encouraging the removal of darkness through the pursuit of wisdom.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for self-awareness and the quest for knowledge. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts, such as education, personal development, and spiritual growth. By recognizing the hidden truths in our lives and striving to illuminate our understanding, we can navigate the complexities of existence with greater clarity and purpose. This mantra serves as a reminder that the pursuit of knowledge is a vital aspect of human experience, leading to a more enlightened and fulfilling life.
