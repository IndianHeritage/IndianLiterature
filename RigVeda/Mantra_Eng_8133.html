गिरा जात इह स्तुत इन्दुर इन्द्राय धीयते |विर योना वसताव इव ||
girā jāta iha stuta indur indrāya dhīyate | vir yonā vasatāv iva ||
  • girā: This word translates to "speech" or "word." It signifies the power of communication and expression.
  • jāta: Meaning "born" or "arisen," it indicates the emergence of something new or the manifestation of ideas.
  • iha: This means "here" or "in this world," emphasizing the relevance of the mantra in our current existence.
  • stuta: This translates to "praised" or "celebrated," suggesting the act of honoring or recognizing something significant.
  • indur: Referring to "the moon," it symbolizes illumination, guidance, and the cyclical nature of life.
  • indrāya: This means "to Indra," who is a deity associated with power, rain, and the heavens, representing strength and authority.
  • dhīyate: This translates to "is contemplated" or "is meditated upon," indicating a focus on wisdom and understanding.
  • vir: Meaning "hero" or "brave," it signifies valor and the qualities of a leader.
  • yonā: This word means "womb" or "source," representing the origin of life and creativity.
  • vasatāv: This translates to "dwelling" or "residing," indicating a place of stability and comfort.
  • iva: Meaning "like" or "as if," it draws a comparison, suggesting a similarity or analogy.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to recognize and celebrate the power of speech and wisdom in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of contemplation and the role of divine guidance (symbolized by the moon and Indra) in our journey. The mention of "hero" and "womb" suggests that true strength comes from nurturing and creativity, and that our thoughts and words have the power to shape our reality.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In communication, it reminds us to use our words wisely and to honor the power of speech in building relationships and communities.
  • In leadership, it encourages leaders to be brave and nurturing, guiding their teams with wisdom and strength.
  • In personal development, it highlights the importance of self-reflection and contemplation in achieving clarity and understanding in our lives.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call to honor the power of words and wisdom in our lives. It serves as a reminder that our thoughts and expressions can illuminate our paths and that true strength is found in nurturing creativity and courage. By applying these principles, we can foster a more harmonious and enlightened existence.
