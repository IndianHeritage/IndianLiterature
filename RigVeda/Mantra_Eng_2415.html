गोमद ऊषु नासत्याश्वाद यातम अश्विनाः |वर्ति रुद्राः नृपाय्यम् ||
gomad ū ṣu nāsatyāśvāvad yātam aśvinā | vartī rudrā nṛpāyyam ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • gomad: Refers to "the one who has cows" or "wealthy," symbolizing prosperity and abundance.
    • ū: A particle that can mean "indeed" or "certainly," emphasizing the statement.
    • ṣu: Often interpreted as "good" or "well," indicating positivity.
    • nāsatyāśvāvad: Refers to the "Nāsatyas," the twin deities (Ashvins) known for their healing and benevolent nature, often depicted as horsemen.
    • yātam: Means "to go" or "to move," suggesting action or progress.
    • aśvinā: Again refers to the Ashvins, highlighting their role in the mantra.
    • vartī: Means "to be" or "to exist," indicating presence or existence.
    • rudrā: Refers to "Rudra," a form of Shiva, associated with storms and the hunt, symbolizing power and transformation.
    • nṛpāyyam: Translates to "for the kings" or "for the rulers," indicating a connection to leadership and governance.
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of the Ashvins, the divine twins known for their healing and protective qualities.
    • It emphasizes the importance of prosperity (gomad) and the positive actions (yātam) that lead to well-being.
    • The mention of Rudra suggests a need for strength and transformation in leadership (nṛpāyyam), indicating that rulers should embody these qualities for the benefit of their people.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, a leader who embodies the qualities of the Ashvins—such as innovation and care for employees—can lead their company to prosperity.
    • In healthcare, the invocation of healing powers can be seen in the work of doctors who strive to provide the best care, akin to the Ashvins' role in healing.
    • In governance, leaders who are strong (like Rudra) and compassionate can create a society that thrives, ensuring the well-being of their citizens.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine guidance in achieving prosperity and well-being.
    • It highlights the need for leaders to embody strength and compassion, drawing on the qualities of the Ashvins and Rudra.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder for individuals in positions of power to act with integrity and care, fostering a positive environment for growth and healing.
