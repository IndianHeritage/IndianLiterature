त्वं पुरं चरिष्ण्वं वधैः शुष्णस्य सम पिणक् |त्वं भा अनु करो अध द्विता यद इन्द्र हव्यो भुवः ||
tvam puraṁ cariṣṇvaṁ vadhaiḥ śuṣṇasya sam piṇak | tvam bhā anu caro adha dvitā yad indra havyo bhuvaḥ ||
  • tvam puraṁ cariṣṇvaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "You are the one who moves through the city." Here, "tvam" means "you," "puraṁ" refers to "city," and "cariṣṇvaṁ" means "moving" or "wandering." This can symbolize the presence of a divine force or consciousness that pervades all aspects of life and existence.
  • vadhaiḥ śuṣṇasya sam piṇak: This part translates to "You are the one who is bound by the ties of the world." "Vadhaiḥ" means "by ties," "śuṣṇasya" refers to "of the world," and "sam piṇak" can be interpreted as "tightly bound." This suggests the idea of being interconnected with the world and the responsibilities that come with it.
  • tvam bhā anu caro adha: This translates to "You are the light that moves beneath." "Bhā" means "light," "anu" means "following," "caro" means "moving," and "adha" means "beneath." This can signify the underlying truth or wisdom that guides our actions and decisions.
  • dvitā yad indra havyo bhuvaḥ: This phrase means "You are the second, O Indra, who is worthy of worship." "Dvitā" means "second," "yad" means "who," "indra" refers to the king of gods, and "havyo bhuvaḥ" means "worthy of worship." This highlights the importance of recognizing and honoring the divine presence in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, the divine presence that guides us, and the importance of recognizing our responsibilities in the world. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to our daily interactions, reminding us to be aware of our actions and their impact on others. It encourages us to seek wisdom and light in our decisions, fostering a sense of community and responsibility. By acknowledging the divine in ourselves and others, we can cultivate a more harmonious and meaningful existence.
