अभि प्रियाः दिवस पदाः सोमो हिन्वानो अर्षति |विप्रस्य धारणया कविः ||
abhi priyā divas padā somo hinvāno arṣati | viprasya dhārayā kaviḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • priyā: beloved, dear
    • divas: day, sky
    • padā: path, way
    • somo: the moon, often symbolizing nourishment and tranquility
    • hinvāno: shining, illuminating
    • arṣati: to rise, to ascend
    • viprasya: of the wise, learned
    • dhārayā: by holding, supporting
    • kaviḥ: the poet, seer, or wise one
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the beloved day (or sky) and the moon that rises and shines brightly.
    • The moon is often associated with calmness and nourishment, suggesting a sense of peace and stability.
    • The reference to the wise one (kavi) indicates the importance of knowledge and wisdom in guiding one's path.
    • The phrase "by holding" suggests that wisdom supports and sustains us as we navigate through life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we often seek guidance from wise individuals, such as mentors or teachers, who illuminate our paths much like the moon lights up the night sky.
    • During challenging times, the calmness associated with the moon can serve as a reminder to remain composed and seek clarity.
    • In nature, the rising of the moon can symbolize new beginnings, encouraging us to embrace change and growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom and guidance in our lives.
    • It reminds us to seek knowledge and support from those who are learned, as they can help us navigate through life's challenges.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages us to appreciate the beauty and tranquility that comes with knowledge, much like the serene light of the moon in the night sky.
