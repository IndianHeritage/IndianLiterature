अरुषस्य दुहितरा विरूपे स्त्रीभिर अन्यā पिपीषे सूरो अन्यā |मिथस्तुराविचरन्ती पावके मनमा श्रुतं नक्षत ऋच्यमाने ||
aruṣasya duhitarā virūpe stṛbhir anyā pipiśe sūro anyā | mithasturā vicarantī pāvake manma śrutaṁ nakṣata ṛcyamāne ||
  • aruṣasya: This word translates to "of the red" or "of the dawn." It symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the arrival of light after darkness.
  • duhitarā: Meaning "daughter," it signifies nurturing and the feminine aspect of creation. In real life, this can be related to the role of women in society as caregivers and creators.
  • virūpe: This translates to "in various forms" or "diverse." It reflects the idea of diversity in nature and life, emphasizing that beauty can be found in different forms.
  • stṛbhir: Meaning "with the women" or "by the women," it highlights the importance of collaboration and community, particularly among women.
  • anyā: This word means "other" or "different," suggesting the presence of alternatives or choices in life.
  • pipiśe: Translated as "to drink" or "to consume," it can symbolize the act of taking in knowledge, experiences, or nourishment.
  • sūro: Meaning "hero" or "brave one," it represents strength and valor, qualities that are essential in overcoming challenges.
  • mithasturā: This can be interpreted as "in the midst of" or "among," indicating a sense of being surrounded by various influences or forces.
  • vicarantī: Meaning "wandering" or "moving about," it suggests exploration and the journey of life, where one learns and grows through experiences.
  • pāvake: Translated as "in the fire," it symbolizes purification and transformation, akin to how fire can refine and change materials.
  • manma: This word means "my" or "of me," indicating a personal connection to the experiences and lessons being conveyed.
  • śrutaṁ: Meaning "heard" or "listened to," it emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom that comes from listening and learning.
  • nakṣata: This translates to "stars," symbolizing guidance, aspirations, and the vastness of the universe.
  • ṛcyamāne: Meaning "being recited" or "being chanted," it highlights the significance of oral tradition and the power of words in shaping culture and beliefs.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates themes of diversity, collaboration, and the journey of life. It emphasizes the importance of nurturing relationships, the strength found in community, and the transformative power of experiences. In real life, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as fostering teamwork in professional settings, embracing diversity in social interactions, and valuing the wisdom gained through listening and learning from others. The mantra serves as a reminder to appreciate the beauty in differences and to seek knowledge and growth through our connections with one another.
