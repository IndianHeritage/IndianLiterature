अश्वावतीर गोमतीर विश्ववारा यतमानā रश्मिभिः सूर्यस्य |परा च यन्ति पुनर आ च यन्ति भद्रā नाम वहमानā उषासः ||
aśvāvatīr gomatīr viśvavārā yatamānā raśmibhiḥ sūryasya | parā ca yanti punar ā ca yanti bhadrā nāma vahamānā uṣāsaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "aśvāvatīr gomatīr viśvavārā yatamānā raśmibhiḥ sūryasya | parā ca yanti punar ā ca yanti bhadrā nāma vahamānā uṣāsaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvāvatīr: One who is like a horse, symbolizing speed and strength.
    • gomatīr: One who is like a cow, representing nourishment and abundance.
    • viśvavārā: All-encompassing, protective, or universal.
    • yatamānā: Striving or making efforts.
    • raśmibhiḥ: By the rays (of the sun).
    • sūryasya: Of the sun.
    • parā ca: And beyond.
    • yanti: They go or proceed.
    • punar: Again or once more.
    • ā ca: And also.
    • bhadrā: Auspicious or beneficial.
    • nāma: By name or called.
    • vahamānā: Carrying or bringing.
    • uṣāsaḥ: Dawn or the early morning light.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the qualities of speed, nourishment, and universality, represented by the horse and cow.
    • The mention of the sun's rays signifies enlightenment, knowledge, and the dispelling of darkness.
    • The phrase "parā ca yanti" suggests that these qualities transcend ordinary existence, indicating a higher state of being.
    • The repetition of "yanti" emphasizes the cyclical nature of life and the continuous journey towards auspiciousness.
    • The dawn, or "uṣāsaḥ," symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the promise of a new day.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, the qualities of speed and nourishment can be seen in the pursuit of education and career growth, where individuals strive to achieve their goals.
    • The protective and universal aspect can be related to community service, where individuals work together for the welfare of all.
    • The dawn represents opportunities; for instance, starting a new project or initiative can be seen as a new dawn in one's professional life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of striving for knowledge, nourishment, and protection in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to embrace new beginnings and work towards the greater good, fostering a sense of community and universal well-being.
    • In real-life scenarios, the teachings of this mantra can guide personal growth, community involvement, and the pursuit of knowledge, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
