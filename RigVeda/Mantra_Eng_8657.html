प्र सुन्वानस्यांधसो मार्तो न वृता तद वचः |अप श्वानम् अराधसं हता मखं न भृगवः ||
pra sunvānasyāndhaso marto na vṛta tad vacaḥ | apa śvānam arādhasaṁ hatā makhaṁ na bhṛgavaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: before, in front of
    • sunvānasyāndhaso: of the one who is blind (or ignorant) to the truth
    • marto: mortal, human
    • na: not
    • vṛta: covered, concealed
    • tad: that
    • vacaḥ: speech, words
    • apa: away from
    • śvānam: the dog (symbolizing loyalty or guidance)
    • arādhasaṁ: the one who is not to be harmed
    • hatā: killed, destroyed
    • makhaṁ: sacrifice, offering
    • na: not
    • bhṛgavaḥ: the wise or learned ones
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the importance of clarity and truth in communication.
    • It suggests that when one is blind to the truth, their words may be concealed or ineffective.
    • The reference to a dog symbolizes loyalty and the need for guidance in seeking truth.
    • The mantra warns against harming those who are innocent or loyal, emphasizing the value of sacrifice and offerings in a spiritual context.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, clear communication is essential. If a manager is unaware of the truth behind a situation, their decisions may lead to misunderstandings and conflict.
    • In personal relationships, being open and honest can prevent the concealment of feelings, leading to stronger bonds.
    • The loyalty of a friend (symbolized by the dog) can guide one through difficult times, highlighting the importance of surrounding oneself with trustworthy individuals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of truthfulness and clarity in our words and actions.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek guidance and to be mindful of the impact our words can have on others.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to healthier relationships, effective communication, and a deeper understanding of oneself and others.
