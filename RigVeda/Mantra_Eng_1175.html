तन्न इन्द्रस्तद वरुणस्तद अग्निस्तद आर्यमात्त सविता चनो धात् |तन्नो मित्रो वरुणो माऽमहन्ताम् अदितिः सिन्धुः पृथिवी उता द्यौः ||
tan na indras tad varuṇas tad agnis tad aryamā tat savitā cano dhāt | tan no mitro varuṇo māmahantām aditiḥ sindhuḥ pṛthivī uta dyauḥ ||
  • tan na indras tad varuṇas tad agnis tad aryamā tat savitā cano dhāt
    • tan - This word translates to "that" or "those," indicating a connection to divine forces.
    • na - Means "not," suggesting a negation or a request for something to be absent.
    • indras - Refers to Indra, the king of gods, associated with rain, storms, and strength.
    • varuṇas - Refers to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water, symbolizing moral and natural law.
    • agnis - Represents Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • aryamā - A deity associated with the path of the sun and the journey of the soul.
    • tat - Means "that," reinforcing the connection to the divine.
    • savitā - Refers to Savitar, the sun god, representing life and illumination.
    • cano - Means "may," indicating a wish or desire.
    • dhāt - Means "to support" or "to uphold," suggesting a plea for divine assistance.
  • tan no mitro varuṇo māmahantām aditiḥ sindhuḥ pṛthivī uta dyauḥ
    • tan - Again, "that," referring to the divine forces.
    • no - Means "our," indicating a personal connection to the divine.
    • mitro - Refers to Mitra, the god of friendship and contracts, symbolizing harmony and cooperation.
    • varuṇo - Again, Varuna, emphasizing the importance of cosmic order.
    • māmahantām - Means "greatness" or "magnificence," suggesting a desire for divine blessings.
    • aditiḥ - Refers to Aditi, the mother of gods, symbolizing infinity and the nurturing aspect of the universe.
    • sindhuḥ - Means "ocean" or "river," representing the flow of life and abundance.
    • pṛthivī - Refers to the earth, symbolizing stability and nourishment.
    • uta - Means "and," connecting the elements of the mantra.
    • dyauḥ - Refers to the sky or heaven, symbolizing the vastness of existence.

Conclusion:

This mantra invokes various deities, seeking their blessings and support in life. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, the cosmos, and human existence. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In times of difficulty, one can recite this mantra to seek strength and guidance from divine forces.
  • In communal gatherings, it can be used to foster harmony and cooperation among individuals, reflecting the essence of Mitra.
  • In rituals or ceremonies, it serves as a reminder of the importance of balance and respect for nature, as represented by the earth and the sky.

Overall, the mantra signifies a deep respect for the divine and the natural order, encouraging individuals to seek harmony within themselves and with the world around them.
