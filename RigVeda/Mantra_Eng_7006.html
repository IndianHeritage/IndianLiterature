त्वं हि नस् तन््वः सोम गोपा गात्रे-गात्रे निषसत्था नृचक्षाः |यत ते वयम् प्रामिनाम व्रतानि स नो मृळा सुशाखा देव वस्यः ||
tvaṁ hi nas tanvaḥ soma gopā gātre-gātre niṣasatthā nṛcakṣāḥ | yat te vayam pramināma vratāni sa no mṛḻa suṣakhā deva vasyaḥ ||
  • tvaṁ hi nas tanvaḥ: This phrase translates to "You are indeed our body." Here, "tvaṁ" means "you," "hi" emphasizes "indeed," and "tanvaḥ" refers to "body." This suggests a deep connection between the divine and the physical form of the individual.
  • soma gopā: "Soma" refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. "Gopā" means "protector" or "guardian." Together, this can be interpreted as a plea for divine protection and nourishment.
  • gātre-gātre niṣasatthā nṛcakṣāḥ: This line can be understood as "You reside in every limb of our body, O divine seer." "Gātre-gātre" means "in every limb," and "nṛcakṣāḥ" refers to "human seer" or "divine observer." It emphasizes the omnipresence of the divine within us.
  • yat te vayam pramināma vratāni: This translates to "We offer our vows to you." "Yat te" means "to you," "vayam" means "we," and "pramināma" refers to "offer" or "dedicate." "Vratāni" means "vows" or "sacred promises." This line signifies devotion and commitment to the divine.
  • sa no mṛḻa suṣakhā deva vasyaḥ: This can be interpreted as "May you grant us happiness and well-being, O divine one." "Sa no" means "may you," "mṛḻa" refers to "grant," "suṣakhā" means "happiness," and "deva vasyaḥ" means "O divine one." This is a prayer for blessings and prosperity.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, the recognition of the divine presence within oneself, and the plea for protection and well-being. It highlights the interconnectedness of the individual with the divine and the universe.
  • Real-life Application: In daily life, this mantra can be recited during meditation or prayer to foster a sense of connection with the divine. It serves as a reminder to seek protection and guidance in challenging times, and to express gratitude for the blessings received. For instance, before starting a new venture or facing a difficult situation, one might recite this mantra to invoke divine support and ensure a positive outcome.
