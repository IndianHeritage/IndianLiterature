ते नो नावं उरुष्यत दिवा नक्तं सुदानवः |अरिष्यन्तो नि पायुभिः सचेमहि ||
te no nāvam uruṣyata divā naktaṁ sudānavaḥ | ariṣyanto ni pāyubhiḥ sacemahi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • te: to you
    • no: our
    • nāvam: boat or vessel
    • uruṣyata: may it be strong or stable
    • divā: by day
    • naktaṁ: by night
    • sudānavaḥ: well-built or well-constructed
    • ariṣyanto: may they not be harmed
    • ni: from
    • pāyubhiḥ: enemies or obstacles
    • sacemahi: we may attain or achieve
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer for stability and strength in our endeavors, symbolized by a boat that can navigate through the challenges of life.
    • The mention of "day and night" signifies the continuous nature of life's journey, where one must remain steadfast regardless of the time or circumstances.
    • The term "sudānavaḥ" emphasizes the importance of being well-prepared and equipped to face life's challenges, much like a sturdy vessel that can withstand storms.
    • The plea for protection from "ariṣyanto ni pāyubhiḥ" highlights the desire for safety from adversities and obstacles that may arise in our path.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be likened to a startup seeking stability in a competitive market. Just as a boat needs to be well-constructed to navigate rough waters, a business must be well-planned and resilient to succeed.
    • In personal life, individuals often face challenges such as health issues or relationship problems. The mantra serves as a reminder to remain strong and seek support, much like a sturdy boat that keeps its passengers safe during turbulent times.
    • In education, students can relate to this mantra as they prepare for exams. They need to be well-prepared (sudānavaḥ) and resilient (uruṣyata) to overcome the pressures of academic life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of resilience, preparation, and the pursuit of safety in the face of life's uncertainties.
    • Its significance lies in its universal applicability, reminding us to build our "boats" strong enough to navigate through the challenges we encounter, whether in personal, professional, or academic spheres.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset of strength and preparedness, ensuring they are equipped to handle whatever life may throw their way.
