आसां पूर्वासां अहसु स्वसृणाम् अपरा पूर्वाम् अभ्य एति पश्चात् |ताः प्रत्नवन नव्यसीर नूनम् अस्मे रेवद उचन्तु सुदिना उषासः ||
āsām pūrvāsām ahasu svasṝṇām aparā pūrvām abhy eti paścāt | tāḥ pratnavan navyasīr nūnam asme revad ucchantu sudinā uṣāsaḥ ||
  • Transliteration: āsām pūrvāsām ahasu svasṝṇām aparā pūrvām abhy eti paścāt | tāḥ pratnavan navyasīr nūnam asme revad ucchantu sudinā uṣāsaḥ ||
  • Meaning:
    • āsām: of these
    • pūrvāsām: of the earlier ones
    • ahasu: in the light
    • svasṝṇām: of the sisters
    • aparā: the later
    • pūrvām: the earlier
    • abhy eti: approaches
    • paścāt: afterwards
    • tāḥ: they
    • pratnavan: who are rich in treasures
    • navyasīr: newly born
    • nūnam: surely
    • asme: for us
    • revad: may they shine
    • ucchantu: may they rise
    • sudinā: with good fortune
    • uṣāsaḥ: the dawns
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the dawns (uṣāsaḥ) that come to us, highlighting the transition from darkness to light.
    • The reference to "sisters" (svasṝṇām) can symbolize the various aspects of life or different opportunities that arise.
    • The phrase "approaches afterwards" (abhy eti paścāt) suggests that new opportunities or insights come after the initial ones, emphasizing the importance of patience and awareness.
    • The mantra encourages us to seek the treasures (pratnavan) that life offers, which can be interpreted as knowledge, experiences, or relationships.
    • The invocation for the dawns to shine with good fortune (sudinā) indicates a desire for positivity and prosperity in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional context, this mantra can be applied to career growth. Just as the dawn brings new light, new job opportunities may arise after one has gained experience in their current role.
    • In personal relationships, the idea of "sisters" can represent friends or family members who support us through different phases of life, reminding us to cherish those connections.
    • In education, the mantra can inspire students to recognize that learning is a continuous process, where new knowledge builds upon previous understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the cyclical nature of life, where new beginnings follow previous experiences.
    • It encourages us to remain open to new opportunities and to appreciate the support we receive from those around us.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to seek positivity and fortune in our endeavors, aligning our actions with the natural rhythms of life.
