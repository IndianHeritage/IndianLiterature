स त्वामदद वृषा मदः सोमः श्येनाभृतः सुतः |येन वृतं निर अद्भ्यो जगन्थ वज्रिन्न ओजसार्चन्न अनु स्वराज्यम् ||
sa tvāmadad vṛṣā madaḥ somaḥ śyenābhṛtaḥ sutaḥ | yenā vṛtraṁ nir adbhyo jaghantha vajrinn ojasārcann anu svarājyam ||
  • sa tvāmadad vṛṣā madaḥ somaḥ śyenābhṛtaḥ sutaḥ
    • sa - This word translates to "that" or "he," indicating a reference to a divine entity or force.
    • tvā - Meaning "you," it signifies a direct address to the divine.
    • madad - This term can be interpreted as "intoxication" or "ecstasy," often associated with the divine experience.
    • vṛṣā - This word means "strong" or "powerful," suggesting the might of the divine.
    • madaḥ - Refers to "joy" or "delight," emphasizing the blissful state that comes from divine connection.
    • somaḥ - Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
    • śyenābhṛtaḥ - This term refers to "the one who is carried by the eagle," symbolizing strength and vision.
    • sutaḥ - Meaning "son" or "offspring," it indicates a lineage or connection to the divine.
  • yenā vṛtraṁ nir adbhyo jaghantha vajrinn ojasārcann anu svarājyam
    • yenā - Translates to "by whom," indicating the agency of the divine in action.
    • vṛtraṁ - Refers to "Vritra," a serpent or dragon symbolizing obstacles or challenges.
    • nir - Meaning "from" or "out of," it indicates separation or removal.
    • adbhyo - This word means "the waters," often symbolizing the source of life and purity.
    • jaghantha - Translates to "defeated" or "overcome," indicating victory over challenges.
    • vajrinn - Refers to "the thunderbolt," a symbol of power and divine strength.
    • ojasārcann - Meaning "with energy" or "with vigor," it emphasizes the force behind the action.
    • anu - This word means "after" or "following," indicating a sequence of events.
    • svarājyam - Refers to "the realm of sound" or "the kingdom of the divine," symbolizing a higher state of existence.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of divine strength, joy, and the overcoming of obstacles. It highlights the relationship between the divine and the individual, emphasizing the importance of connection to a higher power for achieving success and overcoming challenges in life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during times of struggle or when facing significant challenges, serving as a reminder of the strength and support available through spiritual connection. It encourages individuals to seek divine inspiration and strength, much like how one might turn to a mentor or a source of wisdom during difficult times. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire courage and resilience, reminding us that with divine support, we can overcome any obstacle and achieve our goals.
