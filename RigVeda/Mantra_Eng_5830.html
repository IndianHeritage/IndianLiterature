मन्त्र: रदत् पथो वरुणः सूर्याय प्रार्थनांसी समुद्रियाः नदीनाम् |सर्गो न सृष्टो अर्वतीर ऋतायञ्च चकार महीर अवनीर अहभ्यः ||
radat patho varuṇaḥ sūryāya prārṇāṁsi samudriyā nadīnām | sargo na sṛṣṭo arvatīr ṛtāyañ cakāra mahīr avanīr ahabhyaḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O Varuna, who moves along the paths, to the Sun, I offer my prayers. The rivers of the ocean, which are not created, have been made for the sake of truth, and the Earth has been established for the benefit of all beings."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • radat: moving or flowing
    • patho: paths or ways
    • varuṇaḥ: Varuna, the deity associated with water and cosmic order
    • sūryāya: to the Sun, representing light and life
    • prārṇāṁsi: I offer my prayers or invocations
    • samudriyā: of the ocean, symbolizing vastness and depth
    • nadīnām: of the rivers, representing the flow of life
    • sargo: creation or emergence
    • na: not
    • sṛṣṭo: created
    • arvatīr: for the sake of truth or righteousness
    • ṛtāyañ: for the sake of order or cosmic law
    • cakāra: has been established or made
    • mahīr: the Earth or the great one
    • avanīr: the ground or land
    • ahabhyaḥ: for the benefit of all beings
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The invocation to Varuna can be seen in the context of environmental conservation. Just as Varuna governs water, we must respect and protect our water bodies, ensuring they remain pure and abundant for all life forms.
    • The reference to the Sun highlights the importance of solar energy in our lives today. Harnessing solar power is a modern application of the reverence for the Sun, promoting sustainability and reducing our carbon footprint.
    • The mention of rivers and oceans can be related to the global efforts in preserving marine life and ecosystems, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all living beings and the need for collective responsibility.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for harmony with nature and the cosmic order. It reminds us of our responsibilities towards the environment and the importance of truth and righteousness in our actions. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to engage in sustainable practices, respect natural resources, and foster a sense of unity with all living beings. By embodying these principles, we contribute to a balanced and harmonious existence on Earth.
