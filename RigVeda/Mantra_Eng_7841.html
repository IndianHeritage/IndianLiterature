आ कलशेषु धावति पवित्रे परि शिच्यते |उक्तैः यज्ञेषु वर्धते ||
ā kalaśeṣu dhāvati pavitre pari ṣicyate | ukthair yajñeṣu vardhate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • kalaśeṣu: in the pots (kalaśa refers to a pot, often used in rituals)
    • dhāvati: flows or moves
    • pavitre: in purity
    • pari: all around
    • ṣicyate: is sprinkled or poured
    • ukthair: with the utterance of mantras or hymns
    • yajñeṣu: in sacrifices or rituals
    • vardhate: increases or flourishes
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the sacredness and purity associated with rituals.
    • The act of flowing or moving from the pots signifies the essence of offerings made during rituals.
    • When the offerings are made with purity and accompanied by the chanting of mantras, they are believed to enhance the spiritual atmosphere.
    • The phrase "in sacrifices" indicates that these rituals are not just ceremonial but are meant to invoke divine blessings.
    • The overall message is that purity in intention and action leads to spiritual growth and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern-day practices, many people perform rituals during festivals, such as Diwali or Navratri, where offerings are made with pure intentions.
    • During weddings, the couple often participates in rituals that symbolize purity and the flow of blessings from the divine.
    • In personal life, maintaining purity in thoughts and actions can lead to positive outcomes, much like the mantra suggests.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on purity and the power of rituals.
    • It teaches that when we engage in actions with a pure heart and intention, we can attract positive energies and blessings into our lives.
    • This principle is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal growth, relationships, and community interactions.
    • By understanding and applying the essence of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
