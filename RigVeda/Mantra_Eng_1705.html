मन्त्र: असि यमो अस्य आदित्यो अर्वन्न असि तृतो गुह्येन व्रतेन | असि सोमेन समया विपृक्त आहुस ते त्रिणि दिवि बन्धनानि ||
asi yamo asy ādityo arvann asi trito guhyena vratena | asi somena samayā vipṛkta āhus te trīṇi divi bandhanāni ||
  • asi yamo asy ādityo arvann: This phrase can be translated to "This is the restraint of the sun, the one who is the lord of the day." Here, "yamo" refers to restraint or control, while "ādityo" signifies the sun, representing light, knowledge, and energy. In real life, this can be seen as the importance of self-control and discipline in harnessing one's energy and potential.
  • asi trito guhyena vratena: This translates to "This is the third, hidden by the vow." The term "trito" indicates the third aspect, which can refer to various dimensions of existence or consciousness. "Guhyena" means hidden or secret, and "vratena" refers to a vow or commitment. This suggests that there are deeper truths or paths that require dedication and commitment to uncover. In everyday life, this can relate to the pursuit of knowledge or spiritual growth, where one must commit to a path to discover deeper insights.
  • asi somena samayā: This means "This is the time of the moon." The moon often symbolizes emotions, intuition, and the subconscious. "Samayā" refers to time or season. This can be interpreted as the importance of recognizing and honoring our emotional cycles and the need for balance in our lives. For instance, just as the moon goes through phases, individuals experience emotional highs and lows, and understanding this can lead to better emotional health.
  • vipṛkta āhus te trīṇi divi bandhanāni: This translates to "They say that you have three bonds in the sky." "Vipṛkta" means to be separated or distinct, while "trīṇi" refers to three. "Divi" means in the sky or heavens, and "bandhanāni" means bonds or ties. This can signify the three fundamental aspects of existence: physical, mental, and spiritual. In practical terms, this can be seen in how individuals balance their physical health, mental well-being, and spiritual practices to lead a harmonious life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of restraint, commitment, emotional awareness, and the interconnectedness of different aspects of life. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to practice self-discipline, pursue knowledge with dedication, honor their emotional cycles, and recognize the balance between physical, mental, and spiritual well-being. By applying these principles, one can achieve a more harmonious and fulfilling life, aligning with the deeper truths of existence.
