स त्वं दाक्षस्यावृको वृधो भूः आर्यः परस्यान्तरस्य तरुषः | रायः सूनो सहसो मर्त्येष्वा चर्दिर यच्छ वीतहव्याय सप्रथो भरद्वाजाय सप्रथाḥ ||
sa tvaṁ dakṣasyāvṛko vṛdho bhūr aryaḥ parasyāntarasya taruṣaḥ | rāyaḥ sūno sahaso martyeṣv ā chardir yaccha vītahavyāya sapratho bharadvājāya saprathaḥ ||
  • sa tvaṁ: This phrase translates to "you are" or "you are that." It signifies the connection between the individual and the divine or universal consciousness.
  • dakṣasyāvṛko: This can be interpreted as "the skilled wolf" or "the capable one." In real life, this could represent someone who is adept at navigating challenges, much like a skilled leader or strategist.
  • vṛdho: Meaning "great" or "powerful," this term emphasizes strength and capability. For example, a powerful leader in a community who inspires others can be seen as embodying this quality.
  • bhūr: This word means "abundant" or "plentiful." It can refer to the richness of life, such as the abundance found in nature or in relationships.
  • aryaḥ: Translated as "noble" or "honorable," this term reflects the qualities of integrity and virtue. An example would be a person who stands up for justice and fairness in society.
  • parasyāntarasya: This phrase means "of the other" or "of the beyond." It suggests a connection to something greater than oneself, such as the universe or a higher power.
  • taruṣaḥ: This translates to "of the trees" or "of nature." It highlights the importance of nature in our lives, reminding us of our connection to the environment.
  • rāyaḥ: Meaning "wealth" or "prosperity," this term emphasizes the importance of material and spiritual wealth in life.
  • sūno: This word means "of the son" or "descendant." It can represent the legacy we leave for future generations, such as values, traditions, and knowledge.
  • sahaso: Translated as "with strength" or "with power," this term emphasizes the importance of resilience and determination in overcoming obstacles.
  • martyeṣv: This means "among mortals" or "in the human realm." It signifies our existence in the physical world and the challenges we face as humans.
  • ā chardir: This phrase can be interpreted as "may there be no harm" or "may there be no destruction." It reflects a desire for peace and safety in our lives.
  • yaccha: Meaning "that which" or "whatever," this term emphasizes the importance of acceptance and understanding in life.
  • vītahavyāya: This can be interpreted as "to the one who is worthy of offerings." It signifies respect and reverence for those who contribute positively to society.
  • sapratho: This means "with abundance" or "with plenty." It reflects the idea of sharing and generosity in our interactions with others.
  • bharadvājāya: This refers to a sage or a wise person, emphasizing the importance of wisdom and knowledge in guiding our actions.
  • saprathaḥ: This term means "with plenty" or "with abundance," reinforcing the idea of sharing and community support.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, nobility, and connection to the divine and nature. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom, abundance, and the legacy we leave for future generations. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to strive for personal growth, foster community relationships, and respect the environment. It serves as a reminder to live with integrity and to contribute positively to the world around us, ensuring a harmonious existence among all beings.
