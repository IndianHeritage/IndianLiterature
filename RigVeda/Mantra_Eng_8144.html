त्वं समुद्रियाऽपो 'गृह्यो वाच इरयन् |पवस्व विश्वमेजय ||
tvaṁ samudriyā apo 'griyo vāca īrayan | pavasva viśvamejaya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • samudriyā: Oceanic or vast
    • apo: Waters
    • 'griyo: Of the earth or worldly
    • vāca: Speech or voice
    • īrayan: Flowing or moving
    • pavasva: Purify or cleanse
    • viśvamejaya: All-pervading or universal victory
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the vastness of the ocean and the purifying qualities of water.
    • It emphasizes the importance of speech and communication, suggesting that words have the power to flow and influence.
    • The call to "pavasva" indicates a desire for purification, both of the self and the environment.
    • "Viśvamejaya" signifies a universal victory, hinting at the interconnectedness of all beings and the triumph of collective harmony.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In environmental contexts, this mantra can be related to the importance of clean water and oceans, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
    • In communication, it serves as a reminder of the impact of our words, encouraging mindful speech that promotes understanding and unity.
    • In personal development, the idea of purification can be applied to mental and emotional cleansing, fostering a healthier mindset.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of purity, communication, and universal connection.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to respect and protect our natural resources, to communicate thoughtfully, and to strive for collective well-being.
    • In daily life, it encourages us to be mindful of our actions and words, promoting a harmonious existence with ourselves and the world around us.
