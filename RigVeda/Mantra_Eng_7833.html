प्र पुनानस्य चेतसा सोमः पवित्रे अर्षति |क्रत्वा सदस्थं आसदत ||
pra punānasya cetasā somaḥ pavitre arṣati | kratvā sadhastham āsadat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: before, in front of
    • punānasya: of the purifying, cleansing
    • cetasā: with the mind, intellect
    • somaḥ: the divine nectar, often associated with the moon and immortality
    • pavitre: pure, sacred
    • arṣati: shines, radiates
    • kratvā: by the power of action, through effort
    • sadhastham: in the assembly, gathering
    • āsadat: they sit, they dwell
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of the purifying power of the divine nectar (Soma) that shines with a pure mind.
    • It emphasizes the importance of collective effort and action in a sacred gathering or assembly.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community service project, individuals come together with a pure intention to help others, reflecting the essence of "kratvā sadhastham āsadat." Their collective effort purifies their minds and strengthens their bond.
    • During a meditation retreat, participants focus on purifying their thoughts and intentions, akin to "pra punānasya cetasā," allowing them to connect deeply with themselves and each other.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of purity of mind and intention in any collective endeavor.
    • It can be applied in various aspects of life, such as teamwork in professional settings, community gatherings, or spiritual practices.
    • By fostering a pure mindset and working together, individuals can achieve greater harmony and success in their pursuits.
