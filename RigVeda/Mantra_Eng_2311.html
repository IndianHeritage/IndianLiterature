तं वः शर्धं मारुतं सुम्नयुर गीरोपा ब्रुवे नमसां दैव्यं जनम् |यथा रयिं सर्ववीरं नशामहा अपत्यसाचं श्रुत्यं दिवे-दिवे ||
taṁ vaḥ śardham mārutaṁ sumnayur giropa bruve namasā daivyaṁ janam | yathā rayiṁ sarvavīraṁ naśāmahā apatyasācaṁ śrutyaṁ dive-dive ||
  • taṁ vaḥ - This phrase can be translated as "to that" or "to Him." It signifies a direction of reverence towards a divine entity or force.
  • śardham - This word refers to "together" or "in unity." It emphasizes the importance of collective effort and harmony among individuals.
  • mārutaṁ - This term is associated with the "Maruts," the storm deities in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and the forces of nature.
  • sumnayur - This word means "auspicious" or "beneficial," indicating a desire for positive outcomes and well-being.
  • giropa - This can be interpreted as "to speak" or "to express," highlighting the significance of communication and sharing of thoughts.
  • bruve - This means "to declare" or "to proclaim," reinforcing the idea of expressing intentions and desires openly.
  • namasā - This term translates to "with reverence" or "with respect," indicating a humble approach towards the divine or higher powers.
  • daivyaṁ janam - This phrase means "divine beings" or "the divine community," suggesting a connection to a higher spiritual existence.
  • yathā rayiṁ - This translates to "as wealth" or "as prosperity," indicating a desire for abundance and success.
  • sarvavīraṁ - This means "all heroes" or "all strong ones," emphasizing the collective strength and valor of individuals.
  • naśāmahā - This can be interpreted as "we do not perish" or "we do not decline," suggesting a quest for immortality or enduring legacy.
  • apatyasācaṁ - This term refers to "offspring" or "descendants," highlighting the importance of lineage and continuity.
  • śrutyaṁ - This means "hearing" or "listening," indicating the value of knowledge and wisdom passed down through generations.
  • dive-dive - This phrase translates to "day by day," suggesting a continuous process or daily practice.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about unity, reverence, and the pursuit of prosperity and strength within a community. It emphasizes the importance of collective efforts and the sharing of knowledge for the betterment of society. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community gatherings, team-building exercises, or any collaborative efforts aimed at achieving common goals. By invoking the spirit of togetherness and respect for the divine, individuals can foster a sense of belonging and purpose, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
