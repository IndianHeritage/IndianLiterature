यत् प्रायासिष्ट पृषतीभिर अश्वैर वीलुपविभिर मरुतो रथेभिः |क्षोदन्त आपो रिणते वनान्य अवोश्रियो वृषभः क्रन्दतु द्युः ||
yat prāyāsiṣṭa pṛṣatībhir aśvair vīḻupavibhir maruto rathebhiḥ | kṣodanta āpo riṇate vanāny avosriyo vṛṣabhaḥ krandatu dyauḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yat prāyāsiṣṭa pṛṣatībhir aśvair vīḻupavibhir maruto rathebhiḥ | kṣodanta āpo riṇate vanāny avosriyo vṛṣabhaḥ krandatu dyauḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yat: whatever
    • prāyāsiṣṭa: you have attained
    • pṛṣatībhir: with the swift ones
    • aśvair: with the horses
    • vīḻupavibhir: with the strong ones
    • maruto: O Winds
    • rathebhiḥ: with the chariots
    • kṣodanta: flowing
    • āpo: waters
    • riṇate: resounding
    • vanāny: forests
    • avosriyo: the unrestrained
    • vṛṣabhaḥ: the bull
    • krandatu: may he create
    • dyauḥ: the sky
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the forces of nature, particularly the winds and waters, to bless the seeker with abundance and strength.
    • The phrase "yat prāyāsiṣṭa" suggests a quest for what has been achieved or attained, emphasizing the importance of recognizing one's accomplishments.
    • The mention of "pṛṣatībhir aśvair" refers to the swift horses, symbolizing speed and agility, which can be related to the need for quick decision-making in life.
    • "Maruto rathebhiḥ" highlights the winds and chariots, representing movement and progress, akin to how we navigate through life's challenges.
    • The term "kṣodanta āpo" refers to the flowing waters, which can be likened to the flow of life and the importance of adaptability.
    • "Vanāny avosriyo" speaks to the unrestrained forests, symbolizing the untamed aspects of nature and life, reminding us to embrace the wild and unpredictable.
    • The concluding part "vṛṣabhaḥ krandatu dyauḥ" calls for the creation of strength and stability in the sky, which can be interpreted as a desire for a solid foundation in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can inspire entrepreneurs to recognize their achievements and adapt quickly to market changes, much like the swift horses.
    • In personal development, it encourages individuals to embrace their strengths and navigate through life's challenges with resilience, akin to the flowing waters.
    • In environmental awareness, it serves as a reminder of the importance of nature and the need to respect and protect our forests and waters.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of nature and human endeavors, urging us to recognize our achievements while remaining adaptable and resilient.
    • Its application in real life is vast, from personal growth to environmental consciousness, reminding us to harness the forces of nature for our betterment.
    • Ultimately, it calls for a harmonious relationship with the world around us, fostering strength, adaptability, and respect for nature.
