यज्ञा-यज्ञा वो अग्नये गिरा-गिरा च दाक्षसे |प्र-प्र वयम् अमृतं जातवेदसम प्रियं मित्रं न शंषिषम् ||
yajñā-yajñā vo agnaye girā-girā ca dakṣase | pra-pra vayam amṛtaṁ jātavedasam priyam mitraṁ na śaṁsiṣam ||
  • yajñā-yajñā: This term refers to the concept of sacrifice or offerings. In the Vedic context, yajña is a ritual of offerings made to the deities, symbolizing the connection between humans and the divine.
  • vo: This word means "to you," indicating a direction of reverence and respect towards the divine.
  • agnaye: This refers to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the gods. Fire is a central element in Vedic rituals, representing purity and transformation.
  • girā-girā: This term signifies the power of speech or sound. It emphasizes the importance of mantras and hymns in invoking divine presence and blessings.
  • ca: This means "and," linking the previous concepts together.
  • dakṣase: This word means "to the skilled one," referring to Agni as a deity who is adept in performing the rituals and fulfilling the desires of the devotees.
  • pra-pra: This repetition emphasizes the act of offering and the intention behind it, suggesting a deep commitment to the ritual.
  • vayam: This means "we," indicating the collective participation of the community in the ritual.
  • amṛtaṁ: This term means "immortal" or "nectar," symbolizing the ultimate goal of life, which is to attain immortality or spiritual enlightenment.
  • jātavedasam: This refers to the all-knowing aspect of Agni, who is aware of all offerings and intentions.
  • priyam: This means "beloved" or "dear," indicating the affection and devotion of the devotees towards Agni.
  • mitraṁ: This translates to "friend," highlighting the relationship between humans and the divine, where the gods are seen as allies in the journey of life.
  • na: This means "not," indicating a negation or a protective aspect.
  • śaṁsiṣam: This term means "to be harmed" or "to be troubled," suggesting a plea for protection from negativity or harm.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of Vedic rituals, emphasizing the importance of offerings, the role of Agni as a divine mediator, and the collective intention of the community. It signifies the aspiration for immortality and spiritual connection, while also seeking protection from harm. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal or communal rituals, invoking blessings for health, prosperity, and spiritual growth. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of community, and the pursuit of higher consciousness through devotion and sacrifice.
