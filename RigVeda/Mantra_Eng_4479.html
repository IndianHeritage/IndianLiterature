पावकया यश्चितयन्त्या कृपा क्षामन रुरुच उषसो न भानुना |तूर्वन न यामन्न एतशस्य नू रण आ यो घृणे न तत्रिषाणो अजरोः ||
pāvakayā yaś citayantyā kṛpā kṣāman ruruca uṣaso na bhānunā | tūrvan na yāmann etaśasya nū raṇa ā yo ghṛṇe na tatṛṣāṇo ajaraḥ ||
  • pāvakayā: This word translates to "by the purifying" or "by the sacred." It signifies the essence of purity and the transformative power of fire or light.
  • yaś: Meaning "fame" or "glory," this term emphasizes the importance of reputation and the impact of one's actions on their legacy.
  • citayantyā: This can be interpreted as "those who are aware" or "those who contemplate." It refers to individuals who are mindful and reflective.
  • kṛpā: This word means "compassion" or "grace." It highlights the significance of kindness and empathy in human interactions.
  • kṣāman: Translated as "forgiveness," this term underscores the importance of letting go of grievances and fostering harmony.
  • ruruca: This word can mean "to shine" or "to illuminate," suggesting the idea of enlightenment and clarity.
  • uṣaso: Referring to "dawn" or "morning," it symbolizes new beginnings and the hope that comes with each new day.
  • na bhānunā: This phrase means "without the sun," indicating a state of darkness or ignorance that can be overcome.
  • tūrvan: This word translates to "swift" or "quick," suggesting the urgency of action and the need to respond promptly to life's challenges.
  • na yāmann: Meaning "not to those who," it implies a distinction between those who are aware and those who are not.
  • etaśasya: This term can be interpreted as "of this," referring to the context or situation at hand.
  • nū: This word means "indeed" or "certainly," reinforcing the certainty of the statement being made.
  • raṇa: Translated as "battle" or "struggle," it signifies the challenges and conflicts one faces in life.
  • ā yo: This phrase means "whoever," indicating inclusivity and the universality of the message.
  • ghṛṇe: This word translates to "compassion" or "affection," emphasizing the importance of love and understanding.
  • na tatṛṣāṇo: Meaning "not desiring," it suggests a state of contentment and the avoidance of greed.
  • ajaraḥ: This term means "immortal" or "eternal," indicating the lasting impact of one's actions and virtues.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates profound themes of purity, compassion, forgiveness, and the pursuit of enlightenment. It serves as a reminder of the importance of being mindful and reflective in our actions, fostering kindness and understanding in our relationships, and striving for personal growth and clarity. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as conflict resolution, personal development, and community building. By embodying these virtues, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and enlightened society, ultimately leading to a legacy of goodness and compassion that transcends time.
