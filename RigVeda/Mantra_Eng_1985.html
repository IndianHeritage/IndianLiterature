उपस्तुतिं नमसा उद्यतिं च श्लोकं यं सत्त सवितेव प्र बाहू |अस्य क्रत्वाहन्यो यो अस्ति मृगो न भीमो अरक्षस तुविष्मान ||
upastutiṁ namasa udyatiṁ ca ślokaṁ yaṁsat saviteva pra bāhū | asya kratvāhanyo3 yo asti mṛgo na bhīmo arakṣasas tuviṣmān ||
  • Upastutiṁ: This term refers to praise or worship. It signifies the act of honoring a deity or a higher power through words and actions.
  • Namasa: This word means 'with reverence' or 'with respect.' It emphasizes the importance of approaching the divine with humility and devotion.
  • Udyatiṁ: This can be interpreted as 'rising' or 'elevating.' It suggests a sense of awakening or enlightenment that comes from spiritual practices.
  • Ślokaṁ: A 'śloka' is a verse or hymn, often used in Vedic literature. It represents the structured form of prayer or invocation.
  • Yaṁsat: This term means 'who' or 'that which.' It connects the previous elements to the subject of the mantra.
  • Saviteva: Referring to 'Savita,' the Sun God, this term symbolizes illumination, energy, and life-giving force.
  • Pra bāhū: This phrase translates to 'with outstretched arms.' It signifies openness and readiness to receive blessings or guidance.
  • Asya: Meaning 'of this' or 'of this mantra,' it indicates the focus on the specific invocation being made.
  • Krattvāhanyo: This term can be understood as 'the one who performs sacrifices or rituals.' It highlights the importance of action in spiritual practice.
  • Yo asti: Meaning 'who exists' or 'who is present,' it refers to the divine presence that is being invoked.
  • Mṛgo: This word means 'deer' or 'animal,' often symbolizing gentleness and grace in nature.
  • Na bhīmo: This translates to 'not fearful,' indicating a state of courage and strength in the face of challenges.
  • Arakṣasas: Referring to 'not being protected' or 'without fear,' it suggests a sense of vulnerability that can lead to growth.
  • Tuviṣmān: This term can be interpreted as 'the one who is wise' or 'the knowledgeable one,' emphasizing the importance of wisdom in navigating life.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, courage, and the pursuit of wisdom. It encourages individuals to approach the divine with reverence and to seek enlightenment through spiritual practices. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in moments of prayer, meditation, or any act of seeking guidance and strength. The mantra serves as a reminder that through worship and the pursuit of knowledge, one can rise above fear and vulnerability, embodying grace and wisdom in their journey through life.
