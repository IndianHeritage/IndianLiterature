मां नो अग्नि 'व सृजो अघायाविष्यवे रिपवे दुष्चुनायै |मां दत्वते दशते मांदते नो मां रीषते सहसावन परा दाह ||
mā no agne 'va sṛjo aghāyāviṣyave ripave ducchunāyai | mā datvate daśate mādate no mā rīṣate sahasāvan parā dāḥ ||
  • mā no agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni, may you not harm us." Agni, the fire god, represents energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, this can be seen as a plea for protection from destructive forces, whether they be natural disasters or personal challenges.
  • 'va sṛjo aghāyāviṣyave: This means "may you not create obstacles for us." It emphasizes the desire for a smooth path in life, free from hindrances. For example, in a professional setting, this could relate to seeking clarity and support in achieving career goals without unnecessary barriers.
  • ripave ducchunāyai: This translates to "may we not be harmed by enemies." This can be interpreted as a call for safety and security from those who may wish to do us harm, whether in personal relationships or in broader societal contexts, such as conflicts or competition.
  • mā datvate daśate: This means "may you not strike us down." It reflects a desire for resilience and strength in the face of adversity. In everyday life, this can relate to overcoming challenges, such as health issues or financial difficulties, and seeking the strength to endure.
  • mādate no: This translates to "may you not cause us to suffer." It highlights the importance of compassion and understanding in our interactions with others. In real life, this can be seen in the context of fostering healthy relationships and communities where empathy prevails.
  • mā rīṣate sahasāvan parā dāḥ: This phrase means "may we not be overwhelmed by sudden calamities." It signifies the need for stability and peace in our lives. For instance, in times of crisis, such as natural disasters or personal loss, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek support and maintain hope.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound plea for protection, resilience, and compassion in the face of life's challenges. Its significance lies in its universal applicability, reminding us to seek safety from harm, to foster understanding in our relationships, and to maintain strength during difficult times. In real life, this mantra can be invoked during moments of uncertainty, whether in personal struggles, professional endeavors, or communal challenges, serving as a guiding principle for navigating the complexities of existence.
