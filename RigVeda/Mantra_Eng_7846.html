मन्त्र: परि सुवानो गिरिष्ठाः पवित्रे सोमो अक्षाः |मदेषु सर्वधाऽसि ||
pari suvāno giriṣṭhāḥ pavitre somo akṣāḥ | madeṣu sarvadhā asi ||
  • Mantra: "pari suvāno giriṣṭhāḥ pavitre somo akṣāḥ | madeṣu sarvadhā asi ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pari: around, surrounding
    • suvāno: well-spoken, auspicious
    • giriṣṭhāḥ: the highest, the best among mountains
    • pavitre: pure, sacred
    • somo: the moon, often associated with nectar or soma
    • akṣāḥ: eyes, vision
    • madeṣu: in the intoxication, in the ecstasy
    • sarvadhā: in all ways, in every aspect
    • asi: you are, you exist
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine qualities that surround us, emphasizing purity and auspiciousness.
    • The phrase "pari suvāno" suggests that the environment around us is filled with positive and uplifting energies.
    • "Giriṣṭhāḥ" refers to the highest mountains, symbolizing strength and stability, which can be seen in real life as the support systems we have, such as family and friends.
    • "Pavitre somo akṣāḥ" indicates that the essence of purity and clarity (like the moon) is present in our vision and perception.
    • The term "madeṣu" relates to the state of bliss or ecstasy, which can be experienced during moments of joy, celebration, or spiritual practices.
    • Finally, "sarvadhā asi" reinforces the idea that these qualities are inherent in all aspects of life, suggesting a holistic view of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, the positive energy and support from friends and family can be seen as "pari suvāno," creating an uplifting atmosphere.
    • During a hike in the mountains, one can experience the strength and stability of nature, reflecting the essence of "giriṣṭhāḥ."
    • Practicing mindfulness or meditation can help one connect with the purity of thought and vision, akin to "pavitre somo akṣāḥ."
    • Celebrating festivals or personal milestones often brings about a state of "madeṣu," where joy and ecstasy are shared among loved ones.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of positivity, purity, and holistic existence.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us to recognize and cultivate the auspicious energies around us, fostering a sense of community and connection.
    • In real life, it encourages us to seek joy in our surroundings, appreciate the support systems we have, and maintain a pure perspective in our thoughts and actions.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a guide to living a balanced and fulfilling life, embracing the divine qualities that exist within and around us.
