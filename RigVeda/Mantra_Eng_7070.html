शतं श्वेतास उक्षणो दिवि तारो न रोचन्ते |महना दिवं न तस्तभुः ||
śataṁ śvetāsa ukṣaṇo divi tāro na rocante | mahnā divaṁ na tastabhuḥ ||
  • śataṁ - This word translates to "hundred" or "many." It signifies abundance or a multitude of something.
  • śvetāsa - This term means "white" or "bright," often associated with purity, clarity, and enlightenment.
  • ukṣaṇo - This can be interpreted as "those who are swift" or "those who move quickly," indicating agility or speed.
  • divi - This word means "in the sky" or "heaven," representing the celestial realm or higher consciousness.
  • tāro - This translates to "stars," which can symbolize guidance, aspirations, and the vastness of the universe.
  • na - This is a negation, meaning "not" or "no."
  • rocante - This means "shine" or "illuminate," referring to brightness or radiance.
  • mahnā - This word means "great" or "mighty," indicating something of significant importance or power.
  • divaṁ - This translates to "heaven" or "day," often associated with light, clarity, and divine presence.
  • na - Again, this is a negation.
  • tastabhuḥ - This means "to be established" or "to be firm," indicating stability or permanence.

The mantra can be interpreted as a reflection on the nature of existence and the celestial realm. It suggests that while there are many bright and swift entities in the sky (like stars), they do not shine as brightly as the great and established divine presence. This can be seen as a metaphor for the pursuit of higher knowledge and understanding, where the ultimate truth or enlightenment is more significant than the many distractions or lesser lights that may exist in our lives.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the stars may shine, they can also distract us from our true purpose. Focusing on what is truly important (the "great" or "divine") can lead to personal enlightenment.
  • Decision Making: In a world filled with choices (the many stars), it is essential to seek clarity and guidance from a higher source or principle to make the right decisions.
  • Spiritual Practice: The mantra encourages individuals to look beyond the superficial and seek deeper spiritual truths, emphasizing the importance of meditation and reflection.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder to prioritize the pursuit of higher truths and values over the distractions of the material world. It encourages individuals to seek clarity and stability in their lives, ultimately leading to a more profound understanding of existence and one's place within it.
