ऋभुर ऋभुभिर अभि वः स्याम विभ्वो विभुभिः शवसां शवांसी |वाजो अस्मां अवतु वाजसाताव इन्द्रेण युजा तरुषेम वृतम् ||
ṛbhur ṛbhubhir abhi vaḥ syāma vibhvo vibhubhiḥ śavasā śavāṁsi | vājo asmām̐ avatu vājasātāv indreṇa yujā taruṣema vṛtram ||
  • ṛbhur ṛbhubhir abhi vaḥ syāma: This phrase can be translated to "May we be endowed with the qualities of the righteous." Here, "ṛbhur" refers to the righteous or the virtuous, and "syāma" means "may we become." This emphasizes the importance of embodying virtuous qualities in our lives.
  • vibhvo vibhubhiḥ śavasā śavāṁsi: This part translates to "With the strength of the powerful, may we overcome obstacles." "Vibhvo" refers to the powerful or mighty, and "śavasā" means strength. This suggests that we should seek strength from those who are powerful to help us in our endeavors.
  • vājo asmām̐ avatu vājasātāv: This translates to "May the energy protect us." "Vājo" refers to energy or vitality, and "avatu" means to protect. This highlights the need for protection and support from higher energies or forces in our lives.
  • indreṇa yujā taruṣema vṛtram: This can be interpreted as "With the help of Indra, may we conquer our challenges." "Indra" is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and power, while "vṛtram" refers to obstacles or challenges. This signifies the importance of divine assistance in overcoming life's hurdles.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of righteousness, strength, protection, and divine assistance in our lives. It encourages individuals to embody virtuous qualities, seek strength from powerful allies, and rely on higher energies for protection. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during challenging times when one seeks guidance and support, or in moments of decision-making where moral integrity is essential. By invoking these principles, individuals can navigate life's complexities with resilience and purpose.
