तत् सूर्यस्य देवत्वं तन् महित्वं मध्याः कर्तोर् विततं सं जभारा |यदेद् अयुक्त हरितः सदस्थाद् आद् रात्रि वासस तनुते सिमस्मै ||
tat sūryasya devatvaṁ tan mahitvam madhyā kartor vitataṁ saṁ jabhāra | yaded ayukta haritaḥ sadhasthād ād rātrī vāsas tanute simasmai ||
  • tat sūryasya devatvaṁ: This phrase translates to "that divine nature of the Sun." The Sun is often regarded as a symbol of life, energy, and illumination in various cultures. In real life, we can see the Sun's importance in agriculture, as it provides the necessary light and warmth for crops to grow.
  • tan mahitvam: This means "its greatness." The greatness of the Sun can be observed in its role in sustaining life on Earth. For example, without the Sun, photosynthesis would not occur, leading to a collapse of the food chain.
  • madhyā kartor vitataṁ: This translates to "the expanse of the middle of the doers." This can be interpreted as the central role that the Sun plays in the universe, influencing the actions of all living beings. In our daily lives, we often plan our activities around the Sun's position, such as waking up with the sunrise and resting at sunset.
  • saṁ jabhāra: This means "has spread." The Sun's rays spread across the Earth, providing light and warmth. A real-life example is how sunlight can affect our mood; many people feel happier and more energetic on sunny days.
  • yaded ayukta haritaḥ: This translates to "that which is not bound by the green." This can refer to the Sun's ability to shine over all landscapes, regardless of their fertility. For instance, even in deserts, the Sun's rays penetrate and can be harnessed for solar energy, showcasing its omnipresence.
  • sadhasthād: This means "from the assembly." This can symbolize the gathering of energies and forces that the Sun influences. In a community context, the Sun can be seen as a unifying force, bringing people together for activities like festivals that celebrate harvests and nature.
  • ād rātrī vāsas: This translates to "the abode of the night." It signifies the balance between day and night, where the Sun's absence gives way to the night. In real life, this balance is crucial for various ecosystems, as many animals are nocturnal and rely on the darkness for their survival.
  • tanute simasmai: This means "may it shine upon us." This is a prayer for the Sun's blessings. In practical terms, this can be seen in rituals and practices where people seek the Sun's energy for health, prosperity, and well-being, such as yoga practices done in the morning sunlight.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of the Sun as a divine entity that embodies greatness and sustains life. Its rays symbolize energy, unity, and balance, which are essential in our daily lives. By recognizing the Sun's role, we can appreciate the interconnectedness of nature and our dependence on it for survival and well-being. This mantra can be applied in real-life scenarios such as agricultural practices, community gatherings, and personal wellness routines, reminding us to honor and harness the energy of the Sun in our lives.
