इमे हि ते कारवो वावशुर धियाऽ विप्रासो मेधसातये |स तवं नो मगवन्न इन्द्र गिरवणो वेनो न श्रिणुधी हवम् ||
ime hi te kāravo vāvaśur dhiyā viprāso medhasātaye | sa tvaṁ no maghavann indra girvaṇo veno na śṛṇudhī havam ||
  • ime hi te kāravo: This phrase translates to "These are indeed your actions." It emphasizes the importance of one's deeds and contributions. In real life, this can be related to the idea that our actions define us, much like how a person's reputation is built on their behavior and choices.
  • vāvaśur dhiyā viprāso: This means "may the wise ones, with their intellect, be favorable." It highlights the value of wisdom and intellect in guiding our actions. For example, in a workplace, decisions made with careful thought and insight often lead to better outcomes than impulsive choices.
  • medhasātaye: This translates to "for the sake of intelligence." It suggests that the pursuit of knowledge and understanding is paramount. In modern education, this can be seen in the emphasis on critical thinking and problem-solving skills, which are essential for personal and professional growth.
  • sa tvaṁ no maghavann indra: This phrase means "may you, O Indra, be our benefactor." Indra is often seen as a deity of strength and power. In real life, this can be interpreted as seeking support from those in positions of authority or influence, such as mentors or leaders who can guide us in our endeavors.
  • girvaṇo veno na śṛṇudhī havam: This translates to "may you hear our call, O Indra." It signifies the importance of communication and being heard. In everyday life, this can relate to the need for individuals to express their needs and aspirations clearly, whether in personal relationships or professional settings.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of actions, wisdom, and communication in achieving success and support in life. It teaches us that our deeds define us, and seeking knowledge and guidance from wise individuals can lead to favorable outcomes. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in education, professional environments, and personal relationships, where the interplay of action, intellect, and communication is crucial for growth and success.
