मन्त्र: निष्कं वा घा कृत्नवते स्रजं वा दुधितर दिवः | त्रिते दुष्ष्वप्न्यं सर्वम् आप्त्ये परि दद्मस्य अनहसो वा ऊतयः सुतयः वा ऊतयः ||
niṣkaṁ vā ghā kṛṇavate srajaṁ vā duhitar divaḥ | trite duṣṣvapnyaṁ sarvam āptye pari dadmasy anehaso va ūtayaḥ suūtayo va ūtayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • niṣkaṁ: without a price, free from attachment
    • vā: or
    • ghā: to give, to bestow
    • kṛṇavate: those who perform actions, doers
    • srajaṁ: garland, symbol of beauty and honor
    • vā: or
    • duhitar: daughter
    • divaḥ: of the heavens, celestial
    • trite: in the third
    • duṣṣvapnyaṁ: difficult to attain, hard to achieve
    • sarvam: all, everything
    • āptye: in fulfillment, in attainment
    • pari: around, in all directions
    • dadmasy: we give, we bestow
    • anehaso: without darkness, in brightness
    • va: or
    • ūtayaḥ: those who rise, those who elevate
    • suūtayo: those who are well-versed, knowledgeable
    • va: or
    • ūtayaḥ: those who rise, those who elevate
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the act of giving and the nature of fulfillment.
    • It emphasizes the importance of selflessness and the idea that true gifts are given without attachment or expectation of return.
    • The mention of "garland" and "daughter of the heavens" symbolizes beauty, honor, and the divine nature of relationships.
    • The phrase "difficult to attain" suggests that the most valuable things in life often require effort and dedication to achieve.
    • In a broader sense, it reflects the idea that knowledge and enlightenment come to those who are willing to rise above ignorance and strive for understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a mentor who shares knowledge and skills with a mentee without expecting anything in return embodies the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal relationships, parents who nurture their children selflessly, providing them with love and guidance, reflect the idea of giving without attachment.
    • Community service, where individuals volunteer their time and resources to help others, illustrates the principle of selfless giving and the pursuit of collective well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of selfless giving and the pursuit of knowledge and fulfillment.
    • It teaches us that true value lies in our actions and intentions, rather than in material possessions or recognition.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger relationships, personal growth, and a more compassionate society.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to rise above our limitations and strive for a higher understanding of ourselves and our connections with others.
