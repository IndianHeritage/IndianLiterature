प्र वाता वान्ति पतयन्ति विद्युत उद ओषधीर जिहते पिन्वते स्वः |इरा विश्वस्मै bhuvanāya जायते यत पार्जन्यः पृथिवीं रेतसावति ||
pra vātā vānti patayanti vidyuta ud oṣadhīr jihate pinvate svaḥ | irā viśvasmai bhuvanāya jāyate yat parjanyaḥ pṛthivīṁ retasāvati ||
  • Translation: "The winds blow, the lightning strikes, and the herbs grow; they all strive for the sky. The atmosphere gives birth to all beings, and the rain, which is the essence of the earth, nourishes it."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra vātā vānti: The winds blow
    • patayanti: They strike or move
    • vidyuta: Lightning
    • ud: Upwards or towards the sky
    • oṣadhīr: Herbs or plants
    • jihate: Strive or aspire
    • pinvate: To nourish or sustain
    • svaḥ: The sky or heaven
    • irā: The atmosphere
    • viśvasmai: To all beings
    • bhuvanāya: For the creation or birth
    • jāyate: Is born or comes into existence
    • yat: That which
    • parjanyaḥ: Rain or the rain-giver
    • pṛthivīṁ: The earth
    • retasāvati: Essence or vitality
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of natural elements and their roles in sustaining life.
    • The winds and lightning symbolize the dynamic forces of nature that contribute to the growth of plants, which are essential for life.
    • The phrase "strive for the sky" indicates the aspiration of all living beings to reach their highest potential.
    • The atmosphere is depicted as a nurturing force that gives birth to all beings, emphasizing the importance of a healthy environment.
    • Rain is portrayed as the essence of the earth, signifying its critical role in agriculture and the sustenance of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The importance of wind in pollination and seed dispersal, which are vital for plant reproduction.
    • Lightning's role in nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil and supports plant growth.
    • The reliance on rain for agriculture, highlighting the need for sustainable water management practices.
    • The aspiration of individuals and communities to grow and thrive, akin to plants reaching for sunlight.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the delicate balance in nature and the interdependence of all elements.
    • It encourages us to respect and nurture our environment, recognizing that our well-being is tied to the health of the earth.
    • In real life, it is applicable in discussions about environmental conservation, sustainable agriculture, and the importance of clean air and water.
    • Ultimately, it inspires a holistic view of life, urging us to strive for harmony with nature and within ourselves.
