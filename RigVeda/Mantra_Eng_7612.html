त्वं हि शाश्वतीनां इन्द्र दर्ता पुराम् असि |हन्ता दस्योः मनोः वृद्धः पतिर्म दिवः ||
tvaṁ hi śaśvatīnām indra dartā purām asi | hantā dasyor manor vṛdhaḥ patir divaḥ ||
  • tvaṁ hi - "You indeed" - This phrase emphasizes the importance of the subject, indicating that the focus is on a divine entity, in this case, Indra.
  • śaśvatīnām - "of the eternal ones" - This refers to beings or entities that are everlasting, highlighting the timeless nature of the divine.
  • indra - "Indra" - The king of the gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength, power, and authority.
  • dartā - "the one who strikes" - This term signifies action, particularly in the context of overcoming obstacles or enemies.
  • purām - "the city" or "the realm" - This can refer to a physical place or a metaphorical space where life and challenges occur.
  • asi - "you are" - A declaration of existence and presence, reinforcing the role of Indra in the lives of beings.
  • hantā - "the slayer" - This term indicates the power to destroy or remove threats, symbolizing protection and strength.
  • dasyor - "of the enemies" - Refers to those who oppose or challenge, representing obstacles in life.
  • manor - "of the mind" - This signifies the mental aspect, indicating that the challenges faced are often psychological or emotional.
  • vṛdhaḥ - "the one who grows" - This suggests development and progress, indicating that overcoming challenges leads to growth.
  • patir - "the lord" - This term denotes authority and leadership, reinforcing Indra's role as a protector and guide.
  • divaḥ - "of the heavens" - This signifies a higher realm, indicating that the divine influence extends beyond the earthly plane.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine strength and protection, emphasizing the role of Indra as a powerful force against adversities. In real life, this can be interpreted as a call for inner strength and resilience when facing challenges. Just as Indra is depicted as a protector who overcomes enemies, individuals can draw inspiration from this mantra to confront their own struggles, whether they be personal, professional, or emotional. The significance lies in recognizing that divine support is available, encouraging individuals to seek strength within themselves and to trust in a higher power during difficult times.
