अत्रा वि नेमिर एषां उरां न धूनुते वृकः |दिवो अमुष्य शासतो दिवं यया दिवावसो ||
atrā vi nemir eṣām urāṁ na dhūnute vṛkaḥ | divo amuṣya śāsato divaṁ yaya divāvaso ||
  • atrā: This word translates to "here" or "in this place." It signifies the importance of the present moment and the environment we are in.
  • vi nemir: "vi" means "apart" or "away," and "nemir" refers to "the path" or "the way." Together, they suggest a divergence from the usual path or a search for a new direction.
  • eṣām: This means "of these" or "of those," indicating a connection to a specific group or context.
  • urāṁ: This word translates to "the strong" or "the powerful," often referring to strength or might.
  • na dhūnute: "na" means "not," and "dhūnute" means "to shake" or "to disturb." This phrase implies stability and the idea that true strength does not waver easily.
  • vṛkaḥ: This translates to "wolf," a symbol of cunning and strength in many cultures. It can represent challenges or adversaries that one must face.
  • divo: Meaning "of the sky" or "heaven," this word connects the mantra to higher realms or divine aspects.
  • amuṣya: This means "of that" or "of this," indicating a specific reference to something significant.
  • śāsato: This translates to "of the ruler" or "of the one who governs," suggesting authority and control.
  • divaṁ: Meaning "heaven" or "the divine," it emphasizes the connection to higher powers or spiritual realms.
  • yaya: This word means "by which" or "through which," indicating a means or method.
  • divāvaso: This translates to "the one who dwells in the day" or "the daytime," symbolizing clarity, awareness, and enlightenment.

Conclusion: The mantra speaks to the importance of finding strength and stability in the present moment while navigating challenges. It emphasizes the need to seek new paths and maintain composure in the face of adversity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal challenges, professional obstacles, or even societal issues. By remaining grounded and focused, one can overcome difficulties and achieve clarity and enlightenment. This mantra serves as a reminder to harness inner strength and seek guidance from higher powers, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and purposeful life.
