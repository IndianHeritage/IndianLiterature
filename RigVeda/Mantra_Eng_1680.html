मन्त्र: दिवा यान्ति मरुतो भूम्याग्निर अयं वातो अन्तरिक्षेण याती | अद्भिर याती वरुणः समुद्रिर युष्मां इच्छन्तः शवसो नपातः ||
divā yānti maruto bhūmyāgnir ayaṁ vāto antarikṣeṇa yāti | adbhir yāti varuṇaḥ samudrair yuṣmām̐ icchantaḥ śavaso napātaḥ ||
  • Translation: "The Maruts (storm deities) travel through the sky; the Earth and Fire are their companions. The Wind moves through the atmosphere, and Varuna (the god of cosmic order) travels with the waters of the oceans, seeking you, O mortals, who are the offspring of the dead."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • divā: in the sky
    • yānti: they go or travel
    • maruto: the Maruts, storm deities
    • bhūmyā: of the Earth
    • agnir: Fire
    • ayaṁ: this
    • vāto: Wind
    • antarikṣeṇa: through the atmosphere
    • yāti: goes or travels
    • adbhir: with waters
    • yāti: travels
    • varuṇaḥ: Varuna, the god of cosmic order
    • samudrair: with the oceans
    • yuṣmām̐: you (plural)
    • icchantaḥ: seeking or desiring
    • śavaso: of the dead
    • napātaḥ: offspring or descendants
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of natural elements and divine forces. The Maruts represent the dynamic aspects of nature, while Varuna symbolizes the moral and cosmic order.
    • It emphasizes the importance of recognizing our place within the larger cosmos and the forces that govern it.
    • In real life, this can be seen in how we rely on natural elements like wind, water, and fire for survival and how they are essential for agriculture, energy, and climate.
    • It also serves as a reminder of our responsibilities towards nature and the need to maintain harmony with the environment.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In environmental conservation, this mantra can inspire actions to protect natural resources and promote sustainability.
    • In personal life, it encourages mindfulness of our actions and their impact on the world around us, fostering a sense of responsibility towards future generations.
    • In spiritual practices, it can be a reminder to seek balance and harmony in our lives, aligning ourselves with the natural rhythms of the universe.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a profound reminder of the interconnectedness of all life and the divine forces that guide us. It encourages us to live in harmony with nature and to recognize our role as stewards of the Earth.
    • By understanding and applying the teachings of this mantra, we can cultivate a deeper appreciation for the natural world and our place within it, leading to a more sustainable and fulfilling life.
