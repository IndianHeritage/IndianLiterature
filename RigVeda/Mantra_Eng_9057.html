सं यद वयं यवसादो जनानाम अहं यवाद उर्वज्रे अन्तः |अत्र युक्तो 'वसातारम इच्छाद अथो अयुक्तं युनजद ववन्वान ||
saṁ yad vayaṁ yavasādo janānām ahaṁ yavāda urvajre antaḥ | atrā yukto 'vasātāram icchād atho ayuktaṁ yunajad vavanvān ||
  • saṁ yad vayaṁ yavasādo janānām: This phrase can be translated as "Whatever we are, we are the essence of the people." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of individuals within a community. For example, in a workplace, the success of a project often relies on the collaboration and contributions of all team members, highlighting the importance of unity.
  • ahaṁ yavāda urvajre antaḥ: This translates to "I am the one who is within the great strength." It suggests that each individual possesses an inner strength that contributes to the greater good. In real life, this can be seen in athletes who draw on their inner resilience to overcome challenges, inspiring others in the process.
  • atrā yukto 'vasātāram icchād: This means "Here, the one who is connected desires." It indicates that those who are connected to their community or environment have aspirations and desires that align with the collective. For instance, a community leader who seeks to improve local education reflects this connection and desire for collective progress.
  • atho ayuktaṁ yunajad vavanvān: This translates to "Otherwise, the unconnected one is left behind." It serves as a warning that those who do not engage with their community or environment may miss out on opportunities and support. In modern society, individuals who isolate themselves from social networks may find it challenging to succeed, as collaboration often leads to greater achievements.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of community, inner strength, and the importance of connection. It teaches us that our individual strengths contribute to the collective, and that being part of a community can enhance our aspirations and opportunities. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as teamwork in professional settings, community service, and personal relationships. By fostering connections and recognizing our shared strengths, we can create a more supportive and successful environment for everyone.
