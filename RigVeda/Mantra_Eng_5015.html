सरस्वत्यभिनो नेषि वस्यो माप स्फरीः पयसा मा न आ धक् |जुषस्व नः सख्या वेश्याऽ च मा त्वत क्षेत्राण्य अरणानि गन्म ||
sarasvaty abhi no neṣi vasyo māpa spharīḥ payasā mā na ā dhak | juṣasva naḥ sakhyā veśyā ca mā tvat kṣetrāṇy araṇāni ganma ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Sarasvaty: Referring to Saraswati, the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning.
    • Abhi: To us or for us.
    • No: Our.
    • Neṣi: Lead or guide.
    • Vasya: Under control or mastery.
    • Māpa: May not.
    • Spharīḥ: Be disturbed or agitated.
    • Payasā: With nourishment or sustenance.
    • Mā: Not.
    • Na: Not.
    • Ādhak: To be hindered or obstructed.
    • Juṣasva: May you enjoy or be pleased.
    • Naḥ: Our.
    • Sakhyā: Friendship or companionship.
    • Veśyā: The abode or place.
    • Ca: And.
    • Mā: Not.
    • Tvat: Your.
    • Kṣetrāṇy: Fields or domains.
    • Araṇāni: The boundaries or limits.
    • Ganma: We go or we approach.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the goddess Saraswati, asking her to guide and lead us towards knowledge and wisdom.
    • It emphasizes the importance of maintaining control over our thoughts and actions, ensuring that we are not disturbed or hindered in our pursuits.
    • The plea for enjoyment in friendship and companionship highlights the significance of social bonds and support in our journey of learning and growth.
    • It also expresses a desire to approach the fields of knowledge and wisdom without obstacles, seeking a clear path to understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a student's life, this mantra can be a reminder to seek knowledge with a clear mind, free from distractions, and to value the support of friends in academic pursuits.
    • In a professional setting, it can serve as a motivation to collaborate with colleagues, fostering a supportive environment that encourages learning and innovation.
    • In personal development, it can inspire individuals to seek wisdom and understanding while maintaining focus and control over their thoughts and actions.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for guidance from Saraswati, the embodiment of knowledge and wisdom.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from education to professional growth, emphasizing the importance of companionship and a clear path in the pursuit of knowledge.
    • By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset that values learning, collaboration, and the mastery of one's thoughts and actions, ultimately leading to personal and collective growth.
