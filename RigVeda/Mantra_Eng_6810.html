अग्निर्देवेषु संवासुः स विक्षु यज्ञियास्वा | स मुदा काव्यां पुरु विश्वं भूमेव पुष्यति देवो देवेषु यज्ञियो नभन्तामन्यके समे ||
agnir deveṣu saṁvasuḥ sa vikṣu yajñiyāsv ā | sa mudā kāvyā puru viśvam bhūmeva puṣyati devo deveṣu yajñiyo nabhantām anyake same ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Agnir: Fire, often symbolizing energy and transformation.
    • Deveṣu: Among the gods or divine beings.
    • Saṁvasuḥ: One who resides or dwells.
    • Sa: He or that.
    • Vikṣu: The one who is to be worshipped or revered.
    • Yajñiyāsv: In the context of sacrifice or offerings.
    • Ā: A particle indicating emphasis or affirmation.
    • Sa mudā: He with joy or happiness.
    • Kāvyā: Poetic or artistic expression.
    • Puru: Many or all.
    • Viśvam: The universe or all that exists.
    • Bhūmeva: Like the earth or as the earth.
    • Puṣyati: Nourishes or sustains.
    • Devo: The divine or god.
    • Deveṣu: Among the gods.
    • Yajñiyo: Related to sacrifice or offerings.
    • Nabhantām: The heavens or the sky.
    • Anyake: Other beings or entities.
    • Same: In the same manner or equally.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of Agni (fire) as a divine presence that dwells among the gods.
    • Agni is revered as a source of energy and transformation, essential for rituals and sacrifices.
    • The mantra emphasizes the joy and nourishment that Agni provides to all beings, akin to how the earth sustains life.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of the divine and the earthly, suggesting that offerings made to the divine are also beneficial to the world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, lighting a fire during ceremonies symbolizes the presence of Agni, representing purification and transformation.
    • Just as Agni nourishes life, we can see parallels in how our actions and contributions to society can uplift and sustain our communities.
    • Environmental sustainability can be viewed through the lens of this mantra, where nurturing the earth is akin to honoring the divine presence in nature.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the divine in our everyday lives and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It teaches us to approach life with joy and gratitude, understanding that our actions can have a profound impact on the world around us.
    • In a practical sense, it encourages us to engage in acts of kindness, sustainability, and reverence for nature, reflecting the essence of Agni in our lives.
