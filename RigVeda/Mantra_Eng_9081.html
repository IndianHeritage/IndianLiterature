शशः क्षुरं प्रत्यञ्चं जगाराद्रिं लोगेन व्य अभेदम् आरात् |बृहन्तं चिद ऋहते रन्धयानि वयद वत्सो वृषभं शूशुवानः ||
śaśaḥ kṣuram pratyañcaṁ jagārādriṁ logena vy abhedam ārāt | bṛhantaṁ cid ṛhate randhayāni vayad vatso vṛṣabhaṁ śūśuvānaḥ ||
  • śaśaḥ: This word refers to the "hare" or "rabbit," symbolizing agility and the ability to adapt to various situations.
  • kṣuram: Translated as "the sharp" or "the cutting," it can represent clarity of thought or the ability to discern truth from falsehood.
  • pratyañcaṁ: This term means "the opposite" or "the contrary," indicating the duality present in life and the need to understand both sides of a situation.
  • jagārādriṁ: This can be interpreted as "the awakened mountain," symbolizing stability and strength, akin to a solid foundation in life.
  • logena: Meaning "by the people" or "in the world," it emphasizes the importance of community and social interaction.
  • vy abhedam: This phrase translates to "without division," suggesting unity and harmony among individuals.
  • ārāt: This word can mean "from the enemy" or "from opposition," indicating the challenges one faces in life.
  • bṛhantaṁ: Meaning "the great" or "the vast," it refers to the expansive nature of existence and the universe.
  • cid: This term means "consciousness" or "awareness," highlighting the importance of being aware of one's surroundings and inner self.
  • ṛhate: Translated as "to hold" or "to support," it signifies the role of support systems in our lives.
  • randhayāni: This word means "to bind" or "to connect," indicating the relationships and bonds we form with others.
  • vayad: Meaning "of age" or "of time," it reflects the passage of time and its impact on our lives.
  • vatso: This term translates to "calf" or "young one," symbolizing innocence and the nurturing aspect of life.
  • vṛṣabhaṁ: Meaning "the bull," it represents strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • śūśuvānaḥ: This word can be interpreted as "the one who has given birth," symbolizing creation and the cycle of life.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of adaptability, clarity, unity, and strength in the face of challenges. It teaches us to be aware of our surroundings and the relationships we form, highlighting the interconnectedness of all beings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in community building, conflict resolution, and personal growth. By understanding the dualities of life and fostering unity, we can create a harmonious environment that supports both individual and collective well-being.
