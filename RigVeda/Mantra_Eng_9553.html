वनस्पते रशनया नियूया देवानाम् पाथ उप वक्षि विद्वान् |स्वदाति देवः कृणवद् धवींष्य अवतां द्यावापृथिवी हवम् मे ||
vanaspate raśanayā niyūyā devānām pātha upa vakṣi vidvān | svadāti devaḥ kṛṇavad dhavīṁṣy avatāṁ dyāvāpṛthivī havam me ||
  • vanaspate: This term refers to the "plant" or "vegetation." In a broader sense, it symbolizes nature and the life force that sustains all living beings.
  • raśanayā: This word means "by the thread" or "by the connection." It signifies the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe.
  • niyūyā: This translates to "by the guidance" or "by the direction." It implies that there is a divine guidance in the natural order of things.
  • devānām: This means "of the gods." It indicates the divine presence and influence in the world.
  • pātha: This word means "path" or "way." It suggests a journey or a method of achieving something.
  • upa vakṣi: This translates to "I will speak" or "I will declare." It indicates the act of sharing knowledge or wisdom.
  • vidvān: This means "the wise" or "the knowledgeable." It refers to those who possess understanding and insight.
  • svadāti: This term means "to enjoy" or "to relish." It signifies the appreciation of the divine or the blessings of life.
  • devaḥ: This translates to "the god" or "the divine being." It represents the higher powers that govern existence.
  • kṛṇavad: This means "to create" or "to make." It signifies the act of bringing something into existence.
  • dhavīṁṣy: This term refers to "the offerings" or "the sacrifices." It indicates the importance of giving back to the divine.
  • avatāṁ: This translates to "to descend" or "to come down." It signifies the divine presence manifesting in the material world.
  • dyāvāpṛthivī: This phrase means "heaven and earth." It represents the duality of existence and the balance between the spiritual and the physical realms.
  • havam me: This translates to "my offering" or "my invocation." It signifies the personal connection and devotion to the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, the divine guidance present in our lives, and the importance of knowledge and wisdom. It calls upon the divine to bless our offerings and acknowledges the relationship between heaven and earth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in practices such as environmental conservation, where we recognize the importance of nature and our responsibility towards it. It also highlights the significance of seeking wisdom and guidance in our actions, fostering a sense of community and respect for the divine forces that shape our existence. By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a deeper appreciation for life, nature, and the divine, leading to a more harmonious existence.
