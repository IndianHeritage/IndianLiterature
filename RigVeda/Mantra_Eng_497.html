मां वो घ्नन्तं मां शपन्तं प्रति वोच देवयन्तं |सुम्नैर इद व आ विवासे ||
mā vo ghnantam mā śapantam prati voce devayantam | sumnair id va ā vivāse ||
  • mā vo ghnantam: This phrase translates to "May they not harm you." It emphasizes the importance of protection and safety in our lives. In real life, this can be related to the idea of safeguarding oneself from negative influences, whether they be physical, emotional, or spiritual.
  • mā śapantam: This means "May they not curse you." Cursing here can be understood as wishing ill upon someone. In everyday life, this can be seen in the context of maintaining positive relationships and avoiding conflicts that could lead to harmful words or actions.
  • prati voce devayantam: This translates to "In response to the divine." It suggests that our actions and words should align with a higher purpose or divine will. In practical terms, this can be related to ethical living and making choices that reflect our values and beliefs.
  • sumnair id va ā vivāse: This phrase means "May you dwell in auspiciousness." It highlights the desire for a life filled with good fortune and positivity. In real life, this can be connected to the pursuit of happiness and well-being, as well as creating a supportive environment for oneself and others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound wish for protection, positivity, and alignment with divine principles. Its significance lies in the universal desire for safety and well-being, which is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as fostering harmonious relationships, making ethical decisions, and creating a nurturing environment. By embodying the essence of this mantra, individuals can strive for a life that is not only free from harm but also rich in positivity and purpose.
