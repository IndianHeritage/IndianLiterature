यो मे धेनूनां शतं वैददश्विर यथा ददात् |तरन्त इव मंहनाः ||
yo me dhenūnāṁ śataṁ vaidadaśvir yathā dadat | taranta iva maṁhanā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • me: to me
    • dhenūnāṁ: of the cows
    • śataṁ: a hundred
    • vaidadaśvir: endowed with knowledge
    • yathā: as
    • dadat: they give
    • taranta: swiftly
    • iva: like
    • maṁhanā: the strong
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the blessings and abundance that come from the divine, likening them to the gifts provided by a hundred cows.
    • The term "dhenūnāṁ" refers to cows, which in Vedic culture symbolize wealth, nourishment, and prosperity.
    • The phrase "vaidadaśvir" suggests that these blessings are not just material but also imbued with wisdom and knowledge.
    • The comparison to "taranta iva maṁhanā" indicates that these blessings come swiftly and powerfully, much like the strength of a mighty being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, a farmer may pray this mantra for a bountiful harvest, symbolizing the cows as a metaphor for the fruits of their labor.
    • In a business context, an entrepreneur might recite this mantra to attract prosperity and wise decision-making, akin to receiving the gifts of knowledge and resources.
    • In personal life, individuals may use this mantra to seek guidance and strength in their endeavors, hoping for swift success and support from the universe.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine blessings for abundance and wisdom in various aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder that true wealth encompasses not just material possessions but also knowledge and strength.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in prayers for prosperity, guidance in decision-making, and the pursuit of personal and professional goals.
