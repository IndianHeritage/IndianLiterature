रोहिच छ्यावा सुमदंशुर ललामीर द्युक्षा राया ऋज्राश्वस्य |वृषण्वन्तं बिभ्रती धूर्षु रथम मन्द्रा चिकेत नाहुषीषु विक्षु ||
rohic chyāvā sumadaṁśur lalāmīr dyukṣā rāya ṛjrāśvasya | vṛṣaṇvantam bibhratī dhūrṣu ratham mandrā ciketa nāhuṣīṣu vikṣu ||
  • Rohic Chyāvā: This phrase can be interpreted as "the radiant one" or "the shining one." In real life, this can symbolize the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, akin to how the sun illuminates the world.
  • Sumadaṁśur: This term refers to "the one with good thoughts" or "the wise one." It emphasizes the importance of positive thinking and wisdom in our daily lives, similar to how a wise mentor guides us through challenges.
  • Lalāmīr: This word can be understood as "the one who brings prosperity." In practical terms, it can relate to individuals or actions that lead to abundance, such as a successful business venture or a fruitful relationship.
  • Dyukṣā: This can be interpreted as "the one who is powerful" or "the one who achieves." It reflects the idea of striving for success and overcoming obstacles, much like an athlete training hard to win a competition.
  • Rāya ṛjrāśvasya: This phrase translates to "the one who is a ruler or leader." It signifies the qualities of leadership and governance, akin to a community leader who inspires and guides others towards a common goal.
  • Vṛṣaṇvantam: This term means "the one who possesses strength." It highlights the importance of resilience and determination in facing life's challenges, similar to how a tree stands firm against strong winds.
  • Bibhratī: This word translates to "the one who carries" or "the one who bears." It can symbolize the responsibilities we take on in life, such as a parent nurturing their child or a leader supporting their team.
  • Dhūrṣu: This can be interpreted as "the one who is swift" or "the one who moves quickly." It reflects the need for agility and adaptability in our fast-paced world, much like a business that quickly pivots in response to market changes.
  • Ratham Mandrā: This phrase refers to "the chariot of the wise." It symbolizes the journey of life guided by wisdom, akin to how a well-planned road trip leads to a fulfilling destination.
  • Ciketa: This term means "to understand" or "to perceive." It emphasizes the importance of awareness and insight in decision-making, similar to how a detective pieces together clues to solve a mystery.
  • Nāhuṣīṣu Vikṣu: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who is vigilant among the wise." It signifies the value of being alert and attentive in our pursuits, much like a student who actively engages in learning rather than passively absorbing information.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, strength, leadership, and the pursuit of knowledge. Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to embody these qualities in their daily lives. By striving for enlightenment, maintaining positive thoughts, and being resilient, we can navigate the complexities of life more effectively. This mantra serves as a reminder that wisdom and strength are essential in both personal and communal contexts, guiding us towards a prosperous and fulfilling existence.
