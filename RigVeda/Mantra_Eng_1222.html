याभी रसां क्षोदसोद्नः पिपिवथुर अनश्वं याभी रथम आवतं जिषे |याभिस त्रिशोक उष्रियā उदाजत ताभिर ūषु ऊतिभिर अश्विनā गतम् ||
yābhī rasāṁ kṣodasodnaḥ pipinvathur anaśvaṁ yābhī ratham āvataṁ jiṣe | yābhis triśoka usriyā udājata tābhir ū ṣu ūtibhir aśvinā gatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yābhī: By whom
    • rasāṁ: The essence or juice
    • kṣodasodnaḥ: Of the one who is nourished
    • pipinvathur: Who is filled or satisfied
    • anaśvaṁ: Without a horse (symbolizing speed or power)
    • yābhī: By whom
    • ratham: The chariot
    • āvataṁ: That is protected or sheltered
    • jiṣe: Victorious or triumphant
    • yābhis: By whom
    • triśoka: The three sorrows or pains
    • usriyā: With the help or support
    • udājata: Has arisen or emerged
    • tābhir: By those
    • ū: O
    • ṣu: Good or auspicious
    • ūtibhir: With strength or vigor
    • aśvinā: By the Ashvins (the twin deities)
    • gatam: Gone or departed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine forces that nourish and protect us.
    • The "essence" or "juice" symbolizes the vital energy or resources that sustain life.
    • The mention of "without a horse" can be interpreted as a state of being that is not reliant on conventional means of power or speed, suggesting a deeper, spiritual form of strength.
    • The "chariot" represents the journey of life, which is safeguarded by divine intervention.
    • The "three sorrows" may refer to the challenges and pains we face in life, which can be alleviated through divine support.
    • The "Ashvins" are known as the divine physicians, symbolizing healing and restoration.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek spiritual guidance or support, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • People may find strength in community or faith during difficult times, akin to the protection offered by the divine chariot.
    • In healthcare, the role of doctors and caregivers can be likened to the Ashvins, who provide healing and comfort to those in need.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine support and nourishment in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our struggles and that there are forces at work that protect and guide us.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek help, whether through spiritual means, community support, or professional assistance, to navigate through difficulties.
