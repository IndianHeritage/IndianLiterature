यदि स्तुतस्य मरुतो अधीथेत्था विप्रस्य वाजिनो हविमान् |मक्षू रायः सुवीर्यस्य दाता नू चिद यं अन्य आदभद अरावाः ||
yadi stutasya maruto adhīthetthā viprasya vājino havīman | makṣū rāyaḥ suvīryasya dāta nū cid yam anya ādabhad arāvā ||
  • yadi stutasya maruto adhīthetthā viprasya vājino havīman
    • yadi - If
    • stutasya - of the one who is praised
    • maruto - O Winds (Maruts, the storm deities)
    • adhīthetthā - you are invoked
    • viprasya - of the wise one (the learned or the sage)
    • vājino - of the one who possesses strength or energy
    • havīman - the one who offers oblations
  • makṣū rāyaḥ suvīryasya dāta nū cid yam anya ādabhad arāvā
    • makṣū - the bees (symbolizing wealth or abundance)
    • rāyaḥ - riches or prosperity
    • suvīryasya - of the one who is strong or valiant
    • dāta - grant or bestow
    • nū - indeed
    • cid - if
    • yam - whom
    • anya - other
    • ādabhad - has not taken away
    • arāvā - the one who is free from obstacles

This mantra invokes the Maruts, the storm deities, and emphasizes the importance of praise and offerings made by the wise and strong. It suggests that if the Maruts are invoked by the learned and those who offer with strength, they will bestow prosperity and abundance, akin to bees gathering nectar.


In real life, this can be seen in various contexts:

  • Community Support: When a community comes together to support a cause, invoking the spirit of cooperation and strength, they can achieve great things, much like the Maruts bringing rain for crops.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals who seek knowledge and wisdom, and who actively contribute to their communities, often find that they are rewarded with opportunities and prosperity.
  • Business Ventures: In business, when leaders praise their teams and foster a strong, supportive environment, they often see increased productivity and success, similar to the blessings of the Maruts.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and the importance of wise leadership. It teaches that through praise, cooperation, and the invocation of divine energies, one can attract prosperity and overcome obstacles in life. This principle is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal development to community initiatives and business success.
