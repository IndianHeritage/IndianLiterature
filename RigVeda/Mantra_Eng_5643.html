यद गोपावद अदितिः शर्म भद्रम मित्रो यच्छन्ति वरुणः सुदासे |तस्मिन्न आतोकं तनयं दधानā मां कर्म देवहेळनं तुरासः ||
yad gopāvad aditiḥ śarma bhadram mitro yacchanti varuṇaḥ sudāse | tasminn ā tokaṁ tanayaṁ dadhānā mā karma devaheḻanaṁ turāsaḥ ||
  • yad gopāvad: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which is protected by the cowherd." In a broader sense, it signifies the nurturing and protective aspects of life, akin to how a cowherd cares for his cattle. In real life, this can be related to the role of guardians or caretakers in our lives, such as parents or mentors who provide guidance and protection.
  • aditiḥ: Aditi is often referred to as the mother of the gods in Vedic literature. She symbolizes boundlessness and the infinite. In our lives, this can represent the concept of unconditional love and support that one receives from family or a higher power, encouraging us to strive for our goals without limitations.
  • śarma bhadram: This translates to "auspicious shelter" or "blessed protection." It emphasizes the importance of a safe and nurturing environment. In practical terms, this can relate to the need for a supportive community or a safe home where individuals can thrive and grow.
  • mitro yacchanti: "Friends give." This highlights the value of friendship and the support we receive from our peers. In everyday life, this can be seen in how friends help each other through difficult times, providing emotional and practical support.
  • varuṇaḥ sudāse: Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water, representing truth and moral authority. "Sudāse" refers to those who are righteous. This part of the mantra suggests that adhering to truth and righteousness leads to divine support. In real life, this can be applied to ethical decision-making in personal and professional contexts, where integrity leads to positive outcomes.
  • tasminn ā tokaṁ tanayaṁ dadhānā: This means "in that, may we have offspring." It reflects the desire for prosperity and continuation of lineage. In modern terms, this can relate to the aspirations for family and the importance of nurturing future generations, ensuring they are equipped to face life's challenges.
  • mā karma devaheḻanaṁ turāsaḥ: This translates to "may our actions not lead to the wrath of the gods." It serves as a reminder to act with mindfulness and responsibility. In real life, this can be interpreted as the need to consider the consequences of our actions, promoting a sense of accountability in our personal and professional lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of protection, support, truth, and responsibility. Its significance lies in the reminder to cultivate a nurturing environment, value friendships, act ethically, and consider the impact of our actions on future generations. In real life, these principles can guide us in building strong relationships, making ethical choices, and fostering a supportive community, ultimately leading to a harmonious and prosperous life.
