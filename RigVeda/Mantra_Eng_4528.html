गर्भे मातुः पितुः पितā विदिद्युतानो अक्षरे |सीदन्न ऋतस्य योनिṁ ā ||
garbhe mātuḥ pituṣ pitā vididyutāno akṣare | sīdann ṛtasya yonim ā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • garbhe: in the womb
    • mātuḥ: of the mother
    • pituṣ: of the father
    • pitā: father
    • vididyutāno: one who is knowledgeable or enlightened
    • akṣare: in the imperishable or eternal
    • sīdann: sitting or residing
    • ṛtasya: of truth or cosmic order
    • yonim: womb or source
    • ā: towards
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the sacredness of the womb, emphasizing the roles of both the mother and father in the creation of life.
    • The term "vididyutāno" suggests that the parents are enlightened beings, indicating the importance of wisdom and knowledge in parenting.
    • The phrase "akṣare" refers to the eternal aspect of existence, hinting at the spiritual dimension of life that transcends the physical realm.
    • By mentioning "ṛtasya yonim," the mantra connects the act of creation to the cosmic order, suggesting that life is a manifestation of universal truth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern parenting, the emphasis on nurturing a child's intellectual and emotional growth reflects the idea of enlightened parenting. Parents who engage in continuous learning and self-improvement can better guide their children.
    • The significance of the womb can be seen in discussions around maternal health and prenatal care, highlighting the need for a supportive environment for both the mother and the unborn child.
    • In various cultures, rituals and practices are performed to honor the parents and the unborn child, recognizing the sacredness of the creation process.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra underscores the profound connection between parents and the creation of life, emphasizing the importance of wisdom, truth, and the spiritual dimension of existence.
    • It serves as a reminder of the responsibilities that come with parenthood and the need for a nurturing environment that fosters growth and enlightenment.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to reflect on their roles as parents, caregivers, and members of society, encouraging them to contribute positively to the cycle of life.
