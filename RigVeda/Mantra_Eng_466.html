अध स्वनान मरुतां विश्वम् आ सद्म पार्थिवम् |अरेजन्त प्र मानुषाः ||
adha svanān marutāṁ viśvam ā sadma pārthivam | arejanta pra mānuṣāḥ ||
  • Adha svanān marutāṁ viśvam ā sadma pārthivam: This phrase can be broken down into several components:
    • Adha: This means "down" or "below," indicating a grounding or foundational aspect.
    • Svanān: This translates to "the sounds" or "the voices," suggesting communication or expression.
    • Marutāṁ: Refers to the "Maruts," the storm deities in Vedic literature, symbolizing power, energy, and the forces of nature.
    • Viśvam: Means "the universe" or "all," indicating inclusivity and the entirety of existence.
    • Ā sadma: Translates to "the abode" or "the dwelling," which can refer to both physical and spiritual homes.
    • Pārthivam: Means "earthly" or "of the earth," emphasizing the material world.
  • Arejanta pra mānuṣāḥ: This part can also be analyzed:
    • Arejanta: This means "they have moved" or "they have acted," indicating a sense of action or dynamism.
    • Pra: A prefix that can mean "forth" or "out," suggesting a movement towards something.
    • Mānuṣāḥ: Refers to "humans" or "mankind," highlighting the human experience and existence.
  • Overall Meaning: The mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of the earthly realm and the divine forces represented by the Maruts. It suggests that the sounds or expressions of the universe resonate within the earthly abode, and that humans are actively participating in this cosmic dialogue.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Nature and Communication: Just as the Maruts represent the forces of nature, we can observe how natural phenomena (like storms) communicate with us through their sounds and impacts. For instance, the sound of thunder can evoke feelings of awe and respect for nature's power.
    • Community and Connection: In human societies, the idea of communication is vital. Just as the mantra suggests a connection between the divine and the earthly, communities thrive on shared experiences and expressions, whether through art, music, or conversation.
    • Environmental Awareness: The emphasis on the earthly abode reminds us of our responsibility towards the environment. Actions taken to protect our planet can be seen as a response to the call of nature, aligning with the mantra's theme of active participation.
  • Conclusion: This mantra highlights the significance of recognizing the relationship between the divine forces of nature and human existence. It encourages us to listen to the "voices" of the universe and to act in harmony with our environment. In real life, this can manifest as a commitment to sustainability, community engagement, and a deeper appreciation for the natural world. By understanding our place within this cosmic framework, we can foster a more meaningful and responsible existence.
