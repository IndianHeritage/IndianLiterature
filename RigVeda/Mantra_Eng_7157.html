त्वं ईशिषे सुतानाम् इन्द्र त्वम् असुतानाम् |त्वं राजा जनानाम् ||
tvam īśiṣe sutānām indra tvam asutānām | tvaṁ rājā janānām ||
  • tvam - This word translates to "you" or "thou," indicating a direct address to a powerful entity.
  • īśiṣe - This means "of the ruler" or "of the lord," referring to someone who has authority and power.
  • sutānām - This translates to "of the sons" or "of the offspring," indicating a connection to progeny or those who are nurtured.
  • indra - Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and the king of gods, symbolizing strength and leadership.
  • tvam - Repeated for emphasis, reinforcing the direct address to the divine.
  • asutānām - This means "of those who are not born" or "of the uncreated," which can refer to the eternal or the divine essence that transcends physical birth.
  • tvaṁ - Again, "you," emphasizing the personal connection to the divine.
  • rājā - This translates to "king" or "ruler," indicating authority and governance.
  • janānām - This means "of the people" or "of the beings," referring to the collective humanity or society.

The mantra can be interpreted as a direct invocation to Indra, recognizing him as the ruler of both the created and uncreated beings, and acknowledging his authority over the people. It emphasizes the relationship between the divine and humanity, highlighting the importance of leadership and guidance.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra is seen as a protector and leader, modern leaders can draw inspiration from this mantra to embody qualities of strength, wisdom, and responsibility towards their people.
  • Parenting: The reference to "sons" can be related to the nurturing role of parents, emphasizing the importance of guiding the next generation with care and wisdom.
  • Community Building: The idea of being a ruler of the people can inspire individuals to take active roles in their communities, fostering unity and support among members.

Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of divine guidance in leadership and the responsibility that comes with authority. It serves as a reminder for individuals in positions of power to act with integrity and care for those they lead, ensuring the well-being of society as a whole. By invoking the qualities of Indra, one can strive to be a compassionate and effective leader in various aspects of life.
