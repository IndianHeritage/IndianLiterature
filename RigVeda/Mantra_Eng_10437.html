यस् ते हन्ति पतयन्तं निषात्स्नुं यः सरीसृपम् |जातं यस् ते जिघांसति तं इतो नाशयामसि ||
yas te hanti patayantaṁ niṣatsnuṁ yaḥ sarīsṛpam | jātaṁ yas te jighāṁsati tam ito nāśayāmasi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • hanti: kills
    • patayantaṁ: the one who falls
    • niṣatsnuṁ: the one who is restless
    • yaḥ: who
    • sarīsṛpam: the creature (often referring to serpents or beings)
    • jātaṁ: born
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • jighāṁsati: desires to kill
    • tam: him
    • ito: from here
    • nāśayāmasi: we destroy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the protection from harmful entities or forces.
    • It emphasizes the idea of safeguarding oneself from those who wish to cause harm, whether they are physical beings or metaphorical threats.
    • The mantra can be seen as a call for divine protection against negativity and malevolence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, this mantra can be applied when dealing with toxic colleagues or competitive environments where one feels threatened.
    • In personal relationships, it can serve as a reminder to distance oneself from individuals who bring negativity or harm.
    • In a broader sense, it can be related to societal issues, where communities come together to protect themselves from external threats or injustices.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for vigilance and protection against harm.
    • It encourages individuals to be aware of their surroundings and to take action against those who may wish to do them harm.
    • In real life, it serves as a powerful reminder to seek safety and to foster environments that promote well-being and positivity.
