यद आसु मर्तो अमृतासु निस्पृक सं क्षोणीभिः क्रतुभिर न पृङ्क्ते |तā आतयो न तन््वः शुम्भत स्वा अश्वासो न क्रीळयो दण्डशानाः ||
yad āsu marto amṛtāsu nispṛk saṁ kṣoṇībhiḥ kratubhir na pṛṅkte | tā ātayo na tanvaḥ śumbhata svā aśvāso na krīḻayo dandaśānāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • āsu: in existence
    • marto: mortal
    • amṛtāsu: in the immortal
    • nispṛk: without desire
    • saṁ: together
    • kṣoṇībhiḥ: with the earth
    • kratubhir: with sacrifices
    • na: not
    • pṛṅkte: to be bound
    • tā: those
    • ātayo: who are wise
    • na: not
    • tanvaḥ: in their bodies
    • śumbhata: may they prosper
    • svā: in their own
    • aśvāso: horses
    • na: not
    • krīḻayo: to be played with
    • dandaśānāḥ: those who wield the staff
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the relationship between the mortal and the immortal, emphasizing the importance of living a life free from desires.
    • The phrase "yad āsu marto amṛtāsu" suggests that whatever exists in the mortal realm is transient, while the immortal is eternal.
    • The term "nispṛk" indicates a state of being without desire, which is crucial for achieving true wisdom and understanding.
    • By stating "kratubhir na pṛṅkte," it implies that wise individuals are not bound by earthly desires or material attachments.
    • The mantra encourages individuals to seek prosperity and wisdom, as indicated by "tā ātayo na tanvaḥ śumbhata," which means that those who are wise should prosper in their own right.
    • The mention of "aśvāso na krīḻayo" suggests that true strength and power should not be frivolously played with, but rather respected and utilized wisely.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom over material wealth. For instance, individuals who prioritize education and personal growth often find more fulfillment than those who chase after money.
    • In the context of leadership, wise leaders who act without selfish desires and focus on the greater good tend to create more sustainable and prosperous communities.
    • In personal relationships, practicing detachment from desires can lead to healthier interactions, where individuals appreciate each other without possessiveness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda serves as a profound reminder of the importance of wisdom, detachment from desires, and the pursuit of a higher purpose in life.
    • Its significance lies in guiding individuals to live a life that values the eternal over the transient, fostering a mindset that seeks knowledge and understanding.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to personal growth, better relationships, and a more meaningful existence.
