ग्रावाण उपरेष्वा महीयन्ते सजोषसः |वृष्णे दधतो वृष्ण्यम् ||
grāvāṇa upareṣv ā mahīyante sajoṣasaḥ | vṛṣṇe dadhato vṛṣṇyam ||
  • Mantra: "grāvāṇa upareṣv ā mahīyante sajoṣasaḥ | vṛṣṇe dadhato vṛṣṇyam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • grāvāṇa: Those who are strong or powerful.
    • upareṣv: Above or in the higher realms.
    • ā: Indeed or certainly.
    • mahīyante: Are glorified or honored.
    • sajoṣasaḥ: With joy or happiness.
    • vṛṣṇe: To Vṛṣṇa, a deity associated with strength and prosperity.
    • dadhato: Holding or possessing.
    • vṛṣṇyam: The essence or quality of Vṛṣṇa.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of powerful beings who are exalted in higher realms and are celebrated with joy.
    • It emphasizes the qualities of strength and prosperity associated with the deity Vṛṣṇa.
    • The mantra suggests that those who embody these qualities are honored and revered.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who demonstrate strength and integrity are often celebrated by their teams, similar to how the powerful beings are honored in the mantra.
    • In sports, athletes who exhibit exceptional skill and perseverance are revered by fans, reflecting the joy and honor mentioned in the mantra.
    • In community service, individuals who contribute positively and uplift others are recognized and celebrated, embodying the essence of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of strength, honor, and joy in both spiritual and worldly contexts.
    • It encourages individuals to embody the qualities of Vṛṣṇa, promoting prosperity and well-being in their lives and communities.
    • In real life, the mantra serves as a reminder to recognize and celebrate those who uplift and inspire others, fostering a culture of appreciation and positivity.
