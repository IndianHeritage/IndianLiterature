विष्वान् देवाम् आवह सोमपीतये अन्तरिक्षाद् उषस् त्वम् |सास्मासु धा गोमद अश्वावद् उक्त्यम् उषो वाजं सुवीर्यम् ||
viśvān devām̐ ā vaha somapītaye 'ntarikṣād uṣas tvam | sāsmāsu dhā gomad aśvāvad ukthya1m uṣo vājaṁ suvīryam ||
  • Translation: "O all-encompassing deities, we invoke you for the sake of the soma drink; from the sky, O dawn, you arise. In these offerings, may we have the strength of cows and horses, and may our hymns be fruitful, O dawn, bestowing us with excellent vigor."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viśvān: all-encompassing, universal
    • devām̐: deities, divine beings
    • ā vaha: we invoke, we call upon
    • somapītaye: for the sake of the soma drink, which is a sacred offering
    • 'ntarikṣād: from the sky, from the heavens
    • uṣas: dawn, the personification of dawn
    • tvam: you
    • sāsmāsu: in these offerings
    • dhā: may we have, may we possess
    • gomad: strength of cows, symbolizing wealth and nourishment
    • aśvāvad: strength of horses, symbolizing power and energy
    • ukthya1m: hymns, praises
    • uṣo: O dawn
    • vājaṁ: vigor, strength
    • suvīryam: excellent, powerful
  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energies for strength and vitality. The mention of soma, a sacred drink, signifies the connection between the divine and the earthly, where offerings are made to gain blessings. The dawn symbolizes new beginnings and the potential for growth and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be a reminder to seek divine guidance and strength in our endeavors, whether in personal growth, professional challenges, or community service.
    • For instance, before starting a new project or venture, one might recite this mantra to invoke the energies of the universe for success and vitality.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to appreciate the natural cycles of life, such as the dawn representing new opportunities and the strength derived from nature, like livestock, which can be seen in agricultural practices.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful invocation for strength, vitality, and divine support. It highlights the interconnectedness of the divine, nature, and human endeavors. By recognizing and honoring these connections, individuals can cultivate a sense of purpose and resilience in their lives.
