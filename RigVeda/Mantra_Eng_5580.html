स्तेनं राया सारमेय तस्करं वा पुनःसार |स्तोत्रिन् इन्द्रस्य रायसि किम अस्मान दुष्चुनायसे नि षु स्वप ||
stenaṁ rāya sārameya taskaraṁ vā punaḥsara | stotṝn indrasya rāyasi kim asmān ducchunāyase ni ṣu svapa ||
  • stenaṁ: This word translates to "thief" or "robber." It signifies someone who takes away what does not belong to them.
  • rāya: This means "wealth" or "prosperity." It represents the abundance and resources that one possesses.
  • sārameya: This term refers to "the one who is like a dog," often symbolizing loyalty or vigilance.
  • taskaraṁ: This is another term for "thief," emphasizing the act of stealing or taking away.
  • vā: This means "or," indicating an alternative or choice.
  • punaḥsara: This can be interpreted as "the one who returns" or "the one who comes back," suggesting a cycle or repetition.
  • stotṝn: This word means "praise" or "hymn," indicating a form of worship or acknowledgment.
  • indrasya: This refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power and authority.
  • rāyasi: This means "in wealth" or "in prosperity," relating to the abundance associated with Indra.
  • kim: This translates to "what," often used to pose a question or inquiry.
  • asmān: This means "us," referring to the collective group or community.
  • ducchunāyase: This can be interpreted as "to be harmed" or "to be affected negatively," indicating a sense of danger or threat.
  • ni: This is a negation, meaning "not" or "no."
  • ṣu: This means "good" or "well," often used to denote positivity.
  • svapa: This translates to "sleep" or "dream," suggesting a state of rest or unconsciousness.

The mantra essentially addresses the themes of theft, wealth, and divine protection. It invokes the power of Indra to safeguard against those who would take away prosperity and well-being. The speaker expresses a desire for protection from thieves and harmful influences, while also acknowledging the importance of wealth and abundance in life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various situations where individuals or communities feel threatened by loss or harm. For example:

  • In a business context, companies may face threats from competitors or economic downturns. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek divine protection and maintain integrity in their pursuits.
  • In personal life, individuals may encounter situations where they feel their resources or relationships are at risk. Reciting this mantra can provide a sense of security and encourage vigilance against negative influences.
  • In a broader societal context, communities facing crime or injustice can use this mantra to invoke a sense of collective strength and protection, fostering unity against common threats.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine protection against theft and harm, emphasizing the value of wealth and prosperity. It serves as a reminder to remain vigilant and seek support from higher powers in times of uncertainty. By incorporating this mantra into daily life, individuals and communities can cultivate a sense of security and resilience, ultimately fostering a more prosperous and harmonious existence.
