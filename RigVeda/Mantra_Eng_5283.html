अर्णांसी चित पप्रथाना सुदासा इन्द्रो गाधान्य अकृत्नोत सुपारा |शर्धन्तं शिम्युम उचथस्य नव्यः शापं सिन्धूनाम अकृतोद अशस्तीर् ||
arṇāṁsi cit paprathānā sudāsa indro gādhāny akṛṇot supārā | śardhantaṁ śimyum ucathasya navyaḥ śāpaṁ sindhūnām akṛṇod aśastīḥ ||
  • arṇāṁsi: This term refers to the "streams" or "flowing waters." In a broader sense, it symbolizes the flow of life and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • cit: Meaning "consciousness" or "awareness," this word emphasizes the importance of being aware of one's surroundings and the deeper truths of existence.
  • paprathānā: This can be interpreted as "spreading" or "expanding," suggesting the dissemination of knowledge or virtues.
  • sudāsa: Referring to a "good servant" or "devotee," it highlights the value of service and dedication in life.
  • indro: This term refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
  • gādhāny: This word can be associated with "Gadh," a term that may refer to a specific lineage or tradition, indicating the importance of heritage.
  • akṛṇot: Meaning "he created" or "he made," it signifies the act of creation and manifestation in the world.
  • supārā: This term can be interpreted as "well-prepared" or "excellent," suggesting the importance of quality and readiness in endeavors.
  • śardhantaṁ: This word refers to "the gathering" or "assembly," indicating the significance of community and collective effort.
  • śimyum: This can be understood as "the one who is strong" or "powerful," emphasizing the qualities of resilience and strength.
  • ucathasya: Meaning "of the utterance" or "of the speech," it highlights the power of words and communication in shaping reality.
  • navyaḥ: This term means "new" or "fresh," symbolizing innovation and the importance of new ideas.
  • śāpaṁ: Referring to "curse" or "oath," it signifies the weight of promises and the consequences of actions.
  • sindhūnām: This word means "of the rivers," symbolizing the flow of life and the nurturing aspect of nature.
  • akṛṇod: Again meaning "he created" or "he made," reinforcing the theme of creation.
  • aśastīḥ: This term can be interpreted as "the good" or "the virtuous," emphasizing the importance of righteousness in actions.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, consciousness, and community. It emphasizes the importance of being aware of our surroundings, the value of service, and the strength found in unity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in professional settings, community service, and personal growth through awareness and dedication. The mantra serves as a reminder that our actions, words, and intentions shape our reality and that we should strive for excellence and virtue in all endeavors.
