प्र धारा मध्वो अग्रियो महीर अपो वि गाहते |हविर हविष्सु वन्द्यः ||
pra dhārā madhvo agriyo mahīr apo vi gāhate | havir haviṣṣu vandyaḥ ||
  • pra dhārā: This phrase can be translated as "the flow of abundance" or "the support of nourishment." It signifies the essential resources and sustenance that life requires.
  • madhvo: This word refers to "sweetness" or "delight." It symbolizes the joy and pleasure derived from life's experiences and the sweetness of relationships.
  • agriyo: Meaning "the foremost" or "the best," this term emphasizes the importance of prioritizing what is most valuable in life, whether it be relationships, knowledge, or experiences.
  • mahīr: This word translates to "great" or "mighty," indicating strength and significance. It can refer to powerful forces in nature or the strength of human spirit.
  • apo: This term means "water," which is essential for life. It symbolizes purity, clarity, and the flow of life itself.
  • vi gāhate: This phrase means "they flow together" or "they unite." It signifies the interconnectedness of all elements of life and the importance of harmony.
  • havir: This word refers to "offerings" or "sacrifices." It highlights the importance of giving and sharing in relationships and communities.
  • haviṣṣu: This term means "in the offerings" or "in the sacrifices," emphasizing the significance of what we contribute to the world around us.
  • vandyaḥ: This word translates to "worthy of worship" or "to be revered." It signifies the respect and honor we should give to the forces of nature, our relationships, and the values we hold dear.

Conclusion: The mantra "pra dhārā madhvo agriyo mahīr apo vi gāhate | havir haviṣṣu vandyaḥ ||" encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of abundance, joy, strength, and interconnectedness. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal relationships, it encourages us to prioritize love and support, creating a nurturing environment.
  • In community settings, it highlights the importance of sharing resources and working together for the common good.
  • In environmental contexts, it reminds us to respect and honor nature, recognizing the vital role it plays in our lives.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate a life filled with abundance, joy, and respect for all that sustains us, fostering harmony in our personal and communal lives.
