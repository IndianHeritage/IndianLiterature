शतब्रध्न इषुस तव सहस्रपर्ण एक इत् |यम् इन्द्र चक्रिषे युजम् ||
śatabradhna iṣus tava sahasraparṇa eka it | yam indra cakṛṣe yujam ||
  • śatabradhna: This term can be broken down into "śata," meaning "hundred," and "bradhna," which refers to "strength" or "power." Together, it signifies immense strength or a hundredfold power.
  • iṣus: This word translates to "arrows." In the context of the mantra, it symbolizes the tools or means through which one can achieve their goals or objectives.
  • tava: This means "your," indicating possession or belonging. It signifies a connection to a higher power or deity.
  • sahasraparṇa: This term combines "sahasra," meaning "thousand," and "parṇa," which means "leaves" or "feathers." It can be interpreted as having a multitude of resources or support.
  • eka: This translates to "one" or "single," suggesting unity or singular focus amidst the multitude.
  • it: This word means "thus" or "so," indicating a conclusion or result.
  • yam: This means "whom," referring to a specific entity or deity, in this case, Indra.
  • indra: Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and strength. He represents power and authority.
  • cakṛṣe: This term means "you have drawn" or "you have created," indicating an action taken by Indra.
  • yujam: This translates to "the team" or "the pair," suggesting collaboration or partnership.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for immense strength and resources, invoking the deity Indra to assist in achieving one's goals through unity and collaboration. It emphasizes the importance of having a strong foundation (hundredfold power) and the right tools (arrows) to succeed.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Teamwork in Organizations: Just as the mantra speaks of unity and collaboration, in a workplace, effective teamwork can lead to achieving common goals. For instance, a project team working together with a shared vision can accomplish tasks more efficiently.
  • Personal Goals: When pursuing personal ambitions, having a clear focus (eka) and the right resources (sahasraparṇa) can lead to success. For example, an athlete training for a competition needs both physical strength and mental focus.
  • Community Support: The mantra highlights the importance of collective strength. In community initiatives, when individuals come together (yujam) to support a cause, they can create significant change, such as organizing a charity event.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity, the importance of having the right tools, and the strength that comes from collaboration. It encourages individuals and groups to seek divine support in their endeavors, reinforcing the idea that with the right mindset and resources, one can achieve great things.
