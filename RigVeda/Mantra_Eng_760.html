दुरोकशोचिः क्रतुर न नित्यो जायत योनाव अरं विश्वस्मै ||
durokaśociḥ kratur na nityo jāyeva yonāv araṁ viśvasmai ||
  • Mantra: "durokaśociḥ kratur na nityo jāyeva yonāv araṁ viśvasmai"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • durokaśociḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who shines brightly from afar" or "the one who is far-reaching in brilliance."
    • kratur: Refers to "intellect" or "willpower," indicating the mental strength and determination.
    • na: Means "not," indicating negation.
    • nityo: Translates to "eternal" or "everlasting," suggesting something that is constant and unchanging.
    • jāyeva: Means "like birth" or "as if born," indicating a sense of emergence or creation.
    • yonāv: Refers to "womb" or "source," symbolizing the origin or place of creation.
    • araṁ: Can be interpreted as "to be" or "existence," indicating the state of being.
    • viśvasmai: Means "to all" or "universal," suggesting inclusivity and a connection to the whole.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of a powerful intellect that shines brightly and is far-reaching.
    • It emphasizes the importance of mental strength and determination in achieving one's goals.
    • The notion of eternal existence suggests that true wisdom and understanding are timeless and universal.
    • By likening this wisdom to a source or womb, it implies that knowledge and enlightenment are born from a deep understanding of existence.
    • The mantra encourages individuals to connect with this universal wisdom and apply it in their lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the field of science, researchers often draw upon the collective knowledge of past discoveries to innovate and create new technologies, embodying the idea of a "far-reaching brilliance."
    • In personal development, individuals who cultivate mental strength and resilience can overcome challenges, reflecting the power of "kratur."
    • In community building, fostering a sense of inclusivity and shared purpose can lead to collective growth, resonating with the concept of "viśvasmai."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of mental strength, wisdom, and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding, not just for personal gain but for the benefit of the larger community.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, innovation, and a more harmonious society.
