स्पर्धन्ते वा उ देवहूये अत्र येषु ध्वजेषु दिद्यवः पतन्ति |युवं तां इन्द्रावरुणाव अमित्रान हतम पराचः शर्वा विषूचः ||
spardhante vā u devahūye atra yeṣu dhvajeṣu didyavaḥ patanti | yuvaṁ tām̐ indrāvaruṇāv amitrān hatam parācaḥ śarvā viṣūcaḥ ||
  • Spardhante vā u devahūye: This phrase can be translated as "The gods are in competition or striving." It suggests a sense of rivalry or striving among divine beings, which can be likened to how individuals or teams compete in various fields, such as sports or academics, to achieve excellence.
  • Atra yeṣu dhvajeṣu didyavaḥ patanti: This means "Here, those who have fallen under the banners." It refers to those who have succumbed to the challenges or battles represented by the banners. In real life, this can be seen in contexts like military conflicts or competitive sports, where individuals or teams may face defeat but still learn valuable lessons from their experiences.
  • Yuvaṁ tām̐ indrāvaruṇāv: This translates to "You two, Indra and Varuna." Indra and Varuna are significant deities in Vedic literature, representing power and cosmic order, respectively. In modern life, this can symbolize the balance of strength and wisdom in leadership, where effective leaders must embody both qualities to guide their teams or communities successfully.
  • Amitrān hatam parācaḥ: This means "The enemies are defeated." It signifies overcoming obstacles or adversaries. In everyday life, this can relate to personal challenges, such as overcoming fears or achieving goals despite opposition, whether in professional settings or personal endeavors.
  • Śarvā viṣūcaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "The all-pervading one." It refers to a universal presence or consciousness that connects all beings. In a real-life context, this can be seen in the interconnectedness of humanity, where our actions and decisions impact others, emphasizing the importance of empathy and understanding in our interactions.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of competition, resilience, and the interplay of strength and wisdom. It highlights the significance of striving for excellence, learning from failures, and the importance of leadership qualities in overcoming challenges. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, teamwork, and community building, reminding us that while we may face adversities, the pursuit of knowledge, strength, and unity can lead to greater achievements and harmony.
