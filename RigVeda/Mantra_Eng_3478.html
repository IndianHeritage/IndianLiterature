तं नो वाजाऽऋभुक्षण इन्द्र नासत्याऽरयिम् |सम अश्वं चर्षणिभ्य ā पुरु शस्त मगत्तये ||
taṁ no vājā ṛbhukṣaṇa indra nāsatyā rayim | sam aśvaṁ carṣaṇibhya ā puru śasta maghattaye ||
  • taṁ no vājā ṛbhukṣaṇa indra nāsatyā rayim: This phrase can be broken down into several components:
    • taṁ: "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity.
    • no: "to us," indicating a plea or invocation.
    • vājā: "wealth" or "prosperity," often associated with strength and abundance.
    • ṛbhukṣaṇa: "the one who bestows," referring to a deity who grants blessings.
    • indra: "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power and authority.
    • nāsatyā: "the Asvins," twin deities representing health and well-being.
    • rayim: "wealth" or "prosperity," emphasizing the desire for abundance.
  • sam aśvaṁ carṣaṇibhya ā puru śasta maghattaye: This part can also be analyzed:
    • sam: "together" or "united," indicating harmony.
    • aśvaṁ: "horses," symbolizing strength, speed, and vitality.
    • carṣaṇibhya: "from the fields," suggesting a connection to agriculture and sustenance.
    • ā: "to" or "towards," indicating direction or purpose.
    • puru: "many" or "abundant," reinforcing the idea of plenty.
    • śasta: "well-being" or "prosperity," again emphasizing health and abundance.
    • maghattaye: "for greatness," indicating a desire for significant blessings.

  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for prosperity and well-being, invoking the blessings of Indra and the Asvins.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and strength, symbolized by horses, which are often associated with power and success in various cultures.
    • The invocation of divine entities reflects a deep-rooted belief in seeking help from higher powers for achieving abundance in life.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be related to business ventures where individuals seek prosperity and success through collaboration and teamwork.
    • In agriculture, farmers may recite this mantra to invoke blessings for a bountiful harvest, reflecting the connection to the land and its resources.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new business openings, this mantra can be chanted to seek blessings for a prosperous future.

  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for prosperity, health, and well-being.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine, encouraging a harmonious approach to life.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts, from personal aspirations to communal efforts, reinforcing the idea that seeking blessings and working together can lead to greater success.
