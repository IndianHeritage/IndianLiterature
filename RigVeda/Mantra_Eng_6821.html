यां नु श्वेताव अवो दिव उच्छरात उप द्युभिः |इन्द्राग्न्योः अनु व्रतम उहानां यान्ति सिन्धवो यान सीम बन्धाद अमुञ्चतां नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
yā nu śvetāv avo diva uccarāta upa dyubhiḥ | indrāgnyor anu vratam uhānā yanti sindhavo yān sīm bandhād amuñcatāṁ nabhantām anyake same ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yā nu: who indeed
    • śvetāv: white, pure
    • avo: from the heavens
    • diva: of the sky
    • uccarāta: having risen high
    • upa: towards
    • dyubhiḥ: the heavens
    • indrāgnyor: of Indra and Agni
    • anu: following
    • vratam: vow, commitment
    • uhānā: those who are invoked
    • yanti: go, proceed
    • sindhavo: rivers
    • yān: which
    • sīm: boundaries
    • bandhād: from the bond
    • amuñcatāṁ: release, liberate
    • nabhantām: from the sky
    • anyake: other
    • same: in the same way
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine forces represented by Indra and Agni, who are invoked to aid in the liberation of the rivers from their constraints.
    • The "white" and "pure" elements symbolize clarity and truth, suggesting that the divine powers are called upon to bring forth purity and enlightenment.
    • The rivers represent the flow of life and knowledge, and their liberation signifies the removal of obstacles that hinder progress and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, this mantra can be applied when one seeks clarity in decision-making. Just as the rivers are liberated, individuals can seek to free themselves from confusion and find their true path.
    • In a community setting, invoking the essence of this mantra can inspire collective efforts to overcome societal challenges, much like how rivers nourish the land and support life.
    • In environmental contexts, it can symbolize the importance of preserving natural water bodies, ensuring they flow freely and sustain ecosystems.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in achieving clarity, liberation, and progress.
    • It emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, nature, and the divine, reminding us that seeking higher guidance can lead to overcoming obstacles.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals and communities to strive for purity of purpose and to work towards the liberation of knowledge and resources for the greater good.
