वनस्पतेऽव सृजोऽप देवान अग्निः हविः शमितासूदयाति |सेदु होताऽसत्यतरो यजाती यथा देवानां जनिमानि वेद ||
vanaspate 'va sṛjopa devān agnir haviḥ śamitā sūdayāti | sed u hotā satyataro yajāti yathā devānāṁ janimāni veda ||
  • vanaspate: This term refers to the "lord of the plants" or "vegetation." In a real-life context, it can symbolize the importance of nature and the environment in sustaining life.
  • va sṛjopa: This phrase means "may you create" or "may you bring forth." It emphasizes the act of creation and the nurturing aspect of nature.
  • devān: This word translates to "gods" or "divine beings." It signifies the higher powers or forces that govern the universe.
  • agnir: Meaning "fire," this element is often associated with energy, transformation, and purification. In daily life, fire can represent both physical fire and metaphorical fire, such as passion or ambition.
  • haviḥ: This term refers to offerings or sacrifices made to the divine. It highlights the importance of gratitude and giving back to the forces that sustain us.
  • śamitā: This means "calm" or "peaceful." It suggests the need for tranquility in our lives, which can be achieved through mindfulness and meditation.
  • sūdayāti: This translates to "he who nourishes" or "he who sustains." It emphasizes the role of nurturing in both nature and human relationships.
  • sed u hotā: This phrase means "and the priest" or "the one who invokes." It signifies the importance of rituals and the role of individuals who connect the material and spiritual worlds.
  • satyataro: This means "the truest" or "the most truthful." It emphasizes the value of truth and integrity in our actions and thoughts.
  • yajāti: This translates to "he sacrifices" or "he worships." It highlights the importance of devotion and the act of giving in various forms.
  • yathā: Meaning "as" or "just as," it draws a comparison or analogy, suggesting a connection between different elements or ideas.
  • devānāṁ: This means "of the gods." It reinforces the connection to the divine and the importance of recognizing higher powers in our lives.
  • janimāni: This translates to "births" or "origins." It signifies the cycles of life and the continuous process of creation and rebirth.
  • veda: Meaning "knowledge" or "wisdom," it emphasizes the importance of learning and understanding in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the interconnectedness of nature, divinity, and human existence. It highlights the significance of nurturing, truth, and the cyclical nature of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to respect the environment, engage in acts of gratitude, and seek truth in their actions. It serves as a reminder of the importance of rituals and the divine forces that guide us, encouraging a harmonious relationship with the world around us.
