स्व आ दमे सुदुघा यस्य धेनुः स्वधाम पीपाय सुभ्व अन्नम् अत्ति |सो अपां नपाद ऊर्जयन्न अप्स्व अन्तर वसुदेयान्य विधते विभाति ||
sva ā dame sudughā yasya dhenuḥ svadhām pīpāya subhv annam atti | so apāṁ napād ūrjayann apsv a1ntar vasudeyāya vidhate vi bhāti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sva: one's own
    • ā dame: in the home
    • su-dughā: well-nourished or rich in milk
    • yasya: whose
    • dhenuḥ: cow
    • svadhām: in its own essence
    • pīpāya: nourishing or providing sustenance
    • subhv: good or auspicious
    • annam: food
    • atti: eats
    • so: he
    • apāṁ: of waters
    • napād: of the foot or the path
    • ūrjayann: energizing or invigorating
    • apsv: in the waters
    • antar: within
    • vasudeyāya: for the prosperity of the earth
    • vidhate: creates or establishes
    • vi bhāti: shines or manifests
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the importance of nourishment and prosperity in one's home and environment.
    • The "cow" symbolizes wealth and sustenance, representing the essential resources that provide for a family or community.
    • The phrase "whose cow" indicates a personal connection to these resources, emphasizing the idea of ownership and responsibility.
    • The mantra highlights the significance of good food ("subhv annam") as a source of strength and vitality.
    • The reference to "waters" suggests the importance of natural resources in sustaining life and promoting growth.
    • Overall, the mantra conveys a message of harmony between one's personal resources and the broader environment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often rely on their livestock (like cows) for milk and other products, which directly contributes to their livelihood.
    • In a family setting, the quality of food prepared at home can significantly impact the health and well-being of its members.
    • Communities that prioritize sustainable practices, such as conserving water and protecting natural resources, exemplify the principles outlined in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the interconnectedness of personal well-being, community prosperity, and environmental sustainability.
    • It serves as a reminder to cherish and responsibly manage the resources we have, ensuring that they nourish not only ourselves but also the world around us.
    • In modern life, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to adopt sustainable practices, prioritize healthy living, and foster a sense of responsibility towards nature.
