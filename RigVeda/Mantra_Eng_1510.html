अस्मत्रा गन्तम उप नो 'र्वाञ्चा सोमपीतये |अयं वाम मित्रावरुणा नृभिः सुतः सोम आ पीतये सुतः ||
asmatrā gantam upa no 'rvāñcā somapītaye | ayaṁ vām mitrāvaruṇā nṛbhiḥ sutaḥ soma ā pītaye sutaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "asmatrā gantam upa no 'rvāñcā somapītaye | ayaṁ vām mitrāvaruṇā nṛbhiḥ sutaḥ soma ā pītaye sutaḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "May we approach the one who is the source of our strength, the one who is the essence of the Soma, and may this Soma, which is the son of Mitra and Varuna, be offered to us for our nourishment."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • asmatrā: "from us" or "our"
    • gantam: "to approach" or "to reach"
    • upa: "near" or "towards"
    • no: "us" or "for us"
    • 'rvāñcā: "the one who is the essence" or "the source"
    • somapītaye: "for the drinking of Soma" or "for the nourishment from Soma"
    • ayaṁ: "this"
    • vām: "your" or "of you"
    • mitrāvaruṇā: "Mitra and Varuna" (the deities representing friendship and cosmic order)
    • nṛbhiḥ: "by men" or "by people"
    • sutaḥ: "son" or "offspring"
    • soma: "Soma" (a sacred drink in Vedic rituals)
    • ā pītaye: "for drinking" or "for consumption"

  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking strength and nourishment from divine sources, particularly through the sacred drink Soma, which symbolizes vitality and spiritual enlightenment.
    • Mitra and Varuna represent the principles of friendship, harmony, and cosmic order, suggesting that our well-being is interconnected with the universe and the relationships we cultivate.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied to the practice of mindfulness and gratitude, encouraging individuals to seek support from their community and the natural world around them.

  • Real-Life Application:
    • In moments of stress or uncertainty, individuals can reflect on this mantra to remind themselves of the strength that comes from community and the support of friends and family.
    • During rituals or gatherings, invoking this mantra can serve as a reminder to appreciate the nourishment provided by nature and the bonds we share with others.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages a holistic approach to life, where one seeks balance and harmony with the environment and the people around them.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of seeking divine nourishment, and the value of community support.
    • By incorporating its teachings into daily life, individuals can foster a sense of unity, strength, and purpose, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
