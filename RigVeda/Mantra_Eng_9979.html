उद्नो ह्रदं अपिबज जर्हृषाणः कूटं स्म तृंहद अभिमातिम एति |प्र मुष्कभारः श्रव इच्छमानो 'जिरं बाहू अभरत सिषासन ||
udno hradam apibaj jarhṛṣāṇaḥ kūṭaṁ sma tṛṁhad abhimātim eti | pra muṣkabhāraḥ śrava icchamāno 'jiram bāhū abharat siṣāsan ||
  • udno hradam apibaj: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who has entered the lake of the waters." In a metaphorical sense, it signifies the journey into the depths of knowledge and consciousness.
  • jarhṛṣāṇaḥ: This term refers to "the one who is filled with joy or delight." It emphasizes the importance of joy in the pursuit of knowledge and understanding.
  • kūṭaṁ sma tṛṁhad abhimātim eti: This can be translated as "the one who approaches the peak with a strong intention." It suggests that with determination and focus, one can reach great heights in their endeavors.
  • pra muṣkabhāraḥ: This phrase means "the one who is burdened with the weight of knowledge." It highlights the responsibility that comes with wisdom and understanding.
  • śrava icchamāno: This translates to "the one who desires to hear." It signifies the importance of listening and learning from others in the quest for knowledge.
  • 'jiram bāhū abharat siṣāsan: This can be interpreted as "the one who has strong arms and supports the teachings." It emphasizes the role of strength and support in the dissemination of knowledge.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom. It highlights the importance of joy, determination, responsibility, and the willingness to learn from others. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various fields such as education, leadership, and personal development. For instance, a teacher who approaches their role with joy and determination can inspire students to reach their full potential. Similarly, a leader who listens to their team and supports their growth fosters a positive and productive environment. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder that the journey of knowledge is not just about acquiring information, but also about the joy and responsibility that comes with it.
