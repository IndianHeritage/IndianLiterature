इमा नारीर अविधवाः सुपत्नीर आँजनेन सर्पिषा सं विशन्तु |अनश्रवो 'नमीवाः सुरत्ना आ रोहन्तु जनयो योनिम अग्रे ||
imā nārīr avidhavāḥ supatnīr āñjanena sarpiṣā saṁ viśantu | anaśravo 'namīvāḥ suratnā ā rohantu janayo yonim agre ||
  • imā nārīr avidhavāḥ: This phrase translates to "these women, who are not widows." It emphasizes the strength and vitality of women in society, highlighting their role as nurturers and caretakers.
  • supatnīr: Meaning "well-wed," this term signifies women who are in harmonious and supportive relationships. It reflects the importance of healthy partnerships in fostering a stable family and community environment.
  • āñjanena sarpiṣā saṁ viśantu: This can be interpreted as "may they come together with the essence of ghee (clarified butter)." Ghee is often associated with purity and nourishment in Indian culture, symbolizing the idea of unity and prosperity among women.
  • anaśravo 'namīvāḥ: This phrase means "may they be free from sorrow." It expresses a wish for happiness and fulfillment in the lives of women, encouraging a life devoid of grief and hardship.
  • suratnā ā rohantu: Translated as "may they rise with joy," this line emphasizes the importance of joy and positivity in life, encouraging women to thrive and flourish.
  • janayo yonim agre: This means "may they bring forth life." It highlights the role of women in procreation and nurturing future generations, underlining their significance in the continuity of society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the reverence for women, celebrating their strength, joy, and essential role in society. It serves as a reminder of the importance of supportive relationships and the nurturing of future generations. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as promoting gender equality, supporting women's rights, and fostering healthy family dynamics. By recognizing and honoring the contributions of women, society can work towards a more balanced and harmonious existence.
