ऋतावानं ऋतायवो यज्ञस्य साधनं गिरा | उपो एनं जुजुषुर नमसस पदे ||
ṛtāvānam ṛtāyavo yajñasya sādhanaṁ girā | upo enaṁ jujuṣur namasas pade ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ṛtāvānam: One who is established in truth or righteousness.
    • ṛtāyavo: Those who are devoted to truth or righteousness.
    • yajñasya: Of the sacrifice or ritual.
    • sādhanaṁ: The means or method to achieve something.
    • girā: By speech or through words.
    • upo: Approaching or coming near.
    • enaṁ: This or him.
    • jujuṣur: They desire or wish for.
    • namasas: With reverence or humility.
    • pade: In the path or in the way.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of truth and righteousness in the context of rituals and sacrifices.
    • It suggests that those who are committed to truth should approach the rituals with reverence and humility.
    • The use of "girā" indicates that speech and intention play a crucial role in the effectiveness of these rituals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to ethical practices in business. For instance, a company that operates with transparency and integrity is more likely to gain the trust of its customers.
    • In personal relationships, approaching conflicts with honesty and a desire for resolution reflects the principles of this mantra.
    • In community service, individuals who engage in charitable acts with genuine intentions and respect for those they help embody the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for individuals to align their actions with truth and righteousness, especially in the context of rituals and communal activities.
    • It serves as a reminder that the effectiveness of our actions is enhanced when they are rooted in sincerity and respect.
    • In a world where ethical dilemmas are common, this mantra encourages us to uphold values that foster trust and harmony in our interactions.
