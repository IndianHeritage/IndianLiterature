द्वेष्टि श्वश्रूर अप जाया रुṇद्धि न नाथितो विन्दते मार्डितारम् |अश्वस्येव जरतो वसन्यस्य नाहं विन्दामि किटवस्य भोगम् ||
dveṣṭi śvaśrūr apa jāyā ruṇaddhi na nāthito vindate marḍitāram | aśvasyeva jarato vasnyasya nāhaṁ vindāmi kitavasya bhogam ||
  • Mantra: "dveṣṭi śvaśrūr apa jāyā ruṇaddhi na nāthito vindate marḍitāram | aśvasyeva jarato vasnyasya nāhaṁ vindāmi kitavasya bhogam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dveṣṭi: hates
    • śvaśrūr: mother-in-law
    • apa: away from
    • jāyā: wife
    • ruṇaddhi: does not complain
    • na: not
    • nāthito: being protected
    • vindate: obtains
    • marḍitāram: the one who is beaten
    • aśvasyeva: like a horse
    • jarato: of the old
    • vasnyasya: of the one who is weak
    • nāhaṁ: I do not
    • vindāmi: obtain
    • kitavasya: of the deceitful
    • bhogam: enjoyment or pleasure
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects the complexities of human relationships, particularly within familial structures.
    • The first part suggests a scenario where a mother-in-law may harbor negative feelings towards her daughter-in-law, yet the daughter-in-law does not voice her grievances.
    • It highlights the silent struggles and the emotional burden that can exist in such relationships.
    • The second part compares the situation to an old horse that is weak and unable to enjoy the pleasures of life, indicating that one may not gain the rewards or joys due to deceitful actions or relationships.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the relationship between a mother-in-law and daughter-in-law can be fraught with tension. This mantra serves as a reminder of the need for open communication and understanding.
    • In workplaces, individuals may feel oppressed or mistreated but choose not to speak up, leading to a toxic environment. This mantra encourages individuals to address issues rather than suffer in silence.
    • In friendships, deceit can lead to a breakdown of trust, preventing individuals from enjoying the companionship that should be fulfilling.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of addressing negative feelings and the consequences of silence in relationships.
    • It serves as a reminder that avoiding confrontation may lead to a lack of fulfillment and joy in life.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to foster open communication, seek resolution, and avoid deceitful behaviors to enhance their relationships and overall well-being.
