इन्द्रो वलं रक्षितारं दुधानां करेणेव विचकर्त रवेण |स्वेदाञ्जिभिर आशिरम इच्छमानो 'रोदयत पणिम आग गामुष्णात ||
indro valaṁ rakṣitāraṁ dughānāṁ kareṇeva vi cakartā raveṇa | svedāñjibhir āśiram icchamāno 'rodayat paṇim ā gā amuṣṇāt ||
  • Indro: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Valaṁ: Means "the protector" or "the one who shields," indicating a sense of security and guardianship.
  • Rakṣitāraṁ: Translates to "the one who protects," emphasizing the role of a guardian or protector.
  • Dughānāṁ: Refers to "the milkers" or "those who provide nourishment," symbolizing sustenance and care.
  • Kareṇeva: Means "like a hand," suggesting a personal touch or direct involvement in the act of protection or nurturing.
  • Vi cakartā: Translates to "he who acts," indicating the active role of the protector in ensuring safety and well-being.
  • Raveṇa: Refers to "the sun," symbolizing light, energy, and life-giving force.
  • Svedāñjibhir: Means "with the sweat," indicating effort, hard work, and dedication in the process of nurturing and protecting.
  • Āśiram: Translates to "the abode" or "the place of refuge," suggesting a safe haven or sanctuary.
  • Icchamāno: Means "desiring" or "wishing," indicating intention and purpose behind actions.
  • 'Ro dayat: Translates to "he weeps" or "he cries," suggesting a deep emotional connection to the act of protection and nurturing.
  • Paṇim: Refers to "the hand," symbolizing action and the means through which protection is enacted.
  • Ā gā: Means "come," indicating an invitation or call to action.
  • Amuṣṇāt: Translates to "he has warmed" or "he has made warm," symbolizing comfort, care, and emotional warmth.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of protection, nurturing, and the emotional bonds that exist between a protector and those they care for. It highlights the active role of guardianship, akin to a parent or a leader who provides for and shields their dependents. In real life, this can be seen in various scenarios such as a parent caring for their child, a leader supporting their team, or even communities coming together to protect their members. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the responsibilities we hold towards others and the emotional connections that drive us to protect and nurture those in our care.
