अधासु मन्द्रो अरतिर विभावाव स्यति द्विवर्तनिर वनेषाट् |ऊर्ध्वा यच छ्रेणिर न शिशुर दान मक्षू स्थिरं शेवृधं सूत माता ||
adhāsu mandro aratir vibhāvāva syati dvivartanir vaneṣāṭ | ūrdhvā yac chreṇir na śiśur dan makṣū sthiraṁ śevṛdhaṁ sūta mātā ||
  • adhāsu mandro aratir vibhāvāva syati dvivartanir vaneṣāṭ: This line can be broken down into several components.
    • adhāsu: Refers to something that is below or beneath.
    • mandro: Can mean slow or gentle.
    • aratir: Suggests a sense of movement or flow.
    • vibhāvāva: Indicates a manifestation or expression.
    • syati: Means to be or to exist.
    • dvivartanir: Refers to a turning or revolving.
    • vaneṣāṭ: Relates to the forest or wilderness.
    This line can be interpreted as the gentle flow of life and existence that manifests in the natural world, particularly in the wilderness, where cycles of turning and transformation occur.
  • ūrdhvā yac chreṇir na śiśur dan makṣū sthiraṁ śevṛdhaṁ sūta mātā: This line also has significant components.
    • ūrdhvā: Means upward or elevated.
    • yac: Translates to "that which."
    • chreṇir: Refers to a path or a way.
    • na: Means "not."
    • śiśur: Refers to a child or offspring.
    • dan: Can mean wealth or abundance.
    • makṣū: Refers to a bee or something that gathers.
    • sthiraṁ: Means stable or firm.
    • śevṛdhaṁ: Refers to something that is nourished or supported.
    • sūta mātā: Can be interpreted as the mother or source of nourishment.
    This line suggests that the upward path, which is not just for the young or the weak, is one that leads to stability and nourishment, akin to how bees gather resources for their hive.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, nature, and the cycles of existence. It highlights the importance of gentle movement and transformation in the natural world, as well as the stability and nourishment that comes from following a higher path. In real life, this can be applied to personal growth, where individuals are encouraged to embrace change and seek stability through nurturing relationships and environments. It serves as a reminder that, like the cycles in nature, our lives are also a series of transformations that lead to greater understanding and fulfillment.
