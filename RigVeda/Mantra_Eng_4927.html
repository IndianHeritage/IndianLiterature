विष्वे देवाः ऋतावृद्ध ऋतुभिः हवनश्रुतः |जुषन्तां युज्यम् पयः ||
viśve devā ṛtāvṛdha ṛtubhir havanaśrutaḥ | juṣantāṁ yujyam payaḥ ||
  • Translation: "All the gods, who are nourished by the truth, listen to our invocations; may they, who are united, enjoy the offerings."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • viśve devā: Refers to "all the gods," indicating a collective of divine beings.
    • ṛtāvṛdha: Means "nourished by truth," emphasizing the importance of truth and righteousness in the divine realm.
    • ṛtubhir: Translates to "by the seasons," suggesting a connection to the natural cycles and the order of the universe.
    • havanaśrutaḥ: Means "listening to our invocations," indicating that the gods are attentive to human prayers and offerings.
    • juṣantāṁ: Translates to "may they enjoy," expressing a desire for the gods to partake in the offerings made by humans.
    • yujyam payaḥ: Means "united, enjoy the offerings," highlighting the idea of unity and shared enjoyment between humans and the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals and prayers are performed to invoke the blessings of deities, similar to how this mantra calls upon the gods to listen and partake in offerings.
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate a festival, they often share food and offerings, symbolizing unity and the joy of sharing with the divine.
    • In personal practices, individuals may meditate or pray, seeking truth and righteousness in their lives, aligning with the idea of being nourished by truth.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of truth and righteousness in our lives and the importance of invoking the divine presence. It teaches us that the gods are attentive to our prayers and that there is a reciprocal relationship between humans and the divine. In real life, this can be applied through rituals, community gatherings, and personal practices that foster unity, truth, and a connection to something greater than ourselves. By recognizing the divine in our lives and striving for righteousness, we can create a harmonious existence that benefits both ourselves and the world around us.
