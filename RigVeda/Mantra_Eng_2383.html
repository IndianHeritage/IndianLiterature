जोष्य अग्नि समिधं जोष्य आहुतिं जोषि ब्रह्म जन्यं जोषि सुश्रुतिम् |विश्वेभिर विश्वाम् ऋतुनā वसो मह उषन् देवाम् उषतः पालयā हविः ||
joṣy agne samidhaṁ joṣy āhutiṁ joṣi brahma janyaṁ joṣi suṣṭutim | viśvebhir viśvām̐ ṛtunā vaso maha uśan devām̐ uśataḥ pāyayā haviḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • joṣy: to be pleased or satisfied
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • samidhaṁ: with the offerings
    • āhutiṁ: the oblation or sacrifice
    • brahma: the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit
    • janyaṁ: born of or generated
    • suṣṭutim: good praise or commendation
    • viśvebhir: with all beings
    • viśvām̐: all or universal
    • ṛtunā: with the seasons
    • vaso: O Vasu (a class of deities)
    • maha: great
    • uśan: O Usha (the dawn)
    • devām̐: O divine beings
    • uśataḥ: well-being or prosperity
    • pāyayā: may you nourish or sustain
    • haviḥ: the offering or sacrifice
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • It emphasizes the importance of offerings and sacrifices, suggesting that they should be made with the intention of pleasing the divine.
    • The mention of "brahma" indicates a connection to the ultimate reality, highlighting the spiritual significance of the offerings.
    • By invoking "viśvebhir viśvām̐," the mantra acknowledges the interconnectedness of all beings and the universe.
    • The reference to "ṛtunā" signifies the cyclical nature of life and the importance of aligning with the seasons and natural rhythms.
    • It calls upon the divine beings to bestow well-being and prosperity, indicating a desire for harmony and abundance in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, lighting a fire during ceremonies symbolizes the presence of Agni, representing purification and the connection to the divine.
    • Offering food or other items in a ritual can be seen as a way to express gratitude and seek blessings, similar to the oblations mentioned in the mantra.
    • In agricultural practices, farmers often pray for favorable seasons, reflecting the importance of "ṛtunā" in ensuring a good harvest.
    • Community gatherings that focus on shared well-being and prosperity resonate with the mantra's call for universal harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of offerings, the interconnectedness of all beings, and the importance of aligning with natural rhythms.
    • It serves as a reminder to cultivate gratitude, seek harmony, and recognize the divine presence in everyday life.
    • In a world that often feels disconnected, the teachings of this mantra encourage us to foster community, respect nature, and strive for collective well-being.
