याभिर नराः त्रसदस्युम आवतं कृत्व्ये धनैः |ताभिः स्व अ1स्मां अश्विनाः प्रावतम् वाजसातये ||
yābhir narā trasadasyum āvataṁ kṛtvye dhane | tābhiḥ ṣv a1smām̐ aśvinā prāvataṁ vājasātaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yābhir: By whom
    • narā: Men or people
    • trasadasyum: Who are frightened or distressed
    • āvataṁ: Protecting or sheltering
    • kṛtvye: For the purpose of doing
    • dhane: Wealth or riches
    • tābhiḥ: By them
    • ṣv: Our
    • a1smām̐: In us
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin deities)
    • prāvataṁ: Bestow or grant
    • vājasātaye: For the purpose of strength or vigor
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Ashvins, the twin deities known for their healing and protective qualities.
    • It emphasizes the need for protection and support in times of distress, particularly in relation to wealth and prosperity.
    • The mantra suggests that by invoking the Ashvins, one can seek refuge and strength to overcome fears and challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of financial uncertainty, individuals may recite this mantra to seek divine support and protection over their wealth.
    • During challenging situations, such as health crises or personal struggles, people may turn to this mantra for comfort and strength.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be used in rituals to invoke collective strength and protection for the well-being of all members.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine intervention in times of fear and distress, particularly concerning material wealth and overall well-being.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking support from higher powers during difficult times, reinforcing the belief in the protective and healing aspects of the divine.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in personal prayers, community gatherings, and rituals aimed at fostering resilience and prosperity.
