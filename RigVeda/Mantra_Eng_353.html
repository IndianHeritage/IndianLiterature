त्वं अग्नि प्रथमो मातरिश्वना आवीर भव सुक्रतूयां विवस्वते |अरेजेतां रोधसी होत्रिवूर्येऽसघ्नोर भारं अयजो महो वसो ||
tvam agne prathamo mātariśvana āvir bhava sukratūyā vivasvate | arejetāṁ rodasī hotṛvūrye 'saghnor bhāram ayajo maho vaso ||
  • tvam agne: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni." Agni is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, light, and transformation. In real life, Agni can be seen as a metaphor for the inner fire or passion that drives individuals to achieve their goals.
  • prathamo mātariśvana: This means "the first among the Mātariśvan." Mātariśvan refers to the winds or the life force. This highlights the importance of Agni as a primary force in the universe, akin to how the wind is essential for life. In our lives, this can be related to the importance of foundational elements that support our existence.
  • āvir bhava: This translates to "manifest yourself." It is a call for Agni to reveal his presence. In a practical sense, this can be seen as a plea for clarity and guidance in our lives, urging us to seek out our true purpose and potential.
  • sukratūyā vivasvate: This means "for the well-doers, for the radiant one." It emphasizes the idea that those who act with good intentions and righteousness will be favored. In real life, this can be related to the concept of karma, where good deeds lead to positive outcomes.
  • arejetāṁ rodasī: This translates to "may the two worlds be pleased." It signifies a balance between the earthly and the spiritual realms. In our lives, this can be interpreted as the need to maintain harmony between our material pursuits and spiritual growth.
  • hotṛvūrye: This means "in the strength of the priest." It refers to the power of rituals and prayers. In modern life, this can be seen in the importance of community and shared beliefs that strengthen our resolve and purpose.
  • 'saghnor bhāram: This translates to "the burden of the strong." It suggests that strength comes with responsibility. In real life, this can be related to leaders and individuals who bear the weight of their decisions and actions on their shoulders.
  • ayajo maho vaso: This means "may we not be deprived of great wealth." It is a prayer for abundance and prosperity. In practical terms, this can be interpreted as a desire for success and fulfillment in various aspects of life, including health, relationships, and career.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of Agni as a transformative force in our lives. It calls for clarity, righteousness, balance, and the strength to bear responsibilities. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to pursue our passions, act with good intentions, maintain harmony between our material and spiritual lives, and seek abundance while being mindful of our responsibilities. By embodying these principles, we can navigate our lives with purpose and integrity.
