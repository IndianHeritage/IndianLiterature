यो रायोऽवनिर्महान् सुपारः सुन्वतः सखा |तम इन्द्रम् अभि गायत ||
yo rāyo3 'vanir mahān supāraḥ sunvataḥ sakhā | tam indram abhi gāyata ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yo: who
    • rāyo: of wealth or prosperity
    • avanir: the one who brings or bestows
    • mahān: great
    • supāraḥ: well-guarded or well-protected
    • sunvataḥ: of those who listen or hear
    • sakhā: friend or companion
    • tam: that
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods
    • abhi: towards
    • gāyata: sing or chant
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of a great benefactor who brings wealth and prosperity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of friendship and companionship in achieving success.
    • The invocation of Indra suggests a call for divine support and blessings in one's endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied when seeking partnerships that enhance growth and prosperity.
    • In personal relationships, it highlights the value of having supportive friends who contribute to one’s success.
    • During community gatherings or events, invoking the spirit of cooperation and collective effort can lead to greater achievements.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of wealth, friendship, and divine support.
    • It encourages individuals to seek out and nurture relationships that foster mutual growth and prosperity.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more fulfilling and successful existence, both personally and professionally.
