यो न इन्दुः पितरो हृत्सु पीतो 'मर्त्यो मर्त्यां आविवेश |तस्मै सोमाय हविषा विद्हेम मृळीके अस्य सुमतौ स्याम ||
yo na induḥ pitaro hṛtsu pīto 'martyo martyām̐ āviveśa | tasmai somāya haviṣā vidhema mṛḻīke asya sumatau syāma ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • na: not
    • induḥ: the moon or soma (nectar)
    • pitaro: fathers or ancestors
    • hṛtsu: in the hearts
    • pīto: having drunk
    • 'martyo: the mortal
    • martyām̐: into mortality
    • āviveśa: entered
    • tasmai: to that
    • somāya: to the soma
    • haviṣā: with offerings
    • vidhema: we may invoke
    • mṛḻīke: for prosperity
    • asya: of this
    • sumatau: with good thoughts
    • syāma: may we be
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the connection between the divine (soma) and the mortal realm.
    • It emphasizes the importance of ancestors and the wisdom they impart to us.
    • The act of drinking soma symbolizes the absorption of divine knowledge and blessings.
    • It suggests that through offerings and good intentions, we can invoke prosperity and wisdom in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, honoring ancestors through rituals is a way to seek guidance and blessings. For instance, during the Day of the Dead in Mexico, families create altars to remember and celebrate their deceased loved ones.
    • In modern life, the concept of "soma" can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment through education and self-improvement. Just as the mantra suggests invoking prosperity, individuals often seek mentors or role models to guide them.
    • Incorporating mindfulness and positive intentions in daily life can lead to better decision-making and overall well-being, reflecting the essence of "sumatau syāma" (may we be with good thoughts).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of connecting with our roots and the wisdom of our ancestors.
    • It teaches us that through reverence, offerings, and positive intentions, we can invite prosperity and wisdom into our lives.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages us to cultivate a mindset of gratitude and mindfulness, which can lead to a fulfilling and enriched life.
