नकीष ṭं कर्मणा नशद यश चकार सदावृधम् | इन्द्रं न यज्ञैर विश्वगूर्तम् ऋभ्वसम अधृष्टं धृष्णवोजसम ||
nakiṣ ṭaṁ karmaṇā naśad yaś cakāra sadāvṛdham | indraṁ na yajñair viśvagūrtam ṛbhvasam adhṛṣṭaṁ dhṛṣṇvojasam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nakiṣ: Not (negative particle)
    • ṭaṁ: That (referring to actions or deeds)
    • karmaṇā: By actions or deeds
    • naśad: Not (again a negative particle)
    • yaś: Fame or glory
    • cakāra: Created or made
    • sadāvṛdham: Ever-increasing or always flourishing
    • indraṁ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • na: Not
    • yajñair: By sacrifices or offerings
    • viśvagūrtam: All-pervading or universal
    • ṛbhvasam: Strong or powerful
    • adhṛṣṭaṁ: Unseen or invisible
    • dhṛṣṇvojasam: With strength and valor
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of actions and their outcomes.
    • It suggests that mere actions without the right intention or purpose do not lead to lasting fame or success.
    • Indra, as a symbol of strength and power, represents the ideal qualities one should aspire to embody.
    • The mantra also highlights that true strength and valor come from unseen efforts and sacrifices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an employee may work hard (karmaṇā) but if their actions are not aligned with the organization's goals (yaś), they may not achieve recognition (naśad yaś).
    • A student may study diligently but if they do not understand the subject deeply (sadāvṛdham), their efforts may not yield the desired results.
    • In sports, an athlete may train rigorously, but without the right mindset and strategy (adhṛṣṭaṁ), they may not perform well in competitions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra teaches us that actions must be purposeful and aligned with our goals to achieve true success and recognition.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate inner strength and resilience, which often goes unnoticed but is crucial for achieving greatness.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various fields such as education, career, and personal development, reminding us that the journey and intention behind our actions are as important as the actions themselves.
