अवीराम इव मां अयं शरारुर अभि मन्यते |उताहम अस्मि वीरिणीन्द्रपत्नी मरुत्सखा विश्वस्माद इन्द्र उत्तरः ||
avīrām iva mām ayaṁ śarārur abhi manyate | utāham asmi vīriṇīndrapatnī marutsakhā viśvasmād indra uttaraḥ ||
  • Mantra: "avīrām iva mām ayaṁ śarārur abhi manyate | utāham asmi vīriṇīndrapatnī marutsakhā viśvasmād indra uttaraḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • avīrām: not to be restrained or held back
    • iva: like or as
    • mām: me or my
    • ayaṁ: this
    • śarārur: one who is armed or equipped
    • abhi: towards or against
    • manyate: thinks or considers
    • utāham: and I
    • asmi: am
    • vīriṇīndrapatnī: the wife of the powerful Indra
    • marutsakhā: friend of the Maruts (storm deities)
    • viśvasmād: from all or universal
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • uttaraḥ: superior or higher
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for strength and recognition. The speaker feels as if they are being considered as someone who is not to be restrained, akin to a warrior ready for battle.
    • The reference to "vīriṇīndrapatnī" suggests a connection to divine power and protection, indicating that the speaker seeks the blessings of Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, who is often associated with strength and valor.
    • The mention of "marutsakhā" highlights the importance of friendship and alliances in achieving one's goals, suggesting that collaboration with powerful forces can lead to success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive work environment, an individual may feel the need to assert their capabilities and seek recognition from their superiors, much like the speaker in the mantra.
    • In sports, athletes often draw upon the strength of their teammates and coaches, paralleling the idea of seeking support from powerful allies to achieve victory.
    • In personal challenges, individuals may invoke their inner strength and seek guidance from mentors or spiritual figures, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of self-assertion, divine support, and the value of alliances in overcoming challenges.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that one should not shy away from seeking strength and recognition, while also valuing the relationships that empower us.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as career advancement, personal growth, and teamwork, encouraging individuals to embrace their potential and seek the support of others.
