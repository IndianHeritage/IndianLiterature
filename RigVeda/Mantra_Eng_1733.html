अवः परेण पितरं यो अस्यानुवेद पर एनावरेण |कवियमानः का इह प्र वोचद देवम् मनः कुतो अधि प्रजातम् ||
avaḥ pareṇa pitaraṁ yo asyānuveda para enāvareṇa | kavīyamānaḥ ka iha pra vocad devam manaḥ kuto adhi prajātam ||
  • avaḥ pareṇa pitaraṁ: This phrase can be translated as "May we invoke the supreme father." Here, "avaḥ" means to invoke or call upon, "pareṇa" refers to the supreme or higher, and "pitaraṁ" means father. In a real-life context, this can be seen as a call for guidance and support from a higher power or parental figure, emphasizing the importance of seeking wisdom from those who have more experience.
  • yo asyānuveda para enāvareṇa: This translates to "who knows the secrets of the universe." "Yo" means who, "asyānuveda" refers to the knowledge or secrets of this world, and "para enāvareṇa" implies the higher or transcendent knowledge. In practical terms, this can relate to the pursuit of knowledge and understanding in our lives, whether through education, spirituality, or personal experiences.
  • kavīyamānaḥ: This word means "the one who is wise or learned." It emphasizes the value of wisdom and knowledge in navigating life. In real life, this can be seen in the respect given to teachers, mentors, and elders who guide us through challenges.
  • ka iha pra vocad devam: This translates to "who can speak of the divine here?" "Ka" means who, "iha" means here, "pra vocad" means can speak, and "devam" refers to the divine or god. This line reflects the idea that understanding the divine or spiritual truths is a profound challenge, often requiring deep contemplation and insight.
  • manaḥ kuto adhi prajātam: This means "from where does the mind arise?" "Manaḥ" refers to the mind, "kuto" means from where, and "adhi prajātam" implies the source of creation or existence. This line invites us to reflect on the origins of our thoughts and consciousness, encouraging introspection and self-awareness.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound inquiry into the nature of existence, knowledge, and the divine. It emphasizes the importance of seeking wisdom from higher sources, understanding the secrets of the universe, and reflecting on the origins of our thoughts. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as education, spirituality, and personal growth. By invoking higher wisdom and contemplating our existence, we can navigate life's challenges with greater clarity and purpose. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, spirituality, and the quest for understanding in our lives.
