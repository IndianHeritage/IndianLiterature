अङ्गिरोभिर आगहि यज्ञियेबिर यम वैरूपैर इह मादयस्व |विवस्वन्तं हुवे यः पिता तेऽस्मिन यज्ञे बरहिष्य आ निषद्य ||
aṅgirobhir ā gahi yajñiyebhir yama vairūpair iha mādayasva | vivasvantaṁ huve yaḥ pitā te 'smin yajñe barhiṣy ā niṣadya ||
  • Mantra: "aṅgirobhir ā gahi yajñiyebhir yama vairūpair iha mādayasva | vivasvantaṁ huve yaḥ pitā te 'smin yajñe barhiṣy ā niṣadya ||"
  • Translation: "With the Angirasas, come here, O Yama, with the offerings of the Yajnas, be pleased with us. I invoke Vivasvant, who is your father, to sit here in this Yajna."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • aṅgirobhir: Referring to the Angirasas, a group of sages known for their wisdom and knowledge.
    • ā gahi: "Come here" or "approach us," indicating a call for divine presence.
    • yajñiyebhir: "With the offerings of the Yajnas," referring to the sacred rituals performed to please the deities.
    • yama: The god of death and the underworld, representing the transition from life to afterlife.
    • vairūpair: "With the Vairūpa," which can refer to the various forms of divine manifestations.
    • iha: "Here," indicating the physical space where the ritual is taking place.
    • mādayasva: "Be pleased with us," a request for divine favor and blessings.
    • vivasvantaṁ: Referring to the sun god, often associated with enlightenment and knowledge.
    • huve: "I invoke," a declaration of calling upon the divine.
    • yaḥ: "Who," referring to the subject of the invocation.
    • pitā: "Father," indicating a familial relationship with the divine.
    • te: "Your," indicating possession or relationship.
    • 'smin: "In this," referring to the context of the Yajna.
    • yajñe: "In the Yajna," emphasizing the ritualistic aspect.
    • barhiṣy: "On the sacred grass," a reference to the traditional setting of Yajnas.
    • ā niṣadya: "To sit down," indicating a request for the divine to take a seat in the ritual.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of rituals (Yajnas) in connecting with the divine and seeking blessings.
    • It highlights the relationship between humans and deities, where humans invoke divine presence for guidance and support.
    • The invocation of Yama and Vivasvant signifies the acknowledgment of life, death, and the cyclical nature of existence.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to honor our ancestors and seek wisdom from those who came before us.
    • It encourages individuals to engage in rituals or practices that foster a sense of community and connection with the divine.
    • The themes of life, death, and the importance of knowledge can be applied in personal growth, decision-making, and understanding the transient nature of existence.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of rituals, and the need for divine guidance.
    • By invoking the divine through rituals, individuals can seek blessings, wisdom, and a deeper understanding of their place in the universe.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a respectful and mindful approach to life, death, and the pursuit of knowledge.
