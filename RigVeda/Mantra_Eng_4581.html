मन्तर: नृवत त इन्द्र नृतमाभिर ऊती वंसीमहि वामं श्रोमतेभिः |ईक्षे हि वस्व उभयस्य राजन धा रत्नं महि स्थूरम् बृहन्तम् ||
nṛvat ta indra nṛtamābhir ūtī vaṁsīmahi vāmaṁ śromatebhiḥ | īkṣe hi vasva ubhayasya rājan dhā ratnam mahi sthūram bṛhantam ||
  • nṛvat: This term can be interpreted as "human" or "mankind." It emphasizes the connection between humans and the divine, suggesting that the actions and intentions of humans are significant in the cosmic order.
  • ta indra: "Indra" is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. The phrase can be understood as a call to Indra, invoking his power and presence.
  • nṛtamābhir ūtī: This can be translated as "with the truth of mankind." It suggests that the essence of humanity is rooted in truthfulness and righteousness, which is essential for harmony in the universe.
  • vaṁsīmahi: This term means "we aspire" or "we seek." It indicates a collective desire or aspiration among people, highlighting the importance of community and shared goals.
  • vāmaṁ: This word translates to "pleasant" or "good." It signifies the pursuit of goodness and positivity in life, which is essential for personal and communal well-being.
  • śromatebhiḥ: This can be interpreted as "with our efforts" or "through our actions." It emphasizes the importance of hard work and dedication in achieving one's goals.
  • īkṣe hi vasva: This phrase means "look upon everything." It encourages a holistic view of life, urging individuals to be aware of their surroundings and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • ubhayasya rājan: "Rājan" means "king" or "ruler." This phrase can be understood as a call to the ruler of both the material and spiritual realms, suggesting that true leadership encompasses both aspects.
  • dhā ratnam mahi: This translates to "bear the jewel of the earth." It signifies the importance of valuing and nurturing the resources and treasures that the earth provides.
  • sthūram bṛhantam: "Sthūram" means "stable" or "firm," and "bṛhantam" means "great" or "vast." Together, they suggest the need for a strong foundation in life, both materially and spiritually.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of human existence, emphasizing truth, community, goodness, and the importance of leadership. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to various aspects such as governance, where leaders are encouraged to act with integrity and for the welfare of their people. It also highlights the significance of collective aspirations and efforts in achieving common goals, reminding us that our actions should be rooted in truth and positivity. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for individuals and communities to strive for a harmonious and prosperous life, respecting the interconnectedness of all beings and the resources of the earth.
