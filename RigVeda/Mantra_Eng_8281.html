वाचो जन्तुः कवीनाम् पवस्व सोम धारणया |देवेषु रत्नधा असि ||
vāco jantuḥ kavīnām pavasva soma dhārayā | deveṣu ratnadhā asi ||
  • vāco: This word translates to "speech" or "words." It signifies the power of communication and expression.
  • jantuḥ: Meaning "creature" or "being," it refers to all living entities, emphasizing the interconnectedness of life.
  • kavīnām: This term means "of the poets" or "of the wise." It highlights the role of wisdom and creativity in understanding the world.
  • pavasva: Translated as "may you purify" or "may you cleanse," it suggests a call for purification, both of the mind and spirit.
  • soma: Referring to the sacred drink in Vedic tradition, soma symbolizes inspiration, vitality, and divine knowledge.
  • dhārayā: This means "holding" or "sustaining," indicating the importance of maintaining and nurturing wisdom and inspiration.
  • deveṣu: This word translates to "among the gods," suggesting a connection to the divine and the higher realms of existence.
  • ratnadhā: Meaning "possessor of jewels," it signifies the treasures of knowledge and wisdom that one can attain.
  • asi: This translates to "you are," affirming the existence and presence of these qualities in the divine and in ourselves.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for the purification of speech and thought, encouraging beings to seek wisdom and inspiration from the divine. It emphasizes the importance of communication and creativity in our lives, urging us to connect with the higher aspects of existence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Communication: In our daily interactions, the mantra reminds us to speak with clarity and intention, fostering understanding and connection.
  • Creativity: For artists, writers, and thinkers, it serves as an inspiration to tap into their inner wisdom and express it through their work.
  • Personal Growth: The call for purification encourages individuals to reflect on their thoughts and actions, striving for self-improvement and spiritual growth.
  • Community Building: In group settings, such as workshops or discussions, the mantra can inspire collective wisdom and collaboration, leading to richer outcomes.

In conclusion, this mantra holds significant value in promoting the virtues of wisdom, creativity, and purification in our lives. By invoking these qualities, we can enhance our communication, foster personal growth, and connect with the divine, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
