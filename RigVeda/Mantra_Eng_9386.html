अभीष्व आर्यः पौंस्यैर भवेम द्यौर न भूमिं गिरयो नाज्रान् |तां नो विश्वानि जरिता चिकेट परातरं सु निरृतिर जिहीताम् ||
abhī ṣv a1ryaḥ pauṁsyair bhavema dyaur na bhūmiṁ girayo nājrān | tā no viśvāni jaritā ciketa parātaraṁ su nirṛtir jihītām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhī: towards, in the direction of
    • ṣv: may we, let us
    • a1ryaḥ: noble, honorable
    • pauṁsyair: by the power of manliness, strength
    • bhavema: may we become, may we exist
    • dyaur: the sky, heaven
    • na: not
    • bhūmiṁ: the earth
    • girayo: mountains
    • nājrān: not shaken, not disturbed
    • tā: those
    • no: our
    • viśvāni: all, universal
    • jaritā: the one who brings forth, creator
    • ciketa: may he understand, may he perceive
    • parātaraṁ: the highest, supreme
    • su: good, well
    • nirṛtir: misfortune, calamity
    • jihītām: may it be removed, may it be dispelled
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a desire for strength and nobility, urging individuals to strive for greatness.
    • It emphasizes the importance of stability and resilience, both in the physical realm (earth and mountains) and in the spiritual or mental realm (heaven).
    • The invocation of the creator suggests a connection to a higher power, seeking guidance and understanding from the universe.
    • It also highlights the need to overcome misfortunes and challenges, promoting a mindset of positivity and resilience.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often seek to cultivate strength and resilience in the face of adversity, much like the mantra suggests.
    • In leadership, the qualities of nobility and strength are essential for guiding teams through challenges, ensuring stability and progress.
    • In community building, fostering a sense of unity and strength can help overcome societal challenges, echoing the mantra's call for collective resilience.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of strength, nobility, and resilience in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek higher understanding and to work towards dispelling negativity and misfortune.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in various aspects of life, including personal growth, leadership, and community engagement, promoting a holistic approach to overcoming challenges.
