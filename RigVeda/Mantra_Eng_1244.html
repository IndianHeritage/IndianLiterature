उषो यद अग्निं समिधे चकर्थ विह यद आवश चक्षसा सूर्यस्य |यन् मानवशान यक्ष्यमाणां अजिगास तद देवेषु चक्रिषे भद्रम अप्नः ||
uṣo yad agniṁ samidhe cakartha vi yad āvaś cakṣasā sūryasya | yan mānuṣān yakṣyamāṇām̐ ajīgas tad deveṣu cakṛṣe bhadram apnaḥ ||
  • uṣo: This word refers to the dawn or the early morning light, symbolizing new beginnings and the dispelling of darkness.
  • yad: This means "that" or "which," indicating a connection or a cause.
  • agniṁ: This translates to "fire," representing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • samidhe: This means "when it is kindled," suggesting the act of igniting or awakening something within us.
  • cakartha: This means "you have made" or "you have created," indicating an action taken by a divine force.
  • vi: This is a prefix that can mean "in" or "with," often used to denote a relationship.
  • yad āvaś: This phrase means "that which is necessary" or "that which is essential," pointing to the importance of certain elements in life.
  • cakṣasā: This translates to "with vision" or "with sight," emphasizing the importance of perception and awareness.
  • sūryasya: This means "of the sun," symbolizing light, knowledge, and life-giving energy.
  • yan: This means "that which," referring to something specific that is being highlighted.
  • mānuṣān: This translates to "of humans," indicating the focus on human beings and their experiences.
  • yakṣyamāṇām̐: This means "being offered" or "being sacrificed," suggesting the act of giving or dedicating something valuable.
  • ajīgas: This translates to "to attain" or "to achieve," indicating a goal or aspiration.
  • tad: This means "that," referring back to the previous elements mentioned.
  • deveṣu: This means "among the gods," indicating a connection to the divine or higher powers.
  • cakṛṣe: This translates to "you have created" or "you have made," again emphasizing the act of creation.
  • bhadram: This means "auspicious" or "good," indicating positive outcomes or blessings.
  • apnaḥ: This translates to "for us" or "to us," suggesting a personal connection to the blessings being sought.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to the divine forces, particularly the dawn and the sun, to bring forth auspiciousness and blessings into our lives. It emphasizes the importance of fire as a transformative element, the necessity of vision and awareness, and the connection between human efforts and divine support.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Starting a new project or venture at dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and the hope for success.
  • Engaging in rituals or practices that involve fire, such as lighting candles or performing havans, to invoke positive energy.
  • Seeking clarity and vision in decision-making processes, ensuring that actions are aligned with higher purposes.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine blessings. It encourages individuals to seek auspiciousness and clarity in their lives, fostering a sense of purpose and direction. By invoking the energies of the dawn and the sun, one can aspire to achieve their goals while remaining grounded in the support of the divine.
