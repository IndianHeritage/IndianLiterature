शृणुतं जारितुर हवं कृष्णस्य स्तुवतो नराः |मध्वः सोमस्य पीतये ||
śṛṇutaṁ jaritur havaṁ kṛṣṇasya stuvato narā | madhvaḥ somasya pītaye ||
  • śṛṇutaṁ: This word translates to "listen" or "hear." It emphasizes the importance of being attentive and receptive to divine messages or teachings.
  • jaritur: This term refers to "the one who is born" or "the one who ages." It signifies the transient nature of life and the inevitability of aging and death.
  • havaṁ: This word means "offering" or "sacrifice." It indicates the act of giving or dedicating something to the divine, which is a common practice in many cultures.
  • kṛṣṇasya: This refers to "of Krishna," a significant deity in Hinduism known for his wisdom, compassion, and playful nature. Krishna represents the divine aspect of love and guidance.
  • stuvato: This means "of those who praise" or "of the worshippers." It highlights the importance of devotion and the act of praising the divine.
  • narā: This translates to "men" or "humans." It signifies the human condition and the collective experience of life.
  • madhvaḥ: This word means "sweet" or "pleasant." It often refers to the sweetness of life, joy, and the divine nectar of existence.
  • somasya: This refers to "of Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition that symbolizes immortality and divine bliss.
  • pītaye: This means "for drinking" or "to be consumed." It indicates the act of partaking in the divine essence or blessings.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to listen to the teachings of Krishna, acknowledging the transient nature of life while engaging in acts of devotion and praise. It encourages humans to offer their lives and actions to the divine, seeking the sweetness and bliss that comes from spiritual connection.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Mindfulness and Awareness: Just as the mantra begins with "listen," it serves as a reminder to be present and aware in our daily lives. For example, practicing mindfulness during meditation or while engaging in conversations can enhance our understanding and connection with others.
  • Acts of Kindness: The concept of offering (havaṁ) can be translated into acts of kindness and service. Volunteering at a local charity or helping a neighbor in need reflects the essence of giving and devotion.
  • Celebrating Life: Acknowledging the sweetness of life (madhvaḥ) can be seen in how we celebrate milestones, such as birthdays or anniversaries, by expressing gratitude and joy with loved ones.
  • Spiritual Practices: Engaging in spiritual practices, such as prayer or rituals that honor deities like Krishna, can deepen one's connection to the divine and provide a sense of purpose and fulfillment.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of listening, devotion, and the transient nature of life. It encourages individuals to engage in acts of kindness, celebrate the sweetness of existence, and seek a deeper connection with the divine. By applying these principles in our daily lives, we can cultivate a more meaningful and fulfilling existence.
