ईशानकृतो धुनयो ऋशादसो वातान विद्युतस्तविषीभिराकृतः |दुहन्त्यूदहर दिव्यानि धूतयो भूमिम पिन्वन्ति पयसा परिज्रयः ||
īśānakṛto dhunayo riśādaso vātān vidyutas taviṣībhir akrata | duhanty ūdhar divyāni dhūtayo bhūmim pinvanti payasā parijrayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • īśāna: Lord or Master
    • kṛto: Created or made
    • dhunayo: The forces or energies
    • riśādaso: Of the powerful or strong
    • vātān: Winds or air
    • vidyutas: Lightning or illumination
    • taviṣībhir: With great strength or power
    • akrata: Not created or not made
    • duhanty: They churn or extract
    • ūdhar: From the depths or the ocean
    • divyāni: Divine or celestial
    • dhūtayo: Purified or cleansed
    • bhūmim: The earth
    • pinvanti: They nourish or sustain
    • payasā: With milk or nourishment
    • parijrayaḥ: By the surrounding or encompassing
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine forces that govern nature, particularly the winds and lightning, which are seen as manifestations of the Lord's power.
    • The phrase "īśānakṛto" suggests that these forces are created by a higher power, emphasizing the connection between the divine and the natural world.
    • The winds and lightning are described as strong and powerful, indicating their ability to influence the environment and life on earth.
    • The act of "duhanty ūdhar" refers to the process of churning, which can be likened to the way in which nature extracts resources from the earth, much like how we extract milk from cows.
    • The "divyāni dhūtayo" signifies the divine essence that purifies and nourishes the earth, suggesting that nature provides sustenance and life through its processes.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the forces of nature such as wind and rain are essential for crop growth. Farmers rely on these natural elements to nourish their fields, akin to the "payasā" mentioned in the mantra.
    • The concept of churning can be seen in the production of dairy products, where milk is churned to create butter. This process symbolizes the extraction of nourishment from nature.
    • In a broader sense, the mantra reflects the interconnectedness of all life forms and the importance of respecting and nurturing our environment, as it sustains us.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of recognizing the divine forces at play in nature and our dependence on them for sustenance and life.
    • It serves as a reminder to honor and respect the environment, understanding that our well-being is intricately linked to the health of the earth.
    • In modern life, this mantra can inspire sustainable practices and a deeper appreciation for the natural world, encouraging us to live in harmony with the forces that sustain us.
