यो वां नासत्याव ऋषिर गीरभिर वत्सो अवीवृधत् |तस्मै सहस्रनिर्णिजम् इषं धत्तं घृतश्चुतम् ||
yo vāṁ nāsatyāv ṛṣir gīrbhir vatso avīvṛdhat | tasmai sahasranirṇijam iṣaṁ dhattaṁ ghṛtaścutam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • vāṁ: your
    • nāsatyāv: of the Nāsatyas (the twin deities, often associated with healing and truth)
    • ṛṣir: sage or seer
    • gīrbhir: with hymns or words
    • vatso: calf or young one
    • avīvṛdhat: has been nourished or protected
    • tasmai: to him
    • sahasranirṇijam: thousand-fold or abundant
    • iṣaṁ: power or strength
    • dhattaṁ: may be given
    • ghṛtaścutam: clarified butter or ghee
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of the Nāsatyas, who are revered for their wisdom and healing abilities.
    • The reference to a "sage" suggests the importance of knowledge and spiritual insight in achieving one's goals.
    • The imagery of a "calf" signifies innocence and potential, indicating that nurturing the young leads to growth and prosperity.
    • The phrase "thousand-fold" emphasizes abundance and the idea that blessings can multiply when shared or given freely.
    • The mention of "clarified butter" symbolizes purity and nourishment, suggesting that the essence of life is found in purity and generosity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied to the nurturing of children, where the collective effort of the community leads to the growth of future generations.
    • In a business context, the idea of sharing resources and knowledge can lead to mutual growth and success, akin to the blessings of the Nāsatyas.
    • In personal development, seeking wisdom from mentors (the "sages") can help individuals realize their potential and achieve their goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom, nurturing, and the sharing of resources in achieving abundance and prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder that by seeking knowledge and supporting one another, we can create a thriving environment for ourselves and future generations.
    • In essence, it encourages a holistic approach to life, where spiritual, communal, and personal growth are interconnected.
