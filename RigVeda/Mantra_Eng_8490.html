अग्निर्न यो वन आ सृज्यमानो वृतः पाजांसी कृणुते नदिषु |जनो न युध्वा महत उपब्दिर इयर्ति सोमः पवमान ऊर्मिम् ||
agnir na yo vana ā sṛjyamāno vṛthā pājāṁsi kṛṇute nadīṣu | jano na yudhvā mahata upabdir iyarti somaḥ pavamāna ūrmim ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Agnir: Fire, representing energy and transformation.
    • na: Not.
    • yo: Who.
    • vana: Forest or wilderness, symbolizing nature.
    • ā sṛjyamāno: Being created or generated.
    • vṛthā: In vain or without purpose.
    • pājāṁsi: Offerings or sacrifices.
    • kṛṇute: Makes or creates.
    • nadīṣu: In the rivers, representing flow and life.
    • jano: People or community.
    • na yudhvā: Not fighting or engaging in conflict.
    • mahata: Great or significant.
    • upabdir: Disturbance or turmoil.
    • iyarti: Moves or flows.
    • somaḥ: Soma, a sacred drink symbolizing vitality and immortality.
    • pavamāna: Purifying or cleansing.
    • ūrmim: Waves or currents, indicating movement and change.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the essence of fire (Agnir) and its role in creation and transformation.
    • It emphasizes that actions taken without purpose (vṛthā) do not yield fruitful results, much like offerings made without sincerity.
    • The mention of rivers (nadīṣu) signifies the importance of flow and adaptability in life.
    • It highlights the need for communities (jano) to avoid conflict (na yudhvā) and instead seek harmony.
    • The reference to Soma suggests the pursuit of vitality and purity in our actions and intentions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team that works without a clear purpose may struggle to achieve its goals, similar to the offerings made in vain.
    • Environmental conservation efforts can be likened to the rivers, where maintaining the flow of natural resources is crucial for sustainability.
    • In personal relationships, avoiding unnecessary conflicts and focusing on understanding can lead to a more harmonious existence.
    • Practices like meditation and yoga can be seen as ways to purify the mind and body, akin to the cleansing aspect of Soma.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of intention and purpose in our actions.
    • It encourages individuals and communities to seek harmony and avoid conflict, promoting a peaceful coexistence.
    • By embracing the principles of transformation, flow, and purity, we can lead more meaningful and fulfilling lives.
    • Ultimately, the mantra highlights the interconnectedness of all beings and the significance of living in alignment with nature and each other.
