प्रत्यङ् देवानां विशः प्रत्यङ् उदेषि मानवशान् |प्रत्यङ् विश्वं स्वर दृष्टे ||
pratyaṅ devānāṁ viśaḥ pratyaṅṅ ud eṣi mānuṣān | pratyaṅ viśvaṁ svar dṛśe ||
  • Mantra: "pratyaṅ devānāṁ viśaḥ pratyaṅṅ ud eṣi mānuṣān | pratyaṅ viśvaṁ svar dṛśe ||"
  • Translation: "The divine beings (devas) are present in all directions; they manifest themselves to humans. The entire universe is visible in the light of the divine."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • pratyaṅ: In all directions or everywhere.
    • devānāṁ: Of the divine beings or gods.
    • viśaḥ: The universe or the world.
    • ud eṣi: To manifest or to reveal.
    • mānuṣān: To humans or mankind.
    • viśvaṁ: The entire or all.
    • svar: Light or divine light.
    • dṛśe: To see or to perceive.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we often see the manifestation of divine qualities in the beauty of a sunset or the complexity of a flower. These can be seen as reflections of the divine in our world.
    • In moments of inspiration or creativity, many people feel a connection to something greater than themselves, which can be interpreted as the divine revealing itself to them.
    • In various cultures, rituals and prayers are performed to invoke the presence of divine beings, acknowledging their omnipresence and seeking guidance or blessings.

  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the omnipresence of the divine and encourages individuals to recognize the divine in their surroundings and within themselves.
    • It serves as a reminder to be aware of the interconnectedness of all beings and the universe, fostering a sense of unity and respect for all forms of life.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can inspire mindfulness practices, encouraging individuals to see the beauty and divinity in everyday life, thus enhancing their spiritual awareness and connection to the world.
