अस्मभ्यम् इन्दव इन्द्रयुर् मध्वः पवस्व धारणया |पर्जन्यो वृष्टिमाम् इव ||
asmabhyam indav indrayur madhvaḥ pavasva dhārayā | parjanyo vṛṣṭimām̐ iva ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmabhyam: For us
    • indav: O Indra (the god of rain and thunder)
    • indrayur: O Indra, the one who gives life
    • madhvaḥ: Sweetness or pleasantness
    • pavasva: Bestow or grant
    • dhārayā: With support or sustenance
    • parjanyo: O Parjanya (the rain god)
    • vṛṣṭimām̐: The rain or downpour
    • iva: Like or as
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Indra and Parjanya, seeking their blessings for abundant rain and sustenance. It emphasizes the importance of rain for life and prosperity, invoking the deities to shower their blessings upon us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain during the planting season, as adequate rainfall is crucial for crop growth. This mantra reflects that sentiment, highlighting the dependence on divine forces for agricultural success.
    • In many cultures, rituals and prayers are performed during droughts to invoke rain. This mantra can be recited in such contexts to seek the favor of the rain gods.
    • Communities often come together to celebrate the arrival of the monsoon season, recognizing its significance for water supply and food security. This mantra encapsulates that communal hope and gratitude.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of the vital role of rain in sustaining life. It serves as a reminder of our dependence on natural elements and the divine for our survival and prosperity. In real-life scenarios, it can be applicable in agricultural practices, community rituals, and environmental awareness, emphasizing the need for harmony with nature and gratitude for its blessings.
