प्र ह्यच्छा मनिषाः स्पार्हा यन्ति नियुतः |प्र दसरा नियुद्रथः पूषा अविष्टु माहिनः ||
pra hy acchā manīṣāḥ spārhā yanti niyutaḥ | pra dasrā niyudrathaḥ pūṣā aviṣṭu māhinaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: forward, towards
    • hy: indeed
    • acchā: good, auspicious
    • manīṣāḥ: thoughts, intentions
    • spārhā: desires, aspirations
    • yanti: go, move towards
    • niyutaḥ: in a disciplined manner, with control
    • pra: forward, towards
    • dasrā: the one who sees, the seer
    • niyudrathaḥ: with disciplined strength
    • pūṣā: the nourishing one, often referring to the Sun
    • aviṣṭu: not disturbed, stable
    • māhinaḥ: of the great, the powerful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of good intentions and disciplined aspirations.
    • It emphasizes that when one has positive thoughts and desires, they move forward in a controlled and purposeful manner.
    • The reference to "the seer" and "the nourishing one" suggests that wisdom and sustenance come from a stable and powerful source.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when individuals set clear and positive goals (good intentions) and work towards them with discipline, they are more likely to achieve success.
    • In personal development, maintaining a positive mindset and focusing on constructive desires can lead to better mental health and overall well-being.
    • In community service, when people come together with a shared vision and disciplined efforts, they can create significant positive change.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of aligning one's thoughts and desires with positive intentions and disciplined actions.
    • It serves as a reminder that stability and nourishment in life come from a clear vision and purposeful movement towards one's goals.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, professional success, and community development.
