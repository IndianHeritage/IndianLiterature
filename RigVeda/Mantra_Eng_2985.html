देवास त्वष्टा सविता विश्वरूपः पूपोष प्रजाः पुरुधा जजाना |इमा च विश्वा भुवनानि अस्य महद देवानाम असुरत्वम् एकम् ||
devas tvaṣṭā savitā viśvarūpaḥ pupoṣa prajāḥ purudhā jajāna | imā ca viśvā bhuvanāny asya mahad devānām asuratvam ekam ||
  • Devas: This term refers to the divine beings or gods in Vedic literature. In a real-life context, it can symbolize the higher ideals or aspirations that guide human behavior.
  • Tvaṣṭā: Tvaṣṭā is the divine craftsman or the creator. This can be likened to the role of an artist or a skilled professional in society who shapes and creates new realities.
  • Savitā: Savitā is the sun god, representing energy, life, and illumination. In everyday life, this can be seen as the source of inspiration and vitality that drives individuals to achieve their goals.
  • Viśvarūpaḥ: This means "of universal form." It signifies the all-encompassing nature of divinity, reminding us that everything is interconnected, much like the global community we live in today.
  • Pupoṣa: This term means "nourished" or "sustained." It reflects the idea of nurturing and supporting life, akin to how families and communities provide for one another.
  • Prajāḥ: This refers to beings or creatures, emphasizing the importance of life and existence. In a modern context, it can represent the diverse population of the world and the need for coexistence.
  • Purudhā: This means "in many ways" or "variously." It highlights the diversity of life experiences and perspectives, similar to how different cultures enrich our global society.
  • Jajāna: This term means "to be born" or "to come into being." It signifies the process of creation and the continuous cycle of life, akin to the birth of new ideas and innovations in society.
  • Imā ca viśvā: This phrase translates to "these and all worlds." It emphasizes the vastness of existence and the interconnectedness of all realms, much like the various fields of knowledge and disciplines in our world.
  • Bhuvanāny: This means "worlds" or "realms," indicating the different dimensions of existence. In real life, this can refer to the various environments we inhabit, from our local communities to the global stage.
  • Asya: This means "of this" or "of this world," grounding the mantra in the present reality we experience.
  • Mahad: This term means "great" or "mighty," signifying the power and significance of the divine forces at play in our lives.
  • Devānām: This translates to "of the gods," indicating the divine influence that shapes our existence.
  • Asuratvam: This term refers to "the nature of the divine" or "the essence of the gods," suggesting that divinity is inherent in all aspects of life.
  • Ekam: This means "one," emphasizing unity amidst diversity, akin to the idea of global citizenship where we recognize our shared humanity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, diversity, and interconnectedness in the universe. It signifies the importance of divine guidance in nurturing life and fostering unity among all beings. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to our understanding of community, the importance of collaboration, and the need to respect and celebrate diversity. By recognizing the divine in ourselves and others, we can work towards a more harmonious and inclusive world.
