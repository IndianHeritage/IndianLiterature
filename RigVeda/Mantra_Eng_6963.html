महः सु वो अरम् इषे स्तवामहे मीळ्हुषे अरङ्गमाय जग्मये |यज्ञेभिर गीरभिर विश्वमनुषाम मरुताम इयक्षसि गाये त्वा नमसā गिरा ||
mahaḥ su vo aram iṣe stavāmahe mīḻhuṣe araṁgamāya jagmaye | yajñebhir gīrbhir viśvamanuṣām marutām iyakṣasi gāye tvā namasā girā ||
  • mahaḥ su vo aram iṣe stavāmahe: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke you, O great one, with our praises." Here, "mahaḥ" signifies greatness, and "stavāmahe" indicates the act of praising or invoking. In real life, this can be seen in various cultures where people invoke the blessings of deities or great leaders through prayers or songs during significant events, such as weddings or festivals.
  • mīḻhuṣe araṁgamāya jagmaye: This part translates to "May you come to us, O divine one, who brings prosperity." "Mīḻhuṣe" refers to the one who brings abundance or prosperity. In modern contexts, this can relate to individuals or organizations seeking guidance or blessings for success in their endeavors, such as starting a new business or embarking on a new project.
  • yajñebhir gīrbhir viśvamanuṣām: This translates to "With offerings and hymns, we address all beings." "Yajñebhir" refers to offerings, often in the context of rituals, while "gīrbhir" means hymns or chants. This reflects the idea of community and collective effort in seeking blessings, similar to how communities come together for charitable causes or social gatherings, emphasizing unity and shared purpose.
  • marutām iyakṣasi gāye tvā namasā girā: This can be interpreted as "We sing your praises, O Maruts, with reverence." The "Maruts" are storm deities associated with strength and power. This line highlights the importance of acknowledging and respecting forces greater than ourselves, akin to how people express gratitude towards nature or the universe for its bounties, such as during harvest festivals.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of invoking divine blessings through praise, offerings, and collective efforts. It reflects the human desire for prosperity, unity, and respect for greater forces. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as community gatherings, rituals, and personal endeavors where individuals seek guidance and support. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of reverence in our pursuits.
