उरौ महां अनिबाधे ववर्धापो अग्निं यशसः सं हि पूर्वीः |ऋतस्य योनाव अशयद दामूनां जामीनाम अग्निर अपसि स्वसृणाम ||
urau mahām̐ anibādhe vavardhāpo agniṁ yaśasaḥ saṁ hi pūrvīḥ | ṛtasya yonāv aśayad damūnā jāmīnām agnir apasi svasṝṇām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • urau: in the heavens
    • mahām̐: great
    • anibādhe: unimpeded, unhindered
    • vavardhāpo: may he increase, may he grow
    • agniṁ: the fire
    • yaśasaḥ: of glory, fame
    • saṁ hi: indeed
    • pūrvīḥ: the ancients, the predecessors
    • ṛtasya: of truth, of order
    • yonāv: the source, the womb
    • aśayad: has established
    • damūnā: with restraint, with control
    • jāmīnām: of the earth
    • agnir: fire
    • apasi: in the waters
    • svasṝṇām: of the waters, of the streams
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the great fire (Agni) that is unimpeded and is a source of glory.
    • It acknowledges the ancient wisdom and the truth that has been established on Earth.
    • Agni is seen as a vital force that connects the heavens and the earth, symbolizing transformation and purification.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of restraint and control in harnessing this energy for constructive purposes.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, Agni can be likened to the energy we harness for progress, such as technological advancements.
    • The concept of restraint can be applied in various aspects, such as environmental conservation, where we must control our consumption to protect natural resources.
    • In personal development, the mantra encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom from the past while striving for growth and glory in their endeavors.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of harnessing energy (Agni) for growth and glory while being mindful of the truth and wisdom of our predecessors.
    • It teaches us that with great power comes the responsibility of restraint and control, applicable in various fields such as technology, environmentalism, and personal growth.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to seek balance between progress and preservation, ensuring that our actions today honor the wisdom of the past.
