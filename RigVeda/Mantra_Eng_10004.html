ऊती शचीवास तव वीर्येण वयो दधाना उषिज ऋतज्ञाः |प्रजावद इन्द्र मनुषो दुरोणे तस्थुर गृणन्तः सदहमाद्यासः ||
ūtī śacīvas tava vīryeṇa vayo dadhānā uśija ṛtajñāḥ | prajāvad indra manuṣo duroṇe tasthur gṛṇantaḥ sadhamādyāsaḥ ||
  • ūti: This word refers to the concept of strength or power. In real life, this can be seen in individuals or communities that exhibit resilience in the face of challenges, such as athletes overcoming injuries or communities rebuilding after natural disasters.
  • śacīvas: This term signifies the quality of being capable or competent. For example, a skilled leader who effectively guides their team through a crisis embodies this quality.
  • tava: Meaning "your," this word emphasizes possession or connection. It can relate to personal responsibility, such as a parent caring for their child or a mentor guiding a student.
  • vīryeṇa: This translates to "with strength" or "by virtue." In a practical sense, this can be seen in the determination of individuals who pursue their goals despite obstacles, like entrepreneurs who persist in building their businesses.
  • vayo: This word means "age" or "youth." It highlights the importance of vitality and the energy of youth, which can be observed in young activists who drive social change.
  • dadhānā: This means "holding" or "possessing." It can refer to individuals who hold onto their values and principles, such as environmentalists advocating for sustainability.
  • uśija: This term refers to "wise" or "intelligent." It can be exemplified by educators who impart knowledge and wisdom to their students, shaping future generations.
  • ṛtajñāḥ: This translates to "those who know the truth." In real life, this can be seen in scientists and researchers who seek to uncover and share knowledge for the betterment of society.
  • prajāvad: Meaning "progeny" or "offspring," this word emphasizes the importance of future generations. It can relate to parents nurturing their children or communities investing in education.
  • indra: Referring to the deity of strength and rain, Indra symbolizes power and authority. In a modern context, this can be likened to leaders who inspire and empower others.
  • manuṣo: This means "human" or "mankind." It highlights the collective responsibility of humanity to work together for a common good, such as global efforts to combat climate change.
  • duroṇe: This term signifies "difficulties" or "challenges." It reflects the struggles individuals face, such as overcoming personal hardships or societal issues.
  • tasthur: Meaning "they have established," this word emphasizes the importance of building foundations for success, such as communities creating support systems for their members.
  • gṛṇantaḥ: This translates to "singing" or "praising." It can be seen in celebrations of achievements, such as communities coming together to honor local heroes.
  • sadhamādyāsaḥ: This term refers to "those who are united in purpose." It highlights the significance of collaboration and teamwork, as seen in social movements where individuals unite for a common cause.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, wisdom, and unity in overcoming challenges and nurturing future generations. It serves as a reminder that individuals and communities must work together, drawing on their strengths and knowledge, to create a better world. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire people to take action, whether through personal development, community service, or collective efforts to address societal issues. Its significance lies in fostering resilience, collaboration, and a commitment to truth and values in our daily lives.
