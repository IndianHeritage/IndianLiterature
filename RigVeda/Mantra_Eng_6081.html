मां भीम मां श्रमिष्मोग्रस्य सख्ये तव |महत ते वृष्णो अभिचक्ष्यं कृतं पश्येम तुर्वशं यदुम ||
mā bhema mā śramiṣmograsya sakhye tava | mahat te vṛṣṇo abhicakṣyaṁ kṛtam paśyema turvaśaṁ yadum ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "Do not be angry, do not be distressed, O friend of the mighty; may we see the great deeds of the Vṛṣṇa, the one who is the protector of the world."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • mā bhema: "Do not be angry" - A plea for calmness and understanding in relationships.
    • mā śramiṣmograsya: "Do not be distressed" - A request to avoid unnecessary stress or anxiety.
    • sakhye tava: "O friend of the mighty" - Acknowledgment of a powerful ally or friend.
    • mahat te: "May we see the great" - A wish to witness greatness or significant achievements.
    • vṛṣṇo: "of the Vṛṣṇa" - Referring to a divine or heroic figure, often associated with strength and protection.
    • abhicakṣyaṁ: "deeds" - Actions or accomplishments that are noteworthy.
    • kṛtam: "done" - Referring to actions that have been completed.
    • paśyema: "may we see" - A desire for perception or understanding.
    • turvaśaṁ: "the protector" - A term denoting someone who safeguards or defends.
    • yadum: "the world" - Referring to the broader universe or community.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied when conflicts arise among colleagues. Instead of reacting with anger or distress, individuals can strive for understanding and collaboration, fostering a positive work environment.
    • In personal relationships, this mantra encourages open communication. For instance, during a disagreement with a friend, one can remind themselves to remain calm and seek to understand the other person's perspective, thus strengthening the bond.
    • In community settings, leaders can invoke this mantra to promote peace and unity, especially during times of crisis. By focusing on the greater good and the achievements of the community, they can inspire collective action and resilience.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on maintaining composure and fostering positive relationships. It serves as a reminder to approach conflicts with a mindset of understanding and collaboration. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, workplaces, or communities, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more harmonious interactions and a greater sense of unity. By focusing on the greatness of our connections and the achievements we can accomplish together, we can navigate challenges with grace and strength.
