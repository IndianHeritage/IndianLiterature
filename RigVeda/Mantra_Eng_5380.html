मन्त्र: उतो घा ते पुरुष्याः इद आसन येषाम पूर्वेषाम अशृणोः ऋषीणाम् |अधाहं त्वा मगवञ् जोहवीमि त्वं न इन्द्रासि प्रमतिः पितेव ||
uto ghā te puruṣyā3 id āsan yeṣām pūrveṣām aśṛṇor ṛṣīṇām | adhāhaṁ tvā maghavañ johavīmi tvaṁ na indrāsi pramatiḥ piteva ||
  • Translation: "O Maghavan (Indra), I invoke you, who are the embodiment of strength and power. You are the one who is revered by the ancient sages, and I offer my prayers to you, just as a father would to his son."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uto: O
    • ghā: strength, power
    • te: your
    • puruṣyā: embodiment of manliness or strength
    • id: indeed
    • āsan: who are seated or established
    • yeṣām: of whom
    • pūrveṣām: the ancient ones
    • aśṛṇor: of the sages
    • ṛṣīṇām: of the seers
    • adhāhaṁ: I offer
    • tvā: you
    • maghavañ: O Maghavan (Indra)
    • johavīmi: I invoke
    • tvaṁ: you
    • na: not
    • indrāsi: you are Indra
    • pramatiḥ: the one who is wise
    • piteva: like a father
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and wisdom.
    • It reflects the relationship between the devotee and the divine, akin to a father-son relationship, emphasizing respect and reverence.
    • In real life, this can be seen in the way individuals seek guidance and support from mentors or parental figures, invoking their wisdom and strength in times of need.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In moments of challenge or uncertainty, individuals can draw inspiration from this mantra by seeking strength and guidance from their own mentors or role models.
    • It encourages a sense of community and connection, reminding us to honor those who have come before us and to seek their wisdom in our endeavors.
    • This mantra can be recited during personal challenges, invoking the strength of the divine and the wisdom of past sages to guide one's actions.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of strength, wisdom, and the bonds we share with those who guide us.
    • It highlights the significance of invoking divine support in our lives, fostering a sense of humility and respect for the wisdom of the past.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to seek strength within themselves and from their connections with others, embodying the values of respect, reverence, and the pursuit of wisdom.
