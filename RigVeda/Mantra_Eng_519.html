जुष्टो हि दूतोऽसि हव्यवाहनोऽग्नेरथीरध्वराणाम् |सजूर अश्विभ्यां उषसा सुवीर्यमस्मे धेहि श्रवो बृहत् ||
juṣṭo hi dūto asi havyavāhano 'gne rathīr adhvarāṇām | sajūr aśvibhyām uṣasā suvīryam asme dhehi śravo bṛhat ||
  • juṣṭo hi dūto asi havyavāhano: This phrase can be translated as "You are indeed the one who is pleased, the messenger who carries offerings." Here, the term "dūto" refers to a messenger or envoy, while "havyavāhano" signifies one who carries offerings to the divine. In real life, this can be likened to individuals who act as intermediaries in various contexts, such as diplomats or community leaders who facilitate communication and understanding between different groups.
  • agne rathīr adhvarāṇām: This translates to "O Agni, the charioteer of the sacrifices." Agni, the fire god, is often associated with the sacrificial fire in Vedic rituals. The term "rathīr" implies a charioteer, symbolizing guidance and direction. In modern life, this can be seen in leaders or mentors who guide others through challenging situations, much like a charioteer guiding a chariot through a race.
  • sajūr aśvibhyām uṣasā: This means "with the two Ashvins, the dawn." The Ashvins are twin deities associated with health and medicine, and "uṣasā" refers to dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and hope. In everyday life, this can represent the importance of collaboration and teamwork, where individuals come together to bring about positive change, much like the dawn brings light after darkness.
  • suvīryam asme dhehi: This translates to "bestow upon us strength." The word "suvīryam" signifies great strength or valor. In practical terms, this can be related to the pursuit of personal and communal strength, whether it be physical, mental, or emotional. For instance, communities often come together to support one another during difficult times, showcasing collective strength.
  • śravo bṛhat: This means "the great fame." "Śravo" refers to fame or glory, while "bṛhat" means great or vast. This can be interpreted as a call for recognition and respect in society. In real life, this can be seen in the achievements of individuals or groups who strive for excellence in their fields, gaining recognition for their contributions to society.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of communication, guidance, collaboration, strength, and recognition. In a world where these elements are crucial for personal and communal growth, the teachings of this mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios. Whether it is through leadership, teamwork, or the pursuit of excellence, the essence of this mantra encourages individuals to strive for a harmonious and prosperous existence, fostering connections that lead to collective well-being.
