अहं सुवे पितरम् अस्य मूर्धन मम योनिर अप्स्व अन्तः समुद्रे |ततो वि तिष्ठे भुवनानु विश्वोतामुं द्यां वर्ष्मणोप स्पृशामि ||
ahaṁ suve pitaram asya mūrdhan mama yonir apsv a1ntaḥ samudre | tato vi tiṣṭhe bhuvanānu viśvotāmūṁ dyāṁ varṣmaṇopa spṛśāmi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ahaṁ: I
    • suve: who is well-born or auspicious
    • pitaram: father
    • asya: of this
    • mūrdhan: on the head
    • mama: my
    • yonir: womb or source
    • apsu: in the waters
    • antaḥ: within
    • samudre: ocean
    • tato: from there
    • vi tiṣṭhe: I stand or exist
    • bhuvanānu: in the worlds
    • viśvotāmūṁ: the highest or supreme
    • dyāṁ: sky or heaven
    • varṣmaṇ: light or radiance
    • upa: near
    • spṛśāmi: I touch or I reach
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep connection between the individual and the cosmic elements.
    • The speaker identifies themselves as auspicious and acknowledges their father, symbolizing respect for lineage and heritage.
    • The mention of "womb" and "ocean" signifies the source of life and the nurturing aspect of nature.
    • By stating "I stand in the worlds," it emphasizes the individual's existence and role within the universe.
    • The reference to "the highest sky" and "light" suggests a pursuit of enlightenment and higher consciousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, honoring one's ancestors and recognizing the source of life is a common practice, reflecting the importance of heritage.
    • Environmental awareness can be linked to the idea of the ocean as a source of life, reminding us of our responsibility to protect natural resources.
    • The pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment is a universal theme, seen in educational institutions and spiritual practices worldwide.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of life, the importance of heritage, and the pursuit of higher knowledge.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals seek to understand their place in the universe, respect their roots, and strive for enlightenment.
    • By reflecting on this mantra, one can cultivate a sense of responsibility towards nature and a commitment to personal growth.
