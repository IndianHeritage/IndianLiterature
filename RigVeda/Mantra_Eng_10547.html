समुद्रादर्णवादधिसंवत्सरोजायता | अहोरात्राणि विदधद्विश्वस्यमिषतोवशी ||
samudrād arṇavād adhi saṁvatsaro ajāyata | ahorātrāṇi vidadhad viśvasya miṣato vaśī ||
  • Mantra: "samudrād arṇavād adhi saṁvatsaro ajāyata | ahorātrāṇi vidadhad viśvasya miṣato vaśī ||"
  • Translation: "From the ocean, from the abyss, the year was born; it gives the day and night to the universe, controlling all that exists."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • samudrād: From the ocean - symbolizes the source of life and abundance.
    • arṇavād: From the abyss - represents the depths of existence and the unknown.
    • adhi: Above or upon - indicates a higher state or control.
    • saṁvatsaro: The year - signifies the cycle of time and the rhythm of life.
    • ajāyata: Was born - denotes the act of creation or emergence.
    • ahorātrāṇi: Day and night - represents the duality of time and existence.
    • vidadhad: Gives or provides - implies the act of bestowing or distributing.
    • viśvasya: Of the universe - refers to all that exists, the totality of creation.
    • miṣato: Of all beings - indicates the diversity within the unity of existence.
    • vaśī: Controlling or governing - suggests authority and power over creation.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The ocean is often seen as a source of life, much like how the mantra describes the ocean as the origin of the year. For instance, the ocean provides food, regulates climate, and supports biodiversity.
    • The concept of day and night is fundamental to human life, influencing our daily routines, work schedules, and even our biological rhythms, such as sleep cycles.
    • In agriculture, the cycle of seasons (year) is crucial for planting and harvesting crops, demonstrating the importance of understanding time in relation to nature.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of time, nature, and existence. It highlights how the ocean and the abyss are not just physical entities but also metaphors for the depths of knowledge and the cycles of life. In real life, this mantra can inspire us to respect the natural rhythms of the world, understand the significance of time in our lives, and recognize our place within the vast universe. It serves as a reminder of the balance and control that governs all existence, urging us to live in harmony with the cycles of nature and time.
