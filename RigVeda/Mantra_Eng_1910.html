युवां पूषेवाश्विनां पुरंधिर अग्निम उषां न जरत हविष्मान् |हुवे यद वां वरिवस्य गृणानो विद्यामेषं वृजनं जीरदानुम् ||
yuvām pūṣevāśvinā puraṁdhir agnim uṣāṁ na jarate haviṣmān | huve yad vāṁ varivasyā gṛṇāno vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum ||
  • yuvām - This word translates to "you two," referring to the twin deities Ashvins, who are known for their youthful energy and vitality.
  • pūṣevāśvinā - "Pūṣa" means nourishment or sustenance, and "Ashvinā" refers to the Ashvins. Together, this phrase signifies the nourishing aspect of the Ashvins, who are often invoked for health and well-being.
  • puraṁdhir - This term means "the one who is the master of the house" or "the one who brings forth." It signifies a creator or a provider, often associated with the divine.
  • agnim - This word means "fire," which is a symbol of energy, transformation, and purification in many cultures, including Vedic traditions.
  • uṣāṁ - This translates to "of the dawn," symbolizing new beginnings, hope, and the dispelling of darkness.
  • na jarate - This phrase means "does not age" or "is eternal," indicating the timeless nature of the divine and the qualities being invoked.
  • haviṣmān - This term refers to "the one who is worthy of offerings," highlighting the importance of devotion and the act of giving in spiritual practices.
  • huve - This means "I invoke" or "I call upon," indicating a plea or request to the divine.
  • yad - This translates to "that," referring to the qualities or blessings being sought.
  • vāṁ - This means "you," again addressing the Ashvins directly.
  • varivasyā - This term means "the one who is all-encompassing" or "the one who protects," emphasizing the protective nature of the divine.
  • gṛṇāno - This translates to "singing" or "praising," indicating the importance of devotion and expression of gratitude.
  • vidyāmeṣaṁ - This means "knowledge" or "wisdom," highlighting the pursuit of understanding and enlightenment.
  • vṛjanaṁ - This term refers to "the group" or "the community," emphasizing the collective aspect of spiritual practice.
  • jīradānum - This translates to "the one who gives life" or "the life-giver," indicating the nurturing and sustaining qualities of the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda invokes the Ashvins, the divine twins associated with health, vitality, and the dawn. It emphasizes the importance of nourishment, protection, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals for health and well-being, in community gatherings that promote collective wisdom, or in personal reflections that seek to invoke positive energy and transformation. The significance lies in recognizing the divine presence in our lives and the importance of gratitude, devotion, and the pursuit of knowledge for personal and communal growth.
