केतुं कृत्वा दिवस परि विश्वा रूपाभ्यः अर्षसि |समुद्रः सोम पिन्वसे ||
ketuṁ kṛṇvan divas pari viśvā rūpābhy arṣasi | samudraḥ soma pinvase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Ketuṁ: The banner or flag, often symbolizing victory or a guiding principle.
    • Kṛṇvan: To create or to make.
    • Divas: The sky or heaven.
    • Pari: Around or surrounding.
    • Viśvā: All or universal.
    • Rūpābhy: Forms or shapes.
    • Arṣasi: You shine or you manifest.
    • Samudraḥ: The ocean or sea.
    • Soma: The moon or a divine nectar.
    • Pinvase: You nourish or you fill.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the creation of a guiding principle (Ketu) that encompasses the entire sky (Divas) and manifests in various forms (Rūpābhy).
    • The reference to the ocean (Samudraḥ) and the moon (Soma) suggests a connection to nourishment and the cyclical nature of life, as the moon influences the tides and agricultural cycles.
    • It emphasizes the idea of universal presence and the interconnectedness of all forms of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, a strong guiding principle (Ketu) can unify a team, much like a flag that represents a common goal.
    • In nature, the moon (Soma) influences the tides, which in turn affects marine life and ecosystems, illustrating the interconnectedness of all living beings.
    • In personal growth, embracing various forms of knowledge and experiences (Viśvā Rūpābhy) can lead to a more enriched life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of having a guiding principle that encompasses all aspects of life and existence.
    • It teaches us to recognize the interconnectedness of all things and the nourishment that comes from understanding and embracing diversity.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in leadership, personal development, and environmental awareness, reminding us to create harmony and unity in our actions and thoughts.
