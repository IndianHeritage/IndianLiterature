समीरथं न भुरिजोऽहेषत दश स्वसारो अदितेर उपस्थ आ |जिगाद उप ज्रयति गोर अपीच्यम पदं यद अस्य मतुथा अजीजनन ||
sam ī rathaṁ na bhurijor aheṣata daśa svasāro aditer upastha ā | jigād upa jrayati gor apīcyam padaṁ yad asya matuthā ajījanan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sam: together, in harmony
    • īrathaṁ: the chariot, symbolizing movement and progress
    • na: not
    • bhurijor: of the strong, powerful
    • aheṣata: who has not been harmed
    • daśa: ten, representing completeness or wholeness
    • svasāro: brothers or kin
    • aditer: of Aditi, the mother of gods
    • upastha: approaching, coming near
    • ā: to, towards
    • jigād: he spoke, he declared
    • upa: near, close
    • jrayati: he who is strong, powerful
    • gor: of the cow, symbolizing nourishment and wealth
    • apīcyam: the best, the highest
    • padaṁ: place, position
    • yat: that which
    • asya: of this
    • matuthā: by thought, by intention
    • ajījanan: has given birth, has created
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of unity and strength, emphasizing the importance of coming together in harmony.
    • The chariot symbolizes the journey of life, where each individual plays a role in the collective progress.
    • The mention of "ten brothers" signifies the completeness of relationships and the support system we have in our lives.
    • Aditi, as the mother of gods, represents the nurturing aspect of the universe, guiding us towards our highest potential.
    • The mantra encourages us to speak and act with intention, recognizing the power of our thoughts in shaping our reality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family, the unity and support among siblings can be likened to the "ten brothers," where each member contributes to the overall strength and harmony.
    • In a workplace, teamwork and collaboration can lead to greater achievements, reflecting the idea of the chariot moving forward together.
    • In community service, individuals coming together for a common cause exemplify the power of collective action, nurturing the community like Aditi nurtures the gods.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity, strength, and intention in our lives.
    • It teaches us that by coming together and supporting one another, we can achieve greater heights and create a nurturing environment.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in families, workplaces, or communities, the principles of this mantra can guide us towards harmonious relationships and collective success.
