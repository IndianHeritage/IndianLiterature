परी पूषा परस्ताद् धस्तं दधातु दक्षिणम् |पुनर नो नष्टम आजतु ||
pari pūṣā parastād dhastaṁ dadhātu dakṣiṇam | punar no naṣṭam ājatu ||
  • Mantra: "pari pūṣā parastād dhastaṁ dadhātu dakṣiṇam | punar no naṣṭam ājatu ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pari: all around, everywhere
    • pūṣā: nourishment, sustenance
    • parastād: from beyond, from the other side
    • dhastaṁ: the one who is in need, the one who is lacking
    • dadhātu: may they provide, may they grant
    • dakṣiṇam: the right side, auspiciousness
    • punar: again, once more
    • no: for us
    • naṣṭam: lost, gone
    • ājatu: may it return, may it come back
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine force of nourishment and sustenance, asking for support from all directions.
    • It emphasizes the importance of receiving help from beyond our immediate surroundings, suggesting that assistance can come from unexpected places.
    • The phrase "may they provide" indicates a plea for the universe or divine entities to grant us what we need, particularly in times of lack or difficulty.
    • The mention of "the right side" symbolizes auspiciousness and positive energy, reinforcing the idea that the help we seek should be beneficial and constructive.
    • Finally, the mantra expresses a desire for what has been lost to return to us, highlighting the human experience of loss and the hope for recovery.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of financial hardship, individuals may recite this mantra to seek support and guidance, hoping for opportunities to arise from unexpected sources.
    • During personal crises, such as the loss of a loved one, this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek comfort and healing from both familiar and unfamiliar places.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be used to foster unity and collective support, encouraging members to help one another and to look for assistance beyond their immediate circle.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra holds significant relevance in our daily lives, reminding us of the interconnectedness of existence and the importance of seeking help from all around us.
    • It encourages a mindset of openness to receive support, whether it be emotional, spiritual, or material, and reinforces the idea that what is lost can be regained.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of hope and resilience, fostering a belief that nourishment and support are always available, even in challenging times.
