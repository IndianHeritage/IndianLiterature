मन्त्र: भद्रो भद्रयाऽसचमान आगात् स्वसारं जारो अभ्य एति पश्चात् |सुप्रकेतैर द्युभिर अग्निर वितिṣṭ्हन् रुशद्भिर वर्णैर अभि रामम् अस्तात् ||
bhadro bhadrayā sacamāna āgāt svasāraṁ jāro abhy eti paścāt | supraketair dyubhir agnir vitiṣṭhan ruśadbhir varṇair abhi rāmam asthāt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bhadro: auspicious, good
    • bhadrayā: with auspiciousness
    • sacamāna: having good thoughts or intentions
    • āgāt: has come
    • svasāraṁ: to the one who is dear or beloved
    • jāro: the one who is born or created
    • abhy eti: approaches or comes near
    • paścāt: afterwards or later
    • supraketair: with good arrangements or preparations
    • dyubhir: with the heavens or celestial bodies
    • agnir: fire
    • vitiṣṭhan: standing or residing
    • ruśadbhir: with bright or shining
    • varṇair: colors or qualities
    • abhi: towards
    • rāmam: the one who is pleasing or delightful
    • asthāt: has stood or established
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of auspiciousness and the arrival of good intentions.
    • It emphasizes the importance of approaching beloved or cherished entities with good thoughts.
    • The mention of fire and celestial bodies signifies the divine presence and the illumination of knowledge.
    • It suggests that with proper arrangements and intentions, one can achieve a state of delight and fulfillment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a wedding ceremony, the couple is surrounded by loved ones, and the atmosphere is filled with good intentions and auspiciousness, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In a business meeting, when team members come together with positive thoughts and well-prepared plans, they are more likely to achieve successful outcomes.
    • During festivals, the lighting of lamps symbolizes the arrival of knowledge and joy, akin to the fire mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of auspiciousness and good intentions in our lives.
    • It teaches us to approach our relationships and endeavors with positivity and preparation.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, professional settings, or spiritual practices, embodying the principles of this mantra can lead to harmony, success, and fulfillment.
