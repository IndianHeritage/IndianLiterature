कां ते अस्त्य अरंक्रतिः सूक्तैः कदा नूनं ते मगवन दाशेम |विश्वा मतीर आ तातने त्वायाधा मा इन्द्र श्रिणवो हवेमा ||
kā te asty araṁkṛtiḥ sūktaiḥ kadā nūnaṁ te maghavan dāśema | viśvā matīr ā tatane tvāyādhā ma indra śṛṇavo havemā ||
  • kā te asty araṁkṛtiḥ: This phrase translates to "What is your form, O Maghavan?" Here, "kā" means "what," "te" means "your," and "araṁkṛtiḥ" refers to "form" or "shape." This line expresses a quest for understanding the divine nature of the deity Indra, who is often associated with strength and power.
  • sūktaiḥ kadā nūnaṁ te maghavan dāśema: This part can be interpreted as "When shall we, O Maghavan, sing your praises?" "Sūktaiḥ" refers to hymns or praises, "kadā" means "when," and "dāśema" means "we shall sing." This line emphasizes the importance of devotion and the desire to connect with the divine through worship.
  • viśvā matīr ā tatane tvāyādhā: This translates to "May all thoughts converge upon you." "Viśvā" means "all," "matīr" refers to "thoughts" or "intellect," and "tatane" means "to converge." This line highlights the idea of unity in thought and purpose, suggesting that all minds should focus on the divine.
  • ma indra śṛṇavo havemā: This can be understood as "O Indra, may we hear your call." "Ma" means "may," "indra" refers to the deity Indra, "śṛṇavo" means "we hear," and "havemā" means "we invoke." This line signifies the importance of listening to divine guidance and responding to it through action.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for understanding and connection with the divine, specifically with Indra, who represents strength and protection. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as seeking guidance in difficult times, striving for unity in community efforts, or engaging in collective worship. The significance lies in the recognition of a higher power and the importance of aligning one's thoughts and actions towards a common goal, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose. This mantra serves as a reminder to remain open to divine influence and to actively participate in the spiritual journey, both individually and collectively.
