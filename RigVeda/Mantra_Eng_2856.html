तुभ्येन्द्र स्व ओक्ये सोमं चोदामि पीतये |एष रारन्तु ते हृदि ||
tubhyed indra sva okye3 somaṁ codāmi pītaye | eṣa rārantu te hṛdi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tubhyed: to you (Indra)
    • indra: O Indra, the king of gods
    • sva: your own
    • okye: in the words
    • somaṁ: soma, a sacred drink
    • codāmi: I invoke or inspire
    • pītaye: for drinking
    • eṣa: this
    • rārantu: may it flow or come
    • te: to you
    • hṛdi: in the heart
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the chief deity in the Rigveda, asking him to accept the sacred soma drink.
    • The speaker expresses a desire for the essence of soma to flow into Indra's heart, symbolizing a deep connection and devotion.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, offerings are made to deities to seek blessings, similar to how soma was offered to Indra.
    • In personal life, invoking a higher power or seeking inspiration can be likened to asking for guidance in difficult situations.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of devotion and the relationship between humans and divine forces.
    • It teaches us to seek inspiration and strength from higher powers in our daily lives, especially during challenges.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of purpose and connection with the universe.
