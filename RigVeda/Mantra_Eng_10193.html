जानन्तो रूपम् अकृपन्त विप्राः मृगस्य घोषं महिषस्य हि गमन् |ऋतेन यन्तो अधि सिन्धुम अस्तुर विदद् गन्धर्वो अमृतानि नाम ||
jānanto rūpam akṛpanta viprā mṛgasya ghoṣam mahiṣasya hi gman | ṛtena yanto adhi sindhum asthur vidad gandharvo amṛtāni nāma ||
  • jānanto: This word translates to "knowing" or "understanding." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and awareness in our lives.
  • rūpam: Meaning "form" or "appearance," this word suggests that understanding the essence or true nature of things is crucial.
  • akṛpanta: This can be interpreted as "not being harmed" or "not being affected." It indicates resilience and the ability to remain unaffected by external circumstances.
  • viprā: Referring to "the wise" or "the learned," this term highlights the value of wisdom and intellect in navigating life.
  • mṛgasya: Meaning "of the deer," this word symbolizes grace and agility, often representing the pursuit of knowledge and truth.
  • ghoṣam: This translates to "sound" or "call," suggesting the importance of communication and expression in sharing knowledge.
  • mahiṣasya: Referring to "the buffalo," it symbolizes strength and stability, representing the grounding aspects of life.
  • hi: A conjunction meaning "indeed" or "certainly," reinforcing the statements made in the mantra.
  • gman: This word can be interpreted as "to go" or "to move," indicating progress and the journey of life.
  • ṛtena: Meaning "by truth" or "in accordance with the cosmic order," it emphasizes the significance of living in harmony with universal principles.
  • yanto: This translates to "moving towards" or "heading for," suggesting a purposeful direction in life.
  • adhi: Meaning "above" or "over," it indicates a higher perspective or understanding.
  • sindhum: Referring to "the ocean" or "the sea," it symbolizes vastness and the depths of knowledge and experience.
  • asthur: This can be interpreted as "having been established" or "settled," indicating stability in one's pursuits.
  • vidad: Meaning "to give" or "to bestow," it emphasizes the act of sharing knowledge and wisdom.
  • gandharvo: Referring to "the celestial musicians," it symbolizes creativity and the arts, representing the beauty of expression.
  • amṛtāni: Meaning "immortal" or "eternal," it signifies the timeless nature of knowledge and wisdom.
  • nāma: This translates to "name" or "fame," indicating the recognition and legacy of wisdom.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of knowledge, wisdom, and resilience in navigating life's challenges. It encourages individuals to seek understanding and truth while remaining grounded and stable. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as education, personal development, and leadership. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can foster a deeper connection with themselves and the world around them, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and meaningful life.
