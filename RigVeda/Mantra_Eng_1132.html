स जातूभर्मा श्रद्धधाना ओजः पुरो विभिन्दन्न अचरत विदासीः |विद्वान् वज्रिन दस्यवे हेतिम अस्यार्यं सहो वर्धयाद्युम्नम इन्द्र ||
sa jātūbharmā śraddadhāna ojaḥ puro vibhindann acarad vi dāsīḥ | vidvān vajrin dasyave hetim asyāryaṁ saho vardhayā dyumnam indra ||
  • sa jātūbharmā: This phrase can be interpreted as "he who is born with strength" or "he who possesses inherent power." In real life, this can refer to individuals who are naturally gifted or talented in certain areas, such as athletes or artists who excel due to their innate abilities.
  • śraddadhāna: This term means "with faith" or "with devotion." It emphasizes the importance of belief and trust in one's actions. For example, a student who studies diligently with faith in their abilities is more likely to succeed in their exams.
  • ojaḥ: This word signifies "vitality" or "energy." In a practical sense, it can relate to the physical and mental energy one needs to pursue goals, such as an entrepreneur who channels their energy into building a successful business.
  • puro vibhindann: This phrase translates to "breaking through obstacles." It highlights the idea of overcoming challenges. For instance, a scientist who faces numerous failures in experiments but continues to push forward exemplifies this concept.
  • acarad vi dāsīḥ: This can be understood as "the one who moves forward with determination." It reflects the spirit of perseverance. A real-life example could be an athlete who trains tirelessly for a competition, embodying the essence of determination.
  • vidvān vajrin: This means "the wise one who is strong." It suggests that true strength comes from knowledge and wisdom. For example, a leader who makes informed decisions based on experience and understanding demonstrates this principle.
  • dasyave hetim: This phrase can be interpreted as "the cause of victory over enemies." It signifies the importance of strategy and intelligence in overcoming adversaries. In business, a company that outsmarts its competitors through innovative strategies exemplifies this idea.
  • asyāryaṁ saho: This translates to "with courage and strength." It emphasizes the need for bravery in facing challenges. A firefighter who risks their life to save others embodies this courage in real life.
  • vardhayā dyumnam indra: This phrase means "may Indra increase our glory." Indra, in Vedic literature, is often associated with power and success. This can be seen in the context of a community coming together to support a cause, thereby enhancing their collective reputation and success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, faith, perseverance, wisdom, and courage. It serves as a reminder that success is not merely a result of inherent talent but also of dedication, strategic thinking, and the ability to overcome obstacles. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, professional endeavors, and community efforts, encouraging individuals to harness their inner strength and work towards their goals with determination and faith.
