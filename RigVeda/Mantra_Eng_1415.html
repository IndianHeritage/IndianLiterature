उप क्षरन्ति सिन्धवो मयोभुव ījānaṁ च यक्ष्यमाणं च धेनवः |पृणन्तं च पपुरिं च श्रवस्यवो घृतस्य धाराऽ उप यन्ति विश्वतः ||
upa kṣaranti sindhavo mayobhuva ījānaṁ ca yakṣyamāṇaṁ ca dhenavaḥ | pṛṇantaṁ ca papuriṁ ca śravasyavo ghṛtasya dhārā upa yanti viśvataḥ ||
  • upa kṣaranti sindhavo: This phrase translates to "the rivers flow down." It signifies the natural flow of life and resources, much like how rivers nourish the land and sustain life.
  • mayobhuva ījānaṁ ca: This can be interpreted as "the essence of existence and the act of worship." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing the divine in our daily lives and the need for spiritual practices.
  • yakṣyamāṇaṁ ca dhenavaḥ: This means "the cows that are being milked." Cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and nourishment in many cultures, representing the sustenance that comes from nature.
  • pṛṇantaṁ ca papuriṁ ca: This translates to "the one who fills the city." It suggests the idea of abundance and prosperity, where the community thrives through shared resources and efforts.
  • śravasyavo ghṛtasya dhārā: This phrase means "the streams of ghee (clarified butter)." Ghee is a sacred substance in many cultures, often used in rituals and cooking, symbolizing purity and nourishment.
  • upa yanti viśvataḥ: This translates to "they flow from all directions." It signifies the interconnectedness of all things and how resources and blessings come from various sources.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of abundance, spirituality, and the interconnectedness of life. It teaches us to appreciate the natural resources around us, recognize the divine in our daily activities, and understand the importance of community and shared prosperity. In real life, this can be applied by fostering a sense of gratitude for the resources we have, engaging in spiritual practices that connect us to our essence, and working collaboratively within our communities to create a thriving environment for all. The mantra serves as a reminder that just as rivers nourish the land, our actions and intentions can create a flow of abundance in our lives and the lives of others.
