स वज्रभृत् दस्युहा भीम उग्रः सहस्रचेताः शतनीतः ऋभ्वा |चम्रीषो न शवसा पाञ्चजन्यो मरुत्वान् नो भवतु इन्द्र ऊती ||
sa vajrabhṛd dasyuhā bhīma ugraḥ sahasracetāḥ śatanītha ṛbhvā | camrīṣo na śavasā pāñcajanyo marutvān no bhavatv indra ūtī ||
  • sa vajrabhṛd: This phrase translates to "he who holds the thunderbolt." In real life, this can symbolize strength and power, akin to a leader who possesses the ability to make impactful decisions.
  • dasyuhā: Meaning "the one who destroys enemies," this can be related to overcoming challenges in life, such as a student who faces academic difficulties and ultimately triumphs through hard work.
  • bhīma ugraḥ: This translates to "terrible and fierce." In a real-world context, this could refer to natural disasters, like hurricanes or earthquakes, which remind us of nature's power and unpredictability.
  • sahasracetāḥ: Meaning "having a thousand thoughts," this can represent the complexity of human emotions and thoughts, similar to how a person might juggle multiple responsibilities in their daily life.
  • śatanītha: This means "having a hundred paths." It signifies the various choices we face in life, akin to a career decision where one must choose between multiple job offers.
  • ṛbhvā: This translates to "the one who is wise." Wisdom is essential in making informed decisions, such as a mentor guiding a young professional in their career.
  • camrīṣo: Meaning "the one who is swift," this can be likened to the rapid advancements in technology that require individuals to adapt quickly.
  • na śavasā: This phrase means "not with the dead." It emphasizes the importance of life and vitality, similar to how a community thrives on the active participation of its members.
  • pāñcajanyo: Referring to "the one who is like the conch," this symbolizes the call to action, much like a leader rallying their team for a project.
  • marutvān: This means "the one who is powerful like the winds." It can represent the unseen forces in our lives, such as motivation or inspiration that drives us forward.
  • no bhavatv: This translates to "may it be ours." It expresses a desire for blessings and support, similar to a community coming together to support a common cause.
  • indra ūtī: Meaning "the strength of Indra," this invokes the idea of divine support in our endeavors, akin to a person feeling a sense of purpose and guidance in their life journey.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates themes of strength, wisdom, and the importance of life choices. It serves as a reminder of the power we hold within ourselves to overcome challenges and make impactful decisions. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios, such as leadership, personal growth, and community support, encouraging individuals to harness their inner strength and wisdom to navigate life's complexities.
