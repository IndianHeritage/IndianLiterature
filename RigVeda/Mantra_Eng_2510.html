महान् सधस्थे ध्रुवा आ निषत्तोऽन्तर dyāvā माहिने हर्यमाणः |आस्क्रे sapatnī अजरे अमृत्क्ते सबर्दुघे उरुगायस्य धेनू ||
mahān sadhasthe dhruva ā niṣatto 'ntar dyāvā māhine haryamāṇaḥ | āskre sapatnī ajare amṛkte sabardughe urugāyasya dhenū ||
  • mahān sadhasthe: This phrase can be translated as "great strength" or "mighty power." It signifies the immense potential and energy that exists in the universe. In real life, this can be likened to the strength found in nature, such as the power of a river or the resilience of a mountain.
  • dhruva ā niṣatto: "Dhruva" means "constant" or "immovable," while "niṣatto" refers to being steadfast or unwavering. This part of the mantra emphasizes the importance of stability and reliability in our lives. For example, a person who remains committed to their values and principles, despite challenges, embodies this steadfastness.
  • 'ntar dyāvā māhine: This translates to "the heavens and the earth." It reflects the duality of existence and the interconnectedness of all things. In practical terms, this can be seen in how human actions impact the environment, reminding us of our responsibility to maintain harmony between nature and our lives.
  • haryamāṇaḥ: This word means "moving swiftly" or "in motion." It suggests the dynamic nature of life and the constant changes we experience. For instance, the rapid advancements in technology illustrate how we must adapt and evolve in response to new challenges and opportunities.
  • āskre sapatnī: "Āskre" refers to "in the midst of," and "sapatnī" means "rival" or "enemy." This part of the mantra highlights the presence of challenges and adversities in life. A real-life example could be a competitive workplace where individuals must navigate rivalries to achieve their goals.
  • ajare amṛkte: "Ajare" means "immortal" or "everlasting," and "amṛkte" refers to "the nectar of immortality." This signifies the pursuit of eternal truths and wisdom. In life, this can be related to the quest for knowledge and understanding that transcends time, such as the teachings of great philosophers or spiritual leaders.
  • sabardughe: This term can be interpreted as "with great effort" or "with diligence." It emphasizes the importance of hard work and perseverance in achieving our goals. For example, athletes train rigorously to excel in their sports, demonstrating the value of dedication and effort.
  • urugāyasya dhenū: "Urugāyasya" refers to "the one who is vast and expansive," while "dhenū" means "cows" or "nourishing." This symbolizes abundance and the nurturing aspects of life. In a real-world context, this can be seen in agriculture, where the nurturing of crops and livestock sustains communities and economies.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound truths about strength, stability, interconnectedness, and the dynamic nature of life. It serves as a reminder of the importance of resilience in the face of challenges, the pursuit of knowledge, and the value of hard work. In our daily lives, we can apply these teachings by striving for personal growth, maintaining our principles, and nurturing our relationships with others and the environment. Ultimately, this mantra encourages us to recognize the vast potential within ourselves and the world around us, guiding us toward a more harmonious and fulfilling existence.
