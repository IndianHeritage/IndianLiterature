दिवा नः पृथिवी इमं सिद्ध्रम अद्य दिविष्पृशम् |यज्ञं देवेषु यच्छताम् ||
dyāvā naḥ pṛthivī imaṁ sidhram adya divispṛśam | yajñaṁ deveṣu yacchatām ||
  • dyāvā naḥ - This phrase translates to "May the heavens be ours." It signifies a connection to the divine and the celestial realm, suggesting that the heavens are a source of inspiration and guidance.
  • pṛthivī - This means "the earth." It represents the physical world we inhabit, emphasizing the importance of our earthly existence and the need to respect and nurture it.
  • imaṁ sidhram - This translates to "this auspiciousness." It indicates a desire for prosperity and well-being in our lives, highlighting the importance of positive outcomes and blessings.
  • adya - Meaning "today," this word emphasizes the immediacy of our intentions and actions, urging us to focus on the present moment.
  • divispṛśam - This means "touched by the heavens." It suggests a divine influence or connection, reinforcing the idea that our actions can resonate with higher powers.
  • yajñaṁ - This translates to "sacrifice" or "offering." In the Vedic context, it refers to rituals performed to honor the gods and seek their blessings, symbolizing selflessness and devotion.
  • deveṣu - Meaning "to the gods," this word highlights the importance of acknowledging and respecting divine forces in our lives.
  • yacchatām - This translates to "may they grant." It expresses a plea for the gods to bestow their blessings upon us, indicating a reliance on divine grace.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates a profound connection between the heavens, the earth, and the divine. It emphasizes the importance of living in harmony with our surroundings while seeking blessings for prosperity and well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • During rituals or ceremonies where individuals seek blessings for new beginnings, such as weddings or housewarming events.
  • In personal reflections or meditative practices, where one focuses on gratitude for the earth and the universe.
  • In environmental initiatives, where the mantra serves as a reminder to respect and protect our planet, acknowledging its sacredness.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity between the celestial and terrestrial realms, urging individuals to live with intention, gratitude, and a sense of responsibility towards both the divine and the earth.
