योगक्षेमं व आदायाहं भूयासं उत्तमा आ वो मूर्धानम् अक्रमीम् |अधस्पदान मा उद वदत मण्डूकाः इवोदकान मण्डूकाः उदकाद् इव ||
yogakṣemaṁ va ādāyāham bhūyāsam uttama ā vo mūrdhānam akramīm | adhaspadān ma ud vadata maṇḍūkā ivodakān maṇḍūkā udakād iva ||
  • yogakṣemaṁ: This term can be broken down into two parts: "yoga" meaning union or connection, and "kṣema" meaning well-being or prosperity. Together, they signify the pursuit of both spiritual and material well-being.
  • va ādāyāham: This phrase translates to "I take" or "I accept." It indicates a personal commitment to the pursuit of well-being.
  • bhūyāsam: This word means "may I become" or "let me be." It expresses a desire for growth and improvement.
  • uttama: This translates to "the best" or "the highest." It signifies the aspiration for excellence in all endeavors.
  • ā vo: This phrase means "to you all" or "to all of you," indicating a collective aspect of the mantra, emphasizing community and shared goals.
  • mūrdhānam: This term refers to "the head" or "the top," symbolizing leadership and guidance.
  • akramīm: This means "without obstruction" or "unhindered," suggesting a smooth path towards achieving goals.
  • adhaspadān: This translates to "from below" or "from the lower realms," indicating a grounding or foundational aspect.
  • ma ud vadata: This phrase means "do not speak" or "do not say," suggesting a call for silence or contemplation.
  • maṇḍūkā ivodakān: This part translates to "like frogs in water," which can symbolize adaptability and the ability to thrive in various environments.
  • maṇḍūkā udakād iva: This means "frogs from the water," reinforcing the idea of transformation and emergence from one's surroundings.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the pursuit of holistic well-being, both individually and collectively. It emphasizes the importance of striving for excellence while remaining grounded and adaptable. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, community building, and leadership. For instance, in a workplace setting, a leader can embody the principles of this mantra by fostering a culture of collaboration and continuous improvement, ensuring that all team members feel valued and empowered to contribute to the collective success. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our aspirations and the importance of nurturing both our personal and communal well-being.
