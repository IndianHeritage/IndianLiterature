अरूरुचद उषसः पृष्णिर अग्रिय उक्षाऽ बिभर्ति भुवनानि वाजयुः |मायाविनो ममिरे अस्य मायया नृचक्षसः पितरो गर्भम् आ दधुः ||
arūrucad uṣasaḥ pṛśnir agriya ukṣā bibharti bhuvanāni vājayuḥ | māyāvino mamire asya māyayā nṛcakṣasaḥ pitaro garbham ā dadhuḥ ||
  • arūrucad uṣasaḥ: This phrase refers to the dawn or the early morning light. It symbolizes new beginnings and the dispelling of darkness, both literally and metaphorically.
  • pṛśnir agriya ukṣā: Here, "pṛśnir" can be interpreted as the bright or shining aspect of the dawn, while "agriya ukṣā" suggests the foremost or the best of the dawn. This emphasizes the importance of the dawn as a time of clarity and enlightenment.
  • bibharti bhuvanāni vājayuḥ: This part indicates that the dawn carries or supports the worlds ("bhuvanāni") and brings forth strength or energy ("vājayuḥ"). It highlights the idea that the dawn is not just a time marker but a powerful force that sustains life.
  • māyāvino mamire asya māyayā: "Māyāvino" refers to the illusionary or deceptive nature of the world. This line suggests that the dawn also brings awareness to the illusions that surround us, allowing us to see beyond them.
  • nṛcakṣasaḥ pitaro garbham ā dadhuḥ: This final part speaks of the ancestors ("pitaro") who have given birth to life ("garbham"). It implies a connection between the dawn, the cycle of life, and the wisdom passed down through generations.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the significance of dawn as a symbol of new beginnings, enlightenment, and the cyclical nature of life. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to moments of transition, such as starting a new project, entering a new phase in life, or seeking clarity in difficult situations. The mantra encourages individuals to embrace the dawn as a time for reflection, growth, and the pursuit of truth, reminding us that with each new day comes the opportunity to rise above illusions and connect with our roots and heritage.
