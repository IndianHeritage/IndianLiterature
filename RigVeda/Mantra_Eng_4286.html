उता यातं संगवे प्रातर अह्नो मध्यंदिन उदीत सूर्यस्य |दिवा नक्तम अवसा शान्तमेना नेदानीं पीतिर अश्विनाऽततान ||
utā yātaṁ saṁgave prātar ahno madhyaṁdina uditā sūryasya | divā naktam avasā śaṁtamena nedānīm pītir aśvinā tatāna ||
  • Mantra: "utā yātaṁ saṁgave prātar ahno madhyaṁdina uditā sūryasya | divā naktam avasā śaṁtamena nedānīm pītir aśvinā tatāna ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • utā: and also
    • yātaṁ: gone or traveled
    • saṁgave: in the assembly or gathering
    • prātar: in the morning
    • ahno: of the day
    • madhyaṁdina: at noon
    • uditā: risen or emerged
    • sūryasya: of the sun
    • divā: during the day
    • naktam: at night
    • avasā: having come to rest
    • śaṁtamena: with peace or tranquility
    • nedānīm: not now
    • pītir: the drink or nourishment
    • aśvinā: by the Ashvins (the twin gods)
    • tatāna: they have spread or extended
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the journey of the sun and the cycles of day and night.
    • The phrase "utā yātaṁ saṁgave" suggests the idea of coming together in unity, which can be likened to community gatherings or family reunions where individuals share experiences and strengthen bonds.
    • "prātar ahno madhyaṁdina uditā sūryasya" refers to the sun rising in the morning and reaching its zenith at noon, symbolizing the passage of time and the importance of each moment in our daily lives.
    • The mention of "divā naktam avasā" highlights the transition from day to night, reminding us of the balance between activity and rest, akin to the work-life balance that many strive for in modern society.
    • "śaṁtamena nedānīm" emphasizes the need for peace and tranquility, which can be related to the importance of mental health and finding calm in our busy lives.
    • The reference to "pītir aśvinā tatāna" signifies nourishment provided by the divine, akin to the support we receive from our loved ones and the community, which sustains us through life's challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of time, community, and the divine support we receive in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us to appreciate the cycles of life, the importance of gathering with others, and the need for peace amidst chaos.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek balance, foster connections, and cultivate a sense of tranquility in their daily routines.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to recognize the divine presence in our lives and the nourishment that comes from both nature and community.
