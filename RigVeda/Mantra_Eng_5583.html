या आस्ते यश्च चरति यश्च पश्यति नो जनः |तेषां सं हन्मो अक्षाणि यथेदं हर्म्यं तथा ||
ya āste yaś ca carati yaś ca paśyati no janaḥ | teṣāṁ saṁ hanmo akṣāṇi yathedaṁ harmyaṁ tathā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who
    • āste: sits
    • yaś: who
    • ca: and
    • carati: moves
    • yaś: who
    • ca: and
    • paśyati: sees
    • no: not
    • janaḥ: person
    • teṣāṁ: of them
    • saṁ: together
    • hanmo: we destroy
    • akṣāṇi: the eyes
    • yathedaṁ: as this
    • harmyaṁ: dwelling
    • tathā: so
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nature of perception and awareness.
    • It suggests that those who sit, move, or see are not merely individuals but part of a larger collective.
    • The phrase "we destroy the eyes of them" can be interpreted as a metaphor for limiting or altering perception.
    • The reference to "this dwelling" indicates a specific context or environment where these actions take place.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader may observe how employees interact (sitting, moving, seeing) and realize that their perceptions of the workplace culture can be altered through effective communication and team-building activities.
    • In a community, when individuals come together to address social issues, they may collectively change how they perceive their environment, leading to improved relationships and understanding.
    • In personal relationships, the way we perceive each other can be influenced by our actions and interactions, highlighting the importance of empathy and communication.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of individuals and the impact of collective perception on reality.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions and awareness shape not only our own experiences but also those of others around us.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork, community engagement, and personal relationships, where understanding and altering perceptions can lead to positive outcomes.
