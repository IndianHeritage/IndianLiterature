त्रय इन्द्रस्य सोमाः सुतासः सन्तु देवस्य |स्वे क्षये सुतपावनः ||
traya indrasya somāḥ sutāsaḥ santu devasya | sve kṣaye sutapāvnaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "traya indrasya somāḥ sutāsaḥ santu devasya | sve kṣaye sutapāvnaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "May the three (soma) be the sons of Indra, may they be the divine ones; may they be pure in their own essence."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Traya: Three - signifies completeness or a triad, often representing the three realms: physical, mental, and spiritual.
    • Indrasya: Of Indra - Indra is the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
    • Somāḥ: Soma - a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, representing vitality, immortality, and divine inspiration.
    • Sutāsaḥ: Sons - indicates offspring or results, often referring to the outcomes of divine blessings.
    • Santu: May they be - a wish or prayer for the manifestation of the qualities mentioned.
    • Devasya: Of the divine - refers to the higher spiritual beings or gods.
    • Sve: In their own - emphasizes the intrinsic nature or essence of the subjects.
    • Kṣaye: In destruction or dissolution - can refer to the end of something, often leading to transformation.
    • Sutapāvnaḥ: Pure essence - signifies purity and the divine quality of the essence being referred to.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of "three" can be seen in various aspects of life, such as the mind, body, and spirit, which need to be balanced for overall well-being.
    • Indra's strength can be likened to leadership qualities in modern contexts, where a leader must embody strength, wisdom, and the ability to inspire others.
    • Soma, as a symbol of vitality, can be related to the importance of nutrition and health in our lives, emphasizing the need for a balanced diet to maintain energy and well-being.
    • The idea of purity in essence can be reflected in personal integrity and authenticity, where individuals strive to be true to themselves and their values.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine qualities and the pursuit of purity and strength in our lives. It serves as a reminder to seek balance in our physical, mental, and spiritual realms. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate their inner strength, maintain their integrity, and strive for a harmonious existence. By invoking the blessings of the divine, one can aspire to achieve a state of purity and vitality, ultimately leading to personal and communal growth.
