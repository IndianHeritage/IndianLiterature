विभूतारातिं विप्र चित्रशोचिषम् अग्निम ईळिष्व यन्तुरम् |अस्य मेधस्य सोम्यस्य शोभरे प्रेम अध्वराय पूर्व्यम् ||
vibhūtarātiṁ vipra citraśociṣam agnim īḻiṣva yanturam | asya medhasya somyasya sobhare prem adhvarāya pūrvyam ||
  • vibhūtarātiṁ: This term can be broken down into "vibhūti," meaning glory or wealth, and "rāti," which refers to the essence or nature. Together, it signifies the essence of divine glory or the wealth of spiritual knowledge.
  • vipra: This word refers to a learned person or sage, someone who possesses wisdom and insight. In real life, this can be likened to a teacher or mentor who guides others through knowledge.
  • citraśociṣam: "Citra" means colorful or diverse, and "śociṣam" refers to brightness or radiance. This phrase suggests the brilliance of diverse knowledge and perspectives, akin to how different colors contribute to a beautiful painting.
  • agnim: This word means fire, which in many cultures symbolizes transformation, energy, and purification. In practical terms, fire can represent the passion and drive one has in pursuing their goals.
  • īḻiṣva: This term can be interpreted as a call to worship or to invoke. It emphasizes the importance of reverence and intention in our actions, similar to how one might prepare for a significant event with mindfulness.
  • yanturam: This word suggests movement or progression. It can be related to the journey of life, where one is constantly evolving and moving towards their goals.
  • asya: This means "of this" or "of this person," indicating a personal connection to the mantra's message.
  • medhasya: This term refers to intelligence or wisdom. It highlights the importance of mental clarity and understanding in achieving success.
  • somyasya: "Somya" means gentle or pleasant. This suggests that wisdom should be accompanied by kindness and compassion, much like how a gentle approach can lead to better relationships.
  • sobhare: This word can be interpreted as beauty or radiance. It emphasizes the idea that wisdom and knowledge should be beautiful and uplifting, akin to how art can inspire and elevate the human spirit.
  • prem: This term means love. It signifies that love is a fundamental aspect of all actions and intentions, similar to how love can drive people to achieve great things.
  • adhvarāya: This refers to the path of sacrifice or the journey of life. It suggests that life is a sacred journey that requires dedication and effort.
  • pūrvyam: This word means "from the past" or "ancient." It indicates that the wisdom and knowledge we seek are rooted in ancient traditions and teachings, much like how history informs our present actions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine knowledge and wisdom while emphasizing the importance of love, kindness, and reverence in our actions. In real life, it serves as a reminder to approach our goals with passion and intention, to value diverse perspectives, and to cultivate a gentle and loving attitude towards ourselves and others. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in education, personal development, and community building, where the integration of wisdom, love, and respect can lead to a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
