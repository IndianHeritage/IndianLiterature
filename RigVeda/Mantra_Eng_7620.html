मात्स्वा सुशिप्र हरिवस तद इमहे तवे भूषन्ति वेधसः |तव श्रवांस्य उपमान्य उक्थ्यā सुतेष्व इन्द्र गिर्वणः ||
matsvā suśipra harivas tad īmahe tve ā bhūṣanti vedhasaḥ | tava śravāṁsy upamāny ukthyā suteṣv indra girvaṇaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • matsvā: "our" or "of us"
    • suśipra: "well-known" or "famous"
    • harivas: "like a horse" or "swift as a horse"
    • tad: "that"
    • īmahe: "we meditate" or "we contemplate"
    • tve: "you"
    • ā: "to" or "towards"
    • bhūṣanti: "adorn" or "decorate"
    • vedhasaḥ: "of the wise" or "of the learned"
    • tava: "your"
    • śravāṁsy: "fame" or "glory"
    • upamāny: "to be compared" or "to be likened"
    • ukthyā: "by hymns" or "by praises"
    • suteṣv: "in the offerings" or "in the gifts"
    • indra: "Indra" (the king of gods)
    • girvaṇaḥ: "of the singers" or "of the poets"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of the gods, seeking his blessings and fame.
    • The phrase "matsvā suśipra" indicates a collective intention, emphasizing community and shared aspirations.
    • By likening the qualities of Indra to that of a swift horse, it highlights the importance of speed and agility in achieving goals.
    • The invocation of "tava śravāṁsy" suggests that the speaker is acknowledging the glory and fame of Indra, which they aspire to emulate.
    • The mention of "ukthyā" indicates the importance of hymns and praises in connecting with the divine, showcasing the role of art and expression in spirituality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, teams often come together to celebrate achievements, invoking the spirit of collaboration and shared success, much like the collective prayer in this mantra.
    • Sports teams may chant or sing praises to their deities or symbols of strength before a game, seeking the swiftness and agility represented by the horse in the mantra.
    • In personal development, individuals may meditate on their goals, seeking inspiration and guidance from figures they admire, akin to invoking Indra's qualities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of community, aspiration, and the pursuit of excellence.
    • It serves as a reminder of the power of collective intention and the importance of honoring those who inspire us.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals and groups to seek blessings and guidance in their endeavors, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
