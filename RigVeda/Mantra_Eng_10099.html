इन्द्रस्यात्र तविषीभ्यो विरप्शिन ऋघायतो अरंहयन्त मन्यवे |वृत्रं यद उग्रो व्य अवृश्चद ओजसापो बिभ्रतं तमसा परिवृतम् ||
indrasyātra taviṣībhyo virapśina ṛghāyato araṁhayanta manyave | vṛtraṁ yad ugro vy avṛścad ojasāpo bibhrataṁ tamasā parīvṛtam ||
  • Indrasyātra: This term refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, who is often associated with strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • Taviṣībhyo: This word can be interpreted as "to those who are strong" or "to the powerful," indicating a focus on strength and valor.
  • Virapśina: This term suggests "the one who is fierce" or "the one who is brave," emphasizing the qualities of courage and determination.
  • Ṛghāyato: This can be understood as "those who are wise" or "those who possess knowledge," highlighting the importance of wisdom in overcoming challenges.
  • Araṁhayanta: This word means "to protect" or "to safeguard," indicating a sense of guardianship and support.
  • Manyave: This term refers to "thought" or "intention," suggesting that the mindset and intentions behind actions are crucial.
  • Vṛtraṁ: Vṛtra is a demon in Vedic texts, symbolizing obstacles or challenges that one must face.
  • Yad: This means "that" or "which," linking the previous concepts to the challenges faced.
  • Ugro: This term means "fierce" or "powerful," reinforcing the idea of confronting formidable challenges.
  • Vy avṛścad: This can be interpreted as "having been defeated" or "overcome," indicating the triumph over obstacles.
  • Ojasāpo: This term refers to "strength" or "energy," emphasizing the need for vigor in overcoming challenges.
  • Bibhrataṁ: This means "to bear" or "to carry," suggesting the weight of challenges that one must endure.
  • Tamasā: This term means "darkness" or "ignorance," representing the obstacles that obscure clarity and understanding.
  • Parīvṛtam: This means "surrounded" or "enveloped," indicating the pervasive nature of challenges in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of facing and overcoming challenges with strength, wisdom, and courage. It emphasizes the importance of intention and the need for protection against the obstacles that life presents. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various situations, such as personal struggles, professional challenges, or societal issues. By embodying the qualities of Indra—strength, bravery, and wisdom—individuals can navigate through difficulties and emerge victorious. This mantra serves as a reminder that with the right mindset and support, one can overcome even the fiercest of challenges.
