विष्रयन्ताम् उर्वियाः हूयमानाः द्वारो देविः सुप्रायणाः नमोभिः |व्यचस्वतीर् वि प्रथन्ताम् अजुर्व्याः वर्णम् पुनानाः यशसां सुवीरम् ||
vi śrayantām urviyā hūyamānā dvāro devīḥ suprāyaṇā namobhiḥ | vyacasvatīr vi prathantām ajuryā varṇam punānā yaśasaṁ suvīram ||
  • vi śrayantām urviyā: This phrase can be interpreted as "to those who are supported by the vastness." Here, "urviyā" refers to the expansive nature of the universe or the earth, suggesting a connection to the infinite possibilities and resources available to us.
  • hūyamānā dvāro devīḥ: This translates to "the doors of the divine are being invoked." It signifies the act of calling upon divine energies or forces, which can be seen as seeking guidance or blessings in our endeavors.
  • suprāyaṇā namobhiḥ: This means "with salutations to those who are well-versed." It emphasizes respect and reverence towards those who possess knowledge and wisdom, acknowledging their role in our lives.
  • vyacasvatīr vi prathantām: This can be understood as "may the radiant ones shine forth." It suggests the idea of enlightenment and the emergence of clarity and understanding in our lives.
  • ajuryā varṇam punānā: This phrase translates to "the unblemished color that purifies." It symbolizes purity and the transformative power of knowledge and virtue, which can cleanse and elevate our existence.
  • yaśasaṁ suvīram: This means "the glorious and valiant." It refers to the qualities of bravery and honor, encouraging us to embody these traits in our actions and pursuits.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, wisdom, and purity in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of acknowledging the vastness of the universe and the support it offers, while also recognizing the value of knowledge and virtue.
  • Real-life Application: In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as:
    • In decision-making processes, invoking divine guidance can help individuals navigate challenges with clarity and confidence.
    • In educational pursuits, respecting and learning from knowledgeable individuals can enhance personal growth and understanding.
    • In personal development, striving for purity in thoughts and actions can lead to a more fulfilling and honorable life.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, virtue, and divine support in our lives. By embracing these principles, we can cultivate a life that is not only successful but also meaningful and impactful.
