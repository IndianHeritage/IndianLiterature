इदं हि वाम प्रदिवि स्थानम् ओक इमे गृहाऽश्विनेदं दुरोणम् |आ नो दिवो बृहतः पर्वताद आद्भ्यो यातम् इषम् ऊर्जं वहन्ता ||
idaṁ hi vām pradivi sthānam oka ime gṛhā aśvinedaṁ duroṇam | ā no divo bṛhataḥ parvatād ādbhyo yātam iṣam ūrjaṁ vahantā ||
  • Translation: "This indeed is your place in the heavens; these are your homes, O Ashvins. From the great mountain of the sky, may we be carried forth with strength and energy."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • idaṁ hi: This indeed
    • vām: your
    • pradivi: in the heavens
    • sthānam: place
    • oka: homes
    • ime: these
    • gṛhā: houses or abodes
    • aśvinedaṁ: O Ashvins (the twin deities associated with health and medicine)
    • duroṇam: from the great mountain
    • ā no: may we
    • divo: of the sky
    • bṛhataḥ: great
    • parvatād: from the mountain
    • ādbhyo: may we be carried
    • yātam: forth
    • iṣam: strength
    • ūrjaṁ: energy
    • vahantā: carrying

  • Significance: This mantra invokes the Ashvins, who are revered as divine physicians and bringers of health and vitality. It emphasizes the connection between the divine and the earthly, suggesting that the blessings of the heavens can manifest in our homes and lives.

  • Real-Life Application:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be recited during rituals or prayers for health and well-being, especially when seeking strength in challenging times.
    • It can also be used in ceremonies that celebrate new beginnings, such as housewarming events, where the blessings of the divine are invited into a new home.
    • In a broader sense, it serves as a reminder to seek strength and energy from higher powers, encouraging individuals to connect with their spiritual beliefs for support and guidance.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support for strength and vitality in our lives. It highlights the importance of recognizing the connection between the heavens and our earthly existence, encouraging us to invite positive energies into our homes and lives. By invoking the Ashvins, we acknowledge the need for health and well-being, making it a significant part of spiritual practices and daily affirmations.
