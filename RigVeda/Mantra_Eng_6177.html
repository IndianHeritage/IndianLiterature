इमां सु पूर्व्यां धियम् मधोर घृतस्य पिप्युषीम् |कण्वा उक्थेन वावृधुः ||
imāṁ su pūrvyāṁ dhiyam madhor ghṛtasya pipyuṣīm | kaṇvā ukthena vāvṛdhuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imāṁ: this
    • su: good, well
    • pūrvyāṁ: from the past, ancient
    • dhiyam: intellect, understanding
    • madhor: of the sweet, pleasant
    • ghṛtasya: of ghee, clarified butter
    • pipyuṣīm: nourishing, rich
    • kaṇvā: the wise, learned
    • ukthena: with the hymn, invocation
    • vāvṛdhuḥ: they have increased, they have grown
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of invoking the ancient wisdom and intellect that is nourishing and sweet, akin to the richness of ghee.
    • The phrase "imāṁ su pūrvyāṁ dhiyam" suggests a call to embrace and enhance our understanding that has been passed down through generations.
    • The reference to "ghṛtasya pipyuṣīm" emphasizes the importance of nurturing our intellect with rich and wholesome knowledge, much like how ghee is a vital ingredient in many traditional dishes.
    • The wise individuals, referred to as "kaṇvā," are those who utilize this ancient wisdom through hymns and invocations to foster growth and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, this mantra can be applied by encouraging students to learn from historical texts and ancient philosophies, enriching their intellect with time-tested knowledge.
    • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their past experiences and the wisdom gained from them, using this understanding to make better decisions in the present.
    • In community gatherings, invoking the collective wisdom of elders can help guide discussions and decisions, ensuring that the community benefits from the rich experiences of its members.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of connecting with our roots and the wisdom of the past to enhance our present understanding and future growth.
    • It teaches us that nourishing our intellect with rich, wholesome knowledge can lead to personal and communal growth.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts such as education, personal reflection, and community engagement, reminding us to value and learn from the wisdom that has come before us.
