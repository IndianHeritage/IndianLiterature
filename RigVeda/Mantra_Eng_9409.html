कृष्णा यद गोष्व अरुणीषु सीदद दिवो नपाताश्विनां हुवे वाम् |वीतम मे यज्ञं आ गतं मे अन्नं ववन्वांसा नेषं अस्मृतधृ ||
kṛṣṇā yad goṣv aruṇīṣu sīdad divo napātāśvinā huve vām | vītam me yajñam ā gatam me annaṁ vavanvāṁsā neṣam asmṛtadhrū ||
  • kṛṣṇā yad goṣv aruṇīṣu sīdad: This phrase can be translated to "O Krishna, who resides in the cows and the dawn." Here, "Krishna" symbolizes divinity and abundance, while "goṣv" refers to cows, which are often seen as sacred and a source of nourishment in Hindu culture. "Arunīṣu" relates to the dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and hope.
  • dīvo napātāśvinā huve vām: This translates to "I invoke you, O Ashvins, the divine twin horsemen of the sky." The Ashvins are deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They represent the duality of life and the balance between opposites, such as day and night, health and illness.
  • vītam me yajñam ā gatam me annaṁ: This means "May my sacrifice be accepted, and may my food be plentiful." Here, "yajñam" refers to the act of sacrifice or offering, which is a central theme in Vedic rituals. The desire for abundance in food signifies the importance of sustenance and nourishment in life.
  • vavanvāṁsā neṣam asmṛtadhrū: This can be interpreted as "May the offerings I make be remembered and may they bring me prosperity." The emphasis on remembrance highlights the significance of intentions behind actions and the hope that they yield positive results.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for abundance, health, and prosperity. It reflects the interconnectedness of nature, spirituality, and human existence.
  • Real-life Application: In daily life, this mantra can be invoked during rituals, prayers, or even in moments of reflection. For instance, farmers may recite it to seek blessings for a bountiful harvest, while individuals may use it to express gratitude for the food they receive and to ask for continued sustenance.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of gratitude, the sacredness of life, and the need for balance in our pursuits. By acknowledging the divine and the natural world, we cultivate a sense of harmony and purpose in our lives.
