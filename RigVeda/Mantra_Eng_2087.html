अग्नि यजस्व हविषा यजीयां छ्रुष्टि देष्णम अभि गृणीहि राधः |त्वं ह्यसि रयिपती रयीणां त्वं शुक्रस्य वचसो मनोता ||
agne yajasva haviṣā yajīyāñ chruṣṭī deṣṇam abhi gṛṇīhi rādhaḥ | tvaṁ hy asi rayipatī rayīṇāṁ tvaṁ śukrasya vacaso manotā ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic tradition, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Yajasva: This means "to worship" or "to offer," indicating the act of devotion and reverence towards the divine.
  • Haviṣā: This translates to "with offerings," referring to the materials or substances offered in a ritual.
  • Yajīyāñ: This word means "worthy of worship," emphasizing the importance of the deity being addressed.
  • Chruṣṭī: This can be interpreted as "to hear" or "to listen," suggesting a call for divine attention and response.
  • Deṣṇam: This means "to the one who is invoked," referring to the deity being called upon in the mantra.
  • Abhi: This word means "towards" or "in the direction of," indicating a focus on the divine presence.
  • Gṛṇīhi: This translates to "to praise" or "to sing," highlighting the act of expressing reverence and gratitude.
  • Rādhaḥ: This refers to "prosperity" or "wealth," symbolizing abundance and well-being.
  • Tvaṁ: This means "you," directly addressing the deity, establishing a personal connection.
  • Hy: This is an emphatic particle, reinforcing the statement that follows.
  • Asi: This translates to "are," affirming the existence and presence of the divine.
  • Rayipatī: This means "lord of wealth," indicating the deity's role as a provider of prosperity.
  • Rayīṇāṁ: This translates to "of riches," emphasizing the abundance that the deity oversees.
  • Śukrasya: This refers to "of brightness" or "of purity," suggesting the divine's nature.
  • Vacaso: This means "of speech," indicating the power of words and communication in the divine context.
  • Manotā: This translates to "of the mind," suggesting the connection between thought, intention, and divine will.

Conclusion: This mantra is a call to Agni, the fire god, to accept offerings and bless the devotee with prosperity and abundance. It emphasizes the importance of worship, the power of words, and the connection between the divine and human intentions. In real life, this mantra can be applied in rituals, prayers, and meditative practices aimed at seeking blessings for wealth, success, and spiritual growth. It serves as a reminder of the significance of devotion and the pursuit of a prosperous life through righteous actions and intentions.
