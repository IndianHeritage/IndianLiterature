प्र भूर जयंतम महां विपोधाम मूरा अमूरम पुरां धर्माणम |नयन्तो गर्भं वनां धियं धुर हिरीश्मश्रुं नार्वाणं धनर्चम् ||
pra bhūr jayantam mahāṁ vipodhām mūrā amūram purāṁ darmāṇam | nayanto garbhaṁ vanāṁ dhiyaṁ dhur hiriśmaśruṁ nārvāṇaṁ dhanarcam ||
  • pra bhūr jayantam: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who is born from the earth" or "the one who is manifesting." It signifies the emergence of life and consciousness from the material world.
  • mahāṁ vipodhām: This translates to "the great awakening" or "the supreme knowledge." It emphasizes the importance of enlightenment and understanding in our lives.
  • mūrā amūram purāṁ darmāṇam: Here, "mūrā" refers to "the essence" or "the core," while "amūram" means "immortal" or "eternal." "Purāṁ darmāṇam" can be understood as "the ancient laws" or "the principles of righteousness." Together, this suggests that the eternal truths and principles of life are fundamental to our existence.
  • nayanto garbhaṁ vanāṁ dhiyaṁ: This part can be interpreted as "guiding the womb of the forest" or "nurturing the potential within." It highlights the nurturing aspect of nature and the importance of protecting our environment.
  • dhuḥ hiriśmaśruṁ nārvāṇaṁ dhanarcam: "Dhuḥ" can mean "to carry" or "to bear," while "hiriśmaśruṁ" refers to "the light of wisdom." "Nārvāṇaṁ" signifies "the state of liberation" or "freedom," and "dhanarcam" can be understood as "the wealth of knowledge." This suggests that through wisdom and understanding, one can achieve liberation and true wealth in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of life, knowledge, and the interconnectedness of nature and humanity. It emphasizes the importance of awakening to our true potential, understanding the eternal principles that govern our existence, and nurturing both ourselves and the environment around us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek knowledge, protect nature, and strive for personal and collective enlightenment. It serves as a reminder that true wealth lies not in material possessions but in wisdom and understanding, guiding us towards a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
