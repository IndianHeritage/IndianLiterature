इन्द्रं इद गाथिनो बृहद् इन्द्रं अर्केभिर अर्किणः |इन्द्रं वाणीर अनूषत ||
indram id gāthino bṛhad indram arkebhir arkiṇaḥ | indraṁ vāṇīr anūṣata ||
  • Indram: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
  • id: This word can be translated as "this" or "here," indicating a focus on the present moment or the immediate context.
  • gāthino: This term relates to hymns or verses, suggesting the importance of expression through poetry or song.
  • bṛhad: Meaning "great" or "vast," it emphasizes the magnitude of Indra's qualities or actions.
  • arkebhir: This word refers to "light" or "radiance," often associated with knowledge, enlightenment, and divine inspiration.
  • arkiṇaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who praise" or "those who sing," indicating the role of devotees or followers in honoring the divine.
  • vāṇīr: Refers to "speech" or "voice," highlighting the significance of communication and expression in connecting with the divine.
  • anūṣata: This word means "to follow" or "to pursue," suggesting a continuous effort to seek and understand the divine presence.

  • Meaning: The mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking Indra through hymns and praises, recognizing his greatness and the light he brings into the world. It highlights the role of speech and expression in connecting with the divine and the continuous pursuit of understanding and following the divine path.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a leadership context, one can draw parallels to how effective leaders inspire their teams through powerful communication and vision, much like how Indra is invoked through hymns.
    • In personal development, the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment can be seen as a modern-day equivalent of seeking the divine light represented by Indra.
    • In community gatherings, the act of singing or reciting verses can foster a sense of unity and shared purpose, echoing the communal praise of Indra.

  • Conclusion: This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of divine leadership, the power of expression, and the continuous pursuit of enlightenment. In real life, it encourages individuals and communities to honor their leaders, seek knowledge, and express their devotion through words and actions. By embodying these principles, one can cultivate a deeper connection with the divine and foster a more harmonious existence.
