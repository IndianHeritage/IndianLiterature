आ नस् ते गन्तु मत्सरो वृषा मदो वरेण्यः | सहावाम् इन्द्र सानसिः पृतनाशाल् अमर्त्यः ||
ā nas te gantu matsaro vṛṣā mado vareṇyaḥ | sahāvām̐ indra sānasiḥ pṛtanāṣāḻ amartyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • nas: us
    • te: your
    • gantu: to go
    • matsaro: jealousy
    • vṛṣā: strong
    • mado: intoxication
    • vareṇyaḥ: worthy of worship
    • sahāvām̐: let us be together
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • sānasiḥ: in the assembly
    • pṛtanāṣāḻ: with great strength
    • amartyaḥ: immortal
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of gods, asking for the removal of jealousy and intoxication from our lives.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and strength in the community, urging individuals to come together for a common purpose.
    • The phrase "let us be together" signifies the need for collaboration and support among people, which is essential for achieving collective goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied to foster teamwork. When employees work together without jealousy or competition, they can achieve greater success.
    • In community service, individuals can come together to help those in need, setting aside personal differences and focusing on the collective good.
    • In personal relationships, this mantra encourages open communication and understanding, helping to eliminate feelings of jealousy and fostering a supportive environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and the elimination of negative emotions like jealousy and intoxication.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, including workplaces, communities, and personal relationships, where collaboration and support are essential for success.
    • By embracing the teachings of this mantra, individuals can create a more harmonious and productive environment, ultimately leading to personal and collective growth.
