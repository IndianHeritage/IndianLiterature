आ नो मित्रावरुणा घृतैर गव्यूतिम उक्षताम् |मध्वा राजांसि सुक्रतू ||
ā no mitrāvaruṇā ghṛtair gavyūtim ukṣatam | madhvā rajāṁsi sukratū ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: May (we) approach
    • no: us
    • mitrāvaruṇā: (with) Mitra and Varuna (the deities of friendship and cosmic order)
    • ghṛtair: with ghee (clarified butter)
    • gavyūtim: (to) the abundance of cows (wealth)
    • ukṣatam: may (they) bestow
    • madhvā: with sweetness
    • rajāṁsi: (the) riches
    • sukratū: (of) good deeds
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer for blessings from the deities Mitra and Varuna, asking for abundance and prosperity, symbolized by cows, which were a measure of wealth in ancient times. It emphasizes the importance of good deeds and the sweetness of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, cows represent not just wealth but also sustenance. A farmer praying for a good harvest and healthy livestock embodies the essence of this mantra.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be interpreted as a call for collaboration and harmony in relationships, whether in business or personal life, reflecting the roles of Mitra (friendship) and Varuna (cosmic order).
    • In community gatherings, invoking this mantra can serve as a reminder to engage in good deeds and foster a spirit of generosity and kindness among members.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of prosperity, good deeds, and harmonious relationships. It serves as a reminder that true wealth is not just material but also rooted in ethical living and community bonds.
    • In daily life, individuals can apply the teachings of this mantra by striving for integrity in their actions, fostering friendships, and contributing positively to their communities.
    • In spiritual practices, reciting this mantra can invoke a sense of gratitude and awareness of the abundance in one's life, encouraging a mindset of positivity and generosity.
