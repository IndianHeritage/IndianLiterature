आ भण्डमाने उपाके नक्तोषासाऽ सुपेशसाः |यह्वी ऋतस्य मातराऽ सिदताम् बर्हिर् आ सुमत् ||
ā bhandamāne upāke naktoṣāsā supeśasā | yahvī ṛtasya mātarā sīdatām barhir ā sumat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • bhandamāne: bondage or attachment
    • upāke: to the one who is devoted or dedicated
    • naktoṣāsā: the night or darkness
    • supeśasā: with good intentions or auspiciousness
    • yahvī: who
    • ṛtasya: of truth or righteousness
    • mātarā: motherly or nurturing
    • sīdatām: may it be established or may it settle
    • barhir: on the ground or earth
    • ā: from
    • sumat: good thoughts or wisdom
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of liberation from bondage and the importance of nurturing truth and righteousness.
    • The phrase "ā bhandamāne" suggests a desire to be free from attachments that bind us, similar to how one might seek to overcome personal challenges or addictions in life.
    • "Upāke naktoṣāsā" indicates a call to those who are devoted to rise above darkness, akin to individuals who strive to bring positivity and light into their communities.
    • The mention of "ṛtasya mātarā" emphasizes the nurturing aspect of truth, much like a mother who guides her children towards moral values and ethical living.
    • "Sīdatām barhir ā sumat" can be seen as a prayer for wisdom to take root in our lives, encouraging us to cultivate good thoughts and intentions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often seek to break free from negative habits or toxic relationships, reflecting the essence of "ā bhandamāne."
    • Community leaders and activists work tirelessly to uplift their neighborhoods, embodying the spirit of "upāke naktoṣāsā" by promoting education and awareness.
    • Parents and mentors play a crucial role in instilling values in the younger generation, paralleling "ṛtasya mātarā" as they nurture future leaders.
    • Mindfulness practices, such as meditation, encourage individuals to cultivate "sumat," leading to a more peaceful and thoughtful existence.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking liberation from attachments and embracing truth and righteousness in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement to nurture good thoughts and intentions, which can lead to personal growth and positive societal change.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can help individuals and communities overcome challenges, foster resilience, and promote a harmonious existence.
