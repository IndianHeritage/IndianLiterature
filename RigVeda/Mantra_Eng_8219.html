तं त्वा धार्तारम ओण्योः पवमान स्वर्दृशम् |हिन्वे वाजेषु वाजिनम् ||
taṁ tvā dhartāram oṇyo3ḥ pavamāna svardṛśam | hinve vājeṣu vājinam ||
  • taṁ tvā - This phrase translates to "that you" or "to you." It signifies a direct address to a divine entity or force, indicating a personal connection or invocation.
  • dhartāram - This word means "the supporter" or "the sustainer." It refers to a force or deity that provides stability and support in life, akin to a guardian or protector.
  • oṇyoḥ - This term can be interpreted as "of the other" or "of the different." It suggests a duality or a relationship between two entities, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all things.
  • pavamāna - This word means "the purifying one" or "the wind." It symbolizes the cleansing and revitalizing aspects of nature, often associated with the breath of life or spiritual awakening.
  • svardṛśam - This translates to "the one who sees the light" or "the radiant one." It represents enlightenment, wisdom, and the pursuit of higher knowledge.
  • hinve - This term means "to bestow" or "to grant." It indicates an act of giving or providing, often in the context of blessings or gifts from the divine.
  • vājeṣu - This word translates to "in the riches" or "in the wealth." It signifies abundance, prosperity, and the material aspects of life that contribute to well-being.
  • vājinam - This means "the one who is strong" or "the powerful one." It refers to a force or entity that embodies strength and capability, often associated with success and achievement.

In summary, this mantra invokes a divine force that supports and sustains life, purifies and enlightens, and bestows blessings of wealth and strength. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking higher knowledge and spiritual awakening.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in daily life as a reminder to seek support and guidance from higher powers during challenging times.
  • It encourages individuals to pursue knowledge and enlightenment, fostering personal growth and development.
  • The invocation of abundance and strength can inspire confidence in one's abilities and the pursuit of goals, whether in personal or professional life.
  • In moments of uncertainty, reciting this mantra can serve as a source of comfort and motivation, reinforcing the belief in divine support and the interconnectedness of all beings.
