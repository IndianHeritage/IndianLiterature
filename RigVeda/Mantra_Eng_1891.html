अगस्त्यः खनमानः खनित्रैः प्रजाम् अपत्यं बलम् इच्छमानः |उभौ वर्णाव ऋषिः उग्रः पुपोष सत्या देवेषु आशिषो जगाम ||
agastyaḥ khanamānaḥ khanitraiḥ prajām apatyam balam icchamānaḥ | ubhau varṇāv ṛṣir ugraḥ pupoṣa satyā deveṣv āśiṣo jagāma ||
  • Agastyaḥ: Refers to the sage Agastya, a revered figure in Hindu mythology known for his wisdom and knowledge.
  • Khanamānaḥ: This term can be interpreted as one who is capable of creating or manifesting. It signifies the ability to bring forth or generate.
  • Khanitraiḥ: This word relates to the act of digging or excavating, symbolizing the effort to uncover or reveal deeper truths.
  • Prajām: Means 'offspring' or 'creatures', indicating the importance of progeny and the continuation of life.
  • Apatyam: Refers to descendants or progeny, emphasizing the significance of lineage and heritage.
  • Balam: Translates to 'strength' or 'power', highlighting the importance of vitality and energy in life.
  • Icchamānaḥ: Means 'desiring' or 'wishing', indicating the aspirations and intentions of individuals.
  • Ubhau: Means 'both', suggesting a duality or balance in the context of the mantra.
  • Varṇāv: Refers to 'colors' or 'qualities', which can symbolize diversity and the various aspects of life.
  • Ṛṣir: Means 'sage' or 'seer', indicating wisdom and insight.
  • Ugraḥ: Translates to 'fierce' or 'powerful', suggesting strength and intensity.
  • Pupoṣa: Means 'nourished' or 'sustained', indicating the importance of nurturing and support.
  • Satyā: Refers to 'truth', emphasizing the value of honesty and integrity.
  • Deveṣv: Means 'among the gods', indicating a divine context or connection.
  • Āśiṣo: Refers to 'blessings', highlighting the significance of divine favor and support.
  • Jagāma: Means 'went' or 'arrived', indicating a journey or progression.

The mantra speaks to the themes of creation, strength, and the pursuit of truth. It emphasizes the role of wisdom and the importance of nurturing both progeny and aspirations. The sage Agastya, as a central figure, represents the quest for knowledge and the ability to manifest desires through effort and intention.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In parenting, it highlights the importance of nurturing children (prajām) with wisdom and strength (balam) to help them grow into capable individuals.
  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to dig deep (khanitraiḥ) into their own potential and aspirations (icchamānaḥ) to achieve their goals.
  • In community building, it signifies the need for diverse qualities (varṇāv) and the collective strength (bala) of individuals working together towards a common purpose.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of wisdom, strength, and truth. It serves as a reminder to cultivate our aspirations and nurture our relationships, both with our progeny and within our communities, to create a harmonious and prosperous existence.
