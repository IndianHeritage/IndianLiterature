स ईं पाहि य ऋजिषी तरुत्रो यः शिप्रवान वृषभो यो मतीनाम् |यो गोत्रभिद् वज्रभृत् यो हरिष्ठाः स इन्द्र चित्रां अभि तृन्धि वाजान् ||
sa īm pāhi ya ṛjīṣī tarutro yaḥ śipravān vṛṣabho yo matīnām | yo gotrabhid vajrabhṛd yo hariṣṭhāḥ sa indra citrām̐ abhi tṛndhi vājān ||
  • sa īm pāhi: This phrase translates to "may he protect us." It signifies a plea for divine protection and guidance. In real life, this can be seen in the way people seek safety and security in their daily lives, whether through community support, family, or spiritual practices.
  • ya ṛjīṣī: This means "who is the one who is powerful." It refers to a deity or force that embodies strength and capability. In modern contexts, this can relate to leaders or figures in society who inspire strength and resilience, such as community leaders or activists.
  • tarutro yaḥ: This translates to "who is the protector of the trees." Trees symbolize life, growth, and sustainability. In today's world, this can be connected to environmental movements that advocate for the protection of nature and biodiversity.
  • śipravān vṛṣabho: This phrase means "the one who is swift and strong." It highlights the qualities of agility and power. In real life, this can be likened to athletes or individuals who excel in their fields through a combination of speed and strength, such as sprinters or martial artists.
  • yo matīnām: This translates to "who is the source of thoughts." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and intellect. In contemporary society, this can be seen in educators, philosophers, and thinkers who shape our understanding and perspectives.
  • yo gotrabhid vajrabhṛd: This means "who is the one who knows the lineage and carries the thunderbolt." It signifies the importance of heritage and the power that comes from understanding one's roots. In real life, this can relate to individuals who take pride in their ancestry and use that knowledge to empower themselves and their communities.
  • yo hariṣṭhāḥ: This translates to "who is the best among the best." It denotes excellence and superiority. In modern contexts, this can refer to top performers in various fields, such as scientists, artists, or leaders who set benchmarks for success.
  • sa indra citrām̐ abhi tṛndhi vājān: This phrase means "may he, Indra, grant us the splendid wealth." Indra is often associated with rain, thunder, and prosperity. In real life, this can be interpreted as a call for abundance and prosperity in our endeavors, whether in business, personal growth, or community development.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for protection, strength, wisdom, and prosperity. Its significance lies in the holistic approach to life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of nature, intellect, heritage, and excellence. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as community initiatives for environmental protection, personal development through education, and the pursuit of excellence in professional fields. By invoking these qualities, individuals and communities can strive for a balanced and prosperous existence.
