अभि त्वा शूर नोनुमो 'दुग्धा इव धेनवः |ईशानम अस्य जगतः स्वदृशम् ईशानम इन्द्र तस्थुषः ||
abhi tvā śūra nonumo 'dugdhā iva dhenavaḥ | īśānam asya jagataḥ svardṛśam īśānam indra tasthuṣaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, for
    • tvā: you
    • śūra: brave, heroic
    • nonumo: we invoke, we praise
    • 'dugdhā: like milked, nourished
    • iva: like
    • dhenavaḥ: cows, nurturers
    • īśānam: lord, master
    • asya: of this
    • jagataḥ: of the world
    • svardṛśam: of radiant vision
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • tasthuṣaḥ: may you stand, may you be present
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer invoking the bravery and strength of a divine figure, often associated with Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature. It expresses a desire for the divine to be present and to bless the world with their nurturing qualities, akin to how cows provide milk.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, leaders often invoke the spirit of bravery and strength when facing challenges, much like how this mantra calls upon divine strength.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek inspiration from this mantra during difficult times, reminding themselves to embody bravery and resilience.
    • In educational settings, teachers may use this mantra to encourage students to be courageous in their pursuits, fostering a nurturing environment similar to that of a mother cow providing milk.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine strength and nurturing qualities in the world. It emphasizes the importance of bravery and the role of leaders in guiding their communities. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek strength in adversity, promote nurturing relationships, and encourage a sense of community support. By invoking these qualities, we can create a more resilient and compassionate society.
