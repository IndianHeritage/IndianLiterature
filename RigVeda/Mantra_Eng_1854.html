त्वं राजेन्द्र ये च देवाः रक्षां नृणां पाहि असुर त्वम् अस्मान् |त्वं सत्पतिः मगवाः नस् तरुत्रस् त्वं सत्यो वसवानः सहोदाः ||
tvaṁ rājendra ye ca devā rakṣā nṝn pāhy asura tvam asmān | tvaṁ satpatir maghavā nas tarutras tvaṁ satyo vasavānaḥ sahodāḥ ||
  • tvaṁ rājendra: This phrase translates to "You, O King of Kings." It signifies the supreme authority and leadership qualities attributed to a divine figure. In real life, this can be related to leaders who inspire and guide their communities, such as a respected political leader or a community elder.
  • ye ca devā: Meaning "and the gods," this part acknowledges the presence of divine beings. In everyday life, this can be seen in the reverence people have for spiritual leaders or mentors who guide them in their moral and ethical decisions.
  • rakṣā nṝn: This translates to "protect us, O beings." It is a plea for protection and safety. In a real-world context, this can be likened to the role of law enforcement or community support systems that work to ensure the safety and well-being of individuals.
  • pāhy asura tvam asmān: This means "protect us from the demons." Here, "demons" can symbolize negative forces or challenges in life, such as crime, corruption, or personal struggles. This reflects the need for resilience and support in overcoming life's adversities.
  • tvaṁ satpatir: This translates to "You, the true path." It emphasizes the importance of following a righteous and ethical path in life. In practical terms, this can relate to individuals who choose to live by principles of honesty and integrity, serving as role models for others.
  • maghavā nas: Meaning "may you grant us abundance," this part expresses a desire for prosperity and success. In real life, this can be seen in the aspirations of individuals and communities striving for economic growth and well-being.
  • tarutras: This translates to "through the trees," which can symbolize growth and nurturing. In a modern context, this can relate to environmental conservation efforts and the importance of maintaining a healthy ecosystem for future generations.
  • tvaṁ satyo vasavānaḥ sahodāḥ: This means "You, the true and prosperous ones." It highlights the qualities of truthfulness and prosperity. In real life, this can be reflected in businesses and organizations that prioritize ethical practices and social responsibility, contributing positively to society.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for divine protection, guidance, and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of ethical leadership, community support, and resilience against negative forces. In today's world, the principles derived from this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in leadership roles, community service, and personal development. By embodying these values, individuals and societies can strive for a harmonious and prosperous existence.
