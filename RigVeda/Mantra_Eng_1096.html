तं अप्सन्त शवास उत्सवेषु नरो नरं अवसे तं धनाय |सो अन्धे चित तमसि ज्योतिर विदन मरुत्वान नो भवत्व इन्द्र ऊती ||
tam apsanta śavasa utsaveṣu naro naram avase taṁ dhanāya | so andhe cit tamasi jyotir vidan marutvān no bhavatv indra ūtī ||
  • tam apsanta: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which is tranquil" or "that which is peaceful." In real life, this can refer to a state of calmness that one seeks in meditation or during stressful situations.
  • śavasa utsaveṣu: Here, "śavasa" means "in the midst of celebrations" or "in the context of festivities." This can relate to how we often seek peace and tranquility even during joyous occasions, such as weddings or festivals, where chaos can sometimes overshadow the joy.
  • naro naram avase: This translates to "humans among humans." It emphasizes the importance of community and connection. In real life, this can be seen in how people come together to support each other in times of need, such as during natural disasters or community service events.
  • taṁ dhanāya: This phrase means "that for wealth" or "that for prosperity." It signifies the pursuit of wealth not just in material terms but also in terms of knowledge, relationships, and spiritual growth. For example, investing time in education or nurturing relationships can lead to a wealthier life experience.
  • so andhe cit tamasi: This can be interpreted as "he who is in darkness." It reflects the human condition of ignorance or lack of awareness. In real life, this can be related to individuals who are unaware of their potential or the opportunities around them, often due to societal or personal limitations.
  • jyotir vidan: This means "the light of knowledge." It signifies the importance of enlightenment and understanding. In practical terms, this can be seen in the pursuit of education, where knowledge acts as a guiding light in one's life journey.
  • marutvān no bhavatv indra ūtī: This phrase can be interpreted as "may the powerful Indra bless us." Indra is often seen as a deity of strength and power. In real life, this can symbolize the need for strength and support from higher powers or the universe in overcoming challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking peace and prosperity amidst the chaos of life. It emphasizes the importance of community, knowledge, and divine support in navigating through life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during times of celebration, personal growth, or when facing difficulties, reminding us to seek tranquility, support one another, and pursue knowledge for a fulfilling life. It serves as a guiding principle for individuals striving for balance and enlightenment in their daily lives.
