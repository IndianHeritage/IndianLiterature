यद इमा वाजयन्न अहम् ओषधीर हस्त आदधे |आत्मा यक्ष्मस्य नश्यति पुरा जीवगृह्भो यथा ||
yad imā vājayann aham oṣadhīr hasta ādadhe | ātmā yakṣmasya naśyati purā jīvagṛbho yathā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • imā: these
    • vājayann: bestowing strength or vigor
    • aham: I
    • oṣadhīr: herbs or plants
    • hasta: hand
    • ādadhē: I take or grasp
    • ātman: self or soul
    • yakṣmasya: of illness or disease
    • naśyati: perishes or is destroyed
    • purā: in the past
    • jīvagṛbho: as one who grasps life
    • yathā: just as
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the connection between the self and the natural world, particularly the healing properties of herbs.
    • The phrase "yad imā vājayann aham oṣadhīr" suggests that the speaker is invoking the strength and healing qualities of plants.
    • By saying "hasta ādadhe," it indicates an active engagement, where the speaker takes these healing herbs into their own hands.
    • The latter part, "ātma yakṣmasya naśyati purā jīvagṛbho yathā," reflects on the idea that the self can overcome illness, much like how one who grasps life can avoid destruction.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern herbal medicine, many people turn to natural remedies for healing, reflecting the ancient wisdom of the RigVeda.
    • For instance, turmeric is often used for its anti-inflammatory properties, embodying the idea of taking herbs into one's hands for health.
    • Yoga and meditation practices also emphasize the connection between the self and nature, promoting holistic well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of nature in healing and the empowerment of individuals to take charge of their health.
    • It serves as a reminder of the ancient belief in the interconnectedness of life, health, and the natural world.
    • In contemporary life, it encourages us to explore natural remedies and to recognize the importance of self-care and holistic approaches to health.
