एवेद एते प्रतिमā रोचमानā अनेद्यः श्रव एषो दधानाः |सञ्चक्ष्यā मरुतश चन्द्रवर्णā अच्छान्त मे छदायाथā च नूनम् ||
eved ete prati mā rocamānā anedyaḥ śrava eṣo dadhānāḥ | saṁcakṣyā marutaś candravarṇā acchānta me chadayāthā ca nūnam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eved: indeed, this
    • ete: these
    • prati: towards
    • mā: not
    • rocamānā: shining, illuminating
    • anedyaḥ: pure, untainted
    • śrava: fame, glory
    • eṣo: this
    • dadhānāḥ: holding, possessing
    • saṁcakṣyā: with the Maruts (storm deities)
    • marutaś: of the Maruts
    • candravarṇā: moon-colored, radiant
    • acchānta: peaceful, calm
    • me: my
    • chadayāthā: may you cover, protect
    • ca: and
    • nūnam: certainly, indeed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine qualities of purity and illumination.
    • It emphasizes the importance of holding onto fame and glory that is untainted.
    • The reference to the Maruts suggests a connection to natural forces, indicating that these qualities are essential for harmony in life.
    • The plea for protection signifies a desire for guidance and safety in one's endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, maintaining integrity and purity in one's work can lead to a lasting reputation.
    • In personal relationships, being honest and transparent fosters trust and strengthens bonds.
    • During challenging times, seeking guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders can provide clarity and protection.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of purity, integrity, and the pursuit of untainted glory.
    • It encourages individuals to seek protection and guidance from higher powers or natural forces in their journey.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence, both personally and professionally.
