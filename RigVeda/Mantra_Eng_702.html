अस्येदेव शवसां शुषन्तं वि वृश्चद वज्रेण वृतं इन्द्रः |गा न व्राणां अवनीर अमुñचद अभि श्रवो दावने सचेताḥ ||
asyed eva śavasā śuṣantaṁ vi vṛścad vajreṇa vṛtram indraḥ | gā na vrāṇā avanīr amuñcad abhi śravo dāvane sacetāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "asyed eva śavasā śuṣantaṁ vi vṛścad vajreṇa vṛtram indraḥ | gā na vrāṇā avanīr amuñcad abhi śravo dāvane sacetāḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra speaks of Indra, the king of gods, who wields the thunderbolt (vajra) to vanquish the demon Vritra, representing obstacles and ignorance. It calls upon the divine to release the waters (gā) and to bestow strength and wisdom (śravo) upon those who are devoted and aware (sacetāḥ).

  • Word Breakdown:
    • Indra: The chief deity in Vedic mythology, symbolizing power and strength.
    • Vritra: A serpent or dragon that represents drought and obstacles, often seen as a metaphor for ignorance or challenges in life.
    • Vajra: The thunderbolt, symbolizing the power to overcome challenges and ignorance.
    • Gā: Refers to the waters, which symbolize nourishment, abundance, and life.
    • Śravo: Represents fame, glory, and wisdom, indicating the importance of knowledge and awareness.
    • Sacetāḥ: Those who are aware or conscious, emphasizing the need for mindfulness and understanding.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, when facing challenges such as job loss or health issues, one can invoke the spirit of Indra by seeking strength and clarity to overcome these obstacles, much like how Indra defeated Vritra.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can inspire collective action to address societal issues, such as drought or poverty, by encouraging individuals to come together and share resources, akin to the release of waters.
    • In educational settings, the emphasis on wisdom (śravo) can motivate students to pursue knowledge and awareness, fostering a culture of learning and critical thinking.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, awareness, and the overcoming of obstacles. It serves as a reminder that, like Indra, we have the power to confront and conquer the challenges in our lives. By invoking the qualities of mindfulness and collective action, we can create a more abundant and harmonious existence. This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal struggles to community challenges, encouraging individuals to seek strength and wisdom in their endeavors.
