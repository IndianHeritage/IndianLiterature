एवा रातिस तुवीमघ विश्वेभिर धायि धातृभिः |अधाऽ चिद इन्द्र मे सचा ||
evā rātis tuvīmagha viśvebhir dhāyi dhātṛbhiḥ | adhā cid indra me sacā ||
  • evā rātis: This phrase can be translated as "this is the delight" or "this is the joy." It signifies the essence of happiness and fulfillment in life.
  • tuvīmagha: This term refers to "your great power" or "your strength." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and harnessing one's inner strength and capabilities.
  • viśvebhir dhāyi dhātṛbhiḥ: This part translates to "with all the sustaining forces." It suggests the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe that support and nurture life.
  • adhā cid indra me sacā: This can be interpreted as "may my offerings reach you, O Indra, with truth." Indra is often seen as a deity of strength and rain, symbolizing abundance and prosperity.

The mantra as a whole can be understood as a prayer or invocation for joy and strength, recognizing the supportive forces of the universe, and seeking the blessings of Indra for truth and abundance in life.


Real-Life Examples:

  • In personal development, the idea of "this is the delight" can be applied when individuals pursue their passions, leading to a fulfilling life.
  • Recognizing "your great power" can inspire people to overcome challenges, such as a student facing academic pressure or an athlete striving for excellence.
  • The concept of "sustaining forces" can be seen in community support systems, where individuals come together to help each other in times of need.
  • Offering prayers or intentions to a higher power, like Indra, can be a practice in various cultures, where people seek guidance and blessings for their endeavors.

Conclusion:

This mantra highlights the significance of joy, strength, and interconnectedness in our lives. It encourages individuals to recognize their inner power and the support they receive from the universe. In real-life scenarios, this can manifest in personal growth, community support, and spiritual practices. By invoking these principles, one can cultivate a more fulfilling and abundant life.
