यत् ते भूमिं चतुर्भृष्टिम मनो जगाम दूरकम् |तत् त आ वर्तयामसिह क्षयाय जीवसे ||
yat te bhūmiṁ caturbhṛṣṭim mano jagāma dūrakam | tat ta ā vartayāmasīha kṣayāya jīvase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yat: that which
    • te: of you
    • bhūmiṁ: the earth
    • caturbhṛṣṭim: fourfold (or four directions)
    • mano: mind
    • jagāma: has gone
    • dūrakam: far away
    • tat: that
    • ta: you
    • ā: indeed
    • vartayāmasi: we are moving
    • ha: indeed
    • kṣayāya: for destruction
    • jīvase: for life
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the connection between the mind and the earth, emphasizing the importance of awareness and intention in our actions.
    • The phrase "yat te bhūmiṁ caturbhṛṣṭim" suggests that the earth, in all its dimensions and directions, is influenced by our thoughts and intentions.
    • "mano jagāma dūrakam" indicates that our minds can travel far, transcending physical boundaries, which can be likened to how our thoughts can impact our reality.
    • The latter part, "tat ta ā vartayāmasīha kṣayāya jīvase," implies that our actions, driven by our thoughts, can lead to either destruction or the sustenance of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In environmental conservation, the mantra can be applied to the idea that our collective thoughts and intentions towards the earth can lead to either its preservation or degradation. For instance, community efforts to clean up local parks reflect the positive impact of mindful actions.
    • In personal development, the concept of "mind traveling" can be seen in practices like meditation, where individuals visualize their goals and aspirations, thereby influencing their reality and life choices.
    • In social movements, the mantra resonates with the idea that collective consciousness and intention can lead to significant societal changes, such as movements for equality and justice.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the profound connection between our thoughts, actions, and the world around us.
    • It serves as a reminder that our intentions can shape our reality, urging us to be mindful of our thoughts and actions.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, from personal growth to environmental stewardship, highlighting the importance of conscious living.
