मर्त्यो न शुभ्रस् तन्म मृजानोऽत्यो न सृत्वा सानये धनानाम् |वृषेवं यूथा परि कोशम् अर्षन् कनिक्रदच चाम्वोऽर आविवेश ||
maryo na śubhras tanvam mṛjāno 'tyo na sṛtvā sanaye dhanānām | vṛṣeva yūthā pari kośam arṣan kanikradac camvo3r ā viveśa ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • maryo: mortal, human being
    • na: not
    • śubhras: pure, auspicious
    • tanvam: body, form
    • mṛjāno: having been cleansed
    • 'tyo: beyond, transcending
    • na: not
    • sṛtvā: having created
    • sanaye: for the purpose of wealth
    • dhanānām: of the wealthy
    • vṛṣeva: like a bull
    • yūthā: herd, group
    • pari: around
    • kośam: treasure, wealth
    • arṣan: of the seer, sage
    • kanikradac: having made a sound, noise
    • camvo3r: of the chariot, vehicle
    • ā: from
    • viveśa: entered
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the human condition and the pursuit of purity and wealth.
    • The phrase "maryo na śubhras tanvam" suggests that while we are mortal beings, we strive for a pure and auspicious existence.
    • The idea of "mṛjāno 'tyo" indicates a cleansing or purification process that transcends mere physical existence.
    • Wealth, represented by "dhanānām," is acknowledged as a significant aspect of life, but it is not the ultimate goal.
    • The metaphor of "vṛṣeva yūthā" likens the pursuit of wealth to a bull leading a herd, emphasizing strength and unity in this endeavor.
    • The mantra concludes with the imagery of entering a chariot, symbolizing the journey of life and the choices we make along the way.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, individuals often seek wealth and success, but the mantra reminds us to pursue these goals with purity of intention and ethical means.
    • For instance, a businessperson may strive for financial success while ensuring that their practices are ethical and contribute positively to society.
    • The concept of community and support, as suggested by "yūthā," can be seen in collaborative work environments where teamwork leads to shared success.
    • Moreover, the idea of cleansing oneself from negative influences can be applied in personal development, where individuals work on self-improvement and mindfulness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of balancing the pursuit of wealth with the need for purity and ethical living.
    • It serves as a reminder that while material success is valuable, it should not come at the cost of our moral and ethical values.
    • In real life, this mantra can guide individuals in their personal and professional lives, encouraging them to seek success while remaining grounded in their principles.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the journey of life as a chariot ride, where the choices we make define our path and destination.
