त्वं हि नः पिता वसो त्वं माता शतक्रतो बभूविथ |अधाऽते सुम्नम इमहे ||
tvaṁ hi naḥ pitā vaso tvam mātā śatakrato babhūvitha | adhā te sumnam īmahe ||
  • tvaṁ hi naḥ pitā vaso: This phrase translates to "You are indeed our father, O Vasu." In this context, "Vasu" refers to a deity associated with wealth and prosperity. The term "pitā" signifies a paternal figure, indicating a source of guidance and support.
  • tvam mātā śatakrato babhūvitha: This means "You are our mother, O Śatakratu." Here, "mātā" represents a maternal figure, emphasizing nurturing and care. "Śatakratu" is another name for Indra, the king of gods, known for his strength and valor.
  • adhā te sumnam īmahe: This translates to "We invoke your auspiciousness." The word "sumnam" refers to good fortune or auspiciousness, indicating a desire for blessings and positive outcomes in life.

  • Significance: This mantra highlights the duality of divine parental figures, representing both the protective and nurturing aspects of the divine. It acknowledges the importance of seeking blessings from these higher powers for prosperity and well-being.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be invoked during significant events such as weddings, childbirth, or new ventures, where blessings from divine parental figures are sought for success and happiness. For instance, a couple may recite this mantra during their wedding ceremony to invoke blessings for a prosperous and harmonious marriage.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking guidance and blessings from higher powers in our lives. It emphasizes the need for both strength and nurturing, reflecting the balance required for a fulfilling life. By invoking these divine figures, individuals can cultivate a sense of security and hope, fostering a positive environment for growth and prosperity.
