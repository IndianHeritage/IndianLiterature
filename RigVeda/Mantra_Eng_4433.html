अपाम उपस्थे महिषा अगृभ्णत विषो राजानम उप तस्थुर ऋग्मियम् |आ दूतो अग्निम अभरद विवस्वतो वैश्वानरम् मातरिश्वा परावतः ||
apām upasthe mahiṣā agṛbhṇata viśo rājānam upa tasthur ṛgmiyam | ā dūto agnim abharad vivasvato vaiśvānaram mātariśvā parāvataḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apām: of the waters
    • upasthe: in the presence
    • mahiṣā: the great one (often referring to the Earth or a powerful entity)
    • agṛbhṇata: has grasped
    • viśo: of the people or community
    • rājānam: the king or ruler
    • upa: near or towards
    • tasthur: they have approached
    • ṛgmiyam: the hymns or praises
    • ā: from
    • dūto: the messenger
    • agnim: fire
    • abharad: has brought
    • vivasvato: of the shining one (often referring to the Sun)
    • vaiśvānaram: the universal fire
    • mātariśvā: the wind or the one who moves in the sky
    • parāvataḥ: the highest or supreme
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the waters and their connection to the Earth, symbolizing nourishment and life.
    • The "great one" signifies a powerful force, possibly the Earth itself, which supports life.
    • The mention of the king indicates the importance of leadership and guidance within a community.
    • The approach of hymns suggests the significance of praise and reverence in spiritual practices.
    • The messenger bringing fire symbolizes the transmission of knowledge, energy, and transformation.
    • The reference to the universal fire and the wind highlights the interconnectedness of all elements in nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the waters (rivers, rain) are essential for crops, representing the nurturing aspect of nature.
    • Leadership in communities can be likened to a king who guides and protects, much like modern leaders in society.
    • Spiritual practices often involve hymns and praises, reflecting the importance of gratitude and connection to the divine.
    • The concept of fire can be seen in various cultures as a symbol of transformation, such as in cooking or rituals.
    • The wind, as a natural force, reminds us of the unseen energies that influence our lives, such as emotions and thoughts.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of nature, community, and spirituality in our lives.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the interconnectedness of all elements and the importance of leadership and reverence.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to nurture our environment, respect our leaders, and engage in spiritual practices that promote harmony.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of our responsibility towards nature and each other, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
