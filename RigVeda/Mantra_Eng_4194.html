मन्त्र: उता मे वोकतादिति सुतसोमे रथवीतौ |न कामो अप वेति मे ||
uta me vocatād iti sutasome rathavītau | na kāmo apa veti me ||
  • Translation: "He calls me, the one who is born of the Soma, in the chariot of the sun; no desire can take me away."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • uta: a call or invocation, often used in the context of a divine call.
    • me: refers to 'me' or 'myself', indicating a personal connection.
    • vocātād: derived from 'vocare', meaning 'to call' or 'to invoke'.
    • iti: means 'thus' or 'so', often used to indicate a conclusion or a statement.
    • sutasome: 'Soma' refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing vitality and divine inspiration.
    • rathavītau: 'ratha' means 'chariot', and 'vītau' refers to 'in the context of' or 'in the presence of'.
    • na: means 'not', indicating negation.
    • kāmo: refers to 'desire' or 'wish', often associated with worldly attachments.
    • apa: means 'away' or 'from', indicating separation.
    • veti: means 'to know' or 'to take', often used in the context of understanding or grasping something.

  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra reflects a deep spiritual connection and the idea of being called by a higher power or divine force.
    • The reference to 'Soma' symbolizes the essence of life and the divine inspiration that guides individuals on their journey.
    • The 'chariot of the sun' can be interpreted as the journey of life, where the sun represents enlightenment, knowledge, and the pursuit of truth.
    • The phrase 'no desire can take me away' emphasizes the importance of detachment from worldly desires, suggesting that true fulfillment comes from spiritual connection rather than material pursuits.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, individuals often feel overwhelmed by desires and material pursuits. This mantra serves as a reminder to focus on spiritual growth and inner peace.
    • For instance, a person may find themselves caught in the rat race of career advancement, but by reflecting on this mantra, they may choose to prioritize their mental well-being and personal relationships instead.
    • In meditation practices, this mantra can be used to center oneself, reminding practitioners to listen to their inner voice and seek guidance from a higher power.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of spiritual calling and the importance of remaining grounded amidst the distractions of life.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to seek a deeper connection with the divine and to prioritize spiritual fulfillment over transient desires.
    • In various life scenarios, whether in personal struggles or during moments of reflection, this mantra can serve as a guiding principle, encouraging individuals to stay true to their spiritual path.
