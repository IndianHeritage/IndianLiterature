अभि प्र वः सुराधसम इन्द्रं अर्च यथा विदे |यो जरीतृभ्यो मगवाः पुरूवसुः सहस्रेणेव शिक्षति ||
abhi pra vaḥ surādhasam indram arca yathā vide | yo jaritṛbhyo maghavā purūvasuḥ sahasreṇeva śikṣati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards
    • pra: forth
    • vaḥ: you (plural)
    • surādhasam: the best of the gods
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods
    • arca: worship
    • yathā: as
    • vide: known
    • yo: who
    • jaritṛbhyo: to the singers
    • maghavā: the powerful one
    • purūvasuḥ: the one who has many riches
    • sahasreṇeva: like a thousand
    • śikṣati: teaches
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the king of gods, and emphasizes the importance of worshipping him.
    • It suggests that one should approach the divine with reverence and seek guidance.
    • The phrase "who teaches like a thousand" indicates the immense wisdom and knowledge that Indra imparts to his devotees.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders can be seen as modern-day Indras, guiding their teams with wisdom and experience.
    • In educational institutions, teachers embody the essence of this mantra by imparting knowledge and shaping the future of their students.
    • In personal life, individuals who seek guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders can relate to the idea of worshipping and learning from a higher power.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and wisdom in all aspects of life.
    • It encourages individuals to approach their goals with reverence and to learn from those who possess greater knowledge.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in leadership, education, or personal growth, the principles of respect, learning, and guidance are universally applicable.
