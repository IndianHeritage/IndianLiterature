अति तृष्टं ववक्षिथाथैव सुमना असि |प्र-प्राण्ये यान्ति पर्य अन्य आसते येषां सख्ये असि श्रितः ||
ati tṛṣṭaṁ vavakṣithāthaiva sumanā asi | pra-prānye yanti pary anya āsate yeṣāṁ sakhye asi śritaḥ ||
  • ati tṛṣṭaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "beyond thirst" or "exceeding desire." It signifies a state of fulfillment or satisfaction that transcends basic needs.
  • vavakṣithāthaiva: This means "you have spoken" or "you have declared." It indicates the act of communication or expression of thoughts and intentions.
  • sumanā asi: Translated as "you are of good mind" or "you are benevolent." This phrase emphasizes the importance of a positive and kind disposition.
  • pra-prānye: This can be understood as "in the vital breath" or "in the essence of life." It refers to the fundamental life force that sustains existence.
  • yanti pary anya: This translates to "they go towards another" or "they move towards something else." It suggests a journey or transition towards a different state or understanding.
  • āsate: This means "they dwell" or "they reside." It indicates a state of being or existence in a particular condition or place.
  • yeṣāṁ sakhye asi śritaḥ: This phrase translates to "those who are your friends are supported." It highlights the significance of friendship and support in one's life.

In summary, this mantra emphasizes the importance of transcending basic desires and achieving a state of fulfillment through positive thoughts and benevolence. It suggests that true support and friendship are essential for a fulfilling life. The mantra can be applied in real-life scenarios such as:


  • In personal relationships, fostering a positive mindset and supporting friends can lead to deeper connections and mutual growth.
  • In professional settings, collaboration and kindness can create a harmonious work environment, leading to greater productivity and satisfaction.
  • In spiritual practices, seeking fulfillment beyond material desires can lead to inner peace and a more meaningful existence.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for a higher state of being, where one seeks fulfillment through positive relationships and a benevolent mindset, ultimately leading to a more enriched life.
