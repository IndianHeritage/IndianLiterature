अस्माकम् अत्र पितरस् त आसन् सप्त ऋषयो दौरगहे बद्ध्यमाने | त आयजन्त त्रसदस्युम अस्य इन्द्रं न वृत्रतुरम् अर्धदेवम् ||
asmākam atra pitaras ta āsan sapta ṛṣayo daurgahe badhyamāne | ta āyajanta trasadasyum asyā indraṁ na vṛtraturam ardhadevam ||
  • Translation: "Our fathers who are here, the seven sages, when bound by difficulties, invoked Indra, the slayer of Vritra, the half-god."

  • Meaning of Key Terms:
    • asmākam: Our, indicating a sense of belonging and community.
    • atra: Here, referring to the present moment or situation.
    • pitaras: Fathers, symbolizing ancestors or forefathers who guide and protect.
    • sapta ṛṣayo: Seven sages, representing wisdom and knowledge.
    • daurgahe: In difficulties, highlighting the challenges faced in life.
    • badhyamāne: When bound or constrained, indicating a state of struggle.
    • āyajanta: Invoked or worshipped, showing the act of seeking divine help.
    • trasadasyum: Indra, the powerful deity, known for his strength and ability to overcome obstacles.
    • vṛtraturam: Slayer of Vritra, symbolizing the removal of obstacles and challenges.
    • ardhadevam: Half-god, indicating the divine nature of Indra and his role in human affairs.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, such as losing a job or facing health issues, individuals often turn to their family and community for support, much like invoking the guidance of ancestors.
    • When facing academic challenges, students may seek the wisdom of teachers or mentors, akin to the seven sages, to help them navigate their difficulties.
    • In moments of despair, people may engage in prayer or meditation, calling upon a higher power for strength and guidance, similar to the invocation of Indra.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of community, wisdom, and divine intervention in overcoming life's challenges. It serves as a reminder that in times of difficulty, we can seek support from our ancestors, the wisdom of those who came before us, and the strength of the divine. This principle is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal struggles to collective challenges faced by society. By invoking the support of our forebears and the divine, we can find the strength to overcome obstacles and emerge victorious, just as Indra did against Vritra.
