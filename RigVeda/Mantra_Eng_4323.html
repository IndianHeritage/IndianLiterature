उतेषिषे प्रसवस्य त्वम् एक इद उ पूषा भवसि देव यामभिः |उतेदं विश्वं भुवनं वि राजसि श्यावाश्वस ते सवितः स्तोमम् आनशे ||
uteśiṣe prasavasya tvam eka id uta pūṣā bhavasi deva yāmabhiḥ | utedaṁ viśvam bhuvanaṁ vi rājasi śyāvāśvas te savitaḥ stomam ānaśe ||
  • Mantra: "uteśiṣe prasavasya tvam eka id uta pūṣā bhavasi deva yāmabhiḥ | utedaṁ viśvam bhuvanaṁ vi rājasi śyāvāśvas te savitaḥ stomam ānaśe ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uteśiṣe: In the context of creation or emergence.
    • prasavasya: Of the birth or origin.
    • tvam: You (referring to a divine entity).
    • eka: One or singular.
    • id: Here or now.
    • uta: And or also.
    • pūṣā: Nourisher or sustainer.
    • bhavasi: You become or you are.
    • deva: God or divine being.
    • yāmabhiḥ: By whom or through whom.
    • utedaṁ: This or here.
    • viśvam: All or the universe.
    • bhuvanaṁ: Worlds or realms.
    • vi: In or among.
    • rājasi: You rule or you govern.
    • śyāvāśvas: Dark or black steeds (often referring to the sun).
    • te: Your.
    • savitaḥ: Of the sun or solar deity.
    • stomam: Praise or hymn.
    • ānaśe: To be offered or to be invoked.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine essence that is responsible for creation and sustenance.
    • The phrase "you become one" emphasizes the unity of the divine with the universe, suggesting that all creation stems from a singular source.
    • The reference to "pūṣā" highlights the nurturing aspect of the divine, akin to how a mother nourishes her child.
    • In real life, this can be seen in the way communities come together to support one another, much like a family unit.
    • The mantra also acknowledges the divine's role in governing the universe, similar to how leaders guide and protect their people.
    • The imagery of "dark steeds" can be interpreted as the cycles of day and night, representing the balance of light and darkness in life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine, reminding us that we are all part of a greater whole.
    • It encourages us to recognize the nurturing and governing aspects of the divine in our lives, inspiring us to act with compassion and responsibility.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in daily life by fostering community support, practicing gratitude for the sustenance we receive, and acknowledging the balance of forces in our lives.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the divine presence in every aspect of existence, urging us to live harmoniously with ourselves and others.
