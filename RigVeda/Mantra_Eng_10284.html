यं कुमार नवं रथम अचक्रम मनसाकृत्णोः |एकेषं विश्वतः प्राञ्चम् अपश्यन्न अधि तिष्ठसि ||
yaṁ kumāra navaṁ ratham acakram manasākṛṇoḥ | ekeṣaṁ viśvataḥ prāñcam apaśyann adhi tiṣṭhasi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: who
    • kumāra: young one or prince
    • navaṁ: new
    • ratham: chariot
    • acakram: without wheels or motion
    • manasākṛṇoḥ: of the mind or mental creation
    • ekeṣaṁ: alone or singular
    • viśvataḥ: from all directions
    • prāñcam: the breath or life force
    • apaśyann: having seen or perceived
    • adhi: upon or over
    • tiṣṭhasi: you stand or remain
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a young prince or a new beginning that is represented by a chariot that is not in motion.
    • The phrase "manasākṛṇoḥ" suggests that this chariot is a creation of the mind, indicating that it symbolizes thoughts, ideas, or aspirations.
    • The term "ekeṣaṁ" implies a singular focus or purpose, while "viśvataḥ prāñcam" indicates a universal life force that is perceived from all directions.
    • Finally, "adhi tiṣṭhasi" suggests a state of being or existence that is grounded and stable.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to focus on our aspirations (the chariot) and to ensure that our thoughts (the mind) are aligned with our goals.
    • In leadership, a leader may embody the "young prince" who must navigate challenges (the unmoving chariot) with a clear vision and purpose (the singular focus).
    • In meditation, practitioners may visualize the chariot as a representation of their journey towards enlightenment, where the stillness of the chariot signifies the importance of inner peace and clarity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of mental clarity and focus in achieving one's goals and aspirations.
    • It serves as a reminder that while we may have grand ideas (the chariot), it is essential to ensure that our thoughts and intentions are aligned and directed towards a singular purpose.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, leadership, and mindfulness practices, encouraging individuals to remain grounded while pursuing their dreams.
