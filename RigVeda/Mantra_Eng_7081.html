पानाय्यं तद अश्विनाः कृतं वां वृषभो दिवो रजसः पृथिव्याः |सहस्रं शंसाऽउ त ये गविष्टौ सर्वांं इति तां उप याता पिबध्यै ||
panāyyaṁ tad aśvinā kṛtaṁ vāṁ vṛṣabho divo rajasaḥ pṛthivyāḥ | sahasraṁ śaṁsā uta ye gaviṣṭau sarvām̐ it tām̐ upa yātā pibadhyai ||
  • Mantra: "panāyyaṁ tad aśvinā kṛtaṁ vāṁ vṛṣabho divo rajasaḥ pṛthivyāḥ | sahasraṁ śaṁsā uta ye gaviṣṭau sarvām̐ it tām̐ upa yātā pibadhyai ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • panāyyaṁ: that which is to be enjoyed or consumed
    • tad: that
    • aśvinā: by the Ashvins (the twin deities associated with health and medicine)
    • kṛtaṁ: made or created
    • vāṁ: for you
    • vṛṣabho: the bull, symbolizing strength and vitality
    • divo: of the sky
    • rajasaḥ: of the atmosphere or the realm of action
    • pṛthivyāḥ: of the earth
    • sahasraṁ: a thousand
    • śaṁsā: praises or blessings
    • uta: or
    • ye: those who
    • gaviṣṭau: who are in the cows (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • sarvām̐: all
    • it: this
    • tām̐: that
    • upa: near or towards
    • yātā: having gone
    • pibadhyai: may drink or consume
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the Ashvins, who are revered as divine physicians and bringers of health and prosperity.
    • The phrase "panāyyaṁ tad aśvinā kṛtaṁ" suggests that the offerings made to the Ashvins are meant to be enjoyed and consumed, highlighting the importance of nourishment and well-being.
    • The reference to "vṛṣabho divo rajasaḥ pṛthivyāḥ" symbolizes the connection between the heavens and the earth, emphasizing the balance between spiritual and material aspects of life.
    • The mention of "sahasraṁ śaṁsā" indicates the abundance of blessings and praises that can be received, suggesting that gratitude and recognition of these blessings are essential.
    • The concluding part "uta ye gaviṣṭau sarvām̐ it tām̐ upa yātā pibadhyai" implies that those who are connected to the source of nourishment (symbolized by cows) will receive all that is necessary for sustenance and growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the importance of health and wellness. Just as the Ashvins are called upon for healing, individuals today seek medical help and holistic practices to maintain their health.
    • The balance between spiritual and material needs can be seen in practices like yoga and meditation, which promote mental well-being while also encouraging physical health.
    • The concept of abundance and gratitude is reflected in practices such as mindfulness and appreciation for the resources we have, whether they be food, health, or relationships.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of health, nourishment, and gratitude in our lives.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking balance between our spiritual and material needs, and recognizing the blessings we receive.
    • In a world where stress and imbalance are common, this mantra encourages us to cultivate a sense of appreciation and connection to both the divine and the earthly, leading to a more fulfilling life.
