यं नु नकीः पृथनासु स्वराजं द्वितातरति नृतमं हरिष्टाम् |इनतमः सत्वभिर्यो ह शूषैः पृथुज्रया अमिनाद आयुर दस्योः ||
yaṁ nu nakiḥ pṛtanāsu svarājaṁ dvitā tarati nṛtamaṁ hariṣṭhām | inatamaḥ satvabhir yo ha śūṣaiḥ pṛthujrayā aminād āyur dasyoḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yaṁ nu nakiḥ pṛtanāsu svarājaṁ dvitā tarati nṛtamaṁ hariṣṭhām | inatamaḥ satvabhir yo ha śūṣaiḥ pṛthujrayā aminād āyur dasyoḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: who
    • nu: indeed
    • nakiḥ: the one who is not known
    • pṛtanāsu: in the realms of existence
    • svarājaṁ: self-rule or sovereignty
    • dvitā: twice or dual
    • tarati: transcends or surpasses
    • nṛtamaṁ: the best among men
    • hariṣṭhām: the most excellent
    • inatamaḥ: the lowest or the least
    • satvabhir: with strength or vitality
    • yo: who
    • ha: indeed
    • śūṣaiḥ: with the dry or barren
    • pṛthujrayā: with the vastness of the earth
    • aminād: without any hindrance
    • āyur: life or longevity
    • dasyoḥ: of the enemies or adversaries
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the concept of sovereignty and the qualities that define a true leader or an excellent person.
    • The phrase "who indeed is the one who transcends the realms of existence" suggests that true greatness lies in surpassing ordinary limitations.
    • It emphasizes the importance of strength and vitality, indicating that a person who possesses these qualities can overcome challenges, even those posed by adversaries.
    • For example, in a corporate setting, a leader who demonstrates resilience and strategic thinking can navigate through tough market conditions, much like the mantra suggests transcending challenges.
    • The mention of "the most excellent among men" highlights the ideal qualities of a leader, such as integrity, wisdom, and compassion.
    • In personal life, this can relate to individuals who strive to be the best version of themselves, overcoming personal struggles and adversities.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for self-improvement and the pursuit of excellence.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate inner strength and resilience, which are essential in both personal and professional realms.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to better leadership, improved relationships, and a more fulfilling life.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that true greatness is not just about external achievements but also about the inner qualities that define a person.
