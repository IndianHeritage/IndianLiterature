तं नो द्यावापृथिवी तन् न आप इन्द्रः श्रिण्वन्तु मरुतो हवं वचः |मा शूने भूमा सूर्यस्य संदृशि भद्रं जीवन्तो जरणाम अशीमहि ||
taṁ no dyāvāpṛthivī tan na āpa indraḥ śṛṇvantu maruto havaṁ vacaḥ | mā śūne bhūma sūryasya saṁdṛśi bhadraṁ jīvanto jaraṇām aśīmahi ||
  • taṁ no dyāvāpṛthivī: This phrase translates to "May that (divine) be ours, the sky and the earth." It signifies a connection between the heavens and the earth, emphasizing the importance of both realms in our lives.
  • tan na āpa indraḥ: This means "May the waters (or the divine) listen to us." Here, 'Indra' represents a powerful deity associated with rain and thunder, symbolizing the vital role of water in sustaining life.
  • śṛṇvantu maruto havaṁ vacaḥ: This translates to "May the Maruts (storm deities) hear our call." The Maruts are associated with storms and winds, indicating the need for nature's forces to be in harmony with human intentions.
  • mā śūne bhūma sūryasya saṁdṛśi: This means "May we not be devoid of the sight of the sun." The sun symbolizes life, energy, and vitality, highlighting the importance of light and warmth in our existence.
  • bhadraṁ jīvanto jaraṇām aśīmahi: This translates to "May we live well and prosper." It emphasizes the desire for a good life, health, and well-being, which is a universal aspiration.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates a prayer for harmony between the elements of nature and human existence. It reflects a deep understanding of the interconnectedness of life, urging us to seek blessings from the divine forces that govern the natural world.
  • Real-life Application: In today's context, this mantra can be applied in various ways:
    • Environmental Awareness: The call for the waters and the winds to listen can be seen as a reminder of our responsibility towards nature. For instance, participating in conservation efforts or advocating for sustainable practices aligns with the essence of this mantra.
    • Community Well-being: The desire for prosperity and a good life can inspire community initiatives aimed at improving health and well-being, such as organizing health camps or educational programs.
    • Personal Growth: On an individual level, this mantra encourages us to seek balance in our lives, fostering a connection with nature, and striving for personal and spiritual growth.
