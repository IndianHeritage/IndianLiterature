पितुः प्रात्नस्य जनमनā वदामसि सोमस्य जिव्हा प्र जिगाति चक्षसा |यद ईम इन्द्रं शम्य ऋक्वाण आशताद इन नामानि यज्ञियानि दधिरे ||
pituḥ pratnasya janmanā vadāmasi somasya jihvā pra jigāti cakṣasā | yad īm indraṁ śamy ṛkvāṇa āśatād in nāmāni yajñiyāni dadhire ||
  • pituḥ pratnasya janmanā vadāmasi: This phrase can be translated as "We speak of the birth of the father of the essence." Here, "pituḥ" refers to the father or creator, "pratnasya" signifies essence or substance, and "janmanā" means birth or origin. This can be related to the idea of parental influence in our lives, where the values and teachings of our parents shape our character and essence.
  • somasya jihvā pra jigāti cakṣasā: This translates to "The tongue of the moon (Soma) speaks forth with vision." In this context, "Soma" represents the moon, which is often associated with nourishment and tranquility. The "jihvā" (tongue) symbolizes communication and expression, while "cakṣasā" (with vision) implies clarity and insight. This can be likened to how effective communication, when combined with wisdom, can lead to better understanding and harmony in relationships.
  • yad īm indraṁ śamy ṛkvāṇa: This means "Whatever we have, we offer to Indra, the powerful." "Indra" is a deity representing strength and power, and "śamy" refers to the act of offering or sharing. This can be seen in real life when individuals or communities come together to support a cause or share resources, reflecting the importance of generosity and collaboration.
  • āśatād in nāmāni yajñiyāni dadhire: This translates to "They have established the names of the offerings." "Yajñiyāni" refers to offerings or sacrifices made in rituals. This highlights the significance of intention behind our actions and the importance of recognizing and honoring the contributions of others in our lives. For example, in a community event, acknowledging the efforts of volunteers can foster a sense of belonging and appreciation.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of family, communication, strength, and community. It teaches us the value of our origins, the power of effective expression, the importance of generosity, and the recognition of contributions. In real life, these principles can guide us in building strong relationships, fostering community spirit, and living with intention and gratitude. By embodying these values, we can create a more harmonious and supportive environment for ourselves and those around us.
