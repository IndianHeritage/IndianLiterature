त्रिविष्टिधातु प्रतिमानम् ओजसस् तिस्रो भूमीर नृपते त्रिणि रोचनाः |अतीदं विश्वं भुवनं ववक्षिथाशत्रुर इन्द्र जनुषा सनाद् असि ||
triviṣṭidhātu pratimānam ojasas tisro bhūmīr nṛpate trīṇi rocanā | atīdaṁ viśvam bhuvanaṁ vavakṣithāśatrur indra januṣā sanād asi ||
  • triviṣṭidhātu: This term refers to the three realms or worlds—heaven, earth, and the underworld. In a real-life context, this can be understood as the interconnectedness of different aspects of existence, such as the physical, mental, and spiritual realms.
  • pratimānam: This means 'measure' or 'form'. It signifies the importance of having a defined structure or form in life. For example, in architecture, a well-measured design leads to a stable and beautiful building.
  • ojasas: This word denotes 'vital energy' or 'strength'. In everyday life, this can be related to the energy we derive from good health, nutrition, and mental well-being, which empowers us to face challenges.
  • tisro bhūmīr: This phrase translates to 'three earths'. It can symbolize the various dimensions of our lives—personal, professional, and social. Balancing these aspects is crucial for a fulfilling life.
  • nṛpate: Meaning 'king' or 'ruler', this term emphasizes leadership and responsibility. In a modern context, it can refer to anyone in a position of authority, such as a manager or community leader, who must guide others wisely.
  • trīṇi rocanā: This translates to 'three illuminations' or 'three sources of light'. It can represent knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment. For instance, education serves as a light that guides individuals toward personal and professional growth.
  • atīdaṁ viśvam bhuvanaṁ: This phrase means 'the entire universe'. It signifies the vastness of existence and our place within it. In real life, it reminds us of our responsibilities toward the environment and society.
  • vavakṣithāśatrur: This term can be interpreted as 'the one who overcomes enemies'. In a practical sense, it can refer to overcoming personal challenges or adversities in life, such as financial struggles or health issues.
  • indra: Referring to the king of gods, Indra symbolizes power and authority. In a contemporary context, this can relate to influential leaders or figures in society who shape our world.
  • januṣā sanād asi: This phrase means 'you are born from the essence of life'. It emphasizes the idea that we are all interconnected and part of a larger whole, which can inspire compassion and empathy in our interactions with others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of existence, emphasizing the importance of balance, leadership, and interconnectedness in our lives. It serves as a reminder to cultivate our inner strength, seek knowledge, and fulfill our responsibilities toward ourselves and society. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can guide individuals in their personal development, leadership roles, and interactions with the world around them, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
