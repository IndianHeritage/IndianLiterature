तत् सु नः शर्म यच्छतादित्य यन् मुमोचति |एनस्वन्तं चिद एनसः सुदानवः ||
tat su naḥ śarma yacchatādityā yan mumocati | enasvantaṁ cid enasaḥ sudānavaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tat: that
    • su: good, auspicious
    • naḥ: to us
    • śarma: shelter, protection
    • yacchat: may grant
    • ādityā: O Sun (Aditya)
    • yan: which
    • mumocati: releases, liberates
    • enasvantaṁ: from the evil, harmful
    • cid: consciousness, awareness
    • enasaḥ: of the harmful
    • sudānavaḥ: the good, auspicious
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to the Sun God, Aditya, asking for protection and auspiciousness. It seeks the Sun's blessings to grant us a good shelter and to liberate us from harmful influences. The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine protection and the pursuit of goodness in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek protection and guidance during challenging times, such as during illness or personal crises. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek divine help and maintain a positive outlook.
    • In a professional context, when facing competition or unethical practices, one can invoke this mantra to seek strength and protection from negative influences, encouraging a focus on integrity and goodness.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, people often recite prayers or mantras to invoke blessings for a prosperous and harmonious future, similar to the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine protection and the pursuit of goodness. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios where individuals seek refuge from negativity and aspire for a positive, auspicious life. By reciting this mantra, one can cultivate a mindset of seeking higher consciousness and protection, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
