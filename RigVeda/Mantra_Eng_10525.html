अहं गर्भं अदधाम् ओषधिषु अहं विश्वेषु भुवनेषु अन्तः |अहम् प्रजा जनयाम् पृथिव्याम् अहं जनिभ्यो अपरीषु पुत्रान् ||
ahaṁ garbham adadhām oṣadhīṣv ahaṁ viśveṣu bhuvaneṣv antaḥ | aham prajā ajanayam pṛthivyām ahaṁ janibhyo aparīṣu putrān ||
  • ahaṁ garbham adadhām: This phrase translates to "I have placed the embryo." Here, "ahaṁ" means "I," "garbham" refers to "embryo" or "fetus," and "adadhām" means "I have placed" or "I have established." This can be understood as the divine force or energy that initiates life within the womb.
  • oṣadhīṣv: This word means "in the plants" or "in the herbs." It signifies the connection between life and nature, emphasizing that life is sustained and nourished by the natural world around us.
  • ahaṁ viśveṣu bhuvaneṣv antaḥ: This translates to "I am within all beings in the worlds." "Viśveṣu" means "in all," "bhuvaneṣv" refers to "worlds," and "antaḥ" means "within." This indicates the omnipresence of the divine or the universal spirit that resides in every living being.
  • aham prajā ajanayam pṛthivyām: This means "I have created beings on the earth." "Prajā" refers to "beings" or "creatures," "ajanayam" means "I have created," and "pṛthivyām" means "on the earth." This highlights the role of the divine in the creation and sustenance of life on our planet.
  • aham janibhyo aparīṣu putrān: This translates to "I have given birth to sons from the wombs." "Janibhyo" means "from the wombs," "aparīṣu" refers to "in many," and "putrān" means "sons" or "children." This emphasizes the nurturing aspect of life, where the divine facilitates the birth and growth of new generations.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, life, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It signifies the divine presence in the process of birth and growth, highlighting the importance of nature in sustaining life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to understand the significance of nurturing and protecting the environment, as it is the source of life. It also serves as a reminder of the sacredness of life and the responsibility we hold in caring for future generations. By recognizing the divine in all beings, we can foster compassion and unity in our communities, promoting a harmonious existence with nature and each other.
