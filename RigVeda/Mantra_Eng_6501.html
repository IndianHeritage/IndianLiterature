इन्द्रो वा घेद इयन मघं सरस्वती वा सुभगा ददिर वसु |त्वं वा चित्र दाशुषे ||
indro vā ghed iyan maghaṁ sarasvatī vā subhagā dadir vasu | tvaṁ vā citra dāśuṣe ||
  • Indro vā ghed iyan maghaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, to bestow his blessings. "Indro" refers to Indra, while "maghaṁ" signifies wealth or abundance. In real life, this can be seen as a plea for prosperity and success in one's endeavors.
  • Sarasvatī vā subhagā dadir vasu: Here, "Sarasvatī" refers to the goddess of knowledge, music, and arts, while "subhagā" means auspicious or fortunate. "Dadir vasu" translates to the granting of wealth or good fortune. This part emphasizes the importance of knowledge and creativity in achieving success. For example, in a student's life, seeking knowledge and wisdom can lead to academic and professional success.
  • Tvaṁ vā citra dāśuṣe: This phrase can be understood as a request for the divine to grant unique and varied blessings. "Citra" means colorful or diverse, and "dāśuṣe" refers to the act of giving. This highlights the idea that life is enriched by diversity and the multitude of experiences. In a practical sense, this can relate to the importance of embracing different perspectives and experiences in our daily lives, such as in teamwork or community engagement.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for divine blessings in the realms of wealth, knowledge, and diverse experiences. Its significance lies in the recognition that success is not solely dependent on material wealth but also on wisdom and the richness of life experiences. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in education, business, and personal growth, where individuals seek to balance material success with intellectual and experiential richness. By invoking these divine qualities, one can aspire to lead a fulfilling and prosperous life.
