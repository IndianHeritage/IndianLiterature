अविन्दन् ते अतिहितं यद आसीद यज्ञस्य धाम परमं गुहा यत् |धातुर द्युतानात् सवितुः च विष्णोर भरद्वाजो बृहद् आ चक्रे अग्नेः ||
avindan te atihitaṁ yad āsīd yajñasya dhāma paramaṁ guhā yat | dhātur dyutānāt savituś ca viṣṇor bharadvājo bṛhad ā cakre agneḥ ||
  • avindan te atihitaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "they found you, O divine one." It signifies the search for the divine presence or truth in the universe.
  • yad āsīd yajñasya dhāma paramaṁ: This means "that which is the supreme abode of the sacrifice." In Vedic tradition, yajna (sacrifice) is a central theme, representing the act of giving and the connection between humans and the divine.
  • guhā yat: Translated as "that which is hidden." This refers to the deeper, often concealed aspects of existence and spirituality that require introspection to understand.
  • dhātur dyutānāt: This can be interpreted as "from the brilliance of the creator." It emphasizes the idea that all creation emanates from a divine source, highlighting the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • savituś ca viṣṇor: This phrase refers to "of the sun and Vishnu." The sun is often seen as a symbol of life and energy, while Vishnu represents preservation and protection in the cosmic order.
  • bharadvājo bṛhad ā cakre agneḥ: This translates to "Bharadvaja (a sage) has made a great effort for Agni (the fire god)." It signifies the importance of devotion and effort in spiritual practices, particularly in the context of rituals and sacrifices.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking the divine through sacrifice and devotion. It highlights the importance of understanding the hidden aspects of life and the interconnectedness of all beings through the divine source. In real life, this can be applied in various ways, such as:

  • Engaging in selfless acts and community service, which can be seen as modern-day yajnas.
  • Practicing mindfulness and introspection to uncover deeper truths about oneself and the universe.
  • Recognizing the importance of nature and the environment, akin to honoring the sun and its life-giving properties.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of spiritual practices and the pursuit of knowledge, urging individuals to seek a deeper connection with the divine and the world around them.
