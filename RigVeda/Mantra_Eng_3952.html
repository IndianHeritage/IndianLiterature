को नु वाम मित्रावरुणाव ऋतायन दिवो वा महः पार्थिवस्य वा दे |ऋतस्य वा सदसि त्रासीतां नो यज्ञायते वा पशुषो न वाजान ||
ko nu vām mitrāvaruṇāv ṛtāyan divo vā mahaḥ pārthivasya vā de | ṛtasya vā sadasi trāsīthāṁ no yajñāyate vā paśuṣo na vājān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ko nu: Who indeed
    • vām: You (plural)
    • mitrāvaruṇāv: Mitra and Varuna (the deities representing friendship and cosmic order)
    • ṛtāyan: The one who upholds truth and order
    • dīvo: Of the heavens
    • vā: Or
    • mahaḥ: Greatness
    • pārthivasya: Of the earthly realm
    • vā: Or
    • de: Of the divine
    • ṛtasya: Of truth/order
    • vā: Or
    • sadasi: In the assembly
    • trāsīthāṁ: May we be protected
    • no: Our
    • yajñāyate: For the sacrifice
    • vā: Or
    • paśuṣo: Of the animals
    • na: Not
    • vājān: The offerings
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer seeking protection and blessings from the deities Mitra and Varuna, who symbolize friendship and the cosmic order.
    • The speaker is inquiring about who can truly uphold the principles of truth and order, whether in the heavens or on earth.
    • It emphasizes the importance of maintaining harmony and balance in both spiritual and earthly matters.
    • The mention of "sadasi" refers to the assembly or gathering, indicating the significance of community and collective responsibility in upholding righteousness.
    • The mantra concludes with a plea for protection in the context of sacrifices, highlighting the importance of offerings and the sanctity of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, this mantra can be related to the importance of community service and social responsibility, where individuals come together to support one another and uphold moral values.
    • In the context of environmental conservation, the plea for protection can be seen in efforts to safeguard wildlife and natural resources, recognizing the interconnectedness of all life.
    • In personal relationships, the values of friendship and trust embodied by Mitra and Varuna can be applied to foster healthy and supportive connections among individuals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of truth, order, and community in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek protection and blessings not only for themselves but also for the collective well-being of society.
    • By embodying the principles of Mitra and Varuna, we can contribute to a more harmonious and balanced world, where the sanctity of life is respected and upheld.
