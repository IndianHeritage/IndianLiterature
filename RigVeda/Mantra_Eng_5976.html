अवक्रक्षिणं वृषभं यथाजुरं गां न चर्षणीसहं |विद्वेषणं संवननोभयंकरं मंहिष्ठम उभयाविनम् ||
avakrakṣiṇaṁ vṛṣabhaṁ yathājuraṁ gāṁ na carṣaṇīsaham | vidveṣaṇaṁ saṁvananobhayaṁkaram maṁhiṣṭham ubhayāvinam ||
  • avakrakṣiṇaṁ: This term can be broken down into "ava" (down) and "krakṣiṇa" (to protect or guard). It signifies the act of safeguarding or protecting something that is valuable.
  • vṛṣabhaṁ: This word translates to "bull," which is often a symbol of strength, power, and fertility in various cultures. In the context of the mantra, it represents a strong and protective force.
  • yathājuraṁ: This can be interpreted as "as it is," indicating a state of being or existence that is true to its nature.
  • gāṁ: This word means "earth" or "land," which is essential for sustenance and life. It symbolizes the physical realm and our connection to it.
  • na carṣaṇīsaham: "Na" means "not," and "carṣaṇī" refers to "the plow" or "tillage." This phrase suggests a resistance to disturbance or disruption in the natural order of things.
  • vidveṣaṇaṁ: This term means "to create enmity" or "to cause division." It highlights the negative aspects of conflict and discord.
  • saṁvananobhayaṁkaram: "Saṁvanana" refers to "communication" or "dialogue," while "ubhayaṁkaram" means "that which creates both sides." This phrase emphasizes the importance of dialogue in resolving conflicts.
  • maṁhiṣṭham: This translates to "the best" or "the most excellent," indicating a pursuit of the highest ideals or values.
  • ubhayāvinam: This term means "both sides," suggesting inclusivity and the need to consider multiple perspectives in any situation.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of protection, strength, and the preservation of harmony in our lives. It calls for safeguarding our values and the earth while resisting conflict and division. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal relationships, fostering open communication to resolve conflicts and maintain harmony.
  • In environmental conservation, protecting the earth and its resources from exploitation and harm.
  • In community building, promoting inclusivity and understanding among diverse groups to prevent division.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of strength, protection, and dialogue in creating a balanced and harmonious existence.
