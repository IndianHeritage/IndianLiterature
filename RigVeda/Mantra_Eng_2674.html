अग्नि वीहि पुरोळाशम् आहुतं तिरोहन्यम् |सहसः सूनुर अस्य अध्वरे हितः ||
agne vīhi puroḻāśam āhutaṁ tiroahnyam | sahasaḥ sūnur asy adhvare hitaḥ ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying and transformative force.
  • Vīhi: This means "to bring" or "to lead." It signifies a request for the presence or guidance of Agni.
  • Puroḻāśam: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is offered." It emphasizes the act of offering to the divine.
  • Āhutaṁ: This means "that which is offered" or "the oblation." It refers to the offerings made to Agni during rituals.
  • Tiroahnyam: This can be understood as "hidden" or "concealed." It suggests the idea of uncovering or revealing something that is not immediately visible.
  • Sahasaḥ: This word means "with strength" or "powerfully." It indicates the intensity of the invocation.
  • Sūnur: This translates to "son" or "descendant." It can symbolize the continuity of tradition and the passing down of knowledge.
  • Asy: This means "of this" or "of this offering." It connects the invocation to the specific ritual being performed.
  • Adhvare: This refers to "the sacrifice" or "the ritual." It highlights the importance of the ritualistic aspect of the offering.
  • Hitaḥ: This means "beneficial" or "auspicious." It signifies the positive outcomes that arise from the ritual and offerings made to Agni.

The mantra can be interpreted as a powerful invocation to Agni, asking for his presence and guidance in the ritual. It emphasizes the importance of offerings and the desire for beneficial outcomes from the sacrifices made. The hidden aspects of life and the strength of tradition are also highlighted, suggesting that through rituals, one can uncover deeper truths and insights.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: It can be used during religious ceremonies to invoke divine blessings and ensure that the rituals are performed with sincerity and strength.
  • Personal Growth: The mantra encourages individuals to seek guidance and clarity in their lives, especially when facing challenges or uncertainties.
  • Community and Tradition: It emphasizes the importance of passing down knowledge and traditions, fostering a sense of community and continuity.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of rituals, the power of invocation, and the benefits that arise from sincere offerings. It encourages individuals to seek divine guidance and to uncover the hidden aspects of their lives, ultimately leading to personal and communal growth.
