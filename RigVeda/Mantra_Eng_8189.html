ऊर्मिर्यस्ते पवित्रा देवाविः पर्यक्षरत् |सीदन्न ऋतस्य योनीमाऽ ||
ūrmir yas te pavitra ā devāvīḥ paryakṣarat | sīdann ṛtasya yonim ā ||
  • ūrmir: This word translates to "wave" or "ocean." In a broader sense, it can symbolize the vastness of life and the experiences that come with it.
  • yas: This means "who" or "that." It indicates a connection or relationship.
  • te: This translates to "your," indicating possession or belonging.
  • pavitra: Meaning "pure" or "sacred," this word emphasizes the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and surroundings.
  • ā: A preposition that can mean "from" or "out of," indicating a source or origin.
  • devāvīḥ: This term refers to "gods" or "divine beings," highlighting the spiritual aspect of the mantra.
  • paryakṣarat: This means "manifested" or "revealed," suggesting that the divine presence is evident in the world.
  • sīdann: This translates to "sitting" or "residing," indicating a state of being or existence.
  • ṛtasya: Meaning "of truth" or "of order," this word emphasizes the importance of righteousness and cosmic order.
  • yonim: This means "womb" or "source," symbolizing the origin of creation and life.
  • ā: Again, this preposition indicates a source or origin.

  • Meaning of the Mantra: The mantra speaks of the divine waves of purity that manifest from the source of truth and righteousness. It emphasizes the connection between the divine and the earthly realm, suggesting that purity and truth are essential for spiritual growth and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, one can relate this mantra to the importance of maintaining purity in thoughts and actions. For instance, practicing honesty in personal and professional relationships reflects the essence of ṛtasya.
    • In environmental contexts, the idea of ūrmir can be seen in the waves of the ocean, reminding us of the need to protect our natural resources and maintain the purity of our surroundings.
    • In spiritual practices, meditation can be a way to connect with the divine, allowing individuals to experience the devāvīḥ within themselves, leading to a deeper understanding of their purpose.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for purity, truth, and the recognition of the divine in our lives. It serves as a reminder to strive for righteousness and to acknowledge the interconnectedness of all beings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to lead a life of integrity, respect for nature, and spiritual awareness, ultimately contributing to personal and collective harmony.
