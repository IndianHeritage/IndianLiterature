कथा महाम अवृधत कस्य होतुर यज्ञं जुषाणो अभि सोमं ऊधः |पिबन्न उषानो जुषमाणो अन्धो ववक्ष ऋष्वः शुचते धनाय ||
kathā mahām avṛdhat kasya hotur yajñaṁ juṣāṇo abhi somam ūdhaḥ | pibann uśāno juṣamāṇo andho vavakṣa ṛṣvaḥ śucate dhanāya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Kathā: How
    • Mahām: Great
    • Avṛdhat: Has been revealed
    • Kasya: Whose
    • Hotur: Of the priest or the one who offers
    • Yajñaṁ: Sacrifice or offering
    • Juṣāṇo: Enjoying or partaking
    • Abhi: Towards
    • Somam: Soma (a sacred drink)
    • Ūdhaḥ: Uplifting or elevating
    • Pibann: Drinking
    • Uśāno: Of the dawn
    • Juṣamāṇo: Enjoying
    • Andho: Blind
    • Vavakṣa: Has been revealed
    • Ṛṣvaḥ: The wise or the seer
    • Śucate: Shines or becomes pure
    • Dhanāya: For wealth or prosperity
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the process of revelation and the importance of sacrifice in attaining higher states of consciousness.
    • The mention of "Soma" refers to a sacred drink that symbolizes divine inspiration and the elixir of life, which can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment in our lives.
    • The phrase "enjoying the dawn" signifies the arrival of new beginnings and opportunities, encouraging individuals to embrace change and growth.
    • The reference to the "blind" suggests that without awareness and understanding, one may miss the essence of life and its offerings.
    • The wise or seer ("Ṛṣvaḥ") represents those who have attained knowledge and clarity, guiding others towards prosperity and purity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, the mantra can be applied to the importance of teamwork and collaboration (sacrifice) in achieving common goals (wealth and prosperity).
    • In personal development, the pursuit of knowledge (Soma) through education and self-reflection can lead to enlightenment and a fulfilling life.
    • Embracing new opportunities (dawn) in one's career or personal life can lead to significant growth and success.
    • Awareness of one's surroundings and the ability to learn from experiences can prevent one from being "blind" to opportunities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of sacrifice, knowledge, and awareness in achieving prosperity and enlightenment.
    • It serves as a reminder to embrace new beginnings and to seek wisdom in our actions and decisions.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, successful relationships, and a deeper understanding of one's purpose.
