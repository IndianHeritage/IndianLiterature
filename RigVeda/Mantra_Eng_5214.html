अयम् उष्य सुमहाम् अवेदि होता मन्द्रो मनुषो यह्वो अग्निः |विभा अकाḥ ससृजानाḥ पृथिव्यां कृष्णपविर ओषधिभिर ववक्षे ||
ayam u ṣya sumahām̐ avedi hotā mandro manuṣo yahvo agniḥ | vi bhā akaḥ sasṛjānaḥ pṛthivyāṁ kṛṣṇapavir oṣadhībhir vavakṣe ||
  • ayam u ṣya sumahām̐ avedi hotā mandro manuṣo yahvo agniḥ
    • ayam - This word translates to "this" or "this one," indicating a specific focus or subject.
    • u ṣya - Refers to "the dawn" or "the light," symbolizing new beginnings and enlightenment.
    • sumahām̐ - Means "great" or "powerful," suggesting the significance of the subject being discussed.
    • avedi - Translates to "knows" or "understands," indicating awareness or knowledge.
    • hotā - Refers to "the priest" or "the one who invokes," highlighting the role of a mediator in spiritual practices.
    • mandro - Means "the wise" or "the learned," emphasizing the importance of wisdom in understanding the divine.
    • manuṣo - Translates to "human" or "mankind," indicating the connection between the divine and humanity.
    • yahvo - Refers to "who" or "that which," pointing to the subject of the invocation.
    • agniḥ - Means "fire," symbolizing purity, transformation, and the divine presence.
  • vi bhā akaḥ sasṛjānaḥ pṛthivyāṁ kṛṣṇapavir oṣadhībhir vavakṣe
    • vi - Translates to "in" or "within," indicating a sense of presence or existence.
    • bhā - Means "light" or "radiance," symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
    • akaḥ - Refers to "the sky" or "the heavens," representing the divine realm.
    • sasṛjānaḥ - Means "created" or "brought forth," indicating the act of creation.
    • pṛthivyāṁ - Refers to "on earth," grounding the divine in the physical realm.
    • kṛṣṇapavir - Translates to "the black" or "the dark," which can symbolize the unknown or the hidden aspects of life.
    • oṣadhībhir - Means "herbs" or "plants," representing nature and its healing properties.
    • vavakṣe - Refers to "to express" or "to declare," indicating the act of sharing knowledge or wisdom.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the connection between the divine (Agni, the fire) and humanity, highlighting the importance of knowledge, wisdom, and the natural world. It signifies the role of the priest or mediator in invoking divine presence and understanding the mysteries of existence. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In spiritual practices, where individuals seek to connect with the divine through rituals and prayers.
  • In environmental awareness, recognizing the importance of nature and its resources for human well-being.
  • In personal development, where the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom is essential for growth and transformation.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking knowledge and understanding in our lives.
