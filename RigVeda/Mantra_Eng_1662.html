ते हि द्यावापृथिवी विश्वशम्भुवा ऋतावरī रजसो धारयत्कवी |सुजनमनी धिषणे अन्तर ईयते देवो देवी धर्मणा सूर्यः शुचिः ||
te hi dyāvāpṛthivī viśvaśambhuva ṛtāvarī rajaso dhārayatkavī | sujanmanī dhiṣaṇe antar īyate devo devī dharmaṇā sūryaḥ śuciḥ ||
  • te hi dyāvāpṛthivī: This phrase translates to "Indeed, the heavens and the earth." It signifies the duality and unity of the cosmos, representing both the spiritual and material realms. In real life, this can be seen in the balance we seek between our spiritual pursuits and earthly responsibilities.
  • viśvaśambhuva: Meaning "the all-pervading," this term emphasizes the omnipresence of divinity in all aspects of life. For example, one might find divinity in nature, relationships, and even in the challenges we face, suggesting that everything is interconnected.
  • ṛtāvarī: This word refers to "the one who upholds truth and order." In practical terms, it can be related to the importance of integrity and honesty in our daily lives, such as in business dealings or personal relationships, where maintaining truth is crucial for harmony.
  • rajaso dhārayatkavī: This translates to "the poet who holds the essence of passion." It highlights the role of creativity and inspiration in life. For instance, artists, writers, and musicians channel their passions into their work, influencing society and culture.
  • sujanmanī dhiṣaṇe: This means "the wise one who guides." It reflects the importance of mentorship and guidance in our lives. In a real-world context, teachers, leaders, and elders play a vital role in shaping our understanding and decisions.
  • antar īyate: This phrase translates to "the divine enters within." It suggests introspection and the search for inner truth. In practice, this can be seen in meditation and self-reflection, where individuals seek to understand their true selves and their place in the universe.
  • devo devī dharmaṇā: This means "the divine, both male and female, embodying righteousness." It emphasizes the balance of masculine and feminine energies in the pursuit of dharma (righteousness). In real life, this can be observed in the collaboration between genders in various fields, promoting equality and justice.
  • sūryaḥ śuciḥ: This translates to "the sun is pure." The sun symbolizes enlightenment, knowledge, and purity. In everyday life, this can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, as well as the importance of maintaining a clear and honest approach in our actions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of balance, truth, creativity, guidance, and purity in life. Its significance lies in the reminder to seek harmony between the spiritual and material worlds, to uphold truth and righteousness, and to embrace the interconnectedness of all beings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to reflect on their actions, seek knowledge, and foster relationships that promote integrity and balance, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
