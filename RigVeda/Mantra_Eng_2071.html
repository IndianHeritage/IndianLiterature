स विद्वां ā च पिप्रयो यक्षि चिकीत्व āनुषक | आ चास्मिन सति बरहिषि ||
sa vidvām̐ ā ca piprayo yakṣi cikitva ānuṣak | ā cāsmin satsi barhiṣi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sa: he, that
    • vidvām̐: wise, knowledgeable
    • ā: towards, in the direction of
    • ca: and
    • piprayo: may he nourish, may he provide
    • yakṣi: may he protect, may he guard
    • cikitva: having understood, having perceived
    • ānuṣak: in accordance with, following
    • ā: towards, in the direction of
    • cāsmin: in this, in this place
    • satsi: being, existing
    • barhiṣi: in the sacred grass, in the holy place
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a wise individual who is directed towards nourishing and protecting others.
    • The phrase "may he nourish" suggests the importance of providing sustenance, both physically and spiritually, to those around us.
    • "May he protect" emphasizes the role of guardianship and care in relationships, whether familial, communal, or societal.
    • The mention of understanding and perception indicates that wisdom is not just about knowledge but also about awareness and insight into the needs of others.
    • The reference to "sacred grass" symbolizes a place of purity and sanctity, suggesting that these actions should take place in a respectful and holy context.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A teacher who nourishes the minds of students by providing knowledge and guidance exemplifies the essence of this mantra.
    • A community leader who protects the interests of their community members, ensuring their safety and well-being, embodies the protective aspect.
    • In a family setting, parents who understand the needs of their children and provide for them emotionally and physically reflect the wisdom and care described in the mantra.
    • In a workplace, a manager who fosters a supportive environment and encourages the growth of their team members demonstrates the principles of nourishment and protection.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom, nourishment, and protection in our interactions with others.
    • It serves as a reminder that true knowledge is not just about intellectual understanding but also about empathy and care.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger relationships, healthier communities, and a more compassionate society.
    • By embodying these values, we contribute to a sacred space where individuals can thrive and feel secure.
