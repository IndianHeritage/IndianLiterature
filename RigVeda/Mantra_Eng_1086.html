पृष्टो दिवि पृष्टो अग्निः पृथिव्यां पृष्टो विश्वा ओषधीर आविवेश | वैश्वानरः सहसा पृष्टो अग्निः स नो दिवा स ऋषः पातु नक्तम् ||
pṛṣṭo divi pṛṣṭo agniḥ pṛthivyām pṛṣṭo viśvā oṣadhīr ā viveśa | vaiśvānaraḥ sahasā pṛṣṭo agniḥ sa no divā sa riṣaḥ pātu naktam ||
  • pṛṣṭo divi: This phrase translates to "the one who is invoked in the heavens." It signifies the divine presence that is accessible and omnipresent in the celestial realm.
  • pṛṣṭo agniḥ: Here, "Agni" refers to the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. The term "pṛṣṭo" indicates that Agni is invoked or called upon.
  • pṛthivyām: This means "on the earth," emphasizing the connection between the divine and the earthly realm. It suggests that the divine energy is present in all aspects of life.
  • pṛṣṭo viśvā oṣadhīr: This phrase translates to "the universal herbs and plants." It highlights the importance of nature and its healing properties, which are essential for human well-being.
  • ā viveśa: This means "has entered" or "has pervaded," indicating that the divine energy is not only present but also actively involved in the world.
  • vaiśvānaraḥ: This term refers to the universal aspect of Agni, representing the fire that sustains life and connects all beings.
  • sahasā: This means "swiftly" or "suddenly," suggesting the immediacy of divine intervention or presence in our lives.
  • sa no divā: This translates to "may he protect us by day," indicating a plea for divine protection and guidance during the daylight hours.
  • sa riṣaḥ: This means "may he protect us at night," extending the plea for protection into the night, symbolizing the need for safety and security in all aspects of life.
  • pātu naktam: This translates to "may he guard us at night," reinforcing the idea of divine guardianship and the importance of feeling secure in both day and night.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the omnipresence of the divine, particularly through the element of fire, which symbolizes transformation and purification. It highlights the interconnectedness of the heavens, earth, and nature, reminding us of the importance of seeking divine guidance and protection in our daily lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in rituals, meditation, or moments of reflection where individuals seek strength, healing, and safety. It serves as a reminder to acknowledge the divine forces at play in our lives and to cultivate a sense of gratitude and awareness of the natural world around us.
