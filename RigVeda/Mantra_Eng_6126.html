आ वहेत्य पराकात पूर्वीर अश्नन्ताव अश्विनाः |इषो दासीर अमर्त्याः ||
ā vahethe parākāt pūrvīr aśnantāv aśvinā | iṣo dāsīr amartyā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vahethe: "We invoke" or "We call upon."
    • parākāt: "From the beyond" or "from the higher realms."
    • pūrvīr: "Of the ancients" or "of the previous ones."
    • aśnantāv: "Those who are nourished" or "those who provide sustenance."
    • aśvinā: "By the Ashvins," referring to the twin deities associated with health and medicine.
    • iṣo: "Of the power" or "of the energy."
    • dāsīr: "Servants" or "those who serve."
    • amartyā: "Immortal" or "beyond death."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine forces, particularly the Ashvins, who are known for their healing and protective qualities.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the ancient wisdom and the nourishment that comes from higher realms.
    • The invocation suggests a desire for empowerment and support from these divine beings, highlighting the importance of seeking help from higher powers in times of need.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, individuals often seek guidance and support through meditation, prayer, or rituals, similar to invoking the Ashvins for assistance.
    • Healthcare professionals may embody the qualities of the Ashvins by providing care and healing to those in need, reflecting the nourishment aspect of the mantra.
    • In times of crisis, communities may come together to support one another, invoking a sense of collective strength and resilience, akin to the call for divine assistance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in our lives, especially during challenging times.
    • It reminds us of the interconnectedness of ancient wisdom and modern practices, encouraging us to draw upon higher powers for nourishment and strength.
    • In a broader sense, it highlights the significance of community and the role of individuals as caretakers and healers, embodying the spirit of the Ashvins in our daily lives.
