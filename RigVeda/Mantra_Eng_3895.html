महि महे तवसे दीध्ये नृन इन्द्रायेत्था तवसे अतव्यān |यो अस्मै सुमतिं वाजसातौ स्तुतो जन समर्यश् चिकेत ||
mahi mahe tavase dīdhye nṝn indrāyetthā tavase atavyān | yo asmai sumatiṁ vājasātau stuto jane samaryaś ciketa ||
  • mahi mahe tavase dīdhye: This phrase can be translated as "Great, may we meditate upon your greatness." Here, "mahi" signifies greatness, and "tavase" refers to the divine power or energy. This part emphasizes the importance of recognizing and meditating on the vastness and power of the divine.
  • nṝn indrāyetthā tavase atavyān: This translates to "May you inspire us, O Indra, with your divine energy." Indra is often regarded as the king of the gods and a symbol of strength and power. This line suggests a plea for inspiration and guidance from a higher power, which can be likened to seeking motivation from a mentor or leader in our lives.
  • yo asmai sumatiṁ vājasātau: This means "He who grants wisdom and strength." The term "sumatiṁ" refers to wisdom or good understanding, while "vājasātau" relates to strength or vigor. This part highlights the importance of wisdom and strength in achieving success, akin to how a student seeks knowledge and resilience to excel in their studies or career.
  • stuto jane samaryaś ciketa: This translates to "He who is praised by the wise and the learned." This line emphasizes the value of recognition and respect that comes from those who are knowledgeable. In real life, this can be seen in how leaders or innovators are often celebrated by their peers for their contributions and insights.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep reverence for divine power, wisdom, and the importance of seeking guidance from higher forces. It serves as a reminder to individuals to aspire for greatness, seek inspiration, and value wisdom in their pursuits. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as in education, leadership, and personal development, where individuals strive to achieve their goals with the support of mentors and the pursuit of knowledge. By meditating on these principles, one can cultivate a mindset geared towards growth, resilience, and respect for the wisdom of others.
