ईयुर गावो न यवसाद अगोपाऽ यथाकृतम् अभि मित्रं चितासः | पृष्णिगावः पृष्णिनिप्रेषितासः श्रुष्टिं चक्रुर नियुतो रन्तयश्च ||
īyur gāvo na yavasād agopā yathākṛtam abhi mitraṁ citāsaḥ | pṛśnigāvaḥ pṛśninipreṣitāsaḥ śruṣṭiṁ cakrur niyuto rantayaś ca ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • īyur: May they live.
    • gāvo: Cows.
    • na: Not.
    • yavasād: From the grains.
    • agopā: Without the cowherds.
    • yathākṛtam: As it has been made.
    • abhi: Towards.
    • mitraṁ: Friend.
    • citāsaḥ: With consciousness.
    • pṛśnigāvaḥ: The cows of the sun.
    • pṛśninipreṣitāsaḥ: Those sent by the sun.
    • śruṣṭiṁ: Creation.
    • cakrur: They made.
    • niyuto: Bound.
    • rantayaś: Joyful.
    • ca: And.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the vitality and sustenance of life, symbolized by cows, which are often seen as a source of nourishment and prosperity in Vedic culture.
    • The phrase "īyur gāvo" emphasizes the wish for longevity and health, not just for cows but metaphorically for all living beings.
    • The mention of "yavasād" suggests that life should not solely depend on material resources (grains) but also on spiritual and communal support.
    • "Agopā" indicates the importance of community and caretakers (cowherds) in nurturing life, highlighting the interconnectedness of society.
    • The term "mitraṁ citāsaḥ" reflects the idea of friendship and consciousness, suggesting that relationships and awareness are vital for a fulfilling life.
    • "Pṛśnigāvaḥ" and "pṛśninipreṣitāsaḥ" refer to the divine or celestial aspects of life, indicating that creation is influenced by higher powers.
    • The concluding part of the mantra, "śruṣṭiṁ cakrur niyuto rantayaś ca," signifies that creation is a joyful act, bound by the laws of nature and the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the mantra can be applied to sustainable farming practices that respect both the land and the community, ensuring that resources are shared and preserved.
    • In personal relationships, the emphasis on "mitraṁ" can be seen in the importance of nurturing friendships and community ties, which contribute to emotional well-being.
    • In environmental conservation, the idea of interconnectedness can inspire efforts to protect ecosystems, recognizing that all life forms are dependent on one another.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of health, community, and the divine connection in creation.
    • Its significance lies in promoting a holistic approach to living, where physical, emotional, and spiritual well-being are interconnected.
    • In modern contexts, it encourages us to foster relationships, practice sustainability, and recognize the joy in creation and existence.
