मन्त्र: परो दिवा पर एनā पृथिव्यā परो देवैभिर असुरैर यद अस्ति | कं स्विद गर्भं प्रथमं दध्र आपो यत्र देवाः समपश्यन्त विश्वे ||
paro divā para enā pṛthivyā paro devebhir asurair yad asti | kaṁ svid garbham prathamaṁ dadhra āpo yatra devāḥ samapaśyanta viśve ||
  • paro divā: This phrase translates to "beyond the heavens." It signifies the vastness and the transcendental nature of the divine or the ultimate reality that exists beyond our physical understanding.
  • para enā pṛthivyā: This means "beyond the earth." It emphasizes the idea that there is more to existence than what we perceive in our earthly realm, suggesting a higher plane of existence or consciousness.
  • paro devebhir asurair yad asti: This translates to "beyond the gods and the demons, whatever exists." It highlights the duality of existence, where both divine and demonic forces play a role, yet there is a reality that transcends both.
  • kaṁ svid garbham prathamaṁ dadhra: This means "who holds the first womb?" It poses a question about the origin of life and existence, prompting contemplation on the source of creation.
  • āpo yatra devāḥ samapaśyanta viśve: This translates to "where the gods see all." It suggests a place or state of being where divine beings have complete awareness and understanding of the universe.

In summary, this mantra from the Rigveda invites us to reflect on the nature of existence, the duality of life, and the quest for understanding the ultimate reality. It encourages us to look beyond the physical world and recognize the interconnectedness of all beings, both divine and demonic. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:


  • In personal growth, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding beyond their immediate experiences, fostering a sense of curiosity and exploration.
  • In conflict resolution, recognizing the duality of perspectives can help in finding common ground and understanding differing viewpoints.
  • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder to connect with the higher self and the universe, promoting mindfulness and awareness.

The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire introspection and a deeper understanding of our place in the cosmos. It encourages us to transcend our limitations and seek a higher truth, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
