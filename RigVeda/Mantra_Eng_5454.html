सजूर देवैभिर अपां नपातं सखायं कृध्वं शिवो नो अस्तु ||
sajūr devebhir apāṁ napātaṁ sakhāyaṁ kṛdhvaṁ śivo no astu ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • sajūr: This term can be interpreted as "together" or "in harmony." It signifies unity and collaboration.
    • devebhir: This word means "by the gods" or "from the divine." It indicates a connection to higher powers or spiritual entities.
    • apāṁ: This translates to "of waters." In the Vedic context, waters often symbolize purity, life, and nourishment.
    • napātaṁ: This means "descendant" or "offspring." It refers to the continuation of life and lineage.
    • sakhāyaṁ: This word means "friend" or "companion." It emphasizes the importance of relationships and support systems.
    • kṛdhvaṁ: This translates to "may we create" or "may we establish." It is a call to action, encouraging the formation of positive outcomes.
    • śivo: This term means "auspicious" or "beneficial." It denotes a state of well-being and harmony.
    • no: This means "for us" or "to us," indicating a personal or collective appeal.
    • astu: This translates to "let it be" or "may it be," expressing a wish or desire for a certain outcome.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation for harmony and auspiciousness in life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity among people and the divine, suggesting that when we come together in friendship and collaboration, we can create a beneficial environment.
    • The reference to "waters" symbolizes the nurturing aspects of life, indicating that just as water sustains life, so too does the support of friends and the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied to foster collaboration among neighbors to create a supportive environment, much like a community garden where everyone contributes and benefits.
    • In a workplace, it can serve as a reminder for teamwork and the importance of building strong relationships among colleagues to achieve common goals.
    • In personal relationships, it highlights the significance of friendship and support, encouraging individuals to nurture their connections with others for mutual growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra "sajūr devebhir apāṁ napātaṁ sakhāyaṁ kṛdhvaṁ śivo no astu" serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of unity, friendship, and divine support in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the call for collaboration and the creation of a harmonious environment, whether in personal relationships, community settings, or professional spaces.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a life filled with auspiciousness and well-being, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
