इदं वचः शतसाह संसहस्रमुद अग्नये जनिषीष्ट द्विबार्हाः |शं यत स्तोत्रिभ्य आपये भवाति द्युमद अमीवचातनं रक्षोहा ||
idaṁ vacaḥ śatasāḥ saṁsahasram ud agnaye janiṣīṣṭa dvibarhāḥ | śaṁ yat stotṛbhya āpaye bhavāti dyumad amīvacātanaṁ rakṣohā ||
  • idaṁ vacaḥ - This phrase translates to "these words" or "this speech." It signifies the importance of communication and the power of words in shaping our reality.
  • śatasāḥ - Meaning "hundreds," this word emphasizes abundance and the multiplicity of thoughts and expressions that can arise from a single idea.
  • saṁsahasram - This translates to "thousands," further reinforcing the idea of vastness and the potential impact of collective speech.
  • ud agnaye - This means "to the fire" or "to the divine." Fire is often seen as a purifying force and a medium through which offerings are made to the gods.
  • janiṣīṣṭa - This word means "born" or "created." It suggests the act of bringing forth something new, whether it be ideas, creations, or even life itself.
  • dvibarhāḥ - This translates to "two-fold" or "double." It can refer to duality in nature, such as the balance between opposing forces.
  • śaṁ - Meaning "peace" or "well-being," this word highlights the ultimate goal of human endeavors: to achieve harmony and tranquility.
  • yat stotṛbhya - This means "that which is praised." It refers to the divine qualities or entities that are worthy of worship and reverence.
  • āpaye - This translates to "to bring forth" or "to manifest." It signifies the act of making something real or tangible.
  • bhavāti - Meaning "becomes," this word indicates transformation and the process of change that occurs through intention and action.
  • dyumad - This translates to "radiant" or "brilliant," often associated with light and enlightenment.
  • amīvacātanaṁ - This means "the protection from evil" or "the safeguarding from negativity." It emphasizes the need for spiritual and mental protection.
  • rakṣohā - This translates to "of demons" or "of negative forces." It signifies the challenges and adversities that one may face in life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the power of words and collective thought in manifesting peace and protection from negativity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, using positive affirmations to shape one's mindset and reality.
  • In community settings, fostering open communication to build harmony and understanding among diverse groups.
  • In spiritual practices, invoking divine protection through prayers and rituals to safeguard against negative influences.

This mantra serves as a reminder of the profound impact of our words and intentions, encouraging us to speak and act with purpose to create a positive and harmonious environment.
