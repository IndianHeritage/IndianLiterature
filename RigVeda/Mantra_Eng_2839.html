यदन्तरā परावतं arvāvataṁ च हूयसे |इन्द्रेह तता आगहि ||
yad antarā parāvatam arvāvataṁ ca hūyase | indreha tata ā gahi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • antarā: within
    • parāvatam: the highest or supreme
    • arvāvataṁ: the one who is invoked or called upon
    • ca: and
    • hūyase: you are called or invoked
    • indreha: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • tata: therefore
    • ā: come
    • gahi: grasp or hold
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the supreme power that resides within and calls upon Indra, the deity representing strength and power.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the inner divine and the external forces, suggesting that true strength comes from within.
    • By calling upon Indra, the mantra seeks assistance in harnessing this inner strength to overcome challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In moments of personal crisis, individuals often turn inward to find their strength, much like the invocation in this mantra. For instance, someone facing a significant life change may meditate or reflect to connect with their inner resilience.
    • In leadership, a person may invoke the qualities of Indra—courage, decisiveness, and strength—to guide their team through difficult times, demonstrating the application of this mantra in a professional context.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that true power and strength come from within, and that by invoking higher energies, one can access this inner reservoir.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal development, leadership, and overcoming adversity, encouraging individuals to seek strength from their inner selves while also recognizing the support of higher powers.
