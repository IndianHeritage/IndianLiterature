इहेह वो मनसा बन्धुतā नर उशिजो जग्मुर अभि तानि वेदसā |याभिर मायाभिः प्रतिजूतिवार्पसः साउधन्वना यज्ञियम भागम् आनश ||
iheha vo manasā bandhutā nara uśijo jagmur abhi tāni vedasā | yābhir māyābhiḥ pratijūtivarpasaḥ saudhanvanā yajñiyam bhāgam ānaśa ||
  • iheha: This term translates to "here" or "in this world." It emphasizes the importance of our current existence and the actions we take in the present.
  • vo: This means "your" or "to you," indicating a connection or relationship with the audience or the community.
  • manasā: This word refers to the "mind" or "intellect." It highlights the role of thought and intention in our actions.
  • bandhutā: This translates to "brotherhood" or "kinship," suggesting a sense of unity and connection among individuals.
  • nara: This means "man" or "human," emphasizing the human experience and the collective journey of humanity.
  • uśijo: This term can be interpreted as "those who are wise" or "the learned," pointing to the importance of knowledge and wisdom in guiding our actions.
  • jagmur: This means "they have gone" or "they have moved," indicating a journey or progression, possibly towards enlightenment or understanding.
  • abhi: This translates to "towards" or "in the direction of," suggesting a purposeful movement or intention.
  • tāni: This means "those" or "these," referring to the things or concepts being discussed.
  • vedasā: This term relates to "knowledge" or "wisdom," particularly the sacred knowledge found in the Vedas, which are ancient texts of Hindu philosophy.
  • yābhir: This translates to "by which" or "through which," indicating a means or method.
  • māyābhiḥ: This term refers to "illusions" or "deceptions," suggesting that there are distractions or falsehoods that can lead us astray.
  • pratijūtivarpasaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the power of the promise" or "the strength of commitment," emphasizing the importance of dedication and resolve.
  • saudhanvanā: This means "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating the material and spiritual abundance that can be achieved through right action.
  • yajñiyam: This term refers to "sacrifice" or "offering," highlighting the significance of selflessness and contribution to the greater good.
  • bhāgam: This translates to "portion" or "share," suggesting that everyone has a role to play in the collective experience.
  • ānaśa: This means "to attain" or "to achieve," indicating the goal of reaching a higher state of being or understanding.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of humanity and the importance of collective wisdom and intention. It calls for individuals to be mindful of their thoughts and actions, fostering a sense of brotherhood and shared purpose. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as community service, teamwork in professional settings, or even in personal relationships where understanding and cooperation are essential. By recognizing the illusions that may distract us and committing to a path of knowledge and sacrifice, we can achieve both material and spiritual prosperity, ultimately contributing to a harmonious society.
