जुषस्व सप्रथस्तमं वचो देवाप्सरस्तमम् |हव्यā जुह्वान आसनिः ||
juṣasva saprathastamaṁ vaco devapsarastamam | havyā juhvāna āsani ||
  • juṣasva: This word translates to "may you enjoy" or "may you be pleased." It signifies a call for joy and satisfaction.
  • saprathastamaṁ: This term means "the most excellent" or "the best among." It emphasizes the idea of superiority and excellence.
  • vaco: This translates to "speech" or "words." In the context of the mantra, it refers to the power of words and communication.
  • devapsarastamam: This phrase means "the most divine among the celestial beings." It highlights the connection to the divine and the celestial realm.
  • havyā: This word refers to offerings or sacrifices made to the deities. It signifies the act of giving and devotion.
  • juhvāna: This translates to "offering" or "sacrificing." It indicates the act of presenting something valuable to the divine.
  • āsani: This term means "to sit" or "to be present." It suggests a state of being or existence, often in a sacred context.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation, asking for the enjoyment of the best words that are divine and celestial, while also emphasizing the importance of offerings made to the deities. It reflects a deep respect for the power of speech and communication, as well as the significance of devotion and sacrifice in spiritual practices.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Communication: The emphasis on "vaco" highlights the importance of using words wisely and positively. In everyday life, this can translate to practicing effective communication, being mindful of the impact of our words on others, and striving for clarity and kindness in our interactions.
  • Devotion: The concept of "havyā" and "juhvāna" encourages individuals to engage in acts of devotion, whether through rituals, community service, or personal sacrifices for the greater good. This can manifest in volunteering, helping those in need, or simply being present for loved ones.
  • Excellence: The mantra's call for "saprathastamaṁ" inspires individuals to pursue excellence in their endeavors, whether in their careers, education, or personal growth. It serves as a reminder to strive for the best in all aspects of life.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra encapsulates the essence of joy, divine communication, and the importance of offerings. Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals to communicate thoughtfully, engage in acts of devotion, and pursue excellence in their lives. By embodying these principles, one can cultivate a more meaningful and fulfilling existence.
