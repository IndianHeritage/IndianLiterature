स इद दानाय दभ्याय वन्बञ्च च्यवानः सूदैर अमिमीत वेदिम् |तूर्वयाणो गूर्तवचस्तमः क्षोदो न रेत इतौति सिञ्चत ||
sa id dānāya dabhyāya vanvañ cyavānaḥ sūdair amimīta vedim | tūrvayāṇo gūrtavacastamaḥ kṣodo na reta itaūti siñcat ||
  • sa id dānāya: This phrase can be translated as "this is for the purpose of giving." It emphasizes the importance of generosity and sharing resources with others.
  • dabhyāya: This word refers to the act of offering or giving, particularly in a ritualistic context. It highlights the significance of sacrificial offerings in Vedic traditions.
  • vanvañ: This term can be understood as "to the forest" or "to nature." It signifies a connection to the natural world and the importance of respecting and nurturing it.
  • cyavānaḥ: This word means "to be driven away" or "to be removed." It can symbolize the removal of negativity or obstacles in life.
  • sūdair: This term refers to "the good ones" or "the virtuous." It emphasizes the role of righteous individuals in society.
  • amimīta vedim: This phrase translates to "the one who has not been measured." It suggests the infinite nature of knowledge and wisdom, which cannot be confined or quantified.
  • tūrvayāṇo: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is strong" or "the powerful." It signifies the strength that comes from knowledge and virtue.
  • gūrtavacastamaḥ: This phrase means "the one who is wise and knowledgeable." It highlights the importance of wisdom in guiding actions and decisions.
  • kṣodo na reta: This can be understood as "the one who does not lose vitality." It signifies the importance of maintaining one's energy and enthusiasm in life.
  • itaūti siñcat: This phrase translates to "may it be poured forth." It symbolizes the act of sharing blessings and abundance with others.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the values of generosity, wisdom, and the connection to nature. It teaches us the importance of sharing our resources and knowledge with others, fostering a sense of community and support. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways, such as through charitable acts, environmental conservation efforts, and the pursuit of knowledge. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and balanced society, where the well-being of all is prioritized.
