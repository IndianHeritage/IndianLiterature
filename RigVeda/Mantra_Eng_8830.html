ऋतस्य हि वर्त्तनायः सुजातम इषो वाजाय प्रदिवः साचन्ते |अधिवासं रोदसी वावसाने घृतैर अन्नैर वावृद्धाते मधूनाम् ||
ṛtasya hi vartanayaḥ sujātam iṣo vājāya pradivaḥ sacante | adhīvāsaṁ rodasī vāvasāne ghṛtair annair vāvṛdhāte madhūnām ||
  • ṛtasya hi vartanayaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "the path of truth is indeed the way." Here, "ṛta" refers to the cosmic order or truth, emphasizing the importance of living in harmony with the natural laws of the universe.
  • sujātam iṣo vājāya pradivaḥ sacante: This part means "the well-born (sujātam) are those who are endowed with strength (vājāya) and who are capable of achieving their goals." It highlights the significance of strength and capability in achieving success and fulfilling one's duties.
  • adhīvāsaṁ rodasī vāvasāne: This translates to "the abode of the heavens (rodasī) is where one finds fulfillment." It suggests that true contentment and success come from aligning oneself with higher principles and values.
  • ghṛtair annair vāvṛdhāte madhūnām: This phrase means "with ghee (ghṛta) and food (anna), one is nourished and enriched (vāvṛdhāte) like honey (madhūnām)." It symbolizes the importance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual, in leading a fulfilling life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of living in accordance with truth and cosmic order. It teaches that strength, capability, and alignment with higher values lead to fulfillment and success. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various aspects such as personal development, professional endeavors, and community service. By embodying these principles, individuals can create a harmonious and prosperous life, contributing positively to society and the world at large.
