वाचं सु मित्रावरुणाव इरावतīm पर्जन्यश चित्रां वदति त्विषीमतīm |अभ्रा वसत मरुतः सु मायया द्यां वर्षयतम अरुणाम् अरेपसम् ||
vācaṁ su mitrāvaruṇāv irāvatīm parjanyaś citrāṁ vadati tviṣīmatīm | abhrā vasata marutaḥ su māyayā dyāṁ varṣayatam aruṇām arepasam ||
  • vācaṁ: This word translates to "speech" or "voice." It signifies the power of communication and expression.
  • su: Meaning "good" or "well," it emphasizes the positive nature of the speech being referred to.
  • mitrāvaruṇāv: This compound word refers to the deities Mitra and Varuna, who represent friendship and cosmic order, respectively. They symbolize harmony and balance in the universe.
  • irāvatīm: This term can be interpreted as "the one that brings forth," often associated with abundance and prosperity.
  • parjanyaś: This word means "the rain-giver" or "the cloud," representing nourishment and sustenance through rain.
  • citrāṁ: Translated as "varied" or "colorful," it suggests diversity and richness in experiences or expressions.
  • vadati: This means "speaks" or "utters," indicating the act of communication.
  • tviṣīmatīm: This word refers to "brilliance" or "radiance," suggesting the enlightening power of speech.
  • abhrā: Meaning "clouds," it signifies the source of rain and fertility.
  • vasata: This translates to "dwelling" or "residing," indicating a state of being or existence.
  • marutaḥ: Referring to the "winds" or "storm gods," it symbolizes the dynamic forces of nature.
  • su māyayā: This phrase means "with good illusion" or "with good magic," suggesting the transformative power of perception and reality.
  • dyāṁ: This word means "sky" or "heaven," representing the vastness and potential of the universe.
  • varṣayatam: Translated as "to rain," it signifies the act of providing nourishment and sustenance.
  • aruṇām: This term refers to "the reddish" or "the dawn," symbolizing new beginnings and hope.
  • arepasam: This word can be interpreted as "to be invoked" or "to be called upon," indicating the act of seeking blessings or assistance.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of harmonious communication and the nurturing aspects of nature. It highlights the importance of expressing oneself positively and the role of divine forces in providing sustenance and abundance. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as fostering good relationships, promoting teamwork, and encouraging open dialogue in communities. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking balance and harmony in our interactions with others and the environment.
