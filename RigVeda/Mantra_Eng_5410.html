उद इन nv अस्य रिच्यते 'ंशो धनं न जिग्युषः |य इन्द्रो हरिवान न दभन्ति तं रिपो दक्षं दधाति सोमिनि ||
ud in nv asya ricyate 'ṁśo dhanaṁ na jigyuṣaḥ | ya indro harivān na dabhanti taṁ ripo dakṣaṁ dadhāti somini ||
  • ud in nv asya ricyate: This phrase can be translated as "the one who rises up in this world." It signifies the importance of striving for success and elevation in life. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who work hard to achieve their goals, such as students who study diligently to excel in their exams or professionals who seek promotions through dedication and effort.
  • 'ṁśo dhanaṁ na jigyuṣaḥ: This translates to "wealth does not come to those who are lazy." It emphasizes the value of hard work and effort in acquiring wealth and prosperity. For example, entrepreneurs who invest time and resources into their businesses often reap the rewards of their labor, while those who do not put in the effort may struggle financially.
  • ya indro harivān na dabhanti: This means "those who are favored by Indra (the king of gods) are not defeated." It highlights the idea that divine blessings and support can lead to success and protection from adversities. In real life, this can be likened to individuals who feel a sense of purpose and guidance in their lives, often attributing their successes to a higher power or their own moral integrity.
  • taṁ ripo dakṣaṁ dadhāti somini: This translates to "the one who is skilled and wise is supported by the moon (Soma)." It suggests that wisdom and skill are essential for success and that they are nurtured by a supportive environment. For instance, a mentor guiding a young professional can be seen as the 'moon' that nurtures their growth, leading to their eventual success in their career.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of hard work, wisdom, and divine support in achieving success and prosperity. It teaches that elevation in life comes from effort and that those who are diligent and skilled are favored and protected. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various fields such as education, business, and personal development, where individuals are encouraged to strive for excellence, seek guidance, and remain resilient in the face of challenges. The mantra serves as a reminder that success is not merely a result of luck but a combination of hard work, wisdom, and the right support system.
