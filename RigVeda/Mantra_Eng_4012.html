यादृग एव ददृशे तादृग उच्यते सं छायया दधिरे सिद्धरायाप्स्व् आ |महिम् अस्मभ्यम् उरुषाम् उरु ज्रयो बृहत् सुवीरम् अनपच्युतं सहः ||
yādṛg eva dadṛśe tādṛg ucyate saṁ chāyayā dadhire sidhrayāpsv ā | mahīm asmabhyam uruṣām uru jrayo bṛhat suvīram anapacyutaṁ sahaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yādṛg: "as it is seen" or "in the manner that it is perceived"
    • eva: "only" or "just"
    • dadṛśe: "to that which is seen"
    • tādṛg: "of that kind" or "such"
    • ucyate: "is said" or "is declared"
    • saṁ: "together" or "with"
    • chāyayā: "shadow" or "protection"
    • dadhire: "they have taken" or "they have held"
    • sidhrayā: "with success" or "with accomplishment"
    • apsv: "in the waters" or "in the fluid"
    • mahīm: "the earth" or "the great"
    • asmabhyam: "for us" or "to us"
    • uruṣām: "of the strong" or "of the powerful"
    • uru: "vast" or "great"
    • jrayo: "of strength" or "of vigor"
    • bṛhat: "the great" or "the vast"
    • suvīram: "good heroes" or "noble warriors"
    • anapacyutaṁ: "not falling" or "not declining"
    • sahaḥ: "together" or "with"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nature of perception and reality. It suggests that what we perceive is a reflection of our understanding and experience.
    • The phrase "as it is seen, so it is said" emphasizes the subjective nature of reality. For example, two people can witness the same event but interpret it differently based on their backgrounds and beliefs.
    • The mention of "shadow" indicates protection or guidance, suggesting that our perceptions are influenced by external factors, much like how shadows are cast by light.
    • The reference to "the earth" and "the strong" signifies stability and strength, implying that a solid foundation is necessary for growth and success.
    • The idea of "good heroes" and "not falling" suggests that perseverance and noble qualities are essential for achieving greatness.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as leadership, personal development, and conflict resolution.
    • In leadership, understanding that perceptions can vary helps leaders to communicate effectively and address concerns from different perspectives.
    • In personal development, recognizing that our experiences shape our reality encourages self-reflection and growth.
    • In conflict resolution, acknowledging differing perceptions can lead to more empathetic and constructive dialogues.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that our understanding of the world is shaped by our perceptions and experiences.
    • It encourages us to seek a deeper understanding of ourselves and others, fostering empathy and collaboration.
    • Ultimately, it teaches that strength, stability, and noble qualities are essential for achieving success and harmony in life.
