पुनानो वरिवस कृत्ध्यूरजं जनाय गिरवणः |हरे सृजान आशिरम् ||
punāno varivas kṛdhy ūrjaṁ janāya girvaṇaḥ | hare sṛjāna āśiram ||
  • Mantra: "punāno varivas kṛdhy ūrjaṁ janāya girvaṇaḥ | hare sṛjāna āśiram ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • punāno: Purifying or renewing
    • varivas: Best or excellent
    • kṛdhy: Create or make
    • ūrjaṁ: Energy or strength
    • janāya: For the people or for humanity
    • girvaṇaḥ: Of the seers or sages
    • hare: O Lord (often referring to Vishnu or Krishna)
    • sṛjāna: Creating or manifesting
    • āśiram: Abode or dwelling
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of renewal and the creation of energy for the benefit of humanity.
    • It calls upon divine forces (often represented by Lord Vishnu) to manifest strength and vitality in the world.
    • The reference to "sages" suggests that wisdom and knowledge are essential in this process of creation and renewal.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied to initiatives aimed at environmental sustainability, where the focus is on renewing natural resources for the benefit of all.
    • In personal development, individuals can use the essence of this mantra to inspire themselves to create positive changes in their lives, harnessing their inner strength and energy.
    • In leadership, this mantra can guide leaders to create a nurturing environment that fosters growth and well-being among their team members.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for renewal and the creation of positive energy for the collective good.
    • It serves as a reminder that wisdom and strength are essential in our endeavors to uplift ourselves and those around us.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire actions that promote sustainability, personal growth, and effective leadership, ultimately contributing to a harmonious society.
