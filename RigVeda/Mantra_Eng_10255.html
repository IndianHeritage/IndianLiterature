कुविद अङ्ग यवमन्तो यवं चिद यथा दान्त्य अनुपूर्वं वियूय |इहेहैषां कृणुहि भोजानानि ये बरहिषो नमोवृक्तिं न जग्मुः ||
kuvid aṅga yavamanto yavaṁ cid yathā dānty anupūrvaṁ viyūya | ihehaiṣāṁ kṛṇuhi bhojanāni ye barhiṣo namovṛktiṁ na jagmuḥ ||
  • kuvid: This term can be interpreted as "those who know" or "the wise." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in life.
  • aṅga: Meaning "parts" or "aspects," this word suggests that life is made up of various components that need to be harmonized.
  • yavamanto: This refers to "those who are engaged in the pursuit of grains" or "the seekers of sustenance." It highlights the significance of nourishment and sustenance in our lives.
  • yavaṁ: Translated as "barley" or "grain," it symbolizes the basic necessities of life that sustain us physically and spiritually.
  • cid: This word means "consciousness" or "awareness," indicating the importance of being aware of our surroundings and our actions.
  • yathā: Meaning "as" or "like," it draws a comparison, suggesting that certain actions or states of being are interconnected.
  • dānty: This term can be understood as "calm" or "peaceful," emphasizing the need for tranquility in our lives.
  • anupūrvaṁ: This means "following the order" or "in accordance with tradition," highlighting the importance of adhering to established practices and values.
  • viyūya: Translated as "having gathered" or "having assembled," it suggests the importance of community and coming together for a common purpose.
  • ihehaiṣāṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "in this world" or "in this life," grounding the mantra in our current existence.
  • kṛṇuhi: Meaning "make" or "create," it emphasizes the act of bringing something into existence, whether it be food, relationships, or knowledge.
  • bhojanāni: This translates to "food" or "nourishment," reinforcing the idea that sustenance is vital for life.
  • ye: Meaning "those who," it refers to a group of people or individuals who share a common trait or action.
  • barhiṣo: This term can be understood as "sacred" or "holy," indicating the importance of reverence in our actions, especially regarding nourishment.
  • namovṛktiṁ: This phrase means "the act of bowing" or "offering respect," highlighting the significance of gratitude and humility.
  • na jagmuḥ: Translated as "they do not go" or "they do not depart," suggesting a sense of permanence or commitment to the values expressed in the mantra.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of knowledge, community, and sustenance in our lives. It teaches us that being aware of our actions and maintaining a sense of calm and order is essential for a harmonious existence. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in family gatherings where food is shared, in community service where we come together to support one another, or in personal growth where we seek knowledge and understanding. The mantra encourages us to respect the sacredness of life and to be grateful for the nourishment we receive, both physically and spiritually. By embodying these principles, we can create a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
