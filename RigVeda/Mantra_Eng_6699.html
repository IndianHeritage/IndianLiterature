यः संस्थे चिच्छतक्रतुरादīm कृत्नोति वृत्रहा |जरीतृभ्यः पुरूवसुः ||
yaḥ saṁsthe cic chatakratur ād īṁ kṛṇoti vṛtrahā | jaritṛbhyaḥ purūvasuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaḥ: who
    • saṁsthe: in the assembly or gathering
    • cic: indeed, truly
    • chatakratur: the one who has a hundred powers or abilities
    • ād: to, towards
    • īṁ: the divine or the supreme
    • kṛṇoti: creates or brings forth
    • vṛtrahā: the slayer of Vritra (a demon representing obstacles)
    • jaritṛbhyaḥ: from the wise or learned
    • purūvasuḥ: the one who is rich in resources or wealth
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra speaks of a divine being who, in the assembly of the wise, truly possesses immense powers and creates towards the supreme. This being is recognized as the slayer of Vritra, symbolizing the removal of obstacles, and is associated with those who are rich in wisdom and resources.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who can navigate challenges and inspire their team can be seen as a modern-day "vṛtrahā," overcoming obstacles to achieve success.
    • In personal life, an individual who seeks knowledge and wisdom, and uses it to create positive change in their community embodies the essence of "purūvasuḥ."
    • In education, a teacher who empowers students with knowledge and skills can be likened to the divine being in the mantra, fostering growth and overcoming barriers to learning.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the power of wisdom, the importance of overcoming obstacles, and the role of divine guidance in our lives. It teaches us that by gathering knowledge and resources, we can face challenges effectively and create a better future. In real-life scenarios, whether in leadership, education, or personal development, the principles encapsulated in this mantra encourage us to strive for wisdom and to be proactive in overcoming the hurdles we encounter.
