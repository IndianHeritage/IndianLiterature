अक्रन्दद अग्निः स्तनयन्न इव द्यौः क्षामā रेरिहद वीरुधः समञ्जन |सद्योजज्नानो वि हीम इद्धो अख्यद आ रोडसी भानुना भात्य अन्तः ||
akrandad agniḥ stanayann iva dyauḥ kṣāmā rerihad vīrudhaḥ samañjan | sadyo jajñāno vi hīm iddho akhyad ā rodasī bhānunā bhāty antaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • akrandat: cried out
    • agniḥ: fire
    • stanayann: nourishing or sustaining
    • iva: like
    • dyauḥ: the sky or heaven
    • kṣāmā: the earth or the one who is patient
    • rerihad: has become strong or powerful
    • vīrudhaḥ: the plants or vegetation
    • samañjan: harmonizing or bringing together
    • sadyo: immediately
    • jajñāno: born or created
    • vi: indeed
    • hīm: warmth or energy
    • iddho: kindled or ignited
    • akhyad: has declared or revealed
    • ā: towards
    • rodasī: the two worlds or realms
    • bhānunā: by the light or radiance
    • bhāty: shines or illuminates
    • antaḥ: within or inside
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the powerful and nurturing aspects of fire (Agni) and its connection to the heavens and the earth.
    • Agni is often seen as a mediator between the divine and the earthly realms, symbolizing transformation and energy.
    • The phrase "akrandad agniḥ" suggests that fire has a voice, crying out for attention, which can be interpreted as a call to recognize the importance of fire in our lives.
    • The nurturing aspect of fire is highlighted by "stanayann iva dyauḥ," indicating that just as the sky nourishes the earth, fire sustains life through warmth and energy.
    • The mantra also emphasizes the immediate creation and revelation of energy, as seen in "sadyo jajñāno vi hīm iddho," which can be related to the instant energy we experience from fire in our daily lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In cooking, fire transforms raw ingredients into nourishing meals, demonstrating the nurturing aspect of Agni.
    • In rituals, fire is often used as a medium to connect with the divine, symbolizing the bridge between the earthly and spiritual realms.
    • In nature, wildfires can rejuvenate ecosystems, allowing new growth to emerge, reflecting the cycle of destruction and creation.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the essential role of fire in sustaining life and connecting different realms of existence.
    • It teaches us to respect and harness the power of fire, recognizing its dual nature as both a creator and a destroyer.
    • In our daily lives, we can apply this understanding by being mindful of our energy consumption, respecting nature, and using fire in a way that promotes harmony and balance.
