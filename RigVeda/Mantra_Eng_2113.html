धिष्वा शवः शूर येन वृत्रम अवाभिनद दानुम और्णवाभम् | अपावृणोर ज्योतिर आर्याय नि सव्यतः सादि दस्युर इन्द्र ||
dhiṣvā śavaḥ śūra yena vṛtram avābhinad dānum aurṇavābham | apāvṛṇor jyotir āryāya ni savyataḥ sādi dasyur indra ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • dhiṣvā: This term can be interpreted as "with wisdom" or "with understanding."
    • śavaḥ: Refers to "the dead" or "the lifeless," often symbolizing obstacles or challenges.
    • śūra: Means "brave" or "valiant," indicating strength and courage.
    • yena: Translates to "by whom," indicating agency or the one who acts.
    • vṛtram: Refers to "Vritra," a serpent or dragon symbolizing chaos and obstacles.
    • avābhinad: Means "to strike down" or "to overcome."
    • dānum: Refers to "the body" or "the form," often symbolizing the material world.
    • aurṇavābham: Can be interpreted as "the golden" or "the radiant," symbolizing purity and enlightenment.
    • apāvṛṇor: Means "of the one who is unbound" or "the limitless."
    • jyotir: Translates to "light," symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
    • āryāya: Refers to "for the noble" or "for the worthy."
    • ni: Means "not" or "without."
    • savyataḥ: Refers to "from the left" or "from the direction of the left," often symbolizing the unknown or the subconscious.
    • sādi: Means "to begin" or "to initiate."
    • dasyur: Refers to "the enemy" or "the adversary."
    • indra: The king of gods, symbolizing power and authority.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the power of wisdom and courage in overcoming obstacles and challenges represented by Vritra.
    • It emphasizes the importance of enlightenment and knowledge (jyotir) in achieving victory over adversities.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies the need for divine support and strength in facing life's battles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when facing a challenging project, one can draw upon the wisdom and courage to tackle the obstacles, much like Indra overcoming Vritra.
    • In personal life, during times of emotional turmoil, seeking enlightenment through meditation or knowledge can help one navigate through difficulties.
    • In community efforts, collective wisdom and bravery can lead to overcoming societal challenges, akin to the strength of Indra in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of wisdom, courage, and divine support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and strength in the face of adversity.
    • Ultimately, it inspires a proactive approach to life's difficulties, urging one to harness inner strength and seek enlightenment.
