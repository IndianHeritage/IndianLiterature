यस् ते श्रिङ्गवृषो नपात प्रणपात कुण्डपाय्यः |न्य अस्मिन दध्र आ मनः ||
yas te śṛṅgavṛṣo napāt praṇapāt kuṇḍapāyyaḥ | ny asmin dadhra ā manaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • śṛṅgavṛṣo: the one with the horns (often referring to a deity or a powerful being)
    • napāt: descendant or offspring
    • praṇapāt: the one who is revered or worshipped
    • kuṇḍapāyyaḥ: the one who is associated with the sacred or the divine
    • ny: not
    • asmin: in this
    • dadhra: to hold or to maintain
    • ā: towards
    • manaḥ: mind or consciousness
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine being who is powerful and revered, emphasizing the connection between the divine and the human mind.
    • It suggests that the divine presence is essential for maintaining a focused and pure mind.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek guidance or inspiration from higher powers or deities when faced with challenges. For instance, a student may pray for concentration and clarity before an important exam.
    • In moments of stress or confusion, people may meditate or engage in spiritual practices to connect with their inner selves, reflecting the idea of holding the mind towards the divine.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of spiritual connection in achieving mental clarity and focus.
    • It can be applied in various scenarios, such as during meditation, prayer, or any practice aimed at enhancing mental well-being.
    • By invoking the divine, individuals can find strength and guidance, leading to a more balanced and harmonious life.
