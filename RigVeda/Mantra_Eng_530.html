श्रुधि श्रुतकर्ण वाह्निभिर देवैर अग्ने सयावभिः |आ सीदन्तु बर्हिषि मित्रो आर्यमा प्रातर्यावाणो अध्वरम् ||
śrudhi śrutkarṇa vahnibhir devair agne sayāvabhiḥ | ā sīdantu barhiṣi mitro aryamā prātaryāvāṇo adhvaram ||
  • śrudhi: This word translates to "listen" or "hear." It emphasizes the importance of being attentive and receptive to divine knowledge and wisdom.
  • śrutkarṇa: This means "having ears to hear." It suggests that one should not only hear but also understand and internalize the teachings and messages conveyed.
  • vahnibhir: This refers to "by the fires" or "by the flames." In Vedic tradition, fire is a symbol of purity, transformation, and divine presence.
  • devair: This means "by the gods." It indicates that the invocation is directed towards divine beings, seeking their blessings and guidance.
  • agne: This is a direct address to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
  • sayāvabhiḥ: This translates to "with the offerings." It signifies the act of giving and sharing, which is essential in rituals and prayers.
  • ā sīdantu: This means "may they sit" or "may they come." It is an invitation for the divine presence to manifest in the ritual space.
  • barhiṣi: This refers to "on the grass" or "on the sacred grass." It symbolizes the sacredness of the space where the rituals are performed.
  • mitro: This means "friend." It highlights the importance of friendship and harmony in relationships, both with humans and the divine.
  • aryamā: This refers to a deity associated with the sun and truth, representing righteousness and moral order.
  • prātaryāvāṇo: This means "the one who brings forth in the morning." It signifies new beginnings, hope, and the arrival of light after darkness.
  • adhvaram: This translates to "the sacrifice" or "the ritual." It emphasizes the significance of rituals in connecting with the divine and the community.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of listening, understanding, and inviting divine presence into our lives through rituals and offerings. It highlights the significance of friendship, righteousness, and new beginnings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal prayers, community gatherings, or significant life events like weddings and festivals. By invoking the divine and fostering a sense of community, individuals can create a harmonious environment that promotes spiritual growth and well-being.
