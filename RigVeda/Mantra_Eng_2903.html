आकरे वासोर जरीता पनस्यते 'नेहसः स्तुभ इन्द्रो दुवस्यति |विवस्वतः सदन आ हि पिप्रियें सत्रासाहम अभिमातिहनं स्तुहि ||
ākare vasor jaritā panasyate 'nehasaḥ stubha indro duvasyati | vivasvataḥ sadana ā hi pipriye satrāsāham abhimātihanaṁ stuhi ||
  • Ākare: This word can be interpreted as "in the form" or "in the manifestation." It signifies the importance of physical existence and the tangible aspects of life.
  • Vasor: This refers to "the abode" or "the dwelling." It can symbolize the home or the place where one resides, emphasizing the significance of community and belonging.
  • Jaritā: This term means "the one who brings forth" or "the creator." It highlights the role of creativity and innovation in life.
  • Panasyate: This can be understood as "to be nourished" or "to be sustained." It reflects the idea of sustenance, both physical and spiritual.
  • 'Nehasaḥ: This word denotes "the one who is connected" or "the one who is united." It emphasizes the importance of relationships and connections in life.
  • Stubha: This means "to praise" or "to extol." It signifies the act of recognizing and honoring the divine or the good in life.
  • Indro: Referring to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature, it symbolizes power, strength, and leadership.
  • Duvasyati: This can be interpreted as "to be invoked" or "to be called upon." It reflects the idea of seeking guidance and support from higher powers.
  • Vivasvataḥ: This term refers to "the sun" or "the radiant one," symbolizing enlightenment, knowledge, and clarity.
  • Sadana: This means "the dwelling place" or "the home." It emphasizes the importance of a safe and nurturing environment.
  • Ā hi: This phrase can be understood as "indeed" or "truly," reinforcing the truth of the statement that follows.
  • Pipriye: This means "to desire" or "to long for." It reflects human aspirations and the pursuit of goals.
  • Satrāsāham: This term signifies "the one who is strong in the assembly" or "the one who is powerful in gatherings," emphasizing the importance of community and collective strength.
  • Abhimātihanaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is worthy of praise" or "the one who deserves recognition," highlighting the value of acknowledging contributions and achievements.
  • Stuhi: This means "to praise" or "to celebrate," reinforcing the theme of recognition and appreciation.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of existence, the importance of community, and the role of creativity and leadership in life. It encourages individuals to recognize and honor the divine and the good in their surroundings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as fostering teamwork in professional settings, nurturing relationships within families, and promoting community engagement. By acknowledging the contributions of others and celebrating achievements, we create a harmonious and supportive environment that fosters growth and well-being.
