देवा एतस्याम् अवदन्त पूर्वे सप्तऋषयः तपसे ये निषेदुः |भीमा जाया ब्राह्मणस्योपनीता दुर्धां दधाति परम व्योमन ||
devā etasyām avadanta pūrve saptaṛṣayas tapase ye niṣeduḥ | bhīmā jāyā brāhmaṇasyopanītā durdhāṁ dadhāti parame vyoman ||
  • Translation: "The gods have spoken about this; the seven sages of old, who engaged in austerity, have settled here. The mighty one, born of the Brahman, brings forth the difficult to attain in the supreme sky."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • devā: gods or divine beings, representing higher powers or forces in the universe.
    • etasyām: about this, indicating a specific context or subject matter.
    • avadanta: have spoken or declared, suggesting the importance of divine communication.
    • pūrve: of old or ancient, referring to the wisdom of past sages.
    • saptaṛṣayas: the seven sages, who are revered figures in Vedic literature known for their wisdom and spiritual practices.
    • tapase: in austerity, highlighting the significance of disciplined spiritual practice.
    • ye: who, referring to the sages mentioned earlier.
    • niṣeduḥ: have settled, indicating a place of importance or sanctity.
    • bhīmā: mighty or powerful, often associated with strength and resilience.
    • jāyā: born or produced, referring to the manifestation of something significant.
    • brāhmaṇasya: of the Brahman, the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit in Hindu philosophy.
    • upanītā: brought forth or delivered, suggesting the emergence of knowledge or wisdom.
    • durdhāṁ: difficult to attain, indicating the challenges in achieving certain spiritual or material goals.
    • dadhāti: brings forth or provides, emphasizing the act of creation or manifestation.
    • parame: supreme or highest, referring to the ultimate state of existence.
    • vyoman: sky or space, symbolizing the vastness of the universe and the spiritual realm.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of divine guidance can be seen in various cultures where people seek blessings or advice from higher powers before making significant decisions, such as starting a new business or entering a marriage.
    • The practice of meditation and yoga, which are modern forms of austerity, reflects the teachings of the sages, emphasizing discipline and self-control to achieve personal growth and enlightenment.
    • In scientific endeavors, the pursuit of knowledge often requires rigorous study and perseverance, akin to the austerity practiced by the sages, leading to breakthroughs that can be seen as 'difficult to attain' achievements.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine wisdom and the role of ancient sages in guiding humanity through their spiritual practices. It signifies that through discipline and connection with the divine, one can attain profound knowledge and understanding. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, decision-making, and the pursuit of knowledge, reminding us that the journey towards enlightenment often requires effort and dedication. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine, the sages, and the seekers of truth in our quest for meaning and purpose in life.
