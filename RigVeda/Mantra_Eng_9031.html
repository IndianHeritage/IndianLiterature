मन्त्र: उता व्रतानि सोम ते प्राहम् मिनामि पाक्याः |अधा पितेव सूनवे वि वो मदे मृळा नो अभि चिद वधाद विवक्षसे ||
uta vratāni soma te prāham mināmi pākyā | adhā piteva sūnave vi vo made mṛḻā no abhi cid vadhād vivakṣase ||
  • uta vratāni: This phrase can be translated as "O Soma, I invoke the vows." Here, "Soma" refers to a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing divine inspiration and the essence of life.
  • te prāham mināmi pākyā: This means "I offer you my pure intentions." The word "pākyā" signifies purity, indicating that the offerings made are sincere and untainted.
  • adhā piteva sūnave: Translated as "like a father to his son," this phrase emphasizes the nurturing and protective nature of the divine. It suggests a relationship of care and guidance.
  • vi vo made: This can be interpreted as "in your joy." It reflects the idea of celebrating the divine presence and the joy that comes from spiritual connection.
  • mṛḻā no abhi cid vadhād: This translates to "may we not be harmed by any obstacles." It expresses a desire for protection from negativity and challenges that may arise in life.
  • vivakṣase: This means "you who are the source of all desires." It acknowledges the divine as the ultimate provider of aspirations and needs.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of devotion, purity of intention, and the nurturing relationship between the divine and humanity. In real life, it serves as a reminder to approach our goals and aspirations with sincerity and purity, seeking guidance and protection from higher powers. It is applicable in various scenarios, such as during personal challenges, spiritual practices, or when making important decisions. By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of connection with the divine, fostering resilience and clarity in their pursuits.
