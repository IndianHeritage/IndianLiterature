विष्वान् देवान् हवामहे मरुतः सोमपीतये |उग्राः हि पृष्निमातरः ||
viśvān devān havāmahe marutaḥ somapītaye | ugrā hi pṛśnimātaraḥ ||
  • viśvān devān havāmahe: This phrase translates to "We invoke all the gods." The term "viśvān" means "all" or "universal," and "devān" refers to "gods." This invocation signifies a holistic approach to spirituality, recognizing the presence and importance of various divine forces in our lives.
  • marutaḥ somapītaye: Here, "marutaḥ" refers to the Maruts, the storm gods associated with strength and vitality, while "somapītaye" means "for the sake of the drink of Soma." Soma is a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight. This part of the mantra emphasizes the need for divine blessings and strength in our endeavors.
  • ugrā hi pṛśnimātaraḥ: The term "ugrā" means "fierce" or "powerful," and "pṛśnimātaraḥ" can be interpreted as "those who are the most powerful." This line acknowledges the formidable nature of the divine forces we are calling upon, suggesting that we seek their strength and protection in our lives.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support and strength from various gods, particularly in challenging situations. It reflects a universal approach to spirituality, where one acknowledges the interconnectedness of all divine energies.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during significant life events like weddings, new ventures, or even during times of crisis. For instance, when starting a new business, invoking the blessings of all divine forces can provide the necessary strength and guidance to navigate challenges.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine assistance and recognizing the multifaceted nature of spirituality. By invoking the strength of the gods, individuals can find courage and resilience in their daily lives, fostering a sense of unity and support in their spiritual journey.
