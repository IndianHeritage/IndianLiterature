कविर न निञ्यं विदथानि साधन वृषा यत सेकं विपिपानो अर्चात् |दिव इत्तथा जीजनत सप्त कारून अह्ना चिच चक्रुर वयुनाऽ गृणन्तः ||
kavir na niṇyaṁ vidathāni sādhan vṛṣā yat sekaṁ vipipāno arcāt | diva itthā jījanat sapta kārūn ahnā cic cakrur vayunā gṛṇantaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Kavir: The wise or the poet, often referring to a divine being or a sage.
    • Na: Not.
    • Niṇyaṁ: To be restrained or limited.
    • Vidathāni: The various forms or manifestations.
    • Sādhan: The means or methods.
    • Vṛṣā: The strong or powerful.
    • Yat: That which.
    • Sekaṁ: The act of pouring or offering.
    • Vipipāno: The one who is being nourished or sustained.
    • Arcāt: In worship or reverence.
    • Diva: Heaven or the divine realm.
    • Itthā: Thus or in this manner.
    • Jījanat: To create or to bring forth.
    • Sapta: Seven.
    • Kārūn: The creators or the makers.
    • Ahna: Day or time.
    • Cic: To know or to understand.
    • Cakrur: They have created or formed.
    • Vayunā: With strength or power.
    • Gṛṇantaḥ: Singing or praising.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine wisdom that transcends limitations and manifests in various forms.
    • The wise (Kavir) is not bound by the constraints of the material world (Na Niṇyaṁ) and can create (Jījanat) through the power of knowledge and understanding (Cic).
    • The act of offering (Sekaṁ) in worship (Arcāt) nourishes the divine (Vipipāno), leading to the creation of various forms of existence (Vidathāni).
    • The seven creators (Sapta Kārūn) symbolize the different aspects of creation, working together in harmony (Vayunā) to bring forth life and sustenance.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and collaboration in the process of creation and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team project, the diverse skills and knowledge of each member (the seven creators) come together to create a successful outcome, demonstrating the power of collaboration.
    • In nature, various elements work together harmoniously (like the sapta) to sustain life, such as the interaction between plants, animals, and the environment.
    • In personal growth, wisdom gained through experiences (Kavir) allows individuals to overcome challenges and create new opportunities (Jījanat).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom, collaboration, and the divine process of creation.
    • It teaches us that by understanding and respecting the interconnectedness of all things, we can contribute to a harmonious existence.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to personal growth, successful teamwork, and a deeper appreciation for the natural world.
