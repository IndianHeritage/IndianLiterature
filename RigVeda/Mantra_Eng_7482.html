मन्त्र: खे रथस्य खे 'नसः खे युगस्य शतक्रतो |अपालाम इन्द्र त्रिष पूत्व्य अकृत्णोः सूर्यत्वचम् ||
khe rathasya khe 'nasaḥ khe yugasya śatakrato | apālām indra triṣ pūtvy akṛṇoḥ sūryatvacam ||
  • khe rathasya: This phrase translates to "in the sky of the chariot." Here, "khe" refers to the sky or space, and "rathasya" means chariot. The chariot is often symbolic of the journey of life or the soul's journey through existence.
  • khe 'nasaḥ: This means "in the sky of the breath." "Anasaḥ" refers to breath or life force. This can be interpreted as the vital energy that sustains life, emphasizing the connection between the cosmos and individual existence.
  • khe yugasya: This translates to "in the sky of the ages." "Yugasya" refers to the different epochs or cycles of time in Hindu cosmology. This phrase suggests a broader perspective on time and existence, indicating that all actions and events are part of a larger cosmic order.
  • śatakrato: This term refers to "the one with a hundred powers" or "the one who has a hundred abilities." It is often associated with Indra, the king of the gods, symbolizing strength, power, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • apālām: This means "to protect" or "to guard." It signifies the importance of safeguarding one's life and the lives of others, highlighting the role of divine protection in human affairs.
  • indra triṣ pūtvy: This phrase can be interpreted as "Indra, the purifier of three." "Triṣ" refers to three, which could symbolize the three realms: physical, mental, and spiritual. It emphasizes the need for purification and balance in all aspects of life.
  • akṛṇoḥ: This means "to create" or "to make." It signifies the act of bringing forth or manifesting something into existence, highlighting the creative power inherent in the universe.
  • sūryatvacam: This translates to "the radiance of the sun." "Sūrya" refers to the sun, which is often seen as a source of life, energy, and enlightenment. This phrase emphasizes the importance of light and knowledge in dispelling darkness and ignorance.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound themes of cosmic connection, the journey of life, and the interplay of divine powers. It serves as a reminder of the importance of protection, purification, and the creative forces at work in the universe. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek balance in their lives, recognize the interconnectedness of all beings, and strive for personal growth and enlightenment. It encourages us to harness our inner strength and creativity while remaining aware of the larger cosmic order that influences our existence.
