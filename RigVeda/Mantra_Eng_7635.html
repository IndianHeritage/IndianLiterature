समुद्रे अन्तः शयत उद्ना वज्रो अभिवृतः |भरन्त्य अस्मै संयतः पुरःप्रस्रवणā बलिम् ||
samudre antaḥ śayata udnā vajro abhīvṛtaḥ | bharanty asmai saṁyataḥ puraḥprasravaṇā balim ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samudre: in the ocean
    • antaḥ: within
    • śayata: lying down or resting
    • udnā: the waters or the waves
    • vajro: like a thunderbolt or strong
    • abhīvṛtaḥ: surrounded or enveloped
    • bharanty: they carry or bear
    • asmai: to him or to that
    • saṁyataḥ: controlled or restrained
    • puraḥprasravaṇā: flowing forth or emerging
    • balim: offerings or sacrifices
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra describes a powerful imagery of the ocean, where the waves lie within and are surrounded by a strong force, akin to a thunderbolt.
    • The ocean can be seen as a metaphor for the vastness of life and experiences, while the waves represent the challenges and emotions we face.
    • The phrase "they carry offerings" suggests that despite the tumultuous nature of life, there is a flow of energy and resources that can be directed towards higher purposes.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, the ocean can symbolize the market, while the waves represent competition and challenges. A company that navigates these challenges effectively can harness the energy of the market to create value.
    • In personal life, individuals often face emotional waves—stress, joy, sorrow. Recognizing that these emotions are part of a larger ocean of experience can help in managing them better.
    • In environmental discussions, the ocean represents nature, and the waves can symbolize climate change. The mantra reminds us of our responsibility to offer solutions and sacrifices for the betterment of the planet.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of understanding the forces at play in our lives and the need to channel our energies towards constructive outcomes.
    • It teaches us that while we may be surrounded by challenges (the waves), we have the power to control our responses and direct our efforts towards meaningful contributions (the offerings).
    • In essence, it serves as a reminder to remain resilient and purposeful, regardless of the circumstances we find ourselves in.
