अभि त्रिपृष्टं वृषणं वयोधाम् आङ्गूषाणाम् अवावशन्त वाणिः |वना वसानो वरुणो न सिन्धून् वि रत्नधा दयते वार्याणि ||
abhi tripṛṣṭhaṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ vayodhām āṅgūṣāṇām avāvaśanta vāṇīḥ | vanā vasāno varuṇo na sindhūn vi ratnadhā dayate vāryāṇi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • tripṛṣṭhaṁ: threefold, having three aspects
    • vṛṣaṇaṁ: the one who is strong or powerful
    • vayodhām: of the strong, of the powerful
    • āṅgūṣāṇām: of the fingers, or the small parts
    • avāvaśanta: to be protected or sheltered
    • vāṇīḥ: the voice, the sound, or the speech
    • vanā: in the forest, or in nature
    • vasāno: residing, dwelling
    • varuṇo: Varuna, the god of water and cosmic order
    • na: not
    • sindhūn: rivers
    • vi: indeed, certainly
    • ratnadhā: the one who bestows treasures
    • dayate: gives, bestows
    • vāryāṇi: the waters, or the sacred waters
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful entity that is threefold in nature, representing strength and protection.
    • The reference to "vayodhām" suggests a connection to the powerful forces of nature, which can be likened to the strength found in community or teamwork.
    • The mention of "vāṇīḥ" indicates the importance of communication and expression, which can be seen in how we share knowledge and ideas in our daily lives.
    • "Varuṇo" symbolizes the divine order and the natural laws that govern our existence, much like the laws of physics that dictate how we interact with the world.
    • The phrase "dayate vāryāṇi" emphasizes the act of giving and sharing resources, akin to how communities come together to support one another in times of need.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team project, the strength of each member contributes to the overall success, reflecting the threefold nature of strength.
    • In nature, the balance of ecosystems showcases the importance of communication and interaction among different species.
    • Community service initiatives demonstrate the act of giving and sharing resources, embodying the essence of "dayate vāryāṇi."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of strength, communication, and the natural order.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us of our responsibilities towards each other and the environment.
    • In real life, it encourages us to work together, communicate effectively, and support one another, fostering a sense of community and harmony.
