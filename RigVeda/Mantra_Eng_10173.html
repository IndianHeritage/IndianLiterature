या आत्मदां बलदां यस्य विश्व उपासते प्रशीषं यस्य देवाः |यस्य छायामृतं यस्य मृत्युः कसमै देवाय हविषा विद्हेम ||
ya ātmadā baladā yasya viśva upāsate praśiṣaṁ yasya devāḥ | yasya chāyāmṛtaṁ yasya mṛtyuḥ kasmai devāya haviṣā vidhema ||
  • ya ātmadā baladā: This phrase can be translated as "He who is the source of strength and vitality." In real life, this can be related to individuals who inspire and empower others, such as leaders or mentors who provide guidance and support.
  • yasya viśva upāsate: This means "Whom the entire universe worships." This can be seen in the context of universal values or principles that are revered across cultures, such as love, compassion, and justice.
  • praśiṣaṁ yasya devāḥ: This translates to "Whose commands are followed by the gods." In a practical sense, this can refer to the moral and ethical guidelines that govern human behavior, akin to the laws of nature or societal norms that are respected and upheld.
  • yasya chāyāmṛtaṁ: This means "In whose shadow is immortality." This can be interpreted as the influence of great leaders or thinkers whose legacies continue to inspire future generations, such as Mahatma Gandhi or Martin Luther King Jr.
  • yasya mṛtyuḥ: This translates to "Whose death is." This can signify the inevitability of death and the importance of living a meaningful life, reminding us to focus on what truly matters.
  • kasmai devāya haviṣā vidhema: This means "To which deity shall we offer our oblation?" This reflects the human quest for connection with the divine or the search for purpose in life, prompting us to consider what we value most and how we express our gratitude.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound philosophical concepts about strength, universality, divine influence, immortality, and the quest for purpose. Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to seek strength within themselves, recognize the universal values that bind humanity, and strive for a life that honors ethical principles. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire personal growth, community leadership, and a deeper understanding of our place in the universe, guiding us to live with intention and reverence for the interconnectedness of all beings.
