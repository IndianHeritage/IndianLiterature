उग्रबाहुर म्रक्षकर्त्वा पुरंदरो यदि मे श्रिणवद धवम् | वसूयवो वसुपतिं शतक्रतुं स्तोमैर इन्द्रं हवामहे ||
ugrabāhur mrakṣakṛtvā puraṁdaro yadi me śṛṇavad dhavam | vasūyavo vasupatiṁ śatakratuṁ stomair indraṁ havāmahe ||
  • ugrabāhur: This term can be broken down into two parts: "ugra" meaning powerful or fierce, and "bāhur" meaning strong-armed. It signifies the might and strength of the deity being invoked.
  • mrakṣakṛtvā: This word translates to "having protected" or "as a protector." It emphasizes the role of the deity as a guardian and protector of the faithful.
  • puraṁdaro: This term refers to "the destroyer of cities," often associated with Indra, the king of gods, who is known for his valor and ability to conquer enemies.
  • yadi me: This phrase means "if you (Indra) listen to me." It expresses a plea for attention and favor from the deity.
  • śṛṇavad: This translates to "having heard" or "listening." It indicates the expectation that the deity will heed the call of the devotee.
  • dhavam: This word means "to rush" or "to come quickly." It conveys urgency in the invocation, asking the deity to respond swiftly.
  • vasūyavo: This term refers to "the wealth-givers" or "the prosperous ones," highlighting the blessings of abundance and prosperity that the deity can bestow.
  • vasupatiṁ: This translates to "the lord of wealth," again referring to Indra, who is seen as a provider of riches and prosperity.
  • śatakratuṁ: This term means "the one with a hundred powers" or "the one with a hundred sacrifices," emphasizing the immense capabilities and strength of Indra.
  • stomair: This word means "with hymns" or "with praises," indicating that the invocation is accompanied by reverence and worship.
  • indraṁ: This is the name of the deity being invoked, Indra, who is revered as the god of rain, thunder, and war.
  • havāmahe: This translates to "we invoke" or "we call upon," signifying the act of worship and the desire for divine intervention.

Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation to Indra, seeking his protection, blessings, and swift response to the needs of the devotees. In real-life scenarios, it can be applicable in times of distress or when one seeks strength and support in overcoming challenges. For instance, individuals facing difficulties in their personal or professional lives can recite this mantra to invoke the protective and empowering qualities of Indra, hoping for guidance and prosperity. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect the devotee with divine strength and abundance, fostering a sense of security and hope in challenging times.
