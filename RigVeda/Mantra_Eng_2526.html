वृषायन्ते महे अत्याय पूर्वीर वृष्णे चित्राय रश्मयः सुयामाः |देव होतर मन्द्रतारश चिकीत्वान महो देवान रोडसी इह वक्षि ||
vṛṣāyante mahe atyāya pūrvīr vṛṣṇe citrāya raśmayaḥ suyāmāḥ | deva hotar mandrataraś cikitvān maho devān rodasī eha vakṣi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vṛṣāyante: They (the rays) are powerful or strong.
    • mahe: Great or mighty.
    • atyāya: Beyond measure or limit.
    • pūrvīr: From the ancients or the past.
    • vṛṣṇe: To Vishnu or the divine.
    • citrāya: To the colorful or the diverse.
    • raśmayaḥ: Rays or beams of light.
    • suyāmāḥ: Well-formed or auspicious.
    • deva: God or divine being.
    • hotar: The priest or the one who offers.
    • mandrataraś: With wisdom or thoughtful.
    • cikitvān: Having knowledge or understanding.
    • maho: Great or vast.
    • devān: The gods.
    • rodasī: The two worlds or realms.
    • eha: Here or in this place.
    • vakṣi: You will speak or declare.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the powerful rays of light that emanate from the divine, symbolizing knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment.
    • The reference to "Vishnu" indicates a connection to preservation and sustenance, suggesting that these rays are essential for life and growth.
    • The mention of "ancients" implies that this wisdom is timeless and has been passed down through generations.
    • The "priest" or "hotar" signifies the role of individuals who facilitate communication with the divine, emphasizing the importance of ritual and intention in spiritual practices.
    • The phrase "you will speak" suggests that the knowledge gained from these divine rays should be shared and communicated, fostering a sense of community and collective wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment can be seen in educational institutions where individuals seek to gain wisdom and share it with others.
    • Spiritual practices such as meditation and yoga can be viewed as ways to connect with the divine and harness the "rays" of knowledge and understanding.
    • Community gatherings and discussions about philosophical or spiritual topics reflect the idea of sharing wisdom and insights, akin to the "hotar" role in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine knowledge and the importance of sharing it within communities.
    • It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and understanding, not just for personal growth but for the benefit of society as a whole.
    • In real life, this can be applied through education, spiritual practices, and community engagement, fostering a culture of learning and enlightenment.
