मन्त्र: वोचेमेद इन्द्रं मगवाणं एनं महो रायो राधसो यद ददन नः |यो अर्चतो ब्रह्मकृतिम अविष्टो यूम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
vocemed indram maghavānam enam maho rāyo rādhaso yad dadan naḥ | yo arcato brahmakṛtim aviṣṭho yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Vocemed Indram: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke Indra." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength, power, and the forces of nature. In real life, invoking Indra can symbolize seeking strength and guidance in challenging situations.
  • Maghavānam enam: This means "the powerful one." It refers to the might and influence of Indra. In our daily lives, we often seek the support of powerful figures or forces, whether they be mentors, leaders, or even inner strength during tough times.
  • Maho rāyo rādhaso: This translates to "the great wealth of the radiant." Here, "wealth" can be interpreted not just in material terms but also as knowledge, health, and prosperity. In modern life, this can relate to the pursuit of holistic well-being and abundance in various forms.
  • Yad dadan naḥ: This means "that which you give us." It signifies a request for blessings and support. In practical terms, this can be seen in our daily prayers or meditations where we seek guidance and assistance from higher powers or the universe.
  • Yo arcato: This translates to "who is worshipped." It emphasizes the importance of reverence and respect towards the divine or the forces we believe in. In real life, this can be reflected in how we honor our traditions, values, and the people we look up to.
  • Brahmakṛtim: This means "the creation of the divine." It refers to the idea that everything in existence is a manifestation of a higher power. In our lives, recognizing this can lead to a deeper appreciation for nature, art, and the interconnectedness of all things.
  • Aviṣṭho: This translates to "unobstructed" or "free." It signifies the importance of clarity and freedom in our pursuits. In a real-life context, this can relate to the need for mental clarity and the removal of obstacles in our personal and professional lives.
  • Yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ: This means "may you protect us with auspiciousness." It is a plea for safety and well-being. In everyday life, this can be seen in our desire for protection from harm, whether it be physical, emotional, or spiritual.
  • Sadā naḥ: This translates to "always for us." It emphasizes the desire for continuous support and blessings. In practical terms, this can relate to the ongoing need for guidance and positivity in our lives, reminding us to seek and cultivate supportive relationships.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for strength, guidance, and prosperity from the divine. Its significance lies in the recognition of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking support from higher powers. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of challenge, during rituals, or in personal reflections, reminding us to remain open to the blessings and guidance that surround us. By invoking these qualities, we can foster a sense of empowerment and well-being in our daily lives.
