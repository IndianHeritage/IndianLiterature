स भीक्षमाणो अमृतस्य चारुण उभे dyāvā काव्येना वि शश्रथे |तेजिष्ठा अपो मंहनā परि व्यत यदी देवस्य श्रवसा सदो विदुः ||
sa bhikṣamāṇo amṛtasya cāruṇa ubhe dyāvā kāvyenā vi śaśrathe | tejiṣṭhā apo maṁhanā pari vyata yadī devasya śravasā sado viduḥ ||
  • sa bhikṣamāṇo: This phrase can be translated as "he who is seeking alms" or "the one who is in need." It signifies the importance of humility and the recognition of one's needs.
  • amṛtasya: This word means "of immortality" or "eternal." It represents the quest for eternal truth and the divine essence that transcends the physical realm.
  • cāruṇa: This term translates to "beautiful" or "charming." It emphasizes the aesthetic and spiritual beauty found in the pursuit of knowledge and truth.
  • ubhe dyāvā: This means "both the heavens." It signifies the connection between the earthly and the divine, suggesting that both realms are essential in the quest for understanding.
  • kāvyenā: This word refers to "poetry" or "art." It highlights the role of creativity and expression in the pursuit of knowledge and spirituality.
  • vi śaśrathe: This can be interpreted as "with clarity" or "with insight." It suggests that true understanding comes from a clear and insightful perspective.
  • tejiṣṭhā: This term means "the most radiant" or "the most powerful." It refers to the divine energy or light that guides individuals on their spiritual journey.
  • apo: This word translates to "waters." In many cultures, water symbolizes life, purity, and the flow of knowledge.
  • maṁhanā: This means "to nourish" or "to sustain." It signifies the importance of nurturing one's spiritual and intellectual growth.
  • pari vyata: This phrase can be interpreted as "surrounding" or "encompassing." It suggests that the divine presence is all around us, guiding and supporting us.
  • yadī devasya: This means "if of the divine." It emphasizes the connection between the individual and the divine, suggesting that understanding the divine is crucial for personal growth.
  • śravasā: This word translates to "fame" or "glory." It signifies the recognition and reverence that comes from understanding and embodying divine principles.
  • sado viduḥ: This phrase means "those who know the truth." It highlights the importance of wisdom and knowledge in the spiritual journey.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking knowledge and understanding through humility, creativity, and connection with the divine. It teaches us that the pursuit of truth is a beautiful journey that requires clarity, nourishment, and recognition of the divine presence in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in education, where students seek knowledge with humility and creativity, or in spiritual practices, where individuals strive to connect with the divine through art and insight. Ultimately, it reminds us that the quest for understanding is a holistic journey that encompasses both the material and spiritual realms.
