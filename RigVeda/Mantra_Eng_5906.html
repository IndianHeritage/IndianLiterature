स हि शुचिः शतपत्रः स शुन्ध्युर हिरण्यवाशीषिर इषिरः स्वरषाः | बृहस्पतिः स स्वावेश ऋष्वः पुरू सखिभ्य आसुतिं करिष्टः ||
sa hi śuciḥ śatapatraḥ sa śundhyur hiraṇyavāśīr iṣiraḥ svarṣāḥ | bṛhaspatiḥ sa svāveśa ṛṣvaḥ purū sakhibhya āsutiṁ kariṣṭhaḥ ||
  • sa hi śuciḥ śatapatraḥ: This phrase can be translated as "He who is pure and has a hundred wings." In a metaphorical sense, purity can refer to clarity of thought and intention. For example, a person who practices honesty and integrity in their daily life embodies this purity.
  • sa śundhyur hiraṇyavāśīr: This translates to "He who is the lord of the golden light." This can symbolize enlightenment and wisdom. In real life, this could refer to a teacher or mentor who guides others towards knowledge and understanding, much like a beacon of light in darkness.
  • iṣiraḥ svarṣāḥ: This means "the one who is powerful and has a radiant form." This can be likened to individuals who possess charisma and influence, such as leaders or public figures who inspire others through their actions and words.
  • bṛhaspatiḥ: Referring to the deity associated with wisdom and knowledge, Bṛhaspati represents the pursuit of learning and understanding. In a practical sense, this could be seen in the dedication of students and scholars who strive for academic excellence.
  • sa svāveśa ṛṣvaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "He who is the embodiment of divine inspiration." This can relate to artists, writers, and creators who channel their inner inspiration to produce works that resonate with others, such as a poet who captures the essence of human experience in their verses.
  • purū sakhibhya āsutiṁ kariṣṭhaḥ: This translates to "He who grants strength and vitality to many friends." This can be seen in the context of community leaders or supportive friends who uplift and empower those around them, fostering a sense of belonging and strength within a group.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the qualities of purity, wisdom, inspiration, and the ability to empower others. In real life, these attributes are significant in various contexts, such as leadership, education, and community building. By embodying these qualities, individuals can contribute positively to society, inspire others, and create a supportive environment that fosters growth and understanding. The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of these virtues in our daily lives and interactions with others.
