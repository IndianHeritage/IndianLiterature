न घा स माम अप जोषं जभाराभिम आस त्वक्षसा वीर्येण |ईर्मा पुरंधर अजहाद अरातीर उता वाताम् अतारच शूषुवानः ||
na ghā sa mām apa joṣaṁ jabhārābhīm āsa tvakṣasā vīryeṇa | īrmā puraṁdhir ajahād arātīr uta vātām̐ atarac chūśuvānaḥ ||
  • na ghā sa mām apa joṣaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as a plea for protection and support. The term "na" means "not," while "ghā" refers to harm or injury. "Sa mām" translates to "he (or it) does not harm me." This suggests a desire for safety and the absence of negative influences.
  • jabhārābhīm āsa tvakṣasā vīryeṇa: Here, "jabhārā" can be understood as "to bring forth" or "to manifest." "Āsa" means "hope" or "expectation." "Tvakṣasā" refers to the skin or body, and "vīryeṇa" means "with strength" or "power." This part of the mantra emphasizes the importance of manifesting strength and hope through one's physical presence and vitality.
  • īrmā puraṁdhir ajahād arātīr: "Īrmā" signifies "to be strong" or "to be powerful." "Puraṁdhir" refers to a protector or guardian. "Ajahād" means "he who does not abandon," while "arātīr" can be interpreted as "enemies" or "obstacles." This line conveys the idea of a steadfast protector who remains with us, helping us overcome adversities.
  • uta vātām̐ atarac chūśuvānaḥ: "Uta" means "and" or "also," while "vātām̐" refers to the wind or breath. "Atarac" translates to "to cross over" or "to transcend," and "chūśuvānaḥ" can be understood as "those who are nourished" or "those who thrive." This final part suggests that with the help of the divine or protective forces, we can transcend challenges and thrive in life.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message of seeking protection, strength, and the ability to overcome obstacles. In real life, this can be applicable in various scenarios such as facing personal challenges, health issues, or even during times of uncertainty. For instance, when someone is going through a difficult phase in their career or personal life, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder of the inner strength and support available to them. It emphasizes the importance of hope, resilience, and the belief that one can overcome adversities with the right mindset and support.
  • This mantra can be particularly significant in spiritual practices, meditation, or during rituals where individuals seek guidance and protection. It encourages individuals to connect with their inner strength and the protective energies around them, fostering a sense of security and empowerment.
