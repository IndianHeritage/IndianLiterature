यो नो दास आर्यो वा पुरुष्टुतादेव इन्द्र युधये चिकेटति |अस्माभिषṭे सुषहाः सन्तु शत्रवस् त्वया वयं तान वनुयाम संगमे ||
yo no dāsa āryo vā puruṣṭutādeva indra yudhaye ciketati | asmābhiṣ ṭe suṣahāḥ santu śatravas tvayā vayaṁ tān vanuyāma saṁgame ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • no: our
    • dāsa: servant or devotee
    • āryo: noble or honorable
    • vā: or
    • puruṣṭutā: praised or honored
    • deva: god or divine
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • yudhaye: for battle or conflict
    • ciketati: thinks or considers
    • asmābhiṣ: may we be blessed
    • ṭe: your
    • suṣahāḥ: well-being or prosperity
    • santu: may they be
    • śatravas: enemies
    • tvayā: by you
    • vayaṁ: we
    • tān: those
    • vanuyāma: we shall overcome
    • saṁgame: in the assembly or gathering
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine, specifically Indra, to bless and protect the devotees.
    • It emphasizes the importance of noble intentions and the need for divine support in overcoming adversities.
    • The phrase "may we be blessed" signifies a collective aspiration for prosperity and well-being.
    • It acknowledges the presence of enemies and the need for strength and strategy to overcome them.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive work environment, individuals often face challenges and rivalries. This mantra can serve as a reminder to seek divine guidance and support in navigating these challenges.
    • During times of conflict, whether personal or societal, invoking this mantra can inspire individuals to approach the situation with nobility and a focus on overcoming adversities together.
    • In sports, teams often face opponents. This mantra can be a source of motivation, encouraging players to unite and strive for victory with the belief that they are supported by a higher power.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in overcoming challenges and adversaries.
    • It teaches the importance of unity, noble intentions, and the pursuit of well-being for all.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal struggles, professional challenges, and competitive situations, reminding individuals to seek strength and support from the divine.
