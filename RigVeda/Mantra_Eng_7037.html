यो नो दातार वसूनां इन्द्रं तं हूमहे वयम् |विद्मा ह्य अस्य सुमतिं नवीयसीं गमेमा गोमती व्रजे ||
yo no dātā vasūnām indraṁ taṁ hūmahe vayam | vidmā hy asya sumatiṁ navīyasīṁ gamema gomati vraje ||
  • Translation: "We invoke that Indra, who is the giver of wealth; we know his excellent wisdom, may we attain the best of the best in our endeavors."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • yo: who
    • no: our
    • dātā: giver
    • vasūnām: of wealth
    • indraṁ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • taṁ: that
    • hūmahe: we invoke
    • vayam: we
    • vidmā: we know
    • hy: indeed
    • asya: of this
    • sumatiṁ: excellent wisdom
    • navīyasīṁ: the best of the best
    • gamema: may we attain
    • gomati: with wealth
    • vraje: in our endeavors

  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, who is often associated with rain, thunder, and the prosperity that comes from nature. In a broader sense, Indra represents the forces that bring abundance and success.
    • The invocation of Indra as the "giver of wealth" highlights the importance of seeking divine assistance in achieving prosperity. In real life, this can be seen in various cultures where people pray for blessings before starting new ventures, such as opening a business or embarking on a significant project.
    • The phrase "we know his excellent wisdom" suggests that understanding and wisdom are crucial in making decisions that lead to success. For example, in business, having a strategic plan and understanding market dynamics can be likened to invoking Indra's wisdom.
    • The desire to "attain the best of the best" reflects a universal aspiration for excellence. In personal development, this can be related to setting high goals and striving for continuous improvement, whether in education, career, or personal relationships.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking divine guidance and wisdom in our pursuits. It serves as a reminder that while we work hard towards our goals, invoking higher powers can provide us with the clarity and resources needed to achieve success.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during important life events, business launches, or personal milestones, where individuals seek blessings for prosperity and wisdom.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a holistic approach to success, combining effort, wisdom, and spiritual invocation to attain the best outcomes in life.
