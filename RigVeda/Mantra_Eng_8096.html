सम इन्द्रेणोत वायुनā सुत एति पवित्र आ |सं सूर्यस्य रश्मिभिः ||
sam indreṇota vāyunā suta eti pavitra ā | saṁ sūryasya raśmibhiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "sam indreṇota vāyunā suta eti pavitra ā | saṁ sūryasya raśmibhiḥ ||"
  • Translation: "May the son of Indra, purified by the wind, come forth; together with the rays of the Sun."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • sam: together, in harmony
    • indreṇo: of Indra, the king of gods and god of rain and thunderstorms
    • vāyunā: by the wind, representing the life force and movement
    • suta: son, offspring, or progeny
    • eti: comes forth, arrives
    • pavitra: pure, sacred, or cleansed
    • ā: a particle that can denote direction or emphasis
    • saṁ: together, in union
    • sūryasya: of the Sun, representing light, energy, and life
    • raśmibhiḥ: by the rays, indicating illumination and warmth
  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra, the god associated with strength and power, particularly in the context of rain and fertility.
    • The phrase "purified by the wind" suggests a cleansing process, indicating that the arrival of this divine energy is not only powerful but also pure and beneficial.
    • The mention of the Sun's rays symbolizes enlightenment, vitality, and the nurturing aspect of nature, which is essential for growth and sustenance.
    • In real life, this mantra can be seen as a call for harmony and balance in our lives, encouraging us to seek purity in our actions and thoughts while embracing the energy of nature.
    • For example, in a community setting, this mantra can inspire collective efforts towards environmental sustainability, where individuals come together to purify their surroundings and promote growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and purity, emphasizing the importance of divine blessings in our endeavors.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as community gatherings, environmental initiatives, and personal growth practices.
    • By invoking the energies of Indra and the Sun, individuals can seek strength, clarity, and a sense of purpose in their lives, fostering a deeper connection with nature and each other.
