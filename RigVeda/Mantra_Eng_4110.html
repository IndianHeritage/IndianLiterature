मन्त्र: स्तुहि भोजान स्तुवतो अस्य यामनि रणन गावो न यवसे |यतः पूर्वाम् इव सखींर अनु ह्वय गिरा गृणीहि कामिनः ||
stuhi bhojān stuvato asya yāmani raṇan gāvo na yavase | yataḥ pūrvām̐ iva sakhīm̐r anu hvaya girā gṛṇīhi kāminaḥ ||
  • stuhi: This word translates to "praise" or "worship." It signifies the act of expressing reverence or admiration.
  • bhojān: This means "food" or "nourishment." In a broader sense, it can refer to the sustenance that supports life.
  • stuvato: This term means "of those who praise." It indicates a collective or community aspect of worship and gratitude.
  • asya: This translates to "of this" or "of this being," referring to a divine entity or force.
  • yāmani: This word means "who is to be invoked" or "who is to be called upon," suggesting a connection to the divine through invocation.
  • raṇan: This means "to roar" or "to make a sound," often associated with the power and might of the divine.
  • gāvo: This translates to "cows," which in Vedic literature symbolize wealth, nourishment, and prosperity.
  • na: This means "not," indicating negation or absence.
  • yavase: This word means "in abundance" or "in plenty," suggesting a state of prosperity and fulfillment.
  • yataḥ: This translates to "from where" or "from which," indicating a source or origin.
  • pūrvām̐: This means "previous" or "earlier," suggesting a connection to past traditions or practices.
  • iva: This translates to "like" or "as," indicating a comparison or similarity.
  • sakhīm̐r: This means "friends" or "companions," emphasizing the importance of community and relationships.
  • anu: This translates to "after" or "following," indicating a sequence or continuation.
  • hvaya: This means "to call" or "to invoke," reinforcing the theme of invocation and connection to the divine.
  • girā: This translates to "speech" or "voice," suggesting the power of words in invoking the divine.
  • gṛṇīhi: This means "sing" or "praise," indicating an active participation in worship through song.
  • kāminaḥ: This translates to "desiring" or "longing," suggesting a yearning for connection, fulfillment, or divine blessings.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of praise, community, and the invocation of divine forces to attain nourishment and prosperity. It highlights the interconnectedness of individuals within a community and their collective relationship with the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in rituals, communal gatherings, or personal prayers where individuals come together to express gratitude and seek blessings for abundance and well-being. It serves as a reminder of the power of words and intentions in shaping our reality and fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
