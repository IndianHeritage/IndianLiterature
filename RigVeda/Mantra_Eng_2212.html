विष्वजिते धनजिते स्वर्जिते सत्राजिते नृजित उर्वराजिते | अश्वजिते गोजिते अभजिते भरेंद्राय सोमं यजताय हर्यतम ||
viśvajite dhanajite svarjite satrājite nṛjita urvarājite | aśvajite gojite abjite bharendrāya somaṁ yajatāya haryatam ||
  • viśvajite: This term can be interpreted as "one who conquers all" or "universal conqueror." It signifies the ability to overcome all obstacles and challenges in life.
  • dhanajite: This means "one who conquers wealth." It emphasizes the importance of not just acquiring wealth but mastering it and using it wisely.
  • svarjite: This translates to "one who conquers the heavens" or "achieves celestial status." It suggests striving for higher spiritual or moral achievements.
  • satrājite: This term means "one who conquers enemies." It can be understood as overcoming adversities or challenges posed by others.
  • nṛjita: This means "one who conquers men" or "master of humanity." It reflects leadership qualities and the ability to inspire and guide others.
  • urvarājite: This translates to "one who conquers the earth" or "master of the land." It signifies a connection to nature and the importance of stewardship of the earth.
  • aśvajite: This means "one who conquers horses," symbolizing mastery over resources and the ability to harness power effectively.
  • gojite: This translates to "one who conquers cattle," representing prosperity and abundance, as cattle were a symbol of wealth in ancient times.
  • abjite: This means "one who conquers water," indicating the importance of managing resources and the environment.
  • bharendrāya: This term refers to "to the great lord" or "to the supreme being," indicating a connection to divinity and the importance of spiritual guidance.
  • somaṁ: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. It represents the pursuit of higher knowledge and enlightenment.
  • yajatāya: This means "to the one who is worshipped," emphasizing the importance of devotion and reverence in life.
  • haryatam: This translates to "the most auspicious" or "the best," indicating the pursuit of excellence and positivity in all endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of mastery over various aspects of life, including wealth, nature, and personal challenges. It encourages individuals to strive for excellence, both materially and spiritually. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various fields such as leadership, entrepreneurship, and personal development. By embodying the qualities of a conqueror in different domains, one can achieve a balanced and fulfilling life, ultimately leading to a harmonious existence with oneself and the universe.
