येषामर्णो न सप्रथो नाम त्वेषं शाश्वताम एकं इद्भुजे |वयो न पितृयं सहः ||
yeṣām arṇo na sapratho nāma tveṣaṁ śaśvatām ekam id bhuje | vayo na pitryaṁ sahaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yeṣām: of whom
    • arṇo: the ocean or the vastness
    • na: not
    • sapratho: with a name or title
    • nāma: name
    • tveṣaṁ: this
    • śaśvatām: eternal or everlasting
    • ekam: one
    • id: this
    • bhuje: in the world or in existence
    • vayo: of age or life
    • na: not
    • pitryaṁ: ancestral or paternal
    • sahaḥ: together or in harmony
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nature of existence and the essence of identity.
    • It suggests that those who do not have a name or title in the vastness of the ocean (symbolizing the universe) are eternal and singular in their essence.
    • The phrase "vayo na pitryaṁ sahaḥ" indicates a separation from ancestral ties, suggesting that true identity transcends lineage and age.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, individuals often seek to define themselves beyond their family names or societal labels. For instance, artists and innovators like Steve Jobs or Frida Kahlo are recognized for their unique contributions rather than their familial backgrounds.
    • The mantra can also be related to the concept of self-actualization, where individuals strive to realize their true potential, independent of societal expectations or ancestral legacies.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of individual identity and the quest for self beyond societal and familial constraints.
    • It encourages us to explore our true selves, suggesting that our essence is eternal and not limited by our names or lineage.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, career choices, and creative expressions, where one seeks to break free from traditional molds and define their own path.
