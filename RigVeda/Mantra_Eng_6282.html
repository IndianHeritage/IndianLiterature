इमं स्तोमं अभिष्टये घृतं न पूतम् अद्रिवः |येना नु सद्य ओजसा ववक्षिथ ||
imaṁ stomam abhiṣṭaye ghṛtaṁ na pūtam adrivaḥ | yenā nu sadya ojasā vavakṣitha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imaṁ: this
    • stomam: hymn or praise
    • abhiṣṭaye: for the purpose of invocation or offering
    • ghṛtaṁ: clarified butter or ghee
    • na: not
    • pūtam: impure or contaminated
    • adrivaḥ: without obstacles or hindrances
    • yenā: by which
    • anu: following or in accordance with
    • sadya: immediately or promptly
    • ojasā: with strength or energy
    • vavakṣitha: you have manifested or revealed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer or invocation that emphasizes the purity and significance of offerings made to the divine.
    • The use of "ghṛtaṁ" (ghee) symbolizes nourishment and the essence of life, often used in rituals to invoke blessings.
    • The phrase "na pūtam adrivaḥ" suggests that the offerings should be pure and free from any impurities or obstacles, highlighting the importance of intention and purity in spiritual practices.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "yenā nu sadya ojasā vavakṣitha," indicates that through this pure offering, one can immediately receive strength and blessings from the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, offerings made during rituals are often accompanied by pure substances, such as ghee in Hindu rituals, symbolizing the offering of one's best to the divine.
    • In personal practices, individuals may create a sacred space for meditation or prayer, ensuring that the environment is clean and free from distractions, reflecting the idea of purity in offerings.
    • In a broader context, this mantra can be applied to daily life by encouraging individuals to approach their tasks and responsibilities with purity of intention and clarity, ensuring that their actions are free from negativity and obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on purity, intention, and the immediate benefits of sincere offerings.
    • It serves as a reminder that in both spiritual and everyday practices, the quality of our intentions and the purity of our actions can lead to positive outcomes and blessings.
    • By incorporating the principles of this mantra into our lives, we can cultivate a deeper connection with the divine and enhance our overall well-being.
