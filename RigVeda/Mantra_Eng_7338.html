तस्मै नूनमभिद्यवे वाचां विरूप नित्यया |वृष्णे चोदस्व सुष्टुतिम् ||
tasmai nūnam abhidyave vācā virūpa nityayā | vṛṣṇe codasva suṣṭutim ||
  • tasmai - This word translates to "to him" or "to that one." It indicates a direction of reverence or devotion towards a specific entity.
  • nūnam - This means "indeed" or "certainly," emphasizing the certainty of the statement that follows.
  • abhidyave - This term can be interpreted as "to the one who is knowledgeable" or "to the one who possesses wisdom." It signifies a being of great understanding.
  • vācā - This translates to "with words" or "through speech," indicating the importance of communication and expression.
  • virūpa - This word means "having a different form" or "unusual." It suggests the idea of diversity or uniqueness in existence.
  • nityayā - This means "eternally" or "perpetually," indicating something that is everlasting or constant.
  • vṛṣṇe - This refers to "to Vishnu" or "to the divine," highlighting the connection to a higher power or deity.
  • codasva - This translates to "inspire" or "encourage," suggesting a call to action or motivation.
  • suṣṭutim - This means "good praise" or "excellent commendation," indicating the importance of expressing gratitude and appreciation.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation directed towards a wise and divine being, asking for inspiration and the ability to express good praise. It emphasizes the significance of communication, wisdom, and the acknowledgment of the divine in our lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Education: In a learning environment, students can invoke this mantra to seek wisdom and clarity in their studies, encouraging them to express their thoughts and ideas effectively.
  • Public Speaking: Individuals preparing for speeches or presentations can use this mantra to inspire confidence and articulate their messages with clarity and purpose.
  • Creative Expression: Artists, writers, and musicians can draw upon the essence of this mantra to seek inspiration and to express their unique perspectives through their work.
  • Spiritual Practice: In a spiritual context, practitioners can recite this mantra to connect with the divine, seeking guidance and the ability to express gratitude for the wisdom received.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, communication, and the divine in our lives. It encourages us to seek inspiration and to express our thoughts and gratitude in meaningful ways, fostering a deeper connection with ourselves and the world around us.
