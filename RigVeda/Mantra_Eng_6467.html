प्रति वो वृषदञ्जयो वृष्णे शर्धाय मारुताय भरध्वम् |हव्यā वृषप्रयाव्णे ||
prati vo vṛṣadañjayo vṛṣṇe śardhāya mārutāya bharadhvam | havyā vṛṣaprayāvṇe ||
  • Mantra: "prati vo vṛṣadañjayo vṛṣṇe śardhāya mārutāya bharadhvam | havyā vṛṣaprayāvṇe ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prati: towards, in the direction of
    • vo: you (plural)
    • vṛṣadañjaya: the one who brings prosperity and strength
    • vṛṣṇe: to the Vṛṣṇi (a clan or tribe, often associated with Lord Krishna)
    • śardhāya: with devotion or faith
    • mārutāya: to the Maruts (the storm deities)
    • bharadhvam: may you carry or bring forth
    • havyā: offerings or oblations
    • vṛṣaprayāvṇe: to the one who is worthy of offerings
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine forces, particularly the Maruts, who are associated with storms and strength.
    • The phrase "prati vo" indicates a direction towards the divine, suggesting a plea or invocation.
    • By addressing "vṛṣadañjaya," the mantra emphasizes the importance of prosperity and strength, which can be interpreted as a request for abundance in life.
    • The mention of "śardhāya" highlights the need for faith and devotion in our endeavors, reminding us that success often requires a spiritual foundation.
    • Invoking the Maruts signifies a desire for dynamic energy and movement, akin to how storms can bring both destruction and renewal.
    • The concluding part, "havyā vṛṣaprayāvṇe," reinforces the idea of offering, suggesting that one should give back to the divine forces that support them.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to approach ventures with faith and a focus on ethical practices, ensuring that prosperity is achieved through righteous means.
    • In personal life, it encourages individuals to cultivate strength and resilience, especially during challenging times, much like how storms can lead to new growth.
    • In community settings, it can inspire collective efforts towards a common goal, invoking the idea that when people come together with devotion, they can achieve great things.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of devotion, strength, and the importance of giving back.
    • Its significance lies in the understanding that true prosperity is not just about material wealth but also about spiritual fulfillment and community support.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to act with integrity, faith, and a sense of responsibility towards the divine and each other.
