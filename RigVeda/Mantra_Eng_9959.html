अपामिवां सविता सावीषन् न्यग् वरीय इद अप सेधन्त्व् अद्रयः |ग्रावा यत्र मधुशूद उच्यते बृहद् आ सर्वतातिम् अदितिं वृणीमहे ||
apāmīvāṁ savitā sāviṣan nya1g varīya id apa sedhantv adrayaḥ | grāvā yatra madhuṣud ucyate bṛhad ā sarvatātim aditiṁ vṛṇīmahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apāmīvāṁ: Of the waters
    • savitā: The Sun or the creator
    • sāviṣan: Who is endowed with power
    • nya1g: In every direction
    • varīya: The most excellent
    • id: This
    • apa: Waters
    • sedhantv: May they flow
    • adrayaḥ: Without obstruction
    • grāvā: Where the sweet (honey) is spoken
    • yatra: Where
    • madhuṣud: The destroyer of sweetness
    • ucyate: Is spoken
    • bṛhad: Great
    • ā: From
    • sarvatātim: All-pervading
    • aditiṁ: Aditi, the infinite
    • vṛṇīmahe: We choose or invoke
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine essence of water and the Sun, recognizing their vital roles in sustaining life.
    • The phrase "may they flow without obstruction" emphasizes the importance of unimpeded natural resources, akin to how rivers nourish the land and support agriculture.
    • The reference to "where the sweet is spoken" can be interpreted as a call for harmony and abundance in our lives, similar to how communities thrive when they share resources and knowledge.
    • The invocation of Aditi symbolizes the infinite potential and the nurturing aspect of the universe, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, ensuring that water flows freely to crops is essential for growth, paralleling the mantra's call for unobstructed waters.
    • In community settings, fostering open communication and sharing resources can lead to a more harmonious environment, reflecting the sweetness mentioned in the mantra.
    • Environmental conservation efforts, such as protecting rivers and ecosystems, resonate with the mantra's emphasis on the importance of water and nature.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of water and the Sun as life-giving forces, urging us to respect and protect these elements.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of harmony in our communities.
    • In modern contexts, it encourages sustainable practices and the preservation of natural resources for future generations.
