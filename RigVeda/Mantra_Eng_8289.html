यदन्ति यच च दूरके भयं विन्दति मां इह |पवमान विह तज जहि ||
yad anti yac ca dūrake bhayaṁ vindati mām iha | pavamāna vi taj jahi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • anti: they may be
    • yac: whatever
    • ca: and
    • dūrake: far away
    • bhayaṁ: fear
    • vindati: finds
    • mām: me
    • iha: here
    • pavamāna: the purifying one
    • vi: away
    • taj: that
    • jahi: remove
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of overcoming fear and negativity.
    • The phrase "yad anti yac ca dūrake" suggests that whatever fears or obstacles may be present, whether they are close or far away, they can be addressed.
    • "Pavamāna" refers to a purifying force, indicating that there is a divine or spiritual energy that can help cleanse one of these fears.
    • The call to "jahi" signifies an active effort to remove these fears and obstacles from one's life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual may face fears related to public speaking. By acknowledging these fears ("yad anti") and seeking support or training ("pavamāna"), they can work to overcome them ("jahi").
    • In personal relationships, one might have fears of vulnerability. Recognizing these fears and addressing them through open communication can lead to stronger connections.
    • In the context of mental health, individuals may experience anxiety or depression. Seeking therapy or engaging in mindfulness practices can serve as a purifying force to help alleviate these feelings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and confronting fears, whether they are immediate or distant.
    • Its significance lies in the empowerment it offers, encouraging individuals to take proactive steps towards purification and healing.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, from personal growth to professional development, highlighting the universal need to address and overcome fears for a more fulfilling life.
