द्वे श्रुती अश्रिणवम् पितृणाम् अहं देवानाम् उता मार्त्यानाम् |ताभ्याम् इदं विश्वम् एजात् सम एति यद् अन्तरā पितरम् मातरं च ||
dve srutī aśṛṇavam pitṝṇām ahaṁ devānām uta martyānām | tābhyām idaṁ viśvam ejat sam eti yad antarā pitaram mātaraṁ ca ||
  • Translation: "I have heard the twofold (knowledge) of the ancestors; I am (the knowledge) of the gods and of mortals. This entire universe is energized by them, as it moves towards the father and mother within."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • dve: Twofold, indicating duality or two aspects of knowledge.
    • srutī: Hearing or knowledge, often referring to sacred knowledge or scriptures.
    • aśṛṇavam: I have heard, suggesting the act of receiving wisdom.
    • pitṝṇām: Of the ancestors, highlighting the importance of lineage and heritage.
    • ahaṁ: I am, indicating self-identity and consciousness.
    • devānām: Of the gods, representing divine knowledge and guidance.
    • uta: And, connecting the realms of the divine and the mortal.
    • martyānām: Of mortals, emphasizing human existence and experience.
    • tābhyām: By them, referring to the ancestors and gods.
    • idaṁ: This, pointing to the universe or the world around us.
    • viśvam: Universe, the totality of existence.
    • ejat: Is energized or activated, indicating movement and dynamism.
    • sam eti: Moves towards, suggesting a journey or progression.
    • yad: That which, referring to the essence of existence.
    • antarā: Within, indicating introspection or inner exploration.
    • pitaram: Father, symbolizing guidance and authority.
    • mātaraṁ: Mother, representing nurturing and care.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of duality can be seen in various aspects of life, such as the balance between work and personal life, where both are essential for overall well-being.
    • In many cultures, honoring ancestors is a common practice, reflecting the importance of lineage and the wisdom passed down through generations.
    • The idea of being energized by the universe can be related to how individuals draw inspiration from nature, art, or spirituality to fuel their creativity and motivation.
    • In family dynamics, the roles of father and mother can be seen as guiding forces that shape an individual's values and beliefs.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of knowledge, existence, and the roles of divine and ancestral influences in our lives. It serves as a reminder to honor our roots while recognizing the duality of our experiences. In real life, this can manifest in how we seek wisdom from our past, balance our responsibilities, and find inspiration in the world around us. By acknowledging both the divine and mortal aspects of our existence, we can navigate life with a deeper understanding and appreciation for the journey we undertake.
