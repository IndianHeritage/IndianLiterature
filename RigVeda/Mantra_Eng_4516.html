स हि यो मानवाः युगाः सीदत धोतā कविक्रतुः |दूतश्च हव्यवाहनः ||
sa hi yo mānuṣā yugā sīdad dhotā kavikratuḥ | dūtaś ca havyavāhanaḥ ||
  • sa hi yo mānuṣā yugā: This phrase can be translated to "Indeed, those who are human in their age." Here, "mānuṣā" refers to humans, and "yugā" signifies an era or age. This suggests that the mantra is addressing the qualities or characteristics of humanity in a particular time period.
  • sīdad dhotā kavikratuḥ: This part translates to "the one who is the mover, the wise one." "Sīdad" implies a sense of stability or grounding, while "dhotā" refers to one who stirs or moves. "Kavikratuḥ" indicates a wise or knowledgeable person, often associated with creativity and insight. This suggests that wisdom and action are essential qualities for humans to embody.
  • dūtaś ca havyavāhanaḥ: This translates to "and the messenger who carries offerings." "Dūtaś" means messenger, and "havyavāhanaḥ" refers to one who carries offerings or sacrifices. This implies the importance of communication and the act of giving in human relationships and society.

  • Real-Life Example: In modern society, the qualities of wisdom, action, and communication are vital. For instance, a leader in a community must embody these traits to effectively guide and inspire others. A wise leader (kavikratuḥ) who takes action (dhotā) can bring about positive change, while also serving as a messenger (dūtaś) who communicates the needs and aspirations of the people.
  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of human qualities such as wisdom, action, and communication. It serves as a reminder that in every era (yugā), individuals must strive to embody these traits to contribute positively to society.
  • Application in Real Life: The teachings of this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, including leadership, community service, and personal development. By cultivating wisdom, taking meaningful actions, and effectively communicating, individuals can enhance their relationships and contribute to the greater good.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of what it means to be human in any age. It calls for individuals to be wise, active, and communicative, highlighting the interconnectedness of these qualities in fostering a harmonious society. By embodying these principles, we can navigate the complexities of life and contribute to a better world.
