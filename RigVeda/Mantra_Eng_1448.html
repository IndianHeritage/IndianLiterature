अस्माकं ब्रह्मोतये 'वापृच्छुṣu कासु चित् |न हि त्वा शत्रुः स्तारते स्तृणोषि यं विश्वं शत्रुं स्तृणोषि यम् ||
asmākam brahmotaye 'vā pṛtsuṣu kāsu cit | nahi tvā śatruḥ starate stṛṇoṣi yaṁ viśvaṁ śatruṁ stṛṇoṣi yam ||
  • Translation: "In our pursuit of the ultimate reality, we do not consider you as an enemy; you who protect us from all adversities, you who shield us from every foe."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • asmākam: Our
    • brahmotaye: In the pursuit of the ultimate reality or Brahman
    • vā: Or
    • pṛtsuṣu: In the realms of existence
    • kāsu: In whom
    • cit: Consciousness or awareness
    • nahi: Not
    • tvā: You
    • śatruḥ: Enemy
    • starate: To be considered or regarded
    • stṛṇoṣi: You protect or shield
    • yaṁ: Whom
    • viśvaṁ: All or every
    • śatruṁ: Foe or adversary
    • stṛṇoṣi: You shield or protect

  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the idea of unity and protection in the quest for spiritual truth.
    • It suggests that in the journey towards understanding the ultimate reality (Brahman), one should not view others as enemies, but rather as protectors or allies.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in conflict resolution, where seeing others as adversaries can hinder progress. Instead, recognizing that everyone is on their own journey can foster cooperation and understanding.
    • For example, in a workplace setting, rather than viewing a colleague as a competitor, one can see them as a partner in achieving common goals, leading to a more harmonious and productive environment.

  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for compassion and understanding in the face of challenges.
    • It teaches us to transcend the notion of enmity and to embrace a perspective of unity, which is essential in both personal and communal growth.
    • In a world often divided by conflict, this mantra serves as a reminder that we can find strength in togetherness and that true protection comes from understanding and supporting one another.
