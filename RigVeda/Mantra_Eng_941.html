ते 'वर्धन्त स्वतवसो महित्वना नाकं तस्थुर उरु चक्रिरे सदः |विष्णुर यद धावद वृषणं मदाच्युतं वयो न सीदन्न अधि बरहिषि प्रिये ||
te 'vardhanta svatavaso mahitvanā nākaṁ tasthur uru cakrire sadaḥ | viṣṇur yad dhāvad vṛṣaṇam madacyutaṁ vayo na sīdann adhi barhiṣi priye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • te: they
    • vardhanta: who are growing or increasing
    • svatavaso: in their own power or strength
    • mahitvanā: with greatness or majesty
    • nākaṁ: the sky or heaven
    • tasthur: they have established
    • uru: wide or vast
    • cakrire: they have created or formed
    • sadaḥ: always or eternally
    • viṣṇur: Vishnu, the preserver
    • yad: who
    • dhāvad: runs or moves
    • vṛṣaṇam: the bull or strength
    • madacyutaṁ: intoxicated with joy or bliss
    • vayo: our age or time
    • na: not
    • sīdann: falling or declining
    • adhi: upon or over
    • barhiṣi: on the sacred grass or altar
    • priye: dear or beloved
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine forces that are ever-increasing in their power and majesty.
    • It highlights the establishment of a vast and eternal presence in the heavens, symbolizing the omnipresence of divinity.
    • Vishnu, representing preservation and sustenance, is depicted as moving with strength and joy, ensuring that the cycle of life continues without decline.
    • The reference to "barhiṣi" indicates a connection to rituals and sacred practices, emphasizing the importance of spirituality in daily life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal growth, individuals often experience a sense of increasing strength and capability, akin to the "vardhanta" aspect of the mantra.
    • In community building, the establishment of supportive networks can be seen as creating a "vast" space where everyone thrives together.
    • In environmental conservation, the idea of preserving nature reflects Vishnu's role in maintaining balance and harmony in the world.
    • During rituals or significant life events, the invocation of divine presence can be likened to the "barhiṣi" aspect, where sacredness is acknowledged and celebrated.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and nurturing the divine forces within and around us.
    • It teaches us to embrace growth, strength, and joy while maintaining a connection to our spiritual practices.
    • In real life, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide us in personal development, community engagement, and environmental stewardship.
    • Ultimately, it reminds us that through our actions and intentions, we can create a lasting impact that resonates with the divine essence of existence.
