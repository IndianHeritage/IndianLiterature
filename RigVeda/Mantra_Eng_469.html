अच्छा वद तना गिरा जरायै ब्रह्मणस पतिम् |अग्निम् मित्रं न दर्शतम् ||
acchā vadā tanā girā jarāyai brahmaṇas patim | agnim mitraṁ na darśatam ||
  • acchā vadā: This phrase translates to "speak well" or "speak positively." It emphasizes the importance of communication and the impact of words on our lives.
  • tanā girā: This means "with a pure voice" or "with a clear expression." It suggests that clarity and purity in speech are essential for effective communication.
  • jarāyai: This word refers to "old age" or "decay." It signifies the transient nature of life and the inevitability of aging.
  • brahmaṇas patim: This translates to "the lord of the Brahman" or "the supreme being." It indicates a connection to the divine or higher consciousness.
  • agnim: This word means "fire" and symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. Fire is often seen as a sacred element in many cultures.
  • mitraṁ: This translates to "friend" or "ally." It highlights the importance of friendship and support in our lives.
  • na darśatam: This means "not to be seen" or "not to be revealed." It suggests that some truths or aspects of life may remain hidden or beyond our immediate perception.

The mantra emphasizes the significance of positive and clear communication, the acceptance of life's transience, and the connection to the divine. It encourages individuals to speak with intention and purity, recognizing the power of words in shaping our reality. The mention of fire symbolizes the transformative power of our actions and relationships, while the concept of friendship underscores the importance of support and connection in navigating life's challenges.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • In personal relationships, practicing clear and positive communication can strengthen bonds and foster understanding.
  • In professional settings, effective communication can lead to better teamwork and collaboration, enhancing productivity.
  • In spiritual practices, recognizing the transient nature of life can encourage mindfulness and appreciation for the present moment.
  • In community building, fostering friendships and alliances can create a supportive environment for growth and development.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of words, the importance of clarity in communication, and the value of relationships. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate life's complexities with grace and purpose, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
