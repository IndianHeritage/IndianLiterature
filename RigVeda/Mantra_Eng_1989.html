सं यं स्तुभो 'वनयो न यन्ति समुद्रं न श्रवतो रोधचक्राः |स विद्वां उभयं चष्टे अन्तर बृहस्पतिस तारा आपश च गृध्रः ||
saṁ yaṁ stubho 'vanayo na yanti samudraṁ na sravato rodhacakrāḥ | sa vidvām̐ ubhayaṁ caṣṭe antar bṛhaspatis tara āpaś ca gṛdhraḥ ||
  • saṁ yaṁ stubho 'vanayo na yanti samudraṁ na sravato rodhacakrāḥ
  • sa vidvām̐ ubhayaṁ caṣṭe antar bṛhaspatis tara āpaś ca gṛdhraḥ

  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • saṁ - together, in harmony
    • yaṁ - who, that
    • stubho - praised, glorified
    • 'avanayo - the one who does not fall
    • na - not
    • yanti - go, move
    • samudraṁ - ocean, vastness
    • na - not
    • sravato - flowing, streaming
    • rodhacakrāḥ - the wheel of obstruction
    • sa - he, that
    • vidvām̐ - the wise one
    • ubhayaṁ - both, dual
    • caṣṭe - moves, acts
    • antar - within, inside
    • bṛhaspatis - the great teacher, Jupiter
    • tara - beyond, surpassing
    • āpaś - waters, streams
    • ca - and
    • gṛdhraḥ - the vulture, a bird of prey

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of a harmonious existence where the wise one, represented by Bṛhaspati, navigates through the challenges of life.
    • The phrase "saṁ yaṁ stubho 'vanayo" suggests that those who are praised and revered do not succumb to the overwhelming forces of nature, symbolized by the ocean and the wheel of obstruction.
    • In real life, this can be likened to individuals who, despite facing significant challenges (like the vastness of the ocean), maintain their composure and wisdom, much like leaders or mentors who guide others through turbulent times.
    • The second part of the mantra emphasizes the duality of existence, where the wise one acts within the complexities of life, represented by the interplay of water and the vulture, which symbolizes the ability to rise above challenges.
    • For example, a teacher who inspires students to overcome their fears and obstacles can be seen as embodying the qualities of Bṛhaspati, guiding them to navigate their own 'oceans' of challenges.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of wisdom and resilience in the face of life's challenges.
    • It teaches us that by embracing knowledge and maintaining harmony, we can navigate through the complexities of existence without being overwhelmed.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, education, and personal development, where wisdom and guidance are essential for overcoming obstacles.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and act wisely, ensuring that they remain steadfast and unyielding, much like the wise one who transcends the tumultuous waters of life.
