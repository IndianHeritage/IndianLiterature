वायं इद अग्ने अग्नयस् ते अन्ये त्वे विश्वे अमृताः मादयन्ते |वैश्वानर नाभिर असि क्षितीनां स्थूणेव जनां उपमिद ययन्त ||
vayā id agne agnayas te anye tve viśve amṛtā mādayante | vaiśvānara nābhir asi kṣitīnāṁ sthūṇeva janām̐ upamid yayantha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vayā: O, the one who is invoked
    • id: this
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • agnayas: of the fires
    • te: your
    • anye: others
    • tve: you
    • viśve: all
    • amṛtā: immortal
    • mādayante: delight
    • vaiśvānara: universal
    • nābhir: center
    • asi: you are
    • kṣitīnāṁ: of the earth
    • sthūṇeva: like a great mass
    • janām̐: people
    • upamid: who are nourished
    • yayantha: who are led
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Agni, the fire god, acknowledging his role as a source of energy and life.
    • It emphasizes the connection between Agni and the various forms of fire, suggesting that all fires are manifestations of the divine.
    • The phrase "viśve amṛtā mādayante" indicates that all beings are delighted by the presence of this divine energy, which is essential for life.
    • Agni is described as "vaiśvānara," representing the universal aspect of fire that connects all living beings.
    • The metaphor of "sthūṇeva janām̐" likens Agni to a great mass that supports and nourishes people, highlighting the importance of fire in human existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, fire is essential for cooking, heating, and energy production, symbolizing the nurturing aspect of Agni.
    • In many cultures, fire rituals are performed to honor the divine, reflecting the reverence for Agni as a life-giving force.
    • Modern science recognizes the importance of energy (like fire) in sustaining life, paralleling the ancient understanding of Agni.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the vital role of Agni in the universe and human life, representing energy, transformation, and nourishment.
    • It teaches us to respect and honor the natural elements that sustain us, reminding us of our interconnectedness with all beings.
    • In contemporary life, this mantra can inspire sustainable practices and a deeper appreciation for the resources we often take for granted.
