सं यन् मदाय शुष्मिण एना ह्य अस्योदरे |समुद्रो न व्यचो दधे ||
saṁ yan madāya śuṣmiṇa enā hy asyodare | samudro na vyaco dadhe ||
  • saṁ yan: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which is connected" or "that which is united." It signifies the importance of unity and interconnectedness in the universe.
  • madāya: This word translates to "for the sake of joy" or "for the purpose of delight." It emphasizes the pursuit of happiness and fulfillment in life.
  • śuṣmiṇa: This term refers to "the one who is radiant" or "the one who shines." It symbolizes enlightenment, knowledge, and the pursuit of wisdom.
  • enā: This word means "this" or "here." It indicates a focus on the present moment and the immediate reality.
  • hy: This is an emphatic particle, often translated as "indeed" or "certainly," reinforcing the importance of the preceding concepts.
  • asyodare: This term can be understood as "in the womb of this" or "within this." It suggests the idea of potential and the nurturing aspect of existence.
  • samudro: This word means "ocean" or "sea." It represents vastness, depth, and the collective consciousness of humanity.
  • na vyaco: This phrase translates to "does not speak" or "does not express." It implies the idea of silence or the unexpressed potential within.
  • dadhe: This term means "to hold" or "to contain." It signifies the capacity to embrace and nurture the unexpressed potential.

In summary, this mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of all beings and the pursuit of joy and enlightenment. It highlights the importance of recognizing the potential within ourselves and the universe, much like the vast ocean that holds countless mysteries beneath its surface. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as:


  • In personal relationships, fostering unity and joy can lead to deeper connections and understanding.
  • In educational settings, the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment can inspire individuals to reach their full potential.
  • In community building, recognizing the collective potential and nurturing it can lead to a more harmonious society.

Ultimately, the significance of this mantra lies in its reminder to seek joy, embrace unity, and recognize the potential within ourselves and the world around us. It encourages us to cultivate a deeper understanding of our interconnectedness and the importance of nurturing our inner light.
