शं नो भव चक्षसा शं नो अह्ना शम् भानुना शं हिमाः शं घृणेन |यथा शम् अध्वञ् चम असद दुरोणे तात सूर्य द्रविणं धेहि चित्रम् ||
śaṁ no bhava cakṣasā śaṁ no ahnā śam bhānunā śaṁ himā śaṁ ghṛṇena | yathā śam adhvañ cham asad duroṇe tat sūrya draviṇaṁ dhehi citram ||
  • śaṁ no bhava cakṣasā: This phrase translates to "May we be blessed with auspiciousness through vision." It emphasizes the importance of perception and insight in our lives. For example, in a business context, having a clear vision can lead to successful decision-making and strategic planning.
  • śaṁ no ahnā: This means "May we be blessed with auspiciousness through our actions." It highlights the significance of our deeds and their impact on our lives. In real life, this can be seen in how acts of kindness and integrity can lead to positive outcomes and relationships.
  • śam bhānunā: This translates to "May we be blessed with auspiciousness through the light." Light symbolizes knowledge and enlightenment. For instance, education and learning can illuminate our paths, guiding us toward better choices and opportunities.
  • śaṁ himā: This means "May we be blessed with auspiciousness through the cold." Cold can represent challenges or hardships. Overcoming difficulties can lead to personal growth and resilience, much like how athletes train in adverse conditions to build strength.
  • śaṁ ghṛṇena: This translates to "May we be blessed with auspiciousness through compassion." Compassion is vital in fostering connections and understanding among people. In everyday life, showing empathy can strengthen relationships and create a supportive community.
  • yathā śam adhvañ: This means "As we seek auspiciousness on our paths." It signifies the journey of life and the pursuit of positivity. In real life, setting goals and striving for personal development can lead to a fulfilling life journey.
  • cham asad duroṇe: This translates to "May we be free from obstacles." It reflects the desire to overcome challenges that hinder progress. For example, in a career, navigating through obstacles can lead to promotions and advancements.
  • tat sūrya draviṇaṁ dhehi citram: This means "O Sun, grant us your radiant wealth." The Sun symbolizes energy, vitality, and abundance. In practical terms, this can relate to seeking opportunities that bring prosperity and well-being into our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the importance of vision, action, knowledge, resilience, compassion, and the pursuit of auspiciousness. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, from personal growth to professional success. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate their paths with clarity and purpose, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and prosperous existence.
