अर्वाण्चं त्वा सुखे रथे वहताम इन्द्र केशिना |घृतस्नू बरहिर आसदे ||
arvāñcaṁ tvā sukhe rathe vahatām indra keśinā | ghṛtasnū barhir āsade ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • arvāñcaṁ: The one who is swift or quick.
    • tvā: You (referring to Indra).
    • sukhe: In happiness or joy.
    • rathe: In the chariot.
    • vahatām: Those who carry or transport.
    • indra: The king of gods, associated with strength and power.
    • keśinā: With hair or flowing hair, often symbolizing vitality.
    • ghṛtasnū: One who is nourished by ghee (clarified butter), symbolizing richness and abundance.
    • barhir: The seat or the place, often referring to a sacred space.
    • āsade: To sit or to dwell.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who is often seen as a protector and a provider of abundance.
    • The phrase "arvāñcaṁ tvā sukhe rathe vahatām" suggests a desire for Indra to be carried in a joyful chariot, symbolizing the journey of life filled with happiness and prosperity.
    • The mention of "ghṛtasnū" indicates a connection to nourishment and richness, emphasizing the importance of sustenance in achieving happiness.
    • Overall, the mantra reflects a plea for divine support in leading a fulfilling and prosperous life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a wedding ceremony, couples often seek blessings for a joyful and prosperous life together, similar to invoking Indra's presence in a chariot of happiness.
    • During harvest festivals, communities celebrate abundance and seek divine blessings for continued prosperity, resonating with the themes of nourishment and joy in this mantra.
    • In personal development, individuals may meditate on this mantra to attract positivity and abundance in their lives, aligning their goals with the divine energy of Indra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine support in achieving happiness and abundance in life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as celebrations, personal growth, and community gatherings, where the focus is on joy, prosperity, and nourishment.
    • By invoking the energy of Indra, individuals and communities can align themselves with the forces of nature that promote well-being and fulfillment.
