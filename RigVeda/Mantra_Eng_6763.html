हंसाव इव पतथो अध्वगाव इव सोमं सुतं महिषेवावा गच्छथः |सजोषसां उषसा सूर्येण च त्रिर वर्तिर यातम अश्विनाः ||
haṁsāv iva patatho adhvagāv iva somaṁ sutam mahiṣevāva gacchathaḥ | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa ca trir vartir yātam aśvinā ||
  • haṁsāv iva patatho: This phrase translates to "like swans in flight." Swans are often seen as symbols of grace and beauty. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of excellence and the importance of striving for a higher purpose, much like how swans glide effortlessly across water.
  • adhvagāv iva somaṁ sutam: This means "like birds, the soma is poured." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing vitality and immortality. In contemporary life, this can be likened to the nourishment we seek, whether it be physical, emotional, or spiritual, emphasizing the importance of what we consume and how it affects our well-being.
  • mahiṣevāva gacchathaḥ: This translates to "like the great ones, may you proceed." This can be interpreted as a call to follow the path of great leaders or enlightened beings. In real life, this encourages individuals to seek mentorship and guidance from those who have achieved success or wisdom, fostering personal growth and development.
  • sajoṣasā uṣasā: This means "with the dawn, with the light." Dawn symbolizes new beginnings and opportunities. In everyday life, this can be seen as a reminder to embrace each day with optimism and the potential for renewal, encouraging individuals to start fresh and pursue their goals with vigor.
  • sūryeṇa ca trir vartir: This translates to "with the sun, three times may you thrive." The sun is a powerful symbol of energy and life. This phrase can be related to the idea of harnessing energy and positivity in our lives, suggesting that by aligning ourselves with positive forces, we can achieve success and prosperity.
  • yātam aśvinā: This means "may you be guided by the Ashvins." The Ashvins are twin deities associated with health and medicine. In a practical sense, this can be interpreted as a call to seek healing and balance in our lives, whether through physical health, mental well-being, or spiritual practices.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of grace, nourishment, guidance, renewal, energy, and healing. Its significance lies in the encouragement to pursue excellence, seek nourishment for the body and soul, follow the paths of great leaders, embrace new beginnings, harness positive energy, and seek guidance for health and well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra serves as a reminder to live purposefully, cultivate positivity, and strive for personal growth, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal development to health and wellness practices.
