परशुं चिद् वि तपति शिम्बलं चिद् वि वृश्चति |उखां चिद् इन्द्र येषन्ती प्रयस्तां फेनम् अस्यति ||
paraśuṁ cid vi tapati śimbalaṁ cid vi vṛścati | ukhā cid indra yeṣantī prayastā phenam asyati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • paraśuṁ: axe or tool
    • cid: indeed or certainly
    • vi: apart or separately
    • tapati: burns or shines
    • śimbalaṁ: a type of tree or plant, often associated with strength
    • vṛścati: grows or flourishes
    • ukhā: oven or hearth
    • indra: a deity, often representing power and strength
    • yeṣantī: those who are present or existing
    • prayastā: striving or making an effort
    • phenam: foam or froth
    • asyati: produces or creates
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the transformative power of tools and effort.
    • The "axe" symbolizes the ability to cut through obstacles, representing determination and strength.
    • The "śimbalaṁ" tree signifies resilience and growth, indicating that with the right tools and effort, one can flourish.
    • The "oven" represents the process of transformation, where raw potential is refined into something valuable.
    • Indra, as a powerful deity, embodies the strength and support one can draw upon in times of need.
    • The mention of "foam" suggests the results of hard work, which may appear ephemeral but are a product of significant effort.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, using the right tools (like software or methodologies) can help individuals overcome challenges and achieve success.
    • In personal development, the effort one puts into learning and self-improvement can lead to significant growth, much like a tree flourishing with care.
    • In relationships, the "oven" can symbolize the nurturing environment needed for love and understanding to grow, requiring effort from both parties.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of tools, effort, and the transformative processes in life.
    • It teaches that with determination and the right resources, one can overcome obstacles and achieve growth.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in work, personal growth, or relationships, the principles of effort and transformation are universally applicable.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that success is not merely a product of desire but of the tools we wield and the effort we invest.
