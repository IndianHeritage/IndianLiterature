रुशद्वत्सा रुशती श्वेत्यागाद आरिगु कृष्णा सदनान्य अस्याः |समानबन्धू अमृत अनूची dyाव वरṇं चरत आमिनाने ||
ruśadvatsā ruśatī śvetyāgād āraig u kṛṣṇā sadanāny asyāḥ | samānabandhū amṛte anūcī dyāvā varṇaṁ carata āmināne ||
  • ruśadvatsā: This term can be broken down into "ruśa" meaning "to be angry" or "to be fierce" and "dvatsā" which can imply "having a quality." Thus, it refers to a fierce or powerful entity.
  • ruśatī: This word suggests a state of being fierce or intense, often associated with strength or vigor.
  • śvetyāgād: "śvetya" means "white" or "pure," and "gād" can imply "to go" or "to move." Together, it can signify a movement towards purity or enlightenment.
  • āraig: This term can be interpreted as "to be in a state of harmony" or "to be in alignment," suggesting a balance in existence.
  • u kṛṣṇā: "kṛṣṇā" refers to the color black or dark, often symbolizing the unknown or the mysteries of life. The conjunction "u" can mean "and" or "also," indicating a duality.
  • sadanāny asyāḥ: "sadanāny" refers to "abodes" or "homes," and "asyāḥ" means "of this." This phrase can be interpreted as the various homes or realms of existence.
  • samānabandhū: This term means "having similar connections" or "relatives," indicating a sense of unity or kinship among beings.
  • amṛte: This word translates to "immortal" or "eternal," suggesting a connection to the divine or the everlasting nature of the soul.
  • anūcī: This term can mean "to speak" or "to express," often in the context of sharing wisdom or knowledge.
  • dyāvā: This word refers to "heaven" or "the sky," symbolizing the higher realms of existence.
  • varṇaṁ: This term means "color" or "variety," often used to denote diversity in creation.
  • carata: This word means "to move" or "to flow," indicating the dynamic nature of life.
  • āmināne: This term can be interpreted as "to be present" or "to exist," emphasizing the importance of existence in the present moment.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of duality, unity, and the pursuit of purity in existence. It highlights the importance of recognizing the fierce and gentle aspects of life, the connection between beings, and the eternal nature of the soul. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal relationships, understanding the balance between strength and gentleness can lead to healthier interactions.
  • In spiritual practices, the pursuit of purity and enlightenment can guide individuals towards a more meaningful existence.
  • In community building, recognizing the kinship among diverse individuals can foster unity and cooperation.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of striving for harmony in our lives.
