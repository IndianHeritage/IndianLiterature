यत् पुरुषं व्यादधुः कति धा व्यकल्पयन् |मुखं किमस्य कौ बाहू का ऊरू पादाः उच्यते ||
yat puruṣaṁ vy adadhuḥ katidhā vy akalpayan | mukhaṁ kim asya kau bāhū kā ūrū pādā ucyete ||
  • Translation: "What is that Purusha (Cosmic Being) that they created? How many forms did they assign to him? What is his face? What are his arms? What are his thighs and feet?"

  • Meaning of Key Terms:
    • Purusha: Refers to the cosmic being or the universal spirit, representing the essence of all existence.
    • Adadhuḥ: Means 'they created' or 'they fashioned', indicating the act of creation.
    • Kalpayan: Refers to 'assigning forms' or 'shaping', suggesting the diversity of existence.
    • Mukhāṁ: Translates to 'face', symbolizing the aspect of identity or perception.
    • Bāhū: Means 'arms', representing action and capability.
    • Ūrū: Refers to 'thighs', indicating strength and support.
    • Pādā: Means 'feet', symbolizing stability and grounding.

  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra reflects the philosophical inquiry into the nature of existence and the universe. It poses fundamental questions about the creation and the various forms that the cosmic being takes.
    • In real life, this can be related to the diversity of cultures, beliefs, and identities that exist in the world. Just as the Purusha is multifaceted, so too are human experiences and expressions.
    • For example, in a multicultural society, individuals from different backgrounds contribute unique perspectives, much like the different forms assigned to the Purusha.

  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of recognizing the divine in every aspect of life.
    • It encourages individuals to explore their own identities and roles within the larger tapestry of existence, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various fields such as community building, conflict resolution, and personal development, where understanding and appreciating diversity leads to harmony.
