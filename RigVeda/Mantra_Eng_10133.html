या आद्राय चकमानाय पित्वोऽन्नवान सन् रफितायोपजग्मुषे |स्थिरं मनः कृतुते सेवते पुरतो चित स मर्दितारं न विन्दते ||
ya ādhrāya cakamānāya pitvo 'nnavān san raphitāyopajagmuṣe | sthiram manaḥ kṛṇute sevate puroto cit sa marḍitāraṁ na vindate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who
    • ādhrāya: by supporting
    • cakamānāya: for the one who is striving
    • pitvo: by the father
    • annavān: who has food
    • san: being
    • raphitāyopajagmuṣe: who is engaged in the act of nourishment
    • sthiram: stable
    • manaḥ: mind
    • kṛṇute: creates
    • sevate: serves
    • puroto: in front
    • cit: consciousness
    • sa: that
    • marḍitāraṁ: which is well-formed
    • na: not
    • vindate: finds
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the importance of support and nourishment in our lives.
    • It emphasizes the role of a father or parental figure in providing sustenance and stability.
    • The striving individual is encouraged to maintain a stable mind and serve with consciousness.
    • It suggests that without a stable foundation and support, one may struggle to find fulfillment or success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, a supportive parent can provide the necessary resources and emotional backing for their children to thrive.
    • In a workplace, a mentor can guide a novice employee, helping them to develop a stable mindset and skills necessary for success.
    • In community settings, organizations that provide support and resources can help individuals achieve their goals and find stability.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of support systems in our lives, whether they be familial, social, or professional.
    • It teaches us that a stable mind and conscious service are essential for personal growth and fulfillment.
    • In real-life scenarios, recognizing and nurturing these support systems can lead to greater success and well-being.
