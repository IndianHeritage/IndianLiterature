विदा चिन नु महान्तो ये व एव ब्रवाम दसमां वारणं दधानाः |वयश चन सुभ्व1 आव यन्ति क्षुभा मर्तं अनुयतं वधस्नैः ||
vidā cin nu mahānto ye va evā bravāma dasmā vāryaṁ dadhānāḥ | vayaś cana subhva1 āva yanti kṣubhā martam anuyataṁ vadhasnaiḥ ||
  • vidā cin nu mahānto: This phrase can be translated as "those who are wise and great." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and greatness in individuals.
  • ye va evā bravāma: This means "who indeed speak the truth." It highlights the value of truthfulness and integrity in communication.
  • dasmā vāryaṁ dadhānāḥ: This translates to "they who hold the power." It suggests that those who possess wisdom and truth also hold a certain power or influence in society.
  • vayaś cana subhva1: This can be interpreted as "we who are of good fortune." It indicates a sense of community and shared prosperity among those who embody these virtues.
  • āva yanti kṣubhā: This means "they go towards the good." It signifies the direction of their actions and intentions towards positive outcomes.
  • martam anuyataṁ vadhasnaiḥ: This translates to "following the path of mortals." It reflects the idea that even those who are wise and powerful must navigate the human experience and its challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom, truthfulness, and the power that comes from these virtues. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as leadership, where a wise leader who speaks the truth can inspire and guide their community towards prosperity. It also highlights the importance of collective well-being, as those who embody these qualities contribute positively to society. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that wisdom and integrity are essential for navigating life's challenges and achieving a harmonious existence.
