इन्द्रं प्रातर् हवामहे इन्द्रं प्रयत्य अध्वरे |इन्द्रं सोमस्य पीतये ||
indram prātar havāmaha indram prayaty adhvare | indraṁ somasya pītaye ||
  • Indram: This refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. Indra symbolizes power and leadership.
  • Prātar: This means "in the morning." It signifies the beginning of a new day, a time for new opportunities and fresh starts.
  • Havāmaha: This translates to "we invoke" or "we offer." It indicates a call to the divine, a ritualistic offering made with reverence.
  • Prayaty: This means "to strive" or "to exert effort." It emphasizes the importance of action and determination in achieving one's goals.
  • Adhvare: This refers to a ritual or sacrifice. It highlights the significance of rituals in connecting with the divine and seeking blessings.
  • Somasya: This relates to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine qualities. It symbolizes nourishment and spiritual elevation.
  • Pītaye: This means "for drinking" or "to be consumed." It signifies the act of receiving blessings and the importance of sharing divine gifts.

The mantra can be understood as a morning invocation to Indra, seeking his blessings and strength as one embarks on the day's endeavors. It emphasizes the importance of rituals and the effort one must put forth to achieve success. Just as Indra is called upon for his power, individuals are encouraged to invoke their inner strength and strive towards their goals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Morning Routines: Just as the mantra is recited in the morning, individuals can start their day with positive affirmations or rituals that set a tone of strength and determination.
  • Goal Setting: The emphasis on striving (prayaty) can inspire individuals to set clear goals and work diligently towards achieving them, much like a ritualistic offering to the divine.
  • Community and Sharing: The reference to soma and sharing blessings can be reflected in community service or collaborative efforts, where individuals come together to support one another.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of invocation, the importance of effort, and the significance of rituals in our lives. It encourages individuals to seek strength from the divine while actively working towards their aspirations, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
