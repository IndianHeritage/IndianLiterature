यद ऊवध्यम उदरस्यापवाति य आमस्य क्रविषो गन्धो अस्ति |सुकृतā तच चमितारः कृत्वन्तूत मेधं श्रितपाकं पचन्तु ||
yad ūvadhyam udarasyāpavāti ya āmasya kraviṣo gandho asti | sukṛtā tac chamitāraḥ kṛṇvantūta medhaṁ śṛtapākam pacantu ||
  • yad ūvadhyam udarasyāpavāti: This phrase can be translated as "that which is offered from the belly." In a metaphorical sense, it refers to the essence or the core of something that is being shared or given. In real life, this can be likened to the act of sharing one's knowledge or resources with others, much like a parent providing for their child.
  • ya āmasya kraviṣo gandho asti: This translates to "that which has the fragrance of the offerings." Here, the 'fragrance' symbolizes the positive outcomes or the good deeds that arise from our actions. For example, when someone performs a charitable act, the 'fragrance' of their kindness can inspire others to do the same, creating a ripple effect of goodwill in the community.
  • sukṛtā tac chamitāraḥ kṛṇvantūta: This part means "may those who are virtuous create that." It emphasizes the importance of virtuous actions and the role of good people in manifesting positive outcomes. In everyday life, this can be seen in community service initiatives where individuals come together to improve their surroundings, showcasing the power of collective good.
  • medhaṁ śṛtapākam pacantu: This translates to "may they cook the well-prepared food." Here, 'cooking' symbolizes the process of nurturing and developing ideas or actions that are beneficial. In a practical sense, this can relate to the way we cultivate our skills and knowledge to contribute positively to society, much like a chef perfecting a recipe to create a delightful dish.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of sharing, virtuous actions, and the nurturing of positive outcomes in our lives. It teaches us that our actions, much like offerings, can have a profound impact on ourselves and others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, education, and personal relationships, where the essence of giving, kindness, and nurturing leads to a harmonious and prosperous society.
