त्वं अग्नि शशमानाय सुन्वते रत्नं यविष्ट देवतातिम इन्बसि | तं त्वा नु नव्यं सहसो युवन वयं भगं न कारे महिरत्न धीमहि ||
tvam agne śaśamānāya sunvate ratnaṁ yaviṣṭha devatātim invasi | taṁ tvā nu navyaṁ sahaso yuvan vayam bhagaṁ na kāre mahiratna dhīmahi ||
  • tvam agne: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni." Agni is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, light, and transformation. In real life, fire represents both physical and metaphorical energy, such as passion and creativity.
  • śaśamānāya: This means "to the one who is ever shining." It signifies the eternal and unchanging nature of Agni, akin to the sun that provides light and warmth, essential for life.
  • sunvate: This word means "to those who are auspicious." It emphasizes the positive and beneficial aspects of Agni, suggesting that invoking Agni brings good fortune and prosperity.
  • ratnaṁ yaviṣṭha: This translates to "the most excellent jewel." Here, "ratna" symbolizes wealth, knowledge, or valuable insights. In life, this can refer to the pursuit of wisdom and the treasures of experience.
  • devatātim: This means "the supreme deity." It highlights Agni's divine status, suggesting that the energy and transformation he represents are essential for spiritual growth.
  • invasi: This translates to "you enter." It indicates the presence of Agni in our lives, suggesting that we should invite this transformative energy into our daily existence.
  • taṁ tvā nu navyaṁ: This means "we invoke you anew." It signifies a fresh approach to spirituality and life, encouraging individuals to seek renewal and rejuvenation.
  • sahaso yuvan: This translates to "with strength and youth." It emphasizes the importance of vitality and vigor in pursuing our goals and aspirations.
  • vayam bhagaṁ: This means "we seek blessings." It reflects the human desire for divine support and guidance in our endeavors.
  • na kāre: This translates to "not in vain." It suggests that our efforts should be meaningful and purposeful, resonating with the idea that hard work leads to fruitful outcomes.
  • mahiratna dhīmahi: This means "we meditate on the great jewel." It signifies the importance of contemplation and reflection on the valuable aspects of life, such as wisdom, love, and compassion.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a transformative force in our lives. It encourages individuals to seek blessings, pursue wisdom, and invite positive energy into their daily existence. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied by fostering a mindset of growth, embracing change, and recognizing the value of spiritual and material wealth. By meditating on the qualities of Agni, we can cultivate a life filled with purpose, vitality, and meaningful connections.
