इयम् मनीषा बृहती बृहन्तोऽरुक्रमाऽ तवसा वर्धयन्ती |रेरे वां स्तोमं विदथेषु विष्णो पिन्वतम इषो वृजनेष्व इन्द्र ||
iyam manīṣā bṛhatī bṛhantorukramā tavasā vardhayantī | rare vāṁ stomaṁ vidatheṣu viṣṇo pinvatam iṣo vṛjaneṣv indra ||
  • iyam manīṣā: This phrase can be translated as "this thought" or "this intention." It signifies the power of thought and intention in shaping our reality.
  • bṛhatī: Meaning "great" or "vast," this word emphasizes the expansive nature of the thought being referred to.
  • bṛhantorukramā: This can be interpreted as "the one who is great and powerful." It suggests a divine or higher power that is involved in the manifestation of thoughts.
  • tavasā: This word means "by your strength" or "through your power," indicating that the strength of the divine or the universe plays a crucial role in the realization of intentions.
  • vardhayantī: Translated as "nurturing" or "increasing," this term highlights the idea that thoughts and intentions can grow and develop over time.
  • rare vāṁ stomaṁ: This phrase can be understood as "we praise you" or "we offer our hymns." It reflects the act of devotion and recognition of the divine forces at play.
  • vidatheṣu: Meaning "in the gatherings" or "in the assemblies," this word suggests the importance of community and collective intention in achieving goals.
  • viṣṇo: Referring to Lord Vishnu, this term signifies preservation and protection, indicating that the divine is involved in the nurturing of our intentions.
  • pinvatam: This can be translated as "to those who are worthy" or "to those who deserve," suggesting that the fulfillment of intentions is linked to one's merit and actions.
  • iṣo: Meaning "of the ruler" or "of the lord," this word emphasizes the authority and power behind the intentions being expressed.
  • vṛjaneṣv: This term can be understood as "in the realms of victory" or "in the domains of success," indicating that the intentions are aimed at achieving success and overcoming obstacles.
  • indra: Referring to the king of gods, Indra symbolizes strength, power, and leadership, reinforcing the idea of divine support in our endeavors.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the significance of thoughts and intentions in shaping our reality. It highlights the role of divine power and community in nurturing these intentions. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, teamwork, and spiritual practices. By focusing on positive thoughts and intentions, and recognizing the support of higher powers, individuals can manifest their goals and achieve success. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our intentions, the divine, and the community around us, encouraging us to cultivate a mindset of growth and positivity.
