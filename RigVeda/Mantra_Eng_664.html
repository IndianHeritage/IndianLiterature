वियत तिरो धरुणम् अच्युतं रजो 'तिष्ठिपो दिव आतासु बरहणा |स्वर्मील्हे यन् मद इन्द्र हर्ष्याहन वृतं निर अपाम औब्जो अर्णवम् ||
vi yat tiro dharuṇam acyutaṁ rajo 'tiṣṭhipo diva ātāsu barhaṇā | svarmīḻhe yan mada indra harṣyāhan vṛtraṁ nir apām aubjo arṇavam ||
  • vi yat tiro dharuṇam acyutaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which is beyond the reach of the unchanging." Here, "vi yat" means "that which," "tiro" means "beyond," "dharuṇam" refers to "the unchanging," and "acyutaṁ" means "the imperishable." This suggests a concept of something eternal and unchanging, which can be related to the idea of truth or the ultimate reality in life.
  • rajo 'tiṣṭhipo diva ātāsu barhaṇā: This part translates to "the one who stands firm in the heavens, adorned with splendor." "Rajo" refers to "firmness" or "stability," "atiṣṭhipo" means "one who stands," "diva" refers to "heavens," and "ātāsu barhaṇā" means "adorned with splendor." This can be likened to individuals who achieve greatness and stability in their lives, such as leaders or visionaries who inspire others.
  • svarmīḻhe yan mada indra harṣyāhan: This translates to "the one who brings joy and delight, like Indra." "Svarmīḻhe" means "bringer of joy," "yan" means "who," "mada" refers to "delight," "indra" is a reference to the king of gods, and "harṣyāhan" means "delightful." This can be related to people in our lives who uplift us, such as friends or mentors who provide support and happiness.
  • vṛtraṁ nir apām aubjo arṇavam: This phrase means "the one who conquers the obstacles of the waters." "Vṛtraṁ" refers to "obstacles," "nir" means "without," "apām" means "of the waters," and "aubjo arṇavam" means "the ocean." This can symbolize overcoming challenges in life, akin to how we face and conquer difficulties in our personal or professional journeys.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of stability, joy, and overcoming obstacles. It signifies the importance of seeking eternal truths, standing firm in our beliefs, and finding joy in our relationships. In real life, this can be applied by striving for personal growth, supporting others, and facing challenges with resilience. The mantra serves as a reminder that despite the transient nature of life, there are eternal values and truths that guide us through our journeys.
