तā हि मध्यं भराणां इन्द्राग्नī अधिक्षितः |तā उ कवित्वना कवī पृच्छ्यमाना सखीयते सं धीतम अशनुतं नरā नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
tā hi madhyam bharāṇām indrāgnī adhikṣitaḥ | tā u kavitvanā kavī pṛcchyamānā sakhīyate saṁ dhītam aśnutaṁ narā nabhantām anyake same ||
  • tā hi madhyam bharāṇām: This phrase can be translated as "they are indeed the middle of the bearers." In a real-life context, this can refer to individuals or groups that act as mediators or central figures in a community, such as leaders or peacemakers who hold the responsibility of guiding others.
  • indrāgnī adhikṣitaḥ: This means "Indra and Agni are the overseers." Indra, the king of gods, represents strength and power, while Agni symbolizes fire and transformation. In practical terms, this can be seen in leaders who harness their strength and wisdom to bring about change, much like a CEO steering a company through challenges.
  • tā u kavitvanā kavī: This translates to "they are also poets." This suggests that those in power or leadership roles are not just warriors or overseers but also creators and visionaries. In real life, this can be likened to innovators in technology or art who inspire others through their creativity.
  • pṛcchyamānā sakhīyate: This means "when asked, they become friends." This highlights the importance of communication and connection. In everyday life, this can be seen in how leaders or mentors build relationships by being approachable and open to dialogue, fostering a sense of community.
  • saṁ dhītam aśnutaṁ narā: This translates to "the wise ones consume it." This implies that those who are wise and knowledgeable benefit from the insights and experiences shared within a community. In real life, this can be observed in educational settings where students learn from each other and grow together.
  • nabhantām anyake same: This means "they do not fall into the same trap." This suggests that wise individuals learn from the experiences of others and avoid repeating mistakes. In practical terms, this can be seen in businesses that analyze past failures to improve future strategies.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of leadership, creativity, communication, and wisdom in fostering a harmonious and progressive society. It highlights the role of individuals who act as mediators and visionaries, encouraging collaboration and learning from one another. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various fields such as business, education, and community development, where effective leadership and open communication lead to collective growth and success.
