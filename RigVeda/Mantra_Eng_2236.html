बृहस्पते अति यद आर्यो अर्हाद द्युमद विभाति क्रतुमज जनेषु |यद दीदायच छवसा ऋत्प्रजाता तद अस्मासु द्रविणं धेहि चित्रम् ||
bṛhaspate ati yad aryo arhād dyumad vibhāti kratumaj janeṣu | yad dīdayac chavasa ṛtaprajāta tad asmāsu draviṇaṁ dhehi citram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bṛhaspate: O Bṛhaspati, the lord of wisdom and eloquence.
    • ati: exceedingly or very.
    • yad: that which.
    • aryo: noble or worthy.
    • arhād: deserving or worthy of respect.
    • dyumad: shining or radiant.
    • vibhāti: shines or manifests.
    • kratumaj: born of intention or will.
    • janeṣu: among people or in society.
    • yad: that which.
    • dīdayac: brings forth or produces.
    • chavasa: glory or brilliance.
    • ṛtaprajāta: born of truth or righteousness.
    • tad: that.
    • asmāsu: in us or among us.
    • draviṇaṁ: wealth or resources.
    • dhehi: grant or bestow.
    • citram: wonderful or diverse.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Bṛhaspati, the deity associated with wisdom, learning, and eloquence, asking for his blessings.
    • The phrase "ati yad aryo arhād dyumad vibhāti" emphasizes the importance of noble qualities that shine brightly in society.
    • It highlights the significance of intentions and actions that are born from a place of righteousness and truth.
    • The mantra requests the bestowal of wealth and resources that are diverse and beneficial, reflecting the idea that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses wisdom and virtue.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of Bṛhaspati can inspire their team to work with integrity and purpose, leading to a productive and harmonious work environment.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who act with noble intentions and respect can foster deeper connections and mutual understanding.
    • In community service, those who engage in actions that are righteous and beneficial to society can create a positive impact, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, noble intentions, and righteousness in our actions.
    • It encourages individuals to seek blessings for both material and spiritual wealth, emphasizing that true prosperity comes from virtuous living.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a more harmonious society.
