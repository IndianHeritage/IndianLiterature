अभि क्रत्वेन्द्र भूः अध जमान न ते विव्याङ् महिमानं राजांसि |स्वेनाऽ हि वृतं शवसां जगन्त न शत्रुर अन्तं विविदद युधा ते ||
abhi kratvendra bhūr adha jman na te vivyaṅ mahimānaṁ rajāṁsi | svenā hi vṛtraṁ śavasā jaghantha na śatrur antaṁ vividad yudhā te ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards
    • kratvendra: the powerful or the strong
    • bhūr: the earth or the world
    • adha: below
    • jman: born or created
    • na: not
    • te: your
    • vivyaṅ: to be known or recognized
    • mahimānaṁ: greatness or glory
    • rajāṁsi: kings or rulers
    • svenā: by themselves
    • hi: indeed
    • vṛtraṁ: the obstacle or enemy
    • śavasā: with strength
    • jaghantha: to destroy or to overcome
    • na: not
    • śatrur: the enemy
    • antaṁ: the end or limit
    • vividad: to engage or to fight
    • yudhā: in battle
    • te: your
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the strength and power of the divine, urging the powerful to rise and overcome obstacles.
    • The phrase "abhi kratvendra" suggests a call to the strong to act decisively in the world.
    • It emphasizes the importance of recognizing one's own greatness and the potential to overcome challenges.
    • The mention of "vṛtraṁ" symbolizes the obstacles or enemies that one must confront in life.
    • It highlights the idea that true strength comes from within and that one must engage in the battle of life with courage and determination.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied to individuals facing challenges in their careers. For instance, a person striving for a promotion may encounter various obstacles, but by recognizing their own capabilities and working hard, they can overcome these challenges.
    • In personal life, someone dealing with health issues may find inspiration in this mantra to fight against their condition, drawing strength from within to pursue recovery.
    • In social movements, leaders often invoke the spirit of this mantra to rally people together to confront societal injustices, emphasizing the collective strength needed to bring about change.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call to action for individuals to recognize their inner strength and the importance of overcoming obstacles in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that challenges are a part of existence, and with determination and courage, one can rise above them.
    • This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, encouraging individuals to engage in their personal battles, whether in career, health, or social justice, and to emerge victorious through self-belief and action.
