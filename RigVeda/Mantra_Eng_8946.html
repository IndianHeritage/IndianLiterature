शीतिके शीतिकावति ह्लादिके ह्लादिकावति |मण्डूक्याः सु सं गम इमं स्वाग्निं हर्षय ||
śītike śītikāvati hlādike hlādikāvati | maṇḍūkyā3 su saṁ gama imaṁ sv a1gniṁ harṣaya ||
  • śītike: This word refers to something that is cool or refreshing. In a real-life context, it can be likened to the soothing feeling of a gentle breeze on a hot day or the refreshing taste of cold water.
  • śītikāvati: This term suggests a state of being cool or having a cooling effect. For example, a calm and peaceful environment can provide a sense of tranquility, much like a serene lake on a summer afternoon.
  • hlādike: This word means to bring joy or happiness. In everyday life, it can be associated with moments of celebration, such as a family gathering or a festival where people come together to share joy.
  • hlādikāvati: Similar to hlādike, this term emphasizes the quality of bringing happiness. For instance, a kind gesture from a friend can uplift one's spirits and create a joyful atmosphere.
  • maṇḍūkyā: This word can be interpreted as referring to a specific type of being or entity. In a broader sense, it can symbolize the interconnectedness of all living beings, much like how different species in an ecosystem rely on each other.
  • su: This term means good or auspicious. In real life, it can be related to positive outcomes, such as achieving a goal or receiving good news.
  • saṁ gama: This phrase suggests coming together or uniting. An example can be seen in community events where people gather for a common cause, fostering a sense of belonging and cooperation.
  • imaṁ: This word means 'this' and can refer to the present moment or situation. It emphasizes the importance of being mindful and aware of our current experiences.
  • sv a1gniṁ: This term refers to the sacred fire or energy. In many cultures, fire symbolizes transformation and purification, much like how challenges in life can lead to personal growth.
  • harṣaya: This word means to inspire or evoke joy. In real life, it can be related to motivational speeches or acts of kindness that uplift others and encourage them to pursue their dreams.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of harmony, joy, and the interconnectedness of life. It emphasizes the importance of creating a cooling and joyful environment, both internally and externally. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as fostering positive relationships, promoting community unity, and encouraging personal growth through challenges. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and joyful existence for themselves and those around them.
