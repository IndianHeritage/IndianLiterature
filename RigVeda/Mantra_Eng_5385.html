वयं ते त इन्द्र ये च देव स्तवन्त शूर ददतो मगानि |यच्छा सूरिभ्य उपमं वरूथं स्वाभुवो जरणाम अश्नवन्त ||
vayaṁ te ta indra ye ca deva stavanta śūra dadato maghāni | yacchā sūribhya upamaṁ varūthaṁ svābhuvo jaraṇām aśnavanta ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vayaṁ: We
    • te: Your
    • ta: Those
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • ye: Who
    • ca: And
    • deva: Gods
    • stavanta: Praise
    • śūra: Brave
    • dadato: Bestowing
    • maghāni: Wealth
    • yacchā: Whatever
    • sūribhya: To the brave
    • upamaṁ: Comparison
    • varūthaṁ: Protection
    • svābhuvo: Of our own
    • jaraṇām: Of the aged
    • aśnavanta: Swift
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the chief deity in the Vedic pantheon, asking for blessings and wealth.
    • It emphasizes the importance of bravery and the act of praising the divine, which is believed to attract prosperity.
    • The mention of "sūribhya" highlights the value of courage and strength in achieving success and protection.
    • The phrase "upamaṁ varūthaṁ" suggests that the protection and blessings we seek should be comparable to the best, indicating a desire for the highest quality of divine support.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team may invoke this mantra during a meeting to inspire courage and unity among members, seeking divine guidance for success in their projects.
    • During a sports event, athletes might recite this mantra to instill bravery and a sense of teamwork, aiming for victory and recognition.
    • In personal life, individuals may use this mantra during challenging times, such as preparing for an important exam or facing a difficult decision, to seek strength and clarity.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support and the recognition of the virtues of bravery and praise.
    • It serves as a reminder that invoking higher powers can lead to the best outcomes in various aspects of life, from personal challenges to collective endeavors.
    • In essence, this mantra encourages individuals and communities to seek strength and prosperity through courage and devotion, making it applicable in numerous real-life scenarios.
