ईक्षे रायः क्षयस्य चर्षणीनाम उता व्रजम् अपवर्तासि गोनाम् | शिक्षानरः समिथेषु प्रहावान वस्वो राशिम अभिनेतासि भूर्म् ||
īkṣe rāyaḥ kṣayasya carṣaṇīnām uta vrajam apavartāsi gonām | śikṣānaraḥ samitheṣu prahāvān vasvo rāśim abhinetāsi bhūrim ||
  • Transliteration: "īkṣe rāyaḥ kṣayasya carṣaṇīnām uta vrajam apavartāsi gonām | śikṣānaraḥ samitheṣu prahāvān vasvo rāśim abhinetāsi bhūrim ||"
  • Meaning:
    • īkṣe: "I see" or "I observe."
    • rāyaḥ: "wealth" or "prosperity."
    • kṣayasya: "of destruction" or "of loss."
    • carṣaṇīnām: "of the fields" or "of the pastures."
    • uta: "and" or "also."
    • vrajam: "the herd" or "the group."
    • apavartāsi: "you turn away" or "you divert."
    • gonām: "of the cows" or "of the cattle."
    • śikṣānaraḥ: "the one who teaches" or "the instructor."
    • samitheṣu: "in the assemblies" or "in the gatherings."
    • prahāvān: "the one who strikes" or "the one who acts."
    • vasvo: "of the wealth" or "of the resources."
    • rāśim: "the multitude" or "the collection."
    • abhinetāsi: "you are the one who brings forth" or "you are the creator."
    • bhūrim: "abundant" or "plentiful."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the observation of wealth and prosperity while acknowledging the potential for loss and destruction.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and the role of leadership in guiding and protecting resources, particularly in agricultural and pastoral contexts.
    • The reference to "cows" symbolizes not just livestock but also the wealth and sustenance they provide, which is crucial in agrarian societies.
    • The "instructor" or "teacher" role highlights the need for wisdom and guidance in managing resources effectively.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern agriculture, farmers often gather in cooperatives to share knowledge and resources, reflecting the assembly aspect of the mantra.
    • Community leaders play a vital role in ensuring that resources are used sustainably, akin to the "one who teaches" in the mantra.
    • In times of economic downturn, the mantra serves as a reminder to observe and redirect resources wisely to prevent loss.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the significance of observing wealth and the responsibilities that come with it.
    • It teaches the importance of community, leadership, and sustainable practices in managing resources.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals and communities to be vigilant and proactive in safeguarding their wealth and resources, ensuring abundance for all.
