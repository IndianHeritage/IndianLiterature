ऋषिमना य ऋषिकृत स्वर्षाः सहस्रणीथः पदवीः कवीनाम् |तृतीयं धाम महिषः सिषासन सोमो विराजम् अनु राजति ष्टुप् ||
ṛṣimanā ya ṛṣikṛt svarṣāḥ sahasraṇīthaḥ padavīḥ kavīnām | tṛtīyaṁ dhāma mahiṣaḥ siṣāsan somo virājam anu rājati ṣṭup ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ṛṣimanā: The one who is wise or knowledgeable.
    • ya: Who.
    • ṛṣikṛt: Created by the sages or seers.
    • svarṣāḥ: The celestial or divine.
    • sahasraṇīthaḥ: Thousands or numerous.
    • padavīḥ: Paths or ways.
    • kavīnām: Of the poets or wise ones.
    • tṛtīyaṁ: The third.
    • dhāma: Abode or realm.
    • mahiṣaḥ: The great or powerful.
    • siṣāsan: Who rules or governs.
    • somo: The moon or soma, often associated with nourishment and immortality.
    • virājam: Shining or radiant.
    • anu: Following or in accordance with.
    • rājati: Reigns or rules.
    • ṣṭup: A type of meter or poetic form.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the wisdom and knowledge that is created by the sages, emphasizing the importance of divine guidance in our lives.
    • The mention of "thousands of paths" suggests that there are numerous ways to achieve wisdom and understanding, reflecting the diversity of human experience.
    • The "third abode" can be interpreted as a higher state of consciousness or enlightenment that one can attain through knowledge and wisdom.
    • The reference to the moon (soma) symbolizes nourishment, both physical and spiritual, indicating that wisdom nourishes the soul.
    • The mantra concludes with the idea of a radiant ruler, suggesting that true leadership is characterized by wisdom and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern education, the pursuit of knowledge can be seen as a path to enlightenment, where students explore various disciplines to find their own "paths" to wisdom.
    • In leadership, effective leaders often embody the qualities of wisdom and understanding, guiding their teams with insight and clarity, much like the "radiant ruler" mentioned in the mantra.
    • Spiritual practices, such as meditation and mindfulness, can be viewed as ways to reach that "third abode" of higher consciousness, promoting personal growth and understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom and knowledge in our lives, encouraging individuals to seek enlightenment through various paths.
    • It serves as a reminder that true leadership and governance should be rooted in wisdom, guiding others towards a brighter and more informed future.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles outlined in this mantra can be applied in education, leadership, and personal development, fostering a society that values knowledge and understanding.
