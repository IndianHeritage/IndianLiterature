युयोतā शरुम अस्मद आम् आदित्यास उतामतिम् |ऋधग द्वेषः कृतुता विश्ववेदसः ||
yuyotā śarum asmad ām̐ ādityāsa utāmatim | ṛdhag dveṣaḥ kṛṇuta viśvavedasaḥ ||
  • yuyotā: This word can be interpreted as "to strive" or "to engage in battle." It signifies the importance of effort and determination in overcoming challenges.
  • śarum: This term refers to "the weapon" or "the means of protection." It symbolizes the tools or resources we use to defend ourselves or achieve our goals.
  • asmad: Meaning "us" or "our," this word emphasizes the collective aspect of the mantra, highlighting the importance of community and shared purpose.
  • ām̐: This is a particle that can denote "indeed" or "truly," reinforcing the sincerity of the statement that follows.
  • ādityāsa: This term relates to "the sun" or "solar deities," representing light, knowledge, and enlightenment. It signifies the pursuit of wisdom and clarity in our endeavors.
  • utāmatim: This word means "the highest" or "the best," indicating the aspiration towards excellence and the highest ideals in our actions.
  • ṛdhag: This term can be understood as "to remove" or "to eliminate," suggesting the need to clear obstacles or negativity from our path.
  • dveṣaḥ: Meaning "hatred" or "enmity," this word highlights the importance of overcoming negative emotions and fostering harmony.
  • kṛṇuta: This is a command form meaning "to create" or "to make," urging action towards positive outcomes.
  • viśvavedasaḥ: This term refers to "the all-knowing" or "the wise," indicating the importance of knowledge and understanding in our pursuits.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of collective effort, the pursuit of knowledge, and the elimination of negativity in our lives. It encourages individuals to strive for excellence while working together as a community.
  • Real-life Application: In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied by fostering teamwork and collaboration. For instance, when a team faces a challenging project, they can draw on their collective strengths (yuyotā) and resources (śarum) to achieve the best results (utāmatim) while maintaining a positive and harmonious environment (ṛdhag dveṣaḥ kṛṇuta).

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity, the pursuit of knowledge, and the importance of overcoming negativity. By applying these principles in our daily lives, we can create a more harmonious and successful environment, whether in personal relationships, professional settings, or community engagements.
