स गृणानो अद्भिर देववान इति सुभन्धुर नमसाऽ सूक्तैः |वर्धद उक्थैर वचोभिर आ हि नूनं व्य अध्वैति पयसा उष्रियायाः ||
sa gṛṇāno adbhir devavān iti subandhur namasā sūktaiḥ | vardhad ukthair vacobhir ā hi nūnaṁ vy adhvaiti payasa usriyāyāḥ ||
  • sa gṛṇāno: This phrase can be translated as "he who sings" or "he who praises." It emphasizes the importance of vocal expression and the act of devotion through song.
  • adbhir: This word means "with waters" or "with the essence of life." It signifies the connection between the divine and the natural elements, particularly water, which is essential for life.
  • devavān: This term translates to "the one who is divine" or "the godly one." It refers to a being or entity that embodies divine qualities, suggesting a connection to higher spiritual realms.
  • iti subandhur: This phrase can be interpreted as "thus, he is well-connected" or "therefore, he is auspicious." It highlights the idea of being in harmony with the divine and the universe.
  • namasā sūktaiḥ: This means "with reverence and hymns." It indicates the importance of offering respect and devotion through sacred verses or mantras.
  • vardhad: This word means "to grow" or "to increase." It signifies the idea of expansion, both in a spiritual and material sense.
  • ukthair vacobhir: This translates to "with words of speech." It emphasizes the power of words and communication in manifesting intentions and desires.
  • ā hi nūnaṁ: This phrase can be understood as "indeed, surely." It adds emphasis to the preceding statements, reinforcing their truth.
  • vy adhvaiti: This means "he leads" or "he guides." It suggests a sense of direction and purpose, indicating that the divine presence guides individuals on their paths.
  • payasa usriyāyāḥ: This translates to "with the essence of nourishment." It signifies the importance of sustenance, both physical and spiritual, in one's life journey.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, the significance of divine connection, and the power of words in shaping our reality. It teaches us that through reverence and praise, we can align ourselves with the divine and experience growth in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in practices such as meditation, prayer, and rituals where individuals seek to connect with higher energies, express gratitude, and manifest their intentions. By recognizing the importance of our words and actions, we can cultivate a more harmonious existence, both within ourselves and in our interactions with the world around us.
