सोमा असृग्रम् आशवो मधोर मदस्य धारया | अभि विश्वानि काव्याः ||
somā asṛgram āśavo madhor madasya dhārayā | abhi viśvāni kāvyā ||
  • Translation: The mantra translates to "The soma, the invigorating drink, is poured forth; may it bring us the essence of joy and inspiration, and may it bestow upon us all the poetic expressions."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Somā: Refers to the sacred drink, soma, which is often associated with immortality and divine inspiration in Vedic texts.
    • Asṛgram: Means to pour forth or to flow, indicating the act of offering or sharing the essence of soma.
    • Āśavo: Signifies vigor or energy, suggesting that the drink brings vitality and strength.
    • Madhor: Translates to sweetness or joy, emphasizing the pleasurable aspects of the experience.
    • Madasya: Refers to intoxication or ecstasy, highlighting the euphoric effects of the soma.
    • Dhārayā: Means to hold or sustain, indicating the nurturing aspect of the drink.
    • Abhi: A prefix meaning 'towards' or 'for', indicating intention or purpose.
    • Viśvāni: Means all or universal, suggesting inclusivity and the idea of encompassing everything.
    • Kāvyā: Refers to poetry or poetic expressions, indicating the creative and artistic inspiration derived from the experience.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern contexts, the concept of 'soma' can be likened to experiences that invigorate and inspire individuals, such as art, music, or nature. For instance, attending a concert can evoke feelings of joy and creativity, similar to the effects of soma.
    • In a corporate setting, team-building activities that promote collaboration and creativity can be seen as a modern-day 'soma', fostering a sense of unity and inspiration among team members.
    • In personal life, engaging in hobbies like painting or writing can serve as a source of 'soma', providing joy and a sense of fulfillment.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its celebration of joy, creativity, and the nurturing aspects of life. It emphasizes the importance of seeking inspiration and vitality through shared experiences. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios where individuals seek to enhance their creativity, foster connections, and cultivate joy, whether through artistic endeavors, communal activities, or personal growth. By embracing the essence of 'soma', one can lead a more enriched and inspired life.
