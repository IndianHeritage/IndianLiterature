आग्निं न स्ववृक्तिभिर् होतारं त्वा वृणीमहे |यज्ञाय स्तीर्णबरिषे वि वो मदे शीरम् पावकशोचिषं विवक्षसे ||
āgniṁ na svavṛktibhir hotāraṁ tvā vṛṇīmahe | yajñāya stīrṇabarhiṣe vi vo made śīram pāvakaśociṣaṁ vivakṣase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āgniṁ: Agni, the fire god, representing energy and transformation.
    • na: Not.
    • svavṛktibhir: By our own actions or offerings.
    • hotāraṁ: The one who is invoked or called upon, often referring to the priest or the divine.
    • tvā: You, referring to Agni.
    • vṛṇīmahe: We choose or invoke.
    • yajñāya: For the sacrifice or offering.
    • stīrṇabarhiṣe: With firm or well-prepared grass, symbolizing the sacred space for rituals.
    • vi: O, or to you.
    • vo: Your, referring to the collective divine.
    • made: In joy or ecstasy.
    • śīram: The essence or the best part.
    • pāvakaśociṣaṁ: The one who is pure and illuminating.
    • vivakṣase: You express or manifest.
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, invoking his presence and blessings. It emphasizes the importance of our actions in the ritual and the desire to connect with the divine through offerings. The mantra highlights the significance of purity and joy in the process of worship.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, Agni is often invoked at the beginning of ceremonies, symbolizing the transformation of offerings into divine energy. For instance, during a wedding ceremony, the couple circles the sacred fire, signifying their commitment and the divine witness of their union.
    • In daily life, the concept of invoking Agni can be seen in the practice of lighting a lamp or candle during prayers, representing the light of knowledge and purity dispelling darkness.
    • In a broader sense, the mantra can be applied to any situation where one seeks to bring positive energy and transformation, such as starting a new project or endeavor, where one prepares the ground (like the firm grass) and invokes the right intentions.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance and the acknowledgment of the sacredness of rituals. It teaches us the importance of intention, purity, and joy in our actions. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to approach their tasks with a sense of purpose and reverence, fostering a connection with the divine in everyday activities. By invoking positive energy and transformation, we can create a harmonious environment in our personal and communal lives.
