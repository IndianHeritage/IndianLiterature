प्र वाम महि द्यवी अभ्य उपस्तुतिम भरामहे |शुचī उप प्रशस्तये ||
pra vām mahi dyavī abhy upastutim bharāmahe | śucī upa praśastaye ||
  • pra vām mahi dyavī abhy upastutim bharāmahe
    • pra - This word can be understood as "forth" or "towards," indicating a direction or intention.
    • vām - This means "your," referring to a divine entity or force.
    • mahi - Translates to "great" or "mighty," signifying the vastness and power of the divine.
    • dyavī - This refers to "heaven" or "sky," representing the celestial realm.
    • abhy - This can mean "towards" or "in the direction of," indicating a movement or approach.
    • upastutim - This word means "praise" or "worship," highlighting the act of reverence towards the divine.
    • bharāmahe - This translates to "we bring" or "we offer," suggesting an act of giving or presenting something valuable.
  • śucī upa praśastaye
    • śucī - This means "pure" or "clean," indicating a state of clarity and sanctity.
    • upa - This translates to "near" or "approaching," suggesting closeness or intimacy.
    • praśastaye - This means "for the praise" or "for the glory," indicating the purpose of the offering or worship.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine, expressing a desire to offer praise and reverence towards the great celestial powers. It emphasizes the importance of purity and the intention behind worship, suggesting that one should approach the divine with a clean heart and mind.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or prayer, focusing on the purity of their intentions and the greatness of the divine.
  • Community Gatherings: In communal worship or festivals, this mantra can be used to unite people in a shared intention of reverence and gratitude towards the divine forces that govern the universe.
  • Personal Reflection: It serves as a reminder to maintain purity in thoughts and actions, encouraging individuals to reflect on their intentions and the impact they have on their surroundings.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for purity and reverence towards the divine. It encourages individuals to approach their spiritual practices with a clean heart and a sincere intention to praise the greatness of the universe. By embodying these principles in daily life, one can foster a deeper connection with the divine and promote harmony within themselves and their communities.
