यत् त्वा देव प्रपिबन्ति ततो अप्यायसे पुनः |वायुः सोमस्य रक्षितā समानाम् मास आकृतिः ||
yat tvā deva prapibanti tata ā pyāyase punaḥ | vāyuḥ somasya rakṣitā samānām māsa ākṛtiḥ ||
  • yat tvā deva prapibanti: This phrase translates to "that which the gods drink." It signifies the divine essence or energy that is consumed by the deities, representing nourishment and sustenance from a higher power.
  • tata ā pyāyase punaḥ: This means "therefore, you are nourished again." It implies a cycle of renewal and rejuvenation, suggesting that the energy or essence consumed by the gods is returned to the world, promoting life and vitality.
  • vāyuḥ somasya rakṣitā: This translates to "the wind is the protector of the soma." In Vedic tradition, 'soma' is a sacred drink associated with immortality and divine inspiration. The wind (vāyu) symbolizes the vital life force that protects and carries this divine essence.
  • samānām māsa ākṛtiḥ: This means "the form of the month is the same." It suggests a cyclical nature of time and existence, where each month brings a similar pattern of life and energy, reflecting the rhythms of nature and the universe.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the interconnectedness of divine energy, nature, and the cyclical patterns of life. It emphasizes the importance of nourishment from the divine and the protective forces of nature that sustain life.
  • Real-life Application: In our daily lives, this mantra can remind us of the importance of seeking nourishment not just from physical food but also from spiritual and emotional sources. For example, engaging in meditation or spending time in nature can be seen as ways to 'drink' from the divine essence, rejuvenating our spirits and promoting well-being.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the cyclical nature of life and the importance of divine nourishment. It encourages us to recognize the protective forces in our lives, such as community and nature, that help sustain us. By acknowledging these connections, we can cultivate a deeper appreciation for the rhythms of life and the divine energies that support our existence.
