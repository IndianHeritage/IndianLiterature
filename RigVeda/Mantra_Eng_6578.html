यथा वरो सुशाम्णे सनीभ्य आवहो रयिम् |व्यश्वेभ्यः सुभगे वाजिनीवति ||
yathā varo suṣāmṇe sanibhya āvaho rayim | vyaśvebhyaḥ subhage vājinīvati ||
  • Mantra: "yathā varo suṣāmṇe sanibhya āvaho rayim | vyaśvebhyaḥ subhage vājinīvati ||"
  • Translation: "As the best (of the best) we call forth wealth, may it come to us from all directions, may it be auspicious and bring us strength."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • yathā: As, in the manner of
    • varo: Best, excellent
    • suṣāmṇe: Auspicious, well-being
    • sanibhya: From all directions
    • āvaho: We call forth, we invite
    • rayim: Wealth, prosperity
    • vyaśvebhyaḥ: From all sides, from everywhere
    • subhage: Auspicious, good fortune
    • vājinīvati: Bringing strength, power

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of inviting prosperity and well-being into our lives.
    • The phrase "yathā varo" suggests that we should strive for the best in all aspects of life, whether it be in our personal endeavors, professional careers, or spiritual pursuits.
    • By calling forth "rayim," we acknowledge the need for wealth—not just in a material sense but also in terms of health, happiness, and fulfillment.
    • The invocation of auspiciousness ("subhage") indicates that we should seek blessings that enhance our lives positively.
    • In real life, this can be seen in practices such as setting intentions during a new year, where individuals focus on their goals and aspirations, inviting success and abundance into their lives.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of intention and the importance of inviting positive energies into our lives.
    • It is applicable in various scenarios, such as during rituals, personal goal-setting, or even in daily affirmations where one seeks to attract prosperity and strength.
    • By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset that is open to receiving blessings from all directions, fostering a life filled with abundance and well-being.
