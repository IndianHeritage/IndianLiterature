तं शाश्वतीषु मातृषु वन आवीतम अशृतम् |चित्रं सन्तं गुहा हितं सुवेदं कूचिदर्थिनम् ||
taṁ śaśvatīṣu mātṛṣu vana ā vītam aśritam | citraṁ santaṁ guhā hitaṁ suvedaṁ kūcidarthinam ||
  • taṁ - This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • śaśvatīṣu - This means "in the eternal" or "in the everlasting," indicating something that is timeless and unchanging.
  • mātṛṣu - This translates to "in the mothers," symbolizing nurturing, care, and the source of life.
  • vana - This means "forest" or "woodland," representing nature, tranquility, and the interconnectedness of life.
  • ā vītam - This can be interpreted as "having been freed" or "having been released," suggesting liberation from constraints.
  • aśritam - This means "sheltered" or "protected," indicating a state of safety and security.
  • citraṁ - This translates to "variegated" or "colorful," symbolizing diversity and richness in life.
  • santaṁ - This means "peaceful" or "calm," representing a state of tranquility and contentment.
  • guhā - This translates to "cave" or "hidden place," symbolizing depth, introspection, and the inner self.
  • hitaṁ - This means "beneficial" or "good," indicating something that is advantageous or positive.
  • suvedaṁ - This translates to "well-knowing" or "wise," representing knowledge and understanding.
  • kūcidarthinam - This can be interpreted as "one who seeks the essence" or "one who understands the purpose," indicating a quest for meaning and truth.

The mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of life, the nurturing aspect of nature, and the quest for knowledge and liberation. It emphasizes the importance of finding peace and wisdom in the natural world, suggesting that true understanding comes from introspection and connection with the environment.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In parenting, it highlights the nurturing role of mothers and the importance of providing a safe and loving environment for children.
  • In environmental conservation, it underscores the significance of protecting natural habitats and recognizing the beauty and diversity of ecosystems.
  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding, fostering a sense of inner peace and purpose.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the eternal connection between humanity and nature, the importance of nurturing relationships, and the pursuit of wisdom. It encourages us to find solace in the natural world and to seek deeper understanding in our lives, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
