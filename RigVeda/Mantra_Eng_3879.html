वातस्य युक्तान सुयुजश्चिद अश्वान कविश्चिद एषो अजगन्न अवश्युः |विश्वे ते अत्र मरुतः सखाय इन्द्र ब्रह्माणि तविषीम अवर्धन ||
vātasya yuktān suyujaś cid aśvān kaviś cid eṣo ajagann avasyuḥ | viśve te atra marutaḥ sakhāya indra brahmāṇi taviṣīm avardhan ||
  • vātasya: This word refers to the wind or air, symbolizing the vital force or energy that sustains life.
  • yuktān: Meaning "connected" or "joined," it signifies the importance of unity and collaboration.
  • suyujaś: This term can be interpreted as "well harnessed" or "well controlled," indicating the need for discipline and proper management of resources.
  • cid: A word that denotes consciousness or awareness, emphasizing the role of intellect and understanding in our actions.
  • aśvān: Referring to "horses," which in Vedic literature often symbolize strength, speed, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • kaviś: This means "poet" or "sage," representing wisdom and the ability to perceive deeper truths.
  • cid: Again, this word appears, reinforcing the theme of consciousness and awareness.
  • eṣo: Meaning "this," it points to the immediate presence of the divine or the forces at play in our lives.
  • ajagann: This term can be translated as "the one who has not been born," often referring to the eternal or the unmanifested aspect of existence.
  • avasyuḥ: Meaning "inevitable" or "certain," it suggests that certain outcomes are destined to occur.
  • viśve: This word means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • te: Meaning "you," it addresses the divine or the forces of nature directly.
  • atra: This translates to "here," emphasizing the present moment and the importance of being aware of our surroundings.
  • marutaḥ: Referring to the storm gods or winds, symbolizing power and the dynamic forces of nature.
  • sakhāya: Meaning "friends" or "allies," it highlights the importance of companionship and support in our endeavors.
  • indra: The king of gods, representing strength, leadership, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • brahmāṇi: This term refers to the ultimate reality or the cosmic spirit, indicating the connection to the divine.
  • taviṣīm: Meaning "powerful" or "strong," it emphasizes the need for strength in our actions.
  • avardhan: This translates to "increase" or "enhance," suggesting growth and the expansion of potential.

In conclusion, this mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of unity, strength, and consciousness in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of collaboration and the harnessing of our inner resources to navigate the challenges we face. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork in professional settings, community building, and personal development. By recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine forces at play, we can cultivate a sense of purpose and direction in our lives, ultimately leading to growth and fulfillment.
