त्वां इद धि सहसस् पुत्र मर्त्य उपब्रूते धन हिते |सुवीर्यं मरुत आ स्वश्व्यं दधीत यो व आचके ||
tvām id dhi sahasas putra martya upabrūte dhane hite | suvīryam maruta ā svaśvyaṁ dadhīta yo va ācake ||
  • tvām id dhi sahasas: This phrase can be translated as "to you, indeed, with great speed." It signifies the importance of swift action and responsiveness in life.
  • putra martya upabrūte: This translates to "the mortal speaks to you, O son." Here, "putra" (son) can symbolize the relationship between humanity and the divine, indicating that humans seek guidance and support from higher powers.
  • dhane hite: This means "for the benefit of wealth." It emphasizes the pursuit of prosperity and well-being, suggesting that wealth should be used for good and beneficial purposes.
  • suvīryam maruta: This translates to "the strong one, O Maruts." The Maruts are storm deities in Vedic literature, representing strength and vitality. This phrase highlights the importance of strength in overcoming challenges.
  • ā svaśvyaṁ dadhīta: This means "may you bestow upon us your own." It reflects a desire for divine blessings and support in our endeavors.
  • yo va ācake: This translates to "whoever speaks." It suggests that communication and expression are vital in seeking help and guidance.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking divine guidance and support in our lives, particularly in the pursuit of wealth and strength. It teaches us that swift action, communication, and the intention to use our resources for good are essential in our journey. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in business, where quick decision-making and ethical practices lead to success, or in personal relationships, where open communication fosters understanding and support. Ultimately, it reminds us of the interconnectedness between humanity and the divine, urging us to act with purpose and integrity in all our endeavors.
