तत् ते यज्ञो अजायत तद् अर्क उता हस्कृतिः |तद् विश्वम् अभिभूर् असि यज जातं यच च जन्तवम् ||
tat te yajño ajāyata tad arka uta haskṛtiḥ | tad viśvam abhibhūr asi yaj jātaṁ yac ca jantvam ||
  • tat te yajño ajāyata: This phrase can be translated as "that sacrifice was born for you." In the context of the RigVeda, it signifies the importance of sacrifice (yajña) in the cosmic order. Sacrifice is not merely a ritual but a means of connecting with the divine and the universe.
  • tad arka uta haskṛtiḥ: This translates to "that light and brilliance." Here, "arka" refers to light or radiance, symbolizing knowledge, enlightenment, and the divine presence. "Haskṛtiḥ" can be interpreted as the essence or the quality of brilliance, suggesting that through sacrifice, one can attain enlightenment and clarity.
  • tad viśvam abhibhūr asi: This means "you are the one who pervades the entire universe." It emphasizes the omnipresence of the divine, indicating that the essence of the divine is present in all aspects of the universe. This can be related to the idea that everything in existence is interconnected.
  • yaj jātaṁ yac ca jantvam: This translates to "the sacrifice that has been born and all living beings." It highlights the idea that all forms of life and existence arise from the same source, which is the divine. It suggests a unity among all beings, reinforcing the concept of interconnectedness.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of sacrifice, light, and the divine's omnipresence. It teaches that through selfless actions and sacrifices, one can attain enlightenment and connect with the universe.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various ways. For instance, in community service, individuals often engage in selfless acts that benefit others, reflecting the spirit of yajña. Additionally, in personal growth, the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment can be seen as a form of sacrifice, where one dedicates time and effort to learning and self-improvement.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of selflessness, the pursuit of knowledge, and the interconnectedness of all beings. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a harmonious existence and foster a deeper understanding of their place in the universe.
