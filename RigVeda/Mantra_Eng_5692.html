तच चक्षुर देवहितं शुक्रम उच्चरात् |पश्येम शरदः शतम् जीवेम शरदः शतम् ||
tac cakṣur devahitaṁ śukram uccarat | paśyema śaradaḥ śataṁ jīvema śaradaḥ śatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tac: that
    • cakṣur: eye (or vision)
    • devahitaṁ: divine or beneficial
    • śukram: pure or bright
    • uccarat: may it rise or shine
    • paśyema: may we see
    • śaradaḥ: autumn (or a season of clarity)
    • śataṁ: a hundred
    • jīvema: may we live
    • śaradaḥ: autumn (repeated for emphasis)
    • śatam: a hundred (repeated for emphasis)
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer for clarity and purity of vision.
    • It seeks divine guidance to perceive the truth and live a long, fulfilling life.
    • The repetition of "śaradaḥ" and "śatam" emphasizes the desire for longevity and clarity throughout life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied when making important decisions. Seeking clarity and insight can lead to better outcomes.
    • In personal relationships, the desire to see the truth in interactions can foster understanding and harmony.
    • In education, students can invoke this mantra to enhance their focus and comprehension, aiming for a deeper understanding of their subjects.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine clarity and purity in perception.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, encouraging individuals to seek truth and wisdom in their actions and thoughts.
    • By embodying the essence of this mantra, one can aspire to live a life filled with insight, understanding, and longevity.
