तां मे अश्व्यानां हरिणां नितोषणा | उतो नु कृत्व्यानां नृवाहसा ||
tā me aśvyānāṁ harīṇāṁ nitośanā | uto nu kṛtvyānāṁ nṛvāhasā ||
  • tā me aśvyānāṁ harīṇāṁ nitośanā: This phrase can be translated to "May those who are swift and powerful, like horses, come to me." Here, "aśvyānāṁ" refers to horses, which symbolize strength, speed, and vitality in Vedic literature. Horses were essential in ancient Indian society, often associated with warriors and nobility.
  • uto nu kṛtvyānāṁ nṛvāhasā: This part translates to "And may those who are accomplished and noble also come to me." The term "kṛtvyānāṁ" refers to those who have achieved their goals or are successful, while "nṛvāhasā" signifies nobility or leadership among humans.

  • Significance: This mantra expresses a desire for the company and blessings of those who are strong, swift, and accomplished. It reflects the importance of surrounding oneself with positive influences and role models in life.
  • Real-life Application: In modern contexts, this mantra can be applied to personal and professional growth. For instance, in a workplace setting, one might seek mentorship from successful colleagues or leaders who embody the qualities of strength and accomplishment. Similarly, in personal life, one may choose friends and partners who inspire and uplift them, fostering a supportive environment.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the value of community and the impact of relationships on individual success. By aspiring to connect with those who exhibit strength and achievement, individuals can enhance their own lives and strive for greater accomplishments.
