आ नो देवैभिर उप यातम अर्वाक सजोषसा नासत्याः रथेन |युवोर हि नः सख्या पितृयाणि समानो बन्धुर उता तस्य वित्तम् ||
ā no devebhir upa yātam arvāk sajoṣasā nāsatyā rathena | yuvor hi naḥ sakhyā pitryāṇi samāno bandhur uta tasya vittam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: May we (us)
    • no: our
    • devebhir: by the gods
    • upa: towards
    • yātam: may we go
    • arvāk: openly
    • sajoṣasā: with joy
    • nāsatyā: with the Asvins (the twin gods)
    • rathena: in the chariot
    • yuvor: of the two (Asvins)
    • hi: indeed
    • naḥ: our
    • sakhyā: friendship
    • pitryāṇi: paternal
    • samāno: equal
    • bandhur: a relative
    • uta: or
    • tasya: of that
    • vittam: wealth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer for guidance and support from the divine.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and friendship, particularly in familial and social relationships.
    • The mention of the Asvins, who are known for their healing and protective qualities, signifies a request for their blessings in our endeavors.
    • The phrase "may we go openly with joy" suggests a desire for transparency and happiness in our actions and interactions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, this mantra can be applied to foster strong bonds among family members, encouraging open communication and mutual support.
    • In a workplace, it can serve as a reminder to collaborate joyfully and supportively, creating a positive work environment.
    • During community gatherings, invoking this mantra can help strengthen friendships and promote a sense of belonging and unity.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance and the importance of relationships in our lives.
    • It teaches us to seek joy and openness in our interactions, whether with family, friends, or colleagues.
    • By embodying the principles of friendship and support, we can create a harmonious environment that benefits everyone involved.
