यच चिद्धि त्वा जनाः इमे नानाः हवन्त ūtaye |अस्माकं ब्रह्मेदम इन्द्र भूत्वे तेऽहा विश्वा च वर्धनम् ||
yac cid dhi tvā janā ime nānā havanta ūtaye | asmākam brahmedam indra bhūtu te 'hā viśvā ca vardhanam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yac - whatever
    • cid - indeed
    • dhi - thought or intellect
    • tvā - you
    • janā - people or beings
    • ime - these
    • nānā - various or diverse
    • havanta - offering or invoking
    • ūtaye - for the purpose of elevation or upliftment
    • asmākam - our
    • brahm - the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit
    • edam - this
    • indra - the king of gods, representing strength and power
    • bhūtu - to become or manifest
    • te - your
    • 'hā - indeed
    • viśvā - all or universal
    • ca - and
    • vardhanam - growth or prosperity
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a collective invocation by people who are diverse in nature, seeking the upliftment and growth of their community.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity among various individuals in the pursuit of a common goal, which is the realization of the ultimate reality (Brahman) and the manifestation of strength (Indra).
    • The mantra suggests that through collective thought and intention, individuals can invoke higher powers for their benefit and the benefit of all.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, diverse teams often come together to achieve a common project goal. The mantra reflects how different skills and perspectives can contribute to the success of the project, much like the various offerings made to the divine.
    • In community service, individuals from different backgrounds unite to uplift their community, showcasing the essence of collective effort for a greater cause.
    • In spiritual gatherings, people come together to meditate or pray, invoking a higher power for guidance and support, which aligns with the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and collective effort in the pursuit of higher ideals and growth.
    • It teaches us that despite our differences, we can come together to invoke strength and prosperity, both individually and collectively.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork, community service, and spiritual practices, reminding us of the power of collective intention and action.
