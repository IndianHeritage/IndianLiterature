मन्त्र: स्थूरं राधः शताश्वं कुरुंगस्य दिविष्टिषु | राज्ञस तवेषस्य सुभगस्य रातिषु तुर्वशेष्व अमन्महि ||
sthūraṁ rādhaḥ śatāśvaṁ kuruṅgasya diviṣṭiṣu | rājñas tveṣasya subhagasya rātiṣu turvaśeṣv amanmahi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sthūraṁ: solid, strong, or substantial
    • rādhaḥ: the one who is praised or honored
    • śatāśvaṁ: having a hundred horses, symbolizing wealth and power
    • kuruṅgasya: of the deer, representing grace and agility
    • diviṣṭiṣu: in the heavens or celestial realms
    • rājñas: of the king or ruler
    • tveṣasya: of this one, referring to a specific entity or person
    • subhagasya: auspicious or fortunate
    • rātiṣu: in the gatherings or assemblies
    • turvaśeṣv: in the realms of the Turvaśa, a mythical group
    • amanmahi: we do not forget or we do not neglect
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of strength and honor, emphasizing the importance of being substantial and respected in society.
    • The mention of "a hundred horses" symbolizes immense wealth and power, which can be likened to modern-day success in business or leadership roles.
    • The reference to "deer" suggests the need for grace and agility in navigating life's challenges, similar to how athletes or performers must balance strength with finesse.
    • The phrase "in the heavens" indicates aspirations and high goals, akin to individuals striving for excellence in their careers or personal lives.
    • The focus on kings and rulers highlights the significance of leadership and the qualities that make a leader respected and fortunate.
    • The mantra concludes with a reminder to not forget the values and principles that guide us, which is essential in both personal and professional relationships.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A successful entrepreneur who builds a strong company (sthūraṁ) while being respected in their community (rādhaḥ) can be seen as embodying the essence of this mantra.
    • In sports, an athlete who combines strength (hundred horses) with agility (deer) exemplifies the balance needed to excel.
    • In leadership, a manager who is both powerful and respected (king) while maintaining a focus on team values (not forgetting) demonstrates the application of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the qualities that lead to success and respect in life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of strength, grace, and leadership while also highlighting the need to uphold values and principles.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in business, sports, or personal relationships, embodying these qualities can lead to a fulfilling and respected life.
