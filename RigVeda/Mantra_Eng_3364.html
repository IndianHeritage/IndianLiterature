आ हि ष्मा याती नर्यश्चिकित्वान्हूयमानः सोत्रभिरुप यज्ञम् |स्वश्वो यो अभीरुर् मन्यमानः सुष्वाणेभिर् मदति सं ह वीरैः ||
ā hi ṣmā yāti naryaś cikitvān hūyamānaḥ sotṛbhir upa yajñam | svaśvo yo abhīrur manyamānaḥ suṣvāṇebhir madati saṁ ha vīraiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā hi: indeed, truly
    • ṣmā: we are, we shall be
    • yāti: going, moving
    • naryaś: the noble ones, the leaders
    • cikitvān: being wise, thoughtful
    • hūyamānaḥ: being invoked, called upon
    • sotṛbhir: with the hearers, those who listen
    • upa: towards, near
    • yajñam: the sacrifice, the offering
    • svaśvo: our own, belonging to us
    • yo: who
    • abhīrur: is fearless, brave
    • manyamānaḥ: being considered, thought of
    • suṣvāṇebhir: with good qualities, virtuous
    • madati: rejoices, is delighted
    • saṁ: together, with
    • ha: indeed, truly
    • vīraiḥ: with heroes, the brave ones
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the importance of community and collective action.
    • It emphasizes the role of wise leaders who guide and inspire others.
    • The invocation of the sacrifice suggests a call to participate in something greater than oneself.
    • The mention of fearlessness and virtue highlights the qualities that are essential for success and harmony.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a manager who leads with wisdom and encourages teamwork can create a productive environment.
    • In community service, individuals coming together to support a cause exemplify the spirit of collective sacrifice.
    • In sports, a coach who inspires players to be brave and work together can lead the team to victory.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the power of unity, wisdom, and courage in achieving common goals.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as leadership, teamwork, and community engagement.
    • By embodying the qualities of fearlessness and virtue, individuals can contribute to a harmonious and successful society.
