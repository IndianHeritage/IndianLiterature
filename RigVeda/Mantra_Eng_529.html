यद् देवानां मित्रमहः पुरोहितोऽन्तरो यासि दूत्यम् |सिन्धोर iva प्रस्वनितास उर्मयोऽग्नेर भ्राजन्ते अर्चयः ||
yad devānām mitramahaḥ purohito 'ntaro yāsi dūtyam | sindhor iva prasvanitāsa ūrmayo 'gner bhrājante arcayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • devānām: of the gods
    • mitramahaḥ: the great friend
    • purohito: the priest or the one who performs rituals
    • 'ntaro: within
    • yāsi: you go
    • dūtyam: as a messenger
    • sindhor: of the ocean
    • iva: like
    • prasvanitāsa: the waves that rise
    • ūrmayo: of the waves
    • 'gner: of fire
    • bhrājante: shine or radiate
    • arcayaḥ: worship or praise
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine connection between the gods and the rituals performed by priests.
    • The phrase "yad devānām mitramahaḥ" suggests that the gods are seen as great friends, indicating a relationship of trust and support.
    • The term "purohito" refers to the priest who acts as a mediator between the divine and the human realms.
    • When it mentions "dūtyam," it highlights the role of the priest as a messenger, conveying the prayers and offerings of the people to the gods.
    • The imagery of "sindhor iva prasvanitāsa ūrmayo" compares the divine messages to the rising waves of the ocean, suggesting that they are powerful and dynamic.
    • The reference to "agner bhrājante" indicates that the divine presence shines brightly, much like fire, symbolizing purity and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern spiritual practices, individuals often seek guidance from spiritual leaders or mentors, akin to the role of the purohito, who helps them navigate their spiritual journeys.
    • In community rituals, the priest acts as a bridge between the community and the divine, similar to how the mantra describes the priest's role.
    • The concept of divine friendship can be seen in various cultures where people pray to their deities for support and guidance, reflecting the trust and relationship emphasized in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of the relationship between humans and the divine, highlighting the role of rituals and the priest as a mediator.
    • Its significance lies in the understanding that divine support is always available, and through rituals, individuals can connect with the divine energy that guides and protects them.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to seek spiritual guidance and to recognize the power of community and ritual in fostering a connection with the divine.
