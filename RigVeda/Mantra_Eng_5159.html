जुषस्व नः समिधम् अग्ने अद्य शोचाऽ बृहद् यजतं धूमम् ऋण्वन् |उप स्पृश दिव्यं सानु स्तूपैः सं रश्मिभिः तातनः सूर्यस्य ||
juṣasva naḥ samidham agne adya śocā bṛhad yajataṁ dhūmam ṛṇvan | upa spṛśa divyaṁ sānu stūpaiḥ saṁ raśmibhis tatanaḥ sūryasya ||
  • juṣasva: This word translates to "may you be pleased" or "may you accept." It signifies a call for divine favor and acceptance.
  • naḥ: Meaning "our," it indicates a collective appeal, suggesting that the speaker is addressing a higher power on behalf of a group.
  • samidham: Refers to "the fuel" or "the offerings." In a ritual context, this represents the materials used for sacrifice or worship.
  • agne: This is a direct address to Agni, the fire god, who symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine messenger between humans and the gods.
  • adya: Meaning "today," it emphasizes the immediacy of the request or offering being made.
  • śocā: Translates to "shining" or "radiant," indicating the brightness and purity associated with the divine.
  • bṛhad: Meaning "great" or "vast," it signifies the importance and magnitude of the offering or the divine presence being invoked.
  • yajataṁ: This word means "to be worshipped" or "to be sacrificed," indicating the act of devotion and reverence towards the divine.
  • dhūmam: Refers to "smoke," which is often associated with offerings made to the fire, symbolizing the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • ṛṇvan: Meaning "with a debt," it suggests a sense of obligation or gratitude towards the divine for blessings received.
  • upa: Translates to "near" or "approach," indicating a desire for closeness to the divine.
  • spṛśa: Meaning "touch" or "influence," it signifies the wish for divine presence to be felt in a tangible way.
  • divyaṁ: This word means "divine" or "heavenly," emphasizing the sacred nature of the request.
  • sānu: Meaning "with reverence" or "with respect," it indicates the manner in which the offering is made.
  • stūpaiḥ: Refers to "with praises" or "with hymns," suggesting that the offering is accompanied by words of adoration.
  • saṁ: Meaning "together" or "with," it indicates a collective effort in worship.
  • raśmibhis: Translates to "rays" or "light," often associated with the sun and divine illumination.
  • tatanaḥ: Meaning "of the sun," it connects the offering to solar energy and its life-giving properties.
  • sūryasya: Refers to "of the sun," reinforcing the connection to solar divinity and its significance in life.

Conclusion: This mantra is a heartfelt invocation to Agni, the fire god, asking for his acceptance of offerings made with reverence and gratitude. It emphasizes the importance of divine connection and the role of fire as a medium between humans and the divine. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, ceremonies, or personal prayers where individuals seek blessings, guidance, and a deeper connection with the divine. It serves as a reminder of the importance of gratitude and reverence in our daily lives, encouraging us to acknowledge the divine presence in our actions and offerings.
