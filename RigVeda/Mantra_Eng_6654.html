ते नो गोपा अपाच्यास त उदक त इथ्था न्यक् |पुरस्तात सर्वयाऽविशा ||
te no gopā apācyās ta udak ta itthā nyak | purastāt sarvayā viśā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • te: you (plural)
    • no: our
    • gopā: protectors or guardians
    • apācyās: from the west
    • ta: those
    • udak: water
    • ta: those
    • itthā: thus
    • nyak: in the right way
    • purastāt: in front
    • sarvayā: all
    • viśā: beings or creatures
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the protectors or guardians, asking them to safeguard us from the west.
    • The mention of "water" signifies purity and life, suggesting that the guardians should protect our essential needs.
    • The phrase "in the right way" emphasizes the importance of righteousness and ethical conduct in our lives.
    • By addressing "all beings," it highlights the interconnectedness of life and the need for universal protection and harmony.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied when people come together to protect their environment, ensuring that natural resources like water are preserved for future generations.
    • In personal life, individuals can reflect on this mantra when making decisions that affect not only themselves but also their families and communities, ensuring that their actions are ethical and beneficial to all.
    • In a corporate context, businesses can adopt this mantra by prioritizing corporate social responsibility, protecting the interests of all stakeholders, including employees, customers, and the environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for protection and ethical conduct, reminding us of our responsibilities towards ourselves and others.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal decision-making to community initiatives and corporate practices.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a holistic approach to life, where the well-being of all beings is considered, fostering a sense of unity and shared responsibility.
