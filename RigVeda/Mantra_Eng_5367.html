इन्द्रं नरो नेमधिता हवन्ते यत् पार्यां युनजते धियस् ताः |शूरो नृषातां शवसश चकान आ गोमती व्रजे भजाऽ त्वं नः ||
indraṁ naro nemadhitā havante yat pāryā yunajate dhiyas tāḥ | śūro nṛṣātā śavasaś cakāna ā gomati vraje bhajā tvaṁ naḥ ||
  • Indraṁ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Naro: Means "men" or "humans," indicating the collective strength and unity of people.
  • Nemadhitā: Translates to "those who are guided" or "those who are directed," suggesting the importance of guidance and direction in life.
  • Havante: Means "they invoke" or "they call upon," indicating the act of seeking help or blessings from a higher power.
  • Yat: Means "that which," introducing a clause that describes the actions or qualities being referred to.
  • Pāryā: Refers to "the wise" or "the intelligent," emphasizing the value of wisdom and intellect.
  • Yunajate: Means "they unite" or "they come together," highlighting the importance of collaboration and unity.
  • Dhiyas: Refers to "thoughts" or "intellect," indicating the role of mental faculties in achieving goals.
  • Śūro: Means "the brave" or "the heroic," celebrating courage and valor in individuals.
  • Nṛṣātā: Refers to "the people" or "the community," emphasizing the collective strength of society.
  • Śavasaś: Means "those who are strong" or "those who are powerful," reinforcing the theme of strength.
  • Cakāna: Means "they have achieved" or "they have accomplished," indicating success and achievement.
  • Ā: A preposition meaning "to" or "towards," indicating direction or purpose.
  • Gomati: Refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," often associated with cattle in Vedic culture, symbolizing abundance.
  • Vraje: Means "in the pasture" or "in the field," suggesting a place of growth and nurturing.
  • Bhajā: Means "to worship" or "to serve," indicating devotion and reverence.
  • Tvaṁ: Means "you," referring to Indra, the deity being addressed.
  • Naḥ: Means "our," indicating a collective plea or request from the community.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, strength, and wisdom in the pursuit of success and prosperity. It calls upon Indra, the divine leader, to guide and bless the collective efforts of humanity. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork in organizations, community development projects, or even in personal endeavors where collaboration and shared goals are essential. The mantra serves as a reminder that when individuals come together with a common purpose, guided by wisdom and courage, they can achieve great things and foster prosperity for all.
