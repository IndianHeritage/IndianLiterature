सं यत त इन्द्र मन्यवः सं चक्राणि दधन्विरे |अध तवे अध सूर्ये ||
saṁ yat ta indra manyavaḥ saṁ cakrāṇi dadhanvire | adha tve adha sūrye ||
  • saṁ yat: This phrase can be translated as "that which is together" or "that which is united." It signifies the importance of unity and collective strength.
  • ta indra manyavaḥ: "Indra" refers to the king of the gods, often associated with strength and power. "Manyavaḥ" means "thoughts" or "intentions." This part emphasizes the need for powerful intentions and thoughts, akin to the leadership qualities of Indra.
  • saṁ cakrāṇi dadhanvire: "Cakra" means "circle" or "wheel," and "dadhanvire" refers to "those who wield" or "those who hold." This suggests the idea of wielding power or control over cycles of life and nature, much like how a leader manages their domain.
  • adha tve adha sūrye: "Adha" means "below" or "down," while "sūrye" refers to the "sun." This can be interpreted as a call to bring forth light and clarity from below, suggesting that true strength and enlightenment come from understanding and harnessing the foundational aspects of life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of unity, powerful intentions, and the ability to wield control over life's cycles. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in leadership contexts, where a leader must unite their team with a shared vision and strong intentions. For example, in a corporate setting, a manager who fosters collaboration and encourages innovative thinking can lead their team to success, much like Indra leading the gods. The mantra also reminds us that true enlightenment and strength come from understanding our roots and the foundational aspects of our lives, encouraging us to seek knowledge and clarity in our pursuits.
