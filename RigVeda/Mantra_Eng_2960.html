नासत्याऽ मे पितरā बन्धुपृच्छा सजात्यम् अश्विनोश् चारु नाम |युवं हि स्थो रयिदौ नो रयीणां दात्रं रक्षेते अकवैर अदब्धा ||
nāsatyā me pitarā bandhupṛcchā sajātyam aśvinoś cāru nāma | yuvaṁ hi stho rayidau no rayīṇāṁ dātraṁ rakṣethe akavair adabdhā ||
  • nāsatyā me pitarā - "O my parents, who are not deceitful" - This phrase emphasizes the importance of truthfulness and integrity in relationships. In real life, this can be seen in the bond between parents and children, where honesty forms the foundation of trust.
  • bandhupṛcchā - "inquire about my relatives" - This suggests the significance of family and community ties. For example, in many cultures, family gatherings and celebrations are essential for maintaining these connections.
  • sajātyam - "of the same kind" - This refers to the idea of unity and shared values among people. In a workplace, for instance, teams that share common goals and values tend to work more effectively together.
  • aśvinoś cāru nāma - "the beautiful name of the Ashvins" - The Ashvins are twin deities associated with health and medicine. This highlights the importance of well-being and the pursuit of beauty in life. In modern times, this can relate to the emphasis on mental and physical health.
  • yuvaṁ hi stho - "for you are young" - This acknowledges the vitality and potential of youth. In real life, this can be seen in the encouragement of young people to pursue their dreams and ambitions, fostering innovation and progress.
  • rayidau no rayīṇāṁ - "may you protect our wealth" - This line speaks to the desire for prosperity and security. In practical terms, this can relate to financial planning and the importance of safeguarding one's resources for future generations.
  • dātraṁ rakṣethe - "may you protect the giver" - This emphasizes the value of generosity and the protection of those who share their resources. In everyday life, this can be seen in charitable acts and community support systems.
  • akavair adabdhā - "unharmed by enemies" - This line conveys a wish for safety and protection from harm. In a broader context, this can relate to the importance of peace and security in society, as well as personal safety in daily life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates themes of truth, family, unity, health, youth, prosperity, generosity, and protection. Its significance lies in the reminder of the values that bind us together as individuals and communities. In real life, these principles can guide our actions and relationships, fostering a harmonious and supportive environment. By embodying these ideals, we contribute to a more compassionate and resilient society.
