इह प्रजाम् इह रयिं रराणाऽ इह श्रवो वीरवत् तक्षता नः |येन वयं चितयेमात्य अन्यान तं वाजं चित्रम ऋभवो ददात नः ||
iha prajām iha rayiṁ rarāṇā iha śravo vīravat takṣatā naḥ | yena vayaṁ citayemāty anyān taṁ vājaṁ citram ṛbhavo dadā naḥ ||
  • iha prajām: This phrase translates to "here, the progeny" or "here, the people." It emphasizes the importance of community and the collective well-being of society.
  • iha rayiṁ rarāṇā: This means "here, wealth is to be cultivated." It suggests the need to nurture and grow resources, both material and spiritual, for the benefit of all.
  • iha śravo vīravat takṣatā naḥ: This translates to "here, may we be endowed with strength and valor." It calls for the acquisition of courage and strength to face challenges and adversities.
  • yena vayaṁ citayemāty anyān: This means "by which we may think of others." It highlights the importance of empathy and consideration for others in our thoughts and actions.
  • taṁ vājaṁ citram ṛbhavo dadā naḥ: This translates to "may that splendid wealth be given to us." It expresses a desire for prosperity and abundance that is beneficial and uplifting.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of community, prosperity, strength, and empathy. It serves as a reminder that individual success is intertwined with the well-being of others.
  • Real-life Application: In modern society, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:
    • Community Development: Initiatives that focus on collective growth, such as local businesses supporting each other or community gardens that provide food for all.
    • Teamwork: In workplaces, fostering a culture of collaboration where team members support each other to achieve common goals.
    • Social Responsibility: Encouraging individuals and corporations to give back to society, ensuring that their success contributes to the welfare of the community.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a guiding principle for living harmoniously within a community. It emphasizes that true wealth and strength come from nurturing relationships and supporting one another. By applying these teachings in our daily lives, we can create a more compassionate and prosperous society.
