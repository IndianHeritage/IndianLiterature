दश क्षिपः पूर्व्यं सीम अजीजनन सुजातम् मातृषु प्रियम् |अग्निं स्तुहि दैववातं देवश्रवो यो जनानाम् असद वशी ||
daśa kṣipaḥ pūrvyaṁ sīm ajījanan sujātam mātṛṣu priyam | agniṁ stuhi daivavātaṁ devaśravo yo janānām asad vaśī ||
  • daśa kṣipaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "ten swift ones." In a broader context, it may refer to the ten directions or aspects of life that one should be aware of. For example, in real life, this can relate to the tenets of good living, such as honesty, integrity, compassion, and wisdom.
  • pūrvyaṁ sīm ajījanan: This translates to "the boundary that was created in the past." It signifies the importance of respecting traditions and the foundations laid by our ancestors. In modern life, this can be seen in how cultural practices and family values shape our identities and decisions.
  • su jātam mātṛṣu priyam: This means "well-born and dear to mothers." It emphasizes the value of nurturing and the importance of maternal love and care. In real life, this can be reflected in the way we cherish and honor our mothers and caregivers, recognizing their role in our development and well-being.
  • agniṁ stuhi: This phrase translates to "praise the fire." Fire is often seen as a symbol of transformation and purification. In everyday life, this can relate to the idea of overcoming challenges and emerging stronger, much like how fire transforms raw materials into something valuable.
  • daivavātaṁ devaśravo: This can be interpreted as "the divine wind that brings fame." It suggests the importance of divine blessings and the pursuit of a life that is recognized for its virtues. In practical terms, this can be seen in how we strive for excellence in our endeavors, seeking recognition for our hard work and integrity.
  • yo janānām asad vaśī: This translates to "who controls the untruthful." It highlights the need for discernment and the ability to navigate through falsehoods in life. In a real-world context, this can be applied to the importance of critical thinking and the pursuit of truth in an age of misinformation.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates essential values such as respect for tradition, the significance of nurturing relationships, the transformative power of challenges, the pursuit of excellence, and the importance of truth. In our daily lives, these principles can guide us in making ethical decisions, fostering meaningful connections, and striving for personal growth. By embodying these teachings, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling existence, both individually and collectively.
