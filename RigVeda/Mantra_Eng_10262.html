तां वाम मित्रावरुणा धारयत्क्षिती सुषुम्नेषितत्वता यजामसि |युवोः क्राणाय सख्यैर अभि ष्यामा रक्षसः ||
tā vām mitrāvaruṇā dhārayatkṣitī suṣumneṣitatvatā yajāmasi | yuvoḥ krāṇāya sakhyair abhi ṣyāma rakṣasaḥ ||
  • tā vām mitrāvaruṇā: This phrase can be translated as "May those two, Mitra and Varuna, support us." Mitra and Varuna are deities representing the sun and the cosmic order, respectively. In real life, this can be seen as a call for harmony and balance in our relationships and interactions with others.
  • dhārayatkṣitī: This means "may the earth support us." It emphasizes the importance of nature and the environment in our lives. For example, sustainable practices in agriculture and conservation efforts reflect this idea of being supported by the earth.
  • suṣumneṣitatvatā: This can be interpreted as "with the best of intentions." It suggests that our actions should be guided by positive and noble intentions. In everyday life, this can relate to acts of kindness and integrity in our personal and professional dealings.
  • yajāmasi: This translates to "we perform rituals." It signifies the importance of rituals and traditions in maintaining cultural identity and community bonds. For instance, family gatherings during festivals can be seen as a way to strengthen relationships and honor traditions.
  • yuvoḥ krāṇāya: This means "for the young." It highlights the significance of youth and the future generation. In real life, this can be reflected in educational initiatives and mentorship programs aimed at empowering young people.
  • sakhyair abhi ṣyāma: This translates to "may we be united in friendship." It emphasizes the value of friendship and collaboration. In a practical sense, this can be seen in teamwork in workplaces or community service projects where individuals come together for a common cause.
  • rakṣasaḥ: This word means "from evil or harm." It signifies a plea for protection from negative influences. In real life, this can relate to the importance of creating safe environments, whether at home, in schools, or in communities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of nature, community, and individual intentions. Its significance lies in the call for harmony, support, and protection in our lives. By applying these principles, we can foster a more compassionate and sustainable world, where relationships are nurtured, and the environment is respected. This mantra serves as a reminder to act with integrity and to prioritize the well-being of ourselves and others, ultimately contributing to a more peaceful and united society.
