तदिद आस bhuवनेषु ज्येष्ठं यतो जजña उग्रस्त्वेषनृम्णः |सद्योजजñानो निऋणाति शत्रून् अनु यं विश्वे मदन्त्यूमाः ||
tad id āsa bhuvaneṣu jyeṣṭhaṁ yato jajña ugras tveṣanṛmṇaḥ | sadyo jajñāno ni riṇāti śatrūn anu yaṁ viśve madanty ūmāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • id: indeed
    • āsa: is/exists
    • bhuvaneṣu: in the worlds
    • jyeṣṭham: the greatest
    • yato: from which
    • jajña: was born
    • ugras: fierce/strong
    • tveṣanṛmṇaḥ: of the powerful
    • sadyo: immediately
    • jajñāno: those who are born
    • ni: not
    • riṇāti: are defeated
    • śatrūn: enemies
    • anu: after
    • yaṁ: whom
    • viśve: all
    • madanty: rejoice
    • ūmāḥ: the divine beings
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a supreme power or entity that is the greatest among all in the universe.
    • It emphasizes the origin of strength and power, suggesting that from this source, fierce and powerful beings are born.
    • The mantra conveys that those who are born from this source are not defeated by their enemies.
    • It also highlights the joy and celebration of all divine beings in relation to this supreme power.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a strong leader can be seen as the "greatest" source of inspiration and strength for their team. Just as the mantra suggests, a good leader helps their team overcome challenges (enemies) and fosters a sense of unity and joy among team members.
    • In personal life, individuals who draw strength from their values or beliefs (the supreme power) often find themselves resilient in the face of adversity, not easily defeated by challenges.
    • In community settings, leaders or influential figures who embody strength and wisdom can uplift and inspire others, leading to collective joy and success.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the source of strength and resilience in our lives.
    • It teaches us to recognize and connect with the greater powers that inspire and empower us, whether they be spiritual, personal, or communal.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals and communities to draw upon their strengths and support one another in overcoming challenges, leading to collective joy and success.
