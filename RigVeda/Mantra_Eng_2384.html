उदु ष्य देवः सविता सवाय शश्वत्तमं तदपाव वाह्निर अस्तात् |नूनं देवभ्यो वि हि धाति रत्नम अथाभजद वीतिहोत्रं स्वस्तौ ||
ud u ṣya devaḥ savitā savāya śaśvattamaṁ tadapā vahnir asthāt | nūnaṁ devebhyo vi hi dhāti ratnam athābhajad vītihotraṁ svastau ||
  • ud u ṣya devaḥ savitā savāya śaśvattamaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "The divine Savita, the radiant one, who is eternal and supreme." Here, "Savita" refers to the Sun, symbolizing light, energy, and life. In real life, the Sun is essential for growth, agriculture, and sustaining life on Earth.
  • tadapā vahnir asthāt: This means "that fire (vahnir) has been established." Fire is often seen as a purifying force and a source of energy. In practical terms, fire is used in various rituals and daily activities, such as cooking and heating, symbolizing transformation and renewal.
  • nūnaṁ devebhyo vi hi dhāti ratnam: This translates to "Indeed, it is the deities who bestow the jewel (ratnam)." This jewel symbolizes blessings, prosperity, and wisdom. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way people seek blessings from higher powers for success in their endeavors, whether in business, education, or personal growth.
  • athābhajad vītihotraṁ svastau: This means "and thus, the one who performs the Vītihotra (a specific ritual) attains well-being (svastau)." The Vītihotra is a ritual that involves offerings to the deities, promoting harmony and balance in life. In real life, rituals and practices that promote mindfulness and gratitude can lead to a sense of well-being and community.

  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine energy, the transformative power of fire, and the blessings bestowed by the deities. It highlights the significance of rituals in achieving well-being and prosperity. In real life, this can be applied through practices that honor nature, seek blessings, and promote community well-being, reminding us of our connection to the divine and the importance of gratitude in our daily lives.
