समुद्रज्येष्ठाः सलिलस्य मध्यात पुनानां यन्त्य अनिविशमानाः | इन्द्रो या वज्री वृषभो रराद ता आपो देवीर इह माम अवन्तु ||
samudrajyeṣṭhāḥ salilasya madhyāt punānā yanty aniviśamānāḥ | indro yā vajrī vṛṣabho rarāda tā āpo devīr iha mām avantu ||
  • Mantra: "samudrajyeṣṭhāḥ salilasya madhyāt punānā yanty aniviśamānāḥ | indro yā vajrī vṛṣabho rarāda tā āpo devīr iha mām avantu ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samudrajyeṣṭhāḥ: Those who are in the ocean or the great waters.
    • salilasya: Of the waters.
    • madhyāt: From the middle or center.
    • punānā: Purifying or cleansing.
    • yanty: They go or move.
    • aniviśamānāḥ: Not being obstructed or hindered.
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods.
    • yā: Who.
    • vajrī: The one who wields the thunderbolt.
    • vṛṣabho: The bull, symbolizing strength.
    • rarāda: Roared or thundered.
    • tā: Those.
    • āpo: Waters or divine waters.
    • devīr: Goddesses or divine beings.
    • iha: Here.
    • mām: Me.
    • avantu: May they protect or guide.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine powers of the waters, symbolizing their purifying and nurturing qualities.
    • The phrase "samudrajyeṣṭhāḥ salilasya madhyāt" suggests that those who dwell in the depths of the ocean are called upon for their cleansing abilities.
    • The idea of "punānā" emphasizes the importance of purification, which can be related to personal growth and spiritual cleansing in our lives.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents strength and protection, and his association with the waters signifies the power of nature in providing sustenance and safety.
    • The mention of "āpo devīr" highlights the divine aspect of water, reminding us of its sacredness and the need to respect and protect our natural resources.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, water is seen as a source of life and is used in rituals for purification, such as baptism in Christianity or the Ganga Aarti in Hinduism.
    • Environmental movements often emphasize the importance of clean water, reflecting the mantra's call for protection and reverence for water bodies.
    • In personal development, the concept of cleansing oneself from negativity can be likened to the purifying qualities of water, encouraging individuals to seek emotional and spiritual renewal.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the vital role that water plays in our lives, both physically and spiritually.
    • It encourages us to seek purification and protection from the divine, while also urging us to respect and care for our natural environment.
    • In a world facing water scarcity and pollution, the significance of this mantra is more relevant than ever, calling for collective action to preserve our water resources for future generations.
