अधेनुं दास्रा स्तर्यं विषक्ताम अपिन्वतं शयवे अश्विनां गाम् |युवं शचीभिर विमदाया जायाṁ न्यूहतुः पुरुमित्रस्य योषाम् ||
adhenuṁ dasrā starya1ṁ viṣaktām apinvataṁ śayave aśvinā gām | yuvaṁ śacībhir vimadāya jāyāṁ ny ūhathuḥ purumitrasya yoṣām ||
  • adhenuṁ dasrā staryaṁ viṣaktām apinvataṁ śayave aśvinā gām
    • adhenuṁ: This word refers to a cow, which is often seen as a symbol of nourishment and abundance in Vedic literature.
    • dasrā: This term can be interpreted as 'ten', which may symbolize completeness or wholeness.
    • staryaṁ: This word means 'to be strong' or 'to be powerful', indicating vitality and energy.
    • viṣaktām: This can be understood as 'attached' or 'connected', suggesting a bond or relationship.
    • apinvataṁ: This term means 'to be filled' or 'to be satisfied', indicating fulfillment.
    • śayave: This word means 'to lie down' or 'to rest', which can symbolize peace and tranquility.
    • aśvinā: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities of health and medicine in Vedic mythology, representing healing and well-being.
    • gām: This means 'the village' or 'the community', emphasizing the importance of social connections.
  • yuvaṁ śacībhir vimadāya jāyāṁ ny ūhathuḥ purumitrasya yoṣām
    • yuvaṁ: This word means 'young' or 'youthful', symbolizing vitality and potential.
    • śacībhir: This term refers to 'with power' or 'with strength', indicating the importance of empowerment.
    • vimadāya: This can be interpreted as 'to bring joy' or 'to create happiness', emphasizing the role of positivity.
    • jāyāṁ: This word means 'wife' or 'partner', highlighting the significance of relationships.
    • ny ūhathuḥ: This means 'to bring together' or 'to unite', indicating the importance of community and collaboration.
    • purumitrasya: This term refers to 'of the good friend' or 'of the noble one', emphasizing the value of friendship and support.
    • yoṣām: This means 'of women', highlighting the role of women in society and relationships.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of community, relationships, and the importance of nurturing bonds. It highlights the significance of strength, fulfillment, and joy in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to the importance of family and community support, the empowerment of individuals, especially women, and the need for collaboration and unity in achieving common goals. The mantra serves as a reminder to cherish relationships, promote well-being, and foster a sense of belonging in our communities.
