अन्व आह मासा अन्व इद वनान्य अन्व ओषधीर अनु पर्वतासः |अन्व इन्द्रं रोदसी वावशाने अन्व आपो अजिहत जायमानम् ||
anv aha māsā anv id vanāny anv oṣadhīr anu parvatāsaḥ | anv indraṁ rodasī vāvaśāne anv āpo ajihata jāyamānam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • anv: following, in accordance with
    • aha: day
    • māsā: months
    • id: this
    • vanāny: forests
    • oṣadhīr: herbs, plants
    • anu: after, along with
    • parvatāsaḥ: mountains
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods
    • rodasī: the two worlds, the sky and the earth
    • vāvaśāne: may we attain
    • āpo: waters
    • ajihata: unrestrained, flowing
    • jāyamānam: being born, emerging
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the interconnectedness of various elements of nature, emphasizing the cyclical nature of life.
    • The phrase "anv aha māsā" suggests that each day and month follows a natural order, reflecting the passage of time.
    • By mentioning "vanāny" and "oṣadhīr," it highlights the importance of forests and plants in sustaining life.
    • The reference to "parvatāsaḥ" indicates the mountains as a source of strength and stability in the natural world.
    • Invoking "Indra," the mantra seeks blessings for prosperity and protection from the divine.
    • The mention of "rodasī" signifies the duality of existence, encompassing both the earthly and celestial realms.
    • Finally, "āpo ajihata" emphasizes the vital role of water in nurturing life, symbolizing purity and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, understanding the cycles of nature (days and months) is crucial for planting and harvesting crops, ensuring food security.
    • Forests and plants provide essential resources such as oxygen, medicine, and materials, highlighting the need for environmental conservation.
    • Mountains often serve as natural barriers and sources of water, playing a vital role in ecosystems and human settlements.
    • In many cultures, invoking deities like Indra during rituals symbolizes a request for rain and fertility, reflecting the reliance on divine forces for agricultural success.
    • Water bodies are essential for life, and their preservation is critical in combating climate change and ensuring sustainable development.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of harmony between humans and nature, urging respect and reverence for the environment.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder of our dependence on natural elements for survival and prosperity.
    • In contemporary society, this mantra can inspire sustainable practices, environmental awareness, and a deeper connection to the earth.
    • By acknowledging the cycles of nature and the divine, we can foster a more balanced and respectful relationship with our surroundings.
