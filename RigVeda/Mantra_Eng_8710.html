आ सोम सुवानो अद्रिभिस तिरो वाराण्य अव्यया |जनो न पुरी चम्वोर विशद धरीः सदो वनेषु दधिषे ||
ā soma suvāno adribhis tiro vārāṇy avyayā | jano na puri camvor viśad dhariḥ sado vaneṣu dadhiṣe ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā soma: Refers to the sacred drink 'Soma', which is often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • suvāno: Means 'well-formed' or 'good', indicating something that is beneficial or auspicious.
    • adribhis: Translates to 'from the mountains', symbolizing strength and stability.
    • tiro: Means 'beyond' or 'across', suggesting transcendence or moving past limitations.
    • vārāṇy: Refers to 'the protectors' or 'guardians', indicating a sense of safety and security.
    • avyayā: Means 'imperishable' or 'eternal', signifying something that lasts forever.
    • jano: Refers to 'people' or 'human beings', emphasizing the collective aspect of humanity.
    • na puri: Translates to 'not in the city', suggesting a return to nature or simplicity.
    • camvor: Means 'of the brave' or 'valiant', highlighting courage and strength.
    • viśad: Refers to 'clear' or 'pure', indicating clarity of thought or purpose.
    • dhariḥ: Means 'bearer' or 'holder', suggesting responsibility or guardianship.
    • sado: Refers to 'in the abode' or 'home', indicating a place of comfort and belonging.
    • vaneṣu: Means 'in the forests', symbolizing nature and the wild.
    • dadhiṣe: Translates to 'you bestow', indicating a sense of giving or sharing.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine essence of Soma, which is not just a drink but a symbol of spiritual nourishment and enlightenment.
    • The reference to mountains signifies strength and stability, suggesting that true wisdom and inspiration come from solid foundations.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and the collective strength of humanity, especially in times of adversity.
    • The contrast between urban life ('na puri') and the natural world ('vaneṣu') highlights the need to reconnect with nature for clarity and purity of thought.
    • The mantra calls upon the guardians ('vārāṇy') to protect and bestow blessings upon the people, indicating a reliance on divine protection and guidance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, many people seek spiritual practices that connect them with nature, such as hiking or meditation in forests, reflecting the mantra's call to return to the natural world.
    • Communities often come together in times of crisis, showcasing the collective strength and bravery ('camvor') of humanity, much like the mantra suggests.
    • Incorporating rituals that honor nature and seek blessings can be seen in various cultures, where offerings are made to ensure protection and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of spiritual nourishment, community strength, and the need to reconnect with nature.
    • Its significance lies in its call for clarity, courage, and the recognition of the eternal aspects of life, encouraging individuals to seek balance between the material and spiritual worlds.
    • In real life, it can inspire practices that promote well-being, environmental consciousness, and a sense of belonging within a community.
