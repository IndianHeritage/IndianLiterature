स त्वं अग्ने प्रतीकैना प्रत्योष यातुधान्यः |उरुक्षयेṣu दीद्यत ||
sa tvam agne pratīkena praty oṣa yātudhānyaḥ | urukṣayeṣu dīdyat ||
  • sa tvam agne: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni." Agni is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine spark within all beings. In real life, Agni can be seen as a metaphor for the inner drive or passion that fuels our actions and aspirations.
  • pratīkena: This means "by the sign" or "by the symbol." It suggests that there are deeper meanings and representations behind our actions and experiences. For example, a simple act of kindness can symbolize compassion and humanity, serving as a sign of our values.
  • praty oṣa yātudhānyaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who brings forth the harvest." Here, "yātudhānyaḥ" refers to the one who provides sustenance and abundance. In a practical sense, this can relate to the efforts we put into our work and relationships, which yield fruitful results over time.
  • urukṣayeṣu: This translates to "in the vast fields." It symbolizes the expansive opportunities and potential that life offers us. Just as a farmer cultivates a vast field, we too must cultivate our skills and relationships to reap the benefits.
  • dīdyat: This means "to shine" or "to illuminate." It signifies the importance of enlightenment and clarity in our lives. For instance, gaining knowledge through education can illuminate our path and help us make informed decisions.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of inner fire (Agni) as a driving force in our lives, the importance of symbols and signs in understanding our experiences, and the need to cultivate our potential in the vast fields of life. It teaches us that through our efforts and enlightenment, we can achieve abundance and fulfillment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue their passions, recognize the deeper meanings in their actions, and strive for personal growth and success.
