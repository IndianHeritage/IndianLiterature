अयम् इन्द्र वृषाकपिः परस्वन्तं हतम् विदत् |असिं सूनां नवं चरुम् आद एधस्यान आचितं विश्वस्माद इन्द्र उत्तरः ||
ayam indra vṛṣākapiḥ parasvantaṁ hataṁ vidat | asiṁ sūnāṁ navaṁ carum ād edhasyāna ācitaṁ viśvasmād indra uttaraḥ ||
  • ayam indra vṛṣākapiḥ: This phrase refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with strength and power. The term "vṛṣākapiḥ" can be interpreted as "the one who is strong like a bull" or "the one who brings rain." Indra is often invoked for his might and ability to protect and provide.
  • parasvantaṁ hataṁ vidat: This part translates to "knowing the one who has been struck down." It suggests a recognition of challenges or adversities faced by individuals or communities. In real life, this can relate to understanding the struggles of others and the importance of empathy and support.
  • asiṁ sūnāṁ navaṁ carum: Here, "asiṁ" means "sword," and "sūnāṁ" refers to "of the strong." "Navaṁ carum" can be interpreted as "newly sharpened." This symbolizes readiness for battle or challenges. In everyday life, this can represent the preparation and resilience needed to face obstacles, whether in personal endeavors or societal issues.
  • ād edhasyāna ācitaṁ: This phrase can be understood as "having gathered strength." It emphasizes the importance of collective effort and unity in overcoming difficulties. In a real-world context, this can be seen in community initiatives where people come together to support a common cause, such as disaster relief or social justice movements.
  • viśvasmād indra uttaraḥ: This translates to "Indra, the one who rises above all." It signifies triumph and the ability to overcome challenges. In life, this can be reflected in personal achievements, where individuals rise above their circumstances through determination and hard work.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, resilience, and the importance of community support in overcoming adversities. It serves as a reminder that, like Indra, we can rise above challenges by recognizing the struggles of others, preparing ourselves for battles, and uniting our strengths. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to work together towards common goals, fostering empathy and collective action in the face of difficulties.
