पुषा त्वेतो नयतु हस्तगृह्याश्विनाऽ त्वा प्र वहतां रथेन |गृहान गच्छ गृहपत्नी यथासो वशिनि त्वं विदथम आ वदासि ||
pūṣā tveto nayatu hastagṛhyāśvinā tvā pra vahatāṁ rathena | gṛhān gaccha gṛhapatnī yathāso vaśinī tvaṁ vidatham ā vadāsi ||
  • pūṣā: This word refers to the deity Pūṣan, who is associated with nourishment, prosperity, and the protection of travelers. In real life, this can be likened to the role of a guide or mentor who provides support and sustenance to individuals on their journey.
  • tveto: This translates to "may you lead" or "may you guide." It emphasizes the importance of guidance in our lives, similar to how a teacher or a leader directs their followers towards a goal.
  • nayatu: This means "to lead" or "to conduct." In practical terms, it can be seen in leadership roles where individuals are responsible for steering a group towards success, such as a project manager leading a team to complete a project.
  • hastagṛhyāśvinā: This phrase refers to the Ashvins, the twin deities known for their healing and protective qualities. The term "hastagṛhya" suggests the idea of being held or embraced, which can be related to the comfort and safety one feels in the presence of loved ones or caregivers.
  • tvā pra vahatāṁ: This means "we carry you" or "we support you." It signifies the importance of community and support systems in our lives, akin to how friends and family come together to help one another during difficult times.
  • rathena: This translates to "with a chariot." The chariot symbolizes progress and movement. In modern life, this can be compared to the vehicles we use to achieve our goals, whether they are literal vehicles or metaphorical ones like education and skills.
  • gṛhān gaccha: This means "go to the home." It emphasizes the significance of home as a place of safety and belonging, much like how returning to one’s family after a long journey provides comfort and reassurance.
  • gṛhapatnī: This refers to the mistress of the house or the householder's wife, symbolizing the nurturing aspect of home life. In real life, this can be seen in the role of caregivers who maintain the emotional and physical well-being of the family.
  • yathāso: This means "as you wish" or "as you desire." It highlights the importance of individual desires and aspirations in shaping one’s life path, similar to how personal goals drive career choices.
  • vaśinī: This term means "one who is in control" or "one who has power." It reflects the idea of empowerment, where individuals take charge of their lives and decisions, akin to a person who confidently pursues their dreams.
  • tvaṁ vidatham ā vadāsi: This translates to "you speak of the truth." It emphasizes the value of honesty and integrity in communication, which is essential in building trust in relationships, whether personal or professional.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of guidance, support, and the nurturing aspects of life. It signifies the importance of community, home, and the roles individuals play in each other's lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various situations, such as leadership in the workplace, the role of family in personal development, and the significance of honesty in communication. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our journeys and the importance of supporting one another along the way.
