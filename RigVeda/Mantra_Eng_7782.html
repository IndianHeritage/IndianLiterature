आपानासो विवस्वतो जनन्त उषसो भागम् |सूरा अण्वं वि तन्वते ||
āpānāso vivasvato jananta uṣaso bhagam | sūrā aṇvaṁ vi tanvate ||
  • Mantra: āpānāso vivasvato jananta uṣaso bhagam | sūrā aṇvaṁ vi tanvate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āpānāso: Referring to the vital energies or breaths that are downward-moving.
    • vivasvato: Pertaining to the sun or the radiant one, often symbolizing enlightenment and knowledge.
    • jananta: Those who are aware or conscious, indicating a state of understanding.
    • uṣaso: Referring to the dawn or the early morning, symbolizing new beginnings and hope.
    • bhagam: Blessings or wealth, often associated with prosperity and abundance.
    • sūrā: The divine or the heroic, often referring to those who are strong and virtuous.
    • aṇvaṁ: The subtle or the minute, indicating the importance of small things or details.
    • vi tanvate: They spread or extend, suggesting the act of sharing or distributing.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the vital energies that are connected to the sun and the dawn, emphasizing the importance of awareness and consciousness in our lives.
    • The mention of "uṣaso" (dawn) signifies the arrival of new opportunities and the potential for growth and renewal.
    • The term "bhagam" (blessings) highlights the significance of prosperity and abundance that can be achieved through awareness and virtuous actions.
    • The reference to "sūrā" (the divine) suggests that those who embody strength and virtue can effectively share their blessings with others.
    • The phrase "aṇvaṁ vi tanvate" indicates that even the smallest actions can have a significant impact when they are shared with intention and purpose.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, when employees are aware of their roles and responsibilities (jananta), they can contribute to a positive environment (uṣaso) that fosters growth and success (bhagam).
    • In personal relationships, being conscious of the small gestures of kindness (aṇvaṁ) can strengthen bonds and create a supportive community (sūrā).
    • In community service, individuals who are aware of the needs of others can extend their resources and blessings (vi tanvate) to those in need, creating a ripple effect of positivity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of awareness, prosperity, and the sharing of blessings.
    • It serves as a reminder that by being conscious of our actions and their impact on others, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous environment.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, stronger relationships, and a more compassionate society.
