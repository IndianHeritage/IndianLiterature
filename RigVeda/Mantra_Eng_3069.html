नेषत तमो दुधितं रोचत द्यौर उद देव्या उषसो भानुर अर्त |आ सूर्यो बृहतस् तिष्ठद अज्रां ऋजु Marteṣu वृजिना च पश्यन् ||
neśat tamo dudhitaṁ rocata dyaur ud devyā uṣaso bhānur arta | ā sūryo bṛhatas tiṣṭhad ajrām̐ ṛju marteṣu vṛjinā ca paśyan ||
  • neśat: This word can be interpreted as "not to be hidden" or "revealed." It signifies the importance of clarity and truth in our lives.
  • tamo: This translates to "darkness," representing ignorance or confusion that can cloud our understanding.
  • dudhitaṁ: Meaning "illuminated" or "made clear," it suggests the process of enlightenment and gaining knowledge.
  • rocata: This means "shining" or "radiant," indicating the brilliance that comes from knowledge and understanding.
  • dyaur: Referring to "the sky" or "heaven," it symbolizes the vastness of knowledge and the higher realms of existence.
  • ud: This word means "up" or "above," suggesting elevation or ascension in understanding.
  • devyā: This translates to "by the divine" or "goddess," indicating the role of divine forces in guiding us towards enlightenment.
  • uṣaso: Meaning "of dawn," it represents new beginnings and the arrival of knowledge.
  • bhānur: This word means "the sun," symbolizing the ultimate source of light and knowledge.
  • arta: This translates to "truth" or "reality," emphasizing the importance of understanding the essence of existence.
  • ā: This word means "from" or "out of," indicating the source from which knowledge emerges.
  • sūryo: Referring again to "the sun," it reinforces the idea of illumination and enlightenment.
  • bṛhatas: This means "great" or "vast," suggesting the expansive nature of knowledge.
  • tiṣṭhad: Meaning "stands" or "remains," it indicates the permanence of truth and knowledge.
  • ajrām̐: This translates to "immortal," symbolizing the eternal nature of wisdom.
  • ṛju: Meaning "straight" or "direct," it emphasizes the importance of a clear and honest path in the pursuit of knowledge.
  • marteṣu: This refers to "among mortals," indicating the relevance of these teachings in human life.
  • vṛjinā: This means "obstacles" or "impurities," representing the challenges we face in our quest for knowledge.
  • ca: This translates to "and," connecting the various elements of the mantra.
  • paśyan: Meaning "seeing" or "perceiving," it signifies the act of gaining insight and understanding.

In conclusion, this mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the journey from darkness (ignorance) to light (knowledge). It highlights the importance of divine guidance in this process and the need for clarity and truth in our lives. The imagery of the sun and dawn symbolizes new beginnings and the eternal nature of wisdom. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, education, and the pursuit of truth in various aspects of life, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and overcome obstacles with clarity and purpose.
