आयजी वाजसातमा ता ह्य उ1च्चा विजर्भृतः |हरि इवांधांसी बप्सता ||
āyajī vājasātamā tā hy u1ccā vijarbhṛtaḥ | harī ivāndhāṁsi bapsatā ||
  • Mantra: āyajī vājasātamā tā hy u1ccā vijarbhṛtaḥ | harī ivāndhāṁsi bapsatā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āyajī: One who is worshipped or invoked.
    • vājasātamā: The best of strength or power.
    • tā: That or those.
    • hy: Indeed or surely.
    • u1ccā: High or elevated.
    • vijarbhṛtaḥ: Those who are free from old age or decay.
    • harī: The divine or the one who removes obstacles.
    • ivāndhāṁsi: Like the blind or those who are in darkness.
    • bapsatā: To be nourished or sustained.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of invoking a powerful divine presence that is free from decay and old age.
    • The term "vājasātamā" signifies the ultimate strength, suggesting that the divine being invoked possesses unparalleled power.
    • The phrase "harī ivāndhāṁsi" draws a comparison between the divine and those who are blind or in darkness, indicating that the divine brings light and clarity to those who are lost or confused.
    • In a real-life context, this can be seen as a call for guidance and strength in times of uncertainty or difficulty.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine support and strength in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that even in challenging times, there is a higher power that can provide clarity and direction.
    • In practical terms, individuals can apply this mantra during moments of doubt or fear, invoking the strength and guidance of the divine to overcome obstacles.
    • It can be used in meditation or prayer, fostering a sense of connection to a higher power and reinforcing one's inner strength.
