स हि विश्वाति पार्थिवा रयं दाशन महित्वना |वन्वन्न अवातो अस्तृतः ||
sa hi viśvāti pārthivā rayiṁ dāśan mahitvanā | vanvann avāto astṛtaḥ ||
  • sa hi viśvāti: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, it pervades all." The term "viśvāti" suggests a universal presence, indicating that the essence being referred to is omnipresent and integral to all aspects of existence.
  • pārthivā rayiṁ: Here, "pārthivā" refers to the earthly or material wealth, while "rayiṁ" signifies prosperity or abundance. Together, this phrase emphasizes the importance of earthly wealth and resources in our lives.
  • dāśan mahitvanā: "Dāśan" means "to give" or "to bestow," and "mahitvanā" translates to "greatness" or "magnificence." This suggests that true greatness comes from the act of giving and sharing wealth or resources with others.
  • vanvann avāto astṛtaḥ: "Vanvann" implies a sense of flourishing or thriving, while "avāto" means "protected" or "sheltered." "Astṛtaḥ" can be interpreted as "being established" or "firm." This part of the mantra conveys that when we share our resources and wealth, we create a thriving and protected environment for ourselves and others.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the significance of sharing wealth and resources. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as community service, charitable giving, and sustainable practices. By recognizing that our prosperity is linked to the well-being of others, we can foster a sense of unity and support within our communities. The mantra serves as a reminder that true greatness lies in generosity and the flourishing of all, leading to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
