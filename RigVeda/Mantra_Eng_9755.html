सनााद अग्ने मृणसि यातुधानान न त्वा रक्षांसी पृतनासु जिग्युः |अनु दह सहमूरान क्रव्यादो मा ते हेत्याऽ मुक्षत दैव्यायाः ||
sanād agne mṛṇasi yātudhānān na tvā rakṣāṁsi pṛtanāsu jigyuḥ | anu daha sahamūrān kravyādo mā te hetyā mukṣata daivyāyāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sanād: From the beginning, or from the ancient times.
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god).
    • mṛṇasi: You who are born from the earth.
    • yātudhānān: From the evil spirits or malevolent beings.
    • na: Not.
    • tvā: You.
    • rakṣāṁsi: Protections or safeguards.
    • pṛtanāsu: In the battles or conflicts.
    • jigyuḥ: Have conquered.
    • anu: After.
    • daha: Burn.
    • sahamūrān: The evil spirits or malevolent beings.
    • kravyādo: Flesh-eating beings or spirits.
    • mā: Do not.
    • te: Your.
    • hetyā: By the means of death.
    • mukṣata: Release or free.
    • daivyāyāḥ: Of the divine or celestial beings.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a protector and a purifying force.
    • The phrase "mṛṇasi yātudhānān" suggests a plea for protection from malevolent spirits that may arise from the earth.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine protection in overcoming challenges and adversities, particularly in conflicts or battles.
    • It highlights the need to burn away negativity and evil influences, symbolized by "anu daha," which means to burn after.
    • The invocation of Agni serves as a reminder of the transformative power of fire, which can purify and protect.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis or conflict, individuals often seek spiritual guidance or protection, similar to how this mantra seeks the protection of Agni.
    • In various cultures, fire rituals are performed to ward off evil spirits and bring about purification, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • People may use affirmations or prayers to invoke strength and protection when facing challenges, akin to the invocation of Agni in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine protection and guidance in overcoming adversities.
    • It teaches us to confront and burn away negative influences in our lives, allowing for personal growth and transformation.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles of this mantra can be applied through spiritual practices, rituals, and personal affirmations that promote resilience and protection against negativity.
