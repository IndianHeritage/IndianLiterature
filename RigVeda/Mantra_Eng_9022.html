माकिर न एनाऽसख्या वियौषुस तव चन्द्र विमदस्य च ऋषेः |विद्मा हि ते प्रमतिं देव जामिवद अस्मे ते सन्तु सख्या शिवानि ||
mākir na enā sakhyā vi yauṣus tava cendra vimadasya ca ṛṣeḥ | vidmā hi te pramatiṁ deva jāmivad asme te santu sakhyā śivāni ||
  • mākir na enā sakhyā vi yauṣus tava cendra vimadasya ca ṛṣeḥ
    • mākir: This word can be interpreted as "may we not be deprived" or "may we not be separated."
    • na enā: This phrase means "from this" or "from here."
    • sakhyā: Refers to "friendship" or "companionship."
    • vi yauṣus: This can be understood as "in your strength" or "through your power."
    • tava: Means "your."
    • cendra: Refers to "the divine" or "the celestial."
    • vimadasya: This word can be interpreted as "of wisdom" or "of knowledge."
    • ca ṛṣeḥ: Refers to "and of the sage" or "and of the seer."
  • vidmā hi te pramatiṁ deva jāmivad asme te santu sakhyā śivāni
    • vidmā: Means "we know" or "we understand."
    • hi: This is an emphatic particle, meaning "indeed" or "certainly."
    • te: Refers to "your."
    • pramatiṁ: This can be interpreted as "the understanding" or "the insight."
    • deva: Refers to "God" or "divine being."
    • jāmivad: This means "like a mother" or "as a nurturing figure."
    • asme: Means "to us" or "for us."
    • te santu: This phrase means "may there be" or "let there be."
    • sakhyā: Again refers to "friendship" or "companionship."
    • śivāni: This word can be interpreted as "auspicious" or "beneficial."

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the importance of companionship, wisdom, and divine guidance. It expresses a desire for strong friendships and the nurturing qualities of the divine, akin to a mother's love. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal relationships, fostering strong bonds with friends and family.
  • In community building, encouraging collaboration and support among individuals.
  • In professional settings, promoting teamwork and mutual respect among colleagues.

The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, understanding, and the pursuit of wisdom, which are essential for a harmonious and fulfilling life.
