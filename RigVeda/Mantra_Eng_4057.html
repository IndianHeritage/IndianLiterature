प्र ये वसुभ्य इवद आ नमो दुर ये मित्रे वरुणे सूक्तवाचः |अवैत्व अभ्वं कृतना वरियो दिवस्पृथिव्यो अवसामदेम ||
pra ye vasubhya īvad ā namo dur ye mitre varuṇe sūktavācaḥ | avaitv abhvaṁ kṛṇutā varīyo divaspṛthivyor avasā madema ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "To those who are the givers of wealth, I bow down; to Mitra and Varuna, I offer my words of praise. May they grant us the best of what is in the heavens and on the earth, so that we may be fulfilled."
  • Meaning of Key Terms:
    • Vasubhya: Refers to the givers of wealth or prosperity. In real life, this can be interpreted as those who contribute positively to our lives, such as family, friends, and mentors.
    • Mitra: The deity representing friendship and harmony. This can be related to the importance of maintaining good relationships in our personal and professional lives.
    • Varuna: The deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority. This signifies the importance of ethics and righteousness in our actions.
    • Sūktavācaḥ: Meaning "words of praise," this emphasizes the significance of expressing gratitude and respect towards those who support us.
    • Divaspṛthivyor: Refers to the heavens and the earth, symbolizing the entirety of existence and the interconnectedness of all aspects of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, acknowledging the contributions of colleagues (vasubhya) fosters a positive environment and enhances teamwork.
    • In personal relationships, nurturing friendships (Mitra) leads to a supportive network that can help us navigate life's challenges.
    • Practicing ethical behavior (Varuna) in business dealings builds trust and long-term relationships with clients and partners.
    • Expressing gratitude (sūktavācaḥ) towards mentors or family members can strengthen bonds and encourage further support.
    • Recognizing the balance between our aspirations (heavens) and responsibilities (earth) helps us lead a fulfilling life.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for gratitude, ethical living, and the recognition of the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, it serves as a reminder to appreciate the wealth of relationships and support we have, to act with integrity, and to seek harmony in our interactions. By embodying these principles, we can create a more fulfilling and prosperous life, both personally and collectively.
