अस्मे ता त इन्द्र सन्तु सत्याहिंसन्तीर उपस्पृशः |विद्याम यासां भुजो धेनूनां न वज्रिवः ||
asme tā ta indra santu satyāhiṁsantīr upaspṛśaḥ | vidyāma yāsām bhujo dhenūnāṁ na vajrivaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asme: for us
    • tā: that
    • ta: those
    • indra: O Indra (the god of rain and thunder)
    • santu: may they be
    • satyāhiṁsantīr: truthful and non-violent
    • upaspṛśaḥ: may they touch
    • vidyāma: we know
    • yāsām: of whom
    • bhujo: the enjoyers
    • dhenūnāṁ: of the cows
    • na: not
    • vajrivaḥ: like the thunderbolt
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, asking for blessings that are truthful and non-violent.
    • It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding, as well as the enjoyment of life’s bounties, symbolized by cows, which represent wealth and nourishment.
    • The phrase "not like the thunderbolt" suggests a desire for gentle and nurturing blessings rather than harsh or destructive ones.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied when leaders seek to promote peace and truthfulness among members, fostering an environment where everyone feels safe and valued.
    • In personal relationships, individuals can reflect on this mantra by striving to communicate honestly and compassionately, avoiding harm in their interactions.
    • In educational contexts, teachers can embody the essence of this mantra by imparting knowledge in a way that encourages understanding and respect, rather than fear or intimidation.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for a harmonious existence based on truth and non-violence.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek knowledge and understanding while enjoying the blessings of life in a gentle manner.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can guide individuals and communities towards creating a more peaceful and nurturing environment, emphasizing the importance of compassion and integrity in all aspects of life.
