न यं दिप्सन्ति दिप्सवो न द्रुह्वाणो जनानाम् |न देवम् अभिमातयः ||
na yaṁ dipsanti dipsavo na druhvāṇo janānām | na devam abhimātayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na: not
    • yaṁ: whom
    • dipsanti: desire
    • dipsavo: those who desire
    • na: not
    • druhvāṇo: harming
    • janānām: of people
    • na: not
    • devam: the divine
    • abhimātayaḥ: with intentions
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nature of desire and intention.
    • It emphasizes that those who harbor desires do not harm others.
    • It also suggests that the divine does not have intentions that lead to harm.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a team member may desire a promotion. If their desire is genuine and not at the expense of others, it aligns with the mantra's teaching.
    • In community service, volunteers may have the desire to help others without any intention of causing harm, reflecting the essence of the mantra.
    • In personal relationships, individuals may wish for love and companionship, but if their intentions are pure and respectful, they do not harm others in the process.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of pure intentions and the need to avoid harm in the pursuit of desires.
    • It serves as a reminder to evaluate our desires and intentions, ensuring they align with the well-being of others.
    • In real life, applying this mantra can lead to healthier relationships, ethical decision-making, and a more harmonious society.
