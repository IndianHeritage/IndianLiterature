सपर्यवो भरमानाऽभिज्ञु प्र वृञ्जते नमसā बर्हिर अग्नौ |आजुह्वानā घृतपृष्ठम् पृषद्वद अध्वर्यवो हविषा मरजयध्वम् ||
saparyavo bharamāṇā abhijñu pra vṛñjate namasā barhir agnau | ājuhvānā ghṛtapṛṣṭham pṛṣadvad adhvaryavo haviṣā marjayadhvam ||
  • Mantra: "saparyavo bharamāṇā abhijñu pra vṛñjate namasā barhir agnau | ājuhvānā ghṛtapṛṣṭham pṛṣadvad adhvaryavo haviṣā marjayadhvam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • saparyavo: Those who are engaged in worship or service.
    • bharamāṇā: Carrying or bearing (the offerings).
    • abhijñu: Knowledgeable or wise.
    • pra vṛñjate: They are chosen or selected.
    • namasā: With reverence or respect.
    • barhir: The sacred grass or the seat of offerings.
    • agnau: In the fire (representing Agni, the fire god).
    • ājuhvānā: Invoking or calling upon.
    • ghṛtapṛṣṭham: With clarified butter (ghee) on top.
    • pṛṣadvad: Like a protector or guardian.
    • adhvaryavo: The priests or those performing the ritual.
    • haviṣā: With offerings or oblations.
    • marjayadhvam: Purify or cleanse.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of ritualistic worship and the role of knowledgeable individuals in performing these rituals.
    • The phrase "saparyavo bharamāṇā" suggests that those who are engaged in worship should carry their offerings with dedication and respect.
    • In real life, this can be likened to how individuals prepare for significant events, such as weddings or festivals, where offerings and rituals are performed with care and reverence.
    • The invocation of Agni, the fire god, symbolizes the purification and transformation of offerings, akin to how we seek to purify our intentions and actions in daily life.
    • The mention of "ghṛtapṛṣṭham" indicates the importance of quality in offerings, similar to how we strive for excellence in our work and personal endeavors.
    • Furthermore, "adhvaryavo haviṣā marjayadhvam" highlights the role of priests or leaders in guiding the community, akin to how mentors or leaders in society help to purify and elevate the collective spirit.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of ritual, respect, and the role of knowledgeable individuals in guiding spiritual practices.
    • It teaches us that our actions, when performed with reverence and purity, can lead to personal and communal growth.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, family gatherings, or any situation where collective effort and respect for traditions are essential.
    • Ultimately, it reminds us that the essence of any ritual or offering lies in the intention behind it, encouraging us to approach our daily lives with mindfulness and dedication.
