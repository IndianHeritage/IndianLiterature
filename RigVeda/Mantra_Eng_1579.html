अग्नि जुषस्व प्रतिहर्य तद्वचो मन्द्र स्वधाव ऋतजात सुक्रतो |यो विश्वतः प्रत्यङ्ग्न् असि दर्शतो रण्वः संदृष्टौ पितुमाम् इव क्षयः ||
agne juṣasva prati harya tad vaco mandra svadhāva ṛtajāta sukrato | yo viśvataḥ pratyaṅṅ asi darśato raṇvaḥ saṁdṛṣṭau pitumām̐ iva kṣayaḥ ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic tradition, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Juṣasva: This means "may you be pleased" or "may you accept," indicating a call for divine favor.
  • Prati harya: This translates to "towards us," suggesting a desire for the divine presence to come closer.
  • Tad vaco: Meaning "that speech," it refers to the sacred words or mantras that invoke the divine.
  • Mandra: This means "sweet" or "pleasant," indicating the nature of the offerings or prayers being made.
  • Svadhāva: This term refers to "self-essence" or "self-nature," emphasizing the intrinsic qualities of the offerings.
  • Ṛtajāta: This means "born of truth," highlighting the importance of truthfulness in the offerings and prayers.
  • Sukrato: This translates to "good deeds" or "virtuous actions," indicating that the offerings should be accompanied by righteous actions.
  • Yo: Meaning "who," it introduces the subject of the mantra.
  • Viśvataḥ: This means "from all directions," suggesting a universal presence or influence.
  • Pratyaṅṅ: This translates to "in the opposite direction," indicating a duality or balance in the divine presence.
  • Asi: Meaning "you are," affirming the existence of the divine in various forms.
  • Darśato: This means "of the seer," referring to the vision or insight provided by the divine.
  • Raṇvaḥ: This translates to "the one who is in the battle," symbolizing the struggles of life.
  • Saṁdṛṣṭau: Meaning "in the sight," indicating awareness or perception of the divine.
  • Pitumām̐: This means "like a father," suggesting a nurturing and protective aspect of the divine.
  • IVA: This translates to "like," drawing a comparison.
  • Kṣayaḥ: Meaning "destruction" or "diminution," indicating the transient nature of life and existence.

Conclusion: This mantra is a profound invocation to Agni, seeking his blessings and presence in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of truth, virtuous actions, and the divine connection that guides us through life's struggles. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during rituals, personal prayers, or moments of reflection, reminding us to align our actions with truth and goodness. It signifies the need for divine guidance in overcoming challenges and the importance of maintaining a connection with the higher self, much like seeking wisdom from a mentor or a guiding figure in our lives.
