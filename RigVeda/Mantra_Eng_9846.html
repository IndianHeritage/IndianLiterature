यज्ञे-यज्ञे स मार्त्यो देवान् सपर्णति |यः सुम्नैर दीर्घश्रुत्तम आविवासत्य एनान् ||
yajñe-yajñe sa martyo devān saparyati | yaḥ sumnair dīrghaśruttama āvivāsaty enān ||
  • yajñe-yajñe: This phrase refers to the act of sacrifice or offering. In Vedic tradition, yajna (sacrifice) is a central ritual that symbolizes the connection between humans and the divine.
  • sa martyo: This translates to "the mortal" or "the human being." It emphasizes the human condition and the role of individuals in the cosmic order.
  • devān saparyati: This means "worships the gods." It highlights the importance of reverence and devotion towards the divine forces that govern the universe.
  • yaḥ sumnair: This translates to "who with good intentions." It suggests that the actions taken should be rooted in positivity and goodwill.
  • dīrghaśruttama: This means "the one with long-lasting fame." It indicates that those who perform sacrifices with sincerity and devotion will be remembered and honored.
  • āvivāsaty enān: This phrase can be interpreted as "will bring forth prosperity." It signifies that through sincere offerings and worship, one can attract blessings and abundance.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of performing rituals and sacrifices with a pure heart and good intentions. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, charitable acts, and personal growth. By engaging in selfless actions and honoring the divine, individuals can foster a sense of connection with the universe and contribute to the greater good. The mantra serves as a reminder that our actions, when rooted in devotion and positivity, can lead to lasting recognition and prosperity, both for ourselves and for those around us.
