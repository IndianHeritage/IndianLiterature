एह वाम पृथितप्सवो वयो वहन्तु पर्णिनः |अच्छा स्वध्वरं जनम् ||
eha vām pruṣitapsavo vayo vahantu parṇinaḥ | acchā svadhvaraṁ janam ||
  • eha: This word translates to "here" or "in this place." It signifies the importance of the present moment and the immediate environment.
  • vām: Meaning "your," it indicates a sense of possession or belonging, often referring to the divine or the community.
  • pruṣitapsavo: This term can be broken down into "pruṣita," meaning "to be nourished" or "to be sustained," and "tapsavo," which refers to those who are engaged in austerity or spiritual practices. Together, it suggests a community or individuals who are spiritually nourished.
  • vayo: This word means "age" or "youth," indicating vitality and the energy of life.
  • vahantu: Translated as "may they carry" or "may they bring," it expresses a wish or a prayer for something to be delivered or manifested.
  • parṇinaḥ: This term refers to "leaves" or "foliage," symbolizing growth, sustenance, and the natural world.
  • acchā: Meaning "good" or "auspicious," it conveys a sense of positivity and well-being.
  • svadhvaraṁ: This word can be interpreted as "self-chosen" or "self-directed," indicating autonomy and personal agency.
  • janam: This translates to "people" or "community," emphasizing the collective aspect of humanity.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation for the nourishment and well-being of the community, emphasizing the importance of spiritual sustenance and the vitality of life. It calls for the divine or spiritual forces to support and guide the people in their endeavors, ensuring that they thrive in their environment.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Community Gatherings: During festivals or communal events, this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings for collective well-being and harmony.
  • Personal Reflection: Individuals can use this mantra in meditation to focus on their spiritual nourishment and personal growth, reminding themselves of their connection to the community and the natural world.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to "leaves" and "foliage" can inspire actions towards environmental conservation, emphasizing the interconnectedness of life and the importance of nurturing our surroundings.

In conclusion, this mantra highlights the significance of spiritual nourishment, community well-being, and the importance of being present in the moment. It serves as a reminder that our actions and intentions can contribute to the greater good, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in our lives.
