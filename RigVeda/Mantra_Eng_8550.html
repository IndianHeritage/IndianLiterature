शिशुं जज्ञानाṁ हर्यतम मृजन्ति शुम्भन्ति वह्निम मरुतो गणेन |कविर गीरभिः काव्येन कविः सन् सोमः पवित्रं अत्य एति रेब्हन ||
śiśuṁ jajñānaṁ haryatam mṛjanti śumbhanti vahnim maruto gaṇena | kavir gīrbhiḥ kāvyenā kaviḥ san somaḥ pavitram aty eti rebhan ||
  • śiśuṁ jajñānaṁ: This phrase refers to the birth or emergence of a child or new life. In real life, this can be related to the joy and hope that comes with the arrival of a newborn, symbolizing new beginnings and potential.
  • haryatam: This term can be interpreted as the most auspicious or favorable. It signifies the importance of positive energy and good fortune in our lives. For example, when starting a new venture, having a positive mindset can greatly influence the outcome.
  • mṛjanti: This word means to cleanse or purify. In a practical sense, it can relate to the importance of maintaining mental and physical health through practices like meditation or yoga, which help in cleansing the mind and body.
  • śumbhanti: This term suggests the act of bringing forth or manifesting. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way individuals manifest their goals and dreams through hard work and determination.
  • vahnim: This word refers to fire, which symbolizes transformation and energy. In real life, fire can represent passion and the drive to achieve one's goals, much like how a fire fuels a flame of ambition.
  • maruto gaṇena: This phrase refers to the group of Maruts, the storm deities, symbolizing strength and power. In a real-world context, this can be likened to teamwork and collaboration, where a group of individuals can achieve more together than alone.
  • kavir gīrbhiḥ: This means the wise one or poet through words. It emphasizes the power of communication and expression. In real life, effective communication can lead to better relationships and understanding among people.
  • kāvyenā kaviḥ san: This suggests that the poet or wise person uses poetry or art to convey deeper truths. In modern life, art and literature serve as powerful tools for social change and personal expression.
  • somaḥ pavitram: Soma represents purity and divine nectar. This can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, which are essential for personal growth and enlightenment.
  • aty eti rebhan: This phrase indicates transcending or going beyond. In a practical sense, it can be seen in the journey of self-improvement, where individuals strive to surpass their limitations and achieve greatness.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of new beginnings, purification, manifestation, and the power of collaboration and communication. Its significance lies in the encouragement to embrace positivity, work towards personal and collective goals, and strive for continuous growth. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as parenting, teamwork, artistic expression, and personal development, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
