त्वं न इन्द्रासां हस्ते शविष्ठ दावने |धानानां न सं गृभायास्मयुर द्विः सं गृभायास्मयुः ||
tvaṁ na indrāsāṁ haste śaviṣṭha dāvane | dhānānāṁ na saṁ gṛbhāyāsmayur dviḥ saṁ gṛbhāyāsmayuḥ ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a deity or a higher power.
  • na - This means "not," suggesting a negation or a refusal.
  • indrāsāṁ - Referring to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature, and "āsāṁ" implies "of the Indras," indicating a connection to divine strength or power.
  • haste - This translates to "in the hand," symbolizing control or possession.
  • śaviṣṭha - Meaning "the most powerful" or "the strongest," emphasizing the supreme strength of the addressed entity.
  • dāvane - This word can be interpreted as "in the fire" or "in the blaze," suggesting a context of purification or transformation.
  • dhānānāṁ - This means "of the wealth" or "of the riches," indicating abundance or prosperity.
  • na - Again, this negation reinforces the previous context.
  • saṁ gṛbhāyāsmayur - This phrase can be understood as "to take or grasp," with "asmayuḥ" referring to "the stone" or "the material," suggesting a connection to the physical world.
  • dviḥ - Meaning "twice," indicating repetition or duality.
  • saṁ gṛbhāyāsmayuḥ - Reiterating the act of grasping or taking, emphasizing the importance of wealth and material possession.

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to a divine power, asking for strength and control over wealth and prosperity. The speaker is addressing a higher power, possibly Indra, and expressing a desire to not be deprived of wealth or strength, especially in times of challenge or transformation.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In business, individuals may recite this mantra to seek strength and control over their financial endeavors, ensuring they do not lose their wealth during challenging times.
  • During personal challenges, one might invoke this mantra to gain the inner strength needed to overcome obstacles, much like how fire transforms materials through intense heat.
  • In community settings, this mantra can serve as a reminder of the importance of collective strength and prosperity, encouraging individuals to support one another in achieving common goals.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in maintaining strength and wealth. It emphasizes the importance of resilience and control in the face of challenges, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to communal prosperity. By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset of abundance and empowerment, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and the material world.
