धुनेतयः सुप्रकेतम् मदन्तो बृहस्पते अभि ये नस् ततास्रे |पृषन्तं सृप्रम् अदब्दम् ऊर्वम् बृहस्पते रक्षताद अस्य योनिम ||
dhunetayaḥ supraketam madanto bṛhaspate abhi ye nas tatasre | pṛṣantaṁ sṛpram adabdham ūrvam bṛhaspate rakṣatād asya yonim ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dhunetayaḥ: Those who are engaged in the pursuit of knowledge or wisdom.
    • supraketam: Well-formed or well-structured; signifies clarity and purpose.
    • madanto: Those who are joyful or intoxicated with knowledge.
    • bṛhaspate: Referring to Brihaspati, the deity of wisdom and eloquence.
    • abhi: Towards or in the direction of.
    • ye: Those who.
    • nas: Us or our.
    • tatasre: Have bestowed or granted.
    • pṛṣantaṁ: Calm or peaceful.
    • sṛpram: Swift or quick.
    • adabdham: Unharmed or untroubled.
    • ūrvam: The earth or the ground.
    • rakṣatād: May protect or safeguard.
    • asya: This or of this.
    • yonim: Womb or source.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Brihaspati, the deity associated with wisdom and knowledge.
    • It emphasizes the importance of pursuing knowledge with clarity and joy, suggesting that those who seek wisdom are protected and guided.
    • The phrase "may protect this womb" signifies the nurturing aspect of knowledge, suggesting that wisdom is the source from which all good things arise.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In educational settings, students who approach their studies with joy and clarity often excel and find fulfillment in their learning journey.
    • Professionals who seek knowledge and wisdom in their fields tend to make better decisions and contribute positively to their organizations.
    • In personal development, individuals who cultivate a mindset of continuous learning and joy often experience growth and resilience in the face of challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of wisdom and knowledge in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to pursue knowledge joyfully and with purpose, assuring them of protection and guidance in their endeavors.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, professional success, and a deeper understanding of the world around us.
