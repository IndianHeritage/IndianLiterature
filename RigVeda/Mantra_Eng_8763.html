देवेभ्यस्त्वा वृत्था पाजसेऽपो वसानं हरिम मृजन्ति ||
devebhyas tvā vṛthā pājase 'po vasānaṁ harim mṛjanti ||
  • Mantra: "devebhyas tvā vṛthā pājase 'po vasānaṁ harim mṛjanti"
  • Translation: "The gods, having no need for you, cleanse the waters that are your abode."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • devebhyas: Refers to the gods or divine beings.
    • tvā: You (referring to a divine entity or force).
    • vṛthā: In vain or without purpose.
    • pājase: To cleanse or purify.
    • 'po: Waters or liquid, often symbolizing life or sustenance.
    • vasānaṁ: Abode or dwelling place.
    • harim: Referring to a divine force, often associated with Vishnu or a similar deity.
    • mṛjanti: They cleanse or purify.

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra reflects the relationship between the divine and the natural elements, particularly water.
    • The phrase "the gods, having no need for you" suggests that the divine forces operate independently of human desires or needs.
    • The act of cleansing the waters symbolizes purification and renewal, which is essential for life.
    • In real life, this can be seen in rituals where water is blessed or purified before being used in ceremonies, signifying its importance in spiritual practices.
    • Additionally, this mantra can be interpreted in the context of environmental conservation, where the cleansing of water bodies is crucial for maintaining ecological balance.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine forces in the natural world and the importance of purity.
    • It serves as a reminder of our responsibility to protect and purify our environment, particularly water sources, which are vital for survival.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to seek spiritual cleansing and renewal, aligning themselves with the divine order of nature.
    • Thus, the mantra is applicable in both spiritual practices and real-life scenarios, promoting a harmonious relationship with nature and the divine.
