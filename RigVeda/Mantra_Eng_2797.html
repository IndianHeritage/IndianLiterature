ह्रदाऽ इव कुक्षयः सोमधानाः सम ī विव्याच सवनाऽ पूरूणि |अन्ना यद इन्द्रः प्रथमा व्याश वृत्रं जगहन्वाम् अवृणीत सोमम् ||
hradā iva kukṣayaḥ somadhānāḥ sam ī vivyāca savanā purūṇi | annā yad indraḥ prathamā vy āśa vṛtraṁ jaghanvām̐ avṛṇīta somam ||
  • Mantra: "hradā iva kukṣayaḥ somadhānāḥ sam ī vivyāca savanā purūṇi | annā yad indraḥ prathamā vy āśa vṛtraṁ jaghanvām̐ avṛṇīta somam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • hradā: like a lake
    • iva: as if
    • kukṣayaḥ: wombs
    • somadhānāḥ: those who have gathered the soma
    • sam: together
    • ī: indeed
    • vivyāca: have revealed
    • savanā: offerings
    • purūṇi: many
    • annā: food
    • yad: that which
    • indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • prathamā: first
    • vy āśa: has spoken
    • vṛtraṁ: Vritra (the dragon or demon)
    • jaghanvām̐: has struck down
    • avṛṇīta: has taken
    • somam: soma (a sacred drink)
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra metaphorically compares the wombs of creation to lakes, suggesting that they are fertile and nurturing.
    • The term "somadhānāḥ" refers to those who have gathered the essence of life, symbolized by soma, which is often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • The phrase "sam ī vivyāca savanā purūṇi" indicates that these offerings are made collectively, emphasizing the importance of community and shared rituals.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, is depicted as the one who first spoke of the food (annā) that sustains life, highlighting the divine connection to nourishment.
    • The mention of Vritra signifies the challenges or obstacles that must be overcome to attain this sustenance, representing the struggles in life that one must face to achieve abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the concept of nurturing the land (like a lake) to produce food can be seen in sustainable farming practices that respect the environment.
    • Community gatherings for harvest festivals reflect the idea of collective offerings and gratitude for the sustenance provided by nature.
    • In personal development, overcoming obstacles (like Vritra) to achieve one's goals can be likened to the struggles individuals face in their careers or personal lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, the importance of community, and the divine nature of sustenance.
    • It serves as a reminder to appreciate the resources we have and to work together to overcome challenges.
    • In modern life, this mantra can inspire individuals to foster community spirit, practice gratitude, and strive for personal growth despite adversities.
