स रोचयज जनुषा रोडसी उभे स मात्रोः अभवत पुत्र ईड्यः | हव्यवाह अग्निर अजरोऽनहितो दूलभो विश्वाम अतिथिर विभावसुः ||
sa rocayaj januṣā rodasī ubhe sa mātror abhavat putra īḍyaḥ | havyavāḻ agnir ajaraś canohito dūḻabho viśām atithir vibhāvasuḥ ||
  • sa rocayaj januṣā rodasī: This phrase can be translated as "He who shines in the two worlds." Here, "sa" refers to the divine or the supreme being, "rocayaj" means to shine or illuminate, "januṣā" refers to the two realms or worlds, and "rodasī" signifies the sky or the heavens. This can be likened to the sun, which illuminates both the earth and the sky, symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
  • ubhe sa mātror abhavat putra īḍyaḥ: This translates to "He became the son of both mothers, worthy of worship." The term "ubhe" means both, "mātror" refers to mothers, "abhavat" means became, and "putra" means son. This can be interpreted in a real-life context as someone who embodies qualities from both parents, thus becoming a well-rounded individual. For example, a child who inherits the wisdom of one parent and the creativity of another can be seen as a "worthy of worship" individual in society.
  • havyavāḻ agnir ajaraś canohito: This means "the fire that is offered, eternal and unblemished." "Havyavāḻ" refers to offerings made to the divine, "agnir" means fire, "ajaraś" signifies eternal or ageless, and "canohito" means unblemished or pure. In real life, this can be related to the concept of purity in intentions and actions. For instance, when one performs a charitable act with a pure heart, it can be seen as an offering to the divine, akin to the sacred fire in rituals.
  • dūḻabho viśām atithir vibhāvasuḥ: This translates to "the rare guest of the people, the illuminator." "Dūḻabho" means rare, "viśām" refers to the people or community, "atithir" means guest, and "vibhāvasuḥ" signifies the one who illuminates or brings light. This can be exemplified by a leader or a mentor who brings knowledge and guidance to a community, acting as a rare and valuable presence that enlightens others.

  • Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of divine illumination, the importance of heritage, purity in actions, and the value of rare wisdom in society. In real life, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge, honor their roots, act with purity of intention, and recognize the value of those who bring enlightenment to their communities. It serves as a reminder that each person has the potential to shine brightly in the world, contributing positively to the lives of others.
