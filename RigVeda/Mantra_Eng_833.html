तवे अग्ने सुमतिम् भिक्षमाणाऽ दिवि श्रवो दधिरे यज्ञियासः |नक्तā च चक्रुर उषसा विरूपे कृष्णं च वर्णम् अरुणं च सं धुः ||
tve agne sumatim bhikṣamāṇā divi śravo dadhire yajñiyāsaḥ | naktā ca cakrur uṣasā virūpe kṛṣṇaṁ ca varṇam aruṇaṁ ca saṁ dhuḥ ||
  • tve agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni," referring to the Vedic fire god, who symbolizes energy, light, and purity. In real life, Agni can be seen as a representation of the transformative power of fire, which is essential in cooking, warmth, and rituals.
  • sumatim bhikṣamāṇā: This means "seeking good understanding." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in our lives. For example, in a professional setting, seeking good understanding can lead to better decision-making and teamwork.
  • divi śravo dadhire: This translates to "they established fame in the heavens." It suggests that through righteous actions and offerings, one can achieve recognition and honor. In modern life, this can be likened to how individuals gain respect and reputation through their contributions to society.
  • yajñiyāsaḥ: This term refers to "those who perform sacrifices." It highlights the significance of selfless actions and contributions to the community. For instance, volunteering for social causes can be seen as a modern-day yajña, where individuals give their time and resources for the greater good.
  • naktā ca cakrur: This means "the nights have turned." It symbolizes the cyclical nature of time and the importance of each phase in life. Just as night turns to day, challenges can lead to new opportunities. For example, overcoming a setback can lead to personal growth and new beginnings.
  • uṣasā virūpe: This translates to "the dawn is beautiful." It signifies hope and new beginnings. In real life, every new day brings opportunities for change and improvement, much like how a fresh start can lead to success in various endeavors.
  • kṛṣṇaṁ ca varṇam: This means "the black and the red." It represents duality and balance in life. For instance, in nature, the balance between light and dark is essential for harmony. In personal life, balancing work and leisure is crucial for well-being.
  • aruṇaṁ ca saṁ dhuḥ: This translates to "and they have mixed the red." It suggests the blending of different aspects of life, such as emotions, experiences, and relationships. In real life, this can be seen in how diverse cultures and ideas come together to create a richer society.

  • Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of wisdom, selfless actions, and the cyclical nature of life. It teaches us that through righteous actions and seeking understanding, we can achieve recognition and contribute positively to society. In real life, these principles can guide us in our personal and professional lives, encouraging us to embrace change, seek balance, and work towards the greater good.
