वीरेण्यः क्रतुर इन्द्रः सुशस्तिर उतापि धेनाऽपुरुहूतम ईट्टे |आरद्यद वृत्रम अकृणोद उ लोकं ससाहे शक्रः पृतना अभिष्टिः ||
vīreṇyaḥ kratur indraḥ suśastir utāpi dhenā puruhūtam īṭṭe | ārdayad vṛtram akṛṇod u lokaṁ sasāhe śakraḥ pṛtanā abhiṣṭiḥ ||
  • vīreṇyaḥ: This term refers to the "heroic" or "valiant" aspect, often associated with strength and courage. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who stand up for justice or protect others, such as firefighters or soldiers.
  • kratur: This word signifies "intention" or "will." It reflects the importance of having a strong purpose or goal in life. For example, a student who is determined to excel in their studies embodies this concept.
  • indraḥ: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power and authority. In modern contexts, this can be likened to leaders or influential figures who guide and protect their communities.
  • suśastir: This term means "well-formed" or "excellent." It emphasizes the value of quality and excellence in our endeavors, such as a craftsman who takes pride in their work.
  • utāpi: This word can be interpreted as "to uplift" or "to elevate." It suggests the importance of supporting and encouraging others, similar to mentors who help guide their mentees towards success.
  • dhenā: This refers to "nourishment" or "sustenance." In a real-life context, it can represent the resources we provide to others, such as food, education, or emotional support.
  • puruhūtam: This term means "invoked by many." It signifies the collective effort and unity of people working towards a common goal, much like a community coming together for a charitable cause.
  • īṭṭe: This word means "to praise" or "to worship." It highlights the importance of gratitude and recognition in our lives, akin to appreciating the efforts of those around us.
  • ārdayad: This term means "to break" or "to destroy." It can symbolize overcoming obstacles or challenges, such as a person who perseveres through adversity to achieve their dreams.
  • vṛtram: This refers to "the obstacle" or "the enemy." In life, this can represent personal challenges or societal issues that need to be addressed, such as poverty or discrimination.
  • akṛṇod: This means "to create" or "to establish." It emphasizes the power of creation and innovation, similar to entrepreneurs who build new businesses and solutions.
  • u lokaṁ: This translates to "the world" or "the universe." It signifies our interconnectedness and the impact of our actions on a global scale, such as environmental conservation efforts.
  • sasāhe: This term means "to bear" or "to endure." It reflects resilience and the ability to withstand difficulties, much like individuals who face personal hardships with strength.
  • śakraḥ: This refers to "the powerful" or "the mighty." It symbolizes the strength within us to overcome challenges, akin to athletes who push their limits to achieve greatness.
  • pṛtanā: This means "the path" or "the journey." It emphasizes the importance of the journey we undertake in life, similar to the experiences that shape our character and values.
  • abhiṣṭiḥ: This term signifies "blessings" or "prosperity." It highlights the importance of seeking and sharing blessings in our lives, such as acts of kindness and generosity towards others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, purpose, and community. It teaches us the significance of courage, intention, and collective effort in overcoming obstacles and achieving excellence. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership, education, and social responsibility. By embodying the values expressed in this mantra, individuals can contribute positively to their communities and foster a spirit of unity and resilience.
