पीवोन्नामं रयिवृद्धः सुमेधाः श्वेतः सिषक्ति नियुताम् अभिश्रीः |ते वायवे समनसो वि तस्थुर विश्वेन नरः स्वपत्यानि चक्रुः ||
pīvoannām̐ rayivṛdhaḥ sumedhāḥ śvetaḥ siṣakti niyutām abhiśrīḥ | te vāyave samanaso vi tasthur viśven naraḥ svapatyāni cakruḥ ||
  • pīvoannām̐: This term can be broken down into "pīvo" meaning "to drink" and "annām̐" meaning "food" or "nourishment". It signifies the act of consuming nourishment, which is essential for life.
  • rayivṛdhaḥ: This word translates to "increasing wealth" or "prosperity". It emphasizes the importance of growth and abundance in one's life.
  • sumedhāḥ: This term means "good intellect" or "wisdom". It highlights the value of having a sharp mind and the ability to make wise decisions.
  • śvetaḥ: Translated as "white" or "pure\ this word symbolizes purity and clarity, often associated with positive qualities and virtuous actions.
  • siṣakti: This term refers to "power" or "energy". It indicates the potential and strength that individuals possess to achieve their goals.
  • niyutām: Meaning "of the forces" or "of the energies\ this word suggests the collective power that can be harnessed when individuals come together.
  • abhiśrīḥ: This translates to "prosperity" or "glory". It signifies the success and honor that come from righteous actions.
  • te vāyave: "Te" means "they" and "vāyave" refers to "the winds" or "the forces of nature". This phrase connects human actions with the natural world.
  • samanaso: This term means "of one mind" or "united". It emphasizes the importance of unity and collective effort.
  • vi tasthur: This translates to "they have established" or "they have created". It indicates the act of bringing something into existence through effort.
  • viśven: Meaning "with all" or "universal\ this word signifies inclusivity and the idea that all beings are interconnected.
  • naraḥ: This term means "humans" or "people". It emphasizes the role of humanity in the context of the mantra.
  • svapatyāni: This translates to "dreams" or "aspirations". It highlights the importance of having goals and visions for the future.
  • cakruḥ: Meaning "they have made" or "they have done\ this word signifies the actions taken to realize dreams and aspirations.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of nourishment, wisdom, unity, and collective effort in achieving prosperity and success. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in a workplace, community development projects, or even personal growth where individuals strive for betterment through shared knowledge and resources. The mantra serves as a reminder that when people come together with a common purpose, harnessing their collective strengths and wisdom, they can create a prosperous and harmonious existence. It encourages individuals to seek purity in their actions and to work towards their dreams with a united mindset, ultimately leading to a fulfilling life.
