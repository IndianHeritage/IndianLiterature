सहस्रधारः पवते समुद्रो वाचमीङ्खयः |सोमः पति रयीणां सखेंद्रस्य दिवे-दिवे ||
sahasradhāraḥ pavate samudro vācamīṅkhayaḥ | somaḥ patī rayīṇāṁ sakhendrasya dive-dive ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahasradhāraḥ: One who has a thousand streams or channels.
    • pavate: Blows or moves (often referring to the wind).
    • samudro: The ocean or sea.
    • vācamīṅkhayaḥ: Bestower of speech or voice.
    • somaḥ: The moon or a divine drink that grants immortality.
    • patī: Lord or master.
    • rayīṇāṁ: Of wealth or prosperity.
    • sakhendrasya: Of the friend or companion of the Indra (the king of gods).
    • dive-dive: Day by day or every day.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine forces of nature, particularly the wind and the ocean, symbolizing the vastness and interconnectedness of life.
    • The mention of "sahasradhāraḥ" suggests the abundance of resources and opportunities available to us, akin to the many streams of a river that nourish the land.
    • "Pavate" signifies the movement of air, which is essential for life, representing change and the passage of time.
    • The "samudro" or ocean symbolizes depth, mystery, and the vast potential of the universe, much like the challenges and opportunities we face in life.
    • "Vācamīṅkhayaḥ" highlights the importance of communication and expression, essential for human connection and understanding.
    • "Somaḥ" represents nourishment and rejuvenation, akin to how we seek sustenance and inspiration in our daily lives.
    • The phrase "rayīṇāṁ sakhendrasya" emphasizes the importance of friendship and support in achieving prosperity, reflecting the value of community and collaboration.
    • "Dive-dive" reminds us of the cyclical nature of life, where each day brings new opportunities and challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, the mantra can be seen as a reminder to embrace change (pavate) and to communicate effectively (vācamīṅkhayaḥ) with colleagues to foster a supportive work environment (rayīṇāṁ sakhendrasya).
    • In personal relationships, it emphasizes the importance of nurturing connections and expressing oneself openly to build strong bonds.
    • In environmental discussions, the reference to the ocean (samudro) can inspire us to respect and protect our natural resources, recognizing their vastness and significance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of abundance, communication, and the interconnectedness of life.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us to appreciate the resources around us, to communicate openly, and to support one another in our journeys.
    • In real life, it encourages us to embrace change, seek prosperity through collaboration, and recognize the cyclical nature of our experiences.
