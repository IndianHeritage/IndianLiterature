माकिर नेशन माकीं ऋषन माकीं सं शारी केवटे |अथारिष्टाभिर आगहि ||
mākir neśan mākīṁ riṣan mākīṁ saṁ śāri kevaṭe | athāriṣṭābhir ā gahi ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • mākir: This term can be interpreted as "one who is skilled" or "expert." It signifies proficiency and mastery in a particular field.
    • neśan: This word can mean "to lead" or "to guide." It emphasizes the importance of leadership and direction.
    • mākīṁ: This is a form of the word "mākir," reinforcing the idea of skillfulness.
    • riṣan: This term can be understood as "to strive" or "to exert effort." It highlights the necessity of hard work and determination.
    • saṁ: This word means "together" or "with," indicating collaboration and unity.
    • śāri: This can refer to "the essence" or "the core," suggesting a focus on fundamental values or truths.
    • kevaṭe: This term can be interpreted as "to the one who is devoted" or "to the one who is committed."
    • athāriṣṭābhir: This word can mean "with great strength" or "with powerful forces," indicating the presence of strong support or backing.
    • ā gahi: This phrase translates to "come forth" or "approach," suggesting an invitation or call to action.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of skillful leadership and the need for collaboration in achieving goals.
    • It suggests that through collective effort and commitment, one can overcome challenges and attain success.
    • The invocation of strength and support indicates that individuals should seek assistance from powerful allies or forces in their endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a skilled manager (mākir) leads a team (neśan) to achieve a project goal. The team members work together (saṁ) and strive (riṣan) to meet deadlines, showcasing their commitment (kevaṭe).
    • In community service, volunteers come together (saṁ) to support a cause, demonstrating their collective strength (athāriṣṭābhir) and commitment to making a difference (mākīṁ).
    • In sports, a coach (mākir) guides the team (neśan) to practice hard (riṣan) and work together (saṁ) to win a championship, relying on the strength of their training and teamwork (athāriṣṭābhir).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of skillful leadership, collaboration, and the pursuit of excellence in various aspects of life.
    • It is applicable in personal, professional, and communal contexts, encouraging individuals to strive for their goals while seeking support from others.
    • Ultimately, it highlights that success is often a collective effort, requiring dedication, skill, and the strength of unity.
