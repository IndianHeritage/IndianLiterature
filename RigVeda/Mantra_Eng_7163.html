कं ते दाना असक्षत वृत्रहन् कं सुवीर्याः |उक्ते का उ स्विद अन्तमः ||
kaṁ te dānā asakṣata vṛtrahan kaṁ suvīryā | ukthe ka u svid antamaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kaṁ: Who
    • te: Your
    • dānā: Gifts or offerings
    • asakṣata: Unable to protect or defend
    • vṛtrahan: Slayer of Vritra (a demon representing chaos)
    • kaṁ: Who
    • suvīryā: Of great strength or valor
    • ukthe: In the utterance or speech
    • ka: Who
    • u: Or
    • svid: Where
    • antamaḥ: The ultimate or the end
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a series of questions, seeking to understand the nature of divine gifts and strength.
    • It reflects on the idea of who can truly offer protection and strength, especially in the face of chaos and adversity.
    • The mention of "vṛtrahan" signifies the importance of overcoming obstacles and challenges, much like the hero's journey in many cultures.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader may embody the qualities of "suvīryā" by inspiring their team to overcome challenges, much like a hero facing a formidable foe.
    • In personal life, individuals often seek guidance or support from mentors who can provide the "gifts" of wisdom and strength during tough times.
    • In community service, volunteers act as "vṛtrahan" by addressing social issues, thereby protecting and uplifting those in need.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking strength and protection in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to reflect on who provides them with support and how they can embody those qualities themselves.
    • In essence, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, support, and the ability to overcome life's challenges.
