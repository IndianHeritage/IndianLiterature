मन्त्र: परि सुवानश चक्षसे देवमादनः क्रतुर इन्दुर विचक्षणः ||
pari suvānaś cakṣase devamādanaḥ kratur indur vicakṣaṇaḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pari: around, encompassing
    • suvānaś: well-spoken, eloquent
    • cakṣase: to the eye, vision
    • devamādanaḥ: divine, godly, pertaining to the gods
    • kratur: intention, will, or thought
    • indur: moon, representing calmness and serenity
    • vicakṣaṇaḥ: wise, perceptive, insightful
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine qualities of perception and wisdom.
    • It emphasizes the importance of eloquence and clarity in communication.
    • The reference to the moon symbolizes tranquility and the ability to reflect light, akin to wisdom illuminating the darkness of ignorance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, effective communication is crucial. A leader who speaks clearly and eloquently can inspire and motivate their team, much like the "suvānaś" aspect of the mantra.
    • In personal relationships, being perceptive and understanding, akin to "vicakṣaṇaḥ," helps in resolving conflicts and fostering deeper connections.
    • In moments of stress or confusion, seeking calmness and clarity, represented by "indur," can lead to better decision-making and emotional stability.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, eloquence, and calmness in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate these qualities to navigate the complexities of life effectively.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can enhance personal and professional relationships, leading to a more harmonious existence.
