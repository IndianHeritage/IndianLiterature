यं जनासो हविष्मन्तो मित्रं न सर्पिरासुतिम् |प्रशंसन्ति प्रशस्तिभिः ||
yaṁ janāso haviṣmanto mitraṁ na sarpirāsutim | praśaṁsanti praśastibhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: who
    • janāso: among people
    • haviṣmanto: who is worthy of offerings
    • mitraṁ: friend
    • na: not
    • sarpir: ghee (clarified butter)
    • āsutim: the essence or the best part
    • praśaṁsanti: praise
    • praśastibhiḥ: with commendations
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks about a person who is recognized and praised among people for their worthiness of offerings, but not for superficial or materialistic reasons.
    • It emphasizes the importance of genuine qualities and virtues over mere external attributes.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a colleague who consistently helps others and contributes positively to the team may be praised for their character rather than just their achievements.
    • In a community, a volunteer who dedicates time and effort to help those in need is often recognized for their selflessness rather than for any material gain.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra teaches us to value and recognize the intrinsic qualities of individuals, such as kindness, integrity, and generosity.
    • It encourages us to foster relationships based on genuine appreciation rather than superficial judgments.
    • In modern society, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in education, where teachers can focus on nurturing character development alongside academic achievements.
