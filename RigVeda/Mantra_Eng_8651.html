अयम् पूषा रयिः भगः सोमः पुनानो अर्षति |पतिः विश्वस्य भूमानो व्याख्याद rodasī उभे ||
ayam pūṣā rayir bhagaḥ somaḥ punāno arṣati | patir viśvasya bhūmano vy akhyad rodasī ubhe ||
  • ayam pūṣā: This phrase refers to "this Pusha," where Pusha is a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity. In real life, this can be likened to the importance of sustenance in our daily lives, whether it be through food, knowledge, or emotional support.
  • rayir bhagaḥ: "Rayir" means wealth or abundance, and "bhagaḥ" refers to fortune or prosperity. This can be seen in the context of striving for success in various aspects of life, such as career achievements or personal growth, where one seeks to accumulate wealth and good fortune.
  • somaḥ punāno arṣati: "Somaḥ" refers to the sacred drink that symbolizes immortality and divine inspiration. "Punāno" means purifying or renewing, and "arṣati" indicates the act of rising or elevating. This can be related to the idea of rejuvenation in our lives, such as through meditation, exercise, or spiritual practices that help us rise above challenges.
  • patir viśvasya bhūmano: "Patir" means lord or master, "viśvasya" refers to the universe or all beings, and "bhūmano" means the mind or consciousness. This suggests a connection to the universal consciousness and the idea that we are all interconnected. In real life, this can be reflected in our relationships and the importance of empathy and understanding towards others.
  • vy akhyad rodasī ubhe: "Vy akhyad" means to declare or reveal, "rodasī" refers to the two realms (often interpreted as the heavens and the earth), and "ubhe" means both. This can be understood as the revelation of knowledge that bridges the earthly and the divine, emphasizing the importance of wisdom in navigating life’s challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of nourishment, prosperity, rejuvenation, universal consciousness, and the revelation of wisdom. Its significance lies in the holistic approach to life, encouraging individuals to seek balance in material wealth and spiritual growth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire us to cultivate a sense of gratitude for our resources, engage in practices that promote mental and physical well-being, and foster connections with others, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
