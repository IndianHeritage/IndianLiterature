त्वया वयम् उत्तमं धीमहे वयो बृहस्पते पप्रिणा सस्निना युजा |मा नो दुःशंसो अभिदिप्सुर ईशत प्र सुशंसा मतीभिः तारीषीमहि ||
tvayā vayam uttamaṁ dhīmahe vayo bṛhaspate papriṇā sasninā yujā | mā no duḥśaṁso abhidipsur īśata pra suśaṁsā matibhis tāriṣīmahi ||
  • tvayā vayam uttamaṁ dhīmahe: This phrase translates to "We meditate upon you, O Supreme One." Here, "tvayā" refers to the divine or supreme entity, while "uttamaṁ" signifies the highest or the best. This indicates a call for guidance and wisdom from a higher power.
  • vayo bṛhaspate: "Vayo" means "of the mind" and "bṛhaspate" refers to the deity associated with wisdom and intellect. This part emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and understanding from the divine source.
  • papriṇā sasninā yujā: This can be interpreted as "may we be united with the divine through our prayers." "Papriṇā" suggests a connection or bond, while "yujā" indicates union or joining. It highlights the significance of prayer and devotion in achieving unity with the divine.
  • mā no duḥśaṁso abhidipsur: This translates to "may no evil desires approach us." "Duḥśaṁso" refers to harmful or negative intentions, while "abhidipsur" means to desire or wish for. This part serves as a protective plea against negativity and harmful influences.
  • īśata pra suśaṁsā: "īśata" means "may we be blessed" and "suśaṁsā" refers to good fortune or auspiciousness. This indicates a desire for blessings and positive outcomes in life.
  • matibhis tāriṣīmahi: This translates to "may we be guided by our intellect." "Matibhis" refers to wisdom or intellect, while "tāriṣīmahi" means to be led or guided. This emphasizes the importance of using our intellect and wisdom in decision-making and life choices.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a profound spiritual message that emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance, wisdom, and protection from negativity. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or prayer to seek clarity and wisdom in their personal and professional lives.
  • Community Well-being: Groups or communities can use this mantra in gatherings to foster unity, positivity, and collective growth.
  • Decision Making: Before making significant life choices, one can reflect on this mantra to ensure that their decisions are guided by wisdom and free from harmful desires.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of connecting with the divine for guidance, seeking wisdom, and maintaining a positive mindset in all aspects of life.
