मन्त्र: उता नो 'हिर बुद्ध्यः श्रिणोत्व अज एकपात पृथिवी समुद्रः | विश्वे देवाः ऋतावृधो हुवानाः स्तुताः मन्त्राः कविशस्ताः अवन्तु ||
uta no 'hir budhnyaḥ śṛṇotv aja ekapāt pṛthivī samudraḥ | viśve devā ṛtāvṛdho huvānāḥ stutā mantrāḥ kaviśastā avantu ||
  • uta no 'hir budhnyaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "may the radiant one (the sun) hear us." Here, "uta" signifies a call or invocation, while "hir" refers to brightness or radiance, often associated with the sun or divine light. In real life, this can be seen as a metaphor for seeking guidance and enlightenment in our daily lives.
  • śṛṇotv aja ekapāt: This translates to "may the unborn (aja) one (ekapāt) listen." The term "aja" refers to something that is eternal or not born, often used to describe the divine or the ultimate reality. In practical terms, this can be related to the idea of seeking wisdom from timeless truths or universal principles that guide our actions.
  • pṛthivī samudraḥ: This means "the earth (pṛthivī) and the ocean (samudraḥ)." It symbolizes the vastness of nature and the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe. In our lives, this can remind us of our responsibility towards the environment and the importance of preserving nature for future generations.
  • viśve devā ṛtāvṛdho: This translates to "all the gods (viśve devā) who are established in truth (ṛtāvṛdho)." It emphasizes the idea of divine beings who embody righteousness and truth. In a real-life context, this can inspire individuals to strive for honesty and integrity in their personal and professional lives.
  • huvānāḥ stutā mantrāḥ: This means "the invocations (huvānāḥ) that are praised (stutā) through mantras." It highlights the power of words and prayers in connecting with the divine. In everyday life, this can be seen in the practice of meditation, affirmations, or any form of positive expression that uplifts the spirit.
  • kaviśastā avantu: This translates to "may the wise (kaviśastā) protect us." It signifies a plea for guidance and protection from those who possess knowledge and wisdom. In practical terms, this can relate to seeking mentorship or guidance from experienced individuals in various fields of life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound invocation for guidance, wisdom, and protection from the divine and the wise. It emphasizes the importance of seeking enlightenment, living in harmony with nature, and upholding truth and integrity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can serve as a reminder to cultivate mindfulness, respect for the environment, and a commitment to personal growth and ethical living. By integrating these principles into our daily lives, we can foster a deeper connection with ourselves, others, and the universe.
