आ त एता वचोयुजा हरि गृभ्णे सुमद्रथा |यद ईम ब्रह्मभ्य इद ददः ||
ā ta etā vacoyujā harī gṛbhṇe sumadrathā | yad īm brahmabhya id dadaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • ta: that
    • etā: these
    • vacoyujā: with words (or speech)
    • harī: the divine or the Lord
    • gṛbhṇe: I grasp or I hold
    • sumadrathā: with good intentions or auspiciousness
    • yad: which
    • īm: I (or we)
    • brahmabhya: to the wise or the learned
    • id: this
    • dadaḥ: I give or I offer
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra expresses a prayer or invocation where the speaker seeks to grasp the divine essence through words, with good intentions, and offers it to the wise or learned individuals. It emphasizes the importance of communication and intention in connecting with the divine and sharing wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Communication: In our daily lives, effective communication is crucial. Just as the mantra emphasizes the power of words, in real life, clear and kind communication can strengthen relationships and foster understanding.
    • Intentions: The phrase "with good intentions" reminds us that our motivations matter. For instance, when helping others, approaching them with genuine care can lead to more positive outcomes than merely fulfilling an obligation.
    • Sharing Knowledge: The act of offering wisdom to others, as mentioned in the mantra, can be seen in mentorship. A mentor shares their knowledge and experiences with a mentee, helping them grow and learn.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the power of words, the importance of good intentions, and the value of sharing wisdom. In real life, it encourages us to communicate thoughtfully, act with purpose, and contribute to the growth of others. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and enlightened society.
