आपो न सिंधुम अभि यत समक्षरन् सोमास इन्द्रं कुल्या इव ह्रदम् |वर्धन्ति विप्राः महो अस्य साधने यवम् न वृष्टिः दिव्येन दानुना ||
āpo na sindhum abhi yat samakṣaran somāsa indraṁ kulyā iva hradam | vardhanti viprā maho asya sādane yavaṁ na vṛṣṭir divyena dānunā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āpo: Waters
    • na: Not
    • sindhum: Ocean
    • abhi: Towards
    • yat: Which
    • samakṣaran: Flowing
    • somāsa: Like the moon
    • indraṁ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • kulyā: Streams
    • iva: Like
    • hradam: A lake
    • vardhanti: They increase
    • viprā: The wise
    • maho: Great
    • asya: Of this
    • sādane: Abode
    • yavaṁ: Barley
    • na: Not
    • vṛṣṭir: Rain
    • divyena: Divine
    • dānunā: By the gift
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the waters and their connection to the divine.
    • It suggests that the waters, like the streams flowing towards a lake, are essential for life and prosperity.
    • The reference to Indra indicates the importance of divine blessings in ensuring abundance.
    • The wise (viprā) are those who understand the significance of these blessings and work towards creating a prosperous environment.
    • The mention of barley (yavaṁ) symbolizes nourishment and sustenance, which is vital for survival.
    • The absence of rain (na vṛṣṭir) highlights the necessity of divine intervention for agricultural success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers pray for rain and favorable conditions to ensure a good harvest, reflecting the need for divine support.
    • Communities often come together in rituals to invoke blessings for water resources, recognizing their importance for life and prosperity.
    • In modern times, environmental conservation efforts can be seen as a way to honor the waters and ensure their abundance for future generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, divinity, and human effort.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of respecting and nurturing our natural resources.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals and communities to seek harmony with nature and to recognize the divine forces that support life.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the significance of wisdom and collective action in achieving prosperity and sustainability.
