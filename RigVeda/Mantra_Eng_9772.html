यदेद एनम अदधुर यज्ञियासो दिवि देवाः सूर्यं आदितेयं |यदा चरिष्णू मिथुनाव अभूताम आद इति प्रापश्यन् भुवनानि विश्वा ||
yaded enam adadhur yajñiyāso divi devāḥ sūryam āditeyam | yadā cariṣṇū mithunāv abhūtām ād it prāpaśyan bhuvanāni viśvā ||
  • Mantra: "yaded enam adadhur yajñiyāso divi devāḥ sūryam āditeyam |
  • Translation: "What the gods, who are the enjoyers of sacrifices, have established in the heavens, that is the Sun, the son of Aditi."
  • Explanation:
    • Yajñiyāso: Refers to the gods as the enjoyers of sacrifices. In real life, this can be likened to the idea that our actions and offerings (like kindness, charity, or service) are appreciated by the universe or a higher power.
    • Divi devāḥ: The term signifies celestial beings or gods. This can be related to the concept of divine forces in nature that govern our lives, such as the sun providing light and energy essential for life.
    • Sūryam āditeyam: This phrase identifies the Sun as the offspring of Aditi, representing the source of light and life. In practical terms, the sun is crucial for agriculture, influencing food production and, consequently, human survival.
  • Next Line: "yadā cariṣṇū mithunāv abhūtām ād it prāpaśyan bhuvanāni viśvā"
  • Translation: "When the two, the Sun and the Earth, were established, they beheld all the worlds."
  • Explanation:
    • Cariṣṇū: Refers to the act of moving or traveling. This can symbolize the journey of life and the importance of progress and exploration.
    • Mithunāv: Indicates the duality or partnership, which can be seen in the relationship between the Sun and the Earth. This partnership is essential for the balance of ecosystems and the cycle of life.
    • Bhuvanāni viśvā: This phrase means "all the worlds," suggesting a holistic view of existence. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all life forms and the environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra highlights the significance of the Sun and Earth in sustaining life and the importance of our actions in relation to the divine.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder of our responsibilities towards nature and the universe, encouraging us to live harmoniously and contribute positively to the world.
    • This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as environmental conservation efforts, promoting sustainable living, and fostering a sense of gratitude towards the natural world.
