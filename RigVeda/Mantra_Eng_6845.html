हरयो धूमकेतवो वातजूताः उप द्यवि |यतन्ते वृथग अग्नयः ||
harayo dhūmaketavo vātajūtā upa dyavi | yatante vṛthag agnayaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "harayo dhūmaketavo vātajūtā upa dyavi | yatante vṛthag agnayaḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "The smoke-colored ones, the wind-born, rise up to the sky; they strive in vain, like the flames of fire."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Harayo: Refers to the "smoke-colored" or "dark" entities, often interpreted as clouds or beings associated with the atmosphere.
    • Dhūmaketavo: This term means "those who are like smoke," suggesting something ephemeral or transient.
    • Vātajūtā: Translates to "born of the wind," indicating a connection to the air or the ethereal.
    • Upa dyavi: Means "up to the sky," suggesting elevation or aspiration towards higher realms.
    • Yatante: This means "they strive" or "they exert effort," indicating a sense of struggle or endeavor.
    • Vṛthag: Translates to "in vain" or "fruitless," implying that the efforts may not yield the desired results.
    • Agnayaḥ: Refers to "the flames of fire," symbolizing energy, passion, or transformation.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects the transient nature of existence, emphasizing how certain efforts may seem significant but ultimately lead to nothing.
    • It can be seen as a metaphor for human aspirations and endeavors that, despite their intensity, may not always result in success.
    • For example, consider the pursuit of a career or a personal goal. Many individuals work tirelessly towards their ambitions, but not all efforts lead to the expected outcomes. This mantra serves as a reminder of the impermanence of such pursuits.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In the context of personal growth, this mantra encourages individuals to reflect on their goals and the means they employ to achieve them. It suggests that one should evaluate whether their efforts are fruitful or if they are merely striving in vain.
    • In a broader societal context, it can be applied to movements or initiatives that, despite being well-intentioned, may not achieve their goals due to various obstacles or misalignments.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to provoke thought about the nature of effort and success. It teaches that not all endeavors will bear fruit, and it is essential to discern which paths are worth pursuing.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to focus on meaningful pursuits and to accept that some efforts may not lead to the desired outcomes, encouraging resilience and adaptability in the face of challenges.
