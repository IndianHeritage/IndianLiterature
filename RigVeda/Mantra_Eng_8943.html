यो अग्निः क्रव्यवाहनः पितृन् यक्षद ऋतावृद्धः |प्रेदु हव्यानि वोकति देवेषु च पितृभ्य आ ||
yo agniḥ kravyavāhanaḥ pitṝn yakṣad ṛtāvṛdhaḥ | pred u havyāni vocati devebhyaś ca pitṛbhya ā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • agniḥ: fire
    • kravyavāhanaḥ: bearer of offerings
    • pitṝn: ancestors
    • yakṣad: has invoked
    • ṛtāvṛdhaḥ: who increases the truth
    • pred u: the offerings
    • havyāni: sacred offerings
    • vocati: calls out
    • devebhyaś: to the gods
    • ca: and
    • pitṛbhya: to the ancestors
    • ā: from
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of Agni, the sacred fire, as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • Agni is depicted as the one who carries offerings to the ancestors (pitṛn) and the gods (devebhya).
    • The phrase "ṛtāvṛdhaḥ" suggests that Agni not only carries offerings but also enhances the truth and righteousness associated with these offerings.
    • In essence, this mantra emphasizes the importance of honoring both the divine and ancestral spirits through rituals involving fire.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, lighting a fire during rituals symbolizes purification and the presence of the divine. For instance, in Hindu weddings, the sacred fire (Agni) is central to the ceremony, representing the couple's commitment to each other and their families.
    • During festivals like Diwali, offerings are made to both gods and ancestors, showcasing the belief in the continuity of blessings and the importance of remembering one's roots.
    • In modern contexts, the act of gathering around a fire for celebrations or memorials can be seen as a way to connect with both the past and the divine, reinforcing community bonds.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of Agni as a vital link between the material and spiritual worlds.
    • It teaches us the importance of honoring our ancestors and the divine through rituals, which can foster a sense of belonging and continuity.
    • In real life, the principles embodied in this mantra can be applied in various ceremonies, family gatherings, and personal reflections, reminding us to respect our heritage while seeking divine guidance.
