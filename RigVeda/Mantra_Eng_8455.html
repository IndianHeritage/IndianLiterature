असश्चतः शतधारा अभिश्रियो हरिं नवन्तेऽव त उदन्युवः |क्षिपो मृजन्ति परि गोभिर आवृतं तृतीये पृष्ठे अधि रोचने दिवः ||
asaścataḥ śatadhārā abhiśriyo hariṁ navante 'va tā udanyuvaḥ | kṣipo mṛjanti pari gobhir āvṛtaṁ tṛtīye pṛṣṭhe adhi rocane divaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asaścataḥ: From the many (or countless)
    • śatadhārā: Hundred streams or flows
    • abhiśriyo: With auspiciousness or prosperity
    • hariṁ: The divine or the Lord (often referring to Vishnu)
    • navante: They are newly born or emerging
    • 'va: Or
    • tā: Those
    • udanyuvaḥ: Who are rising or elevating
    • kṣipo: They throw or cast
    • mṛjanti: They cleanse or purify
    • pari: All around
    • gobhir: With the cows (or wealth)
    • āvṛtaṁ: Surrounded or enveloped
    • tṛtīye: In the third
    • pṛṣṭhe: On the back or support
    • adhi: Above or upon
    • rocane: In the shining or brilliance
    • divaḥ: Of the sky or heaven
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine energies that emerge from the multitude of sources, symbolized by "asaścataḥ" and "śatadhārā." It suggests that from many, there is a unity that brings forth prosperity and auspiciousness.
    • The reference to "hariṁ" indicates the presence of a divine force, often associated with sustenance and protection, which is essential in our lives.
    • The idea of "newly born" energies ("navante") signifies the continuous renewal and rebirth of opportunities and blessings in our lives.
    • The act of cleansing and purifying ("mṛjanti") with the wealth represented by cows ("gobhir") emphasizes the importance of nurturing and protecting our resources and relationships.
    • The mention of "tṛtīye pṛṣṭhe" suggests a foundation or support that is elevated, indicating that our spiritual and material pursuits should be aligned with higher principles and values.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to the idea of collaboration and teamwork. Just as many streams come together to form a river, diverse talents and perspectives can create a successful enterprise.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra encourages us to cleanse our interactions, ensuring that we nurture our connections with love and respect, much like how one would care for livestock.
    • In environmental practices, the emphasis on renewal and protection can inspire sustainable living, where we recognize the importance of preserving our natural resources for future generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity, renewal, and the divine presence in our lives. It teaches us that from many sources, we can find strength and prosperity.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to embrace collaboration, nurture our relationships, and protect our resources, aligning our actions with higher values.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that the divine is present in our efforts to create a harmonious and prosperous life, guiding us towards a brighter future.
