मन्त्र: इन्द्राय सोम पवसे देवभ्यः सदहमाद्यः |इन्दो वाजं सिषाससि ||
indrāya soma pavase devebhyaḥ sadhamādyaḥ | indo vājaṁ siṣāsasi ||
  • Indrāya: This word refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. Indra represents power and leadership.
  • Soma: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. It is often associated with the moon and is considered a source of energy and vitality.
  • Pavase: This term means "to purify" or "to cleanse." It signifies the act of making something pure, which is essential in spiritual practices.
  • Devebhyaḥ: This word translates to "to the gods." It indicates the offering or dedication of the mantra to divine beings, acknowledging their presence and power.
  • Sadhamādyaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is well-prepared" or "the one who is ready." It emphasizes the importance of being prepared and focused in one's endeavors.
  • Indo: This refers to the moon or the divine essence associated with it. The moon is often seen as a symbol of calmness and reflection.
  • Vājaṁ: This word means "strength" or "power." It signifies the energy and vigor needed to achieve one's goals.
  • Siṣāsasi: This term can be understood as "you bestow" or "you grant." It indicates the act of giving or providing, often in the context of blessings or support from the divine.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Indra and the divine forces, seeking purification, strength, and readiness to receive blessings. It emphasizes the importance of being prepared and open to divine guidance in our lives.


Real-Life Application: In daily life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Before starting a new project or endeavor, one can recite this mantra to seek strength and clarity, ensuring they are well-prepared for the challenges ahead.
  • Spiritual Practices: During meditation or rituals, this mantra can be used to invoke divine support, helping individuals to cleanse their minds and focus on their spiritual goals.
  • Leadership: Leaders can draw inspiration from this mantra, embodying the qualities of Indra by being strong, decisive, and ready to guide their teams effectively.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in achieving purity, strength, and readiness. By invoking these qualities, individuals can navigate life's challenges with greater confidence and clarity, fostering personal and spiritual growth.
