या इमा विश्वा जातान्य आश्रावयति श्लोकेन |प्र च सुवाति सविता ||
ya imā viśvā jātāny āśrāvayati ślokena | pra ca suvāti savitā ||
  • Mantra: "ya imā viśvā jātāny āśrāvayati ślokena | pra ca suvāti savitā ||"
  • Translation: "He who brings forth all these beings through the hymn, may Savitar, the Sun God, grant us good fortune."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ya: He who
    • imā: these
    • viśvā: all beings
    • jātāny: born or created
    • āśrāvayati: brings forth or inspires
    • ślokena: through the hymn or verse
    • pra: may
    • ca: and
    • suvāti: grant good fortune
    • savitā: Savitar, the Sun God
  • Context and Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the power of hymns and prayers in bringing forth life and prosperity. It acknowledges the divine force of Savitar, who is often associated with the sun and its life-giving energy.
    • In real life, this can be likened to the importance of positive affirmations and intentions. Just as the mantra calls upon Savitar for blessings, individuals often seek to manifest their desires through focused thoughts and prayers.
    • For example, in a community setting, when people come together to chant or pray for a common cause, such as health or prosperity, they are invoking a collective energy that can lead to positive outcomes.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of all beings and the role of divine energy in nurturing life. It serves as a reminder of the importance of spirituality in everyday life.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in rituals, meditation, or even daily affirmations where one seeks to align with positive energies and intentions.
    • By invoking Savitar, individuals can cultivate a mindset of gratitude and openness to receiving blessings, which can lead to improved well-being and success in various endeavors.
