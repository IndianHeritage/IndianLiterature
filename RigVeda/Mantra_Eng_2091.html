श्रूयाग्निश्चित्रभानुरहवम् मे विश्वाभिर्गीरभिरमृतोविचेताः |श्यावारथं वहतोरोहितावोतारुषाह चक्रे विभृतः ||
śrūyā agniś citrabhānur havam me viśvābhir gīrbhir amṛto vicetāḥ | śyāvā rathaṁ vahato rohitā votāruṣāha cakre vibhṛtraḥ ||
  • śrūyā: This word translates to "listen" or "hear." It emphasizes the importance of being attentive and receptive to divine messages or wisdom.
  • agniś: Refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic tradition, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. Fire is often seen as a medium that connects the earthly realm with the divine.
  • citrabhānur: This means "the one with a brilliant light" or "radiant." It signifies the illuminating nature of Agni, representing knowledge and enlightenment.
  • havam me: Translates to "my offering." This indicates a personal connection and devotion, suggesting that the speaker is making a heartfelt offering to Agni.
  • viśvābhir: This means "with all" or "universal." It implies inclusivity and the idea that the offering is made with the participation of all beings.
  • gīrbhir: Refers to "songs" or "chants." This highlights the importance of vocal expression in rituals, where hymns are sung to invoke divine presence.
  • amṛto: This translates to "immortal" or "eternal." It signifies the timeless nature of the divine and the spiritual essence that transcends physical existence.
  • vicetāḥ: Means "the one who knows" or "the wise." This refers to the enlightened state of being that comes from understanding and wisdom.
  • śyāvā: This word means "dark" or "black," often associated with the unknown or the mysteries of life.
  • rathaṁ: Refers to a "chariot," symbolizing movement, progress, and the journey of life.
  • vahato: This means "carrying" or "bearing," indicating the act of transporting or guiding.
  • rohitā: This translates to "red" or "the one who is red," often associated with vitality and energy.
  • votāruṣāha: This means "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is called upon," emphasizing the act of seeking divine assistance.
  • cakre: Refers to "to create" or "to make," indicating the act of bringing something into existence.
  • vibhṛtraḥ: This means "the one who is vast" or "expansive," symbolizing the infinite nature of the universe and the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of devotion, the pursuit of knowledge, and the connection between the individual and the divine. It emphasizes the importance of listening to divine wisdom, making heartfelt offerings, and recognizing the vastness of existence. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during rituals, meditation, or moments of reflection, where one seeks guidance and enlightenment. It serves as a reminder to be open to the wisdom of the universe and to engage in practices that foster spiritual growth and understanding.
