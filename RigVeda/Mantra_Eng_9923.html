यā ओषधिः सोमराज्ञीर विष्ठिताः पृथिवīm अनु | बृहस्पतिप्रसूता अस्यै सं दत्त वीर्यम् ||
yā oṣadhīḥ somarājñīr viṣṭhitāḥ pṛthivīm anu | bṛhaspatiprasūtā asyai saṁ datta vīryam ||
  • yā oṣadhīḥ: This phrase refers to the "herbs" or "plants" that are considered sacred and beneficial. In real life, this can be related to the use of medicinal plants in Ayurveda, which emphasizes the healing properties of various herbs.
  • somarājñīr: This term translates to "the queen of the soma," where soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition believed to confer immortality and divine qualities. In modern terms, it can symbolize the pursuit of higher consciousness or spiritual awakening.
  • viṣṭhitāḥ pṛthivīm anu: This means "established upon the earth." It signifies the grounding of these divine qualities in the physical world. In practical terms, it can be seen in how we apply spiritual principles to our daily lives, ensuring that our actions are rooted in reality.
  • bṛhaspatiprasūtā: This translates to "born of Brihaspati," who is the deity of wisdom and eloquence. This can be related to the importance of knowledge and wisdom in making decisions in our lives, akin to seeking guidance from mentors or wise individuals.
  • asyai: This word means "to her" or "to it," indicating a connection or offering to the divine or the sacred herbs mentioned earlier.
  • saṁ datta vīryam: This phrase means "may strength be given." It emphasizes the importance of receiving strength and vitality from the divine or nature, which can be reflected in how we seek support from our environment, community, or spiritual practices.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the relationship between nature, spirituality, and wisdom. It signifies the importance of grounding spiritual practices in the physical world and seeking strength from the divine. In real life, this can be applied by integrating holistic practices, such as using herbal remedies for health, pursuing knowledge for personal growth, and fostering a connection with nature to enhance our well-being. The mantra serves as a reminder to honor the sacredness of life and the wisdom that comes from both nature and our experiences.
