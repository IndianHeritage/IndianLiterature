नवो-नवो भवति जायमानोऽह्नां केतुर्वुषसामेत्याग्रम् |भागं देवभ्यो विदधात्यायन प्र चन्द्रमास तिरते दीर्घमायुः ||
navo-navo bhavati jāyamāno 'hnāṁ ketur uṣasām ety agram | bhāgaṁ devebhyo vi dadhāty āyan pra candramās tirate dīrgham āyuḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "The new one is born anew every day; the light of dawn comes first. The divine beings grant a share of their blessings, and the moon travels, bestowing long life."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • navo-navo: New, new; signifies renewal and rebirth.
    • bhavati: Becomes; indicates the process of becoming or transformation.
    • jāyamāno: Being born; refers to the act of birth or emergence.
    • 'hnāṁ: Of the day; relates to the daily cycle of life.
    • ketur: Light or guidance; symbolizes enlightenment and clarity.
    • uṣasām: Of the dawn; represents new beginnings and hope.
    • ety: Comes; indicates arrival or manifestation.
    • agram: First; signifies priority or the initial phase.
    • bhāgaṁ: Share or portion; refers to the blessings or gifts.
    • devebhyo: To the divine beings; acknowledges the higher powers or deities.
    • vi dadhāty: Bestows; indicates the act of giving or sharing.
    • āyan: Abode or dwelling; refers to a place of residence or stability.
    • pra: Towards; indicates direction or focus.
    • candramās: The moon; symbolizes calmness, beauty, and the cyclical nature of life.
    • tirate: Travels; signifies movement or progression.
    • dīrgham: Long; refers to duration or longevity.
    • āyuḥ: Life; represents existence and vitality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of renewal can be seen in nature, such as the changing seasons. Each spring, life is reborn, symbolizing hope and new beginnings.
    • In personal growth, individuals often experience transformations, akin to the daily rebirth mentioned in the mantra. For instance, someone overcoming challenges may feel like they are born anew each day.
    • The moon's phases can be related to human emotions and experiences, where each phase represents different aspects of life, such as growth, reflection, and renewal.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the cyclical nature of life, the importance of renewal, and the blessings bestowed by divine forces. It teaches us to embrace each day as a new opportunity for growth and transformation. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek positivity and renewal in their daily lives, reminding them that every day is a chance to start afresh and receive the blessings of the universe.
