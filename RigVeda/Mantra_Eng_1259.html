तवेषं वयं रुद्रं यज्ञसाधं वङ्कुं कविम अवसे न ह्वयामहे |आरे अस्मद दैव्यम हेळो अस्यतु सुमतिम इद वयं अस्य वृणीमहे ||
tveṣaṁ vayaṁ rudraṁ yajñasādhaṁ vaṅkuṁ kavim avase ni hvayāmahe | āre asmad daivyaṁ heḻo asyatu sumatim id vayam asyā vṛṇīmahe ||
  • tveṣaṁ vayaṁ rudraṁ: This phrase refers to invoking Rudra, a form of the divine associated with storms and the hunt. Rudra represents the fierce and protective aspects of nature.
  • yajñasādhaṁ: This term signifies the importance of rituals and sacrifices (yajnas) in connecting with the divine. It emphasizes that through these acts, one can attain spiritual fulfillment.
  • vaṅkuṁ kavim: Here, "vaṅkuṁ" can be interpreted as the one who is wise or knowledgeable, while "kavim" refers to a poet or seer. This suggests the importance of wisdom and insight in understanding the divine.
  • avase ni hvayāmahe: This line indicates a call to the divine for assistance and guidance. It reflects the human desire for support in times of need.
  • āre asmad daivyaṁ: This phrase can be understood as a plea for divine intervention or blessings. It highlights the relationship between humans and the divine, where humans seek help from higher powers.
  • heḻo asyatu sumatim: "Heḻo" suggests a request for clarity or understanding, while "sumatim" refers to good thoughts or wisdom. This line emphasizes the importance of seeking wisdom and clarity in life.
  • id vayam asyā vṛṇīmahe: This concluding line expresses a collective intention or desire to choose or embrace the divine guidance and wisdom offered.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and wisdom through rituals and prayers. It highlights the importance of connecting with the divine, especially in challenging times, and emphasizes the role of wisdom in navigating life's complexities. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various situations such as during personal challenges, community gatherings, or spiritual practices where individuals seek clarity, support, and a deeper connection with the divine. By invoking these sentiments, one can foster a sense of community and shared purpose, ultimately leading to personal and collective growth.
