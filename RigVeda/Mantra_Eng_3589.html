यावयद्वेषसङ् त्वा चिकीर्ति सूनृतावरि |प्रतिस्तोमैर अभुत्स्महि ||
yāvayaddveṣasaṁ tvā cikitvit sūnṛtāvari | prati stomair abhutsmahi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yāvayad: As long as
    • veṣasaṁ: Enmity or hostility
    • tvā: You
    • cikitvit: To be aware or to understand
    • sūnṛtāvari: With truth or righteousness
    • prati: In response to
    • stomair: Hymns or praises
    • abhutsmahi: We shall invoke or we shall call upon
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of overcoming enmity and hostility.
    • It suggests that as long as there is awareness and understanding of the divine, one should strive to invoke truth and righteousness.
    • The use of hymns or praises signifies the role of devotion and spirituality in addressing conflicts and negativity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal relationships, this mantra can be applied when resolving conflicts. For instance, when two friends have a disagreement, understanding each other's perspectives and invoking a spirit of truth can help mend the relationship.
    • In a workplace setting, fostering a culture of open communication and mutual respect can reduce hostility among colleagues, leading to a more harmonious work environment.
    • On a larger scale, this mantra can be relevant in community or national conflicts, where dialogue and understanding are essential for peace-building.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for awareness and understanding in the face of enmity.
    • It teaches that through truth and righteousness, one can overcome negativity and foster positive relationships.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a more peaceful society.
