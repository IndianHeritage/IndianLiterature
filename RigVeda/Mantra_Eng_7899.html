अरुषो जनयन गिरः सोमः पवता आयुषक् |इन्द्रं गच्छन् कविक्रतुः ||
aruṣo janayan giraḥ somaḥ pavata āyuṣak | indraṁ gacchan kavikratuḥ ||
  • Aruṣo: This word translates to "the red one" or "the bright one," often associated with the sun or a divine entity that brings light and energy.
  • Janayan: Meaning "giving birth" or "creating," this term signifies the act of generation or manifestation.
  • Giraḥ: This refers to "speech" or "words," indicating the power of communication and expression.
  • Somaḥ: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. It represents the essence of life and vitality.
  • Pavata: This means "to blow" or "to inspire," often associated with the wind or breath, symbolizing the life force.
  • Āyuṣak: This translates to "long-lived" or "bestowing longevity," emphasizing the desire for a long and prosperous life.
  • Indraṁ: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, representing strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • Gacchan: Meaning "going" or "moving," this term signifies progress or the journey towards a goal.
  • Kavikratuḥ: This refers to "the wise one" or "the one with intellect," indicating the importance of wisdom and strategic thinking in achieving success.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for the generation of life, vitality, and wisdom. It emphasizes the importance of communication and the divine inspiration that leads to longevity and strength. The mention of Indra suggests a call for divine assistance in overcoming challenges and achieving one's goals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek wisdom and clarity in their thoughts and actions, much like how one would approach a challenging situation with a clear mind.
  • In health and wellness, the invocation of longevity and vitality can inspire people to adopt healthier lifestyles, focusing on nutrition and exercise to enhance their life force.
  • In leadership, the qualities of Indra can be emulated by those in positions of power, encouraging them to lead with strength, wisdom, and a focus on the greater good.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, wisdom, and divine inspiration. It encourages individuals to strive for longevity and strength while fostering effective communication and thoughtful leadership in their personal and professional lives.
