अग्निं स्तोमेन बोधय समिधानो अमर्त्यम् |हव्याः देवेषु नो दधत ||
agniṁ stomena bodhaya samidhāno amartyam | havyā deveṣu no dadhat ||
  • Agniṁ: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying and transformative force.
  • Stomena: This term means "with praise" or "through hymns." It indicates the act of invoking or honoring through words.
  • Bodhaya: This means "to awaken" or "to enlighten." It suggests a call for awareness or understanding.
  • Samidhāno: This word translates to "offering" or "sacrificing." It implies the act of giving or dedicating something to the divine.
  • Amartyam: This term means "immortal" or "eternal." It signifies the quest for transcendence or the divine nature of existence.
  • Havyā: This refers to "offerings" or "sacrifices" made to the gods. It emphasizes the importance of giving in the spiritual context.
  • Deveṣu: This means "to the gods." It indicates the recipients of the offerings and praises.
  • No: This translates to "our" or "to us," indicating a collective aspect of the invocation.
  • Dadhat: This means "to bestow" or "to grant." It suggests a request for blessings or gifts from the divine.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to Agni, asking for enlightenment and blessings through offerings made with devotion. It emphasizes the importance of honoring the divine and seeking a deeper understanding of life and existence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or rituals to invoke clarity and wisdom in their lives.
  • Community Gatherings: In communal settings, such as festivals or ceremonies, this mantra can be chanted to foster unity and collective spiritual growth.
  • Personal Reflection: The themes of sacrifice and devotion can inspire individuals to reflect on their contributions to society and the importance of selflessness.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for awakening and enlightenment through devotion and offerings. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of honoring the divine, and the pursuit of higher understanding. By incorporating its teachings into daily life, individuals can cultivate a sense of purpose, community, and spiritual growth.
