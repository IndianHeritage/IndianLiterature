क्रीळन्त्य अस्य सूनृताऽआपो न प्रवता यतीः |अयां धियā य उच्यते पतिर्दिवः ||
krīḻanty asya sūnṛtā āpo na pravatā yatīḥ | ayā dhiyā ya ucyate patir divaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • krīḻanty: They flow or move.
    • asya: Of this.
    • sūnṛtā: Truthful or in accordance with truth.
    • āpo: Waters.
    • na: Not.
    • pravatā: Flowing or moving away.
    • yatīḥ: Those who are restrained or disciplined.
    • ayā: By that.
    • dhiyā: Intellect or understanding.
    • ya: Who.
    • ucyate: Is spoken or is said.
    • patir: Lord or master.
    • divaḥ: Of the heavens or sky.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the flowing waters that embody truth and the disciplined individuals who do not let these waters flow away.
    • It emphasizes the importance of maintaining a connection with the truth and the wisdom that comes from understanding.
    • The phrase "patir divaḥ" refers to a higher power or divine authority that is recognized through this understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, this mantra can be related to the importance of honesty in relationships. Just as waters flow, so does the truth; if we allow dishonesty to prevail, we lose the essence of our connections.
    • In a professional setting, maintaining integrity in business practices ensures that the 'waters' of trust do not flow away, leading to sustainable relationships with clients and partners.
    • On a broader scale, this mantra can be applied to environmental consciousness. The waters symbolize nature, and by respecting and protecting our environment, we honor the truth of our existence and our responsibility towards it.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for truthfulness and discipline in our thoughts and actions.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate wisdom and understanding, recognizing the divine presence in the pursuit of truth.
    • In real life, this mantra serves as a reminder to uphold integrity, whether in personal relationships, professional dealings, or our interactions with the environment.
