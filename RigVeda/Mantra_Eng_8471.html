अञ्जते व्य अञ्जते सम अञ्जते क्रतुं रिहन्ति मधुनाभ्य अञ्जते |सिन्धोर उच्छ्वासे पतयन्तम उक्षणं हिरण्यपावाः पशुम आसु गृभ्णते ||
añjate vy añjate sam añjate kratuṁ rihanti madhunābhy añjate | sindhor ucchvāse patayantam ukṣaṇaṁ hiraṇyapāvāḥ paśum āsu gṛbhṇate ||
  • añjate vy añjate sam añjate kratuṁ rihanti madhunābhy añjate
    • añjate: This term can be translated as "to move" or "to act." It signifies the dynamic nature of existence and the importance of action in life.
    • vy añjate: The prefix "vy" adds a sense of completeness or thoroughness to the action, suggesting that all aspects of life are interconnected and should be engaged with.
    • sam añjate: "Sam" implies harmony or balance, indicating that actions should be taken in a way that maintains equilibrium in life.
    • kratuṁ: This word refers to "intellect" or "willpower." It emphasizes the role of mental strength and determination in achieving one's goals.
    • rihanti: This term means "to release" or "to set free," suggesting that through action and willpower, one can liberate oneself from limitations.
    • madhunābhy añjate: "Madhunā" refers to "honey," symbolizing sweetness and abundance. This phrase implies that through harmonious actions and willpower, one can attain the sweetness of life.
  • sindhor ucchvāse patayantam ukṣaṇaṁ hiraṇyapāvāḥ paśum āsu gṛbhṇate
    • sindhor: This word means "of the ocean," representing vastness and depth, akin to the ocean of knowledge and experience.
    • ucchvāse: This term translates to "breath" or "exhalation," symbolizing life force and vitality.
    • patayantam: Meaning "to fly" or "to soar," it suggests the idea of rising above challenges and limitations.
    • ukṣaṇaṁ: This refers to "the act of sowing" or "planting," indicating the importance of nurturing growth and potential.
    • hiraṇyapāvāḥ: This term means "golden" or "pure," symbolizing prosperity and purity in intentions.
    • paśum: Referring to "the animal" or "creature," it signifies the interconnectedness of all living beings.
    • āsu gṛbhṇate: This phrase means "to grasp" or "to hold," indicating the importance of seizing opportunities and nurturing life.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the significance of action, harmony, and willpower in achieving a fulfilling life. It teaches us that through our actions, we can liberate ourselves from limitations and attain the sweetness of existence. The imagery of the ocean and the act of sowing highlights the importance of nurturing growth and embracing the interconnectedness of all life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, teamwork, and community building, where collective action and harmony lead to prosperity and fulfillment.
