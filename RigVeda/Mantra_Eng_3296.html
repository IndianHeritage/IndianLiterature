आ यात्व इन्द्रो दिव आ पृथिव्यां मक्षू समुद्राद उता वा पुरिषात् |स्वर्णराद अवसे नो मरुत्वान् परावतो वा सदनाद ऋतस्य ||
ā yātv indro diva ā pṛthivyā makṣū samudrād uta vā purīṣāt | svarṇarād avase no marutvān parāvato vā sadanād ṛtasya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yātv: May he come forth
    • indro: Indra (the king of gods)
    • diva: from the heavens
    • ā pṛthivyā: and from the earth
    • makṣū: from the waters
    • samudrād: from the ocean
    • uta: or
    • vā purīṣāt: from the excrement (referring to the earth's fertility)
    • svarṇarād: from the golden (prosperity)
    • avase: may he dwell
    • no: for us
    • marutvān: the powerful (referring to the Maruts, storm gods)
    • parāvato: from the high (or elevated)
    • vā: or
    • sadanād: from the abode
    • ṛtasya: of truth or cosmic order
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the king of gods, asking him to come forth from various sources: the heavens, the earth, the waters, and even from the excrement, which symbolizes the cycle of life and fertility.
    • The mention of "svarṇarād" signifies a request for prosperity and abundance, indicating that the divine presence is sought in all aspects of life.
    • The reference to the Maruts emphasizes the power of nature and the forces that govern it, suggesting a connection between the divine and the natural world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain and favorable weather, similar to invoking Indra for blessings from the heavens and the earth.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek guidance and support from higher powers during challenging times, reflecting the desire for divine intervention in their struggles.
    • Communities often come together in rituals to celebrate the abundance of nature, acknowledging the interconnectedness of life and the divine forces that sustain it.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine blessings from various elements of nature, emphasizing the interconnectedness of life, prosperity, and cosmic order.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of harmony with nature and the divine, applicable in various real-life scenarios such as agriculture, personal challenges, and community celebrations.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to recognize and appreciate the abundance and support that comes from both the natural world and the divine realm.
