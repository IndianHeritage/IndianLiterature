यस्यामितानि वीर्याणि न राधः पर्येतवे |ज्योतिः न विश्वम् अभ्य अस्ति दक्षिणा ||
yasyāmitāni vīryā3 na rādhaḥ paryetave | jyotir na viśvam abhy asti dakṣiṇā ||
  • yasyāmitāni vīryāḥ: This phrase can be translated to "whose powers are immeasurable." It signifies the boundless strength and capabilities of a divine entity or force. In real life, this can be likened to the vast potential within each individual, where one can achieve great things when they harness their inner strength.
  • na rādhaḥ paryetave: This translates to "there is no limit to the reach of this power." It emphasizes that the influence and effectiveness of this power are not confined by any boundaries. For example, consider the impact of technology in today's world; it transcends geographical limitations and connects people globally, showcasing how certain powers can extend far beyond their origin.
  • jyotir na viśvam abhy asti: This means "there is no light that does not exist in the universe." It suggests that light, or knowledge and enlightenment, is omnipresent. In practical terms, this can be seen in the pursuit of education and wisdom, where knowledge is available to all who seek it, regardless of their background.
  • dakṣiṇā: This word translates to "the south" but can also imply generosity or offerings. In a broader context, it can represent the idea of giving back to the community or the world. For instance, charitable acts and social responsibility reflect this principle, where individuals or organizations contribute to the welfare of others.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of limitless potential, the omnipresence of knowledge, and the importance of generosity. In real life, it serves as a reminder that each person possesses immense capabilities that can impact the world positively. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge, push beyond their limits, and contribute to the greater good, fostering a sense of community and interconnectedness.
