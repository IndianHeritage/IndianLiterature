परागावो यवसं कचिदाघृणे नित्यं रेक्णो अमर्त्यः |अस्माकं पूषन्न अविताशिवो भव मंहिष्ठो वाजसातये ||
parā gāvo yavasaṁ kac cid āghṛṇe nityaṁ rekṇo amartya | asmākam pūṣann avitā śivo bhava maṁhiṣṭho vājasātaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • parā: supreme, beyond
    • gāvo: cows, representing wealth and nourishment
    • yavasaṁ: food, sustenance
    • kac: whether
    • cid: at all
    • āghṛṇe: to be invoked, to be called upon
    • nityaṁ: always, eternally
    • rekṇo: of the one who is immortal
    • amartya: immortal, divine
    • asmākam: our
    • pūṣann: O nourisher, provider
    • avitā: protector
    • śivo: auspicious, benevolent
    • bhava: become, may you be
    • maṁhiṣṭho: the best, the most excellent
    • vājasātaye: for the purpose of strength, power, and prosperity
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine forces for the attainment of prosperity and nourishment.
    • The mention of "cows" symbolizes wealth and sustenance, which were vital in ancient agrarian societies.
    • The phrase "O nourisher, provider" emphasizes the importance of divine support in our lives.
    • It reflects a desire for protection and auspiciousness in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for a good harvest, invoking divine blessings for prosperity.
    • In business, entrepreneurs seek guidance and protection from higher powers to ensure success and sustainability.
    • During festivals, people perform rituals to invoke blessings for health, wealth, and happiness in their families.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine support in our daily lives for prosperity and protection.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as agriculture, business, and personal well-being.
    • By invoking these divine qualities, individuals can foster a sense of security and abundance in their lives.
