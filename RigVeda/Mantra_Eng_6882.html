जुषाणो अङ्गिरस्तमेमā हव्याण्य āनुṣक |अग्नि यज्ञं नय ऋतुथा ||
juṣāṇo aṅgirastamemā havyāny ānuṣak | agne yajñaṁ naya ṛtuthā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • juṣāṇo: One who is pleased or satisfied.
    • aṅgirastamemā: O Agni, the divine fire, we invoke you.
    • havyāny: Offerings or oblations.
    • ānuṣak: To be accepted or received.
    • agne: O Agni, the fire god.
    • yajñaṁ: Sacrifice or worship.
    • naya: Lead or guide.
    • ṛtuthā: In accordance with truth or righteousness.
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking him to accept the offerings made during a sacrifice and to guide the worshippers in a righteous manner. It emphasizes the importance of divine acceptance and the need for guidance in performing rituals correctly.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is seen as a purifying element. For instance, in Hindu rituals, the sacred fire (Havan) is central to ceremonies, symbolizing the connection between the physical and spiritual realms.
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the idea of seeking guidance in our actions. For example, when making important decisions, individuals often seek advice from mentors or spiritual leaders to ensure they are on the right path.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of intention and purity in our actions. Just as offerings to Agni are made with devotion, our daily actions should be performed with sincerity and a sense of purpose.
    • In a broader context, it serves as a reminder to seek guidance and wisdom in our endeavors, ensuring that our choices align with truth and righteousness.
    • In contemporary society, this can be applied in various fields such as leadership, education, and personal development, where ethical decision-making and integrity are paramount.
