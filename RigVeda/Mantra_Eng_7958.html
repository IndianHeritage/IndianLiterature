प्र वाजम् इन्दुर इष्यति सिषासन वाजसः ऋषिः |व्रता विदान आयुधा ||
pra vājam indur iṣyati siṣāsan vājasā ṛṣiḥ | vratā vidāna āyudhā ||
  • pra vājam: This phrase can be translated as "the powerful or strong force." In a real-life context, this can refer to the strength and determination one needs to achieve their goals, much like a student preparing for an important exam or an athlete training for a competition.
  • indur: This word means "the moon" or "the divine." The moon is often associated with calmness and reflection. In daily life, this can symbolize the importance of taking time to reflect on our actions and decisions, similar to how one might meditate or practice mindfulness to gain clarity.
  • iṣyati: This translates to "is desired" or "is sought after." It emphasizes the importance of aspiration and ambition in life. For instance, a person may desire a promotion at work, which drives them to improve their skills and work ethic.
  • siṣāsan: This term can be interpreted as "the one who commands" or "the ruler." In a practical sense, this could refer to a leader in a community or organization who guides others towards a common goal, much like a project manager leading a team to complete a project successfully.
  • vājasā: This word means "strength" or "power." It signifies the inner strength that individuals possess. For example, someone overcoming personal challenges, such as health issues or financial difficulties, exemplifies this strength in their journey towards recovery and stability.
  • ṛṣiḥ: This translates to "sage" or "wise person." It highlights the value of wisdom and knowledge in making informed decisions. In real life, this can be seen in mentors or teachers who guide others with their experience and insights.
  • vratā: This word means "vow" or "commitment." It signifies the importance of dedication to one's goals. For instance, someone who commits to a fitness regimen or a study plan demonstrates this vow through their consistent efforts.
  • vidāna: This translates to "knowledge" or "understanding." It emphasizes the significance of acquiring knowledge in various aspects of life. For example, lifelong learners who pursue education and self-improvement embody this principle.
  • āyudhā: This word means "weapons" or "tools." In a metaphorical sense, it refers to the tools and resources we use to achieve our goals. For instance, a writer uses their pen and creativity as tools to express their thoughts and ideas.

Conclusion: The mantra "pra vājam indur iṣyati siṣāsan vājasā ṛṣiḥ | vratā vidāna āyudhā ||" encapsulates the essence of strength, wisdom, and commitment in the pursuit of one's goals. It serves as a reminder that to achieve success, one must harness their inner strength, seek knowledge, and remain dedicated to their aspirations. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to reflect on their ambitions, embrace their challenges, and utilize their resources effectively, ultimately leading to personal growth and fulfillment.
