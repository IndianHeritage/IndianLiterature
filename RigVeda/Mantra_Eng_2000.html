सूर्ये विषम् आ सजामि दृतिं सुरावतो गृहे |सो चिन नु न माराति नो वयम् मारामारे अस्य योजनाṁ हरिष्ठा मधु त्वा मधुला चकार ||
sūrye viṣam ā sajāmi dṛtiṁ surāvato gṛhe | so cin nu na marāti no vayam marāmāre asya yojanaṁ hariṣṭhā madhu tvā madhulā cakāra ||
  • Mantra: "sūrye viṣam ā sajāmi dṛtiṁ surāvato gṛhe | so cin nu na marāti no vayam marāmāre asya yojanaṁ hariṣṭhā madhu tvā madhulā cakāra ||"
  • Translation: "I place my strength in the sun, in the house of the divine. May he not harm me, nor may I be harmed by the one who is fierce. O sweet one, you have created sweetness for me."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • sūrye: Refers to the sun, symbolizing light, energy, and life.
    • viṣam: Means poison or something harmful, indicating challenges or negativity.
    • ā sajāmi: I place or I establish, suggesting an act of intention or focus.
    • dṛtiṁ: Strength or determination, representing inner resilience.
    • surāvato: Of the divine or celestial, indicating a higher power or spiritual influence.
    • gṛhe: In the house, symbolizing the personal space or environment.
    • so: He or that one, referring to the sun or divine energy.
    • cin: May he or may it be, a wish or prayer for protection.
    • nu: Not, indicating a negation of harm.
    • na marāti: May he not harm, a plea for safety.
    • no vayam: Nor may we, emphasizing collective protection.
    • marāmāre: Be harmed or killed, a strong expression of fear of danger.
    • asya: Of this, referring to the situation or context.
    • yojanaṁ: Plan or intention, indicating a strategy for life.
    • hariṣṭhā: Sweetness or pleasantness, representing positive outcomes.
    • madhu: Sweet, often used to denote something desirable or beneficial.
    • tvā: You, referring to the divine or the sun.
    • madhulā: Sweetness, reinforcing the theme of positivity.
    • cakāra: Created or made, indicating the act of bringing forth.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek strength and resilience when facing challenges, much like the mantra emphasizes placing strength in the sun. For instance, someone going through a tough time may find solace in nature, particularly in sunlight, which can uplift their spirits.
    • The idea of not being harmed can be seen in various protective rituals or practices, such as meditation or prayer, where individuals seek divine protection against negativity or harm.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can resonate with collective efforts to create a safe and nurturing environment, where everyone supports each other to overcome difficulties.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of strength, protection, and the creation of positivity in life. It serves as a reminder to seek inner resilience and divine support in the face of challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in personal struggles, community support systems, and spiritual practices aimed at fostering a safe and harmonious environment. By focusing on the positive and seeking divine guidance, individuals can navigate life's adversities with greater confidence and hope.
