ते नो वृष्टिं दिवस परि पवन्ताम् आ सुवीर्यम् |सुवानां देवास इन्दवः ||
te no vṛṣṭiṁ divas pari pavantām ā suvīryam | suvānā devāsa indavaḥ ||
  • te no vṛṣṭiṁ: This phrase translates to "may that rain (vṛṣṭi) be ours." In this context, "rain" symbolizes nourishment and abundance, essential for life and prosperity.
  • divas pari pavantām: This means "may it blow around us from the heavens." Here, "divas" refers to the sky or heavens, indicating a divine source of blessings.
  • ā suvīryam: This translates to "may it bring forth strength." The term "suvīryam" implies not just physical strength but also vitality and vigor in all aspects of life.
  • suvānā devāsa indavaḥ: This phrase means "the divine beings (devāsa) who are the bestowers of good fortune (suvānā)." "Indavaḥ" refers to the divine forces that grant prosperity and abundance.

The mantra as a whole can be interpreted as a prayer for divine blessings in the form of rain, which is essential for agriculture and sustenance. It expresses a desire for strength and prosperity to be bestowed upon the community by the divine forces.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Agricultural Practices: Farmers often pray for rain to ensure a good harvest. This mantra can be recited during rituals to invoke divine blessings for favorable weather conditions.
  • Community Gatherings: In times of drought or scarcity, communities can come together to recite this mantra, fostering a sense of unity and collective hope for abundance.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to seek strength and resilience in their personal and professional lives, invoking the idea that divine support is available for those who seek it.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine intervention in the form of rain, which symbolizes nourishment and prosperity. It highlights the interconnectedness of nature and human life, emphasizing the importance of seeking strength and abundance through spiritual means. In contemporary life, it serves as a reminder to appreciate the blessings of nature and to cultivate resilience in the face of challenges.
