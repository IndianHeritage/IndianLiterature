अस्मे इन्द्रो वरुणो मित्रो आर्यमाः dyumnaṁ यच्छन्तु महि शर्म सप्रथः |अवध्रं ज्योतिर्दितेर ऋतावृधो देवस्य श्लोकं सवितुर्मना महे ||
asme indro varuṇo mitro aryamā dyumnaṁ yacchantu mahi śarma saprathaḥ | avadhraṁ jyotir aditer ṛtāvṛdho devasya ślokaṁ savitur manāmahe ||
  • asme: This word translates to "to us" or "for us," indicating a personal or collective appeal.
  • indro: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • varuṇo: Refers to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water, representing truth and moral authority.
  • mitro: Means "friend," suggesting the importance of companionship and support in life.
  • aryamā: Represents the noble or honorable, emphasizing virtue and righteousness.
  • dyumnaṁ: Translates to "glory" or "fame," indicating the pursuit of honor and recognition.
  • yacchantu: Means "may they grant," expressing a wish or prayer for blessings.
  • mahi: Refers to "great" or "vast," often associated with the universe or the earth.
  • śarma: Means "comfort" or "shelter," highlighting the need for safety and security.
  • saprathaḥ: Translates to "together" or "in unity," emphasizing the importance of community and collaboration.
  • avadhraṁ: Means "unharmed" or "safe," indicating a desire for protection.
  • jyotir: Refers to "light," symbolizing knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment.
  • aditer: Refers to Aditi, the mother of gods, representing nurturing and abundance.
  • ṛtāvṛdho: Means "upholder of truth," emphasizing the importance of honesty and integrity.
  • devasya: Means "of the divine," indicating a connection to the spiritual or higher powers.
  • ślokaṁ: Refers to a "verse" or "hymn," indicating the poetic and sacred nature of the mantra.
  • savituh: Refers to Savitar, the sun god, symbolizing energy, vitality, and life-giving force.
  • manāmahe: Means "we meditate" or "we contemplate," indicating a focus on spiritual practice and reflection.

This mantra is a prayer for blessings from various deities, seeking strength, friendship, truth, and protection. It emphasizes the importance of community and the pursuit of glory while also highlighting the need for safety and enlightenment. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:


  • In personal relationships, fostering friendship and support among peers.
  • In leadership roles, embodying the qualities of strength and moral authority.
  • In community initiatives, working together for the common good and safety of all.
  • In personal development, seeking knowledge and enlightenment through meditation and reflection.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, encouraging individuals to seek strength, truth, and unity while also emphasizing the importance of spiritual growth. It serves as a reminder to cultivate positive relationships, uphold moral values, and strive for personal and communal well-being.
