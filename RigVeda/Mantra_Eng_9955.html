इन्द्रोऽस्मे सुमना अस्तु विश्वहā राजा सोमः सुवितस्याध्य एतु नः |यथा-यथा मित्रधितानि संधधुर आ सर्वतातिम अदितिं वृणीमहे ||
indro asme sumanā astu viśvahā rājā somaḥ suvitasyādhy etu naḥ | yathā-yathā mitradhitāni saṁdadhur ā sarvatātim aditiṁ vṛṇīmahe ||
  • Indro: Refers to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Asme: Means "may we be" or "let us be," indicating a desire or aspiration.
  • Sumanā: Translates to "good thoughts" or "auspicious intentions," emphasizing the importance of positive thinking.
  • Astu: Means "let there be" or "may it be," expressing a wish for something to come into existence.
  • Viśvahā: Refers to "all" or "universal," suggesting inclusivity and the idea of collective well-being.
  • Rājā: Means "king," symbolizing authority and governance.
  • Somaḥ: Refers to the sacred drink, soma, which is associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
  • Suvitasyādhy: Translates to "may he (Indra) come to us with good fortune," indicating a plea for blessings and prosperity.
  • Etū: Means "may he come," reinforcing the desire for divine presence.
  • Naḥ: Means "to us," indicating a personal connection to the divine.
  • Yathā-yathā: Means "as" or "in the way that," suggesting a conditional or comparative aspect.
  • Mitradhitāni: Refers to "friendship" or "allies," emphasizing the importance of relationships and cooperation.
  • Saṁdadhur: Means "to unite" or "to bring together," highlighting the significance of unity.
  • Ā: A preposition meaning "from" or "out of," indicating a source or origin.
  • Sarvatātim: Means "all-pervading" or "everywhere," suggesting omnipresence.
  • Aditiṁ: Refers to Aditi, the mother of gods, symbolizing boundlessness and the infinite.
  • Vṛṇīmahe: Means "we choose" or "we invoke," indicating an active participation in the process of seeking blessings.

Conclusion: This mantra is a prayer for divine guidance, strength, and unity. It emphasizes the importance of good thoughts, relationships, and the collective well-being of all. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as community gatherings, leadership roles, and personal development. By invoking positive intentions and seeking unity among friends and allies, individuals can foster a harmonious environment that promotes growth and prosperity. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine blessings and the aspiration for a life filled with auspiciousness and cooperation.
