तां बाहवां सुचेतुना प्र यन्तम अस्मा आर्चते |शेवं हि जार्यं वां विश्वासु क्षासु योगुवे ||
tā bāhavā sucetunā pra yantam asmā arcate | śevaṁ hi jāryaṁ vāṁ viśvāsu kṣāsu joguve ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tā: those
    • bāhavā: with strong arms
    • sucetunā: with pure intellect
    • pra: towards
    • yantam: the one who is moving
    • asmā: us
    • arcate: worships
    • śevaṁ: auspiciousness
    • hi: indeed
    • jāryaṁ: the one who is venerable
    • vāṁ: your
    • viśvāsu: in all
    • kṣāsu: in the realms
    • joguve: may be attained
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the reverence and worship directed towards those who possess strength and pure intellect.
    • It emphasizes the importance of auspiciousness and venerability in all realms of existence.
    • In a practical sense, this can be related to leaders or mentors in our lives who guide us with wisdom and strength.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a manager who leads with integrity and intelligence can be seen as embodying the qualities described in this mantra.
    • In education, a teacher who inspires students through their knowledge and character can be viewed as a figure of worship and respect.
    • In personal life, family members who provide support and guidance during tough times reflect the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in recognizing and honoring those who contribute positively to our lives through their strength and wisdom.
    • It encourages us to seek auspiciousness and guidance in all aspects of life, fostering a sense of community and respect.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger relationships, better leadership, and a more harmonious society.
