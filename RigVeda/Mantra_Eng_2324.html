मन्त्र: सिनीवाली पृथुष्टुके या देवानाम् असि स्वसा |जुषस्व हव्यं आहुतं प्रजां देवी दिदिद्धि नः ||
sinīvāli pṛthuṣṭuke yā devānām asi svasā | juṣasva havyam āhutam prajāṁ devi didiḍḍhi naḥ ||
  • sinīvāli: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is nourished" or "the one who provides nourishment." It signifies a nurturing aspect, often associated with the Earth or a mother figure.
  • pṛthuṣṭuke: This word refers to "the vastness" or "the expansive nature." It suggests a sense of abundance and the wide-reaching influence of the divine.
  • yā: This is a relative pronoun meaning "who" or "that," connecting the previous terms to the divine beings being addressed.
  • devānām: This translates to "of the gods," indicating that the mantra is directed towards divine entities.
  • asi: Meaning "you are," this word emphasizes the presence and essence of the divine beings being invoked.
  • svasā: This term means "sister," which can symbolize kinship, support, and unity among beings.
  • juṣasva: This is an imperative form meaning "may you enjoy" or "may you accept," indicating a request for the divine to partake in the offerings made.
  • havyam: Referring to "the offerings" or "sacrifices," this term highlights the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine.
  • āhutam: This means "that which is invoked" or "the offerings that are called upon," reinforcing the idea of ritualistic invocation.
  • prajāṁ: This translates to "the progeny" or "the beings," referring to the creation and the community of living beings.
  • devi: This term means "goddess," addressing the divine feminine aspect, which is often associated with creation and nurturing.
  • didiḍḍhi: This means "may you grant" or "may you bestow," indicating a plea for blessings or support from the divine.
  • naḥ: This translates to "us," indicating a collective plea or invocation for the community or individuals involved.

Conclusion: The mantra "sinīvāli pṛthuṣṭuke yā devānām asi svasā | juṣasva havyam āhutam prajāṁ devi didiḍḍhi naḥ ||" emphasizes the relationship between the divine and the earthly realm. It calls upon the nurturing aspects of the divine, asking for blessings and support for the community. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during rituals, community gatherings, or personal prayers where individuals seek guidance, nourishment, and protection from higher powers. It signifies the importance of connection, unity, and the role of the divine in the sustenance of life, reminding us to honor and acknowledge the forces that nurture and support us in our daily lives.
