पिन्वन्त्य अपो मरुतः सुदानवः पयो घृतवत् विदथेष्व आबुभवः |अत्यं न मिहे वि नयन्ति वाजिनं उत्सं दूहन्ति स्तनयन्तम् अक्षितम् ||
pinvanty apo marutaḥ sudānavaḥ payo ghṛtavad vidatheṣv ābhuvaḥ | atyaṁ na mihe vi nayanti vājinam utsaṁ duhanti stanayantam akṣitam ||
  • Pinvanty apo marutaḥ sudānavaḥ payo ghṛtavad vidatheṣv ābhuvaḥ:
    • Pinvanty: This word can be interpreted as "to bring forth" or "to produce." It signifies the act of creation or manifestation.
    • Apo: Refers to "waters," symbolizing nourishment, life, and purity.
    • Marutaḥ: This term denotes the "Maruts," the storm deities associated with wind and storms, representing power and dynamism.
    • Sudānavaḥ: Meaning "goodly" or "noble," it emphasizes the positive qualities of the elements being invoked.
    • Payo: Translates to "milk," a symbol of nourishment and sustenance.
    • Ghṛtavad: Meaning "like ghee," it signifies richness and purity, often associated with offerings in rituals.
    • Vidatheṣv: Refers to "in the gatherings" or "in the assemblies," indicating the importance of community and collective efforts.
    • Ābhuvaḥ: This word means "having arisen" or "having come into being," suggesting the emergence of positive forces.
  • Atyaṁ na mihe vi nayanti vājinam:
    • Atyaṁ: This can be interpreted as "beyond" or "transcending," indicating a higher state of being.
    • Na mihe: Means "I do not desire" or "I do not wish," suggesting a sense of detachment from material desires.
    • Vi nayanti: Translates to "they lead" or "they guide," indicating the role of divine forces in guiding individuals.
    • Vājinam: Refers to "the charioteer" or "the one who drives," symbolizing control and direction in life.
  • Utsaṁ duhanti stanayantam akṣitam:
    • Utsaṁ: This word means "the one who is strong" or "the powerful," indicating strength and resilience.
    • Duhanti: Translates to "they extract" or "they draw out," suggesting the process of gaining strength or resources.
    • Stanayantam: Refers to "the one who nourishes" or "the one who provides sustenance," emphasizing the nurturing aspect.
    • Akṣitam: Means "unfailing" or "eternal," indicating the everlasting nature of the divine forces at play.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, divine forces, and human existence. It highlights the importance of community, nourishment, and guidance in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to the way we seek support from our environment and each other, recognizing the divine presence in our daily interactions. The mantra serves as a reminder to appreciate the abundance around us and to cultivate a sense of gratitude and responsibility towards nature and our community. By embodying these principles, we can lead more fulfilling and harmonious lives.
