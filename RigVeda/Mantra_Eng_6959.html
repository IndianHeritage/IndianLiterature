स नो वाजेष्व अविता पुरूवसुः पुरःस्थाता मगवाऽ वृत्रहा भुवत ||
sa no vājeṣv avitā purūvasuḥ puraḥsthātā maghavā vṛtrahā bhuvat ||
  • sa - This word translates to "he" or "that," indicating a presence or a divine entity.
  • no - This means "our," suggesting a sense of belonging or connection.
  • vājeṣv - This term refers to "in the victories" or "in the wealth," often associated with success and prosperity.
  • avitā - This means "may protect" or "may guard," indicating a plea for divine protection.
  • purūvasuḥ - This can be interpreted as "the one who is rich in offerings," referring to a generous and bountiful being.
  • puraḥsthātā - This translates to "the one who stands before," suggesting a guiding presence or leader.
  • maghavā - This word means "the mighty one," often referring to a powerful deity or force.
  • vṛtrahā - This means "the slayer of Vritra," where Vritra symbolizes obstacles or challenges, indicating the removal of hindrances.
  • bhuvat - This translates to "may it be," expressing a wish or desire for something to come into being.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer for divine protection and guidance in achieving success and overcoming obstacles. It invokes a powerful presence that stands before us, ready to assist in our endeavors and ensure our victories.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: When facing challenges in personal development, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek guidance and protection from higher powers.
  • Business Ventures: Entrepreneurs may use this mantra to invoke success and protection in their business endeavors, ensuring they overcome obstacles and achieve prosperity.
  • Academic Pursuits: Students can recite this mantra before exams or important presentations, seeking the strength and clarity to succeed.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine support in our pursuits. It reminds us that with guidance and protection, we can overcome challenges and achieve our goals. By incorporating this mantra into our daily lives, we can foster a mindset of resilience and openness to the abundance that life has to offer.
