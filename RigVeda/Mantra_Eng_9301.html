अहं दां गृणते पूर्व्यं वस्व अहं ब्रह्म कृतवम् मह्यं वर्धनम् |अहम् भुवं यजमानस्य चोदितायज्वनः साक्षि विश्वस्मिन् भरे ||
ahaṁ dāṁ gṛṇate pūrvyaṁ vasv aham brahma kṛṇavam mahyaṁ vardhanam | aham bhuvaṁ yajamānasya coditāyajvanaḥ sākṣi viśvasmin bhare ||
  • ahaṁ dāṁ gṛṇate: This phrase translates to "I give" or "I offer." It signifies the act of giving or offering something valuable, which can be interpreted as a gesture of generosity or sharing.
  • pūrvyaṁ vasv: This means "the ancient wealth" or "the wealth of the past." It refers to the knowledge, traditions, and resources that have been passed down through generations, emphasizing the importance of heritage.
  • aham brahma kṛṇavam: This translates to "I create the ultimate reality" or "I am the essence of the universe." It highlights the connection between the individual and the cosmos, suggesting that each person has the potential to manifest their own reality.
  • mahyaṁ vardhanam: This means "growth for me" or "nourishment for me." It indicates a desire for personal development and the nurturing of one's own potential.
  • aham bhuvaṁ yajamānasya: This translates to "I am the one who offers to the world." It signifies the role of the individual in contributing to the greater good and participating in the collective rituals of life.
  • coditāyajvanaḥ: This means "inspired by the act of sacrifice." It refers to the motivation behind actions that benefit others, emphasizing the importance of selflessness and service.
  • sākṣi viśvasmin bhare: This translates to "as a witness in this universe." It signifies the awareness and mindfulness of one's actions and their impact on the world.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of selflessness, personal growth, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It encourages individuals to recognize their role in the universe, to give generously, and to draw upon the wisdom of the past while striving for personal and collective development. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, environmental conservation, and personal relationships, where the act of giving and nurturing leads to a more harmonious existence. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute positively to society and foster a sense of unity and purpose in their lives.
