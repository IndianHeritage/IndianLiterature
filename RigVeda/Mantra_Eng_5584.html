सहस्रशृङ्गो वृषभो यः समुद्राद उदाचरत् |तेन सहस्येन वयं न जनान स्वापयामसि ||
sahasraśṛṅgo vṛṣabho yaḥ samudrād udācarat | tenā sahasyenā vayaṁ ni janān svāpayāmasi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahasraśṛṅgo: One with a thousand horns
    • vṛṣabha: Bull or strong being
    • yaḥ: Who
    • samudrād: From the ocean
    • udācarat: Has emerged or arisen
    • tena: By that
    • sahasyenā: With strength or power
    • vayaṁ: We
    • ni: Not
    • janān: People or beings
    • svāpayāmasi: We make to sleep or rest
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful entity, often interpreted as a divine force or deity, that emerges from the ocean with immense strength.
    • The "thousand horns" symbolize the vastness and multifaceted nature of this power, suggesting that it can manifest in various forms and aspects.
    • The act of making beings "sleep" can be understood as providing peace, tranquility, or a state of rest, which can be both physical and spiritual.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, the ocean is often seen as a source of life and power. For instance, the emergence of a strong storm from the ocean can be likened to the "bull" that arises with strength, affecting everything in its path.
    • In a personal context, when individuals face overwhelming challenges, they may seek strength from within or from a higher power, akin to the divine force described in the mantra, to find peace and rest amidst turmoil.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of harnessing strength and power from the divine or nature to overcome challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that even in the face of adversity, there is a source of strength that can help us find peace and rest.
    • In real life, this can be applicable in various scenarios such as during stressful times, when seeking guidance, or when striving for personal growth and resilience.
