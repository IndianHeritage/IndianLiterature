तन्नस्तुरींपमध पोषयित्नु देव तवष्टर विगराणः स्यस्व |यतो वीरः कर्मण्यः सुदक्षो युक्तग्रावा जायते देवकामः ||
tan nas turīpam adha poṣayitnu deva tvaṣṭar vi rarāṇaḥ syasva | yato vīraḥ karmaṇyaḥ sudakṣo yuktagrāvā jāyate devakāmaḥ ||
  • tan nas turīpam: This phrase can be translated as "may that which is our strength." Here, "tan" refers to "that" or "that which," and "turīpam" signifies "strength" or "power." In real life, this can be understood as a call to harness our inner strength and capabilities to face challenges.
  • adha poṣayitnu: This means "nurture us from below." The word "adha" means "from below" or "beneath," and "poṣayitnu" refers to "nurturing" or "sustaining." This can be related to the idea of grounding oneself and drawing strength from one's roots, such as family, culture, or community support.
  • deva tvaṣṭar: This translates to "O divine craftsman." "Deva" means "divine" or "god," and "tvaṣṭar" refers to a creator or craftsman. In a real-life context, this can symbolize the importance of creativity and craftsmanship in our lives, encouraging us to be innovative and resourceful.
  • vi rarāṇaḥ syasva: This phrase means "may you be the one who creates." "Vi" can mean "in various ways," "rarāṇaḥ" refers to "creating," and "syasva" means "may you be." This encourages individuals to take initiative and be proactive in their endeavors, whether in personal projects or community efforts.
  • yato vīraḥ: This translates to "where the hero." "Yato" means "where," and "vīraḥ" refers to "hero" or "brave one." This can be interpreted as a reminder that true heroism lies in taking action and standing up for what is right, much like everyday heroes who make a difference in their communities.
  • karmaṇyaḥ sudakṣo: This means "skilled in action." "Karmaṇyaḥ" refers to "in action" or "in deeds," and "sudakṣo" means "skilled" or "capable." This emphasizes the importance of skill and diligence in our work, encouraging individuals to hone their abilities and strive for excellence in their chosen fields.
  • yuktagrāvā: This translates to "those who are united." "Yukta" means "united" or "connected," and "grāvā" refers to "those who gather." This highlights the significance of collaboration and teamwork in achieving common goals, as seen in successful organizations and communities that thrive on collective effort.
  • jāyate devakāmaḥ: This means "born of divine desire." "Jāyate" refers to "born" or "arising," and "devakāmaḥ" signifies "divine desire" or "wish of the gods." This can be interpreted as the idea that our aspirations and dreams are often aligned with a higher purpose, encouraging individuals to pursue their passions with dedication and faith.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about strength, nurturing, creativity, action, skill, unity, and divine purpose. Its significance lies in the encouragement it provides to individuals to harness their inner strength, seek support from their roots, and take proactive steps towards their goals. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, teamwork in professional settings, and community building. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can contribute positively to their lives and the lives of those around them, fostering a sense of purpose and fulfillment.
