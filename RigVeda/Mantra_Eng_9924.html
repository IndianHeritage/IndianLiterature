मां वो ऋषत खनिता यस्मै चाहं खनामि वः |द्विपच चतुष्पद अस्माकं सर्वमस्त्व अनातुरम् ||
mā vo riṣat khanitā yasmai cāhaṁ khanāmi vaḥ | dvipac catuṣpad asmākaṁ sarvam astv anāturam ||
  • mā vo riṣat: This phrase translates to "Do not be angry with us." It expresses a plea for understanding and compassion. In real life, this can be seen in situations where individuals seek forgiveness or understanding from others after a misunderstanding or conflict.
  • khanitā yasmai cāhaṁ khanāmi vaḥ: This means "I dig for you." The act of digging can symbolize effort, labor, or the pursuit of knowledge. In modern contexts, this can relate to the hard work one puts into relationships, careers, or personal growth, emphasizing the importance of dedication and effort in achieving goals.
  • dvipac catuṣpad: This translates to "two-footed and four-footed." It refers to all living beings, encompassing both humans (two-footed) and animals (four-footed). This highlights the interconnectedness of all life forms. In real life, this can be seen in ecological conservation efforts where humans strive to protect animal habitats and promote biodiversity.
  • asmākaṁ sarvam astv anāturam: This means "May all that is ours be free from harm." It is a wish for safety and well-being for oneself and one's community. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of creating safe environments, whether at home, in schools, or in workplaces, where individuals can thrive without fear of harm.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of compassion, effort, interconnectedness, and safety. It serves as a reminder to foster understanding in relationships, work diligently towards our goals, respect all forms of life, and create secure environments for ourselves and others. In today's world, these principles can guide personal interactions, community building, and environmental stewardship, making it a relevant and significant teaching for contemporary life.
