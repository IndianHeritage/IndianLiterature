प्रति प्रयाणम् असुरस्य विद्वान् सूक्तैः देवं सवितारं दुवास्य |उप ब्रुवीत नमसā विजानन् ज्येष्ठं च रत्नं विभजन्तम् आयोः ||
prati prayāṇam asurasya vidvān sūktair devaṁ savitāraṁ duvasya | upa bruvīta namasā vijānañ jyeṣṭhaṁ ca ratnaṁ vibhajantam āyoḥ ||
  • Mantra: "prati prayāṇam asurasya vidvān sūktair devaṁ savitāraṁ duvasya | upa bruvīta namasā vijānañ jyeṣṭhaṁ ca ratnaṁ vibhajantam āyoḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prati: towards
    • prayāṇam: the departure or journey
    • asurasya: of the demons or negative forces
    • vidvān: the wise or knowledgeable one
    • sūktair: with hymns or praises
    • devaṁ: the divine or god
    • savitāraṁ: the sun or the source of light
    • duvasya: of the two worlds or realms
    • upa: near or close
    • bruvīta: should speak or utter
    • namasā: with reverence or salutation
    • vijānañ: knowing or understanding
    • jyeṣṭhaṁ: the elder or the most significant
    • ca: and
    • ratnaṁ: the jewel or precious thing
    • vibhajantam: distributing or sharing
    • āyoḥ: of the two (or duality)
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the wise individual who, with the power of hymns, addresses the divine source of light, represented by Savitar, as they embark on a journey away from negative forces.
    • The phrase "prati prayāṇam asurasya" suggests a conscious effort to move away from darkness or ignorance, akin to how one might strive to overcome personal challenges or negative influences in life.
    • The invocation of Savitar symbolizes the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, much like how individuals seek guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders in their lives.
    • The act of "upa bruvīta namasā" emphasizes the importance of humility and respect when seeking wisdom, similar to how students approach their teachers with reverence.
    • The reference to "jyeṣṭhaṁ ca ratnaṁ vibhajantam" highlights the significance of sharing knowledge and resources, akin to how communities come together to support one another in times of need.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking knowledge and enlightenment while consciously distancing oneself from negativity.
    • It encourages individuals to approach wisdom with humility and to share their insights and resources with others, fostering a sense of community and support.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in educational settings, personal development journeys, and community-building efforts, where the focus is on growth, understanding, and mutual assistance.
