रात्रि व्याख्यद आयती पुरुत्रा देवी अ1क्षभिः |विश्वा अधि श्रियोऽधिता ||
rātrī vy akhyad āyatī purutrā devy a1kṣabhiḥ | viśvā adhi śriyo 'dhita ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • rātrī: Night
    • vy akhyad: has revealed
    • āyatī: the one who comes
    • purutrā: from all directions
    • devy: divine
    • a1kṣabhiḥ: with her eyes
    • viśvā: all
    • adhi: upon
    • śriyo: prosperity
    • adhita: has established
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the night as a divine entity that reveals itself and brings forth prosperity from all directions.
    • The night is personified as a goddess who watches over the world with her eyes, ensuring that all beings are protected and nurtured.
    • It emphasizes the importance of night in the cycle of life, as it is a time for rest, reflection, and rejuvenation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the night is associated with peace and tranquility. For instance, people often find solace in the quiet of the night, using it as a time for meditation or prayer.
    • Nighttime rituals, such as stargazing or moon worship, can be seen as ways to connect with the divine and seek guidance.
    • In modern life, the concept of "work-life balance" highlights the need for rest and recovery, akin to the nurturing aspect of the night described in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the night as a source of divine energy and prosperity.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the balance between activity and rest, reminding us that both are essential for a fulfilling life.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to create spaces in our lives for reflection and rejuvenation, allowing us to harness the benefits of both day and night.
