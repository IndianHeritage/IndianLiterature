विवेष यन् मां धिषणा जजाना स्तवै पुरा पार्याद इन्द्रं अहनः |अंहसो यत्र पीपरद यथा नो नावेव यान्तम् उभये हवन्ते ||
viveṣa yan mā dhiṣaṇā jajāna stavai purā pāryād indram ahnaḥ | aṁhaso yatra pīparad yathā no nāveva yāntam ubhaye havante ||
  • Viveṣa: This term can be understood as "to enter" or "to manifest." It signifies the act of coming into being or existence.
  • Yan: This word translates to "that" or "which," indicating a connection or reference to something specific.
  • Mā: Meaning "not," it serves as a negation, suggesting the absence of something.
  • Dhiṣaṇā: This refers to "intellect" or "wisdom," emphasizing the importance of mental clarity and understanding.
  • Jajāna: This term means "to be born" or "to arise," indicating the emergence of ideas or concepts.
  • Stavai: This can be interpreted as "to praise" or "to invoke," highlighting the act of reverence or acknowledgment.
  • Purā: Meaning "in the past," it connects the present understanding with historical or traditional knowledge.
  • Pāryād: This term can be understood as "surrounding" or "encompassing," suggesting a holistic view.
  • Indram: Referring to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
  • Ahnaḥ: This means "of the day," indicating the importance of time and the cyclical nature of existence.
  • Aṁhaso: This term refers to "light" or "brightness," symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
  • Yatra: Meaning "where," it indicates a location or state of being.
  • Pīparad: This can be interpreted as "to nourish" or "to sustain," emphasizing the importance of support and sustenance.
  • Yathā: Meaning "as" or "like," it draws a comparison or analogy.
  • No: This means "not," serving as a negation again.
  • Nāveva: This term refers to "a boat," symbolizing a means of navigation or journey.
  • Yāntam: Meaning "going" or "moving," it indicates progress or movement towards a goal.
  • Ubhaye: This means "both," suggesting duality or the coexistence of different elements.
  • Havante: This term refers to "offering" or "sacrifice," indicating the act of giving or devotion.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for the manifestation of wisdom and understanding, invoking the strength of Indra to guide and nourish our journey through life. It emphasizes the importance of clarity of thought and the need for support as we navigate our paths.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: When facing challenges, one can invoke the wisdom and strength symbolized by Indra to overcome obstacles and make informed decisions.
  • Leadership: Leaders can draw inspiration from this mantra to guide their teams with clarity and purpose, ensuring that they are well-supported in their endeavors.
  • Education: In the pursuit of knowledge, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek wisdom and understanding, fostering an environment of learning and growth.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the interplay between wisdom, strength, and the journey of life. It encourages individuals to seek clarity and support as they navigate their paths, reminding us that both knowledge and nourishment are essential for progress. By invoking these principles, we can enhance our personal and collective journeys, fostering a deeper connection with ourselves and the world around us.
