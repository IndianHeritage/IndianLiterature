साकंजानां सप्ततं आहुर एकजं षट् इद्यमाः ऋषयो देवजा इति |तेषां इष्टानि विहितानि धामशः स्थात्रे रेजन्ते विकृतानि रूपशः ||
sākaṁjānāṁ saptatham āhur ekajaṁ ṣaḻ id yamā ṛṣayo devajā iti | teṣām iṣṭāni vihitāni dhāmaśaḥ sthātre rejante vikṛtāni rūpaśaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sākaṁjānāṁ: Of those who are united or connected.
    • saptatham: Sevenfold or seven.
    • āhur: They say or declare.
    • ekajaṁ: One born or one entity.
    • ṣaḻ: Six.
    • id: This.
    • yamā: The Yamas or moral codes.
    • ṛṣayo: The sages or seers.
    • devajā: Born of the gods.
    • iti: Thus or so.
    • teṣām: Of them.
    • iṣṭāni: Desirable or worshipful things.
    • vihitāni: Established or set forth.
    • dhāmaśaḥ: In the abode or realm.
    • sthātre: In the place or position.
    • rejante: They shine or radiate.
    • vikṛtāni: Transformed or altered.
    • rūpaśaḥ: In forms or shapes.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the unity and interconnectedness of beings, emphasizing the importance of moral codes (Yamas) as established by sages.
    • The mention of "sevenfold" suggests completeness and the idea that there are multiple dimensions to existence.
    • The "one born" indicates a singular essence that connects all beings, while the "six" may refer to the various aspects of life or the senses.
    • The sages, being the seers of truth, guide us towards understanding the divine and the moral framework that governs our actions.
    • The "desirable things" signify the goals and aspirations that are aligned with higher principles, which are essential for spiritual growth.
    • The idea of shining in transformed forms suggests that through adherence to these principles, individuals can achieve enlightenment and radiate positivity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, when individuals come together with a shared purpose (sākaṁjānāṁ), they can achieve greater goals, much like a team working towards a common project.
    • The concept of Yamas can be seen in practices like yoga, where ethical guidelines help individuals lead a balanced and harmonious life.
    • In personal development, understanding one's singular essence (ekajaṁ) can lead to self-discovery and fulfillment, akin to finding one's passion or calling.
    • In organizations, establishing a set of core values (iṣṭāni) can guide decision-making and foster a positive work environment.
    • Transforming negative experiences (vikṛtāni) into learning opportunities can lead to personal growth and resilience.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity, moral principles, and the transformative power of understanding our interconnectedness.
    • It serves as a reminder that by adhering to ethical guidelines and recognizing our shared essence, we can create a more harmonious and enlightened society.
    • In real life, applying these teachings can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a deeper understanding of our place in the universe.
