पताति कुण्डृणाच्या दूरं वातो वनाद अधि |आ तु न इन्द्र शंसय गोष्व अश्वेषु शुभ्रिषु सहस्रेषु तुवीमघ ||
patāti kuṇḍṛṇācyā dūraṁ vāto vanād adhi | ā tū na indra śaṁsaya goṣv aśveṣu śubhriṣu sahasreṣu tuvīmagha ||
  • patāti kuṇḍṛṇācyā dūraṁ vāto vanād adhi: This line can be translated to mean "The wind blows far away from the forest." Here, "patāti" refers to the action of blowing or moving, "kuṇḍṛṇācyā" signifies something that is far or distant, "vāto" means wind, and "vanād" refers to the forest. This imagery evokes the idea of the wind carrying messages or energies from one place to another, emphasizing the connection between nature and the elements.
  • ā tū na indra śaṁsaya: This translates to "O Indra, do not hesitate." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, rain, and the heavens. This line calls upon Indra to act without doubt or hesitation, highlighting the importance of confidence and decisiveness in leadership and action.
  • goṣv aśveṣu śubhriṣu sahasreṣu tuvīmagha: This can be interpreted as "In the thousands of cows and horses, may we be blessed." "Goṣv" refers to cows, "aśveṣu" to horses, and "sahasreṣu" indicates a multitude or thousands. This line reflects the agricultural and pastoral lifestyle of the Vedic people, where wealth and prosperity were often measured in livestock. The invocation for blessings signifies a desire for abundance and success in one's endeavors.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates themes of nature, divine intervention, and prosperity. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of the elements, the importance of decisive action, and the blessings of abundance.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can serve as a reminder to embrace the natural world, act with confidence in our decisions, and seek abundance in our personal and professional lives. For instance, in business, leaders can draw inspiration from the call to act decisively, while individuals can reflect on their connection to nature and the importance of nurturing their environment.
