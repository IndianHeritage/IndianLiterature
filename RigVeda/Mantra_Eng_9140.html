अन्ये जायाṃ परि मृशन्त्य अस्य यस्यागृधद वेदने वाज्य अ1क्षः |पिता माता भ्रातर एनम् आहुर न जानीमो नयतā बद्धम् एतम् ||
anye jāyām pari mṛśanty asya yasyāgṛdhad vedane vājy a1kṣaḥ | pitā mātā bhrātara enam āhur na jānīmo nayatā baddham etam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • anye: others
    • jāyām: born
    • pari: around
    • mṛśanty: perceive or understand
    • asya: of this
    • yasyāgṛdhad: who has not grasped
    • vedane: in knowledge or understanding
    • vājy: in the context of the world
    • a1kṣaḥ: the eye or perception
    • pitā: father
    • mātā: mother
    • bhrātara: brother
    • enam: this
    • āhur: they say
    • na: not
    • jānīmo: we know
    • nayatā: by the guide
    • baddham: bound or tied
    • etam: this
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects on the nature of perception and understanding.
    • It suggests that while others may be born into this world and perceive it, they may not fully grasp the deeper knowledge or truth.
    • The mention of family (father, mother, brother) indicates that even those closest to us may not fully understand our experiences or the essence of our being.
    • It emphasizes the limitations of human understanding and the complexity of knowledge.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, parents may not fully understand the aspirations or struggles of their children, despite their close relationship.
    • In a workplace, colleagues may perceive a project differently, but only a few may grasp its true significance or potential impact.
    • In society, individuals may follow trends or beliefs without understanding their origins or implications, highlighting the difference between surface perception and deeper knowledge.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the limitations of our understanding and the importance of seeking deeper knowledge.
    • It encourages individuals to look beyond superficial perceptions and strive for a more profound comprehension of life and relationships.
    • In real-life scenarios, it is applicable in fostering empathy, encouraging open communication, and promoting a culture of understanding in families, workplaces, and communities.
