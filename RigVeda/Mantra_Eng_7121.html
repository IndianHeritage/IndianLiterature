अविप्रो वा यद अविधद विप्रो वेंद्र ते वचः |स प्र ममन्दत त्वायā शतक्रतो प्राचामन्यो अहंसन ||
avipro vā yad avidhad vipro vendra te vacaḥ | sa pra mamandat tvāyā śatakrato prācāmanyo ahaṁsana ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • avipro: Not a priest or a learned person
    • vā: or
    • yad: whatever
    • avidhad: has not done
    • vipro: a learned person or priest
    • vendra: O Indra, the king of gods
    • te: your
    • vacaḥ: words or speech
    • sa: he
    • pra: forth
    • mamandat: has come to me
    • tvāyā: to you
    • śatakrato: O hundred-folded (referring to Indra)
    • prācāmanyo: the ancient one
    • ahaṁsana: I am
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, the king of gods, and reflects a dialogue between a seeker and the divine.
    • The speaker acknowledges their own limitations, stating that they are not a learned person and have not performed the rituals or duties expected of a priest.
    • Despite this, the speaker calls upon Indra, expressing a desire for divine guidance and support.
    • The phrase "sa pra mamandat" indicates that the speaker has received inspiration or insight from Indra, suggesting that divine wisdom can transcend formal learning.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can resonate with individuals who feel unqualified or inadequate in their pursuits, such as students or professionals who doubt their abilities.
    • For instance, a student preparing for an important exam may feel overwhelmed and question their knowledge. This mantra serves as a reminder that seeking help from a higher power or wisdom can provide clarity and confidence.
    • Similarly, in leadership roles, a manager may feel unprepared to guide their team through challenges. By invoking the spirit of this mantra, they can seek inspiration and support from their experiences and intuition.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of humility and the recognition that knowledge and wisdom can come from various sources, not just formal education or status.
    • It encourages individuals to seek divine guidance and to trust in their own inner strength, regardless of their perceived limitations.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of self-doubt, encouraging individuals to reach out for support and to remain open to inspiration from the universe.
