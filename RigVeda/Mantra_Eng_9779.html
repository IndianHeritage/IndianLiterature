मन्त्र: कत्य अग्नयः कति सूर्यासः कत्य उषासः कत्य उ स्विद आपः |नोपस्पिजं वः पितरो वदामि पृच्छामि वः कवयो विद्मने कम ||
katy agnayaḥ kati sūryāsaḥ katy uṣāsaḥ katy u svid āpaḥ | nopaspijaṁ vaḥ pitaro vadāmi pṛcchāmi vaḥ kavayo vidmane kam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • katy: How many
    • agnayaḥ: of the fires
    • kati: how many
    • sūryāsaḥ: of the suns
    • kati: how many
    • uṣāsaḥ: of the dawns
    • kati: how many
    • u: and
    • svid: where
    • āpaḥ: are the waters
    • nopaspijaṁ: I do not know
    • vaḥ: your
    • pitaro: fathers
    • vadāmi: I speak
    • pṛcchāmi: I ask
    • vaḥ: your
    • kavayo: wise ones
    • vidmane: in the knowledge
    • kam: who
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a series of questions about the elements of nature, specifically the number of fires, suns, dawns, and waters.
    • It reflects a deep curiosity about the universe and the forces that govern it, indicating a quest for knowledge and understanding.
    • The speaker expresses a sense of humility by admitting their lack of knowledge and seeking answers from the wise.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern science, we often ask similar questions about the universe, such as the number of stars in the galaxy or the sources of energy that sustain life on Earth.
    • Environmentalists might reflect on the importance of water sources and their preservation, paralleling the inquiry about "where are the waters."
    • In personal growth, individuals often seek wisdom from mentors or knowledgeable figures, akin to asking the "wise ones" for guidance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of inquiry and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • It teaches us to recognize our limitations and the value of seeking wisdom from others.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as education, mentorship, and environmental awareness, encouraging a continuous quest for understanding and respect for nature.
