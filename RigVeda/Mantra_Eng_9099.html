यो वो वृताभ्यः अकृतोऽ उ लोकं यो वो मह्याः अभिशस्तेर अमुञ्चत् |तस्मा इन्द्राय मधुमन्तं ऊर्मिं देवमादनं प्र हिणोतनापः ||
yo vo vṛtābhyo akṛṇod u lokaṁ yo vo mahyā abhiśaster amuñcat | tasmā indrāya madhumantam ūrmiṁ devamādanam pra hiṇotanāpaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • vo: for you
    • vṛtābhyo: from the covered or hidden
    • akṛṇod: has created
    • u: and
    • lokaṁ: the world
    • yo: who
    • vo: for you
    • mahyā: for the great
    • abhiśaster: of the powerful
    • amuñcat: has released
    • tasmā: therefore
    • indrāya: to Indra
    • madhumantam: the sweet or rich
    • ūrmiṁ: wave or flow
    • devamādanam: divine delight
    • pra: forth
    • hiṇotanāpaḥ: may bring forth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine power that creates and sustains the world.
    • It emphasizes the importance of Indra, the king of gods, who is associated with strength, rain, and the nurturing of life.
    • The phrase "from the covered or hidden" suggests that there are forces at play in the universe that are not immediately visible but are essential for creation and sustenance.
    • The invocation of Indra for the "sweet wave" signifies a request for abundance, prosperity, and the flow of positive energy in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain and favorable conditions, similar to invoking Indra for blessings. The mantra can be seen as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature and the divine.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek guidance and strength during challenging times, looking for the "sweet wave" of support from friends, family, or a higher power.
    • In community gatherings, the mantra can be recited to foster unity and collective strength, invoking the divine to bless the community with prosperity and harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine intervention in the creation and sustenance of life.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek strength and abundance from higher powers, whether in personal struggles or communal efforts.
    • In essence, it encourages individuals to recognize the unseen forces that support and nurture life, fostering gratitude and reverence for the divine.
