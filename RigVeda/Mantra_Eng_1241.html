क्षत्राय तवṁ श्रवसे तवṁ महीयाऽइष्टये तवṁ अर्थमिव तवṁ इत्यै |विसदृशा जीविताभिप्रचक्षु उषा अजिगर भुवनानि विश्वा ||
kṣatrāya tvaṁ śravase tvam mahīyā iṣṭaye tvam artham iva tvam ityai | visadṛśā jīvitābhipracakṣa uṣā ajīgar bhuvanāni viśvā ||
  • kṣatrāya tvaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "You are for the Kshatriya." The Kshatriyas are the warrior class in the traditional Indian social hierarchy. This indicates a connection to strength, protection, and governance.
  • śravase tvam: This means "You are for fame." It emphasizes the importance of reputation and honor, suggesting that the subject of the mantra is associated with glory and recognition.
  • mahīyā: Translated as "greatness," this word signifies the vastness and importance of the subject, indicating that it holds a significant place in the universe.
  • iṣṭaye tvam: This translates to "You are for the desires." It suggests that the subject fulfills the aspirations and wishes of individuals, highlighting its role in achieving goals.
  • artham iva tvam: This means "You are like wealth." Here, 'artha' refers to material wealth and prosperity, indicating that the subject is a source of abundance and resources.
  • ityai: This word can be understood as "thus" or "therefore," linking the previous statements to a conclusion or a deeper understanding.
  • visadṛśā: This translates to "with clear vision." It implies clarity and insight, suggesting that the subject provides understanding and wisdom.
  • jīvitābhipracakṣa: This phrase means "the one who reveals life." It indicates that the subject has the power to illuminate and enhance life experiences.
  • uṣā: Translated as "dawn," it symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the arrival of light after darkness.
  • ajīgar: This means "the one who encompasses." It suggests that the subject has a vast reach and influence over the world.
  • bhuvanāni viśvā: This translates to "all the worlds." It indicates that the subject has a universal presence and significance across different realms of existence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, clarity, and abundance. It signifies the importance of having a clear vision and the ability to fulfill desires while also emphasizing the role of greatness and fame in one's life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to leadership roles, where individuals are expected to protect and guide others while also striving for personal and communal goals. It serves as a reminder that true greatness comes from the ability to inspire and uplift others, much like the dawn that brings light to the world. This mantra encourages individuals to seek clarity in their pursuits and to recognize the interconnectedness of their aspirations with the greater universe.
