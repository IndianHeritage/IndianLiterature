एष छागः पुरो अश्वेन वाजिना पूष्णो भागो नीयते विश्वदेव्यः | अभिप्रियं यत पुरोळाशम अरवतā त्वष्टेद एनं सौश्रवसाया जिन्वति ||
eṣa cchāgaḥ puro aśvena vājinā pūṣṇo bhāgo nīyate viśvadevyaḥ | abhipriyaṁ yat puroḻāśam arvatā tvaṣṭed enaṁ sauśravasāya jinvati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣa: this
    • cchāgaḥ: goat (often symbolizes sacrifice)
    • puro: in front
    • aśvena: by the horse (symbolizing speed and power)
    • vājinā: by the charioteer (representing guidance)
    • pūṣṇo: of the nourisher (Pūṣa is a deity associated with nourishment)
    • bhāgo: portion or share
    • nīyate: is led or taken
    • viśvadevyaḥ: of all the deities
    • abhipriyaṁ: that which is dear or pleasing
    • yat: which
    • puroḻāśam: the one who is in front (often refers to the leader or the foremost)
    • arvatā: of the strong or powerful
    • tvaṣṭed: may you create or fashion
    • enaṁ: this
    • sauśravasāya: for the one who is renowned or famous
    • jinvati: may it thrive or prosper
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the imagery of a goat being led by a powerful horse and charioteer, symbolizing the journey of life and the guidance we receive from divine forces.
    • The goat represents sacrifice and the offerings we make in our lives, while the horse signifies strength and speed, indicating the importance of moving forward with purpose.
    • Pūṣa, as the nourisher, emphasizes the need for sustenance—both physical and spiritual—as we navigate our paths.
    • The mention of "viśvadevyaḥ" highlights the interconnectedness of all deities, suggesting that our actions and sacrifices are recognized and supported by a greater cosmic order.
    • The phrase "may you create or fashion" indicates a plea for divine assistance in shaping our destinies, while "for the one who is renowned" suggests that our efforts should lead to recognition and success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to seek guidance from mentors (the charioteer) as we pursue our career goals (the horse) while being willing to make sacrifices (the goat) for our aspirations.
    • In personal relationships, it emphasizes the importance of nourishing connections and being supportive of one another's journeys, recognizing that we are all part of a larger community (viśvadevyaḥ).
    • In spiritual practices, it encourages individuals to offer their intentions and efforts to a higher power, trusting that their sacrifices will lead to growth and recognition in their spiritual journey.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of guidance, sacrifice, and nourishment in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the divine forces that support us as we navigate our paths, urging us to make meaningful contributions and sacrifices.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek guidance, support one another, and trust in the process of growth and recognition, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
