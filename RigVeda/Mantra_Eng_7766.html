मघोन आ पवस्व नो जहि विश्वा अप द्विषः |इन्दो सखायम् आ विश ||
maghona ā pavasva no jahi viśvā apa dviṣaḥ | indo sakhāyam ā viśa ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • maghona: of great strength or power
    • ā: to, towards
    • pavasva: may you purify or cleanse
    • no: our
    • jahi: do not abandon or forsake
    • viśvā: all, every
    • apa: away, from
    • dviṣaḥ: enemies or those who oppose
    • indo: O Indra, the deity
    • sakhāyam: as a friend or companion
    • ā: to, towards
    • viśa: all, every
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the god of rain and thunder, asking for his strength and support.
    • The speaker requests Indra to purify their surroundings and protect them from all enemies.
    • It emphasizes the importance of friendship and unity in overcoming challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community facing natural disasters, this mantra can be a source of hope, invoking divine protection and strength.
    • In personal relationships, it can symbolize the need for support and loyalty among friends during tough times.
    • In a workplace, it can represent teamwork and collaboration to overcome challenges and adversities.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance and the importance of unity in facing adversities.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as community resilience, personal relationships, and teamwork.
    • By invoking the strength of Indra, individuals and communities can find the courage to confront challenges and foster a spirit of cooperation.
