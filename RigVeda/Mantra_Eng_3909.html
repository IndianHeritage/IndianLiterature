न पञ्चभिर दशभिर वष्ट्य आरभं नासुन्वता सचते पुष्यतां चन |जिनाति वेद अमुयां हन्ति वा धुनिरा देवयुम्भजति गोमती व्रजे ||
na pañcabhir daśabhir vaṣṭy ārabhaṁ nāsunvatā sacate puṣyatā cana | jināti ved amuyā hanti vā dhunir ā devayum bhajati gomati vraje ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na: not
    • pañcabhir: by five
    • daśabhir: by ten
    • vaṣṭy: in the midst
    • ārabhaṁ: beginning
    • nāsunvatā: with the breath
    • sacate: to be present
    • puṣyatā: with prosperity
    • cana: indeed
    • jināti: conquers
    • ved: knowledge
    • amuyā: this knowledge
    • hanti: destroys
    • vā: or
    • dhunir: the sound
    • ā: to
    • devayum: divine
    • bhajati: worships
    • gomati: with wisdom
    • vraje: in the realm
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of knowledge and wisdom in overcoming challenges.
    • The phrase "na pañcabhir daśabhir" suggests that neither five nor ten can begin the journey without the right knowledge.
    • "nāsunvatā sacate puṣyatā cana" implies that true presence and prosperity come from understanding and wisdom.
    • The mantra emphasizes that knowledge conquers ignorance and can destroy obstacles, as indicated by "jināti ved amuyā hanti vā."
    • It also highlights the significance of divine worship and wisdom in achieving success in one's endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, students who seek knowledge and understanding are more likely to succeed than those who rely solely on rote memorization.
    • In business, companies that prioritize innovation and knowledge management often outperform their competitors.
    • In personal development, individuals who invest time in learning and self-improvement tend to navigate life's challenges more effectively.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the transformative power of knowledge and wisdom in our lives.
    • It teaches us that true prosperity and success are rooted in understanding and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in education, business, or personal growth, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide us toward achieving our goals and overcoming obstacles.
