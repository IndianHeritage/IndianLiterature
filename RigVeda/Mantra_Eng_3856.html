अवाचचक्षम् पदम् अस्य सस्वर उग्रं निधातुर अन्वायम् इच्छन् |अपृच्छम् अन्याम् उता ते मा आहुर इन्द्रं नरो बुबुधानाऽशेम ||
avācacakṣam padam asya sasvar ugraṁ nidhātur anv āyam icchan | apṛccham anyām̐ uta te ma āhur indraṁ naro bubudhānā aśema ||
  • avācacakṣam: This term can be broken down into "ava" (down) and "cacakṣam" (to see or perceive). It suggests a deep understanding or insight into something that is often hidden or not easily perceived.
  • padam: This means "foot" or "step," often symbolizing a path or direction in life.
  • asya: This translates to "of this," indicating possession or relation to something specific.
  • sasvar: This word means "with sound" or "resounding," which can imply clarity or the presence of truth.
  • ugraṁ: This term means "fierce" or "powerful," often associated with strength and intensity.
  • nidhātur: This means "to place" or "to establish," indicating the act of creating or setting something in motion.
  • anv āyam: This can be interpreted as "towards the goal" or "in pursuit of," suggesting a journey or quest.
  • icchan: This means "desiring" or "wishing," indicating intention or aspiration.
  • apṛccham: This translates to "I asked" or "I inquired," suggesting a quest for knowledge or understanding.
  • anyām̐: This means "other" or "different," indicating a comparison or contrast with something else.
  • uta: This word means "or," presenting an alternative or choice.
  • te: This translates to "your," indicating possession or relation to the subject being addressed.
  • ma: This means "not" or "no," often used to negate something.
  • āhur: This translates to "they said" or "they declared," indicating a statement or belief held by others.
  • indraṁ: This refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power, authority, and protection.
  • naro: This means "man" or "human," indicating the subject of the inquiry or the one seeking knowledge.
  • bubudhānā: This translates to "who are wise" or "who understand," referring to those with insight or knowledge.
  • aśema: This means "may we prosper" or "may we thrive," indicating a wish for success and well-being.

The mantra can be interpreted as a profound inquiry into the nature of existence and the pursuit of knowledge. It reflects a desire to understand the deeper truths of life, to seek guidance from powerful forces (like Indra), and to aspire for prosperity and wisdom.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek deeper understanding and insight into their lives, fostering a mindset of inquiry and growth.
  • In leadership, it emphasizes the importance of wisdom and strength in guiding others, akin to how Indra leads the gods.
  • In community building, it highlights the significance of collective wisdom and the pursuit of common goals for prosperity.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for introspection, the pursuit of knowledge, and the aspiration for a prosperous life. It serves as a reminder that true understanding often requires asking the right questions and seeking guidance from those who possess wisdom. In a world filled with distractions, this mantra encourages individuals to focus on their goals and the deeper truths that govern their existence.
