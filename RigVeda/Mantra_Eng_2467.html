तिस्रो यह्वस्य समिधः परिज्मनोऽग्नेर अपुनन्न उशिजो अमृत्यवः |तासां एकाम अदधुर मर्त्ये भुजम् उ लोकम् उ द्वे उप जामिम ईयतुः ||
tisro yahvasya samidhaḥ parijmano 'gner apunann uśijo amṛtyavaḥ | tāsām ekām adadhur martye bhujam u lokam u dve upa jāmim īyatuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tisro: three
    • yahvasya: of the sacrifice
    • samidhaḥ: the fuel or offerings
    • parijmano: surrounding
    • 'gner: of fire
    • apunann: not returning
    • uśijo: the wise or learned
    • amṛtyavaḥ: those who are immortal
    • tāsām: of them
    • ekām: one
    • adadhur: they placed
    • martye: in the mortal
    • bhujam: the enjoyment or consumption
    • u: and
    • lokam: the world
    • u: and
    • dve: two
    • upa: near
    • jāmim: the offspring or progeny
    • īyatuḥ: may they go or may they proceed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the offerings made in a sacrificial fire, emphasizing the importance of the three types of offerings that surround the fire.
    • The phrase "not returning" suggests that these offerings are made with the intention of achieving something greater, perhaps spiritual enlightenment or connection with the divine.
    • The wise or learned individuals are highlighted as those who understand the significance of these offerings, leading to immortality or transcendence beyond the physical realm.
    • The mantra concludes with a call for the blessings of the offerings to be directed towards the mortal world and its inhabitants, ensuring prosperity and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the practice of making sacrifices for the greater good, such as volunteering time or resources to help others.
    • It can also be seen in the context of environmental conservation, where individuals and communities make sacrifices to protect nature for future generations.
    • In personal development, the mantra encourages individuals to invest in their growth and learning, understanding that the knowledge gained can lead to a more fulfilling life.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the importance of selfless offerings and the pursuit of higher knowledge.
    • It teaches that true fulfillment comes from contributing to the well-being of others and the world around us.
    • In a practical sense, it encourages individuals to reflect on their actions and consider how they can make meaningful contributions to society, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
