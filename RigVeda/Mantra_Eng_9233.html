आराच्छत्रुमप बाधस्व दूरम् उग्रो यः शम्भः पुरुहूत तेन |अस्मे धेहि यवमद गोमद इन्द्र कृधī धियं जारित्रे वाजरत्नाम ||
ārāc chatrum apa bādhasva dūram ugro yaḥ śambaḥ puruhūta tena | asme dhehi yavamad gomad indra kṛdhī dhiyaṁ jaritre vājaratnām ||
  • Transliteration: ārāc chatrum apa bādhasva dūram ugro yaḥ śambaḥ puruhūta tena | asme dhehi yavamad gomad indra kṛdhī dhiyaṁ jaritre vājaratnām ||
  • Meaning:
    • ārāc: From here or from this place.
    • chatrum: Enemies or obstacles.
    • apa: Away or remove.
    • bādhasva: May you not hinder or obstruct.
    • dūram: Far away.
    • ugro: Powerful or fierce.
    • yaḥ: Who.
    • śambaḥ: A term often associated with strength or might.
    • puruhūta: One who is invoked or called upon frequently.
    • tena: By that or through that.
    • asme: To us.
    • dhehi: Grant or bestow.
    • yavamad: The best of grains or nourishment.
    • gomad: Wealth or prosperity, often associated with cattle.
    • indra: The king of gods, representing strength and power.
    • kṛdhī: May you inspire or empower.
    • dhiyaṁ: Intellect or understanding.
    • jaritre: In the context of protection or safeguarding.
    • vājaratnām: The jewel of strength or the powerful one.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for protection and strength against adversities and obstacles in life.
    • It invokes the divine power of Indra, asking for the removal of enemies and hindrances that may obstruct one's path.
    • The reference to "yavamad" and "gomad" signifies a request for nourishment and prosperity, highlighting the importance of sustenance and wealth in achieving success.
    • In real life, this mantra can be related to situations where individuals face challenges, such as in competitive environments like exams, job interviews, or business ventures.
    • For example, a student preparing for an important exam may recite this mantra to seek clarity of mind and the removal of distractions or stress.
    • Similarly, an entrepreneur may use this mantra to invoke strength and protection against market competition and financial hurdles.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to empower individuals to overcome challenges and seek divine assistance in their endeavors.
    • It serves as a reminder that with strength, intellect, and divine support, one can navigate through life's obstacles effectively.
    • This mantra can be applicable in various real-life scenarios, including personal struggles, professional challenges, and even in collective efforts such as community projects.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a mindset of resilience and faith in the face of adversity, fostering a sense of hope and determination.
