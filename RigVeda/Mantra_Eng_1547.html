त्वया ह्य अग्ने वरुणो धृतव्रतो मित्रः शाशाद्रे आर्यमाः सुदानवः |यत सिम अनु क्रतुनā विश्वथा विभुर अरान न नेमिः परिभूर अजायथाḥ ||
tvayā hy agne varuṇo dhṛtavrato mitraḥ śāśadre aryamā sudānavaḥ | yat sīm anu kratunā viśvathā vibhur arān na nemiḥ paribhūr ajāyathāḥ ||
  • tvayā hy agne - "Indeed, O Agni,"
  • varuṇo dhṛtavrato - "Varuna, the one who holds the vow,"
  • mitraḥ - "Mitra, the friend,"
  • śāśadre - "the eternal,"
  • aryamā sudānavaḥ - "Aryama, the one who brings prosperity,"
  • yat sīm anu kratunā - "Whatever is achieved through effort,"
  • viśvathā vibhur - "may it be abundant everywhere,"
  • arān na nemiḥ - "may the non-evil not be obstructed,"
  • paribhūr ajāyathāḥ - "may the all-encompassing not be born."

This mantra invokes the divine forces of Agni (the fire god), Varuna (the god of cosmic order), and Mitra (the god of friendship and harmony). It emphasizes the importance of unity and cooperation among these divine entities to ensure prosperity and well-being in the world.


In real life, this mantra can be seen as a call for collaboration and harmony among individuals and communities. For example, in a workplace setting, when team members work together with a shared vision and mutual respect, they can achieve greater success. Similarly, in a community, when people come together to support each other, they create a stronger and more resilient society.


The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of working together for the common good. It encourages individuals to strive for collective goals while respecting the diverse roles that each person plays in the larger tapestry of life.


In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the values of cooperation, harmony, and shared purpose. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to community building and organizational success, highlighting the need for unity in diversity.
