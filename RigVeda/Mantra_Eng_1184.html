यद इन्द्राग्नī अवमस्यां पृथिव्यां मध्यमस्यां परमस्यां उता स्थः |अतः परि वृषणाव् आ हि यातम् अथ सोमस्य पिबतं सुतस्य ||
yad indrāgnī avamasyām pṛthivyām madhyamasyām paramasyām uta sthaḥ | ataḥ pari vṛṣaṇāv ā hi yātam athā somasya pibataṁ sutasya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • indrāgnī: Indra and Agni (the deities representing strength and fire)
    • avamasyām: in the lower realms
    • pṛthivyām: on the earth
    • madhyamasyām: in the middle realms
    • paramasyām: in the higher realms
    • uta: and
    • sthaḥ: established
    • ataḥ: therefore
    • pari: all around
    • vṛṣaṇāv: the powerful ones
    • ā hi: indeed
    • yātam: let us go
    • athā: and then
    • somasya: of Soma (the divine nectar)
    • pibataṁ: drink
    • sutasya: of the brewed (soma)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the deities Indra and Agni, representing strength and fire, to bless the earth and the realms above.
    • It emphasizes the interconnectedness of different realms—lower, middle, and higher—suggesting a holistic view of existence.
    • The call to drink Soma symbolizes the pursuit of divine knowledge and spiritual enlightenment, akin to seeking wisdom in our daily lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, invoking the strength of Indra and Agni can be likened to fostering teamwork and innovation, where each member contributes to a common goal.
    • In personal development, the idea of drinking Soma can be compared to engaging in continuous learning and self-improvement, much like attending workshops or reading books to gain knowledge.
    • In environmental conservation, recognizing the earth (pṛthivyām) and its resources can inspire actions that promote sustainability and respect for nature.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking strength and wisdom from the divine while acknowledging the interconnectedness of all realms of existence.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to community building and environmental stewardship.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can strive for a balanced and enlightened life, contributing positively to society and the world.
