दूनाशं सख्यं तव गौर असि वीर गव्यते |अश्वो अश्वायते भव ||
dūṇāśaṁ sakhyaṁ tava gaur asi vīra gavyate | aśvo aśvāyate bhava ||
  • Mantra: "dūṇāśaṁ sakhyaṁ tava gaur asi vīra gavyate | aśvo aśvāyate bhava ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dūṇāśaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the one who brings together" or "the unifier."
    • sakhyaṁ: This means "friendship" or "companionship."
    • tava: This translates to "your" or "of you."
    • gaur: This refers to "the cow," which is often symbolic of wealth and nourishment in Vedic literature.
    • asi: This means "you are" or "you exist."
    • vīra: This translates to "hero" or "brave one."
    • gavyate: This can be understood as "is being praised" or "is being honored."
    • aśvo: This means "horse," symbolizing strength and speed.
    • aśvāyate: This can be interpreted as "becoming like a horse" or "gaining the qualities of a horse."
    • bhava: This translates to "be" or "become."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and friendship, suggesting that through companionship, one can achieve greatness.
    • The cow symbolizes nourishment and prosperity, indicating that through strong relationships, one can attain wealth and sustenance.
    • The mention of the horse signifies strength and agility, suggesting that one should aspire to embody these qualities in their life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, fostering a sense of camaraderie among colleagues can lead to increased productivity and a supportive environment.
    • In personal relationships, nurturing friendships can provide emotional support and lead to a fulfilling life.
    • In sports, teamwork and unity among players can enhance performance and lead to victory.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of friendship and unity in achieving success and prosperity.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate strong relationships and embody qualities of strength and agility in their pursuits.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, enhanced relationships, and overall well-being.
