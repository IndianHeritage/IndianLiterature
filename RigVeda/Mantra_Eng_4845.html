दिवस पृथिव्याः पर्य ओज उdbhृतं वनस्पतिभ्यः पर्य आभृतं सहः |अपाम ओज्मानं परि गोभिर आवृतं इन्द्रस्य वज्रं हविषा रथं यज ||
divas pṛthivyāḥ pary oja udbhṛtaṁ vanaspatibhyaḥ pary ābhṛtaṁ sahaḥ | apām ojmānam pari gobhir āvṛtam indrasya vajraṁ haviṣā rathaṁ yaja ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • divas: of the heavens
    • pṛthivyāḥ: of the earth
    • pary: surrounding
    • oja: energy or strength
    • udbhṛtaṁ: that which is brought forth
    • vanaspatibhyaḥ: from the plants or vegetation
    • sahaḥ: together
    • apām: of the waters
    • ojmānam: the energy of the waters
    • pari: all around
    • gobhir: with the cows or cattle
    • āvṛtam: enveloped or surrounded
    • indrasya: of Indra (the king of gods)
    • vajraṁ: the thunderbolt or weapon
    • haviṣā: with offerings
    • rathaṁ: the chariot
    • yaja: to worship or to offer
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the interconnectedness of nature, emphasizing the energy derived from the heavens and the earth.
    • It highlights the importance of plants and water in sustaining life, suggesting that they are sources of strength and vitality.
    • The reference to Indra and his thunderbolt symbolizes power and protection, indicating that divine forces are invoked through offerings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain and good harvests, recognizing the vital role of water and soil in their livelihoods.
    • In modern environmental movements, there is a growing awareness of the need to protect natural resources, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on the energy of nature.
    • In spiritual practices, rituals involving offerings to deities can be seen as a way to connect with divine energies, similar to the worship of Indra in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of harmony between humans and nature, urging us to recognize and respect the energies that sustain life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from agriculture to environmental conservation and spiritual practices, reminding us of our dependence on the natural world.
    • Ultimately, it calls for a reverence towards the divine forces that govern our existence, encouraging us to live in balance with the earth and its resources.
