ओ त्ये नर इन्द्रं ऊतये गुरु नू चित तान सद्यो अध्वनो जगम्यत् |देवासो मन्युं दासस्य श्चमनन् ते न आ वक्षन् सुविताय वर्णम् ||
o tye nara indram ūtaye gur nū cit tān sadyo adhvano jagamyāt | devāso manyuṁ dāsasya ścamnan te na ā vakṣan suvitāya varṇam ||
  • Mantra: "o tye nara indram ūtaye gur nū cit tān sadyo adhvano jagamyāt | devāso manyuṁ dāsasya ścamnan te na ā vakṣan suvitāya varṇam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • o tye: O you (plural),
    • nara: men or humans,
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods,
    • ūtaye: for the purpose of elevation or upliftment,
    • gur: great or heavy,
    • nū: indeed,
    • cit: knowledge or consciousness,
    • tān: those,
    • sadyo: immediately,
    • adhvano: on the path,
    • jagamyāt: may we reach or attain,
    • devāso: the gods,
    • manyuṁ: anger or wrath,
    • dāsasya: of the servant or devotee,
    • ścamnan: may they not harm,
    • te: you (plural),
    • na: not,
    • ā: towards,
    • vakṣan: speak or declare,
    • suvitāya: for the well-being,
    • varṇam: color or quality.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the collective strength and wisdom of humanity, asking for the blessings of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who symbolizes power and leadership.
    • The phrase "for the purpose of elevation" suggests a desire for spiritual and moral upliftment, which can be seen in real-life scenarios such as community service or social justice movements where individuals come together to improve society.
    • The call for "knowledge" emphasizes the importance of wisdom in guiding actions, akin to how educators and mentors shape the future of individuals and communities.
    • The mention of "immediate attainment on the path" reflects the urgency of pursuing goals, similar to how athletes train rigorously to achieve their dreams, emphasizing dedication and focus.
    • The plea for the gods to not harm the servant indicates a desire for protection and support, which resonates with the human experience of seeking guidance and safety in times of uncertainty.
    • The final part about "well-being" highlights the importance of harmony and balance in life, akin to practices like yoga and meditation that promote mental and physical health.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine, urging individuals to seek knowledge, upliftment, and protection in their lives.
    • Its significance lies in the call for collective action and support, applicable in various real-life scenarios such as community building, education, and personal development.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and enlightened society, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
