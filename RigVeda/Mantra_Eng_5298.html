न त इन्द्र सुमतयो न रायः संचक्षे पूर्वा उषसो न नूत्नाः | देवकं चिन् मान्यमानं जगन्थावा तमनā बृहतः शम्बरं भेत ||
na ta indra sumatayo na rāyaḥ saṁcakṣe pūrvā uṣaso na nūtnāḥ | devakaṁ cin mānyamānaṁ jaghanthāva tmanā bṛhataḥ śambaram bhet ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na: not
    • ta: those
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • sumatayo: of good thoughts
    • na: not
    • rāyaḥ: wealth or prosperity
    • saṁcakṣe: gathered or accumulated
    • pūrvā: earlier or previous
    • uṣaso: dawns or mornings
    • na: not
    • nūtnāḥ: new or fresh
    • devakaṁ: divine or godly
    • cin: having or possessing
    • mānyamānaṁ: being respected or honored
    • jaghanthāva: those who are strong or powerful
    • tmanā: with the self or soul
    • bṛhataḥ: of the great or vast
    • śambaram: auspicious or peaceful
    • bhet: fear or anxiety
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects the idea that true prosperity and strength do not come from mere accumulation of wealth or resources.
    • It emphasizes the importance of good thoughts and intentions, suggesting that these are the foundations of a fulfilling life.
    • The reference to Indra signifies the need for divine guidance and support in our endeavors.
    • The mantra also highlights the significance of respecting and honoring the divine, which can lead to a more peaceful and prosperous existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a company that prioritizes ethical practices and values its employees often sees greater success than one that focuses solely on profit.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who cultivate respect and understanding tend to have stronger and more fulfilling connections.
    • Communities that come together with a shared vision and positive intentions often thrive, showcasing the power of collective goodwill.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder that the essence of life lies not in material wealth but in the quality of our thoughts and actions.
    • It encourages individuals to seek divine guidance and to honor the principles of respect and integrity in all aspects of life.
    • By applying these teachings, one can cultivate a life filled with peace, prosperity, and meaningful connections.
