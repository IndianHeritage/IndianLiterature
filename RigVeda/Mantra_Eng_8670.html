परि वाराण्य अव्यया गोभिर अञ्जानो अर्षति |त्री षधस्थाः पुनानः कृतुते हरिः ||
pari vārāṇy avyayā gobhir añjāno arṣati | trī ṣadhasthā punānaḥ kṛṇute hariḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pari vārāṇy avyayā gobhir añjāno arṣati | trī ṣadhasthā punānaḥ kṛṇute hariḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pari: around, surrounding
    • vārāṇy: the protectors or guardians
    • avyayā: imperishable, eternal
    • gobhir: with the cows, often symbolizing wealth and nourishment
    • añjāno: the one who is praised or honored
    • arṣati: to shine or to be exalted
    • trī: three
    • ṣadhasthā: standing in six places or aspects
    • punānaḥ: purifying or cleansing
    • kṛṇute: makes or creates
    • hariḥ: the divine, often associated with Vishnu or the concept of the divine presence
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine presence that surrounds and protects us, symbolized by the guardians (vārāṇy) and the cows (gobhir), which represent nourishment and abundance.
    • The term "avyayā" emphasizes the eternal nature of this divine protection, suggesting that it is always present and unchanging.
    • The phrase "añjāno arṣati" indicates that this divine presence is honored and exalted, reflecting the reverence we should have towards it.
    • The mention of "trī ṣadhasthā" suggests a completeness or wholeness in the divine aspects, indicating that the divine operates in multiple dimensions or forms.
    • "Punānaḥ kṛṇute hariḥ" implies that this divine presence not only protects but also purifies and creates a harmonious environment for us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we often seek protection and guidance from higher powers, whether through prayer, meditation, or rituals. This mantra serves as a reminder of the omnipresent divine support that surrounds us.
    • In times of distress or uncertainty, individuals may find solace in the idea that they are protected by a higher force, much like the guardians mentioned in the mantra.
    • The concept of nourishment can be related to both physical and spiritual sustenance. Just as cows provide milk, the divine presence offers spiritual nourishment through wisdom and guidance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine protection, nourishment, and purification. It serves as a powerful reminder of the eternal support we have in our lives.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to seek a connection with the divine, fostering a sense of security and peace in their lives.
    • By acknowledging the divine presence, we can cultivate gratitude and mindfulness, leading to a more harmonious existence.
