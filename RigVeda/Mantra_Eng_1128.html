त्वं जिगेथ न धनā रुरोधिथार्भेष्व आजा मगवन महत्सु च |त्वाम उग्रं अवसे सं शिशीमस्य अथा न इन्द्र हवनेषु चोदय ||
tvaṁ jigetha na dhanā rurodhithārbheṣv ājā maghavan mahatsu ca | tvām ugram avase saṁ śiśīmasy athā na indra havaneṣu codaya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • jigetha: conquer
    • na: not
    • dhanā: wealth
    • rurodhithārbheṣv: in the riches of the world
    • ājā: O Maghavan (a name for Indra)
    • maghatsu: in the great ones
    • ca: and
    • tvām: you
    • ugram: fierce
    • avase: we invoke
    • saṁ: together
    • śiśīmasy: we seek
    • athā: and then
    • na: not
    • indra: O Indra
    • havaneṣu: in the sacrifices
    • codaya: inspire
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of the gods, asking for his blessings and strength.
    • The speaker acknowledges that true wealth is not just material riches but the strength and support of divine forces.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance and inspiration from higher powers in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied when seeking success in ventures. Entrepreneurs often pray for guidance and strength to overcome challenges, recognizing that success is not solely based on financial resources.
    • In personal life, individuals may invoke this mantra during times of difficulty, seeking inner strength and divine support to navigate through life's challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that true power and success come from a combination of divine support and personal effort.
    • It teaches us to look beyond material wealth and to seek inspiration and strength from higher powers, which can be applicable in various aspects of life, including personal growth, professional success, and spiritual development.
