प्र वो यज्ञेषु देवयन्तो अर्चन dyāvā नमोभिः पृथिवी इषध्यै |येषाम् ब्रह्माण्य् असमानि विप्रā विश्वग् वियन्ति वनिनो न शाखाः ||
pra vo yajñeṣu devayanto arcan dyāvā namobhiḥ pṛthivī iṣadhyai | yeṣām brahmāṇy asamāni viprā viṣvag viyanti vanino na śākhāḥ ||
  • Translation: "May the divine beings who are invoked in the sacrifices, with our offerings, be pleased with us. The heavens and the earth, with their various forms, are honored by us. Those who are wise and knowledgeable, who understand the ultimate reality, are like the branches of a tree that spread out in all directions."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra vo: For you all
    • yajñeṣu: In the sacrifices
    • devayanto: Divine beings
    • arcan: With our offerings
    • dyāvā: Heavens
    • namobhiḥ: With our salutations
    • pṛthivī: Earth
    • iṣadhyai: With their various forms
    • yeṣām: Those who
    • brahmāṇy: Ultimate reality
    • asamāni: Unmatched
    • viprā: Wise ones
    • viṣvag: All-pervading
    • viyanti: Spread out
    • vanino: Like branches
    • na: Not
    • śākhāḥ: Branches
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine connection through rituals and offerings.
    • It highlights the relationship between the heavens and the earth, suggesting a unity in diversity.
    • The wise individuals, represented as branches, signify the spread of knowledge and wisdom in various directions, contributing to the collective understanding of the ultimate reality.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in the context of community gatherings where people come together to celebrate and honor nature and the divine.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of collective wisdom.
    • In personal development, it serves as a reminder to seek knowledge and understanding, much like the branches of a tree that reach out to gather sunlight and nourishment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of rituals, the interconnectedness of life, and the pursuit of wisdom.
    • By honoring the divine and recognizing the branches of knowledge, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and enlightened society.
