वि द्वीपानि पापतान तिष्ठद दुच्छुनोभे युजन्त रोदसी |प्र धन्वान्य ऐरात शुभ्रखादयो यद एजथा स्वभानवः ||
vi dvīpāni pāpatan tiṣṭhad ducchunobhe yujanta rodasī | pra dhanvāny airata śubhrakhādayo yad ejatha svabhānavaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vi: apart, separately
    • dvīpāni: islands
    • pāpatan: falling, descending
    • tiṣṭhad: standing, remaining
    • ducchunobhe: with bad intentions
    • yujanta: joining, connecting
    • rodasī: the two worlds, the heavens and the earth
    • pra: forth, forward
    • dhanvāny: the bow, symbolizing strength or power
    • airata: the one who is strong or powerful
    • śubhrakhādayo: those who are auspicious and bright
    • yad: that which
    • ejatha: you have created
    • svabhānavaḥ: the self-luminous, the radiant
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the separation and connection of different realms or aspects of existence.
    • The "islands" can symbolize individual lives or communities that may seem isolated but are interconnected.
    • The phrase "with bad intentions" warns against negative actions that can lead to downfall.
    • The "two worlds" represent the physical and spiritual realms, emphasizing the importance of balance between them.
    • The "bow" symbolizes strength and the ability to defend oneself against negativity.
    • The "auspicious and bright" refers to positive energies and intentions that lead to creation and harmony.
    • The "self-luminous" signifies the inner light or wisdom that guides individuals in their actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, individuals may feel isolated (like islands) but can come together to support each other, creating a stronger bond.
    • In personal relationships, negative intentions can lead to misunderstandings and conflicts, highlighting the need for positive communication.
    • Balancing work (physical realm) and personal growth (spiritual realm) is essential for overall well-being.
    • Strengthening oneself through knowledge and skills (the bow) can help in overcoming challenges in life.
    • Surrounding oneself with positive influences (auspicious and bright) can lead to a more fulfilling life.
    • Trusting one's inner wisdom (self-luminous) can guide decisions and actions in daily life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life and the importance of maintaining positive intentions.
    • It serves as a reminder to balance the physical and spiritual aspects of existence.
    • By fostering positive relationships and actions, individuals can create a harmonious environment for themselves and others.
    • Ultimately, the mantra encourages self-awareness and the pursuit of inner wisdom to navigate life's challenges.
