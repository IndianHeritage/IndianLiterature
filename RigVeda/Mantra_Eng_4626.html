ब्रह्माणि हि चक्रिषे वर्धनानि तावत त इन्द्र मतीभिर विविष्मः |सुते सोमे सुतपाः शान्तमानी राण्ड्याः क्रियास्म वक्षाणानि यज्ञैः ||
brahmāṇi hi cakṛṣe vardhanāni tāvat ta indra matibhir viviṣmaḥ | sute some sutapāḥ śaṁtamāni rāṇḍyā kriyāsma vakṣaṇāni yajñaiḥ ||
  • Translation: "Indeed, we have created the Brahman, the source of all, and we seek to attain the blessings of Indra with our intellect. Through the offerings of Soma, we aim to achieve peace and prosperity in our actions and rituals."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Brahmāṇi: Refers to the ultimate reality or the cosmic spirit, often associated with creation and the universe.
    • Cakṛṣe: Means "we have created" or "we have established," indicating an active role in the process of creation.
    • Vardhanāni: Signifies "blessings" or "prosperity," suggesting the outcomes we seek from our actions.
    • Indra: The king of gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • Matibhir: Refers to "with our intellect" or "with our understanding," emphasizing the importance of wisdom in our endeavors.
    • Sute: Means "through offerings," particularly referring to the ritualistic offerings made to the deities.
    • Some: Refers to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration.
    • Sutapāḥ: Indicates "those who are wise" or "those who have clarity of thought," highlighting the need for wisdom in rituals.
    • Śaṁtamāni: Means "peaceful" or "tranquil," suggesting the desired state of being that comes from successful rituals.
    • Rāṇḍyā: Refers to "actions" or "deeds," emphasizing the importance of our actions in achieving our goals.
    • Kriyāsma: Means "our actions," reinforcing the idea that our deeds are crucial in the pursuit of blessings.
    • Vakṣaṇāni: Refers to "offerings" or "sacrifices," which are integral to Vedic rituals.
    • Yajñaiḥ: Means "through sacrifices," indicating that rituals and offerings are essential for attaining divine favor.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of "Brahman" can be seen in the pursuit of knowledge and understanding of the universe, akin to scientific exploration and philosophical inquiry.
    • The role of "Indra" can be likened to leadership in organizations, where wise decision-making and strength are essential for overcoming challenges.
    • Rituals and offerings, such as community service or charitable acts, can be viewed as modern-day "yajñas," where individuals seek to create a positive impact in society.
    • The importance of "wisdom" in decision-making is evident in business strategies, where informed choices lead to success and prosperity.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of creation, wisdom, and the pursuit of blessings through actions and rituals. In real life, it teaches us that our understanding and actions shape our reality. By seeking knowledge and engaging in meaningful deeds, we can attain peace and prosperity, much like the offerings made to the divine in Vedic traditions. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our thoughts, actions, and the universe, encouraging us to live with intention and purpose.
