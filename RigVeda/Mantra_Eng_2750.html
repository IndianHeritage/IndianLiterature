यज्ञेनन्द्रम अवसाचक्रे अर्वाग ऐनं सुम्नाय नव्यसे ववृत्याम् |यः स्तोमेभिर वावृधे पूर्व्येभिर यो मध्यमेभिर उता नूतनेभिः ||
yajñenendram avasā cakre arvāg ainaṁ sumnāya navyase vavṛtyām | yaḥ stomebhir vāvṛdhe pūrvyebhir yo madhyamebhir uta nūtanebhiḥ ||
  • yajñenendram avasā cakre: This phrase can be translated as "Through the sacrifice, we have established Indra." Here, "yajña" refers to a ritual or sacrifice, which is a central theme in Vedic traditions. Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength and power. This line emphasizes the importance of rituals in connecting with divine forces.
  • arvāg ainaṁ sumnāya: This translates to "May he (Indra) come to us with auspiciousness." The word "sumnāya" signifies good fortune and blessings. In real life, this can be related to the idea of inviting positive energy and blessings into our lives through good deeds and intentions.
  • navyase vavṛtyām: This means "in the newness of the dawn." It symbolizes new beginnings and opportunities. In everyday life, this can be seen as a reminder to embrace change and look forward to new possibilities, much like starting a new day with hope and enthusiasm.
  • yaḥ stomebhir vāvṛdhe: This translates to "He who is praised by hymns." It highlights the power of words and prayers in invoking divine presence. In modern contexts, this can be likened to the impact of positive affirmations and the importance of expressing gratitude and appreciation in our lives.
  • pūrvyebhir yo madhyamebhir uta nūtanebhiḥ: This means "He who is nourished by the ancient and the new." It signifies the continuity of tradition while also embracing innovation. In real life, this can be applied to the balance between respecting cultural heritage and adapting to contemporary changes in society.

  • Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of rituals, the invocation of divine blessings, and the balance between tradition and modernity. In our daily lives, we can apply these teachings by engaging in practices that promote positivity, embracing new opportunities, and respecting our cultural roots while being open to change. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the past, present, and future, encouraging us to live harmoniously with ourselves and the world around us.
