तं अर्केभिः तं सामभिः तं गायत्रैश्चरषणयः | इन्द्रं वर्धन्ति क्षितयः ||
tam arkebhis taṁ sāmabhis taṁ gāyatraiś carṣaṇayaḥ | indraṁ vardhanti kṣitayaḥ ||
  • tam arkebhis: This phrase can be translated as "that which is praised by the hymns." In real life, this can refer to individuals or entities that are celebrated for their virtues or contributions, such as leaders, teachers, or even natural phenomena like the sun or the earth.
  • taṁ sāmabhis: This means "that which is sung in harmony." It emphasizes the importance of unity and collective effort. For example, in a team setting, when everyone works together harmoniously towards a common goal, they can achieve greater success than when working in isolation.
  • taṁ gāyatraiś carṣaṇayaḥ: This translates to "that which is sung by the bards." It highlights the role of storytelling and music in preserving culture and history. In modern times, this can be seen in how songs and stories are used to convey messages, inspire movements, or celebrate heritage.
  • indraṁ vardhanti: This phrase means "they enhance Indra." Indra is often seen as a deity of strength and power. In a contemporary context, this can relate to how communities or organizations uplift their leaders or heroes, providing them with support and recognition to achieve greater feats.
  • kṣitayaḥ: This word refers to "the earth" or "the ground." It symbolizes stability and foundation. In real life, this can be interpreted as the importance of having a strong base, whether it be in relationships, education, or career, to ensure growth and success.

Conclusion: The mantra "tam arkebhis taṁ sāmabhis taṁ gāyatraiś carṣaṇayaḥ | indraṁ vardhanti kṣitayaḥ ||" emphasizes the significance of praise, harmony, storytelling, and support in enhancing strength and stability. In real life, it teaches us the value of collective effort, the power of unity, and the importance of recognizing and uplifting those who lead or inspire us. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as teamwork in professional settings, community building, and cultural preservation through art and music. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and supportive environment in our lives and communities.
