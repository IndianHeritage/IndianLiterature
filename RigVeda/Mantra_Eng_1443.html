स हव्यां मानवाणां इळा कृतानि पत्यते |स नस् त्रासते वरुणस्य धूर्तेर महो देवस्य धूर्तेः ||
sa havyā mānuṣāṇām iḻā kṛtāni patyate | sa nas trāsate varuṇasya dhūrter maho devasya dhūrteḥ ||
  • sa havyā mānuṣāṇām: This phrase can be translated as "that which is offered by humans." In the context of the Rigveda, it refers to the offerings made by people to the divine. In real life, this can be likened to the various forms of offerings or sacrifices made in different cultures, such as food offerings in Hindu rituals or the act of giving to charity in modern society.
  • iḻā kṛtāni patyate: This translates to "the actions of the Earth are established." Here, "iḻā" refers to the Earth or the divine feminine energy associated with it. This can be seen in real life through our connection to nature and the importance of environmental conservation. For example, sustainable practices in agriculture and urban development reflect the acknowledgment of the Earth’s role in our lives.
  • sa nas trāsate varuṇasya: This means "that which protects us from the fear of Varuna." Varuna is the Vedic deity associated with cosmic order and moral law. In contemporary terms, this can be related to the concept of justice and the rule of law in society, which provides a sense of security and order. For instance, legal systems are designed to protect citizens from wrongdoing and maintain societal harmony.
  • dhūrter maho devasya dhūrteḥ: This translates to "the great power of the divine." It emphasizes the strength and influence of the divine in our lives. In real life, this can be seen in the way people draw strength from their faith or spirituality during challenging times. For example, individuals often turn to prayer or meditation to find solace and guidance in difficult situations.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the relationship between humanity, the Earth, and the divine. It signifies the importance of our actions and offerings, the need for protection from chaos, and the acknowledgment of a higher power guiding us. In real life, this mantra encourages us to engage in sustainable practices, uphold justice, and seek spiritual strength, reminding us of our interconnectedness with nature and the cosmos. By embodying these principles, we can foster a more harmonious existence for ourselves and future generations.
