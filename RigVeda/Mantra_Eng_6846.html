मन्त्र: एते त्ये वृथग अग्नय इद्धासः सम अदृक्षत |उषसाम इव केतवः ||
ete tye vṛthag agnaya iddhāsaḥ sam adṛkṣata | uṣasām iva ketavaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ete tye vṛthag agnaya iddhāsaḥ sam adṛkṣata | uṣasām iva ketavaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "These (gods) who are like the fire, have been seen as the ones who bring forth the dawn, like the celestial bodies."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ete: these
    • tye: who are
    • vṛthag: like fire
    • agnaya: the gods or divine beings
    • iddhāsaḥ: have been seen or perceived
    • sam: together or in unison
    • adṛkṣata: have looked or observed
    • uṣasām: of the dawn
    • iva: like
    • ketavaḥ: celestial bodies or stars
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of divine beings that are compared to fire, symbolizing purity, transformation, and energy.
    • The reference to "dawn" signifies new beginnings, hope, and enlightenment, much like how the sunrise brings light after darkness.
    • The phrase "like the celestial bodies" suggests that these divine entities are constant and reliable, guiding forces in the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is seen as a purifying element. For instance, in Hindu rituals, fire (Agni) is central to ceremonies, symbolizing the presence of the divine.
    • The dawn is often associated with new opportunities. For example, starting a new job or a new phase in life can be seen as a 'dawn' in one's personal journey.
    • Celestial bodies, like stars, have historically guided travelers. In modern times, they can symbolize aspirations and goals, reminding us to look up and strive for our dreams.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine guidance and the transformative power of fire and light.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek enlightenment and new beginnings, reminding us that every dawn brings a chance for renewal.
    • By recognizing the divine in our lives, we can find strength and direction, much like the stars that guide us through the night.
