मन्त्र: उता ज्ञा अग्निर अध्वर उतो गृहपतिर दमे | उता ब्रह्मा नि शीदति ||
uta gnā agnir adhvara uto gṛhapatir dame | uta brahmā ni ṣīdati ||
  • uta - This word can be translated as "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition.
  • gnā - This term refers to "knowledge" or "wisdom," emphasizing the importance of understanding and awareness.
  • agnir - This means "fire," which in Vedic literature symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification.
  • adhvara - This word translates to "sacrifice" or "ritual," highlighting the significance of spiritual practices and offerings.
  • uto - Similar to "uta," it means "and" or "also," reinforcing the idea of connection.
  • gṛhapatir - This term refers to the "lord of the house," symbolizing domesticity, stability, and the importance of family and home.
  • dame - This means "in the house" or "at home," emphasizing the significance of the domestic sphere.
  • uta - Again, this word serves as a connector, reiterating the continuity of thought.
  • brahmā - This refers to the "cosmic spirit" or "ultimate reality," representing the source of all creation and knowledge.
  • ni - This is a preposition that can mean "down" or "in," indicating a state of being or presence.
  • ṣīdati - This means "sits" or "resides," suggesting a state of stability or permanence.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation that acknowledges the interconnectedness of knowledge, fire (energy), domestic stability, and the cosmic spirit. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in rituals and the sanctity of the home as a space where divine presence resides.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can incorporate this mantra into their daily rituals, reminding themselves of the importance of knowledge and the transformative power of energy in their lives.
  • Family and Home Life: The emphasis on the "lord of the house" encourages individuals to create a nurturing and stable environment for their families, fostering love and understanding.
  • Personal Growth: The invocation of wisdom can inspire individuals to seek knowledge and understanding in their personal and professional lives, leading to growth and transformation.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of various aspects of life—spirituality, family, and knowledge. It encourages individuals to cultivate wisdom, create a harmonious home environment, and recognize the divine presence in their lives. By embodying these principles, one can lead a more fulfilling and meaningful life.
