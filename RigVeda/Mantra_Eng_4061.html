यत्र वह्निर अभिहितो दुद्रवद् द्रोण्यः पशुः |नृमणा वीरपस्त्योऽर्णा धीर एव सन्तता ||
yatra vahnir abhihito dudravad droṇyaḥ paśuḥ | nṛmaṇā vīrapastyo 'rṇā dhīreva sanitā ||
  • Mantra: "yatra vahnir abhihito dudravad droṇyaḥ paśuḥ | nṛmaṇā vīrapastyo 'rṇā dhīreva sanitā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yatra: where
    • vahnir: fire
    • abhihito: invoked or called upon
    • dudravad: moving or flowing
    • dronyaḥ: the waters or streams
    • paśuḥ: animals or living beings
    • nṛmaṇā: through human effort or action
    • vīrapastyo: the strength of heroes
    • 'rṇā: without debt or obligation
    • dhīreva: with wisdom or intelligence
    • sanitā: established or firm
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a place where fire is invoked and flows like water, symbolizing the transformative power of knowledge and action.
    • The mention of animals signifies the interconnectedness of all living beings in this process.
    • The phrase "through human effort" emphasizes the importance of human agency in achieving strength and wisdom.
    • Being "without debt" suggests a state of purity and freedom from negative influences, allowing for clear thinking and action.
    • The term "with wisdom" indicates that true strength comes from understanding and intelligence, not just physical power.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community project, when individuals come together (like fire) to work towards a common goal, their collective efforts can lead to significant change, much like the flowing waters that nourish the land.
    • In personal development, when one seeks knowledge (fire) and applies it wisely (with intelligence), they can overcome challenges and grow stronger, akin to a hero facing adversities.
    • In environmental conservation, recognizing the interconnectedness of all living beings (paśuḥ) can lead to sustainable practices that benefit both nature and humanity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of invoking inner strength and wisdom through collective human effort.
    • It teaches us that true power lies not just in physical might but in the ability to act wisely and compassionately.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to work together towards common goals, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
