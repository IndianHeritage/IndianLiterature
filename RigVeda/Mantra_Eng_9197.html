मन्त्र: चोदयतं सूनृताः पिन्वतं धिया उत पुरंधीः ईरयतं तद उष्मसि |यशसम भागं कृतुं नो अश्विनाः सोमं न चारुम् मगवात्सु नस् कृतम् ||
codayataṁ sūnṛtāḥ pinvataṁ dhiya ut puraṁdhīr īrayataṁ tad uśmasi | yaśasam bhāgaṁ kṛṇutaṁ no aśvinā somaṁ na cārum maghavatsu nas kṛtam ||
  • codayataṁ: This word can be interpreted as "inspiring" or "encouraging." It signifies the act of motivating individuals or groups towards a common goal.
  • sūnṛtāḥ: This term refers to "truthful" or "righteous." It emphasizes the importance of honesty and integrity in actions and thoughts.
  • pinvataṁ: This means "to nourish" or "to strengthen." It suggests the idea of providing sustenance, both physically and spiritually, to foster growth and well-being.
  • dhiya: This word translates to "intellect" or "understanding." It highlights the significance of wisdom and knowledge in decision-making and life choices.
  • ut: This is a conjunction meaning "and" or "also," linking ideas together.
  • puraṁdhīr: This term can be understood as "the one who knows the past." It refers to the wisdom gained from experiences and history, which guides present actions.
  • īrayataṁ: This means "to inspire" or "to lead." It indicates the role of leadership in guiding others towards a positive direction.
  • tad: This word means "that," referring to the previous concepts discussed.
  • uśmasi: This translates to "in warmth" or "in light," symbolizing positivity and enlightenment.
  • yaśasam: This means "fame" or "glory," indicating the recognition and respect earned through righteous actions.
  • bhāgaṁ: This term refers to "share" or "portion," suggesting the idea of distributing benefits or rewards fairly.
  • kṛṇutaṁ: This means "to create" or "to make," emphasizing the act of bringing something into existence through effort.
  • no: This word means "our," indicating a collective ownership or responsibility.
  • aśvinā: This refers to the divine twins in Vedic mythology, symbolizing health and vitality.
  • somaṁ: This term refers to the sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing nourishment and spiritual enlightenment.
  • na: This means "not," indicating a negation or absence.
  • cārum: This translates to "beautiful" or "pleasant," referring to the qualities that are desirable in life.
  • maghavatsu: This term refers to the powerful or mighty, often associated with divine forces or energies.
  • nas: This means "us," indicating a collective group.
  • kṛtam: This translates to "done" or "achieved," referring to the actions that have been completed.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of inspiration, truthfulness, nourishment, and wisdom in our lives. It calls for collective efforts to create a positive and nourishing environment, where individuals can thrive and achieve glory through righteous actions. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as leadership in organizations, community building, and personal development. By fostering a culture of integrity and support, we can create a society that values knowledge and collective well-being, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
