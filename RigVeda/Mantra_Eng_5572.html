प्र द्यावाऽयज्ञैः पृथिवी नमोभिः सबाधा ईळे बृहतī यजत्रे |ते चिद्धि पूर्वे कवयो गृणन्तः पुरो महī दधिरे देवपुत्रे ||
pra dyāvā yajñaiḥ pṛthivī namobhiḥ sabādha īḻe bṛhatī yajatre | te cid dhi pūrve kavayo gṛṇantaḥ puro mahī dadhire devaputre ||
  • pra dyāvā yajñaiḥ: This phrase translates to "the heavens (dyāvā) are honored through sacrifices (yajñaiḥ)." In real life, this can be understood as the importance of rituals and offerings in various cultures, where people express gratitude and seek blessings from higher powers. For example, in many traditions, festivals involve offerings to deities, symbolizing respect and reverence.
  • pṛthivī namobhiḥ: Here, "pṛthivī" refers to the Earth, and "namobhiḥ" means "with salutations." This signifies the respect and reverence for the Earth. In contemporary society, this can be related to environmental movements where people honor and protect the planet, recognizing its significance for life. For instance, Earth Day celebrations emphasize the need to care for our environment.
  • sabādha īḻe bṛhatī yajatre: "Sabādha" means "with all beings," "īḻe" refers to "worship," and "bṛhatī" signifies "the great." This part emphasizes collective worship and the importance of community in spiritual practices. In real life, this can be seen in communal prayers or gatherings, such as during Diwali or Christmas, where people come together to celebrate and worship.
  • te cid dhi pūrve kavayo gṛṇantaḥ: "Te" means "they," "cid" refers to "consciousness," "pūrve" means "ancient," and "kavayo" translates to "sages." This suggests that ancient sages were aware of the deeper consciousness and wisdom. In modern contexts, this can relate to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom through education and philosophy, where individuals seek to understand the world and their place in it.
  • puro mahī dadhire devaputre: "Puro" means "in front," "mahī" refers to "the great," "dadhire" means "they established," and "devaputre" translates to "the sons of the gods." This indicates that the great ones (sages or enlightened beings) established a foundation for spiritual practices. In real life, this can be seen in how spiritual leaders and philosophers have laid down teachings that guide humanity, such as the teachings of Buddha or the principles of Gandhi.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of the cosmos, the Earth, and humanity through rituals, respect, and collective consciousness. It highlights the significance of honoring both the divine and the natural world, encouraging a harmonious relationship with our surroundings. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to engage in sustainable practices, collective worship, and the pursuit of wisdom, fostering a deeper understanding of our place in the universe and the importance of living in harmony with nature and each other.
