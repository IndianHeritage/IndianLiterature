तन् न इन्द्रो वरुणो मित्रो अग्निः आपः ओषधीर वनिनो जुषन्तः |शर्मन् स्याम मारुताम उपस्थे युयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
tan na indro varuṇo mitro agnir āpa oṣadhīr vanino juṣanta | śarman syāma marutām upasthe yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • tan na indro varuṇo mitro agnir āpa oṣadhīr vanino juṣanta
    • tan - This word translates to "that" or "may that." It signifies a request or invocation.
    • na - Meaning "not," it negates the previous term, indicating a desire for something positive.
    • indro - Refers to Indra, the king of gods, associated with strength and rain.
    • varuṇo - Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water, representing moral authority.
    • mitro - Mitra, the god of friendship and harmony, emphasizing social bonds.
    • agnir - Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy and transformation.
    • āpa - Refers to water, essential for life and purity.
    • oṣadhīr - Meaning "herbs" or "plants," indicating the importance of nature and healing.
    • vanino - Refers to "the forest" or "the wild," symbolizing the natural world.
    • juṣanta - Meaning "may they enjoy," indicating a wish for blessings and abundance.
  • śarman syāma marutām upasthe
    • śarman - Refers to "shelter" or "protection," indicating a desire for safety.
    • syāma - Meaning "may we be," suggesting a collective aspiration.
    • marutām - Refers to the Maruts, storm deities associated with strength and vitality.
    • upasthe - Meaning "in our presence," indicating a call for divine presence in our lives.
  • yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ
    • yūyam - Meaning "you," referring to the deities being invoked.
    • pāta - Meaning "protect," indicating a plea for divine safeguarding.
    • svastibhiḥ - Refers to "well-being" or "auspiciousness," emphasizing health and prosperity.
    • sadā - Meaning "always," indicating a continuous desire for protection.
    • naḥ - Meaning "us," indicating the collective group seeking blessings.

Conclusion:

This mantra is a profound invocation for protection, well-being, and harmony from various deities. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, the divine, and human existence. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • During rituals or prayers for health and safety, invoking the blessings of the gods.
  • In community gatherings, fostering a sense of unity and collective well-being.
  • In personal meditation, focusing on the elements of nature and their significance in our lives.

The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, encouraging individuals to seek balance, protection, and harmony in their daily existence. It serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives and the importance of nurturing our relationships with nature and each other.
