नदस्य माऽरुधतः काम आगन्न इता आजातो अमुतः कुतश्चित् |लोपाामुद्रा वृषणं नी रिणाति धीर्म अधीराऽ धयति श्वसन्तम् ||
nadasya mā rudhataḥ kāma āgann ita ājāto amutaḥ kutaś cit | lopāmudrā vṛṣaṇaṁ nī riṇāti dhīram adhīrā dhayati śvasantam ||
  • nadasya: This word refers to sound or vibration. In a broader sense, it can symbolize the essence of communication and connection in the universe.
  • mā: This is a negation, meaning "not" or "do not." It emphasizes the importance of restraint or avoidance.
  • rudhataḥ: This term can be interpreted as "to be obstructed" or "to be hindered." It suggests the idea of barriers or challenges in life.
  • kāma: This word signifies desire or longing. It represents the human experience of wanting and striving for various goals.
  • āgann: Meaning "coming" or "arriving," it indicates the manifestation of desires or the arrival of experiences.
  • ita: This means "from here" or "from this place," suggesting a point of origin or a starting point.
  • ājāto: This term means "born" or "created," indicating the emergence of something new.
  • amutaḥ: This can be interpreted as "from the immortal" or "from the eternal," suggesting a connection to the divine or the everlasting.
  • kutaś cit: This phrase means "from somewhere" or "from any place," indicating the universality of the experience being described.
  • lopāmudrā: This refers to a sage or a wise person, symbolizing knowledge and wisdom.
  • vṛṣaṇaṁ: This word means "strength" or "power," representing the force that drives actions and decisions.
  • nī: This means "to lead" or "to guide," indicating the importance of direction in life.
  • riṇāti: This term means "to pay" or "to offer," suggesting the idea of giving back or reciprocating.
  • dhīram: This word signifies "calm" or "steady," representing the importance of composure in the face of challenges.
  • adhīrā: This means "not steady" or "restless," indicating the struggles one may face in maintaining balance.
  • dhayati: This term means "to bear" or "to endure," emphasizing resilience in overcoming obstacles.
  • śvasantam: This refers to "breathing" or "life," symbolizing vitality and the essence of existence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the journey of human experience, from the emergence of desires to the challenges faced in their pursuit. It highlights the importance of wisdom, strength, and resilience in navigating life's complexities. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, where individuals strive to overcome obstacles and achieve their goals while maintaining a sense of calm and composure. It serves as a reminder that while desires may lead us, it is our wisdom and strength that guide us through the journey of life.
