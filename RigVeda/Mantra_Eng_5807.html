युवां हवन्त उभयासा आजिष्व इन्द्रं च वस्वो वरुणं च सातये |यत्र राजभिर दशभिर निबाधितं प्र सुदासं आवतं तृत्सुभिः सह ||
yuvāṁ havanta ubhayāsa ājiṣv indraṁ ca vasvo varuṇaṁ ca sātaye | yatra rājabhir daśabhir nibādhitam pra sudāsam āvataṁ tṛtsubhiḥ saha ||
  • yuvāṁ havanta ubhayāsa ājiṣv: This phrase can be translated as "You two, who are invoked, are both powerful." Here, "yuvāṁ" refers to the two deities or forces being addressed, emphasizing their strength and significance.
  • indraṁ ca vasvo varuṇaṁ ca sātaye: This part translates to "Indra, Vasu, and Varuna, we call upon you for support." Indra is often seen as the king of the gods, associated with rain and thunderstorms; Vasu represents the elemental forces, and Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water. This invocation highlights the importance of these deities in maintaining balance and harmony in nature.
  • yatra rājabhir daśabhir nibādhitam: This means "where the ten kings are bound together." It signifies unity and cooperation among leaders, suggesting that collective strength is essential for achieving goals.
  • pra sudāsam āvataṁ tṛtsubhiḥ saha: This translates to "May Sudasa, along with the Tṛtsus, be protected." Sudasa is a historical figure, a king known for his righteousness, and the Tṛtsus refer to a group of people or tribes. This line emphasizes the importance of leadership and community in overcoming challenges.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of invoking divine powers for support and guidance in leadership and community. It highlights the importance of unity among leaders and the need for divine blessings to achieve success. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in governance, teamwork, and community building, where collaboration and seeking higher guidance can lead to prosperity and harmony. The mantra serves as a reminder that strength lies not only in individual capabilities but also in collective efforts and divine support.
