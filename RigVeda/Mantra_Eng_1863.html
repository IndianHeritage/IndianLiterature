त्वं अस्माकं इन्द्र विश्वधा स्याऽवृकटमो नरां नृपाताः |स नो विश्वासां स्पृधां सहोदā विद्यामेषं वृजनं जीरदानुम् ||
tvam asmākam indra viśvadha syā avṛkatamo narāṁ nṛpātā | sa no viśvāsāṁ spṛdhāṁ sahodā vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum ||
  • tvam asmākam: This phrase translates to "You are ours." It signifies a sense of belonging and connection to a higher power or deity.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. He represents power and authority.
  • viśvadha: This term means "all-encompassing" or "universal." It suggests that the deity has control over all aspects of life and the universe.
  • syā: This word translates to "may you be" or "let it be." It expresses a wish or a prayer for the deity's presence and support.
  • avṛkatamo: This means "the most protective" or "the one who shields." It emphasizes the deity's role as a guardian and protector.
  • narāṁ nṛpātā: This phrase translates to "of men, the lord." It indicates that Indra is the leader or protector of humanity.
  • sa no: This means "may he" or "let him." It is a call for the deity's blessings and guidance.
  • viśvāsāṁ: This term means "trust" or "faith." It highlights the importance of belief and confidence in the divine.
  • spṛdhāṁ: This translates to "in our endeavors" or "in our actions." It signifies the need for divine support in our pursuits.
  • sahodā: This means "brotherhood" or "togetherness." It emphasizes unity and collaboration among people.
  • vidyāmeṣaṁ: This translates to "knowledge" or "wisdom." It signifies the importance of learning and understanding in life.
  • vṛjanaṁ: This term means "prosperity" or "wealth." It indicates the desire for abundance and success.
  • jīradānum: This translates to "life-giving" or "nurturing." It emphasizes the nurturing aspect of the divine in sustaining life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep reverence for Indra as a protector and guide for humanity. It highlights the importance of trust, unity, and the pursuit of knowledge in achieving prosperity and well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as seeking guidance in leadership roles, fostering collaboration in communities, and emphasizing the value of education and wisdom in personal and professional growth. By invoking the qualities of Indra, individuals can aspire to create a supportive and prosperous environment for themselves and others.
