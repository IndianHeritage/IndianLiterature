तं उ स्तुष इन्द्रं यो विदानो गिर्वहसं गीरभिर यज्ञवृद्धम् |यस्य दिवं अति मह्ना पृथिव्याः पुरुमायस्य रिरिचे महित्वम् ||
tam u stuṣa indraṁ yo vidāno girvāhasaṁ gīrbhir yajñavṛddham | yasya divam ati mahnā pṛthivyāḥ purumāyasya ririce mahitvam ||
  • tam u stuṣa indraṁ: This phrase translates to "We praise that Indra." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunder, and war. In real life, this can be likened to how people express gratitude or reverence towards forces of nature or leaders who bring prosperity and protection.
  • yo vidāno girvāhasaṁ: This means "who knows the hymns of the sky." Here, "vidāno" refers to knowledge or wisdom, and "girvāhasaṁ" relates to the celestial or heavenly aspects. In contemporary terms, this can be compared to individuals who possess deep knowledge in their fields, such as scientists or scholars who understand the complexities of the universe.
  • gīrbhir yajñavṛddham: This translates to "who is enhanced by the hymns of sacrifice." "Yajña" refers to a ritual of offerings, and "vṛddham" means to be increased or enhanced. In modern life, this can be seen in the context of community service or charitable acts, where collective efforts lead to greater good and societal enhancement.
  • yasya divam ati mahnā: This means "whose greatness surpasses the heavens." It emphasizes the vastness and supremacy of the divine. In real life, this can be related to the idea of striving for greatness, where individuals or organizations aim to achieve extraordinary goals that inspire others.
  • pṛthivyāḥ purumāyasya: This translates to "of the earth and its fullness." It signifies the connection between the divine and the earthly realm. In practical terms, this can be seen in environmental conservation efforts, where the richness of the earth is acknowledged and preserved for future generations.
  • ririce mahitvam: This means "may we attain greatness." It is a call for aspiration and achievement. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as a motivation for personal and collective growth, encouraging individuals to pursue their dreams and contribute positively to society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of reverence towards divine forces, the pursuit of knowledge, and the importance of community and environmental consciousness. Its significance lies in inspiring individuals to strive for greatness while acknowledging the interconnectedness of the divine, nature, and humanity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in educational pursuits, community service, and environmental stewardship, encouraging a holistic approach to life that honors both the spiritual and material worlds.
