तां ईं वर्धन्ति मह्य अस्य पौंस्यं न मातरā नयति रेतसे भुजे | दधाति पुत्रो 'वरं परम पितुर नाम तृतीयम् अधि रोचने दिवः ||
tā īṁ vardhanti mahy asya pauṁsyaṁ ni mātarā nayati retase bhuje | dadhāti putro 'varam param pitur nāma tṛtīyam adhi rocane divaḥ ||
  • tā īṁ vardhanti: This phrase can be translated as "they (the divine forces) increase or enhance." It signifies the idea of growth and prosperity, suggesting that divine blessings lead to an increase in well-being.
  • mahy asya pauṁsyaṁ: This means "for me, this manliness or strength." It emphasizes the importance of personal strength and vitality, which can be interpreted as the inner qualities that empower an individual.
  • ni mātarā nayati: Translated as "the mothers lead," this phrase highlights the nurturing aspect of femininity and the guidance provided by maternal figures. In real life, this can be seen in the role of mothers or caregivers who guide and support individuals in their growth.
  • retase bhuje: This translates to "in the strength of the body." It refers to physical strength and vitality, which are essential for achieving one's goals and aspirations. For example, athletes rely on their physical strength to excel in their sports.
  • dadhāti putro 'varam param: This means "the son grants the supreme boon." It signifies the importance of lineage and the blessings that come from family. In many cultures, the continuation of family values and traditions is seen as a significant aspect of life.
  • pitur nāma tṛtīyam: Translated as "the third name of the father," this phrase emphasizes the connection between generations and the importance of honoring one's ancestors. In real life, this can be observed in rituals and traditions that pay homage to forebears.
  • adhi rocane divaḥ: This means "in the brilliance of the heavens." It signifies the aspiration for higher ideals and the pursuit of enlightenment. In modern life, this can be related to the pursuit of knowledge, wisdom, and spiritual growth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of growth, strength, and the nurturing role of family, particularly mothers. It emphasizes the importance of personal strength and the blessings that come from familial connections. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in family dynamics, personal development, and the pursuit of higher ideals. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of nurturing relationships, and the pursuit of personal and spiritual growth.
