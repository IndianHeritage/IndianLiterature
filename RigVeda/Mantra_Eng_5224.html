अग्नि याहि दूत्यम् मा ऋषण्यो देवाम् acchā ब्रह्मकृतā गणेन |सरस्वतीं मरुतो अश्विनापो यक्षि देवान् रत्नधेयाय विश्वान् ||
agne yāhi dūtya1m mā riṣaṇyo devām̐ acchā brahmakṛtā gaṇena | sarasvatīm maruto aśvināpo yakṣi devān ratnadheyāya viśvān ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god, who is a central figure in Vedic rituals and symbolizes purity, energy, and transformation.
  • yāhi: This means "come" or "approach," indicating a call to the divine.
  • dūtya1m: This term can be interpreted as "messenger" or "envoy," suggesting a request for divine guidance or intervention.
  • mā: This means "not," serving as a negation in the mantra.
  • riṣaṇyo: This word can be understood as "to be disturbed" or "to be harmed," indicating a plea for protection.
  • devām̐: This refers to the "gods," acknowledging the presence of divine beings in the invocation.
  • acchā: This means "well" or "good," suggesting a desire for positive outcomes.
  • brahmakṛtā: This term refers to "those who perform the sacred rites," emphasizing the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • gaṇena: This means "with the group" or "with the assembly," highlighting the communal aspect of worship.
  • sarasvatīm: This refers to Sarasvati, the goddess of knowledge, music, and arts, symbolizing wisdom and learning.
  • maruto: This word refers to the Maruts, the storm gods, representing strength and vitality.
  • aśvināpo: This term refers to the Aśvins, the twin gods of medicine and health, indicating a plea for well-being.
  • yakṣi: This means "to bestow" or "to grant," indicating a request for blessings.
  • devān: This again refers to the "gods," reinforcing the connection to the divine.
  • ratnadheyāya: This term means "for the wealth of the world," indicating a desire for prosperity and abundance.
  • viśvān: This means "all" or "universal," suggesting inclusivity in the invocation.

Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation that calls upon various deities for protection, guidance, and blessings. It emphasizes the importance of community and ritual in connecting with the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during significant life events such as weddings, new ventures, or times of uncertainty, where individuals seek divine support and wisdom. By reciting this mantra, one can cultivate a sense of connection to the divine, fostering a spirit of cooperation and abundance in their lives.
