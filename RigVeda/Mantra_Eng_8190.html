स नो अर्ष पवित्रा आमदो यो देववीतमः |इन्दव इन्द्राय पीतये ||
sa no arṣa pavitra ā mado yo devavītamaḥ | indav indrāya pītaye ||
  • sa no arṣa pavitra ā mado yo devavītamaḥ
    • sa: This word translates to "may he" or "let him." It indicates a desire or a prayer for something beneficial.
    • no: This means "us" or "our," suggesting a collective intention or wish.
    • arṣa: This term can be interpreted as "sacred" or "divine," referring to something that is pure and holy.
    • pavitra: Meaning "pure" or "clean," it emphasizes the importance of purity in thoughts and actions.
    • ā mado: This phrase can be understood as "may we be filled with joy" or "may we experience bliss." It reflects a longing for happiness and fulfillment.
    • yo: This means "who" or "that," referring to a divine entity or force.
    • devavītamaḥ: This translates to "the most divine" or "the supreme among the gods," indicating a connection to the highest spiritual power.
  • indav indrāya pītaye
    • indav: This word refers to "Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
    • indrāya: This means "for Indra," indicating a dedication or offering to this divine figure.
    • pītaye: This translates to "for drinking" or "to be consumed," suggesting a ritualistic offering or a desire for blessings from Indra.

Conclusion:

The mantra "sa no arṣa pavitra ā mado yo devavītamaḥ | indav indrāya pītaye ||" encapsulates a prayer for purity, joy, and divine blessings. It emphasizes the importance of maintaining a sacred and pure state of being while seeking the grace of the supreme divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, personal meditations, or communal gatherings where individuals seek to elevate their spiritual consciousness and foster a sense of unity and joy among themselves. By invoking the blessings of Indra, it encourages individuals to strive for strength and leadership in their lives, promoting a harmonious and fulfilling existence. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to seek purity in their thoughts and actions while fostering a connection with the divine, ultimately leading to a more meaningful and joyful life.
