इमां च नः पृथिवीं विश्वधायां उप क्षेति हितमित्रो न राजा |पुरःसदः शर्मसदो न वीराः महद देवानाम असुरत्वम् एकम् ||
imāṁ ca naḥ pṛthivīṁ viśvadhāyā upa kṣeti hitamitro na rājā | puraḥsadaḥ śarmasado na vīrā mahad devānām asuratvam ekam ||
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "May this Earth, which is all-encompassing, protect us. The friend of the righteous is not a king; the one who is in front is a source of comfort, and the great ones are the divine beings, united in their essence."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • imāṁ ca naḥ: "this (Earth) and for us" - indicating a plea for protection and support from the Earth.
    • pṛthivīṁ: "Earth" - symbolizes the physical world and our connection to it.
    • viśvadhāyā: "all-encompassing" - refers to the Earth as a nurturing and inclusive entity.
    • upa kṣeti: "may protect" - a request for safeguarding and support.
    • hitamitro: "friend of the righteous" - emphasizes the importance of virtue and righteousness in relationships.
    • na rājā: "not a king" - suggests that true leadership is not about power but about being a protector and friend.
    • puraḥsadaḥ: "the one who is in front" - refers to a guiding figure or source of comfort.
    • śarmasado: "source of comfort" - highlights the role of supportive figures in our lives.
    • na vīrā: "not the heroes" - indicates that true strength lies in unity and divine essence rather than individual valor.
    • mahad: "great" - signifies the importance of greatness in character and spirit.
    • devānām: "of the divine beings" - refers to the higher powers or deities that guide and protect us.
    • asuratvam: "essence of unity" - emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • ekam: "one" - signifies the oneness of existence and the unity of all life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In environmental conservation, this mantra reminds us of our responsibility to protect the Earth, as it nurtures and sustains us.
    • In community building, the idea of "friend of the righteous" can be seen in leaders who prioritize the well-being of their people over power, such as community organizers or social activists.
    • In personal relationships, the concept of being a "source of comfort" can be reflected in the role of friends and family who provide support during difficult times.
    • In spiritual practices, the recognition of the "divine beings" can be found in various traditions that emphasize the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of unity.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for protection, unity, and the recognition of true leadership and support. It teaches us that the Earth is a nurturing force, and that our relationships should be built on righteousness and mutual support. In real life, this mantra can guide us in our interactions with others, our approach to leadership, and our responsibility towards the environment, reminding us that we are all interconnected and part of a greater whole.
