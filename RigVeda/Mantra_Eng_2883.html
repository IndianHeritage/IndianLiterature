मन्त्र: उता ऋतुभिर ऋतुपाः पाहि सोमं इन्द्र देवैभिः सखिभिः सुतं नः | यां आभजो मरुतो ये त्वान्व अहन वृत्रम अदधुस तुभ्यम ओजः ||
uta ṛtubhir ṛtupāḥ pāhi somam indra devebhiḥ sakhibhiḥ sutaṁ naḥ | yām̐ ābhajo maruto ye tvānv ahan vṛtram adadhus tubhyam ojaḥ ||
  • Translation: "O Indra, protect our soma with the help of the seasons and the powers of nature. You, who are supported by the divine beings and your friends, have vanquished Vritra with your strength."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • uta: O
    • ṛtubhir: with the seasons
    • ṛtupāḥ: powers of nature
    • pāhi: protect
    • somam: soma (a sacred drink)
    • indra: the king of gods
    • devebhiḥ: by the divine beings
    • sakhibhiḥ: with friends
    • sutaṁ: our offering
    • naḥ: for us
    • yām̐: whom
    • ābhajo: who are supported
    • maruto: the storm gods
    • ye: who
    • tvānv: you
    • ahan: have vanquished
    • vṛtram: Vritra (a demon)
    • adadhus: have placed
    • tubhyam: for you
    • ojaḥ: strength
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, who is often associated with strength and power.
    • The mention of "soma" refers to a sacred drink that symbolizes divine inspiration and is often used in rituals to connect with the divine.
    • The reference to "Vritra" signifies the overcoming of obstacles and challenges, as Vritra is a demon that represents drought and chaos.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In agricultural societies, invoking Indra for rain and protection of crops is crucial. Farmers may recite this mantra during rituals to ensure a good harvest.
    • In personal life, the mantra can be seen as a call for strength and support from friends and divine forces when facing challenges or adversities.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and collaboration, as indicated by the mention of "friends" and "divine beings," which can be applied in teamwork and collective efforts in various fields.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of nature, divinity, and human effort. It serves as a reminder to seek strength and support from both the divine and our community.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the challenges we face and the need for collective strength to overcome them, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from agriculture to personal struggles.
