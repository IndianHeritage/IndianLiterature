इदं विष्णुर् वि चक्रमे त्रिधा न दधे पदम् |समूढम् अस्य पांसुर् ||
idaṁ viṣṇur vi cakrame tredhā ni dadhe padam | samūḻham asya pāṁsure ||
  • idaṁ - This word translates to "this" or "here." It signifies the presence of something significant in the context of the mantra.
  • viṣṇur - Refers to Lord Vishnu, one of the principal deities in Hinduism, known as the preserver and protector of the universe.
  • vi - A prefix that can mean "in" or "through," indicating the manner in which Vishnu operates in the world.
  • cakrame - This word means "to revolve" or "to turn." It suggests the cyclical nature of existence and the role of Vishnu in maintaining cosmic order.
  • tredhā - Translates to "threefold" or "in three ways," which can refer to the three aspects of time (past, present, future) or the three realms (heaven, earth, and the underworld).
  • ni - A prefix that can mean "down" or "into," indicating a grounding or stabilizing force.
  • dadhe - Means "to place" or "to establish," suggesting the act of creating a foundation or a base.
  • padam - Translates to "foot" or "step," often symbolizing a point of stability or a place of refuge.
  • samūḻham - Means "entire" or "complete," indicating wholeness or totality.
  • asya - Refers to "this" or "of this," pointing to the subject being discussed, which is the creation or the universe.
  • pāṁsure - Translates to "dust" or "earth," symbolizing the material world and its transient nature.

The mantra can be interpreted as follows: "This is Vishnu, who revolves in three ways, establishing a foundation in the complete essence of this material world." It emphasizes the role of Vishnu in maintaining the balance and order of the universe, acting as a stabilizing force amidst the chaos of existence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied to various situations where stability and balance are needed. For instance:

  • In personal life, when facing challenges, one can invoke the essence of Vishnu to seek balance and stability, reminding oneself to remain grounded.
  • In environmental contexts, the mantra can serve as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of preserving the earth, as it symbolizes the material world.
  • In community settings, it can inspire collective efforts to create a harmonious environment, reflecting the threefold nature of existence and the need for cooperation among different realms of society.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the divine presence that maintains order and stability in the universe. It encourages individuals to seek balance in their lives and to recognize the interconnectedness of all beings. By understanding and applying the teachings of this mantra, one can cultivate a sense of peace and harmony in both personal and communal aspects of life.
