सरस्वती या सरथं ययाथ स्वधाभिर देवी पितृभिर मदन्ती |आसद्यास्मिन बर्हिषि मादयस्वानमीव इषा आ धेहि अस्मे ||
sarasvati yā sarathaṁ yayātha svadhābhir devi pitṛbhir madantī | āsadyāsmin barhiṣi mādayasvānamīvā iṣa ā dhehy asme ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Sarasvati: Refers to the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning.
    • yā: Who.
    • sarathaṁ: With chariots or vehicles, symbolizing movement and progress.
    • yayātha: By whom.
    • svadhābhir: With offerings or sacrifices.
    • devi: O goddess.
    • pitṛbhir: By the ancestors or forefathers.
    • madantī: Who is intoxicated or filled with joy.
    • āsadyāsmin: In this place or in this assembly.
    • barhiṣi: On the sacred grass or altar.
    • mādayasvānam: May we be filled with joy or happiness.
    • īvā: Like or as if.
    • iṣa: The desire or wish.
    • ā dhehy: May it be fulfilled.
    • asme: For us.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the goddess Sarasvati, emphasizing her role in guiding and enriching our lives through knowledge and wisdom.
    • The mention of "sarathaṁ" signifies the importance of progress and movement in life, akin to how vehicles help us reach our destinations.
    • By referring to "svadhābhir," the mantra highlights the significance of offerings and sacrifices, which can be interpreted as the efforts we put into our pursuits.
    • The term "pitṛbhir" connects us to our ancestors, reminding us of the wisdom and values passed down through generations.
    • "Madantī" suggests a state of joy and fulfillment, which can be seen in real-life scenarios where knowledge leads to personal and communal happiness.
    • The phrase "āsadyāsmin barhiṣi" indicates a gathering or assembly, symbolizing the importance of community and collaboration in achieving goals.
    • Finally, the mantra expresses a wish for joy and fulfillment in our endeavors, emphasizing the importance of intention and desire in our actions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra is significant as it encapsulates the essence of seeking knowledge and wisdom while honoring our roots and community.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in educational settings, family gatherings, and community events where knowledge sharing and collective joy are paramount.
    • By invoking Sarasvati, we remind ourselves to pursue our goals with dedication, respect our heritage, and strive for happiness in our lives and the lives of others.
