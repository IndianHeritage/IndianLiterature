येन टोकाय तनयाय धान्यम् बीजं वहध्वे अक्षितम् |अस्मभ्यं तद धत्तन यद व इमहे राधो विश्वायु सौभगम् ||
yena tokāya tanayāya dhānya1m bījaṁ vahadhve akṣitam | asmabhyaṁ tad dhattana yad va īmahe rādho viśvāyu saubhagam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yena: by whom
    • tokāya: for the offspring
    • tanayāya: for the children
    • dhānyaṁ: grains or food
    • bījaṁ: seeds
    • vahadhve: may you carry
    • akṣitam: unblemished or pure
    • asmabhyaṁ: for us
    • tad: that
    • dhattana: may you bestow
    • yad: which
    • va: or
    • īmahe: we invoke
    • rādho: prosperity
    • viśvāyu: universal energy
    • saubhagam: auspiciousness or good fortune
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for abundance and prosperity, specifically focusing on the nourishment and well-being of future generations.
    • It emphasizes the importance of pure and unblemished resources, such as grains and seeds, which are essential for sustenance.
    • The invocation of universal energy and auspiciousness highlights the interconnectedness of all beings and the need for collective well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for a good harvest, seeking blessings for their crops to be healthy and abundant, ensuring food security for their families and communities.
    • In family gatherings, blessings are often sought for the health and prosperity of children, reflecting the desire for future generations to thrive.
    • Communities may come together to celebrate festivals that honor the earth and its resources, reinforcing the idea of collective prosperity and well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of nurturing not just ourselves but also future generations through the provision of pure and abundant resources.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals and communities seek to ensure sustainability, health, and prosperity for all.
    • By invoking universal energy and auspiciousness, we remind ourselves of our responsibility towards the environment and each other, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.
