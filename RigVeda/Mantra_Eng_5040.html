वहन्ति सīm अरुणासो रुशन्तो गावाḥ सुभगाम उर्वियā प्रथानाम् |अपेजते शूरो अस्तेव शत्रून बाधते तमो अजिरो न वोḻ्हा ||
vahanti sīm aruṇāso ruśanto gāvaḥ subhagām urviyā prathānām | apejate śūro asteva śatrūn bādhate tamo ajiro na voḻhā ||
  • Vahanti: This word translates to "they carry" or "they bear." It signifies the act of carrying or supporting something important.
  • Sīm: This means "the boundary" or "the limit." It can refer to the edges of a territory or the limits of understanding.
  • Aruṇāso: This term can be interpreted as "the red ones" or "the dawn." It symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the arrival of light after darkness.
  • Ruśanto: This means "shining" or "radiant." It conveys a sense of brilliance and positivity.
  • Gāvaḥ: This translates to "cows," which in Vedic culture symbolize wealth, nourishment, and prosperity.
  • Subhagām: This means "auspicious" or "fortunate." It indicates something that brings good fortune or blessings.
  • Urviya: This term refers to "the vastness" or "the expanse." It can denote the universe or the broadness of life experiences.
  • Prathānām: This means "of the first" or "of the foremost." It signifies priority or importance.
  • Apejate: This translates to "the brave" or "the courageous." It emphasizes the qualities of valor and strength.
  • Śūro: This means "hero" or "warrior." It represents someone who stands up against challenges.
  • Asteva: This can be interpreted as "indeed" or "truly." It adds emphasis to the statement being made.
  • Śatrūn: This translates to "enemies" or "opponents." It signifies challenges or adversities one may face.
  • Bādhate: This means "to obstruct" or "to hinder." It refers to the act of facing obstacles.
  • Tamo: This translates to "darkness" or "ignorance." It symbolizes the challenges that obscure clarity and understanding.
  • Ajiro: This means "swift" or "quick." It indicates the speed at which one can overcome challenges.
  • Na voḻhā: This translates to "not to be hindered" or "not to be obstructed." It emphasizes resilience and determination.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, resilience, and the pursuit of auspicious beginnings. It highlights the importance of courage in overcoming obstacles and the significance of nurturing prosperity and positivity in life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various situations, such as facing challenges in personal or professional life, striving for success, and maintaining a positive outlook despite adversities. It serves as a reminder to embrace new beginnings with courage and to remain steadfast in the face of difficulties, ultimately leading to growth and fulfillment.
