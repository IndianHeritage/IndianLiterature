अग्नेमृळीकं वरुणे सचा विदो मरुत्सु विश्वभानुषु |तोकाय तुजे शुशुचाना शं कृध्य अस्मभ्यं दसम शं कृधि ||
agne mṛḻīkaṁ varuṇe sacā vido marutsu viśvabhānuṣu | tokāya tuje śuśucāna śaṁ kṛdhy asmabhyaṁ dasma śaṁ kṛdhi ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a vital force in Vedic literature. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • Mṛḻīkaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "to be kind" or "to be gentle." It emphasizes the need for compassion and benevolence in our actions.
  • Varuṇe: Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water. He represents the moral and ethical aspects of the universe, ensuring that natural laws are upheld.
  • Sacā: This word means "truth" or "sincerity." It highlights the importance of honesty in our thoughts and actions.
  • Vido: This term translates to "knowledge" or "wisdom." It signifies the pursuit of understanding and enlightenment.
  • Marutsu: Referring to the Maruts, the storm deities, this word symbolizes the power of nature and the forces that can bring both destruction and renewal.
  • Viśvabhānuṣu: This term means "in all beings" or "in the universe." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all life and the divine presence within it.
  • Tokāya: This word can be interpreted as "to grant" or "to bestow." It reflects the act of giving and sharing blessings.
  • Tuje: This term means "to you," indicating a direct address to the divine or a higher power.
  • Śuśucāna: This word signifies "to be pleased" or "to be satisfied." It reflects the joy that comes from harmony and fulfillment.
  • Śaṁ: This term means "peace" or "well-being." It is a call for tranquility and harmony in life.
  • Kṛdhy: This word translates to "to act" or "to do." It emphasizes the importance of taking action in alignment with one's values.
  • Asmabhyaṁ: This term means "to us," indicating a collective appeal for blessings and guidance.
  • Dasma: This word can be interpreted as "from here" or "from this place," suggesting a grounding in the present moment.
  • Śaṁ kṛdhi: This phrase means "may you grant peace" or "may you bestow well-being." It is a prayer for divine intervention in achieving harmony.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a profound appeal to the divine forces of Agni and Varuna, seeking their blessings for kindness, truth, knowledge, and peace. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to act with compassion and integrity, fostering a sense of interconnectedness with all beings. By invoking these qualities, individuals can create a harmonious environment in their personal and communal lives, promoting well-being and understanding. The mantra encourages us to recognize the divine presence in our actions and the importance of striving for peace and fulfillment in our daily interactions.
