एतावान् अस्य महिमातो ज्यायांश्च पुरुषः |पादोऽस्य विश्वा भूतानि त्रिपादस्यामृतं दिवि ||
etāvān asya mahimāto jyāyām̐ś ca pūruṣaḥ | pādo 'sya viśvā bhūtāni tripād asyāmṛtaṁ divi ||
  • etāvān asya mahimāto: This phrase translates to "This is the extent of the greatness of that being." It emphasizes the vastness and significance of the divine or cosmic entity being referred to.
  • jyāyām̐ś ca pūruṣaḥ: Here, "jyāyām̐ś" means "greater than" and "pūruṣaḥ" refers to the cosmic being or the universal spirit. This suggests that the essence of this being transcends all.
  • pādo 'sya viśvā bhūtāni: This translates to "One quarter of this being encompasses all beings." It indicates that a part of this cosmic entity is present in all forms of life, highlighting the interconnectedness of existence.
  • tripād asyāmṛtaṁ divi: This means "Three quarters of it is immortal in the heavens." It signifies that the majority of this being exists in a divine, eternal state, beyond the physical realm.

  • Real-Life Example: Consider the concept of nature and the universe. Just as the mantra suggests that a part of the divine is present in all beings, we can observe how ecosystems are interconnected. For instance, the way trees provide oxygen for animals, and animals produce carbon dioxide for trees illustrates this interdependence.
  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the idea of unity in diversity. It teaches us that while we may appear as separate entities, we are all part of a larger whole. This can be applied in real life through practices like community building, where individuals come together to form a supportive network, recognizing that each person's contribution is vital.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the vastness of existence and the divine presence within all beings. It encourages us to recognize our interconnectedness and the importance of each individual in the grand scheme of life. By understanding this, we can foster compassion, cooperation, and a sense of responsibility towards one another and the environment.
