इन्द्र इति सोमपा एक इन्द्रः सुतपा विश्वायुः |अन्तर देवान मार्त्यांश च ||
indra it somapā eka indraḥ sutapā viśvāyuḥ | antar devān martyām̐ś ca ||
  • Indra: Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He symbolizes strength and power.
  • It: This word can be interpreted as "this" or "that," indicating a focus on the subject at hand.
  • Somapā: This term refers to the one who drinks soma, a sacred ritual drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight.
  • Eka: Meaning "one" or "single," it emphasizes unity or singularity in purpose or action.
  • Sutapā: This term can be translated as "well-prepared" or "wise," indicating a state of readiness and intelligence.
  • Viśvāyuḥ: This word means "all-pervading" or "universal," suggesting a connection to the vastness of existence.
  • Antar: Meaning "within" or "inside," it indicates an inner dimension or aspect.
  • Devān: This refers to the "gods" or divine beings, highlighting the spiritual aspect of the mantra.
  • Martyām̐ś: This term means "mortals" or "human beings," contrasting the divine with the human experience.

  • Meaning: The mantra can be interpreted as a call to Indra, the powerful deity, who is the singular drinker of soma, embodying wisdom and universal presence. It acknowledges the relationship between the divine and mortals, suggesting that the divine influences human life.
  • Real-life Example: In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of seeking wisdom and strength in our actions. For instance, a leader in a community may embody the qualities of Indra by making wise decisions that benefit all, much like Indra's role in the cosmos.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the connection between the divine and human realms, highlighting the importance of wisdom, unity, and strength in our lives. It serves as a reminder to seek guidance from higher powers while recognizing our own potential.
  • Application: In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate inner strength and wisdom, whether in personal challenges, leadership roles, or community service. It encourages a holistic approach to life, integrating spiritual insights with practical actions.
