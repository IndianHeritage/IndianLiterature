त्वां अग्नि प्रथमं देवयन्तो देवं मर्ता अमृत मन्द्रजिह्वम् |द्वेषोयुतम आ विवासन्ति धिभिर दामूनसं गृहपतिम अमूरम् ||
tvām agne prathamaṁ devayanto devam martā amṛta mandrajihvam | dveṣoyutam ā vivāsanti dhībhir damūnasaṁ gṛhapatim amūram ||
  • tvām agne - "to you, O Agni" - This phrase addresses Agni, the fire god, who is considered a divine presence in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying force and a medium for offerings to the gods.
  • prathamaṁ devayanto - "the first among the gods" - Agni is revered as the foremost deity, symbolizing the importance of fire in rituals and daily life.
  • devam martā - "the divine among mortals" - This highlights the connection between the divine and human realms, suggesting that through Agni, mortals can access divine blessings.
  • amṛta mandrajihvam - "with a tongue of immortality" - This phrase signifies the transformative power of fire, which can purify and elevate the soul, leading to spiritual immortality.
  • dveṣoyutam - "with enmity" - This indicates the presence of negativity or obstacles that may hinder spiritual progress.
  • ā vivāsanti dhībhir - "they are driven away by wisdom" - Wisdom and knowledge are essential in overcoming challenges and negativity in life.
  • damūnasaṁ gṛhapatim amūram - "the lord of the house, the unassailable" - This refers to the importance of maintaining a strong and positive environment in one's home, where Agni (fire) symbolizes warmth, protection, and prosperity.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a divine force that connects the mortal and immortal realms. It teaches us the importance of wisdom in overcoming negativity and maintaining a positive environment in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied by fostering a harmonious home atmosphere, practicing mindfulness, and seeking knowledge to navigate challenges. The mantra serves as a reminder of the transformative power of fire, both literally and metaphorically, encouraging us to embrace positivity and spiritual growth.
