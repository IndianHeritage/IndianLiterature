ये अग्निदग्धा ये अनाग्निदग्धा मध्य दिवः स्वधया मादयन्ते |तेभिः स्वराल् असुनीतिम एतां यथावशं तन्वं कल्पयस्व ||
ye agnidagdhā ye anagnidagdhā madhye divaḥ svadhayā mādayante | tebhiḥ svarāḻ asunītim etāṁ yathāvaśaṁ tanvaṁ kalpayasva ||
  • Mantra: "ye agnidagdhā ye anagnidagdhā madhye divaḥ svadhayā mādayante | tebhiḥ svarāḻ asunītim etāṁ yathāvaśaṁ tanvaṁ kalpayasva ||"
  • Translation: "Those who are burnt by fire, and those who are not burnt by fire, in the middle of the heavens, by their own will, they rejoice. With them, O divine one, create this body as you wish."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ye: Those who
    • agnidagdhā: burnt by fire
    • ye: those who
    • anagnidagdhā: not burnt by fire
    • madhye: in the middle
    • divaḥ: of the heavens
    • svadhayā: by their own will
    • mādayante: they rejoice
    • tebhiḥ: with them
    • svarāḻ: O divine one
    • asunītim: this body
    • etāṁ: as you wish
    • yathāvaśaṁ: as you desire
    • tanvaṁ: create
    • kalpayasva: make it manifest

  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra speaks to the duality of existence, represented by those who are affected by fire and those who are not. It symbolizes the challenges and trials of life (the fire) and the resilience or immunity some may have against these challenges.
    • In real life, this can be seen in various scenarios such as individuals facing hardships (like illness or loss) versus those who seem to navigate life with ease. The mantra encourages us to recognize both experiences and to find joy and strength in our own circumstances.
    • The phrase "by their own will" emphasizes the power of choice and intention in shaping our lives. It suggests that regardless of external circumstances, we have the ability to create our own happiness and reality.

  • Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra can be applied in personal development and mental health. It serves as a reminder that while we may face difficulties (the fire), we can choose how to respond and create our own path to joy and fulfillment.
    • In leadership and teamwork, understanding that team members may have different responses to stress can foster a supportive environment where everyone can thrive, regardless of their challenges.
    • In spiritual practices, this mantra can be used as a meditation focus, encouraging individuals to reflect on their own experiences of adversity and resilience, and to visualize their desired outcomes.

  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra encapsulates the essence of human experience—facing challenges and the power of will in shaping our lives. It teaches us that regardless of our circumstances, we have the ability to create our own reality and find joy.
    • By embracing both the trials and the triumphs, we can cultivate a deeper understanding of ourselves and others, leading to a more compassionate and fulfilling life.
