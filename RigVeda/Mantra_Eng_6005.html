आ यद अश्वान वनन्वतः श्रद्धया हं रथे रुहम् |उत वाamas्य वसनश चिकेटति यो अस्ति याद्वः पशुः ||
ā yad aśvān vananvataḥ śraddhayāhaṁ rathe ruham | uta vāmasya vasunaś ciketati yo asti yādvaḥ paśuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā - from
    • yad - whatever
    • aśvān - horses
    • vananvataḥ - who are swift
    • śraddhayā - with faith
    • haṁ - I
    • rathe - in the chariot
    • ruham - I rise
    • uta - or
    • vāmasya - of the left
    • vasunaś - of wealth
    • ciketati - he thinks
    • yo - who
    • asti - is
    • yādvaḥ - whatever
    • paśuḥ - animal
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the connection between faith, action, and the pursuit of wealth and prosperity.
    • The mention of "aśvān" (horses) symbolizes strength and speed, often associated with the chariot of life, which we navigate through our actions and decisions.
    • The phrase "with faith" emphasizes the importance of belief in oneself and the divine while undertaking any journey or task.
    • The reference to "wealth" and "animal" can be interpreted as the material and spiritual aspects of life, suggesting a balance between the two.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to entrepreneurs who must have faith in their vision and work diligently (like the horses pulling a chariot) to achieve success.
    • In personal development, individuals often face challenges that require them to rise above difficulties with faith and determination, akin to rising in a chariot.
    • In relationships, the balance between material wealth and emotional fulfillment can be seen as a reflection of the mantra's teachings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of faith and action in achieving one's goals, whether they are material or spiritual.
    • It teaches us that with the right mindset and determination, we can navigate the challenges of life effectively.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more balanced and fulfilling existence, where both personal and material aspirations are harmonized.
