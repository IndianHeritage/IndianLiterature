बृहस्पते परि दीया रथेन रक्षोहमित्रां अपबाधमानः | प्रभञ्जन सेनाः प्रमृणो युधा जयन्न अस्माकं एध्य अविता रथानाम् ||
bṛhaspate pari dīyā rathena rakṣohāmitrām̐ apabādhamānaḥ | prabhañjan senāḥ pramṛṇo yudhā jayann asmākam edhy avitā rathānām ||
  • Mantra: "bṛhaspate pari dīyā rathena rakṣohāmitrām̐ apabādhamānaḥ | prabhañjan senāḥ pramṛṇo yudhā jayann asmākam edhy avitā rathānām ||"
  • Translation: This mantra invokes Bṛhaspati, the deity of wisdom and eloquence, asking for protection and victory in battles. It emphasizes the importance of divine guidance in overcoming adversaries and achieving success.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • bṛhaspate: Refers to Bṛhaspati, the guru of the gods, symbolizing wisdom and knowledge.
    • pari dīyā: Means "bestow upon us," indicating a plea for blessings.
    • rathena: Translates to "with the chariot," symbolizing strength and readiness for battle.
    • rakṣohāmitrām̐: Refers to "enemies of the demons," indicating the protection against malevolent forces.
    • apabādhamānaḥ: Means "removing obstacles," highlighting the desire for the removal of hindrances in one's path.
    • prabhañjan: Refers to "the one who brings light," symbolizing clarity and guidance.
    • senāḥ: Means "armies," representing collective strength.
    • pramṛṇo: Translates to "victorious," indicating success in endeavors.
    • yudhā: Means "in battle," emphasizing the context of conflict.
    • jayann: Means "winning," reinforcing the theme of victory.
    • asmākam: Means "our," indicating a collective plea.
    • edhy: Means "come forth," a call to action.
    • avitā: Means "protect," emphasizing the need for safeguarding.
    • rathānām: Means "of the chariots," symbolizing readiness and strength in battle.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive business environment, leaders often seek wisdom and guidance to navigate challenges, similar to invoking Bṛhaspati for clarity and strategy.
    • During sports competitions, teams may chant mantras or motivational phrases to foster unity and focus, akin to the collective strength of "senāḥ" in the mantra.
    • In personal struggles, individuals may seek support from mentors or spiritual guides, paralleling the plea for divine intervention in overcoming obstacles.
  • Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of seeking wisdom, protection, and victory in various aspects of life. It highlights the need for guidance in overcoming challenges, whether in personal endeavors, professional pursuits, or collective efforts. By invoking divine support, individuals and groups can find strength and clarity to navigate their paths successfully.
