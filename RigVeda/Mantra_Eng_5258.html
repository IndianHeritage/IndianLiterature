अधā महī न āयस्य अनाधृत्स्तो नृपीतये |पूर भवā शतभुजिः ||
adhā mahī na āyasy anādhṛṣṭo nṛpītaye | pūr bhavā śatabhujiḥ ||
  • adhā - This word translates to "down" or "below." It signifies a grounding or foundational aspect.
  • mahī - Refers to "earth" or "the great." It symbolizes stability, nourishment, and the physical realm.
  • na - This is a negation, meaning "not." It indicates the absence of something.
  • āyasy - This term can be interpreted as "to be disturbed" or "to be shaken." It suggests a state of tranquility or stability.
  • anādhṛṣṭo - Meaning "unseen" or "not perceived." It implies a deeper understanding or insight that is not immediately visible.
  • nṛpītaye - This translates to "for the king" or "for the ruler." It denotes authority, governance, and leadership.
  • pūr - This word means "full" or "complete." It signifies abundance and fulfillment.
  • bhavā - Refers to "existence" or "being." It relates to the essence of life and the universe.
  • śatabhujiḥ - This term can be understood as "having a hundred arms" or "multifaceted." It symbolizes versatility, strength, and the ability to handle multiple tasks or responsibilities.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for stability and strength in leadership, emphasizing the importance of a ruler who is grounded and unshaken by external disturbances. It suggests that true leadership comes from a place of deep understanding and insight, which may not always be visible to others. The imagery of being "full" and "multifaceted" indicates that a good leader should possess a wide range of abilities and be capable of addressing various challenges.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Leadership in Organizations: A leader in a corporate setting should remain grounded and composed, even in challenging situations. For example, during a financial crisis, a leader who maintains clarity and insight can guide their team effectively.
  • Community Leadership: Local leaders or community organizers can embody the qualities of this mantra by being approachable and understanding the needs of their community, thus fostering a sense of trust and stability.
  • Personal Development: Individuals can reflect on this mantra to cultivate inner strength and resilience, ensuring they remain unshaken by life's challenges and uncertainties.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the significance of grounded leadership that is both insightful and multifaceted. It serves as a reminder that true strength lies in the ability to remain stable and composed while navigating the complexities of life and leadership. By embodying these qualities, individuals can foster a more harmonious and effective environment in their personal and professional lives.
