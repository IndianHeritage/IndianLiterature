अजीजनो हि पवमान सूर्यं विधारे शक्मना पयः |गोजीरयाऽरंहमाणः पुरंढ्या ||
ajījano hi pavamāna sūryaṁ vidhāre śakmanā payaḥ | gojīrayā raṁhamāṇaḥ puraṁdhyā ||
  • ajījano hi pavamāna: This phrase can be translated as "the one who is born again, indeed, the purifying one." Here, "ajījano" refers to the concept of rebirth or renewal, while "pavamāna" signifies purity and the act of purification. In real life, this can be related to the idea of personal growth and transformation, where individuals often go through phases of renewal in their lives, such as overcoming challenges or changing their perspectives.
  • sūryaṁ vidhāre: This translates to "the sun that sustains." The sun is often seen as a source of life and energy. In practical terms, this can be likened to the importance of the sun in agriculture, where sunlight is essential for the growth of crops. It also symbolizes the vital energy that sustains all living beings, reminding us of our dependence on natural forces.
  • śakmanā payaḥ: This means "with strength, the waters." Here, "śakmanā" refers to strength or power, and "payaḥ" means water. This can be interpreted as the strength that comes from water, which is essential for life. In real life, this can be seen in the context of water conservation and management, emphasizing the need to respect and utilize water resources wisely for sustainability.
  • gojīrayā raṁhamāṇaḥ: This phrase can be understood as "the one who brings joy to the cows." Cows are often considered sacred in many cultures, particularly in Hinduism. This can symbolize the nurturing aspect of life, where caring for animals and nature brings happiness and prosperity. In a broader sense, it reflects the importance of compassion and kindness towards all living beings.
  • puraṁdhyā: This term can be translated as "the one who is the creator of the city or the abode." It signifies the idea of creation and establishment. In real life, this can relate to urban development and the importance of creating sustainable and harmonious living environments. It emphasizes the role of individuals and communities in shaping their surroundings for the betterment of society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of renewal, sustenance, strength, joy, and creation. Its significance lies in the interconnectedness of life and the natural world. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to embrace personal growth, respect natural resources, show compassion towards all beings, and actively participate in creating a better environment. By embodying these principles, we can contribute to a more harmonious and sustainable world.
