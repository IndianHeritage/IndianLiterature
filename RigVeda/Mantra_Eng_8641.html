पवस्व वाजसातमः पवित्रे धारयासुतः |इन्द्राय सोम विष्णवे देवभ्यो मधुमत्तमः ||
pavasva vājasātamaḥ pavitre dhārayā sutaḥ | indrāya soma viṣṇave devebhyo madhumattamaḥ ||
  • pavasva vājasātamaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "O Wind, the best of the strong." Here, "pavasva" refers to the wind or the life force, while "vājasātamaḥ" signifies the strongest or the best among the strong. In real life, this can be likened to the importance of harnessing our inner strength and vitality to overcome challenges.
  • pavitre dhārayā sutaḥ: This translates to "May you, the pure one, hold the offspring." "Pavitre" means pure, and "dhārayā sutaḥ" refers to the act of nurturing or holding the offspring. This can be related to the nurturing aspect of parenting or mentorship, where purity of intention and care is essential for the growth of the next generation.
  • indrāya soma viṣṇave: This part means "To Indra, to Soma, to Vishnu." Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength and power, Soma symbolizes the divine nectar or elixir of life, and Vishnu represents preservation and protection. In a real-life context, this can be seen as a call to seek strength, nourishment, and protection in our lives, whether through community, spirituality, or personal development.
  • devebhyo madhumattamaḥ: This translates to "To the gods, the sweetest." "Devebhyo" refers to the gods, and "madhumattamaḥ" means the sweetest or the most delightful. This can be interpreted as an offering or a prayer for the blessings of the divine, which can be seen in various cultural practices where people seek divine favor for prosperity and happiness.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, purity, nurturing, and divine blessings in our lives. It serves as a reminder to harness our inner strength, nurture those around us, seek protection and nourishment, and remain connected to the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts such as parenting, community building, personal development, and spiritual practices. By embodying these principles, individuals can create a harmonious and fulfilling life.
