आ नो द्रप्सा मधुमन्तो विशन्त्व इन्द्र देहि अधिरथं सहस्रम् |नि शीदा होत्रं ऋतुथा यजस्व देवान देवापे हविषा सपर्य ||
ā no drapsā madhumanto viśantv indra dehy adhirathaṁ sahasram | ni ṣīda hotram ṛtuthā yajasva devān devāpe haviṣā saparya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā no: May we be blessed
    • drapsā: with nourishment
    • madhumanto: sweet and pleasant
    • viśantv: may they enter
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • dehy: grant us
    • adhirathaṁ: a thousand chariots
    • sahasram: a thousand
    • ni ṣīda: sit down
    • hotram: as a priest
    • ṛtuthā: in accordance with truth
    • yajasva: perform the sacrifice
    • devān: to the gods
    • devāpe: to the divine beings
    • haviṣā: with offerings
    • saparya: with reverence
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer invoking the blessings of Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, who represents strength, power, and prosperity.
    • The phrase "ā no drapsā madhumanto" emphasizes the desire for nourishment that is sweet and pleasant, symbolizing not just physical sustenance but also emotional and spiritual well-being.
    • By asking Indra to grant us "adhirathaṁ sahasram," the mantra seeks the strength and resources equivalent to a thousand chariots, which can be interpreted as a request for abundant resources and support in life.
    • The call to "ni ṣīda hotram" signifies the importance of performing rituals and sacrifices with sincerity and truth, highlighting the role of spirituality in achieving one's goals.
    • Finally, the mantra concludes with a request to the divine beings to accept the offerings made with reverence, indicating the importance of gratitude and respect in our interactions with the divine.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra can be recited during personal or family rituals to invoke blessings for prosperity and well-being.
    • In times of difficulty, individuals may reflect on the essence of this mantra to seek inner strength and resilience, akin to the strength of a thousand chariots.
    • It serves as a reminder to approach life with a sense of gratitude and reverence, whether in personal relationships, professional endeavors, or spiritual practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing nourishment, strength, truth, and reverence.
    • It encourages individuals to seek not just material wealth but also spiritual fulfillment and emotional well-being.
    • In a world often focused on material success, this mantra serves as a guiding principle to balance our pursuits with gratitude and respect for the divine and the universe.
