मन्त्र: शुचिः क्ष्मा यस्मा अतृवात प्र स्वधितीव रीयते | सुषूर असूत माता क्राणā यद आनशे भागम् ||
śuciḥ ṣma yasmā atrivat pra svadhitīva rīyate | suṣūr asūta mātā krāṇā yad ānaśe bhagam ||
  • śuciḥ: This word translates to "pure" or "clean." In a real-life context, it can refer to the importance of maintaining purity in thoughts, actions, and surroundings. For example, a clean environment promotes health and well-being.
  • ṣma: This term means "peace" or "tranquility." It emphasizes the need for inner peace, which can be achieved through practices like meditation or mindfulness. For instance, individuals who practice yoga often find a sense of calm and balance in their lives.
  • yasmā: This means "because of" or "due to." It indicates a cause-and-effect relationship, suggesting that purity and peace lead to positive outcomes.
  • atrivat: This word can be interpreted as "like the one who is present" or "in the manner of." It suggests that one should embody the qualities of purity and peace in their actions.
  • pra svadhitīva: This phrase can be understood as "may it be well-placed" or "may it be established." It implies the importance of setting intentions and ensuring that one's actions are aligned with their values.
  • rīyate: This means "to flourish" or "to prosper." It indicates that when one embodies purity and peace, they are likely to experience growth and success in various aspects of life.
  • suṣūr: This term refers to "nourishing" or "supportive." It highlights the significance of having a supportive environment or community that fosters growth.
  • asūta: This means "to give birth" or "to create." It signifies the act of bringing forth new ideas, projects, or relationships that are rooted in purity and peace.
  • mātā: This translates to "mother," symbolizing nurturing and care. It emphasizes the role of nurturing in personal and communal growth.
  • krāṇā: This word can mean "to act" or "to perform." It suggests that actions should be taken with intention and mindfulness.
  • yad: This means "that" or "which," referring to the outcomes of the actions taken.
  • ānaśe: This term means "to be nourished" or "to be sustained." It indicates the importance of sustaining oneself and others through positive actions.
  • bhagam: This translates to "blessings" or "prosperity." It signifies the rewards that come from living a life of purity and peace.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of purity, peace, and nurturing in our lives. It teaches us that by embodying these qualities, we can create a supportive environment that fosters growth and prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways, such as maintaining a clean and peaceful home, practicing mindfulness, and nurturing relationships. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that our actions, rooted in purity and peace, lead to blessings and a flourishing life.
