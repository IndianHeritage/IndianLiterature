मन्त्र: सप्त दिशो नानासूर्याः सप्त होतार ऋत्विजः |देवा आदित्याः ये सप्त तैभिः सोमाभिः रक्ष न इन्द्रायेंदो परि श्रव ||
sapta diśo nānāsūryāḥ sapta hotāra ṛtvijaḥ | devā ādityā ye sapta tebhiḥ somābhi rakṣa na indrāyendo pari srava ||
  • Mantra: "sapta diśo nānāsūryāḥ sapta hotāra ṛtvijaḥ | devā ādityā ye sapta tebhiḥ somābhi rakṣa na indrāyendo pari srava ||"
  • Translation: "The seven directions are filled with various suns; the seven priests are the officiants of the rituals. The deities, the seven Adityas, protect us with their soma, and may Indra, the lord of the heavens, pour forth his blessings upon us."

  • Explanation of Key Terms:
    • Sapta Diśo: Refers to the seven directions (north, south, east, west, up, down, and the center), symbolizing the totality of existence.
    • Nānāsūryāḥ: Indicates the presence of various suns, representing different forms of energy and light in the universe.
    • Sapta Hotāra Ṛtvijaḥ: The seven priests or officiants who perform rituals, signifying the importance of guidance and tradition in spiritual practices.
    • Devā Ādityā: The seven Adityas are solar deities, embodying various aspects of life and nature.
    • Soma: A sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine nourishment.
    • Indra: The king of the gods, representing strength, power, and protection.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of the seven directions can be applied in various fields such as architecture and urban planning, where the orientation of buildings is crucial for energy flow and harmony.
    • The idea of multiple suns can be seen in the diversity of cultures and perspectives in our world, emphasizing the importance of embracing different viewpoints.
    • Rituals performed by priests can be likened to modern-day ceremonies that mark significant life events, such as weddings and graduations, highlighting the continuity of tradition.
    • The Adityas can be compared to the various roles that sunlight plays in our lives, from agriculture to mental well-being, showcasing the interconnectedness of nature and humanity.
    • Soma, as a symbol of nourishment, can be related to the importance of healthy food and lifestyle choices in achieving physical and mental well-being.
    • Indra's blessings can be interpreted as the support and protection we seek in our endeavors, whether in personal or professional life.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of the universe, the importance of rituals, and the divine protection that guides us. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek balance in all directions, embrace diversity, and honor traditions that enrich our lives. By recognizing the significance of these elements, we can cultivate a deeper understanding of our place in the world and the forces that shape our existence.
