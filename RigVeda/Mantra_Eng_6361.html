यस्य द्विभरसो बृहत्सहो दाधार रोडसी |गिरींर अज्रां अपः स्वर वृषत्वना ||
yasya dvibarhaso bṛhat saho dādhāra rodasī | girīm̐r ajrām̐ apaḥ svar vṛṣatvanā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • dvibarhaso: two-fold (or dual) strength
    • bṛhat: great or vast
    • saho: who supports or holds
    • dādhāra: has upheld or maintained
    • rodasī: the two realms (heavens and earth)
    • girīm: the mountains
    • ajrām: unaging or eternal
    • apaḥ: waters
    • svar: the sky or celestial realm
    • vṛṣatvanā: with strength or power
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine entity or force that possesses dual strength and vastness.
    • The phrase "who supports or holds" indicates a nurturing aspect, suggesting that this force upholds the balance between the heavens and the earth.
    • The mention of "mountains" and "waters" signifies the natural elements that are essential for life, representing stability and nourishment.
    • The term "unaging" implies a timeless quality, suggesting that this divine force is eternal and unchanging, much like the natural laws that govern our world.
    • Finally, "with strength" emphasizes the power inherent in this force, which can be seen in the resilience of nature and the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The dual strength can be likened to the balance of opposites in life, such as day and night, or joy and sorrow, which are essential for a harmonious existence.
    • The nurturing aspect can be observed in parental figures who support their children, providing them with the strength to face life's challenges.
    • The mountains and waters represent the natural environment, which sustains life. For instance, rivers provide water for agriculture, while mountains can symbolize stability and endurance.
    • The concept of timelessness can be reflected in the enduring principles of nature, such as the cycle of seasons, which continues regardless of human intervention.
    • The strength of this divine force can be seen in the resilience of communities that come together in times of crisis, showcasing the power of unity and support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of balance, support, and the eternal nature of the universe.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us of the interconnectedness of all elements in life and the importance of nurturing relationships, both with nature and with each other.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek balance in their lives, appreciate the natural world, and foster supportive communities.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a deeper understanding of the forces that sustain us and the importance of maintaining harmony in our lives.
