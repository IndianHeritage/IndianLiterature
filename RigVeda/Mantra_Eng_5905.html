तं शग्मासो अरुषासो अश्वा बृहस्पतिं सहवाहो वहन्ति |सहश्चिद यस्य नीलवत सदस्थं नभो न रूपं अरुशं वसानाḥ ||
taṁ śagmāso aruṣāso aśvā bṛhaspatiṁ sahavāho vahanti | sahaś cid yasya nīlavat sadhasthaṁ nabho na rūpam aruṣaṁ vasānāḥ ||
  • taṁ śagmāso aruṣāso aśvā bṛhaspatiṁ sahavāho vahanti
    • taṁ - This word refers to "that" or "him," indicating a focus on a specific entity or deity.
    • śagmāso - This term can be interpreted as "those who are strong" or "powerful," often referring to divine beings or forces.
    • aruṣāso - This word means "red" or "radiant," symbolizing energy, vitality, and the dawn.
    • aśvā - This translates to "horses," which in Vedic literature often symbolize speed, strength, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • bṛhaspatiṁ - Referring to "Bṛhaspati," the deity of wisdom and eloquence, often associated with knowledge and guidance.
    • sahavāho - This means "those who carry together," indicating collaboration and unity in purpose.
    • vahanti - This translates to "they carry" or "they bring," suggesting the act of delivering or manifesting something significant.
  • sahaś cid yasya nīlavat sadhasthaṁ nabho na rūpam aruṣaṁ vasānāḥ
    • sahaś - This means "together" or "with," emphasizing the importance of unity.
    • cid - This word translates to "consciousness" or "awareness," indicating a higher state of understanding.
    • yasya - Meaning "whose," it refers to possession or belonging.
    • nīlavat - This term means "like the blue," often symbolizing depth, stability, and the vastness of the sky or consciousness.
    • sadhasthaṁ - This translates to "situated in the assembly," indicating a place of gathering or community.
    • nabho - Meaning "of the sky" or "heaven," it represents the divine or spiritual realm.
    • na rūpam - This means "without form," suggesting the formless nature of the ultimate reality.
    • aruṣaṁ - Again referring to "red" or "radiant," symbolizing energy and life.
    • vasānāḥ - This translates to "those who dwell," indicating a state of being or existence.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, strength, and wisdom in our lives. It suggests that when we come together with a common purpose, guided by knowledge and awareness, we can achieve great things. The imagery of horses symbolizes the power and speed we can harness when we work collaboratively. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork in professional settings, community building, and even in personal relationships where mutual support and understanding are essential. The significance lies in recognizing that our collective efforts, guided by wisdom and consciousness, can lead to profound transformations and achievements.
