यः कुक्षिः सोमपातमः समुद्र इव पिन्वते |उर्वीर आपो न काकुदः ||
yaḥ kukṣiḥ somapātamaḥ samudra iva pinvate | urvīr āpo na kākudaḥ ||
  • yaḥ - This word translates to "who" or "that one." It indicates a subject or a person being referred to.
  • kukṣiḥ - This means "womb" or "source." In a broader sense, it can refer to the origin or the place from which something emerges.
  • somapātamaḥ - This term can be broken down into "soma," which refers to a sacred drink or elixir in Vedic tradition, and "pātamaḥ," meaning "the one who drinks." Together, it signifies "the one who drinks the soma," often associated with divine or spiritual nourishment.
  • samudra - This word means "ocean" or "sea." It symbolizes vastness, depth, and the source of life.
  • iva - This translates to "like" or "as," indicating a comparison or simile.
  • pinvate - This means "to flow" or "to pour." It suggests the act of something being released or made available.
  • urvīr - This term means "broad" or "expansive." It can refer to the vastness of the earth or the universe.
  • āpo - This translates to "waters." In Vedic literature, water is often associated with purity, life, and sustenance.
  • na - This means "not," indicating negation or absence.
  • kākudaḥ - This term can be interpreted as "the one who is obstructed" or "the one who is hindered." It suggests a lack of flow or blockage.

The mantra can be interpreted as follows: "Who, like the ocean, flows from the womb of the sacred drink, the waters are not obstructed." This suggests a profound connection between the divine source of life (the womb) and the vast, flowing nature of existence (the ocean). It emphasizes the idea that true nourishment and sustenance come from a divine source, which is abundant and unimpeded.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Spiritual Growth: Just as the ocean flows freely, individuals can seek spiritual nourishment from their experiences and the divine, allowing them to grow and expand in their understanding of life.
  • Emotional Well-being: The idea of flowing waters can symbolize emotional health. When one is not obstructed by negativity or blockages, they can experience a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to waters and the ocean can remind us of the importance of preserving our natural resources. Just as the waters should flow freely, we must ensure that our environment is protected from pollution and obstruction.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of divine nourishment and the unimpeded flow of life. It encourages individuals to seek spiritual and emotional growth while also reminding us of our responsibility to care for the environment. By embracing the essence of this mantra, we can cultivate a life that is expansive, fulfilling, and connected to the greater whole.
