अस्य मन्दानो मध्वो वज्रहस्तोऽहिम इन्द्रो अर्णोवृतं वि वृष्चत् |प्र यद वयो न स्वसरााण्य अच्छा प्रयांसी च नदीनां चक्रमन्त ||
asya mandāno madhvo vajrahasto 'him indro arṇovṛtaṁ vi vṛścat | pra yad vayo na svasarāṇy acchā prayāṁsi ca nadīnāṁ cakramanta ||
  • Mantra: "asya mandāno madhvo vajrahasto 'him indro arṇovṛtaṁ vi vṛścat | pra yad vayo na svasarāṇy acchā prayāṁsi ca nadīnāṁ cakramanta ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "Indra, the mighty one with a thunderbolt, who is surrounded by the waters, may you bestow your blessings upon us. As we navigate through the currents of life, may we be protected and guided by your strength."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Indra: The king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength and power.
    • Vajrahasto: Literally means "one with a thunderbolt in hand," representing the might and authority of Indra.
    • Arṇovṛtaṁ: Refers to being surrounded by waters, symbolizing the challenges and emotions in life.
    • Vṛścat: To bestow or grant, indicating a request for blessings.
    • Nadīnāṁ: Refers to rivers or currents, symbolizing the flow of life and experiences.
    • Cakramanta: To navigate or move through, indicating the journey of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek strength and guidance, much like invoking Indra for support. For instance, someone facing a difficult decision may pray for clarity and strength to make the right choice.
    • In a community setting, when facing natural disasters like floods, people come together to seek divine protection and strength, similar to how this mantra calls upon Indra to navigate through life's turbulent waters.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and strength in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that, like rivers that flow and change course, life is full of uncertainties, and one must navigate through them with courage and wisdom.
    • By invoking Indra, individuals acknowledge the need for external support and the power of faith in overcoming obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal struggles to communal challenges, highlighting the universal need for strength and guidance.
    • It encourages individuals to remain resilient and seek help when needed, reinforcing the idea that one is not alone in their journey.
    • Ultimately, it teaches the value of faith, community, and the strength that comes from acknowledging a higher power in navigating the complexities of life.
