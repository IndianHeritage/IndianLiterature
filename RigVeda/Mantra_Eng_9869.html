वृषा वो अंशुर न किला ऋषाथनेळावन्तः सदम इत स्थानाशिताः |रैवतयेव महसा चारवः स्थान यस्य ग्रावाणो अजुषध्वम् अध्वरम् ||
vṛṣā vo aṁśur na kilā riṣāthaneḻāvantaḥ sadam it sthanāśitāḥ | raivatyeva mahasā cāravaḥ sthana yasya grāvāṇo ajuṣadhvam adhvaram ||
  • vṛṣā vo aṁśur: This phrase can be interpreted as "the strong one (vṛṣā) is your portion (aṁśur)." In a real-life context, this can signify the importance of strength and resilience in facing challenges. For example, athletes often rely on their physical strength to overcome obstacles in competitions.
  • na kilā riṣāthaneḻāvantaḥ: This translates to "surely, there is no anger (riṣā) in the abode (sthāna)." This can be related to the idea of maintaining peace and harmony in relationships. For instance, in a workplace, fostering a collaborative environment can lead to better productivity and morale.
  • sadam it sthanāśitāḥ: This means "they who dwell (sadan) in the abode (sthāna) of stability." This can be seen in the context of individuals who find stability in their lives through education or career. For example, a person who has a stable job may feel more secure and content.
  • raivatyeva mahasā cāravaḥ: This can be interpreted as "like the great (mahasā) Raivata (a celestial being), they move (cāravaḥ)." This suggests the idea of striving for greatness and moving forward in life. An example could be entrepreneurs who take risks to achieve their dreams, embodying the spirit of Raivata.
  • sthana yasya grāvāṇo: This translates to "the place (sthana) where the gathering (grāvāṇo) occurs." This can refer to community gatherings or social events that foster unity and collaboration. For instance, community service events bring people together for a common cause, strengthening social bonds.
  • ajuṣadhvam adhvaram: This means "do not neglect (ajuṣadhvam) the sacred ritual (adhvaram)." This emphasizes the importance of rituals and traditions in maintaining cultural identity. For example, family gatherings during festivals can reinforce cultural values and traditions.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, harmony, stability, striving for greatness, community, and the importance of rituals in life. In real-life scenarios, these concepts can be applied to personal development, relationship building, community engagement, and cultural preservation. By embodying these principles, individuals can lead more fulfilling and meaningful lives, contributing positively to society.
