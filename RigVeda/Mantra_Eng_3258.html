नू ष्टुत इन्द्र नू गृणाना इषं जारित्रे नद्यो न पीपेः |अकारि ते हरिवो ब्रह्म नव्यं धियाऽस्याम रथ्यः सदासाः ||
nū ṣṭuta indra nū gṛṇāna iṣaṁ jaritre nadyo3 na pīpeḥ | akāri te harivo brahma navyaṁ dhiyā syāma rathyaḥ sadāsāḥ ||
  • nū ṣṭuta indra: This phrase can be translated as "O Indra, who is praised." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunder, and war. In real life, this can be likened to leaders or figures of authority who are recognized for their contributions and are revered by their followers.
  • nū gṛṇāna iṣaṁ: This means "we sing your praises." Singing praises can be seen in various cultures where people express gratitude and admiration for their leaders, mentors, or deities through songs and hymns, such as national anthems or religious chants.
  • jaritre nadyo3 na pīpeḥ: This translates to "the rivers that flow, do not dry up." Rivers are vital for sustenance and life, symbolizing abundance and continuity. In real life, this can be related to the importance of preserving natural resources and ensuring that communities have access to clean water.
  • akāri te harivo: This means "you have made the horses." Horses in Vedic culture symbolize strength, speed, and power. In modern contexts, this can be compared to the development of technology and infrastructure that empower societies, such as transportation systems that enhance connectivity.
  • brahma navyaṁ: This translates to "new and fresh knowledge." The pursuit of knowledge is a continuous journey. In real life, this can be seen in the context of education and innovation, where new ideas and discoveries drive progress in various fields, from science to the arts.
  • dhiyā syāma rathyaḥ sadāsāḥ: This means "may we always be endowed with wisdom." Wisdom is essential for making informed decisions. In everyday life, this can be reflected in the importance of critical thinking and ethical considerations in personal and professional choices.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of praise, abundance, strength, knowledge, and wisdom. It highlights the interconnectedness of these elements in fostering a thriving community. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as leadership, environmental conservation, technological advancement, and the pursuit of education. By embodying these principles, individuals and societies can work towards a harmonious and prosperous existence.
