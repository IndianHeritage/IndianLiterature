असूता पृष्णिर महते रणाय त्वेषम् अयासाम् मरुताम् अनीकम् |ते साप्सरासो जनयन्ताभवम् आदित् स्वधाम् इषिराम् पर्य अपश्यन् ||
asūta pṛśnir mahate raṇāya tveṣam ayāsām marutām anīkam | te sapsarāso 'janayantābhvam ād it svadhām iṣirām pary apaśyan ||
  • asūta: This word can be interpreted as "to create" or "to bring forth." It signifies the act of manifestation or emergence.
  • pṛśnir: This term refers to "the great one" or "the powerful." It denotes strength and significance in the context of the mantra.
  • mahate: Meaning "great" or "mighty," this word emphasizes the importance of the subject being discussed.
  • raṇāya: This translates to "for battle" or "for conflict." It suggests a readiness to engage in challenges or confrontations.
  • tveṣam: This word means "your" or "of you," indicating possession or connection to the subject.
  • ayāsām: This can be interpreted as "of the unyielding" or "of those who do not tire," suggesting resilience and strength.
  • marutām: Referring to "the Maruts," the storm deities in Vedic literature, symbolizing power and dynamic energy.
  • anīkam: This means "a host" or "a group," indicating a collective force or unity among the Maruts.
  • te: This translates to "they" or "those," referring back to the Maruts or the powerful forces mentioned earlier.
  • sapsarāso: This term can be understood as "the beautiful" or "the graceful," often associated with celestial beings or divine qualities.
  • ajanayantābhvam: This means "they who give birth" or "they who create," emphasizing the generative aspect of the Maruts.
  • ād: This word means "from" or "out of," indicating a source or origin.
  • it: This translates to "that" or "there," pointing to a specific context or situation.
  • svadhām: Meaning "of one's own power" or "self-generated," it highlights the autonomy and strength of the forces at play.
  • iṣirām: This term refers to "the arrows" or "the forces," symbolizing the tools or means of action.
  • pary apaśyan: This translates to "they have seen all around" or "they have observed everything," indicating awareness and perception.

Conclusion: The mantra speaks to the emergence of powerful forces, particularly the Maruts, who are depicted as resilient and dynamic entities ready for battle. It emphasizes the importance of unity, strength, and the ability to create and manifest in the face of challenges. In real life, this can be applied to situations where individuals or groups face adversity, encouraging them to harness their inner strength and work together to overcome obstacles. The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of collective action and the importance of resilience in achieving goals.
