स वर्धिता वर्धनः पूयमानः सोमो मीढ्वाम् अभि नो ज्योतिषावीत् |येना नः पूर्वे पितरः पदज्ञाः स्वरविदो अभि गा अद्रिम उष्णन् ||
sa vardhitā vardhanaḥ pūyamānaḥ somo mīḍhvām̐ abhi no jyotiṣāvīt | yenā naḥ pūrve pitaraḥ padajñāḥ svarvido abhi gā adrim uṣṇan ||
  • sa vardhitā vardhanaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "that which is nourished and nourishes." It signifies the reciprocal relationship between growth and sustenance, highlighting the importance of mutual support in life.
  • pūyamānaḥ: This word means "being filled" or "being purified." It suggests a state of completeness and purity, which is essential for spiritual and personal growth.
  • somo: Referring to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, it symbolizes divine inspiration and the elixir of life. Soma is often associated with the moon and represents the nurturing aspects of nature.
  • mīḍhvām̐: This term means "to be sweet" or "to be pleasant." It emphasizes the importance of sweetness in life, both in experiences and relationships.
  • abhi no jyotiṣāvīt: This phrase translates to "may it shine upon us." It is a prayer for enlightenment and guidance, seeking the light of knowledge and wisdom to illuminate our paths.
  • yenā naḥ pūrve pitaraḥ: This means "by which our ancestors." It acknowledges the wisdom and guidance of our forefathers, emphasizing the importance of heritage and tradition in shaping our lives.
  • padajñāḥ: This word means "those who know the path." It refers to enlightened beings or wise individuals who can guide us on our journey through life.
  • svarvido: This term translates to "those who know the heavens." It signifies the pursuit of higher knowledge and understanding of the universe.
  • abhi gā adrim uṣṇan: This phrase means "may they sing to us from the heights." It is a call for inspiration and support from higher realms, encouraging us to seek guidance from the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of growth, nourishment, and the pursuit of wisdom. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, the importance of seeking guidance from our ancestors and enlightened beings, and the need for purity and sweetness in our experiences. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, relationships, and spiritual practices. By nurturing ourselves and others, seeking knowledge, and honoring our heritage, we can create a fulfilling and enlightened life. This mantra serves as a reminder to strive for balance, wisdom, and connection in our daily lives.
