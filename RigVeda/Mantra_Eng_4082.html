अरहन्तो ये सुदानवो नरो असामिशवसः |प्र यज्ञं यज्ञियेब्यो दिवो आर्चा मरुद्भ्यः ||
arhanto ye sudānavo naro asāmiśavasaḥ | pra yajñaṁ yajñiyebhyo divo arcā marudbhyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • arhanto: worthy, deserving
    • ye: who
    • sudānavo: those who are well-versed in the sacred knowledge
    • naro: men, humans
    • asāmiśavasaḥ: those who are free from desires or attachments
    • pra: for, towards
    • yajñaṁ: sacrifice, offering
    • yajñiyebhyo: to those who are worthy of offerings
    • divo: of the heavens
    • arcā: worship, reverence
    • marudbhyaḥ: to the winds, the forces of nature
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of recognizing and honoring those who are deserving and knowledgeable.
    • It emphasizes the value of selflessness and the need to be free from desires in order to truly connect with the divine.
    • The act of offering (yajña) is highlighted as a means to express reverence towards the divine and the forces of nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, individuals who contribute selflessly, such as teachers or volunteers, embody the qualities of arhanto and sudānavo. Recognizing their efforts through community events or awards can strengthen social bonds.
    • Practicing mindfulness and detachment from material desires can lead to a more fulfilling life, as seen in various spiritual practices like meditation or yoga.
    • Participating in rituals or community service can be seen as a modern form of yajña, where individuals come together to support a cause, reflecting the essence of offering to the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of recognizing and honoring those who contribute positively to society.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate selflessness and a connection to the divine through acts of service and reverence.
    • In today's world, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger communities, personal growth, and a deeper understanding of our relationship with nature and the divine.
