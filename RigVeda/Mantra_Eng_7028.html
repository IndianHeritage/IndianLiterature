प्र वीरम् उग्रं विविचिं धनस्पृतम् विभूतिं राधसो महः |उद्रीव वज्रिन्न अवतो वसुत्वना सदा पीपेत दाशुषे ||
pra vīram ugraṁ viviciṁ dhanaspṛtaṁ vibhūtiṁ rādhaso mahaḥ | udrīva vajrinn avato vasutvanā sadā pīpetha dāśuṣe ||
  • pra vīram ugraṁ: This phrase can be translated as "to the mighty hero." The term "vīram" refers to a hero or a valiant person, while "ugraṁ" signifies strength or intensity. In real life, this can be related to individuals who display exceptional courage and bravery, such as firefighters or soldiers who risk their lives for others.
  • viviciṁ dhanaspṛtaṁ: This translates to "the one who is endowed with wealth." "Dhanaspṛtaṁ" refers to wealth or prosperity, and "viviciṁ" suggests discernment or clarity. This can be seen in entrepreneurs who, through their wisdom and discernment, create wealth not just for themselves but also for their communities.
  • vibhūtiṁ rādhaso mahaḥ: Here, "vibhūtiṁ" means glory or magnificence, while "mahaḥ" signifies greatness. "Rādhaso" can be interpreted as relating to Radha, a symbol of devotion and love. This can be exemplified in the lives of individuals who achieve greatness through their dedication and love for their work or community, such as philanthropists who contribute to social causes.
  • udrīva vajrinn: This phrase can be understood as "to the one who wields the thunderbolt." "Udrīva" suggests strength and power, while "vajrinn" refers to the thunderbolt, a symbol of indomitable strength. This can be likened to leaders who inspire and empower others, much like a coach who motivates a sports team to victory.
  • avato vasutvanā: This translates to "may he protect the wealth." "Avato" means to protect, and "vasutvanā" refers to wealth or resources. This can be related to the role of guardians or protectors in society, such as environmentalists who strive to protect natural resources for future generations.
  • sadā pīpetha dāśuṣe: This phrase means "may he always nourish the devotee." "Sadā" signifies always, "pīpetha" means to nourish, and "dāśuṣe" refers to the devotee or servant. This can be seen in the relationship between mentors and their mentees, where the mentor provides guidance and support to help the mentee grow and succeed.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, discernment, glory, protection, and nourishment in our lives. It signifies that true greatness comes from a combination of courage, wisdom, and devotion. In real-life scenarios, these qualities are essential for personal and communal growth, whether in leadership, entrepreneurship, mentorship, or social responsibility. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute positively to society and inspire others to do the same.
