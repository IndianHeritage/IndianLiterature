अस्मा उ ते महि महे विद्हेम नमोभिर अग्ने समिधोता हव्यैः |वेदी सूनो सहसो गीरभिर उक्थैर आ ते भद्रायां सुमतौ यतेमा ||
asmā u te mahi mahe vidhema namobhir agne samidhota havyaiḥ | vedī sūno sahaso gīrbhir ukthair ā te bhadrāyāṁ sumatau yatema ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmā: We
    • u: and
    • te: your
    • mahi: great
    • mahe: we make great
    • vidhema: we meditate
    • namobhir: with our salutations
    • agne: O Agni (Fire)
    • samidhota: you who are kindled
    • havyaiḥ: with offerings
    • vedī: the altar
    • sūno: of the son
    • sahaso: with strength
    • gīrbhir: with hymns
    • ukthair: with utterances
    • ā: to
    • te: your
    • bhadrāyām: auspiciousness
    • sumatau: with good thoughts
    • yatema: we may attain
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the Vedic fire god, symbolizing purity, transformation, and the divine messenger between humans and the gods.
    • The invocation begins with a collective acknowledgment of the divine, emphasizing unity and shared purpose among the worshippers.
    • By saying "we meditate," it highlights the importance of contemplation and focus in spiritual practices.
    • The phrase "with our salutations" indicates respect and reverence towards Agni, which is essential in any form of worship.
    • Agni is described as "kindled," representing the active and dynamic nature of fire, which is crucial in rituals and sacrifices.
    • The mention of "offerings" signifies the act of giving, which is a fundamental aspect of many spiritual traditions, reflecting gratitude and devotion.
    • The altar, referred to as "vedī," is a sacred space where rituals are performed, symbolizing the connection between the material and spiritual worlds.
    • The invocation of strength and hymns suggests that the worshippers seek empowerment and divine support through their prayers.
    • Finally, the mantra concludes with a desire for auspiciousness and good thoughts, indicating that the ultimate goal of this prayer is to attain a harmonious and prosperous life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during family gatherings or community events where collective intentions are set for well-being and harmony.
    • In personal development, the emphasis on meditation and good thoughts can inspire individuals to practice mindfulness and positive thinking, leading to improved mental health.
    • In environmental contexts, the reverence for fire can translate into a respect for nature and the elements, encouraging sustainable practices and a deeper connection to the earth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of community, respect for the divine, and the pursuit of auspiciousness in life.
    • Its teachings can be integrated into daily practices, fostering a sense of unity, gratitude, and mindfulness.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to seek a deeper connection with the divine and to cultivate positive thoughts and actions in their lives.
