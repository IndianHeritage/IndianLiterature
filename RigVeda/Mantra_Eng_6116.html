मन्त्र: उता नो दिव्या इषा उता सिंधूम् र अहर्विदा | अप द्वारेव वर्षथः ||
uta no divyā iṣa uta sindhūm̐r aharvidā | apa dvāreva varṣathaḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra translates to "May the divine powers (iṣa) come to us, may the rivers (sindhūm̐r) flow abundantly every day (aharvidā). May the waters (apa) rain down upon us from the doors (dvāreva) of the heavens."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • uta: A word that signifies a call or invocation.
    • no: Means "our," indicating a collective appeal.
    • divyā: Refers to the divine or celestial, suggesting a higher power.
    • iṣa: Denotes power or energy, often associated with blessings.
    • sindhūm̐r: Refers to rivers, symbolizing abundance and nourishment.
    • aharvidā: Means "every day," emphasizing the continuity of blessings.
    • apa: Refers to water, essential for life.
    • dvāreva: Means "from the doors," suggesting a source or origin.
    • varṣathaḥ: Implies the act of raining or showering, indicating abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain to ensure a good harvest. This mantra can be seen as a spiritual invocation for the divine forces to bless the land with water, which is crucial for crops.
    • In daily life, individuals may seek blessings for prosperity and abundance, akin to how this mantra calls for divine powers to flow into their lives, much like rivers that nourish the earth.
    • During festivals, communities come together to celebrate and invoke divine blessings for health, wealth, and happiness, reflecting the collective appeal of this mantra.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine blessings in our lives, particularly in the form of water, which is essential for survival and prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder of our dependence on nature and the divine for sustenance, encouraging gratitude and respect for the environment.
    • In a broader context, it can be applied in various aspects of life, such as seeking guidance in difficult times, fostering community spirit, and promoting environmental awareness.
