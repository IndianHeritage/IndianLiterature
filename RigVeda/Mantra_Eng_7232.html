ऐषु चेतद वृषाण्वत्य अन्तर ऋज्रेष्व अरुशी |स्वभिषुः काशावतिः ||
aiṣu cetad vṛṣaṇvaty antar ṛjreṣv aruṣī | svabhīśuḥ kaśāvatī ||
  • aiṣu cetad: This phrase can be interpreted as "if this is your thought" or "if this is your intention." It emphasizes the importance of one's mindset and intentions in shaping outcomes.
  • vṛṣaṇvaty: This word relates to strength or power. It signifies the qualities of a strong and capable individual, suggesting that one should embody these traits in their actions.
  • antar ṛjreṣv: This can be understood as "within the realms of righteousness" or "in the midst of truth." It highlights the significance of aligning one's actions with moral and ethical principles.
  • aruṣī: This term refers to the dawn or the beginning of something new. It symbolizes hope, renewal, and the potential for new beginnings.
  • svabhīśuḥ: This word means "in one's own nature" or "according to one's own qualities." It suggests that individuals should act in accordance with their true selves and inherent qualities.
  • kaśāvatī: This term can be interpreted as "like a strong and capable woman" or "possessing the qualities of a strong individual." It emphasizes the importance of strength and resilience in one's character.

In conclusion, this mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of intention, strength, righteousness, renewal, authenticity, and resilience. It encourages individuals to align their thoughts and actions with their true nature while embodying strength and moral integrity. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership, and ethical decision-making. For instance, a leader who embodies these qualities can inspire their team to work towards a common goal with integrity and strength, fostering a positive and productive environment. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of intention and the importance of living authentically and ethically in all aspects of life.
