उरुं नो लोकम् अनु नेषि विद्वान् स्वर्वज ज्योतिर अभयं स्वस्ति |ऋष्वा त इन्द्र स्थविरस्य बाहू उप स्थेयाम शरणा बृहन्ता ||
uruṁ no lokam anu neṣi vidvān svarvaj jyotir abhayaṁ svasti | ṛṣvā ta indra sthavirasya bāhū upa stheyāma śaraṇā bṛhantā ||
  • uruṁ no lokam anu neṣi vidvān svarvaj jyotir abhayaṁ svasti
    • uruṁ: This word can be interpreted as "great" or "vast." It signifies the expansive nature of the universe and the importance of seeking greater knowledge.
    • no: This means "our," indicating a collective identity or community.
    • lokam: Refers to "world" or "realm," suggesting the physical and spiritual dimensions of existence.
    • anu: This means "following" or "in accordance with," indicating a sense of alignment with higher principles.
    • neṣi: Translates to "guide" or "lead," emphasizing the role of wisdom in navigating life.
    • vidvān: This means "wise" or "knowledgeable," referring to those who possess deep understanding.
    • svarvaj: This term means "all-pervading light," symbolizing enlightenment and the pursuit of truth.
    • jyotir: Refers to "light," representing knowledge, clarity, and illumination in life.
    • abhayaṁ: This means "fearless," indicating a state of confidence and security in one's path.
    • svasti: Translates to "well-being" or "prosperity," highlighting the importance of health and harmony.
  • ṛṣvā ta indra sthavirasya bāhū upa stheyāma śaraṇā bṛhantā
    • ṛṣvā: This word means "strong" or "powerful," indicating the strength required to face challenges.
    • ta: This means "that," referring to the divine or higher power.
    • indra: Refers to the king of gods, symbolizing authority, strength, and protection.
    • sthavirasya: This means "of the strong," emphasizing the importance of resilience and stability.
    • bāhū: Refers to "arms," symbolizing support and protection from higher powers.
    • upa: This means "near" or "close," indicating the proximity of divine assistance.
    • stheyāma: This translates to "we may stand," suggesting the need for strength and support in difficult times.
    • śaraṇā: Means "shelter" or "refuge," indicating the importance of seeking safety and guidance.
    • bṛhantā: This means "great" or "vast," reinforcing the idea of seeking a higher purpose and strength.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking wisdom and guidance in our lives. It encourages individuals to pursue knowledge and enlightenment while fostering a sense of community and collective well-being. The invocation of divine strength and protection signifies the need for resilience in the face of challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal development, where individuals strive for knowledge and self-improvement.
  • In community building, where collective efforts lead to shared prosperity and well-being.
  • In times of crisis, where seeking divine guidance and strength can provide comfort and support.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, strength, and community in achieving a fulfilling and prosperous life.
