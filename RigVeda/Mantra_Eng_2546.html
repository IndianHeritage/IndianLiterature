तद्भद्रं तव दंशना पाकाय चिच्छदयति |त्वां यद अग्ने पशवः समासते समिद्धम् अपिशर्वरे ||
tad bhadraṁ tava daṁsanā pākāya cic chadayati | tvāṁ yad agne paśavaḥ samāsate samiddham apiśarvare ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • bhadraṁ: auspicious, good
    • tava: your
    • daṁsanā: by the bite or the action of biting
    • pākāya: to the pure, to the sacred
    • cic: indeed
    • chadayati: covers, protects
    • tvāṁ: you
    • yad: when
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • paśavaḥ: the animals, creatures
    • samāsate: gather, assemble
    • samiddham: well-kindled, fully ignited
    • apiśarvare: in the great, vast (contextually refers to the universe or the cosmos)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, emphasizing the auspiciousness and purity associated with his presence.
    • The phrase "tad bhadraṁ" suggests a desire for good fortune and blessings, indicating that the speaker seeks protection and auspiciousness in their life.
    • The mention of "daṁsanā" implies a transformative power, where the act of biting or consuming can lead to purification and sanctification.
    • When it states "tvāṁ yad agne paśavaḥ samāsate," it highlights the gathering of creatures around Agni, symbolizing unity and the importance of fire in sustaining life.
    • The term "samiddham apiśarvare" reflects the idea of a well-kindled fire, which can be seen as a metaphor for enlightenment, knowledge, and the energy that fuels existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is a symbol of purification. For instance, in Hindu rituals, offerings are made to Agni, signifying the purification of intentions and the seeking of blessings.
    • In a modern context, the mantra can be related to the importance of community and gathering for a common purpose, much like how people come together for celebrations or communal activities around a bonfire.
    • The idea of transformation through the act of consuming (daṁsanā) can be likened to personal growth experiences, where challenges (biting) lead to a more refined and pure self.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking auspiciousness and purity in our lives through the divine presence of Agni.
    • It teaches us about the transformative power of challenges and the necessity of community in achieving collective goals.
    • In real life, it encourages us to embrace the purifying aspects of our experiences and to gather together in unity for a greater purpose, reflecting the interconnectedness of all beings.
