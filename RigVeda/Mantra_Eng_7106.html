मन्त्र: नहि ते अग्नि वृषभ प्रतिधृषे जम्बासो यद विटिष्ठसे |स त्वं नो होतः सुहुतं हविष कृत् धि वंस्वा नो वार्याऽपुरु ||
nahi te agne vṛṣabha pratidhṛṣe jambhāso yad vitiṣṭhase | sa tvaṁ no hotaḥ suhutaṁ haviṣ kṛdhi vaṁsvā no vāryā puru ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nahi: not
    • te: your
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • vṛṣabha: the strong one or the bull
    • pratidhṛṣe: in the presence of
    • jambhāso: of the great ones
    • yat: that which
    • vitiṣṭhase: you uphold
    • sa: he
    • tvaṁ: you
    • no: for us
    • hotaḥ: O priest or invoker
    • suhutaṁ: well-invoked
    • haviṣ: offerings
    • kṛdhi: make
    • vaṁsvā: for our benefit
    • no: for us
    • vāryā: protectors
    • puru: many or numerous
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The speaker acknowledges Agni's strength and presence, asking for his blessings and protection.
    • It emphasizes the importance of invoking Agni properly to ensure that offerings are accepted and blessings are bestowed.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, Agni is invoked during ceremonies such as weddings and homas (fire sacrifices) to ensure the presence of divine energy.
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of intention and respect in any form of communication or offering, whether in personal relationships or professional settings.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support and the recognition of the power of Agni as a facilitator of connection between the earthly and the divine.
    • It teaches us the value of respect, intention, and the need for guidance in our endeavors, applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to community building.
    • By invoking the right energies and maintaining a respectful approach, we can create a harmonious environment conducive to growth and protection.
