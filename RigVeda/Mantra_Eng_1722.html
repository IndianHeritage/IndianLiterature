इह ब्रवीतु य इम अङ्ग वेदास्य वामस्य निहितं पदं वेः |शीर्ष्णः क्षीरं दुह्रते गावो अस्य ववृं वसाना उदकम् पदापुः ||
iha bravītu ya īm aṅga vedāsya vāmasya nihitam padaṁ veḥ | śīrṣṇaḥ kṣīraṁ duhrate gāvo asya vavriṁ vasānā udakam padāpuḥ ||
  • Mantra: "iha bravītu ya īm aṅga vedāsya vāmasya nihitam padaṁ veḥ |
  • Translation: "Here, let them speak, those who know the essence of the Veda, the hidden path of the Vama."
  • Explanation:
    • iha bravītu: "Here, let them speak" suggests a call for knowledge and wisdom to be shared.
    • ya īm aṅga: "those who know the essence" refers to individuals who have deep understanding and insight.
    • vedāsya vāmasya: "of the Veda, the hidden path" indicates the profound and often esoteric knowledge contained within the Vedas.
    • nihitam padaṁ veḥ: "the hidden path" emphasizes the importance of uncovering deeper truths and meanings.
  • Next Part: "śīrṣṇaḥ kṣīraṁ duhrate gāvo asya vavriṁ vasānā udakam padāpuḥ ||"
  • Translation: "The cows, drawing milk from the udder, are the ones who bring forth the waters of this knowledge."
  • Explanation:
    • śīrṣṇaḥ: "from the head" symbolizes the source of wisdom and knowledge.
    • kṣīraṁ duhrate: "drawing milk" represents the nurturing aspect of knowledge, akin to how milk nourishes life.
    • gāvo asya: "the cows" can be seen as a metaphor for those who seek and share knowledge.
    • vavriṁ vasānā: "bring forth the waters" signifies the flow of wisdom and understanding that nourishes the mind and spirit.
    • udakam padāpuḥ: "the waters of this knowledge" highlights the essential nature of knowledge in sustaining life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of sharing knowledge and wisdom, particularly the deeper truths found in sacred texts like the Vedas.
    • In real life, this can be applied in educational settings, where teachers and mentors are encouraged to share their insights and experiences with students.
    • It also highlights the nurturing aspect of knowledge, akin to how a mother nourishes her child, suggesting that wisdom should be shared generously to foster growth and understanding.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that knowledge is a vital resource, much like water, that sustains and enriches our lives.
