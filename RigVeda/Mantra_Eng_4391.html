वेṣi ह्यध्वरियताम् अग्ने होतā दमे विश्वाम् |समृद्धो विश्वते कृणु जुषस्व हव्यं अंगिरः ||
veṣi hy adhvarīyatām agne hotā dame viśām | samṛdho viśpate kṛṇu juṣasva havyam aṅgiraḥ ||
  • veṣi hy adhvarīyatām: This phrase can be translated as "O Agni, you who are the master of the sacrifice." Here, Agni represents the divine fire and is often seen as a mediator between humans and the gods. In real life, this can be likened to the role of a leader or a facilitator in any community or organization, guiding the process and ensuring that the objectives are met.
  • agne hotā dame viśām: This translates to "O Agni, you who are the priest of the community." The term "hotā" refers to the priest who performs the rituals. In a modern context, this can be compared to someone who brings people together for a common cause, such as a community organizer or a spiritual leader who fosters unity and purpose among individuals.
  • samṛdho viśpate kṛṇu: This means "make us prosperous, O Lord of the Universe." The word "samṛdho" signifies prosperity and abundance. In everyday life, this can be related to the pursuit of success and well-being, whether in personal finances, health, or relationships. It emphasizes the importance of seeking blessings for growth and prosperity in various aspects of life.
  • juṣasva havyam aṅgiraḥ: This translates to "enjoy the offerings, O Angira." "Havyam" refers to the offerings made to the divine. In practical terms, this can be seen as the importance of gratitude and recognition of the efforts and contributions of others. It highlights the need to appreciate what we receive and to share our blessings with the community.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a central figure in rituals and community life. It reflects the values of leadership, unity, prosperity, and gratitude. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as in workplaces where leaders inspire and guide their teams, in communities where individuals come together for common goals, and in personal lives where one seeks to cultivate abundance and express gratitude. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine, encouraging us to foster relationships that promote growth and harmony.
