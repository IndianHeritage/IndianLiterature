यदि क्षितायुर यदि वा परेतो यदि मृत्युर्वन्तिकं नीत एव |तम आ हरामि निरृतेर उपस्थाद् अस्पर्शम् एनं शतशारदाय ||
yadi kṣitāyur yadi vā pareto yadi mṛtyor antikaṁ nīta eva | tam ā harāmi nirṛter upasthād aspārṣam enaṁ śataśāradāya ||
  • Mantra: "yadi kṣitāyur yadi vā pareto yadi mṛtyor antikaṁ nīta eva | tam ā harāmi nirṛter upasthād aspārṣam enaṁ śataśāradāya ||"
  • Translation: "If I am to be taken by the earth, or if I am to be taken by the other world, or if I am to be taken close to death, I bring that (life) to me, untouched by death, for a hundred autumns."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yadi: if
    • kṣitāyur: by the earth (referring to life on earth)
    • pareto: by the other world (referring to the afterlife)
    • mṛtyor: of death
    • antikaṁ: close to
    • nīta: taken
    • tam: that (referring to life)
    • ā harāmi: I bring
    • nirṛter: of death or destruction
    • upasthād: presence
    • aspārṣam: untouched
    • enaṁ: this (referring to life)
    • śataśāradāya: for a hundred autumns (symbolizing longevity)
  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra reflects a deep understanding of life, death, and the desire for longevity.
    • The speaker expresses a wish to be free from the grasp of death, whether it be from earthly life or the afterlife.
    • It emphasizes the importance of life and the desire to live a long, fulfilling life, untouched by the fears of mortality.
    • In real life, this can be related to the human instinct to seek health, wellness, and longevity through various means such as diet, exercise, and spiritual practices.
    • For example, people often engage in yoga, meditation, and healthy eating to prolong their lives and maintain vitality.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the fragility of life and the inevitability of death.
    • It encourages individuals to cherish their lives and strive for longevity while remaining untouched by the fear of death.
    • In a broader context, it can be applied to various aspects of life, including health, relationships, and personal growth.
    • Ultimately, it signifies the human aspiration for a meaningful existence, free from the shadows of mortality, and encourages a proactive approach to living well.
