व्रातं-व्रातं गणं-गणं सुशस्तिभिर अग्नेर भामं मरुताम ओज इमहे |पृषदश्वासो अनवभ्रराधसो गन्तारो यज्ञं विदथेṣu धीराः ||
vrātaṁ-vrātaṁ gaṇaṁ-gaṇaṁ suśastibhir agner bhāmam marutām oja īmahe | pṛṣadaśvāso anavabhrarādhaso gantāro yajñaṁ vidatheṣu dhīrāḥ ||
  • vrātaṁ-vrātaṁ: This term refers to a group or a community, often associated with a collective effort or unity. In real life, this can be likened to a team working together towards a common goal, such as a sports team or a project group in a workplace.
  • gaṇaṁ-gaṇaṁ: Similar to "vrātaṁ," this emphasizes the importance of gathering and collaboration. For instance, in a community service project, individuals come together to make a significant impact, showcasing the power of collective action.
  • suśastibhir: This word means "with good intentions" or "well-structured." It highlights the importance of having a positive mindset and clear objectives when working together. In a business context, this could refer to a well-planned strategy that leads to successful outcomes.
  • agner: Referring to "Agni," the fire god, this symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way we approach challenges, using our inner strength to overcome obstacles.
  • bhāmam: This term can be interpreted as "brilliance" or "radiance." It signifies the importance of shining in one's endeavors, much like a student excelling in academics or an artist showcasing their talent.
  • marutām: Referring to the Maruts, the storm deities, this represents the dynamic forces of nature. In real life, this can be related to the unpredictable challenges we face, akin to a sudden storm that tests our resilience.
  • oja: This word means "strength" or "vitality." It emphasizes the need for physical and mental strength in our pursuits, similar to athletes who train rigorously to achieve their goals.
  • īmahe: This term translates to "we invoke" or "we seek." It reflects the act of calling upon higher powers or energies for support, much like individuals seeking guidance through meditation or prayer during difficult times.
  • pṛṣadaśvāso: This can be interpreted as "those who are swift and agile." It highlights the importance of adaptability and quick thinking in various situations, such as a business leader making rapid decisions in a crisis.
  • anavabhrarādhaso: This term suggests "those who are unyielding" or "steadfast." It signifies the importance of perseverance, akin to a student who continues to study despite facing challenges.
  • gantāro: Meaning "those who move forward," this emphasizes progress and the journey towards goals. In real life, this can be seen in individuals pursuing their dreams, regardless of the obstacles they encounter.
  • yajñaṁ: This refers to a sacrifice or offering, often in the context of rituals. In modern terms, it can symbolize the sacrifices we make for our loved ones or for a greater cause, such as volunteering for a charity.
  • vidatheṣu: This term means "in the assemblies" or "in gatherings." It highlights the importance of community and collaboration in achieving success, similar to how brainstorming sessions can lead to innovative ideas.
  • dhīrāḥ: This word translates to "wise" or "intelligent." It signifies the value of wisdom and thoughtful decision-making in our actions, much like a mentor guiding a young professional in their career.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, collaboration, and strength in our endeavors. It teaches us that by coming together with good intentions and wisdom, we can overcome challenges and achieve our goals. In real life, this mantra is applicable in various scenarios, such as teamwork in professional settings, community service, and personal growth. It serves as a reminder that collective effort, resilience, and a positive mindset are essential for success in any aspect of life.
