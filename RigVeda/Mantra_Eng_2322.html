राकाम अहं सुहवां सुश्रुतī हुवे श्रिणोतु नः सुभगा बोधतु तमनā |सिव्यत्व अपः सूच्याच्छिद्यमानया ददातु वीरं शतदायम उक्त्यम् ||
rākām ahaṁ suhavāṁ suṣṭutī huve śṛṇotu naḥ subhagā bodhatu tmanā | sīvyatv apaḥ sūcyācchidyamānayā dadātu vīraṁ śatadāyam ukthyam ||
  • rākām ahaṁ: This phrase translates to "I am the one who desires." It signifies the importance of intention and desire in our lives. For example, when someone aspires to achieve a goal, such as completing a marathon, their desire fuels their training and commitment.
  • suhavāṁ: This means "pleasant" or "beautiful." It reflects the idea that our desires should lead us to experiences that are enriching and fulfilling. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of a career that brings joy and satisfaction, rather than just financial gain.
  • suṣṭutī huve: This translates to "I invoke the well-spoken praise." It emphasizes the power of words and positive affirmations. For instance, in a workplace, recognizing and praising colleagues can foster a supportive environment and enhance productivity.
  • śṛṇotu naḥ: This means "may it be heard by us." It suggests a call for attention and understanding from the universe or higher powers. In everyday life, this can relate to the importance of being open to guidance and wisdom from mentors or experiences.
  • subhagā bodhatu tmanā: This translates to "may the auspiciousness enlighten our minds." It highlights the significance of wisdom and enlightenment in our pursuits. For example, education and self-reflection can lead to better decision-making in personal and professional life.
  • sīvyatv apaḥ: This phrase means "may the waters be sown." It symbolizes the nurturing aspect of life, akin to planting seeds for future growth. In real life, this can be seen in community service or environmental conservation efforts, where individuals contribute to the well-being of society and nature.
  • sūcyācchidyamānayā: This translates to "may the obstacles be removed." It reflects the desire for overcoming challenges. For instance, in a student's journey, facing and overcoming academic challenges can lead to personal growth and success.
  • dadātu vīraṁ: This means "may it grant us strength." It emphasizes the need for resilience and courage in our endeavors. In real life, athletes often draw on their inner strength to push through difficult training sessions or competitions.
  • śatadāyam ukthyam: This translates to "the hundredfold offering." It signifies abundance and the idea that our efforts can yield great rewards. For example, investing time in relationships can lead to a rich and fulfilling social life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of desire, intention, and the pursuit of wisdom and strength in our lives. It serves as a reminder that our aspirations should be aligned with positive outcomes and that we should seek enlightenment and support in our journeys. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal development, professional growth, and community engagement, encouraging individuals to cultivate their desires in a way that benefits themselves and those around them.
