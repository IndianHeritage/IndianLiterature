एव हि ते विभूतय ūtय इन्द्र मावते |सद्यश्चित्सन्ति दाशुषे ||
evā hi te vibhūtaya ūtaya indra māvate | sadyaś cit santi dāśuṣe ||
  • evā hi te vibhūtaya: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, your glories" or "Truly, your manifestations." Here, "vibhūtaya" refers to the divine manifestations or powers of a deity, in this case, Indra, who is often associated with strength and rain.
  • ūtaya indra māvate: This part translates to "O Indra, may you come quickly." The word "ūtaya" implies a call for action or a request for the deity to arrive or assist. "Māvate" indicates a sense of urgency in the plea for help.
  • sadyaś cit santi dāśuṣe: This can be interpreted as "May there be immediate blessings for the devotee." "Sadyaś" means immediate or instant, "cit" refers to consciousness or awareness, and "dāśuṣe" means for the devotee or servant. This line emphasizes the desire for swift divine intervention and blessings.

  • Significance: This mantra highlights the relationship between the devotee and the divine, particularly the need for divine assistance in times of need. It reflects the belief in the power of prayer and the importance of invoking the divine for support and blessings.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, individuals often face challenges that require immediate solutions, whether they are personal, professional, or spiritual. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek help from higher powers during difficult times. For example, someone facing a critical decision at work may recite this mantra to invoke clarity and guidance from the divine.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion and the belief in the efficacy of prayer. It encourages individuals to reach out for divine support, reinforcing the idea that one is not alone in their struggles. By acknowledging the presence of divine powers and seeking their assistance, individuals can find strength and reassurance in their journey through life.
