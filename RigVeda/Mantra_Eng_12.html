वायो तव प्रपृñचती धेनā जिगाति दाशुषे |उरूचī सोमपीतये ||
vāyo tava prapṛñcatī dhenā jigāti dāśuṣe | urūcī somapītaye ||
  • vāyo: This word refers to "Vayu," the wind god in Hindu mythology. Vayu is often associated with the breath of life and the vital force that sustains all living beings.
  • tava: This means "your," indicating possession or connection to Vayu.
  • prapṛñcatī: This term can be translated as "spreading" or "expanding," suggesting the vastness and omnipresence of the wind.
  • dhenā: This word means "nourishing" or "sustaining," often used in the context of a cow that provides milk, symbolizing abundance and sustenance.
  • jigāti: This translates to "moves" or "flows," indicating the dynamic nature of the wind and its ability to traverse great distances.
  • dāśuṣe: This term refers to "the giver" or "the one who bestows," emphasizing the benevolent aspect of Vayu as a provider of life and resources.
  • urūcī: This means "great" or "vast," often used to describe something that is expansive and powerful.
  • somapītaye: This term can be broken down into "soma," which refers to a sacred drink or elixir, and "pītaye," meaning "for drinking." It signifies the divine and nourishing qualities associated with the wind and its connection to life-giving substances.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Vayu, acknowledging his role as a vital force that nourishes and sustains life. It highlights the importance of the wind in the natural world, as well as its symbolic representation of abundance and vitality.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Environmental Awareness: Understanding the significance of wind and air quality in our ecosystem can lead to better environmental practices. For example, promoting clean air initiatives and reducing pollution can be seen as a way to honor Vayu.
  • Health and Well-being: The breath is essential for life. Practices such as yoga and meditation emphasize the importance of breath control (pranayama) for physical and mental health, aligning with the essence of Vayu.
  • Spiritual Connection: Many cultures view the wind as a messenger of the divine. Engaging in rituals that honor nature can foster a deeper spiritual connection and appreciation for the forces that sustain us.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the vital role that natural elements, like wind, play in our existence. By recognizing and respecting these forces, we can cultivate a more harmonious relationship with the environment and enhance our overall well-being.
