कुमारम् माता युवतिः समुब्दं गुहा बिभर्ति न ददाति पित्रे |अनीकम् अस्य न मिनज जनासः पुरः पश्यन्ति निहितम् अरतौ ||
kumāram mātā yuvatiḥ samubdhaṁ guhā bibharti na dadāti pitre | anīkam asya na minaj janāsaḥ puraḥ paśyanti nihitam aratau ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kumāram: young one or child
    • mātā: mother
    • yuvatiḥ: young woman or maiden
    • samubdhaṁ: well-formed or well-nurtured
    • guhā: cave or hidden place
    • bibharti: holds or contains
    • na: not
    • dadāti: gives
    • pitre: to the father
    • anīkam: group or assembly
    • asya: of this
    • na: not
    • minaj: to diminish or reduce
    • janāsaḥ: people or beings
    • puraḥ: in front or before
    • paśyanti: see or perceive
    • nihitam: hidden or concealed
    • aratau: in the chariot or vehicle
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nurturing aspect of a mother and the protective nature of youth.
    • The "young one" (kumāram) is held by the "mother" (mātā) and the "young woman" (yuvatiḥ) symbolizes the nurturing qualities of femininity.
    • The "hidden cave" (guhā) represents the inner sanctum of safety and protection, where the young are nurtured.
    • The phrase "na dadāti pitre" suggests that the nurturing and protective qualities of the mother are not given away to the father, indicating a unique bond between mother and child.
    • The "group" (anīkam) and "people" (janāsaḥ) signify the community or society that observes the nurturing process, yet they do not diminish the essence of the child.
    • The "hidden" (nihitam) aspect emphasizes the unseen efforts and love that go into raising a child, which may not always be visible to the outside world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the bond between a mother and her child is revered, showcasing the protective and nurturing role of mothers in raising their children.
    • Consider a single mother working multiple jobs to provide for her child, embodying the essence of the mantra where the nurturing is not shared but solely her responsibility.
    • In a community setting, the collective observation of a child's growth and development reflects the societal role in nurturing, yet the unique bond between mother and child remains paramount.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of maternal love and the protective nature of youth, emphasizing the unique bond that exists between a mother and her child.
    • It serves as a reminder of the unseen efforts and sacrifices made by mothers in nurturing their children, which may often go unnoticed by society.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to appreciate and acknowledge the vital role of mothers and caregivers in the upbringing of future generations.
