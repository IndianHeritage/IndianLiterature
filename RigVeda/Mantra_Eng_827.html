रयिर न यः पितृवित्तो वयोधाः सुप्रणीतिश चिकीषो न शासुः |स्योनशीर अतिथिर न प्रीणानो होतेव सद्म विधतो वि तारीत ||
rayir na yaḥ pitṛvitto vayodhāḥ supraṇītiś cikituṣo na śāsuḥ | syonaśīr atithir na prīṇāno hoteva sadma vidhato vi tārīt ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "Those who are endowed with the wealth of their ancestors, who are wise and knowledgeable, do not suffer from misfortune. The guest who is welcomed with joy is like a deity, and the one who provides a home is a true benefactor."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • rayir: wealth or riches
    • na: not
    • yaḥ: who
    • pitṛvitto: endowed with ancestral wealth
    • vayodhāḥ: wise or knowledgeable
    • supraṇītiś: good governance or wise conduct
    • cikituṣo: those who are knowledgeable
    • na: not
    • śāsuḥ: suffer or experience misfortune
    • syonaśīr: the guest
    • atithir: guest or visitor
    • na: not
    • prīṇāno: welcomed or pleased
    • hoteva: like a deity or divine being
    • sadma: home or dwelling
    • vidhato: the one who provides
    • vi: indeed
    • tārīt: benefactor or helper
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the concept of honoring one's ancestors is prevalent. For instance, during festivals like Diwali in India, families remember and pay homage to their ancestors, which strengthens familial bonds and instills a sense of identity.
    • In a workplace, a leader who is knowledgeable and wise can guide their team effectively, ensuring that they do not face misfortunes in their projects. This reflects the idea of 'supraṇītiś' or good governance.
    • Welcoming guests with joy is a common practice in many cultures. For example, in Middle Eastern cultures, hospitality is a significant value, where guests are treated with utmost respect and generosity, akin to treating them as deities.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the importance of wisdom, ancestral wealth, and hospitality. It teaches us that those who are knowledgeable and honor their heritage are less likely to face misfortunes. Furthermore, it highlights the value of treating guests with respect and joy, which fosters community and strengthens relationships. In real life, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as family gatherings, workplace dynamics, and cultural practices, promoting a harmonious and prosperous society.
