नहि मे रोडसी उभे अन्यं पक्षं चन प्रति |कुवित सोमस्यापाम इति ||
nahi me rodasī ubhe anyam pakṣaṁ cana prati | kuvit somasyāpām iti ||
  • Translation: "I do not see any other wing than this one; this is the way of the wise."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • nahi: not
    • me: my
    • rodasī: the two worlds (heavens and earth)
    • ubhe: both
    • anyam: other
    • pakṣaṁ: wing
    • cana: at all
    • prati: towards
    • kuvit: wise or knowledgeable
    • somasyāpām: the way of the wise
    • iti: thus
  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the idea of singularity in perspective and wisdom. The speaker asserts that there is no other path or option available to them, highlighting a commitment to a chosen way of life or understanding.
    • The metaphor of "wings" suggests freedom and the ability to soar, but it also implies that one must choose a specific direction or path to truly achieve that freedom.
    • In real life, this can be likened to making a significant life choice, such as pursuing a particular career or educational path. For instance, a student may decide to focus solely on medicine, recognizing that this is the path they wish to take, despite other options being available.
    • Moreover, it can also reflect the importance of dedication and focus in achieving one's goals. Just as a bird cannot fly in two directions at once, individuals must commit to their chosen path to succeed.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for clarity and commitment in one's life choices. It encourages individuals to recognize the importance of focusing on a single path to achieve wisdom and success.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as career decisions, personal relationships, and spiritual journeys. By understanding that one must choose a direction and commit to it, individuals can navigate their lives with greater purpose and intention.
    • Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder that while options may be plentiful, true wisdom and fulfillment come from choosing a path and dedicating oneself to it wholeheartedly.
