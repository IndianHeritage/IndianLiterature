स्वादुः पवस्व दिव्याय जनमने स्वादुर इन्द्राय सुहवीतुनामने |स्वादुर मित्राय वरुणाय वायवे बृहस्पतये मधुमाम् अदाभ्यः ||
svāduḥ pavasva divyāya janmane svādur indrāya suhavītunāmne | svādur mitrāya varuṇāya vāyave bṛhaspataye madhumām̐ adābhyaḥ ||
  • svāduḥ pavasva: This phrase translates to "may the sweet (or pleasant) one blow." It signifies the desire for positive and beneficial energies to flow into our lives. In real life, this can be related to the importance of surrounding ourselves with positivity, whether through relationships, environments, or experiences.
  • divyāya janmane: This means "for the divine birth." It emphasizes the connection to the divine or higher consciousness. In practical terms, it can be seen in the way individuals strive for spiritual growth and enlightenment, seeking to connect with something greater than themselves.
  • svādur indrāya: This translates to "sweet for Indra." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, often associated with rain and thunderstorms. This line suggests a plea for blessings from the divine, akin to how people today seek guidance or support from higher powers during challenging times.
  • suha vītunāmne: This means "for the pleasant name." It reflects the importance of reputation and how one's name or legacy can impact their life. In modern society, this can relate to personal branding and the significance of how we are perceived by others.
  • svādur mitrāya: This translates to "sweet for Mitra." Mitra is associated with friendship and harmony. This line highlights the value of friendship and community in our lives, reminding us of the importance of nurturing relationships that bring joy and support.
  • varuṇāya vāyave: This means "for Varuna and Vayu." Varuna is the god of water and cosmic order, while Vayu is the god of wind. Together, they symbolize the balance of nature. In real life, this can be interpreted as a reminder to respect and maintain harmony with the environment and the natural world.
  • bṛhaspataye: This translates to "to Brihaspati," the guru of the gods and a symbol of wisdom and knowledge. This line emphasizes the pursuit of wisdom and learning, which is essential in our personal and professional lives. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and guidance from mentors.
  • madhumām̐ adābhyaḥ: This means "may I receive sweetness." It signifies a desire for abundance and joy in life. In practical terms, it can relate to the pursuit of happiness and fulfillment, encouraging individuals to seek out experiences that bring them joy and satisfaction.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for positivity, divine blessings, wisdom, and harmonious relationships. Its significance lies in the holistic approach to life, emphasizing the importance of spiritual growth, community, and respect for nature. In real life, this mantra can be applied by fostering positive relationships, seeking knowledge, and striving for a balanced and fulfilling life. It serves as a reminder to cultivate sweetness in our experiences and interactions, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
