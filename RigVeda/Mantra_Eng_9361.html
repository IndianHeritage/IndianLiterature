वाज्य असि वाजिनेन सुवेनीः सुवितः स्तोमं सुवितो दिवं गाः |सुवितो धर्म प्रथमाणु सत्याऽ सुवितो देवान सुवितोऽनु पत्म ||
vājy asi vājinenā suvenīḥ suvitaḥ stomaṁ suvito divaṁ gāḥ | suvito dharma prathamānu satyā suvito devān suvito 'nu patma ||
  • vājy asi: This phrase translates to "You are the one who is worthy of offerings." It signifies the importance of recognizing and honoring the divine or the forces of nature that provide sustenance and support in our lives.
  • vājinenā: This means "with strength" or "with vigor." It emphasizes the need for strength in our endeavors, whether they are spiritual, personal, or communal.
  • suvenīḥ: This translates to "good and auspicious." It highlights the importance of positive intentions and actions in our lives, suggesting that we should strive for goodness in all our pursuits.
  • suvitaḥ: Meaning "well-formed" or "well-made," this word suggests that our actions and creations should be of high quality and integrity, reflecting our values and beliefs.
  • stomaṁ: This refers to "praise" or "hymn." It indicates the significance of expressing gratitude and reverence, whether through words, actions, or rituals.
  • suvito divaṁ gāḥ: This phrase means "the well-formed songs of the heavens." It suggests that there is a connection between our earthly actions and the divine, and that our praises can resonate with higher powers.
  • suvito dharma: This translates to "well-formed righteousness." It emphasizes the importance of living a life aligned with dharma, or moral duty, which is essential for personal and societal harmony.
  • prathamānu satyā: This means "the first truth." It signifies the foundational truths that guide our lives and decisions, reminding us to seek authenticity and honesty in our actions.
  • suvito devān: This translates to "well-formed deities." It suggests that our understanding and relationship with the divine should be nurtured and developed with care and respect.
  • suvito 'nu patma: This means "following the well-formed path." It encourages us to pursue a righteous and meaningful path in life, guided by the principles of goodness and truth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of living a life of purpose, strength, and righteousness. It serves as a reminder to honor the divine, pursue goodness, and align our actions with truth and moral duty. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal development, community service, and spiritual practices. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and fulfilling existence, both for themselves and for those around them.
