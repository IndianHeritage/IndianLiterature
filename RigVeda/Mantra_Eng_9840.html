ते हि dyāvāpṛthivī भूरिरेतसā नराशंसश चतुरङ्गो यमो 'दितिः |देवस् त्वष्टा द्रविणोदā ऋभुक्षणः प्र रोदसी मरुतो विष्णुर अरिहिरे ||
te hi dyāvāpṛthivī bhūriretasā narāśaṁsaś caturaṅgo yamo 'ditiḥ | devas tvaṣṭā draviṇodā ṛbhukṣaṇaḥ pra rodasī maruto viṣṇur arhire ||
  • te hi dyāvāpṛthivī: This phrase translates to "Indeed, the heavens and the earth." It signifies the duality and unity of the cosmos, representing the physical and spiritual realms. In real life, this can be seen in the balance we seek between our material needs and spiritual growth.
  • bhūriretasā: This means "with abundant energy." It emphasizes the importance of vitality and strength in our lives. For example, athletes train rigorously to harness their energy for peak performance, reflecting the essence of this phrase.
  • narāśaṁsaś caturaṅgo: This translates to "the four-angled man." It symbolizes completeness and balance in human nature, suggesting that we should strive for a well-rounded character. In modern life, this can be related to the importance of developing multiple skills and attributes, such as emotional intelligence, physical health, intellectual growth, and social skills.
  • yamo 'ditiḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "Yama, the restraint." Yama represents self-control and discipline, which are crucial for personal development. For instance, individuals practicing mindfulness or meditation often emphasize the importance of restraint in thoughts and actions to achieve inner peace.
  • devas tvaṣṭā: This means "the divine craftsman." It refers to the creative force in the universe, akin to the idea of a higher power or inspiration in our lives. Artists, writers, and innovators often draw upon this divine inspiration to create their works, reflecting the essence of this phrase.
  • draviṇodā: This translates to "the giver of wealth." It signifies abundance and prosperity. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of generosity and sharing wealth, as seen in philanthropic efforts by successful individuals who aim to uplift society.
  • ṛbhukṣaṇaḥ: This means "the one who nourishes." It highlights the importance of sustenance and care in our lives. For example, caregivers and parents embody this principle by providing for their families and communities, ensuring that everyone is nurtured and supported.
  • pra rodasī: This translates to "the vast expanse." It signifies the limitless potential of the universe and our aspirations. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of dreams and goals, encouraging individuals to reach for the stars and explore their full potential.
  • maruto viṣṇur arhire: This phrase refers to "the winds, Vishnu, and the divine." It symbolizes the interconnectedness of nature and divinity. In our daily lives, this can be seen in the respect we should have for nature and the environment, recognizing that our actions impact the world around us.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of balance, energy, self-discipline, creativity, abundance, nourishment, limitless potential, and interconnectedness. Its significance lies in guiding individuals towards a holistic approach to life, encouraging them to cultivate their physical, emotional, and spiritual well-being. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in personal development, community service, environmental stewardship, and the pursuit of one's passions, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
