आर्चन्न अत्र मरुतः सस्मिन्न आजौ विश्वे देवासो अमदन्न अनु त्वा |वृत्रस्य यद भ्र्ष्टिमताऽवधेन न त्वं इन्द्र प्रत्य आनाṁ जगहन्थ ||
ārcann atra marutaḥ sasminn ājau viśve devāso amadann anu tvā | vṛtrasya yad bhṛṣṭimatā vadhena ni tvam indra praty ānaṁ jaghantha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ārcann: Worshipping or invoking
    • atra: Here
    • marutaḥ: The Maruts, the storm deities
    • sasminn: In this
    • ājau: Powerful or strong
    • viśve: All or universal
    • devāso: Gods or divine beings
    • amadann: They have given immortality or nectar
    • anu: After or following
    • tvā: You
    • vṛtrasya: Of Vritra, the demon of drought
    • yad: That which
    • bhṛṣṭimatā: With great strength or power
    • vadhena: By killing or slaying
    • ni: Not
    • tvam: You
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • praty: Towards or against
    • ānām: The one who is coming
    • jaghantha: To strike down or defeat
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer invoking the Maruts, the storm gods, to come and bless the worshippers with their powerful presence.
    • It acknowledges the divine beings who provide immortality and sustenance, emphasizing the importance of divine support in overcoming challenges.
    • The reference to Vritra symbolizes obstacles or adversities that one may face in life, and the mantra calls upon Indra to help defeat these challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal struggle, such as facing a health crisis or financial difficulties, individuals may invoke divine support through prayer or meditation, similar to how the mantra calls upon the Maruts and Indra.
    • In a community setting, when facing natural disasters like floods or droughts, people may come together to perform rituals or prayers, seeking the blessings of the gods to overcome these adversities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine intervention in overcoming life's challenges and the importance of community and collective worship.
    • It serves as a reminder that, like the Maruts and Indra, we can seek strength and support from higher powers or from within ourselves to face and conquer our obstacles.
    • In modern life, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate resilience and seek help, whether through spiritual practices, community support, or personal determination.
