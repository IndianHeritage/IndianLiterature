अकारि त इन्द्र गोतमैभिर ब्रह्माण्य ओक्तā नमसā हरिभ्यां |सुपेशसङ् वाजम् आ भरā नः प्रातर मक्षू धियावसुर जगम्यāt ||
akāri ta indra gotamebhir brahmāṇy oktā namasā haribhyām | supeśasaṁ vājam ā bharā naḥ prātar makṣū dhiyāvasur jagamyāt ||
  • Mantra: "akāri ta indra gotamebhir brahmāṇy oktā namasā haribhyām | supeśasaṁ vājam ā bharā naḥ prātar makṣū dhiyāvasur jagamyāt ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • akāri: made, created
    • ta: that
    • indra: O Indra, the king of gods
    • gotamebhir: by the sages (Gotama refers to a lineage of sages)
    • brahmāṇy: the sacred knowledge or the ultimate reality
    • oktā: spoken, uttered
    • namasā: with reverence, bowing
    • haribhyām: to the divine beings, often associated with Vishnu
    • supeśasaṁ: with good qualities, auspicious
    • vājam: strength, power, or nourishment
    • ā bharā: may you bring, may you bestow
    • naḥ: to us
    • prātar: in the morning
    • makṣū: with intelligence, wisdom
    • dhiyā: with understanding, intellect
    • avasur: may it come, may it arrive
    • jagamyāt: may it be attained, may it be realized
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of gods, asking for blessings and strength.
    • The invocation of sages (Gotama) signifies the importance of wisdom and knowledge in our lives.
    • By bowing with reverence to the divine, we acknowledge the higher powers that guide us.
    • The request for "supeśasaṁ vājam" emphasizes the need for auspicious qualities and strength in our endeavors.
    • Asking for blessings "in the morning" symbolizes the start of new opportunities and fresh beginnings.
    • The focus on wisdom and understanding ("makṣū dhiyā") highlights the importance of intellect in achieving our goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be recited before starting a new project to invoke the blessings of wisdom and strength.
    • Students may chant this mantra before exams to seek clarity of thought and intelligence.
    • During significant life transitions, such as marriage or moving to a new city, individuals can use this mantra to seek auspicious beginnings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine guidance and wisdom in our daily lives.
    • It emphasizes the need for reverence towards higher powers and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • By incorporating this mantra into our routines, we can cultivate a mindset that values strength, intelligence, and auspicious beginnings, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life.
