अति वा यो मरुतो मन्यते नो ब्रह्म वा यः क्रियमाणं निनित्सात् |तपूंषि तस्मै वृजिनानि सन्तु ब्रह्मद्विषम् अभि तं शोकतु द्यौः ||
ati vā yo maruto manyate no brahma vā yaḥ kriyamāṇaṁ ninitsāt | tapūṁṣi tasmai vṛjināni santu brahmadviṣam abhi taṁ śocatu dyauḥ ||
  • ati vā yo maruto manyate: This phrase can be translated as "He who thinks of the Maruts (the storm deities)". The Maruts are often associated with strength, power, and the forces of nature. In real life, this can be likened to individuals who harness the power of nature, such as meteorologists or environmentalists who study and respect the forces that govern our climate.
  • no brahma vā yaḥ kriyamāṇaṁ ninitsāt: This translates to "not Brahman, or he who is engaged in action". Here, Brahman refers to the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit in Hindu philosophy. This line suggests a distinction between the divine and the actions of individuals. In practical terms, it can be seen in the context of people who are engaged in their daily tasks, often forgetting the larger spiritual or cosmic significance of their actions.
  • tapūṁṣi tasmai vṛjināni santu: This means "may the powers of austerity (tapas) remove his sins". Austerity refers to self-discipline and spiritual practice. In real life, this can be related to individuals who engage in meditation, yoga, or other forms of self-discipline to purify themselves and seek personal growth. It emphasizes the importance of inner strength and discipline in overcoming challenges.
  • brahmadviṣam abhi taṁ śocatu dyauḥ: This translates to "may the heavens lament for him who is hostile to Brahman". This line suggests that those who oppose the ultimate truth or reality will face consequences. In a contemporary context, this can be seen in the actions of individuals or organizations that harm the environment or society, ultimately leading to their own downfall. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the repercussions of our actions.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the relationship between human actions, the forces of nature, and the ultimate reality. It serves as a reminder to respect the power of nature, engage in self-discipline, and recognize the consequences of our actions. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as environmental conservation efforts, personal development practices, and ethical decision-making. By understanding and embodying the teachings of this mantra, individuals can strive for a harmonious existence that honors both the natural world and the spiritual essence of life.
