त्वां सुतस्य पीतये प्रात्नं इन्द्र हवामहे |कुशिकासो अवश्यवः ||
tvāṁ sutasya pītaye pratnam indra havāmahe | kuśikāso avasyavaḥ ||
  • tvāṁ - This word translates to "you" and refers to the divine or a higher power, often associated with Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
  • sutasya - This means "of the son" or "of the offspring," indicating a connection to lineage or progeny.
  • pītaye - This translates to "for the drink" or "for the nourishment," suggesting a request for sustenance or support.
  • pratnam - This word means "we invoke" or "we call upon," indicating a plea or prayer directed towards the divine.
  • indra - Referring to Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, symbolizing strength, power, and protection.
  • havāmahe - This translates to "we offer" or "we worship," indicating an act of devotion or reverence.
  • kuśikāso - This term refers to the descendants of the sage Kuśika, linking the mantra to a specific lineage or tradition.
  • avasyavaḥ - This means "we are in need" or "we are dependent," expressing a sense of reliance on the divine for support and guidance.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation directed towards Indra, seeking his blessings and nourishment for the offspring or lineage of the worshippers. It emphasizes the importance of divine support in sustaining life and ensuring prosperity.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts:

  • Family and Lineage: The invocation highlights the significance of family and the blessings required for the well-being of future generations. For instance, parents may recite this mantra during family gatherings or rituals to seek protection and prosperity for their children.
  • Community Support: The mantra can also be relevant in community settings where collective well-being is sought. For example, during festivals or communal prayers, people may come together to invoke divine blessings for the entire community.
  • Personal Growth: On a personal level, individuals may use this mantra as a reminder of their dependence on higher powers for guidance and support in their life journeys, especially during challenging times.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine intervention and support for the sustenance of life and lineage. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals, families, and communities, emphasizing the need for spiritual nourishment and guidance in navigating life's challenges. By invoking Indra, worshippers express their reliance on divine strength and protection, making it a powerful tool for fostering a sense of unity and purpose in both personal and communal contexts.
