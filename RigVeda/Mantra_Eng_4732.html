समिद्धे अग्नौ सुत इन्द्र सोम ā त्वा वहन्तु हरयो वहिष्ठाः |त्वायतā मनसा जोहवीमीन्द्रा याहि सुविता य महे नः ||
samiddhe agnau suta indra soma ā tvā vahantu harayo vahiṣṭhāḥ | tvāyatā manasā johavīmīndrā yāhi suvitāya mahe naḥ ||
  • Translation: "In the kindled fire, may the swift horses of Indra and Soma carry you forth; may they, with their best strength, bring you to us, O Indra, who are worthy of our thoughts and prayers."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • samiddhe: kindled or ignited, representing the initiation of energy or spiritual awakening.
    • agnau: in the fire, symbolizing purification and transformation.
    • suta: the one who is poured or offered, often referring to offerings made to deities.
    • indra: the king of gods, representing strength, power, and protection.
    • soma: a sacred drink, symbolizing vitality and divine inspiration.
    • tvā: you, addressing the divine presence directly.
    • vahantu: may they carry, indicating a request for support and guidance.
    • harayo: swift horses, symbolizing speed and efficiency in achieving goals.
    • vahiṣṭhāḥ: the best or the most excellent, emphasizing the quality of the offerings.
    • tvāyatā: with your mind, indicating the importance of intention and focus.
    • manasā: with the mind, reinforcing the idea of mental engagement in spiritual practices.
    • johavīmīndrā: we invoke you, O Indra, calling upon the deity for assistance.
    • yāhi: come, a direct invitation for divine presence.
    • suvitāya: for our well-being, indicating the purpose of the invocation.
    • mahe: great or mighty, referring to the strength of the invocation.
    • naḥ: for us, emphasizing the communal aspect of the prayer.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a traditional Hindu ceremony, offerings are made to the fire (Agni) to invoke blessings from deities like Indra and Soma. This practice symbolizes the transformation of material offerings into spiritual energy.
    • In modern life, the mantra can be applied during challenging times when one seeks strength and guidance. For instance, during a job interview, one might mentally invoke the qualities of Indra for confidence and clarity.
    • In team settings, invoking the spirit of collaboration and support can be likened to the swift horses carrying the intentions of the group towards a common goal.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine support and guidance in our endeavors. It highlights the significance of intention and collective effort in achieving our goals. In real life, this can be applied in various situations, from personal challenges to communal efforts, reminding us that with the right mindset and support, we can overcome obstacles and achieve greatness. The invocation of Indra and Soma serves as a reminder of the strength and vitality that can be harnessed through spiritual practices and community support.
