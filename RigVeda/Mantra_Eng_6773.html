धेनूर जिन्वतम उता जिन्वतां विश्व हतम रक्षांसी सेधतम अमीवाः |सजोषसां उषसा सूर्येण च सोमं सुन्वतो अश्विनाः ||
dhenūr jinvatam uta jinvataṁ viśo hataṁ rakṣāṁsi sedhatam amīvāḥ | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa ca somaṁ sunvato aśvinā ||
  • Mantra: "dhenūr jinvatam uta jinvataṁ viśo hataṁ rakṣāṁsi sedhatam amīvāḥ | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa ca somaṁ sunvato aśvinā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dhenūr: cow, symbolizing nourishment and wealth.
    • jinvatam: victorious, representing success and triumph.
    • uta: and, a conjunction linking ideas.
    • jinvataṁ: those who are victorious.
    • viśo: community or people, indicating collective strength.
    • hataṁ: defeated or harmed, referring to challenges faced.
    • rakṣāṁsi: protections or safeguards, emphasizing the need for security.
    • sedhatam: to be established or firm, indicating stability.
    • amīvāḥ: those who are not enemies, suggesting harmony.
    • sajoṣasā: with joy or happiness, indicating a positive state.
    • uṣasā: dawn, symbolizing new beginnings.
    • sūryeṇa: with the sun, representing energy and vitality.
    • ca: and, another conjunction.
    • somaṁ: soma, a drink of immortality, symbolizing nourishment and vitality.
    • sunvato: those who are nourished or enriched.
    • aśvinā: the twin gods, representing health and well-being.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of nourishment and prosperity, symbolized by the cow (dhenūr). In real life, this can be related to the sustenance we derive from our environment, such as food, resources, and community support.
    • The idea of victory (jinvatam) and community (viśo) suggests that success is not just an individual achievement but a collective effort. For example, in a workplace, team collaboration often leads to greater success than individual endeavors.
    • The mention of protections (rakṣāṁsi) highlights the need for security in our lives, whether it be physical safety, emotional support, or financial stability. This can be seen in the importance of having a supportive family or a secure job.
    • The dawn (uṣasā) symbolizes new beginnings, reminding us that every day brings new opportunities. This can be applied in personal growth, where each day is a chance to improve oneself.
    • The reference to the sun (sūryeṇa) and soma (nourishment) signifies the importance of energy and vitality in our lives. In practical terms, this can relate to maintaining a healthy lifestyle through proper diet and exercise.
    • The aśvinā, representing health and well-being, underscores the significance of taking care of our physical and mental health, which is essential for a fulfilling life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nourishment, community, and well-being. It emphasizes that our success and happiness are deeply rooted in the support we receive from others and the environment.
    • In real life, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious existence, where we prioritize health, community support, and collective success.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to seek balance in our lives, ensuring that we are not only focused on personal achievements but also on the well-being of those around us.
