युक्ष्वा हि वृत्रहन्तम हरि इन्द्र परावतः |अर्वाचीनो मगहन सोमपीतय उग्र ऋष्वेभिर आगहि ||
yukṣvā hi vṛtrahantama harī indra parāvataḥ | arvācīno maghavan somapītaya ugra ṛṣvebhir ā gahi ||
  • yukṣvā hi vṛtrahantama harī indra parāvataḥ
    • yukṣvā: This term can be interpreted as "having been yoked" or "joined together." It signifies unity and collaboration.
    • vṛtrahantama: Refers to "the slayer of Vritra," where Vritra symbolizes obstacles or challenges. Indra, the king of gods, is often depicted as the one who overcomes these challenges.
    • harī: This word means "the one who is swift" or "the one who moves quickly," often associated with the divine qualities of Indra.
    • indra: The name of the chief deity in Vedic tradition, representing strength, power, and leadership.
    • parāvataḥ: This term can be translated as "the one who is beyond," indicating a higher state of existence or consciousness.
  • arvācīno maghavan somapītaya ugra ṛṣvebhir ā gahi
    • arvācīno: This word means "new" or "recent," suggesting a fresh perspective or approach.
    • maghavan: Refers to "the one who is rich" or "the benefactor," often associated with abundance and prosperity.
    • somapītaya: This term relates to "the one who drinks soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals that symbolizes immortality and divine inspiration.
    • ugra: Meaning "fierce" or "powerful," it denotes strength and intensity.
    • ṛṣvebhir: This word refers to "the seers" or "the wise ones," indicating the importance of wisdom and insight.
    • ā gahi: This phrase means "come here" or "approach," inviting the divine presence or blessings.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, strength, and wisdom in overcoming challenges. It calls upon Indra, the powerful deity, to assist in achieving prosperity and abundance. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to unite their strengths and wisdom to overcome obstacles in their lives.
  • In leadership, it serves as a reminder for leaders to harness collective efforts and insights to achieve common goals.
  • In community building, it highlights the significance of collaboration and support in fostering a prosperous environment.

Overall, this mantra serves as a powerful invocation for strength, unity, and divine guidance in the pursuit of success and well-being.
