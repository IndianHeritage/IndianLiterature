सोमाः पवन्त इन्दवोऽस्मभ्यं गातुवित्तमाः |मित्राः सुवानाः अरेपसः स्वाध्यः स्वरविदः ||
somāḥ pavanta indavo 'smabhyaṁ gātuvittamāḥ | mitrāḥ suvānā arepasaḥ svādhyaḥ svarvidaḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra translates to "The soma (sacred drink) flows, the Indus (gods) grant us the best of riches. The friends (Mitra) are well-disposed, they are the ones who know the essence of the sound (svara)."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Somāḥ: Refers to 'soma', a ritual drink in Vedic culture, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
    • Pavanta: Means 'to flow' or 'to purify', indicating the movement of divine energy.
    • Indavo: Refers to 'Indus', representing the divine beings or gods who bestow blessings.
    • Gātuvittamāḥ: Translates to 'the best of riches', indicating prosperity and abundance.
    • Mitrāḥ: Refers to 'friends', symbolizing companionship and support.
    • Suvānā: Means 'well-disposed' or 'beneficial', indicating positive relationships.
    • Arepasaḥ: Refers to 'those who know', indicating wisdom and understanding.
    • Svādhyaḥ: Means 'essence' or 'self-study', indicating the importance of self-awareness.
    • Svarvidaḥ: Refers to 'knowledge of sound', indicating the significance of communication and expression.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of 'soma' can be likened to modern practices of mindfulness and meditation, where individuals seek to connect with their inner selves and attain a state of peace and clarity.
    • The idea of 'friends' and 'well-disposed' relationships can be seen in community support systems, where individuals come together to uplift and assist one another in times of need.
    • Understanding 'svādhya' or self-study is crucial in personal development, where individuals engage in self-reflection to improve their lives and relationships.
    • The importance of 'svara' or sound can be related to effective communication in personal and professional settings, emphasizing the need for clear and constructive dialogue.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the interconnectedness of divine blessings, personal relationships, and self-awareness. In real life, it encourages individuals to seek prosperity not just in material wealth but also in nurturing relationships and understanding oneself. By embodying the principles of friendship, wisdom, and effective communication, one can create a harmonious and fulfilling life, reflecting the essence of the mantra in everyday actions and interactions.
