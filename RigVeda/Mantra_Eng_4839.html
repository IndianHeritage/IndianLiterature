मन्त्र: दिवे-दिवे सदृशीर अन्यं अर्धं कृष्णा असेद्हद अप सद्मनो जाः |अहन दासाऽ वृषभो वसनयन्तोदव्रजे वर्चिनं शम्बरं च ||
dive-dive sadṛśīr anyam ardhaṁ kṛṣṇā asedhad apa sadmano jāḥ | ahan dāsā vṛṣabho vasnayantodavraje varcinaṁ śambaraṁ ca ||
  • Mantra: "dive-dive sadṛśīr anyam ardhaṁ kṛṣṇā asedhad apa sadmano jāḥ | ahan dāsā vṛṣabho vasnayantodavraje varcinaṁ śambaraṁ ca ||"
  • Translation: This mantra speaks of the divine and the interconnectedness of all beings. It suggests that in the celestial realm, there exists a similarity among all entities, and it emphasizes the importance of unity and harmony.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • dive-dive: Refers to the celestial or divine realm.
    • sadṛśīr: Similar or alike.
    • anyam: Another or different.
    • ardhaṁ: Half or part.
    • kṛṣṇā: Often associated with the color black or dark, but can also refer to the divine aspect of Lord Krishna.
    • asedhad: To be present or to exist.
    • apa: Away or apart.
    • sadmano: Of the mind or consciousness.
    • jāḥ: To go or to move.
    • ahan: Day or light.
    • dāsā: Servants or devotees.
    • vṛṣabho: Bull, often symbolizing strength and power.
    • vasnayantodavraje: To bring forth or to manifest in the world.
    • varcinaṁ: Radiant or shining.
    • śambaraṁ: A reference to a powerful being or entity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, individuals may come from different backgrounds but share common goals, much like the mantra's emphasis on unity despite differences.
    • In nature, various species coexist and contribute to the ecosystem, demonstrating the interconnectedness highlighted in the mantra.
    • In personal relationships, understanding and empathy can bridge gaps between individuals, reflecting the idea of similarity and harmony.
  • Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the divine presence in all beings and the interconnectedness of life. It encourages individuals to embrace unity, understanding, and compassion in their interactions. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in community building, environmental conservation, and fostering harmonious relationships, reminding us that despite our differences, we are all part of a greater whole.
