स नः पावक दीदिहि द्युमद अस्मे सुवीर्यम् |भवास्तोत्रिभ्यो अन्तमः स्वस्तये ||
sa naḥ pāvaka dīdihi dyumad asme suvīryam | bhavā stotṛbhyo antamaḥ svastaye ||
  • sa naḥ - "May that (divine force) be ours"
  • pāvaka - "O Purifier" or "O Fire"
  • dīdihi - "Illuminate" or "Shine forth"
  • dyumad - "Radiant" or "Brilliant"
  • asme - "For us"
  • suvīryam - "Great strength" or "Heroic power"
  • bhavā - "Become" or "May you be"
  • stotṛbhyo - "To the worshippers" or "To those who praise"
  • antamaḥ - "The ultimate" or "The highest"
  • svastaye - "For well-being" or "For prosperity"

This mantra from the Rigveda is a prayer invoking the divine force, often associated with fire, to illuminate and empower us. It emphasizes the importance of purification and strength in our lives. The invocation of "O Purifier" suggests a desire for spiritual and moral cleansing, which is essential in our daily lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Just as fire purifies, individuals can seek to cleanse their thoughts and actions, striving for personal development and moral integrity.
  • Community Well-being: The call for strength and illumination can be seen in community efforts to uplift and empower each other, fostering a supportive environment.
  • Spiritual Practices: Many spiritual traditions emphasize purification rituals, which align with the essence of this mantra, encouraging individuals to seek clarity and strength in their spiritual journeys.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for purification, illumination, and strength. It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking higher ideals and virtues in our lives. By invoking these qualities, we can strive for personal and communal well-being, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal development to community engagement and spiritual practices.
