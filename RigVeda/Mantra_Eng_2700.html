यस्मै धायुर अदधा मार्त्यायाभक्तं चिद्भजते गेह्यं सः |भद्रा त इन्द्र सुमतिर घृताचि सहस्रदाना पुरुहूत रातिः ||
yasmai dhāyur adadhā martyāyābhaktaṁ cid bhajate gehya1ṁ saḥ | bhadrā ta indra sumatir ghṛtācī sahasradānā puruhūta rātiḥ ||
  • yasmai dhāyur adadhā martyāyābhaktaṁ cid bhajate gehyaṁ saḥ
    • yasmai: "To whom" - This refers to a divine entity or a higher power.
    • dhāyur: "Life" - This signifies the essence of life or vitality.
    • adadhā: "Gives" - This indicates the act of bestowing or granting.
    • martyāyā: "To mortals" - This refers to human beings or those who are subject to death.
    • abhaktaṁ: "Without devotion" - This implies a lack of reverence or worship.
    • cid: "Consciousness" - This denotes awareness or the essence of being.
    • bhajate: "Worships" - This indicates the act of devotion or reverence.
    • gehyaṁ: "In the household" - This refers to domestic life or the home.
    • saḥ: "He" - This refers to the divine or the one being addressed.
  • bhadrā ta indra sumatir ghṛtācī sahasradānā puruhūta rātiḥ
    • bhadrā: "Auspicious" - This signifies something that is beneficial or favorable.
    • ta: "That" - This refers to the divine or the higher power being invoked.
    • indra: "Indra" - The king of gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength and power.
    • sumatir: "Good thought" - This indicates wisdom or positive thinking.
    • ghṛtācī: "With ghee" - Ghee is a symbol of purity and nourishment in Vedic rituals.
    • sahasradānā: "Thousand-fold giver" - This signifies abundance and generosity.
    • puruhūta: "Many offerings" - This indicates the act of giving and devotion.
    • rātiḥ: "Joy" - This signifies happiness or fulfillment.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the importance of devotion and the relationship between the divine and mortals. It suggests that true life and vitality are bestowed upon those who worship with sincerity, even within the confines of their domestic lives. The invocation of Indra highlights the need for strength and wisdom in our actions, while the mention of ghee symbolizes the purity of intentions in our offerings.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to our daily practices of gratitude and mindfulness. For instance, when we express appreciation for the small blessings in our lives, we cultivate a sense of joy and fulfillment. Additionally, it encourages us to approach our responsibilities with a sense of devotion, whether in our families, workplaces, or communities. By embodying the principles of generosity and positive thinking, we can create a more auspicious and harmonious environment around us.
