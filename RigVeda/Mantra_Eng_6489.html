सीदन्तस ते वयो यथा गोश्रीति मधौ मदिरे विवक्षणे |अभि त्वाम इन्द्र नोनुमः ||
sīdantas te vayo yathā gośrīte madhau madire vivakṣaṇe | abhi tvām indra nonumaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sīdantas: Those who are seated or settled.
    • te: Your.
    • vayo: Age or life span.
    • yathā: As or like.
    • gośrīte: In the context of cows or cattle.
    • madhau: In the sweetness or essence.
    • madire: In the intoxication or delight.
    • vivakṣaṇe: In the act of speaking or expressing.
    • abhi: Towards or for.
    • tvām: You.
    • indra: O Indra, the king of gods.
    • nonumaḥ: We bow or we offer our respects.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep reverence towards Indra, the deity associated with strength, rain, and the heavens.
    • The phrase "sīdantas te vayo" suggests a state of stability and longevity, akin to the way cattle are nurtured and cared for, symbolizing prosperity and abundance.
    • The reference to "madhau madire" indicates a state of bliss or intoxication, which can be interpreted as the joy derived from life’s experiences and the richness of existence.
    • By invoking Indra, the speaker seeks blessings for strength and support in their endeavors, much like how farmers pray for rain to nourish their crops.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, rituals are often performed to seek blessings from deities like Indra for a bountiful harvest, reflecting the importance of divine support in human efforts.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during challenging times, seeking strength and stability, akin to how one might turn to a mentor or a guiding figure for support.
    • During celebrations, such as weddings or festivals, people may invoke blessings for joy and prosperity, paralleling the "madhau madire" aspect of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support for stability, prosperity, and joy in life.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of a higher power that aids in human endeavors, reminding us of the interconnectedness of life and the divine.
    • In contemporary life, it serves as a reminder to seek strength and support from both spiritual and community resources, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
