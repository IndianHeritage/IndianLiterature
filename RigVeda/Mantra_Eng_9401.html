यथा युगं वरत्रयā नह्यन्ति धरुणाय कम् |एवा दाधार ते मनो जीवातवे न मृत्यवे 'थो अरिष्टतातये ||
yathā yugaṁ varatrayā nahyanti dharuṇāya kam | evā dādhāra te mano jīvātave na mṛtyave 'tho ariṣṭatātaye ||
  • yathā yugaṁ varatrayā nahyanti dharuṇāya kam: This phrase can be translated to "As the threefold protection does not abandon the one who is steadfast." Here, "yugaṁ" refers to a cycle or age, "varatrayā" signifies the threefold protection, and "dharuṇāya" means steadfastness or stability. This suggests that those who remain steadfast in their beliefs and actions will be protected by a higher power.
  • evā dādhāra te mano: This translates to "Thus, may your mind be supported." The word "dādhāra" implies support or sustenance, indicating that a strong mind is essential for navigating life's challenges. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who maintain mental resilience in the face of adversity, such as athletes who train their minds as much as their bodies.
  • jīvātave na mṛtyave 'tho ariṣṭatātaye: This part means "For the sake of life, not for death or misfortune." It emphasizes the importance of living life fully and positively, rather than succumbing to fear or negativity. In practical terms, this can be related to people who choose to focus on their health and well-being, actively seeking joy and fulfillment rather than dwelling on potential failures or losses.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of resilience, mental strength, and the pursuit of a positive life. It teaches us that steadfastness in our beliefs and actions, along with a strong and supported mind, can lead to protection and fulfillment in life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, mental health, and even in professional settings where perseverance and a positive mindset are crucial for success. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate the complexities of life with greater ease and purpose.
