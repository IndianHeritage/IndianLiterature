अग्निं तं मन्ये यो वसुर अस्तं यं यान्ति धेनवः |अस्तम arvanta आशवोऽस्तं नित्यासो वाजिन इषं स्तोत्रिभ्य आ भर ||
agniṁ tam manye yo vasur astaṁ yaṁ yanti dhenavaḥ | astam arvanta āśavo 'staṁ nityāso vājina iṣaṁ stotṛbhya ā bhara ||
  • Agniṁ tam manye: This phrase translates to "I meditate upon that Agni." Here, "Agni" refers to fire, which symbolizes energy, transformation, and divine presence. In real life, fire is essential for cooking, warmth, and rituals, representing both physical and spiritual nourishment.
  • yo vasur astaṁ: This means "who is the wealth of the world." "Vasur" signifies wealth or abundance. In contemporary terms, this can relate to the resources we have, such as knowledge, relationships, and material wealth, which are vital for a fulfilling life.
  • yaṁ yanti dhenavaḥ: This translates to "which the cows go to." Cows are often seen as symbols of prosperity and nourishment in many cultures, especially in India. This line emphasizes the importance of nurturing and sustaining life, akin to how we rely on agriculture and livestock for sustenance.
  • astam arvanta āśavo: This means "may the strong ones come to us." It suggests a call for strength and support from powerful forces or deities. In real life, this can be interpreted as seeking guidance and strength from mentors, leaders, or even spiritual practices that empower us.
  • 'staṁ nityāso: This translates to "may they be eternal." This phrase emphasizes the desire for lasting stability and continuity in life. In practical terms, it can relate to the pursuit of enduring values, relationships, and legacies that we wish to leave behind.
  • vājina iṣaṁ: This means "the one who brings victory." "Vājina" refers to the one who is victorious or successful. In everyday life, this can be seen in the pursuit of goals, whether personal or professional, where we strive for success and achievement.
  • stotṛbhya ā bhara: This translates to "may you bring to the worshippers." It signifies a plea for blessings and abundance to those who seek and honor the divine. In real life, this can be related to the idea of community support, where collective efforts and prayers lead to shared prosperity and well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and support in our lives. It highlights the importance of fire (Agni) as a transformative force, the value of wealth and nourishment, and the pursuit of strength and victory. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals for prosperity, in personal development practices, or in community gatherings where collective aspirations are shared. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of nurturing our resources, and the pursuit of lasting success and fulfillment.
