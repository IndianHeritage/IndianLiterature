जातो जायते सुदिनत्वे अह्नां समर्य आ विदथे वर्धमानः |पुनन्ति धीराः अपसो मनीषा देवयाः विप्र उद इयर्ति वाचम् ||
jāto jāyate sudinatve ahnāṁ samarya ā vidathe vardhamānaḥ | punanti dhīrā apaso manīṣā devayā vipra ud iyarti vācam ||
  • jāto jāyate: This phrase translates to "born, is born." It signifies the beginning of existence or the emergence of life. In real life, this can be related to the birth of a child, symbolizing new beginnings and potential.
  • sudinatve: This word means "in good fortune" or "with auspiciousness." It reflects the idea of favorable conditions that lead to success. For example, a student who studies diligently and receives good grades is experiencing sudinatve in their academic journey.
  • ahnāṁ: This term refers to "day" or "time." It emphasizes the importance of time in the process of growth and development. In a practical sense, this can be seen in how businesses grow over time through consistent effort and adaptation.
  • samarya: This means "to be united" or "to come together." It highlights the significance of collaboration and teamwork. For instance, a sports team that works together harmoniously to achieve a common goal exemplifies samarya.
  • ā vidathe: This phrase translates to "in the assembly" or "in the gathering." It suggests the importance of community and collective effort. A real-life example is a community coming together to support a local cause or charity event.
  • vardhamānaḥ: This means "growing" or "increasing." It signifies progress and development. For example, a startup that expands its operations and customer base is experiencing vardhamānaḥ.
  • punanti: This term translates to "purify" or "refine." It indicates the process of improvement and elevation. In life, this can be seen in personal growth, where individuals learn from their experiences and become better versions of themselves.
  • dhīrā: This word means "wise" or "intelligent." It emphasizes the value of wisdom in decision-making. For instance, a leader who makes thoughtful choices for their team demonstrates dhīrā.
  • apaso: This term refers to "water" or "flow." It symbolizes nourishment and sustenance. In a practical context, access to clean water is essential for health and well-being, highlighting the importance of apaso.
  • manīṣā: This means "intellect" or "thought." It signifies the power of the mind in shaping our reality. For example, innovative thinkers who create groundbreaking technologies embody manīṣā.
  • devayā: This term translates to "by the divine" or "through the gods." It suggests a connection to higher powers or spiritual guidance. In real life, people often seek divine intervention in times of need, reflecting the essence of devayā.
  • vipra: This word means "sage" or "wise person." It represents the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment. For instance, a mentor who guides others on their educational journey exemplifies vipra.
  • ud iyarti: This phrase translates to "uplift" or "raise up." It signifies the act of elevating oneself or others. In a community context, initiatives that empower marginalized groups reflect ud iyarti.
  • vācam: This means "speech" or "voice." It emphasizes the importance of communication in expressing thoughts and ideas. For example, public speaking can inspire and mobilize people towards a common cause, showcasing the power of vācam.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, growth, and the importance of community and wisdom. It highlights how individuals and groups can come together to create positive change and progress. In real-life scenarios, the principles of this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as education, teamwork, personal development, and community service. By embracing these values, we can foster a more harmonious and prosperous society.
