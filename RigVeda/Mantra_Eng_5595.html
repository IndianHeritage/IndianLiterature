प्रियā वो नाम हुवे तुराणाम् ā यत तृपन मरुतो वावशानाः ||
priyā vo nāma huve turāṇām ā yat tṛpan maruto vāvaśānāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "priyā vo nāma huve turāṇām ā yat tṛpan maruto vāvaśānāḥ"
  • Translation: "May your name be dear to us, O Maruts, as you fulfill our desires."
  • Explanation:
    • Priyā: This word means "dear" or "beloved." It signifies the importance of relationships and connections in our lives.
    • Vo: This translates to "your," indicating a sense of possession or belonging.
    • Nāma: Meaning "name," it represents identity and recognition. In many cultures, a name carries significant weight and is often associated with one's character or essence.
    • Huve: This means "may it be" or "let it be," expressing a wish or a prayer.
    • Turāṇām: This term refers to "the swift ones," which in this context relates to the Maruts, the storm deities known for their speed and power.
    • Ā yat: This means "as" or "like," drawing a comparison or connection.
    • Tṛpan: This word means "to fulfill" or "to satisfy," indicating the act of meeting desires or needs.
    • Maruto: Refers to the Maruts, the group of storm gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, vitality, and the forces of nature.
    • Vāvaśānāḥ: This term can be interpreted as "who are invoked" or "who are called upon," suggesting a plea for assistance or intervention.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal relationships, expressing that someone's name or presence is dear to us can strengthen bonds and foster deeper connections.
    • In a community setting, invoking the name of a leader or a revered figure can unite people and inspire collective action.
    • In times of need, calling upon the forces of nature, such as rain during a drought, reflects the human desire for support from higher powers.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of relationships, identity, and the fulfillment of desires. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine forces that influence our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in personal prayers, community gatherings, and moments of seeking guidance or support from nature or higher powers. By acknowledging the importance of names and relationships, we cultivate a sense of gratitude and connection in our daily lives.
