सरस्वतीं याम पितरो हवन्ते दक्षिणा यज्ञं अभिनक्षमाणाः | सहस्रार्घम इळो अत्र भागं रायस पोषं यजमानेषु धेहि ||
sarasvatīṁ yām pitaro havante dakṣiṇā yajñam abhinakṣamāṇāḥ | sahasrārgham iḻo atra bhāgaṁ rāyas poṣaṁ yajamāneṣu dhehi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Sarasvatīm: Referring to the goddess Saraswati, the embodiment of knowledge, wisdom, and learning.
    • yām: Whom.
    • pitaro: The ancestors or forefathers.
    • havante: Offer or invoke.
    • dakṣiṇā: The offerings or gifts given in a ritual.
    • yajñam: The sacrificial ritual or ceremony.
    • abhinakṣamāṇāḥ: Those who are performing or conducting the ritual.
    • sahasrārgham: A thousand offerings or gifts.
    • iḻo: The divine or the sacred.
    • atra: Here or in this place.
    • bhāgaṁ: Portion or share.
    • rāyas: Wealth or prosperity.
    • poṣaṁ: Nourishment or sustenance.
    • yajamāneṣu: Among those who are performing the sacrifice.
    • dhehi: Bestow or grant.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the goddess Saraswati, asking for her blessings in the form of knowledge and wisdom.
    • It emphasizes the importance of honoring ancestors and performing rituals with sincerity and dedication.
    • The mention of "dakṣiṇā" highlights the significance of offerings in rituals, which can be seen as a way to express gratitude and seek blessings.
    • The phrase "sahasrārgham" suggests the abundance of offerings, indicating a desire for prosperity and well-being.
    • Overall, the mantra reflects a deep connection between knowledge, ritual practice, and the blessings of the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In educational institutions, students often seek the blessings of Saraswati before exams, symbolizing the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom.
    • During family rituals, honoring ancestors through offerings can strengthen familial bonds and instill a sense of gratitude.
    • In business, the practice of making offerings or donations can be seen as a way to seek prosperity and success in endeavors.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of knowledge, respect for ancestors, and the role of rituals in seeking blessings.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including education, family traditions, and professional pursuits.
    • By invoking the blessings of Saraswati and honoring the past, individuals can cultivate wisdom and prosperity in their lives.
