आ चन त्वा चिकित्सामो 'धि चन त्वा नेमसि | शनैर इव शनकैर इवेन्द्रायन्दो परि श्रव ||
ā cana tvā cikitsāmo 'dhi cana tvā nemasi | śanair iva śanakair ivendrāyendo pari srava ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā cana: O, you who are (the one) who can heal.
    • tvā: you.
    • cikitsāmo: the healer or the one who provides treatment.
    • 'dhi: indeed.
    • cana: (to) you.
    • tvā: you.
    • nemasi: (to) protect or safeguard.
    • śanair: slowly or gently.
    • iva: like.
    • śanakair: with care or gradually.
    • ivendrāyendo: like the lord of the senses (Indra).
    • pari srava: may flow around or may surround.
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra invokes a divine healer, asking for protection and gentle care. It emphasizes the importance of healing and safeguarding, akin to the nurturing qualities of a deity like Indra, who is often associated with strength and protection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Healthcare: In a hospital setting, this mantra can be seen as a call for the medical professionals to provide gentle and effective care to patients, ensuring their healing process is supported with compassion.
    • Mental Health: In the context of mental health, it can represent the need for a supportive environment where individuals feel safe and cared for, allowing them to heal from emotional wounds.
    • Community Support: In a community, this mantra can symbolize the collective effort to protect and nurture each other, especially during times of crisis or distress.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for healing and protection. It serves as a reminder of the importance of compassion and care in our interactions with others. In real-life scenarios, whether in healthcare, mental health, or community support, the principles of gentle healing and safeguarding resonate deeply, encouraging us to foster environments where individuals can thrive and recover. By embodying these values, we contribute to a more nurturing and supportive society.
