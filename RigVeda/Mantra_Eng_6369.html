त्वं वृषा जनानां मंहिष्ठ इन्द्र जज्ञिषे |सत्रा विश्वा स्वपत्यानि दधिषे ||
tvaṁ vṛṣā janānām maṁhiṣṭha indra jajñiṣe | satrā viśvā svapatyāni dadhiṣe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • vṛṣā: The best or the most excellent
    • janānām: Of the people or of the beings
    • maṁhiṣṭha: The most powerful or the strongest
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • jajñiṣe: You who are born or who have been born
    • satrā: In the assembly or in the gathering
    • viśvā: All or universal
    • svapatyāni: The one who rules or governs
    • dadhiṣe: You provide or you bestow
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra addresses Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, acknowledging his supreme power and role as a protector and provider for the people. It emphasizes his excellence among all beings and his ability to govern and bestow blessings in gatherings or assemblies.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, leaders often take on the role of 'Indra' by guiding and providing for the needs of the people, ensuring that everyone is cared for and that the community thrives.
    • In a corporate environment, a strong leader can be seen as the 'Indra' of the organization, making decisions that benefit all employees and fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
    • During festivals or communal gatherings, the role of a host can be likened to Indra, as they provide for the needs of guests, ensuring a harmonious and enjoyable experience for all.
  • Conclusion and Significance: The mantra signifies the importance of leadership, strength, and community support. It highlights the role of a powerful figure who not only leads but also nurtures and provides for the collective well-being. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts such as family, community, and organizational leadership, where the focus is on serving others and fostering unity. The essence of this mantra encourages individuals to embody these qualities in their own lives, promoting a culture of care and responsibility.
