विश्वा वसूनि संजयन् पवस्व सोम धारया |इन् द्वेषांसी सध्र्यक ||
viśvā vasūni saṁjayan pavasva soma dhārayā | inu dveṣāṁsi sadhryak ||
  • viśvā vasūni saṁjayan: This phrase can be translated to "unite all the good things." Here, "viśvā" means "all" or "universal," and "vasūni" refers to "good things" or "prosperities." The idea is to bring together all positive aspects of life, such as health, wealth, and happiness.
  • pavasva soma dhārayā: This part translates to "may the Soma (a sacred drink) support us." "Pavasva" means "to purify" or "to make pure," and "Soma" is often associated with a divine nectar that grants immortality and wisdom. This suggests a plea for divine support and purification in our endeavors.
  • inu dveṣāṁsi sadhryak: This can be interpreted as "remove all enmities." "Dveṣāṁsi" refers to "enmities" or "hatred," and "sadhryak" means "to remove" or "to eliminate." This emphasizes the importance of overcoming negativity and fostering harmony among individuals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message of unity, purification, and the elimination of negativity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal relationships, it encourages individuals to focus on the positive aspects of their connections and work towards resolving conflicts.
  • In community settings, it promotes collaboration and understanding among diverse groups, fostering a sense of belonging and mutual respect.
  • In professional environments, it highlights the importance of teamwork and the need to eliminate rivalry and competition that can hinder progress.

This mantra serves as a reminder that by uniting positive forces, seeking divine support, and removing enmity, we can create a harmonious and prosperous life for ourselves and those around us.
