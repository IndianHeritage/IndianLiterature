प्रस्तोक इनु राधसस् त इन्द्र दश कोशयीर्द दश वाजिनो दात् |दिवोदासाद अतिथिग्वस्य राधः शांबरं वसु प्रत्य अग्रभिष्म ||
prastoka in nu rādhasas ta indra daśa kośayīr daśa vājino 'dāt | divodāsād atithigvasya rādhaḥ śāmbaraṁ vasu praty agrabhīṣma ||
  • Mantra: "prastoka in nu rādhasas ta indra daśa kośayīr daśa vājino 'dāt | divodāsād atithigvasya rādhaḥ śāmbaraṁ vasu praty agrabhīṣma ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prastoka: a term that can refer to a kind of offering or invocation.
    • in nu: signifies a call or an appeal.
    • rādhasas: related to the concept of abundance or prosperity.
    • ta: means 'that' or 'those'.
    • indra: the king of gods, representing power and authority.
    • daśa: means 'ten', often symbolizing completeness or fullness.
    • kośayīr: refers to 'treasures' or 'wealth'.
    • vājino: means 'of the strong' or 'powerful'.
    • 'dāt: means 'to give' or 'to bestow'.
    • divodāsād: refers to a divine or celestial being.
    • atithigvasya: means 'of the guest' or 'of the host'.
    • rādhaḥ: signifies 'prosperity' or 'wealth'.
    • śāmbaraṁ: refers to 'abundance' or 'riches'.
    • vasu: means 'wealth' or 'goodness'.
    • praty: means 'towards' or 'in the direction of'.
    • agrabhīṣma: signifies 'the foremost' or 'the best'.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra, the king of gods, to bestow wealth and prosperity.
    • The mention of 'ten treasures' symbolizes a complete and fulfilling life, encompassing various aspects of wealth—material, spiritual, and emotional.
    • It emphasizes the importance of hospitality and generosity, as indicated by the reference to the guest and host relationship.
    • The mantra can be seen as a prayer for abundance not just for oneself but for the community and guests, reflecting the values of sharing and collective prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to the idea of ethical entrepreneurship, where a business not only seeks profit but also aims to contribute positively to society.
    • During festivals or family gatherings, invoking this mantra can remind individuals to be generous and welcoming to guests, ensuring that everyone feels valued and included.
    • In personal finance, the concept of 'ten treasures' can inspire individuals to diversify their investments and seek a balanced approach to wealth accumulation.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of wealth, generosity, and community.
    • Its significance lies in promoting a holistic view of prosperity that encompasses not just material wealth but also the richness of relationships and shared experiences.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to strive for abundance while being mindful of their responsibilities towards others, fostering a culture of giving and hospitality.
