उत स्य न उषिजाम उर्विय कवीर अहिः श्रिणोतु बुध्न्यो हवीमनि |सूर्यामासा विचरन्ता दिविक्षिता धियः शमीनहुशी अस्य बोधतम ||
uta sya na uśijām urviyā kavir ahiḥ śṛṇotu budhnyo3 havīmani | sūryāmāsā vicarantā divikṣitā dhiyā śamīnahuṣī asya bodhatam ||
  • uta sya na uśijām urviyā: This phrase can be translated as "May the wise one, who is the creator of the vast universe, listen to our call." Here, "uta" signifies a call or invocation, while "uśijām" refers to the wise or knowledgeable beings. "Urviya" denotes the vastness of the universe.
  • kavir ahiḥ śṛṇotu budhnyo3 havīmani: This part translates to "May the seer, the serpent, hear our offerings." "Kavir" refers to a seer or sage, "ahiḥ" means serpent, which can symbolize wisdom and transformation, and "budhnyo3" indicates the act of listening or understanding. "Havīmani" refers to our offerings or sacrifices.
  • sūryāmāsā vicarantā divikṣitā: This translates to "The sun, who travels through the sky, is the one we seek." "Sūrya" is the sun, "āmāsā" means to travel or move, and "divikṣitā" refers to the celestial or divine realm.
  • dhiyā śamīnahuṣī asya bodhatam: This can be interpreted as "May our intellect be awakened by the divine." "Dhiyā" refers to intellect or understanding, "śamīnahuṣī" signifies the divine or sacred, and "asya bodhatam" means to awaken or enlighten.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking wisdom and divine guidance in our lives. It highlights the connection between the vast universe, the sun, and our own intellect. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in moments of decision-making, seeking clarity, or during rituals where one seeks blessings for knowledge and understanding. The significance lies in recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the universe, encouraging us to seek wisdom and enlightenment in our daily lives.
