यā नः पीपरद अश्विनā ज्योतिष्मती तमस तिरः |ताम अस्मे रासाथाम इषम् ||
yā naḥ pīparad aśvinā jyotiṣmatī tamas tiraḥ | tām asme rāsāthām iṣam ||
  • yā naḥ: This phrase translates to "that which is ours" or "that which belongs to us." It signifies a sense of possession or connection to something greater.
  • pīparad: This word means "to bestow" or "to grant." It indicates an act of giving or providing something valuable.
  • aśvinā: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between opposites.
  • jyotiṣmatī: This term means "radiant" or "illuminated." It represents light, knowledge, and enlightenment, suggesting a higher understanding or awareness.
  • tamas: This word translates to "darkness" or "ignorance." It signifies the absence of knowledge or clarity, often associated with confusion or obstacles.
  • tiraḥ: Meaning "to cross over" or "to transcend." It implies moving beyond limitations or barriers.
  • tām: This word means "that" or "her," referring to the object of desire or the goal one wishes to achieve.
  • asme: This translates to "to us" or "for us," indicating a personal connection to the subject being discussed.
  • rāsāthām: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is the source of nourishment" or "the one who provides sustenance." It emphasizes the importance of support and sustenance in life.
  • iṣam: This word means "power" or "strength." It signifies the ability to act, influence, or achieve goals.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation for enlightenment and guidance. It expresses a desire for the Aśvins to bestow upon us the radiant light that helps us transcend darkness and ignorance. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in overcoming obstacles and gaining clarity in our lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: When facing challenges or confusion in life, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek knowledge and clarity, encouraging individuals to overcome their limitations.
  • Health and Well-being: The Aśvins are associated with health; thus, this mantra can be used in healing practices, invoking their blessings for physical and mental well-being.
  • Decision Making: In moments of uncertainty, this mantra can guide individuals to seek enlightenment and make informed choices, helping them navigate through life's complexities.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking light and knowledge to overcome darkness and ignorance. It serves as a powerful reminder of the divine support available to us in our journey towards personal growth, health, and clarity in decision-making.
