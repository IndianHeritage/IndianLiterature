इदं वाम आस्ये हविः प्रियं इन्द्राबृहस्पति | उक्तं मदश्च शस्यते ||
idaṁ vām āsye haviḥ priyam indrābṛhaspatī | uktham madaś ca śasyate ||
  • idaṁ - This word translates to "this" or "here." It signifies the present moment or the offering being made.
  • vām - Meaning "to you," it indicates a direction of reverence or devotion towards the deities being addressed.
  • āsye - This means "in the mouth" or "in the speech," suggesting the importance of words and expressions in rituals and prayers.
  • haviḥ - Refers to "offering" or "sacrifice," which is a central theme in Vedic rituals, symbolizing the act of giving and devotion.
  • priyam - This translates to "beloved" or "dear," indicating the affection and respect towards the deities.
  • indrābṛhaspatī - This compound word refers to the combined powers of Indra, the king of gods, and Brihaspati, the god of wisdom and eloquence. It signifies the invocation of strength and wisdom.
  • uktham - Meaning "utterance" or "saying," it emphasizes the significance of spoken words in rituals and their power to manifest intentions.
  • madaś - This word means "intoxication" or "joy," suggesting a state of bliss that comes from divine blessings.
  • ca - Simply means "and," connecting the ideas presented in the mantra.
  • śasyate - This translates to "is nourished" or "is cultivated," indicating growth and prosperity that comes from the divine blessings invoked.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation where the speaker offers their heartfelt devotion and respect to the deities, particularly Indra and Brihaspati. It emphasizes the importance of spoken words in rituals, suggesting that what we say holds power and can influence our reality. The offering made is not just material but also spiritual, as it seeks to cultivate joy and prosperity in life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: During religious ceremonies, individuals can recite this mantra to invoke blessings for strength and wisdom in their endeavors.
  • Public Speaking: The emphasis on the power of words can inspire individuals to be mindful of their speech, especially in public speaking or leadership roles, where their words can influence others.
  • Personal Growth: The idea of nourishing one's life with positive intentions can be applied in personal development practices, encouraging individuals to speak affirmatively and cultivate joy in their lives.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of offerings, the power of words, and the blessings of strength and wisdom in our lives. It encourages individuals to be mindful of their intentions and expressions, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and promoting personal and communal growth.
