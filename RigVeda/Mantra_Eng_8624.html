स वां यज्ञेषु मानवī इन्दुर जनिष्ट रोदसी | देवो देवī गिरिष्ठा अस्रेद्धन तं तुविष्वणि ||
sa vāṁ yajñeṣu mānavī indur janiṣṭa rodasī | devo devī giriṣṭhā asredhan taṁ tuviṣvaṇi ||
  • sa vāṁ yajñeṣu mānavī indur janiṣṭa rodasī
    • sa vāṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which is yours" or "that which belongs to you." It signifies a connection or relationship.
    • yajñeṣu: Refers to "sacrifices" or "rituals." In the Vedic context, yajnas are important for maintaining harmony in the universe.
    • mānavī: This term means "human" or "mankind." It emphasizes the role of humans in the cosmic order.
    • indur: Translates to "moon" or "lunar." The moon is often associated with nourishment, fertility, and the passage of time.
    • janiṣṭa: Means "born" or "created." It indicates the act of coming into existence or manifestation.
    • rodasī: Refers to "the two worlds" or "the heavens and the earth." It symbolizes the duality of existence.
  • devo devī giriṣṭhā asredhan taṁ tuviṣvaṇi
    • devo devī: This phrase means "gods and goddesses." It highlights the divine presence in all aspects of life.
    • giriṣṭhā: Translates to "the highest" or "the supreme." It signifies the ultimate reality or the highest state of being.
    • asredhan: Means "to be nourished" or "to be sustained." It indicates the importance of sustenance in life.
    • taṁ: Refers to "that" or "him." It points to the divine or the ultimate truth.
    • tuviṣvaṇi: This term can be interpreted as "in all beings" or "in the universe." It emphasizes the omnipresence of the divine.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the interconnectedness of humanity, the divine, and the cosmos. It emphasizes the importance of rituals and sacrifices in maintaining harmony and balance in life. In real-life scenarios, this can be seen in various cultural practices where rituals are performed to seek blessings, ensure prosperity, and foster community spirit. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that we are all part of a larger cosmic order, and our actions, whether in rituals or daily life, contribute to the overall harmony of existence. By recognizing this connection, individuals can cultivate a sense of responsibility towards themselves, others, and the environment, leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
