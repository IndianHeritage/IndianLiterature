उद वयं तमसस् परि ज्योतिष पश्यन्त उत्तराम् |देवं देवत्रा सूर्यं अगन्म ज्योतिर उत्तमम् ||
ud vayaṁ tamasas pari jyotiṣ paśyanta uttaram | devaṁ devatrā sūryam aganma jyotir uttamam ||
  • ud vayaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "we rise" or "we ascend." It signifies the act of moving upwards, both physically and spiritually.
  • tamasas: This word means "from darkness." It represents ignorance, confusion, or the challenges we face in life.
  • pari: This means "around" or "surrounding." It indicates the encompassing nature of our experiences and the world around us.
  • jyotiṣ: Translated as "light," it symbolizes knowledge, enlightenment, and clarity. Light is often associated with wisdom and understanding.
  • paśyanta: This means "seeing" or "perceiving." It emphasizes the importance of awareness and insight in our lives.
  • uttaram: This translates to "higher" or "superior." It suggests striving for a higher state of being or understanding.
  • devaṁ: This word means "divine" or "god." It represents the higher powers or forces that guide and influence our lives.
  • devatrā: This can be interpreted as "through the divine" or "by the gods." It indicates the connection between the divine and our earthly existence.
  • sūryam: This translates to "the sun." The sun is a powerful symbol of life, energy, and illumination.
  • aganma: This means "we have approached" or "we have come to." It signifies the act of seeking or striving towards something greater.
  • jyotir: Again, this means "light," reinforcing the theme of enlightenment and knowledge.
  • uttamam: This translates to "the highest" or "the best." It signifies the ultimate goal or aspiration in life.

In summary, this mantra speaks to the journey of rising from darkness to light, seeking higher knowledge and understanding through divine guidance. It emphasizes the importance of awareness and striving for a superior state of being. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, education, and spiritual development. For instance, when facing challenges or confusion, one can reflect on this mantra to find motivation to seek clarity and enlightenment. It encourages individuals to look beyond their immediate circumstances and aspire for greater wisdom and understanding, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and enlightened life.
