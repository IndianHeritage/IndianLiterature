त्यं चित पर्वतं गिरिं शतवंतं सहस्रिणम् |वि स्तोत्रिभ्यो रुरोजिथ ||
tyaṁ cit parvataṁ giriṁ śatavantaṁ sahasriṇam | vi stotṛbhyo rurojitha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tyaṁ: that
    • cit: which is known or perceived
    • parvataṁ: mountain
    • giriṁ: hill or peak
    • śatavantaṁ: having many hundred (or abundant)
    • sahasriṇam: having many thousands
    • vi: in various ways
    • stotṛbhyo: to the worshippers or those who praise
    • rurojitha: you have manifested or revealed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a great mountain, symbolizing strength, stability, and abundance.
    • The mountain is described as having countless peaks and being a source of inspiration for worshippers.
    • It suggests that the divine or the ultimate reality is vast and multifaceted, much like a mountain with many summits.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, mountains often represent challenges and obstacles that one must overcome, similar to life's difficulties.
    • Just as a mountain provides resources like water and minerals, individuals can draw strength and inspiration from their experiences and surroundings.
    • In a spiritual context, the mountain can symbolize the journey of self-discovery and enlightenment, where each peak represents a new level of understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its representation of the divine as a source of strength and abundance.
    • It encourages individuals to seek inspiration from the challenges they face, much like climbing a mountain.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in moments of struggle, reminding us to look for the strength within ourselves and the support of our surroundings.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us to appreciate the vastness of life and the many ways in which we can connect with the divine and the world around us.
