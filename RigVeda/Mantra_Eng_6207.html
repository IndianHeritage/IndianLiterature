विद्युद्धस्तā अभिद्यवः शिप्राः शीर्षण हिरण्ययीः | शुभ्रā व्य अञ्जत श्रीये ||
vidyuddhastā abhidyavaḥ śiprāḥ śīrṣan hiraṇyayīḥ | śubhrā vy añjata śriye ||
  • Vidyuddhastā: This term can be broken down into "vidyut," meaning "lightning" or "knowledge," and "dhastā," which implies "those who possess." Thus, "vidyuddhastā" refers to those who possess the brilliance of knowledge or enlightenment.
  • Abhidyavaḥ: This word can be interpreted as "those who are aware" or "those who have insight." It suggests a state of consciousness that goes beyond mere knowledge to a deeper understanding of reality.
  • Śiprāḥ: This term means "swift" or "quick." It indicates the speed at which knowledge or enlightenment can manifest in one's life, akin to the rapid flash of lightning.
  • Śīrṣan: This word translates to "with heads" or "with intellect." It emphasizes the importance of intellect and wisdom in the pursuit of knowledge.
  • Hiraṇyayīḥ: This term means "golden" or "radiant." It symbolizes the preciousness of knowledge and the value it holds in our lives.
  • Śubhrā: This word means "pure" or "auspicious." It signifies the purity of knowledge and the positive impact it can have on an individual's life.
  • Vy añjata: This phrase can be interpreted as "they are directed" or "they are guided." It suggests that knowledge and enlightenment guide individuals towards a better path.
  • Śriye: This term translates to "prosperity" or "wealth." It indicates that the ultimate goal of acquiring knowledge and enlightenment is to achieve prosperity in various aspects of life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of knowledge and enlightenment in achieving prosperity and a fulfilling life. It highlights the importance of possessing swift intellect and pure understanding, guiding individuals towards a path of wisdom. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in educational pursuits, personal development, and professional growth. By seeking knowledge and understanding, individuals can navigate challenges more effectively and attain success in their endeavors. The mantra serves as a reminder that true wealth lies in the enlightenment of the mind and spirit, leading to a prosperous and meaningful existence.
