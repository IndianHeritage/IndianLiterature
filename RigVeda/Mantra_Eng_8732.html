या उष्रियाऽप्यां अन्तर अश्मनो निर गा अकृतद ओजसा |अभि व्रजं तात्निषे गव्यं अश्व्यं वर्मिव धृष्णवाऽ रुज ||
ya usriyā apyā antar aśmano nir gā akṛntad ojasā | abhi vrajaṁ tatniṣe gavyam aśvyaṁ varmīva dhṛṣṇav ā ruja ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who
    • usriyā: with the dawn
    • apyā: even
    • antar: within
    • aśmano: of the stones
    • nir: without
    • gā: song
    • akṛntad: has cut
    • ojasā: with energy
    • abhi: towards
    • vrajaṁ: the herd
    • tatniṣe: that which is strong
    • gavyam: of the cows
    • aśvyaṁ: of the horses
    • varmīva: like a shield
    • dhṛṣṇav: with courage
    • ā: towards
    • ruja: pain
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power of dawn and the energy it brings.
    • The phrase "who with the dawn, even within the stones, has cut without song" suggests a force that operates quietly yet powerfully, akin to the way dawn breaks without fanfare.
    • The reference to "the herd" and "that which is strong" indicates a connection to nature and the importance of strength and vitality in life.
    • The imagery of "like a shield" conveys protection and resilience, emphasizing the need for courage in facing life's challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, the dawn symbolizes new beginnings. Just as the sun rises each day, we too can embrace new opportunities and challenges.
    • The strength of the herd can be likened to teamwork in a workplace, where collective effort leads to success.
    • Facing challenges with courage, much like a shield protects a warrior, is essential in overcoming personal and professional obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of energy, strength, and courage in our lives.
    • It teaches us to embrace new beginnings, work together for common goals, and face challenges with resilience.
    • In a world filled with uncertainties, the principles derived from this mantra can guide us in our personal growth and interactions with others.
