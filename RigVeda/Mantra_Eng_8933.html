मैनम् अग्नि विदहो माभि शोको मास्य त्वचं चिक्षिपो मा शरीरम् |यदा श्रुतं कृणवो जातवेदोऽथेम एनम् प्र हिणुतात् पितृभ्यः ||
mainam agne vi daho mābhi śoco māsya tvacaṁ cikṣipo mā śarīram | yadā śṛtaṁ kṛṇavo jātavedo 'them enam pra hiṇutāt pitṛbhyaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "mainam agne vi daho mābhi śoco māsya tvacaṁ cikṣipo mā śarīram | yadā śṛtaṁ kṛṇavo jātavedo 'them enam pra hiṇutāt pitṛbhyaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "O Agni, do not burn me with your flames; do not let my skin be scorched. When I call upon you, O Jātavedas, may you protect me and lead me to the ancestors."
  • Explanation:
    • Agni: In Vedic literature, Agni represents the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, fire can be seen as both a creator and a destroyer, much like the challenges we face that can either harm or help us grow.
    • Do not burn me: This plea signifies a desire for protection from harm. In modern life, this can relate to seeking safety in various aspects, such as emotional well-being, physical health, or even financial security.
    • Skin and body: The reference to skin and body emphasizes the physical aspect of existence. It reminds us to take care of our health and well-being, akin to how we protect ourselves from harmful situations or toxic relationships.
    • Jātavedas: This term refers to the all-knowing aspect of Agni, who is aware of all beings and their needs. In our lives, this can be likened to seeking guidance from a mentor or a wise figure who understands our struggles and can provide support.
    • Connection to ancestors: The invocation to ancestors highlights the importance of lineage and heritage. In contemporary society, this can be seen in the value placed on family traditions and the wisdom passed down through generations, which guide our decisions and values.
  • Significance: This mantra serves as a reminder of the dual nature of fire—its ability to purify and protect, as well as its potential to harm. It encourages us to seek balance in our lives, acknowledging the challenges we face while also calling for guidance and protection from higher powers or our own inner wisdom.
  • Real-life Application: In daily life, this mantra can be applied during moments of stress or uncertainty. For instance, when facing a difficult decision, one might recite this mantra to invoke clarity and protection, ensuring that their choices are aligned with their true self and values. It also encourages mindfulness about the influences we allow into our lives, promoting a protective stance against negativity.
