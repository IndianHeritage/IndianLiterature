अच्छा हि सोमः कलशाम् असीष्यदद अत्यो न वोळ्हा रघुवर्तनिर्वृषा |अथा देवानाम् उभयस्य जनमो विद्वाम् अशन्यत् अमुत इतः च यत् ||
acchā hi somaḥ kalaśām̐ asiṣyadad atyo na voḻhā raghuvartanir vṛṣā | athā devānām ubhayasya janmano vidvām̐ aśnoty amuta itaś ca yat ||
  • Mantra: "acchā hi somaḥ kalaśām̐ asiṣyadad atyo na voḻhā raghuvartanir vṛṣā | athā devānām ubhayasya janmano vidvām̐ aśnoty amuta itaś ca yat ||"
  • Translation: "Indeed, the Soma is the best of all offerings; it is the source of strength and prosperity. The one who knows the essence of both the divine and the mortal can attain the ultimate bliss and fulfillment."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • Acchā: Indeed, good or excellent.
    • Soma: A sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine energy.
    • Kalaśām̐: Of the pot or vessel, often used to hold offerings.
    • Asiṣyadad: To bestow or grant blessings.
    • Atyo: Beyond or transcending.
    • Na: Not.
    • Voḻhā: To be restrained or limited.
    • Raghuvartanir: The lineage of Raghu, symbolizing nobility and strength.
    • Vṛṣā: Strength or power.
    • Devānām: Of the gods or divine beings.
    • Ubhayasya: Of both, referring to the divine and the mortal.
    • Janmano: Of birth or creation.
    • Vidvām̐: The knowledgeable or wise one.
    • Aśnoty: Can attain or achieve.
    • Amuta: Immortality or eternal bliss.
    • Itas: From here or this place.
    • Ca: And.
    • Yat: That which.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of Soma can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom in modern life. Just as Soma is considered a source of strength, education and learning empower individuals to achieve their goals.
    • The idea of transcending limitations resonates with personal development. For instance, someone overcoming adversity, such as a person rising from poverty to success, embodies the essence of "atyō na voḻhā."
    • The notion of divine and mortal can be seen in the balance between spirituality and everyday life. Many people engage in practices like meditation or yoga to connect with their inner selves while navigating the challenges of the material world.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of understanding the interplay between the divine and the mortal. It signifies that true strength and fulfillment come from knowledge and the ability to transcend limitations. In real life, this can be applied through continuous learning, personal growth, and finding balance in our spiritual and material pursuits. By embodying the wisdom of this mantra, individuals can aspire to achieve their highest potential and experience lasting happiness.
