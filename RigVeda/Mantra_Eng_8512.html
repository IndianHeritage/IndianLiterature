एवā पुनानो अपः स्वार गā अस्मभ्यं तोकā तनयāनि भūरी |शं नः क्षेत्रम् उरु ज्योतींषि सोम ज्योङ नः सूर्यं दृष्टये रिरिहि ||
evā punāno apaḥ sva1r gā asmabhyaṁ tokā tanayāni bhūri | śaṁ naḥ kṣetram uru jyotīṁṣi soma jyoṅ naḥ sūryaṁ dṛśaye rirīhi ||
  • evā punāno apaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "may the waters be purified again." In many cultures, water symbolizes purity and life. For example, in real life, we often purify water for drinking, which is essential for health and well-being.
  • sva1r gā asmabhyaṁ: This means "may they come to us." It reflects a desire for blessings or positive energies to be bestowed upon us. In a practical sense, this can relate to seeking support from friends, family, or community during challenging times.
  • tokā tanayāni bhūri: This translates to "may we have many offspring." This can be interpreted as a wish for prosperity and the continuation of lineage. In modern life, this can relate to the importance of family and community, where raising children is seen as a way to ensure cultural and familial continuity.
  • śaṁ naḥ kṣetram: This means "may our field be prosperous." Fields symbolize not just agricultural land but also the broader concept of our endeavors and efforts. For instance, in a business context, this could mean wishing for success in our professional pursuits.
  • uru jyotīṁṣi: This translates to "great lights." This can refer to knowledge, wisdom, or enlightenment. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of education and personal growth, where individuals seek to illuminate their paths with knowledge.
  • soma jyoṅ naḥ: This means "may the soma (a sacred drink) be with us." Soma represents nourishment and vitality. In contemporary terms, this could relate to the importance of nutrition and health in our lives, emphasizing the need for a balanced diet.
  • sūryaṁ dṛśaye rirīhi: This translates to "may we see the sun." The sun symbolizes life, energy, and positivity. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as a desire for clarity and positivity in our lives, encouraging us to seek out the good and the bright aspects of our existence.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for purity, prosperity, knowledge, and positivity in life. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of our desires for health, family, success, and enlightenment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek balance in our lives, to nurture our relationships, and to pursue knowledge and well-being. It encourages us to appreciate the blessings we have and to strive for a brighter future, both individually and collectively.
