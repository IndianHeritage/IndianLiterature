अध स्म यस्यार्चयः सम्यक् संयन्ति धूमिनः |यद ईम अह त्रितो दिव्य उप ध्मातेव धमति शिशीते ध्मातरी यथा ||
adha sma yasyārcayaḥ samyak saṁyanti dhūminaḥ | yad īm aha trito divy upa dhmāteva dhamati śiśīte dhmātarī yathā ||
  • Adha sma yasyārcayaḥ samyak saṁyanti dhūminaḥ:
    • Adha: This word translates to "down" or "below," indicating a grounding or foundational aspect.
    • Sma: This is often used to denote "indeed" or "certainly," emphasizing the truth of the statement.
    • Yasyārcayaḥ: Refers to "those who worship" or "those who offer reverence," highlighting the importance of devotion.
    • Samyak: Means "properly" or "correctly," suggesting that worship should be done with sincerity and correctness.
    • Saṁyanti: This term means "they come together" or "they unite," indicating a gathering or collective effort.
    • Dhūminaḥ: Refers to "the smoky ones," which can symbolize the obscured or the hidden aspects of existence.
  • Yad īm aha trito divy upa dhmāteva dhamati:
    • Yad: Translates to "that" or "which," pointing to a specific quality or action.
    • Īm: This can mean "we" or "us," indicating a collective identity.
    • Aha: Refers to "I" or "my," suggesting a personal connection to the divine or the universal.
    • Trito: This word can mean "third," which may symbolize a triad or a balance of forces.
    • Divy: Means "divine" or "heavenly," indicating a connection to higher realms or spiritual truths.
    • Upa: Translates to "near" or "towards," suggesting an approach to the divine.
    • Dhmāteva: Refers to "as if blowing," which can symbolize the act of creation or inspiration.
    • Dhamati: Means "to blow" or "to inspire," indicating the act of bringing forth life or energy.
  • Śiśīte dhmātarī yathā:
    • Śiśīte: This word means "nourishes" or "sustains," indicating a nurturing aspect.
    • Dhmātarī: Refers to "the one who blows" or "the creator," often associated with the divine force of creation.
    • Yathā: Means "as" or "like," suggesting a comparison or analogy.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the importance of sincere worship and the collective effort of individuals coming together in reverence. It highlights the nurturing aspect of the divine, akin to a creator who sustains life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to community gatherings, spiritual practices, or any situation where individuals unite for a common purpose, fostering a sense of belonging and shared devotion. The significance lies in recognizing the divine presence in our collective actions and the importance of approaching life with sincerity and unity.
