त्वं नो अग्नि तव देव पायुभिर मघोनो रक्ष तन्वश च वन्द्य |त्रातā तोकस्य तनये गवाम अस्य अनिमेषं रक्षमानस तव व्रते ||
tvaṁ no agne tava deva pāyubhir maghono rakṣa tanvaś ca vandya | trātā tokasya tanaye gavām asy animeṣaṁ rakṣamāṇas tava vrate ||
  • tvaṁ no agne: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni." Agni is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, light, and purity. In real life, fire is essential for cooking, warmth, and rituals, representing transformation and sustenance.
  • tava deva pāyubhir: This means "may your divine powers protect us." It emphasizes the need for divine protection in our lives. For example, people often seek blessings for safety and well-being in their daily activities, such as before a journey or a significant event.
  • maghono rakṣa: This translates to "protect us with your abundance." It signifies the desire for prosperity and abundance in life. In modern contexts, this can relate to seeking financial stability or success in one's career.
  • tanvaś ca vandya: This means "and may we honor your body." It reflects the importance of respecting and honoring the divine presence in our lives. In practice, this can be seen in rituals and ceremonies where offerings are made to deities, acknowledging their significance.
  • trātā tokasya tanaye: This translates to "be the protector of our offspring." It highlights the importance of safeguarding future generations. In real life, this can relate to parental responsibilities, education, and creating a safe environment for children to thrive.
  • gavām asy animeṣaṁ: This means "of the cows, may you be vigilant." Cows are considered sacred in Vedic culture, symbolizing nourishment and wealth. In contemporary society, this can relate to the importance of sustainable agriculture and animal welfare.
  • rakṣamāṇas tava vrate: This translates to "protect us in your vow." It signifies the commitment to uphold one's promises and duties. In everyday life, this can be seen in the importance of integrity and reliability in personal and professional relationships.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of divine protection, abundance, and the responsibility towards future generations. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as seeking blessings for safety, honoring traditions, and ensuring the well-being of loved ones. By invoking Agni's presence, individuals can cultivate a sense of security and prosperity in their lives, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and the community around them.
