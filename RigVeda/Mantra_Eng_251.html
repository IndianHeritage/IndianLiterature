इदं आपः प्र वहत यत किं च दुरितं मयि |यद वाहम् अभिदुद्रोह यद वा शेप उतानृतम् ||
idam āpaḥ pra vahata yat kiṁ ca duritam mayi | yad vāham abhidudroha yad vā śepa utānṛtam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • idam: this
    • āpaḥ: waters
    • pra vahata: may flow forth
    • yat: whatever
    • kiṁ ca: and anything
    • duritam: misfortune or sin
    • mayi: in me
    • yad: whatever
    • vāham: I carry
    • abhidudroha: that which is harmful or obstructive
    • yad: whatever
    • vā: or
    • śepa: that which is false
    • utānṛtam: untruth or deceit
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a plea for purification and the removal of obstacles.
    • The speaker calls upon the waters, symbolizing cleansing and renewal, to wash away any misfortunes or sins that may reside within them.
    • It acknowledges the burdens one carries, whether they are harmful actions or falsehoods, and seeks to release them.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often face challenges and burdens, such as stress from work or personal relationships. This mantra can be seen as a reminder to seek mental and emotional cleansing through practices like meditation or therapy.
    • In a community setting, when conflicts arise, this mantra encourages open communication and honesty to clear misunderstandings and restore harmony.
    • In environmental contexts, the invocation of waters can symbolize the importance of clean water and the need to protect natural resources, reminding us of our responsibility towards nature.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for introspection and the desire for purification.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to community healing and environmental stewardship.
    • By embracing the essence of this mantra, individuals can strive to overcome their inner obstacles and contribute positively to their surroundings.
