अवावशन्त धीतयो वृषभस्याधि रेतसि |सूनोर वत्सस्य मातरः ||
avāvaśanta dhītayo vṛṣabhasyādhi retasi | sūnor vatsasya mātaraḥ ||
  • Mantra: "avāvaśanta dhītayo vṛṣabhasyādhi retasi | sūnor vatsasya mātaraḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • avāvaśanta: Those who are endowed with wisdom or intelligence.
    • dhītayo: The wise or learned ones.
    • vṛṣabhasya: Of the bull, symbolizing strength and power.
    • ādhi: In the context of, or upon.
    • retasi: In the essence or the vital energy.
    • sūnor: Of the son.
    • vatsasya: Of the calf or young one.
    • mātaraḥ: Mothers or nurturing figures.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the importance of wisdom and intelligence, particularly in the context of nurturing and strength.
    • The "bull" represents not just physical strength but also the strength of character and intellect.
    • The mention of "mothers" signifies the nurturing aspect of wisdom, suggesting that true intelligence is often cultivated in a caring environment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In educational settings, teachers (often seen as nurturing figures) play a crucial role in fostering the intelligence of their students, much like the "mātaraḥ" in the mantra.
    • In the workplace, leaders who embody wisdom and strength can guide their teams effectively, ensuring that the collective intelligence of the group is harnessed for success.
    • In family dynamics, parents who nurture their children with wisdom and strength help them grow into capable and intelligent individuals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom and nurturing in the development of strength and intelligence.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, including education, leadership, and family, highlighting the interconnectedness of wisdom, strength, and nurturing.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that true power lies not just in physical strength but in the wisdom that guides and nurtures future generations.
