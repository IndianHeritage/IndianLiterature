सुभगः स प्रयज्यवो मरुतो अस्तु मार्त्यः |यस्य प्रयांसी पर्षथ ||
subhagaḥ sa prayajyavo maruto astu martyaḥ | yasya prayāṁsi parṣatha ||
  • Subhagaḥ: This word translates to "auspicious" or "fortunate." It signifies the presence of good fortune and blessings in one's life.
  • Sa: This means "he" or "that," referring to a divine entity or force.
  • Prayajyavo: This term can be interpreted as "those who are engaged in sacrifice" or "those who perform rituals." It emphasizes the importance of actions and offerings in spiritual practices.
  • Maruto: This refers to the "Maruts," the storm deities in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and the forces of nature.
  • Astu: This means "let there be" or "may it be," expressing a wish or a prayer for something to manifest.
  • Martyaḥ: This translates to "mortal" or "human," indicating the human condition and the transient nature of life.
  • Yasya: This means "whose," indicating possession or belonging.
  • Prayāṁsi: This term refers to "sacrifices" or "offerings," highlighting the significance of giving and devotion in spiritual practices.
  • Parṣatha: This can be interpreted as "to be fulfilled" or "to be accomplished," suggesting the realization of desires or goals.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer for auspiciousness and blessings from the divine forces, particularly the Maruts, for those who engage in rituals and sacrifices. It acknowledges the human condition and the need for divine support in achieving one's goals and fulfilling desires.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra during personal development practices, such as meditation or goal-setting, to invite positive energy and support from the universe.
  • Community Rituals: In communal gatherings or religious ceremonies, this mantra can be chanted to invoke blessings for the collective well-being and success of the community.
  • Life Challenges: During difficult times, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder of the importance of resilience and the belief that divine forces are at work to assist in overcoming obstacles.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine blessings and the acknowledgment of the human experience. It serves as a reminder that through dedication, rituals, and a connection to the divine, individuals can navigate life's challenges and achieve their aspirations. By incorporating this mantra into daily life, one can cultivate a sense of purpose, positivity, and spiritual connection.
