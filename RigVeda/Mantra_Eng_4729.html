नू गृणानो गृṇते प्रात्न राजन्न इषः पिन्व वसुदेया पूर्वीः |अप ओषधीर अविषा वनानि गा अर्वतो नृन ऋचसे रिरिहि ||
nū gṛṇāno gṛṇate pratna rājann iṣaḥ pinva vasudeyāya pūrvīḥ | apa oṣadhīr aviṣā vanāni gā arvato nṝn ṛcase rirīhi ||
  • nū gṛṇāno gṛṇate: This phrase can be translated as "We sing praises" or "We are singing." It emphasizes the act of expressing gratitude and reverence through song.
  • pratna rājann: This means "to the king of the earth" or "to the ruler." It signifies respect towards authority and leadership, acknowledging the role of a king or leader in society.
  • iṣaḥ pinva: Translated as "may you fill," this phrase suggests a desire for abundance and prosperity. It reflects the aspiration for the leader to provide for the people.
  • vasudeyāya pūrvīḥ: This can be interpreted as "for the well-being of the land" or "for the prosperity of the earth." It highlights the connection between leadership and the welfare of the community and environment.
  • apa oṣadhīr: This means "from the herbs" or "from the plants." It indicates a relationship with nature and the importance of natural resources for sustenance.
  • aviṣā vanāni: Translated as "the forests," this phrase emphasizes the significance of natural habitats and ecosystems in providing resources and balance to life.
  • gā arvato nṝn: This means "to the men who are strong" or "to the brave." It acknowledges the valor and strength of individuals in society, particularly those who contribute to the community's well-being.
  • ṛcase rirīhi: This can be interpreted as "may you be nourished" or "may you thrive." It is a blessing for growth and prosperity, both for individuals and the community as a whole.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of leadership, community, and nature in achieving prosperity and well-being. It reflects a holistic view of life where the ruler is responsible for the welfare of the land and its people, and where nature plays a crucial role in sustaining life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as environmental conservation efforts, community leadership, and governance. It serves as a reminder that true leadership involves nurturing both the people and the environment, fostering a sense of responsibility towards the earth and its resources. By singing praises and expressing gratitude, individuals can cultivate a positive and harmonious relationship with their leaders and the natural world, ultimately leading to a thriving society.
