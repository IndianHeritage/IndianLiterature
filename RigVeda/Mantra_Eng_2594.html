अच्छिद्रां शर्म जरीतः पुरूणि देवां अच्‍छा दीद्यानाḥ सुमेधाḥ | रथो न सस्निर अभि वक्षि वाजम् अग्ने त्वं रोधसी नः सुमेके ||
acchidrā śarma jaritaḥ purūṇi devām̐ acchā dīdyānaḥ sumedhāḥ | ratho na sasnir abhi vakṣi vājam agne tvaṁ rodasī naḥ sumeke ||
  • acchidrā: This word translates to "unbroken" or "whole." It signifies completeness and integrity.
  • śarma: This means "comfort" or "shelter." It represents a safe haven or a protective space.
  • jaritaḥ: This term refers to "the one who is born" or "the one who has come into being." It can symbolize new beginnings or the emergence of new ideas.
  • purūṇi: This means "the ancient" or "the eternal." It connects to the idea of timeless wisdom and knowledge.
  • devām̐: This translates to "gods" or "divine beings." It indicates the presence of higher powers or spiritual entities.
  • acchā: This word means "good" or "auspicious." It signifies positivity and favorable conditions.
  • dīdyānaḥ: This term refers to "shining" or "radiant." It symbolizes enlightenment and clarity.
  • sumedhāḥ: This means "good intellect" or "wisdom." It represents the importance of knowledge and understanding.
  • ratho: This translates to "chariot." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent the journey of life or the means to achieve one's goals.
  • na: This means "not." It negates the following term.
  • sasnir: This term means "to be hindered" or "to be obstructed." It signifies obstacles or challenges.
  • abhi: This means "towards" or "in the direction of." It indicates movement or progress.
  • vakṣi: This translates to "to speak" or "to declare." It represents communication and expression.
  • vājam: This means "strength" or "power." It signifies the energy needed to overcome challenges.
  • agne: This refers to "O Agni," the fire god. Agni symbolizes transformation, purification, and the divine spark within.
  • tvaṁ: This means "you." It addresses the divine or the higher self.
  • rodasī: This translates to "the two worlds" or "the heavens and the earth." It signifies the connection between the spiritual and the material realms.
  • naḥ: This means "our." It indicates a collective identity or shared experience.
  • sumeke: This means "well-disposed" or "favorable." It signifies positive outcomes and good fortune.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking completeness, wisdom, and divine guidance in our lives. It encourages individuals to strive for a harmonious existence, free from obstacles, while being open to the wisdom of the ancient and the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during challenging times when one seeks clarity and direction, or when embarking on new ventures that require courage and intellect. By invoking the qualities of integrity, wisdom, and divine support, individuals can navigate their journeys with confidence and purpose.
