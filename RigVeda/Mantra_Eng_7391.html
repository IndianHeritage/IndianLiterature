मन्त्र: नह्य अन्यं बलाकरं मर्दितारं शतक्रतो |त्वं न इन्द्र मृळय ||
nahy a1nyam baḻākaram marḍitāraṁ śatakrato | tvaṁ na indra mṛḻaya ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • nahy: Not, no
    • a1nyam: Another, different
    • baḻākaram: One who is strong, powerful
    • marḍitāraṁ: One who is praised, revered
    • śatakrato: One who has a hundred powers, referring to Indra
    • tvaṁ: You
    • na: Not
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • mṛḻaya: To be appeased, to be calmed
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra expresses a plea or a declaration to Indra, the king of gods, emphasizing that there is no other powerful being like him.
    • The speaker acknowledges Indra's strength and power, indicating that he is the one who deserves reverence and praise.
    • It suggests a desire for Indra's favor and a request for him to be calm and benevolent towards the speaker.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team might invoke the spirit of collaboration and leadership, recognizing that a strong leader (akin to Indra) is essential for success. They may express gratitude and seek guidance from their leader to navigate challenges.
    • In personal relationships, individuals may acknowledge the strength and support of a partner or friend, recognizing their unique qualities and seeking their understanding and calmness during difficult times.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of recognizing and honoring strength and leadership in various aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek guidance from those who possess wisdom and power, whether in personal, professional, or spiritual contexts.
    • By acknowledging the strengths of others, we foster respect and collaboration, which can lead to more harmonious and productive relationships.
