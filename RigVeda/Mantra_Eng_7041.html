यस्यायं विश्व आर्यो दासः शेवधिपा अरिः |तिरश्चिद आर्ये रुशमे परीरवि तुभ्येत सो अज्ञते रयिः ||
yasyāyaṁ viśva āryo dāsaḥ śevadhipā ariḥ | tiraś cid arye ruśame parīravi tubhyet so ajyate rayiḥ ||
  • yasyāyaṁ: This word translates to "whose" or "of whom." It indicates possession or belonging.
  • viśva: Meaning "all" or "universal," it signifies inclusiveness and the entirety of existence.
  • āryo: This term refers to "noble" or "honorable," often associated with righteousness and virtue.
  • dāsaḥ: Translated as "servant" or "slave," it denotes a person in service, often implying humility and dedication.
  • śevadhipā: This means "the one who is the lord of the good," suggesting a figure of authority who embodies goodness.
  • ariḥ: Meaning "enemy," it represents opposition or challenges that one may face.
  • tiraś: This word translates to "beyond" or "across," indicating a sense of transcendence or moving past obstacles.
  • cid: Meaning "consciousness" or "awareness," it refers to the state of being aware or enlightened.
  • arye: This term can be interpreted as "noble" or "worthy," reinforcing the theme of virtue.
  • ruśame: This means "to be angry" or "to rage," indicating emotional responses that can arise in challenging situations.
  • parīravi: Translated as "to call out" or "to invoke," it suggests the act of seeking help or guidance.
  • tubhyet: Meaning "to you," it indicates a direction of reverence or supplication towards a higher power.
  • so: This word means "he" or "that," referring to a subject previously mentioned.
  • ajyate: Translated as "is invoked" or "is called upon," it signifies the act of seeking assistance or blessings.
  • rayiḥ: Meaning "wealth" or "prosperity," it represents abundance and the good things in life.

This mantra speaks to the relationship between the noble and the servant, emphasizing the importance of virtue and righteousness in overcoming challenges. It suggests that by invoking the higher powers and maintaining a noble character, one can transcend difficulties and attract prosperity.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In leadership roles, where a leader embodies noble qualities and serves their team, fostering an environment of respect and collaboration.
  • In personal development, where individuals strive to overcome their inner challenges (the "enemies") by cultivating awareness and seeking guidance.
  • In community service, where serving others with humility and dedication can lead to collective prosperity and well-being.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for nobility, service, and the pursuit of righteousness. It teaches that by embodying these qualities and seeking higher guidance, individuals can navigate life's challenges and attract abundance. This principle is applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to leadership and community engagement, highlighting the interconnectedness of virtue and prosperity.
