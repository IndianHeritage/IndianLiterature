शुचī ते चक्रे यात्यā व्यानो अक्ष आहतः |अनो मनस्मयं सूर्यारोहत प्रयती पतिम् ||
śucī te cakre yātyā vyāno akṣa āhataḥ | ano manasmayaṁ sūryārohat prayatī patim ||
  • śucī te cakre: The term "śucī" means pure or clean, and "cakre" refers to a wheel or cycle. This phrase can be interpreted as the purity of your intentions or actions that drive the cycle of life.
  • yātyā vyāno akṣa āhataḥ: "yātyā" means to go or move, "vyāno" refers to the winds or energies, "akṣa" means unbroken or eternal, and "āhataḥ" means struck or affected. This suggests that the pure intentions lead to a movement or journey that is influenced by the eternal energies of the universe.
  • ano manasmayaṁ: "ano" means small or subtle, "manasmayaṁ" refers to the mind or mental realm. This indicates that even the smallest thoughts or intentions can have a significant impact on our journey.
  • sūryārohat: "sūrya" means sun, and "arohaṭ" means to rise or ascend. This symbolizes enlightenment, growth, and the pursuit of higher knowledge or consciousness.
  • prayatī patim: "prayatī" means effort or striving, and "patim" refers to the lord or master. This emphasizes the importance of effort in reaching one's goals or attaining mastery over oneself.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the significance of purity in intentions and actions, the influence of subtle thoughts, and the importance of striving towards enlightenment and mastery. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, maintaining pure intentions can lead to positive outcomes in relationships and career.
  • In mindfulness practices, being aware of subtle thoughts can help in achieving mental clarity and emotional stability.
  • In spiritual growth, striving for enlightenment can guide individuals towards a more fulfilling and purposeful life.

Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder that our thoughts, intentions, and efforts shape our journey in life, encouraging us to cultivate purity and strive for higher consciousness.
