यद ध प्राचीर अजगन्तोरो मण्डूरधाणिकीः |हताऽ इन्द्रस्य शत्रवः सर्वे बुद्बुदयाशवः ||
yad dha prācīr ajagantoro maṇḍūradhāṇikīḥ | hatā indrasya śatravaḥ sarve budbudayāśavaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad - that which
    • dha - holds or contains
    • prācīr - the eastern direction
    • ajagantoro - of the one who has not been defeated
    • maṇḍūradhāṇikīḥ - the wealth of the one who is strong
    • hatā - having been defeated
    • indrasya - of Indra (the king of gods)
    • śatravaḥ - enemies
    • sarve - all
    • budbudayāśavaḥ - like bubbles or transient
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the strength and resilience of a powerful entity, possibly referring to Indra, who is often depicted as a warrior and protector.
    • The phrase "yad dha prācīr ajagantoro" suggests that there is something significant in the eastern direction, which can symbolize new beginnings or enlightenment.
    • The mention of "maṇḍūradhāṇikīḥ" indicates the wealth or resources that come from strength, emphasizing the importance of being strong to attain prosperity.
    • Furthermore, "hatā indrasya śatravaḥ" implies that all enemies of Indra have been defeated, highlighting the triumph of good over evil.
    • The term "budbudayāśavaḥ" suggests that the enemies are transient, like bubbles, which can be interpreted as a reminder that challenges and adversities are temporary.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to the idea that a strong and resilient company can overcome its competitors (enemies) and thrive in the market.
    • In personal life, individuals facing challenges can draw inspiration from this mantra, understanding that their struggles are temporary and that strength will lead to eventual success.
    • In a community setting, leaders can embody the qualities of Indra, using their strength to protect and uplift their community, ensuring that adversities are overcome.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of strength, resilience, and the transient nature of challenges.
    • It encourages individuals and communities to harness their inner strength to overcome obstacles and achieve prosperity.
    • In essence, it teaches that with determination and courage, one can defeat adversities and emerge victorious, much like Indra defeating his enemies.
