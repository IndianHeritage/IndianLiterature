त्रायन्ताम् इह देवास् त्रायताम् मरुतां गणः |त्रायन्तां विश्वा भूतानि यथायम् अरपाऽसत् ||
trāyantām iha devās trāyatām marutāṁ gaṇaḥ | trāyantāṁ viśvā bhūtāni yathāyam arapā asat ||
  • Mantra: "trāyantām iha devās trāyatām marutāṁ gaṇaḥ | trāyantāṁ viśvā bhūtāni yathāyam arapā asat ||"
  • Translation: "May the gods protect us here; may the Maruts (storm deities) protect us; may all beings protect us, just as the unmanifested (or the eternal) protects the transient."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • trāyantām: Protect
    • iha: Here
    • devās: Gods
    • marutāṁ: Of the Maruts (storm deities)
    • gaṇaḥ: Group or assembly
    • viśvā: All or universal
    • bhūtāni: Beings
    • yathāyam: Just as
    • arapā: Unmanifested or eternal
    • asat: Transient or temporary

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the protection of divine forces, emphasizing the importance of spiritual and physical safety in our lives.
    • The mention of "Maruts" signifies the forces of nature, which can be seen as a metaphor for the challenges and storms we face in life.
    • The phrase "viśvā bhūtāni" highlights the interconnectedness of all beings, suggesting that protection is not just an individual concern but a collective one.
    • The comparison between the "unmanifested" and the "transient" reflects the idea that while our physical existence is temporary, there is a deeper, eternal essence that provides stability and protection.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of crisis, such as natural disasters, communities often come together, invoking spiritual beliefs for protection and support, similar to the collective plea in this mantra.
    • Individuals facing personal challenges, such as health issues or emotional turmoil, may seek guidance and protection through prayer or meditation, reflecting the essence of seeking divine support.
    • Environmental movements often emphasize the protection of nature, resonating with the idea of the Maruts as guardians of the natural world.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking protection and support from higher powers, both in personal and communal contexts.
    • It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the need for collective well-being, urging us to look beyond ourselves for strength and guidance.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various situations, from personal struggles to community challenges, reinforcing the belief that we are never alone in our journey.
