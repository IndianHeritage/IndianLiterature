पर्जन्यवृद्धं महिषं तं सूर्यस्य दुहिताभरत् |तं गन्धर्वाः प्रत्य अगृभ्णन् तं सोमे रसम् आदधुर इन्द्रायन्दो परि श्रव ||
parjanyavṛddham mahiṣaṁ taṁ sūryasya duhitābharat | taṁ gandharvāḥ praty agṛbhṇan taṁ some rasam ādadhur indrāyendo pari srava ||
  • Parjanyavṛddham: This term refers to the increase or growth of rain or moisture. In real life, this can be related to the importance of rain for agriculture, as it is essential for crop growth and sustenance.
  • Mahiṣaṁ: This word means 'the great one' or 'the powerful one.' It can symbolize strength and resilience, akin to a strong leader or a mighty force in nature.
  • Sūryasya: This translates to 'of the Sun.' The Sun is often seen as a source of life and energy, representing vitality and growth in various cultures.
  • Duhitābharat: This phrase means 'the daughter of the Sun.' It can be interpreted as the nurturing aspect of nature, akin to how the sun nurtures life on Earth.
  • Gandharvāḥ: Referring to celestial beings or musicians, this term symbolizes harmony and beauty in the universe, akin to the arts and music that enrich human life.
  • Praty agṛbhṇan: This means 'they have grasped' or 'they have taken.' It signifies the act of receiving or accepting something valuable, much like how we accept knowledge or wisdom in our lives.
  • Some rasam: This refers to the essence or juice of the moon (Soma). In many cultures, the moon is associated with emotions and intuition, representing the softer, nurturing aspects of life.
  • Ādadhur: This means 'they have placed' or 'they have established.' It signifies the act of creating or building something meaningful, similar to how we build relationships or communities.
  • Indrāyendo: This term refers to Indra, the king of gods, and 'Indra's moon.' It symbolizes power and authority, akin to leadership in human society.
  • Pari srava: This means 'to flow all around.' It can be related to the interconnectedness of life, where everything is in a constant state of flow and change, much like the cycles of nature.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, the importance of nurturing elements like rain and sunlight, and the roles of strength, beauty, and leadership in life. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to our understanding of environmental sustainability, the significance of community and relationships, and the balance between power and nurturing. By recognizing these elements, we can strive for a harmonious existence that respects both nature and our social structures.
