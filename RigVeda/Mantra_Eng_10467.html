सम प्रेरेटे अनु वातस्य विष्ठा ऐनं गच्छन्ति समनं न योषाः | ताभिः सायुक सरथं देव ईयते 'स्य विश्वस्य भुवनस्य राजा ||
sam prerate anu vātasya viṣṭhā ainaṁ gacchanti samanaṁ na yoṣāḥ | tābhiḥ sayuk sarathaṁ deva īyate 'sya viśvasya bhuvanasya rājā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sam prerate: "together they inspire" - This suggests a collective motivation or encouragement.
    • anu vātasya: "following the wind" - This can symbolize following natural forces or the flow of life.
    • viṣṭhā: "place" - Refers to a specific location or position.
    • ainaṁ: "this" - Indicates something specific that is being referred to.
    • gacchanti: "they go" - Implies movement or progression.
    • samanaṁ: "together" - Emphasizes unity and collaboration.
    • na yoṣāḥ: "not the women" - This could imply a distinction between genders in a specific context.
    • tābhiḥ: "with them" - Refers to a group or collective.
    • sayuk: "joined" - Indicates a coming together or unification.
    • sarathaṁ: "with chariots" - Symbolizes readiness for action or movement.
    • deva: "God" - Represents divinity or higher powers.
    • īyate: "is invoked" - Suggests a calling upon or seeking assistance from the divine.
    • 'sya: "of this" - Refers to something previously mentioned.
    • viśvasya: "of the universe" - Indicates a universal or cosmic context.
    • bhuvanasya: "of the worlds" - Refers to different realms or dimensions of existence.
    • rājā: "king" - Symbolizes authority, leadership, or governance.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the power of collective inspiration and the importance of unity in achieving goals.
    • The reference to "following the wind" can be seen as an encouragement to adapt to circumstances and flow with life's changes.
    • The distinction made with "not the women" may highlight specific roles or responsibilities within a community or society.
    • The invocation of the divine suggests that in our endeavors, we seek guidance and support from higher powers.
    • The imagery of "chariots" indicates readiness for action, suggesting that we must prepare ourselves for the journey ahead.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team project at work, the collective motivation of team members can lead to successful outcomes, much like the "together they inspire" aspect of the mantra.
    • In nature, animals often move in herds or flocks, following the wind and adapting to their environment, which reflects the idea of "following the wind."
    • In community service, different roles are often assigned based on strengths, similar to the distinction made in the mantra.
    • In times of crisis, people often turn to their faith or spiritual beliefs for guidance, paralleling the invocation of the divine in the mantra.
    • Sports teams prepare and strategize before a game, embodying the readiness symbolized by "chariots."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, collective effort, and divine guidance in our pursuits.
    • It teaches us that by working together and adapting to circumstances, we can achieve greater success.
    • In real life, whether in personal relationships, professional settings, or community involvement, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide us toward harmonious and fruitful endeavors.
