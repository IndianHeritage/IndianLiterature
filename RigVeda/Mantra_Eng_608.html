अनुव्रताय रन्धयन्न अपवृतानाभूभिर इन्द्रः शनथयन्न अनाभुवः |वृद्धस्य चिद् वर्धतो द्यां इनक्षतः स्तवानो वम्रो विं जग्हान संधिः ||
anuvratāya randhayann apavratān ābhūbhir indraḥ śnathayann anābhuvaḥ | vṛddhasya cid vardhato dyām inakṣataḥ stavāno vamro vi jaghāna saṁdihaḥ ||
  • anuvratāya: This term can be understood as "to those who are devoted" or "to those who follow the path." It signifies the importance of dedication and commitment in one's life.
  • randhayann: This word means "to bind" or "to connect." It suggests the idea of unity and the importance of relationships in our lives.
  • apavratān: This refers to "those who are not following the path" or "those who have deviated." It highlights the challenges faced by individuals who stray from their commitments.
  • ābhūbhir: This term can be interpreted as "with the help of the divine" or "through the blessings of the gods." It emphasizes the role of divine support in our endeavors.
  • indraḥ: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership. His mention signifies the need for strong leadership in guiding others.
  • śnathayann: This means "to strengthen" or "to empower." It reflects the idea of uplifting others and providing support to those in need.
  • anābhuvaḥ: This term can be understood as "those who are without support" or "those who lack guidance." It points to the importance of mentorship and guidance in life.
  • vṛddhasya: This means "of the elder" or "of the wise." It signifies the value of wisdom and experience in guiding the younger generation.
  • cid: This term refers to "consciousness" or "awareness." It highlights the importance of being aware and mindful in our actions.
  • vardhato: This means "to grow" or "to flourish." It signifies the potential for growth and development in individuals and communities.
  • dyām: This refers to "the sky" or "the heavens," symbolizing higher aspirations and goals.
  • inakṣataḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who are vigilant" or "those who are watchful." It emphasizes the need for awareness and attentiveness in our pursuits.
  • stavāno: This means "to praise" or "to honor." It reflects the importance of gratitude and recognition in our relationships.
  • vamro: This term can be understood as "the one who protects" or "the guardian." It signifies the role of protectors in society.
  • vi: This means "in all" or "throughout." It emphasizes the universality of the message being conveyed.
  • jaghāna: This means "to strike" or "to overcome." It reflects the idea of overcoming challenges and obstacles in life.
  • saṁdihaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "to unite" or "to bring together." It signifies the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving common goals.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of dedication, unity, and guidance in our lives. It highlights the role of strong leadership and the importance of supporting one another in overcoming challenges. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as community building, mentorship programs, and leadership roles in organizations. By fostering a sense of commitment and collaboration, we can create a supportive environment that encourages growth and development for all individuals involved.
