अजातशत्रुम् अजराऽस्वर्वती अनु स्वधामिता दशमं ईयते |सुनोतन पचत ब्रह्मवाहसे पुरुष्टुताय प्रतरं दधातन ||
ajātaśatrum ajarā svarvaty anu svadhāmitā dasmam īyate | sunotana pacata brahmavāhase puruṣṭutāya prataraṁ dadhātana ||
  • ajātaśatrum: This term translates to "one who has no enemies" or "without adversaries." It signifies a state of peace and harmony, where an individual or entity is free from conflict.
  • ajarā: Meaning "immortal" or "everlasting," this word emphasizes the idea of eternal existence, transcending the limitations of time and decay.
  • svarvaty: This can be interpreted as "pervading the heavens" or "universal," indicating a connection to the divine or the cosmic order.
  • anu: This word means "following" or "after," suggesting a sequence or continuity in the context of the mantra.
  • svadhāmitā: This term refers to "self-sustaining" or "self-existent," highlighting the idea of independence and self-reliance.
  • dasmam: This translates to "the tenth," which can symbolize completeness or a higher state of being.
  • īyate: Meaning "is attained" or "is reached," this word indicates the achievement of a particular state or goal.
  • sunotana: This term means "well-known" or "renowned," suggesting recognition and respect in the community or society.
  • pacata: This translates to "cooking" or "preparing," which can symbolize the process of transformation or development.
  • brahmavāhase: This term refers to "the one who carries the essence of Brahman," indicating a connection to the ultimate reality or universal consciousness.
  • puruṣṭutāya: Meaning "to the one who is praised," this word signifies reverence and acknowledgment of greatness.
  • prataraṁ: This translates to "crossing over" or "transcending," suggesting the idea of overcoming obstacles or limitations.
  • dadhātana: Meaning "to bestow" or "to grant," this term indicates the act of giving or sharing blessings.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound philosophical concepts such as peace, immortality, self-sufficiency, and transcendence. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied to personal development and interpersonal relationships. For instance, striving to be "ajātaśatrum" can lead to a more harmonious life, free from unnecessary conflicts. Embracing the idea of being "ajarā" encourages individuals to focus on their spiritual growth and the pursuit of eternal truths. The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of self-reliance and the pursuit of higher ideals, ultimately guiding individuals towards a more fulfilling and meaningful existence.
