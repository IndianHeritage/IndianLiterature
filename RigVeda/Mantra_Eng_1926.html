अस्मे ऊ षु वृषाणां माद्येथाम् उत पाणीं हतम् ऊर्म्यां मदन्ता |श्रुतं मे अच्‍छोक्‍तिभिर् मतीनां एष्टा नराः निचेतारा च कर्णैः ||
asme ū ṣu vṛṣaṇā mādayethām ut paṇīm̐r hatam ūrmyā madantā | śrutam me acchoktibhir matīnām eṣṭā narā nicetārā ca karṇaiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asme: for us
    • ū: may (it) be
    • ṣu: in (the) good
    • vṛṣaṇā: with strength or vigor
    • mādayethām: may (it) delight
    • ut: and
    • paṇīm̐r: (with) hands
    • hatam: struck or hit
    • ūrmyā: by the waves
    • madantā: intoxicated or filled with joy
    • śrutam: heard or listened
    • me: my
    • acchoktibhir: with good speech
    • matīnām: of the minds
    • eṣṭā: desired or sought
    • narā: men or people
    • nicetārā: who are awake or alert
    • ca: and
    • karṇaiḥ: with ears
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a desire for strength and joy in our lives.
    • It emphasizes the importance of being alert and receptive to good thoughts and ideas.
    • The reference to "hands" and "waves" suggests an active engagement with the world, where we are encouraged to take action and embrace the experiences that come our way.
    • The phrase "with good speech" highlights the significance of communication and the impact of our words on ourselves and others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can inspire teamwork and collaboration, where individuals work together with strength and joy to achieve common goals.
    • In personal relationships, it encourages open communication and active listening, fostering deeper connections and understanding.
    • In educational environments, it can motivate students to engage actively in their learning, embracing challenges with enthusiasm and a positive mindset.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, joy, and awareness in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to be proactive, to communicate effectively, and to remain open to the experiences that life offers.
    • By embodying the principles expressed in this mantra, individuals can cultivate a more fulfilling and enriched life, both personally and collectively.
