तं इति सुहव्यं अङ्गिरः सुदेवं सहसो यहो |जनाः आहुः सुभरिषम् ||
tam it suhavyam aṅgiraḥ sudevaṁ sahaso yaho | janā āhuḥ subarhiṣam ||
  • tam - This word translates to "that" or "him," indicating a reference to a divine entity or principle.
  • it - This means "here" or "in this place," suggesting a connection to the present or immediate surroundings.
  • suhavyam - This can be interpreted as "well-formed" or "auspicious," referring to something that is beneficial or favorable.
  • aṅgiraḥ - A reference to the Angiras, a group of sages or seers known for their wisdom and knowledge in Vedic literature.
  • sudevaṁ - This means "good deity" or "auspicious god," indicating a divine presence that is benevolent and supportive.
  • sahaso - This translates to "with strength" or "powerfully," suggesting the presence of energy or force.
  • yaho - This word can mean "to whom" or "for whom," indicating a direction or purpose.
  • janā - This means "people" or "human beings," referring to the collective of humanity.
  • āhuḥ - This translates to "they say" or "they declare," indicating a consensus or common belief among people.
  • subarhiṣam - This can be interpreted as "well-spoken" or "auspiciously spoken," referring to words or expressions that are beneficial and positive.

The mantra can be understood as a call to recognize and invoke the auspicious qualities of a divine presence that is well-formed and powerful. It emphasizes the importance of collective human acknowledgment of this divine energy, which is beneficial and supportive in nature.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Community Gatherings: When people come together for a common purpose, invoking the qualities of unity and strength can lead to positive outcomes.
  • Personal Development: Individuals can reflect on their own strengths and the auspicious qualities they wish to cultivate, aligning themselves with positive energies.
  • Spiritual Practices: In rituals or meditative practices, this mantra can be used to invoke divine support and guidance, fostering a sense of connection with the higher self and the universe.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals and communities with divine energies that promote well-being and strength. By acknowledging and invoking these auspicious qualities, people can enhance their lives and the lives of those around them, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in their endeavors.
