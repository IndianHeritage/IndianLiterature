उप स्तृणीतमात्रये हिमेन घर्मम अश्विनाः |अन्ति षड्भूतु वाम अवः ||
upa stṛṇītam atraye himena gharmam aśvinā | anti ṣad bhūtu vām avaḥ ||
  • upa stṛṇītam: This phrase can be translated as "having spread out" or "having laid down." It signifies the act of preparation or creating a conducive environment.
  • atraye: This word refers to "for us" or "to us," indicating a personal or communal aspect of the mantra, suggesting that the blessings or actions are directed towards the speaker or the community.
  • himena: Translated as "with the cold" or "with the frost," this term evokes the idea of the natural elements, particularly the coldness of winter or the chill of the night.
  • gharmam: This means "warmth" or "heat," contrasting with the previous term. It symbolizes comfort, safety, and the nurturing aspects of warmth in life.
  • aśvinā: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They represent duality and balance in life.
  • anti: This word means "to bring" or "to lead," indicating an action of guidance or provision.
  • ṣad: Translated as "six," this number often symbolizes completeness or a holistic approach in Vedic texts.
  • bhūtu: This term refers to "beings" or "entities," encompassing all forms of life and existence.
  • vām: Meaning "your" or "of you," it personalizes the mantra, addressing the divine or the forces at play.
  • avaḥ: This means "bring" or "lead," similar to "anti," reinforcing the idea of guidance and provision.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for the Aśvins to bring warmth and comfort to the community, especially during cold times. It emphasizes the importance of creating a nurturing environment, both physically and spiritually. The duality of cold and warmth reflects the balance necessary in life, where challenges (cold) are met with support and comfort (warmth).


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Community Support: Just as the Aśvins are invoked for warmth, communities can come together to support each other during difficult times, such as during winter or economic hardships.
  • Healthcare: The Aśvins are associated with health; thus, this mantra can be a reminder of the importance of seeking medical help and maintaining well-being, especially during illness.
  • Environmental Awareness: The contrast between cold and warmth can also symbolize the need for balance in nature, urging individuals to take care of the environment to ensure a sustainable future.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of community, and the balance between challenges and support. It encourages individuals to seek warmth and comfort in their lives while also being a source of warmth for others, fostering a sense of unity and care in society.
