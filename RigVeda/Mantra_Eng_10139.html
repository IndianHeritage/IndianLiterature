एकपाद भूयो द्विपदो वि चक्रमे द्विपात त्रिपादम् अभ्य एति पश्चात् | चतुष्पाद एति द्विपदाम् अभिस्वरे सम्पश्यन् पङ्क्तीर उपतिष्ठमानः ||
ekapād bhūyo dvipado vi cakrame dvipāt tripādam abhy eti paścāt | catuṣpād eti dvipadām abhisvare sampaśyan paṅktīr upatiṣṭhamānaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ekapād: one-footed
    • bhūyo: again
    • dvipado: two-footed
    • vi: indeed
    • cakrame: moves or revolves
    • dvipāt: two-footed (again)
    • tripādam: three-footed
    • abhy: towards
    • eti: goes
    • paścāt: afterwards
    • catuṣpād: four-footed
    • eti: goes
    • dvipadām: of the two-footed
    • abhisvare: with great sound
    • sampaśyan: observing
    • paṅktīr: rows or lines
    • upatiṣṭhamānaḥ: standing or established
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra describes a cosmic process of evolution and movement through different forms of existence.
    • The terms "ekapād," "dvipado," "tripādam," and "catuṣpād" represent various stages of life or forms of beings, from one-footed to four-footed creatures.
    • It suggests a progression or transformation, where beings evolve from simpler forms to more complex ones.
    • The phrase "abhisvare sampaśyan" indicates a conscious observation of this evolution, highlighting the importance of awareness in the process of growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we can observe the evolution of species, where simpler organisms gradually develop into more complex forms, akin to the transition from one-footed to four-footed beings.
    • In human life, this mantra can be related to personal growth and development, where individuals start with basic skills and knowledge and progressively acquire more complex abilities and understanding.
    • In education, students begin with foundational concepts (ekapād) and advance to more intricate subjects (catuṣpād), emphasizing the importance of a structured learning process.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the continuous journey of evolution and growth in various forms of existence.
    • It teaches us that awareness and observation are crucial in understanding our own development and the world around us.
    • In real life, it encourages us to embrace change, recognize our progress, and strive for higher levels of understanding and existence.
