गन्ता नो यज्ञं यज्ञियाः सुशमि श्रोता हवम अरक्ष एवयामरुत् |ज्येष्ठासो न पर्वतासो व्योमनि यूयं तस्य प्रचेतसः स्यात दुर्धर्तवो निदः ||
gantā no yajñaṁ yajñiyāḥ suśami śrotā havam arakṣa evayāmarut | jyeṣṭhāso na parvatāso vyomani yūyaṁ tasya pracetasaḥ syāta durdhartavo nidaḥ ||
  • Meaning of the Mantra:
    • gantā: The one who leads or guides.
    • no: Our.
    • yajñaṁ: Sacrifice or offering.
    • yajñiyāḥ: Those who are engaged in the sacrifice.
    • suśami: Well-versed or skilled.
    • śrotā: The hearers or listeners.
    • havam: The invocation or call.
    • arakṣa: Without protection or care.
    • evayāmarut: O Wind Gods or divine forces.
    • jyeṣṭhāso: The elder or superior ones.
    • na: Not.
    • parvatāso: Mountains or great beings.
    • vyomani: In the sky or atmosphere.
    • yūyaṁ: You all.
    • tasya: Of that.
    • pracetasaḥ: With great wisdom or intelligence.
    • syāta: May be.
    • durdhartavo: Difficult to overcome or challenging.
    • nidaḥ: The source or origin.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine forces, particularly the Wind Gods, to guide and protect those who are engaged in sacred rituals or sacrifices.
    • It emphasizes the importance of skilled practitioners and attentive listeners in the process of spiritual offerings.
    • The mention of "elder ones" and "mountains" suggests a reverence for wisdom and strength, indicating that these qualities are essential in overcoming challenges.
    • The phrase "difficult to overcome" highlights the struggles one may face in life, and the need for divine assistance and guidance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team may face challenges in completing a project. The mantra can be interpreted as a call for collaboration and guidance from experienced team members (the "elder ones") to navigate these difficulties.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek spiritual guidance during tough times, invoking the support of higher powers or mentors to help them through their struggles.
    • In community rituals, the mantra can be recited to invoke collective strength and wisdom, ensuring that the efforts of all participants are harmonized for a successful outcome.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking guidance and support from both divine forces and wise individuals in our lives.
    • It highlights the significance of community, collaboration, and the need for skilled practitioners in any endeavor.
    • In essence, it encourages us to remain connected to our roots, seek wisdom, and face challenges with the support of those around us, ultimately leading to success in our personal and communal pursuits.
