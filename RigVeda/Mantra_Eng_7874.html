मन्त्र: एते सोमास आाशवो रथाऽ इव प्र वाजिनः |सर्गाः सृष्टा अहेषत ||
ete somāsa āśavo rathā iva pra vājinaḥ | sargāḥ sṛṣṭā aheṣata ||
  • Mantra: "ete somāsa āśavo rathā iva pra vājinaḥ | sargāḥ sṛṣṭā aheṣata ||"
  • Translation: "These are the invigorating (soma) drinks, like horses, that have been created for the purpose of bringing forth (the essence of life) and have been produced without hindrance."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ete: these
    • somāsa: invigorating drinks, often associated with the soma plant, which is considered sacred and energizing
    • āśavo: swift or powerful, often referring to horses
    • rathā: chariots or vehicles, symbolizing movement and progress
    • iva: like or as
    • pra: for the purpose of
    • vājinaḥ: those who bring forth or produce
    • sargāḥ: creations or manifestations
    • sṛṣṭā: created or produced
    • aheṣata: without hindrance or obstruction
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • The mantra speaks to the essence of life and vitality, symbolized by the soma drink, which is often associated with divine inspiration and rejuvenation.
    • Horses are a metaphor for strength and speed, indicating that the invigorating qualities of soma can propel individuals forward in their endeavors.
    • The reference to creations being produced without hindrance suggests a state of flow and abundance, where creativity and life can manifest freely.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of "soma" can be likened to practices that enhance well-being, such as meditation, yoga, or consuming nutritious foods that energize the body and mind.
    • The idea of being "like horses" can be seen in the pursuit of goals, where individuals harness their energy and drive to achieve success, much like athletes training for a competition.
    • Creativity in art, music, or innovation can be viewed as a manifestation of the "sargāḥ" or creations, where individuals express themselves freely and without barriers.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of vitality, creativity, and the unimpeded flow of life. It serves as a reminder to seek out sources of inspiration and nourishment that propel us forward.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to engage in activities that enhance their well-being and creativity, fostering an environment where ideas and life can flourish.
    • Ultimately, the mantra highlights the interconnectedness of life, energy, and creativity, urging us to embrace these elements in our daily lives for holistic growth and fulfillment.
