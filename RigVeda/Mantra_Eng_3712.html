सं यद इषो वनामहे सं हव्यā मानवशाणाम् |उत द्युम्नस्य शवस ऋतस्य रश्मिम आ ददे ||
saṁ yad iṣo vanāmahe saṁ havyā mānuṣāṇām | uta dyumnasya śavasa ṛtasya raśmim ā dade ||
  • saṁ yad iṣo vanāmahe: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke that which is worthy of worship." Here, "iṣo" refers to the divine or the supreme power, and "vanāmahe" means to invoke or to call upon. In real life, this can be seen in various rituals and prayers where individuals seek blessings or guidance from a higher power.
  • saṁ havyā mānuṣāṇām: This translates to "We offer our oblations to the mortals." "Havyā" refers to offerings, and "mānuṣāṇām" means of humans. This highlights the importance of community and the act of sharing and giving. For example, in many cultures, communal meals or offerings during festivals symbolize unity and gratitude.
  • uta dyumnasya: This means "and of glory." "Dyumna" signifies brightness or glory, suggesting that the invocation is not just for sustenance but also for achieving greatness. In a modern context, this can relate to striving for success in one’s career or personal life, where individuals seek to achieve their goals and shine in their endeavors.
  • śavasa ṛtasya raśmim ā dade: This translates to "may we receive the rays of truth." "Śavasa" refers to the essence or spirit, "ṛtasya" means of truth or order, and "raśmim" signifies rays or light. This part emphasizes the pursuit of truth and righteousness. In everyday life, this can be seen in the quest for knowledge, ethical living, and the importance of integrity in personal and professional relationships.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound invocation for divine guidance, community support, the pursuit of glory, and the quest for truth. Its significance lies in the holistic approach it advocates—recognizing the interconnectedness of the divine, humanity, and the pursuit of higher ideals. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in selfless acts, strive for personal excellence, and uphold truth and integrity in their lives. It serves as a reminder that our actions, guided by higher principles, contribute to the greater good of society.
