अवां नु कं ज्यायान यज्ञवनसो महीं त ओमात्रां कृष्टयो विदुः |असो नु कम अजरो वर्धाश च विश्वेद एता सवना तूतुमा कृषे ||
avā nu kaṁ jyāyān yajñavanaso mahīṁ ta omātrāṁ kṛṣṭayo viduḥ | aso nu kam ajaro vardhāś ca viśved etā savanā tūtumā kṛṣe ||
  • avā nu kaṁ jyāyān: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the divine or a higher power, seeking guidance or assistance. The word "jyāyān" suggests greatness or superiority, indicating a desire for wisdom or strength.
  • yajñavanaso mahīṁ: "Yajñavanaso" refers to the act of sacrifice or offering, which is a central theme in Vedic rituals. "Mahīṁ" means the earth or the world, suggesting a connection between the divine and the earthly realm.
  • ta omātrāṁ kṛṣṭayo viduḥ: This part implies that those who are knowledgeable or wise ("viduḥ") understand the essence of the offerings made to the divine. "Omātrāṁ" can be seen as a reference to the sacred sound "Om," which represents the universe and the ultimate reality.
  • aso nu kam ajaro: Here, "aso" means "not" or "without," and "ajaro" refers to immortality or eternal existence. This suggests a quest for something that transcends the physical realm, seeking the eternal truth.
  • vardhāś ca viśved: "Vardhāś" means growth or increase, while "viśved" refers to all beings or the universe. This indicates a desire for the flourishing of all life and the interconnectedness of existence.
  • etā savanā tūtumā kṛṣe: This closing phrase can be interpreted as a plea for blessings or support in the act of cultivation or creation ("kṛṣe"). "Savanā" refers to offerings or sacrifices, emphasizing the importance of gratitude and acknowledgment of the divine in our endeavors.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and understanding the interconnectedness of life through offerings and sacrifices. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom, growth, and the eternal nature of existence. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their actions and seek guidance from a higher power to make wise decisions.
  • In community settings, the mantra can inspire collective efforts towards growth and prosperity, recognizing the contributions of all members.
  • In environmental practices, it serves as a reminder of our responsibility to nurture the earth and acknowledge the divine in nature.

Overall, the mantra encourages a harmonious relationship with the universe, fostering a sense of gratitude and interconnectedness in our daily lives.
