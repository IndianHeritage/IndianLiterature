यो होतासीत् प्रथमो देवजुष्टो यं समाञ्जन्न आज्येन वृतानाः |स पतत्रीत्वरं स्था जगद यच्छ्वात्रम् अग्निरकृणोज जातवेदाः ||
yo hotāsīt prathamo devajuṣṭo yaṁ samāñjann ājyenā vṛṇānāḥ | sa patatrītvaraṁ sthā jagad yac chvātram agnir akṛṇoj jātavedāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • hotāsīt: was the first to invoke
    • prathamo: the first
    • devajuṣṭo: endowed with divine qualities
    • yaṁ: whom
    • samāñjann: by offering
    • ājyenā: with clarified butter
    • vṛṇānāḥ: the priests or those who perform the rituals
    • sa: he
    • patatrītvaraṁ: swift as a bird
    • sthā: stands
    • jagad: the world
    • yac: that which
    • shvātram: the covering or protection
    • agnir: fire
    • akṛṇoj: has created
    • jātavedāḥ: the one who knows all births
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine fire (Agni) as the first deity invoked in rituals.
    • Agni is portrayed as a swift and powerful force that protects and sustains the world.
    • The act of offering clarified butter (ājya) symbolizes the nourishment and respect given to the divine.
    • The priests (vṛṇānāḥ) play a crucial role in invoking the divine presence through their rituals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, the concept of invoking a higher power can be seen in various religious ceremonies where offerings are made to seek blessings.
    • The idea of protection and sustenance can be related to how communities come together to support each other in times of need, much like the role of Agni in the mantra.
    • Just as Agni is a source of light and warmth, individuals can be sources of inspiration and guidance in their communities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine forces in our lives, recognizing their role in providing protection and sustenance.
    • It highlights the significance of rituals and community participation in connecting with the divine.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to seek out and honor the sources of light and guidance in their lives, whether they be spiritual, communal, or personal.
