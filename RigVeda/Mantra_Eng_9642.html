किं स्विद वनं का उ स वृक्ष आस यतो द्यावापृथिवी निष्टतक्षुः |मनिषिणो मनसा पृच्छते उ तद यद अध्यतिष्ठद भुवनानि धारयन ||
kiṁ svid vanaṁ ka u sa vṛkṣa āsa yato dyāvāpṛthivī niṣṭatakṣuḥ | manīṣiṇo manasā pṛcchated u tad yad adhyatiṣṭhad bhuvanāni dhārayan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kiṁ svid: What is it?
    • vanaṁ: Forest or grove.
    • ka: Who?
    • u: Or.
    • sa: He/that.
    • vṛkṣa: Tree.
    • āsa: Is/exists.
    • yato: From where.
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: Heaven and Earth.
    • niṣṭatakṣuḥ: With steady vision.
    • manīṣiṇo: The wise or intelligent ones.
    • manasā: With the mind.
    • pṛcchated: Ask or inquire.
    • u: And.
    • tad: That.
    • yat: Which.
    • adhyatiṣṭhad: Has established.
    • bhuvanāni: Worlds or realms.
    • dhārayan: Sustaining or holding.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a series of profound questions about existence and the nature of reality.
    • It invites contemplation on the relationship between the natural world (represented by the forest and trees) and the cosmic order (heaven and earth).
    • The phrase "niṣṭatakṣuḥ" suggests a need for clarity and insight, urging individuals to seek understanding with a steady mind.
    • The wise, referred to as "manīṣiṇo," are encouraged to inquire deeply into the essence of existence and the interconnectedness of all things.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In environmental conservation, this mantra can inspire individuals to ask questions about the sustainability of forests and the importance of trees in maintaining ecological balance.
    • In personal development, it encourages self-inquiry and reflection, prompting individuals to seek deeper understanding of their purpose and place in the world.
    • In philosophical discussions, it can serve as a starting point for exploring the nature of reality, existence, and the universe.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of inquiry and understanding in both personal and collective contexts.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement to seek knowledge and wisdom about the world around us, fostering a sense of responsibility towards nature and existence.
    • In real life, it can be applied in various fields such as environmentalism, philosophy, and personal growth, reminding us to maintain a curious and reflective mindset.
