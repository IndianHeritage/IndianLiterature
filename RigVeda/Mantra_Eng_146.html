युक्ष्वा ह्य अरुशी रथे हरितो देव रोहितः |ताभिर देवां इह वहा ||
yukṣvā hy aruṣī rathe harito deva rohitaḥ | tābhir devām̐ ihā vaha ||
  • Mantra: "yukṣvā hy aruṣī rathe harito deva rohitaḥ | tābhir devām̐ ihā vaha ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yukṣvā: With the yoke or harnessed
    • hy: Indeed
    • aruṣī: Red or bright
    • rathe: In the chariot
    • harito: Green or flourishing
    • deva: God or divine being
    • rohitaḥ: Red or the one who is red
    • tābhir: With these
    • devām̐: The divine beings
    • ihā: Here or in this place
    • vaha: Carry or bring
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine forces, represented by the red and flourishing aspects of nature.
    • The imagery of a chariot suggests movement, progress, and the journey of life.
    • The use of "yukṣvā" indicates a call for harnessing these divine energies to aid in our endeavors.
    • The colors red and green symbolize vitality, growth, and the life force that sustains us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for divine blessings to ensure a bountiful harvest, reflecting the "harito" aspect of flourishing crops.
    • In personal development, individuals may seek motivation and strength (the "aruṣī" or bright energy) to overcome challenges, akin to harnessing divine support.
    • During festivals, communities come together to celebrate life and nature, embodying the essence of invoking divine energies for collective well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of connecting with divine energies to facilitate growth and progress in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our journeys; we can harness the support of the divine to navigate challenges.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to seek harmony with nature and the divine, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
