मन्त्र: विश्वेषाम इराज्यवो देवानां वार महः |विश्वे हि विश्वमहसो विश्वे यज्ञेषु यज्ञियाः ||
viśveṣām irajyavo devānāṁ vār mahaḥ | viśve hi viśvamahaso viśve yajñeṣu yajñiyāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viśveṣām: of all, universal
    • irajyavo: those who are worthy of worship
    • devānāṁ: of the gods
    • vār: glory, praise
    • mahaḥ: great, magnificent
    • viśve: all, everyone
    • hi: indeed
    • viśvamahaso: of all who are powerful
    • viśve: all, everyone
    • yajñeṣu: in sacrifices
    • yajñiyāḥ: those who are to be worshipped in sacrifices
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the universality of the divine and the importance of worshipping all gods.
    • It suggests that all beings, regardless of their power or status, are worthy of reverence and respect.
    • The mention of sacrifices indicates that rituals and offerings are a means to connect with the divine and acknowledge their greatness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, people celebrate festivals that honor various deities, showcasing the idea of universal worship.
    • Community gatherings, such as Diwali or Thanksgiving, can be seen as modern-day sacrifices where people come together to express gratitude and reverence.
    • Acts of kindness and respect towards all individuals, regardless of their background, reflect the essence of this mantra in daily life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the divine presence in all aspects of life.
    • It encourages inclusivity and respect for all beings, promoting harmony and unity in society.
    • By applying the teachings of this mantra, individuals can foster a sense of community and shared reverence, leading to a more compassionate and understanding world.
