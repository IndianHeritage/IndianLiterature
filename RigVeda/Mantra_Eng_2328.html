त्वादत्तेभिः रुद्र शान्तमेभिः शतं हिमाऽशीय भेषजैः |व्यस्मद् द्वेषो वितरं व्यंहो व्यमीवाश्चातयस्व विषूचिः ||
tvādattebhī rudra śaṁtamebhiḥ śataṁ himā aśīya bheṣajebhiḥ | vy a1smad dveṣo vitaraṁ vy aṁho vy amīvāś cātayasvā viṣūcīḥ ||
  • tvādattebhī: This word can be broken down into "tvā" meaning "you" and "dattebhī" which refers to "those who give." This suggests a plea or invocation directed towards a divine entity, likely Rudra, asking for blessings or assistance.
  • rudra: Rudra is a significant deity in the Rigveda, often associated with storms, wind, and the hunt. He embodies both destructive and protective qualities, representing the dual nature of existence.
  • śaṁtamebhiḥ: This term can be understood as "with peace" or "calmness." It indicates a desire for tranquility and harmony, which is essential in overcoming chaos and turmoil in life.
  • śataṁ: Meaning "hundred," this word often symbolizes abundance or a multitude of blessings. It suggests a request for numerous benefits or protections.
  • himā: This word translates to "cold" or "frost." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent challenges or hardships that one may face in life.
  • aśīya: This term means "to be free from" or "to be liberated." It indicates a desire for relief from suffering or difficulties.
  • bheṣajebhiḥ: This word refers to "medicines" or "healing." It signifies the need for remedies or solutions to life's problems, emphasizing the importance of healing in both physical and emotional contexts.
  • vy a1smad: This can be interpreted as "our" or "of us," indicating a collective identity or community aspect in the invocation.
  • dveṣo: Meaning "hatred" or "enmity," this word highlights the negative emotions that can arise in human interactions, suggesting a plea for protection against such feelings.
  • vitaraṁ: This term means "to distribute" or "to spread." It indicates a desire for the divine to share blessings and peace among people.
  • vy aṁho: This can be understood as "our" or "of us," reinforcing the communal aspect of the mantra.
  • vy amīvāś: This phrase can be interpreted as "our enemies" or "those who oppose us," suggesting a plea for protection against adversaries.
  • cātayasvā: This term means "to be free from" or "to be liberated," indicating a desire for freedom from negativity and conflict.
  • viṣūcīḥ: This word can be understood as "disease" or "affliction," emphasizing the need for healing and protection from various forms of suffering.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound plea for peace, healing, and protection from adversities. It acknowledges the dual nature of existence, represented by Rudra, and seeks blessings for abundance and tranquility. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during times of personal crisis, communal conflicts, or health challenges. By invoking the essence of this mantra, individuals and communities can strive for harmony, healing, and the dispelling of negativity, fostering a more peaceful and supportive environment.
