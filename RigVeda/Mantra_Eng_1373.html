ममत्तु नः परिज्मा वसर्हा ममत्तु वातो अपां वृषण्वान् |शिशीतम इन्द्रापर्वतā युवं नस् तन् नो विश्वे वरिवस्यन्तु देवाः ||
mamattu naḥ parijmā vasarhā mamattu vāto apāṁ vṛṣaṇvān | śiśītam indrāparvatā yuvaṁ nas tan no viśve varivasyantu devāḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May the winds and waters, which are powerful, protect us. May the youthful Indra, the mountain, bless us, and may all the gods bestow their abundance upon us."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • mamattu: May it be ours
    • naḥ: for us
    • parijmā: protection or covering
    • vasarhā: powerful or strong
    • vāto: winds
    • apāṁ: waters
    • vṛṣaṇvān: powerful or strong
    • śiśītam: youthful or fresh
    • indrāparvatā: Indra, the mountain
    • yuvaṁ: youthful
    • nas: us
    • tan: that
    • no: our
    • viśve: all
    • varivasyantu: may bestow abundance
    • devāḥ: gods
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra invokes the natural elements—winds and waters—as powerful forces that can provide protection and abundance.
    • Indra, often regarded as the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizes strength and vitality. His association with mountains suggests stability and support.
    • The plea for blessings from all gods emphasizes the interconnectedness of various divine forces in ensuring prosperity and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for favorable weather conditions, recognizing the importance of winds and waters for crop growth. This mantra reflects that sentiment.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies, people seek blessings from deities for protection and abundance, mirroring the essence of this mantra.
    • In times of crisis, communities may come together to invoke divine protection, similar to the collective appeal made in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of nature and divine forces in our lives. It encourages us to seek harmony with the environment and to recognize the blessings that come from both natural and spiritual realms.
    • Its application in real life is evident in various cultural practices, emphasizing the need for protection, abundance, and the acknowledgment of higher powers in our daily existence.
