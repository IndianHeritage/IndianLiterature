सप्त धामानि पर्यन्न अमर्त्यो दाशद दाशुषे सुखृतं मामहस्व |सुवीरेंण रयिणाग्ने स्वाभुवां यस् त आनाṭ समिधा तं जुषस्व ||
sapta dhāmāni pariyann amartyo dāśad dāśuṣe sukṛte māmahasva | suvīreṇa rayiṇāgne svābhuvā yas ta ānaṭ samidhā taṁ juṣasva ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "The seven realms are enveloped by the immortal; the tenfold offerings are for the one who is worthy. With the valorous and abundant wealth, O Agni, may you accept the offerings that are made to you."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Sapta dhāmāni: Refers to the seven realms or dimensions of existence, which can symbolize various aspects of life and the universe.
    • Pariyann: Means enveloped or surrounded, indicating the omnipresence of the divine or the spiritual.
    • Amartyo: Translates to immortal, representing the eternal nature of the soul or the divine.
    • Dāśad dāśuṣe: Refers to the tenfold offerings, which can symbolize the various aspects of life that one should honor and nurture.
    • Sukṛte: Means good deeds or righteousness, emphasizing the importance of virtuous actions.
    • Māmahasva: Translates to "may you be great" or "may you be honored," a call for recognition and respect.
    • Suvīreṇa: Means with valor or strength, indicating the importance of courage in life.
    • Rayiṇāgne: Refers to wealth or abundance, invoking Agni, the fire god, as a symbol of prosperity.
    • Svābhuvā: Means self-generated or inherent, suggesting that true wealth comes from within.
    • Yas ta ānaṭ: Translates to "those who are worthy," indicating the importance of deserving what one seeks.
    • Samidhā: Refers to offerings or sacrifices made to the divine.
    • Taṁ juṣasva: Means "may you enjoy" or "may you accept," a plea for the divine to accept the offerings made.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, the concept of "sapta dhāmāni" can be likened to the various departments within a company, each contributing to the overall success of the organization.
    • The idea of "amartyo" can be reflected in the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, which transcends physical existence and contributes to one's legacy.
    • Engaging in "sukṛte" can be seen in community service, where individuals perform good deeds to uplift society, thereby creating a positive impact.
    • The invocation of "Agni" can be related to the importance of passion and drive in achieving one's goals, as fire symbolizes energy and transformation.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of various realms, the importance of virtuous actions, and the need for courage and strength in pursuing one's goals. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to honor all aspects of existence, to act righteously, and to seek abundance not just materially but also spiritually. By embodying these principles, individuals can lead a fulfilling and meaningful life, contributing positively to their communities and the world at large.
