अनानुदो वृषभो जग्मिर आवहं निष्टप्ता शत्रुम पृथनासु सासहिḥ |असि सत्य ऋणया ब्रह्मणस पत उग्रस्य चिद दामिता वीळुहर्षिणः ||
anānudo vṛṣabho jagmir āhavaṁ niṣṭaptā śatrum pṛtanāsu sāsahiḥ | asi satya ṛṇayā brahmaṇas pata ugrasya cid damitā vīḻuharṣiṇaḥ ||
  • anānudo: This term can be interpreted as "without a rival" or "unmatched." It signifies a state of being unparalleled or unique.
  • vṛṣabho: Translated as "the bull," this word symbolizes strength, power, and virility. In many cultures, the bull is a representation of fertility and agricultural prosperity.
  • jagmir: This means "to come forth" or "to emerge." It indicates the act of rising or manifesting, often associated with new beginnings or the arrival of something significant.
  • āhavaṁ: This term refers to "invocation" or "calling." It is often used in the context of summoning divine forces or energies.
  • niṣṭaptā: Meaning "burnt" or "scorched," this word can symbolize purification through trials or challenges.
  • śatrum: This translates to "enemy" or "opponent." It represents obstacles or challenges that one may face in life.
  • pṛtanāsu: This term can be understood as "in battles" or "in struggles." It emphasizes the context of conflict or competition.
  • sāsahiḥ: Meaning "with strength" or "with power," this word highlights the importance of resilience and fortitude in overcoming challenges.
  • asi: This translates to "you are" or "you exist," affirming presence and existence.
  • satya: Meaning "truth," this term emphasizes the importance of honesty and integrity in one's actions and thoughts.
  • ṛṇayā: This can be interpreted as "debt" or "obligation," often referring to the responsibilities one has towards others or the universe.
  • brahmaṇas: This term refers to "the divine" or "the ultimate reality," indicating a connection to higher spiritual truths.
  • pata: Meaning "lord" or "master," this word signifies authority and guidance.
  • ugrasya: This translates to "fierce" or "powerful," often associated with strong forces of nature or divine energies.
  • cid: Meaning "consciousness" or "awareness," this term emphasizes the importance of being aware and mindful.
  • damitā: This translates to "controlled" or "restrained," indicating the importance of self-discipline.
  • vīḻuharṣiṇaḥ: This term can be understood as "the one who brings joy" or "the one who is blissful," representing the ultimate state of happiness and fulfillment.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of overcoming challenges with strength and truth. It emphasizes the importance of resilience in the face of adversities, invoking divine support and guidance. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as personal struggles, professional challenges, or even in the pursuit of spiritual growth. By embodying the qualities of strength, truth, and awareness, individuals can navigate through life's battles with grace and emerge victorious, ultimately leading to a state of bliss and fulfillment.
