शतैर अपद्रान पणय इन्द्रात्र दशोणये कवये 'र्कसातौ |वधैः शुष्णस्याशुषस्य मायाः पित्वो नारिरेचीत किं चन प्र ||
śatair apadran paṇaya indrātra daśoṇaye kavaye 'rkasātau | vadhaiḥ śuṣṇasyāśuṣasya māyāḥ pitvo nārirecīt kiṁ cana pra ||
  • śatair apadran: This phrase can be interpreted as "with a hundred obstacles" or "with many challenges." It signifies the numerous difficulties one may face in life.
  • paṇaya: This word refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." It highlights the importance of material and spiritual wealth in overcoming challenges.
  • indrātra: This term relates to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership. It suggests seeking divine assistance in times of need.
  • daśoṇaye: This can be translated as "to the ten directions," indicating a holistic approach to life, encompassing all aspects and directions.
  • kavaye: This word means "to the wise" or "to the poet," suggesting the importance of wisdom and creativity in navigating life's challenges.
  • 'rkasātau: This phrase can be interpreted as "in the realm of the sun," symbolizing enlightenment, clarity, and the pursuit of knowledge.
  • vadhaiḥ: This means "with slaughters" or "with sacrifices," indicating that overcoming obstacles often requires effort and sacrifice.
  • śuṣṇasyāśuṣasya: This can be understood as "of the dry" or "of the barren," representing the challenges of scarcity or lack in life.
  • māyāḥ: This term refers to "illusion" or "deception," reminding us to be aware of the illusions that can distract us from our goals.
  • pitvo: This word means "having drunk" or "having consumed," suggesting the importance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual.
  • nārirecīt: This can be interpreted as "not to be rejected" or "not to be abandoned," emphasizing the need for perseverance and resilience.
  • kiṁ cana pra: This phrase means "anything at all," indicating openness to possibilities and opportunities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of facing life's challenges with wisdom, strength, and resilience. It teaches us that while obstacles may be numerous, seeking divine guidance and maintaining a holistic approach can lead to prosperity and enlightenment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied when facing difficulties in personal or professional life, reminding us to stay focused, seek knowledge, and not shy away from sacrifices. It encourages us to embrace challenges as opportunities for growth and to remain open to the possibilities that life presents.
