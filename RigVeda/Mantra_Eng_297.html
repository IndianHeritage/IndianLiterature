स्वग्नयो हि वारणं देवासो दधिरे च नः |स्वग्नयो मनामहे ||
svagnayo hi vāryaṁ devāso dadhire ca naḥ | svagnayo manāmahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • svagnayo: Referring to the divine or celestial beings, often interpreted as the gods or deities.
    • hi: Indeed or certainly, used for emphasis.
    • vāryaṁ: Strength, power, or might.
    • devāso: Of the gods or divine beings.
    • dadhire: They have bestowed or granted.
    • ca: And.
    • naḥ: To us or for us.
    • manāmahe: We meditate upon or we invoke.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra expresses a reverence towards the divine beings, acknowledging their strength and the blessings they bestow upon humanity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of invoking the divine for strength and guidance in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal struggle, individuals often turn to prayer or meditation, seeking strength and guidance from a higher power, similar to the invocation in this mantra.
    • In various cultures, rituals are performed to honor deities, asking for their blessings and support in overcoming challenges, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support and strength, reminding us of the interconnectedness between humanity and the divine.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals seek empowerment, guidance, and support, whether in personal challenges, community efforts, or spiritual practices.
    • By meditating on this mantra, one can cultivate a sense of strength and connection to the divine, fostering resilience in the face of adversity.
