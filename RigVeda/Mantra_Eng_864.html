तं उ त्वा वृत्रहन्तमं यो दस्यूम् अवधूनुशे |द्यम्नैर अभि प्रणोनुमः ||
tam u tvā vṛtrahantamaṁ yo dasyūm̐r avadhūnuṣe | dyumnair abhi pra ṇonumaḥ ||
  • tam u tvā: This phrase can be translated as "to you" or "to that." It signifies a direction of reverence or focus towards a divine entity or force.
  • vṛtrahantamaṁ: This term refers to "the slayer of Vritra." In Vedic mythology, Vritra is a serpent or dragon that represents chaos and obstruction. The slayer of Vritra symbolizes the overcoming of obstacles and the restoration of order.
  • yo dasyūm̐r avadhūnuṣe: This part translates to "who has vanquished the Dasyus." The Dasyus are often depicted as malevolent beings or forces that oppose the divine order. This line emphasizes the triumph over negativity and evil.
  • dyumnair abhi pra ṇonumaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "we praise with glory." It indicates an act of worship or acknowledgment of the divine power that brings victory and prosperity.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of overcoming challenges and adversities in life. It serves as a reminder of the power of divine intervention in defeating chaos and negativity.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, individuals often face obstacles, whether they are personal struggles, professional challenges, or societal issues. This mantra can be invoked as a source of strength and motivation to confront and overcome these difficulties.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of faith and resilience in the face of adversity. By recognizing and invoking the divine power that helps in overcoming challenges, individuals can find the courage to face their own "Vritras" and "Dasyus," leading to a more fulfilling and victorious life.
