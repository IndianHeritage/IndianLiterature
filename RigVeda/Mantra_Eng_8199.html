अभि वेना अनूषतेयक्षन्ति प्रचेतसः |मज्जन्त्य अविचेतसः ||
abhi venā anūṣateyakṣanti pracetasaḥ | majjanty avicetasaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "abhi venā anūṣateyakṣanti pracetasaḥ | majjanty avicetasaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • venā: the one who is leading or guiding
    • anūṣate: those who are following or adhering
    • yakṣanti: they are able to perceive or understand
    • pracetasaḥ: those who are wise or knowledgeable
    • majjanty: they are immersed or absorbed
    • avicetasaḥ: those who are not aware or conscious
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the dynamics between those who are guided and those who follow.
    • The phrase "abhi venā" suggests a leader or guiding force that directs others.
    • "anūṣate" indicates the followers who are adhering to this guidance.
    • The wise individuals, referred to as "pracetasaḥ," are capable of understanding the guidance provided.
    • In contrast, "avicetasaḥ" refers to those who are not aware or conscious, suggesting a state of ignorance or lack of understanding.
    • The mantra highlights the importance of wisdom and awareness in following guidance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a manager (the leader) provides direction to their team (the followers). The team members who are knowledgeable (pracetasaḥ) can effectively implement the manager's strategies, while those who lack awareness (avicetasaḥ) may struggle to understand their roles.
    • In education, a teacher (the guiding force) imparts knowledge to students (the followers). Students who engage actively and seek to understand (pracetasaḥ) benefit greatly, while those who are disengaged (avicetasaḥ) may miss out on valuable learning opportunities.
    • In personal development, a mentor can guide an individual towards self-improvement. Those who are open to learning (pracetasaḥ) will grow, while those who resist change (avicetasaḥ) may remain stagnant.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of guidance, wisdom, and awareness in various aspects of life.
    • It teaches us that following a leader or a guiding principle is essential, but understanding and awareness are crucial for effective implementation.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in professional, educational, or personal contexts, being aware and knowledgeable allows individuals to make the most of the guidance they receive.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to seek wisdom and remain conscious in our pursuits, ensuring that we are not merely following but truly understanding and engaging with the guidance provided.
