दशस्यन्तो नो मरुतो मृळन्तु वरिवस्यन्तो रोधसी सुमेके |आरे गोहां नृहा वधो वो अस्तु सुम्नैभिर अस्मे वसवो नमध्वम् ||
daśasyanto no maruto mṛḻantu varivasyanto rodasī sumeke | āre gohā nṛhā vadho vo astu sumnebhir asme vasavo namadhvam ||
  • daśasyanto: This term can be broken down into "daśa" meaning ten and "syanto" meaning end or limit. It signifies completeness or a full cycle.
  • no maruto: "no" means "our" and "maruto" refers to the Maruts, the storm deities associated with wind and storms. This phrase invokes the blessings of these powerful forces.
  • mṛḻantu: This word means "may they be gentle" or "may they calm down." It suggests a desire for peace and tranquility amidst chaos.
  • varivasyanto: "varivas" means "to flow" or "to be abundant," and "syanto" again refers to the end. This can be interpreted as a call for abundance and prosperity.
  • rodasī: This term refers to the "two worlds" or "the sky and the earth," symbolizing the connection between the divine and the earthly realms.
  • sumeke: This means "well-disposed" or "favorable." It indicates a wish for positive outcomes and harmony.
  • āre gohā: "āre" means "to bring" and "gohā" refers to "cattle" or "wealth." This phrase emphasizes the importance of prosperity and sustenance.
  • nṛhā: This term means "humans" or "mankind," highlighting the connection between divine blessings and human existence.
  • vadho vo astu: "vadho" means "may there be" and "vo" means "for you." This phrase expresses a wish for the well-being of the Maruts and, by extension, humanity.
  • sumnebhir: This word means "with good intentions" or "with auspiciousness," reinforcing the desire for positive outcomes.
  • asme vasavo: "asme" means "we" and "vasavo" refers to the "Vasus," another group of deities associated with abundance and prosperity. This phrase calls for their blessings.
  • namadhvam: This means "we bow" or "we honor," indicating reverence and respect towards the divine forces invoked.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a profound invocation for peace, abundance, and divine blessings. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of the divine forces (Maruts and Vasus) with human existence, highlighting the importance of harmony between nature and humanity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during rituals for prosperity, during times of distress to seek calmness, or in communal gatherings to foster a sense of unity and shared purpose. Its significance lies in the recognition of the need for balance and the acknowledgment of the forces that govern our lives, urging us to live in harmony with the world around us.
