शृणोतु न ऊर्जाम् पतिर गिरः स नभस तरीयाम् इषिरः परिज्मा |शृण्वन्तु आपः पुरो न शुभ्राः परि स्रुको बबृहाणस्याद्रेः ||
śṛṇotu na ūrjām patir giraḥ sa nabhas tarīyām̐ iṣiraḥ parijmā | śṛṇvantv āpaḥ puro na śubhrāḥ pari sruco babṛhāṇasyādreḥ ||
  • śṛṇotu: This word means "may he hear" or "let him listen." It emphasizes the importance of listening and being receptive to knowledge and wisdom.
  • na ūrjām: "na" means "not," and "ūrjām" refers to "energy" or "strength." Together, it suggests a plea for the absence of obstacles to strength.
  • patir: This translates to "lord" or "master," indicating a higher power or divine presence that is being addressed.
  • giraḥ: This means "words" or "speech," highlighting the significance of communication and expression in our lives.
  • sa nabhas: "sa" means "he" and "nabhas" refers to "the sky" or "the heavens," suggesting a connection to the divine or cosmic order.
  • tarīyām̐: This word means "to cross" or "to transcend," indicating a journey or movement beyond current limitations.
  • iṣiraḥ: This translates to "the powerful" or "the strong," referring to a force that aids in overcoming challenges.
  • parijmā: This means "to encompass" or "to surround," suggesting protection and support from all sides.
  • śṛṇvantv: This is a call for others to listen or pay attention, reinforcing the communal aspect of wisdom sharing.
  • āpaḥ: This word means "waters," symbolizing purity, nourishment, and life.
  • puro: This translates to "in front" or "before," indicating a presence that is guiding or leading.
  • na śubhrāḥ: "na" means "not," and "śubhrāḥ" refers to "pure" or "auspicious," suggesting a desire for clarity and positivity.
  • pari sruco: This means "to flow all around," indicating abundance and the free flow of resources or blessings.
  • babṛhāṇasyādreḥ: "babṛhāṇa" means "of the great" or "the vast," and "ādreḥ" refers to "mountain" or "peak," symbolizing stability and strength.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of listening, the pursuit of strength, and the connection to divine wisdom. It calls for the removal of obstacles to energy and encourages a communal sharing of knowledge. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork, where effective communication and collective strength are essential for overcoming challenges. It also highlights the significance of seeking guidance from higher powers or wisdom in our endeavors, reminding us to remain open to learning and growth. The mantra serves as a reminder that by listening and being receptive, we can transcend our limitations and achieve greater heights in our personal and communal lives.
