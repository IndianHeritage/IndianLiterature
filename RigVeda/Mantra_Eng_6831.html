यस्मिन विश्वानि काव्याः चक्रे नाभिः इव श्रिता | तृतं जूती सपर्यत व्रजे गावो न संयुज्यते युज्यते अश्वाम् आयुक्षत नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
yasmin viśvāni kāvyā cakre nābhir iva śritā | tritaṁ jūtī saparyata vraje gāvo na saṁyuje yuje aśvām̐ ayukṣata nabhantām anyake same ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasmin: in which
    • viśvāni: all, everything
    • kāvyā: poetic, creative
    • cakre: have created
    • nābhir: like a navel
    • iva: as if
    • śritā: supported, upheld
    • tritaṁ: the third
    • jūtī: the one who is united
    • saparyata: with reverence
    • vraje: in the pasture
    • gāvo: cows
    • na: not
    • saṁyuje: to be joined
    • yuje: to be united
    • aśvām̐: horses
    • ayukṣata: they were not separated
    • nabhantām: from the sky
    • anyake: other
    • same: in the same way
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a cosmic unity where everything is interconnected, much like how the navel connects the body to the mother.
    • The reference to "cows" and "horses" symbolizes abundance and strength, respectively, which are essential for sustenance and progress in life.
    • The phrase "with reverence" indicates the importance of respect and gratitude towards nature and its resources.
    • In a real-life context, this can be seen in sustainable farming practices where farmers respect the land and its resources, ensuring that they do not exploit them but rather work in harmony with nature.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and interconnectedness in the universe.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the resources we have and to treat them with respect, which is crucial in today's world where environmental degradation is a pressing issue.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages collaboration and harmony among individuals and communities, reminding us that we are all part of a larger whole.
