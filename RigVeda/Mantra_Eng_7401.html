आ तु न इन्द्र क्षुमन्तं चित्रं ग्राभं सं गृभाय |महाहस्ती दक्षिणेन ||
ā tū na indra kṣumantaṁ citraṁ grābhaṁ saṁ gṛbhāya | mahāhastī dakṣiṇena ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: From
    • tū: You
    • na: Not
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • kṣumantaṁ: The one who is hungry or in need
    • citraṁ: Colorful or diverse
    • grābhaṁ: The one who grasps or holds
    • saṁ: Together
    • gṛbhāya: To grasp or to hold firmly
    • mahāhastī: The great elephant
    • dakṣiṇena: With the right hand or direction
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the deity associated with strength, rain, and thunder, asking him to not let the hungry or needy go unfulfilled.
    • The term "citraṁ" suggests diversity and richness, indicating that the blessings sought are not just material but also encompass various aspects of life.
    • The phrase "mahāhastī dakṣiṇena" symbolizes the strength and power of the elephant, often associated with wisdom and memory, suggesting that one should approach challenges with strength and wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied when addressing the needs of the less fortunate. For instance, during a food drive, invoking the spirit of this mantra can remind volunteers to ensure that no one goes hungry.
    • In personal life, when facing challenges, one can reflect on the strength of the elephant and the support of Indra, encouraging resilience and the pursuit of diverse solutions to problems.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of compassion and support for those in need, urging individuals to grasp opportunities and challenges with strength and wisdom.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that we should not only seek our own fulfillment but also ensure that others are cared for, fostering a sense of community and shared responsibility.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can guide actions in social service, personal resilience, and community building, promoting a holistic approach to life’s challenges.
