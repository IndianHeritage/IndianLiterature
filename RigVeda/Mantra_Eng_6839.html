आ वां ग्रावाणो अश्विनाऽ धीभिर् विप्रा अचुच्यवुः |नासत्यā सोमपीतये नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
ā vāṁ grāvāṇo aśvinā dhībhir viprā acucyavuḥ | nāsatyā somapītaye nabhantām anyake same ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vāṁ: "We invoke you" - a call to the divine.
    • grāvāṇo: "who are the protectors" - referring to the Aśvins as guardians.
    • aśvinā: "O Aśvins" - the twin deities associated with health and medicine.
    • dhībhir: "with wisdom" - invoking intelligence and insight.
    • viprā: "the wise ones" - referring to the learned or enlightened individuals.
    • acucyavuḥ: "have come forth" - indicating the arrival or manifestation of the divine.
    • nāsatyā: "O Nāsatyas" - another name for the Aśvins, emphasizing their dual nature.
    • somapītaye: "for the sake of the soma" - soma being a sacred drink that symbolizes immortality and divine connection.
    • nabhantām: "to the heavens" - a reference to the celestial realm.
    • anyake: "other beings" - indicating the presence of other entities or forces.
    • same: "in harmony" - a call for unity and balance.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the Aśvins, the divine twin brothers in Vedic mythology, who are often associated with healing, health, and the dawn.
    • The invocation of wisdom and the wise indicates a desire for enlightenment and guidance in life.
    • The reference to soma highlights the importance of spiritual nourishment and the quest for immortality, both physically and spiritually.
    • The mention of harmony suggests a need for balance in our lives, whether in personal relationships, community interactions, or within ourselves.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern healthcare, the Aśvins can be seen as symbols of medical professionals who strive to heal and protect the well-being of individuals.
    • In personal development, seeking wisdom and guidance from mentors or wise individuals can lead to better decision-making and a more fulfilling life.
    • Practices such as meditation and yoga can be viewed as modern-day equivalents of soma, providing spiritual nourishment and a connection to the divine.
    • In community building, fostering harmony and understanding among diverse groups can lead to a more peaceful and cooperative society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine guidance and wisdom in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek balance and harmony in our interactions with others and the world around us.
    • By recognizing the significance of spiritual nourishment and the pursuit of knowledge, we can lead more meaningful and enriched lives.
    • Ultimately, the mantra encourages us to connect with the divine, seek wisdom, and strive for harmony in all aspects of life.
