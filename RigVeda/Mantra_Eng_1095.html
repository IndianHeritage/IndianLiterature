तं ऊतयो रणयञ्च चूरसातौ तं क्षेमस्य क्षितयः कृत्वता त्राम् |स विश्वस्य करुणस्येश एको मरुत्वान नो भवत्व इन्द्र ऊती ||
tam ūtayo raṇayañ chūrasātau taṁ kṣemasya kṣitayaḥ kṛṇvata trām | sa viśvasya karuṇasyeśa eko marutvān no bhavatv indra ūtī ||
  • tam ūtayo raṇayañ chūrasātau: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the divine for strength and valor in battles. The word "ūtayo" refers to the act of rising or elevating, while "raṇayañ" signifies the battlefield or war. "Chūrasātau" implies the presence of courageous warriors. In real life, this can be related to situations where individuals or communities face challenges that require bravery, such as standing up against injustice or fighting for a cause.
  • taṁ kṣemasya kṣitayaḥ kṛṇvata trām: Here, "kṣemasya" refers to safety or well-being, and "kṣitayaḥ" means the earth or the world. "Kṛṇvata" suggests the act of creating or establishing. This line emphasizes the importance of creating a safe and secure environment for all. In contemporary terms, this can relate to efforts in community building, disaster management, or creating safe spaces for vulnerable populations.
  • sa viśvasya karuṇasyeśa eko marutvān: "Viśvasya" means universal or all-encompassing, while "karuṇasyeśa" refers to the lord of compassion. "Eko marutvān" suggests the singularity of the divine force that governs the winds and storms. This line highlights the idea that compassion is a universal principle that should guide our actions. In real life, this can be seen in humanitarian efforts, where individuals and organizations come together to help those in need, regardless of their background.
  • no bhavatv indra ūtī: "No bhavatv" translates to "may it be for us," and "indra ūtī" refers to the blessings of Indra, the king of gods, often associated with strength and power. This closing line is a prayer for divine support and blessings in our endeavors. In practical terms, this can be related to seeking guidance and strength in personal or collective challenges, such as pursuing education, career goals, or social justice initiatives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message of seeking strength, safety, compassion, and divine support in our lives. Its significance lies in the universal themes of courage in the face of adversity, the importance of creating safe environments, and the need for compassion in our actions. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to work together towards common goals, whether in personal struggles, social justice movements, or community development initiatives. By embodying these principles, we can contribute to a more harmonious and compassionate world.
