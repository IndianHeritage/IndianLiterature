यस्य दूतः असि क्षये वेषि हव्यानि वीटये |तस्मात् कृणोष्य अध्वरम् ||
yasya dūto asi kṣaye veṣi havyāni vītaye | dasmat kṛṇoṣy adhvaram ||
  • Mantra: "yasya dūto asi kṣaye veṣi havyāni vītaye | dasmat kṛṇoṣy adhvaram ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • dūto: messenger or envoy
    • asi: you are
    • kṣaye: in destruction or in the end
    • veṣi: you who are present
    • havyāni: offerings or oblations
    • vītaye: for the purpose of liberation or release
    • dasmat: therefore
    • kṛṇoṣy: you should perform
    • adhvaram: the sacrifice or ritual
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine presence and the role of a messenger in the context of sacrifice.
    • The phrase "of whom you are the messenger" suggests a connection between the divine and the individual, emphasizing the importance of communication and intention in rituals.
    • The mention of "destruction" and "offerings" indicates that sacrifices are made not just for material gain but for spiritual liberation and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the idea of being a messenger of peace and goodwill. For instance, when someone advocates for environmental conservation, they act as a messenger for the Earth, promoting the idea of sustainability.
    • In personal relationships, being a good communicator can be seen as fulfilling the role of a messenger, where one conveys feelings and intentions clearly to avoid misunderstandings.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often act as messengers of the company's vision, ensuring that all employees are aligned with the goals and values of the organization.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of intention and communication in rituals and daily life.
    • It teaches us that our actions, whether in spiritual practices or everyday interactions, should be aimed at fostering understanding and connection.
    • By recognizing ourselves as messengers, we can strive to contribute positively to our communities and the world, ultimately seeking liberation from ignorance and fostering harmony.
