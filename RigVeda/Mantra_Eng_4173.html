अज्येष्ठासो अकनिष्ठासा एते सम भ्रातरः वावृधुः सौभागाय |युवा पिता स्वपā रुद्र एषां सुदुघा पृष्णिः सुदिनां मरुद्भ्यः ||
ajyeṣṭhāso akaniṣṭhāsa ete sam bhrātaro vāvṛdhuḥ saubhagāya | yuvā pitā svapā rudra eṣāṁ sudughā pṛśniḥ sudinā marudbhyaḥ ||
  • ajyeṣṭhāso: This term can be broken down into "aj" meaning "not" and "yeṣṭha" meaning "the best" or "the elder." It refers to those who are not the eldest or the most prominent.
  • akaniṣṭhāsa: This word combines "a" meaning "not" and "kaniṣṭha" meaning "the youngest" or "the least." It signifies those who are neither the youngest nor the least.
  • ete: This translates to "these," indicating a group of individuals or entities being referred to.
  • sam bhrātaro: "Sam" means "together," and "bhrātaro" means "brothers." This phrase emphasizes unity among the brothers.
  • vāvṛdhuḥ: This term means "they have grown" or "they have increased," suggesting a sense of development or prosperity.
  • saubhagāya: This translates to "for good fortune" or "for auspiciousness," indicating a positive outcome or blessing.
  • yuvā: Meaning "young" or "youthful," it refers to the vitality and energy of youth.
  • pitā: This means "father," representing authority, guidance, and protection.
  • svapā: This term can be interpreted as "dream" or "vision," suggesting aspirations or goals.
  • rudra: A significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with storms and the hunt, symbolizing power and transformation.
  • eṣāṁ: This means "of these," referring back to the group mentioned earlier.
  • sudughā: This translates to "very difficult" or "hard to achieve," indicating challenges that may arise.
  • pṛśniḥ: This term refers to "the earth" or "the ground," symbolizing stability and foundation.
  • sudinā: Meaning "with good days" or "auspicious days," it signifies favorable circumstances.
  • marudbhyaḥ: This refers to "the winds" or "the storm gods," representing dynamic forces in nature.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and growth among individuals, particularly brothers, while acknowledging the challenges they may face. It highlights the role of guidance (father) and aspirations (dreams) in achieving good fortune and stability. In real life, this can be applied to family dynamics, teamwork in professional settings, and community building, where collaboration and support lead to collective success. The invocation of divine forces (like Rudra and the Maruts) suggests that seeking blessings and guidance from higher powers can aid in overcoming difficulties and achieving prosperity.
