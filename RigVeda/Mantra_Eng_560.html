त्रिषधस्थे बर्हिषि विश्ववेदसां मध्वा यज्ञं मिमिक्षतम् |कण्वासो वां सुतसोमा अभिद्यवो युवां हवन्ते अश्विनाः ||
triṣadhasthe barhiṣi viśvavedasā madhvā yajñam mimikṣatam | kaṇvāso vāṁ sutasomā abhidyavo yuvāṁ havante aśvinā ||
  • Mantra: "triṣadhasthe barhiṣi viśvavedasā madhvā yajñam mimikṣatam | kaṇvāso vāṁ sutasomā abhidyavo yuvāṁ havante aśvinā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • triṣadhasthe: Refers to the threefold nature or aspects, possibly indicating the three realms of existence: physical, mental, and spiritual.
    • barhiṣi: This term relates to the sacred grass used in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and the connection to the divine.
    • viśvavedasā: Means "with the knowledge of the universe," suggesting a comprehensive understanding of all that exists.
    • madhvā: Translates to "sweetness," indicating the pleasant and harmonious aspects of life and offerings.
    • yajñam: Refers to the sacrificial offering or ritual, a central theme in Vedic practices that signifies devotion and connection to the divine.
    • mimikṣatam: Implies the act of seeking or desiring, particularly in the context of spiritual or material fulfillment.
    • kaṇvāso: Refers to the wise or learned individuals, possibly the sages or seers who guide others.
    • vāṁ: Means "your," indicating a personal connection or address to the divine or the audience.
    • sutasomā: Refers to the "sons of Soma," which can symbolize the divine or celestial beings associated with the moon and intoxication, representing inspiration and creativity.
    • abhidyavo: Means "those who are aware" or "those who know," indicating a state of enlightenment or understanding.
    • yuvāṁ: Translates to "you both," addressing the duality of existence or the partnership between the divine and humanity.
    • havante: Means "they invoke" or "they call upon," indicating the act of prayer or supplication.
    • aśvinā: Refers to the Aśvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology associated with health, medicine, and the dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and healing.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all aspects of life—physical, mental, and spiritual. In real life, this can be seen in holistic practices that integrate body, mind, and spirit for overall well-being.
    • The invocation of the Aśvins highlights the importance of seeking health and healing. In modern contexts, this can relate to the pursuit of medical knowledge and the importance of wellness in our lives.
    • The concept of yajña (sacrifice) is relevant in today's world as it encourages selflessness and the idea of giving back to the community, whether through charitable acts or environmental stewardship.
    • The mantra serves as a reminder to seek knowledge and understanding, encouraging individuals to pursue education and wisdom in their personal and professional lives.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking harmony and balance in life through knowledge, sacrifice, and divine connection. Its teachings are applicable in various aspects of modern life, from personal health to community service, emphasizing the timeless relevance of Vedic wisdom in fostering a fulfilling and meaningful existence.
