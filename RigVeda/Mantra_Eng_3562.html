मन्त्र: इन्द्राबृहस्पति वयं सुतैः गीरिभिः हवामहे | अस्य सोमस्य पीतये ||
indrābṛhaspatī vayaṁ sute gīrbhir havāmahe | asya somasya pītaye ||
  • Indrābṛhaspatī: This term refers to the combined powers of Indra, the king of gods, and Brihaspati, the guru of the gods. Indra symbolizes strength, power, and leadership, while Brihaspati represents wisdom and knowledge.
  • Vayaṁ: This word means "we" or "us," indicating a collective identity or community.
  • Sute: This term translates to "in the offerings" or "in the sacrifices," suggesting a connection to rituals and the act of giving.
  • Gīrbhir: This means "with hymns" or "with words," emphasizing the importance of speech, prayers, and chants in invoking divine powers.
  • Havāmahe: This translates to "we invoke" or "we call upon," indicating a plea or request directed towards the divine.
  • Asya: This word means "of this," referring to the subject of the invocation, which is the soma, a sacred drink in Vedic rituals.
  • Somasya: This term refers to "of soma," highlighting the significance of this drink in rituals, believed to confer immortality and divine insight.
  • Pītaye: This translates to "for drinking" or "for consumption," indicating the act of partaking in the soma, which is associated with spiritual nourishment.

  • Meaning: The mantra expresses a collective invocation to the divine powers of Indra and Brihaspati, asking for their blessings through offerings and hymns. It emphasizes the importance of community in spiritual practices and the role of soma as a sacred element that enhances spiritual experiences.
  • Real-life Example: In modern contexts, this mantra can be related to community gatherings where people come together to celebrate, share knowledge, and seek blessings for prosperity and wisdom. For instance, during festivals or communal prayers, individuals invoke divine support for collective well-being.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the power of community, the importance of knowledge and wisdom, and the sacredness of offerings. It teaches us that invoking divine blessings is not just an individual act but a collective endeavor that can lead to greater harmony and prosperity. In real life, this mantra can inspire group activities that promote unity, shared goals, and spiritual growth.
