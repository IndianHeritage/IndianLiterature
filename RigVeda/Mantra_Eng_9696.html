अश्रीरात tanūr भवति रुशती पापयामुया |पतिर यद वध्वो वाससा स्वम अङ्गम अभिधित्सते ||
aśrīrā tanūr bhavati ruśatī pāpayāmuyā | patir yad vadhvo3 vāsasā svam aṅgam abhidhitsate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśrīrā: Without sorrow or misfortune
    • tanūr: Body or form
    • bhavati: Becomes or is
    • ruśatī: Shining or radiant
    • pāpayāmuyā: Free from sin or wrongdoing
    • patir: Lord or master
    • yad: That which
    • vadhvo: Of the one who is slain
    • vāsasā: By clothing or covering
    • svam: One's own
    • aṅgam: Body or limb
    • abhidhitsate: Desires or seeks
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the transformation of the body into a state of purity and radiance.
    • It emphasizes the importance of being free from sin and misfortune, suggesting that a pure heart and mind lead to a radiant existence.
    • The reference to the "lord" indicates a higher power or divine presence that oversees the actions and intentions of individuals.
    • The phrase "that which the one who is slain seeks by clothing" can be interpreted as the desire for protection and sanctity in one's life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied to the pursuit of ethical living, where individuals strive to act with integrity and avoid wrongdoing.
    • For instance, a person who practices honesty in their business dealings may find that their reputation shines brightly, reflecting the "radiance" mentioned in the mantra.
    • Additionally, in times of personal crisis or loss, individuals may seek solace in spiritual practices that promote healing and the shedding of negative emotions, aligning with the idea of becoming free from sorrow.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for purity, both in thought and action, leading to a life that is radiant and free from the burdens of sin.
    • It serves as a reminder that our intentions and actions shape our existence, and by striving for goodness, we can achieve a state of inner peace and external radiance.
    • This mantra can be applicable in various life scenarios, such as personal development, conflict resolution, and spiritual growth, encouraging individuals to seek a higher purpose and live a life of integrity.
