तुभ्येदम इन्द्र परिषिच्यते मधु त्वं सुतस्य कलशस्य राजसि |त्वं रयिम पुरुवीराम उ नस् कृधि त्वं तपः परितप्याजयः स्वः ||
tubhyedam indra pari ṣicyate madhu tvaṁ sutasya kalaśasya rājasi | tvaṁ rayim puruvīrām u nas kṛdhi tvaṁ tapaḥ paritapyājayaḥ svaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tubhyam: to you
    • edam: this
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • pariṣicyate: is being poured
    • madhu: sweet (nectar)
    • tvaṁ: you
    • sutasya: of the son
    • kalaśasya: of the pot
    • rājasi: you who are royal
    • tvaṁ: you
    • rayim: wealth
    • puruvīrām: of the many heroes
    • u: and
    • nas: to us
    • kṛdhi: do
    • tvaṁ: you
    • tapaḥ: austerity
    • paritapyājayaḥ: may you bestow
    • svaḥ: heaven
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of gods, asking him to pour forth sweet nectar (madhu) from the pot of wealth (kalaśa) that belongs to the son (suta).
    • It emphasizes the royal nature of Indra and requests him to grant us wealth and prosperity, akin to the blessings bestowed upon many heroes (puruvīrām).
    • Furthermore, it calls upon Indra to bestow upon us the benefits of austerity (tapaḥ), leading us towards heavenly bliss (svaḥ).
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be likened to seeking blessings for success in endeavors, such as starting a new business or project. Just as the mantra requests wealth and prosperity, individuals often pray for success and abundance in their professional lives.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or festivals, people invoke divine blessings for happiness and prosperity, similar to how this mantra seeks the favor of Indra.
    • In personal development, the concept of austerity (tapaḥ) can be related to self-discipline and commitment to goals, where individuals strive for excellence and fulfillment in their lives.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of divine blessings in achieving success and prosperity in life.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of spiritual practices and material success, emphasizing that true wealth comes from both external and internal sources.
    • In daily life, individuals can apply the teachings of this mantra by cultivating gratitude, seeking divine guidance, and practicing self-discipline to achieve their goals.
