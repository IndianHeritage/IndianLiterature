अप्स्व अग्नि सदिष् तव सुषधीर अनु रुध्यसे |गर्भे सञ्जायसे पुनः ||
apsv agne sadhiṣ ṭava sauṣadhīr anu rudhyase | garbhe sañ jāyase punaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apsv: In the waters or in the essence
    • agne: O Agni (the fire or divine energy)
    • sadhiṣ: You who are the best or the most excellent
    • ṭava: Your
    • sauṣadhīr: Healing or medicinal herbs
    • anu: Following or in accordance with
    • rudhyase: You are restrained or held
    • garbhe: In the womb or in the core
    • sañ: Together or in union
    • jāyase: You are born or you arise
    • punaḥ: Again or once more
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the divine fire, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • The phrase "apsv agne" suggests the presence of Agni in the waters, indicating the interconnectedness of elements.
    • "Sadhiṣ ṭava sauṣadhīr" emphasizes the excellence of Agni in relation to healing and medicinal properties, highlighting the importance of nature in health.
    • The line "anu rudhyase" implies that Agni is restrained or contained, which can be interpreted as the control of energy for constructive purposes.
    • "Garbhe sañ jāyase punaḥ" speaks to the idea of rebirth or regeneration, suggesting that from the core or essence, new life or energy emerges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern medicine, the use of herbal remedies reflects the "sauṣadhīr" aspect, where natural elements are utilized for healing.
    • The concept of transformation through fire can be seen in various cultural rituals, such as the use of fire in ceremonies to symbolize purification and new beginnings.
    • In environmental science, understanding the interconnectedness of elements (like water and fire) is crucial for sustainable practices, reflecting the essence of "apsv agne."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine energy (Agni) in the natural world and its role in healing, transformation, and regeneration.
    • It teaches us to respect and harness natural elements for our well-being and emphasizes the cyclical nature of life and rebirth.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire practices in holistic health, environmental sustainability, and personal transformation, reminding us of our connection to nature and the divine.
