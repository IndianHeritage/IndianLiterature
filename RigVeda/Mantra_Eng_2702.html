मन्त्र: नि सामनाम इषिराम इन्द्र भूमिम महीम अपारां सदने ससत्थ |अस्तभ्नाद द्यां वृषभो अन्तरिक्षम अर्षन्त्व आपस त्वयेह प्रसूता: ||
ni sāmanām iṣirām indra bhūmim mahīm apārāṁ sadane sasattha | astabhnād dyāṁ vṛṣabho antarikṣam arṣantv āpas tvayeha prasūtāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ni: down, towards
    • sāmanām: of the hymns
    • iṣirām: of the arrows
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • bhūmim: the earth
    • mahīm: the vastness
    • apārāṁ: immeasurable
    • sadane: in the abode
    • sasattha: having established
    • astabhnād: may he establish
    • dyāṁ: the sky
    • vṛṣabho: the bull (symbolizing strength)
    • antarikṣam: the space between
    • arṣantv: may they be strong
    • āpas: the waters
    • tvayeha: in you
    • prasūtāḥ: born or produced
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the king of gods, asking him to establish the hymns and arrows in the vastness of the earth.
    • It emphasizes the immeasurable nature of the earth and the sky, suggesting a connection between the divine and the natural world.
    • The mention of "the bull" symbolizes strength and power, indicating that the divine forces are strong and protective.
    • The waters are also invoked, representing life and nourishment, which are essential for existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray to Indra for rain, as water is crucial for crops. This mantra can be seen as a call for divine intervention in ensuring a bountiful harvest.
    • In personal challenges, individuals may seek strength and guidance from higher powers, similar to how this mantra seeks the establishment of strength and support from Indra.
    • In environmental conservation, the reference to the earth and waters can inspire actions to protect natural resources, recognizing their immeasurable value.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine support in establishing strength and stability in the world.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of nature, the divine, and human existence, reminding us to seek balance and harmony.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to recognize their dependence on natural resources and the divine for sustenance and strength, fostering a sense of responsibility towards the environment and community.
