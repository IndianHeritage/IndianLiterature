कविः कवित्वा दिवि रूपम् आसजद अप्रभूतिः वरुणो निर अपः सृजत् |क्षेमं कृत्वाना जनयो न सिंधवस्तास्य वर्णं शुचयो भरिभ्रति ||
kaviḥ kavitvā divi rūpam āsajad aprabhūtī varuṇo nir apaḥ sṛjat | kṣemaṁ kṛṇvānā janayo na sindhavas tā asya varṇaṁ śucayo bharibhrati ||
  • kaviḥ kavitvā: The term "kaviḥ" refers to a poet or seer, someone who possesses deep insight and wisdom. "Kavitvā" signifies the act of creation or composition, particularly in the realm of poetry or thought.
  • divi rūpam āsajad: This phrase translates to "established the form in the heavens." It suggests the idea of creating or manifesting something divine or celestial, indicating a connection between the earthly and the heavenly.
  • aprabhūtī varuṇo: "Aprabhūtī" means abundance or richness, while "Varuṇo" refers to Varuna, the Vedic god of water and cosmic order. This part of the mantra emphasizes the richness and vastness of Varuna's domain.
  • nir apaḥ sṛjat: This translates to "created the waters without limit." It signifies the boundless nature of water, which is essential for life and sustenance.
  • kṣemaṁ kṛṇvānā: "Kṣemaṁ" means well-being or safety, and "kṛṇvānā" refers to the act of creating or establishing. This phrase highlights the importance of fostering safety and prosperity among people.
  • janayo na sindhavas: "Janayo" means to generate or create, while "sindhavas" refers to rivers or bodies of water. This part emphasizes the role of water in nurturing life and community.
  • tā asya varṇaṁ śucayo bharibhrati: This translates to "those who carry the pure color of this." It suggests the idea of purity and the importance of maintaining a clear and virtuous path in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, abundance, and the nurturing aspects of nature, particularly water. It signifies the interconnectedness of the divine and the earthly, emphasizing the role of wisdom and purity in fostering well-being. In real life, this mantra can be applied to various scenarios, such as environmental conservation, where the abundance of water is crucial for sustaining life. It also highlights the importance of creativity and insight in addressing societal challenges, encouraging individuals to act with wisdom and integrity for the betterment of their communities. By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can contribute to a harmonious and prosperous existence, both personally and collectively.
