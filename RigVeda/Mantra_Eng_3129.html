इदं मे अग्नि कियते पावकामिनते गुरुं भारं न मन्मा |बृहद् दधाथ धृतता गभीरं यह्वं पृष्ठं प्रयसा सप्तधातु ||
idam me agne kiyate pāvakāminate gurum bhāraṁ na manma | bṛhad dadhātha dhṛṣatā gabhīraṁ yahvam pṛṣṭham prayasā saptadhātu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • idam: this
    • me: to me
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • kiyate: what is to be done
    • pāvakāminate: desiring purity
    • gurum: the great one
    • bhāraṁ: burden
    • na: not
    • manma: my mind
    • bṛhad: vast
    • dadhātha: you bestow
    • dhṛṣatā: with strength
    • gabhīraṁ: deep
    • yahvam: that which
    • pṛṣṭham: is behind
    • prayasā: with effort
    • saptadhātu: seven elements
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, seeking guidance and strength.
    • The speaker expresses a desire for purity and clarity in their thoughts and actions.
    • It acknowledges the burdens of life and asks for the strength to carry them without losing focus.
    • The mention of "saptadhātu" refers to the seven elements that constitute life, emphasizing the holistic approach to existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging work environment, one might recite this mantra to seek clarity and strength to overcome obstacles.
    • During personal struggles, such as health issues or emotional turmoil, this mantra can serve as a reminder to focus on inner strength and purity of intention.
    • In community gatherings or rituals, invoking Agni can symbolize the collective effort to purify intentions and strengthen bonds.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance in our endeavors.
    • It teaches us to acknowledge our burdens while striving for purity and strength in our actions.
    • In real life, it is applicable in various scenarios, from personal challenges to communal efforts, reminding us of the power of intention and the support of the divine.
