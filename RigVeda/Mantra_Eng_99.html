आश्रुतकर्ण श्रुधी हवं नू चिद दधिष्व मे गिरः |इन्द्र स्तोमम् इमम् मम कृष्वा युजश चिद अन्तरम् ||
āśrutkarṇa śrudhī havaṁ nū cid dadhiṣva me giraḥ | indra stomam imam mama kṛṣvā yujaś cid antaram ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • āśrutkarṇa: Refers to one who has ears that listen attentively.
    • śrudhī: Implies understanding or comprehension.
    • havaṁ: A call or invocation.
    • nū: A particle that emphasizes the request.
    • cid: Indicates a sense of certainty or affirmation.
    • dadhiṣva: Means to grant or bestow.
    • me: Refers to 'my' or 'to me.'
    • giraḥ: Refers to words or speech.
    • indra: The king of gods, often associated with strength and power.
    • stomam: A hymn or praise.
    • imam: This or these.
    • mama: My or mine.
    • kṛṣvā: To make or to create.
    • yujaś: To join or connect.
    • cid: Again, indicating certainty.
    • antaram: Refers to the inner essence or core.
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra is a plea for divine attention and understanding.
    • The speaker is invoking Indra, asking him to listen to their words and grant them the wisdom and strength to articulate their thoughts effectively.
    • It emphasizes the importance of being heard and understood, not just by the divine but also in human interactions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a classroom setting, a student may feel the need to express their thoughts clearly to their teacher, seeking understanding and guidance.
    • In a workplace, an employee may invoke this sentiment when presenting ideas to management, hoping for acknowledgment and support.
    • During personal relationships, individuals often seek to be heard and understood by their partners, highlighting the universal need for effective communication.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of communication, understanding, and the divine support in articulating one's thoughts.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios where effective communication is crucial, such as education, professional settings, and personal relationships.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can remind themselves of the power of words and the necessity of being heard, fostering better connections with others.
