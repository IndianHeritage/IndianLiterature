ऊरुभ्यां ते अष्टिवद्भ्यां पार्ष्णिभ्यां प्रपदाभ्यां |यक्ष्मं श्रोणिभ्यां भासदाद भंससो वि वृहामि ते ||
ūrubhyāṁ te aṣṭhīvadbhyām pārṣṇibhyām prapadābhyām | yakṣmaṁ śroṇibhyām bhāsadād bhaṁsaso vi vṛhāmi te ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ūrubhyāṁ: From the thighs
    • te: Your
    • aṣṭhīvadbhyām: Like bones
    • pārṣṇibhyām: From the knees
    • prapadābhyām: From the feet
    • yakṣmaṁ: Illness or disease
    • śroṇibhyām: From the hips
    • bhāsadād: May I shine or be radiant
    • bhaṁsaso: From the ashes or remnants
    • vi: Indeed
    • vṛhāmi: I grow or increase
    • te: Your
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for health and vitality, invoking strength from various parts of the body.
    • It emphasizes the importance of physical well-being, suggesting that each part of the body contributes to overall health.
    • The reference to "ashes" can symbolize overcoming past ailments or challenges, indicating a rebirth or renewal of strength.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In sports, athletes often focus on strengthening their legs and core, as these areas are crucial for performance. This mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of physical training and health.
    • In yoga and meditation practices, individuals often chant mantras to promote healing and well-being, similar to the invocation of strength in this mantra.
    • During recovery from illness, patients may visualize their body healing and growing stronger, akin to the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of body parts and their collective role in maintaining health.
    • It serves as a reminder to take care of our physical health, recognizing that each part of our body contributes to our overall well-being.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in health practices, fitness routines, and during moments of recovery, encouraging individuals to focus on holistic health and vitality.
