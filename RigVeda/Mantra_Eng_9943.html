स यःव्योऽवनीर गोष्व अर्वा जुहोति प्रधान्यासु सस्रिः | अपादो यत्र युज्यासोऽरथाऽद्रोण्यश्वास इरते घृतं वाः ||

  • sa yahvyo3 'vanīr goṣv arvā juhoti pradhanyāsu sasriḥ
    • sa - This word translates to "he" or "that," indicating a subject or a divine entity.
    • yahvyo - Refers to "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is called upon," suggesting a connection to a higher power.
    • 'vanīr - This can mean "the one who is in the forest" or "the one who is connected to nature," symbolizing harmony with the environment.
    • goṣv - This word means "cattle" or "herds," representing wealth, prosperity, and sustenance in ancient agrarian societies.
    • arvā - Translates to "the one who is strong" or "the one who is powerful," indicating strength and resilience.
    • juhoti - Means "offers" or "sacrifices," highlighting the act of giving and devotion.
    • pradhanyāsu - Refers to "in the important matters" or "in the significant aspects," emphasizing the importance of the context in which the offering is made.
    • sasriḥ - This word can mean "with abundance" or "with plenty," suggesting that the offerings are made with a sense of generosity and fullness.
  • apādo yatra yujyāso 'rathā droṇyaśvāsa īrate ghṛtaṁ vāḥ
    • apādo - This translates to "where there are no obstacles" or "where there is freedom," indicating a state of ease and flow.
    • yatra - Means "where," pointing to a specific place or condition.
    • yujyāso - Refers to "those who are united" or "those who are connected," emphasizing the importance of community and collaboration.
    • 'rathā - This word means "chariots," symbolizing movement, progress, and the journey of life.
    • droṇyaśvāsa - Translates to "the breath of the horses," indicating vitality, energy, and the spirit of life.
    • īrate - Means "to flow" or "to move," suggesting a dynamic and active state.
    • ghṛtaṁ - Refers to "ghee" or "clarified butter," which is often used in rituals and symbolizes purity and nourishment.
    • vāḥ - This word can mean "may it be" or "let it be," expressing a wish or a prayer for the fulfillment of desires.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of connection with nature, community, and the divine. It highlights the act of offering and the significance of doing so in a state of abundance and freedom from obstacles. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal growth, where one seeks to overcome obstacles and connect with their inner strength.
  • In community building, where collaboration and unity are essential for progress.
  • In environmental consciousness, where harmony with nature is vital for sustainability.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of generosity, and the pursuit of a harmonious existence.
