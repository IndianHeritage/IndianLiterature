पुरूणि दसमो नि रिणाति जम्बैर आद रोचते वन आ विभावाः |आद अस्य वातो अनु वाति शोचिर अस्तुर न शर्याम असनाम अनु dyūn ||
purūṇi dasmo ni riṇāti jambhair ād rocate vana ā vibhāvā | ād asya vāto anu vāti śocir astur na śaryām asanām anu dyūn ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • purūṇi: abundant, full
    • dasmo: the earth, ground
    • ni riṇāti: does not diminish
    • jambhair: with fruits, with produce
    • ād: indeed, certainly
    • rocate: shines, radiates
    • vana: forest, nature
    • ā vibhāvā: manifests, reveals
    • ād: indeed
    • asya: of this
    • vāto: the wind
    • anu vāti: blows after, follows
    • śocir: bright, clear
    • astur: is, exists
    • na: not
    • śaryām: of the trees
    • asanām: of the living beings
    • anu: after, following
    • dyūn: the heavens, sky
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the abundance and richness of nature, emphasizing that the earth does not diminish in its offerings.
    • The phrase "purūṇi dasmo ni riṇāti" suggests that the earth is full and continues to provide without depleting its resources.
    • The mention of "jambhair" indicates the fruits and produce that nature provides, symbolizing nourishment and sustenance.
    • "Ād rocate vana ā vibhāvā" highlights the beauty and radiance of nature, suggesting that it reveals its splendor to those who appreciate it.
    • The wind ("vāto") is depicted as a gentle force that follows and enhances the clarity and brightness of the environment.
    • The mantra concludes with a reminder of the interconnectedness of all living beings and the natural world, emphasizing that life thrives in harmony with nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, sustainable practices ensure that the earth remains fertile and productive, reflecting the idea that the earth does not diminish when treated with respect.
    • Environmental conservation efforts, such as reforestation, embody the principle of "ā vibhāvā," as they reveal the beauty of nature and its ability to regenerate.
    • Mindfulness practices that encourage individuals to connect with nature can lead to a greater appreciation of the "rocate" aspect, where one finds joy and peace in the natural world.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the abundance of nature and the importance of living in harmony with the environment.
    • Its significance lies in promoting sustainability, respect for natural resources, and the interconnectedness of all life.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to adopt practices that protect and cherish the earth, ensuring that its richness continues for future generations.
