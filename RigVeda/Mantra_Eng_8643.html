पवमान महि श्रवश्चित्रेभिर्यासी रश्मिभिः |शर्धन तमांसी जिघ्नसे विश्वानि दाशुषो गृहे ||
pavamāna mahi śravaś citrebhir yāsi raśmibhiḥ | śardhan tamāṁsi jighnase viśvāni dāśuṣo gṛhe ||
  • pavamāna: This term refers to the "purifying" or "sublime" aspect, often associated with the wind or breath. In a real-life context, it can symbolize the cleansing of negative thoughts or energies, akin to how fresh air revitalizes a space.
  • mahi: Meaning "great" or "vast," this word signifies the expansive nature of the universe or the divine. It can be related to the vastness of human potential and the greatness of collective human endeavors.
  • śravaś: This translates to "fame" or "renown." In practical terms, it can be linked to the pursuit of knowledge and recognition in one’s field, emphasizing the importance of leaving a legacy.
  • citrebhir: This word means "with various forms" or "colorful." It can represent the diversity of life experiences and perspectives, highlighting the beauty found in differences among people.
  • yāsi: This means "you go" or "you proceed." It signifies movement or progress, which can be related to personal growth and the journey of life.
  • raśmibhiḥ: This translates to "with rays" or "with light." It symbolizes enlightenment and knowledge, akin to how sunlight illuminates the world, allowing us to see clearly.
  • śardhan: This term refers to "darkness" or "obscurity." It can represent challenges or ignorance that one must overcome in life.
  • tamāṁsi: Meaning "the dark" or "the unknown," it emphasizes the importance of confronting fears and uncertainties in our lives.
  • jighnase: This means "to destroy" or "to overcome." It signifies the power of will and determination to conquer obstacles.
  • viśvāni: This translates to "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • dāśuṣo: Meaning "of the servants" or "of the devotees," it highlights the importance of humility and service to others.
  • gṛhe: This means "in the home" or "in the dwelling," symbolizing the importance of family and community as the foundation of society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the journey of purification and enlightenment, emphasizing the importance of overcoming darkness and ignorance through knowledge and service. In real life, it serves as a reminder to embrace diversity, pursue greatness, and foster connections within our communities. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and enlightened society.
