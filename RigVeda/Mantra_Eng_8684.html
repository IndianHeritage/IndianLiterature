गोमन न इन्दो अश्ववत सुतः सुदक्ष धन्वा |शुचिं ते वर्णम अधि गोषु दीधराम ||
goman na indo aśvavat sutaḥ sudakṣa dhanva | śuciṁ te varṇam adhi goṣu dīdharam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • goman: O possessor of cows, referring to a person or deity who is rich in resources.
    • na: not.
    • indo: O Indra, the king of gods, often associated with strength and power.
    • aśvavat: like a horse, symbolizing speed, strength, and vitality.
    • sutaḥ: son or offspring, indicating lineage or progeny.
    • sudakṣa: well-skilled or proficient, suggesting expertise or capability.
    • dhanva: bow, representing strength and the ability to protect or defend.
    • śucim: pure or clean, indicating clarity and righteousness.
    • te: your, indicating possession or belonging.
    • varṇam: color or quality, referring to the attributes or characteristics.
    • adhi: upon or over, indicating a position or state.
    • goṣu: among the cows, symbolizing wealth and prosperity.
    • dīdharam: one who holds or possesses, indicating ownership or guardianship.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra, asking for strength and skill akin to that of a horse.
    • It emphasizes the importance of purity and righteousness in one's actions and character.
    • The reference to cows symbolizes wealth and prosperity, suggesting that true richness comes from virtuous living.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A farmer who treats his cattle with care and respect embodies the essence of this mantra, as he is rich in both resources and virtue.
    • A leader who demonstrates skill and integrity in guiding their team reflects the qualities of being "sudakṣa" and "śucim."
    • In sports, an athlete who trains hard and competes with fairness exemplifies the strength and purity called for in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of strength, skill, and purity in achieving success and prosperity.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal development, leadership, and ethical living.
    • By embodying the qualities of diligence, integrity, and respect for resources, individuals can cultivate a fulfilling and prosperous life.
