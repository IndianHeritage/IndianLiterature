प्र वो देवायाग्नये बरहिष्ठम आर्चास्मै |गमद देवैभिर आ स नो यजिष्ठो बरहिर आ सदत ||
pra vo devāyāgnaye barhiṣṭham arcāsmai | gamad devebhir ā sa no yajiṣṭho barhir ā sadat ||
  • Translation: "To the divine Agni, the most excellent of the gods, we offer our prayers. May he, the best of the worshippers, bring us closer to the deities."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra: A prefix meaning "forth" or "towards," indicating direction or intention.
    • vo: A form of "you," addressing the divine beings.
    • devāyā: "To the gods," referring to the divine entities that are being invoked.
    • agnaye: "To Agni," the fire god, symbolizing purity, transformation, and the divine messenger.
    • barhiṣṭham: "The most excellent," indicating the highest quality or the best among the offerings.
    • arcāsmai: "We offer our prayers," a call to worship and reverence.
    • gamad: "May he bring," a request for action or assistance.
    • devebhir: "By the deities," acknowledging the presence and power of the divine beings.
    • ā: A preposition meaning "towards" or "from," indicating direction.
    • sa: "He," referring to Agni.
    • no: "Us," indicating the worshippers or the community.
    • yajiṣṭho: "The best of the worshippers," highlighting the importance of devotion and sincerity in worship.
    • barhir: "Offering," referring to the act of giving or presenting something sacred.
    • ā: Again, indicating direction.
    • sadat: "Always," suggesting continuity and permanence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be seen in rituals where fire is used as a medium to connect with the divine, such as during weddings or festivals like Diwali, where offerings are made to Agni.
    • In daily life, individuals may invoke this mantra during meditation or prayer, seeking guidance and blessings from higher powers to navigate challenges.
    • Communities often come together in collective worship, reflecting the essence of this mantra by uniting their intentions towards a common goal, such as peace or prosperity.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on devotion, community, and the transformative power of fire as a divine element. It teaches us the importance of sincere worship and the role of Agni as a mediator between humans and the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals and communities to come together in prayer and action, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in their spiritual journeys.
