रूपं-रूपम् प्रतिरूपो बभूव तद अस्य रूपम् प्रतिचक्षणाय |इन्द्रो मायाभिः पुरुरूप ईयते युक्ता ह्य अस्य हरयः शतादश ||
rūpaṁ-rūpam pratirūpo babhūva tad asya rūpam praticakṣaṇāya | indro māyābhiḥ pururūpa īyate yuktā hy asya harayaḥ śatā daśa ||
  • rūpaṁ-rūpam pratirūpo babhūva: This phrase can be translated to "form and form, the reflected form has come into being." It suggests the idea of duality and reflection, where one form mirrors another. In real life, this can be seen in relationships where individuals reflect each other's qualities, such as in friendships or partnerships.
  • tad asya rūpam praticakṣaṇāya: This means "that form is for the purpose of perception." It emphasizes the importance of perception in understanding reality. For example, in art, the way a viewer perceives a painting can vary greatly based on their personal experiences and emotions, highlighting the subjective nature of perception.
  • indro māyābhiḥ pururūpa īyate: This translates to "Indra moves in many forms through illusions." Indra, a significant deity in Vedic literature, represents power and strength. This line suggests that even powerful entities can take on various forms and navigate through illusions. In modern life, this can be likened to leaders who adapt their strategies and personas to suit different situations, demonstrating flexibility and adaptability.
  • yuktā hy asya harayaḥ śatā daśa: This means "the hundred and ten forces are indeed united." It indicates a collective strength and unity among various forces. In a real-world context, this can be seen in teamwork, where diverse individuals come together to achieve a common goal, such as in corporate settings or community projects.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of duality, perception, adaptability, and unity. Its significance lies in the understanding that life is a reflection of various forms and perceptions, and that strength often comes from collaboration and unity. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in personal relationships, professional environments, and community interactions, reminding us of the importance of understanding different perspectives and working together harmoniously.
