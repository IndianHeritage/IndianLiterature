वयं उ त्वा शतक्रतो गावो न यवसेष्वा | उक्तेषु रणयामसि ||
vayam u tvā śatakrato gāvo na yavaseṣv ā | uktheṣu raṇayāmasi ||
  • Mantra: "vayam u tvā śatakrato gāvo na yavaseṣv ā | uktheṣu raṇayāmasi ||"
  • Translation: "We, O Hundred-Minded One (Indra), do not yield to the enemies; we engage in battle with our words."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vayam: We
    • u: O
    • tvā: You (referring to Indra)
    • śatakrato: Hundred-Minded One (a name for Indra, symbolizing his vast wisdom and power)
    • gāvo: Cows (symbolizing wealth and prosperity)
    • na: Not
    • yavaseṣv: Yielding to enemies
    • uktheṣu: In words
    • raṇayāmasi: We engage in battle
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, who is often associated with strength, courage, and the protection of his devotees.
    • The phrase "Hundred-Minded One" signifies the multifaceted nature of Indra's wisdom and his ability to strategize in various situations.
    • The mention of "cows" symbolizes not just material wealth but also the nurturing aspect of life, as cows are revered in Vedic culture for their contributions to agriculture and sustenance.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of verbal strength and the power of words in overcoming adversities, suggesting that communication and intention can be as potent as physical strength in battles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied when teams face challenges or competition. Instead of yielding to pressure, they can strategize and communicate effectively to overcome obstacles.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra serves as a reminder that conflicts can often be resolved through open dialogue and understanding rather than aggression.
    • In social movements, the power of words and collective action can lead to significant change, demonstrating that peaceful resistance can be more effective than violence.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of resilience and the power of communication in the face of challenges.
    • It teaches that one should not succumb to fear or adversity but instead engage actively and thoughtfully, using words as a tool for empowerment.
    • In essence, it encourages individuals and communities to harness their inner strength and wisdom to confront and overcome life's battles, making it applicable in various real-life scenarios.
