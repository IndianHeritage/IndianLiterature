ओजिष्टं ते मध्यतो मेद उद्भृतं प्र ते वयं ददामहे |श्चोतन्ति ते वसो स्तोकाऽधि त्वचि प्रति तान देवशो विहि ||
ojiṣṭhaṁ te madhyato meda udbhṛtam pra te vayaṁ dadāmahe | ścotanti te vaso stokā adhi tvaci prati tān devaśo vihi ||
  • ojiṣṭhaṁ: This word can be interpreted as "the best" or "the most excellent." It signifies the highest quality or the utmost excellence in a particular context.
  • te: This means "to you" or "for you," indicating a direction of reverence or offering towards a divine entity.
  • madhyato: Translated as "from the middle" or "from the center," it suggests a central position or a balanced state.
  • meda: This refers to "fat" or "essence," often symbolizing richness, abundance, or nourishment.
  • udbhṛtam: Meaning "that which is produced" or "that which arises," it indicates the manifestation of something significant or valuable.
  • pra: This prefix means "forth" or "forward," suggesting an action of giving or presenting.
  • te vayaṁ: This translates to "we give to you," indicating an offering or a gift from the speaker to the divine.
  • dadāmahe: Meaning "we offer" or "we present," it emphasizes the act of giving with sincerity and devotion.
  • ścotanti: This word means "they shine" or "they illuminate," suggesting a sense of brightness or enlightenment.
  • te vaso: Translated as "your abode" or "your dwelling," it refers to the place where the divine resides.
  • stokā: This means "small" or "little," indicating something that may seem insignificant but holds value.
  • adhi: Meaning "above" or "over," it suggests a position of elevation or superiority.
  • tvaci: This translates to "on your body" or "upon you," indicating a close connection or relationship with the divine.
  • prati: Meaning "towards" or "in the direction of," it indicates movement or intention towards the divine.
  • tān: This word means "those" or "them," referring to the offerings or aspects being presented.
  • devaśo: Translated as "of the gods," it signifies the divine nature of the subject being addressed.
  • vihi: This means "to remove" or "to dispel," indicating a desire to eliminate negativity or obstacles.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the act of offering the best of one's resources and intentions to the divine, highlighting the importance of quality and sincerity in our actions. It reflects a deep reverence for the divine presence and acknowledges the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal relationships, offering love and support to others with genuine intentions.
  • In professional settings, striving for excellence in work and contributing positively to the community.
  • In spiritual practices, dedicating time and effort to connect with the divine through meditation, prayer, or acts of kindness.

This mantra serves as a reminder to focus on the quality of our offerings, whether they be material, emotional, or spiritual, and to recognize the divine presence in all aspects of life.
