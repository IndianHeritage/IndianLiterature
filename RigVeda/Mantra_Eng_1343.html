युवं पेदवे पूर्वारम् अश्विनां स्पृधां श्वेतं तरुतारं दुवस्यथः | शर्यैर अभिद्युम पृतनासु दुष्टारं चर्कृत्यम इन्द्रं इव चर्षणीसहं ||
yuvam pedave puruvāram aśvinā spṛdhāṁ śvetaṁ tarutāraṁ duvasyathaḥ | śaryair abhidyum pṛtanāsu duṣṭaraṁ carkṛtyam indram iva carṣaṇīsaham ||
  • yuvam: This word translates to "you both," referring to the twin deities, the Ashvins, who are known for their youthful energy and vitality.
  • pedave: This means "to be invoked" or "to be called upon," indicating a plea for assistance or intervention.
  • puruvāram: This can be interpreted as "the one who has gone before" or "the ancient," suggesting a connection to tradition and wisdom.
  • aśvinā: Referring specifically to the Ashvins, the divine horsemen and physicians of the gods, symbolizing healing and protection.
  • spṛdhāṁ: This word means "with speed" or "swiftly," emphasizing the urgency and immediacy of the request.
  • śvetaṁ: Translates to "white," often symbolizing purity, clarity, and auspiciousness.
  • tarutāraṁ: This means "the one who moves swiftly through the trees," possibly referring to the natural world and the interconnectedness of life.
  • duvasyathaḥ: This can be understood as "may you come" or "may you arrive," a call for the Ashvins to manifest their presence.
  • śaryair: This word means "with the help of the trees," indicating reliance on nature and its resources.
  • abhidyum: This translates to "to be invoked" or "to be called upon," reinforcing the theme of seeking divine assistance.
  • pṛtanāsu: This means "in the struggles" or "in the challenges," highlighting the difficulties faced in life.
  • duṣṭaraṁ: This translates to "difficult" or "hard to overcome," emphasizing the challenges that may seem insurmountable.
  • carkṛtyam: This means "the act of circling" or "the process of going around," which can symbolize the cyclical nature of life and challenges.
  • indram: Referring to Indra, the king of the gods, associated with strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • iva: This means "like" or "as," drawing a comparison to Indra's strength.
  • carṣaṇīsaham: This can be interpreted as "the one who bears the burden," indicating resilience and the ability to endure hardships.

Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation to the Ashvins, seeking their swift assistance in overcoming life's challenges. It emphasizes the importance of calling upon divine forces in times of need, particularly when faced with difficulties that seem overwhelming. In real life, this can be applicable in various situations such as health crises, personal struggles, or any scenario where one feels the need for support and guidance. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of nature, and the strength that can be drawn from divine sources. By invoking the Ashvins, individuals can find hope and resilience in their journeys, encouraging them to face challenges with courage and determination.
