जनासो वृक्तबरहिषो हविष्मन्तो अरंक्रितः | युवां हवन्ते अश्विनाः ||
janāso vṛktabarhiṣo haviṣmanto araṁkṛtaḥ | yuvāṁ havante aśvinā ||
  • Mantra: "janāso vṛktabarhiṣo haviṣmanto araṁkṛtaḥ | yuvāṁ havante aśvinā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • janāso: of the people or beings
    • vṛktabarhiṣo: with the sacred grass (barhiṣ) that is spread or offered
    • haviṣmanto: those who are rich in offerings (havis)
    • araṁkṛtaḥ: who are prepared or arranged
    • yuvāṁ: you two (referring to the Aśvins)
    • havante: who are invoked or called upon
    • aśvinā: O Aśvins (the twin deities of health and medicine)
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a call to the Aśvins, the divine twin brothers in Vedic mythology, who are often associated with healing, health, and the welfare of humanity.
    • The phrase "janāso vṛktabarhiṣo" suggests a connection to the community or people who are making offerings, indicating a collective effort in seeking blessings.
    • "Haviṣmanto araṁkṛtaḥ" emphasizes the importance of being prepared and rich in offerings, which can be interpreted as being ready to give and share in a community.
    • The invocation of the Aśvins signifies a request for their divine assistance, particularly in matters of health and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be recited during health camps or wellness programs, where people come together to promote health and well-being.
    • In personal life, individuals can reflect on the importance of being prepared and generous in their offerings, whether it be time, resources, or support to others in need.
    • During festivals or rituals, invoking the Aśvins can symbolize the community's collective hope for health and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of community, preparation, and the divine assistance of the Aśvins in achieving health and well-being.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals within a community and the importance of collective efforts in seeking blessings.
    • In real life, it encourages us to be proactive in our health and to support one another, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.
