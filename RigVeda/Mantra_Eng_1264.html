उप ते स्तोमान पशुपा इवाकरं रास्वा पितर मरुतां सुम्नम अस्मे |भद्रा हि ते सुमतिर मृळयत्तमाथा वयं अव इत ते वृणीमहे ||
upa te stomān paśupā ivākaraṁ rāsvā pitar marutāṁ sumnam asme | bhadrā hi te sumatir mṛḻayattamāthā vayam ava it te vṛṇīmahe ||
  • upa te stomān: This phrase can be translated as "we approach your praises." It signifies the act of seeking divine blessings through hymns and prayers. In real life, this can be likened to how people often seek guidance or support through rituals or prayers in various cultures.
  • paśupā ivākaraṁ: This translates to "like the shepherd of the animals." Here, the term "paśupā" refers to a protector or guide, akin to a shepherd who cares for their flock. In modern contexts, this can be related to leaders or mentors who guide and protect their followers or students.
  • rāsvā pitar marutāṁ: This means "the father of the Maruts." The Maruts are storm deities in Vedic literature, representing the forces of nature. The term "pitar" (father) indicates a source of strength and protection. In everyday life, this can be seen in the role of parental figures or guardians who provide support and guidance during turbulent times.
  • sumnam asme: This translates to "may we be blessed." It expresses a desire for well-being and prosperity. In practical terms, this can be related to the common practice of wishing well for oneself and others, such as during celebrations or significant life events.
  • bhadrā hi te sumatir: This means "your thoughts are indeed auspicious." It acknowledges the positive and beneficial nature of divine thoughts or intentions. In real life, this can be compared to the importance of positive thinking and intentions in achieving success and happiness.
  • mṛḻayattamāthā: This can be interpreted as "the most soothing." It refers to the calming and comforting aspects of divine presence. In contemporary life, this can be related to the comfort one finds in spirituality or meditation practices that help alleviate stress and anxiety.
  • vayam ava it te vṛṇīmahe: This translates to "we choose you." It signifies a conscious decision to seek the divine or higher power. In real life, this can be seen in the choices individuals make to follow a particular path, whether it be spiritual, ethical, or personal development.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and blessings while acknowledging the protective and nurturing aspects of higher powers. Its significance lies in the recognition of the importance of spiritual connection and the role of positive intentions in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during personal prayers, rituals, or moments of reflection, reminding individuals of the support they can seek from the divine and the importance of choosing a path that aligns with their values and aspirations.
