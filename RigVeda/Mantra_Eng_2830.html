शुनं हुवेम मगवाणं इन्द्रं अस्मिन भरे नृतमं वाजसातौ | श्रिण्वन्तं उग्रं ऊतये समत्सु घ्नन्तं वृत्राणि संजितं धनानाम् ||
śunaṁ huvema maghavānam indram asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau | śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi saṁjitaṁ dhanānām ||
  • śunaṁ huvema: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke the powerful." The term "śunaṁ" refers to something that is auspicious or beneficial, while "huvema" indicates the act of invoking or calling upon.
  • maghavānam indram: Here, "maghavānam" refers to the mighty or powerful, and "indram" is a reference to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature. This line emphasizes the invocation of Indra's strength and power.
  • asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau: "Asmin bhare" means "in this place" or "in this context," while "nṛtamaṁ" refers to the best or the most excellent. "Vājasātau" can be interpreted as "in the context of strength or power." This line suggests that we are calling upon Indra's might in our current situation.
  • śṛṇvantam ugram: "Śṛṇvantam" means "listening" or "hearing," and "ugram" refers to something fierce or powerful. This indicates that we are calling upon Indra to listen to our invocation with great attention and strength.
  • ūtaye samatsu: "Ūtaye" means "for the purpose of protection" or "for assistance," while "samatsu" refers to "in the midst of challenges" or "in difficult situations." This line highlights the need for divine support during trying times.
  • ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi: "Ghnantaṁ" means "destroying" or "overcoming," and "vṛtrāṇi" refers to the obstacles or enemies. This suggests that we seek Indra's help in overcoming challenges and adversities.
  • saṁjitaṁ dhanānām: "Saṁjitaṁ" means "gathered" or "assembled," and "dhanānām" refers to wealth or resources. This line implies that we are invoking Indra to help us gather our resources and wealth effectively.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda is a powerful invocation for strength, protection, and the overcoming of obstacles. It emphasizes the importance of divine assistance in our endeavors, particularly in challenging situations. In real life, this can be applicable in various scenarios such as seeking guidance during difficult times, striving for success in personal or professional goals, or even in collective efforts within a community. By invoking the strength of Indra, individuals and groups can find the courage and resources needed to face adversities and achieve their aspirations. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine support, encouraging us to seek help when needed and to recognize the power of unity in overcoming challenges.
