वैश्वानरः प्रत्नथा नाकम् आरुहद दिवस पृष्ठम् भण्डमानः सुमन्मभिः |स पूर्ववज जनयञ जन्तवे धनं समानम् अज्मन् पर्य एति जागृविः ||
vaiśvānaraḥ pratnathā nākam āruhad divas pṛṣṭham bhandamānaḥ sumanmabhiḥ | sa pūrvavaj janayañ jantave dhanaṁ samānam ajmam pary eti jāgṛviḥ ||
  • vaiśvānaraḥ: This term refers to the universal or cosmic being, often associated with the fire element and the divine presence in all living things. In real life, this can be likened to the idea of a universal consciousness that connects all beings.
  • pratnathā: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is called upon." In practical terms, it signifies the act of seeking guidance or support from a higher power, similar to how individuals may pray or meditate for clarity in difficult situations.
  • nākam: This translates to "heaven" or "the celestial realm." It represents the ultimate goal or aspiration in life, akin to achieving one's dreams or reaching a state of enlightenment.
  • āruhad: Meaning "ascended" or "elevated," this word suggests the idea of rising above challenges. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals overcoming obstacles to achieve personal growth or success.
  • divas: This term means "day" or "light," symbolizing knowledge, clarity, and enlightenment. It can be related to the pursuit of education and wisdom in our lives.
  • pṛṣṭham: This word means "the back" or "the support." It signifies the importance of having a strong foundation or support system in life, such as family, friends, or mentors who help us navigate challenges.
  • bhandamānaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is bound" or "the one who is connected." It reflects the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of relationships in our lives.
  • sumanmabhiḥ: This term means "with good thoughts" or "with auspicious intentions." It emphasizes the significance of positive thinking and intentions in shaping our reality.
  • sa pūrvavaj: This phrase translates to "he, like before," suggesting continuity and tradition. It highlights the importance of learning from the past and applying those lessons to the present.
  • janayañ: Meaning "to create" or "to generate," this word signifies the act of bringing something into existence, whether it be ideas, relationships, or projects.
  • jantave: This term refers to "living beings" or "creatures." It underscores the significance of life and the responsibility we have towards all living entities.
  • dhanam: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." It can refer to material wealth as well as spiritual richness, emphasizing the balance between the two in our lives.
  • samānam: Meaning "equal" or "similar," this word reflects the idea of equality and fairness in our interactions with others.
  • ajmam: This term can be interpreted as "the unborn" or "the eternal." It signifies the essence of life that transcends physical existence, reminding us of our spiritual nature.
  • pary eti: This phrase means "to go around" or "to encompass." It suggests the idea of exploration and understanding the world around us.
  • jāgṛviḥ: This term translates to "the awakened one" or "the one who is aware." It emphasizes the importance of consciousness and mindfulness in our daily lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking higher knowledge, understanding our interconnectedness, and striving for personal and spiritual growth. It serves as a reminder to cultivate positive thoughts, learn from the past, and support one another in our journeys. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, community building, and fostering a sense of unity among diverse individuals. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, we can create a more harmonious and enlightened society.
