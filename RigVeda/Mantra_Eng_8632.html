तं उक्षमानम् अव्यये वारे पुनन्ति धरिणसिम् |दूतं न पूर्वचित्तय आ शासते मनिषिणः ||
tam ukṣamāṇam avyaye vāre punanti dharṇasim | dūtaṁ na pūrvacittaya ā śāsate manīṣiṇaḥ ||
  • tam ukṣamāṇam: This phrase can be translated as "that which is being cultivated" or "that which is being nurtured." It signifies the importance of growth and development, whether in a personal, spiritual, or communal context.
  • avyaye: This word means "imperishable" or "eternal." It suggests that the essence of what is being cultivated is beyond the transient nature of life, hinting at the pursuit of lasting values and truths.
  • vāre: This term translates to "in the waters" or "in the flow." It can symbolize the fluidity of life and the importance of adaptability in our endeavors.
  • punanti: This means "purifies" or "cleanses." It indicates a process of refinement, suggesting that through nurturing and cultivation, one can achieve a state of purity or clarity.
  • dharṇasim: This word refers to "the one who holds" or "the supporter." It emphasizes the role of stability and support in the process of growth and purification.
  • dūtaṁ: This translates to "messenger" or "envoy." It signifies the importance of communication and the transmission of knowledge or wisdom.
  • na pūrvacittaya: This phrase means "not from previous thoughts" or "not from past intentions." It suggests the need to transcend past experiences and biases in order to embrace new insights.
  • ā śāsate: This means "they command" or "they govern." It indicates authority and the power of wise individuals to guide others.
  • manīṣiṇaḥ: This term refers to "the wise" or "the enlightened." It highlights the importance of wisdom in leadership and guidance.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of nurturing growth and development in a way that is eternal and pure. It teaches us that true wisdom comes from transcending past experiences and being open to new insights. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, leadership, and community building. For instance, a leader who cultivates a supportive environment fosters growth among team members, encouraging them to learn from their experiences while also being open to new ideas. This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, adaptability, and the continuous journey of self-improvement and purification in our lives.
