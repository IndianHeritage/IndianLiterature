पिबतं च तृप्णुतं चा च गच्छतं प्रजां च धत्तं द्रविणं च धत्तम् |सजोषसां उषसा सूर्येण चोर्जं नो धत्तम् अश्विनाः ||
pibataṁ ca tṛpṇutaṁ cā ca gacchatam prajāṁ ca dhattaṁ draviṇaṁ ca dhattam | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa corjaṁ no dhattam aśvinā ||
  • Mantra: "pibataṁ ca tṛpṇutaṁ cā ca gacchatam prajāṁ ca dhattaṁ draviṇaṁ ca dhattam | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa corjaṁ no dhattam aśvinā ||"
  • Translation: "May we drink and be satisfied; may we go forth with our progeny and wealth. With the dawn and the sun, may we not be deprived of energy, O Ashvins."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • pibataṁ: "drink" - This signifies the act of consuming, which can be interpreted as taking in knowledge, experiences, or nourishment.
    • tṛpṇutaṁ: "be satisfied" - This refers to the state of contentment and fulfillment, both physically and spiritually.
    • gacchatam: "may we go forth" - This implies a journey or progression in life, moving towards goals and aspirations.
    • prajāṁ: "progeny" - This represents offspring or the continuation of lineage, emphasizing the importance of family and community.
    • draviṇaṁ: "wealth" - This can refer to material wealth as well as spiritual richness, highlighting the need for abundance in life.
    • sajoṣasā: "with the dawn" - Symbolizes new beginnings and opportunities that come with each day.
    • uṣasā: "dawn" - Represents enlightenment and the arrival of knowledge.
    • sūryeṇa: "with the sun" - The sun is a source of energy and life, indicating vitality and strength.
    • corjaṁ: "energy" - Refers to the vigor and enthusiasm needed to pursue life’s endeavors.
    • no dhattam: "may we not be deprived" - A plea for abundance and the avoidance of scarcity.
    • aśvinā: "O Ashvins" - The Ashvins are twin deities associated with health, medicine, and vitality, invoking their blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, this mantra can be applied during daily rituals or prayers, where individuals seek fulfillment in their lives, both materially and spiritually.
    • In a community setting, it can be recited during gatherings or festivals to invoke blessings for prosperity and well-being for all members.
    • In a professional environment, it can serve as a reminder to pursue goals with energy and enthusiasm, ensuring that one does not lose sight of their aspirations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of nourishment, satisfaction, and the pursuit of wealth and progeny, reflecting a holistic approach to life.
    • Its significance lies in the balance it promotes between physical needs and spiritual fulfillment, encouraging individuals to seek abundance in all aspects of life.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a guiding principle for personal growth, community well-being, and professional success, reminding us to embrace each day with vigor and purpose.
