अतस्त्वा रयिमभि राजानं सुक्रतो दिवः |सुपर्णो व्यथिर भारत ||
atas tvā rayim abhi rājānaṁ sukrato divaḥ | suparṇo avyathir bharat ||
  • atas tvā rayim abhi rājānaṁ sukrato divaḥ
    • atas - This word translates to "therefore" or "hence," indicating a conclusion or a result.
    • tvā - This means "you," referring to a divine entity or a higher power.
    • rayim - This word signifies "wealth" or "prosperity," often in a spiritual or material sense.
    • abhi - This translates to "towards" or "in the direction of," suggesting an approach or a focus.
    • rājānaṁ - This means "kings" or "rulers," indicating authority figures or leaders.
    • sukrato - This translates to "of good deeds" or "well-doing," emphasizing virtuous actions.
    • divaḥ - This means "of the heavens" or "divine," connecting the mantra to celestial or spiritual realms.
  • suparṇo avyathir bharat
    • suparṇo - This word means "well-winged" or "having good wings," often symbolizing freedom, elevation, or divine qualities.
    • avyathir - This translates to "untroubled" or "without fear," indicating a state of peace and security.
    • bharat - This refers to "Bharat," which is another name for India, symbolizing the land and its people.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking prosperity and well-being through virtuous actions and divine guidance. It suggests that by focusing on good deeds and aligning oneself with higher powers, one can attain wealth and success. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can strive to perform good deeds and act ethically, leading to personal and professional growth.
  • In leadership, rulers and leaders are encouraged to act with integrity and virtue, ensuring the prosperity of their communities.
  • In spiritual practices, individuals can seek connection with the divine, fostering a sense of peace and security in their lives.

Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of good actions, divine support, and the attainment of prosperity, applicable in both personal and communal contexts.
