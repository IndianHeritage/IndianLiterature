श्रिये ते पृष्णिर उपसेचनī भूच्छ्रिये दर्वीर अरेपाः |यया स्वे पात्रे सिञ्चस उत ||
śriye te pṛśnir upasecanī bhūc chriye darvir arepāḥ | yayā sve pātre siñcasa ut ||
  • śriye te: This phrase translates to "for your prosperity" or "for your wealth." It signifies the importance of seeking abundance and well-being in life.
  • pṛśnir upasecanī: "Pṛśnir" refers to the earth or the ground, while "upasecanī" means "that which nourishes." Together, this suggests a connection to the nurturing aspects of the earth, emphasizing the need for a supportive environment for growth.
  • bhūc chriye: "Bhūc" means "earth" or "land," and "chriye" refers to "prosperity." This part of the mantra highlights the relationship between the earth and prosperity, indicating that a fertile land is essential for abundance.
  • darvir arepāḥ: "Darvir" can be interpreted as "wealth" or "riches," while "arepāḥ" suggests "to be attained" or "to be achieved." This phrase emphasizes the pursuit of wealth and the means to achieve it.
  • yayā sve pātre siñcasa ut: "Yayā" means "by which," "sve" refers to "one's own," "pātre" means "vessel" or "container," and "siñcasa ut" translates to "to pour forth." This part of the mantra conveys the idea of nurturing one's own potential and resources to achieve prosperity.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of prosperity, the nurturing qualities of the earth, and the importance of utilizing one's own resources to achieve wealth. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as sustainable farming practices, where individuals work in harmony with the land to cultivate crops that provide for their families and communities. It also resonates with the idea of personal development, where individuals are encouraged to invest in their own skills and talents to create opportunities for success. Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing both the earth and oneself to attain true prosperity.
