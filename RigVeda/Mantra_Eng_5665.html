यत्रा चक्रुर अमृतā गातुम अस्मै श्येनो न दीयन्न अन्व एति पाठः |प्रति वां सूर उदिते विद्हेम नमोभिर मित्रावरुणोता हव्यैः ||
yatrā cakrur amṛtā gātum asmai śyeno na dīyann anv eti pāthaḥ | prati vāṁ sūra udite vidhema namobhir mitrāvaruṇota havyaiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yatrā cakrur amṛtā gātum asmai śyeno na dīyann anv eti pāthaḥ | prati vāṁ sūra udite vidhema namobhir mitrāvaruṇota havyaiḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Where the immortal ones (amṛtā) have set their course, may the swift (śyena) not deny him the path. As the hero (sūra) rises, we shall invoke with our offerings (havyaiḥ) to Mitra and Varuna."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • yatrā: Where
    • cakrur: have set (as in a course or path)
    • amṛtā: immortal or eternal
    • gātum: to go or to proceed
    • asmai: to him
    • śyeno: swift or eagle, symbolizing speed and agility
    • na dīyann: may not deny
    • anv eti: follows or pursues
    • pāthaḥ: path or way
    • prati: towards
    • vāṁ: you (plural)
    • sūra: hero or warrior
    • udite: rising or emerging
    • vidhema: we shall invoke
    • namobhir: with our salutations or offerings
    • mitrāvaruṇota: to Mitra and Varuna (deities representing friendship and cosmic order)
    • havyaiḥ: with offerings or sacrifices

  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of following a righteous path that leads to immortality and enlightenment.
    • The mention of "immortal ones" signifies the pursuit of eternal truths and values that transcend the physical realm.
    • The swift eagle (śyena) symbolizes the need for agility and quickness in decision-making and actions in life.
    • Invoking Mitra and Varuna highlights the significance of maintaining harmony and balance in relationships and the universe.

  • Real-Life Application:
    • In personal development, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek a path of integrity and purpose, encouraging them to pursue their goals with determination and speed.
    • In relationships, it serves as a reminder to foster connections based on trust and mutual respect, akin to the friendship represented by Mitra.
    • In community and social settings, it encourages collective efforts towards a common good, invoking the spirit of cooperation and harmony as embodied by Varuna.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a profound reminder of the values of integrity, speed in action, and the importance of harmonious relationships.
    • Its teachings are applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to community building, urging individuals to align their actions with higher principles.
    • By invoking these ideals, one can navigate life's challenges with wisdom and purpose, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
