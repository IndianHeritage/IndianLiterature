आ नो विश्व आस्क्रागमन्तु देवाः मित्रो अर्यमाः वरुणः सजोषाः |भुवन यथा नो विश्वे वृद्धासः करण सुशाहाः विथुरं न शवः ||
ā no viśva āskrā gamantu devā mitro aryamā varuṇaḥ sajoṣāḥ | bhuvan yathā no viśve vṛdhāsaḥ karan suṣāhā vithuraṁ na śavaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā no: May we (us)
    • viśva: all (the universe)
    • āskrā: come together (unite)
    • gamantu: may they go (move)
    • devā: the gods
    • mitro: friend (Mitra, the god of friendship)
    • aryamā: noble (Aryaman, a deity associated with social order)
    • varuṇaḥ: Varuna (the god of cosmic order and water)
    • sajoṣāḥ: with harmony (together in joy)
    • bhuvan: the world (universe)
    • yathā: as (in the way)
    • no: us (our)
    • viśve: all (the universe)
    • vṛdhāsaḥ: may they grow (prosper)
    • karan: doing (action)
    • suṣāhā: good (well)
    • vithuraṁ: wealth (prosperity)
    • na: not
    • śavaḥ: dead (lifeless)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the unity of all beings and the divine forces to come together for the welfare of humanity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of friendship, harmony, and cooperation among individuals and communities.
    • The mention of gods like Mitra, Aryaman, and Varuna signifies the divine support in maintaining social order and prosperity.
    • It encourages collective growth and prosperity, urging that actions should lead to beneficial outcomes for all.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, fostering a culture of teamwork and collaboration can lead to greater productivity and job satisfaction.
    • Community initiatives that bring people together for a common cause, such as environmental clean-ups or charity events, exemplify the spirit of this mantra.
    • In personal relationships, nurturing friendships and supporting one another can create a harmonious and fulfilling life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity, cooperation, and divine support in achieving collective goals.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, community building, and professional environments.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and societies can work towards a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
