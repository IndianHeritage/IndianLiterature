प्राव स्तोतारं मगवन्न अव त्वां पिबा सोमं मदाय कं शतक्रतो |यं ते भागम् अधारयन विश्वाः सेहानः पृतना उरु ज्रयः सम अप्सुजिन मरुत्वां इन्द्र सत्पते ||
prāva stotāram maghavann ava tvām pibā somam madāya kaṁ śatakrato | yaṁ te bhāgam adhārayan viśvāḥ sehānaḥ pṛtanā uru jrayaḥ sam apsujin marutvām̐ indra satpate ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O Indra, the great one, we invoke you as the stotāra (the one who is praised). May you drink the soma for our delight, O hundred-folded one. Those who have upheld your share, may all beings, with their strength, support you, O Indra, the lord of the satpati (the one who is worthy of praise)."
  • Meaning of Key Terms:
    • Indra: The king of the gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength, power, and the force of nature.
    • Soma: A sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
    • Stotāra: One who is praised or worshipped, indicating the importance of reverence in spiritual practices.
    • Śatakrato: Literally means "the one with a hundred powers," referring to Indra's immense capabilities.
    • Viśvāḥ: All beings or the universe, emphasizing the interconnectedness of life.
    • Marut: The storm deities, representing the forces of nature and the power of the wind.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, the invocation of Indra can be seen in rituals where people seek strength and guidance in overcoming challenges, much like athletes invoking their inner strength before a competition.
    • The concept of soma can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, akin to how individuals today seek inspiration through meditation or spiritual practices.
    • The idea of supporting a leader or a cause resonates in community efforts where individuals come together to uplift their leaders or support social movements, reflecting the collective strength mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support and strength in the face of challenges. It emphasizes the importance of community and collective effort in achieving goals. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as team sports, community service, or any collaborative effort where individuals come together to support a common cause. By invoking the qualities of Indra, individuals can find inspiration to harness their inner strength and work towards a greater purpose, fostering unity and resilience in their endeavors.
