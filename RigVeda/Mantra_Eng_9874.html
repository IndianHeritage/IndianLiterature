हये जये मनसा तिष्ठ घोरे वचांसि मिश्राकृतवावहै नु |न नउ मन्त्राः अनुदितास एते मयस् करण परतरे चनाहन् ||
haye jāye manasā tiṣṭha ghore vacāṁsi miśrā kṛṇavāvahai nu | na nau mantrā anuditāsa ete mayas karan paratare canāhan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • haye: O, (an invocation)
    • jāye: to arise or to come forth
    • manasā: with the mind
    • tiṣṭha: stand or remain
    • ghore: fierce or intense
    • vacāṁsi: words or speech
    • miśrā: mixed or blended
    • kṛṇavāvahai: we create or we bring forth
    • nu: indeed or surely
    • na: not
    • nau: nine (often symbolic of completeness)
    • mantrā: mantras or sacred utterances
    • anuditāsa: unspoken or unheard
    • ete: these
    • mayas: of the mind or mental
    • karan: actions or deeds
    • paratare: beyond or superior
    • canāhan: to be realized or to be understood
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the power of the mind and speech, emphasizing the importance of focused thought and expression.
    • The phrase "haye jāye manasā tiṣṭha" suggests that one should stand firm with their mind, indicating the need for mental strength and clarity.
    • "Ghore vacāṁsi miśrā" points to the intensity of words, highlighting that our speech can be powerful and impactful, whether positive or negative.
    • The mantra also reflects on the creation of reality through our thoughts and words, as seen in "kṛṇavāvahai nu," suggesting that we manifest our experiences through our mental and verbal expressions.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "na nau mantrā anuditāsa ete," implies that there are unspoken truths and potential within us that can be realized through conscious action and thought.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often emphasize the power of positive thinking and affirmations, which aligns with the idea of manifesting reality through focused thought.
    • In communication, the impact of words can be seen in various contexts, such as public speaking, where the choice of words can inspire or demoralize an audience.
    • In conflict resolution, understanding the intensity of speech can help in navigating difficult conversations, ensuring that words are chosen carefully to foster understanding rather than hostility.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the profound connection between our thoughts, words, and the reality we create.
    • It encourages mindfulness in our speech and mental processes, urging us to harness the power of our minds to create positive outcomes in our lives.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in daily life by practicing self-awareness, choosing words wisely, and cultivating a positive mindset to influence our surroundings and relationships.
