विष्वेभ्यो हि त्वा भुवनेभ्यस् परि त्वष्टाजनत साम्नः-साम्नः कविः |स ऋणचिद ऋणया ब्रह्मणस् पतिर् द्रुहो हन्ता मह ऋतस्य धार्तरि ||
viśvebhyo hi tvā bhuvanebhyas pari tvaṣṭājanat sāmnaḥ-sāmnaḥ kaviḥ | sa ṛṇacid ṛṇayā brahmaṇas patir druho hantā maha ṛtasya dhartari ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O Creator, you are the one who has created all beings in the universe. You are the wise one who knows everything. You are the one who brings forth the essence of all things, and you are the protector of truth and righteousness."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • viśvebhyo: From all beings or all creatures.
    • hi: Indeed or surely.
    • tvā: You.
    • bhuvanebhyas: From the worlds or the universe.
    • pari: Around or in all directions.
    • tvaṣṭā: The creator or the one who shapes.
    • janat: Of beings or creatures.
    • sāmnaḥ-sāmnaḥ: Of the essence or the fundamental nature.
    • kaviḥ: The wise one or the seer.
    • sa: He or that one.
    • ṛṇacid: Who knows or understands.
    • ṛṇayā: By the essence or by the truth.
    • brahmaṇas: Of the ultimate reality or the cosmic spirit.
    • patir: The lord or the master.
    • druho: The one who removes obstacles or negativity.
    • hantā: The destroyer or the one who eliminates.
    • maha: Great or supreme.
    • ṛtasya: Of truth or righteousness.
    • dhartari: The one who sustains or upholds.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of creation and the interconnectedness of all beings. For instance, in environmental conservation, recognizing that all living beings are part of a larger ecosystem can inspire actions to protect nature.
    • The idea of a wise creator can be seen in leadership roles where individuals are expected to guide and shape their communities with knowledge and foresight, similar to how a teacher shapes the minds of students.
    • The concept of truth and righteousness is applicable in everyday life, such as in ethical decision-making in business, where integrity and honesty are crucial for long-term success.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for recognizing the divine presence in all aspects of life. It encourages individuals to embrace their role as creators and protectors of truth and righteousness. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire actions that promote harmony, sustainability, and ethical behavior, reminding us that we are all interconnected and responsible for the well-being of the universe.
