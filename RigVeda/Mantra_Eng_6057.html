अभि त्वा पूर्वपीतय इन्द्र स्तोमेभिर आयवः |समीचीनासा ऋभवः सम अस्वरन् रुद्राः गृणन्त पूर्व्यम् ||
abhi tvā pūrvapītaya indra stomebhir āyavaḥ | samīcīnāsa ṛbhavaḥ sam asvaran rudrā gṛṇanta pūrvyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, for
    • tvā: you
    • pūrvapītaya: ancient, primordial
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • stomebhir: with hymns, praises
    • āyavaḥ: we invoke, we call upon
    • samīcīnāsa: those who are well-versed, knowledgeable
    • ṛbhavaḥ: the wise, the enlightened
    • sam: together, in unison
    • asvaran: they sing, they chant
    • rudrā: the fierce, the powerful (often referring to Rudra, a form of Shiva)
    • gṛṇanta: they praise, they sing
    • pūrvyam: the ancient, the primordial
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine, specifically to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic tradition. It emphasizes the importance of invoking the ancient powers and wisdom.
    • The phrase "abhi tvā pūrvapītaya" signifies a respectful approach towards the divine, acknowledging the ancient wisdom and strength that Indra embodies.
    • By using "stomebhir āyavaḥ," the mantra highlights the significance of hymns and praises in connecting with the divine. In real life, this can be likened to how people use music and poetry to express their emotions and connect with something greater than themselves.
    • The mention of "samīcīnāsa ṛbhavaḥ" suggests that those who are knowledgeable and wise are being called upon. This can be related to seeking guidance from mentors or elders in our lives who possess wisdom and experience.
    • The collective action of "sam asvaran rudrā gṛṇanta" indicates a communal effort in praising the divine. This can be seen in modern-day practices where communities come together for worship, celebrations, or even social causes, emphasizing unity and collective strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine wisdom and strength through collective praise and respect. It teaches us that in our lives, we should seek guidance from the wise and come together as a community to honor and celebrate the forces that guide us.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as community gatherings, spiritual practices, or even in professional settings where teamwork and collective effort are essential for success.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of wisdom, community, and the divine, encouraging us to honor these elements in our daily lives.
