किम अन्ये पर्यासतेऽस्मत् स्तोमेभिर अश्विनाः |पुत्रः कण्वस्य वाम ऋषिः गीरभिः वत्सो अवीवृधत् ||
kim anye pary āsate 'smat stomebhir aśvinā | putraḥ kaṇvasya vām ṛṣir gīrbhir vatso avīvṛdhat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kim anye: What else
    • pary āsate: is there that surrounds
    • 'smat: from us
    • stomebhir: with praises
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin deities)
    • putraḥ: the son
    • kaṇvasya: of Kanva (a sage)
    • vām: your
    • ṛṣir: sage
    • gīrbhir: with hymns
    • vatso: the calf
    • avīvṛdhat: has grown
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses the Ashvins, who are revered as divine physicians and bringers of health and prosperity.
    • The speaker is inquiring about what else surrounds them, emphasizing the importance of community and support.
    • The mention of "praises" indicates a call for recognition of the divine and the blessings that come from it.
    • The reference to the son of Kanva suggests a lineage of wisdom and the importance of knowledge passed down through generations.
    • The phrase "with hymns" highlights the role of music and poetry in spiritual practices, serving as a medium to connect with the divine.
    • The "calf" symbolizes new beginnings, growth, and nurturing, indicating that the blessings of the Ashvins lead to prosperity and development.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of community support can be seen in various forms, such as family gatherings, community service, and social networks that provide emotional and practical assistance.
    • The practice of praising and recognizing the contributions of others can be observed in workplaces where employee recognition programs foster a positive environment.
    • In educational settings, the passing down of knowledge from one generation to another is crucial, as seen in mentorship programs where experienced individuals guide the youth.
    • Health and wellness practices today often incorporate music and art, similar to the hymns mentioned in the mantra, to promote mental and emotional well-being.
    • The nurturing aspect represented by the "calf" can be related to parenting and mentorship, where individuals help others grow and develop their potential.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of individuals within a community and the importance of divine blessings in fostering growth and prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder to appreciate the support we receive from others and to recognize the divine presence in our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, the teachings of this mantra can be applied to enhance relationships, promote well-being, and encourage the sharing of knowledge and resources.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the significance of gratitude, community, and the nurturing of growth in all aspects of life.
