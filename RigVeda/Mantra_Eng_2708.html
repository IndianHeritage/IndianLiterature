इन्द्र दृष्य यामकोशा अभूवन यज्ञाय शिक्ष गृणते सखिभ्यः |दुर्मायवो दुरेव मर्त्यासो निषङ्गिणो रिपवो हन्त्वासः ||
indra dṛhya yāmakośā abhūvan yajñāya śikṣa gṛṇate sakhibhyaḥ | durmāyavo durevā martyāso niṣaṅgiṇo ripavo hantvāsaḥ ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
  • Dṛhya: Means firm or strong, indicating stability and resilience.
  • Yāmakośā: Can be interpreted as the one who is well-protected or safeguarded.
  • Abhūvan: Translates to "they have become" or "they have manifested," suggesting a transformation or realization.
  • Yajñāya: Refers to sacrifice or offerings, often associated with rituals that seek divine blessings.
  • Śikṣa: Means instruction or teaching, indicating the importance of knowledge and guidance.
  • Gṛṇate: Translates to "they sing" or "they praise," highlighting the act of devotion and reverence.
  • Sakhibhyaḥ: Refers to friends or allies, emphasizing the importance of community and support.
  • Durmāyavo: Means those who are difficult to overcome or challenging, representing obstacles or adversities.
  • Durevā: Refers to the difficult or formidable, often associated with negative forces or enemies.
  • Martyāso: Translates to "mortals," indicating human beings and their struggles.
  • Niṣaṅgiṇo: Means those who are armed or equipped, suggesting readiness for battle or conflict.
  • Ripavo: Refers to enemies or adversaries, highlighting the presence of challenges in life.
  • Hantvāsaḥ: Translates to "having defeated" or "having vanquished," indicating victory over challenges.

This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, community, and the pursuit of knowledge in overcoming life's challenges. It invokes the divine support of Indra, who represents power and leadership, encouraging individuals to seek guidance and support from their allies. The mantra also acknowledges the presence of difficulties and adversities in life, but it reassures that with resilience and the right teachings, one can overcome these obstacles.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Personal Development: Individuals can draw strength from their community and seek knowledge to overcome personal challenges.
  • Teamwork: In professional settings, the mantra highlights the importance of collaboration and support among team members to achieve common goals.
  • Conflict Resolution: The acknowledgment of adversities encourages individuals to face conflicts head-on, armed with knowledge and support from allies.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the significance of strength, community, and knowledge in navigating the complexities of life. It encourages individuals to seek divine guidance, support from friends, and the wisdom to overcome challenges, ultimately leading to personal and collective growth.
