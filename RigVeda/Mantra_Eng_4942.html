याम् पूषण ब्रह्मचोदनीम् आराम् बिभर्ष्य आगृह्णे |तया समस्यम् हृदयम् आ रिख किकिरा कृणु ||
yām pūṣan brahmacodanīm ārām bibharṣy āghṛṇe | tayā samasya hṛdayam ā rikha kikirā kṛṇu ||
  • yām pūṣan: This phrase refers to "O Pūṣan," where Pūṣan is a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity. In real life, this can be likened to the importance of seeking sustenance and support in our daily lives, whether it be through food, relationships, or spiritual guidance.
  • brahmacodanīm: This term translates to "the divine inspiration or guidance." It emphasizes the need for higher wisdom and direction in our actions. For example, in a professional setting, seeking mentorship or guidance from experienced individuals can lead to better decision-making and success.
  • ārām bibharṣy āghṛṇe: This can be interpreted as "may you carry the essence of the sacred." It suggests the importance of embodying values and principles that are considered sacred or virtuous. In everyday life, this could mean living with integrity, compassion, and respect for others.
  • tayā samasya hṛdayam: This phrase means "with that, may our hearts be united." It highlights the significance of unity and harmony among individuals. In a community or family setting, fostering understanding and cooperation can lead to stronger bonds and collective well-being.
  • ā rikha kikirā kṛṇu: This translates to "may we attain the highest truth." It signifies the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment. In real life, this can be seen in the quest for education, self-improvement, and the search for deeper meaning in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking nourishment, divine guidance, unity, and the pursuit of truth. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, such as personal growth, community building, and ethical living. By embodying the principles expressed in this mantra, individuals can strive for a more fulfilling and harmonious existence, both personally and collectively.
