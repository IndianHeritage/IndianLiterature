मन्त्र: दिवस्पृथिव्योऽव आ वृणीमहे मातृन् सिन्धून पर्वतान् चर्यणावतः | अनागास्त्वं सूर्यं उषासम् ईमहे भद्रं सोमः सुवानो अद्य कृतोतु नः ||
divaspṛthivyor ava ā vṛṇīmahe mātṝn sindhūn parvatāñ charyaṇāvataḥ | anāgāstvaṁ sūryam uṣāsam īmahe bhadraṁ somaḥ suvāno adyā kṛṇotu naḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • divaspṛthivyor: of the heavens and the earth
    • ava: down
    • ā: towards
    • vṛṇīmahe: we choose
    • mātṝn: mothers
    • sindhūn: rivers
    • parvatāñ: mountains
    • charyaṇāvataḥ: who are the source of nourishment
    • anāgāstvaṁ: free from sin
    • sūryam: the sun
    • uṣāsam: the dawn
    • īmahe: we invoke
    • bhadraṁ: auspicious
    • somaḥ: the moon
    • suvāno: well-formed
    • adyā: today
    • kṛṇotu: may grant
    • naḥ: to us
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer that seeks blessings from the divine forces of nature, specifically the heavens and the earth.
    • It emphasizes the importance of nurturing relationships with the elements that sustain life, represented by "mātṝn" (mothers), which can be interpreted as the rivers and mountains that provide resources and nourishment.
    • The invocation of "anāgāstvaṁ" (free from sin) signifies a desire for purity and righteousness in our actions and thoughts.
    • By calling upon "sūryam" (the sun) and "uṣāsam" (the dawn), the mantra acknowledges the cyclical nature of life and the importance of light and energy in our daily existence.
    • The mention of "bhadraṁ somaḥ" (auspicious moon) reflects the balance between the sun and the moon, symbolizing harmony in life.
    • Overall, the mantra is a holistic prayer for well-being, prosperity, and spiritual growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for the blessings of the sun and rain (rivers) to ensure a good harvest, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek to cultivate positive relationships with their environment, akin to nurturing the "mātṝn" (mothers) in the mantra.
    • During festivals, people invoke the sun and moon for prosperity and health, mirroring the invocation in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of nature and humanity, urging us to respect and honor the elements that sustain us.
    • Its significance lies in promoting a balanced and harmonious life, encouraging us to seek blessings from both the physical and spiritual realms.
    • In modern times, this mantra can inspire environmental consciousness and a deeper appreciation for the natural world, reminding us of our responsibility to protect and cherish it.
