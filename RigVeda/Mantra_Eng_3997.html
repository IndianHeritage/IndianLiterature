अच्छा मही बृहतī शान्तमā गीर दूतो न गन्त्व अश्विनā हुवध्यै |मयोभुवā सरथā यातम अर्वाग गन्तं निधिं धुरम् आणिर न नाभिम ||
acchā mahī bṛhatī śaṁtamā gīr dūto na gantv aśvinā huvadhyai | mayobhuvā sarathā yātam arvāg gantaṁ nidhiṁ dhuram āṇir na nābhim ||
  • acchā - This word translates to "good" or "excellent." It signifies positivity and auspiciousness.
  • mahī - Refers to the "earth" or "greatness." It symbolizes the vastness and nurturing aspect of the earth.
  • bṛhatī - Meaning "great" or "broad," it emphasizes the expansive nature of existence.
  • śaṁtamā - This translates to "peaceful" or "calm," indicating a state of tranquility.
  • gīr - Refers to "speech" or "words," highlighting the importance of communication and expression.
  • dūto - Meaning "messenger," it signifies the role of communication in conveying messages and ideas.
  • na gantv - Translates to "not going," suggesting a sense of stability or remaining in place.
  • aśvinā - Refers to the "Ashvins," the twin deities associated with health and medicine, symbolizing healing and well-being.
  • huvadhyai - Meaning "to invoke," it indicates the act of calling upon divine forces or energies.
  • mayobhuvā - Translates to "with my mind," emphasizing the power of thought and intention.
  • sarathā - Meaning "with chariots," it symbolizes movement and progress in life.
  • yātam - Refers to "to go" or "to move," indicating the journey of life.
  • arvāg - Meaning "forward," it signifies progress and advancement.
  • gantaṁ - Translates to "to go" or "to reach," indicating the goal or destination.
  • nidhiṁ - Refers to "treasure" or "wealth," symbolizing the valuable aspects of life.
  • dhuram - Meaning "heavy" or "burden," it indicates the challenges and responsibilities we carry.
  • āṇir - Translates to "command" or "order," emphasizing the importance of direction and leadership.
  • na nābhim - Meaning "not the navel," it suggests a point of origin or center, indicating stability and grounding.

The mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of life, emphasizing the importance of communication, intention, and the journey we undertake. It highlights the need for peace and stability while acknowledging the challenges we face. The invocation of the Ashvins suggests a call for healing and well-being in our lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Personal Growth: The emphasis on intention and communication can guide individuals in their personal development journeys, encouraging them to express themselves and seek healing.
  • Community Building: The idea of being a messenger can inspire individuals to foster connections within their communities, promoting understanding and collaboration.
  • Leadership: The call for direction and command can be relevant in leadership roles, where guiding others towards a common goal is essential.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of maintaining peace, expressing ourselves, and moving forward in life while acknowledging the challenges we face. It encourages us to seek healing and connection, making it applicable in various aspects of our daily lives.
