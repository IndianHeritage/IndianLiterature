एष विप्रैः अभिष्टुतोऽपो देवो वि गाहते |दधद् रत्नानि दाशुषे ||
eṣa viprair abhiṣṭuto 'po devo vi gāhate | dadhad ratnāni dāśuṣe ||
  • Mantra: eṣa viprair abhiṣṭuto 'po devo vi gāhate | dadhad ratnāni dāśuṣe ||
  • Translation: "This (sacrifice) is praised by the wise; the divine waters flow forth. May He bestow treasures upon the worshipper."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • eṣa: This - Referring to the act of worship or sacrifice.
    • viprair: By the wise - Those who possess knowledge and understanding, often referring to sages or learned individuals.
    • abhiṣṭuto: Praised - Acknowledged and honored through words and actions.
    • 'apo: Waters - Symbolizing purity, life, and nourishment, often associated with divine blessings.
    • devo: Divine - Pertaining to the gods or higher powers.
    • vi: Forth - Indicating movement or flow, often used in the context of blessings or gifts being bestowed.
    • gāhate: Flow - The act of flowing, often used to describe the movement of water or blessings.
    • dadhad: Bestow - To give or grant, especially in a generous manner.
    • ratnāni: Treasures - Referring to valuable gifts, both material and spiritual.
    • dāśuṣe: Worshipper - The one who performs the rituals or sacrifices, seeking divine favor.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate a festival, they often perform rituals that are praised by the elders (viprair). This collective act is similar to the mantra, where the community seeks blessings (apo) for prosperity and harmony.
    • In personal life, when an individual engages in acts of kindness and charity, they are often recognized and appreciated by others. This recognition can be seen as a form of praise (abhiṣṭuto) that encourages further good deeds.
    • In environmental conservation efforts, when communities work together to protect water bodies, they are essentially invoking the divine (devo) blessings for a sustainable future, akin to the flow of divine waters (gāhate).

  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the power of collective worship and the pursuit of divine blessings through wise actions. It highlights the importance of community, knowledge, and the flow of positive energy in our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as community service, environmental sustainability, and personal growth. By engaging in acts that are praised by the wise and seeking blessings for ourselves and others, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous society.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that our actions, when aligned with wisdom and community spirit, can lead to the bestowal of treasures—both material and spiritual—upon ourselves and those around us.
