इळाम अग्ने पुरुदंसं सनिं गोः शश्वत्तमं हवमानाया साधा |स्यान नः सूनुस तनयो विजावाग्ने सा ते सुमतिर भूत्व अस्मे ||
iḻām agne purudaṁsaṁ saniṁ goḥ śaśvattamaṁ havamānāya sādha | syān naḥ sūnus tanayo vijāvāgne sā te sumatir bhūtv asme ||
  • iḻām agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni," referring to the Vedic fire god, Agni, who symbolizes energy, light, and the divine messenger between humans and the gods.
  • purudaṁsaṁ: This means "the one who is rich in strength" or "the powerful one," highlighting Agni's role as a source of strength and vitality.
  • saniṁ goḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is the protector of the cattle," indicating Agni's role in safeguarding wealth and prosperity, particularly in agrarian societies where cattle were vital.
  • śaśvattamaṁ: This means "the eternal one," emphasizing Agni's timeless and unchanging nature, representing the eternal flame of life and knowledge.
  • havamānāya: This translates to "for the one who is invoked," suggesting that Agni is called upon for blessings and guidance in rituals and daily life.
  • sādha: This means "to fulfill" or "to accomplish," indicating the desire for Agni to help achieve one's goals and aspirations.
  • syān naḥ sūnus: This translates to "may our offspring be," expressing a wish for prosperity and the continuation of lineage.
  • tanayo vijāvāgne: This means "may our children be wise," indicating the importance of wisdom and intelligence in future generations.
  • sā te sumatir bhūtv asme: This translates to "may we have good thoughts from you," emphasizing the desire for divine guidance in cultivating positive and constructive thoughts.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for strength, prosperity, wisdom, and positive thoughts. It reflects the interconnectedness of human aspirations with divine support.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be recited during significant events such as weddings, childbirth, or new ventures, where blessings for strength, wisdom, and prosperity are sought. It serves as a reminder to cultivate positive thoughts and intentions in our daily lives.

  • Conclusion: The mantra highlights the importance of invoking divine energy for personal and communal growth. By recognizing the role of Agni as a source of strength and wisdom, individuals can align their aspirations with higher principles, fostering a life of purpose and fulfillment.
