आ यद इषे नृपतिं तेज आनाṭ छुचि रेतो निषिक्तं द्यौर अभीक | अग्निः शर्धम अनवद्यं युवानं स्वाध्यं जनयत सूदयच च ||
ā yad iṣe nṛpatiṁ teja ānaṭ chuci reto niṣiktaṁ dyaur abhīke | agniḥ śardham anavadyaṁ yuvānaṁ svādhyaṁ janayat sūdayac ca ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yad: Whatever
    • iṣe: Is desired
    • nṛpatiṁ: The king or ruler
    • teja: Radiance or brilliance
    • ānaṭ: May it come
    • chuci: Pure
    • reto: Energy or vitality
    • niṣiktaṁ: Infused or filled
    • dyaur: The sky or heavens
    • abhīke: With auspiciousness
    • agniḥ: Fire
    • śardham: In the season
    • anavadyaṁ: Without blemish
    • yuvānaṁ: Young or youthful
    • svādhyaṁ: Self-sufficient or self-reliant
    • janayat: Generates or creates
    • sūdayac: May it purify
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer for the arrival of pure energy and brilliance that can empower a ruler or leader.
    • The invocation of Agni (fire) symbolizes transformation, purification, and the generation of vitality.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of purity and auspiciousness in leadership, suggesting that a ruler should embody these qualities to effectively govern.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies integrity and clarity can inspire their team, much like the "radiance" mentioned in the mantra.
    • In community leadership, individuals who act with purity of intention and seek the welfare of others can create a positive impact, reflecting the qualities of a "nṛpatiṁ" or ruler.
    • During significant events, such as weddings or festivals, invoking the essence of Agni can symbolize the purification of intentions and the generation of positive energy among participants.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of purity, brilliance, and the transformative power of fire in leadership and personal conduct.
    • It serves as a reminder that effective leadership is rooted in self-reliance and the ability to inspire others through one's actions and intentions.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more effective governance, stronger communities, and personal growth.
