मन्त्र: स्थिरं हि जानम् एषां वयो मातुर निरेतवे | यत सिम अनु द्विताश शवः ||
sthiraṁ hi jānam eṣāṁ vayo mātur niretave | yat sīm anu dvitā śavaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sthiraṁ: stable, firm, or enduring
    • hi: indeed
    • jānam: the people or beings
    • eṣāṁ: of these
    • vayo: age or life span
    • mātur: of the mother
    • niretave: who is not to be disturbed or shaken
    • yat: that which
    • sīm: boundary or limit
    • anu: following or in accordance with
    • dvitā: the second or dual
    • śavaḥ: corpse or dead body
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the stability and endurance of life, particularly in relation to the mother and the life she nurtures.
    • It emphasizes the importance of a stable foundation in life, which is often provided by maternal care and guidance.
    • The reference to "the second" and "corpse" suggests a contemplation on life and death, and the boundaries that define our existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, a mother's role is often seen as the cornerstone of stability. For instance, a mother who provides emotional support and guidance helps her children navigate life's challenges, ensuring they grow up with a strong sense of self.
    • In a broader societal context, communities that prioritize maternal health and support systems tend to have better outcomes in terms of education, health, and overall well-being.
    • The concept of boundaries can be seen in personal relationships, where understanding and respecting limits can lead to healthier interactions and prevent conflicts.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of stability in life, particularly through the nurturing role of mothers.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of creating a supportive environment for growth and development, both personally and within communities.
    • In real life, applying the principles of stability, respect for boundaries, and nurturing relationships can lead to a more harmonious existence.
