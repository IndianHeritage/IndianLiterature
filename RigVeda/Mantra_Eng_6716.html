यो विश्वान्य अभि व्रतā सोमस्य मदे अन्धसः | इन्द्रो देवेषु चेतति ||
yo viśvāny abhi vratā somasya made andhasaḥ | indro deveṣu cetati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • viśvāny: all, every
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • vratā: vows, commitments
    • somasya: of the Soma (a sacred drink)
    • made: intoxication, ecstasy
    • andhasaḥ: of the blind, darkness
    • indro: Indra (the king of gods)
    • deveṣu: among the gods
    • cetati: thinks, contemplates
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine being, Indra, who is aware of the commitments made by all beings towards the sacred Soma.
    • The reference to "Soma" symbolizes not just a drink but also the essence of life, joy, and spiritual ecstasy.
    • The phrase "abhi vratā" indicates the importance of vows and commitments in life, suggesting that one should strive towards higher ideals.
    • The mention of "andhasaḥ" implies overcoming ignorance or darkness through knowledge and divine guidance.
    • Indra's contemplation among the gods signifies the importance of divine wisdom and leadership in guiding humanity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the commitments we make in our personal and professional lives. For instance, a person who vows to uphold ethical standards in their workplace is embodying the spirit of "vratā."
    • The concept of "Soma" can be likened to the pursuit of happiness and fulfillment in life, where individuals seek experiences that bring joy and ecstasy, such as art, music, or community service.
    • Overcoming "andhasaḥ" can be seen in educational pursuits where individuals strive to gain knowledge and wisdom, moving from ignorance to enlightenment.
    • Indra's role as a leader can be mirrored in contemporary leaders who inspire and guide their communities towards collective goals and aspirations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of commitment, the pursuit of joy, and the importance of divine guidance in overcoming ignorance.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to uphold our vows, seek joy in our endeavors, and strive for knowledge and wisdom.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to reflect on their actions and their impact on the world, fostering a sense of responsibility and purpose.
