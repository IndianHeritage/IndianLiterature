त्वाम īḻते अजिरं दूत्याय हविष्मन्तः सदम इन मानवासः |यस्य देवैर आसदो बर्हिर अग्ने 'हाण्य अस्मै सुदिनाऽ भवन्ति ||
tvām īḻate ajiraṁ dūtyāya haviṣmantaḥ sadam in mānuṣāsaḥ | yasya devair āsado barhir agne 'hāny asmai sudinā bhavanti ||
  • tvām - This word translates to "you," referring to Agni, the fire god, who is central to Vedic rituals.
  • īḻate - This means "to be invoked" or "to be worshipped," indicating the act of calling upon Agni for blessings.
  • ajiraṁ - This term means "immortal" or "everlasting," highlighting the eternal nature of Agni.
  • dūtyāya - This translates to "as a messenger," suggesting that Agni serves as a conduit between humans and the divine.
  • haviṣmantaḥ - This word refers to "those who offer oblations," emphasizing the importance of offerings in rituals.
  • sadam - This means "in the abode" or "in the dwelling," indicating a sacred space where rituals take place.
  • in mānuṣāsaḥ - This phrase translates to "among humans," signifying the connection between the divine and human realms.
  • yasya - This means "whose," referring to Agni's attributes and powers.
  • devair - This translates to "by the gods," indicating the divine support and blessings that Agni receives.
  • āsado - This means "sitting" or "residing," suggesting Agni's presence in the sacred space.
  • barhir - This term refers to "the sacred grass" used in rituals, symbolizing purity and sanctity.
  • agne - This is a direct address to Agni, reinforcing the focus of the mantra on the fire god.
  • 'hāny - This means "may he grant," indicating a request for blessings and support.
  • asmai - This translates to "to him," referring to Agni as the recipient of the offerings.
  • sudinā - This means "with good fortune," suggesting the desire for auspicious outcomes.
  • bhavanti - This translates to "may they become," indicating the transformation of offerings into blessings.

The mantra essentially invokes Agni, the fire god, as a messenger between humans and the divine, emphasizing the importance of rituals and offerings in establishing a connection with the divine. It highlights Agni's eternal nature and his role in bestowing blessings upon those who worship him.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: The mantra can be recited during religious ceremonies to invoke divine blessings, ensuring that the rituals are performed with sincerity and devotion.
  • Personal Growth: The invocation of Agni can symbolize the inner fire or passion that drives individuals to pursue their goals and aspirations, encouraging them to seek divine guidance in their endeavors.
  • Community Gatherings: In communal settings, reciting this mantra can foster a sense of unity and shared purpose, as participants collectively seek blessings for prosperity and well-being.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of divine connection through rituals and offerings. It emphasizes the role of Agni as a mediator between the human and divine realms, encouraging individuals to seek blessings for good fortune and auspicious outcomes in their lives.
