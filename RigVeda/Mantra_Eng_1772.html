अतो वयं अन्तमेभिर्युजानाḥ स्वकक्षेत्रभिस् तन्‍वः शुम्भमानाः |महोभिर् एतां उप युज्महे न्व इन्द्र स्वधाम् अनु हि नो बभूथा ||
ato vayam antamebhir yujānāḥ svakṣatrebhis tanva1ḥ śumbhamānāḥ | mahobhir etām̐ upa yujmahe nv indra svadhām anu hi no babhūtha ||
  • Translation: "Therefore, we, who are united with the ultimate, with our own bodies, are endowed with strength. With great power, we join together, O Indra, may we not be deprived of our own abode."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Ato: Therefore
    • Vayam: We
    • Antamebhir: With the ultimate
    • Yujānāḥ: United
    • Svakṣatrebhis: With our own bodies
    • Tanvaḥ: Endowed with strength
    • Śumbhamānāḥ: With great power
    • Mahobhir: With great
    • Etām̐: This
    • Upa: Together
    • Yujmahe: We join
    • Nv: O
    • Indra: Indra (the deity)
    • Svadhām: Our own abode
    • Anu: Following
    • Hi: Indeed
    • No: Us
    • Babhūtha: May not be deprived
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and strength among individuals. It suggests that when people come together with a common purpose, they can achieve great things.
    • In real life, this can be seen in various scenarios such as teamwork in a corporate environment, where collaboration leads to successful project completion.
    • Another example is community service, where individuals unite to help those in need, showcasing the power of collective effort.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra highlights the significance of unity and strength in achieving goals. It serves as a reminder that when individuals come together, they can create a powerful force that can overcome challenges.
    • In modern life, this principle can be applied in various fields such as education, sports, and social movements, where collective action leads to positive change.
    • Ultimately, the mantra encourages us to recognize the strength in unity and to strive for collaboration in our endeavors.
