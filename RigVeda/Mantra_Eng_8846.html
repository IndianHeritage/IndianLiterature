स्वयं यजस्व दिवि देव देवान किं ते पाकः कृणवद अप्रचेताः |यथायज ऋतुभिर देव देवान एव यजस्व तन्वं सुजात ||
svayaṁ yajasva divi deva devān kiṁ te pākaḥ kṛṇavad apracetāḥ | yathāyaja ṛtubhir deva devān evā yajasva tanvaṁ sujāta ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • svayaṁ: Yourself
    • yajasva: Perform worship
    • divi: In the heavens
    • deva: God
    • devān: The divine beings
    • kiṁ: What
    • te: To you
    • pākaḥ: The cook or the one who prepares
    • kṛṇavad: Who has made
    • apracetāḥ: Without understanding
    • yathāyaja: As you perform
    • ṛtubhir: With the seasons
    • deva devān: O divine beings
    • evā: Indeed
    • yajasva: Perform worship
    • tanvaṁ: Your body
    • sujāta: Well-born or well-formed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra encourages individuals to engage in self-worship and devotion to the divine.
    • The phrase "svayaṁ yajasva" emphasizes the importance of personal involvement in spiritual practices.
    • It suggests that one should not rely solely on others for spiritual nourishment but should actively participate in their own spiritual journey.
    • The reference to "divi deva devān" highlights the connection between the worshipper and the divine, suggesting that the divine beings are always present and attentive.
    • The line "kiṁ te pākaḥ kṛṇavad apracetāḥ" warns against superficial or mindless worship, urging individuals to seek understanding and awareness in their practices.
    • The mantra concludes with a call to worship the divine with one's own body, "yajasva tanvaṁ sujāta," indicating that the physical self is a vessel for spiritual expression.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various forms of personal development, such as meditation, yoga, or any spiritual practice where one actively engages with their inner self.
    • For instance, someone practicing mindfulness meditation is embodying the essence of this mantra by taking the time to connect with their thoughts and emotions, thus performing their own spiritual work.
    • In community settings, this mantra can inspire group worship or collective spiritual activities where individuals come together to share their experiences and insights, fostering a deeper understanding of their beliefs.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on personal responsibility in spiritual practices.
    • It encourages individuals to take charge of their spiritual journey, ensuring that their worship is meaningful and informed.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra serves as a reminder to engage actively in one's spiritual life, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and oneself.
    • Ultimately, it teaches that true worship comes from understanding, awareness, and personal involvement, making spirituality a personal and transformative experience.
