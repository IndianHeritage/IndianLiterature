समुद्रो अप्सु मामृजे विष्टम्भो धरुणो दिवः |सोमः पवित्रे अस्मयुः ||
samudro apsu māmṛje viṣṭambho dharuṇo divaḥ | somaḥ pavitre asmayuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samudro: ocean or sea
    • apsu: in the waters
    • māmṛje: may cleanse or purify me
    • viṣṭambho: the supporter or sustainer
    • dharuṇo: the firm or stable
    • divaḥ: of the heavens or sky
    • somaḥ: the moon or soma (a sacred drink)
    • pavitre: pure or purified
    • asmayuḥ: of the body or physical form
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra invokes the cleansing and purifying powers of the ocean and waters, asking for support from the firm and stable elements of the heavens, while also recognizing the purifying essence of the moon and its connection to the physical body.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Purification Rituals: In many cultures, water is seen as a purifying element. For instance, in Hindu rituals, bathing in sacred rivers like the Ganges is believed to cleanse one of sins and impurities.
    • Emotional Cleansing: Just as the ocean can wash away physical dirt, individuals often seek emotional cleansing through meditation or therapy, allowing them to release negative thoughts and feelings.
    • Connection to Nature: The mantra emphasizes the importance of nature in our lives. For example, spending time by the ocean can provide a sense of peace and rejuvenation, reflecting the mantra's call for purification and support from natural elements.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for purification and support from both the natural world and the cosmos. It reminds us of the interconnectedness of all elements—water, earth, and sky—and their roles in our physical and spiritual well-being. In real life, this mantra can inspire practices of self-care, environmental awareness, and the pursuit of inner peace through connection with nature.
