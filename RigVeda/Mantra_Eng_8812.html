यं त्वा dyāvāpṛthivī यं त्वापस तवषṭा यं त्वा सुजनिमाऽ जजाना |पन्थाम् अनु प्रविद्वान् पितृयाणं द्युमद अग्ने समिधानो वि भाहि ||
yaṁ tvā dyāvāpṛthivī yaṁ tvāpas tvaṣṭā yaṁ tvā sujanimā jajāna | panthām anu pravidvān pitṛyāṇaṁ dyumad agne samidhāno vi bhāhi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: whom
    • tvā: you
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: heaven and earth
    • yaṁ: whom
    • tvāpas: waters
    • tvaṣṭā: the creator
    • yaṁ: whom
    • tvā: you
    • suja: well-born
    • nimā: born
    • jajāna: has given birth
    • panthām: the path
    • anu: following
    • pravidvān: knowing
    • pitṛyāṇaṁ: the path of ancestors
    • dyumad: shining
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • samidhāno: kindled
    • vi: in
    • bhāhi: may you shine
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence of Agni, the fire god, who is seen as a mediator between the earthly and the divine realms.
    • The repetition of "yaṁ tvā" emphasizes the connection and reverence towards the elements of nature, including heaven, earth, and water.
    • The phrase "suja nimā jajāna" highlights the importance of being well-born or having a noble lineage, which can be interpreted as a call for moral and ethical living.
    • "Panthām anu pravilvān" suggests the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, following the path laid down by our ancestors.
    • The concluding part, "samidhāno vi bhāhi," is a plea for Agni to shine brightly, symbolizing the illumination of knowledge and the warmth of divine blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the importance of respecting nature and understanding our connection to the environment, much like how we rely on natural resources for sustenance.
    • The emphasis on lineage and moral values can be seen in family traditions where ethical teachings are passed down through generations, shaping individual character and societal norms.
    • The pursuit of knowledge is relevant in educational settings, where students are encouraged to follow the paths of great thinkers and innovators, much like the ancestors mentioned in the mantra.
    • In rituals and ceremonies, invoking Agni symbolizes the importance of fire in various cultures, representing purification, transformation, and the divine presence in our lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of all elements of life—nature, ancestry, and knowledge.
    • It serves as a reminder to honor our roots, seek wisdom, and live in harmony with the world around us.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of responsibility towards the environment, uphold ethical values, and pursue continuous learning.
    • Ultimately, the mantra calls for the divine light of Agni to guide us on our journey, illuminating our path with knowledge and virtue.
