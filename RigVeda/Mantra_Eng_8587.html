दिवो न सर्गा अससृग्रम अह्नां राजा न मित्रं प्र मिनाति धीराḥ |पितुर न पुत्रः क्रतुभिर यतान आ पवस्व विशे अस्याऽजीतिम ||
divo na sargā asasṛgram ahnāṁ rājā na mitram pra mināti dhīraḥ | pitur na putraḥ kratubhir yatāna ā pavasva viśe asyā ajītim ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Divo: Of the heavens
    • Na: Not
    • Sargā: Creation or birth
    • Asasṛgram: To be born or to be created
    • Ahnaṁ: Of the day
    • Rājā: King or ruler
    • Na: Not
    • Mitram: Friend
    • Pra: For
    • Mināti: To befriend or to support
    • Dhīraḥ: The wise or the prudent
    • Pitur: Of the father
    • Na: Not
    • Putraḥ: Son
    • Kratu: Intention or will
    • Yatāna: Efforts or endeavors
    • Ā: To
    • Pavasva: To flow or to move
    • Viśe: In the community or among people
    • Asyā: Of this
    • Ajītim: Immortality or eternal existence
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects on the nature of existence and the relationships between individuals and their roles in society.
    • It emphasizes that the wise (Dhīraḥ) do not rely solely on the heavens (Divo) for creation or support, nor do they depend on familial ties (Pitur na Putraḥ) for their identity or purpose.
    • Instead, it suggests that true strength and support come from one's own intentions and efforts (Kratu) and the connections made within the community (Viśe).
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, an employee may not solely depend on their manager (Rājā) for guidance but instead seek collaboration with colleagues (Mitram) to achieve success.
    • In personal relationships, individuals may find that their friendships (Mitram) and community support are more valuable than just familial connections (Pitur na Putraḥ).
    • In education, students are encouraged to take initiative (Kratu) in their learning rather than relying solely on teachers for knowledge.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra teaches the importance of self-reliance, community, and the pursuit of wisdom in achieving one's goals.
    • It highlights that while familial and divine connections are significant, the essence of life lies in our own actions and the relationships we cultivate.
    • In modern life, this mantra can inspire individuals to take charge of their destinies, seek meaningful connections, and strive for personal growth and community involvement.
