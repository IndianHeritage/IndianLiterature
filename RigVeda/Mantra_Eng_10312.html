विष्वावसुं सोम गन्धर्वम् आपो ददृशुषीस तद ऋतेन व्यायन |तद अन्ववैद इन्द्रो रारहाण आसाम परि सूर्यस्य परिधीर्म अपश्यत ||
viśvāvasuṁ soma gandharvam āpo dadṛśuṣīs tad ṛtenā vy āyan | tad anvavaid indro rārahāṇa āsām pari sūryasya paridhīm̐r apaśyat ||
  • viśvāvasuṁ: This term can be interpreted as "all-encompassing" or "universal wealth." It signifies the abundance of resources and blessings that are available to all beings.
  • soma: Soma refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes the essence of life and spiritual enlightenment.
  • gandharvam: Gandharvas are celestial musicians and beings associated with joy and beauty. This term represents the aesthetic and harmonious aspects of life.
  • āpo: This word means "waters" and signifies the life-giving and purifying qualities of water, essential for sustenance and growth.
  • dadṛśuṣīs: This can be understood as "those who have seen" or "witnessed," indicating a sense of awareness and perception of the divine or the truth.
  • tad ṛtenā: "By that truth" suggests the importance of adhering to cosmic order and righteousness (ṛta) in our actions and thoughts.
  • vy āyan: This phrase can be interpreted as "they have come forth" or "they have manifested," indicating the realization of potential and the emergence of blessings.
  • tad anvavaid: This means "that which is sought after," emphasizing the pursuit of higher knowledge and spiritual fulfillment.
  • indro: Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength, power, and leadership. This term signifies the importance of courage and authority in achieving one's goals.
  • rārahāṇa: This can be interpreted as "having taken action" or "having moved forward," indicating the necessity of proactive behavior in life.
  • āsām: This word means "of these" or "of those," referring to the collective experiences and actions of individuals.
  • pari: This means "around" or "surrounding," suggesting a holistic view of life and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • sūryasya: This term refers to the sun, symbolizing light, knowledge, and vitality. The sun is often seen as a source of energy and life.
  • paridhīm̐r: This can be interpreted as "the encompassing" or "the protective," indicating the need for safeguarding and nurturing our environment.
  • apaśyat: This means "to see" or "to perceive," emphasizing the importance of awareness and understanding in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of universal abundance, spiritual enlightenment, and the interconnectedness of life. It encourages individuals to seek truth, act with purpose, and recognize the divine presence in all aspects of existence. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to foster a sense of community, promote environmental stewardship, and inspire personal growth. By embracing these principles, individuals can cultivate a harmonious and fulfilling life, contributing positively to society and the world at large.
