मन्त्र: कदā वसो स्तोत्रं हर्यत आवा श्मशा रुधद वाह् |दीर्घं सुतं वाताप्याय ||
kadā vaso stotraṁ haryata āva śmaśā rudhad vāḥ | dīrghaṁ sutaṁ vātāpyāya ||
  • kadā - This word translates to "when" or "at what time." It signifies a quest for understanding the timing of events or actions in life.
  • vaso - Meaning "of the dwelling" or "of the home," it refers to the place where one resides, symbolizing comfort and security.
  • stotraṁ - This means "praise" or "hymn." It indicates the act of expressing reverence or admiration, often directed towards deities or higher powers.
  • haryata - This word can be interpreted as "to be joyful" or "to rejoice." It reflects the emotional state of happiness and contentment.
  • āva - Meaning "to come" or "to arrive," it suggests the arrival of something significant, whether it be a person, event, or realization.
  • śmaśā - This translates to "the cremation ground" or "the place of the dead." It symbolizes the transient nature of life and the inevitability of death.
  • rudhad - Meaning "to be strong" or "to be powerful," it emphasizes resilience and strength in the face of challenges.
  • vāḥ - This word means "you" or "your," indicating a personal connection or address to the listener or the divine.
  • dīrghaṁ - This translates to "long" or "lasting," suggesting endurance and the desire for longevity in life.
  • sutaṁ - Meaning "son" or "offspring," it represents the continuation of lineage and the importance of family.
  • vātāpyāya - This can be interpreted as "to be protected by the winds" or "to be safeguarded." It signifies the need for protection and guidance in life.

In summary, this mantra reflects a deep understanding of life’s journey, emphasizing the importance of home, joy, strength, and the transient nature of existence. It encourages individuals to seek joy and strength in their lives while acknowledging the inevitability of death. The mantra serves as a reminder to cherish relationships, particularly with family, and to seek protection and guidance through life's challenges.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as:

  • During family gatherings, where the importance of home and relationships is celebrated.
  • In moments of reflection on life and death, prompting individuals to appreciate their time and connections.
  • When facing challenges, reminding individuals to draw strength from their experiences and relationships.

Ultimately, the significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, encouraging individuals to find joy, strength, and meaning in their existence while being mindful of the transient nature of life.
