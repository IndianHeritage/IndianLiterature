मन्त्र: उता प्रधिमुद अहन्न अस्य विद्वान उपायुनग वंसगम अत्र शिक्षन् |इन्द्र उद आवात पतिम अग्न्यानाम अरंहता पद्याभिः ककुद्मान ||
uta pradhim ud ahann asya vidvān upāyunag vaṁsagam atra śikṣan | indra ud āvat patim aghnyānām araṁhata padyābhiḥ kakudmān ||
  • uta pradhim ud ahann: This phrase can be translated as "May the one who is wise and knowledgeable awaken us." Here, "uta" signifies a call or invocation, while "pradhim" refers to wisdom or intelligence. This part emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and enlightenment in our lives.
  • asya vidvān upāyunag vaṁsagam: This translates to "The knowledgeable one who leads us towards prosperity." "Vidvān" means a learned or wise person, and "upāyunag" suggests guidance or leadership. This highlights the role of wise individuals in guiding society towards success and well-being.
  • atra śikṣan: This means "Here, we learn." It emphasizes the importance of education and learning in our lives. It suggests that knowledge is not just theoretical but should be applied in practical scenarios.
  • indra ud āvat patim aghnyānām: This can be interpreted as "Indra, the powerful one, protects the wise." "Indra" is often seen as a deity representing strength and protection. This part signifies that those who possess wisdom are safeguarded by greater forces.
  • araṁhata padyābhiḥ kakudmān: This translates to "With the strength of the wise, may we rise." "Kakudmān" refers to strength or power. This closing phrase emphasizes the idea that wisdom and strength go hand in hand, and through wisdom, we can achieve greater heights.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking wisdom and guidance in our lives. It highlights the significance of education, the role of wise leaders, and the protection that comes with knowledge. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as education systems, leadership roles, and personal development. By fostering a culture of learning and valuing wisdom, individuals and societies can thrive and overcome challenges. The mantra serves as a reminder that wisdom is not just an individual pursuit but a collective journey towards prosperity and strength.
