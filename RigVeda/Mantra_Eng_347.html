न्याघ्न्यस्य मूर्धनि चक्रं रथस्य येमथुः |परिद्यां अन्यद ईयते ||
ny a1ghnyasya mūrdhani cakraṁ rathasya yemathuḥ | pari dyām anyad īyate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ny - not
    • a1ghnyasya - of the one who is not to be harmed
    • mūrdhani - on the head or at the top
    • cakraṁ - wheel or circle
    • rathasya - of the chariot
    • yemathuḥ - who is the one that moves or travels
    • pari - around or about
    • dyām - the sky or heavens
    • anyad - other or different
    • īyate - is moved or is influenced
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine chariot that is guided by a force that cannot be harmed.
    • The "wheel" symbolizes the cyclical nature of life and the universe, suggesting that everything is interconnected.
    • The reference to the "sky" indicates a higher realm or consciousness that influences our lives.
    • It emphasizes the idea that while we may be on a journey (represented by the chariot), there are forces at play that guide and protect us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal journey, one might face challenges and obstacles, but the belief in a higher power or guiding force can provide comfort and direction.
    • In a team setting, the collective effort of individuals can be likened to a chariot, where each member plays a crucial role in steering towards a common goal, protected by shared values and mutual respect.
    • In nature, the cycles of seasons can be seen as the wheels of the universe, where each season influences the next, demonstrating the interconnectedness of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the protective and guiding forces in our lives.
    • It teaches us to trust in the journey, understanding that while we may face difficulties, there is a greater plan at work.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal growth, teamwork, and our relationship with nature, reminding us of the cyclical and interconnected nature of existence.
