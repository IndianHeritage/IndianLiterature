त्वं कुत्सेनाभि शुष्णम इन्द्राशुषं युध्य कुयवं गविष्टौ |दश प्रपित्वे अध सूर्यास्य मुषायश चक्रम अविवे रपांसि ||
tvaṁ kutsenābhi śuṣṇam indrāśuṣaṁ yudhya kuyavaṁ gaviṣṭau | daśa prapitve adha sūryasya muṣāyaś cakram avive rapāṁsi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • kutsenābhi: With the power of the curse
    • śuṣṇam: The one who is dry or parched
    • indrāśuṣaṁ: The one who is Indra-like or powerful
    • yudhya: Fight or battle
    • kuyavaṁ: The deceitful or treacherous
    • gaviṣṭau: In the midst of the cows or in the realm of abundance
    • daśa: Ten
    • prapitve: In the act of giving
    • adha: Below
    • sūryasya: Of the sun
    • muṣāyaś: Of the one who is hidden or concealed
    • cakram: Wheel or cycle
    • avive: Without discrimination
    • rapāṁsi: The waters or the essence
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes a powerful force, suggesting a battle against deceit and negativity.
    • The reference to "Indra" symbolizes strength and the ability to overcome challenges, akin to how a leader might confront adversity in a corporate setting.
    • The mention of "cows" can be interpreted as a metaphor for abundance and prosperity, similar to how one might seek to protect their resources in business or personal life.
    • The phrase "with the power of the curse" indicates the use of one's inner strength to combat negativity, much like how individuals might use their skills to navigate difficult situations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace scenario, when faced with unethical practices, one might draw upon their inner strength and moral compass to address the issue, much like the mantra suggests fighting against deceit.
    • In personal life, when dealing with toxic relationships, the mantra serves as a reminder to stand firm and protect one's well-being, akin to the protective nature of Indra.
    • In environmental activism, the reference to abundance can inspire individuals to fight for the preservation of nature, ensuring that resources are not exploited deceitfully.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of inner strength and the fight against deceit and negativity in various aspects of life.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to harness one's power to confront challenges, protect resources, and maintain integrity.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to be vigilant and proactive in safeguarding their values and well-being, making it a timeless guide for personal and communal growth.
