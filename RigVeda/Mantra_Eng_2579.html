स नः शर्माणि वीतये अग्निर यच्छतु शान्तमा |यतो नः पृथिवद वसु दिवि क्षितिभ्यः आप्सु आ ||
sa naḥ śarmāṇi vītaye 'gnir yacchatu śaṁtamā | yato naḥ pruṣṇavad vasu divi kṣitibhyo apsv ā ||
  • sa naḥ - "May he" or "Let him" - This phrase invokes a higher power or deity, suggesting a plea or request for assistance.
  • śarmāṇi - "comfort" or "protection" - This word signifies the desire for safety, security, and well-being.
  • vītaye - "for the purpose of" - This indicates the intention behind the request, emphasizing the goal of achieving comfort.
  • 'gnir - "fire" - Fire symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. In many cultures, fire is also associated with divine presence.
  • yacchatu - "may grant" - This is a wish or hope that the deity will bestow the desired blessings.
  • śaṁtamā - "the most peaceful" - This term highlights the ultimate goal of achieving peace and tranquility.
  • yato - "from where" - This word introduces a source or origin, indicating that the blessings should come from a specific place.
  • naḥ - "to us" - This reinforces the personal nature of the request, emphasizing that the blessings are sought for the speaker and their community.
  • pruṣṇavad - "like the sun" - This metaphor suggests brightness, clarity, and life-giving energy, akin to the sun's role in sustaining life.
  • vasu - "wealth" or "prosperity" - This term refers to material and spiritual abundance, indicating a desire for overall well-being.
  • divi - "in the heavens" - This suggests a divine or celestial source of blessings, linking the request to higher powers.
  • kṣitibhyo - "from the earth" - This indicates a connection to the physical world, emphasizing the importance of earthly resources and stability.
  • apsv - "from the waters" - Water symbolizes life, nourishment, and purification, highlighting the need for sustenance and emotional well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra "sa naḥ śarmāṇi vītaye 'gnir yacchatu śaṁtamā | yato naḥ pruṣṇavad vasu divi kṣitibhyo apsv ā ||" is a profound invocation for comfort, peace, and prosperity. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of divine blessings, earthly resources, and the nurturing qualities of water. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during times of stress or uncertainty, where individuals seek solace and stability. It serves as a reminder to look for support from both spiritual and material sources, fostering a holistic approach to well-being. By invoking this mantra, one can cultivate a sense of peace and abundance in their life, encouraging a balanced existence that honors both the divine and the natural world.
