मन्त्र: ब्राह्मणाद इन्द्र राधसः पिबा सोमं ऋतूम् अनु | तवेधि सख्यम अस्तृतम् ||
brāhmaṇād indra rādhasaḥ pibā somam ṛtūm̐r anu | taved dhi sakhyam astṛtam ||
  • brāhmaṇād: This word refers to the Brahman, the priestly class or the learned individuals in society who possess knowledge and wisdom.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He symbolizes strength and power.
  • rādhasaḥ: This term can be interpreted as 'from the place of abundance' or 'from the realm of prosperity', indicating a source of wealth or nourishment.
  • pibā: This means 'drink' or 'consume', suggesting an act of taking in or absorbing something beneficial.
  • somam: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. It represents the essence of life and vitality.
  • ṛtūm̐r: This word relates to 'season' or 'time', indicating the cyclical nature of life and the importance of timing in various aspects of existence.
  • anu: This means 'following' or 'after', suggesting a sequence or continuity in actions or events.
  • taved: This term implies 'you have', indicating possession or the act of having something valuable.
  • hdi: This can be interpreted as 'in the heart' or 'within', suggesting an internal or emotional aspect.
  • sakhyam: This word means 'friendship' or 'companionship', highlighting the importance of relationships and social bonds.
  • astṛtam: This term can be understood as 'unbroken' or 'continuous', indicating stability and permanence in relationships or situations.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to Indra to partake in the sacred Soma, which is derived from the abundance of the Brahman, emphasizing the importance of divine blessings in fostering friendship and stability in life. It suggests that by consuming the essence of life (Soma) during the right season, one can cultivate strong bonds and a prosperous existence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal relationships, it encourages individuals to nurture friendships and connections, drawing on shared experiences and mutual support.
  • In professional settings, it highlights the importance of collaboration and teamwork, where individuals work together harmoniously to achieve common goals.
  • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder to seek divine guidance and blessings, fostering a sense of gratitude and connection to the greater universe.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the interconnectedness of life, the importance of divine blessings, and the cultivation of strong relationships. It teaches us that by embracing abundance and nurturing our connections, we can lead a fulfilling and prosperous life.
