वावर्त येषां राया युक्तैषां हिरण्ययी |नेमधिता न पौंस्यां वृथेव विष्टान्ता ||
vāvarta yeṣāṁ rāyā yuktaiṣāṁ hiraṇyayī | nemadhitā na pauṁsyā vṛtheva viṣṭāntā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vāvarta: revolving or turning
    • yeṣāṁ: of whom
    • rāyā: wealth or prosperity
    • yuktaiṣāṁ: those who are endowed with wisdom or intelligence
    • hiraṇyayī: golden or precious
    • nemadhitā: not obstructed or hindered
    • na: not
    • pauṁsyā: of men or humanity
    • vṛtheva: as if in vain
    • viṣṭāntā: those who are established or settled
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the cyclical nature of wealth and prosperity.
    • It emphasizes that true prosperity is achieved by those who possess wisdom and intelligence.
    • The phrase "not obstructed" suggests that such individuals are free from hindrances that may prevent them from achieving their goals.
    • It warns against the futility of efforts that lack wisdom, indicating that without intelligence, even the most diligent efforts may lead to failure.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the business world, entrepreneurs who combine hard work with strategic thinking often find greater success than those who work tirelessly without a plan.
    • Students who study smartly, using effective techniques and understanding concepts, tend to perform better than those who merely memorize information without comprehension.
    • In personal finance, individuals who educate themselves about investments and savings strategies are more likely to build wealth than those who save without a clear strategy.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of wisdom and intelligence in achieving prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder that hard work alone is not sufficient; one must also apply knowledge and strategy to attain meaningful success.
    • In real life, this principle can be applied in various fields, including business, education, and personal development, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and understanding as a foundation for their efforts.
