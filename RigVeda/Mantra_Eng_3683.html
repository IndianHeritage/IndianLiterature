वधेन दस्युम् प्र हि चातयस्व वयः कृत्वानस् तन् वे3 स्वायै |पिपर्षि यत सहसस् पुत्र देवान्त सो अग्नि पाही नृतम वाजे अस्मान् ||
vadhena dasyum pra hi cātayasva vayaḥ kṛṇvānas tanve3 svāyai | piparṣi yat sahasas putra devānt so agne pāhi nṛtama vāje asmān ||
  • Mantra: "vadhena dasyum pra hi cātayasva vayaḥ kṛṇvānas tanve3 svāyai | piparṣi yat sahasas putra devānt so agne pāhi nṛtama vāje asmān ||"
  • Translation: "With the power of destruction, O Agni, drive away the enemies; grant us strength and vitality. May the divine beings protect us, and may you, O Agni, safeguard us from harm and grant us abundance."
  • Word-by-word Explanation:
    • Vadhena: With the power of destruction or killing.
    • Dasyum: Enemies or adversaries.
    • Pra hi: Drive away or remove.
    • Cātayasva: Grant us strength or vitality.
    • Vayaḥ: Our age or life span.
    • Kṛṇvānas: Creating or making.
    • Tanve3: In our bodies or physical forms.
    • Svāyai: For our own benefit or welfare.
    • Piparṣi: Protect or safeguard.
    • Yat: That which.
    • Sahasas: With strength or power.
    • Putra: Sons or offspring.
    • Devānt: Divine beings or gods.
    • So: That.
    • Agne: O Agni (the fire god).
    • Pāhi: Protect or preserve.
    • Nṛtama: From harm or danger.
    • Vāje: Abundance or wealth.
    • Asmān: Us or our.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of conflict or personal challenges, individuals often seek strength and protection, much like the invocation of Agni in this mantra. For instance, a person facing a difficult situation at work may recite this mantra to seek inner strength and the removal of obstacles.
    • Parents may use this mantra to bless their children, asking for divine protection and strength as they navigate life's challenges, similar to how one would wish for their child's success and safety in a competitive environment.
    • In community gatherings, this mantra can be recited to invoke collective strength and protection against societal issues, fostering unity and resilience among members.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine intervention to remove obstacles and provide strength. It emphasizes the importance of seeking protection from adversities, whether they are external threats or internal struggles. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal, familial, and communal contexts, serving as a reminder of the power of faith and the need for support in overcoming challenges. By invoking Agni, individuals and communities can foster resilience and a sense of security, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
