मन्त्र: अस्वापयद दभीतये सहस्रां त्रिंशतं हठैः |दासानाम इन्द्रो मायया ||
asvāpayad dabhītaye sahasrā triṁśataṁ hathaiḥ | dāsānām indro māyayā ||
  • Mantra: "asvāpayad dabhītaye sahasrā triṁśataṁ hathaiḥ | dāsānām indro māyayā ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as "Indra, the lord of the people, has given strength to the fearful, with his thousand and thirty mights, through his magical power."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • asvāpayad: To give strength or support.
    • dabhītaye: For those who are fearful or in need of courage.
    • sahasrā: Thousand.
    • triṁśataṁ: Thirty.
    • hathaiḥ: With might or strength.
    • dāsānām: Of the servants or people.
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods in Vedic tradition.
    • māyayā: Through magical power or illusion.
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra highlights the role of Indra as a protector and provider of strength to those who are fearful.
    • In real life, this can be seen in various scenarios where leaders or mentors empower individuals who lack confidence, helping them to overcome their fears.
    • For example, a teacher who encourages a shy student to participate in class discussions embodies the essence of this mantra.
    • Similarly, in a workplace, a manager who supports an anxious employee in taking on new responsibilities reflects the same principle.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of support and empowerment in overcoming fear.
    • It serves as a reminder that strength can be derived from external sources, such as community, leadership, and guidance.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to seek help and to be a source of strength for others.
    • In today's world, where mental health and emotional well-being are crucial, this mantra can inspire initiatives that promote support systems in various environments, such as schools, workplaces, and communities.
