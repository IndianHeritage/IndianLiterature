अग्नि शकेम ते वयं यमं देवस्य वाजिनः |अति द्वेषांसी तरेमा ||
agne śakema te vayaṁ yamaṁ devasya vājinaḥ | ati dveṣāṁsi tarema ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • Śakema: This term means "may we be strong" or "may we have power." It emphasizes the desire for strength and capability.
  • Te: This is a pronoun meaning "your," indicating a connection to the divine.
  • Vayaṁ: This translates to "we," representing the collective group or community seeking blessings.
  • Yamaṁ: This word can refer to "control" or "restraint," often associated with the moral and ethical dimensions of life.
  • Devasya: Meaning "of the divine," this word highlights the connection to higher powers or deities.
  • Vājinaḥ: This term means "of the strong" or "the powerful," often referring to those who are capable and resourceful.
  • Ati: This means "beyond" or "exceeding," suggesting a desire to surpass limitations.
  • Dveṣāṁsi: This word translates to "hatred" or "enmity," indicating negative emotions or obstacles that one may face.
  • Tarema: This means "may we cross over" or "may we overcome," expressing a wish to transcend difficulties.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for strength and guidance from the divine, specifically invoking Agni. It expresses a collective desire to overcome hatred and obstacles in life, seeking the power to navigate challenges with the help of divine energy.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra when facing personal challenges, such as overcoming fears or negative emotions, to seek strength and resilience.
  • Community Unity: Groups or communities can use this mantra during gatherings or rituals to foster unity and collective strength, emphasizing the importance of overcoming divisive feelings.
  • Conflict Resolution: In situations of conflict, this mantra can serve as a reminder to rise above hatred and work towards harmony and understanding.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, unity, and the ability to overcome negativity. It serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of divine support in navigating life's challenges and encourages individuals and communities to strive for harmony and resilience in the face of adversity.
