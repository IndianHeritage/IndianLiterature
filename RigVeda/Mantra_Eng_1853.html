एष स्तोमा इन्द्र तुभ्यं अस्मे एतेन गातुं हरिवो विदो नः |आ नो ववृत्याः सुविताया देव विद्यामेषं वृजनं जीरदानुम् ||
eṣa stoma indra tubhyam asme etena gātuṁ harivo vido naḥ | ā no vavṛtyāḥ suvitāya deva vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum ||
  • eṣa stoma indra tubhyam: This phrase translates to "This hymn is for you, Indra." Here, "stoma" refers to a hymn or praise, and "Indra" is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength.
  • asme etena gātuṁ harivo vido naḥ: This part means "May this hymn bring us strength and knowledge." "Gātuṁ" signifies strength or power, while "harivo" refers to the divine or the gods. "Vido" means knowledge, and "naḥ" means "to us."
  • ā no vavṛtyāḥ suvitāya deva: This translates to "O divine one, may you bestow upon us good fortune." "Vavṛtyāḥ" implies protection or blessings, and "suvitāya" means good fortune or auspiciousness.
  • vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum: This means "We seek knowledge and nourishment." "Vidyā" refers to knowledge, "vṛjanaṁ" can mean a group or community, and "jīradānum" signifies nourishment or sustenance.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of seeking strength, knowledge, and divine blessings in our lives. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra seeks knowledge and strength, individuals can strive for personal development through education and self-improvement.
  • Community Support: The reference to a community ("vṛjanaṁ") highlights the significance of collective strength and support in achieving common goals.
  • Spiritual Practices: Reciting this mantra can be a part of spiritual rituals, invoking divine blessings for guidance and protection in daily life.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, knowledge, and divine support in our journey through life.
