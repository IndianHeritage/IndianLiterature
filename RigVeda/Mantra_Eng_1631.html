यस्य त्रī पूर्णा मधुनā पदान्य अक्षीयमाणā स्वधया मदन्ति |य उ त्रिधातु पृथिवīm उता द्यां एको दाधार भुवनानि विश्वा ||
yasya trī pūrṇā madhunā padāny akṣīyamāṇā svadhayā madanti | ya u tridhātu pṛthivīm uta dyām eko dādhāra bhuvanāni viśvā ||
  • Mantra: "yasya trī pūrṇā madhunā padāny akṣīyamāṇā svadhayā madanti | ya u tridhātu pṛthivīm uta dyām eko dādhāra bhuvanāni viśvā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • yasya: of whom
    • trī: three
    • pūrṇā: full, complete
    • madhunā: with sweetness, honey
    • padāny: feet, steps
    • akṣīyamāṇā: not diminishing, eternal
    • svadhayā: by one's own nature
    • madanti: they rejoice, they are delighted
    • ya: who
    • u: and
    • tridhātu: three realms (earth, atmosphere, heaven)
    • pṛthivīm: the earth
    • uta: and
    • dyām: the sky
    • eko: one
    • dādhāra: supports, holds
    • bhuvanāni: worlds, creations
    • viśvā: all, universal
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine entity who embodies completeness and sweetness, representing the essence of life and existence.
    • The reference to "three" signifies the interconnectedness of the physical, mental, and spiritual realms.
    • The phrase "not diminishing" suggests the eternal nature of this divine presence, which is always present and supportive.
    • It emphasizes the unity of all worlds, indicating that this divine force sustains and nurtures all aspects of creation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, the balance of ecosystems can be seen as a reflection of the "three realms" where every element plays a crucial role in maintaining harmony.
    • In personal life, individuals often find joy and fulfillment when they align their actions with their true nature, akin to the "svadhayā" aspect of the mantra.
    • The concept of sustainability in environmental practices mirrors the idea of supporting all worlds, as it emphasizes the importance of nurturing our planet for future generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of recognizing the interconnectedness of all life and the divine presence that supports it.
    • It encourages individuals to seek harmony within themselves and with the world around them, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in various fields such as environmental conservation, personal development, and community building, reminding us of our role in the larger tapestry of existence.
