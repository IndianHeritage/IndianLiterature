यस्य त्वं अग्ने अध्वरं जुजोषो देवो मर्तस्य सुधितं रराणः |प्रीतेद असद धोत्रा सा यविष्ठासामा यस्य विधतो वृद्धासः ||
yasya tvam agne adhvaraṁ jujoṣo devo martasya sudhitaṁ rarāṇaḥ | prīted asad dhotrā sā yaviṣṭhāsāma yasya vidhato vṛdhāsaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • tvam: you
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • adhvaraṁ: the sacrificial rite
    • jujoṣo: who is invoked
    • devo: the divine
    • martasya: of mortals
    • sudhitaṁ: well-prepared
    • rarāṇaḥ: who is to be worshipped
    • prīted: with joy
    • asad: not false
    • dhotrā: the priest
    • sā: that
    • yaviṣṭhāsāma: who is the best
    • yasya: of whom
    • vidhato: who is ordained
    • vṛdhāsaḥ: the prosperous
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Agni, the fire god, emphasizing the importance of invoking him during sacrificial rites.
    • It highlights the relationship between the divine and mortals, suggesting that the rituals performed with sincerity and joy lead to divine blessings.
    • The mention of a well-prepared offering indicates the significance of preparation and intention in any endeavor.
    • The priest's role is crucial, as he acts as a mediator between the divine and the human, ensuring that the offerings are made correctly.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the importance of preparation and intention in achieving success. For instance, a student preparing for an exam must study diligently and approach the exam with a positive mindset to achieve good results.
    • In a corporate setting, a team that prepares thoroughly for a presentation and approaches it with enthusiasm is more likely to impress clients and achieve their goals.
    • The role of a leader can be likened to that of the priest in the mantra, where effective leadership involves guiding a team towards a common goal with clarity and purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine support through sincere efforts and well-prepared actions.
    • It teaches that joy and sincerity in our endeavors can lead to prosperity and success, whether in personal, academic, or professional life.
    • By recognizing the divine in our actions and preparing ourselves adequately, we can create a harmonious relationship between our efforts and the outcomes we desire.
