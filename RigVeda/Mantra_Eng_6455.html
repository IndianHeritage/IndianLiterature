यम आदित्यासो अद्रुहः पारं नयथ मर्त्यम् |मघोनां विश्वेषां सुदानवः ||
yam ādityāso adruhaḥ pāraṁ nayatha martyam | maghonāṁ viśveṣāṁ sudānavaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yam ādityāso adruhaḥ pāraṁ nayatha martyam | maghonāṁ viśveṣāṁ sudānavaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yam: whom
    • ādityāso: the sons of Aditi (the celestial beings or deities)
    • adruhaḥ: without enmity or harm
    • pāraṁ: the ultimate or the farthest
    • nayatha: lead or guide
    • martyam: the mortal or human
    • maghonāṁ: of the wealthy or prosperous
    • viśveṣāṁ: of all beings
    • sudānavaḥ: the good or auspicious
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the celestial beings, specifically the sons of Aditi, who are often associated with the sun and light.
    • It emphasizes the importance of leading humanity towards the ultimate truth or goal without causing harm or enmity.
    • The phrase "maghonāṁ viśveṣāṁ sudānavaḥ" suggests a collective prosperity and well-being for all beings, highlighting the interconnectedness of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied to promote harmony and cooperation among individuals, encouraging them to work together towards common goals without conflict.
    • In leadership, it serves as a reminder for leaders to guide their followers with compassion and integrity, ensuring that their actions benefit the greater good.
    • In personal relationships, it encourages individuals to approach each other with kindness and understanding, fostering a supportive environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity, compassion, and guidance in human interactions.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from community building to personal relationships, emphasizing the need for harmony and collective prosperity.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more peaceful and prosperous society.
