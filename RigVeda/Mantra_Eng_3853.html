एता विश्वा चक्रवाम् इन्द्र भूय aparīto जनुषा वीर्येण | या चिन् नु वज्रिन कृणवो दधृष्वान न ते वार्ता तविष्याऽस्ति तस्याः ||
etā viśvā cakṛvām̐ indra bhūry aparīto januṣā vīryeṇa | yā cin nu vajrin kṛṇavo dadhṛṣvān na te vartā taviṣyā asti tasyāḥ ||
  • etā viśvā cakṛvām̐: This phrase can be translated as "All these beings are created." It signifies the interconnectedness of all life forms and the universe. In real life, this can be seen in ecosystems where every organism, from the smallest bacteria to the largest mammals, plays a crucial role in maintaining balance.
  • indra bhūry aparīto: Here, "Indra" refers to the king of gods, symbolizing strength and power. "Bhūry aparīto" means "abundant and limitless." This suggests that the divine power of Indra is vast and can provide for all. In practical terms, this can be related to the abundance found in nature, such as the vast resources of the earth that sustain life.
  • januṣā vīryeṇa: This translates to "with the strength of beings." It emphasizes the importance of collective strength and unity among individuals. In a real-world context, this can be observed in communities coming together to support each other during crises, showcasing the power of collaboration.
  • yā cin nu vajrin kṛṇavo: "Yā cin nu" means "whatever it is that we desire," and "vajrin" refers to the one who wields the thunderbolt, often associated with Indra. This part of the mantra expresses the idea of striving for our desires with determination and strength. For example, in personal development, individuals often pursue their goals with the same vigor and focus.
  • dadhṛṣvān na te vartā: This translates to "may there be no obstacles in your path." It conveys a wish for the removal of barriers that hinder progress. In everyday life, this can be seen in the support systems we create, such as mentors or friends who help us overcome challenges.
  • taviṣyā asti tasyāḥ: This means "there is no limit to that." It signifies the boundless potential that exists when one is aligned with their purpose and the universe. In practical terms, this can be related to the idea of limitless possibilities in innovation and creativity, where individuals or groups push the boundaries of what is achievable.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of unity, strength, and the limitless potential of life. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine support available to us. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as community building, personal growth, and overcoming challenges. By recognizing our collective strength and the abundance around us, we can strive towards our goals with determination and resilience, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
