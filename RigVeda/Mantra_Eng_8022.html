परिष्कृतास इन्दवो योषेव पितृयावती |वायुः सोमाऽसृक्षत ||
pariṣkṛtāsa indavo yoṣeva pitryāvatī | vāyuṁ somā asṛkṣata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pariṣkṛtāsa: Refined or purified
    • indavo: The divine or celestial beings
    • yoṣeva: Like a woman or in a feminine aspect
    • pitryāvatī: Pertaining to the ancestors or forefathers
    • vāyuṁ: The wind or air
    • somā: The moon or soma, often associated with nectar or immortality
    • asṛkṣata: They protected or preserved
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine beings who are refined and pure, akin to the nurturing qualities of a woman, and are connected to the lineage of ancestors.
    • The reference to "vāyuṁ" (wind) and "somā" (moon) suggests a connection to natural elements that are vital for life and sustenance.
    • The act of protection or preservation indicates a safeguarding of traditions, values, and the essence of life itself.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the role of women is often seen as nurturing and protective, similar to how the divine beings are portrayed in this mantra. For instance, mothers often embody the qualities of care and preservation in families.
    • The wind (vāyu) is essential for life, as it carries oxygen and helps in the pollination of plants. This reflects the interconnectedness of all living beings and the importance of maintaining a healthy environment.
    • The moon (somā) is often associated with cycles and time, reminding us of the importance of tradition and the wisdom passed down through generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of purity, protection, and the nurturing aspects of life. It highlights the importance of honoring our ancestors and the natural elements that sustain us.
    • In real life, this can be applied by fostering a sense of community, respecting traditions, and ensuring the well-being of future generations through sustainable practices.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to cherish the connections we have with our heritage, nature, and each other, promoting a holistic approach to life.
