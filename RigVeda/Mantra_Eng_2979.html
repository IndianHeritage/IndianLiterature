मन्त्र: अन्यस्य वत्सं रिहती मिमाय कया भुवा नि दधे धेनुर ऊधः |ऋतस्य सा पयसा पिन्वतेषा महद देवानाम असुरत्वम् एकम् ||
anyasyā vatsaṁ rihatī mimāya kayā bhuvā ni dadhe dhenur ūdhaḥ | ṛtasya sā payasāpinvateḻā mahad devānām asuratvam ekam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • anyasyā: of another
    • vatsaṁ: calf
    • rihatī: is nourished
    • mimāya: by the one who is great
    • kayā: by what
    • bhuvā: in the world
    • ni: not
    • dadhe: does place
    • dhenur: the cow
    • ūdhaḥ: above
    • ṛtasya: of truth
    • sā: that
    • payasā: with milk
    • pinvateḻā: flows forth
    • mahad: great
    • devānām: of the gods
    • asuratvam: the essence of non-deception
    • ekam: one
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the interconnectedness of life and the nurturing aspect of nature.
    • The "calf" symbolizes innocence and the potential for growth, while the "cow" represents nourishment and sustenance.
    • The phrase "by what in the world does not place" suggests that true nourishment comes from a higher source, not merely from material possessions.
    • The reference to "truth" and "milk" indicates that purity and honesty are essential for spiritual and physical sustenance.
    • The "greatness of the gods" emphasizes the divine nature of truth and the importance of aligning oneself with it.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the relationship between farmers and their livestock reflects this mantra. Farmers nurture their animals, which in turn provide sustenance, illustrating the cycle of giving and receiving.
    • In personal relationships, the idea of nurturing others while being nurtured in return can be seen in friendships and family bonds, where support and care flow both ways.
    • In a corporate setting, ethical business practices that prioritize honesty and integrity can lead to a thriving environment, akin to the nourishing flow of milk from the cow.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of nurturing relationships, both in nature and human interactions.
    • It teaches us that true sustenance comes from honesty and integrity, which are essential for personal and communal growth.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to healthier relationships, ethical practices, and a deeper connection with the world around us.
