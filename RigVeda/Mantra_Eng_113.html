अग्नि देवां इह वह जज्ञानो वृक्तबरिषे |असि होता न ईड्यः ||
agne devām̐ ihā vaha jajñāno vṛktabarhiṣe | asi hotā na īḍyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • Devām̐: divine beings or gods
    • ihā: here, in this world
    • vaha: carry or bring forth
    • jajñāno: those who are born or created
    • vṛktabarhiṣe: with the sacred grass (barhis) that is spread out
    • asi: you are
    • hotā: the one who offers or invokes
    • na: not
    • īḍyaḥ: worthy of worship or praise
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking him to bring forth the divine beings into this world. It acknowledges Agni as the one who performs the offerings and highlights the importance of sacred rituals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, Agni is often invoked during ceremonies such as weddings and yajnas (sacrificial offerings). The fire symbolizes purity and the divine presence.
    • In modern life, the concept of 'Agni' can be seen in the way we seek to bring forth positive energy and divine qualities in our actions, such as kindness, honesty, and integrity.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energies in our lives. It serves as a reminder to seek the blessings of higher powers in our endeavors.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to engage in rituals or practices that promote spiritual growth and connection with the divine, such as meditation, prayer, or community service.
    • By recognizing the role of Agni as a purifying force, we can apply this understanding to our daily lives by striving to eliminate negativity and foster a positive environment.
