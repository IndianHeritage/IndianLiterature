शं नो अदितिः भवतु व्रतेभिः शं नो भवन्तु मरुतः स्वर्काः |शं नो विष्णुः शमुपूषा नो अस्तु शं नो भवित्रं शम व अस्तु वायुः ||
śaṁ no aditir bhavatu vratebhiḥ śaṁ no bhavantu marutaḥ svarkāḥ | śaṁ no viṣṇuḥ śam u pūṣā no astu śaṁ no bhavitraṁ śam v astu vāyuḥ ||
  • śaṁ no aditir bhavatu vratebhiḥ: This phrase can be translated as "May Aditi (the mother of gods) bless us with her grace through our vows." Aditi represents the infinite and the boundless, often associated with the nurturing aspect of the universe. In real life, this can be seen as a call for divine support in our commitments and promises, encouraging individuals to uphold their responsibilities.
  • śaṁ no bhavantu marutaḥ svarkāḥ: This means "May the Maruts (storm deities) bring us peace and prosperity." The Maruts are often associated with strength and vitality. In practical terms, this can be related to the idea of teamwork and collaboration, where the collective efforts of individuals lead to success and harmony in various endeavors, such as in workplaces or community projects.
  • śaṁ no viṣṇuḥ: This translates to "May Vishnu grant us peace." Vishnu is the preserver in the Hindu trinity and symbolizes stability and protection. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as seeking balance and security in our lives, whether through personal relationships, career stability, or mental well-being.
  • śam u pūṣā no astu: This means "May Pūṣā (the nourisher) provide us with peace." Pūṣā is associated with nourishment and growth. This can be applied in real life by emphasizing the importance of nurturing our physical and mental health, as well as fostering supportive relationships that help us thrive.
  • śaṁ no bhavitraṁ: This translates to "May we be blessed with purity." Purity here can refer to moral integrity and clarity of thought. In practical terms, this can be seen in the importance of ethical behavior in personal and professional life, promoting honesty and transparency in our actions.
  • śam v astu vāyuḥ: This means "May the wind (vāyu) bring us peace." The wind symbolizes change and movement. In real life, this can be interpreted as embracing change and being adaptable in the face of challenges, allowing us to grow and evolve in our personal and professional lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a prayer for peace, prosperity, and divine blessings in various aspects of life. It emphasizes the importance of commitment, teamwork, stability, nourishment, purity, and adaptability. In a world that often feels chaotic, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek harmony and support from both the divine and our community. By embodying these principles, individuals can create a more balanced and fulfilling life, contributing positively to society as a whole.
