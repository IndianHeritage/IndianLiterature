भागस्य स्वसा वरुणस्य जामिर उषः सूनृतै प्रथमा जरस्वा |पश्चा स दघ्या यो अगस्य धाता जयेम तं दक्षिणया रथेन ||
bhagasya svasā varuṇasya jāmir uṣaḥ sūnṛte prathamā jarasva | paścā sa daghyā yo aghasya dhātā jayema taṁ dakṣiṇayā rathena ||
  • bhagasya svasā: This phrase can be interpreted as "the sister of prosperity." In real life, this can refer to the idea that prosperity is often accompanied by support and nurturing, much like a sister would provide. For example, in a business context, a supportive partner or team can lead to greater success.
  • varuṇasya jāmir: This translates to "the offspring of Varuna," where Varuna is the deity associated with cosmic order and water. This can symbolize the importance of maintaining harmony and balance in our lives. For instance, in environmental conservation, respecting water bodies and their ecosystems reflects the principles of Varuna.
  • uṣaḥ sūnṛte: "The dawn of truth." This signifies the arrival of new beginnings and the importance of truth in our lives. In a practical sense, this can relate to the idea of starting fresh each day with honesty and integrity, whether in personal relationships or professional endeavors.
  • prathamā jarasva: This means "the first of the aged." It suggests wisdom that comes with age and experience. In real life, this can be seen in the respect given to elders in many cultures, where their life experiences provide valuable lessons for younger generations.
  • paścā sa daghyā: This translates to "after that, the one who is fierce." This can represent the challenges and adversities we face in life. For example, overcoming obstacles in a career can lead to personal growth and resilience.
  • yo aghasya dhātā: "The one who is the creator of the unworthy." This phrase can be interpreted as a reminder that even those who seem unworthy have a role in the grand scheme of things. In real life, this can relate to social justice, where every individual deserves respect and opportunities, regardless of their background.
  • jayema taṁ dakṣiṇayā rathena: "May we conquer him with the chariot of the south." This symbolizes the pursuit of victory and success through strategic means. In practical terms, this can be applied to achieving goals through careful planning and teamwork, much like navigating a journey with a well-prepared vehicle.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of prosperity, truth, wisdom, and the challenges we face in life. It teaches us to value support systems, respect for nature, the importance of honesty, and the wisdom of experience. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide us in personal growth, social responsibility, and achieving our goals. By embodying these values, we can navigate our lives with purpose and integrity, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
