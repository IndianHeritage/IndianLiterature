तस्य वयं सुमतौ यज्ञियस्यापि भद्रे सुमनसे स्याम |स सुत्रामां स्ववाम् इन्द्रो अस्मे आराच चिद् द्वेषः सन्तर युयोतु ||
tasya vayaṁ sumatau yajñiyasyāpi bhadre saumanase syāma | sa sutrāmā svavām̐ indro asme ārāc cid dveṣaḥ sanutar yuyotu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tasya: of that
    • vayaṁ: we
    • sumatau: with good thoughts
    • yajñiyasyāpi: of the one who is worthy of worship
    • bhadre: auspicious
    • saumanase: with a pleasant mind
    • syāma: may we be
    • sa: he
    • sutramā: with good connections
    • svavām̐: our own
    • indro: Indra (the king of gods)
    • asme: may we be
    • ārāc: from the front
    • cid: may we be free
    • dveṣaḥ: of enmity
    • sanutar: may we be strong
    • yuyotu: may he fight
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a desire for unity and harmony among individuals.
    • It emphasizes the importance of good thoughts and auspiciousness in our actions and relationships.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies the need for strength and protection against negativity and enmity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, fostering a culture of collaboration and positive thinking can lead to better teamwork and productivity.
    • In personal relationships, approaching conflicts with a pleasant mindset can help resolve issues amicably.
    • Communities can benefit from collective efforts to promote peace and understanding, reducing enmity and fostering cooperation.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of positive thinking and the importance of unity.
    • It encourages individuals to seek strength in community and to combat negativity with good intentions.
    • In essence, it is applicable in various aspects of life, from personal relationships to larger societal interactions, promoting a harmonious existence.
