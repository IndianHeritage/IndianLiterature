वावृद्धानः शवसाऽ भूर्योजाः शत्रुर दासाय भीयसं दधाति |अव्यनच च व्यनच च सस्नि सं ते नवन्त प्रभरिता मदेषु ||
vāvṛdhānaḥ śavasā bhūryojāḥ śatrur dāsāya bhiyasaṁ dadhāti | avyanac ca vyanac ca sasni saṁ te navanta prabhṛtā madeṣu ||
  • vāvṛdhānaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who are growing" or "those who are expanding." It signifies the importance of growth and development in various aspects of life.
  • śavasā: This word refers to "strength" or "vigor." It emphasizes the need for physical and mental strength to overcome challenges.
  • bhūryojāḥ: This can be translated as "abundant energy" or "great power." It suggests that one should harness their energy effectively to achieve their goals.
  • śatrur: This means "enemy" or "adversary." It highlights the presence of challenges or obstacles in life that one must confront.
  • dāsāya: This term translates to "for the servant" or "for the one who serves." It indicates the importance of service and humility in the face of adversity.
  • bhiyasaṁ: This word means "fear" or "apprehension." It acknowledges the fears that can arise when facing challenges.
  • dadhāti: This translates to "gives" or "provides." It signifies the act of offering support or assistance to others.
  • avyanac: This term can be interpreted as "not to be neglected" or "not to be overlooked." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and addressing issues promptly.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking the ideas presented in the mantra.
  • sasni: This word refers to "the one who is wise" or "the knowledgeable." It highlights the value of wisdom in navigating life's challenges.
  • saṁ: This term means "together" or "with," indicating the importance of community and collaboration.
  • te: This translates to "you" or "your," addressing the listener directly and making the message personal.
  • navanta: This word means "new" or "fresh." It signifies the potential for renewal and new beginnings.
  • prabhṛtā: This term can be interpreted as "those who are endowed" or "those who possess." It suggests that individuals have inherent qualities that can be developed.
  • madeṣu: This translates to "in the intoxication" or "in the joy." It indicates the importance of finding joy and fulfillment in life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of growth, strength, and service in overcoming challenges. It encourages individuals to recognize their fears and seek wisdom while fostering a sense of community. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, teamwork, and leadership. For instance, in a workplace setting, employees can draw on their strengths and collaborate to overcome obstacles, while also supporting one another in their growth journeys. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that through collective effort and a focus on personal development, individuals can navigate life's challenges and find joy in their endeavors.
