प्रत्यार्धिर यज्ञानाम् अश्वहयो रथानाम् |ऋषिः स यो मनुर्हितो विप्रस्य यावयत्सखाḥ ||
pratyardhir yajñānām aśvahayo rathānām | ṛṣiḥ sa yo manurhito viprasya yāvayatsakhaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pratyardhir: One who is steadfast or resolute.
    • yajñānām: Of the sacrifices or rituals.
    • aśvahayo: The one who is the master of horses or chariots.
    • rathānām: Of the chariots.
    • ṛṣiḥ: Sage or seer.
    • sa: He or that.
    • yo: Who.
    • manurhito: Guided by Manu (the progenitor of mankind).
    • viprasya: Of the wise or learned.
    • yāvayatsakhaḥ: As a friend or companion.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of steadfastness and dedication in the performance of rituals and sacrifices.
    • The reference to "master of horses" and "chariots" symbolizes strength, power, and the ability to navigate through life's challenges.
    • The sage or seer represents wisdom and insight, suggesting that true guidance comes from those who are learned.
    • The mention of Manu indicates a connection to humanity and the moral and ethical principles that guide human behavior.
    • Being a "friend" or "companion" highlights the significance of relationships and support systems in achieving one's goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who is steadfast in their vision and supports their team can be likened to the "master of horses," guiding the organization through challenges.
    • In personal relationships, being a reliable friend who offers wisdom and support reflects the essence of the mantra, fostering strong bonds.
    • In community service, individuals who dedicate themselves to helping others embody the spirit of sacrifice and commitment highlighted in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the virtues of steadfastness, wisdom, and the importance of supportive relationships in both personal and communal contexts.
    • Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to remain resolute in their pursuits while valuing the guidance of learned individuals and the strength of friendships.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, stronger relationships, and a more harmonious community.
