यस्य वर्णं मधुश्चुतं हरिं हिन्वन्त्य अद्रिभिः |इन्दुम इन्द्राय पीतये ||
yasya varṇam madhuścutaṁ hariṁ hinvanty adribhiḥ | indum indrāya pītaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • varṇam: color or hue
    • madhuścutaṁ: sweet and delightful
    • hariṁ: the divine or the Lord
    • hinvanty: who is carried or borne
    • adribhiḥ: by the mountains
    • indum: the moon
    • indrāya: for Indra (the king of gods)
    • pītaye: for drinking or to drink
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine entity whose color is sweet and delightful, symbolizing beauty and positivity.
    • The phrase "carried by the mountains" suggests a connection between the divine and nature, indicating that the mountains serve as a foundation or support for the divine presence.
    • The mention of the moon and Indra signifies the importance of celestial bodies in Vedic thought, where the moon is often associated with nourishment and fertility, while Indra represents strength and power.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we often find beauty in the colors of the sunset or the blooming of flowers, which can be seen as manifestations of the divine presence in our lives.
    • Mountains are often revered in various cultures as sacred spaces, where people go to seek peace, reflection, and connection with the divine.
    • The moon plays a significant role in agriculture, as many farmers rely on lunar cycles for planting and harvesting, reflecting the interconnectedness of nature and divine influence.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the relationship between the divine, nature, and human existence.
    • It serves as a reminder to appreciate the beauty around us and recognize the divine presence in our daily lives.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to find harmony with nature, seek strength in our challenges, and acknowledge the cycles of life that are influenced by celestial bodies.
