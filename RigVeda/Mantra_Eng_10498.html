ग्रावाणो अप दुष्चुनाम अप सेधत दुर्मतिम् |उष्राः कर्तन भेषजम् ||
grāvāṇo apa ducchunām apa sedhata durmatim | usrāḥ kartana bheṣajam ||
  • Mantra: "grāvāṇo apa ducchunām apa sedhata durmatim | usrāḥ kartana bheṣajam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • grāvāṇo: One who is powerful or strong.
    • apa: Away from or against.
    • ducchunām: Those with bad thoughts or intentions.
    • apa sedhata: May they be removed or kept away.
    • durmatim: Evil or harmful thoughts.
    • usrāḥ: The wise or knowledgeable.
    • kartana: To create or to perform.
    • bheṣajam: Healing or remedy.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra calls upon the powerful forces to drive away negative thoughts and intentions from individuals.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in creating remedies for the challenges faced in life.
    • The mantra suggests that by focusing on positive thoughts and actions, one can overcome negativity and harmful influences.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, if a team is facing conflicts due to misunderstandings, invoking this mantra can symbolize the need to clear negative thoughts and promote a collaborative environment.
    • In personal relationships, when misunderstandings arise, this mantra can serve as a reminder to focus on positive communication and understanding rather than harboring ill feelings.
    • In mental health practices, individuals may use this mantra as a meditative focus to dispel negative thoughts and cultivate a mindset of healing and positivity.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to seek wisdom and healing while actively working to eliminate negativity from their lives.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, including personal growth, conflict resolution, and mental well-being.
    • By embracing the teachings of this mantra, individuals can foster a more positive and constructive environment for themselves and those around them.
