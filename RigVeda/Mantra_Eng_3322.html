द्रुहं जिघांसन् ध्वरसम अनिन्द्रां तेतिक्ते तिग्मा तुजसे अनीकां |ऋणा चिद यत्र ऋणया न उग्रो दूरे अज्ञाता उषसो बबाधे ||
druhaṁ jighāṁsan dhvarasam anindrāṁ tetikte tigmā tujase anīkā | ṛṇā cid yatra ṛṇayā na ugro dūre ajñātā uṣaso babādhe ||
  • Mantra: "druhaṁ jighāṁsan dhvarasam anindrāṁ tetikte tigmā tujase anīkā | ṛṇā cid yatra ṛṇayā na ugro dūre ajñātā uṣaso babādhe ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • druhaṁ: hostility or enmity
    • jighāṁsan: desiring to harm
    • dhvarasam: one who is powerful or strong
    • anindrāṁ: without blame or fault
    • tetikte: may you be freed
    • tigmā: sharp or intense
    • tujase: to you (addressing a deity)
    • anīkā: without fear or anxiety
    • ṛṇā: debt or obligation
    • cid: if
    • yatra: where
    • ṛṇayā: by obligation
    • na: not
    • ugro: fierce or terrible
    • dūre: far away
    • ajñātā: unknown or hidden
    • uṣaso: of the dawn
    • babādhe: has been obstructed or hindered
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the desire to overcome hostility and harmful intentions.
    • The phrase "druhaṁ jighāṁsan" indicates a wish to eliminate enmity, which can be related to personal conflicts in everyday life, such as disputes between friends or family members.
    • "Anindrāṁ tetikte" suggests a plea for liberation from blame, which can be applicable in situations where one feels unjustly accused or misunderstood.
    • The mention of "tigmā tujase anīkā" emphasizes the need for strength and fearlessness in facing challenges, akin to how individuals might confront their fears in professional settings, such as public speaking or job interviews.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "ṛṇā cid yatra ṛṇayā na ugro," reflects on the idea of obligations and debts, urging one to navigate these responsibilities without fear or aggression, similar to managing financial debts or personal commitments.
    • Finally, "dūre ajñātā uṣaso babādhe" can be interpreted as a call to dispel the unknown fears that hinder progress, much like overcoming anxiety before a new beginning, such as starting a new job or moving to a new city.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder to seek peace and resolution in the face of conflict, encouraging individuals to rise above negativity and fear.
    • Its significance lies in promoting a mindset of strength, clarity, and responsibility, which is essential in both personal and professional realms.
    • In real life, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to healthier relationships, better conflict resolution, and a more fearless approach to life's challenges.
