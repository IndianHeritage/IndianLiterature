मन्त्र: अनुष्पष्टो भवत्य एषो अस्य यो अस्मै रेवान न सुनोति सोमम् | निर अरत्नौ मगवाऽं तं दधाति ब्रह्मद्विषो हन्त्य अनानुदिष्टः ||
anuspaṣṭo bhavaty eṣo asya yo asmai revān na sunoti somam | nir aratnau maghavā taṁ dadhāti brahmadviṣo hanty anānudiṣṭaḥ ||
  • anuspaṣṭo bhavaty: This phrase can be translated as "becomes clear" or "becomes evident." It suggests the importance of clarity and understanding in our actions and thoughts.
  • eṣo asya: This means "this is his" or "this belongs to him." It indicates possession or a connection to something significant.
  • yo asmai revān: Translated as "who brings him wealth," this part emphasizes the idea of prosperity and abundance that comes to those who are deserving.
  • na sunoti somam: This translates to "does not hear the soma." Soma is often associated with divine nectar or the essence of life. This line suggests that neglecting the divine or spiritual aspects can lead to a lack of fulfillment.
  • nir aratnau: Meaning "without obstacles," this phrase highlights the importance of a clear path or the absence of hindrances in achieving one's goals.
  • maghavā taṁ dadhāti: This translates to "the mighty one bestows it." It refers to the idea that a powerful force or deity grants blessings or success to those who are worthy.
  • brahmadviṣo hanty: This means "destroys the enemies of the divine." It emphasizes the protection of spiritual values and the eradication of negativity that opposes them.
  • anānudiṣṭaḥ: This translates to "unseen" or "not directed." It suggests that some forces operate beyond our perception, guiding us in ways we may not fully understand.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of clarity, prosperity, and spiritual awareness. It teaches us that clarity in our intentions and actions leads to the manifestation of abundance in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal development, business endeavors, and spiritual practices. By fostering a clear understanding of our goals and remaining open to divine guidance, we can navigate obstacles and attract positive outcomes. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder to remain connected to our spiritual roots while pursuing material success, ensuring a balanced and fulfilling life.
