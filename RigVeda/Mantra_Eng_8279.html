आयं सोमः कपरदिने घृतं न पवते मधु |आ भक्षत कन्यासु नः ||
ayaṁ somaḥ kapardine ghṛtaṁ na pavate madhu | ā bhakṣat kanyāsu naḥ ||
  • ayaṁ somaḥ: This phrase refers to "this Soma," where Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
  • kapardine: This term means "with matted hair," often used to describe ascetics or deities like Shiva, who embody renunciation and spiritual power.
  • ghṛtaṁ: This word translates to "ghee" or clarified butter, which is considered pure and is used in various rituals and offerings.
  • na pavate: This means "does not blow" or "is not disturbed," indicating a state of tranquility or stability.
  • madhu: This translates to "honey," symbolizing sweetness, nourishment, and the essence of life.
  • ā bhakṣat: This phrase means "may it be consumed" or "may it be enjoyed," suggesting a desire for the blessings of the offerings.
  • kanyāsu: This term refers to "young maidens" or "daughters," symbolizing purity, potential, and the future generation.
  • naḥ: This means "for us," indicating a collective desire or prayer for blessings and prosperity.

  • Meaning and Interpretation: The mantra invokes the essence of Soma, a divine drink that symbolizes spiritual nourishment and immortality. It emphasizes the importance of purity (ghṛtaṁ) and stability (na pavate) in our lives. The reference to young maidens (kanyāsu) suggests a focus on the future and the nurturing of new generations.
  • Real-Life Examples: In modern life, this mantra can be related to the importance of maintaining purity in our thoughts and actions, much like how ghee is used in rituals for its purity. The idea of stability can be seen in the need for mental peace in our fast-paced lives. Additionally, the focus on young maidens can be connected to the empowerment of women and the nurturing of future leaders.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for spiritual nourishment, purity, and stability in our lives. It serves as a reminder to cherish and empower the younger generation while seeking divine blessings for a prosperous and harmonious life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate inner peace, support the growth of future generations, and maintain a connection to spiritual traditions.
