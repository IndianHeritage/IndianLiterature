सजोषा धीराः पदैर अनु गमन्युप तव सीदन विश्वे यजत्राः ||
sajoṣā dhīrāḥ padair anu gmann upa tvā sīdan viśve yajatrāḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • sajoṣā: This term can be interpreted as "with harmony" or "in unison." It emphasizes the importance of collective effort and unity.
    • dhīrāḥ: This word means "wise" or "intelligent." It refers to individuals who possess discernment and understanding.
    • padair: This translates to "by the steps" or "by the paths." It signifies the journey or the methods taken to achieve a goal.
    • anu: Meaning "following" or "in accordance with," it suggests alignment with a particular principle or guidance.
    • gmann: This can be interpreted as "to go" or "to move towards." It indicates progress or the act of advancing.
    • upa: This means "near" or "towards." It signifies approaching something with intention.
    • tvā: This translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a higher power or deity.
    • sīdan: Meaning "to sit" or "to dwell," it implies a state of being or presence.
    • viśve: This word means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity and the collective nature of existence.
    • yajatrāḥ: This term refers to "those who perform sacrifices" or "worshippers," highlighting the act of devotion and ritual.
  • Overall Meaning: The mantra can be interpreted as a call for wise individuals to come together in harmony, following a path towards a higher purpose, and to dwell in the presence of the divine while engaging in acts of devotion.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied to team collaboration. When team members work together harmoniously, utilizing their individual strengths (dhīrāḥ), they can achieve common goals (sajoṣā) effectively.
    • In community service, individuals from diverse backgrounds can unite (viśve) to address social issues, following a shared vision (padair anu) that leads to positive change.
    • In personal development, one can reflect on their journey (gmann) towards self-improvement, seeking wisdom (dhīrāḥ) and aligning their actions with their values (anu).
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on unity, wisdom, and purposeful action. It serves as a reminder that collective efforts, guided by intelligence and harmony, can lead to meaningful outcomes in various aspects of life, from personal growth to community engagement. By embodying these principles, individuals can create a more connected and purposeful existence.
