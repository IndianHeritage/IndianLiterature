महां ऋषिर देवजा देवजूतोऽस्तभ्नात सिंधुम अर्णवं नृचक्षाः |विश्वामित्रो यद अवहत सुदासम अप्रियायत कुशिकेभिर इन्द्रः ||
mahām̐ ṛṣir devajā devajūto 'stabhnāt sindhum arṇavaṁ nṛcakṣāḥ | viśvāmitro yad avahat sudāsam apriyāyata kuśikebhir indraḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "The great sage, born of the gods, has stabilized the ocean of the waters; the men with human eyes, like Vishwamitra, have brought forth Sudasa, who was not pleased with the Kusha."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • mahām̐ ṛṣir: Great sage or seer, indicating a person of profound wisdom and spiritual insight.
    • devajā: Born of the gods, suggesting a divine origin or connection.
    • stabhnāt: To stabilize or support, indicating the act of bringing order or balance.
    • sindhum arṇavaṁ: The ocean of waters, symbolizing vastness and depth, often representing the unconscious or the source of life.
    • nṛcakṣāḥ: Men with human eyes, referring to those who perceive the world through a human perspective.
    • viśvāmitro: A revered sage in Vedic literature, known for his wisdom and contributions to the Vedas.
    • yad avahat: Who has brought forth, indicating the act of creation or manifestation.
    • sudāsam: Sudasa, a king known for his righteousness and valor.
    • apriyāyata: Not pleased or displeased, suggesting a state of dissatisfaction or conflict.
    • kuśikebhir: Referring to the Kusha, a group or lineage, often associated with challenges or adversities.
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods, representing power, strength, and authority.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of a "great sage" can be likened to modern-day leaders or thinkers who guide society through wisdom and insight, such as Mahatma Gandhi or Nelson Mandela.
    • The "ocean of waters" can symbolize the vast challenges we face in life, such as emotional turmoil or societal issues, which require stabilization through wisdom and action.
    • The reference to "men with human eyes" can be seen in everyday individuals who strive to make a difference in their communities, reflecting the importance of human perspective in addressing issues.
    • Vishwamitra's role as a sage can be compared to contemporary figures in science or philosophy who challenge norms and bring forth new ideas, much like how he challenged the status quo in his time.
    • The conflict represented by "not pleased" can be related to personal or societal struggles, where individuals or groups face dissatisfaction and seek resolution through dialogue and understanding.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the power of wisdom and divine guidance in stabilizing the chaos of life. It highlights the importance of human perception and the role of leaders in addressing challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek wisdom, act with integrity, and strive for balance in their lives and communities. It serves as a reminder that through collective effort and insight, we can navigate the complexities of existence and foster harmony in our surroundings.
