आ न इळाभिर विदथे सुशस्ति विश्वानरः सविता देव एतु |अपि यथा युवानो मत्सथā नो विश्वं जगद अभिपित्वे मनीषा ||
ā na iḻābhir vidathe suśasti viśvānaraḥ savitā deva etu | api yathā yuvāno matsathā no viśvaṁ jagad abhipitve manīṣā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • na: not
    • iḻābhir: with the powers
    • vidathe: in the creation
    • suśasti: well-formed
    • viśvānaraḥ: all beings
    • savitā: the Sun
    • deva: O God
    • etu: may come
    • api: also
    • yathā: as
    • yuvāno: youthful
    • matsathā: in our thoughts
    • no: our
    • viśvaṁ: the whole world
    • jagad: universe
    • abhipitve: to protect
    • manīṣā: wisdom
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine powers of creation and the Sun, asking for their guidance and protection.
    • It emphasizes the importance of collective well-being and the role of wisdom in safeguarding the universe.
    • The phrase "O God, may you come" signifies a plea for divine intervention in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be recited during gatherings to invoke unity and collective strength.
    • In personal life, individuals may reflect on this mantra during challenging times, seeking wisdom and guidance to navigate their circumstances.
    • In environmental discussions, it can serve as a reminder of our responsibility to protect the world and all beings within it.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support and the importance of wisdom in our actions.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal challenges to community efforts and environmental stewardship.
    • By embracing the essence of this mantra, individuals and communities can foster a sense of unity, purpose, and responsibility towards the world.
