एवाऽहि ते शं सवना समुद्र आपो यत त आसु मदन्ति देविः |विश्वा ते अनु जोष्याऽभूद गौः सूरींष चिद यदि धिषा वेषि जनान् ||
evā hi te śaṁ savanā samudra āpo yat ta āsu madanti devīḥ | viśvā te anu joṣyā bhūd gauḥ sūrīm̐ś cid yadi dhiṣā veṣi janān ||
  • evā hi te śaṁ savanā samudra āpo yat ta āsu madanti devīḥ
    • evā hi te: This phrase can be interpreted as "Indeed, for you" or "Truly, for you." It signifies a deep reverence and acknowledgment of a higher power.
    • śaṁ savanā: "Savanā" refers to offerings or sacrifices, while "śaṁ" implies peace or auspiciousness. Together, it suggests that the offerings made are intended to bring peace and harmony.
    • samudra āpo: "Samudra" means ocean, and "āpo" refers to waters. This can symbolize the vastness and depth of the universe, as well as the life-giving properties of water.
    • yat ta āsu madanti devīḥ: This translates to "that which the goddesses delight in." It indicates that the offerings made are pleasing to the divine, suggesting a connection between the material and spiritual realms.
  • viśvā te anu joṣyā bhūd gauḥ sūrīm̐ś cid yadi dhiṣā veṣi janān
    • viśvā te: "Viśvā" means all or universal, and "te" means your. This phrase emphasizes the universality of the divine presence.
    • anu joṣyā: This can be interpreted as "may they be pleased" or "may they be satisfied." It reflects the desire for harmony and goodwill.
    • bhūd gauḥ: "Bhūd" refers to existence or being, and "gauḥ" means cow, which is often seen as a symbol of nourishment and abundance in Vedic literature.
    • sūrīm̐ś: This term refers to strength or power, often associated with divine qualities.
    • cid yadi dhiṣā veṣi janān: "Cid" means consciousness or awareness, "yadi" means if, "dhiṣā" refers to direction or guidance, and "veṣi" means to protect. This suggests that if one is guided by consciousness, they will be protected and supported.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings and the connection between the material and spiritual worlds. It highlights the significance of peace, harmony, and the nurturing aspects of nature, represented by water and cows. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Practicing gratitude and mindfulness in daily life, recognizing the abundance around us.
  • Engaging in acts of kindness and service to others, fostering a sense of community and connection.
  • Promoting environmental consciousness, understanding the importance of water and nature in sustaining life.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine, encouraging us to live in harmony with ourselves, others, and the universe.
