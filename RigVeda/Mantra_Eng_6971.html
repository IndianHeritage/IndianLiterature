आ नो वायो महे तने याहि मखाय पाजसे |वयं हि ते चक्रमा भूरी दावने सद्यश्चिन महि दावने ||
ā no vāyo mahe tane yāhi makhāya pājase | vayaṁ hi te cakṛmā bhūri dāvane sadyaś cin mahi dāvane ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: O (a call or invocation)
    • no: our
    • vāyo: O Wind (or life force)
    • mahe: great
    • tane: body
    • yāhi: come
    • makhāya: to the sacrifice
    • pājase: for the worship
    • vayaṁ: we
    • hi: indeed
    • te: your
    • cakṛmā: we have made
    • bhūri: abundant
    • dāvane: in the offering
    • sadyaś: immediately
    • cin: to perceive
    • mahi: great
    • dāvane: in the offering
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer invoking the Wind, symbolizing the life force and energy that sustains all living beings.
    • The speaker calls upon the Wind to come to their sacrifice, indicating a desire for divine presence and blessings during rituals.
    • The phrase "we have made abundant offerings" suggests a sense of community and shared purpose in worship, emphasizing the importance of collective efforts in spiritual practices.
    • The invocation of the Wind also represents the connection between the physical and spiritual realms, as it is a vital force that carries prayers and intentions to the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals involving the elements, such as wind, water, and fire, are performed to seek blessings and harmony. For instance, Native American ceremonies often honor the wind as a spirit that brings messages and guidance.
    • In modern practices, yoga and meditation often incorporate breathwork, which is akin to invoking the life force (vāyo) to enhance spiritual awareness and connection.
    • Community gatherings for festivals or religious observances can be seen as modern-day sacrifices where people come together to celebrate and offer their intentions for peace and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of invoking natural forces in spiritual practices, reminding us of our interconnectedness with the universe.
    • It teaches the importance of community in worship and the power of collective intentions in manifesting positive outcomes.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in practices that honor nature and foster a sense of unity, whether through rituals, community service, or environmental stewardship.
