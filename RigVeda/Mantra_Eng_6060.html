येन समुद्रम असृजो महीर अपस तद इन्द्र वृष्णि ते शवः |सद्यः सो अस्य महिमा न सन्नशे यं क्षोणीर अनुचक्रदे ||
yenā samudram asṛjo mahīr apas tad indra vṛṣṇi te śavaḥ | sadyaḥ so asya mahimā na saṁnaśe yaṁ kṣoṇīr anucakrade ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yenā: by whom
    • samudram: the ocean
    • asṛjo: created
    • mahīr: the great earth
    • apas: waters
    • tad: that
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • vṛṣṇi: of the Vṛṣhṇi clan (a lineage of warriors)
    • te: your
    • śavaḥ: body
    • sadyaḥ: immediately
    • so: he
    • asya: of this
    • mahimā: glory
    • na: not
    • saṁnaśe: perish
    • yaṁ: who
    • kṣoṇīr: the earth
    • anucakrade: has praised
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine power that created the ocean and the earth.
    • It invokes Indra, the king of gods, highlighting the importance of divine intervention in the natural world.
    • The phrase "immediately he of this glory does not perish" suggests that the glory of the divine is eternal and unchanging.
    • The reference to the earth praising the divine indicates a connection between nature and spirituality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In environmental conservation, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of respecting and preserving nature, as it is a creation of the divine.
    • In moments of crisis, such as natural disasters, people often turn to spirituality and prayer, invoking divine powers for protection and restoration.
    • The mantra can also be reflected in the way communities come together to celebrate nature, such as during harvest festivals, acknowledging the earth's bounty as a divine gift.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of the divine, nature, and humanity.
    • It serves as a reminder of the eternal glory of the divine and the importance of recognizing and honoring that connection in our daily lives.
    • By understanding and applying the teachings of this mantra, individuals can foster a deeper appreciation for the environment and a sense of responsibility towards its preservation.
