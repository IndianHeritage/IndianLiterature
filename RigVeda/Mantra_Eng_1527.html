अभि द्विजन्मा त्रिवृत् अन्नं ऋज्यते संवत्सरे वावृधे जग्धम् ई पुनः |अन्यस्यासां जिह्वया जेन्यो वृषा न्यान्येन वनिनो मृष्ट वारणः ||
abhi dvijanmā trivṛd annam ṛjyate saṁvatsare vāvṛdhe jagdham ī punaḥ | anyasyāsā jihvayā jenyo vṛṣā ny a1nyena vanino mṛṣṭa vāraṇaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "abhi dvijanmā trivṛd annam ṛjyate saṁvatsare vāvṛdhe jagdham ī punaḥ | anyasyāsā jihvayā jenyo vṛṣā ny a1nyena vanino mṛṣṭa vāraṇaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, for
    • dvijanmā: born twice, referring to the learned or the wise
    • trivṛd: threefold, indicating abundance or variety
    • annam: food, nourishment
    • ṛjyate: is obtained, is achieved
    • saṁvatsare: in the year, over time
    • vāvṛdhe: increases, grows
    • jagdham: consumed, taken
    • ī: indeed, certainly
    • punaḥ: again, once more
    • anyasyāsā: of another, from others
    • jihvayā: by the tongue, through speech
    • jenyo: that which is produced, created
    • vṛṣā: strong, powerful
    • ny: not
    • anyena: by another, through others
    • vanino: of the forest, wild
    • mṛṣṭa: mixed, blended
    • vāraṇaḥ: elephant, symbolizing strength and wisdom
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nourishment that is obtained through wisdom and learning.
    • The phrase "abhi dvijanmā" suggests that those who are wise and learned are blessed with abundance.
    • The term "trivṛd annam" indicates that this nourishment is not just physical but also spiritual and intellectual.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of time ("saṁvatsare") in the process of growth and nourishment.
    • It also highlights the role of communication and expression ("jihvayā") in sharing knowledge and wisdom.
    • The reference to "vāraṇaḥ" symbolizes the strength and wisdom that comes from a deep understanding of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, students who engage deeply with their subjects and communicate effectively with peers often achieve greater understanding and success.
    • In a workplace, collaboration and sharing of knowledge can lead to innovative solutions and growth for the organization.
    • In personal relationships, open communication fosters deeper connections and mutual understanding, leading to a more fulfilling life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of wisdom, communication, and the nourishment that comes from learning and sharing knowledge.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including education, professional environments, and personal relationships.
    • By embracing the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can cultivate a richer, more fulfilling life, characterized by growth and understanding.
