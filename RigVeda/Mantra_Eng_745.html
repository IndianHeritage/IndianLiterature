नू ष्ठिरम् मरुतो वीरवन्तम् ऋतीषाहं रयिम अस्मासु धत्त |सहस्रिणं शतिनं शूषुवांसम् प्रातर मक्षू धियावसुर जगम्याट ||
nū ṣṭhiram maruto vīravantam ṛtīṣāhaṁ rayim asmāsu dhatta | sahasriṇaṁ śatinaṁ śūśuvāṁsam prātar makṣū dhiyāvasur jagamyāt ||
  • nū ṣṭhiram: This phrase can be interpreted as "O steadfast one." It addresses a powerful entity, possibly a deity or a force of nature, emphasizing stability and strength.
  • maruto: Refers to the Maruts, the storm deities in Vedic literature. They symbolize the forces of nature, particularly storms and winds, representing both destruction and renewal.
  • vīravantam: This means "the one who is brave" or "the valiant." It highlights the courage and valor associated with the Maruts, suggesting that strength is essential in overcoming challenges.
  • ṛtīṣāhaṁ: This can be translated as "I am the one who upholds truth." It signifies the importance of righteousness and truth in one's actions and decisions.
  • rayim asmāsu dhatta: This phrase means "bestow upon us wealth." It is a plea for prosperity and abundance, indicating a desire for material and spiritual wealth.
  • sahasriṇaṁ: This translates to "thousandfold," suggesting a request for abundance that is vast and plentiful.
  • śatinaṁ: This word means "of the wealthy" or "prosperous." It emphasizes the importance of wealth in achieving a fulfilling life.
  • śūśuvāṁsam: This can be interpreted as "the one who is well-formed" or "the one who is strong." It suggests the importance of physical and mental strength in life.
  • prātar: This means "in the morning," symbolizing new beginnings and the potential for growth and renewal each day.
  • makṣū: This word can be translated as "with intelligence" or "with wisdom," indicating the need for thoughtful action and decision-making.
  • dhiyāvasur: This means "may we attain wisdom." It is a call for enlightenment and understanding in our endeavors.
  • jagamyāt: This translates to "may we reach" or "may we achieve," indicating a desire for progress and success in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, truth, and prosperity in life. It emphasizes the importance of courage and wisdom in overcoming challenges and achieving one's goals. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects, such as personal growth, professional endeavors, and spiritual pursuits. By invoking the qualities of steadfastness, bravery, and intelligence, individuals can navigate the complexities of life and strive for a fulfilling existence. The mantra serves as a reminder that with the right mindset and virtues, one can attain abundance and success in all endeavors.
