सरस्वती सरयुः सिन्धुर ऊर्मिभिर महो महीर अवसां यन्तु वक्षणीः |देवीर आपो मातरः सूदयित्वो घृतवत् पयो मधुमान नो अर्चत ||
sarasvatī sarayuḥ sindhur ūrmibhir maho mahīr avasā yantu vakṣaṇīḥ | devīr āpo mātaraḥ sūdayitnvo ghṛtavat payo madhuman no arcata ||
  • Sarasvatī: This refers to the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning. In real life, one can relate this to the pursuit of education and the importance of knowledge in personal and professional growth.
  • Sarayuḥ: This is a river, symbolizing the flow of life and the importance of water in sustaining life. Just as rivers nourish the land, knowledge nourishes the mind.
  • Sindhur: This translates to 'ocean' or 'river', representing vastness and depth. In a real-life context, it can symbolize the vast ocean of knowledge and experiences that one can explore.
  • Ūrmibhir: Meaning 'waves', this can be seen as the challenges and changes in life that one must navigate, much like a sailor navigating the waves of the sea.
  • Maho mahīr: This phrase can be interpreted as 'great and powerful', emphasizing the strength that comes from knowledge and wisdom. In real life, this can be seen in leaders and innovators who use their knowledge to make impactful decisions.
  • Avasā yantu: This means 'may they come together', suggesting unity and collaboration. In a practical sense, this can be applied in teamwork and community efforts where collective knowledge leads to greater outcomes.
  • Vakṣaṇīḥ: Referring to 'the wise', this highlights the importance of wisdom in decision-making. In everyday life, wise choices lead to better results in both personal and professional spheres.
  • Devīr āpo: This translates to 'the divine waters', symbolizing purity and the nurturing aspect of knowledge. In real life, this can be related to the idea that pure intentions and knowledge lead to positive outcomes.
  • Mātaraḥ: Meaning 'mothers', this signifies nurturing and care. In a broader context, it can represent the role of educators and mentors in shaping individuals' lives.
  • Sūdayitnvo: This can be interpreted as 'to purify', indicating the transformative power of knowledge. In real life, education and learning can purify one's thoughts and actions, leading to personal growth.
  • Ghṛtavat payo: This means 'like ghee and milk', symbolizing richness and nourishment. In practical terms, this can be seen in how knowledge enriches our lives, much like nutritious food sustains our bodies.
  • Madhuman: This translates to 'sweet', representing the joy and satisfaction that comes from acquiring knowledge. In real life, the pursuit of knowledge often leads to fulfilling experiences.
  • No arcata: Meaning 'may we worship', this emphasizes the importance of reverence for knowledge and wisdom. In everyday life, this can be reflected in how we value education and learning.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the significance of knowledge, wisdom, and the nurturing aspects of learning. It highlights the importance of unity, collaboration, and the transformative power of education in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue knowledge, work together for common goals, and appreciate the richness that learning brings to their lives. By embodying these principles, one can navigate the challenges of life with wisdom and grace, ultimately leading to personal and communal growth.
