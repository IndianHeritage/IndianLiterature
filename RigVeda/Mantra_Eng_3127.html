प्र तां अग्निर बभषत तिग्मजम्भस तपिष्ठेन शोचिषा यः सुराधाः |प्र ये मिनन्ति वरुणस्य धाम प्रियामित्रस्य चेततो ध्रुवाणि ||
pra tām̐ agnir babhasat tigmajambhas tapiṣṭhena śociṣā yaḥ surādhāḥ | pra ye minanti varuṇasya dhāma priyā mitrasya cetato dhruvāṇi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: before, in front of
    • tām̐: that
    • agnir: fire, representing energy and transformation
    • babhasat: shone, radiated
    • tigmajambhas: with sharpness, intensity
    • tapiṣṭhena: with heat, warmth
    • śociṣā: with brightness, light
    • yaḥ: who
    • surādhāḥ: the divine, the celestial beings
    • pra: before, in front of
    • ye: those who
    • minanti: dwell, reside
    • varuṇasya: of Varuna, the god of cosmic order
    • dhāma: abode, realm
    • priyā: beloved, dear
    • mitrasya: of Mitra, the god of friendship and contracts
    • cetato: of the mind, consciousness
    • dhruvāṇi: steadfast, eternal
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the energy of fire (Agni), symbolizing transformation and purity.
    • It highlights the importance of divine beings (Suradhā) who radiate light and warmth, representing knowledge and enlightenment.
    • The second part emphasizes the abode of Varuna, the god of cosmic order, and Mitra, the god of friendship, suggesting a connection between divine order and human relationships.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, the concept of Agni can be seen in the transformative power of education, where knowledge ignites personal growth.
    • The idea of divine beings can be related to mentors or guides in our lives who illuminate our paths and help us navigate challenges.
    • The relationship between Varuna and Mitra can be reflected in the importance of maintaining harmony and trust in our friendships and communities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interplay between divine energy and human existence, emphasizing the need for transformation, enlightenment, and harmonious relationships.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals seek guidance, strive for personal growth, and foster meaningful connections with others.
    • By invoking these principles, one can cultivate a life that is aligned with cosmic order and enriched by the warmth of friendship and knowledge.
