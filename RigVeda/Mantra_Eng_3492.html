दधिक्रावण इषा ऊर्जो महो यद अमनमहि मरुतां नाम भद्रम् |स्वस्तये वरुणं मित्रं अग्निं हवामह इन्द्रं वज्रबाहुम् ||
dadhikrāvṇa iṣa ūrjo maho yad amanmahi marutāṁ nāma bhadram | svastaye varuṇam mitram agniṁ havāmaha indraṁ vajrabāhum ||
  • Translation: "We invoke the powerful energy of the Maruts, who are the bringers of good fortune. We call upon Varuna, Mitra, and Agni for our well-being, and we seek the blessings of Indra, the wielder of the thunderbolt."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dadhikrāvṇa: to invoke or call upon
    • iṣa: energy or power
    • ūrjo: strength or vigor
    • maho: great or mighty
    • yad: which
    • amanmahi: we meditate upon or contemplate
    • marutāṁ: of the Maruts, the storm deities
    • nāma: name or invocation
    • bhadram: auspiciousness or good fortune
    • svastaye: for well-being or welfare
    • varuṇam: Varuna, the god of cosmic order
    • mitram: Mitra, the god of friendship and contracts
    • agniṁ: Agni, the fire god
    • havāmaha: we invoke or offer
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods
    • vajrabāhum: the one with a thunderbolt arm
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energies for strength and auspiciousness in our lives.
    • The Maruts represent the forces of nature and the power of storms, symbolizing change and dynamism.
    • Varuna and Mitra signify the importance of cosmic order and friendship, reminding us of our responsibilities towards each other and the universe.
    • Agni represents transformation and purification, highlighting the need for inner growth and clarity.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, embodies leadership and strength, encouraging us to seek guidance and protection in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be recited during challenging times to invoke strength and resilience.
    • It can be used in rituals or ceremonies to seek blessings for new ventures, ensuring auspicious beginnings.
    • In moments of conflict or uncertainty, invoking the energies of friendship and cosmic order can help restore harmony.
    • Professionals can use this mantra to seek clarity and guidance in decision-making processes.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all forces in the universe and the importance of seeking divine guidance.
    • By invoking these energies, we align ourselves with the greater cosmic order, fostering well-being and auspiciousness in our lives.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to cultivate strength, friendship, and transformation, essential qualities for personal and communal growth.
