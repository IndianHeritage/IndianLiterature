अधā हīन्द्र गिर्वण उप त्वā कामān महः ससृज्महे |उदेव यन्त उदभिः ||
adhā hīndra girvaṇa upa tvā kāmān mahaḥ sasṛjmahe | udeva yanta udabhiḥ ||
  • adhā: This word translates to "down" or "below." It signifies a grounding or foundational aspect, suggesting that the following actions or thoughts are rooted in something fundamental.
  • hīndra: Referring to "Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership. Indra is often associated with rain and thunderstorms, representing the life-giving aspects of nature.
  • girvaṇa: This term means "the one who is praised" or "the one who is invoked." It indicates the act of calling upon divine forces or energies for assistance or blessings.
  • upa: Meaning "towards" or "near," this word suggests a movement towards a goal or a divine presence, indicating a desire for closeness to the divine.
  • tvā: This translates to "you," referring directly to the divine or the higher power being addressed in the mantra.
  • kāmān: Meaning "desires" or "wishes," this word highlights the human aspect of longing and aspiration, emphasizing the importance of intentions in our lives.
  • mahaḥ: This word means "great" or "mighty," indicating the significance and magnitude of the desires being expressed.
  • sasṛjmahe: This translates to "we create" or "we produce," suggesting an active role in manifesting desires and intentions.
  • udeva: Meaning "O divine one," this is a respectful address to the higher power, reinforcing the connection between the individual and the divine.
  • yanta: This word means "to move" or "to guide," indicating a desire for direction and support from the divine in the pursuit of one's goals.
  • udabhiḥ: This translates to "from the waters," symbolizing the source of life, nourishment, and abundance, often associated with the flow of creativity and inspiration.

In summary, this mantra expresses a deep yearning for connection with the divine, invoking Indra to assist in the manifestation of our desires. It emphasizes the importance of grounding our aspirations in a higher purpose and seeking guidance from divine forces. The mantra can be applied in real-life scenarios such as:

  • When setting personal goals, one can recite this mantra to align their intentions with a higher purpose, seeking strength and guidance from the universe.
  • In moments of uncertainty or challenge, invoking this mantra can provide a sense of support and direction, reminding individuals of their inherent power to create and manifest their desires.
  • During rituals or meditative practices, this mantra can be used to cultivate a deeper connection with the divine, enhancing spiritual growth and understanding.

The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to recognize their potential and the importance of divine support in their endeavors. It serves as a reminder that our desires, when aligned with a higher purpose, can lead to meaningful and fulfilling outcomes.
