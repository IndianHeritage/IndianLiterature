वियस्य ते ज्रयसानस्याजर धक्षोर न वाताः परि सन्त्य अच्युताः |आरण्वासो युयुधयो न सत्वनं तृतं नशन्त प्र शिषन्त इष्टये ||
vi yasya te jrayasānasyājara dhakṣor na vātāḥ pari santy acyutāḥ | ā raṇvāso yuyudhayo na satvanaṁ tritaṁ naśanta pra śiṣanta iṣṭaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vi: of whom
    • yasya: whose
    • te: your
    • jrayasānasyā: of the one who is not subject to decay
    • ājara: immortal
    • dhakṣor: of the one who is powerful
    • na: not
    • vātāḥ: winds
    • pari: around
    • santy: exist
    • acyutāḥ: imperishable
    • ā: to
    • raṇvāso: those who are engaged in battle
    • yuyudhayo: those who fight
    • na: not
    • satvanaṁ: of the strong
    • tritaṁ: the third
    • naśanta: those who perish
    • pra: for
    • śiṣanta: those who remain
    • iṣṭaye: for the purpose of worship
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the eternal and imperishable nature of the divine.
    • It emphasizes that the true essence of existence is beyond decay and destruction.
    • The reference to "winds" suggests that external forces cannot affect the eternal truth.
    • The phrase "those who are engaged in battle" can be interpreted as the struggles we face in life.
    • It highlights that while many may perish in their struggles, those who remain steadfast in their purpose will endure.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the context of personal challenges, individuals may face hardships that seem overwhelming, yet those who maintain their integrity and purpose often emerge stronger.
    • In a professional setting, companies that adapt to change and remain true to their core values tend to thrive despite market fluctuations.
    • In relationships, those who nurture their bonds with love and respect, even in difficult times, often find their connections deepening.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the imperishable nature of the spirit and the importance of resilience in the face of adversity.
    • It encourages individuals to focus on their inner strength and purpose, rather than being swayed by external circumstances.
    • In real life, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to a more fulfilling and meaningful existence, as one learns to navigate challenges with grace and determination.
