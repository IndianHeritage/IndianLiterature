अभि द्विजन्मा त्रीरोचनानि विश्वा राजांसि शुशुचानो अस्तात् |होतā यजिष्ठो अपां सधस्थे ||
abhi dvijanmā trī rocanāni viśvā rajāṁsi śuśucāno asthāt | hotā yajiṣṭho apāṁ sadhasthe ||
  • abhi - This word can be interpreted as "towards" or "in the direction of." It signifies an intention or a goal.
  • dvijanmā - This term refers to "twice-born," which in Vedic literature often symbolizes those who have undergone a spiritual rebirth or initiation, such as Brahmins.
  • trī - This means "three," indicating a triad or a set of three important elements.
  • rocanāni - This word translates to "shining" or "radiant," suggesting brightness or enlightenment.
  • viśvā - This means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusiveness or the entirety of existence.
  • rajāṁsi - This term refers to "kingdoms" or "realms," which can symbolize different aspects of life or the universe.
  • śuśucāno - This word can be interpreted as "having heard" or "listening attentively," suggesting the importance of awareness and understanding.
  • asthāt - This means "stood" or "established," indicating stability or permanence.
  • hotā - This term refers to the "priest" or "invoker," someone who performs rituals and connects the material and spiritual worlds.
  • yajiṣṭho - This means "the most excellent in sacrifice," highlighting the value of selflessness and devotion in actions.
  • apāṁ - This translates to "of waters," which can symbolize the flow of life, emotions, or the subconscious.
  • sadhasthe - This means "in the right place" or "well-placed," indicating the importance of being in harmony with one's surroundings.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of spiritual awakening and the interconnectedness of all aspects of life. It highlights the importance of being aware, listening, and establishing oneself in a stable and harmonious manner. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal growth, community building, and the pursuit of knowledge. By striving for enlightenment and understanding, individuals can contribute positively to their environments, fostering unity and cooperation. The mantra serves as a reminder that through selfless actions and awareness, one can achieve a greater purpose and connect with the universal essence of existence.
