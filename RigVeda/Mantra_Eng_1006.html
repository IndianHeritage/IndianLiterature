यā ते धामानि हविषा यजन्ति ता ते विश्वा परिभूर अस्तु यज्ञम् |गयस्पानः प्रतरणः सुवीरो 'वीरहा प्र चरā सोम दुर्यān ||
yā te dhāmāni haviṣā yajanti tā te viśvā paribhūr astu yajñam | gayasphānaḥ prataraṇaḥ suvīro 'vīrahā pra carā soma duryān ||
  • yā te dhāmāni haviṣā yajanti: This phrase can be translated as "Those who worship you with offerings." Here, "yā" refers to "those," "te" means "you," "dhāmāni" signifies "abodes" or "places," "haviṣā" refers to "offerings," and "yajanti" means "worship." This part emphasizes the importance of offerings and worship in connecting with the divine.
  • tā te viśvā paribhūr astu yajñam: This translates to "May all these offerings be accepted by you." "Tā" means "these," "te" again refers to "you," "viśvā" means "all," "paribhūr" signifies "encompassing," and "astu" means "may it be." "Yajñam" refers to "sacrifice" or "ritual." This line highlights the universality of worship and the hope that all offerings are embraced by the divine.
  • gayasphānaḥ prataraṇaḥ suvīro: This can be interpreted as "The strong and valiant one who overcomes obstacles." "Gayasphānaḥ" refers to "the one who is strong," "prataraṇaḥ" means "overcoming," and "suvīro" signifies "valiant" or "brave." This part of the mantra speaks to the qualities of strength and bravery in facing life's challenges.
  • 'vīrahā pra carā soma duryān: This translates to "May the brave one who moves forward be blessed with strength." "'vīrahā" means "the brave one," "pra" signifies "forward," "carā" means "to move," and "soma duryān" refers to "the essence of strength." This line encourages the pursuit of courage and the importance of moving forward in life with strength.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, strength, and the importance of offerings in spiritual practice. It emphasizes the connection between the worshipper and the divine, highlighting the hope that all offerings are accepted and that one is blessed with strength and courage.
  • Real-life Application: In daily life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios. For instance, when facing challenges at work or in personal relationships, one can invoke the qualities of bravery and strength. Additionally, it serves as a reminder to engage in acts of kindness and generosity, akin to making offerings, which can foster a sense of community and connection with others.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of worship, the acceptance of offerings, and the cultivation of strength and bravery in the face of life's challenges. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate their lives with purpose and resilience, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and the world around them.
