शतानीक एव प्र जिगाति धृष्णुया हन्ति वृत्राणि दाशुषे |गिरेर इव प्र रसाऽस्य पिन्विरे दात्राणि पुरुभोजसः ||
śatānīkeva pra jigāti dhṛṣṇuyā hanti vṛtrāṇi dāśuṣe | girer iva pra rasā asya pinvire datrāṇi purubhojasaḥ ||
  • śatānīkeva: This term can be broken down into "śatāni" meaning "hundreds" and "īkeva" suggesting "like" or "as if." It implies a multitude or a large number, indicating strength in numbers.
  • pra jigāti: "Pra" means "forward" or "before," and "jigāti" translates to "conquers" or "overcomes." Together, it suggests a proactive approach to overcoming challenges.
  • dhṛṣṇuyā: This word is derived from "dhṛṣṇu," which means "courage" or "bravery." It emphasizes the importance of having courage in the face of adversity.
  • hanti: This translates to "kills" or "destroys," indicating a forceful action against obstacles or enemies.
  • vṛtrāṇi: "Vṛtra" refers to a demon or obstacle, often symbolizing ignorance or negativity. "Vṛtrāṇi" thus refers to the challenges or adversities one faces.
  • dāśuṣe: This term can be interpreted as "to the one who is devoted" or "to the servant." It suggests a sense of duty or commitment in overcoming challenges.
  • girer iva: "Girer" means "of the mountain," and "iva" translates to "like." This phrase draws a comparison to the strength and stability of mountains.
  • pra rasā: "Pra" again means "forward," and "rasā" refers to "essence" or "juice." This can symbolize the vital energy or essence that propels one forward.
  • asya: This means "of this" or "of it," indicating possession or belonging.
  • pinvire: This term means "to nourish" or "to strengthen," suggesting the act of providing support or sustenance.
  • datrāṇi: "Datrā" refers to "givers" or "providers," indicating those who offer help or resources.
  • purubhojasaḥ: This can be broken down into "puru" meaning "many" and "bhojasaḥ" meaning "enjoyers" or "those who partake." It suggests a community or collective that benefits from shared resources.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the power of collective strength, courage, and the importance of overcoming obstacles. It teaches that with bravery and unity, one can conquer challenges that may seem insurmountable, much like a mountain that stands firm against the elements. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in a corporate environment, community support during crises, or personal challenges where one seeks to overcome fears and doubts. The mantra serves as a reminder that with the right mindset and support, individuals can achieve great things and nourish each other in the process.
