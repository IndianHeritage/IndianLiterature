मन्त्र: चत्वार इम बिभ्रति क्षेमयन्तो दश गर्भं चरसे धापयन्ते | त्रिधातवः परमाऽस्य गावो दिवश्चरण्ति परि सद्यो अन्तान् ||
catvāra īm bibhrati kṣemayanto daśa garbhaṁ carase dhāpayante | tridhātavaḥ paramā asya gāvo divaś caranti pari sadyo antān ||
  • catvāra īm bibhrati: This phrase translates to "the four (elements) hold." In this context, it refers to the four fundamental aspects of existence that support life. These can be interpreted as earth, water, fire, and air, which are essential for sustaining life on our planet.
  • kṣemayanto: This word means "bringing well-being" or "promoting safety." It emphasizes the importance of these four elements in ensuring a harmonious and safe environment for all living beings.
  • daśa garbhaṁ: This translates to "ten wombs." In a broader sense, it can be understood as the ten directions or realms of existence, symbolizing the diversity of life and experiences that arise from the interplay of the four elements.
  • carase dhāpayante: This means "they move and nourish." It signifies the dynamic nature of these elements and their role in nurturing life, suggesting that they are not static but constantly interacting and evolving.
  • tridhātavaḥ: This term refers to "the three realms" or "three states of existence." It can be interpreted as the physical, mental, and spiritual dimensions of life, highlighting the interconnectedness of all aspects of existence.
  • paramā asya gāvo: This phrase means "the supreme cows of this." In Vedic literature, cows symbolize abundance, nourishment, and prosperity. This line suggests that the ultimate source of sustenance and wealth comes from a harmonious relationship with nature and the elements.
  • divaś caranti: This translates to "they move in the sky." It can be interpreted as the celestial bodies or divine forces that govern the universe, indicating that the elements are not only earthly but also connected to the cosmos.
  • pari sadyo antān: This means "surrounding the immediate ends." It suggests that these elements and their nurturing qualities encompass all aspects of life, providing support and sustenance at every level of existence.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of the four fundamental elements of life and their role in promoting well-being and harmony. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to environmental conservation, where understanding the balance of nature is crucial for sustaining life. For instance, practices such as sustainable agriculture, water conservation, and air quality management reflect the principles outlined in this mantra. By recognizing the significance of these elements and their nurturing qualities, individuals and communities can work towards a more harmonious existence with nature, ensuring prosperity and well-being for all living beings.
