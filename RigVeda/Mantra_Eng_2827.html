इन्द्रो मधु संभरतम् उष्रियायाम् पद्वद् विवेद शफवन नामे गोः |गुहा हितं गुह्यं गूढम् अप्सु हस्ते दधे दक्षिणे दक्षिणावान् ||
indro madhu sambhṛtam usriyāyām padvad viveda śaphavan name goḥ | guhā hitaṁ guhyaṁ gūḻham apsu haste dadhe dakṣiṇe dakṣiṇāvān ||
  • Indro: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Madhu: Means honey, often representing sweetness, nourishment, and the essence of life.
  • Sambhṛtam: Translates to 'that which is gathered' or 'accumulated', indicating abundance and prosperity.
  • Usriyāyām: Refers to the dawn or the early morning, symbolizing new beginnings and enlightenment.
  • Padvad: Means 'to tread' or 'to walk', suggesting a journey or path taken.
  • Viveda: Translates to 'to know' or 'to understand', indicating wisdom and insight.
  • Śaphavan: Refers to 'the one who is auspicious' or 'beneficial', highlighting positivity and good fortune.
  • Name: Means 'name' or 'fame', indicating recognition and identity.
  • Goḥ: Refers to 'cows', which are symbols of wealth and nourishment in Vedic culture.
  • Guhā: Means 'cave' or 'hidden', representing the depths of knowledge and the subconscious.
  • Hitaṁ: Translates to 'beneficial' or 'good', indicating positive outcomes.
  • Guhyaṁ: Means 'secret' or 'mystical', suggesting hidden truths and deeper understanding.
  • Gūḻham: Refers to 'hidden' or 'concealed', emphasizing the importance of uncovering hidden knowledge.
  • Apsu: Means 'in the waters', symbolizing the source of life and purity.
  • Haste: Refers to 'hand', indicating action and the ability to create or manifest.
  • Dadhe: Means 'to place' or 'to establish', suggesting the act of grounding or setting something in motion.
  • Dakṣiṇe: Refers to 'the right side' or 'south', often associated with auspiciousness and prosperity.
  • Dakṣiṇāvān: Means 'one who is generous' or 'one who gives', highlighting the importance of generosity and sharing.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge, understanding the hidden aspects of life, and recognizing the abundance around us. It encourages individuals to tread the path of wisdom, to be generous, and to appreciate the sweetness of life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways, such as in personal growth, community service, and fostering relationships. By embodying the qualities of Indra, one can lead with strength and wisdom, contributing positively to society and nurturing the environment around them.
