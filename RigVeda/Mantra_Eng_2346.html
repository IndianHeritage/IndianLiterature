इन्धन्वभिर धेनुभी रप्शदूदभिर अध्वस्मभिः पथिभिर भ्राजदृष्टयः |आ हंसासो न स्वसराणि गन्तन मधोर मदाया मरुतः समन्यवः ||
indhanvabhir dhenubhī rapśadūdhabhir adhvasmabhiḥ pathibhir bhrājadṛṣṭayaḥ | ā haṁsāso na svasarāṇi gantana madhor madāya marutaḥ samanyavaḥ ||
  • Indhanvabhir: This term refers to the "fuel" or "sustenance" that is essential for life and growth. In a real-life context, this can be likened to the resources we need, such as food, water, and energy, to thrive.
  • Dhenubhī: This word means "with the cows" or "with the nurturing." Cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and nourishment in many cultures. In modern life, this can represent the importance of agriculture and animal husbandry in sustaining communities.
  • Rapśadūdhabhir: This term can be interpreted as "with the essence of milk" or "nourishing substances." Milk is a vital source of nutrition, and in a broader sense, it signifies the importance of nurturing and caring for one another.
  • Adhvasmabhiḥ: This means "with our paths" or "with our journeys." It emphasizes the significance of the paths we choose in life, whether they are personal, professional, or spiritual.
  • Pathibhir: This translates to "with the ways" or "with the routes." It highlights the various methods and approaches we take to achieve our goals and navigate through life.
  • Bhrājadṛṣṭayaḥ: This term means "with shining visions" or "with clear sight." It suggests the importance of having clarity and vision in our endeavors, akin to setting clear goals in our personal and professional lives.
  • Ā haṁsāso: This can be interpreted as "the swans" or "the pure ones." Swans are often associated with beauty and grace, symbolizing the pursuit of purity and excellence in our actions.
  • Na svasarāṇi: This means "not to the relatives" or "not to the kin." It suggests a focus beyond familial ties, encouraging individuals to think about the broader community and society.
  • Gantana: This translates to "to go" or "to move forward." It emphasizes the importance of progress and the journey of life.
  • Madhor: This means "of the sweet" or "of the pleasant." It signifies the pursuit of joy and happiness in our lives.
  • Madāya: This term can be interpreted as "for intoxication" or "for delight." It suggests that life should be enjoyed and celebrated, akin to finding joy in our daily experiences.
  • Marutaḥ: This refers to "the winds" or "the forces of nature." It symbolizes the dynamic and ever-changing aspects of life, reminding us to be adaptable and resilient.
  • Samanyavaḥ: This means "common" or "shared." It emphasizes the importance of community and collective efforts in achieving common goals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of sustenance, nurturing, clear vision, and community. It teaches us that our paths in life should be guided by clarity and purpose, while also reminding us to find joy in our journey. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal development, community building, and the pursuit of happiness. It encourages individuals to seek nourishment not just for themselves but for the collective good, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose in society.
