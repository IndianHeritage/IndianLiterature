त्वया वयम् मगवन्न इन्द्र शत्रून अभि ष्यामा महतो मन्यमानान् |त्वं त्राताऽ त्वम् उ नो वृधे भूः विद्यामेषं वृजनं जीरदानुम् ||
tvayā vayam maghavann indra śatrūn abhi ṣyāma mahato manyamānān | tvaṁ trātā tvam u no vṛdhe bhūr vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum ||
  • tvayā vayam maghavann: This phrase translates to "With your help, O Maghavan (Indra), we shall." Here, "tvayā" means "with you," and "maghavann" refers to Indra, the king of gods, known for his strength and power.
  • indra śatrūn abhi ṣyāma: This means "we shall overcome our enemies." "Indra" is invoked as a protector, and "śatrūn" refers to enemies or obstacles that one may face in life.
  • mahato manyamānān: This translates to "considering the great." "Mahato" means "great," and "manyamānān" refers to those who are esteemed or respected. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and valuing greatness in our endeavors.
  • tvaṁ trātā: This means "you are our protector." It is a direct appeal to Indra, acknowledging his role as a guardian and savior in times of need.
  • tvam u no vṛdhe bhūr: This translates to "you increase our wealth." "Vṛdhe" means "to increase," and "bhūr" refers to abundance or wealth, both material and spiritual.
  • vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum: This means "this knowledge is our strength." "Vidyā" refers to knowledge or wisdom, and "jīradānum" implies that this knowledge sustains and empowers us.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine support in overcoming challenges and achieving greatness. It highlights the role of knowledge and wisdom as essential tools for success.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during difficult times when one seeks guidance and strength. For instance, students preparing for exams can recite this mantra to invoke focus and clarity, while professionals facing challenges in their careers can use it to seek support and protection from obstacles.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of faith, knowledge, and the importance of seeking divine assistance in our endeavors. It encourages individuals to recognize their strengths and the support available to them, fostering a sense of resilience and determination in the face of adversity.
