आत्मा पितुस तनूर वास ओजोधा अभ्यञ्जनम् |तुरीयम् इद रोहितस्य पाकस्थामानम् भोजं दातारम् अब्रवम् ||
ātmā pitus tanūr vāsa ojodā abhyañjanam | turīyam id rohitasya pākasthāmānam bhojaṁ dātāram abravam ||
  • ātmā: This word translates to "soul" or "self." It signifies the essence of an individual, representing consciousness and identity.
  • pitus: Meaning "of the father," this term emphasizes the connection to paternal lineage and the wisdom passed down through generations.
  • tanūr: This refers to the "body" or "form." It highlights the physical aspect of existence, which is temporary and subject to change.
  • vāsa: Translated as "dwelling" or "residence," it indicates where the self resides, both in a physical and spiritual sense.
  • ojodā: This word means "vital energy" or "strength." It represents the life force that sustains all living beings.
  • abhyañjanam: This term can be understood as "nourishment" or "enhancement." It signifies the importance of nurturing the self and the body.
  • turīyam: This word means "the fourth" or "beyond the three states of consciousness." It refers to a higher state of awareness or spiritual realization.
  • id: This translates to "this," indicating a focus on the present moment or the current state of being.
  • rohitasya: Meaning "of the red" or "of the sun," it symbolizes vitality, warmth, and the life-giving force of the sun.
  • pākasthāmānam: This term refers to "the one who is to be offered" or "the sacrificial offering," indicating the importance of giving and sharing.
  • bhojaṁ: This means "food" or "nourishment," emphasizing the necessity of sustenance for both body and soul.
  • dātāram: Translated as "the giver," it signifies generosity and the act of providing for others.
  • abravam: This means "I speak" or "I declare," indicating the importance of communication and expression in sharing wisdom.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the interconnectedness of the self, the body, and the vital energies that sustain life. It emphasizes the significance of nurturing both the physical and spiritual aspects of existence. In real life, this can be applied through practices such as meditation, healthy living, and fostering relationships that nourish the soul. By recognizing the importance of our roots (paternal lineage) and the energy that sustains us, we can strive for a balanced life that honors both our physical needs and spiritual growth. This mantra serves as a reminder to be generous (dātāram) and to communicate (abravam) our experiences and wisdom with others, fostering a community of support and nourishment.
