अग्निं होतारं प्र वृणे मियेदे गृत्सं कविं विश्वविदम् अमूरम् | स नो यक्षद देवताता यजीयान रायें वाजाय वनते मघानि ||
agniṁ hotāram pra vṛṇe miyedhe gṛtsaṁ kaviṁ viśvavidam amūram | sa no yakṣad devatātā yajīyān rāye vājāya vanate maghāni ||
  • Agniṁ hotāram: This phrase refers to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a vital force in Vedic rituals. Agni is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
  • Pra vṛṇe: This means "I choose" or "I invoke." It signifies the act of selecting or calling upon a deity or force for assistance.
  • Miyedhe: This term can be interpreted as "may he come" or "may he be present," indicating a desire for the divine presence in one's life.
  • Gṛtsaṁ: This word refers to the "sacred" or "pure," emphasizing the importance of purity in rituals and intentions.
  • Kaviṁ viśvavidam: "Kavi" means "sage" or "poet," and "viśvavidam" means "one who knows everything." This phrase acknowledges the wisdom and knowledge of the divine.
  • Amūram: This translates to "immortal" or "eternal," highlighting the everlasting nature of the divine and its teachings.
  • Sa no yakṣad: This means "may he protect us," expressing a plea for divine protection and guidance.
  • Devataṭā: This refers to the "divine nature" or "essence," emphasizing the importance of connecting with the divine in our lives.
  • Yajīyān: This term means "worthy of worship," indicating the reverence that should be given to the divine forces.
  • Rāye: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity," suggesting a desire for abundance in life.
  • Vājāya: This means "for strength" or "for power," indicating a wish for empowerment and vigor in one's endeavors.
  • Vanate: This term can be interpreted as "to flourish" or "to grow," symbolizing the growth and success one seeks in life.
  • Maghāni: This translates to "great blessings" or "riches," emphasizing the aspiration for receiving divine blessings and prosperity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for divine presence, protection, and blessings in one's life. It emphasizes the importance of purity, wisdom, and the connection with the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during rituals, prayers, or moments of reflection when one seeks guidance, strength, and prosperity. By invoking Agni and the divine forces, individuals can cultivate a sense of purpose and direction, fostering a harmonious relationship with the universe and its energies.
