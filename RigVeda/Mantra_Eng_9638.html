अग्नये ब्रह्म ऋभवस् तातक्षुर अग्निम महाम् अवोचामā सुवृक्तिम् |अग्ने प्राव जरितारं यविष्ठाग्ने महि द्रविणम् ā यजस्व ||
agnaye brahma ṛbhavas tatakṣur agnim mahām avocāmā suvṛktim | agne prāva jaritāraṁ yaviṣṭhāgne mahi draviṇam ā yajasva ||
  • Agnaye: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Brahma: In this context, it signifies the ultimate reality or the cosmic spirit, representing the source of all creation.
  • ṛbhavas: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is rich in offerings" or "the one who is worthy of praise," indicating the importance of reverence towards Agni.
  • Tatakṣur: This word means "to see" or "to perceive," suggesting the need for clarity and insight in our actions and thoughts.
  • Agnim: Again referring to Agni, emphasizing the central role of fire in rituals and as a divine presence.
  • Mahām: This means "great" or "mighty," highlighting the power and significance of Agni.
  • Avocāmā: This translates to "we speak" or "we declare," indicating a collective expression of intent or prayer.
  • Suvṛktim: This term means "good speech" or "auspicious utterance," emphasizing the importance of positive communication and intentions.
  • Agne: Again invoking Agni, reinforcing the connection to the divine fire.
  • Prāva: This means "to lead" or "to guide," suggesting that Agni leads us towards righteousness.
  • Jaritāraṁ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is called upon," indicating the act of seeking Agni's presence.
  • Yaviṣṭhā: This term means "the best" or "the most excellent," suggesting that we seek the highest qualities in our endeavors.
  • Mahi: This means "great" or "vast," indicating the expansive nature of Agni's influence.
  • Draviṇam: This refers to "wealth" or "resources," suggesting that Agni is a source of abundance.
  • Āyajasva: This means "to be worshipped" or "to be honored," indicating the importance of reverence towards Agni.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a divine force that guides, purifies, and transforms. It highlights the importance of positive intentions, communication, and reverence in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as rituals, personal development, and community gatherings where the focus is on collective growth and the pursuit of higher ideals. By invoking Agni, we seek to align ourselves with the principles of clarity, abundance, and righteousness, fostering a harmonious environment in our personal and communal lives.
