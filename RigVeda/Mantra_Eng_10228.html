यावयाऽवृक्यं वृकम् यवय स्तेनम् ऊर्म्ये |अथा नः सुतरा भव ||
yāvayā vṛkya1ṁ vṛkaṁ yavaya stenam ūrmye | athā naḥ sutarā bhava ||
  • yāvayā: This word can be interpreted as "as long as" or "until." It signifies a condition or a duration.
  • vṛkyaṁ: This term refers to "the wolf" or "the wild." In a broader sense, it can symbolize challenges or adversities in life.
  • vṛkaṁ: This is another form of "wolf," emphasizing the presence of difficulties or obstacles.
  • yavaya: This word can mean "to be free from" or "to be liberated." It suggests a desire for freedom from troubles.
  • stenam: This translates to "thief" or "deceiver." It represents negative forces or influences that can disrupt peace.
  • ūrmye: This word means "in the waves" or "in the currents." It can symbolize the flow of life and the challenges that come with it.
  • athā: This means "then" or "therefore," indicating a transition or a conclusion based on the previous statements.
  • naḥ: This translates to "to us," indicating a plea or a request directed towards a higher power.
  • sutarā: This means "better" or "more favorable," suggesting a desire for improvement or enhancement in circumstances.
  • bhava: This translates to "be" or "become," indicating a wish for transformation or change.

Meaning and Interpretation:

The mantra expresses a plea for liberation from the challenges and negative influences represented by the "wolves" and "thieves" in life. It acknowledges the presence of difficulties (vṛkyaṁ and vṛkaṁ) and the deceptive forces (stenam) that can disrupt one's peace and progress (ūrmye). The speaker seeks a transformation (bhava) towards a more favorable state (sutarā) as long as these challenges exist (yāvayā).


Real-Life Examples:

  • In a professional setting, an employee may face challenges from competitors (vṛkyaṁ) and deceptive practices (stenam) that hinder their growth. By invoking this mantra, they seek support to overcome these obstacles and achieve success (sutarā).
  • In personal relationships, one might encounter misunderstandings or conflicts (vṛkaṁ) that feel overwhelming. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek clarity and resolution (bhava) for a healthier relationship.
  • In the context of mental health, individuals may struggle with anxiety or depression (ūrmye) that feels like a thief of joy. This mantra encourages seeking help and striving for a better state of mind (sutarā).

Conclusion:

The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for overcoming life's adversities and seeking a more favorable existence. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal struggles to professional challenges. By acknowledging the presence of difficulties and expressing a desire for transformation, individuals can find strength and support in their journey towards a better life.
