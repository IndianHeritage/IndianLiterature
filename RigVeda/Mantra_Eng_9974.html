उभे धुरौ वह्निर आपिब्दमानोऽन्तर योनेव चरति द्विजानि: |वनस्पतिं वन आस्थापयध्वं न शू दधिध्वम् अखनन्त उत्सम् ||
ubhe dhurau vahnir āpibdamāno 'ntar yoneva carati dvijāniḥ | vanaspatiṁ vana āsthāpayadhvaṁ ni ṣū dadhidhvam akhananta utsam ||
  • Mantra: "ubhe dhurau vahnir āpibdamāno 'ntar yoneva carati dvijāniḥ | vanaspatiṁ vana āsthāpayadhvaṁ ni ṣū dadhidhvam akhananta utsam ||"
  • Translation: "Both the fires (vahnir) are moving in the same direction, like two birds (dvijāniḥ) flying together. Establish the plant (vanaspati) in the forest (vana) and do not let the unbroken (akhananta) energy (utsam) be wasted."

  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ubhe: both
    • dhurau: moving or carrying
    • vahnir: fire
    • āpibdamāno: consuming or taking in
    • 'ntar: within
    • yoneva: like a bird
    • carati: moves
    • dvijāniḥ: two birds
    • vanaspatiṁ: plant or vegetation
    • vana: forest
    • āsthāpayadhvaṁ: establish or set
    • ni: not
    • ṣū: unbroken or continuous
    • dadhidhvam: let it be
    • akhananta: unbroken or eternal
    • utsam: energy or essence

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the duality of existence, represented by the two fires that symbolize different aspects of life or energy.
    • The imagery of two birds flying together suggests harmony and cooperation, which can be seen in real-life relationships, such as partnerships in business or marriage.
    • The call to establish the plant in the forest signifies the importance of nurturing growth and sustainability in our environment, akin to community gardening or conservation efforts.
    • The phrase about not wasting unbroken energy emphasizes the need to utilize our resources wisely, similar to how we should manage our time and efforts in personal and professional endeavors.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of balance, cooperation, and sustainability in our lives.
    • It encourages us to work together harmoniously, whether in personal relationships or community efforts, to foster growth and development.
    • In a broader context, it serves as a reminder to respect and nurture our environment, ensuring that our actions contribute positively to the world around us.
    • Ultimately, the mantra teaches us to harness our energies effectively, promoting a life of purpose and interconnectedness.
