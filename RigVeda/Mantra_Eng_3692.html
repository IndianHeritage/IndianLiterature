ऊर्णम्रदा वि प्रथस्वाभ्य अ1र्का अनूषत |भव नः शुभ्र सातये ||
ūrṇamradā vi prathasvābhy a1rkā anūṣata | bhavā naḥ śubhra sātaye ||
  • ūrṇamradā: This term can be broken down into two parts: "ūrṇa" meaning "abundance" or "wealth," and "mradā" which can be interpreted as "nourishing" or "sustaining." Together, it signifies a source of abundant nourishment.
  • vi prathasvābhy: "vi" means "through" or "by," while "prathasvābhy" can be understood as "to spread" or "to expand." This phrase suggests the idea of spreading or expanding abundance in various aspects of life.
  • a1rkā: This term is often associated with "light" or "radiance." It symbolizes knowledge, enlightenment, and clarity. In a practical sense, it can refer to the illumination of the mind and spirit.
  • anūṣata: This word can be interpreted as "to be present" or "to exist." It emphasizes the importance of being aware and conscious of one's surroundings and experiences.
  • bhavā naḥ: "bhavā" translates to "be" or "exist," and "naḥ" means "for us." This phrase expresses a desire for existence or being that is beneficial and positive for the speaker and their community.
  • śubhra: This word means "pure," "bright," or "auspicious." It conveys a sense of positivity and goodness, suggesting that the desired existence should be one that is virtuous and beneficial.
  • sātaye: This term can be understood as "for the purpose" or "for the attainment." It indicates a goal or intention behind the desire for a pure and auspicious existence.

Conclusion: The mantra "ūrṇamradā vi prathasvābhy a1rkā anūṣata | bhavā naḥ śubhra sātaye ||" emphasizes the importance of seeking abundant nourishment, spreading positivity, and striving for a pure and auspicious existence. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, community building, and fostering relationships. For instance, in a workplace, promoting a culture of support and collaboration can lead to collective growth and success. In personal life, nurturing relationships with positivity and abundance can lead to a fulfilling and harmonious existence. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate an environment that encourages growth, positivity, and enlightenment for oneself and others.
