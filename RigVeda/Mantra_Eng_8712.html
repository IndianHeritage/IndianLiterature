प्र सोम देववीतये सिंधुर न पिप्ये अर्णसा |अंशोः पयसा मदिरो न जागृविर अच्छा कोशम् मधुश्चुतम् ||
pra soma devavītaye sindhur na pipye arṇasā | aṁśoḥ payasā madiro na jāgṛvir acchā kośam madhuścutam ||
  • Pra Soma Devavītaye: This phrase refers to the offering of Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, to the deities. Soma symbolizes divine inspiration and the essence of life.
  • Sindhur na pipye arṇasā: Here, "sindhur" means "ocean" or "river," and "na pipye" suggests not being consumed. This can be interpreted as the divine essence not being depleted, emphasizing the abundance of spiritual resources.
  • Aṁśoḥ payasā madiro: "Aṁśoḥ" refers to a part or portion, while "payasā" means "milk." "Madiro" indicates intoxication or bliss. This line suggests that a portion of divine bliss is derived from the nurturing essence of life, akin to how milk nourishes and sustains.
  • Na jāgṛvir acchā kośam: "Na jāgṛvir" means "not waking" or "not alert," and "acchā kośam" refers to the "good treasure" or "wealth." This implies that true wealth is not merely material but lies in spiritual awakening and consciousness.
  • Madhuścutam: This term translates to "sweetness" or "honey," symbolizing the ultimate bliss and joy that comes from spiritual fulfillment and connection with the divine.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of spiritual nourishment and the importance of divine connection in our lives. It signifies that true wealth and bliss come from spiritual awareness and the nurturing aspects of life, rather than material possessions. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied by seeking deeper connections with our spiritual selves, engaging in practices that promote mindfulness, and recognizing the abundance of resources available to us. By embracing these principles, we can cultivate a more fulfilling and enriched life, ultimately leading to a greater sense of purpose and joy.
