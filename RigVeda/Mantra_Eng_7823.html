एष पुरू धियायते बृहते देवतातये |यत्रामृतासा आसते ||
eṣa purū dhiyāyate bṛhate devatātaye | yatrāmṛtāsa āsate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣa: this
    • purū: many, abundant
    • dhiyāyate: is contemplated, is thought upon
    • bṛhate: great, vast
    • devatātaye: for the divine, pertaining to the gods
    • yatra: where
    • amṛtāsa: the immortal ones, the eternal beings
    • āsate: dwell, reside
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of a contemplation or thought process that is vast and abundant, directed towards the divine.
    • It suggests that in this contemplation, one can connect with the eternal beings or the essence of immortality.
    • The phrase implies a space or state where the divine and the immortal coexist, highlighting the importance of spiritual reflection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In meditation practices, individuals often contemplate on the vastness of the universe and their connection to the divine, similar to the essence of this mantra.
    • In moments of deep reflection, such as during prayer or spiritual gatherings, people seek to connect with a higher power, embodying the idea of dwelling with the immortal.
    • Philosophical discussions about the nature of existence and the divine can also reflect the themes present in this mantra, as individuals explore the relationship between the material and the spiritual.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of contemplation and reflection on the divine and the eternal.
    • It serves as a reminder that through deep thought and spiritual practice, one can connect with higher realms of existence.
    • In real life, this can be applicable in various spiritual practices, encouraging individuals to seek a deeper understanding of their place in the universe and their relationship with the divine.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the significance of spirituality in achieving a sense of peace and connection with the eternal aspects of life.
