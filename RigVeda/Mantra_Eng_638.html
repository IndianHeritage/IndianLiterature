त्वं एतान् जनराज्ञो द्विर दशाबन्धुना सुश्रवसोपजग्मुषः |षष्टिं सहस्राणि नवतीं नव श्रुतो न चक्रेण रथ्या दुष्पदावृणक ||
tvam etāñ janarājño dvir daśābandhunā suśravasopajagmuṣaḥ | ṣaṣṭiṁ sahasrā navatiṁ nava śruto ni cakreṇa rathyā duṣpadāvṛṇak ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvam: You
    • etāñ: These
    • janarājño: Of the people or the king of people
    • dvir: Twice
    • daśābandhunā: By the ten bonds or connections
    • suśravasopajagmuṣaḥ: Those who have come with good fame or reputation
    • ṣaṣṭiṁ: Sixty
    • sahasrā: Thousand
    • navatiṁ: Ninety
    • nava: Nine
    • śruto: Heard or known
    • ni: Not
    • cakreṇa: By the wheel or cycle
    • rathyā: Of the chariot or vehicle
    • duṣpadāvṛṇak: Those who are difficult to reach or attain
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine presence that connects the people and their leaders.
    • The mention of "twice" and "ten bonds" suggests the importance of relationships and connections in society.
    • The phrase "good fame" indicates that reputation and honor are essential for leaders and their followers.
    • The numbers "sixty," "ninety," and "nine" may symbolize completeness and abundance in various aspects of life.
    • The reference to "the wheel" signifies the cyclical nature of life and the importance of movement and progress.
    • "Difficult to reach" implies that certain goals or ideals may require significant effort and perseverance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, a leader who is well-respected and has a good reputation can inspire trust and cooperation among the people.
    • Building strong relationships, akin to the "ten bonds," is crucial in both personal and professional settings, such as teamwork in a corporate environment.
    • The concept of striving for excellence, represented by the numbers, can be seen in educational pursuits where students aim for high grades and achievements.
    • The cyclical nature of life can be observed in the seasons, where each cycle brings new opportunities and challenges, much like the journey of personal growth.
    • Overcoming challenges to reach difficult goals, such as completing a marathon or achieving a significant career milestone, reflects the essence of "duṣpadāvṛṇak."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of leadership, reputation, and the interconnectedness of society.
    • It serves as a reminder that achieving goals often requires strong relationships and perseverance.
    • In real life, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide individuals and communities toward harmony, progress, and success.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to strive for excellence while maintaining strong bonds with others, fostering a supportive and thriving environment.
