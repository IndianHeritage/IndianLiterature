त्वेषम् इत्त्था समरणं शिमीवतोर इन्द्राविष्णू सुतपा वाम उरुष्यति |या मार्त्याय प्रतिधीयमानम् इत कृशानोर अस्तुर असनाम उरुष्यतः ||
tveṣam itthā samaraṇaṁ śimīvator indrāviṣṇū sutapā vām uruṣyati | yā martyāya pratidhīyamānam it kṛśānor astur asanām uruṣyathaḥ ||
  • tveṣam itthā samaraṇaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "in this form, the battle is fought." It signifies the importance of being present and engaged in the struggles of life.
  • śimīvator indrāviṣṇū: Referring to the divine powers of Indra and Vishnu, this part emphasizes the strength and protection that these deities provide to those who seek their guidance.
  • sutapā vām uruṣyati: "The one who is well-prepared and has good intentions." This highlights the value of preparation and moral integrity in achieving success.
  • yā martyāya pratidhīyamānam: This translates to "for the mortal who is striving." It acknowledges the human condition and the efforts individuals make in their lives.
  • it kṛśānor astur asanām: This can be understood as "may the weak find strength." It reflects the idea of empowerment and support for those who are struggling.
  • uruṣyathaḥ: This word suggests "to be strong" or "to endure." It encourages resilience in the face of challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of human struggle, divine support, and the importance of preparation and resilience. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as facing personal challenges, pursuing goals, or seeking guidance in difficult times. For instance, a student preparing for exams can draw strength from the idea of divine support and the importance of being well-prepared. Similarly, individuals facing health issues can find solace in the notion that they are not alone in their struggles and that strength can be found through perseverance and support from others. This mantra serves as a reminder that while life may present battles, with the right mindset and support, one can overcome obstacles and emerge stronger.
