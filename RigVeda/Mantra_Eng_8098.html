उच्चा ते जातमन्धसो दिवि षड्भूम्याददे |उग्रं शर्मा महि श्रवः ||
uccā te jātam andhaso divi ṣad bhūmy ā dade | ugraṁ śarma mahi śravaḥ ||
  • Uccā te jātam andhaso: This phrase can be translated as "Your birth is high, O blind one." Here, "uccā" means high or elevated, "te" means your, "jātam" means born, and "andhaso" refers to the blind or those who lack vision. This can symbolize the idea that even those who may seem to lack insight or understanding can have a significant and elevated purpose in life.
  • Divi ṣad bhūmy ā dade: This translates to "In the heavens, you give six realms." "Divi" means in the heavens, "ṣad" means six, "bhūmy" refers to realms or worlds, and "ā dade" means you give or provide. This can be interpreted as the divine offering of various dimensions of existence, suggesting that life encompasses multiple experiences and realities.
  • Ugraṁ śarma mahi śravaḥ: This can be translated as "The fierce protection of great fame." "Ugraṁ" means fierce or powerful, "śarma" refers to protection or shelter, "mahi" means great, and "śravaḥ" means fame or renown. This indicates that true protection comes from a powerful source, and that greatness is often accompanied by recognition and respect.

  • Real-Life Example: Consider a person who has faced significant challenges in life, perhaps someone who has overcome adversity such as a disability or a difficult upbringing. Despite these challenges, they rise to prominence in their field, becoming a source of inspiration for others. This reflects the idea of "uccā te jātam andhaso," where their elevated status is achieved despite initial limitations.
  • Application in Daily Life: The mantra encourages individuals to recognize the potential within themselves and others, regardless of perceived limitations. It serves as a reminder that everyone has a unique contribution to make, and that the journey through various realms of life can lead to profound growth and understanding.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its message of empowerment and recognition of the multifaceted nature of existence. It teaches us that greatness can emerge from unexpected places and that true protection and fame come from embracing one's unique path. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with confidence, knowing that their experiences, no matter how challenging, contribute to their ultimate success and recognition.
