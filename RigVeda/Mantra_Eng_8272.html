इन्दुर हिन्वानो अर्षति तिरो वाराण्य अव्यया |हरिर वाजम् अचिक्रदत ||
indur hinvāno arṣati tiro vārāṇy avyayā | harir vājam acikradat ||
  • Indur: This word translates to "the moon." In Vedic literature, the moon is often associated with nourishment, fertility, and the passage of time.
  • Hinvāno: This means "one who is moving or advancing." It signifies progress or the act of moving forward.
  • Arṣati: This term can be interpreted as "shining" or "radiating." It denotes brilliance or the act of illuminating.
  • Tiro: This translates to "beyond" or "across." It suggests a sense of crossing boundaries or transcending limitations.
  • Vārāṇy: This word refers to "the protectors" or "the guardians." It implies a sense of safety and support.
  • Avyayā: This means "imperishable" or "eternal." It signifies something that is everlasting and unchanging.
  • Harir: This term is often associated with "the divine" or "the Lord." It represents a higher power or spiritual essence.
  • Vājam: This translates to "strength" or "power." It denotes the essence of vigor and capability.
  • Acikradat: This means "to have achieved" or "to have accomplished." It signifies success and fulfillment of goals.

The mantra can be interpreted as a celebration of the divine forces that guide and protect us as we strive for progress and illumination in our lives. The imagery of the moon shining brightly while moving forward suggests a journey of growth and enlightenment, supported by eternal guardianship.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the moon waxes and wanes, individuals can experience phases of growth and challenges. This mantra encourages embracing change and seeking guidance from higher powers or mentors.
  • Community Support: The idea of protectors resonates in community settings where individuals support one another. This can be seen in volunteer organizations or community groups that work together for a common cause.
  • Spiritual Journey: The mantra emphasizes the importance of spiritual guidance. Many people turn to meditation, prayer, or spiritual practices to connect with the divine and seek clarity in their lives.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the interplay of divine guidance, personal progress, and the eternal support that surrounds us. It serves as a reminder to seek illumination in our endeavors and to recognize the protective forces that aid us in our journey through life.
