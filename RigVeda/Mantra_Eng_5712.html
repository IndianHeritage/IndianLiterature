मन्त्र: उता त्यं भुज्युम् अश्विनाः सखायो मध्ये जहुर् दुरेवासः समुद्रे |निर्ईम् पर्षद् अरावाः यो युवाकुः ||
uta tyam bhujyum aśvinā sakhāyo madhye jahur durevāsaḥ samudre | nir īm parṣad arāvā yo yuvākuḥ ||
  • uta - This word can be translated as "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition to what has been previously mentioned.
  • tyam - This means "that" or "those," referring to something specific that is being discussed.
  • bhujyum - This term refers to "the strong" or "the powerful," often associated with strength and capability.
  • aśvinā - This refers to the "Aśvins," the twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing and benevolent qualities.
  • sakhāyo - This means "friends" or "companions," emphasizing the importance of camaraderie and support.
  • madhye - This translates to "in the midst" or "among," indicating a central position or presence.
  • jahur - This means "they have offered" or "they have given," suggesting an act of giving or sacrifice.
  • durevāsaḥ - This term can be interpreted as "the distant" or "the far-reaching," often referring to something that transcends physical boundaries.
  • samudre - This means "in the ocean," symbolizing vastness, depth, and the unknown.
  • nir - This translates to "without" or "free from," indicating a state of liberation or absence.
  • īm - This word means "to us" or "for us," suggesting a personal connection or appeal.
  • parṣad - This refers to "the assembly" or "the council," indicating a gathering of wise or knowledgeable individuals.
  • arāvā - This term can mean "the young" or "the youthful," often associated with vitality and energy.
  • yo - This means "who," introducing a relative clause that describes a person or entity.
  • yuvākuḥ - This translates to "the one who is young" or "the youthful one," emphasizing the qualities of youthfulness and potential.

The mantra speaks to the themes of strength, friendship, and the divine assistance of the Aśvins. It highlights the importance of community and support in overcoming challenges, as well as the vastness of existence represented by the ocean. The invocation of the Aśvins suggests a reliance on divine guidance and healing in times of need.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal relationships, it emphasizes the importance of having supportive friends who stand by you in difficult times.
  • In community settings, it encourages collaboration and collective efforts to achieve common goals.
  • In health and wellness, it can be a reminder to seek help and healing from both human and divine sources.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the strength found in companionship, and the importance of seeking support from both the community and the divine. It encourages individuals to embrace their youthfulness and potential while recognizing the vastness of the world around them.
