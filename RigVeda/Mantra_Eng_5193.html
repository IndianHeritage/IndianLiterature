तव त्रिधातु पृथिवी उता द्यौर वैश्वानर व्रतम अग्ने साचन्त |त्वं भासां रोदसी आ ततान्थाजस्रेण शोचिषा शोशुचानाḥ ||
tava tridhātu pṛthivī uta dyaur vaiśvānara vratam agne sacanta | tvam bhāsā rodasī ā tatanthājasreṇa śociṣā śośucānaḥ ||
  • tava tridhātu pṛthivī uta dyaur: This phrase translates to "Your three realms, the Earth and the Sky." Here, "tridhātu" refers to the three realms of existence: the physical world (Earth), the celestial realm (Sky), and the spiritual realm. In real life, this can be understood as the interconnectedness of our physical existence with the universe and the spiritual dimensions of life.
  • vaiśvānara vratam agne sacanta: This means "the vow of the universal fire, Agni, is being fulfilled." Agni, the fire god, symbolizes transformation and purification. In practical terms, this can be related to the rituals and practices we engage in to bring about change in our lives, such as setting goals or making resolutions. The idea of a "vow" emphasizes commitment and dedication to personal growth.
  • tvam bhāsā rodasī ā tatanthājasreṇa: This translates to "You, with your light, have spread across the horizons." This line highlights the illuminating power of knowledge and wisdom. In everyday life, this can be seen in the pursuit of education and enlightenment, where individuals seek to expand their understanding and share their insights with others.
  • śociṣā śośucānaḥ: This means "shining with brilliance." It signifies the purity and clarity that comes from knowledge and understanding. In real life, this can be exemplified by individuals who strive to live ethically and with integrity, thereby inspiring others through their actions and choices.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of interconnectedness between the physical, celestial, and spiritual realms. It highlights the importance of commitment to personal transformation through the metaphor of Agni, the fire god. In our daily lives, this mantra encourages us to seek knowledge, embrace change, and live with integrity. By doing so, we not only enhance our own lives but also contribute positively to the world around us. The teachings of this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, community service, and environmental stewardship, reminding us of our role in the greater cosmos.
