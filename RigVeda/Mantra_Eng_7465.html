प्र व इन्द्राय बृहत मरुतो ब्रह्मार्चत |वृत्रं हन्ति वृत्रहां शतक्रतुर वज्रेण शतपर्वणा ||
pra va indrāya bṛhate maruto brahmārcata | vṛtraṁ hanati vṛtrahā śatakratur vajreṇa śataparvaṇā ||
  • pra va indrāya: This phrase can be translated as "to Indra, the great one." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • bṛhate: This word means "great" or "vast," emphasizing the magnitude of Indra's power and influence.
  • maruto: Referring to the Maruts, the storm deities or wind gods, who are often depicted as companions of Indra. They symbolize the forces of nature and the dynamic aspects of the environment.
  • brahmārcata: This term can be interpreted as "worshipped with devotion." It signifies the act of reverence and the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • vṛtraṁ hanati: This translates to "he slays Vritra." Vritra is a serpent or dragon-like figure representing chaos and drought. Indra's victory over Vritra symbolizes the triumph of order over chaos.
  • vṛtrahā: This term means "the slayer of Vritra," reinforcing Indra's role as a protector and a bringer of prosperity.
  • śatakratur: This word refers to "the one with a hundred powers," highlighting Indra's multifaceted abilities and his role as a powerful deity.
  • vajreṇa: Meaning "with the thunderbolt," this signifies Indra's weapon, which is a symbol of his strength and ability to overcome obstacles.
  • śataparvaṇā: This can be interpreted as "with a hundred folds," suggesting the complexity and depth of Indra's power and the many layers of existence he governs.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of Indra as a powerful deity who brings order, prosperity, and protection against chaos. In real life, this can be seen as a metaphor for overcoming challenges and adversities. Just as Indra defeats Vritra to bring rain and fertility to the land, individuals can draw inspiration from this mantra to confront their own struggles and strive for success. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the importance of strength, resilience, and the divine support available to those who seek it through devotion and righteous action.
