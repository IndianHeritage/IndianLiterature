अजीजनो अमृत मर्त्येष्व आमं ऋतस्य धर्मन्न अमृतस्य चारुणः |सदासरो वाजम् अच्छा सनीष्यदत ||
ajījano amṛta martyeṣv ām̐ ṛtasya dharmann amṛtasya cāruṇaḥ | sadāsaro vājam acchā saniṣyadat ||
  • ajījano: This term refers to the eternal or the immortal aspect of existence. It signifies the unchanging nature of the soul or consciousness that transcends physical life.
  • amṛta: Meaning "immortal" or "nectar of immortality," this word emphasizes the idea of eternal life and the divine essence that sustains life beyond death.
  • martyeṣv: This translates to "among mortals," indicating the realm of human existence, where life is temporary and subject to change.
  • ām̐: A syllable often used in mantras, it can signify the essence of the universe or the sound of creation.
  • ṛtasya: This word means "of truth" or "of order," referring to the cosmic law that governs the universe and the moral order of life.
  • dharmann: This term relates to "dharma," which signifies duty, righteousness, and moral law. It emphasizes the importance of living in accordance with one's responsibilities and ethical principles.
  • amṛtasya: Similar to "amṛta," it reinforces the concept of immortality and the divine essence that is everlasting.
  • cāruṇaḥ: This word can be interpreted as "beautiful" or "radiant," suggesting the positive and uplifting qualities of the immortal essence.
  • sadāsaro: This term means "always present" or "everlasting," indicating the continuous nature of the divine or the eternal truth.
  • vājam: This translates to "strength" or "power," often associated with the energy that sustains life and the universe.
  • acchā: Meaning "good" or "auspicious," it highlights the positive aspects of existence and the importance of good deeds.
  • saniṣyadat: This term can be interpreted as "to bestow" or "to grant," indicating the act of giving or sharing the divine qualities with others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound philosophical concepts about the nature of existence, the interplay between mortality and immortality, and the importance of living a life aligned with truth and righteousness. It serves as a reminder that while we exist in a transient state, there is an eternal essence within us that connects us to the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to reflect on their actions, embrace their responsibilities, and strive for a life that honors both their own spiritual journey and the well-being of others. It can be applied in daily practices such as meditation, ethical decision-making, and fostering a sense of community and compassion among individuals.
