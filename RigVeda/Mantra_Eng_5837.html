प्र शुन्ध्युवं वरुणाय प्रेष्ठाम मतीं वसिष्ठ मीळ्हुṣe भरस्व |य इम अरवांञ्चं कराते यजत्रं सहस्रामघं वृषणं बृहन्तम् ||
pra śundhyuvaṁ varuṇāya preṣṭhām matiṁ vasiṣṭha mīḻhuṣe bharasva | ya īm arvāñcaṁ karate yajatraṁ sahasrāmaghaṁ vṛṣaṇam bṛhantam ||
  • pra śundhyuvaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we attain purity" or "may we be cleansed." In real life, this can relate to the importance of mental and spiritual cleanliness, akin to practices like meditation or mindfulness that help individuals clear their minds and focus on positive thoughts.
  • varuṇāya: Varuna is the deity associated with water, the celestial ocean, and the moral order of the universe. This signifies the importance of natural elements in our lives. For example, water is essential for life, and respecting its sanctity can be seen in practices like conservation and sustainable living.
  • preṣṭhām matiṁ: This translates to "the best intellect" or "the highest wisdom." In a practical sense, this can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom in our daily lives, such as through education, critical thinking, and ethical decision-making.
  • vasiṣṭha: Vasiṣṭha is a revered sage in Hindu tradition, symbolizing wisdom and enlightenment. This can be connected to the idea of seeking guidance from wise mentors or teachers in our personal and professional lives.
  • mīḻhuṣe bharasva: This phrase can be interpreted as "may you bestow strength" or "may you grant us power." In real life, this can relate to the empowerment of individuals and communities, encouraging people to support one another and work towards collective strength.
  • ya īm arvāñcaṁ: This can be understood as "whoever performs the sacrifice." Sacrifices in this context can refer to the efforts and dedication one puts into achieving goals, whether in personal endeavors or community service.
  • karate yajatraṁ: This translates to "who performs the ritual." In modern terms, this can be likened to the importance of rituals in our lives, such as celebrating milestones, which help in building community and personal identity.
  • sahasrāmaghaṁ: This means "thousands of blessings" or "abundant rewards." It signifies the idea that hard work and dedication can lead to great rewards, similar to the concept of reaping what you sow in life.
  • vṛṣaṇam bṛhantam: This phrase can be interpreted as "the great strength" or "the powerful one." It emphasizes the importance of resilience and strength in overcoming challenges, akin to how individuals face adversities in life with courage and determination.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking purity, wisdom, strength, and the importance of rituals in life. Its significance lies in the holistic approach it advocates for personal and communal growth. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to strive for mental clarity, respect for nature, the pursuit of knowledge, and the empowerment of oneself and others. By embodying these principles, one can lead a fulfilling and meaningful life, contributing positively to society.
