आ त्वा गीरभिर महाम उरुं हुवे गाम इव भोजसे |इन्द्र सोमस्य पीतये ||
ā tvā gīrbhir mahām uruṁ huve gām iva bhojase | indra somasya pītaye ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā: A preposition meaning "from" or "towards."
    • tvā: Meaning "you," referring to the deity Indra.
    • gīrbhir: Meaning "with hymns" or "with praises."
    • mahām: Meaning "great" or "mighty."
    • uruṁ: Meaning "broad" or "vast."
    • huve: Meaning "I invoke" or "I call upon."
    • gām: Meaning "the earth" or "the world."
    • iva: Meaning "like" or "as."
    • bhojase: Meaning "to enjoy" or "to partake."
    • indra: Referring to the king of gods, associated with strength and power.
    • somasya: Meaning "of Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals.
    • pītaye: Meaning "for drinking" or "to drink."
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra is a call to Indra, the mighty god, to come forth and be praised with hymns.
    • The invocation suggests a desire for Indra's vast power to be present in the world.
    • It emphasizes the importance of Soma, a drink that symbolizes immortality and divine inspiration.
    • The phrase "like the earth" indicates a connection to the material world and the sustenance it provides.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a metaphor for seeking strength and support from greater forces, whether they be spiritual, communal, or natural.
    • For instance, during challenging times, individuals may invoke the support of their community or higher powers to gain strength and resilience.
    • The reference to Soma can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and inspiration, akin to how people seek education and experiences that enrich their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine support and strength in our lives.
    • It highlights the relationship between the divine and the earthly, reminding us that we can draw upon greater powers for sustenance and inspiration.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to seek help and guidance from their communities, traditions, or spiritual beliefs when facing challenges.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the support we can find in the world around us.
