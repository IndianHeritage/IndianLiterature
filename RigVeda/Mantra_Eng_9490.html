पावीरवी तान्यतुर एकपाद अजो दिवो धार्ता सिन्धुर आपः समुद्रियः |विश्वे देवासः श्रिणवन् वचांसि मे सरस्वती सह धिभिः पुरन्ध्या ||
pāvīravī tanyatur ekapād ajo divo dhartā sindhur āpaḥ samudriyaḥ | viśve devāsaḥ śṛṇavan vacāṁsi me sarasvatī saha dhībhiḥ puraṁdhyā ||
  • pāvīravī tanyatur ekapād: This phrase can be translated as "the one who is pure and has a single foot." In a metaphorical sense, it refers to the idea of stability and purity in one's actions and thoughts. For example, a person who practices mindfulness and remains grounded in their values can be seen as having a "single foot" in their approach to life.
  • ajo divo dhartā: This means "the unborn one who supports the heavens." It signifies a divine force that is eternal and unchanging, much like the principles of nature that govern our lives. For instance, the laws of physics are constant and support the universe, similar to how this divine force supports the heavens.
  • sindhur āpaḥ samudriyaḥ: This translates to "the rivers and the ocean." It symbolizes the flow of life and the interconnectedness of all beings. Just as rivers flow into the ocean, our actions and relationships contribute to the larger tapestry of existence. In real life, this can be seen in how communities come together to support one another, much like tributaries feeding into a larger body of water.
  • viśve devāsaḥ: This means "to all the gods." It acknowledges the presence of divine energies in various forms. In our daily lives, we can recognize the different aspects of divinity in nature, art, and human kindness, reminding us to appreciate the sacred in the mundane.
  • śṛṇavan vacāṁsi me: This translates to "listening to my words." It emphasizes the importance of communication and understanding. In real life, effective communication is crucial in relationships, whether personal or professional, as it fosters connection and collaboration.
  • sarasvatī saha dhībhiḥ puraṁdhyā: This means "Saraswati, along with wisdom, grants us knowledge." Saraswati is the goddess of knowledge and learning. In practical terms, this can be seen in the pursuit of education and the importance of wisdom in decision-making. For example, students who seek knowledge and apply it wisely in their lives embody this principle.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of purity, stability, divine support, interconnectedness, communication, and the pursuit of knowledge. Its significance lies in guiding individuals to lead a life that is grounded in values, aware of the divine presence in all aspects of life, and committed to continuous learning. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in personal development, community building, and fostering harmonious relationships, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and meaningful existence.
