अवीवृद्धद् वो अमृताऽमन्दीद् एकद्यूर देवाऽउत याश्च देविः |तस्मा उ राधः कृत नुत प्रशस्तम् प्रातर् मक्षू धियावसुर जगम्यत् ||
avīvṛdhad vo amṛtā amandīd ekadyūr devā uta yāś ca devīḥ | tasmā u rādhaḥ kṛṇuta praśastam prātar makṣū dhiyāvasur jagamyāt ||
  • avīvṛdhad: This term can be interpreted as "having no limitations" or "unbounded." It signifies the idea of infinite potential and possibilities.
  • vo: This means "to you," indicating a direction of the mantra's message towards the audience or the divine.
  • amṛtā: Translated as "immortal" or "eternal," this word emphasizes the concept of everlasting existence, often associated with the divine or spiritual essence.
  • amandīd: This can be understood as "unwavering" or "steady," suggesting a state of stability and consistency in one's pursuits or beliefs.
  • ekadyūr: Meaning "one day" or "one time," it reflects the idea of unity in time, emphasizing the importance of the present moment.
  • devā: This refers to "gods" or "divine beings," highlighting the connection to the spiritual realm and the guidance one can receive from higher powers.
  • uta: This translates to "and" or "also," linking the previous concepts together.
  • yāś: Meaning "glory" or "fame," it signifies the achievements and recognition that come from virtuous actions.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," further connecting the ideas presented.
  • devīḥ: Referring to "goddesses," this term emphasizes the feminine aspect of divinity and the nurturing qualities associated with it.
  • tasmā: This means "therefore," indicating a conclusion or a result derived from the previous statements.
  • u: A particle that can mean "and" or "also," reinforcing the connection between ideas.
  • rādhaḥ: This can be interpreted as "prosperity" or "growth," suggesting the flourishing of life and endeavors.
  • kṛṇuta: Meaning "make" or "create," it emphasizes the active role one must take in manifesting their desires.
  • praśastam: This translates to "excellent" or "praiseworthy," indicating the quality of actions that should be pursued.
  • prātar: Meaning "in the morning," it symbolizes new beginnings and the potential of each new day.
  • makṣū: This can be interpreted as "with intelligence" or "wisdom," highlighting the importance of thoughtful action.
  • dhiyā: Referring to "intellect" or "understanding," it emphasizes the need for clarity and insight in decision-making.
  • avasur: This term can mean "to attain" or "to reach," indicating the goal of one's efforts.
  • jagamyāt: Meaning "to go" or "to move," it signifies the journey one undertakes in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of striving for immortality and excellence through wisdom and divine guidance. It encourages individuals to embrace their infinite potential, act with purpose, and seek prosperity in their endeavors. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, professional growth, and spiritual pursuits. By recognizing the divine support available and committing to virtuous actions, one can navigate life's challenges with clarity and purpose, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and meaningful existence.
