आयम् पुनान उषसो वि रोचयद अयं सिन्धुभ्यो अभवद उ लोककृत् |अयं त्रिः सप्त दुदुहान आशिरं सोमो हृदे पवते चारु मत्सरः ||
ayam punāna uṣaso vi rocayad ayaṁ sindhubhyo abhavad u lokakṛt | ayaṁ triḥ sapta duduhāna āśiraṁ somo hṛde pavate cāru matsaraḥ ||
  • ayam punāna uṣaso vi rocayad: This phrase can be translated to "This pure one, the dawn, shines forth." Here, "punāna" signifies purity, and "uṣaso" refers to the dawn. The dawn symbolizes new beginnings and the dispelling of darkness, both literally and metaphorically.
  • ayaṁ sindhubhyo abhavad u lokakṛt: This translates to "This has emerged from the rivers, the creator of the world." "Sindhubhyo" refers to rivers, which are often seen as sources of life and nourishment. This line emphasizes the importance of water and nature in sustaining life and creating the world around us.
  • ayaṁ triḥ sapta duduhāna: This means "This one, three times seven, gives nourishment." The number "three times seven" can symbolize abundance and the cyclical nature of life. It suggests that nourishment comes in various forms and is plentiful.
  • āśiraṁ somo hṛde pavate cāru matsaraḥ: This translates to "The beautiful one, the moon, flows in the heart, bringing joy." "Somo" refers to the moon, which is often associated with calmness and tranquility. The moon's presence in the heart signifies inner peace and emotional well-being.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates themes of purity, nourishment, and emotional well-being. It highlights the interconnectedness of nature and the human experience, emphasizing how natural elements like the dawn and rivers contribute to our lives.
  • Real-life Application: In daily life, this mantra can inspire individuals to appreciate the beauty of nature and its role in our existence. For example, one might reflect on the importance of clean water sources and the need to protect our environment. Additionally, it encourages mindfulness and emotional health, reminding us to seek inner peace and joy, much like the calming influence of the moon.
