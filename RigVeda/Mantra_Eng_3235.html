एभिः नृभिः इन्द्र त्वायुभिष् त्वा मगवद्भिः मगवन् विश्व आजौ | dyāvo न द्युम्नैर अभि सन्तो आर्यः क्षपो मदेम शरदश् च पूर्वीः ||
ebhir nṛbhir indra tvāyubhiṣ ṭvā maghavadbhir maghavan viśva ājau | dyāvo na dyumnair abhi santo aryaḥ kṣapo madema śaradaś ca pūrvīḥ ||
  • ebhir nṛbhir indra: This phrase can be translated as "O Indra, with these men." Here, Indra is addressed as a powerful deity, often associated with strength and leadership. In real life, this can be likened to a leader who inspires and guides a group of people towards a common goal.
  • tvāyubhiṣ: This means "with your life." It signifies the importance of vitality and energy in achieving success. In everyday life, this can be seen in how individuals invest their time and energy into their passions and careers, leading to fulfillment and achievement.
  • ṭvā maghavadbhir maghavan: This translates to "you, the one who is rich in blessings." It emphasizes the idea of abundance and prosperity. In a practical sense, this can relate to how generosity and sharing resources can lead to a more prosperous community.
  • viśva ājau: This means "all beings are in your command." It reflects the idea of universal control and influence. In real life, this can be compared to how leaders or influential figures can shape the lives of many through their decisions and actions.
  • dyāvo na dyumnair abhi santo: This phrase translates to "the heavens are not filled with glory." It suggests that true glory comes from actions and deeds rather than mere appearances. In modern life, this can be seen in the importance of authenticity and integrity in personal and professional relationships.
  • aryaḥ kṣapo madema: This means "may we be blessed with noble qualities." It highlights the aspiration for virtue and moral excellence. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of personal development and ethical living, striving to be better individuals in society.
  • śaradaś ca pūrvīḥ: This translates to "and the previous seasons." It signifies the cyclical nature of life and the importance of learning from the past. In practical terms, this can be applied to how we reflect on our experiences to make better decisions in the future.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of leadership, vitality, abundance, and moral excellence. It teaches us that true glory comes from our actions and that we should strive for noble qualities while learning from our past. In real life, these principles can guide us in our personal and professional lives, encouraging us to be leaders in our communities, invest our energy wisely, and act with integrity and generosity. By embodying these values, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous society.
