आजिपते नृपते त्वं इद धि नो वाजा आ वक्षि सुक्रतो |वीती होत्राभिर उता देववीतिभिः ससवांसो वि श्रिण्विरे ||
ājipate nṛpate tvam id dhi no vāja ā vakṣi sukrato | vītī hotrābhir uta devavītibhiḥ sasavāṁso vi śṛṇvire ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ājipate: O Lord of the Earth
    • nṛpate: O King of Men
    • tvam: You
    • id: indeed
    • hi: for
    • no: us
    • vāja: strength or power
    • ā: towards
    • vakṣi: speak or declare
    • sukrato: with good thoughts or intentions
    • vītī: with offerings
    • hotrābhir: with the priests
    • uta: and
    • devavītibhiḥ: with divine blessings
    • sasavāṁso: who are endowed with strength
    • vi: indeed
    • śṛṇvire: may they hear
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer addressed to the divine, acknowledging the power and authority of the Lord of the Earth and the King of Men.
    • It emphasizes the importance of strength and good intentions in our actions and communications.
    • The invocation of priests and divine blessings signifies the need for spiritual guidance and support in our endeavors.
    • Real-life example: In a corporate setting, a leader may invoke the support of their team (akin to priests) and seek divine guidance (through meditation or reflection) before making significant decisions.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of leadership, strength, and spirituality.
    • It serves as a reminder that true power comes from good intentions and the support of others.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as community leadership, family decisions, or even personal growth, where one seeks to act with integrity and purpose.
