न त्वा शतं चन हृतो राधो दित्सन्तम आ मिनन |यत पुनानो मखस्यसे ||
na tvā śataṁ cana hruto rādho ditsantam ā minan | yat punāno makhasyase ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • na: not
    • tvā: you
    • śataṁ: a hundred
    • cana: at all
    • hruto: taken away
    • rādho: praise or glory
    • ditsantam: desiring
    • ā: towards
    • minan: to be achieved or attained
    • yat: that which
    • punāno: purifying or making pure
    • makhasyase: of the sacrifice or offering
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra expresses a sentiment of not desiring to take away the glory or praise from anyone.
    • It emphasizes the importance of purity and the essence of sacrifice in achieving true fulfillment.
    • The phrase "that which purifies the sacrifice" suggests that the true value of offerings lies in their purity and intention.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, when a team achieves success, it is crucial to acknowledge everyone's contributions rather than taking credit for oneself. This aligns with the idea of not desiring to take away glory from others.
    • In personal relationships, being supportive and uplifting rather than competitive fosters a healthier environment, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In community service, the act of giving back should be done with pure intentions, focusing on the impact rather than seeking recognition, which resonates with the notion of purity in sacrifice.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra teaches the significance of humility and the importance of recognizing the contributions of others.
    • It highlights that true fulfillment comes from pure intentions and selfless actions, especially in the context of sacrifices made for the greater good.
    • In real life, applying this wisdom can lead to stronger relationships, a more collaborative work environment, and a more compassionate society.
