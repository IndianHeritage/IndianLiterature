अग्नेमरुद्भिः शुभयद्भिर ऋक्वभिः सोमं पिब मन्दसानो गणश्रीभिः |पावकेभिर विश्वमिन्वेभिर आयुभिर वैश्वानर प्रदिवा केतुनā सजूः ||
agne marudbhiḥ śubhayadbhir ṛkvabhiḥ somam piba mandasāno gaṇaśribhiḥ | pāvakebhir viśvaminvebhir āyubhir vaiśvānara pradivā ketunā sajūḥ ||
  • Agne: Refers to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Marudbhiḥ: Refers to the Maruts, the storm deities, representing strength and power.
  • Śubhayadbhir: Means "with auspiciousness," indicating the positive and beneficial aspects of life.
  • Ṛkvabhiḥ: Refers to the hymns or praises, emphasizing the importance of words and expressions in invoking divine blessings.
  • Somam: Refers to Soma, a sacred drink that symbolizes immortality and divine inspiration.
  • Piba: Means "drink," suggesting the act of receiving blessings and nourishment from the divine.
  • Mandasāno: Refers to being seated or settled, indicating a state of calmness and receptivity.
  • Gaṇaśribhiḥ: Refers to the groups of divine beings or energies, highlighting the collective aspect of spirituality.
  • Pāvakebhir: Means "with purifying forces," emphasizing the cleansing aspect of fire and divine energy.
  • Viśvaminvebhir: Refers to the universal energies, indicating the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Āyubhir: Means "with life," suggesting the importance of vitality and longevity.
  • Vaiśvānara: Refers to the universal aspect of fire, symbolizing the all-pervading energy in the universe.
  • Pradivā: Means "in the sky" or "in the heavens," indicating a connection to the divine realm.
  • Ketunā: Refers to the banner or standard, symbolizing guidance and direction.
  • Sajūḥ: Means "to be united" or "to come together," emphasizing the importance of community and harmony.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda invokes the energies of Agni and the Maruts, seeking their blessings for vitality, purity, and auspiciousness. It emphasizes the importance of collective spiritual practices and the nourishment that comes from divine sources. In real life, this mantra can be applied in rituals, prayers, and meditative practices aimed at fostering community, enhancing personal energy, and seeking divine guidance. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of purity and auspiciousness in our lives.
