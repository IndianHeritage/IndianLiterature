द्विमाता होता विदथेṣu सम्राज् अन्व अग्रं चरति क्षेति बुध्नः |प्र रण्यानि रण्यवाचो भरण्ते महद देवानाम असुरत्वम् एकम् ||
dvimātā hotā vidatheṣu samrāḻ anv agraṁ carati kṣeti budhnaḥ | pra raṇyāni raṇyavāco bharante mahad devānām asuratvam ekam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dvimātā: Two mothers
    • hotā: The priest or the one who offers
    • vidatheṣu: In the assemblies or gatherings
    • samrāḻ: The ruler or the one who governs
    • anv: Following or pursuing
    • agraṁ: The foremost or the best
    • carati: Moves or acts
    • kṣeti: Dwells or resides
    • budhnaḥ: Of the wise or the enlightened
    • pra: Towards or in favor of
    • raṇyāni: The beautiful or the auspicious
    • raṇyavāco: Words of beauty or auspicious speech
    • bharante: They carry or bear
    • mahad: Great or significant
    • devānām: Of the gods
    • asuratvam: The quality of being divine or celestial
    • ekam: One or unified
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the duality of motherhood, symbolizing the nurturing aspects of life.
    • The term "hotā" refers to the priest, indicating the importance of rituals and offerings in maintaining harmony.
    • "Vidatheṣu" suggests the significance of community gatherings where decisions are made and wisdom is shared.
    • The phrase "samrāḻ anv agraṁ" emphasizes the pursuit of excellence and leadership in society.
    • It highlights the role of the wise ("budhnaḥ") in guiding actions and decisions.
    • The mantra also speaks to the power of beautiful words and auspicious speech ("raṇyāni raṇyavāco") in fostering positive relationships and community spirit.
    • Finally, it underscores the unity of divine qualities ("asuratvam ekam") that bind humanity and the divine together.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, the concept of "dvimātā" can be seen in the dual roles of mothers, both biological and societal, nurturing future generations.
    • Community gatherings, such as town hall meetings, reflect the "vidatheṣu" aspect where collective wisdom is sought for governance.
    • Leadership in organizations often requires the qualities of "samrāḻ" to inspire and guide teams towards common goals.
    • The importance of wise counsel in decision-making is evident in corporate boardrooms where experienced leaders ("budhnaḥ") provide guidance.
    • Positive communication and the use of uplifting language in personal and professional relationships embody "raṇyāni raṇyavāco," fostering collaboration and understanding.
    • The unity of diverse perspectives and qualities in a team or community reflects the essence of "asuratvam ekam," promoting harmony and collective strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of nurturing, community, leadership, wisdom, and positive communication in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that we are all interconnected, and our actions and words can have profound effects on those around us.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more harmonious relationships, effective leadership, and a stronger sense of community.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to recognize the divine qualities within ourselves and others, fostering unity and collaboration in our shared journey.
