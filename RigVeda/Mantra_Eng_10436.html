यस् ते गर्भं अमीवाऽ दुर्णामा योनिम आशये |अग्निषṭम् ब्रह्मणा सह निष्क्रव्यादम् अनीनशत् ||
yas te garbham amīvā durṇāmā yonim āśaye | agniṣ ṭam brahmaṇā saha niṣ kravyādam anīnaśat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • garbham: womb or embryo
    • amīvā: like a mother
    • durṇāmā: difficult to comprehend or understand
    • yonim: source or origin
    • āśaye: in the intention or desire
    • agniṣ: with fire
    • ṭam: that
    • brahmaṇā: with the divine or the ultimate reality
    • saha: together
    • niṣ: out of
    • kravyādam: the one who is to be sacrificed
    • anīnaśat: not to be harmed or injured
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the divine connection between the womb (garbham) and the ultimate reality (brahmaṇā).
    • It emphasizes the nurturing aspect of creation, where the womb is compared to a mother, highlighting the importance of protection and care.
    • The term "durṇāmā" suggests that the process of creation and understanding the divine is complex and often beyond human comprehension.
    • The invocation of fire (agniṣ) symbolizes transformation, purification, and the energy required for creation.
    • The mantra calls for the protection of the unborn (kravyādam) and emphasizes the sanctity of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, this mantra can be related to the importance of prenatal care, where the health of the mother and the unborn child is prioritized.
    • It can also be seen in the context of environmental protection, where nurturing the earth (the womb of life) is essential for future generations.
    • The complexity of understanding life and creation can be reflected in scientific endeavors, such as genetics and embryology, where researchers strive to comprehend the intricacies of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the sacredness of life and the divine connection between the creator and creation.
    • It serves as a reminder of the responsibilities we hold towards nurturing and protecting life, both in human terms and in our relationship with nature.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages us to seek understanding and wisdom in the complexities of existence, fostering a sense of reverence for the mysteries of life.
