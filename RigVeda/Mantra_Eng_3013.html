प्र स मित्र मार्तो अस्तु प्रयस्वान यस् त आदित्य शिक्षति व्रतेन |न हन्यते न जीयते त्वतो नैनम अंहो अश्नोत्य अन्तितो न दूरात् ||
pra sa mitra marto astu prayasvān yas ta āditya śikṣati vratena | na hanyate na jīyate tvoto nainam aṁho aśnoty antito na dūrāt ||
  • Translation: "May the one who is friendly and benevolent, who is the source of strength, be with us. He who is taught by the sun through his vows, is not harmed, does not die; he is not touched by the evil, nor does he suffer from afar."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: May (a prefix indicating a wish or prayer)
    • sa: he (referring to a divine entity)
    • mitra: friend (symbolizing benevolence and support)
    • marto: mortal (referring to human beings)
    • astu: be (a wish for existence or presence)
    • prayasvān: strong or powerful (indicating strength)
    • yas: who (referring to the divine)
    • ta: that (indicating the sun)
    • āditya: sun (symbolizing light, knowledge, and life)
    • śikṣati: teaches (imparting knowledge or wisdom)
    • vratena: through vows (indicating commitment and discipline)
    • na: not (negation)
    • hanyate: harmed (indicating injury or damage)
    • jīyate: dies (referring to death)
    • tvoto: from you (indicating a source)
    • nainam: him (referring to the individual)
    • aṁho: evil (indicating negativity or harm)
    • aśnoty: suffers (indicating pain or distress)
    • antito: from afar (indicating distance)
    • na: not (negation)
    • dūrāt: from far away (indicating separation)
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek the support of friends and family, embodying the essence of 'mitra' (friend) in their lives.
    • People who practice mindfulness and meditation can be seen as embodying the strength of 'prayasvān', as they cultivate inner peace and resilience.
    • Commitment to ethical living and personal vows, such as those made in various spiritual traditions, reflect the idea of 'vratena', guiding individuals towards a life free from harm.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of friendship, strength, and the protective power of knowledge and commitment. In real life, it serves as a reminder that through our connections with others and our dedication to personal growth, we can navigate challenges without succumbing to negativity or harm. It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and support from both the divine and their community, fostering resilience and well-being in the face of adversity.
