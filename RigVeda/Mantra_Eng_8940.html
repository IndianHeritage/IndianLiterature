इमं अग्ने चमसं मा वि जिह्वरः प्रियो देवानाम् उता सोम्यानाम् |एष यश चमसो देवपानस तस्मिन् देवाः अमृताः मादयन्ते ||
imam agne camasam mā vi jihvaraḥ priyo devānām uta somyānām | eṣa yaś camaso devapānas tasmin devā amṛtā mādayante ||
  • imam agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni," referring to the fire god in Vedic tradition. Agni is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine, symbolizing purity and transformation.
  • camasam mā vi jihvaraḥ: This means "may you not be angry with this offering." It reflects the importance of approaching the divine with respect and humility, acknowledging that offerings should be made with a pure heart.
  • priyo devānām uta somyānām: This translates to "beloved of the gods and the soothing ones." It emphasizes the idea that the offerings made to Agni are cherished by the deities, and that they bring peace and harmony.
  • eṣa yaś camaso devapānas: This means "this is the fame of the divine drink." It refers to the offerings made to the gods, which are often likened to a divine drink that nourishes and sustains them.
  • tasmin devā amṛtā mādayante: This translates to "in that, the gods, immortal, rejoice." It signifies that when the offerings are made with sincerity, the gods are pleased and bestow blessings upon the devotees.

  • Real-Life Example: In many cultures, rituals and offerings are made to express gratitude and seek blessings. For instance, during Diwali, the festival of lights in India, people light lamps and offer sweets to deities, symbolizing the same respect and devotion as expressed in this mantra.
  • Significance: This mantra highlights the importance of intention behind offerings. It teaches that sincerity and respect in our actions can lead to divine blessings and harmony in our lives.
  • Application in Real Life: In everyday life, this mantra can inspire individuals to approach their goals and relationships with respect and humility. Whether in personal endeavors or professional settings, the essence of making sincere efforts and valuing connections can lead to positive outcomes.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of offerings and the attitude with which they are made. It encourages individuals to cultivate a spirit of gratitude and respect, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and with others in their lives.
