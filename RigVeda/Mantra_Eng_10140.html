समौ चिद्धस्तौ न समं विविष्टः सम्मातराऽचिन न समं दूहाते |यमयोश्चिन न समा वीर्याणि ज्ञाति चित्सन्तौ न समं पृणीतः ||
samau cid dhastau na samaṁ viviṣṭaḥ sammātarā cin na samaṁ duhāte | yamayoś cin na samā vīryāṇi jñātī cit santau na samam pṛṇītaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samau: equal, alike
    • cid: indeed, certainly
    • dhastau: in the actions, in the deeds
    • na: not
    • samaṁ: equal, same
    • viviṣṭaḥ: distinguished, separated
    • sammātarā: more worthy, more deserving
    • cin: indeed, certainly
    • na: not
    • samaṁ: equal, same
    • duhāte: to produce, to yield
    • yamayoś: of the two, of the pairs
    • cin: indeed, certainly
    • na: not
    • samā: equal, same
    • vīryāṇi: powers, strengths
    • jñātī: knowledge, understanding
    • cit: indeed, certainly
    • santau: the wise, the learned
    • na: not
    • samam: equal, same
    • pṛṇītaḥ: to fill, to satisfy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the concept of equality and distinction in actions and virtues.
    • It emphasizes that not all actions or deeds are equal, and that some are more worthy than others.
    • The mantra suggests that true strength and knowledge do not come from mere equality but from understanding the differences in virtues and actions.
    • For example, in a workplace, two employees may have the same title, but their contributions and the impact of their work can be vastly different. Recognizing and valuing these differences can lead to a more productive environment.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In education, teachers often encounter students with varying abilities. Recognizing that not all students learn at the same pace allows for tailored teaching methods that can help each student excel.
    • In relationships, understanding that each person brings unique strengths and weaknesses can foster deeper connections and mutual respect.
    • In community service, acknowledging that different contributions can have varying impacts encourages diverse participation and collaboration.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for discernment in recognizing the value of actions and virtues.
    • It teaches us that while equality is important, it is equally crucial to appreciate the unique contributions of individuals.
    • In real life, applying this understanding can lead to more harmonious relationships, effective teamwork, and a deeper appreciation for diversity in strengths and abilities.
