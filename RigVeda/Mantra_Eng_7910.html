एष गव्युर अचिक्रदत पवमानो हिरण्ययुः |इन्दुः सत्राजिद अस्तृतः ||
eṣa gavyur acikradat pavamāno hiraṇyayuḥ | induḥ satrājid astṛtaḥ ||
  • eṣa - This word translates to "this" or "he." It indicates a specific subject or entity being referred to.
  • gavyur - This term means "the one who is related to cows" or "the cow." In Vedic culture, cows are considered sacred and symbolize wealth, nourishment, and abundance.
  • acikradat - This word means "has cried out" or "has called." It suggests a vocal expression or a call to attention.
  • pavamāno - This translates to "the purifying one" or "the one who purifies." It refers to the wind or air, which is often associated with purification in Vedic texts.
  • hiraṇyayuḥ - This term means "golden life" or "one who has a golden lifespan." It symbolizes prosperity and vitality.
  • induḥ - This word means "moon" or "nectar." In Vedic literature, the moon is often associated with nourishment and the mind.
  • satrājid - This translates to "the conqueror of the sacrifice." It refers to the one who is victorious in rituals and offerings.
  • astṛtaḥ - This word means "is established" or "is firm." It indicates stability and permanence.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine forces that embody purity, prosperity, and nourishment. It highlights the importance of the cow as a symbol of wealth and the wind as a purifying force. The mention of the moon signifies the nurturing aspect of life, while the idea of conquering sacrifices emphasizes the significance of rituals in achieving success and stability.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or rituals to invoke purity and prosperity in their lives.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to the cow can inspire people to respect and protect animals, promoting a sustainable and compassionate lifestyle.
  • Personal Growth: The idea of conquering sacrifices can motivate individuals to overcome challenges and strive for success in their personal and professional lives.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of purity, prosperity, and the importance of rituals in achieving stability in life. By understanding and applying its teachings, individuals can enhance their spiritual practices, promote environmental consciousness, and foster personal growth.
