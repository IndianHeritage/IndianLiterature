प्रति द्युतानाम् अरुषासो अश्वाश् चित्रा अदृश्रन्न उषसं वहन्तः |याति शुभ्रा विश्वपिशा रथेन दधाति रत्नं विधते जनाय ||
prati dyutānām aruṣāso aśvāś citrā adṛśrann uṣasaṁ vahantaḥ | yāti śubhrā viśvapiśā rathena dadhāti ratnaṁ vidhate janāya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prati: towards
    • dyutānām: of the shining ones
    • aruṣāso: of the red dawns
    • aśvāś: of the horses
    • citrā: colorful or varied
    • adṛśrann: unseen or hidden
    • uṣasaṁ: the dawn
    • vahantaḥ: carrying or bringing
    • yāti: goes or moves
    • śubhrā: bright or pure
    • viśvapiśā: of the all-encompassing
    • rathena: with the chariot
    • dadhāti: brings or bestows
    • ratnaṁ: jewel or treasure
    • vidhate: creates or establishes
    • janāya: for the people or for humanity
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the dawn as a powerful and vibrant force, represented by the "shining ones" and "red dawns." It highlights the beauty and energy of the morning, often associated with new beginnings.
    • The mention of "horses" and "chariots" symbolizes speed, strength, and the journey of life. Just as horses pull chariots, our actions and efforts drive us forward in life.
    • The "unseen" aspects of dawn suggest that there are hidden opportunities and potential that come with each new day, waiting to be discovered.
    • The mantra emphasizes the idea of bringing forth treasures ("ratnaṁ") for humanity, indicating that the dawn not only marks the start of a new day but also brings with it the potential for growth, prosperity, and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Every morning, as the sun rises, people often feel a renewed sense of hope and motivation. This can be seen in the way individuals start their day with exercise, meditation, or planning, symbolizing the "new beginnings" that the dawn represents.
    • In many cultures, dawn is celebrated through rituals and practices that honor the light and the opportunities it brings. For instance, yoga practitioners often perform sun salutations at dawn to connect with the energy of the new day.
    • The metaphor of the chariot can be related to personal goals and ambitions. Just as a chariot moves forward, individuals must harness their strengths and resources to achieve their aspirations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the beauty and potential that each new day holds. It encourages individuals to embrace the dawn, recognizing it as a time for reflection, renewal, and the pursuit of goals.
    • In practical terms, it can inspire people to start their day with intention, seek out opportunities, and contribute positively to the world around them.
    • Ultimately, the significance of this mantra lies in its ability to motivate and uplift, reminding us that with each dawn comes the chance to create something valuable for ourselves and for humanity.
