सास्मा अरम् बाहुभ्यां यं पिताकृत्नोद विश्वस्माद आ जनुषो वेदसस परी |येना पृथिव्यां निक्रिविं शयध्यै वज्रेण हत्व्य अवृणक तुविष्वणिः ||
sāsmā aram bāhubhyāṁ yam pitākṛṇod viśvasmād ā januṣo vedasas pari | yenā pṛthivyāṁ ni kriviṁ śayadhyai vajreṇa hatvy avṛṇak tuviṣvaṇiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sāsmā: From this
    • aram: (to) the plough
    • bāhubhyāṁ: (with) the arms
    • yam: which
    • pitākṛṇod: the father has made
    • viśvasmād: from all
    • ā: (to) the
    • januṣo: of the beings
    • vedasas: of the Vedas
    • pari: all around
    • yenā: by which
    • pṛthivyāṁ: on the earth
    • ni: (to) the
    • kriviṁ: (to) the creation
    • śayadhyai: (to) the one who lies down
    • vajreṇa: with the thunderbolt
    • hatvy: (to) the one who is struck
    • avṛṇak: (to) the one who is not covered
    • tuviṣvaṇiḥ: (to) the one who is all-pervading
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of creation and the role of the father figure in shaping the world.
    • The reference to "plough" symbolizes hard work and cultivation, which can be related to agricultural practices in real life.
    • The mention of "arms" signifies strength and effort, highlighting the importance of human endeavor in achieving success.
    • The "Vedas" represent knowledge and wisdom, suggesting that understanding and learning are crucial for progress.
    • The earth is depicted as a nurturing ground for all beings, emphasizing the interconnectedness of life.
    • The "thunderbolt" symbolizes power and the ability to overcome obstacles, akin to facing challenges in life.
    • The phrase "all-pervading" indicates the omnipresence of divine energy in creation, reminding us of the spiritual aspect of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the act of ploughing represents the hard work farmers put into cultivating their land, which is essential for food production.
    • In personal development, the mantra can inspire individuals to harness their inner strength and knowledge to overcome life's challenges.
    • In community building, the interconnectedness of beings can be seen in collaborative efforts to support one another, reflecting the essence of unity.
    • In education, the pursuit of knowledge through the "Vedas" can be likened to modern learning systems that empower individuals to grow and contribute to society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the significance of hard work, knowledge, and the divine presence in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that through effort and wisdom, we can create a better world for ourselves and others.
    • In real-life scenarios, the teachings of this mantra can be applied in various fields such as agriculture, education, and community service, promoting a holistic approach to life.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to recognize the divine energy that permeates all existence and to act with purpose and strength in our endeavors.
