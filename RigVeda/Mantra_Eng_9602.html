तदु श्रेष्ठं सवनाṁ सुनोतनात्यो न हस्तयतो अद्रिः सोतरी |विदध ध्य आ1र्यो अभिभूति पौंश्यं महो राये चित तरुते यद अरवतः ||
tad u śreṣṭhaṁ savanaṁ sunotanātyo na hastayato adriḥ sotari | vidad dhy a1ryo abhibhūti pauṁsyam maho rāye cit tarute yad arvataḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • u: indeed
    • śreṣṭhaṁ: the best or the highest
    • savanaṁ: the offering or the sacrifice
    • sunotanātyo: who is well-pleased or who is satisfied
    • na: not
    • hastayato: by the hands
    • adriḥ: the mountain
    • sotari: the one who is moving or flowing
    • vidad: may he give
    • dhy: to the one who is wise
    • aryo: noble or honorable
    • abhibhūti: prosperity or abundance
    • pauṁsyam: of manhood or strength
    • maho: great
    • rāye: wealth or riches
    • cit: consciousness or awareness
    • tarute: may he carry or bring
    • yad: that which
    • arvataḥ: of the one who is strong or powerful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the highest offering that is pleasing to the divine.
    • It emphasizes the importance of noble actions and the pursuit of prosperity through wisdom.
    • The reference to mountains symbolizes stability and strength, suggesting that true wealth comes from a solid foundation.
    • The idea of consciousness and awareness indicates that one should be mindful of their actions and their impact on the world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied to ethical business practices where the highest offering is integrity and transparency.
    • In personal relationships, it highlights the importance of noble intentions and actions that foster trust and prosperity.
    • In community service, it encourages individuals to contribute positively, ensuring that their actions lead to the well-being of others.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for individuals to strive for the highest ideals in their actions.
    • It serves as a reminder that true prosperity is achieved through wisdom, noble actions, and a strong foundation.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, stronger relationships, and a more harmonious society.
