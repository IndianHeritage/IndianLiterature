यम एरिरे भृगवो विश्ववेदसं नाभा पृथिव्या bhuvanasya majmanā | अग्निं तं गीरभिर हिनुहि स्व आ दमे य एको वस्वो वरुणो न राजति ||
yam erire bhṛgavo viśvavedasaṁ nābhā pṛthivyā bhuvanasya majmanā | agniṁ taṁ gīrbhir hinuhi sva ā dame ya eko vasvo varuṇo na rājati ||
  • yam erire bhṛgavo viśvavedasaṁ: This phrase refers to the one who is revered by the wise and knowledgeable. The term "bhṛgava" can be associated with the sage Bhrigu, who is known for his wisdom and understanding of the universe. In real life, this can be likened to individuals who are respected for their knowledge, such as teachers, scientists, or philosophers.
  • nābhā pṛthivyā bhuvanasya majmanā: This line speaks of the connection between the heavens (nābhā) and the earth (pṛthivyā). It emphasizes the unity of the universe and the importance of balance between the spiritual and material worlds. In practical terms, this can be seen in environmental conservation efforts, where the balance of nature is crucial for the well-being of all living beings.
  • agniṁ taṁ gīrbhir hinuhi: Here, "agni" refers to fire, which symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. The phrase suggests invoking this energy through hymns (gīrbhir). In everyday life, this can be related to the importance of passion and motivation in achieving goals, as well as the need for purification in our thoughts and actions.
  • sva ā dame: This part translates to "in your own abode" or "in your own home." It signifies the importance of personal space and the need to cultivate a harmonious environment. In real life, this can be reflected in creating a peaceful home atmosphere that fosters well-being and growth.
  • ya eko vasvo varuṇo na rājati: This line refers to the singular essence of the divine (varuṇo) that governs the universe. It suggests that true authority comes from understanding and aligning with this universal truth rather than seeking power for its own sake. In practical terms, this can be seen in leadership styles that prioritize service and integrity over dominance.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound wisdom about the interconnectedness of knowledge, nature, personal space, and divine authority. Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to seek wisdom, maintain balance in their lives, and cultivate a sense of harmony within themselves and their surroundings. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as education, environmental stewardship, personal development, and ethical leadership, guiding us towards a more enlightened and harmonious existence.
