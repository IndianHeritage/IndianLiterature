अतिथिम् मानवशाणां सूनुं वनस्पतिनाम् |विप्राः अग्निम् अवसे प्रात्नम् ईळते ||
atithim mānuṣāṇāṁ sūnuṁ vanaspatīnām | viprā agnim avase pratnam īḻate ||
  • atithim: This word translates to "guest" or "visitor." In many cultures, including Indian culture, guests are treated with great respect and honor. The concept of hospitality is deeply ingrained in societal values.
  • mānuṣāṇāṁ: This means "of humans" or "among humans." It emphasizes the human aspect of the mantra, focusing on interpersonal relationships and social conduct.
  • sūnuṁ: This translates to "son" or "offspring." It signifies the importance of family and lineage, highlighting the role of the younger generation in carrying forward traditions and values.
  • vanaspatīnām: This means "of the plants" or "of the trees." It reflects the connection between humans and nature, emphasizing the importance of the environment and the natural world in our lives.
  • viprā: This word refers to "the wise" or "the learned." It signifies the role of knowledge and wisdom in guiding human actions and decisions.
  • agnim: This translates to "fire." In Vedic literature, fire is often seen as a purifying element and a symbol of energy and transformation.
  • avase: This means "to invoke" or "to call upon." It indicates the act of seeking blessings or guidance from higher powers.
  • pratnam: This translates to "offering" or "sacrifice." It signifies the importance of giving and sharing in human relationships.
  • īḻate: This means "to shine" or "to illuminate." It reflects the idea of enlightenment and the pursuit of knowledge.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to honor guests, recognize the importance of family and nature, and seek wisdom and guidance through offerings. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of humans, nature, and the divine.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Hospitality: In many cultures, including Indian, treating guests with respect and kindness is paramount. This mantra serves as a reminder to uphold these values in our daily lives.
  • Family Values: The mention of "son" highlights the importance of nurturing the younger generation and instilling in them the values of respect, kindness, and responsibility.
  • Environmental Awareness: By acknowledging the connection to nature, this mantra encourages us to respect and protect our environment, promoting sustainability and ecological balance.
  • Wisdom and Knowledge: The role of the wise in guiding actions reminds us to seek knowledge and learn from those who are experienced, fostering a culture of continuous learning.
  • Acts of Giving: The emphasis on offerings encourages generosity and sharing, which can strengthen community bonds and promote social harmony.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates essential values of hospitality, family, respect for nature, the pursuit of wisdom, and the importance of giving. Its significance lies in its timeless relevance, guiding individuals and communities in fostering harmonious relationships and a balanced existence in the world.
