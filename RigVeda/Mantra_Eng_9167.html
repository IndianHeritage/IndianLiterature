विष्वस्मान् नो अदितिः पातु अंहसो माता मित्रस्य वरुणस्य रेवतः |स्वर्वज ज्योतिर अवृकं नशीमहि तद देवानाम् अवो अद्या वृणीमहे ||
viśvasmān no aditiḥ pātv aṁhaso mātā mitrasya varuṇasya revataḥ | svarvaj jyotir avṛkaṁ naśīmahi tad devānām avo adyā vṛṇīmahe ||
  • viśvasmān: This term refers to the universal or all-encompassing aspect of existence. It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the universe.
  • no: This is a pronoun meaning "our," indicating a personal connection to the divine or universal forces.
  • aditiḥ: Aditi is often seen as the mother of the gods, representing infinity and the boundless nature of the universe. She embodies nurturing and protection.
  • pātv: This verb means "may she protect" or "may she nourish," suggesting a plea for guidance and support from the divine.
  • aṁhaso: This term refers to "strength" or "vigor," indicating the importance of vitality and energy in life.
  • mātā: Meaning "mother," it emphasizes the nurturing aspect of the divine, akin to how a mother cares for her children.
  • mitrasya: This word means "of friendship" or "of the sun," highlighting the importance of relationships and the warmth of companionship.
  • varuṇasya: Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water, symbolizing the balance and harmony in nature.
  • revataḥ: This term can be associated with prosperity and abundance, indicating a desire for wealth and well-being.
  • svarvaj: Meaning "all light" or "universal light," it signifies enlightenment and knowledge.
  • jyotir: This translates to "light," representing clarity, wisdom, and the dispelling of ignorance.
  • avṛkaṁ: This term means "without wolves," symbolizing safety and protection from harm.
  • naśīmahi: This is a plea for realization or attainment, indicating a desire to achieve something significant.
  • tad: Meaning "that," it refers to the divine or the higher state of being.
  • devānām: This means "of the gods," indicating a connection to the divine and the spiritual realm.
  • avo: This is a call for assistance or support, asking for divine intervention.
  • adyā: Meaning "today," it emphasizes the immediacy of the request or intention.
  • vṛṇīmahe: This term means "we choose" or "we invoke," indicating an active participation in the process of seeking divine blessings.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a profound plea for protection, guidance, and enlightenment from the divine forces represented by Aditi, Mitra, and Varuna. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing relationships, both with the divine and among ourselves. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during times of uncertainty or when seeking clarity in decision-making. It serves as a reminder to seek support from the universe and to foster harmonious relationships, ultimately leading to a more balanced and fulfilling life.
