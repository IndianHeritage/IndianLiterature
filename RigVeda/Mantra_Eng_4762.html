पातासुतं इन्द्रो अस्तु सोमं हन्ता वृतं वज्रेण मन्दसानः |गन्ता यज्ञं परावतश्चिदच्छा वसुर धीनाम् अविताकारुधायः ||
pātā sutam indro astu somaṁ hantā vṛtraṁ vajreṇa mandasānaḥ | gantā yajñam parāvataś cid acchā vasur dhīnām avitā kārudhāyāḥ ||
  • pātā sutam indro astu somaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "May Indra, the king of gods, be pleased with the offerings." Here, "Indra" represents strength and power, while "soma" refers to a sacred drink that symbolizes divine inspiration and vitality.
  • hantā vṛtraṁ vajreṇa mandasānaḥ: This part means "May he, who wields the thunderbolt, slay Vritra." Vritra is a serpent or dragon that represents chaos and obstacles. Indra's victory over Vritra symbolizes the triumph of order over chaos, which is a recurring theme in many cultures.
  • gantā yajñam parāvataś: This translates to "He who goes to the sacrifice, the supreme one." This emphasizes the importance of rituals and sacrifices in connecting with the divine and seeking blessings.
  • cid acchā vasur dhīnām avitā kārudhāyāḥ: This can be interpreted as "May the divine essence protect the wealth of the wise." It highlights the significance of wisdom and the protection of one's resources, suggesting that true wealth comes from knowledge and understanding.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for strength, wisdom, and the ability to overcome obstacles. It reflects the human desire for protection and prosperity through spiritual means.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during important decisions, rituals, or challenges. For instance, individuals may recite this mantra before starting a new venture or during significant life events to invoke strength and guidance.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine support in our endeavors. It encourages individuals to confront challenges with courage and wisdom, reinforcing the belief that spiritual practices can lead to personal and communal prosperity.
