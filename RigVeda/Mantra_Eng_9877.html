सां वसु दधती श्वशुराय वय उषो यदि वष्ट्य अन्तिगृहात् |अस्तं ननक्षे यस्मिञ्चाकन दिवा नक्तं श्नथिता वैतसेन ||
sā vasu dadhatī śvaśurāya vaya uṣo yadi vaṣṭy antigṛhāt | astaṁ nanakṣe yasmiñ cākan divā naktaṁ śnathitā vaitasena ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sā: she, that
    • vasu: wealth, good things
    • dadhatī: holding, possessing
    • śvaśurāya: to the father-in-law
    • vaya: our
    • uṣo: dawn, light
    • yadi: if
    • vaṣṭy: she is pleased
    • antigṛhāt: from the house, abode
    • astaṁ: setting, going down
    • nanakṣe: in the constellation
    • yasmiñ: in which
    • cākan: the sun
    • divā: by day
    • naktaṁ: by night
    • śnathitā: having been made to shine
    • vaitasena: with great power
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the dawn (uṣo) as a powerful entity that brings wealth and prosperity (vasu) to those who honor it.
    • The reference to the father-in-law (śvaśurāya) suggests a connection to familial bonds and the importance of relationships in the pursuit of prosperity.
    • The phrase "if she is pleased" indicates that the blessings of the dawn and the wealth it brings are contingent upon respect and reverence.
    • The mention of the sun (cākan) and its cycles (day and night) emphasizes the natural order and the importance of time in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the dawn is celebrated as a time of new beginnings. For instance, people often perform rituals at sunrise to invoke blessings for the day ahead.
    • In business, the idea of nurturing relationships (like those with family or mentors) can lead to greater success, mirroring the familial respect mentioned in the mantra.
    • Practices such as yoga or meditation at dawn are common, as they align with the idea of harnessing the energy of the new day for personal growth and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of dawn as a symbol of hope, renewal, and the potential for wealth and prosperity.
    • It teaches us the importance of respect for relationships and the natural cycles of life, suggesting that our actions and attitudes can influence the blessings we receive.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to start their day with intention, cultivate meaningful relationships, and appreciate the wealth of opportunities that each new day brings.
