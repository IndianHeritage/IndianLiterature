आ त्वा ब्रह्मयुजा हरī वहताम इन्द्र केशिना |उप ब्रह्माणि नः श्रिणु ||
ā tvā brahmayujā harī vahatām indra keśinā | upa brahmāṇi naḥ śṛṇu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: From
    • tvā: You
    • brahmayujā: With the power of Brahman (the ultimate reality)
    • harī: O Lord (often referring to Vishnu or a divine being)
    • vahatām: Carrying or leading
    • indra: Indra (the king of gods, representing strength and power)
    • keśinā: With hair (often symbolizing strength or vitality)
    • upa: Near or towards
    • brahmāṇi: The ultimate reality or Brahman
    • naḥ: Our
    • śṛṇu: Listen or hear
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence, asking for guidance and support from a higher power.
    • The phrase "ā tvā brahmayujā" suggests a connection to the ultimate reality, indicating that the speaker seeks to align with this higher state of being.
    • By addressing "harī," the mantra acknowledges the divine qualities of protection and sustenance.
    • The reference to "indra" symbolizes strength and leadership, suggesting that the speaker desires to embody these qualities in their life.
    • The call to "śṛṇu" emphasizes the importance of listening and being receptive to divine wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In moments of decision-making, individuals can recite this mantra to seek clarity and guidance from a higher power, much like how leaders consult their advisors before making important choices.
    • During challenging times, people may find strength in this mantra, similar to how athletes draw on their mental fortitude to overcome obstacles in competitions.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be used to foster unity and collective strength, akin to how teams come together to support one another in achieving common goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness between the individual and the divine, encouraging individuals to seek guidance and strength from a higher source.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to inspire confidence and resilience, making it applicable in various life scenarios, from personal challenges to communal efforts.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of purpose and direction, aligning their actions with the greater good and the ultimate reality.
