अधā ते अप्रतिष्कुतं देवी शुष्मं सपर्यतः | उभे सुशिप्र रोधसी ||
adhā te apratiṣkutaṁ devī śuṣmaṁ saparyataḥ | ubhe suśipra rodasī ||
  • adhā te: This phrase translates to "below you" or "under you." It signifies a foundation or a base, suggesting that there is something that supports or is subordinate to the divine presence being addressed.
  • apratiṣkutaṁ: This word means "unobstructed" or "unhindered." It implies a state of purity or clarity, free from any obstacles or impurities that might hinder spiritual or divine connection.
  • devī: This term means "goddess" or "divine feminine." It represents the nurturing, creative, and protective aspects of divinity, often associated with various forms of goddesses in Hinduism.
  • śuṣmaṁ: This word translates to "subtle" or "refined." It indicates a level of existence that is beyond the physical, pointing towards the spiritual or ethereal realms.
  • saparyataḥ: This means "worshipped" or "adored." It signifies the act of reverence and devotion towards the divine, acknowledging the importance of worship in connecting with the higher powers.
  • ubhe: This translates to "both" or "two." It suggests duality, indicating that there are two aspects or realms being referred to in the mantra.
  • suśipra: This term means "well-formed" or "beautiful." It denotes something that is not only aesthetically pleasing but also harmonious and balanced.
  • rodasī: This word refers to "the two worlds" or "the two realms." It signifies the connection between the earthly and the divine, the physical and the spiritual.

Conclusion: The mantra "adhā te apratiṣkutaṁ devī śuṣmaṁ saparyataḥ | ubhe suśipra rodasī ||" emphasizes the importance of recognizing the divine presence that supports and nurtures us. It highlights the unobstructed nature of the divine, encouraging us to engage in worship and reverence. In real life, this can be applied through practices such as meditation, prayer, and acts of kindness, which help us connect with the subtle and refined aspects of existence. By acknowledging both the earthly and spiritual realms, we can cultivate a balanced life that honors both our material needs and our spiritual aspirations. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all things and the importance of maintaining a harmonious relationship with the divine and the world around us.
