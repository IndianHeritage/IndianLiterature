यस्त्वा दोषा य उषसि प्रशंसात प्रियं वा त्वा कृणवते हविष्मान् |अश्वो न स्वे दमा आ हेव्यावान् तं अम्हसः पीपरो दाश्वांसम् ||
yas tvā doṣā ya uṣasi praśaṁsāt priyaṁ vā tvā kṛṇavate haviṣmān | aśvo na sve dama ā hemyāvān tam aṁhasaḥ pīparo dāśvāṁsam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • tvā: you
    • doṣā: faults
    • ya: who
    • uṣasi: in the dawn
    • praśaṁsāt: praised
    • priyaṁ: dear
    • vā: or
    • tvā: you
    • kṛṇavate: who brings
    • haviṣmān: offerings
    • aśvo: horse
    • na: not
    • sve: in his own
    • dama: abode
    • ā: to
    • hemyāvān: golden
    • tam: that
    • aṁhasaḥ: of the light
    • pīparo: of the strong
    • dāśvāṁsam: of the swift
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine qualities of the one who is praised, highlighting the importance of recognizing both their virtues and faults.
    • The mention of "dawn" symbolizes new beginnings and the potential for growth and transformation.
    • The reference to offerings suggests the importance of gratitude and acknowledgment in our relationships with the divine and with others.
    • The imagery of the horse represents strength, speed, and the ability to overcome obstacles, which can be applied to personal challenges in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, acknowledging both the strengths and weaknesses of a colleague can foster a more collaborative environment.
    • Starting a new project at dawn can symbolize a fresh start, encouraging individuals to approach challenges with optimism.
    • Practicing gratitude, such as thanking a mentor for their guidance, can strengthen relationships and promote a positive atmosphere.
    • Embracing the qualities of resilience and determination, akin to the horse in the mantra, can help individuals navigate personal and professional hurdles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing both the positive and negative aspects of ourselves and others, promoting a balanced perspective.
    • It encourages us to approach life with gratitude and to embrace new beginnings, reminding us of the potential for growth and transformation.
    • In real-life scenarios, the teachings of this mantra can be applied to enhance relationships, foster collaboration, and cultivate resilience in the face of challenges.
