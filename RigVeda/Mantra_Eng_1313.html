अजोहवीद अश्विनाऽ वर्तिका वाम आस्नो यत सीम अमुञ्चतं वृक्षस्य |विजयुषा ययथुः सान्व अद्रेर जातं विश्वाचो अहतं विषेण ||
ajohavīd aśvinā vartikā vām āsno yat sīm amuñcataṁ vṛkasya | vi jayuṣā yayathuḥ sānv adrer jātaṁ viṣvāco ahataṁ viṣeṇa ||
  • ajohavīd: This term can be interpreted as "unconquerable" or "invincible." It signifies a state of being that is beyond defeat, representing strength and resilience.
  • aśvinā: Refers to the Aśvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing powers and ability to bring prosperity. They symbolize duality and balance in life.
  • vartikā: This word can mean "to revolve" or "to turn." It suggests the cyclical nature of life and the importance of adaptability in various situations.
  • vām: Translates to "your" or "of you," indicating a personal connection or relationship with the divine or the forces at play.
  • āsno: This term means "to be present" or "to exist." It emphasizes the importance of being aware and conscious in the moment.
  • yat: Meaning "that" or "which," it serves as a connector in the mantra, linking various concepts together.
  • sīm: This can be interpreted as "boundary" or "limit," suggesting the importance of understanding one's limits and the space one occupies.
  • amuñcataṁ: This term means "to release" or "to let go." It signifies the act of freeing oneself from burdens or limitations.
  • vṛkasya: Refers to "wolf" or "predator," symbolizing challenges or adversities that one may face in life.
  • vi: A prefix meaning "with" or "through," indicating a connection or means of achieving something.
  • jayuṣā: This term means "with strength" or "with vigor," emphasizing the importance of energy and vitality in overcoming obstacles.
  • yayathuḥ: This can be interpreted as "they who have gone" or "those who have traveled," suggesting the journey of life and the experiences that shape us.
  • sānv: Meaning "with" or "together," it emphasizes unity and collaboration in facing challenges.
  • adreḥ: Refers to "mountain" or "rock," symbolizing stability and strength in the face of adversity.
  • jātaṁ: This term means "born" or "created," indicating the emergence of new possibilities or ideas.
  • viṣvāco: Meaning "all" or "universal," it signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and experiences.
  • ahataṁ: This term means "unharmed" or "unscathed," suggesting resilience and the ability to withstand challenges.
  • viṣeṇa: Refers to "with a purpose" or "with intention," emphasizing the importance of having a clear goal or direction in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of resilience, strength, and the importance of being present and aware in the face of life's challenges. It encourages individuals to embrace their journey, recognize their limits, and release burdens that hinder their progress. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, overcoming obstacles, and fostering unity and collaboration in communities. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can navigate life's complexities with grace and strength, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
