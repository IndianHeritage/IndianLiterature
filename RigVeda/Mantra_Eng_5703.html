एकस्मिन योगे भुरणा समाने परि वां सप्त श्रवत रथो गात् |न वायन्ति सुभ्वो देवयुक्ता ये वां धूर्षु तरणयो वहन्ति ||
ekasmin yoge bhuraṇā samāne pari vāṁ sapta sravato ratho gāt | na vāyanti subhvo devayuktā ye vāṁ dhūrṣu taraṇayo vahanti ||
  • ekasmin yoge: This phrase translates to "in one union" or "in one connection." It signifies the importance of unity and harmony in various aspects of life.
  • bhuraṇā samāne: This means "the great ones are together." It emphasizes the idea of collaboration and collective strength among individuals or forces.
  • pari vāṁ: This translates to "around you." It indicates the presence of supportive energies or entities surrounding an individual.
  • sapta sravato ratho gāt: This phrase means "the seven chariots are moving." The number seven often symbolizes completeness and can refer to various aspects of life, such as the seven days of the week or the seven chakras in spiritual practices.
  • na vāyanti subhvo: This translates to "they do not move towards the good." It suggests that there are forces or influences that may hinder progress or positive outcomes.
  • devayuktā: This means "those who are connected to the divine." It highlights the importance of spiritual alignment and connection to higher powers.
  • ye vāṁ dhūrṣu taraṇayo vahanti: This translates to "those who carry you through the challenges." It signifies the support and guidance one receives during difficult times.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, collaboration, and divine support in overcoming challenges. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to teamwork in professional settings, where collective efforts lead to success. It also highlights the importance of spiritual connection, reminding individuals to seek guidance from higher powers during tough times. By fostering unity and seeking divine support, one can navigate life's challenges more effectively and achieve a sense of completeness and fulfillment.
