एतत् त्यत् त इन्द्रियम् आचेति येनावधीर वरशिखस्य शेषः |वज्रस्य यत् ते निहतस्य शुष्मात् स्वनाच् चिद् इन्द्र परमो ददारे ||
etat tyat ta indriyam aceti yenāvadhīr varaśikhasya śeṣaḥ | vajrasya yat te nihatasya śuṣmāt svanāc cid indra paramo dadāra ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • etat: this
    • tyat: that
    • ta: those
    • indriyam: senses or faculties
    • aceti: does not perceive
    • yenā: by which
    • avadhīr: the wise or the discerning
    • varaśikhasya: of the best or the supreme
    • śeṣaḥ: the remainder or the residue
    • vajrasya: of the thunderbolt or the powerful
    • yat: which
    • te: your
    • nihatasya: of the defeated or the fallen
    • śuṣmāt: from the subtle
    • svanāc: from the sound
    • cid: consciousness
    • indra: the king of gods
    • paramo: the supreme
    • dadāra: has given
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the relationship between perception, wisdom, and the divine.
    • It suggests that true understanding transcends mere sensory experience.
    • The wise (avadhīr) are able to perceive beyond the physical senses (indriyam) and grasp the essence of reality.
    • The mention of "vajrasya" symbolizes strength and resilience, indicating that true wisdom is powerful.
    • The phrase "from the subtle" implies that deeper truths are often not immediately apparent and require discernment to uncover.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as a conflict at work, a wise person may look beyond the immediate emotions and reactions (senses) to understand the underlying issues and find a resolution.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who practice empathy and active listening can perceive feelings and needs that are not verbally expressed, leading to deeper connections.
    • In the realm of science, researchers often look beyond initial observations to uncover deeper truths about nature, much like the discerning wisdom described in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and discernment in understanding the world.
    • It teaches that true knowledge comes from looking beyond the surface and engaging with the deeper aspects of existence.
    • In real life, applying this wisdom can lead to better decision-making, improved relationships, and a more profound understanding of oneself and the universe.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to cultivate their inner strength and awareness, aligning with the divine essence represented by Indra.
