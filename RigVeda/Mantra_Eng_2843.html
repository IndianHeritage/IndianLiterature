रारन्धि सवनेषु ण एषु स्तोमेषु वृत्रहन् |उक्तेष्व इन्द्र गिरवणः ||
rārandhi savaneṣu ṇa eṣu stomeṣu vṛtrahan | uktheṣv indra girvaṇaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • rārandhi: To be invoked or called upon.
    • savaneṣu: In the offerings or sacrifices.
    • ṇa: Not or without.
    • eṣu: In these.
    • stomeṣu: In the hymns or praises.
    • vṛtrahan: Slayer of Vritra (a demon representing obstacles).
    • uktheṣv: In the utterances or declarations.
    • indra: The king of gods, representing strength and power.
    • girvaṇaḥ: Of the seers or sages, often referring to wisdom and knowledge.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a call to Indra, the god of rain and thunder, to be present in the offerings and sacrifices.
    • It emphasizes the importance of invoking divine strength and wisdom in our prayers and actions.
    • The reference to Vritra signifies the removal of obstacles and challenges in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as preparing for an important exam, one might recite this mantra to seek strength and clarity of mind.
    • During a community gathering or festival, invoking Indra through this mantra can symbolize the collective effort to overcome difficulties and celebrate unity.
    • In personal struggles, such as health issues, this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek divine assistance and wisdom in overcoming obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with divine strength and wisdom.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios where one seeks to overcome challenges, whether personal or communal.
    • By invoking Indra, individuals can find the courage and support needed to face life's obstacles, fostering a sense of resilience and hope.
