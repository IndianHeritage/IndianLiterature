तव श्रीये मरुतो मरजयन्त रुद्र यत ते जनिम चाऽरु चित्रम् |पदं यद विष्णोर उपमं निधायि तेन पाशी गुह्यं नाम गोनाम ||
tava śriye maruto marjayanta rudra yat te janima cāru citram | padaṁ yad viṣṇor upamaṁ nidhāyi tena pāsi guhyaṁ nāma gonām ||
  • tava śriye - "For your glory": This phrase emphasizes the importance of divine glory and the reverence towards the divine. In real life, this can be seen in how people honor their cultural or spiritual beliefs, often dedicating their achievements and successes to a higher power.
  • maruto marjayanta - "The Maruts (storm deities) who purify": The Maruts are associated with storms and winds, symbolizing the cleansing and purifying forces of nature. In everyday life, this can be likened to how rain cleanses the earth, or how challenges in life can lead to personal growth and purification of the soul.
  • rudra yat te janima - "O Rudra, from whom we are born": Rudra is often associated with destruction and transformation. This line reflects the idea that all life originates from a divine source, and it can be related to the cycles of life and death in nature, where destruction leads to new beginnings.
  • cāru citram - "Beautiful and wondrous": This phrase highlights the beauty and wonder of creation. In real life, this can be observed in the appreciation of art, nature, and human relationships, where beauty inspires and uplifts the spirit.
  • padaṁ yad viṣṇor upamaṁ nidhāyi - "The place that is comparable to Vishnu": Vishnu represents preservation and sustenance. This line suggests seeking a state of stability and peace that is akin to the qualities of Vishnu. In practical terms, this can be seen in the pursuit of harmony in personal and communal life, where individuals strive for balance and sustainability.
  • tena pāsi guhyaṁ nāma gonām - "By that, protect the secret names of beings": This emphasizes the importance of safeguarding the essence and identity of all living beings. In a real-world context, this can relate to the protection of cultural identities, languages, and traditions, which are vital for the diversity of human experience.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates themes of divine glory, purification, creation, beauty, preservation, and protection. Its significance lies in the recognition of the interconnectedness of life and the divine. In real life, it encourages individuals to honor their spiritual beliefs, appreciate the beauty around them, seek balance and harmony, and protect the identities of all beings. This mantra serves as a reminder of the sacredness of life and the importance of living in alignment with these divine principles.
