किमाद अमात्रं सख्यं सकिभ्यः कदाऽनु ते भ्रातृं प्र ब्रवाम |श्रिये सुदृशो वपुर अस्य सर्गाः स्वर्ण न चितृतमम इषा आगोः ||
kim ād amatraṁ sakhyaṁ sakhibhyaḥ kadā nu te bhrātram pra bravāma | śriye sudṛśo vapur asya sargāḥ sva1r ṇa citratamam iṣa ā goḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kim: what
    • ād: from the beginning
    • amatraṁ: without measure
    • sakhyaṁ: friendship
    • sakhibhyaḥ: to friends
    • kadā: when
    • nu: indeed
    • te: your
    • bhrātram: brother
    • pra: forth
    • bravāma: we speak
    • śriye: for prosperity
    • su: good
    • dṛśo: of vision
    • vapur: form
    • asya: of this
    • sargāḥ: creations
    • sva1r: of the self
    • ṇa: not
    • citratamam: most colorful
    • iṣa: desire
    • ā: to
    • goḥ: of the cow
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects on the essence of friendship and brotherhood, questioning the nature of true companionship.
    • It emphasizes the importance of speaking forth about the bonds we share with our friends and brothers, especially in the context of prosperity and well-being.
    • The mention of "most colorful creations" suggests that life is enriched by the diversity of relationships and experiences.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, fostering friendships among colleagues can lead to a more collaborative and productive environment.
    • In families, the bond between siblings can provide emotional support and a sense of belonging, enhancing overall family dynamics.
    • In communities, friendships can lead to collective efforts in social causes, promoting unity and shared goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of nurturing relationships and the role they play in our lives.
    • It teaches us to value our connections with others, as they contribute to our personal growth and societal harmony.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger bonds, greater empathy, and a more fulfilling existence.
