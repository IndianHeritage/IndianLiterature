कनिक्रदात कलशे गोभिर अज्ञसे व्यव्ययं समया वारम् अर्षसि |मार्मृज्यमानो अत्यो न सानसिर इन्द्रस्य सोम जठरे सम अक्षरः ||
kanikradat kalaśe gobhir ajyase vy a1vyayaṁ samayā vāram arṣasi | marmṛjyamāno atyo na sānasir indrasya soma jaṭhare sam akṣaraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kanikradat: Referring to the act of calling or invoking.
    • kalaśe: In a pot or vessel, often symbolizing abundance or nourishment.
    • gobhir: With the cows, which are often associated with wealth and prosperity in Vedic literature.
    • ajyase: Referring to the clarified butter (ghee), a sacred offering in rituals.
    • vy a1vyayaṁ: Indicating something that is eternal or unchanging.
    • samayā: In due time or at the right moment.
    • vāram: A term that can mean a boon or a blessing.
    • arṣasi: Referring to the act of being invoked or called upon.
    • marmṛjyamāno: Being purified or cleansed.
    • atyō: Beyond or transcending.
    • na: Not.
    • sānasir: Referring to the head or the top, often symbolizing the highest state.
    • indrasya: Of Indra, the king of gods, representing power and authority.
    • soma: A sacred drink, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • jaṭhare: In the belly or womb, symbolizing creation and sustenance.
    • sam: Together or in harmony.
    • akṣaraḥ: The imperishable or eternal.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence, calling upon the eternal essence that resides in all beings.
    • The reference to cows and ghee symbolizes abundance and the nurturing aspects of life, suggesting that prosperity comes from a harmonious relationship with nature.
    • The idea of purification and transcendence indicates a journey towards spiritual enlightenment, where one seeks to rise above the mundane and connect with the divine.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents strength and authority, suggesting that through divine blessings, one can achieve great power and success.
    • The mention of soma highlights the importance of spiritual nourishment, which is essential for personal growth and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied during rituals or prayers where individuals seek blessings for prosperity and abundance.
    • In a corporate setting, invoking the essence of this mantra can inspire teamwork and collaboration, leading to collective success.
    • During personal challenges, reflecting on the themes of purification and transcendence can motivate individuals to rise above difficulties and seek spiritual growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of invoking divine blessings for prosperity, purification, and spiritual growth.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing relationships with nature and the divine.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek harmony, abundance, and enlightenment, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal development to community building.
