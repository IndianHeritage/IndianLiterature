इमं इन्द्र सुतं पिब ज्येष्ठम् अमर्त्यं मदम् |शुक्रस्य त्वाभ्य अक्षरन् धाराऽऋतस्य साधने ||
imam indra sutam piba jyeṣṭham amartyam madam | śukrasya tvābhy akṣaran dhārā ṛtasya sādane ||
  • Translation: "Drink this, O Indra, the elder son, the immortal nectar; the flow of the pure, unchanging essence, for the establishment of truth."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • Imam: This refers to "this" or "this one," indicating something specific that is being offered.
    • Indra: The king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
    • Sutam: Meaning "son" or "offspring," often used to denote a divine or noble lineage.
    • Piba: A command form meaning "drink," suggesting an action of consumption or acceptance.
    • Jyeṣṭham: Translates to "the elder" or "the greatest," indicating superiority or primacy.
    • Amartyam: Meaning "immortal" or "eternal," referring to something that transcends death.
    • Madam: Refers to "nectar" or "intoxicating essence," often associated with divine bliss or enlightenment.
    • Śukrasya: Meaning "of the pure" or "bright," often associated with clarity and truth.
    • Tvābhy: Meaning "to you," indicating the recipient of the offering.
    • Akṣaran: Translates to "unchanging" or "imperishable," referring to the eternal nature of truth.
    • Dhārā: Meaning "flow" or "stream," suggesting a continuous and nourishing essence.
    • Ṛtasya: Refers to "truth" or "cosmic order," emphasizing the importance of righteousness.
    • Sādane: Meaning "for the establishment" or "to achieve," indicating a purpose or goal.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership roles, one can draw parallels to Indra, where the leader must embody strength and wisdom, guiding their team towards a common goal.
    • The concept of "drinking the nectar" can be likened to embracing knowledge and wisdom, which can lead to personal and professional growth.
    • In a community setting, the establishment of truth and righteousness can be seen in initiatives that promote justice and equality, reflecting the essence of Ṛta.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of embracing wisdom, strength, and the pursuit of truth. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek knowledge and act with integrity, whether in personal relationships, professional environments, or community engagements. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and just society, reflecting the eternal values represented in the mantra.
