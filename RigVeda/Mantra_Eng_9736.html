पर्शुर ह नाम मानवī साकं ससुव विम्शतिम् |भद्रम भल तस्याऽभूद यस्याऽउदरम् आमयद विश्वस्माद इन्द्र उत्तरः ||
parśur ha nāma mānavī sākaṁ sasūva viṁśatim | bhadram bhala tyasyā abhūd yasyā udaram āmayad viśvasmād indra uttaraḥ ||
  • Mantra: "parśur ha nāma mānavī sākaṁ sasūva viṁśatim | bhadram bhala tyasyā abhūd yasyā udaram āmayad viśvasmād indra uttaraḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • parśur: Refers to the axe or a tool, symbolizing strength and power.
    • ha: An affirmation, often translated as "indeed" or "certainly."
    • nāma: Name or identity.
    • mānavī: Human or humanity.
    • sākaṁ: Together or in conjunction.
    • sasūva: To give birth or to produce.
    • viṁśatim: Twenty, often symbolizing completeness or a full cycle.
    • bhadram: Auspiciousness or goodness.
    • bhala: Strength or power.
    • tyasyā: Of that or from that.
    • abhūd: Has come to be or has manifested.
    • yasyā: Of whom or whose.
    • udaram: Womb or source.
    • āmayad: To be free from illness or to be healthy.
    • viśvasmād: From all or from the universe.
    • indra: The king of gods, representing power and authority.
    • uttaraḥ: Superior or higher.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks to the strength and identity of humanity, suggesting that through unity and strength, one can achieve auspiciousness and health.
    • The reference to "parśur" (axe) symbolizes the tools and strength we possess to carve our destinies.
    • The phrase "sākaṁ sasūva viṁśatim" indicates that together, humans can create a complete and fulfilling existence.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and collaboration in achieving well-being and prosperity.
    • The mention of "bhadram bhala" highlights that goodness and strength are essential for a fruitful life.
    • Furthermore, the mantra suggests that from the womb of the universe, health and auspiciousness can emerge, indicating a connection between the individual and the cosmos.
    • Finally, invoking "indra uttaraḥ" suggests that by aligning ourselves with higher powers or principles, we can attain greater strength and success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of strength, unity, and auspiciousness in human life.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in community building, teamwork, and collaborative efforts where individuals come together to achieve common goals.
    • It encourages individuals to harness their inner strength and work collectively for the greater good, leading to a healthier and more prosperous society.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that by connecting with higher ideals and working together, we can overcome challenges and create a better future.
