सोमो अर्षति धरिणसिर दधाना इन्द्रियं रसम् |सुवीरो अभिशस्तिपाः ||
somo arṣati dharṇasir dadhāna indriyaṁ rasam | suvīro abhiśastipāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • somo: Refers to 'Soma', a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing vitality and immortality.
    • arṣati: Means 'shines' or 'radiates', indicating brilliance or enlightenment.
    • dharṇasir: Translates to 'one who holds' or 'possessor', often referring to strength or support.
    • dadhāna: Means 'holding' or 'maintaining', suggesting stability and continuity.
    • indriyaṁ: Refers to 'senses' or 'organs', indicating the faculties of perception.
    • rasam: Means 'essence' or 'flavor', often symbolizing the core experience of life.
    • suvīro: Translates to 'well-heroic' or 'valiant', indicating strength and courage.
    • abhiśastipāḥ: Means 'those who are blessed' or 'those who are endowed', suggesting divine favor or grace.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the essence of vitality and strength that comes from a divine source.
    • The reference to 'Soma' highlights the importance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual, in achieving a radiant life.
    • It emphasizes the need to maintain our senses and perceptions to fully experience the richness of life.
    • The qualities of being 'well-heroic' and 'blessed' suggest that true strength comes from a harmonious balance of inner and outer experiences.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, individuals who maintain a balance between their work (dharṇasir) and personal life (indriyaṁ) often find greater success and fulfillment.
    • In health and wellness, consuming nourishing foods (somo) and engaging in activities that promote mental clarity (arṣati) can lead to a more vibrant life.
    • In relationships, being 'suvīro' means showing courage and support to loved ones, fostering a sense of community and belonging.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of nurturing our inner vitality and strength through conscious living.
    • It teaches us that by maintaining our senses and perceptions, we can fully engage with the world around us.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more balanced, fulfilling, and heroic existence, where we are both strong and blessed.
