दृशेन्यो यो महिना समिद्धो 'रोचत दिवiyonir विभावाः |तस्मिन्न अग्नौ सूक्तवाकेन देवाः हविः विश्व आजुहवुस तनूपाः ||
dṛśenyo yo mahinā samiddho 'rocata diviyonir vibhāvā | tasminn agnau sūktavākena devā havir viśva ājuhavus tanūpāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "dṛśenyo yo mahinā samiddho 'rocata diviyonir vibhāvā | tasminn agnau sūktavākena devā havir viśva ājuhavus tanūpāḥ ||"
  • Translation: "He who is kindled by the great light, shining in the divine realm, the gods, through the sacred utterance, have offered their oblations into that fire, which is the universal essence."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • dṛśenyo: Refers to the one who is visible or perceivable.
    • mahinā: Means great or powerful.
    • samiddho: Signifies being kindled or ignited.
    • 'rocata: Indicates shining or radiating light.
    • diviyonir: Pertains to the divine or celestial realm.
    • vibhāvā: Refers to the manifestation or presence.
    • agnau: Means in the fire.
    • sūktavākena: Through the sacred utterance or hymn.
    • devā: Refers to the gods.
    • havir: Means offerings or oblations.
    • viśva: Signifies universal or all-encompassing.
    • ājuhavus: Indicates the act of offering or invoking.
    • tanūpāḥ: Refers to the essence or the physical forms.

  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine light and the act of offering. It suggests that when one is illuminated by a higher power, they can connect with the universal essence.
    • The act of offering into the fire symbolizes the surrender of one's ego and desires to a greater cause, which is a common theme in many spiritual practices.
    • In real life, this can be seen in rituals where individuals offer their intentions, prayers, or even material items into a fire, symbolizing purification and the release of attachments.

  • Real-Life Application:
    • In various cultures, fire rituals are performed to signify transformation and renewal. For example, in Hinduism, the Homa or Yajna involves offerings to the fire, invoking divine blessings.
    • In a metaphorical sense, this mantra can be applied to personal growth. When individuals face challenges, they can visualize their struggles as offerings to a greater purpose, allowing them to rise above their circumstances.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in community service or charitable acts, where they offer their time and resources for the benefit of others, thus connecting with the universal essence of humanity.

  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of divine light and the importance of selfless offerings. It encourages individuals to seek a connection with the greater universe through acts of devotion and service.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can cultivate a sense of purpose and fulfillment in their life, contributing positively to the world around them.
