न ते दूरे परमाचिद्रजांस्याऽतु प्र याहि हरिवो हरिभ्यां |स्थिराय वृष्णे सवनाकृतिमा युक्ता ग्रावाणः समिधाने अग्नौ ||
na te dūre paramā cid rajāṁsy ā tu pra yāhi harivo haribhyām | sthirāya vṛṣṇe savanā kṛtemā yuktā grāvāṇaḥ samidhāne agnau ||
  • Translation: "Do not go far away from the supreme king; rather, come here, O Haris, with your companions. For the steadfast Vṛṣṇa, who is engaged in the sacrifice, is united with the offerings made to the fire."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • na te dūre: "Do not go far away" - This suggests the importance of staying close to divine or higher principles.
    • paramā cid rajāṁsy: "the supreme king" - Refers to a higher power or ultimate reality, often interpreted as God or the universal spirit.
    • ā tu pra yāhi: "rather, come here" - An invitation to approach or connect with the divine.
    • harivo haribhyām: "O Haris, with your companions" - Haris can refer to divine beings or followers of the divine, emphasizing community and togetherness in spiritual pursuits.
    • sthirāya vṛṣṇe: "for the steadfast Vṛṣṇa" - Vṛṣṇa symbolizes strength and stability, representing the unwavering nature of the divine.
    • savanā kṛtemā: "who is engaged in the sacrifice" - Highlights the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine.
    • yuktā grāvāṇaḥ: "is united with the offerings" - Indicates that the divine presence is invoked through sincere offerings and actions.
    • samidhāne agnau: "made to the fire" - Fire is a sacred element in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purification and the divine connection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals and offerings are made to honor a higher power, similar to the Vedic practice of making offerings to Agni (the fire god) to seek blessings.
    • Community gatherings, such as festivals or religious ceremonies, reflect the idea of coming together to connect with the divine, as emphasized in the mantra.
    • In personal life, staying connected to one's values and principles can be seen as staying close to the "supreme king," guiding decisions and actions.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of staying connected to higher principles and the divine through community, rituals, and steadfastness. In real life, this can be applied by engaging in community service, participating in spiritual practices, and maintaining a strong moral compass. The essence of the mantra encourages individuals to seek unity with the divine and to recognize the importance of collective spiritual endeavors in achieving a fulfilling life.
