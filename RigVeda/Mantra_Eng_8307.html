त्वां मृजन्ति दश योषणाḥ सुतं सोम ऋषिभिर मतीभिर धीतिभिर हितम् | अव्यो वारेभिर उता देवहूतिभिर नृभिर यतो वाजम् आ दर्षि सातये ||
tvām mṛjanti daśa yoṣaṇaḥ sutaṁ soma ṛṣibhir matibhir dhītibhir hitam | avyo vārebhir uta devahūtibhir nṛbhir yato vājam ā darṣi sātaye ||
  • tvām mṛjanti: This phrase translates to "they cleanse you." It signifies the act of purification, which can be understood in a real-life context as the importance of maintaining purity in thoughts and actions. For example, in daily life, individuals often engage in practices such as meditation or yoga to cleanse their minds and bodies.
  • daśa yoṣaṇaḥ: This means "ten maidens." In a broader sense, it can represent various aspects of life or different energies that contribute to one's well-being. For instance, in a family, each member plays a unique role, much like the ten maidens, contributing to the harmony and balance of the household.
  • sutaṁ soma: "Soma" refers to a divine drink or nectar, often associated with immortality and enlightenment. "Sutaṁ" means "offspring" or "son." This can be interpreted as the nurturing of wisdom and knowledge, akin to how parents nurture their children to grow into wise individuals. For example, parents often encourage their children to pursue education and personal growth.
  • ṛṣibhir matibhir dhītibhir hitam: This translates to "by the sages, with wisdom and understanding." It emphasizes the importance of guidance from wise individuals in our lives. In real life, mentors and teachers play a crucial role in shaping our understanding and decision-making processes, much like sages guiding the seekers of knowledge.
  • avyō vārebhir uta devahūtibhir: This phrase can be interpreted as "with the divine offerings and the blessings of the gods." It highlights the significance of divine grace and support in our endeavors. In practical terms, people often seek blessings through rituals or prayers before starting new ventures, believing that divine support will lead to success.
  • nṛbhir yato vājam ā darṣi sātaye: This means "where the strength is seen by men." It signifies the recognition of strength and power in human endeavors. In real life, this can be seen in various fields such as sports, business, or community leadership, where individuals showcase their strength and capabilities, inspiring others to follow suit.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of purification, wisdom, divine support, and the recognition of strength in human endeavors. It serves as a reminder that in our pursuit of success and enlightenment, we must seek guidance, maintain purity in our actions, and acknowledge the divine forces that aid us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal development, family dynamics, and community leadership, encouraging individuals to strive for balance, wisdom, and strength in their lives.
