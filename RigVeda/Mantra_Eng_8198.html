आ यद योनीं हिरण्ययम् आशुर ऋतस्य सीदति |जहात्य अप्रचेतसः ||
ā yad yoniṁ hiraṇyayam āśur ṛtasya sīdati | jahāty apracetasaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yad: "that which" or "whatever"
    • yoniṁ: "womb" or "source"
    • hiraṇyayam: "golden" or "radiant"
    • āśur: "the divine" or "the powerful"
    • ṛtasya: "of truth" or "of order"
    • sīdati: "sits" or "resides"
    • jahāty: "releases" or "lets go"
    • apracetasaḥ: "of the unwise" or "of those without understanding"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the source of creation, often interpreted as the divine womb from which all existence emerges.
    • The term "hiraṇyayam" suggests a radiant or golden quality, indicating purity and divinity.
    • The phrase "āśur ṛtasya" emphasizes the connection between divine power and the fundamental truth or order of the universe.
    • The latter part of the mantra warns against the consequences of ignorance, as it states that those who lack understanding will miss out on the divine essence that resides in the source of creation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a practical sense, this mantra can be related to the importance of understanding our origins and the interconnectedness of life. For instance, in environmental conservation, recognizing the "source" of our resources can lead to more sustainable practices.
    • In personal development, the idea of "releasing" ignorance can be seen in the journey of education and self-awareness. Individuals who seek knowledge and understanding often find a deeper connection to their purpose and the world around them.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of recognizing the divine source of all existence and the importance of wisdom in understanding our place within it.
    • It serves as a reminder that ignorance can lead to disconnection from the truth, while knowledge and awareness can illuminate our path.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as personal growth, environmental stewardship, and fostering a deeper understanding of our relationships with others and the universe.
