यस् पतिर वारणाम् असि रध्रस्य चोदिता | इन्द्र स्तोत्रिणाम् अविताऽ वि वो मदे द्विषो नः पाहि अंहसो विवक्षसे ||
yas patir vāryāṇām asi radhrasya coditā | indra stotṝṇām avitā vi vo made dviṣo naḥ pāhy aṁhaso vivakṣase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • patir: lord or master
    • vāryāṇām: of the strong or powerful
    • asi: you are
    • radhrasya: of the one who is swift or quick
    • coditā: urged or inspired
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • stotṝṇām: of the worshippers or those who praise
    • avitā: protector or guardian
    • vi: from or away from
    • vo: your
    • made: intoxication or delight
    • dviṣo: from enemies
    • naḥ: us
    • pāhy: protect
    • aṁhaso: from harm or injury
    • vivakṣase: you wish or desire
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, who represents strength, power, and protection.
    • The speaker acknowledges Indra as the lord of the powerful and swift, seeking his guidance and protection from harm and enemies.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine support in overcoming challenges and adversities in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive work environment, individuals often seek the support of mentors or leaders who can guide them through challenges, similar to how the mantra seeks divine guidance.
    • During times of conflict or personal struggles, people may turn to spiritual practices or prayers for protection and strength, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine protection and guidance in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as facing challenges in personal or professional life, where one seeks strength and support from a higher power.
    • By invoking the essence of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of security and confidence in their endeavors, knowing they are supported in their journey.
