अयम् उ वाम् पुरुतमः रयीयं चश्वत्तमम् अवसे जोहवीति |सजोषाव इन्द्रावरुणा मरुद्भिर दिवा पृथिव्या श्रिणुतं हवम् मे ||
ayam u vām purutamo rayīyañ chaśvattamam avase johavīti | sajoṣāv indrāvaruṇā marudbhir divā pṛthivyā śṛṇutaṁ havam me ||
  • ayam u vām purutamo rayīyañ chaśvattamam avase johavīti
    • ayam - This means "this" or "this one," indicating a focus on the present moment or entity.
    • u - This is a conjunction meaning "and," connecting thoughts or ideas.
    • vām - This translates to "you," addressing the divine or the audience directly.
    • purutamo - Meaning "most excellent" or "foremost," it signifies the highest quality or status.
    • rayīyañ - This refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," emphasizing abundance.
    • chaśvattamam - This means "the most eternal," highlighting the everlasting nature of the divine or the blessings sought.
    • avase - This translates to "may you grant" or "may you bestow," indicating a plea or request.
    • johavīti - This means "I invoke" or "I call upon," expressing the act of seeking divine attention or favor.
  • sajoṣāv indrāvaruṇā marudbhir divā pṛthivyā śṛṇutaṁ havam me
    • sajoṣāv - This means "with joy" or "joyfully," suggesting a positive and uplifting approach to the invocation.
    • indrāvaruṇā - Referring to the deities Indra and Varuna, symbolizing strength and cosmic order, respectively.
    • marudbhir - This translates to "with the Maruts," the storm deities, representing power and dynamism.
    • divā - Meaning "by day," indicating the time of invocation, which can symbolize clarity and enlightenment.
    • pṛthivyā - This means "of the earth," grounding the invocation in the physical realm.
    • śṛṇutaṁ - This translates to "listen" or "hear," a call for attention from the divine beings.
    • havam me - This means "my offering," indicating a personal connection and dedication in the act of worship.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine blessings for prosperity and eternal well-being. It highlights the connection between the individual and the divine, calling upon powerful deities to listen to the offerings made with joy and sincerity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during rituals, prayers, or moments of reflection when seeking guidance, strength, and abundance. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the individual with the universe and the importance of approaching life with gratitude and reverence.
