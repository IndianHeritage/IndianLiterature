आ यद धरी इन्द्र विव्रता वेर आ ते वज्रं जरतā बाह्वोर धात् |येनाविहर्यतक्रतो अमित्रान पुर इष्णासि पुरुहूत पूर्वीः ||
ā yad dharī indra vivratā ver ā te vajraṁ jaritā bāhvor dhāt | yenāviharyatakrato amitrān pura iṣṇāsi puruhūta pūrvīḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yad: Whatever
    • dharī: Holds or supports
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • vivratā: Open or revealed
    • ver: Strength or power
    • ā te: From you
    • vajraṁ: Thunderbolt or weapon
    • jaritā: Wielded or used
    • bāhvor: Of the arms
    • dhāt: Element or essence
    • yenā: By which
    • viharyatakrato: You who are active in deeds
    • amitrān: Enemies
    • pura: In the city or place
    • iṣṇāsi: You conquer
    • puruhūta: O many invoked one
    • pūrvīḥ: The ancient or previous
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, emphasizing his strength and power.
    • It highlights the importance of divine support in overcoming challenges and enemies.
    • The reference to "vajraṁ" symbolizes the power and might that can be wielded to protect and defend.
    • It suggests that through divine intervention, one can conquer adversities and emerge victorious.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal struggle, such as facing a difficult situation at work or in relationships, individuals often seek strength from a higher power or their inner resilience.
    • Sports teams may invoke the spirit of their champions, akin to calling upon Indra, to inspire them to victory against their opponents.
    • In community efforts, when facing challenges like natural disasters, people come together, drawing on collective strength and support, reminiscent of the divine assistance sought in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine strength and support in overcoming obstacles and adversaries.
    • It teaches that with faith and the right intentions, one can harness inner and outer strength to face life's challenges.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek help, whether from the divine, community, or personal resilience, to conquer difficulties and achieve success.
