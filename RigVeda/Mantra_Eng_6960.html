अभि वो वीरम् अन्धसो मादेषु गाया गिरा महा विचेतसम् | इन्द्रं नाम श्रुत्यं शाकिनं वचो यथा ||
abhi vo vīram andhaso madeṣu gāya girā mahā vicetasam | indraṁ nāma śrutyaṁ śākinaṁ vaco yathā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards
    • vo: your
    • vīram: hero or valiant one
    • andhaso: of the blind or ignorant
    • madeṣu: in the intoxication or ecstasy
    • gāya: sing
    • girā: with words or speech
    • mahā: great
    • vicetasam: of the wise or enlightened
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods
    • nāma: by name
    • śrutyaṁ: by hearing or through knowledge
    • śākinaṁ: the one who is powerful or capable
    • vaco: words
    • yathā: as
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra calls upon the divine qualities of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, symbolizing strength and power.
    • It emphasizes the importance of using one's words and knowledge to uplift oneself and others from ignorance.
    • The phrase "sing with words" suggests the power of expression and communication in achieving greatness.
    • It highlights the contrast between the enlightened (vicetasam) and the ignorant (andhaso), urging individuals to seek wisdom and clarity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a leader who communicates effectively can inspire their team to overcome challenges, much like Indra leading the gods.
    • In education, teachers who impart knowledge can help students rise above ignorance, fostering a culture of learning and enlightenment.
    • In personal relationships, open and honest communication can resolve conflicts and strengthen bonds, reflecting the power of words.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of wisdom, communication, and the pursuit of knowledge in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to strive for enlightenment and to use their words and actions to uplift themselves and others.
    • In a world often clouded by ignorance, this mantra inspires us to seek clarity and strength through understanding and expression.
