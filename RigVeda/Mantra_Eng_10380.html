प्रियं श्रद्धे ददतः प्रियं श्रद्धे दिदासतः |प्रियं भोजेषु यज्वस्व इदं मा उदितं कृधि ||
priyaṁ śraddhe dadataḥ priyaṁ śraddhe didāsataḥ | priyam bhojeṣu yajvasv idam ma uditaṁ kṛdhi ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "To those who give with love, to those who share with love, may this offering be pleasing to the gods." This mantra emphasizes the importance of love and devotion in our actions, particularly in the context of offerings and sacrifices.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • priyaṁ: dear, beloved, or pleasing.
    • śraddhe: faith, trust, or devotion.
    • dadataḥ: those who give.
    • didāsataḥ: those who share or distribute.
    • bhojeṣu: in offerings or sacrifices.
    • yajvasv: pertaining to the act of worship or sacrifice.
    • idam: this.
    • ma: may it not be.
    • uditaṁ: raised or elevated.
    • kṛdhi: do or make.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, when individuals come together to support a cause, such as a charity event, their collective efforts can be seen as an offering made with love and devotion. The success of such events often hinges on the genuine intentions of the participants.
    • In personal relationships, when one partner gives gifts or performs acts of kindness with love and sincerity, it strengthens the bond between them. This mantra reminds us that the spirit in which we give is as important as the act of giving itself.
    • In a workplace, when employees collaborate on a project with a shared sense of purpose and mutual respect, the outcome is often more successful. This reflects the idea that when we work together with love and dedication, we create something greater than ourselves.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the values of love, faith, and devotion in our actions. It teaches us that the quality of our intentions and the spirit in which we give are crucial in making our offerings meaningful. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, community service, or professional collaborations, embodying these principles can lead to more fulfilling and successful outcomes. By practicing love and devotion in our daily lives, we not only enhance our own experiences but also contribute positively to the lives of others.
