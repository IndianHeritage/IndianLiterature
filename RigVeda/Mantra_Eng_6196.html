अधिव यद गिरिणां यामं शुभ्राऽचिध्वम् |सुवाणैर मन्दध्व इन्दुभिः ||
adhīva yad girīṇāṁ yāmaṁ śubhrā acidhvam | suvānair mandadhva indubhiḥ ||
  • adhīva: This word can be translated as "to meditate" or "to contemplate." It signifies the importance of focused thought and reflection in our lives.
  • yad: Meaning "that" or "which," it connects the action of meditation to the subject of contemplation.
  • girīṇāṁ: This term refers to "mountains" or "hills," symbolizing stability, strength, and the grandeur of nature. Mountains often represent challenges that one must overcome.
  • yāmaṁ: This word means "to go" or "to move towards." It suggests a journey or progression, both physically and spiritually.
  • śubhrā: Translated as "pure" or "auspicious," this word emphasizes the importance of purity in thoughts and actions.
  • acidhvam: This term means "to be free from division" or "to be undivided." It suggests unity and harmony, both within oneself and with the universe.
  • suvānair: Meaning "with good intentions" or "with auspiciousness," this word highlights the significance of positive thoughts and actions in our endeavors.
  • mandadhva: This term can be interpreted as "to be gentle" or "to be calm." It encourages a peaceful approach to life and interactions with others.
  • indubhiḥ: This word translates to "with the moon" or "by the moonlight," symbolizing illumination, guidance, and the nurturing aspects of nature.

In summary, this mantra emphasizes the importance of meditation and contemplation on the stability and strength represented by mountains. It encourages individuals to pursue purity and unity in their thoughts and actions, fostering a sense of calm and auspiciousness in their lives. The reference to the moonlight suggests that guidance and illumination are available to those who seek it.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in daily life by encouraging individuals to take time for meditation and reflection, helping them to navigate challenges with a clear and focused mind.
  • In a world filled with distractions, the call for purity and unity can inspire people to cultivate positive intentions and harmonious relationships.
  • The gentle approach advocated in the mantra can be particularly relevant in conflict resolution, promoting peace and understanding in interpersonal interactions.
  • By seeking guidance and illumination, individuals can find clarity in their decisions and actions, leading to a more fulfilling and purposeful life.
