अग्नि यं यज्ञं अध्वरं विश्वतः परिभूर् असि |स इद् देवेषु गच्छति ||
agne yaṁ yajñam adhvaraṁ viśvataḥ paribhūr asi | sa id deveṣu gacchati ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying force and a medium through which offerings are made to the deities.
  • Yaṁ: This translates to "whom" or "that which," indicating a connection to the subject of the mantra.
  • Yajñam: This means "sacrifice" or "offering." In the Vedic context, yajña is a ritual of offerings made to the gods, often involving fire.
  • Adhvaraṁ: This term refers to the "sacred ritual" or "sacrifice" itself, emphasizing the importance of the ritualistic aspect of the offering.
  • Viśvataḥ: This means "from all sides" or "universally," suggesting that the offering is made with a sense of inclusivity and universality.
  • Paribhūr: This translates to "you are encompassing" or "you are all-pervading," indicating the omnipresence of Agni in the universe.
  • Asi: This means "you are," affirming the identity of Agni as a central figure in the ritual.
  • Sa: This means "he" or "that," referring back to Agni.
  • Id: This translates to "this," indicating the immediacy of the offering.
  • Deveṣu: This means "to the gods," highlighting the recipients of the offerings made through the ritual.
  • Gacchati: This means "goes" or "reaches," suggesting that the offerings made reach the divine realm.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to Agni, the fire god, to accept the offerings made in the sacred ritual, emphasizing that these offerings are made universally and are intended for all deities. It highlights the significance of fire as a medium for communication with the divine and the importance of rituals in Vedic culture.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: In Hindu weddings and other significant ceremonies, fire is often a central element, symbolizing purity and the presence of the divine.
  • Personal Growth: The concept of sacrifice can be related to personal development, where individuals may need to give up certain comforts or habits to achieve their goals.
  • Community and Inclusivity: The idea of making offerings universally can be applied to community service, where individuals contribute to the welfare of all, regardless of background.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of rituals, the role of Agni as a mediator between humans and the divine, and the universal nature of offerings. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the significance of selfless acts in our lives.
