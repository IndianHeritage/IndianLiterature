वृत्रस्य त्वा श्वसथाद ईषमाणा विश्वे देवाः अजहुर ये सखायः |मरुद्भिर इन्द्र सख्यं ते अस्त्व अथेमा विश्वाḥ पृथना जयासि ||
vṛtrasya tvā śvasathād īṣamāṇā viśve devā ajahur ye sakhāyaḥ | marudbhir indra sakhyaṁ te astv athemā viśvāḥ pṛtanā jayāsi ||
  • vṛtrasya: Refers to Vritra, a serpent or dragon-like being in Vedic mythology, often associated with drought and obstacles.
  • tvā: Means "you," addressing a deity, in this case, Indra, the king of gods.
  • śvasathād: Translates to "from the breath," indicating a connection to life force or vitality.
  • īṣamāṇā: Means "desiring," suggesting a longing or aspiration for something.
  • viśve devā: Refers to "all the gods," indicating a collective of divine beings.
  • ajahur: Means "they have invoked" or "they have called upon," indicating a plea or request to the divine.
  • ye sakhāyaḥ: Translates to "those who are friends," emphasizing the importance of camaraderie and support among beings.
  • marudbhir: Refers to the Maruts, the storm deities, symbolizing power and strength.
  • indra: The name of the chief deity, representing strength, courage, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • sakhyaṁ: Means "friendship" or "alliance," highlighting the importance of unity and cooperation.
  • te astv: Translates to "may it be with you," a blessing or wish for the recipient.
  • athemā: Means "may there be," indicating a desire for abundance or prosperity.
  • viśvāḥ: Refers to "all," emphasizing inclusivity and universality.
  • pṛtanā: Means "victory" or "success," indicating the achievement of goals.
  • jayāsi: Translates to "you are victorious," affirming the strength and triumph of the addressed deity.

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Indra, seeking his blessings for strength, friendship, and victory over obstacles represented by Vritra. It emphasizes the importance of unity among the gods and the need for divine support in overcoming challenges.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Teamwork: Just as the mantra highlights the importance of friendship and unity among the gods, it can inspire individuals to work collaboratively in teams, fostering a spirit of cooperation to achieve common goals.
  • Overcoming Challenges: The invocation to Indra for strength can be a reminder for individuals facing personal or professional obstacles to seek support from friends and allies, reinforcing the idea that challenges can be overcome with collective effort.
  • Spiritual Growth: The mantra encourages individuals to connect with the divine and seek guidance, which can be relevant in times of uncertainty or when making significant life decisions.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the significance of friendship, unity, and divine support in overcoming life's challenges. It encourages individuals to seek strength from their community and the divine, fostering a sense of togetherness and resilience in the face of adversity.
