महि वो महताम् अवो वरुण मित्र दाशुषे |यम् आदित्याः अभि द्रुहो रक्षथा नेम अगं नशद अनहसो व ऊतयः सूतयः व ऊतयः ||
mahi vo mahatām avo varuṇa mitra dāśuṣe | yam ādityā abhi druho rakṣathā nem aghaṁ naśad anehaso va ūtayaḥ suūtayo va ūtayaḥ ||
  • Translation: "O Varuna and Mitra, may we be protected by your great power. May the Adityas, who are free from deceit, safeguard us from all that is harmful and grant us prosperity."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Mahi: Great or powerful.
    • Voh: You (plural), referring to the deities.
    • Mahātām: Of the great ones, indicating the divine nature of Varuna and Mitra.
    • Avo: May you come or may you protect.
    • Varuṇa: The god of cosmic order and water, representing moral and natural law.
    • Mitra: The god of friendship and alliances, symbolizing harmony and cooperation.
    • Dāśuṣe: To the one who is devoted or to the servant.
    • Yam: Whom.
    • Ādityā: The Adityas, a group of solar deities.
    • Abhi: Towards or in the direction of.
    • Drukho: Deceit or falsehood.
    • Rakṣathā: Protect us.
    • Nem: From or away from.
    • Aghaṁ: Sin or harm.
    • Naśad: We may not be harmed.
    • Anehaso: From the darkness or ignorance.
    • Va: Or.
    • Ūtayaḥ: Prosperity or abundance.
    • Suūtayo: Good prosperity or auspiciousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of uncertainty, individuals often seek guidance and protection from higher powers, similar to how the mantra invokes Varuna and Mitra for safeguarding against harm.
    • In a community setting, fostering cooperation and harmony among members can be seen as a reflection of the principles embodied by Mitra, promoting peace and collective well-being.
    • In personal relationships, the invocation of trust and honesty can be likened to the plea for protection from deceit, emphasizing the importance of integrity in interactions.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine protection and guidance from the forces of truth and harmony. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking support from higher powers during challenging times, fostering cooperation in communities, and upholding integrity in personal relationships. By invoking the qualities of Varuna and Mitra, individuals can aspire to lead lives that are not only prosperous but also aligned with moral and ethical values, ultimately contributing to a more harmonious society.
