अजा अन्यस्य वहनयो हरि अन्यस्य संभृताः |ताभ्यां वृत्राणि जिघ्नते ||
ajā anyasya vahnayo harī anyasya sambhṛtā | tābhyāṁ vṛtrāṇi jighnate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ajā: unbounded, limitless, or eternal
    • anyasya: of another, or different
    • vahnayo: of the fires, or energies
    • harī: the one who removes, or the divine force
    • sambhṛtā: gathered, or accumulated
    • tābhyāṁ: by them, or through them
    • vṛtrāṇi: the obstacles, or challenges
    • jighnate: to overcome, or to destroy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the eternal and limitless nature of existence, emphasizing the power of divine energies.
    • The phrase "harī anyasya" suggests that there are different forces at play, and the divine energy is capable of overcoming challenges.
    • The term "vṛtrāṇi" refers to obstacles or adversities that one may face in life, which can be both internal (like fears) and external (like societal challenges).
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often face internal obstacles such as self-doubt. The mantra reminds us that through harnessing our inner strength (harī), we can overcome these challenges.
    • In a professional setting, teams may encounter external challenges like market competition. The collective effort and energy of the team can be seen as the "vahnayo" that helps them to overcome these hurdles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the divine energies within and around us that can help us overcome life's challenges.
    • It teaches us that by gathering our strengths and focusing our energies, we can confront and conquer the obstacles we face.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, from personal struggles to collective efforts in communities, reminding us of the power of unity and resilience.
