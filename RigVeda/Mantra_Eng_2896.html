इन्द्रः स्वाहा पिबतु यस्य सोम आगत्य तुम्रो वृषभो मरुत्वान् |ओरुव्यचाः पृणताम् एभिर् अन्नैर् आस्य हविस् तन्वः कामम् ऋध्याः ||
indraḥ svāhā pibatu yasya soma āgatyā tumro vṛṣabho marutvān | oruvyacāḥ pṛṇatām ebhir annair āsya havis tanva1ḥ kāmam ṛdhyāḥ ||
  • Indraḥ svāhā: This phrase invokes Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, and "svāhā" is a term used to offer oblations. It signifies respect and reverence towards divine forces.
  • Pibatu yasya soma: "Pibatu" means to drink, and "soma" refers to a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight. This suggests a desire for the blessings and wisdom of the divine.
  • Āgatyā tumro vṛṣabho marutvān: "Āgatyā" means coming or arrival, "tumro" refers to your, "vṛṣabho" means bull or strong one, and "marutvān" relates to the Maruts, the storm deities. This line emphasizes the strength and power of Indra and the Maruts, invoking their presence and assistance.
  • Oruvyacāḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who are strong" or "powerful." It highlights the importance of strength in achieving one's goals.
  • Pṛṇatām ebhir annair: "Pṛṇatām" means to be filled or satisfied, and "ebhir annair" refers to food or nourishment. This line expresses a desire for abundance and fulfillment in life.
  • Āsya havis tanvaḥ kāmam ṛdhyāḥ: "Āsya" means of this, "havis" refers to offerings, "tanvaḥ" means body or form, "kāmam" means desires, and "ṛdhyāḥ" means to be fulfilled. This suggests that through offerings and devotion, one can achieve their desires and aspirations.

  • Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for strength, nourishment, and fulfillment of desires. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as:
    • Personal Growth: Just as one seeks the blessings of Indra for strength, individuals can strive for personal development through education, self-discipline, and resilience.
    • Community Support: The invocation of abundance and nourishment can be reflected in community efforts to support one another, ensuring that everyone has access to resources and opportunities.
    • Spiritual Practices: Regular offerings and rituals can help individuals connect with their spiritual beliefs, fostering a sense of purpose and fulfillment in life.
  • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, nourishment, and the pursuit of desires, encouraging individuals to seek divine guidance in their endeavors.
