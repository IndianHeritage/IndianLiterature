शीरम् पावकशोचिषं ज्येष्ठो यो दमेष्वा | दीदाय दीर्घश्रुत्तमः ||
śīram pāvakaśociṣaṁ jyeṣṭho yo dameṣv ā | dīdāya dīrghaśruttamaḥ ||
  • śīram: This word translates to "head" or "intellect." It signifies the importance of wisdom and knowledge in guiding one's actions.
  • pāvakaśociṣaṁ: This compound word can be broken down into "pāvaka," meaning "pure" or "sacred," and "śociṣaṁ," which relates to "brightness" or "radiance." Together, it emphasizes the idea of a pure and enlightened mind.
  • jyeṣṭho: This term means "eldest" or "most excellent." It suggests a sense of superiority or primacy, often associated with leadership and responsibility.
  • yo: This is a relative pronoun meaning "who" or "that." It connects the qualities of the previous words to the subject being described.
  • dameṣv: This word refers to "in the realms of control" or "in the domains of governance." It indicates the importance of authority and the ability to manage one's environment.
  • ā: A preposition that can mean "towards" or "in the direction of," indicating movement or intention.
  • dīdāya: This term means "to give" or "to bestow." It highlights the act of sharing or distributing knowledge and wisdom.
  • dīrghaśruttamaḥ: This compound word can be broken down into "dīrgha," meaning "long" or "far-reaching," and "śruttamaḥ," which means "the best heard" or "most renowned." Together, it signifies the importance of long-lasting and widely acknowledged wisdom.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for the cultivation of a pure and enlightened intellect, which is essential for effective leadership and governance. It emphasizes the importance of sharing wisdom and knowledge, particularly in positions of authority.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: Leaders in any field, whether in business, politics, or community organizations, can embody the qualities described in this mantra by striving for wisdom and purity of intention in their decision-making processes.
  • Education: Educators can take inspiration from this mantra by fostering an environment of enlightenment and sharing knowledge with their students, ensuring that the wisdom they impart is both profound and accessible.
  • Personal Development: Individuals can apply the principles of this mantra in their personal lives by seeking to cultivate a clear and pure mind, making informed decisions, and sharing their insights with others.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of wisdom, purity, and the responsibility that comes with leadership. It encourages individuals to strive for excellence in their thoughts and actions, ultimately contributing to a more enlightened and harmonious society.
