सुज्योतिषः सूर्य दक्षपितृन् अनागास्त्वे सुमहो वीहि देवान् |द्विजन्मानो य ऋतसापः सत्याः स्वर्वन्तो यजता अग्निजिह्वाः ||
sujyotiṣaḥ sūrya dakṣapitṝn anāgāstve sumaho vīhi devān | dvijanmāno ya ṛtasāpaḥ satyāḥ svarvanto yajatā agnijihvāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sujyotiṣaḥ: Of good light or auspicious brightness.
    • sūrya: Sun, representing energy and life.
    • dakṣapitṝn: To the skilled ancestors or forefathers.
    • anāgāstve: Without sin or fault.
    • sumaho: Great or significant.
    • vīhi: May you bring or bestow.
    • devān: The gods or divine beings.
    • dvijanmāno: Born again or twice-born, often referring to the spiritually awakened.
    • ya: Who.
    • ṛtasāpaḥ: Those who are truthful and adhere to the cosmic order.
    • satyāḥ: Truthful or honest.
    • svarvanto: Those who are radiant or luminous.
    • yajatā: Who worship or perform rituals.
    • agnijihvāḥ: With the tongue of fire, symbolizing the sacred fire or Agni.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the auspicious light of the sun, symbolizing knowledge, energy, and life.
    • It pays homage to the ancestors, recognizing their wisdom and guidance in our lives.
    • The phrase "without sin" emphasizes purity of thought and action, encouraging individuals to lead a virtuous life.
    • By asking the gods to bestow their blessings, it highlights the importance of divine support in our endeavors.
    • The reference to the "twice-born" signifies spiritual awakening and the pursuit of higher knowledge.
    • It encourages adherence to truth and cosmic order, which is essential for harmony in life.
    • The mention of "worship" and "fire" signifies the importance of rituals and the sacredness of offerings in spiritual practices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, one can embody the essence of this mantra by striving for honesty and integrity in personal and professional relationships.
    • Engaging in rituals or practices that honor ancestors, such as family gatherings or remembrance ceremonies, can strengthen familial bonds and respect for heritage.
    • Practicing mindfulness and seeking knowledge can be seen as a modern interpretation of being "twice-born," where individuals continuously evolve and grow spiritually.
    • Incorporating daily affirmations or meditative practices that focus on truth and purity can help individuals align with the cosmic order.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of truth, and the need for divine guidance.
    • Its significance lies in promoting a life of virtue, respect for ancestors, and the pursuit of spiritual awakening.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to lead a life filled with integrity, honor their roots, and seek enlightenment through knowledge and rituals.
