युवं कवī ष्ठः पर्य अश्विनā रथं विश्व न कुत्सो जरितुर नशायथः |युवोर ह मक्षā पर्य अश्विनā मध्व आसā भरत निष्कृतं न योषणा ||
yuvaṁ kavī ṣṭhaḥ pary aśvinā rathaṁ viśo na kutso jaritur naśāyathaḥ | yuvor ha makṣā pary aśvinā madhv āsā bharata niṣkṛtaṁ na yoṣaṇā ||
  • Mantra: "yuvaṁ kavī ṣṭhaḥ pary aśvinā rathaṁ viśo na kutso jaritur naśāyathaḥ | yuvor ha makṣā pary aśvinā madhv āsā bharata niṣkṛtaṁ na yoṣaṇā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yuvaṁ: young, youthful
    • kavī: poet, wise one
    • ṣṭhaḥ: standing, established
    • pary: around, in all directions
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin deities of health and medicine)
    • rathaṁ: chariot
    • viśo: of the people, community
    • na: not
    • kutso: old age
    • jaritur: decay, deterioration
    • naśāyathaḥ: do not let perish
    • yuvor: of the young
    • ha: indeed
    • makṣā: strength, power
    • madhv: sweetness, joy
    • āsā: hope, aspiration
    • bharata: O Bharata (a term often referring to the people or the land)
    • niṣkṛtaṁ: free from sin, purified
    • na: not
    • yoṣaṇā: by women, feminine energy
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the youthful energy and wisdom of the Ashvins, who are often associated with health, vitality, and the rejuvenation of life.
    • The phrase "yuvaṁ kavī ṣṭhaḥ" emphasizes the importance of youth and wisdom working together, suggesting that the vigor of youth should be guided by the insight of experience.
    • The mantra also speaks against the decay of old age ("na kutso jaritur"), highlighting a desire for longevity and vitality, which is a common aspiration in many cultures.
    • The reference to "madhv āsā" indicates a longing for joy and sweetness in life, which can be seen in the pursuit of happiness in everyday experiences, such as family gatherings, celebrations, and personal achievements.
    • Furthermore, the mention of "niṣkṛtaṁ na yoṣaṇā" suggests that the purity and strength of the community are not solely dependent on masculine energy but also on the contributions and roles of women, emphasizing gender equality and the importance of collaboration.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of youth, wisdom, and the balance of energies in society. It encourages individuals to embrace their youthful spirit while being guided by wisdom.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as mentorship programs where experienced individuals guide the younger generation, or in community initiatives that promote health and well-being.
    • Moreover, it highlights the need for inclusivity and the recognition of women's roles in society, which is increasingly relevant in today's discussions about gender equality and empowerment.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to cherish youth, seek wisdom, and foster a harmonious community that values contributions from all its members.
