इन्द्रासोमा वर्तयतं दिवस पर्य अग्नितप्तेभिर युवम अश्महनमभिः |तपुरवधेभिर अजरेभिर अत्रीणो नि पार्शाने विध्यतां यन्तु निस्वरम् ||
indrāsomā vartayataṁ divas pary agnitaptebhir yuvam aśmahanmabhiḥ | tapurvadhebhir ajarebhir atriṇo ni parśāne vidhyataṁ yantu nisvaram ||
  • Indrāsomā: This term refers to the divine entities Indra and Soma. Indra is the king of the gods, associated with rain, storms, and strength, while Soma represents the sacred drink that grants immortality and is linked to the moon.
  • Vartayataṁ: This word means "to flow" or "to move." It signifies the dynamic nature of life and the universe.
  • Divas: This translates to "heaven" or "day," indicating a celestial or divine context.
  • Pary: Meaning "around" or "surrounding," it suggests a sense of encompassing or inclusion.
  • Agnitaptebhir: This term combines "Agni" (fire) and "taptebhir" (heated or warmed), symbolizing the transformative power of fire and its role in purification and energy.
  • Yuvam: This means "you two," referring to the duality of Indra and Soma, emphasizing their partnership.
  • Aśmahanmabhiḥ: This can be interpreted as "with stones" or "with strength," indicating stability and resilience.
  • Tapurvadhebhir: "Tapas" refers to austerity or spiritual discipline, while "vadhebhir" suggests a sense of destruction or overcoming obstacles.
  • Ajarebhir: This term means "immortal" or "eternal," highlighting the quest for longevity and spiritual enlightenment.
  • Atriṇo: Referring to the sage Atri, this signifies wisdom and knowledge.
  • Ni parśāne: "Ni" means "down" or "within," and "parśāne" refers to "the realms" or "the worlds," indicating a deep exploration of existence.
  • Vidhyataṁ: This means "to be established" or "to be created," suggesting the manifestation of ideas or realities.
  • Yantu: This translates to "may they go" or "may they proceed," indicating a desire for progress or movement.
  • Nisvaram: Meaning "without sound" or "silent," it implies a state of peace or tranquility.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of duality, transformation, and the pursuit of spiritual enlightenment. It emphasizes the importance of collaboration between divine forces (Indra and Soma) and the need for resilience (aśmahan) in the face of challenges. In real life, this can be applied to the balance between strength and gentleness, action and contemplation. For instance, in personal development, one might draw on the strength of Indra while also nurturing the introspective qualities of Soma. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek harmony in our lives, embracing both the dynamic and the tranquil aspects of existence.
