शानैश्चिद्यन्तो अद्रिवोऽश्वावन्तः शतग्विनः |विवक्षणा अनहसः ||
śanaiś cid yanto adrivo 'śvāvantaḥ śatagvinaḥ | vivakṣaṇā anehasaḥ ||
  • śanaiś cid yanto: This phrase can be translated as "slowly, with awareness, they move." It emphasizes the importance of taking deliberate and mindful steps in life. In real life, this can be seen in practices like meditation or yoga, where individuals focus on their breath and movements, promoting a sense of calm and clarity.
  • adrivo 'śvāvantaḥ: This part means "like horses moving towards the mountains." Horses are often symbols of strength and freedom. In a practical sense, this can relate to pursuing one's goals with vigor and determination, akin to how horses gallop towards their destination. For example, athletes train rigorously to achieve their dreams, embodying the spirit of 'śvāvantaḥ' as they strive for excellence.
  • śatagvinaḥ: This term translates to "with a hundred powers." It signifies the potential and capabilities that one possesses. In everyday life, this can be reflected in teamwork, where individuals combine their strengths to achieve a common goal. For instance, in a corporate setting, diverse teams bring together various skills and perspectives, enhancing creativity and problem-solving.
  • vivakṣaṇā anehasaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "those who express their intentions clearly." Clear communication is vital in all aspects of life, whether in personal relationships or professional environments. For example, effective leaders articulate their vision to inspire and guide their teams, ensuring everyone is aligned and motivated.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of mindful progress, strength in pursuit, the power of collaboration, and the importance of clear communication. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, teamwork, and leadership. By embodying these values, individuals can navigate life's challenges more effectively and achieve their aspirations with purpose and clarity.
