यः पुरुषेयेण क्रविषा समङ्क्ते यो अश्व्येन पशुना यातुधानः |यो अघ्न्यायाऽभरति क्षीरम अग्ने तेषां शीरषाणि हरसापि वृश्च ||
yaḥ pauruṣeyeṇa kraviṣā samaṅkte yo aśvyena paśunā yātudhānaḥ | yo aghnyāyā bharati kṣīram agne teṣāṁ śīrṣāṇi harasāpi vṛśca ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaḥ: who
    • pauruṣeyeṇa: with human effort
    • kraviṣā: with the strength of the body
    • samaṅkte: who is united
    • yo: who
    • aśvyena: with the help of horses
    • paśunā: with cattle
    • yātudhānaḥ: the one who is a master of the arts
    • yo: who
    • aghnyāyā: with the non-violent
    • bharati: carries
    • kṣīram: milk
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • teṣāṁ: of them
    • śīrṣāṇi: the heads
    • harasāpi: even the ones that are taken away
    • vṛśca: and the strong
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the importance of human effort and unity in achieving success.
    • The reference to "aśvyena" and "paśunā" highlights the significance of animals, particularly horses and cattle, in ancient society, symbolizing strength and sustenance.
    • "Yātudhānaḥ" refers to a skilled individual, suggesting that mastery in one's craft is essential for progress.
    • The mention of "aghnyāyā" indicates a non-violent approach, emphasizing the importance of peace and harmony in one's endeavors.
    • Finally, invoking Agni signifies the need for divine support and guidance in our pursuits.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern agriculture, the collaboration between farmers (human effort) and livestock (cattle) is crucial for productivity.
    • In business, teamwork (unity) among employees can lead to greater innovation and success.
    • Artists and craftsmen (yātudhānaḥ) who master their skills often achieve recognition and success in their fields.
    • In conflict resolution, adopting a non-violent approach (aghnyāyā) can lead to more sustainable and peaceful outcomes.
    • Seeking guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders (Agni) can provide clarity and direction in challenging times.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of human effort, unity, mastery, non-violence, and divine guidance in achieving success.
    • Its teachings are applicable in various aspects of life, including personal development, professional collaboration, and conflict resolution.
    • By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate challenges more effectively and contribute positively to society.
