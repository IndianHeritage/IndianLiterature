यत् त्वा यामि दद्धि तन् न इन्द्र बृहन्तं क्षयम् असमं जनानाम् |अभि तद् dyāvāpṛthivī गृणीताम् अस्मभ्यं चित्रं वृषणं रयिं दाह् ||
yat tvā yāmi daddhi tan na indra bṛhantaṁ kṣayam asamaṁ janānām | abhi tad dyāvāpṛthivī gṛṇītām asmabhyaṁ citraṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ rayiṁ dāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yat: that which
    • tvā: you
    • yāmi: I go
    • daddhi: I give
    • tan: that
    • na: not
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • bṛhantaṁ: great
    • kṣayam: destruction
    • asamaṁ: unequal
    • janānām: of the people
    • abhi: towards
    • tad: that
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: heaven and earth
    • gṛṇītām: may sing
    • asmabhyaṁ: for us
    • citraṁ: wonderful
    • vṛṣaṇaṁ: strong
    • rayiṁ: wealth
    • dāḥ: grant
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer to Indra, the deity associated with strength and rain, asking for blessings and protection.
    • The speaker acknowledges the greatness of Indra and seeks to avoid destruction and inequality among people.
    • It emphasizes the importance of harmony between heaven and earth, suggesting a balance in nature and society.
    • The request for wealth and strength indicates a desire for prosperity and well-being for all.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be reflected in efforts to promote equality and support among its members, ensuring that no one is left behind.
    • In environmental initiatives, the balance between heaven (nature) and earth (human activity) can be seen in sustainable practices that respect both.
    • In personal life, individuals can embody the spirit of this mantra by striving for personal growth while also uplifting those around them.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and prosperity, highlighting the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek balance in our lives and communities, fostering an environment where everyone can thrive.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can inspire social justice movements, environmental stewardship, and personal development, encouraging individuals to contribute positively to society.
