यत् पुरुषेण हविषा देवाः यज्ञं अतन्वत |वसन्तो अस्यासीद् आज्यं ग्रीष्म इध्मः शरद् धविः ||
yat puruṣeṇa haviṣā devā yajñam atanvata | vasanto asyāsīd ājyaṁ grīṣma idhmaḥ śarad dhaviḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yat puruṣeṇa haviṣā devā yajñam atanvata | vasanto asyāsīd ājyaṁ grīṣma idhmaḥ śarad dhaviḥ ||"
  • Translation: "The gods, having been invoked by the offerings made by the Purusha, performed the sacrifice. The seasons, having been established, provided the clarified butter; summer brought the fuel, and autumn brought the offerings."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • yat: that which
    • puruṣeṇa: by the Purusha (the cosmic being or man)
    • haviṣā: with offerings
    • devā: the gods
    • yajñam: the sacrifice
    • atanvata: performed or established
    • vasanta: spring or the season of growth
    • asyāsīd: was present or existed
    • ājyaṁ: clarified butter (ghee)
    • grīṣma: summer
    • idhmaḥ: fuel or wood for the fire
    • śarad: autumn
    • dhaviḥ: offerings or sacrifices

  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of the universe, where the divine, the human, and the natural world collaborate in the act of sacrifice.
    • The Purusha represents the cosmic being, symbolizing humanity's role in the universe. The offerings made to the gods signify the importance of gratitude and recognition of divine forces.
    • The seasons mentioned (spring, summer, autumn) illustrate the cyclical nature of life and the importance of timing in rituals and offerings.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, the changing seasons dictate the timing of planting and harvesting. Just as the mantra suggests, offerings to the gods for a good harvest are common in many cultures, reflecting gratitude for nature's bounty.
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied to the concept of sustainability. Recognizing the interconnectedness of human actions and the environment encourages practices that honor and protect nature, such as eco-friendly farming and conservation efforts.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of harmony between humanity, the divine, and nature. It highlights the significance of rituals and offerings in acknowledging the forces that sustain life.
    • In contemporary society, the principles derived from this mantra can guide individuals and communities towards a more respectful and sustainable relationship with the environment, fostering a sense of responsibility and interconnectedness.
