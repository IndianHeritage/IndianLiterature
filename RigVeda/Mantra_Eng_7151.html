अस्य वृष्णो व्योदन उरु क्रमिष्ट जीवसे |यवं न पश्वा आ ददे ||
asya vṛṣṇo vyodana uru kramiṣṭa jīvase | yavaṁ na paśva ā dade ||
  • Mantra: "asya vṛṣṇo vyodana uru kramiṣṭa jīvase | yavaṁ na paśva ā dade ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asya: of this
    • vṛṣṇo: of Vṛṣṇa (a deity associated with prosperity and abundance)
    • vyodana: the act of giving or bestowing
    • uru: vast or great
    • kramiṣṭa: the one who is to be worshipped or revered
    • jīvase: for life or living
    • yavaṁ: barley or grain
    • na: not
    • paśva: cattle or livestock
    • ā: towards or for
    • dade: to give or to grant
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer or invocation directed towards Vṛṣṇa, seeking blessings for abundance and sustenance.
    • The phrase "asya vṛṣṇo" indicates a connection to the divine, emphasizing the importance of seeking support from higher powers.
    • "Vyodana uru" suggests the vastness of the blessings one seeks, indicating a desire for not just material wealth but also spiritual richness.
    • The mention of "yavaṁ" (barley) signifies nourishment and sustenance, which is essential for life.
    • By stating "na paśva ā dade," the mantra implies a request for abundance that is not limited to livestock but extends to all forms of sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, this mantra can be recited during harvest festivals to invoke blessings for a bountiful crop yield.
    • Individuals seeking prosperity in their careers may chant this mantra to attract opportunities and success.
    • In times of scarcity, families may use this mantra as a form of prayer to seek divine intervention for their needs.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine blessings for sustenance and abundance in life.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of spiritual and material wealth, emphasizing that true prosperity encompasses both.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts, from agriculture to personal aspirations, reinforcing the belief that divine support is essential for achieving one's goals.
