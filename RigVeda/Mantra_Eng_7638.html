सखे विष्णो वितरं वि क्रमस्व द्यौर देहि लोकं वज्राय विष्कभे |हनावा वृतं रिणचावा सिंधून इन्द्रस्य यन्तु प्रसवे विसृष्टाः ||
sakhe viṣṇo vitaraṁ vi kramasva dyaur dehi lokaṁ vajrāya viṣkabhe | hanāva vṛtraṁ riṇacāva sindhūn indrasya yantu prasave visṛṣṭāḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O friend Vishnu, spread your power; grant us the heavens and the world, and let us be strong like the thunderbolt. May we overcome the Vritra and let the rivers flow freely, as Indra, may we be born from the creative force."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Sakhe: O friend
    • Viṣṇo: Vishnu, the preserver and protector
    • Vitaraṁ: Spread or distribute
    • Vi: In or with
    • Kramasva: Move or progress
    • Dyaur: Heaven or sky
    • Dehi: Grant or bestow
    • Lokaṁ: World or realm
    • Vajrāya: Like a thunderbolt
    • Viṣkabhe: Overcome or conquer
    • Hanāva: To defeat or destroy
    • Vṛtraṁ: Vritra, a demon representing obstacles
    • Riṇacāva: Let flow or let go
    • Sindhūn: Rivers
    • Indrasya: Of Indra, the king of gods
    • Yantu: May they go or may they be
    • Prasave: In creation or birth
    • Visṛṣṭāḥ: Released or set free
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of friendship and collaboration, much like how teams in workplaces or sports rely on each other to achieve common goals.
    • The reference to overcoming obstacles (Vritra) can be likened to personal challenges individuals face, such as overcoming fears or achieving career milestones.
    • The imagery of rivers flowing freely can be related to the importance of emotional expression and communication in relationships, allowing for a healthy flow of feelings and thoughts.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in overcoming obstacles and achieving harmony in life. It highlights the interconnectedness of individuals and the need for support from higher powers, as well as from each other. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek collaboration, face challenges with courage, and foster open communication, ultimately leading to personal and communal growth.
