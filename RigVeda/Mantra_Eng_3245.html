सत्राहणं दाधृषिं तुम्रम इन्द्रं महाम अपारं वृषभं सुवज्रम् |हन्ता यो वृत्रं सानितोत वाजं दाता मग्हानि मगवाः सुराधाः ||
satrāhaṇaṁ dādhṛṣiṁ tumram indram mahām apāraṁ vṛṣabhaṁ suvajram | hantā yo vṛtraṁ sanitota vājaṁ dātā maghāni maghavā surādhāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • satrāhaṇaṁ: The one who destroys enemies or obstacles.
    • dādhṛṣiṁ: The one who holds or supports.
    • tumram: The powerful or strong one.
    • indram: Referring to Indra, the king of gods and god of rain and thunderstorms.
    • mahām: Great or immense.
    • apāraṁ: Boundless or infinite.
    • vṛṣabhaṁ: The bull, symbolizing strength and power.
    • suvajram: The one who is well-armed or possesses great strength.
    • hantā: The slayer or destroyer.
    • yo: Who.
    • vṛtraṁ: The demon or obstacle, often representing ignorance or challenges.
    • sanitota: The one who is praised or revered.
    • vājaṁ: Wealth or prosperity.
    • dātā: The giver or bestower.
    • maghāni: Abundance or blessings.
    • maghavā: The powerful one, often referring to Indra.
    • surādhāḥ: The one who is worthy of worship or respect.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the strength and power of Indra, who is seen as a protector and a destroyer of obstacles.
    • It emphasizes the importance of overcoming challenges (vṛtraṁ) in life, which can be interpreted as personal struggles, societal issues, or even existential dilemmas.
    • Indra is depicted as a mighty figure who not only destroys enemies but also supports and provides prosperity (vājaṁ) to his devotees.
    • The mantra can be related to real-life scenarios where individuals face significant challenges, such as overcoming personal fears, societal barriers, or professional obstacles.
    • For example, a student preparing for a competitive exam may feel overwhelmed by the pressure and competition (vṛtraṁ). By invoking the strength of Indra through this mantra, they can find the courage and determination to persevere.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire strength and resilience in the face of adversity.
    • It serves as a reminder that with determination and support, one can overcome any obstacle and achieve prosperity.
    • This mantra can be applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as during challenging times in personal life, professional endeavors, or even in collective societal movements.
    • By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset of empowerment and courage, enabling them to tackle their challenges head-on.
