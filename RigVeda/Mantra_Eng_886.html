सहस्रं साकं अर्चत परि ष्टोभत विंशतिः |शतैनम अन्व अनोनवुर इन्द्राय ब्रह्मोद्यतम अर्चन्न अनु स्वराज्यम ||
sahasraṁ sākam arcata pari ṣṭobhata viṁśatiḥ | śatainam anv anonavur indrāya brahmodyatam arcann anu svarājyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahasraṁ: thousand
    • sākam: together
    • arcata: worshipped
    • pari: around
    • ṣṭobhata: praised
    • viṁśatiḥ: twenty
    • śatainam: hundred
    • anv: following
    • anonavur: not to be neglected
    • indrāya: to Indra
    • brahmodyatam: raised by Brahman
    • arcann: worshipping
    • anu: after
    • svarājyam: sovereignty
  • Detailed Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the collective worship of a thousand beings, emphasizing the importance of unity in devotion.
    • The phrase "sahasraṁ sākam arcata" suggests that when people come together to worship, their collective energy is magnified.
    • The mention of "viṁśatiḥ" and "śatainam" indicates a structured approach to worship, where specific numbers hold significance, reflecting the importance of discipline in spiritual practices.
    • "Indrāya" refers to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength and leadership. This highlights the need for divine guidance in our endeavors.
    • The term "brahmodyatam" signifies that the worship is elevated by the ultimate reality, Brahman, suggesting that true worship transcends mere rituals and connects with the universal consciousness.
    • The phrase "anu svarājyam" implies that through such worship, one can attain sovereignty or mastery over oneself and one's circumstances.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be related to community gatherings for a common cause, such as environmental clean-up drives, where collective effort leads to significant impact.
    • In workplaces, team-building exercises that emphasize collaboration and unity can be seen as a form of "worship" to the goals of the organization, leading to greater success.
    • Spiritual retreats that focus on group meditation or prayer can embody the essence of this mantra, where participants come together to elevate their consciousness and seek guidance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the power of collective worship and the importance of unity in spiritual practices.
    • It teaches that when individuals come together with a common purpose, they can achieve greater heights and connect with the divine.
    • In real life, this principle can be applied in various scenarios, from community service to workplace collaboration, highlighting the significance of teamwork and shared intentions in achieving success and spiritual growth.
