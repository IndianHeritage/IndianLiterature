तदस्येदं पश्यतां भूषितं श्रद इन्द्रस्य धत्तन वीर्याय। स गा अविन्दत सो अविन्दद अश्वान स ओषधिः सो अपः स वनानि॥
tad asyedam paśyatā bhūri puṣṭaṁ śrad indrasya dhattana vīryāya | sa gā avindat so avindad aśvān sa oṣadhīḥ so apaḥ sa vanāni ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • asyedam: this
    • paśyatā: by seeing
    • bhūri: abundant
    • puṣṭaṁ: nourishment
    • śrad: faith
    • indrasya: of Indra (the king of gods)
    • dhattana: bestowing
    • vīryāya: for strength
    • sa: he
    • gā: found
    • avindat: discovered
    • aśvān: horses
    • so: he
    • oṣadhīḥ: herbs
    • so: he
    • apaḥ: waters
    • sa: he
    • vanāni: forests
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine qualities of Indra, who is often associated with strength, power, and abundance.
    • It emphasizes the importance of faith and the act of seeing or perceiving the abundance that surrounds us.
    • Indra is depicted as a provider of nourishment, strength, and resources, symbolizing the blessings that come from a higher power.
    • The mention of horses, herbs, waters, and forests signifies the various elements of nature that contribute to life and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for the blessings of nature, much like invoking Indra for a bountiful harvest. The mantra can be seen as a reminder to appreciate the resources provided by the earth.
    • In personal development, having faith in one's abilities and the support of the universe can lead to discovering one's strengths and potential, akin to how Indra bestows strength.
    • In environmental conservation, recognizing the importance of forests, waters, and herbs reflects the need to protect our natural resources, which are essential for life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the abundance that comes from nature and divine blessings.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate faith and awareness of the resources available to them, fostering a sense of gratitude and responsibility towards the environment.
    • In a broader sense, it highlights the significance of recognizing and honoring the forces that sustain life, urging us to live in harmony with nature.
