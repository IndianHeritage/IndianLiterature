ये चित पूर्व ऋतसाप ऋतावान ऋतावृद्धः |पितृन् तपस्वतो यम तांश्चिदेवापि गच्छतात् ||
ye cit pūrva ṛtasāpa ṛtāvāna ṛtāvṛdhaḥ | pitṝn tapasvato yama tām̐ś cid evāpi gacchatāt ||
  • ye cit pūrva ṛtasāpa ṛtāvāna ṛtāvṛdhaḥ: This phrase can be translated to "Those who are wise and have attained the essence of truth and righteousness." Here, "ye" refers to "those," "cit" means "wise" or "intelligent," "pūrva" indicates "previous" or "ancient," "ṛtasāpa" refers to "the essence of truth," "ṛtāvāna" means "those who possess righteousness," and "ṛtāvṛdhaḥ" signifies "those who have grown in righteousness." This part emphasizes the importance of wisdom and adherence to truth and righteousness in one's life.
  • pitṝn tapasvato yama: This translates to "the ancestors of the ascetics." "Pitṝn" means "ancestors" or "forefathers," "tapasvato" refers to "those who practice austerity," and "yama" signifies "control" or "restraint." This line highlights the significance of honoring and remembering our ancestors, particularly those who have led a life of discipline and self-control.
  • tām̐ś cid evāpi gacchatāt: This can be interpreted as "may they also attain that." "Tām̐ś" refers to "that" or "those," "cid" means "indeed" or "certainly," "evāpi" translates to "also," and "gacchatāt" means "may they go" or "may they reach." This concluding line expresses a wish or prayer that those who embody wisdom and righteousness may also attain the ultimate truth or liberation.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, truth, and righteousness in life. It serves as a reminder to honor our ancestors, especially those who have lived a life of discipline and integrity. The mantra encourages individuals to strive for personal growth in these virtues.
  • Real-life Application: In today's world, this mantra can be applied in various ways:
    • Personal Development: Individuals can reflect on their actions and decisions, striving to live a life rooted in truth and righteousness.
    • Family Values: Honoring ancestors can be practiced through family traditions, remembering their teachings, and passing down values to future generations.
    • Community Leadership: Leaders can embody the principles of wisdom and righteousness, guiding their communities towards ethical practices and decisions.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a profound reminder of the values of wisdom, truth, and respect for our ancestors. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a more ethical and harmonious society, ensuring that the legacy of righteousness continues through generations.
