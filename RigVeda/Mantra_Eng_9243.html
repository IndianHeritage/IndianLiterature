विषं-विषं मगवाऽपर्यशायत जनानां धेनाऽवचाकशद वृषा |यस्याह शक्रः सवनेषु रण्यति स तīv्रैः सोमैः सहते पृथन्यतः ||
viśaṁ-viśam maghavā pary aśāyata janānāṁ dhenā avacākaśad vṛṣā | yasyāha śakraḥ savaneṣu raṇyati sa tīvraiḥ somaiḥ sahate pṛtanyataḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viśaṁ-viśam: The diverse or various beings.
    • maghavā: The powerful or mighty one, often referring to Indra.
    • pary aśāyata: Surrounds or protects.
    • janānāṁ: Of the people or beings.
    • dhenā: The cow, symbolizing nourishment and wealth.
    • avacākaśad: To be protected or safeguarded.
    • vṛṣā: The bull, representing strength and virility.
    • yasyāha: Whose.
    • śakraḥ: Indra, the king of gods.
    • savanṣu: In the offerings or sacrifices.
    • raṇyati: Fights or engages in battle.
    • sa: He or that one.
    • tīvraiḥ: With fierce or intense.
    • somaiḥ: With the offerings of soma, a sacred drink.
    • sahate: Endures or withstands.
    • pṛtanyataḥ: From the earth or the ground.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the protection and nurturing of various beings by a powerful entity, often identified as Indra.
    • The cow and bull symbolize wealth, nourishment, and strength, indicating the importance of these elements in society.
    • Indra's role in engaging in battles and enduring challenges highlights the need for strength and resilience in the face of adversity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the cow is revered for its role in providing milk and sustenance, reflecting the nurturing aspect of the mantra.
    • In modern society, the concept of protecting diverse communities can be seen in social justice movements that advocate for the rights and welfare of all individuals.
    • The idea of enduring challenges resonates with individuals facing personal struggles, emphasizing the importance of resilience and support from the community.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of strength, protection, and nourishment in society.
    • It highlights the role of powerful entities (like leaders or deities) in safeguarding the well-being of diverse communities.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder of the importance of resilience, community support, and the nurturing of all beings for a harmonious existence.
