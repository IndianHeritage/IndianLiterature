मां नो अग्ने सख्यां पितृयाणि प्र मरिष्ठा अभि विदुष कविः सन् |नभो न रूपं जरीमा मिनाति पुरा तस्याभिशस्तेर अधीहि ||
mā no agne sakhyā pitryāṇi pra marṣiṣṭhā abhi viduṣ kaviḥ san | nabho na rūpaṁ jarimā mināti purā tasyā abhiśaster adhīhi ||
  • mā no agne sakhyā pitryāṇi pra marṣiṣṭhā abhi viduṣ kaviḥ san
    • mā no agne: "O Agni, do not harm us." Agni represents the divine fire and is often seen as a mediator between humans and the gods.
    • sakhyā: "as friends." This implies a relationship of trust and companionship with the divine.
    • pitryāṇi: "the ancestors." This refers to the lineage and the respect for those who came before us.
    • pra marṣiṣṭhā: "who are most revered." This highlights the importance of honoring those who have wisdom and experience.
    • abhi viduṣ: "may we know." This expresses a desire for knowledge and understanding.
    • kaviḥ san: "as a poet." This suggests the importance of creativity and expression in understanding the divine.
  • nabho na rūpaṁ jarimā mināti purā tasyā abhiśaster adhīhi
    • nabho: "the sky." This symbolizes the vastness of the universe and the divine presence within it.
    • na rūpaṁ: "without form." This indicates the formless nature of the divine, transcending physical limitations.
    • jarimā mināti: "old age does not diminish." This suggests the eternal nature of the divine, unaffected by time.
    • purā: "in the past." This connects the present with the ancient wisdom and traditions.
    • tasyā: "of that." Referring to the divine or the ultimate reality.
    • abhiśaster adhīhi: "may we understand." This is a plea for insight and comprehension of the divine mysteries.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the relationship between humans and the divine, particularly through the figure of Agni. It calls for protection, wisdom, and understanding, while also acknowledging the importance of ancestors and the eternal nature of the divine. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In moments of uncertainty or fear, invoking Agni can serve as a reminder of the protective forces in our lives.
  • In seeking guidance, one can reflect on the wisdom of ancestors and the importance of learning from past experiences.
  • In creative endeavors, the reference to "kaviḥ san" encourages individuals to express their thoughts and feelings, fostering a deeper connection with the divine.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of seeking knowledge, and the eternal nature of the divine presence in our lives.
