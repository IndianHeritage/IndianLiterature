सोमो वधूयुर अभवद अश्विनास्ताम उभा वरा |सूर्यां यत पत्ये शंसन्तीम् मनसा सविताददात ||
somo vadhūyur abhavad aśvināstām ubhā varā | sūryāṁ yat patye śaṁsantīm manasā savitādadāt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • somo: Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • vadhūyur: The bride or the beloved, symbolizing beauty and grace.
    • abhavad: Became or manifested, indicating a transformation or emergence.
    • aśvināstām: By the Ashvins, the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn.
    • ubhā: Both or together, suggesting a union or partnership.
    • varā: Excellent or auspicious, denoting something of high quality or value.
    • sūryāṁ: The sun, representing light, energy, and life.
    • yat: That which, referring to a specific quality or action.
    • patye: To the husband or protector, indicating a relationship of care and support.
    • śaṁsantīm: Praising or glorifying, suggesting an act of reverence or acknowledgment.
    • manasā: With the mind, indicating intention or thought.
    • savitādadāt: Savita, the sun god, gives or bestows, emphasizing the act of granting or providing.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine connection between the sacred drink Soma and the bride, highlighting the transformative power of love and partnership.
    • The Ashvins, as divine healers, symbolize the importance of health and well-being in relationships.
    • The mention of the sun signifies the life-giving energy that sustains relationships and nurtures growth.
    • The act of praising the husband reflects the importance of mutual respect and admiration in a partnership.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In weddings, couples often partake in rituals that symbolize the union of their lives, akin to the merging of Soma and the bride.
    • Health and wellness practices, such as yoga and meditation, can be seen as modern-day interpretations of the Ashvins' role in promoting well-being.
    • In relationships, expressing gratitude and admiration for one another fosters a deeper connection, similar to the praise mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of love, partnership, and mutual respect in relationships.
    • It highlights the transformative power of divine energy and the importance of health and well-being.
    • In real life, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide individuals in nurturing their relationships, fostering a sense of unity and appreciation.
