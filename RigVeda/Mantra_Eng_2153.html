अध्वर्यवः कर्तना श्रुष्टिम अस्मै वने निपूतं वन उं नयध्वम् |जुषाणो हस्त्यम अभि वावशे व इन्द्राय सोमं मदिरं जुहोत ||
adhvaryavaḥ kartanā śruṣṭim asmai vane nipūtaṁ vana un nayadhvam | juṣāṇo hastyam abhi vāvaśe va indrāya somam madiraṁ juhota ||
  • adhvaryavaḥ: This term refers to the priest or the officiant in a Vedic ritual, particularly in the context of sacrifices. The adhvaryu is responsible for the physical aspects of the ritual.
  • kartanā: This word means 'to create' or 'to perform'. It signifies the act of bringing something into existence or executing a task.
  • śruṣṭim: This translates to 'creation' or 'manifestation'. It indicates the process of bringing forth life or the universe.
  • asmai: This word means 'to him' or 'for him', indicating a direction of devotion or offering towards a deity.
  • vane nipūtaṁ: 'vane' means 'in the forest', and 'nipūtaṁ' refers to 'hidden' or 'concealed'. Together, they suggest a sacred or hidden aspect of nature.
  • vana un nayadhvam: This phrase can be interpreted as 'lead us to the forest' or 'guide us into the wilderness', emphasizing a journey or a quest for knowledge and connection with nature.
  • juṣāṇo: This means 'enjoying' or 'delighting in', indicating a sense of appreciation or reverence.
  • hastyam: This term refers to 'the elephant', which is often a symbol of strength and wisdom in various cultures.
  • abhi vāvaśe: This phrase can be understood as 'may we attain' or 'may we reach', expressing a desire for achievement or fulfillment.
  • va indrāya: 'va' means 'or', and 'indrāya' refers to 'to Indra', the king of gods in Vedic tradition, associated with rain, thunder, and strength.
  • somam: This word refers to 'soma', a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration.
  • madiraṁ: This translates to 'intoxicating' or 'delightful', suggesting the euphoric effects of the soma drink.
  • juhota: This means 'offer' or 'sacrifice', indicating the act of giving something valuable in a ritual context.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of Vedic rituals, emphasizing the importance of creation, nature, and divine connection. It highlights the role of the adhvaryu in performing rituals that honor the gods, particularly Indra, through offerings like soma. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek harmony with nature, appreciate the beauty around them, and engage in acts of gratitude and reverence. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of rituals in fostering a sense of community and spirituality.
