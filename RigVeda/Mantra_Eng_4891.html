मिम्यक्षा येषु रोडसी नु देवी सिषक्ति पूषा अभ्यर्धयज्वा |श्रुत्वा हवम् मरुतो यद धा याथ भूमा रेजन्ते अध्वनि प्रविक्ते ||
mimyakṣa yeṣu rodasī nu devī siṣakti pūṣā abhyardhayajvā | śrutvā havam maruto yad dha yātha bhūmā rejante adhvani pravikte ||
  • mimyakṣa: This term can be interpreted as "to listen" or "to pay attention." It emphasizes the importance of being receptive to divine guidance and wisdom.
  • yeṣu rodasī: "In whom the two worlds (heavens and earth) exist." This phrase signifies the connection between the spiritual and material realms, highlighting the unity of existence.
  • nu devī: "O Goddess!" This invocation acknowledges the divine feminine energy, representing nurturing, wisdom, and power.
  • siṣakti: "You who possess power." This refers to the inherent strength and capability of the divine to influence and guide the world.
  • pūṣā: "Nourisher" or "sustainer." This term signifies the role of the divine in providing sustenance and support to all beings.
  • abhyardhayajvā: "Those who perform the sacrifice." This indicates the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine and seeking blessings.
  • śrutvā havam: "Hearing the invocation." This phrase emphasizes the responsiveness of the divine to the prayers and offerings made by devotees.
  • maruto: "The Maruts," a group of storm deities associated with strength and vitality. They symbolize the forces of nature and the dynamic aspects of life.
  • yad dha: "When indeed." This phrase indicates a condition or a moment of realization or action.
  • yātha bhūmā: "As the earth." This signifies grounding and stability, suggesting that the divine presence is as constant and reliable as the earth itself.
  • rejante: "They shine" or "they illuminate." This indicates the radiance and positive influence of divine energy in the world.
  • adhvani pravikte: "In the path that is revealed." This phrase suggests that the divine guides individuals along their life journeys, illuminating the way forward.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of divine connection, the importance of rituals, and the nurturing aspect of the divine. It signifies that by paying attention to the divine and engaging in acts of devotion, individuals can receive guidance and sustenance in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied through practices such as meditation, prayer, and community rituals that foster a sense of connection to the divine and to each other. The mantra encourages individuals to recognize the divine presence in their lives, seek guidance, and act with intention, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
