इळाम अग्ने पुरुदंसं सनिं गोः शश्वत्तमं हवमानाय साधा |स्यान नः सूनुस तनयो विजावाग्ने सा ते सुमतिर भूत्व अस्मे ||
iḻām agne purudaṁsaṁ saniṁ goḥ śaśvattamaṁ havamānāya sādha | syān naḥ sūnus tanayo vijāvāgne sā te sumatir bhūtv asme ||
  • iḻām agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni," referring to the Vedic fire god, Agni, who symbolizes energy, light, and the divine messenger between humans and the gods.
  • purudaṁsaṁ: This means "of great glory" or "of great fame," indicating the revered status of Agni in Vedic rituals and his importance in the cosmic order.
  • saniṁ goḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is the protector of the cattle," highlighting Agni's role in safeguarding wealth and prosperity, often represented by cattle in ancient societies.
  • śaśvattamaṁ: This means "the eternal" or "the most enduring," emphasizing the timeless nature of Agni and his perpetual presence in rituals and life.
  • havamānāya: This translates to "to the one who is invoked," indicating the act of calling upon Agni during sacrifices and prayers.
  • sādha: This means "to fulfill" or "to accomplish," suggesting the intention behind the invocation of Agni for achieving specific goals or desires.
  • syān naḥ sūnus: This phrase means "may he be our son," expressing a desire for Agni to bless the devotee's lineage and descendants.
  • tanayo vijāvāgne: This translates to "the one who is wise," indicating the aspiration for wisdom and intelligence to be bestowed upon the family through Agni's blessings.
  • sā te sumatir bhūtv asme: This means "may we have good thoughts from you," highlighting the importance of positive thinking and wisdom in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of Agni as a divine force that brings prosperity, wisdom, and protection to one's family and lineage. In real-life scenarios, invoking Agni can be seen as a metaphor for seeking guidance and enlightenment in our endeavors. Just as Agni is central to Vedic rituals, we can incorporate the essence of this mantra into our daily lives by fostering positive thoughts, seeking wisdom, and striving for accomplishments that benefit not only ourselves but also our families and communities. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of divine blessings, and the pursuit of a meaningful existence.
