अचिक्रदद वृषा हरिर महान मित्रो न दर्षतः |सं सूर्येण रोचते ||
acikradad vṛṣā harir mahān mitro na darśataḥ | saṁ sūryeṇa rocate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • acikradad: The one who has cried out or called out.
    • vṛṣā: The strong or powerful one.
    • harir: The divine or the one who brings joy.
    • mahān: The great or the mighty.
    • mitro: The friend or ally.
    • na: Not.
    • darśataḥ: Seen or perceived.
    • saṁ: Together or in union.
    • sūryeṇa: With the sun or by the sun.
    • rocate: Shines or radiates.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful entity that calls out but is not seen, suggesting a presence that is felt rather than visually perceived.
    • The term "harir" indicates a divine quality, suggesting that this entity brings joy and positivity, much like the sun brings light and warmth.
    • The phrase "mitro na darśataḥ" implies that true friendship or connection may not always be visible, yet it exists in a profound way.
    • The concluding part, "saṁ sūryeṇa rocate," emphasizes the idea of unity with the sun, symbolizing enlightenment, energy, and life force.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In our daily lives, we often feel the presence of supportive friends or family members who may not always be physically present but whose influence and support are felt deeply.
    • The sun can be seen as a metaphor for positivity and hope; just as the sun rises every day, bringing light and warmth, we can find joy in the presence of those who uplift us.
    • In moments of challenge, the unseen strength we draw from our beliefs, values, or loved ones can be likened to the unseen forces that guide us, much like the sun's energy that sustains life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unseen forces in our lives, whether they be divine, emotional, or social.
    • It teaches us to recognize and appreciate the intangible connections we have with others and the world around us.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to cultivate relationships and values that may not always be visible but are essential for our well-being and growth.
    • Ultimately, it reminds us that true strength and joy often come from within and from the connections we nurture, much like the sun that shines regardless of whether we can see it at all times.
