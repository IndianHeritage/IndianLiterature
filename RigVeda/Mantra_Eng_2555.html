अग्नि यजिष्ठो अध्वरि देवान देवयते यज |होता मन्द्रो वि राजस्य अति श्रिधः ||
agne yajiṣṭho adhvare devān devayate yaja | hotā mandro vi rājasy ati sridhaḥ ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic tradition. Fire is often seen as a purifying force and a medium for offerings to the deities.
  • Yajiṣṭho: This term means "most worshipful" or "the one who is to be worshipped." It emphasizes the importance of Agni in rituals and sacrifices.
  • Adhvare: This word signifies a ritual or sacrifice. It indicates the context in which Agni is being invoked.
  • Devān: This means "the gods." It highlights the connection between the worshipper and the divine beings being invoked.
  • Devayate: This term translates to "for the gods" or "to the divine." It indicates the purpose of the worship and offerings.
  • Yaja: This means "to worship" or "to offer." It is an action word that signifies the act of performing the ritual.
  • Hotā: This refers to the priest or the one who offers the oblations. It emphasizes the role of the human intermediary in the ritual.
  • Mandro: This word can mean "sweet" or "pleasant." It suggests that the offerings made are to be pleasing to the gods.
  • Vi: This is a preposition meaning "in" or "with." It connects the action of worship to the divine.
  • Rājasy: This term means "of the king" or "royal." It indicates the importance of the ritual in the context of leadership and governance.
  • Ati: This means "exceedingly" or "very." It emphasizes the intensity or significance of the action being performed.
  • Sridhaḥ: This word translates to "prosperous" or "wealthy." It indicates the desired outcome of the worship and offerings.

Conclusion: The mantra "agne yajiṣṭho adhvare devān devayate yaja | hotā mandro vi rājasy ati sridhaḥ ||" encapsulates the essence of Vedic rituals where Agni is invoked as a central figure in worship. It signifies the importance of performing rituals with devotion and the intention of pleasing the divine. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during significant life events like weddings, housewarming ceremonies, or any occasion where blessings are sought. The act of worship, whether through fire rituals or other forms, serves to connect individuals with higher powers, fostering a sense of community, purpose, and prosperity. By understanding and applying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a deeper spiritual connection and invite positive energies into their lives.
