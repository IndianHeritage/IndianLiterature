यā ते अष्ट्रā गूपशाघृणे पशुसाधनी |तस्यास ते सुम्नम ईमहे ||
yā te aṣṭrā goopaśāghṛṇe paśusādhanī | tasyās te sumnam īmahe ||
  • yā te - "that which is yours" or "that which belongs to you"
  • aṣṭrā - "weapons" or "instruments of power"
  • goopaśāghṛṇe - "to the protector of cattle" or "to the one who safeguards the wealth"
  • paśusādhanī - "the means of livelihood" or "the source of sustenance"
  • tasyās - "of that" or "of it"
  • te - "to you" or "for you"
  • sumnam - "auspiciousness" or "well-being"
  • īmahe - "we invoke" or "we seek"

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation directed towards a divine entity or force that is associated with protection, prosperity, and sustenance. The speaker acknowledges the importance of the divine in safeguarding their resources, particularly livestock, which is often a symbol of wealth and livelihood in agrarian societies.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Agriculture and Livestock Management: Farmers can recite this mantra to seek blessings for the health and protection of their animals, which are crucial for their livelihood.
  • Business and Wealth Protection: Entrepreneurs may use this mantra to invoke prosperity and protection over their business ventures, ensuring that their resources are safeguarded.
  • Community Welfare: In a broader sense, communities can come together to recite this mantra to seek collective well-being and protection of their shared resources.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the divine's role in providing protection and prosperity. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness between human efforts and divine blessings. By invoking auspiciousness, individuals and communities can foster a sense of security and abundance in their lives, making it applicable in various real-life scenarios, from agriculture to business and community welfare.
