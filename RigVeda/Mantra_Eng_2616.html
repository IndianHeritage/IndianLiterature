भूरिणि हि त्वे दधिरे अनीकाग्ने देवस्य यज्यवो जनासः |स आ वह देवतातिं यविष्ठ शर्धो यद अद्य दिव्यं यजासि ||
bhūrīṇi hi tve dadhire anīkāgne devasya yajyavo janāsaḥ | sa ā vaha devatātiṁ yaviṣṭha śardho yad adya divyaṁ yajāsi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bhūrīṇi: Abundant, plentiful
    • hi: Indeed
    • tve: You
    • dadhire: Have established
    • anīkāgne: In the form of the divine fire
    • devasya: Of the deity
    • yajyavo: Those who perform sacrifices
    • janāsaḥ: The people
    • sa: He
    • ā: May
    • vaha: Carry
    • devatātiṁ: The divine essence
    • yaviṣṭha: The most excellent
    • śardho: In the season
    • yad: That
    • adya: Today
    • divyaṁ: Divine
    • yajāsi: You are worshipping
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the abundance and establishment of divine energy through the act of worship and sacrifice.
    • The phrase "bhūrīṇi hi tve dadhire" emphasizes the idea that the divine has provided plentiful resources and blessings to those who engage in spiritual practices.
    • The reference to "anīkāgne" suggests the importance of fire in rituals, symbolizing transformation and purification.
    • "Devasyā yajyavo janāsaḥ" indicates that the people who perform these sacrifices are in communion with the divine, highlighting the collective aspect of worship.
    • The mantra culminates in a call to recognize the divine presence in the act of worship, particularly in the context of the current time ("adya") and season ("śardho").
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, community gatherings for festivals can be seen as a form of collective worship, where people come together to celebrate and express gratitude for the abundance in their lives.
    • Rituals such as lighting candles or bonfires during festivals symbolize the divine fire and the transformation it brings, akin to the "anīkāgne" mentioned in the mantra.
    • Practices like meditation and prayer can be viewed as personal sacrifices that connect individuals to a higher power, reflecting the essence of "yajyavo" in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and honoring the divine presence in our lives through acts of worship and community.
    • It teaches us that by engaging in spiritual practices, we can tap into the abundant blessings that the universe offers.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages us to foster a sense of community, gratitude, and connection to the divine, reminding us that our actions can have a profound impact on our lives and the lives of others.
