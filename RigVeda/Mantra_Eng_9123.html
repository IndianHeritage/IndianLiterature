प्र वो 'च्छा रिरिचे देवयुष पदम् एको रुद्रभिर याति तुर्वणिः |जरा वा येष्व अमृतेषु दावने परि वा ऊमेभ्यः सिञ्चतामधु ||
pra vo 'cchā ririce devayuṣ padam eko rudrebhir yāti turvaṇiḥ | jarā vā yeṣv amṛteṣu dāvane pari va ūmebhyaḥ siñcatā madhu ||
  • Translation: "May we, with our prayers, reach the divine abode of the gods, where the Rudras (the storm deities) reside. Those who are free from old age and death, may they pour forth sweetness upon us like honey."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra vo: A call to the divine, seeking attention and blessings.
    • 'cchā: To reach or attain.
    • ririce: To be nourished or fulfilled.
    • devayuṣ: The abode of the gods.
    • padam: A place or position.
    • eko: One or singular.
    • rudrebhir: By the Rudras, the storm deities.
    • yāti: To go or to move towards.
    • turvaṇiḥ: A term often associated with strength or power.
    • jarā: Old age.
    • vā: Or.
    • yeṣv: Those who are in.
    • amṛteṣu: In the realm of immortality.
    • dāvane: In the context of fire or burning.
    • pari: Around or about.
    • va: And.
    • ūmebhyaḥ: From the divine or celestial beings.
    • siñcatā: Pouring forth or bestowing.
    • madhu: Sweetness or honey.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra can be seen as a prayer for guidance and support in times of difficulty, similar to how individuals seek help from mentors or spiritual leaders when facing challenges.
    • The reference to "honey" symbolizes the sweetness of life and the blessings we seek, akin to how people celebrate achievements with sweet treats, representing joy and fulfillment.
    • In a modern context, the idea of reaching a divine abode can be compared to striving for personal goals, such as career aspirations or personal growth, where one seeks to attain a higher state of being or success.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine blessings and the pursuit of a life free from the burdens of aging and mortality. It emphasizes the importance of seeking spiritual nourishment and the sweetness of life, which can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as personal development, community support, and the quest for happiness. By invoking the divine and acknowledging the power of the Rudras, individuals can find strength and resilience in their journeys, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
