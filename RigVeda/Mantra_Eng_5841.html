क्व त्यानी नौ सख्याऽबभूवुः सचावहे यद अवृकम् पुरा चित् | बृहन्तं मानं वरुण स्वधावः सहस्रद्वारं जगाम गृहम् ते ||
kva1 tyāni nau sakhyā babhūvuḥ sacāvahe yad avṛkam purā cit | bṛhantam mānaṁ varuṇa svadhāvaḥ sahasradvāraṁ jagamā gṛhaṁ te ||
  • kva1 tyāni nau sakhyā babhūvuḥ: This phrase can be translated to "Where are those friendships that we have formed?" It reflects on the importance of relationships and camaraderie in our lives. In real life, this can be seen in the bonds we create with friends, family, and colleagues, emphasizing the value of support systems.
  • sacāvahe yad avṛkam purā cit: This part means "And those that have been gathered together in unity." It signifies the strength found in unity and collaboration. For example, in a workplace, when team members come together to achieve a common goal, they can accomplish much more than they could individually.
  • bṛhantam mānaṁ varuṇa svadhāvaḥ: This translates to "The great measure of Varuna, the lord of cosmic order." Varuna represents the moral and cosmic order in the universe. In our lives, this can be related to the principles of justice and ethics that guide our actions and decisions, such as fairness in business practices or honesty in personal relationships.
  • sahasradvāraṁ jagamā gṛhaṁ te: This means "Your house has a thousand doors." This metaphor suggests that there are numerous opportunities and paths available to us. In practical terms, it can be seen in the various career options one might explore or the different ways to approach a problem in life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of friendships, unity, moral order, and the multitude of opportunities in life. It teaches us to value our relationships, work collaboratively, uphold ethical standards, and remain open to the various paths that life presents. In real-life scenarios, applying these principles can lead to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence, whether in personal relationships, professional environments, or community interactions.
