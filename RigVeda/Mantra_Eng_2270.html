विद्याम आदित्याऽवसो वोऽस्य यद् आर्यमन् भयाच्छिन्मयभूः। युष्माकं मित्रावरुणा प्रणीतौ परि स्वभ्रेव दुरितानि वृत्यां॥
vidyām ādityā avaso vo asya yad aryaman bhaya ā cin mayobhu | yuṣmākam mitrāvaruṇā praṇītau pari śvabhreva duritāni vṛjyām ||
  • Vidyām ādityā avaso vo asya: This phrase can be translated to "Let the knowledge of the sun (Aditya) be with you." Here, "Vidyām" refers to knowledge or wisdom, and "Aditya" symbolizes the sun, which is often associated with enlightenment and clarity.
  • Yad aryaman bhaya ā cin mayobhu: This translates to "Whatever is fearful, may it be removed from us." "Aryaman" refers to a divine being or a protector, while "bhaya" means fear. This line emphasizes the desire to be free from fear and negativity, seeking protection from higher powers.
  • Yuṣmākam mitrāvaruṇā praṇītau: This means "May the divine friends, Mitra and Varuna, guide us." Mitra and Varuna are deities representing friendship and cosmic order, respectively. This line highlights the importance of divine guidance in our lives.
  • Pari śvabhreva duritāni vṛjyām: This translates to "May we be freed from all misfortunes." "Duritāni" refers to misfortunes or obstacles, and "vṛjyām" means to be removed or avoided. This line expresses a wish for liberation from difficulties and challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the pursuit of knowledge, the removal of fear, and the importance of divine guidance in overcoming life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as education, where the quest for knowledge is paramount; in personal development, where overcoming fears is essential for growth; and in seeking support from friends and mentors who can guide us through difficult times. The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, encouraging individuals to seek wisdom, embrace positivity, and rely on divine or supportive forces to navigate through adversities.
