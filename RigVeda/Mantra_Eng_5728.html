यो वां यज्ञो नासत्याः हविष्मान् कृतब्रह्मा समर्यो भवाति | उप प्र यातं वरम् आवसिष्ठम् इमा ब्रह्माण्य ऋच्यन्ते युवभ्यां ||
yo vāṁ yajño nāsatyā haviṣmān kṛtabrahmā samaryo3 bhavāti | upa pra yātaṁ varam ā vasiṣṭham imā brahmāṇy ṛcyante yuvabhyām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • vāṁ: your
    • yajño: sacrifice or worship
    • nāsatyā: of the Asvins (the twin deities)
    • haviṣmān: the one who is worthy of offerings
    • kṛtabrahmā: having performed the sacred act
    • samaryo: becomes united or harmonized
    • bhavāti: becomes
    • upa: towards
    • pra: forth
    • yātaṁ: the one who has gone
    • varam: the best or the most excellent
    • ā: from
    • vasiṣṭham: the most excellent or supreme
    • imā: these
    • brahmāṇy: the sacred or divine
    • ṛcyante: are invoked or recited
    • yuvabhyām: by the two young ones (the Asvins)
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the importance of sacrifice and the divine connection between humans and the deities, particularly the Asvins, who are known for their healing and protective qualities.
    • The phrase "yo vāṁ yajño nāsatyā" emphasizes the idea that the sacrifices made by individuals are recognized and accepted by the divine, particularly by the Asvins.
    • The term "haviṣmān" refers to the offerings that are made, which are considered sacred and worthy of the gods.
    • "Kṛtabrahmā" indicates that through these acts of worship, individuals achieve a state of spiritual fulfillment and harmony.
    • The mantra suggests that by performing these sacred acts, one can attain the best outcomes in life, symbolized by "varam ā vasiṣṭham."
    • The invocation of the Asvins, "yuvabhyām," highlights the youthful energy and vitality that they bring, which can be seen in real life as the rejuvenating aspects of nature and health.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the practice of mindfulness and gratitude. Just as sacrifices were made in ancient times, today, people can offer their time and energy to help others, which can be seen as a form of modern-day sacrifice.
    • Community service, volunteering, and acts of kindness can be viewed as contemporary yajñas (sacrifices) that bring about harmony and unity in society.
    • In personal development, the idea of "kṛtabrahmā" can be applied to the pursuit of goals and aspirations, where individuals work diligently towards their objectives, leading to personal fulfillment and success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of connecting with the divine through acts of sacrifice and worship, which leads to personal and communal harmony.
    • It teaches that through our actions, we can invoke divine blessings and achieve the best outcomes in our lives.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to engage in selfless acts that benefit others, fostering a sense of community and interconnectedness.
