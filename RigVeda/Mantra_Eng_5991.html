सोता हि सोमं अद्रिभिर एषं अप्सु धावता |गव्यā वस्त्रेवा वासयन्त इन नरो निर धुक्षन् वक्षाणाभ्यः ||
sotā hi somam adribhir em enam apsu dhāvata | gavyā vastreva vāsayanta in naro nir dhukṣan vakṣaṇābhyaḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "The one who drinks the Soma, like a river flowing, is carried by the waters; like a cow, they are clothed in the garments of the world, and men are nourished by the strength of their hearts."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sotā: One who drinks or consumes.
    • hi: Indeed or certainly.
    • somam: Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
    • adribhir: By the mountains or hills.
    • em: This or that.
    • enam: Him or it.
    • apsu: In the waters.
    • dhāvata: Flowing or running.
    • gavyā: Like a cow, symbolizing nourishment and sustenance.
    • vastreva: Like a garment or covering.
    • vāsayanta: Clothed or adorned.
    • in: In or within.
    • naro: Men or humans.
    • nir: Without or free from.
    • dhukṣan: Strength or power.
    • vakṣaṇābhyaḥ: From the hearts or chests.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of consuming knowledge and inspiration (symbolized by Soma) for personal growth and nourishment.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of nature and humanity, suggesting that just as rivers flow and nourish the land, so too should individuals seek to nourish their minds and spirits.
    • The imagery of being clothed like a cow suggests that sustenance comes from both physical and spiritual nourishment, indicating a holistic approach to life.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom. Just as one consumes food for physical health, one should also seek out experiences and education for mental and spiritual well-being.
    • In a corporate setting, this can translate to the importance of continuous learning and development, where employees are encouraged to "drink the Soma" of knowledge to enhance their skills and contribute to the organization.
    • On a personal level, it encourages individuals to engage with nature and community, recognizing that our well-being is tied to the health of our environment and relationships.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the vital role that knowledge, nature, and community play in our lives. By embracing these elements, we can achieve a balanced and fulfilling existence, much like the harmonious flow of a river nourishing the land.
