गृणे तद इन्द्र ते शव उपमं देवतातये |यद धंसि वृत्रं ओजसा शचीपते भद्राः इन्द्रस्य रातयः ||
gṛṇe tad indra te śava upamaṁ devatātaye | yad dhaṁsi vṛtram ojasā śacīpate bhadrā indrasya rātayaḥ ||
  • gṛṇe: This word translates to "I sing" or "I praise." It signifies the act of devotion and reverence towards the divine.
  • tad: Meaning "that," it refers to the divine qualities or actions being praised.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and strength. He represents power and authority.
  • te: This means "to you," indicating a direct address to Indra.
  • śava: Translates to "the body" or "the form," often referring to the physical manifestation of strength or valor.
  • upamaṁ: This means "like" or "similar," suggesting a comparison to something great or powerful.
  • devatātaye: This term refers to "the divine nature" or "the essence of the gods," emphasizing the spiritual aspect of the praise.
  • yad: Meaning "that which," it introduces a clause that describes an action or quality.
  • dhaṁsi: This translates to "you have crushed," indicating a powerful action taken by Indra against adversaries.
  • vṛtram: Refers to "Vritra," a serpent or dragon-like demon representing chaos and drought, which Indra defeats.
  • ojasā: Meaning "with strength" or "with vigor," it highlights the power behind Indra's actions.
  • śacīpate: This term means "O Lord of Strength," acknowledging Indra's might and authority.
  • bhadrā: Translates to "auspicious" or "good," indicating the positive outcomes of Indra's actions.
  • indrasya: Meaning "of Indra," it connects the qualities and actions back to the deity.
  • rātayaḥ: This means "the realms" or "the domains," referring to the various aspects of life and nature that Indra governs.

The mantra essentially praises Indra for his strength and valor, particularly in overcoming chaos and bringing order and prosperity. It acknowledges his divine nature and the positive impact of his actions on the world.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra is a powerful leader among the gods, individuals in positions of authority can draw inspiration from this mantra to lead with strength and wisdom.
  • Overcoming Challenges: The act of crushing Vritra symbolizes overcoming obstacles. In personal or professional life, one can invoke this mantra to find the strength to face and conquer challenges.
  • Community Welfare: The auspicious outcomes of Indra's actions can inspire efforts towards community service and welfare, promoting harmony and prosperity.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of strength, leadership, and the pursuit of auspicious outcomes in our lives. It encourages individuals to embody the qualities of Indra, striving for excellence and overcoming adversities for the greater good.
