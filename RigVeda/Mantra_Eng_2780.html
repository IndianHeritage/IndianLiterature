उपाजीराऽपुरुहूताय सप्तī हरिष रथस्य धूर्ष्व ā युनज्मि |द्रवद यथा संभृतं विश्वतश्चिदुपेमं यज्ञमावहात इन्द्रम् ||
upājirā puruhūtāya saptī harī rathasya dhūrṣv ā yunajmi | dravad yathā sambhṛtaṁ viśvataś cid upemaṁ yajñam ā vahāta indram ||
  • upājirā puruhūtāya: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the divine, specifically to the deity who is invoked in various forms. "Puruhūtāya" refers to the one who is worshipped in many ways, highlighting the multifaceted nature of divinity.
  • saptī harī rathasya: "Saptī" means seven, and "harī" refers to the divine or the auspicious. "Rathasya" means chariot. This part of the mantra suggests the presence of a divine chariot that is guided by seven auspicious forces, which can be seen as representing the seven days of the week or the seven colors of the rainbow, symbolizing completeness and harmony.
  • dhūrṣv ā yunajmi: This phrase translates to "I unite with the powerful." It signifies the act of connecting with strength and energy, which can be interpreted as a call for empowerment and support in one's endeavors.
  • dravad yathā sambhṛtaṁ: "Dravad" refers to fluidity or movement, while "sambhṛtaṁ" means gathered or collected. This suggests the idea of gathering resources or energies in a fluid manner, akin to how water collects and flows, adapting to its surroundings.
  • viśvataś cid upemaṁ: "Viśvataś" means from all directions, and "cid" refers to consciousness or awareness. "Upemaṁ" can be interpreted as a comparison or a metaphor. This part of the mantra emphasizes the importance of being aware and open to all possibilities and perspectives, suggesting a holistic approach to understanding life.
  • yajñam ā vahāta indram: "Yajñam" refers to sacrifice or offering, and "ā vahāta" means to carry or bring forth. "Indram" refers to Indra, the king of the gods. This closing phrase signifies the importance of making offerings to the divine, suggesting that through our actions and sacrifices, we can invoke the blessings of higher powers.

  • Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and empowerment through a holistic approach to life. It emphasizes the importance of connecting with the divine in various forms, gathering resources fluidly, and being open to all perspectives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in personal development, decision-making, and community building. By recognizing the multifaceted nature of divinity and the interconnectedness of all things, individuals can navigate challenges with strength and awareness, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
