मन्त्र: चरुर न यस तं ईङ्खयन्दो न दानम् ईङ्खय | वधैर वधस्नव ईङ्खय ||
carur na yas tam īṅkhayendo na dānam īṅkhaya | vadhair vadhasnav īṅkhaya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • carur: beautiful, good, or auspicious
    • na: not
    • yas: who
    • tam: that
    • īṅkhayendo: may not be harmed or destroyed
    • na: not
    • dānam: gift or donation
    • īṅkhaya: may not be taken away
    • vadhair: by killing or destruction
    • vadhasnav: may not be killed or destroyed
    • īṅkhaya: may not be taken away
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for protection and preservation of goodness and beauty.
    • It emphasizes the importance of safeguarding what is good and valuable in life from harm and destruction.
    • The repetition of "īṅkhaya" signifies a strong desire for the preservation of these qualities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal relationships, this mantra can be applied to the protection of love and trust. For instance, a couple may seek to safeguard their relationship from misunderstandings and external influences.
    • In a community context, it can represent the collective effort to protect cultural heritage and values from being lost or destroyed.
    • In environmental terms, it can be a call to protect nature and biodiversity, ensuring that beautiful landscapes and ecosystems are preserved for future generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for the protection of goodness and beauty in various aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder to actively engage in preserving what is valuable, whether it be relationships, cultural heritage, or the environment.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals and communities can foster a mindset of protection and appreciation for the positive elements in their lives.
