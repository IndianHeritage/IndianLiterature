त्रयः कृत्वन्ति भुवनेषु रेतस तिस्रः प्रजा आर्याः ज्योतिर्ग्राहः |त्रयो घर्मास उषसं साचन्ते सर्वां इति तां अनु विदुर वसिष्ठाः ||
trayaḥ kṛṇvanti bhuvaneṣu retas tisraḥ prajā āryā jyotiragrāḥ | trayo gharmāsa uṣasaṁ sacante sarvām̐ it tām̐ anu vidur vasiṣṭhāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "trayaḥ kṛṇvanti bhuvaneṣu retas tisraḥ prajā āryā jyotiragrāḥ | trayo gharmāsa uṣasaṁ sacante sarvām̐ it tām̐ anu vidur vasiṣṭhāḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • trayaḥ: three
    • kṛṇvanti: create or bring forth
    • bhuvaneṣu: in the worlds or realms
    • retas: essence or energy
    • tisraḥ: three
    • prajā: beings or progeny
    • āryā: noble or honorable
    • jyotiragrāḥ: those who are enlightened or possess light
    • trayo: three
    • gharmāsa: warmth or heat
    • uṣasaṁ: dawn or light
    • sacante: they bring forth
    • sarvām̐: all
    • it: thus
    • tām̐: that
    • anu: following or after
    • vidur: they know or understand
    • vasiṣṭhāḥ: the best or most excellent
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the creation and nurturing of life across different realms.
    • The "three" mentioned can symbolize various triads in life, such as the past, present, and future, or the mind, body, and spirit.
    • The term "retas" refers to the vital energy or essence that is essential for creation, akin to the energy we derive from food, relationships, and experiences.
    • "Prajā āryā" emphasizes the importance of noble beings, suggesting that the quality of life is enhanced by virtuous actions and thoughts.
    • The phrase "jyotiragrāḥ" indicates that those who are enlightened or possess knowledge are crucial in guiding others, similar to how mentors or teachers illuminate the path for their students.
    • The reference to "uṣasaṁ" and "gharmāsa" signifies the dawn and warmth, representing new beginnings and the nurturing aspect of life, much like how the sun provides warmth and sustenance to all living beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, the collaboration of individuals (the "three") can lead to the creation of a supportive environment, much like how families work together to nurture children.
    • In personal development, understanding the balance of mind, body, and spirit can lead to a more fulfilling life.
    • Teachers and mentors play a vital role in shaping the future of their students, akin to the "jyotiragrāḥ" guiding the "prajā."
    • New ventures or projects often begin with a "dawn" of ideas, where the warmth of collaboration fosters growth and innovation.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of life and the importance of noble actions and enlightened guidance in creating a thriving existence.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that we are all part of a larger whole, and our contributions can lead to the betterment of society.
    • In real life, applying the principles of collaboration, enlightenment, and nurturing can lead to personal growth and community development.
