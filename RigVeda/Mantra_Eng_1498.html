अति वायो ससतो याहि शाश्वतो यत्र ग्रावाऽवदति तत्र गच्छतं गृहं इन्द्रश्च गच्छताम् |वि सूनृताऽददृशे रीयते घृतम् आ पूर्णया नियुतā याथो अध्वरम् इन्द्रश्च याथो अध्वरम् ||
ati vāyo sasato yāhi śaśvato yatra grāvā vadati tatra gacchataṁ gṛham indraś ca gacchatam | vi sūnṛtā dadṛśe rīyate ghṛtam ā pūrṇayā niyutā yātho adhvaram indraś ca yātho adhvaram ||
  • ati vāyo sasato yāhi: This phrase can be translated as "O Wind, come here swiftly." In this context, the wind symbolizes the vital force or energy that drives life and movement. In real life, we can relate this to the importance of harnessing energy, whether it be through physical activity, motivation, or even the use of renewable energy sources like wind power.
  • śaśvato yatra grāvā vadati: This means "where the voice of the community is heard." It emphasizes the significance of collective wisdom and the importance of community in decision-making. In modern society, this can be seen in community meetings, local governance, or even social media platforms where voices are amplified.
  • tatra gacchataṁ gṛham indraś ca gacchatam: Translated, this means "there, let us go to the home of Indra." Indra is often regarded as the king of the gods and a symbol of strength and power. This can be interpreted as a call to seek strength and guidance from higher powers or leaders in our lives, whether they be spiritual, familial, or societal.
  • vi sūnṛtā dadṛśe: This phrase translates to "with truth, I have seen." It highlights the importance of truth and integrity in our actions and perceptions. In everyday life, this can be reflected in the value of honesty in relationships, business dealings, and personal conduct.
  • rīyate ghṛtam ā pūrṇayā niyutā: This means "the clarified butter is made full." Clarified butter (ghee) is often used in rituals and signifies purity and nourishment. In a practical sense, this can represent the idea of nurturing oneself and others, whether through food, care, or emotional support.
  • yātho adhvaram indraś ca yātho adhvaram: This translates to "where the sacrifice is made, there Indra also goes." This emphasizes the importance of sacrifice and effort in achieving goals. In real life, this can be seen in the dedication required in various fields, such as education, career, and personal development, where sacrifices often lead to greater rewards.

  • Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking energy, community, strength, truth, nourishment, and the importance of sacrifice. Its significance lies in the holistic approach it advocates for living a balanced and purposeful life. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional journeys, encouraging them to harness their energies, value community input, seek guidance, uphold truth, nurture themselves and others, and recognize the importance of sacrifices in achieving their aspirations.
