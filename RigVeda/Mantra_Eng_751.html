अत्यो नाज्मन सर्गप्रतक्तः सिन्धुर न क्षोदः का ईं वराते ||
atyo nājman sargaprataktaḥ sindhur na kṣodaḥ ka īṁ varāte ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • atyo: Beyond, transcending.
    • nājman: Not born, or beyond birth.
    • sargaprataktaḥ: The one who is manifest in creation.
    • sindhur: Ocean or vastness.
    • na: Not.
    • kṣodaḥ: To be disturbed or agitated.
    • ka: Who.
    • īṁ: A sound or vibration, often associated with the divine.
    • varāte: To be desired or sought after.
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of a state of existence that transcends birth and creation.
    • It suggests a vastness, akin to an ocean, that remains undisturbed despite the chaos of life.
    • The phrase implies a quest for something beyond the physical realm, something that is desired and sought after.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In moments of meditation, individuals often seek a state of peace that is beyond the turmoil of daily life, akin to the "ocean" mentioned in the mantra.
    • Philosophers and spiritual leaders, like the Buddha, have spoken of transcending the cycle of birth and death, which resonates with the idea of being "not born."
    • In the context of personal growth, people strive to achieve a state of inner calm and clarity, which can be seen as a pursuit of the "desired" state mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking a deeper understanding of existence beyond the physical and transient.
    • It encourages individuals to find peace and stability within themselves, despite external disturbances.
    • In real life, this can be applied through practices such as meditation, mindfulness, and philosophical inquiry, leading to a more fulfilling and serene life.
