यत् त्वा पृच्छाद ईजानः कुहया कुह्याकृतैः | एषो अपशृतो वलो गोमतीं अव तिष्ठति ||
yat tvā pṛcchād ījānaḥ kuhayā kuhayākṛte | eṣo apaśrito valo gomatīm ava tiṣṭhati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yat: that which
    • tvā: you
    • pṛcchād: inquired or asked
    • ījānaḥ: those who are knowledgeable or wise
    • kuhayā: in secret or hidden
    • kuhayākṛte: for the purpose of secrecy
    • eṣo: this
    • apaśrito: having taken refuge
    • valo: the one who is strong or powerful
    • gomatīm: with wealth or resources
    • ava: down or below
    • tiṣṭhati: stands or remains
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the nature of inquiry and the quest for knowledge.
    • It suggests that when wise individuals seek answers, they may do so in hidden or secretive ways.
    • The phrase "this one who has taken refuge" indicates a source of strength or support that is grounded in wealth or resources.
    • It emphasizes the importance of understanding the deeper meanings behind our inquiries and the strength that comes from knowledge.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a manager may seek insights from experienced employees in a discreet manner to understand the underlying issues affecting team performance.
    • In academia, researchers often explore hidden aspects of a subject through thorough investigation, leading to significant discoveries.
    • In personal relationships, individuals may ask probing questions to understand their partner's feelings, often in a gentle or indirect way.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking knowledge and understanding in a thoughtful and sometimes discreet manner.
    • It teaches us that true strength lies in the wisdom we acquire and the resources we gather, which can help us navigate life's complexities.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in professional, academic, or personal contexts, the principles of inquiry and the pursuit of knowledge remain essential for growth and success.
