इमं तं पश्य वृषभस्य युञ्जं काष्ठायां मध्ये द्रुघणं शयनम् |येन जिगाय शतवत सहस्रं गवाम् मुद्गलः पृतनाज्येषु ||
imaṁ tam paśya vṛṣabhasya yuñjaṁ kāṣṭhāyā madhye drughaṇaṁ śayānam | yena jigāya śatavat sahasraṁ gavām mudgalaḥ pṛtanājyeṣu ||
  • imaṁ tam paśya: This phrase translates to "look at this." It invites the listener to observe something significant, suggesting the importance of awareness and mindfulness in our lives.
  • vṛṣabhasya: This word means "of the bull." The bull is often a symbol of strength, power, and fertility in various cultures. In real life, it can represent hard work and perseverance, much like a farmer who relies on bulls for plowing fields.
  • yuñjaṁ: This means "joining" or "uniting." It emphasizes the importance of collaboration and teamwork. For example, in a workplace, when team members unite their skills and efforts, they can achieve greater success.
  • kāṣṭhāyā madhye: This translates to "in the midst of wood." It can symbolize being grounded and connected to nature. In real life, spending time in nature can provide clarity and peace, much like a retreat in a forest.
  • drughaṇaṁ: This means "the one who is lying down." It can represent rest and rejuvenation. In our busy lives, taking time to rest is crucial for maintaining mental and physical health.
  • śayānam: This translates to "sleeping" or "lying down." It reinforces the idea of rest and the necessity of sleep for overall well-being.
  • yena jigāya: This means "by which one has attained." It suggests that through certain actions or qualities, one can achieve success or fulfillment. For instance, dedication and hard work can lead to career advancement.
  • śatavat sahasraṁ: This translates to "hundreds and thousands." It signifies abundance and prosperity. In real life, this can relate to the idea of wealth, whether material or spiritual, that comes from hard work and unity.
  • gavām: This means "of cows." Cows are often seen as symbols of nourishment and sustenance. In many cultures, they represent the importance of agriculture and the sustenance it provides to society.
  • mudgalaḥ: This refers to "the one who is strong." It emphasizes the value of strength and resilience in overcoming challenges. For example, athletes train rigorously to build strength and endurance.
  • pṛtanājyeṣu: This translates to "in the fields." It symbolizes the importance of hard work and cultivation, both literally in agriculture and metaphorically in personal development.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of awareness, unity, strength, and the importance of rest and rejuvenation in achieving success and abundance. In real life, it teaches us to be mindful of our surroundings, work collaboratively, and value the strength that comes from both individual effort and community support. By applying these principles, we can cultivate a fulfilling and prosperous life, much like a farmer who nurtures his land and livestock to reap a bountiful harvest.
