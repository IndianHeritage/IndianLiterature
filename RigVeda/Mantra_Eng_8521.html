उत प्र पिप्य ऊधर अग्न्यायाऽ इन्दुर धाराभिः साचते सुमेधा: |मूर्धानं गावा: पयसा चमूष्व अभि श्रीणन्ति वसुभिर न निक्तै: ||
uta pra pipya ūdhar aghnyāyā indur dhārābhiḥ sacate sumedhāḥ | mūrdhānaṁ gāvaḥ payasā camūṣv abhi śrīṇanti vasubhir na niktaiḥ ||
  • uta pra pipya: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we drink" or "let us consume." It signifies the act of receiving nourishment or sustenance, both physically and spiritually.
  • ūdhar aghnyāyā: This translates to "from the non-violent source." It emphasizes the importance of obtaining nourishment from pure and non-harmful sources, promoting a lifestyle that respects all forms of life.
  • indur dhārābhiḥ: "Indur" refers to the moon or a source of light, while "dhārābhiḥ" means "with streams." This can symbolize the nourishing qualities of the moon, which is often associated with fertility and growth, suggesting that we should seek nourishment from gentle and nurturing sources.
  • sacate sumedhāḥ: This phrase means "may we be endowed with good thoughts." It highlights the importance of mental and spiritual nourishment, encouraging individuals to cultivate wisdom and positive thinking.
  • mūrdhānaṁ gāvaḥ: "Mūrdhānaṁ" refers to the head or the highest point, while "gāvaḥ" means "cows." In Vedic culture, cows are revered as symbols of abundance and nourishment. This line suggests that the highest form of nourishment comes from the sacred and revered aspects of life.
  • payasā camūṣv: "Payasā" means "with milk," and "camūṣv" refers to "in the herds." This indicates the importance of dairy and the nurturing qualities of milk, which is a staple in many cultures, symbolizing sustenance and care.
  • abhi śrīṇanti: This translates to "they bestow prosperity." It signifies the blessings and abundance that come from following the principles of nourishment and care.
  • vasubhir na niktaiḥ: "Vasubhir" refers to the deities or the divine, while "na niktaiḥ" means "without being obstructed." This suggests that divine blessings and prosperity flow freely when one adheres to the principles of non-violence and purity in nourishment.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking nourishment from pure and non-violent sources, both physically and spiritually. It encourages individuals to cultivate good thoughts and to appreciate the abundance provided by nature, particularly through revered symbols like cows and milk. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied by promoting a lifestyle that values ethical consumption, mindfulness in dietary choices, and the pursuit of wisdom. By adhering to these principles, individuals can foster a harmonious relationship with nature and contribute to their own well-being and that of the community.
