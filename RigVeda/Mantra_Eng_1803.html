आस्थापयन्त युवतिं युवानः शुभे निमिष्लां विदथेṣu पज्राम |अर्को यद् वो मरुतो हविष्मान गायत गाथं सुतसोमो दुवास्यन् ||
āsthāpayanta yuvatiṁ yuvānaḥ śubhe nimiślāṁ vidatheṣu pajrām | arko yad vo maruto haviṣmān gāyad gāthaṁ sutasomo duvasyan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āsthāpayanta: establishing or setting up
    • yuvatiṁ: the young woman
    • yuvānaḥ: the young men
    • śubhe: auspicious or beautiful
    • nimiślāṁ: with a glance or a wink
    • vidatheṣu: in the gatherings or assemblies
    • pajrām: we praise or invoke
    • arko: the sun or light
    • yad: that which
    • vo: for you
    • maruto: the storm gods or winds
    • haviṣmān: the one who is worthy of offerings
    • gāyad: singing or chanting
    • gāthaṁ: the hymn or song
    • sutasomo: the one who is sweet or pleasant
    • duvasyan: who brings forth or produces
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the interaction between young men and women, emphasizing the beauty and auspiciousness of their relationships.
    • The act of establishing connections in gatherings is highlighted, suggesting the importance of community and social bonds.
    • The reference to the sun and storm gods indicates a connection to nature and the divine, suggesting that these relationships are blessed and supported by higher powers.
    • Real-life example: In modern society, this can be seen in social gatherings, festivals, or community events where young people come together to form connections, celebrate, and create bonds.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of relationships and community in human life.
    • It encourages individuals to engage with one another in a positive and auspicious manner, fostering connections that are beneficial and harmonious.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as weddings, festivals, and community service, where the spirit of togetherness and celebration is paramount.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the beauty of human connections and the blessings that come from nurturing these relationships.
