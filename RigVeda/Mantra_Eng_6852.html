उक्षान्नाय वशान्नाय सोमपृष्ठाय वेधसे |स्तोमैर विधेमाग्नये ||
ukṣānnāya vaśānnāya somapṛṣṭhāya vedhase | stomair vidhemāgnaye ||
  • ukṣānnāya: This term can be broken down into "ukṣā," which refers to the act of awakening or rising, and "nāya," meaning to lead or guide. Together, it signifies the idea of being led towards awakening or enlightenment.
  • vaśānnāya: Here, "vaśa" means control or mastery, and "nāya" again refers to leading. This phrase suggests the importance of mastering one's desires and emotions, guiding them towards a higher purpose.
  • somapṛṣṭhāya: "Soma" is often associated with a divine drink that grants immortality and enlightenment, while "pṛṣṭha" means the back or support. This term can be interpreted as seeking the support of divine energy or consciousness that leads to spiritual awakening.
  • vedhase: This word refers to the act of penetrating or understanding deeply. It implies a quest for knowledge and wisdom, particularly in a spiritual context.
  • stomair vidhemāgnaye: "Stomair" refers to hymns or praises, "vidhema" means to offer or invoke, and "agnaye" refers to Agni, the fire god. This phrase emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energy through hymns and prayers, particularly to Agni, who represents transformation and purification.

Conclusion: The mantra "ukṣānnāya vaśānnāya somapṛṣṭhāya vedhase | stomair vidhemāgnaye ||" encapsulates the essence of spiritual awakening, mastery over desires, and the pursuit of knowledge through divine invocation. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as meditation practices, where individuals seek to awaken their inner consciousness and gain control over their thoughts and emotions. It also highlights the significance of rituals and prayers in connecting with higher energies, promoting personal transformation and growth. By understanding and embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can navigate their lives with greater awareness, purpose, and spiritual depth.
