नियुत्वान वायव आ गह्य अयं शुक्रो आयामि ते |गन्तासि सुन्वतो गृहम् ||
niyutvān vāyav ā gahy ayaṁ śukro ayāmi te | gantāsi sunvato gṛham ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • niyutvān: One who is swift or quick.
    • vāyav: Of the wind or air.
    • ā gahy: Come here or approach.
    • ayaṁ: This.
    • śukro: Bright, pure, or auspicious.
    • ayāmi: I go or I move.
    • te: To you.
    • gantāsi: You will go.
    • sunvato: Of the one who is well-spoken or praised.
    • gṛham: Home or dwelling.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the swift movement of the wind and its connection to purity and auspiciousness.
    • The phrase "niyutvān vāyav" suggests the idea of speed and agility, akin to how the wind moves effortlessly through the environment.
    • "śukro ayāmi te" indicates a sense of purity and brightness, suggesting that the speaker is moving towards something auspicious or beneficial.
    • The concluding part "gantāsi sunvato gṛham" implies that the listener will reach a place of praise or a well-regarded home, emphasizing the importance of a positive environment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional context, this mantra can be related to the swift and efficient movement towards achieving goals, much like a team that works harmoniously to reach a common objective.
    • In personal life, it can symbolize the journey towards a peaceful and harmonious home, where positivity and purity of intentions are valued.
    • In nature, the wind can be seen as a metaphor for change and transformation, reminding us to embrace the swift currents of life and move towards brighter opportunities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of swift action and the pursuit of purity and auspiciousness in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to move towards positive environments and to embrace change with an open heart.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to act quickly and purposefully, ensuring that our actions lead us to a place of praise and fulfillment.
