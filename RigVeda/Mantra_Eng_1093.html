स सूनुभिर न रुद्रैभिर ऋभ्वा नृषाह्ये सासह्वाम् अमित्रान् | सनीलेभिः श्रवस्यानि तूर्वन मरुत्वान् नो भवत्व इन्द्र ऊती ||
sa sūnubhir na rudrebhir ṛbhvā nṛṣāhye sāsahvām̐ amitrān | sanīḻebhiḥ śravasyāni tūrvan marutvān no bhavatv indra ūtī ||
  • sa sūnubhir: This phrase can be translated as "with the sons" or "with the offspring." It signifies the importance of lineage and community, emphasizing the role of family and relationships in achieving success.
  • na rudrebhir: This means "not with the Rudras." The Rudras are often associated with storm and destruction. This part of the mantra suggests a desire to avoid conflict and negativity, seeking instead a path of peace and harmony.
  • ṛbhvā nṛṣāhye: Translated as "with strength and valor," this phrase highlights the qualities of bravery and resilience. It encourages individuals to face challenges with courage and determination.
  • sāsahvām̐ amitrān: This means "we shall overcome our enemies." It reflects the aspiration to triumph over adversities and adversaries, promoting a sense of empowerment and victory.
  • sanīḻebhiḥ: This can be interpreted as "with the support of the wise." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and guidance from knowledgeable individuals in navigating life's challenges.
  • śravasyāni: This translates to "with fame and glory." It signifies the pursuit of honor and recognition, suggesting that one's actions should lead to a positive legacy.
  • tūrvan marutvān: This phrase means "may we be swift like the winds." It invokes the idea of agility and speed in achieving goals, encouraging individuals to act decisively and efficiently.
  • no bhavatv indra ūtī: This translates to "may the blessings of Indra be upon us." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength and power. This part of the mantra seeks divine support and guidance in endeavors.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the importance of community, wisdom, courage, and divine support. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, leadership, and teamwork. For instance, in a corporate setting, fostering a supportive environment (sa sūnubhir) while avoiding conflicts (na rudrebhir) can lead to a more productive workplace. Additionally, embracing challenges with bravery (ṛbhvā nṛṣāhye) and seeking guidance from mentors (sanīḻebhiḥ) can enhance personal growth. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to strive for success while maintaining integrity and seeking the blessings of higher powers in our endeavors.
