उदु ष्टुतः समिधा यह्वो अद्यौद वर्ष्मन् दिवो अधि नाभा पृथिव्याः |मित्रो अग्निर ईड्यो मातरिश्वा दूतो वक्षद यजथाय देवान् ||
ud u ṣṭutaḥ samidhā yahvo adyaud varṣman divo adhi nābhā pṛthivyāḥ | mitro agnir īḍyo mātariśvā dūto vakṣad yajathāya devān ||
  • ud u ṣṭutaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "rising up" or "elevated." It signifies the importance of elevation in spiritual and material pursuits.
  • samidhā: This term refers to "kindling" or "igniting." It symbolizes the initiation of energy or the beginning of a sacred act, such as a ritual or prayer.
  • yahvo: This word means "who" or "that which." It indicates a connection to a higher power or divine entity.
  • adyaud: Translated as "today," it emphasizes the immediacy and relevance of the invocation in the present moment.
  • varṣman: This term means "wealth" or "prosperity." It highlights the aspiration for abundance in life.
  • divo adhi nābhā: This phrase translates to "the space above" or "the sky's center." It represents the cosmic order and the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • pṛthivyāḥ: Meaning "of the earth," it signifies the grounding aspect of existence and the importance of nature in our lives.
  • mitro: This word means "friend." In this context, it refers to the friendly and benevolent nature of the divine.
  • agnir: Translated as "fire," it symbolizes transformation, purification, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • īḍyo: This term means "worthy of praise." It indicates the reverence and respect owed to the divine forces.
  • mātariśvā: Referring to the "wind" or "air," it symbolizes the vital force and the connection between the physical and spiritual realms.
  • dūto: This word means "messenger." It signifies the role of divine forces as intermediaries between humans and the divine.
  • vakṣad: Translated as "to protect" or "to guard," it emphasizes the safeguarding aspect of the divine in our lives.
  • yajathāya: This term refers to "sacrifice" or "offering." It highlights the importance of selflessness and devotion in spiritual practices.
  • devān: Meaning "gods," it signifies the divine entities that are invoked for blessings and guidance.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine connection and blessings in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of elevating oneself spiritually, kindling the inner fire of devotion, and recognizing the interconnectedness of the earthly and divine realms. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during rituals, prayers, or moments of reflection, reminding us to seek prosperity, protection, and guidance from the divine forces. It encourages us to be selfless in our pursuits and to honor the natural world around us, fostering a sense of harmony and balance in our lives.
