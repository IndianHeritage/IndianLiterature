ईळे गिरा मनुर्हितं यं देवाः दूतं अरतिं न्येरिरे | यजिष्ठं हव्यवाहनम् ||
īḻe girā manurhitaṁ yaṁ devā dūtam aratiṁ nyerire | yajiṣṭhaṁ havyavāhanam ||
  • īḻe: This word can be interpreted as "to praise" or "to invoke." It signifies the act of calling upon divine forces or energies.
  • girā: This translates to "speech" or "sound." In the context of the mantra, it emphasizes the power of words and their ability to connect with the divine.
  • manurhitaṁ: This term refers to "the will of Manu," where Manu is often considered the progenitor of humanity in Vedic texts. It suggests alignment with divine order and human duty.
  • yaṁ: This is a pronoun meaning "who" or "that," referring to the divine or the deity being invoked.
  • devā: This means "gods" or "deities," indicating the higher powers that are being called upon in the mantra.
  • dūtam: This translates to "messenger." It signifies the role of divine messengers in conveying messages or blessings from the gods to humans.
  • aratiṁ: This word means "discontent" or "sorrow." It highlights the human condition and the need for divine intervention to alleviate suffering.
  • nyerire: This means "to lead" or "to guide." It suggests the guidance provided by the divine in overcoming challenges.
  • yajiṣṭhaṁ: This translates to "the most worthy of worship." It emphasizes the importance of devotion and reverence in spiritual practices.
  • havyavāhanam: This term refers to "the one who carries offerings." It signifies the act of making offerings to the divine, which is a central aspect of Vedic rituals.

In summary, this mantra invokes the divine powers, emphasizing the importance of praise, speech, and alignment with the will of the gods. It acknowledges the human experience of discontent and the need for divine guidance to overcome it. The mantra highlights the significance of worship and offerings in establishing a connection with the divine.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in daily life by recognizing the power of words and intentions. Just as the mantra invokes the divine, individuals can use positive affirmations and prayers to manifest their desires and seek guidance.
  • In moments of discontent or sorrow, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek divine support and guidance, encouraging individuals to turn to spirituality for comfort and direction.
  • The emphasis on offerings can be translated into acts of kindness and service in everyday life, fostering a sense of community and connection with others.
