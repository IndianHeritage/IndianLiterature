विष्वो ह्य अन्यो अरिर आजगाम ममेद अह श्वशुरो न जगाम |जक्षीयाद धाना उता सोमं पपीयात स्वाशितः पुनर अस्तं जगायात ||
viśvo hy a1nyo arir ājagāma mamed aha śvaśuro nā jagāma | jakṣīyād dhānā uta somam papīyāt svāśitaḥ punar astaṁ jagāyāt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viśvo: universal, all-encompassing
    • hy: indeed, certainly
    • a1nyo: another, different
    • arir: enemy, adversary
    • ājagāma: has come, arrived
    • mamed: my, belonging to me
    • aha: I, myself
    • śvaśuro: father-in-law, a respected elder
    • nā: not
    • jagāma: went, departed
    • jakṣīyād: may it be fulfilled, may it happen
    • dhānā: wealth, prosperity
    • uta: or, and
    • somam: soma, a sacred drink, often associated with immortality
    • papīyāt: may it be more abundant, may it increase
    • svāśitaḥ: being well-fed, nourished
    • punar: again, once more
    • astaṁ: setting, going down
    • jagāyāt: may it rise, may it come up
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the universal nature of existence and the interplay between adversaries and allies.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wealth and nourishment, both physical and spiritual.
    • The reference to the father-in-law signifies respect for elders and the wisdom they impart.
    • The invocation of soma suggests a desire for spiritual and material abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied to the dynamics of teamwork where different individuals (arir) come together (ājagāma) to achieve a common goal.
    • In personal relationships, the respect for elders (śvaśuro) can guide younger generations in making wise decisions.
    • The pursuit of wealth (dhānā) and nourishment (svāśitaḥ) can be seen in the efforts of individuals striving for a better quality of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity in diversity, the importance of respect for elders, and the pursuit of both material and spiritual wealth.
    • It serves as a reminder that while adversities may arise, the focus should remain on collective growth and nourishment.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek balance in their lives, fostering relationships that promote mutual respect and abundance.
