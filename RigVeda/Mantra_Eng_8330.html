हितो न सप्तिर अभि वाजम् अर्षेन्द्रस्येन्दो जठरम् ā पवस्व |नाव न सिन्धुम अति पर्षि विद्वाँश चूरो न युध्यन्न अव नो निदः स्पः ||
hito na saptir abhi vājam arṣendrasyendo jaṭharam ā pavasva | nāvā na sindhum ati parṣi vidvāñ chūro na yudhyann ava no nidaḥ spaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "hito na saptir abhi vājam arṣendrasyendo jaṭharam ā pavasva | nāvā na sindhum ati parṣi vidvāñ chūro na yudhyann ava no nidaḥ spaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • hito: beneficial, auspicious
    • na: not
    • saptir: seven, often referring to the seven realms or directions
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • vājam: strength, power, or energy
    • arṣendrasyendo: of the great Indra, the king of gods
    • jaṭharam: belly, or the core
    • ā: from, out of
    • pavasva: may you purify or cleanse
    • nāvā: boat, vessel
    • sindhum: ocean, sea
    • ati: beyond, surpassing
    • parṣi: to cross, to traverse
    • vidvāñ: the wise one, knowledgeable
    • chūro: the hero, the brave one
    • na: not
    • yudhyann: fighting, engaging in battle
    • ava: down, away
    • no: our
    • nidaḥ: protection, shelter
    • spaḥ: light, brilliance
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra, the king of gods, asking for strength and purification.
    • The reference to "seven" can symbolize completeness or the totality of existence, suggesting that the blessings sought are all-encompassing.
    • The plea for purification from the "belly" signifies a desire for inner strength and clarity, essential for overcoming life's challenges.
    • The mention of a "boat" crossing the "ocean" symbolizes navigating through life's difficulties, with wisdom and bravery as guiding forces.
    • It emphasizes the importance of not engaging in unnecessary conflict, instead seeking protection and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek inner strength and clarity, akin to the purification mentioned in the mantra. For instance, someone facing a difficult decision may meditate on their core values to find direction.
    • In leadership, the mantra's call for wisdom and bravery can be seen in leaders who navigate their teams through challenges without resorting to conflict, focusing instead on collaboration and understanding.
    • In environmental contexts, the metaphor of a boat crossing the ocean can relate to sustainable practices, where individuals and communities work together to protect their environment, seeking knowledge and innovative solutions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking inner strength, wisdom, and protection in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the universal themes of purification, bravery, and the pursuit of knowledge, applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to leadership and environmental stewardship.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can navigate life's challenges with grace and resilience, fostering a harmonious existence.
