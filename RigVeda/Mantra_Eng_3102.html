ऋतेन हि स्मा वृषभश्चिदक्तः pumām̐ अग्निः पयसा पृष्ठ्येन |अस्पन्दमानो अचरत् वयोधाऽवृषा शुक्रं दुदुहे पृष्णिरूधः ||
ṛtena hi ṣmā vṛṣabhaś cid aktaḥ pumām̐ agniḥ payasā pṛṣṭhyena | aspandamāno acarad vayodhā vṛṣā śukraṁ duduhe pṛśnir ūdhaḥ ||
  • ṛtena hi ṣmā: This phrase translates to "Indeed, through truth." The concept of "ṛta" refers to cosmic order and truth, emphasizing the importance of living in harmony with the universe.
  • vṛṣabhaś cid aktaḥ pumām̐: This means "the bull, indeed, is the one who is active." The bull symbolizes strength and vitality, representing the active force in creation and life.
  • agniḥ payasā pṛṣṭhyena: Here, "Agni" refers to fire, which is a central element in Vedic rituals. "Payasā" means nourishment, indicating that fire provides sustenance and energy.
  • aspandamāno acarad: This translates to "the one who does not tremble, moved." It suggests stability and resilience, qualities essential for progress and growth.
  • vayodhā vṛṣā: This phrase means "the strong one of the forces." It highlights the idea of strength in the face of challenges, akin to a warrior or protector.
  • śukraṁ duduhe pṛśnir ūdhaḥ: This translates to "the bright one, who is nourished by the earth." It signifies the importance of nurturing and being nurtured, reflecting the interconnectedness of life.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of truth, strength, and nourishment in life. It emphasizes the importance of living in accordance with cosmic order (ṛta) and harnessing one's inner strength (vṛṣabha) to overcome challenges. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal growth, leadership, and community building. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute positively to their surroundings, fostering harmony and resilience in their lives and the lives of others.
