मन्त्र: शुभागान नो देवाः कृतुता सुरत्नान अस्मान स्तोत्रं मरुतो वावृद्धानाः |अधि स्तोत्रस्य सख्यस्य गात सना धि वो रत्नधेयानि सन्ति ||
subhāgān no devāḥ kṛṇutā suratnān asmān stotṝn maruto vāvṛdhānāḥ | adhi stotrasya sakhyasya gāta sanād dhi vo ratnadheyāni santi ||
  • Subhāgān no devāḥ kṛṇutā: This phrase translates to "May the gods grant us auspiciousness." Here, "subhāgān" refers to auspiciousness or good fortune, and "devāḥ" refers to the divine beings or gods. In real life, this can be related to seeking blessings for success in endeavors, such as starting a new business or embarking on a significant life journey.
  • Suratnān asmān stotṝn maruto vāvṛdhānāḥ: This part means "May the Maruts, the storm gods, enhance our praises." The Maruts are associated with storms and winds, symbolizing strength and vitality. This can be likened to the support one receives from friends or mentors during challenging times, enhancing one's efforts and praises in various pursuits.
  • Adhi stotrasya sakhyasya: This translates to "In the context of the friendship of praises." It emphasizes the importance of camaraderie and collective effort in achieving goals. In real life, this can be seen in teamwork, where collaboration leads to greater success, such as in sports teams or project groups in workplaces.
  • Gāta sanād dhi vo ratnadheyāni santi: This means "There are treasures that are bestowed upon you." It signifies the rewards and blessings that come from devotion and hard work. In practical terms, this can be related to the outcomes of persistent efforts, such as academic achievements or career advancements that result from dedication and teamwork.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for auspiciousness, the importance of collective efforts, and the rewards that come from dedication and teamwork. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek support from the divine and from each other, fostering a sense of community and collaboration in our endeavors. Whether in personal relationships, professional settings, or spiritual pursuits, the principles outlined in this mantra encourage us to work together harmoniously and to appreciate the blessings that come our way.
