एना वयं पयसा पिन्वमानाऽनु योनिं देवकृतं चरन्तीः |न वर्तवे प्रसवः सर्गतक्तः किम्युर विप्रो नद्यो जोहवीति ||
enā vayam payasā pinvamānā anu yoniṁ devakṛtaṁ carantīḥ | na vartave prasavaḥ sargataktaḥ kiṁyur vipro nadyo johavīti ||
  • enā vayam payasā pinvamānā: This phrase can be translated to "We are nourished by the essence of life." Here, "payasā" refers to nourishment, often symbolizing milk or sustenance, which is essential for growth and development. In real life, this can be likened to the importance of education and knowledge as nourishment for the mind and spirit.
  • anu yoniṁ devakṛtaṁ carantīḥ: This translates to "Following the divine path created by the gods." The term "yoni" signifies a source or origin, while "devakṛta" refers to something that is divinely created. This can be related to the pursuit of one's purpose or dharma in life, where individuals strive to follow their unique paths that align with their values and beliefs.
  • na vartave prasavaḥ sargataktaḥ: This means "There is no obstruction to the flow of creation." "Prasava" refers to birth or creation, and "sargataktaḥ" implies the act of bringing forth. In a real-life context, this can be seen in the creative processes in art, science, and innovation, where ideas flow freely without hindrance, leading to new discoveries and advancements.
  • kiṁyur vipro nadyo johavīti: This can be interpreted as "What is the role of the wise in guiding the rivers?" Here, "vipro" refers to the wise or learned individuals, and "nadyo" symbolizes rivers, which can represent the flow of knowledge and wisdom. This highlights the importance of mentorship and guidance in society, where knowledgeable individuals help navigate the complexities of life, much like rivers guide water to the ocean.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nourishment, divine purpose, creativity, and wisdom. It signifies that just as physical nourishment is vital for life, so too is the nourishment of the mind and spirit through knowledge and purpose. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in education, where teachers nourish students' minds, or in creative fields, where individuals are encouraged to follow their passions without fear of obstruction. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the importance of guidance and the flow of wisdom in our lives, encouraging us to seek our paths and contribute positively to the world around us.
