त्वां īḻे अध द्विता भरतो वाजिभिः शुनम् |īजे यज्ञेषु यज्ञियम ||
tvām īḻe adha dvitā bharato vājibhiḥ śunam | īje yajñeṣu yajñiyam ||
  • tvām - This word translates to "you" or "to you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • īḻe - This means "I worship" or "I praise," expressing devotion and reverence.
  • adha - This translates to "below" or "under," suggesting a position of humility or submission.
  • dvitā - This means "the second" or "the dual," which can imply a duality in existence or the presence of multiple aspects of divinity.
  • bharato - This can be interpreted as "the one who brings" or "the one who carries," often associated with strength and support.
  • vājibhiḥ - This word means "with offerings" or "with gifts," indicating the act of giving in worship.
  • śunam - This translates to "the best" or "the most excellent," referring to the quality of the offerings or the worship itself.
  • īje - This means "I perform" or "I carry out," indicating an action of worship or ritual.
  • yajñeṣu - This translates to "in sacrifices" or "in rituals," referring to the context of Vedic rituals and offerings.
  • yajñiyam - This means "related to sacrifice" or "sacrificial," emphasizing the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation where the speaker expresses their devotion to a divine entity, acknowledging their presence and the importance of offerings in rituals. The speaker humbly worships the divine, recognizing the duality of existence and the strength that comes from the divine support.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Spiritual Practice: Individuals can use this mantra during meditation or prayer to cultivate a sense of connection with the divine, fostering humility and gratitude.
  • Community Rituals: In communal gatherings, this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings and unity among participants, emphasizing the importance of collective offerings and shared intentions.
  • Personal Growth: The themes of humility and recognition of support can be applied in personal development, encouraging individuals to acknowledge their strengths and the help they receive from others.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on devotion, humility, and the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine. It serves as a reminder of the duality of existence and the strength derived from divine support. In various aspects of life, from personal spirituality to community engagement, this mantra encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of reverence and gratitude, fostering a deeper connection with the world around them.
