यदि मे रारणः सुत उक्ते वा दधसे चनः |आराद उप स्वधां गहि ||
yadi me rāraṇaḥ suta ukthe vā dadhase canaḥ | ārād upa svadhā gahi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yadi: if
    • me: my
    • rāraṇaḥ: offspring or progeny
    • suta: son or child
    • ukthe: in the utterance or speech
    • vā: or
    • dadhase: you bestow or grant
    • canaḥ: anything
    • ārād: from the beginning or from the source
    • upa: near or close
    • svadhā: self-offering or self-sacrifice
    • gahi: hold or grasp
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea or invocation, where the speaker is addressing a divine entity or force.
    • The speaker is asking for blessings or support regarding their offspring or progeny, emphasizing the importance of lineage and continuity.
    • The mention of "self-offering" suggests a theme of sacrifice and devotion, indicating that one must be willing to give of oneself for the greater good.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the well-being of children is a primary concern. Parents often pray for their children's success and happiness, reflecting the sentiment of this mantra.
    • In a community setting, individuals may come together to support a child in need, showcasing the idea of collective responsibility and self-offering for the benefit of future generations.
    • In educational institutions, teachers often dedicate themselves to nurturing and guiding their students, embodying the spirit of sacrifice and commitment to the growth of the next generation.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of lineage, the importance of nurturing future generations, and the value of self-sacrifice.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions today shape the lives of those who come after us, urging us to invest in the well-being of our children and communities.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in acts of kindness, support, and mentorship, fostering a sense of responsibility towards the future.
