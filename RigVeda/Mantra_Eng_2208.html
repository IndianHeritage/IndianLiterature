स ह श्रुत इन्द्रो नाम देव ऊर्ध्वो भुवन मनुषे दसमतमः |अव प्रियम अर्शसानस्य साह्वाण चिरो भरद दासस्य स्वधावान ||
sa ha śruta indro nāma deva ūrdhvo bhuvan manuṣe dasmatamaḥ | ava priyam arśasānasya sāhvāñ chiro bharad dāsasya svadhāvān ||
  • sa ha śruta indro nāma deva ūrdhvo bhuvan manuṣe dasmatamaḥ
    • sa ha: This phrase can be interpreted as "thus" or "therefore," indicating a conclusion or a result.
    • śruta: Refers to "hearing" or "knowledge," emphasizing the importance of wisdom and understanding.
    • indro: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power and authority.
    • nāma: Means "name," suggesting the significance of identity and recognition.
    • deva: Translates to "god," indicating divine qualities or attributes.
    • ūrdhvo: Means "above" or "higher," suggesting elevation or transcendence.
    • bhuvan: Refers to "world" or "universe," indicating the vastness of existence.
    • manuṣe: Means "human" or "mankind," connecting the divine to human experience.
    • dasmatamaḥ: Translates to "the most excellent" or "the best," indicating superiority or excellence.
  • ava priyam arśasānasya sāhvāñ chiro bharad dāsasya svadhāvān
    • ava: Means "down" or "from," indicating a descent or a grounding aspect.
    • priyam: Refers to "beloved" or "dear," emphasizing affection and value.
    • arśasānasya: Can be interpreted as "of the one who sits on the throne," referring to authority and governance.
    • sāhvāñ: Means "with strength" or "powerful," indicating resilience and capability.
    • chiro: Refers to "long" or "eternal," suggesting longevity and permanence.
    • bharad: Means "bearing" or "carrying," indicating responsibility and support.
    • dāsasya: Refers to "servant" or "devotee," indicating humility and service.
    • svadhāvān: Means "self-moving" or "self-driven," indicating autonomy and initiative.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the connection between the divine and human realms, highlighting the importance of wisdom, authority, and service. It suggests that true excellence comes from understanding and recognizing the divine within ourselves and others. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as leadership, where a leader must embody wisdom and strength while remaining humble and service-oriented. It also encourages individuals to strive for personal excellence and to recognize the divine qualities in themselves and those around them, fostering a sense of community and mutual respect.
