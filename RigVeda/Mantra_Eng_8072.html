त्वं इन्द्राय विष्णवे स्वादुर इन्दो परि श्रव || नृन् स्तोत्रं पाहि अंहसः ||
tvam indrāya viṣṇave svādur indo pari srava | nṝn stotṝn pāhy aṁhasaḥ ||
  • tvam - This word translates to "you" or "thou," indicating a direct address to a divine entity.
  • indrāya - Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • viṣṇave - This refers to Vishnu, the preserver and protector in Hindu mythology, representing stability and sustenance.
  • svādur - Meaning "sweet" or "pleasant," this word conveys a sense of harmony and positivity.
  • indo - This term can refer to the moon or the divine, often associated with nourishment and tranquility.
  • pari srava - Translates to "flowing around" or "spreading," suggesting abundance and the sharing of blessings.
  • nṝn - Meaning "for men" or "for humans," indicating the mantra's relevance to human beings.
  • stotṝn - This word means "praise" or "worship," emphasizing the act of devotion and recognition of the divine.
  • pāhy - Translates to "protect" or "guard," indicating a plea for safety and guidance.
  • aṁhasaḥ - Refers to "from the darkness" or "from ignorance," suggesting a desire for enlightenment and clarity.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation to the divine forces represented by Indra and Vishnu, asking for their blessings and protection. It emphasizes the importance of harmony, strength, and sustenance in our lives. The invocation of these deities signifies a request for guidance in overcoming challenges and achieving a state of well-being.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra during times of uncertainty or difficulty, seeking strength and clarity in their decisions.
  • Community Well-being: It can be used in community gatherings to invoke collective strength and harmony, fostering a sense of unity and support among members.
  • Spiritual Practices: In meditation or prayer, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence and the importance of seeking protection from ignorance and negativity.

In conclusion, this mantra holds significant meaning as it encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and protection. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, sustenance, and harmony in our lives. By invoking these divine qualities, individuals can navigate life's challenges with greater resilience and clarity.
