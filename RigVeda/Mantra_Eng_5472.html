शं नः सूर्य उरुचक्षाऽ उद एतु शं नश्चतस्रः प्रदिशो भवन्तु |शं नः पर्वताः ध्रुवयो भवन्तु शं नः सिन्धवः शम् उ सन्त्व आपः ||
śaṁ naḥ sūrya urucakṣā ud etu śaṁ naś catasraḥ pradiśo bhavantu | śaṁ naḥ parvatā dhruvayo bhavantu śaṁ naḥ sindhavaḥ śam u santv āpaḥ ||
  • śaṁ naḥ sūrya urucakṣā ud etu: This phrase can be translated as "May the sun with wide-open eyes shine upon us." Here, the sun symbolizes knowledge, enlightenment, and vitality. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, where the sun represents the illumination of the mind.
  • śaṁ naś catasraḥ pradiśo bhavantu: This means "May the four directions bring us peace." The four directions symbolize the entirety of the universe. In practical terms, this can be seen in the importance of harmony and balance in our lives, whether it be in personal relationships, community interactions, or global affairs.
  • śaṁ naḥ parvatā dhruvayo bhavantu: Translated as "May the mountains be steadfast for us." Mountains represent stability and strength. In real life, this can be related to having strong foundations in our lives, such as family support, education, and moral values that help us navigate challenges.
  • śaṁ naḥ sindhavaḥ śam u santv āpaḥ: This translates to "May the rivers bring us peace." Rivers are often seen as sources of life and nourishment. In a real-world context, this can be interpreted as the importance of resources and the flow of life, emphasizing the need for sustainable practices to ensure that our environment continues to support us.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature and human existence. It calls for blessings from the sun, directions, mountains, and rivers, highlighting the importance of peace, stability, and sustenance in our lives. In a modern context, this mantra can be applied to our daily lives by fostering a sense of gratitude for the natural world, promoting environmental sustainability, and striving for harmony in our relationships and communities. By recognizing these elements, we can cultivate a more balanced and fulfilling life.
