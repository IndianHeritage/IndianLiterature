इमं नरः पर्वतास तुभ्यम आपः सम इन्द्र गोभिर मधुमन्तम अकृन् |तस्यागत्यā सुमना ऋष्व पाहि प्रजानन विद्वान् पथ्याऽनु स्वाह ||
imaṁ naraḥ parvatās tubhyam āpaḥ sam indra gobhir madhumantam akran | tasyāgatyā sumanā ṛṣva pāhi prajānan vidvān pathyā3 anu svāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imaṁ: this
    • naraḥ: man or human
    • parvatās: mountains
    • tubhyam: to you
    • āpaḥ: waters
    • sam: together
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • gobhir: with the cows (or wealth)
    • madhumantam: sweet or rich
    • akran: we offer
    • tasyāgatyā: by that (offering)
    • sumanā: with good thoughts
    • ṛṣva: protect
    • pāhi: guard or preserve
    • prajānan: the progeny or creation
    • vidvān: the wise or knowledgeable
    • pathyā: on the right path
    • anu: following
    • svāḥ: a term of affirmation or auspiciousness
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the mountains and waters, symbolizing nature's abundance and the divine forces that govern them.
    • It emphasizes the importance of offering to Indra, who represents strength and prosperity, suggesting that one should seek blessings for a fruitful life.
    • The phrase "with good thoughts" indicates that intentions matter; one should approach life with positivity and wisdom.
    • The call for protection of progeny signifies the importance of safeguarding future generations and nurturing them wisely.
    • Overall, the mantra reflects a harmonious relationship between humans, nature, and the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, this mantra can be related to environmental conservation efforts. Just as the mantra calls for respect towards nature, individuals and communities can engage in activities that protect mountains and water bodies.
    • In personal life, the emphasis on good thoughts can be applied in daily interactions. Practicing kindness and positivity can lead to better relationships and a more fulfilling life.
    • In parenting, the call to protect progeny can be seen in the efforts parents make to provide a safe and nurturing environment for their children, ensuring they grow up with values and wisdom.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, urging individuals to respect nature, seek divine blessings, and foster positive intentions.
    • Its significance lies in promoting a balanced approach to life, where one acknowledges the importance of both material and spiritual wealth.
    • In essence, it encourages a holistic lifestyle that values wisdom, protection of future generations, and a harmonious existence with the environment.
