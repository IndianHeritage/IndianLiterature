अच्छा नृचक्षाऽसरत् पवित्रे नाम दधानाḥ कविरस्य योनौ |सीदन् होतेव सदने चमूषूपेम अग्मन् ऋषयः सप्त विप्राः ||
acchā nṛcakṣā asarat pavitre nāma dadhānaḥ kavir asya yonau | sīdan hoteva sadane camūṣūpem agmann ṛṣayaḥ sapta viprāḥ ||
  • acchā nṛcakṣā: This phrase can be interpreted as "the good eyes of man." Here, "acchā" means good or auspicious, and "nṛcakṣā" refers to the eyes of humans. This suggests the importance of perception and insight in human life.
  • asarat pavitre: "Asarat" means to be free or unencumbered, while "pavitre" means pure. Together, this can imply a state of purity and freedom from distractions or impurities, which is essential for spiritual and personal growth.
  • nāma dadhānaḥ: This translates to "holding the name." In many cultures, names carry significant power and meaning. This phrase emphasizes the importance of identity and the essence of being, suggesting that one should be aware of their true self and purpose.
  • kavir asya yonau: "Kavir" refers to a wise or knowledgeable person, and "asya yonau" means "in this origin." This indicates that wisdom is rooted in one's origins or foundational beliefs, highlighting the importance of understanding one's background and heritage.
  • sīdan hoteva sadane: "Sīdan" means to sit or reside, and "hoteva" refers to the act of invoking or calling upon. "Sadane" means in the abode or home. This suggests that wisdom and purity should reside within one's home or heart, emphasizing the need for a peaceful and wise environment.
  • camūṣūpem agmann: "Camūṣūpem" can be interpreted as "the army of the wise," while "agmann" means to be led or guided. This phrase suggests that wisdom should guide one's actions and decisions, akin to an army following a leader.
  • ṛṣayaḥ sapta viprāḥ: "ṛṣayaḥ" refers to sages or seers, and "sapta viprāḥ" means seven wise ones. This highlights the significance of collective wisdom and the guidance of enlightened individuals in one's life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, purity, and self-awareness in human life. It suggests that by cultivating good perception, understanding one's origins, and allowing wisdom to guide our actions, we can create a harmonious and fulfilling existence. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, leadership, and community building. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute positively to their surroundings and foster a culture of wisdom and understanding.
