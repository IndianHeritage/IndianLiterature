स्रुवेव यस्य हरिणी विपेततुः शिप्रे वाजाय हरिणी दविद्वतः |प्र यत कृतै चामसे मर्मृजद धरी पीत्वा मदस्य हर्यतस्यांधसः ||
sruveva yasya hariṇī vipetatuḥ śipre vājāya hariṇī davidhvataḥ | pra yat kṛte camase marmṛjad dharī pītvā madasya haryatasyāndhasaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sruveva: Like a stream or flow
    • yasya: Whose
    • hariṇī: Deer or female deer, symbolizing grace and agility
    • vipetatuḥ: May it be swift or may it move quickly
    • śipre: In the swift or rapid
    • vājāya: For the purpose of strength or power
    • davidhvataḥ: May it be directed or guided
    • pra: For or towards
    • yat: That which
    • kṛte: Is done or performed
    • camase: In the vessel or container
    • marmṛjad: May it be purified or cleansed
    • dharī: The earth or the ground
    • pītvā: Having drunk
    • madasya: Of the intoxication or joy
    • haryatasyāndhasaḥ: Of the one who is blind to joy or happiness
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the swift movement and grace, symbolized by the deer, which is often associated with agility and beauty in nature.
    • The invocation of strength and power suggests a desire for vitality and energy in one's endeavors.
    • The reference to purification indicates a need for cleansing, both physically and spiritually, to achieve clarity and focus.
    • The mention of intoxication can be interpreted as a metaphor for the joy and ecstasy that comes from achieving one's goals or experiencing life fully.
    • Finally, the phrase about being blind to joy suggests a reminder to remain aware and appreciative of the happiness that surrounds us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive environment, such as sports or business, the mantra can inspire individuals to move swiftly and gracefully towards their goals, much like a deer navigating through a forest.
    • In personal development, the idea of purification can be applied through practices like meditation or yoga, which help clear the mind and body, allowing for better focus and energy.
    • In relationships, recognizing and appreciating the joy in small moments can prevent one from becoming blind to happiness, fostering deeper connections with others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of agility, strength, and purification in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to remain aware of the joy and beauty around us, encouraging us to pursue our goals with grace and clarity.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in various aspects of life, from personal growth to professional endeavors, highlighting the interconnectedness of our actions and the joy they can bring.
