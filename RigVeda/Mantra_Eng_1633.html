तां वां वास्तून्य उष्मसि गमध्यै यत्र गावो भूरीशृङ्गा अयासः |अत्राह तद उरुगायस्य वृष्णः परमं पदम अव भाति भूरी ||
tā vāṁ vāstūny uśmasi gamadhyai yatra gāvo bhūriśṛṅgā ayāsaḥ | atrāha tad urugāyasya vṛṣṇaḥ paramam padam ava bhāti bhūri ||
  • tā vāṁ vāstūny uśmasi gamadhyai: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the divine to bless the dwelling or the space where one resides. The term "vāstūny" refers to the physical space or environment, while "uśmasi" implies warmth or comfort. In real life, this can relate to the importance of creating a harmonious living environment, such as a home that is filled with love and positivity.
  • yatra gāvo bhūriśṛṅgā ayāsaḥ: Here, "gāvo" refers to cows, which are often seen as symbols of wealth and prosperity in Vedic literature. "Bhūriśṛṅgā" suggests abundance and strength. This line emphasizes the significance of nurturing and protecting resources that contribute to one's well-being. For example, in agricultural communities, the care of livestock is crucial for sustenance and economic stability.
  • atrāha tad urugāyasya vṛṣṇaḥ: "Urugāyasya" refers to the one who is all-pervading and powerful, often associated with divine qualities. "Vṛṣṇaḥ" can denote strength and valor. This part of the mantra highlights the importance of divine support and strength in overcoming challenges. In everyday life, this can be seen in the reliance on faith or spirituality during difficult times, providing individuals with the courage to face adversities.
  • paramam padam ava bhāti bhūri: This phrase translates to the ultimate state or goal that shines brightly. "Paramam padam" signifies the highest achievement or realization, while "bhūri" indicates abundance. This line encourages individuals to strive for their highest potential and to seek enlightenment or fulfillment in life. In practical terms, this could relate to personal growth, education, or spiritual development, where one aims to reach their fullest capabilities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of creating a nurturing environment, valuing resources, seeking divine strength, and striving for personal excellence. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, from maintaining a harmonious home to pursuing one's goals with the support of a higher power. In a world where challenges are abundant, this mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate positivity, protect our resources, and seek growth, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
