त्रिंशद् धाम विराजति वाक् पतङ्गाय धीयते |प्रति वस्तोर अह द्युिभिः ||
triṁśad dhāma vi rājati vāk pataṁgāya dhīyate | prati vastor aha dyubhiḥ ||
  • triṁśad: This word translates to "thirty." In the context of the mantra, it signifies completeness or a full cycle, often associated with the thirty days of a lunar month.
  • dhāma: This means "abode" or "place." It refers to a divine or sacred space, often associated with higher realms of existence.
  • vi rājati: This phrase translates to "shines" or "rules." It indicates a state of prominence or authority, suggesting that the divine presence is powerful and influential.
  • vāk: This word means "speech" or "word." It represents communication, expression, and the power of language in shaping reality.
  • pataṁgāya: This term refers to "the one who flies" or "the bird." It symbolizes freedom, transcendence, and the ability to rise above earthly limitations.
  • dhīyate: This means "is contemplated" or "is meditated upon." It emphasizes the importance of reflection and understanding in the pursuit of knowledge.
  • prati: This word means "towards" or "in relation to." It indicates a connection or relationship between different elements.
  • vastor: This translates to "of the object" or "of the essence." It signifies the core or fundamental nature of things.
  • aha: This means "I" or "myself." It represents the individual self or consciousness.
  • dyubhiḥ: This word means "by the heavens" or "from the sky." It suggests a connection to the divine or celestial realms.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to recognize the divine presence that shines in the abode of completeness, where speech and expression take flight. It emphasizes the importance of contemplation and understanding in relation to the essence of existence, guided by the heavens.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra encourages contemplation, individuals can reflect on their thoughts and words, striving for clarity and purpose in their communication.
  • Leadership: The idea of ruling and shining can inspire leaders to embody qualities of wisdom and integrity, guiding their teams with a clear vision.
  • Creativity: The symbolism of flight can motivate artists and creators to transcend boundaries, allowing their ideas to soar and reach new heights.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for self-awareness, the power of communication, and the pursuit of higher understanding. It serves as a reminder that by contemplating our essence and the divine, we can navigate our lives with purpose and clarity, ultimately leading to personal and collective growth.
