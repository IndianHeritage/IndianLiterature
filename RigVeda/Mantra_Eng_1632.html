तदस्य प्रियं अभि पाठो अश्यां नरो यत्र देवयवो मदन्ति |उरुक्रमस्य स हि बन्धुर इत्तथा विष्णोः पदे परम मध्व उत्सः ||
tad asya priyam abhi pātho aśyāṁ naro yatra devayavo madanti | urukramasya sa hi bandhur itthā viṣṇoḥ pade parame madhva utsaḥ ||
  • tad asya priyam abhi pātho: This phrase can be translated as "that which is dear to him, let it be recited." It emphasizes the importance of devotion and the act of recitation in spiritual practices.
  • aśyāṁ naro yatra devayavo madanti: This part means "where the divine beings rejoice." It suggests a place or state where the divine presence is felt, and where beings are in harmony with the divine.
  • urukramasya sa hi bandhur itthā: This translates to "for the one of great deeds, he is indeed a friend." It refers to Lord Vishnu, who is known for his vast and benevolent actions, indicating that those who follow him are in a supportive relationship with the divine.
  • viṣṇoḥ pade parame madhva utsaḥ: This means "in the supreme abode of Vishnu, there is great joy." It highlights the ultimate goal of reaching a state of bliss and fulfillment through devotion to Vishnu.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, the joy of divine presence, and the importance of recitation in connecting with the divine. It serves as a reminder of the supportive relationship between the devotee and the divine, particularly in the context of Lord Vishnu.
  • Real-life Application: In daily life, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in practices that foster a sense of connection with the divine, such as prayer, meditation, and community service. For example, participating in a community service project can be seen as a way to embody the values of compassion and support that this mantra promotes.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of devotion and the joy that comes from connecting with the divine. It encourages individuals to seek out and create spaces where they can experience this divine presence, whether through personal practices or communal activities. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a deeper sense of purpose and fulfillment in their lives.
