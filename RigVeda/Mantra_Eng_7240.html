उद यद ब्रह्मनस्य विष्टपं गृहम इन्द्रश्च गन्भहि |मध्वः पीत्वा सचेवहि त्रिः सप्त सख्युः पदे ||
ud yad bradhnasya viṣṭapaṁ gṛham indraś ca ganvahi | madhvaḥ pītvā sacevahi triḥ sapta sakhyuḥ pade ||
  • ud yad bradhnasya viṣṭapaṁ gṛham: This phrase can be translated as "wherever the abode of the prosperous is." Here, "ud" means "where," "yad" means "that," "bradhnasya" refers to "of the prosperous," "viṣṭapaṁ" means "abode" or "home," and "gṛham" means "house." This part emphasizes the importance of a prosperous and harmonious home environment.
  • indraś ca ganvahi: This translates to "and may Indra come to us." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. The invocation of Indra signifies a request for divine blessings and support in achieving prosperity and well-being.
  • madhvaḥ pītvā sacevahi: This means "having drunk the sweet (nectar), may you come." "Madhvaḥ" refers to something sweet or nectar-like, often symbolizing joy and abundance. "Pītvā" means "having drunk," and "sacevahi" translates to "may you come." This part suggests that after experiencing joy and abundance, one should invite prosperity and good fortune into their lives.
  • triḥ sapta sakhyuḥ pade: This translates to "three times seven friends in the path." "Triḥ" means "three," "sapta" means "seven," "sakhyuḥ" refers to "friends," and "pade" means "in the path." This phrase emphasizes the importance of companionship and support in one's journey through life, suggesting that having a strong network of friends is essential for success and happiness.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking prosperity, divine blessings, and the importance of community in achieving a fulfilling life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Creating a positive and nurturing home environment that fosters growth and happiness.
  • Invoking support from higher powers or mentors in times of need, whether through prayer, meditation, or community support.
  • Celebrating achievements and sharing joy with friends and family, reinforcing bonds that contribute to personal and collective success.
  • Recognizing the value of teamwork and collaboration in professional settings, where collective efforts lead to greater outcomes.

This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of prosperity, divine grace, and the strength found in community, encouraging individuals to cultivate these aspects in their lives for holistic well-being.
