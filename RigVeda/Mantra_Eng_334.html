ऊर्ध्वस तिष्ठा न ऊतये 'स्मिन वाजे शतक्रतो |सम अन्येषु ब्रवावहै ||
ūrdhvas tiṣṭhā na ūtaye 'smin vāje śatakrato | sam anyeṣu bravāvahai ||
  • ūrdhvas tiṣṭhā: This phrase translates to "stand upright" or "rise high." It signifies the importance of maintaining a strong and upright position in life, both physically and morally.
  • na ūtaye 'smin: This means "not for the purpose of destruction." It emphasizes that one's actions should not lead to harm or negativity, but rather should be constructive and beneficial.
  • vāje śatakrato: This refers to "the one who is a hundredfold strong" or "the one who possesses great power." It can be interpreted as a call to harness one's inner strength and capabilities.
  • sam anyeṣu bravāvahai: This translates to "let us speak together with others." It highlights the importance of communication, collaboration, and unity with others in our endeavors.

In essence, this mantra encourages individuals to rise to their highest potential while ensuring that their actions are constructive and not harmful. It calls for the harnessing of inner strength and the importance of working together with others.


Real-Life Examples:

  • In a workplace setting, an employee may strive to "stand upright" by maintaining integrity and ethical standards, ensuring that their work contributes positively to the organization.
  • In community service, individuals can embody the mantra by collaborating with others to uplift those in need, ensuring that their efforts are aimed at constructive outcomes rather than division.
  • In personal development, one might focus on self-improvement and resilience, rising above challenges while fostering positive relationships with others.

Conclusion:

This mantra holds significant relevance in today's world, where the need for integrity, strength, and collaboration is paramount. It serves as a reminder to individuals to rise above challenges, act constructively, and work together for the greater good. By embodying these principles, one can contribute to a more harmonious and productive society.
