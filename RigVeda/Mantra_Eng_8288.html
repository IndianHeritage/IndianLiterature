एष तुन्नो अभिष्टुतः पवित्रम् अति गाहते |रक्षोहा वारम् अव्ययम् ||
eṣa tunno abhiṣṭutaḥ pavitram ati gāhate | rakṣohā vāram avyayam ||
  • eṣa: This word translates to "this" or "he." It indicates a specific entity or presence that is being referred to.
  • tunno: This term can mean "strong" or "powerful." It emphasizes the strength of the subject being discussed.
  • abhiṣṭutaḥ: This word means "praised" or "worshipped." It signifies the reverence and acknowledgment given to the subject.
  • pavitram: Translated as "pure" or "sacred," this term highlights the sanctity and cleanliness associated with the subject.
  • ati: This word means "exceedingly" or "very." It intensifies the quality of the following word.
  • gāhate: This term means "to carry" or "to bear." It suggests the act of holding or supporting something significant.
  • rakṣohā: This word translates to "protection" or "defense." It indicates the safeguarding aspect of the subject.
  • vāram: This term means "shield" or "barrier." It represents a form of defense against negative forces.
  • avyayam: This word means "imperishable" or "eternal." It signifies the everlasting nature of the subject being discussed.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for the presence of a powerful and pure entity that is praised and revered. This entity is seen as a protector, providing an imperishable shield against negative influences. The emphasis on purity and strength suggests that this presence is not only a source of protection but also embodies qualities that are essential for spiritual and physical well-being.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios. For instance:

  • In times of personal crisis or emotional turmoil, individuals may recite this mantra to invoke a sense of strength and protection, helping them navigate through challenges.
  • In a community setting, this mantra can be used during rituals or gatherings to create a sacred space, fostering a sense of unity and collective strength among participants.
  • In the context of mental health, the idea of invoking a protective presence can be a form of positive affirmation, encouraging individuals to feel safe and supported in their environment.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of invoking strength, purity, and protection in our lives. It encourages individuals to seek out and acknowledge the powerful forces that can aid them in overcoming obstacles and maintaining a sense of safety and sanctity in their lives. By integrating the essence of this mantra into daily practices, one can cultivate a deeper connection to the protective energies that surround them.
