संवत्सरं शशयानां ब्राह्मणा व्रतचारीणः |वाचं वर्षाजिन्वितां प्र मण्डूकाः अवादिषुः ||
saṁvatsaraṁ śaśayānā brāhmaṇā vratacāriṇaḥ | vācam parjanyajinvitām pra maṇḍūkā avādiṣuḥ ||
  • saṁvatsaraṁ: This term refers to a year or a cycle of time. In the context of the mantra, it signifies the importance of time and the cyclical nature of life.
  • śaśayānā: This word can be translated as "those who are like the moon" or "moon-like." The moon is often associated with calmness, nurturing, and the passage of time, reflecting the phases of life.
  • brāhmaṇā: This refers to the Brahmins, who are traditionally the priestly class in Hindu society. They are seen as the custodians of knowledge and rituals, playing a crucial role in spiritual and cultural practices.
  • vratacāriṇaḥ: This term means "those who observe vows or commitments." It emphasizes the importance of discipline and dedication in one's spiritual and personal life.
  • vācam: This translates to "speech" or "words." It highlights the power of communication and the significance of what we say.
  • parjanyajinvitām: This word refers to something that is invoked or inspired by rain (parjanya). Rain is a symbol of nourishment and abundance, essential for life and growth.
  • pra: This prefix often means "forth" or "forward," indicating a sense of movement or progression.
  • maṇḍūkā: This term means "frog." Frogs are often associated with transformation and the cycle of life, as they undergo metamorphosis from tadpoles to adult frogs.
  • avādiṣuḥ: This means "they spoke" or "they expressed." It signifies the act of communication and sharing knowledge or experiences.

The mantra speaks to the cyclical nature of time and life, emphasizing the importance of discipline, communication, and the nurturing aspects of existence. It suggests that those who are dedicated to their spiritual practices (brāhmaṇā and vratacāriṇaḥ) can invoke the nourishing aspects of life (parjanyajinvitām) through their words (vācam). The reference to frogs (maṇḍūkā) may symbolize transformation and the ability to adapt to different phases of life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the moon goes through phases, individuals can reflect on their own growth and transformation over time, embracing change and learning from experiences.
  • Communication: The power of words is significant in our daily interactions. Practicing mindful communication can lead to better relationships and understanding among individuals.
  • Commitment to Values: Observing vows or commitments in personal and professional life can lead to a more fulfilling and disciplined existence, much like the dedication of the Brahmins in their spiritual practices.
  • Environmental Awareness: The mention of rain and frogs can serve as a reminder of the importance of nature and the cycles of life, encouraging individuals to be more conscious of their environmental impact.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of time, transformation, and the power of words. It encourages individuals to reflect on their commitments, communicate effectively, and embrace the cycles of life, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
