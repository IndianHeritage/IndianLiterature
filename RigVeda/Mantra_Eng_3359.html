त्वा युजा न खिदत सूर्यस्येन्द्रश चक्रं सहसा सद्य इन्दो |अधि ष्णुना बृहतां वर्तमानं महो द्रुहो अप विश्वायु धायि ||
tvā yujā ni khidat sūryasyendraś cakraṁ sahasā sadya indo | adhi ṣṇunā bṛhatā vartamānam maho druho apa viśvāyu dhāyi ||
  • tvā yujā: This phrase can be interpreted as "to you, united" or "to you, connected." It signifies a sense of togetherness or alignment with a higher power or the universe.
  • ni khidat: This means "may it not be disturbed" or "may it remain undisturbed." It reflects a desire for stability and peace in one's life.
  • sūryasyendraś: This translates to "of the sun, the lord." The sun is often seen as a symbol of energy, vitality, and enlightenment in many cultures.
  • cakraṁ: This word means "wheel" or "circle," often representing cycles of life, time, and the universe.
  • sahasā: This means "suddenly" or "swiftly," indicating a quick or immediate action or change.
  • sadya indo: This can be interpreted as "O Indra," where Indra is a deity associated with rain, storms, and strength. It invokes the divine for assistance.
  • adhi ṣṇunā: This means "upon the seat" or "in the place of." It suggests a foundation or a stable position from which to act.
  • bṛhatā: This translates to "great" or "vast," indicating something of significant magnitude or importance.
  • vartamānam: This means "existing" or "present," referring to the current state of affairs or reality.
  • maho druho: This can be interpreted as "great deception" or "great treachery," suggesting the presence of challenges or obstacles that may arise.
  • apa viśvāyu: This translates to "away from all evil" or "removing all negativity," indicating a desire for protection and positivity.
  • dhāyi: This means "to hold" or "to support," reflecting the idea of stability and strength in the face of adversity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a plea for unity, stability, and divine support in navigating the challenges of life. It emphasizes the importance of remaining undisturbed amidst the chaos and seeking strength from a higher power. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during times of uncertainty or difficulty, such as during personal crises, conflicts, or when facing significant life changes. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of peace, resilience, and connection to the universe, allowing them to navigate their circumstances with greater clarity and strength.
