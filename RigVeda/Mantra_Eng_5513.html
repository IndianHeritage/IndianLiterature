मित्रस्तन नो वरुणो रोधसी च द्युभक्तम इन्द्रो आर्यमा ददातु |दिदेष्टु देव्य अदिती रेक्णो वायुः च यन् नियुवैते भागश च ||
mitras tan no varuṇo rodasī ca dyubhaktam indro aryamā dadātu | dideṣṭu devy aditī rekṇo vāyuś ca yan niyuvaite bhagaś ca ||
  • Mitras: This term refers to "friends" or "allies." In the context of the mantra, it signifies the importance of friendship and cooperation in life.
  • Tan: This means "that" or "this," indicating a connection to the subject being discussed.
  • Varuṇo: Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water, representing the moral and ethical aspects of life.
  • Rodasī: This refers to the "two worlds" or "heavens and earth," symbolizing the connection between the spiritual and material realms.
  • Dyubhaktam: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is in the sky," referring to the divine or celestial beings.
  • Indro: Indra is the king of the gods, associated with strength, power, and protection.
  • Aryamā: This is a deity associated with the path of righteousness and moral conduct.
  • Dadātu: This means "may he give," indicating a request for blessings or support.
  • Dideṣṭu: This term means "may he bestow," further emphasizing the desire for divine favor.
  • Devy: This refers to the divine feminine, representing nurturing and protective qualities.
  • Aditī: Aditī is the mother of the gods, symbolizing abundance and the nurturing aspect of the universe.
  • Rekṇo: This term can be interpreted as "the one who protects," indicating a guardian or protector role.
  • Vāyuś: Vayu is the god of wind, representing movement, change, and the breath of life.
  • Ca: This means "and," connecting the various elements of the mantra.
  • Yan: This means "that which," referring to the actions or qualities being discussed.
  • Niyuvaite: This term means "may he direct," indicating guidance or leadership.
  • Bhagaś: This refers to "prosperity" or "wealth," symbolizing abundance and success.

The mantra essentially calls upon various deities for blessings, protection, and guidance in life. It emphasizes the importance of friendship, moral conduct, and the interconnectedness of the spiritual and material worlds. The invocation of different gods signifies a holistic approach to life, where one seeks support from various aspects of the divine.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in achieving a balanced and prosperous life. In real-life scenarios, it can be applicable in moments of decision-making, seeking guidance in relationships, or during times of uncertainty. By invoking these divine qualities, individuals can foster a sense of community, moral integrity, and abundance in their lives.
