कृतं न श्वघ्नī वि चिनोति देवने संवरगं यन् मगवाऽ सूर्यं जयत् |न तत्ते अन्यो अनु वीर्यं शकन न पुराणो मगवन नोत नूतनः ||
kṛtaṁ na śvaghnī vi cinoti devane saṁvargaṁ yan maghavā sūryaṁ jayat | na tat te anyo anu vīryaṁ śakan na purāṇo maghavan nota nūtanaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kṛtaṁ: done, accomplished
    • na: not
    • śvaghnī: the one who kills a dog (a metaphor for a sinful act)
    • vi: indeed
    • cinoti: takes, receives
    • devane: to the divine, to the gods
    • saṁvargaṁ: the gathering, the assembly
    • yan: which
    • maghavā: the powerful one, often referring to Indra or a deity
    • sūryaṁ: the sun
    • jayat: conquers, wins
    • na: not
    • tat: that
    • te: to you
    • anyo: another
    • anu: after, following
    • vīryaṁ: strength, valor
    • śakan: can, able
    • na: not
    • purāṇo: ancient, old
    • maghavan: the bountiful one, often referring to a deity
    • nota: nor
    • nūtanaḥ: new, modern
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the actions that are not tainted by sin and how they are recognized by the divine.
    • It emphasizes that the deeds done with purity and righteousness are acknowledged by the gods.
    • The reference to the sun symbolizes enlightenment, truth, and the ultimate victory of good over evil.
    • The mantra suggests that neither ancient nor modern actions can match the strength of those that are pure and divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, ethical business practices can be seen as "kṛtaṁ" (accomplished) actions that lead to long-term success, while unethical practices (śvaghnī) can lead to downfall.
    • In personal relationships, acts of kindness and honesty are recognized and rewarded, akin to the divine acknowledgment mentioned in the mantra.
    • In community service, selfless acts that benefit others resonate with the idea of actions that are not tainted by sin and are celebrated by society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of performing righteous actions that are free from sin.
    • It serves as a reminder that true strength and valor come from purity of intent and action.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as personal integrity, ethical business practices, and community involvement, reinforcing the idea that good deeds lead to divine recognition and success.
