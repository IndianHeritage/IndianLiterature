मन्त्र: प्रतिः श्रुताय वो धृतश तूर्णाशं न गिरेर अधि | हुवे सुशिप्रम ऊतये ||
prati śrutāya vo dhṛṣat tūrṇāśaṁ na girer adhi | huve suśipram ūtaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prati: towards, in response to
    • śrutāya: to the one who has heard, to the one who listens
    • vo: your
    • dhṛṣat: courage, strength
    • tūrṇāśaṁ: swift, quick
    • na: not
    • girer: of the mountain, of the high place
    • adhi: above, over
    • huve: I invoke, I call upon
    • suśipram: very auspiciously, with great benefit
    • ūtaye: for the purpose of elevation, for upliftment
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra can be understood as a call for strength and courage directed towards those who listen and respond.
    • The phrase "tūrṇāśaṁ na girer adhi" suggests that the strength we seek is not just lofty or distant, like a mountain, but can be accessed swiftly and effectively.
    • The invocation "huve suśipram ūtaye" emphasizes the importance of calling upon auspicious energies for personal and collective upliftment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as preparing for a major exam or presentation, one might recite this mantra to invoke courage and clarity of thought.
    • In team settings, such as sports or collaborative projects, this mantra can serve as a reminder to support each other and draw upon collective strength for swift success.
    • During times of personal crisis, individuals may find solace in the idea that they can call upon inner strength and auspicious energies to navigate their challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its powerful message of invoking courage and strength in the face of challenges.
    • It teaches us that we can access our inner resources quickly and effectively, rather than waiting for external circumstances to change.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various situations where support, strength, and upliftment are needed, reminding us of the power of collective and individual resilience.
