नृधूतो अदृषुतो बर्हिषि प्रियः पतिर्गवाम् प्रदिव इन्दुर ऋत्वियः |पुरंढिवान् मनुषो यज्ञसाधनः शुचिर् धियā पवते सोम इन्द्र ते ||
nṛdhūto adriṣuto barhiṣi priyaḥ patir gavām pradiva indur ṛtviyaḥ | puraṁdhivān manuṣo yajñasādhanaḥ śucir dhiyā pavate soma indra te ||
  • nṛdhūto: This term can be interpreted as "one who is moved or stirred." In a real-life context, it can refer to someone who is inspired or motivated to take action, much like a leader who inspires their team to achieve a common goal.
  • adriṣuto: This word means "one who is unseen" or "hidden." It can symbolize the unseen forces in our lives, such as intuition or the subconscious mind, which guide our decisions and actions.
  • barhiṣi: This refers to "the sacred grass" used in rituals. In modern life, it can represent the importance of rituals and traditions that connect us to our heritage and community.
  • priyaḥ patir: Translated as "beloved lord" or "dear master," this phrase can signify a figure of authority or a mentor in our lives who guides us with love and wisdom.
  • gavām: Meaning "of the cows," this term symbolizes nourishment and sustenance. In contemporary terms, it can represent the importance of agriculture and the nurturing aspects of life.
  • pradiva: This word means "in the sky" or "heavenly." It can be associated with aspirations and goals that are lofty and idealistic, encouraging individuals to reach for their dreams.
  • indur: Referring to "the moon," it symbolizes calmness and serenity. In real life, it can represent the need for peace and tranquility amidst chaos.
  • ṛtviyaḥ: This term means "truthful" or "righteous." It emphasizes the importance of living a life of integrity and honesty, which is essential in personal and professional relationships.
  • puraṁdhivān: This translates to "the one who is the master of the city" or "the ruler." It can represent leadership and governance, highlighting the qualities of a good leader who serves their community.
  • manuṣo: Meaning "human," it emphasizes our shared humanity and the importance of compassion and empathy in our interactions with others.
  • yajñasādhanaḥ: This term refers to "the one who performs sacrifices or rituals." In modern life, it can symbolize the importance of dedication and effort in achieving our goals.
  • śucir: Meaning "pure," it signifies the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions, which is crucial for personal growth and spiritual development.
  • dhiyā: This word means "with wisdom" or "intellect." It highlights the importance of knowledge and understanding in making informed decisions.
  • pavate: Translated as "to blow" or "to purify," it can symbolize the cleansing of negative energies and the importance of maintaining a positive environment.
  • soma: Referring to the sacred drink, it symbolizes nourishment for the soul and the importance of spiritual sustenance in our lives.
  • indra: This term refers to "the king of gods" and symbolizes strength and power. In real life, it can represent the qualities of a strong leader who protects and guides their people.
  • te: Meaning "to you," it signifies a connection or offering to the divine or to a higher purpose.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, integrity, and the importance of rituals in our lives. It emphasizes the need for wisdom, purity, and the nurturing aspects of life, encouraging individuals to strive for their goals while remaining connected to their roots and community. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can serve as a reminder to lead with compassion, uphold truthfulness, and engage in practices that promote personal and communal well-being. It highlights the significance of balancing aspirations with groundedness, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal development to community leadership.
