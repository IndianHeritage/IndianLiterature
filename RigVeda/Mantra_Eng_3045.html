देवस्य सवितुर्वयं वाजयन्तः पुरन्ध्या |भागस्य रातिम ईमहे ||
devasya savitur vayaṁ vājayantaḥ puraṁdhyā | bhagasya rātim īmahe ||
  • Devasya: This word refers to the divine or the godly aspect. It signifies the presence of a higher power or consciousness that governs the universe.
  • Savitur: This term is associated with the Sun, representing light, energy, and life. It symbolizes the source of all creation and the sustainer of life.
  • Vayaṁ: This means "we" or "our," indicating a collective identity or community. It emphasizes the importance of unity among individuals.
  • Vājayantaḥ: This word translates to "those who are strong" or "those who are powerful." It suggests the idea of strength, courage, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • Puraṁdhyā: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is the creator of the universe" or "the architect of the world." It highlights the creative aspect of the divine.
  • Bhagasya: This word refers to "prosperity" or "wealth." It signifies abundance and the blessings that come from the divine.
  • Rātim: This means "joy" or "delight." It emphasizes the importance of happiness and contentment in life.
  • Īmahe: This translates to "we meditate" or "we contemplate." It indicates a practice of reflection and connection with the divine.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation to the divine source of light and energy, asking for strength, prosperity, and joy. It emphasizes the importance of community and collective strength in seeking blessings from the divine.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use this mantra as a daily affirmation to seek strength and positivity in their lives, encouraging them to overcome challenges.
  • Community Building: Groups or communities can chant this mantra together to foster unity and collective strength, promoting a sense of belonging and shared purpose.
  • Spiritual Practice: It can be incorporated into meditation or yoga practices, helping individuals connect with their inner selves and the divine energy around them.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance and support in achieving strength, prosperity, and joy. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking blessings from a higher power. By incorporating this mantra into daily life, individuals and communities can cultivate a sense of purpose, resilience, and harmony.
