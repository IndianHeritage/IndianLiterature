अर्वन्तो न श्रवसो भिक्षमाणाः इन्द्रवायू सुश्रुतिभिर वसिष्ठाः |वाजयन्तः स्व अवसे हुवेम यूम पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
arvanto na śravaso bhikṣamāṇā indravāyū suṣṭutibhir vasiṣṭhāḥ | vājayantaḥ sv avase huvema yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • arvanto: Those who are strong or powerful.
    • na: Not.
    • śravaso: Of fame or glory.
    • bhikṣamāṇā: Seeking or desiring.
    • indravāyū: Indra, the god of rain and thunder, and Vayu, the god of wind.
    • suṣṭutibhir: With excellent praises.
    • vasiṣṭhāḥ: The best or the most excellent.
    • vājayantaḥ: Those who bring strength or nourishment.
    • sv: Our or own.
    • avase: In our abode or dwelling.
    • huvema: We invoke or call upon.
    • yūyam: You (plural).
    • pāta: Protect or safeguard.
    • svastibhiḥ: With auspiciousness or well-being.
    • sadā: Always.
    • naḥ: Us or our.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the powerful deities Indra and Vayu, seeking their blessings and protection.
    • It emphasizes the importance of strength and glory, not just for oneself but for the community.
    • The phrase "with excellent praises" suggests that one should always honor and respect the divine forces.
    • By calling upon these deities, the mantra seeks to ensure well-being and auspiciousness in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, people often invoke blessings from higher powers for strength and unity, similar to how this mantra calls upon Indra and Vayu.
    • During festivals, rituals are performed to honor deities, reflecting the idea of praising and seeking protection from divine forces.
    • In personal challenges, individuals may seek strength and guidance through prayer or meditation, resonating with the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine strength and protection in our lives.
    • It teaches us to honor the forces that sustain us and to seek their blessings for our well-being and success.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal struggles or community events, the principles of strength, unity, and respect for the divine remain relevant and essential.
