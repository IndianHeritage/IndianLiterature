उत नः प्रियाः प्रियासु सप्तस्वसा सुजुष्टा |सरस्वती स्तोम्या भूत्वा ||
uta naḥ priyā priyāsu saptasvasā sujuṣṭā | sarasvatī stomyā bhūt ||
  • uta naḥ - This phrase translates to "may it be for us." It expresses a desire or a prayer for something beneficial to come to us.
  • priyā priyāsu - This means "dear to us" or "beloved." It emphasizes the importance of love and affection in our lives.
  • saptasvasā - This term can be interpreted as "sevenfold" or "having seven qualities." In many cultures, the number seven is considered sacred and represents completeness or perfection.
  • sujuṣṭā - This word means "well-formed" or "beautiful." It signifies the importance of beauty and harmony in our surroundings and lives.
  • sarasvatī - Sarasvatī is the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning in Hindu mythology. Invoking her signifies a quest for knowledge and enlightenment.
  • stomyā - This term refers to "praise" or "hymn." It indicates the act of expressing gratitude and reverence, particularly towards divine entities.
  • bhūt - This means "existence" or "being." It reflects the essence of life and the importance of being present in the moment.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates a prayer for blessings that are dear to us, emphasizing the importance of love, beauty, and knowledge in our lives. It encourages us to seek completeness and harmony while expressing gratitude for the existence of these qualities. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal reflection, in educational pursuits, or in creative endeavors. By invoking the essence of Sarasvatī, individuals can strive for wisdom and artistic expression, fostering a deeper connection with themselves and the world around them.
