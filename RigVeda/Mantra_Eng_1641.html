यः पूर्व्याय वेधसे नवीयसे सुमज्जानये विष्णवे ददाशति |यो जातम अस्य महतो महि ब्रवत् सेदु श्रवोभिर युज्यं चिद अभ्य असत् ||
yaḥ pūrvyāya vedhase navīyase sumajjānaye viṣṇave dadāśati | yo jātam asya mahato mahi bravat sed u śravobhir yujyaṁ cid abhy asat ||
  • yaḥ pūrvyāya vedhase: This phrase can be translated as "He who is the ancient seer." It refers to a divine figure or deity who possesses profound wisdom and knowledge, often associated with the Vedic tradition.
  • navīyase: This word means "new" or "fresh." It signifies the renewal of knowledge and the continuous evolution of understanding in life.
  • sumajjānaye: Translated as "to the well-disposed," this term indicates a benevolent and kind-hearted nature, suggesting that the divine is approachable and supportive of those who seek wisdom.
  • viṣṇave: This refers to Lord Vishnu, a principal deity in Hinduism known as the preserver and protector of the universe. His role is crucial in maintaining cosmic order.
  • dadāśati: This means "gives" or "bestows." It implies that the divine grants blessings, knowledge, and support to those who are deserving.
  • yo jātam asya mahato mahi: This translates to "He who is born from the great earth." It emphasizes the connection between the divine and the material world, highlighting the importance of nature and the earth in spiritual practices.
  • bravat: This word means "speaks" or "proclaims." It suggests the act of sharing knowledge and wisdom, reinforcing the idea of communication between the divine and humanity.
  • sed u śravobhir: This phrase can be interpreted as "with the best of glories." It indicates that the divine is associated with the highest forms of honor and respect.
  • yujyaṁ cid abhy asat: This translates to "united with consciousness." It signifies the importance of awareness and mindfulness in connecting with the divine and understanding one's place in the universe.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine wisdom and guidance through a connection with nature and consciousness. It emphasizes the importance of being well-disposed and open to receiving knowledge from the divine, particularly from Lord Vishnu, who represents preservation and protection. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in practices such as meditation, where individuals seek to connect with their inner selves and the universe, fostering a sense of peace and understanding. It serves as a reminder to approach life with kindness and an open heart, allowing for the continuous renewal of knowledge and wisdom in our daily lives.
