मन्त्र: न हि त्वा रोदसी उभे ऋघायमानम् इन्बतः |जेषः स्वर्वतीर अपः सं गा अस्मभ्यं धूनुहि ||
nahi tvā rodasī ubhe ṛghāyamāṇam invataḥ | jeṣaḥ svarvatīr apaḥ saṁ gā asmabhyaṁ dhūnuhi ||
  • Translation: "Indeed, you do not obstruct the two worlds; the one who is being invoked, the victorious waters, may they cleanse us."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • nahi: indeed, not
    • tvā: you
    • rodasī: the two worlds (often interpreted as the physical and spiritual realms)
    • ubhe: both
    • ṛghāyamāṇam: being invoked or called upon
    • invataḥ: victorious or triumphant
    • jeṣaḥ: the one who conquers
    • svarvatīr: waters, often symbolizing purity and cleansing
    • apaḥ: waters
    • saṁ: together
    • gā: may they sing or chant
    • asmabhyaṁ: for us
    • dhūnuhi: may they cleanse or purify

  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra emphasizes the idea of divine presence that transcends both the physical and spiritual realms.
    • The invocation of "victorious waters" symbolizes the cleansing and purifying aspects of nature, which can be related to the importance of water in various cultures as a source of life and renewal.
    • In real life, this can be seen in rituals where water is used for purification, such as in Hindu ceremonies where water is sprinkled to cleanse the space and participants.

  • Significance:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all realms of existence and the importance of seeking purity and clarity in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to invoke higher powers for guidance and cleansing, which can be applicable in various situations, such as during times of stress or confusion.
    • In a broader sense, it highlights the significance of nature and its elements, urging us to respect and honor them in our daily lives.

  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine intervention for purification and clarity, applicable in both personal and communal contexts.
    • By recognizing the power of the elements, particularly water, we can foster a deeper connection with nature and enhance our spiritual practices.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a call to action for individuals to embrace the cleansing power of nature and the divine, leading to a more harmonious existence.
