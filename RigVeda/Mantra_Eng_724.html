त्वं सत्य इन्द्र धृष्णुर एतान त्वम् ऋभुक्षा नार्यस त्वं शाट् |त्वं शुष्णं वृजन पृक्ष आणौ यूने कुत्साय द्युमते सचाहन ||
tvaṁ satya indra dhṛṣṇur etān tvam ṛbhukṣā naryas tvaṁ ṣāṭ | tvaṁ śuṣṇaṁ vṛjane pṛkṣa āṇau yūne kutsāya dyumate sacāhan ||
  • tvaṁ satya indra dhṛṣṇur: This phrase translates to "You are the truth, O Indra, the strong one." Here, "tvaṁ" means "you," "satya" means "truth," "indra" refers to the king of gods, and "dhṛṣṇur" means "the strong one." This highlights the importance of truth and strength in leadership.
  • etān tvam ṛbhukṣā naryas: This means "You are the one who nourishes these men." "Etān" means "these," "tvam" means "you," "ṛbhukṣā" refers to "nourisher," and "naryas" means "men." This emphasizes the role of a leader or divine figure in providing sustenance and support to people.
  • tvaṁ ṣāṭ: This translates to "You are the one who is wise." "Ṣāṭ" signifies wisdom or intelligence. It suggests that wisdom is a crucial attribute for anyone in a position of authority.
  • tvaṁ śuṣṇaṁ vṛjane: This means "You are the one who brings prosperity." "Śuṣṇaṁ" refers to prosperity or wealth, and "vṛjane" means "to bring." This indicates that the divine or leaders have the power to bestow prosperity upon their followers.
  • pṛkṣa āṇau: This translates to "You are the one who sees everything." "Pṛkṣa" means "to see," and "āṇau" refers to "in all directions." This suggests that true leaders or divine beings possess the ability to perceive the needs and challenges of their people.
  • yūne kutsāya dyumate sacāhan: This phrase can be interpreted as "You are the one who brings light and joy." "Yūne" means "to bring," "kutsāya" refers to "joy," "dyumate" means "light," and "sacāhan" signifies "truth." This highlights the role of divine or wise leaders in illuminating the path for their followers and bringing happiness into their lives.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the qualities of truth, strength, wisdom, and the ability to nourish and bring prosperity. In real-life scenarios, these attributes are essential for effective leadership, whether in governance, community leadership, or personal relationships. Leaders who embody these qualities can inspire trust, provide support, and guide their followers towards a prosperous and joyful existence. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that true leadership is rooted in integrity and the well-being of others.
