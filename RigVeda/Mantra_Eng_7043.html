यथा मनौ विवस्वति सोमं शक्रापिभः सुतम् |यथा तृते छन्द इन्द्र जुजोषस्य आयौ मादयसे सचा ||
yathā manau vivasvati somaṁ śakrāpibaḥ sutam | yathā trite chanda indra jujoṣasy āyau mādayase sacā ||
  • yathā manau: This phrase translates to "as in the mind." It suggests the importance of intention and thought in our actions and decisions.
  • vivasvati: Referring to the sun or the radiant one, it symbolizes enlightenment, knowledge, and clarity. In real life, this can be likened to the pursuit of wisdom and understanding.
  • somaṁ: Soma is often associated with a divine drink that grants immortality and bliss. It represents the essence of life and vitality, akin to the nourishment we seek in our daily lives.
  • śakrāpibaḥ: This term refers to Indra, the king of gods, who is often depicted as a powerful figure. The phrase suggests the act of drinking or consuming, which can be interpreted as taking in knowledge or experiences that empower us.
  • sutam: Meaning "the son" or "the offspring," it signifies the results of our actions and the legacy we leave behind. This can relate to how our choices impact future generations.
  • yathā trite: This translates to "as in the third," indicating a connection to the triad of existence—past, present, and future. It emphasizes the continuity of life and the importance of learning from history.
  • chanda: Referring to rhythm or meter, it symbolizes the harmony in life. Just as music requires rhythm, our lives require balance and order.
  • indra: Again invoking the name of Indra, it highlights the need for strength and courage in facing challenges. In real life, this can be seen in leaders who inspire others to overcome obstacles.
  • jujoṣasy: This means "to enjoy" or "to delight in," suggesting that joy and fulfillment come from our experiences and connections with others.
  • āyau: This term can be interpreted as "to come" or "to arrive," indicating the importance of welcoming new opportunities and experiences into our lives.
  • mādayase: Meaning "to be intoxicated" or "to be filled with joy," it reflects the idea of being overwhelmed by positive experiences, akin to moments of happiness we encounter.
  • sacā: This translates to "with truth" or "with sincerity," emphasizing the importance of honesty and integrity in our actions and relationships.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of living a life filled with intention, knowledge, joy, and integrity. It serves as a reminder that our thoughts and actions shape our reality and influence those around us. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, education, and personal relationships, where the pursuit of wisdom, the enjoyment of life, and the commitment to truth are paramount. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling existence, both for ourselves and for future generations.
