वायव आ याहि दर्शतेमे सोमाऽरंकृताः |तेषां पाहि श्रुधी हवम् ||
vāyav ā yāhi darśateme somā araṁkṛtāḥ | teṣām pāhi śrudhī havam ||
  • vāyav: This word refers to the wind or air, symbolizing movement, life force, and the unseen energies that surround us.
  • ā yāhi: This phrase translates to "come here" or "approach," indicating a call for the wind or life force to come closer or to be present.
  • darśateme: This means "show me" or "reveal to me," suggesting a desire for clarity, understanding, or insight.
  • somā: Referring to the sacred drink or elixir, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration, symbolizing nourishment for the soul.
  • araṁkṛtāḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who are prepared" or "those who are adorned," indicating readiness and the importance of being in a state of receptivity.
  • teṣām: Meaning "of them," referring to the entities or forces being called upon.
  • pāhi: This translates to "protect" or "safeguard," indicating a plea for safety and guidance.
  • śrudhī: This word means "intelligence" or "wisdom," emphasizing the importance of discernment and understanding in our actions.
  • havam: Referring to offerings or invocations, suggesting a call to the divine or higher powers for assistance.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation to the wind, asking it to come closer and reveal its wisdom. It emphasizes the importance of being prepared and receptive to the divine energies that surround us, seeking nourishment for the soul and protection through wisdom.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Seeking Guidance: When faced with difficult decisions, one can recite this mantra to invite clarity and insight into their situation.
  • Preparation for Challenges: Before embarking on a new journey or project, this mantra serves as a reminder to be prepared and open to the energies that will support you.
  • Mindfulness and Awareness: In moments of stress or confusion, invoking this mantra can help center oneself, reminding us to seek wisdom and protection from the chaos around us.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for connection with the life force and divine wisdom. It teaches us the importance of being prepared and receptive to the energies that guide us. By incorporating this mantra into our daily lives, we can cultivate a deeper understanding of ourselves and the world around us, leading to a more harmonious existence.
