को nv अत्र मरुतो मामहे वः प्र यातन सखींर अच्छा सखायः |मनमानि चित्रा अपिवातयन्त एषां भूत नवेद मा ऋतानाम् ||
ko nv atra maruto māmahe vaḥ pra yātana sakhīm̐r acchā sakhāyaḥ | manmāni citrā apivātayanta eṣām bhūta navedā ma ṛtānām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ko: who
    • nv: indeed
    • atra: here
    • maruto: O Winds
    • māmahe: we invoke
    • vaḥ: your
    • pra: for
    • yātana: effort
    • sakhīm̐r: friend
    • acchā: good
    • sakhāyaḥ: companions
    • manmāni: my thoughts
    • citrā: diverse
    • apivātayanta: even as they blow
    • eṣām: of them
    • bhūta: beings
    • navedā: new
    • ma: not
    • ṛtānām: of the seasons
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to the Maruts, the divine winds or storm deities in Vedic literature.
    • The speaker is invoking the Maruts to come forth and assist in their endeavors, emphasizing the importance of friendship and companionship.
    • The phrase "my thoughts" suggests a desire for clarity and direction, as the winds are seen as carriers of messages and inspiration.
    • The mention of "new beings" and "seasons" indicates a connection to change and renewal, highlighting the cyclical nature of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be likened to seeking collaboration and support from colleagues to achieve a common goal.
    • In personal relationships, it reflects the importance of having good friends who provide encouragement and positivity during challenging times.
    • In nature, the changing seasons remind us of the inevitability of change and the need to adapt, much like the winds that bring new life and energy.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on community, support, and the dynamic nature of existence.
    • It teaches us to value our relationships and to seek help when needed, recognizing that we are not alone in our journeys.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, from teamwork in the workplace to nurturing friendships and embracing the changes that life brings.
