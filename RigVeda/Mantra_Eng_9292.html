मह्यं त्वष्टा वज्रम अतक्षद आयसम मयि देवासो 'वृजन्ति अपि क्रतुम् |ममानीकं सूर्यस्येव दुष्टारं माम आर्यन्ति कृतेन कर्त्वेन च ||
mahyaṁ tvaṣṭā vajram atakṣad āyasam mayi devāso 'vṛjann api kratum | mamānīkaṁ sūryasyeva duṣṭaram mām āryanti kṛtena kartvena ca ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mahyaṁ: to me
    • tvaṣṭā: O Tvaṣṭa (the divine craftsman)
    • vajram: the thunderbolt or weapon
    • atakṣad: has not struck
    • āyasam: iron or metal
    • mayi: in me
    • devāso: the gods
    • 'vṛjann: having not abandoned
    • api: even
    • kratum: the intention or resolve
    • mamānīkaṁ: my assembly or group
    • sūryasyeva: like the sun
    • duṣṭaram: difficult or troublesome
    • mām: me
    • āryanti: protect or save
    • kṛtena: by action
    • kartvena: by the doer
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Tvaṣṭa, the divine craftsman, asking for protection and strength.
    • The reference to the "vajram" symbolizes power and resilience, suggesting that the speaker seeks to be fortified against challenges.
    • The phrase "mayi devāso 'vṛjann api kratum" indicates that even the gods do not abandon the speaker's resolve, emphasizing the importance of inner strength and determination.
    • The comparison to the sun ("sūryasyeva") highlights the idea of radiance and clarity, suggesting that the speaker wishes to shine brightly despite difficulties.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when facing challenges at work, one can draw strength from this mantra, reminding oneself of the inner resolve and support from colleagues (the "gods").
    • In personal life, during tough times such as illness or loss, invoking the spirit of this mantra can help individuals find resilience and the courage to move forward.
    • In community settings, leaders can use the essence of this mantra to inspire unity and collective strength, ensuring that no one feels abandoned in their struggles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of inner strength, divine support, and resilience in the face of adversity.
    • It serves as a reminder that even in difficult times, one can find protection and encouragement from both within and from the community.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, from personal challenges to collective efforts, reinforcing the idea that strength and support are always available.
