त्वां इद अत्र वृणते त्वायवो होतारं अग्ने विदथेṣu वेधसः |यद देवयन्तो दधति प्रयांसी ते हविष्मन्तो मनवो वृक्तबरिषः ||
tvām id atra vṛṇate tvāyavo hotāram agne vidatheṣu vedhasaḥ | yad devayanto dadhati prayāṁsi te haviṣmanto manavo vṛktabarhiṣaḥ ||
  • tvām id atra vṛṇate: This phrase translates to "We invoke you here." It signifies the act of calling upon a divine presence, which can be likened to how people seek guidance or blessings in various rituals or prayers in everyday life.
  • tvāyavo hotāram: This means "as the one who is the hota (priest) for us." In Vedic rituals, the hota is the one who offers the oblations to the fire. This can be compared to a leader or a guide in modern contexts, such as a teacher or a mentor who helps individuals navigate through challenges.
  • agne vidatheṣu vedhasaḥ: Here, "Agni" refers to the fire god, symbolizing energy and transformation. "Vidatheṣu" means "in the gatherings," and "vedhasaḥ" refers to the wise or knowledgeable. This can be related to how fire is used in various cultures for purification and transformation, such as in cooking or in ceremonies that mark significant life events.
  • yad devayanto dadhati: This translates to "what the divine beings establish." It emphasizes the connection between human actions and divine will, similar to how people often feel that their efforts are guided by a higher power or fate in their personal and professional lives.
  • prayāṁsi te haviṣmanto: This means "your offerings are abundant." It reflects the idea of generosity and the importance of giving back, which is evident in many cultures through acts of charity and community service.
  • manavo vṛktabarhiṣaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the minds that are focused on the sacred." It highlights the significance of intention and mindfulness in actions, akin to how individuals today practice mindfulness and meditation to achieve clarity and purpose in their lives.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine guidance, the role of leaders and mentors, and the transformative power of energy (symbolized by fire). It teaches us to recognize the connection between our actions and the divine, encouraging generosity and mindfulness. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, community service, and spiritual practices, reminding us to seek guidance, act with intention, and contribute positively to the world around us.
