गुहा शिरो निहितं ऋधग अक्षी असिन्वन्न अत्ति जिव्हया वनानि |अत्राण्य अस्मै पद्भिः सम भरन्त्य उत्तानहस्तā नमसाधि विक्षु ||
guhā śiro nihitam ṛdhag akṣī asinvann atti jihvayā vanāni | atrāṇy asmai paḍbhiḥ sam bharanty uttānahastā namasādhi vikṣu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • guhā: cave or hidden place
    • śiro: head or top
    • nihitam: placed or situated
    • ṛdhag: in a prosperous or beneficial manner
    • akṣī: eyes or vision
    • asinvann: moving or flowing
    • atti: to eat or consume
    • jihvayā: by the tongue
    • vanāni: forests or woods
    • atrāṇy: here or in this place
    • asmai: to him or to that one
    • paḍbhiḥ: by the words or hymns
    • sam bharanty: they carry together
    • uttānahastā: with raised hands
    • namasādhi: to bow or pay homage
    • vikṣu: to the seeker or the one who is searching
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a hidden or profound truth that is situated at the top or in the mind.
    • The "eyes" represent perception or understanding, while "moving" suggests the dynamic nature of knowledge.
    • The act of "eating by the tongue" symbolizes the consumption of knowledge through speech or communication.
    • The forests represent the vastness of knowledge and experiences that one can explore.
    • The phrase "they carry together" indicates the collective effort in seeking wisdom.
    • Raising hands in homage signifies respect and reverence towards the pursuit of knowledge.
    • The seeker is encouraged to bow down to the wisdom that is being sought.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, students often explore various subjects (the forests) to gain knowledge (the hidden truth).
    • In discussions, sharing ideas (eating by the tongue) helps in collective learning.
    • In spiritual practices, individuals often raise their hands in prayer or meditation, showing respect for the knowledge they seek.
    • In teamwork, members contribute their insights (carrying together) to achieve a common goal.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and wisdom in a respectful and collective manner.
    • It highlights the dynamic nature of learning and the need to explore various avenues (forests) to uncover deeper truths.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to approach knowledge with humility and reverence, recognizing the interconnectedness of all learning experiences.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to be seekers of wisdom, honoring the journey of discovery.
