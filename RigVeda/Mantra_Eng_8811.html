विष्वेषां ह्यध्वराणां अनीकं चित्रं केतुं जनिता त्वा जजाना | स आ यजस्व नृवतीर अनु क्षाः स्पार्हा इषः क्षुमतीर विष्वजन्याः ||
viśveṣāṁ hy adhvarāṇām anīkaṁ citraṁ ketuṁ janitā tvā jajāna | sa ā yajasva nṛvatīr anu kṣāḥ spārhā iṣaḥ kṣumatīr viśvajanyāḥ ||
  • viśveṣāṁ hy adhvarāṇām: This phrase translates to "of all the sacrifices." It emphasizes the universality and inclusiveness of the offerings made to the divine. In real life, this can be seen in various cultural practices where people come together to celebrate and honor a higher power, such as community festivals or religious gatherings.
  • anīkaṁ citraṁ ketuṁ: This means "a unique banner or emblem." The term "citraṁ" suggests diversity and beauty, while "ketuṁ" refers to a flag or standard. In a modern context, this can be likened to how different organizations or movements adopt symbols that represent their values and aspirations, such as the rainbow flag for LGBTQ+ rights.
  • janitā tvā jajāna: This translates to "the one who has given birth to you." It signifies the source or origin of something. In everyday life, this can relate to the idea of nurturing and creation, such as parents raising their children or mentors guiding their protégés.
  • sa ā yajasva: This means "may he worship you." It reflects the act of devotion and reverence towards the divine. In contemporary society, this can be seen in various forms of worship, whether through prayer, meditation, or acts of kindness and service to others.
  • nṛvatīr anu kṣāḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who follows the path of righteousness." It emphasizes the importance of ethical living and moral conduct. In real life, this can be exemplified by individuals who strive to make ethical choices in their personal and professional lives, such as whistleblowers who expose wrongdoing.
  • spārhā iṣaḥ kṣumatīr: This translates to "the desire for prosperity and well-being." It highlights the human aspiration for success and happiness. In modern contexts, this can be seen in the pursuit of career goals, financial stability, and personal fulfillment through education and self-improvement.
  • viśvajanyāḥ: This means "of all that is born." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the shared experience of life. In real life, this can be reflected in the global movement towards sustainability and environmental conservation, recognizing that all life forms are interdependent.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of universality, devotion, ethical living, and the interconnectedness of all beings. Its significance lies in the reminder that our actions, whether in worship, ethical choices, or the pursuit of prosperity, contribute to a greater whole. In real life, this mantra encourages individuals to engage in practices that honor their origins, promote well-being, and foster a sense of community and responsibility towards one another and the environment.
