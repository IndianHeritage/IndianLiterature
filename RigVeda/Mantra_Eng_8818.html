अस्य शुष्मासो ददृशानपवेर्जहमानस्य स्वनयन नीयुद्भिः |प्रत्नेभिर्यो रुशद्भिर देवतमो वि रेब्हद्भिर अरतिर भाति विभ्वा ||
asya śuṣmāso dadṛśānapaver jehamānasya svanayan niyudbhiḥ | pratnebhir yo ruśadbhir devatamo vi rebhadbhir aratir bhāti vibhvā ||
  • Mantra Breakdown:
    • asya: This word translates to "of this" or "of this person." It indicates a connection to the speaker or the subject being addressed.
    • śuṣmāso: Refers to "the radiant" or "the luminous." It signifies brightness or clarity, often associated with divine qualities.
    • dadṛśānapaver: This can be interpreted as "having seen" or "having perceived." It suggests a sense of awareness or enlightenment.
    • jehamānasya: This word means "of the one who is moving" or "of the one who is active." It implies dynamism and the pursuit of goals.
    • svanayan: Translates to "with the eyes" or "with vision." It emphasizes the importance of perception and insight.
    • niyudbhiḥ: This means "with the bonds" or "with the connections." It suggests the idea of relationships or ties that bind.
    • pratnebhir: Refers to "with the offerings" or "with the gifts." It indicates the act of giving or sharing.
    • yo: This means "who" or "that one." It introduces a subject or a person of significance.
    • ruśadbhir: Translates to "with the powerful" or "with the strong." It denotes strength and capability.
    • devatamo: This means "the most divine" or "the supreme deity." It refers to a higher power or ultimate reality.
    • vi: This can be interpreted as "in" or "within." It indicates a sense of presence or existence.
    • rebhadbhir: Refers to "with the radiant" or "with the shining." It emphasizes brilliance and illumination.
    • aratir: This means "the one who is free" or "the one who is liberated." It signifies freedom from constraints.
    • bhāti: Translates to "shines" or "illuminates." It indicates the presence of light or clarity.
    • vibhvā: This means "with abundance" or "with richness." It suggests prosperity and fulfillment.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of perception, awareness, and the pursuit of enlightenment. It encourages individuals to seek clarity and understanding in their lives.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as:
      • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra speaks of moving towards enlightenment, individuals can strive for self-improvement and personal development through education and self-reflection.
      • Relationships: The mention of bonds and connections highlights the importance of nurturing relationships with family, friends, and the community, fostering a sense of belonging and support.
      • Spiritual Practices: The invocation of divine qualities encourages individuals to engage in spiritual practices, such as meditation or prayer, to connect with a higher power and seek guidance.
      • Community Service: The idea of offerings and gifts can inspire acts of kindness and generosity, promoting a culture of giving and support within society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the pursuit of knowledge, and the importance of divine guidance. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can lead more fulfilling and purposeful lives.
