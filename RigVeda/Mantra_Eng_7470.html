आ नो विश्वासु हव्य इन्द्रः समत्सु भूषतु |उप ब्रह्माणि सवनानि वृत्रहा परमज्या ऋचिषमः ||
ā no viśvāsu havya indraḥ samatsu bhūṣatu | upa brahmāṇi savanāni vṛtrahā paramajyā ṛcīṣamaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: towards
    • no: us
    • viśvāsu: in all things
    • havya: worthy of offering
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods
    • samatsu: in the assembly
    • bhūṣatu: may he adorn
    • upa: near
    • brahmāṇi: the sacred chants
    • savanāni: the offerings
    • vṛtrahā: the slayer of Vritra (a demon)
    • paramajyā: the supreme glory
    • ṛcīṣamaḥ: we sing praises
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, who represents strength, power, and the forces of nature.
    • The phrase "ā no viśvāsu havya indraḥ" signifies a call for Indra to come towards us in all aspects of life, suggesting a desire for his blessings in various endeavors.
    • "samatsu bhūṣatu" implies that in gatherings or assemblies, Indra should adorn us, indicating the importance of community and collective strength.
    • The mention of "upa brahmāṇi savanāni" highlights the significance of sacred chants and offerings, which are essential in rituals to invoke divine presence.
    • "vṛtrahā" refers to Indra as the slayer of Vritra, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil, and the removal of obstacles in our lives.
    • "paramajyā ṛcīṣamaḥ" expresses the intent to sing praises of Indra's supreme glory, emphasizing the importance of gratitude and recognition of divine power.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be interpreted as a call for teamwork and collaboration, where collective efforts lead to success, much like how Indra is invoked for strength in unity.
    • During community gatherings or festivals, chanting this mantra can serve as a reminder of the importance of togetherness and shared goals, invoking blessings for prosperity and harmony.
    • In personal challenges, one might recite this mantra to seek strength and guidance, symbolizing the overcoming of obstacles, akin to Indra's victory over Vritra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support in all aspects of life, emphasizing community, strength, and the importance of sacred rituals.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that we are not alone in our struggles; divine forces are invoked to aid us in overcoming challenges and achieving our goals.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to come together, recognize their strengths, and seek blessings for a prosperous and harmonious existence.
