स होताऽयस्य रोदसी चिदुर्वी यज्ञं-यज्ञमभि वृद्धे गृणीतः |प्राची अध्वरिव तस्थतुः सुमेके ऋतावरि ऋतजातस्य सत्ये ||
sa hotā yasya rodasī cid urvī yajñaṁ-yajñam abhi vṛdhe gṛṇītaḥ | prācī adhvareva tasthatuḥ sumeke ṛtāvarī ṛtajātasya satye ||
  • sa hotā: This phrase refers to the "one who invokes" or "the priest" in the context of a ritual. In real life, this can be likened to a leader or facilitator who guides a group towards a common goal.
  • yasya rodasī: This translates to "whose two worlds" or "the sky and the earth." It signifies the connection between the spiritual and the material realms, much like how our actions in the physical world can have spiritual implications.
  • cid urvī: "cid" means "consciousness" and "urvī" means "the vastness" or "the earth." This can be interpreted as the awareness of the vastness of existence, reminding us to be mindful of our surroundings and the impact of our actions on the environment.
  • yajñaṁ-yajñam: This refers to the "sacrifice" or "offering," which is a central theme in many cultures. In modern terms, it can be seen in acts of charity or community service, where individuals give of themselves for the benefit of others.
  • abhi vṛdhe: This means "to grow" or "to increase." It emphasizes the importance of growth, both personal and communal, akin to how businesses or communities thrive through collaboration and support.
  • gṛṇītaḥ: This translates to "they sing" or "they celebrate." It highlights the importance of joy and gratitude in our lives, similar to how people come together to celebrate achievements or milestones.
  • prācī adhvareva: "prācī" means "the east" and "adhvareva" refers to "the ritual." This can symbolize new beginnings and the importance of rituals in marking significant life events, such as weddings or graduations.
  • tasthatuḥ: This means "may they stand" or "may they endure." It speaks to the idea of stability and resilience, much like how families or communities support each other through challenges.
  • sumeke: This translates to "with good fortune" or "auspiciously." It reflects the desire for positive outcomes in our endeavors, akin to how people often seek blessings before starting a new project.
  • ṛtāvarī: This means "the one who upholds truth." It emphasizes the importance of integrity and honesty in our actions, similar to how ethical practices are crucial in business and personal relationships.
  • ṛtajātasya: This translates to "born of truth." It signifies the idea that true success and fulfillment come from living authentically and in alignment with one's values.
  • satye: This means "in truth." It reinforces the significance of truthfulness in all aspects of life, echoing the age-old adage that honesty is the best policy.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of interconnectedness between the spiritual and material worlds, emphasizing the importance of rituals, growth, truth, and community. In real life, it serves as a reminder to engage in acts of service, celebrate achievements, uphold integrity, and remain mindful of our impact on the world around us. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
