मो ते रिषन् ये अच्छोक्तिभिर वसो 'ग्ने केभिश् चिदेवैः |कीरिश् चिद् धि त्वाम् ईट्टे दूत्याय रातहव्यः स्वध्वरः ||
mo te riṣan ye acchoktibhir vaso 'gne kebhiś cid evaiḥ | kīriś cid dhi tvām īṭṭe dūtyāya rātahavyaḥ svadhvaraḥ ||
  • mo te riṣan: This phrase can be interpreted as "I am not angry with you" or "I do not harbor resentment towards you." It signifies a state of forgiveness and understanding.
  • ye acchoktibhir vaso 'gne: This translates to "those who speak well of you, O Agni." Here, Agni represents the divine fire, often symbolizing purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • kebhiś cid evaiḥ: This means "with whom you are associated." It emphasizes the importance of relationships and connections in our lives.
  • kīriś cid dhi tvām: This can be understood as "may your glory be established." It reflects the desire for recognition and honor, both for oneself and for others.
  • īṭṭe dūtyāya: This phrase means "I invoke you for the purpose of communication." It highlights the significance of communication in fostering relationships and understanding.
  • rātahavyaḥ svadhvaraḥ: This translates to "the one who is the lord of the night." It symbolizes the protective and guiding aspects of the divine, especially during challenging times.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of forgiveness, positive communication, and the recognition of divine presence in our lives. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts, such as resolving conflicts, fostering healthy relationships, and seeking guidance during difficult times. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of peace, understanding, and connection with others, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
