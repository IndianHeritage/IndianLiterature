आ नो रत्नानि बिभ्रताव अश्विनाः गच्छतं युवम् |रुद्राः हिरण्यवर्तनी जुषाणां वाजिनीवासु माध्वी मम श्रुतं हवम् ||
ā no ratnāni bibhratāv aśvinā gacchataṁ yuvam | rudrā hiraṇyavartanī juṣāṇā vājinīvasū mādhvī mama śrutaṁ havam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā no: May you bring to us
    • ratnāni: precious things or treasures
    • bibhratāv: holding or possessing
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin deities)
    • gacchataṁ: may you go or proceed
    • yuvam: you two (the Ashvins)
    • rudrā: O Rudra (a form of Shiva)
    • hiraṇyavartanī: golden or radiant path
    • juṣāṇā: who are pleasing or delightful
    • vājinīvasū: the bestowers of strength and wealth
    • mādhvī: sweet or delightful
    • mama: my
    • śrutaṁ: heard or known
    • havam: offering or invocation
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer invoking the Ashvins, the twin deities associated with health, wealth, and well-being. The speaker requests them to bring precious treasures and blessings, while also calling upon Rudra, who represents transformation and strength. The mantra emphasizes the desire for delightful and sweet offerings that are beneficial and uplifting.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, individuals may recite this mantra during times of need, such as seeking health or prosperity in their lives. For instance, someone starting a new business may chant this mantra to attract success and abundance.
    • In community settings, this mantra can be used during rituals or ceremonies aimed at invoking blessings for the collective well-being, such as during harvest festivals or health camps.
    • In a spiritual context, practitioners may use this mantra as part of their daily meditation or prayer routine, focusing on the qualities of the Ashvins and Rudra to cultivate strength, health, and prosperity in their lives.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine energies that promote health, wealth, and transformation. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individual well-being and the blessings of the divine. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in personal prayers, community rituals, and spiritual practices, encouraging individuals to seek and acknowledge the abundance and support available to them through divine grace.
