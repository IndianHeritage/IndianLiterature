अधुक्षत प्रियम् मधु धाराऽसुतस्य वेधसः |अपो वसिष्ठ सुक्रतुः ||
adhukṣata priyam madhu dhārā sutasya vedhasaḥ | apo vasiṣṭa sukratuḥ ||
  • adhukṣata: This term can be interpreted as "he who has been invoked" or "he who is called upon." It signifies the act of invoking divine presence or energy.
  • priyam: Meaning "beloved" or "dear," this word emphasizes the importance of love and affection in our lives.
  • madhu: Translated as "honey," it symbolizes sweetness, nourishment, and the essence of life. Honey is often associated with prosperity and abundance.
  • dhārā: This word means "flow" or "stream." It represents the continuous flow of energy, resources, or blessings in life.
  • sutasya: Meaning "of the son" or "of the offspring," it highlights the significance of lineage, family, and the continuation of values through generations.
  • vedhasaḥ: This term refers to "the wise" or "the learned." It underscores the importance of knowledge and wisdom in guiding our actions and decisions.
  • apo: Meaning "from the waters," it signifies the source of life and the essential nature of water in sustaining existence.
  • vasiṣṭa: This word can be interpreted as "the best" or "the most excellent," indicating the pursuit of excellence in all endeavors.
  • sukratuḥ: Meaning "good intention" or "good thought," it emphasizes the importance of having a positive mindset and intentions in our actions.

In summary, this mantra encapsulates the essence of invoking divine blessings for love, prosperity, wisdom, and positive intentions. It highlights the interconnectedness of family, knowledge, and the natural elements that sustain life. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to seek harmony and abundance in their lives while fostering a sense of community and responsibility towards others.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as:

  • In family gatherings, invoking this mantra can create an atmosphere of love and unity.
  • During rituals or ceremonies, it can be recited to seek blessings for prosperity and well-being.
  • In personal development, reflecting on the meanings of this mantra can guide individuals towards positive intentions and actions.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the values that contribute to a fulfilling and harmonious life, encouraging individuals to cultivate love, wisdom, and a sense of responsibility towards themselves and others.
