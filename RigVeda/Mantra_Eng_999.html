गयस्पहानो अमीवाहा वसुवित पुष्टिवर्धनः |सुमित्रः सोम नो भव ||
gayasphāno amīvahā vasuvit puṣṭivardhanaḥ | sumitraḥ soma no bhava ||
  • Mantra: "gayasphāno amīvahā vasuvit puṣṭivardhanaḥ | sumitraḥ soma no bhava ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • gayasphāno: One who is full of joy or happiness.
    • amīvahā: One who brings forth or carries the essence of life.
    • vasuvit: The one who is rich in wealth or prosperity.
    • puṣṭivardhanaḥ: The one who enhances or increases nourishment and growth.
    • sumitraḥ: One who is of good counsel or has good intentions.
    • soma: Referring to the divine nectar or the moon, symbolizing nourishment and immortality.
    • no bhava: May you become or manifest for us.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes a sense of joy and prosperity, emphasizing the importance of happiness in life.
    • The term "amīvahā" suggests the nurturing aspect of life, akin to how a mother nurtures her child, highlighting the significance of care and support in our relationships.
    • "Vasuvit" points to the abundance of resources, which can be interpreted in modern life as the importance of financial stability and material wealth for a fulfilling life.
    • "Puṣṭivardhanaḥ" relates to growth and nourishment, which can be seen in the context of personal development, education, and health.
    • "Sumitraḥ" emphasizes the value of wise counsel, reminding us to seek guidance from those who have our best interests at heart.
    • The reference to "soma" symbolizes the essence of life and immortality, suggesting that spiritual nourishment is as crucial as physical sustenance.
    • Finally, "no bhava" is a plea for these qualities to manifest in our lives, indicating a desire for a holistic approach to well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, fostering a joyful environment can lead to increased productivity and employee satisfaction, reflecting "gayasphāno."
    • In family dynamics, nurturing relationships and providing emotional support can be seen as embodying "amīvahā."
    • Financial planning and investment in education can be viewed as practical applications of "vasuvit" and "puṣṭivardhanaḥ."
    • Seeking mentorship or advice from experienced individuals aligns with the concept of "sumitraḥ."
    • Engaging in practices that promote mental and spiritual well-being, such as meditation or community service, resonates with the essence of "soma."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of a balanced life, where joy, prosperity, nourishment, and wise counsel are interwoven.
    • Its significance lies in the holistic approach it advocates, encouraging individuals to seek happiness, support, and growth in all aspects of life.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more fulfilling and enriched existence, fostering both personal and communal well-being.
