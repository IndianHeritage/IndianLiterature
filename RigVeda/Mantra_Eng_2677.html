अग्नि वृद्धान आहुतिम पुरोळाशं जातवेदः |जुषस्व तिरोहन्यम् ||
agne vṛdhāna āhutim puroḻāśaṁ jātavedaḥ | juṣasva tiroahnyam ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic tradition, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Vṛdhāna: This term means 'increasing' or 'growing,' indicating the desire for growth and prosperity.
  • Āhutim: This translates to 'offering' or 'sacrifice,' representing the act of giving or dedicating something valuable.
  • Puroḻāśaṁ: This word can be understood as 'the one who is invoked' or 'the one who is worshipped,' referring to the divine presence of Agni.
  • Jātavedaḥ: This term means 'the one who knows all births,' signifying the omniscience of Agni and his role as a mediator between humans and the divine.
  • Juṣasva: This means 'may you enjoy' or 'may you be pleased,' expressing a wish for the deity's favor and blessings.
  • Tiroahnyam: This term can be interpreted as 'in the night' or 'during the darkness,' symbolizing the challenges and uncertainties of life.

  • Meaning: The mantra is a prayer to Agni, asking for his blessings and support in overcoming challenges and achieving growth. It emphasizes the importance of offerings and the relationship between humans and the divine.
  • Real-life Example: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during significant life events like weddings, new ventures, or rituals where individuals seek divine guidance and support. For instance, when starting a new business, one might perform a small fire ceremony (homa) to invoke Agni's blessings for success and prosperity.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in navigating life's challenges and fostering growth. It serves as a reminder of the importance of gratitude, offerings, and the connection between the material and spiritual realms. In real-life scenarios, invoking Agni through this mantra can inspire individuals to seek guidance, maintain a positive outlook, and strive for success in their endeavors.
