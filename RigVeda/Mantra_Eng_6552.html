शवसाऽ ह्यसि श्रुतो वृत्रहत्येन वृत्रहा |मघैर मघोनो अति शूर दाशसि ||
śavasā hy asi śruto vṛtrahatyena vṛtrahā | maghair maghono ati śūra dāśasi ||
  • śavasā hy asi - This phrase translates to "You are indeed the one who is powerful." Here, "śavasā" refers to strength or power, and "asi" means "you are." This emphasizes the inherent strength within an individual or entity.
  • śruto vṛtrahatyena - This part means "by the knowledge of slaying Vritra." Vritra is a mythical serpent or dragon in Vedic literature, representing obstacles or challenges. The phrase suggests that through knowledge and wisdom, one can overcome significant challenges.
  • vṛtrahā - This term means "the slayer of Vritra." It signifies the act of overcoming obstacles and challenges, akin to a hero's journey where one confronts and defeats their fears or adversities.
  • maghair maghono - This translates to "with great abundance." "Maghono" refers to wealth or prosperity, indicating that abundance is a result of overcoming challenges and utilizing one's strength.
  • ati śūra dāśasi - This means "you are exceedingly brave." "Ati" means "exceedingly," and "śūra" refers to bravery or valor. This phrase highlights the importance of courage in achieving success and overcoming life's hurdles.

  • Real-Life Example: Consider a student preparing for a challenging exam. They may feel overwhelmed (vṛtrahā) by the vast syllabus. However, through diligent study (śavasā hy asi), they gain knowledge (śruto vṛtrahatyena) and ultimately succeed, showcasing their bravery (ati śūra dāśasi) in facing the challenge.
  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of inner strength, knowledge, and bravery in overcoming life's challenges. It serves as a reminder that obstacles can be conquered through wisdom and courage, leading to abundance and success.
  • Application in Real Life: In various scenarios, such as personal struggles, professional challenges, or societal issues, this mantra can inspire individuals to harness their inner strength and knowledge to overcome difficulties. It encourages a proactive approach to facing challenges, fostering resilience and determination.
