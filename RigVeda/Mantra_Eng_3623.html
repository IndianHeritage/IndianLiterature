क्षेत्रस्य पतिना वयं हितेनेव जयामसि |गाम अश्वं पोषयित्वा स नो मृळातीदृशे ||
kṣetrasya patinā vayaṁ hiteneva jayāmasi | gām aśvam poṣayitnv ā sa no mṛḻātīdṛśe ||
  • kṣetrasya patinā: This phrase translates to "by the lord of the field." In this context, "kṣetra" refers to a field or a domain, which can be interpreted as the physical world or the environment we inhabit. The "patinā" signifies the ruler or the protector of this domain.
  • vayaṁ hiteneva jayāmasi: This means "we achieve victory through goodness." Here, "vayaṁ" refers to "we," "hiteneva" translates to "through goodness," and "jayāmasi" means "we achieve victory." This emphasizes the importance of virtuous actions and intentions in attaining success.
  • gām aśvam poṣayitnv: This translates to "may we nourish the cow and the horse." In ancient Indian culture, cows and horses were symbols of wealth, strength, and sustenance. "Gām" refers to the cow, and "aśvam" refers to the horse, while "poṣayitnv" means "to nourish" or "to sustain." This line highlights the significance of nurturing resources that contribute to prosperity.
  • ā sa no mṛḻātīdṛśe: This phrase means "may we be protected from harm." "Ā" signifies "may," "sa no" translates to "us," and "mṛḻātīdṛśe" means "from harm or danger." This line expresses a plea for safety and protection in our endeavors.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of virtuous actions, the nurturing of resources, and the need for protection in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways:

  • In personal relationships, fostering goodness and support can lead to stronger bonds and mutual success.
  • In business, nurturing resources such as employees and clients can lead to prosperity and growth.
  • In community living, protecting each other from harm and working together for the common good can create a harmonious environment.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder that through goodness, nurturing, and protection, we can achieve success and well-being in our lives and communities.
