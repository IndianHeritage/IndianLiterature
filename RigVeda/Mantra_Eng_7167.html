यद इन्द्र प्राग अपाग उदङ न्यग हूयसे नृभिः |आ याहि तुयम् आशुभिः ||
yad indra prāg apāg udaṅ nyag vā hūyase nṛbhiḥ | ā yāhi tūyam āśubhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods and god of rain and thunderstorms
    • prāg: before
    • apāg: not to be abandoned
    • udaṅ: upward
    • nyag: downward
    • vā: or
    • hūyase: you are invoked
    • nṛbhiḥ: by men
    • ā: come
    • yāhi: you should go
    • tūyam: to you
    • āśubhiḥ: with evil or inauspiciousness
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to Indra, asking him to come and assist in overcoming obstacles and negativity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of invoking divine help in times of need, particularly in the face of challenges.
    • The duality of "upward" and "downward" suggests a balance between aspirations and grounding, indicating that one should seek higher goals while remaining rooted in reality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when facing a challenging project, a team might invoke the spirit of collaboration and support, akin to calling upon Indra for assistance.
    • During personal struggles, individuals may seek guidance through prayer or meditation, symbolically inviting divine presence to help them navigate their difficulties.
    • In community gatherings, people often come together to support one another, reflecting the collective invocation of strength and positivity against adversity.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the power of divine assistance and the importance of community support.
    • It teaches us to seek help when needed and to remain hopeful in the face of challenges.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various situations, from personal struggles to collective efforts in communities, reinforcing the idea that we are not alone in our endeavors.
