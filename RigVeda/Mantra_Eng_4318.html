एषा प्रतिची दुहिता दिवो नृन योषेव भद्रा नि रिणीते अप्सः |व्यूर्णवती दाशुषे वार्याणि पुनर ज्योतिर्व्युवतिः पूर्वथाकः ||
eṣā pratīcī duhitā divo nṝn yoṣeva bhadrā ni riṇīte apsaḥ | vyūrṇvatī dāśuṣe vāryāṇi punar jyotir yuvatiḥ pūrvathākaḥ ||
  • eṣā pratīcī duhitā divo: This phrase translates to "This daughter of the sky." In a real-life context, it can be interpreted as a reference to the divine feminine energy or the nurturing aspects of nature that are essential for life. For example, the moon, often personified as a feminine figure, influences tides and agricultural cycles, symbolizing the nurturing aspect of nature.
  • nṝn yoṣeva bhadrā: This means "like a good woman among men." It highlights the qualities of virtue, strength, and support that a woman can provide in society. In modern life, this can be seen in the role of women leaders who inspire and uplift their communities, such as Malala Yousafzai advocating for girls' education.
  • ni riṇīte apsaḥ: This translates to "she who brings forth abundance." This can be related to the idea of prosperity and fertility in agriculture. For instance, a farmer who practices sustainable farming techniques can be seen as someone who brings forth abundance from the earth, ensuring food security for the community.
  • vyūrṇvatī dāśuṣe vāryāṇi: This means "she who is adorned with the riches of the earth." This can be interpreted as the wealth of knowledge and resources that nature provides. In real life, this can be exemplified by environmentalists who work to protect natural resources, ensuring that future generations can benefit from the earth's riches.
  • punar jyotir yuvatiḥ pūrvathākaḥ: This translates to "the young woman who is the light of the past." This phrase emphasizes the importance of learning from history and the wisdom of previous generations. In contemporary society, this can be seen in the way young activists draw inspiration from historical movements, such as the civil rights movement, to advocate for social justice today.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of feminine energy, abundance, and the importance of learning from the past. It signifies the role of women as nurturers and leaders in society, highlighting their contributions to prosperity and wisdom. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to recognize and honor the feminine qualities within themselves and their communities, promoting a balanced and harmonious society. It encourages us to appreciate the resources provided by nature and to learn from the experiences of those who came before us, fostering a sense of responsibility towards future generations.
