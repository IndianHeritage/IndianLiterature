अग्निम् ईळे पुरोहितं यज्ञस्य देवम् ऋत्विजम् |होतारं रत्नधातमम् ||
agnim īḻe purohitaṁ yajñasya devam ṛtvijam | hotāraṁ ratnadhātamam ||
  • Agnim: This word translates to "Agni," which means fire. In Vedic tradition, Agni is not just a physical element but also represents the divine energy that transforms and purifies. Fire is essential in rituals and symbolizes the presence of the divine.
  • īḻe: This means "I invoke" or "I praise." It indicates a call to the divine, expressing reverence and the desire for connection with the higher powers.
  • Purohitaṁ: This term refers to the "priest" or "one who is in front." The purohita is responsible for conducting rituals and is seen as a mediator between the divine and the devotees.
  • Yajñasya: This means "of the sacrifice." In Vedic culture, yajna refers to a ritual of offerings made to the deities, often involving fire. It signifies the importance of selfless action and devotion.
  • Devam: This translates to "the divine" or "god." It emphasizes the spiritual aspect of the mantra, acknowledging the presence of a higher power.
  • Ṛtvijam: This word means "the one who is knowledgeable about the rituals." It refers to the priest or the one who performs the rituals with understanding and expertise.
  • Hotāraṁ: This means "the one who invokes." It refers to the priest who calls upon the deities during the yajna, playing a crucial role in the ritual.
  • Ratnadhātamam: This term translates to "the one who possesses the most precious jewels." It signifies the richness of spiritual knowledge and the blessings that come from the divine.

In summary, this mantra is a prayer to Agni, the divine fire, invoking the priest who conducts the yajna and acknowledging the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine. It highlights the roles of the purohita and the hotāra, emphasizing the need for knowledge and reverence in spiritual practices.


Significance: The mantra signifies the importance of rituals in spiritual life and the role of the priest as a mediator. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to various aspects of life where rituals and traditions play a crucial role, such as weddings, festivals, and other significant events. It reminds us of the importance of intention, knowledge, and reverence in our actions, encouraging us to approach life with a sense of purpose and connection to the divine.
