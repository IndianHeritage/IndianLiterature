अदर्शि गातुवित्तमो यस्मिन व्रतान्य आदधुः |उपो षु जातम आर्यस्य वर्धनम अग्निं नक्षन्त नो गिरः ||
adarśi gātuvittamo yasmin vratāny ādadhuḥ | upo ṣu jātam āryasya vardhanam agniṁ nakṣanta no giraḥ ||
  • adarśi: This word translates to "he who sees" or "the seer." It implies a person with insight or vision.
  • gātuvittamo: This can be interpreted as "the best among those who sing" or "the most excellent in expression." It refers to someone who excels in communication or artistic expression.
  • yasmin: Meaning "in whom" or "in which," it indicates a connection or relationship.
  • vratāny: This word means "vows" or "sacred commitments." It signifies the importance of promises made in a spiritual or moral context.
  • ādadhuḥ: This translates to "they have taken" or "they have accepted," indicating an action of embracing or adopting something.
  • upo: Meaning "near" or "towards," it suggests proximity or closeness.
  • ṣu: This word can mean "good" or "well," often used to denote positivity.
  • jātam: This translates to "born" or "arisen," indicating the emergence of something new.
  • āryasya: Meaning "of the noble" or "of the honorable," it refers to qualities associated with nobility and virtue.
  • vardhanam: This means "growth" or "nourishment," indicating the process of development or enhancement.
  • agniṁ: This word refers to "fire," which in Vedic literature symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification.
  • nakṣanta: This translates to "they have directed" or "they have aimed," indicating intention or purpose.
  • no: Meaning "our," it signifies possession or belonging.
  • giraḥ: This word means "words" or "speech," emphasizing the power of communication and expression.

The mantra speaks to the qualities of insight, expression, and commitment. It highlights the importance of noble intentions and the transformative power of fire, both literally and metaphorically. In real life, this can be seen in various contexts:


  • Leadership: A leader who possesses vision (adarśi) and communicates effectively (gātuvittamo) can inspire their team to embrace their commitments (vratāny) and work towards a common goal.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals who adopt noble values (āryasya) and strive for personal development (vardhanam) can transform their lives through dedication and hard work.
  • Community Building: In a community, when members come together with shared intentions (no) and express their thoughts (giraḥ), they can create a supportive environment that fosters growth and transformation (agniṁ).

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the significance of vision, commitment, and the power of words in fostering growth and transformation. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal development to leadership and community building, reminding us of the importance of noble intentions and effective communication in our endeavors.
