वियद वाचं कीस्तासो भरण्ते शंसन्ति के चिन् निविदो मनानाः |आद वाम बृवाम सत्यान्य उक्ताः नकिरे देवैभिर यतथो महित्वा ||
vi yad vācaṁ kīstāso bharante śaṁsanti ke cin nivido manānāḥ | ād vām bravāma satyāny ukthā nakir devebhir yatatho mahitvā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vi: indeed
    • yad: whatever
    • vācaṁ: speech or words
    • kīstāso: who are they (those who)
    • bharante: bear or carry
    • śaṁsanti: praise or commend
    • ke cin: some or anyone
    • nivido: knowledgeable or wise
    • manānāḥ: thinkers or contemplators
    • ād: therefore
    • vām: to you
    • bravāma: we speak or declare
    • satyāny: truths
    • ukthā: hymns or praises
    • nakir: not
    • devebhir: by the gods
    • yatatho: as much as
    • mahitvā: greatness or majesty
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects on the nature of speech and the importance of wise and thoughtful communication.
    • It questions who truly carries the weight of words and who is capable of praising or understanding them.
    • The mantra emphasizes the significance of truth and the power of spoken words, suggesting that they should be aligned with divine principles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, effective communication is crucial. Leaders who speak truthfully and thoughtfully can inspire their teams and foster a positive work environment.
    • In personal relationships, the way we communicate can either strengthen or weaken our bonds. Honest and respectful dialogue is essential for maintaining trust.
    • In public discourse, politicians and public figures are often scrutinized for their words. Those who speak with integrity and wisdom are more likely to gain the respect of the public.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for mindful communication and the pursuit of truth.
    • It serves as a reminder that our words have power and should reflect our highest values and understanding.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more meaningful interactions, stronger relationships, and a more harmonious society.
