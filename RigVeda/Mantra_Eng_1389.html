गृहं-गृहं अहनां यात्य अच्छा दिवे-दिवे अधि नामा दधानां |सिषासन्ती द्योतनां शश्वद आगाद अग्रं-अग्रं इद्भजते वसूनाम् ||
gṛhaṁ-gṛham ahanā yāty acchā dive-dive adhi nāmā dadhānā | siṣāsantī dyotanā śaśvad āgād agram-agram id bhajate vasūnām ||
  • gṛhaṁ-gṛham ahanā yāty: This phrase can be translated as "the house goes to the house." It signifies the interconnectedness of homes and families, suggesting that our lives are intertwined with those around us.
  • acchā dive-dive adhi nāmā dadhānā: This part means "goodness is established day by day." It emphasizes the importance of daily actions and intentions in cultivating a positive environment and character.
  • siṣāsantī dyotanā: Translated as "the light of knowledge shines," this phrase highlights the value of wisdom and enlightenment in guiding our lives and decisions.
  • śaśvad āgād agram-agram: This means "always moving forward, progressing." It reflects the idea of continuous growth and advancement in life, both personally and collectively.
  • id bhajate vasūnām: This can be interpreted as "this is what the wealth of the world cherishes." It suggests that the principles of unity, goodness, knowledge, and progress are valued by society and contribute to the well-being of all.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of community, personal growth, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real life, it serves as a reminder that our actions and relationships with others shape our environment. For instance, in a workplace, fostering a collaborative atmosphere can lead to collective success, much like how homes are interconnected. By striving for goodness daily, seeking knowledge, and embracing progress, individuals can contribute to a harmonious society. This mantra encourages us to reflect on our roles within our communities and the impact of our daily choices on the greater good.
