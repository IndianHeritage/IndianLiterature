त्वेषं गणं तवसं खादिहस्तं धुनिव्रतम मायिनं दातिवारम् |मयोभुवो ये अमिताः महित्वा वन्दस्व विप्र तुविराधसो नृन् ||
tveṣaṁ gaṇaṁ tavasaṁ khādihastaṁ dhunivratam māyinaṁ dātivāram | mayobhuvo ye amitā mahitvā vandasva vipra tuvirādhaso nṝn ||
  • tveṣaṁ gaṇaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "your group" or "your assembly." It signifies the collective energy or forces that are under the influence of a higher power.
  • tavasaṁ: This means "of your strength" or "of your power." It emphasizes the divine strength that governs the universe.
  • khādihastaṁ: Translated as "with hands that consume," this suggests the idea of action and the ability to take or absorb energy or resources.
  • dhunivratam: This can be understood as "the vow of the one who shakes" or "the one who stirs." It indicates a dynamic force that brings about change.
  • māyinaṁ: This refers to "the one who creates illusion" or "the magician." It points to the power of creation and the complexities of existence.
  • dātivāram: This means "the giver of boons" or "the one who grants." It highlights the aspect of generosity and the ability to bestow blessings.
  • mayobhuvo: This translates to "of the one who is made of illusion" or "of the one who is magical." It reflects the nature of reality as perceived through the lens of illusion.
  • ye amitā mahitvā: This means "those who are immeasurable in greatness." It speaks to the vastness and boundlessness of divine qualities.
  • vandasva: This is an invocation to "worship" or "honor." It is a call to recognize and pay respect to the divine forces at play.
  • vipra: This term refers to "the wise one" or "the learned." It signifies the importance of knowledge and wisdom in understanding the divine.
  • tuvirādhaso: This can be interpreted as "of the powerful" or "of the strong." It emphasizes strength and resilience.
  • nṝn: This means "of humans" or "of mankind." It connects the divine to human existence, highlighting the relationship between the two.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine power, the interplay of illusion and reality, and the importance of wisdom in navigating life. It serves as a reminder of the strength that lies within the collective and the need to honor the forces that shape our existence. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in moments of decision-making where one seeks guidance from a higher power, or in times of challenge where the strength of community and wisdom can lead to overcoming obstacles. It encourages individuals to recognize the divine in their lives and to act with intention and respect towards the greater forces at play.
