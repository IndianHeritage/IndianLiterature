भास्वती नेत्री सूनृतानां दिवः स्तवे दुहिता गोतमैभिः |प्रजावतो नृवतो अश्वबुध्यान उषो गोआग्रां उप मासि वाजान ||
bhāsvatī netrī sūnṛtānāṁ divaḥ stave duhitā gotamebhiḥ | prajāvato nṛvato aśvabudhyān uṣo goagrām̐ upa māsi vājān ||
  • bhāsvatī netrī: This phrase translates to "the radiant eye" or "the shining eye." It symbolizes clarity, vision, and enlightenment. In real life, this can be related to having a clear perspective or insight in decision-making, akin to how a leader must have a vision for their team or organization.
  • sūnṛtānāṁ: This means "of the truthful" or "of those who speak the truth." It emphasizes the importance of honesty and integrity. In everyday life, this can be seen in the value of trust in relationships, whether personal or professional, where truthfulness fosters stronger bonds.
  • divaḥ stave: This translates to "in praise of the heavens." It signifies reverence for the divine or the higher powers. In a practical sense, this can be related to moments of gratitude and appreciation for the beauty of nature or the universe, such as during a sunrise or sunset, which can inspire awe and reflection.
  • duhitā gotamebhiḥ: This means "the daughter of the Gotama." It can refer to a divine feminine aspect or a nurturing figure. In real life, this can be likened to the role of mothers or caregivers who provide support and guidance, embodying nurturing qualities that help individuals grow and thrive.
  • prajāvato nṛvato: This translates to "of the wise and the learned." It highlights the value of wisdom and knowledge. In practical terms, this can be seen in the importance of education and lifelong learning, where individuals seek knowledge to make informed decisions and contribute positively to society.
  • aśvabudhyān: This means "those who are aware of the horses." Horses symbolize strength, freedom, and movement. In a real-life context, this can represent individuals who are aware of their resources and capabilities, using them wisely to achieve their goals, much like an athlete harnessing their physical abilities to excel in sports.
  • uṣo goagrām̐ upa māsi vājān: This translates to "the dawn approaches with the herds." It signifies new beginnings and the arrival of opportunities. In everyday life, this can be related to the start of a new day, where each morning brings fresh possibilities and the chance to pursue one's aspirations, much like how a farmer looks forward to the dawn to tend to their fields.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of vision, truth, reverence, nurturing, wisdom, awareness, and new beginnings. Its significance lies in the reminder to cultivate these qualities in our lives. By embracing clarity of thought, honesty in our actions, gratitude for the divine, nurturing relationships, the pursuit of knowledge, awareness of our strengths, and the optimism that comes with new opportunities, we can lead more fulfilling and purposeful lives. This mantra serves as a guiding principle for personal growth and harmonious living in a community.
