न नूनं ब्रह्मणाम ऋणं प्राशूनाम अस्ति सुन्वताम् |न सोमो अप्रताप पापे ||
na nūnam brahmaṇām ṛṇam prāśūnām asti sunvatām | na somo apratā pape ||
  • Translation: The mantra translates to "Indeed, there is no debt for the wise and learned; there is no sin for those who are virtuous." This highlights the idea that true wisdom and virtue free individuals from the burdens of debt and sin.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • na nūnam: Indeed, not.
    • brahmaṇām: For the wise or learned individuals.
    • ṛṇam: Debt or obligation.
    • prāśūnām: Those who are virtuous or righteous.
    • asti: There is.
    • sunvatām: For those who are virtuous or good.
    • na somo: There is no sin.
    • apratā: Unpunished or unaccounted.
    • pape: For sins or wrongdoings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Wise Decision-Making: A person who makes informed and wise decisions in their financial dealings, such as investing wisely or avoiding unnecessary debt, exemplifies the idea of being free from debt.
    • Virtuous Living: An individual who practices honesty, integrity, and kindness in their daily life, such as volunteering or helping others, embodies the concept of being free from sin.
    • Community Impact: A community that promotes education and moral values can create an environment where individuals are less likely to incur debts or engage in wrongful acts, reflecting the essence of the mantra.
  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom and virtue in leading a fulfilling and responsible life. It suggests that when individuals cultivate knowledge and moral integrity, they can navigate life without the burdens of debt and sin. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in personal finance, ethical decision-making, and community engagement, encouraging individuals to strive for wisdom and virtue in their actions.
