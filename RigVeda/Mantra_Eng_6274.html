विप्रं विप्रासोऽवसे देवं मर्तास ऊतये। अग्निं गीरभिर हवामहे॥
vipraṁ viprāso 'vase devam martāsa ūtaye | agniṁ gīrbhir havāmahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vipraṁ: The learned or wise one, often referring to a sage or a scholar.
    • viprāso: In the context of the wise, it implies the collective wisdom of learned individuals.
    • 'vase: To invoke or to bring under control.
    • devam: The divine or God, representing higher powers or deities.
    • martāsa: Mortals or human beings, indicating the human condition.
    • ūtaye: For the purpose of elevation or upliftment.
    • agniṁ: Fire, which symbolizes energy, transformation, and purity.
    • gīrbhir: Through hymns or chants, indicating the power of words and prayers.
    • havāmahe: We invoke or we call upon, emphasizing the act of worship or prayer.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and the collective knowledge of learned individuals.
    • It suggests that through the invocation of divine powers, mortals can seek elevation and transformation.
    • The reference to Agni (fire) symbolizes the transformative power of knowledge and the energy that comes from spiritual practices.
    • The act of invoking through hymns highlights the significance of communication with the divine and the role of prayer in human life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In educational settings, the wisdom of teachers (vipraṁ) is crucial for the upliftment of students (martāsa). Just as this mantra calls upon the divine for guidance, students often seek the wisdom of their mentors to navigate challenges.
    • In community gatherings, collective wisdom (viprāso) is sought to address social issues, invoking a sense of unity and purpose among members.
    • During rituals, the invocation of Agni represents the transformative power of knowledge and spirituality, similar to how individuals seek personal growth through meditation and self-reflection.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of wisdom and the divine in the journey of human life.
    • It teaches that through collective knowledge and spiritual practices, individuals can elevate their existence and transform their lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in educational, social, and spiritual contexts, reminding us of the power of wisdom, community, and divine connection in our pursuit of growth and understanding.
