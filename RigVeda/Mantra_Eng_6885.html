अग्नि निपाहि नस् त्वं प्रति स्म देव रिषतः |भिन्दि द्वेषः सहस्कृत ||
agne ni pāhi nas tvam prati ṣma deva rīṣataḥ | bhindhi dveṣaḥ sahaskṛta ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • ni pāhi: This translates to "protect us" or "safeguard us." It indicates a plea for divine protection and guidance.
  • nas: This means "us," referring to the collective group or community seeking protection.
  • tvam: This means "you," addressing Agni directly, emphasizing the personal relationship between the devotee and the deity.
  • prati: This translates to "towards" or "in the direction of," indicating a focus on the divine.
  • ṣma: This word means "peace" or "tranquility," suggesting a desire for a harmonious existence.
  • deva: This means "god" or "divine being," reinforcing the sacred nature of the invocation.
  • rīṣataḥ: This translates to "with strength" or "with vigor," indicating the power of the divine being invoked.
  • bhindhi: This means "break" or "shatter," suggesting a desire to overcome obstacles or negativity.
  • dveṣaḥ: This translates to "hatred" or "enmity," indicating the negative emotions that one wishes to dispel.
  • sahaskṛta: This means "with effort" or "with strength," emphasizing the need for active engagement in overcoming challenges.

The mantra can be understood as a heartfelt invocation to Agni, seeking protection and peace while asking for the removal of hatred and negativity. It reflects a deep desire for divine assistance in overcoming obstacles and fostering a harmonious environment.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties in life, such as stress or conflict, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek inner peace and divine support.
  • Community Harmony: In situations of discord within a community, this mantra can be used to promote unity and understanding, encouraging individuals to break down barriers of hatred.
  • Rituals and Ceremonies: During religious or spiritual gatherings, this mantra can be chanted to invoke the presence of the divine and create a sacred atmosphere.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine protection and the removal of negativity. It serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of seeking peace and harmony in our lives and communities. By invoking Agni, we acknowledge the transformative power of the divine and our own role in fostering a positive environment.
