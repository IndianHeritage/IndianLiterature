उत नः कर्णशोभना पुरूणि धृष्णव आ भर |त्वं हि श्रिण्विषे वसो ||
uta naḥ karṇaśobhanā purūṇi dhṛṣṇav ā bhara | tvaṁ hi śṛṇviṣe vaso ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and
    • naḥ: our
    • karṇaśobhanā: beautiful ears
    • purūṇi: many
    • dhṛṣṇav: strong or powerful
    • ā bhara: may you fill
    • tvaṁ: you
    • hi: indeed
    • śṛṇviṣe: listen
    • vaso: of the dwelling or abode
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer for the enhancement of our ability to listen and understand.
    • It expresses a desire for the divine to fill our ears with the wisdom of many powerful teachings.
    • The invocation acknowledges the importance of listening as a means to gain knowledge and insight.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a classroom setting, a student who actively listens to the teacher is more likely to grasp complex concepts and perform better academically.
    • In relationships, effective listening can lead to better communication and understanding between partners, fostering a deeper connection.
    • In a workplace, leaders who listen to their team members can make more informed decisions and create a more collaborative environment.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of listening as a foundational skill for learning and growth.
    • It can be applied in various aspects of life, including education, personal relationships, and professional settings.
    • By cultivating the ability to listen deeply, individuals can enhance their understanding, empathy, and overall effectiveness in their interactions.
