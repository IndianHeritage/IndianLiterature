आपो रेवतीः क्षयथा हि वस्वः क्रतुं च भद्रम बिभृतामृतं च |रायश च स्थ स्वपत्यमस्य पत्निः सरस्वती तद गृणते वयो धात ||
āpo revatīḥ kṣayathā hi vasvaḥ kratuṁ ca bhadram bibhṛthāmṛtaṁ ca | rāyaś ca stha svapatyasya patnīḥ sarasvatī tad gṛṇate vayo dhāt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āpo: Waters
    • revatīḥ: Prosperous
    • kṣayathā: May you diminish
    • hi: Indeed
    • vasvaḥ: All
    • kratuṁ: Intellect or will
    • ca: And
    • bhadram: Auspiciousness
    • bibhṛtha: You may hold
    • amṛtaṁ: Immortality
    • ca: And
    • rāyaḥ: Wealth
    • ca: And
    • stha: Remain
    • svapatyasya: Of the one who is awake
    • patnīḥ: Wives or partners
    • sarasvatī: Goddess of knowledge and wisdom
    • tad: That
    • gṛṇate: Sing or praise
    • vayo: Our age or life
    • dhāt: May it be established
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the waters, symbolizing purity and life, and asks for the removal of obstacles and negativity.
    • The mention of "kratuṁ ca bhadram" emphasizes the importance of intellect and auspiciousness in our lives.
    • It highlights the significance of wealth and prosperity, not just in material terms but also in terms of wisdom and knowledge, represented by Sarasvatī.
    • The phrase "gṛṇate vayo dhāt" suggests a collective effort to praise and establish a harmonious life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied during rituals or prayers for prosperity, where individuals seek to remove negativity and invite positive energies.
    • In educational settings, invoking Sarasvatī can inspire students to seek knowledge and wisdom, emphasizing the importance of learning.
    • In business, the mantra can serve as a reminder to focus on ethical practices and the well-being of all stakeholders, not just profit.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of life, knowledge, and prosperity.
    • It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and auspiciousness in their endeavors while fostering a sense of community and collective growth.
    • By applying the principles of this mantra in real life, one can cultivate a balanced and fulfilling existence, promoting both personal and communal well-being.
