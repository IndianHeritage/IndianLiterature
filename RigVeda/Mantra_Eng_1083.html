द्विषो नो विश्वतोमुखाति नावेव पारय |अप नः शोशुचदाघम् ||
dviṣo no viśvatomukhāti nāveva pāraya | apa naḥ śośucad agham ||
  • Mantra: "dviṣo no viśvatomukhāti nāveva pāraya | apa naḥ śośucad agham ||"
  • Translation: "May those who hate us be far away from us; may they not approach us from any direction. Remove our sins and purify us."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • dviṣo: From the root "dviṣ," meaning "to hate" or "to be hostile." This refers to those who harbor animosity.
    • no: "our," indicating possession or belonging.
    • viśvatomukhāti: "from all directions," where "viśva" means "all" and "mukhāti" refers to "faces" or "approaches."
    • nāveva: "not at all," emphasizing the desire for complete separation from negativity.
    • pāraya: "to approach" or "to come near," indicating the action of coming close.
    • apa: "away" or "remove," suggesting a desire to distance oneself from negativity.
    • naḥ: "us," again indicating possession.
    • śośucad: "purify" or "cleanse," derived from the root "śu," which means "to be clean."
    • agham: "sins" or "offenses," referring to negative actions or thoughts that may hinder spiritual progress.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be invoked to foster a positive environment, distancing oneself from colleagues who may harbor ill intentions or negativity.
    • In personal relationships, it can serve as a reminder to focus on positive interactions and to seek distance from toxic influences.
    • During meditation or spiritual practices, individuals may recite this mantra to cleanse their minds of negative thoughts and emotions, promoting inner peace.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of maintaining a safe and positive space, free from hostility and negativity.
    • It serves as a reminder to actively seek purification from sins and negative influences, promoting personal growth and spiritual well-being.
    • In real life, it can be applied in various contexts, such as personal relationships, professional environments, and spiritual practices, encouraging individuals to cultivate positivity and distance themselves from harmful energies.
