स ग्रामेभिः सनिता स रथेभिर विदे विश्वाभिः कृष्टिभिर nv अद्य |स पौंस्येभिर अभिभूर अशस्तीर मरुत्वान नो भवत्व इन्द्र ऊती ||
sa grāmebhiḥ sanitā sa rathebhir vide viśvābhiḥ kṛṣṭibhir nv a1dya | sa pauṁsyebhir abhibhūr aśastīr marutvān no bhavatv indra ūtī ||
  • Mantra: "sa grāmebhiḥ sanitā sa rathebhir vide viśvābhiḥ kṛṣṭibhir nv a1dya | sa pauṁsyebhir abhibhūr aśastīr marutvān no bhavatv indra ūtī ||"
  • Translation: "May Indra, who is surrounded by the people of the village, who rides in the chariots, who is invoked by all, and who is the master of strength, grant us prosperity and protection from all adversities."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • sa: he/that
    • grāmebhiḥ: by the villagers
    • sanitā: surrounded
    • sa rathebhir: he who rides in chariots
    • vide: in the world
    • viśvābhiḥ: by all
    • kṛṣṭibhir: by the efforts of cultivation
    • nv a1dya: may he grant us
    • sa pauṁsyebhir: he who is surrounded by strength
    • abhibhūr: the master
    • aśastīr: of strength
    • marutvān: may he be powerful
    • no: for us
    • bhavatv: may he be
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • ūtī: protection

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes community and collective strength. For instance, in a village, when people come together to support each other during harvest time, they embody the essence of "sa grāmebhiḥ sanitā."
    • The reference to "sa rathebhir" can be likened to teamwork in modern organizations where individuals work together towards a common goal, much like chariots moving in unison.
    • The invocation of Indra for protection and prosperity can be seen in various cultural practices, such as community prayers for good weather during planting seasons, reflecting the reliance on divine forces for success.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of community, strength, and divine support in achieving prosperity and overcoming challenges.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that collective efforts and unity can lead to success, whether in agricultural practices, community projects, or organizational goals.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek support from their communities and to work together harmoniously, invoking a sense of shared responsibility and strength.
