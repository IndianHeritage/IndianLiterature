आयुज्रान त इन्द्र विश्वकृष्टीर विदानासो निष्सिधो मार्त्यत्रा |मरुताम पृत्सुतिर हासमानā स्वर्मील्हस्य प्रधानस्य सातौ ||
ayujran ta indra viśvakṛṣṭīr vidānāso niṣṣidho martyatrā | marutām pṛtsutir hāsamānā svarmīḻhasya pradhanasya sātau ||
  • ayujran: This term can be interpreted as "long-lived" or "having longevity." It signifies the importance of life and vitality.
  • ta: This is a pronoun meaning "that" or "those," referring to the subjects of the mantra.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, rain, and thunder. He represents power and authority.
  • viśvakṛṣṭīr: This word can be broken down into "viśva" (universal) and "kṛṣṭīr" (cultivation or creation). It implies the universal creation or the nurturing aspect of life.
  • vidānāso: This term means "those who know" or "the wise." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in life.
  • niṣṣidho: This means "forbidden" or "restricted," indicating certain limitations or boundaries that should be respected.
  • martyatrā: This refers to "in the mortal realm," highlighting the human experience and the challenges faced in life.
  • marutām: The Maruts are storm deities, representing the forces of nature and the dynamic aspects of life.
  • pṛtsutir: This can be interpreted as "the path of the earth" or "the earthly way," suggesting a grounded approach to life.
  • hāsamānā: This term means "joyful" or "delighting," indicating the importance of happiness and positivity.
  • svarmīḻhasya: This refers to "the one who is radiant" or "the shining one," symbolizing enlightenment and clarity.
  • pradhanasya: This means "the principal" or "the chief," referring to the most important aspects of life.
  • sātau: This term can be interpreted as "in truth" or "in reality," emphasizing the significance of authenticity and honesty.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of longevity, wisdom, and joy while acknowledging the challenges of the mortal realm. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge, respect boundaries, and embrace the dynamic forces of nature. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, leadership, and community building. By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can strive for a balanced and fulfilling life, fostering a sense of purpose and connection with the universe.
