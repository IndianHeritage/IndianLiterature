इमं तृतो भूर्ज अविन्दद इच्छन् वैभूवसो मूर्धन्य अग्न्यायाः |स शेवृधो जात आ हरम्येषु नाभिर युवा भवति रोचनस्य ||
imaṁ trito bhūry avindad icchan vaibhūvaso mūrdhany aghnyāyāḥ | sa śevṛdho jāta ā harmyeṣu nābhir yuvā bhavati rocanasya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imaṁ: this
    • trito: third
    • bhūry: abundant
    • avindad: has found
    • icchan: desiring
    • vaibhūvaso: of the glorious
    • mūrdhany: on the head
    • aghnyāyāḥ: of the non-violent
    • sa: he
    • śevṛdho: who is well-versed
    • jāta: born
    • ā: in
    • harmyeṣu: in the palaces
    • nābhir: from the navel
    • yuvā: young
    • bhavati: becomes
    • rocanasya: of the radiant
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a third entity that has found abundance and desires the glory that comes from non-violence.
    • The reference to "mūrdhany" (on the head) suggests a position of authority or leadership, indicating that this abundance and glory is associated with wisdom and non-violence.
    • The phrase "in the palaces" implies that this wisdom and abundance are not just for the common people but are also relevant in the higher echelons of society.
    • The mention of "yuvā" (young) indicates that this wisdom and glory are accessible to the youth, suggesting a future-oriented perspective.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern leadership, the idea of leading with wisdom and non-violence can be seen in figures like Mahatma Gandhi, who advocated for peaceful resistance and found great success in his endeavors.
    • In the corporate world, companies that prioritize ethical practices and social responsibility often find themselves thriving, as they attract customers who value integrity and non-violence in business.
    • Young activists today, such as those advocating for climate change, embody the spirit of this mantra by seeking abundance and glory through peaceful means, aiming for a sustainable future.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom, non-violence, and the pursuit of glory through ethical means.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal leadership to corporate ethics and social activism.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that true abundance and success come from a foundation of peace and wisdom, encouraging individuals and societies to strive for these ideals.
