वृषायमानो 'वृणीत सोमं त्रिकद्रुकेष्व अपिबत सुतस्य |आ सायकं मगवात्त वज्रम अहन्न एनम् प्रथमजाम अहीनाम् ||
vṛṣāyamāṇo 'vṛṇīta somaṁ trikadrukeṣv apibat sutasya | ā sāyakam maghavādatta vajram ahann enam prathamajām ahīnām ||
  • Mantra Breakdown:
    • vṛṣāyamāṇo: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is strong" or "the powerful one." It signifies strength and vitality.
    • 'vṛṇīta: This means "to choose" or "to select." It indicates a conscious decision-making process.
    • somaṁ: Soma refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • trikadrukeṣv: This term can be understood as "in the three realms" or "in the three worlds," which may refer to the physical, mental, and spiritual dimensions of existence.
    • apibat: This means "to drink" or "to consume," suggesting the act of taking in something vital.
    • sutasya: This refers to "the son" or "the offspring," often symbolizing the results of one's actions or efforts.
    • ā sāyakam: This can be interpreted as "the one who is armed" or "the one who possesses weapons," indicating readiness and protection.
    • maghavādatta: This term refers to "the one who is powerful" or "the mighty one," often associated with the deity Indra.
    • vajram: This means "thunderbolt" or "weapon," symbolizing strength and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • ahann: This means "to strike" or "to hit," indicating action and decisiveness.
    • enam: This refers to "this" or "him," pointing to the subject of the mantra.
    • prathamajām: This means "the firstborn" or "the original," suggesting something that is primary or fundamental.
    • ahīnām: This can be interpreted as "of the serpents" or "of the enemies," indicating challenges or adversaries.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the power of choice and the importance of consuming the divine essence (soma) that nourishes the spirit.
    • It emphasizes the need for strength and readiness in facing challenges, symbolized by the thunderbolt (vajram) and the act of striking (ahann).
    • The reference to the three realms suggests a holistic approach to life, encompassing physical, mental, and spiritual well-being.
    • By invoking the mighty one (maghavā), the mantra calls for divine assistance in overcoming obstacles and adversaries.
  • Real-Life Applications:
    • In personal development, this mantra can inspire individuals to make conscious choices that lead to growth and empowerment.
    • In challenging situations, such as conflicts or competitions, it serves as a reminder to harness inner strength and seek divine guidance.
    • In a broader context, it can be applied to societal challenges, encouraging communities to unite and draw upon collective strength to overcome adversities.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, conscious decision-making, and the pursuit of divine inspiration.
    • It encourages individuals to embrace their power, make wise choices, and seek support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of physical, mental, and spiritual realms, urging us to strive for balance and harmony in our lives.
