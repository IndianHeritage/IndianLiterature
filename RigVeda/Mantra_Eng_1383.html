मन्दामहे दशतयस्य धासेर द्विर यत् पञ्च बिभ्रतो यन्त्यन्ना |किमिष्टाश्व इष्टरश्मिर एता ईशानासस तरुष ऋञ्जते नृन ||
mandāmahe daśatayasya dhāser dvir yat pañca bibhrato yanty annā | kim iṣṭāśva iṣṭaraśmir eta īśānāsas taruṣa ṛñjate nṝn ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mandāmahe: We meditate or contemplate.
    • daśatayasya: Of the ten (or the multitude).
    • dhāser: Of the holder or the possessor.
    • dvir: Twice or twofold.
    • yat: That which.
    • pañca: Five.
    • bibhrato: Bearing or carrying.
    • yanty: They go or they move.
    • annā: Food or nourishment.
    • kim: What.
    • iṣṭāśva: Desired horse or the best of horses.
    • iṣṭaraśmir: Desired light or the best of light.
    • eta: This.
    • īśānāsas: Of the lord or the ruler.
    • taruṣa: Of the trees or the plants.
    • ṛñjate: They shine or they flourish.
    • nṝn: For men or for humans.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra begins with a call to meditate on the divine essence that encompasses the multitude of existence.
    • The reference to "ten" can symbolize completeness or the totality of life experiences.
    • The phrase "twice that which bears five" can be interpreted as the dual nature of sustenance and nourishment, emphasizing the importance of food in sustaining life.
    • The mention of "desired horse" and "desired light" signifies the aspirations and goals that drive human existence.
    • Finally, the mantra concludes with a reflection on the flourishing of life, represented by trees, which symbolize growth, stability, and nourishment for humanity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, meditation can be a practice that helps individuals connect with their inner selves and the universe, promoting mental well-being.
    • The concept of nourishment extends beyond physical food to include emotional and spiritual sustenance, such as relationships and personal growth.
    • Aspiring for goals, represented by the "desired horse" and "light," can be seen in the pursuit of education, career ambitions, and personal development.
    • The flourishing of trees can be likened to community growth, where individuals support each other, leading to a thriving society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of meditation, nourishment, aspirations, and community growth.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us to seek balance in our lives, nurturing our physical, emotional, and spiritual needs.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence, both individually and collectively.
