परेयिवांसम प्रवतो महीर अनु बहुभ्यः पन्थाम अनुपस्पशानम् |वैवस्वतं संगमनं जनानां यमं राजानं हविषा दुवस्य ||
pareyivāṁsam pravato mahīr anu bahubhyaḥ panthām anupaspaśānam | vaivasvataṁ saṁgamanaṁ janānāṁ yamaṁ rājānaṁ haviṣā duvasya ||
  • Mantra: "pareyivāṁsam pravato mahīr anu bahubhyaḥ panthām anupaspaśānam | vaivasvataṁ saṁgamanaṁ janānāṁ yamaṁ rājānaṁ haviṣā duvasya ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pareyivāṁsam: The one who is beyond, transcendent.
    • pravato: The one who is moving or traveling.
    • mahīr: The great or vast.
    • anu: Following or in accordance with.
    • bahubhyaḥ: From many or various.
    • panthām: The path or way.
    • anupaspaśānam: Not seen or not perceived.
    • vaivasvataṁ: Pertaining to Yama, the god of death.
    • saṁgamanaṁ: The coming together or meeting.
    • janānāṁ: Of the people or beings.
    • yamaṁ: Yama, the lord of death.
    • rājānaṁ: The king or ruler.
    • haviṣā: With offerings or sacrifices.
    • duvasya: Of the two or duality.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of a transcendent being who travels through the vastness of existence, following various paths that are often unseen by many.
    • It invokes Yama, the god of death, emphasizing the inevitability of death and the importance of understanding the paths of life and death.
    • The mention of offerings suggests a need for respect and acknowledgment of the forces that govern life and death.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In our daily lives, we often encounter situations where we must make choices that affect our future. This mantra reminds us to be mindful of the paths we choose.
    • For instance, when faced with ethical dilemmas at work, understanding the consequences of our actions can guide us in making decisions that align with our values.
    • The concept of Yama can be seen in the way we treat others, as our actions can have lasting impacts on their lives and our own.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and death, urging us to reflect on our choices and their implications.
    • It highlights the importance of being aware of the unseen paths we traverse in life, encouraging us to live with intention and respect for the greater forces at play.
    • In a broader sense, it teaches us to honor the cycle of life and death, fostering a deeper understanding of our existence and the legacy we leave behind.
