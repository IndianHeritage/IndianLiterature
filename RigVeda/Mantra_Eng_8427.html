नाके सुपर्णम उपपप्तिवांसं गिरो वेनानाम अकृपन्त पूर्वीः |शिशुं रिहन्ति मतयः पनिपनतं हिरण्ययं शकुनं क्षामणि स्थाम ||
nāke suparṇam upapaptivāṁsaṁ giro venānām akṛpanta pūrvīḥ | śiśuṁ rihanti matayaḥ panipnataṁ hiraṇyayaṁ śakunaṁ kṣāmaṇi sthām ||
  • nāke suparṇam upapaptivāṁsaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "In the sky, the well-winged one (the bird) is soaring." Here, "nāke" refers to the sky, and "suparṇa" signifies a bird, often associated with the divine or spiritual realms. In real life, this can symbolize the pursuit of higher knowledge or enlightenment, akin to how a bird flies freely in the vast sky.
  • giro venānām akṛpanta pūrvīḥ: This translates to "The words of the wise are not to be disregarded." "Giro" refers to words or speech, while "venānām" means wise or knowledgeable individuals. This emphasizes the importance of listening to wisdom and guidance from those who have experience. In practical terms, it suggests that we should value the advice of mentors or elders in our lives.
  • śiśuṁ rihanti matayaḥ: This means "The thoughts of the wise nurture the young." "Śiśuṁ" refers to the young or children, and "rihanti" means to nurture or support. This highlights the responsibility of the wise to guide and educate the younger generation. In a real-life context, this can be seen in the role of teachers and parents who shape the future through their guidance.
  • panipnataṁ hiraṇyayaṁ: This translates to "The one who is adorned with gold." "Hiraṇya" means gold, often symbolizing wealth, prosperity, and success. This can represent the rewards of hard work and dedication. In everyday life, it can be related to achieving one's goals through perseverance and effort.
  • śakunaṁ kṣāmaṇi sthām: This phrase means "The auspicious bird (omen) resides in the abode of the wise." "Śakuna" refers to an omen or a bird that brings good fortune, while "kṣāmaṇi" signifies wisdom. This suggests that good fortune is found in the company of the wise. In practical terms, it implies that surrounding oneself with knowledgeable and wise individuals can lead to positive outcomes in life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom, guidance, and nurturing in our lives. It teaches us to value the insights of the wise, to nurture the younger generation, and to strive for higher knowledge. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in educational settings, family dynamics, and personal development. By embracing these principles, we can create a more enlightened and prosperous society, where wisdom is cherished and shared for the benefit of all.
