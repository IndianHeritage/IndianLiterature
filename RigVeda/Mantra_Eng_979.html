ऋजुनीति नो वरुणो मित्रो नयतु विद्वान् |आर्यमा देवैः सजोषाः ||
ṛjunītī no varuṇo mitro nayatu vidvān | aryamā devaiḥ sajoṣāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ṛjunītī: May he lead us straight or guide us rightly.
    • no: Our.
    • varuṇo: Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water.
    • mitro: Mitra, the god of friendship and harmony.
    • nayatu: May he guide or lead.
    • vidvān: The wise or knowledgeable one.
    • aryamā: Aryama, a deity associated with the ancestors and the afterlife.
    • devaiḥ: With the gods or divine beings.
    • sajoṣāḥ: In harmony or together.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for guidance and support from divine forces.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and the role of deities in leading individuals towards righteousness.
    • Varuna and Mitra symbolize the balance of cosmic order and friendship, suggesting that one should seek guidance in both personal and universal aspects of life.
    • Aryama represents the connection to ancestors, indicating that wisdom is not only personal but also collective, passed down through generations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as making a significant life decision, individuals often seek guidance from mentors or wise friends, akin to invoking the support of Varuna and Mitra.
    • In community settings, fostering harmony and cooperation among members can be seen as a practical application of the principles embodied by this mantra.
    • During family gatherings, honoring ancestors and sharing wisdom can create a sense of unity and purpose, reflecting the essence of Aryama.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking guidance from divine forces and wise individuals in our lives.
    • It encourages us to strive for harmony, both within ourselves and in our relationships with others.
    • By acknowledging the wisdom of our ancestors and the importance of community, we can navigate life's challenges with greater clarity and purpose.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our journeys; divine and communal support is always available to guide us towards righteousness.
