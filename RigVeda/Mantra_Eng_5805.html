इन्द्रावरुणा वधनाभिर अप्रतिभेदं वंवन्ता प्र सुदासम् आवतम् |ब्रह्माण्य एषां श्रिणुतं हविमनि सत्याः तृत्सूनाम् अभवत् पुरोहितः ||
indrāvaruṇā vadhanābhir aprati bhedaṁ vanvantā pra sudāsam āvatam | brahmāṇy eṣāṁ śṛṇutaṁ havīmani satyā tṛtsūnām abhavat purohitiḥ ||
  • Indrāvaruṇā: This term refers to the deities Indra and Varuna, who represent the forces of nature and cosmic order. Indra is often associated with rain and thunderstorms, while Varuna embodies the moral and cosmic law.
  • Vadhanābhir: This word means "with faces" or "with mouths," indicating the various aspects or manifestations of these deities.
  • Aprati bhedaṁ: This phrase translates to "without division" or "undivided," suggesting unity and harmony among the forces represented by Indra and Varuna.
  • Vanvantā: This term can be interpreted as "those who possess" or "those who have," indicating the abundance and richness of nature.
  • Pra sudāsam: This refers to "the one who is well-pleased" or "the one who is satisfied," possibly referring to the offerings made to the deities.
  • Āvatam: This means "may they protect" or "may they shelter," invoking the deities' blessings for safety and guidance.
  • Brahmāṇy: This term relates to the ultimate reality or the divine principle, emphasizing the sacredness of the offerings.
  • Eṣāṁ: This means "of these" or "of those," referring to the offerings or the worshippers.
  • Śṛṇutaṁ: This translates to "listen" or "hear," calling upon the deities to pay attention to the prayers and offerings.
  • Havīmani: This refers to "the offerings" or "the oblations," highlighting the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • Satyā: This means "truth" or "truthful," emphasizing the significance of sincerity in worship and offerings.
  • Tṛtsūnām: This term can be interpreted as "of those who seek" or "of those who desire," indicating the aspirations of the worshippers.
  • Abhavat: This means "has become" or "has manifested," suggesting the realization of desires through divine blessings.
  • Purohitiḥ: This refers to "the priest" or "the one who performs the rituals," highlighting the role of the mediator in connecting the worshippers with the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the unity and harmony of natural forces represented by Indra and Varuna, invoking their protection and blessings through sincere offerings. In real life, it signifies the importance of respecting nature and seeking divine guidance in our endeavors. The mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during rituals, ceremonies, or personal prayers, where individuals seek to connect with the divine for protection, abundance, and fulfillment of their desires. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the need for harmony in our actions and intentions.
