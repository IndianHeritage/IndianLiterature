अग्निष्वात्ताः पितर एहा गच्छत सदः-सदः सदता सुप्रणीतयः |अत्तā हविंषि प्रयतानि बरहिष्य अथ रयिं सर्ववीरं दधातन ||
agniṣvāttāḥ pitara eha gacchata sadaḥ-sadaḥ sadata supraṇītayaḥ | attā havīṁṣi prayatāni barhiṣy athā rayiṁ sarvavīraṁ dadhātana ||
  • Agniṣvāttāḥ: This term refers to the divine fire (Agni) and the ancestors (Pitara). In Vedic tradition, Agni is not just a physical fire but symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence. It is often invoked in rituals to connect with the spiritual realm.
  • Pitara eha gacchata: This phrase translates to "O ancestors, come here." It signifies a call to the forefathers, inviting their presence and blessings. In real life, this can be seen in rituals like Shraddha, where descendants honor their ancestors, seeking their guidance and blessings.
  • Sadaḥ-sadaḥ: This means "always, always." It emphasizes the continuity and eternal nature of the connection between the living and the ancestors. In modern life, this can be reflected in the way families maintain traditions and values passed down through generations.
  • Sadata supraṇītayaḥ: This translates to "may they (the ancestors) bestow well-being." It expresses a desire for the ancestors to provide guidance and prosperity. In practical terms, this can be seen in how people seek wisdom from their elders or ancestors when making important life decisions.
  • Attā havīṁṣi: This means "we offer our oblations." It signifies the act of giving and sharing, which is central to many cultures. In everyday life, this can be related to acts of charity and community service, where individuals contribute to the welfare of others.
  • Prayatāni barhiṣy: This phrase means "with effort, we prepare." It highlights the importance of diligence and preparation in achieving goals. In real life, this can be seen in the dedication people show in their careers, education, and personal development.
  • Athā rayiṁ sarvavīraṁ dadhātana: This translates to "may they grant us wealth and strength." It is a prayer for abundance and vitality. In contemporary society, this can be interpreted as the pursuit of success and well-being, both materially and spiritually.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of honoring ancestors, seeking their blessings, and striving for prosperity through hard work and dedication. Its significance lies in the recognition of our roots and the importance of maintaining a connection with those who came before us. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to uphold family traditions, seek wisdom from elders, and contribute positively to their communities, fostering a sense of continuity and purpose in their lives.
