द्यमत्तमं दक्षं धेहि अस्मे सेधā जनानां पूर्वीर अरातिः |वर्षीयो वयः कृणुहि शचीभिर धनस्य साताव अस्मां अविड्डि ||
dyumattamaṁ dakṣaṁ dhehy asme sedhā janānām pūrvīr arātīḥ | varṣīyo vayaḥ kṛṇuhi śacībhir dhanasya sātāv asmām̐ aviḍḍhi ||
  • dyumattamaṁ: This term can be translated as "the most luminous" or "the brightest." It signifies the highest form of light or enlightenment.
  • dakṣaṁ: This word means "skilled" or "capable." It refers to the ability to perform tasks with proficiency and expertise.
  • dhehy: This is a form of the verb "to be" or "to exist," suggesting a state of being or presence.
  • asme: This translates to "for us," indicating a collective or communal aspect.
  • sedhā: This term means "to unite" or "to bring together," emphasizing the importance of harmony and cooperation.
  • janānām: This means "of the people" or "of humanity," highlighting the focus on community and society.
  • pūrvīr: This word refers to "the ancients" or "the predecessors," acknowledging the wisdom and knowledge passed down through generations.
  • arātīḥ: This term can be interpreted as "obstacles" or "hindrances," referring to challenges that may arise in life.
  • varṣīyo: This means "prosperous" or "abundant," indicating a state of wealth and well-being.
  • vayaḥ: This translates to "age" or "time," suggesting the importance of the right timing in achieving goals.
  • kṛṇuhi: This is a call to action, meaning "make" or "create," urging individuals to take initiative.
  • śacībhir: This term refers to "with strength" or "with power," emphasizing the need for vigor and determination.
  • dhanasya: This means "of wealth" or "of resources," highlighting the significance of material abundance.
  • sātāv: This can be interpreted as "to be protected" or "to be safeguarded," indicating the need for security and stability.
  • asmām̐: This translates to "in us," reinforcing the idea of internal strength and capability.
  • aviḍḍhi: This term means "to not be disturbed" or "to remain undisturbed," suggesting a state of peace and tranquility.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of enlightenment, skill, unity, and prosperity in the lives of individuals and communities. It calls for collective action to overcome obstacles and achieve abundance while remaining undisturbed by challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in teamwork, community development, and personal growth. By fostering cooperation and utilizing our skills, we can create a harmonious environment that leads to prosperity and well-being for all. The significance of this mantra lies in its timeless message of unity and strength, encouraging individuals to work together towards common goals while drawing inspiration from the wisdom of the past.
