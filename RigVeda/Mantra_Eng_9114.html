किं स्विद वनं का उ स वृक्ष आस यतो द्यावापृथिवी निष्टतक्षुः |सन्तस्थाने अजरे इतौती अहानि पूर्वीर उषसो जरन्त ||
kiṁ svid vanaṁ ka u sa vṛkṣa āsa yato dyāvāpṛthivī niṣṭatakṣuḥ | saṁtasthāne ajare itaūtī ahāni pūrvīr uṣaso jaranta ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kiṁ svid: What is this?
    • vanaṁ: Forest or grove.
    • ka: Who?
    • u: Or.
    • sa: He/that.
    • vṛkṣa: Tree.
    • āsa: Is/exists.
    • yato: From where.
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: Heaven and Earth.
    • niṣṭatakṣuḥ: With steady gaze.
    • saṁtasthāne: In the place of stability.
    • ajare: Immortal.
    • itaūtī: Here and there.
    • ahāni: Days.
    • pūrvīr: Of the ancients.
    • uṣaso: Dawn.
    • jaranta: Aging.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a philosophical inquiry about the nature of existence and the elements of the universe.
    • It questions the essence of the forest and the trees, seeking to understand their origin and connection to the cosmos.
    • The mention of "dyāvāpṛthivī" signifies the duality of heaven and earth, representing the balance of nature.
    • The phrase "niṣṭatakṣuḥ" suggests a perspective that observes the world with clarity and steadiness, emphasizing the importance of awareness.
    • Furthermore, the mantra reflects on the concept of immortality ("ajare") and the cyclical nature of time ("ahāni"), indicating that while days may pass and things may age, there is a continuity that transcends time.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In environmental conservation, this mantra can inspire individuals to appreciate and protect forests and trees, recognizing their vital role in sustaining life on Earth.
    • In personal development, the idea of observing life with a steady gaze can encourage mindfulness practices, helping individuals to remain present and aware in their daily lives.
    • The concept of immortality can be reflected in the legacies we leave behind, prompting us to consider how our actions today impact future generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all things in the universe, urging us to seek understanding and awareness in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to inspire reflection on our relationship with nature, time, and existence itself.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to cultivate a sense of responsibility towards the environment and to live mindfully, appreciating the present while acknowledging the continuity of life.
