प्र साकामुक्षे अर्चतā गणाय यो दैव्यस्य धाम्नस् तुविष्मान् |उत क्षोदन्ति रोडसी महित्वा नक्षन्ते नाकं निरृतेर अवंशात् ||
pra sākamukṣe arcatā gaṇāya yo daivyasya dhāmnas tuviṣmān | uta kṣodanti rodasī mahitvā nakṣante nākaṁ nirṛter avaṁśāt ||
  • pra sākamukṣe: This phrase can be interpreted as "to the one who is the source of all creation" or "to the one who liberates." It signifies a divine presence that is both a creator and a liberator.
  • arcatā: This means "worship" or "adoration." It emphasizes the importance of reverence and devotion towards the divine.
  • gaṇāya: This translates to "to the multitude" or "to the community." It highlights the collective aspect of worship and the importance of community in spiritual practices.
  • yo daivyasya dhāmnas: This means "who resides in the divine abode." It refers to the ultimate reality or the divine essence that exists beyond the physical realm.
  • tuviṣmān: This term can be understood as "the one who is powerful" or "the one who is capable." It denotes the strength and capability of the divine.
  • uta kṣodanti rodasī: This phrase translates to "and the heavens are filled." It suggests the omnipresence of the divine in the universe.
  • mahitvā: This means "greatness" or "majesty." It signifies the grandeur of the divine and its manifestations in the world.
  • nakṣante nākaṁ: This translates to "the stars shine in the sky." It symbolizes the beauty and order of the cosmos, reflecting the divine's influence in the universe.
  • nirṛter avaṁśāt: This means "from the essence of the eternal." It emphasizes the timeless nature of the divine and its connection to the eternal truths of existence.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of divine worship, community, and the omnipresence of the divine in the universe. It signifies the importance of recognizing the divine in all aspects of life, from the grandeur of the cosmos to the collective experience of worship. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in community worship, appreciate the beauty of nature, and recognize the interconnectedness of all beings. It serves as a reminder to seek liberation through devotion and to acknowledge the divine presence in everyday life.
