ऊर्ध्वो वाम अग्निर अध्वरिष्व अस्तात प्र रात्रि एति जूर्णिनी घृताचि | प्र होता गूर्तमनाऽ उराणो युक्त यो नासत्याः हविमन् ||
ūrdhvo vām agnir adhvareṣv asthāt pra rātir eti jūrṇinī ghṛtācī | pra hotā gūrtamanā urāṇo 'yukta yo nāsatyā havīman ||
  • ūrdhvo: This word translates to "upward" or "higher." It signifies the aspiration towards higher ideals and spiritual elevation.
  • vām: Meaning "your" or "of you," it indicates a personal connection or relationship.
  • agnir: This refers to "fire," which symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. In many cultures, fire is seen as a divine element that connects the earthly realm with the spiritual.
  • adhvareṣv: This term means "in the sacrifices" or "in the rituals." It emphasizes the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • asthāt: Translated as "has stood" or "has established," it indicates stability and permanence in the context of spiritual practices.
  • pra: This prefix means "forth" or "forward," suggesting movement or progression.
  • rātir: Meaning "delight" or "joy," it signifies the emotional and spiritual fulfillment that comes from devotion and connection with the divine.
  • eti: This means "comes" or "arrives," indicating the manifestation of spiritual experiences or blessings.
  • jūrṇinī: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is nourished" or "the one who is sustained," referring to the nurturing aspect of the divine.
  • ghṛtācī: This translates to "with ghee" or "with clarified butter," which is often used in rituals as an offering, symbolizing purity and richness.
  • pra hotā: Meaning "the one who invokes," it refers to the priest or the person who performs the rituals, highlighting the role of guidance in spiritual practices.
  • gūrtamanā: This term means "with a focused mind" or "with a clear intention," emphasizing the importance of mental clarity in spiritual endeavors.
  • urāṇo: This translates to "the one who is wise" or "the one who knows," indicating the value of wisdom in understanding spiritual truths.
  • 'yukta: Meaning "connected" or "united," it signifies the importance of unity in spiritual practices and community.
  • yo: This means "who," referring to the individual or the seeker.
  • nāsatyā: This term refers to "the truthful" or "the honest," emphasizing the importance of integrity in spiritual pursuits.
  • havīman: This translates to "the one who offers," indicating the act of giving and sharing in the context of rituals and spirituality.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of spiritual aspiration, the importance of rituals, and the nurturing aspect of the divine. It emphasizes the need for clarity of mind, integrity, and the role of community in spiritual practices. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, community service, and the pursuit of higher knowledge. By engaging in rituals with a focused mind and a pure heart, individuals can experience joy and fulfillment, ultimately leading to a deeper connection with the divine and a more meaningful life.
