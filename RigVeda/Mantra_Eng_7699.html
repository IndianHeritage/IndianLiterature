अभीमम् अग्न्या उता श्रीणन्ति धेनवः शिशुम् |सोमं इन्द्राय पातवे ||
abhī3mam aghnyā uta śrīṇanti dhenavaḥ śiśum | somam indrāya pātave ||
  • abhī3mam: This word can be interpreted as "that which is to be approached" or "that which is desirable." It signifies something that is worthy of pursuit or attention.
  • aghnyā: This term means "not to be harmed" or "sacred." It refers to something that is pure and should be protected.
  • uta: This word translates to "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition to the previous thought.
  • śrīṇanti: This means "they who are prosperous" or "they who are blessed." It refers to those who are favored by fortune or divine grace.
  • dhenavaḥ: This term means "cows" or "nourishing beings." In the Vedic context, cows are often seen as symbols of wealth, nourishment, and sustenance.
  • śiśum: This word translates to "the child" or "the young one." It signifies innocence, new beginnings, and the future.
  • somam: This refers to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals that symbolizes immortality and divine ecstasy.
  • indrāya: This term means "to Indra," the king of gods in Vedic mythology, who represents strength, power, and rain.
  • pātave: This means "for the drinker" or "for the one who consumes." It indicates the act of offering or sharing.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation that seeks blessings and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of nurturing and protecting what is sacred and valuable, represented by the cows and the child. The reference to soma and Indra highlights the connection to divine strength and the sustenance that comes from the earth.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Environmental Protection: Just as the mantra emphasizes the protection of sacred beings (like cows), it can inspire individuals to engage in environmental conservation efforts, recognizing the importance of nurturing our planet.
  • Child Welfare: The mention of "the child" signifies the importance of caring for the younger generation. This can translate into initiatives focused on education, health, and well-being for children.
  • Community Prosperity: The idea of prosperity and blessings can be applied to community development projects that aim to uplift and support local economies, ensuring that everyone has access to resources.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of nurturing what is sacred, and the pursuit of prosperity through divine blessings. It encourages individuals and communities to protect their resources, care for the vulnerable, and seek strength from the divine in their endeavors.
