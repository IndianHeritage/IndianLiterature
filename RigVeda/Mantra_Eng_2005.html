इयत्तकः कुषुम्भकस्तकं भिनद्म्य अश्मना |ततो विषं प्र वावृते पराचीर अनु संवतः ||
iyattakaḥ kuṣumbhakas takam bhinadmy aśmanā | tato viṣam pra vāvṛte parācīr anu saṁvataḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • iyattakaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "that which is bound" or "that which is connected."
    • kuṣumbhakas: Refers to "the plant" or "the herb," often associated with medicinal properties.
    • takam: Means "to break" or "to separate."
    • bhinadmy: Translates to "I break" or "I shatter."
    • aśmanā: Means "with stone" or "by means of stone."
    • tato: Means "therefore" or "from that."
    • viṣam: Refers to "poison" or "toxic substance."
    • pra: Means "forth" or "outward."
    • vāvṛte: Translates to "it has been revealed" or "it has come forth."
    • parācīr: Refers to "the boundary" or "the edge."
    • anu: Means "following" or "after."
    • saṁvataḥ: Refers to "the cycle" or "the process."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the process of breaking down something that is bound or connected, specifically referring to a plant with medicinal properties.
    • The act of breaking with stone symbolizes the use of tools or methods to extract or reveal something hidden, such as the beneficial properties of the plant.
    • The mention of poison suggests that there are harmful elements that need to be addressed or transformed into something beneficial.
    • The idea of boundaries and cycles indicates a process of transformation that is continuous and necessary for growth and healing.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In herbal medicine, practitioners often use stones or tools to crush herbs to extract their medicinal properties, similar to the breaking mentioned in the mantra.
    • In life, we often face toxic situations or relationships that need to be addressed. The mantra can symbolize the need to confront and break away from these harmful influences to reveal a healthier state of being.
    • The concept of cycles can be seen in nature, where plants grow, die, and regenerate, reflecting the continuous process of life and healing.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of breaking down barriers and addressing toxic elements in our lives to reveal healing and growth.
    • It serves as a reminder that transformation is a necessary part of life, and by confronting challenges, we can uncover beneficial aspects that lead to well-being.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, healing practices, and environmental sustainability, where breaking down harmful elements leads to positive outcomes.
