समाने अहन त्रिर अवद्यगोहना त्रिर अद्य यज्ञं मधुनā मिमिक्षतम् |त्रिर वाजवतीर इषो अश्विनā युवं दोषा अस्मभ्यम् उषसश् च पिन्वतम् ||
samāne ahan trir avadyagohanā trir adya yajñam madhunā mimikṣatam | trir vājavatīr iṣo aśvinā yuvaṁ doṣā asmabhyam uṣasaś ca pinvatam ||
  • Mantra: "samāne ahan trir avadyagohanā trir adya yajñam madhunā mimikṣatam | trir vājavatīr iṣo aśvinā yuvaṁ doṣā asmabhyam uṣasaś ca pinvatam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samāne: equal, similar
    • ahan: day
    • trir: three times
    • avadyagohanā: without any fault or sin
    • adya: today
    • yajñam: sacrifice, offering
    • madhunā: with sweetness, honey
    • mimikṣatam: may we see, may we perceive
    • vājavatīr: like a horse, powerful
    • iṣo: of the desires, wishes
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins, the divine twin horsemen
    • yuvaṁ: young, youthful
    • doṣā: faults, defects
    • asmabhyam: to us
    • uṣasaś: of the dawn
    • ca: and
    • pinvatam: may we enjoy, may we receive
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the harmony and balance of the day, invoking the divine for blessings.
    • The repetition of "trir" emphasizes the importance of the actions being performed three times, symbolizing completeness and thoroughness.
    • The reference to "yajñam" indicates the significance of sacrifice and offerings in life, which can be interpreted as the need for selflessness and contribution to the greater good.
    • The mention of "madhunā" suggests that our actions should be sweet and pleasant, akin to honey, which can be related to the idea of kindness and compassion in our interactions.
    • The invocation of the "aśvinā" highlights the youthful energy and vitality that we should embody, reminding us to approach life with enthusiasm and vigor.
    • The phrase "doṣā asmabhyam" indicates a plea for the removal of faults, which can be seen as a call for personal growth and self-improvement.
    • Finally, "uṣasaś ca pinvatam" signifies the desire to enjoy the blessings of the dawn, representing new beginnings and opportunities that each day brings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of balance, selflessness, and the pursuit of personal growth.
    • In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as in our daily routines, where we strive to maintain harmony in our actions and relationships.
    • It encourages us to approach challenges with a youthful spirit and to seek sweetness in our endeavors, fostering a positive environment around us.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to embrace each day with gratitude and a commitment to bettering ourselves and our communities.
