सूता अनु स्वं आरजोऽभ्यर्षन्ति बभ्रवः | इन्द्रं गच्छन्त इन्दवः ||
sutā anu svam ā rajo 'bhy arṣanti babhravaḥ | indraṁ gacchanta indavaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Sutā: Sons or offspring, often referring to those who are born of a particular lineage.
    • anu: Following or in accordance with.
    • svam: Own or personal.
    • ā: To or towards.
    • rajo: Dust or earth, symbolizing the material world.
    • 'bhy: From or out of.
    • arṣanti: They are inspired or they arise.
    • babhravaḥ: They who are strong or powerful.
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods, representing strength and power.
    • gacchanta: They go or they move.
    • indavaḥ: The celestial beings or the divine, often associated with the moon or nectar.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the offspring who follow their own path, inspired by the material world.
    • It highlights the strength and power of these beings as they seek the divine, represented by Indra.
    • The imagery of dust signifies the earthly challenges and experiences that shape one's journey.
    • Indra symbolizes the ultimate goal or aspiration, representing strength, leadership, and divine guidance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family business, the next generation (sutā) may follow their own vision (anu svam) while being inspired by the legacy of their predecessors (rajo).
    • Students pursuing their dreams in education often face challenges (rajo) but are motivated by their aspirations (indraṁ) to achieve greatness.
    • In a community, individuals who are strong (babhravaḥ) and inspired (arṣanti) can lead others towards collective goals (gacchanta indavaḥ).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of following one's own path while being inspired by the strength and guidance of the divine.
    • It teaches us that while we may face challenges in the material world, our aspirations and the strength we derive from our lineage can guide us towards our goals.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal growth, leadership, and community building, reminding us to stay true to ourselves while seeking higher ideals.
