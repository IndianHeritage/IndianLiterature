प्र राजा वाचं जनयन्न असीष्यदद अपो वसानो अभि गा इयक्षति | गृभ्णाति रिप्रम अविर अस्य तान्वा शुद्धो देवानाम उप याती निष्कृतम् ||
pra rājā vācaṁ janayann asiṣyadad apo vasāno abhi gā iyakṣati | gṛbhṇāti ripram avir asya tānvā śuddho devānām upa yāti niṣkṛtam ||
  • pra rājā vācaṁ janayann asiṣyadad: This phrase can be translated as "the king, by uttering words, creates." Here, "pra rājā" refers to a ruler or king, and "vācaṁ" means speech or words. This highlights the power of speech and communication in leadership.
  • apo vasāno abhi gā iyakṣati: This part translates to "the one who resides in the waters, who moves towards the light." "Apo" refers to water, which is often seen as a source of life and purity. This can symbolize the importance of nurturing and sustaining life through wise governance.
  • gṛbhṇāti ripram avir asya: This means "he who takes away the enemy, who is visible." "Gṛbhṇāti" means to seize or take, and "ripram" refers to the enemy. This emphasizes the role of a leader in protecting their people from adversaries.
  • tānvā śuddho devānām upa yāti niṣkṛtam: This translates to "the pure one approaches the divine." "Tānvā" means pure, and "devānām" refers to the gods. This suggests that purity of intention and action leads one closer to divine favor and guidance.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, communication, and the divine connection that a ruler must maintain. In real life, this can be applied to various scenarios such as:

  • In politics, leaders must communicate effectively to inspire and unite their constituents.
  • In personal relationships, clear and honest communication can help resolve conflicts and strengthen bonds.
  • In business, leaders who protect their teams and foster a positive environment can drive success and innovation.

This mantra serves as a reminder of the responsibilities that come with leadership and the importance of maintaining purity of purpose and action to achieve harmony and success in any endeavor.
