इध्मेनाग्न इच्छमानो घृतेन जुहोमि हव्यम् तरसे बलाय |यावद ईशे ब्रह्मणा वन्दमान इमां धियं शतसेयाय देविम् ||
idhmenāgna icchamāno ghṛtena juhomi havyaṁ tarase balāya | yāvad īśe brahmaṇā vandamāna imāṁ dhiyaṁ śataseyāya devīm ||
  • Idhmenāgna: This phrase can be translated as "with this offering, O Agni." Agni, the fire god, is a central figure in Vedic rituals, symbolizing the divine messenger between humans and the gods.
  • Icchamāno: This means "desiring" or "wishing." It reflects the intention behind the offering, emphasizing the importance of desire in spiritual practices.
  • Ghṛtena: This translates to "with ghee" (clarified butter), which is a sacred substance in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and nourishment.
  • Juhomi: This means "I offer" or "I sacrifice." It indicates the act of giving, which is fundamental in many spiritual traditions.
  • Havyaṁ: This refers to "the offering" or "the oblation," which is the substance being offered to the deities.
  • Tarase: This means "swiftly" or "quickly," suggesting the urgency and importance of the offering.
  • Balāya: This translates to "for strength" or "for power," indicating the purpose of the offering is to gain strength or vitality.
  • Yāvad: This means "as long as" or "until," setting a condition or duration for the offering.
  • Īśe: This translates to "to the Lord" or "to the divine," referring to the ultimate source of power and guidance.
  • Brahmaṇā: This means "with the divine knowledge" or "with the essence of the universe," emphasizing the connection between the offering and the cosmic order.
  • Vandamāna: This translates to "worshiping" or "adoring," indicating reverence towards the divine.
  • Imāṁ dhiyaṁ: This means "this intellect" or "this understanding," referring to the mental and spiritual clarity sought through the offering.
  • Śataseyāya: This translates to "for a hundredfold" or "for abundance," indicating the desire for prosperity and success.
  • Devīm: This means "to the goddess" or "to the divine feminine," acknowledging the role of feminine energy in creation and sustenance.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of offering and devotion in Vedic tradition. It signifies the importance of intention, purity, and reverence in spiritual practices. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, personal prayers, or even in daily life when one seeks strength and clarity. By offering our intentions and desires to a higher power, we align ourselves with the cosmic order and invite abundance into our lives. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing our spiritual selves through acts of devotion and gratitude.
