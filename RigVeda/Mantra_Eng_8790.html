यत्र ज्योतिर्ज्यस्रं यस्मिं लोके स्वरहितम् |तस्मिन् मां धेहि पवमानामृत लोके अक्षित इन्द्रायन्दो परि श्रव ||
yatra jyotir ajasraṁ yasmim̐l loke svar hitam | tasmin māṁ dhehi pavamānāmṛte loke akṣita indrāyendo pari srava ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yatra: where
    • jyotir: light or illumination
    • ajasraṁ: constantly or perpetually
    • yasmim̐l: in which
    • loke: world or realm
    • svar: well-being or prosperity
    • hitam: beneficial or favorable
    • tasmin: in that
    • māṁ: me
    • dhehi: place or establish
    • pavamānāmṛte: in the pure, immortal state
    • loke: in the world
    • akṣita: imperishable or eternal
    • indrāyendo: to Indra, the king of gods
    • pari: around or about
    • srava: flow or abundance
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a place or state where there is constant light and well-being.
    • It emphasizes the importance of being in a realm that is beneficial and prosperous.
    • The invocation to Indra suggests a plea for divine support and guidance in achieving this state.
    • It reflects the desire for purity and immortality, indicating a quest for spiritual elevation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, creating an environment that fosters positivity and well-being can lead to greater productivity and job satisfaction.
    • In personal life, surrounding oneself with supportive and uplifting individuals can enhance one's mental and emotional health.
    • In community settings, initiatives that promote health, education, and prosperity can lead to a thriving society.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for a harmonious and prosperous existence.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek out and cultivate environments that are beneficial to oneself and others.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, community building, and workplace culture, emphasizing the importance of light, purity, and divine guidance in our pursuits.
