अग्नि जारितर विश्वपतिस् तेपानो देव रक्षसः |अप्रोषिवान गृहपतिर्महाम् असि दिवस् पायुर दुरोनयुः ||
agne jaritar viśpatis tepāno deva rakṣasaḥ | aproṣivān gṛhapatir mahām̐ asi divas pāyur duroṇayuḥ ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying force and a source of energy.
  • Jaritar: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is born" or "the one who is generated." It signifies the life-giving aspect of fire.
  • Viśpatis: This means "lord of all beings" or "ruler of the universe," indicating the omnipresence and authority of Agni.
  • Tepāno: This word suggests "he who warms" or "he who provides heat," emphasizing the nurturing aspect of fire.
  • Deva: This translates to "god" or "divine being," reinforcing the sacred nature of Agni.
  • Rakṣasaḥ: This term refers to "protectors" or "guardians," indicating the protective qualities associated with fire.
  • Aproṣivān: This can be understood as "he who is invoked" or "he who is called upon," highlighting the importance of rituals and prayers in connecting with the divine.
  • Gṛhapatir: This means "lord of the house," symbolizing the domestic aspect of fire, particularly in the context of home and family.
  • Mahām̐: This translates to "great" or "mighty," signifying the immense power of Agni.
  • Asi: This word means "you are," affirming the presence and essence of Agni in the universe.
  • Divas: This translates to "heaven" or "day," indicating the connection between fire and the celestial realm.
  • Pāyur: This means "protector" or "sustainer," emphasizing the role of Agni in safeguarding life.
  • Duroṇayuḥ: This term can be interpreted as "long-lived" or "enduring," suggesting the eternal nature of fire and its significance in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the multifaceted nature of Agni, representing not only a physical element but also a spiritual force that nurtures, protects, and sustains life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be invoked during rituals, ceremonies, and personal prayers to seek blessings for health, prosperity, and protection. The significance of Agni in domestic life is profound, as it symbolizes the warmth of home and the unity of family. By recognizing the divine qualities of fire, individuals can cultivate a deeper connection with their surroundings and the universe, fostering a sense of gratitude and reverence for the life-giving forces at play in their lives.
