गौर धयति मरुतां श्रवस्युर मातā मघोनाम् |युक्ता वह्नी रथानाम् ||
gaur dhayati marutāṁ śravasyur mātā maghonām | yuktā vahnī rathānām ||
  • gaur dhayati: The term "gaur" refers to the cow, which is often seen as a symbol of nourishment and abundance in Vedic literature. "Dhayati" means to nurture or to support. Thus, this phrase can be interpreted as "the cow nurtures" or "the nurturing aspect of the cow."
  • marutāṁ: This word refers to the Maruts, the storm deities in Vedic mythology. They are often associated with strength, power, and the forces of nature. The Maruts are known to bring rain and are considered protectors of the earth.
  • śravasyur: This term can be translated as "the one who is renowned" or "the one who is celebrated." It signifies a being or entity that is worthy of praise and recognition.
  • mātā: This word means "mother." In the context of this mantra, it signifies the nurturing and protective qualities of a mother figure, which can be extended to nature and the earth itself.
  • maghonām: This term translates to "of the rich" or "of the prosperous." It indicates abundance and wealth, often in a spiritual or material sense.
  • yuktā: This word means "joined" or "connected." It implies a sense of unity and harmony among different elements.
  • vahnī: This term refers to fire, which is a significant element in Vedic rituals. Fire symbolizes transformation, purification, and energy.
  • rathānām: This word means "of the chariots." In Vedic literature, chariots are often associated with movement, progress, and the journey of life.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of nurturing, strength, and abundance. It highlights the interconnectedness of nature, the divine, and human existence. The cow, as a symbol of nourishment, represents the sustenance provided by the earth, while the Maruts signify the powerful forces that govern nature.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be applied in various ways. For instance, it can inspire individuals to appreciate and protect nature, recognizing the vital role it plays in our sustenance. It can also serve as a reminder of the importance of nurturing relationships, akin to the bond between a mother and her child. Furthermore, the emphasis on unity and connection can encourage collaboration and harmony in communities.

  • Conclusion: The mantra "gaur dhayati marutāṁ śravasyur mātā maghonām | yuktā vahnī rathānām ||" serves as a profound reminder of the nurturing aspects of nature and the divine. It encourages us to recognize the abundance around us and to foster connections that promote growth and harmony. By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can lead more fulfilling lives, grounded in respect for the earth and each other.
