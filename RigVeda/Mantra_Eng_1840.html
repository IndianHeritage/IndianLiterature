तृणस्कन्दस्य नु विशः परि वृङ्क्त सुदानवः |ऊर्ध्वान नः कर्त जīvसे ||
tṛṇaskandasya nu viśaḥ pari vṛṅkta sudānavaḥ | ūrdhvān naḥ karta jīvase ||
  • tṛṇaskandasya: This term can be broken down into two parts: "tṛṇa," which means grass, and "skanda," which refers to the stem or base. Together, it symbolizes the foundation or support of life, akin to how grass supports the ecosystem.
  • nu: This word is often used to indicate a question or an inquiry, suggesting a search for understanding or clarity.
  • viśaḥ: This translates to "the world" or "the universe," encompassing all living beings and the environment around us.
  • pari vṛṅkta: "Pari" means around or surrounding, while "vṛṅkta" refers to being protected or enveloped. Together, they imply a sense of being surrounded by protection or support.
  • sudānavaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "good nourishment" or "beneficial sustenance," indicating the importance of nourishment for life.
  • ūrdhvān: This word means "upward" or "higher," suggesting aspirations, growth, and elevation in life.
  • naḥ: This translates to "our," indicating a collective or communal aspect.
  • karta: This means "doer" or "creator," referring to the force or entity that brings things into existence.
  • jīvase: This translates to "for life" or "to live," emphasizing the essence of existence and vitality.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for the protection and nourishment of life, seeking support from the universe to elevate our existence. It reflects a deep connection between the individual and the cosmos, emphasizing the importance of community and the nurturing aspects of life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Environmental Awareness: Just as grass supports the ecosystem, we must recognize our role in protecting the environment. This mantra encourages us to nurture our surroundings, ensuring that we provide a safe habitat for all living beings.
  • Community Support: The idea of being surrounded by protection can be seen in the importance of community. In times of crisis, such as natural disasters or personal hardships, the support of friends and family can uplift individuals, much like the protective embrace described in the mantra.
  • Personal Growth: The upward aspiration signifies the pursuit of personal goals and self-improvement. This mantra serves as a reminder to strive for higher achievements in life, whether in education, career, or personal development.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nourishment, protection, and growth. It serves as a guiding principle for living harmoniously with nature and within our communities, inspiring us to seek higher purposes and contribute positively to the world around us.
