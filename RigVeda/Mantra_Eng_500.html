यो नः पूषन्नघो वृषको दुःशेवा आदिदेशति |अप स्म तं पथो जहि ||
yo naḥ pūṣann agho vṛko duḥśeva ādideśati | apa sma tam patho jahi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • naḥ: our
    • pūṣann: O nourisher
    • agho: sin or evil
    • vṛko: wolf or predator
    • duḥśeva: difficult to deal with
    • ādideśati: commands or instructs
    • apa: away
    • sma: indeed
    • tam: that
    • patho: path or way
    • jahi: remove or destroy
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to the divine, asking for the removal of sins and obstacles that hinder our spiritual and material progress. The speaker addresses the divine as a nourisher, seeking guidance and protection from negative influences symbolized by the wolf, which represents predatory forces or challenges in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, one might face unethical competition (the "wolf") that threatens their integrity. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek divine support in overcoming such challenges.
    • In personal relationships, misunderstandings or conflicts can arise (symbolizing "sin"). Reciting this mantra can help individuals focus on resolving issues and fostering harmony.
    • During times of stress or anxiety, individuals may feel overwhelmed by negative thoughts (the "difficult to deal with"). This mantra encourages seeking inner strength and clarity to navigate through tough times.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in overcoming life's challenges and moral dilemmas. It emphasizes the importance of seeking nourishment for the soul and guidance to stay on the right path. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal growth, ethical decision-making, and conflict resolution, reminding us to seek higher wisdom and support in our endeavors.
