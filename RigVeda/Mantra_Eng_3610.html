आ पर्वतस्य मरुताम् अवांसी देवस्य त्रातुर अव्रि भागस्य |पत् पतिर जन्याद अंहसो नो मित्रो मित्रियाद उता न उरुष्येत ||
ā parvatasya marutām avāṁsi devasya trātur avri bhagasya | pāt patir janyād aṁhaso no mitro mitriyād uta na uruṣyet ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • parvatasya: of the mountain
    • marutām: of the winds
    • avāṁsi: protect
    • devasya: of the deity
    • trātur: protector
    • avri: grant
    • bhagasya: of wealth or fortune
    • pāt: lord
    • patir: master
    • janyād: born from
    • aṁhaso: of the light
    • no: our
    • mitro: friend
    • mitriyād: may he be friendly
    • uta: or
    • na: not
    • uruṣyet: may he be distant
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the protective powers of the winds and the mountains, symbolizing strength and stability.
    • The reference to the deity suggests a plea for divine intervention and support in times of need.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of friendship and community, asking for the blessings of wealth and fortune to be close to us.
    • It also expresses a desire for protection from negativity or harmful influences, urging that they remain distant.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of natural disasters, such as floods or storms, communities often come together to seek protection and support, much like the invocation of the winds and mountains in this mantra.
    • In personal relationships, fostering friendships and maintaining a supportive network can be seen as a modern application of the mantra's call for companionship and protection.
    • In business, seeking divine blessings for prosperity and protection from competition can resonate with the mantra's themes of wealth and safeguarding.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature, community, and spirituality.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the forces that protect and nurture us, urging us to seek harmony with our surroundings and relationships.
    • In real life, it encourages us to cultivate friendships, seek divine blessings, and remain resilient in the face of challenges.
