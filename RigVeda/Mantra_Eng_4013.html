वेति अग्रुर जनिवान वा अति स्पृधः समर्यतां मनसा सूर्यः कविः |घ्रंसं रक्षन्तं परि विश्वतो गयम् अस्माकं शर्मा वनवत स्वावसुः ||
vety agrur janivān vā ati spṛdhaḥ samaryatā manasā sūryaḥ kaviḥ | ghraṁsaṁ rakṣantam pari viśvato gayam asmākaṁ śarma vanavat svāvasuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vety: knows
    • agrur: the foremost
    • janivān: the creator or the one who gives birth
    • vā: or
    • ati: exceedingly
    • spṛdhaḥ: desirous
    • samaryatā: with control or restraint
    • manasā: with the mind
    • sūryaḥ: the sun
    • kaviḥ: the poet or the seer
    • ghraṁsaṁ: the one who protects
    • rakṣantam: the protector
    • pari: all around
    • viśvato: from all sides
    • gayam: the place of joy or happiness
    • asmākaṁ: our
    • śarma: comfort or shelter
    • vanavat: like a forest
    • svāvasuḥ: the one who is self-sufficient
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the supreme knowledge and creativity of the divine, often associated with the sun.
    • The phrase "vety agrur janivān" emphasizes the idea that the foremost creator knows everything, suggesting a divine omniscience.
    • "ati spṛdhaḥ samaryatā manasā" indicates the importance of controlling desires and thoughts, which is crucial in achieving mental peace and clarity.
    • The reference to "sūryaḥ kaviḥ" connects the sun with enlightenment and wisdom, portraying it as a source of inspiration and creativity.
    • "ghraṁsaṁ rakṣantam pari viśvato gayam" suggests that the divine protector surrounds us with joy and happiness, providing a sense of security and comfort.
    • The concluding part "asmākaṁ śarma vanavat svāvasuḥ" implies that this divine protection is akin to a forest, offering shelter and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be reflected in the pursuit of knowledge and creativity, encouraging individuals to seek wisdom and express themselves artistically.
    • The emphasis on controlling desires can be seen in practices like meditation and mindfulness, which help individuals achieve mental clarity and emotional balance.
    • The idea of divine protection can be related to the support we receive from our families and communities, creating a safe environment for personal growth.
    • In nature, forests symbolize abundance and shelter, reminding us of the importance of nurturing our surroundings and finding solace in them.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine knowledge, creativity, and protection in our lives.
    • It encourages us to cultivate wisdom, control our desires, and seek comfort in the support of our communities.
    • By recognizing the divine presence in our lives, we can foster a sense of security and joy, much like the shelter provided by a forest.
    • Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, creativity, and the nurturing aspects of life.
