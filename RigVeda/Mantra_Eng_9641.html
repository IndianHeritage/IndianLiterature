विष्वतश्चक्षुरुत विष्वतोंमुखो विष्वतोबाहुरुत विष्वतस्पात् |सम्बाहुभ्यां धामति सम्पतत्रैर्यवाभूमी जनयन् देव एकः ||
viśvataścakṣur uta viśvatomukho viśvatobāhur uta viśvataspāt | sam bāhubhyāṁ dhamati sam patatrair dyāvābhūmī janayan deva ekaḥ ||
  • viśvataścakṣur: This term translates to "the eye that sees everywhere." It signifies the omnipresence and all-seeing nature of the divine. In real life, this can be likened to the idea of awareness and mindfulness, where one is conscious of their surroundings and actions.
  • uta viśvatomukho: This means "with a face turned in all directions." It represents the ability to perceive and understand multiple perspectives. For example, in conflict resolution, understanding different viewpoints is crucial for finding common ground.
  • viśvatobāhur: This translates to "with arms that reach everywhere." It symbolizes the capacity to embrace and support all beings. In a practical sense, this can be seen in acts of kindness and charity, where individuals extend help to those in need, regardless of their background.
  • uta viśvataspāt: This means "with wings that spread in all directions." It signifies freedom and the ability to transcend limitations. In real life, this can be related to pursuing one's dreams and aspirations, breaking free from societal constraints.
  • sam bāhubhyāṁ dhamati: This phrase translates to "he who unites with both arms." It emphasizes the importance of unity and collaboration. In a workplace setting, teamwork is essential for achieving common goals, showcasing how collective effort leads to success.
  • sam patatrair: This means "with wings that are united." It symbolizes harmony and cooperation among different entities. For instance, in nature, various species coexist and support each other, demonstrating the interconnectedness of life.
  • dyāvābhūmī: This translates to "the sky and the earth." It represents the duality of existence and the balance between different elements. In our lives, maintaining a balance between work and personal life is crucial for overall well-being.
  • janayan deva ekaḥ: This means "the one God who creates." It signifies the singularity of the divine source of creation. In a broader sense, this can be interpreted as the idea that all life is interconnected and originates from a common source, promoting a sense of unity among humanity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of unity, awareness, and the interconnectedness of all beings. Its significance lies in the reminder that we are all part of a larger whole, encouraging us to embrace diversity, support one another, and strive for harmony in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to practice mindfulness, foster collaboration, and promote kindness, ultimately leading to a more compassionate and understanding society.
