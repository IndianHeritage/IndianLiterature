तमा आसीत तमसा गूढम् अग्रे प्रकेतं सलिलं सर्वम् आ इदं |तुच्छ्येनाभ्व अपिहितं यद आसीत तपसस् तन् महिनाजायतैकम् ||
tama āsīt tamasā gūḻham agre 'praketaṁ salilaṁ sarvam ā idam | tucchyenābhv apihitaṁ yad āsīt tapasas tan mahinājāyataikam ||
  • tama āsīt: This phrase translates to "there was darkness" or "darkness existed." It signifies the primordial state of the universe before creation, representing ignorance or the unknown.
  • tamasā gūḻham agre: This means "hidden by darkness at the beginning." It emphasizes the idea that before creation, everything was enveloped in darkness, suggesting a lack of clarity and understanding.
  • praketaṁ salilaṁ sarvam ā idam: This translates to "the waters were everywhere." In many cultures, water symbolizes life and creation. Here, it indicates that the waters were the source of all existence, a nurturing element from which life emerged.
  • tucchyenābhv apihitaṁ: This means "that which was hidden by emptiness." It suggests that within the darkness and chaos, there was a potential for creation that was not yet realized.
  • yad āsīt tapasas: This translates to "that which existed through austerity." Austerity here refers to the disciplined practices that lead to spiritual awakening and creation, indicating that creation is a result of focused energy and intention.
  • tan mahinājāyataikam: This means "from that, the great one was born." It signifies the emergence of the universe or the divine from the primordial state, highlighting the idea of creation arising from chaos and darkness.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of creation in the universe, illustrating the transition from darkness to light, chaos to order. It reflects the philosophical concept that from the unknown and unmanifested, the universe and life emerge through a process of transformation.
  • Real-life Application: In our lives, we often encounter periods of uncertainty and confusion, akin to the "darkness" described in the mantra. Just as creation arises from this state, we can find clarity and purpose through introspection and disciplined practices, such as meditation or mindfulness. These practices can help us navigate through our challenges, leading to personal growth and enlightenment.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder that even in the darkest times, there is potential for creation and transformation. It encourages us to embrace our struggles as opportunities for growth and to seek clarity through discipline and introspection. By understanding this cycle of creation, we can apply its wisdom to our daily lives, fostering resilience and a deeper connection to the universe.
