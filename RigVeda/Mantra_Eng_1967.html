प्राचीनं बर्हिरोजसा सहस्रवीरम् अस्तृणन् |यत्रादित्याः विराजथ ||
prācīnam barhir ojasā sahasravīram astṛṇan | yatrādityā virājatha ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "The ancient one, with great energy, has spread out the thousand-fold strength, where the sun shines brilliantly."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • prācīnam: Ancient or old, referring to something that has been established over time.
    • barhir: A term that can mean a covering or a layer, often associated with the earth or nature.
    • ojasā: Energy or vitality, indicating a powerful force or strength.
    • sahasravīram: Thousand-fold strength or power, suggesting immense capability or potential.
    • astṛṇan: To spread out or to cover, indicating the act of encompassing or enveloping.
    • yatrādityā: Where the sun (Aditya) shines, symbolizing light, knowledge, and enlightenment.
    • virājatha: To shine or to be resplendent, often associated with glory and brilliance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra can be seen as a metaphor for the power of nature and the universe. For instance, consider the way sunlight nourishes plants, allowing them to grow and thrive. Just as the sun spreads its energy, we too can harness our inner strength to achieve our goals.
    • In a personal development context, the idea of "thousand-fold strength" can be applied to the potential within each individual. For example, when someone works hard and remains dedicated, they can achieve remarkable results, much like how the sun's energy supports life on Earth.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can inspire collective strength. When individuals come together, they can create a powerful force for change, similar to how sunlight can illuminate a dark space, bringing clarity and hope.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its celebration of ancient wisdom and the immense power of nature. It reminds us of the energy that surrounds us and the potential we hold within. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal growth, community efforts, or our relationship with nature, this mantra encourages us to recognize and harness our strengths. By acknowledging the ancient forces at play, we can strive for enlightenment and success in our endeavors, much like the sun that shines brightly, illuminating our paths.
