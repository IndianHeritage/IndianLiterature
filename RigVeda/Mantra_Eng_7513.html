मां न इन्द्राभ्य आ3दिशः सूरो अक्तुष्व आ यमन |त्वा युजां वनेम तत ||
mā na indrābhy ā3diśaḥ sūro aktuṣv ā yaman | tvā yujā vanema tat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mā: not
    • na: not
    • indrābhy: from Indra (the king of gods)
    • ā3diśaḥ: may I be directed
    • sūro: the hero
    • aktuṣv: in the battles
    • ā: towards
    • yaman: the one who is to be controlled
    • tvā: you
    • yujā: with the connection
    • vanema: I may attain
    • tat: that
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for guidance and strength from the divine, specifically from Indra, who is often associated with power and victory.
    • The speaker seeks to be directed away from negative influences and towards the path of heroism and valor.
    • It emphasizes the importance of connection and alignment with higher powers to achieve success in endeavors, particularly in challenging situations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive work environment, an individual may recite this mantra to seek clarity and strength to navigate challenges, ensuring they remain focused on their goals.
    • A student preparing for exams might use this mantra to invoke concentration and determination, asking for guidance to overcome distractions and perform well.
    • During a sports competition, athletes may reflect on this mantra to channel their inner strength and seek divine support in their pursuit of victory.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and strength in the face of challenges.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios where individuals face obstacles, whether in personal, academic, or professional pursuits.
    • By invoking this mantra, one can cultivate a mindset of resilience and determination, fostering a deeper connection with their inner strength and the support of higher powers.
