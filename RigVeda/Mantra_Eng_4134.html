मृळत नो मरुतो मा वधिष्ठनास्मभ्यं शर्मा बहुलं वि यन्तना |अधि स्तोत्रस्य सख्यस्य गातन शुभं याताम अनु रथा अवृत्सत ||
mṛḻata no maruto mā vadhiṣṭanāsmabhyaṁ śarma bahulaṁ vi yantana | adhi stotrasya sakhyasya gātana śubhaṁ yātām anu rathā avṛtsata ||
  • mṛḻata no maruto: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the Maruts, the storm deities in Vedic literature, to bestow their blessings upon us. The term "mṛḻata" suggests a plea for protection and support.
  • mā vadhiṣṭanāsmabhyaṁ: This part translates to "may they not abandon us." It emphasizes the importance of divine presence and support in our lives, highlighting the need for guidance and protection from higher powers.
  • śarma bahulaṁ vi yantana: Here, "śarma" refers to comfort or shelter, and "bahulaṁ" means abundant. This line expresses a desire for abundant comfort and safety, indicating that we seek a nurturing environment in our lives.
  • adhi stotrasya sakhyasya: This phrase can be understood as a reference to the friendship or alliance with the divine. "Stotrasya" relates to hymns or praises, suggesting that through devotion and reverence, we can foster a close relationship with the divine.
  • gātana śubhaṁ yātām: This translates to "may we attain auspiciousness." It reflects the aspiration for good fortune and positive outcomes in our endeavors, emphasizing the importance of seeking blessings for success.
  • anu rathā avṛtsata: The final part can be interpreted as a request for the divine to guide our chariots (or paths) in life. "Rathā" symbolizes the journey of life, and "avṛtsata" suggests a smooth and prosperous journey.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a profound plea for divine support, comfort, and auspiciousness in our lives. It signifies the importance of maintaining a connection with the divine forces that guide and protect us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable during times of uncertainty or challenges, such as starting a new venture, facing personal struggles, or seeking guidance in decision-making. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of security and hope, reinforcing the belief that they are not alone in their journeys and that divine assistance is always available. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek comfort, foster relationships with the divine, and aspire for positive outcomes in all aspects of life.
