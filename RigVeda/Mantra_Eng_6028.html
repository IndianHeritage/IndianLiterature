मो ष्व आद्य दुर्हणावान सायं करदारे अस्मत् |अश्रीर इव जामाता ||
mo ṣv a1dya durhaṇāvān sāyaṁ karad āre asmat | aśrīra iva jāmātā ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • mo: This can be interpreted as "my" or "mine," indicating possession or personal connection.
    • ṣv: This is often associated with "to be" or "to exist," suggesting a state of being.
    • a1dya: This term can mean "today" or "now," emphasizing the present moment.
    • durhaṇāvān: This can be translated as "one who is difficult to approach" or "hard to reach," indicating a sense of challenge or complexity.
    • sāyaṁ: This word means "evening" or "night," representing a time of reflection or transition.
    • karad: This can mean "to do" or "to act," suggesting the importance of action.
    • āre: This term can imply "to protect" or "to guard," indicating a sense of safety or security.
    • asmat: This means "us" or "our," indicating a collective identity or community.
    • aśrīra: This can be interpreted as "without form" or "formless," suggesting a spiritual or abstract concept.
    • iva: This means "like" or "as," used for comparison.
    • jāmātā: This term can refer to "the one who is related by marriage," indicating familial or social connections.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • The mantra speaks to the personal connection one has with the divine or the universe, emphasizing the importance of being present in the moment.
    • It acknowledges the challenges one faces in life, represented by the term "durhaṇāvān," suggesting that these challenges are part of the journey.
    • The reference to "evening" signifies a time for introspection and contemplation, a moment to reflect on the day's actions and their significance.
    • By stating "to protect us," the mantra highlights the need for safety and support from the divine or from our community.
    • The mention of "formless" suggests a connection to the spiritual realm, where the essence of existence transcends physical form.
    • Finally, the reference to "jāmātā" emphasizes the importance of relationships and community, reminding us that we are not alone in our struggles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, one might face challenges at work or in personal relationships. This mantra encourages individuals to acknowledge these difficulties while remaining grounded in the present.
    • During times of stress, such as during exams or important life decisions, taking a moment to reflect in the evening can provide clarity and peace.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can serve as a reminder of the importance of supporting one another, especially during tough times.
    • In spiritual practices, the concept of formlessness can be related to meditation, where one seeks to connect with a higher state of consciousness beyond the physical realm.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of facing challenges, and the need for community support.
    • It encourages individuals to be present, to reflect on their actions, and to seek protection and guidance from the divine.
    • In essence, it highlights the significance of relationships and the spiritual journey, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to community building.
