यम् इन्द्र दधिषे त्वम् अश्वं गाम भागम् अव्ययम् |यजमानै सून्वति दक्षिणावति तस्मिन् तं धेहि मा पणौ ||
yam indra dadhiṣe tvam aśvaṁ gām bhāgam avyayam | yajamāne sunvati dakṣiṇāvati tasmin taṁ dhehi mā paṇau ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yam: who
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • dadhiṣe: you bestow
    • tvam: you
    • aśvaṁ: the horse
    • gām: the cow
    • bhāgam: the share or portion
    • avyayam: imperishable or eternal
    • yajamāne: while being worshipped
    • sunvati: may it prosper
    • dakṣiṇāvati: may it be favorable
    • tasmin: in that
    • taṁ: that
    • dhehi: grant or bestow
    • mā: do not
    • paṇau: in the trade or business
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Indra, asking him to bestow blessings in the form of wealth and prosperity, symbolized by the horse and cow. It emphasizes the importance of divine favor in achieving success in endeavors, particularly in business and trade.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for favorable weather and good harvests, similar to how this mantra seeks divine blessings for prosperity.
    • Business owners may recite this mantra during rituals to invoke success and stability in their ventures, hoping for Indra's blessings to ensure their efforts yield fruitful results.
    • During festivals, people may perform rituals that echo the sentiments of this mantra, seeking abundance and prosperity in their lives.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine support for prosperity and success. It highlights the belief that spiritual blessings are essential for achieving material goals. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as business, agriculture, and personal endeavors, where individuals seek to align their efforts with divine will for favorable outcomes. By recognizing the interplay between spiritual and material success, individuals can cultivate a mindset that values both hard work and divine grace.
