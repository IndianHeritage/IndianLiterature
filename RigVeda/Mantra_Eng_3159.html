सद्योजातस्य ददृशानम् ओजो यदस्य वातो अनुवाति शोचिः |वृणक्ति तिग्माम् अतसेषु जिह्वां स्थिरा चिदन्ना दयते वि जम्बैः ||
sadyo jātasya dadṛśānam ojo yad asya vāto anuvāti śociḥ | vṛṇakti tigmām ataseṣu jihvāṁ sthirā cid annā dayate vi jambhaiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sadyo: immediately, suddenly
    • jātasya: of the born, of the created
    • dadṛśānam: of the seer, of the observer
    • ojo: energy, strength, vigor
    • yad: that which
    • asya: of this, of it
    • vāto: the wind, the air
    • anuvāti: blows, moves along
    • śociḥ: shining, illuminating
    • vṛṇakti: chooses, selects
    • tigmām: sharp, intense
    • ataseṣu: in the plants, in the herbs
    • jihvāṁ: tongue
    • sthirā: stable, firm
    • cid: consciousness, awareness
    • annā: food, nourishment
    • dayate: gives, provides
    • vi: with, by
    • jambhaiḥ: with the fruits, with the results
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the immediate emergence of energy and strength from creation.
    • It highlights the role of the wind (vāto) as a vital force that moves and influences life.
    • The shining aspect (śociḥ) signifies enlightenment and clarity that comes from this energy.
    • The selection of sharpness (tigmām) in plants indicates the importance of choosing the right nourishment for health.
    • The stable tongue (jihvāṁ sthirā) symbolizes the need for firm communication and expression of thoughts.
    • Ultimately, it emphasizes the connection between nourishment (annā) and the fruits of our actions (jambhaiḥ).
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a practical sense, this mantra can be related to the importance of making conscious choices in our diet, selecting foods that provide energy and vitality.
    • In communication, it reminds us to express ourselves clearly and firmly, ensuring our thoughts are conveyed effectively.
    • The influence of the wind can be seen in how ideas and innovations spread, much like how fresh air revitalizes our environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of energy, nourishment, and communication in our lives.
    • It teaches us to be mindful of our choices, whether in food, expression, or the ideas we embrace.
    • By understanding and applying these principles, we can enhance our well-being and contribute positively to our surroundings.
