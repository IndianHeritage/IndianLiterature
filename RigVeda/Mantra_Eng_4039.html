देवानाम् पत्नीरुशतीर् अवन्तु नः प्रावन्तु नस् तुजये वाजसातये |याः पार्थिवासो याः अपाम् अपि व्रते ता नो देविः सुहवाः शर्म यच्छता ||
devānām patnīr uśatīr avantu naḥ prāvantu nas tujaye vājasātaye | yāḥ pārthivāso yā apām api vrate tā no devīḥ suhavāḥ śarma yacchata ||
  • Translation: "May the wives of the gods, the auspicious ones, protect us; may they bring us strength and victory. Those who are earthly and those who are in the waters, may those goddesses grant us good fortune and happiness."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • devānām: of the gods
    • patnīr: wives or consorts
    • uśatīr: auspicious or beneficial
    • avant: protect or guard
    • naḥ: us
    • prāvantu: may they bring
    • tujaye: strength or victory
    • vājasātaye: for the purpose of success
    • yāḥ: those who
    • pārthivāso: earthly beings
    • yā: those who
    • apām: of the waters
    • api: also
    • vrate: in the vow or commitment
    • tā: those
    • no: to us
    • devīḥ: goddesses
    • suhavāḥ: good fortune or auspiciousness
    • śarma: happiness or comfort
    • yacchata: grant or bestow
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of divine feminine energy, represented by the wives of the gods, to provide protection and strength.
    • In real life, this can be seen as a call for support from nurturing figures, such as mothers, partners, or mentors, who play a crucial role in our success and well-being.
    • The mention of "earthly beings" and "those in the waters" symbolizes the interconnectedness of all life forms and the importance of harmony with nature.
    • For example, in a community setting, this mantra can be applied when seeking collective strength and support during challenging times, such as natural disasters or social upheavals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine support and the role of nurturing energies in achieving success and happiness.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek and appreciate the guidance and protection from those around us, whether they are family, friends, or spiritual entities.
    • In daily life, one can recite this mantra during moments of uncertainty or when embarking on new ventures, invoking the blessings of the divine for strength and auspiciousness.
