ये ते सन्ति दशग्विनः शतिनो ये सहस्रिणः |अश्वासो ये ते वृषाणो रघुद्रुवस तेषां नस्तूयम् आगहि ||
ye te santi daśagvinaḥ śatino ye sahasriṇaḥ | aśvāso ye te vṛṣaṇo raghudruvas tebhir nas tūyam ā gahi ||
  • ye te santi daśagvinaḥ: This phrase can be translated to "those who are tenfold strong." The term "daśagvinaḥ" refers to a group or individuals who possess immense strength or power, symbolizing resilience and capability.
  • śatino ye sahasriṇaḥ: Here, "śatino" means "those who are strong" and "sahasriṇaḥ" refers to "those who are a thousandfold." This part emphasizes the idea of strength multiplied, suggesting that true power comes from unity and collective strength.
  • aśvāso ye te vṛṣaṇo: "aśvāso" translates to "those who are like horses," indicating speed, agility, and vitality. "vṛṣaṇo" refers to "the powerful" or "the mighty." This line highlights the importance of being dynamic and vigorous in one's pursuits.
  • raghudruvas tebhir nas tūyam ā gahi: "raghudruvas" can be interpreted as "those who are steadfast and unwavering." "tebhir nas" means "with these," and "tūyam ā gahi" translates to "may you hold us." This concluding line is a plea for support and guidance from these powerful entities.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, unity, and resilience in overcoming challenges. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in a corporate environment, where collective effort leads to success, or in personal relationships where mutual support is crucial. The mantra serves as a reminder that true power lies not just in individual strength but in the ability to come together and support one another, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
