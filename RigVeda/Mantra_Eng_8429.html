प्र त आषवः पवमान धीजवो मदाऽर्षन्ति रघुजा इव त्मना |दिव्याः सुपर्णा मधुमन्त इन्दवो मदिन्तमासः परि कोशम् आसते ||
pra ta āśavaḥ pavamāna dhījavo madā arṣanti raghujā iva tmanā | divyāḥ suparṇā madhumanta indavo madintamāsaḥ pari kośam āsate ||
  • pra ta āśavaḥ pavamāna: This phrase can be interpreted as "O swift-moving (or purifying) ones, who are full of energy." Here, "āśavaḥ" refers to the vital energies or life forces, while "pavamāna" signifies the purifying aspect of these energies.
  • dhījavo madā arṣanti: This translates to "those who are wise and full of inspiration." "Dhījavo" refers to the wise or intelligent beings, and "madā" indicates a state of bliss or intoxication, often associated with divine knowledge or enlightenment.
  • raghujā iva tmanā: This means "like the descendants of Raghu." The Raghu dynasty is known for its valor and righteousness, particularly represented by Lord Rama. This line suggests that the wise and inspired beings possess qualities akin to those of noble lineage.
  • divyāḥ suparṇā madhumanta indavo: This phrase can be understood as "the divine, beautiful beings who are sweet like honey." "Suparṇā" refers to those who are well-formed or beautiful, and "madhumanta" signifies sweetness, often associated with divine qualities.
  • madintamāsaḥ pari kośam āsate: This translates to "those who dwell in the highest realms." "Madintamāsaḥ" refers to the most exalted or divine beings, and "pari kośam" indicates the idea of encompassing or surrounding, suggesting a protective or nurturing presence.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine energies and wisdom in our lives. It highlights the qualities of purity, inspiration, and beauty that we should aspire to embody. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal development, where individuals strive to cultivate wisdom and positive qualities akin to those of noble figures. It encourages us to seek enlightenment and to surround ourselves with uplifting energies, much like the divine beings described in the mantra. By embodying these qualities, we can contribute to a more harmonious and enlightened society.
