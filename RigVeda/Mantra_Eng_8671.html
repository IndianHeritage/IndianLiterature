परी कोषम् मधुश्चुतम् अव्यये वारे अर्षति |अभि वाणीर ऋषीणां सप्त नूषत ||
pari kośam madhuścutam avyaye vāre arṣati | abhi vāṇīr ṛṣīṇāṁ sapta nūṣata ||
  • Mantra: "pari kośam madhuścutam avyaye vāre arṣati | abhi vāṇīr ṛṣīṇāṁ sapta nūṣata ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pari: around, encompassing
    • kośam: treasure, wealth
    • madhuścutam: sweet, nectar-like
    • avyaye: imperishable, eternal
    • vāre: in the waters, in the flow
    • arṣati: shines, radiates
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • vāṇīr: speech, voice
    • ṛṣīṇāṁ: of the sages, seers
    • sapta: seven
    • nūṣata: to be praised, to be honored
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a treasure that is sweet and eternal, suggesting that true wealth is not merely material but also spiritual and intellectual.
    • The phrase "around the treasure" indicates that this wealth is accessible and envelops us, much like the knowledge and wisdom that surround us in our daily lives.
    • The reference to "the waters" symbolizes the flow of life and knowledge, suggesting that wisdom is abundant and can be drawn upon at any time.
    • The "voice of the sages" implies that the teachings and insights of wise individuals are invaluable and should be sought after and honored.
    • The number "seven" often represents completeness and perfection in various cultures, indicating that the wisdom of the sages is comprehensive and multifaceted.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, the mantra can be applied to the pursuit of knowledge and skills. Just as one seeks to enhance their career, they should also seek the wisdom of mentors and experts, recognizing that their insights are a form of treasure.
    • In personal development, the idea of "sweet nectar" can be related to the joy and fulfillment that comes from learning and self-improvement, emphasizing that true happiness often lies in the journey of growth rather than in material possessions.
    • In community and relationships, the mantra encourages individuals to honor and respect the voices of elders and experienced individuals, understanding that their life lessons are invaluable treasures that can guide younger generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking and valuing wisdom, both from within and from external sources. It teaches us that true wealth is found in knowledge, understanding, and the relationships we build with others.
    • In real life, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to a more fulfilling and enriched existence, where individuals prioritize learning and the sharing of wisdom over material gains.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that the pursuit of knowledge and the honoring of wise voices are essential for personal and communal growth.
