पुनानः कलशेष्वा वस्त्राण्य अरुषो हरिः |परि गव्याण्य अव्यत ||
punānaḥ kalaśeṣv ā vastrāṇy aruṣo hariḥ | pari gavyāny avyata ||
  • punānaḥ: This word translates to "purifying" or "cleansing." It signifies the act of making something pure or free from impurities.
  • kalaśeṣv: Refers to "in the vessels" or "in the pots." In a broader sense, it can symbolize the containers of life, such as our bodies or the environment around us.
  • ā: A preposition that can mean "towards" or "in the direction of." It indicates movement or intention.
  • vastrāṇy: This means "clothes" or "garments." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent the outer layers of our identity or the roles we play in society.
  • aruṣo: Translates to "red" or "brilliant." This color often symbolizes vitality, energy, and life force.
  • hariḥ: This word is often associated with the divine, particularly with Lord Vishnu, who is known as the preserver and protector in Hindu mythology.
  • pari: Means "around" or "surrounding." It indicates a sense of completeness or wholeness.
  • gavyāny: Refers to "the cows" or "the cattle." In Vedic culture, cows are considered sacred and symbolize wealth, nourishment, and abundance.
  • avyata: This can be interpreted as "not disturbed" or "not affected." It suggests a state of stability and peace.

The mantra can be understood as a call for purification and protection. It emphasizes the importance of cleansing our inner and outer selves, represented by the vessels and garments, while invoking the divine presence of Hari (Vishnu) to surround us with abundance and stability.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as we cleanse our physical bodies, we should also strive to purify our thoughts and intentions. This can be achieved through practices like meditation, yoga, or self-reflection.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to vessels and the need for purity can extend to our environment. We must take care of our surroundings, ensuring that we maintain a clean and sustainable habitat.
  • Community and Relationships: The idea of garments can symbolize the roles we play in our communities. By purifying our interactions and fostering positive relationships, we contribute to a harmonious society.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of purification in all aspects of life—physical, mental, and spiritual. It encourages us to seek the divine presence in our lives, ensuring that we are surrounded by abundance and stability. By applying its teachings, we can lead more fulfilling and harmonious lives.
