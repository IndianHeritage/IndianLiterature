प्र शंसā गोष्व अग्न्यं क्रीळं यच छर्दो मारुतम् |जम्भे रसस्य वावृधे ||
pra śaṁsā goṣv aghnyaṁ krīḻaṁ yac chardho mārutam | jambhe rasasya vāvṛdhe ||
  • pra śaṁsā: This term can be translated as "praise" or "glorification." It signifies the act of acknowledging and honoring something or someone.
  • goṣv: This word refers to "cattle" or "cows," which are often seen as symbols of wealth and prosperity in Vedic literature.
  • aghnyaṁ: This means "not to be killed" or "sacred." In the context of the mantra, it emphasizes the sanctity of cows and their importance in Vedic culture.
  • krīḻaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "play" or "to engage in activities." It suggests a sense of joy and liveliness associated with the subject being praised.
  • yac: This word translates to "that which" or "whatever." It introduces a clause that elaborates on the subject of praise.
  • chardho: This term refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." It indicates the abundance that comes from the blessings of the divine.
  • mārutam: This word is associated with the "winds" or "air," often personified as deities in Vedic texts. It signifies the dynamic and life-giving forces of nature.
  • jambhe: This term can be interpreted as "to be born" or "to arise." It suggests the emergence of something significant.
  • rasasya: This means "of essence" or "of flavor." It indicates the richness and depth of experience that comes from the subject being discussed.
  • vāvṛdhe: This term translates to "to grow" or "to increase." It signifies the expansion and flourishing of the qualities being praised.

In summary, this mantra praises the sacredness of cattle, particularly cows, and acknowledges their role in bringing prosperity and joy. It highlights the importance of respecting and nurturing these animals, which are integral to agricultural societies and symbolize wealth and sustenance.


The significance of this mantra lies in its call to honor nature and the resources it provides. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways:

  • Promoting sustainable farming practices that respect animal welfare and the environment.
  • Encouraging communities to value and protect livestock as vital contributors to their livelihoods.
  • Fostering a sense of gratitude towards nature and its gifts, leading to a more harmonious existence.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of reverence for nature, and the blessings that come from living in harmony with the world around us.
