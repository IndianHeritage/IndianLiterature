मन्त्र: न हि ते क्षत्रं न सहो न मन्युः वयश्चनामी पतयन्त आपुः |नेमा आपो अनिमिषं चरन्तीर न ये वातस्य प्रमिनन्त्य अभ्वम् ||
nahi te kṣatraṁ na saho na manyuṁ vayaś canāmī patayanta āpuḥ | nemā āpo animiṣaṁ carantīr na ye vātasya praminanty abhvam ||
  • Translation: "There is no power, no strength, no anger, and no fear that can overcome those who are united. The waters flow without hesitation, and they do not get disturbed by the winds."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • nahi: not
    • te: your
    • kṣatraṁ: power or strength
    • na: no
    • saho: strength
    • na: no
    • manyuṁ: anger
    • vayaś: fear
    • canāmī: those who are united
    • patayanta: who are flowing or moving
    • āpuḥ: waters
    • nemā: they do not
    • āpo: waters
    • animiṣaṁ: without hesitation
    • carantīr: flowing
    • na: not
    • ye: those who
    • vātasya: of the wind
    • praminanty: disturb
    • abhvam: the flow or movement
  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the strength found in unity. It suggests that when individuals come together, they can overcome any challenges, including power, anger, and fear.
    • The metaphor of water illustrates how unity allows for a smooth and uninterrupted flow, akin to how water moves freely without being disturbed by external forces like wind.
    • In real life, this can be seen in various scenarios such as teamwork in a corporate environment, where collaboration leads to success, or in social movements where collective action brings about change.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the power of unity and collaboration. It teaches that when individuals work together, they can achieve greater things and navigate through challenges without being swayed by negativity or external pressures.
    • This principle is applicable in everyday life, whether in personal relationships, community efforts, or professional settings, highlighting the importance of solidarity and collective strength.
