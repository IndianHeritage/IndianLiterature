एकं च यो विंशतिं च श्रवस्याऽ वैकर्णयोः जनान राजा न्य अस्तः |दस्मो न सद्मन् निःशिशाति बर्हिः शूरः सर्गं अकृणोद इन्द्र एषाम् ||
ekaṁ ca yo viṁśatiṁ ca śravasyā vaikarṇayor janān rājā ny astaḥ | dasmo na sadman ni śiśāti barhiḥ śūraḥ sargam akṛṇod indra eṣām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ekaṁ - one
    • ca - and
    • yo - who
    • viṁśatiṁ - twenty
    • ca - and
    • śravasyā - of the famous
    • vaikarṇayor - of the two (Vaikarṇa)
    • janān - people
    • rājā - king
    • ny astaḥ - does not fall
    • dasmo - the tenth
    • na - not
    • sadman - in the abode
    • ni - in
    • śiśāti - resides
    • barhiḥ - the sacred grass
    • śūraḥ - the brave
    • sargam - creation
    • akṛṇod - created
    • indra - Indra (the king of gods)
    • eṣām - of these
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the unity and strength of a king who does not fall from his position.
    • The reference to "one and twenty" suggests a balance between singularity and multiplicity, indicating that a true leader embodies both individual strength and collective wisdom.
    • The mention of "famous" and "brave" highlights the qualities that are essential for leadership, emphasizing that a king must be both respected and courageous.
    • The phrase "does not fall" signifies stability and resilience, which are crucial traits for anyone in a position of authority.
    • The "sacred grass" (barhiḥ) symbolizes purity and the foundation of life, suggesting that a leader must be grounded in values and ethics.
    • Indra, as the creator, represents the divine aspect of leadership, indicating that true authority comes from a higher moral ground.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern governance, leaders who embody the qualities of integrity, bravery, and wisdom are often the most successful. For instance, leaders like Nelson Mandela and Mahatma Gandhi are revered for their moral courage and ability to unite people.
    • In the corporate world, CEOs who prioritize ethical practices and foster a culture of respect and collaboration tend to lead their companies to greater success, much like the king described in the mantra.
    • In personal life, individuals who take responsibility for their actions and lead by example inspire those around them, creating a positive impact in their communities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of strong, ethical leadership that is both individual and collective in nature.
    • It serves as a reminder that true authority is not just about power but also about responsibility, integrity, and the ability to inspire others.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to better leadership in various spheres, including politics, business, and personal relationships, ultimately contributing to a more harmonious society.
