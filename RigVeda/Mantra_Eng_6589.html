अक्ष्णश्चिद्गातुवित्तरानुल्बणेन चक्षसा |निचिन्तिमिषन्ता निचिरा न चिकीतुः ||
akṣṇaś cid gātuvittarānulbaṇena cakṣasā | ni cin miṣantā nicirā ni cikyatuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • akṣṇaś: Not blind, having vision or insight.
    • cid: Consciousness or awareness.
    • gātuvittarānulbaṇena: By the power of the one who knows the truth or the seer.
    • cakṣasā: With the eye or vision.
    • ni: Down or below.
    • cin: To perceive or to know.
    • miṣantā: Those who are engaged in the act of perception.
    • nicirā: For a long time.
    • ni: Down or below.
    • cikyatuḥ: They may attain or achieve.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of having insight and awareness in our lives.
    • It emphasizes that true perception comes from a deeper understanding of reality, guided by those who possess knowledge.
    • The phrase "not blind" suggests that one should strive to see beyond the superficial and grasp the essence of things.
    • It implies that through sustained effort and awareness, individuals can achieve a higher state of understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, a student who actively engages with the material and seeks deeper understanding, rather than rote memorization, exemplifies the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal relationships, someone who listens empathetically and seeks to understand the feelings and perspectives of others embodies the vision described in the mantra.
    • In professional settings, leaders who are aware of their team's dynamics and foster an environment of open communication reflect the insights of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for awareness and insight in all aspects of life.
    • It encourages individuals to look beyond the surface and engage with the deeper truths of existence.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as education, relationships, and leadership, where understanding and awareness lead to better outcomes.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that true vision and perception are essential for personal growth and harmonious living.
