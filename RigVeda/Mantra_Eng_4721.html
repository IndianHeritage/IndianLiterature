दूराच्चिदावसतोऽस्य कर्णा घोषाद इन्द्रस्य तण्यति ब्रुवाणः |एयं एनं देवहूतिरववृत्यान मद्र्यं इन्द्रं इयं ऋच्यमाना ||
dūrāc cid ā vasato asya karṇā ghoṣād indrasya tanyati bruvāṇaḥ | eyam enaṁ devahūtir vavṛtyān madrya1g indram iyam ṛcyamānā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dūrāc: From afar
    • cid: Indeed
    • ā: To
    • vasato: Those who dwell
    • asya: Of this
    • karṇā: Ears
    • ghoṣād: From the sound
    • indrasya: Of Indra
    • tanyati: Is stretched
    • bruvāṇaḥ: Speaking
    • eyam: This
    • enaṁ: Him
    • devahūtir: Divine invocation
    • vavṛtyān: Has been made manifest
    • madrya: My delight
    • indram: Indra
    • iyam: This
    • ṛcyamānā: Is being recited
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine presence of Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, and emphasizes the importance of invoking him from a distance.
    • The phrase "dūrāc cid ā vasato asya karṇā" suggests that even from afar, the ears of Indra are attentive to the sounds of devotion and prayer.
    • The invocation "devahūtir" indicates a call to the divine, highlighting the significance of spiritual communication and the power of words in connecting with higher realms.
    • In real life, this can be likened to the way people often seek guidance or support from mentors or leaders, even if they are not physically present. For example, a student may feel inspired by a teacher's teachings long after the class has ended.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra underscores the belief that divine forces are always listening and responsive to sincere prayers and invocations, regardless of physical distance.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to maintain a connection with their spiritual beliefs and practices, reinforcing the idea that faith can transcend physical barriers.
    • In modern contexts, this can be seen in practices such as meditation, prayer, or even mindfulness, where individuals seek to connect with a higher power or their inner selves, often feeling a sense of peace and guidance.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of intention and communication in our spiritual lives, encouraging us to express our thoughts and desires openly, knowing that they are heard.
