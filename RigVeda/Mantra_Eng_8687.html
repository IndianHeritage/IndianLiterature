इन्द्रम् अच््छ सुताः इमे वृषणं यन्तु हरयः |श्रुष्टिः जातास इन्दवः स्वरविदः ||
indram accha sutā ime vṛṣaṇaṁ yantu harayaḥ | śruṣṭī jātāsa indavaḥ svarvidaḥ ||
  • Indram: This refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunder, and war.
  • Accha: This word can be interpreted as "good" or "excellent," indicating a positive quality or state.
  • Sutā: This means "sons" or "offspring," often referring to the progeny of a deity or a lineage.
  • Ime: This translates to "these," pointing to the subjects or entities being referred to in the mantra.
  • Vṛṣaṇaṁ: This term can mean "strength" or "power," often associated with the might of Indra.
  • Yantu: This means "may they go" or "may they proceed," suggesting a movement or action towards a goal.
  • Harayaḥ: This refers to the "horses" or "swift beings," often symbolizing speed, energy, and vitality.
  • Śruṣṭī: This word means "creation" or "manifestation," indicating the act of bringing forth or producing.
  • Jātāsa: This translates to "born" or "arisen," referring to something that has come into existence.
  • Indavaḥ: This term can mean "the moon" or "the shining ones," often symbolizing illumination and guidance.
  • Svarvidaḥ: This means "knowledge of the heavens" or "celestial knowledge," indicating a higher understanding or wisdom.

The mantra essentially invokes the blessings of Indra and his offspring, asking for strength and vitality to proceed towards creation and enlightenment. It emphasizes the importance of divine guidance and the power of creation in achieving one's goals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Personal Growth: Just as Indra represents strength and leadership, individuals can seek to embody these qualities in their personal and professional lives.
  • Community Development: The idea of creation and manifestation can inspire community leaders to work towards building a better society, drawing on the strength of their collective efforts.
  • Spiritual Enlightenment: The reference to celestial knowledge encourages individuals to seek wisdom and understanding beyond the material world, fostering a deeper connection with the universe.

Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of divine strength, the power of creation, and the pursuit of knowledge. It serves as a reminder that with the right guidance and inner strength, individuals can manifest their desires and contribute positively to the world around them.
