यो अप्स्वा शुचिनā दैवेन ऋतावाजस्र उर्वियā विभाति |वयā इद् अन्यā भुवनान्य अस्य प्र जायतें वीरुधश च प्रजाभिः ||
yo apsv ā śucinā daivyena ṛtāvājasra urviyā vibhāti | vayā id anyā bhuvanāny asya pra jāyante vīrudhaś ca prajābhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • apsv: in the waters
    • ā: from
    • śucinā: pure
    • daivyena: divine
    • ṛtāvājasra: with the power of truth
    • urviyā: vast
    • vibhāti: shines
    • vayā: we
    • id: indeed
    • anyā: other
    • bhuvanāny: worlds
    • asya: of this
    • pra jāyante: are born
    • vīrudhaś: flourishing
    • ca: and
    • prajābhiḥ: by the progeny
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence that shines forth from the pure waters, symbolizing purity and truth.
    • The phrase "ṛtāvājasra" emphasizes the power of truth, suggesting that truth is a guiding force in the universe.
    • The mention of "vayā id anyā bhuvanāny asya" indicates that from this divine source, various worlds and beings are born, highlighting the interconnectedness of all life.
    • In real life, this can be likened to the way water nourishes life on Earth, providing sustenance and purity, much like the divine essence described in the mantra.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of purity, truth, and the divine connection that sustains life.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek truth and purity in our actions and thoughts, reflecting the divine qualities that lead to flourishing and growth.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various aspects of life, such as in personal relationships, environmental stewardship, and ethical decision-making.
    • By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and flourishing world.
