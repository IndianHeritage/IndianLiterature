आर्यो वा गिरो अभ्य आर्च विद्वान ऋषीणां विप्रः सुमतिं चकारनः |ते स्याम ये रणयन्त सोमैर एनोत तुभ्यं रथोऽल्ह भक्षैः ||
aryo vā giro abhy arca vidvān ṛṣīṇāṁ vipraḥ sumatiṁ cakānaḥ | te syāma ye raṇayanta somair enota tubhyaṁ rathoḻha bhakṣaiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aryo: noble, honorable
    • vā: or
    • giro: words, speech
    • abhy: towards
    • arca: worship, praise
    • vidvān: the wise, knowledgeable
    • ṛṣīṇāṁ: of the sages, seers
    • vipraḥ: the learned, the wise
    • sumatiṁ: good thought, wisdom
    • cakānaḥ: having created, having made
    • te: they
    • syāma: may we be
    • ye: who
    • raṇayanta: who engage in battle
    • somair: with soma (sacred drink)
    • enota: may they bring
    • tubhyaṁ: to you
    • rathoḻha: great chariot
    • bhakṣaiḥ: with offerings, sacrifices
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of noble speech and wisdom.
    • It calls upon the learned and wise to create good thoughts and actions.
    • The reference to engaging in battle signifies the struggles and challenges in life.
    • The mention of soma suggests the importance of spiritual nourishment and offerings.
    • Overall, it is a prayer for guidance and support in overcoming life's challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a team that communicates honorably and wisely can overcome challenges more effectively.
    • In personal relationships, fostering good thoughts and open communication can lead to stronger bonds.
    • During difficult times, seeking spiritual guidance or engaging in practices that nourish the soul can provide strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of noble thoughts and words in our lives.
    • It highlights the significance of wisdom in navigating challenges and the importance of spiritual nourishment.
    • In essence, it encourages us to strive for goodness and wisdom in our actions and interactions.
