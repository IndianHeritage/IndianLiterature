अदब्देभिष तव गोपाभिर इष्टेऽस्माकं पाहि त्रिषधस्थ सूरिन् |रक्षा च नो ददुषां शर्धो अग्ने वैश्वानर प्र च तारीः स्तवाणः ||
adabdhebhis tava gopābhir iṣṭe 'smākam pāhi triṣadhastha sūrīn | rakṣā ca no daduṣāṁ śardho agne vaiśvānara pra ca tārīḥ stavānaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "adabdhebhis tava gopābhir iṣṭe 'smākam pāhi triṣadhastha sūrīn | rakṣā ca no daduṣāṁ śardho agne vaiśvānara pra ca tārīḥ stavānaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "O Agni, the universal fire, may you protect us with your divine energy, as we invoke you with our offerings. May you safeguard our well-being and grant us strength, as we call upon you, the all-pervading light."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • Adabdhebhis: Refers to the offerings or invocations made with sincerity.
    • Tava: Your, indicating a connection to the divine.
    • Gopābhir: With the guardians or protectors, symbolizing the divine forces that aid us.
    • Iṣṭe: Invoked or offered, highlighting the act of worship.
    • ‘Smākam: Us, indicating a collective appeal for protection.
    • Pāhi: Protect, a plea for safeguarding.
    • Triṣadhastha: Those who are in the three realms (heaven, earth, and the underworld), representing the comprehensive nature of divine protection.
    • Sūrīn: The strong or powerful beings, often referring to deities or divine forces.
    • Rakṣā: Protection, emphasizing the need for safety and security.
    • Ca: And, connecting the plea for protection with the next invocation.
    • No: Our, indicating a personal connection to the divine.
    • Daduṣāṁ: Grant, a request for bestowal of blessings.
    • Śardho: Strength or vigor, highlighting the importance of resilience.
    • Agne: O Fire, a direct address to Agni, the fire god.
    • Vaiśvānara: The universal fire, symbolizing the all-encompassing nature of divinity.
    • Pra: Forth, indicating a call to action.
    • Ca: And, again connecting thoughts.
    • Tārīḥ: Light or illumination, representing knowledge and enlightenment.
    • Stavānaḥ: Praise, indicating the act of worship and reverence.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of uncertainty, such as during a pandemic, people often turn to spiritual practices for comfort and protection, similar to invoking Agni for safety.
    • Communities come together in rituals, offering prayers for collective well-being, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on communal protection.
    • In personal challenges, individuals may seek strength through meditation or prayer, paralleling the plea for divine support in the mantra.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine protection and strength in our lives. It serves as a reminder that in times of need, we can seek the support of higher powers through sincere offerings and collective prayers. The invocation of Agni symbolizes the light of knowledge and the warmth of community, which are essential for overcoming challenges. This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal struggles to communal crises, reinforcing the belief that we are never alone in our journey and that divine forces are always ready to assist us.
