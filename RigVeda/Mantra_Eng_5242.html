समिधा जातवेदसे देवाय देवहूतिभिः |हविर्भिः शुक्रशोचिषे नमस्विनो वयं दाशेमाग्नये ||
samidhā jātavedase devāya devahūtibhiḥ | havirbhiḥ śukraśociṣe namasvino vayaṁ dāśemāgnaye ||
  • samidhā: This word refers to the act of offering or igniting. In a ritual context, it signifies the initiation of a sacred fire.
  • jātavedase: This term is often translated as "the one who knows all births" or "the fire that knows." It represents Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
  • devāya: This means "to the divine" or "to the gods." It emphasizes the reverence and devotion towards the divine entities.
  • devahūtibhiḥ: This can be interpreted as "with the offerings of the gods." It signifies the importance of offerings in rituals to please the deities.
  • havirbhiḥ: This word refers to the offerings made to the fire, particularly in the form of ghee or other sacred substances.
  • śukraśociṣe: This translates to "radiant and pure." It describes the nature of the offerings and the fire, highlighting their purity and brightness.
  • namasvino: This means "to those who are worthy of reverence." It indicates respect and honor towards the divine forces.
  • vayaṁ: This translates to "we" or "us," indicating a collective participation in the ritual.
  • dāśemāgnaye: This means "we offer to Agni." It is a direct invocation to the fire god, expressing the intention of the offerings.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation to Agni, the fire god, emphasizing the importance of offerings and the purity of intentions in rituals. It reflects a deep respect for the divine and the belief that fire serves as a bridge between the material and spiritual worlds.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: During religious ceremonies, the act of lighting a fire and making offerings can be seen as a way to connect with the divine, similar to how people today might light candles or incense during prayers.
  • Symbol of Purity: The emphasis on purity and radiance can be reflected in personal practices, such as maintaining a clean and sacred space for meditation or reflection.
  • Community Gatherings: The collective aspect of the mantra ("we offer to Agni") can be mirrored in community events where people come together to celebrate, share, and honor their traditions.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of reverence, purity, and community in spiritual practices. It serves as a reminder of the connection between the material and spiritual realms, encouraging individuals to engage in rituals that honor the divine and foster a sense of unity among participants.
