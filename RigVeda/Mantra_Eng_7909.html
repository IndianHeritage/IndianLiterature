एष नृभिर विनीयते दिवो मूर्धा वृषा सुतः |सोमो वनेषु विश्ववित ||
eṣa nṛbhir vi nīyate divo mūrdhā vṛṣā sutaḥ | somo vaneṣu viśvavit ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣa: this
    • nṛbhir: by men
    • vi nīyate: is led or guided
    • divo: of the sky or heaven
    • mūrdhā: the head or summit
    • vṛṣā: the bull or strong one
    • sutaḥ: the son or offspring
    • somo: the moon or soma (a sacred drink)
    • vaneṣu: in the forests
    • viśvavit: all-knowing or omniscient
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine force or energy that is guided by human actions.
    • The reference to "the head of the sky" suggests a connection to higher consciousness or divine wisdom.
    • The term "vṛṣā" symbolizes strength and power, indicating that this divine energy is potent and influential.
    • "Soma" represents not just the moon but also the sacred drink that symbolizes immortality and divine inspiration.
    • The mention of "forests" indicates a connection to nature, suggesting that this divine energy is present in the natural world.
    • "Viśvavit" emphasizes the omniscience of this divine force, suggesting that it encompasses all knowledge and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine. For instance, when people come together for a common cause, such as environmental conservation, they channel a collective energy that can lead to significant change.
    • The idea of being guided by a higher power can be reflected in personal experiences where individuals feel a sense of purpose or direction in their lives, often attributed to spiritual beliefs or practices.
    • The strength symbolized by "vṛṣā" can be observed in community efforts where collective strength leads to overcoming challenges, such as during natural disasters when communities unite to support each other.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine guidance in human actions and the power of collective strength.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions can lead to greater understanding and connection with the divine, especially when we are in harmony with nature.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to seek higher wisdom and work together towards common goals, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
