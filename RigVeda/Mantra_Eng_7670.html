अग्नि घृतस्य धीतिभिः तेपानो देव शोचिषा |आ देवान वक्षि यक्षि च ||
agne ghṛtasya dhītibhis tepāno deva śociṣā | ā devān vakṣi yakṣi ca ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying and transformative force.
  • Ghṛtasya: This translates to "of ghee" or "of clarified butter." Ghee is considered sacred and is used in various rituals and offerings.
  • Dhītibhis: This means "with wisdom" or "with understanding." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and intellect in our actions.
  • Tepāno: This word means "radiating" or "shining." It signifies the brilliance and energy associated with Agni.
  • Deva: This translates to "god" or "divine being." It represents the higher powers and spiritual entities in the universe.
  • Śociṣā: This means "with brightness" or "with light." It highlights the illuminating aspect of divine presence.
  • Ā: This is a preposition that can mean "to" or "towards," indicating direction or intention.
  • Devān: This refers to "the gods" or "divine beings," emphasizing the connection to the divine realm.
  • Vakṣi: This means "you will speak" or "you will declare." It indicates communication and expression.
  • Yakṣi: This translates to "you will protect" or "you will guard." It signifies the role of the divine in safeguarding and nurturing.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to Agni, asking for wisdom and divine guidance. It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and the transformative power of fire, both literally and metaphorically. In real life, this can be seen in various contexts:


  • Personal Growth: Just as fire transforms materials, individuals can undergo personal transformation through knowledge and wisdom. For example, someone who seeks education and self-improvement embodies the essence of this mantra.
  • Spiritual Practices: In rituals, ghee is often offered to Agni, symbolizing the surrender of the self to the divine. This practice can be seen in various cultures where offerings are made to seek blessings and guidance.
  • Community and Leadership: Leaders who act with wisdom and protect their communities reflect the qualities of Agni as described in the mantra. They radiate positivity and guidance, much like the divine light mentioned.

Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine wisdom and guidance in our lives. It teaches us to embrace knowledge, protect what is sacred, and radiate positivity. In a world filled with challenges, invoking the qualities of Agni can inspire individuals to transform themselves and their communities through wisdom and divine connection.
