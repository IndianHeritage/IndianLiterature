अश्विनाऽ स्व ऋषे स्तुहि कुवित ते श्रवतो हवम् |नेदीयसः कूळयातः पाणींर उता ||
aśvinā sv ṛṣe stuhi kuvit te śravato havam | nedīyasaḥ kūḻayātaḥ paṇīm̐r uta ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • aśvinā: Refers to the Aśvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology associated with health, medicine, and the dawn.
    • sv: Means "your" or "of you," indicating a personal connection.
    • ṛṣe: Refers to the sages or seers, those who have attained knowledge and wisdom.
    • stuhi: Means "praise" or "worship," indicating an act of reverence.
    • kuvit: Translates to "good thought" or "noble intention," emphasizing the importance of positive thinking.
    • te: Means "your," again indicating a personal connection.
    • śravato: Refers to "fame" or "glory," highlighting the significance of reputation.
    • havam: Means "offering" or "sacrifice," indicating a gesture of devotion.
    • nedīyasaḥ: Refers to "those who are wise" or "intelligent," emphasizing the value of wisdom.
    • kūḻayātaḥ: Means "to be fulfilled" or "to be satisfied," indicating the attainment of desires.
    • paṇīm̐r: Refers to "hands," symbolizing action and effort.
    • uta: Means "or," indicating an alternative or additional thought.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to the Aśvins, asking them to listen to the praises and offerings made by the seers.
    • It emphasizes the importance of good thoughts and noble intentions in our actions.
    • The mention of fame and glory suggests that our actions should be guided by a desire for positive recognition and impact.
    • Wisdom is highlighted as a crucial element in achieving fulfillment and satisfaction in life.
    • The reference to hands symbolizes the need for active participation and effort in realizing our goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied by encouraging teamwork and collaboration, where individuals bring their best intentions and efforts to achieve a common goal.
    • In personal relationships, it serves as a reminder to communicate positively and supportively, fostering an environment of respect and understanding.
    • In health and wellness, it can inspire individuals to seek out positive thoughts and actions that contribute to their overall well-being, much like the Aśvins who are associated with healing.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine support in our endeavors while maintaining a focus on positive thoughts and actions.
    • It teaches us that wisdom and effort are essential in achieving our desires and that our actions should be guided by noble intentions.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a more fulfilling life.
