आ पुत्रासो न मातरं विभृत्राः सानौ देवासो बरहिषः सदन्तु |आ विश्वाची विदथ्याम अनक्त्व अग्ने मा नो देवताता मृधस कः ||
ā putrāso na mātaraṁ vibhṛtrāḥ sānau devāso barhiṣaḥ sadantu | ā viśvācī vidathyām anaktv agne mā no devatātā mṛdhas kaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • putrāso: sons
    • na: not
    • mātaraṁ: mother
    • vibhṛtrāḥ: those who support or nourish
    • sānau: together
    • devāso: gods
    • barhiṣaḥ: with offerings
    • sadantu: may they be present
    • ā: from
    • viśvācī: all-encompassing
    • vidathyām: in the assembly
    • anaktv: without delay
    • agne: O Fire
    • mā: may not
    • no: our
    • devatātā: divine nature
    • mṛdhas: from the earth
    • kaḥ: who
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer for the well-being and support of the family, particularly the sons, while acknowledging the nurturing role of the mother.
    • It invokes the presence of the gods and emphasizes the importance of offerings in spiritual practices.
    • The mention of Agni (Fire) signifies the divine energy that purifies and connects the material and spiritual realms.
    • The phrase "may they be present" reflects a desire for divine blessings and guidance in life’s endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, this mantra can be recited during rituals or ceremonies to seek blessings for the health and prosperity of children.
    • In community gatherings, invoking the gods and making offerings can foster a sense of unity and shared purpose among participants.
    • In personal development, the reference to Agni can symbolize the inner fire or motivation that drives individuals to pursue their goals and aspirations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of familial bonds, the role of divine support, and the importance of spiritual practices in everyday life.
    • It serves as a reminder to honor our roots, seek blessings, and maintain a connection with the divine through rituals and offerings.
    • In contemporary life, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide individuals in nurturing relationships, fostering community, and pursuing personal growth with a sense of purpose and divine guidance.
