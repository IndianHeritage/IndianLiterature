इन्द्रो अस्मां अरदद वज्रबाहुर अपाहन् वृतम् परिधिं नदीनाम् |देवो 'नयत सविता सुपाणिस तस्य वयं प्रसवे याम उर्वीः ||
indro asmām̐ aradad vajrabāhur apāhan vṛtram paridhiṁ nadīnām | devo 'nayat savitā supāṇis tasya vayam prasave yāma urvīḥ ||
  • Indro asmām̐: This phrase refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, and "asmām̐" means "to us." It signifies a call for divine assistance and protection.
  • Aradad: This term means "to grant" or "to bestow." It indicates a request for blessings or gifts from the divine.
  • Vajrabāhur: "Vajra" means "thunderbolt" or "diamond," symbolizing strength and power, while "bāhur" means "armed" or "with arms." This refers to Indra's might and ability to protect and conquer.
  • Apāhan: This means "to remove" or "to dispel." It signifies the act of driving away obstacles or enemies.
  • Vṛtram: Vṛtra is a serpent or dragon-like demon that represents chaos and obstruction. The mantra calls for the removal of such obstacles.
  • Paridhiṁ nadīnām: "Paridhiṁ" means "the boundaries" or "the limits," and "nadīnām" refers to "of the rivers." This can symbolize the natural flow of life and the need to maintain harmony within it.
  • Devo 'nayat: "Devo" means "the divine" and "anayat" means "to lead" or "to guide." This is a plea for divine guidance in our endeavors.
  • Savitā: This refers to the Sun God, representing illumination, energy, and life. It emphasizes the importance of light and knowledge in our lives.
  • Supāṇis: "Supāṇis" means "with good hands" or "with auspicious hands," indicating the need for benevolent actions and intentions.
  • Tasya vayam: "Tasya" means "of that" and "vayam" means "we." This signifies a collective acknowledgment of the divine presence and support.
  • Prasave: This means "in creation" or "in birth," highlighting the importance of new beginnings and the creative process.
  • Yāma urvīḥ: "Yāma" means "to go" or "to move," and "urvīḥ" refers to "the earth" or "the wide expanse." This signifies the journey we undertake in life and our connection to the earth.

Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation for divine assistance, strength, and guidance in overcoming obstacles and achieving success. It emphasizes the importance of seeking help from higher powers in our endeavors, whether in personal challenges, professional pursuits, or spiritual growth. In real life, this can be applicable in various scenarios, such as facing difficulties in work, seeking clarity in decision-making, or striving for personal development. By acknowledging the divine and asking for support, individuals can find the strength to navigate life's challenges and move towards their goals with confidence and purpose.
