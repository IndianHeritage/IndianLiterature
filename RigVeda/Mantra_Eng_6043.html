प्रभर्ता रथं गव्यन्तम् अपाकाच चिद्यं अवति |इनो वसु स हि वोळ्हा ||
prabhartā rathaṁ gavyantam apākāc cid yam avati | ino vasu sa hi voḻhā ||
  • Mantra: "prabhartā rathaṁ gavyantam apākāc cid yam avati | ino vasu sa hi voḻhā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prabhartā: The one who initiates or begins.
    • rathaṁ: Chariot or vehicle.
    • gavyantam: Moving or driven by cows (symbolizing wealth and nourishment).
    • apākāc: From below or from the earth.
    • cid: Consciousness or awareness.
    • yam: Who or that which.
    • avati: Protects or sustains.
    • ino: Of us or our.
    • vasu: Wealth or good fortune.
    • sa: He or that.
    • hi: Indeed or surely.
    • voḻhā: Brings or leads.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine force that initiates the journey (represented by the chariot) and is associated with prosperity and sustenance.
    • The chariot driven by cows symbolizes the movement towards abundance and nourishment, indicating that the journey is not just physical but also spiritual and material.
    • The phrase "from below" suggests a grounding in reality, emphasizing the importance of being connected to the earth and our roots.
    • The consciousness mentioned indicates a higher awareness that guides and protects us on our journey.
    • It highlights the importance of community and shared wealth, as it refers to "us" and "our," suggesting that prosperity is collective.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to the initiation of a new project or venture, where the leader (the initiator) guides the team (the chariot) towards success (wealth and nourishment).
    • In personal life, it can represent the journey of self-discovery and growth, where one is protected and guided by their inner consciousness and values.
    • In community settings, it emphasizes the importance of collaboration and shared resources, where the success of one contributes to the well-being of all.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of initiation, guidance, and protection in our journeys, whether they are personal, professional, or communal.
    • It teaches us to be aware of our roots and the collective nature of wealth and prosperity, reminding us that our journeys are interconnected.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more fulfilling and prosperous existence, where we support and uplift each other in our endeavors.
