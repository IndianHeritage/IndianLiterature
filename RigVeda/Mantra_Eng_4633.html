अक्षो न चक्र्योः शूर बृहन प्र ते मह्ना रिरिचे रोडस्यः |वृक्षस्य नु ते पुरुहूत वयाः व्यūतयो रुरुहुर इन्द्र पूर्वीः ||
akṣo na cakryoḥ śūra bṛhan pra te mahnā ririce rodasyoḥ | vṛkṣasya nu te puruhūta vayā vy ū3tayo ruruhur indra pūrvīḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • akṣo: of the eyes
    • na: not
    • cakryoḥ: of the wheel (or cycle)
    • śūra: brave or heroic
    • bṛhan: great or vast
    • pra: for or towards
    • te: your
    • mahnā: with greatness
    • ririce: to be desired or sought
    • rodasyoḥ: of the heavens or sky
    • vṛkṣasya: of the tree
    • nu: indeed
    • te: your
    • puruhūta: many offerings or praises
    • vayā: strength or power
    • vy ū3tayo: of the various forms
    • ruruhur: to roar or to be powerful
    • indra: the god Indra
    • pūrvīḥ: of the east or the past
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power and bravery associated with the divine, particularly invoking Indra, the king of gods in Vedic tradition.
    • The phrase "akṣo na cakryoḥ" suggests that the eyes (or perception) are not bound by the cycles of life, indicating a higher understanding or awareness.
    • The mention of "śūra bṛhan" emphasizes the greatness of bravery, suggesting that true courage is vast and transcendent.
    • “Rodasyoḥ” refers to the heavens, indicating a connection between the earthly and the divine, where the desires of the heart are aligned with cosmic forces.
    • The reference to "vṛkṣasya" (the tree) symbolizes growth, stability, and the interconnectedness of life, suggesting that strength comes from deep roots and a strong foundation.
    • “Puruhūta” implies that there are many ways to honor and praise the divine, highlighting the diversity of spiritual practices and beliefs.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, the mantra can be interpreted as a call for leaders to be brave and visionary, guiding their teams with a clear and expansive vision that transcends immediate challenges.
    • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek a higher understanding of their purpose, reminding them that true strength lies in their ability to grow and adapt, much like a tree that withstands storms.
    • In community building, the emphasis on diverse offerings can inspire inclusivity, where different voices and practices are celebrated, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of bravery, and the pursuit of higher understanding.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals and communities to seek greatness, embrace diversity, and cultivate strength through unity.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in leadership, personal growth, and community engagement, encouraging a holistic approach to challenges and aspirations.
