सम अस्य मन्यवे विषो विश्वा नमन्त कृष्टयः |समुद्रायेव सिन्धवः ||
sam asya manyave viśo viśvā namanta kṛṣṭayaḥ | samudrāyeva sindhavaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "sam asya manyave viśo viśvā namanta kṛṣṭayaḥ | samudrāyeva sindhavaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sam: together, in harmony
    • asya: of this, of us
    • manyave: with thought, with intention
    • viśo: of the people, of the community
    • viśvā: all, universal
    • namanta: bowing, showing respect
    • kṛṣṭayaḥ: to the cultivated, to the productive
    • samudrāyeva: like the ocean
    • sindhavaḥ: rivers, streams
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and collective thought among people.
    • It suggests that when individuals come together with a shared intention, they can create a powerful and harmonious community.
    • The comparison to rivers flowing into the ocean signifies that individual contributions, when combined, can lead to a greater whole.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, when team members collaborate with a common goal, their combined efforts can lead to innovative solutions and success.
    • In community service, when individuals come together to address social issues, their collective action can bring about significant change.
    • In family settings, mutual respect and understanding foster a nurturing environment, leading to stronger relationships.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra highlights the significance of unity, respect, and collective effort in achieving common goals.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as teamwork, community building, and family dynamics.
    • By embracing the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and productive society.
