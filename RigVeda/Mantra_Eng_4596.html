त्वं धुनिर इन्द्र धुनिमतीर ऋणोर अपः सीरा न श्रवन्तीः | प्र यत समुद्रम अति शूर परषि पारयाऽ तुर्वशं यदुं स्वस्ति ||
tvaṁ dhunir indra dhunimatīr ṛṇor apaḥ sīrā na sravantīḥ | pra yat samudram ati śūra parṣi pārayā turvaśaṁ yaduṁ svasti ||
  • tvaṁ dhunir indra dhunimatīr: This phrase can be translated as "You, O Indra, are the one who shakes." Here, Indra is recognized as a powerful deity, often associated with storms and strength. The term "dhunir" implies movement or shaking, suggesting the dynamic nature of Indra's power.
  • ṛṇor apaḥ sīrā na sravantīḥ: This translates to "the waters of the debt do not flow." In this context, "ṛṇor" refers to debts or obligations, while "apaḥ" means waters. This line can be interpreted as a call for the removal of burdens or debts, indicating a desire for liberation from constraints.
  • pra yat samudram ati śūra: This means "when the ocean is crossed by the brave." The "samudram" refers to the ocean, symbolizing challenges or vast obstacles. "Ati śūra" implies those who are courageous enough to face these challenges. This line emphasizes the importance of bravery in overcoming life's difficulties.
  • parṣi pārayā turvaśaṁ yaduṁ svasti: This can be understood as "may you, O Turvaśa, grant us well-being." "Turvaśa" is a term that can refer to a group or a community, and "svasti" signifies well-being or prosperity. This line is a prayer for collective welfare and harmony.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates themes of strength, liberation from burdens, bravery in the face of challenges, and the pursuit of collective well-being. It highlights the importance of divine support in overcoming obstacles and achieving prosperity.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:
    • In personal development, individuals can draw inspiration from the idea of facing challenges with courage, much like crossing an ocean.
    • In community efforts, the call for collective well-being can resonate in initiatives aimed at social welfare, where the focus is on uplifting the community as a whole.
    • In financial matters, the desire to be free from debts can motivate individuals to seek financial literacy and responsible management of resources.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of divine strength, the importance of courage, and the need for communal harmony. It encourages individuals to seek liberation from burdens and to strive for collective prosperity, making it relevant in both personal and societal contexts.
