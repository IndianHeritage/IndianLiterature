वृषा त्वा वृषणं वर्धतु द्यौर वृषा वृषभ्यां वहसे हरिभ्यां |स नो वृषा वृषरथः सुशिप्र वृषक्रतो वृषा वज्रिन भरे धाः ||
vṛṣā tvā vṛṣaṇaṁ vardhatu dyaur vṛṣā vṛṣabhyāṁ vahase haribhyām | sa no vṛṣā vṛṣarathaḥ suśipra vṛṣakrato vṛṣā vajrin bhare dhāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "vṛṣā tvā vṛṣaṇaṁ vardhatu dyaur vṛṣā vṛṣabhyāṁ vahase haribhyām | sa no vṛṣā vṛṣarathaḥ suśipra vṛṣakrato vṛṣā vajrin bhare dhāḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vṛṣā: Strength, power, or vigor.
    • tvā: You (addressing a deity or force).
    • vṛṣaṇaṁ: The one who brings strength.
    • vardhatu: May it increase or grow.
    • dyaur: Sky or heaven.
    • vṛṣabhyāṁ: By the strong ones.
    • vahase: You carry or bear.
    • haribhyām: By the divine or auspicious ones.
    • sa: He or that (referring to a deity).
    • vṛṣarathaḥ: The chariot of strength.
    • suśipra: Well-formed or auspicious.
    • vṛṣakrato: The one who is wise in strength.
    • vajrin: The one who wields thunder or power.
    • bhare: May you fill or bestow.
    • dhāḥ: O you (addressing a deity).
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes strength and power, asking the divine to increase these qualities in the speaker's life.
    • The phrase "vṛṣā tvā vṛṣaṇaṁ vardhatu" can be understood as a plea for the enhancement of strength, akin to how athletes train to improve their physical capabilities.
    • "Dyaur vṛṣā vṛṣabhyāṁ vahase haribhyām" suggests that the strength is not just personal but is supported by divine forces, similar to how teamwork in sports can elevate individual performance.
    • The mention of "vṛṣarathaḥ" symbolizes the chariot of strength, which can be likened to the tools and resources we use in our daily lives to achieve our goals, such as education and technology.
    • In a real-life scenario, this mantra can be applied in situations where one seeks to overcome challenges, such as preparing for a competitive exam or facing a difficult project at work, invoking the strength and support needed to succeed.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in enhancing one's strength and capabilities.
    • It serves as a reminder that while personal effort is crucial, support from higher powers or the community can amplify our abilities.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be recited before important tasks or challenges, fostering a mindset of empowerment and resilience.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to seek both inner strength and external support, creating a holistic approach to achieving success in various aspects of life.
