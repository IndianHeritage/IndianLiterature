स सूनुर मातरा शुचिर जातो जाते अरोचयत् |महान मही ऋतावृद्धा ||
sa sūnur mātarā śucir jāto jāte arocayat | mahān mahī ṛtāvṛdhā ||
  • sa sūnur: This phrase translates to "he is the son." It signifies the importance of lineage and the connection between generations. In real life, this can be seen in family structures where the values and teachings of parents are passed down to their children.
  • mātarā: This means "by the mother." It highlights the nurturing aspect of motherhood and the role of mothers in shaping the character and values of their children. For example, a mother’s influence can be seen in how children develop empathy and social skills.
  • śucir: This word means "pure" or "clean." It refers to purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions. In a real-life context, this can relate to ethical living, where individuals strive to maintain integrity and honesty in their dealings.
  • jāto jāte: This translates to "born at birth." It emphasizes the significance of the moment of birth and the potential that comes with it. In practical terms, this can be related to the opportunities that arise at the beginning of new ventures, such as starting a business or a new phase in life.
  • arocayat: This means "he shone" or "he radiated." It signifies the idea of brilliance and excellence. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who excel in their fields, inspiring others through their achievements and positive energy.
  • mahān: This translates to "great." It denotes greatness in character, achievements, or contributions to society. For instance, leaders who make significant impacts in their communities exemplify this greatness.
  • mahī: This means "earth" or "the world." It represents the physical realm and our connection to it. In a real-life scenario, this can relate to environmental stewardship and the responsibility we have to care for our planet.
  • ṛtāvṛdhā: This translates to "increasing the order" or "enhancing the truth." It signifies the importance of maintaining harmony and truth in the world. In practical terms, this can be seen in efforts to promote justice and equality in society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the roles of family, purity, greatness, and the importance of truth and harmony in the world. Its significance lies in the reminder that our actions and values shape not only our lives but also the lives of future generations. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to strive for excellence, uphold ethical standards, and contribute positively to society, fostering a sense of responsibility towards both family and the larger community.
