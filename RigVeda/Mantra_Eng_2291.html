पर ऋणā सावीर्द अध मत्कृतानि माहं राजन्न अन्यकृतेन भोजम् | अव्युष्टा इन् नु भूयसीर उषासा आ नो जीवान वरुण तासु शाधि ||
para ṛṇā sāvīr adha matkṛtāni māhaṁ rājann anyakṛtena bhojam | avyuṣṭā in nu bhūyasīr uṣāsa ā no jīvān varuṇa tāsu śādhi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • para ṛṇā: "beyond debts" or "transcending obligations"
    • sāvīr: "with strength" or "with valor"
    • adha: "below" or "under"
    • matkṛtāni: "my actions" or "what I have done"
    • māhaṁ: "I" or "myself"
    • rājann: "O King" or "O ruler"
    • anyakṛtena: "by other actions" or "by different deeds"
    • bhojam: "food" or "nourishment"
    • avyuṣṭā: "not obstructed" or "unhindered"
    • in nu: "indeed" or "truly"
    • bhūyasīr: "may you be greater" or "may you excel"
    • uṣāsa: "dawn" or "morning"
    • ā: "to" or "towards"
    • no: "us" or "our"
    • jīvān: "life" or "living beings"
    • varuṇa: "O Varuna" (the deity associated with cosmic order)
    • tāsu: "in those" or "in these"
    • śādhi: "may you grant" or "may you bestow"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for strength and support from a higher power, specifically addressing a ruler or divine entity.
    • The speaker seeks to transcend their obligations and actions, asking for nourishment and life without hindrance.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine assistance in achieving greatness and overcoming challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an employee may invoke this mantra when seeking guidance and support from their superiors to excel in their career.
    • A student may reflect on this mantra during exams, asking for clarity and strength to overcome academic challenges.
    • In times of personal struggle, individuals may recite this mantra to seek divine intervention for a better life and nourishment for their soul.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking strength and support from higher powers in our endeavors.
    • It serves as a reminder that while we strive for success, we are not alone; divine forces can aid us in our journey.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various situations, such as seeking guidance in leadership, education, and personal growth.
