अच्छा नो मित्रमहो देव देवान अग्नि वोचः सुमतिं रोडस्योः |वीहि स्वस्तिं सुख्षितिं दिवो नृण द्विषो अंहांसी दुरिता tarema ता tarema तवावसां tarema ||
acchā no mitramaho deva devān agne vocaḥ sumatiṁ rodasyoḥ | vīhi svastiṁ sukṣitiṁ divo nṝn dviṣo aṁhāṁsi duritā tarema tā tarema tavāvasā tarema ||
  • acchā no mitramaho deva devān: This phrase can be translated as "May the divine beings, the gods, be our friends." It emphasizes the importance of seeking friendship and support from higher powers or divine entities. In real life, this can be seen in the way people seek guidance and support from mentors, leaders, or even spiritual figures during challenging times.
  • agne vocaḥ sumatiṁ rodasyoḥ: Here, "Agni" refers to the fire god, symbolizing energy and transformation. "Vocah" means to speak or invoke, and "sumatiṁ" refers to good thoughts or wisdom. This line suggests invoking Agni for wisdom and clarity. In practical terms, this can relate to how individuals often turn to meditation or prayer to seek clarity in decision-making or to ignite their inner potential.
  • vīhi svastiṁ sukṣitiṁ divo nṝn: "Vīhi" means to bring forth, "svastiṁ" refers to well-being or prosperity, and "sukṣitiṁ" means good fortune. This line calls for the blessings of prosperity and well-being from the heavens. In everyday life, this can be reflected in the way communities come together to support each other, fostering an environment of mutual growth and prosperity.
  • dviṣo aṁhāṁsi duritā tarema: This translates to "May we overcome the obstacles posed by our enemies." "Dviṣo" refers to enemies, and "duritā" means difficulties or misfortunes. This line highlights the human struggle against adversities. In real life, this can be seen in how individuals and communities face challenges, whether personal or societal, and strive to overcome them through resilience and unity.
  • tā tarema tavāvasā tarema: This phrase means "May we be liberated from all troubles." It emphasizes the desire for freedom from suffering and challenges. In practical terms, this can relate to the pursuit of mental health and well-being, where individuals seek to free themselves from stress and anxiety through various means such as therapy, community support, or self-care practices.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound desire for divine friendship, wisdom, prosperity, and liberation from adversities. Its significance lies in the universal human experience of seeking support and guidance in times of need. In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during personal challenges, community struggles, or even in the pursuit of collective well-being. By invoking these ideals, individuals and communities can foster a sense of unity, resilience, and hope, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
