तां वां नराः स्व अवसे सुजाताः हवामहे अश्विनाः नाधमानाः |आ न उप वसुमता रथेन गिर्व juṣāṇā सुविता यातम् ||
tā vāṁ narā sv avase sujātā havāmahe aśvinā nādhamānāḥ | ā na upa vasumatā rathena giro juṣāṇā suvitāya yātam ||
  • tā vāṁ narā sv avase sujātā havāmahe: This phrase can be translated to "We invoke you, O Aśvins, who are born of the divine, to come to us." Here, the Aśvins are twin deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between opposites.
  • aśvinā nādhamānāḥ: This means "O Aśvins, who are swift and powerful." The Aśvins are often depicted as swift charioteers, representing the speed of thought and action. In real life, this can be related to the importance of being proactive and quick in decision-making, especially in critical situations.
  • ā na upa vasumatā rathena: This translates to "Come to us with your chariot." The chariot symbolizes movement and progress. In a modern context, this can be seen as a call for assistance in overcoming obstacles and moving forward in life, whether in personal endeavors or professional projects.
  • giro juṣāṇā suvitāya yātam: This means "May your words be pleasing and lead us to prosperity." This emphasizes the power of communication and the importance of positive speech. In everyday life, this can be applied to the way we interact with others, highlighting the significance of kind and constructive dialogue in fostering relationships and achieving success.

  • Conclusion: The mantra invokes the Aśvins, seeking their blessings for health, speed, and prosperity. Its significance lies in the recognition of the divine forces that aid us in our journey through life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects, such as health and wellness, the importance of swift action in decision-making, and the value of positive communication. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate challenges more effectively and foster a harmonious existence.
