हरī यस्य सुयुजा विव्रता वेर अर्वन्तानु शेपा |उभा रजी न केशिना पतिर्दान ||
harī yasya suyujā vivratā ver arvantānu śepā | ubhā rajī na keśinā patir dan ||
  • Harī: This term refers to the divine or the supreme being, often associated with Lord Vishnu in Hinduism. It signifies the source of all creation and the sustainer of life.
  • Yasya: Meaning "whose," this word indicates possession or belonging, linking the divine to the qualities that follow.
  • Suyujā: This translates to "well-connected" or "well-joined." It suggests harmony and unity, emphasizing the importance of relationships and connections in life.
  • Vivratā: Meaning "revealed" or "unfolded," this word indicates the disclosure of truth or knowledge, highlighting the importance of understanding and enlightenment.
  • Ver: This term can be interpreted as "strength" or "power," representing the force that drives creation and sustains existence.
  • Arvantānu: This word refers to "the one who is powerful" or "the strong one," emphasizing the qualities of resilience and strength in overcoming challenges.
  • Śepā: Meaning "protection" or "shelter," this term signifies the divine's role in safeguarding and guiding individuals through life's journey.
  • Ubhā: This translates to "both," indicating duality or the presence of two aspects, which can represent balance in life.
  • Rajī: Meaning "ruler" or "king," this word signifies authority and leadership, highlighting the importance of guidance in various aspects of life.
  • Na: This word means "not," indicating negation or absence, which can imply the need to overcome obstacles or challenges.
  • Keśinā: Referring to "hair" or "the one with hair," this term can symbolize the natural and untamed aspects of life, representing freedom and individuality.
  • Patir: Meaning "lord" or "master," this word signifies a figure of authority or guidance, emphasizing the need for leadership in various contexts.
  • Dan: This translates to "gift" or "offering," indicating the importance of generosity and sharing in human relationships.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine connection, strength, protection, and the importance of relationships in life. It emphasizes the need for unity and understanding, as well as the role of leadership and generosity in fostering harmony. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal relationships, community building, and leadership roles, reminding individuals of the significance of connection, strength, and the protective guidance of the divine in navigating life's challenges.
