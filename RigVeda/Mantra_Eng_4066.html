आयं सोमश्चमू सुतो 'मात्रे परि षिच्यते |प्रिय इन्द्राय वायवे ||
ayaṁ somaś camū suto 'matre pari ṣicyate | priya indrāya vāyave ||
  • ayaṁ: This word translates to "this" or "here." It indicates a presence or a specific subject being referred to.
  • somaś: Refers to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes the essence of life and vitality.
  • camū: This means "army" or "host." In a broader sense, it can refer to a collective force or group working towards a common goal.
  • suto: Translates to "born of" or "produced." It signifies the origin or source of something.
  • 'matre: This word means "to the mother" or "to the source." It emphasizes the nurturing aspect of creation and existence.
  • pariṣicyate: This means "is poured" or "is sprinkled." It indicates an act of offering or bestowing something sacred.
  • priya: Translates to "beloved" or "dear." It signifies affection and the importance of the recipient.
  • indrāya: Refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic mythology, associated with rain, thunder, and strength. It symbolizes power and authority.
  • vāyave: This means "to Vayu," the god of wind. Vayu represents movement, breath, and the vital force of life.

  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of Soma, suggesting that it is being offered to the divine forces represented by Indra and Vayu.
    • The act of pouring Soma can be seen as a ritualistic offering, symbolizing the connection between the physical and spiritual realms.
    • It highlights the importance of nurturing and honoring the forces of nature and divinity that sustain life.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the practice of gratitude and respect towards nature. For instance, when we celebrate harvest festivals, we acknowledge the earth's bounty, similar to offering Soma.
    • In a corporate setting, the concept of teamwork (camū) can be seen as a collective effort towards a common goal, where each member contributes their unique strengths, akin to the divine forces working together.
    • Environmental conservation efforts can be viewed as a way of honoring Vayu (the wind) and the natural elements, recognizing their role in sustaining life.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and honoring the divine forces that govern our existence.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the interconnectedness of life, the environment, and the spiritual realm.
    • In real life, it encourages us to practice gratitude, teamwork, and respect for nature, fostering a harmonious relationship with the world around us.
