ते हिन्विरे अरुणं जेन्यं वस्व एकं पुत्रं तिसृणाम् |ते धामान्य अमृताः मार्त्यानाम् अदब्धा अभि चक्षते ||
te hinvire aruṇaṁ jenyaṁ vasv ekam putraṁ tisṝṇām | te dhāmāny amṛtā martyānām adabdhā abhi cakṣate ||
  • te hinvire aruṇaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "They who are the red ones" or "They who are the dawn." In a real-life context, this can symbolize new beginnings or the arrival of hope, much like the dawn brings light after darkness.
  • jenyaṁ vasv ekam putraṁ tisṝṇām: This translates to "the one who is born from the universe, the son of the three." Here, "the universe" can be interpreted as the source of all creation, while "the three" may refer to the three fundamental aspects of existence: creation, preservation, and destruction. In practical terms, this can relate to the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of nurturing our relationships with others.
  • te dhāmāny amṛtā martyānām: This means "they who are the abode of immortality for mortals." This can be understood as a reference to the eternal truths or values that guide human life. In everyday life, this could relate to the pursuit of knowledge, wisdom, and ethical living, which contribute to a fulfilling existence.
  • adabdhā abhi cakṣate: This translates to "they who are unblemished, see clearly." This can signify the importance of clarity of thought and purity of intention. In real life, this can be applied to the idea of making decisions based on integrity and honesty, leading to a more meaningful life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of new beginnings, the interconnectedness of life, the pursuit of eternal truths, and the importance of clarity and integrity. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional lives, encouraging them to embrace change, foster relationships, seek wisdom, and act with honesty. By embodying these values, one can lead a more purposeful and fulfilling life, contributing positively to society and the world at large.
