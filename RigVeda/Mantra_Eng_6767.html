हतम् च शत्रून् यततम् च मित्रिणः प्रजां च धत्तं द्रविणं च धत्तम् |सजोषसां उषसां सूर्येण चोर्जं नो धत्तम् अश्विनाः ||
hataṁ ca śatrūn yatataṁ ca mitriṇaḥ prajāṁ ca dhattaṁ draviṇaṁ ca dhattam | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa corjaṁ no dhattam aśvinā ||
  • hataṁ ca śatrūn: This phrase translates to "defeating the enemies." In real life, this can be understood as overcoming obstacles or challenges that one faces, whether in personal life, career, or relationships. For example, a student may face academic challenges, and by working hard and seeking help, they can "defeat" these challenges.
  • yatataṁ ca mitriṇaḥ: This means "striving for friendship." It emphasizes the importance of building and maintaining relationships. In a workplace, for instance, collaboration and teamwork are essential for success. By fostering good relationships with colleagues, one can create a supportive environment that enhances productivity.
  • prajāṁ ca dhattaṁ: This translates to "nurturing the people." It signifies the responsibility of caring for others, whether in a family, community, or society. A real-life example could be a community leader who organizes events to support local families in need, thereby nurturing the community.
  • draviṇaṁ ca dhattam: This means "providing resources." It highlights the importance of sharing wealth and resources for the betterment of society. For instance, a successful entrepreneur might invest in local businesses or charities, thereby contributing to the economic growth of the community.
  • sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa: This phrase can be interpreted as "with the dawn and the sun." It symbolizes new beginnings and the energy that comes with them. In real life, this can be seen in how people often start fresh each day, setting new goals and intentions at sunrise, much like a new project or a personal resolution.
  • corjaṁ no dhattam aśvinā: This translates to "may we not be harmed by thieves." It serves as a reminder to protect oneself from negative influences or harmful situations. In a modern context, this could relate to safeguarding one's mental health by avoiding toxic relationships or environments.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of overcoming challenges, fostering relationships, nurturing communities, and protecting oneself from negativity. Its significance lies in its holistic approach to life, encouraging individuals to strive for personal growth while also contributing positively to society. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal development, community service, and professional environments, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
