अभि त्वा योषणो दश जारं न कन्यानूषत |मृज्यसे सोम सातये ||
abhi tvā yoṣaṇo daśa jāraṁ na kanyānūṣata | mṛjyase soma sātaye ||
  • Mantra: "abhi tvā yoṣaṇo daśa jāraṁ na kanyānūṣata | mṛjyase soma sātaye ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, for
    • tvā: you
    • yoṣaṇo: of the women, feminine energy
    • daśa: ten
    • jāraṁ: old, aged
    • na: not
    • kanyānūṣata: the maidens, young women
    • mṛjyase: you are purified
    • soma: the sacred drink, often associated with immortality
    • sātaye: for the purpose of well-being, prosperity
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine feminine energy and its relationship with the sacred.
    • The phrase "abhi tvā yoṣaṇo" suggests an invocation or a call towards the feminine aspect, emphasizing its importance in life.
    • The mention of "daśa jāraṁ" indicates a contrast between the old and the young, possibly highlighting the wisdom of age versus the vitality of youth.
    • "na kanyānūṣata" implies that the essence of femininity is not confined to youth alone, but encompasses a broader spectrum of experience and wisdom.
    • The latter part, "mṛjyase soma sātaye," indicates a purification process through the sacred drink, symbolizing rejuvenation and the quest for well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the feminine energy is revered and celebrated, such as in festivals dedicated to goddesses, which symbolize strength, wisdom, and nurturing.
    • In modern society, the balance between youth and age is often discussed in the context of leadership, where the experience of older generations is valued alongside the innovative ideas of the youth.
    • Practices like yoga and meditation often emphasize the importance of inner purification and well-being, akin to the "mṛjyase soma sātaye" aspect of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of recognizing and honoring the feminine energy in all its forms, from youth to age.
    • It serves as a reminder that wisdom and vitality coexist and that both are essential for holistic well-being.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek balance in their lives, appreciating the contributions of all ages and genders, and striving for personal and communal prosperity.
