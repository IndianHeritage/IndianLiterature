इमां मे मरुतो गिरं इमं स्तोमं ऋभुक्षणः |इमं मे वनतां हवम् ||
imām me maruto giram imaṁ stomam ṛbhukṣaṇaḥ | imam me vanatā havam ||
  • Translation: The mantra translates to "O Maruts, grant me this hymn; O Ṛbhukṣaṇa, accept this offering; I call upon you, O Vanatā."
  • Explanation of Terms:
    • Maruts: The Maruts are storm deities in Vedic literature, often associated with wind, storms, and the power of nature. They symbolize strength and vitality.
    • Giram: This term refers to a hymn or song, indicating the importance of vocal expression in invoking divine powers.
    • Stomam: A hymn or praise, emphasizing the act of worship and the significance of offering praises to the deities.
    • Ṛbhukṣaṇa: A deity associated with the act of receiving offerings, representing the acceptance of prayers and sacrifices.
    • Vanatā: This term can refer to a deity or a divine force related to the natural world, often associated with the forest and wilderness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, we often seek strength and support from nature, much like the Maruts. For instance, during challenging times, people may go for hikes or spend time in nature to rejuvenate their spirits.
    • The act of singing or chanting hymns can be seen in various cultures, such as in churches or during yoga sessions, where vocal expressions are used to connect with the divine.
    • Offering prayers or sacrifices, whether in a religious context or through acts of kindness and charity, reflects the essence of Ṛbhukṣaṇa, as we seek acceptance and blessings for our actions.
    • Vanatā can be related to environmental conservation efforts, where individuals and communities work to protect forests and natural habitats, recognizing the divine presence in nature.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support and recognition of the interconnectedness of nature and spirituality. It emphasizes the importance of vocal expression in worship and the need for acceptance of our offerings. In real life, this mantra encourages individuals to seek strength from nature, engage in acts of kindness, and appreciate the divine presence in the environment. By invoking the Maruts and other deities, we acknowledge our dependence on the natural world and the spiritual forces that guide us.
