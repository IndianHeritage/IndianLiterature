यत् त्वा तुरीयं ऋतुभिर द्रविणोदो यजामहे |अध स्मा नो ददिर भव ||
yat tvā turīyam ṛtubhir draviṇodo yajāmahe | adha smā no dadir bhava ||
  • Mantra: "yat tvā turīyam ṛtubhir draviṇodo yajāmahe | adha smā no dadir bhava ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yat: that which
    • tvā: you
    • turīyam: the fourth (often interpreted as the ultimate or transcendent state)
    • ṛtubhir: with the seasons or rhythms
    • draviṇodo: wealth or abundance
    • yajāmahe: we worship or invoke
    • adha: below or here
    • smā: may we
    • no: our
    • dadir: grant or bestow
    • bhava: become or manifest
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for the divine presence to manifest in our lives.
    • The term "turīyam" refers to a higher state of consciousness, often associated with spiritual awakening or enlightenment.
    • By invoking this state with the rhythms of life ("ṛtubhir"), we seek to align ourselves with the natural order and abundance of the universe.
    • The phrase "adha smā no dadir bhava" expresses a desire for this divine presence to grant us blessings and prosperity in our earthly existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals may recite this mantra during meditation or yoga practices to connect with their higher self and seek guidance.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be chanted during rituals or ceremonies to invoke collective prosperity and harmony.
    • People may use this mantra in times of uncertainty, hoping to attract positive energy and abundance into their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for spiritual awakening and the manifestation of abundance in our lives.
    • It teaches us to recognize the interconnectedness of our spiritual and material worlds, encouraging us to seek balance.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal growth, community well-being, and during times of need, reminding us of the power of intention and collective energy.
