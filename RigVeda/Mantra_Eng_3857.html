प्र नु वयं सुते या ते कृतानिंद्र ब्रवाम यानि नो जुजोषः |वेदद अविद्वां छृणवच च विद्वान वहते 'यम मगवाऽ सर्वसेनः ||
pra nu vayaṁ sute yā te kṛtānīndra bravāma yāni no jujoṣaḥ | vedad avidvāñ chṛṇavac ca vidvān vahate 'yam maghavā sarvasenaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra nu: We invoke or call upon.
    • vayaṁ: We (the speakers).
    • sute: In the context of offerings or sacrifices.
    • yā: That which.
    • te: Your (referring to Indra).
    • kṛtānī: Accomplished or performed.
    • indra: The king of gods, representing strength and power.
    • bravāma: We speak or declare.
    • yāni: Those which.
    • no: Our.
    • jujoṣaḥ: Have been enjoyed or experienced.
    • veda: Knowledge or wisdom.
    • avidvāñ: The ignorant or unwise.
    • chṛṇavac: Listening or hearing.
    • ca: And.
    • vidvān: The wise or knowledgeable.
    • vahate: Carries or bears.
    • 'yam: This.
    • maghavā: The powerful or mighty one.
    • sarvasenaḥ: With all forces or armies.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine, specifically to Indra, the god of rain and thunder, to bless the speakers with strength and wisdom.
    • It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and the distinction between the wise and the ignorant.
    • The invocation suggests that through offerings and devotion, one can attain the blessings of the divine, leading to prosperity and success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be likened to a team calling upon their leader (akin to Indra) for guidance and support in achieving their goals.
    • In education, students may seek knowledge and wisdom from their teachers, recognizing the difference between those who are informed and those who are not.
    • During challenging times, individuals may turn to spiritual practices, invoking divine support to gain strength and clarity in decision-making.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine support in our endeavors, recognizing the value of wisdom over ignorance.
    • It teaches us that through sincere efforts and devotion, we can attract positive energies and guidance in our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal challenges, professional pursuits, or educational journeys, the essence of this mantra encourages us to seek strength and knowledge from higher powers and wise individuals.
