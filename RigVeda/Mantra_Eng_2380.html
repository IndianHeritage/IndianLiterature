मेद्यन्तु ते वह्नयो येभिर ईयसे 'रिषण्यन् वीलयस्व वनस्पते |आयूयां धृष्णो अभिगूर्याऽत्वं नेष्ट्रात सोमं द्रविणोदः पिब ऋतुभिः ||
medyantu te vahnayo yebhir īyase 'riṣaṇyan vīḻayasvā vanaspate | āyūyā dhṛṣṇo abhigūryā tvaṁ neṣṭrāt somaṁ draviṇodaḥ piba ṛtubhiḥ ||
  • medyantu te vahnayo: This phrase can be translated as "may your fires (or energies) be beneficial." Here, "vahnayo" refers to the divine energies or forces, often associated with the sun or fire, which are seen as purifying and life-giving.
  • yebhir īyase 'riṣaṇyan: This means "by which you move or travel." It suggests a dynamic aspect of these energies, indicating that they are not static but rather active forces that guide and influence life.
  • vīḻayasvā vanaspate: Translated as "may you, O plant, be strong." "Vanaspate" refers to vegetation or plants, emphasizing the importance of nature and its vitality in sustaining life.
  • āyūyā dhṛṣṇo: This phrase means "may you have a long life." It is a blessing for longevity and vitality, highlighting the desire for health and endurance.
  • abhigūryā tvaṁ neṣṭrāt: This translates to "we invoke you, O Soma." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing nourishment and immortality, often associated with divine inspiration and ecstasy.
  • draviṇodaḥ piba ṛtubhiḥ: This means "drink the wealth of the seasons." It signifies the importance of seasonal cycles and the abundance they bring, encouraging us to embrace the gifts of nature throughout the year.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, nature, and divine energies. It serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing our environment and recognizing the blessings that come from it. In real life, this can be applied by promoting sustainable practices, respecting natural resources, and fostering a sense of gratitude for the abundance that nature provides. By invoking these energies and blessings, we can cultivate a harmonious relationship with the world around us, leading to a healthier and more fulfilling life.
