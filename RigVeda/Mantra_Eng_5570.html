मित्रस्तन नो वरुणो मामहन्त शर्म तोकाय तनयाय गोपाः |मा वो भुजेमान्यजातम एनो मा तत कर्म वसवो यच चयध्वे ||
mitras tan no varuṇo māmahanta śarma tokāya tanayāya gopāḥ | mā vo bhujemānyajātam eno mā tat karma vasavo yac cayadhve ||
  • Mitras: This term refers to "friends" or "allies." In a broader sense, it signifies the importance of friendship and cooperation in life.
  • Tan no: This phrase means "may he (the deity) bestow upon us." It reflects a plea for blessings and support.
  • Varuṇo: Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water. He represents the moral and ethical aspects of life, emphasizing the importance of righteousness.
  • Māmahanta: This translates to "may he protect us." It is a call for divine protection and guidance in our endeavors.
  • Śarma: This word means "comfort" or "shelter." It signifies the need for security and peace in life.
  • Tokāya: This term refers to "for the offspring" or "for the children," highlighting the importance of family and future generations.
  • Tanayāya: Similar to Tokāya, it emphasizes the significance of children and the continuity of life.
  • Gopāḥ: This means "the protectors" or "the guardians," often referring to those who safeguard the community or family.
  • Mā vo bhujemānyajātam: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we not be harmed by any evil." It is a plea for protection from negativity and harm.
  • Eno: This word means "may it be." It expresses a desire for a certain outcome or blessing.
  • Mā tat karma vasavo: This translates to "may our actions be in harmony with the divine." It emphasizes the importance of aligning one's actions with higher principles.
  • Yac cayadhve: This means "whatever you desire." It reflects the idea that our intentions and desires should be pure and aligned with the greater good.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine protection, friendship, and ethical living. It emphasizes the importance of community, family, and the moral responsibilities we hold towards one another. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as fostering harmonious relationships, ensuring the well-being of future generations, and striving for actions that contribute positively to society. By invoking these principles, individuals can create a supportive and nurturing environment, both personally and collectively, leading to a more balanced and fulfilling life.
