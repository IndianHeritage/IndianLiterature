स्वायुधस्य ते सतो भुवनस्य पते वयं |इन्दो सखित्वं उष्मसि ||
svāyudhasya te sato bhuvanasya pate vayam | indo sakhitvam uśmasi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • svāyudhasya: Of the one who possesses weapons or strength.
    • te: Your.
    • sato: Of the good or virtuous.
    • bhuvanasya: Of the world or universe.
    • pate: O Lord or Master.
    • vayam: We.
    • indo: O Indra (the king of gods).
    • sakhitvam: Friendship or companionship.
    • uśmasi: You bestow or grant.
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of gods, acknowledging his strength and virtue. It expresses a desire for friendship and support from him, emphasizing the importance of divine companionship in overcoming challenges in the world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal struggle, individuals often seek strength from a higher power, much like how this mantra seeks the support of Indra. For instance, someone facing a difficult decision may pray for guidance and strength.
    • In a community setting, people may come together to support one another, reflecting the idea of seeking companionship and strength from each other, akin to the friendship sought from Indra in the mantra.
    • In leadership, a leader may embody the qualities of strength and virtue, inspiring their team to work together harmoniously, similar to how the mantra invokes the qualities of Indra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support and the recognition of the strength that comes from virtuous companionship. In real life, it serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking help from higher powers or supportive communities during challenging times. It encourages individuals to cultivate strength and virtue within themselves and to foster friendships that can help navigate the complexities of life.
