मन्त्र: क्व नूनं कद वो अर्थं गन्ता दिवो न पृथिव्याः | क्व वो गावो न रण्यन्ति ||
kva nūnaṁ kad vo arthaṁ gantā divo na pṛthivyāḥ | kva vo gāvo na raṇyanti ||
  • Mantra: "kva nūnaṁ kad vo arthaṁ gantā divo na pṛthivyāḥ | kva vo gāvo na raṇyanti ||"
  • Translation: "Where indeed is the purpose of your journey, if it does not lead to the heavens or the earth? Where are your cows that do not moo?"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • kva: Where
    • nūnaṁ: indeed
    • kad: when
    • vo: your
    • arthaṁ: purpose or meaning
    • gantā: to go or to journey
    • divo: of the heavens
    • na: not
    • pṛthivyāḥ: of the earth
    • gāvo: cows
    • raṇyanti: moo or make sound
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra poses profound questions about the purpose of life and the journey one undertakes.
    • It suggests that if one's actions do not lead to a higher purpose or connection with the divine (the heavens) or the material world (the earth), then what is the value of those actions?
    • The mention of cows, which are often seen as symbols of wealth and sustenance in Vedic culture, emphasizes the importance of recognizing the fruits of one's labor and the sounds of life around us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional context, this mantra can be reflected in the pursuit of a career. If an individual works tirelessly but does not find fulfillment or contribute positively to society, they may question the purpose of their efforts.
    • In personal relationships, one might consider the significance of their connections. If relationships do not bring joy or support, one may wonder about their value.
    • In spiritual practices, individuals often seek a connection with something greater than themselves. This mantra encourages reflection on whether one's spiritual journey is meaningful and leads to growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder to evaluate the purpose behind our actions and the significance of our journeys.
    • It encourages individuals to seek alignment between their goals and their values, ensuring that their efforts contribute to both personal fulfillment and the greater good.
    • In essence, it calls for a balance between the material and spiritual aspects of life, urging us to listen to the 'moo' of our existence and recognize the importance of our choices.
