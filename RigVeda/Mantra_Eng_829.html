देवो न यः पृथिवीं विश्वधायां उपक्षेति हितमित्रो न राजा |पुरःसदः शर्मसदः न वीराः अनवद्याः पतिजुष्टेव नारी ||
devo na yaḥ pṛthivīṁ viśvadhāyā upakṣeti hitamitro na rājā | puraḥsadaḥ śarmasado na vīrā anavadyā patijuṣṭeva nārī ||
  • Devo na yaḥ: This phrase translates to "the divine being who." It emphasizes the presence of divinity in our lives and the importance of recognizing higher powers.
  • Pṛthivīṁ viśvadhāyā: This means "the earth that supports all." It signifies the nurturing aspect of the earth, which provides sustenance and shelter to all living beings.
  • Upakṣeti: This translates to "protects" or "supports." It highlights the role of divine forces in safeguarding and nurturing life.
  • Hitamitro na rājā: This means "the friend of the good." It suggests that true leadership is characterized by benevolence and the welfare of others, rather than mere authority.
  • Puraḥsadaḥ: This translates to "the one who is in front." It can refer to a leader or a guiding figure who leads by example.
  • Śarmasado: This means "the one who brings comfort." It emphasizes the importance of providing solace and security to others.
  • Na vīrā: This translates to "not the brave." It suggests that true strength lies not just in bravery but in wisdom and compassion.
  • Anavadyā: This means "without fault." It indicates the ideal qualities of a leader or divine being, who is pure and virtuous.
  • Patijuṣṭeva nārī: This translates to "like a woman who is devoted." It highlights the qualities of devotion and loyalty, often associated with feminine virtues.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine protection, the nurturing aspect of the earth, and the qualities of true leadership. It emphasizes that a leader should be a friend to the good, providing comfort and guidance while embodying virtues such as compassion and purity.
  • Real-life Application: In today's world, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in leadership roles, community service, and personal relationships. For instance, a leader in a community should prioritize the welfare of their constituents, embodying the qualities of a protector and a nurturer. Similarly, in personal relationships, one can strive to be a source of comfort and support for loved ones, reflecting the ideals presented in this mantra.
