स पवस्व मदिन्तम गोभिर अञ्जानो अक्तुभिः |इन्दव इन्द्राय पीतये ||
sa pavasva madintama gobhir añjāno aktubhiḥ | indav indrāya pītaye ||
  • sa pavasva: This phrase can be translated as "may you blow" or "may you inspire." It signifies a call for divine energy or inspiration to flow into the individual or the community.
  • madintama: This word means "the most intoxicating" or "the best of all." It refers to the highest form of bliss or ecstasy, often associated with spiritual experiences or divine love.
  • gobhir añjāno: This translates to "with the cows" or "through the wealth of the earth." Cows in Vedic culture symbolize abundance, nourishment, and prosperity. This phrase emphasizes the connection between spiritual fulfillment and material wealth.
  • aktubhiḥ: This word means "with the actions" or "through deeds." It suggests that the attainment of spiritual and material wealth requires active participation and effort.
  • indav: This term refers to "the divine" or "the gods." It signifies the higher powers that govern the universe and influence human life.
  • indrāya: This means "to Indra," who is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology. Indra represents strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • pītaye: This translates to "for drinking" or "for enjoyment." It implies the consumption or enjoyment of the divine nectar, which symbolizes spiritual wisdom and enlightenment.

In summary, this mantra is a prayer for divine inspiration and abundance. It calls upon the higher powers to bless individuals with spiritual ecstasy and material prosperity through their actions. The mantra emphasizes the importance of actively seeking both spiritual and material fulfillment in life.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in daily life by encouraging individuals to seek inspiration and motivation in their endeavors, whether personal or professional.
  • It highlights the importance of balancing spiritual pursuits with material needs, reminding us that both aspects are essential for a fulfilling life.
  • In practical terms, one might recite this mantra before starting a new project or venture, seeking divine guidance and support to achieve success.
  • Moreover, it serves as a reminder to appreciate the abundance in our lives, such as relationships, resources, and opportunities, and to actively engage in actions that promote growth and prosperity.
