अग्निं दूतं वृष्णीमहे होतारं विश्ववेदसम् |अस्य यज्ञस्य सुक्रतुम् ||
agniṁ dūtaṁ vṛṇīmahe hotāraṁ viśvavedasam | asya yajñasya sukratum ||
  • Agniṁ: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying force and a medium for offerings to the deities.
  • Dūtaṁ: This translates to "messenger." In this context, Agni is considered a messenger between humans and the divine.
  • Vṛṇīmahe: This means "we choose" or "we invoke." It signifies the act of selecting or calling upon Agni for assistance.
  • Hotāraṁ: This refers to "the one who offers" or "the priest." It emphasizes the role of Agni in the sacrificial rituals where offerings are made.
  • Viśvavedasam: This means "the one who knows all." It highlights Agni's omniscience and his ability to connect various realms of existence.
  • Asya: This translates to "of this" or "of this sacrifice." It indicates the context of the mantra, which is related to a specific yajna (sacrifice).
  • Yajñasya: This means "of the sacrifice." It refers to the ritualistic offerings made to the deities.
  • Sukratum: This translates to "good action" or "auspicious deed." It signifies the positive outcomes that arise from the sacrifice.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer invoking Agni, the fire god, as a messenger and priest who knows all. It emphasizes the importance of Agni in the sacrificial rituals, where he acts as a bridge between the human and divine realms. By choosing Agni, the practitioners seek to ensure that their sacrifices yield auspicious results.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: In Hindu weddings and other significant ceremonies, fire is often a central element, symbolizing purity and the divine presence.
  • Personal Growth: The invocation of Agni can be seen as a metaphor for personal transformation, where individuals seek to purify their intentions and actions for better outcomes in life.
  • Community Gatherings: In communal rituals, invoking Agni can foster a sense of unity and shared purpose among participants, reinforcing social bonds.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the vital role of Agni in connecting humans with the divine through rituals. It highlights the importance of intention and purity in actions, reminding us that our efforts, when aligned with good intentions, can lead to positive outcomes in our lives and communities.
