पृषदश्वा मरुतः पृष्णिमातरः शुभंयावानो विदथेषु जग्मयः |अग्निजिह्वा मनवः सूरचक्षसो विश्वे नो देवाः अवसागमन निह ||
pṛṣadaśvā marutaḥ pṛśnimātaraḥ śubhaṁyāvāno vidatheṣu jagmayaḥ | agnijihvā manavaḥ sūracakṣaso viśve no devā avasā gamann iha ||
  • pṛṣadaśvā marutaḥ: This phrase refers to the "swift horses of the Maruts," the storm deities in Vedic literature. The Maruts are often associated with strength, speed, and the power of nature. In real life, this can be likened to the forces of nature that drive change and transformation, such as storms that bring rain essential for agriculture.
  • pṛśnimātaraḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who are born from the earth." It signifies the connection between the divine and the earthly realm. In a modern context, this can represent our roots and the importance of being grounded in our values and heritage.
  • śubhaṁyāvāno: This word means "those who bring auspiciousness." It highlights the role of positive energies and good fortune in our lives. For example, when we engage in acts of kindness or community service, we often experience a sense of fulfillment and positivity that can be seen as auspicious.
  • vidatheṣu jagmayaḥ: This phrase translates to "they who gather in the assemblies." It emphasizes the importance of community and collaboration. In today's world, this can be seen in how people come together for social causes, fostering unity and collective strength.
  • agnijihvā manavaḥ: This means "the flame-tongued beings." It symbolizes the power of communication and expression. In real life, effective communication is crucial in personal relationships and professional settings, as it can ignite passion and understanding among individuals.
  • sūracakṣaso: This term refers to "the divine beings with keen vision." It suggests the importance of insight and awareness. In our daily lives, being observant and aware of our surroundings can lead to better decision-making and a deeper understanding of the world around us.
  • viśve no devā avasā gamann iha: This translates to "all the gods are present here." It signifies the omnipresence of divine energy in our lives. In a practical sense, this can be interpreted as recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine presence in every aspect of life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, community, auspiciousness, communication, and divine presence. Its significance lies in the reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the importance of collaboration and positivity in our actions. In real life, we can apply these teachings by fostering community spirit, engaging in positive actions, and being mindful of our communication, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
