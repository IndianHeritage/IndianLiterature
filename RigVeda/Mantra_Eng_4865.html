आ मां पूषन्न उप द्रव शंसिषं नु ते अपिकर्ण आगृह्णे |अघा आर्यो अरातयः ||
ā mā pūṣann upa drava śaṁsiṣaṁ nu te apikarṇa āghṛṇe | aghā aryo arātayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā mā: "Do not" or "not for me"
    • pūṣann: "O nourisher" or "O provider"
    • upa: "towards" or "near"
    • drava: "flow" or "movement"
    • śaṁsiṣaṁ: "to bring peace" or "to grant tranquility"
    • nu: "indeed" or "certainly"
    • te: "your"
    • apikarṇa: "with attentive ears" or "listening closely"
    • āghṛṇe: "to the one who is gracious" or "to the benevolent"
    • aghā: "without sin" or "pure"
    • aryo: "noble" or "worthy"
    • arātayaḥ: "to those who are hostile" or "to the adversaries"
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer for protection and nourishment from the divine.
    • It expresses a desire for peace and tranquility, urging the divine to listen attentively to the supplicant's needs.
    • The mantra acknowledges the presence of adversities and seeks to overcome them with purity and nobility.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek solace through prayer or meditation, similar to the essence of this mantra.
    • In a community facing conflict, leaders may invoke such prayers to foster unity and peace among members.
    • During challenging times, people may turn to spiritual practices that emphasize listening and understanding, reflecting the attentive nature of the divine as mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It highlights the need for purity of intention and the pursuit of peace, both personally and within communities.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to remain noble in our actions and to seek harmony, even in the face of adversity.
