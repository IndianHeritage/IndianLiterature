यस् ते चित्रश्रवस्तमो य इन्द्र वृत्रहन्तमः |य ओजोदातमः मदः ||
yas te citraśravastamo ya indra vṛtrahantamaḥ | ya ojodātamo madaḥ ||
  • yas te citraśravastamo: This phrase can be broken down into three parts. "yas" means "who," "te" means "your," and "citraśravastamo" refers to "the one with the most glorious fame." This suggests a being or entity that possesses exceptional qualities and is renowned for their deeds.
  • ya indra vṛtrahantamaḥ: Here, "ya" means "who," "indra" refers to the deity Indra, who is often associated with strength and power, and "vṛtrahantamaḥ" means "the slayer of Vritra." Vritra is a serpent or dragon in Vedic mythology that represents chaos and obstacles. This line emphasizes the power of Indra in overcoming challenges.
  • ya ojodātamo madaḥ: In this phrase, "ya" again means "who," "ojo" refers to "energy" or "vigor," "dātamo" means "the giver," and "madaḥ" can be interpreted as "intoxication" or "delight." This suggests a being that provides energy and joy, enhancing the vitality of life.

  • Significance: This mantra highlights the qualities of strength, glory, and vitality. It invokes the essence of a powerful deity, specifically Indra, who is celebrated for his ability to conquer chaos and provide energy and joy to his followers.
  • Real-life Application: In our daily lives, we often face challenges that can feel overwhelming, much like the chaos represented by Vritra. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek strength and support from higher powers or within ourselves to overcome obstacles. For instance, during difficult times, individuals may find solace in prayer or meditation, invoking the qualities of strength and glory to navigate their struggles.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength and vitality in the face of adversity. It encourages individuals to recognize their inner power and the support they can draw from the divine or the universe. By embodying the qualities of Indra, one can approach life's challenges with courage and a sense of purpose, ultimately leading to personal growth and fulfillment.
