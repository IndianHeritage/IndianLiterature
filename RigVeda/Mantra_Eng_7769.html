मन्त्र: परि प्रियाः दिवः कविर वयांसी नप्त्योर हितः |सुवाणो याति कविक्रतुः ||
pari priyā divaḥ kavir vayāṁsi naptyor hitaḥ | suvāno yāti kavikratuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pari: around, in all directions
    • priyā: beloved, dear
    • dvaḥ: of the heavens
    • kavir: the wise one, the poet, the seer
    • vayāṁsi: the powers, the energies
    • naptyor: of the descendants, of the progeny
    • hitaḥ: beneficial, good
    • suvāno: well-spoken, auspicious
    • yāti: goes, moves
    • kavikratuḥ: the wise one's intention, the plan of the seer
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the interconnectedness of wisdom and the energies that surround us.
    • The "wise one" or "kavir" represents those who possess knowledge and insight, guiding others towards beneficial paths.
    • The mention of "naptyor" emphasizes the importance of lineage and the impact of wisdom on future generations.
    • The phrase "suvāno yāti" suggests that the wise intentions lead to auspicious outcomes, highlighting the power of positive speech and thought.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, a wise elder who shares knowledge and values can significantly influence the younger generation, ensuring that beneficial practices are passed down.
    • In a workplace, a leader who communicates effectively and encourages a positive environment can enhance team morale and productivity, leading to successful outcomes.
    • In community settings, individuals who engage in constructive dialogue and share wisdom can foster unity and progress, impacting the collective well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom and its role in shaping the future.
    • It teaches us that our words and intentions can have a profound impact on those around us, especially the younger generations.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more harmonious relationships, effective leadership, and a positive community atmosphere.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to be mindful of our speech and actions, as they contribute to the greater good.
