तं उ त्वा गोतमो गिरा रायस्कामो दुवस्यति |द्यम्नैर अभि प्रणोनुमः ||
tam u tvā gotamo girā rāyaskāmo duvasyati | dyumnair abhi pra ṇonumaḥ ||
  • tam u tvā: This phrase can be translated as "to you" or "to that." It signifies a direction of reverence or devotion towards a higher power or entity.
  • gotamo: This term refers to "Gautama," which is often associated with the sage or the enlightened one. In a broader sense, it can symbolize wisdom and knowledge.
  • girā: This word means "speech" or "words." It emphasizes the power of communication and the importance of expressing thoughts and ideas.
  • rāyaskāmo: This term translates to "desiring wealth" or "seeking prosperity." It reflects the human aspiration for abundance and success in life.
  • duvasyati: This word means "to be fulfilled" or "to be satisfied." It indicates the attainment of desires and the realization of goals.
  • dyumnair: This term refers to "glory" or "fame." It signifies the recognition and honor that come from achievements.
  • abhi pra ṇonumaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "we offer our praises" or "we bow down in reverence." It denotes an act of worship or acknowledgment of a higher power.

The mantra as a whole can be understood as a prayer or invocation directed towards a higher wisdom, seeking prosperity and fulfillment through the power of speech and communication. It emphasizes the importance of expressing one's desires and aspirations while recognizing the divine or enlightened presence that guides these pursuits.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Development: Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to articulate their goals and desires clearly, seeking wisdom and guidance in their journey towards success.
  • Business and Career: In professional settings, the mantra encourages open communication and collaboration, fostering an environment where ideas can flourish and prosperity can be achieved.
  • Spiritual Growth: The mantra serves as a tool for meditation and reflection, helping individuals connect with their inner selves and the greater universe, promoting a sense of fulfillment and purpose.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking wisdom, prosperity, and fulfillment through the power of words and communication. It serves as a guiding principle in various aspects of life, encouraging individuals to express their desires while remaining connected to a higher source of knowledge and enlightenment.
