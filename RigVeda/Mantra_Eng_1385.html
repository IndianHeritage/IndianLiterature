मन्त्र: चत्वारो मा मशर्शारस्य शिश्वस त्रयो राज्ञ आयवसस्य जित्नोः | रथो वाम मित्रावरुणा दीर्घाप्साः स्यूमगभस्तिः सूरो नाद्यौत ||
catvāro mā maśarśārasya śiśvas trayo rājña āyavasasya jiṣṇoḥ | ratho vām mitrāvaruṇā dīrghāpsāḥ syūmagabhastiḥ sūro nādyaut ||
  • catvāro mā maśarśārasya śiśvas trayo rājña āyavasasya jiṣṇoḥ
    • catvāro - This word means "four." It signifies completeness and stability, often representing the four directions or the four elements in various cultures.
    • mā - This translates to "not" or "do not," indicating a negation or a warning against something.
    • maśarśārasya - Refers to "the one who is armed with arrows," symbolizing a warrior or protector.
    • śiśvas - This means "to be strong" or "to be powerful," emphasizing the importance of strength in overcoming challenges.
    • trayo - Meaning "three," it can represent the triad of mind, body, and spirit or the three stages of life.
    • rājña - This translates to "of the king," indicating authority, leadership, and governance.
    • āyavasasya - Refers to "the one who is prosperous," highlighting the importance of wealth and abundance.
    • jiṣṇoḥ - This means "of the victorious," suggesting success and triumph in endeavors.
  • ratho vām mitrāvaruṇā dīrghāpsāḥ syūmagabhastiḥ sūro nādyaut
    • ratho - This means "chariot," symbolizing movement, progress, and the journey of life.
    • vām - Translates to "your," indicating a personal connection or possession.
    • mitrāvaruṇā - Refers to "Mitra and Varuna," the deities representing friendship and cosmic order, respectively.
    • dīrghāpsāḥ - This means "long-lasting," emphasizing endurance and sustainability.
    • syūmagabhastiḥ - Refers to "the one who is strong and powerful," indicating resilience and capability.
    • sūro - This translates to "the hero," representing bravery and valor.
    • nādyaut - Means "does not go away," suggesting permanence and stability in one's achievements.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, leadership, and prosperity in life. It highlights the need for resilience and the support of divine forces (Mitra and Varuna) in achieving success. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal development, where one must cultivate inner strength and seek guidance from mentors or supportive communities. The imagery of the chariot symbolizes the journey of life, reminding us to stay focused on our goals and values while navigating challenges. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to strive for victory and abundance while maintaining strong relationships and a sense of purpose.
