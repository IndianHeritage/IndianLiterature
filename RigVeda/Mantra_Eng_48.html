त्वां स्तोमा अवीवृधन त्वाम उक्था शतक्रतो |त्वां वर्धन्तु नो गिरः ||
tvāṁ stomā avīvṛdhan tvām ukthā śatakrato | tvāṁ vardhantu no giraḥ ||
  • tvāṁ stomā avīvṛdhan: This phrase can be translated as "the hymns (stomas) have revealed you." Here, "tvāṁ" refers to "you," indicating a divine presence or deity, while "stomā" refers to the praises or hymns sung in reverence. The term "avīvṛdhan" suggests the act of unveiling or revealing.
  • tvām ukthā śatakrato: This part translates to "we invoke you, O hundred-powered one." "Ukthā" means to invoke or call upon, and "śatakrato" refers to a deity with a hundred powers, often associated with Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature. This highlights the strength and capabilities of the divine being being addressed.
  • tvāṁ vardhantu no giraḥ: This can be interpreted as "may our words (giraḥ) enhance you." "Vardhantu" means to increase or enhance, and "no giraḥ" refers to "our words." This suggests a desire for the divine to be nourished or empowered by the praises and invocations of the devotees.

Conclusion:

  • The mantra emphasizes the power of hymns and invocations in connecting with the divine. It reflects the belief that through sincere praise and worship, one can reveal and invoke the presence of the divine in their lives.
  • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as during rituals, prayers, or personal meditations where individuals seek to connect with a higher power or divine energy. It serves as a reminder of the importance of words and intentions in spiritual practices.
  • Furthermore, it highlights the idea that our expressions of devotion can have a transformative effect, not only on the divine but also on ourselves, as we seek to enhance our spiritual journey through sincere worship.
