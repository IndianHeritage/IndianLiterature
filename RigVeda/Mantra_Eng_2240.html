मन्त्र: ब्रह्मणस पते त्वम् अस्य यन्ता सूक्तस्य बोधि तनयं च जिन्वा |विश्वं तद भद्रं यद अवन्ति देवाः बृहद् वदेम विदथे सुवीराः ||
brahmaṇas pate tvam asya yantā sūktasya bodhi tanayaṁ ca jinva | viśvaṁ tad bhadraṁ yad avanti devā bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ ||
  • Brahmaṇas pate: This phrase translates to "O Lord of the Brahmanas." Here, "Brahmanas" refers to the learned or wise individuals, often associated with spiritual knowledge and wisdom. In real life, this can be likened to teachers or mentors who guide others in their pursuit of knowledge.
  • Tvam asya yantā: This means "You are the one who leads." It signifies the importance of guidance and leadership in our lives. For example, a good leader in a workplace or community can inspire and direct others towards achieving common goals.
  • Sūktasya bodhi tanayaṁ ca jinva: This translates to "May you bring forth the offspring of wisdom." "Sūktasya" refers to the wise sayings or hymns, while "bodhi" means enlightenment or wisdom. This can be related to the idea of nurturing knowledge and wisdom in future generations, much like how parents and educators instill values and learning in children.
  • Viśvaṁ tad bhadraṁ: This means "May that be auspicious for all." It emphasizes the collective well-being and prosperity of all beings. In a real-life context, this can be seen in community initiatives that aim to improve the quality of life for everyone, such as environmental conservation efforts or social welfare programs.
  • Yad avanti devā: This translates to "What the gods have brought forth." It suggests a recognition of divine blessings and the importance of acknowledging higher powers in our lives. For instance, many people express gratitude for their achievements, attributing them to divine intervention or support.
  • Bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ: This means "Let us speak of the great in the assembly of the brave." It highlights the significance of courage and strength in discussions and decision-making. In real life, this can be related to forums or discussions where individuals come together to address challenges and propose solutions, showcasing bravery in the face of adversity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, guidance, collective well-being, and the recognition of divine influence in our lives. Its significance lies in the encouragement of nurturing knowledge, fostering community spirit, and promoting bravery in discussions. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in educational settings, community gatherings, and leadership roles, where the focus is on uplifting others and working towards a common good. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and enlightened society.
