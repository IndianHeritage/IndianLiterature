यत सोमो वाजम् अर्षति शतं धाराऽपस्युवः |इन्द्रस्य सख्यम आविशन् ||
yat somo vājam arṣati śataṁ dhārā apasyuvaḥ | indrasya sakhyam āviśan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yat: that which
    • somo: the soma (a sacred drink)
    • vājam: the strength or vigor
    • arṣati: that which is attained or achieved
    • śataṁ: a hundred
    • dhārā: streams or flows
    • apasyuvaḥ: those who are perceptive or wise
    • indrasya: of Indra (the king of gods)
    • sakhyam: friendship or companionship
    • āviśan: entered or manifested
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the essence of the soma, a divine drink that symbolizes immortality and enlightenment.
    • The phrase "yat somo vājam arṣati" suggests that the soma grants strength and vigor, which can be interpreted as the energy and vitality one gains from spiritual practices or knowledge.
    • The mention of "śataṁ dhārā apasyuvaḥ" indicates that those who are wise and perceptive can recognize and harness the streams of energy and wisdom that flow from this divine source.
    • Finally, "indrasya sakhyam āviśan" highlights the importance of friendship and companionship, particularly in the context of divine or spiritual relationships, suggesting that such bonds can lead to greater understanding and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of "soma" can be likened to practices that enhance mental and physical well-being, such as meditation, yoga, or even engaging in creative pursuits that invigorate the spirit.
    • The idea of "streams of wisdom" can be seen in the way knowledge is shared in communities, where individuals learn from each other, much like in study groups or collaborative projects.
    • Friendship and companionship are crucial in personal and professional settings, where support and collaboration can lead to success and fulfillment, echoing the sentiment of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of spiritual nourishment, the pursuit of wisdom, and the value of companionship.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek out practices that enhance our vitality, to remain open to learning from others, and to cultivate meaningful relationships that enrich our lives.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to recognize the divine energy that flows through our connections and experiences, guiding us towards a more enlightened existence.
