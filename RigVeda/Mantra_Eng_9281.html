यं त्वा देवाः दधिरे हव्यवाहं पुरुष्पृहो मानुषासो यजत्रम् |स यामन्न अग्ने स्तुवते वयो धाः प्र देवयन यशसः सं हि पूर्वीः ||
yaṁ tvā devā dadhire havyavāham puruspṛho mānuṣāso yajatram | sa yāmann agne stuvate vayo dhāḥ pra devayan yaśasaḥ saṁ hi pūrvīḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yaṁ tvā devā dadhire havyavāham puruspṛho mānuṣāso yajatram | sa yāmann agne stuvate vayo dhāḥ pra devayan yaśasaḥ saṁ hi pūrvīḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Whom the gods have established as the bearer of offerings, the one who is desired by many, the humans worship. That Agni, whom they praise with their hymns, may he grant us strength and glory, as he has done in the past."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • yaṁ: Whom
    • tvā: You
    • devā: Gods
    • dadhire: Have established
    • havyavāham: Bearer of offerings
    • puruspṛho: Desired by many
    • mānuṣāso: Humans
    • yajatram: Worship
    • sa: That
    • yāmann: Whom
    • agne: Agni (the fire god)
    • stuvate: Praise
    • vayo: Strength
    • dhāḥ: Grant
    • pra: For
    • devayan: Divine path
    • yaśasaḥ: Glory
    • saṁ: Together
    • hi: Indeed
    • pūrvīḥ: As in the past
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of Agni, the fire god, as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • It highlights the act of worship and the desire for strength and glory, which are universal human aspirations.
    • In real life, this can be seen in various rituals and ceremonies where fire is used as a sacred element, symbolizing purification and the presence of the divine.
    • For example, in Hindu weddings, the sacred fire (Agni) is a witness to the vows taken by the couple, signifying the importance of divine blessings in their union.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the connection between the divine and human realms, encouraging individuals to seek strength and glory through devotion and righteous actions.
    • It is applicable in various life scenarios, such as personal growth, community service, and spiritual practices, where individuals strive to achieve their goals while remaining connected to a higher purpose.
    • Ultimately, it teaches that through sincere worship and dedication, one can attain the blessings of the divine, leading to a fulfilling and meaningful life.
