तं उद्युमः पूर्वणीका होतर अग्ने अग्निभिर मनुष इधानः |स्तोमं यं अस्मै ममतेव शूषं घृतं न शुचि मतयः पवन्ते ||
tam u dyumaḥ purvaṇīka hotar agne agnibhir manuṣa idhānaḥ | stomaṁ yam asmai mamateva śūṣaṁ ghṛtaṁ na śuci matayaḥ pavante ||
  • tam u dyumaḥ purvaṇīka hotar agne agnibhir manuṣa idhānaḥ
    • tam u dyumaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which is in the sky" or "the celestial." It signifies the divine or higher realms.
    • purvaṇīka: This term refers to the "ancient" or "previous" aspects, suggesting a connection to tradition and the wisdom of the past.
    • hotar: This word means "the priest" or "the one who invokes," indicating the role of a mediator between the divine and the human.
    • agne: This is a direct address to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • agnibhir manuṣa idhānaḥ: This phrase translates to "with the fires, humans are established," highlighting the importance of fire in human life, both literally and metaphorically.
  • stomaṁ yam asmai mamateva śūṣaṁ
    • stomaṁ: This refers to "praise" or "hymn," indicating the act of worship and reverence.
    • yam asmai: This means "to whom it is directed," suggesting a focus on the divine or the object of worship.
    • mamateva: This can be interpreted as "like a mother," indicating nurturing and care.
    • śūṣaṁ: This term means "pure" or "clean," emphasizing the importance of purity in thoughts and actions.
  • ghṛtaṁ na śuci matayaḥ pavante
    • ghṛtaṁ: This refers to "ghee" or "clarified butter," which is often used in rituals and symbolizes nourishment and sustenance.
    • na śuci: This means "not impure," indicating the need for purity in intentions and actions.
    • matayaḥ pavante: This phrase translates to "thoughts that are pure and elevate," suggesting that pure thoughts lead to spiritual elevation.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the significance of connecting with the divine through rituals and pure intentions. It highlights the role of Agni as a transformative force in human life, symbolizing energy and purification. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to practices such as meditation, prayer, and rituals that promote mindfulness and purity of thought. By invoking the divine and focusing on pure intentions, individuals can foster a deeper connection with their spiritual selves and the universe, leading to personal growth and transformation.
