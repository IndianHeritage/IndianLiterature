एव पतिं द्रोणसाचं सचेतसम् ऊर्जः स्कम्भं धरुण आ वृषायसे |ओजः कृष्व सं गृभाय त्वे अप्य असो यथा केनिपानाम् इनो वृद्धे ||
evā patiṁ droṇasācaṁ sacetasam ūrjaḥ skambhaṁ dharuṇa ā vṛṣāyase | ojaḥ kṛṣva saṁ gṛbhāya tve apy aso yathā kenipānām ino vṛdhe ||
  • evā patiṁ: This phrase translates to "this lord" or "this master." It signifies a call to a higher power or divine entity, often representing the ultimate source of strength and guidance.
  • droṇasācaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is like a vessel" or "the one who holds." It symbolizes stability and support, akin to how a vessel holds water, representing the nurturing aspect of life.
  • sacetasam: Meaning "with consciousness" or "with awareness," this word emphasizes the importance of being mindful and aware in our actions and thoughts.
  • ūrjaḥ: This translates to "energy" or "vitality." It represents the life force that drives all actions and is essential for growth and development.
  • skambhaṁ: This word means "pillar" or "support." It symbolizes strength and stability, much like a pillar supports a structure, indicating the need for a strong foundation in life.
  • dharuṇa: This term can be understood as "firm" or "unyielding." It reflects resilience and the ability to withstand challenges, much like a strong tree that withstands storms.
  • ā vṛṣāyase: This phrase means "to you, the one who brings forth." It signifies the act of creation and manifestation, highlighting the importance of bringing ideas and intentions into reality.
  • ojaḥ: This translates to "strength" or "power." It emphasizes the need for inner strength to overcome obstacles and achieve goals.
  • kṛṣva: Meaning "to draw" or "to attract," this word signifies the act of pulling in energy or resources necessary for growth and success.
  • saṁ gṛbhāya: This phrase means "to grasp" or "to hold together." It emphasizes the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving common goals.
  • tve apy aso: This translates to "even you, O Asu," where "Asu" can refer to life force or breath. It signifies the connection between the divine and the life force within us.
  • yathā kenipānām: This phrase means "as if by some means." It suggests the idea of seeking assistance or guidance from a higher power in our endeavors.
  • ino vṛdhe: This translates to "in our growth." It emphasizes the importance of nurturing and supporting each other in our journeys of growth and development.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, stability, and support from a higher power while emphasizing the importance of awareness, resilience, and collaboration in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, teamwork, and spiritual growth. For instance, in a workplace setting, fostering a supportive environment where team members are aware of each other's strengths and challenges can lead to collective success. Similarly, in personal life, recognizing the need for inner strength and seeking guidance can help individuals navigate through life's challenges effectively. Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of support, and the power of collective growth.
