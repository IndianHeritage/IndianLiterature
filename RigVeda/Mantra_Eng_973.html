तं ईशानं जगतस्तस्थुषस पतिं धियं जिन्वम अवसे हूमहे वयम् |पूषा नो यथा वेदसाम असद वृधे रक्षितā पायुर अदब्धः स्वस्तये ||
tam īśānaṁ jagatas tasthuṣas patiṁ dhiyaṁjinvam avase hūmahe vayam | pūṣā no yathā vedasām asad vṛdhe rakṣitā pāyur adabdhaḥ svastaye ||
  • tam īśānaṁ: This phrase refers to the divine lord or the supreme being who governs the universe. In real life, this can be likened to the concept of a higher power or universal consciousness that many people believe in, guiding and overseeing the cosmos.
  • jagatas: This word means "of the world" or "of the universe." It emphasizes the vastness and interconnectedness of all existence. For example, in environmental discussions, we often talk about the impact of our actions on the planet, highlighting our responsibility towards the world.
  • tasthuṣas patiṁ: This translates to "the lord of the dawn." It symbolizes new beginnings and the hope that comes with each day. In daily life, this can be seen in the way people start their mornings with rituals or practices that set a positive tone for the day ahead.
  • dhiyaṁjinvam: This means "the one who inspires thought." It reflects the importance of wisdom and knowledge in our lives. For instance, educators and mentors play a crucial role in shaping our thoughts and guiding us towards enlightenment.
  • avase hūmahe vayam: This phrase can be interpreted as "we invoke or call upon." It signifies the act of seeking divine assistance or guidance. In real life, people often pray or meditate to connect with a higher power during challenging times, seeking strength and clarity.
  • pūṣā no yathā: This means "may the nourisher (Pūṣa) protect us." Pūṣa is a deity associated with nourishment and sustenance. This can be related to the importance of community support and resources that help individuals thrive, such as family, friends, and social networks.
  • vedasām asad vṛdhe: This translates to "in the growth of knowledge." It emphasizes the pursuit of wisdom and understanding. In modern society, this can be seen in the value placed on education and lifelong learning as essential for personal and professional development.
  • rakṣitā pāyur: This means "may we be protected." It highlights the desire for safety and security in life. In real life, this can be reflected in the various measures people take to ensure their well-being, such as health insurance, safety protocols, and community support systems.
  • adabdhaḥ svastaye: This translates to "for the well-being." It signifies the ultimate goal of seeking peace and prosperity. In everyday life, this can be seen in the efforts individuals and communities make to promote health, happiness, and harmony, such as through charitable work or community service.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound invocation for divine guidance, protection, and the pursuit of knowledge. Its significance lies in the recognition of a higher power that nurtures and sustains life, urging individuals to seek wisdom and support from their communities. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire people to engage in practices that promote well-being, foster connections, and contribute positively to society, ultimately leading to a harmonious existence.
