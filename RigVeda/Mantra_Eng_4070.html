सजूर विश्वेभिर देवभिर अश्विभ्यां उषसा सजूः |आ याह्य अग्ने अत्रिवत सुत रेण ||
sajūr viśvebhir devebhir aśvibhyām uṣasā sajūḥ | ā yāhy agne atrivat sute raṇa ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sajūr: united, together
    • viśvebhir: with all, by all
    • devebhir: with the gods
    • aśvibhyām: with the Ashvins (the twin gods)
    • uṣasā: with the dawn
    • sajūḥ: united, together
    • ā: come, approach
    • yāhy: let us go
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • atrivat: like this, in this manner
    • sute: in the offering, in the sacrifice
    • raṇa: in battle, in conflict
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the unity of all beings and deities, emphasizing the importance of collective effort and harmony.
    • The mention of the Ashvins signifies the importance of health, vitality, and the duality of life, as they are often associated with healing and protection.
    • Agni, the fire god, represents transformation, energy, and the sacredness of offerings, highlighting the significance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
    • The call to "come" and "let us go" suggests a journey or a quest, which can be interpreted as a call to action for individuals and communities to unite in purpose.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied during festivals or gatherings where people come together to celebrate and honor their traditions, fostering unity and cooperation.
    • In a corporate environment, it can symbolize teamwork and collaboration, where individuals work together towards a common goal, much like the collective effort of the gods and beings in the mantra.
    • In personal life, it can inspire individuals to seek harmony in relationships, emphasizing the importance of understanding and support among family and friends.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the power of unity and collective action, reminding us that we are stronger together.
    • It encourages us to seek the blessings of the divine in our endeavors, whether in personal, social, or professional contexts.
    • By invoking the energies of Agni and the Ashvins, we are reminded of the importance of transformation, health, and the sacredness of our actions.
    • Ultimately, this mantra serves as a timeless reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of working together for a greater purpose.
