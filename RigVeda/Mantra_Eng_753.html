यद वातजूतो वनाः व्यस्थाद अग्निः ह दाति रोमाः पृथिव्याः ||
yad vātajūto vanā vy asthād agnir ha dāti romā pṛthivyāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yad vātajūto vanā vy asthād agnir ha dāti romā pṛthivyāḥ"
  • Translation: "Whatever is born from the wind, whatever is established in the forest, fire gives it strength and vitality on the earth."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • yad: whatever
    • vātajūto: born from the wind
    • vanā: in the forest
    • vy asthād: established
    • agnir: fire
    • ha: indeed
    • dāti: gives
    • romā: strength/vitality
    • pṛthivyāḥ: of the earth

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of nature and the elements.
    • The phrase "born from the wind" can be interpreted as the life force or energy that is carried by the wind, which is essential for growth and sustenance.
    • "Established in the forest" signifies the importance of nature as a nurturing environment where life flourishes.
    • The mention of "fire" symbolizes transformation, energy, and the vital force that empowers all living beings.
    • Finally, "gives it strength and vitality on the earth" emphasizes the role of these elements in providing life and sustenance to all forms of existence.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the wind helps in pollination, which is crucial for the growth of plants. The forest provides a habitat for various species, and fire can be used to clear land for new growth, demonstrating the cycle of life.
    • In personal development, one can draw parallels to the mantra by recognizing that challenges (the wind) can lead to growth (the forest), and the energy (fire) we harness from these experiences can empower us to thrive in our lives.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of the natural elements and their roles in sustaining life.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the interconnectedness of all things and the importance of nurturing our environment.
    • In real life, it encourages us to harness our experiences and the energy around us to foster growth and vitality, both personally and collectively.
