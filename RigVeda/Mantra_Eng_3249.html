कियत स्विद इन्द्रो अध्य एति मातुः कियत पितुर जनितुर यो जजाना |यो अस्य शुष्मं मुहुकैर इयर्ति वातो न जूतः स्तनयद्भिर अभ्रैः ||
kiyat svid indro adhy eti mātuḥ kiyat pitur janitur yo jajāna | yo asya śuṣmam muhukair iyarti vāto na jūtaḥ stanayadbhir abhraiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "kiyat svid indro adhy eti mātuḥ kiyat pitur janitur yo jajāna | yo asya śuṣmam muhukair iyarti vāto na jūtaḥ stanayadbhir abhraiḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kiyat: how much
    • svid: where
    • indro: Indra (the king of gods)
    • adhy: today
    • eti: comes
    • mātuḥ: of the mother
    • kiyat: how much
    • pitur: of the father
    • janitur: of the one who gives birth
    • yo: who
    • jajāna: has been born
    • yo: who
    • asya: of this
    • śuṣmam: dry
    • muhukair: with the winds
    • iyarti: moves
    • vāto: the wind
    • na: not
    • jūtaḥ: bound
    • stanayadbhir: by the breasts
    • abhraiḥ: by the clouds
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a series of questions about the nature of existence and the divine.
    • It reflects on the roles of the mother and father, emphasizing the importance of parental figures in our lives.
    • The mention of Indra suggests a connection to divine power and protection, indicating that the divine is ever-present in our lives.
    • The imagery of the wind and clouds symbolizes the unseen forces that influence our lives, much like the challenges and blessings we encounter.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, the roles of parents can be seen as foundational to a child's development, much like the mantra suggests.
    • In times of uncertainty, individuals often seek guidance from spiritual or divine sources, reflecting the quest for understanding and support from higher powers.
    • The metaphor of the wind can be likened to life's unpredictable nature, where we must adapt and move forward despite challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of understanding our origins and the divine forces that shape our lives.
    • It encourages reflection on the roles of our parents and the unseen influences that guide us.
    • In real life, this can be applicable in moments of introspection, family gatherings, or spiritual practices where one seeks to connect with their roots and the divine.
