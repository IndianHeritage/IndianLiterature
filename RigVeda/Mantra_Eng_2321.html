अहेळतां मनसा श्रुष्टिम आवह दूहानां धेनुम पिप्युषीम असश्चतम् |पद्याभिर आशुं वचसा च वाजिनं त्वां हिनोमि पुरुहूत विश्वहा ||
aheḻatā manasā śruṣṭim ā vaha duhānāṁ dhenum pipyuṣīm asaścatam | padyābhir āśuṁ vacasā ca vājinaṁ tvāṁ hinomi puruhūta viśvahā ||
  • Mantra: "aheḻatā manasā śruṣṭim ā vaha duhānāṁ dhenum pipyuṣīm asaścatam | padyābhir āśuṁ vacasā ca vājinaṁ tvāṁ hinomi puruhūta viśvahā ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine for the manifestation of abundance and prosperity. It expresses a desire for the flow of nourishment and the fulfillment of needs through the power of thought and speech.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • aheḻatā: Refers to the act of bringing forth or manifesting.
    • manasā: Means 'with the mind' or 'through thought.'
    • śruṣṭim: Refers to creation or manifestation.
    • ā vaha: Means 'bring forth' or 'carry forth.'
    • duhānāṁ: Refers to the act of milking or extracting nourishment.
    • dhenum: Means 'the cow,' symbolizing abundance and sustenance.
    • pipyuṣīm: Refers to 'nourishing' or 'rich in essence.'
    • asaścatam: Means 'to be fulfilled' or 'to be satisfied.'
    • padyābhir: Refers to 'with words' or 'through speech.'
    • āśuṁ: Means 'quickly' or 'swiftly.'
    • vacasā: Refers to 'with speech' or 'through words.'
    • ca: Means 'and.'
    • vājinaṁ: Refers to 'the one who brings strength' or 'the powerful.'
    • tvāṁ: Means 'you,' addressing the divine.
    • hinomi: Means 'I invoke' or 'I call upon.'
    • puruhūta: Refers to 'the one who is invoked many times' or 'the widely praised.'
    • viśvahā: Means 'the one who carries all' or 'the universal.'
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be seen as a call for creativity and innovation. Entrepreneurs often visualize their goals and use affirmations to manifest success.
    • In personal life, individuals may use this mantra to focus their thoughts on abundance, leading to a mindset that attracts opportunities and resources.
    • In community settings, this mantra can inspire collective efforts to bring forth prosperity, such as community gardens or cooperative businesses that nourish all members.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the power of thought and speech in manifesting abundance and prosperity. It teaches us that our intentions and words can shape our reality. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal aspirations, business ventures, or community initiatives, invoking this mantra can serve as a reminder to focus on positive outcomes and to actively seek nourishment and fulfillment in various aspects of life. By aligning our thoughts and words with our desires, we can create a more abundant and prosperous existence.
