उच्छन्त्यां मे यजतā देवकṣatre रुशद्गवि | सुतं सोमं न हस्तिभिर आपड्भिर धावतं नराऽ बिभ्रताव् अर्चनानसम् ||
ucchantyām me yajatā devakṣatre ruśadgavi | sutaṁ somaṁ na hastibhir ā paḍbhir dhāvataṁ narā bibhratāv arcanānasam ||
  • ucchantyām me yajatā devakṣatre: This phrase can be translated as "In the divine assembly, may my offerings be accepted." Here, "ucchantyām" refers to the act of being uplifted or elevated, while "yajatā" means "by the worshippers." "Devakṣatre" signifies the divine realm or assembly of gods.
  • ruśadgavi: This term can be interpreted as "in the presence of the powerful." "Ruśad" refers to strength or power, and "gavi" can denote a cow, which is often a symbol of wealth and nourishment in Vedic literature.
  • sutaṁ somaṁ: This translates to "the son of Soma." In Vedic texts, Soma is both a deity and a sacred drink that is believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. "Sutaṁ" refers to the offspring or product, indicating the importance of Soma in rituals.
  • na hastibhir ā paḍbhir: This phrase means "not by the hands of elephants." Here, "hastibhir" refers to elephants, which are often symbols of strength and power, while "ā paḍbhir" suggests a sense of movement or action.
  • dhāvataṁ narā bibhratāv arcanānasam: This can be interpreted as "may the men who carry out the rituals be blessed." "Dhāvataṁ" means "those who run or strive," "narā" refers to men or humans, and "bibhratāv" indicates carrying or holding. "Arcanānasam" relates to the act of worship or ritual offerings.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine acceptance of offerings made by worshippers. It highlights the role of strength and power in the divine assembly and the significance of Soma in rituals. The mention of elephants suggests that true strength comes not from brute force but from the sincerity of worship and the intent behind the offerings.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during religious ceremonies, personal prayers, or community gatherings. It serves as a reminder that the essence of worship lies in the purity of intention and the collective effort of individuals striving for spiritual elevation. For instance, in a community festival, the collective offerings made with devotion can be seen as a modern-day interpretation of this mantra, where the focus is on unity and shared purpose rather than individual power.
