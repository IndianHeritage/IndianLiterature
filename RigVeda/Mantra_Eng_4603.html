तं पृच्छन्तो 'वरासः पराणि प्रात्ना त इन्द्र श्रुत्यानु येमुः |अर्चामसि वीर ब्रह्मवाहो याद एव विद्म तात त्वा महान्तम् ||
tam pṛcchanto 'varāsaḥ parāṇi pratnā ta indra śrutyānu yemuḥ | arcāmasi vīra brahmavāho yād eva vidma tāt tvā mahāntam ||
  • tam pṛcchanto 'varāsaḥ parāṇi pratnā ta indra śrutyānu yemuḥ
    • tam pṛcchanto: This phrase translates to "those who inquire" or "those who seek." It emphasizes the importance of asking questions and seeking knowledge.
    • ‘varāsaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "the best" or "the most excellent." It signifies the pursuit of the highest truth or wisdom.
    • parāṇi pratnā: This means "the ultimate truths" or "the supreme realities." It suggests that there are deeper truths that one must strive to understand.
    • ta indra: Referring to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature, this part invokes divine guidance and strength in the quest for knowledge.
    • śrutyānu yemuḥ: This translates to "by listening to the revealed knowledge." It highlights the importance of tradition and teachings passed down through generations.
  • arcāmasi vīra brahmavāho:
    • arcāmasi: This means "we worship" or "we offer our reverence." It indicates an act of devotion and respect towards the divine.
    • vīra: This translates to "the hero" or "the brave one." It signifies the qualities of courage and valor that are essential in the pursuit of truth.
    • brahmavāho: This term means "one who carries the ultimate reality" or "the bearer of Brahman." It refers to the divine essence that connects all beings.
  • yād eva vidma tāt tvā mahāntam:
    • yād eva vidma: This translates to "that which we know." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in our spiritual journey.
    • tāt tvā mahāntam: This means "to you, the great one." It is a direct address to the divine, acknowledging its greatness and significance in our lives.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking knowledge and understanding the ultimate truths of existence. It emphasizes the importance of inquiry, reverence, and the pursuit of wisdom, guided by divine strength. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In education, where students are encouraged to ask questions and seek deeper understanding.
  • In personal development, where individuals strive to uncover their true potential and purpose.
  • In spiritual practices, where devotees seek connection with the divine through worship and reverence.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of knowledge, courage, and devotion in our journey through life, guiding us towards a greater understanding of ourselves and the universe.
