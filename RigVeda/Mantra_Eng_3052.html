गृणानां जमदग्निना योनाव ऋतस्य सीदतम् |पातं सोमं ऋतावृद्धा ||
gṛṇānā jamadagninā yonāv ṛtasya sīdatam | pātaṁ somam ṛtāvṛdhā ||
  • gṛṇānā: This word can be translated as "singing" or "praising." It signifies the act of expressing reverence or gratitude, often through hymns or chants.
  • jamadagninā: This term refers to "Jamadagni," a sage known for his wisdom and power. In this context, it symbolizes the connection to divine knowledge and the importance of seeking guidance from enlightened beings.
  • yonāv: This word means "womb" or "source." It represents the origin of life and the nurturing aspect of nature, emphasizing the importance of creation and sustenance.
  • ṛtasya: This translates to "of truth" or "of order." It signifies the cosmic order and the principles that govern the universe, highlighting the importance of living in harmony with these truths.
  • sīdatam: This word means "may it settle" or "may it be established." It expresses a desire for stability and permanence in the context of truth and order.
  • pātaṁ: This translates to "let it flow" or "let it come." It indicates a wish for abundance and the flow of positive energies or blessings.
  • somam: This refers to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition that symbolizes immortality and divine nourishment. It represents the essence of life and spiritual enlightenment.
  • ṛtāvṛdhā: This term means "in the increase of truth" or "in the growth of order." It emphasizes the importance of nurturing and expanding the principles of truth and righteousness in our lives.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, establishing truth, and nurturing life. It encourages individuals to praise and connect with higher wisdom, recognize the importance of cosmic order, and strive for stability and abundance in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to seek knowledge and wisdom from mentors or spiritual guides.
  • In community settings, it can inspire collective efforts to promote truth, harmony, and sustainability, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
  • In environmental practices, the emphasis on nurturing the source of life can encourage sustainable living and respect for nature.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its call to align oneself with the principles of truth and order, fostering a life that is abundant, harmonious, and spiritually enriched.
