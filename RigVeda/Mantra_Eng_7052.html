सम इन्द्रो रायो बृहतीर अधूनुत सं क्षोणी सम उ सूर्यं |सं शुक्रासः शुचयः सं गवाशिरः सोमाः इन्द्रं अमन्दिषुः ||
sam indro rāyo bṛhatīr adhūnuta saṁ kṣoṇī sam u sūryam | saṁ śukrāsaḥ śucayaḥ saṁ gavāśiraḥ somā indram amandiṣuḥ ||
  • Mantra: "sam indro rāyo bṛhatīr adhūnuta saṁ kṣoṇī sam u sūryam | saṁ śukrāsaḥ śucayaḥ saṁ gavāśiraḥ somā indram amandiṣuḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sam: together, in harmony
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods, representing strength and power
    • rāyo: wealth, prosperity
    • bṛhatīr: great, vast
    • adhūnuta: may he inspire, may he lead
    • saṁ: together, in unity
    • kṣoṇī: earth, land
    • u: and
    • sūryam: sun, light
    • śukrāsaḥ: pure, bright
    • śucayaḥ: clean, sacred
    • gavāśiraḥ: the abode of cows, symbolizing nourishment and wealth
    • somā: soma, a sacred drink, representing immortality and divine energy
    • indram: Indra, again referring to the divine power
    • amandiṣuḥ: may he be pleased, may he grant blessings
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, who is often associated with strength and prosperity.
    • The phrase "sam indro rāyo bṛhatīr" emphasizes the collective strength and vastness of wealth that can be achieved when people come together.
    • In real life, this can be seen in community efforts, such as crowdfunding for a local project or cooperative farming, where individuals unite for a common goal, leading to greater prosperity.
    • The mention of "kṣoṇī" and "sūryam" signifies the importance of the earth and the sun, which are essential for agriculture and life. This reflects the interconnectedness of nature and human existence.
    • "śukrāsaḥ" and "śucayaḥ" highlight the need for purity and cleanliness, which can be related to environmental sustainability and the importance of maintaining a clean habitat for future generations.
    • The reference to "gavāśiraḥ" symbolizes nourishment and abundance, akin to the importance of livestock in agrarian societies, which provide food and resources.
    • Finally, the invocation of "somā indram amandiṣuḥ" suggests a plea for divine blessings and support, which can be interpreted as a call for guidance and strength in our endeavors.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity, the significance of nature, and the importance of purity in our lives.
    • In a modern context, it can inspire collective action towards sustainability, community building, and the pursuit of prosperity through cooperation.
    • By invoking the blessings of Indra, it encourages individuals to seek strength and guidance in their personal and communal endeavors, fostering a sense of responsibility towards each other and the environment.
