मन्त्र: सत्रा सोमाऽभवन् अस्य विश्वे सत्रा मदासो बृहतो मदीष्ठाः | सत्राभवो वसुपतिर वसूनां दात्रे विश्वा अधिता इन्द्र कृष्टीः ||
satrā somā abhavann asya viśve satrā madāso bṛhato madiṣṭhāḥ | satrābhavo vasupatir vasūnāṁ datre viśvā adhithā indra kṛṣṭīḥ ||
  • Mantra: "satrā somā abhavann asya viśve satrā madāso bṛhato madiṣṭhāḥ | satrābhavo vasupatir vasūnāṁ datre viśvā adhithā indra kṛṣṭīḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • satrā: gatherings or assemblies
    • somā: the moon or soma, often associated with nourishment and immortality
    • abhavann: have arisen or have come into being
    • asya: of this
    • viśve: all or universal
    • madāso: intoxicated or filled with joy
    • bṛhato: of the great or vast
    • madiṣṭhāḥ: those who are in the highest position or status
    • vasupatir: lord of wealth or prosperity
    • vasūnāṁ: of the wealth or riches
    • datre: giver or bestower
    • viśvā: all or everything
    • adhithā: has established or set
    • indra: the king of gods, associated with strength and power
    • kṛṣṭīḥ: cultivation or prosperity
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the gatherings that arise from the essence of the moon (soma), symbolizing nourishment and joy.
    • The phrase "satrā somā abhavann" suggests that these assemblies are not just physical gatherings but also spiritual and emotional connections that bring people together.
    • In real life, this can be likened to community events, family gatherings, or festivals where people come together to celebrate and share joy, much like the full moon celebrations in various cultures.
    • The mention of "vasupatir vasūnāṁ" indicates the presence of a divine force that bestows wealth and prosperity, akin to how a successful business or a thriving community can uplift its members.
    • Furthermore, "indra kṛṣṭīḥ" emphasizes the importance of cultivation, not just in agriculture but in nurturing relationships and fostering growth in various aspects of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of community, joy, and prosperity in our lives.
    • It teaches us that gatherings, whether they are family reunions, cultural festivals, or community service events, play a crucial role in our emotional and spiritual well-being.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages us to cultivate not only our physical surroundings but also our relationships and connections with others, leading to a more fulfilling and prosperous life.
    • Thus, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of coming together to celebrate life and share in the abundance that surrounds us.
