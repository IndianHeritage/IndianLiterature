न तं तिग्मं चन त्यजो न द्रासद अभि तं गुरु |यस्मा उ शर्म सप्रथा आदित्यासो अराध्वम् अनहसो व ऊतयः सुतयः व ऊतयः ||
na taṁ tigmaṁ cana tyajo na drāsad abhi taṁ guru | yasmā u śarma sapratha ādityāso arādhvam anehaso va ūtayaḥ suūtayo va ūtayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na: not
    • taṁ: that
    • tigmaṁ: sharp or intense
    • cana: at all
    • tyajo: abandon or give up
    • na: not
    • drāsad: to be frightened or disturbed
    • abhi: towards
    • taṁ: that
    • guru: teacher or guide
    • yasmā: from whom
    • u: indeed
    • śarma: comfort or protection
    • sapratha: together with others
    • ādityāso: the sun or solar deities
    • arādhvam: worship or honor
    • anehaso: without darkness or ignorance
    • va: or
    • ūtayaḥ: those who are elevated or exalted
    • suūtayo: those who are well-versed or knowledgeable
    • va: or
    • ūtayaḥ: those who are elevated or exalted
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of not abandoning the guidance of a wise teacher, even in the face of challenges or fears.
    • It suggests that true comfort and protection come from the wisdom imparted by such a teacher.
    • The reference to the sun symbolizes enlightenment, knowledge, and the dispelling of ignorance.
    • Worshiping the sun or solar deities signifies the pursuit of higher knowledge and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a student's life, a mentor or teacher plays a crucial role in guiding them through academic challenges. Abandoning their guidance can lead to confusion and setbacks.
    • In professional settings, seeking advice from experienced colleagues can illuminate paths to success, especially when facing difficult decisions.
    • In personal development, engaging with spiritual leaders or life coaches can provide clarity and direction, helping individuals navigate through life's complexities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of mentorship and guidance in our lives.
    • It highlights the importance of seeking knowledge and wisdom from those who are experienced, especially during challenging times.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to honor and worship the sources of enlightenment, such as teachers, mentors, and spiritual guides, as they lead us towards a more fulfilling and enlightened existence.
