मन्त्र: अस्ति सोमो अयं सुतः पिबन्त्य अस्य मरुतः | उता स्वराजो अश्विनाः ||
asti somo ayaṁ sutaḥ pibanty asya marutaḥ | uta svarājo aśvinā ||
  • Translation: "This is the soma, the drink of immortality, which the Maruts (storm deities) drink; and the Aśvins (divine twin horsemen) are the ones who bring it."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Asti: This means "is" or "exists," indicating the presence of something significant.
    • Somo: Refers to "soma," a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration.
    • Ayaṁ: Translates to "this," pointing to the specific soma being referred to.
    • Sutaḥ: Means "the drink" or "the beverage," emphasizing the act of consuming the soma.
    • Pibanty: Means "they drink," indicating the action of consumption by the deities.
    • Asya: Means "of this," referring back to the soma being discussed.
    • Marutaḥ: Refers to the "Maruts," the storm gods associated with strength and vitality.
    • Uta: Means "and" or "also," connecting the two parts of the mantra.
    • Svarājo: Refers to "the Aśvins," who are known as the divine horsemen and healers.
    • Aśvinā: The plural form of Aśvin, indicating the two deities together.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of "soma" can be likened to modern-day rituals or practices that promote well-being and vitality, such as meditation or yoga, which are believed to enhance one's life force.
    • The Maruts can be compared to natural forces like storms or winds that bring change and renewal, similar to how challenges in life can lead to personal growth.
    • The Aśvins symbolize the duality of life, representing balance and harmony, much like the importance of teamwork and collaboration in achieving success in various fields.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of divine nourishment and the transformative power of nature. It highlights the importance of seeking vitality and inspiration from higher sources, whether through spiritual practices or the natural world. In real life, this can be applied by recognizing the forces that rejuvenate us, whether they be through community, nature, or personal growth practices. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek out and embrace the sources of strength and vitality in our lives, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and the world around us.
