आ सोतā परि षिञ्चताश्वं न स्तोमम् अप्तुरं रजस्तुरम् | वनकृष्कं उदप्रुतम् ||
ā sotā pari ṣiñcatāśvaṁ na stomam apturaṁ rajasturam | vanakrakṣam udaprutam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā sotā: "from the source" or "from the flow"
    • pari: "around" or "surrounding"
    • ṣiñcatā: "pouring" or "sprinkling"
    • śvaṁ: "the horse"
    • na: "not"
    • stomam: "praise" or "hymn"
    • apturaṁ: "attained" or "achieved"
    • rajasturam: "the one who is full of energy" or "the one who is vibrant"
    • vanakrakṣam: "the protector of the forest"
    • udaprutam: "having risen above" or "having emerged"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine source that nourishes and energizes.
    • The imagery of a horse suggests strength, vitality, and the ability to move forward.
    • The phrase "not the hymn attained" implies that true praise or recognition is not merely about words but about actions and results.
    • The mention of "the protector of the forest" indicates a connection to nature and the importance of safeguarding our environment.
    • Overall, the mantra emphasizes the importance of nurturing our strengths and the environment around us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the importance of sustainable living. Just as the mantra speaks of protecting the forest, we can take steps to protect our environment by reducing waste and conserving resources.
    • The strength of the horse can symbolize personal growth and resilience. For instance, individuals facing challenges in their careers can draw inspiration from this mantra to persevere and achieve their goals.
    • In community settings, this mantra can inspire collective action towards environmental conservation, encouraging groups to come together to protect local ecosystems.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, nature, and the importance of genuine actions over mere words.
    • Its significance lies in encouraging individuals and communities to nurture their strengths while also being responsible stewards of the environment.
    • In a world facing numerous environmental challenges, the teachings of this mantra can guide us towards a more harmonious and sustainable way of living.
