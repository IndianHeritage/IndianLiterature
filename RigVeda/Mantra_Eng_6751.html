आ नो गव्याण्य अश्व्याः सहस्राः शूर दर्धृहि |दिवो अमुष्य शासतो दिवं यय दिवावसो ||
ā no gavyāny aśvyā sahasrā śūra dardṛhi | divo amuṣya śāsato divaṁ yaya divāvaso ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: towards
    • no: our
    • gavyāny: cows (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • aśvyā: horses (symbolizing strength and power)
    • sahasrā: thousands
    • śūra: brave or valiant
    • dardṛhi: may you protect or take care
    • divo: of the heavens
    • amuṣya: of that (referring to the divine)
    • śāsato: of the ruler or authority
    • divaṁ: heaven
    • yaya: by which
    • divāvaso: those who dwell in the day (referring to the enlightened or wise)
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer for the protection and abundance of our resources, symbolized by cows and horses.
    • It emphasizes the importance of bravery and strength in achieving success and prosperity.
    • The reference to the heavens suggests a connection to divine guidance and authority.
    • It calls upon the enlightened beings who dwell in the light of knowledge to aid in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers pray for the protection of their livestock and crops, similar to the invocation of cows and horses in the mantra.
    • In business, entrepreneurs seek strength and bravery to face challenges, akin to the qualities of the 'śūra' mentioned.
    • In personal development, individuals strive for enlightenment and wisdom, paralleling the 'divāvaso' who dwell in knowledge.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of strength, wisdom, and divine protection in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to professional success, reminding us to seek guidance and protection in our endeavors.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to cultivate bravery and wisdom while being mindful of the resources we have, ensuring their protection and abundance.
