अमी ये देवाः स्थन त्रिष्वा रोचने दिवः |कद व ऋतं कद अनृतं क्व प्रत्ना व आहुतिर वित्तं मे अस्य रोदसी ||
amī ye devāḥ sthana triṣv ā rocane divaḥ | kad va ṛtaṁ kad anṛtaṁ kva pratnā va āhutir vittam me asya rodasī ||
  • amī ye devāḥ - "These gods" refers to the divine beings or deities that are revered in Vedic tradition. In real life, this can symbolize the various forces of nature or principles that guide our lives.
  • sthana triṣv ā rocane divaḥ - "In the three realms of heaven" indicates the three worlds: the physical, the mental, and the spiritual. This can be related to how we navigate our existence across different dimensions of life.
  • kad va ṛtaṁ kad anṛtaṁ - "When is truth, when is untruth?" This line raises questions about morality and ethics, prompting us to reflect on our actions and their consequences. In everyday life, this can be seen in situations where we must choose between honesty and deceit.
  • kva pratnā va āhutir - "Where is the offering or invocation?" This suggests the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine. In modern contexts, this can be likened to the practices of gratitude and mindfulness that help us appreciate life.
  • vittam me asya rodasī - "Wealth is in this expanse." This line emphasizes the abundance of resources available to us in the universe. In practical terms, it can be interpreted as recognizing and utilizing the opportunities and wealth around us, whether material or spiritual.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound philosophical inquiries about truth, morality, and the divine connection in our lives. Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to seek truth, engage in meaningful practices, and recognize the abundance in their surroundings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire ethical decision-making, foster a sense of gratitude, and promote a deeper understanding of our place in the universe.
