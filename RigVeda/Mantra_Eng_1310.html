युवं च्यवामन अश्विनाऽजरन्तं पुनर्युवानं चक्रतुḥ शचीभिः |युवो रथं दुहितां सूर्यस्य सह श्रीया नासत्यावृणीत ||
yuvaṁ cyavānam aśvinā jarantam punar yuvānaṁ cakrathuḥ śacībhiḥ | yuvo rathaṁ duhitā sūryasya saha śriyā nāsatyāvṛṇīta ||
  • yuvaṁ: This word means "young" or "youthful." It signifies vitality and energy, often associated with the potential for growth and new beginnings.
  • cyavānam: This translates to "falling" or "declining." It can represent the challenges or setbacks that youth may face in their journey.
  • aśvinā: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing and restorative powers. They symbolize hope and rejuvenation.
  • jarantam: This means "aging" or "growing old." It highlights the inevitable passage of time and the wisdom that comes with it.
  • punar: This word means "again" or "once more," indicating renewal or a second chance.
  • yuvānaṁ: This is another form of "youth," emphasizing the idea of being young and full of potential.
  • cakrathuḥ: This translates to "may you create" or "may you establish," suggesting action and the importance of taking initiative.
  • śacībhiḥ: This word means "with strength" or "with power," indicating the support and resources available to the youth.
  • yuvo: Again referring to the youth, reinforcing the focus on the younger generation.
  • rathaṁ: This means "chariot," symbolizing the journey of life and the means to navigate through challenges.
  • duhitā: This translates to "daughter," representing the nurturing aspect of life and the importance of family.
  • sūryasya: Meaning "of the sun," it signifies light, energy, and life-giving force.
  • saha: This means "together" or "with," emphasizing collaboration and unity.
  • śriyā: This translates to "prosperity" or "wealth," indicating the rewards of hard work and dedication.
  • nāsatyāvṛṇīta: This means "may the Nāsatyas (Aśvins) choose," invoking the divine for guidance and support in the journey of life.

In summary, this mantra speaks to the journey of youth, acknowledging the challenges of aging and decline while emphasizing the potential for renewal and growth. It calls upon the divine Aśvins to support and guide the youth in their endeavors, encouraging them to take action with strength and unity. The imagery of the chariot suggests that life is a journey that requires direction and purpose, and the mention of the sun symbolizes hope and vitality.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects of life, such as education, career development, and personal growth. It serves as a reminder for young individuals to embrace their potential, seek guidance, and work collaboratively towards their goals. The significance lies in the encouragement to face challenges with resilience and to recognize the importance of support from both the divine and the community.
