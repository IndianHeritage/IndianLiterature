वयं इन्द्र त्वायवोऽभि प्रणोनुमो वृषन् |विद्धी त्व अस्य नो वसो ||
vayam indra tvāyavo 'bhi pra ṇonumo vṛṣan | viddhī tv a1sya no vaso ||
  • Mantra: "vayam indra tvāyavo 'bhi pra ṇonumo vṛṣan | viddhī tv a1sya no vaso ||"
  • Translation: "We, O Indra, invoke you with our prayers; you are the strong one. Know this, O Indra, our protector."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vayam: We
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods, representing strength and power)
    • tvāyavo: to you (indicating devotion and direction of prayer)
    • abhi: with (indicating the manner of invocation)
    • pra ṇonumo: we praise or invoke
    • vṛṣan: strong or powerful
    • viddhī: know or understand
    • tv a1sya: this (referring to the speaker's needs or desires)
    • no: our
    • vaso: protector or guardian
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards Indra, who is often seen as a deity of strength, rain, and thunder in Vedic literature.
    • The invocation reflects a deep sense of reliance on divine strength and protection, which can be paralleled in modern life when individuals seek guidance or support during challenging times.
    • For example, during a difficult project at work, one might invoke the strength of a mentor or leader, similar to how the mantra seeks the strength of Indra.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra can be applied in various life situations where one seeks strength and protection, such as during exams, competitions, or personal challenges.
    • In a community setting, invoking collective strength can lead to successful outcomes, much like how teams rally together for a common goal.
    • It serves as a reminder to acknowledge and seek support from those who embody strength, whether they are mentors, leaders, or even spiritual guides.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine strength and protection, emphasizing the importance of seeking help beyond oneself.
    • In a world filled with challenges, this mantra encourages individuals to recognize their vulnerabilities and the power of collective strength.
    • Ultimately, it teaches that invoking support, whether from the divine or from our community, can lead to resilience and success in our endeavors.
