मात्रे नु ते सुमिते इन्द्र पूर्वी द्यौर मञ्जनā पृथिवी काव्येन |वराय ते घृतवन्तः सुतासः स्वाद्मन भवन्तु पीतये मधूनि ||
mātre nu te sumite indra pūrvī dyaur majmanā pṛthivī kāvyena | varāya te ghṛtavantaḥ sutāsaḥ svādman bhavantu pītaye madhūni ||
  • mātre nu te sumite: This phrase can be translated as "O Indra, may we be united with you, like a mother." Here, the term "mātre" (mother) signifies nurturing and protection, suggesting a deep bond and reliance on divine guidance.
  • indra pūrvī dyaur: "Indra, the first of the heavens." Indra is often regarded as the king of gods and a symbol of strength and power. This line emphasizes the importance of divine support in our endeavors.
  • majmanā pṛthivī kāvyena: This translates to "may the earth, through our actions, be enriched." It highlights the idea that our deeds can enhance the world around us, suggesting a reciprocal relationship between humanity and nature.
  • varāya te ghṛtavantaḥ sutāsaḥ: "May your sons, who are rich in ghee (nourishment), be blessed." Ghee symbolizes purity and nourishment, indicating that the blessings we seek are tied to our ability to provide sustenance and care for others.
  • svādman bhavantu pītaye madhūni: "May they enjoy the sweetness of life." This line speaks to the desire for joy and fulfillment, suggesting that the ultimate goal of our actions is to create a life filled with happiness and abundance.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, nurturing relationships, and the importance of our actions in enriching the world. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as family dynamics, community service, and environmental stewardship. By fostering connections with others and acting with intention, we can create a harmonious existence that benefits not only ourselves but also the larger community. The mantra serves as a reminder of our responsibilities and the potential for joy that arises from living a life of purpose and care.
