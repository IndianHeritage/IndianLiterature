अग्नि पूर्वा अनूषसो विभावसो दीदetha विश्वदर्शतः |असि ग्रामेष्व अवितā पुरोहितो 'सि यज्ञेषु मानवः ||
agne pūrvā anūṣaso vibhāvaso dīdetha viśvadarśataḥ | asi grāmeṣv avitā purohito 'si yajñeṣu mānuṣaḥ ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god, who is a central figure in Vedic rituals and symbolizes purity, energy, and transformation.
  • Pūrvā: Meaning "of the past" or "earlier," it signifies the importance of tradition and the wisdom passed down through generations.
  • Anūṣaso: This term translates to "of the dawn," representing new beginnings, hope, and the arrival of light after darkness.
  • Vibhāvaso: This word means "illuminating" or "radiant," emphasizing the enlightening qualities of Agni and the knowledge that comes with enlightenment.
  • Dīdetha: This means "may you shine" or "may you illuminate," a call for Agni to bring light and clarity to our lives.
  • Viśvadarśataḥ: This term translates to "of universal vision," suggesting a broader perspective and understanding of the world around us.
  • Asi: Meaning "you are," it acknowledges the presence and power of Agni in our lives.
  • Grāmeṣv: This word means "in the villages," indicating the importance of community and collective rituals.
  • Avitā: This translates to "protector" or "guardian," highlighting Agni's role in safeguarding the community.
  • Purohito: Meaning "the priest" or "the one who leads the rituals," it signifies the importance of guidance and leadership in spiritual practices.
  • Yajñeṣu: This term refers to "in sacrifices" or "in offerings," emphasizing the significance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • Mānuṣaḥ: Meaning "human," it reminds us of our role in the cosmic order and our responsibilities towards each other and the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of Agni as a symbol of light, knowledge, and protection within the community. It highlights the importance of rituals and traditions in fostering a sense of unity and purpose among individuals. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to community gatherings, spiritual practices, and moments of reflection where individuals seek guidance and illumination in their lives. By invoking Agni, we are reminded to embrace new beginnings, protect our communities, and strive for a deeper understanding of our place in the universe.
