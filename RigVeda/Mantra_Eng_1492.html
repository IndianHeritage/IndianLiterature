तुभ्यं हि पूर्वपीतये देवā देवाय येमिरे |प्र ते सुतासो मधुमन्तो अस्तिरन् मदाय क्रत्वे अस्तिरन् ||
tubhyaṁ hi pūrvapītaye devā devāya yemire | pra te sutāso madhumanto asthiran madāya kratve asthiran ||
  • tubhyaṁ hi pūrvapītaye: This phrase translates to "For you, O Divine, who have been worshipped in the past." It acknowledges the reverence and worship that has been offered to the divine in previous times. This can be likened to how people today honor their ancestors or historical figures who have made significant contributions to society.
  • devā devāya yemire: This means "the gods have come to you." It signifies the presence and attention of the divine. In real life, this can be compared to moments when individuals feel a strong connection to a higher power during prayer or meditation, believing that their intentions are being heard.
  • pra te sutāso madhumanto: This translates to "for you, the sweet offspring." It refers to the blessings and prosperity that come from the divine. In practical terms, this can be seen in the joy and fulfillment that comes from family and community, where the 'sweet offspring' can represent the fruits of one's labor and relationships.
  • asthiran madāya kratve asthiran: This phrase means "steadfast in the pursuit of strength." It emphasizes the importance of determination and resilience in achieving one's goals. In everyday life, this can be illustrated by individuals who face challenges but remain committed to their aspirations, such as students striving for academic success or athletes training for competitions.

Conclusion: This mantra from the RigVeda encapsulates the essence of devotion, divine connection, and the pursuit of strength. Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the past, the blessings of the divine, and the importance of resilience in our endeavors. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to honor our roots, seek divine guidance, and remain steadfast in our pursuits, whether in personal growth, family, or community engagement. By embodying these principles, individuals can cultivate a fulfilling and purposeful life.
