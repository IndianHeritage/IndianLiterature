नराशंसः प्रति धामान्य अञ्जन तिस्रो दिवः प्रति मह्ना स्वर्चिः |घृतप्रुषा मनसा हव्यं उन्दन मूर्धन यज्ञस्य सम अनक्तु देवान् ||
narāśaṁsaḥ prati dhāmāny añjan tisro divaḥ prati mahnā svarciḥ | ghṛtapruṣā manasā havyam undan mūrdhan yajñasya sam anaktu devān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • narāśaṁsaḥ: Refers to the "noble man" or "human being" who is virtuous and righteous.
    • prati: Means "towards" or "in accordance with."
    • dhāmāny: Refers to "abodes" or "places of residence."
    • añjan: Can mean "eye salve" or "something that enhances vision," symbolizing clarity and insight.
    • tisro: Means "three," often representing the three realms or dimensions of existence.
    • divaḥ: Refers to "heaven" or "the celestial realm."
    • prati: Again means "towards" or "in accordance with."
    • mahnā: Means "great" or "powerful."
    • svarciḥ: Refers to "light" or "radiance," often associated with divine illumination.
    • ghṛtapruṣā: Refers to "the one who is nourished by ghee," symbolizing abundance and prosperity.
    • manasā: Means "with the mind," indicating the importance of intention and thought.
    • havyam: Refers to "offering" or "sacrifice," particularly in the context of rituals.
    • undan: Means "to rise" or "to elevate," symbolizing growth and ascension.
    • mūrdhan: Refers to "the head" or "the highest point," indicating leadership or authority.
    • yajñasya: Means "of the sacrifice" or "of the ritual," emphasizing the importance of sacred acts.
    • sam: Means "together" or "in harmony."
    • anaktu: Refers to "to be invoked" or "to be called upon."
    • devān: Means "the gods" or "divine beings."
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of virtuous living and the pursuit of higher knowledge and insight.
    • It suggests that through righteous actions and offerings, one can connect with the divine and elevate their existence.
    • The reference to "three realms" indicates a holistic approach to life, encompassing the physical, mental, and spiritual dimensions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of "narāśaṁsaḥ" inspires their team to work towards common goals, fostering a culture of integrity and collaboration.
    • In personal life, individuals who practice mindfulness and intention in their actions can create a positive impact on their relationships and community.
    • Participating in community service or charitable acts can be seen as a modern-day "havyam," where individuals offer their time and resources for the greater good.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of living a life of virtue, intention, and service.
    • It encourages individuals to seek clarity and insight in their actions, fostering a connection with the divine and the community.
    • In today's world, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, harmonious relationships, and a more compassionate society.
