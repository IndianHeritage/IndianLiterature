मन्त्र: cite tad vāṁ surādhasā rātiḥ sumatir aśvinā | ā yan naḥ sadane pṛthau samane parṣatho narā ||
cite tad vāṁ surādhasā rātiḥ sumatir aśvinā | ā yan naḥ sadane pṛthau samane parṣatho narā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • cite: to invoke or call upon
    • tad: that
    • vāṁ: your
    • surādhasā: with the strength of the divine
    • rātiḥ: joy or delight
    • sumatir: good understanding or wisdom
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the divine twin horsemen)
    • ā: may
    • yan: that which
    • naḥ: for us
    • sadane: in the abode or dwelling
    • pṛthau: on the earth
    • samane: equal or balanced
    • parṣatho: in the assembly or gathering
    • narā: men or people
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to the divine Ashvins, invoking their blessings for joy, wisdom, and harmony in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of divine guidance in achieving a balanced and fulfilling existence on Earth, particularly in communal settings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, invoking the Ashvins can symbolize the collective hope for wisdom and joy among the participants, fostering a sense of unity.
    • In personal life, one might recite this mantra during challenging times, seeking divine support to navigate through difficulties with a clear mind and a joyful heart.
    • In educational settings, teachers can use this mantra to inspire students to seek knowledge and understanding, promoting a harmonious learning environment.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in achieving joy, wisdom, and balance in our lives. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal challenges to communal gatherings, reminding us of the importance of seeking higher guidance and fostering harmony among people. By invoking the blessings of the Ashvins, we can aspire to lead lives filled with understanding and joy, both individually and collectively.
