अर्वाञ्चं त्वा पुरुष्टुत प्रियमेधस्तुतā हरī |सोमपेयाय वक्षतः ||
arvāñcaṁ tvā puruṣṭuta priyamedhastutā harī | somapeyāya vakṣataḥ ||
  • arvāñcaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is all-pervading" or "the one who encompasses everything." It signifies the universal nature of the divine or the ultimate reality.
  • tvā: This means "you," referring to the divine or the higher power being addressed in the mantra.
  • puruṣṭuta: This word can be broken down into "puruṣa," meaning "man" or "being," and "tuta," which can imply "praise" or "worship." Together, it suggests the worship of the supreme being or the essence of humanity.
  • priyamedhastutā: This can be understood as "the one who is dear and worthy of praise." It emphasizes the affection and reverence towards the divine.
  • harī: This term often refers to "the one who removes sins" or "the divine." It is a name associated with various deities, particularly Vishnu, who is known for his protective and nurturing qualities.
  • somapeyāya: This word can be interpreted as "to the one who drinks the soma," referring to the ritual drink that is believed to confer immortality and divine qualities. It symbolizes the pursuit of higher consciousness and spiritual awakening.
  • vakṣataḥ: This term means "may you be present" or "may you manifest." It is a call for the divine presence to be felt in the lives of the devotees.

Conclusion: The mantra "arvāñcaṁ tvā puruṣṭuta priyamedhastutā harī | somapeyāya vakṣataḥ ||" encapsulates the essence of devotion, reverence, and the quest for spiritual enlightenment. It signifies the acknowledgment of a higher power that is all-encompassing and worthy of praise. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of prayer, meditation, or rituals where individuals seek connection with the divine. It serves as a reminder of the importance of recognizing the universal presence in our lives and the pursuit of higher consciousness through devotion and spiritual practices. By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of peace, purpose, and connection to the greater whole, fostering a deeper understanding of their place in the universe.
