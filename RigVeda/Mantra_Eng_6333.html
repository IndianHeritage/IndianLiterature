मन्त्र: कदā त इन्द्र गिरवणः स्तोतā भवाति शान्तमः |कदā नो गव्ये अश्व्ये वसौ दधः ||
kadā ta indra girvaṇaḥ stotā bhavāti śaṁtamaḥ | kadā no gavye aśvye vasau dadhaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kadā: When
    • ta: That
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • girvaṇaḥ: Of the singers or those who chant
    • stotā: The one who is praised
    • bhavāti: Becomes
    • śaṁtamaḥ: The most peaceful or tranquil
    • kadā: When
    • no: For us
    • gavye: In the cows
    • aśvye: In the horses
    • vasau: Wealth or prosperity
    • dadhaḥ: Bestow or grant
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards Indra, the deity associated with rain, thunder, and strength.
    • The speaker is inquiring about the time when Indra will become the most peaceful and will bestow wealth, particularly in the form of cows and horses.
    • The mention of cows and horses signifies prosperity, as these animals were vital for agriculture and transportation in ancient times.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, the arrival of rain (associated with Indra) is crucial for crop growth. Farmers often pray for favorable weather conditions to ensure a good harvest.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be seen as a metaphor for seeking peace and prosperity in our lives. For instance, individuals may seek tranquility in their personal lives while striving for financial stability.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine blessings for peace and prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder that in our pursuit of wealth and success, we should also seek inner peace and harmony.
    • In contemporary life, this can be applied by balancing material pursuits with spiritual well-being, fostering a holistic approach to life.
