इन्द्रज्येष्ठा मरुद्गणा देवासः पूषरातयः |विश्वे मम श्रुताः हवम् ||
indrajyeṣṭhā marudgaṇā devāsaḥ pūṣarātayaḥ | viśve mama śrutā havam ||
  • Indrajyeṣṭhā: This term refers to the "greatest" or "most powerful" among the Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature. Indra is often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and the warrior spirit.
  • Marudgaṇā: This translates to "the Maruts," who are a group of storm deities or wind gods. They are known for their strength and are often depicted as youthful and energetic.
  • Devāsaḥ: This means "the gods" or "divine beings." It emphasizes the divine nature of the entities being addressed in the mantra.
  • Pūṣarātayaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who are nourished" or "the providers of nourishment." It signifies abundance and sustenance, often associated with the earth and nature.
  • Viśve: This means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity and the collective nature of the beings being invoked.
  • Mama: This translates to "my," indicating a personal connection or ownership in the context of the invocation.
  • Śrutā: This means "heard" or "listened to," suggesting that the invocation is being made with the expectation of being acknowledged by the divine.
  • Havam: This refers to "offering" or "sacrifice," which is a central theme in Vedic rituals, symbolizing the act of giving and devotion.

The mantra can be understood as a call to the most powerful deities, particularly Indra and the Maruts, to listen to the offerings made by the devotee. It emphasizes the importance of divine support and nourishment in one's life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Seeking Strength: Just as Indra represents strength and power, individuals can invoke this mantra during challenging times, seeking the courage and resilience to overcome obstacles.
  • Community and Cooperation: The Maruts symbolize teamwork and collective effort. This mantra can be a reminder of the importance of working together in communities, whether in family, workplace, or social settings.
  • Gratitude for Abundance: By acknowledging the divine as providers of nourishment, individuals can cultivate a sense of gratitude for the resources and support they receive in life, fostering a positive mindset.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful invocation for strength, community, and gratitude. It highlights the significance of divine support in our lives and encourages us to recognize and appreciate the abundance around us. By incorporating these values into our daily lives, we can foster resilience, cooperation, and a deeper connection to the world and its divine aspects.
