अग्निना तुर्वशं यदुम परावत उग्रादेवं हवामहे |अग्निर नयन नववास्त्वम् बृहद्रथं तुर्वीतिं दस्यवे सहः ||
agninā turvaśaṁ yadum parāvata ugrādevaṁ havāmahe | agnir nayan navavāstvam bṛhadrathaṁ turvītiṁ dasyave sahaḥ ||
  • Agninā: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Turvaśaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is powerful" or "the one who is strong," indicating the might of Agni.
  • Yadum: This word means "to us" or "for us," suggesting a personal connection or invocation.
  • Parāvata: This term translates to "the one who is exalted" or "the one who is supreme," emphasizing the elevated status of Agni.
  • Ugrādevaṁ: This phrase means "the fierce deity," highlighting the formidable nature of Agni.
  • Havāmahe: This means "we invoke" or "we call upon," indicating a plea or request for Agni's presence and blessings.
  • Agnir: Again referring to Agni, reinforcing the focus on the fire god.
  • Nayan: This word means "to see" or "to perceive," suggesting a desire for clarity and insight.
  • Navavāstvam: This term can be interpreted as "newness" or "freshness," indicating a longing for renewal or rejuvenation.
  • Bṛhadrathaṁ: This means "the great chariot," symbolizing strength and the ability to carry burdens.
  • Turvītiṁ: This can be understood as "the one who is powerful" or "the one who is strong," reiterating the strength of Agni.
  • Dasyave: This term refers to "the one who is fierce" or "the one who is formidable," again emphasizing Agni's might.
  • Sahaḥ: This means "together" or "with," suggesting a collective invocation or effort.

The mantra essentially invokes Agni, the fire god, as a powerful and fierce deity who brings clarity, strength, and renewal. It expresses a desire for Agni's blessings and guidance in our lives.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Transformation: Just as fire transforms materials, individuals can invoke Agni's energy for personal growth and change, such as overcoming challenges or bad habits.
  • Community and Collaboration: The collective invocation of Agni signifies the importance of working together for a common goal, much like a team striving for success.
  • Renewal and Rejuvenation: In times of stagnation or difficulty, this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek new beginnings and fresh perspectives.

In conclusion, this mantra highlights the significance of invoking divine energy for strength, clarity, and transformation in our lives. It serves as a reminder of the power of collaboration and the potential for renewal, making it applicable in both personal and communal contexts.
