अक्षासा इद अङ्कुशिनो नितोदिनो निकृत्वानस तपनास तापयिष्णवः |कुमारदेष्णा जयतः पुनर्हणो मध्वा सम्पृक्ताः किटवस्य बरहणा ||
akṣāsa id aṅkuśino nitodino nikṛtvānas tapanās tāpayiṣṇavaḥ | kumāradeṣṇā jayataḥ punarhaṇo madhvā sampṛktāḥ kitavasya barhaṇā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • akṣāsa: Refers to the heavens or the sky.
    • id: This word can mean 'this' or 'here'.
    • aṅkuśino: Pertaining to the one who wields a goad or a hook, often symbolizing control or guidance.
    • nitodino: Those who are diligent or who strive.
    • nikṛtvānas: Having made or created.
    • tapanās: Referring to the sun or heat.
    • tāpayiṣṇavaḥ: Those who are capable of heating or energizing.
    • kumāradeṣṇā: The youthful or childlike aspect, often representing new beginnings or potential.
    • jayataḥ: Victorious or triumphant.
    • punarhaṇo: Referring to the act of renewal or rebirth.
    • madhvā: Sweetness or nectar, often symbolizing joy or bliss.
    • sampṛktāḥ: Those who are mixed or combined.
    • kitavasya: Referring to the deceitful or trickster aspect.
    • barhaṇā: The act of elevating or uplifting.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine forces that govern the universe, emphasizing the importance of guidance and control.
    • The reference to the heavens suggests a connection to higher consciousness or spiritual realms.
    • The goad symbolizes the need for direction in life, much like a mentor or teacher guiding a student.
    • The mention of the sun indicates the source of energy and vitality, essential for growth and renewal.
    • The youthful aspect signifies the potential for new beginnings and the importance of embracing change.
    • The idea of victory and renewal suggests that through challenges, one can emerge stronger and more enlightened.
    • The sweetness of life, represented by 'madhvā', reminds us to seek joy and positivity even amidst difficulties.
    • The reference to deceit highlights the need for discernment in our lives, encouraging us to rise above trickery and negativity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a mentor can be seen as the 'aṅkuśino', guiding a young employee through challenges and helping them grow.
    • In personal relationships, the concept of renewal can be applied when individuals work through conflicts and emerge with a stronger bond.
    • In health and wellness, the sun symbolizes the importance of physical activity and exposure to sunlight for mental and physical rejuvenation.
    • In education, the youthful aspect can be reflected in the curiosity and eagerness of children to learn, reminding us to maintain that spirit throughout life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of guidance, renewal, and the pursuit of joy in life.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that we are all on a journey of growth, and with the right guidance and mindset, we can overcome challenges and find sweetness in our experiences.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages us to seek mentors, embrace change, and remain vigilant against negativity, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and victorious existence.
