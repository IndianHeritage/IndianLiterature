प्रेहि प्रेहि पथिभिः पूर्व्येभिर यत्र नः पूर्वे पितरः परेयुः |उभा राजाना स्वधया मदन्ता यमं पश्यासि वरुणं च देवम् ||
prehi prehi pathibhiḥ pūrvyebhir yatrā naḥ pūrve pitaraḥ pareyuḥ | ubhā rājānā svadhayā madantā yamam paśyāsi varuṇaṁ ca devam ||
  • Mantra: "prehi prehi pathibhiḥ pūrvyebhir yatrā naḥ pūrve pitaraḥ pareyuḥ | ubhā rājānā svadhayā madantā yamam paśyāsi varuṇaṁ ca devam ||"
  • Translation: "Come, come, by the paths of our ancestors, where our forefathers have gone. Both the kings, by their own will, see Yama and the god Varuna."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • prehi prehi: "Come, come" - an invitation or call to approach.
    • pathibhiḥ: "by the paths" - referring to the ways or traditions established by ancestors.
    • pūrvyebhir: "of our ancestors" - indicating the lineage and heritage.
    • yatrā: "where" - pointing to a destination or state of being.
    • naḥ: "our" - a possessive pronoun indicating belonging.
    • pūrve: "forefathers" - those who came before us, our ancestors.
    • pitaraḥ: "fathers" - a term of respect for ancestors.
    • pareyuḥ: "have gone" - indicating a journey or transition to another realm.
    • ubhā: "both" - referring to two entities.
    • rājānā: "kings" - leaders or authoritative figures.
    • svadhayā: "by their own will" - acting according to their own decisions.
    • madantā: "see" - to perceive or witness.
    • yamam: "Yama" - the god of death and the afterlife.
    • paśyāsi: "you see" - a direct address to the divine or the observer.
    • varuṇaṁ: "Varuna" - the god of cosmic order and water.
    • ca: "and" - a conjunction linking two ideas.
    • devam: "god" - a divine being or deity.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of following the paths laid down by our ancestors, suggesting that wisdom and guidance can be found in tradition.
    • It acknowledges the roles of Yama and Varuna, highlighting the dual aspects of life: the inevitability of death and the maintenance of cosmic order.
    • In real life, this can be seen in practices such as honoring ancestors during festivals, seeking guidance from elders, and adhering to cultural traditions that promote harmony and respect.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In many cultures, rituals are performed to honor ancestors, such as the Day of the Dead in Mexico, where families remember and celebrate their deceased loved ones.
    • In leadership, the concept of 'servant leadership' reflects the idea of guiding others while being rooted in the wisdom of those who came before.
    • In personal development, individuals often reflect on their heritage and values instilled by their families, using this knowledge to navigate their own life paths.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, death, and the wisdom of our ancestors.
    • It encourages individuals to seek guidance from their heritage while recognizing the roles of divine forces in their lives.
    • By embracing these teachings, one can cultivate a deeper understanding of their place in the world and the legacy they wish to leave behind.
