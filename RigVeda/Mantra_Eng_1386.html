पृतु रथो दक्षिणायाऽयोज्य ऐनं देवासो अमृतासो अस्तुḥ |कृष्णादुद अस्ताद् आर्याः विहायाश्चिकित्सन्ती मानवाय क्षयाय ||
pṛthū ratho dakṣiṇāyā ayojy ainaṁ devāso amṛtāso asthuḥ | kṛṣṇād ud asthād aryā3 vihāyāś cikitsantī mānuṣāya kṣayāya ||
  • pṛthū ratho: This phrase translates to "the broad chariot." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent the journey of life, where the chariot symbolizes the vehicle that carries us through various experiences.
  • dakṣiṇāyā: This word means "towards the south" or "to the right." In many cultures, the right side is associated with auspiciousness and positive energy, suggesting a direction of prosperity and success.
  • ayojy: This term can be interpreted as "not to be obstructed" or "unhindered." It signifies the importance of a smooth journey in life, free from obstacles that can hinder progress.
  • ainaṁ: This word refers to "this" or "that," indicating a focus on the present moment or the current situation in life.
  • devāso: Meaning "of the gods," this term emphasizes the divine influence in our lives, suggesting that higher powers are at play in our journey.
  • amṛtāso: This translates to "immortal" or "eternal," indicating the pursuit of everlasting truths and values in life.
  • asthuh: This word can mean "may they be" or "let them be," expressing a wish or intention for the desired outcomes in life.
  • kṛṣṇād: This term refers to "from the black" or "from darkness," which can symbolize challenges or ignorance that one must overcome.
  • ud asthād: This means "rising up," suggesting the act of overcoming difficulties and striving for higher goals.
  • aryā3: This word can be interpreted as "noble" or "worthy," indicating the importance of maintaining integrity and honor in our actions.
  • vihāyāś: This translates to "having abandoned," suggesting the need to let go of negative influences or past burdens that hinder progress.
  • cikitsantī: This term means "healing" or "curing," emphasizing the importance of self-care and healing in our journey.
  • mānuṣāya: This word refers to "for humanity," indicating that the teachings and guidance are meant for all people.
  • kṣayāya: This translates to "for destruction" or "for the end," suggesting the inevitability of change and the need to embrace transformation in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life's journey, emphasizing the importance of divine guidance, the pursuit of noble values, and the need for healing and transformation. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal growth, where individuals strive to overcome challenges, seek wisdom, and maintain integrity. It serves as a reminder to embrace change and let go of negativity, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and purposeful life.
