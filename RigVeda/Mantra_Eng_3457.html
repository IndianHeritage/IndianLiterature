मन्त्र: शच्याकर्त पितरā युवाना शच्याकर्त चमसं देवपानम् | शच्यā हरि धनुतराव अतष्टेन्द्रवाहाव ऋभवो वाजरत्नाः ||
śacyākarta pitarā yuvānā śacyākarta camasaṁ devapānam | śacyā harī dhanutarāv ataṣṭendravāhāv ṛbhavo vājaratnāḥ ||
  • śacyākarta pitarā yuvānā: This phrase can be translated as "the youthful parents who are the doers of good deeds." Here, "śacyā" refers to the quality of being capable or powerful, while "karta" means "doer." This suggests that the parents are not just passive figures but actively engage in righteous actions.
  • śacyākarta camasaṁ devapānam: This part translates to "the doers of good deeds who partake in the divine drink." "Camasaṁ" refers to a cup or vessel, and "devapānam" means "divine drink." This can symbolize the nourishment and blessings that come from engaging in virtuous actions and connecting with the divine.
  • śacyā harī dhanutarāv: This translates to "the powerful ones, like the swift deer." "Harī" refers to a deer, which is often associated with grace and agility. This suggests that those who embody goodness and virtue are swift and agile in their pursuits, much like a deer in the wild.
  • ataṣṭendravāhāv: This phrase means "those who are carried by the divine." "Indravāhāv" refers to being borne by Indra, the king of gods, symbolizing divine support and guidance in one's endeavors.
  • ṛbhavo vājaratnāḥ: This translates to "the wise ones who possess the jewel of strength." "Vājaratnāḥ" refers to the "jewel of strength," indicating that wisdom and strength are intertwined, and those who are wise are also strong in their convictions and actions.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of virtuous actions and the divine support that comes from them. It highlights the qualities of youthfulness, agility, and wisdom, suggesting that those who engage in good deeds are not only blessed but also empowered. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as community service, ethical business practices, and personal relationships. By embodying these qualities, individuals can create a positive impact in their surroundings, fostering a sense of community and divine connection. The mantra serves as a reminder that our actions, guided by wisdom and virtue, can lead to a fulfilling and meaningful life.
