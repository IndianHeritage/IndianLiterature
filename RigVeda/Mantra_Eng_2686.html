कृṇोता धूमं वृषणं सखायोऽस्रेदन्त इतान वाजम् अच्‍छ || अयम् अग्निः पृतनाशाट सुवीरो येन देवासो असहन्त दस्यून् ||
kṛṇota dhūmaṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ sakhāyo 'sredhanta itana vājam accha | ayam agniḥ pṛtanāṣāṭ suvīro yena devāso asahanta dasyūn ||
  • kṛṇota dhūmaṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ sakhāyo 'sredhanta itana vājam accha
    • kṛṇota: This word means "to create" or "to make." It signifies the act of bringing something into existence.
    • dhūmaṁ: This translates to "smoke," which can symbolize transformation or the process of purification.
    • vṛṣaṇaṁ: This term refers to "strength" or "power," often associated with the might of a warrior or a strong force.
    • sakhāyo: Meaning "friends" or "companions," it emphasizes the importance of community and support.
    • 'sredhanta: This can be interpreted as "to unite" or "to come together," highlighting the significance of collaboration.
    • itana: This word means "this" or "here," indicating a focus on the present moment.
    • vājam: Translated as "strength" or "power," it reinforces the theme of might and capability.
    • accha: This means "good" or "well," suggesting a positive outcome or result.
  • ayam agniḥ pṛtanāṣāṭ suvīro yena devāso asahanta dasyūn
    • ayam: This means "this" or "here," again emphasizing the present.
    • agniḥ: Referring to "fire," which symbolizes energy, transformation, and divine presence.
    • pṛtanāṣāṭ: This can be interpreted as "the one who destroys obstacles," indicating a force that clears the path.
    • suvīro: Meaning "the brave one," it highlights courage and valor.
    • yena: This translates to "by whom," indicating the agent or cause of action.
    • devāso: Referring to "the gods," it signifies divine support and guidance.
    • asahanta: This means "to overcome" or "to conquer," suggesting triumph over challenges.
    • dasyūn: This term refers to "enemies" or "obstacles," representing the challenges one faces.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of collaboration, strength, and divine support in overcoming obstacles. It highlights the role of community and friendship in achieving goals and the transformative power of fire as a symbol of energy and purification. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as teamwork in professional settings, community support during challenging times, or personal growth through overcoming adversities. It serves as a reminder that with unity, strength, and divine guidance, one can conquer challenges and achieve success.
