गिरिर न यः स्वतवाम ऋष्व इन्द्रः सनाद एव सहसे जात उग्रः |आदर्ता वज्रं स्थविरं न भीमा उद्नेव कोशं वसुनā न्यृष्टम् ||
girir na yaḥ svatavām̐ ṛṣva indraḥ sanād eva sahase jāta ugraḥ | ādartā vajraṁ sthaviraṁ na bhīma udneva kośaṁ vasunā nyṛṣṭam ||
  • girir na yaḥ svatavām̐ ṛṣva indraḥ sanād eva sahase jāta ugraḥ
    • girir: This word translates to "mountain" or "the one who is strong and stable." In real life, mountains symbolize strength and endurance, much like the challenges we face.
    • na yaḥ: This means "who does not." It emphasizes the qualities that are absent in a strong individual.
    • svatavām̐: This translates to "self-possessed" or "self-reliant." It highlights the importance of self-sufficiency in achieving goals.
    • ṛṣva: This means "powerful" or "mighty." It signifies the strength that comes from within.
    • indraḥ: Referring to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing leadership and authority.
    • sanād eva: This phrase means "from the very beginning." It suggests that these qualities have been inherent from the start.
    • sahase: This translates to "with strength" or "with courage." It emphasizes the need for bravery in facing life's challenges.
    • jāta ugraḥ: This means "born fierce" or "born powerful." It indicates that true strength is innate.
  • ādartā vajraṁ sthaviraṁ na bhīma udneva kośaṁ vasunā nyṛṣṭam
    • ādartā: This means "the one who supports" or "the one who upholds." It signifies the importance of support systems in our lives.
    • vajraṁ: This translates to "thunderbolt" or "diamond," symbolizing indestructibility and power.
    • sthaviraṁ: This means "ancient" or "everlasting," indicating the timeless nature of true strength.
    • na bhīma: This translates to "not fearful." It emphasizes the importance of courage in the face of adversity.
    • udneva: This means "like the rising sun," symbolizing hope and new beginnings.
    • kośaṁ: This translates to "treasure" or "wealth," indicating the value of inner strength.
    • vasunā: This means "with wealth" or "with abundance," suggesting that true wealth comes from within.
    • nyṛṣṭam: This translates to "bestowed" or "given," indicating that strength and support are gifts that can be shared.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of inner strength, self-reliance, and the support systems we build around us. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as overcoming personal challenges, leading teams, or facing adversities with courage. Just as mountains stand tall and unyielding, we too can cultivate resilience and strength within ourselves. The mantra serves as a reminder that true power comes from within and that we should embrace our innate abilities while also supporting one another in our journeys.
