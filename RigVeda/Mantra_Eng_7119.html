त्वं ह्येहि चेरवे विदा भागं वसुत्तये |उद्वावृषस्व मगवन गविष्टय उद इन्द्राश्वमिष्टये ||
tvaṁ hy ehi cerave vidā bhagaṁ vasuttaye | ud vāvṛṣasva maghavan gaviṣṭaya ud indrāśvamiṣṭaye ||
  • tvaṁ hy ehi cerave vidā bhagaṁ vasuttaye
    • tvaṁ - "You" or "Thou": This refers to a divine entity or a higher power, often interpreted as a deity.
    • hy - "Indeed": This word emphasizes the certainty or truth of the statement.
    • ehi - "Come": A call to the divine to approach or manifest.
    • cerave - "To the one who knows": This suggests wisdom or knowledge, indicating a connection to understanding and enlightenment.
    • vidā - "Knowledge": This reinforces the theme of wisdom and understanding.
    • bhagaṁ - "Prosperity" or "Wealth": This term signifies abundance and blessings.
    • vasuttaye - "For the well-being": This indicates a desire for health, prosperity, and overall welfare.
  • ud vāvṛṣasva maghavan gaviṣṭaya
    • ud - "Up" or "Higher": This suggests elevation or ascension, both physically and spiritually.
    • vāvṛṣasva - "Shower down": A request for blessings or abundance to be poured forth.
    • maghavan - "O Magnificent One": This is a reference to a powerful deity, often associated with wealth and prosperity.
    • gaviṣṭaya - "In the cows": Cows are often symbolic of wealth and nourishment in Vedic literature, representing sustenance and prosperity.
  • ud indrāśvamiṣṭaye
    • ud - "Up" or "Higher": Again, this indicates a call for elevation.
    • indrāśvamiṣṭaye - "For the best of Indra's horses": Indra is a prominent deity associated with strength and power, and his horses symbolize speed, strength, and victory.

Conclusion:

This mantra is a call for divine blessings, prosperity, and knowledge. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and the desire for well-being, both materially and spiritually. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • In personal prayers for success and abundance in one's endeavors.
  • In community gatherings where collective well-being and prosperity are sought.
  • In rituals or ceremonies aimed at invoking blessings for health and wealth.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge, prosperity, and divine support in their journeys.
