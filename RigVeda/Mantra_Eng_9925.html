याश्चेदमुपश्रिण्वन्ति याश्च दूरम् परागताः |सर्वाः संगत्य वीरुधोऽस्यै सं दत्त वीर्यम् ||
yāś cedam upaśṛṇvanti yāś ca dūram parāgatāḥ | sarvāḥ saṁgatya vīrudho 'syai saṁ datta vīryam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yāś: Those who
    • cedam: This
    • upaśṛṇvanti: Listen to
    • yāś: Those who
    • ca: And
    • dūram: Far away
    • parāgatāḥ: Have gone
    • sarvāḥ: All
    • saṁgatya: Coming together
    • vīrudho: United
    • 'syai: Of this
    • saṁ: Together
    • datta: Given
    • vīryam: Strength or power
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of unity and collective strength.
    • It emphasizes the importance of listening and understanding, whether one is near or far.
    • It suggests that when individuals come together, they can achieve greater strength and success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Community Initiatives: In local communities, when people unite for a common cause, such as environmental conservation or social justice, they can create significant change. For instance, community clean-up drives often see greater participation and impact when individuals come together.
    • Team Sports: In sports, a team that communicates well and works together can outperform a group of individually talented players. The synergy created by teamwork exemplifies the mantra's message of collective strength.
    • Workplace Collaboration: In a corporate setting, departments that collaborate effectively can drive innovation and productivity. For example, cross-functional teams that share knowledge and resources often lead to successful project outcomes.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the power of unity and collaboration.
    • It teaches that listening to one another, regardless of distance, fosters understanding and strengthens bonds.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in communities, sports, or workplaces, the principles of this mantra can lead to enhanced cooperation and success.
