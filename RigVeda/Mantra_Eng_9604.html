अप हत रक्षसो भङ्गुरावतः स्कभायत निरृतिं सेधतामातिम् |आ नो रयिं सर्ववीरं सुनोतन देवाव्यम् भरत श्लोकम् अद्रयः ||
apa hata rakṣaso bhaṅgurāvataḥ skabhāyata nirṛtiṁ sedhatāmatim | ā no rayiṁ sarvavīraṁ sunotana devāvyam bharata ślokam adrayaḥ ||
  • apa hata rakṣaso: This phrase translates to "having been struck down, the demons." Here, "rakṣasa" refers to malevolent beings or obstacles in life. In real life, this can symbolize overcoming challenges or negative influences that hinder personal growth.
  • bhaṅgurāvataḥ: This means "like the fragile." It suggests the transient nature of life and the importance of recognizing that everything is temporary. For example, relationships, success, and even challenges can be fleeting, reminding us to cherish the present moment.
  • skabhāyata: This term can be interpreted as "to be supported" or "to be upheld." It emphasizes the need for support systems in our lives, such as family, friends, or community, which help us navigate through difficulties.
  • nirṛtiṁ sedhatāmatim: This translates to "may we overcome misfortune." "Nirṛti" represents misfortune or adversity, while "sedhatāmatim" implies the desire to transcend these challenges. In practical terms, this can relate to resilience in the face of life's hardships, such as losing a job or facing health issues.
  • ā no rayiṁ sarvavīraṁ: This phrase means "may we attain wealth and strength." It signifies the aspiration for prosperity and empowerment. In everyday life, this can be seen in the pursuit of education, career advancement, or personal development, all aimed at achieving a fulfilling life.
  • sunotana devāvyam: This translates to "may the divine grant us." It reflects the belief in divine support and guidance. In real life, this can be related to seeking inspiration or help from a higher power during challenging times, such as praying for guidance before making significant life decisions.
  • bharata ślokam adrayaḥ: This means "let us chant the verses of the wise." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge. In practical terms, this can be applied by engaging in learning, reading, or sharing knowledge with others, fostering a culture of wisdom in our communities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the journey of overcoming obstacles, recognizing the transient nature of life, and seeking divine support for prosperity and wisdom. Its significance lies in its applicability to real-life scenarios where individuals face challenges, seek strength, and strive for personal growth. By embracing the teachings of this mantra, one can cultivate resilience, appreciate the present, and foster a supportive community, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and empowered life.
