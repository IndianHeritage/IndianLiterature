ते मा आहुर य आययुर उप द्युभिर विभिर मदे |नरो मर्याऽरेपस इमान पश्यन्न इति ष्टुहि ||
te ma āhur ya āyayur upa dyubhir vibhir made | naro maryā arepasa imān paśyann iti ṣṭuhi ||
  • Translation: "They say that those who have attained the heavens through their deeds, who have reached the celestial realms, are the ones who see the light of truth."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • te: they
    • ma: not
    • āhur: say
    • ya: who
    • āyayur: have attained
    • upa: towards
    • dyubhir: heavens
    • vibhir: through deeds
    • made: in joy
    • naro: man
    • maryā: mortal
    • arepasa: who sees
    • imān: these
    • paśyann: seeing
    • iti: thus
    • ṣṭuhi: praise
  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of actions and deeds in achieving a higher state of existence or enlightenment.
    • It suggests that true understanding and enlightenment come from the experiences and actions one undertakes in life.
    • For example, consider a person who dedicates their life to helping others, such as a social worker. Their actions not only uplift their community but also lead them to a deeper understanding of life and humanity.
    • Similarly, a scientist who works tirelessly to discover a cure for a disease is also seen as someone who is reaching towards the heavens through their noble deeds.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as in personal development, community service, and professional ethics.
    • In personal development, it encourages individuals to engage in meaningful actions that contribute to their growth and understanding.
    • In community service, it highlights the significance of selfless acts that benefit others, reinforcing the idea that such deeds lead to a more fulfilling life.
    • In professional settings, it serves as a reminder that ethical practices and contributions to society can elevate one's career and personal satisfaction.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra signifies that the path to enlightenment and understanding is paved with righteous actions and deeds.
    • It teaches that true vision and insight come from actively engaging with the world and contributing positively to it.
    • In essence, it encourages individuals to live a life of purpose, where their actions resonate with the greater good, ultimately leading to personal and collective upliftment.
