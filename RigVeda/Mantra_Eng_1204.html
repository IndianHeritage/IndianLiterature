निष्चर्मण ऋभवो गाम अपिंशत सं वत्सेनासृजता मातरम् पुनः |सौधन्वनासः स्वपस्यया नरो जीव्री युवाना पितराकृतनोतना ||
niś carmaṇa ṛbhavo gām apiṁśata saṁ vatsenāsṛjatā mātaram punaḥ | saudhanvanāsaḥ svapasyayā naro jivrī yuvānā pitarākṛṇotana ||
  • niś carmaṇa: This phrase can be interpreted as "the night (niś) is a protector (carmaṇa)." In real life, night often symbolizes rest and rejuvenation, much like how we seek protection and comfort during the night.
  • ṛbhavo: This word refers to "the wise" or "the enlightened." In our daily lives, we often look up to wise individuals for guidance, whether they are teachers, mentors, or elders.
  • gām apiṁśata: This translates to "the earth (gām) is abundant (apiṁśata)." It signifies the richness of nature and resources available to us. For example, agriculture relies on the earth's fertility to provide food for sustenance.
  • saṁ vatsenāsṛjatā: This means "by the nurturing (vatsena) of the mother (mātaram)." It highlights the importance of nurturing and care, akin to how parents nurture their children for growth and development.
  • punaḥ: This word means "again" or "once more." It signifies renewal and the cyclical nature of life, such as the changing seasons or the cycle of life and death.
  • saudhanvanāsaḥ: This can be interpreted as "those who possess wealth (saudhanvanā)." In modern society, wealth can refer not only to material possessions but also to knowledge and relationships that enrich our lives.
  • svapasyayā: This translates to "with vision (svapasyayā)." It emphasizes the importance of foresight and awareness in decision-making, similar to how businesses strategize for future growth.
  • naro jivrī: This means "the human (naro) is alive (jivrī)." It reflects the essence of being human, which is to live fully and experience life in all its dimensions.
  • yuvānā: This word refers to "the youth." It signifies the energy, potential, and creativity of young individuals, who often drive change and innovation in society.
  • pitarākṛṇotana: This phrase means "the ancestors (pitarā) are honored (kṛṇotana)." It highlights the importance of respecting and remembering our roots and heritage, which can be seen in various cultural practices around the world.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of protection, wisdom, abundance, nurturing, renewal, wealth, vision, life, youth, and respect for ancestors. Its significance lies in the interconnectedness of these elements in our lives. In real-life scenarios, we can apply these teachings by seeking wisdom from our elders, nurturing our relationships, respecting nature, and honoring our heritage. This mantra serves as a reminder of the values that contribute to a balanced and fulfilling life, encouraging us to live with awareness and gratitude.
