आद इन मातृर आविशद यास्व आ शुचिर अहिंस्यमान उर्वियā वि वावृधे |अनु यत पूर्वा अरुहत सनाुज्वो नि नव्यसीष्व अवरासु धावते ||
ād in mātṝr āviśad yāsv ā śucir ahiṁsyamāna urviyā vi vāvṛdhe | anu yat pūrvā aruhat sanājuvo ni navyasīṣv avarāsu dhāvate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ād: Beginning or initiation
    • in: In or within
    • mātṝr: Mother or maternal
    • āviśad: Entered or pervaded
    • yāsv: Where or in which
    • ā: Not or without
    • śucir: Pure or clean
    • ahiṁsyamāna: Non-violent or peaceful
    • urviyā: Vast or expansive
    • vi: In or with
    • vāvṛdhe: Flourished or thrived
    • anu: Following or after
    • yat: That which
    • pūrvā: Previous or ancient
    • aruhat: Ascended or risen
    • sanājuvo: With good fortune or auspiciousness
    • ni: Down or below
    • navyasīṣv: In new or fresh
    • avarāsu: In the lower or inferior
    • dhāvate: Runs or moves
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the essence of purity and non-violence that permeates the maternal aspect of existence.
    • The phrase "ād in mātṝr āviśad" suggests that the beginning of life and creation is rooted in the nurturing and protective qualities of the mother.
    • The emphasis on "ahiṁsyamāna" highlights the importance of non-violence, which is essential for a harmonious life.
    • When it mentions "urviyā vi vāvṛdhe," it indicates that such purity and non-violence lead to flourishing and growth in a vast manner.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "anu yat pūrvā aruhat sanājuvo," reflects on the continuity of these values from ancient times, suggesting that they have always been a source of good fortune and auspiciousness.
    • Finally, "ni navyasīṣv avarāsu dhāvate" implies that even in new beginnings, the essence of these values should guide us, preventing us from falling into lower or inferior states of being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In parenting, the nurturing qualities of a mother can be seen in how she instills values of kindness and compassion in her children, promoting a non-violent approach to conflict resolution.
    • In community building, organizations that prioritize non-violence and inclusivity often thrive, creating a supportive environment that fosters growth and collaboration.
    • In personal development, individuals who practice mindfulness and non-violence in their thoughts and actions tend to experience greater peace and fulfillment in their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of purity, non-violence, and the nurturing qualities associated with motherhood.
    • It serves as a reminder that these values are foundational for personal and communal growth, encouraging us to cultivate them in our daily lives.
    • By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and flourishing existence, both for ourselves and for those around us.
