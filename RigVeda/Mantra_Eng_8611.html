महिमे अस्य वृषनाम शूषे माम्श्चत्वे वा पृशाने वा वधात्रे |अस्वापयन् निगुतः स्नेहयच चापामित्राम् अपाचितो अचेतः ||
mahīme asya vṛṣanāma śūṣe mām̐ścatve vā pṛśane vā vadhatre | asvāpayan nigutaḥ snehayac cāpāmitrām̐ apācito acetaḥ ||
  • mahīme: This word translates to "greatness" or "majesty." It signifies the vastness and importance of the subject being addressed.
  • asya: Meaning "of this," it indicates possession or relation to the greatness being discussed.
  • vṛṣanāma: This term can be interpreted as "the name of the bull" or "the powerful one." In Vedic literature, the bull often symbolizes strength and virility.
  • śūṣe: This word means "to the one who is powerful" or "to the one who is strong." It emphasizes the strength of the subject.
  • mām̐ścatve: This can be understood as "to me" or "for me," indicating a personal connection or appeal.
  • vā: This word means "or," suggesting an alternative or choice in the context.
  • pṛśane: This term refers to "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is called upon," indicating a plea or request for assistance.
  • vā: Again, this word means "or," reinforcing the idea of options or alternatives.
  • vadhatre: This means "the one who protects" or "the one who sustains," highlighting the protective aspect of the subject.
  • asvāpayan: This term can be interpreted as "to bring forth" or "to produce," suggesting a creative or generative force.
  • nigutaḥ: Meaning "hidden" or "concealed," it implies that there are deeper meanings or truths that may not be immediately visible.
  • snehayac: This word translates to "affection" or "love," indicating a bond or connection that is emotional in nature.
  • cāpāmitrām̐: This can be understood as "to the enemy" or "to the adversary," suggesting a confrontation or challenge.
  • apācito: This term means "to be invoked" or "to be called upon," indicating a plea for help or intervention.
  • acetaḥ: This translates to "without consciousness" or "unaware," suggesting a state of ignorance or lack of awareness.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates themes of strength, protection, and the interplay between affection and conflict. It reflects the duality of existence, where one seeks strength and protection while navigating relationships with both allies and adversaries.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be applied in situations where individuals seek strength in their endeavors, whether in personal relationships, professional challenges, or spiritual journeys. For instance, when facing a difficult decision or conflict, one might invoke the qualities of strength and protection, seeking guidance and support from a higher power or inner strength.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of recognizing both the strengths and challenges in life. It encourages individuals to seek protection and strength while fostering connections based on love and understanding, even in the face of adversity. By embodying these principles, one can navigate life's complexities with greater resilience and awareness.
