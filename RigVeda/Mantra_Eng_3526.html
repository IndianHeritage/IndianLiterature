सिन्धुर ह वां रसया सिञ्चद अश्वान घृणा वयो 'रुषासः परि गमन |तद ऊषु वाम अजिरं चेति यानं येन पति भवथः सूर्यायाः ||
sindhur ha vāṁ rasayā siñcad aśvān ghṛṇā vayo 'ruṣāsaḥ pari gman | tad ū ṣu vām ajiraṁ ceti yānaṁ yena patī bhavathaḥ sūryāyāḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O rivers, may you pour forth your waters, and may the horses be nourished by your streams. May you, O rivers, grant us the swift chariot that leads us to the sun."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sindhur: rivers or bodies of water
    • ha: indeed or surely
    • vāṁ: your
    • rasayā: with the essence or nourishment
    • siñcad: may pour forth or flow
    • aśvān: horses
    • ghṛṇā: with care or affection
    • vayo: streams or currents
    • 'ruṣāsaḥ: with strength or vigor
    • pari: all around or everywhere
    • gman: to lead or guide
    • tad: that
    • ū: indeed or surely
    • ṣu: good or auspicious
    • vām: your
    • ajiraṁ: swift or quick
    • ceti: chariot or vehicle
    • yānaṁ: journey or path
    • yena: by which
    • patī: lord or master
    • bhavathaḥ: you become or you are
    • sūryāyāḥ: of the sun
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of rivers as sources of life and sustenance. In real life, rivers like the Ganges or Nile have been central to the civilizations that flourished along their banks, providing water for agriculture, transportation, and trade.
    • The mention of horses symbolizes strength and vitality. In ancient times, horses were crucial for transportation and warfare. Today, they can represent the drive and energy needed to pursue our goals, much like how athletes train to harness their physical capabilities.
    • The idea of a chariot leading to the sun can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment. Just as a chariot carries its passengers, education and wisdom guide individuals toward a brighter future.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of nature's elements—water, strength, and light—as essential for life and progress. It teaches us to respect and harness the resources around us, whether they be natural or personal. In modern life, this can be applied by recognizing the importance of sustainable practices, nurturing our physical and mental well-being, and striving for enlightenment through education and self-improvement. The mantra serves as a reminder that our journey toward success and fulfillment is interconnected with the world around us.
