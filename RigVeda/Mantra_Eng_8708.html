मन्त्र: सोम उ षुवाणः सोत्रभिर अधि ष्णुभिर अवीनाम् | अश्वयेव हरिता याति धारणया मन्द्रया याति धारणया ||
soma u ṣuvāṇaḥ sotṛbhir adhi ṣṇubhir avīnām | aśvayeva haritā yāti dhārayā mandrayā yāti dhārayā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Soma: Refers to the sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • u: A particle that can mean "and" or "also," indicating addition.
    • ṣuvāṇaḥ: Meaning "the one who is well-formed" or "the one who is auspicious."
    • sotṛbhir: Translates to "with the singers" or "with those who chant."
    • adhi: Means "upon" or "over," indicating a sense of elevation or superiority.
    • ṣṇubhir: Refers to "the ones who are connected" or "the ones who are united."
    • avīnām: Means "to the ones who are not separated" or "to the united ones."
    • aśvayeva: Translates to "like the horse," symbolizing strength and vitality.
    • haritā: Means "green" or "prosperous," often associated with growth and fertility.
    • yāti: Means "goes" or "moves," indicating progress or movement.
    • dhārayā: Refers to "by holding" or "by supporting," indicating stability and strength.
    • mandrayā: Means "by the gentle" or "by the soft," indicating a nurturing aspect.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of Soma, symbolizing the divine and the life-giving properties of nature.
    • The mention of "singers" and "those who are united" emphasizes the importance of community and collective effort in achieving harmony and prosperity.
    • The comparison to a horse signifies strength, speed, and the ability to overcome obstacles, while the terms "green" and "prosperous" highlight the importance of growth and fertility in life.
    • The duality of "holding" and "gentleness" suggests that strength can coexist with compassion, and both are necessary for a balanced life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied to emphasize teamwork and collaboration, where individuals come together to support each other, much like a choir harmonizing to create beautiful music.
    • In personal development, the mantra encourages individuals to harness their inner strength (like the horse) while also being nurturing and gentle with themselves and others.
    • In agriculture, the concepts of growth and prosperity can be seen in sustainable farming practices that respect nature and promote ecological balance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of life, the importance of community, and the balance between strength and gentleness.
    • It serves as a reminder that true prosperity comes from unity and collective effort, and that nurturing relationships and environments lead to growth and fulfillment.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more harmonious relationships, successful teamwork, and a deeper appreciation for the natural world.
