राज्ञो नु ते वरुणस्य व्रतानि बृहद् गभीरं तव सोम धाम | शुचिष् ṭवम असि प्रियो न मित्रो दक्षाय्यो आर्यमेवासी सोम ||
rājño nu te varuṇasya vratāni bṛhad gabhīraṁ tava soma dhāma | śuciṣ ṭvam asi priyo na mitro dakṣāyyo aryamevāsi soma ||
  • Translation: "O Varuna, the king, your great and profound vows are like the essence of Soma. You are pure, and you are dear to us; you are the friend who is skillful, and you are Aryama, the essence of Soma."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • rājño: of the king
    • nu: indeed
    • te: your
    • varuṇasya: of Varuna (the god of cosmic order)
    • vratāni: vows or promises
    • bṛhad: great
    • gabhīraṁ: profound or deep
    • tava: your
    • soma: the sacred drink, representing divine inspiration
    • dhāma: essence or abode
    • śuciṣ: pure
    • ṭvam: you
    • asi: are
    • priyo: dear
    • na: not
    • mitro: friend
    • dakṣāyyo: skillful
    • aryamevāsi: you are Aryama (another deity associated with friendship and harmony)
    • soma: again referring to the essence of Soma
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra invokes Varuna, the deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority, emphasizing the importance of vows and promises in maintaining harmony in society.
    • The reference to Soma highlights the idea of divine inspiration and the sacredness of life, suggesting that purity and devotion are essential for spiritual growth.
    • By calling Varuna a friend, the mantra illustrates the relationship between humans and the divine, where the divine is seen as a protector and guide.
  • Real-Life Applications:
    • In personal relationships, the mantra can remind individuals of the importance of keeping promises and being trustworthy, which are foundational for strong bonds.
    • In leadership, it serves as a reminder for leaders to uphold their commitments and act with integrity, fostering trust within their communities.
    • In spiritual practices, it encourages individuals to seek purity in thoughts and actions, aligning themselves with higher ideals and divine guidance.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of vows, purity, and the divine connection in our lives. It serves as a reminder to uphold integrity, foster relationships based on trust, and seek divine inspiration in our actions. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a harmonious and balanced society, reflecting the cosmic order that Varuna represents.
