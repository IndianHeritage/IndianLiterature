आत्मा ते वातो रज आ नवीनोत पशुर न भूर्णिर यवसे ससवान् |अन्तर मही बृहती रोडसीमे विश्वा ते धाम वरुण प्रियाणि ||
ātmā te vāto raja ā navīnot paśur na bhūrṇir yavase sasavān | antar mahī bṛhatī rodasīme viśvā te dhāma varuṇa priyāṇi ||
  • ātmā te vāto raja ā navīnot: This phrase can be translated as "Your spirit, O Wind, is ever new." Here, "ātmā" refers to the essence or spirit, while "vāto" signifies the wind or breath. This suggests a connection between the divine and the natural elements, emphasizing the idea that the spirit is rejuvenated and constantly in motion.
  • paśur na bhūrṇir yavase sasavān: This translates to "The creature does not perish; it thrives in abundance." "Paśur" refers to living beings or creatures, and "bhūrṇir" implies destruction or death. This line conveys the idea of resilience and the continuity of life, suggesting that despite challenges, life persists and flourishes.
  • antar mahī bṛhatī rodasīme: This can be interpreted as "Within the vastness of the earth and the heavens." "Antar" means within, "mahī" refers to the earth, and "bṛhatī" signifies vastness. This line highlights the interconnectedness of all existence, suggesting that everything is part of a larger cosmic order.
  • viśvā te dhāma varuṇa priyāṇi: This translates to "All realms are your abode, O Varuna, beloved." "Viśvā" means all or universal, "dhāma" refers to abode or dwelling, and "Varuna" is a deity associated with cosmic order and water. This line emphasizes the omnipresence of the divine and the idea that all aspects of life are under the watchful eye of a higher power.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates profound philosophical concepts about the nature of existence, the resilience of life, and the omnipresence of the divine. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In moments of personal struggle, the mantra serves as a reminder of the enduring spirit and the ability to thrive despite challenges.
  • In environmental discussions, it highlights the interconnectedness of all living beings and the importance of nurturing our planet.
  • In spiritual practices, it encourages individuals to recognize the divine presence in all aspects of life, fostering a sense of unity and reverence for the cosmos.

Overall, this mantra encourages a holistic view of life, urging individuals to embrace change, recognize resilience, and acknowledge the divine presence in the universe.
