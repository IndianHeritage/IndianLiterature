द्यम्नेषु पृतनाज्ये पृत्सुतूर्षु श्रवःसु च |इन्द्र साक्ष्वाभिमातिषु ||
dyumneṣu pṛtanājye pṛtsutūrṣu śravaḥsu ca | indra sākṣvābhimātiṣu ||
  • dyumneṣu: This word refers to the celestial or heavenly realms. It signifies the vastness of the sky and the universe, representing the divine and the spiritual.
  • pṛtanājye: This term can be interpreted as the act of invoking or calling upon. It suggests a connection to the divine through prayer or meditation.
  • pṛtsutūrṣu: This word relates to the earth and its various forms. It emphasizes the importance of the physical world and our connection to it.
  • śravaḥsu: This term means fame or glory. It indicates the pursuit of recognition and honor, both in the spiritual and material realms.
  • indra: Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, power, and the protection of the cosmos. He represents the force that overcomes obstacles.
  • sākṣvābhimātiṣu: This phrase can be understood as the direct perception or realization of divine will or intention. It suggests a deep understanding and connection with the divine.

The mantra as a whole can be interpreted as a call to the divine, seeking guidance and strength from the celestial realms while acknowledging the importance of the earthly existence. It emphasizes the balance between the spiritual and material worlds, urging individuals to seek fame and glory through righteous actions and a connection with the divine.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Spiritual Practice: Individuals can use this mantra during meditation or prayer to connect with higher consciousness and seek guidance in their lives.
  • Personal Growth: The emphasis on seeking fame and glory can inspire individuals to pursue their passions and strive for excellence in their chosen fields, while remaining grounded in their values.
  • Community Engagement: The mantra encourages individuals to contribute positively to society, recognizing that their actions can have a lasting impact on the world around them.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the spiritual and material worlds. It encourages individuals to seek divine guidance while actively engaging in their earthly pursuits, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and meaningful life.
