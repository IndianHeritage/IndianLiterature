यं वर्धयन्तीद गिरः पतिं तुरस्य राधसः |तम इन् nv अस्य rodasī देवी शुष्मं सपर्यतः ||
yaṁ vardhayantīd giraḥ patiṁ turasya rādhasaḥ | tam in nv asya rodasī devī śuṣmaṁ saparyataḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: whom
    • vardhayantī: nourish or increase
    • giraḥ: words or speech
    • patiṁ: lord or master
    • turasya: of the swift or powerful
    • rādhasaḥ: of the radiant or shining
    • tam: that
    • in: in
    • nv: indeed
    • asya: of this
    • rodasī: the two worlds or realms
    • devī: goddess or divine being
    • śuṣmaṁ: the most excellent or supreme
    • saparyataḥ: with reverence or worship
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power of words and speech in nourishing and elevating the divine presence.
    • The phrase "whom words nourish" suggests that the right words can uplift and empower individuals, much like how positive affirmations can boost self-esteem in real life.
    • The reference to "the lord of the swift" indicates a connection to speed and efficiency, which can be likened to how effective communication can lead to quick resolutions in personal and professional settings.
    • The mention of "the two worlds" signifies the connection between the physical and spiritual realms, emphasizing the importance of maintaining a balance between material pursuits and spiritual growth.
    • The invocation of the goddess suggests a call for divine guidance and support in our endeavors, similar to how people often seek inspiration from mentors or spiritual leaders in their lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, effective communication can lead to better teamwork and project success, illustrating the power of words in a professional setting.
    • In personal relationships, expressing love and appreciation can strengthen bonds, showcasing how nourishing words can enhance connections.
    • In spiritual practices, chanting or reciting mantras can create a sense of peace and connection to the divine, reflecting the significance of reverence in our lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of words and communication in our lives, urging us to use them wisely to uplift ourselves and others.
    • It serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our endeavors and the importance of maintaining a balance between the material and spiritual aspects of life.
    • In essence, it encourages us to approach our interactions with reverence and mindfulness, recognizing the power of our words to shape our reality.
