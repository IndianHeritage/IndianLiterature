वाजेभिर नो वाजसाताव अविढ्ध्य ऋभुमाम् इन्द्र चित्रम आ दर्षि राधः |तन नो मित्रो वरुणो मामहन्ताम् अदितिः सिन्धुः पृथिवी उता द्यौः ||
vājebhir no vājasātāv aviḍḍhy ṛbhumām̐ indra citram ā darṣi rādhaḥ | tan no mitro varuṇo māmahantām aditiḥ sindhuḥ pṛthivī uta dyauḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vājebhir: With strength or vigor
    • no: Our
    • vājasātāv: In the form of strength or power
    • aviḍḍhy: Not to be harmed
    • ṛbhumām̐: O Indra, the one who is worthy of praise
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • citram: Wonderful or diverse
    • ā darṣi: May you show or reveal
    • rādhaḥ: Prosperity or wealth
    • tan: That
    • no: Our
    • mitro: O Mitra, the friend
    • varuṇo: O Varuna, the cosmic order
    • māmahantām: May you be great or powerful
    • aditiḥ: Aditi, the infinite or boundless
    • sindhuḥ: The ocean or river
    • pṛthivī: The earth
    • uta: And
    • dyauḥ: The sky or heaven
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine powers of Indra, Mitra, and Varuna, seeking their blessings for strength and protection.
    • The phrase "vājebhir no vājasātāv" emphasizes the desire for strength and vigor in our lives, akin to how athletes train to enhance their physical capabilities.
    • Indra is called upon to reveal prosperity ("rādhaḥ") in a wonderful manner, similar to how a mentor guides a student towards success.
    • Mitra and Varuna represent friendship and cosmic order, reminding us of the importance of relationships and harmony in our lives, much like how teamwork is essential in any collaborative effort.
    • The mention of Aditi signifies the boundless potential we all possess, akin to the limitless opportunities available in the modern world.
    • The elements of nature—ocean, earth, and sky—highlight the interconnectedness of all things, similar to how environmental conservation efforts aim to protect our planet.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the strength and support we can seek from the divine and the universe.
    • It encourages us to cultivate relationships, embrace our potential, and strive for prosperity in a harmonious manner.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in personal development, teamwork, and environmental stewardship, emphasizing the importance of balance and cooperation in achieving our goals.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us to recognize the divine presence in our lives and the strength that comes from unity and purpose.
