मन्त्र: भद्राः ते अग्नि स्वनिका संदृग घोरस्य सतो विषुणस्य चारुः |न यत् ते शोचिस तमसा वरन्त न ध्वस्मानस् तन्वीं रेपा आ धुः ||
bhadrā te agne svanīka saṁdṛg ghorasya sato viṣuṇasya cāruḥ | na yat te śocis tamasā varanta na dhvasmānas tanvī3 repa ā dhuḥ ||
  • bhadrā te agne: This phrase translates to "May your blessings, O Agni (the Fire God), be auspicious." Agni is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine, representing purity and transformation.
  • svanīka: This word means "with a pleasant sound" or "melodious." It signifies the importance of harmony and positivity in our lives.
  • saṁdṛg: This term can be interpreted as "to see" or "to perceive." It emphasizes the need for awareness and understanding in our actions and thoughts.
  • ghorasya sato viṣuṇasya: This translates to "of the fierce and true." It suggests that even in the face of challenges or fierce situations, one must remain truthful and steadfast.
  • cāruḥ: Meaning "beautiful" or "pleasant," this word highlights the importance of beauty in our lives, whether in nature, relationships, or experiences.
  • na yat te śocis: This phrase means "do not let sorrow come to you." It serves as a reminder to avoid negativity and sorrow in our lives.
  • tamasā varanta: This translates to "to dispel darkness." It signifies the importance of knowledge and enlightenment in overcoming ignorance and despair.
  • na dhvasmānas: Meaning "do not let the body decay," this phrase emphasizes the significance of maintaining physical and mental well-being.
  • tanvī3 repa ā dhuḥ: This can be interpreted as "may the body be strong and healthy." It calls for the nurturing of our physical form to lead a fulfilling life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about seeking blessings, maintaining positivity, and striving for truth and beauty in life. It encourages individuals to be aware of their surroundings, dispel negativity, and focus on health and well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in daily practices such as meditation, affirmations, and conscious living. By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can cultivate a life filled with auspiciousness, harmony, and strength, ultimately leading to personal and communal growth.
