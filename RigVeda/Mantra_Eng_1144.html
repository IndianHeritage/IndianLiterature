अधां मण्ये श्रत ते अस्मा अधायि वृषा चोदस्व महते धनाय |मा नो अकृत पुरुहूत योनाव इन्द्र क्षुध्यद्भ्यो वय आसुतिं दाह ||
adhā manye śrat te asmā adhāyi vṛṣā codasva mahate dhanāya | mā no akṛte puruhūta yonāv indra kṣudhyadbhyo vaya āsutiṁ dāḥ ||
  • adhā manye śrat te asmā: This phrase can be translated as "I think of you, O Indra, with great attention." Here, the speaker expresses a deep reverence and focus on the deity Indra, who is often associated with strength and power.
  • adhāyi vṛṣā codasva mahate dhanāya: This part means "You who are the great bull, inspire us towards great wealth." Indra is referred to as a "bull," symbolizing strength and fertility, and the plea is for inspiration towards prosperity and abundance.
  • mā no akṛte puruhūta: This translates to "Do not let us be harmed, O many-named one." The speaker seeks protection from harm, invoking Indra's multiple names, which signify his various attributes and powers.
  • yonāv indra kṣudhyadbhyo vaya āsutiṁ dāḥ: This can be interpreted as "O Indra, grant us strength against those who are hostile." The speaker asks for strength and resilience in the face of adversities and challenges.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and protection in our lives. It reflects a deep connection with the divine, acknowledging the need for strength, wealth, and safety.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, individuals often face challenges that require resilience and support. This mantra can be recited during times of difficulty, such as financial struggles or personal conflicts, to invoke a sense of strength and protection. For example, a business owner facing tough competition might recite this mantra to seek inspiration and fortitude to overcome obstacles.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of faith and the importance of seeking help from higher forces. It encourages individuals to remain focused on their goals while also being aware of the need for protection and guidance in their endeavors. By invoking the qualities of Indra, one can cultivate a mindset of strength and resilience, applicable in various aspects of life, from personal challenges to professional pursuits.
