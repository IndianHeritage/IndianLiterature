शेवारे वार्याऽपुरु देवो मर्ताय दाशुषे |स सुन्वते च स्तुवते च रासते विश्वगूर्तो अरिष्टुतः ||
śevāre vāryā puru devo martāya dāśuṣe | sa sunvate ca stuvate ca rāsate viśvagūrto ariṣṭutaḥ ||
  • śevāre vāryā puru devo martāya dāśuṣe
    • śevāre: This term can be interpreted as "in the evening" or "at dusk," symbolizing a time of reflection and transition.
    • vāryā: This word means "to be protected" or "to be shielded," indicating a desire for safety and security.
    • puru: This translates to "great" or "many," often referring to the vastness of the universe or the multitude of beings.
    • devo: Meaning "god" or "divine," it signifies the presence of a higher power or spiritual essence.
    • martāya: This term refers to "mortals" or "human beings," highlighting the connection between the divine and humanity.
    • dāśuṣe: This can be interpreted as "to the one who serves" or "to the devotee," emphasizing the importance of service and devotion.
  • sa sunvate ca stuvate ca rāsate viśvagūrto ariṣṭutaḥ
    • sa: This means "he" or "that," referring to the divine presence mentioned earlier.
    • sunvate: This translates to "those who listen" or "those who hear," indicating the importance of being receptive to divine guidance.
    • ca: Meaning "and," it connects the ideas presented in the mantra.
    • stuvate: This means "those who praise" or "those who worship," emphasizing the act of devotion and reverence.
    • rāsate: This can be interpreted as "those who rejoice" or "those who celebrate," highlighting the joy that comes from spiritual connection.
    • viśvagūrto: This term means "the teacher of the universe," indicating a universal wisdom that transcends individual understanding.
    • ariṣṭutaḥ: This translates to "from all that is harmful" or "from all obstacles," suggesting a plea for protection from negativity and adversity.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates a profound relationship between the divine and humanity, emphasizing the importance of protection, devotion, and the pursuit of wisdom. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios:

  • During challenging times, individuals can recite this mantra to seek divine protection and guidance, fostering a sense of security.
  • In moments of reflection, such as during meditation or prayer, this mantra serves as a reminder to listen to divine wisdom and to express gratitude through praise.
  • In community gatherings or spiritual practices, the mantra can be chanted to create a collective sense of joy and celebration, reinforcing the bonds of unity and shared purpose.

Ultimately, this mantra encourages individuals to cultivate a deeper connection with the divine, to serve others, and to seek wisdom that transcends personal challenges, fostering a harmonious existence in the universe.
