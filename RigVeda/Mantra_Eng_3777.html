अस्य वासाऽउ अर्चिषा य आयुक्त तुजा गिरा |दिवो न यस्य रेतसा बृहच् शोचन्त्य् अर्चयः ||
asya vāsā u arciṣā ya āyukta tujā girā | divo na yasya retasā bṛhac chocanty arcayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asya: of this
    • vāsā: dwelling or abode
    • u: and
    • arciṣā: by the light or radiance
    • ya: who
    • āyukta: is connected or united
    • tujā: with your (feminine)
    • girā: speech or sound
    • divo: of the sky or heaven
    • na: not
    • yasya: whose
    • retasā: by the essence or energy
    • bṛhac: great or vast
    • shocanty: they shine or illuminate
    • arcayaḥ: offerings or praises
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine abode that is illuminated by the radiance of a higher power.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the divine and the earthly through speech and offerings.
    • The phrase "divo na yasya retasā" suggests that the essence of the divine is not limited to the heavens but is also present in the earthly realm.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be related to the idea of finding divinity in our surroundings, such as in nature or in acts of kindness.
    • For instance, when we engage in community service, we are creating a connection between the divine and the earthly, embodying the essence of the mantra.
    • Moreover, in moments of reflection or meditation, we can visualize the radiance of our thoughts and words as a way to connect with a higher power.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine.
    • It encourages us to recognize the power of our words and actions in creating a harmonious existence.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as in communication, relationships, and spiritual practices, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
