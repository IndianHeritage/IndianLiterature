पृक्षप्रयजो द्रविणः सुवाचः सुकेतव उषसो रेवद ऊषुः |उतो चिद अग्ने महिना पृथिव्याः कृतं चिद एनः सम महे दशस्य ||
pṛkṣaprayajo draviṇaḥ suvācaḥ suketava uṣaso revad ūṣuḥ | uto cid agne mahinā pṛthivyāḥ kṛtaṁ cid enaḥ sam mahe daśasya ||
  • pṛkṣaprayajo: This term can be broken down into "pṛkṣa" meaning "to be vigilant" or "to observe" and "prayajo" which refers to "the one who is born from" or "the one who is produced." This suggests a call for awareness and mindfulness in our actions.
  • draviṇaḥ: This word translates to "wealth" or "resources." It signifies the importance of material and spiritual wealth in our lives, emphasizing that both are essential for a fulfilling existence.
  • suvācaḥ: Meaning "well-spoken" or "good speech," this term highlights the value of communication and the impact of our words on others. It reminds us to speak positively and constructively.
  • suketava: This can be interpreted as "good fortune" or "auspiciousness." It suggests that we should strive for positive outcomes and blessings in our endeavors.
  • uṣaso: Referring to "dawn" or "morning," this symbolizes new beginnings and opportunities. It encourages us to embrace each day with hope and optimism.
  • revad ūṣuḥ: This phrase can be understood as "the one who brings forth" or "the one who generates." It signifies the creative forces in nature and within ourselves that lead to growth and development.
  • uto cid agne: "Agne" refers to "fire" or "the divine light," often associated with knowledge and enlightenment. This part of the mantra calls upon the divine for guidance and illumination in our lives.
  • mahinā pṛthivyāḥ: This translates to "with the greatness of the earth." It emphasizes the connection between the divine and the material world, reminding us of our roots and the importance of nature in our lives.
  • kṛtaṁ cid enaḥ: This phrase means "whatever has been done" or "that which has been created." It reflects on the actions we take and their consequences, urging us to be mindful of our choices.
  • sam mahe daśasya: "Sam" means "together" or "in harmony," while "daśasya" refers to "the ten" or "the complete." This suggests the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving our goals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the significance of awareness, communication, and connection with both the material and spiritual realms. In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to be mindful of our actions, to communicate positively, and to seek harmony in our relationships. By embracing these principles, we can foster personal growth, cultivate good fortune, and contribute to a more harmonious society. This mantra serves as a reminder to approach each day with optimism and a commitment to making meaningful choices that benefit ourselves and those around us.
