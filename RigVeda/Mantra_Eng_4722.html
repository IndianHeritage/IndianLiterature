तं वो धियाऽ परमया पुराजाम् अजरोम् इन्द्रम् अभ्य अनूष्य अर्कैः |ब्रह्मा च गिर्वण दधिरे सम अस्मिन् महाम् च स्तोमोऽधि वर्धद इन्द्रे ||
taṁ vo dhiyā paramayā purājām ajaram indram abhy anūṣy arkaiḥ | brahmā ca giro dadhire sam asmin mahām̐ś ca stomo adhi vardhad indre ||
  • taṁ vo dhiyā paramayā purājām: This phrase can be translated to "May we attain that supreme wisdom which is ancient." Here, "taṁ" refers to "that," "vo" means "to you," "dhiyā" translates to "wisdom," "paramayā" means "supreme," and "purājām" refers to "ancient." This suggests a quest for timeless knowledge that transcends generations.
  • ajaram indram abhy anūṣy arkaiḥ: This part means "the imperishable Indra, who is invoked with hymns." "Ajaram" signifies "imperishable," "Indram" refers to the deity Indra, and "anūṣy arkaiḥ" means "invoked with hymns." Indra represents strength and power, and this line emphasizes the importance of invoking divine forces through devotion and praise.
  • brahmā ca giro dadhire sam asmin: This translates to "Brahma and the words have established themselves in this." "Brahma" refers to the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit, "giro" means "words," and "dadhire" translates to "have established." This indicates that the essence of creation and the power of speech are intertwined, suggesting that words hold significant power in shaping reality.
  • mahām̐ś ca stomo adhi vardhad indre: This means "the great hymn has increased in Indra." "Mahām̐ś" refers to "great," "stomo" means "hymn," "adhi" translates to "in," and "vardhad" means "has increased." This line highlights the idea that through devotion and the recitation of hymns, one can enhance their connection with the divine, particularly with Indra.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the pursuit of supreme wisdom, the importance of invoking divine powers, and the transformative nature of words and hymns. It reflects the belief that through devotion and the right use of language, one can connect with higher realities and enhance their spiritual journey.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various ways. For instance, individuals seeking knowledge and wisdom can engage in practices such as meditation, prayer, or studying ancient texts. The invocation of divine powers can be seen in rituals and ceremonies where people seek blessings for strength and guidance. Additionally, the power of words is evident in communication, where positive affirmations and constructive dialogue can lead to personal and communal growth.
