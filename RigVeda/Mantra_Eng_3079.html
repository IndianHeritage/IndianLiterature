यस् ते भराद् अन्नियते चिद् अन्नं निषिषन् मन्द्रम् अतिथिम उदीरत् |आ देवयुर इनधते दुरोणे तस्मिन् रयिर ध्रुवो अस्तु दास्वान् ||
yas te bharād anniyate cid annaṁ niśiṣan mandram atithim udīrat | ā devayur inadhate duroṇe tasmin rayir dhruvo astu dāsvān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • bharād: from the abundance
    • anniyate: is nourished
    • cid: consciousness
    • annaṁ: food
    • niśiṣan: in the night
    • mandram: gentle
    • atithim: guest
    • udīrat: is invoked
    • ā: to
    • devayur: divine energy
    • inadhate: is established
    • duroṇe: in the abode
    • tasmin: in that
    • rayir: wealth
    • dhruvo: stable
    • astu: may it be
    • dāsvān: for the giver
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nourishment that comes from abundance and the importance of consciousness in our lives.
    • It emphasizes the significance of hospitality, as it mentions invoking the guest with gentleness and care.
    • The reference to divine energy suggests that when we align ourselves with higher principles, we can establish stability and wealth in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, sharing food with guests reflects the essence of this mantra. It shows respect and care, fostering relationships.
    • In a business context, creating a nurturing environment for employees can lead to increased productivity and stability, akin to the wealth mentioned in the mantra.
    • Practicing mindfulness and consciousness in daily activities can enhance our understanding and appreciation of abundance in our lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of nourishment, consciousness, and hospitality.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to cultivate a nurturing environment for ourselves and others, leading to stability and abundance.
    • In real life, applying these principles can enhance personal relationships, workplace dynamics, and overall well-being.
