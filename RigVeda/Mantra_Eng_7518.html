स न इन्द्रः शिवः सखाश्वावद गोमद यवमत् |उरुधारेव दोहते ||
sa na indraḥ śivaḥ sakhāśvāvad gomad yavamat | urudhāreva dohate ||
  • sa na indraḥ: This phrase translates to "may Indra be with us." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. In real life, this can be interpreted as seeking divine support and guidance in our endeavors.
  • śivaḥ: The term "śivaḥ" means "auspicious" or "beneficial." It signifies the importance of positivity and good fortune in our lives. For example, when starting a new project or venture, invoking auspiciousness can lead to better outcomes.
  • sakhāśvāvad: This word can be understood as "like a friend." It emphasizes the value of friendship and companionship. In real life, having supportive friends can significantly impact our mental well-being and success in various aspects of life.
  • gomad: This term refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." It highlights the importance of material and spiritual wealth in our lives. For instance, financial stability can provide security and opportunities for personal growth.
  • yavamat: This word means "may we attain." It expresses a desire for achievement and fulfillment. In practical terms, this can relate to setting goals and striving to reach them, whether in career, education, or personal development.
  • urudhāreva: This phrase translates to "like a strong support." It signifies the need for a solid foundation in our lives. For example, having a supportive family or community can provide the strength needed to overcome challenges.
  • dohate: This term means "to nourish" or "to provide." It emphasizes the importance of nurturing relationships and environments that foster growth. In real life, this can be seen in the way we care for our loved ones or contribute to our communities.

Conclusion: The mantra "sa na indraḥ śivaḥ sakhāśvāvad gomad yavamat | urudhāreva dohate ||" encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support, auspiciousness, friendship, prosperity, achievement, strong foundations, and nourishment in our lives. Its significance lies in the holistic approach it advocates for personal and communal well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied when embarking on new journeys, fostering relationships, and striving for success, reminding us to seek support and positivity in all our endeavors.
