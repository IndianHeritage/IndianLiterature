आक्ष्णयावानो वहन्त्य अन्तरिक्षेण पततः |धातारः स्तुवते वयः ||
ākṣṇayāvāno vahanty antarikṣeṇa patataḥ | dhātāraḥ stuvate vayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ākṣṇayā: With the eyes or vision.
    • vāno: The winds or the air.
    • vahanty: Carrying or transporting.
    • antarikṣeṇa: In the atmosphere or space.
    • patataḥ: Falling or descending.
    • dhātāraḥ: The creators or the divine beings.
    • stuvate: Praise or worship.
    • vayaḥ: Our or us.
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra speaks of the divine forces that carry the winds through the atmosphere, descending from the heavens. It emphasizes the act of praise towards these creators, acknowledging their role in the natural order and the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Nature's Balance: Just as the winds carry seeds and pollen, facilitating growth and reproduction in plants, this mantra reminds us of the importance of respecting and nurturing our environment.
    • Human Connection: The winds can symbolize communication and connection among people. Just as air carries sound, our words and actions can connect us to others, highlighting the importance of positive interactions.
    • Spiritual Reflection: The act of praising the divine can be seen in various cultures through rituals and prayers, reminding us to express gratitude for the forces that sustain life.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of the divine forces that govern nature and the importance of gratitude. In real life, it encourages us to appreciate the interconnectedness of all things, to respect the environment, and to foster positive relationships with others. By acknowledging these forces, we cultivate a sense of harmony and balance in our lives.
