उपहूताः पितरः सोम्यासो बरहिष्येषु निधिषु प्रियेषु | त आ गमन्तु त इह श्रुवन्त्व अधि ब्रुवन्तु ते 'वन्त्व अस्मान् ||
upahūtāḥ pitaraḥ somyāso barhiṣyeṣu nidhiṣu priyeṣu | ta ā gamantu ta iha śruvantv adhi bruvantu te 'vantv asmān ||
  • upahūtāḥ pitaraḥ: This phrase translates to "the invoked fathers" or "the ancestors who are called upon." In many cultures, including Hinduism, ancestors are revered and called upon for blessings and guidance.
  • somyāso: This word means "with a pleasant disposition" or "joyful." It signifies the positive and benevolent nature of the ancestors when they are invoked.
  • barhiṣyeṣu: This term refers to "the sacred grass" or "the offerings." In Vedic rituals, barhis (sacred grass) is often used as a symbol of purity and is associated with offerings made to deities and ancestors.
  • nidhiṣu: This means "in the treasures" or "in the wealth." It suggests that the blessings and wisdom of the ancestors are like treasures that can be accessed through invocation.
  • priyeṣu: This translates to "in the beloved" or "in the dear ones." It indicates that the blessings are not just for the individual but also for their loved ones.
  • ta ā gamantu: This phrase means "let them come here." It is an invitation for the ancestors to join the ritual and bless the participants.
  • ta iha śruvantv: This translates to "let them hear here." It emphasizes the importance of communication and connection with the ancestors during the ritual.
  • adhi bruvantu: This means "let them speak." It signifies the desire for the ancestors to impart their wisdom and guidance to the living.
  • te 'vantv asmān: This phrase translates to "may they come to us." It is a plea for the presence and blessings of the ancestors in the lives of the participants.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of invoking ancestors and seeking their blessings. In real-life scenarios, this can be applicable during family gatherings, religious ceremonies, or personal moments of reflection. By honoring our ancestors, we acknowledge their contributions to our lives and seek their guidance in our endeavors. This practice fosters a sense of connection to our roots and reinforces the values of family and community. The mantra serves as a reminder that the wisdom of our forebears can guide us in our present and future, enriching our lives with their blessings.
