सँजार्भुराणस तरुभिः सुतेगृहं वयाकिनं चित्तगर्भासु सुस्वरुः |धारवाकेष्व ऋजुगाथा शोभसे वर्धस्व पत्नीर अभि जीवो अध्वरे ||
saṁjarbhurāṇas tarubhiḥ sutegṛbhaṁ vayākinaṁ cittagarbhāsu susvaruḥ | dhāravākeṣv ṛjugātha śobhase vardhasva patnīr abhi jīvo adhvare ||
  • saṁjarbhurāṇas: This term can be interpreted as "the one who brings together" or "the one who unites." In real life, this can refer to a leader or a person who fosters collaboration among a group.
  • tarubhiḥ: This word means "with the trees" or "among the trees." Trees symbolize life, growth, and stability. For example, a community that plants trees together can symbolize unity and environmental consciousness.
  • sutegṛbhaṁ: This can be translated as "the one who is well-established" or "the one who has a good home." It signifies a stable and nurturing environment, akin to a family that provides support and love.
  • vayākinaṁ: This term refers to "the one who is wise" or "the one who speaks well." In a real-life context, this could represent a mentor or a teacher who imparts knowledge and wisdom to others.
  • cittagarbhāsu: This phrase means "in the hearts" or "in the minds." It emphasizes the importance of inner thoughts and feelings, suggesting that true understanding comes from within.
  • susvaruḥ: This translates to "having a good sound" or "pleasant voice." It can refer to someone whose words inspire and uplift others, much like a motivational speaker or a leader who encourages their team.
  • dhāravākeṣv: This term means "in the bearers" or "in the supporters." It highlights the significance of those who support and carry forward a cause or a mission, similar to volunteers in a community service project.
  • ṛjugātha: This can be interpreted as "the straight path" or "the righteous way." It signifies integrity and honesty, akin to a person who always chooses to do the right thing, even when faced with challenges.
  • śobhase: This word means "you shine" or "you are glorious." It reflects the idea of radiating positivity and excellence, much like a successful individual who inspires others through their achievements.
  • vardhasva: This translates to "may you grow" or "may you prosper." It is a blessing for growth and success, applicable in various life scenarios, such as wishing success for a friend starting a new business.
  • patnīr: This term means "wives" or "partners." It emphasizes the importance of relationships and partnerships in life, akin to the support one receives from family and friends.
  • abhi: This word means "towards" or "to." It indicates direction and intention, suggesting a purposeful approach to life.
  • jīvo: This translates to "live" or "be alive." It signifies the essence of life and the importance of living fully and authentically.
  • adhvare: This term means "in the sacrifice" or "in the offering." It reflects the idea of giving back to the community or making sacrifices for a greater good, similar to charitable acts or community service.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, wisdom, and support in our lives. It encourages individuals to foster collaboration, maintain integrity, and inspire others through their actions and words. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community building, leadership, and personal relationships. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can contribute to a harmonious and prosperous society, ultimately leading to personal and collective growth.
