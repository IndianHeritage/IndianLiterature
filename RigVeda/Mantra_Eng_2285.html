तव स्याम पूर्वीरस्य शर्मन् उरुशंसस्य वरुण प्रणेताḥ |यूयं नः पुत्रा अदितेर अदब्धा अभि क्षमध्वं युज्याय देवाः ||
tava syāma puruvīrasya śarmann uruśaṁsasya varuṇa praṇetaḥ | yūyaṁ naḥ putrā aditer adabdhā abhi kṣamadhvaṁ yujyāya devāḥ ||
  • tava syāma puruvīrasya: This phrase can be translated as "Your (O Varuna) dark (or powerful) one, of the many heroes." Here, "tava" means "your," "syāma" can refer to "dark" or "powerful," and "puruvīrasya" refers to "of many heroes." This highlights the strength and valor associated with Varuna, a deity representing cosmic order and moral authority.
  • śarmann uruśaṁsasya: This translates to "in the shelter of the great one." "Śarman" means "shelter" or "protection," and "uruśaṁsasya" refers to "of the great one." This indicates the importance of seeking refuge in divine protection, emphasizing the need for guidance and safety in life.
  • varuṇa praṇetaḥ: This means "Varuna, the guide." "Praṇetaḥ" refers to a leader or guide, suggesting that Varuna is a source of direction and wisdom. In real life, this can be likened to seeking guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders who help navigate life's challenges.
  • yūyaṁ naḥ putrā aditer: This translates to "You (gods) are our sons of Aditi." "Yūyaṁ" means "you," "naḥ" means "our," "putrā" means "sons," and "Aditi" refers to the mother of the gods. This line signifies a connection to the divine and the idea that the gods are protectors and nurturers, akin to parental figures in our lives.
  • adabdhā abhi kṣamadhvaṁ: This means "unharmed, be forgiving." "Adabdhā" translates to "unharmed" or "untouched," and "kṣamadhvaṁ" means "be forgiving." This emphasizes the importance of forgiveness and resilience in the face of adversity, encouraging individuals to rise above challenges without harboring resentment.
  • yujyāya devāḥ: This translates to "O gods, for our union." "Yujyāya" means "for our union" or "for our connection," and "devāḥ" refers to "gods." This line calls for divine support in fostering unity and harmony among people, which is essential for societal well-being.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, protection, and forgiveness. It highlights the importance of unity and the role of the divine in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as family dynamics, community relationships, and personal growth. By invoking the qualities of strength, protection, and forgiveness, individuals can navigate challenges more effectively and foster a sense of harmony in their surroundings. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine support available to us in our journey through life.
