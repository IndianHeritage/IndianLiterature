क्वेयथा क्वेदासी पुरुत्रा चिद्धि ते मनः |अलर्षि युध्म खजकृत पुरंदर प्र गायत्री अगासिषुः ||
kveyatha kved asi purutrā cid dhi te manaḥ | alarṣi yudhma khajakṛt puraṁdara pra gāyatrā agāsiṣuḥ ||
  • kveyatha: This word can be interpreted as "where" or "in what place." It signifies a quest for understanding or seeking a location of significance.
  • kved: This translates to "where are you?" It reflects a sense of inquiry, often directed towards a divine or higher power.
  • asi: Meaning "you are," this word emphasizes existence and presence, suggesting a connection between the seeker and the divine.
  • purutrā: This term can be understood as "in many places" or "everywhere," indicating the omnipresence of the divine or the universal spirit.
  • cid: This word means "consciousness" or "awareness," pointing to the idea that the divine is not just a distant entity but is also a part of our awareness.
  • dhi: Translated as "intellect" or "thought," it suggests that understanding and wisdom are essential in the pursuit of knowledge and connection with the divine.
  • te: Meaning "your," this word personalizes the inquiry, making it a direct address to the divine.
  • manaḥ: This translates to "mind" or "heart," indicating the emotional and mental aspects of the seeker in their quest for understanding.
  • alarṣi: This term can be interpreted as "not angry" or "calm," suggesting that the divine is approachable and not wrathful.
  • yudhma: Meaning "battle" or "struggle," it reflects the challenges faced in life and the need for divine assistance in overcoming them.
  • khajakṛt: This can be understood as "the creator of the sky," referring to a divine force that is responsible for the vastness of the universe.
  • puraṁdara: This term is often associated with Indra, the king of gods, symbolizing strength and power in overcoming obstacles.
  • pra: Meaning "forth" or "forward," it indicates movement or progression, suggesting a journey towards enlightenment.
  • gāyatrā: This word refers to a sacred verse or hymn, emphasizing the importance of sound and vibration in spiritual practice.
  • agāsiṣuḥ: This can be interpreted as "you have come," indicating the arrival of divine presence or guidance in the seeker’s life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound inquiry into the nature of the divine and its relationship with the seeker. It emphasizes the importance of awareness, intellect, and emotional connection in understanding the omnipresence of the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during moments of introspection, meditation, or when facing challenges. It serves as a reminder that the divine is always present, guiding us through our struggles and encouraging us to seek knowledge and understanding. By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of peace, clarity, and connection with the greater universe, ultimately leading to personal growth and enlightenment.
