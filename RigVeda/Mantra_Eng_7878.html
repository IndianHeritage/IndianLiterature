मन्त्र: एते पृष्ठानि रोधसोर्विप्रयन्तो व्यानशुः | उतेदं उत्तमं रजः ||
ete pṛṣṭhāni rodasor viprayanto vy ānaśuḥ | utedam uttamaṁ rajaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ete: these
    • pṛṣṭhāni: backs or supports
    • rodasor: of the heavens or the sky
    • viprayanto: are separated or distinguished
    • vy ānaśuḥ: they are moving or flowing
    • utedam: this is elevated or supreme
    • uttamaṁ: the best or highest
    • rajaḥ: the energy or essence
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the separation and distinction of the supports or backs of the heavens.
    • It suggests a movement or flow that is significant and elevated, indicating a higher state of being or consciousness.
    • The term "uttamaṁ rajaḥ" implies the highest form of energy or essence, which can be interpreted as the ultimate truth or reality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the context of personal growth, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to distinguish between superficial desires and deeper aspirations.
    • In a professional setting, it encourages individuals to seek the highest form of excellence in their work, striving for the best outcomes.
    • In relationships, it highlights the importance of recognizing the deeper connections that support and elevate our interactions with others.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call to recognize and elevate our understanding of the world around us.
    • It encourages us to seek the highest truths and energies in our lives, promoting a journey towards self-realization and enlightenment.
    • In practical terms, applying this mantra can lead to improved decision-making, enhanced relationships, and a more profound sense of purpose.
