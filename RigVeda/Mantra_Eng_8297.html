उप प्रियं पाणिपातं युवानं आहुतिवृद्धम् |अगन्म बिभ्रतो नमः ||
upa priyam panipnataṁ yuvānam āhutīvṛdham | aganma bibhrato namaḥ ||
  • upa priyam: This phrase translates to "towards what is dear" or "to that which is beloved." It signifies the importance of approaching or seeking out what we hold dear in our lives, whether it be relationships, goals, or values.
  • panipnataṁ: This word means "with hands raised" or "with hands outstretched." It symbolizes readiness and willingness to receive or embrace what is important to us. In real life, this can be seen in moments of gratitude or when we are open to new opportunities.
  • yuvānam: This term refers to "youth" or "young ones." It represents vitality, energy, and the potential for growth. In a practical sense, this can relate to the aspirations and dreams of young individuals who are just starting their journeys in life.
  • āhutīvṛdham: This phrase can be interpreted as "having been called forth" or "having been invoked." It suggests the idea of bringing forth or manifesting something that has been desired or sought after. In everyday life, this can be likened to the process of setting intentions and working towards achieving them.
  • aganma: This word means "I have come" or "I have arrived." It signifies the act of reaching a destination or achieving a goal. This can be seen in personal achievements, such as completing a project or reaching a milestone in one’s career.
  • bibhrato: This term translates to "bearing" or "holding." It implies the responsibility of carrying something significant, whether it be knowledge, values, or relationships. In real life, this can relate to the roles we play in our families or communities, where we bear the weight of expectations and responsibilities.
  • namaḥ: This is a term of reverence or salutation, often translated as "salutations" or "I bow." It reflects humility and respect towards the divine or towards those we hold in high regard. In practice, this can be seen in acts of gratitude, respect, and acknowledgment of others' contributions in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra "upa priyam panipnataṁ yuvānam āhutīvṛdham | aganma bibhrato namaḥ ||" encapsulates the essence of seeking what is dear to us with an open heart and hands, embracing the vitality of youth, and manifesting our desires with respect and humility. Its significance lies in the reminder to approach our goals and relationships with intention and reverence. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as setting personal goals, nurturing relationships, and fostering a sense of community, ultimately guiding us towards a more fulfilling and meaningful existence.
