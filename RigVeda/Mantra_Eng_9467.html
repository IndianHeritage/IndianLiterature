प्र वो वायुं रथयुजं पुरन्धिं स्तोमैः कृतुध्वं सख्याय पूषणम् |ते हि देवस्य सवितुः सवीमनि क्रतुं सचन्ते सचितः सचेतसः ||
pra vo vāyuṁ rathayujam puraṁdhiṁ stomaiḥ kṛṇudhvaṁ sakhyāya pūṣaṇam | te hi devasya savituḥ savīmani kratuṁ sacante sacitaḥ sacetasaḥ ||
  • pra vo vāyuṁ: This phrase can be translated as "to you, O Wind." In this context, "Vāyu" refers to the divine wind or the life force that sustains all beings. In real life, we can relate this to the importance of air and breath in our existence, emphasizing the need to respect and honor nature.
  • rathayujam puraṁdhiṁ: "Rathayujam" means "the one who is yoked to the chariot," and "puraṁdhiṁ" refers to the "one who is the master of the city." This can symbolize the control and guidance of our life journey. In practical terms, it can be seen as the need for direction and purpose in our lives, akin to steering a chariot towards a destination.
  • stomaiḥ kṛṇudhvaṁ: This translates to "with hymns, let us create." It emphasizes the power of words and prayers in shaping our reality. In everyday life, this can be likened to the impact of positive affirmations and the importance of expressing gratitude and intentions.
  • sakhyāya pūṣaṇam: "For the sake of friendship, O Pūṣaṇa." Pūṣaṇa is a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity. This line highlights the significance of community and relationships in our lives. In real life, it reminds us of the value of building connections and supporting one another for mutual growth.
  • te hi devasya savituḥ: "For you are indeed the divine of the Sun." This acknowledges the divine presence in the Sun, which is a source of life and energy. In practical terms, it can be related to the importance of sunlight for health and well-being, as well as the spiritual significance of seeking enlightenment.
  • savīmani kratuṁ: "With your wisdom and intent." This emphasizes the need for wisdom in our actions and decisions. In real life, it can be seen as a call to make informed choices and to act with purpose and clarity.
  • sacante sacitaḥ sacetasaḥ: "Those who are aware, who are conscious." This line speaks to the importance of mindfulness and awareness in our lives. In practical terms, it encourages us to be present and engaged in our experiences, fostering a deeper connection with ourselves and the world around us.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of nature, community, and the divine. It serves as a reminder to honor the forces that sustain us, to seek purpose and direction, and to cultivate awareness in our daily lives. In a real-life scenario, this mantra can be applied in practices such as meditation, community building, and environmental stewardship, encouraging us to live harmoniously with ourselves and the world. By embodying the principles expressed in this mantra, we can foster a more meaningful and fulfilling existence.
