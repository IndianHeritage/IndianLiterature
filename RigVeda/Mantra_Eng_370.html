अहन्न अहिम पर्वते शिश्रियाणं तवष्टास्मै वज्रं स्वर्यं तातक्ष || वाश्राऽ इव धेनवः स्यन्दमानाऽ अञ्जः समुद्रम अव जग्मुर आपः ||
ahann ahim parvate śiśriyāṇaṁ tvaṣṭāsmai vajraṁ svaryaṁ tatakṣa | vāśrā iva dhenavaḥ syandamānā añjaḥ samudram ava jagmur āpaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ahann ahim parvate śiśriyāṇaṁ tvaṣṭāsmai vajraṁ svaryaṁ tatakṣa | vāśrā iva dhenavaḥ syandamānā añjaḥ samudram ava jagmur āpaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ahann: I am
    • ahim: the non-violent, peaceful
    • parvate: on the mountain
    • śiśriyāṇaṁ: who is cooling or refreshing
    • tvaṣṭāsmai: to the creator or the divine craftsman
    • vajraṁ: the thunderbolt or the powerful weapon
    • svaryaṁ: heavenly or divine
    • tatakṣa: he created or fashioned
    • vāśrā: like the cows
    • iva: as if
    • dhenavaḥ: the milking cows
    • syandamānā: flowing or moving
    • añjaḥ: swiftly
    • samudram: to the ocean
    • ava: down or away
    • jagmur: they went
    • āpaḥ: the waters
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine presence and the creative force that brings forth life and sustenance.
    • The phrase "I am the non-violent" suggests a state of being that is peaceful and harmonious, which is essential in our daily interactions.
    • The reference to "the mountain" symbolizes stability and strength, akin to how we seek stability in our lives through relationships, careers, and personal growth.
    • "To the creator" indicates a reverence for the forces that shape our existence, reminding us to appreciate the craftsmanship in nature and human endeavors.
    • The "thunderbolt" represents power and the ability to overcome obstacles, much like how we face challenges in life with resilience.
    • The imagery of "milking cows" flowing to the ocean illustrates the abundance of resources and nourishment available to us, akin to how we seek and share knowledge and support within our communities.
    • The swift movement towards the ocean signifies the journey of life, where we continuously seek deeper understanding and connection.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of peace, creativity, and the interconnectedness of life.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to embrace non-violence in our thoughts and actions, to appreciate the beauty of creation, and to recognize the abundance around us.
    • By embodying these principles, we can foster a more harmonious existence, both individually and collectively, leading to a more fulfilling life.
    • Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of our role in the universe and the impact of our actions on the world around us.
