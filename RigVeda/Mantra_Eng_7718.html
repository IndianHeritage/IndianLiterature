एष दिवं व्यासरत तिरो राजांस्य अस्पृतः |पवमानः स्वध्वरः ||
eṣa divaṁ vy āsarat tiro rajāṁsy aspṛtaḥ | pavamānaḥ svadhvaraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣa: this
    • dvaṁ: heaven
    • vy āsarat: has been made to shine
    • tiro: beyond
    • rajāṁsy: the kings or rulers
    • aspṛtaḥ: untouched or untainted
    • pavamānaḥ: the purifying one
    • svadhvaraḥ: of one's own accord or self-generated
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence that illuminates the heavens.
    • It suggests a state of purity and untouched nature, indicating a higher spiritual realm.
    • The term "pavamānaḥ" refers to a purifying force, which can be interpreted as a divine energy that cleanses and elevates.
    • "Svadhvaraḥ" implies that this divine energy operates independently, suggesting self-sufficiency and inherent power.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often seek to cultivate a sense of purity and clarity in their thoughts and actions, akin to the "pavamānaḥ" aspect of the mantra.
    • In leadership, a true leader is often seen as someone who shines brightly, guiding others while remaining untainted by corruption or negativity, similar to the "rajāṁsy aspṛtaḥ" concept.
    • In environmental contexts, the idea of untouched nature can be reflected in conservation efforts aimed at preserving pristine ecosystems, embodying the essence of purity and self-sustainability.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of purity, self-sufficiency, and divine illumination in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to strive for a higher state of being, free from negativity and corruption.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in various aspects of life, including personal growth, leadership, and environmental stewardship, reminding us of the importance of maintaining purity in our thoughts, actions, and surroundings.
