आयम् एक इथ्था पुरूरु चष्टे वि विश्वपतिः |तस्य व्रतान्य अनु वश चरामसि ||
ayam eka itthā purūru caṣṭe vi viśpatiḥ | tasya vratāny anu vaś carāmasi ||
  • ayam: This word translates to "this" or "he," indicating a specific entity or being.
  • eka: Meaning "one" or "single," it emphasizes unity or singularity.
  • itthā: This can be interpreted as "thus" or "in this manner," suggesting a way of being or existence.
  • purūru: This term refers to "many" or "abundant," indicating a multitude or a vastness.
  • caṣṭe: Meaning "acts" or "performs," it denotes action or behavior.
  • vi viśpatiḥ: This phrase translates to "the lord of all," referring to a supreme being or deity who governs everything.
  • tasya: Meaning "of him" or "of that," it connects back to the previously mentioned entity.
  • vratāny: This word means "vows" or "sacred commitments," indicating promises or principles that guide behavior.
  • anu: Meaning "following" or "in accordance with," it suggests adherence to something.
  • vaś: This translates to "control" or "power," indicating authority or influence.
  • carāmasi: Meaning "we move" or "we act," it signifies the actions taken by individuals or a collective.

The mantra can be interpreted as follows: "This one, in this manner, acts abundantly; he is the lord of all. Following his vows, we act with control." It emphasizes the idea of a singular entity (possibly a deity) who embodies both unity and abundance, guiding the actions of individuals or a community through sacred commitments.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Leadership: Just as the mantra speaks of a "lord of all," effective leaders often embody qualities of unity and abundance, guiding their teams with clear principles and commitments.
  • Community Living: The idea of acting in accordance with vows can be seen in community service, where individuals come together to support a common cause, reflecting the collective action mentioned in the mantra.
  • Personal Development: The emphasis on control and commitment can inspire individuals to set personal goals and adhere to them, fostering growth and abundance in their lives.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of unity, commitment, and the guiding principles that govern our actions. It serves as a reminder that by aligning ourselves with higher ideals and acting collectively, we can achieve abundance and harmony in our lives and communities.
