सो अर्षेन्द्राय पीतये तिरो रोमाण्य अव्यया |सीदन योनां वनेष्वा ||
so arṣendrāya pītaye tiro romāṇy avyayā | sīdan yonā vaneṣv ā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • so: that
    • arṣendrāya: to the lord of the senses (Indra)
    • pītaye: for the one who is to be worshipped or revered
    • tiro: beyond
    • romāṇy: the hair or the fibers
    • avyayā: imperishable or eternal
    • sīdan: may he sit or dwell
    • yonā: in the womb or source
    • vaneṣv: in the forests or wilderness
    • ā: (a particle indicating direction)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the king of the gods, who represents strength, power, and the natural forces.
    • The phrase "to the lord of the senses" suggests a reverence for the divine aspects of perception and understanding.
    • The term "imperishable" indicates the eternal nature of the divine, transcending the physical realm.
    • The mention of "forests" symbolizes the natural world, where one can find peace and connection with the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the importance of mindfulness and being present in the moment, akin to how one might appreciate nature during a hike in the woods.
    • It can also be seen in the context of meditation practices, where individuals seek to connect with their inner selves and the universe, much like the reverence shown to Indra.
    • In a broader sense, it reflects the need for respect towards nature and the environment, encouraging sustainable living and awareness of our surroundings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of connecting with the divine and the natural world.
    • It serves as a reminder of the imperishable nature of the spirit and the importance of reverence in our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek harmony with nature, practice mindfulness, and cultivate a deeper understanding of their existence.
