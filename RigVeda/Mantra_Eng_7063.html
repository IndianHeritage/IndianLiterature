आ नो विश्वे सजोषसो देवासो गन्तनोपा नः |वसवो रुद्राः अवसे न आगमन् श्रिण्वन्तु मरुतो हवम् ||
ā no viśve sajoṣaso devāso gantanopa naḥ | vasavo rudrā avase na ā gamañ chṛṇvantu maruto havam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā no: May they come to us
    • viśve: All
    • sajoṣaso: With harmony
    • devāso: The divine beings
    • gantano: May they approach
    • upa: Near
    • naḥ: Us
    • vasavo: The Vasus (a group of deities)
    • rudrā: The Rudras (another group of deities)
    • avase: May they dwell
    • na: Not
    • ā gamañ: May they not depart
    • chṛṇvantu: May they listen
    • maruto: The Maruts (storm deities)
    • havam: Our invocation
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for the divine beings to come together in harmony and approach us.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and collaboration among the divine forces, represented by the Vasus, Rudras, and Maruts.
    • The invocation seeks their attention and presence, indicating a desire for their blessings and guidance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, people often come together to celebrate festivals, invoking the spirit of unity and harmony, similar to how this mantra calls for divine beings to unite.
    • In workplaces, team-building activities foster collaboration and harmony among employees, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • During family gatherings, the act of coming together for meals or celebrations can be seen as a real-life application of invoking harmony and blessings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity, harmony, and the collective presence of divine forces in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios where collaboration and togetherness are essential, such as in families, communities, and workplaces.
    • By invoking the divine for guidance and blessings, we can foster a sense of belonging and support in our endeavors.
