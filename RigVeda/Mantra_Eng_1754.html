ऋचो अक्षरे परम व्योमन् यस्मिन् देवाः अधि विश्वे निषेदुः |यस् तन् न वेद किम ऋचाऽ करिष्यति य इति तद् विदुः त इमे सम आसते ||
ṛco akṣare parame vyoman yasmin devā adhi viśve niṣeduḥ | yas tan na veda kim ṛcā kariṣyati ya it tad vidus ta ime sam āsate ||
  • ṛco akṣare parame vyoman: This phrase translates to "In the imperishable (akṣara) realm of the ultimate (parame) sky (vyoman) of the hymns (ṛco)." Here, "ṛco" refers to the sacred verses or hymns, which are considered eternal and transcendental.
  • yasmin devā adhi viśve niṣeduḥ: This means "where the gods (devā) reside (niṣeduḥ) in all (viśve)." It suggests a divine presence that encompasses all aspects of existence, indicating that the ultimate reality is inhabited by divine beings.
  • yas tan na veda kim ṛcā kariṣyati: This translates to "What does that (tan) know (veda) which the hymns (ṛcā) will accomplish (kariṣyati)?" This line raises a question about the limitations of knowledge and the power of hymns in understanding the divine.
  • ya it tad vidus ta ime sam āsate: This means "Those who know (vidus) that (tad) are the ones who gather (āsate) here." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in connecting with the divine and the ultimate reality.

  • Significance: This mantra highlights the relationship between the divine, the hymns, and human understanding. It suggests that while hymns are powerful, true knowledge of the ultimate reality transcends mere recitation.
  • Real-life Application: In our daily lives, this mantra can inspire us to seek deeper understanding beyond superficial knowledge. For instance, in education, students are encouraged to not just memorize facts but to understand concepts deeply, much like the pursuit of spiritual knowledge in this mantra.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking true knowledge and understanding the divine presence in all aspects of life. It encourages individuals to look beyond the surface and strive for a deeper connection with the ultimate reality.
