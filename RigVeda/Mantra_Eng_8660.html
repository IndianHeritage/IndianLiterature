अव्यो वारेभिः पवते सोमो गव्ये अधि त्वचि |कणिक्रदद वृषा हरिर इन्द्रस्याभ्य एति निष्कृतम् ||
avyo vārebhiḥ pavate somo gavye adhi tvaci | kanikradad vṛṣā harir indrasyābhy eti niṣkṛtam ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "The one who is praised by the wise, the Soma flows on the earth; the strong one, the Harir, approaches Indra's offering with vigor."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • avyo: This word signifies praise or commendation, often directed towards a deity or a powerful force.
    • vārebhiḥ: Refers to the wise or learned individuals who offer their insights and knowledge.
    • pavate: Means to flow or to move, often used in the context of rivers or divine substances like Soma.
    • somo: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing vitality and divine inspiration.
    • gavyé: Relates to the earth or the ground, indicating a connection to nature and the physical world.
    • adhi: Means 'upon' or 'over', suggesting a sense of elevation or importance.
    • tvaci: Refers to the skin or surface, often symbolizing the outer manifestation of inner qualities.
    • kanikradad: This term can be interpreted as the strong or powerful one, often associated with strength and vigor.
    • vṛṣā: Refers to a bull or a strong being, symbolizing power and fertility.
    • harir: This word means 'the one who shines' or 'the bright one', often used to describe deities.
    • indrasyābhy: Refers to Indra, the king of gods, and his offerings, indicating a connection to divine rituals.
    • eti: Means 'approaches' or 'comes to', indicating movement towards something significant.
    • niṣkṛtam: Refers to the act of offering or sacrifice, often associated with purification and devotion.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate a festival, they often praise the efforts of individuals who contribute to the event, similar to how the wise praise the divine.
    • The act of sharing food and drink during celebrations can be likened to the flowing of Soma, where the essence of life is shared among participants, fostering unity and joy.
    • In nature, the strength of a bull can be seen in agricultural practices where farmers rely on these animals for plowing fields, symbolizing hard work and fertility.
    • During rituals, offerings made to deities can be seen as a way to connect with the divine, much like the offerings made to Indra in the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the connection between the divine and the earthly realm. It highlights the importance of praise, offerings, and the flow of life-giving substances. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as community gatherings, rituals, and agricultural practices, where the essence of life, strength, and unity are celebrated. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of honoring the divine through our actions and offerings.
