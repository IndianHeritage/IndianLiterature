त्वं त्यं इन्द्र सूर्यं पश्चा सन्तं पुरस कृधि |देवानां चित तिरो वशम् ||
tvaṁ tyam indra sūryam paścā santam puras kṛdhi | devānāṁ cit tiro vaśam ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "you," referring to a divine entity, often interpreted as Indra or a higher power.
  • tyam - This means "that," indicating a connection or relationship with the divine.
  • indra - Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • sūryam - This translates to "the sun," representing light, energy, and life-giving force.
  • paścā - Meaning "after" or "behind," suggesting a temporal or spatial relationship.
  • santam - This word means "existing" or "present," indicating a state of being.
  • puras - Translates to "in front" or "before," indicating a position or priority.
  • kṛdhi - This means "create" or "make," suggesting an action of manifestation or formation.
  • devānāṁ - This translates to "of the gods," indicating a divine context.
  • cit - Meaning "consciousness" or "awareness," referring to a higher state of understanding.
  • tiro - This means "beyond" or "transcending," suggesting a movement past ordinary limits.
  • vaśam - This translates to "control" or "influence," indicating power over something.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine, asking for the presence and influence of Indra and the sun in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of divine guidance and the need for strength and clarity in our actions.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra is a symbol of leadership, individuals in positions of authority can invoke this mantra to seek strength and wisdom in their decisions.
  • Personal Growth: The reference to the sun symbolizes enlightenment and energy. Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to seek knowledge and clarity in their personal and professional lives.
  • Spiritual Practice: This mantra can be chanted during meditation or prayer to connect with higher consciousness and seek divine guidance.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine strength and clarity. It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking guidance from higher powers in our lives, whether in leadership, personal growth, or spiritual practices. By embodying the qualities of Indra and the sun, individuals can strive for a more enlightened and empowered existence.
