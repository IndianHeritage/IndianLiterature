आ ते हनू हरिवः शूर शिप्रे रुहत सोमो न पर्वतस्य पृष्ठे |अनु त्वा राजन् अर्वतो न हिन्वन् गीरभिर मदेम पुरुहूत विश्वे ||
ā te hanū harivaḥ śūra śipre ruhat somo na parvatasya pṛṣṭhe | anu tvā rājann arvato na hinvan gīrbhir madema puruhūta viśve ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā te: From you
    • hanū: The jaw or the mouth
    • harivaḥ: O Lord of the horses
    • śūra: Brave or valiant
    • śipre: In the battle or in the fight
    • ruhat: May you rise or may you grow
    • somo: The moon or the soma (a sacred drink)
    • na: Not
    • parvatasya: Of the mountain
    • pṛṣṭhe: On the back
    • anu: Following
    • tvā: You
    • rājann: O King
    • arvato: Of the swift
    • na: Not
    • hinvan: Harming or injuring
    • gīrbhir: With hymns or praises
    • madema: May we be intoxicated or inspired
    • puruhūta: O many-voiced one
    • viśve: All or universal
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to a divine entity, often interpreted as a call for strength and protection in times of conflict.
    • The reference to "O Lord of the horses" symbolizes power and speed, often associated with warriors and chariots in ancient battles.
    • The mention of "may you rise" suggests a desire for growth and success, both in personal endeavors and in the collective efforts of a community.
    • The phrase "not harming you with hymns" indicates a respectful approach to the divine, emphasizing that praise should be uplifting rather than destructive.
    • The invocation of "O King" signifies the importance of leadership and guidance in achieving victory and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be applied in leadership roles where one seeks to inspire and uplift their team, ensuring that their guidance is constructive.
    • In competitive environments, such as sports or business, the call for strength and protection can resonate with individuals striving for success while maintaining ethical standards.
    • During challenging times, such as conflicts or crises, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek strength and unity, fostering a spirit of collaboration rather than division.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, protection, and unity in the face of challenges.
    • It emphasizes the importance of respectful communication with the divine and the need for strong leadership.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to rise together, support one another, and strive for collective success while upholding values of respect and integrity.
