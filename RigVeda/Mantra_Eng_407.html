मन्त्र: क्व त्रī चक्रा त्रिवृतो रथस्य क्व त्रयो वन्धुरो ये सनीलाः | कदा योगो वाजिनो रासभस्य येन यज्ञं नासत्योपयाथः ||
kva1 trī cakrā trivṛto rathasya kva1 trayo vandhuro ye sanīḻāḥ | kadā yogo vājino rāsabhasya yena yajñaṁ nāsatyopayāthaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "kva1 trī cakrā trivṛto rathasya kva1 trayo vandhuro ye sanīḻāḥ | kadā yogo vājino rāsabhasya yena yajñaṁ nāsatyopayāthaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kva1: Where
    • trī: Three
    • cakrā: Wheels or circles
    • trivṛto: Having threefold
    • rathasya: Of the chariot
    • trayo: Three
    • vandhuro: Bound or tied
    • ye: Who
    • sanīḻāḥ: Are restrained
    • kadā: When
    • yogo: Union or connection
    • vājino: Of the swift or strong
    • rāsabhasya: Of the horse
    • yena: By which
    • yajñaṁ: Sacrifice or offering
    • nāsatyopayāthaḥ: To the Asvins (the twin deities)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the concept of three wheels of a chariot, which can symbolize the three stages of life: birth, life, and death.
    • The "threefold" aspect can also refer to the mind, body, and spirit, emphasizing the need for balance among them.
    • The mention of "bound" suggests the constraints we face in life, while "who are restrained" indicates the challenges that come with these limitations.
    • The "union" of the swift horse can represent the connection between our desires and actions, highlighting the importance of aligning our goals with our efforts.
    • The reference to "sacrifice" signifies the importance of selflessness and offering something valuable for the greater good, akin to community service or charity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, understanding the balance between mind, body, and spirit can lead to a more fulfilling life. For instance, practicing yoga integrates physical health with mental clarity and spiritual growth.
    • In a professional setting, teamwork can be seen as a chariot where each member plays a role (the wheels) to achieve a common goal, emphasizing the importance of collaboration.
    • Acts of charity or community service can be viewed as a form of sacrifice, where individuals contribute their time and resources for the benefit of others, reflecting the essence of yajña.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of life’s journey, emphasizing the need for balance, connection, and selflessness.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us to navigate through life's challenges while maintaining harmony among our various aspects.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to engage in self-reflection, teamwork, and community service, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
