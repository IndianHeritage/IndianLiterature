ज्ञानश्च यन्नरश्च वावृद्धन्त विश्वे देवासो नरां स्वगूर्ताः |प्रैभ्य इन्द्रावरुणा महित्वा द्यौश्च पृथिवी भूतम उर्वी ||
gnāś ca yan naraś ca vāvṛdhanta viśve devāso narāṁ svagūrtāḥ | praibhya indrāvaruṇā mahitvā dyauś ca pṛthivi bhūtam urvī ||
  • gnāś ca: This phrase can be translated as "and knowledge" or "and understanding." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in human life.
  • yan naraś ca: This means "and the man" or "and the human." It signifies the role of humanity in the cosmic order.
  • vāvṛdhanta: This term translates to "who are growing" or "who are flourishing." It suggests the idea of progress and development.
  • viśve devāso: This means "all the gods" or "the universal deities." It indicates the presence of divine forces in the universe.
  • narāṁ svagūrtāḥ: This translates to "the men of their own strength." It highlights the importance of self-reliance and personal power.
  • praibhya: This word means "from the two" or "from the duality." It suggests the interconnectedness of various elements in existence.
  • indrāvaruṇā: This refers to "Indra and Varuna," two significant deities in Vedic tradition, representing power and cosmic order, respectively.
  • mahitvā: This translates to "greatness" or "majesty," emphasizing the importance of recognizing and aspiring to greatness.
  • dyauś ca pṛthivi: This means "the sky and the earth," representing the duality of existence and the balance between the spiritual and material worlds.
  • bhūtam urvī: This translates to "the vastness of the earth" or "the essence of the earth," indicating the richness and abundance of the material world.

In conclusion, this mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of knowledge, human potential, and the divine interplay in the universe. It signifies that through wisdom and self-reliance, individuals can grow and flourish, supported by the cosmic forces represented by Indra and Varuna. The balance between the spiritual (dyauś) and the material (pṛthivi) realms is crucial for achieving greatness and understanding one's place in the world. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek knowledge, embrace their strengths, and recognize the interconnectedness of all beings, leading to a more harmonious existence.
