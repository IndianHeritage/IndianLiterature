किं देवेषु त्यज एनश्च कर्त्थाग्न पृच्छामि नु त्वाम् अविद्वान् |अक्रीळन् क्रीळन् हरिर् अतवे 'दन् विं पर्वशश् कर्ता गाम् इवासीः ||
kiṁ deveṣu tyaja enaś cakarthāgne pṛcchāmi nu tvām avidvān | akrīḻan krīḻan harir attave 'dan vi parvaśaś cakarta gām ivāsiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kiṁ: What
    • deveṣu: among the gods
    • tyaja: should I abandon
    • enaś: this
    • cakarthāgne: you have done, O Agni (the fire god)
    • pṛcchāmi: I ask
    • nu: indeed
    • tvām: you
    • avidvān: the ignorant one
    • akrīḻan: not playing
    • krīḻan: playing
    • harir: the Lord
    • attave: in the abode
    • 'dan: that
    • vi: indeed
    • parvaśaś: of the powerful
    • cakarta: has done
    • gām: the world
    • ivāsiḥ: as if you are
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep inquiry into the nature of divine actions and the relationship between the seeker and the divine.
    • The speaker, possibly a devotee or seeker, is questioning what should be abandoned in the pursuit of understanding the divine.
    • Agni, the fire god, symbolizes transformation and purification, suggesting that the seeker is looking for guidance on how to navigate life's challenges.
    • The mention of "avidvān" (the ignorant one) indicates a recognition of one's own limitations in understanding the divine and the world.
    • The contrast between "akrīḻan" (not playing) and "krīḻan" (playing) suggests a balance between seriousness and joy in spiritual practice.
    • The phrase "as if you are" implies a questioning of the divine presence in the world and how it manifests in everyday life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be related to individuals seeking purpose in their careers. For instance, a person may question what aspects of their job they should let go of to find fulfillment.
    • In relationships, one might reflect on what negative patterns to abandon to foster healthier connections, paralleling the inquiry of the mantra.
    • In personal development, the balance between seriousness and playfulness can be seen in how people approach their goals, emphasizing the importance of joy in the journey.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of self-inquiry and the quest for understanding in the spiritual journey.
    • It highlights the need to recognize our limitations and the divine's role in our lives, encouraging a balance between seriousness and joy.
    • In real life, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a deeper connection with the divine.
