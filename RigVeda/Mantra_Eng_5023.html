यद् रोदसी प्रदिवो अस्ति भूमā हेळो देवानाम् उता मार्त्यत्रा |तद् आदित्याः वसवो रुद्रियासो रक्षोयुज् तपुर अगं दधात ||
yad rodasī pradivo asti bhūmā heḻo devānām uta martyatrā | tad ādityā vasavo rudriyāso rakṣoyuje tapur aghaṁ dadhāta ||
  • yad rodasī pradivo asti bhūmā heḻo devānām uta martyatrā
    • yad: This word translates to "that" or "which," indicating a reference to something significant.
    • rodasī: Refers to the "two worlds" or "heavens," symbolizing the cosmic realms.
    • pradivo: Means "in the sky" or "in the heavens," emphasizing the divine aspect of the universe.
    • asti: Translates to "is," affirming existence.
    • bhūmā: Refers to "the earth," grounding the mantra in the physical realm.
    • heḻo: Means "the abode" or "the place," indicating a connection between the divine and the earthly.
    • devānām: Refers to "of the gods," highlighting the divine presence in the universe.
    • uta: Means "or," suggesting an alternative or additional aspect.
    • martyatrā: Refers to "among mortals," indicating the human experience.
  • tad ādityā vasavo rudriyāso rakṣoyuje tapur aghaṁ dadhāta
    • tad: Means "that," referring back to the earlier mentioned divine connection.
    • ādityā: Refers to the "Adityas," the solar deities, symbolizing light and truth.
    • vasavo: Refers to the "Vasu," the deities of abundance and prosperity.
    • rudriyāso: Refers to "Rudras," the deities associated with storms and transformation.
    • rakṣoyuje: Means "to protect," indicating a plea for divine protection.
    • tapur: Refers to "austerity" or "penance," symbolizing spiritual discipline.
    • aghaṁ: Means "sin" or "evil," indicating the removal of negativity.
    • dadhāta: Means "to bestow" or "to grant," indicating a divine offering.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the connection between the divine realms and the earthly existence of mortals. It highlights the importance of divine protection and the role of various deities in ensuring prosperity, transformation, and the removal of negativity. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • Seeking guidance and protection during challenging times, invoking the presence of divine forces.
  • Practicing spiritual disciplines and austerities to cultivate inner strength and resilience.
  • Recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of maintaining harmony between the spiritual and material worlds.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine support available to us and the importance of aligning our actions with higher principles for a fulfilling life.
