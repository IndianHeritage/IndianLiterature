तं इद् दोषा तं उषसि यविष्टम् अग्निम अत्यं न मरजयन्त नरः |निशिशानाऽऽतिथिम अस्य योनौ दीदाय शोचिर आहुतस्य वृष्णः ||
tam id doṣā tam uṣasi yaviṣṭham agnim atyaṁ na marjayanta naraḥ | niśiśānā atithim asya yonau dīdāya śocir āhutasya vṛṣṇaḥ ||
  • tam id doṣā: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which is free from faults." In life, we often strive for perfection in our actions and decisions. For example, in a workplace, a project that is executed flawlessly can be seen as "free from faults," leading to success and recognition.
  • tam uṣasi: This translates to "that which is in the dawn." The dawn symbolizes new beginnings and opportunities. In real life, every new day presents us with a chance to start afresh, much like how a student begins a new academic year with hopes and aspirations.
  • yaviṣṭham agnim: This means "the most excellent fire." Fire is often associated with energy, transformation, and purification. In a practical sense, this can relate to the passion and drive one has in pursuing their goals, akin to an entrepreneur igniting their business idea with enthusiasm.
  • atyam na marjayanta naraḥ: This can be interpreted as "humans do not extinguish the fire." This suggests that the spirit of perseverance and ambition should never be diminished. For instance, athletes who train tirelessly for competitions embody this idea, as they refuse to let setbacks extinguish their determination.
  • niśiśānā atithim asya yonau: This phrase translates to "the guest who arrives in the night." It highlights the importance of hospitality and welcoming others. In real life, this can be seen in cultures where guests are treated with utmost respect and care, reflecting the value of community and connection.
  • dīdāya śocir āhutasya vṛṣṇaḥ: This means "the shining one who is invoked." It signifies the importance of calling upon the divine or higher powers for guidance and support. In everyday life, people often seek inspiration from mentors or spiritual leaders when faced with challenges, embodying this invocation of wisdom.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the pursuit of excellence, the importance of new beginnings, and the value of perseverance and hospitality. In real life, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional lives, encouraging them to strive for perfection, embrace new opportunities, and foster connections with others. By embodying these values, one can lead a fulfilling and impactful life, contributing positively to society.
