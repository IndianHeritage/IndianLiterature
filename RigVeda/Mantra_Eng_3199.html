यं सिम अकृण्वन् तमसे विपृचे ध्रुवक्षेमā अनवस्यन्तो अर्थम् |तं सूर्यं हरितः सप्त यह्वीः स्पशं विश्वस्य जगतो वहन्ति ||
yaṁ sīm akṛṇvan tamase vipṛce dhruvakṣemā anavasyanto artham | taṁ sūryaṁ haritaḥ sapta yahvīḥ spaśaṁ viśvasya jagato vahanti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: who
    • sīm: boundary or limit
    • akṛṇvan: having created or established
    • tamase: darkness
    • vipṛce: dispelling or removing
    • dhruvakṣemā: stable and secure
    • anavasyanto: without disturbance
    • artham: for the purpose or meaning
    • taṁ: that
    • sūryaṁ: sun
    • haritaḥ: green or flourishing
    • sapta: seven
    • yahvīḥ: rays or beams
    • spaśaṁ: touch or contact
    • viśvasya: of the universe
    • jagato: of the world
    • vahanti: carry or support
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine force that creates boundaries and dispels darkness, leading to stability and security.
    • The reference to the sun symbolizes enlightenment, growth, and the nurturing aspect of nature.
    • The "seven rays" can be interpreted as the various aspects of life or the different energies that sustain the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, establishing boundaries can help individuals dispel negativity and create a stable environment for growth.
    • In agriculture, the sun's rays are essential for the growth of crops, symbolizing how nature supports life.
    • In community building, the idea of carrying the universe can relate to how individuals support each other to create a harmonious society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of creating a stable and secure environment while dispelling darkness and negativity.
    • Its significance lies in the understanding that enlightenment and growth are supported by both natural and communal forces.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to establish boundaries, seek enlightenment, and support one another for a flourishing existence.
