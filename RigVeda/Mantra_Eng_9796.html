पुरूणि हि त्वा सवना जनानाम् ब्रह्माणि मन्दन गृणताम् ऋषीणाम् |इमाम् आगोषन्न् अवसाऽ सहूतिṁ तिरो विश्वाम् आर्चतो याह्य् अर्वाङ् ||
purūṇi hi tvā savanā janānām brahmāṇi mandan gṛṇatām ṛṣīṇām | imām āghoṣann avasā sahūtiṁ tiro viśvām̐ arcato yāhy arvāṅ ||
  • Purūṇi hi tvā savanā janānām: This phrase can be translated to "Indeed, you are the one who is invoked by the gatherings of people." It signifies the importance of community and collective worship. In real life, this can be seen in various cultural and religious gatherings where people come together to celebrate, pray, or seek blessings, such as festivals or communal prayers.
  • Brahmāṇi mandan gṛṇatām ṛṣīṇām: This translates to "May the sages sing your praises." Here, the term "Brahmāṇi" refers to the ultimate reality or the divine, while "ṛṣīṇām" refers to the sages or wise individuals. This highlights the role of wisdom and spirituality in society. For example, in modern times, we often look up to spiritual leaders or philosophers who guide us through their teachings and insights.
  • Imām āghoṣann avasā sahūtiṁ: This can be interpreted as "This invocation brings forth the collective strength." It emphasizes the power of unity and collaboration. In real life, this is evident in team sports, community service projects, or any situation where individuals come together to achieve a common goal, showcasing the strength found in unity.
  • Tiro viśvām̐ arcato yāhy arvāṅ: This phrase means "May all beings worship you." It reflects the idea of universal reverence and the interconnectedness of all life. In contemporary society, this can be seen in movements that promote environmental conservation, where people recognize the importance of respecting and honoring all forms of life on Earth.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of community, wisdom, unity, and universal reverence. Its significance lies in the reminder that collective efforts and spiritual wisdom can lead to a harmonious existence. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as community gatherings, environmental movements, and the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom. By embracing these values, individuals and societies can foster a more connected and respectful world.
