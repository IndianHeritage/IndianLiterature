आ दैव्याः वृणीमहे वांसी बृहस्पतिर्नो महा आसखायः |यथा भवेम मील्हुसे अनागा यो नो दाता परावतः पितेव ||
ā daivyā vṛṇīmahe 'vāṁsi bṛhaspatir no maha ā sakhāyaḥ | yathā bhavema mīḻhuṣe anāgā yo no dātā parāvataḥ piteva ||
  • Mantra: "ā daivyā vṛṇīmahe 'vāṁsi bṛhaspatir no maha ā sakhāyaḥ | yathā bhavema mīḻhuṣe anāgā yo no dātā parāvataḥ piteva ||"
  • Translation: "We invoke the divine (ā daivyā) and seek the blessings of the great sage Brihaspati (bṛhaspatir) as our friend (sakhāyaḥ). May we become prosperous (bhavema) like the one who is free from sin (anāgā), who grants us (dātā) the highest knowledge (parāvataḥ) like a father (piteva)."

  • Explanation of Key Terms:
    • ā daivyā: Refers to the divine or celestial forces that guide and protect us.
    • bṛhaspatir: Brihaspati is the deity of wisdom and knowledge, often associated with intellect and learning.
    • sakhāyaḥ: Means friendship or companionship, emphasizing the importance of supportive relationships.
    • mīḻhuṣe: Signifies prosperity and well-being, indicating a state of abundance.
    • anāgā: Refers to being free from sin or guilt, highlighting purity and righteousness.
    • dātā: The giver or bestower, representing the act of sharing knowledge and resources.
    • parāvataḥ: Denotes the highest or supreme, often relating to ultimate knowledge or enlightenment.
    • piteva: Like a father, symbolizing care, guidance, and unconditional support.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, invoking the wisdom of mentors (bṛhaspatir) can lead to better decision-making and success in projects.
    • Building strong friendships (sakhāyaḥ) in personal and professional life can provide emotional support and collaboration opportunities.
    • Striving for prosperity (mīḻhuṣe) can be seen in individuals who pursue education and skill development to enhance their career prospects.
    • Practicing ethical behavior (anāgā) in business can lead to a positive reputation and long-term success.
    • Sharing knowledge (dātā) with others, such as mentoring students or colleagues, fosters a culture of learning and growth.
    • Seeking the highest knowledge (parāvataḥ) can be reflected in lifelong learning and personal development initiatives.
    • Providing guidance and support (piteva) to family and friends can strengthen relationships and create a nurturing environment.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and wisdom in our lives. It highlights the value of friendship, ethical living, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real-life scenarios, applying these principles can lead to personal growth, stronger relationships, and a more fulfilling life. By invoking the qualities of Brihaspati and embodying the values of righteousness and support, individuals can navigate challenges and achieve prosperity in various aspects of life.
