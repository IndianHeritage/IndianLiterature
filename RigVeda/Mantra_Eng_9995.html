इन्द्र आसां नेता बृहस्पतिर दक्षिणा यज्ञः पुर एतु सोमः |देवसेनानाम् अभिभञ्जतीनां जयन्तीनाम् मरुतो यन्त्व् अग्रम् ||
indra āsāṁ netā bṛhaspatir dakṣiṇā yajñaḥ pura etu somaḥ | devasenānām abhibhañjatīnāṁ jayantīnām maruto yantv agram ||
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • Āsāṁ: This term can be interpreted as "of us" or "our," indicating a collective or communal aspect.
  • Netā: Meaning "leader" or "guide," this word emphasizes the importance of leadership in achieving goals.
  • Bṛhaspatir: Referring to Brihaspati, the god of wisdom and eloquence, symbolizing knowledge and guidance.
  • Dakṣiṇā: This term means "offering" or "sacrifice," highlighting the significance of giving in rituals and life.
  • Yajñaḥ: Refers to a sacrificial ritual, which is a central theme in Vedic culture, representing the connection between humans and the divine.
  • Pura: Meaning "to the city" or "to the place," indicating a destination or goal.
  • Etū: This word means "may come" or "may arrive," expressing a desire for blessings or positive outcomes.
  • Somaḥ: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
  • Devasenānām: Meaning "of the divine army," this term refers to the collective strength of divine beings.
  • Abhibhañjatīnāṁ: This word can be interpreted as "those who are victorious" or "who conquer," emphasizing triumph over challenges.
  • Jayantīnām: Meaning "of the victorious," it reinforces the theme of success and achievement.
  • Maruto: Referring to the Maruts, storm deities associated with strength and vitality.
  • Yantv: Meaning "may they go" or "may they lead," indicating a call for guidance and direction.
  • Agram: This term means "to the forefront" or "to the best," suggesting a pursuit of excellence.

Conclusion: This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra and Brihaspati, emphasizing the importance of leadership, wisdom, and sacrifice in achieving success. It highlights the collective strength of the divine and the need for guidance in our endeavors. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership roles, community initiatives, or personal challenges where guidance and support are essential. By recognizing the importance of collaboration, wisdom, and the willingness to give, individuals can navigate their paths more effectively and achieve their goals with the blessings of the divine.
