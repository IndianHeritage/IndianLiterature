वैश्वानरं मनसाग्निं निचाय्य हविष्मन्तो अनुषत्यं स्वरविदम् |सुदानुं देवं रथिरं वसूयवो गीरभिः रण्वं कुशिकासो हवामहे ||
vaiśvānaram manasāgniṁ nicāyyā haviṣmanto anuṣatyaṁ svarvidam | sudānuṁ devaṁ rathiraṁ vasūyavo gīrbhī raṇvaṁ kuśikāso havāmahe ||
  • vaiśvānaram: This term refers to the universal or cosmic aspect of fire, symbolizing the divine presence in all beings. In real life, it can be likened to the warmth and energy that sustains life, much like the sun's rays that nourish plants and provide energy to the ecosystem.
  • manasāgniṁ: This phrase translates to "the fire of the mind." It signifies the importance of intellect and consciousness in guiding our actions. For example, in decision-making, a person uses their mental fire to analyze situations and choose wisely, reflecting the need for clarity and focus in our thoughts.
  • nicāyyā: This word means "having been placed" or "established." It suggests the importance of grounding our intentions and actions. In practical terms, this can be seen in how individuals set goals and create plans to achieve them, ensuring that their efforts are directed and purposeful.
  • haviṣmanto: This refers to offerings or sacrifices made with intention. In everyday life, this can be related to the concept of giving back to the community or contributing to a cause, emphasizing the value of selflessness and generosity.
  • anuṣatyaṁ: This term means "following the path" or "adhering to the truth." It highlights the importance of integrity and righteousness in our actions. For instance, a person who stands by their principles in challenging situations exemplifies this quality.
  • svarvidam: This translates to "the knowledge of the heavens" or "divine wisdom." It signifies the pursuit of higher knowledge and understanding. In real life, this can be seen in the quest for education and enlightenment, where individuals seek to expand their horizons and gain deeper insights into life.
  • sudānuṁ: This word means "the well-formed" or "the auspicious." It represents the idea of harmony and balance in life. For example, maintaining a healthy work-life balance is crucial for overall well-being and happiness.
  • devaṁ: This term means "divine" or "godly." It signifies the connection to the divine or higher powers. In practical terms, this can be reflected in moments of inspiration or creativity, where individuals feel guided by a greater force.
  • rathiraṁ: This translates to "the chariot" and symbolizes the journey of life. It can be compared to the various paths we take in life, each with its own challenges and rewards, much like navigating through different terrains in a journey.
  • vasūyavo: This term refers to "the wealth of the earth" or "prosperity." It emphasizes the importance of abundance and the resources available to us. In real life, this can be seen in the appreciation of nature's bounty and the need to protect our environment for future generations.
  • gīrbhī: This means "with songs" or "with praises." It highlights the significance of expression and communication in our lives. For instance, music and art play vital roles in human culture, allowing individuals to express their emotions and connect with others.
  • raṇvaṁ: This term means "the one who is victorious" or "the conqueror." It signifies the spirit of overcoming challenges and achieving success. In real life, this can be seen in athletes who train hard to win competitions, embodying the essence of perseverance and determination.
  • kuśikāso: This refers to "the descendants of Kuśika," linking to a lineage or tradition. It emphasizes the importance of heritage and the values passed down through generations. In practical terms, this can be reflected in family traditions and cultural practices that shape our identities.
  • havāmahe: This means "we invoke" or "we offer." It signifies the act of calling upon the divine or higher powers for guidance and support. In everyday life, this can be seen in moments of prayer or meditation, where individuals seek connection with the divine for strength and clarity.
