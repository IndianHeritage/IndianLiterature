अग्ने कदā त āनुṣग भुवद देवस्य चेतनम् |अधā हि त्वā जगृभ्रिरे मर्तासो विक्ष्व īḍ्यम् ||
agne kadā ta ānuṣag bhuvad devasya cetanam | adhā hi tvā jagṛbhrire martāso vikṣv īḍyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Agne: O Fire (Agni), representing the divine energy and illumination.
    • Kadā: When.
    • Ta: That.
    • Ānuṣag: We may follow or approach.
    • Bhuvad: To become or to manifest.
    • Devasya: Of the divine or god.
    • Cetanam: Consciousness or awareness.
    • Adhā: Below or beneath.
    • Hi: Indeed.
    • Tvā: You.
    • Jagṛbhrire: Have grasped or taken hold of.
    • Martāso: Of mortals or human beings.
    • Vikṣv: In all directions.
    • Īḍyam: Worthy of praise or worship.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, symbolizing the divine light and energy that guides human consciousness.
    • The phrase "when we may follow that" suggests a quest for enlightenment and understanding of the divine.
    • The reference to "mortals" indicates the human condition, emphasizing our need for divine guidance in our lives.
    • It highlights the importance of seeking knowledge and wisdom from the divine source, which is essential for personal and spiritual growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek guidance through meditation or prayer, similar to how this mantra seeks the divine's consciousness.
    • In moments of decision-making, people may reflect on their values and beliefs, akin to grasping the divine wisdom that the mantra speaks of.
    • In various cultures, fire is used in rituals to symbolize purification and the presence of the divine, reinforcing the connection to Agni.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and understanding in our lives.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals face challenges, seek clarity, or strive for personal growth.
    • By invoking the divine consciousness, we can find direction and purpose, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life.
