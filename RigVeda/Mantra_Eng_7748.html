देवो देवाय धारणेंद्राय पवते सुतः |पयो यद अस्य पीपयत ||
devo devāya dhārayendrāya pavate sutaḥ | payo yad asya pīpayat ||
  • Devo devāya: This phrase translates to "to the divine deity." It signifies the reverence and respect towards the supreme divine power that governs the universe.
  • Dhārayendrāya: This can be interpreted as "to the one who holds the earth." It refers to the sustaining force of nature, often associated with Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, who is responsible for rain and thunderstorms, essential for agriculture.
  • Pavate sutaḥ: This means "the son of the wind." In this context, it refers to Vayu, the god of wind, who is vital for the movement of air and the dispersal of seeds, thus playing a crucial role in the cycle of life.
  • Payo yad asya pīpayat: This translates to "the milk that nourishes." Milk is often seen as a symbol of nourishment and sustenance, representing the abundance and fertility of nature.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of divine forces, nature, and sustenance. It highlights the importance of respecting and acknowledging the divine powers that sustain life on earth.
  • Real-life Application: In our daily lives, this mantra can remind us to appreciate the natural resources we have, such as air, water, and food. It encourages us to live in harmony with nature and to recognize the divine presence in our surroundings.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of gratitude towards the divine and nature. By acknowledging these forces, we can foster a deeper connection with the environment and promote sustainability in our actions.
