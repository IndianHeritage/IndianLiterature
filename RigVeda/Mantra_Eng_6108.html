मन्त्र: नि षु ब्रह्म जनानां याविष्टं तुयम् आ गतम् | मो ष्व अन्याम् उपारतम् ||
ni ṣu brahma janānāṁ yāviṣṭaṁ tūyam ā gatam | mo ṣv a1nyām̐ upāratam ||
  • Mantra: "ni ṣu brahma janānāṁ yāviṣṭaṁ tūyam ā gatam | mo ṣv a1nyām̐ upāratam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ni: not
    • ṣu: in the essence
    • brahma: the ultimate reality or consciousness
    • janānāṁ: of the people
    • yāviṣṭaṁ: that which is pervading
    • tūyam: that which is to be worshipped
    • ā gatam: has come
    • mo: my
    • ṣv: to you
    • a1nyām̐: to others
    • upāratam: to be at peace or to be still
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the essence of Brahman, the ultimate reality that pervades all existence.
    • It emphasizes the importance of recognizing this divine presence in our lives and the lives of others.
    • The phrase "not in the essence of the people" suggests that true understanding of Brahman transcends individual identities and societal constructs.
    • The call to worship "that which has come" indicates an acknowledgment of divine manifestations in the world.
    • Finally, the reference to peace and stillness implies that true realization of this ultimate reality leads to inner tranquility.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a busy urban environment, individuals often feel disconnected from their true selves. This mantra encourages them to seek the divine essence within and around them, fostering a sense of unity and peace.
    • In moments of conflict or stress, reflecting on this mantra can help individuals find calmness by recognizing the shared divine presence in all beings, promoting empathy and understanding.
    • In spiritual practices, such as meditation or yoga, this mantra can serve as a reminder to focus on the ultimate reality, helping practitioners to transcend their individual concerns and connect with a greater whole.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of recognizing the divine essence that permeates all existence.
    • It serves as a reminder to cultivate peace within ourselves and to acknowledge the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to greater harmony, understanding, and spiritual growth.
