प्र वां दंसांस्य अश्विनाव अवोचम अस्य पतिः स्यां सुगवः सुवीरः |उत पश्यन्न अश्नुवन दीर्घम आयुर अस्तम इवे जरीमाणं जगम्याम ||
pra vāṁ daṁsāṁsy aśvināv avocam asya patiḥ syāṁ sugavaḥ suvīraḥ | uta paśyann aśnuvan dīrgham āyur astam ivej jarimāṇaṁ jagamyām ||
  • pra vāṁ daṁsāṁsy aśvināv avocam: This phrase can be translated as "We have called upon the Ashvins." The Ashvins are twin deities in Vedic mythology, often associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between opposites.
  • asya patiḥ syāṁ sugavaḥ suvīraḥ: This translates to "May the lord of this (sacrifice) be strong and valiant." Here, "patiḥ" refers to the lord or master, indicating a plea for strength and valor in leadership. In real life, this can be seen in the context of leaders in various fields, such as politics or business, who are expected to be strong and courageous in their decisions.
  • uta paśyann aśnuvan: This means "And seeing, may we enjoy." It emphasizes the importance of perception and enjoyment in life. In practical terms, this can relate to the idea of mindfulness—being present and appreciating the moment, whether it’s enjoying a meal, a sunset, or a conversation with a friend.
  • dīrgham āyur astam ivej jarimāṇaṁ jagamyām: This translates to "May we attain a long life, as if we are moving away from old age." This line reflects the human desire for longevity and vitality. In modern life, this can be connected to health practices, such as regular exercise, a balanced diet, and mental well-being, all aimed at prolonging life and enhancing its quality.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, invoking divine assistance for health, strength, and longevity. It emphasizes the importance of leadership, mindfulness, and the pursuit of a fulfilling life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek balance in their lives, appreciate the present, and strive for personal growth and well-being. By embodying these principles, one can navigate the complexities of life with grace and resilience.
