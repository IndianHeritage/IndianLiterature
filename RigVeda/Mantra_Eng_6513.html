यद अध्रिगावो अध्रिगू इदाचिद अह्नो अश्विनाऽ हवामहे |वयं गीरभिर विपण्यवः ||
yad adhrigāvo adhrigū idā cid ahno aśvinā havāmahe | vayaṁ gīrbhir vipanyavaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yad adhrigāvo adhrigū idā cid ahno aśvinā havāmahe | vayaṁ gīrbhir vipanyavaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "We invoke the Aśvins, the two horsemen, who are the givers of wealth and prosperity, with our hymns and prayers."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yad: "that" or "which"
    • adhri: "to invoke" or "to call upon"
    • gāvo: "the two" (referring to the Aśvins)
    • adhri: "to invoke" (repeated for emphasis)
    • idā: "now" or "at this moment"
    • cid: "indeed" or "certainly"
    • ahno: "of the day" or "of the dawn"
    • aśvinā: "Aśvins" (the twin deities)
    • havāmahe: "we invoke" or "we call upon"
    • vayaṁ: "we" or "us"
    • gīrbhir: "with hymns" or "with praises"
    • vipanyavaḥ: "the givers of wealth" or "the bestowers of prosperity"
  • Context and Significance:
    • The Aśvins are considered divine twins in Vedic literature, symbolizing health, wealth, and prosperity.
    • This mantra is a call to the Aśvins, seeking their blessings for abundance and well-being.
    • In real life, this can be related to invoking positive energies and seeking support during challenging times, such as starting a new business or embarking on a new journey.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and collective effort, as it is a communal invocation for prosperity.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be recited during significant life events such as weddings, housewarming ceremonies, or business inaugurations to invite blessings.
    • It serves as a reminder to appreciate the support of others and to work together towards common goals, much like the Aśvins who are often depicted as helping those in need.
    • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek help and guidance from mentors or peers, reinforcing the idea that collaboration leads to success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine assistance and the importance of community in achieving prosperity.
    • It highlights the belief that invoking higher powers can lead to positive outcomes in various aspects of life.
    • By understanding and applying the teachings of this mantra, individuals can foster a mindset of gratitude, collaboration, and openness to receiving help from both divine and earthly sources.
