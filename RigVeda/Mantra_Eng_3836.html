अग्नि शर्ध महते साउभागाय तव द्युम्नान्य उत्तमानी सन्तु |सं जास्पत्यं सुघमं आ कृणुष्व शत्रुयताम् अभि तिष्ठा महांसि ||
agne śardha mahate saubhagāya tava dyumnāny uttamāni santu | saṁ jāspatyaṁ suyamam ā kṛṇuṣva śatrūyatām abhi tiṣṭhā mahāṁsi ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Śardha: This term means 'with strength' or 'with vigor,' indicating the power of Agni.
  • Mahate: This translates to 'great' or 'mighty,' emphasizing the grandeur of Agni.
  • Saubhagāya: This word means 'for auspiciousness' or 'for good fortune,' suggesting that the invocation is for positive outcomes.
  • Tava: This means 'your,' referring to Agni's attributes and blessings.
  • Dyumnāny: This translates to 'glories' or 'brilliance,' indicating the radiant qualities of Agni.
  • Uttamāni: This means 'the best' or 'the highest,' suggesting the desire for the highest form of blessings.
  • Santu: This means 'may they be' or 'let them be,' expressing a wish or prayer.
  • Saṁ jāspatyaṁ: This term refers to 'the essence of wisdom' or 'intellect,' indicating a desire for enlightenment.
  • Suyamam: This means 'good deeds' or 'virtuous actions,' emphasizing the importance of righteousness.
  • Ā kṛṇuṣva: This translates to 'make' or 'create,' indicating a call to action.
  • Śatrūyatām: This means 'to overcome enemies' or 'to defeat adversaries,' highlighting the need for protection.
  • Abhi tiṣṭhā: This translates to 'stand firm' or 'be steadfast,' indicating resilience and strength.
  • Mahāṁsi: This means 'great ones' or 'mighty beings,' referring to powerful forces or deities.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to Agni, seeking strength, auspiciousness, and the ability to overcome obstacles. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and virtuous actions in achieving success and protection from adversaries.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra to seek strength and guidance in their personal and professional lives, encouraging them to pursue virtuous actions and wisdom.
  • Conflict Resolution: In situations of conflict or competition, this mantra serves as a reminder to focus on righteousness and to seek peaceful resolutions while standing firm against negativity.
  • Community Well-being: Communities can invoke this mantra during gatherings or rituals to promote collective strength, auspiciousness, and protection against adversities.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, wisdom, and auspiciousness. It serves as a reminder to seek the highest virtues and to stand firm against challenges, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal development to community resilience.
