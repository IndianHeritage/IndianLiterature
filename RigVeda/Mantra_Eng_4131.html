यद अश्वान धूरषु पृथतीर अयुग्ध्वं हिरण्ययान प्रत्य अटकाम् अमुग्ध्वम् |विश्वा इत स्पृधो मरुतो व्य अस्यथा शुभं याताम् अनु रथा अवृत्सत ||
yad aśvān dhūrṣu pṛṣatīr ayugdhvaṁ hiraṇyayān praty atkām̐ amugdhvam | viśvā it spṛdho maruto vy asyatha śubhaṁ yātām anu rathā avṛtsata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • aśvān: horses
    • dhūrṣu: in the race
    • pṛṣatīr: the chariots
    • ayugdhvaṁ: may they not be hindered
    • hiraṇyayān: with golden (wealth)
    • praty: towards
    • atkām̐: desires
    • amugdhvam: may they not be obstructed
    • viśvā: all
    • it: thus
    • spṛdho: of the Maruts (storm deities)
    • maruto: the winds
    • vy: you
    • asyatha: may you be present
    • śubhaṁ: auspicious
    • yātām: may they go
    • anu: after
    • rathā: the chariots
    • avṛtsata: may they be protected
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine forces to ensure that the horses and chariots (representing strength and progress) are not hindered in their journey.
    • The mention of "hiraṇyayān" signifies the importance of wealth and prosperity in achieving one's goals.
    • The "Maruts" symbolize the forces of nature, and their presence is sought for auspiciousness and protection.
    • In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of success in various endeavors, such as business, education, or personal growth, where one seeks the support of favorable circumstances and divine blessings.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in overcoming obstacles and achieving success.
    • It emphasizes the importance of aligning one's desires with the natural forces and seeking harmony in one's pursuits.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be recited before starting a new venture, project, or journey to invoke positive energy and ensure that one’s efforts are fruitful.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to remain focused on one's goals while being open to the support of the universe and the divine.
