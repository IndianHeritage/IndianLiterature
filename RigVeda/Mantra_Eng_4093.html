प्र ये मे बन्ध्वेषे गां वोचन्त सूरयः पृष्णिं वोचन्त मातरम् |अधा पितरम् इष्मिणं रुद्रं वोचन्त शिक्वसः ||
pra ye me bandhveṣe gāṁ vocanta sūrayaḥ pṛśniṁ vocanta mātaram | adhā pitaram iṣmiṇaṁ rudraṁ vocanta śikvasaḥ ||
  • Translation: "Those who call upon my relatives, the Suras (divine beings), call upon the Earth; they call upon my mother, Pṛśni. They call upon my father, the one who is in the midst of the Rudras."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra ye me: "Those who" - indicates a call to action or invocation.
    • bandhveṣe: "relatives" - signifies the importance of family and community.
    • gāṁ: "Earth" - represents the physical world and our connection to it.
    • vocanta: "call upon" - suggests a plea or invocation for support.
    • sūrayaḥ: "divine beings" - refers to higher powers or deities.
    • pṛśniṁ: "mother" - symbolizes nurturing and protection.
    • adhā: "below" or "in addition" - indicates a connection to the foundational aspects of existence.
    • pitaram: "father" - represents authority and guidance.
    • iṣmiṇaṁ: "who is in the midst" - suggests being surrounded or encompassed by something significant.
    • rudraṁ: "Rudras" - refers to a group of deities associated with storms and transformation.
    • śikvasaḥ: "those who call" - emphasizes the act of invoking or seeking assistance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In family gatherings, invoking the presence of ancestors and loved ones can create a sense of unity and support, similar to how the mantra calls upon relatives.
    • Environmental movements often emphasize the importance of caring for the Earth, paralleling the invocation of the Earth in the mantra.
    • In times of crisis, people often turn to spiritual practices, calling upon divine beings for guidance and strength, reflecting the essence of the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of family, the Earth, and divine forces. It highlights the importance of invoking support from both the physical and spiritual realms. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts such as family gatherings, environmental stewardship, and spiritual practices during challenging times. By recognizing our relationships with each other and the world around us, we can foster a sense of community and responsibility, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
