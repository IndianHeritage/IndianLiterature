मित्रो जनान यातयति ब्रुवाणो मित्रो दाधार पृथिवीं उता द्यां |मित्रः कृष्टीर अनिमिषाभि चष्टे मित्राय हव्यम् घृतवज् जुहोत ||
mitro janān yātayati bruvāṇo mitro dādhāra pṛthivīm uta dyām | mitraḥ kṛṣṭīr animiṣābhi caṣṭe mitrāya havyaṁ ghṛtavaj juhota ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Mitro: Friend or ally
    • Janān: People or beings
    • Yātayati: Guides or leads
    • Bruvāṇo: Speaking or uttering
    • Dādhāra: Supports or holds
    • Pṛthivīm: The Earth
    • Uta: And
    • Dyām: The sky or heavens
    • Kṛṣṭīr: Prosperity or abundance
    • Animiṣābhi: With unwavering or steady
    • Caṣṭe: Acts or performs
    • Mitráya: To the friend or ally
    • Havyaṁ: Offerings or sacrifices
    • Ghṛtavaj: Like ghee or clarified butter
    • Juhota: They offer or invoke
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of friendship and support among people.
    • It suggests that a true friend guides and leads others, speaking words of wisdom.
    • The mantra highlights the role of friendship in sustaining both the Earth and the heavens, indicating a connection between human relationships and the natural world.
    • It also speaks to the idea of unwavering support and the prosperity that comes from such relationships.
    • The act of offering, likened to ghee, symbolizes the purity and richness of the intentions behind our actions towards friends and allies.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a supportive colleague who guides you through challenges exemplifies the essence of "Mitro." Their encouragement can lead to personal and professional growth.
    • In community settings, friendships that foster collaboration and mutual support can lead to collective prosperity, much like the mantra suggests.
    • In family dynamics, the unwavering support of family members during tough times reflects the idea of "animiṣābhi caṣṭe," where steadfastness is crucial for overcoming difficulties.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the profound impact of friendship and support in our lives.
    • It teaches us that by nurturing our relationships, we contribute to the well-being of ourselves and the world around us.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of guidance, unwavering support, and the spirit of offering can lead to a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of human relationships and the natural order, urging us to cultivate friendships that uplift and sustain.
