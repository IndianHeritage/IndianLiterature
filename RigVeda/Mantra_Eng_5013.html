त्रिषदस्थाऽ सप्तधातुः पञ्च जाताऽ वर्धयन्ती |वाजे-वाजे हव्याऽ भूत ||
triṣadhasthā saptadhātuḥ pañca jātā vardhayantī | vāje-vāje havyā bhūt ||
  • Mantra: "triṣadhasthā saptadhātuḥ pañca jātā vardhayantī | vāje-vāje havyā bhūt ||"
  • Word Breakdown:
    • triṣadhasthā: Refers to the threefold nature of existence, encompassing the physical, mental, and spiritual realms.
    • saptadhātuḥ: Represents the seven elements or principles that constitute life and the universe, often interpreted as earth, water, fire, air, space, mind, and spirit.
    • pañca jātā: Indicates the five categories or classes of beings, which can be understood as the five elements or the five senses.
    • vardhayantī: Means to nourish, enhance, or promote growth.
    • vāje-vāje: Suggests the idea of abundance and prosperity, often associated with offerings and sacrifices.
    • havyā bhūt: Refers to the offerings made to the divine, which are considered sacred and essential for spiritual growth.
  • Meaning: This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of various aspects of existence. It highlights the importance of nurturing the physical, mental, and spiritual dimensions of life through the elements and offerings. The mantra suggests that by understanding and enhancing these elements, one can achieve a harmonious and prosperous life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Physical Health: Just as the mantra speaks of nourishing the body (triṣadhasthā), individuals can focus on a balanced diet, exercise, and rest to maintain physical well-being.
    • Mental Well-being: Engaging in mindfulness practices or meditation can enhance mental clarity and emotional stability, reflecting the saptadhātuḥ aspect of nurturing the mind.
    • Spiritual Growth: Participating in community service or spiritual practices can be seen as offerings (havyā bhūt) that contribute to personal and collective growth.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life. It teaches that by nurturing all aspects of existence—physical, mental, and spiritual—one can achieve balance and prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied through practices that promote health, mindfulness, and community engagement, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and harmonious life.
