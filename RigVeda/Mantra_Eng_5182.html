अस्य देवस्य संसद्य अनीकं यं मर्तासः श्येतं जगृभ्रे |नियो गृभम् पुरुषेयीं उवोच दुरोकम अग्निर आयवे शुशोच ||
asya devasya saṁsady anīke yam martāsaḥ śyetaṁ jagṛbhre | ni yo gṛbham pauruṣeyīm uvoca durokam agnir āyave śuśoca ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asya: of this
    • devasya: divine
    • saṁsady: assembly or gathering
    • anīke: in the group
    • yam: whom
    • martāsaḥ: mortals
    • śyetaṁ: may see or perceive
    • jagṛbhre: have grasped or taken
    • ni: not
    • yo: who
    • gṛbham: the divine or sacred
    • pauruṣeyīm: of human origin
    • uvoca: spoke or declared
    • durokam: difficult or challenging
    • agnir: fire
    • āyave: for the purpose of
    • šuśoca: has lamented or grieved
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the divine assembly and the perception of mortals towards the divine.
    • It highlights the idea that while mortals may grasp the divine, there are aspects that remain elusive or challenging to understand.
    • The mention of fire (Agnir) symbolizes transformation, energy, and the divine presence that can be both nurturing and destructive.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team may come together (the assembly) to discuss a project (the divine). While they may grasp the goals, the complexities of execution (the challenging aspects) can be daunting.
    • In personal relationships, individuals may perceive love and connection (the divine) but often struggle with communication and understanding (the difficult aspects).
    • In nature, fire can represent both warmth and destruction, reminding us of the duality of existence and the need for respect and understanding of natural forces.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing the divine in our lives while acknowledging the challenges we face in understanding and connecting with it.
    • It serves as a reminder that while we may strive to grasp the divine, there will always be elements that require humility and acceptance of our limitations.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages us to seek understanding and connection with the divine, whether through nature, relationships, or personal growth, while being aware of the complexities involved.
