तद् इन मे चन्तसद् वपुषो वपुष्टारं पुत्रो यज जñानं पितरोर अधीयति |जायां पतिं वहति वाग्नुना सुमत पूंसा इद् भद्रो वहतुः परिष्कृतः ||
tad in me chantsad vapuṣo vapuṣṭaram putro yaj jānam pitror adhīyati | jāyā patiṁ vahati vagnunā sumat puṁsa id bhadro vahatuḥ pariṣkṛtaḥ ||
  • tad in me chantsad: This phrase can be interpreted as a call for divine presence or energy to reside within oneself. It signifies the importance of inner strength and spiritual connection.
  • vapuṣo vapuṣṭaram: Here, "vapuṣo" refers to the physical form or body, while "vapuṣṭaram" suggests an enhancement or improvement of that form. This can be understood as a desire for physical and spiritual well-being.
  • putro yaj jānam: "Putro" means son, and "yaj jānam" refers to the knowledge or wisdom that is passed down through generations. This emphasizes the value of familial bonds and the transmission of wisdom from parents to children.
  • pitror adhīyati: This phrase translates to "he who learns from his ancestors." It highlights the importance of respecting and learning from one’s forebears, which is crucial in many cultures where ancestral wisdom is revered.
  • jāyā patiṁ vahati: "Jāyā" means wife, and "patiṁ" means husband. This part of the mantra reflects the significance of marital relationships and the mutual support that partners provide to each other.
  • vagnunā sumat puṁsa: "Vagnunā" refers to the fire or energy, while "sumat" means good thoughts or intentions. This suggests that a person should be driven by positive energy and thoughts in their actions.
  • id bhadro vahatuḥ pariṣkṛtaḥ: This concluding phrase can be interpreted as a prayer for blessings and purity. "Bhadro" means auspicious, and "pariṣkṛtaḥ" refers to being refined or purified. It signifies the aspiration for a blessed and virtuous life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of spiritual growth, familial bonds, and the importance of positive intentions. In real life, it serves as a reminder to cultivate inner strength, respect ancestral wisdom, and nurture relationships. By embodying these principles, individuals can lead a more harmonious and fulfilling life, fostering a sense of community and connection with others. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as family gatherings, spiritual practices, or personal reflections, encouraging individuals to seek balance and positivity in their lives.
