त्वं ह त्यद इन्द्र सप्त युध्यन् पुरो वज्रिन पुरुकुत्साय दर्धः |बरHIR न यत सुदासे वृथा वर्ग अंहो राजन् वरिवः पूरवे कः ||
tvaṁ ha tyad indra sapta yudhyan puro vajrin purukutsāya dardaḥ | barhir na yat sudāse vṛthā varg aṁho rājan varivaḥ pūrave kaḥ ||
  • tvaṁ ha tyad indra: This phrase translates to "You, indeed, O Indra." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunder, and war. This invocation emphasizes the importance of Indra in the context of the mantra.
  • sapta yudhyan: This means "seven battles." The number seven is significant in many cultures, often representing completeness or perfection. In this context, it may refer to the various challenges or struggles one faces in life.
  • puro vajrin purukutsāya dardaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who wields the thunderbolt, to Purukutsa." Purukutsa is a legendary king in Vedic texts, and the mention of the thunderbolt symbolizes strength and power. It suggests that divine strength is available to those who seek it.
  • barhir na yat sudāse vṛthā varg: This translates to "the one who does not abandon the good deeds." It emphasizes the importance of righteousness and the pursuit of good actions, suggesting that true success comes from adhering to moral principles.
  • aṁho rājan varivaḥ pūrave kaḥ: This phrase can be understood as "O king, who is the one that brings forth abundance?" It highlights the role of leadership and the responsibility of rulers to provide for their people, ensuring prosperity and well-being.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and strength in overcoming life's challenges. It emphasizes the importance of righteousness, leadership, and the pursuit of good deeds.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:
    • In leadership roles, where individuals are called to act with integrity and provide for their communities.
    • In personal challenges, where one can draw strength from the divine or from moral principles to overcome difficulties.
    • In community service, where the focus is on good deeds and helping others, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on righteousness.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the divine support available to those who strive for righteousness and strength in the face of adversity. It encourages individuals to act with integrity and to seek guidance from higher powers, making it relevant in both personal and communal contexts.
