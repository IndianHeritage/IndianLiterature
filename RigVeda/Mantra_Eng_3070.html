आद इद पश्चा बुबुधाना व्य अख्यन्न आद इद रत्नं धारण्त द्युभक्तम् |विश्वे विश्वासु दुर्यासु देवाः मित्र धिये वरुण सत्यं अस्तु ||
ād it paścā bubudhānā vy akhyann ād id ratnaṁ dhārayanta dyubhaktam | viśve viśvāsu duryāsu devā mitra dhiye varuṇa satyam astu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ād: Beginning or initiation
    • it: This
    • paścā: After or later
    • bubudhānā: Those who are wise or enlightened
    • vy: They
    • akhyann: Have declared or revealed
    • ād: Again, beginning
    • id: This
    • ratnaṁ: Jewel or precious thing
    • dhārayanta: Holding or sustaining
    • dyubhaktam: The celestial or heavenly
    • viśve: All or universal
    • viśvāsu: In all aspects or everywhere
    • duryāsu: In difficult or challenging situations
    • devā: Gods or divine beings
    • mitra: Friend or ally
    • dhiye: In wisdom or intellect
    • varuṇa: The god of cosmic order
    • satyam: Truth
    • astu: Let it be or may it be
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of wisdom and enlightenment.
    • It emphasizes the role of divine beings in guiding individuals through challenges.
    • The reference to "jewel" signifies the value of wisdom and truth in life.
    • It suggests that true understanding comes from both the beginning and the end of experiences.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a leader who embodies wisdom can guide their team through difficult projects, much like the divine beings mentioned in the mantra.
    • In personal relationships, having a friend who provides honest feedback can be seen as a "jewel" that helps one navigate through life's challenges.
    • In education, teachers who inspire students to think critically and seek truth are fulfilling the role of the divine in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom, truth, and divine guidance in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to professional development.
    • By embracing the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a deeper understanding of themselves and the world around them.
