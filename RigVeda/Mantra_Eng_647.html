स घा राजा सप्ततिः शूशुवज जनो रातहव्यः प्रति यः शासम इन्बति |उक्ताऽ वा यो अभिगृणाति राधसā दानुर अस्मा उपरā पिन्वते दिवः ||
sa ghā rājā satpatiḥ śūśuvaj jano rātahavyaḥ prati yaḥ śāsam invati | ukthā vā yo abhigṛṇāti rādhasā dānur asmā uparā pinvate divaḥ ||
  • sa ghā rājā satpatiḥ: This phrase can be translated as "the king, the lord of the people." It signifies the importance of leadership and governance in society. A real-life example can be seen in modern democracies where elected leaders are expected to serve the interests of their constituents.
  • śūśuvaj jano rātahavyaḥ: This translates to "the people who are devoted to the truth." It emphasizes the value of honesty and integrity among individuals. In everyday life, this can be observed in communities where trust and transparency are foundational to relationships, such as in family units or workplaces.
  • prati yaḥ śāsam invati: This means "whoever follows the righteous path." It highlights the significance of adhering to moral and ethical principles. For instance, in a corporate setting, companies that prioritize ethical practices often gain a better reputation and customer loyalty.
  • ukthā vā yo abhigṛṇāti: This can be interpreted as "whoever praises or acknowledges." It points to the importance of recognition and appreciation in social interactions. In real life, this is evident in workplaces where employee recognition programs boost morale and productivity.
  • rādhasā dānur asmā: This translates to "the one who gives generously." It underscores the virtue of generosity and selflessness. A practical example is seen in charitable organizations that rely on donations to support their causes, demonstrating the impact of giving back to the community.
  • uparā pinvate divaḥ: This means "they ascend to the heavens." It symbolizes the ultimate reward for virtuous living and good deeds. In many cultures, it is believed that those who lead a righteous life are rewarded in the afterlife, which can motivate individuals to act ethically in their daily lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, integrity, ethical conduct, recognition, generosity, and the pursuit of a higher purpose. Its significance lies in its timeless relevance, encouraging individuals and leaders alike to embody these virtues in their lives. In real-life scenarios, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to stronger communities, ethical workplaces, and a more harmonious society, ultimately guiding individuals towards a fulfilling and meaningful existence.
