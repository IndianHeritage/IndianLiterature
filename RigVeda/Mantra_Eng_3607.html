प्र ये धामानि पूर्व्याण्य arcān वि यद उच्छान वियोतारो अमूराः | विधातारो वि ते दधुर अजस्राः ऋतधीतयो रुरुचन्त दसमाः ||
pra ye dhāmāni pūrvyāṇy arcān vi yad ucchān viyotāro amūrāḥ | vidhātāro vi te dadhur ajasrā ṛtadhītayo rurucanta dasmāḥ ||
  • pra ye dhāmāni pūrvyāṇy: This phrase can be translated as "those who have established the ancient abodes." Here, "dhāmāni" refers to the realms or abodes, and "pūrvyāṇy" indicates the ancient or primordial nature of these places. In real life, this can be likened to the foundational values and traditions that shape our societies, such as family, culture, and heritage.
  • arcān vi yad ucchān: This translates to "those who perform the rituals and offerings." "Arcān" refers to worship or rituals, and "ucchān" implies the act of elevating or raising. This can be seen in modern life through various religious practices where individuals or communities come together to perform rituals, such as Diwali celebrations in Hindu culture, where offerings are made to deities to seek blessings.
  • viyotāro amūrāḥ: This means "the shining ones who are immortal." "Viyotāro" refers to those who shine or illuminate, and "amūrāḥ" indicates immortality. In contemporary terms, this can be related to individuals who inspire others through their actions, such as leaders, activists, or even artists whose legacies continue to influence future generations.
  • vidhātāro vi te dadhur: This translates to "the creators who have established you." "Vidhātāro" refers to creators or those who shape existence, and "dadhur" means to hold or maintain. This can be compared to educators or mentors in our lives who shape our understanding and guide us towards our goals, much like teachers who influence students' futures.
  • ajasrā ṛtadhītayo rurucanta dasmāḥ: This phrase means "the eternal ones who are aware of the truth." "Ajasrā" indicates eternity, "ṛtadhītayo" refers to those who understand the truth, and "rurucanta" means to shine or radiate. In a real-life context, this can be associated with individuals who seek knowledge and truth, such as scientists or philosophers who contribute to our understanding of the universe and human existence.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of ancient wisdom, rituals, and the role of creators and truth-seekers in our lives. It signifies that our foundations are built upon the values and traditions passed down through generations, and that the pursuit of knowledge and truth is a continuous journey. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to honor their heritage, engage in meaningful rituals, and strive for enlightenment, ultimately contributing to a more harmonious and enlightened society.
