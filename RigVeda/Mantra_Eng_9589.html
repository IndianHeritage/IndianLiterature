आ तत्त इन्द्रायवः पनन्ताभि य उर्वं गोमन्तं तितृत्सान् |सकृत्स्वं ये पुरुपुत्राम महीं सहस्रधाराम बृहतीं दुदुक्षन् ||
ā tat ta indrāyavaḥ panantābhi ya ūrvaṁ gomantaṁ titṛtsān | sakṛtsva1ṁ ye puruputrām mahīṁ sahasradhārām bṛhatīṁ dudukṣan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā tat: That which is (or that which is to be attained)
    • ta: To that
    • indrāyavaḥ: Pertaining to Indra, the king of gods
    • panantābhi: With great strength or power
    • ya: Who
    • ūrvaṁ: The vastness or expanse
    • gomantaṁ: The wealth of cows (symbolizing prosperity)
    • titṛtsān: Desiring or longing for
    • sakṛtsvaṁ: Once or at one time
    • ye: Those who
    • puruputrām: The great earth or the vast land
    • mahīṁ: The great one
    • sahasradhārām: With a thousand streams or flows
    • bṛhatīṁ: The vast or expansive
    • dudukṣan: Have sought or have desired
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the aspiration for abundance and strength, invoking the blessings of Indra, who is often associated with rain, storms, and the fertility of the earth.
    • The phrase "wealth of cows" symbolizes prosperity and sustenance, reflecting the agrarian society of ancient India where cows were a measure of wealth.
    • The mention of "the great earth" and "thousand streams" signifies the interconnectedness of nature and the abundance it provides when respected and nurtured.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain and favorable conditions, similar to invoking Indra for blessings to ensure a good harvest.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be related to environmental sustainability, where the "great earth" and "thousand streams" remind us of the importance of preserving natural resources.
    • Communities seeking prosperity and growth can draw inspiration from this mantra, emphasizing the need for collective effort and respect for nature to achieve abundance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, prosperity, and harmony with nature.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that true wealth comes from a respectful relationship with the earth and its resources.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals and communities to strive for abundance while being mindful of their environmental impact, fostering a sustainable future.
