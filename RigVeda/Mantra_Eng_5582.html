मन्त्र: सस्तु माता सस्तु पिता सस्तु श्वा सस्तु विश्वपतिः |ससन्तु सर्वे ज्ञातयः सस्त्व अयम् अभितो जनः ||
sastu mātā sastu pitā sastu śvā sastu viśpatiḥ | sasantu sarve jñātayaḥ sastv ayam abhito janaḥ ||
  • Translation: "May the mother be auspicious, may the father be auspicious, may the dog be auspicious, may the lord of all be auspicious. May all relatives be auspicious, may this entire community be auspicious."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sastu: auspicious, good, or favorable
    • mātā: mother
    • pitā: father
    • śvā: dog
    • viśpatiḥ: lord of all, or the ruler of the community
    • jñātayaḥ: relatives or kin
    • janaḥ: people or community
  • Context and Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of auspiciousness in all relationships and aspects of life.
    • By invoking blessings for the mother and father, it highlights the foundational role of family in an individual's life.
    • The inclusion of the dog signifies the importance of all beings, even those considered less significant, in the community.
    • It acknowledges the lord of the community, suggesting a higher power or authority that governs and protects the collective.
    • Finally, it extends blessings to all relatives and the entire community, promoting harmony and well-being among all.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In family gatherings, this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings for unity and happiness among family members.
    • In community events, such as festivals or celebrations, it can serve as a reminder to foster goodwill and auspiciousness among all participants.
    • In personal reflections or prayers, individuals can use this mantra to cultivate a mindset of gratitude and positivity towards their loved ones and community.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of fostering auspiciousness in our relationships.
    • It encourages individuals to seek blessings not just for themselves but for their families, communities, and even for all living beings.
    • In a world often marked by division, this mantra promotes a sense of unity and collective well-being, making it highly applicable in various real-life scenarios.
