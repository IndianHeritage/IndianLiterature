हुवे वो देवीं अदितिं नमोभिर मृळीकाय वरुणं मित्रं अग्निम् |अभिक्षदाम् आर्यमणं सुशेवं त्रातृन् देवान् सवितारं भगं च ||
huve vo devīm aditiṁ namobhir mṛḻīkāya varuṇam mitram agnim | abhikṣadām aryamaṇaṁ suśevaṁ trātṝn devān savitāram bhagaṁ ca ||
  • Huve vo devīm aditiṁ: This phrase can be translated as "I invoke the divine Aditi." Aditi is often regarded as the mother of the gods and represents the infinite and the boundless. In real life, this can symbolize the quest for knowledge and understanding, as well as the nurturing aspect of the universe.
  • namobhir mṛḻīkāya: This translates to "with reverence to the one who is gentle." This can be interpreted as a call for humility and gentleness in our actions and thoughts. In everyday life, this can remind us to approach others with kindness and compassion, fostering better relationships.
  • varuṇam mitram agnim: Here, Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water, Mitra represents friendship and harmony, and Agni is the fire god. This line emphasizes the importance of maintaining harmony in our lives and respecting the natural order. For example, in environmental conservation, we can see the significance of respecting water bodies (Varuna) and fostering friendships (Mitra) to create a sustainable future.
  • abhikṣadām aryamaṇaṁ suśevaṁ: This can be interpreted as "may we be blessed with abundance and prosperity." It highlights the desire for well-being and success in our endeavors. In practical terms, this can relate to setting goals and working diligently towards them, while also being open to receiving help from others.
  • trātṝn devān savitāram bhagaṁ ca: This line translates to "may the gods protect us and grant us blessings." It signifies the importance of seeking divine protection and guidance in our lives. In real life, this can be seen in the practice of mindfulness and meditation, where individuals seek inner peace and protection from negative influences.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep reverence for the divine, the importance of gentleness, harmony, abundance, and divine protection. Its significance lies in its applicability to our daily lives, reminding us to cultivate kindness, seek knowledge, maintain harmony with nature, and strive for personal and communal well-being. By embodying these principles, we can create a more compassionate and balanced world.
