तं उ त्वा नूनम असुर प्रचेतसं राधो भागम् इवेमहै |महिवा कृत्तिः शरणा त इन्द्र प्र ते सुम्ना नो अश्नवन ||
tam u tvā nūnam asura pracetasaṁ rādho bhāgam ivemahe | mahīva kṛttiḥ śaraṇā ta indra pra te sumnā no aśnavan ||
  • tam u tvā nūnam asura pracetasaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, we invoke you, O Indra, who is the great thinker among the gods." Here, Indra is recognized as a powerful deity, often associated with strength and wisdom. In real life, this can be likened to seeking guidance from a mentor or a leader who possesses great knowledge and insight.
  • rādho bhāgam ivemahe: This translates to "We aspire for the wealth and prosperity that you bestow." This line emphasizes the desire for abundance and success. In contemporary terms, it reflects the aspirations of individuals and communities striving for economic growth and well-being, similar to how businesses seek to thrive in competitive markets.
  • mahīva kṛttiḥ śaraṇā: This means "Like a great work, we take refuge." It suggests that one should seek shelter in great deeds or actions. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as relying on hard work and dedication to achieve goals, much like how athletes train rigorously to excel in their sports.
  • ta indra pra te sumnā no aśnavan: This translates to "O Indra, may we not be deprived of your blessings." It expresses a plea for continued support and favor from the divine. In a modern context, this can be seen in the way people often seek encouragement and positive reinforcement from their peers, family, or community to pursue their dreams.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking wisdom, prosperity, and divine support in our endeavors. It highlights the importance of hard work and the pursuit of greatness while acknowledging the need for guidance from those who are knowledgeable. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, business ventures, and community initiatives, where individuals and groups strive for success while remaining grounded in their values and seeking support from others. The significance of this mantra lies in its timeless message of aspiration, collaboration, and reverence for the forces that guide us towards our goals.
