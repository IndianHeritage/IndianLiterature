तुभ्येद इमा सवना शूर विश्वा तुभ्यम ब्रह्माणि वर्धना कृत्नोमि |त्वं नृभिर हव्यो विश्वधासि ||
tubhyed imā savanā śūra viśvā tubhyam brahmāṇi vardhanā kṛṇomi | tvaṁ nṛbhir havyo viśvadhāsi ||
  • tubhyed imā savanā: This phrase translates to "I offer these invocations to you." It signifies the act of devotion and respect towards a higher power or deity. In real life, this can be seen in various rituals where individuals express gratitude or seek blessings, such as during festivals or personal prayers.
  • śūra viśvā: This means "O all-powerful one." It acknowledges the omnipotence of the divine. In everyday life, this can be related to the belief in a higher force that guides and protects individuals, similar to how people might turn to their faith during challenging times.
  • tubhyam brahmāṇi vardhanā kṛṇomi: This translates to "I create the growth of the divine." It reflects the idea of nurturing and fostering spiritual growth. In practical terms, this can be seen in educational or community initiatives aimed at personal development and enlightenment, such as workshops or spiritual retreats.
  • tvaṁ nṛbhir havyo viśvadhāsi: This means "You are to be invoked by all men." It emphasizes the universality of the divine presence and the importance of collective worship. In real life, this can be observed in communal gatherings, such as religious ceremonies or social movements, where people come together to seek a common purpose or support.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, the recognition of divine power, and the importance of spiritual growth and community. Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals to seek a deeper connection with the divine, foster personal and collective growth, and acknowledge the omnipresence of a higher power in their lives. This mantra can be applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as during personal prayers, community gatherings, or spiritual practices, reminding individuals of their connection to the divine and the importance of nurturing that relationship.
