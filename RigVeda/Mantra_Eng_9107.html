आगमन्न आप उषतीर बर्हिर एदं न्य अध्वरे असदन देवयन्तीः |अध्वर्यवः सुनुतेंद्राय सोमम अभूद उ वः सुशका देवयज्ञा ||
āgmann āpa uśatīr barhir edaṁ ny adhvare asadan devayantīḥ | adhvaryavaḥ sunutendrāya somam abhūd u vaḥ suśakā devayajyā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āgmann: Coming forth or arriving.
    • āpa: Waters or the divine essence.
    • uśatīr: The best or the most excellent.
    • barhir: The sacred grass or the altar.
    • edaṁ: This.
    • ny: Not.
    • adhvare: In the sacrifice or ritual.
    • asadan: Not seated or not established.
    • devayantīḥ: Divine or godly.
    • adhvaryavaḥ: The priests or those who perform the rituals.
    • sunutendrāya: To the one who is well spoken of or praised.
    • somam: The soma, a sacred drink or essence.
    • abhūd: Has become or has arisen.
    • u: And.
    • vaḥ: Your or of you.
    • suśakā: Well spoken or auspicious.
    • devayajyā: The divine worship or sacrifice.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the arrival of divine waters and the establishment of a sacred space for rituals.
    • It emphasizes the importance of the priests in performing the rituals and the significance of the soma, which symbolizes divine nourishment and spiritual awakening.
    • The phrase "not seated in the sacrifice" suggests that without the divine presence, the rituals lack meaning and effectiveness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern spiritual practices, the preparation of a sacred space before meditation or prayer can be seen as a parallel to the establishment of the altar in this mantra.
    • Just as the priests are essential for the rituals, in our lives, mentors and guides play a crucial role in our spiritual and personal development.
    • The concept of 'soma' can be related to the idea of seeking nourishment for the soul, whether through meditation, nature, or community.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine presence in rituals and the importance of preparation and intention in spiritual practices.
    • It teaches us that without a connection to the divine, our actions may lack depth and meaning.
    • In real life, it encourages us to create sacred spaces, seek guidance, and nourish our spiritual selves to lead a fulfilling life.
