स नो नृणां नृतमो रिषादाऽ अग्निर् गीरः अवसात् वेतु धीतिम् |तनाः च ये मगवानाḥ शविष्ठा वाजप्रसूता इषयन्त मनम् ||
sa no nṛṇāṁ nṛtamo riśādā agnir giro 'vasā vetu dhītim | tanā ca ye maghavānaḥ śaviṣṭhā vājaprasūtā iṣayanta manma ||
  • sa no nṛṇāṁ: This phrase translates to "may he (the divine) be with us, the men." It signifies a call for divine presence and support in human endeavors.
  • nṛtamo riśādā: This means "the best among men." It emphasizes the importance of striving for excellence and virtue in our actions and character.
  • agnir giro 'vasā: Here, "Agnir" refers to the fire or the divine energy, and "giro 'vasā" means "may our words be in harmony." This suggests the need for our speech and intentions to align with divine will.
  • vetu dhītim: This translates to "may he know our understanding." It implies a desire for clarity and wisdom in our thoughts and actions.
  • tanā ca ye maghavānaḥ: This means "and those who are powerful." It acknowledges the strength and influence of those who are capable and virtuous.
  • śaviṣṭhā: This term refers to "the most excellent" or "the best." It encourages individuals to aspire to their highest potential.
  • vājaprasūtā: This translates to "born of strength." It signifies the importance of strength and vigor in achieving one's goals.
  • iṣayanta manma: This means "may they inspire my mind." It is a plea for guidance and inspiration from the divine and the wise.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of divine support, excellence in character, and the alignment of our words and actions with higher principles. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as leadership, teamwork, and personal development. For instance, in a workplace setting, striving for excellence and seeking divine guidance can lead to better collaboration and innovation. In personal life, it encourages individuals to pursue their highest potential while remaining connected to a greater purpose. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine influence, urging us to seek wisdom and strength in our endeavors.
