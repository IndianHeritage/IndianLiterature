वृञ्जे ह यन् नमसां बर्हिर अग्नाव् अयामि श्रुग घृतवतिः सुवृक्तिः |अम्यक्षि सद्म सदने पृथिव्या अश्रायि यज्ञः सूर्ये न चक्षुः ||
vṛñje ha yan namasā barhir agnāv ayāmi srug ghṛtavatī suvṛktiḥ | amyakṣi sadma sadane pṛthivyā aśrāyi yajñaḥ sūrye na cakṣuḥ ||
  • vṛñje ha yan namasā barhir agnāv: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the divine, expressing reverence and devotion. The word "vṛñje" signifies a choice or selection, while "namasā" indicates a bowing or salutation. "Barhir" refers to the sacred space or altar, and "agnāv" relates to Agni, the fire god, who is central to Vedic rituals.
  • ayāmi srug ghṛtavatī suvṛktiḥ: Here, "ayāmi" means 'I come' or 'I approach', indicating a movement towards the divine. "Srug" refers to offerings, and "ghṛtavatī" means 'rich in ghee', symbolizing purity and abundance. "Suvṛktiḥ" translates to 'good conduct' or 'noble action', emphasizing the importance of righteous behavior in spiritual practices.
  • amyakṣi sadma sadane pṛthivyā: "Amyakṣi" can be understood as 'with clear vision', while "sadma" refers to a dwelling or home. "Sadane" means 'in the abode', and "pṛthivyā" relates to the earth. This line suggests a connection between the divine and the earthly realm, highlighting the importance of having clarity and vision in our homes and lives.
  • aśrāyi yajñaḥ sūrye na cakṣuḥ: "Aśrāyi" means 'sheltered' or 'protected', while "yajñaḥ" refers to the sacred ritual or sacrifice. "Sūrye" relates to the sun, symbolizing light and knowledge, and "na cakṣuḥ" translates to 'without eyes', indicating a deeper understanding beyond mere physical sight. This line emphasizes the significance of spiritual insight and the protection that comes from engaging in sacred practices.

  • Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, righteous action, and the connection between the divine and the earthly. It serves as a reminder of the importance of approaching spiritual practices with reverence and clarity. In real life, this can be applied by fostering a sense of gratitude and respect in our daily actions, creating a harmonious environment in our homes, and engaging in rituals that promote spiritual growth. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a deeper understanding of their purpose and connection to the universe.
