त्वं विश्वस्य जगतश्चक्षुर इन्द्रासि चक्षुषः | वृषा यद आजिं वृषणा सिषाससि चोदयन् वध्रिणा युजा ||
tvaṁ viśvasya jagataś cakṣur indrāsi cakṣuṣaḥ | vṛṣā yad ājiṁ vṛṣaṇā siṣāsasi codayan vadhriṇā yujā ||
  • tvaṁ viśvasya jagataś cakṣur: This phrase translates to "You are the eye of the universe." Here, "tvaṁ" means "you," "viśvasya" means "of the universe," "jagataḥ" means "of the world," and "cakṣur" means "eye." This signifies the omnipresence and all-seeing nature of the divine, suggesting that the divine is the source of perception and awareness in the universe.
  • indrāsi cakṣuṣaḥ: This translates to "You are Indra among the eyes." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with power, rain, and the heavens. This line emphasizes the idea that the divine is the most powerful and insightful among all forms of perception, akin to how Indra is the king of gods.
  • vṛṣā yad ājiṁ: This means "You who are the strong one." "Vṛṣā" refers to strength or power, and "yad" means "who." This line acknowledges the strength and might of the divine, suggesting that it is a source of support and protection for those who seek it.
  • vṛṣaṇā siṣāsasi: This translates to "You who are the protector." "Siṣāsasi" means "to protect" or "to guide." This line reinforces the idea of the divine as a guardian, providing safety and direction to those who are lost or in need.
  • codayan vadhriṇā yujā: This means "You who unite with strength." "Codayan" refers to the act of uniting or bringing together, "vadhriṇā" means "with strength," and "yujā" means "to join." This line suggests that the divine has the power to unite individuals and communities, fostering harmony and cooperation.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of the divine as the all-seeing protector and unifier of the universe. It highlights the importance of seeking guidance and strength from a higher power in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during times of uncertainty or conflict, where individuals or communities may seek divine intervention for clarity and strength. It serves as a reminder that there is a greater force that watches over us, guiding us towards unity and protection. By invoking this mantra, one can cultivate a sense of trust in the divine and a deeper connection to the universe, fostering resilience and harmony in everyday life.
