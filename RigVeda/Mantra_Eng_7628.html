दधामि ते मधुनो भक्षं अग्रे हितस ते भागः सुतो अस्तु सोमः |असश्च त्वं दक्षिणतः सखा मे 'धा वृत्राणि जङ्घनावा भूरी ||
dadhāmi te madhuno bhakṣam agre hitas te bhāgaḥ suto astu somaḥ | asaś ca tvaṁ dakṣiṇataḥ sakhā me 'dhā vṛtrāṇi jaṅghanāva bhūri ||
  • Translation: "I offer you the honeyed food; may your share be beneficial, O Soma! May you be my friend from the south; may you remove the obstacles and grant me abundance."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dadhāmi: I offer
    • te: to you
    • madhuno: honeyed (sweet)
    • bhakṣam: food
    • agre: in front (or before)
    • hitas: beneficial
    • te: your
    • bhāgaḥ: share
    • suto: may be born (or may come forth)
    • astu: let it be
    • somaḥ: O Soma (a deity associated with the moon and plants)
    • asaś: and
    • ca: also
    • tvaṁ: you
    • dakṣiṇataḥ: from the south
    • sakhā: friend
    • me: my
    • 'dhā: may you place
    • vṛtrāṇi: obstacles
    • jaṅghanāva: may you remove
    • bhūri: abundance
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the deity Soma, who represents the essence of life and is often associated with nourishment and prosperity.
    • The offering of "honeyed food" symbolizes the desire for sweetness and positivity in life, akin to how we seek joy and fulfillment in our daily experiences.
    • Requesting Soma to be a "friend from the south" can be interpreted as seeking guidance and support from a source that is nurturing and protective, much like how we rely on friends and family for assistance in challenging times.
    • The plea to "remove obstacles" reflects a universal human desire to overcome challenges, whether they be personal, professional, or spiritual. For instance, in a work environment, one might seek support from colleagues to navigate through a difficult project.
    • The notion of "granting abundance" resonates with the aspirations of individuals to achieve success and prosperity in various aspects of life, such as career growth, financial stability, and personal happiness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine support in our endeavors and recognizing the interconnectedness of life.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the need for nourishment—both physical and spiritual—and the desire for positive relationships that help us navigate life's challenges.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate gratitude, seek help from their community, and strive for personal growth while remaining open to the abundance that life has to offer.
