धीरासः पदं कवयो नयन्ति नानां हृदा रक्षमाṇा अजुर्म्यं |सिषासन्तः पर्य अपश्यन्त सिंधुम आविर एभ्यो अभवत सूर्यो नृण ||
dhīrāsaḥ padaṁ kavayo nayanti nānā hṛdā rakṣamāṇā ajuryam | siṣāsantaḥ pary apaśyanta sindhum āvir ebhyo abhavat sūryo nṝn ||
  • dhīrāsaḥ: This term refers to the wise or the intelligent. It signifies those who possess deep understanding and insight.
  • padaṁ: This means 'path' or 'way'. It symbolizes the journey or direction one takes in life.
  • kavayo: This translates to 'poets' or 'sages'. It indicates those who have the ability to perceive deeper truths and express them through wisdom.
  • nayanti: This means 'lead' or 'guide'. It suggests the act of directing others towards a goal or understanding.
  • nānā: This word means 'various' or 'many'. It reflects the diversity of thoughts, ideas, and paths available in life.
  • hṛdā: This translates to 'with the heart'. It emphasizes the importance of emotional intelligence and compassion in decision-making.
  • rakṣamāṇā: This means 'protecting' or 'guarding'. It signifies the need to safeguard one's values and beliefs.
  • ajuryam: This term refers to 'truth' or 'eternal truth'. It highlights the significance of adhering to truth in all aspects of life.
  • siṣāsantaḥ: This means 'those who are calm' or 'peaceful'. It indicates the importance of maintaining tranquility amidst chaos.
  • pary: This translates to 'around' or 'surrounding'. It suggests the interconnectedness of all beings and experiences.
  • apaśyanta: This means 'they saw' or 'they perceived'. It reflects the act of observation and understanding the world around us.
  • sindhum: This translates to 'ocean' or 'sea'. It symbolizes vastness, depth, and the collective unconscious of humanity.
  • āvir: This means 'to appear' or 'to manifest'. It signifies the emergence of knowledge or truth.
  • ebhyo: This translates to 'from these' or 'from here'. It indicates the source of wisdom and understanding.
  • abhavat: This means 'became' or 'was born'. It signifies the transformation or realization of knowledge.
  • sūryo: This translates to 'sun'. It symbolizes enlightenment, clarity, and the source of life.
  • nṝn: This means 'of men' or 'of humans'. It emphasizes the human experience and the journey of life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of wisdom, guidance, and the pursuit of truth in our lives. It teaches us that wise individuals can lead us on diverse paths, protecting our hearts and values while navigating through life's complexities. The imagery of the ocean and the sun signifies the vastness of knowledge and the illumination that comes from understanding. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as leadership, education, and personal growth, reminding us to seek wisdom, embrace diversity, and remain true to our values as we journey through life.
