इन्द्र ऋभुभिर वाजिभिर वाजयन्न इह स्तोमं जारितुर उप याहि यज्ञियम् |शतम् केतेभिर इषिरेभिर आयवे सहस्रणीथो अध्वरस्य होमनि ||
indra ṛbhubhir vājibhir vājayann iha stomaṁ jaritur upa yāhi yajñiyam | śataṁ ketebhir iṣirebhir āyave sahasraṇītho adhvarasya homani ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • ṛbhubhir: Means "with the wise" or "with the learned," indicating the importance of knowledge and wisdom in achieving success.
  • vājibhir: Translates to "with the strong" or "with the powerful," emphasizing the need for strength and support in endeavors.
  • vājayann: Means "winning" or "victorious," suggesting the goal of achieving success through collective effort.
  • iha: Means "here" or "in this world," grounding the mantra in the present reality.
  • stomaṁ: Refers to "praise" or "hymn," indicating the importance of expressing gratitude and reverence.
  • jaritur: Means "to be born" or "to arise," symbolizing new beginnings and the emergence of potential.
  • upa yāhi: Translates to "come near" or "approach," inviting divine presence and support.
  • yajñiyam: Refers to "sacred" or "related to sacrifice," highlighting the significance of rituals and offerings in spiritual practices.
  • śataṁ: Means "hundred," often symbolizing abundance and prosperity.
  • ketebhir: Refers to "with the stars" or "with the celestial bodies," indicating a connection to the cosmos and higher powers.
  • iṣirebhir: Means "with the powerful" or "with the capable," reinforcing the idea of collaboration and strength.
  • āyave: Refers to "for the purpose" or "for the aim," emphasizing intention in actions.
  • sahasraṇītho: Means "thousand-fold," symbolizing vastness and limitless potential.
  • adhvarasya: Refers to "of the sacrifice" or "of the ritual," connecting the mantra to the practice of yajna (sacrifice).
  • homani: Means "offering" or "sacrifice," indicating the act of giving and the importance of rituals in spiritual life.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of collaboration, strength, wisdom, and the divine in achieving success and prosperity in life. It highlights the importance of rituals and offerings, suggesting that through collective efforts and reverence, one can attain their goals. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork in professional settings, community service, or personal growth endeavors. By invoking the support of higher powers and working together with others, individuals can navigate challenges and achieve their aspirations, making this mantra a timeless guide for success and fulfillment.
