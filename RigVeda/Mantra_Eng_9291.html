अहम् इन्द्रो रोधो वक्षो अथर्वणस् तृताय गा अजनयम् अहेर अधि |अहम् दस्युभ्यः परि नृम्णम् आ ददē गोत्रा शिक्षन् दधीच मातारिश्वने ||
aham indro rodho vakṣo atharvaṇas tritāya gā ajanayam aher adhi | ahaṁ dasyubhyaḥ pari nṛmṇam ā dade gotrā śikṣan dadhīce mātariśvane ||
  • Translation: "I am Indra, the strong one, the protector of the Atharvans; I have given birth to the threefold (knowledge) and I have created the world. I give strength to the brave and I bestow knowledge to the wise, like Mātariśvan."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • aham: I
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods, symbolizing strength and power
    • rodho: strong, powerful
    • vakṣo: protector, guardian
    • atharvaṇas: related to the Atharva Veda, which emphasizes knowledge and wisdom
    • tritāya: threefold, representing different aspects of knowledge or existence
    • gā: to create or give birth
    • ajanayam: I have created
    • aher: the world
    • adhi: above, in a higher sense
    • dasyubhyaḥ: to the brave or strong
    • pari: around, encompassing
    • nṛmṇam: strength, power
    • ā: to, towards
    • dade: I give
    • gotrā: knowledge, wisdom
    • śikṣan: to teach or impart
    • dadhīce: like Dadhichi, a sage known for his wisdom
    • mātariśvane: referring to the wind or the divine force that connects all
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength and protection, akin to a leader in a community who safeguards the interests of its members.
    • In educational settings, teachers embody the role of imparting knowledge, similar to the way the mantra speaks of giving wisdom to the wise.
    • In personal development, individuals strive to cultivate their inner strength and wisdom, reflecting the essence of the mantra in their daily lives.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, wisdom, and protection. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge and power, urging individuals to seek wisdom and strength in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in leadership, education, and personal growth, encouraging individuals to embody the qualities of Indra and Mātariśvan in their pursuits. By embracing these ideals, one can contribute positively to society and foster a culture of knowledge and strength.
