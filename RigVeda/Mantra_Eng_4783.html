धībhir arvadbhiḥ arvato वाजां इन्द्र श्रवाय्यां |त्वया जेष्म हितं धनम् ||
dhībhir arvadbhir arvato vājām̐ indra śravāyyān | tvayā jeṣma hitaṁ dhanam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dhībhir: With intellects or minds
    • arvadbhir: With the best or most excellent
    • arvato: Of the strong or powerful
    • vājām̐: With wealth or resources
    • indra: O Indra, the king of gods
    • śravāyyān: Who is worthy of praise or glory
    • tvayā: By you
    • jeṣma: We shall conquer or achieve
    • hitaṁ: Beneficial or good
    • dhanam: Wealth or riches
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of gods, asking for the blessings of wealth and prosperity through the use of intellect and strength. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and power in achieving success and prosperity in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied when entrepreneurs seek to make wise decisions that lead to financial success. For instance, a startup founder may rely on their intellect and the strength of their team to navigate challenges and secure funding.
    • In education, students can embody this mantra by using their intellect to excel in their studies, thereby achieving scholarships or other forms of financial aid that can support their educational journey.
    • In personal finance, individuals can apply the principles of this mantra by making informed decisions about investments, thereby increasing their wealth and ensuring a secure future.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for the integration of intellect and strength in the pursuit of wealth and prosperity. It serves as a reminder that success is not merely about material gain but also about the wisdom and power we harness to achieve our goals. In real-life scenarios, whether in business, education, or personal finance, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more fulfilling and prosperous life.
