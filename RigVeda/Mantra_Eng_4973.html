शुक्रं ते अन्यद यजतं ते अन्यद विषुरूपे अहनी द्यौर इवासी |विश्वा हि मायā अवसि स्वधावो भद्रा ते पूषन्न इह रातिर अस्तु ||
śukraṁ te anyad yajataṁ te anyad viṣurūpe ahanī dyaur ivāsi | viśvā hi māyā avasi svadhāvo bhadrā te pūṣann iha rātir astu ||
  • śukraṁ te: This phrase translates to "your brightness" or "your purity." In a real-life context, this can refer to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, akin to how a bright light illuminates a dark room, guiding individuals towards clarity and understanding.
  • anyad yajataṁ te anyad: This means "you are worshipped in different forms." This reflects the idea that divinity can manifest in various ways, similar to how people may express love and respect for their parents, teachers, or mentors in different manners, yet the essence of reverence remains the same.
  • viṣurūpe ahanī: This translates to "in diverse forms." It emphasizes the multiplicity of existence. For example, in nature, we see the same water taking different forms—liquid, ice, vapor—yet it remains fundamentally water. This teaches us to appreciate diversity in life.
  • dyaur ivāsi: This means "as if you are the sky." The sky is vast and encompassing, symbolizing freedom and potential. In our lives, this can be likened to the limitless opportunities we have to grow and expand our horizons, much like the sky that holds the sun, moon, and stars.
  • viśvā hi māyā avasi: This translates to "the whole world is indeed an illusion." This can be interpreted as a reminder to look beyond superficial appearances and understand the deeper truths of life. For instance, social media often presents a curated version of reality, which can be misleading; recognizing this can lead to a more authentic existence.
  • svadhāvo bhadrā te: This means "may your offerings be auspicious." In practical terms, this can relate to the idea that our actions and intentions should be positive and beneficial, much like how a kind gesture can create a ripple effect of goodwill in a community.
  • pūṣann iha rātir astu: This translates to "may there be a pleasant night for you." This can symbolize the importance of rest and rejuvenation in our lives. Just as a good night's sleep restores our energy, taking time for self-care and reflection is essential for our overall well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound wisdom about the nature of existence, the importance of diverse expressions of divinity, and the significance of positive actions. In real life, it encourages us to seek knowledge, appreciate diversity, look beyond illusions, and strive for auspiciousness in our deeds. By embodying these principles, we can lead more fulfilling and harmonious lives, fostering a deeper connection with ourselves and the world around us.
