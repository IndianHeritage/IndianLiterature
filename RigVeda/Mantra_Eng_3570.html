स इद् राजा प्रतिजान्यानी विश्वा शुष्मेण तस्थाव अभि वीर्येण | बृहस्पतिं यः सुभृतं बिभर्ति वल्गूयति वन्दते पूर्वभाजम् ||
sa id rājā pratijanyāni viśvā śuṣmeṇa tasthāv abhi vīryeṇa | bṛhaspatiṁ yaḥ subhṛtam bibharti valgūyati vandate pūrvabhājam ||
  • sa id rājā: This phrase translates to "he is indeed the king." It signifies the importance of leadership and authority in society. In real life, a good leader is someone who inspires and guides others, much like a king would in a kingdom.
  • pratijanyāni: This word means "the promises" or "the commitments." It emphasizes the significance of keeping one's word and fulfilling responsibilities. For example, in a workplace, when a manager makes a commitment to their team, it is crucial for them to follow through to maintain trust and morale.
  • viśvā: This translates to "all" or "universal." It suggests inclusivity and the idea that everyone is part of a larger whole. In community service, for instance, when individuals come together to help those in need, they embody the spirit of viśvā by recognizing the interconnectedness of humanity.
  • šuṣmeṇa: This word means "with strength" or "with vigor." It highlights the importance of resilience and determination. In sports, athletes often demonstrate this quality by pushing through challenges and setbacks to achieve their goals.
  • tasthāv abhi vīryeṇa: This phrase can be interpreted as "he stands firm with strength." It reflects the idea of steadfastness in the face of adversity. For example, a student who perseveres through difficult studies to achieve academic success exemplifies this quality.
  • bṛhaspatiṁ: Referring to the deity of wisdom and knowledge, this term signifies the pursuit of wisdom. In real life, seeking knowledge through education and experience is essential for personal and professional growth.
  • yaḥ subhṛtam bibharti: This translates to "who carries the good." It emphasizes the importance of nurturing positive qualities and actions. For instance, a mentor who supports and uplifts their mentees embodies this principle by fostering growth and development.
  • valgūyati: This word means "he who speaks well" or "he who communicates effectively." Effective communication is vital in all aspects of life, from personal relationships to professional settings, as it fosters understanding and collaboration.
  • vandate pūrvabhājam: This phrase means "he who honors the past." It signifies the importance of respecting traditions and learning from history. In cultural practices, honoring ancestors and traditions helps maintain a sense of identity and continuity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, responsibility, resilience, wisdom, and respect for tradition. Its significance lies in the reminder that effective leadership is not just about authority but also about fulfilling promises, nurturing positive qualities, and honoring the past. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as in workplaces, communities, and personal relationships, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and productive society.
