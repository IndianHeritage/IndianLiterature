इन्द्रेण याथ सаратं सुते सच्चाम् अथो वशानाम् भवथा सह श्रीया | न वः प्रतिमै सुक्रतानि वाघतः सौधन्वना ऋभवो वीर्याणि च ||
indreṇa yātha sarathaṁ sute sacām̐ atho vaśānām bhavathā saha śriyā | na vaḥ pratimai sukṛtāni vāghataḥ saudhanvanā ṛbhavo vīryāṇi ca ||
  • Indreṇa: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Yātha: Means "as" or "in the manner of," indicating a comparison or a way of doing something.
  • Sarathaṁ: Translates to "charioteer," representing guidance and direction in life.
  • Sute: Means "with offerings" or "with gifts," suggesting the importance of generosity and sharing.
  • Sacām̐: Refers to "truth" or "sacredness," emphasizing the value of honesty and integrity.
  • Atho: Means "therefore" or "thus," indicating a conclusion or result.
  • Vaśānām: Refers to "those who are controlled" or "subdued," suggesting the importance of self-control and discipline.
  • Bhavathā: Means "by you" or "from you," indicating a source of strength or support.
  • Saha: Means "together" or "with," emphasizing collaboration and unity.
  • Śriyā: Refers to "prosperity" or "wealth," highlighting the importance of abundance in life.
  • Na: Means "not," indicating negation or absence.
  • Vaḥ: Refers to "you" in a plural sense, addressing a group or community.
  • Pratimai: Means "by the measure" or "by the standard," suggesting a benchmark or criteria.
  • Sukṛtāni: Refers to "good deeds" or "virtuous actions," emphasizing the importance of morality.
  • Vāghataḥ: Means "of speech" or "of words," indicating the power of communication.
  • Saudhanvanā: Refers to "wealthy" or "prosperous," highlighting the significance of material success.
  • Ṛbhavo: Means "strong" or "powerful," indicating the importance of strength in character.
  • Vīryāṇi: Refers to "heroic deeds" or "valorous actions," emphasizing courage and bravery.

This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, guidance, and collaboration in achieving prosperity and success. It suggests that by following the path of truth and virtue, and by working together with others, one can attain great achievements and live a fulfilling life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In leadership roles, where guiding others with strength and integrity is crucial for success.
  • In teamwork, where collaboration and shared goals lead to greater achievements.
  • In personal development, where self-control and virtuous actions contribute to a fulfilling life.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and virtuous living. It serves as a reminder that true prosperity comes from working together, adhering to moral values, and striving for excellence in all endeavors.
