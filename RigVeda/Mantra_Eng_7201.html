मां नः सेतुः सिषेद अयम् महे वृणक्तु नस् परि |इन्द्र इद धि श्रुतो वशी ||
mā naḥ setuḥ siṣed ayam mahe vṛṇaktu nas pari | indra id dhi śruto vaśī ||
  • mā naḥ setuḥ siṣed ayam: This phrase translates to "May this bridge not be broken for us." Here, "setuḥ" refers to a bridge, symbolizing connection and unity. In real life, this can be understood as the importance of maintaining relationships and connections with others, whether in family, friendships, or professional networks.
  • mahe vṛṇaktu nas pari: This means "May it choose us for greatness." The word "mahe" signifies greatness or abundance, while "vṛṇaktu" implies selection or choice. This can be related to the idea of striving for success and the importance of being chosen for opportunities that lead to personal and communal growth.
  • indra id dhi śruto vaśī: This translates to "Indra, indeed, is the one who is powerful." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, power, and leadership. This line emphasizes the need for strong leadership and guidance in our lives, akin to how effective leaders inspire and empower their communities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of connection, aspiration for greatness, and the importance of strong leadership. In real life, it serves as a reminder to nurture our relationships, seek opportunities for growth, and recognize the value of effective leadership in achieving our goals. By embodying these principles, we can create a supportive environment that fosters success and unity in our personal and professional lives.
