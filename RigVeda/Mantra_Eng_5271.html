तं होतारमध्वरस्य प्रचेतसं वह्निं देवाऽकृण्वत | दधाति रत्नं विधते सुवीर्यम अग्निर जनाय दाशुषे ||
taṁ hotāram adhvarasya pracetasaṁ vahniṁ devā akṛṇvata | dadhāti ratnaṁ vidhate suvīryam agnir janāya dāśuṣe ||
  • taṁ hotāram: This phrase refers to the "hotar," or the priest who performs the sacrificial rites. In a broader sense, it symbolizes the guiding force or the one who leads the rituals in life.
  • adhvarasya: This term means "of the sacrifice" or "of the ritual." It signifies the importance of actions and rituals in achieving one's goals.
  • pracetasaṁ: This word translates to "of the wise" or "of the intelligent." It emphasizes the need for wisdom and understanding in our endeavors.
  • vahniṁ: This refers to "fire," which is a central element in Vedic rituals. Fire symbolizes transformation, energy, and purification.
  • devā: This means "the gods" or "divine beings." It indicates the presence of higher powers that influence our lives and actions.
  • akṛṇvata: This translates to "they created" or "they established." It suggests the act of bringing something into existence through intention and effort.
  • dadhāti: This means "he gives" or "he bestows." It signifies the act of sharing or providing, which is essential in any community or relationship.
  • ratnaṁ: This word means "jewel" or "treasure." It symbolizes the valuable outcomes or rewards that come from our actions.
  • vidhate: This translates to "he arranges" or "he organizes." It indicates the importance of planning and structuring our efforts to achieve success.
  • suvīryam: This means "great strength" or "power." It emphasizes the need for resilience and vigor in our pursuits.
  • agnir: This refers to "fire" again, reinforcing its significance as a transformative force.
  • janāya: This means "for the people" or "for humanity." It highlights the collective aspect of our actions and their impact on society.
  • dāśuṣe: This translates to "for the benefactor" or "for the giver." It signifies the importance of generosity and support in our interactions.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of ritual, wisdom, and the transformative power of fire. It teaches us that through wise actions and rituals, we can create valuable outcomes not just for ourselves but for the community at large. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community service, leadership roles, and personal development. By embodying the principles of guidance, wisdom, and generosity, we can contribute positively to society and foster a sense of unity and purpose. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our actions and their significance in the greater tapestry of life.
