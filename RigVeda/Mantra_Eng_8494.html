प्रो स्य वह्निः पथ्याभिर अस्यां दिवो न वृष्टिः पवमानो अक्षाः |सहस्रधारो असदन न्य अस्मे मातुर उपस्थे वन आ च सोमः ||
pro sya vahniḥ pathyābhir asyān divo na vṛṣṭiḥ pavamāno akṣāḥ | sahasradhāro asadan ny a1sme mātur upasthe vana ā ca somaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pro sya vahniḥ pathyābhir asyān divo na vṛṣṭiḥ pavamāno akṣāḥ | sahasradhāro asadan ny a1sme mātur upasthe vana ā ca somaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pro sya: for this
    • vahniḥ: fire
    • pathyābhir: by the paths
    • asyān: of this
    • divo: of heaven
    • na: not
    • vṛṣṭiḥ: rain
    • pavamāno: blowing, purifying
    • akṣāḥ: the eyes
    • sahasradhāro: having thousands of streams
    • asadan: not to be seated
    • ny a1sme: we are not
    • mātur: of the mother
    • upasthe: in the lap
    • vana: forest
    • ā ca: and
    • somaḥ: the soma (sacred drink)
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of fire (vahni) and its purifying qualities. Fire is often seen as a symbol of transformation and energy in various cultures.
    • The phrase "by the paths" suggests that there are specific ways or methods to harness this energy, much like how we can channel our efforts in life towards achieving our goals.
    • The mention of "not rain" indicates a desire for clarity and focus, as rain can sometimes symbolize confusion or obstacles in our path.
    • The term "pavamāno" emphasizes the purifying aspect of fire, which can be related to personal growth and the cleansing of negative influences in our lives.
    • "Having thousands of streams" can be interpreted as the abundance of opportunities available to us, much like the various paths we can take in life.
    • The reference to "the mother" and "in the lap" signifies nurturing and support, reminding us of the importance of our roots and the foundational support we receive from our families and communities.
    • The inclusion of "soma" connects to the idea of nourishment and the sacred, suggesting that our pursuits should be aligned with higher values and spiritual growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of harnessing inner strength and clarity while navigating life's challenges.
    • It encourages us to seek purification and transformation through our actions and choices, much like how fire transforms materials.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal development, decision-making, and the pursuit of goals that align with our values.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to remain grounded in our roots while exploring the vast opportunities life presents, ensuring that our journey is both fulfilling and meaningful.
