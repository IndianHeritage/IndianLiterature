आ मे अस्य प्रतिव्यम् इन्द्रनासत्यागतम् | देवाः देवैभिर अद्य सचनस्तमाः ||
ā me asya pratīvya1m indranāsatyā gatam | devā devebhir adya sacanastamā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā me: "to me" or "for me"
    • asya: "of this" or "of this world"
    • pratīvya: "in the presence" or "in the sight"
    • indranāsatyā: "Indra, the lord of truth" or "Indra, the one who dispels falsehood"
    • gatam: "gone" or "departed"
    • devā: "gods" or "divine beings"
    • devebhir: "by the gods" or "among the divine"
    • adya: "today" or "in the present"
    • sacanastamā: "the one who is the best" or "the supreme truth"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the presence of Indra, the king of gods, who represents strength, power, and truth.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking truth and clarity in our lives, suggesting that one should strive to be in the presence of divine truth.
    • The phrase "for me" indicates a personal plea or desire for guidance and support from the divine.
    • By acknowledging the gods and their role in our lives, we recognize the interconnectedness of the divine and the human experience.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as a difficult decision at work, one might reflect on this mantra to seek clarity and truth in their choices.
    • During moments of doubt or confusion, individuals may recite this mantra as a reminder to align themselves with honesty and integrity.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can serve as a call for collective truth-seeking, encouraging open dialogue and transparency among members.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of truth and divine guidance in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek clarity and strength from higher powers, especially in times of uncertainty.
    • By incorporating the essence of this mantra into daily life, individuals can foster a deeper connection with their values and the divine, leading to more authentic and meaningful experiences.
