त्रातारो देवाः अधि वोकतā नो मा नो निद्रा ईशत मोत जल्पिः |वयं सोमस्य विश्वहा प्रियासः सुवीरासो विदथम आ वदेम ||
trātāro devā adhi vocatā no mā no nidrā īśata mota jalpiḥ | vayaṁ somasya viśvaha priyāsaḥ suvīrāso vidatham ā vadema ||
  • trātāro devā: This phrase translates to "the gods who protect us." It emphasizes the role of divine beings in safeguarding humanity. In real life, this can be likened to the protective figures in our lives, such as parents, mentors, or even community leaders who guide and support us through challenges.
  • adhi vocatā no: This means "may they speak to us." It suggests a call for communication and guidance from the divine. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way we seek advice from trusted individuals or spiritual leaders when faced with difficult decisions.
  • mā no nidrā: This translates to "may we not fall into sleep." Here, "sleep" symbolizes ignorance or inaction. In a practical sense, this can relate to the importance of staying aware and alert in our lives, whether it be in our personal growth, career, or social responsibilities.
  • īśata mota jalpiḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we not be overwhelmed by distractions." It highlights the need to focus on what truly matters. In today's fast-paced world, this resonates with the challenge of maintaining concentration amidst numerous distractions, such as social media or constant notifications.
  • vayaṁ somasya: This means "we are of the essence of Soma." Soma is often associated with vitality and immortality. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of health and well-being, emphasizing the importance of nurturing our physical and mental health.
  • viśvaha priyāsaḥ: This translates to "we are beloved by all." It signifies the interconnectedness of humanity and the importance of love and compassion. In practice, this can be seen in community service or acts of kindness that foster a sense of belonging and support among individuals.
  • suvīrāso vidatham ā vadema: This means "may we speak of noble deeds." It encourages the sharing of positive actions and virtues. In real life, this can be reflected in storytelling, where we share experiences that inspire others to act with integrity and courage.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine protection, communication, awareness, focus, health, love, and the sharing of noble deeds. Its significance lies in guiding individuals to lead a life of purpose and connection. In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to seek guidance, remain vigilant against distractions, prioritize our well-being, and foster a sense of community through acts of kindness and sharing inspiring stories. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling life for ourselves and those around us.
