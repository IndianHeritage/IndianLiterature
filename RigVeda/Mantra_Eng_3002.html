यां ते अग्ने पर्वतस्येव धारासश्चन्ती पीपयद देव चित्रा |तां अस्मभ्यम् प्रमतिं जातवेदो वसो रास्व सुमतिं विश्वजन्यां ||
yā te agne parvatasyeva dhārāsaścantī pīpayad deva citrā | tām asmabhyam pramatiṁ jātavedo vaso rāsva sumatiṁ viśvajanyām ||
  • yā te agne - "O Agni," refers to the divine fire or the god of fire in Vedic tradition, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • parvatasyeva - "like a mountain," signifies strength, stability, and permanence, suggesting that the qualities of Agni are as enduring as a mountain.
  • dhārāsaścantī - "flowing streams," indicates the continuous and nourishing flow of energy and knowledge, akin to how rivers sustain life.
  • pīpayad - "nourishing," emphasizes the role of Agni in providing sustenance and support, much like how fire is essential for cooking and warmth.
  • deva citrā - "O divine one, colorful," highlights the multifaceted nature of Agni, representing various aspects of life and creation.
  • tām asmabhyam - "that to us," indicates a plea or invocation for the blessings and guidance of Agni.
  • pramatiṁ - "intellect," signifies wisdom and understanding, suggesting that Agni can illuminate the path of knowledge.
  • jātavedo - "O born-knower," refers to Agni as the one who knows all, symbolizing enlightenment and awareness.
  • vaso rāsva - "may you dwell with us," expresses a desire for Agni's presence and guidance in our lives.
  • sumatiṁ - "good thought," emphasizes the importance of positive and constructive thinking, which leads to better outcomes.
  • viśvajanyām - "in all creation," signifies the universal aspect of Agni, indicating that its influence is present in every aspect of life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda invokes Agni, the god of fire, as a source of nourishment, wisdom, and stability. It emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance for intellectual growth and positive thinking. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, one can meditate on this mantra to seek clarity and wisdom in decision-making.
  • In community gatherings, invoking Agni can symbolize unity and the collective pursuit of knowledge and well-being.
  • In rituals, the mantra can be recited to honor the transformative power of fire, which is essential in many cultural practices.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in achieving wisdom, stability, and positive outcomes in all aspects of life.
