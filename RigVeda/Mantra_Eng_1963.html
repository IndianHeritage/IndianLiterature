तं त्वा वयम् पितो वचोभिर गावो न हव्याऽसुशूदिमा |देवेभ्यस त्वा सदहमादम् अस्मभ्यं त्वा सदहमादम् ||
taṁ tvā vayam pito vacobhir gāvo na havyā suṣūdima | devebhyas tvā sadhamādam asmabhyaṁ tvā sadhamādam ||
  • taṁ tvā vayam pito vacobhir gāvo na havyā suṣūdima
    • taṁ - that (referring to the divine or the deity)
    • tvā - you (the deity)
    • vayam - we
    • pito - with our prayers or offerings
    • vacobhir - with words (sacred utterances)
    • gāvo - cows (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • na - not
    • havyā - offerings (that which is offered to the gods)
    • suṣūdima - we shall be nourished or satisfied
  • devebhyas tvā sadhamādam asmabhyaṁ tvā sadhamādam
    • devebhyas - to the gods
    • tvā - you (the deity)
    • sadhamādam - we offer our prayers or praises
    • asmabhyaṁ - to us
    • tvā - you (the deity)
    • sadhamādam - we offer our prayers or praises

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the relationship between humans and the divine, particularly through the act of offering and prayer. The invocation begins with a plea to the deity, acknowledging their presence and the importance of offerings made with sincere words and intentions. The mention of "cows" symbolizes not just material wealth but also sustenance and nourishment, which are essential for life.


The second part of the mantra reinforces the idea of devotion and the reciprocal relationship between humans and the divine. By offering prayers to the gods, the devotees seek blessings and nourishment in return. This reflects a fundamental aspect of many spiritual practices where the act of giving is believed to bring about abundance and fulfillment.


Real-Life Examples:

  • In agricultural communities, the act of offering the first fruits of the harvest to the deities is a common practice, symbolizing gratitude and the hope for continued prosperity.
  • In modern contexts, people often engage in acts of charity or community service as a form of offering, believing that such actions will bring blessings and positive energy into their lives.

Conclusion:

The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of gratitude, and the power of words and intentions. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to recognize the value of their contributions to the community and the divine, fostering a sense of responsibility and connection. By practicing gratitude and offering sincere prayers, individuals can cultivate a more fulfilling and abundant life.
