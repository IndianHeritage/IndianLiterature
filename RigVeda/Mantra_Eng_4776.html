त्वं एकस्य वृत्रहन् अविताऽ द्वयोः असि |उतेदृशे यथा वयं ||
tvam ekasya vṛtrahann avitā dvayor asi | utedṛśe yathā vayam ||
  • tvam - This word translates to "you" or "thou," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • ekasya - Meaning "of one" or "the singular," this term emphasizes unity and the oneness of existence.
  • vṛtrahann - This compound word can be broken down into "vṛtra," which refers to a demon or obstacle, and "hanna," meaning "slayer" or "destroyer." Thus, it signifies the act of overcoming challenges or adversities.
  • avitā - This means "protector" or "guardian," suggesting a role of safeguarding and nurturing.
  • dvayor - Translated as "of two," this word indicates duality or the presence of opposing forces.
  • asi - This means "you are," affirming the existence or essence of the addressed entity.
  • utedṛśe - This term can be understood as "in such a way" or "in this manner," indicating a method or approach.
  • yathā - Meaning "as" or "like," it draws a comparison or analogy.
  • vayam - This translates to "we," indicating a collective or communal aspect.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to a divine force that embodies unity, protection, and the ability to overcome obstacles. It acknowledges the presence of both singularity and duality, suggesting that while there may be challenges (vṛtra), there is also a protective force (avitā) that helps navigate through them.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties, one can invoke the spirit of overcoming obstacles, recognizing that there is a protective force (be it inner strength, support from loved ones, or spiritual guidance) that aids in navigating through tough times.
  • Community Support: The collective aspect ("vayam") emphasizes the importance of community. In times of crisis, communities often come together to support one another, reflecting the duality of challenges and the unity in overcoming them.
  • Spiritual Growth: The mantra encourages individuals to seek a deeper understanding of their own existence and the interconnectedness of life. It serves as a reminder that while we face personal demons, we are also part of a larger whole that offers protection and guidance.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its profound message of unity, protection, and resilience. It teaches us that while we may encounter obstacles in life, there is always a protective force available to help us overcome them. This mantra can be a source of inspiration and strength in both personal and communal contexts, reminding us of the power of collective support and the importance of recognizing the divine within ourselves and others.
