शुनं हुवेम मगवाणं इन्द्रं अस्मिन भरे नृतमं वाजसातौ | श्रिण्वन्तं उग्रं ऊतये समत्सु घ्नन्तं वृत्राणि संजितं धनानाम् ||
śunaṁ huvema maghavānam indram asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau | śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi saṁjitaṁ dhanānām ||
  • śunaṁ huvema: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke the powerful." The term "śunaṁ" refers to something that is auspicious or beneficial, while "huvema" indicates the act of invoking or calling upon.
  • maghavānam indram: Here, "maghavānam" refers to the mighty or powerful, and "indram" is a reference to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature. This line emphasizes the invocation of Indra's strength and power.
  • asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau: This translates to "in this place, we offer our best." "Asmin" means "in this," "bhare" refers to "place," "nṛtamaṁ" means "the best," and "vājasātau" relates to offerings or sacrifices. This line signifies the importance of making the best offerings in a sacred space.
  • śṛṇvantam ugram: This means "to the one who listens intently." "Śṛṇvantam" refers to listening or hearing, and "ugram" indicates something fierce or powerful. This line suggests that the divine being we are addressing is attentive and powerful.
  • ūtaye samatsu: This translates to "for the purpose of protection." "Ūtaye" means "for protection," and "samatsu" refers to being in harmony or in a good state. This line emphasizes the need for divine protection in our lives.
  • ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi: This means "destroying the obstacles." "Ghnantaṁ" refers to the act of destroying, and "vṛtrāṇi" refers to obstacles or enemies. This line highlights the importance of overcoming challenges in life.
  • saṁjitaṁ dhanānām: This translates to "the gathered wealth." "Saṁjitaṁ" means "gathered" or "collected," and "dhanānām" refers to wealth or resources. This line signifies the importance of accumulating wealth and resources in a righteous manner.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the invocation of divine strength and protection, particularly from Indra, who symbolizes power and victory over obstacles. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as seeking guidance and strength during challenging times, making the best efforts in our endeavors, and striving for success while ensuring that our actions are righteous. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in overcoming life's challenges and achieving prosperity, reminding us to remain focused on our goals while seeking the support of higher powers.
