देव त्वष्टार यद ध चातुर्वत्म आनड यद अंगिरसाम अभवः सचाभूः |स देवानाम पाठ उप प्र विद्वाम् उशन यक्षि द्रविणोदः सुरत्नः ||
deva tvaṣṭar yad dha cārutvam ānaḍ yad aṅgirasām abhavaḥ sacābhūḥ | sa devānām pātha upa pra vidvām̐ uśan yakṣi draviṇodaḥ suratnaḥ ||
  • Deva Tvaṣṭar: This refers to the divine craftsman or creator, Tvaṣṭar, who is responsible for the creation and shaping of the universe. In real life, this can be likened to the role of an architect or a designer who brings ideas to life through creativity and skill.
  • Yad Dha Cārutvam: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which is beautiful or attractive." In everyday life, beauty can be found in nature, art, and human relationships, reminding us of the importance of aesthetics and harmony in our surroundings.
  • Ānaḍ: This term signifies joy or bliss. In real life, moments of joy can be experienced through personal achievements, relationships, or even simple pleasures like enjoying a sunset or a good meal.
  • Yad Aṅgirasām Abhavaḥ: This refers to the lineage of the Aṅgirasas, a group of sages known for their wisdom and knowledge. In contemporary terms, this can be related to the importance of mentorship and learning from those who have come before us, such as teachers or elders.
  • Sacābhūḥ: This can be understood as "being true" or "existing in truth." In real life, this emphasizes the value of honesty and integrity in our actions and relationships, fostering trust and respect among individuals.
  • Sa Devānām Pātha: This phrase translates to "the path of the gods." It signifies a spiritual journey or the pursuit of higher knowledge and understanding. In modern life, this can be seen in the quest for personal growth, self-discovery, and spiritual enlightenment.
  • Upa Pra Vidvām̐: This means "I seek to know." It highlights the importance of knowledge and wisdom in our lives. In real life, this can be reflected in the pursuit of education, lifelong learning, and the quest for truth in various fields.
  • Uśan Yakṣi: This refers to the divine being associated with wealth and prosperity. In practical terms, this can be related to the pursuit of financial stability and abundance in our lives, as well as the ethical use of resources.
  • Draviṇodaḥ: This term signifies wealth or resources. In real life, it emphasizes the importance of managing our resources wisely, whether they are financial, emotional, or social, to create a fulfilling life.
  • Suratnaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the essence of joy." It reminds us to seek happiness and fulfillment in our lives, whether through relationships, work, or personal passions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, beauty, joy, wisdom, and the pursuit of truth. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of these elements in our lives. By embodying the principles of creativity, integrity, and the quest for knowledge, we can navigate our personal and spiritual journeys more effectively. In a world that often prioritizes material success, this mantra encourages us to seek deeper meaning and fulfillment, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
