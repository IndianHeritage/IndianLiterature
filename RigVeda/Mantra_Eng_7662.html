आयं यथा न आब्भुवत तवष्टा रूपेव तक्ष्या |अस्य क्रत्वा यशस्वतः ||
ayaṁ yathā na ābhuvat tvaṣṭā rūpeva takṣyā | asya kratvā yaśasvataḥ ||
  • ayaṁ - This word translates to "this" or "here." It signifies the present moment or the current state of being.
  • yathā - Meaning "as" or "like," it indicates a comparison or a manner of being.
  • na - This is a negation, meaning "not." It emphasizes the absence of something.
  • ābhuvat - This term can be understood as "to become" or "to manifest." It refers to the process of coming into existence or realization.
  • tvaṣṭā - This word refers to the "creator" or "artisan." In Vedic literature, Tvaṣṭā is often associated with the divine craftsman who shapes and forms the universe.
  • rūpeva - Meaning "like a form" or "in the form," it suggests the idea of manifestation or physical representation.
  • takṣyā - This term relates to "to create" or "to fashion." It emphasizes the act of making or constructing something.
  • asya - This means "of this" or "of this one," indicating possession or relation to the subject at hand.
  • kratvā - This word refers to "by the power of action" or "through effort." It signifies the importance of intention and action in achieving results.
  • yaśasvataḥ - Meaning "of glory" or "of fame," it highlights the outcome of actions that lead to recognition and honor.

The mantra can be interpreted as a reflection on the nature of creation and existence. It suggests that in the process of becoming, one must recognize the role of the creator and the actions taken to manifest glory and success. The emphasis on "not becoming" indicates a caution against stagnation or failure to evolve.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the creator shapes the universe, individuals can shape their own lives through conscious actions and decisions. For example, a student who diligently studies and practices will manifest success in their academic pursuits.
  • Professional Development: In a workplace, employees who take initiative and demonstrate creativity can lead projects to success, much like Tvaṣṭā crafting a masterpiece. A team that collaborates effectively can achieve remarkable results.
  • Community Building: The mantra can also apply to social initiatives where individuals come together to create positive change. By working collectively with intention, communities can transform their environments and achieve recognition for their efforts.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the significance of conscious creation and the power of action in shaping one's destiny. It serves as a reminder that through intention and effort, individuals can manifest their aspirations and achieve greatness in various aspects of life.
