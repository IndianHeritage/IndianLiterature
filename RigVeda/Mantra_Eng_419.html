मन्त्र: हिरण्यपाणिः सविता विचर्षणिर उभे dyावापृथिवी अन्तर ईयते | अपामिवाम बाधते वेति सूर्यं अभि कृष्णेन रजसा dyाम ऋणोति ||
hiraṇyapāṇiḥ savitā vicarṣaṇir ubhe dyāvāpṛthivī antar īyate | apāmīvām bādhate veti sūryam abhi kṛṣṇena rajasā dyām ṛṇoti ||
  • hiraṇyapāṇiḥ: This term translates to "the one with golden hands." In a metaphorical sense, it represents abundance and prosperity. For example, a successful entrepreneur who creates wealth and opportunities for others can be seen as having "golden hands."
  • savitā: This refers to the Sun, the source of life and energy. In real life, the Sun is essential for agriculture, as it provides the necessary light and warmth for crops to grow. Without the Sun, life on Earth would not be sustainable.
  • vicarṣaṇir: This word can be interpreted as "the one who moves swiftly." It signifies the dynamic nature of life and the universe. For instance, the rapid advancements in technology reflect this swift movement, impacting our daily lives significantly.
  • ubhe dyāvāpṛthivī: This phrase means "both heaven and earth." It symbolizes the connection between the spiritual and the material worlds. In our lives, this can be seen in the balance we strive to maintain between our spiritual beliefs and our earthly responsibilities.
  • antar īyate: This translates to "moves within." It suggests an inner journey or exploration. For example, meditation and self-reflection are practices that encourage individuals to look within themselves for understanding and growth.
  • apāmīvām: This term refers to "the waters." Water is vital for life, representing purity and sustenance. In real life, clean water access is crucial for health and well-being, highlighting the importance of environmental conservation.
  • bādhate: This means "to bind" or "to connect." It can be related to relationships and community ties. For instance, family bonds and friendships are essential connections that provide support and love in our lives.
  • veti sūryam: This translates to "the Sun knows." It implies awareness and enlightenment. In a practical sense, knowledge and education illuminate our paths, much like the Sun illuminates the world.
  • abhi kṛṣṇena: This phrase means "with the black or dark one," often referring to the concept of duality. In life, we encounter both light and dark experiences, and understanding this duality helps us navigate challenges.
  • rajasā: This term signifies "with energy" or "passion." It reflects the drive and motivation we have in pursuing our goals. For example, an athlete's dedication and energy in training exemplify this concept.
  • dyām ṛṇoti: This means "he/she/it binds the sky." It symbolizes the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe. In real life, this can be seen in how ecosystems function, where every component plays a role in maintaining balance.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of abundance, energy, and the balance between the spiritual and material realms. Its significance lies in reminding us of our responsibilities towards nature, our relationships, and our inner selves. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages us to seek harmony in our lives, to appreciate the abundance around us, and to recognize the importance of both our inner journeys and our connections with others. By embodying these principles, we can lead more fulfilling and meaningful lives.
