मन्त्र: उता त्या यजता हरि कुमारात साहदेव्यात् |प्रयता सद्य आ ददे ||
uta tyā yajatā harī kumārāt sāhadevyāt | prayatā sadya ā dade ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May we worship the divine energy of the young one, who is the son of the divine, and may we receive blessings swiftly."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • uta: A term often used to invoke or call upon divine energies.
    • tyā: Refers to the act of worship or reverence.
    • yajatā: Indicates the process of performing rituals or sacrifices.
    • harī: A name often associated with the divine, particularly in the context of Vishnu or Krishna.
    • kumārāt: Refers to the young one or child, symbolizing innocence and potential.
    • sāhadevyāt: Suggests a connection to divine feminine energy, often associated with strength and nurturing.
    • prayatā: Means to strive or exert effort.
    • sadya: Implies immediacy or swiftness.
    • ā dade: Means to grant or bestow blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, this mantra can be recited by individuals seeking guidance and blessings in their endeavors, such as starting a new job or project.
    • In community settings, it can be used during festivals or rituals to invoke divine blessings for collective well-being, such as during Diwali or Navratri.
    • In educational environments, students may chant this mantra before exams or important presentations to seek clarity and success.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine energies for support and blessings. It emphasizes the importance of worship and effort in achieving one's goals. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to seek divine guidance and to put in the necessary effort to realize our aspirations. By acknowledging both the divine and our own efforts, we create a harmonious balance that can lead to success and fulfillment in various aspects of life.
