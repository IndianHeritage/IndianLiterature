अपो षु ण इयं शरुर आदित्यā अप दुरमतिः |अस्मद एत्व अजघ्नुषी ||
apo ṣu ṇa iyaṁ śarur ādityā apa durmatiḥ | asmad etv ajaghnuṣī ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apo: waters
    • ṣuṇa: good, auspicious
    • iyam: this
    • śarur: body
    • ādityā: O Adityas (the celestial beings, often associated with the sun)
    • apa: away from
    • durmatiḥ: bad thoughts or ill intentions
    • asmad: our
    • etv: having come here
    • ajaghnuṣī: do not harm
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra invokes the waters and the celestial beings, specifically the Adityas, to bless the speaker with purity and to keep away negative thoughts and harmful intentions. It emphasizes the importance of a pure body and mind, seeking protection from any ill will.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be recited during meditation or yoga practices to cultivate a sense of purity and clarity. For instance, before starting a meditation session, one might chant this mantra to set a positive intention.
    • In a community setting, such as during a festival or gathering, this mantra can be used to invoke harmony and goodwill among participants, ensuring that negative thoughts do not disrupt the collective spirit.
    • In personal relationships, individuals can reflect on the essence of this mantra to foster understanding and compassion, steering clear of misunderstandings and negative emotions.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for purity and protection from negativity. It serves as a reminder to maintain a clear mind and body, which is essential in various aspects of life, including personal well-being, relationships, and community interactions. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can create a more positive and harmonious environment around them, making it applicable in both personal and social contexts.
