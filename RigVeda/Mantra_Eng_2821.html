शुनं हुवेम मगवाणं इन्द्रं अस्मिन भरे नृतमं वाजसातौ | श्रिण्वन्तं उग्रं ऊतये समत्सु घ्नन्तं वृत्राणि संजितं धनानाम् ||
śunaṁ huvema maghavānam indram asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau | śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi saṁjitaṁ dhanānām ||
  • śunaṁ huvema: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke the powerful." The term "śunaṁ" refers to something that is auspicious or beneficial, while "huvema" means to invoke or call upon. In real life, this can be seen as a call to seek the support of positive forces or energies in our endeavors.
  • maghavānam indram: Here, "maghavānam" refers to the mighty or powerful, and "indram" is a reference to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition. This signifies the importance of seeking strength and leadership from higher powers or authorities in our lives, much like how individuals might look up to mentors or leaders for guidance.
  • asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau: This translates to "in this place, we offer our best." "Asmin" means 'in this', "bhare" refers to 'place', "nṛtamaṁ" means 'the best', and "vājasātau" relates to 'the best of offerings'. This can be related to the idea of giving our best efforts in any situation, whether in work, relationships, or personal growth.
  • śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye: "śṛṇvantam" means 'listening', "ugram" refers to 'fierce' or 'powerful', and "ūtaye" means 'for the purpose of upliftment'. This emphasizes the importance of being attentive to powerful teachings or messages that can elevate our lives. For example, actively listening to wise advice from elders or experts can lead to personal development.
  • samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi: "samatsu" means 'in the assembly', "ghnantaṁ" refers to 'striking down', and "vṛtrāṇi" relates to 'obstacles' or 'enemies'. This suggests that in a community or group setting, we should work together to overcome challenges. A real-life example could be teamwork in a project where collective effort is needed to tackle difficulties.
  • saṁjitaṁ dhanānām: This phrase translates to "the gathered wealth." "Saṁjitaṁ" means 'gathered' and "dhanānām" refers to 'wealth'. This can be interpreted as the importance of accumulating resources, whether material or spiritual, through collective effort and unity.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of invoking strength and guidance from higher powers, giving our best efforts, listening to wise teachings, and working together to overcome obstacles. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, teamwork, and community building. By embodying these values, individuals can foster a supportive environment that leads to collective success and prosperity.
