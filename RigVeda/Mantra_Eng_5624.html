यं त्रायध्व इदं-इदं देवासो यं च नयथ || तस्मा अग्ने वरुण मित्रार्यमन मरुतः शर्म यच्छत ||
yaṁ trāyadhva idam-idaṁ devāso yaṁ ca nayatha | tasmā agne varuṇa mitrāryaman marutaḥ śarma yacchata ||
  • yaṁ trāyadhva - "Whom you protect": This phrase emphasizes the idea of divine protection. In real life, this can be likened to the support we receive from our loved ones or mentors during challenging times.
  • idam-idaṁ devāso - "This and that, O Deities": This suggests the omnipresence of divine forces in all aspects of life. For example, in nature, we see how various elements work together harmoniously, reflecting the interconnectedness of all things.
  • yaṁ ca nayatha - "Whom you lead": This indicates guidance provided by the divine. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way leaders guide their teams or how parents guide their children towards making wise decisions.
  • tasmā agne - "Therefore, O Agni": Agni, the fire god, symbolizes transformation and energy. In practical terms, this can relate to how we harness our inner energy to achieve our goals, much like how fire transforms raw materials into useful products.
  • varuṇa mitrāryaman marutaḥ - "Varuna, Mitra, Aryaman, and the Maruts": These deities represent various aspects of nature and human experience, such as water, friendship, nobility, and the winds. In real life, we can see how these elements influence our lives, from the importance of water for survival to the value of friendship in our social interactions.
  • śarma yacchata - "Grant us protection": This is a plea for safety and security. In modern contexts, this can be related to seeking shelter, both physically and emotionally, as we navigate through life's uncertainties.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine protection, guidance, and support from various forces in life. Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine presence that influences our journey. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to seek guidance from mentors, cherish friendships, and recognize the importance of community and nature in our lives. By invoking these principles, we can cultivate a sense of security and purpose, navigating through challenges with the support of both the divine and our social networks.
