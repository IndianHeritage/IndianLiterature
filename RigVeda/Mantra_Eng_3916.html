आ ते 'वो वरेण्यं वृषन्तमस्य हूमहे |वृषजूतिर हि जज्ञिषा आभूभिर इन्द्र तुर्वणिः ||
ā te 'vo vareṇyaṁ vṛṣantamasya hūmahe | vṛṣajūtir hi jajñiṣa ābhūbhir indra turvaṇiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā te: From you
    • ’vo: of you
    • vareṇyaṁ: the most excellent or worthy
    • vṛṣantamasya: of the strongest or most powerful
    • hūmahe: we invoke or call upon
    • vṛṣajūtir: the one who brings strength
    • hi: indeed
    • jajñiṣa: born or manifested
    • ābhūbhir: by the shining or radiant
    • indra: O Indra, the king of gods
    • turvaṇiḥ: the one who is powerful or strong
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a call to Indra, the king of the gods, invoking his strength and excellence.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking the blessings of the divine for strength and power in our endeavors.
    • The phrase "we invoke" signifies a collective effort to seek divine intervention and support.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal or communal challenges, individuals often turn to prayer or meditation, seeking strength and guidance from a higher power.
    • In leadership, invoking the qualities of strength and excellence can inspire teams to work together towards a common goal, much like how communities come together in times of crisis.
    • In sports, athletes often invoke the spirit of champions, seeking the strength and excellence to perform at their best.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine strength and excellence in our lives.
    • It teaches us that seeking support from a higher power can empower us to overcome challenges and achieve our goals.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal struggles, community efforts, or professional endeavors, the essence of this mantra encourages us to recognize and harness the strength within and around us.
