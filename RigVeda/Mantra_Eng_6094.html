धībhiḥ सātāni kāṇvas्य वाजिनः प्रियमेद्हैर अभिद्युभिः |षषṭiṁ सहस्रानु निर्मजाम अजे निर यूथानि गवाम ऋषिः ||
dhībhiḥ sātāni kāṇvasya vājinaḥ priyamedhair abhidyubhiḥ | ṣaṣṭiṁ sahasrānu nirmajām aje nir yūthāni gavām ṛṣiḥ ||
  • dhībhiḥ: This word refers to the intellect or understanding. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and discernment in our actions and decisions.
  • sātāni: This term can be interpreted as 'the best' or 'the most excellent'. It signifies the pursuit of excellence in all endeavors.
  • kāṇvasya: This refers to the sage or seer, specifically the sage Kanva, who is known for his wisdom and insight. It highlights the value of learning from wise individuals.
  • vājinaḥ: This word means 'those who possess strength or power'. It can be associated with the idea of being strong in character and action.
  • priyamedhair: This term translates to 'with beloved offerings'. It suggests the importance of devotion and sincerity in our actions and offerings.
  • abhidyubhiḥ: This can be understood as 'with those who are enlightened'. It emphasizes the significance of surrounding oneself with enlightened individuals for guidance.
  • ṣaṣṭiṁ: This means 'sixty'. In a broader sense, it can symbolize completeness or a full cycle.
  • sahasrānu: This translates to 'thousands'. It signifies abundance and the vastness of possibilities.
  • nirmajām: This term means 'to cleanse or purify'. It indicates the importance of purification, both physically and spiritually.
  • aje: This word means 'the unchanging' or 'eternal'. It signifies the eternal truths that guide our lives.
  • nir yūthāni: This can be interpreted as 'without groups' or 'individually'. It emphasizes the importance of individual strength and responsibility.
  • gavām: This word means 'of cows', which in Vedic literature often symbolizes wealth and nourishment.
  • ṛṣiḥ: This refers to a sage or seer, emphasizing the role of wisdom in guiding our actions.

Conclusion: This mantra from the RigVeda emphasizes the importance of wisdom, strength, and purity in our lives. It encourages individuals to seek excellence, surround themselves with enlightened beings, and engage in sincere offerings. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, leadership, and community building. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute positively to society and foster a sense of unity and purpose. The mantra serves as a reminder of the eternal truths that guide us and the significance of individual responsibility in achieving collective well-being.
