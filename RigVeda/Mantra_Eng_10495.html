येनेंद्रो हविषा कृत्व्य अभवद् dyumny उत्तमः |इदं तद् अकृ देवाः असपत्नः किलाभुवम् ||
yenendro haviṣā kṛtvy abhavad dyumny uttamaḥ | idaṁ tad akri devā asapatnaḥ kilābhuvam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yenendro: By whom Indra (the king of gods)
    • haviṣā: With offerings
    • kṛtvy: Having performed
    • abhavad: Became
    • dyumny: Glorious
    • uttamaḥ: The best
    • idaṁ: This
    • tad: That
    • akri: Not done
    • devā: The gods
    • asapatnaḥ: Without enemies
    • kilābhuvam: Indeed became
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine power and glory that comes from performing rituals and offerings to the gods, particularly Indra, who is often associated with strength and victory.
    • The phrase "by whom Indra became glorious" suggests that through proper offerings and devotion, one can invoke divine blessings and achieve greatness.
    • The latter part, "the gods indeed became without enemies," implies that such devotion leads to peace and protection from adversities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the practice of gratitude and respect towards nature and the universe. For instance, when people engage in community service or environmental conservation, they are essentially making offerings to the greater good, which can lead to a harmonious society.
    • In personal development, individuals who practice mindfulness and gratitude often find themselves in a more positive and fulfilling state, akin to invoking the blessings of the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the power of devotion and the impact of positive actions. It teaches that through sincere offerings and good deeds, one can attain not only personal glory but also contribute to a peaceful and prosperous community.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to engage in acts of kindness, community service, and environmental stewardship, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
