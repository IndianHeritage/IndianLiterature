आ परमाभिर उता मध्यामाभिर नियुद्भिर यातम अवमाभिर अर्वाक् | दृढस्य चिद गोमतो वि व्रजस्य दुरो वर्तं गृणते चित्रराति ||
ā paramābhir uta madhyamābhir niyudbhir yātam avamābhir arvāk | dṛḻhasya cid gomato vi vrajasya duro vartaṁ gṛṇate citrarātī ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • paramābhir: the highest
    • uta: and
    • madhyamābhir: the middle
    • niyudbhir: by the forces
    • yātam: let us go
    • avamābhir: by the lower
    • arvāk: outward
    • dṛḻhasya: of the strong
    • cid: consciousness
    • gomato: of the cow (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • vi: in
    • vrajasya: of the pasture
    • duro: difficult
    • vartaṁ: to be done
    • gṛṇate: sings
    • citrarātī: of the beautiful and varied
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the journey of life and the various paths one can take.
    • The terms "paramābhir" and "madhyamābhir" refer to the highest and middle paths, suggesting that there are different levels of existence and consciousness.
    • The phrase "yātam avamābhir" indicates a movement towards the lower or more material aspects of life, which can be seen as a caution against being overly attached to worldly possessions.
    • The reference to "gomato" symbolizes wealth and nourishment, emphasizing the importance of sustaining oneself and others.
    • The mantra encourages us to navigate through life's challenges ("duro vartaṁ") while appreciating the beauty and diversity of experiences ("citrarātī").
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, one might choose to pursue a high-level position (paramābhir) or a more balanced role (madhyamābhir), weighing the benefits and challenges of each path.
    • In personal relationships, one may find themselves drawn to superficial connections (avamābhir) but ultimately seek deeper, more meaningful interactions (dṛḻhasya).
    • In the context of self-improvement, individuals often face the challenge of balancing material success with spiritual growth, as highlighted by the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the various paths we can take in life and the importance of being mindful of our choices.
    • It encourages us to seek balance between material and spiritual pursuits, recognizing that both are essential for a fulfilling life.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can guide individuals in making decisions that align with their values and aspirations, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
