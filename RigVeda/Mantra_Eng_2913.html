धानावन्तं करम्भिणम् अपूपवन्तम् उक्थिनम् |इन्द्र प्रातर जुषस्व नः ||
dhānāvantaṁ karambhiṇam apūpavantam ukthinam | indra prātar juṣasva naḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dhānāvantaṁ: One who possesses wealth or abundance.
    • karambhiṇam: One who is engaged in actions or deeds.
    • apūpavantam: One who is worthy of praise or is celebrated.
    • ukthinam: One who is invoked or called upon.
    • indra: The king of gods, representing strength and power.
    • prātar: In the morning.
    • juṣasva: May you enjoy or may you be pleased.
    • naḥ: Us or our.
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the god of rain and thunder, asking him to bless us in the morning with wealth, good deeds, and praise. It emphasizes the importance of abundance and the actions that lead to prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be seen as a call for success and prosperity in one's ventures. For instance, entrepreneurs often seek blessings for their new projects, hoping for abundance and recognition in their fields.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during morning rituals to set a positive tone for the day, inviting success and fulfillment in their daily activities.
    • Communities may invoke this mantra during harvest festivals, seeking blessings for a bountiful yield and the prosperity of their families.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the connection between divine blessings and human efforts. It signifies that while one may strive for success through hard work, invoking divine support can enhance those efforts.
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as starting a new job, launching a project, or even during significant life changes, reminding individuals to seek both personal effort and divine guidance.
    • It serves as a reminder to appreciate the abundance in life and to engage in actions that are worthy of praise, fostering a mindset of gratitude and positivity.
