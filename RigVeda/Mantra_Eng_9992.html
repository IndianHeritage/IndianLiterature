बलविज्ञायः स्थविरः प्रवीरः सहस्वान् वाजी सहमान उग्रः |अभिवीरो अभिसत्वा सहोजा जैत्रं इन्द्र रथम आ तिष्ठ गोवित ||
balavijñāyaḥ sthaviraḥ pravīraḥ sahasvān vājī sahamāna ugraḥ | abhivīro abhisatvā sahojā jaitram indra ratham ā tiṣṭha govit ||
  • balavijñāyaḥ: This term can be broken down into two parts: "bala" meaning strength or power, and "vijñāya" meaning knowing or understanding. Together, it refers to the understanding of strength or the wisdom that comes from recognizing one's own power.
  • sthaviraḥ: This word means "the elder" or "the wise one." It signifies the importance of experience and wisdom that comes with age, suggesting that those who are older have valuable insights to offer.
  • pravīraḥ: This translates to "the brave" or "the valiant." It emphasizes the qualities of courage and valor, highlighting the need for bravery in facing challenges.
  • sahasvān: Meaning "one who is powerful" or "strong," this term underscores the importance of inner strength and resilience in overcoming obstacles.
  • vājī: This word means "the one who possesses horses" or "the one who is swift." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent speed, agility, and the ability to move forward quickly in life.
  • sahamāna: This term means "one who is capable" or "one who can endure." It suggests the importance of perseverance and the ability to withstand difficulties.
  • ugraḥ: This translates to "fierce" or "powerful." It indicates a strong, intense energy that can be harnessed for positive outcomes.
  • abhivīro: This means "one who is victorious" or "one who overcomes." It emphasizes the idea of triumph and success in endeavors.
  • abhisatvā: This term can be understood as "one who is accomplished" or "one who has achieved." It highlights the importance of achievement and the recognition of one's accomplishments.
  • sahojā: Meaning "born together" or "coexisting," this word signifies the importance of community and collaboration in achieving goals.
  • jaitram: This translates to "victory" or "success." It encapsulates the ultimate goal of striving for success in various aspects of life.
  • indra: Referring to the king of gods in Vedic tradition, Indra symbolizes power, authority, and leadership. This term invokes the idea of divine support in one's endeavors.
  • ratham: This means "chariot," which can be seen as a vehicle for progress and movement. It represents the journey one takes in life.
  • ā tiṣṭha: This phrase means "stand firm" or "be steadfast." It encourages resilience and determination in the face of challenges.
  • govit: This term can be interpreted as "the protector of cows," symbolizing nourishment, care, and the importance of nurturing one's resources and relationships.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, wisdom, bravery, and community in achieving success. It teaches us that understanding our own power and capabilities, along with the support of experienced individuals, can lead to triumph in our endeavors. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as leadership roles, team collaborations, and personal challenges. By embodying the qualities of resilience, courage, and wisdom, individuals can navigate their journeys effectively, ensuring that they not only strive for success but also uplift those around them. The mantra serves as a reminder that true strength lies in understanding oneself and working together towards common goals.
