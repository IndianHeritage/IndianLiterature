वयं हि वां हवामहे विपण्यवो विप्रासो वाजसातये |तां वल्गू दसरā पुरुदंससā धियाश्विनā श्रुष्ट्य ā गतम् ||
vayaṁ hi vāṁ havāmahe vipanyavo viprāso vājasātaye | tā valgū dasrā purudaṁsasā dhiyāśvinā śruṣṭy ā gatam ||
  • vayaṁ hi vāṁ havāmahe: This phrase translates to "We indeed invoke you." It signifies a call to the divine or a higher power, emphasizing the importance of seeking guidance and support from the universe or the divine forces.
  • vipanyavo viprāso vājasātaye: Here, "vipanyavo" refers to the wise or learned individuals, while "vājasātaye" means for the purpose of strength or power. This part of the mantra highlights the value of wisdom and knowledge in achieving strength and success in life.
  • tā valgū dasrā purudaṁsasā: This can be interpreted as "those who are swift and powerful." It suggests the importance of agility and strength in overcoming challenges. In real life, this can be related to athletes or individuals who excel in their fields through a combination of speed and power.
  • dhiyāśvinā śruṣṭy ā gatam: This phrase translates to "with the intellect of the Ashvins, we have attained creation." The Ashvins are twin deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. This part emphasizes the role of intellect and creativity in achieving success and innovation in various aspects of life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of invoking divine support, valuing wisdom, and recognizing the importance of agility and intellect in achieving success. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various fields such as education, sports, and personal development. By seeking guidance from higher powers, valuing knowledge, and harnessing our strengths, we can navigate challenges and achieve our goals effectively. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of divine support, human intellect, and the pursuit of excellence.
