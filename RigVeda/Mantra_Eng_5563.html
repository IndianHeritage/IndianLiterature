यद्विजामन् पुरुषि वन्दनं भुवदष्टिवन्तौ परि कुल्फौ च देहात् |अग्निष्टच्छोचनं अप बाधताम् इतो मां मां पद्येन रपसां विदत्सरुः ||
yad vijāman paruṣi vandanam bhuvad aṣṭhīvantau pari kulphau ca dehat | agniṣ ṭac chocann apa bādhatām ito mā mām padyena rapasā vidat tsaruḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yad vijāman paruṣi vandanam bhuvad aṣṭhīvantau pari kulphau ca dehat | agniṣ ṭac chocann apa bādhatām ito mā mām padyena rapasā vidat tsaruḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • vijāman: we know or understand
    • paruṣi: with strength or firmness
    • vandanam: salutation or worship
    • bhuvad: of the earth or worldly
    • aṣṭhīvantau: those who possess strength
    • pari: around or in all directions
    • kulphau: the ankles or feet
    • ca: and
    • dehat: of the body
    • agniṣ: to the fire
    • ṭac: that
    • chocann: may it shine or illuminate
    • apa: away or from
    • bādhatām: may it not harm
    • ito: from here
    • mā: not
    • mām: me
    • padyena: by the foot or step
    • rapasā: with strength or vigor
    • vidat: may it know
    • tsaruḥ: the one who is swift or quick
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the strength and resilience of the body and the importance of worship and respect towards the elements of nature, particularly fire.
    • The phrase "yad vijāman paruṣi" emphasizes the knowledge and understanding we have about the strength that exists in the world around us.
    • It highlights the significance of acknowledging and respecting the forces of nature, which can be seen in practices like yoga and meditation where individuals connect with their inner strength and the environment.
    • The reference to "agniṣ" (fire) symbolizes purification and transformation, akin to how fire is used in rituals to cleanse and sanctify.
    • The mantra also serves as a reminder to protect oneself from negative influences, as indicated by "apa bādhatām," which translates to "may it not harm." This can be related to modern practices of mindfulness and self-care.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied when facing challenges. For instance, during stressful situations, one can recite this mantra to invoke strength and resilience.
    • In the context of environmental awareness, acknowledging the power of nature and treating it with respect aligns with the mantra's teachings.
    • Practices such as yoga, where individuals focus on their physical and mental strength, resonate with the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, respect for nature, and self-protection.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize their inner power and the importance of maintaining harmony with the world around them.
    • In a fast-paced modern life, this mantra serves as a reminder to pause, reflect, and connect with both our inner selves and the environment, fostering a sense of balance and well-being.
