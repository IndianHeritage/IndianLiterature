विष्णुर गोपाः परमं पाति पाथः प्रियाः धामान्य अमृतā दधानाḥ |अग्निष्टा विश्वा भुवनानि वेद महद देवानाम् असुरत्वम् एकम् ||
viṣṇur gopāḥ paramam pāti pāthaḥ priyā dhāmāny amṛtā dadhānaḥ | agniṣ ṭā viśvā bhuvanāni veda mahad devānām asuratvam ekam ||
  • viṣṇur gopāḥ: This phrase refers to Vishnu as the protector or guardian. In real life, this can be likened to a leader or a mentor who safeguards the interests of their community or followers, much like a teacher who nurtures and guides students.
  • paramam pāti: This means "the supreme protector." It signifies a higher power that oversees and ensures the well-being of all. In everyday life, this can be seen in the role of parents or guardians who provide unconditional love and support to their children.
  • pāthaḥ priyā: This translates to "the beloved path." It suggests that the journey or the way one chooses to live should be cherished. In a practical sense, this can relate to pursuing one's passions and values, leading to a fulfilling life.
  • dhāmāny amṛtā dadhānaḥ: This phrase means "those who hold the immortal abode." It refers to the idea of eternal truth and wisdom. In real life, this can be associated with individuals who seek knowledge and enlightenment, contributing positively to society.
  • agniṣ ṭā: This means "the fire." Fire symbolizes transformation and purification. In a real-world context, it can represent the challenges and trials one faces, which ultimately lead to personal growth and resilience.
  • viśvā bhuvanāni veda: This translates to "the all-knowing one who understands the worlds." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and awareness. In modern life, this can be seen in the pursuit of education and understanding diverse cultures and perspectives.
  • mahad devānām: This means "the great among the gods." It signifies the importance of recognizing and honoring greatness in various forms, whether in leadership, creativity, or moral integrity. In real life, this can be reflected in the admiration of role models who inspire others.
  • asuratvam ekam: This translates to "the unity of divine and non-divine." It suggests that all beings, regardless of their nature, are interconnected. In practical terms, this can be seen in the importance of community and collaboration, where diverse individuals come together for a common purpose.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of protection, knowledge, and unity. It emphasizes the significance of guidance, the pursuit of a cherished path, and the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership roles, educational pursuits, and community building. By embodying these values, individuals can contribute to a harmonious and enlightened society.
