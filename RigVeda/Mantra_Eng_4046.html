वितन्वते धियः अस्मा अपांसी वस्त्राः पुत्राय मातरः वयन्ति |उपप्रक्षे वृषणो मोदमानाः दिवस पथाः वध्वो यन्त्यच्छ ||
vi tanvate dhiyo asmā apāṁsi vastrā putrāya mātaro vayanti | upaprakṣe vṛṣaṇo modamānā divas pathā vadhvo yanty accha ||
  • vi tanvate: This phrase can be interpreted as "they expand" or "they grow." It signifies the idea of growth and development, which is essential in various aspects of life, such as personal growth, education, and relationships.
  • dhiyo: This word refers to "intellect" or "understanding." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in making informed decisions and navigating life's challenges.
  • asmā: Meaning "for us," this term indicates a collective experience or benefit, highlighting the interconnectedness of individuals within a community or society.
  • apāṁsi: This translates to "of the waters." Water is often associated with life, nourishment, and purity. It symbolizes the essential resources we need for survival and growth.
  • vastrā: This means "clothing" or "covering." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent protection and the various layers of identity we wear in society.
  • putrāya: This word means "for the son" or "for the child." It signifies the importance of nurturing the next generation and the responsibilities that come with parenthood.
  • mātaro: This translates to "mothers." It highlights the role of mothers in nurturing and guiding their children, emphasizing the significance of maternal love and care.
  • vayanti: This means "they move" or "they go." It suggests progress and the journey of life, where individuals and communities strive towards their goals.
  • upaprakṣe: This can be interpreted as "they are endowed" or "they are blessed." It signifies the gifts and talents that individuals possess, which can be utilized for personal and communal growth.
  • vṛṣaṇo: This word refers to "the strong" or "the powerful." It emphasizes the strength and resilience required to overcome challenges and achieve success.
  • modamānā: This translates to "delighting" or "enjoying." It signifies the joy and fulfillment that comes from achieving one's goals and living a meaningful life.
  • divas: This means "day." It symbolizes the passage of time and the opportunities each day brings for growth and achievement.
  • pathā: This translates to "path" or "way." It represents the journey one takes in life, the choices made, and the direction pursued.
  • vadhvo: This means "of the mortals" or "of the beings." It signifies the human experience and the shared journey of all individuals.
  • yanty: This translates to "they go" or "they proceed." It emphasizes the continuous movement and evolution of life.
  • accha: This means "good" or "well." It signifies the positive outcomes that arise from wise choices and actions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of growth, wisdom, and the interconnectedness of life. It emphasizes the importance of nurturing the next generation, the role of mothers, and the strength required to navigate life's journey. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal development, parenting, and community building. It serves as a reminder to cherish the resources we have, support one another, and strive for a fulfilling and meaningful existence. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can contribute positively to their families and society as a whole.
