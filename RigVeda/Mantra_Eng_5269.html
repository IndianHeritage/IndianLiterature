ये राधांसी ददत्य अश्व्या मघा कामेन श्रवसो महः |तां अंहसः पिपृहि पर्तृभिष तवं शतम पूर्विर यविष्ठ्य ||
ye rādhāṁsi dadaty aśvyā maghā kāmena śravaso mahaḥ | tām̐ aṁhasaḥ pipṛhi partṛbhiṣ ṭvaṁ śatam pūrbhir yaviṣṭhya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ye: those who
    • rādhāṁsi: the desires or aspirations
    • dadaty: give or bestow
    • aśvyā: with great speed or swiftness
    • maghā: wealth or abundance
    • kāmena: with desire or longing
    • śravaso: of fame or glory
    • mahaḥ: greatness or magnificence
    • tām: that
    • aṁhasaḥ: of the light or brilliance
    • pipṛhi: nourish or sustain
    • partṛbhiṣ: with the help of the ancestors
    • ṭvaṁ: you
    • śatam: a hundred
    • pūrbhir: from the past
    • yaviṣṭhya: having been established or set forth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the act of bestowing desires and aspirations with swiftness and abundance.
    • It emphasizes the importance of fame and greatness, suggesting that these qualities are nourished by the light of wisdom and the support of ancestors.
    • The phrase "you a hundred" indicates a multiplication of blessings or virtues, suggesting that the recipient is to be endowed with numerous gifts from the past.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when a mentor supports a mentee, they are bestowing knowledge and opportunities, akin to the desires mentioned in the mantra.
    • In family traditions, honoring ancestors during festivals can be seen as a way of nourishing the light of wisdom and greatness that they have passed down.
    • In personal development, setting goals and working towards them with the support of a community can lead to a sense of abundance and fulfillment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of desires, support, and the legacy of greatness.
    • It teaches us that our aspirations can be fulfilled through the guidance of those who came before us and through our own efforts.
    • In real life, it encourages us to seek support from our community and to honor our past while striving for our future goals.
