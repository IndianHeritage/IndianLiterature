महीर अस्य प्रणीतयः पूर्वीर उता प्रशस्तयः |नास्य क्षीयन्त ूतयः ||
mahīr asya praṇītayaḥ pūrvīr uta praśastayaḥ | nāsya kṣīyanta ūtayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mahīr: great, vast, or powerful
    • asya: of this
    • praṇītayaḥ: those who have been led or guided
    • pūrvīr: by the ancients or predecessors
    • uta: or
    • praśastayaḥ: those who are praised or honored
    • nāsya: of this (not)
    • kṣīyanta: diminish or decrease
    • ūtayaḥ: the benefits or rewards
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the greatness and vastness of a guiding force or principle that has been established by the ancients.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge passed down through generations, suggesting that those who follow this guidance will not see their benefits diminish.
    • The phrase "nāsya kṣīyanta ūtayaḥ" indicates that the rewards or benefits derived from this guidance are enduring and will not fade away.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the context of education, this mantra can be related to the importance of learning from the wisdom of past scholars and teachers. For instance, students who study classical literature or philosophy often find that the insights gained from these texts continue to be relevant and beneficial throughout their lives.
    • In a corporate setting, companies that adhere to the foundational principles established by their founders often thrive. For example, companies like Apple and Google have maintained their core values and vision, which have guided their success over the years.
    • In personal development, individuals who seek mentorship from experienced professionals often find that the lessons learned from these relationships provide lasting benefits, helping them navigate their careers effectively.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the value of wisdom and guidance from those who came before us. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and mentorship, ensuring that the benefits of such guidance are not only recognized but also preserved over time.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in education, business, or personal growth, the principles encapsulated in this mantra serve as a foundation for enduring success and fulfillment.
