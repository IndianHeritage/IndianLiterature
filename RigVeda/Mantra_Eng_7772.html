स सप्त धीतिभिर हितो नद्यो अजिन्वद अद्रुहः |या एकं अक्षि वावृधुः ||
sa sapta dhītibhir hito nadyo ajinvad adruhaḥ | yā ekam akṣi vāvṛdhuḥ ||
  • sa sapta dhītibhir: This phrase translates to "with seven thoughts" or "with seven insights." In the context of the Rigveda, the number seven often symbolizes completeness and perfection. It can be related to the seven chakras in the human body, which represent different aspects of consciousness and energy.
  • hito nadyo: This means "the beneficial rivers." Rivers in many cultures symbolize life, nourishment, and the flow of energy. In a real-life scenario, this can be likened to the importance of clean water sources for communities, agriculture, and overall health.
  • ajinvad adruhaḥ: This translates to "not causing harm." It emphasizes the importance of non-violence and peaceful coexistence. In today's world, this can be seen in movements advocating for peace, environmental protection, and social justice.
  • yā ekam akṣi vāvṛdhuḥ: This phrase means "that which has one eye." This can be interpreted as a metaphor for unity and focus. In practical terms, it can refer to the idea of having a singular vision or goal, such as a community working together towards a common purpose, like sustainability or education.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, beneficial actions, and the importance of insights in achieving harmony and peace. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as community development, environmental conservation, and personal growth. By fostering a mindset that values non-harm and collective effort, individuals and societies can work towards a more harmonious existence, reflecting the essence of this ancient wisdom.
