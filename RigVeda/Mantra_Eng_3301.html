वियद्वरांसी पर्वतस्य वृण्वे पयोभिर जिन्वे अपां जवादांसी |विदद गौरस्य गवयस्य गोहे यदी वाजाय सुध्यो वहन्ति ||
vi yad varāṁsi parvatasya vṛṇve payobhir jinve apāṁ javāṁsi | vidad gaurasya gavayasya gohe yadī vājāya sudhyo3 vahanti ||
  • Translation: "I choose the best of the mountains, the waters that flow swiftly, and the strength of the bull. May the excellent ones bring forth the best for us."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vi yad varāṁsi: "I choose the best" - This phrase emphasizes the act of selection, indicating a conscious choice of what is superior.
    • parvatasya: "of the mountains" - Mountains symbolize strength, stability, and endurance in many cultures.
    • vṛṇve: "I choose" - Again, this reinforces the idea of making a deliberate choice.
    • payobhir: "with waters" - Water represents life, nourishment, and purity.
    • jinve: "that flow swiftly" - This suggests the importance of speed and efficiency in achieving goals.
    • apāṁ: "of the waters" - A reiteration of the significance of water in life.
    • javāṁsi: "swift" - Highlights the need for agility and quickness in action.
    • vidad: "may bring forth" - A hopeful invocation for positive outcomes.
    • gaurasya: "of the bull" - The bull is often a symbol of strength and power.
    • gavayasya: "of the excellent" - Refers to the best qualities or attributes.
    • gohe: "in the abode" - Suggests a place of safety or comfort.
    • yadī: "if" - A conditional phrase that introduces possibilities.
    • vājāya: "for the excellent" - Again, emphasizes the pursuit of the best.
    • sudhyo: "of the pure" - Purity is often associated with clarity and truth.
    • vahanti: "they carry" - Indicates the act of bringing forth or delivering something valuable.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In decision-making, individuals often seek the best options available, whether in career choices, relationships, or investments. This mantra encourages a thoughtful selection process.
    • In environmental conservation, choosing the best practices for water management and mountain preservation reflects the essence of this mantra, highlighting the importance of natural resources.
    • In personal development, striving for excellence in one's skills and character can be seen as embodying the qualities of the "excellent ones" mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of making conscious choices in life, particularly in selecting the best resources and qualities that lead to success and fulfillment. It serves as a reminder to seek out strength, purity, and excellence in all endeavors. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as personal growth, environmental stewardship, and effective decision-making, ultimately guiding individuals towards a more meaningful and prosperous existence.
