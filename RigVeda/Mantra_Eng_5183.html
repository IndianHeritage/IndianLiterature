अयं कविरकविषु प्रचेताः Marteṣu अग्निः अमृतो न धायि |स मा नो अत्र जुहुरः सहस्वः सदा त्वे सुमनसः स्याम ||
ayaṁ kavir akaviṣu pracetā marteṣv agnir amṛto ni dhāyi | sa mā no atra juhuraḥ sahasvaḥ sadā tve sumanasaḥ syāma ||
  • ayaṁ kavir: This phrase translates to "this poet" or "this wise one." It signifies the presence of a knowledgeable and insightful being, often associated with divine inspiration.
  • akaviṣu: This term means "among the non-poets" or "those who lack insight." It highlights the distinction between the enlightened and the unenlightened, emphasizing the value of wisdom.
  • pracetā: This word refers to "the one who is perceptive" or "the one who has deep understanding." It suggests a person who is aware of the deeper truths of existence.
  • marteṣv: This translates to "among mortals." It indicates the context of human existence and the challenges faced in the material world.
  • agnir: Meaning "fire," this symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. Fire is often seen as a divine element that can both destroy and create.
  • amṛto: This means "immortal" or "eternal." It represents the idea of transcendence beyond the physical realm, suggesting a connection to the divine or the eternal truth.
  • ni dhāyi: This phrase means "may he place" or "may he bestow." It expresses a desire for the divine to grant blessings or wisdom.
  • sa mā no atra juhuraḥ: This translates to "may he grant us here." It is a plea for divine assistance and guidance in our endeavors.
  • sahasvaḥ: This word means "powerful" or "strong." It emphasizes the strength and capability of the divine or the wise one.
  • sadā tve: This means "always to you." It indicates a continuous devotion or connection to the divine.
  • sumanasaḥ: This translates to "with good thoughts" or "with a pure mind." It emphasizes the importance of maintaining a positive and clear mindset.
  • syāma: This means "may we become" or "may we be." It expresses a desire for transformation and growth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking wisdom and divine guidance in the midst of human existence. It highlights the importance of being perceptive and maintaining a pure mind while navigating life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during moments of decision-making, creative endeavors, or personal growth. By invoking the qualities of insight, strength, and purity of thought, individuals can aspire to transcend their limitations and connect with a higher purpose. This mantra serves as a reminder of the potential for transformation and the importance of seeking wisdom in our daily lives.
