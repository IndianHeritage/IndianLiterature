युवं धेनुं शयवे नाधितायापिन्वतम अश्विनां पूर्व्याय |अमुञ्चतं वर्तिकाम अंहसो निः प्रति जङ्घां विश्पलायाऽधत्तम ||
yuvaṁ dhenuṁ śayave nādhitāyāpinvatam aśvinā pūrvyāya | amuñcataṁ vartikām aṁhaso niḥ prati jaṅghāṁ viśpalāyā adhattam ||
  • Yuvaṁ: This word translates to "young" or "youthful." It signifies vitality, energy, and the potential for growth and development.
  • Dhenuṁ: This means "cow" or "nourishing." In Vedic culture, the cow is often seen as a symbol of abundance and sustenance, representing the nurturing aspect of nature.
  • Śayave: This term refers to "lying down" or "resting." It can symbolize a state of peace or tranquility, suggesting a moment of reflection or contemplation.
  • Nādhitāyā: This can be interpreted as "not having been harnessed" or "unrestrained." It implies freedom and the ability to explore one's potential without limitations.
  • Pinvatam: This word means "to be filled" or "to be endowed." It suggests a state of abundance and fulfillment, where one is enriched by experiences or resources.
  • Aśvinā: Referring to the twin deities, the Aśvins are associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between opposites.
  • Pūrvyāya: This means "to the east" or "towards the dawn." It signifies new beginnings, hope, and the promise of a new day.
  • Amuñcataṁ: This translates to "release" or "let go." It suggests the act of freeing oneself from burdens or constraints.
  • Vartikām: This word can mean "the one who is active" or "the one who moves." It implies dynamism and the importance of action in life.
  • Aṁhaso: This term refers to "strength" or "power." It emphasizes the importance of inner strength and resilience.
  • Niḥ: This means "without" or "free from." It suggests liberation from obstacles or challenges.
  • Prati: This translates to "towards" or "in the direction of." It indicates a movement or progression towards a goal.
  • Jaṅghāṁ: This word means "thigh" or "leg." It can symbolize support and stability, representing the foundation upon which one stands.
  • Viśpalāyā: This term refers to "the one who is expansive" or "the one who spreads." It signifies growth and the ability to reach outwards.
  • Adhattam: This means "to place" or "to establish." It suggests the act of grounding oneself and creating a solid foundation for future endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of youth, nourishment, freedom, and the importance of action and strength. It encourages individuals to embrace their potential, let go of limitations, and move towards new beginnings with vigor and resilience. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, career growth, and overcoming challenges. By embodying the principles of vitality and abundance, one can foster a mindset that is open to opportunities and capable of achieving greatness.
