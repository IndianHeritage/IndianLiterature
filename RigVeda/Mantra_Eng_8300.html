पावमानीर यो अध्येति ऋषिभिः संभृतं रसम् |तस्मै सरस्वती दुहे क्षीरं सर्पिर मधूदकम् ||
pāvamānīr yo adhyety ṛṣibhiḥ sambhṛtaṁ rasam | tasmai sarasvatī duhe kṣīraṁ sarpir madhūdakam ||
  • pāvamānīr: This term refers to something that is pure or sanctified. In a real-life context, it can be likened to the process of cleansing oneself, both physically and spiritually, before engaging in important tasks or rituals.
  • yo adhyety: This phrase translates to "who approaches" or "who seeks." It signifies the act of seeking knowledge or wisdom, much like a student approaching a teacher for guidance.
  • ṛṣibhiḥ: This word means "by the sages" or "by the seers." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge passed down through generations, akin to how modern education systems rely on the teachings of past scholars.
  • sambhṛtaṁ rasam: This translates to "the essence that is gathered." It can be understood as the collective knowledge or experiences that one accumulates over time, similar to how individuals gather insights from various life experiences.
  • tasmai: This means "to that" or "to him/her." It indicates a direction or focus towards a particular entity or concept, much like dedicating efforts towards a goal or aspiration.
  • sarasvatī: Sarasvatī is the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning. In real life, this can be seen in the reverence for education and the arts, where individuals seek inspiration and guidance from these domains.
  • duhe: This term means "to milk" or "to extract." It symbolizes the act of drawing out knowledge or wisdom, akin to how one might extract valuable lessons from experiences or teachings.
  • kṣīraṁ: This translates to "milk," which is often associated with nourishment and sustenance. In a broader sense, it represents the nurturing aspect of knowledge that sustains intellectual and spiritual growth.
  • sarpir: This word means "ghee" or "clarified butter," symbolizing purity and richness. It can be compared to the richness of knowledge that enhances one's life, much like how ghee is used in cooking to enhance flavors.
  • madhūdakam: This translates to "honeyed water," representing sweetness and the joy that comes from knowledge and wisdom. It signifies the delightful experience of learning and understanding.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking pure knowledge and wisdom, as represented by the goddess Sarasvatī. It highlights the process of gathering experiences and insights, akin to milking the essence of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in educational settings, personal development, and spiritual practices, where individuals strive to attain knowledge and wisdom for their growth and the betterment of society. By approaching learning with purity and dedication, one can extract the richness of life, leading to a fulfilling and enlightened existence.
