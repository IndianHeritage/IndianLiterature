इन्द्रं विश्वे सजोषसो देवासो दधिरे पुरः |इन्द्राय विश्वा सवनानि मानवशा रातानि सन्तु मानवशा ||
indraṁ viśve sajoṣaso devāso dadhire puraḥ | indrāya viśvā savanāni mānuṣā rātāni santu mānuṣā ||
  • Indraṁ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Viśve: Means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusiveness and the collective nature of existence.
  • Sajoṣaso: Translates to "with joy" or "with enthusiasm," suggesting a positive and harmonious approach.
  • Devāso: Refers to the gods or divine beings, emphasizing the spiritual aspect of the mantra.
  • Dadhi: Means "to hold" or "to support," indicating the act of nurturing or sustaining.
  • Puraḥ: Means "in front" or "before," suggesting a forward-looking perspective.
  • Indrāya: Again refers to Indra, reinforcing the focus on divine leadership and guidance.
  • Viśvā: Means "all" or "every," reiterating the theme of universality.
  • Savanāni: Refers to offerings or sacrifices, symbolizing the act of giving and sharing.
  • Mānuṣā: Means "human beings," highlighting the connection between the divine and humanity.
  • Rātāni: Translates to "joyful" or "happy," indicating a state of well-being and contentment.
  • Santu: Means "may they be" or "let them be," expressing a wish or blessing.

This mantra can be interpreted as a call for unity and collaboration among all beings, both divine and human. It emphasizes the importance of joy and enthusiasm in our endeavors, suggesting that when we come together with a positive spirit, we can achieve great things. The invocation of Indra signifies the need for strong leadership and guidance in our collective efforts.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Community Building: When people come together to work on community projects, the spirit of collaboration and joy can lead to successful outcomes.
  • Teamwork in Organizations: In a workplace, fostering a positive environment where everyone feels included and valued can enhance productivity and morale.
  • Spiritual Gatherings: During religious or spiritual events, invoking a sense of unity and joy can strengthen the bonds among participants.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for collective action, joy, and divine guidance. It serves as a reminder that when we unite with a positive spirit, we can create a harmonious and prosperous environment for all. This principle is applicable in various aspects of life, from personal relationships to community initiatives, emphasizing the power of collaboration and shared purpose.
