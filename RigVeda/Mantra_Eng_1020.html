पुनः-पुनर जातमाना पुराणी समानं वर्णम् अभि शुम्भमाना |श्वघ्निव कृत्नुर विज आमिनाना मर्तस्य देवी जारयन्त्य आयुः ||
punaḥ-punar jāyamānā purāṇī samānaṁ varṇam abhi śumbhamānā | śvaghnīva kṛtnur vija āminānā martasya devī jarayanty āyuḥ ||
  • punaḥ-punar: This term translates to "again and again" or "repeatedly." It signifies the cyclical nature of life and existence.
  • jāyamānā: This means "being born" or "coming into existence." It reflects the process of birth and rebirth, a fundamental concept in many philosophies.
  • purāṇī: This word means "ancient" or "old." It suggests a connection to traditions and wisdom that have stood the test of time.
  • samānaṁ: This translates to "similar" or "equal." It indicates a sense of uniformity or sameness in the cycle of life.
  • varṇam: This means "color" or "quality." In this context, it can refer to the characteristics or attributes of life.
  • abhi: This term means "towards" or "in the direction of." It indicates movement or progression.
  • śumbhamānā: This translates to "being praised" or "being honored." It reflects the recognition of the divine or the sacred in life.
  • śvaghnīva: This term refers to "like a dog." In a metaphorical sense, it can imply loyalty or the instinctual nature of beings.
  • kṛtnur: This means "the creator" or "the maker." It signifies the divine force that brings life into existence.
  • vija: This translates to "seed" or "origin." It represents the source from which life emerges.
  • āminānā: This means "those who are born." It emphasizes the continuity of life and existence.
  • martasya: This term means "of mortals" or "of human beings." It highlights the human experience within the cycle of life.
  • devī: This translates to "goddess" or "divine feminine." It represents the nurturing and sustaining aspects of life.
  • jarayanty: This means "to bring forth" or "to generate." It signifies the act of creation and the nurturing of life.
  • āyuḥ: This translates to "life" or "longevity." It reflects the essence of existence and the desire for a fulfilling life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the cyclical nature of life, emphasizing the continuous process of birth, existence, and the nurturing force that sustains it. It highlights the importance of recognizing the divine in our lives and the interconnectedness of all beings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to understand the importance of traditions, the value of life experiences, and the need for nurturing relationships. It serves as a reminder to honor the cycles of life and to appreciate the wisdom that comes from ancient teachings, encouraging us to live with awareness and gratitude.
