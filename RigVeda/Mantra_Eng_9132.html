यस्य मां हरितो रथे तिस्रो वहन्ति साधुया |स्तवै सहस्रदक्षिणे ||
yasya mā harito rathe tisro vahanti sādhuyā | stavai sahasradakṣiṇe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • mā: not
    • harito: the green (or auspicious)
    • rathe: in the chariot
    • tisro: three
    • vahanti: carry or lead
    • sādhuyā: with the righteous or virtuous
    • stavai: by praise or worship
    • sahasradakṣiṇe: with a thousand eyes (or in a thousand ways)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence or force that is not only auspicious but also leads the righteous in their endeavors.
    • The "chariot" symbolizes the journey of life, where the "three" could represent various aspects such as the mind, body, and spirit, or the past, present, and future.
    • The phrase "with a thousand eyes" suggests omnipresence and the ability to perceive all aspects of existence, guiding the righteous in their actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of righteousness and virtue can guide their team effectively, ensuring that they navigate challenges with wisdom and foresight.
    • In personal life, individuals who practice mindfulness and ethical living often find themselves supported by their community, akin to being carried by the "three" aspects of their being.
    • In spiritual practices, the idea of being guided by a higher power can provide comfort and direction, much like the "thousand eyes" watching over one's journey.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of righteousness and virtue in our lives, suggesting that those who embody these qualities will be supported and guided through their journeys.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that we are not alone in our endeavors; there is a divine presence that aids us when we act with integrity.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to lead with ethical principles, fostering a sense of community and support in both personal and professional realms.
