को अद्धा वेद क इह प्र वocat कुत āजातā कुत इयं विसृष्टिः |अर्वाग देवाऽस्य विसर्जनेनाथा को वेद यत आबभूव ||
ko addhā veda ka iha pra vocat kuta ājātā kuta iyaṁ visṛṣṭiḥ | arvāg devā asya visarjanenāthā ko veda yata ābabhūva ||
  • Mantra: "ko addhā veda ka iha pra vocat kuta ājātā kuta iyaṁ visṛṣṭiḥ | arvāg devā asya visarjanenāthā ko veda yata ābabhūva ||"
  • Translation: "Who really knows? Who can declare it here? From where was this creation born? The gods came afterwards, with this creation. Who knows from where it has arisen?"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ko: Who
    • addhā: really
    • veda: knows
    • ka: who
    • iha: here
    • pra vocat: can declare
    • kuta: from where
    • ājātā: was born
    • kuta: from where
    • iyaṁ: this
    • visṛṣṭiḥ: creation
    • arvāg: afterwards
    • devā: gods
    • asya: this
    • visarjanenā: with this creation
    • athā: therefore
    • ko: who
    • veda: knows
    • yata: from where
    • ābabhūva: has arisen
  • Significance:
    • This mantra reflects the profound philosophical inquiry into the origins of the universe and existence.
    • It emphasizes the limitations of human knowledge and the mystery surrounding creation.
    • In real life, this can be related to scientific exploration and the quest for understanding the universe, such as the Big Bang theory, which seeks to explain the origins of the cosmos.
    • It also resonates with existential questions faced by individuals, prompting reflection on one's purpose and the nature of existence.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In moments of uncertainty or existential crisis, this mantra can serve as a reminder that not all questions have clear answers, encouraging acceptance of the unknown.
    • In scientific fields, it can inspire researchers to continue exploring the mysteries of life and the universe, acknowledging that knowledge is ever-evolving.
    • In philosophical discussions, it can lead to deeper conversations about belief, creation, and the nature of reality.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra invites contemplation on the origins of existence and the limits of human understanding.
    • It encourages humility in the face of the vast unknown and inspires a continuous quest for knowledge.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a bridge between spirituality and science, highlighting the shared human experience of seeking answers to life's greatest mysteries.
