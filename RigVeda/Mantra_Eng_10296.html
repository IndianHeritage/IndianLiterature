मन्त्र: उता देवाः अवहितं देवाः उन्नयथा पुनः | उतागश्चक्रुषं देवाः देवाः जीवयथा पुनः ||
uta devā avahitaṁ devā un nayathā punaḥ | utāgaś cakruṣaṁ devā devā jīvayathā punaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and
    • devā: the gods
    • avahitaṁ: have brought forth
    • un: not
    • nayathā: as you lead
    • punaḥ: again
    • utāgaś: and they have made
    • cakruṣaṁ: the creation
    • devā: the gods
    • jīvayathā: may you live
    • punaḥ: again
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the role of the gods in creation and sustenance.
    • It emphasizes the idea that the divine forces are responsible for bringing forth life and guiding it.
    • The repetition of "punaḥ" (again) suggests a cyclical nature of existence, where life is continuously renewed.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we see cycles of life and death, such as the changing of seasons, which reflect the idea of renewal and guidance by higher powers.
    • In human life, the birth of a child can be seen as a divine creation, where parents often feel a sense of guidance and support from a higher power.
    • In various cultures, rituals and prayers are performed to honor the gods for their role in sustaining life, reflecting the belief in divine intervention.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine forces in the cycle of life, creation, and sustenance.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize and appreciate the guidance and support they receive from higher powers in their lives.
    • In practical terms, this can inspire a sense of gratitude and mindfulness, prompting individuals to live harmoniously with nature and acknowledge the interconnectedness of all life.
