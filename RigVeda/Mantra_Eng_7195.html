मित्रो नो अत्यंहतिं वरुणः पर्षद् आर्यमाः |आदित्यासो यथा विदुः ||
mitro no aty aṁhatiṁ varuṇaḥ parṣad aryamā | ādityāso yathā viduḥ ||
  • Mantra: "mitro no aty aṁhatiṁ varuṇaḥ parṣad aryamā | ādityāso yathā viduḥ ||"
  • Translation: "May Varuna, the friend, protect us from all harm, as the Adityas know."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Mitro: Friend; signifies companionship and support.
    • No: Us; indicates a collective appeal for protection.
    • Aty aṁhatiṁ: From all harm; emphasizes the desire for safety and security.
    • Varuṇaḥ: Varuna; the deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority.
    • Parṣad: Assembly or council; refers to the divine assembly or the collective of deities.
    • Aryamā: A deity associated with the sun and truth; represents enlightenment and guidance.
    • Ādityāso: The Adityas; a group of solar deities representing various aspects of life and nature.
    • Yathā: As; indicates a comparison or manner.
    • Viduḥ: Know; signifies knowledge and understanding.
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Varuna, a deity who embodies the principles of truth and cosmic order, asking for protection from harm.
    • The mention of "friends" highlights the importance of community and support in overcoming challenges.
    • The reference to the Adityas suggests that just as these solar deities possess knowledge and wisdom, so too can we seek guidance in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often turn to friends and family for support, mirroring the appeal for divine friendship in the mantra.
    • In a workplace setting, teamwork and collaboration can be seen as a modern reflection of the divine assembly, where collective efforts lead to success.
    • Seeking knowledge and wisdom from mentors or elders can be likened to the invocation of the Adityas, guiding us through life's complexities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of friendship, community, and divine guidance in navigating life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our struggles and that seeking support and wisdom is essential for personal growth.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, professional environments, or spiritual journeys, the principles encapsulated in this mantra can lead to a more harmonious and fulfilling existence.
