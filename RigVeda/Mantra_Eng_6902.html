आयम् अग्ने त्वे अपि जरीता भूत्व सन्त्याः |तस्मै पावक मृळय ||
ayam agne tve api jaritā bhūtu santya | tasmai pāvaka mṛḻaya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ayam - this
    • agne - O Agni (the fire god)
    • tve - to you
    • api - also
    • jaritā - who is the one who brings forth
    • bhūtu - existence or being
    • santya - truth or purity
    • tasmai - to that
    • pāvaka - O purifier
    • mṛḻaya - may you be gracious or may you be appeased
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, acknowledging his role in creation and existence.
    • It emphasizes the importance of purity and truth, seeking Agni's grace and favor.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is seen as a symbol of transformation and purification. For instance, in Hindu rituals, fire is used in ceremonies to purify offerings and invoke blessings.
    • In a personal context, one might reflect on the mantra when seeking clarity or truth in a difficult situation, invoking the idea of Agni as a source of illumination and guidance.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking purity and truth in our lives.
    • It can be applied in various scenarios, such as during personal reflection, meditation, or rituals where one seeks to cleanse their mind and spirit.
    • In a broader sense, it serves as a reminder of the importance of integrity and authenticity in our actions and thoughts.
