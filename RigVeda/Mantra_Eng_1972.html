त्वष्टा रूपाणि हि प्रभुः पशून विश्वान समानजे |तेषां नः sphātim ā यज ||
tvaṣṭā rūpāṇi hi prabhuḥ paśūn viśvān samānaje | teṣāṁ naḥ sphātim ā yaja ||
  • tvaṣṭā: This word refers to the divine craftsman or the creator, often associated with the deity Tvashtar in Vedic literature. Tvashtar is known for shaping and creating various forms in the universe.
  • rūpāṇi: This translates to "forms" or "shapes." It signifies the various manifestations of life and existence that the creator brings into being.
  • hi: This is an emphatic particle, often used to emphasize the preceding word or statement.
  • prabhuḥ: Meaning "lord" or "master," this term denotes a higher power or authority, reinforcing the idea of Tvashtar as a supreme creator.
  • paśūn: This word means "animals" or "creatures." It highlights the living beings that inhabit the earth, which are part of the creator's design.
  • viśvān: This translates to "all" or "universal," indicating the inclusiveness of the creator's work across all forms of life.
  • samānaje: This term means "in the same manner" or "equally," suggesting that all creations are made with equal care and intention.
  • teṣāṁ: This means "of them," referring back to the creations or beings mentioned earlier.
  • naḥ: This translates to "our," indicating a collective ownership or connection to the creations.
  • sphātim: This word means "prosperity" or "abundance," suggesting a desire for well-being and flourishing.
  • ā: This is a preposition that can mean "towards" or "for," indicating direction or purpose.
  • yaja: This means "to worship" or "to offer," signifying an act of devotion or reverence towards the creator.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Tvashtar, the divine craftsman, asking for the prosperity and well-being of all living beings created in various forms. It acknowledges the creator's role in shaping life and expresses a desire for abundance and harmony among all creatures.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Environmental Awareness: Recognizing the interconnectedness of all living beings encourages us to protect and preserve the environment. Just as Tvashtar creates diverse forms of life, we must ensure that these forms thrive.
  • Animal Welfare: The mention of "paśūn" highlights the importance of caring for animals. This mantra can inspire actions towards animal rights and welfare, promoting compassion and respect for all creatures.
  • Community and Unity: The idea of "viśvān samānaje" emphasizes equality among all beings. In a societal context, this can foster a sense of community, encouraging inclusivity and mutual respect.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine craftsmanship behind creation and the importance of nurturing and respecting all forms of life. It encourages us to seek prosperity not just for ourselves but for all beings, fostering a harmonious existence in our communities and the world at large.
