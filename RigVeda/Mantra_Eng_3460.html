ये देवासो अभवतā सुखृत्या श्येनā इवेद अधि दिवि निषेदः |ते रत्नं धात शवसो नपातः सौधन्वना अभवतामृतासः ||
ye devāso abhavatā sukṛtyā śyenā ived adhi divi niṣeda | te ratnaṁ dhāta śavaso napātaḥ saudhanvanā abhavatāmṛtāsaḥ ||
  • Translation: "Those divine beings who have come into existence through good deeds, like the swift falcon, reside in the heavens. They, the bearers of wealth, have become immortal."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: those
    • devāso: divine beings or gods
    • abhavatā: who have come into existence
    • sukṛtyā: through good deeds
    • śyenā: like the falcon
    • ived: reside or dwell
    • adhi: in or upon
    • divi: the heavens
    • niṣeda: to settle or reside
    • te: they
    • ratnaṁ: wealth or precious things
    • dhāta: bearers or holders
    • śavaso: of the living or vital forces
    • napātaḥ: descendants or progeny
    • saudhanvanā: those who possess wealth
    • abhavatāmṛtāsaḥ: have become immortal
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of good deeds (sukṛtyā) in achieving a divine or elevated state of existence.
    • The comparison to a falcon (śyenā) suggests swiftness, agility, and a higher perspective, indicating that those who perform good deeds can rise above ordinary existence.
    • The phrase "reside in the heavens" (adhi divi niṣeda) implies a state of spiritual elevation and connection with the divine.
    • The mantra also speaks of wealth (ratnaṁ) and immortality (abhavatāmṛtāsaḥ), suggesting that true wealth comes from virtuous living and that such beings transcend the limitations of mortal life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Individuals who engage in charitable acts, such as donating to the needy or volunteering, often find a sense of fulfillment and purpose, akin to the divine beings mentioned in the mantra.
    • In a corporate setting, companies that prioritize ethical practices and corporate social responsibility often achieve greater success and respect, reflecting the idea of good deeds leading to prosperity.
    • People who practice mindfulness and compassion in their daily lives often experience a sense of peace and connection with others, embodying the qualities of the divine beings described.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of good deeds in our lives. It encourages individuals to act virtuously, suggesting that such actions lead to spiritual elevation and a form of immortality through legacy.
    • In real life, the principles outlined in this mantra can be applied in various contexts, from personal relationships to professional environments, promoting a culture of kindness, integrity, and respect.
    • Ultimately, the mantra highlights that true wealth is not merely material but is deeply rooted in our actions and the positive impact we have on the world around us.
