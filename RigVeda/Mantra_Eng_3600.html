अभूद देवः सविता वन्द्यो नु न इदानीं अह्न उपवाच्यो नृभिः |विः यो रत्ना भजति मानवेष्यः श्रेष्ठं नो अत्र द्रविणं यथा दधत ||
abhūd devaḥ savitā vandyo nu na idānīm ahna upavācyo nṛbhiḥ | vi yo ratnā bhajati mānavebhyaḥ śreṣṭhaṁ no atra draviṇaṁ yathā dadhat ||
  • abhūd devaḥ savitā: This phrase refers to the divine being Savitā, who is often associated with the sun and is considered a source of life and energy. In real life, we can relate this to the importance of the sun in agriculture, as it provides the necessary light and warmth for crops to grow.
  • vandyo nu na idānīm: This translates to "worthy of worship, even now." It emphasizes the timeless nature of reverence towards the divine. In contemporary life, this can be seen in how people continue to seek spiritual guidance and express gratitude, regardless of the era or circumstances.
  • ahna upavācyo nṛbhiḥ: This means "to be spoken of by men." It suggests that the divine should be acknowledged and praised by humanity. In everyday life, this can manifest in community gatherings, prayers, or rituals where people come together to honor their beliefs and values.
  • vi yo ratnā bhajati: This phrase translates to "who bestows treasures." It signifies the blessings and abundance that come from the divine. In practical terms, this can be related to the idea of hard work and dedication leading to success and prosperity, akin to the rewards one reaps from their efforts.
  • mānavebhyaḥ śreṣṭhaṁ no atra draviṇaṁ: This means "the best for mankind, not just material wealth." It highlights the importance of spiritual and moral wealth over mere material possessions. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of knowledge, compassion, and ethical living, which ultimately enriches one's life beyond financial success.
  • yathā dadhat: This translates to "as they bestow." It implies that the divine gives according to one's actions and intentions. In a real-world context, this can be understood as the principle of karma, where one's deeds influence their future experiences and outcomes.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of reverence towards the divine, the importance of spiritual wealth, and the acknowledgment of the blessings bestowed upon humanity. Its significance lies in reminding individuals to seek a balance between material and spiritual pursuits, fostering a sense of community and gratitude. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages people to engage in practices that honor their beliefs, contribute positively to society, and strive for personal growth and ethical living. By embodying these principles, individuals can cultivate a fulfilling and meaningful life, enriched by both spiritual and material abundance.
