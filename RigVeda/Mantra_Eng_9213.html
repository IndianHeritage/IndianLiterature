युवाम मृगेव वारणा मृगाण्यवो दोषा वस्तोर हविषा न ह्वयामहे |युवं होत्राम ऋतुथा जुह्वते नरेषं जनाय वहतः शुभस पती ||
yuvām mṛgeva vāraṇā mṛgaṇyavo doṣā vastor haviṣā ni hvayāmahe | yuvaṁ hotrām ṛtuthā juhvate nareṣaṁ janāya vahathaḥ śubhas patī ||
  • yuvām - This word translates to "you two," indicating a duality or partnership.
  • mṛgeva - Meaning "like a deer," it symbolizes grace, agility, and the natural world.
  • vāraṇā - Refers to "the elephants," representing strength and wisdom.
  • mṛgaṇyavo - This term can be interpreted as "the animals," emphasizing the connection to nature.
  • doṣā - Translates to "faults" or "defects," suggesting the imperfections in life.
  • vastor - Meaning "of the substance," it refers to the essence of things.
  • haviṣā - This word means "with offerings," indicating a sense of gratitude and giving.
  • ni hvayāmahe - Translates to "we invoke," suggesting a call to action or a plea for assistance.
  • yuvaṁ - Again, "you two," reinforcing the idea of partnership.
  • hotrām - Refers to "the priest" or "the one who offers," symbolizing guidance and leadership.
  • ṛtuthā - Meaning "in the right way," it emphasizes righteousness and truth.
  • juhvate - Translates to "they offer," indicating the act of giving and sharing.
  • nareṣaṁ - Meaning "to the man," it signifies humanity and the importance of human connection.
  • janāya - Refers to "for the people," highlighting community and collective well-being.
  • vahathaḥ - Meaning "you carry," suggesting support and nurturing.
  • śubhas patī - Translates to "the lord of auspiciousness," representing positivity and good fortune.

This mantra emphasizes the importance of partnership, community, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It invokes the qualities of grace, strength, and righteousness, encouraging individuals to recognize their faults and strive for improvement. The imagery of deer and elephants symbolizes the balance between agility and strength, which can be applied in real-life scenarios such as teamwork in a workplace or collaboration in community projects.


In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of unity and support among individuals. It encourages us to invoke positivity and auspiciousness in our lives and the lives of others, fostering a sense of community and shared responsibility. By embodying these principles, we can create a harmonious environment that benefits everyone.
