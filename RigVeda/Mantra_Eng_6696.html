मन्त्र: उता नः पितुम् आ भर संरराणो अविक्षितम् |मघवन भूरी ते वसु ||
uta naḥ pitum ā bhara saṁrarāṇo avikṣitam | maghavan bhūri te vasu ||
  • Mantra: "uta naḥ pitum ā bhara saṁrarāṇo avikṣitam | maghavan bhūri te vasu ||"
  • Translation: "O Maghavan, may you bring us abundant wealth, unimpeded by obstacles, as we call upon our father."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: O, an invocation or call.
    • naḥ: our, indicating possession or belonging.
    • pitum: father, symbolizing guidance and support.
    • ā bhara: may you bring or bestow, a request for abundance.
    • saṁrarāṇo: wealth or resources, often associated with prosperity.
    • avikṣitam: unimpeded or unobstructed, indicating a smooth path to success.
    • maghavan: a name for the deity associated with wealth and abundance.
    • bhūri: abundant or plentiful, emphasizing the desire for richness.
    • te: your, referring to the deity's possessions or blessings.
    • vasu: wealth or good fortune, often linked to material and spiritual prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be invoked during a meeting or negotiation to seek success and prosperity in ventures.
    • During family gatherings or rituals, individuals may recite this mantra to bless their household with abundance and harmony.
    • In personal development, one might meditate on this mantra to focus on removing obstacles in their path to achieving goals.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation for wealth and prosperity, both materially and spiritually. It emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance and support from one's roots (symbolized by the father) while striving for success. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as business, family, and personal growth, serving as a reminder to pursue abundance with clarity and purpose, free from hindrances. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset focused on attracting positive outcomes and overcoming challenges in their lives.
