स वावशान इह पाहि सोमं मरुद्भिर इन्द्र सकिभिः सुतं नः |जातं यत त्वा परि देवाः अभूषण महे भराय पुरुहूत विश्वे ||
sa vāvaśāna iha pāhi somam marudbhir indra sakhibhiḥ sutaṁ naḥ | jātaṁ yat tvā pari devā abhūṣan mahe bharāya puruhūta viśve ||
  • sa vāvaśāna iha pāhi somam: This phrase can be translated as "May you, O Indra, protect the soma here." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing vitality and divine inspiration. In real life, this can be likened to seeking protection and nourishment from sources that uplift and inspire us, such as knowledge, relationships, or spiritual practices.
  • marudbhir indra sakhibhiḥ sutaṁ naḥ: This translates to "With the Maruts, O Indra, may you grant us your son." The Maruts are storm deities, representing the forces of nature. This line emphasizes the importance of community and collaboration in achieving goals. In everyday life, it can be seen in teamwork, where collective efforts lead to success.
  • jātaṁ yat tvā pari devā abhūṣan: This means "Whatever has been born, the gods have adorned you." It signifies the idea that all creations are blessed by the divine. In practical terms, this can be related to appreciating the beauty and uniqueness of every individual and creation around us, recognizing that everyone has their own divine spark.
  • mahe bharāya puruhūta viśve: This translates to "O Puruhuta, may you grant us great strength." Puruhuta is another name for Indra, symbolizing the provider of strength and abundance. In real life, this can be interpreted as a call for resilience and support in times of difficulty, encouraging individuals to seek strength from their surroundings, be it through family, friends, or community.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine protection, strength, and community support. It highlights the interconnectedness of life and the importance of divine blessings in our endeavors.
  • Real-life Application: The teachings of this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as in personal growth, community building, and spiritual practices. It encourages individuals to seek inspiration and support from both the divine and their community, fostering a sense of unity and strength.
