को अस्य शुष्मं तविषीं वरात एको धनाऽभरते अप्रतीतः |इमे चिद अस्य ज्रयसो नु देवी इन्द्रस्यौजसो भियसां जिहाते ||
ko asya śuṣmaṁ taviṣīṁ varāta eko dhanā bharate apratītaḥ | ime cid asya jrayaso nu devī indrasyaujaso bhiyasā jihāte ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ko: Who
    • asya: of this
    • śuṣmaṁ: subtle or fine
    • taviṣīṁ: powerful or strong
    • varāta: from the best or the most excellent
    • eko: one
    • dhanā: wealth or riches
    • bharate: in the world or in the earth
    • apratītaḥ: unknown or unrecognized
    • ime: these
    • cid: indeed
    • asya: of this
    • jrayaso: of the aged or the old
    • nu: indeed
    • devī: goddess or divine power
    • indrasy: of Indra (the king of gods)
    • aujaso: strength or energy
    • bhiyasā: with fear or with respect
    • jihāte: strives or seeks
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra begins with a question, "Who is this subtle and powerful one?" It reflects a quest for understanding the essence of strength and wealth in the universe.
    • The phrase "one wealth" suggests that true wealth is singular and transcends material possessions, hinting at spiritual or intellectual riches.
    • The term "unknown" indicates that often, the most valuable aspects of life are not recognized or appreciated by many.
    • The reference to "goddess" and "Indra" signifies the divine forces that govern strength and prosperity, emphasizing the need for reverence towards these powers.
    • The mantra concludes with the idea that these divine energies strive for recognition and respect, suggesting that acknowledging them can lead to greater strength and prosperity in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, the mantra can be applied to the pursuit of innovation. Companies often overlook the subtle yet powerful ideas that can lead to significant breakthroughs.
    • In personal development, individuals may not recognize their inner strengths or talents, which can be likened to the "unknown wealth" mentioned in the mantra.
    • In relationships, acknowledging and respecting the contributions of others can lead to stronger bonds, similar to how the divine energies strive for recognition.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and respecting the subtle yet powerful forces in our lives, whether they be spiritual, intellectual, or relational.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that true wealth is often unrecognized and that by striving to understand and appreciate these forces, we can enhance our own strength and prosperity.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the wisdom of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and greater success in various endeavors.
