तं मार्मृजानं महिषं न सानाव अंशुं दूहन्त्य उक्षणं गिरिष्ठाम् |तं वावशानं मतयः साचन्ते तृतो बिभर्ति वरुणं समुद्रे ||
tam marmṛjānam mahiṣaṁ na sānāv aṁśuṁ duhanty ukṣaṇaṁ giriṣṭhām | taṁ vāvaśānam matayaḥ sacante trito bibharti varuṇaṁ samudre ||
  • tam marmṛjānam: This phrase can be translated as "that which is the essence of life" or "the vital force." In real life, this can be related to the concept of energy or the driving force behind our actions and decisions.
  • mahiṣaṁ: This word means "the great one" or "the powerful." It can refer to a leader or a significant figure in society. For example, a CEO of a company or a political leader who influences many lives.
  • na sānāv aṁśuṁ: This translates to "not a part of the sun." It suggests something that is not merely a fragment but rather something whole and complete. In life, this can be seen in the pursuit of holistic well-being, where one seeks to integrate all aspects of life—physical, mental, and spiritual.
  • duhanty ukṣaṇaṁ: This means "they churn the essence." This can be likened to the process of extracting the best from a situation or experience, similar to how one might extract juice from fruits or knowledge from experiences.
  • giriṣṭhām: This word means "the highest" or "the supreme." It can refer to the ultimate goal or aspiration in life, such as achieving enlightenment or reaching one's full potential.
  • taṁ vāvaśānam: This translates to "that which is the source of all." It signifies the origin or the root of existence, akin to the idea of a universal consciousness or the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • matayaḥ sacante: This means "the thoughts that are united." It emphasizes the importance of collective thinking and collaboration in achieving goals, much like teamwork in a project or community efforts in social causes.
  • trito bibharti: This translates to "the third one bears." It can refer to the idea of balance, where the third element harmonizes the first two, similar to how a mediator resolves conflicts between two parties.
  • varuṇaṁ samudre: This means "Varuna in the ocean." Varuna is often associated with the cosmic order and the moral law. The ocean symbolizes vastness and depth, representing the unknown and the subconscious. This can be related to the exploration of one's inner self and the moral dilemmas faced in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, power, and the pursuit of higher understanding. It emphasizes the importance of unity in thought and action, the quest for holistic well-being, and the exploration of the deeper aspects of existence. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as leadership, teamwork, personal growth, and ethical decision-making. It serves as a reminder to seek balance and harmony in our lives while striving for our highest aspirations.
