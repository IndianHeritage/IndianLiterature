सं त्रि पवित्रा विततान्येष्य अन्व एकं धावसि पूयमानः |असि भगो असि दात्रस्य दातासि मगवा मगवद्भ्य इन्दो ||
saṁ trī pavitrā vitatāny eṣy anv ekaṁ dhāvasi pūyamānaḥ | asi bhago asi dātrasya dātāsi maghavā maghavadbhya indo ||
  • saṁ trī pavitrā vitatāny eṣy: This phrase can be translated as "the three pure (or sacred) things will come together." The number three often symbolizes completeness and harmony in various cultures. In real life, this can be seen in the concept of the mind, body, and spirit working in unison for holistic well-being.
  • anv ekaṁ dhāvasi pūyamānaḥ: This translates to "you run towards one, being filled with abundance." This can be interpreted as the pursuit of a singular goal or purpose in life, while being enriched by the experiences and resources along the way. For example, a student may focus on their education (the one goal) while gaining knowledge and skills (the abundance) that will serve them in their future career.
  • asi bhago asi dātrasya: This means "you are the wealth, you are the giver." It emphasizes the idea that true wealth is not just material but also includes the ability to give and share with others. In a real-life context, this can be seen in charitable acts where individuals or organizations use their resources to uplift others, thereby creating a cycle of giving and receiving.
  • dātāsi maghavā maghavadbhya indo: This translates to "you are the giver of great wealth, O Indra." Indra, in Vedic literature, is often associated with strength and abundance. This line signifies the importance of generosity and the role of divine or higher powers in providing for humanity. In practical terms, this can be reflected in community leaders who work to provide resources and support for those in need, embodying the spirit of Indra.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of unity, purpose, generosity, and the divine provision of abundance. Its significance lies in the reminder that true wealth is multifaceted, encompassing not just material possessions but also the richness of experiences, relationships, and the ability to give back to the community. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with a sense of purpose while remaining open to the abundance that life offers, and to recognize the importance of sharing their wealth—be it knowledge, resources, or kindness—with others. This holistic approach can lead to a more fulfilling and interconnected life.
