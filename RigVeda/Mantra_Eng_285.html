सं नु वोचावहै पुनर यतो मे मध्वाभृतम् |होतेव क्षदसे प्रियम् ||
saṁ nu vocāvahai punar yato me madhv ābhṛtam | hoteva kṣadase priyam ||
  • saṁ nu vocāvahai: This phrase can be translated as "May we speak together" or "Let us communicate." It emphasizes the importance of dialogue and collective expression.
  • punar: This word means "again" or "once more," suggesting a return to a previous state or a renewal of communication.
  • yato me: Translated as "from where I," this phrase indicates a personal connection or source of inspiration.
  • madhv ābhṛtam: This can be interpreted as "sweetness" or "delight," referring to the pleasantness of communication and relationships.
  • hoteva: This term means "like a priest" or "as a priest would," indicating a role of guidance, leadership, or facilitation in communication.
  • kṣadase: This word translates to "to the one who is dear" or "to the beloved," highlighting the importance of affection and connection in relationships.
  • priyam: Meaning "dear" or "beloved," it reinforces the theme of love and cherished relationships.

The mantra as a whole can be understood as a call for harmonious communication and the renewal of relationships, emphasizing the sweetness and affection that should characterize our interactions. It suggests that, like a priest who facilitates sacred rituals, we should strive to create an environment where dialogue is cherished and relationships are nurtured.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In Personal Relationships: It encourages open communication between partners, friends, and family members. For example, during conflicts, returning to a place of understanding and expressing feelings can strengthen bonds.
  • In Professional Settings: In workplaces, fostering a culture of dialogue can lead to better teamwork and collaboration. Regular meetings where team members can express their thoughts and ideas can enhance productivity and morale.
  • In Community Engagement: This mantra can inspire community leaders to promote dialogue among diverse groups, ensuring that everyone feels heard and valued, which is essential for social harmony.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra highlights the significance of communication, affection, and renewal in relationships. It serves as a reminder that through open dialogue and mutual respect, we can create deeper connections and a more harmonious society. By embodying these principles in our daily lives, we can foster environments where love and understanding thrive.
