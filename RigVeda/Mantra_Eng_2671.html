वृषणं त्वा वयं वृषन् वृषणः सम इधीमहि |अग्नि दीद्यतं बृहत् ||
vṛṣaṇaṁ tvā vayaṁ vṛṣan vṛṣaṇaḥ sam idhīmahi | agne dīdyatam bṛhat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vṛṣaṇaṁ: The one who is strong or powerful.
    • tvā: You (referring to Agni, the fire god).
    • vayaṁ: We.
    • vṛṣan: The one who is capable or the strong one.
    • vṛṣaṇaḥ: The strong one (repeated for emphasis).
    • sam: Together or in unison.
    • idhīmahi: We meditate or contemplate.
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god).
    • dīdyatam: Shine or illuminate.
    • bṛhat: Great or vast.
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, expressing a collective intention to meditate on his greatness and power. It emphasizes the strength and illumination that Agni brings to the world and to the lives of those who worship him.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is seen as a source of strength and transformation. For instance, in a campfire setting, people gather around the fire for warmth, light, and community, reflecting the essence of Agni as a unifying force.
    • In modern times, the mantra can be applied in motivational contexts, where individuals or teams come together to harness their collective strength and illuminate their paths towards success, much like how Agni is invoked for guidance and power.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity and collective strength in achieving goals. It reminds us that when we come together with a common purpose, we can harness greater power and illumination in our lives.
    • In practical scenarios, this can be applied in team settings, community gatherings, or even family discussions where the focus is on collaboration and shared aspirations. The invocation of Agni serves as a reminder to seek guidance and strength from a higher source while working together.
