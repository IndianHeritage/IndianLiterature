इमां प्रात्नाय सुष्टुतिं नवीयसीं वोचेम अस्मा उषते श्रिणोतु नः |भूया अन्तराहृद्य अस्य निस्पृशे जायेव पत्या उषती सुवासा: ||
imām pratnāya suṣṭutiṁ navīyasīṁ voceyam asmā uśate śṛṇotu naḥ | bhūyā antarā hṛdy asya nispṛśe jāyeva patya uśatī suvāsāḥ ||
  • imām pratnāya: This phrase can be translated as "this prayer" or "this invocation." It signifies the act of calling upon a higher power or divine presence.
  • suṣṭutiṁ: This word means "well-structured praise" or "excellent commendation." It emphasizes the importance of expressing gratitude and reverence in a meaningful way.
  • navīyasīṁ: This term refers to "new" or "fresh." It suggests the idea of renewal and the continuous cycle of life and spirituality.
  • voceyam: This means "I speak" or "I declare." It indicates the speaker's intention to vocalize their thoughts and prayers.
  • asmā: This translates to "to us" or "for us," highlighting the communal aspect of the prayer.
  • uśate: This word means "may it shine" or "may it illuminate." It expresses a desire for enlightenment and clarity.
  • śṛṇotu: This means "may he hear" or "may it be heard." It is a plea for the divine to listen to the invocation.
  • naḥ: This translates to "our," indicating a collective identity and shared experience.
  • bhūyā: This word means "more" or "greater," suggesting a yearning for deeper connection or understanding.
  • antarā: This translates to "within" or "inside," pointing to the inner dimensions of existence and spirituality.
  • hṛdy: This means "heart" or "soul," emphasizing the emotional and spiritual core of the individual.
  • asya: This translates to "of this" or "of it," referring to the subject of the prayer.
  • nispṛśe: This means "untouched" or "unaffected," suggesting purity and the desire for a connection that transcends the material world.
  • jāyeva: This translates to "as if born" or "like birth," indicating a sense of new beginnings and creation.
  • patya: This word means "spouse" or "partner," symbolizing companionship and unity.
  • uśatī: This means "well-being" or "prosperity," reflecting the desire for a fulfilling life.
  • suvāsāḥ: This translates to "with good fragrance" or "pleasant," suggesting the importance of positivity and harmony in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a profound invocation for divine connection, communal well-being, and spiritual renewal. It emphasizes the importance of heartfelt praise and the desire for enlightenment and prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal prayers, community gatherings, or rituals aimed at fostering unity and positivity. By invoking these sentiments, individuals and communities can cultivate a deeper sense of connection to the divine and to one another, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
