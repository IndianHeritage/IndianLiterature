गर्भं धेहि सिनीवाली गर्भं धेहि सरस्वती |गर्भं ते अश्विनौ देवाव आ धत्ताम पुष्करास्रजा ||
garbhaṁ dhehi sinīvāli garbhaṁ dhehi sarasvati | garbhaṁ te aśvinau devāv ā dhattām puṣkarasrajā ||
  • Garbhaṁ dhehi sinīvāli: This phrase translates to "O Sinīvāli, grant us the womb." Here, "garbha" refers to the womb or the potential for life, while "dhehi" is a request for bestowal. Sinīvāli is a goddess associated with fertility and the nurturing aspect of motherhood.
  • Garbhaṁ dhehi sarasvati: This line means "O Sarasvati, grant us the womb." Sarasvati is the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning. By invoking her, the mantra seeks not just physical fertility but also the intellectual and spiritual growth that comes from knowledge.
  • Garbhaṁ te aśvinau devāv: This translates to "May the two divine Aśvins grant us the womb." The Aśvins are twin deities known for their healing powers and are often associated with the dawn and new beginnings. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between physical and spiritual realms.
  • Ā dhattām puṣkarasrajā: This line means "May they bestow upon us the garland of lotuses." The lotus is a symbol of purity and spiritual awakening, often representing the unfolding of consciousness. The phrase suggests a desire for blessings that lead to enlightenment and growth.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of fertility, both in a physical and spiritual sense. It highlights the interconnectedness of life, knowledge, and divine blessings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as:

  • Fertility Rituals: Couples seeking to conceive may chant this mantra to invoke blessings for a healthy pregnancy and child.
  • Educational Pursuits: Students and scholars can recite this mantra to seek Sarasvati's blessings for wisdom and knowledge in their studies.
  • Spiritual Growth: Individuals on a spiritual path may use this mantra to cultivate inner purity and enlightenment, symbolized by the lotus.

Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder of the divine support available in our pursuits of life, knowledge, and spiritual awakening, encouraging us to seek balance and harmony in all aspects of existence.
