परस्याद्धि संवत एवरा अभ्यातर |यत्राहमस्मि ताम् अव ||
parasyā adhi saṁvato 'varām̐ abhy ā tara | yatrāham asmi tām̐ ava ||
  • Mantra: "parasyā adhi saṁvato 'varām̐ abhy ā tara | yatrāham asmi tām̐ ava ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • parasyā: of the other, or the divine
    • adhi: upon, or in the presence of
    • saṁvato: of the gathering, or assembly
    • avarām̐: the best, or the most excellent
    • abhy: towards, or in the direction of
    • ā tara: to cross over, or to transcend
    • yatrāham: where I am
    • asmi: I exist
    • tām̐: that, or that place
    • ava: to come down, or to descend
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine presence and the gathering of energies that are considered the best or most excellent.
    • It suggests a transcendence towards a higher state of being or understanding, where one can find their true essence.
    • The phrase "yatrāham asmi" emphasizes self-awareness and the realization of one's existence in relation to the divine or the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In moments of meditation or prayer, individuals often seek to connect with a higher power or the universe, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In a community gathering, when people come together for a common purpose, they embody the idea of "saṁvato," creating a space for collective energy and support.
    • When someone overcomes personal challenges or transcends their limitations, they are metaphorically "crossing over" to a better version of themselves, aligning with the mantra's message.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the divine presence in our lives and the power of collective energy.
    • It encourages individuals to seek self-awareness and strive for personal growth, reminding us that we are part of a larger whole.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in practices of mindfulness, community building, and personal development, guiding us towards a more fulfilling existence.
