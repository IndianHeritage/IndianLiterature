अग्नि यद अद्य विश्वो अध्वरस्य होतः पावकशोचे वेष त्वं हि यज्वा |ऋता यजासि महिना वियद भूः हव्याव वह यविष्ट यां ते अद्य ||
agne yad adya viśo adhvarasya hotaḥ pāvakaśoce veṣ ṭvaṁ hi yajvā | ṛtā yajāsi mahinā vi yad bhūr havyā vaha yaviṣṭha yā te adya ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Yad adya: Translates to "what is today," indicating the present moment and the importance of current actions.
  • Viśo: Refers to the community or the collective, emphasizing the role of society in rituals.
  • Adhvarasya: This means "of the sacrifice," highlighting the significance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • Hotaḥ: Refers to the priest or the one who invokes, representing the human element in spiritual practices.
  • Pāvaka: Means "purifier," indicating the transformative power of fire and rituals.
  • Śoce: Translates to "shine" or "illuminate," symbolizing enlightenment and knowledge.
  • Veṣ: Refers to the form or appearance, suggesting the importance of intention in rituals.
  • Tvam hi yajvā: "You are indeed the one who offers," emphasizing the role of the devotee in the act of worship.
  • Ṛtā: Represents truth and cosmic order, indicating the alignment of actions with universal principles.
  • Yajāsi: Means "you perform," highlighting the active participation in rituals.
  • Mahinā: Refers to greatness or abundance, suggesting the rewards of sincere devotion.
  • Vi yad bhūr: "What is vast," indicating the expansive nature of the divine and the universe.
  • Havyā: Refers to offerings, symbolizing the act of giving and sharing with the divine.
  • Vaha: Means "to carry," indicating the transmission of offerings to the divine.
  • Yaviṣṭha: Refers to the best or most excellent, suggesting the pursuit of the highest ideals.
  • Yā te adya: "That which is yours today," emphasizing the immediacy of the connection with the divine.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine. It highlights the role of the individual within the community and the transformative power of sincere devotion.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various ways, such as through community service, where individuals come together to contribute positively to society. It also encourages mindfulness in daily actions, reminding us to act with intention and purity.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals within a community and the significance of rituals in fostering spiritual growth. By engaging in acts of service and devotion, we can cultivate a sense of purpose and connection to the greater whole.
