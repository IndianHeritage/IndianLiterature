स्वादोर इथ्था विषुवतो मध्वः पिबन्ति गौर्यः |या इन्द्रेण सयावरीर वृष्णा मदन्ति शोभसे वस्वीर अनु स्वराज्यम् ||
svādor itthā viṣūvato madhvaḥ pibanti gauryaḥ | yā indreṇa sayāvarīr vṛṣṇā madanti śobhase vasvīr anu svarājyam ||
  • svādor itthā viṣūvato madhvaḥ pibanti gauryaḥ: This line can be translated to mean "The cows, enjoying the sweet nectar, drink from the well of abundance." Here, "svādor" refers to sweetness or pleasure, "madhvaḥ" signifies nectar or something delightful, and "gauryaḥ" refers to cows, which are often seen as symbols of nourishment and abundance in Vedic literature.
  • yā indreṇa sayāvarīr vṛṣṇā madanti śobhase vasvīr anu svarājyam: This part translates to "Those who are blessed by Indra, the powerful, rejoice in the splendor of their kingdom." "Indra" is the king of the gods, representing strength and power, while "vṛṣṇā" refers to the followers of Vishnu, indicating a sense of community and divine protection. "Svarājyam" signifies a prosperous kingdom or realm.

  • Real-Life Example: The imagery of cows drinking sweet nectar can be likened to individuals or communities that thrive in an environment of abundance and joy. For instance, a community that comes together to celebrate a harvest festival can be seen as partaking in this "nectar" of life, where the fruits of their labor are shared and enjoyed.
  • Significance of Indra's Blessing: The mention of Indra highlights the importance of divine favor in achieving success and prosperity. In real life, this can be related to the idea of seeking guidance and support from mentors or leaders who can empower individuals or groups to reach their full potential.

  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the themes of abundance, community, and divine support. It serves as a reminder that joy and prosperity are often found in unity and shared experiences. In practical terms, it encourages individuals to seek out and cultivate relationships that foster growth and happiness, whether in personal life, community initiatives, or professional endeavors.
