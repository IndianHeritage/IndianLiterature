अग्नेद्युम्नेन जागृवे सहसः सूनव आहुत |एदं बर्हिः सदो मम ||
agne dyumnena jāgṛve sahasaḥ sūnav āhuta | edam barhiḥ sado mama ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying and transformative force.
  • Dyumnena: This term means "with brilliance" or "with glory." It signifies the radiant and illuminating nature of Agni.
  • Jāgṛve: This word translates to "I awaken" or "I invoke." It indicates a call to action or a plea for attention.
  • Sahasaḥ: This means "with strength" or "powerfully." It emphasizes the potency of the invocation.
  • Sūnav: This term refers to "sons" or "descendants." It can symbolize the continuity of lineage and the importance of family.
  • Āhuta: This means "offered" or "invoked." It signifies the act of making a sacrifice or offering to the divine.
  • Edam: This word means "this" or "here." It indicates the presence of the offering being made.
  • Barhiḥ: This refers to "sacred grass" or "the altar." It is often used in Vedic rituals as a symbol of purity and sanctity.
  • Sado: This means "to me" or "for me." It indicates a personal connection to the divine.
  • Mama: This translates to "my." It emphasizes ownership and personal relationship with the divine.

The mantra can be interpreted as a powerful invocation to Agni, asking for his brilliance and strength to awaken and bless the speaker and their lineage. It reflects the importance of fire in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purification, transformation, and the divine connection between the individual and the cosmos.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as Agni represents transformation, individuals can invoke this energy in their lives to seek personal development and enlightenment.
  • Family and Legacy: The mention of "sons" emphasizes the importance of family and the continuity of values and traditions. It can inspire individuals to honor their heritage and pass on wisdom to future generations.
  • Rituals and Offerings: In modern practices, this mantra can be recited during rituals to invoke blessings for oneself and loved ones, reinforcing the connection to the divine.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of invocation and the significance of fire as a transformative force. It encourages individuals to seek brilliance and strength in their lives while honoring their lineage and the sacredness of their offerings. The mantra's application in daily life can foster personal growth, strengthen family bonds, and enhance spiritual practices.
