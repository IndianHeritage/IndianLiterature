स्वश्वा सिंधुः सुरथा सुवासा हिरण्ययी सुक्रता वाजिनीवती |ऊर्णावति युवती सीलमावत्य उताधि वस्ते शुभगा मधुवृद्धम् ||
svaśvā sindhuḥ surathā suvāsā hiraṇyayī sukṛtā vājinīvatī | ūrṇāvatī yuvatiḥ sīlamāvaty utādhi vaste subhagā madhuvṛdham ||
  • svaśvā: This term can be interpreted as "one's own" or "belonging to oneself." It emphasizes the importance of personal identity and self-awareness.
  • sindhuḥ: Referring to "the ocean" or "river," this word symbolizes vastness and the flow of life. It can represent the journey of life and the experiences that shape us.
  • surathā: This means "well-being" or "prosperity." It highlights the significance of a fulfilling and prosperous life.
  • suvāsā: Translated as "good fragrance," it signifies positivity and the essence of good deeds and thoughts that attract positivity in life.
  • hiraṇyayī: Meaning "golden," this term represents wealth, abundance, and the richness of life experiences.
  • sukṛtā: This translates to "good deeds" or "virtuous actions," emphasizing the importance of morality and ethics in one's life.
  • vājinīvatī: This word means "powerful" or "strong," indicating the strength and capability one possesses to achieve their goals.
  • ūrṇāvatī: Meaning "full of abundance," it signifies the richness of life and the blessings one receives.
  • yuvatiḥ: This translates to "youth" or "young," symbolizing vitality, energy, and the potential for growth and change.
  • sīlamāvaty: This means "character" or "nature," emphasizing the importance of one's inherent qualities and virtues.
  • utādhi: This term can be interpreted as "to rise" or "to elevate," indicating the aspiration for growth and improvement.
  • vaste: Meaning "to dwell" or "to reside," it signifies the importance of finding a place of belonging and comfort.
  • subhagā: This translates to "auspicious" or "fortunate," highlighting the blessings and good fortune that come from virtuous living.
  • madhuvṛdham: Meaning "sweetness" or "honeyed," it symbolizes the joys and pleasures of life that come from positive actions and thoughts.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of a fulfilling life, emphasizing personal identity, prosperity, good deeds, and the richness of experiences. It serves as a reminder to cultivate virtues, seek abundance, and embrace the journey of life with positivity and strength. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, community building, and fostering relationships. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can create a life that is not only prosperous but also meaningful and fulfilling.
