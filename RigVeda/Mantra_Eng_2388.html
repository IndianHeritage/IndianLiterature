नानौकांसी दुर्यो विश्वं आयुर वि तिष्ठते प्रभवः शोको अग्नेः | ज्येष्ठं माता सूनवे भागम् आधाद अन्व अस्य केतम इषितं सवित्रा ||
nānaukāṁsi duryo viśvam āyur vi tiṣṭhate prabhavaḥ śoko agneḥ | jyeṣṭham mātā sūnave bhāgam ādhād anv asya ketam iṣitaṁ savitrā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nānaukāṁsi: different boats or vessels
    • duryo: difficult or challenging
    • viśvam: the whole or the universe
    • āyur: life or longevity
    • vi tiṣṭhate: stands or exists
    • prabhavaḥ: origin or source
    • śoko: sorrow or grief
    • agneḥ: of Agni (the fire god)
    • jyeṣṭham: the eldest or the most significant
    • mātā: mother
    • sūnave: to the son
    • bhāgam: share or portion
    • ādhād: has given or bestowed
    • anv: following or in accordance with
    • asya: of this
    • ketam: abode or dwelling
    • iṣitaṁ: desired or sought
    • savitṛā: of Savitar (the sun god)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the various challenges and vessels (nānaukāṁsi) that one may encounter in life (viśvam āyur).
    • It emphasizes the importance of Agni, the fire god, as a source of strength and guidance in overcoming sorrow (śoko).
    • The mention of the mother (mātā) and the son (sūnave) signifies the nurturing aspect of life and the importance of familial bonds in providing support.
    • It suggests that the eldest or most significant (jyeṣṭham) aspects of our lives, such as wisdom and experience, are crucial in navigating through difficulties.
    • The mantra concludes with a call to Savitar, the sun god, indicating a desire for enlightenment and clarity in our pursuits.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, such as the loss of a loved one, individuals often seek the support of family (mātā) to help them navigate their grief (śoko).
    • When facing challenges in career or education, one may rely on the wisdom of elders (jyeṣṭham) to guide their decisions and actions.
    • The metaphor of boats (nānaukāṁsi) can be likened to the various paths one can take in life, each with its own set of challenges (duryo) and opportunities for growth.
    • Seeking enlightenment (savitṛā) through education or spiritual practices can help individuals find clarity and direction in their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of familial support, and the guidance of divine forces in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and clarity in their pursuits, recognizing that both joy and sorrow are integral parts of the human experience.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to embrace their journeys, seek support from loved ones, and strive for enlightenment in their actions.
