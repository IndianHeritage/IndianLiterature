शुनं हुवेम मगवाणं इन्द्रं अस्मिन भरे नृतमं वाजसातौ | श्रिण्वन्तं उग्रं ऊतये समत्सु घ्नन्तं वृत्राणि संजितं धनानाम् ||
śunaṁ huvema maghavānam indram asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau | śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi saṁjitaṁ dhanānām ||
  • śunaṁ huvema: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke the powerful." The term "śunaṁ" refers to something that is auspicious or beneficial, while "huvema" means to invoke or call upon. In real life, this can be seen as a call to seek positive energies or forces in our endeavors.
  • maghavānam indram: Here, "maghavānam" refers to the mighty or powerful, and "indram" is a reference to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition. This signifies the importance of seeking strength and leadership in our lives, akin to how we look up to leaders or mentors for guidance.
  • asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau: This translates to "in this place, we offer our best." "Asmin" means 'in this', "bhare" refers to 'place', "nṛtamaṁ" means 'the best', and "vājasātau" relates to 'the best of offerings'. This can be related to the idea of giving our best efforts in any task we undertake, whether in our careers, studies, or personal relationships.
  • śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye: This means "to the one who listens with intensity." "Śṛṇvantam" refers to listening, "ugram" means intense or fierce, and "ūtaye" relates to the act of uplifting or elevating. In a real-life context, this can be seen in the importance of active listening in communication, where truly hearing someone can lead to better understanding and relationships.
  • samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi: This translates to "in the midst of the strong, we conquer obstacles." "Samatsu" means 'in the midst of', "ghnantaṁ" refers to 'conquering', and "vṛtrāṇi" means 'obstacles' or 'enemies'. This can be applied to teamwork and collaboration, where working together with strength can help overcome challenges that may seem insurmountable when faced alone.
  • saṁjitaṁ dhanānām: This phrase means "the gathered wealth." "Saṁjitaṁ" refers to 'gathered' or 'collected', and "dhanānām" means 'of wealth'. In a practical sense, this can relate to the importance of community and collective resources, where pooling together resources can lead to greater prosperity for all involved.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of invoking strength, leadership, and the importance of giving our best in all endeavors. It highlights the power of active listening and collaboration in overcoming obstacles and achieving collective success. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in the workplace, community building, and personal development, reminding us that by seeking positive energies and working together, we can achieve greater heights and overcome challenges.
