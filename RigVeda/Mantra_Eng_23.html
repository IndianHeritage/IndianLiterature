इन्द्रा याहि धियेषितो विप्रजूतः सुतावतः |उप ब्रह्माणि वाघतः ||
indrā yāhi dhiyeṣito viprajūtaḥ sutāvataḥ | upa brahmāṇi vāghataḥ ||
  • Indrā: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • yāhi: A call to come or approach, indicating a request or invitation.
  • dhiyeṣito: Derived from 'dhi', meaning intellect or understanding; 'ṣita' implies being directed or guided. Together, it suggests a call for wisdom or insight.
  • viprajūtaḥ: Refers to those who are well-versed or knowledgeable, often implying scholars or wise individuals.
  • sutāvataḥ: Meaning 'those who are well-spoken' or 'who possess good speech', indicating the importance of communication and eloquence.
  • upa: Meaning 'towards' or 'near', suggesting a movement or approach towards something.
  • brahmāṇi: Refers to the ultimate reality or the divine, often associated with the concept of Brahman in Hindu philosophy.
  • vāghataḥ: Meaning 'the one who speaks' or 'the voice', emphasizing the power of speech and expression.

This mantra can be interpreted as a call to Indra to come forth, bringing wisdom and eloquence, and to guide those who are knowledgeable and articulate towards the ultimate truth or reality. It emphasizes the importance of intellect, communication, and divine guidance in the pursuit of knowledge and understanding.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra is a symbol of leadership, individuals in positions of authority can invoke the qualities of strength and wisdom to lead effectively.
  • Education: The emphasis on knowledge and eloquence highlights the importance of education and effective communication in personal and professional growth.
  • Spiritual Pursuit: The call towards Brahman signifies the quest for spiritual enlightenment, encouraging individuals to seek deeper truths in their lives.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance in the pursuit of wisdom and effective communication. It serves as a reminder that strength and leadership should be accompanied by intellect and eloquence, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal development to leadership roles and spiritual journeys.
