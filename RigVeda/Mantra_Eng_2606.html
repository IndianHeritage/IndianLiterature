अग्निं सुदीतिं सुदृशं गृणन्तो नमस्यामस त्वेढ्यं जातवेदः |त्वां दूतम अरतिं हव्यवाहं देवाः अकृण्वन्न अमृतस्य नाभिम् ||
agniṁ sudītiṁ sudṛśaṁ gṛṇanto namasyāmas tveḍyaṁ jātavedaḥ | tvāṁ dūtam aratiṁ havyavāhaṁ devā akṛṇvann amṛtasya nābhim ||
  • Agniṁ: Refers to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Sudītiṁ: Means "good thought" or "auspiciousness," indicating the importance of positive intentions.
  • Sudṛśaṁ: Translates to "well-seen" or "beautiful," suggesting clarity and vision in one's actions.
  • Gṛṇanto: Means "we sing" or "we praise," emphasizing the act of worship and reverence.
  • Namasyāmas: Translates to "we bow down," indicating humility and respect towards the divine.
  • Tveḍyaṁ: Refers to "you who are to be worshipped," acknowledging Agni's divine status.
  • Jātavedaḥ: Means "the one who knows all births," highlighting Agni's role as a mediator between the earthly and the divine.
  • Tvāṁ: Means "you," directly addressing Agni.
  • Dūtam: Translates to "messenger," indicating Agni's role in conveying prayers to the gods.
  • Aratiṁ: Refers to "the one who removes obstacles," symbolizing Agni's protective qualities.
  • Havyavāhaṁ: Means "the one who carries offerings," emphasizing Agni's role in accepting and delivering sacrifices to the gods.
  • Devā: Refers to "the gods," indicating the divine beings that Agni serves.
  • Akṛṇvann: Means "they do not create," suggesting the importance of divine intervention in human affairs.
  • Amṛtasya: Translates to "of immortality," indicating the pursuit of eternal life or spiritual enlightenment.
  • Nābhim: Refers to "the navel" or "the center," symbolizing the core of existence or the source of life.

The mantra essentially invokes Agni, the fire god, as a source of purity, wisdom, and divine communication. It emphasizes the importance of good thoughts and intentions, as well as the need for humility and respect in our spiritual practices. By addressing Agni as a messenger and protector, the mantra highlights the role of divine forces in guiding and supporting human endeavors.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: By cultivating good thoughts and intentions, individuals can transform their lives positively, much like how fire transforms raw materials into useful forms.
  • Conflict Resolution: Just as Agni removes obstacles, individuals can seek to resolve conflicts with humility and respect, fostering better relationships.
  • Spiritual Practices: The act of worship and reverence can be integrated into daily routines, reminding individuals to stay connected to their spiritual beliefs.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of Agni in our lives, encouraging us to embrace positive thoughts, respect the divine, and seek guidance in our endeavors. It highlights the interconnectedness of our actions, intentions, and the divine forces that shape our existence.
