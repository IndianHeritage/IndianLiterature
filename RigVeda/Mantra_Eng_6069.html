मन्त्र: निर इन्द्र बृहतीभ्यो वृतं धनुभ्यो अस्फुरः | निर अर्बुदस्य मृगयस्य मायिनो निः पर्वतस्य गा आजः ||
nir indra bṛhatībhyo vṛtraṁ dhanubhyo asphuraḥ | nir arbudasya mṛgayasya māyino niḥ parvatasya gā ājaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nir: without
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • bṛhatībhyo: from the great ones
    • vṛtraṁ: the obstacle or the demon
    • dhanubhyo: from the bow
    • asphuraḥ: to strike or to hit
    • nir: without
    • arbudasya: of the mountain
    • mṛgayasya: of the hunt
    • māyino: of the illusionist
    • niḥ: from
    • parvatasya: of the mountain
    • gā: the song or the hymn
    • ājaḥ: to come forth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of overcoming obstacles and challenges, symbolized by "vṛtra," which can represent any form of adversity in life.
    • The mention of "Indra" signifies the divine power and strength that can be invoked to face these challenges.
    • The phrase "without the mountain" suggests that one can rise above great challenges and illusions that may seem insurmountable.
    • The reference to "the hunt" indicates the pursuit of goals and aspirations, emphasizing the need for determination and focus.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual may face significant challenges in their career, akin to "vṛtra." By invoking their inner strength and determination, they can overcome these obstacles, much like Indra conquering demons.
    • In personal life, someone dealing with mental health issues may feel trapped by their circumstances. This mantra serves as a reminder that they can rise above their struggles and seek help, akin to overcoming the "mountain" of their challenges.
    • In sports, athletes often face formidable opponents (vṛtra). By channeling their inner strength and focusing on their training (the hunt), they can achieve victory.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of inner strength and resilience in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that with determination and the right mindset, one can rise above obstacles, whether they are personal, professional, or spiritual.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, encouraging individuals to confront their fears and challenges with courage and confidence.
