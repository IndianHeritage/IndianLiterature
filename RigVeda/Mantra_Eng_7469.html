आमासु पक्वम ऐरय आ सूर्यं रोहयो दिवि |गर्मं न सामन तपतां सुवृक्तिभिर जुष्टं गिरवणसे बृहत् ||
āmāsu pakvam airaya ā sūryaṁ rohayo divi | gharmaṁ na sāman tapatā suvṛktibhir juṣṭaṁ girvaṇase bṛhat ||
  • Transliteration: āmāsu pakvam airaya ā sūryaṁ rohayo divi
  • Translation: "Bring forth the ripened fruits, O Sun, shining in the sky."
  • Meaning: This mantra invokes the Sun, asking it to provide the ripened fruits, symbolizing abundance and prosperity. The Sun is often seen as a source of life and energy, essential for growth and nourishment.

  • Transliteration: gharmaṁ na sāman tapatā suvṛktibhir juṣṭaṁ girvaṇase bṛhat
  • Translation: "May the warmth not be diminished; may it be filled with the best of offerings."
  • Meaning: This part of the mantra emphasizes the importance of maintaining warmth and energy, suggesting that the offerings made should be of the highest quality. It reflects the idea that our efforts and contributions should be sincere and valuable.

  • Significance: The mantra highlights the relationship between nature and human efforts. It signifies the importance of the Sun in agriculture and sustenance, as well as the need for quality in our actions and offerings.
  • Real-life Application: In agriculture, farmers rely on the Sun for the growth of crops. This mantra can be seen as a prayer for a good harvest, emphasizing the need for favorable conditions. In a broader sense, it can apply to any endeavor where effort and quality are paramount, such as in business, education, or personal relationships.

  • Conclusion: This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of nature, and the value of quality in our contributions. It encourages us to seek abundance while ensuring that our efforts are meaningful and impactful in our daily lives.
