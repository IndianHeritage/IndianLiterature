स सोम आमिष्लतमः सुतो भूद यस्मिन पक्तिः पच्यते सन्ति धानाः | इन्द्रं नरः स्तुवन्तो ब्रह्मकारा उक्ताः शंसन्तो देववततमाः ||
sa soma āmiślatamaḥ suto bhūd yasmin paktiḥ pacyate santi dhānāḥ | indraṁ naraḥ stuvanto brahmakārā ukthā śaṁsanto devavātatamāḥ ||
  • sa soma āmiślatamaḥ: This phrase refers to the essence of Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing vitality and immortality. Soma is often associated with the divine and is believed to enhance consciousness.
  • suto bhūd yasmin paktiḥ pacyate santi dhānāḥ: This translates to "may the son (or offspring) be born in whom the essence of nourishment is cooked." It emphasizes the importance of nurturing and the growth of life, suggesting that true nourishment leads to prosperity and well-being.
  • indraṁ naraḥ stuvanto: Here, Indra, the king of gods and a symbol of strength and power, is being praised by humans. This highlights the relationship between humans and the divine, where devotion and reverence are essential for receiving blessings.
  • brahmakārā ukthā: This phrase refers to the hymns or praises that are created with the essence of Brahman, the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit in Hindu philosophy. It signifies the importance of spiritual knowledge and the power of words in manifesting reality.
  • śaṁsanto devavātatamāḥ: This means "those who praise the divine and the bestowers of blessings." It underscores the significance of gratitude and acknowledgment of divine forces in our lives.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of life, nourishment, and the relationship between humans and the divine. It emphasizes the importance of spiritual practices, devotion, and the pursuit of knowledge.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek nourishment not just in a physical sense but also spiritually. For instance, engaging in practices like meditation, yoga, or community service can be seen as ways to cultivate the essence of life and connect with the divine. Additionally, it encourages gratitude and recognition of the forces that support our existence.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of nurturing both body and spirit, and the power of devotion. By embodying these principles, individuals can lead a more fulfilling and harmonious life, fostering a deeper connection with themselves and the universe.
