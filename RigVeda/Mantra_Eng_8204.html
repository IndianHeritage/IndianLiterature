उतो सहस्रभार्णसँ वाचं सोम मखस्युवम् |पुनान इन्दव आ भर ||
uto sahasrabharṇasaṁ vācaṁ soma makhasyuvam | punāna indav ā bhara ||
  • Mantra: "uto sahasrabharṇasaṁ vācaṁ soma makhasyuvam | punāna indav ā bhara ||"
  • Translation: "O Indra, may you bring forth the thousand-fold speech of the Soma, which is the essence of the sacrifices. Purify and bestow it upon us."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Uto: A term of invocation, often used to call upon a deity.
    • Sahasrabharṇa: Refers to "thousand-fold" or "abundant," indicating a vastness or richness.
    • Vācaṁ: Means "speech" or "word," symbolizing communication, expression, and the power of sound.
    • Soma: A sacred drink in Vedic rituals, representing divine inspiration and the essence of life.
    • Makhasyuvam: Relates to "sacrifice" or "offering," emphasizing the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
    • Punāna: Means "purifying" or "cleansing," indicating a desire for spiritual and mental clarity.
    • Indav: Refers to "Indra," the king of gods, symbolizing strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • Ā bhara: Means "bestow" or "bring forth," a request for divine blessings and gifts.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often invoke the spirit of collaboration and communication to foster teamwork, similar to how the mantra calls for the divine speech of Soma to enhance the quality of offerings.
    • During personal challenges, individuals may seek clarity and purification through meditation or prayer, akin to the mantra's request for Indra to purify and bestow blessings.
    • In community gatherings, the sharing of stories and experiences can be seen as a modern-day equivalent of invoking the "thousand-fold speech," where collective wisdom is celebrated and shared.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of divine communication and the importance of purification in our lives. It serves as a reminder that through rituals, whether in personal or communal contexts, we can seek clarity, strength, and inspiration. The invocation of Indra symbolizes the need for guidance and support in overcoming life's challenges, making this mantra applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to community bonding.
