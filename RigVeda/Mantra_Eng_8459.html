प्र रेब्ह एत्य अतिवारं अव्ययं वृषा वनेष्व अव चक्रदद धरीः |सं धीतयो वावशानां अनूषत शिशुं रिहन्ति मतयः पनिप्नतम् ||
pra rebha ety ati vāram avyayaṁ vṛṣā vaneṣv ava cakradad dhariḥ | saṁ dhītayo vāvaśānā anūṣata śiśuṁ rihanti matayaḥ panipnatam ||
  • pra rebha ety ati vāram avyayaṁ vṛṣā vaneṣv ava cakradad dhariḥ
    • pra rebha: This phrase can be interpreted as "come forth" or "arise." It signifies the call to action or the emergence of something significant.
    • ety ati vāram: "ety" means "to go" or "to come," while "ati vāram" can be understood as "beyond limits" or "exceeding boundaries." This suggests a movement towards something greater.
    • avyayaṁ: This term means "imperishable" or "eternal." It indicates a quality that is unchanging and everlasting.
    • vṛṣā: This word can mean "strength" or "power." It often refers to the vigor and vitality found in nature.
    • vaneṣv: "in the forests" or "in the woods," indicating a connection to nature and the natural world.
    • ava cakradad dhariḥ: "ava" means "down" or "from above," "cakradad" refers to "the one who holds the wheel," and "dhariḥ" means "the bearer." This can symbolize a divine force or energy that governs the cycles of life.
  • saṁ dhītayo vāvaśānā anūṣata śiśuṁ rihanti matayaḥ panipnatam
    • saṁ dhītayo: This can be interpreted as "with intentions" or "with thoughts." It emphasizes the importance of mental focus and clarity.
    • vāvaśānā: This term suggests "those who are wise" or "the knowledgeable." It highlights the role of wisdom in guiding actions.
    • anūṣata: This means "to follow" or "to pursue." It indicates a journey or a path that is being taken.
    • śiśuṁ: This word means "child" or "young one," symbolizing innocence, potential, and new beginnings.
    • rihanti: This can be interpreted as "to nurture" or "to care for." It emphasizes the act of supporting growth.
    • matayaḥ: This term refers to "thoughts" or "intellect." It signifies the power of the mind in shaping reality.
    • panipnatam: This means "the most excellent" or "the highest." It suggests striving for the best in all endeavors.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of emerging strength and wisdom in the pursuit of growth and nurturing potential. It calls for individuals to rise above limitations, embrace their inner power, and act with intention and clarity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, leadership, and education. For instance, a teacher nurturing a child's potential embodies the essence of this mantra, guiding them with wisdom and care towards a brighter future. The significance lies in recognizing the eternal qualities within ourselves and the world around us, encouraging us to act wisely and compassionately in our daily lives.
