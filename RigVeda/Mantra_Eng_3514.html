अहम् अपो अपिन्वम् उक्षमाणाऽ धारणयाम् दिवं सदन ऋतस्य |ऋतेन पुत्रो अदितेर ऋतावोऽत त्रिधातु प्रथयद् वि भूमा ||
aham apo apinvam ukṣamāṇā dhārayaṁ divaṁ sadana ṛtasya | ṛtena putro aditer ṛtāvota tridhātu prathayad vi bhūma ||
  • Aham: This word translates to "I" or "myself," indicating a sense of self-awareness and identity.
  • Apo: This means "water," which is often associated with purity, life, and sustenance in Vedic literature.
  • Apinvam: This can be interpreted as "I am obtaining" or "I am acquiring," suggesting an active engagement with the world.
  • Ukṣamāṇā: This term refers to "the one who is flourishing" or "prospering," indicating growth and vitality.
  • Dhārayaṁ: This means "I hold" or "I support," which implies a sense of responsibility and nurturing.
  • Divaṁ: This translates to "heaven" or "the divine," representing higher consciousness or spiritual realms.
  • Sadana: This means "abode" or "dwelling," often referring to a sacred space or a place of stability.
  • Ṛtasya: This word means "of truth" or "of order," emphasizing the importance of righteousness and cosmic law.
  • Ṛtena: This translates to "by truth," indicating that truth is the guiding principle in life.
  • Putro: This means "son," symbolizing lineage, continuity, and the future generation.
  • Aditer: This refers to "Aditi," the mother of gods, representing the source of all creation and abundance.
  • Ṛtāvota: This can be interpreted as "the one who is in accordance with truth," reinforcing the idea of living a life aligned with cosmic order.
  • Tridhātu: This means "three realms" or "three elements," often referring to the physical, mental, and spiritual aspects of existence.
  • Prathayad: This translates to "he who spreads" or "he who manifests," indicating the act of bringing forth or revealing.
  • Vi bhūma: This means "the vastness" or "the great," suggesting the infinite nature of existence and consciousness.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of self-awareness, the importance of truth, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It emphasizes the role of water as a life-giving force and the nurturing aspect of existence. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal growth, environmental consciousness, and the pursuit of truth in our actions. By recognizing our responsibilities and striving for harmony with the universe, we can create a more balanced and fulfilling life. The significance of this mantra lies in its call to align ourselves with the cosmic order, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in our daily lives.
