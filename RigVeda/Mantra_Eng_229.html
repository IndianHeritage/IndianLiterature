तद्विप्रासो विपण्यवो जागृवांसः समिन्दधते |विष्णोर्यत्परमंपदं ||
tad viprāso vipanyavo jāgṛvāṁsaḥ sam indhate | viṣṇor yat paramam padam ||
  • Mantra: "tad viprāso vipanyavo jāgṛvāṁsaḥ sam indhate | viṣṇor yat paramam padam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • viprāso: of the wise or learned
    • vipanyavo: those who are enlightened or knowledgeable
    • jāgṛvāṁsaḥ: who are awake or aware
    • sam indhate: come together or unite
    • viṣṇoḥ: of Vishnu (the preserver in Hinduism)
    • yat: which
    • paramam: supreme or highest
    • padam: place or state
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the unity and collective consciousness of the wise and enlightened individuals.
    • It emphasizes the importance of awareness and knowledge in achieving a higher state of existence.
    • The reference to Vishnu signifies the ultimate goal of reaching a supreme state of being, which is often associated with peace, harmony, and preservation of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, when a team of knowledgeable individuals comes together to solve a problem, their collective wisdom can lead to innovative solutions, reflecting the essence of "sam indhate."
    • In community service, when enlightened individuals unite for a cause, such as environmental conservation, they embody the principles of awareness and collective action, striving for a higher purpose.
    • In education, when students engage in collaborative learning, they enhance their understanding and awareness, mirroring the idea of "viprāso vipanyavo."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity among the wise and the importance of striving for a higher state of consciousness.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as teamwork, community efforts, and educational environments, where collective knowledge and awareness lead to greater achievements.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to seek enlightenment and work together towards a common goal, reflecting the divine qualities of Vishnu.
