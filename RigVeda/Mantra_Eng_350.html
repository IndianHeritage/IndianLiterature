त्वं त्येभिरागहि वाजेभिरदुहितर दिवः | अस्मे रयिं निधारय ||
tvaṁ tyebhir ā gahi vājebhir duhitar divaḥ | asme rayiṁ ni dhāraya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • tyebhir: With these (or those)
    • ā gahi: Come near or approach
    • vājebhir: With the best or most excellent (often referring to strength or power)
    • duhitar: Daughter
    • divaḥ: Of the heavens or sky
    • asme: For us
    • rayiṁ: Wealth or prosperity
    • ni dhāraya: Hold or maintain
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a call to a divine entity, asking it to come closer and bestow strength and prosperity upon us.
    • The reference to "daughter of the heavens" signifies a connection to divine feminine energy, often associated with nurturing and abundance.
    • The plea for wealth and prosperity indicates a desire for material and spiritual well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be seen as a prayer for success and growth. Entrepreneurs often seek divine blessings to attract customers and increase profits.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during times of financial difficulty, hoping to attract opportunities and resources.
    • In family settings, it can be a way to invoke blessings for the well-being and success of children, encouraging them to pursue their dreams with strength and resilience.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine support in our endeavors, whether they are personal, familial, or professional.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition that strength and prosperity often come from a higher source, and by invoking this energy, we align ourselves with abundance.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to remain open to receiving blessings and to actively pursue our goals with the understanding that we are supported by the universe.
