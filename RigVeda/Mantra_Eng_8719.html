तवाहं सोम रारण सख्य इन्दो दिवे-दिवे |पुरूणि बभ्रो नि चरन्ति मां अव परिधीमृं अति तामिहि ||
tavāhaṁ soma rāraṇa sakhya indo dive-dive | purūṇi babhro ni caranti mām ava paridhīm̐r ati tām̐ ihi ||
  • tavāhaṁ: This word translates to "I am yours" or "I belong to you." It signifies a deep sense of devotion and connection.
  • soma: Soma refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes the essence of life and spiritual awakening.
  • rāraṇa: This term can be interpreted as "to be nourished" or "to be sustained." It emphasizes the importance of support and sustenance in life.
  • sakhya: Meaning "friendship" or "companionship," this word highlights the value of relationships and bonds between individuals.
  • indo: This refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic mythology, representing power, strength, and protection.
  • dive-dive: This phrase means "day by day," indicating the continuity and regularity of experiences and relationships.
  • purūṇi: This word translates to "many" or "various," suggesting diversity in experiences and relationships.
  • babhro: This term can mean "to shine" or "to illuminate," symbolizing enlightenment and clarity.
  • ni caranti: This means "they move" or "they travel," indicating the dynamic nature of life and experiences.
  • mām: This translates to "me," referring to the self or the individual seeking connection and understanding.
  • ava: This word means "down" or "below," often used to indicate a grounding or foundational aspect.
  • paridhīm̐r: This term can be interpreted as "surrounding" or "enveloping," suggesting protection and support from the universe.
  • ati: Meaning "beyond" or "exceeding," this word emphasizes the idea of transcending limitations.
  • tām̐: This translates to "that" or "that which," referring to a specific quality or state.
  • ihi: This means "here" or "in this place," grounding the mantra in the present moment.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of devotion, nourishment, friendship, and the dynamic nature of life. It emphasizes the importance of relationships and the support we receive from the universe. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as fostering deep connections with friends and family, seeking spiritual nourishment, and recognizing the continuous journey of life. It serves as a reminder to appreciate the bonds we share and the divine support that surrounds us, encouraging us to live with gratitude and awareness in our daily lives.
