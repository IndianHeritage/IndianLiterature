अग्निः होतार् नो अध्वर्य वाजी सन् परिणीयते |देवो देवेषु यज्ञियः ||
agnir hotā no adhvare vājī san pari ṇīyate | devo deveṣu yajñiyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Agnir: Fire, representing energy and purity.
    • Hotā: The one who offers, the priest or the one who invokes.
    • No: Our.
    • Adhvare: In the sacrifice or ritual.
    • Vājī: The one who brings strength or nourishment.
    • San: Being or existence.
    • Pari ṇīyate: Is invoked or called upon.
    • Devo: God or divine being.
    • Deveṣu: Among the gods.
    • Yajñiyaḥ: Worthy of worship or sacrifice.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of fire (Agnir) in rituals, symbolizing the divine energy that connects humans with the divine.
    • The invocation of Agni (fire) is crucial in Vedic rituals, as it is believed to carry offerings to the gods.
    • The phrase "no adhvare" indicates that this invocation is for our own benefit, highlighting the personal connection to the divine through rituals.
    • "Vājī san pari ṇīyate" suggests that through these rituals, we are strengthened and nourished, both physically and spiritually.
    • The mention of "devo deveṣu yajñiyaḥ" signifies that the divine presence is acknowledged among other gods, reinforcing the idea of unity in worship.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of invoking energy can be seen in practices like meditation and yoga, where individuals seek to connect with a higher power or their inner self.
    • Rituals such as lighting candles or incense can be viewed as a contemporary form of honoring the divine, similar to the Vedic practice of invoking Agni.
    • In community gatherings, the act of sharing food can symbolize nourishment and strength, akin to the offerings made in Vedic rituals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of rituals in connecting with the divine and seeking strength and nourishment in life.
    • It teaches us the importance of intention in our actions, whether in spiritual practices or daily life.
    • By recognizing the divine in our rituals and actions, we can cultivate a deeper sense of purpose and connection in our lives.
