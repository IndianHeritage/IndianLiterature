भिनद वलम् अङ्गिरोभिर गृणानो वि पर्वतस्य दृष्मितानि ऐरात् |रिणग रोधांसि कृत्रिमाण्य एषां सोमस्य ता मद इन्द्रश्चकार ||
bhinad valam aṅgirobhir gṛṇāno vi parvatasya dṛṁhitāny airat | riṇag rodhāṁsi kṛtrimāṇy eṣāṁ somasya tā mada indraś cakāra ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bhinad: breaking or splitting
    • valam: strength or power
    • aṅgirobhir: by the Angiras (a group of sages)
    • gṛṇāno: singing or chanting
    • vi: apart or separately
    • parvatasya: of the mountain
    • dṛṁhitāny: the firm or stable
    • airat: to be made or created
    • riṇag: to roar or make a sound
    • rodhāṁsi: obstacles or barriers
    • kṛtrimāṇy: artificial or man-made
    • eṣāṁ: of these
    • somasya: of Soma (a sacred drink)
    • tā: that
    • mada: intoxication or ecstasy
    • indraś: of Indra (the king of gods)
    • cakāra: created or made
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power of breaking barriers and the strength that comes from unity and collective effort.
    • The reference to the Angiras suggests the importance of wisdom and knowledge in overcoming challenges.
    • The mention of mountains symbolizes stability and the challenges that one must face in life.
    • The roar signifies the strength and determination needed to overcome obstacles.
    • The artificial barriers represent the man-made challenges that can hinder progress.
    • Soma, often associated with divine inspiration and ecstasy, indicates that through spiritual practices, one can achieve a higher state of consciousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team project, individuals may face various challenges (like artificial barriers) that can hinder their progress. By working together (like the Angiras), they can break these barriers and achieve their goals.
    • In personal development, one may encounter mental blocks (the mountains) that seem insurmountable. Through collective wisdom and support from others, these can be overcome.
    • In a community setting, when people unite to address social issues, they can create significant change, akin to the strength derived from the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, strength, and the power of collective wisdom in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that while obstacles may seem daunting, with the right mindset and support, they can be surmounted.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, from personal struggles to community efforts, highlighting the importance of collaboration and spiritual growth.
