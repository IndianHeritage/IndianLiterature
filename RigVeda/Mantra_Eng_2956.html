सुकृत सुपाणिः स्ववाम् ऋतावा देवस् तवष्टावसे तानि नो धात् |पुषण्वन्त ऋभवो मदयध्वम् ऊर्ध्वग्रावाणो अध्वरम् अतष्ट ||
sukṛt supāṇiḥ svavām̐ ṛtāvā devas tvaṣṭāvase tāni no dhāt | pūṣaṇvanta ṛbhavo mādayadhvam ūrdhvagrāvāṇo adhvaram ataṣṭa ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sukṛt: Good deeds or actions.
    • supāṇiḥ: Well-prepared or well-formed hands.
    • svavām̐: Our own or belonging to us.
    • ṛtāvā: The one who is established in truth or righteousness.
    • devas: Gods or divine beings.
    • tvaṣṭāvase: O Tvaṣṭa, the divine craftsman or creator.
    • tāni: Those (good deeds or actions).
    • no: For us.
    • dhāt: To establish or to create.
    • pūṣaṇvanta: O Pūṣaṇa, the nourisher or sustainer.
    • ṛbhavo: O beings of truth or righteousness.
    • mādayadhvam: May you be pleased or joyful.
    • ūrdhvagrāvāṇo: O one who is exalted or elevated.
    • adhvaram: The sacrifice or the ritual.
    • ataṣṭa: May it be established or may it be fulfilled.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine forces, particularly Tvaṣṭa and Pūṣaṇa, to bless the speaker with good deeds and the ability to perform righteous actions.
    • The mention of "sukṛt" emphasizes the importance of good actions in our lives, which can be seen in real-life examples such as volunteering, helping others, or engaging in community service.
    • "Supāṇiḥ" suggests that our actions should be well-prepared and thoughtful, akin to a craftsman who meticulously creates a beautiful piece of art.
    • The call to "ṛtāvā" signifies the need to align our actions with truth and righteousness, which can be reflected in ethical decision-making in business or personal life.
    • By addressing Tvaṣṭa, the divine craftsman, the mantra acknowledges the creative power of the universe and our role in shaping our destinies through our actions.
    • "Pūṣaṇvanta" highlights the nurturing aspect of the divine, reminding us of the importance of support and sustenance in our lives, much like how a community supports its members.
    • The phrase "mādayadhvam" encourages joy and fulfillment in our endeavors, suggesting that happiness is a byproduct of righteous actions.
    • Finally, the mantra concludes with a plea for the establishment of our sacrifices and efforts, indicating that our hard work and dedication should lead to fruitful outcomes.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of good deeds and righteous living in our daily lives.
    • It encourages individuals to engage in actions that are not only beneficial to themselves but also to society as a whole.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as ethical business practices, community service, and personal relationships.
    • Ultimately, the mantra emphasizes that our actions, when aligned with truth and righteousness, can lead to personal fulfillment and a positive impact on the world around us.
