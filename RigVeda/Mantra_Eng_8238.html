आ रयिम आ सुचेतुनम् आ सुक्रतो तनूष्व आ |पान्तम् आ पुरुष्पृहम् ||
ā rayim ā sucetunam ā sukrato tanūṣv ā | pāntam ā puruspṛham ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā rayim: "to wealth" or "to prosperity"
    • ā sucetunam: "to those who are wise" or "to the intelligent"
    • ā sukrato: "to those who perform good deeds"
    • tanūṣv: "in bodies" or "in forms"
    • pāntam: "to the one who is protected" or "to the one who is guided"
    • ā puruspṛham: "to the one who is full of strength" or "to the one who is powerful"
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes prosperity and wisdom, emphasizing the importance of good deeds and strength.
    • It suggests that true wealth comes to those who are wise and act righteously.
    • The mantra highlights the connection between physical strength and moral integrity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, a company that prioritizes ethical practices and wise decision-making often sees long-term success and prosperity.
    • In personal life, individuals who engage in community service and help others tend to build stronger relationships and a supportive network, leading to a fulfilling life.
    • In sports, athletes who train hard (strength) and maintain a good sportsmanship (good deeds) often achieve greater success and respect.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder that true prosperity is not just about material wealth but also about wisdom, good actions, and strength of character.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal development, professional ethics, and community engagement.
    • By embodying the principles of wisdom, good deeds, and strength, individuals can create a more prosperous and harmonious life.
