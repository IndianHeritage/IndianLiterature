अग्निस् त्रीणि त्रिधातून्य् आ क्षेति विदथā कविः |स त्रिम् एकादशाम् इह यक्षच् च पिप्रयच् च नो विप्रो दूतः परिष्कृतो नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
agnis trīṇi tridhātūny ā kṣeti vidathā kaviḥ | sa trīm̐r ekādaśām̐ iha yakṣac ca piprayac ca no vipro dūtaḥ pariṣkṛto nabhantām anyake same ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • agnis: Fire
    • trīṇi: Three
    • tridhātūn: Three realms or aspects
    • ā kṣeti: He who knows or perceives
    • vidathā: The creative or productive force
    • kaviḥ: The seer or wise one
    • sa: He
    • trīm̐r: Threefold
    • ekādaśām̐: Eleven
    • iha: Here
    • yakṣac: The one who grants or bestows
    • ca: And
    • piprayac: He who nourishes or sustains
    • ca: And
    • no: Us
    • vipro: The learned or wise one
    • dūtaḥ: Messenger
    • pariṣkṛto: Perfected or purified
    • nabhantām: The heavens or sky
    • anyake: Other beings
    • same: Equally or in harmony
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine fire (Agni) and its threefold nature, representing the physical, mental, and spiritual realms.
    • The phrase "He who knows" suggests the importance of wisdom and understanding in navigating these realms.
    • The mention of "eleven" could symbolize completeness or the totality of existence, as eleven is often seen as a number that transcends the ordinary.
    • The role of the "messenger" indicates the importance of communication and the sharing of knowledge among beings.
    • In real life, this can be seen in the way we seek knowledge and understanding in various aspects of our lives, whether through education, spiritual practices, or interpersonal relationships.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all aspects of life—physical, mental, and spiritual.
    • It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and understanding, which can lead to personal growth and harmony in relationships.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various fields such as education, where a holistic approach to learning is essential, or in personal development, where one seeks balance in life.
    • Moreover, the idea of communication and sharing knowledge is crucial in today's world, where collaboration and understanding among diverse groups are necessary for progress.
