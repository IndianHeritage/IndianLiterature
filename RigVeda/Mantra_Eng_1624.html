यजामहे वाम महः सजोषा हव्येभिर मित्रावरुणा नमोभिः |घृतैर घृतस्नू अध यद वाम अस्मे अध्वर्यवो न धीतिभिर भरन्ति ||
yajāmahe vām mahaḥ sajoṣā havyebhir mitrāvaruṇā namobhiḥ | ghṛtair ghṛtasnū adha yad vām asme adhvaryavo na dhītibhir bharanti ||
  • yajāmahe: This term translates to "we invoke" or "we worship." It signifies the act of calling upon divine forces or energies.
  • vām: Meaning "your" or "of you," it indicates a connection to the divine or the forces being invoked.
  • mahaḥ: This word means "great" or "mighty," emphasizing the power of the deities being addressed.
  • sajoṣā: This term can be interpreted as "with joy" or "with enthusiasm," suggesting that the worship is performed with a positive and uplifting spirit.
  • havyebhir: This means "with offerings," referring to the ritualistic offerings made to the deities.
  • mitrāvaruṇā: This compound word refers to the deities Mitra and Varuna, who represent friendship, harmony, and cosmic order.
  • namobhiḥ: This translates to "with salutations" or "with reverence," indicating a respectful approach to the divine.
  • ghṛtair: Meaning "with ghee," it refers to the sacred substance used in rituals, symbolizing purity and nourishment.
  • ghṛtasnū: This term can be understood as "with the essence of ghee," further emphasizing the importance of offerings in rituals.
  • adha: This means "below" or "in the lower realms," indicating a connection to the earthly plane.
  • yad: This translates to "that" or "which," referring to the qualities or attributes of the deities being invoked.
  • vām: Again, this means "your," reinforcing the connection to the divine.
  • asme: This means "to us," indicating the desire for the blessings of the deities.
  • adhvaryavo: This term refers to the priests or those who perform the rituals, highlighting the importance of the ritualistic process.
  • na: This means "not," indicating a negation or a desire to avoid something undesirable.
  • dhītibhir: This translates to "by wisdom" or "by understanding," suggesting that the blessings sought are related to knowledge and insight.
  • bharanti: This means "they carry" or "they bestow," indicating the action of the deities in granting blessings.

The mantra essentially invokes the deities Mitra and Varuna, asking for their blessings and guidance. It emphasizes the importance of performing rituals with joy and sincerity, using offerings that symbolize purity and nourishment. The worshippers seek wisdom and understanding, hoping that the divine will bestow these qualities upon them.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: It can be recited during important life events like weddings, housewarming ceremonies, or festivals to invoke blessings for harmony and prosperity.
  • Personal Reflection: Individuals can use this mantra during meditation or personal reflection to seek guidance and wisdom in their daily lives.
  • Community Gatherings: It can be used in community events to foster a sense of unity and collective purpose, invoking the spirit of cooperation and friendship.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of invoking divine energies with sincerity and joy. It highlights the significance of community, wisdom, and the offerings we make in our lives, encouraging us to seek harmony and understanding in our relationships and endeavors.
