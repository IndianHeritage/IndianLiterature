अपां मध्य तस्थिवांसं तृष्णाविदज जरीतारम् |मृळा सुख्षत्र मृळय ||
apām madhye tasthivāṁsaṁ tṛṣṇāvidaj jaritāram | mṛḻā sukṣatra mṛḻaya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apām: of waters
    • madhye: in the midst
    • tasthivāṁsaṁ: standing or residing
    • tṛṣṇāvidaj: quencher of thirst
    • jaritāram: the one who brings forth or creates
    • mṛḻā: gentle or soothing
    • sukṣatra: auspicious or beneficial
    • mṛḻaya: may you soothe or calm
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the essence of water, highlighting its central role in life and its ability to quench thirst.
    • It emphasizes the soothing and gentle nature of water, which is essential for sustenance and well-being.
    • The phrase "standing in the midst of waters" suggests a connection to the source of life, indicating that water is foundational to existence.
    • The term "quencher of thirst" symbolizes not just physical thirst but also spiritual and emotional needs, reflecting the holistic nature of water.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, water is vital for crops. Farmers rely on irrigation systems to ensure their plants receive adequate water, which in turn sustains food production.
    • In wellness practices, such as yoga and meditation, water is often used as a symbol of purification and renewal, helping individuals to calm their minds and bodies.
    • In many cultures, water is considered sacred and is used in rituals and ceremonies to symbolize cleansing and new beginnings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of water as a life-giving force, essential for both physical and spiritual nourishment.
    • Its application in real life is evident in various fields, from agriculture to personal well-being, showcasing the universal importance of water.
    • By recognizing the soothing and nurturing qualities of water, we can foster a deeper appreciation for this vital resource and its role in sustaining life.
