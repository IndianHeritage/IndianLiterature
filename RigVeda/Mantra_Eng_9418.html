तदिन् नव अस्य परिषद्वानो अग्मन पुरू सदन्तो नार्षदं बिभित्सन् |विषुष्णस्य संग्रथितं अनर्वा विदत पुरुप्रजातस्य गुहा यत ||
tad in nv asya pariṣadvāno agman purū sadanto nārṣadam bibhitsan | vi śuṣṇasya saṁgrathitam anarvā vidat puruprajātasya guhā yat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • in: in
    • nv: new
    • asya: of this
    • pariṣadvāno: surrounded by the assembly
    • agman: came
    • purū: many
    • sadanto: with good intentions
    • nārṣadam: the one who is not to be harmed
    • bibhitsan: desiring to protect
    • vi: in a special way
    • śuṣṇasya: of the one who is powerful
    • saṁgrathitam: gathered together
    • anarvā: without any obstacles
    • vidat: knowing
    • puruprajātasya: of the one who has many progeny
    • guhā: the cave or secret
    • yat: which
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence that comes into the assembly of beings, embodying good intentions and a desire to protect.
    • The phrase "surrounded by the assembly" suggests the importance of community and collective strength.
    • The reference to "the one who is not to be harmed" emphasizes the need for safeguarding those who are vulnerable.
    • The "cave" symbolizes the inner depths of knowledge and wisdom, indicating that true understanding comes from within.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a leader who embodies these qualities can create a supportive environment where team members feel safe and valued.
    • In community service, volunteers who come together to protect and uplift the marginalized reflect the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal relationships, being protective and nurturing towards loved ones aligns with the mantra's message of safeguarding and good intentions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of community, protection, and inner wisdom.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as leadership, community service, and personal relationships, where the values of support and safeguarding are essential.
    • By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and compassionate society.
