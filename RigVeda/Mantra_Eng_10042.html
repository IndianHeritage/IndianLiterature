भोजा जिग्युः सुरभिं योनिम अग्रे भोजा जिग्युर वध्वं याऽ सुवासाः |भोजा जिग्युर अन्तःपेयं सुरायाः भोजा जिग्युर ये अहूताः प्रयन्ति ||
bhojā jigyuḥ surabhiṁ yonim agre bhojā jigyur vadhva1ṁ yā suvāsāḥ | bhojā jigyur antaḥpeyaṁ surāyā bhojā jigyur ye ahūtāḥ prayanti ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "Those who partake of the offerings, who are fragrant and pure, who are the source of nourishment, may they be satisfied with our offerings. Those who are the essence of the divine, may they accept our offerings and grant us their blessings."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • bhojā: Refers to offerings or food that is shared.
    • jigyuḥ: Implies satisfaction or fulfillment.
    • surabhiṁ: Denotes something fragrant or divine.
    • yonim: Refers to a source or origin.
    • agre: Means 'in front' or 'before'.
    • vadhvaṁ: Refers to the essence or the divine nature.
    • suvāsāḥ: Indicates purity or pleasantness.
    • antaḥpeyaṁ: Refers to the inner essence or the core of something.
    • surāyā: Relates to the divine or celestial beings.
    • ye ahūtāḥ: Refers to those who are invoked or called upon.
    • prayanti: Means to proceed or to move towards.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a traditional Indian household, during festivals, offerings of food and flowers are made to deities. This practice embodies the essence of the mantra, where the offerings are made with the hope that the divine will be satisfied and bless the household.
    • In a corporate setting, when a team completes a project, they might celebrate with a meal together. This act of sharing food can be seen as a modern interpretation of the mantra, where the team seeks to acknowledge their collective effort and invite positive outcomes for future projects.
  • Significance: The mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings and the act of sharing. It highlights the connection between the material and the divine, suggesting that when we offer something with sincerity, we invite blessings and satisfaction into our lives. This can be applied in various contexts, from personal rituals to communal gatherings, reinforcing the idea that generosity and gratitude are fundamental to human experience.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of offerings in our lives, whether they are physical, emotional, or spiritual. It encourages us to approach life with a sense of gratitude and to recognize the interconnectedness of all beings. By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can foster a sense of community and harmony in our daily interactions.
