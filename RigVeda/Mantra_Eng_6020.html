हृत्सु पीतासो युध्यन्ते दुरमदासो न सुरायाम् |ऊधार न नग्ना जरण्ते ||
hṛtsu pītāso yudhyante durmadāso na surāyām | ūdhar na nagnā jarante ||
  • hṛtsu: This word translates to "in the hearts" or "within the hearts." It signifies the emotional and spiritual core of individuals.
  • pītāso: This term means "those who are intoxicated" or "those who are under the influence." It often refers to individuals who are overwhelmed by desires or passions.
  • yudhyante: This means "they fight" or "they engage in battle." It indicates a struggle or conflict, which can be both internal and external.
  • durmadāso: This translates to "those who are deluded" or "those who are misguided." It refers to individuals who are not in touch with reality or who are led astray by their desires.
  • na surāyām: This phrase means "not in the realm of the divine" or "not among the gods." It suggests a separation from higher spiritual truths or divine guidance.
  • ūdhar: This word means "to uplift" or "to elevate." It signifies the act of raising oneself or others to a higher state of being.
  • na nagnā: This translates to "not naked" or "not exposed." It implies a sense of protection or covering, both physically and metaphorically.
  • jarante: This means "they decay" or "they perish." It indicates the transient nature of life and the inevitability of decline.

The mantra speaks to the internal struggles faced by individuals who are overwhelmed by their desires and passions. It highlights the conflict that arises when one is not aligned with higher spiritual truths. The phrase "in the hearts" suggests that these battles are deeply personal and emotional, while "those who are intoxicated" points to the distractions that lead one away from clarity and purpose.


In real life, this mantra can be applied to situations where individuals find themselves caught in the chaos of their desires, leading to conflicts in relationships, careers, or personal growth. For example, someone might be so focused on material success that they neglect their emotional well-being or relationships with loved ones. The reminder to "uplift" oneself encourages individuals to seek higher truths and values, rather than getting lost in the superficial aspects of life.


In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of self-awareness and alignment with one's true values. It encourages individuals to recognize the internal battles they face and to strive for a higher state of being, free from the delusions of desire. By doing so, one can cultivate a more meaningful and fulfilling life, grounded in spiritual truth and emotional integrity.
