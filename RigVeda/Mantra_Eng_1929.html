एष वां स्तोमो अश्विनाव् अकारी मानेभिर् मगवानां सुवृक्तिः |यातं वर्तिस् तनयाय त्मने चागस्त्ये नासत्यां मदन्ता ||
eṣa vāṁ stomo aśvināv akāri mānebhir maghavānā suvṛkti | yātaṁ vartis tanayāya tmane cāgastye nāsatyā madantā ||
  • eṣa vāṁ stomo aśvināv akāri: This phrase can be translated as "This hymn is dedicated to the Ashvins." The Ashvins are twin deities in Vedic mythology, representing health, medicine, and the dawn. They are often invoked for their healing powers and are seen as bringers of light and prosperity.
  • mānebhir maghavānā suvṛkti: This part means "with the blessings of the powerful ones." Here, "maghavān" refers to the mighty or powerful beings, often associated with wealth and abundance. "Suvṛkti" implies good deeds or auspicious actions. This line emphasizes the importance of seeking blessings from those who are powerful and virtuous.
  • yātaṁ vartis tanayāya tmane: This translates to "May the path be favorable for you." It suggests a wish for a smooth journey or progress in life. "Tanayāya" refers to offspring or descendants, indicating a focus on future generations and their well-being.
  • cāgastye nāsatyā madantā: This can be interpreted as "the Nāsatyas (the Ashvins) are the ones who bring joy." The term "madantā" refers to intoxication or bliss, suggesting that the blessings of the Ashvins lead to happiness and fulfillment in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the significance of invoking divine blessings for health, prosperity, and a favorable journey in life. It emphasizes the importance of good deeds and the role of powerful beings in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable during rituals, prayers, or moments of seeking guidance and support, especially when embarking on new ventures or facing challenges. By acknowledging the divine and seeking their blessings, individuals can cultivate a sense of hope and positivity, fostering a harmonious environment for themselves and their families.
