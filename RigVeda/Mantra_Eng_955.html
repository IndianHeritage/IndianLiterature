युयं तत् सत्यशवस आविष्कर्त महित्वना | विध्यतां विद्युतां रक्षः ||
yūyaṁ tat satyaśavasa āviṣ karta mahitvanā | vidhyatā vidyutā rakṣaḥ ||
  • yūyaṁ - This word translates to "you all" or "you together," indicating a collective or community aspect.
  • tat - Meaning "that," it refers to something specific or significant, often pointing to a higher truth or reality.
  • satyaśavasa - This compound word can be broken down into "satya," meaning "truth," and "śavasa," which can imply "possessing" or "embodying." Together, it suggests "those who embody truth."
  • āviṣ - This means "to manifest" or "to reveal," indicating the act of bringing something into existence or making it known.
  • karta - Translated as "doer" or "creator," it emphasizes action and the role of individuals in shaping their reality.
  • mahitvanā - This word means "with greatness" or "in a grand manner," suggesting that the actions taken should be significant and impactful.
  • vidhyatā - Meaning "by knowledge" or "through wisdom," it highlights the importance of understanding and insight in the process of creation.
  • vidyutā - This translates to "like lightning," symbolizing speed, power, and illumination, suggesting that the truth and knowledge can strike suddenly and with great force.
  • rakṣaḥ - Meaning "protection" or "guardianship," it indicates the need for safeguarding what is valuable, whether it be truth, knowledge, or community.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the collective pursuit of truth and the importance of manifesting it through wise and impactful actions. It suggests that when individuals come together to embody truth and knowledge, they can create a powerful force for good, akin to the sudden illumination of lightning. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community initiatives, social justice movements, or collaborative projects where the focus is on truth, wisdom, and protection of values. The significance lies in the idea that collective action rooted in truth can lead to meaningful change and protection of what is essential for society's well-being.
