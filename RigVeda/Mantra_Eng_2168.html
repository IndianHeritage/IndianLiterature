यस्माद इन्द्राद बृहतः किं चनेम ऋते विश्वान्य अस्मिन संभरताधि वीर्याः |जटहरे सोमं तन्वी सहे महो हस्ते वज्रम भरति शीरषाणि क्रतुम् ||
yasmād indrād bṛhataḥ kiṁ canem ṛte viśvāny asmin sambhṛtādhi vīryā | jaṭhare somaṁ tanvī3 saho maho haste vajram bharati śīrṣaṇi kratum ||
  • yasmād - "from whom" or "because of whom": This word indicates a source or origin, suggesting a divine or powerful entity.
  • indrād - "from Indra": Referring to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic mythology, associated with rain, thunder, and strength.
  • bṛhataḥ - "great" or "mighty": This term emphasizes the vastness and power of the entity being referred to.
  • kiṁ canem - "anything" or "anyone": This phrase suggests that nothing can exist without the influence of the divine.
  • ṛte - "except" or "without": This word indicates a condition or limitation, emphasizing the necessity of the divine presence.
  • viśvāny - "all" or "universal": This term signifies inclusiveness, referring to everything in existence.
  • asmin - "in this": Referring to the current context or situation, indicating the present moment.
  • sambhṛtādhi - "gathered" or "assembled": This word suggests the collection or accumulation of strength or resources.
  • vīryā - "strength" or "power": Referring to the inherent energy or vitality present in all beings.
  • jaṭhare - "in the belly" or "in the womb": This term symbolizes the source of nourishment and life.
  • somaṁ - "soma": A sacred drink in Vedic culture, associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
  • tanvī - "delicate" or "subtle": This word refers to the refined nature of the essence being discussed.
  • saho - "with strength" or "with power": This term emphasizes the strength that accompanies the essence.
  • maho - "great" or "mighty": Reinforcing the idea of vastness and power.
  • haste - "in the hand": Symbolizing control or mastery over something.
  • vajram - "thunderbolt" or "weapon": Referring to a powerful tool or force, often associated with Indra.
  • bharati - "carries" or "bears": This word indicates the act of holding or supporting something significant.
  • śīrṣaṇi - "on the head": Symbolizing authority or the pinnacle of power.
  • kratum - "intention" or "sacrifice": Referring to the purpose or goal behind actions, often linked to rituals.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine source of strength and power. It highlights the importance of recognizing the divine influence in our lives and the necessity of unity and strength in achieving our goals. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to teamwork, where collective strength leads to success, or in personal endeavors where one seeks inspiration and support from a higher power or community. The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of acknowledging the sources of our strength and the interconnectedness of all existence.
