यत् ते अपो यद् ओषधीर मनो जगाम दूरकम् |तत् त आ वर्तयामसिह क्षयाय जीवसे ||
yat te apo yad oṣadhīr mano jagāma dūrakam | tat ta ā vartayāmasīha kṣayāya jīvase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yat: that which
    • te: of you
    • apo: waters
    • yad: that which
    • oṣadhīr: herbs or plants
    • mano: mind
    • jagāma: has gone
    • dūrakam: far away
    • tat: that
    • ta: you
    • ā: indeed
    • vartayāmasi: we are bringing forth
    • ha: indeed
    • kṣayāya: for the purpose of destruction
    • jīvase: for life
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the connection between the waters and the herbs, emphasizing the importance of nature in sustaining life.
    • The phrase "yat te apo" suggests a reverence for the waters, which are essential for growth and nourishment.
    • "Yad oṣadhīr" refers to the plants that thrive with the help of water, indicating the interdependence of elements in nature.
    • The line "mano jagāma dūrakam" implies that the mind can wander far, perhaps suggesting that our thoughts can lead us away from the natural world.
    • Finally, "tat ta ā vartayāmasīha kṣayāya jīvase" indicates a commitment to bringing forth what is necessary for life, even if it means confronting destruction.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, understanding the relationship between water and plants is crucial. Farmers often rely on irrigation systems to ensure that crops receive adequate water, which in turn supports their growth and sustenance.
    • In modern medicine, many herbal remedies are derived from plants that thrive in specific water conditions, highlighting the importance of natural resources in health and wellness.
    • Environmental conservation efforts often focus on protecting water sources to maintain the health of ecosystems, demonstrating the interconnectedness of water, plants, and life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of nature and the interconnectedness of water, plants, and life.
    • It serves as a reminder to respect and protect our natural resources, as they are vital for our survival and well-being.
    • In a world facing environmental challenges, the teachings of this mantra encourage sustainable practices and a deeper appreciation for the natural world.
