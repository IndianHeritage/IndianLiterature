यमाद अहं वैवस्वतात् सुभन्दोर्मना आभारम् |जीवातवे न मृत्यवे 'थो अरिष्टतातये ||
yamād ahaṁ vaivasvatāt subandhor mana ābharam | jīvātave na mṛtyave 'tho ariṣṭatātaye ||
  • yamād: This word translates to "from whom" or "from that," indicating a source or origin.
  • ahaṁ: Meaning "I" or "myself," it signifies the speaker's personal identity.
  • vaivasvatāt: Referring to "Vaivasvata," which is another name for Yama, the god of death and the ruler of the afterlife in Hindu mythology.
  • subandhor: This term can be interpreted as "of good connections" or "of good relationships," suggesting a positive association or bond.
  • mana: Meaning "mind" or "thought," it emphasizes the importance of mental focus and intention.
  • ābharam: This translates to "I bring" or "I carry," indicating an action of bringing forth or presenting something.
  • jīvātave: Meaning "for the living," it highlights the focus on life and existence.
  • na: This word means "not," negating the following term.
  • mṛtyave: Referring to "death," it signifies the end of life or mortality.
  • 'tho: This can be interpreted as "or," presenting an alternative or choice.
  • ariṣṭatātaye: This term can be understood as "to the state of being free from obstacles" or "to the state of well-being," indicating a desire for a life without hindrances.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation that seeks to connect the speaker with the divine, particularly with Yama, the god of death. It expresses a desire to bring forth positive thoughts and intentions for the living, while also acknowledging the inevitability of death. The speaker aims to cultivate a mindset that is free from obstacles and conducive to well-being.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Facing Challenges: When encountering difficulties, one can recite this mantra to remind themselves to focus on positive thoughts and to seek support from their connections, thereby overcoming obstacles.
  • Mindfulness and Meditation: Practicing mindfulness by reflecting on the impermanence of life can help individuals appreciate their current existence and relationships, fostering a sense of gratitude.
  • End-of-Life Care: In contexts such as hospice care, this mantra can serve as a reminder to honor the lives of those nearing death, encouraging a peaceful transition and a focus on the quality of life.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the importance of maintaining a positive mindset and strong relationships while acknowledging the reality of mortality. It serves as a reminder to live fully and mindfully, fostering well-being and resilience in the face of life's challenges.
