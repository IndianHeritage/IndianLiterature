राजा मेधाभिर ईयते पवमानो मनाव अधि |अन्तरिक्षेण यातवे ||
rājā medhābhir īyate pavamāno manāv adhi | antarikṣeṇa yātave ||
  • rājā: This word translates to "king" or "ruler." In a broader sense, it can represent authority, leadership, and governance.
  • medhābhir: This term means "with intelligence" or "with wisdom." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in leadership.
  • īyate: This means "is moving" or "is progressing." It indicates action and the dynamic nature of leadership.
  • pavamāno: This word refers to "purifying" or "sublime." It suggests that the actions of the king should be noble and virtuous.
  • manāv: This translates to "mind" or "thought." It highlights the significance of mental clarity and intention in decision-making.
  • adhi: This means "above" or "over." It signifies a higher perspective or authority.
  • antarikṣeṇa: This term refers to "in the atmosphere" or "in the space." It can symbolize the broader context in which leadership operates.
  • yātave: This means "to the one who travels" or "to the one who moves forward." It indicates progress and the journey of leadership.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for a ruler who is wise, noble, and progressive, leading with a clear mind and a purifying spirit. It emphasizes the importance of intelligence and virtue in leadership, suggesting that a true king is one who not only governs but also inspires and uplifts those around him.


In real life, this mantra can be applied to various leadership scenarios, such as:

  • Political Leadership: Politicians and leaders in government can embody the qualities of wisdom and virtue, making decisions that benefit society as a whole.
  • Corporate Leadership: Business leaders can apply this mantra by leading with integrity, fostering a culture of knowledge and innovation, and making ethical decisions that promote the well-being of employees and stakeholders.
  • Community Leadership: Community leaders and activists can use the principles of this mantra to inspire collective action, promote social justice, and create positive change in their communities.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a timeless reminder of the qualities that define effective leadership. It encourages leaders to act with wisdom, purity, and a forward-thinking mindset, ultimately contributing to the betterment of society. By embodying these principles, leaders can create a positive impact in their respective fields, fostering an environment of growth, understanding, and progress.
