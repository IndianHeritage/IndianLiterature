आयम् मित्रो नमस्यः सुशेवराजाऽसुक्षात्रो जनिषṭ वेधाः |तस्य वयं सुमतौ यज्ञियस्यापि भद्रे सुमनसे स्याम ||
ayam mitro namasyaḥ suśevo rājā sukṣatro ajaniṣṭa vedhāḥ | tasya vayaṁ sumatau yajñiyasyāpi bhadre saumanase syāma ||
  • ayam mitro namasyaḥ: This phrase translates to "This is the friend, we bow to him." In this context, 'Mitro' refers to a divine friend or ally, emphasizing the importance of friendship and camaraderie in life. For example, in a workplace, having supportive colleagues can lead to a more productive and harmonious environment.
  • suśevo rājā: This means "the well-doer king." It signifies a ruler who acts for the welfare of his people. A real-life example can be seen in leaders who prioritize social welfare programs, ensuring that their citizens have access to education, healthcare, and employment opportunities.
  • sukṣatro ajaniṣṭa vedhāḥ: This translates to "the one who has achieved great things through wisdom." It highlights the value of knowledge and wisdom in achieving success. For instance, a scientist who conducts groundbreaking research exemplifies this idea, as their wisdom leads to advancements that benefit society.
  • tasya vayaṁ sumatau: This means "we are of good intention towards him." It reflects the importance of having positive intentions and goodwill towards others. In everyday life, this can be seen in acts of kindness, such as helping a neighbor or volunteering in the community.
  • yajñiyasyāpi bhadre: This translates to "even in the sacred offerings." It emphasizes the significance of rituals and offerings in fostering a sense of community and spirituality. For example, during festivals, people come together to celebrate and perform rituals that strengthen their bonds and cultural identity.
  • saumanase syāma: This means "may we be of a pleasant disposition." It encourages individuals to cultivate a positive and harmonious mindset. In real life, maintaining a positive attitude can lead to better relationships and improved mental health, as seen in practices like mindfulness and gratitude.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of friendship, leadership, wisdom, goodwill, community, and positivity. Its significance lies in the reminder to foster these qualities in our lives. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious society, whether in personal relationships, community involvement, or leadership roles. The teachings of the RigVeda continue to be applicable in modern life, guiding us towards a path of mutual respect, understanding, and collective well-being.
