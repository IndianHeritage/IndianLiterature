पुरुद्रप्सा अञ्जिमन्तः सुदानवस् त्वेषसन्दृशो अनवभ्रराधसः |सुजातासो जनुषा रुक्मवक्षसो दिवो अर्का अमृतं नाम भेजिरे ||
purudrapsā añjimantaḥ sudānavas tveṣasaṁdṛśo anavabhrarādhasaḥ | sujātāso januṣā rukmavakṣaso divo arkā amṛtaṁ nāma bhejire ||
  • Purudrapsā: This term can be interpreted as "those who are rich in offerings" or "abundant in sacrifices." It signifies the importance of generosity and the act of giving in various forms.
  • Añjimantaḥ: This word refers to "those who are active" or "those who are engaged in action." It emphasizes the value of being proactive and involved in one's duties and responsibilities.
  • Sudānavas: This can be understood as "those who are well-fed" or "those who have good nourishment." It highlights the significance of physical and spiritual sustenance for a fulfilling life.
  • Tveṣasaṁdṛśo: This term means "those who are endowed with strength" or "those who possess vigor." It points to the importance of inner strength and resilience in overcoming challenges.
  • Anavabhrarādhasaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "those who are free from obstacles" or "those who have no hindrances." It signifies the pursuit of a life without barriers, both physically and mentally.
  • Sujātāso: This term means "well-born" or "noble." It emphasizes the importance of good character and ethical upbringing in shaping an individual's destiny.
  • Januṣā: This word refers to "the body" or "the physical form." It highlights the connection between the physical and spiritual aspects of life.
  • Rukmavakṣaso: This can be interpreted as "those with golden chests" or "those who are radiant." It symbolizes the idea of inner beauty and the richness of character.
  • Divo: This term means "of the heavens" or "divine." It signifies the connection between the earthly and the celestial, suggesting that our actions have a higher purpose.
  • Arkā: This word refers to "light" or "radiance." It symbolizes knowledge, enlightenment, and the pursuit of truth.
  • Amṛtaṁ: This term means "immortal" or "eternal." It signifies the quest for everlasting wisdom and the pursuit of spiritual truths.
  • Nāma bhejire: This phrase can be interpreted as "they have attained the name" or "they have achieved recognition." It emphasizes the importance of legacy and the impact of one's actions on future generations.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of a fulfilling life through the virtues of generosity, action, nourishment, strength, and nobility. It serves as a reminder that by embodying these qualities, individuals can overcome obstacles and achieve a higher purpose. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, personal development, and leadership. By striving to be well-rounded individuals who contribute positively to society, we can create a legacy that inspires others and fosters a sense of interconnectedness. Ultimately, the mantra encourages us to seek both material and spiritual wealth, leading to a balanced and meaningful existence.
