आ प्र याता मरुतो विष्णो अश्विनā पूषण माकीनेन धियā |इन्द्र आ यातु प्रथमः सनीषुभिर वृषा यो वृत्रहा गृणे ||
ā pra yāta maruto viṣṇo aśvinā pūṣan mākīnayā dhiyā | indra ā yātu prathamaḥ saniṣyubhir vṛṣā yo vṛtrahā gṛṇe ||
  • Mantra Breakdown:
    • ā pra yāta: This phrase can be interpreted as "come forth" or "approach." It signifies a call for divine presence and assistance.
    • maruto: Refers to the Maruts, the storm deities associated with strength and power. They symbolize the forces of nature and the energy of the storm.
    • viṣṇo: This refers to Lord Vishnu, the preserver in Hindu mythology, representing protection and sustenance.
    • aśvinā: The Aśvins are twin deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize healing and new beginnings.
    • pūṣan: A deity associated with nourishment and prosperity, representing the sustenance of life.
    • mākīnayā dhiyā: This can be interpreted as "with a focused mind" or "with wisdom." It emphasizes the importance of clarity and intention in one's thoughts.
    • indra: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods, associated with rain, storms, and war. He represents strength and leadership.
    • ā yātu: This means "may he come" or "let him arrive," indicating a desire for divine intervention.
    • prathamaḥ: Meaning "first" or "foremost," it signifies the importance of the deity being invoked.
    • saniṣyubhir: This term can be understood as "with the best of intentions" or "with good thoughts," highlighting the need for purity in one's desires.
    • vṛṣā: This refers to strength or power, often associated with the might of the deities.
    • yo vṛtrahā: This means "who slays Vritra," referring to the mythological serpent or dragon that represents chaos and obstacles.
    • gṛṇe: This translates to "I sing" or "I praise," indicating an act of devotion and reverence.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra is a call for divine assistance from various deities, emphasizing the importance of collaboration between different forces of nature and divine entities.
    • In real life, this can be applied in situations where one seeks guidance and support in overcoming challenges, much like invoking the help of friends or mentors during difficult times.
    • The emphasis on focused thought and intention suggests that clarity in one's goals and desires is crucial for success, akin to setting clear objectives in personal or professional life.
    • The invocation of strength and protection from deities like Indra and Vishnu can be seen as a metaphor for seeking inner strength and resilience when facing adversities.
    • Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of seeking help, and the power of intention in achieving one's goals.
