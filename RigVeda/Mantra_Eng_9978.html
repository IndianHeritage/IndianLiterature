मन्त्र: अन्तर यच्छ जिघांसतो वज्रम इन्द्राभिदासतः |दासस्य वा मगवन्न आर्यस्य वा सन्तर यवया वधम् ||
antar yaccha jighāṁsato vajram indrābhidāsataḥ | dāsasya vā maghavann āryasya vā sanutar yavayā vadham ||
  • Mantra: "antar yaccha jighāṁsato vajram indrābhidāsataḥ | dāsasya vā maghavann āryasya vā sanutar yavayā vadham ||"
  • Translation: "O Indra, grant us the strength (vajra) to overcome those who wish to harm us from within. Whether they are our enemies or those who are dear to us, may we be protected from their malice."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • antar: within, inside
    • yaccha: grant, give
    • jighāṁsato: those who wish to harm
    • vajram: strength, power, thunderbolt
    • indrābhidāsataḥ: from the might of Indra
    • dāsasya: of the servant, or one who is devoted
    • vā: or
    • maghavann: powerful, mighty
    • āryasya: of the noble
    • sanutar: protection, safeguard
    • yavayā: from harm
    • vadham: destruction, death
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the deity Indra, who is often associated with strength and protection in Vedic literature.
    • The phrase "grant us the strength" emphasizes the need for inner strength to face adversities, whether they come from external enemies or internal conflicts.
    • The mention of "those who wish to harm us" can be interpreted in various real-life scenarios, such as dealing with workplace rivalry, personal conflicts, or even self-doubt.
    • In a practical sense, this mantra can be recited during times of stress or when facing challenges, serving as a reminder to seek inner strength and resilience.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate environment, an employee may face competition from colleagues. Reciting this mantra can help them focus on their strengths and navigate the challenges with confidence.
    • In personal relationships, when conflicts arise, this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek understanding and protection from negative influences.
    • During times of self-doubt or anxiety, individuals can use this mantra to reinforce their inner strength and resolve to overcome their fears.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of inner strength and protection against both external and internal adversities.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals to seek resilience and courage in the face of challenges.
    • In real life, it serves as a powerful reminder to cultivate strength, whether in personal struggles, professional challenges, or emotional turmoil.
