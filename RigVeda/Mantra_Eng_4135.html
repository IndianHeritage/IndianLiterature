युयम् अस्मान् नयत वस्यो अच् छा निर अंहतिभ्यः मरुतो गृणानाः |जुषध्वं नो हव्यदातिं यजत्रा वयं स्याम पतयो रयीणाम् ||
yūyam asmān nayata vasyo acchā nir aṁhatibhyo maruto gṛṇānāḥ | juṣadhvaṁ no havyadātiṁ yajatrā vayaṁ syāma patayo rayīṇām ||
  • yūyam: This word translates to "you" in the plural form, indicating a collective group, often referring to deities or forces of nature.
  • asmān: Meaning "us," it signifies the speaker's group or community, emphasizing a connection with the divine.
  • nayata: This means "lead" or "guide," suggesting a request for direction and support from the divine forces.
  • vasyo: Translated as "submissive" or "controlled," it implies a desire for the forces to be favorable and benevolent towards the speaker.
  • acchā: This word means "good" or "auspicious," indicating a wish for positive outcomes.
  • nir: Meaning "without," it denotes a negation, often used to express the absence of something undesirable.
  • aṁhatibhyo: This term refers to "obstacles" or "hindrances," suggesting a plea for protection from challenges.
  • maruto: Referring to the "Maruts," the storm deities, it invokes powerful natural forces that can bring both destruction and renewal.
  • gṛṇānāḥ: This means "singing" or "praising," indicating an act of devotion and reverence towards the divine.
  • juṣadhvaṁ: Translated as "may you enjoy," it expresses a wish for the deities to take pleasure in the offerings made.
  • no: Meaning "our," it emphasizes the collective identity of the community making the offering.
  • havyadātiṁ: This refers to "the offering" or "sacrifice," highlighting the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • yajatrā: Meaning "in the sacrifice," it underscores the context of the ritual being performed.
  • vayaṁ: This means "we," reinforcing the communal aspect of the prayer.
  • syāma: Translated as "may we be," it expresses a desire for a collective state of being.
  • patayo: This word means "lords" or "masters," referring to the divine beings being addressed.
  • rayīṇām: Meaning "of wealth" or "prosperity," it signifies the blessings sought from the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra is a heartfelt invocation for guidance, protection, and blessings from the divine forces, particularly the Maruts. It emphasizes the importance of community and collective effort in seeking auspiciousness and prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as during communal rituals, festivals, or any gathering where people seek divine favor and support. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine, encouraging a harmonious relationship with nature and the universe.
