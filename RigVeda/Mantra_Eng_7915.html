एष देवः शुभायतेऽधि योनाव अमर्त्यः |वृत्रहां देववीतमः ||
eṣa devaḥ śubhāyate 'dhi yonāv amartyaḥ | vṛtrahā devavītamaḥ ||
  • eṣa devaḥ: This phrase translates to "this deity" or "this god." It signifies the presence of a divine force or energy that is being invoked.
  • śubhāyate: Meaning "is auspicious" or "brings good fortune." This word emphasizes the positive attributes associated with the deity, suggesting that the divine presence is beneficial.
  • 'dhi yonāv: This can be interpreted as "in the realms of creation" or "in the source of existence." It points to the foundational aspects of life and the universe, indicating that the deity is connected to the origins of all things.
  • amartyaḥ: This word means "immortal" or "beyond death." It suggests that the deity embodies eternal qualities, transcending the limitations of the physical world.
  • vṛtrahā: This term translates to "the slayer of Vritra," referring to a mythological serpent or dragon that represents chaos and obstacles. The deity is portrayed as a force that overcomes challenges and brings order.
  • devavītamaḥ: This means "the most revered among the gods." It highlights the supreme status of the deity, indicating that this divine presence is highly respected and holds significant power.

In summary, this mantra invokes a deity that is auspicious, immortal, and a conqueror of chaos, emphasizing the importance of divine intervention in overcoming life's challenges. The mantra serves as a reminder of the positive forces in the universe that can guide and protect individuals in their endeavors.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be recited during times of difficulty or uncertainty, invoking the divine for guidance and support.
  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek the positive and immortal aspects of their own nature, fostering resilience and strength.
  • In community settings, it can be used to promote harmony and cooperation, reminding people of the divine qualities that can help overcome conflicts.

Ultimately, the mantra serves as a powerful affirmation of faith in the divine's ability to bring auspiciousness and order to our lives, encouraging us to connect with these higher energies in our daily experiences.
