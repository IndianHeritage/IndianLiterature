तस्माद् विराज् अजायत विराजोऽधि पूरुषः |स जATO अत्यरिच्यत पश्चाद् भूमिम् अथो पुरः ||
tasmād virāḻ ajāyata virājo adhi pūruṣaḥ | sa jāto aty aricyata paścād bhūmim atho puraḥ ||
  • tasmād virāḻ ajāyata virājo adhi pūruṣaḥ: This line can be translated to "From that (the cosmic being), the virāḻ (the great being) was born; the virājo (the great one) is above the puruṣa (the person or the individual)."
    • The term "virāḻ" refers to a cosmic entity or a universal being, symbolizing the vastness and interconnectedness of existence.
    • "Puruṣa" represents the individual self or consciousness, indicating that the individual is a part of a larger cosmic reality.
  • sa jāto aty aricyata paścād bhūmim atho puraḥ: This translates to "He (the virāḻ) was born and then extended beyond the earth and the universe."
    • This line emphasizes the idea of expansion and the continuous evolution of the universe from a singular source.
    • The phrase "aty aricyata" suggests that the virāḻ transcends physical boundaries, indicating a spiritual or metaphysical dimension.

Conclusion:

  • The mantra reflects the philosophical concept of the unity of the cosmos and the individual, illustrating how all beings are interconnected.
  • In real life, this can be applied to the understanding of our place in the universe, promoting a sense of responsibility towards the environment and society.
  • It encourages individuals to recognize their connection to a greater whole, fostering compassion and empathy towards others.
  • This mantra can be significant in practices such as meditation, where one contemplates the relationship between the self and the universe, leading to personal growth and spiritual awakening.
