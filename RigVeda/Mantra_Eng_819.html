मन्त्र: तिस्रो यद अग्ने शरदस् त्वाम इच्छुचिं घृतेन शुचयः सपर्ण्यान् |नामानि चिद दधिरे यज्ञियान्य असूदयन्त तन्वः सुजाताः ||
tisro yad agne śaradas tvām ic chuciṁ ghṛtena śucayaḥ saparyān | nāmāni cid dadhire yajñiyāny asūdayanta tanva1ḥ sujātāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tisro: three
    • yad: that which
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • śaradas: in the autumn
    • tvām: you
    • ic: desire
    • chuciṁ: pure
    • ghṛtena: with ghee (clarified butter)
    • śucayaḥ: pure offerings
    • saparyān: worship
    • nāmāni: names
    • cid: indeed
    • dadhire: they hold
    • yajñiyāny: those meant for sacrifice
    • asūdayanta: they purify
    • tanvaḥ: bodies
    • sujātāḥ: well-born
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Agni, the fire god, and speaks of the purity and sanctity associated with offerings made to him.
    • The phrase "tisro yad agne" suggests a triad of offerings or aspects that are significant in the worship of Agni.
    • It emphasizes the importance of purity in the offerings, represented by "ghṛtena śucayaḥ," indicating that offerings should be made with pure ghee.
    • The mantra also highlights the significance of names and their power in rituals, as "nāmāni cid dadhire" suggests that the names associated with the offerings hold great importance.
    • Finally, it conveys that these offerings purify the bodies of those who are well-born, indicating a connection between spiritual purity and physical well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, such as weddings or religious ceremonies, the use of pure ingredients like ghee in offerings reflects the same principle of purity emphasized in this mantra.
    • The concept of names holding power can be seen in various cultures where names are chosen with care, believing they influence the individual's destiny.
    • In health and wellness practices, the idea of purifying the body through diet and lifestyle choices resonates with the mantra's emphasis on purity and well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of purity in offerings and the spiritual significance of names in rituals.
    • It teaches us that our intentions and the quality of what we offer in any aspect of life—be it spiritual, personal, or professional—can have profound effects on our well-being and the outcomes we experience.
    • In real life, applying the principles of purity, intention, and respect in our actions can lead to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
