तं नव्यसी हृदा आ जayamānam अस्मत सुखीरतिर्मधुजिह्वम् अश्याः |यम् ऋत्विजो वृजन माणुषासः प्रयस्वन्त आयवो जिजानन्त ||
taṁ navyasī hṛda ā jāyamānam asmat sukīrtir madhujihvam aśyāḥ | yam ṛtvijo vṛjane mānuṣāsaḥ prayasvanta āyavo jījananta ||
  • taṁ - This word refers to "that" or "him," indicating a focus on a specific entity or concept.
  • navyasī - This term can be interpreted as "new" or "fresh," suggesting something that is emerging or being born.
  • hṛda - Meaning "heart" or "mind," it signifies the emotional or intellectual center of a being.
  • ā jāyamānam - This phrase translates to "being born" or "coming into existence," emphasizing the process of creation or emergence.
  • asmat - This word means "our," indicating a collective identity or shared experience.
  • sukīrtir - Translated as "good fame" or "auspicious reputation," it highlights the importance of positive recognition in society.
  • madhujihvam - This term can be understood as "honey-tongued," referring to someone who speaks sweetly or eloquently.
  • aśyāḥ - Meaning "those who are wise" or "intelligent," it points to the value of wisdom in human interactions.
  • yam - This word means "whom," indicating a connection to a specific person or entity.
  • ṛtvijo - Referring to "the priests" or "ritualists," it emphasizes the role of spiritual leaders in society.
  • vṛjane - This term means "in the act of choosing" or "selecting," highlighting the importance of decision-making.
  • mānuṣāsaḥ - Translated as "humans" or "mankind," it signifies the collective human experience.
  • prayasvanta - This word means "those who strive" or "diligent," emphasizing the value of effort and hard work.
  • āyavo - Meaning "long life" or "longevity," it points to the desire for a prosperous and enduring existence.
  • jījananta - This term translates to "they create" or "they bring forth," indicating the act of bringing something into being.

In summary, this mantra from the Rigveda speaks to the themes of creation, collective identity, wisdom, and the importance of positive reputation. It emphasizes the role of individuals and communities in striving for a good life, marked by wisdom and eloquence. The mantra suggests that through collective effort and the guidance of wise leaders, humans can create a prosperous existence.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

This mantra is significant as it encapsulates the essence of human experience—our desires for recognition, wisdom, and a fulfilling life. In real life, it can be applied in various contexts:

  • In personal development, individuals can strive to cultivate wisdom and eloquence in their communication, enhancing their relationships and reputation.
  • In community settings, this mantra encourages collective efforts towards common goals, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.
  • In professional environments, it highlights the importance of hard work and the pursuit of excellence, leading to recognition and success.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of human experiences and the potential for collective growth and prosperity through wisdom and effort.
