शं न इन्द्राग्नि भवताम् अवोभिः शं न इन्द्रावरुणा रातहव्याः |शम् इन्द्रासोमा सुविताया शं योः शं न इन्द्रापूषणā वाजसातौ ||
śaṁ na indrāgnī bhavatām avobhiḥ śaṁ na indrāvaruṇā rātahavyā | śam indrāsomā suvitāya śaṁ yoḥ śaṁ na indrāpūṣaṇā vājasātau ||
  • śaṁ na indrāgnī bhavatām avobhiḥ: This phrase can be translated as "May peace come to us from Indra and Agni." Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength and power, while Agni is the fire god, symbolizing energy and transformation. In real life, this can be understood as a prayer for strength and energy in our endeavors, whether in personal challenges or professional pursuits.
  • śaṁ na indrāvaruṇā rātahavyā: Here, the mantra invokes peace from Indra and Varuna, the god of water and cosmic order. This signifies the importance of balance and harmony in our lives. For example, in a community setting, this could relate to the need for cooperation and understanding among individuals to maintain social harmony.
  • śam indrāsomā suvitāya: This line translates to "May peace come from Indra and Soma, the god of the moon and nectar." Soma represents nourishment and rejuvenation. In practical terms, this can be seen in the importance of mental and emotional well-being, where peace and nourishment of the mind are essential for a fulfilling life.
  • śaṁ yoḥ: This simply means "peace to both." It emphasizes the duality of existence and the need for peace in all aspects of life, whether it be physical, emotional, or spiritual.
  • śaṁ na indrāpūṣaṇā vājasātau: This final line calls for peace from Indra and Pūṣaṇa, the god of nourishment and prosperity. This can be related to the pursuit of abundance in life, not just in material terms but also in relationships and personal growth. For instance, in a business context, this could mean fostering a culture of support and growth among team members.

Conclusion: The mantra as a whole is a prayer for peace and prosperity from various deities, each representing different aspects of life. Its significance lies in the holistic approach to well-being, emphasizing the need for strength, balance, nourishment, and harmony. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal development, community building, and professional environments, reminding us to seek peace and prosperity in all our endeavors. By invoking these qualities, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
