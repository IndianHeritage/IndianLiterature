याम् आभजो मरुत इन्द्र सोम ये त्वाम् अवर्धन्न अभवन गणस् ते |तेभिर एतं सजोषा वावशानोऽग्नः पिब जिह्वया सोमम् इन्द्र ||
yām̐ ābhajo maruta indra some ye tvām avardhann abhavan gaṇas te | tebhir etaṁ sajoṣā vāvaśāno3 'gneḥ piba jihvayā somam indra ||
  • Mantra: "yām̐ ābhajo maruta indra some ye tvām avardhann abhavan gaṇas te | tebhir etaṁ sajoṣā vāvaśāno3 'gneḥ piba jihvayā somam indra ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yām̐: "whom" or "that which"
    • ābhajo: "who are born from" or "who are produced"
    • maruta: "the storm gods" or "the winds"
    • indra: "Indra," the king of gods and god of rain and thunderstorms
    • some: "soma," a sacred drink associated with immortality and divine inspiration
    • ye: "who" or "those who"
    • tvām: "you" (referring to Indra)
    • avardhann: "who have increased" or "who have grown"
    • abhavan: "have become" or "have manifested"
    • gaṇas: "the groups" or "the hosts"
    • te: "your"
    • tebhir: "with them" or "by them"
    • etaṁ: "this"
    • sajoṣā: "with joy" or "with delight"
    • vāvaśāno: "having been invoked" or "having been called upon"
    • gneḥ: "of the fire" or "of the divine light"
    • piba: "drink" or "consume"
    • jihvayā: "with the tongue"
    • somam: "the soma" (the divine drink)
    • indra: "Indra" (repeated for emphasis)
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the king of gods, and the Maruts, the storm deities, emphasizing their connection to the sacred drink, soma.
    • The phrase "yām̐ ābhajo maruta" suggests a reverence for the forces of nature, particularly the winds and storms, which are essential for life and agriculture.
    • By calling upon Indra and the Maruts, the mantra acknowledges their role in providing rain and fertility to the earth, which is crucial for crops and sustenance.
    • The reference to "soma" highlights the importance of this divine drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and spiritual enlightenment.
    • The act of drinking soma "piba jihvayā somam" signifies the consumption of divine knowledge and inspiration, which can lead to personal and communal growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain and favorable weather conditions, similar to how this mantra seeks the blessings of Indra and the Maruts for a bountiful harvest.
    • In modern spiritual practices, the concept of consuming "soma" can be likened to seeking knowledge and wisdom through meditation, education, or community gatherings.
    • Environmental awareness and respect for nature can be seen as a contemporary application of the reverence expressed in this mantra, recognizing the interconnectedness of all life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of nature's forces and the divine connection to the earth's fertility.
    • It emphasizes the need for gratitude and respect towards the elements that sustain life, encouraging a harmonious relationship with the environment.
    • In a broader sense, it calls for the pursuit of knowledge and spiritual growth, highlighting the significance of community and collective well-being.
