ऋतावान ऋतजाता ऋतावृधो घोरासो अनृतद्विषः |तेषां वः सुम्ने सुचर्धिष्टमे नरः स्याम ये च सूरयः ||
ṛtāvāna ṛtajātā ṛtāvṛdho ghorāso anṛtadviṣaḥ | teṣāṁ vaḥ sumne succhardiṣṭame naraḥ syāma ye ca sūrayaḥ ||
  • ṛtāvāna: This term refers to the one who is endowed with ṛta, which signifies truth, order, and cosmic law. In real life, this can be likened to individuals who uphold integrity and righteousness in their actions, such as a judge who ensures justice is served.
  • ṛtajātā: This means born of ṛta, indicating that truth and order are inherent qualities. For example, a person who is honest and fair in their dealings, reflecting the values of their upbringing and culture.
  • ṛtāvṛdho: This term suggests being enriched or supported by ṛta. In practical terms, this could refer to a community that thrives on ethical practices, such as a business that prioritizes sustainability and social responsibility.
  • ghorāso: This word can be interpreted as fierce or powerful. It may represent the strength that comes from adhering to truth and righteousness, similar to a leader who inspires others through their commitment to ethical principles.
  • anṛtadviṣaḥ: This translates to those who are opposed to falsehood. In everyday life, this could be seen in activists who fight against corruption and dishonesty, striving to create a more just society.
  • teṣāṁ vaḥ: This phrase means "of them" or "from those." It emphasizes the collective strength and unity among those who uphold truth.
  • sumne: This term signifies auspiciousness or good fortune. It suggests that those who follow the path of truth are blessed with positive outcomes.
  • succhardiṣṭame: This means "with pure vision" or "with clarity of purpose." It highlights the importance of having a clear and righteous vision in life, akin to a person who sets clear goals based on ethical values.
  • naraḥ syāma: This translates to "may we be men" or "may we become human." It reflects the aspiration to embody the highest qualities of humanity, such as compassion, integrity, and wisdom.
  • ye ca sūrayaḥ: This means "and those who are heroic." It acknowledges the valor of individuals who stand up for truth and justice, much like whistleblowers who expose wrongdoing despite personal risks.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of truth, righteousness, and the collective strength of individuals who embody these values. In real life, it serves as a reminder to uphold integrity in our actions, strive for clarity of purpose, and support one another in the pursuit of justice. By embodying these principles, we contribute to a more harmonious and just society, reflecting the essence of ṛta in our daily lives.
