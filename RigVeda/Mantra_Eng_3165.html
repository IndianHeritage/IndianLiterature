ते स्याम ये अग्नये ददाशुर हव्यदातिभिः |य इम पूष्यन्त इन्द्रते ||
te syāma ye agnaye dadāśur havyadātibhiḥ | ya īm puṣyanta indhate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • te: those
    • syāma: who are black or dark
    • ye: who
    • agnaye: to Agni (the fire god)
    • dadāśur: have given
    • havyadātibhiḥ: with offerings
    • ya: who
    • īm: that which
    • puṣyanta: nourishes or promotes growth
    • indhate: they invoke or call upon
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the offerings made to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The phrase "te syāma ye agnaye dadāśur havyadātibhiḥ" refers to those who have made offerings to Agni, emphasizing the importance of rituals and sacrifices in Vedic culture.
    • The second part, "ya īm puṣyanta indhate," highlights the idea that those who invoke Agni with sincere offerings will receive nourishment and blessings in return.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, the concept of making offerings can be seen in various religious practices where devotees offer prayers, flowers, or food to deities, seeking blessings for prosperity and well-being.
    • In a metaphorical sense, the mantra can be applied to the idea of hard work and dedication. Just as offerings to Agni are believed to yield blessings, putting in effort in one's career or personal life often leads to success and growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of devotion, ritual, and the reciprocal relationship between humans and the divine.
    • It teaches that sincere efforts and offerings, whether in spiritual or worldly pursuits, lead to nourishment and growth in various aspects of life.
    • In a broader context, it encourages individuals to recognize the value of their actions and the impact they have on their lives and the lives of others.
