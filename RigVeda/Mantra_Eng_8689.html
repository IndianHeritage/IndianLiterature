अस्येद इन्द्रो मदेष्वाग्राभं गृभ्णीत सानसिम् |वज्रं च वृषणं भरत सम अप्सुजित् ||
asyed indro madeṣv ā grābhaṁ gṛbhṇīta sānasim | vajraṁ ca vṛṣaṇam bharat sam apsujit ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asyed: This can be interpreted as "may he" or "let him".
    • indro: Refers to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and power.
    • madeṣv: Translates to "in the intoxications" or "in the delights".
    • ā grābhaṁ: Means "to grasp" or "to hold".
    • gṛbhṇīta: This is a form of the verb meaning "may he take" or "may he receive".
    • sānasim: Refers to "the essence" or "the substance".
    • vajraṁ: Translates to "thunderbolt" or "weapon\ symbolizing strength and protection.
    • ca: Means "and".
    • vṛṣaṇam: Refers to "the bull" or "the strong one\ often associated with fertility and strength.
    • bharat: This can mean "to carry" or "to bear".
    • sam: Means "together" or "well".
    • apsujit: Refers to "the one who is born from water" or "the one who is nourished by water".
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, asking him to take hold of the essence of intoxication and strength.
    • It symbolizes the desire for divine strength and protection in life’s endeavors.
    • The mention of "thunderbolt" and "bull" signifies the importance of power and fertility, both in a physical and metaphorical sense.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be seen as a call for strength and resilience in facing challenges, much like a leader who must navigate through turbulent times.
    • In personal life, it can represent the pursuit of inner strength and the ability to grasp opportunities that come one's way, akin to someone who seizes a chance for growth or improvement.
    • In relationships, it can symbolize the nurturing of bonds that are strong and fruitful, much like a partnership that thrives on mutual support and understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine strength and the importance of grasping opportunities in life.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek inner power and resilience, whether in personal growth, professional endeavors, or nurturing relationships.
    • By invoking the qualities of Indra, individuals can aspire to embody strength, protection, and the ability to thrive in various aspects of life.
