परिणः शर्मयन्त्याः धारया सोम विश्वतः |सराः रसेव विष्टपम् ||
pari ṇaḥ śarmayantyā dhārayā soma viśvataḥ | sarā raseva viṣṭapam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pari ṇaḥ: "surrounding" or "encompassing"
    • śarmayantyā: "providing comfort" or "offering protection"
    • dhārayā: "by holding" or "by supporting"
    • soma: "the divine nectar" or "soma, a sacred drink"
    • viśvataḥ: "from all directions" or "universally"
    • sarā: "like a stream" or "flowing"
    • raseva: "like the essence" or "like the taste"
    • viṣṭapam: "the abode" or "the dwelling place"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine essence that surrounds and provides comfort universally.
    • The reference to "soma" symbolizes a sacred drink that is believed to bestow immortality and divine qualities.
    • The imagery of a stream suggests a continuous flow of blessings and nourishment that sustains life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we see how rivers and streams provide water, which is essential for life, much like the "soma" that nourishes the soul.
    • In our daily lives, we often seek comfort and protection from our families and communities, reflecting the essence of "śarmayantyā."
    • Spiritual practices, such as meditation, can be seen as a way to connect with this divine essence, allowing us to feel supported and nourished from within.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine support and the nurturing aspects of life.
    • It reminds us to seek comfort and protection from the universe and to recognize the continuous flow of blessings around us.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to cultivate a sense of community and to appreciate the natural resources that sustain us.
    • Ultimately, it signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine essence that supports life universally.
