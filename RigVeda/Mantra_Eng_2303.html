ऊर्ध्वो ह्यस्थादध्य अन्तरिक्षे 'धा वृत्राय प्र वधं जभारा |मिहं वसाना उप हīm अदुद्रोत तिग्मायुधो अजयच छत्रुम इन्द्रः ||
ūrdhvo hy asthād adhy antarikṣe 'dhā vṛtrāya pra vadhaṁ jabhāra | mihaṁ vasāna upa hīm adudrot tigmāyudho ajayac chatrum indraḥ ||
  • ūrdhvo hy asthād: This phrase translates to "He who is above, standing firm." It signifies strength and stability, suggesting a higher power or divine presence that is unwavering.
  • adhy antarikṣe: This means "in the atmosphere" or "in the space above." It indicates the vastness of the universe and the omnipresence of the divine.
  • 'dhā vṛtrāya pra vadhaṁ jabhāra: This can be interpreted as "He who has brought forth the slayer of Vritra." Vritra is a serpent or dragon in Vedic mythology, representing obstacles or chaos. This line emphasizes the triumph of good over evil.
  • mihaṁ vasāna: This translates to "dwelling in the midst." It suggests the presence of the divine in our lives, guiding and protecting us.
  • upa hīm adudrot: This means "approaching with strength." It signifies the need for courage and determination in facing challenges.
  • tigmāyudho: This translates to "with sharp weapons." It symbolizes the tools or skills we possess to overcome difficulties.
  • ajayac chatrum indraḥ: This means "Indra has conquered the enemy." Indra is the king of gods in Vedic tradition, representing power and victory.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine strength, guidance, and the triumph of good over evil. In real life, it serves as a reminder that we can draw upon inner strength and resilience when faced with challenges. Just as Indra conquers his enemies, we too can overcome obstacles in our lives by harnessing our skills and courage. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during personal struggles, professional challenges, or when seeking guidance in difficult times. It encourages us to remain steadfast and to trust in the divine presence that supports us in our journey.
