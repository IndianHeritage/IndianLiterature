अलातृणो वल इन्द्र व्रजो गोः पुरा हन्तोर भयमानो व्य आरा |सुगान पथो अकृत्नोन निरजे गाः प्रावन वाणिः पुरुहूतं धामन्तीः ||
alātṛṇo vala indra vrajo goḥ purā hantor bhayamāno vy āra | sugān patho akṛṇon niraje gāḥ prāvan vāṇīḥ puruhūtaṁ dhamantīḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • alātṛṇo: One who protects or saves.
    • vala: Strength or power.
    • indra: The king of gods, representing authority and leadership.
    • vrajo: The herd or group, often referring to cattle, symbolizing wealth and prosperity.
    • goḥ: Of the cows, which are sacred in Vedic culture.
    • purā: In the past or ancient times.
    • hantor: One who destroys or removes fear.
    • bhayamāno: Being fearful or in fear.
    • vy āra: To overcome or to go beyond.
    • sugān: Good or auspicious.
    • patho: The path or way.
    • akṛṇon: To make or create.
    • niraje: Without obstacles or hindrances.
    • gāḥ: The cows or the wealth.
    • prāvan: To flow or to be abundant.
    • vāṇīḥ: Speech or communication.
    • puruhūtaṁ: One who is invoked frequently, often referring to Indra.
    • dhamantīḥ: The one who brings forth or manifests.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who is also seen as a protector and provider.
    • It emphasizes the importance of overcoming fear and obstacles in life, particularly in the pursuit of prosperity and well-being.
    • The reference to cows symbolizes wealth and nourishment, highlighting the significance of abundance in one's life.
    • The mantra suggests that through good communication and the right path, one can achieve success and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to overcoming challenges and fears in entrepreneurship. Just as Indra protects and provides, a leader must guide their team through uncertainties.
    • In personal life, individuals may face fears related to health, relationships, or career. This mantra encourages them to seek strength and support, much like how communities come together to support one another.
    • In agriculture, the reference to cows can be seen in the importance of livestock for farmers, symbolizing the need for sustainable practices that ensure abundance and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the protective and nurturing aspects of leadership, encouraging individuals to face their fears and pursue their goals with confidence.
    • Its significance lies in the universal themes of strength, community support, and the pursuit of prosperity, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to professional endeavors.
    • By invoking the qualities of Indra, individuals can find inspiration to overcome obstacles and create a path toward abundance and success.
