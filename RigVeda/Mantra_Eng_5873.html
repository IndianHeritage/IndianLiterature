इमां उषु सोमसुतिम उप न इन्द्राग्नि सुमनसाया यातम् |नू चिद हि परिमन्माथे अस्मान् आ वां शाश्वद्भिर ववृतीय वाजैः ||
imām u ṣu somasutim upa na endrāgnī saumanasāya yātam | nū cid dhi parimamnāthe asmān ā vāṁ śaśvadbhir vavṛtīya vājaiḥ ||
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "May we, with our minds focused on the divine, approach the gods Indra and Agni, who are the embodiments of strength and prosperity. May they bestow upon us their blessings and grant us abundance in our endeavors."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • imām: This means "this" or "here," indicating a focus on the present moment.
    • uṣu: Refers to "dawn" or "light," symbolizing new beginnings and enlightenment.
    • somasutim: "Soma" is a sacred drink, and "sutim" refers to "intelligence" or "wisdom," suggesting a quest for higher knowledge.
    • upa: Means "towards" or "approaching," indicating a movement towards the divine.
    • na: Translates to "not," used here to emphasize the importance of the following deities.
    • endrāgnī: Refers to the deities Indra (the king of gods) and Agni (the fire god), representing power and purification.
    • saumanasāya: Means "for the well-being" or "for happiness," indicating the intention behind the invocation.
    • yātam: Means "may we go" or "may we proceed," suggesting a journey or quest.
    • nū cid: Translates to "indeed" or "certainly," reinforcing the conviction in the prayer.
    • hhi: An emphatic particle, adding strength to the statement.
    • parimamnāthe: Refers to "in the midst of" or "surrounded by," indicating a state of being enveloped by divine presence.
    • asmān: Means "us," referring to the community or the individual invoking the mantra.
    • ā: A preposition meaning "from" or "by," indicating the source of blessings.
    • vāṁ: Means "you," referring to the deities being addressed.
    • śaśvadbhir: Means "everlasting" or "perpetual," indicating the desire for continuous blessings.
    • vavṛtīya: Refers to "may they bestow" or "may they grant," indicating a request for divine favor.
    • vājaiḥ: Means "with strength" or "with vigor," suggesting the nature of the blessings sought.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be invoked during meetings or strategic planning sessions to seek clarity and wisdom in decision-making.
    • During personal challenges, individuals may recite this mantra to invoke strength and guidance from higher powers, fostering resilience and positivity.
    • In community gatherings or rituals, this mantra can be used to unite people in a common purpose, seeking collective blessings for prosperity and well-being.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine energies for guidance, strength, and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of approaching the divine with a focused mind and a pure heart, seeking blessings not just for oneself but for the community as a whole. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, from personal challenges to collective endeavors, reminding us of the interconnectedness of our actions and the divine support available to us. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset of abundance and resilience, fostering a deeper connection with the spiritual realm and enhancing their overall well-being.
