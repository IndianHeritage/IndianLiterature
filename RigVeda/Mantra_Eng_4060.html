मन्त्र: अतो न आ नृन अतिथीन अतः पत्नीर दशस्यत |आरे विश्वं पतेष्ठां द्विषो युयोतु यूयुविः ||
ato na ā nṝn atithīn ataḥ patnīr daśasyata | āre viśvam patheṣṭhāṁ dviṣo yuyotu yūyuviḥ ||
  • ato na ā nṝn atithīn ataḥ patnīr daśasyata
    • ato - This word translates to "therefore" or "hence," indicating a conclusion or a result.
    • na - This means "not," suggesting negation or absence.
    • ā nṝn - Refers to "men" or "humans," emphasizing the collective aspect of humanity.
    • atithīn - This translates to "guests" or "visitors," highlighting the importance of hospitality.
    • ataḥ - Meaning "from this," it connects the previous thoughts to the next idea.
    • patnīr - This word means "wives" or "partners," indicating relationships and familial bonds.
    • daśasyata - This can be interpreted as "of ten," possibly referring to a group or a collective of ten entities.
  • āre viśvam patheṣṭhāṁ dviṣo yuyotu yūyuviḥ
    • āre - This can mean "to praise" or "to invoke," suggesting a call to action or recognition.
    • viśvam - Translates to "the whole" or "universe," indicating inclusivity and the entirety of existence.
    • patheṣṭhāṁ - This means "on the path," suggesting a journey or a way of life.
    • dviṣo - Refers to "enemies" or "those who oppose," indicating conflict or challenges.
    • yuyotu - This means "may they fight" or "may they engage," suggesting action against opposition.
    • yūyuviḥ - This can be interpreted as "together" or "in unity," emphasizing the strength found in collaboration.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of hospitality, unity, and the collective strength of humanity in the face of challenges. It suggests that by fostering relationships and welcoming guests, we create a harmonious environment. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community gatherings, family events, or even in workplaces where collaboration and support are essential. The call to action against opposition highlights the need for resilience and teamwork in overcoming obstacles. Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the values of togetherness, respect, and the significance of nurturing relationships in our lives.
