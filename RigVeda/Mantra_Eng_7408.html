इन्द्र य उ नु ते अस्ति वाजो विप्रेभिः सनित्वः |अस्माभिः सु तं सनुहि ||
indra ya u nu te asti vājo viprebhiḥ sanitvaḥ | asmābhiḥ su taṁ sanuhi ||
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • Ya: This word translates to "who" or "that," indicating a connection or relationship.
  • U: This is an emphatic particle, often used for emphasis or to draw attention.
  • Nu: This word can mean "now" or "indeed," suggesting immediacy or affirmation.
  • Te: This means "to you," indicating a direction or focus towards Indra.
  • Astī: This translates to "is" or "exists," affirming the presence of something.
  • Vājo: This word means "strength" or "power," often associated with the might of Indra.
  • Viprebhiḥ: This refers to "the wise" or "the learned," indicating those who possess knowledge and wisdom.
  • Sanitvaḥ: This can be interpreted as "protection" or "safeguarding," suggesting a sense of security provided by Indra.
  • Asmābhiḥ: This means "by us," indicating a collective or communal aspect.
  • Su: This translates to "well" or "good," indicating positivity or favorable conditions.
  • Taṁ: This means "that," referring back to Indra or the strength he embodies.
  • Sanuhi: This can be interpreted as "we invoke" or "we call upon," suggesting a plea or request for assistance.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation to Indra, asking for his strength and protection, particularly through the wisdom of learned individuals. It emphasizes the importance of community and collective strength in seeking divine assistance.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Community Support: Just as the mantra calls upon the collective wisdom of the learned, communities can come together to support one another in times of need, invoking strength and protection through unity.
  • Leadership: Leaders can embody the qualities of Indra by being strong and wise, guiding their communities with knowledge and ensuring the safety and well-being of their people.
  • Personal Empowerment: Individuals can invoke their inner strength and wisdom, much like Indra, to overcome challenges and protect themselves and their loved ones.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, wisdom, and protection. It highlights the importance of community and the role of knowledgeable individuals in fostering a safe and supportive environment. In everyday life, it serves as a reminder to seek strength from both within and from the collective, ensuring that we are not alone in our struggles and that wisdom guides our actions.
