विष्णोः नु कं वीर्याणि प्र वोचं यः पार्थिवानि विममे राजांसि |यो अस्कभायद उत्तरणं सदस्थं विचक्रमाणस त्रीधोरुगायः ||
viṣṇor nu kaṁ vīryāṇi pra vocaṁ yaḥ pārthivāni vimame rajāṁsi | yo askabhāyad uttaraṁ sadhasthaṁ vicakramāṇas tredhorugāyaḥ ||
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "O Vishnu, who can speak of your mighty deeds? You who measure the earthly realms, who are the king of all, and who have the strength to protect and guide us."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • viṣṇor: Refers to Lord Vishnu, the preserver and protector in Hindu mythology.
    • nu: A particle used for emphasis, often translated as "indeed" or "truly."
    • kaṁ: Means "who" or "what," questioning the identity or nature of something.
    • vīryāṇi: Refers to "mighty deeds" or "strengths," highlighting the power and valor of Vishnu.
    • pra vocaṁ: Means "to speak of" or "to declare," indicating the act of sharing knowledge or praise.
    • yaḥ: Means "who," referring back to Vishnu.
    • pārthivāni: Relates to "earthly" or "worldly," indicating the physical realm.
    • vimame: Means "to measure," suggesting the ability to comprehend or understand.
    • rajāṁsi: Refers to "kingdoms" or "realms," emphasizing the vastness of Vishnu's domain.
    • yo: Another form of "who," again referring to Vishnu.
    • askabhāyad: Means "to protect" or "to guard," indicating Vishnu's role as a protector.
    • uttaraṁ: Means "higher" or "noble," suggesting a superior state or quality.
    • sadhasthaṁ: Refers to "in the assembly" or "in the gathering," indicating a place of importance.
    • vicakramāṇas: Means "those who move" or "those who act," suggesting active participation.
    • tredhorugāyaḥ: Refers to "the three worlds" or "the vastness," indicating the omnipresence of Vishnu.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of crisis, people often turn to their faith for strength and guidance, much like how this mantra invokes Vishnu's protective qualities.
    • In leadership, the qualities of measuring and understanding the needs of the people, as suggested in the mantra, are essential for effective governance.
    • In personal challenges, individuals may seek the strength and valor represented by Vishnu to overcome obstacles, reflecting the mantra's call for divine support.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine strength and protection. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and appreciating the mighty deeds of Vishnu, who is seen as a guardian of the earthly realms. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek guidance in leadership, personal challenges, and during times of crisis, reminding them of the strength that comes from faith and the divine. It serves as a call to acknowledge the higher powers that guide and protect us in our daily lives.
