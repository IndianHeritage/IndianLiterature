त्रीणि सरांसी पृष्णयो दुदुहरे वज्रीणि मदु |उत्सं कवन्धम उद्रिणम् ||
trīṇi sarāṁsi pṛśnayo duduhre vajriṇe madhu | utsaṁ kavandham udriṇam ||
  • Mantra: "trīṇi sarāṁsi pṛśnayo duduhre vajriṇe madhu | utsaṁ kavandham udriṇam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • trīṇi: three
    • sarāṁsi: streams or channels
    • pṛśnayo: of the cows (often symbolizing nourishment or wealth)
    • duduhre: have given or produced
    • vajriṇe: to the one who wields the thunderbolt (often referring to Indra, the king of gods)
    • madhu: honey or sweetness (symbolizing abundance and prosperity)
    • utsaṁ: the rising or elevation
    • kavandham: of the one who is strong or powerful
    • udriṇam: the one who is capable or able
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the three streams that have emerged from the cows, which can be interpreted as the three vital energies or resources in life.
    • The reference to "vajriṇe" indicates a connection to divine strength and power, suggesting that these resources are bestowed by a higher power.
    • The mention of "madhu" symbolizes the sweetness of life and the abundance that comes from these resources.
    • The phrase "utsaṁ kavandham udriṇam" emphasizes the importance of rising to one's potential and harnessing strength to achieve goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, the "three streams" could represent financial resources, human capital, and innovation. A successful entrepreneur harnesses these streams to create a thriving enterprise.
    • In personal development, individuals can view the mantra as a reminder to cultivate their physical, mental, and emotional well-being, drawing strength from each aspect to achieve personal goals.
    • In community building, the mantra can inspire collective efforts where members contribute their unique strengths (the three streams) to create a supportive and prosperous environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of recognizing and utilizing the resources available to us, whether they are material, spiritual, or personal.
    • It encourages individuals to seek abundance and sweetness in life while acknowledging the divine support that empowers them to rise and achieve their potential.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra serves as a motivational reminder to harness our strengths and resources effectively, leading to personal and communal growth.
