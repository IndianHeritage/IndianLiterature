प्रति भद्रā अदृक्षत गवां सर्गा न रश्मयः |ओषा अप्रा उरु ज्रयः ||
prati bhadrā adṛkṣata gavāṁ sargā na raśmayaḥ | oṣā aprā uru jrayaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "prati bhadrā adṛkṣata gavāṁ sargā na raśmayaḥ | oṣā aprā uru jrayaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prati: towards, in the direction of
    • bhadrā: auspicious, good, beneficial
    • adṛkṣata: observed, looked upon
    • gavāṁ: of the cows, symbolizing wealth and nourishment
    • sargā: creation, birth, or generation
    • na: not
    • raśmayaḥ: rays, light, or energy
    • oṣā: dawn, light, or brightness
    • aprā: not far, close
    • uru: wide, vast
    • jrayaḥ: decay, decline, or deterioration
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the auspiciousness that is observed in the creation of wealth and nourishment, symbolized by cows.
    • The phrase "na raśmayaḥ" suggests that there are no rays or energies that hinder this auspiciousness.
    • The mention of "oṣā aprā" indicates that the dawn or new beginnings are close at hand, while "uru jrayaḥ" implies that there is no decay or decline in this auspiciousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of nurturing the land and livestock, ensuring that they thrive without decline.
    • In business, it can symbolize the need for a positive environment where opportunities for growth and prosperity are abundant, free from negative influences.
    • In personal life, it encourages individuals to focus on positive relationships and experiences that foster growth and well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of observing and nurturing auspiciousness in various aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder to create an environment that supports growth, prosperity, and well-being, free from negativity and decline.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more fulfilling and prosperous existence, whether in personal, professional, or communal contexts.
