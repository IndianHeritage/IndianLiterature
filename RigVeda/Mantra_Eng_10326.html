त्वं नो अग्नि अग्निभिर् ब्रह्म यज्ञं च वर्धय |त्वं नो देवतातये रायो दानाय चोदय ||
tvaṁ no agne agnibhir brahma yajñaṁ ca vardhaya | tvaṁ no devatātaye rāyo dānāya codaya ||
  • tvaṁ no agne: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni." Agni is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine messenger. In real life, Agni represents the fire within us that drives our ambitions and desires.
  • agnibhir brahma: This means "with the fires (Agni) of Brahman." Brahman refers to the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit in Hindu philosophy. The connection here emphasizes the importance of spiritual energy and the transformative power of fire in rituals and life.
  • yajñaṁ ca vardhaya: This translates to "increase our sacrifices." Yajña refers to the Vedic rituals of offerings to the gods, which are essential for maintaining cosmic order. In a modern context, this can be seen as the importance of selfless actions and contributions to society.
  • tvaṁ no devatātaye: This means "You, for the sake of the deities." It highlights the relationship between humans and divine forces, suggesting that our actions should align with higher principles and values.
  • rāyo dānāya codaya: This translates to "inspire us towards prosperity and generosity." It emphasizes the importance of seeking abundance not just for oneself but also for sharing with others, fostering a sense of community and support.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and support in our endeavors. It emphasizes the importance of spiritual energy, selfless actions, and the pursuit of prosperity that benefits not just the individual but the community as a whole. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, community service, and fostering a spirit of generosity and collaboration in our daily lives. By invoking these principles, we can create a harmonious and prosperous environment for ourselves and those around us.
