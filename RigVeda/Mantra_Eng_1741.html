उप ह्वये सुदुघां धेनुम एतां सुहस्तो गोधुग उता दोहद एनाम् |श्रेष्ठं सवं सविता सावीषन् नो 'भीद्धो घर्मस तद उ षु प्र वोचम् ||
upa hvaye sudughāṁ dhenum etāṁ suhasto godhug uta dohad enām | śreṣṭhaṁ savaṁ savitā sāviṣan no 'bhīddho gharmas tad u ṣu pra vocam ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "I call upon the well-nourished cow, the one that is strong and gives abundant milk. May the best of all, Savita, the Sun God, bless us with warmth and prosperity, and may we not be deprived of this nourishment."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • upa hvaye: I call upon
    • sudughāṁ: well-nourished
    • dhenum: cow (symbol of nourishment)
    • etāṁ: this
    • suhasto: strong, capable
    • godhug: giver of milk
    • uta: and
    • dohad: nourishment
    • enām: to her
    • śreṣṭhaṁ: the best
    • savaṁ: all
    • savitā: Savita, the Sun God
    • sāviṣan: who bestows
    • no: us
    • 'bhīddho: not deprived
    • gharmas: warmth
    • tad: that
    • u: and
    • ṣu: good
    • pra vocam: I speak forth
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual. The cow is a symbol of abundance and sustenance in Vedic culture, representing not just physical milk but also the nurturing aspects of life.
    • The invocation of Savita, the Sun God, highlights the connection between natural elements and human well-being. Just as the sun provides warmth and energy necessary for life, the mantra seeks blessings for prosperity and health.
  • Real-Life Applications:
    • In agricultural societies, this mantra can be a prayer for a good harvest, invoking the blessings of nature for sustenance.
    • In modern contexts, it can be interpreted as a call for holistic well-being, emphasizing the need for physical health (nourishment) and spiritual warmth (support from the community and nature).
    • It can also serve as a reminder to appreciate the resources we have, such as food and community support, and to seek balance in our lives.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking nourishment and blessings from nature. It serves as a reminder of our dependence on the environment and the importance of gratitude for the resources we receive. In a world where many face food insecurity and disconnection from nature, this mantra encourages us to foster a relationship with our surroundings and seek harmony in our lives.
