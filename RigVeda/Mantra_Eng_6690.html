यः सृबिन्दम अनर्शनिम पिप्रुं दासम अहिषुवम् |वधीद उग्रो रिणन्न अपः ||
yaḥ sṛbindam anarśanim pipruṁ dāsam ahīśuvam | vadhīd ugro riṇann apaḥ ||
  • yaḥ - This word translates to "who" or "that one." It indicates a subject or a person of significance.
  • sṛbindam - This term can be interpreted as "the one who binds" or "the one who connects." It suggests a sense of unity or bringing together.
  • anarśanim - This word means "without anger" or "calm." It emphasizes the importance of maintaining peace and composure.
  • pipruṁ - This can be understood as "the one who is powerful" or "the one who has strength." It denotes a sense of authority or capability.
  • dāsam - This translates to "servant" or "one who serves." It reflects humility and the role of service in society.
  • ahīśuvam - This term can be interpreted as "the one who is swift" or "the one who moves quickly." It suggests agility and responsiveness.
  • vadhīd - This word means "to kill" or "to destroy." It indicates a forceful action or a significant change.
  • ugro - This translates to "fierce" or "powerful." It emphasizes strength and intensity.
  • riṇann - This term means "to roar" or "to make a loud sound." It signifies a call to attention or a declaration.
  • apaḥ - This word translates to "water." In many cultures, water symbolizes life, purity, and sustenance.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to recognize the powerful and calm forces in our lives that bind us together, urging us to serve with humility while being aware of the fierce and transformative energies around us. It highlights the balance between strength and peace, and the importance of being responsive and agile in our actions.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In leadership, it emphasizes the need for leaders to be strong yet calm, binding their teams together while serving their needs.
  • In conflict resolution, it suggests that one should approach disputes without anger, focusing on unity and understanding.
  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to harness their inner strength while remaining humble and responsive to the needs of others.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for balance between strength and humility, urging individuals to act with purpose and awareness. It is applicable in various aspects of life, from personal relationships to professional environments, reminding us of the importance of unity, service, and the transformative power of calmness amidst challenges.
