ऊर्जो नपातमाहुवे 'ग्निम् पावकशोचिषम् |अस्मिन्यज्ञे स्वध्वरे ||
ūrjo napātam ā huve 'gnim pāvakaśociṣam | asmin yajñe svadhvare ||
  • ūrjo: This word translates to "energy" or "vitality." It signifies the life force that sustains all living beings.
  • napātam: This means "descendant" or "offspring." It refers to the generative aspect of energy, indicating that energy begets life.
  • ā huve: This phrase translates to "I invoke" or "I call upon." It expresses the act of invoking divine energy or forces.
  • ‘gnim: This word means "fire." In Vedic literature, fire is often seen as a purifying and transformative element.
  • pāvakaśociṣam: This term can be broken down into "pāvaka," meaning "purifier," and "śociṣam," meaning "brilliant" or "radiant." Together, it refers to the radiant and purifying nature of fire.
  • asmin: This means "in this" or "in this context," indicating the specific ritual or situation being referred to.
  • yajñe: This translates to "sacrifice" or "offering." In Vedic tradition, yajna is a sacred ritual performed to invoke divine blessings.
  • svadhvare: This means "in this offering" or "in this sacrifice," emphasizing the importance of the ritual context.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to invoke the vital energy that descends from the divine, represented by fire, which is both purifying and illuminating. It emphasizes the significance of energy in the process of creation and sustenance of life, and the role of rituals in connecting with this divine energy.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Just as fire transforms materials, individuals can harness their inner energy to transform their lives, pursuing personal development and self-improvement.
  • Environmental Awareness: The invocation of fire as a purifying force can remind us of the importance of respecting and nurturing our environment, as energy is essential for life on Earth.
  • Community Rituals: In communal gatherings, such as festivals or ceremonies, invoking this energy can foster a sense of unity and shared purpose among participants.

In conclusion, this mantra highlights the significance of energy as a life force and the transformative power of rituals. It serves as a reminder of our connection to the divine and the importance of harnessing our inner vitality for personal and communal growth. By invoking this energy in our lives, we can strive for purity, brilliance, and a deeper connection to the world around us.
