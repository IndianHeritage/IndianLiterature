इन्द्रासोमा वर्तयतं दिवो वधं सम पृथिव्याः अघशंसाया तरहणम् |उत तक्षतं स्वर्म्यम् पर्वतेभ्यो येन रक्षो वावृद्धानं निजूर्वतः ||
indrāsomā vartayataṁ divo vadhaṁ sam pṛthivyā aghaśaṁsāya tarhaṇam | ut takṣataṁ svarya1m parvatebhyo yena rakṣo vāvṛdhānaṁ nijūrvathaḥ ||
  • Indrāsomā: This term refers to the divine forces of Indra and Soma. Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength, power, and the forces of nature, while Soma symbolizes the sacred drink that grants immortality and is associated with inspiration and ecstasy.
  • Vartayataṁ: This word means "to maintain" or "to sustain." It implies the continuous action of keeping something in existence or in a state of balance.
  • Divo vadhaṁ: This phrase translates to "the slaying of the sky" or "the destruction of the heavens." It can be interpreted as overcoming obstacles or challenges that are as vast as the sky.
  • Sam pṛthivyā: This means "together with the earth." It signifies the connection between the heavens and the earth, emphasizing the importance of harmony between the two realms.
  • Aghaśaṁsāya: This term refers to "the removal of sin" or "the dispelling of negativity." It suggests a purification process, cleansing oneself of impurities or wrongdoings.
  • Tarhaṇam: This word means "to bring forth" or "to produce." It indicates the act of generating something new or beneficial.
  • Ut takṣataṁ: This translates to "may it be created" or "may it be fashioned." It expresses a desire for the manifestation of positive outcomes.
  • Svaryaṁ: This term means "heavenly" or "divine." It refers to the higher realms of existence and the qualities associated with them.
  • Parvatebhyo: This means "from the mountains." It symbolizes strength, stability, and the source of life, as mountains are often seen as sacred in many cultures.
  • Yena rakṣo: This phrase translates to "by which the demons" or "by which the negative forces." It refers to the challenges or adversities that one may face in life.
  • Vāvṛdhānaṁ: This means "to be driven away" or "to be dispelled." It indicates the act of overcoming obstacles or negative influences.
  • Nijūrvathaḥ: This term refers to "one's own path" or "one's own journey." It emphasizes the importance of personal growth and self-discovery.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking balance and harmony between the divine and earthly realms. It emphasizes the importance of overcoming challenges, purifying oneself from negativity, and striving for personal growth. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during times of personal struggle, when facing obstacles in relationships or career, or when seeking spiritual enlightenment. By invoking the energies of Indra and Soma, individuals can find strength and inspiration to navigate their journeys, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
