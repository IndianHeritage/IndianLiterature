इन्द्रा को वां वरुणा सुम्नम आप स्तोमो हविष्मां अमृतो न होता |यो वां हृदि क्रतुमां अस्मद उक्तः पस्पर्शद इन्द्रावरुणा नमस्वान ||
indrā ko vāṁ varuṇā sumnam āpa stomo haviṣmām̐ amṛto na hotā | yo vāṁ hṛdi kratumām̐ asmad uktaḥ pasparśad indrāvaruṇā namasvān ||
  • Indrā ko vāṁ varuṇā sumnam āpa stomo haviṣmām̐ amṛto na hotā
    • Indrā: Refers to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and power.
    • ko vāṁ: "Who is for you," indicating a plea or invocation.
    • varuṇā: Refers to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water, representing truth and moral authority.
    • sumnam: "Good fortune" or "auspiciousness," suggesting a desire for positive outcomes.
    • āpa: "Waters," which can symbolize life, nourishment, and purification.
    • stomo: "Praise" or "hymn," indicating the act of worship or reverence.
    • haviṣmām̐: "In offerings," referring to the rituals and sacrifices made to the deities.
    • amṛto: "Immortal," signifying the eternal nature of the divine or the soul.
    • na hotā: "Not the one who sacrifices," suggesting a distinction between the divine and the act of sacrifice.
  • yo vāṁ hṛdi kratumām̐ asmad uktaḥ pasparśad indrāvaruṇā namasvān
    • yo: "Who," referring to the divine presence or the gods being invoked.
    • vāṁ: "You," addressing the deities directly.
    • hṛdi: "In the heart," indicating the inner feelings or consciousness.
    • kratumām̐: "Intentions" or "thoughts," referring to the mental and spiritual focus of the worshipper.
    • asmad: "Our," indicating a collective identity or community.
    • uktaḥ: "Spoken" or "declared," referring to the words of the mantra or prayer.
    • pasparśad: "Touched" or "influenced," suggesting a divine interaction or blessing.
    • indrāvaruṇā: "Indra and Varuna," invoking both deities together for their combined blessings.
    • namasvān: "Salutations" or "obeisance," indicating respect and reverence towards the deities.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking the divine forces of Indra and Varuna for blessings of strength, auspiciousness, and moral order. It highlights the significance of heartfelt intentions in worship and the desire for divine interaction in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts such as:

  • During rituals and ceremonies where blessings for strength and prosperity are sought.
  • In personal prayers for guidance and support in challenging situations.
  • As a reminder to align one's intentions with moral and ethical values, fostering a sense of community and collective well-being.

Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of divine forces and human intentions, encouraging individuals to seek a harmonious relationship with the cosmos.
