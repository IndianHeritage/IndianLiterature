अनु त्वाहिघ्ने अध देव देवाः मदन विश्वे कवितमं कवीनाम् |करो यत्र वरिवो बाधिताय दिवे जनाय तन्वे गृणानः ||
anu tvāhighne adha deva devā madan viśve kavitamaṁ kavīnām | karo yatra varivo bādhitāya dive janāya tanve gṛṇānaḥ ||
  • anu tvāhighne: This phrase can be translated as "following you, O high one." It signifies a sense of devotion and reverence towards a higher power or divine entity.
  • adha deva devā: This means "below the gods, O gods." It reflects the idea of humility and the recognition of the divine hierarchy in the universe.
  • madan viśve kavitamaṁ kavīnām: This translates to "the most delightful among all poets." It emphasizes the creative and artistic aspects of divinity, suggesting that the divine is the ultimate source of inspiration and creativity.
  • karo yatra varivo bādhitāya: This can be interpreted as "do where the powerful are hindered." It implies a call to action, encouraging individuals to strive for success even in the face of obstacles.
  • dive janāya tanve gṛṇānaḥ: This means "singing for the people in the sky." It suggests a communal aspect of worship and the importance of collective efforts in seeking divine blessings.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates themes of devotion, humility, creativity, and collective action. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can strive to follow higher ideals and seek inspiration from the divine in their creative pursuits.
  • In community efforts, the mantra encourages collaboration and collective worship, fostering a sense of unity among people.
  • In overcoming challenges, it serves as a reminder to persist and act even when faced with difficulties, drawing strength from the divine.

Overall, this mantra highlights the significance of recognizing the divine in our lives, seeking inspiration, and working together towards common goals.
