त्वं सोम क्रतुभिः सुक्रतुर भूष त्वं दक्षैः सुदक्षो विश्ववेदाः |त्वं वृषा वृषत्वेभिर महित्वा द्युम्नेभिर द्युम्न्य अभवो नृचक्षाः ||
tvaṁ soma kratubhiḥ sukratur bhūs tvaṁ dakṣaiḥ sudakṣo viśvavedāḥ | tvaṁ vṛṣā vṛṣatvebhir mahitvā dyumnebhir dyumny abhavo nṛcakṣāḥ ||
  • tvaṁ soma kratubhiḥ: This phrase refers to the divine essence of Soma, which is often associated with the moon and represents the life-giving and nourishing aspects of nature. In real life, this can be likened to the importance of water and food in sustaining life.
  • sukratur bhūs: "Su" means good, and "kratur" refers to the will or intention. This part emphasizes the importance of good intentions and actions in our lives. For example, when we engage in charitable acts or help others, we are embodying "su" or good intentions.
  • tvaṁ dakṣaiḥ sudakṣo: "Dakṣa" means skillful or capable. This line highlights the value of skill and expertise. In a practical sense, this can be seen in professions where expertise leads to success, such as doctors, engineers, or artists who excel in their fields.
  • viśvavedāḥ: This term refers to the all-knowing or omniscient aspect of the divine. It suggests that true knowledge encompasses all aspects of life. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of education and wisdom, where individuals seek to understand the world around them.
  • tvaṁ vṛṣā vṛṣatvebhir: "Vṛṣā" signifies strength and power. This part of the mantra acknowledges the strength that comes from the divine. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who draw strength from their beliefs or community support during challenging times.
  • mahitvā dyumnebhir: "Mahitvā" refers to greatness or magnificence, while "dyumnebhir" relates to the brilliance of the heavens. This line can be interpreted as a reminder to aspire for greatness in our endeavors, much like how stars shine brightly in the night sky, inspiring us to reach for our goals.
  • dyumny abhavo nṛcakṣāḥ: This phrase suggests the manifestation of brilliance and clarity in human vision. It implies that with divine guidance, we can achieve clarity in our thoughts and actions. In real life, this can be seen in moments of insight or inspiration that lead to significant breakthroughs in personal or professional life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine qualities such as nourishment, good intentions, skill, knowledge, strength, greatness, and clarity. Its significance lies in the reminder that by embodying these qualities, individuals can lead a fulfilling and purposeful life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages us to cultivate good intentions, seek knowledge, develop our skills, and draw strength from our beliefs and communities, ultimately guiding us towards a path of success and enlightenment.
