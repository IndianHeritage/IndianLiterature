अध श्रुतं कवषं वृद्धम् अप्स्व अनु द्रुह्युः न वृणग वज्रबाहुः |वृणाना अत्र सख्याय सख्यं त्वायन्तो ये अमदन्न अनु त्वा ||
adha śrutaṁ kavaṣaṁ vṛddham apsv anu druhyuṁ ni vṛṇag vajrabāhuḥ | vṛṇānā atra sakhyāya sakhyaṁ tvāyanto ye amadann anu tvā ||
  • Adha śrutaṁ: This phrase translates to "having heard below" or "having listened attentively." It emphasizes the importance of listening and understanding the teachings or wisdom that is being imparted.
  • Kavaṣaṁ: Refers to a "wise person" or "sage." This term highlights the value of wisdom and the role of knowledgeable individuals in guiding others.
  • Vṛddham: Meaning "grown" or "mature," this word signifies the importance of experience and the growth that comes with it. It suggests that wisdom is often a product of time and experience.
  • Apsv: This term can be interpreted as "in the waters" or "in the flow." It symbolizes the fluidity of knowledge and the interconnectedness of all things.
  • anu druhyuṁ: Translated as "without enmity" or "without hostility," this phrase underscores the importance of harmony and peaceful relationships among individuals.
  • ni vṛṇag vajrabāhuḥ: This can be understood as "may we choose the one with strong arms" or "may we select the powerful." It suggests the need for strength and support in our endeavors, both physically and metaphorically.
  • vṛṇānā: Meaning "we choose" or "we select," this word emphasizes the act of making conscious choices in life.
  • atra sakhyāya: This translates to "for friendship" or "for companionship." It highlights the significance of relationships and the bonds we form with others.
  • sakhyaṁ tvāyanto: Meaning "we are united in friendship," this phrase reinforces the idea of unity and collaboration among individuals.
  • ye amadann anu tvā: This can be interpreted as "those who have nourished you" or "those who have supported you." It acknowledges the contributions of others in our lives and the importance of gratitude.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom, experience, and harmonious relationships in our lives. It teaches us to listen attentively, choose wisely, and value the bonds we share with others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal relationships, teamwork, and community building. By fostering understanding, support, and unity, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous environment for ourselves and those around us. The teachings of this mantra encourage us to cultivate wisdom and nurture our connections, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life.
