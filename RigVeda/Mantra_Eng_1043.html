शकेम तव समिधं साधयाधियस् तवे देवाः हविः अदन्त्य आहुतम् |त्वम् आदित्यां आ वह तान ह्य उष्मस्य अग्नि सख्ये मा ऋषामाः वयं तव ||
śakema tvā samidhaṁ sādhayā dhiyas tve devā havir adanty āhutam | tvam ādityām̐ ā vaha tān hy u1śmasy agne sakhye mā riṣāmā vayaṁ tava ||
  • śakema tvā samidhaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "May we be able to offer you our sacrifices." Here, "śakema" implies the ability or capacity to perform an act, while "samidhaṁ" refers to the offerings made to the divine. In real life, this can be likened to the efforts we put into our work or relationships, where we strive to give our best to those we care about.
  • sādhayā dhiyas tve devā: This translates to "O divine one, may we attain our goals through our intellect." "Sādhayā" means to achieve or accomplish, and "dhiyas" refers to intellect or wisdom. This can be seen in the context of education and personal development, where individuals seek knowledge and wisdom to achieve their aspirations.
  • havir adanty āhutam: This means "the offerings that are unending." "Havir" refers to offerings, and "adanty" suggests something that is limitless or eternal. In practical terms, this can relate to the continuous efforts we make in our lives, such as nurturing relationships or pursuing lifelong learning, which yield ongoing benefits.
  • tvam ādityām̐ ā vaha: This translates to "You, O Aditya, bring forth." "Aditya" refers to the sun or solar deities, symbolizing light, energy, and life. This can be related to how we draw inspiration and motivation from natural elements, such as the sun, which energizes us to pursue our daily tasks.
  • tān hy uśmasy agne: This means "those who are in the warmth of the fire." "Uśmasy" refers to warmth or heat, and "agne" relates to fire. This can be interpreted as the passion and enthusiasm we bring to our endeavors, akin to how a fire fuels warmth and energy in our lives.
  • sakhye mā riṣāmā vayaṁ tava: This translates to "May we not be angry with you, O friend." "Sakhye" means friend, and "riṣāmā" refers to anger or resentment. This highlights the importance of maintaining harmonious relationships and resolving conflicts amicably, which is essential in both personal and professional settings.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of devotion, intellect, and harmonious relationships in our lives. It encourages us to strive for our goals through wisdom and continuous effort while maintaining a positive and respectful attitude towards others. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in our professional lives where teamwork and collaboration are crucial, or in personal relationships where understanding and support foster growth and happiness. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our actions, intentions, and relationships in achieving a fulfilling life.
