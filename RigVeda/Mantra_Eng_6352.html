उद गा आजद अङ्गिरोभ्य आविष कृत्वान गुहा सतीः |अर्वाण्चं नुनुदे वलम् ||
ud gā ājad aṅgirobhya āviṣ kṛṇvan guhā satīḥ | arvāñcaṁ nunude valam ||
  • ud gā: This phrase can be translated as "to sing forth" or "to rise up in song." It signifies the act of expressing oneself through music or chant, which is a common practice in many cultures to invoke feelings, memories, or spiritual connections.
  • ājad: This word means "to be born" or "to arise." It indicates the emergence of something new, whether it be a thought, a feeling, or a physical entity. In real life, this can be likened to the birth of ideas or innovations that change our perspectives.
  • aṅgirobhya: Referring to the "Angiras," a group of sages or seers in Vedic literature. This term emphasizes the wisdom and knowledge that can be derived from ancient teachings. In modern contexts, we can relate this to the importance of learning from our ancestors and the wisdom they imparted.
  • āviṣ: This means "to cover" or "to envelop." It suggests the idea of protection or safeguarding something precious. In everyday life, this can be seen in how we protect our loved ones or our values from external influences.
  • kṛṇvan: Translated as "to create" or "to make." This word highlights the active role we play in shaping our lives and environments. For instance, individuals create their destinies through their choices and actions.
  • guhā: This term means "cave" or "hidden place." It symbolizes the depths of the mind or the subconscious, where thoughts and feelings reside. In real life, this can be compared to introspection and the exploration of our inner selves to understand our motivations and desires.
  • satīḥ: This word refers to "truth" or "the virtuous." It emphasizes the importance of integrity and righteousness in our actions. In contemporary society, this can be seen in the value placed on honesty and ethical behavior in personal and professional relationships.
  • arvāñcaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "all beings" or "all creatures." It signifies inclusivity and the interconnectedness of life. In real life, this can be reflected in our responsibility towards the environment and all living beings, promoting harmony and sustainability.
  • nunude: This means "to speak" or "to declare." It highlights the power of words and communication in shaping our realities. In modern contexts, this can be related to the impact of social media and public discourse in influencing opinions and actions.
  • valam: This word signifies "strength" or "power." It emphasizes the importance of resilience and fortitude in overcoming challenges. In real life, this can be seen in how individuals and communities come together to face adversities, showcasing their strength and unity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of creation, wisdom, protection, truth, and strength. It serves as a reminder of the importance of expressing ourselves, learning from the past, and embracing our inner truths. In real life, these principles can guide us in our personal growth, relationships, and responsibilities towards others and the environment. By embodying these values, we can contribute to a more harmonious and enlightened society.
