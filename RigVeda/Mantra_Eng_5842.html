या आपीर नित्यो वरुण प्रियः सन् त्वां आगांषि कृतवत् सखा ते |मा त एनेस्वन्तो यक्षिन् भुजेम यन्धि स्मा विप्रः स्तुवते वरूथम ||
ya āpir nityo varuṇa priyaḥ san tvām āgāṁsi kṛṇavat sakhā te | mā ta enasvanto yakṣin bhujema yandhi ṣmā vipraḥ stuvate varūtham ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who
    • āpir: is the one who is eternal
    • nityo: everlasting
    • varuṇa: Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water
    • priyaḥ: beloved
    • san: being
    • tvām: you
    • āgāṁsi: I approach
    • kṛṇavat: as a friend
    • sakhā: friend
    • te: your
    • mā: do not
    • ta: that
    • enasvanto: those who are without strength
    • yakṣin: may I not be harmed
    • bhujema: let me enjoy
    • yandhi: where
    • ṣmā: we
    • vipraḥ: the wise
    • stuvate: praise
    • varūtham: the great order
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra addresses Varuna, the deity associated with the cosmic order and the waters. It emphasizes the eternal nature of Varuna and the relationship between the devotee and the divine.
    • The phrase "I approach you as a friend" signifies the importance of a personal connection with the divine, suggesting that one can seek guidance and support from a higher power.
    • The plea "do not let me be harmed" reflects a universal human concern for safety and well-being, highlighting the need for protection in a world filled with uncertainties.
    • The mention of "the wise" who praise the great order indicates that wisdom and reverence for the cosmic laws are essential for a harmonious life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of distress or uncertainty, individuals often turn to prayer or meditation, seeking a connection with a higher power for guidance and protection, similar to the approach in this mantra.
    • In community gatherings, people may come together to express gratitude and seek blessings, reflecting the idea of collective praise for the divine order.
    • In personal relationships, the concept of approaching someone as a friend can be applied to foster trust and support, mirroring the relationship with the divine as depicted in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking a personal relationship with the divine, recognizing the eternal nature of cosmic order, and the need for protection and wisdom in life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as during personal challenges, community prayers, and fostering supportive relationships, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of reverence and gratitude towards the forces that govern existence, promoting a harmonious and balanced life.
