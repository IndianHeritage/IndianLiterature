तद व उक्तस्य बरह्मेन्द्रायोपस्तृणीषणि |विपो न यस्योतयो वियद रोहन्ति सक्षितः ||
tad va ukthasya barhaṇendrāyopastṛṇīṣaṇi | vipo na yasyotayo vi yad rohanti sakṣitaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • va: indeed
    • ukthasya: of the utterance or hymn
    • barhaṇendrāy: to the great lord of the birds (often interpreted as a reference to the divine or the supreme being)
    • upastṛṇīṣaṇi: may we be blessed or may we be granted
    • vipo: of the wise or learned
    • na: not
    • yasyotayo: whose rising or ascent
    • vi: indeed
    • yad: which
    • rohanti: flourish or grow
    • sakṣitaḥ: those who are aware or conscious
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the power of divine utterance and the blessings that come from it.
    • The reference to "barhaṇendrā" can be seen as a call to the supreme being, invoking their presence and guidance.
    • The phrase "may we be blessed" suggests a desire for spiritual growth and enlightenment.
    • The latter part emphasizes the importance of wisdom and awareness, indicating that true growth comes from understanding and consciousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often invoke the wisdom of their mentors or the collective knowledge of their teams to make informed decisions. This reflects the essence of seeking blessings from a higher source for guidance.
    • In personal development, individuals may meditate or recite affirmations to connect with their inner wisdom, paralleling the idea of flourishing through awareness and conscious living.
    • In education, teachers inspire students to seek knowledge and wisdom, fostering an environment where growth is encouraged through understanding and awareness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine guidance and the pursuit of wisdom in achieving personal and collective growth.
    • It serves as a reminder that true flourishing comes from a deep understanding of oneself and the world, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and remain conscious of their actions.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in leadership, personal development, or education, the principles embedded in this mantra can guide individuals towards a more enlightened and fulfilling existence.
