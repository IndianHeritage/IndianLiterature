वैश्वानरस्य दṁसनाभ्यो बृहद् अरिणाद् एकः स्वपस्यया कविः |उभा पितरा महयन्न अजायताग्निर द्यावापृथिवी भूरेरतसः ||
vaiśvānarasya daṁsanābhyo bṛhad ariṇād ekaḥ svapasyayā kaviḥ | ubhā pitarā mahayann ajāyatāgnir dyāvāpṛthivī bhūriretasā ||
  • vaiśvānarasya: This term refers to the universal or cosmic aspect of fire, symbolizing the divine presence that pervades all existence.
  • daṁsanābhyo: This word can be interpreted as "from the teeth" or "from the bite," suggesting a transformative power that can be both creative and destructive.
  • bṛhad: Meaning "great" or "vast," this word emphasizes the enormity of the cosmic fire and its significance in the universe.
  • ariṇād: This term can be understood as "from the enemy" or "from opposition," indicating the challenges and adversities that one may face.
  • ekaḥ: Meaning "one," this signifies unity and the singular nature of the divine force.
  • svapasyayā: This word refers to "through vision" or "by insight," highlighting the importance of perception and understanding in realizing the divine.
  • kaviḥ: This term means "the seer" or "the poet," representing those who have the ability to perceive deeper truths and convey them through wisdom.
  • ubhā: Meaning "both," this word indicates duality, suggesting the interplay of various forces in the universe.
  • pitarā: This term refers to "the ancestors" or "the forefathers," emphasizing the connection to lineage and heritage.
  • mahayann: Meaning "greatly nourishing," this word signifies the abundance and sustenance provided by the divine.
  • ajāyatā: This term means "was born" or "came into being," indicating the manifestation of life and existence.
  • agnir: Referring to "fire," this symbolizes transformation, purification, and energy.
  • dyāvāpṛthivī: This phrase means "heaven and earth," representing the duality of existence and the balance between the spiritual and material realms.
  • bhūriretasā: This term can be interpreted as "with great energy" or "with abundant vitality," emphasizing the dynamic force of creation.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of the cosmic fire, representing the unity of existence and the transformative power of divine energy. It highlights the importance of perception and wisdom in navigating life's challenges, as well as the connection to our ancestors and the nourishment they provide. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as seeking guidance during difficult times, recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings, and understanding the balance between the spiritual and material aspects of life. It serves as a reminder of the vastness of the universe and the potential for growth and transformation within each individual.
