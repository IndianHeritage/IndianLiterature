व्यूर्णवती दिवो अन्ताम् अबोध्य अप स्वसारं सन्तर युयोति |प्रमिनती मनुष्याः युगानि योषा जारस्य चक्षसा वि भाति ||
vyūrṇvatī divo antām̐ abodhy apa svasāraṁ sanutar yuyoti | praminatī manuṣyā yugāni yoṣā jārasya cakṣasā vi bhāti ||
  • vyūrṇvatī: This term can be interpreted as "spreading" or "expanding." It signifies the idea of growth and development, much like how a tree spreads its branches to reach for the sky.
  • divo antām̐: This phrase translates to "the end of the sky" or "the ultimate realm." It represents the highest aspirations or goals that one seeks to achieve, akin to reaching for one's dreams.
  • abodhy: Meaning "to awaken" or "to enlighten," this word emphasizes the importance of knowledge and awareness in our lives, similar to how education opens doors to new opportunities.
  • apa svasāraṁ: This can be understood as "the source of one's own being" or "the essence of oneself." It highlights the significance of self-awareness and understanding one's true nature, much like a person reflecting on their values and beliefs.
  • sanutar yuyoti: This phrase means "to unite or connect." It suggests the importance of relationships and community, similar to how teamwork in a workplace can lead to greater success.
  • praminatī: This term refers to "to measure" or "to evaluate." It signifies the need for assessment and reflection in our actions, much like how a student evaluates their progress in learning.
  • manuṣyā: This word means "human beings." It emphasizes the collective experience of humanity, reminding us of our shared struggles and triumphs.
  • yugāni: This translates to "ages" or "epochs," indicating the passage of time and the evolution of human experience, similar to how societies change and adapt over generations.
  • yoṣā: Meaning "woman," this term highlights the role of women in society, akin to the contributions of women in various fields such as science, art, and leadership.
  • jārasya: This word can be interpreted as "of the old" or "of the past," suggesting the importance of history and tradition in shaping our present, much like how cultural heritage influences modern identity.
  • cakṣasā: Meaning "with vision" or "with insight," this term emphasizes the need for clarity and understanding in our actions, similar to how a clear vision can guide a business strategy.
  • vi bhāti: This phrase translates to "shines" or "illuminates," symbolizing the light of knowledge and wisdom that guides us through life, much like a mentor who provides guidance and support.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the journey of human existence, emphasizing growth, self-awareness, community, and the importance of knowledge. It serves as a reminder that in our pursuit of goals, we must remain connected to our true selves and to one another. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, teamwork, and the appreciation of cultural heritage. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can strive for a more enlightened and connected existence, ultimately leading to a more harmonious society.
