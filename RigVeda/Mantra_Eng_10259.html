इन्द्रः सुत्रामां स्ववाम् अवोभिः सुमृळीको भवतु विश्ववेदाः | बाधतां द्वेषो अभयं कृतोतु सुवीर्यस्य पतयः स्याम ||
indraḥ sutrāmā svavām̐ avobhiḥ sumṛḻīko bhavatu viśvavedāḥ | bādhatāṁ dveṣo abhayaṁ kṛṇotu suvīryasya patayaḥ syāma ||
  • Indraḥ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Sutrāmā: This can be interpreted as "one who is connected or united," suggesting the importance of unity and coherence in actions and thoughts.
  • Sva-vām̐: Translates to "our own," indicating a sense of personal ownership and responsibility.
  • Avobhiḥ: Means "with the help of," emphasizing the need for support and collaboration in achieving goals.
  • Sumṛḻīko: This term can be understood as "well-being" or "prosperity," highlighting the aspiration for a flourishing life.
  • Bhavatu: Means "may it be," expressing a wish or desire for something to come to fruition.
  • Viśvavedāḥ: Refers to "all-knowing" or "universal knowledge," suggesting the importance of wisdom and understanding in life.
  • Bādhatāṁ: Translates to "may it be removed," indicating a desire to eliminate obstacles or negativity.
  • Dveṣo: Means "hatred" or "enmity," pointing to the need to overcome negative emotions and conflicts.
  • Abhayaṁ: Translates to "fearlessness," emphasizing the importance of courage and confidence in facing challenges.
  • Kṛṇotu: Means "may it create," suggesting the act of bringing forth positive outcomes.
  • Suvīryasya: Refers to "of the strong," indicating the value of strength and resilience in life.
  • Patayaḥ: Means "of the leaders" or "of the rulers," highlighting the role of leadership in guiding others.
  • Syāma: Translates to "may we be," expressing a collective aspiration for unity and strength.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, strength, and wisdom in overcoming challenges and fostering well-being. It encourages individuals to seek support, eliminate negativity, and cultivate fearlessness in their pursuits. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork in professional settings, community building, and personal development. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can strive for a harmonious and prosperous life, contributing positively to their surroundings and relationships.
