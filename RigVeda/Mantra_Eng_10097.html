जज्ञाना एव व्यबाधत स्पृधः प्रापश्यद वीरो अभि पौंस्यं रणम् |अवृश्चद अद्रिम अव सस्यदः सृजद अस्तभ्नान नाकं स्वपस्यया पृथुम् ||
jajñāna eva vy abādhata spṛdhaḥ prāpaśyad vīro abhi pauṁsyaṁ raṇam | avṛścad adrim ava sasyadaḥ sṛjad astabhnān nākaṁ svapasyayā pṛthum ||
  • jajñāna eva vy abādhata: This phrase can be translated as "knowledge alone is the ultimate truth." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in overcoming obstacles and challenges in life.
  • spṛdhaḥ prāpaśyad vīro abhi pauṁsyaṁ raṇam: Here, "the hero who desires to attain strength and valor in battle" signifies the pursuit of courage and strength in the face of adversity. It reflects the human spirit's quest for bravery and excellence.
  • avṛścad adrim ava sasyadaḥ: This part can be interpreted as "he who has overcome the mountains and has brought forth abundance." It symbolizes the triumph over challenges and the ability to create prosperity and growth.
  • sṛjad astabhnān nākaṁ svapasyayā pṛthum: This translates to "he who has established the heavens and has manifested the earth through his vision." It highlights the power of imagination and vision in shaping reality and achieving one's goals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of knowledge, courage, and the power of vision in overcoming life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as education, personal development, and leadership. For instance, a student striving for academic excellence embodies the pursuit of knowledge, while an entrepreneur facing market challenges demonstrates the heroism in seeking strength and innovation. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder that through knowledge and vision, one can navigate the complexities of life and achieve greatness.
