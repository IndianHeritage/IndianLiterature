ईर्मान्यद वपुषे वपुश्चक्रं रथस्य येमथुः |पर्य अन्यā नाहुषा युगā मह्ना राजांसी दीयथाḥ ||
īrmānyad vapuṣe vapuś cakraṁ rathasya yemathuḥ | pary anyā nāhuṣā yugā mahnā rajāṁsi dīyathaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • īrmānyad: O Lord, who is worthy of reverence
    • vapuṣe: in your form
    • vapuś: of your body
    • cakraṁ: the wheel
    • rathasya: of the chariot
    • yemathuḥ: who is the support
    • pary: around
    • anyā: other
    • nāhuṣā: of the great ones
    • yugā: ages
    • mahnā: by the great
    • rajāṁsi: kings
    • dīyathaḥ: may you be granted
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence, recognizing the Lord as a revered figure who embodies strength and support.
    • The reference to the "wheel of the chariot" symbolizes the cyclical nature of life and the journey we undertake.
    • The mention of "great ones" and "kings" suggests a connection to leadership and the responsibilities that come with power.
    • In real life, this can be seen in leaders who guide their communities, embodying strength and wisdom.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of reverence and respect for divine forces in our lives.
    • It highlights the need for strong leadership and the support that leaders must provide to their followers.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in various scenarios, such as in governance, community leadership, and personal development.
    • For instance, a community leader who embodies the qualities of strength and wisdom can inspire others to follow a righteous path.
