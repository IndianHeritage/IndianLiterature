अर्वाचीनो वसो भवास्मे सु मत्स्वान्धसः |सोमानाम इन्द्र सोमपाः ||
arvācīno vaso bhavāsme su matsvāndhasaḥ | somānām indra somapāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "arvācīno vaso bhavāsme su matsvāndhasaḥ | somānām indra somapāḥ ||"
  • Translation: "May I be endowed with the wealth of the present; may I be blessed with good thoughts; O Indra, the lord of the soma, grant me the essence of the soma."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • arvācīno: Refers to the present or contemporary; signifies the current time and its relevance.
    • vaso: Means wealth or prosperity; can be interpreted as both material and spiritual wealth.
    • bhavāsme: Translates to "may I become" or "may I be"; a plea for transformation or attainment.
    • su: Means good or auspicious; indicates a desire for positive outcomes.
    • matsvāndhasaḥ: Refers to good thoughts or wisdom; emphasizes the importance of mental clarity and positive thinking.
    • somānām: Pertains to soma, a sacred drink in Vedic rituals; symbolizes nourishment and divine inspiration.
    • indra: The king of gods in Vedic tradition; represents strength, power, and leadership.
    • somapāḥ: Means "the drinker of soma"; signifies those who partake in the divine essence, often associated with enlightenment and higher consciousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, "arvācīno vaso" can be related to the pursuit of contemporary knowledge and skills that lead to success in today's world, such as learning new technologies or adapting to changing job markets.
    • The phrase "su matsvāndhasaḥ" encourages individuals to cultivate positive thoughts and mental well-being, akin to practices like mindfulness and meditation that promote mental health.
    • Indra, as a symbol of strength, can be seen in leaders who inspire and empower others, demonstrating the importance of good leadership in various fields, from business to community service.
    • The concept of soma can be likened to the nourishment we seek in life, whether through education, relationships, or spiritual practices that enhance our overall well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of being present and seeking prosperity in all aspects of life, both materially and spiritually.
    • It highlights the significance of positive thinking and mental clarity, which are essential for personal growth and success.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in daily practices such as goal setting, mindfulness, and leadership, encouraging individuals to strive for a balanced and fulfilling life.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to seek divine inspiration and strength in our endeavors, fostering a holistic approach to life.
