प्राचीनं बर्हिः प्रदिशा पृथिव्या वस्तोर अस्य वृज्यते अग्रे अह्नाम् |व्युप्रथते वितरं वरियो देवेषु अदितये स्योनम् ||
prācīnam barhiḥ pradiśā pṛthivyā vastor asyā vṛjyate agre ahnām | vy u prathate vitaraṁ varīyo devebhyo aditaye syonam ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "The ancient sacrificial grass is spread out in the directions; it is offered to the earth, and it is not rejected in the presence of the sun. May the divine beings, who are the bestowers of wealth, grant us abundance."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • prācīnam: Ancient or traditional, referring to practices that have been followed for generations.
    • barhiḥ: Sacrificial grass, often used in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and sanctity.
    • pradiśā: Directions, indicating the importance of orientation and space in rituals.
    • pṛthivyā: Earth, representing the physical realm and the sustenance it provides.
    • vastor: Of the offering, emphasizing the significance of what is being presented.
    • asyā: This, referring to the specific offering being made.
    • vṛjyate: Is not rejected, indicating acceptance and approval.
    • agre: In the presence of, highlighting the importance of the context in which the offering is made.
    • ahnām: Of the sun, symbolizing light, energy, and life.
    • vy u: May we, a call for collective intention.
    • prathate: To spread or to manifest, indicating the act of sharing or distributing.
    • vitaraṁ: Abundance or wealth, referring to the blessings sought from the divine.
    • varīyo: The best or the most excellent, emphasizing the quality of what is being requested.
    • devebhyo: To the divine beings, acknowledging the higher powers that govern the universe.
    • aditaye: To the infinite or the boundless, representing the limitless nature of the divine.
    • syonam: Grant us, a plea for blessings and support.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural practices, farmers often perform rituals to honor the earth and seek blessings for a bountiful harvest, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • During festivals, offerings are made to deities, symbolizing gratitude and the desire for prosperity, akin to the invocation of divine beings in the mantra.
    • In personal development, individuals may use the principles of this mantra to cultivate a mindset of abundance and gratitude, focusing on the positive aspects of life.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the connection between the physical and spiritual realms. It highlights the importance of rituals in acknowledging the earth's gifts and seeking divine blessings for abundance. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire practices of gratitude, respect for nature, and the pursuit of prosperity, reminding us of our interconnectedness with the universe and the divine forces that guide our lives.
