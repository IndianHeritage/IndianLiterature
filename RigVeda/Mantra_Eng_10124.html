अस्य पिब क्षुमतः प्रस्थितस्येन्द्र सोमस्य वरम् ā सुतस्य |स्वस्तिदा मनसा मादयस्वार्वाचीनो रेवते सौभागाय ||
asya piba kṣumataḥ prasthitasyendra somasya varam ā sutasya | svastidā manasā mādayasvārvācīno revate saubhagāya ||
  • Mantra: "asya piba kṣumataḥ prasthitasyendra somasya varam ā sutasya | svastidā manasā mādayasvārvācīno revate saubhagāya ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as a call to partake in the blessings of the divine, specifically invoking the essence of the moon (Soma) and the prosperity it brings.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • asya: of this
    • piba: drink or partake
    • kṣumataḥ: from the hungry or those in need
    • prasthitasyendra: of the one who has set forth, referring to a leader or divine figure
    • somasya: of the moon or the divine nectar
    • varam: the best or the boon
    • ā sutasya: of the offspring or the progeny
    • svastidā: giver of well-being
    • manasā: with the mind
    • mādayasva: may you be delighted
    • ār vā cīno: from the ancient or the revered
    • revate: may it shine or prosper
    • saubhagāya: for good fortune or auspiciousness
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of sharing and partaking in the blessings of the divine, particularly in times of need.
    • It highlights the role of leaders and divine figures in guiding and providing for their followers.
    • The invocation of Soma symbolizes nourishment, both physical and spiritual, suggesting that true prosperity comes from a connection to the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied during festivals or gatherings where food and resources are shared, emphasizing the importance of generosity and support.
    • In leadership, it serves as a reminder for leaders to be mindful of their responsibilities towards their community, ensuring that they provide for those in need.
    • In personal life, it encourages individuals to seek spiritual nourishment and well-being, fostering a mindset of gratitude and appreciation for the blessings they receive.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of sharing divine blessings.
    • It encourages a mindset of abundance, gratitude, and responsibility, applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to community welfare.
    • By invoking the essence of prosperity and well-being, individuals can cultivate a life filled with joy, abundance, and positive relationships.
