के ते अग्ने रिपवे बन्धनासः के पायवः सनीषन्त द्युमन्तः |के धासिम अग्ने अनृतस्य पान्ति का आसतो वचसः सन्ति गोपाः ||
ke te agne ripave bandhanāsaḥ ke pāyavaḥ saniṣanta dyumantaḥ | ke dhāsim agne anṛtasya pānti ka āsato vacasaḥ santi gopāḥ ||
  • ke te agne ripave bandhanāsaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "Who are those, O Agni, who are bound by enmity?" Here, "Agni" refers to the fire god, symbolizing purity and the divine. The term "ripave" means enemies, and "bandhanāsaḥ" indicates being bound or tied. This line suggests a questioning of the nature of conflict and enmity in human relationships.
  • ke pāyavaḥ saniṣanta dyumantaḥ: This translates to "Who are those who protect and shine brightly?" The word "pāyavaḥ" refers to protectors or guardians, while "saniṣanta" means to shine or radiate. This line emphasizes the importance of guardianship and the role of those who bring light and protection in our lives, akin to mentors or leaders.
  • ke dhāsim agne anṛtasya pānti: This can be interpreted as "Who are those, O Agni, who nourish the truth?" The term "dhāsim" refers to those who serve or support, while "anṛtasya" means truth or righteousness. This line highlights the significance of nurturing truth and integrity in society, akin to the role of educators or truth-seekers.
  • ka āsato vacasaḥ santi gopāḥ: This translates to "Who are the protectors of the spoken word?" Here, "āsato" refers to the spoken or verbal, and "gopāḥ" means guardians or protectors. This line underscores the importance of safeguarding communication and the power of words, similar to the role of journalists or advocates for free speech.

Conclusion: The mantra reflects on the themes of enmity, protection, truth, and the power of words. In real life, it serves as a reminder to be aware of the conflicts we face, the importance of guardianship in our communities, the need to uphold truth, and the responsibility we have in our communication. It encourages individuals to strive for harmony, protect the vulnerable, and speak truthfully, making it applicable in various scenarios such as conflict resolution, leadership, education, and advocacy. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for introspection and action towards creating a more just and harmonious society.
