अव्ये पुनानं परि वार उर्मिणा हरिं नवन्ते अभि सप्त धेनवः | अपाम उपस्थे अध्य आयवः कविम ऋतस्य योना महिषा अहेषत ||
avye punānam pari vāra ūrmiṇā hariṁ navante abhi sapta dhenavaḥ | apām upasthe adhy āyavaḥ kavim ṛtasya yonā mahiṣā aheṣata ||
  • Mantra: "avye punānam pari vāra ūrmiṇā hariṁ navante abhi sapta dhenavaḥ | apām upasthe adhy āyavaḥ kavim ṛtasya yonā mahiṣā aheṣata ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • avye: unblemished, pure
    • punānam: purification, renewal
    • pari: around, surrounding
    • vāra: protection, shelter
    • ūrmiṇā: with waves, with energy
    • hariṁ: the divine, often referring to Vishnu or a deity
    • navante: new, fresh
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • sapta: seven
    • dhenavaḥ: cows, symbolizing wealth and nourishment
    • apām: of the waters
    • upasthe: in the presence, near
    • adhy: above, over
    • āyavaḥ: those who are seeking, aspirants
    • kavim: the wise one, the poet, the seer
    • ṛtasya: of truth, of order
    • yonā: womb, source
    • mahiṣā: the great one, often referring to a powerful entity
    • aheṣata: has been invoked, has been called upon
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the purity and renewal of the divine energy that surrounds us.
    • The mention of "seven cows" symbolizes abundance and prosperity, which can be seen in real life as the importance of nurturing resources and relationships.
    • The "waters" represent the source of life, emphasizing the need for purity in our thoughts and actions.
    • The invocation of the wise one suggests the importance of seeking knowledge and wisdom in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, the mantra can be applied to the collective effort of maintaining purity in the environment, such as organizing clean-up drives to protect water bodies.
    • In personal life, it can inspire individuals to seek renewal through education and self-improvement, much like how one might pursue a new skill or hobby.
    • In business, the principles of abundance and nurturing can be reflected in ethical practices that promote sustainability and community welfare.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of purity, abundance, and the pursuit of wisdom.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek renewal in our lives, whether through personal growth, community involvement, or environmental stewardship.
    • By embodying these principles, we can create a harmonious existence that benefits not only ourselves but also the world around us.
