यो अश्वस्य दधिक्राव्णो अकारीत समिद्धे अग्ना उषसो व्युष्टौ |अनागसं तं अदितिः कृतोतु स मित्रेण वरुणेन सजोषाः ||
yo aśvasya dadhikrāvṇo akārīt samiddhe agnā uṣaso vyuṣṭau | anāgasaṁ tam aditiḥ kṛṇotu sa mitreṇa varuṇenā sajoṣāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • aśvasya: of the horse
    • dadhikrāvṇo: who has made it strong
    • akārīt: has created
    • samiddhe: kindled
    • agnā: the fire
    • uṣaso: of the dawn
    • vyuṣṭau: in the light
    • anāgasaṁ: free from sin
    • tam: that
    • aditiḥ: Aditi (the mother of gods)
    • kṛṇotu: may create
    • sa: he
    • mitreṇa: with Mitra (the god of friendship)
    • varuṇenā: with Varuna (the god of cosmic order)
    • sajoṣāḥ: with joy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine forces associated with strength, creation, and purity.
    • The reference to the horse symbolizes power and vitality, often associated with the sun and the dawn.
    • The mention of Aditi signifies the nurturing aspect of the universe, representing the infinite and the mother of all gods.
    • Mitra and Varuna represent the dual aspects of friendship and cosmic order, emphasizing the importance of harmony and balance in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive environment, such as sports or business, invoking the strength and vitality of the horse can inspire individuals to perform at their best.
    • In personal relationships, the qualities of Mitra (friendship) and Varuna (cosmic order) can guide individuals to foster trust and balance in their interactions.
    • During challenging times, the nurturing aspect of Aditi can remind individuals to seek support and guidance from their community or family.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, purity, and harmony in life.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek balance in relationships and to draw upon the nurturing aspects of the universe during times of need.
    • By embodying the qualities of strength, friendship, and cosmic order, individuals can navigate life's challenges with grace and resilience.
