मन्त्र: पथस्-पथः परिपतिं वचस्याः कामेन कृतो अभ्य आनाḻ अर्कम् |स नो रासच छुरुधश चन्द्राग्राः धियं-धियं सीषधाति प्र पूषा ||
pathas-pathaḥ paripatiṁ vacasyā kāmena kṛto abhy ānaḻ arkam | sa no rāsac churudhaś candrāgrā dhiyaṁ-dhiyaṁ sīṣadhāti pra pūṣā ||
  • Pathas-pathaḥ paripatiṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the paths of the paths" or "the ways of the ways." It suggests a journey or exploration of various paths in life.
  • Vacasyā kāmena: This translates to "by the desire of speech." It emphasizes the power of words and communication in shaping our experiences and relationships.
  • Kṛto abhy ānaḻ arkam: This means "having made the sun (or light) shine." It signifies enlightenment, knowledge, and the pursuit of truth.
  • Sa no rāsac churudhaś: This can be understood as "may that which is sweet and nourishing come to us." It reflects a desire for abundance and positivity in life.
  • Candrāgrā dhiyaṁ-dhiyaṁ: This phrase refers to "the moon's peak of intelligence." It symbolizes wisdom, intuition, and the nurturing aspects of the mind.
  • Sīṣadhāti pra pūṣā: This translates to "may the nourishing one (Pūṣa) grant us strength." Pūṣa is a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking knowledge, communication, and nourishment in life. It highlights the importance of choosing the right paths, using our words wisely, and striving for enlightenment. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, effective communication in relationships, and the pursuit of knowledge in education. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can foster a more fulfilling and prosperous life, both personally and collectively.
