बृहस्पते तपुषाश्नेव विद्या वृकद्वारसो असुरस्य वीरान् |यथा जगहन्थ धृतता पुरा चिदेव जहि शत्रुम अस्माकं इन्द्र ||
bṛhaspate tapuṣāśneva vidhya vṛkadvaraso asurasya vīrān | yathā jaghantha dhṛṣatā purā cid evā jahi śatrum asmākam indra ||
  • Mantra: "bṛhaspate tapuṣāśneva vidhya vṛkadvaraso asurasya vīrān | yathā jaghantha dhṛṣatā purā cid evā jahi śatrum asmākam indra ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bṛhaspate: Referring to Brihaspati, the deity of wisdom and eloquence.
    • tapuṣā: By the power of austerity or penance.
    • śneva: Like the sun or with the brilliance of the sun.
    • vidhya: Knowledge or wisdom.
    • vṛkadvaraso: The one who is strong like a wolf.
    • asurasya: Of the demons or adversaries.
    • vīrān: Heroes or warriors.
    • yathā: Just as.
    • jaghantha: To defeat or to overcome.
    • dhṛṣatā: With courage or valor.
    • purā: In the past.
    • cid: Indeed or certainly.
    • evā: Thus or in this manner.
    • jahi: To conquer or to destroy.
    • śatrum: The enemy or adversary.
    • asmākam: Our or of us.
    • indra: Referring to Indra, the king of gods and god of thunder and rain.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Brihaspati, the deity associated with wisdom and knowledge, asking for strength and valor to overcome adversaries.
    • The reference to "tapuṣā" emphasizes the importance of discipline and austerity in gaining wisdom and strength, akin to how individuals in real life often undergo rigorous training or education to achieve their goals.
    • By likening the strength of the heroes to that of a wolf, the mantra highlights the qualities of courage and ferocity needed to face challenges, similar to how athletes train to embody these traits in competitions.
    • The phrase "yathā jaghantha dhṛṣatā purā" suggests that just as past heroes have triumphed through courage, we too can draw inspiration from their victories to confront our own challenges.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies the need for divine support in our endeavors, much like how people seek guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders in times of difficulty.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of wisdom, courage, and divine support in overcoming obstacles in life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as facing personal challenges, pursuing academic or professional goals, and engaging in competitive sports.
    • By embodying the qualities of discipline, strength, and valor, individuals can navigate their struggles and emerge victorious, much like the heroes of the past.
