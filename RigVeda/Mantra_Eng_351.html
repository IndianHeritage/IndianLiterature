त्वं अग्नि प्रथमो अङ्गिरा ऋषिर देवो देवानाम् अभवः शिवः सखा |तव व्रते कवयो विद्मनापसोऽजायन्त मरुतो भ्राजदृष्टयः ||
tvam agne prathamo aṅgirā ṛṣir devo devānām abhavaḥ śivaḥ sakhā | tava vrate kavayo vidmanāpaso 'jāyanta maruto bhrājadṛṣṭayaḥ ||
  • tvam agne: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni." Agni is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, light, and transformation. In real life, fire represents both physical and metaphorical energy, such as passion and creativity.
  • prathamo aṅgirā ṛṣir: This means "the first sage, Aṅgiras." Aṅgiras is one of the ancient sages known for wisdom and knowledge. In contemporary terms, this can be likened to the role of educators and mentors who guide us in our quest for knowledge.
  • devo devānām abhavaḥ: This translates to "the god among gods." It signifies the supreme nature of Agni, representing the highest form of divinity. In our lives, this can be seen in the pursuit of excellence and the aspiration to reach our highest potential.
  • śivaḥ sakhā: This means "the auspicious friend." It highlights the importance of companionship and support in our lives. Just as Agni is a friend to the gods, we too need supportive relationships that foster growth and positivity.
  • tava vrate: This translates to "in your vow." It emphasizes commitment and dedication. In real life, this can be related to personal goals and the promises we make to ourselves to achieve them.
  • kavayo vidmanā: This means "the wise ones know." It suggests that true wisdom comes from understanding and experience. In modern contexts, this can be seen in the value of learning from mentors and the importance of knowledge-sharing in communities.
  • apaso 'jāyanta: This translates to "the waters were born." Water symbolizes life and sustenance. In our lives, it can represent the nurturing aspects of nature and the importance of preserving our environment for future generations.
  • maruto bhrājadṛṣṭayaḥ: This means "the Maruts, with their shining vision." The Maruts are storm deities representing strength and vitality. In real life, this can be related to the power of teamwork and collaboration, where collective strength leads to greater achievements.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of divine energy, wisdom, and the importance of relationships and commitments in our lives. It serves as a reminder to seek knowledge, nurture our relationships, and strive for excellence. In practical terms, it encourages us to harness our inner fire (passion) and work collaboratively with others to achieve our goals, while also respecting and preserving the natural world around us. The significance of this mantra lies in its timeless relevance, guiding us in our personal and communal journeys towards growth and fulfillment.
