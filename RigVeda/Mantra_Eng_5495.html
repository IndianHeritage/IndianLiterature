अभि यं देवी निरृतिः चिद ईशे नक्षन्त इन्द्रं शरदः सुपृक्षः |उप त्रिबन्धुर जरदष्टिम एत्य अस्ववेशं यं कृतवन्त मार्ताः ||
abhi yaṁ devī nirṛtiś cid īśe nakṣanta indraṁ śaradaḥ supṛkṣaḥ | upa tribandhur jaradaṣṭim ety asvaveśaṁ yaṁ kṛṇavanta martāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "abhi yaṁ devī nirṛtiś cid īśe nakṣanta indraṁ śaradaḥ supṛkṣaḥ | upa tribandhur jaradaṣṭim ety asvaveśaṁ yaṁ kṛṇavanta martāḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra invokes the goddess Nirṛti, who is associated with misfortune and destruction, and seeks her blessings for protection and prosperity. It acknowledges Indra, the king of gods, and emphasizes the importance of strength and resilience in overcoming challenges.
  • Word-by-word Explanation:
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • yaṁ: whom
    • devī: goddess
    • nirṛtiś: associated with misfortune or destruction
    • cid: consciousness or awareness
    • īśe: lord or ruler
    • nakṣanta: to be seen or perceived
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods
    • śaradaḥ: autumn or the season of harvest
    • supṛkṣaḥ: well-protected or well-guarded
    • upa: near or close
    • tribandhur: one who binds or connects
    • jaradaṣṭim: with a strong or powerful form
    • ety: coming or arriving
    • asvaveśaṁ: one who is not easily defeated
    • yaṁ: whom
    • kṛṇavanta: those who create or make
    • martāḥ: mortals or human beings
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, such as losing a job or facing health issues, individuals often seek divine intervention or guidance, similar to invoking the goddess Nirṛti for protection and strength.
    • During challenging seasons, like economic downturns, communities come together to support each other, reflecting the essence of resilience and the collective strength emphasized in the mantra.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders may invoke the spirit of Indra by fostering teamwork and collaboration to overcome obstacles, ensuring that the organization remains well-guarded against challenges.
  • Conclusion: This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine protection and strength in the face of adversity. It serves as a reminder that even in difficult times, one can find resilience and support through community and faith. The invocation of both the goddess Nirṛti and Indra emphasizes the balance between acknowledging challenges and striving for prosperity and protection. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, from personal struggles to collective efforts in overcoming societal challenges, reinforcing the idea that strength and unity can lead to overcoming misfortune.
