गिर्वणः पाही नः सुतं मधोर धाराभिर अज्यसे |इन्द्र त्वादातमिद यशः ||
girvaṇaḥ pāhi naḥ sutam madhor dhārābhir ajyase | indra tvādātam id yaśaḥ ||
  • girvaṇaḥ: This word refers to the "singer" or "poet," often associated with the divine or the celestial beings who express their thoughts and emotions through hymns.
  • pāhi: This means "protect" or "guard." It signifies a plea for safeguarding and support.
  • naḥ: This translates to "us" or "our," indicating a collective identity or community.
  • sutam: This word means "son" or "offspring," symbolizing the continuation of lineage and the importance of family.
  • madhor: This means "of the sweet" or "pleasant," often referring to something that is enjoyable or desirable.
  • dhārābhir: This translates to "with streams" or "with flows," suggesting abundance and nourishment.
  • ajyase: This means "you are invoked" or "you are called upon," indicating a request for divine attention or intervention.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • tvādātam: This means "from you" or "by you," indicating a source of strength or support.
  • id: This translates to "this" or "here," pointing to the immediate context or situation.
  • yaśaḥ: This means "glory" or "fame," representing the achievements and recognition one seeks in life.

The mantra can be interpreted as a heartfelt invocation for protection and blessings. The speaker is asking for the divine to safeguard their offspring and provide them with the sweetness of life, represented by abundance and nourishment. The mention of Indra emphasizes the need for strength and support from a higher power to achieve glory and success.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Family Protection: Parents often seek blessings for their children, hoping for their safety and success in life. This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing and protecting the next generation.
  • Community Support: In times of crisis, communities come together to support one another. This mantra highlights the collective responsibility to protect and uplift each other.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can invoke this mantra during challenging times, seeking strength and guidance to overcome obstacles and achieve their goals.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the significance of protection, support, and the pursuit of glory. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of family, community, and the divine, encouraging individuals to seek strength from higher powers while nurturing their relationships and aspirations.
