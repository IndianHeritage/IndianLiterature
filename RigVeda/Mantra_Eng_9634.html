अग्निः ह त्यं जरतः कर्णम आवाग्निर अद्भ्यो निर अदहज जरूथम् |अग्निः अत्रिं घरम उरुष्यद अन्तर अग्निः नृमेदहम् प्रजायासृजत सम ||
agnir ha tyaṁ jarataḥ karṇam āvāgnir adbhyo nir adahaj jarūtham | agnir atriṁ gharma uruṣyad antar agnir nṛmedham prajayāsṛjat sam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Agnir: Fire
    • ha: indeed
    • tyaṁ: this
    • jarataḥ: of the one who is born
    • karṇam: ear
    • āvāgnir: may the fire protect
    • adbhyo: from the waters
    • nir: free from
    • adahaj: the burning
    • jarūtham: the one who is born
    • atriṁ: the one who is worthy of praise
    • gharma: warmth
    • uruṣyad: may it spread
    • antar: within
    • nṛmedham: the human being
    • prajayāsṛjat: may it create progeny
    • sam: together
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of fire, symbolizing purity, transformation, and energy.
    • It emphasizes the protective and nurturing qualities of fire, suggesting that it can shield us from negative influences (represented by water and burning).
    • The mantra also highlights the importance of warmth and praise, indicating that fire is not just a physical element but also a metaphor for human relationships and community.
    • It suggests that fire has the power to foster life and create progeny, symbolizing creativity and growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is used in rituals to purify and protect individuals and communities, such as in weddings or religious ceremonies.
    • Fire can also represent passion and creativity in artistic endeavors, where the 'fire' of inspiration leads to the creation of new works.
    • In a more practical sense, fire is essential for cooking and warmth, which are fundamental to human survival and comfort.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the multifaceted role of fire in our lives, serving as a protector, nurturer, and creator.
    • Its application can be seen in various aspects of life, from personal growth and relationships to cultural rituals and practical needs.
    • By understanding and respecting the power of fire, we can harness its energy for positive transformation in our lives and communities.
