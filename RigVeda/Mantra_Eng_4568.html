प्र तुविद्युम्नस्य स्थविरस्य घृष्वर दिवो ररप्शे महिमा पृथिव्याः |नास्य शत्रुर न प्रतिमानम अस्ति न प्रतिष्ठिः पुरुमायस्य सह्योः ||
pra tuvidyumnasya sthavirasya ghṛṣver divo rarapśe mahimā pṛthivyāḥ | nāsya śatrur na pratimānam asti na pratiṣṭhiḥ purumāyasya sahyoḥ ||
  • pra tuvidyumnasya: This phrase can be interpreted as "for the one who is knowledgeable" or "for the one who has attained wisdom." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in life.
  • sthavirasya: This word means "of the steadfast" or "of the stable." It signifies strength and resilience, qualities that are essential for overcoming challenges.
  • ghṛṣver: This term refers to "the one who is radiant" or "the one who shines." It symbolizes enlightenment and the pursuit of truth.
  • dīvo: Meaning "of the heavens," this word connects the mantra to the divine and the cosmic order, suggesting a higher purpose or guidance.
  • rarapśe: This can be understood as "to be praised" or "to be revered," indicating the importance of respect and honor in our lives.
  • mahimā: This word translates to "greatness" or "majesty," highlighting the significance of recognizing and aspiring to greatness in our endeavors.
  • pṛthivyāḥ: Meaning "of the earth," it grounds the mantra in the physical world, reminding us of our connection to nature and the material realm.
  • nāsya: This means "there is not" or "none," indicating the absence of negativity or obstacles.
  • śatrur: This word translates to "enemy," suggesting that with wisdom and strength, one can overcome adversities.
  • na pratimānam asti: This phrase means "there is no comparison," emphasizing the uniqueness of the individual’s journey and experiences.
  • na pratiṣṭhiḥ: This translates to "there is no foundation," indicating that without wisdom and strength, one may lack stability in life.
  • purumāyasya: This term refers to "the ancient" or "the primordial," connecting the mantra to timeless wisdom and traditions.
  • sahyoḥ: This means "of the companions" or "of the allies," highlighting the importance of community and support in achieving greatness.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of wisdom, strength, and community in overcoming challenges and achieving greatness. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, teamwork, and leadership. For instance, in a workplace setting, fostering a culture of knowledge sharing and support can lead to greater innovation and success. Similarly, in personal relationships, valuing wisdom and resilience can help individuals navigate difficulties and grow together. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that with the right qualities, one can rise above adversities and shine brightly in the world.
