गोमायुर एको अजमायुर एकः पृष्णिर एको हरित एक एषाम् |समानं नाम बिभ्रतो विरूपाः पुरुत्रा वाचं पिपिषुर वदन्तः ||
gomāyur eko ajamāyur ekaḥ pṛśnir eko harita eka eṣām | samānaṁ nāma bibhrato virūpāḥ purutrā vācam pipiśur vadantaḥ ||
  • gomāyur: This term refers to the "cow" (go) and signifies nourishment, wealth, and abundance. In many cultures, cows are considered sacred and are a source of sustenance.
  • eko: Meaning "one" or "single," this word emphasizes unity and singularity in existence.
  • ajamāyur: This translates to "the unborn" or "immortal," suggesting a state of eternal existence beyond physical birth and death.
  • pṛśnir: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is green" or "the flourishing one," symbolizing growth, vitality, and prosperity.
  • harita: Meaning "green" or "fresh," it represents life, fertility, and the natural world.
  • eṣām: This word means "of these" or "of them," indicating a connection or relationship among the subjects being discussed.
  • samānaṁ: This translates to "similar" or "equal," suggesting a sense of equality and shared characteristics among beings.
  • nāma: Meaning "name," it signifies identity and the importance of names in defining existence.
  • bibhrato: This term means "those who bear" or "those who hold," indicating responsibility and the act of carrying something significant.
  • virūpāḥ: This translates to "having different forms" or "varied," suggesting diversity in existence and the many forms life can take.
  • purutrā: Meaning "in many places" or "everywhere," it emphasizes the omnipresence of the divine or the essence being discussed.
  • vācam: This term means "speech" or "voice," highlighting the importance of communication and expression in human experience.
  • pipiśur: This translates to "those who drink" or "those who consume," indicating the act of taking in knowledge or experiences.
  • vadantaḥ: Meaning "speaking" or "those who speak," it emphasizes the power of words and the act of sharing wisdom.

Conclusion: The mantra reflects the interconnectedness of life, emphasizing unity, diversity, and the importance of communication. It highlights how different forms of existence, while varied, share a common essence. In real life, this can be seen in the way communities thrive on diversity, where each individual contributes uniquely to the collective. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder to appreciate the unity in diversity, fostering respect and understanding among different cultures and beings. It encourages us to recognize the shared identity and responsibilities we hold in our interactions, promoting harmony and cooperation in our daily lives.
