इन्द्रः सुतेषु सोमेषु क्रतुम् पुनीत उक्त्यम् |विदे वृधस्य दक्षसो महान् हि षः ||
indraḥ suteṣu someṣu kratum punīta ukthyam | vide vṛdhasya dakṣaso mahān hi ṣaḥ ||
  • Indraḥ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Suteṣu: Means "in the offerings" or "in the libations," indicating the context of rituals and sacrifices.
  • Someṣu: Refers to "in the soma," which is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration.
  • Kratu: Translates to "intellect" or "sacrifice," representing the mental and spiritual effort involved in rituals.
  • Punīta: Means "purified" or "sacred," emphasizing the importance of purity in thoughts and actions.
  • Ukthyam: Refers to "the hymn" or "the chant," highlighting the significance of verbal expressions in rituals.
  • Vide: Means "to know" or "to understand," indicating the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom.
  • Vṛdhasya: Refers to "of the strong" or "of the powerful," suggesting the importance of strength in achieving goals.
  • Dakṣaso: Means "of the skilled" or "of the capable," emphasizing the value of skill and competence.
  • Mahān: Translates to "great" or "mighty," indicating the significance of greatness in character and deeds.
  • Hi: A particle that can mean "indeed" or "certainly," reinforcing the statement that follows.
  • Ṣaḥ: Refers to "six," which can symbolize completeness or a holistic approach in various contexts.

  • Meaning: This mantra invokes Indra, emphasizing the importance of offerings and the sacredness of rituals. It highlights the need for purity in thoughts and actions, the pursuit of knowledge, and the value of strength and skill in achieving greatness.
  • Real-life Example: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as leadership in organizations, where a leader must be knowledgeable, skilled, and capable of inspiring others. For instance, a project manager leading a team must ensure that the team is well-prepared (purified) and that their efforts (sacrifices) are directed towards a common goal (greatness).

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the integration of strength, skill, knowledge, and purity in our actions. It serves as a reminder that true greatness is achieved through a combination of these qualities, applicable in personal development, leadership, and community building.
