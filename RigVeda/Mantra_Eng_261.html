उरुं हि राजा वरुणश चकार सूर्याय पन्थाम अन्वेतवा उ |अपदे पादाः प्रतिधातवेऽकर उतापवक्ताऽहृदयाविधश्चित् ||
uruṁ hi rājā varuṇaś cakāra sūryāya panthām anvetavā u | apade pādā pratidhātave 'kar utāpavaktā hṛdayāvidhaś cit ||
  • uruṁ hi rājā varuṇaś cakāra: This phrase translates to "Indeed, the king Varuna has created." Varuna is a deity associated with the cosmic order and the waters. In real life, this can be likened to the role of leaders or governing bodies that establish laws and order in society.
  • sūryāya panthām anvetavā u: This means "He follows the path of the Sun." The Sun symbolizes light, knowledge, and truth. In practical terms, this can be seen in how individuals or societies strive to follow a path of enlightenment and righteousness, akin to pursuing education or moral values.
  • apade pādā pratidhātave: This translates to "In difficulties, he supports the feet." This suggests that in times of trouble, there is a divine or supportive presence that helps individuals stand firm. In everyday life, this can be reflected in the support systems we have, such as family, friends, or community organizations that help us during challenging times.
  • 'kar utāpavaktā hṛdayāvidhaś cit: This means "He who speaks with a heart that knows." This emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in communication. In real life, this can be seen in leaders or mentors who guide others with compassion and insight, fostering a deeper connection and understanding.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, guidance, and support in the face of adversity. It highlights the significance of following a righteous path, seeking knowledge, and the importance of community support. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as governance, education, and personal relationships, reminding us of the values of integrity, wisdom, and compassion in our daily lives.
