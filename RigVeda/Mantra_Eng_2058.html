दधन्वे वा यद ईम अनु वोचद ब्रह्माणि वेरु तत् |परि विश्वानि काव्याः नेमिश चक्रम् इवाभवत् ||
dadhanve vā yad īm anu vocad brahmāṇi ver u tat | pari viśvāni kāvyā nemiś cakram ivābhavat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dadhanve: One who possesses strength or power.
    • vā: or.
    • yad: that which.
    • īm: we invoke.
    • anu: after.
    • vocad: has spoken.
    • brahmāṇi: in the divine or cosmic order.
    • ver: strength or might.
    • u: and.
    • tat: that.
    • pari: all around.
    • viśvāni: all beings or entities.
    • kāvyā: poetic or creative.
    • nemiś: like a wheel.
    • cakram: circle or cycle.
    • ivābhavat: has become or manifested.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of invoking strength and power that is inherent in the cosmic order.
    • It emphasizes the importance of creativity and poetic expression as a means to connect with the divine.
    • The imagery of a wheel suggests the cyclical nature of life and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, invoking the strength of teamwork can lead to creative solutions, much like how the mantra calls for collective strength.
    • Artists and poets often draw inspiration from the universe, reflecting the idea of creativity being a divine expression.
    • The concept of cycles can be seen in nature, such as the changing seasons, which remind us of the continuous flow of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of invoking strength and creativity in our lives.
    • It teaches us to recognize the interconnectedness of all beings and the cyclical nature of existence.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying these principles can lead to personal growth, enhanced creativity, and a deeper understanding of our place in the universe.
