कृतं चिद्धि स्मा सनेमि द्वेषोऽग्न इनोषि मार्तात् |इत्त्था यजमानादृतावः ||
kṛtaṁ cid dhi ṣmā sanemi dveṣo 'gna inoṣi martāt | itthā yajamānād ṛtāvaḥ ||
  • kṛtaṁ cid dhi: This phrase can be translated as "whatever has been done" or "that which is accomplished." It emphasizes the importance of actions and their outcomes in the context of rituals and life.
  • ṣmā sanemi: This means "we are united" or "we come together." It highlights the significance of community and collaboration in achieving common goals.
  • dveṣo 'gna: Translated as "the enmity or hatred," this part warns against negative emotions that can disrupt harmony and unity among individuals.
  • inoṣi martāt: This phrase can be understood as "we are liberated from mortality" or "we transcend death." It speaks to the idea of spiritual elevation and the quest for immortality through righteous actions.
  • itthā yajamānād: This translates to "thus, from the worshipper" or "in this manner, from the one who performs the sacrifice." It indicates the role of the individual in the process of worship and the importance of intention behind actions.
  • ṛtāvaḥ: This term refers to "truth" or "righteousness." It underscores the value of living a life aligned with truth and moral principles.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the significance of collective action, unity, and the pursuit of truth while warning against negative emotions like hatred. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as community building, conflict resolution, and personal development. For instance, in a workplace setting, fostering collaboration and addressing conflicts with understanding can lead to a more productive environment. Similarly, in personal relationships, focusing on shared goals and values can strengthen bonds and promote harmony. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the power of positive actions and the importance of striving for a life rooted in truth and unity.
