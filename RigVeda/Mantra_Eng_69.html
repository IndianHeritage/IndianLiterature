या एकश्चरषणीनां वसूनामिरज्यति |इन्द्रः पञ्च क्षितीनाम् ||
ya ekaś carṣaṇīnāṁ vasūnām irajyati | indraḥ pañca kṣitīnām ||
  • Mantra: "ya ekaś carṣaṇīnāṁ vasūnām irajyati | indraḥ pañca kṣitīnām ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who
    • ekaś: alone
    • carṣaṇīnāṁ: of the seers or sages
    • vasūnām: of the wealth or riches
    • irajyati: is worshipped or revered
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods
    • pañca: five
    • kṣitīnām: of the earths or realms
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a singular entity or force that is revered by the sages and is associated with wealth and prosperity.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, is acknowledged for his power and influence over the five realms of existence.
    • The phrase "ya ekaś" emphasizes the importance of unity and singularity in the pursuit of wealth and spiritual enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the idea of focusing on a single goal or purpose, much like how successful individuals often concentrate their efforts on one primary objective to achieve greatness.
    • The reverence for wealth can be seen in how people strive for financial stability and success, often seeking wisdom from mentors (the "sages") who guide them in their pursuits.
    • Indra's role can be likened to leadership in organizations, where a strong leader (like Indra) guides their team (the five realms) towards achieving collective goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity, focus, and reverence in the pursuit of wealth and success.
    • It teaches us that while individual efforts are important, the guidance of wise mentors and leaders can significantly enhance our journey.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal and professional growth, encouraging individuals to seek wisdom and remain focused on their goals.
