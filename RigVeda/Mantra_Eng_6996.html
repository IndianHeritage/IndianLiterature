यथा कलां यथा शफं यथा ऋणं संनयामसि |एवां दुष्ष्वप्न्यं सर्वम आप्त्ये सं नयामस्य अनहसो व उतयः सुतयः व उतयः ||
yathā kalāṁ yathā śaphaṁ yatha ṛṇaṁ saṁnayāmasi | evā duṣṣvapnyaṁ sarvam āptye saṁ nayāmasy anehaso va ūtayaḥ suūtayo va ūtayaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yathā kalāṁ yathā śaphaṁ yatha ṛṇaṁ saṁnayāmasi | evā duṣṣvapnyaṁ sarvam āptye saṁ nayāmasy anehaso va ūtayaḥ suūtayo va ūtayaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yathā: as, in the way
    • kalāṁ: portion, part
    • yathā: as, in the way
    • śaphaṁ: promise, vow
    • yatha: as, in the way
    • ṛṇaṁ: debt, obligation
    • saṁnayāmasi: we bind, we control
    • evā: thus, in this way
    • duṣṣvapnyaṁ: bad dreams, nightmares
    • sarvam: all, everything
    • āptye: in attainment, in fulfillment
    • saṁ: together, with
    • nayāmasy: of control, of restraint
    • anehaso: without harm, without injury
    • va: or
    • ūtayaḥ: those who are elevated, those who rise
    • suūtayo: those who are well-formed, those who are good
    • va: or
    • ūtayaḥ: those who rise
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the control and management of various aspects of life, including our promises, obligations, and even our dreams.
    • The repetition of "yathā" emphasizes the importance of consistency in our actions and thoughts.
    • It suggests that by binding ourselves to our commitments (kalāṁ and śaphaṁ), we can navigate through life's challenges (ṛṇaṁ).
    • The phrase "duṣṣvapnyaṁ" indicates that we can overcome negative experiences, such as bad dreams or fears, through our conscious efforts.
    • The mantra concludes with the idea that through control and restraint, we can achieve fulfillment and rise above our limitations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when an individual commits to a project (śaphaṁ), they must manage their time and resources (kalāṁ) effectively to meet deadlines (ṛṇaṁ).
    • When facing personal challenges, such as anxiety or fear (duṣṣvapnyaṁ), one can practice mindfulness and meditation to regain control and achieve peace.
    • In relationships, honoring promises and obligations fosters trust and strengthens bonds, leading to a fulfilling connection.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of self-discipline, commitment, and the power of positive thinking in overcoming life's obstacles.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to professional success, emphasizing that through control and dedication, we can rise above challenges and achieve our goals.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that our thoughts and actions shape our reality, and by managing them wisely, we can create a fulfilling life.
