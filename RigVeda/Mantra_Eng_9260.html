दिवस परि प्रथमं जजñe अग्निर अस्मद द्वितीयं परि जातवेदाḥ |तृतीयं अप्सु नृमणा अजस्रम इन्धान एनं जरते स्वाधीḥ ||
divas pari prathamaṁ jajñe agnir asmad dvitīyam pari jātavedāḥ | tṛtīyam apsu nṛmaṇā ajasram indhāna enaṁ jarate svādhīḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • divas: of the sky or heaven
    • pari: around or surrounding
    • prathamaṁ: first
    • jajñe: was born or came into being
    • agnir: fire
    • asmad: our
    • dvitīyam: second
    • jātavedāḥ: the one who knows all births (a name for Agni)
    • tṛtīyam: third
    • apsu: in the waters
    • nṛmaṇā: of mankind or human beings
    • ajasram: constantly or perpetually
    • indhāna: fuel or kindling
    • enaṁ: him or it
    • jarate: ages or grows old
    • svādhīḥ: the wise or knowledgeable
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the creation and significance of Agni (fire) in three stages:
      • The first stage is Agni's birth in the heavens, symbolizing the divine and celestial nature of fire.
      • The second stage refers to Agni as the one who knows all births, indicating his role in human life and rituals.
      • The third stage connects Agni with water and humanity, emphasizing the essential role of fire in sustaining life.
    • Agni is not just a physical element but represents knowledge, transformation, and the connection between the divine and the earthly realms.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is used in rituals and ceremonies, such as weddings or religious offerings, symbolizing purification and the presence of the divine.
    • In modern life, fire is essential for cooking, heating, and energy, showcasing its ongoing importance in daily existence.
    • Metaphorically, Agni can represent the inner fire of passion and creativity that drives individuals to pursue their goals and aspirations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the multifaceted nature of Agni, representing not only a physical element but also a spiritual force that connects humanity with the cosmos.
    • Its significance lies in understanding the balance between the elements and the importance of fire in both practical and spiritual aspects of life.
    • In real-life scenarios, recognizing the role of Agni can inspire individuals to harness their inner fire for personal growth, creativity, and connection with the world around them.
