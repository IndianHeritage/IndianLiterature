अस्य श्रेष्ठा सुभगस्य संदृग देवस्य चित्रतमां मार्त्येषु |शुचि घृतं न तप्तम अग्न्यायाः स्पार्हा देवस्य मंहनैव धेनोः ||
asya śreṣṭhā subhagasya saṁdṛg devasya citratamā martyeṣu | śuci ghṛtaṁ na taptam aghnyāyāḥ spārhā devasya maṁhaneva dhenoḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asya: of this
    • śreṣṭhā: the best or supreme
    • subhagasya: of the fortunate or auspicious
    • saṁdṛg: vision or sight
    • devasya: of the divine or god
    • citratamā: most colorful or diverse
    • martyeṣu: among mortals
    • śuci: pure
    • ghṛtaṁ: clarified butter or ghee
    • na: not
    • taptam: heated or burnt
    • aghnyāyāḥ: of the non-violent or sinless
    • spārhā: desire or aspiration
    • maṁhaneva: like the great
    • dhenoḥ: of the cow
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the supreme vision of the divine that is present among mortals.
    • It emphasizes the purity and auspiciousness of offerings, particularly clarified butter, which should not be burnt or tainted.
    • The reference to the cow symbolizes nourishment and abundance, suggesting that divine blessings are akin to the richness provided by a cow.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be reflected in the way we approach our goals and aspirations. Just as the divine vision is supreme, we should strive for clarity and purity in our intentions.
    • When preparing food or offerings, ensuring that they are pure and untainted can be seen as a metaphor for living a life free from negativity and harm.
    • The cow, revered in many cultures, represents the importance of nurturing and sustaining life, reminding us to respect and care for our environment and resources.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of purity, auspiciousness, and divine vision in our lives.
    • It encourages us to aspire for higher ideals while remaining grounded in our actions and intentions.
    • In a broader context, it serves as a reminder to cultivate a life that is harmonious, respectful, and nurturing, both to ourselves and to the world around us.
