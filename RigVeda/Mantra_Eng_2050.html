अस्य रण्वा स्वस्येव पुष्टिः संदृष्टिर अस्य हियानस्य दक्षोः |विओ भरिभ्रद ओषधिषु जिह्वाम अत्यो न रथ्यो डोधवीति वारान ||
asya raṇvā svasyeva puṣṭiḥ saṁdṛṣṭir asya hiyānasya dakṣoḥ | vi yo bharibhrad oṣadhīṣu jihvām atyo na rathyo dodhavīti vārān ||
  • Mantra: "asya raṇvā svasyeva puṣṭiḥ saṁdṛṣṭir asya hiyānasya dakṣoḥ | vi yo bharibhrad oṣadhīṣu jihvām atyo na rathyo dodhavīti vārān ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asya: of this
    • raṇvā: of the warrior
    • svasyeva: like one's own
    • puṣṭiḥ: nourishment or strength
    • saṁdṛṣṭir: vision or perception
    • asya: of this
    • hiyānasya: of the one who is skilled
    • dakṣoḥ: capable or dexterous
    • vi: who
    • yo: who
    • bharibhrad: bearing or carrying
    • oṣadhīṣu: among the herbs or plants
    • jihvām: tongue
    • atyō: beyond
    • na: not
    • rathyo: of the chariots
    • dodhavīti: moves or travels
    • vārān: the best or the most excellent
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the strength and nourishment that a warrior receives, akin to the support one gets from their own self.
    • It emphasizes the importance of skill and capability in achieving success, suggesting that a skilled person can perceive and utilize resources effectively.
    • The reference to "oṣadhīṣu" (herbs) indicates the connection between nature and human strength, highlighting how natural resources can empower individuals.
    • The mantra also suggests that true excellence is not just about physical prowess but also about the wisdom to navigate challenges without being hindered by obstacles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A soldier preparing for battle draws strength from their training and the support of their comrades, similar to the nourishment described in the mantra.
    • A skilled professional, such as a surgeon, relies on their dexterity and knowledge to perform complex procedures, embodying the essence of "dakṣoḥ."
    • In agriculture, farmers utilize natural resources (oṣadhīṣu) to cultivate crops, demonstrating the connection between nature and sustenance.
    • In sports, athletes often face challenges that require not just physical strength but also mental resilience, reflecting the idea of moving beyond obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of inner strength, skill, and the ability to perceive and utilize resources effectively in life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal development to professional excellence, reminding us that true power comes from a combination of self-awareness, skill, and the ability to navigate challenges.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can strive for excellence in their respective fields, drawing strength from both their inner capabilities and the resources available to them.
