सेहान उग्र पृतना अभि द्रुहः शचीपत इन्द्र विश्वाभिर ऊतिभिः |माध्यंदिनस्य सवनस्य वृत्रहन्न अनेद्य पिबा सोमस्य वज्रिवः ||
sehāna ugra pṛtanā abhi druhaḥ śacīpata indra viśvābhir ūtibhiḥ | mādhyaṁdinasya savanasya vṛtrahann anedya pibā somasya vajrivaḥ ||
  • Sehāna: This term can be interpreted as "together" or "united." It emphasizes the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving goals.
  • Ugra: Meaning "fierce" or "powerful," this word signifies strength and intensity, often associated with divine forces or natural phenomena.
  • Pṛtanā: This refers to "the one who is praised" or "the one who is invoked." It highlights the act of calling upon divine powers for assistance.
  • Abhi Druhaḥ: This phrase can be understood as "to overcome" or "to conquer." It suggests a proactive approach to challenges and adversities.
  • Śacīpata: This term refers to "the lord of strength" or "the powerful one," often associated with the deity Indra, who embodies strength and valor.
  • Indra: A prominent deity in Vedic literature, Indra is known as the king of gods and the god of thunder, rain, and war. He represents the force of nature and the protector of humanity.
  • Viśvābhir ūtibhiḥ: This phrase translates to "with all kinds of blessings" or "with universal prosperity." It signifies the abundance and support that comes from divine forces.
  • Mādhyaṁdinasya: This term means "of the midday" or "the peak of the day," symbolizing a time of clarity, strength, and energy.
  • Savanasya: Referring to "the offering" or "the sacrifice," it emphasizes the importance of giving and sharing in spiritual practices.
  • Vṛtrahann: This term means "the slayer of Vritra," where Vritra represents obstacles or challenges. It signifies the triumph over difficulties.
  • Anedya: This can be interpreted as "unfailing" or "everlasting," suggesting the enduring nature of divine support.
  • Pibā: Meaning "to drink," this word symbolizes the act of receiving blessings or nourishment from the divine.
  • Somasya: Referring to "of the soma," which is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, it represents spiritual nourishment and enlightenment.
  • Vajrivaḥ: This term means "the one who wields the thunderbolt," symbolizing strength, power, and the ability to overcome challenges.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support and strength in overcoming life's challenges. It emphasizes the importance of unity, collaboration, and the proactive approach to facing adversities. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork in professional settings, community support during crises, or personal struggles where one seeks strength and guidance. By invoking the blessings of divine forces, individuals can find the courage and resilience needed to navigate through difficulties, ultimately leading to personal growth and collective prosperity.
