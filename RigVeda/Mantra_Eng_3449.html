सजोषस आदित्यैर माद्यध्वं सजोषस ऋभवः पर्वतेभिः |सजोषसो दैव्येन सवित्रा सजोषसः सिंधुभी रत्नधेबिः ||
sajoṣasa ādityair mādayadhvaṁ sajoṣasa ṛbhavaḥ parvatebhiḥ | sajoṣaso daivyenā savitrā sajoṣasaḥ sindhubhī ratnadhebhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sajoṣasa: Together with joy or happiness
    • ādityair: With the Sun (Adityas)
    • mādayadhvaṁ: May you be delighted or filled with joy
    • ṛbhavaḥ: With the wise or learned
    • parvatebhiḥ: With the mountains
    • daivyenā: By divine means
    • savitrā: By the Sun (Savitar)
    • sindhubhī: With the oceans or rivers
    • ratnadhebhiḥ: With precious gems or treasures
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of the Sun and nature, emphasizing the importance of joy, wisdom, and the abundance of natural resources.
    • It suggests a harmonious relationship between humans and the divine, as well as with the elements of nature, such as mountains and rivers.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be reflected in the way we appreciate nature. For instance, when hiking in the mountains, one can feel a sense of joy and connection to the environment, embodying the essence of "sajoṣasa parvatebhiḥ."
    • In a community gathering, sharing knowledge and wisdom can create a joyful atmosphere, resonating with "mādayadhvaṁ ṛbhavaḥ."
    • Celebrating festivals that honor the Sun, such as Makar Sankranti, can be seen as a way to invoke the blessings of "ādityair" and "savitrā," bringing joy and abundance to the community.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, joy, and the recognition of the divine in nature. It encourages individuals to seek happiness through connection with the environment and each other.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire practices of gratitude, environmental stewardship, and community bonding, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all life.
