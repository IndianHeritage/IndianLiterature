अग्निम्-अग्निं वः समिधा दुवस्यत प्रियं-प्रियं वो अतिथिं गृणीषाणि |उप वो गीरभिर अमृतं विवासत देवो देवेषु वनते हि वार्यं देवो देवेषु वनते हि नो दुवः ||
agnim-agniṁ vaḥ samidhā duvasyata priyam-priyaṁ vo atithiṁ gṛṇīṣaṇi | upa vo gīrbhir amṛtaṁ vivāsata devo deveṣu vanate hi vāryaṁ devo deveṣu vanate hi no duvaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:

    • vāryaṁ: Worthy

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The invocation of Agni signifies the importance of fire in rituals, symbolizing purity, transformation, and energy.
    • The phrase "we invoke you who are dear" emphasizes the reverence and affection towards Agni, indicating that fire is not just a physical element but also a spiritual presence.
    • The mention of "guest" highlights the ancient Indian tradition of hospitality, where guests are treated with utmost respect and honor.
    • The line "may we attain the immortal" suggests a desire for spiritual enlightenment and connection with the divine through the medium of fire.
    • The repetition of "may he be invoked" reinforces the importance of calling upon the divine for blessings and guidance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, lighting a fire during ceremonies symbolizes the presence of Agni, representing purification and the offering of prayers to the divine.
    • In many cultures, the act of sharing a meal with guests around a fire signifies warmth, community, and the sacredness of hospitality.
    • The mantra can be applied in personal meditation practices, where individuals seek to connect with their inner fire, representing passion, creativity, and transformation.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of reverence for fire as a divine entity and its role in human life.
    • It teaches the significance of hospitality, the pursuit of spiritual growth, and the importance of invoking the divine in our daily lives.
    • In a broader context, it reminds us to honor the elements of nature and seek a deeper connection with the universe through rituals and mindfulness.
