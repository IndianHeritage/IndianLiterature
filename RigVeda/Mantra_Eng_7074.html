प्रति ते दस्यवे वृक राधो अदर्श्य अह्रयम् |द्यौर न प्रथिना शवः ||
prati te dasyave vṛka rādho adarśy ahrayam | dyaur na prathinā śavaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "prati te dasyave vṛka rādho adarśy ahrayam | dyaur na prathinā śavaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prati: towards, in relation to
    • te: your
    • dasyave: to the one who is powerful or strong
    • vṛka: wolf
    • rādho: to be praised or honored
    • adarśy: unseen, invisible
    • ahrayam: to be brought forth, to be manifested
    • dyaur: sky, heaven
    • na: not
    • prathinā: by the one who is known, recognized
    • śavaḥ: dead, lifeless
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the relationship between the powerful and the unseen forces in the universe.
    • The "wolf" can symbolize strength and cunning, representing challenges or adversities that one may face.
    • The phrase "adarśy ahrayam" suggests that while these challenges may be invisible or not immediately apparent, they can still be brought to light or understood.
    • The reference to "dyaur" indicates a connection to the heavens or higher powers, suggesting that divine assistance is available.
    • The concluding part, "na prathinā śavaḥ," implies that one should not be lifeless or passive in the face of challenges; rather, one should actively seek to engage with and overcome them.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an employee may face unseen challenges such as office politics or market competition. Recognizing these challenges and actively addressing them can lead to success.
    • In personal life, individuals may encounter emotional struggles that are not visible to others. Acknowledging these feelings and seeking help can lead to healing and growth.
    • In environmental issues, the "invisible" effects of pollution can be addressed through awareness and action, leading to a healthier planet.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and confronting challenges, even when they are not immediately visible.
    • It encourages individuals to seek strength and support from higher powers or the community to overcome adversities.
    • In essence, it serves as a reminder that one should not remain passive in the face of difficulties but should strive to bring forth understanding and action.
