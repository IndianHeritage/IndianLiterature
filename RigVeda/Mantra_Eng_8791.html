यत्र राजा वैवस्वतः यत्रावरोधनं दिवः |यत्रामूर यहवतीर आपस तत्र मां अमृतं कृधीर्येन्दो परि श्रव ||
yatra rājā vaivasvato yatrāvarodhanaṁ divaḥ | yatrāmūr yahvatīr āpas tatra mām amṛtaṁ kṛdhīndrāyendo pari srava ||
  • yatra rājā vaivasvato: This phrase translates to "where the king is Vaivasvata." In Hindu mythology, Vaivasvata is often associated with Yama, the god of death and the ruler of the afterlife. This signifies a realm where justice and order prevail, governed by a righteous king.
  • yatrāvarodhanaṁ divaḥ: This means "where the heavens are opened." It suggests a place of enlightenment and divine presence, where the barriers between the earthly and the celestial are removed, allowing for a connection with the divine.
  • yatrāmūr yahvatīr āpas: This translates to "where the waters flow." Water is often a symbol of life, purity, and sustenance. In this context, it represents the flow of knowledge, wisdom, and spiritual nourishment that sustains life.
  • tatra mām amṛtaṁ kṛdhīndrāyendo pari srava: This phrase means "there, let me attain immortality, flowing like the nectar." Here, "amṛta" refers to immortality or eternal bliss, and "nectar" symbolizes the ultimate truth or divine knowledge that leads to liberation.

Conclusion:

  • The mantra emphasizes the importance of a just and righteous leadership (Vaivasvata) in a realm where divine wisdom and knowledge flow freely.
  • It highlights the significance of seeking enlightenment and spiritual nourishment, akin to how water sustains life.
  • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to leadership roles in society, where ethical governance leads to the well-being of the community.
  • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom, fostering a connection with the divine, which can lead to personal growth and a sense of purpose.
