यो वाम उर्व्यचस्तमं चिकेटति नृपाय्यम् |वर्तिर अश्विनाऽ परि यातम अस्मयु ||
yo vām uruvyacastamaṁ ciketati nṛpāyyam | vartir aśvinā pari yātam asmayū ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • vām: you
    • uruvyacastamaṁ: the one who is vast and powerful
    • ciketati: thinks or contemplates
    • nṛpāyyam: for the benefit of the people or the ruler
    • vartir: existence or being
    • aśvinā: by the Ashvins (the twin gods of medicine and health)
    • pari: all around or everywhere
    • yātam: may it go or may it travel
    • asmayū: with strength or vigor
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine qualities of contemplation and strength.
    • It emphasizes the importance of thinking deeply for the benefit of society.
    • The mention of the Ashvins suggests a connection to health, healing, and well-being.
    • It encourages a holistic approach to existence, where one's actions are beneficial to all.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A leader who contemplates the needs of their community before making decisions embodies the essence of this mantra.
    • Healthcare professionals, like doctors, who work tirelessly to ensure the well-being of their patients reflect the qualities of the Ashvins.
    • In personal life, individuals who strive to maintain a balance between their own needs and the needs of others demonstrate the principle of collective well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of thoughtful leadership and the importance of health and well-being in society.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from governance to personal relationships, where the focus is on collective benefit.
    • By embodying the principles of contemplation and strength, individuals can contribute positively to their communities and promote a harmonious existence.
