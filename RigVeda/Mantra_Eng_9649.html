त āयजन्त द्रविणं सम अस्मा ऋषयः पूर्वे जरतारो न भूना |असूरते सूरते रजसि निषत्ते ये भूतानि समकृत्वन्न इमानी ||
ta āyajanta draviṇaṁ sam asmā ṛṣayaḥ pūrve jaritāro na bhūnā | asūrte sūrte rajasi niṣatte ye bhūtāni samakṛṇvann imāni ||
  • ta āyajanta: This phrase can be translated as "they who have invoked" or "they who have worshipped." It signifies the act of seeking divine blessings or guidance through rituals and prayers.
  • draviṇaṁ: This word means "wealth" or "resources." In a broader sense, it refers to the abundance and prosperity that one seeks in life.
  • sam asmā: This translates to "together with us." It emphasizes the collective aspect of worship and the importance of community in spiritual practices.
  • ṛṣayaḥ pūrve: "The sages of old" or "the ancient seers." This refers to the wise individuals who have come before us, whose knowledge and insights guide our spiritual journey.
  • jaritāro na bhūnā: This can be interpreted as "those who have awakened" or "those who have realized." It speaks to the idea of enlightenment and the pursuit of higher understanding.
  • asūrte sūrte: "In the non-divine and divine." This contrasts the material and spiritual realms, suggesting that both aspects are essential in life.
  • rajasi niṣatte: "In the realm of action." This phrase highlights the importance of action and effort in achieving one's goals and aspirations.
  • ye bhūtāni: "Those beings" or "those entities." This refers to all living beings and the interconnectedness of life.
  • samakṛṇvann imāni: "They who have created these." This emphasizes the role of individuals in shaping their reality and the world around them through their actions and intentions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and the importance of community, wisdom, and action in achieving prosperity and enlightenment. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Engaging in community service or collective worship to foster a sense of belonging and shared purpose.
  • Seeking knowledge from elders or mentors who have experienced life and can provide valuable insights.
  • Balancing material pursuits with spiritual growth, recognizing that both are essential for a fulfilling life.
  • Taking proactive steps in one's career or personal life, understanding that our actions shape our future.

This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of striving for both material and spiritual wealth in our lives.
