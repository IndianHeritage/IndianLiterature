यद वः श्रान्ताय सुन्वते वरूथम अस्ति यच छर्दिः |तेन नो अधि वोकत ||
yad vaḥ śrāntāya sunvate varūtham asti yac chardiḥ | tenā no adhi vocata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • vaḥ: for you
    • śrāntāya: for the weary
    • sunvate: who are listening
    • varūtham: the protection or shelter
    • asti: exists
    • yac: whatever
    • chardiḥ: is the cause of distress or discomfort
    • tena: by that
    • no: us
    • adhi: upon
    • vocata: speak or address
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses the concept of seeking protection and relief from discomfort for those who are weary and listening.
    • It emphasizes the importance of communication and guidance in overcoming challenges.
    • The phrase "whatever exists" suggests that there are various forms of support and protection available to those in need.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, employees who feel overwhelmed may seek guidance from their managers or mentors. This mantra can be seen as a reminder for leaders to provide support and address the concerns of their team members.
    • In a community, individuals facing hardships may turn to local organizations for assistance. The mantra highlights the importance of these organizations in providing shelter and resources to those in distress.
    • In personal relationships, friends and family members often listen and provide comfort to those who are feeling weary. This mantra encourages open communication and support among loved ones.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for compassion and support for those who are struggling.
    • It serves as a reminder that we should be attentive to the needs of others and offer our assistance when they are in distress.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as workplaces, communities, and personal relationships, promoting a culture of empathy and understanding.
