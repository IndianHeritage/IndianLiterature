स घेद उतासी वृत्रहन् समाना इन्द्र गोपतिः |यस् ता विश्वानि चिच्युषे ||
sa ghed utāsi vṛtrahan samāna indra gopatiḥ | yas tā viśvāni cicyuṣe ||
  • sa ghed utāsi vṛtrahan samāna indra gopatiḥ
    • sa ghed: This phrase can be interpreted as "he who has risen" or "he who has awakened." It signifies the emergence of a powerful force or entity.
    • utāsi: This word means "to rise" or "to ascend." It indicates a movement towards a higher state or consciousness.
    • vṛtrahan: This term refers to "the slayer of Vritra," where Vritra symbolizes obstacles or challenges. In a broader sense, it represents overcoming difficulties.
    • samāna: This means "equal" or "similar." It suggests a sense of unity or commonality among beings.
    • indra gopatiḥ: Indra is the king of gods in Vedic literature, and "gopatiḥ" means "lord of the cows" or "protector." This phrase emphasizes the protective and nurturing aspect of divinity.
  • yas tā viśvāni cicyuṣe
    • yas: This word translates to "who" or "that," referring to the divine or powerful entity mentioned earlier.
    • tā: This means "those" or "that," indicating a connection to the previous subject.
    • viśvāni: This term means "all" or "universal," suggesting inclusivity and the encompassing nature of the divine.
    • cicyuṣe: This word can be interpreted as "to protect" or "to preserve." It highlights the role of the divine in safeguarding all beings.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine protection and the overcoming of obstacles. It signifies the awakening of a powerful force that rises to protect and nurture all beings. In real life, this can be seen in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal challenges, individuals often find strength and support from their community or spiritual beliefs, akin to the protective nature of Indra.
  • In leadership, a good leader embodies the qualities of Indra, rising to face challenges and ensuring the welfare of their team or community.
  • In environmental conservation, the idea of protecting all beings resonates with the mantra's call for universal safeguarding.

This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of unity, protection, and the strength to overcome challenges, applicable in both personal and collective contexts. It encourages individuals to rise above difficulties and to act as protectors in their own spheres of influence.
