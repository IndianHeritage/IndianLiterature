भूरि नाम वन्दमानो दधाति पिता वसो यदि तज जोषयासे |कुविद देवस्य सहसा चकानः सुम्नम अग्निर वनते वावृद्धानः ||
bhūri nāma vandamāno dadhāti pitā vaso yadi taj joṣayāse | kuvid devasya sahasā cakānaḥ sumnam agnir vanate vāvṛdhānaḥ ||
  • bhūri nāma: This phrase translates to "with great names" or "many names." It signifies the vastness and diversity of existence, suggesting that everything in the universe has multiple aspects and identities.
  • vandamāno: This means "worshipping" or "paying homage." It emphasizes the importance of reverence and respect towards the divine and the forces of nature.
  • dadhāti: This translates to "provides" or "gives." It indicates the nurturing aspect of the divine, suggesting that the universe is generous and supportive.
  • pitā: Meaning "father," this term symbolizes the protective and guiding nature of a paternal figure, representing authority and care.
  • vaso: This word means "of the dwelling" or "of the home." It signifies stability and comfort, highlighting the importance of a safe environment.
  • yadi taj joṣayāse: This phrase can be interpreted as "if you are pleased with that." It suggests that the divine responds to our actions and intentions, reinforcing the idea of reciprocity in our relationship with the universe.
  • kuvid devasya: This translates to "the wise of the gods." It emphasizes the intelligence and wisdom inherent in divine forces, suggesting that they guide us towards enlightenment.
  • sahasā cakānaḥ: This means "suddenly manifesting." It indicates the unpredictable nature of divine intervention and the sudden changes that can occur in life.
  • sumnam agnir: This translates to "the auspicious fire." Fire is often seen as a purifying and transformative force, symbolizing energy and vitality.
  • vanate vāvṛdhānaḥ: This means "growing and flourishing." It signifies the potential for growth and prosperity when one aligns with the divine forces.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of reverence towards the divine and the interconnectedness of all beings. It highlights the importance of acknowledging the many aspects of existence and the nurturing role of the universe. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Practicing gratitude towards nature and the universe for the resources and support it provides.
  • Creating a harmonious home environment that fosters growth and well-being.
  • Recognizing the wisdom in challenges and changes, understanding that they can lead to personal development.
  • Engaging in rituals or practices that honor the divine, promoting a sense of community and connection.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the reciprocal relationship we share with the universe, encouraging us to live with awareness, respect, and gratitude.
