जेत नृभिर इन्द्रः पृत्सु शूरः श्रोत हवं नाधमानस्य कारोः | प्रभर्ता रथम दाशुष उपाक उद्यन्ता गिरो यदि च तमनā भूत्व् ||
jetā nṛbhir indraḥ pṛtsu śūraḥ śrotā havaṁ nādhamānasya kāroḥ | prabhartā rathaṁ dāśuṣa upāka udyantā giro yadi ca tmanā bhūt ||
  • jetā: This word translates to "the victor" or "the one who conquers." It signifies strength and success in overcoming challenges.
  • nṛbhir: Meaning "by men" or "among men," this term emphasizes the collective effort and unity of people in achieving goals.
  • indraḥ: Referring to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power, authority, and leadership.
  • pṛtsu: This means "in battles" or "in conflicts," indicating the context of struggle and competition.
  • śūraḥ: Translates to "the brave" or "the valiant," highlighting the importance of courage in facing adversities.
  • śrotā: Meaning "the hearer" or "the one who listens," it suggests the value of wisdom and understanding in decision-making.
  • havaṁ: This word can mean "offering" or "sacrifice," indicating the need for dedication and commitment to a cause.
  • nādhamānasya: Translates to "of the one who is not lowly" or "of the one who does not fall," emphasizing resilience and strength of character.
  • kāroḥ: Meaning "of the doer," it signifies action and the importance of taking initiative.
  • prabhartā: This means "the initiator" or "the one who leads," highlighting the role of leadership in guiding others.
  • rathaṁ: Referring to "the chariot," it symbolizes the journey of life and the means to achieve one's goals.
  • dāśuṣa: Meaning "of the ten," it can refer to the ten directions or aspects of life, indicating a holistic approach.
  • upāka: This translates to "the one who is prepared" or "the one who is ready," emphasizing the importance of readiness in facing challenges.
  • udyantā: Meaning "rising" or "emerging," it signifies growth and progress.
  • giro: This word means "of the words" or "of the speech," indicating the power of communication and expression.
  • yadi: Translates to "if," suggesting conditionality and the importance of choices.
  • ca: Meaning "and," it connects ideas and emphasizes unity.
  • tmanā: This means "with the self" or "by oneself," highlighting personal responsibility and self-awareness.
  • bhūt: Referring to "becoming" or "being," it signifies the process of transformation and existence.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of courage, unity, leadership, and personal responsibility in overcoming challenges and achieving success. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork in a corporate environment, where collective effort and strong leadership are essential for success. It also resonates in personal development, where individuals must be brave and proactive in their pursuits. The mantra serves as a reminder that through dedication, resilience, and effective communication, one can navigate the complexities of life and emerge victorious.
