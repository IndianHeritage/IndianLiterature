यद आयुक्ता अरुषा रोहितā रथे वातजूतā वृषभस्येव ते रवः |आद इन्बसि वनिनो धूमकेतुनाग्ने सख्ये मा ऋषामā वयं तव ||
yad ayukthā aruṣā rohitā rathe vātajūtā vṛṣabhasyeva te ravaḥ | ād invasi vanino dhūmaketunāgne sakhye mā riṣāmā vayaṁ tava ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • ayukthā: is not bound or restrained
    • aruṣā: red or fiery
    • rohitā: the one that is red, often referring to the sun or fire
    • rathe: in the chariot
    • vātajūtā: driven by the wind
    • vṛṣabhasyeva: like the bull
    • te: your
    • ravaḥ: roar or sound
    • ād: may
    • invasi: you may invoke
    • vanino: of the forest or wilderness
    • dhūmaketunāgne: O Agni, the smoke-bannered one
    • sakhye: in friendship
    • mā: do not
    • riṣāmā: be angry
    • vayaṁ: we
    • tava: your
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • The imagery of the "red chariot driven by the wind" suggests the dynamic and powerful nature of life and the universe.
    • The phrase "like the bull" indicates strength and stability, emphasizing the importance of being grounded amidst chaos.
    • The plea to Agni not to be angry reflects a desire for harmony and understanding in relationships, both with the divine and with others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we often face challenges that require us to harness our inner strength and energy, much like the fire that Agni represents.
    • When dealing with conflicts, the mantra serves as a reminder to approach situations with calmness and to seek resolution without anger.
    • The concept of invoking Agni can be likened to seeking inspiration or motivation in our endeavors, whether in personal goals or professional projects.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of balance, strength, and the importance of maintaining harmonious relationships.
    • It teaches us to embrace the transformative power of energy while being mindful of our interactions with others.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a deeper understanding of the interconnectedness of all beings.
