साद्युम्नैर्युम्निनी बृहदुपोपश्रवसिश्रवः |दधीत वृततूर्ये ||
sā dyumnair dyumninī bṛhad upopa śravasi śravaḥ | dadhīta vṛtratūrye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sā: she, that
    • dyumnair: by the glories, by the fame
    • dyumninī: the one who is glorious, the one who possesses fame
    • bṛhad: great, vast
    • upopa: may she come near, may she approach
    • śravasi: in fame, in glory
    • śravaḥ: of renown, of reputation
    • dadhīta: may she bestow, may she grant
    • vṛtratūrye: in the battle against Vritra, in the fight against obstacles
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes a divine feminine energy that embodies greatness and glory.
    • It seeks the approach of this energy to bestow fame and success, particularly in overcoming challenges and obstacles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, individuals often seek recognition and success in their careers. This mantra can be seen as a call for the support of a higher power to achieve their goals.
    • In sports, athletes may invoke such energies to gain strength and overcome their competitors, much like how the mantra speaks of overcoming Vritra, a metaphor for challenges.
    • In personal development, individuals may use this mantra as a reminder to strive for greatness and to seek the support of their community or mentors in their journey.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in achieving greatness and overcoming obstacles.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as career advancement, personal growth, and competitive endeavors.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset of striving for excellence and seeking support in their pursuits.
