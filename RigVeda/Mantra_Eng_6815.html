नहि वां वव्रयामहे 'थेंद्रं इद यजामहे शविष्टं नृणां नरम् |स नः कदाचिद अर्वतागमद आ वाजसातये गमद आ मेधसातये नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
nahi vāṁ vavrayāmahe 'thendram id yajāmahe śaviṣṭhaṁ nṛṇāṁ naram | sa naḥ kadā cid arvatā gamad ā vājasātaye gamad ā medhasātaye nabhantām anyake same ||
  • Translation: "We do not worship the one who is not a man; we worship the one who is the best among men. May he, who is powerful, come to us; may he come to us for the sake of strength and intelligence, and may he not be like others."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • nahi: not
    • vāṁ: you (plural)
    • vavrayāmahe: we worship
    • thendram: the one who is powerful
    • id: this
    • yajāmahe: we invoke
    • śaviṣṭhaṁ: the best
    • nṛṇāṁ: among men
    • naram: man
    • sa: he
    • naḥ: to us
    • kadā cid: at any time
    • arvatā: powerful
    • gamad: may come
    • ā: for
    • vājasātaye: strength
    • medhasātaye: intelligence
    • nabhantām: not like others
    • anyake: others
    • same: equal
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and worshipping true strength and intelligence in individuals.
    • It suggests that one should not follow or worship those who do not embody these qualities, but rather seek out and honor those who do.
    • In a real-life scenario, this can be applied to leadership; we should support leaders who demonstrate integrity, wisdom, and strength rather than those who may be popular but lack these essential qualities.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder to seek out and value true qualities of strength and intelligence in ourselves and others.
    • It encourages individuals to strive for personal growth and to surround themselves with those who inspire and uplift them.
    • In a broader context, it can be applied to societal values, urging communities to recognize and support individuals who contribute positively to society.
