अग्निमुक्तैः ऋषयो वि हवयन्ते 'ग्निं नरो यामनि बाधितासः | अग्निं वयो अन्तरिक्षे पतन्तो 'ग्निः सहस्राः परि यान्ति गोनाम ||
agnim ukthair ṛṣayo vi hvayante 'gniṁ naro yāmani bādhitāsaḥ | agniṁ vayo antarikṣe patanto 'gniḥ sahasrā pari yāti gonām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • agnim: Fire
    • ukthair: With words
    • ṛṣayo: The sages or seers
    • vi: Indeed
    • hvayante: Invoke or call upon
    • naro: Men or humans
    • yāmani: Who are devoted or dedicated
    • bādhitāsaḥ: Those who are restrained or controlled
    • vayo: Our (in the context of the divine)
    • antarikṣe: In the atmosphere or space
    • patanto: Falling or descending
    • sahasrā: Thousands
    • pari: All around
    • yāti: Go or move
    • gonām: Of the cattle or beings
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the invocation of Agni, the fire god, by the sages and humans alike.
    • It emphasizes the importance of fire in rituals and daily life, symbolizing purity, transformation, and energy.
    • The phrase "with words" indicates the power of speech and intention in invoking divine forces.
    • The mention of "those who are restrained" suggests that discipline and control are essential in spiritual practices.
    • The imagery of fire descending from the atmosphere signifies the connection between the divine and earthly realms.
    • The reference to "thousands" indicates the abundance and omnipresence of Agni in various forms.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is used in rituals to symbolize purification and transformation, such as in the Hindu practice of Homa or Yajna.
    • In modern life, the concept of invoking energy can be seen in practices like meditation and yoga, where individuals focus their intentions to bring about change.
    • The idea of discipline and control can be related to personal development, where individuals strive to control their thoughts and actions to achieve their goals.
    • Fire is also a metaphor for knowledge and enlightenment, as seen in the phrase "the light of knowledge," which is prevalent in various philosophical teachings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of Agni as a divine force that connects the physical and spiritual worlds.
    • It teaches us the importance of intention, discipline, and the power of words in our spiritual practices.
    • In real life, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide individuals in their personal and spiritual journeys, emphasizing the need for respect towards nature and the divine.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the transformative power of fire, both literally and metaphorically, in our lives.
