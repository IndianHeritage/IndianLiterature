कः स्विद वृक्षो निष्ठितो मध्य अर्णसो यं तौग्र्यो नाधितः पर्यषस्वजत् |पर्णा मृगस्य पतरोर इवारभ उद अश्विनाः ऊहथुः श्रोमताया कम ||
kaḥ svid vṛkṣo niṣṭhito madhye arṇaso yaṁ taugryo nādhitaḥ paryaṣasvajat | parṇā mṛgasya pataror ivārabha ud aśvinā ūhathuḥ śromatāya kam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kaḥ: Who
    • svid: Where
    • vṛkṣo: Tree
    • niṣṭhito: Established
    • madhye: In the middle
    • arṇaso: Of the ocean
    • yaṁ: Which
    • taugryo: The fierce one
    • nādhitaḥ: Not studied
    • paryaṣasvajat: Has been surrounded
    • parṇā: Leaves
    • mṛgasya: Of the deer
    • pataror: Of the tree
    • ivārabha: Like a beginning
    • ud: Up
    • aśvinā: By the Ashvins (divine twin horsemen)
    • ūhathuḥ: They have thought
    • śromatāya: With strength
    • kam: Who
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a question about the identity of a tree that stands firm in the middle of the ocean.
    • It suggests a connection between the tree and the fierce forces of nature, indicating that it has not been fully understood or studied.
    • The imagery of leaves of a deer and the divine Ashvins implies a relationship between nature and divine intervention.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Consider a person who stands firm in the face of adversity, much like a tree in a storm. This can be seen in individuals who advocate for environmental conservation despite opposition.
    • The concept of studying nature and understanding its complexities can be related to modern scientific research, where scientists explore ecosystems and their interdependencies.
    • The Ashvins symbolize healing and protection, akin to modern healthcare professionals who work tirelessly to safeguard public health.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of understanding the natural world and recognizing the strength found within it.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom, much like the pursuit of scientific inquiry.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to remain resilient and grounded, drawing strength from our surroundings and the support of others, much like the tree that stands tall amidst the ocean's waves.
