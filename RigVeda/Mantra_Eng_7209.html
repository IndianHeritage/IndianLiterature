शाश्वद् धि वः सुदानव आदित्याः ऊतिभिर वयम् |पुरा नूनं बुभुज्महे ||
śaśvad dhi vaḥ sudānava ādityā ūtibhir vayam | purā nūnam bubhujmahe ||
  • śaśvad dhi vaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "everlasting, indeed, for you." The term "śaśvad" signifies something that is eternal or perpetual, while "dhi" refers to understanding or wisdom. "Vaḥ" is a pronoun meaning "you" in plural, indicating a collective or community.
  • sudānava: This word translates to "good nourishment" or "well-fed." It implies abundance and prosperity, suggesting that the subject is blessed with resources and sustenance.
  • ādityā: This term refers to the "sons of Aditi," which in Vedic literature often symbolizes the celestial bodies, particularly the sun. It represents light, knowledge, and the divine forces that govern the universe.
  • ūtibhir vayam: This phrase means "we are with the blessings." "Ūtibhih" refers to blessings or boons, while "vayam" means "we." This indicates a collective acknowledgment of the divine support and abundance received.
  • purā nūnam bubhujmahe: This translates to "indeed, we have enjoyed in the past." "Purā" means "in the past," "nūnam" emphasizes certainty, and "bubhujmahe" means "we have enjoyed" or "we have partaken." This reflects on the past experiences of abundance and joy.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of eternal wisdom, abundance, and divine blessings. It serves as a reminder of the importance of recognizing and appreciating the resources and support we have received in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as:


  • Gratitude Practices: Individuals can use this mantra as a daily affirmation to cultivate gratitude for the abundance in their lives, whether it be in terms of relationships, health, or material wealth.
  • Community Gatherings: In communal settings, this mantra can be recited to foster a sense of unity and shared prosperity, reminding everyone of the collective blessings they enjoy.
  • Mindfulness and Reflection: During moments of reflection, this mantra can encourage individuals to look back on their past experiences and recognize the growth and nourishment they have received, promoting a positive outlook on life.

Overall, this mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing the eternal nature of wisdom and the blessings that come from the divine, encouraging individuals and communities to live in gratitude and abundance.
