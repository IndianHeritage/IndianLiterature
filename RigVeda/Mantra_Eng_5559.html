यā आपो दिव्या उता वā श्रवन्ति खानित्रिमā उता वā यāḥ स्वयञ्जāḥ |समुद्रार्थā यāḥ शुचयः पावकास तā आपो देवीर इह मām अवन्तु ||
yā āpo divyā uta vā sravanti khanitrimā uta vā yāḥ svayaṁjāḥ | samudrārthā yāḥ śucayaḥ pāvakās tā āpo devīr iha mām avantu ||
  • Mantra: "yā āpo divyā uta vā sravanti khanitrimā uta vā yāḥ svayaṁjāḥ | samudrārthā yāḥ śucayaḥ pāvakās tā āpo devīr iha mām avantu ||"
  • Translation: "O divine waters, whether you flow from the heavens or arise from the earth, whether you are born from the ocean or are pure and sacred, may you, O divine waters, protect and nourish me."

  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yā: those who
    • āpo: waters
    • divyā: divine
    • uta: or
    • vā: also
    • sravanti: flow
    • khanitrimā: from the earth
    • svayaṁjāḥ: self-born
    • samudrārthā: from the ocean
    • śucayaḥ: pure
    • pāvakās: sacred
    • tā: those
    • devīr: divine
    • iha: here
    • mām: me
    • avantu: may protect

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine qualities of water, recognizing its various forms and sources.
    • It acknowledges the importance of water in sustaining life, both in its celestial (rain) and terrestrial (rivers, lakes) forms.
    • By calling upon the waters to protect and nourish, it emphasizes the need for purity and sanctity in our relationship with nature.
    • In real life, this can be seen in practices such as rituals for rain, conservation of water bodies, and the reverence for rivers in many cultures.

  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the essential role of water in our lives, symbolizing purity, nourishment, and protection.
    • It serves as a reminder of our responsibility to respect and preserve water resources, which are vital for survival.
    • In contemporary society, this mantra can inspire environmental movements focused on clean water access, pollution reduction, and sustainable practices.
    • By invoking the divine aspect of water, it encourages a spiritual connection to nature, fostering a sense of gratitude and stewardship.
