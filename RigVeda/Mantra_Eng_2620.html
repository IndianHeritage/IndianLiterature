अग्नि भूरिणि तव जातवेदो देव स्वधावो 'मृतस्य नाम |याश्च मायामायिनां विश्वमिन्व तवे पूर्वीः संदधुः पृष्ठबन्धो ||
agne bhūrīṇi tava jātavedo deva svadhāvo 'mṛtasya nāma | yāś ca māyā māyināṁ viśvaminva tve pūrvīḥ saṁdadhuḥ pṛṣṭabandho ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god, who is a central figure in Vedic rituals and symbolizes purity, energy, and transformation.
  • Bhūrīṇi: This term means "abundant" or "plentiful," indicating the vastness and richness of Agni's blessings.
  • Tava: This means "your," signifying a personal connection to Agni.
  • Jātavedo: This term translates to "the one who knows all births," referring to Agni's role as a witness to all actions and events in the universe.
  • Deva: This means "god," emphasizing the divine nature of Agni.
  • Svadhāvo: This word refers to the essence or nature of Agni, highlighting its intrinsic qualities.
  • 'Amṛtasya: This translates to "of the immortal," suggesting that Agni is a source of eternal life and sustenance.
  • Nāma: This means "name," indicating the importance of invoking Agni's name in rituals.
  • Yāś: This means "those who," referring to the beings or entities that are connected to Agni.
  • Māyā: This term means "illusion" or "magic," suggesting the transformative power of Agni.
  • Māyināṁ: This refers to "of the magicians," indicating the creative and transformative aspects of Agni.
  • Viśvamin: This means "in all," suggesting Agni's omnipresence and universal significance.
  • Tve: This means "to you," indicating a direct appeal to Agni.
  • Pūrvīḥ: This translates to "the ancients," referring to the wisdom and practices of those who came before.
  • Saṁdadhuḥ: This means "they have united," indicating a coming together of energies or forces.
  • Pṛṣṭabandho: This term refers to "the one who is bound by the back," suggesting a connection to the past and the continuity of tradition.

Conclusion: The mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, as a source of abundance, knowledge, and transformation. It emphasizes the importance of Agni in rituals and the connection between the divine and the mortal. In real life, this mantra can be applied to seek guidance and blessings in times of change or transformation, such as during significant life events like marriage, starting a new job, or moving to a new place. By invoking Agni, individuals can align themselves with the energies of creation and renewal, fostering a sense of connection to the past while embracing the future.
