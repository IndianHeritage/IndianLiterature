यत्रा समुद्रः स्कभितो व्य aunad अपां नपात सविता तस्य वेद |अतो भूः अता उत्थितं रजो 'तो dyावापृथिवी अप्रत्थेताम् ||
yatrā samudraḥ skabhito vy aunad apāṁ napāt savitā tasya veda | ato bhūr ata ā utthitaṁ rajo 'to dyāvāpṛthivī aprathetām ||
  • yatrā samudraḥ skabhito: This phrase can be translated as "where the ocean is spread out." The ocean often symbolizes vastness and depth, representing the infinite possibilities and the unknown in life.
  • vy aunad apāṁ napāt: This translates to "where the waters are born." It signifies the source of life and nourishment, akin to how rivers and streams provide sustenance to the land and its inhabitants.
  • savitā tasya veda: "Savitā" refers to the Sun, the giver of life and energy. "Tasya veda" means "knows this." This suggests that the Sun is aware of the interconnectedness of all life forms and the importance of water in sustaining them.
  • ato bhūr ata ā utthitaṁ rajo: This can be interpreted as "from this, the earth and the atmosphere arise." It emphasizes the relationship between the elements and how they contribute to the creation of life on Earth.
  • 'to dyāvāpṛthivī aprathetām: This means "may the sky and the earth not be separated." It highlights the unity of the cosmos, suggesting that all elements of nature are interconnected and should coexist harmoniously.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature and the importance of water and sunlight in sustaining life. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to environmental conservation efforts, reminding us of the need to protect our natural resources. The mantra serves as a reminder that the elements of nature—water, earth, and sky—are not separate but are part of a larger, interconnected system. By recognizing this unity, we can foster a greater respect for the environment and work towards sustainable living practices that honor the delicate balance of our ecosystem.
