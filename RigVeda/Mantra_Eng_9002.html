इह श्रुत इन्द्रो अस्मे अद्य स्तवे वज्री ऋचिषमः |मित्रो न यो जनेष्वा यशश चक्रे असाम्याः ||
iha śruta indro asme adya stave vajry ṛcīṣamaḥ | mitro na yo janeṣv ā yaśaś cakre asāmy ā ||
  • iha - Here, in this world.
  • śruta - Heard or understood.
  • indro - Indra, the king of gods, symbolizing strength and power.
  • asme - To us, or for us.
  • adya - Today, in the present moment.
  • stave - In praise or worship.
  • vajry - Thunderbolt, representing strength and invincibility.
  • ṛcīṣamaḥ - We offer our hymns or praises.
  • mitro - Friend, indicating companionship and support.
  • na - Not.
  • yo - Who.
  • janeṣv - Among people or in society.
  • ā - To, or towards.
  • yaśaś - Fame or glory.
  • cakre - Created or established.
  • asāmy - Without disparity or inequality.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for strength and support from Indra, emphasizing the importance of unity and friendship among people. It highlights the need for collective strength and the pursuit of glory in a harmonious manner.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Teamwork: Just as the mantra calls for collective strength, in a workplace or sports team, collaboration and mutual support lead to success.
  • Community Building: The emphasis on friendship and unity can inspire community initiatives where individuals come together for a common cause, fostering a sense of belonging.
  • Personal Growth: The call for strength can motivate individuals to seek support from friends and mentors in their personal and professional journeys.

Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of seeking strength and support from divine forces while fostering unity and friendship among people. It serves as a reminder that collective efforts and harmonious relationships are essential for achieving glory and success in various aspects of life.
