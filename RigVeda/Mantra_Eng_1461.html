शुम्भन्तो जेन्यं यथा वाजेषु विप्र वाजिनम् |अत्यं इव शवसे सातये धनā विश्वा धनानि सातये ||
śumbhanto jenyaṁ yathā vājeṣu vipra vājinam | atyam iva śavase sātaye dhanā viśvā dhanāni sātaye ||
  • śumbhanto: This term can be interpreted as "those who are prosperous" or "those who are endowed with wealth." It signifies individuals or entities that are successful and abundant.
  • jenyaṁ: This word means "that which is born" or "that which is produced." It refers to the outcomes or results of one's actions or efforts.
  • yathā: This translates to "as" or "like," indicating a comparison or a manner of doing something.
  • vājeṣu: This term relates to "in the context of strength" or "in the context of power." It often refers to the might or capability of individuals or forces.
  • vipra: This word means "wise" or "learned," often referring to a person who possesses knowledge and insight, particularly in spiritual or philosophical matters.
  • vājinam: This translates to "the one who is strong" or "the powerful one," emphasizing the strength and capability of an individual.
  • atyam: This means "exceeding" or "beyond," suggesting something that surpasses normal limits.
  • iva: This word means "like" or "as if," used to draw a comparison.
  • śavase: This term refers to "in the context of life" or "in the context of vitality," indicating the essence of life or existence.
  • sātaye: This means "for the purpose of" or "for the attainment of," indicating intention or goal.
  • dhanā: This word means "wealth" or "prosperity," referring to material and spiritual abundance.
  • viśvā: This translates to "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity or comprehensiveness.
  • dhanāni: This is the plural form of wealth, referring to various forms of prosperity or resources.

The mantra essentially speaks about the relationship between prosperity, strength, wisdom, and the attainment of wealth. It emphasizes that those who are wise and strong can achieve great wealth and success, much like how a powerful force can yield fruitful results.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In business, individuals who are knowledgeable and strong in their strategies often achieve greater success and financial stability.
  • In personal development, wisdom and strength of character can lead to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
  • In community building, wise leaders who empower others can create a thriving environment for all.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that prosperity is not merely about material wealth but also about the wisdom and strength that one cultivates. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and develop their capabilities, as these qualities are essential for achieving true abundance in life. By embodying these principles, one can navigate challenges effectively and create a life filled with prosperity and fulfillment.
