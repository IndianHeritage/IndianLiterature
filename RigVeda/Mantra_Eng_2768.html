इन्द्रः पूर्वभिद् आतिरद् दासम् अर्कैर विदद्वसुर दयमानो वि शत्रून् |ब्रह्मजूतस् तन्‍वां वावृद्धानो भूридात्र आपृणद् rodasī उभे ||
indraḥ pūrbhid ātirad dāsam arkair vidadvasur dayamāno vi śatrūn | brahmajūtas tanvā vāvṛdhāno bhūridātra āpṛṇad rodasī ubhe ||
  • Indraḥ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Pūrbhid: Means "one who breaks through" or "one who overcomes," indicating the ability to conquer obstacles.
  • Ātirad: Translates to "having great strength" or "powerful," emphasizing Indra's might.
  • Dāsam: Refers to "the enemies" or "those who oppose," highlighting the adversaries faced.
  • Arkair: Means "with hymns" or "with praises," suggesting the importance of devotion and worship.
  • Vidadvasur: Translates to "he who bestows" or "he who gives," indicating generosity and benevolence.
  • Dayamāno: Means "being compassionate" or "showing kindness," reflecting the nurturing aspect of Indra.
  • Vi śatrūn: Refers to "against the enemies," emphasizing the protective role of Indra.
  • Brahmajūtas: Means "those who are endowed with Brahman," indicating a connection to the ultimate reality or consciousness.
  • Tanvā: Refers to "with the body" or "with form," suggesting the physical manifestation of divine energy.
  • Vāvṛdhāno: Translates to "one who increases" or "one who enhances," indicating growth and prosperity.
  • Bhūridātra: Means "the one who gives abundantly," reflecting the idea of generosity and abundance.
  • Āpṛṇad: Refers to "he who fills" or "he who provides," indicating the act of fulfilling needs.
  • Rodasī: Means "the two worlds" or "the heavens and the earth," symbolizing the connection between the spiritual and material realms.
  • Ubhe: Translates to "both," indicating the duality of existence and the balance between different aspects of life.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the qualities of strength, compassion, and abundance embodied by Indra. It highlights the importance of overcoming obstacles, showing kindness to others, and the interconnectedness of the spiritual and material worlds. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate leadership qualities, practice generosity, and strive for personal growth while maintaining a balance between their spiritual and worldly responsibilities. It serves as a reminder that true strength lies not only in power but also in compassion and the ability to uplift others.
