एव ते वयम् इन्द्र भूञ्जतीनां विद्यामा सुमतीनां नवानाम् |विद्यामा वस्तोर अवसाऽ गृणन्तो विश्वामित्रा उता त इन्द्र नूनम् ||
evā te vayam indra bhuñjatīnāṁ vidyāma sumatīnāṁ navānām | vidyāma vastor avasā gṛṇanto viśvāmitrā uta ta indra nūnam ||
  • evā te vayam indra bhuñjatīnāṁ vidyāma sumatīnāṁ navānām
    • evā te vayam: This phrase translates to "Thus, we are yours." It signifies a sense of belonging and devotion to a higher power, in this case, Indra, the king of gods in Vedic tradition.
    • indra: Refers to Indra, the deity associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. Indra is often seen as a protector and a provider of strength and prosperity.
    • bhuñjatīnāṁ: This word means "those who enjoy" or "those who partake." It implies a sense of abundance and the enjoyment of life's offerings.
    • vidyāma: Translates to "we know" or "we possess knowledge." This emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in life.
    • sumatīnāṁ: This means "of the wise" or "of those with good thoughts." It highlights the value of having a clear and positive mindset.
    • navānām: This translates to "of the new" or "of the fresh." It suggests the idea of renewal and the importance of embracing new ideas and perspectives.
  • vidyāma vastor avasā gṛṇanto viśvāmitrā uta ta indra nūnam
    • vidyāma: Again, this emphasizes knowledge and understanding.
    • vastor: This means "of the essence" or "of the substance." It refers to the fundamental nature of things.
    • avasā: This translates to "in the abode" or "in the dwelling." It suggests a sense of stability and grounding.
    • gṛṇanto: This means "singing" or "praising." It indicates the act of expressing gratitude and reverence.
    • viśvāmitrā: Refers to the sage Vishwamitra, known for his wisdom and spiritual prowess. This invokes the idea of seeking guidance from enlightened beings.
    • uta: This means "and" or "also," connecting the ideas presented.
    • ta indra nūnam: This translates to "O Indra, indeed." It is a direct address to Indra, affirming faith and trust in his power.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of knowledge, wisdom, and a positive mindset while invoking the blessings of Indra. It signifies a relationship of devotion and trust in a higher power, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and embrace new ideas. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In educational settings, where the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom is paramount.
  • In personal development, where maintaining a positive mindset and being open to new experiences can lead to growth.
  • In community gatherings, where expressing gratitude and reverence for the blessings received fosters a sense of unity and belonging.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, spirituality, and the pursuit of a fulfilling life.
