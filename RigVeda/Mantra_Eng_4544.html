एवा पाहि प्रत्नथा मन्दतु त्वा श्रुधि ब्रह्म वावृधस्वोता गीरभिः |आविः सूर्यं कृणुहि पीपिहीषो जहि शत्रूम् अभि गा इन्द्र तृन्धि ||
evā pāhi pratnathā mandatu tvā śrudhi brahma vāvṛdhasvota gīrbhiḥ | āviḥ sūryaṁ kṛṇuhi pīpihīṣo jahi śatrūm̐r abhi gā indra tṛndhi ||
  • evā pāhi pratnathā: This phrase can be translated as "Protect us, O Lord." It signifies a plea for divine protection and guidance. In real life, this can be seen in the way individuals seek support from higher powers during challenging times, such as praying for safety during a journey or for the well-being of loved ones.
  • mandatu tvā: This means "May you be pleased." It reflects the importance of pleasing the divine and maintaining a harmonious relationship with the spiritual realm. For example, people often perform rituals or acts of kindness to express gratitude and seek blessings, such as donating to charity or participating in community service.
  • śrudhi brahma: This translates to "Listen, O Supreme Being." It emphasizes the need for the divine to be attentive to our prayers and needs. In everyday life, this can be likened to moments when individuals seek guidance or wisdom, such as consulting a mentor or a spiritual leader during times of confusion.
  • vāvṛdhasvota gīrbhiḥ: This phrase means "May you grow strong through our hymns." It highlights the power of words and prayers in fostering strength and resilience. In practical terms, this can be seen in how motivational speeches or affirmations can inspire individuals to overcome obstacles and achieve their goals.
  • āviḥ sūryaṁ: This translates to "Come forth, O Sun." The Sun is often associated with energy, vitality, and enlightenment. In real life, this can symbolize the pursuit of knowledge and clarity, akin to how students seek education to illuminate their paths in life.
  • kṛṇuhi pīpihīṣo: This means "Fulfill our desires." It reflects the human longing for fulfillment and satisfaction in life. For instance, people often set goals and work diligently to achieve them, whether in their careers, relationships, or personal growth.
  • jahi śatrūm̐r: This translates to "Defeat our enemies." It signifies the struggle against obstacles and adversities. In a real-world context, this can be seen in how individuals face challenges, such as overcoming personal fears or societal issues, and striving for success despite opposition.
  • abhi gā indra tṛndhi: This means "O Indra, lead us." Indra is often regarded as a leader and protector. This can be related to how people look for leadership and guidance in various aspects of life, such as in organizations or communities, where effective leadership can inspire and mobilize others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for divine protection, guidance, and strength. It emphasizes the importance of a harmonious relationship with the divine, the power of words, and the pursuit of knowledge and fulfillment. In real life, these themes resonate with individuals as they navigate challenges, seek enlightenment, and strive for personal and communal growth. The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal to seek support and strength from a higher power, reminding us of the interconnectedness of our spiritual and everyday lives.
