आजुह्वान ईड्यो वन्द्यश्च याह्य अग्ने वसुभिः सजोषाः |त्वं देवानाम् असि यह्वा होताऽस एनान् यक्षीषितो यजीयान् ||
ājuhvāna īḍyo vandyaś cā yāhy agne vasubhiḥ sajoṣāḥ | tvaṁ devānām asi yahva hotā sa enān yakṣīṣito yajīyān ||
  • Mantra: "ājuhvāna īḍyo vandyaś cā yāhy agne vasubhiḥ sajoṣāḥ | tvaṁ devānām asi yahva hotā sa enān yakṣīṣito yajīyān ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ājuhvāna: Invoked or called upon
    • īḍyo: Worthy of praise or worship
    • vandyaś: One who is to be revered
    • cā: And
    • yāhy: Who is present or exists
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • vasubhiḥ: With the wealth or blessings
    • sajoṣāḥ: Together with joy or happiness
    • tvaṁ: You
    • devānām: Of the gods
    • asi: Are
    • yahva: O hotar (the priest or the one who invokes)
    • hotā: The one who offers
    • sa: He
    • enān: These
    • yakṣīṣito: Who are desired or invoked
    • yajīyān: Worthy of worship or sacrifice
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a hymn dedicated to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • It emphasizes the importance of invoking Agni, who is worthy of praise and reverence, and highlights the joy and blessings that come from his presence.
    • The mantra suggests that Agni is not just a deity but also a vital force that connects the worshippers with the divine realm.
    • In real life, this can be likened to the importance of rituals and prayers in various cultures, where fire often symbolizes purification and the divine presence.
    • For example, in Hindu weddings, the sacred fire (Agni) is invoked as a witness to the vows taken by the couple, symbolizing the divine blessing on their union.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine blessings and the recognition of Agni as a central figure in spiritual practices.
    • It teaches us the value of reverence and gratitude towards the forces that sustain our lives, encouraging us to seek harmony and joy in our actions.
    • In contemporary life, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate a sense of gratitude and connection with the world around them, recognizing the divine in everyday experiences.
    • By invoking the spirit of Agni, we can foster a deeper understanding of our place in the universe and the importance of community and shared joy.
