विषाह्य अग्नि गृणते मनिषां खं वेपसां तुविजात स्तवाना: |विश्वेभिर यद वावन: शुक्र देवैस तन् नो रास्व सुमहो भूरे मनमा ||
vi ṣāhy agne gṛṇate manīṣāṁ khaṁ vepasā tuvijāta stavānaḥ | viśvebhir yad vāvanaḥ śukra devais tan no rāsva sumaho bhūri manma ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viṣāhy: O Agni (the divine fire),
    • agne: O Agni,
    • gṛṇate: who is praised,
    • manīṣāṁ: with wisdom,
    • khaṁ: the sky,
    • vepasā: trembling,
    • tuvijāta: born of your own power,
    • stavānaḥ: worthy of praise.
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and the power of Agni in connecting the earthly realm with the heavens.
    • The trembling of the sky signifies the awe and reverence that the divine inspires in the natural world.
    • The phrase "tan no rāsva sumaho bhūri manma" can be interpreted as a request for blessings and abundance in our thoughts and actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we often seek guidance and wisdom in our decisions, much like how the mantra seeks the wisdom of Agni.
    • During rituals and ceremonies, fire is used as a symbol of purity and transformation, reflecting the role of Agni in spiritual practices.
    • In moments of uncertainty, invoking the qualities of Agni can inspire courage and clarity, helping individuals to navigate challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of Agni as a source of wisdom and a bridge between the material and spiritual worlds.
    • It teaches us to seek knowledge and clarity in our lives, reminding us of the importance of reverence for the divine.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to cultivate wisdom and strength in our actions, fostering a deeper connection with the universe.
