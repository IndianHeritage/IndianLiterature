त्वया यथा गृत्समदासो अग्ने गुहा वन्क्त उपराम् अभि ष्युः |सुवीरासो अभिमातिषाहः स्मत सूरिभ्यो गृणते तद वयो धाः ||
tvayā yathā gṛtsamadāso agne guhā vanvanta uparām̐ abhi ṣyuḥ | suvīrāso abhimātiṣāhaḥ smat sūribhyo gṛṇate tad vayo dhāḥ ||
  • tvayā - "by you" or "through you": This word signifies the importance of a higher power or divine entity, often referring to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature.
  • yathā - "as" or "in the way": This indicates a manner or method, suggesting that the following actions or qualities are to be understood in a specific context.
  • gṛtsamadāso - "those who are devoted to you": This refers to individuals who are dedicated and committed to the divine, emphasizing the importance of devotion in spiritual practice.
  • agne - "O Agni": A direct address to Agni, highlighting the reverence and respect for this deity.
  • guhā - "the hidden" or "the secret": This word suggests that there are deeper truths or mysteries that are not immediately visible or understood.
  • vanvanta - "those who dwell in the forest": This can symbolize individuals who live a life of simplicity and connection to nature, often seen as wise or enlightened.
  • uparām̐ - "having attained peace": This indicates a state of tranquility and calmness, which is often sought after in spiritual practices.
  • abhi - "towards" or "in the direction of": This word suggests movement or intention towards a goal or aspiration.
  • ṣyuḥ - "they who are strong": This refers to those who possess strength, both physically and spiritually.
  • suvīrāso - "those who are excellent warriors": This emphasizes the qualities of bravery and valor, suggesting that true strength is not just physical but also moral and ethical.
  • abhimātiṣāhaḥ - "those who have great understanding": This highlights the importance of wisdom and insight in one's actions and decisions.
  • smat - "from us": This indicates a sense of community or collective effort in the pursuit of spiritual goals.
  • sūribhyo - "to the wise ones": This refers to those who are knowledgeable and enlightened, suggesting that their guidance is valuable.
  • gṛṇate - "they sing" or "they praise": This indicates the act of worship or reverence, often expressed through hymns or chants.
  • tad - "that": This word points to something specific, often referring to the divine or the ultimate truth.
  • vayo - "our age" or "our time": This signifies the relevance of the teachings in the current context of life.
  • dhāḥ - "to hold" or "to maintain": This suggests the importance of sustaining the values and teachings that are being conveyed.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of devotion, wisdom, and strength in the pursuit of spiritual growth and understanding. It highlights the importance of community and the guidance of wise individuals in navigating life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects such as leadership, personal development, and the pursuit of knowledge. By embodying the qualities of devotion, strength, and wisdom, individuals can foster a deeper connection with the divine and contribute positively to their communities. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek inner peace and understanding while honoring the collective journey of humanity.
