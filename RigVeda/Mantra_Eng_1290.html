यद आयातं दिवोदासाय वर्तिर भरद्वाजायाश्विनां हयन्ता |रेवद उवाह सचनो रथो वां वृषभश च शिंशुमारश च युक्ता ||
yad ayātaṁ divodāsāya vartir bharadvājāyāśvinā hayantā | revad uvāha sacano ratho vāṁ vṛṣabhaś ca śiṁśumāraś ca yuktā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • ayātaṁ: has come forth
    • divodāsāya: to the divine beings
    • vartir: the one who moves
    • bharadvājāyāśvinā: to the Bharadvaja and the Ashvins
    • hayantā: who are the horsemen
    • revad: the one who is powerful
    • uvāha: has brought forth
    • sacano: the one who is true
    • ratho: the chariot
    • vāṁ: for you
    • vṛṣabhaś: the bull
    • ca: and
    • śiṁśumāraś: the one who is swift
    • ca: and
    • yuktā: who is equipped
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine beings and their connection to the forces of nature and human endeavors.
    • The mention of "Bharadvaja" and "Ashvins" refers to revered sages and deities associated with health, medicine, and the well-being of humanity.
    • The "chariot" symbolizes the journey of life, while the "bull" and "swift one" represent strength and agility, essential qualities for overcoming challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and health. For instance, when we seek medical help, we often rely on the expertise of doctors (akin to the Ashvins) to guide us through our health challenges.
    • The concept of a "chariot" can be seen in the context of personal growth and career development, where individuals must navigate their paths with strength and determination.
    • In sports, athletes embody the qualities of the "bull" and "swift one," demonstrating strength and agility to achieve their goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine guidance and the qualities of strength, agility, and truth in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek wisdom and support from higher powers while also cultivating our own inner strength to navigate life's challenges.
    • In essence, it encourages us to align ourselves with the forces of nature and the divine to achieve our goals and maintain our well-being.
