महि राधो विश्वजन्यं दधानान भरद्वाजान सारञ्जयो अभ्य अयष्ट ||
mahi rādho viśvajanyaṁ dadhānān bharadvājān sārñjayo abhy ayaṣṭa ||
  • mahi: This word translates to "great" or "mighty." It signifies something of immense importance or power.
  • rādho: This term can be interpreted as "to be nourished" or "to be supported." It implies a sense of sustenance and growth.
  • viśvajanyaṁ: This means "universal" or "all-encompassing." It refers to something that is relevant to all beings or aspects of life.
  • dadhānān: This word means "holding" or "possessing." It indicates the act of maintaining or carrying something significant.
  • bharadvājān: This refers to the Bharadvaja clan, known for their wisdom and knowledge in Vedic literature. It symbolizes the importance of learning and intellectual heritage.
  • sārñjayo: This term can be understood as "the one who is connected" or "the one who is united." It emphasizes the idea of unity and interconnectedness.
  • abhy ayaṣṭa: This phrase translates to "has been invoked" or "has been called upon." It signifies the act of seeking assistance or guidance from a higher power.

The mantra as a whole can be interpreted as a call to the great universal forces to support and nourish the wisdom of the Bharadvaja clan, emphasizing the importance of unity and the invocation of divine assistance.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra speaks of nourishment and support, individuals can seek guidance and resources to foster their personal development and well-being.
  • Community Unity: The emphasis on interconnectedness can inspire communities to work together, supporting one another in times of need, much like the Bharadvaja clan's wisdom can guide collective efforts.
  • Seeking Knowledge: The reference to the Bharadvaja clan highlights the value of education and learning. In modern contexts, this can translate to the pursuit of knowledge through formal education or self-study.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for universal support and the importance of wisdom and unity. It serves as a reminder that in our personal and communal lives, we should seek nourishment from the greater forces around us, embrace our interconnectedness, and value the pursuit of knowledge. By invoking these principles, we can create a more harmonious and supportive environment for ourselves and others.
