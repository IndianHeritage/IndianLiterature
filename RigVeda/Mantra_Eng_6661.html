तिग्मम् एको बिभर्ति हस्त आयुधं शुचिर उग्रो जलाशभेषजः ||
tigmam eko bibharti hasta āyudhaṁ śucir ugro jalāṣabheṣajaḥ ||
  • tigmam: This word translates to "sharp" or "piercing." It signifies something that has a cutting edge or is intense in nature.
  • eko: Meaning "one" or "single," this word emphasizes unity or singularity in purpose or action.
  • bibharti: This means "carries" or "holds." It indicates the act of possessing or bearing something significant.
  • hasta: Translated as "hand," it symbolizes action, skill, and the ability to perform tasks.
  • āyudhaṁ: This word means "weapon" or "tool." It represents the means through which one can achieve goals or defend oneself.
  • śucir: Meaning "pure" or "clean," this word denotes clarity, both in thought and action.
  • ugro: This translates to "fierce" or "powerful," suggesting strength and intensity in character or action.
  • jalāṣabheṣajaḥ: This compound word can be broken down into "jala" (water), "aṣa" (to be), and "bheṣajaḥ" (medicine). It refers to the healing properties of water, indicating its purifying and restorative qualities.

The mantra can be interpreted as a powerful invocation of a singular force that wields sharpness and purity, carrying the tools necessary for action and healing. It emphasizes the importance of strength and clarity in one's endeavors, while also acknowledging the restorative power of nature, particularly water.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Development: The idea of carrying a "sharp" mind and "pure" intentions can inspire individuals to pursue knowledge and self-improvement with clarity and focus.
  • Conflict Resolution: The concept of wielding a "weapon" can be metaphorical for using effective communication and negotiation skills to resolve disputes, emphasizing the need for strength in dialogue.
  • Health and Wellness: The reference to water as a healing agent can remind us of the importance of hydration and the therapeutic properties of nature, encouraging practices like mindfulness and meditation by water bodies.

Conclusion: This mantra serves as a reminder of the balance between strength and purity in our actions. It encourages us to harness our inner power while remaining grounded in clarity and healing. In a world filled with challenges, the teachings of this mantra can guide individuals towards a path of constructive action, personal growth, and holistic well-being.
