पातं नो रुद्रā पायुभिर उता त्रायेतां सुत्रात्रा |तुर्यामा दस्यून तनुभिः ||
pātaṁ no rudrā pāyubhir uta trāyethāṁ sutrātrā | turyāma dasyūn tanūbhiḥ ||
  • pātaṁ no rudrā: This phrase can be translated as "May Rudra protect us." Rudra is a deity associated with storms, wind, and the hunt, often seen as a fierce protector. In real life, this can be understood as a call for divine protection in times of trouble or uncertainty.
  • pāyubhir uta trāyethāṁ sutrātrā: This translates to "May He protect us with His arms and save us from dangers." Here, "pāyubhir" refers to the arms or strength of Rudra, symbolizing the protective power that can shield us from harm. In everyday life, this can be related to seeking support from loved ones or community during challenging times.
  • turyāma dasyūn tanūbhiḥ: This means "May we overcome the enemies with our bodies." The term "dasyūn" refers to adversaries or obstacles, while "tanūbhiḥ" relates to our physical form or strength. This can be interpreted as a reminder to face challenges head-on, using our physical and mental capabilities to overcome adversities.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine protection, community support, and personal strength in overcoming life's challenges. It serves as a reminder that while we may face adversities, we are not alone, and we can call upon higher powers and our own resources to navigate difficulties.
  • Real-life Application: In practical terms, this mantra can be invoked during times of stress, conflict, or uncertainty. For instance, during a personal crisis, one might recite this mantra to seek strength and protection, while also relying on friends and family for support. In a broader context, communities can come together, invoking this mantra to foster unity and resilience against common challenges.
