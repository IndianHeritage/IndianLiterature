भरेष्व इन्द्रं सुहवं हवामहे ऽंहोमुचं सुकृतं दैव्यम् जनम् |अग्निम् मित्रं वरुणं सातये भागं dyावापृथिवी मरुतः स्वस्तये ||
bhareṣv indraṁ suhavaṁ havāmahe 'ṁhomucaṁ sukṛtaṁ daivyaṁ janam | agnim mitraṁ varuṇaṁ sātaye bhagaṁ dyāvāpṛthivī marutaḥ svastaye ||
  • bhareṣv indraṁ suhavaṁ havāmahe: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke Indra, the powerful and benevolent one." Indra is often regarded as the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and leadership. In real life, this can be seen as a call for leadership qualities in individuals, encouraging them to be strong and benevolent in their actions.
  • 'ṁhomucaṁ sukṛtaṁ daivyaṁ janam: This translates to "We seek the divine and righteous community." Here, the emphasis is on the importance of a virtuous society. In modern contexts, this can relate to the need for ethical behavior and community service, where individuals work together for the common good, fostering a sense of belonging and support.
  • agnim mitraṁ varuṇaṁ sātaye bhagaṁ: This part means "We honor Agni, the friend, and Varuna, the protector." Agni represents fire and transformation, while Varuna symbolizes cosmic order and moral authority. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as recognizing the importance of both change and stability in our lives, balancing ambition with ethical considerations.
  • dyāvāpṛthivī marutaḥ svastaye: This translates to "May the heavens and the earth, along with the winds, bring us well-being." This line highlights the interconnectedness of nature and humanity. In practical terms, it can be seen in environmental efforts where people strive to maintain harmony with nature for the well-being of all living beings.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of invoking divine qualities such as strength, community, ethical behavior, and harmony with nature. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to embody these virtues, fostering a society that values leadership, righteousness, and environmental stewardship. By integrating these principles into our daily lives, we can contribute to a more just and harmonious world.
