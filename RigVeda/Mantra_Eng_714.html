तदु प्रयक्षतममस्य कर्म दश्मस्य चारुतमम अस्ति दंशः |उपह्वरे यदुपरा अपिन्वन मध्वर्णसो नद्यः चतस्रः ||
tad u prayakṣatamam asya karma dasmasya cārutamam asti daṁsaḥ | upahvare yad uparā apinvan madhvarṇaso nadya1ś catasraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • u: indeed
    • prayakṣatamam: the most excellent or supreme
    • asya: of this
    • karma: action or deed
    • dasmasya: of the ten (or the tenfold)
    • cārutamam: the most beautiful or charming
    • asti: exists
    • daṁsaḥ: the part or portion
    • upahvare: to be offered or presented
    • yad: which
    • uparā: above or higher
    • apinvan: having abundance or richness
    • madhvarṇaso: of the sweet or pleasant color
    • nadyaḥ: rivers
    • catasraḥ: four
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the supreme action and beauty that exists in the universe.
    • It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and offering the best of our actions and deeds.
    • The mention of "four rivers" can symbolize the flow of life, knowledge, and resources that nourish us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can inspire individuals to strive for excellence in their work, ensuring that their contributions are both impactful and beautiful.
    • In personal relationships, it encourages us to offer our best selves to others, fostering love and harmony.
    • The concept of "four rivers" can be likened to the four essential elements of life: love, knowledge, health, and wealth, which we must nurture and balance.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call to action for individuals to recognize the beauty and excellence in their deeds.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions have the power to create a positive impact on ourselves and the world around us.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a more fulfilling life.
