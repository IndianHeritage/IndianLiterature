प्रति त्यं चारुम अध्वरं गोपीथाय प्र हूयसे |मरुद्भिर अग्न आ गहि ||
prati tyaṁ cārum adhvaraṁ gopīthāya pra hūyase | marudbhir agna ā gahi ||
  • Mantra: "prati tyaṁ cārum adhvaraṁ gopīthāya pra hūyase | marudbhir agna ā gahi ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prati: towards, in accordance with
    • tyaṁ: that, this
    • cārum: beautiful, pleasing
    • adhvaraṁ: the sacred ritual or sacrifice
    • gopīthāya: for the protection of the cows (or the essence of life)
    • pra: forth, out
    • hūyase: you are invoked, called upon
    • marudbhir: by the winds, the forces of nature
    • agna: O Agni (the fire god)
    • ā: come
    • gahi: grasp, hold
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to Agni, the fire god, to come forth and bless the sacred rituals.
    • The phrase "cārum adhvaraṁ" emphasizes the beauty and sanctity of the rituals being performed.
    • By invoking Agni, the mantra seeks to ensure the protection and prosperity of life, symbolized by "gopīthāya," which refers to the cows, a vital resource in Vedic culture.
    • The mention of "marudbhir" indicates the connection between the divine and natural forces, suggesting that the rituals are in harmony with nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern agricultural practices, rituals similar to this mantra can be observed during harvest festivals where farmers invoke blessings for a good yield.
    • In many cultures, fire is still considered sacred, and rituals involving fire are performed to seek protection and prosperity, akin to the invocation of Agni in this mantra.
    • Environmental awareness can be linked to the idea of "gopīthāya," where the protection of nature and animals is seen as essential for human survival and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of rituals in connecting with the divine and the natural world.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interdependence between humanity, nature, and the divine forces that govern life.
    • In contemporary life, the principles of this mantra can be applied in practices that promote sustainability, respect for nature, and the importance of community rituals that foster unity and gratitude.
