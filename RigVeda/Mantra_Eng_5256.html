त्वं अग्नि वीरवत् यशो देवश्च सविता भगः | दितिश्च दाति वार्यम् ||
tvam agne vīravad yaśo devaś ca savitā bhagaḥ | ditiś ca dāti vāryam ||
  • tvam - This word translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity.
  • agne - Refers to "Agni," the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • vīravad - Means "possessor of valor," highlighting the qualities of bravery and strength.
  • yaśo - Translates to "fame" or "glory," suggesting the importance of reputation and honor.
  • devaś - Means "divine" or "godly," emphasizing the sacred nature of the addressed entity.
  • ca - A conjunction meaning "and," linking the qualities being described.
  • savitā - Refers to "Savitri," the sun god, representing life, light, and enlightenment.
  • bhagaḥ - Translates to "prosperity" or "wealth," indicating abundance and well-being.
  • ditiś - Means "the one who gives," suggesting a nurturing and generous aspect.
  • ca - Again, a conjunction meaning "and."
  • dāti - Translates to "gives," reinforcing the idea of generosity and provision.
  • vāryam - Means "protection" or "safeguarding," indicating the importance of security and safety.

This mantra invokes the divine qualities of Agni and Savitri, emphasizing valor, fame, prosperity, and protection. It highlights the interconnectedness of these attributes in achieving a fulfilling life. For example, in real life, a leader who embodies bravery (vīravad) and seeks glory (yaśo) can inspire their community, while also ensuring the well-being (bhagaḥ) of those they lead. The nurturing aspect (ditiś) signifies the importance of generosity in relationships, whether in family, friendships, or community service.


In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the virtues that one should aspire to cultivate in life. It is applicable in various scenarios, such as leadership, personal development, and community engagement. By embodying these qualities, individuals can contribute positively to society, ensuring a harmonious and prosperous existence for themselves and others.
