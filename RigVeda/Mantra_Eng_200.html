उत त्यं चमसं नवं तवष्टर देवस्य निष्कृतम् |अकर्ता चतुरः पुनः ||
uta tyaṁ camasaṁ navaṁ tvaṣṭur devasya niṣkṛtam | akarta caturaḥ punaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and
    • tyaṁ: this
    • camasaṁ: vessel or cup
    • navaṁ: new
    • tvaṣṭur: of the divine craftsman or creator
    • devasya: of the deity
    • niṣkṛtam: purified or made free
    • akarta: non-doer or inactive
    • caturaḥ: clever or skillful
    • punaḥ: again
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a new vessel created by the divine craftsman, which is purified and represents a fresh beginning.
    • The term "akarta" suggests a state of non-action, indicating that the divine craftsman operates beyond the limitations of action and inaction.
    • The phrase "caturaḥ punaḥ" implies a cleverness or skill that allows for renewal or recreation, emphasizing the cyclical nature of creation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, one might find themselves in a situation where they need to start anew, akin to the "new vessel." For instance, after a setback, an individual may choose to reinvent themselves, much like the divine craftsman creating a new beginning.
    • In nature, the cycle of seasons can be seen as a manifestation of this mantra. Each spring, nature rejuvenates itself, symbolizing the "new vessel" that emerges from the "purified" remnants of winter.
    • In the context of innovation, companies often undergo transformations to adapt to changing markets, representing the cleverness and skill of the "caturaḥ" in creating new products or services.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of renewal and the divine aspect of creation in our lives.
    • It teaches us that even in moments of inactivity or setbacks, there is potential for cleverness and skill to bring about new beginnings.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, environmental cycles, and innovation in business, reminding us that every end is a precursor to a new beginning.
