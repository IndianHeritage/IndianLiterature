अन्तरिक्षप्रां रजसो विमानिम् उप शिक्षाम्य उर्वशीं वसिष्ठः |उप त्वा रातिः सुखृतस्य तिष्ठान निवार्तस्व हृदयम् तप्यते मे ||
antarikṣaprāṁ rajaso vimānīm upa śikṣāmy urvaśīṁ vasiṣṭhaḥ | upa tvā rātiḥ sukṛtasya tiṣṭhān ni vartasva hṛdayaṁ tapyate me ||
  • antarikṣaprāṁ: This term refers to the "space" or "atmosphere" that surrounds us. In a real-life context, it can be likened to the vastness of the universe, the air we breathe, and the environment that sustains life.
  • rajaso: This word signifies "of the nature of passion" or "activity." It can be associated with the dynamic aspects of life, such as ambition, creativity, and the drive to achieve goals.
  • vimānīm: This translates to "the celestial vehicle" or "a flying chariot." In modern terms, it can be compared to advanced technology, such as airplanes or spacecraft, that allow us to explore beyond our earthly confines.
  • upa: This means "towards" or "near." It indicates a direction or an approach, suggesting the importance of seeking knowledge or guidance.
  • śikṣāmy: This term means "I will teach" or "I will instruct." It emphasizes the role of education and learning in personal and spiritual growth.
  • urvaśīṁ: Referring to "Urvashi," a celestial nymph in Hindu mythology, this symbolizes beauty, grace, and the divine feminine. In real life, it can represent the pursuit of ideals and the appreciation of art and culture.
  • vasiṣṭhaḥ: This refers to "Vasistha," a revered sage known for his wisdom. It highlights the importance of mentorship and guidance from wise individuals in our lives.
  • upa tvā: This phrase means "towards you," indicating a call for connection or communion with the divine or higher consciousness.
  • rātiḥ: This translates to "joy" or "delight." It signifies the emotional and spiritual fulfillment that comes from living a righteous life.
  • sukṛtasya: Meaning "of good deeds," this emphasizes the significance of actions that are virtuous and beneficial to oneself and others.
  • tiṣṭhān: This means "standing" or "remaining." It suggests stability and perseverance in one's pursuits.
  • ni vartasva: This translates to "may you return" or "may you come back." It indicates a desire for the return of positive energies or blessings in life.
  • hṛdayaṁ: This means "heart." It symbolizes the core of our being, emotions, and intentions.
  • tapyate: This translates to "is heated" or "is agitated." It can refer to the passions and desires that drive us, as well as the challenges we face in life.
  • me: This means "my," indicating a personal connection to the feelings and experiences being described.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the journey of life, emphasizing the importance of seeking knowledge, pursuing good deeds, and connecting with the divine. It encourages individuals to strive for joy and fulfillment through virtuous actions and the guidance of wise mentors. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, education, and the pursuit of artistic and spiritual ideals. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can cultivate a meaningful and purposeful existence, ultimately leading to a harmonious and fulfilling life.
