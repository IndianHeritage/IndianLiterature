या उषतां मनसा सोमं अस्मै सर्वहृदा देवकामः सुनोति |न गा इन्द्रस तस्य परा ददाति प्रशस्तमिच्चारुम अस्मै कृणोति ||
ya uśatā manasā somam asmai sarvahṛdā devakāmaḥ sunoti | na gā indras tasya parā dadāti praśastam ic cārum asmai kṛṇoti ||
  • ya uśatā manasā somam asmai sarvahṛdā devakāmaḥ sunoti
    • ya - who
    • uśatā - with the mind that is pure or auspicious
    • manasā - with the mind
    • somam - the essence of life, often associated with the moon or divine nectar
    • asmai - to him
    • sarvahṛdā - from all hearts
    • devakāmaḥ - desiring the divine
    • sunoti - bestows or grants
  • na gā indras tasya parā dadāti praśastam ic cārum asmai kṛṇoti
    • na - not
    • gā - the song or praise
    • indras - Indra, the king of gods
    • tasya - to him
    • parā - supreme or extraordinary
    • dadāti - gives
    • praśastam - commendable or praiseworthy
    • ic - desires
    • cārum - beautiful or charming
    • asmai - to him
    • kṛṇoti - creates or makes

This mantra speaks to the relationship between the divine and the individual, emphasizing the purity of intention and the desire for divine blessings. The first part highlights that a person with a pure mind, who seeks the essence of life (soma), is granted blessings from all hearts, indicating a universal acceptance and support from the divine. This can be likened to how in real life, when individuals approach their goals with sincerity and purity of heart, they often find support from their community and the universe at large.


The second part of the mantra suggests that even the supreme praise of Indra does not compare to the blessings that are bestowed upon such a person. This can be interpreted as a reminder that divine grace and the beauty of life are not merely about accolades or recognition but are rooted in genuine intentions and actions. For example, in a workplace, an employee who works with integrity and a positive mindset may not always receive formal recognition but will often find themselves supported and valued by their colleagues and superiors.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the importance of purity of mind and intention in seeking divine blessings. It teaches that true fulfillment and support come from a sincere heart and that the beauty of life is created through genuine actions. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate a mindset of positivity and integrity, leading to a more harmonious existence and deeper connections with others.
