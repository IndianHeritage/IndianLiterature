नू नो रास्व सहस्रवत् तोकवत् पुष्टिमद् वसु |द्यमद् अग्ने सुवीर्यं वर्षिष्ठम् अनुपक्षितम् ||
nū no rāsva sahasravat tokavat puṣṭimad vasu | dyumad agne suvīryaṁ varṣiṣṭham anupakṣitam ||
  • nū no rāsva sahasravat: This phrase can be interpreted as a call for abundance and prosperity. The term "nū" signifies a request or invocation, while "rāsva" suggests something that is short or limited, and "sahasravat" refers to the idea of thousands or abundance. In real life, this can be seen as a desire for a wealth of resources or opportunities.
  • tokavat puṣṭimad vasu: Here, "tokavat" implies something that is fruitful or productive, and "puṣṭimad" refers to nourishment or sustenance. "Vasu" denotes wealth or good fortune. This part of the mantra emphasizes the importance of nurturing and cultivating resources that lead to prosperity. For example, in agriculture, proper care and nourishment of crops lead to a bountiful harvest.
  • dyumad agne: "Dyumad" means radiant or shining, and "agne" refers to fire or the divine light. This can symbolize the illumination of knowledge and wisdom. In a real-life context, this can relate to the pursuit of education and enlightenment, where knowledge acts as a guiding light in one’s life.
  • suvīryaṁ varṣiṣṭham: "Suvīryaṁ" translates to excellent strength or valor, while "varṣiṣṭham" means the best or most superior. This part of the mantra calls for the cultivation of strength and valor in individuals. In practical terms, this can be seen in leadership roles where courage and strength are essential for guiding others through challenges.
  • anupakṣitam: This term means unimpeded or uninterrupted. It suggests a continuous flow of blessings and resources. In everyday life, this can be related to the idea of maintaining a steady and consistent effort towards achieving one’s goals without any hindrances.

Conclusion: The mantra "nū no rāsva sahasravat tokavat puṣṭimad vasu | dyumad agne suvīryaṁ varṣiṣṭham anupakṣitam ||" encapsulates a profound invocation for abundance, nourishment, knowledge, strength, and uninterrupted blessings. Its significance lies in the holistic approach it advocates for achieving prosperity in various aspects of life. Whether in personal development, professional growth, or community building, the principles outlined in this mantra can be applied to foster a life of fulfillment and success. By nurturing our resources, seeking knowledge, and embodying strength, we can create a life that is rich in both material and spiritual wealth.
