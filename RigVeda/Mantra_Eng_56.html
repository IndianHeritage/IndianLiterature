देवयन्तो यथा मतिṁ अच्छा विदद्वसुं गिरः |महाम अनूषत श्रुतम् ||
devayanto yathā matim acchā vidadvasuṁ giraḥ | mahām anūṣata śrutam ||
  • Devayanto: This term can be translated as "those who are divine" or "the gods." It signifies the higher powers or deities that guide and influence human actions and thoughts.
  • Yathā: This word means "as" or "in the way that." It indicates a comparison or a manner in which something is done.
  • Matim: This translates to "intellect" or "understanding." It refers to the mental faculties that allow individuals to think, reason, and make decisions.
  • Acchā: This means "good" or "well." It denotes a positive quality or state of being.
  • Vidadvasuṁ: This term can be interpreted as "to speak" or "to express." It relates to communication and the sharing of thoughts or ideas.
  • Giraḥ: This translates to "words" or "speech." It emphasizes the importance of verbal expression in conveying thoughts and intentions.
  • Mahām: This means "great" or "mighty." It signifies something of significant importance or power.
  • Anūṣata: This term can be understood as "to be heard" or "to be listened to." It highlights the act of receiving and acknowledging communication.
  • Śrutam: This translates to "that which is heard" or "knowledge." It refers to the wisdom and understanding gained through listening and learning.

  • Meaning: The mantra suggests that divine beings guide our intellect and understanding, allowing us to express ourselves positively through our words. It emphasizes the importance of listening and learning from the wisdom that is shared with us.
  • Real-life Example: In a classroom setting, a teacher (representing divine guidance) helps students (individuals) develop their understanding (matim) of a subject. The students express their thoughts (vidadvasuṁ giraḥ) during discussions, and through active listening (anūṣata), they gain knowledge (śrutam) that enhances their learning experience.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of divine guidance, human intellect, and the power of communication. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek wisdom, express themselves positively, and listen actively to others. This mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as education, personal relationships, and professional environments, where effective communication and understanding are essential for growth and harmony.
