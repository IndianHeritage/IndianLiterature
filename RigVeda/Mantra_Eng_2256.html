मन्त्र: ब्रह्मणस पते त्वम् अस्य यन्ता सूक्तस्य बोधि तनयं च जिन्वा |विश्वं तद भद्रं यद अवन्ति देवाः बृहद् वदेम विदथे सुवीराः ||
brahmaṇas pate tvam asya yantā sūktasya bodhi tanayaṁ ca jinva | viśvaṁ tad bhadraṁ yad avanti devā bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ ||
  • Brahmaṇas pate: This phrase translates to "O Lord of the Brahmanas" or "O Divine Being." It signifies a call to the supreme consciousness or the ultimate reality that governs the universe.
  • Tvam asya yantā: This means "You are the one who leads us." It emphasizes the idea of guidance and direction in life, suggesting that the divine presence is essential for navigating through challenges.
  • Sūktasya bodhi: This translates to "the wisdom of the hymn." It refers to the knowledge and understanding that can be derived from sacred texts and spiritual teachings.
  • Tanayaṁ ca jinva: This means "and may we attain success." It expresses a desire for achievement and prosperity in our endeavors, both material and spiritual.
  • Viśvaṁ tad bhadraṁ: This translates to "that which is auspicious for all." It highlights the importance of universal well-being and harmony, suggesting that true success is beneficial to everyone.
  • Yad avanti devā: This means "that which the gods protect." It indicates the divine blessings that accompany righteous actions and intentions.
  • Bṛhad vadema: This translates to "let us speak of the great." It encourages the sharing of profound truths and wisdom that can elevate human consciousness.
  • Vidathe suvīrāḥ: This means "in the assembly of the brave." It signifies the importance of community and collective strength in achieving goals and overcoming obstacles.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, wisdom, and collective strength in our pursuits. It emphasizes the importance of auspiciousness and the well-being of all beings. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community service, teamwork in professional settings, or spiritual gatherings where individuals come together to share knowledge and support one another. By invoking the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can foster a sense of unity, purpose, and success that benefits not only themselves but also the larger community.
