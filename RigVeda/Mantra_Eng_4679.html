एवेन्द्रः सुहव ऋष्वो अस्तूती अनूतī हिरीशिप्रः सत्वा |एवाऽहि जातो असमात्योजाः पुरू च वृत्राः हन्ति न दस्यून् ||
eved indraḥ suhava ṛṣvo astūtī anūtī hiriśipraḥ satvā | evā hi jāto asamātyojāḥ purū ca vṛtrā hanati ni dasyūn ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eved: Indeed
    • indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods, associated with rain and thunderstorms)
    • suhava: Good or auspicious
    • ṛṣvo: The seers or sages
    • astūtī: Praise or hymns
    • anūtī: Unceasing or continuous
    • hiriśipraḥ: Swift or quick
    • sattvā: Strength or vitality
    • evā: Thus
    • hi: Indeed
    • jāta: Born or created
    • asamātyojāḥ: Without comparison or peerless
    • purū: Many or numerous
    • ca: And
    • vṛtrā: The demon Vritra (symbolizing obstacles)
    • hanati: Kills or overcomes
    • ni: Down or away
    • dasyūn: The enemies or obstacles
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the deity representing strength and power, emphasizing the importance of his blessings in overcoming challenges.
    • The mention of "suhava" and "ṛṣvo" highlights the role of positive energy and wisdom in achieving success.
    • The phrase "purū ca vṛtrā hanati" signifies that with the right strength and support, one can overcome numerous obstacles, akin to how a leader or a team can triumph over challenges in a project or competition.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team may face various challenges (akin to Vritra) while working on a project. With strong leadership (Indra) and teamwork (suhava), they can successfully navigate these challenges.
    • In personal life, individuals often encounter obstacles such as health issues or financial difficulties. Drawing strength from community support and wisdom can help them overcome these hurdles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine support, strength, and wisdom in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It teaches us that with the right mindset and support, we can face and conquer our obstacles, whether they are personal, professional, or spiritual.
    • In essence, it encourages individuals to seek strength from within and from their community, reminding them that they are not alone in their struggles.
