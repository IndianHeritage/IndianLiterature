इन्द्रासोमा युवम् अङ्ग तरुत्रम अपत्यसाचं श्रुत्यं रराथे |युवं शुष्मं नार्यं चरषणिभ्यः सं विव्यथुः पृतनाशाहम् उग्राः ||
indrāsomā yuvam aṅga tarutram apatyasācaṁ śrutyaṁ rarāthe | yuvaṁ śuṣmaṁ naryaṁ carṣaṇibhyaḥ saṁ vivyathuḥ pṛtanāṣāham ugrā ||
  • Indrāsomā: This term refers to the duality of Indra and Soma, representing strength and vitality. Indra is the king of gods, symbolizing power and courage, while Soma represents the divine nectar that grants immortality and joy.
  • Yuvam: This word means "young" or "youthful," indicating freshness, energy, and potential. It emphasizes the importance of youth in achieving goals and aspirations.
  • Aṅga: This translates to "body" or "part," suggesting the physical aspect of existence. It can also imply the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of physical health.
  • Tarutram: This term means "the tree" or "the support," symbolizing stability and growth. Trees are often seen as symbols of life, providing shelter, nourishment, and a connection to nature.
  • Apatyasācaṁ: This refers to "offspring" or "descendants," highlighting the significance of lineage and the continuation of life through generations.
  • Śrutyaṁ: This word means "hearing" or "listening," emphasizing the importance of knowledge and wisdom gained through attentive listening and learning.
  • Rarāthe: This term can be interpreted as "to be invoked" or "to be called upon," suggesting the act of seeking divine assistance or guidance.
  • Yuvaṁ: Reiterating the concept of youth, it reinforces the idea of vitality and the potential for growth and change.
  • Śuṣmaṁ: This means "subtle" or "refined," indicating the importance of inner qualities and virtues that may not be immediately visible but are essential for true strength.
  • Naryaṁ: This term refers to "noble" or "virtuous," highlighting the significance of moral character and integrity in one's actions.
  • Carṣaṇibhyaḥ: This can be interpreted as "from the earth" or "from the ground," emphasizing the connection to nature and the importance of grounding oneself in reality.
  • Saṁ vivyathuḥ: This phrase means "they were inspired" or "they were moved," indicating the transformative power of divine influence and inspiration.
  • Pṛtanāṣāham: This term can be understood as "I am the one who destroys," suggesting the power to overcome obstacles and challenges in life.
  • Ugrā: This means "fierce" or "powerful," symbolizing the strength and determination needed to face life's adversities.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of youth, strength, and the interconnectedness of life. It emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge, maintaining moral integrity, and drawing inspiration from the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, leadership, and the pursuit of one's goals. It serves as a reminder to harness our inner strength, remain grounded, and strive for excellence while being mindful of our responsibilities towards future generations. By invoking the qualities represented in this mantra, individuals can navigate challenges with courage and wisdom, ultimately contributing to a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
