यजस्व वीर प्र विहि मनायतो भद्रम मनः कृणुष्व वृततूर्ये |हविष कृणुष्व सुभगो यथाससि ब्रह्मणस पतेर अव आ वृणीमहे ||
yajasva vīra pra vihi manāyato bhadram manaḥ kṛṇuṣva vṛtratūrye | haviṣ kṛṇuṣva subhago yathāsasi brahmaṇas pater ava ā vṛṇīmahe ||
  • yajasva: This term translates to "perform" or "worship." It signifies the act of engaging in rituals or devotion, which is essential in many cultures today, such as participating in community service or spiritual practices.
  • vīra: Meaning "hero" or "brave one," this word emphasizes the importance of courage and valor. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who stand up for justice or protect others, like firefighters or activists.
  • pra vihi: This phrase can be interpreted as "lead us" or "guide us." It reflects the need for guidance in our lives, akin to seeking mentorship or advice from experienced individuals in various fields.
  • manāyato: This word means "with the mind" or "intellectually." It highlights the importance of thoughtful consideration in our actions, similar to how we make decisions based on careful analysis in business or personal life.
  • bhadram: Translated as "auspicious" or "good," this term signifies the desire for positive outcomes. In everyday life, this can relate to wishing for success in endeavors, such as starting a new job or project.
  • manaḥ: Meaning "mind," it emphasizes the role of mental focus and clarity. This is relevant in practices like meditation or mindfulness, which are increasingly popular for mental well-being.
  • kṛṇuṣva: This translates to "make" or "create." It encourages proactive behavior, akin to entrepreneurs who create new businesses or artists who bring their visions to life.
  • vṛtratūrye: This term can be understood as "overcoming obstacles." It reflects the universal struggle against challenges, similar to how individuals face and conquer personal or professional hurdles.
  • haviṣ: Meaning "offering," this word signifies the importance of giving and sharing. In modern contexts, this can be seen in charitable acts or community support initiatives.
  • subhago: Translated as "fortunate" or "blessed," it conveys a sense of gratitude for the good things in life, similar to how people express appreciation for their health, family, or achievements.
  • yathāsasi: This phrase means "as you wish" or "according to your desire." It reflects the idea of aligning one's actions with personal goals, much like setting and pursuing personal or professional aspirations.
  • brahmaṇas pater: This translates to "of the Lord of creation," emphasizing a connection to a higher power or universal truth. In real life, this can relate to spiritual beliefs or philosophies that guide individuals in their lives.
  • ava ā vṛṇīmahe: This phrase means "we choose" or "we invoke." It signifies the act of making conscious choices, akin to how individuals select their paths in life based on values and beliefs.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the importance of courage, guidance, and positive intention in our lives. It encourages individuals to engage in worship and service, seek guidance, and overcome obstacles with a clear mind. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, community service, and spiritual practices. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can strive for a life filled with purpose, positivity, and resilience, ultimately contributing to their own well-being and that of others around them.
