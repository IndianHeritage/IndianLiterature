पवमान विदा रयिम अस्मभ्यं सोम सुश्रियम् |इन्दो सहस्रवर्चसम् ||
pavamāna vidā rayim asmabhyaṁ soma suśriyam | indo sahasravarcasam ||
  • pavamāna: This term refers to the "purifying" or "cleansing" aspect. In a broader sense, it symbolizes the process of purification, both physically and spiritually.
  • vidā: This word means "to know" or "knowledge." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in our lives.
  • rayim: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional richness.
  • asmabhyaṁ: This means "for us" or "to us," indicating a collective aspect, suggesting that the blessings are sought for a group or community.
  • soma: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. It represents the essence of life and vitality.
  • suśriyam: This term means "beautiful" or "auspicious." It denotes the positive qualities that one seeks in life.
  • indo: Referring to "Indra," the king of gods, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • sahasravarcasam: This translates to "having a thousand rays" or "radiant." It signifies abundance, brilliance, and the divine light that guides us.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for purification and the attainment of wealth and prosperity through knowledge and divine blessings. It emphasizes the importance of seeking not just material wealth but also spiritual enlightenment and communal well-being.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use the essence of this mantra to focus on self-improvement, seeking knowledge and wisdom to enhance their lives.
  • Community Welfare: The collective aspect of the mantra encourages community efforts towards prosperity and well-being, fostering a sense of unity and shared goals.
  • Spiritual Practices: Engaging in rituals or meditative practices that invoke purification and divine blessings can help individuals align with the positive energies represented in the mantra.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the balance between material and spiritual wealth. It serves as a reminder to seek knowledge, foster community, and embrace the divine qualities that lead to a prosperous and fulfilling life.
