कदā सूनुः पितरं जात इच्छाच चक्रन नाश्रु वर्तयद विजानन |को दम्पती समनसा वि यūयोद अध यद अग्निः श्वशुरेषु दीदायत ||
kadā sūnuḥ pitaraṁ jāta icchāc cakran nāśru vartayad vijānan | ko dampatī samanasā vi yūyod adha yad agniḥ śvaśureṣu dīdayat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kadā: When
    • sūnuḥ: the son
    • pitaraṁ: to the father
    • jāta: born
    • icchāc: desiring
    • cakran: the wheel
    • nāśru: tears
    • vartayad: flowing
    • vijānan: knowing
  • Meaning of the Mantra:
    • This mantra reflects on the relationship between a son and his father, emphasizing the emotional bond and the responsibilities that come with it.
    • The phrase "when the son, born to the father, desires" suggests a moment of introspection and realization of one's duties and aspirations.
    • The mention of "tears flowing" indicates the emotional weight of these responsibilities, highlighting the struggles and sacrifices involved.
    • The term "knowing" implies a deeper understanding of one's role within the family and society.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, a son may feel the pressure to fulfill his father's expectations, whether in terms of career choices or personal conduct.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or funerals, the emotional weight of familial duties can lead to tears, reflecting the joy or sorrow of the occasion.
    • In modern society, many individuals struggle with balancing personal aspirations with familial obligations, often leading to moments of reflection and emotional turmoil.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of familial bonds and the emotional complexities that come with them.
    • It encourages individuals to reflect on their roles within their families and to acknowledge the emotional aspects of these relationships.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during family gatherings, in moments of personal crisis, or when making significant life decisions.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the need for understanding, compassion, and communication within family dynamics, fostering stronger relationships and emotional well-being.
