हवामहे त्वा वयम् प्रयस्वन्तः सुते सचा |पुत्रासो न पितरं वाजसातये मंहिष्ठं वाजसातये ||
havāmahe tvā vayam prayasvantaḥ sute sacā | putrāso na pitaraṁ vājasātaye maṁhiṣṭhaṁ vājasātaye ||
  • Mantra: "havāmahe tvā vayam prayasvantaḥ sute sacā | putrāso na pitaraṁ vājasātaye maṁhiṣṭhaṁ vājasātaye ||"
  • Translation: "We invoke you, O divine one, with our efforts and offerings; may we be blessed with strength and prosperity, and may our sons be as strong as the best of fathers."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • havāmahe: We invoke or call upon.
    • tvā: You (referring to a divine entity).
    • vayam: We.
    • prayasvantaḥ: With our efforts or endeavors.
    • sute: Blessings or prosperity.
    • sacā: And (also).
    • putrāso: Sons or offspring.
    • na: Not.
    • pitaraṁ: Father.
    • vājasātaye: For strength or vigor.
    • maṁhiṣṭhaṁ: The best or most excellent.

  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine blessings through our own efforts. It reflects the belief that while divine grace is essential, personal endeavor is equally crucial in achieving success.
    • The mention of "sons" signifies the continuation of lineage and the hope that future generations will embody strength and virtues akin to their forebears.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, parents often pray for the well-being and success of their children, hoping they will grow up to be strong and virtuous individuals. This mantra encapsulates that sentiment.
    • In a professional context, individuals may invoke this mantra before starting a new venture, seeking divine support while also committing to hard work and perseverance.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the balance between divine intervention and human effort. It encourages individuals to strive for excellence while seeking blessings for themselves and their families.
    • Its application can be seen in various aspects of life, from personal aspirations to familial responsibilities, highlighting the interconnectedness of effort, strength, and divine grace.
