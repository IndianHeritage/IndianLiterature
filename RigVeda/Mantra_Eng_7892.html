मन्त्र: इन्दो यद् अद्रिभिः सुतः पवित्रं परिधावसि |अरम् इन्द्रस्य धाम्ने ||
indo yad adribhiḥ sutaḥ pavitram paridhāvasi | aram indrasya dhāmne ||
  • Indo: This term refers to the deity Indra, who is often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and the heavens in Vedic literature. Indra is considered a powerful figure who protects and provides for humanity.
  • Yad: This word translates to "that" or "which," indicating a connection or relationship to what follows.
  • Adribhiḥ: This term means "by the mountains" or "from the mountains." It signifies the natural world and the strength derived from it.
  • Sutaḥ: This word means "son" or "offspring." In this context, it can refer to the blessings or gifts that come from Indra.
  • Pavitram: This translates to "pure" or "sacred." It emphasizes the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and offerings.
  • Paridhāvasi: This term means "you move around" or "you encircle." It suggests a protective or encompassing nature, indicating how the divine presence surrounds and supports us.
  • Aram: This word can mean "to cultivate" or "to work." It implies the effort and labor that go into achieving success and prosperity.
  • Indrasya: This means "of Indra," indicating possession or association with the divine qualities of Indra.
  • Dhāmne: This term translates to "in the abode" or "in the realm." It refers to the space or environment where divine energy resides.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Indra, asking for his blessings and protection. It highlights the connection between nature (represented by the mountains) and the divine, emphasizing the importance of purity and effort in our endeavors.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Environmental Awareness: Just as the mountains are a source of strength and purity, we should strive to protect our natural environment. This can be seen in initiatives aimed at conservation and sustainable living.
  • Personal Growth: The idea of cultivating one's efforts (Aram) resonates with the importance of hard work and dedication in achieving personal goals, whether in education, career, or relationships.
  • Spiritual Practices: The emphasis on purity (Pavitram) can be reflected in our daily practices, encouraging mindfulness, ethical living, and spiritual growth.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature, divinity, and human effort. It encourages us to seek purity in our actions, to honor the natural world, and to work diligently towards our goals, all while being mindful of the divine presence that supports us in our journey.
