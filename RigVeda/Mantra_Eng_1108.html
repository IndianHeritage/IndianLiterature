प्र मन्दिने पितुमद आर्चता वचो यः कृष्णगर्भा निरहन्न ऋजिश्वना | अवस्यवो वृषणं वज्रदक्षिणं मरुत्वन्तं सख्याय हवामहे ||
pra mandine pitumad arcatā vaco yaḥ kṛṣṇagarbhā nirahann ṛjiśvanā | avasyavo vṛṣaṇaṁ vajradakṣiṇam marutvantaṁ sakhyāya havāmahe ||
  • pra mandine: This phrase can be interpreted as "to the one who is pleasing" or "to the one who is delightful." It signifies an appeal to a divine or auspicious presence that brings joy and positivity.
  • pitumad: This term refers to "the one who is nourishing" or "the one who provides sustenance." It highlights the importance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual, in our lives.
  • arcatā: This word means "to be worshipped" or "to be honored." It emphasizes the act of reverence and devotion towards the divine or higher powers.
  • vaco yaḥ: This translates to "the one who speaks" or "the one who communicates." It suggests the significance of communication, whether it be with the divine or among individuals.
  • kṛṣṇagarbhā: This term can be understood as "the one who is born of darkness" or "the one who embodies the essence of creation." It symbolizes the potential and creativity that arises from the unknown or the unseen.
  • nirahann: This means "to be free from" or "to be liberated." It indicates a state of freedom from obstacles or negativity, allowing for growth and progress.
  • ṛjiśvanā: This word refers to "the one who is righteous" or "the one who follows the path of truth." It underscores the importance of righteousness and integrity in our actions.
  • avasyavo: This term means "inevitable" or "certain." It conveys a sense of certainty and assurance in the divine presence and support.
  • vṛṣaṇaṁ: This translates to "the one who is strong" or "the one who is powerful." It signifies strength and resilience, qualities that are essential in overcoming challenges.
  • vajradakṣiṇam: This phrase means "the one who is as strong as a thunderbolt." It symbolizes immense power and the ability to overcome obstacles with force and determination.
  • marutvantaṁ: This term refers to "the one who is associated with the winds" or "the one who embodies movement." It signifies the dynamic nature of life and the importance of adaptability.
  • sakhyāya: This means "for friendship" or "for companionship." It highlights the value of relationships and the support we receive from others.
  • havāmahe: This translates to "we invoke" or "we offer." It signifies the act of calling upon the divine or higher powers for assistance and guidance.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound appeal to divine forces for nourishment, strength, and guidance. It emphasizes the importance of communication, righteousness, and the inevitability of divine support in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during times of need, such as seeking strength in challenging situations, fostering relationships, or invoking positivity and creativity in personal and professional endeavors. By recognizing the divine presence in our lives and honoring it through worship and communication, we can cultivate a sense of peace, resilience, and purpose.
