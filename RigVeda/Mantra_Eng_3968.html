इति चिन् नु प्रजायै पशुमत्यै देवासो वनते मर्त्यो वा देवासो वनते मर्त्यो वः |अत्रा शिवां तंवो धासिम अस्यां जराṁ चिन् मे निरृतिर जाग्रसिता ||
iti cin nu prajāyai paśumatyai devāso vanate martyo va ā devāso vanate martyo vaḥ | atrā śivāṁ tanvo dhāsim asyā jarāṁ cin me nirṛtir jagrasīta ||
  • iti cin nu prajāyai: This phrase can be interpreted as a call for the divine to grant progeny or offspring. In real life, this can relate to the desire for family and the continuation of lineage, which is a fundamental aspect of human life.
  • paśumatyai: This term refers to the wealth of cattle or livestock, symbolizing prosperity and abundance. In many cultures, including agrarian societies, livestock is a sign of wealth and sustenance. For example, a farmer's success is often measured by the health and number of their livestock.
  • devāso vanate martyo: This part suggests that the divine beings (devas) are invoked to assist mortals (martyo). It highlights the relationship between the divine and human realms, where humans seek help from higher powers. In modern life, this can be seen in the way people turn to spirituality or religion during times of need, seeking guidance and support.
  • atrā śivāṁ tanvo dhāsim: This phrase can be understood as a plea for the divine to bestow a healthy and auspicious body. In contemporary terms, this reflects the importance of health and well-being in our lives. For instance, people often engage in practices like yoga, meditation, and healthy eating to maintain their physical and mental health.
  • asyā jarāṁ cin me: This line refers to the desire to overcome aging or decay. In today's world, many individuals seek ways to maintain youthfulness and vitality, whether through skincare, fitness regimes, or medical advancements. The pursuit of longevity and health is a common theme in modern society.
  • nirṛtir jagrasīta: This concluding phrase can be interpreted as a request for the removal of misfortune or obstacles. In real life, this resonates with the human experience of facing challenges and seeking ways to overcome them. People often engage in rituals, prayers, or positive affirmations to attract good fortune and dispel negativity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for prosperity, health, and divine assistance in overcoming life's challenges. Its significance lies in the universal human desires for family, wealth, well-being, and protection from misfortune. In real life, these themes are applicable in various contexts, such as family planning, financial stability, health consciousness, and spiritual practices. By invoking these ideals, individuals can find motivation and guidance in their daily lives, fostering a sense of connection to the divine and the greater universe.
