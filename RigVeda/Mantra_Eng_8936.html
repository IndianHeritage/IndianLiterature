अजो भागस तपसा तं तपस्व तं ते शोचिस तपतु तं ते आर्चिः |यास ते शिवास तन्वो जातवेदस ताभिर वहैणं सुखृताम उ लोकम् ||
ajo bhāgas tapasā taṁ tapasva taṁ te śocis tapatu taṁ te arciḥ | yās te śivās tanvo jātavedas tābhir vahainaṁ sukṛtām u lokam ||
  • Ajo bhāgas tapasā: This phrase can be interpreted as "the unaging, eternal essence through austerity." Here, "ajo" means eternal or unborn, and "tapasā" refers to the practice of austerity or spiritual discipline. In real life, this can be related to the idea that true wisdom and understanding come from inner reflection and self-discipline, much like how individuals often engage in meditation or yoga to achieve mental clarity.
  • Taṁ tapasva taṁ te śocis tapatu: This translates to "may that essence, which is the source of all light, illuminate you." The word "śocis" refers to light or brightness. In practical terms, this can be seen in how knowledge and enlightenment can guide individuals in their lives, similar to how a mentor or teacher can illuminate the path for a student.
  • Taṁ te arciḥ: This means "may that light shine upon you." The term "arciḥ" signifies radiance or brilliance. In everyday life, this can be likened to the inspiration one receives from art, nature, or even acts of kindness, which can uplift and motivate individuals to pursue their goals.
  • Yās te śivās tanvo jātavedas: This phrase can be understood as "those who are blessed with auspiciousness and knowledge." "Jātavedas" refers to the one who knows all births or the one who is aware of all creation. This can be related to the idea that individuals who seek knowledge and understanding are often more successful and fulfilled in their lives, as they are better equipped to navigate challenges.
  • Tābhir vahainaṁ sukṛtām u lokam: This translates to "may you be carried to the realm of the virtuous." The term "sukṛtām" refers to the righteous or virtuous actions. In real life, this can be seen in how good deeds and ethical behavior can lead to a more harmonious and fulfilling life, as well as the idea that positive actions can create a ripple effect in society.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking inner wisdom, enlightenment, and virtuous living. It signifies that through self-discipline and the pursuit of knowledge, individuals can attain a higher state of being and contribute positively to the world around them. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, education, and community service, where the focus is on uplifting oneself and others through righteous actions and the pursuit of truth.
