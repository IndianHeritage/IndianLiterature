मन्त्र: विद्वांऽ अग्ने वयुनानि क्षितीनां व्य आनुशक चुरुधो जीवसे धाः |अन्तर्विद्वांऽ अध्वनो देवयानान अतन्द्रो दूतो अभवो हविर्वाट् ||
vidvām̐ agne vayunāni kṣitīnāṁ vy ānuṣak churudho jīvase dhāḥ | antarvidvām̐ adhvano devayānān atandro dūto abhavo havirvāṭ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vidvām̐: We know
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • vayunāni: the offerings
    • kṣitīnāṁ: of the earth
    • vy: and
    • ānuṣak: the one who is invoked
    • churudho: with a pure heart
    • jīvase: for your life
    • dhāḥ: we offer
    • antarvidvām̐: we know within
    • adhvano: the paths
    • devayānān: of the divine beings
    • atandro: without fatigue
    • dūto: as a messenger
    • abhavo: may you become
    • hivirvāṭ: the offering of the oblation
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The invocation of Agni signifies the importance of fire in rituals, symbolizing purity, transformation, and the divine presence.
    • The phrase "we know" indicates a deep understanding and connection with the divine, suggesting that knowledge and awareness are essential in spiritual practices.
    • The mention of "the paths of the divine beings" emphasizes the importance of following righteous paths in life, akin to making ethical choices in our daily lives.
    • The idea of being a "messenger" without fatigue reflects the need for perseverance and dedication in our pursuits, whether in personal goals or in serving others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, the mantra can inspire leaders to act as messengers of their company's values, promoting ethical practices and transparency.
    • In personal relationships, it encourages individuals to communicate openly and honestly, fostering trust and understanding.
    • In environmental efforts, the invocation of Agni can symbolize the need for sustainable practices, recognizing the earth as a sacred entity that requires care and respect.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of knowledge, purity of intention, and the importance of ethical paths in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions, whether in rituals or daily life, should be guided by a sense of responsibility and connection to the divine.
    • By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute positively to their communities and the world at large, making the teachings of the RigVeda relevant in contemporary life.
