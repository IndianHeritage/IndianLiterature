वषट् ते विष्णव आस आ कृत्नोमि तन् मे जुषस्व शिपिविष्ट हव्यं |वर्धन्तु त्वा सुश्रुतयो गीरः मे युयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
vaṣaṭ te viṣṇav āsa ā kṛṇomi tan me juṣasva śipiviṣṭa havyam | vardhantu tvā suṣṭutayo giro me yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • vaṣaṭ: This is an invocation or a call to the divine, often used in Vedic rituals to attract the attention of the deities.
  • te: This means "to you," indicating a direct address to the deity, in this case, Lord Vishnu.
  • viṣṇav: Refers to Lord Vishnu, the preserver and protector in Hindu mythology, symbolizing sustenance and maintenance of the universe.
  • āsa: This translates to "may you come" or "may you be present," expressing a desire for the deity's presence.
  • ā kṛṇomi: Means "I offer" or "I create," indicating the act of offering something valuable to the deity.
  • tan: This means "that," referring to the offering being made.
  • me: Translates to "my," indicating personal ownership or connection to the offering.
  • juṣasva: This means "may you enjoy" or "may you be pleased," expressing the hope that the deity will accept the offering.
  • śipiviṣṭa: Refers to the "one who is seated in the heart," often associated with the divine presence within all beings.
  • havyam: This means "the offering," typically referring to food or other items offered in a ritual.
  • vardhantu: This translates to "may they grow" or "may they increase," indicating a wish for prosperity and growth.
  • tvā: Means "you," again addressing the deity directly.
  • suṣṭutayo: Refers to "well-sung praises," indicating the importance of praise and devotion in the relationship with the divine.
  • giro: This means "words" or "speech," emphasizing the power of words in invoking the divine.
  • me: Again, "my," reinforcing the personal connection.
  • yūyam: Means "you all," referring to the collective presence of the divine.
  • pāta: This translates to "protect" or "guard," expressing a desire for divine protection.
  • svastibhiḥ: Refers to "with auspiciousness," indicating a wish for well-being and good fortune.
  • sadā: Means "always," indicating a continuous desire for divine presence and protection.
  • naḥ: This means "us," indicating a collective appeal for blessings and protection.

Conclusion: This mantra is a heartfelt invocation to Lord Vishnu, expressing a desire for his presence, acceptance of offerings, and protection. It emphasizes the importance of devotion, the power of words, and the collective aspiration for growth and well-being. In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios such as during rituals, prayers for prosperity, or moments of seeking guidance and protection in challenging times. It serves as a reminder of the divine support available to individuals and communities, encouraging a sense of connection with the divine and the importance of gratitude and praise in daily life.
