यासां राजा वरुणो याति मध्य सत्यानृत एवपश्यञ जनानाम् |मधुश्चुतः शुचयो याः पावकास ता आपो देवीर इह माम् अवन्तु ||
yāsāṁ rājā varuṇo yāti madhye satyānṛte avapaśyañ janānām | madhuścutaḥ śucayo yāḥ pāvakās tā āpo devīr iha mām avantu ||
  • yāsāṁ rājā varuṇo yāti madhye satyānṛte avapaśyañ janānām
    • yāsāṁ: Refers to "those" or "who." It indicates a connection to a group or community.
    • rājā: Means "king" or "ruler," symbolizing authority and governance.
    • varuṇo: Refers to Varuna, the Vedic deity associated with water, cosmic order, and truth.
    • yāti: Means "goes" or "moves," indicating action or progression.
    • madhye: Means "in the midst" or "among," suggesting a central position.
    • satyānṛte: Combines "satya" (truth) and "anṛta" (falsehood), representing the duality of truth and deception.
    • avapaśyañ: Means "seeing" or "perceiving," indicating awareness or understanding.
    • janānām: Refers to "of the people" or "of humanity," emphasizing the collective.
  • madhuścutaḥ śucayo yāḥ pāvakās tā āpo devīr iha mām avantu
    • madhuścutaḥ: Refers to "sweetness" or "honey," symbolizing nourishment and positivity.
    • śucayo: Means "pure" or "sacred," indicating a state of cleanliness or sanctity.
    • yāḥ: Means "who" or "those," again referring to a group.
    • pāvakās: Refers to "purifying" or "sacred," often associated with fire and transformation.
    • tā: Means "those," reinforcing the connection to the previous terms.
    • āpo: Refers to "waters," symbolizing life, sustenance, and purity.
    • devīr: Means "goddesses" or "divine beings," indicating a higher spiritual presence.
    • iha: Means "here" or "in this place," grounding the mantra in the present context.
    • mām: Means "me," indicating a personal connection or invocation.
    • avantu: Means "may protect" or "may guide," suggesting a plea for support and guidance.

Conclusion:

This mantra invokes the divine presence of Varuna, the ruler of cosmic order, to guide and protect the people amidst the dualities of truth and falsehood. It emphasizes the importance of purity, both in thought and action, and seeks the blessings of the divine waters for nourishment and sanctity. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In leadership roles, where one must navigate the complexities of truth and deception while ensuring the welfare of the community.
  • In personal development, where individuals strive for purity of thought and action, seeking guidance from higher powers.
  • In environmental contexts, where the purity of water and nature is essential for sustenance and life.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of truth, purity, and divine guidance in our lives, encouraging us to seek harmony and balance in our actions and relationships.
