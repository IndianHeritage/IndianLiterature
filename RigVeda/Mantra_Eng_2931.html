उप प्रेत कुशिकाश्चेतयध्वम् अश्वं राये प्र मुंचतासुदासः |राजा वृत्रं जङ्घनत प्राग अपाग उदग अथ यजाते वर आ पृथिव्याः ||
upa preta kuśikāś cetayadhvam aśvaṁ rāye pra muñcatā sudāsaḥ | rājā vṛtraṁ jaṅghanat prāg apāg udag athā yajāte vara ā pṛthivyāḥ ||
  • upa preta kuśikāś cetayadhvam: This phrase can be translated as "O beings of the Kuśika lineage, awaken." Here, "Kuśika" refers to a lineage or family, often associated with sage Vasishtha. The call to "awaken" signifies a need for awareness and action, urging individuals to rise to their responsibilities.
  • aśvaṁ rāye pra muñcatā sudāsaḥ: This part translates to "Sudāsa, release the horse for prosperity." The horse is a symbol of strength, power, and wealth in Vedic culture. Releasing the horse signifies the act of letting go of something valuable to gain greater prosperity, akin to investing resources for future benefits.
  • rājā vṛtraṁ jaṅghanat: This means "the king slays Vritra." Vritra is a serpent or dragon figure in Vedic mythology, representing chaos and obstacles. The act of slaying Vritra symbolizes overcoming challenges and adversities, which is essential for progress and stability in society.
  • prāg apāg udag athā yajāte vara ā pṛthivyāḥ: This translates to "from the past, present, and future, let the best be offered to the Earth." This line emphasizes the importance of honoring the past while being mindful of the present and future. It suggests a holistic approach to life, where one acknowledges the interconnectedness of time and the environment.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates themes of awakening, prosperity, overcoming challenges, and honoring the Earth. It serves as a reminder of the responsibilities individuals have towards their lineage, society, and the environment.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:
    • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra calls for awakening, individuals can reflect on their own lives, seeking to become more aware of their actions and their impact on others.
    • Community Leadership: Leaders can take inspiration from the king slaying Vritra, striving to overcome societal challenges and foster a sense of unity and progress.
    • Environmental Responsibility: The emphasis on offering the best to the Earth encourages sustainable practices, reminding us to respect and protect our environment for future generations.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of our actions, the importance of overcoming challenges, and the need to honor our past while striving for a prosperous future. By applying these principles in our daily lives, we can contribute to a more harmonious and sustainable world.
