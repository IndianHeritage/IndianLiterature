त्रिर नो रयिं वहतम अश्विनाऽ युवं त्रिर देवताता त्रिर उतावतं धियः |त्रिः सौभगत्वं त्रिर उता श्रवांसी नस् त्रिष्टं वां सूरे दुहितारुहद रथम ||
trir no rayiṁ vahatam aśvinā yuvaṁ trir devatātā trir utāvataṁ dhiyaḥ | triḥ saubhagatvaṁ trir uta śravāṁsi nas triṣṭhaṁ vāṁ sūre duhitā ruhad ratham ||
  • Mantra: "trir no rayiṁ vahatam aśvinā yuvaṁ trir devatātā trir utāvataṁ dhiyaḥ | triḥ saubhagatvaṁ trir uta śravāṁsi nas triṣṭhaṁ vāṁ sūre duhitā ruhad ratham ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • trir: three times
    • no: our
    • rayiṁ: wealth or prosperity
    • vahatam: may carry or bring
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin deities)
    • yuvaṁ: young or youthful
    • devatātā: divine qualities
    • utāvataṁ: may be elevated or uplifted
    • dhiyaḥ: intellect or understanding
    • saubhagatvaṁ: auspiciousness or good fortune
    • śravāṁsi: fame or glory
    • nas: for us
    • triṣṭhaṁ: may be established
    • vāṁ: your
    • sūre: O hero or strong one
    • duhitā: daughter
    • ruhad: great or large
    • ratham: chariot
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the Ashvins, who are considered the divine physicians and bringers of health and prosperity.
    • The repetition of "trir" emphasizes the desire for abundance and blessings to come to us threefold, indicating a strong yearning for prosperity in various aspects of life.
    • The phrase "may carry wealth" suggests a request for the Ashvins to bring not just material wealth but also spiritual and intellectual richness.
    • In real life, this can be seen as a call for balance in life, where one seeks not only financial success but also wisdom and good health.
    • The mention of "auspiciousness" and "fame" indicates a desire for recognition and success in one's endeavors, which can be applicable in professional settings where one seeks to excel and be acknowledged for their contributions.
    • The reference to "your daughter" and "great chariot" symbolizes the importance of family and the journey of life, suggesting that the blessings sought are not just for oneself but also for loved ones.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of wealth, health, intellect, and family, emphasizing that true prosperity encompasses all these aspects.
    • In a real-life scenario, individuals can apply the essence of this mantra by striving for a balanced life, seeking not just financial success but also nurturing relationships and personal growth.
    • It serves as a reminder to invoke divine blessings in our pursuits, ensuring that we remain grounded and focused on holistic well-being.
