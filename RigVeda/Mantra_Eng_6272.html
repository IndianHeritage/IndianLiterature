मन्त्र: अन्ति चित् सन्तम् अह यज्ञं मार्तस्य रिपोः |नोप वेषि जातवेदः ||
anti cit santam aha yajñam martasya ripoḥ | nopa veṣi jātavedaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • anti: end or conclusion
    • cit: consciousness or awareness
    • santam: peaceful or tranquil
    • aha: I or me
    • yajñam: sacrifice or offering
    • martasya: of the mortal or human
    • ripoḥ: of the enemy or adversary
    • nopa: not or without
    • veṣi: to cover or conceal
    • jātavedaḥ: the one who knows the origin or the divine fire
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the ultimate goal of life, which is to achieve a state of peace and consciousness.
    • The phrase "anti cit santam" suggests that the end goal is a tranquil state of awareness.
    • The term "yajñam" refers to the sacrifices we make in life, which can be interpreted as our efforts and contributions to society.
    • By stating "martasya ripoḥ," it highlights the struggles we face as mortals, including conflicts and adversities.
    • The line "nopa veṣi jātavedaḥ" implies that true knowledge and understanding cannot be hidden or obscured, emphasizing the importance of enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, the mantra can be applied to the idea of teamwork. When individuals contribute their efforts (yajñam) towards a common goal, they can overcome challenges (martasya ripoḥ) and achieve a peaceful and productive environment (anti cit santam).
    • In personal relationships, the mantra encourages open communication and understanding. By being aware of each other's feelings and sacrifices, conflicts can be resolved, leading to a harmonious relationship.
    • In a broader societal context, this mantra can be seen in community service. When individuals come together to help others, they create a peaceful society, overcoming adversities and conflicts.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for awareness, peace, and the importance of sacrifices in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that true knowledge and understanding are essential for personal and collective growth.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious existence, both individually and within communities.
