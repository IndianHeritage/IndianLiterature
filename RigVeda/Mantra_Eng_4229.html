तद ऋतम् पृथिवी बृहच् छ्रवेष ऋषीणाम् |ज्रयसानाव् अरम् पृथ्वि अति क्षरन्ति यामभिः ||
tad ṛtam pṛthivi bṛhac chravaeṣa ṛṣīṇām | jrayasānāv aram pṛthv ati kṣaranti yāmabhiḥ ||
  • tad ṛtam: This phrase translates to "that truth" or "that order." In the context of the Rigveda, it signifies the ultimate reality or cosmic order that governs the universe.
  • pṛthivi: This word means "earth." It represents the physical world and the foundation of life.
  • bṛhac: This translates to "great" or "vast." It emphasizes the enormity and significance of the truth being referred to.
  • chravaeṣa: This term can be understood as "the sound" or "the call." It suggests a communication or resonance that connects beings to the cosmic order.
  • ṛṣīṇām: This means "of the sages" or "of the seers." It refers to those who have attained wisdom and insight into the nature of reality.
  • jrayasānāv: This can be interpreted as "those who are wise" or "those who have knowledge." It highlights the importance of wisdom in understanding the truth.
  • aram: This word means "to move" or "to act." It signifies the dynamic aspect of existence and the actions that arise from understanding the truth.
  • pṛthv: This is a variation of "pṛthivi," again referring to the earth or the material world.
  • ati: This translates to "beyond" or "transcending." It suggests that the truth goes beyond the physical realm.
  • kṣaranti: This means "to flow" or "to move." It indicates the continuous nature of truth and existence.
  • yāmabhiḥ: This can be understood as "by which" or "through which." It connects the actions and movements to the truth.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of understanding the cosmic order and truth that governs both the material and spiritual realms. It highlights the role of sages and wise individuals in perceiving this truth and acting upon it. In real life, this can be applied in various ways, such as seeking knowledge, practicing mindfulness, and striving for harmony with the natural world. By recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of wisdom, individuals can lead more meaningful and purposeful lives, contributing positively to society and the environment.
