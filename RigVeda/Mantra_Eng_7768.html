नृचक्षसं त्वा वयम इन्द्रपीतं स्वरविदम् |भक्षीमहि प्रजाम इषम् ||
nṛcakṣasaṁ tvā vayam indrapītaṁ svarvidam | bhakṣīmahi prajām iṣam ||
  • nṛcakṣasaṁ: This term can be broken down into "nṛ" meaning "human" and "cakṣasaṁ" meaning "eyes" or "vision." Together, it refers to the human perspective or understanding.
  • tvā: This means "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or force.
  • vayam: This translates to "we," suggesting a collective or communal aspect, emphasizing unity among people.
  • indrapītaṁ: This term can be understood as "Indra's seat" or "the throne of Indra," referring to a place of power, authority, or divine governance.
  • svarvidam: This means "knowledge of the heavens" or "celestial knowledge," indicating a higher understanding or wisdom that transcends ordinary human experience.
  • bhakṣīmahi: This translates to "we consume" or "we partake," suggesting an act of receiving or embracing something significant.
  • prajām: This means "creatures" or "beings," referring to all living entities, highlighting the interconnectedness of life.
  • iṣam: This term means "desire" or "will," indicating intention or purpose behind actions.

The mantra can be interpreted as a collective invocation where humans seek the divine wisdom and authority of Indra, the king of gods, to guide and nourish all beings. It emphasizes the importance of unity in seeking higher knowledge and the divine's role in fulfilling the desires of humanity.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Community Gatherings: When people come together for a common purpose, such as festivals or rituals, they can invoke this mantra to seek divine guidance and wisdom for their collective endeavors.
  • Leadership and Governance: Leaders can reflect on the significance of "Indra's seat" as a reminder of their responsibility to govern wisely and justly, ensuring the well-being of all beings under their care.
  • Education and Knowledge Sharing: In educational settings, this mantra can inspire a quest for higher knowledge, encouraging students and teachers to seek wisdom that benefits the entire community.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, wisdom, and the divine's role in human affairs. It serves as a reminder that in our pursuit of knowledge and fulfillment of desires, we must recognize our interconnectedness and the importance of seeking guidance from higher powers. This mantra can be a source of inspiration in various aspects of life, from community building to personal growth.
