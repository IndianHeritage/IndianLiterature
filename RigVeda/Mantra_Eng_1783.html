तन् नु वोचाम रभसाया जनमने पूर्वं महित्वं वृषभस्य केतवे |ऐधेव यामन मरुतस् तुविष्वणो युदेव शक्रास तविषाणि कर्तन ||
tan nu vocāma rabhasāya janmane pūrvam mahitvaṁ vṛṣabhasya ketave | aidheva yāman marutas tuviṣvaṇo yudheva śakrās taviṣāṇi kartana ||
  • tan nu vocāma: This phrase can be translated as "we invoke you" or "we call upon you." It signifies a plea or a request for divine attention and support.
  • rabhasāya janmane: "rabhasāya" refers to the powerful or mighty, while "janmane" means "to birth" or "to be born." Together, this can be interpreted as invoking the power of the divine that has been born from strength.
  • pūrvam mahitvaṁ: "pūrvam" means "previous" or "ancient," and "mahitvaṁ" refers to "greatness" or "glory." This suggests a recognition of the ancient greatness of the divine being invoked.
  • vṛṣabhasya ketave: "vṛṣabhasya" translates to "of the bull" (often symbolizing strength and virility), and "ketave" means "to the one who is the leader" or "the chief." This can be seen as a call to the chief of strength or power.
  • aidheva yāman: "aidheva" means "indeed" or "certainly," while "yāman" refers to "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is called upon." This emphasizes the certainty of the invocation.
  • marutas tuviṣvaṇo: "marutas" refers to the storm gods or winds, and "tuviṣvaṇo" means "of the powerful." This indicates a connection to the forces of nature and their might.
  • yudheva śakrās: "yudheva" means "in battle" and "śakrās" refers to "the powerful" or "the strong." This suggests a call for strength in times of conflict or challenge.
  • taviṣāṇi kartana: "taviṣāṇi" means "to perform" or "to act," and "kartana" refers to "the act of doing." This implies a call to action, urging the divine to assist in the performance of great deeds.

Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation that calls upon divine strength and greatness, particularly in times of challenge or conflict. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing ancient powers and the forces of nature, urging the divine to assist in our endeavors. In real life, this can be applicable in various scenarios such as seeking strength during difficult times, invoking support in personal or professional battles, or even during significant life changes. The mantra serves as a reminder of the strength that exists within and around us, encouraging us to act with courage and determination.
