प्रत्याग्निरुषसामाग्रमख्यद्विभातीनांसुमनारत्नधेम् |यातमश्विनासुकृतोदुरोणमुत्सूर्यो ज्योतिषा देव एति ||
praty agnir uṣasām agram akhyad vibhātīnāṁ sumanā ratnadheyam | yātam aśvinā sukṛto duroṇam ut sūryo jyotiṣā deva eti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • praty: towards
    • agnir: fire
    • uṣasām: of the dawn
    • agram: the foremost
    • akhyad: has declared
    • vibhātīnāṁ: of those who shine
    • sumanā: with good thoughts
    • ratnadheyam: the jewel or treasure
    • yātam: having gone
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin deities)
    • sukṛto: well-done or virtuous
    • duroṇam: of the difficult path
    • ut: and
    • sūryo: the sun
    • jyotiṣā: with light
    • deva: O divine
    • eti: approaches
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the fire of dawn, which is the first light that breaks the darkness, symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
    • The phrase "vibhātīnāṁ sumanā ratnadheyam" suggests that those who are enlightened and possess good thoughts are like treasures, indicating the value of wisdom and virtuous thinking.
    • The invocation of the Ashvins, who are known for their healing and benevolent qualities, emphasizes the importance of seeking help from divine forces in overcoming challenges.
    • The mention of "duroṇam" refers to the difficult paths we encounter in life, while "sūryo jyotiṣā deva eti" signifies that the divine light of the sun (knowledge) guides us through these challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, when facing a challenging situation, such as a difficult decision or a health issue, one might seek guidance through meditation or prayer, invoking the qualities of the Ashvins for support.
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can inspire individuals to approach their work with a mindset of positivity and virtue, recognizing that good thoughts and actions can lead to success and fulfillment.
    • In educational environments, the mantra can serve as a reminder for students to seek knowledge (the light of the sun) and to value the wisdom gained through learning, especially when faced with challenging subjects.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking enlightenment and guidance in the face of life's challenges.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that with good thoughts and divine support, one can navigate through difficulties and emerge with wisdom and strength.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to cultivate positivity, seek knowledge, and rely on both inner strength and external support to overcome obstacles.
