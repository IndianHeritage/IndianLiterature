अस्य सुवानस्य मन्दिनस् तृतस्य न्य अर्बुदं वावृद्धानो अस्तः |अवर्तयत सूर्यो न चक्रं भिनद वलम् इन्द्रो अंगिरस्वान् ||
asya suvānasya mandinas tritasya ny arbudaṁ vāvṛdhāno astaḥ | avartayat sūryo na cakram bhinad valam indro aṅgirasvān ||
  • Mantra: "asya suvānasya mandinas tritasya ny arbudaṁ vāvṛdhāno astaḥ | avartayat sūryo na cakram bhinad valam indro aṅgirasvān ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asya: of this
    • suvānasya: of the good and auspicious
    • mandinas: of the wise or intelligent
    • tritasya: of the third (often refers to a divine or celestial being)
    • ny: not
    • arbudaṁ: a multitude or a great number
    • vāvṛdhāno: having increased or expanded
    • astaḥ: has set or has gone down
    • avartayat: turned or revolved
    • sūryo: the sun
    • na: not
    • cakram: the wheel or cycle
    • bhinad: breaking or splitting
    • valam: strength or power
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • aṅgirasvān: associated with the Angiras, a group of sages or seers
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the auspiciousness and wisdom associated with divine beings.
    • The phrase "asya suvānasya mandinas" emphasizes the importance of good and wise actions in life.
    • The reference to "tritasya" suggests a connection to higher realms or divine guidance.
    • The term "arbudaṁ" indicates the vastness of the universe and the multitude of experiences one can encounter.
    • "Vāvṛdhāno astaḥ" implies that even as the sun sets, there is a continuous cycle of renewal and growth.
    • The sun, "sūryo," symbolizes enlightenment and knowledge, which should not be obstructed by ignorance ("na cakram bhinad").
    • Indra, as a powerful deity, represents strength and the ability to overcome obstacles, while "aṅgirasvān" connects to the wisdom of ancient sages.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek wisdom and act with good intentions, much like a leader who guides their team with integrity.
    • In a community setting, the mantra encourages collective growth and support, akin to how a group of friends uplift each other during challenging times.
    • In personal development, it serves as a reminder to embrace change and renewal, similar to how one might approach a new job or life phase with optimism.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom, auspiciousness, and the cyclical nature of life.
    • It teaches us to embrace knowledge and strength while navigating the challenges of life.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to personal growth, community support, and leadership, reminding us that wisdom and good intentions lead to a prosperous and harmonious existence.
