युक्ष्वा हि वाजिनीवत् अश्वाम् अद्यारुणाम् उषः | अथ नो विश्वा सौभगान्य आवह ||
yukṣvā hi vājinīvaty aśvām̐ adyāruṇām̐ uṣaḥ | athā no viśvā saubhagāny ā vaha ||
  • yukṣvā hi vājinīvaty aśvām̐: This phrase can be translated as "May the horses, like chariots, be swift." Here, "yukṣvā" refers to the act of harnessing or yoking, while "vājinīvaty" suggests the swiftness and strength of horses. In real life, this can be likened to the importance of teamwork and collaboration, where individuals come together to achieve a common goal efficiently.
  • adyāruṇām̐ uṣaḥ: This translates to "the dawn that is red today." The dawn symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the promise of a new day. In everyday life, this can represent opportunities that arise each day, encouraging individuals to embrace change and start afresh.
  • athā no viśvā saubhagāny ā vaha: This means "May all auspicious things come to us." The word "viśvā" signifies universality, while "saubhagāny" refers to good fortune or auspiciousness. This part of the mantra emphasizes the desire for overall well-being and prosperity in life. In practical terms, it can be seen as a call for positivity and abundance in various aspects of life, such as health, relationships, and career.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, new beginnings, and overall prosperity. It reflects the interconnectedness of individuals working together towards a common goal while also embracing the opportunities that each new day brings.
  • Real-life Application: In a corporate setting, this mantra can inspire teams to collaborate effectively, harnessing their collective strengths to achieve success. In personal life, it encourages individuals to remain optimistic and open to new possibilities, fostering a mindset that attracts good fortune and well-being.
