मन्त्र: इन्दुर हियानः सोत्रिभिर मृज्यमानः कनिक्रदत् | इयर्ति वाग्नुम इन्द्रियम् ||
indur hiyānaḥ sotṛbhir mṛjyamānaḥ kanikradat | iyarti vagnum indriyam ||
  • Indur: This word translates to "the moon" in English. The moon is often associated with calmness, reflection, and the passage of time.
  • Hiyānaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "having come down" or "having descended." It signifies a connection between the celestial and earthly realms.
  • Sotṛbhir: This word means "with the streams" or "with the waters." It suggests a relationship with nature, particularly water, which is essential for life.
  • Mṛjyamānaḥ: This translates to "being purified" or "being cleansed." It indicates a process of renewal or transformation.
  • Kanikradat: This term can be understood as "having made a sound" or "having cried out." It implies communication or expression, possibly of emotions or thoughts.
  • Iyarti: This word means "to lead" or "to guide." It suggests a sense of direction or purpose.
  • Vagnum: This translates to "the speech" or "the word." It emphasizes the power of language and communication.
  • Indriyam: This term refers to "the senses" or "the faculties." It highlights the importance of perception and awareness in our experiences.

  • Meaning: The mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of nature, the process of purification, and the significance of communication and awareness. It reflects the cyclical nature of life, where the moon symbolizes change and renewal, while water represents sustenance and life.
  • Real-life Example: Consider a person going through a difficult phase in life. Just as the moon waxes and wanes, they may experience ups and downs. Engaging with nature, such as spending time near a river or lake, can provide a sense of peace and clarity. The act of expressing feelings, whether through conversation or writing, can lead to personal growth and healing.

  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of nature, communication, and self-awareness in our lives. It teaches us that just as the moon influences the tides, our emotions and experiences shape our journey. By embracing the cycles of life and engaging with our surroundings, we can find clarity and purpose. This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal development, emotional healing, and fostering connections with others.
