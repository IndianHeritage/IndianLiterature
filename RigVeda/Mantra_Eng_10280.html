दीर्घं ह्य अङ्कुशं यथा शक्तिम बिभर्षि मन्तुमः |पूर्वेण मगवन् पदाजो वयां यथा यमो देवी जनित्री अजीजनद भद्रा जनित्री अजीजनत ||
dīrghaṁ hy aṅkuśaṁ yathā śaktim bibharṣi mantumaḥ | pūrveṇa maghavan padājo vayāṁ yathā yamo devī janitry ajījanad bhadrā janitry ajījanat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dīrghaṁ: long, enduring
    • hy: indeed
    • aṅkuśaṁ: a goad or a tool for guidance
    • yathā: as
    • śaktim: power or strength
    • bibharṣi: you bear or carry
    • mantumaḥ: the wise or the thoughtful
    • pūrveṇa: by the ancients or predecessors
    • maghavan: the bountiful or the generous
    • padājo: born of the path or the way
    • vayāṁ: our strength or vitality
    • yathā: as
    • yamo: the god of death or restraint
    • devī: goddess
    • janitry: the mother or creator
    • ajījanad: has given birth or produced
    • bhadrā: auspicious or beneficial
    • janitry: the mother or creator
    • ajījanat: has given birth or produced
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the enduring strength and guidance that one can derive from the wisdom of the ancients.
    • The reference to "aṅkuśaṁ" symbolizes the tools we use to navigate life's challenges, much like a goad helps guide an animal.
    • The mention of "maghavan" emphasizes the importance of generosity and abundance in our lives, suggesting that we should strive to be bountiful in our actions and thoughts.
    • The invocation of "yamo devī" highlights the duality of life and death, urging us to respect the cycle of existence and the role of the divine feminine in creation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, one might use the wisdom of mentors (the "pūrveṇa") to guide their career decisions, much like the "aṅkuśaṁ" helps steer a path.
    • In personal relationships, being generous and nurturing (as suggested by "maghavan" and "bhadrā") can lead to stronger bonds and a more fulfilling life.
    • Understanding the balance of life and death can help individuals cope with loss, reminding them of the cyclical nature of existence.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, guidance, and the nurturing aspects of life.
    • It encourages individuals to seek strength from the past while being generous and mindful in their present actions.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to a more harmonious existence, fostering both personal growth and community well-being.
