मन्त्र: असृग्न देववीतये 'त्यासः कृत्व्या इव | क्षरन्तः पर्वतावृद्धः ||
asṛgran devavītaye 'tyāsaḥ kṛtvyā iva | kṣarantaḥ parvatāvṛdhaḥ ||
  • asṛgran: This term can be interpreted as "those who have gathered" or "those who have assembled." It signifies the coming together of forces or entities for a common purpose.
  • devavītaye: This word refers to "for the sake of the divine" or "in the service of the gods." It emphasizes the importance of aligning actions with divine will or higher principles.
  • 'tyāsaḥ: This can be understood as "those who are engaged" or "those who are involved." It suggests active participation in a cause or mission.
  • kṛtvyā: This term means "with duty" or "with responsibility." It highlights the importance of fulfilling one's obligations and duties in life.
  • iva: This word translates to "like" or "as if," indicating a comparison or similarity.
  • kṣarantaḥ: This can be interpreted as "those who are perishing" or "those who are transient." It reflects the impermanence of life and the inevitability of change.
  • parvatāvṛdhaḥ: This term refers to "the mountains that are growing" or "the rising mountains." It symbolizes strength, stability, and the enduring nature of certain aspects of existence.

The mantra can be understood as a call to gather together for a divine purpose, emphasizing the importance of duty and active participation in the face of life's transience. It suggests that while life is fleeting, there are enduring values and principles, represented by the mountains, that we should strive to uphold.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Community Service: When individuals come together to serve a community, they embody the spirit of "asṛgran" and "devavītaye," working for a higher cause.
  • Teamwork in Organizations: In a workplace, employees collaborating on a project reflect the essence of "kṛtvyā" and "tyāsaḥ," fulfilling their responsibilities while contributing to a common goal.
  • Environmental Conservation: Groups working to protect nature can be seen as "kṣarantaḥ parvatāvṛdhaḥ," recognizing the impermanence of ecosystems while striving to preserve and enhance the natural world.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of unity, duty, and the pursuit of higher ideals in a world marked by change and impermanence. It encourages individuals to come together for a greater purpose, fulfilling their responsibilities while acknowledging the enduring values that guide their actions.
