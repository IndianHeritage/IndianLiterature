अस्य तृतः क्रतुनā वव्रे अन्तर इच्छन् धीतिं पितुर एवैः परस्य |सचस्यमानः पित्रोः उपस्थे जामि ब्रुवाण आयुधानि वेति ||
asya tritaḥ kratunā vavre antar icchan dhītim pitur evaiḥ parasya | sacasyamānaḥ pitror upasthe jāmi bruvāṇa āyudhāni veti ||
  • Mantra: "asya tritaḥ kratunā vavre antar icchan dhītim pitur evaiḥ parasya | sacasyamānaḥ pitror upasthe jāmi bruvāṇa āyudhāni veti ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asya: of this
    • tritaḥ: third
    • kratunā: by the power of the sacrifice
    • vavre: I choose
    • antar: within
    • icchan: desiring
    • dhītim: wisdom or understanding
    • pitur: of the father
    • evaiḥ: indeed
    • parasya: of another
    • sacasyamānaḥ: being endowed with strength
    • pitror: of the fathers
    • upasthe: in the presence
    • jāmi: I know
    • bruvāṇa: speaking
    • āyudhāni: weapons
    • veti: he knows
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of choice and the wisdom that comes from the sacrifices made by our ancestors.
    • The phrase "asya tritaḥ kratunā vavre" indicates a conscious decision made with the strength derived from previous sacrifices.
    • It emphasizes the importance of understanding and wisdom ("dhītim") that one seeks from the paternal lineage ("pitur").
    • The mention of "sacasyamānaḥ" suggests that one is endowed with strength, which can be interpreted as the inner strength gained from the knowledge and sacrifices of forefathers.
    • In a real-life context, this can be related to how individuals often draw strength and wisdom from their family history and traditions, using that knowledge to navigate their own lives.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the connection between past sacrifices and present wisdom, suggesting that our choices are influenced by the legacies of those who came before us.
    • It serves as a reminder to honor our ancestors and recognize the strength we inherit from them, which can guide us in making informed decisions.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various scenarios such as family gatherings, cultural celebrations, or personal reflections where one acknowledges their roots and the wisdom passed down through generations.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and strength from their heritage while making choices that shape their future.
