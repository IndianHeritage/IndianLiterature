अग्नि तृतीये सवने हि काणिषः पुरोळाशं सहसः सूनव आहुतम् |अथा देवेष्व अध्वरं विपण्यया धा रत्नवंतम् अमृतेषु जागृविम् ||
agne tṛtīye savane hi kāniṣaḥ puroḻāśaṁ sahasaḥ sūnav āhutam | athā deveṣv adhvaraṁ vipanyayā dhā ratnavantam amṛteṣu jāgṛvim ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • Tṛtīye: In the third (savana or offering)
    • Savane: In the offering
    • Hi: Indeed
    • Kāniṣaḥ: The best or the most excellent
    • Puroḻāśaṁ: The one who is offered (the oblation)
    • Sahasaḥ: With great speed or force
    • Sūnav: The sons or descendants
    • Āhutam: Invoked or called upon
    • Athā: Then
    • Deveṣv: Among the gods
    • Adhvaraṁ: The ritual or sacrifice
    • Vipanyayā: With great care or attention
    • Dhā: May we hold or maintain
    • Ratnavantam: The one who is rich in treasures
    • Amṛteṣu: In the immortal or eternal
    • Jāgṛvim: May we awaken or be vigilant
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The invocation of Agni in the third offering signifies the importance of fire in rituals, symbolizing purity and transformation.
    • The phrase "the best or the most excellent" emphasizes the quality of offerings made to the divine, suggesting that one should strive for excellence in all endeavors.
    • "With great speed or force" indicates the urgency and importance of the offerings, reflecting the need for dedication and commitment in spiritual practices.
    • The mention of "sons or descendants" highlights the continuity of tradition and the role of family in spiritual practices.
    • The latter part of the mantra speaks to the importance of performing rituals with care and attention, ensuring that the offerings are made with sincerity and devotion.
    • "Rich in treasures" symbolizes the abundance and blessings that come from a life lived in accordance with spiritual principles.
    • Finally, "may we awaken or be vigilant" serves as a reminder to remain aware and conscious of our spiritual journey and responsibilities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of dedication, excellence, and mindfulness in spiritual practices.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to approach their goals and responsibilities with the same level of commitment and care as one would in a sacred ritual.
    • It serves as a reminder to maintain a connection with one's heritage and to pass down values and traditions to future generations.
    • Ultimately, the mantra teaches that through sincere efforts and vigilance, one can attain spiritual richness and fulfillment in life.
