उक्ष्णो हि मे पञ्चदश साकं पचन्ति विंशतिम् |उताहम् अद्मि पीव इद उभा कुक्शी पृणन्ति मे विश्वस्माद इन्द्र उत्तरः ||
ukṣṇo hi me pañcadaśa sākam pacanti viṁśatim | utāham admi pīva id ubhā kukṣī pṛṇanti me viśvasmād indra uttaraḥ ||
  • ukṣṇo hi me pañcadaśa sākam pacanti viṁśatim: This line can be translated to "Fifteen (sacred) ones are cooking together, and they prepare twenty." Here, "ukṣṇo" refers to the act of cooking or preparing, while "pañcadaśa" signifies the number fifteen, and "viṁśatim" indicates twenty. This can be interpreted as a metaphor for collaboration and teamwork, where multiple entities come together to create something greater than the sum of their parts.
  • utāham admi pīva id ubhā kukṣī pṛṇanti me: This translates to "I drink the essence, and both wombs nourish me." The term "admi" refers to the act of drinking or consuming, while "pīva" signifies essence or nourishment. The "ubhā kukṣī" can be understood as two sources of nourishment or support. This line emphasizes the importance of sustenance, both physical and spiritual, and the interconnectedness of life.
  • viśvasmād indra uttaraḥ: This means "From all, Indra is the highest." "Indra" is often regarded as a powerful deity in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and leadership. The phrase suggests that from all sources of nourishment and support, there is a supreme force or guiding principle that stands out, representing the ultimate goal or aspiration in life.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of collaboration, nourishment, and the pursuit of higher ideals. It highlights the importance of working together to achieve common goals, drawing strength from various sources, and recognizing the ultimate guiding principles in our lives.
  • Real-life Application: In modern contexts, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:
    • Teamwork in Organizations: Just as the mantra speaks of multiple entities working together, in a corporate environment, diverse teams collaborate to innovate and solve problems, leading to greater success.
    • Community Support: The idea of nourishment from two wombs can be likened to the support systems in communities where individuals uplift each other, sharing resources and knowledge.
    • Personal Growth: On a personal level, recognizing the various influences in our lives that contribute to our growth—be it family, education, or experiences—aligns with the mantra's message of drawing from multiple sources for holistic development.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity, the importance of nourishment in various forms, and the pursuit of higher ideals. By embracing collaboration and recognizing the guiding forces in our lives, we can navigate challenges more effectively and strive for personal and collective growth.
