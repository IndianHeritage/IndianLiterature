मन्त्र: विद्मा हि रुद्रियाणां शुष्मं उग्रं मरुतां शिमीवताम् |विष्णोर एषस्य मीळ्हुषाम् ||
vidmā hi rudriyāṇāṁ śuṣmam ugram marutāṁ śimīvatām | viṣṇor eṣasya mīḻhuṣām ||
  • vidmā: This word translates to "we know" or "we are aware." It signifies knowledge and understanding.
  • hi: This is an emphatic particle, often used to emphasize the preceding word, in this case, "vidmā."
  • rudriyāṇāṁ: This refers to the "Rudras," who are often associated with storm and wind, representing the fierce and powerful aspects of nature.
  • śuṣmam: This means "dry" or "desiccated," indicating a state of barrenness or lack of vitality.
  • ugram: This translates to "fierce" or "terrible," suggesting a powerful and potentially destructive force.
  • marutāṁ: This refers to the "Maruts," the storm deities, who are known for their strength and are often depicted as a group of fierce winds.
  • śimīvatām: This word can be interpreted as "like the shade" or "like the protective cover," suggesting a sense of shelter or safety.
  • viṣṇor: This refers to "Vishnu," the preserver and protector in Hindu mythology, symbolizing stability and sustenance.
  • eṣasya: This means "of this" or "of that," referring to the subject being discussed.
  • mīḻhuṣām: This word can be understood as "of the gentle" or "of the soft," indicating a contrast to the fierceness mentioned earlier.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for understanding the fierce and powerful aspects of nature, represented by the Rudras and Maruts, while also seeking the protective and gentle qualities of Vishnu. It highlights the duality of existence—where destruction and preservation coexist.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios. For instance, during natural disasters like storms or floods, we can recognize the fierce power of nature (Rudras and Maruts) and seek shelter and protection (Vishnu) to ensure safety. It also serves as a reminder of the balance we must maintain in our lives, acknowledging both the challenges we face and the support systems we have in place.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its representation of the dual forces of nature and life. It teaches us to respect the power of the elements while also seeking the protective and nurturing aspects that help us navigate through challenges. This understanding is applicable in our daily lives, encouraging us to find balance and harmony amidst chaos.
