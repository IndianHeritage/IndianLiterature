आ जुहोतā दुवस्यताग्निम प्रयत्य अध्वरे |वृणीध्वं हव्यवाहनम् ||
ā juhotā duvasyatāgnim prayaty adhvare | vṛṇīdhvaṁ havyavāhanam ||
  • Mantra: "ā juhotā duvasyatāgnim prayaty adhvare | vṛṇīdhvaṁ havyavāhanam ||"
  • Translation: "He who offers the oblation to the two-fold fire, striving in the sacrifice, may you choose the one who carries the offerings."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ā juhotā: "He who offers" - This refers to the act of making offerings, which is a central theme in Vedic rituals.
    • duvasyatāgnim: "the two-fold fire" - This symbolizes the dual nature of existence, often interpreted as the physical and spiritual realms.
    • prayaty: "striving" - This indicates effort and dedication in performing rituals or sacrifices.
    • adhvare: "in the sacrifice" - Refers to the sacred act of offering, which is a key component of Vedic traditions.
    • vṛṇīdhvaṁ: "may you choose" - A plea for divine selection or guidance.
    • havyavāhanam: "the one who carries the offerings" - This refers to the deities or forces that accept and benefit from the offerings made.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of making offerings can be seen in various forms, such as charitable donations or acts of kindness, where individuals strive to contribute positively to society.
    • The dual nature of fire can be likened to the balance between material pursuits and spiritual growth, reminding us to nurture both aspects in our lives.
    • Striving in sacrifice can be related to personal goals, where individuals put in effort and dedication to achieve their aspirations, whether in career, education, or personal development.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of intention and effort in our actions, particularly in spiritual or communal contexts.
    • It serves as a reminder that our contributions, whether material or spiritual, should be made with sincerity and dedication.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to seek guidance and support from higher powers or the community in their endeavors, fostering a sense of interconnectedness.
