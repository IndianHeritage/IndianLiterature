तुभ्यं धेनुः सबरदुग्धा विश्वा वसूनी दोहते |अजनयो मरुतो वक्षाणाभ्यो दिव आ वक्षाणाभ्यः ||
tubhyaṁ dhenuḥ sabardughā viśvā vasūni dohate | ajanayo maruto vakṣaṇābhyo diva ā vakṣaṇābhyaḥ ||
  • tubhyaṁ - This word translates to "to you" or "for you," indicating a sense of devotion or offering.
  • dhenuḥ - Refers to a "cow," which in Vedic culture symbolizes wealth, nourishment, and abundance.
  • sabardughā - This term can be interpreted as "the one who gives milk," emphasizing the nurturing aspect of the cow.
  • viśvā - Means "all" or "universal," suggesting inclusivity and the idea that the blessings are for everyone.
  • vasūni - Translates to "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating the material and spiritual abundance one seeks.
  • dohate - Means "to provide" or "to yield," reinforcing the idea of receiving blessings and nourishment.
  • ajanayo - This word means "born" or "produced," often referring to the divine or celestial beings.
  • maruto - Refers to the "Maruts," the storm deities associated with strength and vitality.
  • vakṣaṇābhyo - This term can be understood as "from the chest" or "from the heart," indicating a source of strength and power.
  • diva - Means "heaven" or "sky," representing the divine realm.
  • ā - A preposition meaning "from" or "out of," indicating a source or origin.
  • vakṣaṇābhyaḥ - Similar to the previous term, it emphasizes the source of strength and blessings.

The mantra essentially invokes the divine forces to provide nourishment and prosperity, symbolized by the cow, which is a sacred animal in Vedic culture. It highlights the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of divine blessings in achieving abundance.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Agriculture: Farmers can chant this mantra to seek blessings for a bountiful harvest, as the cow represents fertility and abundance.
  • Business: Entrepreneurs may use this mantra to attract prosperity and success in their ventures, invoking the universal wealth.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra to cultivate a mindset of abundance and gratitude, fostering a positive outlook on life.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with the divine source of nourishment and prosperity. By invoking these blessings, one can enhance their life experiences, promote well-being, and foster a sense of community and shared abundance. It serves as a reminder of the importance of gratitude and the interconnectedness of all beings in the pursuit of wealth and nourishment.
