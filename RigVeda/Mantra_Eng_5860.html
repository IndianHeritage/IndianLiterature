नियुवाना नियुतः स्पार्हवीरा इन्द्रवायू सरथं यातम अर्वाक् |इदं हि वाम प्रभरतम मध्वो अग्रं अध प्रीणानां विं मुमुक्तम अस्मे ||
niyuvānā niyutaḥ spārhavīrā indravāyū sarathaṁ yātam arvāk | idaṁ hi vām prabhṛtam madhvo agram adha prīṇānā vi mumuktam asme ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • niyuvānā: One who is directed or guided.
    • niyutaḥ: Those who are well-ordered or disciplined.
    • spārhavīrā: The brave or valiant ones.
    • indravāyū: The wind of Indra, symbolizing strength and power.
    • sarathaṁ: With chariots or vehicles, indicating readiness for action.
    • yātam: To go or to move forward.
    • arvāk: Towards the front or forward.
    • idaṁ: This or here.
    • hi: Indeed or surely.
    • vām: Your or of you.
    • prabhṛtam: That which is spread out or offered.
    • madhvo: Of sweetness or pleasantness.
    • agram: The foremost or the best.
    • adha: Below or beneath.
    • prīṇānā: Those who are pleased or satisfied.
    • vi: In this context, it can mean 'with' or 'together'.
    • mumuktam: Liberated or freed.
    • asme: To us or for us.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of guidance and direction, emphasizing the importance of being well-ordered and disciplined in one's actions.
    • The reference to "brave ones" suggests that courage is essential when moving forward in life.
    • The mention of "Indra's wind" symbolizes the power and strength that can propel individuals towards their goals.
    • It encourages readiness for action, suggesting that one should be prepared to move forward with determination.
    • The phrase "this is indeed yours" indicates a personal connection to the blessings or opportunities that are being offered.
    • It highlights the importance of sweetness and pleasantness in life, suggesting that one should seek joy and satisfaction in their endeavors.
    • The mantra concludes with a call for liberation and fulfillment, indicating that through discipline, courage, and readiness, one can achieve freedom and satisfaction.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a team that is well-organized and disciplined is more likely to succeed in achieving its goals, much like the "niyuvānā niyutaḥ" aspect of the mantra.
    • Individuals facing challenges in their personal lives can draw strength from the "spārhavīrā" aspect, finding courage to overcome obstacles.
    • In sports, athletes often rely on their "indravāyū" or inner strength to push through difficult moments, embodying the spirit of this mantra.
    • In education, students who are prepared and disciplined in their studies are more likely to excel, reflecting the readiness for action mentioned in the mantra.
    • In relationships, seeking sweetness and pleasantness can lead to more fulfilling connections, aligning with the mantra's emphasis on joy.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of guidance, discipline, and courage in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to be prepared for action and to seek joy in their endeavors.
    • Ultimately, it signifies that through these qualities, one can achieve liberation and fulfillment, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to professional success.
