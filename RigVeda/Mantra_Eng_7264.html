सखायः क्रतुम इच्छत कथā राधाम शरस्य |उपस्तुतिम भोजः सूरीर यो अह्रयः ||
sakhāyaḥ kratum icchata kathā rādhāma śarasya | upastutim bhojaḥ sūrir yo ahrayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sakhāyaḥ: friends or companions
    • kratum: intention or resolve
    • icchata: desiring or wishing
    • kathā: how or in what manner
    • rādhāma: to be praised or honored
    • śarasya: of the bow or weapon
    • upastutim: worship or praise
    • bhojaḥ: the one who enjoys or partakes
    • sūrir: the wise or learned
    • yo: who
    • ahrayaḥ: brings or carries
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of companionship and collective intention.
    • It emphasizes the idea that friends or companions should come together with a shared resolve.
    • The phrase "how to be praised" suggests that the actions and intentions of individuals should lead to honor and respect.
    • The reference to "the bow" symbolizes strength and readiness to face challenges together.
    • Worship and praise are highlighted as essential elements in relationships, indicating that mutual respect and acknowledgment are vital.
    • The wise individuals who partake in this collective effort are seen as the ones who carry forward the intentions and actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, teamwork is crucial. When colleagues come together with a shared goal, they can achieve greater success, much like the companions in the mantra.
    • In community service, individuals who unite for a common cause, such as environmental conservation, embody the essence of this mantra by working together with intention and purpose.
    • In personal relationships, friends who support each other in times of need demonstrate the importance of companionship and shared resolve, leading to mutual respect and honor.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the power of collective intention and the importance of companionship in achieving goals.
    • It teaches us that through mutual respect, praise, and shared efforts, we can overcome challenges and honor one another.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger relationships, successful teamwork, and a more harmonious community.
