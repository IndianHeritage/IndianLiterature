यत्रानन्दाश्च मोदाश्च मुदः प्रमुद आसते |कामस्य यत्राप्ताः कामास तत्र मां अमृतं कृधीन्रायेंदो परि स्रव ||
yatrānandāś ca modāś ca mudaḥ pramuda āsate | kāmasya yatrāptāḥ kāmās tatra mām amṛtaṁ kṛdhīndrāyendo pari srava ||
  • Mantra: "yatrānandāś ca modāś ca mudaḥ pramuda āsate |
  • Translation: "Where there is bliss, joy, and delight, there resides the supreme joy."
  • Explanation:
    • Anandāś: Refers to bliss or ultimate happiness. In real life, this can be seen in moments of deep fulfillment, such as the joy of achieving a long-term goal or the happiness felt during a family gathering.
    • Modāś: Represents joy or pleasure. This can be likened to the joy experienced during celebrations, such as weddings or festivals, where people come together to share happiness.
    • Mudaḥ: Indicates delight. An example of this could be the delight a child feels when playing with friends or receiving a gift.
    • Pramuda: Signifies supreme joy or ecstasy. This can be compared to the overwhelming joy one feels during significant life events, such as the birth of a child or achieving a lifelong dream.
  • Continuation: "kāmasya yatrāptāḥ kāmās tatra mām amṛtaṁ kṛdhīndrāyendo pari srava ||"
  • Translation: "Where desires are fulfilled, there I am the immortal essence, flowing like the moon."
  • Explanation:
    • Kāmasya: Refers to desires or wishes. In everyday life, this can be seen in the pursuit of personal goals, such as career aspirations or personal relationships.
    • Yatrāptāḥ: Means where they are fulfilled. This can be illustrated by achieving a desired outcome, such as landing a dream job or finding true love.
    • Kāmās: Indicates the fulfillment of desires. This can be related to the satisfaction one feels after achieving something they have longed for.
    • Amṛtaṁ: Represents immortality or eternal essence. This can be understood as the lasting impact of our actions and relationships, which continue to resonate even after we are gone.
    • Kṛdhīndrāyendo: Refers to the divine or celestial essence, akin to the moon's light that illuminates the night. This can symbolize guidance and inspiration in our lives.
    • Pari srava: Means to flow or radiate. This can be compared to the way positive energy and love flow from one person to another, creating a sense of community and connection.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of joy, fulfillment, and the divine essence that resides in moments of happiness and satisfaction.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek out and cherish experiences that bring us bliss and joy, as these moments contribute to our overall well-being.
    • It encourages us to recognize the divine presence in our lives, especially when our desires are fulfilled, and to share that joy with others.
    • Ultimately, this mantra teaches us that true happiness is found not just in the fulfillment of desires but in the connections we create and the joy we share with others.
