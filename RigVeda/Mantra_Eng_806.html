साधुर न गृध्नुर अस्तेव शूरो यातेव भीमस् त्वेषः समत्सु ||
sādhur na gṛdhnur asteva śūro yāteva bhīmas tveṣaḥ samatsu ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • sādhur: The term refers to a person who is virtuous, righteous, or good.
    • na: This is a negation, meaning "not."
    • gṛdhnur: This word means "greedy" or "covetous."
    • asteva: This translates to "exists" or "is present."
    • śūro: This means "brave" or "heroic."
    • yāteva: This can be interpreted as "like" or "as if."
    • bhīmas: This refers to someone who is formidable or powerful.
    • tveṣaḥ: This means "your" or "of you."
    • samatsu: This translates to "in battles" or "in conflicts."
  • Meaning:
    • The mantra emphasizes the qualities of a virtuous person who is not greedy.
    • It highlights the importance of bravery and strength in facing challenges.
    • It suggests that true heroism is not just about physical strength but also about moral integrity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A leader who prioritizes the welfare of their community over personal gain exemplifies the virtue of "sādhur."
    • In a corporate setting, an employee who works hard and collaborates with others without being greedy for promotions or recognition embodies the essence of this mantra.
    • In sports, an athlete who plays fair and encourages teammates, rather than focusing solely on personal glory, reflects the qualities of bravery and righteousness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of virtue and integrity in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to be brave and strong in the face of adversity while maintaining moral values.
    • In today's world, where greed and selfishness can often overshadow righteousness, this mantra is a call to action to embody these noble qualities in our personal and professional lives.
