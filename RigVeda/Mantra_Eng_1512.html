हुवे यत् त्वा मयोभुवं देवं सख्याय मार्त्यः |अस्माकं आङ्गूषान द्युम्निनस कृधि वाजेषु द्युम्निनस कृधि ||
huve yat tvā mayobhuvaṁ devaṁ sakhyāya martyaḥ | asmākam āṅgūṣān dyumninas kṛdhi vājeṣu dyumninas kṛdhi ||
  • Huve yat tvā mayobhuvaṁ devaṁ sakhyāya martyaḥ:
    • Huve: This is an invocation, a call to the divine. It signifies the act of addressing a deity or higher power.
    • yat: Meaning "that" or "which," it refers to the qualities or attributes of the divine being invoked.
    • tvā: This means "you," indicating a direct address to the deity.
    • mayobhuvaṁ: This can be interpreted as "I have become," suggesting a personal connection or transformation through the divine.
    • devaṁ: This translates to "god" or "deity," emphasizing the divine nature of the being being addressed.
    • sakhyāya: Meaning "for friendship" or "to befriend," it indicates a desire for a close relationship with the divine.
    • martyaḥ: This refers to "mortals" or "human beings," highlighting the connection between the divine and humanity.
  • asmākam āṅgūṣān dyumninas kṛdhi:
    • asmākam: Meaning "our," it signifies a collective identity or community.
    • āṅgūṣān: This can be interpreted as "with our fingers" or "with our hands," symbolizing action and effort.
    • dyumninas: This translates to "of glory" or "of brilliance," indicating a desire for success and recognition.
    • kṛdhi: Meaning "do" or "create," it is a call to action, urging the divine to assist in achieving glory.
  • vājeṣu dyumninas kṛdhi:
    • vājeṣu: This refers to "in the victories" or "in the achievements," emphasizing the context of success.
    • dyumninas: Again, this signifies "of glory," reinforcing the theme of seeking recognition and success.
    • kṛdhi: Reiterating the call to action, it emphasizes the need for divine assistance in achieving these victories.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking a divine connection for personal and communal success. It emphasizes the importance of friendship with the divine, urging mortals to engage actively in their pursuits while seeking divine blessings. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal endeavors, individuals may recite this mantra to invoke strength and support in their goals, whether in education, career, or personal growth.
  • In community settings, groups may use this mantra during rituals or gatherings to foster unity and collective success, reinforcing the idea that divine support is sought for the greater good.
  • In moments of challenge, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek help from a higher power, encouraging individuals to take action while remaining open to divine guidance.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for a harmonious relationship between humanity and the divine, emphasizing that through collaboration and effort, one can achieve glory and success in life.
