स ते जानाति सुमतिं यविष्ट य ईवते ब्रह्मणे गातुम ऐरत् |विश्वान्य अस्मै सुदिनानि रायो द्युम्नानि आर्यो वि दुरो अभि द्यौत् ||
sa te jānāti sumatiṁ yaviṣṭha ya īvate brahmaṇe gātum airat | viśvāny asmai sudināni rāyo dyumnāny aryo vi duro abhi dyaut ||
  • sa te jānāti: This phrase translates to "he knows you." It emphasizes the idea of a divine or higher power that is aware of our actions and intentions.
  • sumatiṁ yaviṣṭha: "The one who has good understanding or wisdom." This suggests that wisdom is a valuable trait that leads to better decision-making and a more fulfilling life.
  • ya īvate brahmaṇe gātum airat: This can be interpreted as "who is devoted to the ultimate reality or the divine." It highlights the importance of devotion and connection to a higher purpose.
  • viśvāny asmai sudināni rāyo: "All good fortunes and wealth." This part signifies the blessings and prosperity that come to those who are wise and devoted.
  • dyumnāny aryo vi duro abhi dyaut: "May the light of the divine shine upon him." This phrase calls for divine guidance and illumination in one's life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom, devotion, and the awareness of a higher power in our lives. It suggests that by cultivating good understanding and connecting with the divine, we can attract prosperity and blessings. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways, such as making thoughtful decisions in our careers, nurturing relationships with empathy and understanding, and seeking spiritual growth through practices like meditation or prayer. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that our actions and intentions are known to a higher power, encouraging us to live with integrity and purpose.
