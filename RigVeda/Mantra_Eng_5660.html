नू मित्रो वरुणो आर्यमा नस् त्मने तोकाय वरिवो दधन्तु |सुगा नो विश्वा सुपथानि सन्तु यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
nū mitro varuṇo aryamā nas tmane tokāya varivo dadhantu | sugā no viśvā supathāni santu yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • nū mitro varuṇo aryamā: This phrase invokes the deities Mitra, Varuna, and Aryama.
    • Mitra: Represents friendship and harmony, often associated with the sun and light.
    • Varuna: Symbolizes cosmic order and moral authority, governing the waters and the celestial realm.
    • Aryama: Associated with the ancestors and the concept of righteousness.
  • nas tmane tokāya varivo dadhantu: This line expresses a desire for these deities to grant us strength and support.
    • tmane: Refers to strength or power.
    • tokāya: Implies protection or safeguarding.
    • varivo dadhantu: Means to bestow or grant us abundance and prosperity.
  • sugā no viśvā supathāni santu: This part seeks the blessings of the deities for good paths and auspiciousness.
    • sugā: Refers to goodness or auspiciousness.
    • viśvā: Means all or universal.
    • supathāni: Refers to good paths or directions in life.
  • yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: This line is a plea for the deities to protect us always with their blessings.
    • yūyam: Refers to the deities being addressed.
    • pāta: Means to protect or guard.
    • svastibhiḥ: Refers to auspiciousness or well-being.
    • sadā naḥ: Means always for us.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of divine support and guidance in our lives. It highlights the need for friendship, moral order, and protection from the deities. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to our daily prayers for strength, guidance in our decisions, and the pursuit of a righteous path. It serves as a reminder to seek harmony in our relationships and to strive for a life filled with auspiciousness and well-being. By invoking these divine qualities, we can foster a sense of community and support, ensuring that we are not alone in our endeavors.
