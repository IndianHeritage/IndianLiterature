भुवो यज्ञस्य राजसश्च नेताऽ यत्रा नियुद्भिः सच्चसे शिवाभिः |दिवि मूर्धानं दधिषे स्वर्षां जिव्वाम अग्ने चक्रिषे हव्यवाहम् ||
bhuvo yajñasya rajasaś ca netā yatrā niyudbhiḥ sacase śivābhiḥ | divi mūrdhānaṁ dadhiṣe svarṣāṁ jihvām agne cakṛṣe havyavāham ||
  • Mantra: "bhuvo yajñasya rajasaś ca netā yatrā niyudbhiḥ sacase śivābhiḥ | divi mūrdhānaṁ dadhiṣe svarṣāṁ jihvām agne cakṛṣe havyavāham ||"
  • Translation: "O Agni, you are the leader of the sacrifices and the one who brings forth the essence of the universe. You are the one who is invoked with sacred hymns, and you carry the offerings to the divine realms. You are the one who resides in the highest heavens, and you are the one who brings forth the essence of the offerings."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • bhuvo: of the earth or the universe
    • yajñasya: of the sacrifice
    • rajasaś: of the leaders or rulers
    • ca: and
    • netā: the leader
    • yatrā: where
    • niyudbhiḥ: with sacred hymns
    • sacase: with essence
    • śivābhiḥ: with auspiciousness
    • divi: in the heavens
    • mūrdhānaṁ: the highest
    • dadhiṣe: you bring forth
    • svarṣāṁ: the essence
    • jihvām: the tongue
    • agne: O Agni (the fire)
    • cakṛṣe: you create
    • havyavāham: the one who carries offerings
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is seen as a purifying element. For instance, in Hindu rituals, Agni is invoked to carry offerings to the gods, symbolizing the connection between the earthly and divine realms.
    • In modern life, the concept of sacrifice can be seen in acts of charity and community service, where individuals give their time and resources for the greater good, akin to making offerings to a higher purpose.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a mediator between humans and the divine. It highlights the importance of sacrifices, both literal and metaphorical, in our lives. By recognizing the essence of our actions and their impact on the universe, we can strive to lead a life filled with purpose and connection to the greater whole. This mantra serves as a reminder that our offerings, whether they be in the form of prayers, good deeds, or intentions, are essential in fostering harmony and auspiciousness in our lives and the world around us.
