विः ह्याख्यम् मनसा वश्य इच्छन्न इन्द्राग्नि ज्ञास उता वा सजातान् |नान्या युवत प्रमतिर अस्ति मह्यं स वां धियं वाजयन्तीम् अतक्षम् ||
vi hy akhyam manasā vasya icchann indrāgnī jñāsa uta vā sajātān | nānyā yuvat pramatir asti mahyaṁ sa vāṁ dhiyaṁ vājayantīm atakṣam ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "With the mind focused, desiring to know, I invoke Indra and Agni. There is no other young woman who is more attractive to me; may she grant me her thoughts and may I be able to win her over."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vi hy akhyam: "Indeed, I speak" - This indicates the importance of communication and intention in invoking divine powers.
    • manasā: "with the mind" - Emphasizes the role of mental focus and clarity in achieving one's desires.
    • vasya icchann: "desiring to know" - Reflects the quest for knowledge and understanding, particularly in relationships.
    • indrāgnī: "Indra and Agni" - Indra represents strength and power, while Agni symbolizes transformation and energy, both essential for achieving goals.
    • jñāsa uta vā sajātān: "may I know or may I be united" - A plea for connection and understanding, whether in love or knowledge.
    • nānyā yuvat pramatir: "there is no other young woman" - Highlights the uniqueness of the desired connection, emphasizing the importance of individual relationships.
    • asti mahyaṁ: "exists for me" - Acknowledges the personal nature of desires and aspirations.
    • sa vāṁ dhiyaṁ: "may she grant me her thoughts" - A request for mutual understanding and shared intentions.
    • vājayantīm atakṣam: "may I be able to win her over" - Expresses the desire for success in relationships and endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a romantic context, this mantra can be seen as a reflection of the desire to connect deeply with a partner, emphasizing the importance of mental and emotional alignment.
    • In professional settings, it can represent the pursuit of knowledge and collaboration, where one seeks to understand colleagues and work together effectively.
    • In personal development, it highlights the significance of self-awareness and clarity of intention when striving for personal goals.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the power of intention, focus, and the desire for connection. It teaches us that achieving our goals—whether in love, work, or personal growth—requires clarity of thought and a genuine desire to understand and connect with others. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as building relationships, pursuing knowledge, and striving for personal success, reminding us that our thoughts and intentions shape our reality.
