अदात्रयā दयते वारीयाणि पूषा भगो अदितिर वस्त उष्रः |इन्द्रो विष्णुर वरुणो मित्रो अग्निर अहानि भद्रā जनयन्त दसमाः ||
adatrayā dayate vāryāṇi pūṣā bhago aditir vasta usraḥ | indro viṣṇur varuṇo mitro agnir ahāni bhadrā janayanta dasmāḥ ||
  • adatrayā dayate vāryāṇi pūṣā bhago aditir vasta usraḥ
    • adatrayā: This term refers to the act of giving or bestowing. It signifies generosity and the importance of sharing resources.
    • dayate: This means to show compassion or kindness. It emphasizes the value of empathy in human interactions.
    • vāryāṇi: This word translates to "the best" or "the most excellent." It highlights the pursuit of quality and excellence in life.
    • pūṣā: A deity associated with nourishment and prosperity. This represents the importance of sustenance and growth in our lives.
    • bhago: This term refers to wealth or fortune, indicating the blessings of abundance.
    • aditir: A reference to the infinite or the boundless, suggesting the limitless potential of the universe.
    • vasta: This means "to dwell" or "to reside," indicating the importance of a stable and secure environment.
    • usraḥ: This term can be interpreted as "the dawn" or "the light," symbolizing new beginnings and hope.
  • indro viṣṇur varuṇo mitro agnir ahāni bhadrā janayanta dasmāḥ
    • indro: Refers to Indra, the king of gods, symbolizing strength and leadership.
    • viṣṇur: Represents Vishnu, the preserver, indicating the importance of protection and stability in life.
    • varuṇo: Refers to Varuna, the god of cosmic order, emphasizing the significance of law and morality.
    • mitro: This means "friend," highlighting the value of friendship and community.
    • agnir: Refers to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing transformation and purification.
    • ahāni: This means "day" or "time," indicating the cyclical nature of life and the importance of each moment.
    • bhadrā: This term means "auspicious" or "good," suggesting the pursuit of positive outcomes.
    • janayanta: This means "to create" or "to generate," emphasizing the act of bringing forth new life or ideas.
    • dasmāḥ: This term can be interpreted as "from here" or "from this place," indicating a sense of origin or foundation.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates the essence of generosity, compassion, and the pursuit of excellence in life. It highlights the importance of divine forces in guiding human actions towards prosperity, friendship, and moral order. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal relationships, practicing kindness and empathy can lead to stronger bonds and a supportive community.
  • In professional settings, striving for excellence and collaboration can foster a productive work environment.
  • In societal contexts, promoting moral values and cosmic order can lead to a more harmonious and just society.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing positive relationships and environments for growth and prosperity.
