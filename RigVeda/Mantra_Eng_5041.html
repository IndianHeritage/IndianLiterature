सुगोता ते सुपथा पर्वतेष्व अवाते अपस तरसि स्वभानो |साना आवह पृत्युयाम्न ऋष्वे रयिं दिवो दुधितर इषयध्यै ||
sugota te supathā parvateṣv avāte apas tarasi svabhāno | sā na ā vaha pṛthuyāmann ṛṣve rayiṁ divo duhitar iṣayadhyai ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sugota: Well-spoken or auspicious words
    • te: Your
    • supathā: Good path or righteous way
    • parvateṣv: In the mountains
    • avāte: May it protect
    • apas: Waters or streams
    • tarasi: In the swift or flowing
    • svabhāno: Of one's own nature or essence
    • sā: That
    • na: Not
    • ā: Come
    • vaha: Carry or bring
    • pṛthuyāmann: Abundant nourishment
    • ṛṣve: To the seers or sages
    • rayiṁ: Wealth or prosperity
    • divo: Of the heavens
    • duhitar: Daughter
    • iṣayadhyai: May it be attained or achieved
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes auspiciousness and the protection of divine forces.
    • It emphasizes the importance of following a righteous path, especially in challenging environments like mountains.
    • The reference to "waters" signifies the flow of life and the need for sustenance, both physically and spiritually.
    • The mantra seeks to attract wealth and prosperity, not just for oneself but for the community and sages who guide us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied to foster a culture of ethical decision-making and teamwork, ensuring that all actions are aligned with a common good.
    • In personal life, it encourages individuals to seek guidance from wise mentors and to remain grounded in their values, especially during difficult times.
    • In environmental contexts, it can inspire efforts to protect natural resources, recognizing their importance for future generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of righteous living and the pursuit of prosperity that benefits all.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of our actions, the environment, and the wisdom of those who came before us.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and communities can navigate life's challenges while fostering growth and abundance.
