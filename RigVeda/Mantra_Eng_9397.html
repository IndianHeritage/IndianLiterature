यस्येक्ष्वाकुर उप व्रते रेवान मराय्य एधते |दिवीव पञ्च कृष्टयः ||
yasyekṣvākur upa vrate revān marāyy edhate | divīva pañca kṛṣṭayaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yasyekṣvākur upa vrate revān marāyy edhate | divīva pañca kṛṣṭayaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasy: of whom
    • ekṣvākur: the one who sees or perceives
    • upa: near or close
    • vrate: in the vow or commitment
    • revān: the one who is radiant or shining
    • marāyy: the one who is powerful or strong
    • edhate: who is engaged or active
    • divīva: like the heavens
    • pañca: five
    • kṛṣṭayaḥ: the fields or realms
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful individual who is committed to their vows and is perceived as radiant and strong.
    • The phrase "like the heavens" suggests a connection to divine qualities, indicating that the person embodies virtues that are elevated and admirable.
    • The mention of "five fields" can symbolize various aspects of life or realms of existence, suggesting a holistic approach to one's duties and responsibilities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A leader who is dedicated to their community, actively working towards its betterment, can be seen as embodying the qualities described in this mantra.
    • In a corporate setting, an employee who is committed to their work and inspires others through their dedication and strength can be likened to the "radiant" individual mentioned.
    • In personal relationships, someone who remains steadfast in their commitments and supports their loved ones can reflect the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of commitment, strength, and radiance in one's character.
    • It serves as a reminder to embody these qualities in various aspects of life, whether in leadership, personal relationships, or professional endeavors.
    • By striving to be like the "radiant" individual described, one can contribute positively to their surroundings and inspire others to do the same.
