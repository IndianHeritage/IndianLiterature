पिप्रीहि देवां उषतो यविष्ठ विद्वां ऋतूम ऋतुपते यजहे |ये दैव्याः ऋत्विजस्तेभिर अग्ने त्वं होत्रिणामस्य आयजिष्टः ||
piprīhi devām̐ uśato yaviṣṭha vidvām̐ ṛtūm̐r ṛtupate yajeha | ye daivyā ṛtvijas tebhir agne tvaṁ hotṝṇām asy āyajiṣṭhaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • piprīhi: May you be nourished or may you grow.
    • devām̐: O divine beings or gods.
    • uśato: Of the dawn or of the bright.
    • yaviṣṭha: The best or the most excellent.
    • vidvām̐: The wise or the knowledgeable.
    • ṛtūm̐r: The seasons or the cycles.
    • ṛtupate: O lord of the seasons.
    • yajeha: We worship or we offer.
    • ye: Those who.
    • daivyā: Divine or heavenly.
    • ṛtvijas: The priests or the seers of the seasons.
    • tebhir: With them.
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god).
    • tvaṁ: You.
    • hotṝṇām: Of the invokers or the priests.
    • asy: Of this.
    • āyajiṣṭhaḥ: The most worthy of worship.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the divine beings, particularly invoking Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The phrase "piprīhi devām̐" suggests a desire for growth and nourishment, indicating that the speaker seeks blessings from the divine for prosperity and well-being.
    • By addressing the "lord of the seasons" (ṛtupate), the mantra acknowledges the cyclical nature of life and the importance of harmony with the natural world.
    • The invocation of "daivyā ṛtvijas" emphasizes the role of wise individuals or priests who understand the rhythms of nature and can guide others in aligning with these cycles.
    • In a practical sense, this mantra can be related to agricultural practices where farmers pray for favorable seasons and divine assistance in their harvests.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of the interconnectedness of life, nature, and the divine.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek wisdom and guidance from those who understand the natural cycles, promoting a harmonious relationship with the environment.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in rituals, agricultural practices, and personal prayers for growth, prosperity, and understanding of life's rhythms.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of gratitude and reverence for the divine forces that govern the world around them.
