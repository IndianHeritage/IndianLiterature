मन्त्र: उता वः शंसम् उषिजाम इव श्मस्य अहिर बुध्न्योऽज एकपाद उता |तृता ऋभुक्षाः सविता चनो दधेऽपां नपाद आशुहेमाधियā शमि ||
uta vaḥ śaṁsam uśijām iva śmasy ahir budhnyo3 'ja ekapād uta | trita ṛbhukṣāḥ savitā cano dadhe 'pāṁ napād āśuhemā dhiyā śami ||
  • uta vaḥ - This phrase can be interpreted as "may we be blessed" or "let us be granted." It signifies a call for divine blessings and support.
  • śaṁsam - This word means "peace" or "well-being." It emphasizes the importance of harmony and tranquility in life.
  • uśijām - Referring to "the wise" or "those who are knowledgeable," this term highlights the value of wisdom and understanding in our actions.
  • iva - This means "like" or "as," suggesting a comparison or similarity.
  • śmasy - This can be interpreted as "the one who is strong" or "the powerful." It denotes strength and resilience.
  • ahir - Meaning "serpent" or "dragon," this term can symbolize challenges or obstacles that one must overcome.
  • budhnyo - This word refers to "the wise" or "the enlightened," reinforcing the theme of wisdom.
  • aja - Meaning "unborn" or "eternal," this term signifies the timeless nature of the divine or the ultimate reality.
  • ekapād - This translates to "one-footed," which can symbolize stability and balance in life.
  • trita - Referring to "the third," this term can represent a triad or a balance of forces.
  • ṛbhukṣāḥ - This means "the nourisher" or "the provider," indicating the importance of sustenance and support in life.
  • savitā - This refers to "the sun" or "the source of light," symbolizing enlightenment and guidance.
  • cano - Meaning "to grant" or "to bestow," this term emphasizes the act of giving and sharing.
  • dadhe - This means "to establish" or "to create," highlighting the importance of creation and manifestation.
  • pāṁ - This can be interpreted as "the path" or "the way," suggesting a journey or direction in life.
  • napād - Meaning "the foot" or "the step," this term emphasizes the importance of taking steps towards one's goals.
  • āśuhemā - This can be interpreted as "quickly" or "swiftly," indicating the need for prompt action.
  • dhiyā - Referring to "intellect" or "understanding," this term highlights the role of wisdom in decision-making.
  • śami - This word means "the tree" or "the source of support," symbolizing stability and growth.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of seeking blessings, wisdom, and strength in our lives. It encourages individuals to strive for peace and harmony while navigating challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal challenges, decision-making processes, or when seeking guidance in difficult situations. By invoking the qualities of wisdom, strength, and support, individuals can foster a balanced and fulfilling life, ultimately leading to personal growth and enlightenment.
