अग्नि त्वचं यातुधानस्य भिन्दि हिंस्राशनिर हरसां हन्त्व एनम् |प्र पर्वाणि जातवेदः श्रिणीहि क्रव्यात क्रविष्णुर वि चिनोतु वृक्णम् ||
agne tvacaṁ yātudhānasya bhindhi hiṁsrāśanir harasā hantv enam | pra parvāṇi jātavedaḥ śṛṇīhi kravyāt kraviṣṇur vi cinotu vṛkṇam ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Tvacaṁ: This translates to "the skin" or "the covering," indicating the outer layer or protection.
  • Yātudhānasya: This term refers to "the evil spirit" or "demonic forces," representing negativity or harmful influences.
  • Bhindhi: Meaning "to break" or "to cut," suggesting the act of removing or dispelling something.
  • Hiṁsrāśanir: This can be interpreted as "the fierce or violent one," indicating a strong, aggressive force.
  • Harasā: This means "to remove" or "to take away," emphasizing the act of eliminating negativity.
  • Hantv: This translates to "to kill" or "to destroy," reinforcing the idea of overcoming harmful influences.
  • Enam: Refers to "this" or "him," pointing to the specific negativity or evil being addressed.
  • Pra: A prefix meaning "forth" or "forward," indicating movement or action.
  • Parvāṇi: This means "the joints" or "the connections," symbolizing the interconnectedness of life.
  • Jātavedaḥ: Refers to "the one who knows all births," often associated with Agni as the source of knowledge and wisdom.
  • Śṛṇīhi: This translates to "listen" or "hear," suggesting the importance of awareness and understanding.
  • Kravyāt: Refers to "the flesh-eating" or "carnivorous," indicating a predatory or harmful aspect.
  • Kraviṣṇur: This term means "the one who is powerful in flesh," emphasizing strength and dominance.
  • Vi: A prefix meaning "in" or "within," indicating a sense of presence or existence.
  • Cinotu: This translates to "may he find" or "may he discover," suggesting a quest for knowledge or truth.
  • Vṛkṇam: Refers to "the tree" or "the plant," symbolizing growth, life, and sustenance.

The mantra essentially calls upon Agni to dispel negative forces and protect the individual from harm. It emphasizes the importance of breaking free from the influences of evil and seeking knowledge and strength. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to eliminate toxic relationships or negative thoughts that hinder their growth.
  • In a community context, it can serve as a call to action against social evils, encouraging collective efforts to create a safer and more harmonious environment.
  • In spiritual practices, chanting this mantra can help practitioners focus on inner strength and clarity, promoting a sense of peace and protection.

In conclusion, this mantra holds significant relevance in both personal and communal aspects of life. It serves as a powerful invocation for protection, knowledge, and the dispelling of negativity, encouraging individuals to seek strength and clarity in their journeys.
