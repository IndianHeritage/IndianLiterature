आयम् उ त्वा विचर्षणे जनीर इवाभि संवृतः |प्र सोम इन्द्र सर्पतु ||
ayam u tvā vicarṣaṇe janīr ivābhi saṁvṛtaḥ | pra soma indra sarpatu ||
  • Mantra: "ayam u tvā vicarṣaṇe janīr ivābhi saṁvṛtaḥ | pra soma indra sarpatu ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ayam: this
    • u: indeed
    • tvā: you
    • vicarṣaṇe: in the act of moving or wandering
    • janīr: the one who is born or created
    • ivābhi: like or as if
    • saṁvṛtaḥ: surrounded or enveloped
    • pra: forth or forward
    • soma: the divine nectar or drink
    • indra: the king of gods, representing strength and power
    • sarpatu: may he move or glide
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence, suggesting that the speaker is addressing a higher power or deity.
    • The phrase "ayam u tvā vicarṣaṇe" indicates a connection or relationship with the divine, emphasizing the importance of seeking guidance.
    • The imagery of being "surrounded" suggests a protective or nurturing environment, akin to how a mother envelops her child.
    • The reference to "soma" symbolizes the essence of life and vitality, often associated with spiritual enlightenment and strength.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents the ultimate power and authority, suggesting that the speaker seeks empowerment and support from this divine force.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of uncertainty or challenge, individuals often seek guidance through prayer or meditation, similar to the invocation in this mantra.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders may look to their mentors or advisors for support, paralleling the relationship depicted in the mantra.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or births, people often seek blessings from higher powers, reflecting the protective and nurturing aspects of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in our lives.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of life, the nurturing aspects of existence, and the strength derived from higher powers.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek support during challenging times, fostering a sense of community and connection with the divine.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the strength and protection that can be found in faith and spirituality.
