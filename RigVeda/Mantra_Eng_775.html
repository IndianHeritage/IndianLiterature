मन्त्र: चित्तिर अपां दमे विश्वायुः सद्मेव धीराः सम्माय चक्रुः ||
cittir apāṁ dame viśvāyuḥ sadmeva dhīrāḥ sammāya cakruḥ ||
  • cittir: This term can be understood as "mind" or "intellect." It signifies the cognitive aspect of human beings, representing awareness and consciousness.
  • apāṁ: This word translates to "of the waters." In a broader sense, it can refer to the life-giving and purifying qualities of water, which is essential for sustenance and growth.
  • dame: This means "in restraint" or "control." It emphasizes the importance of self-discipline and moderation in one's actions and thoughts.
  • viśvāyuḥ: This term can be interpreted as "universal breath" or "cosmic energy." It represents the vital force that connects all living beings and the universe.
  • sadmeva: This translates to "in the abode" or "in the home." It signifies a place of stability, comfort, and security, where one can thrive.
  • dhīrāḥ: This word means "wise" or "intelligent." It refers to individuals who possess discernment and understanding, capable of making sound decisions.
  • sammāya: This term translates to "with unity" or "in harmony." It emphasizes the importance of collaboration and collective effort in achieving goals.
  • cakruḥ: This means "they have created" or "they have established." It signifies the act of bringing something into existence through conscious effort and intention.

  • Meaning: The mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of mind, nature, and community. It highlights the importance of self-control and wisdom in creating a harmonious environment. The reference to water symbolizes the nurturing aspect of life, while the idea of a home represents a foundation for growth and stability.
  • Real-life Example: In a community setting, individuals who practice self-discipline and work together can create a supportive environment. For instance, in a workplace, when team members communicate openly and collaborate effectively, they can achieve common goals, much like the wise individuals mentioned in the mantra.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for mindfulness, unity, and the nurturing of both the self and the community. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as in personal development, teamwork, and environmental stewardship. By embodying the principles of restraint, wisdom, and collaboration, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and sustainable world.
