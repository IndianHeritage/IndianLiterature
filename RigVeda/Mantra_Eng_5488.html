अच्छायं वो मरुतः श्लोक एत्व अच्छा विष्णुं निषिक्तपाम अवोभिः |उता प्रजायै गृणते वयो धुर यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
acchāyaṁ vo marutaḥ śloka etv acchā viṣṇuṁ niṣiktapām avobhiḥ | uta prajāyai gṛṇate vayo dhur yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • acchāyaṁ: This word can be interpreted as "protection" or "shelter." It signifies the need for safety and security in life.
  • vo: This means "to you," indicating a direct address to the Maruts, the storm deities in Vedic literature.
  • marutaḥ: Refers to the Maruts, who are the personifications of the storm winds. They symbolize strength, power, and the forces of nature.
  • śloka: This translates to "verse" or "hymn," indicating the importance of reciting or chanting for invoking divine blessings.
  • etv: This means "thus" or "therefore," suggesting a connection to the preceding thoughts or actions.
  • acchā: Again refers to "protection" or "goodness," emphasizing the need for positive influences in life.
  • viṣṇuṁ: Refers to Lord Vishnu, the preserver in the Hindu trinity, symbolizing sustenance and protection.
  • niṣiktapām: This can be interpreted as "anointed" or "blessed," indicating a divine touch or favor.
  • avobhiḥ: This means "with strength" or "with vigor," suggesting the need for robust support in life.
  • uta: This translates to "or," indicating a choice or alternative.
  • prajāyai: Refers to "progeny" or "creation," emphasizing the importance of life and continuity.
  • gṛṇate: This means "to sing" or "to praise," highlighting the act of devotion and gratitude.
  • vayo: This translates to "age" or "time," indicating the passage of time and its significance in life.
  • dhur: This means "burden" or "responsibility," suggesting the weight of duties we carry.
  • yūyam: This means "you all," addressing the collective audience or community.
  • pāta: This translates to "may you protect" or "may you guard," invoking a plea for safety.
  • svastibhiḥ: Refers to "well-being" or "auspiciousness," emphasizing the desire for a prosperous life.
  • sadā: This means "always," indicating a continuous need for protection and blessings.
  • naḥ: This translates to "us," indicating a collective plea for divine intervention.

Conclusion: The mantra is a heartfelt invocation to the Maruts and Lord Vishnu, seeking their protection and blessings for well-being and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of community, responsibility, and the need for divine support in navigating life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable during times of uncertainty or when facing difficulties, reminding individuals to seek strength from both the divine and their community. It encourages a sense of unity and collective responsibility, fostering an environment where individuals support one another while invoking divine blessings for a harmonious existence.
