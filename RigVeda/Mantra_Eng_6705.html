पण्य इद उप गायत पण्य उक्तानि शंसत |ब्रह्मा कृणोति पण्य इति ||
panya id upa gāyata panya ukthāni śaṁsata | brahmā kṛṇota panya it ||
  • Mantra: "panya id upa gāyata panya ukthāni śaṁsata | brahmā kṛṇota panya it ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • panya: Wealth or prosperity
    • id: Here
    • upa: Near or close
    • gāyata: Sing or chant
    • ukthāni: Words or hymns
    • śaṁsata: Praise or glorify
    • brahmā: The ultimate reality or cosmic spirit
    • kṛṇota: May create or bestow
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wealth and prosperity in life.
    • It suggests that one should sing or chant praises of wealth, bringing it closer to oneself.
    • The invocation of Brahman (the ultimate reality) indicates that true prosperity is not just material but also spiritual.
    • By glorifying wealth through words and hymns, one can attract it into their life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied by entrepreneurs who focus on positive affirmations and gratitude for their financial success.
    • Individuals may practice this mantra during meditation or prayer, visualizing their goals and expressing gratitude for the abundance they seek.
    • Community gatherings or rituals that celebrate prosperity can incorporate this mantra to inspire collective growth and success.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to wealth, combining both material and spiritual aspects.
    • It teaches that by acknowledging and praising wealth, one can create a positive mindset that attracts prosperity.
    • This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal development to community prosperity, emphasizing the interconnectedness of wealth and spirituality.
