महि त्वाष्ट्रं ऊर्जयन्तीर अजुर्यं स्तभूयमानं वहतो वहन्ति |व्य अङ्गेभिर दिद्युतानः सदस्थ एकाम इव रोदसी आ विवेश ||
mahi tvāṣṭram ūrjayantīr ajuryaṁ stabhūyamānaṁ vahato vahanti | vy aṅgebhir didyutānaḥ sadhastha ekām iva rodasī ā viveśa ||
  • mahi tvāṣṭram: This phrase can be interpreted as "great is the power of the creator." In real life, this can be likened to the immense potential and creativity that exists within individuals and the universe. For example, consider a scientist who discovers a new technology that changes the world; this reflects the creative power of the mind.
  • ūrjayantīr: This word signifies "those who are full of energy or strength." In everyday life, this can refer to individuals or groups that exhibit resilience and vigor, such as athletes training for a competition or activists fighting for a cause. Their energy can inspire others to take action.
  • ajuryaṁ stabhūyamānaṁ: This can be translated as "the unyielding strength." It represents steadfastness and determination. A real-life example could be a person facing adversity, such as a single parent working multiple jobs to provide for their children. Their unyielding strength in the face of challenges is commendable.
  • vahato vahanti: This phrase means "those who carry or support." It can be related to the idea of community support, where individuals come together to help one another. For instance, during natural disasters, communities often unite to provide aid and support to those affected, showcasing the power of collective effort.
  • vy aṅgebhir didyutānaḥ: This translates to "with their limbs shining." This can symbolize the brilliance and potential of individuals when they work together. A real-life example is a team of professionals collaborating on a project, where each member's unique skills contribute to the overall success, shining brightly as a unit.
  • sadhastha ekām iva rodasī: This phrase can be interpreted as "like one in the assembly of the heavens." It suggests unity and harmony in a larger context. In real life, this can be seen in cultural festivals where diverse groups come together to celebrate, creating a sense of belonging and unity among participants.
  • ā viveśa: This means "has entered." It signifies the idea of entering into a state of awareness or consciousness. In practical terms, this can relate to mindfulness practices, where individuals become more aware of their thoughts and surroundings, leading to a more fulfilling life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of creativity, strength, support, unity, and awareness. In real life, these qualities are essential for personal growth and community development. By recognizing and harnessing our inner power, supporting one another, and fostering unity, we can create a more harmonious and productive society. This mantra serves as a reminder of the potential within each of us to contribute positively to the world around us.
