नू नो गोमद वीरवत् धेहि रत्नम् उषो अश्वावत् पुरुभोजो अस्मे |मा नो बर्हिः पुरुषता निदे कर यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
nū no gomad vīravad dhehi ratnam uṣo aśvāvat purubhojo asme | mā no barhiḥ puruṣatā nide kar yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O divine forces, grant us the wealth of strength and valor, like the dawn that brings forth the horses. May we not be deprived of the essence of humanity; may you protect us always with auspiciousness."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • nū: A call for attention or invocation.
    • no: Our, indicating possession or belonging.
    • gomad: Wealth or strength, often associated with cattle in Vedic culture.
    • vīravad: Valor or bravery, a quality admired in warriors.
    • dhehi: Grant or bestow, a request for divine favor.
    • ratnam: Jewel or precious thing, symbolizing wealth or prosperity.
    • uṣo: Dawn, representing new beginnings and enlightenment.
    • aśvāvat: Like horses, symbolizing speed, strength, and vitality.
    • purubhojo: Abundant or rich, indicating a state of plenty.
    • asme: To us, indicating the recipients of the blessings.
    • mā: May not, a plea for protection.
    • barhiḥ: Essence or core, often referring to the essence of humanity.
    • puruṣatā: Humanity or human qualities, emphasizing the importance of being human.
    • nide: Deprived or taken away, a concern for loss.
    • kar: Do or act, a call to action.
    • yūyam: You (plural), referring to the divine forces.
    • pāta: Protect or guard, a request for safety.
    • svastibhiḥ: Auspiciousness or well-being, a state of good fortune.
    • sadā: Always, indicating a continuous need.
    • naḥ: Us, reinforcing the collective aspect of the plea.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be invoked during team meetings to foster a spirit of collaboration and strength among team members, encouraging them to work together towards common goals.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during challenging times, seeking strength and protection from negative influences, much like how one would seek guidance from a mentor or a supportive friend.
    • During community gatherings or festivals, this mantra can be used to invoke blessings for prosperity and well-being for all members, emphasizing the importance of unity and shared values.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in achieving strength, valor, and prosperity while emphasizing the importance of humanity. It serves as a reminder that in our pursuit of success, we must not lose sight of our core values and the essence of being human. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, from personal challenges to collective endeavors, reinforcing the idea that strength and protection come from both within and from the divine. By invoking this mantra, individuals and communities can cultivate a sense of purpose, resilience, and unity in their lives.
