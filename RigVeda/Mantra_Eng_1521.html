यद धा त्याम अङ्गिरोभ्यो धेनुं देवाऽदत्तन |वितां दुहरे आर्यमा कर्तरी सच्चां एष तां वेद मे सचा ||
yad dha tyām aṅgirobhyo dhenuṁ devā adattana | vi tāṁ duhre aryamā kartarī sacām̐ eṣa tāṁ veda me sacā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad - whatever
    • dha - is given
    • tyām - in that
    • aṅgirobhyo - to the Angiras (a group of sages)
    • dhenuṁ - the cow (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • devā - the gods
    • adattana - have given
    • vi - indeed
    • tām - that
    • duhre - in abundance
    • aryamā - the noble one
    • kartarī - as a doer
    • sacām̐ - of truth
    • eṣa - this
    • tām - that
    • veda - knows
    • me - my
    • sacā - of truth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the divine provision of resources, particularly symbolized by the cow, which represents nourishment and wealth.
    • The reference to the Angiras suggests a connection to wisdom and knowledge, indicating that the blessings are bestowed upon those who seek truth and understanding.
    • The phrase "as a doer of truth" emphasizes the importance of action aligned with righteousness and integrity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the cow is often seen as a vital resource for farmers, providing milk and labor. This mantra can be interpreted as a reminder to respect and care for these resources, acknowledging their divine origin.
    • In a corporate setting, the mantra can be applied to ethical business practices, where leaders are encouraged to act with integrity and provide for their employees and community, thus creating a cycle of abundance.
    • In personal life, it can inspire individuals to seek knowledge and act truthfully, leading to personal growth and fulfillment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine blessings in the form of resources and the importance of acting with integrity and truthfulness.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions should align with higher principles, leading to a harmonious and prosperous life.
    • In various aspects of life—be it personal, professional, or spiritual—this mantra encourages us to recognize and honor the abundance we receive and to act as responsible stewards of that abundance.
