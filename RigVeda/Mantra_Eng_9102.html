आवर्वृततीर अध नु द्विधारा गोषुयुधो न नियवं चरन्तीः |ऋषे जनित्रीर भुवनस्य पत्नीर अपो वन्दस्व सवृद्धः सयोनीः ||
āvarvṛtatīr adha nu dvidhārā goṣuyudho na niyavaṁ carantīḥ | ṛṣe janitrīr bhuvanasya patnīr apo vandasva savṛdhaḥ sayonīḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āvarvṛtatīr: "O waters, who are enveloped."
    • adha: "below."
    • nu: "now."
    • dvidhārā: "two streams."
    • goṣuyudho: "in the midst of the herds."
    • na: "not."
    • niyavaṁ: "restrained."
    • carantīḥ: "moving."
    • ṛṣe: "O sage."
    • janitrīr: "the mother."
    • bhuvanasya: "of the universe."
    • patnīr: "the wife."
    • apo: "O waters."
    • vandasva: "we worship."
    • savṛdhaḥ: "with abundance."
    • sayonīḥ: "with good offspring."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses the waters, invoking their sacredness and significance in the natural world.
    • The phrase "two streams" can symbolize the duality of life, such as the balance between material and spiritual pursuits.
    • The reference to "herds" suggests the interconnectedness of life and the importance of community and support.
    • By calling upon the "sage" and "mother of the universe," the mantra emphasizes the nurturing and protective aspects of nature.
    • The invocation of abundance and good offspring reflects a desire for prosperity and well-being in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the importance of water is paramount. Farmers often pray for rain, recognizing that water is essential for crops to thrive.
    • In family life, the nurturing aspect of the "mother" can be seen in how parents care for their children, ensuring they grow up in a supportive environment.
    • The concept of community is evident in social gatherings, where people come together to support one another, much like herds in nature.
    • In personal development, the balance between different aspects of life (work, family, spirituality) can be likened to the "two streams" mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of water as a life-giving force and the interconnectedness of all living beings.
    • It serves as a reminder to respect and honor nature, recognizing its role in our existence.
    • In real life, the principles of nurturing, community support, and balance can guide individuals in their personal and social lives.
    • Ultimately, the mantra encourages a harmonious relationship with the environment and a commitment to fostering abundance and well-being for all.
