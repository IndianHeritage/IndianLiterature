जाम्य अतीतपे धनुर वयोधा अरुहद वनम् |दृष्टदं जिव्हयावधीत ||
jāmy atītape dhanur vayodhā aruhad vanam | dṛṣadaṁ jihvayāvadhīt ||
  • jāmy: This word can be interpreted as "born" or "originating." It signifies the beginning or the source of something.
  • atītape: This term refers to "the sun" or "the heat of the sun." It symbolizes energy, vitality, and the life-giving force of nature.
  • dhanur: This translates to "bow," which can represent strength, readiness, and the ability to defend oneself or achieve goals.
  • vayodhā: This word means "warriors" or "those who are strong." It signifies courage, valor, and the spirit of fighting for what is right.
  • aruhad: This term means "ascended" or "mounted." It indicates rising above challenges or taking a higher position in life.
  • vanam: This translates to "forest" or "wilderness." It symbolizes nature, tranquility, and the untamed aspects of life.
  • dṛṣadaṁ: This word can mean "to see" or "vision." It represents perception, awareness, and understanding.
  • jihvayāvadhīt: This term refers to "the tongue" or "speech." It signifies communication, expression, and the power of words.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to recognize the origins of strength and vitality, to embrace the warrior spirit, and to rise above challenges while being aware of our surroundings and the power of our words. It emphasizes the importance of being grounded in nature and the strength that comes from it.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the sun provides energy, individuals can draw strength from their experiences and origins to grow and overcome obstacles.
  • Leadership: The qualities of a warrior can be embodied in leaders who rise to challenges and inspire others to do the same.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to the forest encourages a connection with nature, promoting sustainability and respect for the environment.
  • Effective Communication: The emphasis on speech highlights the importance of clear and impactful communication in personal and professional relationships.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, nature, and communication. It encourages individuals to harness their inner warrior, rise above challenges, and communicate effectively while remaining grounded in their origins and the natural world around them.
