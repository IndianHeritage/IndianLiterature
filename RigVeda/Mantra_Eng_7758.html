आ मित्रावरुणा भागं मध्वः पवन्त उर्मयः |विदाना अस्य शक्मभिः ||
ā mitrāvaruṇā bhagam madhvaḥ pavanta ūrmayaḥ | vidānā asya śakmabhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: O
    • mitrāvaruṇā: Mitra and Varuna (deities representing friendship and cosmic order)
    • bhagam: the share or portion
    • madhvaḥ: sweet or pleasant
    • pavanta: may they blow or come forth
    • ūrmayaḥ: waves or currents
    • vidānā: knowing or understanding
    • asya: of this
    • śakmabhiḥ: with strength or power
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes the deities Mitra and Varuna, asking for their blessings and the sweet, pleasant aspects of life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of understanding and strength in navigating life's currents.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal relationships, invoking the qualities of Mitra (friendship) can lead to stronger bonds and mutual respect.
    • In challenging situations, understanding the deeper truths (vidānā) can empower individuals to make informed decisions.
    • In a professional setting, the idea of working together harmoniously (like the currents of water) can lead to a more productive and positive environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of friendship, understanding, and strength in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, reminding us to seek harmony and wisdom.
    • By invoking these qualities, we can navigate the complexities of life with grace and resilience.
