शतेनाऽनो अभिष्टिभिर नीयुत्वां इन्द्रसारथिः |वायो सुतस्य तृम्पतम् ||
śatenā no abhiṣṭibhir niyutvām̐ indrasārathiḥ | vāyo sutasya tṛmpatam ||
  • śatenā: This word translates to "with hundreds" or "by hundreds." It signifies abundance and the idea of many or numerous.
  • no: This means "to us" or "for us," indicating a personal or collective appeal.
  • abhiṣṭibhir: This term refers to "blessings" or "boons." It suggests a request for divine favor or support.
  • niyutvām̐: This can be interpreted as "to be directed" or "to be guided." It implies a desire for guidance or leadership.
  • indrasārathiḥ: This compound word means "the charioteer of Indra." Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, rain, and thunder. The charioteer symbolizes guidance and control.
  • vāyo: This translates to "of the wind" or "air," which is often associated with movement, life, and energy.
  • sutasya: This means "of the son" or "of the offspring," indicating lineage or progeny.
  • tṛmpatam: This term can be understood as "the one who is worthy of praise" or "the one who is honored." It denotes respect and reverence.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for guidance and blessings from the divine, particularly from Indra, who is seen as a powerful protector and leader. The speaker is asking for abundant blessings and direction in their life, particularly in relation to their offspring or future generations.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Family Blessings: Parents may recite this mantra to seek blessings for their children, hoping for their success and well-being.
  • Leadership and Guidance: Individuals in positions of leadership can use this mantra to invoke wisdom and guidance in their decision-making processes.
  • Community Support: Communities can come together to chant this mantra during festivals or gatherings, seeking collective blessings for prosperity and harmony.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance and blessings, emphasizing the importance of community, family, and leadership. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine, encouraging us to seek support and direction in our lives. By invoking the blessings of Indra, we acknowledge the need for strength and guidance in our personal and communal journeys.
