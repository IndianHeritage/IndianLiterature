अहम् पुरो मन्दसानो व्य ऐरं नव साकं नवतीः शम्बरस्य |शततमं वेश्यं सर्वताता दिवोदासम् अतिथिग्वं यद आवम् ||
aham puro mandasāno vy airaṁ nava sākaṁ navatīḥ śambarasya | śatatamaṁ veśyaṁ sarvatātā divodāsam atithigvaṁ yad āvam ||
  • Aham: This word translates to "I" or "myself," indicating a sense of self-awareness and identity.
  • Puro: Meaning "in front" or "before," it suggests a position of prominence or leadership.
  • Mandasāno: This can be interpreted as "seated in a comfortable place," symbolizing stability and security.
  • Vy: This word can mean "with" or "together," indicating a sense of community or connection.
  • Airaṁ: This term can refer to "the air" or "the atmosphere," representing the environment or the surrounding world.
  • Nava: Meaning "new," it signifies freshness, innovation, or new beginnings.
  • Sākaṁ: This word translates to "branches" or "parts," which can symbolize growth and expansion.
  • Navatīḥ: This term means "ninety," which can represent abundance or a large quantity.
  • Śambarasya: Referring to "of the Shambara," it may symbolize a powerful entity or force.
  • Śatatamaṁ: Meaning "the best" or "the highest," indicating excellence or superiority.
  • Veśyaṁ: This word can mean "house" or "abode," suggesting a place of comfort and belonging.
  • Sarvatātā: Meaning "everywhere" or "all-pervading," it indicates universality and inclusiveness.
  • Divodāsam: Referring to "the divine," it symbolizes a connection to higher powers or spirituality.
  • Atithigvaṁ: This term means "guest," representing hospitality and the importance of welcoming others.
  • Yad: Meaning "that" or "which," it connects the previous ideas to the following statement.
  • Āvam: This word translates to "we" or "us," emphasizing collective identity and unity.

The mantra speaks to the essence of self-awareness, community, and the importance of a nurturing environment. It emphasizes the significance of new beginnings and growth, while also acknowledging the divine and the importance of hospitality. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios:


  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra speaks of new beginnings, individuals can embrace change and seek personal development in their lives.
  • Community Building: The emphasis on connection and hospitality can inspire people to foster strong relationships within their communities, welcoming others and creating a sense of belonging.
  • Spiritual Awareness: The reference to the divine encourages individuals to seek a deeper understanding of their spirituality and the interconnectedness of all beings.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of self-awareness, community, and the divine in our lives. It encourages us to embrace new beginnings, foster connections, and create a nurturing environment for ourselves and others. By applying these principles, we can lead more fulfilling and harmonious lives.
