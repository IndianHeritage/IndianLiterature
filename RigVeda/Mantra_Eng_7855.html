वृषा पुनान आयुषु स्तनयन्न अधि बर्हिषि |हरिः सन् योनिं आसदत ||
vṛṣā punāna āyuṣu stanayann adhi barhiṣi | hariḥ san yonim āsadat ||
  • vṛṣā punāna āyuṣu: This phrase can be translated as "the bull (or the strong one) purifying (or renewing) life." In this context, "vṛṣā" refers to strength or vitality, often symbolized by a bull, which is a common representation of power and fertility in various cultures.
  • stanayann adhi barhiṣi: This translates to "nourishing (or supporting) on the sacred grass." "Stanayann" implies the act of nurturing or providing sustenance, while "barhiṣi" refers to the sacred grass used in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and the connection to the divine.
  • hariḥ san yonim āsadat: This means "the divine (or the auspicious) one (Hari) sits in the womb (or source)." "Hari" is often associated with Vishnu or the divine presence, and "yonim" refers to the source or origin, indicating a place of creation or birth.

  • Significance of the Mantra: This mantra emphasizes the themes of renewal, nourishment, and divine presence. It reflects the cyclical nature of life, where strength and vitality are essential for growth and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Application: In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life. For instance, in personal development, one can interpret "purifying life" as the need for self-improvement and renewal through education, health, and spiritual practices. The idea of nourishing on sacred grass can be seen as the importance of nurturing relationships and environments that foster growth.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, nourishment, and the divine. It encourages individuals to seek renewal in their lives, to nurture their surroundings, and to recognize the divine presence in all aspects of existence. By embodying these principles, one can lead a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
