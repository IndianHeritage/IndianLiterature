युयं विश्वं परि पाथ वरुणो मित्रो आर्यमाः |युष्माकं शर्मणि प्रिये स्याम सुप्रणीतयोऽति द्विषः ||
yūyaṁ viśvam pari pātha varuṇo mitro aryamā | yuṣmākaṁ śarmaṇi priye syāma supraṇītayo 'ti dviṣaḥ ||
  • yūyaṁ - You (plural), referring to a group or community.
  • viśvam - All or the universe, indicating inclusiveness.
  • pari pātha - Surrounding or protecting, suggesting a sense of security.
  • varuṇo - Varuna, the deity associated with cosmic order and water, symbolizing truth and moral authority.
  • mitro - Mitra, the deity representing friendship and harmony.
  • aryamā - Aryama, a deity associated with the afterlife and the protection of the righteous.
  • yuṣmākaṁ - Your, indicating possession or belonging.
  • śarmaṇi - In safety or comfort, referring to a state of well-being.
  • priye - Beloved or dear, emphasizing affection and care.
  • syāma - May we be, indicating a wish or desire.
  • supraṇītayo - Well-directed or well-ordered, suggesting a sense of purpose and clarity.
  • 'ti dviṣaḥ - From those who wish harm, indicating a desire for protection from negativity or malice.

This mantra expresses a profound wish for collective well-being and protection. It invokes the deities Varuna, Mitra, and Aryama, seeking their blessings for safety, harmony, and righteousness. The mantra emphasizes the importance of community and the interconnectedness of all beings, highlighting the need for mutual support and protection against negativity.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Community Support: In times of crisis, such as natural disasters or social unrest, communities can come together to support one another, embodying the essence of this mantra.
  • Conflict Resolution: In personal or professional relationships, invoking the spirit of friendship and harmony can help resolve conflicts and foster understanding.
  • Personal Well-being: Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to seek safety and comfort in their lives, surrounding themselves with positive influences and relationships.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, protection, and the pursuit of righteousness. It serves as a reminder of the importance of community and the need to support one another in the face of challenges. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and communities can create a more harmonious and safe environment for all.
