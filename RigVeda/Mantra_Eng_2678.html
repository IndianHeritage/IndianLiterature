अस्तीदमधिमन्थनमस्तिप्रजननं कृतम्। एतां विश्पत्नीमाभराग्निमन्थाम पूर्वथा॥
astīdam adhimanthanam asti prajananaṁ kṛtam | etāṁ viśpatnīm ā bharāgnim manthāma pūrvathā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • astīdam: This means "this is" or "there is."
    • adhimanthanam: Refers to "the great churning" or "the process of churning."
    • asti: Again means "there is."
    • prajananaṁ: Translates to "creation" or "birth."
    • kṛtam: Means "done" or "performed."
    • etāṁ: Means "this" or "that."
    • viśpatnīm: Refers to "the lord of the universe" or "the cosmic being."
    • ā bharāgnim: Translates to "bring forth the fire" or "invoke the divine energy."
    • manthāma: Means "we churn" or "we are churning."
    • pūrvathā: Refers to "in the past" or "from ancient times."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the process of creation and the divine energy involved in it.
    • The "great churning" symbolizes the efforts and struggles in life that lead to creation and transformation.
    • It emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energy to aid in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, the "great churning" can be likened to the challenges faced during the startup phase, where hard work and perseverance lead to the birth of a successful enterprise.
    • In personal development, individuals often go through a "churning" process of self-discovery, where they confront their fears and limitations to create a better version of themselves.
    • In nature, the cycle of seasons can be seen as a churning process, where winter gives way to spring, symbolizing rebirth and renewal.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the continuous cycle of creation and transformation in the universe.
    • It teaches us that through effort and divine invocation, we can bring forth new beginnings in our lives.
    • Applicable in various aspects of life, it encourages us to embrace challenges as opportunities for growth and to seek divine guidance in our pursuits.
