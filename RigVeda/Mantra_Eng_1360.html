तुभ्यं पयो यत पितराव अनीतां राधः सुरेतस तुरणे भुरण्यू |शुचि यत ते रेक्ण आयजन्त सबर्दुघायाः पय उष्रियायाः ||
tubhyam payo yat pitarāv anītāṁ rādhaḥ suretas turaṇe bhuraṇyū | śuci yat te rekṇa āyajanta sabardughāyāḥ paya usriyāyāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "tubhyam payo yat pitarāv anītāṁ rādhaḥ suretas turaṇe bhuraṇyū | śuci yat te rekṇa āyajanta sabardughāyāḥ paya usriyāyāḥ ||"
  • Translation: "To you, O divine, we offer the milk that the fathers have brought forth, the one who is pure and has great wisdom, may you bestow upon us the nourishing essence that is beneficial and pure."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • tubhyam: To you (addressing the divine)
    • payo: Milk (symbolizing nourishment and purity)
    • yat: That which
    • pitarāv: The fathers (ancestors or forefathers)
    • anītāṁ: Have brought forth
    • rādhaḥ: Abundance or nourishment
    • suretas: One who is wise or has great understanding
    • turaṇe: In the swift or rapid
    • bhuraṇyū: Bestow or grant
    • śuci: Pure
    • yat: That which
    • te: Your
    • rekṇa: Essence or substance
    • āyajanta: They who worship or offer
    • sabardughāyāḥ: Beneficial or nourishing
    • paya: Milk or nourishment
    • usriyāyāḥ: That which is pure
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, milk is considered a sacred and nourishing substance. For instance, in Hindu rituals, milk is often used in offerings to deities, symbolizing purity and sustenance.
    • In modern life, the mantra can be interpreted as a call for wisdom and guidance from our ancestors. For example, when making important life decisions, individuals often seek advice from family members or mentors, reflecting the wisdom of those who came before them.
    • The emphasis on purity and nourishment can be seen in the growing trend of organic and natural foods, where people are increasingly aware of the quality of what they consume, seeking out pure and beneficial options for their health.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine nourishment and wisdom. It highlights the importance of seeking guidance from our ancestors and the purity of what we consume, both physically and spiritually. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to reflect on their choices, seek wisdom from those who have come before, and strive for purity in their actions and intentions. By doing so, one can cultivate a life that is not only fulfilling but also aligned with the values of nourishment and wisdom.
