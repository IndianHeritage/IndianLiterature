यं त्वा जनासा ईळते सबाधो वाजसातये |स बोधि वृततूर्ये ||
yaṁ tvā janāsa īḻate sabādho vājasātaye | sa bodhi vṛtratūrye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: whom
    • tvā: you
    • janāsa: people
    • īḻate: worship or praise
    • sabādho: with all their strength
    • vājasātaye: for the purpose of strength or power
    • sa: he
    • bodhi: knowledge or enlightenment
    • vṛtratūrye: in overcoming obstacles or challenges
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the collective worship and reverence of a higher power or divine entity by the people.
    • The phrase "whom you (the divine) are worshipped by the people with all their strength" emphasizes the unity and devotion of the community.
    • The latter part, "he (the divine) grants knowledge in overcoming obstacles," suggests that through this worship, individuals gain wisdom and the ability to face challenges in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, when people come together to celebrate a festival, they often engage in rituals that strengthen their bonds and invoke blessings for overcoming difficulties.
    • In personal life, individuals may turn to prayer or meditation during tough times, seeking guidance and strength to navigate their challenges.
    • In workplaces, team-building activities can be seen as a modern equivalent where collective effort and unity lead to overcoming project hurdles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of collective worship and the strength derived from community support.
    • It teaches that through unity and devotion, individuals can gain the wisdom needed to overcome life's challenges.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal struggles or community efforts, the essence of this mantra encourages seeking strength in togetherness and the pursuit of knowledge to navigate obstacles.
