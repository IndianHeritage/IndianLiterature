सोमारुद्राऽधार्येथाम् असुर्यं प्र वाम इष्टयोऽरम अशन्वन्तु |दमे-दमे सप्त रत्ना दधानाः शं नो भूतं द्विपदें शं चतुष्पदें ||
somārudrā dhārayethām asurya1m pra vām iṣṭayo 'ram aśnuvantu | dame-dame sapta ratnā dadhānā śaṁ no bhūtaṁ dvipade śaṁ catuṣpade ||
  • Mantra: "somārudrā dhārayethām asurya1m pra vām iṣṭayo 'ram aśnuvantu | dame-dame sapta ratnā dadhānā śaṁ no bhūtaṁ dvipade śaṁ catuṣpade ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • somārudrā: Referring to the divine energy associated with the moon and Rudra (Shiva), symbolizing tranquility and strength.
    • dhārayethām: May it support or sustain.
    • asurya1m: Without the sun, indicating a state of darkness or ignorance.
    • pra vām: For you, or towards you.
    • iṣṭayo: Desires or wishes.
    • 'ram: May it bring joy or happiness.
    • aśnuvantu: May they attain or receive.
    • dame-dame: In every home or dwelling.
    • sapta ratnā: Seven jewels, symbolizing wealth and prosperity.
    • dadhānā: Holding or possessing.
    • śaṁ: Peace or well-being.
    • no: Our.
    • bhūtaṁ: Beings or creatures.
    • dvipade: Two-legged (humans).
    • śaṁ: Peace or well-being.
    • catuṣpade: Four-legged (animals).
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine energies of the moon and Rudra, seeking their support in overcoming ignorance and darkness.
    • It emphasizes the importance of joy and fulfillment of desires, not just for individuals but for the community as a whole.
    • The mention of "seven jewels" symbolizes the wealth of virtues and resources that should be present in every home, promoting prosperity and harmony.
    • By wishing peace for all beings, both humans and animals, the mantra highlights the interconnectedness of life and the need for compassion and coexistence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied during festivals or gatherings where people come together to celebrate and share their joys, fostering a sense of unity and support.
    • In personal life, individuals can reflect on their desires and seek to fulfill them in a way that brings happiness not only to themselves but also to those around them.
    • In environmental conservation efforts, the mantra's call for peace among all beings can inspire actions that protect both human and animal habitats, promoting a balanced ecosystem.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of divine support in our lives, encouraging us to seek joy and fulfillment while being mindful of the well-being of all creatures.
    • Its significance lies in promoting harmony, prosperity, and peace, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal aspirations to community welfare and environmental stewardship.
