तं त्वा समिद्भिर अंगिरो घृतेन वर्धयामसि |बृहच्चोचाय यविष्ठ्य ||
taṁ tvā samidbhir aṅgiro ghṛtena vardhayāmasi | bṛhac chocā yaviṣṭhya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • taṁ: that (referring to the divine or the essence)
    • tvā: you (addressing the divine)
    • samidbhir: with offerings (specifically, with the sacred wood used in rituals)
    • aṅgiro: O Angiras (a sage or divine being)
    • ghṛtena: with ghee (clarified butter, often used in rituals)
    • vardhayāmasi: we nourish or increase
    • bṛhac: great or vast
    • shocā: light or brilliance
    • yaviṣṭhya: by the offering or through the sacrifice
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra expresses a prayer or invocation to the divine, specifically addressing the sage Angiras. It emphasizes the act of nourishing or enhancing the divine presence through offerings of ghee and sacred wood. The mantra highlights the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine and invoking blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, offerings of ghee are made to deities during ceremonies, symbolizing purity and devotion. For instance, during Diwali, ghee lamps are lit to invite prosperity and dispel darkness.
    • In a modern context, the act of nourishing can be seen in how individuals contribute to their communities, such as through charitable acts or volunteering, which can be viewed as a form of offering to the greater good.
  • Significance and Application: The mantra signifies the importance of rituals and offerings in spiritual practices. It teaches that through sincere offerings and devotion, one can connect with the divine and receive blessings. In real life, this can be applied by engaging in acts of kindness, community service, and maintaining a sense of gratitude and reverence in daily life. The essence of nourishing not just the self but also the community reflects the interconnectedness of all beings.
