अधाकृत्नोः पृथिवीं संदृशे दिवे यो धौतीनाम अहिहन्न आरिणक पथः |तं त्वा स्तोमेभिर उदभिर न वाजिनं देवं देवाऽजनन सास्य उक्थ्यः ||
adhākṛṇoḥ pṛthivīṁ saṁdṛśe dive yo dhautīnām ahihann āriṇak pathaḥ | taṁ tvā stomebhir udabhir na vājinaṁ devaṁ devā ajanan sāsy ukthyaḥ ||
  • adhākṛṇoḥ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who has created" or "the one who has established." It signifies a divine force or entity responsible for creation.
  • pṛthivīṁ: This word means "the Earth" or "the physical world." It represents the tangible reality we live in.
  • saṁdṛśe: This translates to "in the sight of" or "in the presence of." It indicates awareness or observation.
  • dive: Meaning "in the sky" or "heaven," it refers to the celestial realm or the divine.
  • yo: This is a pronoun meaning "who." It connects the divine to the actions being described.
  • dhautīnām: This term refers to "purifiers" or "those who cleanse." It suggests a process of purification, both physical and spiritual.
  • ahihann: This can be interpreted as "the one who has overcome" or "the one who has conquered." It implies strength and victory over challenges.
  • āriṇak: This word means "the path" or "the way." It signifies a journey or a method of achieving something.
  • pathaḥ: This is a noun meaning "path" or "way." It emphasizes the importance of direction in life.
  • taṁ: This pronoun means "that" or "him." It refers to the divine entity being addressed.
  • tvā: This means "you," indicating a direct address to the divine.
  • stomebhir: This term refers to "with hymns" or "with praises." It highlights the act of worship and reverence.
  • udabhir: This translates to "with waters" or "with offerings." It symbolizes the act of giving and the importance of rituals.
  • na: This means "not," indicating a negation or absence.
  • vājinaṁ: This term means "the one who brings strength" or "the powerful." It refers to a divine force that provides support.
  • devaṁ: This word means "god" or "divine being." It signifies the ultimate source of power and guidance.
  • devā: This is the plural form of "deva," meaning "gods." It indicates a collective divine presence.
  • ajanan: This term means "born" or "produced." It signifies creation and the emergence of life.
  • sāsy: This word means "of the harvest" or "of the crops." It represents abundance and prosperity.
  • ukthyaḥ: This translates to "with offerings" or "with praises." It emphasizes the importance of gratitude and acknowledgment.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, purification, and the divine connection between the Earth and the heavens. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing the divine presence in our lives and the need for purification and strength in our journeys. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, environmental consciousness, and spiritual practices. By acknowledging the divine and engaging in acts of purification and gratitude, individuals can foster a deeper connection with the world around them, leading to a more harmonious existence.
