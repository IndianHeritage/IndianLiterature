वि यदास्थाद यजतो वातचोदितो ह्वारो न वक्वा जरणा अनाकृतः |तस्य पत्मन दक्षुषः कृष्णजंहसः शुचिजनमनः रज आ व्यध्वनः ||
vi yad asthād yajato vātacodito hvāro na vakvā jaraṇā anākṛtaḥ | tasya patman dakṣuṣaḥ kṛṣṇajaṁhasaḥ śucijanmano raja ā vyadhvanaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vi yad asthād: "Whatever is established" or "Whatever exists."
    • yajato: "By those who perform sacrifices" or "by worshippers."
    • vātacodito: "Inspired by the wind" or "motivated by the breath of life."
    • hvāro: "The one who calls" or "the one who invokes."
    • na vakvā: "Does not speak" or "is not uttered."
    • jaraṇā: "Old age" or "decay."
    • anākṛtaḥ: "Not formed" or "not created."
    • tasya: "Of that" or "of it."
    • patman: "The lotus" or "the flower."
    • dakṣuṣaḥ: "Skilled" or "capable."
    • kṛṣṇajaṁhasaḥ: "Of the black or dark one" (often referring to Lord Krishna).
    • śucijanmano: "Of pure mind" or "of pure consciousness."
    • raja: "Dust" or "particles."
    • ā vyadhvanaḥ: "They spread out" or "they manifest."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the essence of existence and the interconnectedness of all things.
    • It emphasizes the importance of rituals and sacrifices in connecting with the divine and the natural world.
    • The invocation of the wind symbolizes the breath of life, suggesting that life is dynamic and ever-changing.
    • The mention of "old age" and "not formed" indicates the transient nature of life and the importance of spiritual growth.
    • The "lotus" represents purity and enlightenment, often associated with spiritual awakening.
    • The skilled and pure-minded individuals are those who can navigate the complexities of life and manifest their true potential.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be reflected in the practice of mindfulness and meditation, where individuals seek to connect with their inner selves and the universe.
    • Rituals and ceremonies in various cultures, such as weddings or harvest festivals, embody the principles of sacrifice and gratitude, acknowledging the forces of nature.
    • Environmental conservation efforts can be seen as a modern-day sacrifice, where individuals and communities work to protect the earth, recognizing their interconnectedness with nature.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of understanding our place in the universe and the importance of spiritual practices in achieving harmony.
    • It encourages individuals to embrace change, recognize the transient nature of life, and strive for purity of thought and action.
    • In a world filled with distractions and chaos, the teachings of this mantra remind us to cultivate inner peace and connect with the divine through our actions and intentions.
