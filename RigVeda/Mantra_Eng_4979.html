ओकिवांसा सुत सच्चां अष्वा सप्ती इवदने | इन्द्रा nv अग्नि अवसेह वज्रीणा वयं देवाः हवामहे ||
okivāṁsā sute sacām̐ aśvā saptī ivādane | indrā nv a1gnī avaseha vajriṇā vayaṁ devā havāmahe ||
  • okivāṁsā sute sacām̐: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the divine for blessings and prosperity. The term "okivāṁsā" refers to the offspring or progeny, while "sute" signifies the act of giving or bestowing. "Sacām̐" implies purity or truthfulness.
  • aśvā saptī ivādane: Here, "aśvā" means horses, which are often symbolic of strength and speed in Vedic literature. "Saptī" refers to the number seven, which is significant in many cultures, representing completeness or perfection. "Ivādane" suggests the act of invoking or calling upon.
  • indrā nv a1gnī avaseha: "Indrā" refers to Indra, the king of gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms, symbolizing power and authority. "A1gnī" relates to Agni, the fire god, representing energy and transformation. "Avaseha" indicates the act of coming together or uniting.
  • vajriṇā vayaṁ devā havāmahe: "Vajriṇā" refers to the one who wields the thunderbolt, a title often associated with Indra. "Vayaṁ devā" translates to "we, the gods," indicating a collective invocation. "Havāmahe" means "we offer" or "we invoke," suggesting a ritualistic aspect of the mantra.

Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation that seeks blessings from the divine for prosperity, strength, and unity. It emphasizes the importance of purity and truthfulness in one's endeavors while calling upon the energies of Indra and Agni to aid in achieving success. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts such as during significant life events (like weddings or new ventures), rituals for prosperity, or even in personal meditation practices aimed at fostering strength and unity within oneself and the community. The significance lies in its ability to connect individuals with divine energies, encouraging them to strive for excellence and harmony in their lives.
