अददाऽर्भाम् महते वचस्यवे कक्षीवते वृचयाम् इन्द्र सुन्वते |मेना भवो वृषणश्वस्य सुक्रतो विश्वेत ता ते सवनेषु प्रवाच्या ||
adadā arbhām mahate vacasyave kakṣīvate vṛcayām indra sunvate | menābhavo vṛṣaṇaśvasya sukrato viśvet tā te savaneṣu pravācyā ||
  • adadā arbhām: This phrase can be interpreted as "I have given birth to the child" or "I have created the offspring." It signifies the act of creation and nurturing, which is fundamental in both human and divine contexts. In real life, this can relate to the responsibilities of parents in raising their children and ensuring their well-being.
  • mahate vacasyave: This translates to "to the great speech" or "to the powerful words." It emphasizes the importance of communication and the impact of words. In everyday life, this can be seen in how leaders, teachers, and parents use their words to inspire and guide others.
  • kakṣīvate vṛcayām: This can be understood as "to the one who is strong and powerful." It refers to strength and resilience. In a real-world context, this could relate to individuals or communities that demonstrate strength in adversity, such as athletes overcoming challenges or communities coming together during crises.
  • indra sunvate: "Indra" is often associated with power and leadership, while "sunvate" means "to be praised." This phrase highlights the importance of recognizing and honoring those who lead and protect. In modern society, this can be seen in the appreciation of leaders in various fields, such as healthcare workers during a pandemic.
  • menābhavo vṛṣaṇaśvasya: This phrase can be interpreted as "may there be abundance and prosperity." It speaks to the desire for growth and success. In real life, this can relate to personal goals, such as pursuing education or career advancement, and the collective aspirations of communities for economic development.
  • sukrato viśvet: "Sukrato" means "good deeds" and "viśvet" refers to "all." This emphasizes the importance of collective good actions. In practice, this can be seen in community service initiatives where individuals come together to help those in need, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
  • tā te savaneṣu pravācyā: This translates to "may they be spoken of in the gatherings." It highlights the significance of sharing stories and experiences in communal settings. In contemporary life, this can be reflected in storytelling events, cultural festivals, or even social media, where people share their journeys and inspire others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of creation, communication, strength, leadership, prosperity, collective good, and the importance of sharing experiences. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, from personal development to community engagement. By embodying these principles, individuals and societies can foster a more harmonious and prosperous existence, drawing inspiration from the wisdom of the RigVeda in their daily lives.
