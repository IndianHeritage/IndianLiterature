हविष् पान्तम् अजरोऽस्मिन् स्वर्विदि दिविष्पृश्य आहुतं जुष्टं अग्नौ |तस्य भरमणे भुवनाय देवाः धर्मणे कं स्वधया पप्रथन्त ||
haviṣ pāntam ajaraṁ svarvidi divispṛśy āhutaṁ juṣṭam agnau | tasya bharmaṇe bhuvanāya devā dharmaṇe kaṁ svadhayā paprathanta ||
  • haviṣ: This term refers to offerings or sacrifices made to the deities. In real life, it can be compared to the act of giving or sharing resources for a greater cause, such as donating to charity or helping those in need.
  • pāntam: This word means 'that which is directed or guided.' It signifies the importance of having a purpose or direction in life, akin to setting goals and working towards them.
  • ajaraṁ: Translated as 'immortal' or 'eternal,' this term emphasizes the idea of enduring values and principles that transcend time, similar to the legacy one leaves behind through their actions and contributions.
  • svarvidi: This refers to the knowledge of the heavens or celestial realms. It can be related to the pursuit of higher knowledge and wisdom, much like the quest for education and enlightenment in our lives.
  • divispṛśy: Meaning 'touched by the heavens,' this term suggests a connection to the divine or higher powers. In everyday life, it can be seen in moments of inspiration or when one feels a sense of purpose beyond the mundane.
  • āhutaṁ: This means 'invoked' or 'called upon.' It reflects the idea of seeking guidance or support from a higher power, similar to how individuals pray or meditate for clarity and strength in challenging times.
  • juṣṭam: Translated as 'enjoyed' or 'cherished,' this term highlights the importance of appreciating the blessings and gifts in life, akin to practicing gratitude and mindfulness.
  • agnau: Referring to 'fire,' this symbolizes transformation and purification. In real life, it can be compared to the challenges we face that ultimately lead to personal growth and development.
  • tasya: This means 'of that' or 'of it,' indicating a connection to the previous concepts discussed.
  • bharmaṇe: This term refers to 'the cosmic order' or 'dharma.' It emphasizes the importance of living in harmony with the universe and following one's duty, similar to the ethical principles that guide our actions.
  • bhuvanāya: Meaning 'for the world' or 'for all beings,' this term underscores the interconnectedness of all life and the responsibility we have towards each other and the environment.
  • devā: This refers to 'the gods' or divine beings, representing higher ideals and aspirations that guide humanity.
  • dharmaṇe: This term reiterates the concept of righteousness and moral duty, akin to the principles of justice and fairness that govern our societies.
  • kaṁ: Meaning 'who' or 'whom,' this word invites reflection on the nature of our relationships and interactions with others.
  • svadhayā: This translates to 'by one's own will' or 'self-determined,' emphasizing the importance of personal agency and responsibility in shaping our destinies.
  • paprathanta: Meaning 'they proclaimed' or 'they spread,' this term signifies the dissemination of knowledge and values, much like how ideas and teachings are shared within communities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound wisdom about the interconnectedness of life, the importance of purpose, and the pursuit of higher knowledge. It encourages individuals to live in harmony with the universe, uphold moral values, and contribute positively to the world around them. In real-life scenarios, these teachings can be applied through acts of kindness, ethical decision-making, and the continuous quest for personal and collective growth. By embodying these principles, we can create a more compassionate and enlightened society.
