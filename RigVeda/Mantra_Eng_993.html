त्वं च सोम नो वशो जीवातुं न मरामहे |प्रियस्तोत्रो वनस्पतिः ||
tvaṁ ca soma no vaśo jīvātuṁ na marāmahe | priyastotro vanaspatiḥ ||
  • tvaṁ ca soma no vaśo jīvātuṁ na marāmahe
    • tvaṁ ca - "You also" refers to the divine presence or the essence of life.
    • soma - Soma is often associated with a sacred drink that grants immortality and is symbolic of divine energy and vitality.
    • no - "Our" indicates a collective plea or invocation.
    • vaśo - "In control" or "under your influence," suggesting a desire for guidance and protection.
    • jīvātuṁ - "To live" or "to thrive," emphasizing the importance of life and existence.
    • na marāmahe - "May we not die," a plea for longevity and the avoidance of death.
  • priyastotro vanaspatiḥ
    • priyastotro - "Beloved hymn" or "dear praise," indicating a deep reverence for the subject of the mantra.
    • vanaspatiḥ - "Lord of the forest" or "tree," symbolizing nature, growth, and sustenance.

This mantra expresses a profound connection between the divine, nature, and the essence of life. It is a prayer for vitality and longevity, invoking the divine presence to guide and protect us, while also recognizing the importance of nature as a source of life and nourishment.


Real-Life Examples:

  • In daily life, individuals often seek guidance and protection through meditation or prayer, similar to the invocation in this mantra.
  • People may turn to nature for solace and rejuvenation, reflecting the reverence for vanaspatiḥ, the lord of the forest, as a source of strength and vitality.
  • In health practices, such as Ayurveda, the concept of soma can be related to the pursuit of balance and wellness, emphasizing the importance of living a healthy life.

Conclusion:

The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of the divine, nature, and human existence. It serves as a reminder to seek guidance, cherish life, and respect the natural world around us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate a deeper appreciation for life, engage in practices that promote well-being, and foster a harmonious relationship with nature.
