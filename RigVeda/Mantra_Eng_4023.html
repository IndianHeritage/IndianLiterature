विसूर्यो अमतिं न श्रीयं सादोर्वाद गावां माता जानती गात् |धनवर्णसो नद्यः खादोर्णाः स्थूणेव सुमिता दृंहता द्युः ||
vi sūryo amatiṁ na śriyaṁ sād orvād gavām mātā jānatī gāt | dhanvarṇaso nadya1ḥ khādoarṇāḥ sthūṇeva sumitā dṛṁhata dyauḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vi: apart, away
    • sūryo: sun
    • amatiṁ: not to be diminished
    • na: not
    • śriyaṁ: wealth, prosperity
    • sād: to be achieved
    • orvād: from the earth
    • gavām: of cows
    • mātā: mother
    • jānatī: knows
    • gāt: song, hymn
    • dhanvarṇaso: of the wealth
    • nadyaḥ: rivers
    • khādoarṇāḥ: of the earth
    • sthūṇeva: like a great
    • sumitā: good thoughts
    • dṛṁhata: strengthen
    • dyauḥ: sky, heaven
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the sun as a source of strength and prosperity.
    • The phrase "vi sūryo amatiṁ" suggests that the sun does not diminish in its brilliance or power, symbolizing eternal energy and vitality.
    • "Na śriyaṁ sād orvād" indicates that true wealth and prosperity are not easily attained from the earth alone, but require deeper understanding and connection.
    • The mention of "gavām mātā" refers to cows, which are often seen as symbols of nourishment and sustenance in Vedic culture, emphasizing the importance of nurturing and caring for our resources.
    • "Dhanvarṇaso nadyaḥ" connects wealth with rivers, suggesting that just as rivers flow and nourish the land, so too does wealth flow into our lives when we cultivate good thoughts and actions.
    • The concluding part "sthūṇeva sumitā dṛṁhata dyauḥ" emphasizes the need for strong, positive thoughts to strengthen our connection with the heavens and the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the mantra can be applied by recognizing the importance of sustainable practices that respect the earth and its resources, leading to long-term prosperity.
    • In personal finance, it encourages individuals to seek wealth through ethical means and to cultivate a mindset of abundance rather than scarcity.
    • In relationships, it highlights the significance of nurturing connections, much like a mother caring for her children, to foster a supportive community.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature, wealth, and human thought.
    • Its significance lies in promoting a holistic approach to life, where prosperity is achieved through respect for the environment and positive thinking.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to cultivate a mindset that values sustainability, ethical practices, and nurturing relationships, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and prosperous life.
