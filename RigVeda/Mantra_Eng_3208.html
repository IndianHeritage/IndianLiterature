मन्त्र: परि त्रिविष्ट्य अध्वरं यात्य अग्नीर रथीर इव |आ देवेषु प्रयो दधत ||
pari triviṣṭy adhvaraṁ yāty agnī rathīr iva | ā deveṣu prayo dadhat ||
  • Mantra: "pari triviṣṭy adhvaraṁ yāty agnī rathīr iva | ā deveṣu prayo dadhat ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pari: around, in all directions
    • triviṣṭy: the three worlds (heaven, earth, and the atmosphere)
    • adhvaraṁ: the sacred ritual or sacrifice
    • yāty: goes or moves
    • agnī: fire, often symbolizing energy or divine presence
    • rathīr: like a chariot
    • iva: like, as if
    • ā: towards
    • deveṣu: among the gods
    • prayo: offering, dedication
    • dadhat: placing or establishing
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the movement of fire (Agnī) as it travels through the three realms, akin to a chariot.
    • The phrase "adhvaraṁ" indicates the importance of rituals and sacrifices in connecting with the divine.
    • The mantra emphasizes the act of offering and dedication to the gods, suggesting a harmonious relationship between humans and the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is a symbol of purification and transformation. For instance, in Hindu rituals, the sacred fire (Havan) is used to make offerings to deities, symbolizing the connection between the earthly and the divine.
    • In modern life, the concept of "moving like a chariot" can be seen in the way we navigate our goals and aspirations, driven by our inner energy and purpose.
    • The idea of dedicating our actions to a higher purpose can be reflected in community service, where individuals offer their time and resources for the benefit of others, akin to a ritual of giving.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of rituals and the divine connection through offerings.
    • It teaches us that our actions, when dedicated to a higher purpose, can lead to a harmonious existence.
    • In real life, it encourages us to recognize the energy within us and to channel it towards meaningful endeavors, fostering a sense of community and spirituality.
