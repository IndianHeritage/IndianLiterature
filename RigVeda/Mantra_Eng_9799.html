सहस्रशीर्षा पुरुषः सहस्राक्षः सहस्रपात् |स भूमिं विश्वतो वृत्वा त्यातिष्ठद दशाङ्गुलम् ||
sahasraśīrṣā puruṣaḥ sahasrākṣaḥ sahasrapāt | sa bhūmiṁ viśvato vṛtvāty atiṣṭhad daśāṅgulam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahasraśīrṣā: One with a thousand heads
    • puruṣaḥ: The cosmic being or person
    • sahasrākṣaḥ: One with a thousand eyes
    • sahasrapāt: One with a thousand feet
    • sa: He
    • bhūmiṁ: The earth
    • viśvato: From all sides
    • vṛtvā: Having encompassed
    • atiṣṭhad: He stood
    • daśāṅgulam: Ten fingers in measurement
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra describes the cosmic being, often interpreted as the universal spirit or the essence of creation.
    • The phrase "sahasraśīrṣā puruṣaḥ" signifies the omnipresence and omnipotence of this being, suggesting that it encompasses all aspects of existence.
    • The mention of "sahasrākṣaḥ" and "sahasrapāt" emphasizes the all-seeing and all-moving nature of this cosmic entity, indicating that it is aware of everything and can reach everywhere.
    • The line "sa bhūmiṁ viśvato vṛtvā" illustrates how this being envelops the earth from all directions, symbolizing its foundational role in the universe.
    • The final part "atiṣṭhad daśāṅgulam" can be interpreted as the being standing in a specific measure, which may represent stability and balance in the cosmos.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we can observe the interconnectedness of all living beings, akin to the idea of a cosmic being that encompasses everything.
    • The concept of a universal spirit can be seen in various philosophies and religions, where the divine is viewed as an omnipresent force that guides and sustains life.
    • In modern science, the idea of a unified field theory in physics attempts to explain how all forces and particles are interconnected, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the unity and interconnectedness of all existence, reminding us of the larger cosmic order.
    • It encourages us to recognize the divine presence in all aspects of life, fostering a sense of respect and responsibility towards nature and each other.
    • In practical terms, this understanding can lead to a more harmonious way of living, promoting peace, sustainability, and compassion in our daily interactions.
