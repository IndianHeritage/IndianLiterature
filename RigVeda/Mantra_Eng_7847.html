त्वं विप्रस त्वं कविर मधु प्र जातमन्दसः |मदेषु सर्वधाऽसि ||
tvaṁ vipras tvaṁ kavir madhu pra jātam andhasaḥ | madeṣu sarvadhā asi ||
  • tvaṁ vipras: This phrase translates to "You are the wise one." In this context, "vipra" refers to a person who possesses knowledge and wisdom. It signifies the importance of wisdom in guiding one's actions and decisions.
  • tvaṁ kavir: Here, "kavir" means "the poet" or "the creator." This highlights the creative aspect of divinity, suggesting that the divine is the source of inspiration and creativity in the world.
  • madhu pra jātam andhasaḥ: This part can be interpreted as "from the darkness, sweetness is born." "Madhu" means sweetness, "pra jātam" means "born from," and "andhasaḥ" refers to darkness or ignorance. This line emphasizes the idea that from ignorance or challenges, something beautiful and sweet can emerge.
  • madeṣu sarvadhā asi: This translates to "You are in all forms of intoxication." "Madeṣu" refers to intoxication or ecstasy, and "sarvadhā" means "in all ways." This suggests that the divine presence can be found in all experiences, whether they are joyful or challenging.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, a wise teacher (vipra) inspires students to think critically and creatively (kavir), helping them navigate through the darkness of ignorance to discover their potential (madhu pra jātam andhasaḥ).
    • In art, an artist channels their creativity (kavir) to express emotions and ideas, transforming personal struggles (andhasaḥ) into beautiful works that resonate with others (madhu).
    • In everyday life, individuals often find joy and ecstasy (madeṣu) in various experiences, whether through love, achievement, or even overcoming adversity.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, creativity, and the transformative power of experiences. It teaches us that wisdom and creativity are essential in navigating life's challenges, and that from darkness can emerge sweetness and beauty. This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as education, art, and personal growth, reminding us to seek wisdom and embrace the divine presence in all aspects of life.
