प्र ते पूर्वाणि करणानि विप्राविद्वां आह विदुषे करांसी |यथा-यथा वृष्ण्याणि स्वगूर्तापांसी राजन नार्याविवेषीः ||
pra te pūrvāṇi karaṇāni viprāvidvām̐ āha viduṣe karāṁsi | yathā-yathā vṛṣṇyāni svagūrtāpāṁsi rājan naryāviveṣīḥ ||
  • pra te pūrvāṇi karaṇāni: This phrase translates to "for you, the ancient actions." It refers to the actions or deeds that have been performed in the past, emphasizing the importance of tradition and heritage.
  • viprāvidvām̐: This means "the wise among the learned." It highlights the role of knowledgeable individuals, often sages or scholars, who guide society with their wisdom.
  • āha viduṣe karāṁsi: This translates to "speak to the knowledgeable ones." It suggests the importance of communication and sharing knowledge among those who are learned.
  • yathā-yathā vṛṣṇyāni: This means "as the Vṛṣṇis (a clan or community) do." It indicates the significance of following the practices and traditions of a respected community.
  • svagūrtāpāṁsi: This translates to "in their own homes." It emphasizes the importance of family and community in nurturing values and traditions.
  • rājan naryāviveṣīḥ: This means "O King, do not be indifferent." It is a call to leaders to be attentive and responsive to the needs of their people.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, tradition, and community in guiding actions and decisions. It calls for leaders to be aware of their responsibilities and to engage with the knowledge of the learned.
  • Real-life Application: In modern society, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:
    • Leadership: Leaders in any field, whether in politics, business, or community organizations, should seek the counsel of knowledgeable individuals to make informed decisions.
    • Education: The mantra underscores the importance of learning from the past and valuing the wisdom of elders and scholars in shaping future generations.
    • Community Engagement: It encourages individuals to participate actively in their communities, fostering a sense of belonging and shared responsibility.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, tradition, and leadership. By valuing the wisdom of the past and engaging with learned individuals, society can navigate challenges more effectively and create a harmonious environment for all.
