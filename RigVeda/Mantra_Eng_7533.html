बोधिन्मना इद अस्तु नो वृत्रहा भूयासुतिः |श्रिणोतु शक्र आशिषम् ||
bodhinmanā id astu no vṛtrahā bhūryāsutiḥ | śṛṇotu śakra āśiṣam ||
  • Translation: The mantra translates to "May our minds be enlightened; may we be free from obstacles; may we hear the blessings of the mighty one (Indra)."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • bodhinmanā: "bodhi" means enlightenment or awakening, and "manā" refers to the mind. Together, they signify the aspiration for a clear and enlightened mind.
    • id: This is a particle that can mean "thus" or "so," indicating a desire or intention.
    • astu: This means "let it be" or "may it be," expressing a wish or hope.
    • no: This means "our," indicating a collective aspiration.
    • vṛtrahā: "vṛtra" refers to obstacles or challenges, and "hā" means to remove or overcome. Thus, it signifies the removal of obstacles.
    • bhūryāsutiḥ: "bhūrya" means "abundance" or "plenty," and "āsutiḥ" refers to blessings or boons. Together, they express a desire for abundant blessings.
    • śṛṇotu: This means "may he hear," indicating a call for attention or acknowledgment from a higher power.
    • śakra: Refers to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength and power.
    • āśiṣam: This means "blessings" or "grace," indicating a desire for divine favor.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging work environment, individuals may recite this mantra to seek clarity and enlightenment in their decision-making processes, hoping to overcome obstacles in their projects.
    • Students preparing for exams might use this mantra to focus their minds and seek blessings for success, aiming to remove distractions and enhance their learning.
    • During community gatherings or rituals, this mantra can be chanted to invoke collective strength and blessings, fostering unity and abundance among participants.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for mental clarity, the removal of obstacles, and the invocation of divine blessings. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal challenges, academic pursuits, and communal activities. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset geared towards enlightenment and resilience, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and successful life.
