यद् dyāva इन्द्र ते शतं शतम् भूमीर उता स्युः |न त्वा वज्रिन सहस्रं सूर्याऽनु न जातम् अष्ट rodasī ||
yad dyāva indra te śataṁ śatam bhūmīr uta syuḥ | na tvā vajrin sahasraṁ sūryā anu na jātam aṣṭa rodasī ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • dyāva: heaven
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • te: your
    • śataṁ: hundred
    • śatam: hundred (repeated for emphasis)
    • bhūmīr: earth
    • uta: and
    • syuḥ: may they be
    • na: not
    • tvā: you
    • vajrin: O wielder of the thunderbolt
    • sahasraṁ: thousand
    • sūryā: sun
    • anu: after
    • na: not
    • jātam: born
    • aṣṭa: eight
    • rodasī: horizons
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the king of gods, emphasizing the vastness of his power and the blessings he can bestow.
    • The repetition of "śataṁ" signifies abundance, suggesting that the speaker seeks a hundredfold blessings from both heaven and earth.
    • The phrase "na tvā vajrin sahasraṁ" indicates that no one can match Indra's might, as he is the ultimate source of strength and protection.
    • The mention of "sūryā anu" suggests that even the sun, a powerful celestial body, cannot compare to Indra's glory.
    • The term "aṣṭa rodasī" refers to the eight directions, symbolizing the all-encompassing nature of Indra's influence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of difficulty, people often seek divine intervention, much like how this mantra calls upon Indra for support and blessings.
    • In various cultures, leaders are often compared to gods, and this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the responsibilities that come with power.
    • In modern contexts, the idea of seeking abundance and protection can be applied to personal goals, such as striving for success in career or family life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine support in achieving abundance and strength in life.
    • It serves as a reminder to acknowledge the greater forces at play in our lives and to seek guidance and blessings from them.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to strive for their goals while recognizing the importance of humility and gratitude towards the divine.
