जजñिष इत्त्था गोपीथ्याय हि दधाथ तत पुरूरवो म ओजः |अशासं त्वा विदुषी सस्मिन्न अहन न म आशृणोः किम अभुग वदासि ||
jajñiṣa itthā gopīthyāya hi dadhātha tat purūravo ma ojaḥ | aśāsaṁ tvā viduṣī sasminn ahan na ma āśṛṇoḥ kim abhug vadāsi ||
  • Mantra: "jajñiṣa itthā gopīthyāya hi dadhātha tat purūravo ma ojaḥ | aśāsaṁ tvā viduṣī sasminn ahan na ma āśṛṇoḥ kim abhug vadāsi ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • jajñiṣa: born or created
    • itthā: thus or in this manner
    • gopīthyāya: for the protection of the cowherds or the guardians
    • hi: indeed
    • dadhātha: you have placed or established
    • tat: that
    • purūravo: of the many or abundant
    • ma: not
    • ojaḥ: strength or energy
    • aśāsaṁ: I do not know
    • tvā: you
    • viduṣī: wise or knowledgeable
    • sasminn: in this
    • ahan: I
    • na: not
    • ma: my
    • āśṛṇoḥ: hearing
    • kim: what
    • abhug: you have spoken
    • vadāsi: you say
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the creation and establishment of strength for the protection of the cowherds, symbolizing the nurturing aspect of life.
    • The term "gopīthyāya" refers to the guardianship of the cowherds, which can be interpreted as a metaphor for protecting the innocent and nurturing aspects of society.
    • The speaker expresses a sense of confusion or lack of understanding regarding the wise words spoken by another, indicating a quest for knowledge and clarity.
    • In real life, this can be related to the importance of seeking guidance from knowledgeable individuals in our communities, such as teachers, mentors, or elders.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the importance of strength, protection, and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • It teaches us to value the guardianship of those who nurture and protect, whether it be in our families, communities, or society at large.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can inspire individuals to take on roles of responsibility and care, ensuring that the vulnerable are protected and that wisdom is sought in times of uncertainty.
