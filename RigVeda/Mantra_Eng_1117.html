मादयस्व हरिभिर ये त इन्द्र विष्यास्व शिप्रे विषृजस्व धेनें |आ त्वा सुशिप्र हरयो वहन्तूशान हव्यानि प्रति नो जुषस्व ||
mādayasva haribhir ye ta indra vi ṣyasva śipre vi sṛjasva dhene | ā tvā suśipra harayo vahantūśan havyāni prati no juṣasva ||
  • mādayasva: This word can be translated as "may you be delighted" or "may you be joyful." It signifies a call for happiness and fulfillment.
  • haribhir: This term refers to "the divine beings" or "the gods," particularly those associated with prosperity and well-being.
  • ye: This means "who" or "those who," indicating a connection to the divine beings being addressed.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, often associated with rain, storms, and strength. He represents power and authority.
  • vi ṣyasva: This can be interpreted as "may you bestow" or "may you grant," indicating a request for blessings or gifts from the divine.
  • śipre: This word means "in the form of wealth" or "in the form of resources," suggesting a desire for material abundance.
  • vi sṛjasva: This translates to "may you create" or "may you produce," indicating a wish for the generation of prosperity and resources.
  • dhene: This term refers to "the cow" or "wealth," symbolizing nourishment and sustenance, often associated with agricultural abundance.
  • ā tvā: This means "to you," indicating a direct address to the divine beings or Indra.
  • suśipra: This translates to "well-formed" or "excellent," suggesting a desire for quality and excellence in the blessings received.
  • harayo: This refers to "the horses," which symbolize strength, speed, and vitality, often associated with success in endeavors.
  • vahantū: This means "may they carry" or "may they bring," indicating a request for the divine beings to deliver blessings.
  • śan: This term can be interpreted as "the offerings" or "the sacrifices," referring to the rituals performed to honor the gods.
  • havyāni: This word means "the offerings" or "the oblations," indicating the gifts presented to the divine beings.
  • prati no: This translates to "towards us," indicating a plea for the blessings to be directed towards the supplicants.
  • juṣasva: This means "may you enjoy" or "may you accept," indicating a desire for the divine beings to receive the offerings with favor.

Conclusion: The mantra expresses a heartfelt plea for joy, prosperity, and blessings from the divine, particularly from Indra and the other gods. It emphasizes the importance of material wealth, nourishment, and the strength to achieve success. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as during rituals for prosperity, agricultural blessings, or even in personal prayers for success in endeavors. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness between human efforts and divine blessings, encouraging individuals to seek harmony and abundance in their lives.
