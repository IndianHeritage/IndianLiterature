महो विश्वांमभि षतोऽभि हव्यानी मानवषा |अग्नि नि षत्सि नमसाधि बर्हिषि ||
maho viśvām̐ abhi ṣato3 'bhi havyāni mānuṣā | agne ni ṣatsi namasādhi barhiṣi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • maho: great, vast
    • viśvām: all, universe
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • ṣato: hundred, multitude
    • 'bhi: with, by means of
    • havyāni: offerings, oblations
    • mānuṣā: human beings, mankind
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • niṣatsi: sit down, reside
    • namasādhi: may we worship, may we honor
    • barhiṣi: in the sacred grass, in the place of worship
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the great universal energy and the divine presence of Agni, the fire god.
    • It emphasizes the importance of collective offerings made by humanity towards the divine.
    • The phrase "maho viśvām̐" suggests a connection to the vastness of the universe, indicating that our actions and offerings have a broader significance.
    • By addressing Agni, the mantra acknowledges the role of fire as a purifying and transformative force in rituals and daily life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is used in rituals to symbolize purification and transformation. For instance, in Hindu weddings, the couple circles a sacred fire, signifying their commitment and the divine witness to their vows.
    • In modern contexts, the idea of collective offerings can be seen in community service or charitable acts where individuals come together to support a cause, reflecting the essence of "havyāni mānuṣā."
    • Environmental initiatives, such as tree planting or clean-up drives, can also be viewed as offerings to the Earth, acknowledging our responsibility towards nature and the universe.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of collective human efforts in honoring the divine and the universe.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions, whether in rituals or daily life, contribute to a greater cosmic order.
    • By recognizing the importance of our offerings, we can foster a sense of community and responsibility towards each other and the environment.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to live in harmony with the universe, acknowledging the interconnectedness of all beings.
