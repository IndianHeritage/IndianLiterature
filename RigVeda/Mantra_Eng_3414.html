न त्वा वरन्ते अन्यथा यद दित्ससि स्तुतो मगहम् |स्तोत्रिभ्य इन्द्र गिर्वणः ||
na tvā varante anyathā yad ditsasi stuto magham | stotṛbhya indra girvaṇaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "na tvā varante anyathā yad ditsasi stuto magham | stotṛbhya indra girvaṇaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "O Indra, we do not choose you for any other reason than the greatness you desire; may you grant us the wealth that is sung by the worshippers."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • na: not
    • tvā: you
    • varante: choose
    • anyathā: otherwise
    • yat: that which
    • ditsasi: you desire
    • stuto: praised
    • magham: wealth or greatness
    • stotṛbhya: to the worshippers
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • girvaṇaḥ: through hymns or songs
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, who is often associated with strength, rain, and prosperity in Vedic literature.
    • The essence of the mantra emphasizes the importance of sincerity in devotion. It suggests that the worshippers are not seeking Indra for trivial reasons but are genuinely acknowledging his greatness and seeking his blessings.
    • In real life, this can be likened to individuals who seek help or guidance from leaders or mentors not for superficial gains but out of respect for their wisdom and capabilities.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In professional settings, employees may seek guidance from their managers or leaders not just for promotions or benefits but to learn and grow from their experience.
    • In personal relationships, individuals may choose to support friends or family members not for what they can gain but out of genuine love and respect.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra teaches us the value of genuine intention in our relationships and interactions. It encourages us to seek greatness and wisdom for their own sake, rather than for material or superficial rewards.
    • By embodying this principle, we can foster deeper connections and a more meaningful existence, aligning our actions with our values and the greater good.
