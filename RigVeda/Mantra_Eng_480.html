मन्त्र: असामि हि प्रयज्यवः कण्वं दद प्रचेतसः |असामिभिर मरुत आ न ऊतिभिर गन्ता वृष्टिं न विद्युतः ||
asāmi hi prayajyavaḥ kaṇvaṁ dada pracetasaḥ | asāmibhir maruta ā na ūtibhir gantā vṛṣṭiṁ na vidyutaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "asāmi hi prayajyavaḥ kaṇvaṁ dada pracetasaḥ | asāmibhir maruta ā na ūtibhir gantā vṛṣṭiṁ na vidyutaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Indeed, I am the one who offers the powerful (prayers) to the wise (deities). The Maruts (storm deities) do not come with their lightning and rain without the offerings."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • asāmi: I am
    • hi: indeed
    • prayajyavaḥ: those who offer or invoke
    • kaṇvaṁ: powerful or wise
    • dada: to give or offer
    • pracetasaḥ: to the wise or intelligent
    • asāmibhir: without offerings
    • maruta: the Maruts, storm deities
    • ā: come
    • na: not
    • ūtibhir: with their lightning
    • gantā: to go or arrive
    • vṛṣṭiṁ: rain
    • na vidyutaḥ: without lightning
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings and prayers in invoking divine forces.
    • The Maruts, representing natural phenomena like storms and rain, are depicted as dependent on human offerings for their manifestations.
    • In real life, this can be likened to the idea that our actions and intentions can influence the outcomes we desire. For example, in agriculture, farmers often perform rituals to seek rain for their crops, symbolizing the need for divine intervention in human endeavors.
    • The mantra also highlights the relationship between humans and the divine, suggesting that respect and acknowledgment of higher powers can lead to favorable outcomes.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the reciprocal relationship between humans and the divine, where offerings and prayers are essential for receiving blessings.
    • It teaches us the value of gratitude and respect towards nature and the forces that govern our lives.
    • In modern contexts, this can be applied in various ways, such as environmental conservation efforts, where acknowledging and respecting nature can lead to sustainable outcomes.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that our intentions and actions can shape our reality, encouraging us to engage positively with the world around us.
