उत तिष्ठतावा पश्यतेन्द्रस्य भागं ऋत्वियम् |यदि श्रातो जुहोतन यद्य अश्रातो ममत्तन ||
ut tiṣṭhatāva paśyatendrasya bhāgam ṛtviyam | yadi śrāto juhotana yady aśrāto mamattana ||
  • ut tiṣṭhatāva: This phrase translates to "let us rise" or "let us stand up." It signifies the importance of awakening and being alert to the spiritual and material aspects of life.
  • paśyatendrasya: This means "to see the portion of Indra." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with power, rain, and the heavens. This part emphasizes the need to recognize and understand the divine forces at play in our lives.
  • bhāgam ṛtviyam: This translates to "the share of the priestly class." It highlights the role of rituals and the importance of the priestly class in mediating between the divine and the human realms.
  • yadi śrāto juhotana: This means "if one offers with faith." It underscores the significance of intention and faith in any offering or action we undertake.
  • yady aśrāto mamattana: This translates to "if one does not offer, let it be." It suggests that if one does not have the faith or intention to offer, it is better to refrain from doing so, emphasizing authenticity in spiritual practices.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of spiritual awakening, the recognition of divine forces, and the importance of intention in our actions. It serves as a reminder that true offerings come from a place of genuine faith and understanding.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:
    • In personal growth, one can reflect on the importance of being aware of one's surroundings and the divine influences that guide us.
    • In community rituals or gatherings, it encourages participants to engage with sincerity and intention, ensuring that their contributions are meaningful.
    • In decision-making, it serves as a reminder to act with purpose and authenticity, rather than out of obligation or expectation.

  • Conclusion: The mantra teaches us the value of awareness, intention, and authenticity in our spiritual and everyday lives. By recognizing the divine influences and acting with genuine faith, we can create a more meaningful existence and foster deeper connections with ourselves and others.
