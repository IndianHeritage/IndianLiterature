अहश्च कृष्णम अहर अर्जुनं च विवर्तेते रजसी वेद्याभिः |वैश्वानरो जायमानो न राजावतिरज ज्योतीषाग्निस तमांसि ||
ahaś ca kṛṣṇam ahar arjunaṁ ca vi vartete rajasī vedyābhiḥ | vaiśvānaro jāyamāno na rājāvātiraj jyotiṣāgnis tamāṁsi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ahaś: day
    • ca: and
    • kṛṣṇam: black or dark (often associated with the divine)
    • ahar: day
    • arjunaṁ: Arjuna (a key figure in the Mahabharata, symbolizing the seeker of truth)
    • ca: and
    • vi: indeed
    • vartete: exist or manifest
    • rajasī: in the mode of passion
    • vedyābhiḥ: through knowledge or wisdom
    • vaiśvānaro: the universal fire or the divine energy
    • jāyamāno: being born or arising
    • na: not
    • rājāvātiraj: the king or ruler
    • jyotiṣāgnis: the fire of light or knowledge
    • tamāṁsi: darkness or ignorance
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the interplay of light and darkness, knowledge and ignorance, and the roles of divine energies in our lives.
    • The mention of "Arjuna" signifies the human struggle for understanding and righteousness, as he represents the seeker who is often caught in dilemmas.
    • The "vaiśvānaro" refers to the universal fire, symbolizing the divine energy that sustains life and knowledge.
    • The phrase "rajasī vedyābhiḥ" indicates that through knowledge, one can transcend the mode of passion and rise above ignorance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In our daily lives, we often face challenges that require us to seek knowledge and wisdom, much like Arjuna did on the battlefield of Kurukshetra.
    • For instance, when making important decisions, we may feel torn between our desires (rajasī) and the need for clarity (knowledge). Seeking guidance from mentors or spiritual texts can illuminate our path.
    • The concept of "vaiśvānaro" can be seen in the way we harness our inner strength and energy to overcome obstacles, akin to how fire transforms and purifies.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of knowledge and the divine energy in overcoming ignorance and confusion in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that, like Arjuna, we must seek wisdom to navigate the complexities of existence and rise above the passions that cloud our judgment.
    • In practical terms, this mantra encourages us to engage in continuous learning and self-reflection, allowing us to align ourselves with higher truths and live a more enlightened life.
