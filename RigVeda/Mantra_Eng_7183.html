यद वावन्थ पुरुष्टुत पुरा चिच्छूर नृणाम् |वयं तत त इन्द्र सम भरामसि यज्ञं उक्तं तुरं वचः ||
yad vāvantha puruṣṭuta purā cic chūra nṛṇām | vayaṁ tat ta indra sam bharāmasi yajñam ukthaṁ turaṁ vacaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • vāvantha: you (plural) have desired
    • puruṣṭuta: of the best among men
    • purā: in the past
    • cic: indeed
    • shūra: brave or valiant
    • nṛṇām: of men
    • vayaṁ: we
    • tat: that
    • ta: you (plural)
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • sam: together
    • bharāmasi: we shall bring
    • yajñam: the sacrifice
    • ukthaṁ: the utterance or hymn
    • turaṁ: swift
    • vacaḥ: words
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a collective desire for the blessings and support of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who is often associated with strength and valor.
    • The phrase "whatever you have desired" indicates a recognition of the aspirations and needs of the community.
    • By invoking Indra, the mantra emphasizes the importance of divine assistance in achieving goals, particularly in the context of sacrifices and rituals.
    • The reference to "brave men" suggests that courage and valor are essential qualities in the pursuit of one's desires.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team may come together to seek the support of their leaders (akin to Indra) to achieve a common goal, such as launching a new product.
    • In community gatherings, people often express their collective wishes for prosperity and well-being, similar to the invocation of divine blessings in the mantra.
    • During challenging times, individuals may seek strength and courage from their community or spiritual beliefs to overcome obstacles, reflecting the essence of bravery highlighted in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity, courage, and divine support in achieving collective goals.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as teamwork, community support, and personal challenges, where individuals seek strength and guidance.
    • By invoking higher powers and recognizing the aspirations of the community, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine assistance.
