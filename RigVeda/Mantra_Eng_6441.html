भद्रम् मनः कृणुष्व वृततूर्ये येन समत्सु सासहः |अव स्थिरा तनुहि भूरी शर्धतां वनेमा ते अभिष्टिभिः ||
bhadram manaḥ kṛṇuṣva vṛtratūrye yenā samatsu sāsahaḥ | ava sthirā tanuhi bhūri śardhatāṁ vanemā te abhiṣṭibhiḥ ||
  • bhadram manaḥ kṛṇuṣva: This phrase translates to "May you create a good mind." Here, "bhadram" means good or auspicious, "manaḥ" refers to the mind, and "kṛṇuṣva" means to create or make. This emphasizes the importance of cultivating a positive and constructive mindset.
  • vṛtratūrye: This term can be interpreted as "in the face of challenges." "Vṛtra" refers to obstacles or adversities, and "tūrye" suggests overcoming or transcending them. This part of the mantra encourages resilience and the ability to face difficulties with a strong mind.
  • yenā samatsu sāsahaḥ: This translates to "by which we can be united in harmony." "Yena" means by which, "samatsu" refers to being together or in harmony, and "sāsahaḥ" implies strength or support. This highlights the significance of unity and collaboration in overcoming challenges.
  • ava sthirā tanuhi: This phrase means "may our bodies be stable." "Ava" suggests a request for stability, "sthirā" means firm or steady, and "tanuhi" refers to the body. It emphasizes the need for physical stability and health as a foundation for mental and emotional well-being.
  • bhūri śardhatāṁ: This translates to "may we have abundant strength." "Bhūri" means abundant or plentiful, and "śardhatāṁ" refers to strength or vitality. This part of the mantra calls for an abundance of energy and resilience to face life's challenges.
  • vanemā te abhiṣṭibhiḥ: This means "may we attain your blessings." "Vanemā" suggests a desire to achieve or attain, "te" refers to you (often a divine entity), and "abhiṣṭibhiḥ" means blessings or auspiciousness. This signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and blessings in our endeavors.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of cultivating a positive mindset, resilience in the face of challenges, unity, physical stability, abundant strength, and the pursuit of divine blessings. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • Personal Development: Individuals can use this mantra as a daily affirmation to foster a positive mindset and resilience, especially during tough times.
  • Teamwork: In professional settings, this mantra can inspire teams to work together harmoniously, supporting each other to overcome challenges.
  • Health and Well-being: The emphasis on physical stability can remind individuals to prioritize their health through exercise, nutrition, and mental wellness practices.
  • Spiritual Growth: Seeking divine blessings can encourage individuals to engage in spiritual practices, fostering a deeper connection with their beliefs.

Overall, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of mind, body, and spirit, and its relevance in navigating the complexities of life.
