पवित्रेभिः पवमानो नृचक्षां राजा देवानामुत मार्त्यानाम् |द्विताभुवद रयिपतिः रयीणां ऋतम् भरत सुभृतं चार्व इन्दुः ||
pavitrebhiḥ pavamāno nṛcakṣā rājā devānām uta martyānām | dvitā bhuvad rayipatī rayīṇām ṛtam bharat subhṛtaṁ cārv induḥ ||
  • pavitrebhiḥ: This word translates to "by the pure ones" or "by the purifiers." It signifies the importance of purity, both in thought and action, which is essential for spiritual growth and harmony in society.
  • pavamāno: Meaning "the one who is being purified" or "the purifier," this term emphasizes the process of purification that is vital for achieving higher states of consciousness and spiritual enlightenment.
  • nṛcakṣā: This translates to "the king of men" or "the ruler among humans." It symbolizes leadership and the qualities that make a good leader, such as wisdom, compassion, and strength.
  • rājā devānām uta martyānām: This phrase means "the king of the gods and of mortals." It highlights the connection between divine authority and human governance, suggesting that true leadership is inspired by divine principles.
  • dvitā bhuvad: This can be interpreted as "may there be a second" or "may there be another." It reflects the idea of continuity and the importance of succession in leadership and wisdom.
  • rayipatī rayīṇām: This means "the lord of wealth." It signifies the importance of prosperity and abundance, not just in material terms but also in spiritual and emotional wealth.
  • ṛtam bharat: This translates to "may you uphold the truth." It emphasizes the significance of truth and righteousness in all actions and decisions, which is fundamental for a just society.
  • subhṛtaṁ: Meaning "well-nourished" or "well-maintained," this term suggests the importance of nurturing both physical and spiritual aspects of life for overall well-being.
  • cārv induḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the moon of the night." The moon often symbolizes calmness, reflection, and the nurturing aspects of life, reminding us to seek tranquility and balance.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, purity, and the pursuit of truth. It serves as a reminder that true leaders must embody purity of heart and mind, drawing inspiration from divine principles to govern wisely. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in governance, where leaders are encouraged to act with integrity and compassion, or in personal development, where individuals strive for self-purification and truthfulness in their actions. Ultimately, it highlights the interconnectedness of the divine and human realms, urging us to cultivate a life that is rich in both material and spiritual wealth.
