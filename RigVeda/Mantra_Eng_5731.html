उपायातं दाशुषे मार्त्याय रथेन वामम् अश्विना वहन्ता |युयुतम् अस्मद अनिराम अमीवां दिवा नक्तं माध्वी त्रासीतां नः ||
upāyātaṁ dāśuṣe martyāya rathena vāmam aśvinā vahantā | yuyutam asmad anirām amīvāṁ divā naktam mādhvī trāsīthāṁ naḥ ||
  • upāyātaṁ: This word can be translated as "may it come near" or "may it approach." It signifies a desire for something beneficial to come closer to us.
  • dāśuṣe: This term refers to "the one who is in need" or "the one who is seeking." It highlights the importance of recognizing those who require assistance.
  • martyāya: This word means "to the mortal" or "to the human being." It emphasizes the human condition and the challenges faced in life.
  • rathena: Translated as "by the chariot," it symbolizes the journey of life and the means by which we navigate our experiences.
  • vāmam: This means "the good" or "the auspicious." It indicates a focus on positive outcomes and beneficial actions.
  • aśvinā: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities of health and medicine in Vedic tradition, this word invokes divine assistance and healing.
  • vahantā: This means "carrying" or "bringing." It suggests the act of delivering or providing something valuable.
  • yuyutam: This term means "may we strive" or "may we engage." It encourages active participation and effort in achieving goals.
  • asmad: This translates to "our" or "us," indicating a collective identity and shared experience.
  • anirām: This word means "without fear" or "fearless." It promotes courage and resilience in the face of challenges.
  • amīvāṁ: This can be interpreted as "the enemies" or "those who oppose." It acknowledges the presence of obstacles in life.
  • divā: Meaning "by day," it represents clarity, awareness, and the pursuit of knowledge.
  • naktam: This translates to "by night," symbolizing the unknown, introspection, and the subconscious.
  • mādhvī: This word means "sweet" or "pleasant," indicating the desire for joy and happiness in life.
  • trāsīthāṁ: This means "may we be protected" or "may we be safe." It emphasizes the need for security and well-being.
  • naḥ: This translates to "us" or "our," reinforcing the collective aspect of the mantra.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a profound message about seeking divine assistance in our mortal journey. It emphasizes the importance of striving for goodness, engaging in collective efforts, and maintaining courage in the face of adversity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during challenging times when individuals or communities seek support and healing. It serves as a reminder to focus on positive actions, seek help from divine sources, and work together to overcome obstacles. Ultimately, it highlights the significance of unity, resilience, and the pursuit of a fulfilling life, both individually and collectively.
