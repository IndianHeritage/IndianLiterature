ऋतस्य बुद्धन उषसां इषण्यन वृषा मही रोडसी आ विवेश || मही मित्रस्य वरुणस्य मायां चन्द्रेव भानुं वि दधे पुरुत्रा ||
ṛtasya budhna uṣasām iṣaṇyan vṛṣā mahī rodasī ā viveśa | mahī mitrasya varuṇasya māyā candreva bhānuṁ vi dadhe purutrā ||
  • ṛtasya: This word refers to "truth" or "cosmic order." In real life, it can be related to the importance of honesty and integrity in our actions and decisions.
  • budhna: This term means "to awaken" or "to enlighten." It signifies the process of gaining knowledge and awareness, akin to how education and self-reflection can lead to personal growth.
  • uṣasām: This translates to "of the dawn." The dawn symbolizes new beginnings and opportunities, much like how each day presents a chance to start afresh.
  • iṣaṇyan: This word can be interpreted as "the one who rules" or "the powerful." It reflects the idea of leadership and responsibility, similar to how leaders in society guide and influence others.
  • vṛṣā: This means "the strong" or "the mighty." It can be associated with resilience and strength in overcoming challenges, much like athletes who train hard to achieve their goals.
  • mahī: This word means "the great" or "the vast." It can refer to the universe or the earth, reminding us of our connection to the larger world and the importance of environmental stewardship.
  • rodasī: This translates to "the two worlds" or "the heavens and the earth." It signifies the balance between different realms of existence, similar to how we must balance our personal and professional lives.
  • ā viveśa: This means "has entered" or "has come into being." It reflects the idea of manifestation, akin to how ideas and dreams can become reality through effort and determination.
  • mitrasya: This word means "of friendship" or "of allies." It emphasizes the importance of relationships and community support in achieving our goals.
  • varuṇasya: This refers to "of the cosmic order" or "of the law." It highlights the significance of adhering to moral and ethical principles in our lives.
  • māyā: This translates to "illusion" or "magic." It can be related to the complexities of life and the need to discern reality from illusion, much like navigating through misinformation in today's world.
  • candreva: This means "like the moon." The moon symbolizes calmness and reflection, reminding us to take time for introspection and peace amidst chaos.
  • bhānuṁ: This translates to "the sun" or "light." It represents knowledge and enlightenment, similar to how education illuminates our path in life.
  • vi dadhe: This means "to establish" or "to create." It signifies the act of building and nurturing, akin to how we cultivate relationships and careers.
  • purutrā: This translates to "in all directions" or "everywhere." It emphasizes the omnipresence of truth and knowledge, reminding us that wisdom can be found in various aspects of life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of truth, knowledge, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It serves as a reminder of the importance of integrity, the pursuit of enlightenment, and the value of relationships in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek truth in their actions, foster meaningful connections, and strive for personal and collective growth. By embodying these principles, we can contribute to a harmonious and balanced existence, both personally and within our communities.
