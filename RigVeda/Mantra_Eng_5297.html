आवद इन्द्रं यमुना तृत्सवश्च प्रात्र भेदं सर्वताता मुषायत् |अजासश्च शिग्रवो यक्षवश्च बलिं शीरषाणि जभ्रुर अश्व्यानी ||
āvad indraṁ yamunā tṛtsavaś ca prātra bhedaṁ sarvatātā muṣāyat | ajāsaś ca śigravo yakṣavaś ca baliṁ śīrṣāṇi jabhrur aśvyāni ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āvad: to speak or to call upon
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods, associated with rain and thunderstorms
    • yamunā: Yamuna, a sacred river in India
    • tṛtsavaś: those who are thirsty or in need
    • ca: and
    • prātra: in the morning or at dawn
    • bhedaṁ: separation or division
    • sarvatātā: all-pervading or universal
    • muṣāyat: to remove or to dispel
    • ajāsaś: those who are born or created
    • ca: and
    • śigravo: swift or quick
    • yakṣavaś: those who are powerful or strong
    • ca: and
    • baliṁ: offerings or sacrifices
    • śīrṣāṇi: heads or leaders
    • jabhrur: they have taken or seized
    • aśvyāni: those who are auspicious or beneficial
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the deity who represents strength and power, particularly in the context of rain and fertility.
    • The mention of the Yamuna river symbolizes purity and the life-giving properties of water, essential for sustenance.
    • The phrase "tṛtsavaś ca" highlights the needs of those who are thirsty, indicating a call for help and sustenance.
    • By addressing the "all-pervading" nature of the divine, the mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of unity.
    • The reference to "ajāsaś" and "yakṣavaś" suggests the importance of both creation and strength in overcoming challenges.
    • The act of offering "baliṁ" signifies the importance of gratitude and recognition of the divine forces that support life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of drought, communities often perform rituals invoking Indra to bring rain, reflecting the need for divine intervention in human affairs.
    • The Yamuna river is celebrated in festivals, where people gather to offer prayers and seek blessings for prosperity and health.
    • In personal struggles, individuals may call upon higher powers for strength and guidance, mirroring the invocation of Indra in the mantra.
    • Acts of charity and community support can be seen as modern-day offerings, where individuals come together to help those in need, embodying the spirit of "tṛtsavaś."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine support in times of need and recognizing the interconnectedness of all life.
    • It highlights the significance of gratitude and the role of community in overcoming challenges, making it applicable in various real-life scenarios.
    • By invoking the powers of nature and the divine, individuals can find strength and unity in their pursuits, fostering a sense of belonging and purpose.
