बोधा मे अस्य वचसो यविष्ट मंहिष्ठस्य प्रभृतस्य स्वधावः |पीयति त्वो अनु त्वो गृणाति वन्दारुस ते तन्वं वन्दे अग्ने ||
bodhā me asya vacaso yaviṣṭha maṁhiṣṭhasya prabhṛtasya svadhāvaḥ | pīyati tvo anu tvo gṛṇāti vandārus te tanvaṁ vande agne ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May the words of my speech be well-directed, may they be the best among the best. May I be nourished by your grace, O Agni, and may I sing your praises."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • bodhā: Knowledge or understanding.
    • me: My.
    • asya: Of this.
    • vacaso: Speech or words.
    • yaviṣṭha: Well-directed or best.
    • maṁhiṣṭhasya: Among the best.
    • prabhṛtasya: Of the one who is powerful or great.
    • svadhāvaḥ: By your grace or nourishment.
    • pīyati: Nourished or satisfied.
    • tvo: You.
    • anu: Following or in accordance with.
    • gṛṇāti: Sings or praises.
    • vandārus: O Agni, the divine fire.
    • te: Your.
    • tanvaṁ: Body or form.
    • vande: I bow or I worship.
    • agne: O Agni, the divine fire.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of clear and purposeful communication. It reflects the desire for one's words to be meaningful and impactful.
    • Agni, the fire god, symbolizes transformation and purification. By invoking Agni, the speaker seeks to align their speech with divine qualities, ensuring that their words are constructive and beneficial.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In personal relationships, this mantra can inspire individuals to communicate with clarity and intention, fostering understanding and connection.
    • In professional settings, it encourages effective communication, where one’s words can lead to positive outcomes, such as successful negotiations or team collaborations.
    • In public speaking or leadership, it serves as a reminder to use one’s voice to uplift and inspire others, promoting a culture of respect and encouragement.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the significance of our words and the impact they can have on ourselves and others.
    • By seeking the grace of Agni, we are encouraged to strive for excellence in our communication, ensuring that our speech is not only well-directed but also a source of nourishment and inspiration for those around us.
