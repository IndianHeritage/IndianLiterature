मुनयो वातरशनाः पीशङ्गा वसते मला | वातस्यानु ध्राजिं यान्ति यद देवासो अविक्षत ||
munayo vātaraśanāḥ piśaṅgā vasate malā | vātasyānu dhrājiṁ yanti yad devāso avikṣata ||
  • Munayo: This term refers to the sages or wise individuals who possess deep knowledge and understanding.
  • Vātaraśanāḥ: This can be interpreted as those who are swift like the wind, symbolizing speed and agility in thought and action.
  • Piśaṅgā: This word denotes a yellowish or tawny color, often associated with the earth or nature, suggesting a connection to the natural world.
  • Vasate: This means to dwell or reside, indicating a state of being or existence.
  • Malā: This term refers to impurities or obstacles, which can be both physical and metaphorical in nature.
  • Vātasyānu: This phrase can be understood as the following or the aftermath of the wind, suggesting the consequences of actions or thoughts.
  • Dhrājiṁ: This refers to the one who holds or supports, indicating strength and stability.
  • Yanti: This means to go or to move towards, suggesting a journey or progression.
  • Yad: This translates to "that" or "which," pointing to a specific action or state.
  • Devāso: This term refers to the divine beings or gods, symbolizing higher powers or spiritual entities.
  • Avikṣata: This means to be unshaken or undisturbed, indicating a state of calmness and resilience.

The mantra speaks to the qualities of wisdom, agility, and connection to nature, while also addressing the challenges and impurities that one may face in life. It emphasizes the importance of being grounded and stable in the face of adversity, much like the sages who navigate through life's complexities with grace and understanding.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as sages embody wisdom, individuals can strive to cultivate knowledge and understanding in their own lives, learning from experiences and challenges.
  • Resilience in Adversity: The mantra encourages individuals to remain steadfast and unshaken in the face of difficulties, much like a tree that withstands strong winds.
  • Connection to Nature: The reference to the natural world reminds us of the importance of being in tune with our environment, promoting sustainability and respect for nature.
  • Spiritual Journey: The mention of divine beings suggests a spiritual path, encouraging individuals to seek higher truths and connect with their inner selves.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, resilience, and connection to the natural and spiritual realms. It encourages individuals to navigate life's challenges with grace and to remain grounded in their values and beliefs, ultimately leading to personal growth and fulfillment.
