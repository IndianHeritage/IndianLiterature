प्र तु द्रव परि कोशं न शीदा नृभिः पुनानो अभि वाजम् अर्षा | अश्वं न त्वा वाजिनं मार्जयन्तो 'च्छा बर्ही रशनाभिर नयन्ति ||
pra tu drava pari kośaṁ ni ṣīda nṛbhiḥ punāno abhi vājam arṣa | aśvaṁ na tvā vājinam marjayanto 'cchā barhī raśanābhir nayanti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: forward, towards
    • tu: but, indeed
    • drava: flowing, liquid
    • pari: around, surrounding
    • kośaṁ: sheath, covering
    • ni: down, below
    • ṣīda: sit, reside
    • nṛbhiḥ: by men, by humans
    • punāno: purifying, cleansing
    • abhi: towards, upon
    • vājam: strength, power
    • arṣa: to be desired, worthy
    • aśvaṁ: horse, symbol of power
    • na: not
    • tvā: you
    • vājinam: charioteer, one who drives
    • marjayanto: cleansing, purifying
    • 'cchā: with, together
    • barhī: sacred grass, symbol of purity
    • raśanābhir: with ropes, bonds
    • nayanti: lead, guide
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence that is both flowing and purifying.
    • The imagery of a horse symbolizes strength and vitality, often associated with the sun and the divine.
    • The act of cleansing and guiding suggests a journey towards enlightenment and purity.
    • The mention of "men" indicates the collective human effort in seeking divine guidance and purification.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be reflected in the efforts of individuals coming together to support and uplift each other, much like a team working towards a common goal.
    • In personal life, it can be seen in the practice of meditation or yoga, where individuals seek to purify their minds and bodies, leading to a more harmonious existence.
    • In environmental efforts, the idea of cleansing and purifying can relate to initiatives aimed at restoring nature and promoting sustainability.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of collective effort in seeking purity and strength.
    • It serves as a reminder that through unity and shared purpose, individuals can achieve greater heights and foster a more profound connection with the divine.
    • In real life, it encourages us to engage in practices that promote personal and communal well-being, leading to a more balanced and fulfilling life.
