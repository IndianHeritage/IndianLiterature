मन्त्र: उता त्वा स्त्री शशीयसी पुंसो भवति वस्यसी | अदेवत्राद अराधसः ||
uta tvā strī śaśīyasī puṁso bhavati vasyasī | adevatrād arādhasaḥ ||
  • uta - This word can be translated as "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition.
  • tvā - This means "you," referring to the subject of the mantra, which is likely a divine entity or a person.
  • strī - This translates to "woman" or "female," highlighting the feminine aspect in the context of the mantra.
  • śaśīyasī - This term can be interpreted as "the one who is like the moon," suggesting beauty, calmness, and nurturing qualities associated with femininity.
  • puṁso - This means "of man" or "of the male," indicating a relationship or connection between the feminine and masculine aspects.
  • bhavati - This translates to "becomes" or "is," indicating a transformation or state of being.
  • vasyasī - This term means "is under control" or "is subdued," suggesting a sense of harmony or balance.
  • adevatrād - This can be interpreted as "from the divine," indicating a source or origin that is sacred or spiritual.
  • arādhasaḥ - This term can mean "from the lower" or "from the inferior," suggesting a contrast or a journey from a lesser state to a higher one.

The mantra speaks to the relationship between the feminine and masculine energies, emphasizing the beauty and nurturing qualities of women, akin to the moon. It suggests that through divine influence, one can achieve a state of harmony and balance, transcending lower states of being.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Gender Equality: The mantra highlights the importance of recognizing and valuing the feminine aspect in society, promoting gender equality and respect for women.
  • Personal Relationships: It can serve as a reminder of the balance needed in relationships between men and women, encouraging mutual respect and understanding.
  • Spiritual Growth: The idea of transcending lower states can be applied to personal development, motivating individuals to strive for higher consciousness and self-improvement.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the interconnectedness of masculine and feminine energies, advocating for harmony, respect, and spiritual growth. It serves as a reminder of the divine qualities present in both genders and the importance of balance in all aspects of life.
