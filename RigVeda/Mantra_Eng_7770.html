प्र-प्र क्षयाय पण्यसे जनाय जुष्टो अद्रुहे |वीत्य अर्ष चनिष्ठया ||
pra-pra kṣayāya panyase janāya juṣṭo adruhe | vīty arṣa caniṣṭhayā ||
  • pra-pra: This term can be interpreted as a call for abundance or prosperity. It signifies the desire for growth and flourishing in various aspects of life.
  • kṣayāya: This word refers to the concept of destruction or decline. In this context, it can be understood as a plea to overcome obstacles that lead to deterioration.
  • panyase: This term relates to wealth or resources. It emphasizes the importance of material and spiritual wealth in achieving a fulfilling life.
  • janāya: This word means 'for the people' or 'for humanity.' It highlights the collective aspect of prosperity and well-being.
  • juṣṭo: This term means 'enjoyed' or 'cherished.' It indicates the importance of appreciating and valuing what one has.
  • adruhe: This word translates to 'without enmity' or 'harmoniously.' It suggests the need for peaceful coexistence and mutual respect among individuals.
  • vīty: This term refers to wealth or resources, reinforcing the idea of abundance.
  • arṣa: This word can mean 'sacred' or 'divine,' indicating that the wealth and prosperity sought are not just material but also spiritual in nature.
  • caniṣṭhayā: This term suggests a state of stability or permanence, emphasizing the desire for lasting prosperity and well-being.

In summary, this mantra encapsulates a profound message about the pursuit of prosperity and well-being for oneself and for the community, while emphasizing the importance of harmony and respect. It encourages individuals to seek abundance in a way that is beneficial to all, fostering a sense of unity and collective growth.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as in community development projects where the focus is on creating sustainable resources for all.
  • In personal life, it serves as a reminder to appreciate what one has and to strive for growth without harming others, promoting a culture of gratitude and cooperation.
  • In business, it encourages ethical practices that prioritize the well-being of employees and the community, leading to a more harmonious work environment.

Ultimately, the mantra serves as a guiding principle for living a life that is not only prosperous but also harmonious, fostering a sense of responsibility towards oneself and society.
