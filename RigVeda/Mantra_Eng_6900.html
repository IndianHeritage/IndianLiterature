युवानं विश्वतिं कविं विश्वादम पुरुवेपसम् |अग्निं शुम्भामि मन्मभिः ||
yuvānaṁ viśpatiṁ kaviṁ viśvādam puruvepasam | agniṁ śumbhāmi manmabhiḥ ||
  • yuvānaṁ: This word translates to "young" or "youthful." It signifies vitality, energy, and the potential for growth and development.
  • viśpatiṁ: This means "lord of all" or "master of the universe." It denotes a figure of authority and leadership, someone who governs and guides.
  • kaviṁ: This translates to "poet" or "sage." It represents wisdom, creativity, and the ability to perceive deeper truths about life and existence.
  • viśvādam: This word means "all-encompassing" or "universal." It reflects the idea of inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • puruvepasam: This can be interpreted as "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is worshipped." It indicates a sense of reverence and devotion.
  • agniṁ: This word means "fire." In Vedic literature, fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence. It is often associated with sacrifice and offerings.
  • śumbhāmi: This translates to "I invoke" or "I call upon." It expresses the act of seeking connection with the divine or higher powers.
  • manmabhiḥ: This means "with my thoughts" or "with my mind." It emphasizes the importance of intention and mental focus in the act of invocation.

The mantra can be understood as a call to the youthful, wise, and universal lord, invoking the divine fire with focused thoughts. It reflects a desire for guidance, inspiration, and transformation in life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the youthful energy signifies potential, individuals can use this mantra to inspire themselves to pursue their goals and aspirations with vigor and creativity.
  • Leadership: The concept of "viśpatiṁ" encourages individuals to take on leadership roles in their communities, guiding others with wisdom and inclusivity.
  • Creativity: The invocation of "kaviṁ" can inspire artists, writers, and thinkers to tap into their creative potential and express deeper truths through their work.
  • Spiritual Practice: The act of invoking "agniṁ" can be a part of rituals and meditative practices, symbolizing the purification of the mind and spirit.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the significance of youth, wisdom, and the divine fire in our lives. It serves as a reminder to harness our inner potential, lead with compassion, and seek transformation through focused intention. By invoking these qualities, we can navigate our personal and collective journeys with purpose and clarity.
