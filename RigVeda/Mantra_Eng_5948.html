देवहितिं जुगुपुर द्वादशस्य ऋतुं नरो न प्र मिनन्त्य एते |संपत्सरे प्रावृष्ट्य आगतायां तप्ता घर्मा अशनुवते विसर्गम् ||
devahitiṁ jugupur dvādaśasya ṛtuṁ naro na pra minanty ete | saṁvatsare prāvṛṣy āgatāyāṁ taptā gharmā aśnuvate visargam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • devahitiṁ: Divine order or command
    • jugupur: They have established or created
    • dvādaśasya: Of the twelve (months or cycles)
    • ṛtuṁ: Seasons or periods
    • naro: Man or human beings
    • na: Not
    • pra minanty: They do not diminish or decrease
    • ete: These
    • saṁvatsare: In the year
    • prāvṛṣy: During the rainy season
    • āgatāyāṁ: Having arrived or come
    • taptā: Heated or warmed
    • gharmā: Heat or warmth
    • aśnuvate: They experience or enjoy
    • visargam: Release or liberation
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the divine order that governs the cycles of nature, particularly the seasons.
    • It emphasizes that human beings do not diminish the essence of these cycles, indicating a harmony between humanity and nature.
    • The reference to the rainy season suggests a time of renewal and abundance, where the heat of the previous season gives way to the nourishing rains.
    • In real life, this can be seen in agricultural practices where farmers rely on the seasonal cycles for planting and harvesting crops.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of respecting and understanding the natural cycles that govern life.
    • It serves as a reminder that human actions should align with these cycles to ensure sustainability and harmony.
    • In modern contexts, this can be applied to environmental conservation efforts, where understanding seasonal changes can lead to better resource management.
    • Additionally, it encourages individuals to reflect on their relationship with nature and the importance of maintaining balance in their lives.
