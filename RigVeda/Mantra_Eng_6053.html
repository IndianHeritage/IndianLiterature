इमा उ त्वा पुरूवसो गिरो वर्धन्तु या मम |पावकवर्णाः शुचयो विपश्चितोऽभि स्तोमैर अनूषत ||
imā u tvā purūvaso giro vardhantu yā mama | pāvakavarṇāḥ śucayo vipaścito 'bhi stomair anūṣata ||
  • imā u tvā purūvaso giro vardhantu yā mama
    • imā - This word translates to "these" or "this." It indicates a focus on the present or immediate context.
    • u - This is a conjunction meaning "and" or "also," suggesting a connection or addition.
    • tvā - This means "you," addressing a divine or revered entity.
    • purūvaso - This term refers to "the many" or "the abundant," often associated with the divine or celestial beings.
    • giro - This translates to "words" or "speech," indicating the power of communication and expression.
    • vardhantu - This means "may they increase" or "may they grow," expressing a desire for growth and enhancement.
    • yā mama - This translates to "that is mine," indicating a personal connection or ownership of the words or blessings being invoked.
  • pāvakavarṇāḥ śucayo vipaścito 'bhi stomair anūṣata
    • pāvakavarṇāḥ - This term means "those who are of pure color" or "radiant," often referring to purity and divinity.
    • śucayo - This translates to "pure" or "sacred," emphasizing the importance of purity in thought and action.
    • vipaścito - This means "wise" or "intelligent," referring to those who possess knowledge and insight.
    • 'abhi - This is a prefix meaning "towards" or "for," indicating direction or purpose.
    • stomair - This translates to "hymns" or "praises," referring to the act of worship and reverence.
    • anūṣata - This means "may they be invoked" or "may they be called upon," suggesting a plea for divine presence and support.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine blessings through pure and wise words. It highlights the connection between the speaker and the divine, suggesting that through sincere and sacred communication, one can seek growth and enlightenment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • Prayer and Meditation: Individuals can use this mantra during their spiritual practices to seek clarity and wisdom.
  • Public Speaking: The emphasis on pure and wise words can inspire speakers to communicate effectively and meaningfully.
  • Personal Growth: The desire for growth and purity can motivate individuals to strive for self-improvement and ethical living.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of words and the importance of purity and wisdom in our thoughts and actions.
