पृच्छामि त्वा परम अन्तं पृथिव्याः पृच्छामि यत्र भुवनस्य नाभिः |पृच्छामि त्वा वृष्णो अश्वस्य रेतः पृच्छामि वाचः परमं व्योम ||
pṛcchāmi tvā param antam pṛthivyāḥ pṛcchāmi yatra bhuvanasya nābhiḥ | pṛcchāmi tvā vṛṣṇo aśvasya retaḥ pṛcchāmi vācaḥ paramaṁ vyoma ||
  • pṛcchāmi tvā param antam pṛthivyāḥ: This phrase translates to "I ask you about the ultimate end of the earth." Here, the speaker is inquiring about the deepest truths and the ultimate purpose of existence as it relates to the earth.
  • pṛcchāmi yatra bhuvanasya nābhiḥ: This means "I ask you where the navel of the universe is." The 'navel' symbolizes the center or the source of creation, suggesting a quest for understanding the origin of life and the universe.
  • pṛcchāmi tvā vṛṣṇo aśvasya retaḥ: This translates to "I ask you about the essence of the horse of Vṛṣṇa." The horse is often a symbol of strength and vitality, and in this context, it may represent the life force or energy that drives existence.
  • pṛcchāmi vācaḥ paramaṁ vyoma: This means "I ask you about the supreme word of the sky." Here, 'vyoma' refers to the sky or the cosmos, and 'vācaḥ' signifies the ultimate truth or knowledge that transcends earthly existence.

  • Significance: This mantra reflects a deep philosophical inquiry into the nature of existence, the universe, and the ultimate truths that govern life. It emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and understanding the interconnectedness of all things.
  • Real-life Application: In today's world, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue knowledge and wisdom in various fields, such as science, philosophy, and spirituality. For example, scientists exploring the origins of the universe or philosophers contemplating the meaning of life embody the spirit of this inquiry.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of asking profound questions and seeking deeper understanding. It encourages individuals to look beyond the surface of existence and strive for enlightenment, making it applicable in personal growth, education, and spiritual practices.
