अध त्यं द्रप्सं विभ्वं विचक्षणं विराभरद इषितः श्येनो अध्वरे |यदी विश्वो वृणते दसमं आर्याः अग्निं होतारं अध धीर अजायत ||
adha tyaṁ drapsaṁ vibhvaṁ vicakṣaṇaṁ vir ābharad iṣitaḥ śyeno adhvare | yadī viśo vṛṇate dasmam āryā agniṁ hotāram adha dhīr ajāyata ||
  • Adha tyaṁ drapsaṁ vibhvaṁ vicakṣaṇaṁ vir ābharad iṣitaḥ śyeno adhvare:
    • Adha: This word means "down" or "below," indicating a grounding or foundational aspect.
    • Tyaṁ: Refers to "that" or "this," pointing to something specific.
    • Drapsaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is to be worshipped" or "the one who is to be revered."
    • Vibhvaṁ: Means "wealthy" or "abundant," suggesting a connection to prosperity.
    • Vicakṣaṇaṁ: Translates to "wise" or "perceptive," indicating intelligence and insight.
    • Vir: Refers to "hero" or "brave one," symbolizing strength and valor.
    • Ābharad: Means "brought forth" or "carried," suggesting the act of bringing something into existence.
    • Iṣitaḥ: This word means "commanded" or "desired," indicating intention and purpose.
    • Śyeno: Refers to "the eagle," a symbol of power and vision.
    • Adhvare: Means "sacrifice" or "ritual," indicating a spiritual or religious context.
  • Yadī viśo vṛṇate dasmam āryā agniṁ hotāram adha dhīr ajāyata:
    • Yadī: Means "if," introducing a conditional statement.
    • Viśo: Refers to "the community" or "the people," emphasizing collective action.
    • Vṛṇate: Means "chooses" or "selects," indicating a decision-making process.
    • Dasmam: Refers to "the tenth," which can symbolize completeness or a full cycle.
    • Āryā: Means "noble" or "honorable," suggesting a high moral standard.
    • Agniṁ: Refers to "fire," a central element in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and transformation.
    • Hotāram: Means "the one who offers" or "the priest," indicating a role in the ritual.
    • Adha: Again means "down" or "below," reinforcing the grounding aspect.
    • Dhīr: Means "wise" or "intelligent," emphasizing the importance of wisdom.
    • Ajāyata: Means "was born" or "came into being," indicating the manifestation of something new.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, community, and the sacred act of sacrifice. It highlights the role of a wise leader or hero who brings forth abundance and prosperity through intentional actions. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as leadership in communities, decision-making processes in organizations, and the importance of rituals in maintaining cultural heritage. The mantra serves as a reminder that collective wisdom and noble intentions can lead to the birth of new opportunities and prosperity, much like how a community comes together to support a common cause or project.
