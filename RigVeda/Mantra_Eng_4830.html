इन्द्रः सुत्रामां स्ववाम् अवोभिः सुमृळीको भवतु विश्ववेदाः | बाधतां द्वेषो अभयं कृतोतु सुवीर्यस्य पतयः स्याम ||
indraḥ sutrāmā svavām̐ avobhiḥ sumṛḻīko bhavatu viśvavedāḥ | bādhatāṁ dveṣo abhayaṁ kṛṇotu suvīryasya patayaḥ syāma ||
  • Indraḥ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Sutrāmā: This term can be interpreted as "one who is connected" or "one who binds together," indicating unity and coherence.
  • Svavām̐: Translates to "my own," suggesting a personal connection or ownership.
  • Avobhiḥ: Means "with the help of," indicating reliance on divine support or guidance.
  • Sumṛḻīko: This word can be understood as "prosperous" or "well-being," emphasizing the importance of health and success.
  • Bhavatu: Translates to "may it be," expressing a wish or desire for a certain outcome.
  • Viśvavedāḥ: Refers to "all-knowing" or "universal knowledge," highlighting the pursuit of wisdom and understanding.
  • Bādhatāṁ: Means "may it be removed," indicating a desire to eliminate negativity or obstacles.
  • Dveṣo: Translates to "hatred," representing negative emotions that can hinder progress.
  • Abhayaṁ: Means "fearlessness," suggesting the importance of courage and confidence in facing challenges.
  • Kṛṇotu: Translates to "may it create," indicating a proactive approach to manifesting positive outcomes.
  • Suvīryasya: Refers to "of the strong," emphasizing the value of strength and resilience.
  • Patayaḥ: Means "leaders" or "guides," indicating the role of leadership in achieving goals.
  • Syāma: Translates to "may we be," expressing a collective aspiration for unity and strength.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, strength, and the removal of negativity in our lives. It calls for divine support in achieving prosperity and wisdom while fostering an environment free from hatred and fear.
  • Real-Life Application: In a workplace setting, this mantra can inspire teamwork and collaboration. By promoting a culture of support and understanding, employees can work together more effectively, overcoming challenges and achieving common goals. In personal life, it encourages individuals to cultivate positive relationships and face adversities with courage.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity, the importance of wisdom, and the need to eliminate negativity. By embodying these principles, individuals and communities can create a harmonious and prosperous environment, leading to overall well-being and success.
