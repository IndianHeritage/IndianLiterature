त्वेषं रूपं कृतुते वर्णो अस्य स यत्राशयत समृतā सेधति श्रिधः |अप्सा याती स्वधया दैव्यम् जनं सं सुश्रुतī नासते सं गोअग्रया ||
tveṣaṁ rūpaṁ kṛṇute varṇo asya sa yatrāśayat samṛtā sedhati sridhaḥ | apsā yāti svadhayā daivyaṁ janaṁ saṁ suṣṭutī nasate saṁ goagrayā ||
  • tveṣaṁ rūpaṁ kṛṇute: This phrase can be translated as "Your form creates." It signifies the divine ability to manifest various forms and shapes. In real life, this can be seen in the way individuals adapt their personalities and appearances in different situations, much like an actor taking on different roles.
  • varṇo asya: This means "the color of this." It refers to the essence or nature of something. In a practical sense, this can relate to how people perceive and interpret the world around them, influenced by their backgrounds and experiences, akin to how different cultures have unique interpretations of art and beauty.
  • sa yatrāśayat: Translated as "where it resides," this phrase emphasizes the importance of a place or state of being. In everyday life, this can be related to finding one's true self or purpose, similar to how individuals seek fulfillment in their careers or personal lives.
  • samṛtā sedhati sridhaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is remembered and who brings prosperity." It highlights the value of legacy and the impact one has on others. For example, influential leaders or mentors who inspire and uplift those around them leave a lasting impression, much like historical figures who are celebrated for their contributions.
  • apsā yāti svadhayā: This translates to "the divine essence flows." It suggests a connection to a higher power or spiritual energy. In real life, this can be seen in moments of inspiration or creativity, where individuals feel a surge of motivation that seems to come from beyond themselves, such as artists or writers experiencing a creative breakthrough.
  • daivyaṁ janaṁ: Meaning "divine people," this phrase refers to those who embody divine qualities. In practice, this can relate to individuals who exhibit kindness, compassion, and wisdom, serving as role models in society, like community leaders or activists who work for the greater good.
  • saṁ suṣṭutī nasate: This can be interpreted as "the one who is praised." It emphasizes the importance of recognition and appreciation in human relationships. In real life, this can be seen in how acknowledgment and gratitude can strengthen bonds, such as in workplaces where employees feel valued for their contributions.
  • saṁ goagrayā: This phrase translates to "with great strength." It signifies the power of unity and collective effort. In real life, this can be observed in community initiatives or movements where individuals come together to achieve a common goal, such as environmental activism or social justice campaigns.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine manifestation, the importance of legacy, and the power of unity. It serves as a reminder of the potential within each individual to create, inspire, and uplift others. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, from personal growth and self-discovery to community building and social change. By embodying these qualities, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
