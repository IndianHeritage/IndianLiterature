युयम् अस्मभ्यं धिषणाभ्यस परि विद्वांसो विश्व नर्याणि भोजना |द्यमन्तं वाजं वृषशुष्मम् उत्तमम् आ नो रयिम ऋभवस् तक्षतā वयः ||
yūyam asmabhyaṁ dhiṣaṇābhyas pari vidvāṁso viśvā naryāṇi bhojanā | dyumantaṁ vājaṁ vṛṣaśuṣmam uttamam ā no rayim ṛbhavas takṣatā vayaḥ ||
  • yūyam asmabhyaṁ - "You (plural) are for us": This phrase emphasizes the collective nature of the community, suggesting that the blessings or offerings are directed towards a group rather than an individual. In real life, this can be seen in community gatherings or collective prayers where the focus is on the well-being of all.
  • dhiṣaṇābhyas - "from the place of wisdom": This refers to the source of knowledge and understanding. It highlights the importance of seeking guidance from wise individuals or traditions. For example, in modern education, students often seek mentorship from experienced teachers to gain insights.
  • pari vidvāṁso - "those who are knowledgeable": This phrase acknowledges the presence of learned individuals who possess wisdom. In today's context, this can relate to experts in various fields, such as scientists or philosophers, whose knowledge benefits society.
  • viśvā naryāṇi bhojanā - "all beings are nourished": This signifies the interconnectedness of all life forms and the importance of sustenance. In real life, this can be reflected in sustainable practices that ensure food security for all, such as community gardens or cooperative farming.
  • dyumantaṁ vājaṁ - "brilliant wealth": This phrase refers to prosperity and abundance that shines brightly. In practical terms, this can be seen in successful businesses or initiatives that create wealth for communities, such as social enterprises that focus on ethical practices.
  • vṛṣaśuṣmam uttamam - "the best of the best": This emphasizes striving for excellence in all endeavors. In real life, this can be applied in various fields, such as sports, where athletes aim for peak performance, or in arts, where creators seek to produce their finest work.
  • ā no rayim - "may we attain wealth": This is a prayer for prosperity and success. In contemporary life, this can be related to personal goals, such as career aspirations or financial stability, where individuals work towards achieving their dreams.
  • ṛbhavas takṣatā vayaḥ - "may the divine grant us strength": This phrase calls for divine support in achieving one's goals. In real life, this can be seen in the practice of seeking blessings or guidance through rituals, meditation, or prayer, especially during challenging times.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of community, wisdom, nourishment, prosperity, excellence, and divine support. Its significance lies in the reminder that individual success is intertwined with the well-being of the community. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire collective efforts towards sustainable living, the pursuit of knowledge, and the importance of seeking guidance from the wise. It encourages individuals to strive for excellence while recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings, ultimately fostering a harmonious and prosperous society.
