अभि सुवानास इन्दवो वृष्टयः पृथिवीम् इव | इन्द्रं सोमासो अक्षरन् ||
abhi suvānāsa indavo vṛṣṭayaḥ pṛthivīm iva | indraṁ somāso akṣaran ||
  • Mantra: "abhi suvānāsa indavo vṛṣṭayaḥ pṛthivīm iva | indraṁ somāso akṣaran ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • suvānāsa: well-formed, auspicious
    • indavo: of the Indra, the king of gods
    • vṛṣṭayaḥ: rains, showers
    • pṛthivīm: the earth
    • iva: like, as if
    • indraṁ: Indra, the deity
    • somāso: those who partake of Soma (a sacred drink)
    • akṣaran: imperishable, eternal
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, emphasizing the importance of auspiciousness and prosperity.
    • The phrase "abhi suvānāsa indavo vṛṣṭayaḥ" suggests a call for beneficial rains that nourish the earth, akin to how a farmer prays for rain to ensure a good harvest.
    • The comparison "pṛthivīm iva" indicates that the rains are as vital to the earth as the earth is to life itself, highlighting the interconnectedness of nature.
    • The mention of "indraṁ somāso akṣaran" signifies the eternal nature of Indra and the blessings that come from partaking in sacred rituals, which can be likened to how communities come together to celebrate harvest festivals, invoking divine blessings for abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Farmers often perform rituals and prayers during the monsoon season, seeking the favor of deities like Indra for timely and sufficient rainfall, which is crucial for their crops.
    • In many cultures, the act of sharing food and drink during festivals symbolizes community bonding and gratitude towards nature, reflecting the essence of "somāso" in the mantra.
    • Environmental movements today echo the sentiment of this mantra, advocating for the protection of nature and the importance of sustainable practices to ensure the earth remains fertile and vibrant.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the significance of divine blessings in ensuring prosperity and harmony in nature.
    • It serves as a reminder of our responsibility towards the environment and the importance of rituals that foster community and gratitude.
    • In contemporary life, the principles of this mantra can be applied in sustainable agriculture, environmental conservation, and community celebrations that honor the earth and its resources.
