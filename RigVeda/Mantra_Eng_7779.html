हिन्वानासो रथा इव दधन्विरे गभस्त्योः |भरासः कारीणाम इव ||
hinvānāso rathā iva dadhanvire gabhastyoḥ | bharāsaḥ kāriṇām iva ||
  • Mantra: "hinvānāso rathā iva dadhanvire gabhastyoḥ | bharāsaḥ kāriṇām iva ||"
  • Translation: "Like the horses of a chariot, they who are endowed with strength, are capable of great deeds."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • hinvānāso: Refers to those who are endowed with strength or power.
    • rathā: Means chariot, symbolizing movement, progress, and the journey of life.
    • dadhanvire: Indicates capability or strength, often associated with warriors or those who can achieve great feats.
    • gabhastyoḥ: Refers to the brilliance or light, often symbolizing knowledge or enlightenment.
    • bharāsaḥ: Denotes the essence of carrying or bearing burdens, suggesting responsibility.
    • kāriṇām: Refers to those who are doers or performers, emphasizing action and effort.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team working together can be likened to a chariot, where each member (the horses) contributes their strength to achieve a common goal.
    • In sports, a well-coordinated team exemplifies this mantra, where each player’s strength and capability lead to victory.
    • In personal development, individuals harnessing their inner strength and capabilities can overcome challenges, much like the horses pulling a chariot forward.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, capability, and teamwork in achieving success. It serves as a reminder that just as horses pull a chariot, individuals must work together, leveraging their strengths to navigate the journey of life. This principle is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, highlighting the significance of collaboration and shared responsibility in achieving collective goals.
