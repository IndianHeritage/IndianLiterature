प्र पूषणं वृणीमहे युज्याय पुरूवसुम् |स शक्र शिक्ष पुरुहूत नो धियā तुजे राये विमोचन ||
pra pūṣaṇaṁ vṛṇīmahe yujyāya purūvasum | sa śakra śikṣa puruhūta no dhiyā tuje rāye vimocana ||
  • pra pūṣaṇaṁ vṛṇīmahe: This phrase translates to "We invoke the nourishing one." Here, "pūṣaṇa" refers to a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity. In real life, this can be likened to seeking abundance in our lives, whether it be in terms of food, wealth, or emotional support.
  • yujyāya purūvasum: This means "for the union of the many." The term "purūvasum" can be interpreted as the collective or community. This emphasizes the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving common goals. For example, in a workplace, teamwork is essential for success, and this mantra encourages us to work together harmoniously.
  • sa śakra śikṣa: This translates to "may he, the powerful one, teach us." "Śakra" refers to a powerful deity, often associated with strength and authority. This part of the mantra highlights the value of learning from those who are knowledgeable and experienced. In real life, this can be seen in mentorship relationships where individuals seek guidance from experts in their fields.
  • puruhūta no dhiyā: This means "with wisdom, may he come to us." Here, "dhiyā" refers to wisdom or intellect. This emphasizes the importance of seeking wisdom in our decisions and actions. For instance, in personal relationships, making wise choices can lead to healthier and more fulfilling connections.
  • tuje rāye vimocana: This translates to "liberation for you, O wealth." The term "vimocana" signifies liberation or freedom, while "rāye" refers to wealth or prosperity. This part of the mantra suggests that true wealth is not just material but also involves freedom from limitations and obstacles. In a practical sense, this can relate to financial independence, where individuals strive to free themselves from debt and financial stress.

Conclusion: The mantra "pra pūṣaṇaṁ vṛṇīmahe yujyāya purūvasum | sa śakra śikṣa puruhūta no dhiyā tuje rāye vimocana ||" encapsulates the essence of seeking nourishment, unity, wisdom, and liberation. Its significance lies in its call for collaboration and learning from powerful sources to achieve prosperity and freedom in various aspects of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to work together towards common goals, seek guidance from mentors, and strive for a balanced and liberated existence. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous society.
