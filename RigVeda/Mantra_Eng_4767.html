आ ते वृषन् वृषणो द्रोणम् अस्तुर्घृतप्रुषो नर्मयो मदन्तः | इन्द्र प्र तुभ्यं वृषभिः सुतानां वृष्णे भरन्ति वृषभाय सोमम् ||
ā te vṛṣan vṛṣaṇo droṇam asthur ghṛtapruṣo normayo madantaḥ | indra pra tubhyaṁ vṛṣabhiḥ sutānāṁ vṛṣṇe bharanti vṛṣabhāya somam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā te: To you
    • vṛṣan: O strong one
    • vṛṣaṇo: Of the strong
    • droṇam: The vessel or container
    • asthur: May it be filled
    • ghṛtapruṣo: With clarified butter
    • normayo: With the essence of the gods
    • madantaḥ: With intoxication or joy
    • indra: O Indra, the king of gods
    • pra: For
    • tubhyaṁ: You
    • vṛṣabhiḥ: With strong ones
    • sutānāṁ: Of the offerings
    • vṛṣṇe: O Vṛṣṇa, the divine
    • bharanti: They carry or bring
    • vṛṣabhāya: To the strong one
    • somam: The soma, a sacred drink
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of the gods, asking for strength and abundance.
    • The reference to "vṛṣan" and "vṛṣaṇo" emphasizes the qualities of strength and power, which are essential in overcoming challenges.
    • The mention of "ghṛtapruṣo" indicates the offering of clarified butter, symbolizing purity and nourishment, which is often used in rituals to invoke divine blessings.
    • "Normayo madantaḥ" suggests a state of joy and intoxication, which can be interpreted as a spiritual high or bliss that comes from divine connection.
    • The phrase "bharanti vṛṣabhāya somam" signifies the act of bringing forth the sacred soma, representing the essence of life and vitality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the pursuit of strength and resilience in the face of adversity, such as overcoming personal challenges or achieving professional goals.
    • The act of offering clarified butter in rituals can be likened to the importance of nourishing our bodies and minds with healthy choices, promoting well-being.
    • The joy and intoxication mentioned can be seen in moments of celebration, such as weddings or festivals, where community and divine blessings are sought.
    • Bringing forth the essence of life can be related to the importance of mindfulness and living in the present, appreciating the small joys that life offers.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of strength, nourishment, and joy in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek divine blessings and to cultivate resilience and positivity in our daily experiences.
    • By invoking the qualities of Indra and the essence of soma, we can aspire to lead fulfilling lives, grounded in strength and joy.
    • Ultimately, this mantra encourages us to connect with the divine and to recognize the abundance that life has to offer.
