यस्य ते अग्नि अन्ये अग्नय उपक्षितो वयाः इव |विपो न द्युम्ना न युवे जनानां तव क्षत्राणि वर्धयन ||
yasya te agne anye agnaya upakṣito vayā iva | vipo na dyumnā ni yuve janānāṁ tava kṣatrāṇi vardhayan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • te: your
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • anye: others
    • agnaya: to the fires
    • upakṣito: having been nourished
    • vayā: like the wind
    • iva: as
    • vipo: may we be strong
    • na: not
    • dyumnā: by glory
    • ni: in
    • yuve: you
    • janānāṁ: of the people
    • tava: your
    • kṣatrāṇi: powers
    • vardhayan: increasing
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Agni, the fire god, and acknowledges his role in nourishing and empowering others.
    • The phrase "like the wind" suggests the omnipresence and vital energy of Agni, similar to how the wind is essential for life.
    • The mantra expresses a desire for strength and glory, not just for oneself but for the community, emphasizing collective growth and empowerment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader may embody the qualities of Agni by nurturing their team, fostering an environment where everyone can thrive and grow.
    • In community service, individuals who work together to uplift their neighborhood reflect the essence of this mantra, as they collectively increase the strength and well-being of their community.
    • In personal relationships, supporting friends and family in their endeavors can be seen as a way of nourishing their 'fires' and helping them achieve their goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of nurturing and empowering others while seeking strength and glory not just for oneself but for the collective.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as leadership, community building, and personal relationships, where the focus is on mutual growth and support.
    • By embodying the spirit of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and empowered society.
