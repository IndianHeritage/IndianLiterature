रुवति भीमो वृषभस तविषयया श्रिङ्गे शिशानो हरिणी विचक्षणः |आ योनिं सोमः सुक्रितं न शीदति गव्ययी त्वग भवति निर्णिग अव्ययी ||
ruvati bhīmo vṛṣabhas taviṣyayā śṛṅge śiśāno hariṇī vicakṣaṇaḥ | ā yoniṁ somaḥ sukṛtaṁ ni ṣīdati gavyayī tvag bhavati nirṇig avyayī ||
  • Ruvati bhīmo vṛṣabhas taviṣyayā: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the powerful and mighty forces of nature. The term "bhīmo" signifies strength and might, while "vṛṣabhas" refers to a bull, symbolizing vigor and fertility. In real life, this can be related to the strength found in natural elements, such as the resilience of a mountain or the power of a river.
  • śṛṅge śiśāno hariṇī vicakṣaṇaḥ: Here, "śṛṅge" refers to the horns, which can symbolize protection and strength. "Hariṇī" means doe or female deer, representing grace and gentleness. "Vicakṣaṇaḥ" indicates wisdom or insight. This part of the mantra suggests a balance between strength and gentleness, akin to how a wise leader must balance authority with compassion in their governance.
  • ā yoniṁ somaḥ sukṛtaṁ ni ṣīdati: "Yoni" refers to the source or origin, while "somaḥ" is often associated with the moon or a divine nectar. "Sukṛtaṁ" means good deeds or actions. This line emphasizes the importance of nurturing good actions that stem from a pure source. In everyday life, this can be seen in how individuals or communities strive to create positive environments through acts of kindness and charity.
  • gavyayī tvag bhavati nirṇig avyayī: "Gavyayī" refers to the cow, a symbol of nourishment and sustenance in Vedic culture. "Tvag" means skin or covering, which can symbolize protection. "Nirṇig" suggests something that is unchanging or eternal. This part of the mantra highlights the idea of enduring nourishment and protection that comes from nature and the divine. In practical terms, this can relate to how we rely on natural resources for sustenance and the importance of preserving them for future generations.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of strength, wisdom, nurturing, and the balance between power and gentleness. Its significance lies in the reminder that true strength is not just about might but also about wisdom and compassion. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as leadership, community building, and environmental stewardship. By embodying these principles, individuals and societies can foster a harmonious existence that respects both the power of nature and the need for kindness and understanding among people.
