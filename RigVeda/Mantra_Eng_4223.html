वयं मित्रस्यावसि स्याम सप्रथस्तमे |अनेहसस्त्वोतयः सत्रा वरुणशेषसः ||
vayam mitrasyāvasi syāma saprathastame | anehasas tvotayaḥ satrā varuṇaśeṣasaḥ ||
  • Vayam mitrasyāvasi syāma saprathastame: This phrase can be translated to "May we be united with the friend (Mitra) in the best of ways." Here, "vayam" means "we," "mitrasyā" refers to "of the friend," and "syāma" implies "may we be." The term "saprathastame" suggests "in the best or most excellent manner." This emphasizes the importance of friendship and unity in achieving common goals.
  • Anehasas tvotayaḥ satrā varuṇaśeṣasaḥ: This part translates to "May we be free from obstacles and may the divine Varuna protect us." "Anehasas" means "without obstacles," "tvotayaḥ" refers to "may you protect," and "satrā" signifies "in the assembly or gathering." "Varuṇa" is the deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority, while "śeṣasaḥ" implies "remaining or protecting." This highlights the need for divine guidance and protection in our endeavors.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the values of friendship, unity, and divine protection. It serves as a reminder that collaboration and mutual support are essential for overcoming challenges in life.
  • Real-life Application: In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied to foster teamwork and collaboration among colleagues. For instance, when working on a project, team members can remind each other of the importance of supporting one another to achieve a common goal. Additionally, in personal relationships, this mantra encourages individuals to nurture friendships and seek mutual assistance during difficult times.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of community and the need for divine support in our collective efforts. By embracing these principles, individuals can create a harmonious environment that promotes growth, understanding, and resilience in the face of challenges.
