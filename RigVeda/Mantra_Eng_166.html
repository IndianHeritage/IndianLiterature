विश्वम् इति सवनाṁ सुतं इन्द्रो मदाय गच्छति |वृत्रहā सोमपीतये ||
viśvam it savanaṁ sutam indro madāya gacchati | vṛtrahā somapītaye ||
  • viśvam: This word translates to "all" or "universe." It signifies the totality of existence, encompassing everything in the cosmos.
  • it: This means "here" or "this." It indicates the presence of something in the immediate context.
  • savanaṁ: This refers to "sacrifice" or "offering." In Vedic traditions, offerings are made to deities to seek blessings and prosperity.
  • sutam: This translates to "the drink" or "the essence." It often refers to the sacred drink, soma, which is associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
  • indro: This is a reference to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, known for his strength and power.
  • madāya: This means "for intoxication" or "for delight." It suggests a state of bliss or ecstasy that comes from divine blessings.
  • gacchati: This translates to "goes" or "moves." It indicates the action of moving towards something.
  • vṛtrahā: This means "the slayer of Vritra." Vritra is a serpent or dragon that represents chaos and obstruction. Indra is known for defeating Vritra, symbolizing the triumph of order over chaos.
  • somapītaye: This refers to "for the drinking of soma." It emphasizes the importance of soma in Vedic rituals, symbolizing nourishment and spiritual awakening.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for the universal essence to be offered in sacrifice, invoking Indra to partake in the offerings for the purpose of divine intoxication and the slaying of chaos, represented by Vritra, while also emphasizing the significance of soma in this process.


In real life, this mantra can be seen as a metaphor for seeking harmony and balance in our lives. Just as Indra is called upon to overcome chaos, individuals can strive to conquer their personal challenges and obstacles. The act of offering can be likened to the sacrifices we make in our daily lives for the greater good, whether it be in our relationships, careers, or communities.


Furthermore, the concept of soma can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment. In modern contexts, this could represent the quest for education, self-improvement, and spiritual growth. The mantra encourages us to seek out the essence of life, to engage in practices that uplift us, and to share our blessings with others.


Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of unity, sacrifice, and the pursuit of higher consciousness. It serves as a reminder that by overcoming chaos and embracing the essence of life, we can achieve a state of bliss and fulfillment. In our daily lives, we can apply these teachings by striving for balance, making sacrifices for the greater good, and seeking knowledge and enlightenment.
