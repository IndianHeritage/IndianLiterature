उद वन्दनम् ऐरतम् दंशनाभिर उद रेबं दश्रा वृषणा शचीभिः |निष टौग्रम् पारयथः समुद्रात पुनश्च्यवानं चक्रथुर युवानम् ||
ud vandanam airataṁ daṁsanābhir ud rebhaṁ dasrā vṛṣaṇā śacībhiḥ | niṣ ṭaugryam pārayathaḥ samudrāt punaś cyavānaṁ cakrathur yuvānam ||
  • ud vandanam: This phrase can be interpreted as "the act of offering praise or salutation." In real life, this can be seen in various cultures where people greet each other with respect, such as bowing in Japan or saying "Namaste" in India.
  • airataṁ: This word refers to something that is pure or untainted. In a modern context, it can relate to the importance of maintaining purity in our thoughts and actions, such as practicing mindfulness or engaging in ethical consumption.
  • daṁsanābhir: This term can be understood as "with sharp teeth" or "with strength." It symbolizes the power and determination needed to overcome challenges. For example, athletes often exhibit this strength when they push through pain to achieve their goals.
  • ud rebhaṁ: This phrase can mean "to rise up" or "to elevate." In everyday life, this can be seen in personal growth journeys, where individuals strive to improve themselves, such as pursuing education or overcoming personal obstacles.
  • dasrā vṛṣaṇā: This can be interpreted as "the strong ones" or "the powerful." In a societal context, this could refer to leaders or role models who inspire others through their actions and decisions, such as activists fighting for social justice.
  • śacībhiḥ: This word signifies "with strength" or "with support." It highlights the importance of community and collaboration. For instance, teamwork in a workplace or support groups for mental health exemplify this concept.
  • niṣ ṭaugryam: This term can be understood as "the state of being steadfast" or "resilience." In real life, this is crucial during difficult times, such as individuals facing health crises or economic hardships yet continuing to persevere.
  • pārayathaḥ: This phrase means "to strive" or "to make an effort." It emphasizes the importance of hard work and dedication in achieving success, akin to students studying diligently for exams or professionals working towards career advancement.
  • samudrāt: This word translates to "from the ocean." The ocean can symbolize vastness and depth, representing the limitless opportunities available to us. For example, exploring new career paths or personal interests can be likened to navigating the ocean.
  • punaś cyavānaṁ: This phrase can mean "to return" or "to come back." It signifies the cyclical nature of life, where setbacks can lead to comebacks. A real-life example is an athlete who, after an injury, works hard to return to their sport stronger than before.
  • cakrathur yuvānam: This can be interpreted as "the young ones who are active" or "the youth." It emphasizes the role of the younger generation in driving change and innovation, such as young activists leading movements for climate change or social equality.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, resilience, community, and the importance of striving for personal and collective growth. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, from personal development to social responsibility. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can navigate challenges, support one another, and contribute positively to society, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and progressive world.
