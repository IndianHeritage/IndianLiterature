मन्त्र: दिवो नो वृष्टिम मरुतो ररिध्वं प्र पिन्वत वृष्णो अश्वस्य धाराः |अर्वाङ् एतेन स्तनयित्नुनेहि अपो निषिञ्चन्न असुरः पिता नः ||
divo no vṛṣṭim maruto rarīdhvam pra pinvata vṛṣṇo aśvasya dhārāḥ | arvāṅ etena stanayitnunehy apo niṣiñcann asuraḥ pitā naḥ ||
  • Mantra: "divo no vṛṣṭim maruto rarīdhvam pra pinvata vṛṣṇo aśvasya dhārāḥ | arvāṅ etena stanayitnunehy apo niṣiñcann asuraḥ pitā naḥ ||"
  • Translation: "O Maruts, bring us the rain from the heavens; let the bountiful waters flow like the steeds of Vṛṣṇa. May the waters, which are the essence of life, nourish us, as a father nourishes his children."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • Divo: Of the heavens or sky.
    • No: For us.
    • Vṛṣṭim: Rain or moisture.
    • Maruto: Referring to the Maruts, the storm deities.
    • Rarīdhvam: Bring forth or bestow.
    • Pra: Forth or out.
    • Pinvata: Let flow or let it be abundant.
    • Vṛṣṇo: Referring to Vṛṣṇa, a deity associated with abundance and prosperity.
    • Aśvasya: Of the horses, symbolizing speed and strength.
    • Dhārāḥ: Streams or flows.
    • Arvāṅ: All or every part.
    • Etana: This or these.
    • Stanayitnunehy: Nourishing or sustaining.
    • Apo: Waters.
    • Niṣiñcann: Pouring or sprinkling.
    • Asuraḥ: Divine or powerful beings.
    • Pitā: Father.
    • Naḥ: Our.

  • Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer for rain, which is essential for agriculture and sustenance. In ancient times, as well as today, rain is vital for crops, drinking water, and overall ecological balance.
    • The invocation of the Maruts signifies the acknowledgment of natural forces and their importance in human life. It reflects a deep respect for nature and the divine powers that govern it.
    • The metaphor of the father nourishing his children emphasizes the nurturing aspect of nature, where water is seen as a life-giving force, similar to a parent's care for their offspring.

  • Real-Life Application:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain during dry seasons, recognizing the dependency of crops on water. This mantra can be recited in rituals to invoke rain and ensure a good harvest.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can serve as a reminder of the importance of environmental conservation and sustainable practices to maintain the balance of nature.
    • Communities facing drought can use this mantra as a collective prayer, fostering unity and hope in seeking divine intervention for their needs.

  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra encapsulates the relationship between humans and nature, emphasizing the need for harmony and respect for the natural world.
    • It serves as a reminder of our reliance on natural resources and the importance of nurturing our environment for future generations.
    • By invoking the divine for rain, it highlights the spiritual aspect of seeking assistance from higher powers in times of need, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
