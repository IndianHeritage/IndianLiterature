तं उ त्वा यः पुरासिथ यो वा नूनं हिते धनें | हव्यः स श्रुधी हवम् ||
tam u tvā yaḥ purāsitha yo vā nūnaṁ hite dhane | havyaḥ sa śrudhī havam ||
  • tam u tvā: This phrase can be translated as "that which is you" or "that which is yours." It signifies a connection to the divine or the ultimate reality.
  • yaḥ purāsitha: This means "who has been worshipped" or "who has been revered." It indicates the importance of honoring and respecting the divine forces or energies that govern our lives.
  • yo vā nūnaṁ hite dhane: This translates to "who indeed is beneficial in wealth." It emphasizes the idea that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses spiritual and emotional well-being.
  • havyaḥ sa śrudhī havam: This can be interpreted as "may the offerings be heard." It reflects the belief that our prayers and offerings to the divine are acknowledged and responded to.

The mantra as a whole can be understood as a call to recognize and honor the divine presence in our lives, acknowledging that true wealth comes from spiritual fulfillment and connection. It encourages us to make offerings, whether they be in the form of prayers, good deeds, or acts of kindness, and trust that these will be heard and valued by the universe.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can incorporate this mantra into their daily prayers or meditation, fostering a deeper connection with their spiritual beliefs.
  • Acts of Kindness: By recognizing the importance of beneficial actions, one can engage in community service or charitable activities, understanding that these contribute to a greater sense of wealth and fulfillment.
  • Mindfulness in Wealth: This mantra serves as a reminder to seek balance in life, valuing emotional and spiritual wealth over mere material possessions.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing the divine in our lives and the importance of making meaningful offerings. It teaches us that true wealth is multifaceted, encompassing not just material gain but also spiritual and emotional richness. By applying these principles in our daily lives, we can cultivate a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
