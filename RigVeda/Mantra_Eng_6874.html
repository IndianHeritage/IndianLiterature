तत् ते सहस्व इमहे दात्रं यन् नोपदस्यति |त्वद अग्ने वार्यं वसु ||
tat te sahasva īmahe dātraṁ yan nopadasyati | tvad agne vāryaṁ vasu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tat: that
    • te: your
    • sahasva: may we be strong
    • īmahe: we meditate
    • dātraṁ: giver
    • yan: which
    • nopadasyati: does not abandon
    • tvad: of you
    • agne: O Fire (Agni)
    • vāryaṁ: worthy
    • vasu: wealth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The phrase "tat te sahasva īmahe" expresses a desire for strength and resilience, indicating a collective aspiration for empowerment.
    • "Dātraṁ yan nopadasyati" suggests that the giver (Agni) does not abandon those who seek his blessings, emphasizing the importance of divine support in human endeavors.
    • The term "vāryaṁ vasu" highlights the pursuit of wealth and prosperity, which is not just material but also encompasses spiritual and emotional richness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be invoked during team meetings to inspire collaboration and strength among team members, fostering a supportive environment where everyone feels valued.
    • During personal challenges, individuals may recite this mantra to seek inner strength and resilience, reminding themselves that they are not alone in their struggles.
    • In community gatherings, this mantra can serve as a reminder of the collective strength and support available within the community, encouraging members to uplift one another.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, support, and the pursuit of wealth in all forms.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal challenges to collective endeavors, reminding us of the importance of divine support and community strength.
    • By meditating on this mantra, individuals and groups can cultivate resilience, foster collaboration, and seek prosperity in their lives.
