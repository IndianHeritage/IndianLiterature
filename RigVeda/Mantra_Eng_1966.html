आजुह्वानो न ईड्यो देवां आ वक्षि यज्ञियान |अग्नि सहस्रसां असि ||
ājuhvāno na īḍyo devām̐ ā vakṣi yajñiyān | agne sahasrasā asi ||
  • Mantra: "ājuhvāno na īḍyo devām̐ ā vakṣi yajñiyān | agne sahasrasā asi ||"
  • Translation: "Invoking the deities, we do not worship them in vain; O Agni, you are the one who is worthy of a thousand praises."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ājuhvāno: Invoking or calling upon.
    • na: Not.
    • īḍyo: Worthy of worship or praise.
    • devām̐: Deities or divine beings.
    • ā: Here or in this context.
    • vakṣi: We speak or express.
    • yajñiyān: Related to sacrifice or offerings.
    • agne: O Agni, the fire god.
    • sahasrasā: Thousandfold or in many ways.
    • asi: You are.
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of sincere invocation and worship of deities, particularly Agni, who represents the divine aspect of fire and transformation.
    • In real life, this can be related to the idea of intention behind actions. For example, when someone performs a charitable act, the intention behind the act is crucial. If done sincerely, it is more likely to yield positive outcomes.
    • The mention of Agni signifies the transformative power of fire, which can be seen in various aspects of life, such as cooking, warmth, and even in industrial processes. Just as fire transforms materials, our intentions and actions can transform our lives and the lives of others.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder that worship and invocation should come from a place of genuine respect and intention, rather than mere ritualistic practice.
    • It highlights the importance of recognizing the divine in everyday life and the transformative power of our actions and intentions.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to engage with the world around them thoughtfully and purposefully, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and the community.
