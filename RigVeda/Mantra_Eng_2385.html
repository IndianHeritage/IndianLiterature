विष्वस्य हि श्रुष्टये देव ऊर्ध्वः प्र बाहवा पृथुपाणिः सिसर्ति | आपश्चिदस्य व्रता आ निमृग्रा अयं चिद वातो रमते परिज्मन ||
viśvasya hi śruṣṭaye deva ūrdhvaḥ pra bāhavā pṛthupāṇiḥ sisarti | āpaś cid asya vrata ā nimṛgrā ayaṁ cid vāto ramate parijman ||
  • viśvasya hi śruṣṭaye: This phrase translates to "for the creation of the universe." It emphasizes the divine role in the manifestation of all that exists. In real life, this can be likened to the scientific understanding of the Big Bang, where the universe came into existence from a singular point.
  • deva ūrdhvaḥ pra bāhavā: This means "the divine, who is above, with powerful arms." It signifies the omnipotence of the divine. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way people look up to higher powers or leaders for guidance and strength during challenging times.
  • pṛthupāṇiḥ sisarti: This translates to "with broad hands, he moves." It suggests the idea of abundance and the ability to provide. For instance, a community leader who works tirelessly to support and uplift the community embodies this quality by extending help to those in need.
  • āpaś cid asya vrata: This means "the waters are indeed his vow." Water is often seen as a source of life and purity. In real life, this can be related to the importance of water conservation and the sacredness of water bodies in various cultures, highlighting the need to respect and protect our natural resources.
  • ā nimṛgrā: This translates to "this is indeed a protection." It signifies the safeguarding aspect of the divine. In practical terms, this can be related to the protective measures we take in our lives, such as safety protocols in workplaces or the protective instincts of parents towards their children.
  • ayaṁ cid vāto ramate parijman: This means "this indeed is where the wind delights." The wind symbolizes change and movement. In real life, this can be seen in how people adapt to changes in their environment, such as embracing new technologies or shifting societal norms.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine creation, protection, and the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe. Its significance lies in reminding us of the higher powers that govern our existence and the importance of living in harmony with nature. In real life, it encourages us to seek guidance from the divine, to protect our resources, and to embrace change as a natural part of life. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during personal challenges, community service, or environmental conservation efforts, reinforcing the idea that we are all part of a larger cosmic order.
