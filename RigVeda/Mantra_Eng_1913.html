किम atra दसराः कृतुथः किम आसाथे जनो यः कश्चित् अहविर महीयते |अति क्रमिष्टं जुरतम् पणेः असुं ज्योतिः विप्राय कृतुतं वचस्यवे ||
kim atra dasrā kṛṇuthaḥ kim āsāthe jano yaḥ kaś cid ahavir mahīyate | ati kramiṣṭaṁ juratam paṇer asuṁ jyotir viprāya kṛṇutaṁ vacasyave ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kim: what
    • atra: here
    • dasrā: of the one who is wise or knowledgeable
    • kṛṇuthaḥ: do or perform
    • kim: what
    • āsāthe: is desired or sought
    • jano: person or people
    • yaḥ: who
    • kaś cid: anyone or someone
    • ahavir: offering or sacrifice
    • mahīyate: is made great or exalted
    • ati: beyond
    • kramiṣṭaṁ: the one who has transcended
    • juratam: the most radiant or brilliant
    • paṇer: of the mind or intellect
    • asuṁ: life or vitality
    • jyotir: light or illumination
    • viprāya: for the wise or learned
    • kṛṇutaṁ: let us perform or create
    • vacasyave: with words or speech
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses questions about the actions and desires of individuals.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in performing actions that lead to greatness.
    • The mantra suggests that through wise actions and offerings, one can achieve illumination and vitality.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and to express it through their words and actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader may ask their team, "What can we do here to achieve our goals?" This reflects the essence of seeking wisdom and collective effort.
    • In education, a teacher might encourage students to ask questions and seek knowledge, fostering an environment where learning is valued and actions are informed.
    • In personal development, individuals often reflect on their desires and actions, striving to make choices that lead to personal growth and enlightenment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom, action, and communication in achieving greatness.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as leadership, education, and personal growth.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can strive for a life filled with purpose, knowledge, and illumination.
