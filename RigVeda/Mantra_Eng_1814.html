क्व स्विद अस्य राजसो महास परं क्वावरं मरुतो यस्मिन्न आयय || यच च्यावयथा विथुरेव संहितं व्य अद्रिणा पतथा त्वेषम् अर्णवम् ||
kva svid asya rajaso mahas paraṁ kvāvaram maruto yasminn āyaya | yac cyāvayatha vithureva saṁhitaṁ vy adriṇā patatha tveṣam arṇavam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kva svid: Where indeed
    • asya: of this
    • rajaso: of the king or ruler
    • mahas: great or vast
    • paraṁ: beyond or supreme
    • kvāvaram: where is the best
    • maruto: of the winds or storm gods
    • yasminn: in which
    • āyaya: arrival or coming
    • yac: whatever
    • cyāvayatha: you have cast away
    • vithureva: like a vast expanse
    • saṁhitaṁ: the collection or assembly
    • vy: you
    • adriṇā: by the mountains
    • patatha: you may fall
    • tveṣam: your
    • arṇavam: ocean or vastness
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects a quest for understanding the vastness and the supreme nature of existence.
    • It poses questions about the whereabouts of greatness and the essence of power, particularly in relation to the forces of nature, represented by the winds (Maruts).
    • The mention of "cast away" suggests a reflection on what is deemed unworthy or unnecessary in the pursuit of knowledge and truth.
    • The imagery of mountains and oceans symbolizes the challenges and vastness one encounters in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, one might ask, "Where is the true power of a leader?" This reflects the search for qualities that elevate a person beyond mere authority.
    • In personal growth, individuals often discard negative influences or thoughts, akin to "casting away" what does not serve their higher purpose.
    • In environmental discussions, the forces of nature (like winds) remind us of the power and unpredictability of the natural world, urging us to respect and understand it.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encourages introspection about the nature of power, existence, and the journey of life.
    • It is applicable in various scenarios, from personal development to leadership and environmental awareness.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to seek the profound truths that lie beyond the surface of our experiences.
