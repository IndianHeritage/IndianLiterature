पावकशोचे तव हि क्षयम् परि होतर यज्ञेषु वृक्तबरिषो नरः |अग्नेदुव इच्छमानास आप्यमुपासते द्रविणं धेहि तेभ्यः ||
pāvakaśoce tava hi kṣayam pari hotar yajñeṣu vṛktabarhiṣo naraḥ | agne duva icchamānāsa āpyam upāsate draviṇaṁ dhehi tebhyaḥ ||
  • pāvakaśoce: This term can be broken down into two parts: "pāvaka," meaning "pure" or "sacred," and "śoce," which means "to shine" or "to illuminate." Together, it refers to the divine purity and brilliance of the fire.
  • tava hi kṣayam: "Tava" means "your," and "hi kṣayam" translates to "indeed, the destruction" or "the end." This phrase acknowledges the power of fire to consume and transform.
  • pari hotar yajñeṣu: "Pari" means "around" or "in all directions," "hotar" refers to the priest or the one who offers sacrifices, and "yajñeṣu" means "in the sacrifices." This part emphasizes the role of fire in rituals and sacrifices, where it is central to the offerings made to the deities.
  • vṛktabarhiṣo naraḥ: "Vṛkta" means "to be invoked," "barhiṣo" refers to the sacred grass used in rituals, and "naraḥ" means "humans" or "men." This indicates that humans, through their rituals, invoke the divine presence of fire.
  • agne duva icchamānāsa: "Agne" means "O Fire," "duva" translates to "we desire," and "icchamānāsa" means "with a longing heart." This expresses a heartfelt desire for the blessings of fire.
  • āpyam upāsate: "Āpyam" means "to be attained" or "to be reached," and "upāsate" means "to worship" or "to meditate upon." This signifies the act of worshipping fire to attain its blessings.
  • draviṇaṁ dhehi tebhyaḥ: "Draviṇaṁ" means "wealth" or "prosperity," "dhehi" translates to "bestow," and "tebhyaḥ" means "to them." This is a plea for the divine fire to bestow wealth and prosperity upon the worshippers.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the significance of fire as a sacred element in rituals and daily life. It acknowledges the transformative power of fire, not just as a physical entity but as a divine presence that purifies and blesses. In real life, this can be seen in various cultural practices where fire is central, such as in Hindu rituals (yajnas) where offerings are made to deities through fire. The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of purity, devotion, and the desire for prosperity in our lives. By invoking the divine qualities of fire, individuals seek to align themselves with its transformative power, fostering a sense of connection to the divine and the universe.
