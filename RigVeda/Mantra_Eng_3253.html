गव्यन्त इन्द्रं सख्याय विप्राऽश्वायन्तो वृषणं वाजयन्तः |जनीयन्तो जनिदाम अक्षितोतिम आ च्यावयामोऽवते न कोशम् ||
gavyanta indraṁ sakhyāya viprā aśvāyanto vṛṣaṇaṁ vājayantaḥ | janīyanto janidām akṣitotim ā cyāvayāmo 'vate na kośam ||
  • gavyanta indraṁ: This phrase can be translated as "O Indra, who is the giver of wealth." Indra is often regarded as the king of the gods in Vedic literature, representing strength and prosperity. In real life, this can be likened to seeking blessings for abundance and success in one's endeavors, such as starting a new business or project.
  • sakhyāya viprā: This means "the wise ones who are friends." It emphasizes the importance of camaraderie and wisdom in achieving goals. In a modern context, this can relate to teamwork in a corporate environment where collaboration leads to innovation and success.
  • aśvāyanto vṛṣaṇaṁ: This translates to "those who drive the horses." Horses symbolize strength and speed, and in this context, it refers to those who harness their energy and resources effectively. For example, athletes training rigorously to achieve their peak performance can be seen as "driving their horses."
  • vājayantaḥ: This means "those who bring victory." It signifies the pursuit of success and triumph in various aspects of life. In real life, this can be related to individuals or teams that strive for excellence, such as a sports team aiming for a championship.
  • janīyanto janidām: This phrase translates to "those who give birth to the born." It highlights the role of creators and innovators in society. In contemporary terms, this can refer to entrepreneurs who bring new ideas to life, contributing to economic growth and societal advancement.
  • akṣitotim: This means "the unassailable strength." It represents resilience and the ability to withstand challenges. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who overcome personal hardships, such as a person recovering from illness or adversity.
  • ā cyāvayāmo: This translates to "we shall not be turned away." It signifies determination and steadfastness in one's pursuits. In a practical sense, this can relate to students who remain focused on their studies despite distractions or obstacles.
  • 'vate na kośam: This means "may we not be deprived of wealth." It expresses a desire for abundance and prosperity. In real life, this can be connected to financial planning and the importance of saving and investing wisely to secure one's future.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of prosperity, teamwork, resilience, and the pursuit of success. Its significance lies in the encouragement to seek abundance while fostering friendships and collaboration. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as in business, sports, education, and personal development. By embodying the values expressed in this mantra, individuals can strive for a fulfilling and prosperous life, ensuring that they remain steadfast in their goals and aspirations.
