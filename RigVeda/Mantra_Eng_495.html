स रत्नं मर्त्यो वसु विश्वं तोकम उता तमनाः |अच्छा गच्छत्य अस्तृतः ||
sa ratnam martyo vasu viśvaṁ tokam uta tmanā | acchā gacchaty astṛtaḥ ||
  • sa ratnam: This phrase translates to "that jewel" or "that precious thing." In a real-life context, it can refer to the valuable aspects of life, such as knowledge, relationships, or spiritual wisdom.
  • martyo: This word means "mortal" or "human." It signifies our existence as beings who are subject to life and death, reminding us of our mortality.
  • vasu viśvaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the wealth of the universe" or "the essence of all." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the abundance that exists in the world.
  • tokam uta tmanā: This phrase means "may it come to us with our own efforts." It suggests that we must actively seek and work towards our goals and desires.
  • acchā gacchaty astṛtaḥ: This translates to "may it go well for us." It is a prayer for positive outcomes and success in our endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking valuable experiences and wisdom in our mortal lives while recognizing the abundance of the universe. It encourages individuals to take initiative in their pursuits, emphasizing that success is not merely a matter of fate but also of personal effort. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal development, career aspirations, and spiritual growth. By acknowledging our mortality and the wealth of opportunities around us, we can strive for a fulfilling life, working diligently towards our goals while hoping for positive outcomes. This mantra serves as a reminder to cherish the precious aspects of life and to actively engage in the journey of existence.
