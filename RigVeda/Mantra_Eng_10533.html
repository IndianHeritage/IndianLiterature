मन्त्र: उता वाता पितासि न उता भ्रातोता नः सखा | स नो जीवातवे कृधि ||
uta vāta pitāsi na uta bhrātota naḥ sakhā | sa no jīvātave kṛdhi ||
  • Mantra: "uta vāta pitāsi na uta bhrātota naḥ sakhā | sa no jīvātave kṛdhi ||"
  • Translation: "O Wind, you are our father; you are not our brother or friend. May you grant us life!"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • uta: or, either
    • vāta: wind, air, or breath
    • pitāsi: you are our father
    • na: not
    • bhrātota: brother
    • naḥ: our
    • sakhā: friend
    • sa: he, that, or may he
    • no: us
    • jīvātave: to grant life
    • kṛdhi: do, make, or grant
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra addresses the Wind (Vāta), personifying it as a father figure, which signifies the essential role of air and breath in sustaining life.
    • By stating that the Wind is not a brother or friend, it emphasizes the unique and vital relationship humans have with nature, particularly the elements that sustain them.
    • The plea for life ("sa no jīvātave kṛdhi") reflects a universal human desire for vitality and existence, highlighting the interconnectedness of all living beings with nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the wind is seen as a life-giving force. For instance, farmers depend on favorable winds for pollination and weather conditions that support crop growth.
    • In yoga and meditation practices, breath control (Pranayama) is essential for maintaining life energy (Prana), illustrating the importance of air in spiritual and physical well-being.
    • Environmental movements often emphasize the need to protect air quality, recognizing that clean air is fundamental to health and life, echoing the sentiment of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of our dependence on natural elements, particularly air, for life and vitality.
    • It encourages respect for nature and highlights the importance of maintaining a harmonious relationship with the environment.
    • In contemporary life, it can inspire actions towards environmental conservation and mindfulness about our connection to the natural world.
