आयं दशस्यन् नार्येभिर अस्य दसमो देवैभिर वरुणो न मायि |आयं कनीन ऋतुपा अवेद्य अमिमीताररुं यश चतुष्पात् ||
ayaṁ daśasyan naryebhir asya dasmo devebhir varuṇo na māyī | ayaṁ kanīna ṛtupā avedy amimītāraruṁ yaś catuṣpāt ||
  • ayaṁ daśasyan naryebhir asya dasmo devebhir varuṇo na māyī
    • ayaṁ - This word translates to "this" or "here." It indicates a presence or a specific focus.
    • daśasyan - This can be interpreted as "of ten" or "tenfold," suggesting completeness or a full cycle.
    • naryebhir - This refers to "by men" or "by humans," indicating the involvement of humanity in the context.
    • asya - Meaning "of this," it connects the previous words to the subject at hand.
    • dasmo - This word can mean "stone" or "rock," symbolizing stability and permanence.
    • devebhir - This translates to "by the gods," indicating divine influence or presence.
    • varuṇo - Referring to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water, representing the moral and natural laws.
    • na māyī - This means "not illusory" or "not deceptive," emphasizing truth and reality.
  • ayaṁ kanīna ṛtupā avedy amimītāraruṁ yaś catuṣpāt
    • ayaṁ - Again, "this" or "here," reinforcing the focus on the present context.
    • kanīna - This can be interpreted as "of the small" or "of the minute," suggesting attention to detail.
    • ṛtupā - Meaning "the one who upholds truth," it signifies the importance of righteousness and order.
    • avedya - This translates to "not to be known" or "unseen," indicating the mysteries of existence.
    • amimītāraruṁ - This can be understood as "the one who is beyond measure," suggesting the infinite nature of the divine.
    • yaś - Meaning "who" or "that which," it introduces a subject of reverence.
    • catuṣpāt - This translates to "four-footed," which can symbolize stability and groundedness.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the relationship between humanity, the divine, and the natural order. It highlights the importance of truth, stability, and the recognition of the unseen forces that govern our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to various aspects such as:

  • Personal Integrity: Upholding truth and righteousness in our actions and decisions.
  • Environmental Awareness: Recognizing the stability provided by nature and the importance of preserving it.
  • Spiritual Reflection: Understanding the divine influence in our lives and the mysteries that surround us.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of living in harmony with the cosmic order.
