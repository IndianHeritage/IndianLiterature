अरावीद अंशुः सचमान उर्मिणा देवाव्यं मनुषे पिन्वति त्वचम् | दधाति गर्भं अदितेर उपस्थ आ येन तोकं च तनयं च धामहे ||
arāvīd aṁśuḥ sacamāna ūrmiṇā devāvya1m manuṣe pinvati tvacam | dadhāti garbham aditer upastha ā yena tokaṁ ca tanayaṁ ca dhāmahe ||
  • arāvīd aṁśuḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the radiant light" or "the divine essence." In real life, this can be likened to the inner potential or talent that each individual possesses, which can shine brightly when nurtured.
  • sacamāna ūrmiṇā: This translates to "with the waves of truth." It signifies the importance of truth and authenticity in our lives. For example, in a world filled with misinformation, standing by the truth can create a ripple effect, inspiring others to do the same.
  • devāvya1m manuṣe pinvati tvacam: This can be understood as "the divine essence that nourishes the human body." It emphasizes the connection between the divine and the physical realm. In practical terms, this can relate to how spiritual practices, such as meditation or prayer, can enhance our physical and mental well-being.
  • dadhāti garbham aditer upastha: This means "the womb of Aditi holds the potential." Aditi is often seen as the mother of gods in Vedic literature. This phrase can be related to the nurturing aspect of motherhood and the potential that lies within every individual. For instance, a mother’s love and support can help a child realize their dreams and aspirations.
  • ā yena tokaṁ ca tanayaṁ ca dhāmahe: This translates to "by which we nurture both offspring and progeny." It highlights the importance of nurturing not just our children but also the values and traditions we pass down through generations. In modern society, this can be seen in the emphasis on education and moral upbringing, ensuring that future generations are equipped to face challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of nurturing, truth, and the divine connection that exists within us and around us. Its significance lies in the reminder that we all possess a divine light that can be cultivated through truth and authenticity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to embrace their inner potential, uphold truth, and nurture the next generation with love and wisdom. By doing so, we contribute to a more harmonious and enlightened society.
