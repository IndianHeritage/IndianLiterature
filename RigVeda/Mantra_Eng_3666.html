त्वं अग्नि वरुणो जायसे यत् त्वं मित्रो भवसि यत् समिद्धः |त्वे विश्वे सहसस् पुत्र देवास् त्वं इन्द्रो दाशुषे मार्त्याय ||
tvam agne varuṇo jāyase yat tvam mitro bhavasi yat samiddhaḥ | tve viśve sahasas putra devās tvam indro dāśuṣe martyāya ||
  • tvam agne varuṇo jāyase: This phrase can be translated as "You, O Agni, are born of Varuna." Here, Agni represents the fire god, while Varuna symbolizes the cosmic order and moral authority. This highlights the interconnectedness of natural elements and divine forces.
  • yat tvam mitro bhavasi: This means "You are Mitra." Mitra is the god of friendship and harmony. This line emphasizes the importance of relationships and the role of divine forces in fostering unity among people.
  • yat samiddhaḥ: Translated as "You are kindled," this refers to the act of igniting fire, which symbolizes knowledge, enlightenment, and the spark of life. In real life, this can be seen as the pursuit of knowledge and the importance of education.
  • tve viśve sahasas putra devās: This translates to "You are the universal son of the gods." It signifies the idea that all beings are interconnected and share a common divine origin, promoting the concept of universal brotherhood.
  • tvam indro dāśuṣe martyāya: This means "You are Indra for the mortal." Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength and power. This line suggests that divine support is available to humans, empowering them to overcome challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine presence in various aspects of life, from the natural elements to interpersonal relationships. It signifies the importance of unity, knowledge, and divine support in human endeavors. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as fostering community relationships, pursuing education, and seeking strength in times of adversity. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine forces that guide and support us in our journey through life.
