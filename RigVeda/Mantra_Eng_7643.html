प्र मित्राय प्रार्यम्णे सच्चत्यं ऋतावसो |वरूथ्यं वरुणे चन्द्यं वचः स्तोत्रं राजसु गायत ||
pra mitrāya prāryamṇe sacathyam ṛtāvaso | varūthya1ṁ varuṇe chandyaṁ vacaḥ stotraṁ rājasu gāyata ||
  • pra mitrāya: This phrase can be translated as "to the friend" or "to the ally." In real life, this can refer to the importance of friendship and alliances in our lives. For example, in a workplace, having supportive colleagues can lead to a more productive and harmonious environment.
  • prāryamṇe: This term suggests "to the one who is worthy" or "to the one who is deserving." It emphasizes the value of recognizing and honoring those who have earned respect and trust. In everyday life, this can be seen in how we treat mentors or leaders who guide us.
  • sacathyam: This word means "truth" or "truthfulness." It highlights the significance of honesty in our interactions. For instance, in personal relationships, being truthful fosters trust and strengthens bonds.
  • ṛtāvaso: This can be interpreted as "the one who is established in truth" or "the one who upholds cosmic order." It signifies the importance of living in accordance with universal principles. In a practical sense, this can relate to ethical business practices that contribute to societal well-being.
  • varūthyaṁ: This term refers to "protection" or "safeguarding." It underscores the need for security in our lives, whether it be physical, emotional, or financial. For example, having a supportive family can provide a sense of safety and stability.
  • varuṇe: This word is associated with Varuna, the deity of water and cosmic order. It symbolizes the natural laws that govern our existence. In real life, this can be seen in environmental conservation efforts that aim to protect our planet's resources.
  • chandyaṁ: This term can mean "to be praised" or "to be celebrated." It reflects the human desire for recognition and appreciation. In a community setting, celebrating achievements can motivate individuals and foster a sense of belonging.
  • vacaḥ: This word translates to "speech" or "words." It emphasizes the power of communication in shaping our realities. For instance, positive affirmations can significantly impact one's self-esteem and outlook on life.
  • stotraṁ: This refers to "praise" or "hymn." It signifies the act of expressing gratitude and reverence. In daily life, practicing gratitude can enhance our overall happiness and well-being.
  • rājasu: This term means "among kings" or "in the realm of rulers." It highlights the importance of leadership and governance. In a societal context, effective leadership can lead to progress and development.
  • gāyata: This word means "to sing" or "to chant." It signifies the act of expressing joy and devotion through music or poetry. In real life, participating in communal singing or artistic expressions can strengthen community ties and promote emotional healing.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the values of friendship, truth, protection, and the importance of communication and leadership. It serves as a reminder to uphold these principles in our daily lives, fostering a sense of community and ethical responsibility. By embodying these ideals, we can create a more harmonious and supportive environment for ourselves and others, ultimately contributing to the greater good of society.
