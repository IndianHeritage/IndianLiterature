त्वं अग्न इळितो जातवेदो 'वाड धव्याणि सुरभिणि कृत्वी |प्रादाः पितृभ्यः स्वधया ते अक्षन्नद्धि त्वं देव प्रयताः हविषींषि ||
tvam agna īḻito jātavedo 'vāḍ ḍhavyāni surabhīṇi kṛtvī | prādāḥ pitṛbhyaḥ svadhayā te akṣann addhi tvaṁ deva prayatā havīṁṣi ||
  • tvam agna: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni." Agni is the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine messenger between humans and the gods.
  • īḻito: This means "invoked" or "worshipped." It signifies the act of calling upon Agni for blessings and guidance.
  • jātavedo: This term refers to "the one who knows all births." It highlights Agni's role as a witness to all actions and events, emphasizing the importance of awareness and consciousness in our lives.
  • 'vāḍ ḍhavyāni: This translates to "the offerings that are pleasing." It indicates the offerings made to Agni, which are essential for rituals and ceremonies, symbolizing gratitude and respect.
  • surabhīṇi kṛtvī: This means "having made the fragrant." It suggests the importance of purity and positivity in our actions and offerings, akin to how we should approach our daily lives with good intentions.
  • prādāḥ: This translates to "bestow." It is a call for blessings and gifts from Agni, representing the desire for prosperity and well-being.
  • pitṛbhyaḥ: This means "to the ancestors." It signifies the respect and remembrance of our forebears, acknowledging their role in our lives and the importance of family lineage.
  • svadhayā: This translates to "by one's own will." It emphasizes the importance of personal effort and intention in achieving one's goals.
  • te akṣann: This means "may you not be blind." It is a plea for clarity and insight, urging us to seek knowledge and understanding in our actions.
  • addhi tvaṁ deva: This translates to "indeed, you are a god." It acknowledges the divine nature of Agni and the respect that should be given to the divine forces in our lives.
  • prayatā havīṁṣi: This means "we offer our oblations." It signifies the act of giving and sharing, which is essential in building relationships and community.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of Agni as a divine force that connects humans with the spiritual realm. It teaches us the importance of intention, respect for our ancestors, and the need for clarity in our actions. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as during family gatherings where we honor our ancestors, in rituals that require mindfulness and purity of intention, and in our daily interactions where we strive to offer kindness and respect to others. By invoking Agni, we remind ourselves of the transformative power of our actions and the importance of maintaining a connection with the divine and our heritage.
