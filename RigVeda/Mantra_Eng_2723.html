सतः-सतः प्रतिमानं पुरोभूर विश्वा वेद जनिमां हन्ति शुष्णम् | प्र णो दिवः पदवीर गव्युर अर्चन सखा सखींर्म अमुञ्चन निर अवद्यात् ||
sataḥ-sataḥ pratimānam purobhūr viśvā veda janimā hanti śuṣṇam | pra ṇo divaḥ padavīr gavyur arcan sakhā sakhīm̐r amuñcan nir avadyāt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sataḥ-sataḥ: From the many, from the countless.
    • pratimānam: The measure, the form, or the representation.
    • purobhūr: In front, before, or in the presence.
    • viśvā: All, the universe, or everything.
    • veda: Knowledge, wisdom, or understanding.
    • janimā: The one who gives birth or creates.
    • hanti: Kills or destroys.
    • śuṣṇam: The dry, the barren, or the lifeless.
    • pra ṇo: Of the breath, life force, or energy.
    • divaḥ: Of the heavens or the divine.
    • padavīr: The path or the way.
    • gavyur: The one who is worthy of worship or reverence.
    • arcan: Worship or adoration.
    • sakhā: Friend or companion.
    • sakhīm̐r: The female friend or companion.
    • amuñcan: Releases or liberates.
    • nir avadyāt: Without fault or blemish.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine.
    • It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in creating life and nurturing it.
    • The phrase "from the many" suggests that every individual is part of a larger whole, and our actions can impact the collective.
    • The destruction of the "dry" or "barren" signifies the need to eliminate ignorance and negativity to foster growth and vitality.
    • The reference to "the path of the divine" indicates that there is a righteous way to live that honors both our friendships and our responsibilities to the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, when individuals come together to support each other, they create a nurturing environment that fosters growth and understanding.
    • In education, teachers who impart knowledge help to "create" the future by nurturing the minds of their students.
    • In personal relationships, being a supportive friend can help others overcome challenges, thus "releasing" them from their burdens.
    • In environmental efforts, working to restore barren lands can symbolize the destruction of ignorance and the promotion of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of knowledge, friendship, and the divine path in our lives.
    • It teaches us that our actions have consequences not just for ourselves but for the greater community and the world.
    • By embracing these principles, we can contribute to a more harmonious and flourishing existence.
    • In essence, it calls for a conscious effort to cultivate understanding, compassion, and reverence in our daily lives.
