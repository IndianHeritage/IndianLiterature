तं सिन्धवो मत्सरम् इन्द्रपानम् ऊर्मिम प्र हेत य उभे इयर्ति |मदाच्युतम् औशानं नभोजाम् परि त्रितन्तुं विचरन्तम् उत्सम् ||
taṁ sindhavo matsaram indrapānam ūrmim pra heta ya ubhe iyarti | madacyutam auśānaṁ nabhojām pari tritantuṁ vicarantam utsam ||
  • taṁ sindhavo: This phrase refers to the rivers or bodies of water. In a broader sense, it symbolizes the flow of life and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • matsaram: This word translates to "jealousy" or "envy." It highlights the negative emotions that can arise in human interactions.
  • indrapānam: This term can be interpreted as "the drink of Indra," referring to the divine nectar or the essence of life that brings joy and vitality.
  • ūrmim: This means "wave" or "ocean wave," symbolizing the dynamic and ever-changing nature of existence.
  • pra heta: This phrase means "to be directed towards" or "to be guided." It suggests the importance of intention and purpose in our actions.
  • ya ubhe iyarti: This translates to "who moves both ways," indicating the duality of existence and the balance between opposing forces.
  • madacyutam: This means "the intoxication of joy," representing the blissful state of being that can be achieved through spiritual practices.
  • auśānaṁ: This term refers to "the one who brings forth," symbolizing creativity and the nurturing aspect of life.
  • nabhojām: This translates to "of the sky," indicating the vastness and limitless potential of the universe.
  • pari tritantuṁ: This phrase means "to traverse all paths," emphasizing the journey of life and the exploration of different experiences.
  • vicarantam: This means "to wander" or "to roam," symbolizing the quest for knowledge and understanding.
  • utsam: This term refers to "the essence" or "the core," representing the fundamental truth that underlies all existence.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of intention, the dynamic nature of existence, and the pursuit of joy and understanding. In real life, it serves as a reminder to navigate our emotions, particularly jealousy, and to seek the divine essence in our experiences. By recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the duality of existence, we can cultivate a more harmonious and fulfilling life. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal relationships, where understanding and compassion can help overcome negative emotions, or in spiritual practices that encourage exploration and self-discovery.
