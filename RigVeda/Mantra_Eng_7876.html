मन्त्र: एते पूता विपश्चितः सोमासो दध्याशिरः | विपा व्य आनशुर धियः ||
ete pūtā vipaścitaḥ somāso dadhyāśiraḥ | vipā vy ānaśur dhiyaḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra translates to "These pure and wise ones, who are like the moon, possess a nourishing intellect." It emphasizes the qualities of purity, wisdom, and nourishment.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ete: These
    • pūtā: Pure
    • vipaścitaḥ: Wise or knowledgeable
    • somāso: Like the moon
    • dadhyāśiraḥ: Possessing nourishing intellect
    • vipā: They are
    • vy ānaśur: Who are like the moon
    • dhiyaḥ: Intellect or understanding
  • Contextual Meaning: This mantra highlights the importance of purity and wisdom in individuals. The comparison to the moon signifies calmness, serenity, and the ability to reflect light, which can be interpreted as the ability to reflect knowledge and wisdom in one's life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Education: Just as the moon illuminates the night, education enlightens individuals. A wise teacher embodies the qualities of purity and wisdom, guiding students with a nourishing intellect.
    • Leadership: A good leader is often compared to the moon, providing guidance and clarity. Leaders who are pure in their intentions and wise in their decisions can inspire and uplift their teams.
    • Personal Growth: Individuals striving for self-improvement can reflect on this mantra. By cultivating purity in thoughts and actions and seeking wisdom, one can achieve a more fulfilling life.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for individuals to embody purity and wisdom. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to seek knowledge, maintain integrity, and nurture one's intellect. By doing so, individuals can contribute positively to their communities and lead more meaningful lives.
