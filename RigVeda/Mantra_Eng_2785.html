स्तीर्यं ते बर्हिः सुत इन्द्र सोमः कृताधानाः तव ते हरिभ्यां |तदोकसे पुरुषाकाय वृष्णे मरुत्वते तुभ्यं राता हविषींषि ||
stīrṇaṁ te barhiḥ suta indra somaḥ kṛtā dhānā attave te haribhyām | tadokase puruśākāya vṛṣṇe marutvate tubhyaṁ rātā havīṁṣi ||
  • stīrṇaṁ te barhiḥ: This phrase can be translated as "the well-established offerings to you." Here, "stīrṇaṁ" means well-established or firm, and "barhiḥ" refers to the sacred grass used in rituals. In real life, this can be likened to the importance of having a strong foundation in any endeavor, whether it be in personal relationships, business, or spiritual practices.
  • suta indra somaḥ: This translates to "the son of Indra, the Soma." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain and thunder, while Soma refers to a sacred drink that is believed to confer immortality. This can be seen in real life as the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, where one seeks to gain wisdom (Soma) and strength (Indra) in their life journey.
  • kṛtā dhānā: This means "having been made or created." It signifies the act of creation and manifestation. In practical terms, this can relate to the efforts we put into our work or projects, emphasizing that success comes from diligent effort and creativity.
  • attave te haribhyām: This phrase can be interpreted as "may you be pleased with our offerings." It reflects the idea of seeking approval and blessings from the divine. In everyday life, this can be related to the concept of gratitude and respect towards those who support us, whether they are family, friends, or mentors.
  • tadokase puruśākāya: This translates to "in that place, in the form of a person." It suggests the presence of divinity in human form. This can be understood in the context of recognizing the divine qualities within ourselves and others, promoting compassion and understanding in our interactions.
  • vṛṣṇe marutvate: This means "to you, O Vṛṣṇa, who is like the Maruts." Vṛṣṇa is often associated with strength and protection, while the Maruts are storm deities. This can be applied in real life as a reminder to seek strength and resilience in the face of challenges, drawing inspiration from powerful figures or forces around us.
  • tubhyaṁ rātā havīṁṣi: This translates to "we offer you our praises and offerings." It emphasizes the importance of devotion and recognition of the divine in our lives. In practical terms, this can be seen in acts of kindness, charity, and the acknowledgment of the greater forces at play in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, strength, and the importance of a strong foundation in life. It serves as a reminder to seek knowledge, express gratitude, and recognize the divine within ourselves and others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to approach their endeavors with dedication, to appreciate the support they receive, and to cultivate resilience in the face of adversity. By embodying these principles, one can lead a more fulfilling and meaningful life.
