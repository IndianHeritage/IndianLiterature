उभा जिग्यथुर न परा जयेत न परा जिग्ये कटरश्चनैनोः |इन्द्रश्च विष्णो यद अपस्पृधेतां त्रिधा सहस्रं वि तद एयरयेताम् ||
ubhā jigyathur na parā jayethe na parā jigye kataraś canainoḥ | indraś ca viṣṇo yad apaspṛdhethāṁ tredhā sahasraṁ vi tad airayethām ||
  • Ubhā jigyathur: This phrase can be interpreted as "both (sides) inquire" or "both (parties) seek." It emphasizes the importance of dialogue and mutual understanding in any relationship or situation.
  • Na parā jayethe: This translates to "one should not conquer another." It suggests that victory should not come at the cost of another's defeat, promoting the idea of coexistence and harmony.
  • Na parā jigye: This means "one should not seek to defeat another." It reinforces the previous idea of avoiding conflict and competition, advocating for collaboration instead.
  • Kataraś canainoḥ: This phrase can be understood as "which of the two (sides) is better?" It raises the question of comparison, urging individuals to reflect on their actions and intentions rather than simply striving for superiority.
  • Indraś ca viṣṇo: Referring to the deities Indra and Vishnu, this part highlights the divine qualities of strength and preservation. Indra represents power and leadership, while Vishnu symbolizes protection and sustenance.
  • Yad apaspṛdhethāṁ: This translates to "what they do not touch." It implies that certain actions or intentions should be avoided, particularly those that lead to harm or discord.
  • Tredhā sahasraṁ: This phrase means "thousands in three ways." It suggests that there are multiple paths or approaches to achieving a goal, emphasizing diversity in thought and action.
  • Vi tad airayethām: This can be interpreted as "may that be attained." It expresses a desire for the realization of positive outcomes through the right actions and intentions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of cooperation, understanding, and the avoidance of conflict. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal relationships, workplace dynamics, and community interactions. For instance, in a team project, instead of competing against each other, team members can focus on collaboration and mutual support to achieve a common goal. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for harmony and the recognition that true success is not about defeating others but about uplifting one another and working together towards shared aspirations.
