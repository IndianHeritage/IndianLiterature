यम अश्वी नित्यम उपयाति यज्ञं प्रजावन्तं स्वपत्यम् क्षयम् नः |स्वजनमनाः शेषसां वावृद्धानम् ||
yam aśvī nityam upayāti yajñam prajāvantaṁ svapatyaṁ kṣayaṁ naḥ | svajanmanā śeṣasā vāvṛdhānam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yam: who
    • aśvī: the horse (symbolizing strength and energy)
    • nityam: always
    • upayāti: approaches or comes to
    • yajñam: the sacrifice or offering (often representing selfless action)
    • prajāvantaṁ: the one who is full of progeny or wealth
    • svapatyaṁ: the one who is self-born or self-created
    • kṣayaṁ: destruction or decline
    • naḥ: for us
    • svajanmanā: with our own birth or lineage
    • śeṣasā: the remaining or the rest
    • vāvṛdhānam: may it grow or increase
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine entity or force that is always present and approaches those who engage in selfless actions (yajña).
    • The mention of "aśvī" (horse) symbolizes vitality and strength, suggesting that this divine force brings energy and prosperity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of progeny and wealth, indicating that true fulfillment comes from nurturing life and community.
    • The phrase "kṣayaṁ naḥ" suggests a plea for protection against decline or destruction, highlighting the need for stability and growth in our lives.
    • Finally, the mantra calls for the growth of our lineage and the remaining aspects of our lives, indicating a desire for continuity and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, this mantra can be applied to the importance of nurturing relationships and ensuring the well-being of future generations.
    • In a community context, engaging in selfless service (yajña) can lead to collective prosperity and harmony, reflecting the mantra's call for growth and stability.
    • In personal development, the mantra encourages individuals to focus on their strengths and contributions, fostering a sense of purpose and fulfillment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of selfless actions, and the nurturing of relationships.
    • Its significance lies in its call for growth, stability, and the protection of our lineage, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from family to community and personal development.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous existence for themselves and those around them.
