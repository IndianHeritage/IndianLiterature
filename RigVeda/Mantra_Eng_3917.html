वृषा ह्यसि राधसे जज्ञिषे वृष्णि ते शवः |स्वकक्षत्रं ते धृषण मनः सत्राहम इन्द्र पौंस्यम् ||
vṛṣā hy asi rādhase jajñiṣe vṛṣṇi te śavaḥ | svakṣatraṁ te dhṛṣan manaḥ satrāham indra pauṁsyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vṛṣā: Strong, powerful, or mighty.
    • hy: Indeed or certainly.
    • asi: You are.
    • rādhase: For the one who is worthy of praise or honor.
    • jajñiṣe: Born or manifested.
    • vṛṣṇi: A reference to the Vṛṣṇi clan, often associated with Lord Krishna.
    • te: Your.
    • śavaḥ: Body or form.
    • svakṣatraṁ: Your own realm or domain.
    • te: Your.
    • dhṛṣan: Courage or strength.
    • manaḥ: Mind or intellect.
    • satrāham: I am the one who is engaged in the sacrifice or offering.
    • indra: A deity, often considered the king of gods.
    • pauṁsyam: Related to manhood or masculinity.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses reverence for a powerful entity, likely a deity, who is worthy of praise and honor.
    • The mention of "Vṛṣṇi" connects it to the lineage of Lord Krishna, emphasizing the importance of strength and valor.
    • The phrase "your own realm" suggests a personal connection to one's own identity and responsibilities.
    • It highlights the importance of courage and intellect in achieving one's goals and fulfilling duties.
    • The reference to "sacrifice" indicates that one must be willing to give of oneself for the greater good, a common theme in many spiritual traditions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership roles, individuals often need to embody strength and courage, making decisions that benefit their team or community.
    • In personal development, embracing one's own identity and responsibilities can lead to greater self-awareness and fulfillment.
    • Acts of service and sacrifice, such as volunteering or helping those in need, reflect the essence of this mantra.
    • In relationships, showing honor and respect to others, while also being true to oneself, is crucial for building strong connections.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of strength, honor, and sacrifice in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize their own power and responsibilities while fostering a sense of community and service.
    • In a world where challenges are abundant, embodying the qualities expressed in this mantra can lead to personal growth and positive impact on society.
