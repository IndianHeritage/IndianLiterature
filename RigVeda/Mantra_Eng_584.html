सुपेशसंसुखं रथं यमध्यस्थाऽउषस्त्वम् |तेनासुश्रवसंजनं प्रावाद्यदुहितरदिवः ||
supeśasaṁ sukhaṁ rathaṁ yam adhyasthā uṣas tvam | tenā suśravasaṁ janam prāvādya duhitar divaḥ ||
  • supeśasaṁ: This word can be broken down into "su" meaning good or auspicious, and "peśasaṁ" which refers to a vehicle or chariot. Together, it signifies a well-prepared or auspicious vehicle.
  • sukhaṁ: This translates to happiness or well-being. It indicates a state of joy and contentment.
  • rathaṁ: This means chariot. In the context of the mantra, it symbolizes the journey of life or the means by which one travels through experiences.
  • yam: This word means "which" or "that." It is used to refer to the chariot mentioned earlier.
  • adhyasthā: This term means "established" or "situated." It indicates a sense of stability or grounding.
  • uṣas: This refers to the dawn or the early morning light. It symbolizes new beginnings and the arrival of knowledge or enlightenment.
  • tvam: This means "you," addressing the divine or the higher power that is being invoked.
  • tena: This translates to "by that" or "through that," indicating a means or method.
  • suśravasaṁ: This word means "well-known" or "renowned." It refers to a community or people who are respected and have a good reputation.
  • janam: This means "people" or "community." It signifies the collective group of individuals.
  • prāvādya: This term means "to speak" or "to proclaim." It indicates the act of communication or expression.
  • duhitar: This translates to "daughter." In a broader sense, it can symbolize nurturing and care.
  • dvaḥ: This means "of the heavens" or "divine." It refers to the celestial or spiritual realm.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for a well-prepared and auspicious journey through life, filled with happiness and stability, guided by the light of knowledge and new beginnings. It invokes the divine presence to bless the community with renown and to communicate the values of nurturing and care.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the dawn signifies new beginnings, individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to embrace change and seek happiness in their personal journeys.
  • Community Building: The emphasis on a well-known and respected community highlights the importance of building strong relationships and fostering a supportive environment.
  • Education and Knowledge: The invocation of light and knowledge can inspire individuals to pursue education and wisdom, guiding their actions and decisions.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of a positive journey through life, the value of community, and the pursuit of knowledge. It encourages individuals to seek happiness and stability while nurturing their relationships and aspirations.
