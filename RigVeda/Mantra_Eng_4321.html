यस्य प्रयाणम् अन्व अन्य इद ययुर देवाः देवस्य महिमानम् ओजसा |यः पार्थिवानि विममे स एतशो राजांसि देवः सविता महित्वना ||
yasya prayāṇam anv anya id yayur devā devasya mahimānam ojasā | yaḥ pārthivāni vimame sa etaśo rajāṁsi devaḥ savitā mahitvanā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • prayāṇam: the departure or journey
    • anv anya: in another way
    • id: this
    • yayur: the gods
    • devā: divine beings
    • devasya: of the divine
    • mahimānam: greatness or glory
    • ojasā: with energy or power
    • yaḥ: who
    • pārthivāni: earthly or worldly
    • vimame: measures or evaluates
    • sa: he
    • etaśo: from this
    • rajāṁsi: kings or rulers
    • devaḥ: the god
    • savitā: the sun or the creator
    • mahitvanā: with greatness or majesty
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine journey and the greatness of the gods, particularly focusing on the sun god Savitā.
    • It emphasizes the idea that the divine beings evaluate the worldly affairs and the greatness of rulers.
    • The mantra suggests that the divine presence is involved in the journey of life and the evaluation of human actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, a wise ruler often seeks guidance from higher principles or values, akin to how the gods evaluate the actions of kings.
    • In personal journeys, individuals may reflect on their life choices and seek to align them with a greater purpose, similar to the divine evaluation mentioned in the mantra.
    • In nature, the sun (Savitā) is a source of energy and life, reminding us of the importance of nurturing and valuing our environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of the divine and earthly realms, highlighting the importance of evaluating our actions in light of higher principles.
    • It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and guidance from the divine in their personal and professional lives.
    • In a broader context, it serves as a reminder of the responsibility that comes with leadership and the need for humility in the face of greater forces.
