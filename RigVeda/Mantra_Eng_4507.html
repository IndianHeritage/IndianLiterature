तं उ त्वा दध्यङ् ऋषिः पुत्र इदhe अथर्वणः |वृत्रहणं पुरंदरम् ||
tam u tvā dadhyaṅṅ ṛṣiḥ putra īdhe atharvaṇaḥ | vṛtrahaṇam puraṁdaram ||
  • tam u tvā dadhyaṅṅ: This phrase can be translated as "I dedicate to you" or "I offer to you." It signifies the act of devotion and surrender to a higher power or divine entity.
  • ṛṣiḥ: This term refers to a sage or seer, someone who has attained a high level of spiritual knowledge and insight. In the context of the mantra, it emphasizes the importance of wisdom and guidance in our lives.
  • putra: Meaning "son" or "child," this word symbolizes the continuation of lineage and the importance of family and relationships in human life.
  • īdhe: This translates to "may I invoke" or "may I inspire." It reflects the desire to connect with the divine and seek blessings or guidance.
  • atharvaṇaḥ: This refers to the Atharva Veda, one of the four Vedas in Hinduism, which contains hymns, spells, and incantations. It signifies the importance of knowledge and learning in spiritual practice.
  • vṛtrahaṇam: This means "the slayer of Vritra," where Vritra is a mythical serpent or dragon representing obstacles and challenges. This part of the mantra invokes the power to overcome difficulties and adversities.
  • puraṁdaram: This term refers to "the builder of cities" or "the creator." It symbolizes the creative force in the universe and the ability to manifest one's desires and aspirations.

Conclusion: The mantra "tam u tvā dadhyaṅṅ ṛṣiḥ putra īdhe atharvaṇaḥ | vṛtrahaṇam puraṁdaram ||" encapsulates themes of devotion, wisdom, family, and the overcoming of obstacles. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as seeking guidance in difficult times, invoking blessings for family and relationships, and striving for personal growth and success. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, spirituality, and the human experience, encouraging individuals to seek higher wisdom and to overcome challenges with determination and faith.
