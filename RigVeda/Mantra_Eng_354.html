त्वं अग्नि मनवे द्यां अवाशयः पुरूरवसे सुखृत सुखृत्तरः | श्वात्रेण यत पितरोर मुच्यसे पर्य आ त्वां पूर्वम् अनयन्न आपरम् पुनः ||
tvam agne manave dyām avāśayaḥ purūravase sukṛte sukṛttaraḥ | śvātreṇa yat pitror mucyase pary ā tvā pūrvam anayann āparam punaḥ ||
  • tvam agne: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni." Agni is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, fire is essential for cooking, warmth, and rituals, representing the transformative power of energy in our lives.
  • manave dyām avāśayaḥ: This means "You who support the heavens for mankind." It signifies the role of Agni in sustaining life and the universe. Just as the sun provides light and energy for life on Earth, Agni represents the sustaining forces that support human existence.
  • purūravase sukṛte sukṛttaraḥ: This translates to "You who dwell in the many, the doer of good deeds, the best of the good." It emphasizes the importance of good actions and moral integrity. In everyday life, this can be seen in acts of kindness, community service, and ethical behavior that contribute to societal well-being.
  • śvātreṇa yat pitror mucyase: This means "By the grace of which you are freed from the bonds of your ancestors." It suggests liberation from past karma or familial ties. In modern contexts, this can relate to personal growth and breaking free from negative family patterns or societal expectations.
  • pary ā tvā pūrvam anayann āparam punaḥ: This translates to "You who were brought forth before and will be brought forth again." It reflects the cyclical nature of life, death, and rebirth. This can be related to the concept of renewal in nature, such as the changing seasons, and in personal life, such as starting anew after challenges.

Conclusion: This mantra highlights the significance of Agni as a transformative force in life, emphasizing the importance of good deeds, liberation from past constraints, and the cyclical nature of existence. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to embrace change, act ethically, and recognize the interconnectedness of all beings. By invoking Agni, one seeks guidance and support in their journey towards personal and spiritual growth, making it applicable in rituals, meditation, and daily life decisions.
