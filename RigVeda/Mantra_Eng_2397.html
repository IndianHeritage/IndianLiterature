शृङ्गेव नः प्रथमागन्तमर्वाक्चफाविव जर्भुराणातरुभिः |चक्रवाकेव प्रतिवस्तोरुश्रावञ्चायातं रथ्येव शक्रा ||
śṛṅgeva naḥ prathamā gantam arvāk chaphāv iva jarbhurāṇā tarobhiḥ | cakravākeva prati vastor usrārvāñcā yātaṁ rathyeva śakrā ||
  • śṛṅgeva: This word translates to "like the horn" or "as the horn does." In real life, horns are often associated with strength and power, much like the horns of a bull or a ram.
  • naḥ: This means "our" or "of us," indicating a collective identity or community.
  • prathamā: This translates to "first" or "foremost," suggesting priority or importance.
  • gantam: This means "to go" or "to move," indicating a journey or progression.
  • arvāk: This word means "towards the front" or "forward," emphasizing directionality in movement.
  • chaphāv: This can be interpreted as "like a bird" or "as a bird does," which evokes images of freedom and grace in movement.
  • iva: This means "like" or "as," used for comparison.
  • jarbhurāṇā: This term refers to "the swift" or "the quick," suggesting agility and speed.
  • tarobhiḥ: This translates to "with the trees," indicating a connection to nature and the environment.
  • cakravākeva: This means "like the cakravāka bird," which is known for its love and loyalty, symbolizing devotion.
  • prati: This means "towards" or "in relation to," indicating a direction or focus.
  • vastor: This translates to "of the object" or "of the thing," referring to a specific goal or purpose.
  • usrārvāñcā: This term can be interpreted as "the powerful" or "the strong," suggesting strength and resilience.
  • yātaṁ: This means "to go" or "to proceed," indicating movement towards a goal.
  • rathyeva: This translates to "like a chariot," symbolizing a vehicle of progress and journey.
  • śakrā: This refers to "Indra," the king of gods, representing power and authority.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of collective strength, direction, and devotion in our journeys. It draws parallels between natural elements, such as the horn, birds, and trees, to illustrate the interconnectedness of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to teamwork, where individuals come together with a common purpose, moving forward with agility and strength, much like a chariot driven by a powerful force. It signifies that when we unite our strengths and focus on a shared goal, we can achieve great things, just as the gods guide us in our endeavors.
