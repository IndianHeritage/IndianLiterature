देवेभ्यस्त्वा मदाय कं सृजानम् अति मेष्यः |सं गोभिर वासयामसि ||
devebhyas tvā madāya kaṁ sṛjānam ati meṣyaḥ | saṁ gobhir vāsayāmasi ||
  • Mantra: "devebhyas tvā madāya kaṁ sṛjānam ati meṣyaḥ | saṁ gobhir vāsayāmasi ||"
  • Translation: "We offer you, O Divine Ones, this oblation; may you, who are the creators of the universe, grant us the strength to thrive and prosper."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • devebhyas: To the gods or divine beings.
    • tvā: You (referring to the divine).
    • madāya: By offering or with devotion.
    • kaṁ: Who or what.
    • sṛjānam: The creator or the act of creation.
    • ati: Beyond or surpassing.
    • meṣyaḥ: Strength or power.
    • saṁ: Together or in unity.
    • gobhir: With the cows or with abundance.
    • vāsayāmasi: We dwell or reside.

  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the divine forces, acknowledging their role in creation and seeking their blessings for strength and prosperity.
    • In ancient times, offerings were made to the gods to ensure a good harvest, health, and protection from calamities. This practice is still relevant today in various cultures where people seek divine intervention for their well-being.
    • The mention of "cows" symbolizes abundance and prosperity, as cows were considered sacred and a source of wealth in Vedic culture. In modern times, this can be interpreted as a call for sustainable living and respect for nature's resources.

  • Real-Life Application:
    • In personal life, individuals can recite this mantra during times of uncertainty or when embarking on new ventures, seeking divine guidance and strength.
    • In community settings, this mantra can be used during agricultural festivals or gatherings to invoke blessings for a bountiful harvest and communal prosperity.
    • In a corporate environment, leaders can adopt the essence of this mantra by fostering a culture of collaboration and respect for all stakeholders, aiming for collective success.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing the divine in our lives and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek strength and support from higher powers while also acknowledging our responsibilities towards nature and community.
    • By incorporating the values expressed in this mantra into our daily lives, we can cultivate a sense of gratitude, unity, and purpose, leading to a more harmonious existence.
