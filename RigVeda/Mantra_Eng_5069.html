प्र यद वाम मित्रावरुणा स्पूर्धन प्रिय धाम युवधिता मिनन्ति |न ये देवासा ओहसा न मर्ता यज्ञसाचो अप्यो न पुत्राः ||
pra yad vām mitrāvaruṇā spūrdhan priyā dhāma yuvadhitā minanti | na ye devāsa ohasā na martā ayajñasāco apyo na putrāḥ ||
  • Translation: "When you, O Mitra and Varuna, are pleased, may you grant us the cherished abode of youth. Those who are not divine, nor mortal, nor the offspring of sacrifices, may they not be our progeny."
  • Explanation of Key Terms:
    • Mitra and Varuna: These are two significant deities in the Rigveda. Mitra represents friendship, harmony, and contracts, while Varuna embodies cosmic order, truth, and moral authority.
    • Spūrdhan: This term refers to the act of being pleased or satisfied. It indicates a state of divine favor.
    • Priyā dhāma: This phrase translates to "cherished abode," symbolizing a place of comfort, happiness, and fulfillment.
    • Yuvadhitā: This word signifies youthfulness, vitality, and the energy of life.
    • Ayajñasāco: Refers to those who are not part of the sacrificial tradition, indicating a separation from divine or spiritual lineage.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, the concepts of friendship and moral integrity, as represented by Mitra and Varuna, are crucial in building strong relationships and communities. For instance, in a workplace, fostering a culture of trust and collaboration can lead to greater productivity and job satisfaction.
    • The idea of cherishing youth and vitality can be seen in various wellness movements today, where individuals strive to maintain physical and mental health through exercise, nutrition, and mindfulness practices.
    • The caution against progeny that are not aligned with divine or moral values can be reflected in the importance of nurturing ethical values in children, ensuring they grow up to be responsible and compassionate individuals.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of divine favor and the pursuit of a life filled with integrity, youthfulness, and moral values. In real life, it serves as a reminder to cultivate positive relationships, prioritize well-being, and instill ethical principles in future generations. By aligning ourselves with these ideals, we can create a harmonious and fulfilling existence, both personally and within our communities.
