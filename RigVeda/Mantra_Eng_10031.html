बृहन्तेव गम्भरेषु प्रतिष्ठाम् पादेव गाढं तरते विदाथः |कर्णेव शासुर अनु हि स्मराथोऽंशेव नो भजतं चित्रम् अप्नः ||
bṛhanteva gambhareṣu pratiṣṭhām pādeva gādhaṁ tarate vidāthaḥ | karṇeva śāsur anu hi smarātho 'ṁśeva no bhajataṁ citram apnaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bṛhanteva: Like the vastness or greatness.
    • gambhareṣu: In the depths or profoundness.
    • pratiṣṭhām: Establishment or foundation.
    • pādeva: Like a foot.
    • gādhaṁ: Deep or intense.
    • tarate: To cross over or transcend.
    • vidāthaḥ: The wise or knowledgeable.
    • karṇeva: Like an ear.
    • śāsur: The teacher or guide.
    • anu hi: Indeed or certainly.
    • smarātho: Remembering or recalling.
    • 'ṁśeva: Like a part or aspect.
    • no: Not.
    • bhajataṁ: To worship or honor.
    • citram: Wonderful or diverse.
    • apnaḥ: Of the self or soul.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the vastness and depth of existence, suggesting that just as a foot provides stability, so too does a profound understanding of life provide a foundation for our actions.
    • The reference to crossing over indicates the journey of life, where wisdom helps us navigate challenges.
    • It emphasizes the importance of listening and learning from our guides, akin to how an ear receives sound, suggesting that we should be attentive to the teachings around us.
    • The mantra also highlights the significance of memory and reflection, urging us to remember the lessons learned and the aspects of life that shape our identity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a mentor can be seen as the 'teacher' guiding you through complex projects, much like the 'śāsur' in the mantra. Their wisdom helps you navigate challenges and grow in your career.
    • In personal relationships, the idea of 'remembering' can be applied to how we reflect on past experiences with loved ones, allowing us to build stronger connections based on understanding and empathy.
    • In education, students who actively listen to their teachers and reflect on their lessons are more likely to succeed, embodying the essence of 'karṇeva' and 'smarātho' from the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, guidance, and reflection in our lives. It encourages us to seek knowledge and to be mindful of the foundations we build our lives upon.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal growth, professional development, or relationships, the principles outlined in this mantra can lead to a more fulfilling and meaningful existence.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that by embracing the vastness of knowledge and the depth of our experiences, we can transcend challenges and cultivate a richer life.
