अनूनोद अत्र हस्तयतो अद्रिर आर्चन येन दश मासो नवग्वाः |ऋतं यती सरमा गा अविन्दद विश्वानि सत्याङ्गिराश चकार ||
anūnod atra hastayato adrir ārcan yena daśa māso navagvāḥ | ṛtaṁ yatī saramā gā avindad viśvāni satyāṅgirāś cakāra ||
  • anūnod atra hastayato: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who is not far away, who is close at hand." It signifies the presence of divine or spiritual guidance that is always accessible to us.
  • adrir ārcan yena daśa māso navagvāḥ: This translates to "the mountain that is worshipped, by which the ten months and the nine cows are achieved." Here, the mountain symbolizes stability and strength, while the ten months and nine cows can represent the cycles of life and abundance.
  • ṛtaṁ yatī saramā gā avindad: This means "the truth that Sarama, the dog, found." Sarama is often associated with the search for truth and guidance, indicating that truth is something that can be discovered through perseverance and loyalty.
  • viśvāni satyāṅgirāś cakāra: This translates to "he who created all things with the essence of truth." It emphasizes the idea that the universe and all its creations are rooted in truth and righteousness.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking truth and stability in life. It highlights the importance of divine guidance and the pursuit of righteousness.
  • Real-life Application: In our daily lives, we often face challenges that require us to seek guidance and remain steadfast. For example, during difficult times, individuals may turn to spiritual practices or meditation to find inner peace and clarity, much like the mantra suggests.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder that truth and stability are always within reach. By acknowledging the divine presence and striving for righteousness, we can navigate life's complexities with confidence and purpose.
