आयम् माता यम् पिता यं जीवातुर आगमत् |इदं तव प्रसर्पणं सुभन्दव एहि निर इहि ||
ayam mātāyam pitāyaṁ jīvātur āgamat | idaṁ tava prasarpaṇaṁ subandhav ehi nir ihi ||
  • ayam mātāyam pitāyaṁ jīvātur āgamat
    • ayam - this
    • mātā - mother
    • pitā - father
    • jīvātur - of the living being
    • āgamat - has come
  • idaṁ tava prasarpaṇaṁ subandhav ehi nir ihi
    • idaṁ - this
    • tava - your
    • prasarpaṇaṁ - expansion or manifestation
    • subandhav - auspicious connections
    • ehi - come
    • nir - without
    • ihi - here

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation that acknowledges the roles of mother and father in the life of a living being. It emphasizes the importance of familial bonds and the blessings that come from these relationships. The first part of the mantra recognizes the nurturing aspects of both parents, while the second part calls for the manifestation of auspicious connections in life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Family Relationships: It highlights the significance of honoring and valuing the contributions of parents in one's life. For example, celebrating family gatherings or expressing gratitude towards parents can strengthen these bonds.
  • Community and Connections: The call for auspicious connections can be seen in the importance of building a supportive community. Engaging in community service or participating in local events fosters relationships that can lead to personal and collective growth.
  • Personal Growth: The mantra encourages individuals to seek positive influences and connections in their lives. This can manifest in choosing friends who uplift and inspire, or mentors who guide personal development.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the foundational role that family and community play in our lives. It encourages individuals to appreciate their roots while also seeking out positive relationships that contribute to their overall well-being. By embodying the essence of this mantra, one can cultivate a life filled with love, support, and auspicious connections.
