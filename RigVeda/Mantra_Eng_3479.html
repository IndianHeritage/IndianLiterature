उतो हि वां दात्रा सन्ति पूर्वा या पूर्वुभ्यस त्रसदस्युर नितोषे |क्षेत्रासां ददथुर उर्वरासां घनं दस्युभ्यो अभिभूतिम उग्रं ||
uto hi vāṁ dātrā santi pūrvā yā pūrubhyas trasadasyur nitośe | kṣetrāsāṁ dadathur urvarāsāṁ ghanaṁ dasyubhyo abhibhūtim ugram ||
  • Mantra: "uto hi vāṁ dātrā santi pūrvā yā pūrubhyas trasadasyur nitośe | kṣetrāsāṁ dadathur urvarāsāṁ ghanaṁ dasyubhyo abhibhūtim ugram ||"
  • Translation: "Indeed, the givers (dātrā) are present, those who have come from the east, who have brought forth the strength of the fields, and have given abundance to the rich harvests, overcoming the fierce enemies."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • uto hi: Indeed, or truly, indicating affirmation.
    • vāṁ: You (plural), referring to the collective group of givers or benefactors.
    • dātrā: Givers or donors, those who provide resources or blessings.
    • santi: Are present, indicating existence or presence.
    • pūrvā: From the east, symbolizing new beginnings or the source of light and knowledge.
    • yā: Who, referring to the givers.
    • pūrubhyas: From the rich or prosperous, indicating those who have abundance.
    • trasadasyur: Strength or power, often associated with the ability to overcome challenges.
    • nitośe: Bringing forth, indicating the act of providing or delivering.
    • kṣetrāsāṁ: Of the fields, referring to agricultural abundance and prosperity.
    • dadathur: Have given, indicating the act of providing or bestowing.
    • urvarāsāṁ: Abundant or rich, often used to describe fertile land or plentiful resources.
    • ghanaṁ: Dense or thick, indicating richness or fullness.
    • dasyubhyo: To the fierce enemies, referring to challenges or adversities.
    • abhibhūtim: Overcoming or conquering, indicating victory over challenges.
    • ugram: Fierce or intense, often used to describe formidable obstacles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers who work hard to cultivate their fields and provide food for their communities exemplify the givers mentioned in the mantra. Their efforts lead to bountiful harvests that nourish society.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who invest in their employees' growth and well-being can be seen as modern-day dātrā, fostering a culture of abundance and overcoming market challenges.
    • Community organizations that provide resources and support to those in need reflect the essence of this mantra, as they bring forth strength and abundance to uplift the community.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of generosity, strength, and overcoming challenges through collective effort. It serves as a reminder that abundance comes from the contributions of many, and that by working together, we can conquer adversities.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, teamwork in organizations, and personal relationships, where the spirit of giving and collaboration leads to greater success and fulfillment.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us to recognize and appreciate the givers in our lives and to embody the qualities of generosity and resilience in our own actions.
