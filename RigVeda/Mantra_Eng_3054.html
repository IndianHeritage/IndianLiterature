स भ्रातरं वरुणम अग्न आ ववृत्स्व देवाम् अच् छा सुमती यज्ञवनसं ज्येष्ठं यज्ञवनसम् |ऋतावानम् आदित्यं चर्षणिधृतं राजानं चर्षणिधृतम् ||
sa bhrātaraṁ varuṇam agna ā vavṛtsva devām̐ acchā sumatī yajñavanasaṁ jyeṣṭhaṁ yajñavanasam | ṛtāvānam ādityaṁ carṣaṇīdhṛtaṁ rājānaṁ carṣaṇīdhṛtam ||
  • sa bhrātaraṁ varuṇam: This phrase can be translated as "O Varuna, the brother." Varuna is a deity associated with the cosmic order and moral authority. In real life, this can be seen as a call for unity and brotherhood among people, emphasizing the importance of relationships and community.
  • agna ā vavṛtsva: This means "O Agni, may you come forth." Agni, the fire god, symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals. In practical terms, this can represent the need for inspiration and motivation in our lives, urging individuals to pursue their passions and goals with fervor.
  • devām̐ acchā sumatī: Translated as "may the gods grant us good thoughts." This highlights the significance of positive thinking and divine guidance. In everyday life, this can be applied by encouraging individuals to cultivate a mindset that attracts positivity and constructive ideas, leading to better decision-making.
  • yajñavanasaṁ jyeṣṭhaṁ: This refers to "the elder among those who perform sacrifices." It signifies respect for tradition and the wisdom of elders. In modern contexts, this can be seen in the value placed on mentorship and learning from those with more experience, whether in professional settings or personal growth.
  • yajñavanasam: This term relates to the act of sacrifice or offering. It emphasizes the importance of selflessness and contribution to the greater good. In real life, this can manifest in charitable actions, community service, or any form of giving back to society.
  • ṛtāvānam ādityaṁ: This translates to "the one who upholds truth, the sun." The sun symbolizes clarity, truth, and enlightenment. In practical terms, this can be a reminder to seek truth in our lives and to act with integrity, whether in personal relationships or professional dealings.
  • carṣaṇīdhṛtaṁ rājānaṁ: This means "the one who holds the throne." It signifies leadership and authority. In real life, this can be applied to the qualities of good leadership, such as responsibility, vision, and the ability to inspire others.
  • carṣaṇīdhṛtam: This phrase reiterates the idea of holding or maintaining a position of power or influence. It emphasizes the importance of stability and consistency in leadership roles, which can be crucial in both personal and professional environments.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of unity, inspiration, positive thinking, respect for tradition, selflessness, truth, and leadership. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, encouraging individuals to foster strong relationships, pursue their passions, think positively, learn from elders, contribute to society, uphold truth, and embody good leadership. By integrating these principles into daily life, one can cultivate a harmonious and fulfilling existence, both personally and within the community.
