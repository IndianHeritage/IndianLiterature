इन्द्रो राजा जगतश्चरषणीनाम् अधि क्षमि विषुरूपं यदस्ति |ततो ददाति दाशुषे वसूनि चोदद राध उपस्तुतश्चिदर्वाक् ||
indro rājā jagataś carṣaṇīnām adhi kṣami viṣurūpaṁ yad asti | tato dadāti dāśuṣe vasūni codad rādha upastutaś cid arvāk ||
  • Indro rājā: This phrase refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature. Indra is often associated with strength, power, and leadership.
  • Jagataś carṣaṇīnām: This translates to "of the world and its inhabitants." It signifies the vastness of creation and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Adhi kṣami: This means "I am capable" or "I have the ability." It emphasizes the potential and strength within individuals to overcome challenges.
  • Viṣurūpaṁ yad asti: This translates to "the diverse forms that exist." It acknowledges the variety and multiplicity of life and existence.
  • Tato dadāti: This means "from that, he gives." It signifies the act of sharing and generosity that arises from understanding and strength.
  • Dāśuṣe vasūni: This translates to "wealth and resources for the deserving." It highlights the importance of providing for those who are in need or deserving of support.
  • Codad rādha: This means "he inspires abundance." It reflects the idea that true leadership and strength inspire growth and prosperity in others.
  • Upastutaś cid arvāk: This translates to "the one who is praised and revered." It emphasizes the respect and honor given to those who embody these qualities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, strength, and generosity. It teaches us that true power lies not just in authority but in the ability to uplift others and share resources. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in community leadership, where a leader inspires and supports their community, or in personal relationships, where individuals can offer help and resources to those in need. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for responsible leadership and the importance of recognizing the diverse forms of life, encouraging us to act with compassion and generosity in our daily lives.
