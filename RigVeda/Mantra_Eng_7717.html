एष दिवं विह धावति तिरो राजांसी धारया |पवमानः कनिक्रदत ||
eṣa divaṁ vi dhāvati tiro rajāṁsi dhārayā | pavamānaḥ kanikradat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣa: this
    • divaṁ: heaven or celestial realm
    • vi: in various ways
    • dhāvati: runs or moves
    • tiro: beyond or across
    • rajāṁsi: the kings or rulers
    • dhārayā: holding or sustaining
    • pavamānaḥ: the purifying or sacred one
    • kanikradat: who makes a sound or noise
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine force that moves through the heavens and sustains the rulers or kings.
    • The term "pavamānaḥ" refers to a purifying energy, suggesting a connection to spiritual cleansing and elevation.
    • The phrase "kanikradat" indicates that this divine force is not silent; it resonates and makes its presence known, much like the sound of a river flowing or the wind rustling through trees.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we can observe the flow of rivers, which can be seen as a metaphor for the divine energy that sustains life. Just as rivers nourish the land, the divine sustains the rulers and guides them in their duties.
    • In leadership, a good ruler is often seen as someone who is guided by higher principles and values, akin to the divine force mentioned in the mantra. Their decisions can resonate with the people, much like the sound of the "kanikradat."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine guidance in leadership and governance. It suggests that true rulers are those who are in tune with higher principles and who act in ways that resonate positively with their communities.
    • In real life, this can be applied to various leadership roles, whether in politics, business, or community organizations. Leaders who embody these qualities can inspire and uplift those around them, creating a harmonious and prosperous environment.
