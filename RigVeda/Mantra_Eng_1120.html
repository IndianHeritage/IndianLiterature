अस्य श्रवो नद्यः सप्त बिभ्रति द्यावाक्षामाऽ पृथिवी दर्शतं वपुः |अस्मे सूर्याचन्द्रमसााभिचक्षे श्रद्धे कम इन्द्र चरतो विटर्तुरम् ||
asya śravo nadyaḥ sapta bibhrati dyāvākṣāmā pṛthivī darśataṁ vapuḥ | asme sūryācandramasābhicakṣe śraddhe kam indra carato vitarturam ||
  • Mantra: "asya śravo nadyaḥ sapta bibhrati dyāvākṣāmā pṛthivī darśataṁ vapuḥ | asme sūryācandramasābhicakṣe śraddhe kam indra carato vitarturam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • asya: of this
    • śravo: of fame or glory
    • nadyaḥ: rivers
    • sapta: seven
    • bibhrati: bear or carry
    • dyāvākṣāmā: the heavens and the earth
    • pṛthivī: the earth
    • darśataṁ: manifest or show
    • vapuḥ: form or body
    • asme: for us
    • sūryācandramasābhicakṣe: by the sun and the moon
    • śraddhe: with faith
    • kam: whom
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • carato: moving or wandering
    • vitarturam: to be protected or guided
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of the glory and the life-giving rivers that flow through the earth and sky.
    • The "seven rivers" can be interpreted as the major rivers that sustain life, symbolizing abundance and nourishment.
    • It invokes the divine presence of Indra, asking for guidance and protection as we navigate through life.
    • The reference to the sun and moon signifies the balance of energies and the importance of faith in our journey.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rivers are seen as sacred and are often worshipped for their life-giving properties. For instance, the Ganges River in India is revered as a goddess.
    • The sun and moon are often used as symbols of duality in life, representing day and night, activity and rest, which can be seen in various traditions and practices.
    • Faith plays a crucial role in personal development; for example, individuals often rely on their beliefs during challenging times, seeking guidance and strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, divinity, and human existence.
    • It serves as a reminder to respect and honor the natural elements that sustain us, while also seeking divine guidance in our lives.
    • In a modern context, it encourages us to cultivate faith and trust in the journey of life, recognizing the support we receive from both nature and the divine.
