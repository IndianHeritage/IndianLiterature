स हि द्युतā विद्युतā वेति सामा पृथुं योनिम असुरत्वा ससाद |स सनीलेभिः प्रसहानो अस्य भ्रातुर न ऋते सप्ततस्य मायाः ||
sa hi dyutā vidyutā veti sāma pṛthuṁ yonim asuratvā sasāda | sa sanīḻebhiḥ prasahāno asya bhrātur na ṛte saptathasya māyāḥ ||
  • sa hi dyutā vidyutā veti sāma: This phrase can be translated to "Indeed, the light (or brilliance) is like lightning, it is the essence of the hymn." Here, "dyutā" refers to brightness or radiance, while "vidyutā" signifies lightning. This suggests that the essence of knowledge or enlightenment is as powerful and illuminating as lightning itself.
  • pṛthuṁ yonim asuratvā sasāda: This part means "He who has taken the vast womb (or source) of the non-divine." "Pṛthuṁ" indicates vastness, and "yonim" refers to the source or womb. This can be interpreted as the divine or cosmic source from which all creation emerges, emphasizing the importance of understanding our origins and the vastness of existence.
  • sa sanīḻebhiḥ prasahāno asya: This translates to "He, with the help of the wise, has overcome this." "Sanīḻebhiḥ" refers to the wise or learned individuals. This suggests that wisdom and knowledge are essential in overcoming challenges and obstacles in life.
  • bhrātur na ṛte saptathasya māyāḥ: This means "Without the brother, the illusion of the seventy (or the many) does not exist." Here, "bhrātur" refers to brotherhood or kinship, and "māyāḥ" signifies illusion. This highlights the significance of relationships and connections in navigating through life's illusions and complexities.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of enlightenment, the vastness of existence, the role of wisdom in overcoming challenges, and the significance of relationships. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In education, where the pursuit of knowledge is akin to seeking enlightenment, illuminating one's path in life.
  • In personal development, recognizing the vast potential within oneself and striving to tap into that source.
  • In teamwork and collaboration, where the collective wisdom of a group can help overcome obstacles that an individual might face alone.
  • In fostering relationships, understanding that connections with others can help dispel the illusions and challenges we encounter in life.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, existence, wisdom, and relationships in our journey through life.
