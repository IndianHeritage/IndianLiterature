वृज्यामा ते परि द्विषोऽरं ते शक्र दावने |गमेन्द इन्द्र गोमताḥ ||
vṛjyāma te pari dviṣo 'raṁ te śakra dāvane | gamemed indra gomataḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vṛjyāma: We reject or drive away
    • te: Your
    • pari: All around
    • dviṣo: Enemies or those who hate
    • 'raṁ: May we be free from
    • te: Your
    • śakra: O Indra, the powerful
    • dāvane: In the context of fire or burning
    • gamemed: May we attain or reach
    • indra: O Indra
    • gomataḥ: The one who is the protector of cattle or wealth
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of gods, asking for protection and the removal of enemies or negativity from all around. It emphasizes the desire to be free from harm and to attain prosperity and abundance, symbolized by cattle, which in ancient times represented wealth and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be invoked by entrepreneurs seeking to eliminate competition that may harm their ventures. By focusing on positive intentions and actions, they can create a thriving environment for their business.
    • In personal life, individuals facing conflicts or negativity in relationships can use this mantra as a reminder to reject harmful influences and seek harmony and abundance in their interactions.
    • In community settings, leaders can adopt the essence of this mantra to foster unity and drive away divisive elements, promoting a sense of collective well-being and prosperity.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for protection and the pursuit of prosperity. It serves as a reminder that we have the power to reject negativity and strive for abundance in our lives. By invoking the qualities of Indra, we can cultivate resilience and a positive mindset, applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal challenges to broader societal issues.
