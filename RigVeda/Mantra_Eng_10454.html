हेतिः पक्षिणी न दभात्य अस्मान आष्ट्र्यां पदं कृतुते अग्निधाने |शं नो गोभ्यश च पुरुषेभ्यश चास्तु मा नो हिंसीद इह देवाः कपोतः ||
hetiḥ pakṣiṇī na dabhāty asmān āṣṭryām padaṁ kṛṇute agnidhāne | śaṁ no gobhyaś ca puruṣebhyaś cāstu mā no hiṁsīd iha devāḥ kapotaḥ ||
  • hetiḥ pakṣiṇī: This phrase can be translated as "the bird of sacrifice." In many cultures, birds are often seen as messengers between the earthly and divine realms. They symbolize freedom and the soul's journey. For example, in real life, when we release a bird, it can represent letting go of our burdens or seeking spiritual elevation.
  • na dabhāty asmān: This means "may it not harm us." It reflects a plea for protection and safety. In our daily lives, we often seek safety from various dangers, whether they are physical, emotional, or spiritual. For instance, when we drive a car, we take precautions to ensure our safety, such as wearing seatbelts and following traffic rules.
  • āṣṭryām padaṁ kṛṇute: This translates to "may it establish a firm footing." This can be interpreted as a desire for stability and security in life. In real life, this can relate to finding a stable job or a secure home environment, which provides a foundation for personal growth and happiness.
  • agnidhāne: This refers to "the act of sacrifice." Sacrifice is a common theme in many cultures, representing the idea of giving up something valuable for a greater purpose. For example, parents often sacrifice their time and resources for the well-being of their children, ensuring they have a better future.
  • śaṁ no gobhyaś ca puruṣebhyaś cāstu: This means "may peace be with us and with all beings." It emphasizes the importance of harmony and peace in our lives. In real life, this can be seen in community efforts to promote peace, such as conflict resolution programs or initiatives aimed at fostering understanding among diverse groups.
  • mā no hiṁsīd iha devāḥ kapotaḥ: This translates to "may the divine not harm us." It is a call for divine protection and benevolence. In our daily lives, we often seek blessings or guidance from a higher power, especially during challenging times, such as praying for health during illness or for success during important endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for protection, stability, peace, and divine grace. Its significance lies in the universal human desire for safety and harmony in life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, personal prayers, or community gatherings, where individuals seek to connect with the divine and foster a sense of unity and well-being among all beings. By embodying the principles of sacrifice, peace, and protection, we can create a more harmonious existence for ourselves and those around us.
