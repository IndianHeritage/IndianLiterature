पवमान विदा रयिम अस्मभ्यं सोम दुष्टारम् |यो दूनाशो वनुष्यतā ||
pavamāna vidā rayim asmabhyaṁ soma duṣṭaram | yo dūṇāśo vanuṣyatā ||
  • pavamāna: This term refers to the "purifying" or "cleansing" aspect, often associated with the sacred drink Soma, which is believed to bestow health and vitality.
  • vidā: This word means "to know" or "to understand." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in achieving one's goals.
  • rayim: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional richness.
  • asmabhyaṁ: This means "for us" or "to us," indicating a collective aspect, suggesting that the blessings are sought for a group or community.
  • soma: Soma is a divine drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and enlightenment. It represents the essence of life and the connection to the divine.
  • duṣṭaram: This term means "difficult" or "challenging." It acknowledges the obstacles that may arise in the pursuit of wealth and prosperity.
  • yo: This means "who" and introduces the subject of the mantra, referring to the one who possesses certain qualities.
  • dūṇāśo: This word can be interpreted as "one who is far-reaching" or "one who has a broad vision." It suggests the importance of foresight and planning.
  • vanuṣyatā: This term refers to "the state of being human" or "humanity." It emphasizes the connection between divine blessings and human existence.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer for the purification and enhancement of wealth and prosperity, acknowledging the challenges that may come in this pursuit. It calls upon the divine essence of Soma to bestow knowledge and foresight upon the community, enabling them to navigate difficulties and achieve their goals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Business Ventures: Entrepreneurs can recite this mantra to seek divine guidance and clarity in their business decisions, helping them to overcome challenges and achieve success.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals may use this mantra as a reminder to pursue knowledge and wisdom, fostering personal development and resilience in the face of adversity.
  • Community Welfare: Communities can come together to chant this mantra, invoking collective prosperity and unity, while also recognizing the challenges they face as a group.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of purification, knowledge, and foresight in the pursuit of wealth and prosperity. It highlights the interconnectedness of divine blessings and human efforts, encouraging individuals and communities to strive for a balanced and enriched life.
