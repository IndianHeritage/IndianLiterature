इन्द्रा युवं वरुणा दिद्युम अस्मिन्न ओजिष्ठम उग्रा न वधिष्टं वज्रम् |यो नो दुरेवो वृकटिर दभीतिस तस्मिन मिमाथाम अभिभूत्य ओजः ||
indrā yuvaṁ varuṇā didyum asminn ojiṣṭham ugrā ni vadhiṣṭaṁ vajram | yo no durevo vṛkatir dabhītis tasmin mimāthām abhibhūty ojaḥ ||
  • Indrā: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength and power.
  • Yuvaṁ: Means 'young' or 'youthful', indicating vitality and energy.
  • Varuṇā: Refers to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water, representing the moral and ethical aspects of life.
  • Didyum: Translates to 'brilliance' or 'light', symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
  • Asminn: Means 'in this', referring to the present moment or situation.
  • Ojiṣṭham: Means 'the most powerful' or 'the strongest', indicating supreme strength.
  • Ugrā: Translates to 'fierce' or 'intense', representing the forceful aspects of nature.
  • Ni vadhiṣṭaṁ: Means 'to be established' or 'to be grounded', indicating stability and security.
  • Vajram: Refers to 'thunderbolt' or 'diamond', symbolizing indestructibility and power.
  • Yo: Means 'who', referring to the subject of the mantra.
  • No: Means 'our', indicating possession or belonging.
  • Durevo: Means 'the distant one', referring to challenges or obstacles that seem far away.
  • Vṛkatir: Translates to 'wolf', symbolizing cunning and strength.
  • Dabhītis: Means 'fearlessness', indicating courage in the face of adversity.
  • Tasmin: Means 'in that', referring to the context or situation being addressed.
  • Mimāthām: Means 'to be stirred' or 'to be moved', indicating action or change.
  • Abhibhūty: Refers to 'overcoming' or 'conquering', symbolizing triumph over challenges.
  • Ojaḥ: Means 'energy' or 'vitality', representing the life force that drives action.

The mantra speaks to the invocation of divine powers, particularly those of Indra and Varuna, to bestow strength, courage, and enlightenment upon the individual. It emphasizes the importance of being grounded and stable while facing challenges that may seem distant or formidable. The imagery of the thunderbolt (vajram) signifies the indomitable spirit required to overcome obstacles, while the reference to fearlessness (dabhītis) encourages individuals to confront their fears with confidence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties in life, such as career setbacks or personal losses, invoking the qualities of strength and fearlessness can help individuals navigate through tough times.
  • Leadership: Leaders can draw inspiration from this mantra to embody the qualities of courage and decisiveness, guiding their teams through challenges with confidence.
  • Education: Students can use the principles of enlightenment and knowledge (didyum) to pursue their studies with vigor and determination, overcoming obstacles in their academic journey.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the strength and resilience that lies within each individual. By invoking the divine qualities of Indra and Varuna, one can cultivate the energy and courage needed to face life's challenges, ultimately leading to personal growth and triumph.
