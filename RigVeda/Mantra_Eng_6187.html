नि यद यामाय वो गिरिर नि सिंधवो विधर्मणे |महेशुष्माय येमिरे ||
ni yad yāmāya vo girir ni sindhavo vidharmaṇe | mahe śuṣmāya yemire ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ni: not
    • yad: that which
    • yāmāya: for the purpose of going
    • vo: for you
    • girir: the mountain
    • ni: not
    • sindhavo: of the rivers
    • vidharmaṇe: in the path of righteousness
    • mahe: great
    • śuṣmāya: for the one who is radiant
    • yemire: they have come
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the journey and the obstacles one faces in life.
    • The phrase "not for the purpose of going" suggests that there are paths or choices that may not lead to beneficial outcomes.
    • The mention of "mountain" and "rivers" symbolizes the challenges and resources in life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of following a path of righteousness ("vidharmaṇe") to achieve greatness and radiance ("mahe śuṣmāya").
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, choosing a career path that aligns with one's values and ethics can lead to greater fulfillment and success.
    • In personal relationships, making choices that prioritize honesty and integrity can strengthen bonds and lead to a more harmonious life.
    • In environmental conservation, understanding the impact of our actions on nature (mountains and rivers) can guide us to make sustainable choices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder to evaluate our choices and paths in life.
    • It encourages us to seek righteousness and integrity in our actions, leading to a more meaningful and impactful existence.
    • By aligning our decisions with ethical principles, we can navigate life's challenges effectively and achieve a state of radiance and greatness.
