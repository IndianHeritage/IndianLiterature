या ऋते चिद अभिश्रिषः पुरा जातृभ्य आत्रिदः |संधाता संधिम मगवाऽपुरूवसुर इष्कर्ता विहृतं पुनः ||
ya ṛte cid abhiśriṣaḥ purā jatrubhya ātṛdaḥ | saṁdhātā saṁdhim maghavā purūvasur iṣkartā vihrutam punaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who
    • ṛte: without
    • cid: consciousness
    • abhiśriṣaḥ: having been established
    • purā: in the past
    • jatrubhya: to the one who is the protector
    • ātṛdaḥ: the one who nourishes
    • saṁdhātā: the one who brings together
    • saṁdhim: the agreement or harmony
    • maghavā: the powerful one
    • purūvasur: the one who is rich in resources
    • iṣkartā: the creator or maker
    • vihrutam: the one who is established
    • punaḥ: again
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine entity or force that exists beyond consciousness and is the protector and nourisher of all.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and harmony, suggesting that true power lies in bringing things together and creating balance.
    • The reference to the past indicates that these principles have been established since ancient times, highlighting their timeless relevance.
    • The mantra also acknowledges the richness of resources and the creative force that sustains life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, the mantra can be applied to emphasize the importance of collaboration. Just as the divine brings together various elements, a successful team thrives on unity and shared goals.
    • In personal relationships, the idea of nourishing and protecting one another resonates with the commitment to support loved ones through challenges.
    • In environmental contexts, the mantra can inspire efforts to create harmony with nature, recognizing the interconnectedness of all living beings and the need for sustainable practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of unity, harmony, and the nurturing aspects of existence.
    • Its teachings are applicable in various aspects of life, from personal relationships to teamwork and environmental stewardship.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and balanced world.
