एष देवो विपण्युभिः पवमान ऋतायुभिः |हरिर वाजाय मृज्यते ||
eṣa devo vipanyubhiḥ pavamāna ṛtāyubhiḥ | harir vājāya mṛjyate ||
  • eṣa devo vipanyubhiḥ: This phrase translates to "This deity, with the help of the winds." Here, "devo" refers to a divine being or deity, and "vipanyubhiḥ" signifies the winds or the forces of nature that assist the deity.
  • pavamāna ṛtāyubhiḥ: "Pavamāna" means "the purifying one" or "the wind," and "ṛtāyubhiḥ" refers to the principles of truth and order. This suggests that the deity is associated with purity and righteousness, guided by the natural laws of the universe.
  • harir vājāya mṛjyate: "Harir" refers to the divine or the Lord, "vājāya" means "for the purpose of strength or power," and "mṛjyate" translates to "is invoked" or "is worshipped." This indicates that the deity is called upon for strength and support in endeavors.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the connection between the divine, nature, and human endeavors. It highlights the importance of invoking divine support for strength and purity in our actions.
  • Real-life Application: In daily life, individuals often seek guidance and strength from a higher power when facing challenges. For example, during difficult times, people may pray or meditate, invoking divine assistance to find clarity and strength to overcome obstacles.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interplay between the divine and the natural world. It encourages individuals to seek purity and strength in their actions, aligning themselves with the principles of truth and righteousness. This alignment can lead to personal growth and a deeper connection with the universe.
