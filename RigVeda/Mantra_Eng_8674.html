परी सप्तिर न वाजयुर देवो देवभ्यः सुतः |व्याणशिḥ पवमानो वि धावति ||
pari saptir na vājayur devo devebhyaḥ sutaḥ | vyānaśiḥ pavamāno vi dhāvati ||
  • Mantra: "pari saptir na vājayur devo devebhyaḥ sutaḥ | vyānaśiḥ pavamāno vi dhāvati ||"
  • Translation: "The divine one, the son of the gods, does not waver; he moves swiftly like the wind."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pari saptir: "surrounding seven" - This can symbolize completeness or wholeness, as in the seven directions (north, south, east, west, above, below, and the center).
    • na vājayur: "does not waver" - This indicates stability and strength, suggesting that the divine presence is unwavering and constant.
    • devo devebhyaḥ: "the divine one, the son of the gods" - This refers to a powerful divine entity, often interpreted as a representation of a deity or a higher power.
    • sutaḥ: "son" - This signifies lineage and connection to the divine, emphasizing the importance of heritage and spiritual ancestry.
    • vyānaśiḥ: "moves swiftly" - This suggests agility and the ability to navigate through challenges with ease.
    • pavamāno: "like the wind" - Wind is often associated with change, movement, and the unseen forces that influence our lives.
    • vi dhāvati: "he moves" - This indicates action and the dynamic nature of the divine presence in the world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of uncertainty, individuals often seek stability and guidance from spiritual beliefs, much like the unwavering nature of the divine described in the mantra.
    • The concept of "moving swiftly like the wind" can be seen in how people adapt to change, whether in personal life, career, or societal shifts, demonstrating resilience and agility.
    • In leadership, a strong leader embodies the qualities of stability and swift action, guiding their team through challenges with confidence and clarity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine guidance and the qualities of stability, agility, and unwavering strength in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek inner strength and to remain steadfast in the face of challenges, drawing inspiration from the divine.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, including personal development, leadership, and navigating change, encouraging individuals to embody these qualities in their daily actions.
