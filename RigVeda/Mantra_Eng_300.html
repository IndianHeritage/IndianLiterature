अश्वं न त्वा वारवंतं वन्दध्या अग्निं नमोभिः |सम्राजन्तम अध्वराणाम् ||
aśvaṁ na tvā vāravantaṁ vandadhyā agniṁ namobhiḥ | samrājantam adhvarāṇām ||
  • Mantra: "aśvaṁ na tvā vāravantaṁ vandadhyā agniṁ namobhiḥ | samrājantam adhvarāṇām ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvaṁ: horse
    • na: not
    • tvā: you
    • vāravantaṁ: the one who is powerful or strong
    • vandadhyā: I bow or I worship
    • agniṁ: fire
    • namobhiḥ: with salutations or reverence
    • samrājantam: the one who rules or governs
    • adhvarāṇām: of the sacrifices or rituals
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep reverence for Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The mention of "aśvaṁ" (horse) symbolizes strength, speed, and vitality, which are essential qualities in both physical and spiritual pursuits.
    • By stating "na tvā vāravantaṁ," the speaker acknowledges Agni's powerful nature, emphasizing the importance of recognizing and respecting this force.
    • The act of bowing ("vandadhyā") signifies humility and devotion, which are crucial in any spiritual practice.
    • Agni is referred to as "samrājantam adhvarāṇām," indicating that he is the ruler of all sacrifices, highlighting the significance of fire in rituals and its role in connecting the material and spiritual worlds.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is a symbol of purification and transformation. For instance, in Hindu weddings, the sacred fire (Agni) is central to the ceremony, representing the divine witness to the vows taken by the couple.
    • In modern contexts, the mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of respecting natural elements, such as fire, which can be both a source of warmth and destruction. This duality teaches us to approach nature with reverence and responsibility.
    • The qualities of strength and vitality associated with the horse can be applied to personal development, encouraging individuals to harness their inner power and pursue their goals with determination.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of reverence for Agni, the fire god, and highlights the importance of humility, strength, and the role of rituals in spiritual practice.
    • Its significance extends beyond religious contexts, serving as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of respecting natural forces, and the pursuit of personal growth.
    • In everyday life, this mantra encourages individuals to approach their goals with dedication and respect for the energies that govern their existence, fostering a deeper connection with both the material and spiritual realms.
