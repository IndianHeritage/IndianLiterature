वयं उ त्वा दिवा सुते वयं नक्तं हवामहे |अस्माकं कामम् आ पृण ||
vayam u tvā divā sute vayaṁ naktaṁ havāmahe | asmākaṁ kāmam ā pṛṇa ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vayam: We
    • u: indeed
    • tvā: you
    • divā: in the daytime
    • sute: in the offerings
    • vayaṁ: we
    • naktaṁ: at night
    • havāmahe: we invoke
    • asmākaṁ: our
    • kāmam: desires
    • ā: to
    • pṛṇa: fulfill
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra expresses a collective invocation to a divine entity, asking for the fulfillment of desires both during the day and at night. It emphasizes the importance of offerings and the continuous connection with the divine for the realization of one's aspirations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Daily Prayers: Just as individuals may start their day with prayers or meditation, this mantra reflects the practice of seeking divine guidance and support throughout the day and night.
    • Community Gatherings: In many cultures, communities come together to offer prayers for collective well-being, similar to how this mantra invokes a shared desire for fulfillment.
    • Personal Goals: When setting personal goals, individuals often visualize their aspirations and seek motivation, akin to invoking divine support for achieving those goals.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of maintaining a spiritual connection in daily life, reminding us that our desires and aspirations can be supported through divine intervention.
    • It encourages individuals to be mindful of their intentions and to seek fulfillment not just for themselves but also for their community.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in rituals, personal reflections, and community events where collective aspirations are shared and nurtured.
