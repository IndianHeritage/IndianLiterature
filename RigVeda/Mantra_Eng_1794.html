तद वः सुजातā मरुतो महित्वनं दीर्घं वो दात्रम अदितेर इव व्रतम् | इन्द्रश चन त्यजसā विह्रुणाति तज जनाय यस्मै सुक्रते अराध्वम् ||
tad vaḥ sujātā maruto mahitvanaṁ dīrghaṁ vo dātram aditer iva vratam | indraś cana tyajasā vi hruṇāti taj janāya yasmai sukṛte arādhvam ||
  • tad vaḥ sujātā maruto mahitvanaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "That which is well-born, O Maruts, of great power." Here, "Maruts" refers to the storm deities, symbolizing strength and vitality. In real life, this can be likened to the importance of nurturing and supporting those who are strong and capable in our communities, such as leaders or innovators.
  • dīrghaṁ vo dātram aditer iva vratam: This translates to "May your protection be long-lasting, like the vow of Aditi." Aditi is the mother of the gods and represents boundless space and protection. In practical terms, this can be seen in the way we seek long-term security and stability in our lives, whether through relationships, careers, or community support.
  • indraś cana tyajasā vi hruṇāti: This means "Indra, do not abandon us; protect us." Indra is the king of the gods and symbolizes power and authority. This line emphasizes the need for guidance and protection from higher powers or authorities in our lives, akin to how we look to mentors or leaders for support during challenging times.
  • taj janāya yasmai sukṛte arādhvam: This translates to "To that person, may you offer your good deeds." This line encourages us to direct our positive actions and intentions towards those who are deserving, fostering a sense of community and mutual support. In real life, this can be reflected in acts of kindness, charity, or community service.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, protection, and community support. It highlights the need for long-lasting relationships and the role of higher powers in guiding us. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as seeking mentorship, building supportive communities, and engaging in acts of kindness. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and the nurturing of positive relationships, which are essential for personal and communal growth.
