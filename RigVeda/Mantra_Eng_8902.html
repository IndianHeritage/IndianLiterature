सप्त क्षरन्ति शिशवे मरुत्वते पित्रे पुत्रासो अप्य अवीवतन्न ऋतम् |उभे इद अस्योभयस्य राजत उभे यतेते उभयस्य पुष्यतः ||
sapta kṣaranti śiśave marutvate pitre putrāso apy avīvatann ṛtam | ubhe id asyobhayasya rājata ubhe yatete ubhayasya puṣyataḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Sapta: Seven
    • Kṣaranti: Flowing or moving
    • Śiśave: To the child or offspring
    • Marutvate: To the winds or the forces of nature
    • Pitre: To the father or ancestors
    • Putrāso: Sons or children
    • Api: Also
    • Avīvatann: Not separating or dividing
    • Ṛtam: Truth or cosmic order
    • Ubhe: Both
    • Id: This
    • Asyobhayasya: Of both these
    • Rājata: Shine or radiate
    • Yatete: Strive or work
    • Puṣyataḥ: To flourish or prosper
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the interconnectedness of life, emphasizing the importance of family, nature, and truth.
    • The "seven" could symbolize completeness or the various aspects of life that flow together, much like the seven colors of a rainbow or the seven days of the week.
    • The reference to "winds" and "children" suggests a harmony between the forces of nature and human existence, indicating that both are essential for life to thrive.
    • The mantra also highlights the significance of ancestors and the continuity of lineage, suggesting that the blessings of the past influence the present and future.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals are performed to honor ancestors, acknowledging their role in shaping the present. For instance, during the festival of Pitru Paksha in Hinduism, families pay homage to their forefathers.
    • The concept of environmental stewardship can be linked to the "winds" and "nature" mentioned in the mantra. For example, community efforts to combat climate change reflect the understanding that human actions impact the natural world.
    • In family dynamics, the idea of children being a continuation of their parents' legacy is evident in practices such as passing down family traditions, values, and stories.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of life’s interconnectedness, urging individuals to recognize their place within the larger cosmic order.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to honor our roots, nurture our relationships, and strive for truth and harmony in our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire us to engage in practices that promote family unity, environmental responsibility, and respect for our ancestors, ultimately leading to a more balanced and fulfilling existence.
