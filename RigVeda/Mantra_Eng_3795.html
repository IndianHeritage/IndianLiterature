त्वं हि मानवसे जन एग्ने सुप्रीत इध्यसे |श्रुचस त्वा यन्त्य अनुषक सुजात सर्पिरासुते ||
tvaṁ hi mānuṣe jane 'gne suprīta idhyase | srucas tvā yanty ānuṣak sujāta sarpirāsute ||
  • tvaṁ hi mānuṣe jane 'gne suprīta idhyase
    • tvaṁ hi - "You indeed" - This phrase emphasizes the presence and importance of the addressed entity, often referring to a divine or revered figure.
    • mānuṣe jane - "among human beings" - This indicates the context of human existence and the divine's relationship with humanity.
    • 'gne - "O Agni" - Agni, the fire god, symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
    • suprīta - "well-pleased" - This suggests a state of satisfaction or happiness, often in response to offerings or devotion.
    • idhyase - "you manifest" - This indicates the active presence and influence of the divine in the world.
  • srucas tvā yanty ānuṣak sujāta sarpirāsute
    • srucas - "with offerings" - Refers to the rituals and offerings made to the divine, particularly in the context of fire sacrifices.
    • tvā - "to you" - Directly addressing Agni, indicating the focus of devotion and offerings.
    • yanty - "they go" - This implies the movement or transfer of offerings towards the divine.
    • ānuṣak - "following" - Suggests a sense of continuity and tradition in the act of worship.
    • sujāta - "well-born" - This term often refers to the purity and quality of the offerings made.
    • sarpirāsute - "with ghee" - Ghee is a sacred substance in Vedic rituals, symbolizing nourishment and purity.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the relationship between humanity and the divine, particularly through the figure of Agni. It emphasizes the importance of offerings and rituals in establishing a connection with the divine. In real life, this can be seen in various cultural practices where people engage in rituals to seek blessings, express gratitude, or invoke divine presence. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the sacredness of offerings and the importance of maintaining a harmonious relationship with the divine through acts of devotion. It encourages individuals to recognize the transformative power of rituals in their lives, fostering a sense of community and spiritual connection.
