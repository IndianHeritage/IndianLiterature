अव द्रुग्धानि पितृया सृजा नोऽव यां वयं चक्रिमा तनुभिः |अव राजन पशुतृपं न तायुं सृजा वत्सं न दाम्नो वसिष्ठम् ||
ava drugdhāni pitryā sṛjā no 'va yā vayaṁ cakṛmā tanūbhiḥ | ava rājan paśutṛpaṁ na tāyuṁ sṛjā vatsaṁ na dāmno vasiṣṭham ||
  • Mantra: "ava drugdhāni pitryā sṛjā no 'va yā vayaṁ cakṛmā tanūbhiḥ | ava rājan paśutṛpaṁ na tāyuṁ sṛjā vatsaṁ na dāmno vasiṣṭham ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "May we be blessed with the nourishment from our ancestors; may we create a strong foundation with our bodies. O King, may we not be deprived of our cattle, may we not be deprived of our offspring, and may we not be deprived of the best of our possessions."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ava: a term indicating a request or a plea.
    • drugdhāni: refers to nourishment or sustenance.
    • pitryā: related to ancestors or forefathers.
    • sṛjā: to create or to generate.
    • no: means 'for us' or 'to us.'
    • va: a conjunction meaning 'or' or 'and.'
    • yā: which or that.
    • vayaṁ: we or us.
    • cakṛmā: we have created or we have made.
    • tanūbhiḥ: with our bodies.
    • rājan: O King, a respectful address.
    • paśutṛpaṁ: cattle or livestock.
    • na: not.
    • tāyuṁ: offspring or progeny.
    • vatsaṁ: calf or young one, often used metaphorically for children.
    • dāmno: possessions or wealth.
    • vasiṣṭham: the best or the most excellent.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of ancestral blessings. For instance, in many cultures, people honor their ancestors through rituals, believing that their blessings provide guidance and support in life.
    • The plea for nourishment can be seen in modern contexts where families gather for meals, symbolizing the sustenance provided by previous generations and the importance of family bonds.
    • The reference to cattle and offspring highlights the significance of wealth and family in agricultural societies. For example, farmers often pray for healthy livestock and prosperous harvests, reflecting the mantra's essence of seeking abundance.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of life, the importance of honoring our ancestors, and the need for sustenance—both physical and spiritual. It serves as a reminder to appreciate our roots and the blessings we receive.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in family gatherings, agricultural practices, and rituals that honor ancestors, reinforcing the values of gratitude, respect, and the pursuit of prosperity.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to seek a harmonious balance between honoring the past and nurturing the future, ensuring a legacy of abundance and well-being.
