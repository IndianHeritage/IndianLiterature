आयम् भराय सानसिर इन्द्राय पवते सुतः |सोमो जैत्रस्य चेतति यथा विदे ||
ayam bharāya sānasir indrāya pavate sutaḥ | somo jaitrasya cetati yathā vide ||
  • ayam: This word translates to "this" or "this one," indicating a specific focus or subject.
  • bharāya: This means "to support" or "to uphold," suggesting a sense of responsibility or nurturing.
  • sānasir: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is strong" or "the powerful," often referring to a deity or a force of nature.
  • indrāya: This refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • pavate: This means "to blow" or "to move," often associated with the wind or breath, indicating a dynamic force.
  • sutaḥ: This translates to "son" or "offspring," which can signify lineage or the results of one's actions.
  • somo: This refers to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing vitality and divine inspiration.
  • jaitrasya: This means "of victory," indicating success or triumph in endeavors.
  • cetati: This translates to "to think" or "to reflect," emphasizing the importance of contemplation and awareness.
  • yathā: This means "as" or "like," used to draw comparisons or illustrate a point.
  • vide: This can be interpreted as "to know" or "to understand," highlighting the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom.

The mantra can be understood as a call to the divine forces, particularly Indra and Soma, to support and empower the individual in their pursuits. It emphasizes the importance of strength, victory, and the nurturing of one's endeavors through divine assistance.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: When facing challenges, one can invoke the strength of Indra and the vitality of Soma to overcome obstacles and achieve personal goals.
  • Leadership: Leaders can reflect on this mantra to inspire their teams, reminding them of the importance of support and collective strength in achieving success.
  • Spiritual Practices: During meditation or rituals, reciting this mantra can help individuals connect with divine energies, fostering a sense of empowerment and clarity.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support and strength in the pursuit of victory and knowledge. It serves as a reminder that, with the right mindset and divine assistance, individuals can navigate life's challenges and achieve their aspirations. By integrating its teachings into daily life, one can cultivate resilience, leadership, and a deeper understanding of their purpose.
