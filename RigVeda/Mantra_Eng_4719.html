इन्द्रो वाजस्य स्थविरस्य दातेंद्रो गीरभिर वर्धतां वृद्धमहाः |इन्द्रो वृत्रं हनिष्ठो अस्तु सत्वा ता सूरिः पृणति तूतुजानः ||
indro vājasya sthavirasya dātendro gīrbhir vardhatāṁ vṛddhamahāḥ | indro vṛtraṁ haniṣṭho astu satvā tā sūriḥ pṛṇati tūtujānaḥ ||
  • Indro vājasya sthavirasya dātendro: This phrase refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, who is associated with strength and power. The term "vājasya" signifies the essence of strength and vigor, while "sthavirasya" implies stability and endurance. "Dātendro" suggests the giver of strength.
  • gīrbhir vardhatāṁ vṛddhamahāḥ: Here, "gīrbhir" means through hymns or praises. "Vardhatāṁ" indicates growth or enhancement, and "vṛddhamahāḥ" refers to the great and powerful. This line emphasizes the importance of praise and devotion in enhancing one's strength and stability.
  • indro vṛtraṁ haniṣṭho astu: "Indro" again refers to the deity, while "vṛtraṁ" symbolizes obstacles or challenges. "Haniṣṭho astu" means may he (Indra) destroy or overcome these challenges. This line highlights the role of divine intervention in overcoming difficulties.
  • sattvā tā sūriḥ pṛṇati tūtujānaḥ: "Sattvā" refers to the essence of being or existence, "tā" means that, "sūriḥ" signifies the sun or light, and "pṛṇati" means to nourish or fulfill. "Tūtujānaḥ" refers to the one who is aware or enlightened. This line suggests that the divine light nourishes and fulfills the essence of existence.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, stability, and divine support in overcoming life's challenges. It emphasizes the importance of devotion and praise in enhancing one's capabilities and the role of divine forces in providing guidance and strength.
  • Real-life Application: In daily life, individuals often face various challenges, whether personal, professional, or emotional. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek strength through faith and devotion, to praise the forces that support us, and to trust in the divine to help us overcome obstacles. For example, during difficult times such as job loss or personal crises, reciting this mantra can provide comfort and a sense of empowerment, encouraging individuals to face their challenges with resilience.
