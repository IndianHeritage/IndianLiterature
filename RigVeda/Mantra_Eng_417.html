विसुपर्णो अन्तरिक्षाण्यख्यदगभीरवेपाऽसुरः सुनीतः |क्वेदानिं सूर्यः कश्चिकेत कतमां द्यां रश्मिरस्याततान ||
vi suparṇo antarikṣāṇy akhyad gabhīravepā asuraḥ sunīthaḥ | kve3dānīṁ sūryaḥ kaś ciketa katamāṁ dyāṁ raśmir asyā tatāna ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vi suparṇo: "two beautiful birds" - often interpreted as the duality of the soul or consciousness.
    • antarikṣāṇy: "in the sky" - representing the vastness of the universe.
    • akhyad: "has declared" - indicating a proclamation or revelation.
    • gabhīravepā: "deep and profound" - suggesting depth in understanding or knowledge.
    • asuraḥ: "the divine" or "the powerful" - often referring to higher spiritual beings.
    • sunīthaḥ: "well-directed" - implying guidance or a clear path.
    • kve3dānīṁ: "where now" - questioning the current state or situation.
    • sūryaḥ: "the sun" - symbolizing light, knowledge, and enlightenment.
    • kaś: "who" - a query about identity or existence.
    • ciketa: "has perceived" - indicating awareness or understanding.
    • katamāṁ: "which" - referring to a specific choice or path.
    • dyāṁ: "heaven" - representing the higher realms or spiritual aspirations.
    • raśmir: "ray" - a metaphor for illumination or insight.
    • asyā: "of this" - indicating possession or belonging.
    • tatāna: "has spread" - suggesting the dissemination of knowledge or light.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the duality of existence, represented by the two birds, which can symbolize the physical and spiritual aspects of life.
    • The mention of the sky and the sun emphasizes the vastness of knowledge and the pursuit of enlightenment.
    • It raises questions about perception and understanding, urging individuals to seek clarity in their lives.
    • The reference to divine beings and guidance suggests that there is a higher power or wisdom that can lead us on our journey.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often face choices that reflect the duality of their desires and responsibilities, akin to the two birds.
    • In education, the pursuit of knowledge can be seen as a journey towards enlightenment, much like the rays of the sun illuminating the path.
    • In moments of confusion, asking "who" or "where" can lead to deeper self-reflection and understanding, guiding one towards clarity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encourages individuals to explore the duality of their existence and seek enlightenment through knowledge and self-awareness.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that there is a divine guidance available to those who seek it, and that understanding one's path is essential for personal growth.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as decision-making, personal growth, and the pursuit of knowledge, emphasizing the importance of seeking clarity and direction in our lives.
