ध्वस्रयोः पुरुषन्त्योऽसहस्राणि दद्महे |तरत स मन्दी धावति ||
dhvasrayoḥ puruṣantyor ā sahasrāṇi dadmahe | tarat sa mandī dhāvati ||
  • Mantra: "dhvasrayoḥ puruṣantyor ā sahasrāṇi dadmahe | tarat sa mandī dhāvati ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dhvasrayoḥ: of the two (or more) who are in a state of motion or action
    • puruṣantyor: between the two beings or individuals
    • ā: towards or in the direction of
    • sahasrāṇi: thousands or multitude
    • dadmahe: we offer or we dedicate
    • tarat: swiftly or quickly
    • sa: that or he
    • mandī: slow or sluggish
    • dhāvati: runs or moves
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the dynamic relationship between two entities, emphasizing the act of offering or dedicating something valuable to them.
    • The phrase "sahasrāṇi dadmahe" suggests a sense of abundance and generosity, indicating that we are willing to offer thousands of whatever is being referred to, which can be interpreted as blessings, resources, or support.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "tarat sa mandī dhāvati," contrasts the swift and the slow, possibly indicating the different paces at which individuals or forces operate in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, one might see the dynamic between two team members where one is quick to act (the swift) while the other takes a more measured approach (the slow). Understanding this dynamic can help in assigning roles that play to each person's strengths.
    • In a community, the act of offering resources (like food, time, or support) to those in need can be seen as a practical application of "sahasrāṇi dadmahe," where the community comes together to support its members abundantly.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of recognizing the interplay between different individuals and their varying paces in life.
    • It encourages us to offer our resources generously to others, fostering a sense of community and support.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as teamwork, community service, and interpersonal relationships, reminding us that while some may move quickly, others may need more time, and both are valuable in their own right.
