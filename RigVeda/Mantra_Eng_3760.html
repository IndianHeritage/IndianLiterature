तं अध्वरेषु ईळते देवम् मर्ता अमर्त्यम् | यजिष्टम् मानवसे जनै ||
tam adhvareṣv īḻate devam martā amartyam | yajiṣṭham mānuṣe jane ||
  • tam - This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to the divine or the ultimate reality.
  • adhvareṣv - This term means "in the sacrifices" or "in the rituals," indicating the context of worship and offerings made to the divine.
  • īḻate - This means "shines" or "is worshipped," suggesting the presence and acknowledgment of the divine in the rituals.
  • devam - This translates to "the god" or "the divine being," emphasizing the focus of the worship.
  • martā - This word means "mortal" or "human," highlighting the human condition and the limitations of earthly existence.
  • amartyam - This translates to "immortal" or "eternal," contrasting the mortal nature of humans with the divine's eternal essence.
  • yajiṣṭham - This means "the most worthy of worship," indicating the supreme value of the divine in the context of human life.
  • mānuṣe - This translates to "human" or "of mankind," reinforcing the connection between the divine and humanity.
  • jane - This means "among people" or "in society," suggesting the communal aspect of worship and the importance of collective rituals.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to recognize and worship the divine presence that shines in the rituals, acknowledging the contrast between the mortal human experience and the immortal nature of the divine. It emphasizes the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine and highlights the value of worship in human society.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Rituals and Worship: It encourages individuals and communities to engage in rituals that honor the divine, fostering a sense of connection and spirituality.
  • Recognition of Mortality: It serves as a reminder of the transient nature of human life, prompting individuals to reflect on their actions and the legacy they wish to leave behind.
  • Community and Society: The mantra underscores the importance of collective worship and the role of community in spiritual practices, promoting unity and shared values.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the relationship between the divine and humanity, emphasizing the importance of worship and rituals in bridging the gap between the mortal and the immortal. It serves as a guiding principle for individuals and communities to engage in meaningful spiritual practices that enhance their connection to the divine and to each other.
