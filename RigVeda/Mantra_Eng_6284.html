यो नो देवः परावतः सखित्वनाय मामहे |दिवो न वृष्टिं प्रथयन ववक्षिथ ||
yo no devaḥ parāvataḥ sakhitvanāya māmahe | divo na vṛṣṭim prathayan vavakṣitha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • no: our
    • devaḥ: deity or god
    • parāvataḥ: of the highest or supreme
    • sakhitvanāya: for friendship or companionship
    • māmahe: we invoke or we call upon
    • divo: of the sky or heaven
    • na: not
    • vṛṣṭim: rain or abundance
    • prathayan: spreading or manifesting
    • vavakṣitha: you have declared or you have shown
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the supreme deity, asking for companionship and the blessings of rain or abundance.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine friendship and the need for the deity to manifest prosperity in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain to ensure a good harvest. This mantra can be seen as a call for divine assistance in achieving that abundance.
    • In personal relationships, the idea of invoking a higher power for companionship can be reflected in how individuals seek guidance and support in their friendships and partnerships.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine, suggesting that seeking a relationship with a higher power can lead to blessings in various aspects of life.
    • It is applicable in daily life when individuals face challenges, reminding them to seek support and abundance through spiritual practices, whether in times of need or gratitude.
