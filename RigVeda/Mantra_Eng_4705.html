मन्त्र: कदā भुवन रथक्षयाणि ब्रह्म कदā स्तोत्रे सहस्रपोष्यं दाः |कदā स्तोमं वासयोऽस्य राया कदā धियः करसि वाजरत्नाः ||
kadā bhuvan rathakṣayāṇi brahma kadā stotre sahasrapoṣyaṁ dāḥ | kadā stomaṁ vāsayo 'sya rāyā kadā dhiyaḥ karasi vājaratnāḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "When will the world be free from the destruction of the chariot? When will the offerings be made to the thousand-fold nourisher? When will the hymn be adorned with wealth? When will you, O Divine, bestow your precious thoughts?"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • kadā - When
    • bhuvan - World or universe
    • rathakṣayāṇi - Destruction of the chariot (symbolizing conflict or war)
    • brahma - The ultimate reality or divine consciousness
    • stotre - Offerings or praises
    • sahasrapoṣyaṁ - Thousand-fold nourisher (representing abundance)
    • dāḥ - Bestow or grant
    • stomaṁ - Hymn or praise
    • vāsayo - Adorn or decorate
    • 'sya - Of this
    • rāyā - Wealth or prosperity
    • dhiyaḥ - Thoughts or intellect
    • karasi - You do or bestow
    • vājaratnāḥ - Precious thoughts (symbolizing wisdom and clarity)
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The destruction of the chariot can symbolize the conflicts and wars in our world today. For instance, the ongoing conflicts in various regions remind us of the need for peace and harmony.
    • The concept of the thousand-fold nourisher can be related to nature's abundance, such as the way a single seed can produce a bountiful harvest, emphasizing the importance of sustainability and gratitude towards nature.
    • The hymn adorned with wealth can be seen in cultural practices where communities come together to celebrate and share their resources, such as during festivals or communal meals, fostering unity and prosperity.
    • The plea for divine thoughts can be reflected in the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom in our daily lives, such as through education, meditation, or community discussions that aim to elevate our understanding and consciousness.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for peace, abundance, and wisdom. It encourages individuals and communities to seek harmony in a world often plagued by conflict, to appreciate the richness of life, and to cultivate a mindset of gratitude and clarity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire actions towards conflict resolution, environmental sustainability, and the pursuit of knowledge, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and prosperous existence for all. By embodying these principles, we can contribute to a world that reflects the ideals expressed in this ancient wisdom.
