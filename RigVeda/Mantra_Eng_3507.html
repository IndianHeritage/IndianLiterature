तां वां धियोऽवसे वाजयन्तीर आजिं न जग्मुर युवयूः सुदानू |श्रिये न गावा उप सोमं अस्तुर्इन्द्रं गिर्व वरुणं मे मनिषाः ||
tā vāṁ dhiyo 'vase vājayantīr ājiṁ na jagmur yuvayūḥ sudānū | śriye na gāva upa somam asthur indraṁ giro varuṇam me manīṣāḥ ||
  • tā vāṁ dhiyo 'vase vājayantīr: This phrase can be translated as "May our intellects be directed towards you." Here, "tā vāṁ" refers to the divine or higher consciousness, while "dhiyo" means intellect or understanding. The invocation suggests a desire for guidance and clarity in thought.
  • ājiṁ na jagmur yuvayūḥ sudānū: This part translates to "May we not stray from the path, O youthful ones." The term "yuvayūḥ" signifies youth or vitality, indicating a call for the energy and enthusiasm of youth to remain steadfast and not deviate from righteousness.
  • śriye na gāva upa somam asthur: This translates to "May we attain prosperity and abundance." "Śriye" refers to wealth or prosperity, while "gāva" means cows, which are often symbolic of wealth in Vedic literature. "Upa somam asthur" suggests a yearning for the blessings of abundance and nourishment.
  • indraṁ giro varuṇam me manīṣāḥ: This can be interpreted as "May my thoughts be aligned with Indra and Varuna." Indra is the king of gods and represents strength and power, while Varuna symbolizes cosmic order and truth. This line emphasizes the importance of aligning one's thoughts with divine principles and moral order.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the importance of intellect, moral integrity, prosperity, and alignment with divine forces. It serves as a reminder to seek guidance from higher consciousness and to remain steadfast in one's values.
  • Real-life Application: In today's fast-paced world, individuals often face distractions and moral dilemmas. This mantra can be a source of inspiration for students, professionals, and anyone seeking clarity in their decisions. For instance, a student may recite this mantra before an exam to seek focus and guidance, while a business leader might reflect on it when making ethical decisions.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encourages individuals to cultivate a strong intellect, remain true to their values, and seek prosperity through righteous means. By invoking the divine and aligning one's thoughts with higher principles, one can navigate life's challenges with wisdom and integrity.
