यद् वा प्रवृद्ध सत्पते न मरा इति मन्यसे | उतो तात सत्यं इति तव ||
yad vā pravṛddha satpate na marā iti manyase | uto tat satyam it tava ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • vā: or
    • pravṛddha: increased, grown, or developed
    • satpate: to the lord of truth or the righteous
    • na: not
    • marā: death
    • iti: thus
    • manyase: you think or you believe
    • uto: and
    • tat: that
    • satyam: truth
    • it: this
    • tava: of you
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects a profound philosophical inquiry into the nature of truth and existence.
    • It suggests that one should contemplate the essence of what is true and eternal, beyond the transient nature of life and death.
    • The phrase "yad vā pravṛddha satpate" implies that whatever is developed or grown in the realm of truth is significant.
    • The assertion "na marā iti manyase" challenges the belief in death as an end, suggesting that true existence transcends physical demise.
    • Finally, "uto tat satyam it tava" emphasizes that the essence of truth is inherent to one's being and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the context of personal growth, individuals often face challenges that may seem insurmountable. This mantra encourages them to seek the deeper truth in their experiences, understanding that growth often comes from overcoming adversity.
    • In philosophical discussions, this mantra can be applied to debates about the nature of life and death, prompting individuals to consider the possibility of an existence beyond physical life.
    • In spiritual practices, such as meditation, practitioners may reflect on this mantra to cultivate a sense of inner peace and understanding of their true nature, which is not limited by the physical body.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking truth and understanding the eternal aspects of existence.
    • It encourages individuals to look beyond the superficial and transient, fostering a deeper connection with their true selves and the universe.
    • In real-life scenarios, this understanding can lead to greater resilience, peace, and a more profound appreciation for the journey of life.
