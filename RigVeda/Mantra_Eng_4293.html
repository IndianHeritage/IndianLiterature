सम अश्विनोर्वसानूतनेन मयोभुवा सुप्रणीति गमेमा |आ नो रयिं वहतमोता वीरानां विश्वानि अमृतासौभागानि ||
sam aśvinor avasā nūtanena mayobhuvā supraṇītī gamema | ā no rayiṁ vahatam ota vīrān ā viśvāny amṛtā saubhagāni ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May we, with the new strength of the Aśvins, attain the well-directed path. May we carry the wealth of the brave and may all beings be blessed with immortality and prosperity."
  • Explanation of Key Terms:
    • Aśvins: The Aśvins are twin deities in Vedic mythology, often associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between opposites.
    • Mayobhuvā: This term refers to the new strength or energy that one can harness. It signifies the potential for renewal and rejuvenation.
    • Supranītī: This means a well-directed or righteous path. It emphasizes the importance of following a path that is beneficial and aligned with higher principles.
    • Rayiṁ: This term refers to wealth or prosperity. It encompasses not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional richness.
    • Vīrān: This means the brave or the valiant. It highlights the qualities of courage and strength in facing life's challenges.
    • Amṛtā: This term signifies immortality or eternal life, representing the ultimate goal of human existence.
    • Saubhagāni: This refers to auspiciousness or good fortune, indicating a state of well-being and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, the mantra can be applied when a team embarks on a new project. The Aśvins symbolize the collaboration and dual efforts of team members, while the new strength (mayobhuvā) represents the innovative ideas they bring to the table.
    • In personal life, individuals can invoke this mantra during times of transition, such as starting a new job or moving to a new city. It encourages them to seek a righteous path (supranītī) and to embrace the wealth (rayiṁ) of experiences that come with change.
    • In community service, this mantra can inspire collective efforts to uplift the less fortunate. By embodying the qualities of bravery (vīrān) and seeking prosperity (saubhagāni) for all, communities can work towards a more equitable society.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for renewal, courage, and collective prosperity. It serves as a reminder that by harnessing new strengths and following a righteous path, individuals and communities can achieve not only material wealth but also spiritual fulfillment and well-being. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal growth, professional endeavors, or community initiatives, this mantra encourages a holistic approach to life that values both individual and collective success.
