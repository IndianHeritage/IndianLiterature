आ ते सिञ्चामि कुक्ष्योः अनु गात्रा विघावतु |गृहाय जिव्हया मधु ||
ā te siñcāmi kukṣyor anu gātrā vi dhāvatu | gṛbhāya jihvayā madhu ||
  • Mantra: "ā te siñcāmi kukṣyor anu gātrā vi dhāvatu | gṛbhāya jihvayā madhu ||"
  • Translation: "I pour forth (or sprinkle) for you, may the essence flow through your body; may it be received by your tongue as sweetness."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ā te: "for you" - indicating a personal offering or dedication.
    • siñcāmi: "I pour" or "I sprinkle" - a gesture of bestowing or sharing something valuable.
    • kukṣyor: "from the womb" - symbolizing the source of life and nourishment.
    • anu: "through" or "along" - suggesting a flow or movement.
    • gātrā: "body" - referring to the physical form or being.
    • vi dhāvatu: "may it flow" - a wish for the essence to circulate and permeate.
    • gṛbhāya: "to be received" - indicating acceptance or assimilation.
    • jihvayā: "by the tongue" - the organ of taste and expression.
    • madhu: "sweetness" - representing pleasure, joy, and nourishment.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra can be likened to the act of sharing food at a family gathering, where the essence of love and care is poured into the meal, nourishing both the body and spirit of those present.
    • In a spiritual context, it can be related to the practice of chanting or reciting mantras, where the sound and intention are believed to flow through the practitioner, bringing peace and sweetness to their life.
    • In a therapeutic setting, it can symbolize the importance of positive affirmations and words of encouragement, which can uplift and nourish an individual’s mental and emotional well-being.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of sharing and receiving nourishment, both physically and spiritually. It highlights the interconnectedness of life, where the essence of one can flow into another, creating a cycle of giving and receiving.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to be mindful of the words we speak and the intentions we set, as they can have a profound impact on ourselves and those around us.
    • Ultimately, the mantra encourages us to cultivate sweetness in our interactions and to recognize the sacredness in the act of sharing, whether it be food, words, or love.
