मन्त्र: सुन्वान इति सिषासति सहस्रā वाज्य अवृतः |सुन्वानायेंद्रो ददाति आबुवं रयिं ददाति आबुवम् ||
sunvāna it siṣāsati sahasrā vājy avṛtaḥ | sunvānāyendro dadāty ābhuvaṁ rayiṁ dadāty ābhuvam ||
  • Mantra: "sunvāna it siṣāsati sahasrā vājy avṛtaḥ | sunvānāyendro dadāty ābhuvaṁ rayiṁ dadāty ābhuvam ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "He who listens (to the divine) is endowed with a thousand riches; the divine Indra grants him abundant wealth."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • sunvāna: One who listens or hears.
    • it: This (referring to the divine or the teachings).
    • siṣāsati: To be endowed or to possess.
    • sahasrā: Thousand, symbolizing abundance.
    • vājy: Riches or wealth.
    • avṛtaḥ: Covered or protected.
    • indro: Referring to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic tradition.
    • dadāty: Grants or gives.
    • ābhuvaṁ: Abundant or plentiful.
    • rayiṁ: Wealth or prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Listening to wise counsel: In a corporate setting, a manager who listens to feedback from their team is more likely to make informed decisions that lead to the success of the project, thus gaining 'riches' in terms of productivity and morale.
    • Education: Students who actively listen to their teachers and engage with the material often achieve better academic results, which can lead to scholarships and career opportunities, symbolizing the 'thousand riches' of knowledge and success.
    • Spiritual Growth: Individuals who listen to spiritual teachings and apply them in their lives often find peace and fulfillment, which can be seen as a form of wealth that transcends material possessions.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in the emphasis on the power of listening and the rewards that come from it. In real life, whether in personal relationships, professional environments, or spiritual journeys, the act of listening can lead to greater understanding, prosperity, and fulfillment. By being receptive to wisdom and guidance, individuals can unlock abundant opportunities and enrich their lives in various dimensions. This mantra serves as a reminder that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses knowledge, relationships, and spiritual growth.
