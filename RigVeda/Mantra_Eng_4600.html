स इति तमोऽवयुनं ततान्वत सूर्येण वयुनवच्चकारे |कदाते मर्ता अमृतस्य धामेयक्षन्तो न मिनन्ति स्वधावः ||
sa it tamo 'vayunaṁ tatanvat sūryeṇa vayunavac cakāra | kadā te martā amṛtasya dhāmeyakṣanto na minanti svadhāvaḥ ||
  • sa it tamo 'vayunaṁ tatanvat sūryeṇa vayunavac cakāra
    • sa: This word translates to "he" or "that," indicating a subject or a divine presence.
    • it: Means "here" or "this," suggesting a focus on the present moment or situation.
    • tamo: Refers to "darkness" or "ignorance," symbolizing obstacles or challenges in life.
    • 'vayunaṁ: This can be interpreted as "by the power" or "through the energy," indicating a force that can overcome darkness.
    • tatanvat: Means "spreading" or "expanding," suggesting the dissemination of light or knowledge.
    • sūryeṇa: Refers to "by the sun," symbolizing enlightenment, vitality, and the source of life.
    • vayunavac: This can be understood as "like the power," reinforcing the idea of strength and energy.
    • cakāra: Means "created" or "manifested," indicating the act of bringing forth light or knowledge.
  • kadā te martā amṛtasya dhāmeyakṣanto na minanti svadhāvaḥ
    • kadā: Translates to "when," indicating a time or condition.
    • te: Means "your," referring to a divine entity or higher power.
    • martā: Refers to "mortals" or "human beings," emphasizing the human experience.
    • amṛtasya: Means "of immortality," symbolizing eternal truth or the essence of life.
    • dhāmeyakṣanto: This can be interpreted as "those who seek the abode," referring to the pursuit of higher knowledge or enlightenment.
    • na minanti: Means "do not diminish" or "do not falter," indicating resilience and strength.
    • svadhāvaḥ: Refers to "by their own nature," suggesting that individuals have inherent qualities that guide them.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the transition from darkness to light, both literally and metaphorically. It highlights the power of knowledge and enlightenment, represented by the sun, to overcome ignorance and challenges in life. The mantra suggests that when individuals seek higher truths and remain steadfast in their pursuit, they can attain a state of immortality or eternal wisdom.


In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal growth and development. For instance, when faced with difficulties, individuals can draw upon their inner strength and seek knowledge to navigate through challenges. This mantra serves as a reminder that enlightenment and resilience are attainable through conscious effort and the pursuit of truth.
