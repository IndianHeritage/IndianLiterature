अहम् इन्द्रो न परा जिग्य इद् धनं न मृत्यवेऽव तस्थे कदा चन |सोमं इन् मा सुन्वन्तो याचतā वसु न मे पूर्वः सख्ये ऋषाथन ||
aham indro na parā jigya id dhanaṁ na mṛtyave 'va tasthe kadā cana | somam in mā sunvanto yācatā vasu na me pūravaḥ sakhye riṣāthana ||
  • Translation: "I am Indra, I do not desire wealth that leads to death; may I never be deprived of the soma, which is the essence of life, and may my friends not be harmed."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • aham: I
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength and power
    • na: not
    • parā: supreme or excessive
    • jigya: desire or seek
    • id: this
    • dhanam: wealth
    • mṛtyave: to death
    • va: or
    • tasthe: may I stand or remain
    • kadā: when
    • cana: at any time
    • somam: soma, a sacred drink that symbolizes immortality and vitality
    • in: in
    • mā: not
    • sunvanto: those who listen or hear
    • yācatā: may I ask or request
    • vasu: wealth or good things
    • na: not
    • me: my
    • pūravaḥ: those who are before or my predecessors
    • sakhye: in friendship
    • riṣāthana: may they not be harmed
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking true wealth, which is not material but rather spiritual and communal.
    • It reflects a desire for life and vitality over material possessions that lead to death, highlighting the transient nature of physical wealth.
    • The invocation of Indra symbolizes strength and the pursuit of a life filled with vitality and friendship.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied to the pursuit of meaningful relationships and experiences rather than just material success.
    • For example, individuals may choose to invest time in community service or building friendships rather than solely focusing on career advancement.
    • It encourages a mindset of valuing health, well-being, and the support of friends over the accumulation of wealth that may not bring true happiness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder to prioritize what truly matters in life—relationships, health, and spiritual wealth—over the fleeting nature of material possessions.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a more fulfilling and meaningful existence, fostering connections that enrich their lives and the lives of those around them.
