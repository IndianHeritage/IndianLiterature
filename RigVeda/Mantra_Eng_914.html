बर्हिर वा यत्स्वपत्याय वृत्यते 'रको वा श्लोकमाघोषते दिवि |ग्रावा यत्र वदति कारुरुक्त्यस तस्येद इन्द्रो अभिपित्वेषु रण्यति ||
barhir vā yat svapatyāya vṛjyate 'rko vā ślokam āghoṣate divi | grāvā yatra vadati kārur ukthya1s tasyed indro abhipitveṣu raṇyati ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "Whatever is outside, that which is invoked by the one who sleeps, or the one who sings in the sky, where the powerful one speaks, Indra, may he protect us in our endeavors."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • barhir: Outside or external.
    • vā: Or.
    • yat: That which.
    • svapatyāya: By the one who sleeps or rests.
    • vṛjyate: Is invoked or called upon.
    • 'rko: The one who sings or chants.
    • vā: Or.
    • ślokam: A verse or hymn.
    • āghoṣate: Resounds or echoes.
    • divi: In the sky or heavenly realm.
    • grāvā: The powerful one.
    • yatra: Where.
    • vadati: Speaks or declares.
    • kārur: Powerful or strong.
    • ukthya: Hymn or invocation.
    • tasyed: May he.
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods.
    • abhipitveṣu: In our endeavors or pursuits.
    • raṇyati: Protects or guards.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking external forces or energies in our lives. For instance, when starting a new project, individuals often seek blessings or guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders, akin to invoking Indra's protection.
    • In a corporate setting, teams may hold meetings to discuss strategies and invoke collective wisdom, similar to the hymn's call for powerful voices to speak and guide.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or inaugurations, rituals are performed to seek divine blessings, reflecting the essence of this mantra in seeking protection and success in endeavors.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for external support and divine protection in our endeavors. It teaches us the value of community, collaboration, and seeking guidance from higher powers or collective wisdom. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal or professional contexts, invoking such energies can lead to greater success and fulfillment. The mantra serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our pursuits; we can call upon the powerful forces around us to aid in our journey.
