प्र यद इथ्था महिना नृभ्यो अस्त्य अरं रोधसी कक्ष्ये नास्मै |सं विव्य इन्द्रो वृजनं न भूमाऽ भार्ति स्वधावाम् उपशम इव द्याम् ||
pra yad itthā mahinā nṛbhyo asty araṁ rodasī kakṣye3 nāsmai | saṁ vivya indro vṛjanaṁ na bhūmā bharti svadhāvām̐ opaśam iva dyām ||
  • Mantra: "pra yad itthā mahinā nṛbhyo asty araṁ rodasī kakṣye3 nāsmai | saṁ vivya indro vṛjanaṁ na bhūmā bharti svadhāvām̐ opaśam iva dyām ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: for, towards
    • yad: that which
    • itthā: thus, in this manner
    • mahinā: by the great, powerful
    • nṛbhyo: to men, to humans
    • asty: there is
    • araṁ: a path, a way
    • rodasī: in the sky, in the heavens
    • kakṣye: I will protect, I will guard
    • nāsmai: for him, for that one
    • saṁ: together, in union
    • vivya: to Indra, the god
    • vṛjanaṁ: the group, the assembly
    • na: not
    • bhūmā: the earth, the ground
    • bharti: carries, supports
    • svadhāvām̐: in its own course, naturally
    • opaśam: peace, tranquility
    • iva: like, as if
    • dyām: the sky, the heavens
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine protection and guidance provided by Indra, the king of gods in Vedic tradition.
    • The phrase "pra yad itthā mahinā nṛbhyo asty araṁ" suggests that there is a powerful path available for humans, indicating that divine support is always present.
    • "rodasī kakṣye nāsmai" emphasizes the protective nature of the divine, assuring that the heavens are safeguarding those who seek guidance.
    • The line "saṁ vivya indro vṛjanaṁ" highlights the unity and strength found in the assembly of people, suggesting that collective efforts are supported by divine forces.
    • "na bhūmā bharti svadhāvām̐" indicates that the earth itself is not the only source of support; rather, there is a natural order that brings peace and stability, akin to the tranquility of the sky.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of crisis, individuals often seek community support, reflecting the idea of "saṁ vivya" where collective strength is vital.
    • When facing challenges, people may find solace in spiritual practices, akin to seeking the protective guidance of Indra.
    • In leadership, the mantra can be applied to emphasize the importance of guiding others towards a common goal, ensuring that everyone feels supported and protected.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine guidance and protection in our lives, reminding us that we are not alone in our struggles.
    • It encourages us to seek unity and support from our communities, reinforcing the idea that collective efforts are often more powerful than individual actions.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to trust in a higher power while also fostering connections with others, leading to a more harmonious existence.
