त्वया वयं शाशद्महे रणेषु प्रपश्यन्तो युधेन्यानी भूरी |कोदायामि त आयुधा वचोभिः सं ते शिशामि ब्रह्मणा वयांसी ||
tvayā vayaṁ śāśadmahe raṇeṣu prapaśyanto yudhenyāni bhūri | codayāmi ta āyudhā vacobhiḥ saṁ te śiśāmi brahmaṇā vayāṁsi ||
  • tvayā vayaṁ śāśadmahe: This phrase can be translated as "We meditate upon you, O eternal one." Here, "tvayā" refers to the divine or the ultimate reality, while "vayaṁ" means "we." The term "śāśadmahe" signifies the eternal nature of the divine.
  • raṇeṣu prapaśyanto: This translates to "seeing in battles." It indicates the context of conflict or struggle, which can be interpreted both literally (as in physical battles) and metaphorically (as in life's challenges).
  • yudhenyāni bhūri: This means "many in battles." The word "yudhenyāni" refers to the various forms of struggle or conflict, while "bhūri" signifies abundance or multitude. This suggests that life is filled with numerous challenges.
  • codayāmi ta āyudhā: This translates to "I inspire you with weapons." "Codayāmi" means to inspire or motivate, and "āyudhā" refers to weapons or tools. This can be understood as empowering oneself or others with the necessary tools to face challenges.
  • vacobhiḥ: This word means "with words." It emphasizes the power of communication and the spoken word in motivating and inspiring action.
  • saṁ te śiśāmi brahmaṇā: This translates to "I connect you with the divine." "Saṁ" means together or with, "te" refers to you, and "brahmaṇā" signifies the ultimate reality or the divine essence. This indicates a connection to a higher power or consciousness.
  • vayāṁsi: This word means "our energies" or "our strengths." It refers to the collective strength and capabilities of individuals or a community.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of connecting with the divine to gain strength and inspiration in the face of life's battles and challenges. It highlights the significance of communication and the empowerment that comes from sharing words of encouragement. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in leadership, teamwork, and personal development. For instance, a leader can use the principles of this mantra to inspire their team during challenging projects, fostering a sense of unity and purpose. Additionally, individuals facing personal struggles can meditate on this mantra to seek strength and guidance from a higher power, reminding themselves of their inner capabilities and the support available to them. Overall, it serves as a reminder of the eternal connection between the individual and the divine, encouraging resilience and empowerment in the face of adversity.
