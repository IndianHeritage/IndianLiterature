स इद अस्तेव प्रति धाद असिष्यञ् चिशीत तेजोऽयसो न धाराम् |चित्रध्रजतिर अरतिर यो अक्तोर वेर न दृषद्वा रघुपत्मजंहाḥ ||
sa id asteva prati dhād asiṣyañ chiśīta tejo 'yaso na dhārām | citradhrajatir aratir yo aktor ver na druṣadvā raghupatmajaṁhāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sa: he, that
    • id: indeed, now
    • asteva: exists, is
    • prati: towards, in relation to
    • dhād: to give, to bestow
    • asiṣyañ: blessings, auspiciousness
    • chiśīta: cold, cool
    • tejo: light, brilliance
    • 'yaso: fame, glory
    • na: not
    • dhārām: to hold, to maintain
    • citradhrajatir: varied, colorful
    • aratir: non-attachment, detachment
    • yo: who
    • aktor: of the actor, performer
    • ver: strength, power
    • na: not
    • druṣadvā: seeing, perceiving
    • raghupatmajaṁhāḥ: son of Raghu, referring to Lord Rama
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the existence of a divine presence that bestows blessings and auspiciousness.
    • The mention of "cold light" suggests a calming, serene energy that brings peace and clarity.
    • The phrase "not holding fame" indicates a detachment from worldly recognition, emphasizing humility.
    • The reference to "the son of Raghu" (Lord Rama) signifies the ideal of righteousness and virtue.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, one might strive for success without becoming overly attached to fame or recognition, focusing instead on the quality of work and integrity.
    • In personal relationships, embodying the qualities of humility and selflessness, much like Lord Rama, can lead to deeper connections and mutual respect.
    • Practicing mindfulness and detachment can help individuals navigate challenges with a calm and clear mind, akin to the "cold light" mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of humility, detachment, and the pursuit of inner peace.
    • Its significance lies in guiding individuals to seek blessings and clarity while remaining grounded and virtuous.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence, both personally and professionally.
