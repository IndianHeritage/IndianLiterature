सं गोभिर आङ्गिरसो नक्षमााणो भग इवेद आर्यमणं निनाय || जने मित्रो न दम्पती अनक्ति बृहस्पते वाजयाशूम् इवाजौ ||
saṁ gobhir āṅgiraso nakṣamāṇo bhaga ived aryamaṇaṁ nināya | jane mitro na dampatī anakti bṛhaspate vājayāśūm̐r ivājau ||
  • saṁ gobhir: This phrase can be translated as "with the herds" or "with the cows." In Vedic culture, cows are considered sacred and symbolize wealth, nourishment, and prosperity.
  • āṅgiraso: Refers to the Angirasas, a group of sages known for their wisdom and knowledge. They are often associated with the transmission of Vedic knowledge.
  • nakṣamāṇo: This term means "having the ability" or "capable." It signifies strength and competence in achieving goals.
  • bhaga: Translated as "prosperity" or "wealth," it represents the blessings and abundance that one seeks in life.
  • ived: This word means "to know" or "to understand." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and awareness in one's life.
  • aryamaṇaṁ: Refers to a divine being or a deity associated with the sun and light, symbolizing guidance and illumination.
  • nināya: This means "to lead" or "to guide." It indicates the act of directing or showing the way to others.
  • jane: Translated as "among people," it signifies the social aspect of human life and the importance of community.
  • mitro: This means "friend." It highlights the value of friendship and relationships in human interactions.
  • na dampatī: This phrase means "not a couple" or "not partners," suggesting the importance of individual identity and personal growth.
  • anakti: This term means "not to be neglected" or "not to be overlooked," emphasizing the importance of attention and care in relationships.
  • bṛhaspate: Refers to Brihaspati, the deity of wisdom and eloquence, symbolizing the pursuit of knowledge and understanding.
  • vājayāśūm̐r: This can be interpreted as "swift as a horse," representing speed, agility, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • ivājau: This means "like a horse," reinforcing the idea of strength and capability in achieving one's goals.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of community, knowledge, and personal growth. It highlights the significance of friendship and the need for guidance in life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to various aspects such as teamwork in professional settings, the importance of nurturing relationships, and the pursuit of wisdom in personal development. By fostering connections and seeking knowledge, individuals can navigate life's challenges more effectively and achieve prosperity and fulfillment.
