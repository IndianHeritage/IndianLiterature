मां प्र गाम पथो वयम् मां यज्ञाद इन्द्र सोमिनः | मांताḥ स्थुर नो अरातयः ||
mā pra gāma patho vayam mā yajñād indra sominaḥ | māntaḥ sthur no arātayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mā: not
    • pra: forward
    • gāma: to go
    • patho: on the path
    • vayam: we
    • mā: not
    • yajñād: from the sacrifice
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • sominaḥ: from the soma (sacred drink)
    • māntaḥ: not
    • sthur: strong
    • no: our
    • arātayaḥ: enemies
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a plea for protection and guidance. It expresses a desire to not stray from the right path.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies a call for divine assistance, particularly in overcoming obstacles and adversaries.
    • The reference to soma highlights the importance of spiritual nourishment and the connection to sacred rituals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied when facing challenges at work. For instance, a team may invoke this sentiment when embarking on a new project, seeking guidance to stay on the right path and avoid conflicts.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during times of uncertainty, such as during a major life decision, to seek clarity and protection from negative influences.
    • In community gatherings, this mantra can be used to foster unity and strength against common adversities, encouraging collective resilience.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance and protection. It emphasizes the importance of staying true to one's path and seeking strength against adversities.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to individuals and communities to remain focused on their goals, seek spiritual nourishment, and support each other in overcoming challenges.
    • Ultimately, this mantra encourages a proactive approach to life, fostering a sense of unity and resilience in the face of difficulties.
