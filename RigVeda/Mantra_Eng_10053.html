एवां च त्वं सरमा आजगन्थ प्रबाधिता सहसा दैवेन |स्वसारं त्वा कृत्नवै मा पुनर गा अप ते गवां सुभगे भजाम ||
evā ca tvaṁ sarama ājagantha prabādhitā sahasā daivyena | svasāraṁ tvā kṛṇavai mā punar gā apa te gavāṁ subhage bhajāma ||
  • evā ca tvaṁ - "And you" - This phrase indicates a direct address, suggesting a personal connection or invocation.
  • sarama - "the one who brings joy" - This term can be associated with happiness and fulfillment, akin to how a beloved pet or a close friend brings joy into our lives.
  • ājagantha - "who has come forth" - This implies arrival or manifestation, similar to how new opportunities or ideas emerge in our lives.
  • prabādhitā - "awakened" - This word signifies enlightenment or realization, much like the moment when one gains clarity on a significant life decision.
  • sahasā - "suddenly" - This suggests an unexpected occurrence, akin to a sudden insight or revelation that changes our perspective.
  • daivyena - "by divine grace" - This phrase indicates a higher power or spiritual influence, similar to how people often feel guided by fate or destiny in their lives.
  • svasāraṁ - "your own essence" - This refers to one's true self or nature, much like the journey of self-discovery that many individuals undertake.
  • tvā kṛṇavai - "I invoke you" - This is a call to action, similar to how individuals seek help or guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders.
  • mā punar - "do not return" - This phrase expresses a desire for permanence, akin to the wish for lasting change or improvement in one’s life.
  • gā apa te - "go away from you" - This suggests a desire to distance oneself from negativity or obstacles, similar to how people strive to eliminate toxic relationships or habits.
  • gavāṁ - "of the cows" - Cows are often symbols of abundance and nourishment in many cultures, representing prosperity and sustenance.
  • subhage - "O auspicious one" - This is a term of reverence, indicating respect and admiration, much like how we honor those who bring positivity into our lives.
  • bhajāma - "we worship" - This signifies devotion and gratitude, akin to how people express appreciation for the blessings they receive.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates themes of joy, awakening, divine guidance, and the essence of self. It emphasizes the importance of invoking positive energies and distancing oneself from negativity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as seeking clarity in difficult situations, fostering self-awareness, and cultivating gratitude for the blessings we have. The mantra serves as a reminder to embrace positivity and strive for personal growth, making it significant in both spiritual and everyday contexts.
