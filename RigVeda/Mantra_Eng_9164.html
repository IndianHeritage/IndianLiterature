यं देवासोऽवथ वाजसातौ यं त्रायध्वे यं पिपृथात्यंहः |यो वो गोपीथे न भयस्य वेद ते स्याम देववीतये तुरासः ||
yaṁ devāso 'vatha vājasātau yaṁ trāyadhve yam pipṛthāty aṁhaḥ | yo vo gopīthe na bhayasya veda te syāma devavītaye turāsaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yaṁ devāso 'vatha vājasātau yaṁ trāyadhve yam pipṛthāty aṁhaḥ | yo vo gopīthe na bhayasya veda te syāma devavītaye turāsaḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "Whom the gods have chosen as their protector, whom the earth has nourished, may we, who are free from fear, know that divine essence for our well-being."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • yaṁ: Whom
    • devāso: The gods
    • 'vatha: Have chosen
    • vājasātau: As a protector
    • yaṁ: Whom
    • trāyadhve: You protect
    • yam: Whom
    • pipṛthāty: The earth has nourished
    • aṁhaḥ: For our well-being
    • yo: Who
    • vo: For you
    • gopīthe: In the shelter
    • na: Not
    • bhayasya: Of fear
    • veda: Know
    • te: That
    • syāma: May we be
    • devavītaye: For divine essence
    • turāsaḥ: For our well-being
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine protection and nourishment. For instance, in times of crisis, people often turn to their faith or spiritual practices for comfort and guidance, seeking the protection of a higher power.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be reflected in how people come together to support one another during difficult times, embodying the idea of being nourished and protected by collective strength.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during meditation or prayer, invoking a sense of safety and well-being, especially when facing fears or uncertainties.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine protection and nourishment, reminding us of the interconnectedness of life and the support we can find in the divine and in each other.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals seek reassurance and strength, whether in personal challenges, community support, or spiritual practices.
    • Ultimately, this mantra encourages us to recognize and embrace the divine essence within ourselves and our surroundings, fostering a sense of peace and security in our lives.
