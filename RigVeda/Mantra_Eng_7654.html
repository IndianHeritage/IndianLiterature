वाचोविदं वाचमुदीरयन्तीं विश्वाभिरधीभिरुपतिष्ठमानाम् |देवीं देवभ्यः पर्येयूषीं गामामावृक्तमार्त्यो दभ्रचेताः ||
vacovidaṁ vācam udīrayantīṁ viśvābhir dhībhir upatiṣṭhamānām | devīṁ devebhyaḥ pary eyuṣīṁ gām ā māvṛkta martyo dabhracetāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vacovidaṁ: The knowledge of speech or the essence of words.
    • vācam: Speech or voice.
    • udīrayantīṁ: That which elevates or inspires.
    • viśvābhir: By all or universal.
    • dhībhir: By intellects or minds.
    • upatiṣṭhamānām: That which is established or grounded.
    • devīṁ: Goddess or divine power.
    • devebhyaḥ: To the gods or divine beings.
    • pary: Around or encompassing.
    • eyuṣīṁ: That which is to be attained or reached.
    • gām: The goal or destination.
    • ā: From or out of.
    • māvṛkta: Not covered or obscured.
    • martyo: Mortal or human.
    • dabhracetāḥ: With a focused or clear mind.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of speech and the importance of clear communication.
    • It emphasizes the need for a collective understanding and the elevation of thoughts through shared wisdom.
    • The invocation of the divine suggests that effective communication is not just a human endeavor but is also supported by higher powers.
    • The phrase "māvṛkta martyo dabhracetāḥ" indicates that one should strive to communicate with clarity and focus, free from distractions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, clear communication can lead to better collaboration and successful project outcomes.
    • In education, teachers who articulate their thoughts clearly can inspire and elevate their students' understanding.
    • In personal relationships, open and honest dialogue fosters trust and strengthens bonds.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of effective communication as a means to connect with others and the divine.
    • It serves as a reminder that clarity of thought and expression can lead to greater understanding and harmony in various aspects of life.
    • By practicing the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can enhance their interactions, whether in personal, professional, or spiritual contexts.
