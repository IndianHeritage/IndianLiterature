बृहत्सुम्नः प्रस्वीत निवेशनो जगतः स्थातुर उभयस्य यो वशी |स नो देवः सविता शर्म यच्छत्व अस्मे क्षयाय त्रिवरूथम् अंहसः ||
bṛhatsumnaḥ prasavītā niveśano jagataḥ sthātur ubhayasya yo vaśī | sa no devaḥ savitā śarma yacchatv asme kṣayāya trivarūtham aṁhasaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "bṛhatsumnaḥ prasavītā niveśano jagataḥ sthātur ubhayasya yo vaśī | sa no devaḥ savitā śarma yacchatv asme kṣayāya trivarūtham aṁhasaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bṛhatsumnaḥ: The great and expansive.
    • prasavītā: The one who brings forth or creates.
    • niveśano: The one who establishes or resides.
    • jagataḥ: Of the universe or the world.
    • sthātur: The one who remains or is stable.
    • ubhaya: Both or dual.
    • yo: Who.
    • vaśī: The one who has control or mastery.
    • sa: He or that (referring to the divine).
    • no: Our.
    • devaḥ: God or divine being.
    • savitā: The Sun God, the creator.
    • śarma: Protection, happiness, or peace.
    • yacchatv: May grant or provide.
    • asme: To us.
    • kṣayāya: For the purpose of overcoming or destruction.
    • trivarūtham: Threefold or triple form.
    • aṁhasaḥ: Of the light or radiance.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine essence of the universe, emphasizing the expansive and creative nature of the divine.
    • The phrase "bṛhatsumnaḥ prasavītā" highlights the idea that the divine is the source of all creation, akin to how a seed grows into a vast tree, symbolizing potential and growth.
    • "Niveśano jagataḥ sthātur" suggests that the divine is the stabilizing force in the universe, much like gravity keeps planets in orbit.
    • The mantra calls upon the divine to grant us protection and happiness ("śarma yacchatv asme"), similar to how a parent wishes for their child's well-being and safety.
    • The reference to "kṣayāya" indicates a desire to overcome obstacles or challenges, akin to how individuals strive to overcome personal struggles in life.
    • The "trivarūtham aṁhasaḥ" can be interpreted as the multifaceted nature of existence, reminding us that life has various dimensions, much like the different roles we play in society (e.g., as a parent, professional, and friend).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the divine presence in all aspects of life, encouraging us to seek stability, protection, and growth.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in moments of uncertainty or challenge, reminding us to seek inner strength and divine guidance.
    • By meditating on this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of peace and resilience, fostering a deeper connection with the universe and their own potential.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that we are part of a greater whole, and by aligning ourselves with the divine, we can navigate life's complexities with grace and purpose.
