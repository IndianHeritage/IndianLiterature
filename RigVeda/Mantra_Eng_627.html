त्वं भुवः प्रतिमानं पृथिव्या ऋष्ववीरस्य बृहतः पतिर भूः |विश्वम् आप्रā अन्तरिक्षं महित्वा सत्यं अद्धा नकिरे अन्यस त्वावान् ||
tvam bhuvaḥ pratimānam pṛthivyā ṛṣvavīrasya bṛhataḥ patir bhūḥ | viśvam āprā antarikṣam mahitvā satyam addhā nakir anyas tvāvān ||
  • tvam bhuvaḥ: This phrase translates to "You are the source of existence." It emphasizes the idea that the divine or the ultimate reality is the foundation of all that exists. In real life, this can be related to the concept of interconnectedness in nature, where every being and element is part of a larger whole.
  • pratimānam pṛthivyā: This means "the measure of the earth." It suggests that the divine is not only the creator but also the sustainer of the earth. For example, in environmental conservation, recognizing the earth as a sacred entity encourages us to protect and preserve our planet.
  • ṛṣvavīrasya: This translates to "of the strong and valiant." It signifies the strength and courage that comes from the divine. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who face challenges with bravery, drawing strength from their beliefs or values.
  • bṛhataḥ patir bhūḥ: This means "the great lord of the earth." It acknowledges the divine as the ruler and protector of the earth. In a societal context, this can relate to leadership that prioritizes the welfare of the community and the environment.
  • viśvam āprā: This translates to "the all-encompassing." It reflects the idea that the divine is present in all aspects of life. In practical terms, this can be observed in the diversity of cultures and beliefs that coexist, highlighting the importance of tolerance and understanding.
  • antarikṣam mahitvā: This means "the greatness of the space." It signifies the vastness and the infinite nature of the universe. In real life, this can inspire awe and curiosity about the cosmos, leading to scientific exploration and philosophical inquiry.
  • satyam addhā: This translates to "truth indeed." It emphasizes the importance of truth in our lives. In a practical sense, this can be seen in the value of honesty in personal relationships and the pursuit of knowledge in society.
  • nakir anyas tvāvān: This means "there is no other like you." It highlights the uniqueness of the divine. In real life, this can be related to the appreciation of individuality and the recognition of each person's unique contributions to society.

  • Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of the divine as the source of existence, strength, and truth. Its significance lies in its call for reverence towards nature, the pursuit of truth, and the recognition of the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, it encourages us to live with integrity, protect our environment, and appreciate the diversity of life around us. By embodying these principles, we can foster a more harmonious and sustainable world.
