अन्व अपां खाण्य अतृन्तम् ओजसा सूर्यं ऐरयतं दिवि प्रभुम् |इन्द्रावरुणा मदे अस्य मायिनो 'पिन्वतम् अपितः पिन्वतं धियः ||
anv apāṁ khāny atṛntam ojasā sūryam airayataṁ divi prabhum | indrāvaruṇā made asya māyino 'pinvatam apitaḥ pinvataṁ dhiyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • anv: towards
    • apāṁ: of the waters
    • khāny: the channels
    • atṛntam: unbounded
    • ojasā: with energy
    • sūryam: the sun
    • airayataṁ: shining
    • divi: in the sky
    • prabhum: the lord
    • indrāvaruṇā: Indra and Varuna
    • made: in the intoxication
    • asya: of this
    • māyino: of the illusion
    • 'pinvatam: of the enjoyers
    • apitaḥ: not
    • pinvataṁ: of the enjoyers
    • dhiyaḥ: of the intellect
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine energies and the interconnectedness of nature.
    • The phrase "anv apāṁ khāny atṛntam" suggests a journey towards the boundless waters, symbolizing the quest for knowledge and enlightenment.
    • "ojasā sūryam airayataṁ" refers to the sun's radiant energy, which can be likened to the vital force that sustains life and promotes growth.
    • The mention of "Indrāvaruṇā" signifies the duality of forces in nature, where Indra represents strength and Varuna represents cosmic order.
    • The line "made asya māyino" indicates the intoxication of life, suggesting that while we are caught in the illusions of the material world, we must seek deeper truths.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In our daily lives, we often seek knowledge and wisdom, akin to the journey towards the "unbounded waters." For instance, pursuing education or spiritual practices can be seen as this quest.
    • The sun's energy is essential for life; it can be compared to the motivation and passion we derive from our goals and aspirations.
    • Understanding the balance of forces in our lives, like work and leisure, mirrors the relationship between Indra and Varuna, reminding us to maintain harmony.
    • Recognizing the illusions in our lives, such as material possessions or societal expectations, can lead to a more profound understanding of our true selves.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge, understanding the forces of nature, and recognizing the illusions that cloud our perception.
    • Its significance lies in guiding individuals towards a path of enlightenment and balance in life.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a deeper connection with the universe.
