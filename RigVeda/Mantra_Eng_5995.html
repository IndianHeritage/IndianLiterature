मदेनिषितं मदं उग्रं उग्रेण शवसा |विश्वेषां तरुतारं मदाच्युतं मदे हि स्मा ददाति नः ||
madeneṣitam madam ugram ugreṇa śavasā | viśveṣāṁ tarutāram madacyutam made hi ṣmā dadāti naḥ ||
  • madeneṣitam: This term can be broken down into "madena" meaning "by intoxication" or "by joy" and "iṣitam" meaning "desired" or "sought after". It refers to the state of being inebriated or overwhelmed by joy.
  • madam: This word translates to "intoxication" or "ecstasy". It signifies a heightened state of consciousness or bliss.
  • ugram: This means "fierce" or "powerful". It can denote strength or intensity, often associated with divine forces.
  • ugreṇa: This is the instrumental form of "ugra\ meaning "by the fierce one". It suggests the use of powerful energy or force.
  • śavasā: This term can be interpreted as "with the body" or "in the form of the body". It indicates a physical manifestation of energy or spirit.
  • viśveṣāṁ: This means "of all beings" or "universal". It signifies inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all life.
  • tarutāram: This can be understood as "the one who uplifts" or "the one who supports". It refers to a force or entity that provides strength and elevation.
  • madacyutam: This term combines "madam" (intoxication) and "acyutam" (not falling or not losing). It implies a state of bliss that is stable and enduring.
  • made: This is a repetition of "madam\ emphasizing the importance of joy and ecstasy.
  • hi: This word means "indeed" or "certainly\ reinforcing the statement that follows.
  • ṣmā: This can be interpreted as "to us" or "for us\ indicating a personal connection or appeal.
  • dadāti: This means "gives" or "bestows". It signifies the act of providing or sharing something valuable.
  • naḥ: This translates to "us" or "our\ indicating a collective or communal aspect.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation for the divine to bestow upon us a state of joyful intoxication that is powerful and uplifting. It emphasizes the importance of joy and ecstasy in our lives, suggesting that these states are not only desirable but also essential for our well-being.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Engaging in activities that bring joy and fulfillment, such as pursuing hobbies, spending time with loved ones, or practicing mindfulness, can lead to a state of "madam" that uplifts our spirits.
  • Community Building: The mantra's emphasis on "viśveṣāṁ" highlights the importance of community and interconnectedness. Participating in community service or group activities can foster a sense of belonging and collective joy.
  • Spiritual Practices: Meditation, yoga, and other spiritual practices can help individuals connect with the "ugra" or powerful energies within themselves, leading to a deeper understanding of their own existence and purpose.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of joy and ecstasy in our lives. It encourages us to seek out experiences that uplift us and connect us with others, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
