यो विश्वा दयते वसु होता मन्द्रो जनानाम् |मधोर न पात्रा प्रथमाण्य अस्मै प्र स्तोमा यन्त्य अग्नये ||
yo viśvā dayate vasu hotā mandro janānām | madhor na pātrā prathamāny asmai pra stomā yanty agnaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • viśvā: all, universal
    • dayate: gives, bestows
    • vasu: wealth, prosperity
    • hotā: the one who invokes, the priest
    • mandro: sweet, pleasant
    • janānām: of the people, of mankind
    • madhor: of the sweet
    • na: not
    • pātrā: vessels, containers
    • prathamāny: first, foremost
    • asmai: to him, to that one
    • pra: forth, out
    • stomā: praises, hymns
    • yanty: go, proceed
    • agnaye: to Agni, the fire god
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the universal force that bestows wealth and prosperity upon all.
    • It highlights the role of the priest (hotā) who invokes this divine energy for the benefit of mankind.
    • The term "mandro" signifies the sweetness of life and the pleasantness that comes from divine blessings.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of offering praises (stomā) to Agni, the fire god, who is seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, a priest may recite this mantra to invoke blessings for prosperity and harmony among the people.
    • During festivals, offerings are made to Agni, symbolizing the collective gratitude and the desire for a prosperous future.
    • In personal life, individuals may reflect on this mantra when seeking guidance or blessings for their endeavors, recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine blessings for the welfare of all.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the sweetness of life and the prosperity that comes from collective efforts and divine grace.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages community bonding, gratitude, and the pursuit of a harmonious existence.
