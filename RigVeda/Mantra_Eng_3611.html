नū रोदसी अहिना बुद्ध्येन स्तुवीत देवी अप्येभिर इष्टैः |समुद्रं न संचराणे सनीषवो घर्मस्वरसो नद्योऽ अप व्रण ||
nū rodasī ahinā budhnyena stuvīta devī apyebhir iṣṭaiḥ | samudraṁ na saṁcaraṇe saniṣyavo gharmasvaraso nadyo3 apa vran ||
  • nū rodasī: This phrase can be translated as "O Earth and Sky." It invokes the duality of the universe, representing both the physical and the spiritual realms.
  • ahinā: This word means "with the serpent" or "with the energy of the serpent." In many cultures, serpents symbolize transformation and renewal, reflecting the cyclical nature of life.
  • budhnyena: This term refers to "with wisdom" or "with understanding." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and insight in our actions and decisions.
  • stuvīta devī: This translates to "the goddess who is praised." It signifies the reverence for divine feminine energy, which is often associated with nurturing, wisdom, and creativity.
  • apyebhir iṣṭaiḥ: This phrase means "with offerings." It highlights the practice of giving and gratitude, which is essential in many spiritual traditions.
  • samudraṁ: This word means "ocean." The ocean symbolizes vastness, depth, and the unknown, representing both challenges and opportunities in life.
  • na saṁcaraṇe: This translates to "not in the movement." It suggests a state of stillness or contemplation, encouraging individuals to pause and reflect.
  • saniṣyavo: This term can be interpreted as "with determination" or "with intent." It emphasizes the importance of purpose in our actions.
  • gharmasvaraso: This phrase means "of the warm waters." It can symbolize comfort, healing, and the nurturing aspects of life.
  • nadyo3: This refers to "rivers." Rivers are often seen as symbols of life, flow, and continuity, representing the journey of existence.
  • apa vran: This translates to "remove the wounds." It signifies healing and the removal of obstacles or suffering in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of balance between the earthly and the divine, the importance of wisdom, and the need for healing and nurturing in our lives. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as meditation practices, healing rituals, and moments of reflection. By invoking the energies represented in this mantra, individuals can seek to harmonize their inner and outer worlds, fostering a sense of peace and purpose. The significance lies in its call for gratitude, understanding, and the pursuit of wisdom, which are essential for personal growth and spiritual development.
