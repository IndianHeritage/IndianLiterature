तं ā नो अर्कम् अमृताय जुष्टम् इमे धासुर अमृतासः पुराजाः | शुचिक्रन्दं यजतम पस्त्यानाम बृहस्पतिम अनर्वाणं हुवेम ||
tam ā no arkam amṛtāya juṣṭam ime dhāsur amṛtāsaḥ purājāḥ | śucikrandaṁ yajatam pastyānām bṛhaspatim anarvāṇaṁ huvema ||
  • tam ā no arkam amṛtāya juṣṭam: This phrase can be translated as "May that light, which is imbued with immortality, shine upon us." Here, "tam" refers to "that," "ā no" means "may it come to us," "arkam" signifies "light" or "radiance," and "amṛtāya" means "immortal" or "eternal." This part emphasizes the desire for divine illumination and guidance in our lives.
  • ime dhāsur amṛtāsaḥ purājāḥ: This translates to "These beings, who are immortal, are the ancient ones." "Ime" means "these," "dhāsur" refers to "beings" or "entities," "amṛtāsaḥ" means "immortal," and "purājāḥ" signifies "ancient" or "of old." This line acknowledges the presence of eternal beings or forces that have existed since time immemorial, suggesting a connection to wisdom and knowledge that transcends generations.
  • śucikrandaṁ yajatam pastyānām: This can be interpreted as "We worship the pure and auspicious one." "Śucikrandaṁ" means "pure" or "auspicious," "yajatam" refers to "we worship," and "pastyānām" can be understood as "of the seers" or "of the wise." This line highlights the importance of reverence and devotion towards purity and wisdom, which are essential for spiritual growth.
  • bṛhaspatim anarvāṇaṁ huvema: This translates to "We invoke the great teacher, the one who is unbounded." "Bṛhaspatim" refers to "Brihaspati," the deity of wisdom and knowledge, "anarvāṇaṁ" means "unbounded" or "limitless," and "huvema" signifies "we invoke." This part emphasizes the act of calling upon a higher power for guidance and enlightenment, recognizing the vastness of knowledge that can be accessed through divine connection.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound yearning for divine guidance, wisdom, and purity. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or prayer to seek clarity and enlightenment in their lives.
  • Decision Making: When faced with important choices, invoking the essence of this mantra can help individuals align with their higher selves and make informed decisions.
  • Community and Leadership: Leaders can embody the principles of this mantra by promoting wisdom, purity, and guidance within their communities, fostering an environment of growth and understanding.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking light, wisdom, and purity in our lives, encouraging us to connect with the eternal and the divine for guidance and support.
