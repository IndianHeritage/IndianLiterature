अरं हि ष्म सुतेषु णः सोमेष्व इन्द्र भूषसि |अरं ते शक्र दावने ||
araṁ hi ṣma suteṣu ṇaḥ someṣv indra bhūṣasi | araṁ te śakra dāvane ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • araṁ: This can be interpreted as "to be strong" or "to be powerful."
    • hi: This is an emphatic particle, often translated as "indeed" or "certainly."
    • ṣma: This refers to "the best" or "the highest."
    • suteṣu: This means "in the offerings" or "in the sacrifices."
    • ṇaḥ: This translates to "for us" or "to us."
    • someṣv: This refers to "in the soma" or "in the divine nectar."
    • indra: This is the name of the king of gods, representing strength and power.
    • bhūṣasi: This means "you adorn" or "you are decorated."
    • araṁ: Repeated for emphasis, again meaning "to be strong."
    • te: This means "your."
    • śakra: Another name for Indra, meaning "the powerful one."
    • dāvane: This can be interpreted as "in the battle" or "in the struggle."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the strength and power of Indra, particularly in the context of offerings and sacrifices.
    • It suggests that through the act of sacrifice, one can invoke the strength of Indra, who is seen as a protector and a source of power.
    • The repetition of "araṁ" highlights the importance of strength in overcoming challenges, whether they be physical, emotional, or spiritual.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive environment, such as a workplace or sports, individuals often seek to invoke their inner strength to overcome challenges, much like how the mantra calls upon Indra's strength.
    • During rituals or ceremonies, people may offer prayers or sacrifices to seek blessings for strength and success, paralleling the offerings mentioned in the mantra.
    • In personal development, individuals may meditate on the qualities of strength and resilience, drawing inspiration from the divine attributes of Indra as described in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of strength and resilience in our lives.
    • It highlights the significance of seeking divine support through offerings and sacrifices, reinforcing the idea that we are not alone in our struggles.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal challenges or communal efforts, invoking strength—both from within and from higher powers—can lead to success and fulfillment.
