सीद होतः स्व उ लोके चिकीत्वान साधय यज्ञं सुखृतस्य योनौ |देववीर देवान हविषा यजास्य अग्ने बृहद यजमान वयो धाः ||
sīda hotaḥ sva u loke cikitvān sādayā yajñaṁ sukṛtasya yonau | devāvīr devān haviṣā yajāsy agne bṛhad yajamāne vayo dhāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sīda: Sit or be seated
    • hotaḥ: O priest or officiant
    • sva: Your own
    • loke: In the world
    • cikitvān: Being thoughtful or wise
    • sādayā: With devotion or sincerity
    • yajñaṁ: Sacrifice or offering
    • sukṛtasya: Of the good deeds
    • yonau: In the source or origin
    • devāvīr: O divine heroes
    • devān: The gods
    • haviṣā: With offerings
    • yajāsy: You shall worship
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • bṛhad: Great or vast
    • yajamāne: While being worshipped
    • vayo: Our age or lifespan
    • dhāḥ: May you bestow
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra addresses the priest (hotaḥ) and emphasizes the importance of being thoughtful and sincere while performing rituals (yajña) in the world (loke).
    • It highlights the significance of good deeds (sukṛtasya) as the foundation of these offerings, suggesting that the quality of our actions influences the effectiveness of our rituals.
    • The invocation of divine heroes (devāvīr) and the gods (devān) signifies the need for divine support and blessings in our endeavors.
    • Agni, the fire god, represents the transformative power of fire in rituals, symbolizing purity and the connection between the earthly and divine realms.
    • The mantra concludes with a plea for blessings on our lifespan (vayo), indicating a desire for longevity and prosperity through righteous actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community service or charitable acts, where individuals perform good deeds with sincerity and thoughtfulness.
    • In corporate settings, the principles of ethical business practices and corporate social responsibility resonate with the idea of performing actions that benefit society and the environment.
    • During personal milestones, such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies, individuals often perform rituals that invoke blessings from divine forces, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the importance of intention, sincerity, and the quality of actions in rituals and daily life.
    • It serves as a reminder that our deeds shape our experiences and that seeking divine blessings through righteous actions can lead to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
    • Ultimately, this mantra encourages individuals to engage in thoughtful and sincere actions, fostering a harmonious relationship with the divine and the world around them.
