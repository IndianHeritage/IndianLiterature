एकया प्रतिधापिबत साकं सरांसि त्रिंशतम् | इन्द्रः सोमस्य काणुकाः ||
ekayā pratidhāpibat sākaṁ sarāṁsi triṁśatam | indraḥ somasya kāṇukā ||
  • Mantra: "ekayā pratidhāpibat sākaṁ sarāṁsi triṁśatam | indraḥ somasya kāṇukā ||"
  • Translation: "With one (sacrifice), he (Indra) drinks the thirty (soma) juices together."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ekayā: one, singular
    • pratidhāpibat: to drink, to consume
    • sākaṁ: together, in unison
    • sarāṁsi: juices, often referring to the soma plant
    • triṁśatam: thirty, a significant number in Vedic texts
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods and god of rain and thunderstorms
    • somasya: of soma, a sacred plant and drink in Vedic rituals
    • kāṇukā: a reference to the essence or the vital part
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of the soma drink, which is considered sacred in Vedic rituals. Soma is often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • Indra, as the central figure, represents strength and power. The act of drinking the thirty juices symbolizes the consumption of various energies or aspects of life that contribute to vitality and strength.
    • The number thirty (triṁśatam) can be interpreted in various ways, such as the thirty days of a lunar month, symbolizing the cyclical nature of life and the importance of time in spiritual practices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of consuming various 'juices' can be likened to the idea of integrating different experiences and knowledge to achieve a holistic understanding of life.
    • Just as Indra consumes the soma, individuals today can seek to nourish their minds and bodies through diverse experiences, education, and relationships, fostering personal growth and resilience.
    • The mantra can also be seen as a reminder of the importance of unity and collaboration, as it emphasizes the act of coming together to achieve a common goal, much like teamwork in professional settings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity, strength, and the integration of various aspects of life for personal and spiritual growth.
    • It encourages individuals to seek nourishment from diverse sources, whether they be experiences, knowledge, or relationships, to enhance their vitality and resilience.
    • In a broader sense, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all things and the power of collective effort in achieving greater goals.
