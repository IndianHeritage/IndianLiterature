ये यज्ञेन दक्षिणया समक्तā इन्द्रस्य सख्यम अमृतत्वम् आनश || तेभ्यो भद्रम अङ्गिरसो वो अस्तु प्रति गृभ्णीत मानवं सुमेधसः ||
ye yajñena dakṣiṇayā samaktā indrasya sakhyam amṛtatvam ānaśa | tebhyo bhadram aṅgiraso vo astu prati gṛbhṇīta mānavaṁ sumedhasaḥ ||
  • Translation: "Those who, through their sacrifices, have united with Indra, the friend of the immortal, may they grant you well-being, O Angiras, and may they bestow upon the wise among men."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: Those who
    • yajñena: through sacrifice
    • dakṣiṇayā: with offerings
    • samaktā: united
    • indrasya: of Indra
    • sakhyam: friendship
    • amṛtatvam: immortality
    • ānaśa: to attain
    • tebhyo: to them
    • bhadram: well-being
    • aṅgiraso: O Angiras
    • vo: to you
    • astu: may it be
    • prati: towards
    • gṛbhṇīta: may it bestow
    • mānavaṁ: upon man
    • sumedhasaḥ: the wise
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of sacrifice (yajña) as a means to connect with divine forces, particularly Indra, who symbolizes strength and friendship.
    • In real life, this can be seen in community service or charitable acts, where individuals come together to support a cause, fostering unity and strength among participants.
    • The idea of "immortality" (amṛtatvam) can be interpreted as achieving a lasting impact through one's actions, much like how influential leaders or philanthropists are remembered for their contributions long after their time.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of individuals through shared goals and sacrifices, promoting a sense of community and collective well-being.
    • It serves as a reminder that wisdom (sumedhasaḥ) and thoughtful actions lead to positive outcomes for both individuals and society as a whole.
    • In modern contexts, this can be applied in various fields such as teamwork in corporate settings, collaborative projects in education, or community initiatives aimed at social betterment.
