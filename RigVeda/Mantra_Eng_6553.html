स नः स्तवाना आ भर रयिं चित्रश्रवस्तमम् |निरेके चिद यो हरिवो वसुर ददिः ||
sa naḥ stavāna ā bhara rayiṁ citraśravastamam | nireke cid yo harivo vasur dadiḥ ||
  • sa naḥ - "May he" or "Let him" - This phrase invokes a higher power or deity, suggesting a plea or request for assistance.
  • stavāna - "who is worthy of praise" - This refers to the divine being or force that is being addressed, emphasizing their greatness and the reverence they command.
  • ā bhara - "bring to us" - A call for the divine to bestow blessings or abundance upon the worshippers.
  • rayiṁ - "wealth" or "prosperity" - This signifies not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional richness.
  • citraśravastamam - "of varied and glorious fame" - This highlights the multifaceted nature of the divine, suggesting that the deity has many attributes and is renowned for their deeds.
  • nireke - "without exception" - This implies inclusivity, indicating that the blessings sought are for all, without discrimination.
  • cid - "who" - A pronoun that refers back to the divine being, maintaining the focus on the deity being addressed.
  • yo - "who" - Another reference to the divine, reinforcing the connection between the worshippers and the higher power.
  • harivo - "the one who removes obstacles" - This suggests that the deity has the power to clear the path for the devotees, aiding them in their endeavors.
  • vasur - "the one who is rich" - This term emphasizes the abundance and generosity of the divine being.
  • dadiḥ - "may he grant" - A concluding plea for the divine to bestow the requested blessings upon the worshippers.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda is a heartfelt invocation for divine blessings, prosperity, and the removal of obstacles. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and praising the divine forces in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as during rituals for new beginnings, business ventures, or personal challenges. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset of gratitude and openness to receiving blessings, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose. The significance lies in its universal appeal for support and abundance, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine.
