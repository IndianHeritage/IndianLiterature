प्र याभिर्यासी दाश्वांसमच्छा नियुद्भिर्वायविष्टये दुरोणे |नि नो रयिं सुभोजसं युवस्व न वीरं गव्यं अश्व्यं च राधः ||
pra yābhir yāsi dāśvāṁsam acchā niyudbhir vāyav iṣṭaye duroṇe | ni no rayiṁ subhojasaṁ yuvasva ni vīraṁ gavyam aśvyaṁ ca rādhaḥ ||
  • pra yābhir yāsi dāśvāṁsam: This phrase can be interpreted as "with the horses that you ride." Here, "dāśvāṁsam" refers to horses, which are often symbolic of strength, speed, and power in Vedic literature. In real life, this can relate to the idea of harnessing one's resources or capabilities to achieve goals.
  • acchā niyudbhir vāyav iṣṭaye: This translates to "with the excellent steeds, for the purpose of the wind." The term "vāyav" refers to the wind, which symbolizes movement and change. This can be likened to how we adapt to changing circumstances in life, using our strengths to navigate challenges.
  • duroṇe: This word means "in the battle" or "in the conflict." It signifies the struggles we face in life, whether they are personal, professional, or societal. For example, in a competitive work environment, one must often engage in a metaphorical battle to succeed.
  • ni no rayiṁ subhojasaṁ: This phrase means "may we attain wealth that is auspicious." Here, "rayiṁ" refers to wealth or prosperity, and "subhojasaṁ" implies that it should be beneficial and good. In practical terms, this can relate to the pursuit of not just material wealth, but also spiritual and emotional richness in life.
  • yuvasva ni vīraṁ: This translates to "be youthful and strong." The emphasis on youthfulness can be seen as a call to maintain vigor and enthusiasm in life. For instance, staying physically active and mentally engaged can lead to a more fulfilling life.
  • gavyam aśvyaṁ ca rādhaḥ: This means "the wealth of cattle and horses." In ancient times, cattle and horses were symbols of wealth and status. Today, this can be interpreted as the importance of nurturing our resources, whether they are financial, social, or intellectual, to ensure growth and stability.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of harnessing one's strengths and resources to navigate life's challenges. It encourages the pursuit of auspicious wealth and the maintenance of vigor and enthusiasm. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal development, career growth, and the cultivation of meaningful relationships. By embodying the principles of strength, adaptability, and prosperity, individuals can lead a more fulfilling and successful life.
