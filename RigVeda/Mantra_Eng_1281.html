परावतं नासत्यानुदेथाम् उच्छाबुध्नं चक्रथुर जिह्मबाम् |क्षरन्न आपो न पायनाय राये सहस्राय तृष्यते गोतमस्य ||
parāvataṁ nāsatyānudethām uccābudhnaṁ cakrathur jihmabāram | kṣarann āpo na pāyanāya rāye sahasrāya tṛṣyate gotamasya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • parāvataṁ: The one who is beyond, transcendent.
    • nāsatyā: The Asvins, divine twin deities associated with health and medicine.
    • anudethām: May they uplift or elevate.
    • uccābudhnaṁ: The one who is high or elevated.
    • cakrathur: The one who has a wheel or cycle, symbolizing the cycle of life.
    • jihmabāram: The one who is swift or quick.
    • kṣarann: Flowing or moving.
    • āpo: Waters, often symbolizing life and nourishment.
    • na: Not.
    • pāyanāya: For the purpose of protection or support.
    • rāye: Wealth or prosperity.
    • sahasrāya: Thousandfold or abundant.
    • tṛṣyate: Is thirsty or desires.
    • gotamasya: Of Gotama, referring to the sage or seeker.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Asvins, the divine twins, asking them to elevate and uplift the seeker.
    • The mention of "uccābudhnaṁ" signifies a call for higher consciousness and awareness.
    • The "cakrathur" symbolizes the cyclical nature of existence, reminding us of the interconnectedness of life.
    • The reference to "āpo" emphasizes the importance of water as a life-giving force, essential for sustenance.
    • The phrase "sahasrāya tṛṣyate" indicates a yearning for abundance and prosperity, reflecting a universal human desire.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal or communal crisis, individuals often seek higher guidance or support, akin to invoking the Asvins for upliftment.
    • The cyclical nature of life can be observed in nature, such as the changing seasons, which remind us of the importance of balance and renewal.
    • Water conservation efforts in communities highlight the significance of "āpo," as access to clean water is vital for health and prosperity.
    • In business, the desire for "sahasrāya" can be seen in entrepreneurs striving for growth and abundance, reflecting the universal quest for success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the divine support available to us in our pursuits, encouraging us to seek higher consciousness and abundance.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of interconnectedness, the importance of nourishment (both physical and spiritual), and the aspiration for prosperity.
    • In real life, it can be applied in various contexts, from personal growth to community welfare, emphasizing the need for support, awareness, and abundance in our lives.
