तारत समुद्रम पवमान उर्मिणा राजा देव ऋतम बृहत् |अर्षन् मित्रस्य वरुणस्य धर्मणा प्र हिन्वान ऋतम बृहत् ||
tarat samudram pavamāna ūrmiṇā rājā deva ṛtam bṛhat | arṣan mitrasya varuṇasya dharmaṇā pra hinvāna ṛtam bṛhat ||
  • tārat samudram: This phrase can be translated as "the one who crosses the ocean." In a metaphorical sense, it represents overcoming challenges and obstacles in life. For example, a student who studies hard to pass a difficult exam is crossing the ocean of academic challenges.
  • pavamāna: This term means "the purifying one" or "the one who brings purity." It signifies the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions. In real life, this can be seen in practices like meditation or yoga, where individuals seek to purify their minds and bodies.
  • ūrmiṇā: This word translates to "with waves" or "with energy." It symbolizes the dynamic and ever-changing nature of life. For instance, the waves of the ocean can represent the ups and downs we face, reminding us to adapt and flow with life's changes.
  • rājā deva: This means "king of the gods." It signifies the supreme authority and power of divine forces in the universe. In a practical sense, this can be related to leadership qualities in individuals who inspire and guide others, much like a king would.
  • ṛtam bṛhat: This phrase translates to "the great truth" or "the cosmic order." It emphasizes the importance of living in harmony with universal laws and truths. In everyday life, this can be seen in ethical practices, where individuals strive to act justly and truthfully.
  • arṣan mitrasya varuṇasya: This refers to "the seer of Mitra and Varuna." Mitra and Varuna are deities representing friendship and cosmic order, respectively. This highlights the significance of relationships and the balance of social order in our lives. For example, maintaining friendships and community ties is essential for a harmonious society.
  • dharmaṇā: This term means "by righteousness" or "through duty." It underscores the importance of following one's duty and moral responsibilities. In real life, this can be seen in professionals who adhere to ethical standards in their work, ensuring they contribute positively to society.
  • pra hinvāna: This translates to "removing obstacles." It signifies the act of overcoming barriers that hinder progress. For instance, a person facing financial difficulties may seek education or training to improve their situation, thus removing the obstacles in their path.
  • ṛtam bṛhat: Reiterating the concept of "the great truth," this phrase emphasizes the continuous pursuit of understanding and aligning with universal truths throughout life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound wisdom about overcoming challenges, the importance of purity, the dynamic nature of life, and the significance of righteousness and relationships. In real life, it serves as a guiding principle for individuals to navigate their journeys with integrity, adapt to changes, and strive for harmony within themselves and their communities. By embodying these teachings, one can lead a fulfilling and meaningful life, contributing positively to the world around them.
