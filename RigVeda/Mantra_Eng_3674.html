अव स्पृधि पितरं योद्धि विद्वान् पुत्रो यस् ते सहसः सूना ऊहे |कदाचित् त्वो अभि चक्षसे नोऽग्ने कदाम् ऋतचिद् यातयासे ||
ava spṛdhi pitaraṁ yodhi vidvān putro yas te sahasaḥ sūna ūhe | kadā cikitvo abhi cakṣase no 'gne kadām̐ ṛtacid yātayāse ||
  • ava spṛdhi pitaraṁ: This phrase can be translated as "O Father, inspire us." Here, "ava" means "downward" or "to us," and "spṛdhi" means "to inspire" or "to awaken." The term "pitaraṁ" refers to "father" or "forefather," indicating a plea for guidance from ancestral wisdom.
  • yodhi vidvān: This translates to "the knowledgeable warrior." "Yodhi" means "warrior" or "fighter," and "vidvān" means "wise" or "knowledgeable." This suggests the importance of combining strength with wisdom in facing challenges.
  • putro yas te sahasaḥ sūna ūhe: This can be interpreted as "the son who is strong and courageous." "Putro" means "son," "yas" means "who," "te" means "your," "sahasaḥ" means "brave" or "courageous," and "sūna" refers to "strength." This line emphasizes the qualities of bravery and strength that are desirable in a successor or offspring.
  • kadā cikitvo abhi cakṣase no 'gne: This translates to "when will you, O Fire, grant us insight?" "Kadā" means "when," "cikitvo" means "to think" or "to reflect," "abhi" means "towards," "cakṣase" means "to see," and "agne" refers to "O Fire" or "O Agni," the deity representing fire and knowledge. This line expresses a yearning for enlightenment and clarity.
  • kadām̐ ṛtacid yātayāse: This can be interpreted as "when will the truth guide us?" "Kadām̐" means "when," "ṛtacid" refers to "truth" or "righteousness," and "yātayāse" means "to guide" or "to lead." This line signifies the desire for truth to illuminate the path forward.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking guidance, wisdom, and strength from both ancestral figures and divine forces. It highlights the importance of combining knowledge with courage in the face of life's challenges.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:
    • Leadership: Leaders can draw inspiration from their predecessors while also embodying the qualities of wisdom and strength to guide their teams effectively.
    • Personal Growth: Individuals can reflect on their own strengths and seek knowledge to overcome personal challenges, much like a warrior preparing for battle.
    • Education: Students can strive to be both knowledgeable and courageous in their pursuits, seeking guidance from mentors and ancestors.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of wisdom, strength, and truth. It encourages individuals to seek guidance from their roots while also striving for personal growth and enlightenment. In a world filled with challenges, this mantra inspires us to be courageous and wise, ensuring that we navigate our paths with clarity and purpose.
