अभीं ऋतस्य विष्टपं दूहते पृथ्निमातरः |चारु प्रियतमं हविः ||
abhīm ṛtasya viṣṭapaṁ duhate pṛśnimātaraḥ | cāru priyatamaṁ haviḥ ||
  • abhīm: This word can be interpreted as "fearless" or "brave." It signifies a state of courage and confidence.
  • ṛtasya: This term refers to "truth" or "cosmic order." In the context of the Rigveda, it represents the fundamental principles that govern the universe.
  • viṣṭapaṁ: This word means "the one who is established" or "the one who is firm." It indicates stability and strength.
  • duhate: This can be translated as "nourishing" or "supporting." It implies the act of providing sustenance or care.
  • pṛśnimātaraḥ: This term refers to "the one who is born from the cosmic mother" or "the one who is connected to the divine." It emphasizes a connection to a higher power or source.
  • cāru: This word means "beautiful" or "charming." It denotes something that is pleasing to the senses.
  • priyatamaṁ: This translates to "most beloved" or "dear." It signifies something that is cherished and valued.
  • haviḥ: This term refers to "offering" or "sacrifice." In Vedic rituals, it represents the act of giving to the divine.

The mantra can be understood as a call for the fearless and stable essence of truth to nourish and support the divine connection, leading to beautiful and cherished offerings. It emphasizes the importance of courage and truth in our lives, suggesting that when we align ourselves with these principles, we can create meaningful contributions to the world around us.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Embracing fearlessness and truth can help individuals overcome challenges and pursue their goals with confidence.
  • Community Support: By nurturing and supporting one another, communities can thrive, creating a stable and harmonious environment.
  • Spiritual Practices: The mantra encourages individuals to connect with their higher selves and the divine, fostering a sense of belonging and purpose.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of courage, truth, and connection to the divine in our lives. By embodying these qualities, we can create a positive impact on ourselves and those around us, leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
