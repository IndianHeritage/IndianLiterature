त्वया हितम् अप्यम् अप्सु भागं धन्वान्वा मृगयसो विथस्थुः |वनानि विभ्यः नकिरेस्य तानि व्रता देवस्य सवितुर्मिनन्ति ||
tvayā hitam apyam apsu bhāgaṁ dhanvānv ā mṛgayaso vi tasthuḥ | vanāni vibhyo nakir asya tāni vratā devasya savitur minanti ||
  • tvayā hitam: This phrase translates to "by you, for your benefit." It emphasizes the idea of actions or offerings made for the welfare of others, highlighting the importance of selflessness and community support.
  • apyam apsu: This means "the water that is nourishing." Water is a vital resource for life, symbolizing purity, sustenance, and the essence of existence. In real life, this can be related to the importance of clean water for health and agriculture.
  • bhāgaṁ dhanvānv: This translates to "the share of wealth." It signifies the distribution of resources and prosperity among individuals and communities. In modern contexts, this can be seen in practices like fair trade and equitable resource distribution.
  • ā mṛgayaso vi tasthuḥ: This phrase means "may they dwell in the hunt." It suggests the pursuit of goals and aspirations, akin to hunting for success. In real life, this can be related to striving for one's dreams and ambitions, whether in career or personal life.
  • vanāni vibhyo: This translates to "from the forests." Forests are often seen as symbols of abundance and biodiversity. They provide resources and shelter, reminding us of the importance of environmental conservation and sustainable living.
  • nakir asya tāni: This means "those who are not here." It can refer to the unseen forces or energies that influence our lives. In a real-life context, this can relate to the impact of ancestors, traditions, or even the unseen challenges we face.
  • vratā devasya: This translates to "the vows of the deity." It signifies commitment and dedication to a higher purpose or divine will. In everyday life, this can be seen in the dedication individuals show towards their beliefs, values, or causes they support.
  • savituh minanti: This means "the rays of Savitar." Savitar is a solar deity representing light, energy, and life. The rays symbolize enlightenment and knowledge. In practical terms, this can be related to the pursuit of education and wisdom, illuminating our paths in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of selflessness, the importance of resources, the pursuit of goals, and the reverence for nature and divine forces. Its significance lies in promoting a harmonious relationship with the environment, encouraging equitable sharing of resources, and inspiring individuals to strive for their aspirations while remaining committed to higher ideals. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide personal actions, community initiatives, and environmental stewardship, fostering a balanced and fulfilling existence.
