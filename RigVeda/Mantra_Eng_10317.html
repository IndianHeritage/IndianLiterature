ऊर्जो नपाज जातवेदः सुशस्तिभिर मन्दस्व धीतिभिर हितः |त्वे इषः सं दधुर भूरिवर्पसश्चित्रोतयो वामजाताः ||
ūrjo napāj jātavedaḥ suśastibhir mandasva dhītibhir hitaḥ | tve iṣaḥ saṁ dadhur bhūrivarpasaś citrotayo vāmajātāḥ ||
  • ūrjo: This word translates to "energy" or "vitality." It signifies the life force that sustains all living beings.
  • napāj: This term can be interpreted as "nourishment" or "sustenance," indicating the importance of resources that support life.
  • jātavedaḥ: Referring to the "one who knows the origin," this word is often associated with Agni, the fire god, symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
  • suśastibhir: This means "with good qualities" or "well-formed," emphasizing the importance of virtue and positive attributes in life.
  • mandasva: This translates to "be gentle" or "be calm," suggesting the need for tranquility and peace in our actions and thoughts.
  • dhītibhir: This word means "with wisdom" or "intellect," highlighting the significance of knowledge and understanding in decision-making.
  • hitaḥ: This means "beneficial" or "good," indicating actions that lead to positive outcomes for oneself and others.
  • tve: This translates to "you," addressing the divine or higher power, which can be interpreted as a call for guidance.
  • iṣaḥ: This means "power" or "authority," referring to the strength that comes from divine support.
  • saṁ dadhur: This phrase means "they hold together," suggesting unity and cooperation among individuals or forces.
  • bhūrivarpasaś: This translates to "of great wealth" or "abundance," indicating the richness of life and resources available to us.
  • citrotayo: This means "of various kinds," emphasizing diversity and the multitude of experiences in life.
  • vāmajātāḥ: This can be interpreted as "those who are born from the left," which may symbolize unconventional or alternative paths in life.

In conclusion, this mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of energy, nourishment, wisdom, and virtue in our lives. It calls for a harmonious existence where individuals work together, guided by divine power, to achieve abundance and well-being. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as community building, environmental sustainability, and personal development. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can foster a more peaceful and prosperous society, promoting cooperation and understanding among diverse groups.
