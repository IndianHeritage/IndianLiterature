ऋतं वदन्न ऋताद्युम्न सत्यं वदन सत्यकर्मन् |श्रद्धां वदन सोम राजा धात्रा सोम परिष्कृत इन्द्रायेंदो परि श्रव ||
ṛtaṁ vadann ṛtadyumna satyaṁ vadan satyakarman | śraddhāṁ vadan soma rājan dhātrā soma pariṣkṛta indrāyendo pari srava ||
  • ṛtaṁ vadann: This phrase translates to "speaking the truth" or "proclaiming the cosmic order." In real life, this can be seen in individuals who uphold honesty and integrity in their actions and words, such as a whistleblower who exposes corruption for the greater good.
  • ṛtadyumna: This term refers to "the one who is victorious through truth." It signifies the power of truth in overcoming challenges. For example, a student who diligently studies and remains truthful in their efforts is likely to succeed in their examinations.
  • satyaṁ vadan: This means "speaking the truth." In everyday life, this can be exemplified by leaders who communicate transparently with their teams, fostering trust and collaboration.
  • satyakarman: This translates to "performing righteous actions." It emphasizes the importance of aligning one's actions with truth and morality. A practical example is a business that prioritizes ethical practices over profit, contributing positively to society.
  • śraddhāṁ vadan: This means "speaking with faith." It highlights the significance of belief and conviction in one's words. For instance, a motivational speaker who inspires others through their passionate and faith-filled messages can create a profound impact.
  • soma rājan: This refers to "the king of soma," symbolizing nourishment and vitality. In real life, this can be related to leaders who nurture their communities, ensuring the well-being and growth of their people, much like a good parent or mentor.
  • dhātrā soma: This translates to "the nourisher of soma," indicating a source of sustenance and support. An example can be seen in healthcare professionals who provide care and healing to those in need, embodying the role of a nurturer.
  • pariṣkṛta: This means "purified" or "refined." It signifies the importance of clarity and purity in intentions and actions. For example, an artist who refines their craft through practice and dedication exemplifies this concept.
  • indrāyendo: This term refers to "to Indra, the king of gods." Indra represents strength and power. In a modern context, this can be likened to individuals who lead with courage and strength in challenging situations, such as activists fighting for social justice.
  • pari srava: This means "to flow abundantly." It symbolizes the idea of abundance and prosperity that comes from righteous actions and truth. For instance, a community that works together harmoniously often experiences growth and prosperity.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of truth, righteousness, and faith in our actions and words. It teaches us that by embodying these principles, we can achieve success and contribute positively to society. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, professional environments, or community engagements, the values expressed in this mantra guide us towards a more harmonious and prosperous existence. By speaking the truth and acting with integrity, we not only uplift ourselves but also inspire those around us to do the same.
