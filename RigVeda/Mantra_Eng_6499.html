मां ते अमाजुरो यथा मूरेसा इन्द्र सख्ये त्वावतः |निषदाम सचा सुते ||
mā te amājuro yathā mūrāsa indra sakhye tvāvataḥ | ni ṣadāma sacā sute ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mā te: "May you not" - a plea or wish for something not to happen.
    • amājuro: "become old" - refers to aging or the loss of vitality.
    • yathā: "as" - a comparative term indicating similarity.
    • mūrāsa: "like a mortal" - refers to human beings who are subject to aging and death.
    • indra: "O Indra" - Indra is the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and power.
    • sakhye: "in friendship" - denotes a bond of camaraderie and support.
    • tvāvataḥ: "may you protect" - a request for safeguarding or protection.
    • ni ṣadāma: "we may sit" - implies a desire for stability and comfort.
    • sacā: "with truth" - emphasizes the importance of honesty and integrity.
    • sute: "in the offspring" - refers to progeny or the results of one's actions.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a wish for eternal youth and vitality, contrasting the inevitable aging process that mortals face.
    • It invokes Indra, the god of strength, to provide protection and support in the journey of life, especially in the context of friendships and relationships.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of truth and integrity in our actions, particularly in relation to our offspring and the legacy we leave behind.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, individuals often wish to maintain their health and vitality as they age. This mantra can serve as a reminder to focus on wellness and seek support from friends and family.
    • In professional settings, fostering strong relationships based on trust and integrity can lead to a more harmonious work environment, much like the friendship invoked in the mantra.
    • Parents may reflect on the significance of their actions and values, understanding that they shape the future of their children, akin to the reference to "sute" or offspring.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the universal desire for protection, vitality, and the importance of truth in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to cherish relationships, uphold integrity, and strive for a legacy that positively impacts future generations.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek support from their community while also being mindful of the values they impart to their children and society.
