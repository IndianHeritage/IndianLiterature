तवे अग्नि स्वाहुत प्रियासः सन्तु सूरयः |यन्तारो ये मगवाणो जनानाम ऊर्वान दयन्त गोनाम ||
tve agne svāhuta priyāsaḥ santu sūrayaḥ | yantāro ye maghavāno janānām ūrvān dayanta gonām ||
  • tve agne svāhuta priyāsaḥ santu sūrayaḥ - This line can be translated as "O Agni, may those who are dear to you be blessed with prosperity." Here, Agni represents the divine fire and is often associated with energy, transformation, and the sacred. In real life, this can be seen as a call for blessings upon those who are cherished, emphasizing the importance of community and relationships.
  • yantāro ye maghavāno janānām ūrvān dayanta gonām - This translates to "Those who are powerful and generous, may they bestow their blessings upon the people." This line highlights the role of leaders and influential figures in society who have the capacity to uplift others. In a practical sense, this can relate to leaders in various fields—be it politics, business, or community service—who use their power to benefit the greater good.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine blessings, community, and the role of powerful individuals in uplifting society. It serves as a reminder that prosperity and well-being are interconnected with our relationships and the actions of those in positions of influence.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can inspire individuals to foster positive relationships and support one another. It encourages leaders to act with generosity and responsibility, ensuring that their actions contribute to the welfare of the community. For instance, a business leader who prioritizes ethical practices and community engagement embodies the essence of this mantra.
