क्राणā शिशुर महीनां हिन्वन्न ऋतस्य दीधितिम् |विश्वा परि प्रियā भूवद अध द्वितā ||
krāṇā śiśur mahīnāṁ hinvann ṛtasya dīdhitim | viśvā pari priyā bhuvad adha dvitā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • krāṇā: To create or to bring forth.
    • śiśur: Child or offspring.
    • mahīnāṁ: Of the great or the earth.
    • hinvann: Invoking or calling forth.
    • ṛtasya: Of truth or cosmic order.
    • dīdhitim: Radiance or brilliance.
    • viśvā: All or universal.
    • pari: Around or in all directions.
    • priyā: Beloved or dear.
    • bhuvad: Becoming or manifesting.
    • adha: Below or beneath.
    • dvitā: Duality or twofold.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the creation and nurturing of life, emphasizing the importance of truth and cosmic order.
    • The invocation of the child (śiśur) signifies new beginnings, growth, and the potential that lies within every individual.
    • The reference to the earth (mahīnāṁ) highlights the nurturing aspect of nature, which supports life and growth.
    • By calling forth the radiance of truth (ṛtasya dīdhitim), the mantra suggests that living in accordance with truth brings clarity and enlightenment.
    • The phrase "viśvā pari priyā bhuvad" indicates that the universe is interconnected, and everything that manifests is cherished and significant.
    • The mention of duality (dvitā) reminds us of the balance between opposites, such as light and dark, joy and sorrow, which are essential for a complete understanding of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In parenting, the mantra can be seen as a reminder to nurture children with love and truth, fostering their growth in a supportive environment.
    • In environmental conservation, it emphasizes the importance of respecting and nurturing the earth, recognizing its role in sustaining life.
    • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek truth and clarity in their lives, leading to enlightenment and fulfillment.
    • In relationships, it highlights the significance of cherishing connections and understanding the balance between different perspectives.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, nurturing, and the pursuit of truth.
    • Its significance lies in its universal applicability, reminding us of our responsibilities towards ourselves, others, and the environment.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a harmonious and balanced existence, fostering growth and enlightenment in their lives and communities.
