मन्त्र: एता उ त्ये पतयन्ति श्वयातव इन्द्रं दिप्सन्ति दिप्सवो 'दाभ्यम् | शिशीते शक्रः पीशुनिभ्यः वधं नूनं सृजद अशनिं यातुमद्भ्यः ||
eta u tye patayanti śvayātava indraṁ dipsanti dipsavo 'dābhyam | śiśīte śakraḥ piśunebhyo vadhaṁ nūnaṁ sṛjad aśaniṁ yātumadbhyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eta: this
    • u: indeed
    • tye: you (plural)
    • patayanti: they send forth
    • śvayātava: of the swift-moving
    • indraṁ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • dipsanti: they desire
    • dipsavo: those who desire
    • 'dābhyam: for the two
    • śiśīte: he protects
    • śakraḥ: powerful one (another name for Indra)
    • piśunebhyo: from the envious
    • vadhaṁ: destruction
    • nūnaṁ: certainly
    • sṛjad: he creates
    • aśaniṁ: lightning
    • yātumadbhyaḥ: for those who are worthy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine power of Indra, who is often associated with strength, rain, and thunder.
    • It highlights the idea that those who are swift and powerful (like Indra) can protect and send forth their energy to fulfill desires.
    • The mantra suggests that Indra protects the worthy from the envious and creates powerful forces (like lightning) to aid them.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader (akin to Indra) can inspire and protect their team from negative influences, fostering a positive work environment.
    • In personal life, individuals who strive for their goals can find strength in their community, which acts as a protective force against negativity.
    • In nature, the power of storms can be seen as a metaphor for overcoming challenges, where the lightning represents sudden insights or breakthroughs.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine protection and the power of intention in achieving one's desires.
    • It teaches us that with strength and support, we can overcome obstacles and protect ourselves from negativity.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek strength in community and to be mindful of the energies they project and receive.
