इषम् ऊर्जम् अभ्य अर्षाश्वं गाम उरु ज्योतिः कृणुहि मत्सि देवान् |विश्वानि हि सुशहा तानि तुभ्यम पवमान बाधसे सोम शत्रून् ||
iṣam ūrjam abhy a1rṣāśvaṁ gām uru jyotiḥ kṛṇuhi matsi devān | viśvāni hi suṣahā tāni tubhyam pavamāna bādhase soma śatrūn ||
  • iṣam: This word can be translated as "the power" or "the strength." It signifies the force or energy that drives actions and intentions.
  • ūrjam: Meaning "vigor" or "energy," it emphasizes the importance of vitality in achieving one's goals.
  • abhy a1rṣāśvaṁ: This term refers to "the swift horse," symbolizing speed and agility. In real life, this can be likened to the need for quick decision-making and adaptability in various situations.
  • gām: Translated as "the world" or "the community," it highlights the interconnectedness of individuals within society.
  • uru: Meaning "vast" or "broad," it suggests the expansiveness of opportunities and experiences available to us.
  • jyotiḥ: This word means "light" or "illumination," representing knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment.
  • kṛṇuhi: Translated as "make" or "create," it calls for action and the manifestation of intentions into reality.
  • matsi: This term refers to "the divine" or "the gods," indicating a connection to higher powers or spiritual guidance.
  • devān: Meaning "the deities," it emphasizes the role of divine forces in our lives and the importance of seeking their blessings.
  • viśvāni: This word means "all" or "universal," suggesting inclusivity and the idea that all aspects of life are interconnected.
  • hi: A particle that can be translated as "indeed," reinforcing the truth of the preceding statement.
  • suṣahā: Meaning "well-being" or "prosperity," it signifies the importance of health and success in life.
  • tāni: Translated as "those," referring to the blessings or qualities being sought.
  • tubhyam: Meaning "to you," indicating a direct appeal to the divine or higher powers.
  • pavamāna: This term refers to "the purifying one," symbolizing the cleansing of negativity and obstacles.
  • bādhase: Meaning "to remove" or "to eliminate," it emphasizes the desire to overcome challenges.
  • soma: Referring to "the divine nectar" or "soma," it symbolizes nourishment and spiritual sustenance.
  • śatrūn: Meaning "enemies" or "obstacles," it highlights the challenges we face in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, energy, and divine guidance in overcoming life's challenges. It emphasizes the importance of community, knowledge, and the pursuit of well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied when facing difficulties, whether in personal relationships, professional endeavors, or spiritual journeys. By invoking the qualities of vigor, light, and divine support, individuals can navigate obstacles and strive for a prosperous and fulfilling life.
