अग्नīषोमाव अनेन वां यो वां घृतेन दाशति |तस्मै दीद्यातम बृहत् ||
agnīṣomāv anena vāṁ yo vāṁ ghṛtena dāśati | tasmai dīdayatam bṛhat ||
  • Agnīṣomāv: This term refers to the duality of Agni (fire) and Soma (the moon). Agni symbolizes energy, transformation, and the physical realm, while Soma represents nourishment, tranquility, and the spiritual aspect of life.
  • Anena: This word means "by this" or "through this," indicating a means of connection or offering.
  • Vāṁ: This is a pronoun meaning "you," addressing the divine or the recipient of the mantra.
  • Yo: This means "who" or "that," referring to the one who is being invoked or addressed.
  • Vāṁ ghṛtena dāśati: This phrase translates to "who offers you ghee." Ghee (clarified butter) is a sacred substance in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and nourishment.
  • Tasmai: This means "to him" or "to that one," indicating the recipient of the offerings or prayers.
  • Dīdayatam: This term means "may he shine" or "may he illuminate," suggesting a desire for enlightenment and clarity.
  • Bṛhat: This translates to "great" or "vast," indicating the expansive nature of the divine or the universe.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to the divine forces of Agni and Soma, asking for their blessings and nourishment. It emphasizes the importance of offering ghee, which is a symbol of purity and sustenance, to the divine. The invocation seeks to connect the physical and spiritual realms, highlighting the duality of existence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can use this mantra during meditation or rituals to seek clarity and enlightenment in their lives.
  • Personal Growth: The duality of Agni and Soma can be reflected in the balance between action (Agni) and contemplation (Soma) in one's personal and professional life.
  • Community and Relationships: The offering of ghee symbolizes the importance of nourishing relationships and community ties, encouraging individuals to contribute positively to their surroundings.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the interconnectedness of the physical and spiritual worlds, urging individuals to seek balance and nourishment in their lives. It serves as a reminder of the importance of offerings, both literal and metaphorical, in fostering growth, clarity, and harmony in one's journey.
