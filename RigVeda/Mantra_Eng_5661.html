उद्वेति शुभगो विश्वचक्षाः साधारणः सूर्यो मानवशाणाम् |चक्षुर मित्रस्य वरुणस्य देवश्चर्मेव यः समविव्यक्त तमांसि ||
ud v eti subhago viśvacakṣāḥ sādhāraṇaḥ sūryo mānuṣāṇām | cakṣur mitrasya varuṇasya devaś carmeva yaḥ samavivyak tamāṁsi ||
  • ud v eti: This phrase translates to "rises up" or "ascends." It signifies the act of rising, which can be interpreted as the dawn or the emergence of light from darkness.
  • subhago: Meaning "auspicious" or "blessed," this word conveys a sense of positivity and good fortune. In real life, we often associate auspicious moments with new beginnings, such as starting a new job or moving into a new home.
  • viśvacakṣāḥ: This term means "all-seeing" or "universal eye." It suggests a perspective that encompasses everything, akin to having a broad understanding of life and its complexities. For example, a wise leader who considers the needs of all their constituents embodies this quality.
  • sādhāraṇaḥ: This word translates to "common" or "universal." It emphasizes the shared experiences and connections among all beings. In a community, this can be seen in collective celebrations or shared challenges that bring people together.
  • sūryo: Referring to the "sun," this word symbolizes light, energy, and life. The sun is essential for growth and sustenance, much like how positivity and hope are vital for personal development.
  • mānuṣāṇām: This means "of humans." It highlights the human experience and our connection to the divine and the natural world. It reminds us that we are part of a larger tapestry of existence.
  • cakṣur: This translates to "eye" or "sight." It represents perception and awareness. In real life, having a clear vision or insight into situations can lead to better decision-making.
  • mitrasya: Meaning "of the friend," this word signifies companionship and support. It reflects the importance of relationships in our lives, where friends provide guidance and comfort.
  • varuṇasya: This term refers to "Varuna," the deity associated with water and cosmic order. It symbolizes the balance and harmony in nature, reminding us of the need to respect and protect our environment.
  • devaś: Meaning "of the gods," this word connects the divine with the earthly realm, suggesting that there is a higher power guiding our lives.
  • carmeva: This translates to "like a song" or "melody." It signifies harmony and the beauty of life when lived in accordance with nature and the universe.
  • yaḥ samavivyak: This phrase means "who has spread out" or "who has distributed." It suggests the act of sharing and the importance of generosity in human interactions.
  • tamāṁsi: This translates to "darkness" or "ignorance." It represents the challenges and obstacles we face in life, which can be overcome through knowledge and enlightenment.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of rising towards enlightenment and positivity, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of awareness, friendship, and harmony with nature. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek knowledge, foster relationships, and embrace the light in our lives, guiding us through the darkness of ignorance. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal growth, community building, and environmental stewardship, encouraging us to live in a way that uplifts ourselves and those around us.
