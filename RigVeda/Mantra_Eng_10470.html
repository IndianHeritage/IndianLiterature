मयोभूर्वातोऽभिवातूस्राऽऊर्जस्वतीरोषधीरारिषन्ताम्। पीवस्वतीर्जīvधन्याः पिबन्त्ववसाया पद्वते रुद्रमृळ।
mayobhūr vāto abhi vātūsrā ūrjasvatīr oṣadhīr ā riśantām | pīvasvatīr jīvadhanyāḥ pibantv avasāya padvate rudra mṛḻa ||
  • mayobhūr vāto: This phrase can be translated as "may the wind be full of vitality." Here, "mayobhūr" signifies abundance or fullness, while "vāto" refers to the wind or air. In real life, this can be related to the importance of clean air and its role in sustaining life.
  • abhi vātūsrā: This means "may the winds blow towards us." It emphasizes the need for favorable conditions and support from nature. For example, in agriculture, favorable winds can help in pollination and the growth of crops.
  • ūrjasvatīr oṣadhīr: This translates to "the herbs that are full of energy." "Oṣadhīr" refers to medicinal plants or herbs, which are vital for health and healing. In modern life, we see a resurgence in the use of herbal medicine and natural remedies, highlighting the significance of these plants.
  • ā riśantām: This phrase means "may they be nourished." It suggests a desire for growth and sustenance, applicable in various contexts such as personal development, community growth, and environmental sustainability.
  • pīvasvatīr jīvadhanyāḥ: This can be interpreted as "may the nourishing ones, the life-giving forces, be abundant." This highlights the importance of resources that sustain life, such as water, food, and clean air. In real life, this can relate to sustainable practices that ensure the availability of these resources for future generations.
  • pibantv avasāya: This means "may they drink and be satisfied." It emphasizes the need for fulfillment and satisfaction in life. In a broader sense, it can relate to the pursuit of happiness and contentment in various aspects of life, including relationships, career, and personal goals.
  • padvate rudra mṛḻa: This phrase can be translated as "may Rudra, the lord of the storm, be gentle." Rudra represents a powerful force of nature, and the plea for gentleness signifies a desire for balance and harmony in life. In real life, this can be seen in the need for balance between human activities and nature, advocating for environmental conservation.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, nature, and the need for balance. It calls for the abundance of vital resources, the nourishment of life, and the gentle guidance of powerful forces. In real life, this mantra is applicable in various scenarios, such as environmental conservation, sustainable living, and personal growth. It serves as a reminder of our dependence on nature and the importance of nurturing our surroundings for a harmonious existence.
