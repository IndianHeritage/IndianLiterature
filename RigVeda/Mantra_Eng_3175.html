अस्माकं जोष्य अध्वरम् अस्माकं यज्ञम् अङ्गिरः |अस्माकं श्रिणुधी हवम् ||
asmākaṁ joṣy adhvaram asmākaṁ yajñam aṅgiraḥ | asmākaṁ śṛṇudhī havam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmākaṁ: Our
    • joṣy: To be enjoyed or to be nourished
    • adhvaram: The ritual or sacrifice
    • yajñam: The offering or worship
    • aṅgiraḥ: The divine or the seer (often referring to the sages)
    • śṛṇudhī: Listen or pay attention
    • havam: The invocation or call
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for divine attention and support in our rituals and sacrifices.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and collective effort in spiritual practices.
    • The invocation seeks to ensure that the offerings made are accepted and enjoyed by the divine, represented by the sages and seers.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, such as a wedding or festival, people come together to perform rituals that symbolize unity and shared joy. This mantra reflects the essence of such gatherings, where collective offerings are made to seek blessings.
    • In modern corporate settings, team-building activities can be seen as a form of collective ritual where employees come together to strengthen their bonds and work towards common goals, akin to a yajña.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of community in spiritual practices, reminding us that our individual actions are part of a larger whole.
    • It encourages us to be mindful of our contributions to the collective, whether in spiritual, social, or professional contexts.
    • In daily life, this can translate to being more engaged in community service, participating in group activities, and fostering a sense of belonging and support among peers.
