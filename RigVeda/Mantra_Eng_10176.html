येन द्यौर उग्रā पृथिवī च दृढā येन स्वः स्तभितं येन नाकः |यो अन्तरिक्षे रजसो विमानः कसमि देवाय हविषा विद्हेम ||
yena dyaur ugrā pṛthivī ca dṛḻhā yena svaḥ stabhitaṁ yena nākaḥ | yo antarikṣe rajaso vimānaḥ kasmai devāya haviṣā vidhema ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yena: By whom
    • dyaur: The sky
    • ugrā: Powerful or fierce
    • pṛthivī: The earth
    • ca: And
    • dṛḻhā: Firm or stable
    • yena: By whom
    • svaḥ: The heavens
    • stabhitaṁ: Established or fixed
    • yena: By whom
    • nākaḥ: The celestial realm
    • yo: Who
    • antarikṣe: In the atmosphere or space
    • rajaso: Of the light or brightness
    • vimānaḥ: The flying chariot or vehicle
    • kasmai: To whom
    • devāya: To the deity
    • haviṣā: With offerings
    • vidhema: We offer or we invoke
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the powerful forces of nature, represented by the sky and the earth, and acknowledges their stability and strength.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the heavens and the earth, suggesting that both realms are interconnected and influenced by divine forces.
    • The mention of "antarikṣe" and "vimānaḥ" can be interpreted as a recognition of the vastness of the universe and the potential for exploration and understanding beyond our immediate surroundings.
    • By addressing the deity with offerings, the mantra reflects a deep respect for the divine and the natural order, recognizing the need for harmony and balance in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, this mantra can be related to environmental awareness. Just as the mantra acknowledges the strength of the earth and sky, we must recognize the importance of preserving our natural environment for future generations.
    • The concept of interconnectedness can be seen in various fields, such as ecology, where the health of one ecosystem affects others, similar to how the heavens and earth are interlinked.
    • In the context of space exploration, the reference to "antarikṣe" and "vimānaḥ" resonates with humanity's quest to understand the universe, pushing the boundaries of knowledge and technology.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power and stability of the natural world and the divine forces that govern it.
    • It encourages us to live in harmony with nature, respect the interconnectedness of all things, and recognize the importance of spiritual offerings and gratitude.
    • In a world facing environmental challenges and a quest for knowledge, the teachings of this mantra are more relevant than ever, guiding us towards a balanced and respectful relationship with our surroundings and the cosmos.
