इदं पित्रे मरुतामुच्यते वचः स्वादोः स्वादीयो रुद्राय वर्धनम् |रास्वा च नो अमृत मर्तभोजनं त्मने तोकाय तनयाय मृळ ||
idam pitre marutām ucyate vacaḥ svādoḥ svādīyo rudrāya vardhanam | rāsvā ca no amṛta martabhojanaṁ tmane tokāya tanayāya mṛḻa ||
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "This is spoken for the Maruts (the storm deities) and for the father. It is a sweet and delightful offering to Rudra, the fierce aspect of Shiva, for the enhancement of our well-being. May it bring us the essence of immortality and nourishment, and may it be a source of prosperity for our children."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • idam: This
    • pitre: For the father
    • marutām: Of the Maruts (storm deities)
    • ucyate: Is spoken
    • vacaḥ: Words
    • svādoḥ: Sweetness
    • svādīyo: Delightful
    • rudrāya: To Rudra
    • vardhanam: Enhancement or growth
    • rāsvā: Essence
    • ca: And
    • no: For us
    • amṛta: Immortality
    • martabhojanaṁ: Nourishment of mortals
    • tmane: For our well-being
    • tokāya: For children
    • tanayāya: For offspring
    • mṛḻa: May it be auspicious
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking blessings from divine forces, particularly the Maruts and Rudra, for the well-being of oneself and one's family.
    • It highlights the connection between the divine and the earthly, suggesting that offerings and prayers can lead to prosperity and nourishment.
    • The invocation of immortality signifies a desire for lasting legacy and health, not just for oneself but also for future generations.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be recited during family gatherings or rituals to invoke blessings for health and prosperity.
    • It can serve as a reminder to cherish family bonds and seek divine guidance in nurturing the next generation.
    • In times of difficulty, reciting this mantra can provide comfort and a sense of connection to a greater purpose, encouraging individuals to strive for well-being and abundance.
