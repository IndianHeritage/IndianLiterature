उप त्वा सातये नरो विप्रासो यन्ति धीतिभिः |उपाक्षराः सहस्रिणी ||
upa tvā sātaye naro viprāso yanti dhītibhiḥ | upākṣarā sahasriṇī ||
  • Mantra: "upa tvā sātaye naro viprāso yanti dhītibhiḥ | upākṣarā sahasriṇī ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • upa: towards, near
    • tvā: you
    • sātaye: for truth, for righteousness
    • naro: men, humans
    • viprāso: wise, learned individuals
    • yanti: go, move towards
    • dhītibhiḥ: with understanding, with wisdom
    • upākṣarā: with auspicious letters, with good words
    • sahasriṇī: a thousandfold, in abundance
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking truth and righteousness.
    • It suggests that wise individuals (viprāso) are those who move towards truth with understanding.
    • The phrase "upākṣarā sahasriṇī" indicates that the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom can yield abundant results.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who prioritize ethical practices and transparency often inspire their teams to work towards common goals with integrity.
    • In education, teachers who encourage critical thinking and understanding help students to grasp complex concepts, leading to a deeper appreciation of knowledge.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who communicate openly and truthfully foster trust and understanding, which strengthens their bonds.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of truth and wisdom in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding, which can lead to personal growth and societal progress.
    • In various aspects of life, from personal relationships to professional environments, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide us towards a more harmonious and enlightened existence.
