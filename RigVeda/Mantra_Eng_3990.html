आ धेनवः पयसा तूर्यर्था अमर्धन्तीर उप नो यान्तु मध्वा |महो राये बृहतीः सप्त विप्रो मयोभुवो जरिता जोहवीति ||
ā dhenavaḥ payasā tūrṇyarthā amardhantīr upa no yantu madhvā | maho rāye bṛhatīḥ sapta vipro mayobhuvo jaritā johavīti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā dhenavaḥ: "O, those who are endowed with wealth" - This refers to beings or entities that possess abundance or prosperity.
    • payasā: "with milk" - Milk symbolizes nourishment, purity, and sustenance.
    • tūrṇyarthā: "quickly" - This indicates a sense of urgency or immediacy.
    • amardhantīr: "may they not be obstructed" - A plea for unobstructed flow or progress.
    • upa no yantu: "may they come to us" - A call for blessings or positive energies to approach us.
    • madhvā: "with sweetness" - Sweetness represents joy, happiness, and positive experiences.
    • maho rāye: "for great wealth" - A desire for abundant prosperity and success.
    • bṛhatīḥ: "the vast" - Referring to the expansive nature of wealth or blessings.
    • sapta vipro: "seven sages" - Symbolizing wisdom and guidance from enlightened beings.
    • mayobhuvo: "of the divine" - Indicating a connection to the divine or spiritual realm.
    • jaritā: "the one who brings forth" - A reference to a force or entity that manifests or creates.
    • johavīti: "may they be invoked" - A call to action for invoking blessings or energies.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking abundance and prosperity in life.
    • It highlights the need for nourishment, both physical and spiritual, to achieve success.
    • The invocation of the seven sages symbolizes the importance of wisdom and guidance in our pursuits.
    • The plea for unobstructed flow suggests that obstacles should be removed for progress to occur.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be recited to attract prosperity and success in ventures.
    • Individuals may use this mantra during personal challenges to seek guidance and support from wise mentors.
    • In family settings, it can be a reminder to nurture relationships with love and sweetness, fostering a harmonious environment.
    • During times of difficulty, invoking this mantra can serve as a source of motivation to overcome obstacles and achieve goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of wealth, wisdom, and nourishment.
    • It encourages individuals to seek abundance while remaining open to divine guidance and support.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can cultivate a life filled with prosperity, joy, and fulfillment.
