मन्तर: नृबाहुभ्यां चोदितो धारणया सुतो अनुष्वधम् पवते सोम इन्द्र ते | आप्राः क्रतून सम अजैर अध्वरे मतीर वेर न दृषच चाम्वोर आसदद धरिः ||
nṛbāhubhyāṁ codito dhārayā suto 'nuṣvadham pavate soma indra te | āprāḥ kratūn sam ajair adhvare matīr ver na druṣac camvo3r āsadad dhariḥ ||
  • nṛbāhubhyāṁ: This term refers to "the men with strong arms" or "the powerful ones." It signifies strength and capability, often associated with leaders or warriors in society.
  • codito: This means "inspired" or "urged." It indicates a call to action or motivation, suggesting that the powerful ones are being prompted to take initiative.
  • dhārayā: This translates to "holding" or "sustaining." It implies the responsibility of maintaining order and stability in society.
  • suto: Meaning "son" or "offspring," it symbolizes continuity and the passing of values and responsibilities from one generation to the next.
  • 'anuṣvadham: This term can be interpreted as "in accordance with the truth" or "following the right path." It emphasizes the importance of righteousness in actions.
  • pavate: This means "to blow" or "to inspire," often associated with the wind or divine inspiration, suggesting a connection to the divine or spiritual guidance.
  • soma: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing nourishment and divine bliss. It represents the essence of life and vitality.
  • indra: Indra is the king of gods in Vedic mythology, representing power, strength, and leadership. He is often invoked for protection and guidance.
  • te: This means "your," indicating a personal connection or appeal to the divine or the powerful ones.
  • āprāḥ: This translates to "full" or "complete," suggesting abundance and fulfillment in life.
  • kratūn: Referring to "sacrifices" or "rituals," it highlights the importance of spiritual practices and offerings in maintaining harmony.
  • sam ajair: This means "together" or "united," emphasizing the strength found in unity and collaboration.
  • adhvare: This term refers to "sacred rituals" or "sacrifices," indicating the significance of spiritual practices in achieving goals.
  • matīr: Meaning "intellect" or "wisdom," it underscores the importance of thoughtful decision-making and insight.
  • ver: This translates to "strength" or "power," reinforcing the theme of capability and resilience.
  • na: This means "not," indicating a negation or a caution against certain actions.
  • druṣac: This term means "to see" or "to perceive," highlighting the importance of awareness and perception in life.
  • camvo3r: This can be interpreted as "to be strong" or "to be powerful," reinforcing the theme of strength.
  • āsadad: This means "to sit" or "to dwell," suggesting a sense of stability and presence.
  • dhariḥ: This translates to "the bearer" or "the one who holds," indicating responsibility and support.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, unity, and righteousness in leadership and society. It calls upon powerful individuals to act with wisdom and integrity, ensuring the well-being of the community. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to leadership roles in various fields, such as politics, business, and community service, where the leaders are urged to inspire and guide others towards a common goal while upholding ethical values. The mantra serves as a reminder that true strength lies not just in power, but in the ability to uplift and sustain others through wise and righteous actions.
