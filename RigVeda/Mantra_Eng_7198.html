जीवान नो अभि धेतनादित्याशः पुरा हतात् |कद ध स्थ हवनश्रुतः ||
jīvān no abhi dhetanādityāsaḥ purā hathāt | kad dha stha havanaśrutaḥ ||
  • jīvān: This word translates to "life" or "living beings." It signifies the essence of existence and vitality.
  • no: This is a possessive pronoun meaning "our" or "us," indicating a collective identity or community.
  • abhi: This preposition means "towards" or "in the direction of," suggesting an intention or purpose.
  • dhetanā: This term can be interpreted as "to be nourished" or "to be sustained," emphasizing the importance of support and sustenance in life.
  • ādityāsaḥ: This refers to "the sun" or "solar energy," which is often associated with life-giving properties and illumination.
  • purā: Meaning "before" or "in the past," it indicates a reference to ancient wisdom or traditions.
  • hathāt: This word means "by force" or "suddenly," suggesting an unexpected or powerful occurrence.
  • kad: This translates to "when," indicating a point in time or a condition.
  • dha: This is a verb form meaning "to place" or "to establish," indicating action or intention.
  • stha: Meaning "to stand" or "to remain," it emphasizes stability and permanence.
  • havanaśrutaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "the sound of invocation" or "the call of the sacred," referring to the importance of rituals and prayers in connecting with the divine.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation that seeks the sustenance of life through the energy of the sun, emphasizing the importance of community and collective well-being. It reflects a deep respect for the forces of nature and the ancient wisdom that guides human existence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Environmental Awareness: Just as the sun nourishes life on Earth, we must recognize our responsibility to protect and sustain our environment for future generations.
  • Community Support: The emphasis on "our" life suggests the importance of community. In times of crisis, such as during natural disasters or pandemics, collective support and cooperation become vital for survival.
  • Spiritual Practices: The invocation of the sun can be seen in various spiritual practices where individuals seek enlightenment and guidance. This can be reflected in daily rituals, meditation, or yoga, where practitioners connect with the energy of the sun.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of community, and the reverence for natural forces that sustain us. It encourages us to seek nourishment not just for ourselves but for our collective existence, fostering a sense of responsibility towards each other and the environment.
