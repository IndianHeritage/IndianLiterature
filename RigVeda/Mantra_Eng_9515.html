ते सत्येन मनसा गोपतिं गा इयानासा इषणयन्त धिभिः |बृहस्पतिर मिथोऽवद्यपेभिर उद उष्रियाऽसृजत स्वयुग्भिः ||
te satyena manasā gopatiṁ gā iyānāsa iṣaṇayanta dhībhiḥ | bṛhaspatir mithoavadyapebhir ud usriyā asṛjata svayugbhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • te: you
    • satyena: with truth
    • manasā: with the mind
    • gopatiṁ: the lord of the cows (often interpreted as a metaphor for the protector or guardian)
    • gā: song or hymn
    • iyānāsa: may come or may arrive
    • iṣaṇayanta: guiding or directing
    • dhībhiḥ: with wisdom or intellect
    • bṛhaspatir: the deity associated with wisdom and knowledge
    • mitho: together
    • avadyapebhir: with those who are free from sin
    • ud: up or above
    • usriyā: with the dawn or light
    • asṛjata: created or manifested
    • svayugbhiḥ: with their own powers or abilities
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of truth and wisdom in guiding one's thoughts and actions.
    • The reference to "gopatiṁ" symbolizes a protector or leader, suggesting that one should seek guidance from those who embody truth and wisdom.
    • The invocation of "bṛhaspatir" highlights the role of divine wisdom in the creation and manifestation of knowledge and understanding.
    • The phrase "with those who are free from sin" suggests the importance of purity in thought and action, aligning oneself with virtuous individuals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, a wise leader who embodies truth can inspire their team to work harmoniously towards a common goal, much like a shepherd guiding their flock.
    • In education, teachers who impart knowledge with integrity and wisdom can shape the minds of their students, leading them towards enlightenment.
    • In personal relationships, surrounding oneself with honest and virtuous friends can foster a supportive environment that encourages growth and understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of truth and wisdom in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek guidance from virtuous leaders and to cultivate a mindset that values integrity and knowledge.
    • In a world often filled with distractions and misinformation, this mantra is applicable in various scenarios, from personal development to leadership and community building.
