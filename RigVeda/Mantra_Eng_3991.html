आ सुष्टुती नमसां वर्तयध्यै dyāvā वाजाय पृथिवी अमृतध्रे |पिता माता मधुवचाः सुहस्ता भरे-भरे नो यशसाव अविष्टाम् ||
ā suṣṭutī namasā vartayadhyai dyāvā vājāya pṛthivī amṛdhre | pitā mātā madhuvacāḥ suhastā bhare-bhare no yaśasāv aviṣṭām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • suṣṭutī: well praised
    • namasā: with reverence
    • vartayadhyai: may you bestow
    • dyāvā: heaven
    • vājāya: for the purpose of strength
    • pṛthivī: earth
    • amṛdhre: immortal
    • pitā: father
    • mātā: mother
    • madhuvacāḥ: with sweet words
    • suhastā: with auspicious hands
    • bhare-bhare: may you fill
    • no: for us
    • yaśasāv: with fame
    • aviṣṭām: unbroken
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes the divine forces of heaven and earth, asking for blessings and strength.
    • It emphasizes the importance of reverence and gratitude towards one's parents, symbolizing the foundational support in life.
    • The phrase "with sweet words" suggests the power of positive communication and relationships.
    • The request for "filling us with fame" indicates a desire for recognition and success in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, showing respect and gratitude towards parents can strengthen familial bonds and create a supportive environment.
    • In professional life, using positive communication can enhance teamwork and lead to successful collaborations.
    • Seeking blessings from nature, such as through rituals or meditation, can foster a sense of connection and well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of reverence, communication, and the interconnectedness of life.
    • It serves as a reminder to appreciate our roots and the support we receive from our families and the universe.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to cultivate positive relationships and seek strength from both our heritage and the natural world.
