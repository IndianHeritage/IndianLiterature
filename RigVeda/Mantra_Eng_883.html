अधि सानौ न जिघ्नते वज्रेण शतपर्वणा |मन्दान इन्द्रो अन्धसः सकिभ्यो गातुम इच्छत्य आर्चन्न अनु स्वराज्यम् ||
adhi sānau ni jighnate vajreṇa śataparvaṇā | mandāna indro andhasaḥ sakhibhyo gātum icchaty arcann anu svarājyam ||
  • adhi sānau: This phrase can be translated as "upon the seat" or "in the presence of the assembly." It signifies a gathering or a place of importance where decisions are made.
  • ni jighnate: This means "does not harm" or "does not attack." It suggests a sense of protection or non-violence in the context of the assembly.
  • vajreṇa: Translated as "with the thunderbolt," this word symbolizes strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles. The thunderbolt is often associated with Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
  • śataparvaṇā: This term means "with a hundred folds" or "with great strength." It emphasizes the idea of resilience and the ability to withstand challenges.
  • mandāna: This word refers to "the one who is gentle" or "the one who is kind." It highlights the importance of compassion and gentleness in leadership.
  • indro: This refers to Indra, the chief deity in Vedic mythology, known for his bravery and leadership qualities.
  • andhasaḥ: This means "in darkness" or "in ignorance." It suggests a state of confusion or lack of clarity that needs to be addressed.
  • sakhibhyo: This translates to "to friends" or "to companions." It emphasizes the importance of community and collaboration.
  • gātum icchaty: This phrase means "desires to go" or "wants to move forward." It signifies the aspiration for progress and advancement.
  • arcann: This means "worship" or "to honor." It highlights the significance of reverence and respect in relationships and endeavors.
  • anu svarājyam: This translates to "after the sovereignty" or "following the rule." It suggests the idea of governance and the responsibilities that come with leadership.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of leadership that is both powerful and compassionate. It highlights the need for a leader to be strong like a thunderbolt while also being gentle and kind. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to various leadership roles, whether in politics, business, or community organizations. A leader who embodies these qualities can navigate challenges effectively while fostering a sense of community and collaboration among their peers. The mantra serves as a reminder that true leadership is not just about authority but also about nurturing relationships and guiding others towards a common goal.
