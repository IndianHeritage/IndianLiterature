सो अद्धा दाश्वध्वरोऽग्ने मर्तः सुभग स प्रशंस्यः |स धिभिर अस्तु सनीत ||
so addhā dāśvadhvaro 'gne martaḥ subhaga sa praśaṁsyaḥ | sa dhībhir astu sanitā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • so: thus, so
    • addhā: indeed, truly
    • dāśvadhvaro: O Agni, the one who is worthy of praise
    • agne: O Agni, the divine fire
    • martaḥ: mortal, human
    • subhaga: auspicious, fortunate
    • sa: he, that
    • praśaṁsyaḥ: worthy of praise, commendable
    • sa: he, that
    • dhībhir: with wisdom, intellect
    • astu: may it be, let it be
    • sanitā: well-being, prosperity
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra addresses Agni, the divine fire, acknowledging his role as a source of light and guidance.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and intellect in achieving well-being and prosperity.
    • The phrase "martaḥ subhaga" suggests that even mortals can attain auspiciousness through the right actions and devotion.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek guidance and clarity in their decisions, much like how Agni is invoked for wisdom.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders may embody the qualities of Agni by fostering a culture of transparency and ethical practices, leading to a prosperous work environment.
    • During rituals and ceremonies, fire is often used as a symbol of purity and transformation, representing the divine presence of Agni.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine guidance in our lives, particularly through the qualities of wisdom and intellect.
    • It teaches that by aligning ourselves with higher principles and seeking the blessings of the divine, we can achieve prosperity and well-being.
    • In essence, it serves as a reminder to cultivate inner wisdom and to recognize the divine presence in our everyday actions, leading to a more fulfilling life.
