संवत्सरीणं पय उष्रियायास तस्य माशीद यातुधानो नृचक्षः |पीयूषम अग्ने यतमस तितृप्सात तं प्रत्यञ्चम् आर्चिषा विध्य मर्मन् ||
saṁvatsarīṇam paya usriyāyās tasya māśīd yātudhāno nṛcakṣaḥ | pīyūṣam agne yatamas titṛpsāt tam pratyañcam arciṣā vidhya marman ||
  • saṁvatsarīṇam: This term refers to the cycle of years or the concept of time as it relates to the natural world. In real life, this can be understood as the seasons changing, marking the passage of time and the cycles of life.
  • paya: This word means "nourishment" or "sustenance." In a practical sense, it can be related to the food we consume, which sustains our bodies and provides energy for daily activities.
  • usriyāyās: This term can be interpreted as "for the benefit" or "for the welfare." It emphasizes the importance of actions taken for the greater good, such as community service or environmental conservation efforts.
  • tasya: Meaning "of that" or "of it," this word connects the previous concepts to a specific subject, which in this context refers to the divine or cosmic order.
  • māśīd: This term translates to "may it not be" or "let it not happen." It reflects a desire to avoid negative outcomes, similar to how we might wish for peace and harmony in our lives and communities.
  • yātudhāno: This word refers to "evil spirits" or "malicious beings." In real life, this can symbolize challenges or negative influences we encounter, such as toxic relationships or harmful environments.
  • nṛcakṣaḥ: This term means "human eyes" or "human perception." It highlights the importance of awareness and understanding in navigating life's challenges.
  • pīyūṣam: This word means "nectar" or "elixir," symbolizing the essence of life and joy. In practical terms, it can represent the pursuit of happiness and fulfillment in our lives.
  • agne: This term refers to "Agni," the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, fire can represent passion and the drive to achieve our goals.
  • yatamas: This word means "the best" or "the highest." It encourages striving for excellence in our endeavors, whether in personal growth, education, or professional achievements.
  • titṛpsāt: This term translates to "may it be satisfied" or "may it be fulfilled." It reflects the human desire for contentment and satisfaction in life.
  • tam: Meaning "that," it refers back to the divine or the higher purpose we seek in our lives.
  • pratyañcam: This word means "the opposite" or "the contrary." It suggests the need to be aware of dualities in life, such as joy and sorrow, success and failure.
  • arciṣā: This term translates to "by light" or "by radiance," symbolizing knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment. In real life, this can relate to the pursuit of education and understanding.
  • vidhya: This word means "to know" or "to understand." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge in overcoming challenges and achieving our goals.
  • marman: This term refers to "the essence" or "the core." It signifies the importance of understanding the fundamental truths of life and existence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of nourishment, awareness, and the pursuit of knowledge. It serves as a reminder to strive for excellence and fulfillment while being mindful of the challenges we face. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal development, community service, and the pursuit of happiness, encouraging individuals to seek a deeper understanding of themselves and the world around them. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, one can navigate life's complexities with wisdom and grace.
