मह्य यत पितृ ईं रसं दिवे कर अव त्सरत पृशान्यश चिकीत्वान् |सृजद अस्तां धृतशतां दिद्युम अस्मै स्वायां देवो दुतितरि त्विषिं धात ||
mahe yat pitra īṁ rasaṁ dive kar ava tsarat pṛśanyaś cikitvān | sṛjad astā dhṛṣatā didyum asmai svāyāṁ devo duhitari tviṣiṁ dhāt ||
  • mahe yat pitra īṁ rasaṁ: This phrase can be translated to "Great is that essence of the fathers." Here, "mahe" signifies greatness, "pitra" refers to ancestors or forefathers, and "rasaṁ" denotes essence or essence of life. This highlights the importance of honoring our ancestors and recognizing their contributions to our existence.
  • dive kar ava tsarat: This translates to "Let it shine forth in the heavens." The word "dive" refers to the sky or heavens, while "kar" means to make or create, and "ava tsarat" implies a call to action or a plea for manifestation. This suggests a desire for the blessings and wisdom of our ancestors to be made evident in our lives.
  • pṛśanyaś cikitvān: This can be interpreted as "the wise one who understands the mysteries." "Pṛśanyaś" refers to the one who is knowledgeable or wise, and "cikitvān" means to understand or comprehend. This emphasizes the value of wisdom and understanding in navigating life's complexities.
  • sṛjad astā dhṛṣatā: This phrase means "create with courage." "Sṛjad" translates to create or bring forth, "astā" means with strength or courage, and "dhṛṣatā" refers to bravery. This encourages individuals to act with courage and determination in their endeavors.
  • didyum asmai svāyāṁ: This translates to "may the divine light shine upon him." "Didyum" refers to light or illumination, "asmai" means for him, and "svāyāṁ" implies a sense of divinity or sacredness. This signifies a prayer for divine guidance and enlightenment.
  • devo duhitari tviṣiṁ dhāt: This can be interpreted as "the divine nurtures the one who is worthy." "Devo" refers to the divine or godly, "duhitari" means nurturer or caregiver, and "tviṣiṁ dhāt" implies the act of bestowing or granting. This highlights the belief that the divine supports those who are deserving and strive for goodness.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of honoring our ancestors, seeking wisdom, and acting with courage while inviting divine guidance into our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways, such as:

  • Practicing gratitude towards our ancestors by remembering their sacrifices and teachings.
  • Seeking knowledge and wisdom in our personal and professional lives to make informed decisions.
  • Embracing challenges with courage and determination, knowing that we have the support of our ancestors and the divine.
  • Striving to be worthy of divine blessings by living a life of integrity and compassion.

This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our past, present, and future, encouraging us to honor our roots while striving for enlightenment and courage in our journey through life.
