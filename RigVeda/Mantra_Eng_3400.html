मन्त्र: न हि स्मा ते शतम् चन राधो वरन्त आमुरः |न च्युत्नानि करिष्यतः ||
nahi ṣmā te śataṁ cana rādho varanta āmuraḥ | na cyautnāni kariṣyataḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nahi: not
    • ṣmā: your
    • te: of you
    • śataṁ: hundred
    • cana: at all
    • rādho: praise or glory
    • varanta: those who are worthy
    • āmuraḥ: immortal or eternal
    • na: not
    • cyautnāni: falling away or diminishing
    • kariṣyataḥ: will be done or will happen
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the idea that true glory and praise cannot be diminished or taken away.
    • The phrase "nahi ṣmā te śataṁ cana" suggests that no matter how many praises or accolades one receives, they do not define the essence of a person.
    • The term "varanta āmuraḥ" indicates that those who are truly worthy of praise are eternal and their worth is not subject to the fluctuations of worldly recognition.
    • The latter part, "na cyautnāni kariṣyataḥ," reinforces the notion that the true essence of a person remains intact, regardless of external circumstances or opinions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the world of sports, athletes often face immense pressure to perform and receive accolades. However, their true character and dedication to their sport remain unchanged, regardless of wins or losses.
    • In the corporate world, employees may receive promotions and recognition, but their core values and work ethic define their true worth, not just their titles or achievements.
    • Artists may gain fame and recognition, but their creative spirit and passion for their art are what truly make them immortal in the hearts of their audience.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder that external validation is fleeting and does not define our true selves.
    • It encourages individuals to focus on their intrinsic values and character rather than seeking constant approval from others.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal growth, professional development, and maintaining integrity in the face of challenges.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that our true worth is eternal and cannot be diminished by external circumstances.
