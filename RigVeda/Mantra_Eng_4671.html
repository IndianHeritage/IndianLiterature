युयं गावो मेदयथा कृशं चिद अश्रीरं चित कृणुथा सुप्रतीकम् | भद्रं गृहं कृणुथ भद्रवाचो बृहत् वो वय उच्यते सभासु ||
yūyaṁ gāvo medayathā kṛśaṁ cid aśrīraṁ cit kṛṇuthā supratīkam | bhadraṁ gṛhaṁ kṛṇutha bhadravāco bṛhad vo vaya ucyate sabhāsu ||
  • yūyaṁ - You (plural), referring to a group or community.
  • gāvo - Cows, symbolizing wealth, nourishment, and prosperity in Vedic culture.
  • medayathā - As you are nourished or sustained.
  • kṛśaṁ - Weak or feeble, indicating a state of vulnerability.
  • cid - Indeed, emphasizing certainty.
  • aśrīraṁ - Without a body, referring to the spiritual or non-physical aspect of existence.
  • cit - Consciousness or awareness, highlighting the importance of mental and spiritual clarity.
  • kṛṇuthā - Make or create, suggesting an active role in shaping one's reality.
  • supratīkam - Auspicious or favorable, indicating a positive outcome.
  • bhadraṁ - Good or auspicious, reinforcing the theme of positivity.
  • gṛhaṁ - Home or dwelling, symbolizing safety and comfort.
  • kṛṇutha - Again, make or create, emphasizing the importance of action.
  • bhadravāco - Good speech or words, highlighting the power of communication.
  • bṛhad - Great or vast, suggesting the importance of expansive thinking.
  • vo - Your, indicating possession or belonging.
  • vaya - We or us, fostering a sense of community.
  • ucyate - Is spoken or declared, indicating the importance of expression.
  • sabhāsu - In gatherings or assemblies, emphasizing the social aspect of human interaction.

This mantra emphasizes the importance of community, nourishment, and the creation of a positive environment. It suggests that as a collective, individuals should work towards creating a supportive and prosperous atmosphere, both materially and spiritually. The reference to cows symbolizes the wealth and sustenance that comes from nature, while the call to create a good home and use positive speech highlights the significance of communication and relationships in fostering a harmonious society.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In family settings, where creating a nurturing home environment is essential for the well-being of all members.
  • In workplaces, where positive communication and teamwork can lead to greater productivity and job satisfaction.
  • In community gatherings, where collective efforts can lead to social harmony and mutual support.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for collective action towards creating a prosperous and harmonious environment. It serves as a reminder that through positive communication, nurturing relationships, and a focus on community well-being, individuals can contribute to a better society. This mantra encourages us to be mindful of our words and actions, fostering a spirit of cooperation and support in all aspects of life.
