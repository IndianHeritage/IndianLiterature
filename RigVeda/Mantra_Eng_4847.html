उप श्वासय पृथिवीं उता द्यां पुरुत्रा ते मनुतां विष्ठितं जगत् |स दुण्डुभे सजूर इन्द्रेण देवैर दूराद दवीयो अप सेध शत्रून् ||
upa śvāsaya pṛthivīm uta dyām purutrā te manutāṁ viṣṭhitaṁ jagat | sa dundubhe sajūr indreṇa devair dūrād davīyo apa sedha śatrūn ||
  • upa śvāsaya pṛthivīm uta dyām: This phrase can be translated as "Breathe life into the Earth and the sky." Here, "upa" means "to" or "towards," "śvāsaya" refers to "breathe life" or "inspire," "pṛthivīm" means "the Earth," and "dyām" means "the sky." This suggests a call to invigorate both the physical and spiritual realms.
  • purutrā te manutāṁ viṣṭhitaṁ jagat: This translates to "The world is established through your guidance." "Purutrā" means "in many places," "te" means "your," "manutāṁ" refers to "guidance" or "thought," and "viṣṭhitaṁ jagat" means "the established world." This emphasizes the importance of divine guidance in the creation and sustenance of the universe.
  • sa dundubhe sajūr indreṇa devair: This can be interpreted as "He who is like the drum, with Indra and the gods." "Sa" means "he," "dundubhe" refers to "the drum," "sajūr" means "like," "indreṇa" refers to "with Indra," and "devair" means "with the gods." The drum symbolizes the call to action and the rhythm of life, suggesting that divine forces are at play in the world.
  • dūrād davīyo apa sedha śatrūn: This translates to "From afar, may you remove the enemies." "Dūrād" means "from afar," "davīyo" refers to "the powerful," "apa" means "remove," and "sedha śatrūn" means "the enemies." This is a plea for protection and the removal of obstacles or adversaries that hinder progress.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of divine guidance, the interconnectedness of the Earth and sky, and the call for protection against adversities. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as seeking inspiration in challenging times, recognizing the importance of guidance in our decisions, and invoking strength to overcome obstacles. Whether in personal struggles, community challenges, or global issues, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine support available to us and the need to harmonize our actions with the greater universe.
