सोमा राजन् मृळयाऽनः स्वस्ति तव स्मसि व्रत्याऽस तस्य विद्धि |अलर्ति दक्ष उता मन्युर इन्दो मा नो आर्यो अनुकामम् परा दाहः ||
soma rājan mṛḻayā naḥ svasti tava smasi vratyā3s tasya viddhi | alarti dakṣa uta manyur indo mā no aryo anukāmam parā dāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "soma rājan mṛḻayā naḥ svasti tava smasi vratyā3s tasya viddhi | alarti dakṣa uta manyur indo mā no aryo anukāmam parā dāḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • soma: Refers to the sacred drink, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • rājan: King or ruler, symbolizing authority and leadership.
    • mṛḻayā: To soothe or calm, indicating a desire for peace and tranquility.
    • naḥ: Our, indicating a collective or communal aspect.
    • svasti: Well-being or auspiciousness, a common wish for health and prosperity.
    • tava: Your, referring to the divine or higher power.
    • smasi: To be, indicating existence or presence.
    • vratyā3s: Those who are devoted or committed, often referring to followers or disciples.
    • tasya: Of that, referring to the divine or the higher power mentioned earlier.
    • viddhi: Know or understand, a call for awareness and knowledge.
    • alarti: Without obstacles or hindrances, indicating a smooth path.
    • dakṣa: Skillful or capable, referring to competence and ability.
    • uta: And, a conjunction linking ideas.
    • manyur: Anger or wrath, indicating emotional states that need to be managed.
    • indo: O Indra, a reference to the king of gods, symbolizing strength and power.
    • mā: Not, a negation indicating a desire to avoid something.
    • no: Our, again emphasizing the collective aspect.
    • aryo: Noble or honorable, referring to virtuous qualities.
    • anukāmam: According to desire or wish, indicating alignment with one's intentions.
    • parā: Supreme or beyond, indicating a higher state of being.
    • dāḥ: Give or grant, a request for blessings or gifts.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra invokes the divine for peace, well-being, and the removal of obstacles.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and collective well-being, urging individuals to seek harmony and understanding.
    • The reference to Indra highlights the need for strength and capability in overcoming challenges.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In personal life, this mantra can be a reminder to seek peace and tranquility in times of stress, encouraging individuals to manage their emotions effectively.
    • In leadership roles, it serves as a call to lead with compassion and understanding, fostering a supportive environment for all.
    • In community settings, it encourages collaboration and collective efforts towards common goals, promoting a sense of unity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance for peace, strength, and communal harmony.
    • Its teachings are applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to leadership and community building.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can navigate life's challenges with grace and purpose.
