उद उ ब्रह्माण्य ऐरात श्रवस्येन्द्रं समर्ये महाय वसिष्ठ |आ यो विश्वानि शवसा ततानोपश्रोता मा ईवतो वचांसि ||
ud u brahmāṇy airata śravasyendraṁ samarye mahayā vasiṣṭha | ā yo viśvāni śavasā tatānopaśrotā ma īvato vacāṁsi ||
  • ud u brahmāṇy airata: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the divine or the ultimate reality (Brahman) to rise or manifest. In real life, this can be seen as a metaphor for awakening one's higher consciousness or potential.
  • śravasyendraṁ samarye: Here, "śravasyendraṁ" refers to the lord of fame or glory, while "samarye" suggests a call to action or engagement in a battle. This can be related to striving for recognition and success in one's endeavors, akin to a student working hard to achieve academic excellence.
  • mahayā vasiṣṭha: "Mahayā" means great or significant, and "vasiṣṭha" refers to the best or the most excellent. This can be understood as a pursuit of greatness in one's actions and thoughts, similar to a leader inspiring their team to achieve remarkable results.
  • ā yo viśvāni śavasā tatān: This line speaks to the idea of encompassing all beings or aspects of existence. It emphasizes unity and interconnectedness, much like how a community comes together to support one another during challenging times.
  • upaśrotā ma īvato vacāṁsi: "Upaśrotā" refers to the one who listens or pays attention, while "vacāṁsi" means words or speech. This suggests the importance of communication and understanding in relationships, akin to how effective listening can resolve conflicts in personal or professional settings.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of awakening one's higher self, striving for excellence, and fostering unity and communication. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, teamwork, and community building. By embodying these values, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and successful society.
