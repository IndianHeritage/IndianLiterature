आ पक्तासो भलानसो भनन्तालिनासो विषाणिनः शिवासः |आ यो 'नयत सदहमाऽआर्यस्य गव्याऽतृट्सुभ्यो अजगन युधा नृन ||
ā pakthāso bhalānaso bhanantālināso viṣāṇinaḥ śivāsaḥ | ā yo 'nayat sadhamā āryasya gavyā tṛtsubhyo ajagan yudhā nṝn ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā pakthāso: From the well-prepared (or well-cooked).
    • bhalānaso: With strong or powerful (ones).
    • bhanantālināso: Those who are engaged in the act of speaking or proclaiming.
    • viṣāṇinaḥ: Those who possess horns (symbolizing strength or power).
    • śivāsaḥ: The auspicious or benevolent ones.
    • ā yo: He who.
    • 'nayat: Leads or guides.
    • sadhamā: To the right place or to the right path.
    • āryasya: Of the noble or honorable one.
    • gavyā: Of the cows (symbolizing wealth and nourishment).
    • tṛtsubhyo: From the three (or various) sources.
    • ajagan: The one who is unyielding or steadfast.
    • yudhā: In battle or conflict.
    • nṝn: Of men or humans.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the importance of strength, guidance, and auspiciousness in life.
    • The phrase "ā pakthāso bhalānaso" suggests that one should be well-prepared and strong to face life's challenges.
    • "bhanantālināso viṣāṇinaḥ" indicates the power of communication and the strength that comes from unity and collective effort.
    • The mention of "śivāsaḥ" emphasizes the need for benevolence and auspiciousness in our actions and intentions.
    • When it says "ā yo 'nayat sadhamā āryasya," it highlights the role of a leader or guide who directs us towards the right path, akin to a mentor in our lives.
    • The reference to "gavyā" symbolizes the importance of nurturing and sustaining resources, much like how we care for our families and communities.
    • "tṛtsubhyo ajagan yudhā nṝn" reflects the idea of resilience and steadfastness in the face of adversity, reminding us that battles are a part of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a well-prepared team (ā pakthāso) with strong leadership (bhalānaso) can effectively navigate challenges and achieve success.
    • In personal relationships, open communication (bhanantālināso) fosters understanding and strengthens bonds, much like the horns of a strong animal symbolize protection.
    • In community service, leaders (ā yo 'nayat) guide volunteers towards impactful projects, ensuring that efforts are directed towards noble causes (āryasya).
    • In agriculture, nurturing resources (gavyā) is essential for sustainability, reflecting the importance of caring for our environment.
    • In times of conflict, individuals who remain steadfast (ajagan) and resilient (yudhā) inspire others to persevere through difficulties.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, guidance, and auspiciousness in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that preparation, communication, and benevolence are key to overcoming challenges.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal, professional, or communal contexts, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide us towards success and harmony.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to be resilient and to support one another in our journeys, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
