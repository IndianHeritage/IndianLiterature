यत् सिन्धौ यद् असिक्न्यां यत् समुद्रेषु मरुतः सुभरिषः |यत् पर्वतेषु भेषजम् ||
yat sindhau yad asiknyāṁ yat samudreṣu marutaḥ subarhiṣaḥ | yat parvateṣu bheṣajam ||
  • Mantra: "yat sindhau yad asiknyāṁ yat samudreṣu marutaḥ subarhiṣaḥ | yat parvateṣu bheṣajam ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "Whatever is in the rivers, whatever is in the oceans, whatever is in the winds, and whatever is in the mountains, that is the healing essence."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yat: whatever
    • sindhau: in the rivers
    • asiknyāṁ: in the oceans
    • samudreṣu: in the seas
    • marutaḥ: winds or air
    • subarhiṣaḥ: healing or beneficial
    • parvateṣu: in the mountains
    • bheṣajam: medicine or healing
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature and its elements.
    • It suggests that healing and medicinal properties can be found in various natural sources:
      • Rivers: Freshwater sources often contain minerals and nutrients beneficial for health. For example, many cultures use river plants for herbal remedies.
      • Oceans: The ocean is a source of various marine resources, including fish and seaweeds, which are rich in omega-3 fatty acids and other nutrients.
      • Winds: The air we breathe is essential for life. Clean air is vital for health, and practices like yoga emphasize the importance of breathing exercises (pranayama) for well-being.
      • Mountains: Mountain herbs and plants are often used in traditional medicine. For instance, the Himalayas are known for their unique flora that has medicinal properties.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the holistic approach to health and healing, recognizing that nature provides all the necessary elements for well-being.
    • In real life, it encourages us to respect and utilize natural resources responsibly, promoting sustainability and environmental awareness.
    • It can be applied in various fields such as herbal medicine, environmental conservation, and holistic health practices.
