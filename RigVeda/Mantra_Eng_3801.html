अग्नि चकिद्ध्य अस्य न इदं वचः सहस्यम् |तं त्वा सुशिप्र दम्पते स्तोमैर वर्धन्त्य अत्रयो गीरभिः शुम्भन्त्य अत्रयः ||
agne cikiddhy a1sya na idaṁ vacaḥ sahasya | taṁ tvā suśipra dampate stomair vardhanty atrayo gīrbhiḥ śumbhanty atrayaḥ ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Cikiddhy: This term means to manifest or to make known. It suggests the idea of bringing forth or revealing something.
  • A1sya: This can be interpreted as 'this' or 'of this', indicating a connection to the subject being discussed.
  • Na idaṁ vacaḥ: Translated as 'not this speech', it implies that the words being spoken are not ordinary; they carry deeper significance.
  • Sahasya: This word means 'with strength' or 'powerfully', indicating the potency of the speech or invocation.
  • Taṁ tvā: This phrase means 'to you', addressing Agni directly, showing reverence and devotion.
  • Suśipra: This term means 'well-formed' or 'excellent', suggesting that the offerings or invocations are of high quality.
  • Dampate: This refers to the 'lord' or 'master', indicating a relationship of respect and devotion towards Agni.
  • Stomair: This word means 'hymns' or 'praises', indicating the act of worship through song and poetry.
  • Vardhanty: This means 'to increase' or 'to grow', suggesting that the offerings and praises will enhance the presence of Agni.
  • Atrayo: This term refers to the 'Atri' sages, who are known for their wisdom and connection to divine knowledge.
  • Gīrbhiḥ: This means 'with words' or 'through speech', emphasizing the importance of verbal expression in worship.
  • Śumbhanty: This word means 'to be praised' or 'to be celebrated', indicating the honor given to Agni through these invocations.

The mantra essentially calls upon Agni, the fire god, to manifest his presence and power through the strength of the words spoken. It emphasizes the importance of quality offerings and praises, suggesting that the act of worship is not just a ritual but a heartfelt expression of devotion. The invocation is directed towards Agni, asking for his blessings and presence in a powerful and meaningful way.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: During religious ceremonies, invoking Agni can symbolize the purification of intentions and the illumination of the mind.
  • Personal Growth: The mantra can serve as a reminder to manifest one's inner strength and to express oneself powerfully and authentically.
  • Community Gatherings: In gatherings where collective energy is needed, invoking Agni can symbolize unity and the shared purpose of the group.

In conclusion, this mantra highlights the significance of invoking divine energy through powerful words and quality offerings. It serves as a reminder of the importance of intention and devotion in our actions, whether in spiritual practices or daily life. By recognizing the power of our words and the energy we bring forth, we can create a more meaningful and impactful existence.
