मन्त्र: सस्थावानां यवयसी त्वं एक इच्छ चचिपत इन्द्र विश्वाभिर ऊतिभिः | माध्यंदिनस्य सवनस्य वृत्रहन्न अनेद्य पिबा सोमस्य वज्रिवः ||
sasthāvānā yavayasi tvam eka ic chacīpata indra viśvābhir ūtibhiḥ | mādhyaṁdinasya savanasya vṛtrahann anedya pibā somasya vajrivaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Sasthāvānā: One who is strong or powerful.
    • Yavayasi: You are the one who is capable or able.
    • Tvam: You.
    • Eka: Alone or singular.
    • Ic: Will or desire.
    • Chacīpata: The one who is the master of the light or brilliance.
    • Indra: The king of gods, representing strength and power.
    • Viśvābhir: With all or universal.
    • Ūtibhiḥ: With energies or powers.
    • Mādhyaṁdinasya: Of the midday or noon.
    • Savanasya: Of the offering or sacrifice.
    • Vṛtrahann: Slayer of Vritra, symbolizing the overcoming of obstacles.
    • Anedya: Unconquerable or invincible.
    • Pibā: Drink or consume.
    • Somasya: Of the soma, a sacred drink.
    • Vajrivaḥ: With the strength of a thunderbolt.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the powerful deity, emphasizing his unique strength and capability.
    • It highlights the importance of unity and collective strength, as Indra is called upon with universal energies.
    • The reference to midday signifies a time of peak energy and potential, suggesting that this is a moment for action and realization.
    • The mention of Vritra symbolizes the challenges and obstacles in life, and Indra's role as the slayer of these challenges is a reminder of overcoming difficulties.
    • The consumption of soma represents the nourishment of the spirit and the importance of vitality in achieving one's goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied when a team faces significant challenges. Just as Indra overcomes Vritra, a team can unite to tackle obstacles, drawing on their collective strengths.
    • In personal life, during moments of self-doubt or struggle, one can invoke the spirit of Indra to remind themselves of their inner strength and capability to overcome challenges.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, this mantra can serve as a reminder to harness the peak energy of the moment and to celebrate unity and strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the strength within oneself and the importance of unity in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize their potential and to draw upon collective energies to achieve their goals.
    • In essence, it is a call to action, urging one to embrace their inner power and to face obstacles with courage and determination.
