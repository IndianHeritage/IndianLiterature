इन्धानो अग्निं वनवद वनुष्यतः कृतब्रह्मा शूषुवद रातहव्य इति |जातेन जातमति स प्र सर्सृत्य यं-यं युजं कृत्नुते ब्रह्मणस पतिः ||
indhāno agniṁ vanavad vanuṣyataḥ kṛtabrahmā śūśuvad rātahavya it | jātena jātam ati sa pra sarsṛte yaṁ-yaṁ yujaṁ kṛṇute brahmaṇas patiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • indhāno: igniting or kindling
    • agniṁ: fire
    • vanavad: like a forest
    • vanuṣyataḥ: from the forest
    • kṛtabrahmā: having performed the great act
    • śūśuvad: like a whisper or soft sound
    • rātahavya: the one who is invoked
    • jātena: born or generated
    • jātam: that which is born
    • ati: beyond
    • sa: he or that
    • pra: forth or out
    • sarsṛte: flows or moves
    • yaṁ-yaṁ: whatever
    • yujaṁ: is connected or joined
    • kṛṇute: he makes or creates
    • brahmaṇas: of the divine or the ultimate reality
    • patiḥ: lord or master
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the act of kindling fire, which is a significant ritual in Vedic traditions.
    • The imagery of fire being like a forest suggests the vastness and the natural essence of the divine.
    • The phrase "kṛtabrahmā" indicates the importance of performing actions that align with the ultimate truth or reality.
    • The mantra emphasizes the connection between the divine and the material world, suggesting that whatever is created is a manifestation of the divine will.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is used in rituals to symbolize purification and transformation. For instance, in Hindu weddings, the sacred fire (Agni) is invoked to bless the union.
    • The concept of "kṛtabrahmā" can be seen in modern contexts where individuals strive to align their actions with their values, such as in environmental activism where people work towards sustainability.
    • The idea of connection to the divine can be reflected in practices like meditation or mindfulness, where individuals seek to understand their place in the universe and their relationship with a higher power.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of fire as a symbol of transformation and connection to the divine.
    • It teaches us the importance of aligning our actions with a higher purpose, encouraging us to reflect on our contributions to the world.
    • In real life, this can inspire individuals to engage in practices that promote harmony, sustainability, and a deeper understanding of their existence.
