आ हि ष्मा सूनवे पितापिर यजत्य आपये |सखा सख्ये वरेण्यः ||
ā hi ṣmā sūnave pitāpir yajaty āpaye | sakhā sakhye vareṇyaḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā hi: This can be interpreted as "indeed" or "truly," emphasizing the importance of what follows.
    • ṣmā: Refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," often associated with material and spiritual abundance.
    • sūnave: Means "to the son" or "to the offspring," indicating a focus on lineage and familial bonds.
    • pitāpir: This term combines "pitā" (father) and "apir" (mother), highlighting the parental figures in one's life.
    • yajaty: Translates to "sacrifices" or "offers," suggesting an act of devotion or giving.
    • āpaye: Means "to the one who is invoked" or "to the one who is called upon," indicating a connection to divine or spiritual entities.
    • sakhā: Refers to "friend" or "companion," emphasizing the importance of relationships.
    • sakhye: This is a form of the word "sakhā," reinforcing the theme of friendship.
    • vareṇyaḥ: Means "worthy of worship" or "most excellent," indicating reverence towards the subject of the mantra.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the importance of familial bonds, particularly the relationship between parents and their children.
    • It emphasizes the act of offering and sacrifice, suggesting that true wealth comes from devotion and the relationships we cultivate.
    • The invocation of friends and companions highlights the significance of social connections and support systems in our lives.
    • Overall, the mantra reflects a holistic view of prosperity that encompasses not just material wealth but also spiritual and relational richness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, parents often make sacrifices for their children’s education and well-being, embodying the essence of "yajaty" (offering).
    • In friendships, individuals support each other through challenges, demonstrating the value of "sakhā" (friendship) in personal growth and resilience.
    • Communities often come together to celebrate festivals or rituals, reinforcing the idea of collective offering and shared prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of family, friendship, and community in achieving true prosperity.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize the value of relationships and the sacrifices made for one another.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger bonds, a supportive network, and a more fulfilling life.
