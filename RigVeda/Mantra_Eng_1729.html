सनेमि चक्रं अजरोऽयं विवावृत उत्तानायां दश युक्ता वहन्ति |सूर्यस्य चक्षू रजसैत्य आवृतं तस्मिन्न आरपिता भुवनानि विश्वा ||
sanemi cakram ajaraṁ vi vāvṛta uttānāyāṁ daśa yuktā vahanti | sūryasya cakṣū rajasaity āvṛtaṁ tasminn ārpitā bhuvanāni viśvā ||
  • Sanemi: This word can be interpreted as "we are united" or "we come together." It signifies the importance of unity and collaboration among individuals.
  • Cakram: Refers to a "wheel" or "circle," symbolizing the cyclical nature of life and the universe. It can also represent the concept of time and the continuous flow of existence.
  • Ajaraṁ: Meaning "immortal" or "eternal," this term emphasizes the idea of something that transcends time and does not decay or perish.
  • Vi vāvṛta: Translates to "that which is spread out" or "that which is revealed." It suggests the unveiling of knowledge or truth.
  • Uttānāyāṁ: This word means "in the upward direction" or "towards higher realms," indicating a pursuit of higher knowledge or spiritual elevation.
  • Daśa yuktā: Refers to "tenfold" or "ten connected," which can symbolize completeness or the integration of various aspects of life.
  • Vahanti: Meaning "they carry" or "they bear," this term implies the act of supporting or nurturing something significant.
  • Sūryasya: This word means "of the sun," representing light, energy, and life. The sun is often seen as a source of vitality and enlightenment.
  • Cakṣū: Translates to "eyes," symbolizing perception, awareness, and the ability to see beyond the physical realm.
  • Rajasaity: This term can be interpreted as "in the midst of activity" or "in the realm of action," highlighting the dynamic nature of existence.
  • Āvṛtaṁ: Meaning "enveloped" or "covered," it suggests the idea of being surrounded or protected by something greater.
  • Tasminn: Refers to "in that," indicating a connection to a specific state or condition.
  • Ārpitā: Meaning "offered" or "dedicated," this term emphasizes the act of giving oneself or one's efforts to a higher purpose.
  • Bhuvanāni: Translates to "worlds" or "realms," indicating the various dimensions of existence and experience.
  • Viśvā: Meaning "all" or "universal," this term signifies inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound themes of unity, immortality, and the pursuit of higher knowledge. It emphasizes the importance of collaboration and the cyclical nature of life, urging individuals to come together in their quest for enlightenment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community building, teamwork in professional settings, and spiritual practices that promote collective growth. By recognizing our interconnectedness and striving for higher ideals, we can create a more harmonious and enlightened society.
