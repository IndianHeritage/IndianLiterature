अनु द्रप्सास इन्दव आपो न प्रवतासरण |पुनाना इन्द्रं आशत ||
anu drapsāsa indava āpo na pravatāsaran | punānā indram āśata ||
  • anu drapsāsa: This phrase can be translated as "following the path" or "in accordance with the flow." It signifies the importance of aligning oneself with the natural order and the rhythms of life.
  • indava: This term refers to "the divine" or "the celestial." It often symbolizes higher powers or deities that guide and influence human existence.
  • āpo: This word means "waters." In the Vedic context, water is not just a physical element but also represents purity, nourishment, and the source of life.
  • na pravatāsaran: This phrase can be interpreted as "not to be obstructed" or "not to be hindered." It emphasizes the idea of overcoming obstacles and moving forward without impediments.
  • punānā: This term means "purifying" or "cleansing." It suggests a process of renewal and transformation, both physically and spiritually.
  • indram: This refers to "Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, who represents strength, power, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • āśata: This word means "to dwell" or "to reside." It indicates a sense of stability and permanence in the presence of divine blessings.

  • Real-Life Example: In a practical sense, this mantra can be applied to personal growth and development. For instance, when facing challenges in life, one can reflect on the idea of following the natural flow (anu drapsāsa) and seeking guidance from higher powers (indava) to navigate through difficulties. Just as water (āpo) flows around obstacles, individuals can learn to adapt and find their way through life's challenges without being hindered (na pravatāsaran).
  • Significance: The mantra emphasizes the importance of aligning oneself with the divine and the natural order. It serves as a reminder that purification (punānā) and strength (indram) are essential for overcoming life's obstacles and achieving stability (āśata). This can be particularly relevant in times of stress or uncertainty, where individuals can seek inner peace and clarity by connecting with their spiritual beliefs.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound philosophy of life that encourages individuals to embrace the flow of existence, seek divine guidance, and strive for purification and strength. Its application in real life can lead to a more harmonious and fulfilling existence, as one learns to navigate challenges with resilience and grace.
