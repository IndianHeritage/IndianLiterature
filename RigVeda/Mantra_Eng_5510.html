मन्त्र: ररे हव्यम् मतीभिर्यज्ञियानां नक्षत् कामम् मार्त्यानाम् असिन्वन् |धाता रयिम् अविदस्यं सदासां साक्षीमहि युज्येभिर् नु देवैः ||
rare havyam matibhir yajñiyānāṁ nakṣat kāmam martyānām asinvan | dhātā rayim avidasyaṁ sadāsāṁ sakṣīmahi yujyebhir nu devaiḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May we, through our intellect, obtain the wealth of the yajñis (those who perform sacrifices), and may we be witnesses to the divine blessings that come from the gods, as we strive for the desires of mortals."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • rare havyam: "May we obtain" - a call for acquiring something valuable.
    • matibhir: "through our intellect" - emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding.
    • yajñiyānāṁ: "of the yajñis" - refers to those who perform sacred rituals or sacrifices.
    • nakṣat kāmam: "desires of mortals" - highlights human aspirations and needs.
    • martyānām: "of mortals" - signifies the human condition.
    • asinvan: "may we be witnesses" - a plea for divine observation and support.
    • dhātā: "the giver" - refers to the divine source of blessings.
    • rayim: "wealth" - can be interpreted as both material and spiritual abundance.
    • avidyasyaṁ: "of the ignorant" - a recognition of the limitations of human understanding.
    • sadāsāṁ: "always" - indicates a continuous state of being.
    • sakṣīmahi: "may we witness" - a reiteration of the desire for divine presence.
    • yujyebhir: "through the connections" - suggests the importance of relationships and community.
    • nu devaiḥ: "with the gods" - emphasizes the divine aspect of the blessings sought.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied when teams come together to achieve a common goal, emphasizing the importance of collaboration and shared wisdom to attain success.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders, reflecting the desire to gain knowledge and wealth through wise counsel.
    • During community service, people often come together to support those in need, embodying the spirit of the yajñis by performing acts of kindness and generosity.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for wisdom, community, and divine support in the pursuit of human desires. It teaches us that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses knowledge, relationships, and spiritual fulfillment. In real life, this mantra encourages individuals and communities to work together, seek wisdom, and remain connected to the divine, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
