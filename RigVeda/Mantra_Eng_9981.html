मन्त्र: ककर्दवे वृषभो युक्त आसीद अवावचीत सारथिर अस्य केशी |दुदेर युक्तस्य द्रवतः सहानस ऋच्छन्ति स्मा निष्पदो मुद्गलानीम् ||
kakardave vṛṣabho yukta āsīd avāvacīt sārathir asya keśī | dudher yuktasya dravataḥ sahānasa ṛcchanti ṣmā niṣpado mudgalānīm ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kakardave: Refers to a divine or celestial being, often associated with the sound of a conch or a call.
    • vṛṣabho: Means 'the bull', symbolizing strength, power, and fertility.
    • yukta: Signifies being united or connected.
    • āsīd: Translates to 'was' or 'existed'.
    • avāvacīt: Refers to the act of being protected or shielded.
    • sārathir: Means 'charioteer', symbolizing guidance and control.
    • asya: Means 'of this' or 'of him'.
    • keśī: Refers to 'the one with long hair', often symbolizing a divine or powerful figure.
    • dudher: Means 'of the one who churns', indicating action and effort.
    • yuktasya: Means 'of the one who is united or connected'.
    • dravataḥ: Refers to 'of the flowing' or 'of the liquid'.
    • sahānasa: Means 'the one who is together' or 'the one who is united'.
    • ṛcchanti: Means 'they take' or 'they grasp'.
    • ṣmā: Refers to 'the one who is auspicious' or 'the one who brings good fortune'.
    • niṣpado: Means 'without roots' or 'detached'.
    • mudgalānīm: Refers to 'the one who is like a mudgal (a type of drum)', symbolizing rhythm and harmony.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine being, often interpreted as a representation of strength and guidance.
    • The imagery of a charioteer suggests the importance of having a guide in life, someone who can steer us through challenges.
    • The mention of 'the one who churns' can be seen as a metaphor for effort and the process of transformation, akin to how churning leads to the creation of butter from milk.
    • In real life, this can be related to the importance of teamwork and collaboration, where individuals come together to achieve a common goal.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, strength, and guidance in our lives.
    • In a practical sense, it can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in a workplace, where collaboration leads to success.
    • It also highlights the need for a mentor or guide in personal and professional growth, reminding us that we do not have to navigate life's challenges alone.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to seek strength in unity and to value the guidance of those who lead us.
