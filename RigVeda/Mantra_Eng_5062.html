इयम् मद वाम प्र स्तृणीत मनिषोप प्रिया नमसā बर्हिर अच्छ |यन्तं नो मित्रावरुणाव अधृष्टं छर्दिर यद वां वरूथ्यं सुदानु ||
iyam mad vām pra stṛṇīte manīṣopa priyā namasā barhir accha | yantaṁ no mitrāvaruṇāv adhṛṣṭaṁ chardir yad vāṁ varūthyaṁ sudānū ||
  • iyam mad vām pra stṛṇīte: This phrase can be translated as "This (offering) is for you, may it be spread out." It signifies the act of offering and sharing, which is a fundamental aspect of many cultures, where sharing food or resources is seen as a way to foster community and goodwill.
  • manīṣopa priyā namasā: This translates to "with the intention of love and respect." It emphasizes the importance of approaching others with kindness and reverence, which can be seen in real-life scenarios such as greeting someone with a warm smile or showing appreciation for their efforts.
  • barhir accha: This means "may it be well." It reflects a wish for prosperity and well-being, akin to the common practice of wishing someone good luck or success in their endeavors, such as before an important exam or job interview.
  • yantaṁ no mitrāvaruṇāv: This phrase translates to "may the friends Mitra and Varuna guide us." Mitra and Varuna are deities representing friendship and cosmic order, respectively. In real life, this can be likened to seeking guidance from mentors or friends during challenging times, emphasizing the value of support systems.
  • adhṛṣṭaṁ chardir: This means "the unseen power." It suggests that there are forces beyond our perception that influence our lives. In practical terms, this can relate to the idea of fate or destiny, where individuals may feel that certain events are beyond their control, such as unexpected opportunities or challenges that arise.
  • yad vāṁ varūthyaṁ sudānū: This translates to "whatever is beneficial for you." It highlights the importance of seeking what is truly good for oneself and others, similar to the concept of making choices that lead to personal growth and well-being, such as pursuing a fulfilling career or nurturing healthy relationships.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of offering, respect, guidance, and the pursuit of well-being. Its significance lies in the reminder to approach life with a spirit of generosity and kindness, to seek support from others, and to recognize the unseen forces that shape our experiences. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, personal relationships, and professional environments, fostering a sense of interconnectedness and mutual support.
