आद इन्द्रः सत्रा तविषीर अपत्यत वरियो द्यावापृथिवी अबाधत |अवाभरद धृतितो वज्रम आयसं शेवम् मित्राय वरुणाय दाशुषे ||
ād indraḥ satrā taviṣīr apatyata varīyo dyāvāpṛthivī abādhata | avābharad dhṛṣito vajram āyasaṁ śevam mitrāya varuṇāya dāśuṣe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ād: In the beginning or first.
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods, associated with rain and thunderstorms.
    • satrā: Sacrifices or offerings.
    • taviṣīr: Powerful or strong.
    • apatyata: To be born or to arise.
    • varīyo: The best or most excellent.
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: Heaven and Earth.
    • abādhata: To be untroubled or free from obstacles.
    • avābharad: He (Indra) took or seized.
    • dhṛṣito: With courage or determination.
    • vajram: Thunderbolt or weapon of Indra.
    • āyasaṁ: Iron or metal.
    • śevam: The best or supreme.
    • mitrāya: To Mitra, the god of friendship and contracts.
    • varuṇāya: To Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water.
    • dāśuṣe: For the servants or devotees.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra begins with a call to Indra, emphasizing the importance of his role in the universe.
    • It highlights the significance of sacrifices and offerings, suggesting that they are a means to invoke divine blessings.
    • The phrase "dyāvāpṛthivī abādhata" indicates a harmonious relationship between heaven and earth, symbolizing balance in nature.
    • Indra's act of seizing the thunderbolt represents the power to overcome challenges and obstacles in life.
    • The mention of Mitra and Varuna signifies the importance of friendship, cooperation, and maintaining cosmic order.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of community and collaboration, much like how teams work together to achieve common goals.
    • The concept of making sacrifices for the greater good can be related to charitable acts or community service, where individuals contribute to the welfare of others.
    • The balance between heaven and earth can be interpreted as the need for environmental sustainability, where human actions should not disrupt the natural order.
    • Indra's thunderbolt can symbolize the strength and courage needed to face life's challenges, encouraging individuals to be resilient in the face of adversity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine support, the importance of community, and the need for balance in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that through determination, cooperation, and respect for the natural order, individuals can overcome obstacles and achieve their goals.
    • In a world filled with challenges, the teachings of this mantra can inspire individuals to act with courage, foster friendships, and contribute positively to society.
