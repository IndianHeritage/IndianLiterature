ऋतावानं महिषं विश्वदर्शतम अग्निं सुम्नाय दधिरे पुरो जनाः |श्रुतकर्णं सप्रथस्तमं त्वा गिरा दैव्यम मानवश युगाः ||
ṛtāvānam mahiṣaṁ viśvadarśatam agniṁ sumnāya dadhire puro janāḥ | śrutkarṇaṁ saprathastamaṁ tvā girā daivyam mānuṣā yugā ||
  • ṛtāvānam: This term refers to the one who embodies truth and righteousness. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who uphold ethical standards and act with integrity, such as a judge or a community leader.
  • mahiṣaṁ: This word means 'the great one' or 'the powerful.' It can be related to leaders or influential figures in society who have the ability to inspire and guide others, like a CEO or a teacher.
  • viśvadarśatam: This term signifies 'all-seeing' or 'universal vision.' It can be exemplified by someone who has a broad perspective on life, such as a philosopher or a visionary entrepreneur who sees opportunities where others do not.
  • agniṁ: Agni represents fire, which is a symbol of energy, transformation, and purification. In everyday life, this can be related to the passion and drive one has towards their goals, akin to an artist or a scientist dedicated to their craft.
  • sumnāya: This word means 'for auspiciousness' or 'for good fortune.' It reflects the idea of seeking positivity and success in endeavors, similar to how people set intentions during rituals or prayers for a prosperous outcome.
  • dadhire: This term means 'they have placed' or 'they have established.' It can be seen in the context of building relationships or communities, where individuals come together to create a supportive environment.
  • puro janāḥ: This phrase translates to 'the people of the front' or 'the leaders.' It can refer to those who take initiative in society, like activists or community organizers who lead movements for change.
  • śrutkarṇaṁ: This means 'having ears to hear' or 'attentive.' It emphasizes the importance of listening and understanding, akin to a counselor or a friend who provides support by truly hearing others' concerns.
  • saprathastamaṁ: This term signifies 'with great strength' or 'powerful.' It can be related to individuals who exhibit resilience in the face of challenges, such as athletes or survivors of adversity.
  • tvā girā: This means 'with words' or 'through speech.' It highlights the power of communication, similar to how leaders inspire through speeches or how writers influence society through their words.
  • daivyam: This term refers to 'divine' or 'heavenly.' It can be seen in the context of seeking higher guidance or inspiration, like artists who draw from a sense of the sublime in their work.
  • mānuṣā: This means 'human' or 'mankind.' It emphasizes our shared humanity and the importance of compassion and empathy in our interactions with others.
  • yugā: This term refers to 'ages' or 'epochs.' It signifies the cyclical nature of time and the evolution of human experience, much like how societies progress through different phases of development.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of truth, leadership, and the divine connection in human endeavors. It emphasizes the importance of integrity, vision, and community in achieving auspicious outcomes. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership roles, community building, and personal development. By embodying the principles of righteousness, attentiveness, and strength, individuals can contribute positively to society and foster a sense of unity and purpose. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for individuals to rise above challenges, listen to one another, and work collectively towards a brighter future.
