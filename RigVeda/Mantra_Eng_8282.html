आ कलशेषु धावति श्येनो वर्म विगाहते |अभि द्रोणा कनिक्रदत ||
ā kalaśeṣu dhāvati śyeno varma vi gāhate | abhi droṇā kanikradat ||
  • Mantra: "ā kalaśeṣu dhāvati śyeno varma vi gāhate | abhi droṇā kanikradat ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be interpreted as: "The eagle (śyena) swiftly moves in the sky, seeking the protection (varma) of the pot (kalaśa) and the one who is in the midst of the battle (droṇā) is like a warrior who is alert and ready."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā: A prefix indicating direction or movement.
    • kalaśeṣu: Refers to pots or vessels, symbolizing abundance and protection.
    • dhāvati: Means to run or move swiftly, indicating agility and readiness.
    • śyeno: The eagle, a symbol of strength, vision, and freedom.
    • varma: Protection or armor, representing safety and security.
    • vi: A prefix that can denote separation or distinction.
    • gāhate: To grasp or hold, indicating the act of taking or securing.
    • abhi: Towards or in the direction of, suggesting focus or intent.
    • droṇā: Refers to a battlefield or a place of conflict, symbolizing challenges.
    • kanikradat: To be alert or vigilant, indicating readiness to respond.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, the eagle represents a leader who is vigilant and ready to seize opportunities while ensuring the protection of their team (kalaśa) from market challenges (droṇā).
    • In personal life, an individual preparing for an important exam can embody the qualities of the eagle, moving swiftly through their studies while seeking the protection of knowledge (varma) to succeed.
    • In sports, a team that plays with the agility of an eagle, ready to defend their goal (varma) while attacking the opponent (droṇā), exemplifies the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on agility, protection, and readiness in the face of challenges. It teaches us the importance of being alert and prepared, much like the eagle that soars high while keeping a watchful eye on its surroundings. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership, personal development, and competitive environments, reminding us to be proactive and protective of our goals and values.
