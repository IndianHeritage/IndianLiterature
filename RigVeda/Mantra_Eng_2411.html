शुक्रस्याद्य गवाशिर इन्द्रवायू नियुत्वतः |आ यातं पिबतं नराः ||
śukrasyādya gavāśira indravāyū niyutvataḥ | ā yātam pibataṁ narā ||
  • śukrasyādya: This term can be broken down into two parts: "śukra," which means bright or pure, and "syādya," which implies the state of being or existence. Together, it suggests a state of purity or brightness.
  • gavāśira: This word translates to "the head of the cows" or "the leader of the herd." In Vedic literature, cows are often symbolic of wealth, nourishment, and prosperity.
  • indravāyū: This term combines "Indra," the king of gods and god of rain and thunderstorms, with "vāyū," which means wind. It signifies the powerful forces of nature that Indra commands.
  • niyutvataḥ: This word refers to the act of being directed or guided. It implies a sense of control or influence over something.
  • ā yātam: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we go" or "let us proceed." It indicates a desire for movement or progress.
  • pibataṁ: This term means "to drink" or "to consume." It suggests the act of taking in or absorbing something beneficial.
  • narā: This word translates to "men" or "humans." It refers to the human experience and the collective journey of mankind.

  • Meaning and Interpretation: The mantra can be understood as a call for purity and prosperity, invoking the powerful forces of nature to guide and nourish humanity. It emphasizes the importance of seeking brightness and clarity in life while being open to the blessings of nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain and favorable weather conditions, similar to invoking Indra's blessings for a good harvest.
    • In personal development, individuals may seek clarity and purity in their thoughts and actions, striving to lead a life that is beneficial to themselves and others.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for purity, guidance, and nourishment from the divine forces of nature. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as seeking blessings for prosperity, clarity in decision-making, and the importance of community and collective well-being. By invoking these principles, individuals can strive for a harmonious and fulfilling life, aligning themselves with the natural order and the divine. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking higher wisdom and guidance in our daily lives.
