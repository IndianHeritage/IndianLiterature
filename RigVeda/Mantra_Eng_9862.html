मन्त्र: एते वदन्त्य अविदन्न अनामधु न्यूनखयन्ते अधि पक्व आमिषि |वृक्षस्य शाखाम अरुणस्य बाप्सतस ते सुभर्वा वृषभाः प्रेम अराविषुः ||
ete vadanty avidann anā madhu ny ūṅkhayante adhi pakva āmiṣi | vṛkṣasya śākhām aruṇasya bapsatas te sūbharvā vṛṣabhāḥ prem arāviṣuḥ ||
  • Translation: "These (beings) speak, not knowing, and they do not taste the honey; they are drawn towards the ripened fruit of the tree, the branches of the red (tree). O you who are strong, may you bestow your love upon us."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ete vadanty: "These (beings) speak" - Refers to entities or beings that express thoughts or ideas.
    • avidann: "not knowing" - Indicates ignorance or lack of awareness.
    • anā madhu: "do not taste the honey" - Symbolizes missing out on the sweetness or rewards of life.
    • ny ūṅkhayante: "they are drawn towards" - Suggests attraction or desire.
    • adhi pakva āmiṣi: "the ripened fruit" - Represents maturity, fulfillment, or the rewards of hard work.
    • vṛkṣasya śākhām: "the branches of the tree" - Symbolizes growth, stability, and support.
    • aruṇasya: "the red (tree)" - Could symbolize vitality, energy, or passion.
    • bapsatas: "of the many" - Indicates abundance or plurality.
    • te sūbharvā: "O you who are strong" - A call to powerful beings or forces.
    • vṛṣabhāḥ: "may you bestow" - A request for blessings or support.
    • prem arāviṣuḥ: "your love upon us" - A plea for affection, care, or guidance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, employees may work hard (the tree) but may not always recognize the rewards (the honey) of their efforts. This mantra reminds them to seek out and appreciate the fruits of their labor.
    • In personal relationships, individuals may express feelings without fully understanding their implications. The mantra encourages awareness and deeper connections, urging individuals to seek love and support from those around them.
    • In education, students may be drawn to knowledge (the ripened fruit) but may not fully grasp its importance (the honey). This highlights the need for guidance and mentorship to help them appreciate their learning journey.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of awareness and appreciation in various aspects of life. It serves as a reminder to recognize the sweetness of experiences and the support available from others. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, professional environments, or educational pursuits, this mantra encourages individuals to seek understanding, connection, and the blessings of love and support. By doing so, one can cultivate a more fulfilling and enriched life.
