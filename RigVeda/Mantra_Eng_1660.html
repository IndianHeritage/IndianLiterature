ते मायिनो ममिरे सुप्रचेतसो जामी सायोनी मिथुनā समोकसā |नव्यं-नव्यं तन्तुम ā तन्त्वते दिवि समुद्रे अन्तः कवयः सुदीतयः ||
te māyino mamire supracetaso jāmī sayonī mithunā samokasā | navyaṁ-navyaṁ tantum ā tanvate divi samudre antaḥ kavayaḥ sudītayaḥ ||
  • te māyino: This phrase can be interpreted as "those who are skilled in illusion" or "those who create illusions." In real life, this can refer to individuals or forces that manipulate perceptions, such as media or advertising that shape our understanding of reality.
  • mamire supracetaso: This translates to "they have taken possession of the wise." It suggests that wisdom is a valuable asset that can be claimed or possessed. In a practical sense, this can relate to how knowledge and wisdom are often sought after in various fields, such as education or leadership.
  • jāmī sayonī: This can be understood as "the mother of the earth" or "the nurturing source." It symbolizes the importance of nature and the earth in sustaining life. For example, environmental conservation efforts highlight the need to protect our planet as it provides resources and sustenance.
  • mithunā samokasā: This phrase refers to "the duality or pairs that are united." It can represent relationships, partnerships, or the balance between opposing forces. In real life, this can be seen in various partnerships, such as marriage or teamwork, where collaboration leads to greater outcomes.
  • navyaṁ-navyaṁ tantum ā tanvate: This translates to "they weave new and new threads." It signifies creativity and innovation, suggesting that new ideas and solutions are constantly being developed. In today's world, this can be observed in technology and art, where continuous innovation drives progress.
  • divi samudre antaḥ: This means "within the ocean of the heavens." It evokes a sense of vastness and the infinite possibilities that exist in the universe. In a real-life context, this can relate to exploration, whether it be scientific, spiritual, or personal growth, as we seek to understand our place in the cosmos.
  • kavayaḥ sudītayaḥ: This translates to "the wise poets or seers." It emphasizes the role of wisdom and insight in guiding humanity. In practical terms, this can refer to leaders, philosophers, or artists who inspire and influence society through their vision and creativity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of wisdom, creativity, duality, and the nurturing aspects of nature. Its significance lies in the reminder that we are part of a larger tapestry of existence, where our actions and relationships shape our reality. In real life, this mantra encourages us to seek wisdom, embrace innovation, and recognize the interconnectedness of all beings. It serves as a guiding principle for personal growth, environmental stewardship, and the pursuit of knowledge, urging us to weave our unique threads into the fabric of life.
