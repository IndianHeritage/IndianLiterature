आ प्यायस्व मदिन्तम सोम विश्वेभिर अंशुभिः | भव नः सुश्रवस्तमः सखा वृधे ||
ā pyāyasva madintama soma viśvebhir aṁśubhiḥ | bhavā naḥ suśravastamaḥ sakhā vṛdhe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: From
    • pyāyasva: May you be purified
    • madintama: O best of the intoxicating (Soma)
    • soma: Soma (a sacred drink)
    • viśvebhir: With all
    • aṁśubhiḥ: With rays (or light)
    • bhavā: Become
    • naḥ: Our
    • suśravastamaḥ: Most renowned
    • sakhā: Friend
    • vṛdhe: In growth
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to the divine essence of Soma, asking for purification and the bestowal of blessings. It emphasizes the importance of friendship and growth, invoking the light and energy that comes from the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Purification: Just as one cleanses their body through physical hygiene, this mantra symbolizes the need for mental and spiritual purification. For instance, practicing mindfulness or meditation can help clear the mind of negativity.
    • Friendship: The mention of "sakhā" (friend) highlights the importance of relationships. In real life, having supportive friends can lead to personal growth and happiness, much like how the mantra seeks the divine as a friend in times of need.
    • Growth: The concept of "vṛdhe" (growth) can be seen in various aspects of life, such as career development or personal goals. Just as plants need sunlight and water to grow, individuals need support and positive energy, akin to the rays mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in achieving purity, fostering friendships, and promoting growth. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek spiritual clarity, nurture relationships, and strive for personal development. By embodying these principles, individuals can lead a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
