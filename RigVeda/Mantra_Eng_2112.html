उग्रेष्व इनु शूर मन्दसासनस त्रिकद्रुकेषु पाहि सोमं इन्द्र | प्रदोधुवच छमश्रुषु प्रीणानो याहि हरिभ्यां सुतस्य पीतिम ||
ugreṣv in nu śūra mandasānas trikadrukeṣu pāhi somam indra | pradodhuvac chmaśruṣu prīṇāno yāhi haribhyāṁ sutasya pītim ||
  • ugreṣv in nu śūra mandasānas trikadrukeṣu pāhi somam indra
    • ugreṣv: This term can be interpreted as "in the fierce" or "in the powerful." It signifies strength and intensity.
    • in nu: This phrase can be understood as "indeed" or "truly," emphasizing the certainty of the statement.
    • śūra: This word means "brave" or "heroic," referring to those who exhibit courage and valor.
    • mandasānas: This term translates to "those who are seated gently" or "calmly," indicating a sense of peace amidst strength.
    • trikadrukeṣu: This can be interpreted as "in the three realms" or "in the three worlds," suggesting a universal presence.
    • pāhi: This means "protect" or "guard," indicating a plea for safeguarding.
    • somam: Refers to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing nourishment and vitality.
    • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, representing power, strength, and protection.
  • pradodhuvac chmaśruṣu prīṇāno yāhi haribhyāṁ sutasya pītim
    • pradodhuvac: This term can be understood as "having spoken" or "having declared," indicating a communication or invocation.
    • chmaśruṣu: This word refers to "in the beard" or "in the hair," possibly symbolizing wisdom or age.
    • prīṇāno: This means "pleasing" or "satisfying," suggesting a sense of joy or fulfillment.
    • yāhi: This translates to "come" or "approach," indicating a call to action or invitation.
    • haribhyāṁ: This term refers to "the sons of Hari," which can symbolize divine beings or followers of the divine.
    • sutasya: This means "of the son" or "of the offspring," indicating lineage or heritage.
    • pītim: This word translates to "nourishment" or "satisfaction," emphasizing the importance of sustenance.

Conclusion:

The mantra invokes the powerful deity Indra, asking for protection and nourishment. It highlights the balance between strength and calmness, suggesting that true power lies in the ability to remain composed while being courageous. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as facing challenges with bravery while maintaining inner peace. It serves as a reminder to seek divine protection and nourishment in our endeavors, whether in personal struggles, professional challenges, or spiritual journeys. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for a harmonious blend of strength and serenity, encouraging individuals to approach life's challenges with both valor and tranquility.
