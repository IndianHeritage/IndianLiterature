आ निवर्तना वर्तय न निवर्तना वर्तय |भूम्याश्चतस्रः प्रदिशस्ताभ्य एनानि वर्तय ||
ā nivartana vartaya ni nivartana vartaya | bhūmyāś catasraḥ pradiśas tābhya enā ni vartaya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: away, from
    • nivartana: turning back, returning
    • vartaya: make happen, cause to occur
    • ni: not
    • bhūmyāś: of the earth
    • catasraḥ: four
    • pradiśas: directions, quarters
    • tābhyaḥ: from them
    • enā: these
    • ni vartaya: do not turn back
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks about the concept of direction and movement, urging not to turn back or retreat.
    • It emphasizes the importance of moving forward and embracing the journey ahead, regardless of the challenges that may arise.
    • The mention of "four directions" symbolizes the completeness of the journey, encompassing all aspects of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, this mantra can be applied when one faces obstacles in achieving their goals. For instance, a student preparing for exams may feel overwhelmed but must continue to push forward without retreating to old habits.
    • In a professional context, a business facing challenges in the market must adapt and innovate rather than revert to outdated strategies. This mantra encourages resilience and forward-thinking.
    • In relationships, when conflicts arise, it is essential to communicate and resolve issues rather than retreating into silence or avoidance. The mantra serves as a reminder to confront challenges head-on.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for perseverance and courage in the face of adversity.
    • It teaches us that life is a journey that requires us to face challenges without turning back, embracing growth and learning along the way.
    • In various aspects of life—be it personal, professional, or relational—this mantra serves as a guiding principle to remain steadfast and committed to our paths.
