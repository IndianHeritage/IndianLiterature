इमा गावः सरमे या ऐच्छः परि दिवो अन्तान सुभगे पतन्ती |कस त एनाऽव सृजाद आयुध्व्य उतास्माकं आयुधा सन्ति तिग्मा ||
imā gāvaḥ sarame yā aicchaḥ pari divo antān subhage patantī | kas ta enā ava sṛjād ayudhvy utāsmākam āyudhā santi tigmā ||
  • imā gāvaḥ: This phrase translates to "these cows" or "these beings." In the Vedic context, cows symbolize wealth, nourishment, and prosperity. They are often associated with the sustenance of life and the agricultural economy.
  • sarame yā aicchaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the dogs that desire." Dogs in Vedic literature often represent loyalty and guardianship. They are seen as protectors of the household and are associated with vigilance.
  • pari divo antān: This means "around the heavens." It signifies the vastness of the universe and the divine presence that encompasses all. It can also imply the connection between the earthly and the celestial realms.
  • subhage patantī: Translated as "falling in auspiciousness," this phrase suggests a favorable outcome or blessings that descend upon the devotees or the community. It indicates the arrival of good fortune and prosperity.
  • kas ta enā ava sṛjād: This translates to "who can create them?" It poses a rhetorical question about the power of creation and the divine forces at play in the universe. It emphasizes the mystery of existence and the role of the divine in manifesting life.
  • ayudhvy utāsmākam: This means "we have weapons." In a metaphorical sense, "weapons" can refer to the tools, skills, or resources that individuals or communities possess to face challenges and adversities in life.
  • āyudhā santi tigmā: This translates to "sharp weapons are present." It suggests that one must be prepared and equipped to deal with life's challenges. The sharpness of the weapons symbolizes readiness and the ability to confront difficulties effectively.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of prosperity, protection, divine presence, and preparedness. In real life, it serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing our resources (like the cows), being vigilant and loyal (like the dogs), and recognizing the divine forces that guide us. It encourages individuals to be equipped with the necessary skills and tools to navigate life's challenges. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as in personal development, community building, and spiritual growth, emphasizing the balance between material wealth and spiritual well-being.
