प्र वीरयाऽशुचयो दद्रिरे वाम अध्वर्युभिर मधुमन्तः सुतासः | वह वायो नियुतो याह्य अच्छा पिबा सुतस्यांधसो मदाय ||
pra vīrayā śucayo dadrire vām adhvaryubhir madhumantaḥ sutāsaḥ | vaha vāyo niyuto yāhy acchā pibā sutasyāndhaso madāya ||
  • pra vīrayā: This phrase can be translated as "for the brave" or "for the valiant." It emphasizes the importance of courage and valor in life.
  • śucayo: This word means "pure" or "sacred." It signifies the need for purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions.
  • dadrire: This term translates to "they have come" or "they have arrived." It indicates the arrival of something significant, often associated with blessings or positive outcomes.
  • vām adhvaryubhir: This phrase refers to "with your offerings" or "through your rituals." It highlights the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine.
  • madhumantaḥ: This means "sweet" or "pleasant." It can refer to the sweetness of life, joy, or the rewards of hard work.
  • sutāsaḥ: This translates to "the sons" or "the offspring." It signifies the importance of family, lineage, and the continuation of values through generations.
  • vaha vāyo: This means "carry, O Wind." It invokes the wind as a carrier of blessings and messages, symbolizing the unseen forces that aid us in our endeavors.
  • niyuto: This term means "guided" or "directed." It emphasizes the need for guidance in our actions and decisions.
  • yāhy acchā: This phrase translates to "may you come" or "may you arrive." It expresses a desire for the arrival of good fortune or blessings.
  • pibā sutasyāndhaso: This means "drink the sweet nectar." It symbolizes the enjoyment of life's pleasures and the rewards of one's efforts.
  • madāya: This translates to "for intoxication" or "for joy." It signifies the bliss and joy that come from achieving one's goals and living a fulfilling life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of courage, purity, and the importance of rituals in life. It highlights the blessings that come from hard work and the joy of family and lineage. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to motivate individuals to pursue their goals with bravery and integrity, while also cherishing the relationships and values that shape their lives. It serves as a reminder to seek guidance and to appreciate the sweetness of life, encouraging a balanced approach to both personal and communal well-being.
