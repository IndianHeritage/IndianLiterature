इथा धिवन्तम अद्रिवः काण्वम् मेध्यातिथिम् |मेषो भूतोऽभि यन्न अयः ||
itthā dhīvantam adrivaḥ kāṇvam medhyātithim | meṣo bhūto3 'bhi yann ayaḥ ||
  • itthā: This word translates to "thus" or "in this manner." It indicates a way of being or a state of existence.
  • dhīvantam: This term refers to someone who is wise or possesses intelligence. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in decision-making.
  • adrivaḥ: This means "without obstacles" or "unhindered." It suggests a state of clarity and freedom from distractions.
  • kāṇvam: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is perceptive" or "the one who sees clearly." It highlights the value of perception and insight.
  • medhyātithim: This term refers to a "noble guest" or "honorable visitor." It signifies the importance of hospitality and respect for others.
  • meṣo: This word means "sheep" or "lamb," often symbolizing innocence and purity. It can also represent community and togetherness.
  • bhūto3: This term translates to "become" or "to be." It indicates a state of existence or transformation.
  • 'bhi: This is a particle that can mean "with" or "by." It connects the previous concepts to the following action.
  • yann: This word means "what" or "that which." It introduces a condition or a quality.
  • ayaḥ: This term translates to "strength" or "power." It emphasizes the importance of strength in achieving goals.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to embody wisdom and clarity, to perceive the world without obstacles, and to treat others with respect and hospitality. It suggests that by cultivating these qualities, one can achieve strength and transformation in life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Development: By striving to be wise and perceptive, individuals can make better decisions in their personal and professional lives.
  • Conflict Resolution: Approaching conflicts with clarity and respect for others can lead to more harmonious relationships.
  • Community Building: Emphasizing hospitality and togetherness can strengthen community bonds and foster a sense of belonging.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of wisdom, perception, and respect in our interactions with the world and others. By embodying these qualities, we can navigate life's challenges more effectively and contribute positively to our communities.
