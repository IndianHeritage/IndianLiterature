तदिन् nv अस्य वृषभस्य धेनोर ā नामभिर ममिरे सक्म्यं गोः |अन्यद्-अन्यद् असुर्यं वसाना नि मायिनो ममिरे रूपम् अस्मिन् ||
tad in nv asya vṛṣabhasya dhenor ā nāmabhir mamire sakmyaṁ goḥ | anyad-anyad asurya1ṁ vasānā ni māyino mamire rūpam asmin ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • in: in
    • nv: indeed
    • asya: of this
    • vṛṣabhasya: of the bull (symbolizing strength and power)
    • dhenor: of the cow (symbolizing nourishment and abundance)
    • ā: from
    • nāmabhir: by names
    • mamire: they have come
    • sakmyaṁ: capable
    • goḥ: of the cows
    • anyad-anyad: different, various
    • asuryaṁ: non-solar, dark
    • vasānā: dwelling
    • ni: not
    • māyino: of the illusionist
    • mamire: they have come
    • rūpam: form
    • asmin: in this
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the essence of strength and nourishment, represented by the bull and the cow.
    • The phrase "tad in nv asya vṛṣabhasya dhenor" emphasizes the connection between power (bull) and sustenance (cow), suggesting that true strength comes from the ability to nurture and support life.
    • The mention of "nāmabhir mamire" indicates that these qualities are recognized and celebrated through names, which can be seen as a form of reverence or acknowledgment in society.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "anyad-anyad asuryaṁ vasānā ni māyino," suggests that there are various forms of existence that are not illuminated by the sun, hinting at the presence of illusions or falsehoods in life.
    • This can be interpreted as a reminder to seek the truth and clarity in a world filled with distractions and illusions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the relationship between cattle (bulls and cows) and farming is crucial. Farmers rely on the strength of bulls for plowing and the nourishment provided by cows through milk and other products.
    • In personal development, the mantra can be applied to the idea that true strength is not just physical but also emotional and spiritual. Nurturing relationships and supporting others can lead to a more fulfilling life.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who empower their teams and foster a nurturing environment often see greater success than those who rely solely on authority and power.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of strength and nourishment, urging individuals to recognize the importance of both in their lives.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to seek truth and clarity amidst the illusions of life, encouraging a balanced approach to power and sustenance.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, agriculture, or leadership, the principles derived from this mantra can lead to a more harmonious and successful existence.
