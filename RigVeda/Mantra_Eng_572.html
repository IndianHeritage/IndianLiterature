वियासृजति समानं व्यर्थिनः पदं न वेत्योदती। वयो नकीष्ठे पप्तिवांस आसते व्युष्टौ वाजिनीवति॥
vi yā sṛjati samanaṁ vy a1rthinaḥ padaṁ na vety odatī | vayo nakiṣ ṭe paptivāṁsa āsate vyuṣṭau vājinīvati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vi: apart, separately
    • yā: who, which
    • sṛjati: creates, brings forth
    • samanaṁ: equality, sameness
    • vy: in, within
    • arthinaḥ: of the seekers, those who desire
    • padaṁ: path, way
    • na: not
    • vety: knows, understands
    • odatī: shines, illuminates
    • vayo: age, time
    • nakiṣ: not, no
    • ṭe: to you, for you
    • paptivāṁsa: those who are wise, knowledgeable
    • āsate: dwell, reside
    • vyuṣṭau: in the dawn, in the light
    • vājinīvati: in the strength of horses, powerful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the creation of a path that is equal and accessible to all seekers.
    • It emphasizes that true understanding and illumination come from wisdom and knowledge.
    • The reference to "dawn" symbolizes new beginnings and enlightenment, while "strength of horses" signifies power and capability.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, creating an inclusive environment where all employees feel valued and have equal opportunities reflects the essence of this mantra.
    • In education, providing equal access to resources and knowledge for all students embodies the idea of illuminating the path for every seeker.
    • In personal development, seeking wisdom and understanding in various aspects of life can lead to enlightenment and growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for equality, wisdom, and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as fostering inclusivity in communities, promoting equal opportunities in education and workplaces, and encouraging personal growth through the pursuit of wisdom.
    • Ultimately, it reminds us that the path to enlightenment is open to all who seek it, and that true strength comes from knowledge and understanding.
