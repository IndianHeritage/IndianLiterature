परि यत काव्या कविर नृम्णा वसानो अर्षति |स्वर वाजी सिषासति ||
pari yat kāvyā kavir nṛmṇā vasāno arṣati | svar vājī siṣāsati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pari: around, in all directions
    • yat: that which
    • kāvyā: poetic, related to poetry
    • kavir: the wise one, the poet or seer
    • nṛmṇā: among men, in the human realm
    • vasāno: dwelling, residing
    • arṣati: shines, illuminates
    • svar: sound, voice, or the celestial
    • vājī: the one who is powerful, strong
    • siṣāsati: directs, guides
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of poetry and wisdom.
    • The "kavir" or wise poet is depicted as someone who resides among humans and brings illumination through their words.
    • The phrase "svar vājī siṣāsati" suggests that the celestial sound or voice guides and empowers individuals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In literature, poets like Rumi or Tagore have used their words to inspire and guide people, much like the kavir in the mantra.
    • In modern contexts, motivational speakers and writers can be seen as contemporary "kavirs," using their eloquence to influence and uplift society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom and creativity in human life.
    • It highlights how poetry and powerful words can illuminate minds and guide actions.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various fields such as education, leadership, and personal development, where the right words can inspire change and foster understanding.
