अग्ना यो मार्त्यो दुवो धियं जुजोष धीतिभिः |भसन नु ष प्र पूर्व्य इषं वुरीतावसे ||
agnā yo martyo duvo dhiyaṁ jujoṣa dhītibhiḥ | bhasan nu ṣa pra pūrvya iṣaṁ vurītāvase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • agnā: O Agni (the fire god)
    • yo: who
    • martyo: mortal
    • duvo: two
    • dhiyaṁ: with intellect or understanding
    • jujoṣa: may you be nourished
    • dhītibhiḥ: with wisdom or thoughts
    • bhasan: shining or radiant
    • nu: indeed
    • ṣa: six
    • pra: before or in front of
    • pūrvya: the ancient or previous
    • iṣaṁ: desire or wish
    • vurītāvase: may you dwell or reside
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, asking for his blessings and nourishment. It emphasizes the importance of intellect and wisdom in human life, suggesting that with the right understanding, one can achieve their desires and live a radiant life. The mention of "two" and "six" may symbolize duality and completeness, respectively, indicating the balance needed in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, one might seek the guidance of a mentor (Agni) to illuminate their path (nourishment) and help them make wise decisions (intellect).
    • In personal relationships, understanding and wisdom can lead to harmonious interactions, fulfilling desires for love and companionship.
    • In education, students are encouraged to seek knowledge (Agni) and apply their understanding (dhiyaṁ) to achieve their academic goals.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance and the cultivation of wisdom. It teaches that by nurturing our intellect and understanding, we can achieve our desires and lead a fulfilling life. This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to professional success, reminding us of the importance of seeking knowledge and wisdom in all our endeavors.
