आजासः पूषणं रथे निश्चम्भास ते जनश्रियम् |देवं वहन्तु बिभ्रतः ||
ājāsaḥ pūṣaṇaṁ rathe niśṛmbhās te janaśriyam | devaṁ vahantu bibhrataḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ājāsaḥ: Refers to the one who is born or generated, often associated with the idea of creation or emergence.
    • pūṣaṇaṁ: This term is often translated as "the nourisher" or "the one who provides sustenance." In the context of the Vedas, it can refer to the Sun or a deity that brings life and nourishment.
    • rathe: Means "in the chariot" or "on the vehicle," symbolizing movement, progress, or the journey of life.
    • niśṛmbhās: This word can be interpreted as "to shine" or "to illuminate," suggesting enlightenment or clarity.
    • te: Means "your," indicating a connection or relationship with the divine or the higher power.
    • janaśriyam: Refers to "the glory of the people" or "the prosperity of the community," emphasizing the importance of collective well-being.
    • devaṁ: Translates to "the divine" or "the god," representing a higher power or spiritual essence.
    • vahantu: Means "may they carry" or "may they bring," indicating a desire for the divine to bestow blessings.
    • bibhrataḥ: Refers to "holding" or "bearing," suggesting the act of maintaining or sustaining something.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine essence that nourishes and sustains life, emphasizing the importance of community and collective prosperity.
    • It suggests that as we journey through life (symbolized by the chariot), we should seek enlightenment and clarity, guided by the divine.
    • The mantra highlights the interconnectedness of individuals within a community, urging us to work towards the common good and support one another.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied to initiatives that promote collective welfare, such as community gardens or cooperative businesses that nourish both the land and the people.
    • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their journey and seek guidance from higher principles or values that illuminate their path, much like the sun nourishes the earth.
    • In leadership, this mantra serves as a reminder for leaders to prioritize the well-being of their community, ensuring that their actions contribute to the prosperity and glory of the people they serve.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance and support in our collective journey through life.
    • It encourages us to seek nourishment not just for ourselves but for our communities, fostering a spirit of cooperation and shared prosperity.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire actions that promote sustainability, community welfare, and personal growth, reminding us of our interconnectedness and the importance of divine support in our endeavors.
