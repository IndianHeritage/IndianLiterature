सत्रा मदासस तव विश्वजन्याः सत्रा रायोऽध ये पार्थिवासः |सत्रा वाजानाम अभवो विभक्ताऽद देवेषु धारयथा असुर्यम् ||
satrā madāsas tava viśvajanyāḥ satrā rāyo 'dha ye pārthivāsaḥ | satrā vājānām abhavo vibhaktā yad deveṣu dhārayathā asuryam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • satrā: offerings or sacrifices
    • madāsas: intoxicated or filled with joy
    • tava: your
    • viśvajanyāḥ: universal or all-encompassing
    • rāyo: wealth or prosperity
    • 'dha: below or beneath
    • ye: those who
    • pārthivāsaḥ: earthly beings or mortals
    • vājānām: of the horses or strength
    • abhavo: may become or manifest
    • vibhaktā: divided or distributed
    • yad: that which
    • deveṣu: among the gods
    • dhārayathā: may you hold or sustain
    • asuryam: without the influence of the sun or darkness
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the offerings made to the divine, emphasizing the joy and abundance that these offerings can bring.
    • The phrase "satrā madāsas" suggests that the offerings are not just rituals but are filled with joy and celebration, akin to how people celebrate festivals with food and gifts.
    • It highlights the importance of wealth and prosperity ("rāyo") that can be attained through these offerings, which can be related to how communities come together to support each other during times of need.
    • The mention of "pārthivāsaḥ" indicates that these blessings are meant for all earthly beings, reminding us of the interconnectedness of humanity.
    • The idea of "vājānām abhavo vibhaktā" can be likened to the distribution of resources in society, where strength and prosperity are shared among individuals, similar to how food banks operate to help those in need.
    • Finally, the call to "dhārayathā asuryam" suggests a plea for divine support to sustain these blessings without the overshadowing influence of negativity or darkness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of community, joy, and the sharing of resources for the betterment of all.
    • In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as community service, charitable acts, and the celebration of cultural festivals where people come together to share their wealth and joy.
    • It serves as a reminder that true prosperity is not just about individual wealth but about the collective well-being of society.
    • By invoking the divine through offerings, we seek to create a harmonious environment where everyone can thrive, free from negativity.
