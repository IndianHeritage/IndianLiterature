ईशानां वारणां क्षयन्तीश चर्षणीनाम् |अपो याचामि भेषजम् ||
īśānā vāryāṇāṁ kṣayantīś carṣaṇīnām | apo yācāmi bheṣajam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • īśānā: Lord or Master
    • vāryāṇāṁ: of the powerful or strong
    • kṣayantīś: the one who diminishes or reduces
    • carṣaṇīnām: of the earth or the ground
    • apo: from the waters
    • yācāmi: I request or I seek
    • bheṣajam: medicine or healing
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra can be understood as a prayer to the divine forces that govern strength and power, asking for healing and restoration.
    • The phrase "īśānā vāryāṇāṁ" refers to the supreme power that controls the strong forces of nature, which can be likened to the forces of the universe that govern life and death.
    • "kṣayantīś carṣaṇīnām" suggests a recognition of the transient nature of life and the earth, emphasizing that even the strongest forces can diminish over time.
    • The invocation of "apo yācāmi bheṣajam" indicates a plea for healing from the waters, which can symbolize the life-giving and purifying properties of water.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis or illness, individuals often seek divine intervention or healing, much like the plea in this mantra.
    • Environmental concerns, such as climate change, reflect the diminishing power of nature, reminding us of our responsibility to care for the earth.
    • In healthcare, the search for effective treatments can be seen as a modern-day interpretation of seeking "bheṣajam" or medicine from the divine forces.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of recognizing the power of nature and the divine in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder of the impermanence of life and the need for healing, both physically and spiritually.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek balance, respect for nature, and the pursuit of healing in times of distress.
