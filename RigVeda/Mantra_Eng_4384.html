सजोषस् त्वा दिवो नरो यज्ञस्य केतुम इन्धते |यद ध स्य मानुषो जनः सुम्नायुर जुह्वे अध्वरे ||
sajoṣas tvā divo naro yajñasya ketum indhate | yad dha sya mānuṣo janaḥ sumnāyur juhve adhvare ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sajoṣas: With joy or happiness
    • tvā: You
    • divo: Of the heavens
    • naro: Man or human
    • yajñasya: Of the sacrifice or ritual
    • ketum: The banner or the goal
    • indhate: Establishes or sets up
    • yad: Whatever
    • dha: That
    • sya: Is
    • mānuṣo: Human
    • janaḥ: People or beings
    • sumnāyur: With auspicious life
    • juhve: Offer or invoke
    • adhvare: In the sacrifice or ritual
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the joy and purpose of human life in relation to divine rituals.
    • It emphasizes the importance of human participation in sacred activities, suggesting that through these actions, individuals can connect with the divine.
    • The phrase "sajoṣas tvā divo" indicates a joyful offering to the divine, highlighting the significance of positivity and happiness in spiritual practices.
    • The term "yajñasya ketum indhate" suggests that humans are responsible for establishing the goals of their spiritual endeavors, symbolized by the 'banner' of the sacrifice.
    • Furthermore, "sumnāyur juhve adhvare" implies that when people engage in these rituals with a pure heart, they invite auspiciousness and blessings into their lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be reflected in community service or charitable acts, where individuals come together to create a positive impact, akin to a collective ritual of goodwill.
    • Celebrations such as weddings or festivals can also embody this mantra, where joy and divine connection are sought through rituals that bring people together.
    • In personal development, setting goals and engaging in practices that promote well-being and happiness can be seen as a modern interpretation of establishing one's 'yajña' or sacred purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of joy, community, and purpose in human life.
    • It teaches that through collective rituals and positive actions, individuals can connect with the divine and invite auspiciousness into their lives.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages us to find meaning in our actions and to engage in practices that uplift ourselves and those around us.
