श्रुतं गायतं तकवानस्याहं चिद्धि रिरेभाश्विनां वाम् |आक्षी शुभस पती दन ||
śrutaṁ gāyatraṁ takavānasyāhaṁ cid dhi rirebhāśvinā vām | ākṣī śubhas patī dan ||
  • śrutaṁ: This word translates to "that which is heard" or "knowledge." It signifies the importance of listening and understanding wisdom that is passed down through generations.
  • gāyatraṁ: This refers to "the song" or "the hymn." It emphasizes the power of sound and music in conveying spiritual truths and connecting with the divine.
  • takavānasyāhaṁ: This can be interpreted as "I am the one who possesses strength." It highlights the idea of personal empowerment and the recognition of one's own capabilities.
  • cid: Meaning "consciousness" or "awareness," this word points to the importance of being aware and mindful in our actions and thoughts.
  • dhi: This translates to "intellect" or "understanding." It signifies the role of wisdom in guiding our decisions and actions.
  • rirebhāśvinā: This term can be understood as "with the duality of the Ashvins," referring to the twin deities associated with health and vitality. It suggests the importance of balance and harmony in life.
  • vām: This means "your" or "of you," indicating a connection to the divine or to others in our community.
  • ākṣī: This word translates to "eyes," symbolizing perception and insight. It emphasizes the need to see beyond the surface and understand deeper truths.
  • śubhas: Meaning "auspicious" or "good," this word highlights the importance of positive actions and intentions in our lives.
  • patī: This translates to "lord" or "master," indicating a sense of authority and guidance, whether it be from a divine source or within ourselves.
  • dan: This means "gift" or "wealth," suggesting that true richness comes from knowledge, wisdom, and positive relationships.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of knowledge, awareness, and the balance of dualities in our lives. It encourages us to listen, understand, and empower ourselves while maintaining a connection with the divine and our community. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways, such as in education, where listening and understanding are crucial for personal growth, or in relationships, where awareness and positive intentions foster harmony. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate wisdom and positivity in our daily lives, leading to a more fulfilling and enriched existence.
