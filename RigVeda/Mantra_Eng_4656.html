अहं चन तत सूरिभिर आनश्यां तव ज्याय इन्द्र सुम्नम ओजः |त्वया यत स्तवन्ते साधवीर वीरास त्रिवरूथेन नहुषा शविष्ठ ||
ahaṁ cana tat sūribhir ānaśyāṁ tava jyāya indra sumnam ojaḥ | tvayā yat stavante sadhavīra vīrās trivarūthena nahuṣā śaviṣṭha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ahaṁ: I
    • cana: also
    • tat: that
    • sūribhir: by the strong ones
    • ānaśyāṁ: in the great strength
    • tava: your
    • jyāya: greater
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • sumnam: auspicious
    • ojaḥ: energy or power
    • tvayā: by you
    • yat: that which
    • stavante: praise
    • sadhavīra: with good heroes
    • vīrās: the brave ones
    • trivarūthena: with threefold strength
    • nahuṣā: by Nahusha (a king)
    • śaviṣṭha: the most powerful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for strength and auspiciousness from Indra, the king of gods, who is often associated with rain, thunder, and strength.
    • The speaker acknowledges the power of Indra and seeks to be empowered by the same strength that the strong ones possess.
    • The mention of "good heroes" and "threefold strength" signifies the importance of community and collective strength in achieving goals.
    • The reference to Nahusha, a king who became a god, highlights the potential for human beings to rise to greatness through virtue and strength.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team leader may invoke the spirit of this mantra when rallying their team to work together towards a common goal, emphasizing the importance of collective strength and support.
    • In sports, a coach might use this mantra to inspire athletes to draw upon their inner strength and the support of their teammates, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.
    • In personal development, individuals may reflect on this mantra when seeking to overcome challenges, reminding themselves of the strength they can draw from their community and their own inner resources.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking strength and support from higher powers and the community.
    • It emphasizes that true power comes not just from individual effort but from collective action and the virtues that bind us together.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to recognize their potential and the strength that comes from unity, making it applicable in various scenarios such as teamwork, leadership, and personal growth.
