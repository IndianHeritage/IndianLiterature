पण्यं-पण्यं इति सोतार आ धावत मद्याय |सोमं वीराय शूराय ||
panyam-panyam it sotāra ā dhāvata madyāya | somaṁ vīrāya śūrāya ||
  • Mantra: "panyam-panyam it sotāra ā dhāvata madyāya | somaṁ vīrāya śūrāya ||"
  • Translation: "O Soma, you who flow like a river, may you bring forth the essence of strength and valor for the brave."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Panyam-panyam: Refers to the essence or the essence of life, often associated with the flow of water or nourishment.
    • Sotāra: Means flowing or that which moves like a river, symbolizing the continuous flow of energy or vitality.
    • Ā dhāvata: Translates to "may you bring forth" or "may you flow towards," indicating a desire for abundance and nourishment.
    • Madyāya: Refers to the essence of strength, often linked to the invigorating qualities of Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic rituals.
    • Somaṁ: The divine drink that symbolizes immortality and is associated with the moon and the life force.
    • Vīrāya: Means "for the brave" or "for the heroes," emphasizing courage and valor.
    • Śūrāya: Refers to strength and power, often associated with warriors and those who exhibit bravery.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, the mantra can be interpreted as a call for innovation and creativity, where leaders encourage their teams to flow with ideas and bring forth solutions that embody strength and courage.
    • In personal development, individuals may use this mantra as a motivational tool, reminding themselves to harness their inner strength and bravery in facing challenges, much like a river that flows steadily despite obstacles.
    • In sports, athletes often seek to embody the qualities of Soma, drawing on their inner strength and courage to perform at their best, akin to the warriors referenced in the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of strength, courage, and the continuous flow of life energy. It serves as a reminder that, like a river, we must embrace the challenges we face and draw upon our inner resources to overcome them. In various aspects of life—be it in personal growth, professional endeavors, or athletic pursuits—this mantra encourages us to seek nourishment from our experiences and to embody the qualities of bravery and strength. By doing so, we can navigate the complexities of life with resilience and purpose.
