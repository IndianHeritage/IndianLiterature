ये सवितुः सत्यसवस्य विश्वे मित्रस्य व्रते वरुणस्य देवाः |ते सौभागं वीरवत् गोमद अप्नो दधातन द्रविणं चित्रम् अस्मे ||
ye savituḥ satyasavasya viśve mitrasya vrate varuṇasya devāḥ | te saubhagaṁ vīravad gomad apno dadhātana draviṇaṁ citram asme ||
  • ye savituḥ: This phrase refers to "those who are of the Sun" or "those who belong to Savitar," the Sun God in Vedic tradition. The Sun is often seen as a source of life and energy, symbolizing enlightenment and knowledge.
  • satyasavasya: This term can be translated as "truthful and powerful." It emphasizes the importance of truth and integrity in one's actions and thoughts.
  • viśve mitrasya: This means "of all friends" or "universal friendship." It highlights the idea of unity and harmony among all beings, suggesting that we should foster relationships based on mutual respect and support.
  • vrate varuṇasya: This refers to "in the vow of Varuna," the deity associated with cosmic order and moral law. It signifies the importance of adhering to ethical principles and maintaining order in society.
  • devāḥ: This simply means "the gods," representing divine forces that guide and protect humanity.
  • te saubhagaṁ: This translates to "may they grant us prosperity." It expresses a desire for abundance and well-being in life.
  • vīravad: This word means "like a hero" or "with valor." It encourages individuals to act courageously and with strength in the face of challenges.
  • gomad apno: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we attain wealth." It emphasizes the pursuit of material and spiritual wealth, suggesting that both are essential for a fulfilling life.
  • dadhātana draviṇaṁ: This means "may they bestow riches." It is a prayer for divine blessings in terms of resources and prosperity.
  • citram asme: This translates to "may we have a wonderful life." It encapsulates the aspiration for a life filled with joy, creativity, and fulfillment.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for prosperity, truth, and ethical living. It emphasizes the importance of unity, courage, and the pursuit of both material and spiritual wealth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to live truthfully and courageously, fostering relationships based on mutual respect.
  • In community building, it promotes unity and cooperation among diverse groups, emphasizing the importance of collective well-being.
  • In business and professional settings, it serves as a reminder to uphold ethical practices while striving for success and prosperity.

Overall, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for leading a balanced and fulfilling life, harmonizing material pursuits with spiritual values.
