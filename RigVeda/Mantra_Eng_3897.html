न ते त इन्द्राभ्य अ1स्मद ऋष्वायुक्तासो अब्रह्मता यद आसन |तिष्ठा रथम अधि तं वज्रहस्तā रश्मिं देव यमसे स्वश्वः ||
na te ta indrābhy a1smad ṛṣvāyuktāso abrahmatā yad asan | tiṣṭhā ratham adhi taṁ vajrahastā raśmiṁ deva yamase svaśvaḥ ||
  • na te ta indrābhy a1smad ṛṣvāyuktāso abrahmatā yad asan
    • na te: This phrase translates to "not for you," indicating a sense of separation or distinction.
    • ta indrābhy: Refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power and authority.
    • a1smad: This word can be interpreted as "our" or "of us," suggesting a collective identity.
    • ṛṣvāyuktāso: This term relates to "those who are connected with the seers or sages," emphasizing wisdom and insight.
    • abrahmatā: This means "non-division" or "unity," pointing towards a state of oneness or wholeness.
    • yad asan: Translates to "what exists," referring to the reality or truth of existence.
  • tiṣṭhā ratham adhi taṁ vajrahastā raśmiṁ deva yamase svaśvaḥ
    • tiṣṭhā: Means "stand" or "remain," suggesting stability and presence.
    • ratham: Refers to a "chariot," symbolizing movement, progress, and the journey of life.
    • adhi taṁ: Translates to "above it," indicating a higher perspective or oversight.
    • vajrahastā: Means "one with a thunderbolt in hand," referring to Indra's strength and power.
    • raśmiṁ: This word means "ray" or "light," symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
    • deva: Refers to "god" or "divine," indicating a higher spiritual presence.
    • yamase: Means "to you," suggesting a direct address to the divine.
    • svaśvaḥ: Translates to "our own," indicating a personal connection or belonging.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, wisdom, and divine guidance in our lives. It suggests that while we may feel separate from the divine or powerful forces, there is a collective identity that connects us all. The imagery of the chariot and the thunderbolt signifies the journey of life, where we must remain steadfast and seek higher knowledge to navigate our paths. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to moments of decision-making, where we seek clarity and strength to overcome challenges. It encourages us to connect with our inner wisdom and the divine, reminding us that we are never truly alone in our journeys.
