यस्याजस्रं शवसां मानम् उक्थं परिभुजद् rodasī विश्वतः सीम् |स पारीषत् क्रतुभिर् मन्दसानो मरुत्वान् नो भवत्व् इन्द्र ऊती ||
yasyājasraṁ śavasā mānam uktham paribhujad rodasī viśvataḥ sīm | sa pāriṣat kratubhir mandasāno marutvān no bhavatv indra ūtī ||
  • yasyājasraṁ: This word can be broken down into "yasy" meaning "whose" and "ājasraṁ" meaning "constant" or "ever-present". It refers to the one who is always present or ever-reliable.
  • śavasā: This term translates to "with strength" or "powerful". It signifies the strength that comes from the divine or the cosmic order.
  • mānam: This means "honor" or "respect". It indicates the importance of reverence in our lives.
  • uktham: This translates to "sacred utterance" or "mantra". It emphasizes the power of words and their ability to manifest reality.
  • paribhujad: This means "encompassing" or "pervading". It suggests a presence that surrounds and supports.
  • rodasī: This refers to "the two worlds" or "the sky and the earth". It symbolizes the connection between the spiritual and the material realms.
  • viśvataḥ: This means "from all sides" or "universally". It indicates an all-encompassing nature.
  • sīm: This translates to "boundaries" or "limits". It refers to the idea of containment within the universe.
  • sa: This means "he" or "that one". It refers to the divine or the higher power being addressed.
  • pāriṣat: This term means "assembly" or "gathering". It signifies the collective strength of a community or group.
  • kratubhir: This translates to "with intentions" or "with resolve". It emphasizes the importance of purpose in actions.
  • mandasāno: This means "with gentleness" or "calmly". It suggests a peaceful approach to life.
  • marutvān: This refers to "the storm" or "the winds". It symbolizes change and the dynamic nature of existence.
  • no: This means "our". It indicates a sense of belonging and community.
  • bhavatv: This translates to "may he become" or "may he be". It expresses a wish or a prayer.
  • indra: This refers to "Indra\ the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power and authority.
  • ūtī: This means "blessings" or "grace". It signifies the divine favor that one seeks.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine presence, strength, and the power of words in shaping our reality. It calls upon the higher powers to bless us with strength and guidance, encouraging a sense of community and purpose. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as seeking support during challenging times, fostering unity in communities, and recognizing the power of intentions in our actions. By invoking these qualities, individuals can navigate life's challenges with grace and strength, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
