प्र हंसासस त्रिपलम् मन्यम् अच्छामाद अस्तं वृषगणाः अयासुः |आङ्गूष्यम् पवमानं सखायो दुरमार्षं साकं प्र वदन्ति वाणम् ||
pra haṁsāsas tṛpalam manyum acchāmād astaṁ vṛṣagaṇā ayāsuḥ | āṅgūṣya1m pavamānaṁ sakhāyo durmarṣaṁ sākam pra vadanti vāṇam ||
  • pra haṁsāsas: This term can be interpreted as "the swans" or "the noble ones." In many cultures, swans symbolize grace, beauty, and purity. In real life, this can relate to individuals who embody these qualities, such as artists or leaders who inspire others through their actions.
  • tṛpalam: This word means "threefold" or "triple." It can refer to various triads in life, such as the mind, body, and spirit, or the past, present, and future. For example, in personal development, one must balance these three aspects to achieve holistic growth.
  • manyum: This translates to "anger" or "emotion." It highlights the importance of managing one's emotions. In real life, emotional intelligence is crucial in personal and professional relationships, allowing individuals to navigate conflicts and communicate effectively.
  • acchāmād: This term can be understood as "to be free from" or "to overcome." It signifies the need to rise above challenges. For instance, overcoming adversity in life, such as dealing with loss or failure, can lead to personal growth and resilience.
  • astaṁ: This means "to set" or "to go down." It can symbolize the setting sun, representing the end of a phase or the conclusion of a journey. In life, this can relate to transitions, such as moving on from a job or relationship.
  • vṛṣagaṇā: This term refers to "the group of bulls" or "strength." Bulls are often associated with power and determination. In real life, this can represent teamwork and collaboration, where individuals come together to achieve a common goal.
  • ayāsuḥ: This translates to "in the world" or "in existence." It emphasizes the importance of being present and aware of one's surroundings. Mindfulness practices, such as meditation, can help individuals stay grounded in the present moment.
  • āṅgūṣya1m: This term can be interpreted as "the finger" or "to point." It signifies direction or guidance. In real life, mentors or guides play a crucial role in helping individuals navigate their paths, whether in education or career choices.
  • pavamānaṁ: This means "the purifying one" or "the wind." It symbolizes cleansing and renewal. In life, this can relate to the importance of letting go of negativity and embracing new beginnings, such as starting a new chapter after a difficult experience.
  • sakhāyo: This translates to "friends" or "companions." It highlights the value of relationships and support systems. In real life, having a strong network of friends can provide emotional support and encouragement during challenging times.
  • durmarṣaṁ: This term means "difficult to overcome" or "hard to bear." It acknowledges the challenges that life presents. For example, facing societal pressures or personal struggles can be daunting, but resilience and support can help individuals navigate these difficulties.
  • sākam: This translates to "together" or "in unity." It emphasizes the importance of collaboration and community. In real life, working together towards a common goal, such as in team sports or community service, can lead to greater achievements.
  • pra vadanti vāṇam: This phrase means "they speak the truth" or "they express." It signifies the importance of communication and honesty. In personal and professional relationships, open and truthful communication fosters trust and understanding.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of grace, emotional management, overcoming challenges, and the importance of community and communication. Its significance lies in its applicability to real-life scenarios, encouraging individuals to embody noble qualities, manage their emotions, and work collaboratively towards common goals. By embracing these principles, one can navigate life's complexities with resilience and integrity.
