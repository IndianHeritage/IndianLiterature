उग्रā सन्ता हवामह उपेदं सवनाṁ सुतम् |इन्द्राग्नī एहा गच्छताम् ||
ugrā santā havāmaha upedaṁ savanaṁ sutam | indrāgnī eha gacchatām ||
  • ugrā: This word translates to "powerful" or "fierce." It signifies strength and intensity, often associated with deities or forces of nature.
  • santā: This means "we invoke" or "we call upon." It indicates a plea or request for divine intervention or assistance.
  • havāmaha: This translates to "we offer" or "we present." It reflects the act of giving or dedicating something to the divine, often in the context of a ritual or prayer.
  • upedaṁ: This means "to this" or "towards this." It suggests directionality in the invocation, focusing on a specific goal or intention.
  • savanaṁ: This refers to "sacrifice" or "offering." It is often associated with the rituals performed to please the gods and seek their blessings.
  • sutam: This translates to "the one who is born" or "the offspring." In a broader sense, it can refer to the results or fruits of one's actions.
  • indrāgnī: This compound word refers to the deities Indra and Agni. Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength and rain, while Agni is the fire god, symbolizing energy and transformation.
  • eha: This means "here" or "in this place." It emphasizes the immediacy of the invocation, calling for the presence of the divine in the current moment.
  • gacchatām: This translates to "may they go" or "may they arrive." It is a request for the deities to come forth and accept the offerings made.

The mantra can be understood as a powerful invocation to the deities Indra and Agni, asking for their presence and blessings through offerings. It reflects the essence of Vedic rituals where the devotee seeks to connect with divine forces to gain strength, prosperity, and transformation in life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra invokes powerful deities for assistance, individuals can seek strength and guidance in their personal endeavors, such as pursuing education or career goals.
  • Community Rituals: In communal gatherings, this mantra can be recited to invoke collective strength and unity, fostering a sense of togetherness and shared purpose.
  • Environmental Awareness: The invocation of Indra, associated with rain and fertility, can be a reminder of the importance of nature and the need to protect our environment for future generations.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with divine energies, encouraging them to seek strength and transformation in their lives. It serves as a reminder of the importance of rituals and offerings in fostering a deeper connection with the universe and the forces that govern it. By invoking these powerful deities, individuals can find inspiration and support in their personal and communal journeys.
