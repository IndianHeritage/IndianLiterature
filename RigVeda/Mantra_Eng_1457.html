मदाय हर्यताय ते तुविष्टमाय धायसे |आ त्वा यच्छन्तु हरितो न सूर्यं अहा विश्वेवा सूर्यं ||
madāya haryatāya te tuviṣṭamāya dhāyase | ā tvā yacchantu harito na sūryam ahā viśveva sūryam ||
  • madāya: This word can be translated as "for the sake of joy" or "for the sake of intoxication." It signifies a state of bliss or ecstasy that one experiences.
  • haryatāya: This term refers to "the one who brings happiness" or "the one who is joyful." It emphasizes the importance of joy in life.
  • te: This is a pronoun meaning "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or force.
  • tuviṣṭamāya: This word can be interpreted as "to the one who is most excellent" or "to the one who is supreme." It denotes a sense of reverence towards a higher power.
  • dhāyase: This means "you support" or "you uphold." It suggests the idea of stability and strength provided by the divine.
  • ā tvā: This phrase translates to "to you," again addressing the divine presence.
  • yacchantu: This means "may they grant" or "may they bestow." It expresses a desire for blessings or gifts from the divine.
  • harito: This term refers to "the green" or "the flourishing," symbolizing life, nature, and vitality.
  • na sūryam: This translates to "not the sun," indicating a distinction from the physical sun, perhaps referring to a deeper, spiritual light.
  • ahā: This word means "I" or "my," indicating a personal connection to the divine.
  • viśveva: This means "like all" or "universal," suggesting a connection to the entirety of existence.
  • sūryam: This again refers to "the sun," symbolizing illumination, knowledge, and enlightenment.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to a divine force that brings joy and support, asking for blessings that are not merely material but also spiritual and universal. It emphasizes the importance of joy and the divine's role in sustaining life and providing enlightenment.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In moments of stress or anxiety, one can recite this mantra to invoke a sense of joy and support from the universe.
  • During celebrations or gatherings, it can be used to bless the occasion, inviting happiness and prosperity.
  • In personal reflection or meditation, it serves as a reminder to seek joy and enlightenment beyond material possessions.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with a higher power that embodies joy, support, and enlightenment. It encourages a holistic approach to life, where one seeks not just material success but also spiritual fulfillment and happiness. By incorporating this mantra into daily life, individuals can cultivate a deeper sense of purpose and connection to the universe.
