नापाभूत न वो 'तीतृषामानिःशस्तā ऋभवो यज्ञे अस्मिन् |सम इन्द्रेण मदथा सम मरुद्भिः सं राजभिः रत्नधेयाय देवाः ||
nāpābhūta na vo 'tītṛṣāmāniḥśastā ṛbhavo yajñe asmin | sam indreṇa madatha sam marudbhiḥ saṁ rājabhī ratnadheyāya devāḥ ||
  • nāpābhūta: This term can be broken down into "na" (not) and "āpābhūta" (having been deprived). It suggests a state of abundance or fulfillment, indicating that one should not feel deprived or lacking.
  • na vo 'tītṛṣāmāniḥśastā: "na vo" means "not for you," while "tītṛṣāmāniḥśastā" refers to the state of being thirsty or desiring. This part emphasizes that the divine should not let us feel unfulfilled or thirsty for anything.
  • ṛbhavo yajñe asmin: "ṛbhavo" refers to the wise or enlightened beings, "yajñe" means sacrifice or offering, and "asmin" means in this. This suggests that in this offering or sacrifice, the wise ones are present, guiding us.
  • sam indreṇa madatha: "sam" means together, "indreṇa" refers to Indra, the king of gods, and "madatha" means to be intoxicated or filled with joy. This indicates that when we come together in unity, we can experience divine joy.
  • sam marudbhiḥ: "marudbhiḥ" refers to the storm gods or winds. This signifies the power of nature and the divine forces working together in harmony.
  • saṁ rājabhī ratnadheyāya devāḥ: "saṁ" means together, "rājabhī" refers to kings or rulers, "ratnadheyāya" means for the bestowing of riches or treasures, and "devāḥ" means gods. This part emphasizes that when the divine and rulers work together, they can bestow prosperity and abundance.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of abundance, fulfillment, and divine joy. It teaches us that by coming together in unity, whether in community, family, or society, we can overcome feelings of deprivation and thirst. The significance of this mantra is profound in real-life scenarios, such as in community gatherings, festivals, or any collective effort aimed at achieving a common goal. It reminds us that when we align our intentions with the divine and work together harmoniously, we can attract prosperity and joy into our lives. This mantra serves as a guiding principle for fostering unity and abundance in our daily interactions and endeavors.
