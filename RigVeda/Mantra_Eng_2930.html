हंसा इव कृतुथ श्लोकं अद्रिभिर मदन्तो गीरभिर अध्वरे सुते सचा |देवेभिर विप्राः ऋषयो नृचक्षसो वि पिबध्वं कुशिकाः सोम्यम मधु ||
haṁsā iva kṛṇutha ślokam adribhir madanto gīrbhir adhvare sute sacā | devebhir viprā ṛṣayo nṛcakṣaso vi pibadhvaṁ kuśikāḥ somyam madhu ||
  • haṁsā iva kṛṇutha: This phrase translates to "like the swans, let us create." The swan is often seen as a symbol of purity and wisdom in various cultures. In real life, this can be related to the idea of creating something beautiful and meaningful, much like artists or thinkers who strive to produce work that elevates the human experience.
  • ślokam adribhir madanto: This means "the verses that are sweet like the mountains." Mountains are often associated with stability and grandeur. This can be likened to the way we seek stability in our lives through the wisdom we gain from literature, philosophy, or spiritual teachings.
  • gīrbhir adhvare sute sacā: This translates to "with hymns, we offer the sweet drink." In many cultures, offerings are made to deities or nature as a form of gratitude. This can be seen in real life during festivals or rituals where people come together to celebrate and give thanks, fostering a sense of community and connection.
  • devebhir viprā ṛṣayo nṛcakṣaso: This means "the wise ones, the seers, and the men of insight." This highlights the importance of wisdom and insight in our lives. In a practical sense, this can be related to the role of educators, mentors, and leaders who guide us through knowledge and experience.
  • vi pibadhvaṁ kuśikāḥ somyam madhu: This translates to "drink the sweet nectar of the moon." The moon is often associated with calmness and reflection. In real life, this can symbolize the importance of taking time to reflect and rejuvenate, much like how we might take a moment to enjoy a peaceful evening or meditate.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the creation of beauty and wisdom through collective effort and insight. It signifies the importance of community, reflection, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as collaborative projects, educational endeavors, and spiritual practices. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can foster a deeper connection with themselves and their communities, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
