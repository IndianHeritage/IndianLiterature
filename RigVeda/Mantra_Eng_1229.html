याभिः पत्नीर विमदाय न्यूहथुर आग्ह वा याभिर अरुणीर अशिक्षतम् |याभिः सुदासा ऊहथुः सुदेव्यं ताभिर ऊषु ऊतिभिर अश्विनां गतम् ||
yābhiḥ patnīr vimadāya nyūhathur ā gha vā yābhir aruṇīr aśikṣatam | yābhiḥ sudāsa ūhathuḥ sudevya1ṁ tābhir ū ṣu ūtibhir aśvinā gatam ||
  • yābhiḥ - This word can be translated as "by whom" or "with whom." It indicates a connection or relationship.
  • patnīr - This means "wives" or "partners." It signifies the importance of companionship and partnership in life.
  • vimadāya - This term can be interpreted as "to bring joy" or "to create happiness." It emphasizes the role of relationships in fostering joy.
  • nyūhathur - This word suggests "they have come together" or "they have united." It reflects the idea of unity and collaboration.
  • ā gha vā - This phrase can be understood as "may they come" or "let them arrive." It expresses a desire for positive outcomes.
  • yābhir - Similar to the first word, it means "by whom" or "with whom," reinforcing the theme of relationships.
  • aruṇīr - This term refers to "the dawn" or "the light." It symbolizes new beginnings and hope.
  • aśikṣatam - This can be translated as "they have been taught" or "they have learned." It highlights the importance of knowledge and education.
  • sudāsa - This refers to "good servants" or "noble individuals." It signifies the value of service and dedication.
  • ūhathuḥ - This means "they have united" or "they have come together," reinforcing the theme of collaboration.
  • sudevyaṁ - This term can be interpreted as "good deities" or "noble beings." It emphasizes the presence of positive influences.
  • tābhir - This means "by them" or "with them," again highlighting the importance of relationships.
  • ū ṣu - This phrase can be understood as "in the good" or "in the auspicious." It reflects a focus on positivity.
  • ūtibhir - This term refers to "by the actions" or "through deeds." It emphasizes the significance of actions in achieving goals.
  • aśvinā - This refers to "the Ashvins," who are divine twin brothers in Vedic mythology, symbolizing health and vitality.
  • gatam - This means "gone" or "departed," indicating a transition or change.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of relationships, unity, and the pursuit of knowledge. It highlights how partnerships, whether in marriage or friendship, can bring joy and foster collaboration. The mention of the dawn symbolizes hope and new beginnings, suggesting that through unity and positive actions, individuals can achieve noble goals. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in family dynamics, teamwork in professional settings, and community building, where the collective effort and shared values lead to success and happiness.
