धायोभिर वा यो युज्येभिर अर्कैर विद्युः न दविद्योत स्वेभिः शुष्मैः |शर्धो वा यो मरुतां तातक्ष ऋभुर न त्वेषो रभसानो अद्यौत ||
dhāyobhir vā yo yujyebhir arkair vidyun na davidyot svebhiḥ śuṣmaiḥ | śardho vā yo marutāṁ tatakṣa ṛbhur na tveṣo rabhasāno adyaut ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dhāyobhir: By those who meditate or contemplate.
    • vā: Or.
    • yo: Who.
    • yujyebhir: By those who are connected or united.
    • arkair: With the rays or light.
    • vidyun: Lightning or illumination.
    • na: Not.
    • davidyot: To be illuminated or enlightened.
    • svebhiḥ: By their own.
    • śuṣmaiḥ: Dry or barren.
    • śardho: In the season or time.
    • vā: Or.
    • yo: Who.
    • marutāṁ: Of the winds or storm gods.
    • tatakṣa: Has been struck or has come forth.
    • ṛbhur: The one who is strong or powerful.
    • na: Not.
    • tveṣo: Your form or essence.
    • rabhasāno: With great force or intensity.
    • adyaut: Has shone or appeared.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of meditation and connection with the divine.
    • The first part emphasizes the importance of contemplation and unity with the light, suggesting that true enlightenment comes from within.
    • The mention of "lightning" symbolizes sudden insight or realization, which can occur when one is deeply connected to their inner self.
    • The second part refers to the forces of nature, particularly the winds, indicating that these powerful elements can also be harnessed through spiritual strength.
    • It suggests that one’s essence should not be overwhelmed by external forces but should instead shine brightly, even in challenging circumstances.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a stressful work environment, taking a moment to meditate can help individuals find clarity and insight, much like the "lightning" mentioned in the mantra.
    • During times of personal crisis, connecting with nature (the winds) can provide a sense of grounding and strength, allowing one to navigate through difficulties.
    • Practices such as yoga and mindfulness encourage individuals to unite their mind and body, leading to personal enlightenment and resilience.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of inner strength and the power of meditation in achieving enlightenment.
    • It teaches that true illumination comes from within and that one should remain steadfast against external challenges.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, resilience, and a deeper connection with oneself and the universe.
