वयं उ त्वा तदिदर्था इन्द्र त्वायन्तः सखायः |कण्वा उक्तेभिर जarantे ||
vayam u tvā tadidarthā indra tvāyantaḥ sakhāyaḥ | kaṇvā ukthebhir jarante ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vayam: We
    • u: indeed
    • tvā: you
    • tadidarthā: for that purpose
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • tvāyantaḥ: we are your friends
    • sakhāyaḥ: companions
    • kaṇvā: the wise
    • ukthebhir: with words
    • jarante: they sing
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra expresses a plea or invocation to Indra, acknowledging the bond of friendship and companionship. It emphasizes the collective effort of individuals who come together to seek the blessings of the divine for a common purpose.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Community Support: Just as the mantra highlights companionship, in real life, communities often come together to support each other during difficult times, such as natural disasters or personal crises. For instance, neighbors may unite to help a family affected by a fire, showcasing the essence of friendship and collective action.
    • Teamwork in Organizations: In a workplace, employees collaborate on projects, much like the companions in the mantra. Their combined efforts and shared goals can lead to success, reflecting the idea of working together for a greater purpose.
    • Friendship and Relationships: The mantra can also be seen in the context of personal relationships, where friends support each other through challenges, celebrating successes together, and fostering a sense of belonging.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on unity, friendship, and collective purpose. It serves as a reminder that we are stronger together, whether in our personal lives, communities, or workplaces. By invoking the spirit of companionship and collaboration, we can achieve greater outcomes and navigate life's challenges more effectively. This mantra encourages us to recognize the importance of our relationships and the power of working together towards common goals.
