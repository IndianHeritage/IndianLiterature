वस्यां इन्द्रासि मे पितुर उता भ्रातुर अभुञ्जतः |माता च मे छदयथा समा वसो वसुत्वनाया राधसे ||
vasyām̐ indrāsi me pitur uta bhrātur abhuñjataḥ | mātā ca me chadayathaḥ samā vaso vasutvanāya rādhase ||
  • vasyām̐ - This word can be interpreted as "to be controlled" or "to be subdued." It signifies the idea of having authority or mastery over something.
  • indrāsi - Referring to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
  • me - This means "my," indicating possession or personal connection.
  • pitur - This translates to "father," representing guidance, protection, and authority in one's life.
  • uta - Meaning "or," it presents an alternative or additional thought.
  • bhrātur - This means "brother," symbolizing companionship, support, and familial bonds.
  • abhuñjataḥ - This can be understood as "may enjoy" or "may partake," indicating the act of sharing or receiving blessings.
  • mātā - Meaning "mother," it represents nurturing, care, and unconditional love.
  • ca - This means "and," connecting thoughts or entities.
  • me - Again, "my," reinforcing personal connection.
  • chadayathaḥ - This translates to "may cover" or "may protect," indicating a desire for safety and security.
  • samā - Meaning "equal" or "together," suggesting unity and harmony.
  • vaso - This can mean "dwelling" or "abode," referring to a place of comfort and belonging.
  • vasutvanāya - This word can be interpreted as "for the well-being" or "for prosperity," indicating a desire for abundance and success.
  • rādhase - This can be understood as "to the one who is worthy" or "to the deserving," suggesting respect and acknowledgment of merit.

The mantra expresses a deep yearning for the blessings and protection of both familial and divine figures. It emphasizes the importance of relationships with parents and siblings, as well as the overarching guidance of a higher power, represented by Indra. The speaker seeks to be covered and protected by their mother, while also wishing for the well-being and prosperity of their family.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In family dynamics, it highlights the importance of mutual support and protection among family members, encouraging individuals to cherish and nurture their relationships.
  • In leadership roles, it serves as a reminder for leaders to act with strength and compassion, ensuring the well-being of those they lead.
  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek guidance from their elders while also striving for their own mastery and control over their lives.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the interconnectedness of familial bonds and divine guidance. It serves as a reminder of the importance of love, protection, and support within families, while also acknowledging the strength and authority that comes from a higher power. By embodying these principles, individuals can foster harmonious relationships and strive for personal and collective prosperity.
