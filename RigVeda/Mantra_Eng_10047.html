कीदृङ् इन्द्रः सरमे का दृष्टिका यस्येदं दूतीर असरः पराकात् |आ च गच्छान मित्रम् एनां दधामाथा गवां गोपतीर नो भवाति ||
kīdṛṅṅ indraḥ sarame kā dṛśīkā yasyedaṁ dūtīr asaraḥ parākāt | ā ca gacchān mitram enā dadhāmāthā gavāṁ gopatir no bhavāti ||
  • kīdṛṅṅ indraḥ: This phrase translates to "What kind of Indra." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and war. This line prompts us to reflect on the qualities and attributes of Indra, encouraging us to seek the divine qualities in our lives.
  • sarame kā dṛśīkā: This can be interpreted as "What is the vision of the Sarama." Sarama is a mythical dog in Vedic texts, often seen as a guide or messenger. This line suggests the importance of guidance and vision in our lives, akin to having a mentor or a guiding principle.
  • yasyedaṁ dūtīr asaraḥ parākāt: This translates to "Whose messenger is this, who is not obstructed." It emphasizes the idea of communication and the role of messengers in conveying important messages. In real life, this can relate to the importance of clear communication in relationships and organizations.
  • ā ca gacchān mitram: This means "And going towards friendship." It highlights the value of friendship and camaraderie. In our daily lives, fostering friendships can lead to a supportive network that enhances our well-being.
  • enā dadhāmāthā: This translates to "I place my trust in this." It signifies the act of placing trust in something or someone, which is crucial in both personal and professional relationships. Trust is the foundation of any strong relationship.
  • gavāṁ gopatir no bhavāti: This means "The protector of the cows does not become." In Vedic culture, cows symbolize wealth and nourishment. This line can be interpreted as a reminder of the responsibilities we have towards those we care for and the importance of being a protector in our communities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of divine qualities, guidance, communication, friendship, trust, and protection. In real life, these elements are significant as they remind us to embody the virtues we admire, seek guidance, communicate effectively, nurture friendships, build trust, and protect those who depend on us. By applying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and supportive environment in our personal and communal lives.
