शग्धी नो अस्य यद धा पुरम् आविथ धिय इन्द्र सिषासतः |शग्धि यथा रुशमं श्यावकं कृपम् इन्द्र प्रावः स्वर्णरम् ||
śagdhī no asya yad dha pauram āvitha dhiya indra siṣāsataḥ | śagdhi yathā ruśamaṁ śyāvakaṁ kṛpam indra prāvaḥ svarṇaram ||
  • śagdhī no asya yad dha pauram āvitha dhiya indra siṣāsataḥ
    • śagdhī: This term can be interpreted as "to be strong" or "to be powerful." It signifies the strength and resilience that one should possess.
    • no asya: This translates to "for us" or "to us," indicating a plea or request directed towards a higher power.
    • yad dha: Meaning "whatever is," it emphasizes the importance of accepting and understanding the reality of our circumstances.
    • pauram: This word refers to "the city" or "the community," highlighting the collective aspect of human existence.
    • āvitha: This means "to cover" or "to envelop," suggesting the need for protection or guidance.
    • dhiya: Translated as "intellect" or "understanding," it points to the importance of wisdom in navigating life.
    • indra siṣāsataḥ: "Indra" is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength and leadership. "siṣāsataḥ" implies "may he guide us," indicating a call for divine guidance.
  • śagdhi yathā ruśamaṁ śyāvakaṁ kṛpam indra prāvaḥ svarṇaram
    • śagdhi: Again, this emphasizes strength and resilience.
    • yathā: Meaning "as" or "like," it draws a comparison or analogy.
    • ruśamaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the shining" or "the bright," symbolizing clarity and enlightenment.
    • śyāvakaṁ: Referring to "the dark" or "the obscured," it represents challenges or difficulties that one may face.
    • kṛpam: This term means "compassion" or "kindness," highlighting the importance of empathy in overcoming obstacles.
    • indra prāvaḥ: "Prāvaḥ" means "to lead" or "to guide," reinforcing the idea of seeking divine assistance.
    • svarṇaram: This translates to "golden" or "precious," symbolizing the ultimate goal or aspiration in life.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, wisdom, and divine guidance in navigating the complexities of life. It calls for a collective understanding and protection within the community while recognizing the duality of light and darkness in our experiences. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal challenges, seeking inner strength and wisdom to overcome difficulties.
  • In community settings, fostering a sense of unity and support among individuals.
  • In leadership roles, guiding others with compassion and understanding.

Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to seek strength and guidance from higher powers while cultivating wisdom and compassion in our daily lives.
