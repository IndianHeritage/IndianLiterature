मन्त्र: प्रतिचीने माम अहनीष्वाः पर्णम् इव दधुः |प्रतिचीं जग्रभा वाचं अश्वं रशनया यथा ||
pratīcīne mām ahanīṣvāḥ parṇam ivā dadhuḥ | pratīcīṁ jagrabhā vācam aśvaṁ raśanayā yathā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pratīcīne: In the western direction
    • mām: Me
    • ahanīṣvāḥ: Those who are awake or alert
    • parṇam: Leaves or foliage
    • ivā: Like
    • dadhuḥ: They have given
    • pratīcīṁ: The western direction
    • jagrabhā: They have seized or taken
    • vācam: Speech or voice
    • aśvaṁ: Horse
    • raśanayā: By the rope or bond
    • yathā: Just as
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a connection between the speaker and the forces of nature, particularly in the western direction.
    • The phrase "those who are awake" suggests a call to awareness and mindfulness, emphasizing the importance of being alert to one's surroundings.
    • The imagery of leaves being given can symbolize nourishment and support, akin to how nature provides sustenance to life.
    • The mention of "speech" and "horse" indicates a relationship between communication and strength, where the horse can represent power and movement.
    • The use of "by the rope" suggests a binding or connection, indicating that our words and actions are intertwined with the forces around us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, being "awake" can relate to being aware of market trends and team dynamics, which can lead to better decision-making.
    • In personal relationships, the idea of giving and receiving support (like leaves from a tree) can be seen in how friends and family nurture each other.
    • The metaphor of a horse can be applied to personal ambitions; just as a horse can carry us forward, our words and actions can propel us toward our goals.
    • In environmental contexts, recognizing the importance of nature (the leaves) can lead to sustainable practices that benefit both the planet and humanity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of awareness, connection, and the interplay between our actions and the natural world.
    • It serves as a reminder to be mindful of our surroundings and the impact of our words and actions.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more meaningful relationships, better decision-making, and a deeper appreciation for the interconnectedness of life.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to harness our inner strength and communicate effectively, just as the horse moves forward with purpose.
