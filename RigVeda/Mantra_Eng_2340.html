परि णो हेतī रुद्रस्य वृज्याः परि त्वेषस्य दुरमतिर महī गात् |अव स्थिरा मगवद्भ्यस तनुष्व मīढवस् तोकाय तनयाय मृळ ||
pari ṇo hetī rudrasya vṛjyāḥ pari tveṣasya durmatir mahī gāt | ava sthirā maghavadbhyas tanuṣva mīḍhvas tokāya tanayāya mṛḻa ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pari: around, in all directions
    • ṇo: our
    • hetī: cause, reason
    • rudrasya: of Rudra (a form of Shiva)
    • vṛjyāḥ: to be avoided, rejected
    • pari: around, in all directions
    • tveṣasya: of your (Rudra's) anger
    • durmatir: ill thoughts, bad intentions
    • mahī: great, vast
    • gāt: to go, to move
    • ava: down, away
    • sthirā: stable, firm
    • maghavadbhyas: to the generous, the bounteous
    • tanuṣva: may you be strong, may you be firm
    • mīḍhvas: may you be nourished
    • tokāya: for the offspring
    • tanayāya: for the children
    • mṛḻa: may you be gentle, may you be kind
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Rudra, a deity associated with storms and destruction, asking for protection from negative influences and thoughts.
    • The repetition of "pari" emphasizes the all-encompassing nature of the plea, suggesting a desire for comprehensive protection.
    • The mention of "durmatir" highlights the importance of mental clarity and positive intentions, urging the avoidance of harmful thoughts.
    • The latter part of the mantra seeks blessings for strength and nourishment, particularly for future generations, indicating a focus on family and continuity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied when a team is facing challenges. By invoking a collective mindset to avoid negative thoughts and focus on constructive solutions, the team can foster a more positive work environment.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra serves as a reminder to communicate openly and avoid misunderstandings, thus nurturing a healthy bond.
    • Parents can use the essence of this mantra to instill values of kindness and strength in their children, ensuring they grow up with a positive mindset and resilience against negativity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of mental clarity, positive intentions, and the importance of nurturing future generations.
    • Its application in daily life can lead to improved relationships, better teamwork, and a more positive outlook on challenges.
    • By invoking the protective qualities of Rudra, individuals can seek to create a harmonious environment, both personally and collectively.
