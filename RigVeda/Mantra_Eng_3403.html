अस्मांं अविढ्धि विश्वहेन्द्र राया परीनसां |अस्मान विश्वाभिर ऊतिभिः ||
asmām̐ aviḍḍhi viśvahendra rāyā parīṇasā | asmān viśvābhir ūtibhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmām̐: "from us" or "among us"
    • aviḍḍhi: "do not separate" or "do not divide"
    • viśvahendra: "O Lord of all" or "the supreme being of the universe"
    • rāyā: "wealth" or "prosperity"
    • parīṇasā: "with completeness" or "in fullness"
    • asmān: "us" or "to us"
    • viśvābhir: "with all" or "by all"
    • ūtibhiḥ: "with blessings" or "with abundance"
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer for unity and abundance. It calls upon the supreme being to keep us together and bless us with prosperity.
    • The phrase "do not separate us" emphasizes the importance of community and togetherness, suggesting that division can lead to a loss of strength and resources.
    • The invocation of "wealth" and "blessings" indicates a desire for not just material prosperity but also spiritual and emotional well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied to foster teamwork. When colleagues work together harmoniously, they can achieve greater success and innovation.
    • In a family context, the mantra serves as a reminder to maintain strong bonds and support each other, ensuring that love and resources are shared.
    • In community initiatives, this mantra can inspire collective efforts towards social welfare, emphasizing that when people unite, they can create a more prosperous environment for all.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and abundance. It teaches us that togetherness is essential for achieving prosperity and well-being.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, workplaces, or communities, the principles of collaboration and shared blessings can lead to a more fulfilling and prosperous existence.
    • By embracing the essence of this mantra, individuals and groups can cultivate an environment where everyone thrives, reinforcing the idea that collective strength is greater than individual effort.
