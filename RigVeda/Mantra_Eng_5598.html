अंसेष्वा मरुतः खादयो वो वक्षःसु रुक्मा उपशिश्रियाणाः |विविद्युतो न वृष्टिभी रुचानां अनु स्वधाम आयुधैर यच्छमानाः ||
aṁseṣv ā marutaḥ khādayo vo vakṣaḥsu rukmā upaśiśriyāṇāḥ | vi vidyuto na vṛṣṭibhī rucānā anu svadhām āyudhair yacchamānāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "aṁseṣv ā marutaḥ khādayo vo vakṣaḥsu rukmā upaśiśriyāṇāḥ | vi vidyuto na vṛṣṭibhī rucānā anu svadhām āyudhair yacchamānāḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aṁseṣv: In the limbs or parts
    • ā: O
    • marutaḥ: O Winds or Storms
    • khādayo: You who consume or devour
    • vo: Your
    • vakṣaḥsu: In the chests or hearts
    • rukmā: Golden or radiant
    • upaśiśriyāṇāḥ: Those who are endowed or blessed
    • vi: Indeed
    • vidyuto: Lightning or brilliance
    • na: Not
    • vṛṣṭibhī: By rains or showers
    • rucānā: Of the shining or bright
    • anu: Following
    • svadhām: In your own abode or realm
    • āyudhair: With weapons or instruments
    • yacchamānāḥ: Bestowing or granting
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the Maruts, the storm deities in Vedic literature, who are often associated with strength, power, and the forces of nature.
    • The phrase "aṁseṣv ā marutaḥ" suggests a call to the winds to be present in all parts of life, indicating their omnipresence and influence.
    • "khādayo vo vakṣaḥsu rukmā" refers to the radiant energy that the winds bring, symbolizing vitality and life force that nourishes the heart and spirit.
    • The mention of "vidyuto" and "vṛṣṭibhī" highlights the duality of nature—lightning and rain—representing both destruction and nourishment, which are essential for growth.
    • In a real-life context, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the balance of nature, where storms can bring both challenges and blessings, much like the ups and downs in our lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of acknowledging the forces of nature and their impact on our lives.
    • It teaches us to embrace both the challenges (storms) and the blessings (nourishment) that come our way.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to remain resilient and adaptable, understanding that every experience contributes to our growth and well-being.
    • By recognizing the duality of existence, we can cultivate gratitude and strength, allowing us to navigate life's complexities with grace.
