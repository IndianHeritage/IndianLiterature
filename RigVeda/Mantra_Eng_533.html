मन्त्र: श्रुष्टिवानो हि दाशुषे देवाः अग्ने विचेतसः |तान रोहिदश्व गिरवणस त्रयस्त्रिंशतम आवह ||
śruṣṭīvāno hi dāśuṣe devā agne vicetasaḥ | tān rohidaśva girvaṇas trayastriṁśatam ā vaha ||
  • śruṣṭīvāno hi dāśuṣe: This phrase can be translated as "the one who has created the universe, O Agni." Here, "śruṣṭī" refers to creation, and "vāno" indicates the act of creation. "Dāśuṣe" refers to the divine or the one who is worthy of worship.
  • devā agne vicetasaḥ: This translates to "the gods, O Agni, who are wise." "Devā" means gods, and "vicetasaḥ" implies wisdom or intelligence. This line emphasizes the divine nature of Agni, the fire god, who is not only a creator but also possesses wisdom.
  • tān rohidaśva girvaṇas: This can be interpreted as "bring forth those who are strong and powerful." "Rohidaśva" refers to those who are robust or strong, while "girvaṇas" indicates the powerful or those who can express themselves well, often through speech or song.
  • trayastriṁśatam ā vaha: This translates to "bring us the thirty-three." "Trayastriṁśatam" refers to the thirty-three deities or divine beings in Vedic tradition, and "ā vaha" means to bring forth or to carry us towards them.

In summary, this mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, as a creator and wise deity, asking for the strength and support of the thirty-three divine beings. It highlights the importance of divine wisdom and strength in our lives.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be seen as a call for guidance and support in our endeavors. Just as Agni is a source of light and warmth, we seek enlightenment and strength in our personal and professional lives.
  • In real life, this can be applied in situations where we face challenges or need clarity. For instance, during difficult times, individuals may seek wisdom from mentors or spiritual leaders, akin to invoking the divine for guidance.
  • The mention of the thirty-three deities symbolizes the diverse aspects of life and the need for a holistic approach to challenges. In practice, this could mean seeking help from various sources—friends, family, or community—when facing obstacles.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of creation, wisdom, and strength, encouraging us to seek divine guidance and support in our journey through life.
