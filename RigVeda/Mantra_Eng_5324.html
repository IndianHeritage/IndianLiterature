स न इन्द्र त्वयतायाऽइषे धास तमनā च ये मगवाणो जुन्थि |वस्वीषु ते जारित्रे अस्तु शक्तिर यūयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
sa na indra tvayatāyā iṣe dhās tmanā ca ye maghavāno junanti | vasvī ṣu te jaritre astu śaktir yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Translation: "May Indra, the powerful one, grant us strength and wisdom. May those who are mighty and capable protect us. May your strength be with us in all our endeavors, and may you always keep us safe with your blessings."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Indra: A prominent deity in Vedic literature, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
    • Tvayatāyā: From you, indicating a plea for assistance and guidance.
    • Iṣe: To grant or bestow, emphasizing the act of giving strength and wisdom.
    • Dhās: To protect or support, highlighting the need for safety and security.
    • Maghavāno: Mighty ones, referring to those who possess great power and capability.
    • Jaritre: In all endeavors or actions, indicating the various aspects of life where support is sought.
    • Śaktir: Strength or power, a crucial element in achieving success.
    • Pāta: To protect or guard, reinforcing the desire for safety.
    • Svastibhiḥ: With blessings, indicating the importance of divine favor.
    • Sadā: Always, suggesting a continuous need for support.
    • Naḥ: Us, indicating the collective nature of the plea.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team may invoke this mantra during a meeting to seek collective strength and wisdom in decision-making.
    • During challenging times, individuals may recite this mantra to find courage and support from their community or family.
    • In educational institutions, students may use this mantra before exams to seek blessings for success and protection from failure.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking strength, wisdom, and protection from higher powers. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as in personal challenges, professional endeavors, and communal activities. By invoking this mantra, individuals and groups can foster a sense of unity and purpose, encouraging them to face obstacles with confidence and resilience. The significance lies in the recognition of the need for support and the acknowledgment of the divine presence in our lives, guiding us towards success and safety.
