दिवो नाभा विचक्षणो 'व्यो वारे महीयते |सोमो यः सुक्रतुः कविः ||
divo nābhā vicakṣaṇo 'vyo vāre mahīyate | somo yaḥ sukratuḥ kaviḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Divo: Of the heavens or celestial.
    • Nābhā: Navel or center.
    • Vicakṣaṇo: Wise or perceptive.
    • Avyo: Not limited or bound.
    • Vāre: In the protection or shelter.
    • Mahīyate: Is exalted or becomes great.
    • Somo: The moon or soma (nectar).
    • Yaḥ: Who.
    • Sukratuḥ: Of good intention or virtuous.
    • Kaviḥ: The poet or seer.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the celestial realm and the wisdom that comes from it.
    • The "navel of the heavens" symbolizes a central point of connection to the divine or universal consciousness.
    • The wise one, described as "vicakṣaṇo," is someone who perceives beyond the ordinary, understanding the deeper truths of existence.
    • The phrase "not limited" suggests that true wisdom and understanding are boundless, transcending earthly limitations.
    • The exaltation of the moon, or "soma," represents nourishment, tranquility, and the nurturing aspects of life.
    • The "virtuous seer" embodies the qualities of insight and creativity, guiding others through their wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom in various fields, such as science, philosophy, and art.
    • For instance, a scientist who explores the mysteries of the universe embodies the qualities of "vicakṣaṇo," seeking to understand the fundamental laws of nature.
    • Artists and poets, like the "kaviḥ," use their creativity to express profound truths and emotions, connecting with others on a deeper level.
    • In personal development, individuals who strive for self-awareness and understanding can be seen as embodying the essence of this mantra, seeking to transcend their limitations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, creativity, and the pursuit of higher understanding in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and insight, not just for personal gain but for the betterment of society.
    • In a world filled with distractions and superficiality, this mantra serves as a reminder to connect with the deeper aspects of existence and to strive for a life of purpose and virtue.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the significance of nurturing our inner selves and using our insights to illuminate the path for others.
