धेनुः प्रात्नस्य काम्यं दूहानन्तः पुत्रश्चरति दक्षिणायाः |आ द्योतनिं वहति शुभ्रयामोषसः स्तोमो अश्विनाव् अजिगः ||
dhenuḥ pratnasya kāmyaṁ duhānāntaḥ putraś carati dakṣiṇāyāḥ | ā dyotaniṁ vahati śubhrayāmoṣasaḥ stomo aśvināv ajīgaḥ ||
  • dhenuḥ pratnasya kāmyaṁ: The term "dhenuḥ" refers to a cow, which is often seen as a symbol of wealth and nourishment in Vedic literature. "Pratnasya" means of the pasture or the field, and "kāmyaṁ" signifies something desirable or wished for. This phrase can be interpreted as the cow of the pasture that fulfills desires.
  • duhānāntaḥ putraś carati dakṣiṇāyāḥ: "Duhānāntaḥ" means one who is milking or drawing out, while "putraś" refers to a son or offspring. "Carati" means to move or to act, and "dakṣiṇāyāḥ" indicates the direction of the south. This part of the mantra suggests that the offspring, or the one who is milking, is moving towards the south, which can symbolize prosperity and growth.
  • ā dyotaniṁ vahati: "Ā dyotaniṁ" translates to "that which brings light" or "illuminating." "Vahati" means to carry or to bear. This phrase indicates that something illuminating is being carried, which can be interpreted as the knowledge or wisdom that guides one’s actions.
  • śubhrayāmoṣasaḥ: "Śubhrayāmoṣasaḥ" can be broken down into "śubhra," meaning pure or auspicious, and "yāmoṣasaḥ," which refers to the dawn or the early morning. This suggests the arrival of a pure and auspicious time, symbolizing new beginnings and opportunities.
  • stomo aśvināv ajīgaḥ: "Stomo" refers to a hymn or praise, while "aśvināv" refers to the Aśvins, the twin deities associated with health and medicine. "Ajīgaḥ" means to be invoked or called upon. This part of the mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine blessings for health and well-being.

  • Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates themes of prosperity, nourishment, and divine blessings. It highlights the significance of seeking abundance and well-being through auspicious actions and the invocation of divine forces. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as starting a new venture, seeking health and wellness, or even during rituals and prayers for prosperity. The mantra serves as a reminder to align our actions with positive intentions and to seek the blessings of the divine for a fruitful and fulfilling life.
