इन्द्र गृणिषा उ स्तुषे महाम् उग्र ईशानकृत् |एहि नः सुतं पिब ||
indra gṛṇīṣa u stuṣe mahām̐ ugra īśānakṛt | ehi naḥ sutam piba ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Gṛṇīṣa: Means "to praise" or "to sing," indicating the act of offering respect and devotion.
  • U: A particle that can mean "and" or "also," connecting thoughts or actions.
  • Stuṣe: Translates to "to be praised" or "to be extolled," emphasizing the importance of recognition.
  • Mahām̐: Means "great" or "mighty," highlighting the significance of the subject being discussed.
  • Ugra: Refers to "fierce" or "powerful," often associated with the strength of Indra.
  • Īśānakṛt: Translates to "the one who creates" or "the lord," indicating a divine authority or creator.
  • Ehi: Means "come" or "approach," inviting a presence or a blessing.
  • Naḥ: Means "to us," indicating a personal or collective appeal.
  • Sutam: Refers to "the son" or "the offspring," often symbolizing prosperity or blessings.
  • Piba: Means "drink," suggesting the act of receiving or absorbing something beneficial.

This mantra can be interpreted as a call to Indra, the powerful deity, to come forth and bless the devotees with strength and prosperity. The invocation of Indra signifies a desire for leadership qualities and the ability to overcome challenges. The phrase "ehi naḥ sutam piba" can be seen as a request for divine blessings to be absorbed into the lives of the devotees, symbolizing the importance of receiving guidance and support from higher powers.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as:

  • Leadership: Individuals in positions of authority can recite this mantra to seek strength and wisdom in their decision-making processes.
  • Community Support: Groups or communities facing challenges can use this mantra to invoke unity and collective strength, encouraging members to support one another.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals seeking personal development can recite this mantra to attract positive energy and guidance in their lives.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with divine strength and guidance. By invoking Indra, devotees seek to embody qualities of leadership, resilience, and prosperity. This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of recognizing and absorbing the blessings that come from higher powers, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and empowered life.
