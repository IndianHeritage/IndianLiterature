अक्षैर मा दिव्यः कृषिम इति कृषस्व वित्ते रमस्व बहु मन्यमानः |तत्र गावाḥ किटव तत्र जाया तन मे विं चष्टे सवितायम आर्यः ||
akṣair mā dīvyaḥ kṛṣim it kṛṣasva vitte ramasva bahu manyamānaḥ | tatra gāvaḥ kitava tatra jāyā tan me vi caṣṭe savitāyam aryaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • akṣair: with the eyes
    • mā: not
    • dīvyaḥ: divine
    • kṛṣim: agriculture or farming
    • it: thus
    • kṛṣasva: may you cultivate
    • vitte: wealth
    • ramasva: may you enjoy
    • bahu: much
    • manyamānaḥ: considering
    • tatra: there
    • gāvaḥ: cows
    • kitava: deceitful or false
    • tatra: there
    • jāyā: wife
    • tan: that
    • me: my
    • vi: indeed
    • caṣṭe: may shine or may be visible
    • savitāyam: to the sun
    • aryaḥ: noble or honorable
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of agriculture and wealth in life.
    • It suggests that one should not only focus on divine or spiritual aspects but also engage in practical activities like farming.
    • The mention of cows signifies prosperity and sustenance, as cows are traditionally associated with wealth in agrarian societies.
    • The phrase "may you cultivate" indicates a proactive approach to life, encouraging individuals to take charge of their circumstances.
    • Considering wealth and enjoying it is a reminder that material well-being is also essential for a fulfilling life.
    • The reference to the sun symbolizes enlightenment and the pursuit of knowledge, suggesting that one should seek wisdom in their endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A farmer who diligently works on his land, understanding the importance of both hard work and the blessings of nature, embodies the essence of this mantra.
    • In modern society, entrepreneurs who cultivate their businesses while also considering ethical practices reflect the balance of material and spiritual pursuits.
    • Families that prioritize both education and financial stability for their children demonstrate the significance of wealth and knowledge in achieving a prosperous life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of spiritual and material aspects of life.
    • It encourages individuals to engage in productive activities while also seeking wisdom and enlightenment.
    • In real-life scenarios, it highlights the importance of balancing work, wealth, and knowledge to lead a fulfilling and prosperous life.
    • Ultimately, it teaches that true prosperity comes from a harmonious blend of practical efforts and spiritual awareness.
