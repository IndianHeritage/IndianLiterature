आ दक्षिणा सृज्यते शुष्म्य आसदं वेति द्रुहो रक्षसः पाति जागृविः |हरिर उपशं कृतुते नभस पय उपस्तिरे चंवो ब्रह्म निरञ्जे ||
ā dakṣiṇā sṛjyate śuṣmy ā3sadaṁ veti druho rakṣasaḥ pāti jāgṛviḥ | harir opaśaṁ kṛṇute nabhas paya upastire camvo3r brahma nirṇije ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • dakṣiṇā: the south, often associated with auspiciousness
    • sṛjyate: is created or produced
    • śuṣmy: in the dry or barren state
    • āsadaṁ: the place of rest or abode
    • veti: knows or perceives
    • druho: deceit or treachery
    • rakṣasaḥ: demons or malevolent beings
    • pāti: protects or guards
    • jāgṛviḥ: the vigilant or watchful
    • harir: the divine or the Lord
    • opaśaṁ: brings peace or tranquility
    • kṛṇute: makes or creates
    • nabhas: the sky or atmosphere
    • paya: nourishment or sustenance
    • upastire: provides or offers
    • camvo3r: the essence or the core
    • brahma: the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit
    • nirṇije: in the pure or untainted state
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the creation and sustenance of life, emphasizing the protective and nurturing aspects of the divine.
    • The reference to "dakṣiṇā" suggests a direction that is often associated with prosperity and auspiciousness, indicating that good things come from the south.
    • The mention of "rakṣasaḥ" and "jāgṛviḥ" highlights the importance of vigilance against deceit and malevolent forces in life.
    • The divine presence, referred to as "harir," is portrayed as a source of peace and nourishment, suggesting that spiritual fulfillment is essential for a balanced life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to the importance of ethical practices and vigilance against deceitful practices. For instance, a company that prioritizes transparency and integrity is more likely to thrive.
    • In personal relationships, being watchful and protective against negative influences can lead to healthier interactions. This can be seen in friendships where individuals support each other against toxic behaviors.
    • On a spiritual level, seeking peace and nourishment through meditation or community service can lead to a more fulfilling life, reflecting the essence of "harir opaśaṁ kṛṇute."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, protection, and sustenance, reminding us of the divine's role in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the call for vigilance against negativity while embracing the nurturing aspects of spirituality.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek ethical paths, protect their well-being, and cultivate a sense of peace and fulfillment.
