अरमयः सरपसस् तराय कं तुर्वीतये च वय्याय च श्रुतिम् |नीचाऽ संतम् उद अनयः परावृजम् प्राण्डं श्रोणं श्रवयन सास्य उक्त्यः ||
aramayaḥ sarapasas tarāya kaṁ turvītaye ca vayyāya ca srutim | nīcā santam ud anayaḥ parāvṛjam prāndhaṁ śroṇaṁ śravayan sāsy ukthyaḥ ||
  • aramayaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "to bring peace" or "to create harmony." In real life, this can relate to efforts in conflict resolution, whether in personal relationships or larger societal issues.
  • sarapasas: This word suggests "to be free from obstacles" or "to overcome difficulties." For example, in a professional setting, this could refer to a team overcoming challenges to achieve a common goal.
  • tarāya: This can mean "to guide" or "to lead." In everyday life, this could be seen in mentorship roles where experienced individuals guide others in their personal or professional journeys.
  • kaṁ: This word translates to "who" or "whom," indicating a focus on individuals or groups. It emphasizes the importance of community and relationships in our lives.
  • turvītaye: This term can be understood as "to attain success" or "to achieve goals." In practical terms, this could relate to setting and achieving personal or professional objectives.
  • vayyāya: This word suggests "to be strong" or "to be powerful." In real life, this can be associated with personal empowerment and resilience in the face of adversity.
  • srutim: This translates to "hearing" or "listening." It highlights the importance of communication and understanding in relationships, whether personal or professional.
  • nīcā: This term means "low" or "humble." It serves as a reminder of the value of humility in our interactions with others.
  • santam: This word means "peaceful" or "calm." It emphasizes the importance of maintaining inner peace, which can be crucial in stressful situations.
  • ud anayaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "to uplift" or "to elevate." In real life, this could refer to efforts to support and uplift those around us, fostering a sense of community.
  • parāvṛjam: This term suggests "to protect" or "to safeguard." It can relate to the importance of protecting our environment, communities, and loved ones.
  • prāndhaṁ: This word means "to be enlightened" or "to gain knowledge." It highlights the significance of education and continuous learning in our lives.
  • śroṇaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "to hear" or "to listen." It reinforces the idea that active listening is essential for effective communication.
  • śravayan: This word means "to speak" or "to express." It emphasizes the importance of expressing thoughts and feelings openly and honestly.
  • sāsy ukthyaḥ: This phrase can be understood as "to cultivate" or "to nurture." It relates to the idea of nurturing relationships, ideas, and personal growth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of harmony, guidance, empowerment, and the importance of communication and humility. In real life, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as personal relationships, professional environments, and community interactions. By embodying these values, individuals can contribute to a more peaceful and supportive society, fostering growth and understanding among diverse groups. The significance of this mantra lies in its call to action for individuals to uplift themselves and others, creating a ripple effect of positivity and strength in the world.
