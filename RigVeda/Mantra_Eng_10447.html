अपेहि मनसस पतेऽप क्राम परश्चर |परो निरृत्या आ चक्ष्व बहुधा जीवतो मनः ||
apehi manasas pate 'pa krāma paraś cara | paro nirṛtyā ā cakṣva bahudhā jīvato manaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "apehi manasas pate 'pa krāma paraś cara | paro nirṛtyā ā cakṣva bahudhā jīvato manaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apehi: depart or move away
    • manasas: of the mind
    • pate: O Lord or master
    • 'pa: protect or guard
    • krāma: step or progress
    • paraś: other or external
    • cara: move or act
    • paro: beyond or higher
    • nirṛtyā: by misfortune or adversity
    • ā: towards or to
    • cakṣva: see or perceive
    • bahudhā: in many ways or variously
    • jīvato: of the living or life
    • manaḥ: mind
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a plea to the divine, asking for the removal of negative influences from the mind.
    • It emphasizes the importance of mental clarity and the need to protect one's thoughts from external disturbances.
    • The phrase "O Lord of the mind" signifies a recognition of a higher power that governs mental processes.
    • It acknowledges that misfortunes and adversities can cloud our judgment and perception.
    • The mantra encourages the seeker to look beyond these challenges and perceive life in its many dimensions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In stressful situations, such as during exams or job interviews, individuals often experience anxiety that clouds their thinking. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek mental clarity and focus.
    • In the face of adversity, such as losing a job or facing personal challenges, one can use this mantra to cultivate resilience and a broader perspective on life.
    • Practicing mindfulness and meditation can be seen as a way to embody the essence of this mantra, helping individuals to guard their minds against negativity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of mental well-being and the need to protect one's mind from external negativity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, especially in times of stress, adversity, or confusion.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of peace, clarity, and resilience, enabling them to navigate life's challenges more effectively.
