आ मे हवं नासत्याश्विनाः गच्छतं युवम् |मध्वः सोमस्य पीतये ||
ā me havaṁ nāsatyāśvinā gacchataṁ yuvam | madhvaḥ somasya pītaye ||
  • Mantra: "ā me havaṁ nāsatyāśvinā gacchataṁ yuvam | madhvaḥ somasya pītaye ||"
  • Translation: "May the youthful Ashvins, the divine twin horsemen, come to me. I offer this sweet (madhvaḥ) for the enjoyment of the Soma."
  • Explanation:
    • ā me havaṁ: This phrase translates to "may this come to me." It expresses a desire for divine presence and blessings.
    • nāsatyāśvinā: Refers to the Ashvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing powers and ability to bring prosperity. They symbolize youth, vitality, and the duality of nature.
    • gacchataṁ yuvam: "Come to me, O youthful ones." This is an invocation for the Ashvins to approach and bestow their blessings.
    • madhvaḥ: This word means "sweet" and can refer to the sweetness of life, joy, or offerings made to the deities.
    • somasya pītaye: "For the enjoyment of the Soma." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine ecstasy. It is often associated with the nourishment of the soul.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the invocation of the Ashvins can be seen as a call for health and vitality, much like how people today seek wellness through various means such as yoga, meditation, and healthy living.
    • The concept of offering something sweet can be related to the idea of gratitude and sharing joy with others, such as celebrating achievements with friends and family.
    • The pursuit of "Soma" can be likened to the search for experiences that bring joy and fulfillment, whether through art, nature, or community.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energies for health, vitality, and joy. It serves as a reminder to seek balance and nourishment in life, both physically and spiritually. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of gratitude, seek wellness, and share joy with others, reflecting the timeless values of connection and harmony found in the Vedic tradition.
