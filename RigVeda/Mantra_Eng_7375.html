नकीं वृधीक इन्द्र ते न सुषा न सुदा उता |नान्यस त्वच शूर वाघतः ||
nakīṁ vṛdhīka indra te na suṣā na sudā uta | nānyas tvac chūra vāghataḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Nakīṁ: Not at all
    • Vṛdhīka: Increase or growth
    • Indra: The king of gods, representing power and strength
    • Te: Your
    • Na: Not
    • Suṣā: Good or auspicious
    • Na: Not
    • Sudā: Sweet or pleasant
    • Uta: Also
    • Nānyas: No other
    • Tvac: Your
    • Chūra: Brave or valiant
    • Vāghataḥ: Speech or words
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea or invocation to Indra, the deity associated with strength and power, asking for an increase in strength and auspiciousness.
    • The repetition of "na" emphasizes the negation of any lack of growth or positive attributes, indicating a desire for abundance and prosperity.
    • The mention of "no other" suggests exclusivity in the request, highlighting the importance of Indra's blessings over others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be likened to a company seeking growth and success in a competitive market, invoking the strength and support of its leaders and stakeholders.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during challenging times, seeking inner strength and positive outcomes in their endeavors, such as exams or job interviews.
    • Communities may use this mantra during festivals or rituals, asking for the blessings of prosperity and strength from their deities to ensure a fruitful year ahead.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine strength and auspiciousness, which can be applied in various aspects of life, from personal growth to communal prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder that seeking support from higher powers can be a source of motivation and encouragement in overcoming challenges.
    • Ultimately, this mantra encourages individuals and communities to strive for growth and positivity, reinforcing the belief that with divine blessings, success is attainable.
