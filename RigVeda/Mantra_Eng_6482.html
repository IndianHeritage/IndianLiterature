याभिः सिन्धुम अवथा याभिस तूरवथा याभिर दशस्यथा कृविम् |मयो नो भूतोतिभिर मयोभुवः शिवाभिर असचद्विषः ||
yābhiḥ sindhum avatha yābhis tūrvatha yābhir daśasyathā krivim | mayo no bhūtotibhir mayobhuvaḥ śivābhir asacadviṣaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yābhiḥ: By whom
    • sindhum: The ocean or water
    • avatha: You have brought forth
    • yābhis: By whom (plural)
    • tūrvatha: You have made strong
    • yābhir: By whom (plural)
    • daśasyathā: The ten directions
    • krivim: The creative force or energy
    • mayo: By our essence
    • no: Our
    • bhūtotibhir: With the beings or entities
    • mayobhuvaḥ: Our existence
    • śivābhir: With auspiciousness
    • asacad: Not to be harmed
    • viṣaḥ: By poison or negativity
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine forces that govern the natural world, particularly the oceans and the energies that flow through them.
    • It emphasizes the importance of strength and protection, invoking the powers that create and sustain life.
    • The reference to the "ten directions" symbolizes the all-encompassing nature of these divine forces, suggesting that they are present in every aspect of existence.
    • The mantra also highlights the need for auspiciousness and the avoidance of negativity or harm, which is crucial in our daily lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In environmental conservation, this mantra can be seen as a call to respect and protect our oceans, which are vital for life on Earth.
    • In personal development, the invocation of strength and protection can be applied to overcoming challenges and ensuring mental well-being.
    • In community building, the idea of auspiciousness can guide efforts to create harmonious relationships and environments free from negativity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all life and the divine forces that support and protect us.
    • Its significance lies in its call for strength, protection, and auspiciousness, which are essential in navigating the complexities of life.
    • By applying the principles of this mantra in our daily lives, we can foster a more harmonious existence, both individually and collectively.
