यस्य शाश्वत पपिवां इन्द्र शत्रून अनानुकृत्या रण्या चकर्थ |स ते पुरंढिं तविषीम इयर्ति स ते मदाया सुत इन्द्र सोमः ||
yasya śaśvat papivām̐ indra śatrūn anānukṛtyā raṇyā cakartha | sa te puraṁdhiṁ taviṣīm iyarti sa te madāya suta indra somaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • śaśvat: eternal
    • papivām̐: who drinks
    • indra: Indra (the king of gods)
    • śatrūn: enemies
    • anānukṛtyā: without any effort
    • raṇyā: in battle
    • cakartha: he performed
    • sa: he
    • te: for you
    • puraṁdhiṁ: the stronghold
    • taviṣīm: powerful
    • iyarti: he moves
    • sa: he
    • te: for you
    • madāya: for intoxication
    • suta: the son
    • indra: Indra
    • somaḥ: the soma (a sacred drink)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power and prowess of Indra, the king of gods, who is often associated with strength, courage, and victory in battles.
    • The phrase "yasya śaśvat papivām̐ indra" suggests that Indra is eternally powerful and capable of overcoming enemies effortlessly.
    • The term "raṇyā cakartha" indicates that he performs great deeds in battle, showcasing his valor and leadership.
    • Furthermore, "sa te puraṁdhiṁ taviṣīm iyarti" implies that he moves towards the stronghold, symbolizing the pursuit of goals and overcoming obstacles.
    • The concluding part "sa te madāya suta indra somaḥ" emphasizes the importance of the soma, a drink that symbolizes divine inspiration and strength, which Indra consumes to enhance his capabilities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of Indra would be someone who can navigate challenges and lead their team to success without unnecessary struggle.
    • In sports, an athlete who performs exceptionally well under pressure, demonstrating skill and determination, can be likened to Indra in battle.
    • In personal life, individuals who face adversities with courage and resilience, drawing strength from their experiences, reflect the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of strength, leadership, and the ability to overcome challenges effortlessly.
    • It serves as a reminder that with the right mindset and resources (symbolized by soma), one can achieve greatness and triumph over adversities.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal endeavors, professional challenges, or community leadership, embodying the qualities of Indra can lead to success and fulfillment.
