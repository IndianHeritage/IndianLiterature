अभ्यर्ष बृहद् यशो मगवद्भ्यो ध्रुवं रयिम् |इषं स्तोत्रिभ्य आ भर ||
abhy arṣa bṛhad yaśo maghavadbhyo dhruvaṁ rayim | iṣaṁ stotṛbhya ā bhara ||
  • abhy arṣa: This phrase can be interpreted as "to those who have attained" or "to those who have achieved." It signifies the importance of recognizing and honoring those who have reached a certain level of wisdom or accomplishment.
  • bṛhad yaśo: Translated as "great fame" or "vast glory," this part emphasizes the value of reputation and the impact of one's actions on their legacy. In real life, this can relate to individuals who have made significant contributions to society, such as scientists, artists, or leaders.
  • maghavadbhyo: This term refers to "the powerful" or "the mighty." It acknowledges the strength and influence of those who possess great capabilities, whether in physical prowess, intellectual capacity, or spiritual depth.
  • dhruvaṁ rayim: This phrase means "steady wealth" or "constant prosperity." It highlights the pursuit of stability and abundance in life, which can be interpreted as both material wealth and spiritual richness.
  • iṣaṁ: This word translates to "the desire" or "the wish." It reflects the aspirations and goals that drive individuals to seek fulfillment in their lives.
  • stotṛbhya: Meaning "to the worshippers" or "to those who praise," this part emphasizes the importance of gratitude and recognition of those who contribute positively to our lives.
  • ā bhara: This phrase translates to "bring forth" or "bestow." It is a call to action, encouraging the sharing of blessings and resources with others.

Conclusion: The mantra "abhy arṣa bṛhad yaśo maghavadbhyo dhruvaṁ rayim | iṣaṁ stotṛbhya ā bhara ||" encapsulates the essence of honoring achievements, recognizing the powerful, and striving for steady prosperity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in workplaces where acknowledging the contributions of team members fosters a positive environment, or in communities where sharing resources leads to collective growth. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for gratitude, recognition, and the pursuit of a fulfilling life, encouraging individuals to uplift themselves and others in their journey towards success and well-being.
