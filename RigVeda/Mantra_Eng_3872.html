उद यत सहः सहस आजनिष्ट देदिष्ट इन्द्र इन्द्रियाणि विश्वा |प्राचोदयत सुदुघा वव्रे अन्तर विझ ज्योतिषा संववृतवत तमोऽवः ||
ud yat sahaḥ sahasa ājaniṣṭa dediṣṭa indra indriyāṇi viśvā | prācodayat sudughā vavre antar vi jyotiṣā saṁvavṛtvat tamo 'vaḥ ||
  • ud yat sahaḥ sahasa ājaniṣṭa dediṣṭa indra indriyāṇi viśvā
    • ud yat: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which rises" or "that which is elevated." It signifies the pursuit of higher knowledge and consciousness.
    • sahaḥ: This means "together" or "with." It emphasizes the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving goals.
    • sahasa: This translates to "with strength" or "powerfully." It suggests that one should approach challenges with vigor and determination.
    • ājaniṣṭa: This means "to create" or "to bring forth." It indicates the act of manifesting ideas or intentions into reality.
    • dediṣṭa: This can be understood as "desired" or "wished for." It reflects the aspirations and goals one aims to achieve.
    • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
    • indriyāṇi: This refers to the senses or faculties. It highlights the importance of perception and awareness in our experiences.
    • viśvā: This means "all" or "universal." It signifies inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • prācodayat sudughā vavre antar vi jyotiṣā saṁvavṛtvat tamo 'vaḥ
    • prācodayat: This means "to inspire" or "to guide." It suggests the need for divine guidance in our endeavors.
    • sudughā: This can be interpreted as "well-chosen" or "excellent." It emphasizes the importance of making wise choices.
    • vavre: This means "to choose" or "to select." It indicates the act of making decisions that align with one's goals.
    • antar: This translates to "within" or "inside." It signifies introspection and the importance of inner wisdom.
    • vi: This means "by" or "through." It indicates the means or methods through which one achieves their goals.
    • jyotiṣā: This refers to "light" or "illumination." It symbolizes knowledge, clarity, and enlightenment.
    • saṁvavṛtvat: This means "to surround" or "to encompass." It suggests the idea of being enveloped in a positive environment.
    • tamo 'vaḥ: This translates to "to dispel darkness." It signifies the removal of ignorance and negativity.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of striving for higher consciousness and unity in our pursuits. It encourages individuals to seek guidance, make wise choices, and dispel ignorance through knowledge and awareness. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal development, where individuals seek to elevate their understanding and awareness.
  • In teamwork and collaboration, where unity and collective strength lead to success.
  • In decision-making processes, where wisdom and introspection guide choices.
  • In educational settings, where the pursuit of knowledge dispels ignorance and fosters enlightenment.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the power of collective effort in achieving higher goals.
