इमानी वाम भागधेयानि सिस्रत इन्द्रावरुणा प्र महे सुतेषु वाम |यज्ञे-यज्ञे ह सवना भुरण्यथो यत सुन्वते यजमानाय शिक्षथः ||
imāni vām bhāgadheyāni sisrata indrāvaruṇā pra mahe suteṣu vām | yajñe-yajñe ha savanā bhuraṇyatho yat sunvate yajamānāya śikṣathaḥ ||
  • Translation: "These are your shares, Indra and Varuna, which we are seeking in the great offerings. In the sacrifices, may the Savanas (the offerings) come to you, and may those who are performing the sacrifices be instructed."
  • Key Terms:
    • Indra: The king of gods, associated with thunder, rain, and war.
    • Varuna: The god of water and celestial ocean, representing moral and cosmic order.
    • Savanā: Refers to the offerings made during sacrifices.
    • Yajña: A Vedic ritual of offerings accompanied by chanting of Vedic mantras.
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine blessings from Indra and Varuna during rituals.
    • It highlights the communal aspect of sacrifices, where the offerings are not just for individual gain but for the collective benefit.
    • The invocation of Indra and Varuna signifies the need for both strength (Indra) and harmony (Varuna) in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, community festivals often involve collective rituals that seek blessings for prosperity and harmony, similar to the yajñas of the Vedic period.
    • Environmental movements can be seen as a contemporary form of seeking blessings from nature (akin to Varuna) for sustainable living.
    • In personal life, individuals often seek guidance and strength during challenging times, reflecting the essence of invoking divine support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and blessings in our endeavors.
    • It teaches us the value of community and collective effort in achieving common goals.
    • In real life, it encourages us to maintain a balance between strength and harmony, reminding us that our actions should benefit not just ourselves but also the larger community and environment.
