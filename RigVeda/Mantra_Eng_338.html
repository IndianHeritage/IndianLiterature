तं त्वा वयं विश्ववारा शास्महे पुरुहूत |सखे वसो जरतृभ्यः ||
taṁ tvā vayaṁ viśvavārā śāsmahe puruhūta | sakhe vaso jaritṛbhyaḥ ||
  • taṁ tvā vayaṁ viśvavārā: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke you, O Universal Guardian." Here, "taṁ" refers to "you," "tvā" means "to you," "vayaṁ" translates to "we," and "viśvavārā" signifies "the one who protects all." This highlights the idea of seeking protection and guidance from a higher power that encompasses the universe.
  • śāsmahe puruhūta: This part translates to "We offer our prayers to the one who is invoked by many." "śāsmahe" means "we offer," and "puruhūta" refers to "the one who is called upon by many." This indicates the importance of collective worship and the acknowledgment of a divine presence that is revered by various beings.
  • sakhe vaso jaritṛbhyaḥ: This can be interpreted as "O friend, grant us strength." "sakhe" means "O friend," "vaso" translates to "grant," and "jaritṛbhyaḥ" refers to "strength or vitality." This emphasizes the need for support and strength in our endeavors, suggesting that we are not alone in our struggles.

  • Real-Life Example: In a community setting, this mantra can be applied during gatherings or rituals where people come together to seek guidance and protection for their collective well-being. For instance, during a festival, individuals may chant this mantra to invoke blessings for harmony and safety within their community.
  • Significance: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine protection and strength. It emphasizes the importance of community and collective prayer, reminding us that we are part of a larger universe and that our actions are interconnected.
  • Application in Real Life: This mantra can be relevant in various situations, such as during challenging times when individuals or communities face adversity. By invoking this mantra, people can foster a sense of unity and resilience, encouraging them to support one another and seek strength from a higher power.

  • Conclusion: The mantra "taṁ tvā vayaṁ viśvavārā śāsmahe puruhūta | sakhe vaso jaritṛbhyaḥ" serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of seeking protection and strength from the divine while fostering a sense of community. Its significance lies in its ability to unite individuals in prayer and support, making it applicable in various real-life scenarios where collective strength and guidance are needed.
