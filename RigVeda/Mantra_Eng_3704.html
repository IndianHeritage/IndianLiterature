आ ते अग्न ऋचः हविः शुक्रस्य शोचिषस पते |सुश्चन्द्र दसम विश्वते हव्यवाट तुभ्यं हूयत इषं स्तोत्रिभ्य आ भर ||
ā te agna ṛcā haviḥ śukrasya śociṣas pate | suścandra dasma viśpate havyavāṭ tubhyaṁ hūyata iṣaṁ stotṛbhya ā bhara ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā te: To you
    • agna: O Agni (the fire god)
    • ṛcā: With hymns
    • haviḥ: Offerings
    • śukrasya: Of the pure
    • śociṣas: Of the shining
    • pate: O Lord
    • suścandra: With good moonlight
    • dasma: From the place
    • viśpate: O Lord of all
    • havyavāṭ: To the one who is worthy of offerings
    • tubhyaṁ: To you
    • hūyata: Invoked
    • iṣaṁ: The power
    • stotṛbhya: To the worshippers
    • ā: May it come
    • bhara: Fill
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The invocation of Agni with hymns signifies the importance of rituals and offerings in Vedic culture.
    • The terms "śukrasya" and "śociṣas" highlight the purity and brilliance associated with Agni, suggesting that the offerings made should be of the highest quality.
    • "Suścandra" refers to the auspiciousness and brightness, akin to the moonlight, which symbolizes clarity and positivity in our endeavors.
    • The phrase "havyavāṭ tubhyaṁ" emphasizes the idea of offering to the divine, indicating that the offerings should be made with devotion and sincerity.
    • The mantra concludes with a request for the divine power to fill the worshippers, suggesting a desire for spiritual nourishment and blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied during rituals or ceremonies where fire is used, such as weddings or religious festivals, symbolizing the presence of Agni as a witness and purifier.
    • Individuals can invoke this mantra during personal meditation or prayer, seeking clarity and purity in their thoughts and actions.
    • In a broader context, it can be seen as a reminder to approach life with sincerity and to make offerings of our best efforts in our work and relationships.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of purity, devotion, and the role of divine energy in our lives.
    • It teaches us to approach our actions with sincerity and to seek blessings for clarity and positivity.
    • In essence, it serves as a reminder to honor the divine in our daily lives and to make offerings of our best selves to the world around us.
