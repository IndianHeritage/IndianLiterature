इमा ते वाजिन्न अवमार्जनानिमा शफानां सन्तुर निधाना |अत्रा ते भद्रा रशनाऽपश्यं ऋतस्य या अभिरक्षन्ति गोपाः ||
imā te vājinn avamārjanānīmā śaphānāṁ sanitur nidhānā | atrā te bhadrā raśanā apaśyam ṛtasya yā abhirakṣanti gopāḥ ||
  • imā te vājinn avamārjanānīmā: This phrase can be translated as "These are your offerings, O Lord, which are to be accepted." The term "vājinn" refers to offerings or gifts, often associated with strength and vitality.
  • śaphānāṁ sanitur nidhānā: This part means "the treasures of the well-being." "Śaphānāṁ" refers to abundance or prosperity, while "nidhānā" signifies a storehouse or repository. Together, they imply a source of wealth and nourishment.
  • atrā te bhadrā raśanā: This translates to "Here is the auspicious cord for you." "Bhadrā" means auspicious or beneficial, and "raśanā" refers to a cord or bond, symbolizing connection and support.
  • apaśyam ṛtasya yā abhirakṣanti gopāḥ: This can be interpreted as "the guardians who protect the truth." "Ṛtasya" refers to truth or cosmic order, while "gopāḥ" means protectors or guardians. This emphasizes the importance of safeguarding truth and righteousness.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of offerings, prosperity, auspiciousness, and the protection of truth. In real life, it can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • Spiritual Practices: The mantra can be recited during rituals to invoke blessings for abundance and protection.
  • Community Support: It highlights the importance of supporting one another in a community, akin to how guardians protect their flock.
  • Personal Growth: The emphasis on truth and auspiciousness can inspire individuals to seek honesty and integrity in their lives.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of offerings, prosperity, and the guardianship of truth, encouraging individuals and communities to uphold these values in their daily lives.
