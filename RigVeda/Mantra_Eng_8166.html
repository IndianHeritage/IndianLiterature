आ पवस्व हिरण्यवद अश्वावत सोम वीरवत |वाजं गोमन्तम आ भर ||
ā pavasva hiraṇyavad aśvāvat soma vīravat | vājaṁ gomantam ā bhara ||
  • ā pavasva: This phrase can be interpreted as "come forth" or "approach." It signifies an invitation or a call to a divine presence or energy.
  • hiraṇyavad: This word means "golden" or "radiant." It symbolizes wealth, prosperity, and the brilliance of divine light.
  • aśvāvat: This term translates to "like a horse." In Vedic literature, horses are often associated with strength, speed, and vitality, representing the power of life and energy.
  • soma: Soma refers to a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine ecstasy. It is often associated with the moon and represents nourishment for the soul.
  • vīravat: This word means "brave" or "heroic." It emphasizes courage and valor, qualities that are essential for overcoming challenges.
  • vājaṁ: This term translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies abundance and the resources necessary for a fulfilling life.
  • gomantam: This word means "possessing cows." In ancient Vedic society, cows were a symbol of wealth and sustenance, representing nourishment and prosperity.
  • ā bhara: This phrase means "bring forth" or "bestow." It is a call to manifest abundance and blessings in one's life.

In summary, this mantra is a powerful invocation for prosperity, strength, and divine blessings. It calls upon the energies of wealth, courage, and nourishment, inviting them into one's life. The imagery of gold, horses, and cows reflects the values of abundance and vitality that were central to Vedic culture.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or prayer to attract positive energies and cultivate a mindset of abundance and courage.
  • Business and Career: Entrepreneurs and professionals may use this mantra to invoke prosperity and success in their ventures, aligning their efforts with the energies of wealth and strength.
  • Community and Relationships: The mantra can be used in group settings to foster a sense of unity and shared purpose, encouraging collaboration and mutual support.

Ultimately, the significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to seek and manifest abundance, courage, and divine blessings in their lives, fostering a holistic approach to personal and communal well-being.
