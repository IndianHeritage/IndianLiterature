त्वां रिहन्ति मातरो हरिम पवित्रे अद्रुहः |वत्सं जातं न धेनवः पवमान विधर्मणि ||
tvāṁ rihanti mātaro harim pavitre adruhaḥ | vatsaṁ jātaṁ na dhenavaḥ pavamāna vidharmaṇi ||
  • tvāṁ rihanti mātaro: This phrase translates to "the mothers (or nurturing figures) cherish you." Here, "mātaro" refers to mothers or nurturing entities, symbolizing care and protection. In real life, this can be seen in the unconditional love and support that mothers provide to their children, guiding them through life's challenges.
  • harim pavitre adruhaḥ: This means "the pure one, free from malice." "Harim" often refers to a divine or pure being, while "adruhaḥ" signifies the absence of enmity or malice. In everyday life, this can be related to the importance of maintaining purity of heart and intentions, promoting kindness and compassion in our interactions with others.
  • vatsaṁ jātaṁ na dhenavaḥ: This translates to "the calf that is born is not to be harmed." "Vatsaṁ" refers to a calf, symbolizing innocence and new beginnings, while "dhenavaḥ" refers to the nurturing aspect of motherhood, particularly in the context of cows. This can be seen in the way we protect and nurture the young in society, whether they are children, animals, or even new ideas and projects.
  • pavamāna vidharmaṇi: This means "the one who is pure and upholds righteousness." "Pavamāna" signifies purity, and "vidharmaṇi" refers to the adherence to dharma or righteousness. In real life, this can be exemplified by individuals who strive to live ethically and uphold moral values, contributing positively to their communities.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of nurturing, purity, and righteousness in our lives. It highlights the role of mothers and caregivers in fostering a safe and loving environment, free from malice. In a broader context, it calls for the protection of the innocent and the importance of upholding ethical values. This mantra can be applied in real-life scenarios such as parenting, community service, and personal development, reminding us to cultivate love, kindness, and integrity in our actions and relationships.
