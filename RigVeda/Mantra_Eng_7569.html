इन्द्र शुद्धो न आगहि शुद्धः शुद्धाभिर ऊतिभिः |शुद्धो रयिं न धारणय शुद्धो ममद्धि सोम्यः ||
indra śuddho na ā gahi śuddhaḥ śuddhābhir ūtibhiḥ | śuddho rayiṁ ni dhāraya śuddho mamaddhi somyaḥ ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
  • Śuddho: Means pure or clean, indicating a state of clarity and righteousness.
  • Na ā gahi: Translates to "do not grasp" or "do not hold," suggesting a sense of detachment or non-attachment.
  • Śuddhaḥ: Again emphasizes purity, reinforcing the idea of being untainted or free from impurities.
  • Śuddhābhir ūtibhiḥ: Refers to the pure energies or forces, indicating that one should be guided by pure intentions and actions.
  • Rayiṁ: Means wealth or prosperity, often associated with material and spiritual abundance.
  • Ni dhāraya: Translates to "hold firmly," suggesting the importance of maintaining and nurturing one's wealth or resources.
  • Śuddho mamaddhi somyaḥ: Implies that purity leads to a state of calmness and tranquility, where one can achieve true happiness and fulfillment.

The mantra emphasizes the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions. It suggests that true strength and leadership, as embodied by Indra, come from a place of purity and clarity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios:


  • Personal Relationships: Maintaining purity in intentions can lead to healthier and more fulfilling relationships. For example, being honest and transparent with loved ones fosters trust and connection.
  • Professional Life: In the workplace, leading with integrity and ethical practices can create a positive environment and inspire others. A leader who acts with purity of purpose can motivate their team to achieve collective goals.
  • Spiritual Growth: Engaging in practices that promote mental and emotional clarity, such as meditation or mindfulness, can help individuals cultivate a sense of peace and well-being.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of purity in all aspects of life. By embodying the qualities of Indra and striving for clarity and righteousness, individuals can navigate their lives with strength and purpose, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
