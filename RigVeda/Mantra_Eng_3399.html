उत स्मा सद्य इति परि शशमानाय सुन्वते |पुरू चिन मंहसे वसु ||
uta smā sadya it pari śaśamānāya sunvate | purū cin maṁhase vasu ||
  • uta - This word can be translated as "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition.
  • smā - This term means "to be" or "to exist," suggesting a state of being or presence.
  • sadya - This word translates to "immediately" or "right now," emphasizing urgency or promptness.
  • it - This can mean "that" or "there," often used to refer to something previously mentioned or understood.
  • pari - This means "around" or "surrounding," indicating a sense of encompassing or inclusion.
  • śaśamānāya - This term refers to "the one who is shining" or "radiant," often associated with brightness or enlightenment.
  • sunvate - This means "to speak" or "to declare," indicating communication or expression.
  • purū - This translates to "many" or "numerous," suggesting abundance or variety.
  • cin - This word means "to know" or "to perceive," indicating awareness or understanding.
  • maṁhase - This term refers to "greatness" or "magnitude," often associated with power or significance.
  • vasu - This means "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating material or spiritual abundance.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for immediate recognition and communication of the radiant qualities that surround us, emphasizing the abundance of knowledge and prosperity in our lives. It suggests that by acknowledging and expressing these qualities, we can enhance our existence and the lives of those around us.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal development, recognizing and communicating one's strengths and qualities can lead to greater self-awareness and confidence.
  • In community building, fostering open communication about shared values and resources can create a sense of unity and abundance.
  • In professional settings, acknowledging the contributions of team members can enhance collaboration and lead to greater success.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on immediate recognition and communication of the positive qualities that surround us. By applying its teachings in our daily lives, we can cultivate a sense of abundance, foster meaningful connections, and enhance our overall well-being.
