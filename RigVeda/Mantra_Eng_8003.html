यो अत्य इव मृज्यते गोभिः मदाय हARYतः |तं गीरभिः वासयामसि ||
yo atya iva mṛjyate gobhir madāya haryataḥ | taṁ gīrbhir vāsayāmasi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • atyā: exceedingly
    • iva: like
    • mṛjyate: is worshipped or revered
    • gobhir: by the cows (or by the wise)
    • madāya: for the sake of joy or intoxication
    • haryataḥ: of the horse (or the swift)
    • taṁ: him
    • gīrbhir: with hymns or praises
    • vāsayāmasi: we invoke or we make manifest
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine entity who is revered and worshipped, akin to the way cows are cherished in agrarian societies.
    • The reference to "gobhir" (cows) symbolizes nourishment, sustenance, and the nurturing aspect of life, which is essential for growth and prosperity.
    • The phrase "madāya haryataḥ" suggests a connection to joy and vitality, possibly indicating that this divine presence brings happiness and energy, much like the spirited nature of horses.
    • Finally, the invocation "taṁ gīrbhir vāsayāmasi" emphasizes the importance of expressing reverence through hymns and praises, highlighting the role of language and music in spiritual practices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, cows are often seen as sacred and are treated with great respect. This reflects the idea of reverence for life and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • Horses, known for their strength and speed, symbolize freedom and vitality. In many cultures, they are celebrated in festivals and rituals, representing the joy of life and the spirit of adventure.
    • Music and poetry play a crucial role in many spiritual traditions. For instance, in modern practices, chanting and singing are used to create a sense of community and connection to the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of reverence, joy, and the importance of expressing gratitude through words and music.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to honor the sources of nourishment and joy in our lives, whether they be people, animals, or the divine.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages us to cultivate a sense of gratitude and appreciation for the interconnectedness of all life, fostering a harmonious existence.
