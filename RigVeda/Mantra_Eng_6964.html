ये पातयन्ते अज्मभिर गिरिणां स्नुभिर एषाम् |यज्ञं महिष्वणीनां सुम्नं तुविष्वणीनाम् प्राध्वरे ||
ye pātayante ajmabhir girīṇāṁ snubhir eṣām | yajñam mahiṣvaṇīnāṁ sumnaṁ tuviṣvaṇīnām prādhvare ||
  • Translation: "Those who are the protectors of the mountains, who are nourished by the waters, may the great ones grant us auspiciousness and prosperity."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: Those who
    • pātayante: protect or nourish
    • ajmabhir: by the waters
    • girīṇāṁ: of the mountains
    • snubhir: nourished or sustained
    • eṣām: of these
    • yajñam: the great ones or divine beings
    • mahiṣvaṇīnāṁ: of the great ones
    • sumnaṁ: auspiciousness
    • tuviṣvaṇīnām: of those who are prosperous
    • prādhvare: grant or bestow

  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of nature, particularly mountains and water, in sustaining life.
    • Mountains are often seen as protectors, providing stability and shelter, while water is essential for nourishment and growth.
    • The invocation of "great ones" signifies a plea for blessings from higher powers or deities, seeking prosperity and auspiciousness in life.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, mountains are revered as sacred spaces. For instance, the Himalayas are considered holy in Hinduism, and people often seek blessings from these natural formations.
    • Water conservation efforts, such as rainwater harvesting, reflect the understanding of water's vital role in sustaining communities and agriculture.
    • In modern society, environmental movements advocate for the protection of natural resources, aligning with the mantra's call for respect towards nature.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature and humanity. It highlights the need to respect and protect our environment for the well-being of future generations.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals and communities to engage in sustainable practices, fostering a harmonious relationship with the earth.
    • Ultimately, the mantra calls for a collective effort to seek blessings for prosperity and auspiciousness, rooted in a deep respect for the natural world.
