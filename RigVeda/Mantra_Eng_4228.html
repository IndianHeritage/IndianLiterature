अधā हि काव्यां युवं दक्षस्य पूर्वभिर अद्भुतā |नि केतुनā जनानां चिकेते पूतदक्षसā ||
adhā hi kāvyā yuvaṁ dakṣasya pūrbhir adbhutā | ni ketunā janānāṁ cikethe pūtadakṣasā ||
  • adhā hi kāvyā: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, the poetry (or creation) is below." Here, "adhā" means below or beneath, and "kāvyā" refers to poetry or creative expression. This suggests that the essence of creativity is grounded in something deeper.
  • yuvaṁ dakṣasya: "yuvaṁ" means young or youthful, and "dakṣasya" refers to skillful or capable. Together, this implies that the youthful and skilled individuals are the ones who can bring forth creativity and innovation.
  • pūrbhir adbhutā: "pūrbhir" means from the past or ancient, and "adbhutā" translates to wonderful or extraordinary. This indicates that the extraordinary aspects of creativity are rooted in ancient traditions and knowledge.
  • ni ketunā janānāṁ: "ni ketunā" can be interpreted as "by the guidance" or "by the direction," while "janānāṁ" means of the people. This suggests that the guidance of skilled individuals is essential for the community.
  • cikethe pūtadakṣasā: "cikethe" means to create or to manifest, and "pūtadakṣasā" refers to those who are pure and skilled. This emphasizes that pure and skilled individuals are responsible for the creation and manifestation of extraordinary things.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of creativity and skill, particularly among the youth, and highlights the significance of ancient wisdom in fostering innovation. In real life, this can be seen in various fields such as art, science, and technology, where young, skilled individuals draw inspiration from historical knowledge to create groundbreaking work. The mantra serves as a reminder that the roots of creativity lie in the past, and that guidance from experienced individuals is crucial for the development of society. It encourages us to value both tradition and innovation, recognizing that they are interconnected in the pursuit of excellence.
