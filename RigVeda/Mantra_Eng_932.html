को अग्निम् ईट्टे हविषा घृतेन स्रुचाऽयजाता ऋतुभिः ध्रुवैभिः |कस्मै देवाऽआ वहान् आशु होमा को मंसते वीतिहोत्रः सुदेवः ||
ko agnim īṭṭe haviṣā ghṛtena srucā yajātā ṛtubhir dhruvebhiḥ | kasmai devā ā vahān āśu homa ko maṁsate vītihotraḥ sudevaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ko: Who
    • agnim: Fire
    • īṭṭe: Should be invoked
    • haviṣā: With offerings
    • ghṛtena: With ghee
    • srucā: With a ladle
    • yajātā: Is being worshipped
    • ṛtubhir: With the seasons
    • dhruvebhiḥ: With fixed principles
    • kasmai: To whom
    • devā: The gods
    • ā vahān: May they come
    • āśu: Quickly
    • homa: The sacrificial offering
    • ko: Who
    • maṁsate: Is to be worshipped
    • vītihotraḥ: The one who performs the sacrifice
    • sudevaḥ: The good god
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a call to invoke the divine fire (Agni) with offerings, particularly ghee, which is considered sacred in Vedic rituals.
    • The phrase "who should be invoked" suggests a deep inquiry into the nature of divinity and the appropriate way to connect with it.
    • The mention of "with the seasons" indicates the cyclical nature of life and the importance of aligning rituals with natural rhythms.
    • The invocation of gods to come quickly emphasizes the urgency and sincerity of the worshipper's intent.
    • The term "sacrificial offering" (homa) reflects the practice of making offerings to the divine, which is a common theme in many cultures, such as lighting candles in Christianity or making offerings in Buddhism.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, people may perform rituals during significant life events, such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies, invoking blessings from higher powers.
    • Environmental awareness can be seen as a contemporary application of the mantra's emphasis on aligning with nature, where individuals and communities engage in sustainable practices to honor the earth.
    • In personal development, the idea of invoking one's inner fire (passion, motivation) with dedication and discipline can be likened to the ritualistic aspects of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of ritual, intention, and connection with the divine, emphasizing that offerings made with sincerity can invoke blessings and guidance.
    • Its application in real life can be seen in various forms of worship, personal growth, and environmental consciousness, reminding us of our interconnectedness with the universe.
    • Ultimately, it teaches that through dedication and respect for the natural order, we can cultivate a meaningful relationship with the divine and the world around us.
