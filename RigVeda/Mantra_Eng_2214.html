सत्रासाहो जनभक्षो जनंसहश च्यवनो युध्मो अनु जोषम् उक्षितः |वृतं चयः सहुरिर विक्ष्व आरित इन्द्रस्य वोचं प्र कृतानि वीर्याः ||
satrāsāho janabhakṣo janaṁsahaś cyavano yudhmo anu joṣam ukṣitaḥ | vṛtaṁcayaḥ sahurir vikṣv ārita indrasya vocam pra kṛtāni vīryā ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • satrāsāho: This can be interpreted as "one who is strong in battle" or "warrior-like strength."
    • janabhakṣo: Refers to "one who consumes or protects the people."
    • janaṁsahaś: Indicates "with the people" or "together with the community."
    • cyavano: Can be understood as "the one who is wise" or "intelligent."
    • yudhmo: Means "in battle" or "warrior."
    • anu joṣam: Translates to "following the path" or "in accordance with the rules."
    • ukṣitaḥ: Means "nurtured" or "cultivated."
    • vṛtaṁcayaḥ: Refers to "those who are gathered" or "assembled."
    • sahurir: Indicates "the strong one" or "the powerful."
    • vikṣv: Means "to see" or "to observe."
    • ārita: Refers to "the one who is invoked" or "called upon."
    • indrasya: Relates to "of Indra," the king of gods in Vedic tradition.
    • vocam: Means "I speak" or "I declare."
    • pra kṛtāni: Refers to "the deeds done" or "actions performed."
    • vīryā: Means "strength" or "valor."
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, wisdom, and community in overcoming challenges.
    • The phrase "satrāsāho janabhakṣo" suggests that a true leader or warrior is one who not only possesses strength but also protects and nurtures the people.
    • It highlights the significance of collective effort, as indicated by "janaṁsahaś," which means working together with the community.
    • The mantra also speaks to the importance of wisdom in leadership, as seen in "cyavano," suggesting that intelligence is crucial in navigating conflicts.
    • Furthermore, "anu joṣam ukṣitaḥ" implies that actions should be in accordance with established principles and nurtured for growth.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies the need for divine support and guidance in endeavors, especially in times of conflict.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of strength and wisdom can inspire their team to work together towards common goals, much like a warrior leading their troops.
    • In community service, individuals who protect and nurture their community, such as social workers or volunteers, exemplify the essence of "janabhakṣo."
    • During times of crisis, such as natural disasters, leaders who invoke collective action and wisdom can effectively mobilize resources and support, reflecting the mantra's teachings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the qualities necessary for effective leadership and community building.
    • Its significance lies in the call for strength, wisdom, and unity in facing challenges, whether in personal, professional, or communal contexts.
    • By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and resilient society.
