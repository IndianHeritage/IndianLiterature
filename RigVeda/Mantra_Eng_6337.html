इन्द्र त्वं अवितेद् असीत्था स्तुवतो अद्रिवः |ऋताद् इयर्मि ते धियम् मनोयुजम् ||
indra tvam avited asītthā stuvato adrivaḥ | ṛtād iyarmi te dhiyam manoyujam ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Tvam: Means "you," addressing Indra directly, indicating a personal connection or invocation.
  • Avited: Translates to "you who are invoked" or "you who are called upon," suggesting the act of seeking Indra's presence or assistance.
  • Asītthā: Means "you have been established" or "you have taken your place," indicating Indra's authority and position in the cosmos.
  • Stuvato: Refers to "those who praise" or "the worshippers," highlighting the importance of devotion and recognition of divine power.
  • Adrivaḥ: Translates to "the one who is beyond the ordinary," emphasizing Indra's transcendental nature and superiority over all beings.
  • Ṛtād: Means "from the truth" or "from the cosmic order," indicating that the invocation is rooted in universal principles and righteousness.
  • Iyarmi: Translates to "I seek" or "I desire," expressing the speaker's intention to connect with Indra.
  • Te: Means "your," indicating possession or belonging, reinforcing the connection to Indra.
  • Dhiyam: Refers to "intellect" or "understanding," suggesting a desire for wisdom and clarity.
  • Manoyujam: Translates to "the mind that is focused" or "the mind that is directed," indicating the importance of concentration and intention in spiritual practice.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of invoking divine guidance and wisdom, particularly from Indra, who represents strength and leadership. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in moments of decision-making, leadership roles, or when seeking clarity in challenging situations. By recognizing and honoring the divine forces at play, individuals can cultivate a deeper understanding of their own intentions and actions, aligning them with the greater cosmic order. This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of devotion, the importance of seeking wisdom, and the need for a focused mind in achieving one's goals.
