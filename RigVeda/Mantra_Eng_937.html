गोमातरो यच छुभयन्ते अञ्जिभिस तनूषु शुभ्रा दधिरे विरुक्मतः |बाधन्ते विश्वम् अभिमातिनम् अप वर्त्मान्य एषाम् अनु रीयते घृतम् ||
gomātaro yac chubhayante añjibhis tanūṣu śubhrā dadhire virukmataḥ | bādhante viśvam abhimātinam apa vartmāny eṣām anu rīyate ghṛtam ||
  • gomātaro: This term can be broken down into "go" meaning cow and "mātaro" meaning mothers. In Vedic culture, cows are often revered and considered sacred, symbolizing nourishment and abundance.
  • yac: This translates to "that which" or "whatever." It indicates a reference to something specific that follows.
  • chubhayante: This word means "to shine" or "to be beautiful." It suggests a quality of radiance or positivity.
  • añjibhis: This term refers to "with offerings" or "with adornments." It implies the act of honoring or beautifying something.
  • tanūṣu: This means "in the bodies" or "in the forms." It indicates the physical manifestation of something.
  • śubhrā: This translates to "pure" or "auspicious." It denotes a state of being that is favorable and positive.
  • dadhire: This means "they hold" or "they possess." It indicates a sense of ownership or containment.
  • virukmataḥ: This term can be interpreted as "from the wise" or "from the knowledgeable." It suggests a source of wisdom or insight.
  • bādhante: This means "they obstruct" or "they hinder." It indicates a force that prevents or challenges something.
  • viśvam: This translates to "the universe" or "all beings." It encompasses everything that exists.
  • abhimātinam: This term means "of those who are dear" or "beloved." It indicates a connection to something cherished or valued.
  • apa: This means "away" or "from." It suggests a separation or removal.
  • vartmāny: This translates to "paths" or "ways." It indicates the routes or methods one may take.
  • eṣām: This means "of these" or "of those." It refers back to the subjects previously mentioned.
  • anu: This translates to "following" or "after." It indicates a sequence or continuation.
  • rīyate: This means "is enjoyed" or "is experienced." It suggests a sense of fulfillment or satisfaction.
  • ghṛtam: This term refers to "ghee" or "clarified butter." In Vedic rituals, ghee is often used as an offering and symbolizes purity and nourishment.

In summary, this mantra speaks to the reverence for the sacredness of life and the interconnectedness of all beings. It highlights the importance of purity, wisdom, and the nurturing aspects of existence, symbolized by the cow and ghee. The mantra suggests that through honoring these qualities, one can experience a fulfilling and auspicious life.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as:

  • Environmental Awareness: Just as the cow is revered for its nurturing qualities, we can learn to respect and protect our environment, recognizing the interconnectedness of all life forms.
  • Community and Relationships: The idea of "beloved" beings encourages us to cherish our relationships and foster a sense of community, promoting harmony and support among individuals.
  • Personal Growth: The emphasis on wisdom and purity can inspire individuals to seek knowledge and cultivate a pure heart, leading to personal development and fulfillment.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the values of respect, love, and wisdom in our daily lives, guiding us towards a more harmonious existence.
