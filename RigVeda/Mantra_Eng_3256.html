स्तुत इन्द्रो मगवाऽयद ध वृत्राऽभूरिण्य एको अप्रतिनी हन्ति |अस्य प्रियः जरीताऽयस्य शरमन नकिर्देवाः वारयन्ते न मर्ताः ||
stuta indro maghavā yad dha vṛtrā bhūrīṇy eko apratīni hanti | asya priyo jaritā yasya śarman nakir devā vārayante na martāḥ ||
  • Stuta Indro Maghavā: This phrase refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature, who is praised and revered. "Stuta" means "praised," and "Maghavā" signifies his greatness and power.
  • Yad Dha Vṛtrā Bhūrīṇy: This part speaks of Indra's victory over Vritra, a serpent or dragon that represents chaos and obstacles. "Vṛtrā" symbolizes the challenges we face in life, while "bhūrīṇy" indicates the abundance or multitude of these challenges.
  • Eko Apratīni Hanti: Here, "eko" means "one" or "single," and "apratīni" refers to the unyielding or invincible. This suggests that Indra, as a singular force, can overcome numerous challenges effortlessly.
  • Asya Priyo Jaritā: This phrase indicates that Indra is dear to those who invoke him. "Jaritā" refers to the one who brings forth or creates, emphasizing the nurturing aspect of Indra.
  • Yasya Śarman Nakir: "Yasya" means "whose," and "śarman" refers to happiness or shelter. This implies that those who seek refuge in Indra find joy and protection.
  • Devā Vārayante Na Martāḥ: This part translates to "the gods protect him, not mortals." It highlights the divine protection that comes from aligning oneself with higher powers, rather than relying solely on human efforts.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the power of divine intervention in overcoming life's challenges. It illustrates the importance of seeking guidance and support from higher forces, particularly in times of adversity.
  • Real-Life Application: In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various situations, such as during personal struggles, professional challenges, or when facing societal issues. For instance, individuals may recite this mantra during difficult times to invoke strength and resilience, much like how athletes prepare mentally before a competition or how leaders seek wisdom in decision-making.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the strength that comes from faith and the importance of seeking divine support. It encourages individuals to recognize their challenges and to trust in a higher power for guidance and protection, ultimately leading to personal growth and resilience.
