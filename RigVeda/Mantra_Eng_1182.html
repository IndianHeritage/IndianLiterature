यद इन्द्राग्नī मदथः स्वे दुरोणे यद ब्रह्मणि राजनि वा यजत्रा |अतः परि वृषणाव् आ हि यातम अथ सोमस्य पिबतं सुतस्य ||
yad indrāgnī madathaḥ sve duroṇe yad brahmaṇi rājani vā yajatrā | ataḥ pari vṛṣaṇāv ā hi yātam athā somasya pibataṁ sutasya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • indrāgnī: Indra and Agni (the deities representing strength and fire)
    • madathaḥ: intoxicated or inspired
    • sve: in your own
    • duroṇe: abode or dwelling
    • yad: that which
    • brahmaṇi: in the ultimate reality or the cosmic order
    • rājani: in the realm of kings or governance
    • vā: or
    • yajatrā: in the act of worship or sacrifice
    • ataḥ: therefore
    • pari: all around
    • vṛṣaṇāv: the powerful ones
    • ā hi: indeed
    • yātam: may you go
    • athā: and then
    • somasya: of Soma (the divine nectar)
    • pibataṁ: drink
    • sutasya: of the brewed or prepared (nectar)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the deities Indra and Agni, symbolizing strength and the transformative power of fire.
    • It suggests that when one is inspired or intoxicated by the divine, they can find their place in their own abode or reality.
    • The reference to Brahman and kingship indicates the importance of aligning oneself with the cosmic order and governance.
    • The mantra encourages participation in rituals and sacrifices, emphasizing the significance of community and shared spiritual experiences.
    • The concluding lines highlight the importance of consuming Soma, which represents divine knowledge and bliss, suggesting that one should partake in the fruits of their spiritual practices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders can draw inspiration from this mantra by embodying the qualities of Indra and Agni, fostering a culture of strength and innovation.
    • During community festivals, rituals that honor deities can create a sense of belonging and shared purpose, reflecting the communal aspect of worship.
    • In personal development, individuals can seek to align their actions with their values, akin to finding their place in the cosmic order.
    • Consuming 'Soma' can be likened to engaging in practices that nourish the mind and spirit, such as meditation, yoga, or reading spiritual texts.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of individual and cosmic existence, urging individuals to find their strength and purpose through divine inspiration.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to engage in community rituals and personal practices that elevate one's spiritual journey.
    • In real life, it serves as a guide for leadership, community engagement, and personal growth, encouraging individuals to partake in the divine nectar of knowledge and bliss.
