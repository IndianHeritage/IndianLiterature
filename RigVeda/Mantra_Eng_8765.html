पर्वीषु प्रधन्वा वाजसातये परि वृत्राणि सक्षणिः |द्विषस्तरध्या ऋणया न ईयसे ||
pary ū ṣu pra dhanva vājasātaye pari vṛtrāṇi sakṣaṇiḥ | dviṣas taradhyā ṛṇayā na īyase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • paryūṣu: In the midst of the challenges or obstacles.
    • pra dhanva: The one who possesses great strength or power.
    • vājasātaye: For the purpose of achieving victory or success.
    • pari vṛtrāṇi: To overcome the enemies or obstacles.
    • sakṣaṇiḥ: With great skill or capability.
    • dviṣas: Of the adversaries or those who oppose.
    • taradhyā: To rise above or transcend.
    • ṛṇayā: With the burden or debt.
    • na īyase: Do not be led or influenced.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about facing challenges and adversities with strength and skill.
    • It emphasizes the importance of overcoming obstacles in pursuit of success.
    • The phrase "do not be led" suggests the need for self-determination and resilience against negative influences.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual may face competition (the "dviṣas") in their career. By harnessing their skills ("sakṣaṇiḥ") and focusing on their goals ("vājasātaye"), they can rise above these challenges.
    • A student preparing for exams may encounter various distractions and pressures. By staying focused and determined, they can overcome these obstacles and achieve academic success.
    • In personal relationships, one may face conflicts or misunderstandings. By approaching these issues with strength and skill, one can resolve conflicts and strengthen bonds.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the inner strength and resilience we possess to overcome life's challenges.
    • It encourages individuals to remain steadfast in their pursuits, regardless of the obstacles they may face.
    • In essence, it highlights the significance of self-determination and the ability to rise above adversities, making it applicable in various aspects of life, including career, education, and personal relationships.
