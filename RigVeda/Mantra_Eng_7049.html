कदाचन प्र युच्छस्य उभे न पासी जनमनी |तुरीयादित्य हवनं त इन्द्रियम आ तस्थाव अमृतं दिवि ||
kadā cana pra yucchasy ubhe ni pāsi janmanī | turīyāditya havanaṁ ta indriyam ā tasthāv amṛtaṁ divi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kadā cana: at any time
    • pra yucchasy: you may not be separated
    • ubhe: both
    • ni pāsi: do not hold back
    • janmanī: in birth
    • turīyāditya: the fourth sun or the supreme light
    • havanaṁ: offering or invocation
    • ta: that
    • indriyam: senses or faculties
    • ā tasthāv: may stand or remain
    • amṛtaṁ: immortal or eternal
    • divi: in the heavens
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of unity and the eternal connection between the physical and spiritual realms.
    • It emphasizes the importance of not being separated from both aspects of existence—material and spiritual—throughout one's life.
    • The reference to the "fourth sun" symbolizes a higher state of consciousness or enlightenment that transcends ordinary perception.
    • The invocation of senses suggests that true understanding and connection to the divine can be achieved through our faculties, urging us to remain open and aware.
    • The concept of "immortality" in the heavens indicates a pursuit of eternal truth and wisdom that goes beyond physical existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied in moments of meditation or mindfulness, where one seeks to connect with both their inner self and the external world.
    • For instance, during yoga practice, individuals often strive to harmonize their physical movements with their breath, embodying the unity of body and spirit.
    • In relationships, the mantra encourages individuals to maintain a balance between personal desires and the needs of others, fostering deeper connections.
    • In professional settings, it can inspire leaders to create environments where both productivity and employee well-being are prioritized, reflecting the dual aspects of success.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for balance and unity in life.
    • It serves as a reminder to embrace both the material and spiritual aspects of existence, ensuring that one does not neglect either side.
    • By applying the teachings of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a more holistic approach to life, leading to greater fulfillment and understanding.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a journey towards enlightenment, where the senses and the spirit work in harmony to achieve a higher state of being.
