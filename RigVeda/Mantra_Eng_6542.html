प्रथमं जातवेदसम अग्निं यज्ञेषु पूर्व्यम् |प्रतिस्रुग एति नमसाऽहविष्मती ||
prathamaṁ jātavedasam agniṁ yajñeṣu pūrvyam | prati srug eti namasā haviṣmatī ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prathamaṁ: First
    • jātavedasam: The one who knows all births (Agni, the fire)
    • agniṁ: Fire
    • yajñeṣu: In sacrifices
    • pūrvyam: The foremost or the earliest
    • prati: Towards
    • srug: The offering or the oblation
    • eti: Comes
    • namasā: With reverence
    • haviṣmatī: The one who is rich in offerings
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Agni, the sacred fire, which is considered the mediator between humans and the divine.
    • It emphasizes Agni as the first among the deities in the context of rituals and sacrifices.
    • The phrase "prati srug eti namasā" indicates that offerings are made with reverence, highlighting the importance of intention in rituals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu weddings, the sacred fire (Agni) is invoked as a witness to the vows taken by the couple, symbolizing purity and the divine presence.
    • During festivals like Diwali, lighting lamps and offering prayers to Agni represents the dispelling of darkness and ignorance, and the welcoming of knowledge and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of Agni in rituals, representing the connection between the material and spiritual worlds.
    • It teaches us that reverence and intention are crucial in our actions, whether in spiritual practices or daily life.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages us to recognize the sacredness in our offerings, be it in rituals, relationships, or our contributions to society.
