मन्त्र: कथाकदस्य उषसो व्युष्टौ देवो मार्तस्य सख्यं जुजोषा |कथाकदस्य सख्यं सखिभ्यो ये अस्मिन कामं सुयुजं तातस्रे ||
kathā kad asyā uṣaso vyuṣṭau devo martasya sakhyaṁ jujoṣa | kathā kad asya sakhyaṁ sakhibhyo ye asmin kāmaṁ suyujaṁ tatasre ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Kathā: How
    • Kad: When
    • Aśyā: Of this
    • Uṣaso: Dawn
    • Vyuṣṭau: Having risen
    • Devo: The divine
    • Martasya: Of the mortal
    • Sakhyaṁ: Friendship
    • Jujoṣa: May you enjoy
    • Sakhibhyo: With friends
    • Ye: Who
    • Asmin: In this
    • Kāmaṁ: Desire
    • Suyujaṁ: Well-joined
    • Tatasre: May it be fulfilled
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects a deep inquiry into the nature of friendship and the divine connection between mortals and the divine.
    • The speaker is asking how the dawn, a symbol of new beginnings and enlightenment, can bring about a sense of camaraderie and fulfillment in desires.
    • It emphasizes the importance of friendship and community in achieving one's goals and desires.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, collaboration among colleagues can lead to innovative solutions and a supportive environment, much like the divine friendship mentioned in the mantra.
    • In personal relationships, nurturing friendships can help individuals achieve their personal goals, as friends often provide encouragement and support.
    • Community initiatives, such as local charities or volunteer groups, exemplify how collective efforts can fulfill shared desires for a better society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of friendship and community in the pursuit of desires and goals.
    • It teaches us that divine blessings can be invoked through our relationships with others, and that together, we can achieve more than we can alone.
    • In real life, fostering strong bonds with friends and community members can lead to a more fulfilling and enriched life, where collective aspirations are realized.
