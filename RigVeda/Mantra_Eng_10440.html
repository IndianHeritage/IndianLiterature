यस्त्वा स्वप्नेन तमसा मोहयित्वा निपद्यते |प्रजां यस ते जिघांसति तं इतो नाशयामसि ||
yas tvā svapnena tamasā mohayitvā nipadyate | prajāṁ yas te jighāṁsati tam ito nāśayāmasi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • tvā: you
    • svapnena: by dream
    • tamasā: by darkness
    • mohayitvā: having bewildered
    • nipadyate: is caught
    • prajāṁ: life or beings
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • jighāṁsati: desires to kill
    • tam: him
    • ito: from here
    • nāśayāmasi: we shall destroy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about a force or entity that, through the influence of dreams and darkness, can bewilder or confuse individuals.
    • The phrase "who desires to kill your beings" suggests a malevolent force that seeks to harm or destroy life.
    • The concluding part, "we shall destroy him from here," indicates a resolve to eliminate this negative influence or force.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a psychological context, this mantra can be related to the struggles individuals face with their fears and anxieties, which can often feel overwhelming and dark, much like a nightmare.
    • In a societal context, it can represent the fight against negative influences such as violence, hatred, or oppression that seek to harm communities.
    • In personal development, it can symbolize the journey of overcoming self-doubt and negative thoughts that hinder personal growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call to recognize and combat the forces that seek to confuse and harm us.
    • It serves as a reminder of the power of awareness and intention in overcoming darkness, whether it be internal struggles or external challenges.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as mental health awareness, community activism, and personal empowerment, encouraging individuals to confront and eliminate negativity in their lives.
