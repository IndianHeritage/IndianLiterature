तुभ्येदम अग्ने मधुमत्तमं वचस तुभ्यम मनीषा इयं अस्तु शं हृदे |त्वां गिरः सिंधुम इवावनीर महीर आपृणन्ति शवसां वर्धयन्ति च ||
tubhyedam agne madhumattamaṁ vacas tubhyam manīṣā iyam astu śaṁ hṛde | tvāṁ giraḥ sindhum ivāvanīr mahīr ā pṛṇanti śavasā vardhayanti ca ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tubhyam: to you
    • edam: this
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • madhumattamaṁ: the sweetest
    • vacas: speech or words
    • manīṣā: intelligence or thought
    • iyam: this
    • astu: may it be
    • śaṁ: peace or auspiciousness
    • hṛde: in the heart
    • tvāṁ: you
    • giraḥ: words or hymns
    • sindhum: like a river
    • ivāvanīr: like the earth
    • mahīr: great
    • ā: to
    • pṛṇanti: they fill
    • śavasā: with strength
    • vardhayanti: they increase
    • ca: and
  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification.
    • The speaker expresses a desire for their words and thoughts to be sweet and filled with intelligence, seeking peace in their heart.
    • It emphasizes the importance of communication and the power of words, comparing them to a river that nourishes and sustains life.
    • The invocation of Agni signifies the transformative power of fire, which can purify and elevate one's thoughts and intentions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied in situations where effective communication is crucial, such as in negotiations or public speaking.
    • For instance, a teacher may recite this mantra before addressing their students, seeking to inspire and convey knowledge in a meaningful way.
    • In personal relationships, individuals can use the essence of this mantra to foster understanding and harmony through kind and thoughtful words.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of words and thoughts in shaping our reality and relationships.
    • By invoking Agni, it reminds us of the transformative power of communication and the importance of speaking with intention and clarity.
    • In a world where misunderstandings are common, this mantra serves as a guide to cultivate peace and understanding through our expressions.
