आवो यस्य द्विभर्षो 'रकेषु सानुषग असत् |आजाव इन्द्रस्येन्दो प्रावो वाजेषु वाजिनम् ||
āvo yasya dvibarhaso 'rkeṣu sānuṣag asat | ājāv indrasyendo prāvo vājeṣu vājinam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āvo: Invoking or calling upon
    • yasya: Whose
    • dvibarhaso: Two-fold or dual strength
    • 'rkeṣu: In the hymns or verses
    • sānuṣag: With support or assistance
    • asat: Non-existent or false
    • ājāv: The one who is born or arises
    • indrasyendo: Of Indra, the king of gods
    • prāvo: To be invoked or called
    • vājeṣu: In the context of strength or power
    • vājinam: The one who possesses strength or power
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a call to invoke the divine strength and support of Indra, the king of gods, emphasizing the dual nature of strength found in hymns.
    • It highlights the importance of seeking divine assistance in overcoming challenges and achieving success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as preparing for an important exam or presentation, individuals often seek inspiration and strength from their beliefs or spiritual practices, similar to invoking Indra's support.
    • In team sports, players may come together to invoke a sense of unity and strength, drawing on the collective power of the team, akin to the dual strength mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine support and recognizing the dual aspects of strength in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios where individuals or groups seek guidance, strength, and unity to overcome obstacles and achieve their goals.
    • By acknowledging a higher power and the strength within, one can navigate challenges with greater resilience and purpose.
