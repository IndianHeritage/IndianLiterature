स प्रत्नवान नव्यसे विश्ववार सूक्ताय पथः कृणुहि प्राचः |ये दु:षहासो वनुषा बृहन्तस तांस्ते अश्याम पुरुकृत पुरुक्षो ||
sa pratnavan navyase viśvavāra sūktāya pathaḥ kṛṇuhi prācaḥ | ye duḥṣahāso vanuṣā bṛhantas tām̐s te aśyāma purukṛt purukṣo ||
  • sa pratnavan navyase viśvavāra sūktāya pathaḥ kṛṇuhi prācaḥ
  • ye duḥṣahāso vanuṣā bṛhantas
  • tām̐s te aśyāma purukṛt purukṣo

  • sa pratnavan: This phrase can be interpreted as "he who is endowed with knowledge" or "the one who possesses insight." In real life, this can refer to individuals who are wise and knowledgeable, such as teachers or mentors who guide others on their paths.
  • navyase: This term means "new" or "fresh." It signifies the importance of innovation and new beginnings. For example, in a business context, a startup that introduces a groundbreaking product can be seen as embodying this concept.
  • viśvavāra: This word translates to "universal protector" or "guardian of all." It highlights the role of a leader or a protector in society, akin to community leaders or environmental activists who strive to safeguard the well-being of all.
  • sūktāya pathaḥ kṛṇuhi prācaḥ: This can be understood as "create a path for the wise and knowledgeable." It emphasizes the need to pave the way for those who seek wisdom, similar to how educational institutions create opportunities for learning and growth.
  • ye duḥṣahāso: This phrase refers to "those who are strong in adversity." It speaks to resilience and the ability to overcome challenges, much like athletes who train rigorously to excel in their sports despite obstacles.
  • vanuṣā bṛhantas: This can be interpreted as "those who are great among men." It signifies individuals who stand out due to their contributions, such as philanthropists or leaders who inspire others through their actions.
  • tām̐s te aśyāma: This phrase means "may they be protected." It reflects a desire for safety and well-being, akin to the prayers for peace and protection that people offer in times of crisis.
  • purukṛt purukṣo: This translates to "those who perform great deeds." It emphasizes the importance of action and contribution to society, similar to volunteers who dedicate their time to help those in need.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, protection, resilience, and action. It serves as a reminder of the importance of guiding and supporting those who seek knowledge while also recognizing the strength of individuals who overcome challenges. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in educational settings, community leadership, and personal development, encouraging individuals to strive for greatness and support one another in their journeys.
