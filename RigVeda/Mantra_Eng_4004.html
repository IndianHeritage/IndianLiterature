बृहद्वयो बृहते तुभ्यम अग्ने धियाजुरो मिथुनासः साचन्त |देवो-देवः सुहवो भूत्व मह्यम मा नो माता पृथिवी दुर्मतौ धात ||
bṛhad vayo bṛhate tubhyam agne dhiyājuro mithunāsaḥ sacanta | devo-devaḥ suhavo bhūtu mahyam mā no mātā pṛthivī durmatau dhāt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bṛhad: great, vast
    • vayo: age, life
    • bṛhate: to the great one
    • tubhyam: to you
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • dhiyā: with intellect, wisdom
    • juro: to be strong, powerful
    • mithunāsaḥ: united, in harmony
    • sacanta: may they come together
    • devo-devaḥ: the god of gods
    • suhavo: auspicious, favorable
    • bhūtu: existence, being
    • mahyam: to me
    • mā: may not
    • no: our
    • mātā: mother
    • pṛthivī: earth
    • durmatau: of ill thoughts
    • dhāt: grant, bestow
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification.
    • The invocation of "bṛhad vayo" emphasizes the vastness and greatness of life and existence.
    • By addressing Agni with "tubhyam," the speaker acknowledges the divine presence and seeks guidance.
    • The phrase "dhiyājuro mithunāsaḥ sacanta" suggests a desire for unity and harmony among people, encouraging collaboration and collective strength.
    • Referring to Agni as "devo-devaḥ," the mantra recognizes the supreme nature of this deity, highlighting the importance of divine support in human endeavors.
    • The plea "mā no mātā pṛthivī durmatau dhāt" expresses a wish for the earth (mother) to not grant us ill thoughts, indicating a desire for purity of mind and intention.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied to foster teamwork and collaboration among employees, encouraging them to work together harmoniously towards common goals.
    • In personal relationships, invoking the spirit of unity and understanding can help resolve conflicts and promote a supportive environment.
    • In community gatherings, this mantra can serve as a reminder to focus on collective well-being and to avoid negative thoughts that may disrupt harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and purity of thought, which are essential for personal and communal growth.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, where collaboration and positive intentions are crucial.
    • By invoking the blessings of Agni, individuals and communities can aspire to achieve greater harmony and success in their endeavors.
