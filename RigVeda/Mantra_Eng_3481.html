यं सिम अनु प्रवतेव द्रवन्तं विश्वः पूरुर मदति हर्षमाणः |पद्भिर गृध्यन्तम मेधायुं न शूरं रथतुरं वातम iva ध्रजन्तम् ||
yaṁ sīm anu pravateva dravantaṁ viśvaḥ pūrur madati harṣamāṇaḥ | paḍbhir gṛdhyantam medhayuṁ na śūraṁ rathaturaṁ vātam iva dhrajantam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: who
    • sīm: boundary or limit
    • anu: following
    • pravateva: like a flowing stream
    • dravantaṁ: flowing or moving
    • viśvaḥ: all or universal
    • pūrur: abundant or full
    • madati: rejoices or is delighted
    • harṣamāṇaḥ: filled with joy
    • paḍbhir: with feet or steps
    • gṛdhyantam: desiring or seeking
    • medhayuṁ: wise or intelligent
    • na: not
    • śūraṁ: brave or valiant
    • rathaturaṁ: charioteer or one who drives a chariot
    • vātam: wind or air
    • iva: like
    • dhrajantam: one who is strong or powerful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a universal force or entity that flows abundantly and brings joy to all.
    • The imagery of a flowing stream suggests continuity and the nurturing aspect of nature.
    • The mention of wisdom and bravery indicates that true strength lies not just in physical prowess but in intelligence and the ability to navigate life's challenges.
    • The reference to a charioteer symbolizes guidance and control, suggesting that one must steer their life with wisdom and courage.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, rivers symbolize the flow of life, providing sustenance and joy to all living beings along their banks.
    • In personal development, individuals who seek knowledge and wisdom often find themselves more capable of overcoming obstacles, much like a charioteer skillfully navigating a race.
    • In teamwork, a leader who embodies wisdom and bravery can inspire their team to achieve greater heights, much like a charioteer leading their horses to victory.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, joy, and the nurturing aspects of life.
    • It teaches us that true strength comes from a combination of intelligence and courage, guiding us through the challenges we face.
    • In real life, we can apply these teachings by seeking knowledge, embracing joy, and leading with wisdom in our personal and professional lives.
