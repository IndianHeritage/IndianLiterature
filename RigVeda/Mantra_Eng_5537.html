दधिक्रां वः प्रथमम् अश्विनोषसम अग्निं समिद्धम् भागम् ऊतये हुवे |इन्द्रं विष्णुम पूषणं ब्रह्मणस पतिम आदित्यान द्यावापृथिवी अपः स्वः ||
dadhikrāṁ vaḥ prathamam aśvinoṣasam agniṁ samiddham bhagam ūtaye huve | indraṁ viṣṇum pūṣaṇam brahmaṇas patim ādityān dyāvāpṛthivī apaḥ svaḥ ||
  • Translation: "We invoke the blazing Agni, the first among the Ashvins, for the purpose of our offerings. We call upon Indra, Vishnu, Pusha, the lord of Brahman, and the Adityas, the heavens, the earth, and the waters."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dadhikrāṁ: to invoke or call upon
    • vaḥ: your (plural, addressing the divine)
    • prathamam: first or foremost
    • aśvinoṣasam: related to the Ashvins, the twin deities associated with health and medicine
    • agniṁ: Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy and transformation
    • samiddham: kindled or ignited
    • bhagam: wealth or prosperity
    • ūtaye: for the purpose of offerings
    • huve: I call or invoke
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods, representing strength and power
    • viṣṇum: Vishnu, the preserver, symbolizing protection and sustenance
    • pūṣaṇam: Pusha, the nourisher, associated with growth and fertility
    • brahmaṇas: of Brahman, the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit
    • patim: lord or master
    • ādityān: the Adityas, solar deities representing light and truth
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: heavens and earth, symbolizing the duality of existence
    • apaḥ: waters, representing life and purification
    • svaḥ: the sky or the realm of the divine
  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energies for prosperity, health, and spiritual growth. It highlights the interconnectedness of various deities and elements of nature, suggesting that harmony among them is essential for a balanced life.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be recited during rituals or ceremonies to seek blessings for health and prosperity.
    • It can be used as a reminder to respect and honor the natural elements—fire, water, earth, and sky—by practicing sustainability and environmental consciousness.
    • In times of difficulty, invoking these divine energies can serve as a source of strength and guidance, encouraging individuals to seek support from their community and the universe.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the divine forces that govern our lives and the importance of seeking their blessings. By acknowledging and invoking these energies, individuals can cultivate a sense of purpose, harmony, and connection with the world around them.
