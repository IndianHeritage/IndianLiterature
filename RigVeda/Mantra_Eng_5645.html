सस्वश्चिद्धिसमृतिस्त्वेष्येषामपीच्येनसहसा सहन्ते |युष्मद्भियावृषणोरेजमानादक्षस्यचिनमहिनामृळतानः ||
sasvaś cid dhi samṛtis tveṣy eṣām apīcyena sahasā sahante | yuṣmad bhiyā vṛṣaṇo rejamānā dakṣasya cin mahinā mṛḻatā naḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sasvaś: always, eternal
    • cid: consciousness, awareness
    • dhi: intellect, understanding
    • samṛtis: memory, recollection
    • tveṣy: your, belonging to you
    • eṣām: of these, of those
    • apīcyena: by means of, through
    • sahasā: suddenly, swiftly
    • sahante: endure, bear
    • yuṣmad: your, belonging to you
    • bhiyā: fear, anxiety
    • vṛṣaṇo: of the strong, powerful
    • rejamānā: being agitated, disturbed
    • dakṣasya: of the capable, skilled
    • cin: to perceive, to know
    • mahinā: by greatness, by power
    • mṛḻatā: to be calmed, to be soothed
    • naḥ: for us, to us
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the eternal nature of consciousness and the importance of memory and understanding in our lives.
    • It emphasizes the need to endure challenges and fears, particularly those that arise from powerful forces or situations.
    • The invocation of strength and capability suggests that through awareness and intellect, one can navigate through life's disturbances.
    • Real-life example: In a stressful work environment, maintaining awareness and using one's intellect can help in managing anxiety and making informed decisions.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of mental resilience and the ability to remain calm amidst chaos.
    • It serves as a reminder that through understanding and memory, we can draw upon past experiences to guide us in the present.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various scenarios such as conflict resolution, personal challenges, or even in leadership roles where decision-making under pressure is crucial.
