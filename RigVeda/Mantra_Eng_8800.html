स जातो गर्भोऽसि रोदस्योर अग्ने चारुर विभृत ओषधिषु |चित्रः शिशुः परि तमांस्यक्तून प्र मातृभ्योऽधि कनिक्रदद गाः ||
sa jāto garbho asi rodasyor agne cārur vibhṛta oṣadhīṣu | citraḥ śiśuḥ pari tamāṁsy aktūn pra mātṛbhyo adhi kanikradad gāḥ ||
  • sa jāto garbho asi rodasyor agne cārur vibhṛta oṣadhīṣu
    • sa jāto: "He is born" - This phrase signifies the beginning of life, emphasizing the sacredness of birth.
    • garbho asi: "You are the womb" - This refers to the nurturing aspect of the womb, symbolizing protection and growth.
    • rodasyor: "of the two worlds" - This indicates the connection between the physical and spiritual realms.
    • agne: "O Agni" - Agni, the fire god, represents energy, transformation, and the divine spark within life.
    • cārur vibhṛta: "beautifully spread" - This suggests the abundance and diversity of life, akin to the variety found in nature.
    • oṣadhīṣu: "in the plants" - This highlights the importance of nature and its role in sustaining life.
  • citraḥ śiśuḥ pari tamāṁsy aktūn
    • citraḥ: "varied" - This word reflects the diversity of life experiences and the uniqueness of each individual.
    • śiśuḥ: "child" - Represents innocence, potential, and the future of humanity.
    • pari tamāṁsy: "surrounding darkness" - This can symbolize challenges and obstacles that one faces in life.
    • aktūn: "to be overcome" - Indicates the need to confront and transcend difficulties.
  • pra mātṛbhyo adhi kanikradad gāḥ
    • pra mātṛbhyo: "to the mothers" - Acknowledges the nurturing role of mothers and the importance of maternal care.
    • adhi kanikradad: "who have given birth" - This emphasizes the act of giving life and the responsibilities that come with it.
    • gāḥ: "the cows" - Cows are often seen as symbols of abundance and nourishment in Vedic culture.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of life, birth, and the nurturing aspects of nature and motherhood. It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine energy that fuels life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to appreciate the role of mothers and caregivers, recognize the challenges faced in life, and celebrate the diversity of experiences. It serves as a reminder to honor the natural world and the nurturing forces that sustain us, encouraging a holistic view of life that values both the physical and spiritual dimensions.
