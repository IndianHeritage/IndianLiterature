वयं वो वृक्तबरिषो हितप्रयसा आनुषक् |सुतसोमासो वरुण हवामहे मनुष्वद इद्धाग्नयः ||
vayaṁ vo vṛktabarhiṣo hitaprayasa ānuṣak | sutasomāso varuṇa havāmahe manuṣvad iddhāgnayaḥ ||
  • vayaṁ vo: This phrase translates to "we are yours" or "we belong to you." It signifies a sense of devotion and surrender to a higher power or divine entity.
  • vṛktabarhiṣo: This term refers to "those who are well-versed in the sacred knowledge" or "those who have mastered the Vedic hymns." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in spiritual practices.
  • hitaprayasa: This means "beneficial efforts" or "actions that lead to well-being." It highlights the significance of performing actions that are not only good for oneself but also for the community and the environment.
  • ānuṣak: This word can be interpreted as "following the path" or "adhering to the teachings." It suggests the importance of following a righteous path in life.
  • sutasomāso: This term refers to "those who are born of the Soma," which is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. It represents the pursuit of higher consciousness and spiritual awakening.
  • varuṇa: Varuṇa is the Vedic god associated with cosmic order and moral authority. Invoking Varuṇa signifies a call for justice, truth, and the maintenance of dharma (righteousness).
  • havāmahe: This translates to "we invoke" or "we call upon." It indicates an active engagement in prayer or ritual, seeking the presence and blessings of the divine.
  • manuṣvad: This term means "human-like" or "of human nature." It emphasizes the connection between the divine and humanity, suggesting that divine qualities can be embodied by humans.
  • iddhāgnayaḥ: This phrase refers to "the fire that is invoked" or "the sacred fire." Fire in Vedic tradition symbolizes purification, transformation, and the divine presence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message of devotion, wisdom, and the pursuit of righteousness. It emphasizes the importance of beneficial actions and the connection between the divine and humanity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom, fostering a sense of responsibility towards themselves and society.
  • In community service, it inspires actions that promote well-being and harmony, reflecting the idea of working for the greater good.
  • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder to invoke divine guidance and embody virtues that align with cosmic order and moral authority.

Overall, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for living a life of purpose, integrity, and connection to the divine.
