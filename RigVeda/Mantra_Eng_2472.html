शुचिं न यामन्न इषिरं स्वर्दृशं केतुं दिवो रोचनस्थाम उषर्बुधम् |अग्निम मूर्धानं दिवो अप्रतिष्कुतं तं ईमहे नमसां वाजिनं बृहत् ||
śuciṁ na yāmann iṣiraṁ svardṛśaṁ ketuṁ divo rocanasthām uṣarbudham | agnim mūrdhānaṁ divo apratiṣkutaṁ tam īmahe namasā vājinam bṛhat ||
  • śuciṁ - This word means "pure" or "clean." In a real-life context, purity can refer to moral integrity or the cleanliness of one's environment.
  • na yāmann - This phrase translates to "not to those who are unworthy." It emphasizes the importance of associating with those who are virtuous and righteous.
  • iṣiraṁ - This means "powerful" or "strong." In everyday life, this can relate to individuals who possess strength in character or influence in society.
  • svardṛśaṁ - This translates to "having a divine vision." It suggests the ability to see beyond the ordinary, akin to having insight or foresight in decision-making.
  • ketuṁ - This word means "banner" or "flag." It symbolizes leadership and guidance, much like a leader who inspires others to follow a noble path.
  • divo - This means "of the heavens" or "divine." It indicates a connection to higher powers or spiritual realms.
  • rocanasthām - This translates to "shining" or "radiant." In real life, this can refer to individuals who bring positivity and light into the lives of others.
  • uṣarbudham - This means "the one who is born from the dawn." It symbolizes new beginnings and the potential for growth and renewal.
  • agnim - This word means "fire." Fire is often associated with transformation, energy, and purification in various cultures.
  • mūrdhānaṁ - This translates to "the one who is at the head" or "supreme." It signifies leadership and authority.
  • divo apratiṣkutaṁ - This means "not to be obstructed by the heavens." It suggests a connection to the divine that is unimpeded, representing spiritual freedom.
  • tam īmahe - This translates to "we meditate upon that." It indicates a focus on contemplation and reflection.
  • namasā - This means "with reverence" or "with respect." It emphasizes the importance of humility and gratitude.
  • vājinam - This word means "the one who is swift" or "the one who brings victory." It symbolizes success and achievement.
  • bṛhat - This translates to "great" or "vast." It signifies the importance of aiming for greatness in all endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of purity, strength, divine vision, and leadership. It encourages individuals to seek out virtuous associations, to aspire for greatness, and to maintain a connection with the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal development, leadership roles, and community service, where one strives to embody these qualities and inspire others. By meditating on these principles, individuals can cultivate a life of integrity, purpose, and positive influence, ultimately contributing to the greater good of society.
