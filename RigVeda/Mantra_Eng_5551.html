तं ऊर्मिम आपो मधुमत्तमं वो 'पां नपाद अवत्व आशुहेमा |यस्मिन्न इन्द्रो वसुभिर माधयाते तं अश्याम देवयन्तो वो अद्य ||
tam ūrmim āpo madhumattamaṁ vo 'pāṁ napād avatv āśuhemā | yasminn indro vasubhir mādayāte tam aśyāma devayanto vo adya ||
  • tam ūrmim āpo madhumattamaṁ vo 'pāṁ napād avatv āśuhemā
    • tam - that
    • ūrmim - wave or water
    • āpo - waters
    • madhumattamaṁ - the sweetest
    • vo - for you
    • 'pāṁ - to protect
    • napād - from the feet
    • avatv - may it come
    • āśuhemā - quickly or swiftly
  • yasminn indro vasubhir mādayāte
    • yasminn - in whom
    • indro - Indra, the king of gods
    • vasubhir - with the Vasus (deities of abundance)
    • mādayāte - rejoices or is pleased
  • tam aśyāma devayanto vo adya
    • tam - that
    • aśyāma - we invoke or we praise
    • devayanto - divine beings
    • vo - for you
    • adya - today

This mantra from the Rigveda invokes the divine waters, asking for their sweetness and protection. The first part emphasizes the desire for the pure and nourishing waters to come swiftly, symbolizing the need for sustenance and support in life. The second part highlights the connection with Indra and the Vasus, suggesting that when these divine forces are pleased, they bring abundance and joy. The final invocation calls upon the divine beings to bless us today.


Real-Life Examples:

  • In agriculture, farmers pray for rain and good harvests, similar to invoking the waters for nourishment.
  • In personal life, individuals seek blessings for prosperity and happiness, akin to the joy brought by divine forces.

Conclusion:

This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine support and nourishment in our lives. It teaches us to appreciate the abundance around us and to invoke higher powers for protection and prosperity. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in rituals, prayers, and moments of reflection, reminding us of our connection to nature and the divine.
