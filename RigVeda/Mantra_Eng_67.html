तुञ्जे-तुञ्जे य उत्तरे स्तोमा इन्द्रस्य वज्रीणः |न विन्दे अस्य सुष्टुतिम् ||
tuñje-tuñje ya uttare stomā indrasya vajriṇaḥ | na vindhe asya suṣṭutim ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tuñje-tuñje: This term can be interpreted as "in every way" or "in all aspects."
    • ya: This means "who" or "that."
    • uttare: This translates to "in the higher" or "in the superior."
    • stomā: Refers to "praises" or "hymns."
    • indrasya: This means "of Indra," who is the king of gods in Vedic literature.
    • vajriṇaḥ: This term means "the one who wields the thunderbolt," referring to Indra's power.
    • na: This means "not."
    • vindhe: This translates to "find" or "obtain."
    • asya: This means "of him" or "of this."
    • suṣṭutim: This translates to "good praise" or "excellent commendation."
  • Overall Meaning:
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of praising Indra, the god of rain and thunder, in all aspects and in a superior manner.
    • It suggests that without proper praise and acknowledgment, one may not attain the blessings or the good qualities associated with Indra.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, recognizing and appreciating the contributions of team members can lead to a more productive and harmonious work environment. Just as Indra is praised for his strength and leadership, leaders in organizations should acknowledge the efforts of their teams.
    • In personal relationships, expressing gratitude and appreciation can strengthen bonds. For instance, thanking a friend for their support can enhance the friendship, similar to how the mantra highlights the importance of praise.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the power of recognition and praise in achieving success and harmony in various aspects of life.
    • It teaches us that acknowledging the strengths and contributions of others, much like praising Indra, can lead to greater blessings and positive outcomes.
    • In essence, it encourages a culture of appreciation, which is applicable in personal, professional, and communal settings.
