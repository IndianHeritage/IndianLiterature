वायव इन्द्रश्च चेतथः सुतानां वाजिनीवासू |ताव आ यातम उप द्रवत ||
vāyav indraś ca cetathaḥ sutānāṁ vājinīvasū | tāv ā yātam upa dravat ||
  • vāyav: This word refers to the wind or air, symbolizing movement, energy, and the vital force that sustains life.
  • indraś: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking the concepts of wind and Indra together.
  • cetathaḥ: This term translates to "if you think" or "if you consider," indicating a call to awareness or contemplation.
  • sutānāṁ: This means "of the sons" or "of the offspring," often referring to the blessings or benefits that come from divine favor.
  • vājinīvasū: This word can be interpreted as "the best of the strong" or "the one who brings strength," suggesting a source of power and vitality.
  • tāv: This means "those" or "that," referring to the subjects of the mantra.
  • ā yātam: This phrase translates to "may you go" or "may you arrive," indicating a desire for movement or progress.
  • upa dravat: This means "to flow towards" or "to approach," suggesting a direction or purpose in the journey.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation, calling upon the forces of nature, represented by the wind and Indra, to bless the offspring with strength and vitality. It emphasizes the importance of awareness and intention in seeking blessings and progress in life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the wind symbolizes change and movement, individuals can reflect on their own journeys and the strength they need to overcome challenges.
  • Family and Relationships: The mention of "sons" highlights the importance of nurturing the next generation, encouraging parents to instill values of strength and resilience in their children.
  • Community and Society: The invocation of Indra can be seen as a call for collective strength, urging communities to come together to support one another in times of need.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for awareness, strength, and the pursuit of progress. It serves as a reminder that by invoking the forces of nature and divine power, individuals and communities can find the strength to navigate life's challenges and foster growth and resilience. This mantra can be recited during personal reflection, family gatherings, or community events to inspire strength and unity.
