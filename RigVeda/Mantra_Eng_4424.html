नाभिं यज्ञानां सदनं रयीणाम महाम आहावम अभि सं नवन्त |वैश्वानरं रथ्यम अध्वराणां यज्ञस्य केतुं जनयन्त देवाः ||
nābhiṁ yajñānāṁ sadanaṁ rayīṇām mahām āhāvam abhi saṁ navanta | vaiśvānaraṁ rathyam adhvarāṇāṁ yajñasya ketuṁ janayanta devāḥ ||
  • nābhiṁ: This word translates to "navel" or "center." In a metaphorical sense, it represents the core or the source of energy and sustenance.
  • yajñānāṁ: This means "of sacrifices" or "of rituals." In Vedic tradition, yajñas are sacred rituals performed to invoke divine blessings.
  • sadanaṁ: This translates to "abode" or "place of dwelling." It signifies a space where divine energies reside.
  • rayīṇām: This means "of wealth" or "prosperity." It refers to the abundance and resources that one seeks in life.
  • mahām: This translates to "great" or "mighty," indicating the significance of the energies being invoked.
  • āhāvam: This means "invocation" or "calling forth." It signifies the act of summoning divine forces.
  • abhi: This translates to "towards" or "in the direction of," indicating a movement or intention towards a goal.
  • saṁ: This means "together" or "with," suggesting unity and collective effort.
  • navanta: This translates to "new" or "renewed," indicating the idea of rejuvenation and fresh beginnings.
  • vaiśvānaraṁ: This refers to the universal fire or the divine energy that sustains all life.
  • rathyam: This means "path" or "way," indicating the journey one takes in life.
  • adhvarāṇāṁ: This translates to "of the rituals" or "of the sacrifices," emphasizing the importance of these practices in connecting with the divine.
  • yajñasya: This means "of the sacrifice," reiterating the central theme of the mantra.
  • ketuṁ: This translates to "banner" or "sign," symbolizing guidance and direction.
  • janayanta: This means "they generate" or "they create," indicating the active role of the divine in manifesting energies.
  • devāḥ: This translates to "gods" or "divine beings," representing the higher powers that oversee and bless the rituals.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of rituals and sacrifices in connecting with the divine and generating prosperity and abundance in life. It highlights the importance of invoking divine energies to create a harmonious and prosperous existence. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied through various practices such as meditation, community service, and rituals that promote unity and collective well-being. By recognizing the core energies within ourselves and our surroundings, we can align our actions with the greater cosmic order, leading to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
