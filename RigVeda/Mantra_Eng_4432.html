व्य अस्तभ्नाद rodasī मित्रो अद्भुतो 'न्तर्वावद अकृत्नोज ज्योतिषा तमः |विचर्मणीयव धिषणे अवर्तयद वैश्वानरो विश्वम् अधत्त वृष्ण्यम् ||
vy astabhnād rodasī mitro adbhuto 'ntarvāvad akṛṇoj jyotiṣā tamaḥ | vi carmaṇīva dhiṣaṇe avartayad vaiśvānaro viśvam adhatta vṛṣṇyam ||
  • vy astabhnād rodasī: This phrase can be translated as "He who has established the heavens." Here, "vy" indicates a sense of action or establishment, while "astabhnād" refers to the act of setting or stabilizing. "Rodasī" means the heavens or the sky. This can be likened to how in our lives, we often seek stability and grounding, much like the way the sky provides a constant backdrop to our existence.
  • mitro adbhuto: This translates to "the wondrous friend." "Mitro" means friend, and "adbuto" signifies something extraordinary or marvelous. In real life, this can be seen in relationships where friends support us in unexpected ways, providing help and joy during challenging times.
  • 'ntarvāvad akṛṇoj: This means "he who has created the inner world." "Antarvāvad" refers to the inner or internal aspect, while "akṛṇoj" means to create or form. This can be related to personal growth and self-discovery, where individuals create their inner worlds through experiences, thoughts, and emotions.
  • jyotiṣā tamaḥ: This translates to "light overcoming darkness." "Jyotiṣā" means light, and "tamaḥ" refers to darkness. This can be exemplified in real life through the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, where understanding and wisdom dispel ignorance and fear.
  • vi carmaṇīva dhiṣaṇe: This means "like a skin, he has enveloped." "Vi" indicates a sense of encompassing, "carmaṇīva" refers to skin, and "dhiṣaṇe" means to cover or envelop. This can be compared to how we protect ourselves with our experiences and learnings, much like a skin that shields us from external elements.
  • avartayad vaiśvānaro: This translates to "the universal being who rotates." "Avartayad" means to turn or rotate, and "vaiśvānaro" refers to the universal or cosmic aspect. This can be seen in the cyclical nature of life, where everything is interconnected and constantly in motion, much like the cycles of nature.
  • viśvam adhatta vṛṣṇyam: This means "he has established the universe." "Viśvam" refers to the universe or all that exists, and "adhatta" means to set or establish, while "vṛṣṇyam" signifies strength or vitality. This can be related to how we build our own worlds and communities, establishing connections and relationships that contribute to the greater whole.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, stability, friendship, and the interplay of light and darkness. It signifies the importance of establishing a strong foundation in life, nurturing relationships, and embracing personal growth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek balance, foster connections, and pursue knowledge, ultimately leading to a harmonious existence within the universe. It serves as a reminder that we are all part of a larger cosmic cycle, where our actions and relationships contribute to the greater good.
