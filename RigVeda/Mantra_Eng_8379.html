ते नः पूर्वासा उपरासा इन्दवो महे वाजाय धन्वन्तु गोमते |ईक्षेण्यासो अह्यो न चारवो ब्रह्म-ब्रह्म ये जुजुषुर हविः-हविः ||
te naḥ pūrvāsa uparāsa indavo mahe vājāya dhanvantu gomate | īkṣeṇyāso ahyo3 na cāravo brahma-brahma ye jujuṣur havir-haviḥ ||
  • te naḥ - "to us" or "for us": This phrase indicates a plea or a request directed towards the divine, emphasizing the need for blessings or support.
  • pūrvāsa - "the ancient ones" or "the ancestors": This refers to the wisdom and guidance of those who came before us, suggesting a connection to tradition and heritage.
  • uparāsa - "the higher ones" or "the exalted": This term signifies the elevated beings or deities that are invoked for assistance and protection.
  • indavo - "the divine beings" or "the gods": This word refers to the various deities in the Vedic tradition, highlighting the spiritual aspect of the mantra.
  • mahe - "great" or "mighty": This adjective emphasizes the power and significance of the divine beings being addressed.
  • vājāya - "for strength" or "for vigor": This term indicates a desire for energy, vitality, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • dhanvantu - "may they bestow" or "may they grant": This is a request for the divine to provide blessings or gifts.
  • gomate - "to the one who knows" or "to the wise": This refers to those who possess knowledge and understanding, suggesting the importance of wisdom in receiving blessings.
  • īkṣeṇyāso - "with vision" or "with insight": This term emphasizes the need for clarity and perception in one's actions and decisions.
  • ahyo3 - "not" or "without": This negation indicates a desire to avoid certain negative aspects or outcomes.
  • na cāravo - "not wandering" or "not straying": This phrase suggests the importance of staying focused and true to one's path.
  • brahma-brahma - "the ultimate reality" or "the supreme truth": This term refers to the highest spiritual principle, emphasizing the importance of understanding and aligning with it.
  • ye jujuṣur - "those who desire" or "those who seek": This indicates the aspirants or practitioners who are actively seeking spiritual growth and understanding.
  • havir-haviḥ - "the offering" or "the sacrifice": This term signifies the act of giving, whether in a ritualistic sense or in daily life, highlighting the importance of selflessness and devotion.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a profound request for divine guidance, strength, and wisdom. It emphasizes the importance of connecting with the ancient wisdom of our ancestors and the higher beings, seeking their blessings for clarity and focus in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of decision-making, when one seeks to align with their higher purpose, or during rituals where offerings are made to express gratitude and devotion. The significance lies in recognizing the interconnectedness of knowledge, strength, and spiritual truth, guiding individuals towards a more meaningful and purposeful existence.
