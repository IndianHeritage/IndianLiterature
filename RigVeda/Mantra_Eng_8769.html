अभ्यभि हि श्रवसां ततरदिथोऽत्सं न कं चिज जनपानम् अक्षितम् |शर्याभिर न भरमानो गभस्त्योः ||
abhy-abhi hi śravasā tatardithotsaṁ na kaṁ cij janapānam akṣitam | śaryābhir na bharamāṇo gabhastyoḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhy-abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • hi: indeed, certainly
    • śravasā: with fame, glory
    • tatardithotsaṁ: having risen, having been elevated
    • na: not
    • kaṁ: any, anyone
    • cij: at all
    • janapānam: the people, the community
    • akṣitam: unbroken, intact
    • śaryābhir: with the support of the wise
    • na: not
    • bharamāṇo: bearing, carrying
    • gabhastyoḥ: of the great, the powerful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the elevation of glory and fame that is unbroken and intact.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and the support of wise individuals in achieving greatness.
    • The phrase "not bearing the burden of the great" suggests that one should not be overwhelmed by the expectations or pressures from powerful entities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team that works together with a shared vision can achieve success without being burdened by the pressures of upper management.
    • In personal life, individuals who surround themselves with supportive friends and mentors can rise to their potential without feeling the weight of societal expectations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of community support and the pursuit of glory without being weighed down by external pressures.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as teamwork in organizations, personal development, and community building.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to seek elevation through collaboration and wisdom, fostering an environment where everyone can thrive.
