प्र वः सखायो अग्नये स्तोमं यज्ञं च धृष्णुया |अर्च गाया च वेधसे ||
pra vaḥ sakhāyo agnaye stomaṁ yajñaṁ ca dhṛṣṇuyā | arca gāya ca vedhase ||
  • pra vaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "let us speak" or "let us proclaim." It signifies the importance of communication and expression in rituals and daily life.
  • sakhāyo: This word means "friends" or "companions." It emphasizes the value of community and relationships in achieving common goals.
  • agnaye: Referring to "Agni," the fire god, this term highlights the significance of fire in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity, transformation, and the divine presence.
  • stomaṁ: This translates to "praise" or "hymn." It indicates the act of offering reverence through words, which is a fundamental aspect of spiritual practices.
  • yajñaṁ: Meaning "sacrifice" or "offering," this term represents the concept of selflessness and the importance of giving back to the universe, whether through rituals or acts of kindness.
  • ca: This simple conjunction means "and," linking the various elements of the mantra together.
  • dhṛṣṇuyā: This word can be interpreted as "with strength" or "with determination." It encourages perseverance and commitment in one's endeavors.
  • arca: Meaning "to worship" or "to honor," this term reinforces the idea of reverence towards the divine and the importance of gratitude in life.
  • gāya: This translates to "sing" or "to chant," highlighting the role of music and sound in spiritual practices, which can uplift the spirit and create a sense of community.
  • ca: Again, this conjunction connects the ideas presented in the mantra.
  • vedhase: This term means "to the one who knows" or "to the wise." It signifies the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom as essential components of a fulfilling life.

Conclusion: The mantra "pra vaḥ sakhāyo agnaye stomaṁ yajñaṁ ca dhṛṣṇuyā | arca gāya ca vedhase ||" encapsulates the essence of community, reverence, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real life, it encourages individuals to communicate openly, foster relationships, and engage in acts of kindness and worship. The significance of fire as a transformative element reminds us of the importance of purification and dedication in our actions. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as community gatherings, spiritual practices, and personal development, where the collective effort and shared values lead to a harmonious and fulfilling life.
