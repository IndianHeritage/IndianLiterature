ईशान इमा भुवनानि वीयसे युजाना इन्दो हरितः सुपर्ण्यः |तास ते क्षरन्तु मधुमद घृतं पयस् तव व्रते सोम तिष्ठन्तु कृष्टयः ||
īśāna imā bhuvanāni vīyase yujāna indo haritaḥ suparṇyaḥ | tās te kṣarantu madhumad ghṛtam payas tava vrate soma tiṣṭhantu kṛṣṭayaḥ ||
  • īśāna imā bhuvanāni vīyase: This phrase can be translated as "O Lord, you are the ruler of these worlds." Here, "īśāna" refers to the divine or supreme being, while "bhuvanāni" means the worlds or realms. This highlights the omnipotence of the divine, suggesting that all realms of existence are under its control.
  • yujāna indo haritaḥ suparṇyaḥ: This part translates to "unite us, O Indra, with the green and auspicious." "Indra" is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain and thunder, symbolizing strength and power. "Haritaḥ" means green, representing fertility and prosperity, while "suparṇyaḥ" refers to auspiciousness. This line emphasizes the need for divine guidance to achieve harmony and prosperity in life.
  • tās te kṣarantu madhumad ghṛtam payas: This translates to "let those who are sweet and nourishing flow forth." "Kṣarantu" means to flow or to be released, while "madhumad" refers to sweetness, and "ghṛtam payas" means ghee and milk, which are considered nourishing substances. This line signifies the importance of abundance and nourishment in our lives, both physically and spiritually.
  • tava vrate soma tiṣṭhantu kṛṣṭayaḥ: This can be interpreted as "may the offerings of Soma remain steadfast." "Soma" is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. "Kṛṣṭayaḥ" refers to the offerings or sacrifices made. This line underscores the significance of devotion and the importance of maintaining a connection with the divine through rituals and offerings.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and nourishment in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing the divine's role in the universe and our dependence on it for prosperity and well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, prayers, or moments of reflection, where individuals seek strength, abundance, and a deeper connection with the divine. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of maintaining harmony with nature and the universe.
