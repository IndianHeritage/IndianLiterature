हवाम्य अग्निम प्रथमं स्वस्तये हवामि मित्रावरुणाव इहावसे |हवामि रात्रिं जगतो निवेशनिं हवामि देवं सवितारम ऊतये ||
hvayāmy agnim prathamaṁ svastaye hvayāmi mitrāvaruṇāv ihāvase | hvayāmi rātrīṁ jagato niveśanīṁ hvayāmi devaṁ savitāram ūtaye ||
  • hvayāmy: This term translates to "I invoke" or "I call upon." It signifies the act of summoning or requesting divine presence.
  • agnim: Refers to "Agni," the fire god, who symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, fire is essential for cooking, warmth, and rituals.
  • prathamaṁ: Means "first" or "foremost." This indicates the importance of Agni as the primary deity in Vedic rituals.
  • svastaye: Translates to "for well-being" or "for prosperity." It emphasizes the desire for health and success in life.
  • mitrāvaruṇāv: Refers to the dual deities Mitra and Varuna, representing friendship and cosmic order, respectively. They symbolize harmony and balance in relationships.
  • ihāvase: Means "to dwell here" or "to reside." This indicates the invocation of these deities to be present in our lives.
  • rātrīṁ: Translates to "night." Night is often associated with rest, reflection, and the subconscious mind.
  • jagato: Means "of the world" or "of the universe." It signifies the vastness and interconnectedness of all beings.
  • niveśanīṁ: Refers to "the one who provides shelter" or "the one who encompasses." This highlights the protective aspect of the night.
  • devaṁ: Means "god" or "divine being." It signifies the higher powers we seek guidance from.
  • savitāram: Refers to "Savitri," the sun god, representing illumination, knowledge, and life-giving energy.
  • ūtaye: Translates to "for nourishment" or "for sustenance." It emphasizes the need for spiritual and physical sustenance.

Conclusion: This mantra is a profound invocation that calls upon Agni, Mitra, Varuna, and Savitri for well-being, protection, and nourishment. It signifies the importance of these deities in our lives, representing energy, harmony, and illumination. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during rituals, meditation, or moments of reflection to seek guidance and blessings for health, prosperity, and balance in relationships. It reminds us of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine forces that support our journey through life.
