विः ये दधुः शरदं मासं आद अहः यज्ञं अक्तुं चाद ऋचम् |अनाप्यं वरुणो मित्रो आर्यमाः क्षत्रं राजानः आशत ||
vi ye dadhuḥ śaradam māsam ād ahar yajñam aktuṁ cād ṛcam | anāpyaṁ varuṇo mitro aryamā kṣatraṁ rājāna āśata ||
  • Translation: "Those who perform the sacrifice, day after day, in the autumn season, may Varuna, Mitra, and Aryaman, the rulers of the Kshatriya, grant them protection."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vi ye dadhuḥ: "Those who perform" - Refers to individuals or groups engaged in rituals or sacrifices.
    • śaradam māsam: "the autumn season" - Symbolizes a time of harvest and abundance, a period of reflection and gratitude.
    • ād ahar: "day after day" - Emphasizes the importance of consistency and dedication in spiritual practices.
    • yajñam: "sacrifice" - Represents the act of giving, whether it be offerings to deities or service to the community.
    • aktuṁ: "to be performed" - Indicates the necessity of action in spiritual and communal life.
    • cād ṛcam: "and the hymns" - Refers to the recitation of sacred texts, which is integral to the rituals.
    • anāpyaṁ: "may grant" - A prayerful request for blessings and protection.
    • varuṇo mitro aryamā: "Varuna, Mitra, and Aryaman" - Deities representing cosmic order, friendship, and the moral law, respectively.
    • kṣatraṁ: "the Kshatriya" - Refers to the warrior class, symbolizing strength and protection.
    • rājāna: "the rulers" - Indicates leadership and governance, essential for societal stability.
    • āśata: "grant them protection" - A plea for safety and well-being from the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, community service can be seen as a form of yajña, where individuals come together to support and uplift their community, reflecting the essence of sacrifice.
    • Seasonal festivals, such as harvest festivals, embody the spirit of gratitude and celebration, akin to the autumn season mentioned in the mantra.
    • Consistent practice of mindfulness or meditation can be likened to the "day after day" aspect, emphasizing the importance of regular spiritual practice for personal growth.
  • Conclusion: The mantra highlights the significance of consistent spiritual practice and community service, especially during times of abundance and reflection. It serves as a reminder that through dedication to our duties and responsibilities, we can seek blessings and protection from higher powers. In real life, this can be applied by engaging in regular community service, practicing gratitude, and fostering a sense of unity and support among individuals, thereby creating a harmonious society.
