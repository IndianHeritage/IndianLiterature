आ नः पवस्व वसुमद धिरण्यवद अश्वावद गोमद यवमत सुवीर्यम् |यूयं हि सोम पितरो मम स्थान दिवो मूर्धानः प्रस्थिता वयस्कृतः ||
ā naḥ pavasva vasumad dhiraṇyavad aśvāvad gomad yavamat suvīryam | yūyaṁ hi soma pitaro mama sthana divo mūrdhānaḥ prasthitā vayaskṛtaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā naḥ: "O you" or "to us"
    • pavasva: "may you purify"
    • vasumad: "with wealth"
    • dhiraṇyavad: "like gold"
    • aśvāvad: "like horses"
    • gomad: "with cattle"
    • yavamat: "may you grant"
    • suvīryam: "excellent strength"
    • yūyaṁ: "you all"
    • hi: "indeed"
    • soma: "the divine drink" or "nectar"
    • pitaro: "ancestors" or "fathers"
    • mama: "my"
    • sthāna: "place" or "position"
    • divo: "of the sky" or "heaven"
    • mūrdhānaḥ: "the one who is at the head" or "supreme"
    • prasthitā: "having set forth"
    • vayaskṛtaḥ: "having strength" or "powerful"
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer for purification and the bestowal of wealth, strength, and prosperity.
    • The speaker calls upon divine entities, likening their blessings to gold, horses, and cattle, which are symbols of wealth and power in Vedic culture.
    • It acknowledges the role of ancestors and divine forces in providing strength and support.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a call for personal and communal growth, where individuals seek to purify their intentions and actions to attract prosperity.
    • For instance, in a business setting, leaders may invoke this mantra to inspire their teams to work towards a common goal, emphasizing the importance of integrity and strength in achieving success.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, to seek blessings for a prosperous and harmonious journey.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of wealth, strength, and divine blessings.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek purity in thoughts and actions while acknowledging the support of ancestors and divine forces.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, from personal growth to community development, reinforcing the idea that true prosperity comes from a balanced and virtuous life.
