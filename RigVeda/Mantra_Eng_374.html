अयोद्धेव दुर्मद आ हि जुह्वे महावीरं तुविबाधम् ऋजिषम् |नातारीद अस्य समृतिं वधानां सं रुजानः पिपिष इन्द्रशत्रुः ||
ayoddheva durmada ā hi juhve mahāvīraṁ tuvibādham ṛjīṣam | nātārīd asya samṛtiṁ vadhānāṁ saṁ rujānāḥ pipiṣa indraśatruḥ ||
  • ayoddheva durmada: This phrase can be translated as "O Indra, the one who is invincible and powerful." Here, "ayoddheva" refers to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, known for his strength and valor. "Durmada" signifies someone who is not easily defeated or is formidable.
  • ā hi juhve mahāvīraṁ: This translates to "I invoke you, O great hero." The term "mahāvīraṁ" emphasizes Indra's heroic qualities, suggesting that he is not just powerful but also a protector and a champion of righteousness.
  • tuvibādham ṛjīṣam: This can be interpreted as "who brings forth righteousness." "Tuvibādham" indicates the act of bringing forth or manifesting, while "ṛjīṣam" refers to righteousness or truth. This line highlights the importance of justice and moral order in the universe.
  • nātārīd asya samṛtiṁ: This means "do not let the enemies destroy this remembrance." "Nātārīd" implies a plea for protection against adversaries, while "samṛtiṁ" refers to memory or remembrance, suggesting the importance of preserving knowledge and wisdom.
  • vadhānāṁ saṁ rujānāḥ: This translates to "those who are afflicted by pain." "Vadhānāṁ" refers to those who are suffering or in distress, and "saṁ rujānāḥ" indicates a collective experience of pain or hardship.
  • pipiṣa indraśatruḥ: This can be interpreted as "O Indra, destroyer of enemies." "Pipiṣa" means to crush or destroy, while "indraśatruḥ" refers to the enemies of Indra, symbolizing the forces of chaos and negativity that threaten order and peace.

Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation to Indra, seeking his protection and assistance in overcoming adversities and maintaining righteousness. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in situations where individuals or communities face challenges, whether they be personal struggles, societal issues, or conflicts. By calling upon the qualities of strength, heroism, and justice embodied by Indra, one can find inspiration to confront difficulties and strive for a just and harmonious existence. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the importance of courage, moral integrity, and the collective effort to overcome challenges, making it relevant in both personal and communal contexts.
