ऋतस्य वो रथ्यः पूतदक्षाण ऋतस्य पस्त्यसदो अदब्धान् |तां ā नमोभिर उरुचक्षसो नृन् विश्वान् वा ā नामे महो यजत्राः ||
ṛtasya vo rathyaḥ pūtadakṣān ṛtasya pastyasado adabdhān | tām̐ ā namobhir urucakṣaso nṝn viśvān va ā name maho yajatrāḥ ||
  • ṛtasya: This word refers to "truth" or "cosmic order." In real life, it can be related to the concept of living in harmony with the natural laws and ethical principles that govern our existence.
  • vo: This means "to you," indicating a direction of reverence or offering towards a higher power or the divine.
  • rathyaḥ: Translated as "chariots," it symbolizes the vehicles of progress and movement in life. Just as chariots carry warriors into battle, our actions and decisions drive us forward in our personal journeys.
  • pūtadakṣān: This term means "those who are pure and capable." It emphasizes the importance of purity in thought and action, akin to how individuals in society are respected for their integrity and competence.
  • pastyasado: This can be interpreted as "the abode of strength." It signifies a place or state where strength resides, which can be likened to a supportive community or a strong foundation in one's life.
  • adabdhān: Meaning "unharmed" or "untouched," this word reflects the idea of resilience and protection in the face of challenges, similar to how individuals strive to maintain their well-being amidst adversity.
  • tām̐: This word translates to "that," referring to the divine or the ultimate truth that one seeks.
  • ā namobhir: This phrase means "with reverence," indicating a respectful approach towards the divine or the principles of truth.
  • urucakṣaso: This term means "of wide vision," suggesting the importance of having a broad perspective in life, akin to leaders who consider various viewpoints before making decisions.
  • nṝn: This translates to "of men" or "humankind," emphasizing the collective nature of human experience and the interconnectedness of individuals.
  • viśvān: Meaning "all" or "universal," it signifies inclusivity and the idea that the truth applies to everyone, regardless of background or belief.
  • va: This means "and," connecting ideas and emphasizing unity.
  • ā name: This phrase translates to "we bow down," indicating humility and respect towards the divine or the principles being invoked.
  • maho: Meaning "great," it signifies the vastness and importance of the truth and the divine.
  • yajatrāḥ: This term refers to "those who perform sacrifices," symbolizing the act of giving and the importance of selflessness in achieving higher goals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of living in accordance with truth and cosmic order. It emphasizes the importance of purity, strength, and broad vision in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as leadership, community building, and personal development. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate challenges with resilience and contribute positively to society. The mantra serves as a reminder to approach life with reverence, humility, and a commitment to the greater good, fostering a harmonious existence for all.
