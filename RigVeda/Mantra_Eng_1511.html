अर्चामि सुम्नयन्न अहम् अन्त्यूतिम मयोभुवम् |विश्वस्य यो मन आयुयुवे मखो देव आयुयुवे मखः ||
arcāmi sumnayann aham antyūtim mayobhuvam | viśvasya yo mana āyuyuve makho deva āyuyuve makhaḥ ||
  • arcāmi: This word translates to "I invoke" or "I worship." It signifies the act of calling upon divine energies or deities for blessings and guidance.
  • sumnayann: This term means "with auspiciousness" or "with good fortune." It emphasizes the importance of positive intentions and outcomes in our prayers and actions.
  • aham: This simply means "I," indicating the personal connection and responsibility of the individual in the act of worship.
  • antyūtim: This word can be interpreted as "the ultimate goal" or "the highest achievement." It reflects the aspiration for spiritual and material fulfillment.
  • mayobhuvam: This translates to "that which is born of my mind" or "my creation." It suggests that our thoughts and intentions shape our reality.
  • viśvasya: This means "of the universe" or "of all." It indicates a universal connection and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • yo: This word means "who" or "that which," referring to the divine or universal force being invoked.
  • mana: This translates to "mind" or "thought." It highlights the significance of mental focus and clarity in achieving one's goals.
  • āyuyuve: This means "may it grant longevity" or "may it bestow life." It reflects the desire for health, vitality, and a long life.
  • makho: This term refers to "sacrifice" or "offering." It signifies the importance of giving and sharing in the pursuit of spiritual and material wealth.
  • deva: This translates to "god" or "divine being," representing the higher powers we seek to connect with through our prayers.
  • makhaḥ: This word can be interpreted as "the act of sacrifice" or "the essence of offering." It emphasizes the significance of selflessness and devotion in our spiritual practices.

Conclusion: The mantra "arcāmi sumnayann aham antyūtim mayobhuvam | viśvasya yo mana āyuyuve makho deva āyuyuve makhaḥ ||" encapsulates the essence of invoking divine blessings with positive intentions for the ultimate goals of life. It teaches us that our thoughts and actions shape our reality and that we are interconnected with the universe. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal prayers, meditation, or rituals where one seeks guidance, health, and prosperity. By focusing on auspiciousness and selfless offerings, individuals can cultivate a deeper connection with the divine and manifest their desires in alignment with universal principles.
