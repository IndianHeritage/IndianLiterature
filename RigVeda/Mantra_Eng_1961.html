यत् ते सोम गवाशिरो यवाशिरो भजामहे |वातापे पीव इद्भव ||
yat te soma gavāśiro yavāśiro bhajāmahe | vātāpe pīva id bhava ||
  • Mantra: "yat te soma gavāśiro yavāśiro bhajāmahe | vātāpe pīva id bhava ||"
  • Translation: "We partake of your soma, O giver of strength, O source of nourishment; may you, O Wind, be invigorated."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yat: that which
    • te: your
    • soma: soma, a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and strength
    • gavāśiro: of the cows, symbolizing wealth and nourishment
    • yavāśiro: of the grains, representing sustenance and prosperity
    • bhajāmahe: we partake or we worship
    • vātāpe: O Wind, a deity representing the vital breath and life force
    • pīva: be invigorated or be filled
    • id: this
    • bhava: may you become or may you be
  • Context and Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer for strength and nourishment, invoking the sacred soma, which is often associated with divine energy and vitality.
    • The reference to "cows" and "grains" highlights the importance of agriculture and livestock in sustaining life, reflecting the agrarian society of ancient India.
    • The invocation of the Wind (Vāta) symbolizes the essential life force that sustains all living beings, emphasizing the interconnectedness of nature and life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern agriculture, the importance of sustainable practices can be likened to the reverence for grains and livestock in this mantra. Farmers today strive to cultivate crops and raise animals in harmony with nature, ensuring nourishment for future generations.
    • The concept of "soma" can be related to the modern understanding of health and wellness, where people seek out nutritious foods and supplements to enhance their vitality and well-being.
    • The invocation of the Wind can be seen in practices like yoga and meditation, where breath control (pranayama) is essential for maintaining physical and mental health.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of nourishment—both physical and spiritual—in our lives.
    • It encourages us to seek strength and vitality through our connection with nature and the divine, fostering a sense of gratitude for the resources we have.
    • In a world where many face challenges related to health and sustainability, the teachings of this mantra can inspire us to cultivate a balanced and harmonious life.
