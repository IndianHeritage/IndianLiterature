स युध्मः सत्वा खजकृत समद्वा तुविमरक्षो नदुनामां ऋजीषी |बृहदरेणुश्च्यवनो मानवशीनाम एकः कृष्ण्तीनाम अभवत सहावा ||
sa yudhmaḥ satvā khajakṛt samadvā tuvimrakṣo nadanumām̐ ṛjīṣī | bṛhadreṇuś cyavano mānuṣīṇām ekaḥ kṛṣṭīnām abhavat sahāvā ||
  • sa yudhmaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "he who is engaged in battle" or "the one who fights." It signifies the spirit of struggle and effort in life.
  • satvā: This word means "strength" or "vitality." It represents the inner strength that one possesses to face challenges.
  • khajakṛt: This term can be understood as "the one who creates" or "the maker." It emphasizes the importance of creativity and innovation in overcoming obstacles.
  • samadvā: This means "with equality" or "in harmony." It suggests the need for balance and fairness in our actions and decisions.
  • tuvimrakṣo: This can be translated as "protectors" or "guardians." It highlights the role of those who safeguard and support us in our endeavors.
  • nadanumām̐: This phrase refers to "the one who is praised" or "the one who is celebrated." It signifies the recognition and appreciation of efforts and achievements.
  • ṛjīṣī: This word means "truth" or "righteousness." It emphasizes the importance of adhering to moral values and principles in our actions.
  • bṛhadreṇuś: This can be interpreted as "the great dust" or "the vastness." It symbolizes the expansive nature of existence and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • cyavano: This term refers to "the one who is wise" or "the knowledgeable." It underscores the value of wisdom and understanding in navigating life's challenges.
  • mānuṣīṇām: This means "of humans" or "pertaining to mankind." It highlights the human experience and the shared journey of life.
  • ekaḥ: This word means "one" or "unity." It signifies the importance of coming together and working collaboratively towards common goals.
  • kṛṣṭīnām: This can be translated as "of the cultivated" or "of the productive." It emphasizes the significance of hard work and cultivation in achieving success.
  • abhavat: This means "became" or "was." It indicates a transformation or realization that occurs through effort and dedication.
  • sahāvā: This term refers to "together" or "in unity." It reinforces the idea of collective effort and support in achieving goals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of human struggle, strength, creativity, and the importance of unity and righteousness. In real life, it serves as a reminder that challenges are an integral part of our journey, and by harnessing our inner strength and working together, we can overcome obstacles and achieve our goals. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as teamwork in professional settings, community building, and personal development, where collaboration, moral integrity, and recognition of each other's efforts are crucial for success.
