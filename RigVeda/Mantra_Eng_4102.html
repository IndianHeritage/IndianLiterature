आ याता मरुतो दिव आन्तरिक्षाद अमाद उता |मावा स्थाता परावतः ||
ā yāta maruto diva āntarikṣād amād uta | māva sthāta parāvataḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yāta: "Come forth" or "arrive."
    • maruto: "O Winds" or "O Storms," referring to the Maruts, the storm deities.
    • diva: "from the sky" or "heaven."
    • āntarikṣād: "from the atmosphere" or "from the space between heaven and earth."
    • amād uta: "with great strength" or "with vigor."
    • māva: "do not" or "may not."
    • sthāta: "stand" or "remain."
    • parāvataḥ: "O Supreme Wind" or "O Great Wind."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Maruts, the divine winds, asking them to come forth from the heavens and the atmosphere.
    • It emphasizes the power and strength of these winds, suggesting that they bring vitality and energy.
    • The plea "do not stand" indicates a desire for the winds to remain active and dynamic, rather than stagnant.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, winds play a crucial role in weather patterns, influencing everything from rain to temperature. This mantra can be seen as a call for the natural forces to remain in motion, ensuring a balanced ecosystem.
    • In a metaphorical sense, the winds can represent change and progress in our lives. Just as the winds are essential for the movement of clouds and weather, we too need the winds of change to propel us forward in our personal and professional journeys.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of dynamic forces in both nature and life. It serves as a reminder of the importance of movement, change, and the energy that drives us.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to embrace change and remain active in our pursuits, rather than becoming stagnant. The invocation of the Maruts symbolizes a call for strength and vitality in our endeavors.
    • Overall, the mantra can be applied in various contexts, from environmental awareness to personal growth, emphasizing the need for continuous movement and adaptation in life.
