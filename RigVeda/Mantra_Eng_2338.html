कुमारश्चित्पितरं वन्दमानं प्रति नानाम रुद्रोपयन्तम् |भूरेर दातारं सत्पतिं गृणीषे स्तुतस्त्वं भेषजाः राश्यस्मे ||
kumāraś cit pitaraṁ vandamānam prati nānāma rudropayantam | bhūrer dātāraṁ satpatiṁ gṛṇīṣe stutas tvam bheṣajā rāsy asme ||
  • Kumāraś cit pitaraṁ: This phrase can be translated as "the son who is worthy of respect." In many cultures, including Indian traditions, the respect for one's parents, especially fathers, is paramount. For example, in modern society, children often look up to their fathers as role models, seeking their guidance and wisdom.
  • Vandamānam prati: This means "paying homage." In real life, this can be seen in various rituals and ceremonies where individuals pay respect to their elders or deities. For instance, during festivals like Diwali, people perform puja (worship) to honor their family deities and ancestors.
  • Nānāma rudropayantam: This translates to "the many names of Rudra." Rudra is a form of Shiva, representing the fierce and destructive aspects of nature. In everyday life, this can be related to the various challenges and adversities one faces, which can be seen as manifestations of Rudra's energy. For example, during natural disasters, people often turn to spirituality for strength and resilience.
  • Bhūrer dātāraṁ: This means "the giver of the earth." It signifies the importance of nature and the resources it provides. In contemporary times, this can be linked to environmental conservation efforts, where individuals and organizations work to protect the earth's resources for future generations. For instance, initiatives like tree planting and sustainable farming practices reflect this principle.
  • Satpatiṁ gṛṇīṣe: This translates to "I praise the lord of truth." This can be related to the pursuit of truth and integrity in our lives. In real life, this is applicable in various fields, such as journalism, where the commitment to truth is essential for credibility and trustworthiness.
  • Stutas tvam bheṣajā rāsy asme: This means "we praise you, the source of healing." This highlights the importance of health and well-being. In today's world, this can be seen in the emphasis on holistic health practices, such as yoga and meditation, which promote physical and mental wellness.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes respect for parents, the importance of nature, the pursuit of truth, and the significance of healing. In real life, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and social lives, encouraging them to honor their roots, protect the environment, seek truth, and prioritize health. By embodying these values, one can lead a more fulfilling and harmonious life, contributing positively to society.
