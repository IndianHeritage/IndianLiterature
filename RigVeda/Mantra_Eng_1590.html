कथा ते अग्नि शुचयन्त आयोर्ददाशुर वाजेभिर आशुषाणः |उभे यत टोके तनये दधानाः ऋतस्य सामन रणयन्त देवाः ||
kathā te agne śucayanta āyor dadāśur vājebhir āśuṣāṇāḥ | ubhe yat toke tanaye dadhānā ṛtasya sāman raṇayanta devāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Kathā: How
    • Te: Your
    • Agne: O Agni (the Fire God)
    • Śucayanta: Purifying
    • Āyor: Of life
    • Dadāśur: They have given
    • Vājebhir: With strength
    • Āśuṣāṇāḥ: Quickly
    • Ubhe: Both
    • Yat: That which
    • Toke: In the offspring
    • Tanaye: In the progeny
    • Dadhānā: Holding
    • Ṛtasya: Of truth
    • Sāman: Hymn
    • Raṇayanta: Invoking
    • Devāḥ: The gods
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Agni, the deity of fire, symbolizing purity and transformation.
    • It speaks of how Agni purifies life and provides strength quickly, emphasizing the importance of divine support in our endeavors.
    • The reference to "both" in the context of offspring suggests the significance of family and lineage in the pursuit of truth and righteousness.
    • The invocation of the gods through hymns highlights the role of spirituality and devotion in achieving one's goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, parents often seek blessings for their children to grow up strong and virtuous, akin to invoking Agni's purifying strength.
    • In professional life, individuals may perform rituals or prayers before starting a new venture, seeking divine guidance and support, similar to the invocation of the gods in this mantra.
    • Communities often come together in festivals to honor deities, reinforcing the idea of collective strength and purity in their pursuits.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine support in our lives, emphasizing purity, strength, and the pursuit of truth.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to family well-being and community strength.
    • By invoking the blessings of Agni and the gods, individuals can find guidance and support in their endeavors, fostering a sense of connection to the divine and to each other.
