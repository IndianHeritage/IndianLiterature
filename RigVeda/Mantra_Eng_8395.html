तं त्वा देवभ्यो मधुमत्तमं नरः सहस्रधारं दूहते दश क्षिपः |नृभिः सोम प्रच्युतो ग्रावभिः सुतो विश्वान देवामां आव पवस्वा सहस्रजित ||
taṁ tvā devebhyo madhumattamaṁ naraḥ sahasradhāraṁ duhate daśa kṣipaḥ | nṛbhiḥ soma pracyuto grāvabhiḥ suto viśvān devām̐ ā pavasvā sahasrajit ||
  • taṁ tvā devebhyo - This phrase translates to "to you, O divine one." It signifies a call to the divine, acknowledging the presence of higher powers or deities.
  • madhumattamaṁ - This means "the sweetest" or "the most delightful." It refers to the essence of joy and happiness that one seeks from the divine.
  • naraḥ sahasradhāraṁ - This translates to "a man with a thousand streams." It symbolizes abundance and the flow of resources or blessings in life.
  • duhate daśa kṣipaḥ - This means "who brings forth tenfold." It emphasizes the idea of multiplication and prosperity, suggesting that the divine provides bountiful returns.
  • nṛbhiḥ soma - "With men, the soma." Soma is often associated with a divine drink that grants immortality and bliss, representing the connection between humans and the divine.
  • pracyuto grāvabhiḥ - This translates to "with the flowing streams." It indicates the continuous flow of blessings and the importance of maintaining a connection with the divine.
  • suto viśvān devām̐ - "The son of all gods." This phrase signifies the unity of all divine forces and their role in the universe.
  • ā pavasvā sahasrajit - "Come to us, O thousand-fold." This is a plea for the divine to bestow blessings and abundance upon the supplicant.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the relationship between humans and the divine, highlighting themes of abundance, joy, and the flow of blessings. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as seeking prosperity in business, fostering relationships, or even in personal growth. By invoking the divine and recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings, individuals can cultivate a mindset of gratitude and openness to receive blessings. This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of spiritual connection and the potential for abundance in all aspects of life.
