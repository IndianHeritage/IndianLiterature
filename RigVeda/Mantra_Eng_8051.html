अभ्यर्ष विचक्षण पवित्रं धारयासुतः |अभि वाजम् उता श्रवः ||
abhy arṣa vicakṣaṇa pavitraṁ dhārayā sutaḥ | abhi vājam uta śravaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhy: towards, for
    • arṣa: the seer, sage, or wise person
    • vicakṣaṇa: one who is perceptive or insightful
    • pavitraṁ: pure, sacred
    • dhārayā: by holding, maintaining
    • sutaḥ: offspring, child
    • abhi: towards, for
    • vājam: strength, power, or nourishment
    • uta: and, also
    • śravaḥ: fame, glory, or reputation
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and purity in nurturing the next generation.
    • The term "abhy arṣa" suggests a direction towards the wise, indicating that one should seek guidance from those who possess knowledge.
    • "Vicakṣaṇa" refers to the ability to perceive and understand deeply, which is essential for making informed decisions.
    • "Pavitraṁ" highlights the significance of purity, both in thought and action, as a foundation for raising children.
    • "Dhārayā sutaḥ" implies the responsibility of parents or guardians to hold and guide their children with care and wisdom.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "abhi vājam uta śravaḥ," suggests that by nurturing children with strength and wisdom, they will gain fame and respect in society.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Parents who instill values of honesty and integrity in their children are practicing the essence of this mantra.
    • Teachers who guide students with wisdom and insight help shape the future leaders of society.
    • Community leaders who promote purity of thought and action contribute to a healthier society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the vital role that wisdom, purity, and strength play in the upbringing of the next generation.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as parenting, education, and community leadership.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more enlightened and harmonious society.
