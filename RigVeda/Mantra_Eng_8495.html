राजा सिंधूनाम अवसिष्ट वास ऋतस्य नावम आरुहद रजिष्ठाम् |अप्सु द्रप्सो वावृधे श्येनजूतो दूह इम् पिता दूह इम् पितुर जाम् ||
rājā sindhūnām avasiṣṭa vāsa ṛtasya nāvam āruhad rajiṣṭhām | apsu drapso vāvṛdhe śyenajūto duha īm pitā duha īm pitur jām ||
  • Mantra: "rājā sindhūnām avasiṣṭa vāsa ṛtasya nāvam āruhad rajiṣṭhām | apsu drapso vāvṛdhe śyenajūto duha īm pitā duha īm pitur jām ||"
  • Translation: "The king who has taken his abode in the rivers, has mounted the ship of truth; may he, the one who is like a falcon, increase the waters and nourish the offspring of the father."

  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • rājā: King
    • sindhūnām: of the rivers
    • avasiṣṭa: who has taken abode
    • vāsa: dwelling
    • ṛtasya: of truth
    • nāvam: ship
    • āruhad: has mounted
    • rajiṣṭhām: of the best
    • apsu: in the waters
    • drapso: of the offspring
    • vāvṛdhe: may he increase
    • śyenajūto: like a falcon
    • duha: nourish
    • īm: this
    • pitā: father
    • duha: nourish
    • īm: this
    • pitur: of the father
    • jām: offspring

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of a king who resides in harmony with nature, particularly the rivers, which symbolize life and sustenance.
    • The "ship of truth" signifies a journey or leadership that is guided by righteousness and integrity.
    • The imagery of a falcon suggests strength, vision, and the ability to rise above challenges, reflecting the qualities of a good leader.
    • The call to increase the waters can be interpreted as a plea for abundance and prosperity, not just for oneself but for the community and future generations.
    • In real life, this can be seen in leaders who prioritize environmental sustainability, ensuring that natural resources are preserved for future generations.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of leadership that is rooted in truth and the well-being of the community.
    • It serves as a reminder that true leadership involves nurturing and supporting the growth of others, much like a father caring for his children.
    • In contemporary society, this can be applied to various fields such as politics, business, and community service, where leaders are called to act with integrity and foster a sense of collective responsibility.
    • Ultimately, the mantra encourages us to reflect on our roles as leaders in our own lives, urging us to create a positive impact on our surroundings and future generations.
