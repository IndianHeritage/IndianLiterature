एहि मनुर देवयुर यज्ञकामो 'रंक्रित्य तमसि क्षेष्य अग्ने |सुगान पथः कृणुहि देवयानान वहा हव्यानि सुमनस्यमानः ||
ehi manur devayur yajñakāmo 'raṁkṛtyā tamasi kṣeṣy agne | sugān pathaḥ kṛṇuhi devayānān vaha havyāni sumanasyamānaḥ ||
  • ehi manur devayur: This phrase can be translated as "Come, O Manu, the divine one." In Hindu mythology, Manu is considered the progenitor of humanity and a divine figure. This invocation suggests a call for guidance and wisdom from a higher power.
  • yajñakāmo 'raṁkṛtyā: This translates to "desiring the sacrifice." In Vedic traditions, yajña (sacrifice) is a central theme, symbolizing the act of giving and the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine. It emphasizes the need for selfless actions and contributions to the greater good.
  • tamasi kṣeṣy agne: This means "may you dispel the darkness, O Agni." Agni, the fire god, represents light, knowledge, and purification. This line signifies the desire for enlightenment and the removal of ignorance and negativity from one’s life.
  • sugān pathaḥ kṛṇuhi: Translated as "make the paths easy." This line expresses a wish for smooth and favorable journeys in life, both literally and metaphorically. It reflects the human desire for guidance and support in navigating life's challenges.
  • devayānān vaha havyāni: This means "carry the offerings to the divine." It highlights the importance of devotion and the act of giving to the divine, which is a recurring theme in many spiritual practices. It signifies the connection between the material and spiritual worlds.
  • sumanasyamānaḥ: This translates to "with a joyful mind." It emphasizes the importance of approaching rituals and life with a positive and joyful attitude. A joyful mindset can enhance the quality of our actions and interactions with others.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, the importance of selfless actions, and the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:
    • In personal development, individuals can invoke the spirit of Manu by striving to be better versions of themselves, contributing positively to society.
    • In community service, the idea of yajña can inspire people to engage in selfless acts that benefit others, fostering a sense of unity and cooperation.
    • In education, the call to Agni for dispelling darkness can be seen as a metaphor for the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, encouraging lifelong learning.
    • In daily life, the desire for "easy paths" can motivate individuals to seek support and guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders when facing challenges.
    • Finally, approaching life with a "joyful mind" can enhance mental well-being and improve relationships, reminding us to find joy in our actions and interactions.
