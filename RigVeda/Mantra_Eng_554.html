वावसानā विवस्वति सोमस्य पीत्या गिरा |मनुष्यच चम्भू आगतम् ||
vāvasānā vivasvati somasya pītyā girā | manuṣvac chambhū ā gatam ||
  • vāvasānā: This term can be interpreted as "the one who has reached the end" or "the one who has completed." It signifies the culmination of a journey or a phase in life.
  • vivasvati: This word refers to "the sun" or "radiance." It symbolizes enlightenment, knowledge, and the dispelling of darkness, both literally and metaphorically.
  • somasya: This relates to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition that represents immortality and divine inspiration. It is often associated with the moon and the nourishing aspects of life.
  • pītyā: This term means "through the essence" or "by the nature of." It indicates a process of transformation or realization that occurs through understanding the core of something.
  • girā: This word translates to "speech" or "sound." It emphasizes the power of words and communication in shaping reality and conveying knowledge.
  • manuṣvac: This term can be understood as "human" or "mankind." It highlights the human experience and the journey of individuals within the larger context of existence.
  • chambhū: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is born" or "the one who has emerged." It signifies new beginnings and the potential for growth and development.
  • ā gatam: This phrase means "has gone" or "has arrived." It indicates a sense of completion or achievement, suggesting that a goal has been reached or a journey has concluded.

The mantra encapsulates a profound journey of transformation and realization. It speaks to the human experience of reaching enlightenment through knowledge and communication, symbolized by the sun and the sacred drink of Soma. The essence of this mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as:


  • Personal Growth: Individuals often go through phases of learning and self-discovery. This mantra encourages embracing the journey and recognizing the importance of knowledge and communication in achieving personal goals.
  • Education: In educational settings, the mantra highlights the significance of enlightenment through learning. It serves as a reminder that knowledge is a powerful tool for transformation.
  • Community Building: The emphasis on human connection and communication can be applied in fostering relationships within communities. It encourages dialogue and understanding as means to achieve collective goals.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the journey of life, emphasizing the importance of knowledge, communication, and personal transformation. It serves as a reminder that through understanding and enlightenment, individuals can reach their goals and contribute positively to the world around them.
