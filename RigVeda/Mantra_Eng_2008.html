तवाग्ने होत्रं तव पोत्रं ऋत्वियं तव नेष्ट्रं त्वं अग्निद ऋतायतः |तव प्रशास्त्रं त्वं अध्वरियसि ब्रह्मा चासि गृहपतिश च नो दमे ||
tavāgne hotraṁ tava potram ṛtviyaṁ tava neṣṭraṁ tvam agnid ṛtāyataḥ | tava praśāstraṁ tvam adhvarīyasi brahmā cāsi gṛhapatiś ca no dame ||
  • tavāgne: This word refers to "O Agni," addressing the fire god, Agni, who is a central figure in Vedic rituals and represents the divine messenger between humans and gods.
  • hotraṁ: This means "the offering" or "the sacrifice," indicating the act of worship and the offerings made to the deities, particularly through the medium of fire.
  • tava potram: Translated as "your pot," this symbolizes the vessel or container that holds the offerings, emphasizing the importance of preparation and purity in rituals.
  • ṛtviyaṁ: This term means "the truth" or "the righteous path," highlighting the significance of following dharma (righteousness) in life and rituals.
  • tava neṣṭraṁ: This translates to "your protection," indicating the safeguarding and blessings that come from the divine when one performs rituals with sincerity.
  • tvam agnid: Meaning "you, O Agni," it reiterates the invocation of Agni as a vital force in the universe, representing energy and transformation.
  • ṛtāyataḥ: This word signifies "eternal truth," suggesting that the principles of righteousness and truth are everlasting and fundamental to existence.
  • tava praśāstraṁ: This means "your command" or "your authority," indicating the guidance and wisdom that Agni provides to those who seek it through devotion.
  • tvam adhvarīyasi: Translated as "you are the priest," this emphasizes Agni's role in rituals, acting as a mediator between the divine and the earthly realms.
  • brahmā cāsi: This means "you are Brahman," indicating that Agni embodies the ultimate reality and consciousness, connecting the material and spiritual worlds.
  • gṛhapatiś ca no dame: This translates to "and you are the lord of the house," signifying the importance of Agni in domestic life, representing prosperity, protection, and harmony within the household.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the significance of Agni as a divine force that facilitates communication between humans and the divine. It emphasizes the importance of rituals, truth, and righteousness in daily life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects, such as family gatherings, religious ceremonies, and personal reflections on one's values and actions. By invoking Agni, individuals seek blessings for protection, prosperity, and guidance, reinforcing the idea that spiritual practices can lead to a harmonious and fulfilling life.
