प्रियास इति ते मगवन्न अभिष्टौ नरो मदेम शरणे सखायः |नि तुर्वशं नि याद्वं शिशीह्य अतिथिग्वाय शंस्यं करिष्यन् ||
priyāsa it te maghavann abhiṣṭau naro madema śaraṇe sakhāyaḥ | ni turvaśaṁ ni yādvaṁ śiśīhy atithigvāya śaṁsyaṁ kariṣyan ||
  • Mantra: "priyāsa it te maghavann abhiṣṭau naro madema śaraṇe sakhāyaḥ | ni turvaśaṁ ni yādvaṁ śiśīhy atithigvāya śaṁsyaṁ kariṣyan ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for friendship and support. It expresses a desire for the blessings of the divine (Maghavan) and seeks protection and companionship in times of need.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • priyāsa: dear or beloved
    • it: here or this
    • te: your
    • maghavann: O Maghavan, a name for the divine, often associated with abundance and prosperity
    • abhiṣṭau: blessings or desires
    • naro: man or human
    • madema: may we be joyful or happy
    • śaraṇe: in refuge or protection
    • sakhāyaḥ: friends or companions
    • ni: not
    • turvaśaṁ: to be troubled or disturbed
    • ni yādvaṁ: not to be forgotten
    • śiśīhy: may we be protected
    • atithigvāya: for the guest or visitor
    • śaṁsyaṁ: peace or well-being
    • kariṣyan: may we accomplish or achieve
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be recited during gatherings to invoke a sense of unity and support among members, emphasizing the importance of friendship and collective well-being.
    • In personal relationships, individuals can reflect on this mantra to appreciate their friends and loved ones, fostering a deeper connection and understanding of mutual support.
    • During challenging times, such as illness or loss, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek refuge in the company of friends and to offer support to one another.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the importance of companionship, support, and divine blessings in our lives. It teaches us to cherish our relationships and to seek refuge in the bonds we share with others. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, community gatherings, or during times of hardship, this mantra serves as a guiding principle to foster love, support, and a sense of belonging among individuals.
