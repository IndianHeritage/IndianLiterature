मन्त्र: कर्हि स्वित सा इन्द्र चेत्यासद अगस्य यद भिनदो रक्ष एषत् | मित्रकृवो यच छसने न गावाḥ पृथिव्या आपृग अमुया शयन्ते ||
karhi svit sā ta indra cetyāsad aghasya yad bhinado rakṣa eṣat | mitrakruvo yac chasane na gāvaḥ pṛthivyā āpṛg amuyā śayante ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • karhi: when
    • svit: indeed
    • sā: that
    • ta: that
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • cetyāsad: you who are aware
    • aghasya: of the sin
    • yad: which
    • bhinado: you have broken
    • rakṣa: protect
    • eṣat: this
    • mitrakruvo: O friend of the good
    • yac: whatever
    • chasane: in the sky
    • na: not
    • gāvaḥ: the cows
    • pṛthivyā: of the earth
    • āpṛg: have come
    • amuyā: to this place
    • śayante: lie down
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the deity associated with strength and rain, asking for protection and guidance.
    • The phrase "O Indra, you who are aware of the sin" suggests a plea for divine awareness and intervention in human affairs.
    • The reference to "the cows" symbolizes wealth, prosperity, and nourishment, which are essential for life and community.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking protection from negative influences and ensuring the well-being of the community.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of crisis, such as natural disasters or personal hardships, individuals often turn to spiritual practices or prayers for guidance and protection, similar to the invocation of Indra in this mantra.
    • Communities may come together to seek blessings for a good harvest, reflecting the importance of prosperity and sustenance as highlighted in the mantra.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to be aware of our actions and their consequences, encouraging ethical behavior and community support.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine protection and awareness, emphasizing the interconnectedness of individuals and their community.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek guidance in times of need and to be mindful of our actions, fostering a sense of responsibility towards others.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to come together in times of difficulty, promoting unity and support.
