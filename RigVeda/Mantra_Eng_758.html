दाधार क्षेमम् ओको न रण्वो यवो न पक्वो जेता जनानाम् ||
dādhāra kṣemam oko na raṇvo yavo na pakvo jetā janānām ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • dādhāra: This term can be interpreted as "to hold" or "to support." It signifies the act of providing stability or sustenance.
    • kṣemam: This word translates to "well-being" or "safety." It represents a state of peace and security.
    • oko: This can mean "home" or "shelter," indicating a place of comfort and protection.
    • na: This is a negation, meaning "not." It emphasizes the absence of something.
    • raṇvo: This term relates to "conflict" or "battle." It suggests strife or struggle.
    • yavo: This can be interpreted as "youth" or "vigor," often associated with energy and potential.
    • na pakvo: This phrase means "not ripe" or "not mature," indicating something that is still developing or incomplete.
    • jetā: This word translates to "victor" or "conqueror," referring to someone who overcomes challenges.
    • janānām: This means "of the people" or "of humanity," emphasizing a collective aspect.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • The mantra speaks to the importance of providing support and well-being to individuals and communities.
    • It emphasizes the need for a safe and nurturing environment, free from conflict and strife.
    • The reference to youth and vigor suggests that the mantra is also about fostering potential and growth.
    • By stating "not ripe," it acknowledges that development is a process, and maturity comes with time and experience.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the idea of overcoming challenges and achieving victory as a collective.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, parents provide a nurturing environment for their children, ensuring their safety and well-being, much like the "oko" mentioned in the mantra.
    • In communities, initiatives that promote peace and conflict resolution reflect the essence of "na raṇvo," working towards a harmonious society.
    • Educational programs that support youth development embody the idea of fostering "yavo," helping young individuals realize their potential.
    • Support systems in workplaces that encourage growth and development align with the concept of "jetā," where individuals can overcome challenges together.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of creating supportive environments that promote well-being and growth.
    • Its significance lies in the collective responsibility to nurture and protect one another, fostering a sense of community.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and organizations to prioritize safety, development, and conflict resolution, ultimately leading to a more harmonious society.
