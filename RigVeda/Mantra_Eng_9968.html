मन्त्र: निर आहावान कृतनोतना सं वरत्रा दधातना |सिञ्चामहा अवतम उद्रिणं वयं सुशेकम अनुपक्षितम् ||
nir āhāvān kṛṇotana saṁ varatrā dadhātana | siñcāmahā avatam udriṇaṁ vayaṁ suṣekam anupakṣitam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nir: without
    • āhāvān: calling or invoking
    • kṛṇotana: to create or to make
    • saṁ: together or in union
    • varatrā: with protection or blessings
    • dadhātana: bestowing or giving
    • siñcāma: we pour or we sprinkle
    • hā: indeed
    • avatam: the great or the supreme
    • udriṇaṁ: from the heights or from above
    • vayaṁ: we
    • suṣekam: well-watered or nourished
    • anupakṣitam: without neglect or care
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of invoking a higher power or divine presence to create a state of abundance and protection.
    • The phrase "without calling" suggests a state of being where one does not need to invoke help, as it is inherently present.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and collective effort in achieving goals.
    • The act of pouring or sprinkling signifies the nurturing aspect of life, where blessings and resources are shared generously.
    • It also highlights the significance of being well-nourished and cared for, both physically and spiritually.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied to initiatives where people come together to support each other, such as community gardens or cooperative housing projects.
    • In personal life, it can be reflected in the practice of gratitude, where one acknowledges the abundance in their life without constantly seeking more.
    • In professional environments, it can inspire teamwork and collaboration, where the focus is on collective success rather than individual accolades.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity, abundance, and the nurturing of relationships.
    • It teaches us that by working together and sharing resources, we can create a more prosperous and harmonious existence.
    • In real life, it encourages us to cultivate a mindset of gratitude and collaboration, leading to a more fulfilling and enriched life.
