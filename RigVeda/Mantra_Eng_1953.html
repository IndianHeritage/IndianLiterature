पितुं नु स्तोषम महो धर्माणं तविषीम् |यस्य तृतो व्य ओजसा वृत्रं विपर्वम् अर्दयत ||
pituṁ nu stoṣam maho dharmāṇaṁ taviṣīm | yasya trito vy ojasā vṛtraṁ viparvam ardayat ||
  • pituṁ nu stoṣam: This phrase can be translated as "We praise the father" or "We offer our homage to the father." In this context, "pituṁ" refers to the father or a paternal figure, symbolizing authority, guidance, and protection.
  • maho dharmāṇaṁ taviṣīm: This translates to "the great one of righteousness." Here, "maho" means great, "dharmāṇaṁ" refers to righteousness or moral order, and "taviṣīm" signifies strength or power. This part of the mantra emphasizes the importance of moral integrity and strength in upholding dharma.
  • yasya trito vy ojasā: This can be interpreted as "whose third part is powerful." "Yasya" means whose, "trito" refers to the third, and "ojasā" signifies energy or power. This suggests that there is a divine force or energy that is essential for the sustenance of life and the universe.
  • vṛtraṁ viparvam ardayat: This translates to "who destroys the obstructive forces." "Vṛtraṁ" refers to obstacles or demons, "viparvam" means the one who is opposite or contrary, and "ardayat" signifies the act of destroying. This part of the mantra highlights the idea of overcoming challenges and adversities in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking guidance and strength from a paternal figure or divine source. It emphasizes the importance of righteousness and moral integrity in overcoming life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied when individuals face obstacles or moral dilemmas, reminding them to seek strength from their values and the support of their community or family. It serves as a reminder that with the right guidance and adherence to dharma, one can overcome adversities and achieve a harmonious life.
