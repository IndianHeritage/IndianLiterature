आ याहि वस्व्या धियā मंहिष्ठो जारयनमखः सुदानुभिः ||
ā yāhi vasvyā dhiyā maṁhiṣṭho jārayanmakhaḥ sudānubhiḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā yāhi: This phrase can be interpreted as "come here" or "approach." It signifies an invitation or a call to a higher power or divine presence.
    • vasvyā: This term refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." It embodies the idea of abundance and the blessings that come from the divine.
    • dhiyā: This word means "intellect" or "understanding." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and clarity of thought in one's life.
    • maṁhiṣṭho: This can be translated as "the best" or "the most excellent." It denotes the highest quality or the supreme aspect of something.
    • jārayan: This term relates to "nourishing" or "sustaining." It highlights the need for nurturing and support in our lives.
    • makhaḥ: This word refers to "sacrifice" or "offering." It signifies the importance of giving and the act of devotion.
    • sudānubhiḥ: This term means "with good intentions" or "with auspiciousness." It emphasizes the need for positive actions and thoughts.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • The mantra calls upon divine forces to come forth and bless the individual with prosperity and wisdom.
    • It emphasizes the importance of nurturing one's intellect and understanding, which are essential for achieving the highest quality of life.
    • The act of sacrifice is highlighted, suggesting that giving and devotion are crucial for receiving blessings.
    • Finally, the mantra encourages the pursuit of good intentions and auspicious actions in all endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied when seeking success. Entrepreneurs often call upon their vision and intellect to make wise decisions that lead to prosperity.
    • In personal relationships, nurturing understanding and communication can lead to stronger bonds, reflecting the essence of "jārayan" or nourishing.
    • In community service, the act of giving back can be seen as a form of "makhaḥ," where individuals offer their time and resources for the betterment of society.
    • In education, students are encouraged to approach their studies with good intentions and a desire for knowledge, embodying the spirit of "sudānubhiḥ."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of prosperity, wisdom, and sacrifice in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the call to action for individuals to seek abundance through understanding and to give selflessly for the greater good.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, successful relationships, and a thriving community.
