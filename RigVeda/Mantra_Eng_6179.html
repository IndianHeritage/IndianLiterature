अर्वाञ्चं त्वा पुरुष्टुत प्रियमेधस्तुतā हरī |सोमपेयाय वक्षतः ||
arvāñcaṁ tvā puruṣṭuta priyamedhastutā harī | somapeyāya vakṣataḥ ||
  • arvāñcaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is all-encompassing" or "the one who is everywhere." It signifies the omnipresence of the divine or the universal spirit.
  • tvā: This means "you," referring to the divine or the higher power being addressed in the mantra.
  • puruṣṭuta: This word can be broken down into "puruṣa," meaning "man" or "spirit," and "tuta," which implies "praise" or "worship." Together, it suggests the worship of the universal spirit or the essence of humanity.
  • priyamedhastutā: This can be understood as "the one who is dear and worthy of praise." It emphasizes the affection and reverence towards the divine.
  • harī: This term often refers to "the one who removes sins" or "the divine." It is a name associated with various deities, particularly Vishnu.
  • somapeyāya: This translates to "the one who is offered the soma," which is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
  • vakṣataḥ: This means "may you be present" or "may you manifest." It is a call for the divine presence to be felt in the lives of the devotees.

  • Meaning and Interpretation: The mantra is a prayer that acknowledges the omnipresence of the divine and expresses a desire for the divine to be present in our lives. It highlights the importance of worshiping the universal spirit and recognizing its significance in our existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek guidance and support from a higher power during challenging times, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In community gatherings or spiritual practices, people come together to honor the divine, similar to the collective offering of soma in Vedic rituals.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine presence and acknowledgment of the universal spirit. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of reverence in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of prayer, meditation, and community worship, fostering a sense of unity and spiritual awareness among individuals.
