विष्ट्वी शमी तरणित्वेन वाघतो मर्तासः सन्तो अमृतत्वम् आनशुः |सौधन्वना ऋभवः सूरचक्षसः संवत्सरे सम अपृच्यन्त धीतिभिः ||
viṣṭvī śamī taraṇitvena vāghato martāsaḥ santo amṛtatvam ānaśuḥ | saudhanvanā ṛbhavaḥ sūracakṣasaḥ saṁvatsare sam apṛcyanta dhītibhiḥ ||
  • viṣṭvī: This term can be interpreted as "spread out" or "expanded." It signifies the vastness of existence and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • śamī: Refers to the "Shami tree," which is often associated with strength and resilience. In real life, it can symbolize the importance of having a strong foundation in our lives.
  • taraṇitvena: This word means "by the means of crossing" or "through overcoming." It suggests the idea of transcending challenges and obstacles.
  • vāghato: This term relates to "speech" or "communication." It emphasizes the power of words and how they can influence our reality.
  • martāsaḥ: This refers to "mortals" or "human beings." It highlights the human condition and our journey through life.
  • santo: Meaning "peaceful" or "virtuous," it underscores the importance of cultivating inner peace and moral integrity.
  • amṛtatvam: This term translates to "immortality" or "eternal life." It reflects the aspiration for a lasting legacy and the pursuit of spiritual enlightenment.
  • ānaśuḥ: This means "to attain" or "to achieve." It signifies the journey towards achieving higher states of being.
  • saudhanvanā: This word can be interpreted as "wealthy" or "prosperous." It emphasizes the importance of abundance in various aspects of life.
  • ṛbhavaḥ: Refers to "those who are strong" or "powerful." It highlights the strength that comes from unity and collaboration.
  • sūracakṣasaḥ: This term means "with divine vision" or "with the eyes of the gods." It suggests the importance of having a broader perspective and understanding.
  • saṁvatsare: This translates to "in the year" or "over time." It emphasizes the cyclical nature of life and the importance of patience.
  • sam apṛcyanta: This means "they are sought after" or "they are inquired about." It signifies the quest for knowledge and wisdom.
  • dhītibhiḥ: Refers to "intellect" or "understanding." It highlights the importance of wisdom in navigating life's challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of human existence, emphasizing the interconnectedness of life, the importance of resilience, communication, and the pursuit of wisdom. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to cultivate inner peace, strive for personal growth, and seek knowledge. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate the complexities of life with strength and clarity, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and meaningful existence.
