चिकित्विन्मनसं त्वा देवम् मर्तास ūtaye |वरिण्यस्य ते 'वसा इयानासो अमन्महि ||
cikitvinmanasaṁ tvā devam martāsa ūtaye | vareṇyasya te 'vasa iyānāso amanmahi ||
  • cikitvinmanasaṁ: This word can be broken down into "cikitvi," meaning "to think" or "to reflect," and "manasaṁ," which refers to the "mind." Together, it suggests a deep contemplation or reflection of the mind.
  • tvā: This translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or deity.
  • devam: This means "god" or "deity," emphasizing the divine nature of the being being addressed.
  • martāsa: This word refers to "mortals" or "human beings," highlighting the connection between the divine and human realms.
  • ūtaye: This translates to "for the benefit" or "for the welfare," indicating a purpose behind the invocation.
  • vareṇyasya: This means "of the most excellent" or "the best," suggesting a high regard for the deity being addressed.
  • te: This translates to "your," indicating possession or belonging to the deity.
  • 'vasa: This means "to dwell" or "to reside," implying a request for the deity to be present or to reside with the speaker.
  • iyānāso: This word can be interpreted as "those who are coming" or "those who are approaching," suggesting a sense of movement or arrival.
  • amanmahi: This translates to "we meditate" or "we contemplate," indicating a collective action of reflection or worship.

This mantra can be understood as a heartfelt invocation to a divine being, asking for their presence and guidance in the lives of mortals. It emphasizes the importance of reflection and contemplation in understanding one's relationship with the divine and the world around us.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Reflection: Individuals can use this mantra during meditation or prayer to seek clarity and guidance in their lives, encouraging a deeper connection with their inner selves and the divine.
  • Community Gatherings: In group settings, such as religious or spiritual gatherings, this mantra can be recited to invoke a sense of unity and collective purpose, fostering a shared connection with the divine.
  • Decision Making: When faced with important decisions, individuals can reflect on this mantra to seek divine guidance, reminding themselves of the importance of thoughtful consideration and the presence of higher wisdom.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness between the divine and human realms. It encourages individuals to engage in deep reflection and to seek the presence of the divine in their lives, ultimately guiding them towards a path of wisdom and understanding. Its significance lies in its ability to foster a sense of community, personal growth, and spiritual connection in various aspects of life.
