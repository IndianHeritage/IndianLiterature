तनूनपाद उच्यते गर्भ आसुरो नराशंसो भवति यद्विजायते |मातरिश्वा यदमिमीत मातरि वातस्य सर्गो अभवत सरीमणि ||
tanūnapād ucyate garbha āsuro narāśaṁso bhavati yad vijāyate | mātariśvā yad amimīta mātari vātasya sargo abhavat sarīmaṇi ||
  • tanūnapād ucyate: This phrase can be translated as "the body is referred to." It signifies the physical form or the manifestation of life.
  • garbha āsuro: This means "the embryo is divine." It highlights the sacredness of life from its very inception, suggesting that every being carries a divine essence.
  • narāśaṁso bhavati yad vijāyate: This translates to "the human being is born." It emphasizes the process of birth and the emergence of consciousness in the human form.
  • mātariśvā yad amimīta: This can be interpreted as "the one who moves in the sky." Mātariśvā is often associated with the wind or the life force, indicating the vital energy that sustains life.
  • mātari vātasya sargo abhavat: This means "the creation of the wind." It signifies the importance of air and breath in the cycle of life, as well as the connection between the physical and spiritual realms.
  • sarīmaṇi: This term refers to "the body." It encapsulates the idea that the physical form is a vessel for the divine spirit and consciousness.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the profound connection between the physical body, the divine essence within, and the life force that animates all beings. It emphasizes the sacredness of life from conception to birth and the vital role of the elements, particularly air, in sustaining existence. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire a deeper appreciation for the sanctity of life, encouraging individuals to respect and honor the life force within themselves and others. It serves as a reminder of our interconnectedness with nature and the universe, urging us to live in harmony with the world around us.
