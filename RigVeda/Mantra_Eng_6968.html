षष्टिं सहस्राश्वस्यायुतासनम् उष्ट्राणां विष्टिं शताः |दश श्यावीनां शताः दश त्र्यरुषीणां दश गवां सहस्राः ||
ṣaṣṭiṁ sahasrāśvyasyāyutāsanam uṣṭrānāṁ viṁśatiṁ śatā | daśa śyāvīnāṁ śatā daśa tryaruṣīṇāṁ daśa gavāṁ sahasrā ||
  • Mantra: "ṣaṣṭiṁ sahasrāśvyasyāyutāsanam uṣṭrānāṁ viṁśatiṁ śatā | daśa śyāvīnāṁ śatā daśa tryaruṣīṇāṁ daśa gavāṁ sahasrā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ṣaṣṭiṁ: sixty
    • sahasrāśvyasyā: of a thousand horses
    • ayutāsanam: the seat or place
    • uṣṭrānāṁ: of camels
    • viṁśatiṁ: twenty
    • śatā: hundreds
    • daśa: ten
    • śyāvīnāṁ: of black cattle
    • tryaruṣīṇāṁ: of threefold strength
    • gavāṁ: of cows
    • sahasrā: thousands
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of abundance and prosperity, particularly in terms of livestock, which was a significant measure of wealth in ancient times.
    • The mention of "sixty thousand horses" signifies immense power and resources, as horses were essential for transportation and warfare.
    • The reference to "twenty camels" indicates the importance of trade and travel, as camels were used in arid regions for their endurance and ability to carry heavy loads.
    • The "ten black cattle" and "ten of threefold strength" highlight the value of strong and healthy livestock, which were crucial for agricultural productivity and sustenance.
    • Finally, the "thousands of cows" symbolize nourishment and wealth, as cows were revered in Vedic culture for their milk and agricultural contributions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern agriculture, the significance of livestock remains, as farmers often measure their success by the health and number of their animals.
    • In business, the concept of abundance can be applied to the resources and assets a company possesses, which can lead to growth and stability.
    • In personal life, the mantra can inspire individuals to seek abundance in various forms, whether it be in relationships, knowledge, or financial stability.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of abundance and prosperity in life, encouraging individuals and communities to strive for growth and well-being.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition of resources as a foundation for strength and stability, applicable in various aspects of life, from agriculture to personal development.
    • By reflecting on this mantra, one can cultivate a mindset of abundance, fostering gratitude and a proactive approach to achieving goals.
