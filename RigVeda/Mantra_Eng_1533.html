तं अग्रुवः केशिनिः सं हि रेबिर ऊर्ध्वास तस्थुर ममृषीः प्रायवे पुनः |तासां जराम प्रमुञ्चन्न एति नानदद असुम परं जनयञ जीवम अस्तृतम् ||
tam agruvaḥ keśinīḥ saṁ hi rebhira ūrdhvās tasthur mamruṣīḥ prāyave punaḥ | tāsāṁ jarām pramuñcann eti nānadad asum paraṁ janayañ jīvam astṛtam ||
  • tam agruvaḥ keśinīḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the divine or the higher consciousness. "Agru" refers to the one who is ahead or foremost, while "keśinīḥ" relates to the hair or the flowing energy. This can symbolize the connection to the divine source of energy and wisdom.
  • saṁ hi rebhira ūrdhvās: "Saṁ hi" means "indeed" or "certainly," while "rebhira" refers to the vibrations or sounds that resonate. "Ūrdhvās" means "upward" or "higher." This part emphasizes the importance of elevating one's consciousness through sound and vibration, akin to how music can uplift our spirits in real life.
  • tastur mamruṣīḥ prāyave punaḥ: "Tastur" can be understood as "to be established," while "mamruṣīḥ" refers to the essence or the core of existence. "Prāyave" means "to be renewed" or "to come back." This suggests a cycle of renewal and the importance of returning to one's true essence, similar to how nature rejuvenates itself in cycles.
  • tāsāṁ jarām pramuñcann: "Tāsāṁ" refers to "of them," while "jarām" means "old age" or "decay." "Pramuñcann" indicates the act of releasing or letting go. This part speaks to the idea of transcending the limitations of aging and decay, much like how people often seek to maintain their vitality through healthy living and mindfulness practices.
  • eti nānadad asum: "Eti" means "to go" or "to reach," while "nānadad" refers to joy or bliss. "Asum" relates to the body or physical form. This suggests that by transcending limitations, one can reach a state of bliss and joy, akin to the happiness one feels when achieving personal goals or finding inner peace.
  • paraṁ janayañ jīvam astṛtam: "Paraṁ" means "the ultimate" or "supreme," while "janayañ" refers to the act of generating or creating. "Jīvam" means "life," and "astṛtam" indicates something that is established or firm. This final part emphasizes the creation of a supreme life force that is stable and enduring, similar to how individuals strive to create a fulfilling and meaningful life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the journey of elevating one's consciousness, transcending limitations, and creating a fulfilling life. It signifies the importance of connecting with the divine, embracing renewal, and striving for joy and stability in life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied through practices such as meditation, mindfulness, and self-reflection, which help individuals to connect with their higher selves, rejuvenate their spirits, and cultivate a life of purpose and happiness.
