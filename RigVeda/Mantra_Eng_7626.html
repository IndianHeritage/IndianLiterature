इष्कर्तारम् अनिष्कृतं सहस्कृतं शतमूतिं शतक्रतुम् |समानम् इन्द्रम् अवसे हवामहे वसवानं वसूजुवम् ||
iṣkartāram aniṣkṛtaṁ sahaskṛtaṁ śatamūtiṁ śatakratum | samānam indram avase havāmahe vasavānaṁ vasūjuvam ||
  • iṣkartāram: This term refers to the "doer" or "creator," emphasizing the active role of a divine force in the universe.
  • aniṣkṛtaṁ: This means "unaccomplished" or "not yet achieved," suggesting a state of potential or something that is yet to be realized.
  • sahaskṛtaṁ: This translates to "well-accomplished" or "successfully completed," indicating the fulfillment of goals or tasks.
  • śatamūtiṁ: This term can be understood as "one who has a hundred powers" or "a hundredfold strength," symbolizing immense capability and strength.
  • śatakratum: This refers to "the one who has performed a hundred sacrifices," highlighting the importance of dedication and effort in achieving success.
  • samānam: This means "similar" or "equal," suggesting a sense of unity or harmony among beings or forces.
  • indram: This refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic tradition, representing power, authority, and leadership.
  • avase: This means "we invoke" or "we call upon," indicating a plea or request for divine assistance.
  • havāmahe: This translates to "we offer" or "we worship," emphasizing the act of devotion and reverence.
  • vasavānaṁ: This term refers to "the one who is rich" or "the prosperous one," symbolizing abundance and wealth.
  • vasūjuvam: This means "the one who is full of wealth," reinforcing the idea of prosperity and success.

The mantra essentially invokes the divine forces, particularly Indra, to assist in the realization of potential and the accomplishment of goals. It emphasizes the importance of effort, unity, and the invocation of divine support in achieving success and prosperity.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in the pursuit of success and fulfillment. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, business endeavors, and community efforts. By recognizing the need for both individual effort and divine support, individuals can strive for their goals with a sense of purpose and unity, ultimately leading to a more prosperous and harmonious life.
