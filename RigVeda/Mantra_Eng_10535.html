प्राग्नये वाचमीरय वृषभाय क्षितीनाम् | स नः पार्षद् अति द्विषः ||
prāgnaye vācam īraya vṛṣabhāya kṣitīnām | sa naḥ parṣad ati dviṣaḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • prāgnaye: This term refers to wisdom or knowledge, often associated with the divine or higher understanding.
    • vācam: This means speech or voice, indicating the importance of communication and expression.
    • īraya: This can be interpreted as to inspire or to invoke, suggesting a call to action or a plea for guidance.
    • vṛṣabhāya: This term refers to strength or power, often symbolized by a bull, representing vigor and determination.
    • kṣitīnām: This means of the earth or of the world, indicating a connection to the physical realm and its inhabitants.
    • sa: This translates to 'he' or 'that', referring to a divine presence or force.
    • naḥ: This means 'us', indicating a collective or communal aspect.
    • parṣad: This refers to a council or assembly, suggesting the importance of community and collective decision-making.
    • ati: This means 'beyond' or 'exceeding', indicating a desire to surpass challenges.
    • dviṣaḥ: This translates to 'enemies' or 'those who oppose', highlighting the presence of conflict or adversity.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • The mantra calls upon divine wisdom to guide our speech and actions.
    • It emphasizes the need for strength and determination in facing challenges.
    • It acknowledges the importance of community and collective support in overcoming adversities.
    • The invocation of divine presence suggests a reliance on higher powers for guidance and protection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied when teams face conflicts or challenges. By invoking wisdom and strength, team members can communicate effectively and work together to overcome obstacles.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra serves as a reminder to seek understanding and support from loved ones when facing difficulties, fostering a sense of community.
    • In educational environments, students can use this mantra to inspire themselves to seek knowledge and express their thoughts clearly, while also supporting each other in their learning journeys.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom, communication, and community in overcoming challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that invoking higher powers and seeking collective support can lead to strength and resilience in the face of adversity.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can enhance personal growth, improve relationships, and foster a supportive environment in various aspects of life.
