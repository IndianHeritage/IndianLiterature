ये च पूर्व ऋषयो ये च नूत्ना इन्द्र ब्रह्माणि जनयन्त विप्राः |अस्मे ते सन्तु सख्या शिवानि यूम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
ye ca pūrva ṛṣayo ye ca nūtnā indra brahmāṇi janayanta viprāḥ | asme te santu sakhyā śivāni yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Translation: "Those ancient sages and the new ones, who create the divine knowledge, may they be our friends, bringing us auspiciousness and protection always."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ye ca: "those who" - refers to a group of individuals.
    • pūrva ṛṣayo: "ancient sages" - signifies the wise individuals from the past who have contributed to spiritual knowledge.
    • ye ca nūtnā: "and the new ones" - indicates contemporary sages or thinkers who continue the tradition of wisdom.
    • indra: "Indra" - the king of gods, representing strength and power.
    • brahmāṇi: "divine knowledge" - refers to the ultimate truth or spiritual wisdom.
    • janayanta: "who create" - signifies the act of generating or bringing forth knowledge.
    • viprāḥ: "the wise" - denotes those who possess knowledge and insight.
    • asme: "may they be for us" - a plea or wish for the blessings of these sages.
    • te santu: "may they be" - a wish for their presence and support.
    • sakhyā: "friends" - emphasizes the importance of companionship and support from wise individuals.
    • śivāni: "auspiciousness" - refers to the positive and beneficial aspects of life.
    • yūyam: "you all" - addressing the sages collectively.
    • pāta: "protect" - a request for safeguarding and guidance.
    • svastibhiḥ: "with auspiciousness" - invoking blessings for well-being.
    • sadā: "always" - a desire for continuous support.
    • naḥ: "for us" - indicating the recipients of these blessings.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, elders and knowledgeable individuals often guide the younger generation, similar to the ancient and new sages mentioned in the mantra.
    • In educational institutions, teachers and mentors play a crucial role in shaping the minds of students, akin to the wise individuals creating knowledge.
    • In personal life, seeking advice from experienced friends or family members can lead to better decision-making and a more auspicious life path.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, guidance, and the creation of knowledge by both ancient and contemporary sages. It highlights the need for companionship and support from wise individuals in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as education, mentorship, and community support. By invoking the blessings of these sages, we seek protection and auspiciousness in our endeavors, fostering a harmonious and enlightened existence.
