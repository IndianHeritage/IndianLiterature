वातासो न ये धुनयो जिगतनवो 'ग्नीनां न जिह्वा विरोकिणः |वर्मण्वन्तो न योधाः शिमीवन्तः पितृणां न शंसाः सुरातयः ||
vātāso na ye dhunayo jigatnavo 'gnīnāṁ na jihvā virokiṇaḥ | varmaṇvanto na yodhāḥ śimīvantaḥ pitṝṇāṁ na śaṁsāḥ surātayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vātāso: Those who are swift or quick.
    • na: Not.
    • ye: Who.
    • dhunayo: Who shake or disturb.
    • jigatnavo: Those who are engaged in battle or struggle.
    • 'gnīnāṁ: Of the fires or the divine forces.
    • na: Not.
    • jihvā: The tongue.
    • virokiṇaḥ: Those who are wise or knowledgeable.
    • varmaṇvanto: Those who possess armor or protection.
    • na: Not.
    • yodhāḥ: Warriors or fighters.
    • śimīvantaḥ: Those who are strong or powerful.
    • pitṝṇāṁ: Of the ancestors.
    • na: Not.
    • śaṁsāḥ: Those who are praised or honored.
    • surātayaḥ: Divine beings or celestial entities.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the qualities of individuals who are not easily swayed or disturbed by external forces.
    • It emphasizes the importance of inner strength and wisdom over mere physical prowess or external appearances.
    • The reference to "fires" symbolizes the divine energy or spirit that guides and protects individuals.
    • It suggests that true warriors are those who possess mental fortitude and wisdom, rather than just physical strength.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate environment, a leader who remains calm and composed during a crisis, rather than panicking, exemplifies the qualities described in this mantra.
    • A student who perseveres through challenges and maintains focus on their goals, despite distractions, embodies the essence of this teaching.
    • In sports, an athlete who demonstrates mental resilience and strategic thinking, rather than just relying on physical ability, reflects the wisdom of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of inner strength, wisdom, and resilience in the face of challenges.
    • It teaches that true valor lies not in physical might but in the ability to remain steadfast and wise.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to better decision-making, improved leadership, and a more balanced approach to challenges.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to cultivate their inner qualities to navigate life's battles effectively.
