अवासृजः प्रस्वः श्वञ्चयो गिरीन उद आज उष्रा अपिबो मधु प्रियम् |अवर्धयो वनिनो अस्य दṁससा शुशोच सूर्य ऋतजातया गिरा ||
avāsṛjaḥ prasvaḥ śvañcayo girīn ud āja usrā apibo madhu priyam | avardhayo vanino asya daṁsasā śuśoca sūrya ṛtajātayā girā ||
  • avāsṛjaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who have released" or "those who have let go." It signifies the act of freeing oneself from burdens or attachments.
  • prasvaḥ: This word means "to speak" or "to express." It emphasizes the importance of communication and the sharing of thoughts and feelings.
  • śvañcayo: This can be understood as "the one who brings forth" or "the one who generates." It reflects the idea of creation and the nurturing of new ideas or life.
  • girīn: This term refers to "mountains" or "heights." It symbolizes stability, strength, and the challenges one must overcome in life.
  • ud āja: This phrase means "to rise up" or "to ascend." It signifies progress, growth, and the pursuit of higher goals.
  • usrā: This word translates to "the sun" or "light." It represents knowledge, enlightenment, and the dispelling of darkness or ignorance.
  • apibo: This term means "to drink" or "to consume." It can symbolize the act of taking in knowledge or experiences that nourish the soul.
  • madhu: This word means "honey," often symbolizing sweetness, joy, and the rewards of life.
  • priyam: This translates to "beloved" or "dear." It emphasizes the importance of love and affection in our lives.
  • avardhayo: This term means "to increase" or "to grow." It signifies the idea of expansion and the flourishing of life.
  • vanino: This word refers to "the forest" or "nature." It symbolizes the interconnectedness of life and the importance of the natural world.
  • asya: This means "of this" or "of these." It indicates possession or belonging.
  • daṁsasā: This term can be interpreted as "with teeth" or "with strength." It reflects resilience and the ability to face challenges head-on.
  • šuśoca: This word means "to lament" or "to grieve." It acknowledges the presence of sorrow and the importance of processing emotions.
  • sūrya: This translates to "the sun," reinforcing the themes of light, knowledge, and vitality.
  • ṛtajātayā: This term means "by the truth" or "through righteousness." It emphasizes the importance of living a life aligned with truth and moral values.
  • girā: This word means "speech" or "voice." It highlights the power of words and communication in shaping our reality.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the journey of life, emphasizing the importance of releasing burdens, expressing oneself, and nurturing growth. It encourages individuals to rise above challenges, seek knowledge, and embrace the sweetness of life while acknowledging the interconnectedness of all beings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, communication in relationships, and the pursuit of truth and righteousness. By embodying these principles, individuals can lead more fulfilling and meaningful lives, contributing positively to their communities and the world at large.
