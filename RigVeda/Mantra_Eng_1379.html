जनो यो मित्रावरुणाव अभिध्रुग अपो न वां सुनोति अक्ष्णयाध्रुक् |स्वयं स यक्ष्मं हृदये न धत्त आपा यद ईं होत्राभिर ऋतावा ||
jano yo mitrāvaruṇāv abhidhrug apo na vāṁ sunoty akṣṇayādhruk | svayaṁ sa yakṣmaṁ hṛdaye ni dhatta āpa yad īṁ hotrābhir ṛtāvā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • jano: people or beings
    • yo: who
    • mitrāvaruṇāv: Mitra and Varuna (deities representing friendship and cosmic order)
    • abhidhrug: who are invoked or called upon
    • apo: waters
    • na: not
    • vāṁ: your
    • sunoty: may they hear
    • akṣṇayādhruk: with eyes that see
    • svayaṁ: by themselves
    • sa: that
    • yakṣmaṁ: disease or affliction
    • hṛdaye: in the heart
    • ni dhatta: may they place
    • āpa: waters
    • yad: which
    • īṁ: auspicious
    • hotrābhir: by the priests or those who invoke
    • ṛtāvā: in accordance with truth or cosmic order
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the deities Mitra and Varuna, who symbolize friendship, harmony, and the moral order of the universe.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and collective well-being, suggesting that the divine powers are called upon to protect and guide the people.
    • The reference to "waters" signifies purity, nourishment, and the life-giving aspects of nature, which are essential for health and prosperity.
    • The mantra also highlights the need for awareness and perception ("with eyes that see"), urging individuals to be mindful and conscious of their actions and surroundings.
    • It calls for the removal of afflictions and diseases, both physical and emotional, by invoking divine support and guidance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be recited during gatherings or rituals to foster unity and collective strength, reminding individuals of their interconnectedness.
    • In personal health practices, individuals may use this mantra as a form of meditation or affirmation to seek healing and protection from ailments.
    • In environmental contexts, the invocation of "waters" can serve as a reminder of the importance of preserving natural resources and maintaining ecological balance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the significance of community, health, and the moral order of the universe.
    • Its application in real life can be seen in various contexts, from personal well-being to communal harmony and environmental stewardship.
    • By invoking the divine presence of Mitra and Varuna, individuals and communities can seek guidance, protection, and a deeper understanding of their place within the cosmic order.
