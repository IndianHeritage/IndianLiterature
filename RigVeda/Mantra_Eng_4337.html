यस्य व्रते पृथिवी नन्नमीति यस्य व्रते शफवज जर्भुरिति |यस्य व्रत ओषधीर विश्वरूपाः स नः पार्जन्य महि शर्मा यच्छ ||
yasya vrate pṛthivī nannamīti yasya vrate śaphavaj jarbhurīti | yasya vrata oṣadhīr viśvarūpāḥ sa naḥ parjanya mahi śarma yaccha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • vrate: in the vow or commitment
    • pṛthivī: the earth
    • nannamīti: does not deny food
    • śaphavaj: like a fruitful tree
    • jarbhurīti: like a nourishing source
    • oṣadhīr: the herbs or plants
    • viśvarūpāḥ: having many forms
    • sa: he
    • naḥ: for us
    • parjanya: the rain or the divine source of nourishment
    • mahi: great
    • śarma: comfort or protection
    • yaccha: grant or give
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine connection between the earth, nourishment, and the commitment of an individual.
    • It emphasizes the importance of a vow that ensures the sustenance of life, symbolized by food and nourishment.
    • The reference to the earth and plants signifies the interconnectedness of all living beings and the environment.
    • It highlights the idea that those who are committed to nurturing life will be rewarded with abundance and protection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers who practice sustainable farming methods are often rewarded with bountiful harvests, reflecting the idea of nurturing the earth.
    • Communities that come together to support local food systems demonstrate the principle of not denying food to one another, fostering a sense of unity and care.
    • Individuals who commit to environmental conservation efforts contribute to the health of the planet, ensuring that future generations have access to resources.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of commitment to nurturing life and the environment.
    • It teaches us that our actions towards the earth and each other can lead to abundance and protection.
    • In real life, it encourages us to engage in practices that promote sustainability, community support, and respect for nature.
    • By embodying these principles, we can create a harmonious existence that benefits all living beings.
