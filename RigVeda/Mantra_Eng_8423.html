अत्यं मृजन्ति कलशे दश क्षिपः प्र विप्राणाम् मतयो वाच इरते |पवमानाः अभ्य अर्षन्ति सुश्रुतिम इन्द्रं विशन्ति मदिरासा इन्दवः ||
atyam mṛjanti kalaśe daśa kṣipaḥ pra viprāṇām matayo vāca īrate | pavamānā abhy arṣanti suṣṭutim endraṁ viśanti madirāsa indavaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "atyam mṛjanti kalaśe daśa kṣipaḥ pra viprāṇām matayo vāca īrate | pavamānā abhy arṣanti suṣṭutim endraṁ viśanti madirāsa indavaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • atyam: the ultimate truth or reality
    • mṛjanti: they cleanse or purify
    • kalaśe: in the pot or vessel
    • daśa: ten
    • kṣipaḥ: throw or cast
    • pra: forth or forward
    • viprāṇām: of the wise or learned
    • matayo: thoughts or intentions
    • vāca: speech or words
    • īrate: they are directed or focused
    • pavamānā: being purified or sanctified
    • abhy: towards or in the direction of
    • arṣanti: they attain or reach
    • suṣṭutim: good praise or commendation
    • endram: to Indra, the king of gods
    • viśanti: they enter or come into
    • madirāsa: intoxication or bliss
    • indavaḥ: the moon or the divine nectar
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the purification of thoughts and intentions, emphasizing the importance of clarity and focus in one's mind.
    • The reference to "kalaśe" (pot) symbolizes the vessel of the mind, where thoughts are collected and nurtured.
    • The act of "mṛjanti" (cleansing) suggests that one must actively work to purify their thoughts, much like how one cleans a pot before using it for cooking.
    • The phrase "daśa kṣipaḥ" (ten thrown forth) can be interpreted as the multitude of thoughts that can arise, and the need to direct them wisely.
    • The mantra highlights the role of wise individuals ("viprāṇām") who guide and inspire others through their words and intentions.
    • In real life, this can be seen in leaders or mentors who help others focus their thoughts and actions towards positive outcomes.
    • The mention of "pavamānā" (being purified) indicates that through this process of cleansing thoughts, one can attain a higher state of consciousness or bliss ("madirāsa").
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents the ultimate goal of reaching divine states of being through pure intentions and thoughts.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of mental clarity and the power of positive thoughts in achieving one's goals.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to engage in practices such as meditation, reflection, and mindful communication to purify their intentions.
    • By focusing on positive thoughts and actions, one can create a ripple effect that not only benefits themselves but also those around them.
    • Ultimately, the mantra teaches that through the purification of the mind, one can attain a state of bliss and connection with the divine, leading to a more fulfilling life.
