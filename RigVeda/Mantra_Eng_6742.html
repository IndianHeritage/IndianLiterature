दधामि ते सुतानां वृष्णे न पूर्वपाय्यम् |दिवो अमुष्य शासतो दिवं यया दिवावसो ||
dadhāmi te sutānāṁ vṛṣṇe na pūrvapāyyam | divo amuṣya śāsato divaṁ yaya divāvaso ||
  • Transliteration: dadhāmi te sutānāṁ vṛṣṇe na pūrvapāyyam |
  • Translation: "I bestow upon you, O Vṛṣṇa, sons who are not born before."
  • Context: This mantra is a prayer to Lord Vṛṣṇa, expressing a desire for blessings in the form of progeny who are unique and not a repetition of past generations.

  • Word Breakdown:
    • dadhāmi: I bestow or give.
    • te: to you.
    • sutānāṁ: sons or offspring.
    • vṛṣṇe: O Vṛṣṇa, a deity representing preservation and protection.
    • na: not.
    • pūrvapāyyam: born before or previous.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of new beginnings and the desire for a lineage that brings forth fresh perspectives and qualities. It reflects the aspiration for growth and evolution in one's family or community.

  • Real-Life Application:
    • In family planning, couples may seek blessings for children who embody new traits and strengths, contributing positively to society.
    • In a professional context, organizations may interpret this mantra as a call for innovation, encouraging the development of new ideas and approaches rather than relying solely on past methods.
    • In personal growth, individuals may use this mantra as a reminder to cultivate new skills and perspectives, moving beyond inherited beliefs or limitations.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the potential for renewal and the importance of fostering new life, ideas, and growth. It encourages individuals and communities to seek blessings for unique contributions that enhance the collective experience, making it relevant in various aspects of life, from family to professional endeavors.
