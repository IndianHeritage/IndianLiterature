आदित्यासो अदितयः स्याम पूर देवत्रा वसवो मार्त्यत्रा |सनिमा मित्रावरुणा सनन्तो भवेम द्यावापृथिवी भवन्तः ||
ādityāso aditayaḥ syāma pūr devatrā vasavo martyatrā | sanema mitrāvaruṇā sananto bhavema dyāvāpṛthivī bhavantaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ādityāso: Refers to the Adityas, the solar deities.
    • aditayaḥ: Means "may we be like the Adityas" or "may we attain the qualities of the Adityas."
    • syāma: Translates to "may we be" or "may we become."
    • pūr: Means "full" or "complete."
    • devatrā: Refers to the divine beings or deities.
    • vasavo: Refers to the Vasus, another group of deities associated with natural elements.
    • martyatrā: Means "in the mortal realm" or "among mortals."
    • sanema: Means "may we be united" or "may we come together."
    • mitrāvaruṇā: Refers to the deities Mitra and Varuna, representing friendship and cosmic order.
    • sananto: Means "may we be eternal" or "may we be everlasting."
    • bhavema: Translates to "may we become" or "may we exist."
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: Refers to "heaven and earth," symbolizing the universe.
    • bhavantaḥ: Means "may you all exist" or "may you all thrive."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a desire for unity and harmony among individuals, invoking the qualities of divine beings.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and cooperation, suggesting that we should aspire to embody the virtues of the Adityas and Vasus.
    • The invocation of Mitra and Varuna highlights the significance of friendship, trust, and cosmic order in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can inspire teamwork and collaboration, encouraging employees to work together harmoniously.
    • In community gatherings, it can serve as a reminder of the importance of unity and mutual support among members.
    • In personal relationships, it can encourage individuals to foster trust and understanding, akin to the relationship between Mitra and Varuna.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the aspiration for unity, harmony, and the embodiment of divine qualities in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, including personal relationships, community interactions, and professional environments.
    • By invoking these ideals, we can create a more harmonious and supportive world, reflecting the cosmic order represented by the deities.
