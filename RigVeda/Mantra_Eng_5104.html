इन्द्रासोमा वासयथा उषासम् उत सूर्यं नयथो ज्योतिषा सह |उप द्यां स्कम्भथुः स्कम्भनेनाप्रथतम् पृथिवीम् मातरं वि ||
indrāsomā vāsayatha uṣāsam ut sūryaṁ nayatho jyotiṣā saha | upa dyāṁ skambhathuḥ skambhanenāprathatam pṛthivīm mātaraṁ vi ||
  • Indrāsomā vāsayatha: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to Indra and Soma, the deities representing strength and vitality. Indra is often associated with rain and storms, while Soma symbolizes the moon and its nourishing qualities. In real life, this can be seen as a plea for strength and nourishment in our endeavors.
  • Uṣāsam ut sūryaṁ: Here, Uṣā (the dawn) and Sūrya (the sun) are invoked. This signifies the arrival of light and knowledge, dispelling darkness and ignorance. In practical terms, this can relate to the pursuit of education and enlightenment in our lives, emphasizing the importance of knowledge in overcoming challenges.
  • Nayatho jyotiṣā saha: This translates to guiding with light. It suggests that we should seek guidance and direction in our lives, much like how the sun provides light to navigate through the day. In a real-world context, this can be seen in the importance of mentors and leaders who illuminate the path for others.
  • Upa dyāṁ skambhathuḥ: This phrase can be understood as a call to the heavens to support and uphold us. It reflects the idea of seeking divine assistance in our endeavors. In everyday life, this can be related to the support we seek from our community, family, or even spiritual beliefs during challenging times.
  • Skambhanenāprathatam: This suggests the idea of stability and strength. It emphasizes the need for a solid foundation in our lives, whether it be in our personal relationships, careers, or spiritual practices. For instance, a strong family unit can provide the necessary support to face life's challenges.
  • Pṛthivīm mātaraṁ vi: This phrase refers to the Earth as a nurturing mother. It highlights the importance of our connection to nature and the environment. In real life, this can be seen in the growing movement towards environmental conservation and sustainability, recognizing the Earth as our home that sustains us.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about seeking strength, guidance, and stability in our lives while acknowledging the nurturing aspects of nature. Its significance lies in the reminder to connect with both the divine and the earthly, fostering a balanced approach to life. In practical scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek knowledge, support from their community, and a deeper connection with the environment, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
