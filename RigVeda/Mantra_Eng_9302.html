मां धुर इन्द्रं नाम देवता दिवश च गमश चापां च जन्तवः |अहं हरī वृषणा विव्रता रघू अहं वज्रं शवसे धृष्ण्वा ददे ||
māṁ dhur indraṁ nāma devatā divaś ca gmaś cāpāṁ ca jantavaḥ | ahaṁ harī vṛṣaṇā vivratā raghū ahaṁ vajraṁ śavase dhṛṣṇv ā dade ||
  • māṁ dhur indraṁ nāma devatā: This phrase can be translated as "May Indra, the divine deity, support me." Indra is often regarded as the king of the gods in Vedic literature, representing strength, power, and the forces of nature. In real life, this can be seen as a call for divine assistance in overcoming challenges, much like seeking support from a mentor or leader during difficult times.
  • divaś ca gmaś cāpāṁ ca jantavaḥ: This part translates to "and all beings that dwell in the heavens and on earth." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all life forms, both celestial and terrestrial. In a modern context, this can relate to the importance of environmental conservation and recognizing our responsibility towards all living beings, akin to the global movements for climate action.
  • ahaṁ harī vṛṣaṇā vivratā: This translates to "I am the one who is endowed with strength." The term "harī" signifies a powerful entity, while "vṛṣaṇā" refers to strength or vigor. This can be likened to individuals who harness their inner strength to achieve personal goals, such as athletes training for competitions or individuals overcoming personal struggles.
  • raghū ahaṁ vajraṁ śavase dhṛṣṇv ā dade: This can be interpreted as "I, like the thunderbolt, will strike with force." The word "vajra" symbolizes a thunderbolt, representing indomitable strength and resilience. In real life, this can be seen in moments of decisive action, such as a leader making a bold decision in a crisis or a person standing up for their beliefs in the face of adversity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of divine support, interconnectedness, personal strength, and decisive action. Its significance lies in the reminder that we are not alone in our struggles; we can seek help from higher powers and the community around us. In practical terms, this mantra encourages individuals to recognize their inner strength and the importance of unity with others, fostering a sense of responsibility towards both humanity and nature. It can be applied in various life scenarios, such as during personal challenges, community service, or environmental advocacy, reinforcing the idea that we can draw upon divine and communal support to navigate life's complexities.
