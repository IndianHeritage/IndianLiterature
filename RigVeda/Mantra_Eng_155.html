द्रविणोदाः पिपीषति जुहोत प्र च तिष्ठत | नेष्ट्राद ऋतुभिर इष्यत ||
draviṇodāḥ pipīṣati juhota pra ca tiṣṭhata | neṣṭrād ṛtubhir iṣyata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • draviṇodāḥ: Those who possess wealth or resources.
    • pipīṣati: Squeeze or extract.
    • juhota: Offer or sacrifice.
    • pra: Forth or forward.
    • ca: And.
    • tiṣṭhata: Stand or remain.
    • neṣṭrād: From the best or the most excellent.
    • ṛtubhir: By the seasons or cycles.
    • iṣyata: Is desired or is sought after.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of wealth and resources in the context of offerings and sacrifices.
    • It suggests that those who have wealth should utilize it wisely, offering it for the greater good.
    • The phrase "from the best" indicates that the offerings should be of high quality, reflecting the value of what is being given.
    • The mention of "by the seasons" implies that there are natural cycles and times that are significant for making offerings.
    • Overall, it emphasizes the connection between material wealth and spiritual practices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, charitable donations can be seen as a form of "offering" wealth for the benefit of others.
    • Businesses that engage in corporate social responsibility (CSR) exemplify the idea of using resources for the greater good, aligning with the mantra's message.
    • Seasonal festivals often involve offerings to deities, reflecting the cyclical nature of life and the importance of giving during specific times.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wealth not just as a means of personal gain but as a tool for contributing to society.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize the value of their resources and to use them in a way that benefits others, especially during auspicious times.
    • In essence, it teaches that true wealth lies in the ability to give and support the community, fostering a sense of interconnectedness and responsibility.
