सविता पश्चिमातात सविता पूर्वातात सवितोत्तरात्तात सविताधरात्तात |सविता नः सुवतु सर्वतातिं सविता नो रासतां दीर्घम आयुः ||
savitā paścātāt savitā purastāt savitottarāttāt savitādharāttāt | savitā naḥ suvatu sarvatātiṁ savitā no rāsatāṁ dīrgham āyuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Savitā: The Sun or the divine light.
    • paścātāt: From behind or from the west.
    • purastāt: From the front or from the east.
    • uttarāttāt: From above or from the north.
    • adharāttāt: From below or from the south.
    • naḥ: Our.
    • suvatu: May it nourish or protect.
    • sarvatātiṁ: In all directions or everywhere.
    • rāsatāṁ: May it grant us abundance.
    • dīrgham āyuḥ: Long life.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Savitā, the Sun, as a source of nourishment and protection from all directions.
    • It emphasizes the omnipresence of divine energy, suggesting that it is available to us from every direction—behind, in front, above, and below.
    • The mantra expresses a desire for abundance and longevity, highlighting the importance of these blessings in our lives.
    • In real life, this can be seen as a call for holistic well-being, where one seeks not just physical health but also mental and spiritual nourishment.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra can be recited during morning prayers or meditation to seek the blessings of the Sun for a fruitful day.
    • It serves as a reminder to be aware of the energies around us and to seek balance and nourishment from all aspects of life.
    • In a practical sense, it encourages individuals to cultivate positivity and abundance in their lives, whether through relationships, career, or personal growth.
    • By acknowledging the divine presence in all directions, one can foster a sense of gratitude and interconnectedness with the universe.
