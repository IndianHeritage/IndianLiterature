मन्त्र: वेद वातस्य वर्तनिम उरोर ऋष्वस्य बृहतः |वेदा ये अध्यासते ||
veda vātasya vartanim uror ṛṣvasya bṛhataḥ | vedā ye adhyāsate ||
  • veda: This word translates to "knowledge" or "wisdom." In the context of the Rigveda, it refers to the sacred knowledge that encompasses various aspects of life, spirituality, and the universe.
  • vātasya: This means "of the wind" or "of the air." The wind is often seen as a vital force in nature, symbolizing movement, change, and the unseen energies that influence life.
  • vartanim: This translates to "that which is moving" or "that which is in motion." It signifies the dynamic nature of existence and the constant change that characterizes life.
  • uror: This word means "of the earth" or "of the ground." It represents the physical world and the foundation upon which life is built.
  • ṛṣvasya: This translates to "of the strong" or "of the powerful." It denotes strength, resilience, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • bṛhataḥ: This means "great" or "vast." It signifies the expansive nature of the universe and the boundless possibilities that exist within it.
  • vedā: Again, this refers to "knowledge" or "wisdom," emphasizing its importance in understanding the world.
  • ye: This means "those who" or "whoever." It indicates a group of people or practitioners.
  • adhyāsate: This translates to "study" or "engage with." It implies an active pursuit of knowledge and understanding.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to recognize the interplay between the forces of nature (like the wind and the earth) and the pursuit of knowledge. It emphasizes that those who engage with this sacred knowledge are connected to the vastness of existence and the strength that comes from understanding the world around them.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Environmental Awareness: Understanding the importance of nature and the elements, such as wind and earth, can lead to a greater appreciation for environmental conservation.
  • Personal Growth: The idea of constant motion and change can inspire individuals to embrace personal development and adaptability in their lives.
  • Education and Learning: The emphasis on knowledge encourages lifelong learning and the pursuit of wisdom in various fields, fostering a culture of inquiry and understanding.

In conclusion, this mantra highlights the significance of knowledge as a guiding force in navigating the complexities of life. It encourages individuals to engage with the world around them, recognize the strength in understanding, and embrace the dynamic nature of existence. By applying these principles, one can cultivate a deeper connection with both the physical and spiritual aspects of life.
