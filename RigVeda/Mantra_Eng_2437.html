प्राञ्चं यज्ञं चक्रिम वर्धतां गीः समिद्भिर अग्निं नमसां दुवस्यन् |दिवः शशासुर विदथा कवीनां गृत्साय चित तवसे गातुम ईषुः ||
prāñcaṁ yajñaṁ cakṛma vardhatāṁ gīḥ samidbhir agniṁ namasā duvasyan | divaḥ śaśāsur vidathā kavīnāṁ gṛtsāya cit tavase gātum īṣuḥ ||
  • prāñcaṁ yajñaṁ cakṛma: This phrase can be translated as "We perform the sacrifice with our breath." In this context, "yajña" refers to a ritual or sacrifice, which is a central theme in Vedic literature. It signifies the importance of offering and devotion in our lives. For example, in modern life, this can be seen in the practice of gratitude, where individuals express appreciation for what they have, akin to a personal sacrifice of ego for the greater good.
  • vardhatāṁ gīḥ: This means "may our songs (or hymns) grow." Here, "gīḥ" refers to the hymns or praises sung during rituals. This can be related to the idea of personal growth and the importance of uplifting oneself and others through positive expressions. In real life, this can be exemplified by community gatherings where people come together to share stories, music, and culture, fostering a sense of belonging and growth.
  • samidbhir agniṁ namasā duvasyan: This translates to "with the fuel, we honor the fire." "Agni" represents fire, which is a symbol of purity and transformation. In practical terms, this can be seen in how we fuel our passions and ambitions in life. Just as fire needs fuel to burn brightly, our goals require dedication and effort. For instance, a student dedicating time to study for exams is akin to offering fuel to the fire of knowledge.
  • divaḥ śaśāsur vidathā kavīnāṁ: This phrase means "the wise ones of the heavens have created." It emphasizes the role of wisdom and creativity in shaping our lives. In a real-world context, this can be related to innovators and thinkers who contribute to society through their ideas and inventions. For example, scientists and artists who push the boundaries of knowledge and creativity are modern-day "kavīnāṁ."
  • gṛtsāya cit tavase gātum īṣuḥ: This translates to "may we reach the essence of your brilliance." This line speaks to the aspiration to attain higher knowledge and understanding. In everyday life, this can be reflected in the pursuit of education and self-improvement. For instance, individuals who seek mentorship or engage in lifelong learning are striving to reach the "essence" of their potential.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, growth, and the pursuit of wisdom. It highlights the significance of rituals and sacrifices in our lives, encouraging us to honor our passions and aspirations. In a real-life scenario, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in community service, pursue education, and express gratitude, ultimately leading to personal and collective growth. By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can create a more harmonious and enlightened society.
