न्य ū3 षु वाचम् प्र महे भरामहे गिर इन्द्राय सदने विवस्वतः |नू चिद हि रत्नं ससताम इवाविदन् न दुष्टुतिर द्रविणोदेशु शस्यते ||
ny ū3 ṣu vācam pra mahe bharāmahe gira indrāya sadane vivasvataḥ | nū cid dhi ratnaṁ sasatām ivāvidan na duṣṭutir draviṇodeṣu śasyate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nyū: to us
    • ṣu: good
    • vācam: speech or words
    • pra: forth or forward
    • mahe: we make great
    • bharāmahe: we carry or bear
    • gira: voice or sound
    • indrāya: to Indra (the king of gods)
    • sadane: in the abode or dwelling
    • vivasvataḥ: of the sun or radiant one
    • nū: indeed
    • cid: if
    • hhi: indeed
    • ratnaṁ: jewel or precious thing
    • sasatām: of the prosperous
    • ivāvidan: as if knowing
    • na: not
    • duṣṭutir: bad speech or slander
    • draviṇodeṣu: in wealth or riches
    • śasyate: is praised or is spoken of
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer for the divine guidance of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, to bless us with good speech and wisdom.
    • It emphasizes the importance of positive communication and the pursuit of prosperity without falling into the trap of negative or slanderous speech.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies a request for strength and support in our endeavors, particularly in the realm of communication and wealth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied when teams come together to brainstorm ideas. Encouraging positive and constructive dialogue can lead to innovative solutions.
    • In personal relationships, practicing good communication can help resolve conflicts and strengthen bonds, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In community gatherings, promoting uplifting speech can foster a sense of unity and shared purpose, aligning with the mantra's call for positive expression.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of words and the impact they have on our lives and the lives of others.
    • By invoking the blessings of Indra, we seek to cultivate a mindset that values wisdom, prosperity, and positive communication.
    • In today's world, where negativity can often dominate discourse, this mantra encourages us to strive for a more harmonious and constructive way of interacting with one another.
