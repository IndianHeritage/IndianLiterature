ये चिद्धि मृत्युबंधनव आदित्य मनवः स्मसि |प्र सून न आयुर जीवसे तिरेतन ||
ye cid dhi mṛtyubandhava ādityā manavaḥ smasi | pra sū na āyur jīvase tiretana ||
  • Translation: "Those who are bound by death, O Adityas, may you grant them life; may they cross over to the other shore of existence."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: Those who
    • cid: indeed
    • dhi: bound or tied
    • mṛtyubandhava: by the bonds of death
    • ādityā: O Adityas (the solar deities)
    • manavaḥ: human beings
    • smasi: may you grant
    • pra: may
    • sū: grant or bestow
    • na: not
    • āyur: life
    • jīvase: for living
    • tiretana: may they cross over

  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra reflects a plea for liberation from the cycle of life and death, emphasizing the desire for longevity and a peaceful existence.
    • The invocation of the Adityas signifies a call to divine forces for assistance in overcoming mortality.
    • In real life, this can be related to the human experience of facing mortality, whether through illness, aging, or loss. It resonates with the universal desire for life and the fear of death.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of grief, families often seek solace in spiritual practices, invoking blessings for the departed and hoping for their peaceful transition.
    • During health crises, individuals may recite this mantra as a form of meditation or prayer, seeking strength and healing.
    • In cultural rituals, such as funerals, this mantra can be recited to honor the deceased and wish for their safe passage to the afterlife.

  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal to the divine for protection against the inevitability of death and the hope for a fulfilling life.
    • It serves as a reminder of the transient nature of existence and the importance of cherishing life while seeking spiritual guidance.
    • In various life scenarios, this mantra can be a source of comfort, reflection, and a call to embrace life with gratitude and awareness of its impermanence.
