अध धारणया मध्वा पृचांस तिरो रोमा पवते अद्रिदुग्धः |इन्दुर इन्द्रस्य सख्यं जुषाणो देवो देवस्य मत्सरो मदाय ||
adha dhārayā madhvā pṛcānas tiro roma pavate adridugdhaḥ | indur indrasya sakhyaṁ juṣāṇo devo devasya matsaro madāya ||
  • Adha dhārayā madhvā: This phrase can be translated as "Holding the sweetness below." Here, "adha" means below or beneath, "dhārayā" means to hold or support, and "madhvā" refers to sweetness or nectar. This can symbolize the importance of grounding oneself in sweetness or positivity in life.
  • Pṛcānas tiro roma pavate: This translates to "The wind blows through the hair of the ancient ones." "Pṛcānas" refers to the ancient or wise, "tiro" means through, "roma" refers to hair, and "pavate" means to blow or move. This can be interpreted as the wisdom of the ancients being carried through time, reminding us to respect and learn from our heritage.
  • Adridhugdhaḥ: This word means "the one who is not milked." It can symbolize purity and the untouched essence of life. In real life, this can refer to maintaining one's integrity and authenticity in a world that often seeks to exploit or commodify.
  • Indur indrasya sakhyaṁ: This translates to "The moon is a friend of Indra." "Indur" means moon, "indrasya" refers to Indra, the king of gods, and "sakhyaṁ" means friendship. This highlights the importance of companionship and support in achieving greatness, akin to how friends uplift each other in personal and professional endeavors.
  • Juṣāṇo: This means "enjoying" or "delighting in." It emphasizes the joy that comes from relationships and experiences. In real life, this can be seen in how we cherish moments with loved ones, finding happiness in shared experiences.
  • Devo devasya matsaro madāya: This translates to "The god is not envious of the god." "Devo" means god, "devasya" refers to the divine, "matsaro" means envy, and "madāya" means intoxication or pride. This suggests that true divinity is free from jealousy and pride, encouraging us to cultivate humility and generosity in our interactions with others.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates profound wisdom about life, relationships, and the importance of grounding oneself in positivity and authenticity. It teaches us to respect our heritage, cherish friendships, and embrace humility. In real-life scenarios, these teachings can guide us in personal growth, fostering meaningful connections, and navigating the complexities of modern life with grace and integrity. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling existence.
