उद अपप्तद असौ सूर्यः पुरु विश्वानि जूर्वन |आदित्यः पर्वतेभ्यो विश्वदृष्टो अदृष्टहाः ||
ud apaptad asau sūryaḥ puru viśvāni jūrvan | ādityaḥ parvatebhyo viśvadṛṣṭo adṛṣṭahā ||
  • ud apaptad asau sūryaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "the sun rises from the horizon." Here, "ud" means "up" or "from below," "apaptad" refers to "having risen," and "sūryaḥ" means "the sun." This signifies the daily renewal and the beginning of a new day, symbolizing hope and enlightenment.
  • puru viśvāni jūrvan: This part translates to "the sun, having illuminated all things." "Puru" means "many" or "all," "viśvāni" refers to "things" or "worlds," and "jūrvan" means "illuminating" or "shining." This emphasizes the sun's role in providing light and energy to the entire universe, fostering life and growth.
  • ādityaḥ parvatebhyo: This translates to "the sun, greater than the mountains." "Ādityaḥ" refers to "the sun" or "solar deity," and "parvatebhyo" means "than the mountains." This highlights the sun's supremacy and its vital role in the ecosystem, as it is more powerful than even the tallest mountains, which can be seen as obstacles to light.
  • viśvadṛṣṭo adṛṣṭahā: This can be interpreted as "the all-seeing, unseen one." "Viśvadṛṣṭo" means "all-seeing" or "perceptive," and "adṛṣṭahā" means "unseen" or "invisible." This suggests that while the sun is visible and provides light, it also represents deeper truths and knowledge that may not be immediately apparent.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of the sun as a source of light, life, and knowledge. In real life, this can be applied in various ways:

  • It serves as a reminder of the importance of new beginnings and the hope that each day brings.
  • The sun's role in illuminating the world can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, encouraging individuals to seek enlightenment in their lives.
  • In a broader ecological context, it emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, where the sun is essential for growth and sustenance.

Overall, this mantra encourages us to appreciate the natural world, recognize the power of light and knowledge, and strive for personal and collective growth.
