यस् ते साधिष्ठोऽवस इन्द्र कृतुष्टमाभर |अस्मभ्यं चर्षणीसहं सस्निं वाजेषु दुष्टतम् ||
yas te sādhiṣṭho 'vasa indra kratuṣ ṭam ā bhara | asmabhyaṁ carṣaṇīsahaṁ sasniṁ vājeṣu duṣṭaram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • sādhiṣṭho: most excellent
    • 'vasa: may dwell
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • kratuṣ: with wisdom or intellect
    • ṭam: that
    • ā: may
    • bhara: fill
    • asmabhyaṁ: for us
    • carṣaṇīsahaṁ: with the support of the earth
    • sasniṁ: with strength
    • vājeṣu: in the battles
    • duṣṭaram: difficult or formidable
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the god of rain and thunder, asking for his blessings and support.
    • The speaker seeks the most excellent wisdom and strength to overcome challenges and difficulties in life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine assistance in achieving success and overcoming obstacles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive work environment, individuals often seek guidance and support from mentors or leaders, akin to invoking divine wisdom.
    • Students preparing for exams may recite this mantra to seek focus and strength, believing that divine support can help them overcome academic challenges.
    • In times of personal crisis, people may turn to spiritual practices or prayers, reflecting the desire for external help to navigate through tough situations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and strength in our endeavors.
    • It teaches us that while we strive for success, acknowledging a higher power can provide us with the wisdom and resilience needed to face life's challenges.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various situations, such as during personal struggles, professional challenges, or when making significant life decisions, reminding us to remain humble and open to receiving support from the universe.
