अग्नि तव श्रवो वयो महि भ्राजन्ते अर्चयो विभावसो |बृहद्भानो शवसा वाजम् उक्थ्यं दधासि दाशुषे कवे ||
agne tava śravo vayo mahi bhrājante arcayo vibhāvaso | bṛhadbhāno śavasā vājam ukthya1ṁ dadhāsi dāśuṣe kave ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Tava: Meaning "your," it indicates possession, suggesting a personal connection to Agni.
  • Śravo: This translates to "glory" or "fame," highlighting the esteemed nature of Agni.
  • Vayo: Meaning "of the wind" or "of the atmosphere," it connects Agni to the natural elements.
  • Mahi: This means "great" or "vast," emphasizing the expansive nature of Agni's influence.
  • Bhrājante: This word means "shine" or "radiate," indicating the illuminating quality of Agni.
  • Arcayo: This refers to "worship" or "adoration," suggesting the importance of reverence towards Agni.
  • Vibhāvaso: This term means "the one who shines brightly," further emphasizing Agni's brilliance.
  • Bṛhadbhāno: Meaning "of great light," it signifies the powerful and expansive nature of Agni's illumination.
  • Śavasā: This translates to "with strength," indicating the potency of Agni.
  • Vājam: Meaning "wealth" or "prosperity," it connects Agni to abundance and nourishment.
  • Ukthyaṁ: This refers to "sacrifice" or "offering," highlighting the ritualistic aspect of worshiping Agni.
  • Dadāsi: Meaning "you give," it indicates Agni's role as a provider.
  • Dāśuṣe: This translates to "to the worshippers," emphasizing the relationship between Agni and his devotees.
  • Kave: Meaning "of the poet" or "sage," it suggests the connection between Agni and the creative or intellectual pursuits of humanity.

This mantra invokes Agni, the divine fire, as a source of light, strength, and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of worship and reverence towards Agni, who is seen as a provider of wealth and nourishment. The mantra highlights the relationship between the divine and humanity, suggesting that through worship and offerings, one can receive blessings and abundance.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: The mantra can be recited during fire rituals (Homa) to invoke Agni's blessings for prosperity and success.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can reflect on the qualities of Agni—energy, purity, and strength—as they strive for personal development and enlightenment.
  • Environmental Awareness: The connection to natural elements encourages respect for fire and its role in nature, promoting sustainable practices.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of Agni in Vedic tradition, symbolizing the light of knowledge, the strength of character, and the abundance of life. By acknowledging and honoring Agni, individuals can cultivate a deeper connection to the divine and the natural world, leading to a more fulfilling and prosperous life.
