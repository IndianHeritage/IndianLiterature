चमूषच छ्येनः शकुनो विभृत्वा गोविन्दुर द्रप्स आयुधानि बिभ्रत् |अपाम ऊर्मिं सचमानः समुद्रं तुरीयं धाम महिषो विवक्ति ||
camūṣac chyenaḥ śakuno vibhṛtvā govindur drapsa āyudhāni bibhrat | apām ūrmiṁ sacamānaḥ samudraṁ turīyaṁ dhāma mahiṣo vivakti ||
  • camūṣac chyenaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who is swift and powerful." In real life, this can relate to individuals or entities that are quick in their actions and decisions, such as a successful entrepreneur who adapts rapidly to market changes.
  • śakuno vibhṛtvā: This translates to "the one who possesses strength." This can be likened to a leader who inspires and empowers their team, showcasing strength not just in physical terms but also in character and resolve.
  • govindur drapsa āyudhāni bibhrat: Here, "Govindur" refers to a divine protector or guide, while "drapsa āyudhāni" means "holding weapons." This can symbolize the idea of being prepared and equipped to face challenges. For example, a student preparing for exams by studying diligently is metaphorically "holding weapons" against the challenges of academic life.
  • apām ūrmiṁ sacamānaḥ: This phrase means "the waves of the waters." It can represent the flow of life and the challenges that come with it. Just as waves can be both calming and tumultuous, life presents both serene and challenging moments. An example is how individuals navigate through personal crises, adapting to the changing tides of their circumstances.
  • samudraṁ turīyaṁ dhāma: This translates to "the ocean of the fourth state." The "fourth state" can refer to a higher state of consciousness or awareness. In real life, this can be seen in practices like meditation, where individuals seek to reach a deeper understanding of themselves and the universe.
  • mahiṣo vivakti: This means "the great one speaks." It signifies the importance of wisdom and knowledge. In practical terms, this can relate to mentors or teachers who share their insights and experiences, guiding others on their paths.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, preparedness, adaptability, and wisdom. Its significance lies in the reminder that, like the swift and powerful, we must be ready to face life's challenges with strength and knowledge. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to cultivate resilience, seek higher understanding, and share wisdom with others, ultimately leading to personal growth and collective empowerment.
