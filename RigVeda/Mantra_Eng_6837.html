एवां वन्दस्व वरुणं बृहन्तं नमस्याधीरम् अमृतस्य गोपाम् |स नः शर्म त्रिवरूथं वि यंषत् पातं नो dyावापृथिवी उपस्थे ||
evā vandasva varuṇam bṛhantaṁ namasyā dhīram amṛtasya gopām | sa naḥ śarma trivarūthaṁ vi yaṁsat pātaṁ no dyāvāpṛthivī upasthe ||
  • evā vandasva: This phrase translates to "thus, we worship" or "we bow down." It signifies the act of reverence and devotion towards a higher power.
  • varuṇam: Refers to Varuna, the Vedic deity associated with water, cosmic order, and moral authority. Varuna represents the natural laws and the ethical framework of the universe.
  • bṛhantaṁ: Meaning "the great" or "the vast," this term emphasizes the immense and all-encompassing nature of Varuna.
  • namasyā: This word means "with respect" or "with reverence," indicating the humility and submission of the devotee.
  • dhīram: Translated as "wise" or "sagacious," it highlights the importance of wisdom in understanding the divine and the universe.
  • amṛtasya: Meaning "of immortality," this term refers to the eternal and unchanging nature of the ultimate reality.
  • gopām: This word means "the protector" or "the guardian," suggesting that Varuna is a protector of the cosmic order and the moral law.
  • sa naḥ: Translates to "may he" or "let him," indicating a prayerful request for blessings.
  • śarma: Meaning "happiness" or "shelter," it signifies the desire for peace and security in life.
  • trivarūthaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "threefold" or "triple," possibly referring to the three realms of existence: physical, mental, and spiritual.
  • vi yaṁsat: Translates to "who is worthy" or "who is deserving," indicating a recognition of the qualities that make one worthy of reverence.
  • pātaṁ: Meaning "protection" or "safeguarding," it emphasizes the need for divine protection in our lives.
  • no dyāvāpṛthivī upasthe: This phrase means "may the heavens and the earth be our support," invoking the idea of a harmonious relationship between the celestial and terrestrial realms.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of devotion, wisdom, and the quest for protection and happiness. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing the vastness of the universe and the moral order that governs it. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during times of uncertainty or distress, where individuals seek guidance and protection from higher powers. It serves as a reminder to cultivate wisdom and humility in our lives, fostering a sense of connection with the universe and the moral principles that govern our actions. By invoking the blessings of Varuna, we aspire for peace, security, and a deeper understanding of our place in the cosmos.
