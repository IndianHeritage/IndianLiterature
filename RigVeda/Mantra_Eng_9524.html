अप ज्योतिषा तमो अन्तरिक्षाद उद्नः शीपालम इव वात आजत | बृहस्पतिर अनुमृष्य वालस्याभ्रम इव वात आ चक्र आ गाः ||
apa jyotiṣā tamo antarikṣād udnaḥ śīpālam iva vāta ājat | bṛhaspatir anumṛśyā valasyābhram iva vāta ā cakra ā gāḥ ||
  • apa - away from
  • jyotiṣā - light or illumination
  • tamo - darkness
  • antarikṣād - from the space or the atmosphere
  • udnaḥ - like a wave or a current
  • śīpālam - protector or guardian
  • iva - like
  • vāta - wind or air
  • ājat - born or arisen
  • bṛhaspatir - the deity associated with wisdom and knowledge
  • anumṛśyā - having been touched or influenced
  • valasyābhram - like a cloud
  • ā cakra - to revolve or to turn
  • ā gāḥ - to go or to move

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for guidance and protection from darkness, invoking the powers of light and wisdom. The imagery of wind and waves suggests a dynamic force that can both protect and guide. The mention of Bṛhaspati, the deity of wisdom, emphasizes the importance of knowledge in overcoming ignorance and darkness.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra seeks to dispel darkness with light, individuals can strive to overcome ignorance and fear in their lives through education and self-awareness.
  • Leadership: Leaders can embody the qualities of Bṛhaspati by guiding their teams with wisdom and clarity, ensuring that decisions are made with insight rather than impulse.
  • Community Support: In times of crisis, communities can come together to support one another, acting as a protective force against the challenges that threaten to overwhelm them.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for illumination and guidance in the face of darkness. It serves as a reminder that wisdom and knowledge are powerful tools in navigating life's challenges. By invoking these qualities, individuals and communities can foster resilience and clarity, ultimately leading to a more enlightened and harmonious existence.
