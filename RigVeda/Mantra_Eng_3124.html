वैश्वानराय मीळ्हुṣe सजोषाः कथा दाशेमाग्नये बृहद्भाḥ |अनूननेन बृहता वक्षथेनोप स्तभायद उपमिन न रोधः ||
vaiśvānarāya mīḻhuṣe sajoṣāḥ kathā dāśemāgnaye bṛhad bhāḥ | anūnena bṛhatā vakṣathenopa stabhāyad upamin na rodhaḥ ||
  • Vaiśvānarāya: This term refers to the universal aspect of the divine, representing the all-pervading nature of the Supreme Being. In real life, this can be likened to the interconnectedness of all living beings and the universe, emphasizing that we are all part of a larger whole.
  • Mīḻhuṣe: This word can be interpreted as "to be nourished" or "to be sustained." In practical terms, it signifies the importance of sustenance in our lives, whether it be physical, emotional, or spiritual. For example, just as plants need water and sunlight to thrive, humans require love, support, and nourishment to flourish.
  • Sajoṣāḥ: This term suggests harmony and cooperation. In a real-life context, it can be seen in teamwork and collaboration, where individuals come together to achieve a common goal, much like a sports team working together to win a game.
  • Kathā: This word means "story" or "narrative." Stories are fundamental to human experience, shaping our understanding of the world. For instance, personal stories can inspire others and foster empathy, highlighting the importance of sharing experiences.
  • Dāśemāgnaye: This phrase can be translated as "to the great fire" or "to the divine light." In everyday life, this can symbolize the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, akin to seeking wisdom through education and self-reflection.
  • Bṛhad bhāḥ: This means "great light" or "vastness." It signifies the expansive nature of consciousness and understanding. In practical terms, it can be related to the idea of broadening one's horizons through travel, learning, and exposure to different cultures.
  • Anūnena: This word implies "by following" or "by adhering to." It suggests the importance of guidance and mentorship in our lives. For example, a mentor can help navigate challenges and provide direction, much like a teacher guiding students through their educational journey.
  • Bṛhatā: This term means "greatness" or "largeness." It can be associated with the idea of ambition and striving for greatness in our endeavors, whether in career, personal growth, or community service.
  • Vakṣathenopa: This phrase can be interpreted as "by speaking" or "through communication." Effective communication is crucial in all aspects of life, from personal relationships to professional settings, as it fosters understanding and collaboration.
  • Stabhāyad: This word means "to stabilize" or "to support." In real life, this can refer to the importance of having a strong support system, whether it be family, friends, or community, to help us through difficult times.
  • Upamin na rodhaḥ: This phrase suggests "there is no obstruction." It signifies the idea that with determination and perseverance, one can overcome obstacles. In practical terms, this can be seen in stories of individuals who have triumphed over adversity, inspiring others to do the same.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of unity, nourishment, cooperation, storytelling, enlightenment, guidance, ambition, communication, support, and resilience. Its significance lies in the reminder that we are all interconnected and that our individual journeys contribute to the greater whole. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as fostering teamwork in the workplace, seeking knowledge through education, and building strong relationships with others. By embodying these values, we can create a more harmonious and supportive society.
