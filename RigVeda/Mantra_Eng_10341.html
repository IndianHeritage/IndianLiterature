आयं हि ते अमर्त्य इन्दुर अत्यो न पत्यते |दक्षो विश्वायुर वेधसे ||
ayaṁ hi te amartya indur atyo na patyate | dakṣo viśvāyur vedhase ||
  • ayaṁ hi te: This phrase translates to "this indeed is yours." It signifies possession or a connection to something divine or significant.
  • amartya: This word means "immortal" or "eternal." It reflects the idea of transcendence beyond the physical realm, suggesting a state of being that is not subject to death.
  • indur: This term refers to "the moon" or "the divine light." In many cultures, the moon symbolizes intuition, emotions, and the cyclical nature of life.
  • atyo na patyate: This phrase means "does not fall away" or "is not diminished." It implies a sense of permanence and stability in the face of change.
  • dakṣo: This word translates to "skilled" or "capable." It denotes the ability to perform tasks effectively and with expertise.
  • viśvāyur: This term means "universal life" or "all-encompassing vitality." It suggests a connection to the life force that permeates all existence.
  • vedhase: This word translates to "to the one who knows" or "to the wise." It indicates a reverence for knowledge and wisdom, often associated with the divine or enlightened beings.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of immortality, divine connection, and the pursuit of knowledge. It emphasizes the importance of being skilled and capable in one's endeavors while remaining connected to the eternal aspects of existence.
  • Real-life Application: In practical terms, this mantra can inspire individuals to strive for excellence in their personal and professional lives. For example, a student may embody the qualities of dakṣo by dedicating themselves to their studies, seeking knowledge, and developing skills that will serve them throughout their life. Similarly, a leader may reflect the qualities of viśvāyur by fostering a sense of community and interconnectedness among their team, promoting a culture of collaboration and support.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the eternal aspects of life and the importance of skill and wisdom. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate the challenges of life with grace and purpose, ultimately contributing to a more harmonious and enlightened society.
