न ते अदेवः प्रदिवो न वासते यद एतशेभिः पतारै रथर्यसि | प्राचीनम् अन्यद अनु वर्तते राजा उद अन्येन ज्योतीषा यासि सूर्य ||
na te adevaḥ pradivo ni vāsate yad etaśebhiḥ patarai ratharyasi | prācīnam anyad anu vartate raja ud anyena jyotiṣā yāsi sūrya ||
  • Mantra: "na te adevaḥ pradivo ni vāsate yad etaśebhiḥ patarai ratharyasi | prācīnam anyad anu vartate raja ud anyena jyotiṣā yāsi sūrya ||"
  • Translation: "The divine beings do not dwell in the heavens, where the chariots of the sun travel with their horses. The ancient path leads to the light, and through another light, you move towards the sun."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • na te: not you
    • adevaḥ: divine beings
    • pradivo: in the heavens
    • ni vāsate: do not dwell
    • yad: where
    • etaśebhiḥ: with these chariots
    • patarai: horses
    • ratharyasi: of the sun
    • prācīnam: ancient
    • anyad: another
    • anu vartate: leads to
    • raja: light
    • ud: and
    • anyena: through another
    • jyotiṣā: light
    • yāsi: you move
    • sūrya: towards the sun
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the idea that divine beings are not confined to the heavens but are present in the world around us.
    • It suggests that the journey towards enlightenment and understanding is not just about following the obvious paths (the chariots of the sun) but also about seeking deeper truths (the ancient path).
    • The reference to light symbolizes knowledge, wisdom, and spiritual awakening, indicating that there are multiple ways to achieve enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In our daily lives, this mantra can inspire us to look beyond conventional wisdom and explore alternative perspectives. For example, in a workplace setting, innovative solutions often come from thinking outside the box rather than following established protocols.
    • In personal growth, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge from various sources, such as books, mentors, and experiences, rather than relying solely on mainstream education.
    • In spiritual practices, it reminds us that the path to enlightenment can be unique for each individual, and one should be open to exploring different spiritual traditions and practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder that the divine is not distant but intertwined with our existence.
    • It encourages us to seek knowledge and enlightenment through various means, embracing both the ancient wisdom and contemporary insights.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that the journey towards understanding and enlightenment is personal and multifaceted, urging us to explore the light in our own unique ways.
