अव स्वेदाऽविभितो विश्वक् पतन्तु दिद्यवः |दूर्वायाऽव तन्तवो व्य अस्मद एतु दुरमतिर देवी जनित्र्य अजिजानद भद्रा जनित्र्य अजिजानत ||
ava svedā ivābhito viṣvak patantu didyavaḥ | dūrvāyā iva tantavo vy a1smad etu durmatir devī janitry ajījanad bhadrā janitry ajījanat ||
  • ava svedā ivābhito viṣvak patantu didyavaḥ
    • ava - This word can be interpreted as "down" or "from below," suggesting a grounding or foundational aspect.
    • svedā - Refers to "sweat" or "moisture," symbolizing effort, labor, or the fruits of hard work.
    • ivābhito - Means "like all around," indicating a pervasive presence or influence.
    • viṣvak - Translates to "all" or "universal," emphasizing inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all things.
    • patantu - Means "may they fall" or "may they descend," suggesting a wish for blessings or positive outcomes to come down upon us.
    • didyavaḥ - Refers to "those who are radiant" or "brilliant," indicating a desire for enlightenment and clarity.
  • dūrvāyā iva tantavo
    • dūrvāyā - Refers to "dūrvā," a type of grass often used in rituals, symbolizing purity and auspiciousness.
    • iva - Means "like" or "as," drawing a comparison.
    • tantavo - Refers to "threads" or "strands," suggesting interconnectedness and the weaving of life experiences.
  • vy a1smad etu durmatir devī janitry ajījanad
    • vy a1smad - Can be interpreted as "may it come to us," indicating a call for blessings or positive influences.
    • etu - Means "come" or "arrive," reinforcing the desire for good things to manifest.
    • durmatir - Refers to "bad thoughts" or "negative intentions," highlighting the need to overcome negativity.
    • devī - Means "goddess," representing divine feminine energy and nurturing aspects.
    • janitry - Refers to "the one who gives birth" or "mother," symbolizing creation and nurturing.
    • ajījanad - Means "has given birth," indicating the act of creation and manifestation.
  • bhadrā janitry ajījanat
    • bhadrā - Means "auspicious" or "good," emphasizing positivity and well-being.
    • janitry - Again refers to "the one who gives birth," reinforcing the nurturing aspect.
    • ajījanat - Means "has given birth," indicating the continuous cycle of creation and nurturing.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of nurturing, positivity, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It calls for blessings to descend upon us, much like the moisture that nourishes the earth. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to cultivate positive thoughts and actions, overcoming negativity.
  • In community building, it highlights the importance of nurturing relationships and supporting one another for collective growth.
  • In environmental consciousness, it reminds us of our connection to nature and the importance of caring for our surroundings.

Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder of the divine nurturing energy present in our lives and the significance of fostering positivity and interconnectedness in our daily experiences.
