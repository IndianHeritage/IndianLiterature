इन्द्र ऋभुभिर वाजवद्भिः समुक्षितं सुतं सोमं आ वृषस्वा गभस्त्योः |धियेषितो मगवन दाशुषो गृहे सौधनवनेभिः सह मत्स्वा नृभिः ||
indra ṛbhubhir vājavadbhiḥ samukṣitaṁ sutaṁ somam ā vṛṣasvā gabhastyoḥ | dhiyeṣito maghavan dāśuṣo gṛhe saudhanvanebhiḥ saha matsvā nṛbhiḥ ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • ṛbhubhir: Means "with the wise" or "with the learned," indicating the importance of knowledge and wisdom in achieving goals.
  • vājavadbhiḥ: Translates to "with the strength of horses," symbolizing vigor and energy in endeavors.
  • samukṣitaṁ: Means "well-protected" or "well-guarded," suggesting the need for safety and security in one's pursuits.
  • sutaṁ: Refers to "the son" or "offspring," which can symbolize the fruits of one's labor or efforts.
  • somam: Represents "the moon" or "soma," often associated with nourishment, joy, and the divine elixir.
  • ā vṛṣasvā: Means "may you be strong" or "may you be powerful," emphasizing the desire for strength and resilience.
  • gabhastyoḥ: Refers to "the light" or "the brilliance," indicating the importance of enlightenment and clarity.
  • dhiyeṣito: Means "inspired by intellect," highlighting the role of wisdom in guiding actions.
  • maghavan: Refers to "the generous one," symbolizing abundance and prosperity.
  • dāśuṣo: Means "of the generous," reinforcing the theme of giving and sharing.
  • gṛhe: Translates to "in the home," indicating the significance of family and domestic life.
  • saudhanvanebhiḥ: Refers to "with wealth," emphasizing the importance of material prosperity.
  • saha: Means "together," highlighting the value of community and collaboration.
  • matsvā: Refers to "my own," indicating personal connection and ownership.
  • nṛbhiḥ: Means "with men" or "with people," emphasizing the social aspect of human existence.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, wisdom, and community. It emphasizes the importance of collaboration and the pursuit of knowledge while seeking prosperity and protection. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork in professional settings, nurturing family bonds, and fostering a supportive community. The mantra serves as a reminder that true success is achieved not just through individual effort but through collective strength and wisdom, leading to a harmonious and prosperous life.
