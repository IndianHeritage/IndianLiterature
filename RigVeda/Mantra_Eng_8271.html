त्वं सुष्वाणो अद्रिभिर अभ्य अर्ष कणिक्रदत् |द्यमन्तं शुष्मम् उत्तमम् ||
tvaṁ suṣvāṇo adribhir abhy arṣa kanikradat | dyumantaṁ śuṣmam uttamam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • suṣvāṇo: Well-formed or auspicious
    • adribhir: By the mountains
    • abhy: Towards
    • arṣa: The seer or sage
    • kanikradat: Who has made a sound or noise
    • dyumantaṁ: Shining or radiant
    • śuṣmam: Pure or clean
    • uttamam: The highest or supreme
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses a divine entity, acknowledging its auspicious nature and connection to the mountains, which symbolize stability and strength.
    • The term "suṣvāṇo" suggests that the entity is well-formed, indicating perfection and completeness.
    • The reference to "arṣa" implies a connection to wisdom and insight, as seers are often regarded as wise individuals who possess deep understanding.
    • "Kanikradat" can be interpreted as a call to attention, suggesting that the divine presence is not only powerful but also communicative.
    • The words "dyumantaṁ" and "uttamam" highlight the radiance and supreme nature of the entity, emphasizing its exalted status in the cosmos.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, mountains often serve as a source of inspiration and stability for individuals seeking solace or guidance. For instance, many people hike mountains to connect with nature and find clarity in their thoughts.
    • In personal development, individuals often strive to embody qualities of wisdom and purity, akin to the attributes described in the mantra. This can be seen in practices such as meditation and mindfulness, where one seeks to cultivate a clear and radiant mind.
    • In community settings, leaders who exhibit wisdom and strength, much like the divine entity in the mantra, can inspire and uplift those around them, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking wisdom, purity, and strength in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to connect with the divine and the natural world, recognizing the beauty and power that exists within and around them.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in daily life by striving for personal growth, fostering community connections, and appreciating the stability that nature provides.
