प्रातर जरेथे जरणेव कापयाऽवस्तोर्-वस्तोर् यजतā गच्छथो गृहम् |कस्य ध्वस्रā भवथः कस्य वाऽनराः राजपुत्रेव सवनाऽव गच्छथः ||
prātar jarethe jaraṇeva kāpayā vastor-vastor yajatā gacchatho gṛham | kasya dhvasrā bhavathaḥ kasya vā narā rājaputreva savanāva gacchathaḥ ||
  • Translation: "In the morning, as you rise, may you go to your home with the strength of the earth, as if you are a king's son, and may you be blessed with prosperity."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • prātar: morning, the time of new beginnings.
    • jarethe: to rise or awaken, symbolizing the start of a new day.
    • jaraṇeva: like the earth, representing stability and strength.
    • kāpayā: with the strength or support, indicating reliance on the earth's resources.
    • vastor-vastor: of things, referring to material possessions or wealth.
    • yajatā: may you worship or honor, suggesting a sense of reverence.
    • gacchatho: may you go, indicating movement towards a goal.
    • gṛham: home, the place of comfort and security.
    • kasya: whose, questioning ownership or connection.
    • dhvasrā: blessed or prosperous, indicating a state of well-being.
    • bhavathaḥ: may you become, a wish for transformation.
    • narā: men or people, referring to humanity.
    • rājaputreva: like a king's son, symbolizing privilege and honor.
    • savanāva: blessed or prosperous, reinforcing the idea of abundance.
    • gacchathaḥ: may you go, a repetition emphasizing the journey.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Every morning, people wake up with the hope of achieving their goals, much like the mantra suggests. For instance, a student rising early to study for an important exam embodies the spirit of 'jarethe' and 'gacchatho gṛham.'
    • In a corporate setting, employees may start their day with a morning meeting, drawing strength from their teamwork and resources, akin to 'kāpayā vastor-vastor.'
    • Families often gather in the morning to share breakfast, reinforcing bonds and preparing for the day ahead, reflecting the essence of 'gṛham' as a place of comfort.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of new beginnings, strength, and prosperity. It serves as a reminder to approach each day with reverence and purpose, seeking blessings for success and well-being. In real life, this can be applied by setting daily intentions, fostering a sense of community, and recognizing the resources available to us. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a life filled with purpose and abundance, much like a king's son who is destined for greatness.
