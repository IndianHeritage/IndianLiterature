मारे अस्मद विं मुमुचो हरिप्रियार्वाङ याहि |इन्द्र स्वधावो मत्स्वेह ||
māre asmad vi mumuco haripriyārvāṅ yāhi | indra svadhāvo matsveha ||
  • māre asmad vi mumuco haripriyārvāṅ yāhi
    • māre: This word can be interpreted as "to die" or "to perish." In a broader sense, it signifies the transient nature of life and the inevitability of death.
    • asmad: This translates to "us" or "our," indicating a collective identity or community.
    • vi: This word means "through" or "by means of," suggesting a method or pathway.
    • mumuco: This term can be understood as "to release" or "to let go," indicating a process of liberation or freedom.
    • haripriyārvāṅ: This compound word can be broken down into "Hari" (often associated with Vishnu or the divine) and "priyā" (beloved), suggesting a connection to the divine or a higher power that is cherished.
    • yāhi: This means "come" or "go," indicating a call to action or movement towards something.
  • indra svadhāvo matsveha:
    • indra: Refers to the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
    • svadhāvo: This word can be interpreted as "self-essence" or "self-nature," indicating an intrinsic quality or characteristic.
    • matsveha: This translates to "in my mind" or "in my thoughts," suggesting a personal connection or internal reflection.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates themes of mortality, community, divine connection, and self-awareness. It reflects the human experience of grappling with the transient nature of life while seeking liberation through a connection with the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of reflection, such as during times of loss or change, encouraging individuals to let go of attachments and embrace the divine essence within themselves and their community. It serves as a reminder to seek strength and guidance from higher powers, much like how individuals turn to spirituality or community support during challenging times.
