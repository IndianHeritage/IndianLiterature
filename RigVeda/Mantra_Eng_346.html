समानयोजनो हि वां रथो दश्राव अमर्त्यः |समुद्रे अश्विनीयते ||
samānayojano hi vāṁ ratho dasrāv amartyaḥ | samudre aśvineyate ||
  • Mantra: "samānayojano hi vāṁ ratho dasrāv amartyaḥ | samudre aśvineyate ||"
  • Translation: "The one who unites (or brings together) is indeed your charioteer, O immortal ones; he who is in the ocean (of existence) is the Ashvins."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • samānayojano: The one who unites or brings together.
    • vāṁ: Your (referring to the Ashvins).
    • ratho: Charioteer or the one who drives the chariot.
    • dasrāv: Immortal or divine beings.
    • amartyaḥ: Immortal, often referring to the divine or celestial beings.
    • samudre: In the ocean, symbolizing the vastness of existence.
    • aśvineyate: Pertaining to the Ashvins, the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and well-being.
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • The mantra speaks to the role of a unifying force or charioteer in guiding the divine beings, specifically the Ashvins, who are known for their healing and benevolent nature.
    • In real life, this can be likened to a leader or mentor who brings people together for a common purpose, much like a coach uniting a sports team to achieve victory.
    • The ocean symbolizes the vast and often chaotic nature of life, where the Ashvins, as divine beings, navigate through challenges to bring health and prosperity.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and guidance in achieving success and well-being.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in a corporate environment, community building, or even in personal relationships where collaboration and support are essential.
    • It serves as a reminder that in the vast ocean of life, having a guiding force or a unifying leader can help individuals navigate challenges and reach their goals.
