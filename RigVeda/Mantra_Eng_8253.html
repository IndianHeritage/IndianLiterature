आ पवस्व गविष्टये महे सोम नृचक्षसे |एन्द्रस्य जटहरे विष ||
ā pavasva gaviṣṭaye mahe soma nṛcakṣase | endrasya jaṭhare viśa ||
  • Mantra: "ā pavasva gaviṣṭaye mahe soma nṛcakṣase | endrasya jaṭhare viśa ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: O (a call or invocation)
    • pavasva: may you flow or may you be purified
    • gaviṣṭaye: for the great (or supreme) purpose
    • mahe: great
    • soma: the sacred drink or elixir, often associated with immortality
    • nṛcakṣase: for the one who has the eyes of a man (often referring to Indra)
    • endrasya: of Indra (the king of gods)
    • jaṭhare: in the belly or womb
    • viśa: poison or harmful substance
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the divine Soma, asking it to flow and be purified for a great purpose.
    • Soma is often seen as a symbol of vitality and immortality, representing the essence of life.
    • The reference to Indra suggests a connection to strength and power, as Indra is known for his valor and ability to overcome obstacles.
    • The mention of "viśa" (poison) indicates a desire to transform negativity or harmful influences into something beneficial.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, this mantra can be applied when facing challenges or adversities. For instance, during a difficult time in life, one might invoke this mantra to seek strength and clarity, transforming negative experiences into opportunities for growth.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be relevant during collective struggles, such as natural disasters or social injustices. The invocation of Soma can symbolize the community's resilience and unity in overcoming hardships.
    • In health and wellness, the essence of Soma can be likened to practices that promote well-being, such as meditation or yoga, which help in purifying the mind and body, thus transforming stress (viśa) into peace.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of purification and transformation in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that even in the face of adversity (viśa), there is potential for growth and strength (mahe soma).
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate resilience, seek clarity, and strive for a higher purpose, making it applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal challenges to collective efforts for social change.
