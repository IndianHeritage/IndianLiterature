वर्धस्वा सु पुरुष्टुत ऋषिष्टुताभिर ऊतिभिः |धुक्षस्व पिप्युषीम इषम अवा च नः ||
vardhasvā su puruṣṭuta ṛṣiṣṭutābhir ūtibhiḥ | dhukṣasva pipyuṣīm iṣam avā ca naḥ ||
  • Vardhasvā: This word translates to "may you grow" or "may you prosper." It signifies a desire for growth and abundance in various aspects of life.
  • Su: Meaning "well" or "good," this word emphasizes the quality of the growth or prosperity being sought.
  • Puruṣṭuta: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is praised" or "the one who is worthy." It refers to individuals who are deserving of respect and admiration.
  • Ṛṣiṣṭutābhir: This word relates to the "praise of the sages" or "the acknowledgment of the wise." It highlights the importance of wisdom and the guidance of learned individuals in the pursuit of growth.
  • Ūtibhiḥ: This translates to "with strength" or "with vigor." It suggests that the growth and prosperity should be accompanied by strength and resilience.
  • Dhukṣasva: This term means "may you be nourished" or "may you be sustained." It emphasizes the need for sustenance and support in the journey of growth.
  • Pipyuṣīm: This word refers to "the essence" or "the best part." It signifies the importance of seeking the most valuable aspects of life.
  • Iṣam: Meaning "the goal" or "the aim," this word indicates the direction in which one should strive.
  • Avā ca naḥ: This phrase translates to "and may it not be taken away from us." It expresses a desire for the blessings of growth and nourishment to remain with us.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound aspiration for growth, nourishment, and the pursuit of wisdom. It emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance from the wise and maintaining strength in the face of challenges. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, professional growth, and community building. For instance, in a workplace setting, individuals can strive for growth by seeking mentorship from experienced colleagues, fostering a supportive environment, and focusing on their goals while ensuring that their achievements are sustainable and not fleeting. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate a life of purpose, strength, and continuous improvement, while cherishing the wisdom that guides us along the way.
