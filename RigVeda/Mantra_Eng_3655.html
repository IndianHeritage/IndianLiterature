कं एतं त्वं युवते कुमारं पेषी बिभर्षि महिषी जजाना |पूर्वीर हि गर्भः शरदो ववर्धापश्यं जातं यद असूत माता ||
kam etaṁ tvaṁ yuvate kumāram peṣī bibharṣi mahiṣī jajāna | pūrvīr hi garbhaḥ śarado vavardhāpaśyaṁ jātaṁ yad asūta mātā ||
  • kam etaṁ tvaṁ yuvate kumāram peṣī bibharṣi mahiṣī jajāna
    • kam etaṁ - "Who is this?" This phrase indicates a sense of inquiry or curiosity about a presence or entity.
    • tvaṁ - "You." This word addresses the subject directly, suggesting a personal connection or relationship.
    • yuvate - "young woman." This term signifies youth and vitality, often associated with potential and new beginnings.
    • kumāram - "young boy." This word represents innocence and the future generation, highlighting the importance of nurturing youth.
    • peṣī bibharṣi - "you who bear." This phrase acknowledges the role of the subject in carrying or nurturing life.
    • mahiṣī - "the great one" or "the queen." This term elevates the subject's status, indicating strength and authority.
    • jajāna - "has given birth." This word emphasizes the act of creation and the significance of motherhood.
  • pūrvīr hi garbhaḥ śarado vavardhā
    • pūrvīr - "of the ancients." This term connects the present to the past, suggesting a lineage or tradition.
    • hi - "indeed." This word adds emphasis to the statement that follows.
    • garbhaḥ - "womb" or "embryo." This word symbolizes the potential for life and the nurturing aspect of creation.
    • śarado - "autumn." This season often represents maturity and the fruition of efforts, a time of harvest.
    • vavardhā - "has grown." This term indicates development and the natural progression of life.
  • apaśyaṁ jātaṁ yad asūta mātā
    • apaśyaṁ - "I have seen." This word indicates awareness and recognition of existence.
    • jātaṁ - "born." This term signifies the arrival of new life and the continuation of existence.
    • yad - "that which." This word introduces a clause that describes the subject of the observation.
    • asūta - "was born." This word emphasizes the act of birth and the miracle of life.
    • mātā - "mother." This term highlights the nurturing and protective role of the mother in the cycle of life.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, motherhood, and the continuity of life. It reflects the deep respect for the feminine principle, recognizing the strength and nurturing qualities of women. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Celebrating motherhood and the role of women in society.
  • Encouraging the nurturing of future generations through education and care.
  • Recognizing the importance of heritage and the wisdom passed down through generations.
  • Promoting the idea of growth and maturity, akin to the seasons of life.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the sacredness of life and the vital role that both women and mothers play in the tapestry of existence.
