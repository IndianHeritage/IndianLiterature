अस्मान् समर्ये पवमान चोदय दक्षो देवानाम् असि हि प्रियः मदः |जहि शत्रूम् अभ्य आ भन्दनायतः पिबेन्द्र सोमम् अव नो मृधो जहि ||
asmān samarye pavamāna codaya dakṣo devānām asi hi priyo madaḥ | jahi śatrūm̐r abhy ā bhandanāyataḥ pibendra somam ava no mṛdho jahi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmān: us
    • samarye: in the battle
    • pavamāna: the purifying one
    • codaya: inspire or motivate
    • dakṣo: skilled or capable
    • devānām: of the gods
    • asi: you are
    • hi: indeed
    • priyo: dear or beloved
    • madaḥ: intoxication or joy
    • jahi: conquer or overcome
    • śatrūm̐: enemies
    • abhy: towards
    • ā: from
    • bhandanāyataḥ: bondage or ties
    • pibendra: O Lord of the drink
    • somam: soma (a sacred drink)
    • ava: down or away
    • no: our
    • mṛdho: from the earth
    • jahi: conquer or overcome
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a call for divine assistance in overcoming obstacles and enemies.
    • The speaker seeks the inspiration of the purifying force (pavamāna) to motivate them in their endeavors, especially in challenging situations like battles.
    • The reference to "dakṣo devānām" emphasizes the importance of being skilled and capable, suggesting that divine qualities are essential for success.
    • The term "priyo madaḥ" indicates that the joy derived from divine blessings is cherished, highlighting the relationship between humans and the divine.
    • The mantra also calls for the removal of enemies and the ties that bind, symbolizing the need for liberation from negativity and obstacles.
    • The invocation of "pibendra somam" refers to the consumption of soma, which is often associated with spiritual enlightenment and strength.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive work environment, individuals may recite this mantra to seek motivation and clarity in overcoming challenges posed by colleagues or market competition.
    • During personal struggles, such as health issues or emotional turmoil, one might reflect on this mantra to find inner strength and the courage to face adversities.
    • In sports, athletes often seek inspiration and mental fortitude to conquer their opponents, paralleling the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine support in overcoming life's challenges and adversities.
    • It teaches us to seek inspiration and strength from higher powers while emphasizing the need for skill and capability in our endeavors.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to confront our fears and obstacles with courage, drawing on both internal and external sources of strength.
