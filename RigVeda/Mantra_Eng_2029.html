दां नो अग्नि बृहतो दाः सहस्रिणो दुरो न वाजं श्रुत्या अपा वृधि |प्राची dyावापृथिवी ब्रह्मणा कृतिः स्वर्ण न शुक्रं उषसो विदिद्युतः ||
dā no agne bṛhato dāḥ sahasriṇo duro na vājaṁ śrutyā apā vṛdhi | prācī dyāvāpṛthivī brahmaṇā kṛdhi sva1r ṇa śukram uṣaso vi didyutaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "dā no agne bṛhato dāḥ sahasriṇo duro na vājaṁ śrutyā apā vṛdhi | prācī dyāvāpṛthivī brahmaṇā kṛdhi sva1r ṇa śukram uṣaso vi didyutaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dā: Give
    • no: to us
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • bṛhato: of the great
    • dāḥ: gifts
    • sahasriṇo: of the thousandfold
    • duro: strong
    • na: not
    • vājaṁ: wealth
    • śrutyā: by hearing
    • apā: away
    • vṛdhi: increase
    • prācī: in the east
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: heaven and earth
    • brahmaṇā: by the divine
    • kṛdhi: create
    • sva1r: our own
    • ṇa: not
    • śukram: pure
    • uṣaso: of the dawn
    • vi: indeed
    • didyutaḥ: shine
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for blessings and abundance.
    • The phrase "dā no agne" signifies a request for gifts or blessings from Agni, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • By invoking "bṛhato" and "sahasriṇo," the mantra emphasizes the desire for great and abundant gifts, suggesting a longing for prosperity and strength.
    • The line "duro na vājaṁ" indicates a plea for the removal of obstacles to wealth, highlighting the importance of overcoming challenges in the pursuit of success.
    • "śrutyā apā vṛdhi" suggests that through listening and understanding, one can achieve growth and increase, emphasizing the value of knowledge and wisdom.
    • The invocation of "prācī dyāvāpṛthivī" reflects the connection between heaven and earth, symbolizing the unity of the physical and spiritual realms.
    • Finally, "brahmaṇā kṛdhi sva1r ṇa śukram uṣaso vi didyutaḥ" calls for the creation of purity and brilliance in our lives, particularly at the dawn of new beginnings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied when seeking success and prosperity. Entrepreneurs often face challenges, and invoking the spirit of Agni can symbolize the drive to overcome these obstacles.
    • In personal development, the emphasis on knowledge and understanding can be seen in the pursuit of education and self-improvement, where listening and learning lead to growth.
    • In community gatherings or rituals, this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings for collective prosperity and harmony, reinforcing the idea of unity between individuals and the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of seeking blessings, overcoming obstacles, and striving for purity and brilliance in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the holistic approach it advocates, integrating spiritual, personal, and communal aspects of life.
