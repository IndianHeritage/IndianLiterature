तद वां नराः नासत्याव् अनु ष्याद् यद वाम् मानसा उचतम् अवोचन |अस्माद् अद्य सदसः सोम्याद् आ विद्यामेषं वृजनं जीरदानुम् ||
tad vāṁ narā nāsatyāv anu ṣyād yad vām mānāsa ucatham avocan | asmād adya sadasaḥ somyād ā vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • vāṁ: your
    • narā: men
    • nāsatyāv: of the Asvins (the twin deities)
    • anu: following
    • ṣyād: may be
    • yad: whatever
    • vām: you
    • mānāsa: mental
    • ucatham: utterance
    • avocan: they spoke
    • asmād: from this
    • adya: today
    • sadasaḥ: of the assembly
    • somyād: from the pleasant
    • ā: to
    • vidyāmeṣaṁ: knowledge
    • vṛjanaṁ: of the gathering
    • jīradānum: of the living
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the connection between divine knowledge and human understanding.
    • The Asvins, representing duality and balance, are invoked to guide the mental utterances of men.
    • It emphasizes the importance of collective wisdom and the sharing of knowledge in a community.
    • The phrase "from this assembly" suggests that knowledge is derived from collaboration and discourse.
    • The term "pleasant" indicates that the pursuit of knowledge should be a joyful and enriching experience.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, team meetings can be seen as a modern-day assembly where ideas are shared, leading to innovative solutions.
    • In educational settings, group discussions foster a collaborative environment where students learn from each other.
    • Community gatherings, such as town halls, allow for the exchange of knowledge and collective decision-making.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of collective knowledge and the role of community in personal and societal growth.
    • It teaches us that wisdom is not just an individual pursuit but a shared journey that thrives in an environment of collaboration.
    • In real life, applying this principle can lead to more effective problem-solving and a deeper understanding of complex issues.
