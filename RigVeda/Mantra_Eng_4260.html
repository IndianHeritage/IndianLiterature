युवोर अतृष चिकेटति नराः सुम्नेन चेतसा |गर्मं यद वाम अरेपसं नासत्यासनां भुरण्यति ||
yuvor atriś ciketati narā sumnena cetasā | gharmaṁ yad vām arepasaṁ nāsatyāsnā bhuraṇyati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yuvor: of the young
    • atriś: the one who is wise or knowledgeable
    • ciketati: thinks or contemplates
    • narā: men or people
    • sumnena: with a good or auspicious mind
    • cetasā: with consciousness or intellect
    • gharmaṁ: warmth or heat
    • yad: that which
    • vām: of you
    • arepasaṁ: is to be desired or sought
    • nāsatyāsnā: the one who is truthful or honest
    • bhuraṇyati: brings or provides
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the contemplation of the young and wise individuals.
    • It emphasizes the importance of having a good and auspicious mindset while thinking.
    • The warmth or positive energy that one seeks is associated with truthfulness and honesty.
    • It suggests that those who are truthful and honest are the ones who provide warmth and positivity in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, young professionals who think positively and truthfully contribute to a healthy work environment.
    • In relationships, individuals who approach their interactions with honesty and a good mindset foster deeper connections.
    • In education, students who contemplate their learning with a positive attitude often achieve better results.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of positive thinking and truthfulness in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various scenarios, including personal relationships, professional settings, and educational environments.
    • By embodying the qualities of wisdom, positivity, and honesty, individuals can create a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
