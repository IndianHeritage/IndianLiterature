स सुन्वे यो वसूनां यो रायाम आनेटा य इळानाम् |सोमो यः सुख्षितीनाम् ||
sa sunve yo vasūnāṁ yo rāyām ānetā ya iḻānām | somo yaḥ sukṣitīnām ||
  • sa sunve: This phrase can be translated as "may he listen" or "he who listens." It emphasizes the importance of being receptive and attentive, whether to divine guidance or the needs of others.
  • yo vasūnāṁ: This means "who brings forth wealth" or "who is the source of prosperity." It highlights the idea that true wealth comes from a higher power or divine source.
  • yo rāyām ānetā: This translates to "who brings forth abundance." It suggests that there is a force or deity that facilitates the flow of resources and prosperity into our lives.
  • ya iḻānām: This means "who is the source of nourishment." It indicates that the divine provides sustenance and support, both physically and spiritually.
  • somo yaḥ sukṣitīnām: This can be interpreted as "the one who is like the moon for those who are well-off." The moon is often associated with calmness, beauty, and nourishment, suggesting that this divine presence brings peace and abundance to those who are deserving.

In conclusion, this mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of divine listening, prosperity, and nourishment. It serves as a reminder that true wealth and abundance come from a higher source, and that being receptive to this divine guidance can lead to a fulfilling life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:


  • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their openness to receiving guidance and support from others, fostering a mindset of abundance.
  • In community settings, it can inspire collective efforts to create environments where everyone can thrive, emphasizing the importance of listening to each other's needs.
  • In spiritual practices, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek divine assistance in times of need, reinforcing the belief that one is not alone in their journey.

The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with the divine source of abundance and nourishment, encouraging a life of gratitude, receptiveness, and communal support.
