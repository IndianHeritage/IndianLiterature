मन्त्र: उता ते सुष्टुताऽ हरिः वृषाणः वहतो रथम् |अजुर्वस्य मदिन्तमं यम् ईमहे ||
uta te suṣṭutā harī vṛṣaṇā vahato ratham | ajuryasya madintamaṁ yam īmahe ||
  • Mantra: "uta te suṣṭutā harī vṛṣaṇā vahato ratham | ajuryasya madintamaṁ yam īmahe ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and, also
    • te: your
    • suṣṭutā: well praised, well spoken
    • harī: the divine, often referring to the sun or a deity
    • vṛṣaṇā: the strong, powerful
    • vahato: who carries, who drives
    • ratham: the chariot
    • ajuryasya: of the one who is immortal, eternal
    • madintamaṁ: the most excellent, the best
    • yam: whom
    • īmahe: we invoke, we call upon
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine, acknowledging the strength and power of the divine charioteer.
    • It emphasizes the importance of invoking the eternal and the best aspects of divinity in our lives.
    • The chariot symbolizes the journey of life, where the divine guides and supports us through challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of difficulty, individuals often seek divine guidance, much like invoking this mantra for strength and support.
    • In a team setting, a leader can be seen as the charioteer, guiding their team towards a common goal, invoking the best qualities in each member.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, people often seek blessings, paralleling the invocation of divine support in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and invoking divine strength and guidance in our lives.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the journey of life and the support we receive from higher powers.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to seek help and strength from their beliefs, whether through prayer, meditation, or community support.
