सं घोषः श्रिण्वेऽवमैर अमित्रैर जहीन्येष्व अशनिं तपिष्ठाम् |वृश्चेम अधस्ताद् विरुजा सहस्व जहि रक्षो मगवन रन्धयस्व ||
saṁ ghoṣaḥ śṛṇve 'vamair amitrair jahī ny eṣv aśaniṁ tapiṣṭhām | vṛścem adhastād vi rujā sahasva jahi rakṣo maghavan randhayasva ||
  • saṁ ghoṣaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "the collective sound" or "the unified call." It signifies the importance of unity and harmony among individuals or groups.
  • śṛṇve: This means "to hear" or "to listen." It emphasizes the need for attentiveness and understanding in communication.
  • 'vamair: This term refers to "those who are hostile" or "enemies." It highlights the presence of adversities or challenges in life.
  • amitrair: This translates to "by the non-friends" or "by those who are not allies." It suggests the idea of facing opposition or negativity from others.
  • jahī: This means "to overcome" or "to conquer." It indicates the need for strength and resilience in the face of challenges.
  • ny eṣv: This can be interpreted as "do not let" or "do not allow." It conveys a sense of caution and the need to guard against negativity.
  • aśaniṁ: This word means "the lightning" or "the thunderbolt." It symbolizes power, energy, and the force of nature.
  • tapiṣṭhām: This translates to "the most powerful" or "the strongest." It signifies the ultimate strength that one can harness.
  • vṛścem: This means "to be strong" or "to be powerful." It emphasizes the importance of inner strength and resilience.
  • adhastād: This translates to "from below" or "beneath." It suggests that challenges may arise from unexpected places.
  • vi rujā: This means "from pain" or "from suffering." It acknowledges the existence of difficulties and hardships in life.
  • sahasva: This translates to "be strong" or "be courageous." It encourages individuals to face their fears and challenges head-on.
  • jahi: This means "to defeat" or "to conquer." It reinforces the idea of overcoming obstacles.
  • rakṣo: This refers to "demons" or "evil forces." It symbolizes the negative influences that can affect one's life.
  • maghavan: This term means "the generous one" or "the benefactor." It represents the positive forces or allies that can aid in overcoming challenges.
  • randhayasva: This translates to "to break" or "to shatter." It signifies the act of dismantling negativity or obstacles.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, strength, and resilience in the face of adversity. It teaches us to listen carefully, recognize challenges, and summon the courage to overcome them. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as facing personal struggles, dealing with conflicts in relationships, or overcoming professional obstacles. By fostering a sense of community and support, we can collectively rise above challenges and create a more harmonious environment. The mantra serves as a reminder that while adversities may arise, with strength and unity, we can conquer them and emerge victorious.
