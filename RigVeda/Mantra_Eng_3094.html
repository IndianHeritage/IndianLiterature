आयं योनिष चक्रमा यं वयं ते जायेव पत्या उषती सुवासा: |अर्वाचीनः परिवीतो नि शीदेमा उ ते स्वपाक प्रतिची: ||
ayaṁ yoniś cakṛmā yaṁ vayaṁ te jāyeva patya uśatī suvāsāḥ | arvācīnaḥ parivīto ni ṣīdemā u te svapāka pratīcīḥ ||
  • ayaṁ yoniś cakṛmā: This phrase can be translated as "this womb is created." The term "yoni" refers to the source or womb, symbolizing creation and the origin of life. In real life, this can be related to the nurturing aspect of motherhood and the importance of family as the foundation of society.
  • yaṁ vayaṁ te jāyeva patya uśatī suvāsāḥ: This part means "that which you give birth to, may it be auspicious and well-nurtured." It emphasizes the significance of bringing forth life that is healthy and prosperous. In practical terms, this can be seen in the efforts parents make to provide a good upbringing for their children, ensuring they grow up in a supportive environment.
  • arvācīnaḥ parivīto ni ṣīdemā: This translates to "surrounded by the ancient, may we dwell." It suggests a connection to tradition and the wisdom of the past. In modern life, this can be reflected in how communities value their heritage and the lessons learned from previous generations, which guide current practices and beliefs.
  • u te svapāka pratīcīḥ: This means "may you be the one who prepares the offerings." It signifies the act of preparation and contribution to the community or family. In everyday life, this can be seen in how individuals contribute to their families, workplaces, or communities, preparing for celebrations, rituals, or communal gatherings.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, nurturing, tradition, and community contribution. Its significance lies in the reminder of the responsibilities we hold towards our families and society. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to foster a nurturing environment for future generations, respect their cultural heritage, and actively participate in communal life. By embodying these principles, we can create a harmonious and prosperous society that honors both the past and the future.
