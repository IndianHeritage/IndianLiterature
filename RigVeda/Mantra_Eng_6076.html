यद वां रुमे रुशमे श्यावके कृपा इन्द्र मादयसे सचा |कण्वासस त्वा ब्रह्मभिः स्तोमवाहस इन्द्रा यच्छन्त्य आगहि ||
yad vā rume ruśame śyāvake kṛpa indra mādayase sacā | kaṇvāsas tvā brahmabhiḥ stomavāhasa indrā yacchanty ā gahi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • vā: or
    • rume: in the form of delight
    • ruśame: in the form of strength
    • śyāvake: in the form of the dark one (often referring to the clouds or rain)
    • kṛpa: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • indrā: O Indra
    • mādayase: may you be pleased
    • sacā: with this offering
    • kaṇvāsas: O you who are adorned with glory
    • tvā: you
    • brahmabhiḥ: with hymns of praise
    • stomavāhasa: who carries the praises
    • yachchanty: who are invoked
    • ā: towards
    • gahi: may you accept
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, asking for his blessings and strength.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies the importance of nature and its elements in human life, particularly the need for rain for agriculture.
    • The phrase "may you be pleased with this offering" indicates the practice of making offerings to deities to seek their favor and blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Farmers often pray to Indra during the planting season, hoping for timely rains to ensure a good harvest.
    • In many cultures, rituals and offerings are made to deities to seek blessings for prosperity and protection, similar to the essence of this mantra.
    • In modern times, environmental awareness and respect for nature echo the sentiments expressed in this mantra, as people recognize the importance of sustainable practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the relationship between humans and nature, emphasizing the need for divine support in achieving prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the importance of respecting and nurturing the environment.
    • In contemporary society, the principles of gratitude, respect for nature, and seeking harmony with the environment are more relevant than ever, making this mantra applicable in various contexts, from agriculture to environmental conservation.
