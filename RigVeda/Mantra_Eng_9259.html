बृहस्पतिः नः परि पातु पश्चाद् उत्तरसमाद् अधराद् अघायोः | इन्द्रः पुरस्ताद् उता मध्यतो नः सखा सखिभ्यः वरिवः कृणोतु ||
bṛhaspatir naḥ pari pātu paścād utottarasmād adharād aghāyoḥ | indraḥ purastād uta madhyato naḥ sakhā sakhibhyo varivaḥ kṛṇotu ||
  • Mantra: "bṛhaspatir naḥ pari pātu paścād utottarasmād adharād aghāyoḥ | indraḥ purastād uta madhyato naḥ sakhā sakhibhyo varivaḥ kṛṇotu ||"
  • Translation: "May Brihaspati protect us from behind, from above, and from below, from all evils. May Indra be in front of us and in the middle, and may he grant us friendship and prosperity."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Brihaspati: The deity of wisdom and knowledge, often associated with Jupiter. Represents guidance and intellect.
    • naḥ: Our, indicating a collective plea for protection.
    • pari pātu: May protect, a request for safeguarding from all sides.
    • paścād: From behind, emphasizing the need for protection from unseen dangers.
    • utottarasmād: From above, indicating protection from higher threats.
    • adharād: From below, suggesting safeguarding from foundational or hidden evils.
    • aghāyoḥ: From all evils, a broad term encompassing all forms of negativity.
    • Indra: The king of gods, symbolizing strength and power, often invoked for success and victory.
    • purastād: In front, indicating the need for guidance and strength in the path ahead.
    • uta: And, connecting the various aspects of protection and support.
    • madhyato: In the middle, suggesting balance and stability in life.
    • sakhā: Friend, representing companionship and support.
    • sakhibhyo: To friends, indicating the importance of community and relationships.
    • varivaḥ: Grant, a request for blessings and abundance.
    • kṛṇotu: May he create or bestow, a hopeful plea for positive outcomes.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging work environment, invoking Brihaspati can symbolize seeking wisdom and guidance to navigate complexities.
    • During difficult times, such as illness or loss, this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek support from friends and community, emphasizing the importance of relationships.
    • In educational pursuits, students may recite this mantra to seek protection from distractions and to gain clarity in their studies.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of holistic protection and support in life. It signifies the need for guidance from higher powers, the strength to face challenges, and the value of friendships. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as personal challenges, professional endeavors, and educational pursuits. By invoking these divine energies, individuals can cultivate a sense of security and community, fostering resilience and prosperity in their lives.
