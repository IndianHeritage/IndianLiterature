सूर्यं चक्षुर गच्छतु वातम् आत्मा द्यां च गच्छ पृथिवीं च धर्मणा |अपो वा गच्छ यदि तत्र ते हितम् औषधिषु प्रति तिष्ठा शरीरैः ||
sūryaṁ cakṣur gacchatu vātam ātmā dyāṁ ca gaccha pṛthivīṁ ca dharmaṇā | apo vā gaccha yadi tatra te hitam oṣadhīṣu prati tiṣṭhā śarīraiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sūryaṁ: Sun
    • cakṣur: Eye
    • gacchatu: May go
    • vātam: Wind
    • ātmā: Soul
    • dyāṁ: Sky
    • ca: And
    • gaccha: Go
    • pṛthivīṁ: Earth
    • ca: And
    • dharmaṇā: By righteousness
    • apo: Water
    • vā: Or
    • gaccha: Go
    • yadi: If
    • tatra: There
    • te: Your
    • hitam: Welfare
    • oṣadhīṣu: In herbs
    • prati: In return
    • tiṣṭhā: Remain
    • śarīraiḥ: With bodies
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Sun, the Wind, the Sky, and the Earth, emphasizing the interconnectedness of nature and the importance of righteousness.
    • The phrase "sūryaṁ cakṣur gacchatu" suggests that the Sun is the eye that observes and illuminates the world, symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
    • "vātam ātmā dyāṁ ca gaccha pṛthivīṁ" indicates that the wind and the soul should also traverse the sky and the earth, highlighting the unity of all elements in the universe.
    • The mention of "dharmaṇā" signifies that this journey should be guided by righteousness, suggesting that ethical conduct is essential in our interactions with nature.
    • "apo vā gaccha yadi tatra te hitam" implies that if water is to go, it should do so for the welfare of all, reinforcing the idea of selflessness and service to others.
    • Finally, "oṣadhīṣu prati tiṣṭhā śarīraiḥ" suggests that the benefits of these natural elements should be returned to the bodies, indicating a cycle of giving and receiving in nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for sunlight and rain, recognizing the importance of these elements for the growth of crops, which sustains life.
    • Environmental movements advocate for the protection of nature, emphasizing the need for ethical practices that respect the earth and its resources.
    • Yoga and meditation practices often focus on connecting with nature, promoting a sense of unity with the universe, akin to the mantra's message of interconnectedness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the harmony between humanity and nature, urging us to act with righteousness and care for the environment.
    • Its significance lies in promoting a balanced relationship with the natural world, encouraging sustainable practices that benefit all living beings.
    • In real life, it can inspire individuals and communities to engage in eco-friendly initiatives, fostering a sense of responsibility towards the planet.
