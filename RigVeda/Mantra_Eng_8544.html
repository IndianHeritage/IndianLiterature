त्वया हि नः पितरः सोम पूर्वे कर्माणि चक्रुः पवमान धीराः |वन्वन्न अवातः परिधीर्म् अपोर्णु वीरैभिर अश्वैर मगवाऽ भव नः ||
tvayā hi naḥ pitaraḥ soma pūrve karmāṇi cakruḥ pavamāna dhīrāḥ | vanvann avātaḥ paridhīm̐r aporṇu vīrebhir aśvair maghavā bhavā naḥ ||
  • tvayā hi naḥ pitaraḥ - "For you are our father" - This phrase acknowledges a divine or parental figure, suggesting a relationship of care and guidance. In real life, this can be likened to the role of a mentor or a parental figure who provides support and wisdom.
  • soma pūrve karmāṇi cakruḥ - "The ancient fathers performed actions" - This refers to the ancestors or previous generations who have laid the groundwork for the present. In modern terms, this can be seen in how our forebears' decisions and actions shape our current lives, such as the values instilled in us by our parents or the cultural heritage we inherit.
  • pavamāna dhīrāḥ - "The wise ones who are purified" - This highlights the importance of wisdom and purity in thought and action. In everyday life, this can relate to individuals who strive for integrity and clarity in their decisions, such as leaders who prioritize ethical practices in their governance.
  • vanvann avātaḥ - "May the winds blow" - This symbolizes the movement of life and the forces of nature that influence our existence. In a practical sense, this can be compared to how environmental changes affect our daily lives, such as climate change impacting agriculture and livelihoods.
  • paridhīm̐r aporṇu - "May you fill the circle" - This suggests the idea of completeness and fulfillment. In real life, this can be related to achieving personal goals or finding balance in life, such as maintaining a healthy work-life balance or fulfilling one's potential.
  • vīrebhir aśvair - "With the strong and the swift" - This phrase emphasizes strength and agility, qualities that are essential for overcoming challenges. In contemporary scenarios, this can be seen in athletes who train rigorously to achieve excellence or in businesses that adapt quickly to market changes.
  • maghavā bhavā naḥ - "May you be our great one" - This is a plea for divine support and greatness in our endeavors. In real life, this can be interpreted as seeking guidance from a higher power or striving for greatness in our personal and professional lives, such as aspiring to be leaders in our communities.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking guidance, wisdom, and strength from both divine and ancestral sources. It emphasizes the importance of acknowledging our roots while striving for personal and communal growth. In today's world, the principles of this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, mentorship, and personal development, reminding us to honor our past while actively shaping our future.
