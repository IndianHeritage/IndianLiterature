प्र वां स्तोमाḥ सुवृक्तयो गिरो वर्धन्त्व अश्विनाः |पुरुत्रा वृत्रहन्तमा ता नो भूतम पुरुष्पृहा ||
pra vāṁ stomāḥ suvṛktayo giro vardhantv aśvinā | purutrā vṛtrahantamā tā no bhūtam puruspṛhā ||
  • pra vāṁ stomāḥ: This phrase can be translated as "let our praises" or "let our hymns" be directed towards the divine. In real life, this can be seen as an encouragement to express gratitude and appreciation for the positive aspects of life, whether through prayer, meditation, or simply acknowledging the good around us.
  • suvṛktayo giro: This means "with well-spoken words" or "with eloquent speech." It emphasizes the importance of communication and the power of words. In everyday life, this can relate to how we communicate with others, highlighting the need for kindness, clarity, and positivity in our interactions.
  • vardhantv aśvinā: This translates to "may they be enhanced by the Ashvins." The Ashvins are twin deities associated with health, medicine, and well-being. This part of the mantra suggests a desire for growth and improvement in our lives, invoking the blessings of these divine figures. In practical terms, it can be seen as a call to seek help and support from others, whether through professional guidance or community support.
  • purutrā vṛtrahantamā: This phrase means "the one who destroys obstacles from all sides." It signifies the removal of barriers and challenges in life. In real life, this can be interpreted as a reminder to confront and overcome difficulties, whether they are personal, professional, or societal. It encourages resilience and determination in the face of adversity.
  • tā no bhūtam puruspṛhā: This translates to "may that which is desired come to us." It reflects the human aspiration for fulfillment and success. In practical terms, this can relate to setting goals and working towards them, while also being open to the opportunities that life presents. It emphasizes the importance of intention and action in achieving our desires.

Conclusion: The mantra "pra vāṁ stomāḥ suvṛktayo giro vardhantv aśvinā | purutrā vṛtrahantamā tā no bhūtam puruspṛhā ||" encapsulates a profound message about the power of words, the importance of seeking divine assistance, and the need to overcome obstacles in pursuit of our desires. Its significance lies in its applicability to everyday life, encouraging individuals to express gratitude, communicate effectively, seek support, confront challenges, and actively pursue their goals. By embodying these principles, one can cultivate a more fulfilling and meaningful existence.
