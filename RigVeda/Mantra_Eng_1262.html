मां नो महान्तम उता मां नो अर्बकम् मां न उक्षन्तम उता मां न उक्षितम् |मां नो वधिः पितरम् मोत मातरम् मां नः प्रियास तन्भो रुद्र रीरिषः ||
mā no mahāntam uta mā no arbhakam mā na ukṣantam uta mā na ukṣitam | mā no vadhīḥ pitaram mota mātaram mā naḥ priyās tanvo rudra rīriṣaḥ ||
  • mā no mahāntam uta mā no arbhakam: This phrase can be translated as "Let us not be harmed by the great ones, nor by the small ones." Here, "mahāntam" refers to powerful or significant beings, while "arbham" refers to the vulnerable or lesser beings. In real life, this can be understood as a plea for protection from both those in positions of power and those who may take advantage of the weak.
  • mā na ukṣantam uta mā na ukṣitam: This translates to "Let us not be struck, nor let us strike." This line emphasizes the importance of non-violence and the desire to avoid conflict. In everyday life, this can be applied in situations of disagreement or confrontation, advocating for peaceful resolutions rather than resorting to aggression.
  • mā no vadhīḥ pitaram mota mātaram: This means "Let us not harm our father or mother." This line highlights the significance of familial bonds and respect for one's parents. In a real-life context, it serves as a reminder to honor and care for our parents, recognizing their sacrifices and contributions to our lives.
  • mā naḥ priyās tanvo rudra rīriṣaḥ: This can be interpreted as "Let not our beloved ones be harmed, O Rudra." Rudra is often associated with storms and destruction, but also with healing and protection. This line reflects a deep concern for the well-being of loved ones. In practical terms, it encourages us to create safe environments for our friends and family, ensuring their protection from harm.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound plea for protection and non-violence, emphasizing the importance of safeguarding both the powerful and the vulnerable, as well as our loved ones. Its significance lies in its universal message of compassion, respect, and the pursuit of peace. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can guide individuals in their interactions, promoting harmony in families, communities, and societies at large. By embodying these principles, we contribute to a more peaceful and respectful world.
