मुञ्चन्तु मां शापथ्याद् अथो वरुण्यादुत |अथो यमस्य पद्बीशात् सर्वस्माद् देवकिल्बिषात् ||
muñcantu mā śapathyā3d atho varuṇyād uta | atho yamasya paḍbīśāt sarvasmād devakilbiṣāt ||
  • muñcantu: This word translates to "let them release" or "may they free." It signifies a desire for liberation or release from bondage.
  • mā: This means "not" or "do not," indicating a negation or a plea against something undesirable.
  • śapathyā: This term can be interpreted as "from the curse" or "from the evil." It refers to the negative consequences or afflictions that one may face.
  • atho: This word means "then" or "therefore," suggesting a transition or a consequence of the previous statement.
  • varuṇyād: This refers to "from Varuna," who is the Vedic god of water and cosmic order. Varuna is often associated with moral and natural law.
  • uta: This means "and" or "also," connecting the ideas presented in the mantra.
  • yamasya: This translates to "of Yama," the god of death and the underworld, representing the inevitability of death and the moral order of the universe.
  • paḍbīśāt: This can be interpreted as "from the bonds" or "from the ties," indicating a desire to be free from the constraints imposed by Yama.
  • sarvasmād: This means "from all" or "from every," emphasizing a comprehensive plea for liberation.
  • devakilbiṣāt: This term refers to "from the sins of the gods" or "from divine transgressions," suggesting that even divine beings can impose burdens or curses.

The mantra essentially expresses a plea for liberation from curses, bonds, and the negative influences of both divine and mortal realms. It acknowledges the roles of Varuna and Yama, highlighting the interconnectedness of moral order, death, and the consequences of actions.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Seeking Forgiveness: Individuals may recite this mantra when seeking forgiveness for their wrongdoings, hoping to be released from the burdens of guilt and shame.
  • Overcoming Challenges: It can be used as a meditative tool to overcome personal challenges, such as addiction or negative habits, by invoking a sense of liberation.
  • Facing Mortality: The acknowledgment of Yama reminds individuals to reflect on their lives and actions, encouraging them to live morally and with purpose.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of liberation from negative influences and the moral responsibilities we hold. It encourages individuals to seek freedom from both internal and external constraints, fostering a sense of peace and clarity in their lives.
