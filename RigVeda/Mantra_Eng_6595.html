ते हि ष्मा वनुषो नरो 'भिमातिं कयस्य चित् |तिग्मं न क्षोढः प्रतिघ्नन्ति भूर्णयः ||
te hi ṣmā vanuṣo naro 'bhimātiṁ kayasya cit | tigmaṁ na kṣodaḥ pratighnanti bhūrṇayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • te hi: indeed you
    • ṣmā: are (or exist)
    • vanuṣo: of the forest or wilderness
    • naro: man or human
    • 'bhimātiṁ: who is fearless or brave
    • kayasya: of whom (or whose)
    • cit: thought or consciousness
    • tigmaṁ: sharp or intense
    • na: not
    • kṣodaḥ: wounds or injuries
    • pratighnanti: can harm or affect
    • bhūrṇayaḥ: the strong or powerful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the essence of bravery and resilience in the face of challenges.
    • It suggests that a person who is fearless and possesses a strong consciousness is not easily harmed by the sharp wounds of life.
    • The reference to "forest" symbolizes the wilderness of life, where dangers and uncertainties abound.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A soldier facing the uncertainties of battle embodies the spirit of this mantra, demonstrating bravery and resilience despite the dangers surrounding him.
    • An entrepreneur navigating the challenges of starting a new business reflects the fearless attitude described, as they confront risks and setbacks with determination.
    • A person overcoming personal struggles, such as illness or loss, showcases the strength of character that this mantra encourages.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of courage and mental strength in overcoming life's adversities.
    • It serves as a reminder that a strong mind and fearless heart can protect us from the wounds that life may inflict.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to face their fears, embrace challenges, and emerge stronger from their experiences.
