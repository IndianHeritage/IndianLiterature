प्र शर्ध आर्त प्रथमं विपण्यां ऋतस्य योना वृषभस्य नीले |स्पार्हो युवा वपुष्यो विभावा सप्त प्रियासो जनयन्त वृष्णे ||
pra śardha ārta prathamaṁ vipanyām̐ ṛtasya yonā vṛṣabhasya nīḻe | spārho yuvā vapuṣyo vibhāvā sapta priyāso 'janayanta vṛṣṇe ||
  • pra śardha ārta prathamaṁ vipanyām̐: This phrase can be interpreted as "the first among the strong, the one who brings forth abundance." In real life, this can relate to leaders or pioneers in various fields who initiate change and bring prosperity to their communities.
  • ṛtasya yonā vṛṣabhasya nīḻe: This translates to "the source of truth, the one who is like a bull." Here, the bull symbolizes strength and reliability. In practical terms, this could refer to individuals or organizations that uphold truth and integrity, serving as a foundation for others to build upon.
  • spārho yuvā vapuṣyo vibhāvā: This means "the youthful and vibrant form that is desired." This can be seen in the context of innovation and creativity, where youthful energy drives progress in technology, art, and culture.
  • sapta priyāso 'janayanta vṛṣṇe: This translates to "the seven beloved ones who give birth to abundance." In a real-life scenario, this could refer to the seven days of the week, each representing opportunities for growth and success in various aspects of life, such as work, relationships, and personal development.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, truth, youthfulness, and abundance in life. It signifies that by embodying these qualities, individuals can lead and inspire others, fostering a community that thrives on integrity and innovation. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in leadership roles, personal development, and community building, encouraging people to strive for excellence and support one another in their journeys.
