धेनुष्ट इन्द्र सूनृताय यजमानाय सुन्वते |गाम अश्वं पिप्युषी दुहे ||
dhenuṣ ṭa indra sūnṛtā yajamānāya sunvate | gām aśvam pipyuṣī duhe ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • dhenuṣ: Refers to a cow, symbolizing nourishment and wealth.
    • ṭa: A particle that can indicate a connection or emphasis.
    • indra: The king of gods, representing strength and power.
    • sūnṛtā: Meaning 'truthful' or 'righteous', indicating moral integrity.
    • yajamānāya: Refers to the one who performs a sacrifice or offering.
    • sunvate: Meaning 'to those who are good' or 'to the virtuous'.
    • gām: Refers to 'the cow' or 'the herd', again emphasizing wealth and sustenance.
    • aśvam: Meaning 'horse', symbolizing strength and vitality.
    • pipyuṣī: Refers to 'the one who gives milk', indicating abundance.
    • duhe: Meaning 'to milk', representing the act of extracting nourishment.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the god of rain and thunder, asking for blessings of wealth and nourishment.
    • The mention of cows and horses signifies the importance of livestock in ancient agrarian societies, representing prosperity and sustenance.
    • The mantra emphasizes the value of righteousness and truthfulness in the act of offering, suggesting that moral integrity is essential for receiving blessings.
    • It highlights the relationship between the yajamānā (the one performing the sacrifice) and the divine, indicating that virtuous actions lead to divine favor.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, cows are often seen as a source of wealth and sustenance. The mantra can be related to the importance of caring for livestock and ensuring their well-being for prosperity.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be interpreted as a call for ethical business practices, where truthfulness and integrity lead to success and abundance.
    • During festivals or rituals, offerings made with sincerity and righteousness can be seen as a way to invoke blessings, similar to the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the connection between moral integrity, divine blessings, and prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder that ethical actions and sincerity in our endeavors can lead to abundance in various forms, whether in personal life, business, or community.
    • In today's world, the principles of this mantra can be applied to foster a sense of responsibility towards our resources and relationships, ensuring a harmonious and prosperous existence.
