तद इन्द्रावा आ भर येनाद दंसीष्ठ कृत्वने |द्विताकुत्साय शिष्णाथो न चोदय ||
tad indrāva ā bhara yenā daṁsiṣṭha kṛtvane | dvitā kutsāya śiśnatho ni codaya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • indrāva: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • ā bhara: may you bring forth
    • yenā: by which
    • daṁsiṣṭha: the best or the most excellent
    • kṛtvane: for the one who acts or performs
    • dvitā: twice or again
    • kutsāya: to the one who is envious or malicious
    • śiśnatho: may you not be disturbed
    • ni codaya: do not provoke or incite
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, asking him to bestow his blessings and strength upon the devotee.
    • The phrase "may you bring forth by which the best" suggests a desire for excellence in actions and outcomes.
    • The latter part of the mantra addresses the need for protection against envy and malice, indicating a recognition of the challenges posed by negative forces in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive work environment, individuals often face jealousy from colleagues. This mantra can serve as a reminder to seek the strength and wisdom to rise above such negativity.
    • In personal relationships, one may encounter envious friends or family members. The invocation of Indra's protection can help maintain harmony and peace.
    • In sports, athletes often face pressure and rivalry. This mantra can inspire them to focus on their performance and not be distracted by the negativity of competitors.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its dual focus on seeking divine support for excellence while also recognizing and mitigating the impact of negative influences.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, including professional settings, personal relationships, and competitive environments.
    • By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset of resilience, striving for their best while remaining unaffected by the envy of others.
