स इति सुदानुः स्ववाम् ऋतावेंद्रा यो वां वरुण दाशति तमन् |इषा स द्विषस् तरेद दास्वान वंसद रयिं रयिवतश्च जनान् ||
sa it sudānuḥ svavām̐ ṛtāvendrā yo vāṁ varuṇa dāśati tman | iṣā sa dviṣas tared dāsvān vaṁsad rayiṁ rayivataś ca janān ||
  • sa it sudānuḥ svavām̐ ṛtāvendrā: This phrase can be interpreted as "He who is the giver of good things, the one who is the embodiment of truth and righteousness." In real life, this can be related to leaders or individuals who promote honesty and integrity in their actions, inspiring others to follow suit.
  • yo vāṁ varuṇa dāśati tman: This translates to "He who bestows upon you the protection of Varuna." Varuna is often associated with cosmic order and moral authority. In practical terms, this can be seen in the role of law and justice in society, where systems are in place to protect individuals and maintain order.
  • iṣā sa dviṣas tared dāsvān: This means "May he who is powerful overcome the enemies." This can be applied to personal struggles or conflicts in life, where individuals seek strength and resilience to overcome challenges, whether they be in personal relationships, work environments, or societal issues.
  • vaṁsad rayiṁ rayivataś ca janān: This phrase can be understood as "May he grant wealth and prosperity to the people." In a modern context, this can relate to economic growth and the importance of community support in achieving collective prosperity, such as through local businesses or cooperative initiatives.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of truth, protection, strength, and prosperity. It serves as a reminder of the values that should guide individuals and communities in their daily lives. By embodying these principles, one can contribute to a harmonious society where justice prevails, challenges are met with resilience, and prosperity is shared among all. This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal development to community building, highlighting the interconnectedness of individual actions and collective well-being.
