आयं दिव इयर्ति विश्वम् आरजः सोमः पुनानः कलशेषु सीदति |अद्भिर गोभिर मृज्यते अद्रिभिः सुतः पुनान इन्दुर वरिवो विदत प्रियम् ||
ayaṁ diva iyarti viśvam ā rajaḥ somaḥ punānaḥ kalaśeṣu sīdati | adbhir gobhir mṛjyate adribhiḥ sutaḥ punāna indur varivo vidat priyam ||
  • ayaṁ diva iyarti viśvam ā rajaḥ somaḥ punānaḥ kalaśeṣu sīdati
    • ayaṁ diva: This phrase translates to "this day" or "this sky." It signifies the present moment or the divine aspect of time.
    • iyarti: Means "to move" or "to flow." It indicates the dynamic nature of life and the universe.
    • viśvam: Refers to "the whole" or "the universe." It encompasses all beings and elements within existence.
    • ā rajaḥ: Translates to "the essence" or "the principle." It signifies the core or fundamental nature of things.
    • somaḥ: Refers to the sacred drink or elixir, symbolizing nourishment and spiritual awakening.
    • punānaḥ: Means "purifying" or "cleansing." It indicates the process of renewal and rejuvenation.
    • kalaśeṣu: Refers to "in the vessels" or "in the pots." It symbolizes the receptacles of life and knowledge.
    • sīdati: Means "sits" or "resides." It indicates a state of being or presence.
  • adbhir gobhir mṛjyate adribhiḥ sutaḥ punāna indur varivo vidat priyam
    • adbhir: Means "with waters" or "by the waters." It signifies the importance of water in life and purification.
    • gobhir: Refers to "with cows" or "by the cattle." Cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and sustenance in Vedic culture.
    • mṛjyate: Means "is cleansed" or "is purified." It indicates the act of purification through various means.
    • adribhiḥ: Refers to "by the mountains." Mountains symbolize stability and strength.
    • sutaḥ: Means "the son" or "the offspring." It signifies lineage and continuity.
    • punāna: Again means "purifying" or "renewing." It emphasizes the theme of rejuvenation.
    • indur: Refers to "the moon" or "the divine light." The moon symbolizes calmness and reflection.
    • varivo: Means "the best" or "the most excellent." It signifies the highest quality or virtue.
    • vidat: Means "knows" or "understands." It indicates wisdom and knowledge.
    • priyam: Refers to "the beloved" or "the dear." It signifies love and affection.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe, highlighting the importance of purification, nourishment, and the divine essence present in our lives. It teaches us that just as the waters cleanse and the moon reflects light, we too must seek renewal and understanding in our daily lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to practices such as meditation, environmental conservation, and nurturing relationships, reminding us to purify our thoughts and actions for a harmonious existence. The significance lies in recognizing the divine in the mundane and striving for a balanced and fulfilling life.
