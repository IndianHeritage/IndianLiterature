इन्द्र वाजेषु नोऽव सहस्रप्रधानेषु च |उग्र उग्राभिरूतिभिः ||
indra vājeṣu no 'va sahasrapradhaneṣu ca | ugra ugrābhir ūtibhiḥ ||
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • Vājeṣu: This term refers to "in the battles" or "in the contests." It signifies the challenges and struggles one faces in life.
  • No: This is a pronoun meaning "us" or "our," indicating a collective appeal or prayer.
  • 'Va: This is a conjunction meaning "or," suggesting an alternative or additional aspect to the prayer.
  • Sahasrapradhaneṣu: This term translates to "in the thousandfold powers" or "in the multitude of strengths." It emphasizes the vastness of resources and capabilities available.
  • Ugra: This word means "fierce" or "powerful," indicating the intensity of the forces at play.
  • Ugrābhir: This is a form of the word "ugra," suggesting multiple fierce or powerful forces.
  • Ūtibhiḥ: This term means "with praises" or "with hymns," indicating the act of worship or reverence through words.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to Indra, asking for his strength and support in overcoming challenges and harnessing the multitude of powers available to us. It acknowledges the fierce nature of the struggles we face and seeks divine assistance in navigating them.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties in personal life, such as health issues or emotional struggles, one can invoke the strength of Indra to find resilience and courage.
  • Professional Struggles: In a competitive work environment, this mantra can serve as a reminder to harness one's strengths and seek support from colleagues or mentors to overcome obstacles.
  • Community Support: In times of crisis, such as natural disasters or social issues, communities can come together, invoking the collective strength symbolized by the mantra to support one another.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine strength and support in the face of life's challenges. It encourages individuals and communities to recognize their inner power and the importance of collective effort. By invoking the fierce and powerful aspects of Indra, one can find the courage to confront adversities and emerge victorious.
