परी त्रिधातुर अध्वरं जूर्णिर एति नवीयसी |मध्वा होतारो अञ्जते ||
pari tridhātur adhvaraṁ jūrṇir eti navīyasī | madhvā hotāro añjate ||
  • Mantra: "pari tridhātur adhvaraṁ jūrṇir eti navīyasī | madhvā hotāro añjate ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "The one who is the threefold support of the universe, the one who is the essence of all offerings, brings forth the new and fresh." The term "adhvara" refers to the sacred ritual or offering, while "jūrṇir" signifies the act of renewal or rejuvenation.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Pari: Around or encompassing.
    • Tridhātur: The threefold support, which can be interpreted as the physical, mental, and spiritual aspects of existence.
    • Adhvaraṁ: The sacred ritual or offering.
    • Jūrṇir: Renewal or rejuvenation.
    • Eti: To come or to arrive.
    • Navīyasī: The new or fresh.
    • Madhvā: Sweetness or essence.
    • Hotāro: The priests or those who perform the rituals.
    • Añjate: To invoke or to call upon.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of rituals in our lives. For instance, during festivals, people perform rituals to invoke blessings, which can be seen as a way to connect with the divine and seek renewal in their lives.
    • The concept of "threefold support" can be related to the balance we seek in our lives—physical health, mental well-being, and spiritual growth. For example, a person practicing yoga embodies this balance, nurturing their body, mind, and spirit.
    • The idea of "new and fresh" can be applied to personal growth. Just as nature rejuvenates with each season, individuals can seek renewal through education, travel, or new experiences that enrich their lives.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for balance, renewal, and the importance of rituals in our lives. It teaches us that by invoking the divine through our actions and offerings, we can achieve a sense of rejuvenation and harmony. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to seek balance in their physical, mental, and spiritual lives, reminding us that every new experience can lead to personal growth and transformation.
