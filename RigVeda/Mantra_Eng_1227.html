याभिः पठर्वा जठरस्य मञ्जनाग्निर नादीदेक चित इद्धो अज्मन्ना |याभिः शर्यात्म अवथो महाधने ताभिर ऊषु ऊतिभिर अश्विनाऽ गतम् ||
yābhiḥ paṭharvā jaṭharasya majmanāgnir nādīdec cita iddho ajmann ā | yābhiḥ śaryātam avatho mahādhane tābhir ū ṣu ūtibhir aśvinā gatam ||
  • yābhiḥ: This word can be interpreted as "by whom" or "through which." It signifies the means or the agents involved in a process.
  • paṭharvā: This term refers to "the one who is learned" or "the wise." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in achieving goals.
  • jaṭharasya: This word means "of the belly" or "of the stomach," symbolizing nourishment and sustenance, both physical and spiritual.
  • majmanā: This can be translated as "of the sacrifice" or "of the offering," indicating the significance of rituals and offerings in spiritual practices.
  • agnir: This word means "fire," which is a central element in Vedic rituals, representing purity, transformation, and the divine.
  • nādīdec: This term can be understood as "the channels" or "the pathways," referring to the flow of energy or consciousness.
  • cita: This word means "consciousness" or "awareness," highlighting the importance of being aware and mindful in our actions.
  • iddho: This term signifies "the one who is awakened" or "the enlightened," representing the state of higher consciousness.
  • ajmann: This can be interpreted as "the soul" or "the self," emphasizing the spiritual aspect of existence.
  • śaryātam: This word means "the best" or "the most excellent," indicating the pursuit of excellence in all endeavors.
  • avatho: This term can be understood as "to protect" or "to support," emphasizing the need for guidance and protection in our journey.
  • mahādhane: This means "great wealth" or "abundance," symbolizing prosperity and the richness of life.
  • tābhir: This word translates to "by them" or "through those," indicating the means through which we achieve our goals.
  • ū: This can be interpreted as "to" or "towards," indicating direction or purpose.
  • ṣu: This term means "good" or "well," emphasizing the importance of positive outcomes.
  • ūtibhir: This word refers to "the actions" or "the deeds," highlighting the significance of our actions in shaping our reality.
  • aśvinā: This term refers to "the Ashvins," the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and well-being, symbolizing divine assistance.
  • gatam: This means "gone" or "achieved," indicating the attainment of goals or the realization of desires.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of knowledge, consciousness, and the divine in our pursuit of excellence and abundance. It highlights the importance of rituals, awareness, and the support of divine forces in achieving our goals. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, spiritual practices, and professional endeavors. By cultivating wisdom, being mindful of our actions, and seeking divine guidance, we can navigate the complexities of life and achieve our aspirations.
