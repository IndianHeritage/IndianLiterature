वास्तोष पते शग्मया संसदā ते सक्षीमहि रण्वया गातुमत्यā |पाहि क्षेम उता योगे वरं नो युयाम पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
vāstoṣ pate śagmayā saṁsadā te sakṣīmahi raṇvayā gātumatyā | pāhi kṣema uta yoge varaṁ no yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • vāstoṣ pate: This phrase can be translated as "O Lord of the House" or "O Guardian of the Dwelling." It signifies a divine presence that protects and oversees our homes and living spaces.
  • śagmayā: This word refers to "with strength" or "with power." It emphasizes the importance of strength in the protection and sanctity of our homes.
  • saṁsadā te: This means "we gather together for you." It indicates a collective effort or community coming together in reverence and unity.
  • sakṣīmahi: Translated as "we invoke" or "we call upon," this word expresses the act of seeking divine assistance or guidance.
  • raṇvayā: This term can be understood as "with joy" or "with happiness," suggesting that the gathering is not just for protection but also for celebration and positivity.
  • gātumatyā: This means "with the ability to sing" or "with the power of song." It highlights the importance of expression, creativity, and communication in our lives.
  • pāhi kṣema: This phrase translates to "protect our well-being." It is a direct plea for safety, health, and prosperity.
  • uta yoge: This means "and in union" or "in harmony." It emphasizes the significance of unity and collaboration in achieving common goals.
  • varaṁ no: This translates to "grant us a boon" or "bestow upon us a blessing." It is a request for divine favor and support.
  • yūyam pāta: This means "may you protect" or "may you safeguard." It is a direct invocation for protection from the divine.
  • svastibhiḥ: This word refers to "well-being" or "auspiciousness." It signifies the desire for a prosperous and healthy life.
  • sadā naḥ: This translates to "always for us" or "forever for us," indicating a longing for continuous protection and blessings.

Conclusion: The mantra "vāstoṣ pate śagmayā saṁsadā te sakṣīmahi raṇvayā gātumatyā | pāhi kṣema uta yoge varaṁ no yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||" is a profound invocation for protection, well-being, and unity. It emphasizes the importance of community, strength, and harmony in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during housewarming ceremonies, family gatherings, or community events, where the collective spirit of protection and positivity is sought. It serves as a reminder that our homes and relationships thrive when they are nurtured with love, strength, and a sense of togetherness. By invoking this mantra, individuals can foster a sense of security and well-being in their lives and the lives of those around them.
