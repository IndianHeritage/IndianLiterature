स हव्यवाह अमर्त्य उषिग दूतश्चनोहितः |अग्निः धियā सम ऋण्वति ||
sa havyavāḻ amartya uśig dūtaś canohitaḥ | agnir dhiyā sam ṛṇvati ||
  • sa havyavāḻ: This phrase can be translated as "he who is worthy of offerings." In the context of the Rigveda, it refers to a divine being or a deity who is deserving of respect and reverence. In real life, this can be likened to individuals or leaders who earn respect through their actions and contributions to society.
  • amartya: This term means "immortal" or "eternal." It signifies the divine nature of the being being addressed. In our lives, we often seek to connect with ideals or values that transcend the temporal, such as love, wisdom, and compassion, which can be seen as 'immortal' qualities.
  • uśig: This word translates to "swift" or "quick." It suggests the idea of speed and efficiency. In a modern context, this can relate to the importance of being proactive and responsive in various aspects of life, such as in business or personal relationships.
  • dūtaś: This means "messenger." In the mantra, it refers to a divine messenger who conveys important messages or truths. In everyday life, we can think of this as the role of teachers, mentors, or even friends who guide us and share valuable insights.
  • canohitaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "who is well-disposed" or "who is favorable." It emphasizes the importance of goodwill and positive intentions. In real life, fostering positive relationships and being supportive of others can lead to a harmonious community.
  • agnir: This word means "fire." In Vedic literature, fire is often associated with purity, transformation, and energy. In our lives, fire can symbolize passion and motivation, driving us to pursue our goals and aspirations.
  • dhiyā: This translates to "with intellect" or "with understanding." It highlights the importance of wisdom and discernment in our actions. In practical terms, making informed decisions based on knowledge and experience is crucial in both personal and professional settings.
  • sam ṛṇvati: This phrase means "to be invoked" or "to be called upon." It suggests the act of seeking the presence or assistance of the divine. In real life, this can be seen in practices such as prayer, meditation, or any form of seeking guidance from a higher power or inner self.

Conclusion: The mantra "sa havyavāḻ amartya uśig dūtaś canohitaḥ | agnir dhiyā sam ṛṇvati ||" encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and wisdom. It emphasizes the importance of respect, immortality of values, swiftness in action, the role of messengers, goodwill, the transformative power of fire, and the necessity of intellect in our endeavors. In real life, this mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate these qualities within ourselves and to seek the divine in our actions and relationships, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
