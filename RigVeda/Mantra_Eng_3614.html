उषो मघोन्य ā वह सूनृत वाऱ्यā पुरु |अस्मभ्यं वाजिनिवति ||
uṣo maghony ā vaha sūnṛte vāryā puru | asmabhyaṁ vājinīvati ||
  • uṣo: This word refers to "dawn" or "the early morning light." In many cultures, dawn symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the dispelling of darkness.
  • maghony: This term can be interpreted as "abundance" or "prosperity." It signifies the wealth of resources and blessings that one can receive.
  • ā vaha: This phrase means "bring forth" or "carry to us." It is a call to the divine to bestow blessings upon the supplicant.
  • sūnṛte: This word translates to "with good intentions" or "in a righteous manner." It emphasizes the importance of purity of thought and action.
  • vāryā: This term can be understood as "powerful" or "capable." It denotes strength and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • puru: This word means "man" or "human." It represents humanity and our collective journey through life.
  • asmabhyaṁ: This translates to "to us" or "for us," indicating a plea or request directed towards the divine.
  • vājinīvati: This term can be interpreted as "to grant us strength" or "to empower us." It is a request for the divine to provide the necessary strength to face life's challenges.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer to the dawn, asking for the blessings of abundance and strength to be bestowed upon humanity. It emphasizes the importance of approaching life with good intentions and righteousness, while also recognizing the need for divine support in our endeavors.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Morning Rituals: Many people start their day with a morning ritual, invoking positivity and strength for the day ahead. This mantra can be recited to set a tone of hope and abundance.
  • Personal Goals: When embarking on new projects or personal goals, one can reflect on the themes of this mantra, ensuring that their intentions are pure and that they seek strength and support from the universe.
  • Community Support: In times of collective struggle, such as during natural disasters or economic hardships, communities can come together to recite this mantra, invoking strength and abundance for all.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of new beginnings, the pursuit of abundance, and the need for divine support in our lives. It encourages individuals to act with good intentions and to seek strength not only for themselves but for the collective good of humanity.
