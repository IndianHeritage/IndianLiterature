त्वं त्यां न इन्द्र देव चित्राम इषम् आपो न पीपयः परिज्मन |यया शूर प्रत्य अस्मभ्यं यंसी तमनं ऊर्जं न विश्वध क्षरध्यै ||
tvaṁ tyāṁ na indra deva citrām iṣam āpo na pīpayaḥ parijman | yayā śūra praty asmabhyaṁ yaṁsi tmanam ūrjaṁ na viśvadha kṣaradhyai ||
  • tvaṁ - You (referring to Indra, the king of gods)
  • tyāṁ - That (indicating the connection to the divine)
  • na - Not
  • indra - Indra (the god of rain and thunderstorms)
  • deva - God
  • citrām - Wonderful or colorful
  • iṣam - Power or strength
  • āpo - Waters
  • na - Not
  • pīpayaḥ - To be thirsty or to desire
  • parijman - Surrounding or encompassing
  • yayā - By which
  • śūra - Brave or heroic
  • praty - Towards or against
  • asmabhyaṁ - Us
  • yaṁsi - You (referring to the divine)
  • tmanam - Self or essence
  • ūrjaṁ - Energy or strength
  • na - Not
  • viśvadha - Everywhere or universally
  • kṣaradhyai - To be perishable or mortal

This mantra invokes Indra, emphasizing his divine power and the strength he provides. It highlights the connection between the divine and the mortal, suggesting that through Indra's blessings, one can attain strength and bravery. The mention of "waters" symbolizes nourishment and life, indicating that the divine presence is essential for sustenance and vitality.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios. For instance, during challenging times, individuals can seek strength and courage from a higher power, much like how warriors would invoke Indra before battle. It serves as a reminder that divine support is available to those who seek it, encouraging people to tap into their inner strength and resilience.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with the divine source of strength and energy. It teaches that by acknowledging and invoking higher powers, one can find the courage to face life's challenges. This mantra can be recited in moments of difficulty, during rituals, or as a daily affirmation to cultivate bravery and resilience in everyday life.
