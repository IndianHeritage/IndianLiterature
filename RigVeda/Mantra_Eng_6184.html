यद अङ्ग तविषीयवो यामं शुभ्रा अचिध्वम् |नि पर्वता अहासत ||
yad aṅga taviṣīyavo yāmaṁ śubhrā acidhvam | ni parvatā ahāsata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • aṅga: body or part
    • taviṣīyavo: powerful or strong
    • yāmaṁ: our time or period
    • śubhrā: pure or auspicious
    • acidhvam: do not be divided or separated
    • ni: down or below
    • parvatā: mountains
    • ahāsata: they shone or radiated
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the unity and strength of the body and spirit.
    • It emphasizes the importance of maintaining purity and auspiciousness in our actions and thoughts.
    • The reference to mountains symbolizes stability and strength, suggesting that we should strive to be grounded and resilient.
    • The phrase "do not be divided" encourages us to remain united in purpose and action, avoiding conflicts and divisions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, whether in a workplace or a sports team, unity and collaboration are essential for success. Just as the mantra suggests, when team members work together harmoniously, they can achieve great results.
    • In personal relationships, maintaining purity of intention and open communication can prevent misunderstandings and conflicts, reflecting the essence of "do not be divided."
    • In community service, coming together for a common cause, like environmental conservation, embodies the strength and auspiciousness mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and purity in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, emphasizing the importance of collaboration and shared goals.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and communities can foster a sense of togetherness and resilience, leading to greater achievements and harmony.
