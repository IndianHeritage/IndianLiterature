अस्मभ्यं तां अपा वृधि व्रजाम् अस्तेव गोमतः |नवाभिर इन्द्रोतिभिः ||
asmabhyaṁ tām̐ apā vṛdhi vrajām̐ asteva gomataḥ | navābhir indrotibhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmabhyaṁ: for us
    • tām̐: that
    • apā: away, or to remove
    • vṛdhi: growth, increase, or prosperity
    • vrajām̐: we go or we approach
    • asteva: indeed, or truly
    • gomataḥ: of the cows, or wealth
    • navābhir: with new or fresh
    • indrotibhiḥ: with the blessings of Indra
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra expresses a prayer for prosperity and growth, invoking the blessings of Indra, the king of gods, and emphasizing the importance of wealth, symbolized by cows in Vedic culture. It suggests a journey towards abundance and well-being, seeking to remove obstacles that hinder growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers may recite this mantra before the planting season, hoping for a bountiful harvest and the removal of any challenges that may affect their crops.
    • In business, entrepreneurs might use this mantra during the launch of a new venture, seeking the blessings of prosperity and success while aiming to overcome initial hurdles.
    • Families may chant this mantra during auspicious occasions, such as housewarming ceremonies, to invite growth and happiness into their new home.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine support for growth and prosperity. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios where individuals or communities seek to enhance their well-being, whether in agriculture, business, or personal life. By reciting this mantra, one aligns themselves with the energies of abundance and divine blessings, fostering a mindset geared towards overcoming obstacles and achieving success.
