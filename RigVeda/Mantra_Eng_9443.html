सहस्रदाः ग्रामणीर्मा ऋषन् मनुः सूर्येणास्य यतमानैतु दक्षिणा |सावर्णेर देवाः प्र तिरन्त्व आयुर यस्मिन्न अश्रान्ता असनाम वाजम् ||
sahasradā grāmaṇīr mā riṣan manuḥ sūryeṇāsya yatamānaitu dakṣiṇā | sāvarṇer devāḥ pra tirantv āyur yasminn aśrāntā asanāma vājam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahasradā: With thousands
    • grāmaṇīr: Of villages or communities
    • mā: Not
    • riṣan: To be disturbed or troubled
    • manuḥ: The human or mankind
    • sūryeṇa: By the sun
    • asya: This
    • yatamānaitu: May strive or work towards
    • dakṣiṇā: The south or auspicious direction
    • sāvarṇer: Of the Sāvarṇa (a name associated with a divine being)
    • devāḥ: The gods
    • pra: Forward or towards
    • tirantv: May they lead or guide
    • āyur: Life or longevity
    • yasminn: In whom
    • aśrāntā: Unwearying or tireless
    • asanāma: Not being disturbed
    • vājam: Wealth or prosperity
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a collective aspiration for peace and prosperity among communities.
    • It emphasizes the importance of striving towards a harmonious existence, free from disturbances.
    • The reference to the sun symbolizes enlightenment, energy, and life, suggesting that through divine guidance, communities can achieve longevity and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied during gatherings or festivals where the focus is on unity and collective well-being.
    • In workplaces, it can serve as a reminder for teamwork and collaboration, ensuring that all members work towards common goals without conflict.
    • In personal life, individuals can recite this mantra to seek peace and prosperity in their relationships and endeavors.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, peace, and divine guidance in achieving a prosperous life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from community gatherings to personal aspirations, reminding us of the importance of collective effort and harmony.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals and communities can strive for a life filled with abundance, free from disturbances, and guided by higher principles.
