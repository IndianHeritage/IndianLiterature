अराधि होतā निषदā यजीयān अभि प्रयांसी सुदितानि हि ख्यात् | यजामहि यज्ञियān हन्त देवām̐ īळामहā īḍ्यām̐ āज्येन ||
arādhi hotā niṣadā yajīyān abhi prayāṁsi sudhitāni hi khyat | yajāmahai yajñiyān hanta devām̐ īḻāmahā īḍyām̐ ājyena ||
  • arādhi hotā: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is worshipped." In real life, this can relate to how individuals seek guidance or blessings from higher powers or deities in various cultures, such as praying before an important event or decision.
  • niṣadā: This term means "steadfast" or "firm." It signifies the importance of being resolute in one's beliefs and actions. For example, a person who stands firm in their values during challenging times exemplifies this quality.
  • yajīyān: This word refers to "the one who performs sacrifices" or "the one who offers." In modern contexts, this can be likened to individuals who contribute to their communities or engage in charitable acts, reflecting the spirit of giving and selflessness.
  • abhi prayāṁsi: This phrase translates to "towards the offerings" or "to the sacrifices." It emphasizes the importance of intention behind actions. For instance, when someone volunteers their time for a cause, their intention to help others is a form of offering.
  • sudhitāni: This means "well-thought-out" or "wise." It highlights the value of wisdom in decision-making. In real life, individuals often seek advice from mentors or wise figures before making significant life choices, demonstrating the importance of thoughtful consideration.
  • hi khyat: This can be interpreted as "indeed known" or "well-recognized." It suggests that the actions of the devoted are acknowledged. For example, community leaders who are recognized for their contributions often inspire others to follow suit.
  • yajāmahai: This term means "we perform sacrifices" or "we worship." It signifies collective action and community involvement. In real life, this can be seen in group activities such as community service or collective prayers, where people come together for a common purpose.
  • yajñiyān: This refers to "the divine beings" or "the deities of sacrifice." It emphasizes the connection between humans and the divine. In many cultures, rituals and ceremonies are performed to honor these divine beings, reflecting a deep-rooted belief in the spiritual realm.
  • hanta devām̐: This phrase translates to "O deities!" It is an invocation to the divine. In real life, this can be seen in various religious practices where followers call upon their deities for blessings, guidance, or support during difficult times.
  • īḻāmahā: This term can be interpreted as "greatness" or "majesty." It signifies the reverence held for the divine. For example, in many cultures, places of worship are adorned with symbols of greatness to honor the divine presence.
  • īḍyām̐: This means "worthy of worship" or "adorable." It reflects the qualities that make the divine deserving of reverence. In real life, individuals often express admiration for leaders or figures who embody virtues such as compassion and integrity.
  • ājyena: This term refers to "with ghee" or "with offerings." It symbolizes the act of giving and the importance of offerings in rituals. In modern contexts, this can relate to the idea of sharing resources or helping those in need, akin to making offerings to the community.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of devotion, community, and the importance of wise actions in our lives. It encourages individuals to remain steadfast in their beliefs, perform acts of kindness, and recognize the divine in their everyday actions. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as community service, personal growth, and spiritual practices, reminding us of the interconnectedness of our actions and the greater good. By embodying these principles, we can foster a sense of unity and purpose in our lives and communities.
