सूक्तवाकम् प्रथमम् आद इद अग्निम आद इद धविर जनयन्त देवाः |स एषां यज्ञो अभवत तनूपास तं द्यौर वेद तम पृथिवी तम आपः ||
sūktavākam prathamam ād id agnim ād id dhavir ajanayanta devāḥ | sa eṣāṁ yajño abhavat tanūpās taṁ dyaur veda tam pṛthivī tam āpaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sūktavākam: The one who speaks well or auspiciously.
    • prathamam: First or foremost.
    • ād: To or towards.
    • id: This or here.
    • agnim: Fire, representing energy and transformation.
    • dhavir: The powerful or the strong.
    • ajanayanta: They created or brought forth.
    • devāḥ: The gods or divine beings.
    • sa: He or that.
    • eṣāṁ: Of these or of them.
    • yajño: Sacrifice or worship.
    • abhavat: Became or was.
    • tanūpās: The worship of the body or physical form.
    • taṁ: That.
    • dyaur: The sky or heaven.
    • veda: Knows or is aware.
    • tam: That.
    • pṛthivī: The earth.
    • tam: That.
    • āpaḥ: The waters.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the creation of fire (Agni) by the gods, emphasizing the importance of fire in rituals and life.
    • It highlights the connection between the divine (gods) and the physical world (earth, sky, and waters), suggesting that all elements are interlinked.
    • The act of worship (yajña) is portrayed as a fundamental aspect of existence, where the divine and the material world come together.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is a symbol of transformation and purification. For instance, in Hindu rituals, the sacred fire (Agni) is central to ceremonies like weddings, symbolizing the divine presence and the transformation of the couple's lives.
    • The interconnectedness of nature can be seen in environmental movements that emphasize the importance of preserving the earth (pṛthivī) and water (āpaḥ) for future generations, reflecting the mantra's call for respect towards all elements.
    • In daily life, the concept of sacrifice (yajña) can be applied to acts of kindness and service, where individuals contribute to the well-being of their community, echoing the divine principles of cooperation and harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of fire as a transformative force and the role of divine beings in creation and sustenance.
    • It teaches us to recognize the interconnectedness of all elements of life and the significance of worship and sacrifice in maintaining harmony.
    • In real life, it encourages us to engage in practices that honor nature, promote community well-being, and recognize the divine in our everyday actions.
