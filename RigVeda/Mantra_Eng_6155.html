त्वां इच्छवसस पते कण्वा उक्थेन वावृधुः |त्वां सुतासा इन्दवः ||
tvām ic chavasas pate kaṇvā ukthena vāvṛdhuḥ | tvāṁ sutāsa indavaḥ ||
  • tvām - This word translates to "you" or "to you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • ic - This means "wish" or "desire," expressing a longing or intention.
  • chavasas - This term refers to "strength" or "vigor," often associated with vitality and energy.
  • pate - This translates to "lord" or "master," indicating reverence towards the addressed entity.
  • kaṇvā - This word can mean "the wise" or "the learned," suggesting a connection to knowledge and wisdom.
  • ukthena - This means "with a hymn" or "with a prayer," indicating the act of invoking or praising.
  • vāvṛdhuḥ - This translates to "may they increase" or "may they grow," expressing a desire for abundance or prosperity.
  • tvāṁ - Reiterating "you," emphasizing the focus on the divine entity.
  • sutāsa - This means "sons" or "offspring," often referring to the blessings of progeny or followers.
  • indavaḥ - This translates to "the Indus" or "the divine," often associated with divine blessings or celestial beings.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation directed towards a divine entity, expressing a desire for strength, wisdom, and prosperity. The speaker wishes for the blessings of the divine to increase, particularly in the context of offspring or followers, suggesting a longing for growth and abundance in life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals may recite this mantra when seeking strength and wisdom in their personal or professional lives, aiming for self-improvement and success.
  • Family and Relationships: The desire for blessings on offspring can resonate with parents wishing for the well-being and prosperity of their children.
  • Community and Society: Leaders or community members may use this mantra to invoke collective strength and wisdom for the betterment of society, fostering unity and growth.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine strength and wisdom, emphasizing the importance of growth and prosperity in various aspects of life. It serves as a reminder to seek blessings not only for oneself but also for one's family and community, highlighting the interconnectedness of individual and collective well-being.
