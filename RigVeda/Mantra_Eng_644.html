त्वं दिवो बृहतः सानु कोपयोऽव तमनā धृतता शम्बरं भिनत् |यन् मायिनो व्रन्दिनो मन्दिनā धृतश्चितां गभस्तिम अशनिम पृथन्यसि ||
tvaṁ divo bṛhataḥ sānu kopayo 'va tmanā dhṛṣatā śambaram bhinat | yan māyino vrandino mandinā dhṛṣac chitāṁ gabhastim aśanim pṛtanyasi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • divo: of the heavens
    • bṛhataḥ: great
    • sānu: with strength
    • kopayo: with anger
    • 'va: or
    • tmanā: with the self
    • dhṛṣatā: with courage
    • śambaram: the auspicious
    • bhinat: breaking
    • yan: that
    • māyino: of the illusionist
    • vrandino: of the group
    • mandinā: with wisdom
    • dhṛṣac: with courage
    • chitāṁ: the consciousness
    • gabhastim: the light
    • aśanim: the thunderbolt
    • pṛtanyasi: you are manifesting
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine presence that embodies strength and courage.
    • It emphasizes the importance of breaking through illusions and ignorance with wisdom and consciousness.
    • The reference to "thunderbolt" symbolizes power and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In moments of personal crisis, individuals often draw upon their inner strength to confront challenges, akin to the courage mentioned in the mantra.
    • In leadership, breaking through illusions and guiding a team with wisdom can lead to successful outcomes, reflecting the essence of the mantra.
    • In education, enlightening students with knowledge can be seen as manifesting the "light" and "thunderbolt" of understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of inner strength, wisdom, and the ability to confront and break through illusions.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal growth, leadership, and education, where courage and clarity are essential for overcoming challenges.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can navigate life's complexities with greater resilience and insight.
