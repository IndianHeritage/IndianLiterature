यच चक्रासि परावती यद अर्वावति वृत्रहन् |अतस् त्वा गीरभिर द्युगद इन्द्र केशिभिः सुतावाम् ā विवासति ||
yac chakrāsi parāvati yad arvāvati vṛtrahan | atas tvā gīrbhir dyugad indra keśibhiḥ sutāvām̐ ā vivāsati ||
  • yac chakrāsi parāvati: This phrase can be translated as "Whatever you have created, O Parāvati." Here, "Parāvati" refers to the supreme power or the divine force that manifests in various forms. In real life, this can be seen as the creative energy present in nature, which brings forth life and sustains it.
  • yad arvāvati vṛtrahan: This translates to "Whatever you have made to conquer the obstacles." "Vṛtrahan" refers to the slayer of Vritra, a demon representing chaos and obstacles. This can be related to personal challenges we face in life, such as overcoming fears or adversities. For example, a student preparing for exams must conquer the "Vritra" of anxiety and self-doubt.
  • atas tvā gīrbhir dyugad indra: This means "Therefore, we invoke you with hymns, O Indra." Indra is often seen as the king of gods and a symbol of strength and power. In our lives, we can invoke strength through positive affirmations and support from our community. For instance, during tough times, people often turn to motivational speeches or uplifting music to gather strength.
  • keśibhiḥ sutāvām̐ ā vivāsati: This translates to "May you be pleased with our offerings." This signifies the importance of gratitude and respect towards the divine or the forces that guide us. In real life, this can be reflected in practices such as giving thanks for the good things in life, whether through prayer, meditation, or simply acknowledging the support of friends and family.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing the divine forces in our lives that help us create, overcome obstacles, and find strength. It teaches us the importance of gratitude and the power of invoking positivity through our words and actions. In real life, this can be applied by acknowledging our challenges, seeking strength through community and self-affirmation, and expressing gratitude for the support we receive. This mantra serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our struggles and that there is a greater force that aids us in our journey.
