तुभ्येद इन्द्र मरुत्वते सुताः सोमासो अद्रिवः |हृदा हूयन्त उक्तिनः ||
tubhyed indra marutvate sutāḥ somāso adrivaḥ | hṛdā hūyanta ukthinaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tubhyed: To you (Indra)
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • marutvate: O Maruts (the storm deities)
    • sutāḥ: Sons or offspring (often referring to the offerings)
    • somāso: Of the Soma (a sacred drink)
    • adrivaḥ: From the mountains or hills
    • hṛdā: With the heart or with devotion
    • hūyanta: Invoked or called upon
    • ukthinaḥ: Those who recite hymns or praises
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a call to Indra and the Maruts, inviting them to partake in the offerings made with devotion. It emphasizes the importance of heartfelt invocation and the connection between the divine and the offerings made from the mountains, symbolizing the natural world and its bounty.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Invoking Blessings: Just as people today may pray or perform rituals to seek blessings for health, prosperity, or success, this mantra reflects a similar practice of invoking divine presence for support and guidance.
    • Connection to Nature: The reference to mountains signifies the importance of nature in our lives. For instance, many cultures celebrate harvest festivals, acknowledging the earth's gifts, much like the offerings made in the mantra.
    • Community Gatherings: In modern times, communal prayers or gatherings to celebrate significant events (like weddings or festivals) echo the collective invocation of divine blessings as seen in this mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on devotion, community, and the relationship between humans and the divine. It serves as a reminder of the importance of heartfelt offerings and the interconnectedness of nature and spirituality. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to come together in gratitude and reverence, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in their spiritual practices.
