शग्धि पूर्धि प्र यंसी च शिशीहि प्रास्य उदरम् |पुषन्न इह क्रतुं विदः ||
śagdhi pūrdhi pra yaṁsi ca śiśīhi prāsy udaram | pūṣann iha kratuṁ vidaḥ ||
  • śagdhi: This word can be interpreted as "to unite" or "to bring together." In a real-life context, this can refer to the importance of collaboration and teamwork in achieving common goals, such as in a workplace where diverse skills come together to complete a project.
  • pūrdhi: This term means "fullness" or "completeness." It signifies the idea of abundance and fulfillment. For example, in personal relationships, achieving emotional fullness can lead to stronger bonds and a more satisfying life.
  • pra yaṁsi: This phrase can be understood as "to move forward" or "to progress." In everyday life, this can relate to personal growth, such as pursuing education or career advancements that help individuals move towards their aspirations.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," indicating the connection between the ideas presented in the mantra.
  • śiśīhi: This word translates to "to protect" or "to nurture." In a practical sense, this can be seen in parenting, where caregivers protect and nurture their children to ensure their well-being and development.
  • prāsy: This term can mean "to open" or "to reveal." In a real-life scenario, this could refer to the process of opening oneself to new experiences or knowledge, such as exploring different cultures or ideas that broaden one’s perspective.
  • udaram: This word means "the belly" or "the womb," symbolizing nourishment and the source of life. In a broader context, it can represent the earth or nature, emphasizing the importance of environmental sustainability and caring for our planet.
  • pūṣann: This term refers to "the nourisher" or "the provider." In real life, this can be related to the role of community support systems that provide resources and assistance to those in need, fostering a sense of belonging and care.
  • iha: This word means "here" or "in this place," grounding the mantra in the present moment and emphasizing the importance of being mindful and aware of our current circumstances.
  • kratuṁ: This term signifies "intention" or "willpower." In practical terms, it can relate to setting goals and having the determination to achieve them, such as in personal development or professional endeavors.
  • vidaḥ: This word means "to know" or "to understand." It highlights the importance of knowledge and wisdom in making informed decisions in life, such as in education or critical thinking.

Conclusion: The mantra "śagdhi pūrdhi pra yaṁsi ca śiśīhi prāsy udaram | pūṣann iha kratuṁ vidaḥ ||" encapsulates the essence of unity, fulfillment, progress, protection, revelation, nourishment, and knowledge. Its significance lies in the interconnectedness of these concepts, which are essential for personal and communal growth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to collaborate, nurture relationships, pursue knowledge, and protect the environment, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling existence. By embodying these principles, we can create a supportive and thriving community that benefits all its members.
