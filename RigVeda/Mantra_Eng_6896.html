उत त्वा धीतयो मम गिरी वर्धन्तु विश्वहा |अग्नि सख्यस्य बोधि नः ||
uta tvā dhītayo mama giro vardhantu viśvahā | agne sakhyasya bodhi naḥ ||
  • Mantra: "uta tvā dhītayo mama giro vardhantu viśvahā | agne sakhyasya bodhi naḥ ||"
  • Translation: "May my thoughts and words be enriched by you, O Agni, and may you grant us the wisdom of friendship."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • uta: a particle that can mean "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition.
    • tvā: "you," referring to Agni, the fire god, who symbolizes energy, transformation, and communication.
    • dhītayo: "thoughts" or "intellects," representing the mental faculties and wisdom.
    • mama: "my," indicating possession or personal connection.
    • giro: "words" or "speech," emphasizing the importance of communication.
    • vardhantu: "may they be enriched" or "may they grow," a wish for enhancement and growth.
    • viśvahā: "all" or "universal," suggesting inclusivity and the collective aspect of wisdom.
    • agne: "O Agni," a direct address to the deity, invoking his presence and blessings.
    • sakhyasya: "of friendship," highlighting the value of companionship and support.
    • bodhi: "wisdom" or "enlightenment," referring to the understanding and insight gained through experience.
    • naḥ: "to us," indicating a collective request for blessings.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, this mantra can be applied when individuals come together to collaborate on a project. By invoking the spirit of Agni, team members can seek to enhance their communication and understanding, leading to a more productive and harmonious work environment.
    • In personal relationships, whether friendships or family ties, the essence of this mantra encourages individuals to enrich their conversations and thoughts, fostering deeper connections and mutual support.
    • In educational settings, students can use this mantra to inspire their learning process, seeking wisdom and clarity in their studies while promoting a spirit of camaraderie among peers.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for the enhancement of thoughts and words through the divine influence of Agni. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and friendship in all aspects of life. By applying this mantra in real-life scenarios, individuals can cultivate better communication, foster deeper relationships, and create a supportive environment that encourages growth and understanding. This mantra serves as a reminder that our thoughts and words have the power to shape our experiences and connections with others.
