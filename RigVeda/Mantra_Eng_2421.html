विष्वे देवास आ गत श्रिणुत मा इमं हवम् |एदं बर्हिर न शीदत ||
viśve devāsa ā gata śṛṇutā ma imaṁ havam | edam barhir ni ṣīdata ||
  • Translation: "O all gods, come hither; listen to this invocation of mine. This offering is spread out."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • viśve: All, universal, encompassing everything.
    • devāsa: Gods or divine beings.
    • ā gata: Come hither or approach.
    • śṛṇutā: Listen or pay attention.
    • ma: My or mine, indicating possession.
    • imaṁ: This, referring to the invocation or offering.
    • havam: Invocation or prayer, often used in the context of offerings to deities.
    • edam: This, again referring to the offering.
    • barhir: Spread out or laid out, often referring to the sacred grass used in rituals.
    • ni ṣīdata: Sit down or settle, indicating a place for the gods to receive the offering.
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra is a call to all divine beings, inviting them to partake in the offerings made by the devotee.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and inclusivity in spiritual practices, acknowledging the presence of all gods.
    • The act of spreading out the offering signifies preparation and respect for the divine, creating a sacred space for the ritual.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern spiritual practices, this mantra can be used during rituals or ceremonies where one seeks the blessings of multiple deities or forces.
    • It can serve as a reminder to be inclusive in our prayers and intentions, recognizing the interconnectedness of all spiritual paths.
    • In daily life, the essence of this mantra can be applied by fostering a sense of community, inviting diverse perspectives, and creating a welcoming environment for all.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, respect, and inclusivity in spiritual practices.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the divine presence in all aspects of life, encouraging individuals to seek harmony and connection with the universe.
    • By invoking the presence of all gods, it serves as a powerful reminder of the unity and diversity of spiritual beliefs, applicable in both ritualistic and everyday contexts.
