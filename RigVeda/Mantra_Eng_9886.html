प्रति ब्रवाणि वर्तयते अश्रु चक्रं न क्रन्दद आध्ये शिवायै |प्र तत्ते हिनवाय यत्ते अस्मे परेह्य अस्तं नहि मूषा मापः ||
prati bravāṇi vartayate aśru cakran na krandad ādhye śivāyai | pra tat te hinavā yat te asme parehy astaṁ nahi mūra māpaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prati: towards, in response
    • bravāṇi: I speak, I declare
    • vartayate: it moves, it flows
    • aśru: tears
    • cakran: circle, cycle
    • na: not
    • krandad: cried out, wept
    • ādhye: in the midst
    • śivāyai: to the auspicious one, to Shiva
    • pra: forth, towards
    • tat: that
    • te: your
    • hinavā: I offer, I give
    • yat: which
    • asme: to us
    • parehy: in the higher realms
    • astaṁ: to the end, to the conclusion
    • nahi: not
    • mūra: foolish, ignorant
    • māpaḥ: do not take away
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a dialogue with the divine, where the speaker expresses a flow of emotions, particularly tears, which symbolize deep feelings and devotion.
    • The mention of "śivāyai" indicates a connection to Lord Shiva, representing auspiciousness and transformation.
    • The phrase "na krandad ādhye" suggests that in the midst of sorrow or emotional turmoil, one should not cry out in despair but rather seek solace in the divine.
    • The speaker offers their emotions and experiences to the divine, seeking guidance and support in navigating life's challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of grief, individuals often turn to prayer or meditation, similar to the act of speaking to the divine in this mantra. For instance, someone who has lost a loved one may find comfort in reciting prayers or mantras, channeling their emotions towards healing.
    • People facing difficult decisions may reflect on their experiences and seek divine guidance, much like the offering of emotions in the mantra. This can be seen in practices such as journaling or seeking counsel from spiritual leaders.
    • In moments of stress, individuals might engage in mindfulness or yoga, connecting with their inner selves and the divine, echoing the essence of this mantra's call for emotional expression and divine connection.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of expressing emotions and seeking divine support during challenging times. It teaches that while sorrow is a natural part of life, turning towards the divine can provide comfort and guidance.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various situations, such as during personal loss, emotional struggles, or when facing difficult choices. It encourages individuals to embrace their feelings and seek a higher power for strength and clarity.
    • Ultimately, the significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that we are not alone in our struggles; the divine is always there to listen and guide us through the cycles of life.
