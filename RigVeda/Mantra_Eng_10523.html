अपश्यं त्वा मनसा चेकितानं तपसो जातं तपसो विभूतम् |इह प्रजाम इह रयिं रराणः प्र जायत्स्व प्रजया पुत्रकाम ||
apaśyaṁ tvā manasā cekitānaṁ tapaso jātaṁ tapaso vibhūtam | iha prajām iha rayiṁ rarāṇaḥ pra jāyasva prajayā putrakāma ||
  • apaśyaṁ tvā manasā cekitānaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "I have not seen you with my mind." It signifies the idea of seeking a deeper understanding or connection beyond mere physical perception. In real life, this can relate to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, where one must look beyond surface appearances to grasp the true essence of things.
  • tapaso jātaṁ tapaso vibhūtam: This translates to "born of austerity, the glory of austerity." It emphasizes the importance of discipline and self-control in achieving greatness. For example, athletes often undergo rigorous training (austerity) to achieve their goals, showcasing how dedication can lead to success.
  • iha prajām iha rayiṁ rarāṇaḥ: This means "here, let us obtain progeny and wealth." It reflects the desire for prosperity and the continuation of lineage. In contemporary society, this can be seen in the aspirations of families to provide for their children and ensure a better future, highlighting the importance of both material and spiritual wealth.
  • pra jāyasva prajayā putrakāma: This translates to "may you be born with progeny, desiring sons." It expresses a wish for fertility and the continuation of family lineage. In many cultures, having children is seen as a blessing and a way to carry on traditions and values, illustrating the universal human desire for legacy.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking deeper understanding, the value of discipline, and the aspirations for prosperity and legacy. Its significance lies in the recognition that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses knowledge, family, and spiritual fulfillment. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with dedication, seek meaningful connections, and cherish the importance of family and community in their lives.
