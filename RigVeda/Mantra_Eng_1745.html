अनच छये तुरगातु जीवम् एजद ध्रुवम् मध्य ā पस्त्यानाम् |जीवो मृतस्य चरति स्वधाभिर अमर्त्यो मार्त्येन सयोनिः ||
anac chaye turagātu jīvam ejad dhruvam madhya ā pastyānām | jīvo mṛtasya carati svadhābhir amartyo martyenā sayoniḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • anac: without, not
    • chaye: shadow
    • turagātu: horse-like, swift
    • jīvam: living being, life
    • ejad: moves, stirs
    • dhruvam: stable, firm
    • madhya: in the middle
    • ā: towards
    • pastyānām: of the past
    • jīvo: the living being
    • mṛtasya: of the dead
    • carati: moves, wanders
    • svadhābhir: by its own nature
    • amartyo: immortal
    • martyenā: by the mortal
    • sayoniḥ: womb, source
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the essence of life and the interplay between the mortal and the immortal.
    • The phrase "anac chaye" suggests a state of existence that is free from shadows or negativity, indicating a pure form of life.
    • "Turagātu" symbolizes swiftness and agility, akin to a horse, representing the dynamic nature of life.
    • The term "jīvam ejad dhruvam" emphasizes that life is both active and stable, suggesting that while life is constantly moving, it also has a firm foundation.
    • "Madhya ā pastyānām" indicates a central position in the continuum of existence, bridging the past and the present.
    • The mantra further reflects on the relationship between the living and the dead, where "jīvo mṛtasya carati" implies that the living being moves in the realm of the deceased, suggesting a cycle of life and death.
    • "Svadhābhir amartyo" points to the inherent nature of immortality within the living, while "martyenā sayoniḥ" acknowledges the mortal origin of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the context of personal growth, individuals often experience phases of life that feel shadowed by past experiences. The mantra encourages moving beyond these shadows to embrace a more vibrant existence.
    • In the realm of sports, athletes embody the "turagātu" quality, demonstrating agility and speed, while also maintaining a strong foundation in their training and discipline.
    • The concept of life being a continuum can be seen in family traditions, where the values and lessons from past generations influence the present and future.
    • In the field of psychology, the idea of integrating past experiences (both positive and negative) into one's identity reflects the mantra's theme of living in the middle of the past and present.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of life as a dynamic interplay between the mortal and the immortal, urging individuals to transcend their past and embrace their inherent vitality.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that life is a journey that involves both movement and stability, encouraging a balance between action and reflection.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek personal growth, embrace their heritage, and recognize the interconnectedness of all life experiences.
