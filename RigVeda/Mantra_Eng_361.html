त्वां अग्ने प्रथमम् आयुम् आयवे देवाः अकृण्वन् नहुषस्य विश्पतिम् |इळाम् अकृण्वन् मनुषस्य शासनीम् पितुः यत् पुत्रो ममकस्य जायते ||
tvām agne prathamam āyum āyave devā akṛṇvan nahuṣasya viśpatim | iḻām akṛṇvan manuṣasya śāsanīm pitur yat putro mamakasya jāyate ||
  • tvām agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni," referring to the Vedic fire god, Agni, who symbolizes energy, light, and the divine messenger.
  • prathamam āyum: This means "the first life" or "the first age," indicating the importance of the initial stages of life and existence.
  • āyave devā: This translates to "the gods who grant longevity," emphasizing the divine aspect of life and the blessings of the deities.
  • akṛṇvan nahuṣasya viśpatim: This can be interpreted as "who create the lordship of Nahusha," referring to a mythological king known for his wisdom and power, symbolizing the ideal ruler.
  • iḻām akṛṇvan: This means "who create the wealth," indicating the importance of prosperity and abundance in life.
  • manuṣasya śāsanīm: This translates to "the command of humanity," highlighting the role of human governance and moral authority.
  • pitur yat putro mamakasya jāyate: This means "that which is born as my son from my father," emphasizing the continuity of life and the familial bond.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of life, divine blessings, and the importance of governance and prosperity. It signifies the interconnectedness of divine energy (Agni), the blessings of longevity, and the moral responsibilities of humanity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to family values, the importance of leadership, and the pursuit of prosperity while remaining grounded in ethical principles. It serves as a reminder to honor our roots, seek divine guidance, and strive for a life filled with purpose and abundance.
