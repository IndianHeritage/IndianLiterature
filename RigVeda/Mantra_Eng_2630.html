अग्नि दिवो अर्णम् अच्छा जिगास्य अच्छा देवां उचिषे धिष्ण्या ये |या रोचने परस्तात सूर्यस्य याश्च आवस्ताद उपतिष्ठन्त आपः ||
agne divo arṇam acchā jigāsy acchā devām̐ ūciṣe dhiṣṇyā ye | yā rocane parastāt sūryasya yāś cāvastād upatiṣṭhanta āpaḥ ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god, who is a central figure in Vedic rituals and symbolizes energy, light, and purity.
  • Divo: Meaning "of the heavens," it signifies the divine or celestial realm.
  • Arṇam: This term can be interpreted as "wealth" or "prosperity," often associated with the abundance that fire brings.
  • Acchā: This word means "good" or "auspicious," indicating positive qualities or outcomes.
  • Jigāsy: This translates to "may you inspire" or "may you awaken," suggesting a call for divine intervention or motivation.
  • Devām̐: Referring to the "gods," it emphasizes the connection to the divine and the importance of seeking blessings from higher powers.
  • Ūciṣe: This means "to speak" or "to call upon," indicating a plea or invocation.
  • Dhiṣṇyā: This term refers to "the place of worship" or "sacred space," highlighting the importance of setting in spiritual practices.
  • Ye: Meaning "those who," it points to the subjects of the mantra, the divine beings being addressed.
  • Yā: This word means "who" or "that," referring to the qualities or attributes of the divine.
  • Rocane: This translates to "in brightness" or "in radiance," symbolizing enlightenment and clarity.
  • Parastāt: Meaning "beyond," it suggests a transcendental aspect, looking beyond the material world.
  • Sūryasya: Referring to "the sun," it symbolizes life, energy, and the source of illumination.
  • Yāś: This means "glory" or "fame," indicating the importance of recognition and honor.
  • Cāvastād: This translates to "that which is established," suggesting stability and permanence.
  • Upatiṣṭhanta: Meaning "to stand up" or "to rise," it indicates action and the pursuit of goals.
  • Āpaḥ: Referring to "waters," it symbolizes nourishment, life, and the flow of energy.

Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation that seeks the blessings of Agni and the divine forces associated with prosperity, enlightenment, and stability. It emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energy in our lives, encouraging us to seek inspiration and guidance from higher powers. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in rituals, personal meditations, or during significant life events where one seeks clarity, abundance, and divine support. By recognizing the interconnectedness of fire, water, and celestial energies, individuals can cultivate a deeper understanding of their spiritual journey and the importance of maintaining a harmonious relationship with the universe.
