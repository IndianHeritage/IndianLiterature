त्वं नो अग्ने अद्भुत क्रत्वा दाक्षस्य मंहनाः |त्वे आसुर्यम् आरुहत क्राणाः मित्रो न यज्ञियः ||
tvaṁ no agne adbhuta kratvā dakṣasya maṁhanā | tve asurya1m āruhat krāṇā mitro na yajñiyaḥ ||
  • tvaṁ no agne: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni." Agni is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine messenger. In real life, this can be likened to the role of fire in our daily lives, such as cooking, warmth, and as a source of light.
  • adbhuta kratvā: This means "with wondrous power." It signifies the extraordinary capabilities of Agni. In a modern context, this can be related to the power of innovation and creativity in human endeavors, such as technological advancements that transform our lives.
  • dakṣasya maṁhanā: This translates to "the strength of the skilled." It emphasizes the importance of skill and expertise. In real life, this can be seen in various professions where mastery and skill lead to success, such as in sports, arts, or sciences.
  • tve asurya1m āruhat: This means "you who rise like the sun." The sun is a symbol of life, energy, and enlightenment. In everyday life, this can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, akin to how the sun illuminates the world.
  • krāṇā mitro na yajñiyaḥ: This translates to "may you, O friend, be invoked in our sacrifices." It highlights the importance of community and collaboration in achieving goals. In real life, this can be seen in teamwork and collective efforts in various fields, such as business projects or community service.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of divine energy (Agni) and its transformative power in our lives. It calls for the invocation of this energy to enhance our skills, foster collaboration, and seek enlightenment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to harness their inner potential, work together with others, and strive for knowledge and innovation, ultimately leading to personal and communal growth.
