अङ्गाद-अङ्गाल लोम्नो-लोम्नो जातं पर्वणि-पर्वणि | यक्ष्मं सर्वस्माद आत्मनस तं इदं वि वृहामि ते ||
aṅgād-aṅgāl lomno-lomno jātam parvaṇi-parvaṇi | yakṣmaṁ sarvasmād ātmanas tam idaṁ vi vṛhāmi te ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aṅgād-aṅgāl: From the limbs, or parts of the body.
    • lomno-lomno: From the hair, or the surface of the body.
    • jātam: Born or created.
    • parvaṇi-parvaṇi: From the joints or connections.
    • yakṣmaṁ: Illness or disease.
    • sarvasmād: From all.
    • ātmanas: Of the self or soul.
    • tam: That.
    • idaṁ: This.
    • vi vṛhāmi: I invoke or I call upon.
    • te: For you.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for health and well-being, invoking the divine to protect the individual from all forms of illness.
    • It emphasizes the interconnectedness of the body, suggesting that health is derived from the harmony of all its parts—limbs, hair, and joints.
    • The repetition of terms like "aṅgād" and "lomno" signifies the holistic approach to health, recognizing that every part of the body contributes to overall wellness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern medicine, the concept of holistic health is gaining traction, where practitioners consider the physical, emotional, and spiritual aspects of a person.
    • Yoga and meditation practices often emphasize the importance of balance and harmony within the body, aligning with the mantra's message.
    • Nutritionists advocate for a balanced diet that nourishes all parts of the body, reflecting the idea that health arises from the well-being of every individual component.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of maintaining health and well-being through a holistic approach.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as healthcare, wellness practices, and personal health management.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a deeper awareness of their bodies and the need for balance, ultimately leading to a healthier life.
