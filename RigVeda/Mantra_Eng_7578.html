त्रिः षष्टिस् त्वा मरुतो वावृधाना उष्रा इव राशयो यज्ञियासः |उप त्वेमः कृधि नो भागधेयं शुष्मं त एनाः हविषा विद्हेम ||
triḥ ṣaṣṭis tvā maruto vāvṛdhānā usrā iva rāśayo yajñiyāsaḥ | upa tvemaḥ kṛdhi no bhāgadheyaṁ śuṣmaṁ ta enā haviṣā vidhema ||
  • Mantra: "triḥ ṣaṣṭis tvā maruto vāvṛdhānā usrā iva rāśayo yajñiyāsaḥ | upa tvemaḥ kṛdhi no bhāgadheyaṁ śuṣmaṁ ta enā haviṣā vidhema ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • triḥ: three
    • ṣaṣṭis: sixty
    • tvā: you
    • maruto: O Maruts (the storm deities)
    • vāvṛdhānā: who are increasing
    • usrā: like the dawns
    • iva: like
    • rāśayo: the groups
    • yajñiyāsaḥ: of those who perform sacrifices
    • upa: towards
    • tvemaḥ: this
    • kṛdhi: do
    • no: for us
    • bhāgadheyaṁ: the share of fortune
    • śuṣmaṁ: the subtle
    • ta: that
    • enā: by this
    • haviṣā: with the offering
    • vidhema: we may attain
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Maruts, the storm deities, who are associated with strength, vitality, and the forces of nature.
    • The phrase "triḥ ṣaṣṭis" suggests a powerful invocation, possibly referring to the 63 aspects of divine energy that the Maruts embody.
    • By likening the Maruts to "usrā iva rāśayo," it emphasizes their role in bringing forth light and energy, similar to the dawn breaking over the horizon.
    • The mantra calls upon the Maruts to bestow their blessings and fortune upon the devotees, indicating a desire for prosperity and success in endeavors.
    • The concluding lines express a wish to attain subtle and profound blessings through offerings, highlighting the importance of rituals and sacrifices in Vedic tradition.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, invoking the Maruts can be seen as a way to seek favorable weather conditions for crops, reflecting the connection between nature and sustenance.
    • During festivals, rituals that honor the Maruts can symbolize the community's collective hope for prosperity and well-being, showcasing the importance of unity in seeking divine favor.
    • In personal endeavors, individuals may recite this mantra to seek guidance and strength in overcoming challenges, akin to drawing inspiration from the forces of nature.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine blessings in achieving success and prosperity in life.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of nature, spirituality, and human endeavors, reminding us to seek harmony with the forces around us.
    • In contemporary life, the principles of this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community gatherings, personal aspirations, and environmental consciousness, emphasizing the need for respect and gratitude towards nature.
