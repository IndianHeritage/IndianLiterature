योनीषṭा इन्द्र निषादे अकारी तम आ नि शीदा स्वानो नर्वा |विमुच्याऽवोऽवसायाश्वान दोषा वस्तोर वहीयसः प्रपित्वे ||
yoniṣ ṭa indra niṣade akāri tam ā ni ṣīda svāno nārvā | vimucyā vayo 'vasāyāśvān doṣā vastor vahīyasaḥ prapitve ||
  • Yoniṣ ṭa: This term can be interpreted as "from the womb" or "from the source." It signifies the origin or the beginning of life.
  • Indra: Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and the king of gods. He represents strength and power.
  • Niṣade: This word means "to sit" or "to reside." It implies a state of stability or presence.
  • Akāri: This translates to "did" or "made." It indicates action or creation.
  • Tam: This word means "that" or "he." It refers to a specific entity or concept being discussed.
  • Āniṣīda: This can be understood as "do not sit" or "do not remain." It suggests a call to action or movement.
  • Svāno: This term means "of the self" or "of the soul." It emphasizes individuality and personal essence.
  • Nārvā: This word can be interpreted as "to be free" or "to be liberated." It signifies the importance of freedom in life.
  • Vimucyā: This means "to be released" or "to be liberated." It indicates a desire for freedom from constraints.
  • Vayo: This term refers to "age" or "life span." It highlights the concept of time and existence.
  • 'Avasāyāśvān: This can be interpreted as "the horses of the mind" or "thoughts." It suggests the importance of controlling one's thoughts.
  • Doṣā: This word means "faults" or "defects." It indicates the imperfections that exist in life.
  • Vastor: This translates to "of the object" or "of the material." It refers to the physical world and its elements.
  • Vahīyasaḥ: This means "the best" or "the superior." It signifies the pursuit of excellence.
  • Prapitve: This can be understood as "in the act of giving" or "in the act of sharing." It emphasizes the importance of generosity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, individuality, and the pursuit of freedom. It emphasizes the importance of understanding one's origins and the need to act with purpose. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, where one must recognize their roots and strive for liberation from societal constraints. It also highlights the significance of controlling one's thoughts and actions to achieve excellence and contribute positively to the world. This mantra serves as a reminder to embrace one's individuality while seeking a higher purpose in life.
