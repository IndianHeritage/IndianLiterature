इन्द्र सोमाः सुताः इमे तान दधिष्व शतक्रतो |जटहरे वाजिनीवासो ||
indra somāḥ sutā ime tān dadhiṣva śatakrato | jaṭhare vājinīvaso ||
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • Somāḥ: Somāḥ refers to the sacred drink, soma, which is believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes the connection between the divine and the earthly.
  • Sutā: Sutā means "sons" or "offspring," indicating the blessings of progeny and continuity of lineage.
  • Ime: Ime translates to "these," referring to the offerings or the subjects of the mantra.
  • Tān: Tān means "those," indicating the focus on the blessings or gifts being requested.
  • Dadhiṣva: Dadhiṣva means "bestow" or "grant," a plea for divine favor and blessings.
  • Śatakrato: Śatakrato is another name for Indra, meaning "the one with a hundred powers," emphasizing his omnipotence and ability to grant wishes.
  • Jaṭhare: Jaṭhare means "in the belly" or "within," suggesting a place of nourishment and growth.
  • Vājinīvaso: Vājinīvaso refers to "the one who brings strength" or "the one who provides sustenance," indicating the importance of strength and vitality in life.

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to Indra, asking him to bestow blessings in the form of strength, vitality, and prosperity. The invocation of soma signifies the desire for divine inspiration and the sustenance of life. The mention of "sons" or "offspring" highlights the importance of family and lineage in Vedic culture.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Family and Progeny: It emphasizes the importance of family and the blessings of children, which can be seen in modern rituals and prayers for fertility and family well-being.
  • Strength and Resilience: The plea for strength can be related to personal challenges, where individuals seek inner strength to overcome obstacles in their lives.
  • Divine Inspiration: The reference to soma can be linked to the pursuit of knowledge and creativity, encouraging individuals to seek inspiration in their endeavors.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, encompassing the physical, emotional, and spiritual aspects. It serves as a reminder to seek divine blessings for strength, family, and inspiration, which are essential for a fulfilling life. In contemporary society, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate resilience, nurture their families, and pursue their passions with vigor.
