प्र यत पितुः परमाण नीयते पर्य आपृक्षुधो वीरुधो दंसु रोहति |उभा यद अस्य जनुषं यद इन्बत आद इद यविष्टो अभवद घृणा शुचिः ||
pra yat pituḥ paramān nīyate pary ā pṛkṣudho vīrudho daṁsu rohati | ubhā yad asya januṣaṁ yad invata ād id yaviṣṭho abhavad ghṛṇā śuciḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra speaks about the supreme essence of the father, the nurturing force that sustains life and growth. It highlights the importance of purity and compassion in our actions and thoughts.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra yat: "that which is" or "what is" - signifies the essence or the ultimate truth.
    • pituḥ: "of the father" - represents the paternal figure, often associated with guidance and protection.
    • paramān: "supreme" - indicates the highest state of being or consciousness.
    • nīyate: "is led" or "is directed" - suggests a movement towards a goal or purpose.
    • pary ā: "all around" - signifies the encompassing nature of the divine or the universe.
    • pṛkṣudho: "nurturing" - refers to the act of providing care and sustenance.
    • vīrudho: "growing" - indicates the process of development and flourishing.
    • daṁsu: "to the one who is pure" - emphasizes the importance of purity in thoughts and actions.
    • rohati: "grows" - signifies the idea of growth and evolution.
    • ubhā: "both" - indicates duality or the balance of opposites.
    • yad: "that which" - refers to the qualities or attributes being discussed.
    • asya: "of this" - signifies possession or belonging.
    • januṣaṁ: "of beings" - refers to all living entities.
    • yad invata: "that which is invoked" - suggests the act of calling upon or seeking something.
    • ād id: "from this" - indicates a source or origin.
    • yaviṣṭho: "the one who is established" - refers to stability and permanence.
    • abhavad: "has become" - signifies transformation or change.
    • ghṛṇā: "compassion" - emphasizes the importance of empathy and kindness.
    • śuciḥ: "purity" - refers to cleanliness in thoughts, actions, and intentions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In family dynamics, the father often plays a crucial role in guiding and nurturing children, embodying the qualities of protection and wisdom.
    • In a workplace, a leader who demonstrates compassion and purity in their intentions fosters a positive environment, leading to growth and productivity.
    • In personal development, individuals who strive for purity in their thoughts and actions often experience personal growth and fulfillment.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the qualities of compassion, purity, and growth. It serves as a reminder to embody these virtues in our daily lives, whether in our relationships, professional environments, or personal journeys. By invoking these principles, we can create a nurturing atmosphere that promotes well-being and development for ourselves and those around us.
