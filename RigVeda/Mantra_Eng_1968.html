मन्त्र: विराट सम्राड् विभवीः प्रभवीर बहवीश च भूयसीश च याः | दुरो घृतान्य अक्षरन् ||
virāṭ samrāḍ vibhvīḥ prabhvīr bahvīś ca bhūyasīś ca yāḥ | duro ghṛtāny akṣaran ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • virāṭ: The term signifies the vast, the universal, or the cosmic. It represents the all-encompassing nature of existence.
    • samrāḍ: This word translates to 'emperor' or 'sovereign', indicating authority and power.
    • vibhvīḥ: Meaning 'wealthy' or 'prosperous', it denotes abundance and richness in various forms.
    • prabhvīr: This term refers to 'powerful' or 'mighty', emphasizing strength and capability.
    • bahvīś: Meaning 'many' or 'numerous', it suggests diversity and multiplicity.
    • ca: A conjunction meaning 'and', linking the various attributes together.
    • bhūyasīś: This translates to 'greater' or 'more significant', indicating a sense of elevation or superiority.
    • yāḥ: A relative pronoun meaning 'who' or 'which', referring to the subjects being discussed.
    • duro: Meaning 'difficult' or 'hard', it suggests challenges or obstacles.
    • ghṛtāny: Referring to 'clarity' or 'purity', often associated with ghee, symbolizing nourishment and sustenance.
    • akṣaran: Meaning 'imperishable' or 'eternal', it signifies the everlasting nature of certain truths or principles.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the vastness and authority of the universe, highlighting the qualities of prosperity, power, and significance.
    • It acknowledges the challenges that come with existence but emphasizes the importance of clarity and purity in overcoming them.
    • The mention of 'imperishable' suggests that while life may present difficulties, there are eternal truths and values that remain constant.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, a 'samrāḍ' embodies the qualities of a strong leader who is both powerful and prosperous, guiding their team through challenges.
    • In personal development, embracing the 'virāṭ' aspect encourages individuals to think beyond themselves, recognizing their role in the larger universe.
    • In business, the idea of 'vibhvīḥ' can be seen in companies that thrive by fostering diversity and inclusivity, leading to greater innovation and success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all things and the importance of embracing both challenges and clarity in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to strive for greatness while remaining grounded in eternal values, applicable in leadership, personal growth, and community building.
    • Ultimately, it inspires us to recognize our potential and the power of unity in overcoming life's difficulties.
