स पूर्व्यो वसुविज जातमाणो मृजानो अप्सु दुदुहानो अद्रौ |अभिशस्तिपा भुवनस्य राजा विदद गातुम ब्रह्मणे पूयमानः ||
sa pūrvyo vasuvij jāyamāno mṛjāno apsu duduhāno adrau | abhiśastipā bhuvanasya rājā vidad gātum brahmaṇe pūyamānaḥ ||
  • sa pūrvyo vasuvij: This phrase can be interpreted as "he who is born from the past" or "the one who comes from the earlier beings." It signifies a connection to tradition and the wisdom of ancestors.
  • jāyamāno mṛjāno: This translates to "being born, yet perishing." It reflects the transient nature of life, emphasizing that all beings are born and eventually face mortality.
  • apsu duduhāno adrau: This means "drawing sustenance from the waters." It symbolizes the importance of water as a life-giving force, essential for survival and growth.
  • abhiśastipā bhuvanasya rājā: This can be understood as "the king of the world, who is endowed with blessings." It highlights the role of leadership and the responsibility that comes with power, suggesting that true rulers are those who are blessed and act for the welfare of all.
  • vidad gātum brahmaṇe pūyamānaḥ: This phrase means "he who is engaged in the pursuit of the ultimate truth." It emphasizes the quest for knowledge and understanding, particularly in relation to the divine or the ultimate reality (Brahman).

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound themes of existence, leadership, and the pursuit of truth. In real life, it serves as a reminder of the importance of honoring our roots and the wisdom of our ancestors while acknowledging the impermanence of life. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding, particularly in leadership roles, where the well-being of others is paramount. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, community leadership, and environmental stewardship, where the interconnectedness of life and the importance of nurturing our resources, like water, are crucial for sustainable living.
