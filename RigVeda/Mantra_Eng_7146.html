स प्रत्नथा कविवृद्ध इन्द्रो वाकस्य वक्षाणिः |शिवो अर्कस्य होमन्‍य अस्मत्रा गन्त्व अवसे ||
sa pratnathā kavivṛdha indro vākasya vakṣaṇiḥ | śivo arkasya homany asmatrā gantv avase ||
  • sa pratnathā: This phrase can be interpreted as "he who is the protector" or "he who is the supporter." In real life, this can relate to individuals or systems that provide support and guidance, such as mentors, leaders, or even community structures that help individuals thrive.
  • kavivṛdha: This term refers to "the wise" or "the learned." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in our lives. For example, educators and scholars play a crucial role in shaping society by imparting knowledge and fostering critical thinking.
  • indro: This word signifies "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, power, and leadership. In a modern context, this can symbolize strong leadership in various fields, such as politics, business, or social movements, where decisive action is necessary for progress.
  • vākasya vakṣaṇiḥ: This phrase translates to "the one who speaks the truth" or "the one who is eloquent." It highlights the significance of communication and truthfulness in our interactions. In everyday life, effective communication is vital in personal relationships, workplaces, and public discourse.
  • śivo arkasya: "Śivo" means "auspicious" or "beneficial," while "arkasya" refers to "the sun" or "light." Together, they suggest the idea of seeking positivity and enlightenment. In practical terms, this can relate to pursuing goals that bring joy and fulfillment, such as engaging in activities that promote mental and physical well-being.
  • homany: This term is associated with offerings or sacrifices made in rituals. It signifies the importance of dedication and commitment to one's duties and responsibilities. In real life, this can be seen in the dedication of individuals to their families, careers, or community service.
  • asmatrā gantv avase: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we reach our destination" or "may we achieve our goals." It emphasizes the aspiration for success and fulfillment in life. In a practical sense, this can relate to setting personal or professional goals and working diligently towards achieving them.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of support, wisdom, leadership, truthfulness, positivity, dedication, and aspiration. Its significance lies in the reminder that these qualities are essential for personal growth and societal progress. In real life, we can apply the teachings of this mantra by seeking knowledge, communicating effectively, leading with integrity, and striving for our goals while remaining committed to our responsibilities. By embodying these principles, we contribute to a more harmonious and enlightened society.
