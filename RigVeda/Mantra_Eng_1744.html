आयं स शिङ्क्ते येन गौर अभिवृतā मिमाति मायुं ध्वसनाव अधि श्रिता |सा चित्तिभिर न हि चकार मर्त्यं विद्युद भवन्ती प्रति वव्रिम औहता ||
ayaṁ sa śiṅkte yena gaur abhīvṛtā mimāti māyuṁ dhvasanāv adhi śritā | sā cittibhir ni hi cakāra martyaṁ vidyud bhavantī prati vavrim auhata ||
  • ayaṁ sa śiṅkte: This phrase can be interpreted as "this one who is creating" or "this one who is manifesting." It signifies the act of creation or manifestation in the universe.
  • yena gaur abhīvṛtā: Here, "yena" means "by whom," and "gaur" refers to the "light" or "brightness." This part suggests that the creation is illuminated or made possible by a divine light or energy.
  • mimāti māyuṁ: "Mimāti" means "to move" or "to flow," while "māyuṁ" refers to "the essence" or "the energy." This indicates that the essence of life is in constant motion, flowing and evolving.
  • dhvasanāv adhi śritā: "Dhvasanāv" can be understood as "those who are established" or "those who are grounded." "Adhi" means "above" or "over," and "śritā" refers to "those who take refuge." This suggests that there are beings or energies that are grounded and provide refuge or support.
  • sā cittibhir ni hi cakāra: "Sā" means "that," "cittibhir" refers to "with consciousness," and "ni hi cakāra" translates to "indeed created." This implies that creation is a conscious act, driven by awareness and intention.
  • martyaṁ vidyud bhavantī: "Martyaṁ" refers to "mortals" or "human beings," while "vidyud" means "lightning" or "electricity." "Bhavantī" indicates "becoming" or "manifesting." This part suggests that human beings are capable of becoming enlightened or illuminated, akin to the power of lightning.
  • prati vavrim auhata: "Prati" means "towards" or "in relation to," "vavrim" refers to "to shine" or "to illuminate," and "auhata" means "struck" or "hit." This indicates that there is a force or energy that strikes or inspires illumination in individuals.

Conclusion: This mantra from the RigVeda encapsulates the essence of creation, consciousness, and the transformative power of light. It signifies that the universe is in a constant state of creation, driven by a divine energy that illuminates and inspires. In real life, this can be applied to the understanding that we, as individuals, have the potential to create and manifest our realities through conscious intention and awareness. Just as lightning illuminates the darkness, our thoughts and actions can bring clarity and enlightenment to our lives and the lives of others. This mantra encourages us to seek refuge in the grounded energies around us and to recognize our own power to create and transform our existence.
