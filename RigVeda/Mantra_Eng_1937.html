उर्वी पृथ्वी बहुले दूरान्ते उप ब्रुवे नमसाऽयज्ञे अस्मिन् |दधाते ये सुभगे सुप्रतूर्ति dyावाऽ रक्षतं पृथिवी नो अभ्वात् ||
urvī pṛthvī bahule dūreante upa bruve namasā yajñe asmin | dadhāte ye subhage supratūrtī dyāvā rakṣatam pṛthivī no abhvāt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • urvī: vast, expansive
    • pṛthvī: earth, the world
    • bahule: abundant, plentiful
    • dūreante: far away, distant
    • upa: near, close
    • bruve: I speak, I declare
    • namasā: with reverence, with respect
    • yajñe: in the sacrifice, in the offering
    • asmin: in this
    • dadhāte: they bestow, they grant
    • ye: those who
    • subhage: auspicious, fortunate
    • supratūrtī: well-established, well-formed
    • dyāvā: heaven
    • rakṣatam: may protect, may guard
    • pṛthivī: earth
    • no: our
    • abhvāt: may not be harmed, may not perish
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the vastness of the earth and the abundance it provides.
    • It emphasizes the importance of reverence in our actions, particularly in rituals and offerings.
    • The mantra calls upon the forces of nature, represented by heaven and earth, to protect and sustain us.
    • It highlights the connection between the divine and the earthly, suggesting that our well-being is intertwined with the respect we show to nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often perform rituals to honor the earth and seek blessings for a bountiful harvest, reflecting the reverence mentioned in the mantra.
    • Environmental conservation efforts can be seen as a modern application of this mantra, where protecting the earth (pṛthvī) is crucial for sustaining life.
    • In personal life, showing gratitude for the resources we have, such as food and shelter, aligns with the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for respect towards nature and the divine.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions have consequences on the earth and that we should strive to live harmoniously with our environment.
    • In a world facing environmental challenges, the teachings of this mantra are more relevant than ever, urging us to protect our planet and acknowledge the abundance it offers.
