ये देवासो दिव्य एकादश स्थ पृथिव्यां अध्य एकादश स्थ |अप्सुक्षितो महिनैकादश स्थ ते देवासो यज्ञं इमं जुषध्वम् ||
ye devāso divy ekādaśa stha pṛthivyām adhy ekādaśa stha | apsukṣito mahinaikādaśa stha te devāso yajñam imaṁ juṣadhvam ||
  • Translation: "The eleven divine beings who dwell in the heavens, and the eleven who dwell on the earth, and the eleven who are in the waters, may these divine beings enjoy this sacrifice."
  • Breakdown of Key Terms:
    • ye devāso: "those divine beings" - Refers to the deities or divine forces that govern various aspects of life and nature.
    • divy ekādaśa: "eleven divine" - The number eleven symbolizes completeness and the presence of multiple divine forces.
    • pṛthivyām: "on the earth" - Indicates the connection between the divine and the earthly realm.
    • apsukṣito: "in the waters" - Represents the importance of water as a life-giving force and its divine association.
    • mahinaikādaśa: "the great eleven" - Suggests the significance of these divine beings in the grand scheme of existence.
    • yajñam: "sacrifice" - Refers to the act of offering, which is a central theme in Vedic rituals, symbolizing gratitude and connection to the divine.
    • juṣadhvam: "may enjoy" - A plea for the divine beings to partake in the offerings made by humans.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals and offerings are made to honor deities, such as lighting candles in churches or offering food in temples, reflecting the same spirit of connection and gratitude.
    • Environmental awareness can be seen as a modern interpretation of this mantra, where we recognize the divine presence in nature and the importance of preserving it through sustainable practices.
    • In personal life, individuals often express gratitude through small acts of kindness or offerings, such as donating to charity, which can be seen as a modern form of yajña.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of the divine with the earthly and watery realms, highlighting the importance of acknowledging and honoring these forces through acts of sacrifice and gratitude. In real life, this can manifest in various forms, such as environmental stewardship, community service, and personal rituals that foster a sense of connection to the greater universe. By recognizing the divine in our surroundings and actions, we cultivate a deeper appreciation for life and its myriad forms, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
