यो राध्रस्य चोदिता यः कृशस्य यो ब्रह्मणो नाधमानस्य कीरेः |युक्तग्राव्णो यो 'विता सुशिप्रः सुतसोमस्य स जनासा इन्द्रः ||
yo radhrasya coditā yaḥ kṛśasya yo brahmaṇo nādhamānasya kīreḥ | yuktagrāvṇo yo 'vitā suśipraḥ sutasomasya sa janāsa indraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • radhrasya: of the one who is strong or powerful
    • coditā: who is inspired or motivated
    • yaḥ: who
    • kṛśasya: of the one who is lean or weak
    • yo: who
    • brahmaṇo: of the one who is knowledgeable or wise
    • nādhamānasya: of the one who is not lowly or inferior
    • kīreḥ: of the one who is glorious or famous
    • yuktagrāvṇo: who is well-connected or united
    • yo: who
    • 'avitā: who protects or safeguards
    • suśipraḥ: who is very wise or intelligent
    • sutasomasya: of the one who is a source of happiness or joy
    • sa: that
    • janāsa: of the people or community
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods or a powerful leader
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the qualities of a leader or a powerful figure.
    • It emphasizes the importance of being inspired and motivated, regardless of one's strength or weakness.
    • The mantra suggests that true leadership comes from wisdom, knowledge, and the ability to connect with others.
    • It highlights the role of a leader in protecting and uplifting the community, ensuring that they are not lowly or inferior.
    • Indra, as mentioned in the mantra, symbolizes strength, protection, and the ability to bring joy to the people.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A successful manager in a company who motivates their team, regardless of their individual strengths or weaknesses, embodies the essence of this mantra.
    • A community leader who works to uplift the marginalized and ensures that everyone feels valued and protected reflects the qualities described in the mantra.
    • Teachers who inspire their students to achieve their best, regardless of their starting point, demonstrate the wisdom and protective nature of a true leader.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the qualities that make a great leader: inspiration, wisdom, connection, and protection.
    • In real life, it is applicable in various scenarios, from corporate leadership to community service, emphasizing the importance of uplifting others.
    • By embodying these qualities, individuals can create a positive impact in their communities and foster a sense of unity and strength.
