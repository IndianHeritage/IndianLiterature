पञ्च जनाः मम होत्रं जुषन्तां गोजाताः उता ये यज्ञियासः | पृथिवी नः पार्थिवात पातु अंहसोऽन्तरिक्षं दिव्यात पातु अस्मान् ||
pañca janā mama hotraṁ juṣantāṁ gojātā uta ye yajñiyāsaḥ | pṛthivī naḥ pārthivāt pātv aṁhaso 'ntarikṣaṁ divyāt pātv asmān ||
  • Mantra: "pañca janā mama hotraṁ juṣantāṁ gojātā uta ye yajñiyāsaḥ | pṛthivī naḥ pārthivāt pātv aṁhaso 'ntarikṣaṁ divyāt pātv asmān ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pañca: five
    • janā: beings or people
    • mama: my
    • hotraṁ: offering or sacrifice
    • juṣantāṁ: those who enjoy or partake
    • gojātā: those who are born of cows (referring to wealth or prosperity)
    • uta: and
    • ye: those who
    • yajñiyāsaḥ: who are engaged in sacrifice or worship
    • pṛthivī: the earth
    • naḥ: our
    • pārthivāt: from the earthly
    • pātu: may protect
    • aṁhaso: from the darkness or ignorance
    • antarikṣaṁ: the space or atmosphere
    • divyāt: from the divine
    • pātu: may protect
    • asmān: us
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the five beings who partake in the offerings made during a sacrifice.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and collective participation in rituals, suggesting that the benefits of such acts are shared among all.
    • The reference to "gojātā" symbolizes prosperity and abundance, indicating that the offerings are meant to bring wealth and well-being.
    • The mantra calls upon the earth and the divine to protect us from ignorance and darkness, highlighting the need for spiritual guidance and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, community gatherings, such as festivals or charitable events, reflect the essence of this mantra, where collective efforts lead to shared benefits.
    • Practices like yoga and meditation can be seen as modern-day sacrifices that aim to bring mental clarity and spiritual growth, aligning with the mantra's call for protection from ignorance.
    • In agricultural communities, rituals for a good harvest often involve the entire community, echoing the idea of shared prosperity and collective participation.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of individuals within a community and the importance of collective spiritual practices.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions, especially those aimed at spiritual growth and community welfare, have far-reaching effects.
    • In real life, it encourages us to engage in communal activities that promote well-being, prosperity, and enlightenment, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.
