अग्निः पूर्वे भ्रातरः अर्थं एतं रथीवाद्वानम् अन्व आवरिवुः |तस्माद भियā वरुण दूरम् आयं गौरः न क्षेप्नोर अविजे ज्यायाः ||
agneḥ pūrve bhrātaro artham etaṁ rathīvādhvānam anv āvarīvuḥ | tasmād bhiyā varuṇa dūram āyaṁ gauro na kṣepnor avije jyāyāḥ ||
  • Agneḥ: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic tradition, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Pūrve: Meaning "the ancient" or "the previous," it signifies the wisdom and knowledge passed down through generations.
  • Bhrātaro: This translates to "brothers," indicating unity and collective effort.
  • Artham: Meaning "purpose" or "meaning," it emphasizes the importance of intention behind actions.
  • Etaṁ: This means "this," referring to the specific action or ritual being performed.
  • Rathīvādhvānam: This term can be broken down into "rathi" (chariot) and "vādhvānam" (the act of driving), symbolizing the journey of life and the pursuit of goals.
  • Anv āvarīvuḥ: This means "they have approached" or "they have come together," indicating collaboration and support.
  • Tasmād: Meaning "therefore," it connects the previous thoughts to the conclusion or action that follows.
  • Bhiyā: This translates to "with fear" or "with respect," suggesting a sense of reverence towards the divine.
  • Varuṇa: Referring to the god of cosmic order and water, symbolizing the moral and ethical aspects of life.
  • Dūram: Meaning "far" or "distant," indicating the vastness of the universe and the challenges one may face.
  • Āyaṁ: This means "approach" or "arrival," suggesting the importance of seeking guidance.
  • Gauro: Meaning "the bright" or "the radiant," symbolizing positivity and enlightenment.
  • Na kṣepnor: This translates to "not to be cast away," indicating the importance of holding onto values and principles.
  • Avije: Meaning "without knowledge," suggesting the need for wisdom in our actions.
  • Jyāyāḥ: This means "greater" or "superior," indicating the pursuit of higher ideals and aspirations.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, purpose, and reverence in our actions. It highlights the significance of collaboration and the pursuit of higher ideals while respecting the cosmic order. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in professional settings, family gatherings, or community service, where collective efforts lead to meaningful outcomes. By invoking the spirit of Agni and Varuṇa, individuals are encouraged to approach their goals with respect, wisdom, and a sense of shared purpose, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and enlightened existence.
