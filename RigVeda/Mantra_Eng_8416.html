अभि त्यं गावः पयसा पयोवृद्धं सोमं श्रीणन्ति मतीभिः स्वरविदम् | धनञ्जयः पवते कृत्व्यो रसः विप्रः कविः काव्येनाऽस्वरचनाḥ ||
abhi tyaṁ gāvaḥ payasā payovṛdhaṁ somaṁ śrīṇanti matibhiḥ svarvidam | dhanaṁjayaḥ pavate kṛtvyo raso vipraḥ kaviḥ kāvyenā svarcanāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards
    • tyaṁ: that
    • gāvaḥ: cows (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • payasā: with milk (representing purity and sustenance)
    • payovṛdhaṁ: increasing the essence of milk
    • somaṁ: soma (a sacred drink, symbolizing immortality and divine energy)
    • śrīṇanti: they who are prosperous
    • matibhiḥ: with intellect or wisdom
    • svarvidam: of the celestial or divine sound
    • dhanaṁjayaḥ: the one who conquers wealth
    • pavate: purifies or moves
    • kṛtvyo: those who are to be done
    • raso: essence or flavor
    • vipraḥ: the learned or wise
    • kaviḥ: the poet or seer
    • kāvyenā: through poetry or art
    • svarcanāḥ: illuminating or enlightening
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the abundance and nourishment represented by cows and milk, which are vital for sustenance in life.
    • The reference to soma indicates a connection to divine energy and the pursuit of immortality, suggesting that through wisdom and intellect, one can attain higher states of being.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of wealth, not just in material terms but also in knowledge and creativity, as represented by the learned and the poet.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the nurturing of cows for milk production symbolizes the importance of sustainable practices that provide nourishment to communities.
    • In modern society, the pursuit of knowledge and creativity can be seen in the way individuals strive for personal and professional growth, much like the 'vipraḥ' who seeks wisdom.
    • Art and poetry serve as mediums through which people express their thoughts and emotions, illuminating their experiences and connecting with others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of wealth, nourishment, wisdom, and creativity in achieving a fulfilling life.
    • It teaches us that true wealth is not merely material but encompasses knowledge, creativity, and the ability to uplift others.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious existence, where individuals contribute positively to society while nurturing their own growth.
