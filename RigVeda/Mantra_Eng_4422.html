स चित्र चित्रं चित्रयन्तम अस्मे चित्रक्षत्र चित्रतमं वयोधाम् |चन्द्रं रयिम पुरवीरम् बृहन्तं चन्द्र चन्द्राभिर गृणते युवस्व ||
sa citra citraṁ citayantam asme citrakṣatra citratamaṁ vayodhām | candraṁ rayim puruvīram bṛhantaṁ candra candrābhir gṛṇate yuvasva ||
  • sa citra citraṁ citayantam asme citrakṣatra citratamaṁ vayodhām
    • sa - This word means "that" or "he," referring to a divine entity or force.
    • citra - Translates to "variegated" or "multicolored," symbolizing diversity and richness in life.
    • citrāṁ - This is the accusative form of "citra," emphasizing the action of being colorful or diverse.
    • citayantam - Means "to think" or "to contemplate," indicating a reflective or meditative state.
    • asme - Translates to "to us," suggesting a personal connection or invocation.
    • citrakṣatra - Refers to "the colorful or diverse realm," which can symbolize the universe or the various aspects of life.
    • citratamaṁ - Means "the most colorful" or "the most diverse," indicating the peak of diversity and richness.
    • vayodhām - Refers to "of the warriors" or "of strength," emphasizing valor and courage.
  • candraṁ rayim puruvīram bṛhantaṁ candra candrābhir gṛṇate yuvasva
    • candraṁ - Means "moon," symbolizing calmness, beauty, and the nurturing aspect of nature.
    • rayim - Translates to "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating abundance in life.
    • puruvīram - Refers to "the ancient heroes," suggesting a connection to tradition and valor.
    • bṛhantaṁ - Means "great" or "vast," indicating something of immense significance.
    • candra - Reiterates the moon, emphasizing its importance in the mantra.
    • candrābhir - Means "by the moons," suggesting multiple aspects or phases of the moon.
    • gṛṇate - Translates to "to sing" or "to praise," indicating an act of devotion or celebration.
    • yuvasva - Means "be youthful" or "be vigorous," encouraging vitality and energy.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the beauty of diversity and the importance of contemplation in understanding the universe. It invokes the strength of warriors and the nurturing qualities of the moon, suggesting that true prosperity comes from a balance of courage and calmness. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, embracing diversity in thoughts and experiences can lead to a richer understanding of oneself and the world.
  • In leadership, combining strength with compassion can create a more effective and harmonious environment.
  • In community building, recognizing and celebrating the diverse backgrounds and talents of individuals can foster unity and prosperity.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder to appreciate the multifaceted nature of life and to strive for balance between strength and nurturing qualities.
