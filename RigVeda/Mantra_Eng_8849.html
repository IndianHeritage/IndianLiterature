मुमोद गर्भो वृषभः ककुद्मान अस्रेमाऽ वत्सः शिमिवाम् अरावीत् |स देवतात्य उद्यतानि कृत्वान स्वेषु क्षयेṣu प्रथमो जिगाति ||
mumoda garbho vṛṣabhaḥ kakudmān asremā vatsaḥ śimīvām̐ arāvīt | sa devatāty udyatāni kṛṇvan sveṣu kṣayeṣu prathamo jigāti ||
  • mumoda: This word can be interpreted as "to rejoice" or "to be happy." It signifies a state of joy and fulfillment.
  • garbho: Refers to "the womb" or "the embryo," symbolizing potential and the beginning of life.
  • vṛṣabhaḥ: Translates to "the bull," often representing strength, power, and fertility in Vedic literature.
  • kakudmān: This term can mean "the one who is elevated" or "the one who is exalted," indicating a high status or importance.
  • asremā: This word signifies "the one who is nourished" or "the one who is cared for," emphasizing the importance of nurturing.
  • vatsaḥ: Means "calf" or "young one," symbolizing innocence, growth, and the future generation.
  • śimīvām̐: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is protected" or "the one who is safe," indicating security and safety.
  • arāvīt: Refers to "to be born" or "to emerge," symbolizing the act of coming into existence.
  • sa devatāty: This phrase means "that deity" or "that divine being," indicating a connection to the divine or spiritual realm.
  • udyatāni: Translates to "those who are elevated" or "those who are uplifted," suggesting a rise in status or consciousness.
  • kṛṇvan: Means "to create" or "to make," emphasizing the act of bringing something into being.
  • sveṣu: Refers to "in one's own" or "in one's own realm," indicating a personal or individual context.
  • kṣayeṣu: This term means "in destruction" or "in decline," suggesting the challenges or adversities one may face.
  • prathamo: Translates to "the first" or "the foremost," indicating priority or leadership.
  • jigāti: Means "to conquer" or "to overcome," emphasizing victory and success.

The mantra speaks to the cycle of life, growth, and the divine connection that nurtures and protects. It emphasizes the importance of joy in creation, the strength of life, and the nurturing aspect of existence. The imagery of the bull and the calf symbolizes fertility and the continuation of life, while the references to elevation and protection highlight the spiritual journey and the divine support one receives.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Parenting: The nurturing aspect of the mantra can guide parents in raising their children with love and care, ensuring they grow strong and secure.
  • Personal Growth: The emphasis on joy and elevation can inspire individuals to pursue their passions and strive for personal development, overcoming challenges along the way.
  • Community Building: The idea of creating and nurturing can be applied to building supportive communities where individuals uplift each other and work towards common goals.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of life, growth, and divine support. It serves as a reminder of the joy found in creation, the strength required to overcome challenges, and the importance of nurturing relationships in our lives. By embodying these principles, individuals can lead fulfilling lives and contribute positively to their communities.
