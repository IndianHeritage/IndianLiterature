आ यात्व इन्द्रो 'वस उप न इह स्तुतः सदहमाद अस्तु शूरः |वावृधानस् तविषीर यस्य पूर्वीर द्यौर न क्षत्रम् अभिभूति पुष्यात् ||
ā yātv indro 'vasa upa na iha stutaḥ sadhamād astu śūraḥ | vāvṛdhānas taviṣīr yasya pūrvīr dyaur na kṣatram abhibhūti puṣyāt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yātv: May he come forth
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • 'vasa: to dwell or reside
    • upa: near or close
    • na: not
    • iha: here
    • stutaḥ: praised or worshipped
    • sadhamād: in harmony or together
    • astu: may it be
    • śūraḥ: the brave or the strong
    • vāvṛdhānas: those who increase or grow
    • taviṣīr: powerful or mighty
    • yasya: whose
    • pūrvīr: the ancients or predecessors
    • dyaur: the sky or heaven
    • na kṣatram: not the dominion or power
    • abhibhūti: to overcome or conquer
    • puṣyāt: may it flourish or prosper
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, asking for his presence and support.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and harmony among people, suggesting that strength comes from collective effort.
    • The reference to the ancients indicates a respect for tradition and the wisdom of those who came before us.
    • The mantra also highlights the idea that true power is not about domination but about flourishing together.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, teamwork and collaboration can lead to greater success than individual efforts. Just as the mantra suggests harmony, a team that works together can achieve more.
    • In community initiatives, when people come together for a common cause, they can create significant change, reflecting the idea of collective strength.
    • In personal relationships, mutual respect and support can lead to a flourishing bond, aligning with the mantra's emphasis on harmony and growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity, respect for tradition, and the importance of collective strength.
    • Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, from personal relationships to community efforts and professional environments.
    • By embodying the principles of harmony and collaboration, individuals and groups can overcome challenges and thrive together.
