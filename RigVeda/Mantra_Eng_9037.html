त्वं नो वृत्रहन्तमिन्द्रस्येन्दो शिवः सखा |यत् सीं हवन्ते समिथे वि वो मदे युध्यमानास् तोकसातौ विवक्षसे ||
tvaṁ no vṛtrahantamendrasyendo śivaḥ sakhā | yat sīṁ havante samithe vi vo made yudhyamānās tokasātau vivakṣase ||
  • tvaṁ no vṛtrahantamendrasyendo śivaḥ sakhā: This line addresses a divine entity, invoking the qualities of strength and protection. The term "vṛtrahantam" refers to the slayer of Vritra, a demon representing chaos and obstacles. "Indrasyendo" connects to Indra, the king of gods, symbolizing power and authority. "Śivaḥ sakhā" suggests a friend or ally who is auspicious and benevolent.
  • yat sīṁ havante samithe vi vo made: This part speaks to the offerings made to the divine. "Sīṁ" can be interpreted as strength or vitality, while "havante" refers to the act of offering. "Samithe" indicates a gathering or assembly, suggesting that these offerings are made collectively, emphasizing community and shared purpose.
  • yudhyamānās tokasātau vivakṣase: This line highlights the struggle or battle ("yudhyamānās") that individuals face. "Tokasātau" can be understood as the offspring or results of one's efforts, while "vivakṣase" implies a desire or intention to achieve something. This suggests that through collective effort and divine support, one can overcome challenges and achieve their goals.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine assistance in overcoming obstacles and achieving success. It emphasizes the importance of community and collective effort in spiritual practices.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:
    • Teamwork: In workplaces or group projects, invoking the spirit of collaboration and support can lead to better outcomes.
    • Personal Challenges: When facing personal struggles, individuals can find strength in community support, much like the collective offerings made in the mantra.
    • Spiritual Gatherings: During rituals or community prayers, this mantra can be recited to invoke divine blessings for collective well-being.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of divine support and the strength found in community. It encourages individuals to seek help from higher powers while also emphasizing the importance of working together to overcome life's challenges.
