आ ते सुपर्णा अमिनन्तमं एवैः कृष्णो नोनावा वृषभो यदीदम् | शिवाभिर न स्मयमानाभिर आगात पतन्ति मिहः स्तनयन्त्य अभ्राः ||
ā te suparṇā aminantam̐ evaiḥ kṛṣṇo nonāva vṛṣabho yadīdam | śivābhir na smayamānābhir āgāt patanti mihaḥ stanayanty abhrā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā te: To you
    • suparṇā: Beautiful wings or birds
    • aminantam̐: With great strength
    • evaiḥ: Indeed
    • kṛṣṇo: The black or dark one, often referring to Lord Krishna
    • nonāva: Not to be neglected
    • vṛṣabho: The bull, symbolizing strength and power
    • yadīdam: This
    • śivābhir: With auspiciousness
    • na: Not
    • smayamānābhir: With smiling faces
    • āgāt: Came
    • patanti: They fall
    • mihaḥ: Here
    • stanayanty: They are nurturing
    • abhrā: Clouds
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine presence and the beauty of nature, represented by the birds with beautiful wings.
    • The reference to "kṛṣṇo" suggests a connection to Lord Krishna, who embodies love, compassion, and strength.
    • The "vṛṣabho" symbolizes power and stability, indicating that one should not overlook the strength that comes from divine support.
    • The mention of "śivābhir" implies that auspiciousness and positivity surround us, even when challenges arise.
    • The imagery of clouds nurturing the earth signifies the importance of nature and its role in sustaining life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of difficulty, one can find strength in their faith, much like how the mantra emphasizes divine support.
    • The beauty of nature, represented by birds, can inspire individuals to appreciate their surroundings and find peace in the chaos of life.
    • In relationships, the nurturing aspect of clouds can be likened to the support we provide to one another, fostering growth and understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, nature, and the divine.
    • It encourages individuals to seek strength in their faith and to appreciate the beauty around them.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to personal growth, relationships, and the appreciation of nature, fostering a sense of peace and resilience.
