सुषहा सोम तानि ते पुनानाय प्रभूवसो |वर्धा समुद्रमुक्त्यम् ||
suṣahā soma tāni te punānāya prabhūvaso | vardhā samudram ukthyam ||
  • suṣahā: This word can be translated to mean "well-being" or "prosperity." It signifies a state of happiness and abundance.
  • soma: In Vedic literature, Soma is often associated with a sacred drink that grants immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes the essence of life and vitality.
  • tāni: This term means "those" or "that," referring to the qualities or aspects being addressed in the mantra.
  • te: This word translates to "your," indicating possession or connection to the divine or the higher powers.
  • punānāya: This can be interpreted as "for purification" or "to make pure," suggesting a process of cleansing or renewal.
  • prabhūvaso: This term refers to "the powerful" or "the lordly," indicating a connection to divine authority or strength.
  • vardhā: This means "to increase" or "to grow," suggesting a sense of expansion and enhancement.
  • samudram: Translated as "ocean," it symbolizes vastness, depth, and the source of life.
  • ukthyam: This word means "praise" or "invocation," indicating a call to the divine or a form of worship.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer for well-being and prosperity, invoking the divine essence of life (Soma) to purify and enhance the qualities that lead to abundance. It emphasizes the importance of connecting with higher powers to achieve growth and fulfillment.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or yoga practices to invite prosperity and well-being into their lives.
  • Community Well-being: It can be used in community gatherings or rituals to promote collective prosperity and harmony.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to the ocean can remind us of the importance of preserving our natural resources and the interconnectedness of life.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the significance of well-being, purification, and divine connection in our lives. By invoking these qualities, we can strive for personal and communal growth, fostering a sense of abundance and harmony in our everyday experiences.
