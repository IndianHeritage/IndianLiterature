अभी नवन्ते अद्रुहः प्रियं इन्द्रस्य काम्यम् |वत्सं न पूर्व आयुनी जातं रिहन्ति मातरः ||
abhī navante adruhaḥ priyam indrasya kāmyam | vatsaṁ na pūrva āyuni jātaṁ rihanti mātaraḥ ||
  • abhī navante: This phrase can be translated as "they do not harm" or "they do not cause injury." It emphasizes the importance of non-violence and harmony in relationships.
  • adruhaḥ: This word means "without enmity" or "free from malice." It suggests a state of peace and goodwill among individuals.
  • priyam indrasya kāmyam: This translates to "the beloved desires of Indra." Indra, in Vedic literature, is often seen as a deity representing strength, power, and the forces of nature. This phrase indicates that the desires aligned with Indra are cherished and sought after.
  • vatsaṁ: This word means "calf" or "young one," often symbolizing innocence and new beginnings.
  • na pūrva āyuni: This translates to "not born before" or "not existing previously," indicating something that is new or fresh.
  • jātaṁ rihanti mātaraḥ: This means "the mothers nurture" or "the mothers take care of." It highlights the nurturing aspect of motherhood and the care provided to the young.

In summary, this mantra emphasizes the values of non-violence, goodwill, and nurturing relationships. It suggests that in a harmonious society, individuals should strive to be free from malice and enmity, nurturing the new and innocent, much like a mother cares for her child. The reference to Indra signifies that these values are not only desirable but also aligned with the higher powers of nature and existence.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in everyday life by promoting peaceful interactions and resolving conflicts without hostility.
  • In family dynamics, it encourages parents to nurture their children with love and care, fostering a safe environment for growth.
  • In community settings, it advocates for cooperation and support among individuals, creating a sense of belonging and unity.
  • In professional environments, it highlights the importance of teamwork and collaboration, where colleagues work together without malice or competition.

Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the values that contribute to a harmonious and nurturing society, encouraging individuals to embody these principles in their daily lives.
