त्वां पूर्व ऋषयो गीरभिर आयन त्वाम अध्वरेषु पुरुहूत विश्वे | सहस्राण्य अधिरथान्य अस्मे आ नो यज्ञं रोहिदश्वोप याहि ||
tvām pūrva ṛṣayo gīrbhir āyan tvām adhvareṣu puruhūta viśve | sahasrāṇy adhirathāny asme ā no yajñaṁ rohidaśvopa yāhi ||
  • tvām - This word translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or force.
  • pūrva - Meaning "ancient" or "prior," it suggests a connection to timeless wisdom or traditions.
  • ṛṣayo - Refers to "sages" or "seers," highlighting the importance of wisdom and knowledge in spiritual practices.
  • gīrbhir - Translates to "with hymns" or "with praises," indicating the act of worship through vocal expressions.
  • āyan - Means "come" or "arrive," inviting the divine presence into the ritual.
  • adhvareṣu - Refers to "sacrifices" or "rituals," emphasizing the significance of ceremonial practices in connecting with the divine.
  • puruhūta - This term means "many invoked," suggesting the presence of multiple deities or forces being called upon.
  • viśve - Translates to "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity in the worship and recognition of the divine.
  • sahasrāṇy - Meaning "thousands," it signifies abundance and the vastness of offerings or blessings.
  • adhirathāny - Refers to "chariots" or "vehicles," symbolizing the means through which divine blessings are delivered.
  • asme - Translates to "to us," indicating a plea or request directed towards the divine.
  • ā no - Meaning "may we," it expresses a desire for collective benefit and blessings.
  • yajñaṁ - Refers to "sacrifice" or "offering," a central theme in Vedic rituals that signifies devotion and gratitude.
  • rohidaśvopa - This term can be interpreted as "the red horses," symbolizing strength, vitality, and the dynamic nature of life.
  • yāhi - Meaning "come," it is a call for the divine presence to manifest in the ritual.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of Vedic rituals, emphasizing the importance of invoking divine presence through hymns and sacrifices. It highlights the role of sages and the collective nature of worship, where the community seeks blessings for abundance and strength. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as community gatherings, spiritual ceremonies, and personal rituals where individuals seek guidance, support, and connection with the divine. The significance lies in fostering a sense of unity, gratitude, and reverence towards the forces that govern life, encouraging individuals to engage in practices that promote harmony and well-being in their lives and communities.
