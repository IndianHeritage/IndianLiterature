श्येनो न योनिं सदनं धियाऽकृतं हिरण्ययम् आसदं देव एषति |ए रिणन्ति बर्हिषि प्रियं गिराश्वो न देवाम् अप्य एति यज्ञियः ||
śyeno na yoniṁ sadanaṁ dhiyā kṛtaṁ hiraṇyayam āsadaṁ deva eṣati | e riṇanti barhiṣi priyaṁ girāśvo na devām̐ apy eti yajñiyaḥ ||
  • śyeno: This word translates to "the falcon" or "the bird of prey." In a metaphorical sense, it represents speed, agility, and a higher perspective. Just as a falcon soars high and has a broad view of the landscape, individuals are encouraged to rise above mundane concerns and gain clarity in their thoughts.
  • na yoniṁ: "Na" means "not," and "yoniṁ" refers to "the womb" or "source." This phrase suggests a rejection of limitations or confinement. It encourages individuals to break free from their origins or circumstances that may hold them back.
  • sadanaṁ: This term means "abode" or "place of dwelling." It signifies a stable foundation or a secure environment where one can thrive. In life, this could refer to a supportive community or a nurturing home.
  • dhiyā: This word means "with intellect" or "with understanding." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and discernment in making choices and navigating life’s challenges.
  • kṛtaṁ: Translated as "made" or "created," this word indicates the act of bringing something into existence. It highlights the power of intention and action in shaping one’s reality.
  • hiraṇyayam: This term means "golden" or "radiant." It symbolizes prosperity, success, and the attainment of one’s goals. In a real-life context, it can represent the rewards of hard work and dedication.
  • āsadaṁ: This means "to sit" or "to reside." It suggests a state of being present and grounded in one’s achievements and surroundings.
  • deva: This word means "god" or "divine." It represents higher powers or forces that guide and influence human life.
  • eṣati: Translated as "approaches" or "seeks," this term indicates the pursuit of divine blessings or guidance.
  • e riṇanti: This phrase means "they sing" or "they chant." It signifies the act of expressing gratitude or reverence, often through rituals or prayers.
  • barhiṣi: This term refers to "the sacred grass" used in rituals. It symbolizes purity and the connection to the divine through offerings and ceremonies.
  • priyaṁ: Meaning "beloved" or "dear," this word emphasizes the importance of love and affection in relationships and interactions.
  • girāśvo: This term can be interpreted as "the voice of the horse," symbolizing strength, freedom, and the power of expression. Horses are often associated with nobility and grace.
  • na devām̐: "Na" means "not," and "devām̐" refers to "gods." This phrase suggests that even the divine is not beyond the reach of human effort and devotion.
  • apy eti: This means "even goes" or "also reaches." It indicates that through sincere efforts, one can attain even the highest spiritual goals.
  • yajñiyaḥ: This term refers to "sacrificial" or "ritualistic." It emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine and seeking blessings.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of striving for higher understanding and breaking free from limitations. It encourages individuals to use their intellect and wisdom to create a prosperous and fulfilling life. The significance of this mantra lies in its call to action: to seek divine guidance while remaining grounded in one’s achievements and relationships. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as pursuing personal goals, fostering meaningful connections, and engaging in community service or spiritual practices that uplift oneself and others. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can navigate their lives with purpose and clarity, ultimately leading to a more enriched existence.
