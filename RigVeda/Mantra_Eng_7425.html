अधि न इन्द्रैषां विष्णो सजात्यानाम् |इता मरुतो अश्विनाः ||
adhi na indraiṣāṁ viṣṇo sajātyānām | itā maruto aśvinā ||
  • Adhi: This word translates to "above" or "over." It signifies a higher state or a superior position.
  • Na: This means "not" or "no," indicating negation or absence.
  • Indraiṣāṁ: This term refers to the "powers of Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, authority, and leadership.
  • Viṣṇo: This refers to "Vishnu," the preserver and protector in the Hindu trinity, representing stability and sustenance.
  • Sajātyānām: This means "of those who are of the same kind" or "of the same category," suggesting unity and harmony among similar beings.
  • Itā: This translates to "here" or "in this place," indicating a specific context or situation.
  • Maruto: This refers to the "Maruts," the storm deities associated with wind and storms, symbolizing dynamic energy and movement.
  • Aśvinā: This term refers to the "Aśvins," the twin gods of medicine and health, representing healing and well-being.

  • Meaning: The mantra can be interpreted as a call for the divine powers of Indra and Vishnu to unite and work together harmoniously, especially in the presence of the Maruts and Aśvins. It emphasizes the importance of collaboration among divine forces to achieve a common goal.
  • Real-life Example: In a corporate setting, this mantra can be likened to a team of diverse individuals (representing Indra, Vishnu, Maruts, and Aśvins) coming together to achieve a project. Each member brings their unique strengths and skills, and when they collaborate effectively, they can overcome challenges and achieve success.

  • Significance: This mantra highlights the importance of unity and collaboration among different forces or individuals. It teaches us that when we work together, respecting each other's strengths and differences, we can achieve greater outcomes than when we act alone.
  • Application in Real Life: This principle can be applied in various scenarios, such as teamwork in workplaces, community service projects, or even family dynamics. By fostering an environment of cooperation and mutual respect, we can create a more harmonious and productive society.
