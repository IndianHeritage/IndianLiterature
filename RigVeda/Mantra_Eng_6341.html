आयं दीर्घाय चक्षसे प्राचि प्रयत्य अध्वरे |मिमीत यज्ञं आनुषग विचक्ष्य ||
ayaṁ dīrghāya cakṣase prāci prayaty adhvare | mimīte yajñam ānuṣag vicakṣya ||
  • ayaṁ - This word translates to "this" or "here." It signifies the presence of something important in the context of the mantra.
  • dīrghāya - Meaning "for long" or "for a long duration," this word emphasizes the idea of longevity or endurance.
  • cakṣase - This translates to "for the eye" or "to the sight," indicating perception, vision, or insight.
  • prāci - Meaning "towards the east," this word symbolizes new beginnings, enlightenment, and the dawn of knowledge.
  • prayaty - This means "to strive" or "to exert effort," suggesting the importance of action and dedication in achieving goals.
  • adhvare - This refers to "the sacrifice" or "the ritual," highlighting the significance of spiritual practices and offerings.
  • mimīte - This translates to "he/she/it has measured," indicating the act of assessing or evaluating.
  • yajñam - Meaning "sacrifice" or "offering," this word underscores the importance of selflessness and devotion in spiritual practices.
  • ānuṣag - This means "following" or "in accordance with," suggesting alignment with higher principles or divine will.
  • vicakṣya - This translates to "having insight" or "with wisdom," emphasizing the need for understanding and discernment.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for insight and wisdom in the pursuit of long-lasting goals, particularly in the context of spiritual practices and sacrifices. It encourages individuals to strive towards enlightenment and to measure their actions against higher principles.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra emphasizes striving for insight, individuals can apply this by setting long-term personal development goals, such as pursuing education or self-improvement.
  • Professional Endeavors: In a career context, the mantra encourages professionals to work diligently towards their objectives, ensuring that their efforts are aligned with ethical practices and a greater purpose.
  • Spiritual Practices: The emphasis on sacrifice and offerings can be reflected in acts of service and charity, where individuals contribute to their communities and support those in need.

In conclusion, this mantra from the RigVeda serves as a reminder of the importance of vision, effort, and alignment with higher values in all aspects of life. It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and to engage in meaningful actions that contribute to their personal and collective growth.
