तिग्मायुधौ तिग्महेती सुशेवौ सोमारुद्राव इह सु मृळतं नः |प्र नो मुंचतं वरुणस्य पाशाद गोपायतं नः सुमनस्यमानाः ||
tigmāyudhau tigmahetī suśevau somārudrāv iha su mṛḻataṁ naḥ | pra no muñcataṁ varuṇasya pāśād gopāyataṁ naḥ sumanasyamānā ||
  • tigmāyudhau: This term can be broken down into "tigma" meaning sharp or intense, and "ayudhau" meaning weapons. It refers to the powerful and effective weapons, symbolizing strength and protection.
  • tigmahetī: Similar to the previous term, "tigma" again signifies intensity, while "hetī" refers to a cause or reason. This can be interpreted as the intense causes or reasons that lead to protection and safety.
  • suśevau: This word means well-formed or auspicious. It suggests that the outcomes or results of actions should be positive and beneficial.
  • somārudrāv: "Somā" refers to the moon or a divine drink, while "Rudrā" refers to the fierce aspect of the divine, often associated with Lord Shiva. Together, they symbolize a balance of calmness and intensity.
  • iha: This word means 'here' or 'in this world', indicating the relevance of the mantra in our current existence.
  • su mṛḻataṁ naḥ: "Su" means good, "mṛḻataṁ" refers to being softened or calmed, and "naḥ" means 'for us'. This phrase calls for a calming influence in our lives.
  • pra no muñcataṁ: "Pra" means forward or towards, "no" means 'us', and "muñcataṁ" means to release or liberate. This part of the mantra seeks liberation from negativity or obstacles.
  • varuṇasya pāśād: "Varuṇa" is the god of cosmic order and water, and "pāśād" means from the bonds or ties. This suggests a desire to be freed from the constraints imposed by chaos or disorder.
  • gopāyataṁ naḥ: "Gopāyataṁ" means to protect or guard. This phrase emphasizes the need for divine protection in our lives.
  • sumanasyamānā: "Sumanasya" refers to a state of good mind or happiness, and "mānā" means to be honored or respected. This indicates a wish for a joyful and respected state of being.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a plea for strength, protection, and positive outcomes in life. It emphasizes the importance of divine intervention in overcoming obstacles and achieving a state of peace and happiness. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during challenging times, such as facing personal struggles, health issues, or conflicts. By reciting this mantra, individuals can seek the strength to overcome difficulties, the protection from negative influences, and the guidance towards a more harmonious existence. It serves as a reminder of the balance between intensity and calmness, urging us to strive for positivity and divine support in our daily lives.
