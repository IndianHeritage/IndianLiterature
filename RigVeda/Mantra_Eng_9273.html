इमं विधन्तो अपां सधस्थे पशुं न नष्टं पदैर अनु गमन् |गुहा चतन्तं उशिजो नमोभिर इच्छन्तो धीराः भृगवोऽविन्दन् ||
imaṁ vidhanto apāṁ sadhasthe paśuṁ na naṣṭam padair anu gman | guhā catantam uśijo namobhir icchanto dhīrā bhṛgavo 'vindan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imaṁ: this
    • vidhanto: creating or establishing
    • apāṁ: of waters
    • sadhasthe: in the place of abundance
    • paśuṁ: the animal or creature
    • na: not
    • naṣṭam: lost or destroyed
    • padair: by the feet or paths
    • anu: following
    • gman: to go or move
    • guhā: the cave or hidden place
    • catantam: moving or wandering
    • uśijo: the wise or learned
    • namobhir: with reverence or salutations
    • icchanto: desiring or wishing
    • dhīrā: the wise or prudent
    • bhṛgavo: the Brighus, a clan of sages
    • 'vindan: found or attained
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the creation and establishment of abundance, particularly in relation to water and life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of not losing the essence of life (represented by the animal or creature) as one navigates through life's paths.
    • The mention of "guhā" suggests the hidden aspects of existence, where wisdom and knowledge can be found.
    • The wise individuals (uśijo) are acknowledged for their reverence and desire to attain deeper understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the mantra can be related to sustainable practices that ensure the preservation of water resources while nurturing crops and livestock.
    • In personal development, it reflects the journey of self-discovery, where one must navigate through challenges (paths) without losing their core values (the essence of life).
    • In community building, it highlights the importance of wisdom and respect in fostering relationships that lead to collective growth and understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of life, wisdom, and the natural world.
    • It serves as a reminder to cherish and protect the resources that sustain us while seeking knowledge and understanding.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to act with reverence towards nature and each other, fostering a harmonious existence.
