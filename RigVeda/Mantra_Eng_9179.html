नमो मित्रस्य वरुणस्य चक्षसे महो देवाय तद ऋतं सपर्त्यत |दूरदृशे देवजाताय केतवे दिवस पुत्राय सूर्याय शंसत ||
namo mitrasya varuṇasya cakṣase maho devāya tad ṛtaṁ saparyata | dūredṛśe devajātāya ketave divas putrāya sūryāya śaṁsata ||
  • Namo Mitrasya Varuṇasya: This phrase translates to "Salutations to Mitra and Varuna." Mitra and Varuna are two significant deities in the Rigveda, representing the principles of friendship, harmony, and cosmic order. In real life, this can be seen as a call for unity and cooperation among people, emphasizing the importance of relationships and trust.
  • Cakṣase Mahodevāya: This means "to the great deity with eyes." Here, "cakṣase" refers to the divine vision or insight. In practical terms, this can be interpreted as seeking wisdom and clarity in our actions and decisions, akin to consulting a mentor or wise figure in our lives.
  • Tad ṛtaṁ saparyata: This translates to "that truth should be worshipped." The concept of "ṛta" refers to the cosmic order and truth that governs the universe. In everyday life, this can be related to the importance of honesty and integrity in our dealings, whether in personal relationships or professional environments.
  • Dūredṛśe Devajātāya: This means "to the far-seeing divine being." It suggests the idea of foresight and the ability to see beyond the immediate. In real life, this can be likened to strategic planning in business or personal goals, where one must consider long-term consequences and benefits.
  • Ketave Divas Putrāya: This translates to "to the son of the day." This phrase can be interpreted as a reference to the Sun, symbolizing energy, vitality, and enlightenment. In practical terms, it can represent the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment in our lives, akin to the way we seek education and personal growth.
  • Sūryāya Śaṁsata: This means "let us praise the Sun." The Sun is often seen as a source of life and sustenance. In real life, this can be related to the appreciation of nature and the environment, reminding us of the importance of sustainability and respect for the natural world.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking harmony, wisdom, truth, foresight, enlightenment, and respect for nature. Its significance lies in its call for individuals to cultivate these virtues in their lives. In a world often filled with chaos and uncertainty, this mantra serves as a reminder to strive for unity, integrity, and a deeper understanding of our place in the universe. By embodying these principles, we can foster better relationships, make informed decisions, and contribute positively to society and the environment.
