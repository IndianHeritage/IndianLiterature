बृहद् वरूथम् मरुतां देवं त्रातारम् अश्विनां |मित्रं ईमहे वरुणं स्वस्तये ||
bṛhad varūtham marutāṁ devaṁ trātāram aśvinā | mitram īmahe varuṇaṁ svastaye ||
  • Mantra: "bṛhad varūtham marutāṁ devaṁ trātāram aśvinā | mitram īmahe varuṇaṁ svastaye ||"
  • Translation: "We invoke the great divine protector of the Maruts, the Aśvins, for our welfare and friendship with Varuna."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • bṛhad: Great or vast, indicating the immense power and presence of the divine.
    • varūtham: Protector or guardian, referring to the divine being who safeguards and supports.
    • marutāṁ: Of the Maruts, the storm deities associated with wind and storms, symbolizing strength and energy.
    • devaṁ: Divine, indicating the celestial nature of the being being invoked.
    • trātāram: Protector or savior, emphasizing the role of the divine in providing safety and support.
    • aśvinā: The Aśvins, twin deities representing health, medicine, and the dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and healing.
    • mitram: Friend, indicating the importance of friendship and harmony in relationships.
    • īmahe: We invoke or we call upon, showing the act of seeking divine assistance.
    • varuṇaṁ: Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water, representing truth and moral authority.
    • svastaye: For welfare or well-being, indicating the ultimate goal of invoking these divine beings.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of uncertainty or distress, individuals often seek protection and guidance, similar to how this mantra invokes divine protection. For instance, during natural disasters, communities come together to pray for safety and support.
    • The concept of friendship and harmony is crucial in personal relationships. Just as the mantra emphasizes invoking Varuna for friendship, people often seek to strengthen their bonds with others through communication and understanding.
    • In healthcare, the Aśvins symbolize healing. Modern medicine often invokes the spirit of collaboration among healthcare professionals to ensure the well-being of patients, akin to the divine assistance sought in the mantra.

  • Conclusion: The mantra highlights the significance of invoking divine protection, friendship, and well-being. In real life, it serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking support from higher powers during challenging times, fostering harmonious relationships, and promoting health and healing. By recognizing the interconnectedness of these elements, individuals can cultivate a sense of peace and security in their lives, drawing inspiration from the divine forces represented in the mantra.
