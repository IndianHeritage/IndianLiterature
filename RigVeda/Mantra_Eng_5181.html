स गृत्सो अग्निस तरुणश्चिद अस्तु यतो यविष्टो जनिषṭ मातुः |सं यो वनाय युवते शुचिदान भूरी चिदन्ना समिदत्ति सद्यः ||
sa gṛtso agnis taruṇaś cid astu yato yaviṣṭho ajaniṣṭa mātuḥ | saṁ yo vanā yuvate śucidan bhūri cid annā sam id atti sadyaḥ ||
  • sa gṛtso agnis taruṇaś cid astu yato yaviṣṭho ajaniṣṭa mātuḥ
    • sa gṛtso: This phrase can be interpreted as "he who is connected" or "he who is united." It signifies the importance of unity and connection in life.
    • agnis: Refers to "fire," which symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, fire can represent passion and the drive to achieve goals.
    • taruṇaś: Means "young" or "youthful," indicating vitality and the potential for growth and new beginnings.
    • cid astu: Translates to "may there be consciousness" or "may there be awareness." This emphasizes the importance of mindfulness and being present in our actions.
    • yato yaviṣṭho: This can be understood as "wherever it is established" or "wherever it resides," suggesting that the essence of life and energy can be found in various places.
    • ajaniṣṭa mātuḥ: This phrase means "was born from the mother," highlighting the nurturing aspect of life and the role of the mother or feminine energy in creation.
  • saṁ yo vanā yuvate śucidan bhūri cid annā sam id atti sadyaḥ
    • saṁ yo: Translates to "whoever" or "he who," indicating inclusivity and the universal nature of the message.
    • vanā: Refers to "forest" or "nature," symbolizing the connection to the environment and the importance of nature in our lives.
    • yuvate: Means "youthful" or "young," reinforcing the theme of vitality and the energy of youth.
    • śucidan: Translates to "pure consciousness," emphasizing the significance of clarity and purity in thought and action.
    • bhūri: Means "abundant" or "plentiful," suggesting that there is an abundance of resources and opportunities available to us.
    • cid annā: Refers to "consciousness as food," indicating that awareness and mindfulness nourish our spirit and well-being.
    • sam id atti sadyaḥ: This phrase means "he who eats it immediately," suggesting that the benefits of awareness and connection can be experienced right away.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, the importance of awareness, and the nurturing aspect of creation. It teaches us to recognize the energy within ourselves and the world around us, encouraging us to live mindfully and embrace the abundance of opportunities available to us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal growth, environmental awareness, and fostering relationships. By embodying the principles of unity, consciousness, and abundance, we can lead more fulfilling and meaningful lives.
