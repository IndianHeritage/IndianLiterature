त्वं त्यं इन्द्र मार्त्यम् आस्त्रबुध्नाय वेन्यम् |मुहुः श्रथ्ना मनस्यवे ||
tvaṁ tyam indra martyam āstrabudhnāya venyam | muhuḥ śrathnā manasyave ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "you," referring to a divine entity, in this case, Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
  • tyam - This means "that," indicating a connection or relationship with the divine.
  • indra - Indra is the god of rain, thunder, and war, symbolizing strength and power.
  • martyam - This translates to "mortal" or "human," highlighting the contrast between the divine and human realms.
  • āstrabudhnāya - This term can be broken down into "āstra," meaning weapon or tool, and "budhnāya," which refers to wisdom or intelligence. Together, it suggests the use of divine wisdom or tools for protection or guidance.
  • venyam - This means "to be desired" or "to be sought after," indicating a longing for divine assistance or intervention.
  • muhuḥ - This translates to "again" or "repeatedly," suggesting a continuous or persistent effort in seeking divine help.
  • śrathnā - This word means "to be strong" or "to be steadfast," emphasizing the importance of resilience and determination.
  • manasyave - This term refers to "the mind" or "thoughts," indicating the role of mental focus and intention in the pursuit of divine connection.

The mantra can be interpreted as a plea to Indra, the divine, to provide wisdom and strength to mortals. It emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance repeatedly and maintaining mental focus and resilience in the face of challenges.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties, individuals can invoke this mantra to remind themselves to seek strength and wisdom from a higher power, encouraging them to persist in their efforts.
  • Leadership: Leaders can use the essence of this mantra to inspire their teams, emphasizing the importance of collective strength and wisdom in overcoming obstacles.
  • Education: Students can reflect on this mantra to cultivate a mindset of resilience and focus, seeking knowledge and guidance in their academic pursuits.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the connection between the divine and the mortal, encouraging individuals to seek wisdom and strength in their lives. Its significance lies in the continuous pursuit of guidance and resilience, applicable in various aspects of life, from personal struggles to leadership and education.
