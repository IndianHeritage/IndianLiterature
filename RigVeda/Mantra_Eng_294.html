पूर्व्य होतर अस्य नो मंदस्व सख्यस्य च |इमा उ षु श्रुधी गिरः ||
pūrvya hotar asya no mandasva sakhyasya ca | imā u ṣu śrudhī giraḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pūrvya hotar asya no mandasva sakhyasya ca | imā u ṣu śrudhī giraḥ ||"
  • Translation: "O Hotar (the priest), do not be slow in your offerings; may these words of ours be heard by you."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pūrvya: Refers to the "previous" or "foremost," indicating the importance of tradition and the role of the priest in rituals.
    • hotar: A priest or officiant who performs the rituals and invokes the deities.
    • asya: "of this," referring to the offerings or the ritual being performed.
    • no: "our," indicating a collective voice or community.
    • mandasva: "do not be slow," urging promptness and attentiveness in the performance of duties.
    • sakhyasya: "of friendship," emphasizing the importance of camaraderie and support in the community.
    • imā: "these," referring to the words or prayers being offered.
    • u: "and," connecting thoughts or ideas.
    • ṣu: "well," indicating the quality or effectiveness of the words.
    • śrudhī: "heard," suggesting the desire for the words to be acknowledged and responded to.
    • giraḥ: "words," referring to the spoken prayers or mantras.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when a leader addresses the group, they often call for attention and promptness in participation, similar to the urgency expressed in the mantra.
    • During a wedding ceremony, the officiant (like the Hotar) is expected to perform rituals with care and attentiveness, ensuring that the couple and guests feel the significance of the moment.
    • In a corporate setting, when a team is working on a project, the leader encourages timely contributions from all members, fostering a sense of unity and collaboration, akin to the call for friendship in the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of promptness, attentiveness, and community in rituals and daily life. It serves as a reminder that effective communication and collaboration are essential for achieving common goals. In real-life scenarios, whether in spiritual practices, community events, or professional settings, the principles of being proactive and fostering relationships are crucial for success and harmony. This mantra encourages individuals to be engaged and responsive, ensuring that their contributions are valued and heard.
