मन्त्र: य इन्द्र शुष्मो मगवन ते अस्ति शिक्षां सकिभ्यः पुरुहूत नृभ्यः | त्वं हि दृढा मगवन विचेताऽपां वृद्धि परिवृतं न राधः ||
ya indra śuṣmo maghavan te asti śikṣā sakhibhyaḥ puruhūta nṛbhyaḥ | tvaṁ hi dṛḻhā maghavan vicetā apā vṛdhi parivṛtaṁ na rādhaḥ ||
  • ya indra: This phrase refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength and power.
  • śuṣmo: This word means "the one who is radiant" or "brilliant," indicating the divine qualities of Indra.
  • maghavan: This term translates to "the one who is rich in blessings" or "the generous one," highlighting Indra's role as a benefactor.
  • te asti: This means "you are," affirming the presence and essence of Indra.
  • śikṣā: This word means "instruction" or "teaching," suggesting the importance of guidance in life.
  • sakhibhyaḥ: This translates to "to friends," indicating the communal aspect of learning and sharing knowledge.
  • puruhūta: This term means "the one who is invoked by many," emphasizing the universal appeal of Indra.
  • nṛbhyaḥ: This means "to humans," indicating the connection between the divine and human realms.
  • tvaṁ hi dṛḻhā: This phrase translates to "you are indeed strong," reinforcing the idea of Indra's unwavering strength.
  • maghavan vicetā: This means "the wise one who is rich in blessings," highlighting the importance of wisdom alongside strength.
  • apā vṛdhi: This translates to "may you grant growth," indicating a desire for prosperity and development.
  • parivṛtaṁ: This means "surrounded" or "enveloped," suggesting a protective and nurturing environment.
  • na rādhaḥ: This phrase means "not to be obstructed," indicating the importance of unobstructed progress and success.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of divine guidance, strength, and wisdom in achieving success and prosperity. It highlights the importance of community and the sharing of knowledge among friends and humans. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in leadership, education, and personal development. By invoking the qualities of strength, wisdom, and communal support, individuals can navigate challenges and foster growth in their lives and the lives of those around them. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek guidance and support from both divine and human sources, ensuring a path of unobstructed progress and fulfillment.
