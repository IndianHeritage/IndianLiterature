आहम् पितृन् सुविदात्राम् अवित्सि नपातं च विक्रमणं च विष्णोः |बरहिषदो ये स्वधया सुतस्य भजन्त पित्वस् त इहागमिष्टाः ||
āham pitṝn suvidatrām̐ avitsi napātaṁ ca vikramaṇaṁ ca viṣṇoḥ | barhiṣado ye svadhayā sutasya bhajanta pitvas ta ihāgamiṣṭhāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āham: I
    • pitṝn: ancestors
    • suvidatrām̐: well-disposed or favorable
    • avitsi: I have known
    • napātaṁ: offspring or descendants
    • ca: and
    • vikramaṇaṁ: strength or valor
    • ca: and
    • viṣṇoḥ: of Vishnu
    • barhiṣado: those who are devoted
    • ye: who
    • svadhayā: by their own will or desire
    • sutasya: of the son or progeny
    • bhajanta: worship or serve
    • pitvas: fathers or ancestors
    • ta: they
    • ihāgamiṣṭhāḥ: here in this world
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep connection between the speaker and their ancestors, emphasizing the importance of recognizing and honoring them.
    • The speaker acknowledges their knowledge of the well-being of their ancestors and their descendants, highlighting a sense of continuity and responsibility.
    • It also invokes the strength and valor associated with Vishnu, suggesting that divine support is sought in fulfilling familial duties.
    • The phrase "barhiṣado ye svadhayā" indicates that those who worship their ancestors do so out of their own will, suggesting a personal commitment to honoring family lineage.
    • The mantra concludes with a reminder that these actions and honors are relevant in the present world, emphasizing the importance of ancestral reverence in daily life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, including Hinduism, rituals such as Shraddha are performed to honor ancestors, reflecting the values expressed in this mantra.
    • Family gatherings during festivals often serve as a time to remember and celebrate the contributions of ancestors, reinforcing family bonds and traditions.
    • In modern contexts, individuals may engage in genealogy research to understand their heritage, which aligns with the mantra's emphasis on knowing one's lineage.
    • Acts of service or charity in memory of ancestors can be seen as a way to honor their legacy, demonstrating the ongoing influence of family history in shaping personal values.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of honoring one's ancestors and recognizing the interconnectedness of past, present, and future generations.
    • It serves as a reminder of the responsibilities we hold towards our family lineage and the importance of maintaining these connections through rituals and personal commitment.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to reflect on their roots and the values passed down through generations, fostering a sense of identity and belonging.
    • Ultimately, the mantra underscores the idea that honoring our ancestors enriches our lives and strengthens our communities, making it relevant in various aspects of real life.
