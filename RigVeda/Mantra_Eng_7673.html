मन्त्र: न हि मे अस्त्यघ्न्या न स्वधितिर वनवन्ति | अथैतादृग भरामि ते ||
nahi me asty aghnyā na svadhitir vananvati | athaitādṛg bharāmi te ||
  • Translation: The mantra translates to "I do not have any sin; I do not have any desire for the fruits of my actions. Therefore, I offer this to you."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • nahi: Not
    • me: I have
    • asty: There is
    • aghnyā: Sin or wrongdoing
    • na: No
    • svadhitir: Desire or attachment
    • vananvati: Fruits of actions or results
    • athaitādṛg: Therefore, in this way
    • bharāmi: I offer
    • te: To you
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra reflects a state of purity and selflessness. The speaker asserts that they are free from sin and do not seek personal gain from their actions.
    • It emphasizes the importance of performing actions without attachment to the results, which is a core principle in many spiritual traditions, including Hinduism.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A teacher who dedicates their life to educating students without seeking recognition or rewards embodies the essence of this mantra.
    • A volunteer working in a community service project, focusing solely on helping others without any expectation of personal gain, also reflects this principle.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate a mindset of selflessness and purity in our actions.
    • It encourages individuals to act with integrity and to focus on the act itself rather than the outcomes, fostering a sense of inner peace and fulfillment.
  • Application in Real Life:
    • In professional settings, adopting this mindset can lead to better teamwork and collaboration, as individuals focus on collective goals rather than personal accolades.
    • In personal relationships, practicing selflessness can strengthen bonds and create a more harmonious environment.
