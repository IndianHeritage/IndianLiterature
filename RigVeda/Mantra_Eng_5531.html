एवाग्निं सहस्रं वसिष्ठो रायस्कामो विश्वाप्स्यस्य स्तौत |इषं रयिम पप्रथद वाजम् अस्मे यूम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
evāgniṁ sahasya1ṁ vasiṣṭho rāyaskāmo viśvapsnyasya staut | iṣaṁ rayim paprathad vājam asme yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • evāgniṁ: This word refers to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a vital force in Vedic literature. Fire symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification.
  • sahasyaṁ: This term means strength or power. It signifies the ability to overcome challenges and obstacles.
  • vasiṣṭho: Vasiṣṭha is a revered sage in Hindu tradition, known for his wisdom and knowledge. His presence in the mantra emphasizes the importance of guidance and mentorship.
  • rāyaskāmo: This word translates to the desire for wealth or prosperity. It reflects the human aspiration for abundance and success in life.
  • viśvapsnyasya: This term can be interpreted as the universal or all-encompassing nature of existence. It suggests a connection to the larger cosmos and the interdependence of all beings.
  • staut: This means to praise or worship. It highlights the importance of gratitude and reverence in our lives.
  • iṣaṁ: This word refers to a goal or objective. It signifies the importance of having clear intentions in our pursuits.
  • rayim: This translates to wealth or resources. It emphasizes the need for material and spiritual abundance.
  • paprathad: This term means to spread or distribute. It suggests the importance of sharing and generosity.
  • vājam: This word refers to strength or vigor, often associated with horses in Vedic literature, symbolizing power and speed.
  • asme: This means "to us," indicating a collective aspect of the mantra, emphasizing community and togetherness.
  • yūyam: This term means "you all," addressing the audience or community, reinforcing the idea of unity.
  • pāta: This means to protect or safeguard. It highlights the need for security and safety in our lives.
  • svastibhiḥ: This word translates to auspiciousness or well-being. It signifies the importance of health and prosperity.
  • sadā: This means always or forever, indicating a continuous state of being.
  • naḥ: This means "for us," reinforcing the collective aspect of the mantra.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the importance of strength, guidance, prosperity, and community. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, teamwork, and community building. For instance, in a workplace setting, fostering a culture of support and shared goals can lead to collective success. Similarly, in personal relationships, the principles of gratitude, protection, and shared aspirations can enhance bonds and create a nurturing environment. Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all aspects of life and the importance of striving for balance and harmony.
