स्तम्भीद ध्यां स धरुणं पुरुषायद ऋभुर वाजाय द्रविणं नरो गोः |अनु स्वजाम महिषश चक्षता व्रां मेनाम अश्वस्य परि मातरं गोः ||
stambhīd dha dyāṁ sa dharuṇam pruṣāyad ṛbhur vājāya draviṇaṁ naro goḥ | anu svajām mahiṣaś cakṣata vrām menām aśvasya pari mātaraṁ goḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • stambhīd: to be firm or stable
    • dha: to hold or support
    • dyāṁ: the sky or heavens
    • sa: he or that
    • dharuṇam: the earth or the ground
    • puruṣāyad: to be directed towards a person or being
    • ṛbhur: the wise or the knowledgeable
    • vājāya: for the purpose of strength or power
    • draviṇaṁ: wealth or resources
    • naro: man or human
    • goḥ: of the cow or cattle
    • anu: following or in accordance with
    • svajām: our own or self
    • mahiṣaś: of the buffalo or strong animal
    • cakṣata: to see or perceive
    • vrām: to protect or guard
    • menām: to me or for me
    • aśvasya: of the horse
    • pari: around or surrounding
    • mātaraṁ: mother or nurturing
    • goḥ: of the cow or cattle
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the stability and support provided by both the heavens and the earth.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and strength in human life, suggesting that these qualities lead to prosperity and well-being.
    • The reference to cattle and horses symbolizes wealth and resources, which are essential for sustenance and growth.
    • The mantra also highlights the nurturing aspect of nature, represented by the mother figure, which is crucial for the survival and flourishing of all beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the stability of the earth and the nurturing aspect of nature are vital for successful crop production.
    • In business, wisdom and strength are necessary for making informed decisions that lead to prosperity.
    • The importance of cattle in rural economies illustrates how resources contribute to wealth and sustenance.
    • In family life, the nurturing role of a mother is essential for the emotional and physical well-being of children.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of nature, wisdom, and human life.
    • It teaches us to value stability, strength, and nurturing in our pursuits, whether in personal, professional, or communal contexts.
    • By recognizing the importance of these elements, we can strive for a balanced and prosperous life.
    • In essence, the mantra serves as a reminder to respect and harmonize with the forces of nature and the wisdom that guides us.
