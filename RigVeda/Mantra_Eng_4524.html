यो नो अग्ने दुरेव आ मर्तो वधाय दाशति |तस्मान नः पाहि अंहसः ||
yo no agne dureva ā marto vadhāya dāśati | tasmān naḥ pāhy aṁhasaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • no: our
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • dureva: far away
    • ā: from
    • marto: mortal (human)
    • vadhāya: for the purpose of killing
    • dāśati: he strikes or attacks
    • tasmān: therefore
    • naḥ: us
    • pāhy: protect
    • aṁhasaḥ: from harm or danger
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for protection from harm and danger. It acknowledges the power of Agni, who can strike down mortals from afar, and seeks his safeguarding presence in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of crisis, such as natural disasters or personal challenges, individuals often seek spiritual guidance and protection. This mantra can be recited to invoke a sense of safety and divine intervention.
    • In a community setting, when facing threats or adversities, collective chanting of this mantra can foster unity and a shared sense of security among members.
    • In personal rituals, individuals may use this mantra during meditation or prayer to focus on overcoming fears and seeking strength from a higher power.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine protection and strength. It serves as a reminder of the vulnerabilities of human existence and the need for spiritual support. In real-life scenarios, it can be applicable in moments of fear, uncertainty, or danger, providing comfort and a sense of security through faith in a higher power. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate resilience and a protective shield against life's challenges.
