यद उद्वतो निवतो यासि बप्सत पृथग एषि प्रगर्धिनीव सेनाः |यदाऽते वातो अनुवाति शोचिर वप्तेवा शमश्रु वपसि प्र भूम ||
yad udvato nivato yāsi bapsat pṛthag eṣi pragardhinīva senā | yadā te vāto anuvāti śocir vapteva śmaśru vapasi pra bhūma ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • udvato: from the wind
    • nivato: from the calm
    • yāsi: you go
    • bapsat: you are different
    • pṛthag: separately
    • eṣi: you come
    • pragardhinīva: like a powerful army
    • senā: army
    • yadā: when
    • te: your
    • vāto: winds
    • anuvāti: blow after
    • śocir: shining
    • vapteva: like a flame
    • śmaśru: beard
    • vapasi: you are
    • pra: in front
    • bhūma: earth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the duality of nature, represented by the winds that can be both fierce and calm.
    • It suggests that regardless of the circumstances (whether the winds are strong or gentle), one must navigate through life with strength and purpose.
    • The reference to an army indicates the need for collective strength and unity in facing challenges.
    • The imagery of shining like a flame suggests resilience and the ability to stand out even in adversity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate environment, teams often face challenges that can be likened to strong winds. The mantra encourages individuals to work together (like an army) to overcome obstacles.
    • In personal life, individuals may encounter emotional turmoil (the fierce winds) and moments of peace (the calm). The mantra serves as a reminder to remain steadfast and shine through difficulties.
    • In community settings, collective action during crises (natural disasters, social issues) reflects the essence of this mantra, where unity and strength lead to overcoming challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on resilience, unity, and the ability to adapt to changing circumstances.
    • It teaches that regardless of the external conditions, one should strive to maintain inner strength and work collaboratively with others.
    • This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal struggles to collective challenges faced by communities, reminding us of the power of unity and resilience.
