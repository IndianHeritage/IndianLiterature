मां वो मृगो न यवसे जरिता भूद अजोष्यः |पथा यमस्य गाद उप ||
mā vo mṛgo na yavase jaritā bhūd ajoṣyaḥ | pathā yamasya gād upa ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mā: not
    • vo: your
    • mṛgo: deer (symbolizing desires or goals)
    • na: not
    • yavase: to be caught or trapped
    • jaritā: by the hunter (representing obstacles or challenges)
    • bhūd: may it be
    • ajoṣyaḥ: untroubled or unharmed
    • pathā: by the path
    • yamasya: of Yama (the god of death and justice)
    • gād: to go or proceed
    • upa: near or towards
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for protection from the challenges and obstacles that may hinder one's path in life.
    • The "deer" symbolizes aspirations or goals that one wishes to pursue without being ensnared by difficulties.
    • The reference to "Yama" signifies the inevitability of death and the importance of living a life free from fear and anxiety.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual may face numerous challenges while trying to achieve career goals. This mantra can serve as a reminder to stay focused and not be deterred by setbacks.
    • In personal relationships, one may encounter misunderstandings or conflicts. The mantra encourages individuals to navigate these issues with grace and without losing sight of their intentions.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for resilience and protection against life's adversities.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, reminding individuals to pursue their goals with determination while remaining untroubled by external challenges.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a mindset of courage and clarity, fostering a sense of peace as one navigates the complexities of life.
