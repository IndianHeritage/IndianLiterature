स मातरां न ददृशाना उष्रियो नानदद एति मरुताम इव स्वनः | जानन्न ऋतम् प्रथमं यत् स्वर्णरम् प्रशस्तये कम अवृणीत सुक्रतुः ||
sa mātarā na dadṛśāna usriyo nānadad eti marutām iva svanaḥ | jānann ṛtam prathamaṁ yat svarṇaram praśastaye kam avṛṇīta sukratuḥ ||
  • sa mātarā na dadṛśāna: This phrase can be translated as "the mothers do not see." Here, "mātṛ" refers to the nurturing aspect of nature or the divine feminine. It suggests a protective quality, where the nurturing forces are not visible but are always present.
  • usriyo nānadad eti marutām iva svanaḥ: This part translates to "the winds, like the sound of the Maruts, do not make noise." The Maruts are storm deities associated with the wind and storms in Vedic literature. This line emphasizes the subtlety of nature's forces, indicating that even powerful elements can operate quietly and without disturbance.
  • jānann ṛtam prathamaṁ yat svarṇaram: This translates to "knowing the truth that is first, which is golden." Here, "ṛtam" refers to cosmic order or truth, and "svarṇaram" suggests something precious or valuable, akin to gold. This line highlights the importance of understanding and recognizing the fundamental truths of existence.
  • praśastaye kam avṛṇīta sukratuḥ: This can be interpreted as "who does not choose for praise, the one of good intention." It suggests that true wisdom and intention do not seek external validation or praise but are rooted in genuine purpose and goodness.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of understanding the unseen forces of nature and the importance of recognizing the fundamental truths of life. It teaches us to appreciate the subtlety of existence and the nurturing aspects of the universe that often go unnoticed.
  • Real-life Application: In our daily lives, this mantra can remind us to be aware of the unseen influences around us, such as the support of family, friends, and nature. It encourages us to seek truth and wisdom without the need for external validation. For instance, in a workplace setting, one might focus on doing their job well and contributing positively to the team rather than seeking recognition for their efforts.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all things and the importance of nurturing our inner truth. By embracing the unseen and valuing genuine intentions, we can lead more fulfilling and harmonious lives.
