अभ्यर्षत सुष्टुतिं गव्यं आजिम अस्मासु भद्रā द्रविणानि धत्त | इमं यज्ञं नयत देवतā नो घृतस्य धारā मधुमत पवन्ते ||
abhy arṣata suṣṭutiṁ gavyam ājim asmāsu bhadrā draviṇāni dhatta | imaṁ yajñaṁ nayata devatā no ghṛtasya dhārā madhumat pavante ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhy arṣata: They have come forth or have arisen.
    • suṣṭutiṁ: A well-structured praise or commendation.
    • gavyam: Pertaining to cows or the wealth derived from them.
    • ājim: The swift or the powerful.
    • asmāsu: In our presence or among us.
    • bhadrā: Auspicious or beneficial.
    • draviṇāni: Wealth or resources.
    • dhatta: Bestow or grant.
    • imaṁ: This.
    • yajñaṁ: Sacrifice or ritual offering.
    • nayata: Lead or guide.
    • devatā: Deities or divine beings.
    • no: Our.
    • ghṛtasya: Of ghee or clarified butter.
    • dhārā: Flow or stream.
    • madhumat: Sweet or pleasant.
    • pavante: Blow or move (as in wind).
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of communal praise and the collective effort in rituals.
    • The mention of cows (gavyam) signifies wealth and sustenance, which were vital in ancient agrarian societies.
    • It calls upon the divine (devatā) to guide and bless the offerings made during the yajña (sacrifice).
    • The reference to ghee (ghṛtasya) highlights its significance in rituals, symbolizing purity and nourishment.
    • The flow of sweet offerings (madhumat pavante) suggests the abundance and prosperity that come from divine blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern agricultural practices, the reverence for cows and their products continues, as they are seen as symbols of wealth and sustainability.
    • Community gatherings for festivals often involve collective rituals that echo the essence of this mantra, where people come together to offer prayers and seek blessings.
    • In many cultures, the act of sharing food, especially ghee-based dishes during celebrations, reflects the idea of abundance and gratitude towards divine forces.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of community, nature, and the divine.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of shared resources and the importance of collective efforts in achieving prosperity.
    • In contemporary life, it encourages us to engage in communal activities, respect nature, and seek blessings for a harmonious existence.
