तव त्याद इन्द्रियम् बृहत् तव शुष्मम् उता क्रतुम् |वज्रम् शिशाति धिषणा वरेण्यम् ||
tava tyad indriyam bṛhat tava śuṣmam uta kratum | vajraṁ śiśāti dhiṣaṇā vareṇyam ||
  • tava tyad indriyam bṛhat: This phrase can be translated as "Your vast senses." Here, "tava" means "your," "tyad" refers to "that," "indriyam" means "senses," and "bṛhat" signifies "vast" or "great." This suggests the idea of a higher consciousness or awareness that transcends ordinary perception.
  • tava śuṣmam uta kratum: This translates to "Your essence and intention." "Śuṣmam" refers to "essence" or "subtlety," while "kratum" means "intention" or "will." This part emphasizes the importance of understanding one's true essence and the intentions behind actions.
  • vajraṁ śiśāti dhiṣaṇā vareṇyam: This can be interpreted as "The thunderbolt of wisdom is to be revered." "Vajraṁ" means "thunderbolt," symbolizing strength and clarity, "śiśāti" means "shines" or "illuminates," "dhiṣaṇā" refers to "wisdom," and "vareṇyam" means "to be revered" or "worthy of worship." This highlights the power of wisdom and the importance of respecting and seeking it.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of higher consciousness, the importance of understanding one's true self, and the reverence for wisdom. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, meditation practices, and decision-making processes. By recognizing the vastness of our senses and the significance of our intentions, we can navigate life with greater awareness and purpose. The reverence for wisdom encourages us to seek knowledge and understanding, which can lead to more informed choices and a deeper connection with ourselves and the world around us.
