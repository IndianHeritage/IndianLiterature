असर्जि रथ्यो यथा पवित्रे चाम्वोः सुतः |कार्ष्मन् वाजी न् यक्रमीत् ||
asarji rathyo yathā pavitre camvoḥ sutaḥ | kārṣman vājī ny akramīt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asarji: to be born or produced
    • rathyo: of the chariot
    • yathā: as or like
    • pavitre: pure or sacred
    • camvoḥ: of the horse
    • sutaḥ: son or offspring
    • kārṣman: the one who is strong or powerful
    • vājī: the one who is swift or a victor
    • ny akramīt: did not transgress or did not go beyond
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the birth of a powerful offspring, akin to a chariot that is pure and swift like a horse.
    • It emphasizes the qualities of strength, purity, and speed, which are essential for success and victory in life.
    • The phrase "did not transgress" suggests the importance of adhering to moral and ethical boundaries while striving for success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In sports, an athlete who trains rigorously (like a chariot) and maintains a pure lifestyle (like a sacred horse) often achieves great success.
    • In business, a leader who embodies strength and integrity, while also being swift in decision-making, can lead their team to victory without compromising their values.
    • In personal development, individuals who focus on building their character (purity) and skills (strength) are more likely to achieve their goals without losing their way.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of nurturing qualities such as strength, purity, and ethical conduct in our pursuits.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal growth, professional success, and moral integrity.
    • By embodying these principles, individuals can achieve their goals while maintaining their values, leading to a fulfilling and successful life.
