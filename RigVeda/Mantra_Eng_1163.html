त्रितः कूपे 'वहितो देवान हवता ऊतये |तच्छुश्राव बृहस्पतिः कृत्वान्न अंहूरणाद उरु वित्तं मे अस्य रोधसी ||
tritaḥ kūpe 'vahito devān havata ūtaye | tac chuśrāva bṛhaspatiḥ kṛṇvann aṁhūraṇād uru vittam me asya rodasī ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tritaḥ: Third
    • kūpe: In the well
    • 'vahito: Having been brought forth
    • devān: The gods
    • havata: Invoked
    • ūtaye: For the purpose of nourishment
    • tac: That
    • chuśrāva: Heard
    • bṛhaspatiḥ: The great lord (Bṛhaspati)
    • kṛṇvann: Creating
    • aṁhūraṇād: From the depths
    • uru: Abundant
    • vittam: Wealth
    • me: My
    • asya: Of this
    • rodasī: Of the two worlds
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of invoking the gods for nourishment and abundance.
    • The reference to "the well" symbolizes a source of sustenance and knowledge.
    • Bṛhaspati, the deity of wisdom and eloquence, is called upon to create wealth from the depths.
    • The phrase "of the two worlds" suggests a connection between the material and spiritual realms.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers may chant this mantra to invoke divine blessings for a bountiful harvest.
    • In personal finance, individuals may reflect on this mantra when seeking wisdom in managing their resources.
    • In education, students may recite this mantra to seek guidance and knowledge from higher powers.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine assistance in achieving abundance and wisdom.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including agriculture, finance, and education, highlighting the interconnectedness of the material and spiritual worlds.
    • By invoking the blessings of Bṛhaspati, individuals can aspire to create wealth and knowledge, fostering a holistic approach to life.
