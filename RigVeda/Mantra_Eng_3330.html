मन्त्र: क्रतूयन्ति क्षितयो योग उग्राशुषाणासो मिथो अर्णसातौ |सं यद विश्वो 'ववृत्रन्त युध्मा आद इन नेमा इन्द्रयन्ते अभीक ||
kratūyanti kṣitayo yoga ugrāśuṣāṇāso mitho arṇasātau | saṁ yad viśo 'vavṛtranta yudhmā ād in nema indrayante abhīke ||
  • kratūyanti: This term refers to the act of creating or manifesting through willpower and intention. In real life, this can be likened to setting goals and working diligently towards achieving them, such as a student studying hard to pass an important exam.
  • kṣitayo: This word means 'earth' or 'ground'. It symbolizes stability and foundation. For example, a strong family or community can be seen as the 'earth' that supports individuals in their endeavors.
  • yoga: In this context, yoga signifies union or connection. It can be interpreted as the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, this can be observed in teamwork, where individuals come together to achieve a common goal, such as a sports team working together to win a championship.
  • ugrāśuṣāṇāso: This phrase can be understood as 'those who are fierce and powerful'. It reflects the strength and determination required to overcome challenges. For instance, a person facing adversity, such as a single parent working multiple jobs to provide for their children, embodies this fierce spirit.
  • mitho: This word means 'together' or 'mutually'. It emphasizes the importance of collaboration and unity. In a corporate setting, for example, departments working together on a project demonstrate the power of collective effort.
  • arṇasātau: This term can be interpreted as 'in the midst of conflict or struggle'. It highlights the reality that challenges are a part of life. A real-life example could be a community coming together to rebuild after a natural disaster, showcasing resilience in the face of adversity.
  • saṁ yad: This phrase means 'when' or 'as'. It indicates a condition or a moment in time. It can be related to the idea that certain actions lead to specific outcomes, such as the moment a student decides to pursue higher education, which can change their life trajectory.
  • viśo: This word refers to 'the people' or 'community'. It signifies the collective strength of society. For instance, social movements that arise from the collective voice of the people, such as environmental activism, illustrate the power of community action.
  • avavṛtranta: This term means 'to overcome' or 'to conquer'. It reflects the idea of triumphing over obstacles. A personal example could be someone overcoming addiction, demonstrating the power of will and support systems.
  • yudhmā: This word translates to 'battle' or 'struggle'. It signifies the challenges we face in life. For example, a person fighting a serious illness represents the ongoing battle for health and well-being.
  • ād in nema: This phrase can be interpreted as 'in the presence of divine guidance'. It suggests that there is a higher power or wisdom that supports us in our endeavors. Many people find strength in their faith or spiritual beliefs during difficult times.
  • indrayante: This term refers to 'the senses' or 'the powers'. It highlights the importance of awareness and perception in navigating life. For instance, being mindful of one’s surroundings and emotions can lead to better decision-making.
  • abhīke: This word means 'to be fearless' or 'to be courageous'. It encourages individuals to face their fears and challenges head-on. A real-life example could be someone speaking out against injustice, demonstrating bravery in the face of potential backlash.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of willpower, unity, and resilience in overcoming life's challenges. It teaches us that through collaboration and determination, we can conquer obstacles and achieve our goals. In real life, this mantra is applicable in various scenarios, such as personal struggles, community efforts, and teamwork in professional settings. It serves as a reminder that we are stronger together and that with courage and intention, we can navigate the complexities of life successfully.
