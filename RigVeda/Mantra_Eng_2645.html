अग्निर्यावापृथिवी विश्वजन्ये आ भाति देवी अमृतं अमूरः |क्षयन् वाजैः पुरुषचन्द्रो नमोभिः ||
agnir dyāvāpṛthivī viśvajanye ā bhāti devī amṛte amūraḥ | kṣayan vājaiḥ puruścandro namobhiḥ ||
  • Agnir: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • Dyāvāpṛthivī: This term combines "Dyau" (sky) and "Pṛthivī" (earth), representing the duality of the universe. It signifies the connection between the heavens and the earth.
  • Viśvajanye: This word means "all-encompassing" or "universal," indicating the inclusive nature of the divine and its manifestations in the world.
  • Ā bhāti: Translated as "shines" or "illuminates," this phrase suggests the presence of divine light and knowledge in the universe.
  • Devī: Referring to the goddess or divine feminine energy, it emphasizes the nurturing and protective aspects of the divine.
  • Amṛte: Meaning "immortal," this word highlights the eternal nature of the divine and the soul.
  • Amūraḥ: This term can be interpreted as "without decay" or "imperishable," reinforcing the idea of the eternal and unchanging essence of the divine.
  • Kṣayan: This word means "destruction" or "diminishing," suggesting the transient nature of material existence.
  • Vājaiḥ: Translated as "with strength" or "with vigor," it indicates the power and energy associated with the divine.
  • Puruścandro: This term refers to "the man who is like the moon," symbolizing beauty, calmness, and the reflective nature of the mind.
  • Namobhiḥ: Meaning "with reverence" or "with salutations," this phrase emphasizes the importance of humility and respect towards the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of the divine presence in the universe, highlighting the interconnectedness of all elements—fire, earth, sky, and the divine feminine. It serves as a reminder of the eternal nature of the soul and the importance of reverence in our relationship with the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in rituals, meditation, and daily life as a way to invoke strength, purity, and a deeper understanding of our existence. By recognizing the divine in all aspects of life, we can cultivate a sense of harmony and purpose, leading to a more fulfilling and enlightened existence.
