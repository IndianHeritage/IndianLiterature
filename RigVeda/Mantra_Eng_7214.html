विषु द्वेषो व्यंहतिम आदित्यासो वि संहितम् |विष्वग वि वृहतā रपः ||
vi ṣu dveṣo vy aṁhatim ādityāso vi saṁhitam | viṣvag vi vṛhatā rapaḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • viṣu: This can be interpreted as "to be free from" or "to be liberated from".
    • dveṣo: This means "hatred" or "enmity".
    • vyāṁhatim: This refers to "the one who is unhurt" or "the one who is not affected".
    • ādityāso: This relates to "the sun" or "solar energy\ often symbolizing enlightenment and vitality.
    • vi saṁhitam: This can be understood as "the collective" or "the unity".
    • viṣvag: This means "universal" or "all-encompassing".
    • vi vṛhatā: This translates to "the great" or "the vast".
    • rapaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the essence" or "the substance".
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of transcending hatred and negativity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and collective strength, suggesting that when individuals come together, they can overcome enmity.
    • The reference to the sun symbolizes enlightenment, suggesting that through understanding and knowledge, one can rise above personal grievances.
    • The mantra encourages a universal perspective, promoting the idea that we are all part of a larger whole.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied to foster teamwork. When colleagues work together harmoniously, they can achieve greater success than when they harbor resentment towards one another.
    • In community initiatives, this mantra can inspire individuals to set aside differences and collaborate for a common cause, such as environmental conservation or social justice.
    • On a personal level, individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to let go of grudges and embrace forgiveness, leading to healthier relationships.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and the transcendence of negative emotions.
    • It serves as a reminder that enlightenment and collective strength can help overcome personal and societal challenges.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more harmonious relationships, effective teamwork, and a more compassionate society.
