गिरींर अज्रान रेजमानाम् अधारयद द्यौः क्रन्दद अन्तरिक्षाणि कोपयत् |समीचीन धिषणे वि ष्कभायति वृष्णः पीत्वा मद उक्तानि शंसति ||
girīm̐r ajrān rejamānām̐ adhārayad dyauḥ krandad antarikṣāṇi kopayat | samīcīne dhiṣaṇe vi ṣkabhāyati vṛṣṇaḥ pītvā mada ukthāni śaṁsati ||
  • girīm̐r ajrān rejamānām̐: This phrase can be translated as "the mountains, which are unshaken and stable." In real life, mountains symbolize strength and endurance. For example, consider the Himalayas, which have stood the test of time and natural forces, representing resilience in the face of challenges.
  • adhārayad dyauḥ: This means "the sky supports." The sky is often seen as a metaphor for hope and aspirations. In our lives, we look up to the sky during difficult times, seeking inspiration and guidance, much like how people often turn to their dreams and ambitions for motivation.
  • krandad antarikṣāṇi kopayat: This translates to "the atmosphere cries out and stirs." This can be interpreted as the emotional and physical reactions to events around us. For instance, during a natural disaster, the atmosphere can feel charged with tension, reflecting the turmoil of the situation.
  • samīcīne dhiṣaṇe: This phrase means "in the right place, in the right time." It emphasizes the importance of timing and context in our actions. In real life, being in the right place at the right time can lead to opportunities, such as meeting a mentor or discovering a job opening that aligns with one's skills.
  • vi ṣkabhāyati vṛṣṇaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the powerful one, the bull, is exalted." The bull often symbolizes strength and fertility. In agriculture, for example, bulls are essential for plowing fields, representing hard work and productivity in our daily lives.
  • pītvā mada ukthāni śaṁsati: This translates to "having drunk the intoxicating essence, he praises the hymns." This can be seen as a metaphor for experiencing joy and fulfillment. In life, when we achieve our goals or find happiness, we often feel a sense of euphoria that compels us to express gratitude and share our joy with others.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates themes of strength, support, emotional response, timing, power, and joy. Its significance lies in the reminder that we can draw inspiration from nature and our surroundings, and that our actions and experiences are interconnected. In real life, these principles can guide us in facing challenges, seizing opportunities, and celebrating our achievements, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
