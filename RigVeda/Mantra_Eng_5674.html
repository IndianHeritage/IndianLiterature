तां भूर्पाशाव अनृतस्य सेतु दुर्दत्येतु रिपवे मार्त्याय |ऋतस्य मित्रावरुणा पथā वाम अपो न नाव दुरीतां तरेमा ||
tā bhūripāśāv anṛtasya setū duratyetū ripave martyāya | ṛtasya mitrāvaruṇā pathā vām apo na nāvā duritā tarema ||
  • tā bhūripāśāv anṛtasya setū: This phrase can be translated as "those who are bound by many ties of untruth." Here, "bhūripāśāv" refers to the numerous bonds or ties that can entrap individuals, while "anṛtasya" signifies falsehood or untruth. In real life, this can be likened to the various societal pressures and expectations that can lead individuals away from their true selves.
  • duratyetū ripave martyāya: This part translates to "the difficult to cross bridge of adversities for mortals." The term "duratyetū" indicates something that is hard to overcome, while "ripave" refers to enemies or obstacles. In everyday life, this can represent the challenges we face, such as personal struggles, financial difficulties, or health issues that seem insurmountable.
  • ṛtasya mitrāvaruṇā pathā vām: This translates to "may the friends of truth, Mitra and Varuna, guide us on the path." "ṛtasya" refers to truth or cosmic order, while "Mitra" and "Varuna" are deities representing friendship and cosmic order, respectively. This can be seen in real life as the importance of having supportive friends and mentors who guide us towards honesty and integrity in our actions.
  • apo na nāvā duritā tarema: This phrase means "may we cross over the difficult waters." "apo" refers to waters, "nāvā" means boat, and "duritā" signifies difficulties. This can be interpreted as a metaphor for navigating through life's challenges with the help of supportive relationships and inner strength. In practical terms, it can relate to seeking help from friends or professionals when facing tough situations.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of truth, the challenges of life, and the need for guidance from supportive relationships. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek truth and integrity in our actions, to recognize the adversities we face, and to rely on the support of friends and mentors to navigate through difficult times. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during personal crises, ethical dilemmas, or when making significant life decisions, encouraging individuals to stay true to themselves and seek help when needed.
