मन्त्र: उता स्म ते पुरुष्ण्यां ऊर्णा वसत शुन्ध्यवः | उता पव्याः रथानां अद्रिम भिन्दन्त्य ओजसा ||
uta sma te paruṣṇyām ūrṇā vasata śundhyavaḥ | uta pavyā rathānām adrim bhindanty ojasā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and
    • sma: indeed
    • te: your
    • paruṣṇyām: in the harshness
    • ūrṇā: the threads or fibers
    • vasata: dwelling
    • śundhyavaḥ: those who are pure or clean
    • pavyā: the pure or sacred
    • rathānām: of the chariots
    • adrim: the mountain
    • bhindanty: break or split
    • ojasā: with strength or energy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the duality of strength and purity. It emphasizes the importance of being strong in the face of harshness while also maintaining purity and cleanliness.
    • The mention of "chariots" can symbolize the journey of life, where one must navigate through challenges (the mountains) with both strength and purity of purpose.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual may face tough challenges (the harshness) in their career. By maintaining integrity (purity) and working hard (strength), they can overcome obstacles and achieve success.
    • In personal relationships, one may encounter conflicts or misunderstandings. Approaching these situations with a pure heart and the strength to communicate effectively can lead to resolution and harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the balance between strength and purity in our lives. It teaches us that while we may face difficulties, maintaining our integrity and strength will help us overcome challenges.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal or professional contexts, embodying the principles of this mantra can lead to success, fulfillment, and harmonious relationships.
