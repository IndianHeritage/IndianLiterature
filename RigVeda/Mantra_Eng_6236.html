आ वां विश्वाभिर ऊतिभिः प्रियमेधा अहूषत |राजन्ताव अध्वराणाम अश्विनाः यामहूतिषु ||
ā vāṁ viśvābhir ūtibhiḥ priyamedhā ahūṣata | rājantāv adhvarāṇām aśvinā yāmahūtiṣu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vāṁ: O you (plural), addressing a group.
    • viśvābhir: with all, universal.
    • ūtibhiḥ: with praises or offerings.
    • priyamedhā: with a loving or affectionate mind.
    • ahūṣata: they have called or invoked.
    • rājantāv: shining or ruling.
    • adhvarāṇām: of the sacrifices or rituals.
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins, the divine twin horsemen.
    • yāmahūtiṣu: in the offerings or invocations.
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra invokes the Ashvins, the divine twin deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. It emphasizes the importance of collective praise and offerings made with a loving mind, highlighting the significance of unity and devotion in rituals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate a festival, they often share their joys and express gratitude collectively, similar to the invocation of the Ashvins.
    • In healthcare, the collaboration of medical professionals to provide holistic care can be seen as a modern-day reflection of the Ashvins' role in healing and well-being.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra signifies the power of collective intention and the importance of approaching rituals with a loving and open heart.
    • In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as community service, teamwork in professional settings, and family gatherings where unity and shared purpose are essential.
    • It encourages individuals to come together for a common cause, fostering a sense of belonging and mutual support.
