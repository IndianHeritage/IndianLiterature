यत् किं चेदं वरुण दैव्यम् जनं 'भिद्रोहम् मनुष्याः चरामसि |अचित्ती यत् तव धर्मा युयोपिमा मा नस् तस्माद एनसो देव रीरिषः ||
yat kiṁ cedaṁ varuṇa daivye jane 'bhidroham manuṣyā3ś carāmasi | acittī yat tava dharmā yuyopima mā nas tasmād enaso deva rīriṣaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yat kiṁ cedaṁ varuṇa daivye jane 'bhidroham manuṣyā3ś carāmasi | acittī yat tava dharmā yuyopima mā nas tasmād enaso deva rīriṣaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Whatever transgressions we may have committed, O Varuna, among the divine beings, we are human beings who wander. Whatever is your law, O Varuna, may it not lead us to destruction; do not let us be harmed by your wrath, O God."
  • Explanation:
    • Varuna: In Vedic literature, Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water. He represents the moral and ethical order of the universe.
    • Transgressions: The mantra acknowledges human imperfections and mistakes. It reflects the understanding that humans are prone to errors.
    • Divine Law: The reference to Varuna's law signifies the importance of adhering to moral and ethical principles in life.
    • Human Condition: The phrase "we are human beings who wander" highlights the transient nature of human existence and the journey of life.
    • Seeking Forgiveness: The mantra is a plea for forgiveness and protection from the consequences of one's actions, emphasizing humility and the desire for divine grace.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an employee may make a mistake that affects the team. Acknowledging the error and seeking forgiveness from colleagues can foster a culture of accountability and support.
    • In personal relationships, recognizing one's faults and asking for understanding can strengthen bonds and promote healing.
    • In a broader societal context, communities may seek forgiveness for past injustices, paving the way for reconciliation and unity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of humility, accountability, and the pursuit of ethical living.
    • It encourages individuals to reflect on their actions and seek forgiveness, fostering personal growth and societal harmony.
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied in moments of conflict, decision-making, and self-reflection, guiding individuals towards a path of righteousness and compassion.
