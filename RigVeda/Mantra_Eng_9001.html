कुहा श्रुत इन्द्रः कसमिन्न अद्य जनै मित्रो न श्रूयते |ऋषीणां वा यः क्षये गुहा वा चर्कृषे गिरा ||
kuha śruta indraḥ kasminn adya jane mitro na śrūyate | ṛṣīṇāṁ vā yaḥ kṣaye guhā vā carkṛṣe girā ||
  • kuha - Where
  • śruta - Heard
  • indraḥ - Indra (the king of gods)
  • kasminn - In whom
  • adya - Today
  • jane - Among people
  • mitro - Friend
  • na - Not
  • śrūyate - Is heard
  • ṛṣīṇāṁ - Of the sages
  • vā - Or
  • yaḥ - Who
  • kṣaye - In destruction
  • guhā - Cave
  • vā - Or
  • carkṛṣe - Has spoken
  • girā - Word

The mantra can be translated as: "Where is Indra heard today among people, as a friend? Or who, in the destruction of the sages, has spoken in the cave?"


This mantra raises profound questions about the presence and recognition of divine forces in our lives. It reflects on the search for Indra, a symbol of strength and leadership, and the quest for wisdom among people. The mention of sages and their words suggests a deeper inquiry into knowledge and its preservation or loss.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra is a figure of authority, modern leaders can reflect on their role in society. Are they heard and recognized as friends by their followers? This can be seen in political leaders who strive to connect with their constituents.
  • Wisdom and Knowledge: The reference to sages emphasizes the importance of wisdom. In today's world, we often seek guidance from mentors or experts. This mantra encourages us to value and listen to those who possess knowledge.
  • Community and Relationships: The idea of being a friend among people highlights the significance of building relationships. In our daily lives, fostering connections and being supportive can lead to a more harmonious community.
  • Reflection on Destruction: The mention of destruction prompts us to consider the consequences of our actions. In times of crisis, such as natural disasters or societal upheaval, we must reflect on how we can rebuild and learn from these experiences.

Conclusion: This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of leadership, wisdom, and community in our lives. It encourages us to seek knowledge, build relationships, and reflect on our actions. By embodying these principles, we can navigate the complexities of life with greater awareness and purpose.
