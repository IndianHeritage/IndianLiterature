त्वं त्या चिद वातस्याश्वागा ऋज्रा तमनā वहध्यै |ययोः देवो न मर्त्यो यन्ता नकीर विदाय्यः ||
tvaṁ tyā cid vātasyāśvāgā ṛjrā tmanā vahadhyai | yayor devo na martyo yantā nakir vidāyyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • tyā: By you
    • cid: Indeed
    • vātasyāśvāgā: Of the wind's horses
    • ṛjrā: Swift
    • tmanā: With the mind
    • vahadhyai: Carrying
    • yayor: By whom
    • devo: The divine
    • na: Not
    • martyo: Mortal
    • yantā: Moving
    • nakir: Not
    • vidāyyaḥ: Knowing
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine essence that is beyond mortality and the physical realm.
    • The phrase "vātasyāśvāgā" refers to the swift horses of the wind, symbolizing speed and the ethereal nature of thoughts and ideas.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the divine and the mind, suggesting that through focused thought, one can connect with higher powers.
    • The line "yayor devo na martyo" indicates that the divine is not bound by the limitations of mortality, highlighting the eternal nature of the spirit.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In moments of meditation or prayer, individuals often seek to transcend their physical existence and connect with a higher consciousness, much like the mantra suggests.
    • In the context of leadership, a leader who embodies the qualities of the divine—wisdom, compassion, and vision—can inspire their team to achieve great heights, akin to the swift horses of the wind.
    • In creative pursuits, artists and writers often describe their work as a channeling of divine inspiration, where their thoughts flow freely and swiftly, similar to the essence of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the connection between the divine and the human mind, encouraging individuals to seek higher understanding and inspiration.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the eternal nature of the spirit and the potential for human beings to transcend their limitations through focused thought and intention.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in practices of meditation, leadership, creativity, and any pursuit that seeks to connect with a higher purpose or understanding.
