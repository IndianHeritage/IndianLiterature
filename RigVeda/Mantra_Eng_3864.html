सम अत्र गावोऽभितोऽनवन्तेहेह वत्सैर वियुतā यद आसन |सँ ता इन्द्रो असृजद अस्य शाकैर यद ईं सोमासः सुषुतā अमन्दन ||
sam atra gāvo 'bhito 'navanteheha vatsair viyutā yad āsan | saṁ tā indro asṛjad asya śākair yad īṁ somāsaḥ suṣutā amandan ||
  • Translation: "Here, the cows are not wandering; they are gathered together with their calves. Indra created this place with his branches, where the soma drinkers are blissfully satisfied."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sam atra: Here, in this place
    • gāvo: cows
    • 'bhito: not wandering
    • 'anavanteheha: they are gathered together
    • vatsair: with their calves
    • viyutā: separated or gathered
    • yad: where
    • āsan: they are seated or present
    • saṁ: together
    • tā: that
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • asṛjad: created
    • asya: this
    • śākair: with branches
    • yad: where
    • īṁ: the
    • somāsaḥ: soma drinkers
    • suṣutā: blissfully satisfied
    • amandan: joyful or happy
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra reflects a pastoral scene where cows, symbolizing wealth and nourishment, are gathered peacefully. In many cultures, cows are revered and represent abundance and prosperity.
    • The mention of Indra, a significant deity in Vedic literature, indicates the divine connection to nature and the blessings that come from it. Indra is often associated with rain and fertility, which are crucial for agriculture.
    • The "soma drinkers" refer to those who partake in the sacred soma, a ritual drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight. This highlights the importance of spiritual practices in achieving fulfillment and happiness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In rural communities, the gathering of livestock signifies stability and prosperity. Farmers often celebrate the health of their animals, which directly impacts their livelihood.
    • In modern society, the concept of gathering for communal meals or celebrations echoes the idea of togetherness and shared joy, much like the soma drinkers in the mantra.
    • Environmental sustainability can be linked to the reverence for nature depicted in this mantra. By respecting and nurturing our environment, we can create a harmonious existence, akin to the peaceful gathering of cows.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of community, prosperity, and the divine connection to nature. It serves as a reminder of the importance of gathering together, whether in celebration or in shared purpose.
    • In real life, the principles illustrated in this mantra can be applied to foster community spirit, promote sustainable practices, and appreciate the abundance that nature provides.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to find joy in togetherness and to recognize the blessings that come from both the divine and the natural world.
