यज्ञं च नस् तन्‍वं च प्रजां चादित्यैर इन्द्रः सह चीक्ल्पाति ॥
yajñaṁ ca nas tanvaṁ ca prajāṁ cādityair indraḥ saha cīkḷpāti ||
  • Yajñaṁ: This term refers to a ritual or sacrifice, often performed to please the deities and ensure harmony in the universe. In real life, yajñas can be seen in various forms, such as community gatherings for a common cause, where people come together to contribute for a greater good.
  • Ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," indicating the interconnectedness of the elements mentioned in the mantra.
  • Nas: This word means "us" or "our," emphasizing the collective aspect of the community or group involved in the ritual.
  • Tanvaṁ: This translates to "body" or "physical form," suggesting the importance of the physical presence and participation in the ritual or community effort.
  • Prajāṁ: Meaning "progeny" or "creatures," this term highlights the significance of life and the continuation of generations, which is a central theme in many cultures. For example, community initiatives aimed at education and health can be seen as a way to nurture future generations.
  • Adityair: This refers to the "Adityas," a group of solar deities in Vedic tradition, symbolizing light, truth, and knowledge. In modern life, this can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, such as through education and personal development.
  • Indraḥ: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, representing strength, power, and leadership. In contemporary terms, this can be likened to the role of leaders in society who guide and protect their communities.
  • Saha: This means "together" or "with," emphasizing collaboration and unity in the efforts being made.
  • Cīkḷpāti: This term can be interpreted as "to protect" or "to nurture," indicating the importance of safeguarding the community and its values.

Conclusion: The mantra "yajñaṁ ca nas tanvaṁ ca prajāṁ cādityair indraḥ saha cīkḷpāti" encapsulates the essence of community, collaboration, and the sacredness of life. It signifies the importance of coming together for a common purpose, nurturing future generations, and seeking knowledge and protection through collective efforts. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as community service, educational initiatives, and environmental conservation efforts, where individuals unite to create a positive impact on society and the world at large.
