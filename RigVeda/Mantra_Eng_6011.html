तं ते यवं यथा गोभिः स्वादुम अकर्म श्रीणन्तः |इन्द्र त्वास्मिन सदहमादे ||
taṁ te yavaṁ yathā gobhiḥ svādum akarma śrīṇantaḥ | indra tvāsmin sadhamāde ||
  • taṁ te yavaṁ yathā gobhiḥ: This phrase can be translated to "May we obtain that which is sweet like the essence of grains, just as the cows do." Here, "yavaṁ" refers to grains, which are a staple in many diets, symbolizing nourishment and sustenance. The mention of "gobhiḥ" (cows) highlights the importance of cattle in agrarian societies, where they are seen as a source of wealth and sustenance.
  • svādum akarma śrīṇantaḥ: This part translates to "May we enjoy the sweetness of life without any obstacles." The word "svādum" means sweet, indicating the pleasures and joys of life. "Akarma" refers to actions that are free from negativity or obstacles, suggesting a life lived in harmony and positivity. "Śrīṇantaḥ" implies the attainment of prosperity and well-being.
  • indra tvāsmin sadhamāde: This can be interpreted as "O Indra, may you grant us the strength and support in this endeavor." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. The invocation of Indra signifies a plea for divine assistance in achieving one's goals and aspirations.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for nourishment, both physical and spiritual, while seeking divine support to overcome life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to various aspects such as:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to seek out positive experiences and nourishment in their lives, whether through education, relationships, or self-care.
  • Community Well-being: It can serve as a call for collective efforts in communities to support one another, ensuring that everyone has access to the resources they need to thrive.
  • Spiritual Practice: The invocation of Indra encourages individuals to seek divine guidance and strength in their pursuits, fostering a sense of connection to something greater than themselves.

Overall, this mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking sweetness in life while relying on divine support to navigate challenges, making it relevant in both personal and communal contexts.
