ईळानायावस्यवे यविष्ट दूत नो गिरा | यजिष्ट होतर आ गहि ||
īḻānāyāvasyave yaviṣṭha dūta no girā | yajiṣṭha hotar ā gahi ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • īḻānāyā: This term can be interpreted as "to the one who is worthy" or "to the one who is deserving." It signifies respect and recognition of merit.
    • avasyave: This word means "to the one who is essential" or "to the one who is necessary." It highlights the importance of the subject being addressed.
    • yaviṣṭha: This translates to "the best" or "the most excellent." It denotes superiority and excellence in qualities.
    • dūta: This means "messenger" or "envoy." It refers to someone who carries messages or represents others.
    • no: This is a pronoun meaning "our" or "us," indicating a collective identity.
    • girā: This translates to "speech" or "voice." It emphasizes the power of words and communication.
    • yajiṣṭha: This means "the one who is most worthy of worship" or "the best among those who perform rituals." It signifies reverence and devotion.
    • hotar: This refers to "the priest" or "the one who offers sacrifices." It is a role associated with spiritual leadership and guidance.
    • ā gahi: This phrase means "come here" or "approach." It is an invitation or call to action.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine or a higher power, recognizing the worthiness and essential nature of the subject being addressed.
    • It emphasizes the importance of communication and the role of messengers in conveying messages of significance.
    • The invocation of the "yajiṣṭha hotar" suggests a plea for guidance and support from those who are spiritually elevated and capable of leading rituals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be likened to recognizing and valuing the contributions of team members who are essential to the success of a project.
    • In personal relationships, it reflects the importance of communication and understanding, where one acknowledges the worth of the other person and the need for open dialogue.
    • In spiritual practices, it can be seen as a call to seek guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders who are revered for their wisdom and ability to lead others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing worthiness and the essential nature of individuals in various aspects of life.
    • It highlights the power of communication and the role of spiritual leaders in guiding others.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to appreciate the contributions of others, foster open communication, and seek guidance from those who are knowledgeable and respected.
