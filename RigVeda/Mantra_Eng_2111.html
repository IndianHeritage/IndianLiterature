बृहन्त इनु ये ते तरुत्रोक्तेभिर वा सुम्नम आविवासान |स्तृणानासो बर्हिः पस्त्यावत त्वोतā इद इन्द्र वाजम् अग्मन ||
bṛhanta in nu ye te tarutrokthebhir vā sumnam āvivāsān | stṛṇānāso barhiḥ pastyāvat tvotā id indra vājam agman ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • bṛhanta: Refers to the vastness or greatness, often associated with the divine or the universe.
    • in nu: A phrase that can be interpreted as "indeed" or "truly," emphasizing certainty.
    • ye: Means "those" or "who," indicating a group or collective.
    • te: Translates to "your," indicating possession or relation.
    • tarutrokthebhir: Refers to "by the trees" or "through the trees," symbolizing nature and its connection to the divine.
    • vā: Means "or," presenting an alternative or choice.
    • sumnam: Refers to "auspiciousness" or "well-being," indicating positive outcomes.
    • āvivāsān: Means "having come forth" or "having manifested," suggesting the emergence of something beneficial.
    • stṛṇānāso: Refers to "the one who is covered" or "the one who is protected," indicating safety or shelter.
    • barhiḥ: Refers to "the sacred grass," often used in rituals, symbolizing purity and sanctity.
    • pastyāvat: Means "the one who is strong" or "the powerful," indicating strength and capability.
    • tvotā: Refers to "to you," indicating a direct address to a deity or divine force.
    • id: Means "this," indicating something specific or immediate.
    • indra: Refers to the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power and authority.
    • vājam: Means "food" or "nourishment," indicating sustenance or support.
    • agman: Means "has come" or "arrived," indicating the presence of something significant.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the vastness and greatness of the divine, invoking the presence of Indra, the king of gods, to bring forth auspiciousness and nourishment.
    • The reference to trees symbolizes the connection between nature and the divine, suggesting that the natural world is a source of strength and protection.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine blessings for well-being and sustenance, highlighting the relationship between humanity and the divine forces.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be recited during rituals or ceremonies that seek blessings for health, prosperity, and protection, such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies.
    • Environmental initiatives can also draw inspiration from the connection to nature, promoting sustainability and respect for the earth as a source of life and nourishment.
    • In personal life, individuals may use this mantra as a form of meditation or affirmation, focusing on the vastness of possibilities and the strength derived from nature and the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine support and the recognition of the interconnectedness of life, nature, and the divine.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek blessings for well-being and to honor the natural world as a source of strength and sustenance.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various rituals, personal affirmations, and environmental consciousness, fostering a sense of gratitude and connection to the greater universe.
