स नो रेवत समिधानाḥ स्वस्तये संददस्वान् रयिम अस्मासु दीदिहि |आ नः कृणुष्व सुविता॑या rodasī अग्ने हव्यā मनुषो देव वीतये ||
sa no revat samidhānaḥ svastaye saṁdadasvān rayim asmāsu dīdihi | ā naḥ kṛṇuṣva suvitāya rodasī agne havyā manuṣo deva vītaye ||
  • sa no revat samidhānaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as a call for the divine to bless us with prosperity and well-being. The word "revati" signifies abundance and wealth, while "samidhānaḥ" refers to the act of offering or invoking. In real life, this can be seen as a prayer for success in our endeavors, whether in business, education, or personal growth.
  • svastaye saṁdadasvān: Here, "svastaye" means for our welfare or well-being, and "saṁdadasvān" suggests the act of bringing together or uniting. This can be related to teamwork and collaboration in various aspects of life, such as in workplaces or community projects, where collective efforts lead to greater success.
  • rayim asmāsu dīdihi: "Rayim" refers to wealth or prosperity, "asmāsu" means in our endeavors, and "dīdihi" translates to shining or illuminating. This part of the mantra emphasizes the importance of striving for success and allowing our efforts to shine through. For example, in a competitive job market, showcasing one's skills and achievements can lead to better opportunities.
  • ā naḥ kṛṇuṣva suvitāya rodasī: "Ā naḥ" means to us, "kṛṇuṣva" translates to make or create, "suvitāya" refers to good fortune, and "rodasī" signifies the heavens or the sky. This line can be understood as a request for the divine to create favorable conditions for us. In practical terms, this could relate to seeking guidance and support in challenging situations, such as during exams or important life decisions.
  • agne havyā manuṣo deva vītaye: "Agne" refers to the divine fire or energy, "havyā" means offerings, "manuṣo" signifies humanity, and "deva vītaye" translates to the divine's grace. This part of the mantra highlights the relationship between humans and the divine, suggesting that our offerings and intentions can attract divine blessings. In everyday life, this can be seen in acts of kindness and charity, where giving to others can lead to a sense of fulfillment and connection with the divine.

  • Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for prosperity, well-being, and success in our endeavors. It emphasizes the importance of collaboration, showcasing our efforts, and maintaining a connection with the divine through our actions. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to work together towards common goals, seek guidance in challenging times, and engage in acts of kindness that foster a sense of community and divine connection.
