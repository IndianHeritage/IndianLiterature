उपमं त्वा मघोनां ज्येष्ठं च वृषभाणाम् |पूर्वित्तमम् मघवन् इन्द्र गोविदं ईशानं राया इमहे ||
upamaṁ tvā maghonāṁ jyeṣṭhaṁ ca vṛṣabhāṇām | pūrbhittamam maghavann indra govidam īśānaṁ rāya īmahe ||
  • upamaṁ: This word translates to "comparison" or "similarity." It suggests the idea of finding parallels or drawing analogies.
  • tvā: This means "you," referring to a deity or a revered figure, often used in the context of addressing a divine entity.
  • maghonāṁ: This translates to "of the mighty" or "of the powerful," indicating strength and influence.
  • jyeṣṭhaṁ: Meaning "eldest" or "most excellent," it signifies superiority or the highest rank among peers.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking ideas together.
  • vṛṣabhāṇām: This refers to "the bulls," symbolizing strength, virility, and power, often associated with agricultural prosperity.
  • pūrbhittamam: This means "the best of the past," indicating something that has been established or recognized over time.
  • maghavann: This translates to "the powerful one," often used to describe a deity with great strength and capability.
  • indra: Referring to the king of gods in Vedic tradition, Indra is associated with rain, thunder, and warfare, symbolizing authority and protection.
  • govidam: This means "the protector of cows," which can symbolize nurturing, sustenance, and the importance of agriculture in society.
  • īśānaṁ: This translates to "the lord" or "the ruler," indicating a figure of authority and governance.
  • rāya: Meaning "wealth" or "prosperity," it signifies abundance and success.
  • īmahe: This means "we invoke" or "we call upon," indicating a plea or request for divine assistance.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to a powerful deity, seeking blessings for strength, prosperity, and protection. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring those who are powerful and wise, drawing comparisons to their qualities and contributions.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Leadership: In a corporate setting, leaders can draw inspiration from the qualities of strength and wisdom represented in this mantra, striving to be effective and nurturing in their roles.
  • Community Building: The emphasis on protection and prosperity can be reflected in community initiatives aimed at supporting local agriculture and ensuring the well-being of all members.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use the qualities of the powerful and wise as a benchmark for their own personal development, seeking to embody these traits in their daily lives.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of strength, wisdom, and prosperity in our lives. By invoking these qualities, we can aspire to create a more harmonious and prosperous existence, both individually and collectively. It encourages us to honor those who lead and protect, fostering a sense of gratitude and respect in our communities.
