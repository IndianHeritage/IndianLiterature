सत्यं इति त्वा महेनादि परुष्ण्य अव दिदिशम् |नेम आपो अश्वदातरः शविष्ठाद अस्ति मर्त्यः ||
satyam it tvā mahenadi paruṣṇy ava dediśam | nem āpo aśvadātaraḥ śaviṣṭhād asti martyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • satyam: Truth
    • it: thus
    • tvā: to you
    • mahenadi: great river (symbolizing flow of life)
    • paruṣṇy: harshness or difficulties
    • ava: may (or let)
    • dediśam: grant or bestow
    • nem: not
    • āpo: waters
    • aśvadātaraḥ: swift as a horse
    • śaviṣṭhād: from the best or the most excellent
    • asti: is
    • martyaḥ: mortal or human
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of truth in life.
    • The "great river" symbolizes the continuous flow of life and experiences.
    • It acknowledges that life can be harsh and filled with difficulties.
    • However, it expresses a desire for the best qualities (like swiftness and excellence) to be bestowed upon us.
    • The mention of "mortal" reminds us of our human limitations and the transient nature of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation at work, such as a project deadline, embracing truth and transparency can lead to better teamwork and outcomes.
    • In personal relationships, being truthful can help navigate through difficulties and strengthen bonds.
    • In the face of adversity, like health issues, seeking the truth about one's condition can empower individuals to make informed decisions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of truth in our lives.
    • It encourages us to seek excellence and navigate through life's challenges with honesty.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a deeper understanding of our human experience.
