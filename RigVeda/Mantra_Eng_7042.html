तुरण्यवो मधुमन्तं घृतश्चुतं विप्रासो अर्कम आनृचुः |अस्मे रयिः पप्रथे वृष्ण्यं शवोऽस्मे सुवानास इन्दवः ||
turaṇyavo madhumantaṁ ghṛtaścutaṁ viprāso arkam ānṛcuḥ | asme rayiḥ paprathe vṛṣṇyaṁ śavo 'sme suvānāsa indavaḥ ||
  • turaṇyavo: This term can be interpreted as "swift" or "quick." It signifies the importance of speed and agility in achieving goals.
  • madhumantaṁ: This word translates to "sweet" or "pleasant." It often refers to the sweetness of life, joy, or prosperity.
  • ghṛtaścutaṁ: "ghṛta" means "ghee" or "clarified butter," which is a symbol of nourishment and abundance. "cutaṁ" can mean "that which is poured out," indicating generosity or sharing.
  • viprāso: This term refers to "the wise" or "the learned." It emphasizes the value of knowledge and wisdom in guiding actions.
  • arkam: This word means "light" or "radiance," often associated with the sun or divine illumination.
  • ānṛcuḥ: This can be interpreted as "to sing" or "to praise," indicating the importance of expressing gratitude and reverence.
  • asme: This means "to us," indicating a collective or communal aspect.
  • rayiḥ: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity," highlighting the desire for abundance in life.
  • paprathe: This term means "to spread" or "to expand," suggesting the idea of growth and outreach.
  • vṛṣṇyaṁ: This word refers to "strength" or "power," often associated with vitality and resilience.
  • śavo: This can mean "dead" or "to be dead," but in this context, it may refer to overcoming challenges or obstacles.
  • 'sme: This is a reiteration of "to us," reinforcing the communal aspect.
  • suvānāsa: This term means "good fortune" or "auspiciousness," indicating a desire for favorable outcomes.
  • indavaḥ: This translates to "the moon" or "moon-like," symbolizing calmness, serenity, and the nurturing aspects of life.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of swiftness, wisdom, and the pursuit of prosperity in life. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and share their abundance with others. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as business, where quick decision-making and sharing resources can lead to collective success. It also highlights the significance of gratitude and the pursuit of good fortune, reminding us to appreciate the blessings in our lives and to strive for growth and strength in our endeavors. The mantra serves as a reminder that by working together and valuing wisdom, we can achieve a prosperous and harmonious existence.
