एतावद वां वृषण्वसू अतो वा भूयो अश्विनाः |गृणन्तः सुम्नम इमहे ||
etāvad vāṁ vṛṣaṇvasū ato vā bhūyo aśvinā | gṛṇantaḥ sumnam īmahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • etāvad: thus far, to this extent
    • vāṁ: your
    • vṛṣaṇvasū: O powerful ones, referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities
    • ato: from here, hence
    • vā: or
    • bhūyo: again, more
    • aśvinā: O Aśvins
    • gṛṇantaḥ: singing, praising
    • sumnam: auspiciousness, well-being
    • īmahe: we invoke, we meditate upon
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra expresses a prayer or invocation to the Aśvins, the divine twin horsemen in Vedic mythology, who are associated with health, medicine, and well-being. The speaker acknowledges the power of the Aśvins and seeks their blessings for auspiciousness and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, individuals often seek blessings for health and well-being, especially during times of illness or uncertainty. This mantra can be recited as a form of prayer for healing and recovery.
    • In a community setting, people may come together to chant this mantra during festivals or rituals, invoking the Aśvins for collective prosperity and harmony.
    • Healthcare practitioners may use the essence of this mantra to inspire hope and positivity in their patients, emphasizing the importance of mental and spiritual well-being alongside physical health.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine powers for health and auspiciousness. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of physical, mental, and spiritual well-being. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in personal prayers, community rituals, and healthcare practices, emphasizing the importance of seeking divine support in our pursuit of a healthy and prosperous life.
