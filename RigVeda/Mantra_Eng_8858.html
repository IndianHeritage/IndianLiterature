यो वः शिवतमो रसस् तस्य भाजयतेह नः |उषतीर इव मातरः ||
yo vaḥ śivatamo rasas tasya bhājayateha naḥ | uśatīr iva mātaraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • vaḥ: of you
    • śivatamo: the most auspicious
    • rasas: essence or flavor
    • tasya: of that
    • bhājayate: bestows or shares
    • ha: indeed
    • naḥ: to us
    • uśatīr: like the nourishing or beneficial
    • iva: as if
    • mātaraḥ: mothers
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine essence that is the most auspicious and beneficial.
    • It emphasizes the idea that this essence is shared with us, much like how a mother nourishes her children.
    • The use of "mātaraḥ" (mothers) signifies care, love, and nurturing, suggesting that the divine essence is akin to maternal love.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In everyday life, we often seek guidance and support from those we consider nurturing figures, such as parents, mentors, or friends.
    • For instance, a teacher who provides wisdom and encouragement can be seen as sharing the "most auspicious essence" with their students.
    • In a community, leaders who promote well-being and harmony can be viewed as embodying this nurturing quality, akin to a mother caring for her family.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the importance of nurturing relationships and the sharing of positive qualities.
    • It encourages us to recognize and appreciate the "auspicious essence" in our lives, whether it comes from divine sources or from the people around us.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can inspire us to cultivate kindness, support, and love in our interactions, fostering a more harmonious and nurturing environment.
