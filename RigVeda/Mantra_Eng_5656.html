स सूर्य प्रतिपुरो न उद गा एभिः स्तोमेभिर एतशेभिर एवैः |प्र नो मित्राय वरुणाय वचो 'नागसो आर्यमणे अग्नये च ||
sa sūrya prati puro na ud gā ebhiḥ stomebhir etaśebhir evaiḥ | pra no mitrāya varuṇāya voco 'nāgaso aryamṇe agnaye ca ||
  • sa sūrya prati puro na ud gā ebhiḥ stomebhir etaśebhir evaiḥ
    • sa sūrya: This phrase refers to the Sun, symbolizing light, knowledge, and divinity.
    • prati puro: This means "towards the city" or "towards the abode," indicating a direction or a place of gathering.
    • na ud gā ebhiḥ: Translates to "not to be raised," suggesting a sense of stability or permanence.
    • stomebhir etaśebhir evaiḥ: This refers to hymns or praises, indicating the importance of worship and reverence in approaching the divine.
  • pra no mitrāya varuṇāya voco
    • pra no: This means "for us" or "to us," indicating a plea or request.
    • mitrāya: Refers to Mitra, the deity of friendship and harmony.
    • varuṇāya: Refers to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water, symbolizing truth and moral authority.
    • voco: This means "I speak" or "I call upon," indicating a direct invocation or prayer.
  • nāgaso aryamṇe agnaye ca
    • nāgaso: This can refer to the serpent or the powerful, indicating strength and protection.
    • aryamṇe: Refers to the noble or the righteous, emphasizing moral integrity.
    • agnaye: Refers to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • ca: This means "and," connecting the various elements of the mantra.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine forces for guidance, protection, and harmony in life. It highlights the significance of light (knowledge), friendship, and moral integrity, which are essential for a balanced and fulfilling life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • In personal relationships, fostering friendship and harmony among individuals.
  • In community gatherings, promoting unity and collective well-being.
  • In moments of decision-making, seeking guidance from higher principles and moral values.
  • In rituals and ceremonies, invoking divine energies for protection and transformation.

Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of moral values, and the need for divine guidance in our endeavors.
