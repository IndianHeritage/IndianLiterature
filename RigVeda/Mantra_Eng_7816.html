आद अस्य शुष्मिणो रसे विश्वे देवाः अमत्सत || यदिः गोभिः वसायते ||
ād asya śuṣmiṇo rase viśve devā amatsata | yadī gobhir vasāyate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ād: This can be interpreted as "from" or "out of."
    • asya: Meaning "this" or "of this."
    • śuṣmiṇo: Refers to "the one who is dry" or "the one who is parched."
    • rase: Means "in the essence" or "in the taste."
    • viśve: Translates to "all" or "universal."
    • devā: Refers to "gods" or "divine beings."
    • amatsata: Means "they have become" or "they have attained."
    • yadī: Translates to "if" or "when."
    • gobhir: Refers to "cows" or "nourishing beings."
    • vasāyate: Means "they dwell" or "they reside."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the divine essence that emerges from the parched or dry state, suggesting a transformation or rejuvenation.
    • The mention of "all gods" indicates a universal acknowledgment of divinity in the process of this transformation.
    • The reference to "cows" symbolizes nourishment, abundance, and the sustenance of life, which is crucial in many cultures, especially in agrarian societies.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the mantra can be seen as a metaphor for the importance of water and nourishment for crops. Just as the dry land needs rain to flourish, individuals and communities require spiritual and physical sustenance to thrive.
    • In personal development, the idea of transformation from a "dry" state to one of abundance can be applied to individuals seeking growth. For instance, someone who feels stagnant in their career may seek new skills or education to rejuvenate their professional life.
    • The reverence for cows in many cultures, particularly in India, highlights the importance of nurturing and respecting life forms that provide sustenance, reflecting the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of transformation and the divine essence that can emerge from challenging or barren situations.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of nourishment—both physical and spiritual—and the role of community and divinity in achieving abundance.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek growth and transformation, recognizing the interconnectedness of all life and the divine forces that support us.
