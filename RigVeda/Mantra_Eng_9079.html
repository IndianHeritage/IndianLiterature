एवाऽ हि मां तवसं जज्नुर उग्रं कर्मन्-कर्मन् वृषणं इन्द्र देवाः |वधीं वृत्रं वज्रेण मंदसानोऽप व्रजं महिना दाशुषे वम् ||
evā hi māṁ tavasaṁ jajñur ugraṁ karman-karman vṛṣaṇam indra devāḥ | vadhīṁ vṛtraṁ vajreṇa mandasāno 'pa vrajam mahinā dāśuṣe vam ||
  • evā hi māṁ tavasaṁ jajñur: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, they have given birth to me through their power." Here, the speaker acknowledges the divine forces that have contributed to their existence. In real life, this can be related to the idea of gratitude towards parents, mentors, or any influential figures who shape our lives.
  • ugraṁ karman-karman vṛṣaṇam: This translates to "the fierce actions of the powerful." It signifies the strength and intensity of actions taken by those who are mighty. In a modern context, this can be seen in leaders or activists who take bold steps to bring about change, such as environmental activists fighting against climate change.
  • indra devāḥ: Referring to the "gods" or "deities," particularly Indra, who is often associated with rain, storms, and strength. This can be likened to the forces of nature that play a crucial role in our lives, such as the importance of rain for agriculture and sustenance.
  • vadhīṁ vṛtraṁ vajreṇa: This means "to strike down the enemy with the thunderbolt." It symbolizes overcoming obstacles and challenges. In everyday life, this can be related to personal struggles, such as overcoming fears or challenges in one's career, akin to a student facing and conquering academic difficulties.
  • mandasāno 'pa vrajam: This translates to "with a gentle approach, let us go forth." It emphasizes the importance of a calm and composed demeanor while facing challenges. In real life, this can be seen in conflict resolution, where a peaceful approach can lead to better outcomes than aggression.
  • mahinā dāśuṣe vam: This means "with great strength, we shall overcome." It signifies the power of unity and collective strength. In a real-world scenario, this can be observed in community efforts, such as people coming together to support a cause, like disaster relief efforts after a natural calamity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of gratitude, strength, overcoming challenges, and the importance of a calm approach in the face of adversity. Its significance lies in the reminder that we are shaped by the forces around us, and with unity and strength, we can overcome obstacles. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, from personal growth to community efforts, emphasizing the importance of resilience and collaboration in achieving our goals.
