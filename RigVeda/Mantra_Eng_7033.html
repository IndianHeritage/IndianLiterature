यथा मनौ सांवराणौ सोमं इन्द्रापिभः सुतम् |नीपातिथौ मगवन मेध्यातिथौ पुष्टिगौ श्रुष्टिगौ सचा ||
yathā manau sāṁvaraṇau somam indrāpibaḥ sutam | nīpātithau maghavan medhyātithau puṣṭigau śruṣṭigau sacā ||
  • yathā manau: This phrase translates to "as the minds" or "as the thoughts." It emphasizes the importance of mental focus and intention in achieving one's goals.
  • sāṁvaraṇau: This means "the two protectors" or "the two barriers." It suggests the need for safeguarding one's thoughts and intentions from negative influences.
  • somam: Refers to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing nourishment and spiritual enlightenment.
  • indrāpibaḥ: This translates to "Indra, the drinker," referring to the deity Indra, who represents strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • sutam: Meaning "the son" or "the offspring," it signifies the results or outcomes of one's actions and intentions.
  • nīpātithau: This term can be interpreted as "the two who are invited," indicating the importance of welcoming positive energies and influences into one's life.
  • maghavan: Refers to "the generous one," often associated with abundance and prosperity, highlighting the need for generosity in our actions.
  • medhyātithau: This means "the two who are pure," suggesting the importance of purity in thoughts and actions for achieving success.
  • puṣṭigau: Translates to "the two who provide nourishment," emphasizing the need for support and sustenance in our endeavors.
  • śruṣṭigau: This means "the two who create," indicating the creative power of thoughts and intentions in manifesting reality.
  • sacā: This translates to "and with," suggesting the interconnectedness of all elements in the pursuit of goals.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of mental clarity, protection of thoughts, and the welcoming of positive influences in our lives. It teaches us that our intentions and actions, when aligned with purity and generosity, can lead to abundance and success. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, professional growth, and spiritual practices. By focusing on positive thoughts and inviting supportive energies, individuals can create a conducive environment for achieving their goals and overcoming challenges.
