एषा ययौ परमाद् अन्तर अद्रेः कूचित सतीर उर्वे गा विवेद |दिवो न विद्युत् स्तनयन्त्य अभ्रैः सोमस्य ते पवता इन्द्र धाराः ||
eṣā yayau paramād antar adreḥ kūcit satīr ūrve gā viveda | divo na vidyut stanayanty abhraiḥ somasya te pavata indra dhārā ||
  • eṣā yayau paramād antar adreḥ - This phrase can be translated to "This has gone forth from the highest, from the depths of the mountain." It signifies the emergence of something profound and powerful from the depths of nature, akin to how a river flows from the mountains, symbolizing the source of life and energy.
  • kūcit satīr ūrve gā viveda - This part means "The waters that are pure and clear flow forth." Here, 'kūcit' refers to purity, while 'ūrve' signifies the earth or land. This can be likened to the importance of clean water in our lives, as it is essential for health and sustenance, much like how rivers nourish the land.
  • divo na vidyut stanayanty abhraiḥ - This translates to "The lightning of the sky does not strike the clouds." It suggests that divine energy or power does not manifest in chaos but rather in a controlled manner. In real life, this can be seen in how natural phenomena, like thunderstorms, can be both beautiful and destructive, reminding us of the balance of nature.
  • somasya te pavata indra dhārā - This means "The flow of the nectar of the moon, O Indra, is your blessing." Here, 'soma' represents the divine nectar, often associated with immortality and enlightenment. This can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom in our lives, where seeking enlightenment is akin to receiving blessings from a higher power.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of nature's power, purity, and the divine blessings that flow into our lives. It emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and maintaining a balance with nature. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to our relationship with the environment, the pursuit of clean resources, and the quest for wisdom. By understanding and respecting these elements, we can lead a more harmonious and fulfilling life, much like the flow of pure waters from the mountains that nourish the earth and sustain life.
