शतधारमुत्सम् अक्षीयमानं विपश्चितं पितरं वक्त्वानाम् |मेळिम मदन्तं पित्रोः उपस्थे तं रोधसी पिपृतं सत्यवाचम् ||
śatadhāram utsam akṣīyamāṇaṁ vipaścitam pitaraṁ vaktvānām | meḻim madantam pitror upasthe taṁ rodasī pipṛtaṁ satyavācam ||
  • śatadhāram: This term can be broken down into "śata," meaning "hundred," and "dhāram," meaning "bearer" or "holder." It signifies abundance and the capacity to support many.
  • utsam: This word translates to "the one who rises" or "the one who is elevated." It can refer to a person or a concept that is uplifting or enlightening.
  • akṣīyamāṇaṁ: This term means "that which is not diminished" or "everlasting." It suggests something eternal or unchanging.
  • vipaścitam: This word means "wise" or "intelligent." It refers to someone who possesses deep understanding and insight.
  • pitaraṁ: This translates to "father" or "forefather," indicating respect for ancestral wisdom and lineage.
  • vaktvānām: This term means "of those who speak" or "of the speakers." It emphasizes the importance of communication and expression.
  • meḻim: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is intoxicated" or "the one who is filled with joy." It suggests a state of bliss or ecstasy.
  • madantam: This term refers to "the one who is intoxicated with joy" or "the one who is in a state of bliss." It emphasizes a deep emotional state.
  • pitror: This means "of the fathers" or "of the ancestors," reinforcing the connection to lineage and heritage.
  • upasthe: This term translates to "in the lap" or "in the presence," indicating closeness or intimacy.
  • taṁ: This word means "that" or "him," referring to a specific entity or concept being discussed.
  • rodasī: This term means "the heavens" or "the sky," symbolizing the vastness and the divine.
  • pipṛtaṁ: This word translates to "that which is filled" or "that which is abundant," suggesting richness and fullness.
  • satyavācam: This term means "truthful speech" or "words of truth," emphasizing the importance of honesty and integrity in communication.

Conclusion: The mantra speaks to the significance of wisdom, lineage, and the eternal nature of truth. It highlights the importance of communication and the joy that comes from understanding and connecting with our roots. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as family gatherings where ancestral stories are shared, or in moments of reflection where one seeks to understand their place in the world. It encourages individuals to embrace their heritage, communicate truthfully, and find joy in the wisdom passed down through generations. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of honoring our ancestors, and the pursuit of wisdom and truth in our daily lives.
