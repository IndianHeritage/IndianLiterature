अश्विनā वायुनā युवं सुदक्षā नियुद्भिष च सजोषसā युवानā |नासत्यā तिरोहन्यं जुषाणā सोमं पिबतं अस्रिधā सुदानु ||
aśvinā vāyunā yuvaṁ sudakṣā niyudbhiṣ ca sajoṣasā yuvānā | nāsatyā tiroahnyaṁ juṣāṇā somam pibatam asridhā sudānū ||
  • Mantra: "aśvinā vāyunā yuvaṁ sudakṣā niyudbhiṣ ca sajoṣasā yuvānā | nāsatyā tiroahnyaṁ juṣāṇā somam pibatam asridhā sudānū ||"
  • Translation: "O Ashvins, youthful and skilled, who are swift like the wind, come to us with your divine powers. O Nāsatyas, who are hidden in the night, may you drink the soma with us, bringing us strength and nourishment."
  • Explanation:
    • Ashvins: The Ashvins are twin deities in Vedic mythology, representing health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between opposites.
    • Vāyunā: This term refers to the wind, symbolizing speed and agility. It suggests the importance of being swift in action and thought.
    • Yuvaṁ sudakṣā: "Yuvaṁ" means youthful, and "sudakṣā" means skilled or proficient. This highlights the value of youth and skill in achieving success.
    • Nāsatyā: The Nāsatyas are another name for the Ashvins, emphasizing their role as divine healers and protectors.
    • Tiroahnyaṁ: This term means hidden or concealed, suggesting that divine help may come unexpectedly or from unseen sources.
    • Somam pibatam: "Somam" refers to the soma, a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight. "Pibatam" means to drink, indicating a call for divine blessings and nourishment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern medicine, the pursuit of health and wellness can be likened to the blessings of the Ashvins, where skilled practitioners (doctors) work to heal and restore balance in patients' lives.
    • The concept of agility in decision-making in business can be compared to the swiftness of the wind, where timely actions lead to success.
    • In personal development, embracing youthfulness and skill can inspire individuals to pursue their passions and continuously improve themselves.
    • Unexpected opportunities in life can be seen as the hidden blessings of the Nāsatyas, reminding us to remain open to new experiences.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine assistance, skill, and agility in life. It serves as a reminder to seek help from higher powers, embrace our youthful energy, and remain open to the unexpected. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue health, make swift decisions, and recognize the hidden opportunities that life presents. By invoking the blessings of the Ashvins and Nāsatyas, we can cultivate strength and nourishment in our personal and professional journeys.
