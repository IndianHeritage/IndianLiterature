रथो यो वां त्रिवन्धुर हिरण्याभीशुर अश्विनाः |परि dyāvāpṛthivī भूषति श्रुतस् तेन नासत्यागतम् ||
ratho yo vāṁ trivandhuro hiraṇyābhīśur aśvinā | pari dyāvāpṛthivī bhūṣati śrutas tena nāsatyā gatam ||
  • Mantra: "ratho yo vāṁ trivandhuro hiraṇyābhīśur aśvinā |
  • Translation: "The one who drives the chariot, who is the threefold support, who is golden and who is the Aśvins."
  • Explanation:
    • Ratho: Refers to a chariot, symbolizing movement and progress in life. In real life, this can be likened to the journey we undertake in our personal and professional lives.
    • Trivandhuro: This term can be interpreted as the threefold support, which may represent the mind, body, and spirit. For example, maintaining a balance between mental health, physical fitness, and spiritual well-being is crucial for a fulfilling life.
    • Hiraṇyābhīśur: This word signifies something golden or precious. In a real-life context, it can refer to valuable relationships, experiences, or knowledge that enrich our lives.
    • Aśvinā: The Aśvins are twin deities associated with health and medicine. This can be related to the importance of seeking help and guidance in times of need, whether through medical professionals or support from loved ones.
  • Dyāvāpṛthivī bhūṣati: "The sky and the earth are adorned."
    • This phrase emphasizes the interconnectedness of the universe. In real life, it can be seen in how our actions impact the environment and society. For instance, sustainable practices in our daily lives contribute to the health of our planet.
  • Śrutas tena nāsatyā gatam: "By this knowledge, the untruth is dispelled."
    • This part of the mantra highlights the power of knowledge and truth. In real life, it can be applied to the pursuit of education and understanding, which helps us make informed decisions and dispel ignorance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of progress, balance, value, interconnectedness, and the pursuit of truth. It serves as a reminder to strive for harmony in our lives, seek knowledge, and recognize the impact of our actions on the world around us.
    • In practical terms, we can apply the teachings of this mantra by focusing on personal growth, nurturing relationships, and engaging in sustainable practices that benefit both ourselves and the environment.
