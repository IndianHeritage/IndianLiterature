यो अस्मै हविषाविधान न तं पूषापि मृष्यते |प्रथमो विन्दते वसु ||
yo asmai haviṣāvidhan na tam pūṣāpi mṛṣyate | prathamo vindate vasu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • asmai: to him
    • haviṣā: with offerings
    • vidhan: prepares or arranges
    • na: not
    • tam: that
    • pūṣā: the deity Pūṣa, associated with nourishment and prosperity
    • api: also
    • mṛṣyate: is deceived or misled
    • prathamo: the first
    • vindate: obtains or achieves
    • vasu: wealth or good things
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of sincere offerings and the connection between the devotee and the divine.
    • The phrase "yo asmai haviṣāvidhan" suggests that the one who prepares offerings with devotion is recognized.
    • The line "na tam pūṣāpi mṛṣyate" indicates that even the deity Pūṣa, who represents nourishment and prosperity, does not deceive or overlook such a devotee.
    • Finally, "prathamo vindate vasu" emphasizes that the one who is devoted and sincere in their offerings is the first to attain wealth and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, a person who works diligently and ethically, offering their best efforts, is more likely to achieve success and recognition compared to someone who takes shortcuts.
    • In personal relationships, those who invest time and care into their connections often find deeper and more meaningful bonds, leading to emotional wealth.
    • In community service, individuals who contribute selflessly to their communities often find that their efforts are rewarded with respect and gratitude, enhancing their social wealth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of sincerity and devotion in one's actions, suggesting that true wealth and prosperity come to those who are genuine in their offerings, whether they be material, emotional, or social.
    • It serves as a reminder that in all aspects of life, from personal to professional, the quality of our intentions and efforts determines the richness of our experiences.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a life filled with abundance and fulfillment, reinforcing the idea that sincerity and dedication are key to achieving true success.
