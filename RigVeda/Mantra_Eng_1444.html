विष्वायुः विश्ववेदसं होतारं यजतं कविम् |देवासो रण्वम अवसे वसूयवो गीरभिः रण्वं वसूयवः ||
viśvāyuṁ viśvavedasaṁ hotāraṁ yajataṁ kavim | devāso raṇvam avase vasūyavo gīrbhī raṇvaṁ vasūyavaḥ ||
  • viśvāyuṁ: This term can be translated as "all-pervading" or "universal energy." It signifies the essence that connects all living beings and the universe.
  • viśvavedasaṁ: This means "the one who knows all" or "the universal knowledge." It refers to the wisdom that encompasses all aspects of life and existence.
  • hotāraṁ: This word translates to "the one who offers" or "the priest." In a broader sense, it represents anyone who performs rituals or offers prayers, signifying the importance of devotion and service.
  • yajataṁ: This means "the one who is worshipped" or "the act of worship." It emphasizes the significance of worship in connecting with the divine.
  • kavim: This term translates to "the wise one" or "the poet." It symbolizes creativity, insight, and the ability to express profound truths through art and literature.
  • devāso: This means "the gods" or "divine beings." It represents the higher powers that govern the universe and influence human life.
  • raṇvam: This term can be interpreted as "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the abundance that comes from divine blessings.
  • avase: This means "to be present" or "to reside." It indicates the importance of being grounded and connected to the present moment.
  • vasūyavo: This term translates to "those who are worthy" or "the deserving ones." It highlights the idea that blessings and prosperity come to those who are virtuous and deserving.
  • gīrbhī: This means "through hymns" or "by means of songs." It emphasizes the power of words and music in invoking divine presence and blessings.
  • raṇvaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the call" or "the invocation." It signifies the act of reaching out to the divine for guidance and support.
  • vasūyavaḥ: This term means "those who are blessed" or "the fortunate ones." It represents the outcome of devotion and righteous living.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of universal knowledge, worship, and creativity. It highlights that through devotion and righteous actions, one can invoke divine blessings and prosperity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community service, artistic expression, and spiritual practices. By embodying these principles, individuals can foster a sense of unity, purpose, and abundance in their lives and the lives of others.
