मन्दमान ऋताद् अधि प्रजायै सकिभिर इन्द्र इषिरेभिर अर्थम् |आभिर हि मायाऽ उप दस्युम आगान मिहः प्र ताम्राऽ अवपत तमांसि ||
mandamāna ṛtād adhi prajāyai sakhibhir indra iṣirebhir artham | ābhir hi māyā upa dasyum āgān mihaḥ pra tamrā avapat tamāṁsi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mandamāna: gentle, calm, or soothing
    • ṛtād: from truth or righteousness
    • adhi: upon or over
    • prajāyai: for the progeny or creation
    • sakhibhir: with friends or companions
    • indra: the king of gods, representing power and strength
    • iṣirebhir: with the powerful or mighty
    • artham: for the purpose or meaning
    • ābhir: with the help of
    • hi: indeed
    • māyā: illusion or magic
    • upa: towards or near
    • dasyum: the enemy or adversary
    • āgān: having come
    • mihaḥ: here or in this place
    • pra: forth or forward
    • tamrā: red or fiery
    • avapat: to fall or descend
    • tamāṁsi: darkness or ignorance
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a gentle and righteous approach to creation and existence.
    • It emphasizes the importance of companionship and strength in overcoming challenges.
    • The reference to Indra suggests invoking divine power to aid in achieving goals.
    • The mention of "māyā" indicates the presence of illusions or obstacles that one must navigate.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the need to confront and dispel darkness or ignorance in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, collaboration with colleagues (sakhibhir) can lead to innovative solutions (artham) when facing challenges.
    • In personal relationships, supporting each other through difficult times can help overcome emotional obstacles (dasyum).
    • In education, seeking knowledge (ṛtād) with the guidance of mentors can illuminate paths obscured by confusion (tamāṁsi).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of truth, companionship, and divine support in navigating life's challenges.
    • It teaches us to confront illusions and darkness with strength and righteousness.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to personal growth, stronger relationships, and a clearer understanding of our purpose.
