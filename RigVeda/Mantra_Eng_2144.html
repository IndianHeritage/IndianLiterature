अस्मभ्यं तद वसो दानाय राधः सम अर्थयस्व बहु ते वसव्यं |इन्द्र यच चित्रं श्रवस्याऽनु dyūn बृहद् वदेम विदथे सुवीराः ||
asmabhyaṁ tad vaso dānāya rādhaḥ sam arthayasva bahu te vasavyam | indra yac citraṁ śravasyā anu dyūn bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmabhyaṁ: for us
    • tad: that
    • vaso: of wealth
    • dānāya: for giving
    • rādhaḥ: prosperity
    • sam: together
    • arthayasva: may you bestow
    • bahu: many
    • te: your
    • vasavyam: wealth
  • Indra: the deity representing strength and power
  • yac: whatever
  • citraṁ: beautiful or splendid
  • śravasyā: of fame
  • anu: after
  • dyūn: the heavens
  • bṛhad: great
  • vadema: we may speak
  • vidathe: in the assembly
  • suvīrāḥ: of the brave

  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for abundance and prosperity, asking for wealth to be bestowed upon the community.
    • It emphasizes the importance of collective well-being, suggesting that wealth should be shared and used for the benefit of all.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies the desire for strength and support from divine forces in achieving these goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied during festivals or gatherings where resources are pooled together for common welfare.
    • In business, it can inspire leaders to focus on corporate social responsibility, ensuring that profits are shared with employees and the community.
    • In personal life, it encourages individuals to practice generosity and share their wealth with those in need, fostering a sense of unity and support.

  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for collective prosperity and the sharing of resources.
    • It teaches the value of community and the importance of supporting one another in achieving success.
    • In today's world, where individualism often prevails, this mantra serves as a reminder of the strength found in unity and collaboration.
