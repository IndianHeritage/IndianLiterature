प्र ते धाराऽत्य अण्वानि मेष्यः पुनानस्य संयतो यान्ति रंहयः |यद गोभिर इन्दो चम्वोः समज्यस आ सुवानः सोम कलशेषु सीदसि ||
pra te dhārā aty aṇvāni meṣyaḥ punānasya saṁyato yanti raṁhayaḥ | yad gobhir indo camvoḥ samajyasa ā suvānaḥ soma kalaśeṣu sīdasi ||
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "May the streams of your abundance flow towards me, as the swift-moving waters of the rivers. Just as the cows yield their milk, may the Soma, the divine nectar, reside in the vessels."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra te dhārā: "May your streams" - This refers to the flow of abundance and prosperity.
    • aty aṇvāni: "very swift" - Emphasizes the speed and efficiency of the blessings being sought.
    • meṣyaḥ: "may come to me" - A plea for the blessings to be directed towards the speaker.
    • punānasya: "of the rejuvenating" - Suggests a renewal or revitalization of life and resources.
    • saṁyato yanti: "controlled and directed" - Indicates the importance of intention and focus in receiving blessings.
    • raṁhayaḥ: "prosperity" - Refers to wealth and abundance in various forms.
    • yad gobhir: "as the cows" - Cows are symbols of nourishment and wealth in Vedic culture.
    • indo camvoḥ: "of the divine" - Refers to the divine essence or energy that sustains life.
    • samajyasa: "united" - Suggests harmony and unity in the flow of blessings.
    • ā suvānaḥ: "may reside" - A wish for the blessings to settle and manifest in one's life.
    • soma kalaśeṣu: "in the vessels of Soma" - Soma represents divine nectar, symbolizing spiritual and material nourishment.
    • sīdasi: "you reside" - Acknowledges the presence of divine blessings in one's life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be invoked by entrepreneurs seeking prosperity and success. For instance, a startup founder may recite this mantra to attract investors and resources, ensuring that their venture flourishes.
    • In personal life, individuals may use this mantra during rituals or meditations to invite abundance and positivity into their lives, such as during a housewarming ceremony to bless their new home with prosperity.
    • In agricultural practices, farmers may chant this mantra to invoke the blessings of nature for a bountiful harvest, symbolizing the connection between spiritual practices and material success.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of abundance, prosperity, and divine blessings. It emphasizes the importance of intention and focus in attracting positive energies into one's life. In real-life scenarios, whether in business, personal growth, or community well-being, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of spiritual and material realms. By aligning oneself with the flow of abundance, individuals can cultivate a life filled with prosperity and fulfillment.
