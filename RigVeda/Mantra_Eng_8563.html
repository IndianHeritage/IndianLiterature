मन्त्र: स्तोत्रे राये हरिरर्षा पुनान इन्द्रं मदो गच्छतु ते भराय | देवैर्याहि सरथं राधो अच्छा यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
stotre rāye harir arṣā punāna indram mado gacchatu te bharāya | devair yāhi sarathaṁ rādho acchā yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • stotre rāye: This phrase can be translated as "in praise of wealth" or "in the context of offerings." It signifies the importance of expressing gratitude and reverence towards the divine for the abundance one receives in life.
  • harir arṣā: "Harir" refers to the divine or the Lord, often associated with Vishnu, while "arṣā" can mean "to be exalted" or "to rise." This suggests that the divine presence elevates our existence and brings prosperity.
  • punāna indram: "Punāna" means "purifying" or "cleansing," and "indram" refers to Indra, the king of gods. This indicates a call for divine purification and blessings from Indra, who is often associated with rain and thunder, essential for agriculture and sustenance.
  • mado gacchatu te bharāya: This translates to "may the intoxication of joy come to you." It emphasizes the idea of joy and abundance flowing into our lives, suggesting that happiness is a divine gift that should be welcomed.
  • devair yāhi sarathaṁ: "Devair" means "by the gods," and "yāhi sarathaṁ" can be interpreted as "go with the chariots." This implies a journey or movement guided by divine forces, encouraging individuals to seek divine support in their endeavors.
  • rādho acchā: "Rādho" refers to the act of moving forward or progressing, while "acchā" means "good" or "well." This suggests that the path we take, with divine guidance, leads to positive outcomes.
  • yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ: "Yūyam" means "you all," "pāta" translates to "protect," and "svastibhiḥ" refers to "well-being" or "auspiciousness." This is a prayer for the protection and well-being of all, highlighting the communal aspect of seeking divine blessings.
  • sadā naḥ: This means "always for us," indicating a desire for continuous divine presence and support in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message of seeking divine blessings for prosperity, joy, and protection. It emphasizes the importance of gratitude and the communal aspect of well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during rituals, prayers, or even daily affirmations where individuals seek to connect with the divine for guidance and support. It serves as a reminder that by acknowledging the divine in our lives, we can cultivate a sense of abundance and joy, fostering a positive environment for ourselves and those around us.
