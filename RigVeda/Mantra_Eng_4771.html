अयं द्यावापृथिवी विष्कभायद अयं रथम अयुनक सप्तरश्मिम् |अयं गोषु शच्याऽपक्वम अन्तः सोमो दाधार दशयन्त्रं उत्सम् ||
ayaṁ dyāvāpṛthivī vi ṣkabhāyad ayaṁ ratham ayunak saptaraśmim | ayaṁ goṣu śacyā pakvam antaḥ somo dādhāra daśayantram utsam ||
  • ayaṁ dyāvāpṛthivī vi ṣkabhāyad: This phrase translates to "This is the connection between the sky and the earth." It signifies the unity of the cosmos, where the heavens (dyava) and the earth (pṛthivī) are intertwined. In real life, this can be seen in the balance of nature, where the sky provides rain and sunlight essential for life on earth.
  • ayaṁ ratham ayunak saptaraśmim: Here, "This chariot is drawn by the seven rays." The "seven rays" can be interpreted as the seven colors of the rainbow or the seven days of the week, symbolizing the passage of time and the journey of life. In practical terms, this can relate to how we navigate our lives, with each day bringing new opportunities and challenges.
  • ayaṁ goṣu śacyā pakvam antaḥ: This means "This is the strength within the cows." Cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and nourishment in many cultures. The strength within them can be likened to the sustenance they provide, which is crucial for agricultural societies. In modern life, this can be related to the importance of sustainable farming and animal husbandry.
  • somo dādhāra daśayantram utsam: This translates to "The moon supports the tenfold creation." The moon is often associated with cycles, emotions, and the passage of time. The "tenfold creation" can refer to the various aspects of life and existence. In real life, this can be seen in how the lunar cycle affects tides, agriculture, and even human behavior.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the interconnectedness of the universe, emphasizing the balance between the sky and earth, the journey of life, the importance of sustenance, and the cyclical nature of existence. Its significance lies in reminding us of our place within the cosmos and the need for harmony in our lives. In practical scenarios, this can be applied in environmental conservation, sustainable living, and recognizing the importance of natural cycles in our daily routines.
