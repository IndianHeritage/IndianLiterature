रैभ्य आसीद अनुदेयी नाराशंसि न्योचनि |सूर्यायाः भद्रमिद् वासो गाथयैति परिष्कृतम् ||
raibhy āsīd anudeyī nārāśaṁsī nyocanī | sūryāyā bhadram id vāso gāthayaiti pariṣkṛtam ||
  • raibhy āsīd: This phrase can be interpreted as "from the wise" or "from the learned." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in guiding one's actions and decisions.
  • anudeyī: This term refers to "the one who is not to be disregarded" or "the one who is always present." It signifies the constant presence of wisdom and guidance in our lives.
  • nārāśaṁsī: This word can be understood as "the one who is benevolent" or "the one who brings good fortune." It highlights the role of benevolence and kindness in our interactions with others.
  • nyocanī: This term means "the one who brings forth" or "the one who reveals." It suggests the idea of enlightenment and the unveiling of truth.
  • sūryāyā: This word translates to "to the Sun" or "to the solar energy." The Sun is often seen as a symbol of life, energy, and vitality in many cultures.
  • bhadram: This term means "auspicious" or "good." It indicates the desire for positive outcomes and well-being.
  • id vāso: This phrase can be interpreted as "this dwelling" or "this place." It refers to the environment or the space we inhabit.
  • gāthayaiti: This term means "to sing" or "to chant." It emphasizes the importance of expression and communication in sharing wisdom and experiences.
  • pariṣkṛtam: This word translates to "refined" or "purified." It signifies the process of improvement and the pursuit of excellence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, benevolence, and the pursuit of auspiciousness in our lives. It teaches us that knowledge and kindness are essential in navigating our existence. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in education, where the wisdom of teachers guides students, or in community service, where acts of kindness foster goodwill. By embracing these principles, we can create a more harmonious and enlightened society, reflecting the values of the mantra in our daily interactions and decisions.
