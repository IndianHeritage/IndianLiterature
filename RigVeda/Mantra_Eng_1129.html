विष्वाहेन्द्रो अधिवक्तā नो अस्त्व अपरिह्वृताः सनुयाम वाजम् |तन नो मित्रो वरुणो मामहन्ताम् अदितिः सिन्धुः पृथिवी उता द्यौः ||
viśvāhendro adhivaktā no astv aparihvṛtāḥ sanuyāma vājam | tan no mitro varuṇo māmahantām aditiḥ sindhuḥ pṛthivī uta dyauḥ ||
  • Translation: "May the universal Lord, who is the speaker of truth, protect us from all that is unworthy. May we be blessed with strength and nourishment from Mitra and Varuna, the great deities, and may Aditi, the infinite, bless us with the waters of the ocean, the earth, and the sky."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viśvāhendro: Universal Lord, the one who encompasses all.
    • adhivaktā: The speaker or proclaimer of truth.
    • no astv: May there be for us.
    • aparihṛtāḥ: Free from all that is unworthy or harmful.
    • sanuyāma: Bestowing strength and nourishment.
    • vājam: Power or energy.
    • tan: That (which is desired).
    • no: Our.
    • mitro: Mitra, the deity of friendship and harmony.
    • varuṇo: Varuna, the deity of cosmic order and water.
    • māmahantām: May we be blessed with greatness.
    • aditiḥ: Aditi, the infinite mother of all gods.
    • sindhuḥ: The ocean or waters.
    • pṛthivī: The earth.
    • uta: And.
    • dyauḥ: The sky or heavens.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking protection from negative influences in our lives. For instance, in a workplace setting, one might face toxic relationships or environments. Reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek positive interactions and maintain a harmonious atmosphere.
    • The invocation of Mitra and Varuna highlights the significance of friendship and cosmic order. In personal relationships, fostering trust and understanding can lead to stronger bonds, much like the relationship between these deities.
    • Aditi represents the infinite possibilities and nurturing aspects of life. In times of uncertainty, such as during career changes or personal challenges, this mantra can inspire individuals to embrace new opportunities and trust in the abundance of the universe.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for protection, strength, and nourishment from the divine. It serves as a reminder to seek harmony in our relationships and to trust in the infinite possibilities that life offers. In real-life scenarios, whether facing challenges at work, nurturing personal relationships, or navigating life's uncertainties, this mantra encourages individuals to cultivate positivity and embrace the support of the universe. By invoking the energies of Mitra, Varuna, and Aditi, one can foster a sense of security and abundance in their life journey.
