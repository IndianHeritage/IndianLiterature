आ दैव्याणि व्रता चिकीत्वान आ मानवस्य जनस्य जन्म ||
ā daivyāni vratā cikitvān ā mānuṣasya janasya janma ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā: A prefix meaning "from" or "out of."
    • daivyāni: Derived from "daivi," meaning "divine" or "heavenly."
    • vratā: Refers to "vows" or "resolutions."
    • cikitvān: Means "having considered" or "thoughtful."
    • ā: Again, "from" or "out of."
    • mānuṣasya: Pertaining to "human" or "mankind."
    • janasya: Meaning "of the people" or "of the community."
    • janma: Refers to "birth" or "origin."
  • Translation: "From the divine resolutions, having considered, comes the birth of humanity."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine guidance and thoughtful consideration in the process of human existence.
    • It suggests that our lives and the community we build are influenced by higher principles and the resolutions we make.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, a thoughtful leader considers the well-being of their community, making decisions that reflect divine or ethical principles.
    • In personal life, individuals often reflect on their values and make resolutions (like New Year’s resolutions) that guide their actions and shape their character.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of divine influence and human action.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as decision-making, leadership, and personal growth.
    • By recognizing the divine aspects of our resolutions, we can strive to lead more meaningful and impactful lives.
