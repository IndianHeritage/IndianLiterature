प्रातः सुतम अपिबो हर्यश्व मध्यं दिनं सवनं केवलं ते |सम ऋभुभिः पिबस्व रत्नधेबिः सखीं याम् इन्द्र चक्रिषे सुखृत्या ||
prātaḥ sutam apibo haryaśva mādhyaṁdinaṁ savanaṁ kevalaṁ te | sam ṛbhubhiḥ pibasva ratnadhebhiḥ sakhīm̐r yām̐ indra cakṛṣe sukṛtyā ||
  • Mantra: "prātaḥ sutam apibo haryaśva mādhyaṁdinaṁ savanaṁ kevalaṁ te | sam ṛbhubhiḥ pibasva ratnadhebhiḥ sakhīm̐r yām̐ indra cakṛṣe sukṛtyā ||"
  • Translation: "In the morning, O Indra, drink the sweet nectar of the offerings, the midday libation, which is exclusively yours. With the seers, drink the precious offerings, O friend, which you have created through good deeds."

  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prātaḥ: morning
    • sutam: nectar or drink
    • apibo: drink
    • haryaśva: O Indra, the powerful
    • mādhyaṁdinaṁ: midday
    • savanaṁ: offering or libation
    • kevalaṁ: exclusively
    • te: yours
    • sam ṛbhubhiḥ: with the seers
    • pibasva: drink
    • ratnadhebhiḥ: precious offerings
    • sakhīm̐r: O friend
    • yām̐: which
    • indra: O Indra
    • cakṛṣe: you have created
    • sukṛtyā: through good deeds

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, asking him to partake in the offerings made during the morning and midday rituals.
    • The mention of "nectar" symbolizes the essence of life and prosperity, akin to how we seek nourishment and sustenance in our daily lives.
    • By invoking Indra to drink the offerings, the mantra emphasizes the importance of divine blessings in our endeavors, much like how we seek support from mentors or leaders in our personal and professional lives.
    • The phrase "with the seers" highlights the role of wise individuals or sages in guiding us, similar to how we rely on teachers or experienced individuals for wisdom and direction.
    • The concept of "good deeds" reflects the idea that our actions have consequences, and by performing righteous acts, we can attract positive outcomes, much like how ethical behavior in the workplace can lead to career advancement.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and blessings in our daily lives, particularly in our actions and offerings.
    • It teaches us to value the contributions of wise individuals and to engage in good deeds, which can lead to prosperity and success.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during important decisions, rituals, or when seeking inspiration from mentors, reminding us to act with integrity and purpose.
