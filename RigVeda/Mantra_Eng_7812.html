जुष्ट इन्द्राय मत्सरः पवमान कणिक्रदत् |विश्वा अप द्विषो जहि ||
juṣṭa indrāya matsaraḥ pavamāna kanikradat | viśvā apa dviṣo jahi ||
  • juṣṭa: This word translates to "pleasing" or "delightful." It signifies something that is enjoyable or brings satisfaction.
  • indrāya: Referring to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, this term embodies power, strength, and authority.
  • matsaraḥ: This means "envy" or "jealousy." It highlights negative emotions that can arise in human interactions.
  • pavamāna: This word means "purifying" or "the one who purifies." It is often associated with the wind or air, symbolizing cleansing and renewal.
  • kanikradat: This term can be interpreted as "the one who roars" or "the one who makes a sound," often linked to the power of nature.
  • viśvā: Meaning "all" or "universal," it signifies inclusiveness and the collective aspect of existence.
  • apa: This translates to "away" or "from," indicating a sense of removal or distancing.
  • dviṣo: This means "enemies" or "those who harbor ill will," referring to negative forces or adversaries.
  • jahi: This is a command form meaning "to overcome" or "to conquer," suggesting an active effort to defeat negativity.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Indra, seeking his strength to overcome envy and negativity. It emphasizes the importance of purifying oneself from harmful emotions and influences, promoting a harmonious existence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Relationships: In situations where jealousy or envy arises among friends or family, invoking this mantra can serve as a reminder to focus on positivity and support rather than competition.
  • Work Environment: In a professional setting, where rivalry may lead to toxic behavior, this mantra encourages individuals to rise above negative feelings and foster a collaborative atmosphere.
  • Mental Well-being: Practicing the essence of this mantra can aid in personal growth by promoting self-reflection and the release of negative emotions, leading to a healthier mindset.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength and purification from negative emotions like envy. By embracing its teachings, individuals can cultivate a more positive and harmonious life, both personally and socially. It serves as a powerful reminder to focus on collective well-being and to actively work against divisive feelings, making it applicable in various aspects of daily life.
