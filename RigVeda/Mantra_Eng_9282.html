जगृभ्मा ते दक्षिणम् इन्द्र हस्तं वसूयवो वसुपते वसूनाम् |विद्मा हि त्वा गोपतिं शूर गोनाम् अस्मभ्यं चित्रं वृषणं रयिं दाह् ||
jagṛbhmā te dakṣiṇam indra hastaṁ vasūyavo vasupate vasūnām | vidmā hi tvā gopatiṁ śūra gonām asmabhyaṁ citraṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ rayiṁ dāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "jagṛbhmā te dakṣiṇam indra hastaṁ vasūyavo vasupate vasūnām | vidmā hi tvā gopatiṁ śūra gonām asmabhyaṁ citraṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ rayiṁ dāḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • jagṛbhmā: We grasp or hold.
    • te: Your.
    • dakṣiṇam: Right or auspicious.
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods.
    • hastaṁ: Hand.
    • vasūyavo: Those who are wealthy or prosperous.
    • vasupate: Lord of wealth.
    • vasūnām: Of the prosperous or wealthy.
    • vidmā: We know.
    • hi: Indeed.
    • tvā: You.
    • gopatiṁ: Lord of the cows (protector).
    • śūra: Brave or valiant.
    • gonām: Of the cattle.
    • asmabhyaṁ: For us.
    • citraṁ: Wonderful or diverse.
    • vṛṣaṇaṁ: Strong or powerful.
    • rayiṁ: Wealth or prosperity.
    • dāḥ: Give or grant.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of gods, asking for his blessings and support.
    • The phrase "jagṛbhmā te dakṣiṇam indra hastaṁ" signifies the act of grasping or receiving the auspicious hand of Indra, which symbolizes divine assistance.
    • By referring to Indra as "vasupate," the mantra acknowledges him as the lord of wealth, emphasizing the importance of prosperity in life.
    • The invocation of "gopatiṁ" highlights the protective nature of Indra, akin to a shepherd safeguarding his flock, which can be related to leaders or guardians in our lives.
    • The mantra culminates in a request for "citraṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ rayiṁ dāḥ," asking for diverse and powerful wealth, which can be interpreted as a desire for not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional richness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be recited by entrepreneurs seeking divine guidance and prosperity in their ventures.
    • In personal life, individuals may use this mantra during times of financial uncertainty, invoking Indra's blessings for stability and abundance.
    • Communities may come together to chant this mantra during festivals or gatherings, reinforcing their collective hopes for prosperity and protection.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of divine support and human effort in achieving prosperity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance from higher powers while also recognizing our own roles in creating wealth and abundance.
    • In essence, it teaches us to be brave and proactive in our pursuits while remaining open to the blessings that come from the divine.
