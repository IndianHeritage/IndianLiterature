मन्त्र: अन्यā वो अन्याम् अवत्व् अन्यान्यस्य उपावत् |ताः सर्वाः संविदानाः इदं मे प्रावतā वचः ||
anyā vo anyām avatv anyānyasyā upāvata | tāḥ sarvāḥ saṁvidānā idam me prāvatā vacaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • anyā: other, different
    • vo: your
    • anyām: another
    • avatv: may it protect
    • anyānyasyā: of others
    • upāvata: may it come near
    • tāḥ: those
    • sarvāḥ: all
    • saṁvidānā: knowing, understanding
    • idam: this
    • me: my
    • prāvatā: may it be spoken
    • vacaḥ: words
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and understanding among individuals.
    • It suggests that different people should protect and support each other, fostering a sense of community.
    • The phrase "may it protect" indicates a desire for mutual safeguarding, which is essential in any relationship or society.
    • The call for "words" to be spoken implies the significance of communication in building connections and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, this mantra can be applied by encouraging teamwork and collaboration, where colleagues support each other’s ideas and projects.
    • In a family setting, it can mean fostering an environment where each member feels valued and protected, leading to stronger familial bonds.
    • In community service, it reflects the idea of helping those in need, where individuals come together to uplift others, creating a supportive network.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, protection, and understanding among individuals.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as workplaces, families, and communities, where collaboration and support are essential for growth and harmony.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more compassionate and connected society.
