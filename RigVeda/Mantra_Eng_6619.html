अस्मभ्यं सु वृषण्वसू यातं वर्तिर नृपाय्यम् |विशुद्रुहेव यज्ञं ऊहथुर गिरा ||
asmabhyaṁ su vṛṣaṇvasū yātaṁ vartir nṛpāyyam | viṣudruheva yajñam ūhathur girā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmabhyaṁ: for us
    • su: good, auspicious
    • vṛṣaṇvasū: the one who is strong and powerful
    • yātaṁ: may it come
    • vartir: existence, sustenance
    • nṛpāyyam: for the rulers or leaders
    • viṣudruheva: like a pure offering
    • yajñam: sacrifice, worship
    • ūhathur: they have invoked
    • girā: with words, through speech
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer for the well-being and prosperity of the community.
    • It emphasizes the importance of strong leadership and the role of auspiciousness in governance.
    • The invocation of purity in offerings suggests that actions taken should be sincere and beneficial.
    • It highlights the power of words and communication in manifesting intentions and desires.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders can use this mantra to inspire their teams, focusing on strong leadership and positive communication to achieve collective goals.
    • In community gatherings, invoking this mantra can serve as a reminder for members to work together harmoniously for the common good.
    • During festivals or rituals, this mantra can be recited to ensure that the offerings made are pure and that the intentions behind them are noble.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and purity in actions.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from leadership in organizations to community building and spiritual practices.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and groups can foster a more harmonious and prosperous environment.
