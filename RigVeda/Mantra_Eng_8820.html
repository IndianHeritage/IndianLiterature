प्र ते यक्षि प्र त इयर्मि मनमा भुवो यथा वन्द्यो नो हवेषु | धन्वन्न इव प्रपाऽसि त्वम् अग्न इयक्षवे पूरवे प्रत्न राजा न ||
pra te yakṣi pra ta iyarmi manma bhuvo yathā vandyo no haveṣu | dhanvann iva prapā asi tvam agna iyakṣave pūrave pratna rājan ||
  • pra te yakṣi: This phrase can be interpreted as "I offer to you" or "I invoke you." It signifies the act of devotion and the desire to connect with a higher power.
  • pra ta iyarmi: This translates to "may I be led" or "may I be guided." It reflects the human need for guidance and support in life's journey.
  • manma bhuvo yathā: This means "as I think of the world" or "as I contemplate existence." It emphasizes the importance of mindfulness and awareness in our thoughts and actions.
  • vandyo no haveṣu: This can be understood as "may we be worthy of worship" or "may we be honored." It speaks to the aspiration for respect and recognition in society.
  • dhanvann iva prapā asi: This phrase translates to "like a bow, you are the source." It symbolizes strength and protection, akin to how a bow provides defense and support.
  • tvam agna: This means "you, O fire" or "you, O divine light." Fire is often associated with purity, transformation, and energy in various cultures.
  • iyakṣave pūrave: This can be interpreted as "to the one who is the protector" or "to the one who is the source of abundance." It highlights the role of a guardian or provider in our lives.
  • pratna rājan: This translates to "O king of offerings" or "O ruler of sacrifices." It signifies the importance of giving and the value of offerings in spiritual practices.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of devotion, guidance, mindfulness, respect, strength, and the significance of offerings. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek guidance from higher powers, to be mindful of our thoughts and actions, and to honor those who provide support and protection. It encourages us to be worthy of respect and to recognize the importance of giving back to the community. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during personal challenges, in community service, or in spiritual practices, where one seeks connection and guidance from the divine.
