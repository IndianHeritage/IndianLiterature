शतं वेणूञ्च चतं शुनः शतं चर्माणि म्लातानि |शतम् मे बल्बजस्तुकाः अरुषीणां चतुःशतम् ||
śataṁ veṇūñ chataṁ śunaḥ śataṁ carmāṇi mlātāni | śatam me balbajastukā aruṣīṇāṁ catuḥśatam ||
  • śataṁ veṇūñ: This phrase translates to "a hundred flutes." The flute is often associated with music, harmony, and the divine. In real life, music can bring people together, creating a sense of community and joy.
  • chataṁ śunaḥ: This means "a hundred dogs." Dogs symbolize loyalty and companionship. In our lives, they remind us of the importance of friendship and loyalty in our relationships.
  • śataṁ carmāṇi mlātāni: This translates to "a hundred skins that are shed." This can be interpreted as transformation or renewal, akin to how snakes shed their skin. In life, we often go through phases of change and growth, shedding old habits or identities to embrace new ones.
  • śatam me balbajastukā: This means "a hundred of my strong ones." It signifies strength and resilience. In real life, this can relate to the support systems we build, such as family, friends, or mentors who help us through challenges.
  • aruṣīṇāṁ catuḥśatam: This translates to "four hundred of the red ones." The color red often symbolizes energy, passion, and action. In our lives, it can represent the drive and motivation we need to pursue our goals and dreams.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the themes of harmony, loyalty, transformation, strength, and passion. In real life, these elements are crucial for personal growth and community building. By embracing music, nurturing relationships, accepting change, relying on our support systems, and pursuing our passions, we can lead fulfilling lives. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of these aspects and their significance in our daily experiences.
