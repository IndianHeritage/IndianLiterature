सम उप्र यन्ति धीतयः सर्गासोऽवतामिव |क्रतुं नः सोम जीवसे वि वो मदे धारयामसामिव विवक्षसे ||
sam u pra yanti dhītayaḥ sargāso 'vatām̐ iva | kratuṁ naḥ soma jīvase vi vo made dhārayā camasām̐ iva vivakṣase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sam: together, in harmony
    • u: indeed, certainly
    • pra: forward, towards
    • yanti: they go, they proceed
    • dhītayaḥ: thoughts, intellects
    • sargāso: creations, manifestations
    • avatām̐: like the protective, nurturing
    • iva: like, as if
    • kratuṁ: will, intention
    • naḥ: our
    • soma: the divine nectar, often associated with immortality
    • jīvase: for living, for life
    • vi: in, among
    • vo: your
    • made: joy, bliss
    • dhārayā: holding, sustaining
    • camasām̐: of the cups, vessels
    • iva: like, as if
    • vivakṣase: you express, you articulate
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the unity and harmony of thoughts and intentions.
    • It emphasizes the importance of collective progress and the nurturing aspect of creation.
    • The reference to "soma" symbolizes the essence of life and joy, suggesting that our intentions should be aligned with the pursuit of happiness and fulfillment.
    • In real life, this can be seen in teamwork and collaboration, where individuals come together with a shared purpose, much like a group of friends working towards a common goal.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of collective consciousness and the power of shared intentions.
    • It serves as a reminder that our thoughts and actions can create a nurturing environment, much like a community that supports its members.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various scenarios such as workplaces, families, and social groups where collaboration and mutual support lead to greater achievements.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to align their personal goals with the greater good, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
