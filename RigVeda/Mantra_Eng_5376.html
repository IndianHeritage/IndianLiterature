मन्त्र: वोचेमेद इन्द्रं मगवाणं एनं महो रायो राधसो यद ददन नः |यो अर्चतो ब्रह्मकृतिम अविष्टो यूम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
vocemed indram maghavānam enam maho rāyo rādhaso yad dadan naḥ | yo arcato brahmakṛtim aviṣṭho yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Vocemed Indram: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke Indra." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. In real life, invoking Indra can symbolize seeking strength and support in challenging situations, much like how people pray for guidance during difficult times.
  • Maghavānam enam: This means "the powerful one." It refers to Indra's might and ability to overcome obstacles. In everyday life, this can be likened to seeking the strength to face personal challenges, such as overcoming fears or achieving goals.
  • Maho rāyo rādhaso: This translates to "the great wealth of the prosperous." It emphasizes the importance of abundance and prosperity. In a modern context, this can relate to the pursuit of financial stability and success, as well as the wealth of knowledge and relationships that enrich our lives.
  • Yad dadan naḥ: This means "that which you give us." It signifies a request for blessings and support. In real life, this can be seen in the way people seek help from their community or higher powers when embarking on new ventures or facing hardships.
  • Yo arcato: This translates to "who is worshipped." It highlights the importance of reverence and gratitude towards those who provide support and guidance. In contemporary life, this can be reflected in how we honor mentors, leaders, or even family members who have played a significant role in our success.
  • Brahmakṛtim: This means "the creation of the divine." It refers to the idea that everything in existence is a manifestation of a higher power. In practical terms, this can be understood as recognizing the interconnectedness of all life and the divine presence in our daily experiences.
  • Aviṣṭho: This translates to "unobstructed." It signifies the removal of barriers and obstacles. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of clarity and focus in our goals, ensuring that we are not hindered by distractions or negativity.
  • Yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ: This means "may you protect us with auspiciousness." It is a plea for safety and well-being. In modern life, this can be seen in the way people seek positive energy and support from their environment, whether through community, spirituality, or personal practices like meditation.
  • Sadā naḥ: This translates to "always for us." It emphasizes the desire for continuous support and blessings. In everyday life, this can be reflected in the ongoing relationships we cultivate with friends, family, and mentors who provide us with encouragement and love.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for strength, prosperity, and divine support in our lives. It serves as a reminder of the importance of community, reverence, and the pursuit of abundance. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied when facing challenges, seeking guidance, or striving for personal and professional growth. By invoking these principles, individuals can foster a sense of connection to the divine and to one another, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and prosperous life.
