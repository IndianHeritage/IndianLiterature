एवां तं आहुरुत श्रिण्व इन्द्र एको विभक्त तारणिर्मघानाम् |मिथस्तुर उूतयो यस्य पूर्वीर अस्मे भद्राणि सच्चत प्रियाणि ||
evā tam āhur uta śṛṇva indra eko vibhaktā taraṇir maghānām | mithastura ūtayo yasya pūrvīr asme bhadrāṇi saścata priyāṇi ||
  • evā tam āhur uta śṛṇva indra eko vibhaktā taraṇir maghānām
    • evā tam āhur - "Thus they say" or "This is what they declare". This phrase indicates a consensus or a widely accepted belief.
    • uta śṛṇva - "And listen". This is an invitation to pay attention, suggesting that what follows is of great importance.
    • indra eko - "Indra is one". Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizes strength, power, and leadership.
    • vibhaktā taraṇir maghānām - "The one who is divided among the mighty". This suggests that Indra, while being singular, has many manifestations or aspects that can be seen in various forms of strength and abundance.
  • mithastura ūtayo yasya pūrvīr asme bhadrāṇi saścata priyāṇi
    • mithastura ūtayo - "From the midst of the two". This could refer to the balance or harmony between opposing forces or dualities.
    • yasya pūrvīr - "Whose predecessors". This acknowledges the lineage or the wisdom passed down from previous generations.
    • asme bhadrāṇi - "May we have auspiciousness". This is a prayer for blessings and positive outcomes in life.
    • saścata priyāṇi - "And all that is dear". This emphasizes the importance of relationships and the things we hold dear in our lives.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the unity of strength and the importance of listening to wisdom that has been passed down through generations. It highlights the significance of balance in life, the power of leadership, and the blessings that come from recognizing what is dear to us. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • In leadership roles, understanding that true strength comes from unity and the ability to listen to diverse perspectives.
  • In personal relationships, valuing the connections we have and seeking auspiciousness in our interactions.
  • In community settings, fostering harmony between differing opinions and recognizing the wisdom of our predecessors to guide our actions.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek strength in unity, to listen to the wisdom of the past, and to cherish the relationships that enrich our lives.
