कुविच चकत कुवित करत कुविन नो वश्यसस करत |कुवित पतिद्विषो यतीर इन्द्रेण संगमामहै ||
kuvic chakat kuvit karat kuvin no vasyasas karat | kuvit patidviṣo yatīr indreṇa saṁgamāmahai ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • kuvic: This term can be interpreted as "to be aware" or "to know." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding.
    • chakat: This word refers to "the wheel" or "the cycle," symbolizing the cyclical nature of life and the universe.
    • karat: Meaning "to do" or "to act," it highlights the necessity of action in the pursuit of knowledge and understanding.
    • kvin: This term can be understood as "to be free" or "to liberate," suggesting the importance of freedom in thought and action.
    • vasyasas: This word implies "control" or "mastery," indicating the need to master one's desires and actions.
    • patidviṣo: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is hostile" or "the adversary," representing challenges or obstacles in life.
    • yatīr: Meaning "the seeker" or "the one who strives," it refers to individuals who are on a quest for truth and understanding.
    • indreṇa: This word refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power, strength, and authority.
    • saṁgamāmahai: This term means "we come together" or "we unite," emphasizing the importance of unity and collaboration.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the importance of awareness and knowledge in our actions.
    • It suggests that to achieve mastery over our lives, we must act with intention and understanding.
    • The reference to "the wheel" signifies the cyclical nature of existence, reminding us that our actions have consequences that may return to us.
    • By acknowledging adversities and striving for truth, we can overcome challenges with the strength and guidance of higher powers, represented by Indra.
    • Ultimately, the mantra calls for unity in our pursuits, encouraging collaboration and collective effort in the quest for knowledge and mastery.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied to teamwork. When team members are aware of their roles and responsibilities (kuvic) and act collaboratively (saṁgamāmahai), they can overcome challenges (patidviṣo) and achieve their goals more effectively.
    • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their actions (karat) and strive for self-mastery (vasyasas) by being aware of their thoughts and behaviors, leading to personal growth and liberation (kvin).
    • In education, students who seek knowledge (yatīr) and understand the cyclical nature of learning (chakat) can better navigate their academic journeys, recognizing that challenges are part of the process.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of awareness, action, and unity in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that knowledge and understanding are essential for personal and collective growth.
    • By applying the principles of this mantra in various aspects of life, individuals can foster a sense of mastery and collaboration, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
