इमं इन्द्र गवाशिरं यवाशिरं च नः पिब |आगत्यā वृषभिः सुतम् ||
imam indra gavāśiraṁ yavāśiraṁ ca naḥ piba | āgatyā vṛṣabhiḥ sutam ||
  • Mantra: "imam indra gavāśiraṁ yavāśiraṁ ca naḥ piba | āgatyā vṛṣabhiḥ sutam ||"
  • Translation: "O Indra, drink this offering of ours, both the milk of the cows and the barley. Come to us with your powerful sons."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imam: this
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • gavāśiraṁ: the milk of the cows
    • yavāśiraṁ: the barley
    • ca: and
    • naḥ: our
    • piba: drink
    • āgatyā: coming
    • vṛṣabhiḥ: with powerful sons
    • sutam: the offering
  • Context and Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, who is often associated with strength, rain, and the fertility of the earth.
    • The mention of "milk of the cows" and "barley" signifies the importance of agriculture and livestock in ancient Indian society, which is still relevant today.
    • By invoking Indra, the speaker seeks blessings for prosperity, abundance, and the well-being of their community.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Farmers today still pray for rain and good harvests, similar to how ancient people would invoke Indra for agricultural success.
    • In many cultures, offerings are made to deities for blessings on crops and livestock, reflecting the ongoing human connection to nature and the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine support for sustenance and prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of human efforts, nature, and the divine, applicable in various aspects of life, including agriculture, community well-being, and spiritual practices.
