तद अग्नि द्युम्नम् आ भर यत सासहत सदने कं चिद अत्रिणम् |मन्युः जनस्य दूद्यः ||
tad agne dyumnam ā bhara yat sāsahat sadane kaṁ cid atriṇam | manyuṁ janasya dūḍhyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • dyumnam: glory or brilliance
    • ā bhara: may you bring or bestow
    • yat: which
    • sāsahat: is powerful or strong
    • sadane: in the abode or dwelling
    • kaṁ cid: any or whoever
    • atriṇam: of the one who is worthy or deserving
    • manyuṁ: anger or wrath
    • janasya: of the people or community
    • dūḍhyaḥ: may it be removed or dispelled
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for the bestowal of glory and strength.
    • It emphasizes the importance of dispelling anger and negativity from the community.
    • In a broader sense, it reflects the desire for harmony and prosperity within society.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied when teams face conflicts. By invoking the spirit of Agni, team members can seek to dispel anger and foster a collaborative environment.
    • In community gatherings, leaders can use this mantra to promote unity and encourage individuals to focus on collective goals rather than personal grievances.
    • During family disputes, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to let go of anger and work towards understanding and reconciliation.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength and brilliance, not just in individual pursuits but in the collective spirit of the community.
    • It teaches the importance of overcoming negative emotions like anger, which can hinder progress and harmony.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder to seek unity, strength, and positivity in our interactions with others.
