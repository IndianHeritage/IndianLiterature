प्र रुद्रेण ययिनां यान्ति सिन्धवस् तिरो महीम अरमतिं दधान्विरे |येभिः परिज्मा पर्यन्न उरु ज्रयो वि रोरुवज जटहरे विश्वं उक्षते ||
pra rudreṇa yayinā yanti sindhavas tiro mahīm aramatiṁ dadhanvire | yebhiḥ parijmā pariyann uru jrayo vi roruvaj jaṭhare viśvam ukṣate ||
  • pra rudreṇa: This phrase can be translated as "with the power of Rudra." Rudra is often associated with storm and wind, representing the forces of nature. In real life, this can be seen in how we harness natural elements for energy, such as wind turbines that convert wind energy into electricity.
  • yayinā yanti sindhavas: This means "the rivers that flow." Rivers are vital for life, providing water for drinking, agriculture, and transportation. For example, the Ganges River in India is not only a source of water but also holds spiritual significance for millions.
  • tiro mahīm aramatiṁ dadhanvire: This translates to "they who traverse the vastness." This can refer to the journey of life and the exploration of the unknown. In modern terms, this could relate to space exploration, where humanity seeks to understand the universe beyond our planet.
  • yebhiḥ parijmā pariyann: This phrase means "with which they encompass." It signifies the interconnectedness of all things. In real life, this can be seen in ecosystems where every organism plays a role in maintaining balance, such as bees pollinating flowers, which in turn produce fruits and seeds.
  • uru jrayo vi roruvaj: This can be interpreted as "the great strength that roars." This reflects the power of nature and the strength found in unity. For instance, communities coming together during natural disasters to support each other exemplify this strength.
  • jaṭhare viśvam ukṣate: This translates to "the universe is nourished." It emphasizes the idea of sustenance and care for the world around us. In practical terms, this can relate to sustainable practices, such as organic farming, which nourishes the earth while providing food for people.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of interconnectedness, strength, and the nurturing of the universe. Its significance lies in the reminder that we are all part of a larger whole, and our actions impact the world around us. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as environmental conservation efforts, community support during crises, and the pursuit of knowledge and exploration. By recognizing our connection to nature and each other, we can foster a more harmonious existence.
