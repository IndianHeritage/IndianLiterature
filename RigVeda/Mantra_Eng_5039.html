मन्त्र: भद्रā ददृक्ष उर्वियā वि भास्युत ते शोचिर भानवो द्यां अपप्तन |आविर वक्षः कृतुṣe शुम्भमानोषो देवी रोचमानā महोभिः ||
bhadrā dadṛkṣa urviyā vi bhāsy ut te śocir bhānavo dyām apaptan | āvir vakṣaḥ kṛṇuṣe śumbhamānoṣo devi rocamānā mahobhiḥ ||
  • bhadrā: This word translates to "auspicious" or "good." It signifies the importance of positive and beneficial outcomes in life.
  • dadṛkṣa: Meaning "to see" or "to perceive," it emphasizes the act of observation and awareness in our surroundings.
  • urviyā: This term refers to "the vastness" or "the expanse," indicating the limitless nature of the universe and our experiences within it.
  • vi bhāsy: Translated as "may it shine," this phrase suggests the illumination of knowledge and wisdom, encouraging enlightenment.
  • ut te: Meaning "to you," it signifies a direct address, often invoking a divine presence or higher power.
  • śocir: This word means "radiant" or "brilliant," highlighting the importance of clarity and brightness in thoughts and actions.
  • bhānavo: Referring to "the heavens" or "the celestial," it connects the earthly experience with the divine or cosmic order.
  • dyām: This translates to "the sky" or "the atmosphere," symbolizing the vastness and the infinite possibilities that lie above us.
  • apaptan: Meaning "to attain" or "to reach," it signifies the journey towards achieving goals and aspirations.
  • āvir: This word means "to manifest" or "to appear," indicating the emergence of ideas, thoughts, or divine presence.
  • vakṣaḥ: Referring to "the heart" or "the center," it symbolizes the core of our being and emotions.
  • kṛṇuṣe: Meaning "to create" or "to make," it emphasizes the act of bringing forth new ideas or realities.
  • śumbhamānoṣo: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is praised," indicating the importance of recognition and appreciation.
  • devi: Meaning "goddess," it invokes the feminine divine energy, representing nurturing and wisdom.
  • rocamānā: This translates to "shining" or "radiant," reinforcing the theme of illumination and brilliance.
  • mahobhiḥ: Meaning "with great power" or "with strength," it signifies the importance of strength in our endeavors.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of auspiciousness, awareness, and the pursuit of knowledge. It encourages individuals to seek enlightenment and to recognize the divine presence in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during important decisions, rituals, or moments of reflection. By invoking these qualities, individuals can strive for a life filled with positivity, clarity, and strength, ultimately leading to personal growth and fulfillment.
