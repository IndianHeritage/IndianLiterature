कयā तच्छृण्वे शच्यā शचिष्ठो यया कृत्नोति मुहु काचिदृष्वः |पुरु दाशुषे विचयिष्ठो अंहोऽथा दधाति द्रविणं जारित्रे ||
kayā tac chṛṇve śacyā śaciṣṭho yayā kṛṇoti muhu kā cid ṛṣvaḥ | puru dāśuṣe vicayiṣṭho aṁho 'thā dadhāti draviṇaṁ jaritre ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kayā: with what
    • tac: that
    • chṛṇve: we hear
    • śacyā: with power
    • śaciṣṭho: the one who is capable
    • yayā: by which
    • kṛṇoti: one accomplishes
    • muhu: repeatedly
    • kā cid: any
    • ṛṣvaḥ: noble
    • puru: many
    • dāśuṣe: for the giver
    • vicayiṣṭho: the one who is most capable
    • aṁho: indeed
    • 'thā: thus
    • dadhāti: bestows
    • draviṇaṁ: wealth
    • jaritre: for the one who is strong
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of hearing and understanding.
    • It emphasizes the importance of capability and strength in achieving goals.
    • The repeated effort and noble intentions lead to the bestowal of wealth and resources.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team that listens to feedback and learns from it is more likely to succeed.
    • In personal relationships, understanding and communication can strengthen bonds and lead to mutual support.
    • In education, students who actively engage and seek knowledge repeatedly tend to excel academically.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the power of listening, learning, and the importance of capability in achieving success.
    • It teaches us that wealth, whether material or spiritual, is a result of our efforts and noble intentions.
    • This mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, including personal growth, professional development, and interpersonal relationships.
