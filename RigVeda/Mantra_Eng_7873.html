मन्त्र: एता उ त्ये अवीवशन काष्ठां वाजिनो अक्रत | सतः प्रासाविषुर मती ||
eta u tye avīvaśan kāṣṭhāṁ vājino akrata | sataḥ prāsāviṣur matim ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eta: this
    • u: indeed
    • tye: you (plural)
    • avīvaśan: have not been deprived
    • kāṣṭhāṁ: of wood (or resources)
    • vājino: of the strong or powerful
    • akrata: have not created
    • sataḥ: from the truth or the good
    • prāsāviṣur: have produced or generated
    • matim: intellect or understanding
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of abundance and the importance of not being deprived of essential resources.
    • It emphasizes the role of strength and power in creation and the necessity of aligning with truth to generate wisdom and understanding.
    • In a broader sense, it suggests that when one is connected to their resources and acts from a place of truth, they can cultivate a deeper understanding and insight.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, a company that operates with transparency and integrity (truth) is more likely to innovate and succeed (generate understanding) than one that does not.
    • In personal relationships, being open and honest (not deprived of truth) fosters stronger connections and deeper understanding between individuals.
    • In environmental practices, utilizing natural resources wisely (not being deprived of wood) while respecting nature leads to sustainable living and a better quality of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the importance of truth and resourcefulness in our lives.
    • It teaches us that by aligning our actions with truth and utilizing our resources wisely, we can cultivate wisdom and understanding.
    • This mantra is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal growth, professional integrity, and sustainable living, encouraging us to create a balanced and fulfilling existence.
