मन्त्र: असपत्ना सपत्नघ्नī जयन्त्य अभिभूवारि |आवृक्षम् अन्यासां वर्चो राधो अस्तेयसाम् इव ||
asapatnā sapatnaghnī jayanty abhibhūvarī | āvṛkṣam anyāsāṁ varco rādho astheyasām iva ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asapatnā: Without enemies or rivals
    • sapatnaghnī: One who destroys enemies
    • jayanty: They conquer or triumph
    • abhibhūvarī: The one who is powerful and supreme
    • āvṛkṣam: The tree or the source
    • anyāsāṁ: Of others
    • varco: Light or brilliance
    • rādho: To be nourished or supported
    • astheyasām: Of those who are unyielding or steadfast
    • iva: Like or as
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a state of being where one is free from rivals and enemies, suggesting a sense of peace and security.
    • The phrase "sapatnaghnī" indicates the ability to overcome challenges and adversaries, highlighting the importance of strength and resilience.
    • The term "abhibhūvarī" refers to a supreme power, which can be interpreted as a divine force or inner strength that guides one through difficulties.
    • The mention of "āvṛkṣam" symbolizes a source of nourishment and support, akin to a tree that provides shelter and sustenance.
    • The idea of "varco" and "rādho" emphasizes the importance of light and nourishment, suggesting that one should seek knowledge and support from others.
    • Finally, "astheyasām iva" implies that those who are steadfast and unyielding are akin to this source of strength and brilliance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a team that works harmoniously without internal conflicts can be seen as embodying the essence of "asapatnā." This unity allows them to achieve their goals effectively.
    • Individuals who face challenges in life, such as overcoming personal struggles or adversities, can draw strength from the concept of "sapatnaghnī," using their resilience to conquer difficulties.
    • In times of crisis, people often turn to their inner strength or seek support from loved ones, reflecting the idea of "abhibhūvarī" as a guiding force.
    • Community support systems, like local charities or support groups, can be likened to "āvṛkṣam," providing nourishment and assistance to those in need.
    • Seeking knowledge and wisdom from mentors or educators aligns with "varco" and "rādho," as they illuminate the path forward and help individuals grow.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, unity, and support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that one can achieve greatness and peace by fostering resilience, seeking knowledge, and relying on supportive relationships.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, harmonious relationships, and a fulfilling life.
