वहन्तु त्वा मनोयुजो युक्तासो नवतिर नव |वायव आ चन्द्रेण रथेन याहि सुतस्य पीतये ||
vahantu tvā manoyujo yuktāso navatir nava | vāyav ā candreṇa rathena yāhi sutasya pītaye ||
  • Vahantu: This word translates to "may they carry" or "may they bring." It signifies a request for support or assistance.
  • Tvā: This means "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • Manoyujo: This term can be broken down into "mano" (mind) and "yuj" (to join or unite). It refers to the alignment of thoughts and intentions.
  • Yuktāso: This word means "those who are united" or "those who are connected," suggesting a sense of community or collective effort.
  • Navatir nava: "Navatir" means "new" or "fresh," and "nava" also means "new." Together, they emphasize the idea of renewal or new beginnings.
  • Vāyav: This translates to "of the wind," symbolizing movement, change, and the unseen forces that influence our lives.
  • Ā: A preposition meaning "to" or "towards," indicating direction or purpose.
  • Candreṇa: This means "with the moon," which can symbolize calmness, intuition, and the cyclical nature of life.
  • Rathena: This translates to "with the chariot," representing a vehicle of progress or a means to achieve one's goals.
  • Yāhi: This is a command form meaning "come" or "go," indicating a call to action.
  • Sutasya: This means "of the son" or "of the offspring," which can refer to the next generation or the fruits of one's labor.
  • Pītaye: This translates to "for the enjoyment" or "for the pleasure," suggesting a purpose of fulfillment and joy.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for divine assistance in aligning thoughts and intentions towards new beginnings, while also emphasizing the importance of community and collective effort. It calls upon the forces of nature, represented by the wind and the moon, to guide and support the journey towards fulfillment and joy.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: When embarking on a new project or phase in life, one can recite this mantra to seek clarity and alignment of thoughts.
  • Community Initiatives: In group settings, such as community service or team projects, this mantra can foster unity and collective purpose.
  • Family and Relationships: It can be used to invoke harmony and support within family dynamics, especially when welcoming new members or celebrating milestones.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our thoughts, actions, and the support we receive from the universe. It encourages us to seek renewal and joy in our endeavors while recognizing the importance of community and the natural forces that guide us.
