ज्येष्ठ आह चमसाद्वा करेति कनीयान त्रिं कृणवामेत्य आह |कनिष्ठ आह चतुरस करेति त्वष्ट ऋभवस्तत पनयद वचो वः ||
jyeṣṭha āha camasā dvā kareti kanīyān trīn kṛṇavāmety āha | kaniṣṭha āha caturas kareti tvaṣṭa ṛbhavas tat panayad vaco vaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • jyeṣṭha: elder, superior
    • āha: speaks, says
    • camasā: with the ladle, often referring to offerings
    • dvā: two
    • kareti: does, performs
    • kanīyān: younger, lesser
    • trīn: three
    • kṛṇavāmety: he who makes, creates
    • kaniṣṭha: the smallest, the least
    • caturas: four
    • tvaṣṭa: the divine craftsman, creator
    • ṛbhavas: the wise, the knowledgeable
    • tat: that
    • panayad: speaks, utters
    • vaco: words
    • vaḥ: of you, your
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the dynamics of hierarchy and roles within a community or society.
    • The term "jyeṣṭha" refers to the elder or superior, indicating respect for authority and experience.
    • "Kanīyān" and "kaniṣṭha" represent the younger or lesser, suggesting the importance of nurturing and guidance from the elders.
    • The mention of "trīn" and "caturas" signifies the different roles and contributions individuals make, emphasizing collaboration.
    • "Tvaṣṭa" symbolizes the divine craftsman, representing creativity and the importance of skilled work in society.
    • The phrase "ṛbhavas tat panayad vaco vaḥ" suggests that wise words and guidance are essential for harmony and progress.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, elders often guide the younger members, sharing wisdom and experiences that help shape their decisions.
    • In workplaces, senior employees mentor juniors, fostering a culture of learning and collaboration.
    • In communities, leaders (jyeṣṭha) are expected to listen to the voices of the younger generation (kanīyān) to create inclusive policies.
    • In educational institutions, teachers (jyeṣṭha) play a crucial role in guiding students (kanīyān) towards knowledge and personal growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of respect for hierarchy, the importance of mentorship, and the value of collaboration in society.
    • It teaches us that wisdom and guidance from those with more experience are vital for the growth and development of individuals and communities.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to stronger relationships, better teamwork, and a more harmonious society.
