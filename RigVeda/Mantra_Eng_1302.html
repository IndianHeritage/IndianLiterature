सुषुप्वांसं न निरृतेर उपस्थे सूर्यं न दास्रां तमसि क्षीयन्तम् | शुभे रुक्मं न दर्शतं निखातमुद ūपथुर अश्विनां वन्दनाय ||
suṣupvāṁsaṁ na nirṛter upasthe sūryaṁ na dasrā tamasi kṣiyantam | śubhe rukmaṁ na darśataṁ nikhātam ud ūpathur aśvinā vandanāya ||
  • suṣupvāṁsaṁ: This term can be broken down into "su" meaning good or well, and "ṣupvāṁsaṁ" which refers to the state of being in a deep, peaceful sleep. It signifies a state of tranquility and restfulness.
  • na nirṛter: "na" means not, and "nirṛter" refers to the state of being lost or in darkness. This phrase suggests a rejection of negativity or despair.
  • upasthe: This word means 'in the presence of' or 'near'. It indicates a closeness or connection to something significant.
  • sūryaṁ: This translates to 'the sun', symbolizing light, knowledge, and enlightenment. The sun is often associated with vitality and life.
  • na dasrā: "na" again means not, and "dasrā" refers to the act of being blinded or obscured. This phrase emphasizes clarity and vision.
  • tamasi: This word means 'in darkness', representing ignorance or confusion. The mantra seeks to move away from this state.
  • kṣiyantam: This term means 'to diminish' or 'to fade away'. It suggests the idea of overcoming obstacles or negativity.
  • śubhe: Meaning 'auspicious' or 'good', this word sets a positive tone for the following phrases.
  • rukmaṁ: This translates to 'golden' or 'brilliant', symbolizing purity and value.
  • na darśataṁ: "na" means not, and "darśataṁ" refers to being seen or perceived. This indicates a desire for clarity and understanding.
  • nikhātam: This means 'buried' or 'hidden', suggesting that something valuable is waiting to be uncovered.
  • ud ūpathur: This phrase means 'to rise up' or 'to elevate', indicating a movement towards a higher state of being.
  • aśvinā: Referring to the twin deities of health and medicine in Vedic tradition, the Aśvins symbolize healing and well-being.
  • vandanāya: This means 'for worship' or 'to honor', indicating a sense of reverence and gratitude.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a journey from darkness to light, emphasizing the importance of clarity, positivity, and the pursuit of knowledge. It encourages individuals to seek enlightenment and overcome negativity in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during moments of confusion or despair, serving as a reminder to focus on the positive aspects of life and to seek guidance from higher powers or inner wisdom. It highlights the significance of maintaining a positive mindset and the importance of honoring the forces that promote health and well-being, much like how one might seek the sun's warmth and light to dispel the cold and darkness of night.
