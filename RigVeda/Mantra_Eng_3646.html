जनीष्टा हि जेन्यो अग्रे अह्नां हितो हितेष्व अरुषो वनेषु |दमे-दमे सप्त रत्ना दधानो 'ग्निर होता नि षसादा यजीयान ||
janiṣṭa hi jenyo agre ahnāṁ hito hiteṣv aruṣo vaneṣu | dame-dame sapta ratnā dadhāno 'gnir hotā ni ṣasādā yajīyān ||
  • janiṣṭa hi - "Indeed, it is born" or "It has come into existence". This phrase emphasizes the importance of creation and the beginning of life or ideas.
  • jenyo agre - "the one who is born first" or "the foremost". This suggests a priority or significance in the order of things, highlighting the importance of leadership or the first step in any endeavor.
  • ahnāṁ hito - "for the benefit of the day" or "for the welfare of the day". This indicates a focus on actions that are beneficial and constructive, particularly in the context of daily life.
  • hiteṣv - "in the beneficial" or "in the good". This reinforces the idea of pursuing what is good and beneficial for oneself and others.
  • aruṣo vaneṣu - "in the red (or bright) forests". This can symbolize the richness of nature and the importance of being in harmony with the environment.
  • dame-dame - "in every place" or "in every aspect". This phrase suggests that the principles being discussed are universal and applicable in all areas of life.
  • sapta ratnā - "seven jewels". This can refer to the seven virtues or qualities that are valuable in life, such as wisdom, courage, compassion, integrity, humility, patience, and perseverance.
  • dadhāno - "holding" or "possessing". This indicates the importance of possessing these virtues and qualities in one's life.
  • 'gnir hotā - "the fire that is the priest". This symbolizes the transformative power of knowledge and the role of a guide or teacher in one's life.
  • ni ṣasādā - "has established" or "has set". This suggests the act of creating a foundation or establishing something significant.
  • yajīyān - "the one who is worthy of worship" or "the one who is revered". This emphasizes the importance of respect and reverence for those who embody these virtues.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of creation, leadership, and the pursuit of beneficial actions in life. It highlights the importance of possessing virtues that are universally applicable in all aspects of life. In real-life scenarios, this can be seen in leadership roles where individuals strive to create a positive impact, in personal development where one seeks to embody virtues, and in community building where the focus is on collective welfare. The mantra serves as a reminder to prioritize goodness, wisdom, and respect in our daily lives, fostering a harmonious existence with ourselves and the environment.
