इन्द्र दृष्य मघवन त्वावद इद्भुज इह स्तुतः सुतपाः बोधि नो वृधे |देवेभिर नः सविता प्रावतु श्रुतम आ सर्वतातिम अदितिं वृणीमहे ||
indra dṛhya maghavan tvāvad id bhuja iha stutaḥ sutapā bodhi no vṛdhe | devebhir naḥ savitā prāvatu śrutam ā sarvatātim aditiṁ vṛṇīmahe ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Dṛhya: Means firm or steadfast, indicating stability and reliability.
  • Maghavan: Another name for Indra, often associated with abundance and prosperity.
  • Tvāvad: Translates to "you" or "to you," addressing Indra directly.
  • Id: Means "here" or "this," indicating the present moment or context.
  • Bhuja: Refers to strength or power, often associated with physical prowess.
  • Iha: Means "here" or "in this place," emphasizing the immediacy of the request.
  • Stutaḥ: Means "praised" or "worshipped," indicating reverence towards Indra.
  • Sutapā: Refers to one who is wise or has deep understanding, often associated with enlightenment.
  • bodhi: Means "awakening" or "knowledge," signifying the pursuit of wisdom.
  • No: Means "us," indicating a collective appeal or prayer.
  • Vṛdhe: Means "to grow" or "to increase," indicating a desire for growth and prosperity.
  • Devebhir: Refers to "by the gods," indicating a divine context.
  • Naḥ: Means "our," indicating a collective identity.
  • Savitā: Refers to the sun god, symbolizing illumination and life-giving energy.
  • Prāvatu: Means "may protect," indicating a plea for safety and guidance.
  • Śrutam: Refers to "hearing" or "knowledge," indicating the importance of wisdom.
  • Ā: Means "from" or "out of," indicating a source or origin.
  • Sarvatātim: Means "all-pervading," indicating a universal presence.
  • Aditiṁ: Refers to the mother of gods, symbolizing nurturing and protection.
  • Vṛṇīmahe: Means "we choose" or "we invoke," indicating a collective intention.

  • Significance: This mantra is a prayer for strength, wisdom, and protection from the divine. It emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance from higher powers in times of need.
  • Real-life Application: In daily life, individuals can invoke this mantra during challenging times, such as during personal struggles, decision-making, or when seeking clarity in their lives. It serves as a reminder to seek strength and wisdom from both within and from the universe.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support and wisdom. It highlights the interconnectedness of individuals with the divine and the importance of collective strength and knowledge in overcoming life's challenges.
