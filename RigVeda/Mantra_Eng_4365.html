अपारो वो महिमा वृद्धशवसस् तवेषं शवोऽवत्व एवयामरुत् |स्थातारो हि प्रसितौ संदृशि स्थन ते न उरुष्यतां निदः शुशुक्वांसो नाग्नयः ||
apāro vo mahimā vṛddhaśavasas tveṣaṁ śavo 'vatv evayāmarut | sthātāro hi prasitau saṁdṛśi sthana te na uruṣyatā nidaḥ śuśukvāṁso nāgnayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apāro: boundless, infinite
    • vo: your
    • mahimā: glory, greatness
    • vṛddhaśavasas: of the aged, wise ones
    • tveṣaṁ: this, in this
    • śavo: of the dead, the departed
    • may it protect
    • evayāmarut: O Maruts, the storm deities
    • sthātāro: those who stand firm
    • hi: indeed
    • prasitau: in the assembly
    • saṁdṛśi: in sight, in presence
    • sthāna: place, position
    • te: your
    • na: not
    • uruṣyatā: may it be disturbed
    • nidaḥ: in the abode
    • śuśukvāṁso: of the well-known
    • nāgnayaḥ: not by the fires
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the boundless glory of the divine, particularly the Maruts, who are the storm deities representing strength and vitality.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and the guidance of the aged, suggesting that their experience is crucial in navigating life's challenges.
    • The mantra seeks protection from disturbances and chaos, highlighting the need for stability and peace in our lives.
    • It also reflects on the idea that the presence of the divine can shield us from negative influences, symbolized by the reference to fire, which can represent destruction or turmoil.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, the wisdom of experienced leaders can guide younger employees through complex projects, ensuring stability and success.
    • In personal life, seeking advice from elders during times of crisis can provide clarity and direction, much like the guidance sought from the divine in this mantra.
    • During challenging times, such as natural disasters, communities often come together, invoking the strength of their collective spirit, akin to the Maruts, to restore order and safety.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, stability, and divine protection in our lives.
    • It encourages us to seek guidance from those with experience and to remain grounded in the face of adversity.
    • In a broader sense, it highlights the interconnectedness of community and the divine, suggesting that through unity and strength, we can overcome challenges and achieve greatness.
