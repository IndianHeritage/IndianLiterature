जघन्वां उ हरिभिः संभृतकृतव इन्द्र वृत्रम मनुषे गातुयन्न अपः |अयच्छथा बाह्वोर वज्रम आयसम अधारयो दिव्य आसूर्यं दृष्टे ||
jaghanvām̐ u haribhiḥ sambhṛtakratav indra vṛtram manuṣe gātuyann apaḥ | ayacchathā bāhvor vajram āyasam adhārayo divy ā sūryaṁ dṛśe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • jaghanvām̐: We are invoking or calling upon.
    • u: and.
    • haribhiḥ: with the divine beings or gods.
    • sambhṛtakratav: who are endowed with great power or energy.
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods and god of rain and thunderstorms.
    • vṛtram: the demon or obstacle (often associated with drought or evil).
    • manuṣe: in human affairs or among humans.
    • gātuyann: who is leading or guiding.
    • apaḥ: waters or fluids.
    • ayacchathā: you have given or provided.
    • bāhvor: of the arms.
    • vajram: the thunderbolt or weapon of Indra.
    • āyasam: made of iron or metal.
    • adhārayo: you have supported or upheld.
    • divy: divine or celestial.
    • ā: to or towards.
    • sūryaṁ: the sun.
    • dṛśe: for the sight or vision.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer invoking Indra, asking for his divine assistance in overcoming obstacles (symbolized by Vritra).
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine support in human endeavors, particularly in times of struggle or adversity.
    • The mention of "waters" signifies the life-giving force of rain, essential for agriculture and sustenance.
    • Indra's thunderbolt represents strength and the ability to overcome challenges, suggesting that with divine help, one can conquer difficulties.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain during droughts, symbolizing the need for divine intervention to ensure a good harvest.
    • In personal challenges, individuals may seek guidance through prayer or meditation, invoking a higher power to help them navigate tough situations.
    • In community efforts, people often come together, invoking collective strength and support to overcome social or economic obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine assistance in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It teaches us the value of community, strength, and the importance of faith in achieving our goals.
    • In modern life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as personal struggles, community initiatives, and even in professional settings where teamwork and support are crucial for success.
