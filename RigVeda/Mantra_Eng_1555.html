ईळितो अग्न आ वहेन्द्रं चित्रं इह प्रियं |इयं हि त्वा मतिर्ममाच्छा सुजिह्व वच्यते ||
īḻito agna ā vahendraṁ citram iha priyam | iyaṁ hi tvā matir mamācchā sujihva vacyate ||
  • īḻito: This word can be interpreted as "invoked" or "called upon." It signifies the act of reaching out to a higher power or divine entity.
  • agna: Referring to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic tradition, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • ā vahendraṁ: This phrase translates to "to the powerful one" or "to the lord." It indicates a connection to a higher authority or divine force.
  • citram: Meaning "wonderful" or "extraordinary," this word emphasizes the beauty and significance of the divine presence.
  • iha: This means "here" or "in this world," grounding the invocation in the present reality.
  • priyam: Translated as "beloved" or "dear," it reflects the affection and reverence towards the divine.
  • iyaṁ hi: This phrase means "this indeed," emphasizing the importance of what follows.
  • tvā: Meaning "you," it directly addresses the divine entity being invoked.
  • matir: This translates to "intellect" or "thought," indicating the mental focus and intention behind the invocation.
  • mamā: Meaning "my," it personalizes the connection between the speaker and the divine.
  • acchā: This word can mean "good" or "auspicious," suggesting a positive outcome from the invocation.
  • sujiḥva: This translates to "well-spoken" or "well-expressed," indicating clarity and sincerity in the invocation.
  • vacyate: Meaning "is spoken" or "is expressed," it signifies the act of communication with the divine.

The mantra can be understood as a heartfelt invocation to Agni, the fire god, expressing a desire for divine guidance and blessings. The speaker acknowledges Agni's power and beauty, emphasizing a personal connection and intention for positive outcomes in their life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: When performing rituals, invoking Agni can be a way to seek blessings for auspicious beginnings, such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies.
  • Personal Reflection: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or personal reflection to focus their thoughts and intentions on positive outcomes in their lives.
  • Seeking Guidance: In times of uncertainty, this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek divine guidance and clarity in decision-making.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect the individual with the divine, fostering a sense of purpose and clarity. By invoking Agni, one can cultivate a deeper understanding of their intentions and the transformative power of divine energy in their lives.
