तुभ्यं ता अंगिरस्तम विश्वाः सुख्षितयः पृथक् |अग्ने कामाय येमिरे ||
tubhyaṁ tā aṅgirastama viśvāḥ sukṣitayaḥ pṛthak | agne kāmāya yemire ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tubhyaṁ: to you
    • tā: those
    • aṅgirastama: the best of the Angiras (a group of sages)
    • viśvāḥ: all
    • sukṣitayaḥ: well-being or prosperity
    • pṛthak: separately or distinctly
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • kāmāya: for desires or wishes
    • yemire: they have approached or invoked
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, acknowledging his power and seeking his blessings for prosperity and fulfillment of desires. It emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energies for achieving well-being and success in various aspects of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Invocation of Blessings: Just as individuals may light a candle or incense while praying or meditating, this mantra symbolizes the act of invoking divine presence to seek guidance and support in personal endeavors.
    • Community Gatherings: In many cultures, communal rituals involve invoking deities for collective prosperity, similar to how this mantra calls upon Agni for the well-being of all.
    • Personal Goals: When setting personal goals, individuals often visualize their desires and seek motivation, akin to the invocation of Agni for the fulfillment of wishes.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of the divine as a source of strength and prosperity. It teaches us the importance of seeking higher powers for guidance in our pursuits. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal goal setting, community prayers, and rituals that aim to foster well-being and success. By invoking Agni, we acknowledge the interconnectedness of our desires with the greater universe, promoting a sense of unity and purpose in our lives.
