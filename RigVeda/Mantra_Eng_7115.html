आ वृषस्व पुरूवसो सुतस्येन्द्रांधसः | विद्मा हि त्वा हरिवः पृथ्सु सासहिम अधृष्टं चिद दधृष्वणिम ||
ā vṛṣasva purūvaso sutasyendrāndhasaḥ | vidmā hi tvā harivaḥ pṛtsu sāsahim adhṛṣṭaṁ cid dadhṛṣvaṇim ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vṛṣasva: "Come forth, O powerful one" - a call to a deity or force to manifest strength and vitality.
    • purūvaso: "of the many forms" - referring to the diverse manifestations of the divine or nature.
    • sutasyendrāndhasaḥ: "of the son of the powerful" - indicating a connection to a strong lineage or heritage.
    • vidmā: "we know" - an assertion of knowledge or understanding.
    • hi: "indeed" - emphasizing the certainty of the statement.
    • tvā: "you" - addressing the divine or the force being invoked.
    • harivaḥ: "O bringer of joy" - a title that highlights the benevolent aspect of the deity.
    • pṛtsu: "in the earth" - grounding the invocation in the physical realm.
    • sāsahim: "with strength" - calling for empowerment and support.
    • adhṛṣṭaṁ: "unseen" - referring to the hidden aspects of reality or the divine.
    • cid: "consciousness" - the awareness or essence of being.
    • dadhṛṣvaṇim: "to hold" - a request for stability and support.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a powerful invocation that calls upon a divine force to manifest strength and support in our lives.
    • It acknowledges the many forms of the divine, suggesting that there are various ways in which strength and support can be experienced.
    • The reference to "the son of the powerful" can be seen as a metaphor for the potential within each individual to draw upon their heritage and inner strength.
    • By stating "we know," the mantra emphasizes the importance of awareness and understanding in our spiritual journey.
    • The phrase "O bringer of joy" highlights the positive and uplifting nature of the divine, reminding us that strength can also bring happiness.
    • Grounding the invocation in "the earth" connects the spiritual with the physical, suggesting that our strength and support must be rooted in reality.
    • The mention of "unseen" aspects of reality encourages us to recognize that not all strength is visible; some may come from within or from the divine in ways we cannot perceive.
    • Finally, the call to "hold" signifies a desire for stability and support in our lives, reinforcing the idea that we are not alone in our struggles.
  • Real-Life Applications:
    • This mantra can be used in personal meditation or prayer to invoke strength during challenging times, such as during a difficult project at work or personal struggles.
    • In community settings, it can serve as a reminder of the collective strength that comes from diverse backgrounds and experiences, fostering unity and support.
    • In educational contexts, it can inspire students to recognize their potential and the importance of drawing upon their heritage and knowledge.
    • In moments of uncertainty, reciting this mantra can help individuals find grounding and stability, encouraging them to seek unseen sources of support.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, support, and awareness of the divine presence in our lives.
    • It teaches us that strength can come in many forms and that we are encouraged to seek both visible and unseen sources of support.
    • By integrating this understanding into our daily lives, we can cultivate resilience, joy, and a deeper connection to ourselves and the world around us.
