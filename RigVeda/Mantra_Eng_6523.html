येषाम् आबाध ऋग्मिय इषः पृक्षश्च निग्रभे | उपविदा वाह्निर् विन्दते वसु ||
yeṣām ābādha ṛgmiya iṣaḥ pṛkṣaś ca nigrabhe | upavidā vahnir vindate vasu ||
  • yeṣām - This word translates to "of whom" or "whose." It indicates possession or belonging.
  • ābādha - This term means "unhindered" or "unobstructed." It suggests a state of freedom or lack of obstacles.
  • ṛgmiya - This word refers to "sacred knowledge" or "mantra." It signifies the importance of wisdom and spiritual insight.
  • iṣaḥ - This translates to "desires" or "wishes." It reflects the aspirations and goals of individuals.
  • pṛkṣaś - This term means "to be examined" or "to be scrutinized." It implies the need for careful consideration and evaluation.
  • ca - This is a conjunction meaning "and," connecting the ideas presented in the mantra.
  • nigrabhe - This word means "to be restrained" or "to be held back." It indicates the presence of limitations or challenges.
  • upavidā - This term translates to "the one who knows" or "the knowledgeable." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding.
  • vahnir - This word means "fire." In many cultures, fire symbolizes transformation, purification, and energy.
  • vindate - This translates to "attains" or "receives." It indicates the act of gaining or achieving something.
  • vasu - This term means "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies abundance and the good things in life.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to those who possess unhindered sacred knowledge and desires, urging them to examine their aspirations carefully. It acknowledges the challenges that may restrain them but emphasizes that through wisdom, one can attain prosperity and abundance.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals seeking personal development can reflect on their desires and aspirations, ensuring they are not hindered by obstacles. For example, someone aiming for a promotion at work should evaluate their skills and knowledge, addressing any limitations they may face.
  • Education: Students can relate to this mantra by understanding the importance of knowledge and the need to scrutinize their learning methods. By overcoming challenges in their studies, they can achieve academic success.
  • Business: Entrepreneurs can apply this mantra by recognizing the importance of clear goals and the need to navigate obstacles in their path. By leveraging their knowledge and resources, they can attain prosperity in their ventures.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the significance of knowledge, careful examination of desires, and the pursuit of prosperity despite challenges. It serves as a reminder that with wisdom and determination, one can overcome obstacles and achieve their goals in various aspects of life.
