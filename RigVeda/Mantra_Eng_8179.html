वृषा सोम द्युमाम् असि वृषा देव वृषव्रतः |वृषा धर्माणि दधिषे ||
vṛṣā soma dyumām̐ asi vṛṣā deva vṛṣavrataḥ | vṛṣā dharmāṇi dadhiṣe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vṛṣā: Powerful, strong, or vigorous.
    • soma: Refers to the sacred drink or elixir, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • dyumām̐: Shining, radiant, or luminous.
    • asi: You are or you become.
    • deva: God or divine being.
    • vṛṣavrataḥ: One who is committed to righteousness or the divine path.
    • dharmāṇi: Duties, laws, or principles of righteousness.
    • dadhiṣe: You uphold or maintain.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes the powerful essence of Soma, emphasizing its radiant and divine qualities.
    • It acknowledges the strength and commitment of the divine beings who uphold righteousness and moral principles.
    • The mantra suggests a connection between the divine and the aspirant, encouraging the pursuit of strength and virtue.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of "vṛṣā" can inspire their team to achieve great heights, much like how the divine Soma inspires strength and vitality.
    • In personal life, individuals who commit to their "vṛṣavrataḥ" can be seen in community service, where they uphold moral values and contribute positively to society.
    • In sports, athletes often draw upon their inner strength and commitment to their training, reflecting the essence of "dharmāṇi" as they strive for excellence and fair play.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of strength, commitment, and righteousness in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek divine inspiration and uphold moral principles in their actions.
    • In a world where challenges abound, the essence of this mantra can guide us to remain steadfast in our values and inspire others to do the same.
