अच्छा सिंधुम मातृतमाम अयासं विपाशम उर्वीं सुभगाम अगन्मा |वत्सं इव मातरा संरिहाणे समानं योनिम अनु संचरन्ती ||
acchā sindhum mātṛtamām ayāsaṁ vipāśam urvīṁ subhagām aganma | vatsam iva mātarā saṁrihāṇe samānaṁ yonim anu saṁcarantī ||
  • acchā sindhum: This phrase translates to "the good ocean." In a metaphorical sense, the ocean represents vastness, depth, and the source of life. In real life, we can relate this to the abundance of opportunities and resources available to us.
  • mātṛtamām: This means "motherly" or "nurturing." It signifies the caring and protective nature of a mother. In our lives, this can be seen in the unconditional love and support provided by parents or guardians.
  • ayāsaṁ: This word means "without effort" or "effortlessly." It suggests that certain things in life can be achieved with ease, highlighting the importance of flow and natural progression. For example, when we pursue our passions, we often find that success comes more easily.
  • vipāśam: This translates to "the flowing river." Rivers are often symbols of life, movement, and change. In real life, this can be compared to the journey of life, where we must adapt to the currents and changes around us.
  • urvīṁ: This means "the earth" or "the land." It represents stability and grounding. In our daily lives, we can relate this to our connection with nature and the importance of being rooted in our values and beliefs.
  • subhagām: This translates to "auspicious" or "fortunate." It signifies positivity and good fortune. In real life, this can be seen in moments of serendipity or when we receive unexpected blessings.
  • aganma: This means "we have come" or "we have arrived." It signifies achievement and fulfillment. In our lives, this can be related to reaching our goals or milestones after a journey of hard work.
  • vatsam iva: This translates to "like a calf." The imagery of a calf suggests innocence and the nurturing aspect of life. In real life, this can be seen in the way we care for the young or vulnerable in our communities.
  • mātarā: This means "by the mother." It emphasizes the role of nurturing and guidance in our lives. This can be reflected in the support systems we have, whether from family, friends, or mentors.
  • saṁrihāṇe: This translates to "in gathering" or "in collecting." It signifies the importance of community and collaboration. In real life, this can be seen in teamwork and collective efforts to achieve common goals.
  • samānaṁ yonim: This means "the same womb" or "the same source." It emphasizes unity and shared origins. In our lives, this can be related to the idea of humanity being interconnected and sharing a common purpose.
  • anu saṁcarantī: This translates to "moving along" or "progressing." It signifies the journey of life and the continuous movement towards growth and development. In real life, this can be seen in personal growth and the pursuit of knowledge.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of nurturing, abundance, and the interconnectedness of life. It reminds us of the importance of community, support, and the natural flow of life. In real-life scenarios, we can apply these teachings by fostering relationships, embracing opportunities, and recognizing the significance of our roots and shared experiences. This mantra encourages us to move forward with grace and gratitude, acknowledging the nurturing forces in our lives that guide us along our journey.
