प्रणीतिभिष्टे हर्यश्व सुष्ठोः सुषुम्नस्य पुरुरुचो जनासः |मंहिष्ठाम ऊतिं वितिरे दधानाḥ स्तोतार इन्द्र तव सूनृताभिः ||
praṇītibhiṣ ṭe haryaśva suṣṭoḥ suṣumnasya pururuco janāsaḥ | maṁhiṣṭhām ūtiṁ vitire dadhānāḥ stotāra indra tava sūnṛtābhiḥ ||
  • praṇītibhiḥ: This word can be translated as "by the guidance" or "by the direction." It signifies the importance of leadership and guidance in achieving goals.
  • ṭe: This is a form of the pronoun "you," indicating a direct address to a deity or a higher power.
  • haryaśva: This term refers to "the swift horses" or "the powerful steeds," symbolizing strength, speed, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • suṣṭoḥ: Meaning "well-formed" or "well-structured," this word emphasizes the importance of having a solid foundation in any endeavor.
  • suṣumnasya: This can be interpreted as "of the well-being" or "of the auspicious," indicating a state of prosperity and health.
  • pururuco: This term means "of many people" or "of the multitude," highlighting the collective effort and unity among individuals.
  • janāsaḥ: This translates to "the people" or "the community," emphasizing the role of society in achieving common goals.
  • maṁhiṣṭhām: This word means "the best" or "the most excellent," indicating the pursuit of excellence in all actions.
  • ūtiṁ: This refers to "prosperity" or "wealth," signifying the rewards of hard work and dedication.
  • vitire: Meaning "to distribute" or "to share," this word emphasizes the importance of generosity and sharing resources.
  • dadhānāḥ: This translates to "those who hold" or "those who possess," indicating responsibility and stewardship over resources.
  • stotāra: This term means "the one who praises" or "the worshipper," highlighting the importance of gratitude and recognition of higher powers.
  • indra: Referring to the king of gods in Vedic literature, Indra symbolizes strength, leadership, and protection.
  • tava: This means "your," indicating possession or connection to the divine.
  • sūnṛtābhiḥ: This can be interpreted as "with truthful words" or "with sincere intentions," emphasizing the importance of honesty and integrity in actions.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of guidance, strength, community, and integrity in achieving prosperity and excellence. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as leadership in organizations, teamwork in community projects, and personal development. By recognizing the importance of collective effort and the pursuit of truth, individuals and societies can work towards a harmonious and prosperous existence. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek guidance, act with integrity, and strive for excellence in all endeavors.
