तरणिर इति सिषासति वाजम् पुरन्ध्या युजा |आ व इन्द्रं पुरुहूतं नामे गिरा नेमिं तष्टेव सुद्र्वम् ||
taraṇir it siṣāsati vājam puraṁdhyā yujā | ā va indram puruhūtaṁ name girā nemiṁ taṣṭeva sudrvam ||
  • taraṇir: This word can be interpreted as "the one who crosses" or "the one who helps in crossing." In a real-life context, this can refer to a mentor or guide who helps individuals navigate challenges in their lives.
  • it: This is a particle that can mean "thus" or "so," indicating a continuation or a manner of action.
  • siṣāsati: This term can be understood as "to achieve" or "to accomplish." It signifies the attainment of goals or objectives, which is relevant in various aspects of life, such as career achievements or personal growth.
  • vājam: This word translates to "strength" or "power." In everyday life, this can relate to physical strength, mental resilience, or the power of community support.
  • puraṁdhyā: This term refers to "the one who is the best among the strong" or "the supreme." It can be associated with leaders or influential figures who inspire others through their strength and wisdom.
  • yujā: This means "to unite" or "to join." In real life, this can be seen in teamwork, collaboration, and the importance of unity in achieving common goals.
  • ā: This particle can mean "towards" or "to," indicating direction or intention.
  • va: This means "or," suggesting a choice or alternative.
  • indram: Referring to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power, authority, and protection. In modern contexts, this can represent leaders or figures of authority who provide guidance and support.
  • puruhūtaṁ: This term means "the one who is invoked frequently" or "the one who is called upon." It signifies the importance of seeking help or guidance from those who are knowledgeable and experienced.
  • name: This translates to "I bow" or "I offer my respects." It reflects humility and reverence, which are essential qualities in personal and professional relationships.
  • girā: This word means "speech" or "words." It emphasizes the power of communication and the impact of words in shaping thoughts and actions.
  • nemiṁ: This can be interpreted as "the wheel" or "the cycle," symbolizing the cyclical nature of life and the importance of balance and harmony.
  • taṣṭeva: This term means "to be firm" or "to be steadfast." It highlights the importance of resilience and determination in overcoming obstacles.
  • sudrvam: This translates to "good fortune" or "prosperity." It signifies the blessings and positive outcomes that come from righteous actions and intentions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of guidance, strength, unity, and respect. It emphasizes the importance of seeking help from those who are strong and wise, working together towards common goals, and maintaining humility in our pursuits. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in teamwork, leadership, and personal development. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can navigate challenges more effectively and foster a supportive community that leads to collective success and prosperity.
