अपिबत कद्रुवः सुतं इन्द्रः सहस्रबाहवे |अत्रादेदिष्ट पौंस्यम् ||
apibat kadruvaḥ sutam indraḥ sahasrabāhve | atrādediṣṭa pauṁsyam ||
  • Mantra: "apibat kadruvaḥ sutam indraḥ sahasrabāhve | atrādediṣṭa pauṁsyam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apibat: drank
    • kadruvaḥ: of Kadru (a mythological figure, often associated with the serpent)
    • sutam: the son
    • indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods, associated with rain and thunderstorms)
    • sahasrabāhve: the one with a thousand arms
    • atrādediṣṭa: here is the desired
    • pauṁsyam: of masculinity or male offspring
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of Indra, the king of gods, who is depicted as having a thousand arms, symbolizing his immense power and capability.
    • The reference to "Kadru" connects to the mythological narrative where Kadru is the mother of the serpents, and her son is being referred to here.
    • The act of Indra drinking the essence or the son of Kadru signifies the absorption of strength or power from the serpentine lineage, which can be interpreted as drawing from the primal forces of nature.
    • The phrase "atrādediṣṭa pauṁsyam" suggests that this act is aimed at achieving a desired outcome related to masculinity or strength, which can be seen as a metaphor for the pursuit of power and vitality in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be related to the idea of harnessing one's inner strength and capabilities to overcome challenges, much like how leaders draw from their experiences and backgrounds to lead effectively.
    • In personal development, the concept of "drinking" from the wisdom of past generations (symbolized by Kadru) can be seen in how individuals learn from their ancestors or cultural heritage to build their identity and strength.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often "absorb" the strengths of their teams (akin to Indra) to achieve collective goals, emphasizing the importance of collaboration and drawing from diverse talents.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of drawing strength from one's heritage and the collective power of community.
    • It serves as a reminder that true power comes not just from individual capabilities but also from the wisdom and strength of those who came before us.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as leadership, personal growth, and community building, where understanding and utilizing one's roots can lead to greater achievements.
