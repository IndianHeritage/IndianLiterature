प्र नूनं ब्रह्मणस पतिर मन्त्रं वदत्य उक्त्यम् |यस्मिन्न इन्द्रो वरुणो मित्रो आर्यमा देवाः ओकांसी चक्रिरे ||
pra nūnam brahmaṇas patir mantraṁ vadaty ukthyam | yasminn indro varuṇo mitro aryamā devā okāṁsi cakrire ||
  • Translation: "Indeed, the Lord of the Brahman speaks the mantra; in whom Indra, Varuna, Mitra, and Aryama, the gods, have established their powers."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • pra nūnam: Indeed, certainly
    • brahmaṇas patir: Lord of the Brahman (the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit)
    • mantraṁ vadaty: speaks the mantra
    • ukthyam: that which is uttered or recited
    • yasminn: in whom
    • indro: Indra (the king of gods, associated with rain and thunderstorms)
    • varuṇo: Varuna (the god of cosmic order and water)
    • mitro: Mitra (the god of friendship and contracts)
    • aryamā: Aryama (a deity associated with the afterlife and the path of the righteous)
    • devā: gods
    • okāṁsi: powers or energies
    • cakrire: have established or created

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the power of spoken words and their connection to the divine. In real life, we often see how words can influence situations, such as in motivational speeches that inspire people to take action.
    • The mention of various gods signifies the importance of collaboration and harmony among different forces. In a workplace, for instance, teamwork among diverse individuals can lead to successful project outcomes.
    • The concept of 'Brahman' as the ultimate reality can be related to the pursuit of truth and understanding in our lives. For example, scientists and philosophers seek to uncover the fundamental truths of existence, much like the quest for Brahman.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of the spoken word and its divine connection, reminding us of the power of our expressions and intentions.
    • It teaches us the importance of unity among various forces, whether in personal relationships, community efforts, or professional environments.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to seek deeper truths and understanding in their lives, fostering a sense of purpose and connection to the universe.
