वृषभो न तिग्मशृङ्गोऽन्तर यूथेषु रोर्वत् |मन्थस्त इन्द्र शं हृदे यं ते सुनोति भावयुर विश्वस्माद इन्द्र उत्तरः ||
vṛṣabho na tigmaśṛṅgo 'ntar yūtheṣu roruvat | manthas ta indra śaṁ hṛde yaṁ te sunoti bhāvayur viśvasmād indra uttaraḥ ||
  • vṛṣabho: This word translates to "the bull" or "the strong one." In many cultures, the bull symbolizes strength, power, and fertility. For example, in agriculture, a bull is often seen as a vital asset for plowing fields, representing hard work and productivity.
  • na tigmaśṛṅgo: This phrase means "without sharp horns." It suggests a gentle strength, indicating that true power does not need to be aggressive or violent. In real life, this can be seen in leaders who are strong yet compassionate, guiding their teams without resorting to harshness.
  • 'ntar yūtheṣu roruvat: This translates to "roaring among the groups." It implies a presence that commands attention and respect within a community or group. For instance, a teacher who inspires students can be seen as "roaring" in a positive way, leading discussions and encouraging participation.
  • manthas ta indra: This means "O Indra, the one who churns." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and strength. The act of churning can symbolize the process of extracting wisdom or knowledge from experiences, much like how one learns from challenges in life.
  • śaṁ hṛde: This translates to "peace in the heart." It emphasizes the importance of inner peace and tranquility. In today's fast-paced world, finding moments of calm can be crucial for mental health, such as through meditation or mindfulness practices.
  • yaṁ te sunoti: This means "that which you bless." It highlights the role of divine blessings in achieving success and well-being. In real life, people often seek blessings from elders or mentors before embarking on significant endeavors, believing that these blessings can guide them toward success.
  • bhāvayur viśvasmād: This translates to "from all that exists." It suggests a connection to the universe and the idea that everything is interlinked. In practical terms, this can be seen in environmental movements that emphasize the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of sustainability.
  • indra uttaraḥ: This means "Indra, the supreme." It signifies the ultimate power and authority. In a real-life context, this can be related to the idea of striving for excellence and leadership in one's field, aspiring to be the best version of oneself.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, compassion, and the pursuit of peace and wisdom. It teaches us that true power lies not in aggression but in the ability to inspire and lead with kindness. In our daily lives, we can apply these principles by striving for inner peace, seeking knowledge from our experiences, and leading with empathy in our communities. The significance of this mantra is profound, as it encourages us to harness our strengths while remaining grounded and connected to the world around us.
