मन्त्र: एते वाता इवोरवः पार्जन्यस्येव वृष्टयः |अग्नेर इव भ्रमा वृथा ||
ete vātā ivoravaḥ parjanyasyeva vṛṣṭayaḥ | agner iva bhramā vṛthā ||
  • Translation: "Like the winds, these (clouds) are moving; like the rains of the cloud, they are (the blessings) of the sky; like the flames of fire, they are (the energy) that is scattered."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ete: these
    • vātā: winds
    • ivoravaḥ: like the moving
    • parjanyasyeva: like the rains of the cloud
    • vṛṣṭayaḥ: blessings or showers
    • agner: of fire
    • iva: like
    • bhramā: energy or movement
    • vṛthā: scattered or dispersed

  • Detailed Explanation:
    • This mantra draws a parallel between natural elements—winds, rains, and fire—highlighting their dynamic and transformative qualities.
    • The winds symbolize change and movement, reflecting how life is constantly in flux. For example, in a career, one may experience changes that lead to new opportunities, much like how winds can change direction and bring new weather.
    • The rains represent nourishment and blessings, essential for growth. In real life, this can be likened to the support we receive from family and friends during challenging times, which helps us to flourish.
    • Fire symbolizes energy and transformation. In personal development, this can be seen in how challenges ignite our passion and drive, leading to personal growth and achievement.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of natural forces and their influence on our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that just as nature undergoes constant change, so do we, and that these changes can lead to growth and transformation.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to embrace change, seek nourishment from our relationships, and harness our inner energy to overcome challenges.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us to appreciate the blessings in our lives and recognize the potential for growth in every situation.
