द्युक्षं मित्रस्य सादनम् आर्यम्णो वरुणस्य च |अथा दधाते बृहद् उक्थ्यं वय उपस्तुत्यम् बृहद् वयः ||
dyukṣam mitrasya sādanam aryamṇo varuṇasya ca | athā dadhāte bṛhad ukthya1ṁ vaya upastutyam bṛhad vayaḥ ||
  • dyukṣam: This word can be interpreted as "the abode" or "the dwelling place." It signifies a space that is sacred or significant.
  • mitrasya: This refers to "Mitra," a deity associated with friendship, harmony, and the sun. Mitra represents the principles of truth and order.
  • sādanam: This means "the place" or "the home." It indicates a location that is welcoming and nurturing.
  • aryamṇo: This term relates to "Aryaman," another deity linked to social order and the bonds of kinship. Aryaman embodies the values of community and cooperation.
  • varuṇasya: This refers to "Varuna," the god of cosmic order and water. Varuna represents the moral and ethical laws that govern the universe.
  • ca: This simply means "and," connecting the various elements of the mantra.
  • athā: This translates to "then" or "thereafter," indicating a progression or continuation of thought.
  • dadhāte: This means "they bestow" or "they grant," suggesting an act of giving or providing.
  • bṛhad: This word means "great" or "vast," indicating something of significant magnitude or importance.
  • ukthyaṁ: This refers to "praise" or "hymn," highlighting the importance of expressing reverence and gratitude.
  • vaya: This means "we," indicating a collective identity or community.
  • upastutyam: This translates to "in worship" or "in adoration," emphasizing the act of honoring the divine.
  • bṛhad vayaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "our great selves," suggesting a connection to a higher purpose or collective identity.

In essence, this mantra speaks to the significance of community, harmony, and the divine order that governs our lives. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring the deities that represent these values, such as Mitra, Aryaman, and Varuna. The mantra suggests that by coming together in worship and gratitude, we can create a nurturing and harmonious environment.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Community Building: Just as the mantra emphasizes the importance of kinship and cooperation, it can inspire individuals to foster strong relationships within their communities, promoting harmony and support.
  • Environmental Awareness: With Varuna representing water and cosmic order, this mantra can serve as a reminder of our responsibility to protect the environment and maintain balance in nature.
  • Conflict Resolution: The principles of truth and order embodied by Mitra can guide individuals in resolving conflicts through dialogue and understanding, fostering a peaceful coexistence.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals within a community and the divine principles that guide our actions. By embracing these values, we can create a more harmonious and supportive world.
