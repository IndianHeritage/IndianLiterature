दक्षस्य वादिते जनमनि व्रते राजानां मित्रावरुणा विवाससि |अतूर्तपन्थाः पुरुरथो आर्यमा सप्तहोताऽविषुरूपेषु जन्मसु ||
dakṣasya vādite janmani vrate rājānā mitrāvaruṇā vivāsasi | atūrtapanthāḥ pururatho aryamā saptahotā viṣurūpeṣu janmasu ||
  • dakṣasya: This word refers to the skilled or capable one. In a real-life context, it can be associated with individuals who excel in their professions, such as a talented artist or a proficient engineer.
  • vādite: This means to speak or to express. In everyday life, this can relate to effective communication, where expressing thoughts clearly can lead to better understanding and collaboration.
  • janmani: This translates to birth or existence. It signifies the beginning of life or new opportunities, akin to starting a new job or embarking on a new journey.
  • vrate: This word means vow or commitment. In real life, it can be compared to personal commitments, such as marriage vows or promises made to oneself to achieve certain goals.
  • rājānā: This refers to kings or rulers. In modern terms, it can symbolize leadership roles in society, such as CEOs or community leaders who guide and influence others.
  • mitrāvaruṇā: This term combines 'Mitra' (friend) and 'Varuna' (the cosmic order). It represents the balance of friendship and universal law, similar to how friendships can help maintain harmony in personal and professional relationships.
  • vivāsasi: This means to separate or to distinguish. In life, this can relate to making choices that define our paths, such as choosing a career or lifestyle that aligns with our values.
  • atūrtapanthāḥ: This translates to the path of the untroubled or the peaceful. It signifies the importance of choosing a life path that is free from unnecessary conflict, akin to seeking a peaceful resolution in disputes.
  • pururatho: This means one who has many chariots or resources. In a contemporary context, it can refer to individuals or organizations that are well-equipped to handle various challenges.
  • aryamā: This term refers to the noble or the honorable. It emphasizes the importance of integrity and honor in our actions, similar to how ethical business practices are valued in today's world.
  • saptahotā: This means the seven priests or the seven offerings. It symbolizes the importance of community and collaboration, much like teamwork in achieving common goals.
  • viṣurūpeṣu: This translates to in various forms or manifestations. It highlights the diversity of life experiences, akin to how people from different backgrounds contribute to a richer society.
  • janmasu: This means in births or existences. It signifies the cyclical nature of life and experiences, similar to how we learn and grow from each phase of our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of skill, communication, commitment, leadership, friendship, and the pursuit of a peaceful life. It teaches us to honor our commitments, embrace diversity, and work collaboratively towards common goals. In real life, these principles can guide us in personal relationships, professional endeavors, and community interactions, ultimately leading to a harmonious and fulfilling existence.
