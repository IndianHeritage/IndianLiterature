इदं इथ्था रौद्रं गूर्तवचाऽ ब्रह्म क्रत्वा शच्यां अन्तर आजौ |क्राणā यद अस्य पितरā मंहनिष्ठाः पर्वत पक्षे अहन्न ā सप्त होत्रīn ||
idam itthā raudraṁ gūrtavacā brahma kratvā śacyām antar ājau | krāṇā yad asya pitarā maṁhaneṣṭhāḥ parṣat pakthe ahann ā sapta hotṝn ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • idam: this
    • itthā: thus
    • raudraṁ: fierce or powerful
    • gūrtavacā: with the voice of the seer
    • brahma: the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit
    • kratvā: by the power of the ritual
    • śacyām: in the realm of strength or power
    • antar: within
    • ājau: in the sacrifice
    • krāṇā: to create or to make
    • yad: that
    • asya: of this
    • pitarā: ancestors or forefathers
    • maṁhaneṣṭhāḥ: those who are great or revered
    • parṣat: assembly or gathering
    • pakthe: in the offering
    • ahann: have struck or have achieved
    • ā: indeed
    • sapta: seven
    • hotṝn: priests or those who invoke
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful invocation during a ritual.
    • It emphasizes the importance of the voice of the seer, suggesting that the words spoken during the ritual hold significant power.
    • The reference to ancestors indicates a connection to lineage and the respect for those who came before us.
    • The mention of "seven priests" signifies the importance of collective effort in spiritual practices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern spiritual practices, rituals often involve collective prayers or meditations, highlighting the power of community.
    • Family gatherings during festivals can be seen as a way to honor ancestors, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on lineage.
    • In leadership, the idea of invoking the wisdom of past leaders can be likened to the respect for ancestors in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of rituals and the collective effort in invoking spiritual power.
    • It teaches us to honor our ancestors and recognize the strength that comes from community and tradition.
    • In real life, it encourages us to engage in practices that connect us with our roots and foster unity among people.
