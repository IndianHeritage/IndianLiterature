आ रुक्मैर आ युधा नर ऋष्वा ऋष्टीर असृक्षत |अन्व एनां अह विद्यूतो मरुतो जज्जहतीर इव भानुर आर्त तमनā दिवः ||
ā rukmair ā yudhā nara ṛṣvā ṛṣṭīr asṛkṣata | anv enām̐ aha vidyuto maruto jajjhatīr iva bhānur arta tmanā divaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā rukmair: with golden (or radiant) things
    • ā yudhā: from battles
    • nara: man or human
    • ṛṣvā: strong or powerful
    • ṛṣṭīr: the one who is victorious
    • asṛkṣata: protected or safeguarded
    • anv enām̐: following her
    • aha: I
    • vidyuto: like lightning
    • maruto: the winds or storm gods
    • jajjhatīr: they who are moving or rushing
    • iva: like
    • bhānur: the sun
    • arta: with effort or energy
    • tmanā: with the mind or spirit
    • divaḥ: of the heavens
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the protective and radiant qualities of a powerful human being, who is safeguarded from battles and challenges.
    • The imagery of "golden things" suggests prosperity and abundance, while "like lightning" indicates speed and intensity.
    • The reference to the sun symbolizes enlightenment and clarity, suggesting that the individual is guided by a higher purpose or divine energy.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies these qualities can inspire their team to overcome challenges and achieve success, much like a strong warrior leading their troops.
    • In personal life, individuals who face adversities with resilience and a positive mindset can be seen as embodying the spirit of this mantra, drawing strength from their inner light.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of inner strength, protection, and the pursuit of victory in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that, like the sun and lightning, we can harness our energy and clarity to navigate through life's battles.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek protection from negativity and to strive for success with a strong and focused mind.
