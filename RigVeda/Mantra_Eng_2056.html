मन्त्र: होताजनिष्ठ चेतनः पिता पितृभ्य ऊतये | प्रयक्षं जेन्यं वसु शकेम वाजिनो यमम् ||
hotājaniṣṭa cetanaḥ pitā pitṛbhya ūtaye | prayakṣañ jenyaṁ vasu śakema vājino yamam ||
  • hotājaniṣṭa: This term can be broken down into two parts: "hotā," which refers to the priest or the one who invokes, and "janiṣṭa," meaning the one who is born or created. Together, it signifies the divine or spiritual force that is invoked during rituals.
  • cetanaḥ: This word translates to "consciousness" or "awareness." It represents the idea of being aware and mindful, which is essential in both spiritual practices and daily life.
  • pitā: This means "father" or "parent." In a broader sense, it symbolizes the source of life and guidance, representing both biological and spiritual parentage.
  • pitṛbhya: This term refers to "ancestors" or "forefathers." It emphasizes the importance of lineage and the respect owed to those who came before us, acknowledging their contributions to our existence.
  • ūtaye: This word means "for the purpose of" or "for the benefit of." It indicates the intention behind actions, especially in the context of rituals and offerings.
  • prayakṣañ: This translates to "to see" or "to perceive." It suggests the act of witnessing or experiencing something profound, often in a spiritual context.
  • jenyaṁ: This term means "worthy of praise" or "to be honored." It highlights the importance of recognizing and valuing the divine or virtuous aspects of life.
  • vasu: This word means "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies not just material wealth but also spiritual abundance and well-being.
  • śakema: This translates to "may we be able" or "may we achieve." It expresses a desire for capability and success in our endeavors.
  • vājino: This term means "of the strong" or "of the powerful." It refers to strength, both physical and spiritual, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • yamam: This word can be interpreted as "control" or "restraint." It emphasizes the importance of self-discipline and mastery over one's desires and actions.

Conclusion: The mantra "hotājaniṣṭa cetanaḥ pitā pitṛbhya ūtaye | prayakṣañ jenyaṁ vasu śakema vājino yamam ||" encapsulates the essence of invoking divine consciousness and honoring our ancestors for the purpose of achieving prosperity and strength in life. It teaches us the significance of mindfulness, respect for our lineage, and the pursuit of both material and spiritual wealth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in rituals, personal reflections, and daily practices where one seeks to connect with their roots, cultivate awareness, and strive for success while maintaining self-discipline. By embodying these principles, individuals can foster a deeper connection with their heritage and a more meaningful existence.
