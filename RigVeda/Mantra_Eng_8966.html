आ रोहतायुर जरसां वृणाना अनुपूर्वं यतमानā यति ष्ठा |इह त्वष्टा सुजनिमा सजोषा दीर्घम आयुः करति जीवसे वः ||
ā rohatāyur jarasaṁ vṛṇānā anupūrvaṁ yatamānā yati ṣṭha | iha tvaṣṭā sujanimā sajoṣā dīrgham āyuḥ karati jīvase vaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ā rohatāyur jarasaṁ vṛṇānā anupūrvaṁ yatamānā yati ṣṭha | iha tvaṣṭā sujanimā sajoṣā dīrgham āyuḥ karati jīvase vaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā rohatāyur: May you attain longevity
    • jarasaṁ: free from old age
    • vṛṇānā: by choosing
    • anupūrvaṁ: in a continuous manner
    • yatamānā: striving
    • yati: may you be
    • ṣṭha: stable
    • iha: here
    • tvaṣṭā: the divine creator
    • sujaṇimā: with good birth
    • sajoṣā: with joy
    • dīrgham āyuḥ: long life
    • karati: grants
    • jīvase: to the living
    • vaḥ: for you
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for longevity and vitality, emphasizing the importance of a life free from the burdens of old age.
    • The phrase "ā rohatāyur" signifies a wish for a long life, while "jarasaṁ" indicates a desire to be free from the frailties that come with aging.
    • The mantra encourages continuous effort ("anupūrvaṁ yatamānā") in striving for stability and well-being in life.
    • It acknowledges the role of the divine creator ("tvaṣṭā") in bestowing good birth and joy, suggesting that our existence is intertwined with divine grace.
    • The concluding part of the mantra, "dīrgham āyuḥ karati jīvase vaḥ," reinforces the idea that a long and fulfilling life is a gift that should be cherished and sought after.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern health practices, this mantra can be related to the emphasis on healthy living, such as balanced diets, regular exercise, and mental well-being, all aimed at prolonging life and enhancing its quality.
    • In various cultures, rituals and prayers for longevity are common, reflecting the universal desire for a long and healthy life.
    • Practices like yoga and meditation can be seen as modern interpretations of the striving for stability and vitality mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, health, and divine grace.
    • It encourages individuals to actively pursue a life of purpose and joy while recognizing the role of higher powers in their journey.
    • In a world where stress and health issues are prevalent, the teachings of this mantra can inspire a holistic approach to living, focusing on both physical and spiritual well-being.
