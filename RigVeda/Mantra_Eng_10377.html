अग्निर्देवो देवानां अभवत्पुरोहितोऽग्निम् मनुष्याः ऋषयः समीधिरे |अग्निम् महो धनसाताव अहं हुवे मृळीकं धनसातये ||
agnir devo devānām abhavat purohito 'gnim manuṣyā3 ṛṣayaḥ sam īdhire | agnim maho dhanasātāv ahaṁ huve mṛḻīkaṁ dhanasātaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • agnir: Fire
    • devo: God
    • devānām: of the gods
    • abhavat: has become
    • purohito: the priest or the one who leads
    • manuṣyāḥ: humans
    • ṛṣayaḥ: sages or seers
    • sam īdhire: have come together
    • maho: great
    • dhanasātāv: for wealth or prosperity
    • ahaṁ: I
    • huve: invoke or call upon
    • mṛḻīkaṁ: the beneficial or auspicious
    • dhanasātaye: for the attainment of wealth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is considered the mediator between humans and the divine.
    • Agni is seen as a purifying force, representing transformation and the energy that sustains life.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of Agni in rituals and the role of humans and sages in seeking blessings for prosperity.
    • It highlights the collective effort of humans and sages in invoking divine blessings for wealth and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is used in rituals to symbolize purification and the presence of the divine. For instance, in Hindu weddings, the sacred fire (Agni) is a witness to the vows taken by the couple.
    • In modern contexts, the mantra can be related to teamwork and collaboration in achieving common goals, such as in business settings where collective efforts lead to success.
    • Environmental initiatives often invoke the spirit of Agni, as fire is essential for energy and transformation, reminding us of the need for sustainable practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine energies for prosperity and well-being.
    • It teaches us the value of collaboration and the role of spiritual practices in achieving material and spiritual wealth.
    • In real life, it encourages us to seek balance between our material pursuits and spiritual growth, reminding us that true wealth encompasses both.
