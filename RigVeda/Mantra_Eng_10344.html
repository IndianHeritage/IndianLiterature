यं सुपर्णः परावतः श्येनस्य पुत्र आभरत् |शतचक्रं योऽह्यो वर्तनिः ||
yaṁ suparṇaḥ parāvataḥ śyenasya putra ābharat | śatacakraṁ yo3 'hyo vartaniḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yaṁ suparṇaḥ parāvataḥ śyenasya putra ābharat | śatacakraṁ yo3 'hyo vartaniḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: who
    • suparṇaḥ: the one with beautiful wings (often referring to the divine or the bird Garuda)
    • parāvataḥ: the one who is supreme or transcendent
    • śyenasya: of the eagle or falcon (symbolizing strength and vision)
    • putra: son
    • ābharat: has brought or has carried
    • śatacakraṁ: the hundred-spoked wheel (symbolizing the cycle of life or the universe)
    • yo: who
    • 3 'hyo: from the one who is beyond
    • vartaniḥ: exists or resides
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine entity, often interpreted as Garuda, who is a symbol of strength, speed, and the ability to transcend limitations.
    • The reference to "the son of the eagle" suggests a lineage of power and wisdom, indicating that this divine being carries the essence of strength and vision.
    • The "hundred-spoked wheel" can be seen as a representation of the universe's cycles, including life, death, and rebirth, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • The phrase "from the one who is beyond" suggests a connection to a higher spiritual realm, indicating that this divine presence is not only powerful but also transcendent.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, individuals often seek strength and guidance during challenging times. This mantra can be a source of inspiration, reminding us to tap into our inner strength and wisdom.
    • The concept of the "hundred-spoked wheel" can be related to the cycles we experience in life, such as the seasons, personal growth, and the journey of learning from experiences.
    • In leadership, the qualities of vision and strength, akin to the eagle, are essential. Leaders can embody the essence of this mantra by guiding their teams with clarity and purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the divine strength and wisdom available to us. It encourages us to embrace our inner power and to recognize the cycles of life that shape our experiences.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in moments of decision-making, personal challenges, or when seeking clarity in our life's path.
    • Ultimately, it signifies the importance of connecting with a higher purpose and understanding our place within the vast universe.
