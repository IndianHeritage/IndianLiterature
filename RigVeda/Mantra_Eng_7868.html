मन्त्र: प्रवृण्वन्तो अभियुजः सुष्वये वरिवोविदः |स्वयं स्तोत्रे वयस्कृतः ||
pravṛṇvanto abhiyujaḥ suṣvaye varivovidaḥ | svayaṁ stotre vayaskṛtaḥ ||
  • pravṛṇvanto: This word can be translated as "those who are engaged" or "those who are active." It signifies individuals who are proactive and take initiative in their actions.
  • abhiyujaḥ: This term means "those who are united" or "those who are connected." It emphasizes the importance of collaboration and unity among individuals.
  • suṣvaye: This word translates to "those who are well-versed" or "those who are knowledgeable." It highlights the value of wisdom and understanding in one's endeavors.
  • varivovidaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who are skilled" or "those who possess expertise." It points to the significance of skill and proficiency in achieving success.
  • svayaṁ: This word means "by oneself" or "on one's own." It suggests self-reliance and the ability to take responsibility for one's actions.
  • stotre: This term refers to "praise" or "worship." It indicates the importance of acknowledging and honoring the divine or the higher powers in one's life.
  • vayaskṛtaḥ: This word translates to "those who are strong" or "those who are vigorous." It emphasizes the need for strength and resilience in facing challenges.

The mantra as a whole can be interpreted as a call to action for individuals to engage actively in their pursuits, unite with others, seek knowledge, develop skills, take responsibility, and honor the divine. It reflects a holistic approach to life, where personal growth and community collaboration are essential for success.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • In the workplace: Employees who are proactive (pravṛṇvanto) and work collaboratively (abhiyujaḥ) can create a positive work environment. Knowledgeable individuals (suṣvaye) can mentor others, while skilled workers (varivovidaḥ) contribute to the overall success of projects.
  • In education: Students who take initiative in their learning (pravṛṇvanto) and collaborate with peers (abhiyujaḥ) can enhance their understanding. Seeking knowledge (suṣvaye) and developing skills (varivovidaḥ) are crucial for academic success.
  • In personal development: Individuals who rely on themselves (svayaṁ) and acknowledge their strengths (vayaskṛtaḥ) can overcome personal challenges. Recognizing the importance of praise (stotre) can foster gratitude and a positive mindset.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individual efforts and collective success. It encourages us to be proactive, seek knowledge, develop skills, and honor the divine, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and successful life.
