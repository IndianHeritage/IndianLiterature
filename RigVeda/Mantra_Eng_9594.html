मन्त्र: दिवि स्वनो यतते भूम्योपर्य अनन्तं शुष्ममुद इयर्ति भानुना | अभ्रादिव प्र स्तनयन्ति वृष्टयः सिन्धुर यद एति वृषभो न रोर्वत ||
divi svano yatate bhūmyopary anantaṁ śuṣmam ud iyarti bhānunā | abhrād iva pra stanayanti vṛṣṭayaḥ sindhur yad eti vṛṣabho na roruvat ||
  • Divi svano yatate: This phrase can be translated as "the sound that arises in the heavens." It signifies the celestial or divine vibrations that resonate in the universe.
  • Bhūmyopary anantaṁ: This means "above the earth, the infinite." It suggests the vastness and boundlessness of the universe, emphasizing the connection between the earth and the cosmos.
  • Śuṣmam ud iyarti bhānunā: This translates to "the sun rises and dispels the darkness." It symbolizes enlightenment, knowledge, and the dispelling of ignorance, akin to how the sun brings light to the world.
  • Abhrād iva pra stanayanti: This means "like clouds that pour down." It refers to the nurturing aspect of nature, where clouds bring rain, essential for life and growth.
  • Vṛṣṭayaḥ sindhur yad eti: This translates to "the rivers flow as the rain falls." It illustrates the cycle of water in nature, highlighting the interconnectedness of elements in the ecosystem.
  • Vṛṣabho na roruvat: This means "the bull does not bellow." It signifies strength and stability, suggesting that true power does not need to announce itself loudly.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of nature's cycles, the relationship between the celestial and terrestrial realms, and the importance of knowledge and enlightenment. It reflects the harmony in the universe and the interconnectedness of all elements.
  • Real-life Application: In our daily lives, this mantra can inspire us to seek knowledge and enlightenment, much like the sun dispelling darkness. It encourages us to appreciate the natural cycles around us, such as the importance of water for sustenance and growth. Additionally, it reminds us that true strength lies in quiet confidence rather than loud proclamations.
