गन्तेयान्ति सवनाः हरिभ्यां बभ्रिः वज्रम् पापिः सोमं ददिः गाः |कर्ताऽवीरं नर्यं सर्ववीरं श्रोताः हवं गृणतः स्तोमवाहाः ||
ganteyānti savanā haribhyām babhrir vajram papiḥ somaṁ dadir gāḥ | kartā vīraṁ naryaṁ sarvavīraṁ śrotā havaṁ gṛṇataḥ stomavāhāḥ ||
  • ganteyānti: This term can be interpreted as "they who are called upon" or "those who are invoked." It signifies the act of summoning or invoking divine forces or energies.
  • savanā: Refers to the offerings made during rituals, particularly in the context of Vedic sacrifices. It emphasizes the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • haribhyām: This word means "to the divine beings" or "to the gods," indicating the recipients of the offerings and prayers.
  • babhrir: Translates to "bearing" or "carrying," suggesting the act of holding or sustaining something significant.
  • vajram: Means "thunderbolt" or "weapon," symbolizing strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • papiḥ: This term can mean "the one who is pure" or "the one who is righteous," indicating a connection to virtue and moral integrity.
  • somaṁ: Refers to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration.
  • dadir: Means "to give" or "to bestow," highlighting the act of offering or sharing something valuable.
  • gāḥ: Translates to "songs" or "hymns," representing the expression of devotion and reverence through music and poetry.
  • kartā: Means "the doer" or "the creator," emphasizing the role of individuals in manifesting their intentions and actions.
  • vīraṁ: Refers to "the hero" or "the brave one," symbolizing courage and valor in facing challenges.
  • naryaṁ: This term can mean "the noble one" or "the virtuous," indicating the qualities of honor and integrity.
  • sarvavīraṁ: Translates to "all heroes" or "all brave ones," suggesting a collective strength and unity among those who strive for righteousness.
  • śrotā: Means "the listener" or "the one who hears," emphasizing the importance of being receptive to wisdom and guidance.
  • havaṁ: Refers to "the offering" or "the invocation," indicating the act of calling upon the divine through prayers and rituals.
  • gṛṇataḥ: Means "to sing" or "to praise," highlighting the significance of expressing gratitude and reverence through vocalization.
  • stomavāhāḥ: Refers to "the one who carries the hymns," indicating the role of individuals in transmitting and preserving sacred knowledge.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of invoking divine energies through rituals and offerings. It emphasizes the importance of purity, strength, and collective courage in the pursuit of righteousness. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community gatherings, spiritual practices, and personal endeavors where individuals seek to connect with higher powers, express gratitude, and foster unity among people. The mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of intention, action, and the power of collective devotion in overcoming challenges and achieving noble goals.
