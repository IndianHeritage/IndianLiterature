स हि धीभिर हव्यो अस्त्य उग्र ईशानकृत्न महति वृततूर्ये |स तोकसातां तनये स वज्री वितन्तसाय्यो अभवत समत्सु ||
sa hi dhībhir havyo asty ugra īśānakṛn mahati vṛtratūrye | sa tokasātā tanaye sa vajrī vitantasāyyo abhavat samatsu ||
  • sa hi dhībhir havyo asty: This phrase can be translated to "Indeed, he is worthy of worship through wisdom." Here, "sa" refers to a divine entity or force, while "dhībhir" signifies wisdom or intellect. The term "havyo" indicates something that is worthy of offerings or reverence.
  • ugra īśānakṛn: This translates to "the powerful lord who creates." "Ugra" means powerful or fierce, and "īśāna" refers to a lord or ruler. "Kṛn" implies the act of creation or making. This part emphasizes the might and creative power of the divine.
  • mahati vṛtratūrye: This can be interpreted as "in the great battle against Vritra." "Mahati" means great, and "vṛtratūrye" refers to the battle against Vritra, a serpent or dragon symbolizing chaos and obstacles. This signifies the struggle against challenges and adversities.
  • sa tokasātā tanaye: This translates to "he who is the protector of the offspring." "Tokasātā" refers to progeny or offspring, and "tanaye" means to protect or nurture. This highlights the divine's role in safeguarding future generations.
  • sa vajrī vitantasāyyo: This means "he who wields the thunderbolt." "Vajrī" refers to the thunderbolt, a symbol of strength and power, while "vitantasāyyo" indicates the one who spreads or extends this power. This part emphasizes the protective and destructive capabilities of the divine.
  • abhavat samatsu: This translates to "he became among the strong." "Abhavat" means to become, and "samatsu" refers to the strong or powerful. This signifies the divine's embodiment of strength and resilience.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of divine power, wisdom, and protection. It highlights the importance of intellect in worship, the might of the divine in overcoming chaos, and the nurturing role of the divine in safeguarding future generations. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to situations where individuals face challenges or adversities, reminding them to seek wisdom and strength. It also emphasizes the importance of protecting and nurturing the next generation, encouraging a sense of responsibility towards family and community. Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine's role in our lives and the strength we can draw from it in our daily struggles.
