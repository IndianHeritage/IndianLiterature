अहेळमाना उप याहि यज्ञं तुभ्यम पवन्त इन्दवः सुतासः |गावो न वज्रिन स्वं ओको अच्छेन्द्रा गहि प्रथमो यज्ञियानाम् ||
aheḻamāna upa yāhi yajñaṁ tubhyam pavanta indavaḥ sutāsaḥ | gāvo na vajrin svam oko acchendrā gahi prathamo yajñiyānām ||
  • Mantra: "aheḻamāna upa yāhi yajñaṁ tubhyam pavanta indavaḥ sutāsaḥ | gāvo na vajrin svam oko acchendrā gahi prathamo yajñiyānām ||"
  • Translation: "O Indra, come here to this sacrifice, you who are the bringer of the sweet drink. The cows are not to be harmed; may you, the first among the sacrificial offerings, accept our offerings."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • aheḻamāna: Invoking or calling upon.
    • upa: Here, near, or towards.
    • yāhi: Come or approach.
    • yajñaṁ: Sacrifice or offering.
    • tubhyam: To you.
    • pavanta: Bringer or bestower.
    • indavaḥ: Indra, the deity associated with rain and thunder.
    • sutāsaḥ: Sweet drink or offerings.
    • gāvo: Cows, symbolizing wealth and nourishment.
    • na: Not.
    • vajrin: Harmed or injured.
    • svam: Own or personal.
    • oko: Home or abode.
    • acchendrā: O Indra, the powerful one.
    • gahi: Accept or take.
    • prathamo: First or foremost.
    • yajñiyānām: Among the sacrificial offerings.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of inviting divine presence during rituals and sacrifices.
    • It highlights the need for protection and care for all living beings, symbolized by the mention of cows, which are revered in many cultures.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies the desire for prosperity, abundance, and the blessings of nature.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern rituals and ceremonies, this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings for peace and prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder to treat all living beings with respect and care, promoting a harmonious relationship with nature.
    • In agricultural practices, the invocation of Indra can symbolize the importance of rain and favorable weather for crops, reflecting the interconnectedness of human efforts and natural forces.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of reverence, gratitude, and the interconnectedness of life.
    • By understanding and applying its teachings, individuals can foster a sense of responsibility towards nature and community.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a holistic approach to life, where spiritual practices align with ethical living and environmental stewardship.
