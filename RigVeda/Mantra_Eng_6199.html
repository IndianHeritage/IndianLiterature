उद उ स्वानेभिर ईरता उद रथैर उद उ वायुभिः |उत स्तोमैः पृष्णिमातरः ||
ud u svānebhir īrata ud rathair ud u vāyubhiḥ | ut stomaiḥ pṛśnimātaraḥ ||
  • ud u svānebhir īrata: This phrase can be translated as "with the horses, let us rise up." Here, "ud" signifies upward movement or elevation, while "svānebhir" refers to horses, which symbolize strength and energy. In real life, this can be likened to the idea of harnessing one's inner strength and resources to achieve goals.
  • ud rathair: This translates to "with chariots." Chariots represent progress and the journey of life. In a modern context, this can be seen as the vehicles (literal or metaphorical) that help us navigate through challenges and reach our destinations.
  • ud u vāyubhiḥ: This means "with the winds." The winds symbolize change and the unseen forces that influence our lives. In real life, this can be related to the various external factors, such as opportunities and challenges, that can propel us forward if we are open to them.
  • ut stomaiḥ pṛśnimātaraḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "with hymns, let us invoke the divine." "Stomaiḥ" refers to hymns or praises, and "pṛśnimātaraḥ" suggests a connection to the divine or higher powers. This highlights the importance of spirituality and gratitude in our lives, reminding us to acknowledge and appreciate the forces that guide us.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of rising above challenges with strength, utilizing the resources at our disposal, and being open to the changes that life brings. It encourages us to invoke the divine through our actions and words, fostering a sense of gratitude and connection to something greater than ourselves. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as pursuing personal goals, overcoming obstacles, and maintaining a positive outlook in the face of adversity. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate resilience and a deeper sense of purpose in their lives.
