मो षु त्वा वाघतश्चनारे अस्मन् निरीरमन् |आरात्ताच्चित्सधमादं न आगहिः वाऽसन्नुप श्रुधि ||
mo ṣu tvā vāghataś canāre asman ni rīraman | ārāttāc cit sadhamādaṁ na ā gahīha vā sann upa śrudhi ||
  • mo ṣu tvā: This phrase can be translated as "I invoke you" or "I call upon you." It signifies a deep yearning or request for connection with a higher power or divine entity.
  • vāghataś: This term refers to "the one who is endowed with speech" or "the one who communicates." It highlights the importance of communication and expression in our lives.
  • canāre: This word can be interpreted as "to be present" or "to be here." It emphasizes the significance of being in the moment and aware of one's surroundings.
  • asman ni rīraman: This phrase translates to "may you come to us" or "may you be with us." It expresses a desire for the divine presence to be among us, guiding and supporting us.
  • ārāttāc: This term means "from the source" or "from the origin." It suggests that the wisdom or guidance sought comes from a fundamental source of knowledge.
  • cit: This word translates to "consciousness" or "awareness." It highlights the importance of being aware and mindful in our actions and thoughts.
  • sadhamādaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the good path" or "the righteous way." It signifies the pursuit of virtue and ethical living.
  • na ā gahīha: This phrase means "do not hold back" or "do not restrain." It encourages openness and the willingness to embrace experiences fully.
  • vā sann upa śrudhi: This translates to "may you listen to us" or "may you pay attention to us." It is a plea for understanding and connection, urging the divine to hear our prayers and concerns.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound yearning for divine connection, communication, and guidance. It emphasizes the importance of being present, aware, and open to the wisdom that comes from a higher source. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of prayer, meditation, or reflection, where individuals seek clarity and support in their lives. It serves as a reminder to pursue a righteous path while remaining receptive to the guidance that surrounds us. By invoking this mantra, one can cultivate a deeper sense of awareness and connection to the divine, fostering a more meaningful and purposeful existence.
