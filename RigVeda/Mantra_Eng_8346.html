नाभा पृथिव्या धरुणो महो दिवोऽपाम ऊर्मौ सिन्धुष्वन्तर उक्षितः | इन्द्रस्य वज्रो वृषभो विभूवसुः सोमो हृदे पवते चारु मत्सरः ||
nābhā pṛthivyā dharuṇo maho divo3 'pām ūrmau sindhuṣv antar ukṣitaḥ | indrasya vajro vṛṣabho vibhūvasuḥ somo hṛde pavate cāru matsaraḥ ||
  • nābhā pṛthivyā: This phrase translates to "the navel of the earth." In a metaphorical sense, it represents the central point or the source of life and sustenance on Earth.
  • dharuṇo maho divo: This means "the great support of the heavens." It signifies the strength and stability provided by the divine or cosmic forces that govern the universe.
  • pām ūrmau sindhuṣv: This translates to "may we be nourished by the waves of the oceans." It emphasizes the importance of water and nourishment in sustaining life, drawing a parallel to how oceans provide resources and sustenance.
  • antar ukṣitaḥ: This means "nurtured from within." It suggests that true strength and nourishment come from within oneself, highlighting the importance of inner resources and self-awareness.
  • indrasya vajro: This translates to "the thunderbolt of Indra." Indra is a deity associated with strength and power, and the thunderbolt symbolizes the force of nature and divine intervention.
  • vṛṣabho vibhūvasuḥ: This means "the bull, the possessor of wealth." The bull is often a symbol of strength and fertility, representing prosperity and abundance in life.
  • somo hṛde pavate: This translates to "the soma flows in the heart." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration, suggesting that true wisdom and vitality come from the heart.
  • cāru matsaraḥ: This means "the beautiful, delightful one." It signifies the joy and beauty found in life, encouraging appreciation for the positive aspects of existence.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the interconnectedness of life, nature, and the divine. It emphasizes the importance of inner strength, nourishment from the environment, and the beauty of existence. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to our understanding of sustainability, the need for self-awareness, and the appreciation of the resources provided by nature. By recognizing our connection to the earth and the cosmos, we can cultivate a sense of gratitude and responsibility towards our environment, leading to a more harmonious existence.
