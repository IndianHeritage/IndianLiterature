यस्येमे हिमवान्तो महित्वा यस्य समुद्रं रसया सहाहुः |यस्येमाः प्रदिशो यस्य बाहू कसमै देवाय हविषा विदेम ||
yasyeme himavanto mahitvā yasya samudraṁ rasayā sahāhuḥ | yasyemāḥ pradiśo yasya bāhū kasmai devāya haviṣā vidhema ||
  • yasyeme himavanto mahitvā: This phrase refers to the greatness of the Himalayas. The Himalayas are often seen as a symbol of strength and stability in Indian culture. They are not just geographical features but are also considered sacred in many traditions.
  • yasya samudraṁ rasayā sahāhuḥ: This part speaks of the oceans, which are vast and full of life. The oceans are essential for sustaining life on Earth, providing resources, and influencing climate. The term "rasayā" can be interpreted as the essence or the taste of the ocean, highlighting its richness.
  • yasyemāḥ pradiśo: This phrase can be understood as referring to the directions or the various regions of the world. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all places and the importance of harmony among them.
  • yasya bāhū: This refers to the arms or strength of the divine. It symbolizes the protective and nurturing aspects of the divine, suggesting that the divine is always there to support and guide us.
  • kasmai devāya haviṣā vidhema: This concluding line is a call to offer our prayers and offerings to the divine. It signifies the importance of devotion and the act of seeking blessings from a higher power.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of nature's grandeur and the divine's omnipresence. It teaches us to respect and revere the natural world, recognizing the strength and resources it provides. In real life, this can be applied by fostering a sense of environmental stewardship, understanding the importance of nature in our lives, and cultivating a sense of gratitude towards the divine. By acknowledging the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe, we can strive for harmony in our actions and relationships, ultimately leading to a more balanced and fulfilling life.
