प्र यज्ञ एतु हेत्वो न सप्तिर उद यच्छध्वं समनसो घृताचिः | स्तृणीत बर्हिर अध्वराय साधूर्ध्वा शोचींषि देवयून्य अस्तुः ||
pra yajña etu hetvo na saptir ud yacchadhvaṁ samanaso ghṛtācīḥ | stṛṇīta barhir adhvarāya sādhūrdhvā śocīṁṣi devayūny asthuḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "Let us perform the sacrifice with the right intention; may the seven (sacred) rivers flow towards us, and may the offerings be made with clarity and purity. Let the sacred grass be spread for the ritual, and may the divine beings be pleased with our offerings."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra yajña: "Let us perform the sacrifice" - This emphasizes the importance of intention and purpose in any action.
    • etu hetvo: "with the right intention" - Highlights the significance of motivation behind actions.
    • na saptir: "the seven (sacred) rivers" - Symbolizes abundance and the flow of life, akin to the rivers nourishing the land.
    • ud yacchadhvaṁ: "may they flow towards us" - A call for blessings and prosperity.
    • samanaso: "with clarity and purity" - Stresses the need for purity in thoughts and actions.
    • ghṛtācīḥ: "offerings" - Represents the gifts we present to the divine, which can be material or spiritual.
    • stṛṇīta barhir: "let the sacred grass be spread" - Refers to the preparation of the ritual space, symbolizing respect and sanctity.
    • adhvarāya: "for the ritual" - Indicates the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
    • sādhūrdhvā: "may the divine beings be pleased" - A wish for divine favor and blessings.
    • śocīṁṣi: "with our offerings" - Reinforces the act of giving and sharing.
    • devayūny asthuḥ: "may the divine beings be pleased" - A reminder of the relationship between humans and the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a wedding ceremony, the couple performs rituals with the intention of seeking blessings for a prosperous and harmonious life together, similar to the intention behind the yajña.
    • In community gatherings, people often come together to share food and resources, reflecting the idea of offerings and abundance.
    • In personal development, individuals may set intentions for their goals, ensuring that their motivations are pure and aligned with their values.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on intention, purity, and the connection between humans and the divine. It teaches us that our actions, when performed with the right mindset and respect, can lead to blessings and abundance in our lives. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal rituals, community events, or individual pursuits, the principles of intention and purity can guide us towards a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
