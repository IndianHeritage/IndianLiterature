अन्तिवामा दूरे अमित्रमुच्चोर्वीं गव्यूतिमभयं कृधī नः |यावय द्वेष आ भरā वसूनि चोदय राधो गृणते मघोनि ||
antivāmā dūre amitram ucchorvīṁ gavyūtim abhayaṁ kṛdhī naḥ | yāvaya dveṣa ā bharā vasūni codaya rādho gṛṇate maghoni ||
  • Antivāmā: This term can be interpreted as "those who are far away" or "distant." It suggests a separation from negativity or adversaries.
  • Dūre: Meaning "far away," it emphasizes the desire to keep enemies or negative influences at a distance.
  • Amitram: Translated as "enemy" or "adversary," it represents any form of opposition or negativity in life.
  • Ucchorvīṁ: This can be understood as "high" or "elevated," indicating a state of being that is above negativity.
  • Gavyūtim: Referring to "wealth" or "prosperity," it signifies the abundance that one seeks in life.
  • Abhayaṁ: Meaning "fearless," it denotes a state of confidence and security, free from fear.
  • Kṛdhī naḥ: This phrase translates to "may you create for us," indicating a plea for positive creation and support.
  • Yāvaya: Meaning "as long as," it suggests a duration or continuity in the request being made.
  • Dveṣa: Translated as "hatred" or "enmity," it represents the negative emotions that one wishes to avoid.
  • Ā bharā: This means "bring forth" or "bestow," indicating a request for positive attributes or blessings.
  • Vasūni: Referring to "wealth" or "prosperity," it emphasizes the desire for abundance in life.
  • Codaya: Meaning "inspire" or "encourage," it indicates a call for motivation and support.
  • Rādho: This term can be interpreted as "to praise" or "to celebrate," suggesting an acknowledgment of the positive forces in life.
  • Gṛṇate: Meaning "to sing" or "to chant," it indicates the act of expressing gratitude or devotion.
  • Maghoni: Referring to "greatness" or "power," it signifies the strength and influence of positive energies.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of distancing oneself from negativity and adversaries while seeking prosperity, fearlessness, and inspiration. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, conflict resolution, and fostering positive relationships. By focusing on the positive aspects of life and seeking to eliminate hatred and negativity, individuals can create a more harmonious and prosperous existence. This mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate an environment of support and abundance, encouraging individuals to rise above challenges and celebrate their achievements.
