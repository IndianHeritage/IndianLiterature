एह हरī ब्रह्मयुजā शग्मा वक्षतः सखायम् |गीर्भिः श्रुतं गिरवणसम ||
eha harī brahmayujā śagmā vakṣataḥ sakhāyam | gīrbhiḥ śrutaṁ girvaṇasam ||
  • eha: This word translates to "here" or "in this place." It signifies the importance of the present moment and the immediate environment.
  • harī: Referring to "the divine" or "the Lord," often associated with Vishnu or a higher power. This indicates a connection to spirituality and the divine presence in our lives.
  • brahmayujā: This term can be broken down into "Brahma," which represents the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit, and "yujā," meaning to unite or connect. Together, it suggests a union with the divine or the ultimate truth.
  • śagmā: This word can be interpreted as "strength" or "power." It emphasizes the importance of inner strength and resilience in facing life's challenges.
  • vakṣataḥ: Meaning "from the heart" or "from the core," this word highlights the significance of sincerity and authenticity in our actions and intentions.
  • sakhāyam: This translates to "friendship" or "companionship." It underscores the value of relationships and the support we receive from others in our journey.
  • gīrbhiḥ: This word means "with songs" or "with hymns," indicating the power of expression and communication, particularly through art and music.
  • śrutaṁ: Meaning "heard" or "listened to," it emphasizes the importance of being receptive and open to wisdom and knowledge.
  • girvaṇasam: This term refers to "the one who speaks" or "the speaker," suggesting the role of communication in sharing knowledge and experiences.

In summary, this mantra emphasizes the significance of being present in the moment, connecting with the divine, and recognizing the strength that comes from authenticity and companionship. It highlights the importance of communication and the sharing of wisdom through art and expression.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various ways:

  • In personal relationships, it encourages fostering genuine connections and supporting one another through challenges.
  • In professional settings, it highlights the importance of teamwork and collaboration, where each individual's strengths contribute to a common goal.
  • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder to seek a deeper connection with the divine and to be present in our spiritual journey.
  • In creative endeavors, it inspires individuals to express themselves through art, music, or writing, sharing their unique perspectives and experiences.

Overall, the mantra serves as a guiding principle for living a meaningful and connected life, emphasizing the interplay between the divine, personal strength, and the importance of relationships.
