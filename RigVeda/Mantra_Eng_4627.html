स नो बोधि पुरोळाशं रराणः पिबा तु सोमं गोर्जीकम इन्द्र |एदं बर्हिर यजमानस्य सिदोरुं कृधि त्वायत उ लोकम् ||
sa no bodhi puroḻāśaṁ rarāṇaḥ pibā tu somaṁ goṛjīkam indra | edam barhir yajamānasya sīdoruṁ kṛdhi tvāyata u lokam ||
  • sa no bodhi: This phrase translates to "may we attain wisdom." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in our lives. For example, in a real-life scenario, pursuing education and self-improvement can lead to better decision-making and personal growth.
  • puroḻāśaṁ: This term refers to "the one who is well-versed or knowledgeable." It signifies the value of expertise and guidance. In practical terms, seeking advice from mentors or experts in a particular field can enhance our skills and lead to success.
  • rarāṇaḥ: This word means "to be strong or powerful." It suggests the need for strength in overcoming challenges. For instance, athletes train rigorously to build physical strength, which can be paralleled with mental resilience in facing life's obstacles.
  • pibā tu somaṁ: This phrase means "drink the soma." Soma is often associated with a divine drink that grants immortality and enlightenment. In modern life, this can be interpreted as seeking inspiration and rejuvenation through experiences, such as meditation, nature walks, or engaging in creative activities.
  • goṛjīkam: This term can be understood as "the one who brings joy or happiness." It highlights the importance of joy in our lives. For example, spending time with loved ones or engaging in hobbies can bring happiness and fulfillment.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power and authority. This can be related to leadership in our lives, where effective leaders inspire and guide others towards a common goal, much like Indra leads the deities.
  • edam barhir yajamānasya: This translates to "this offering of the worshipper." It signifies the act of giving and devotion. In real life, acts of charity and community service reflect this principle, where individuals contribute to the welfare of others.
  • sīdoruṁ: This word means "to sit or reside." It can be interpreted as finding a place of stability and peace. For instance, creating a peaceful home environment can lead to mental well-being and harmony.
  • kṛdhi tvāyata: This phrase means "may you create or manifest." It emphasizes the power of intention and action. In practical terms, setting goals and working towards them can lead to the realization of our dreams.
  • u lokam: This translates to "the world or the universe." It signifies our connection to the larger cosmos. In real life, understanding our place in the world can foster a sense of responsibility towards the environment and society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking wisdom, strength, joy, and connection with the universe. Its significance lies in the holistic approach to life, encouraging individuals to pursue knowledge, foster relationships, and contribute positively to society. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide personal development, enhance leadership qualities, and promote a sense of community and responsibility. By embodying the teachings of this mantra, individuals can lead more fulfilling and meaningful lives.
