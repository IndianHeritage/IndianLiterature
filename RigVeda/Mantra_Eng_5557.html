नū देवासो वरिवः कर्तना नो भूता नो विश्वे 'वसे सजोषाः |सम अस्मे इषं वसवो ददीरन् यūयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
nū devāso varivaḥ kartanā no bhūta no viśve 'vase sajoṣāḥ | sam asme iṣaṁ vasavo dadīran yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Translation: "O divine beings, may you be propitious to us; may you not be angry, may you be gracious to all. May we be endowed with wealth and prosperity, and may you protect us always with your blessings."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • nū: A call or invocation, often used to address deities.
    • devāso: Refers to divine beings or gods.
    • varivaḥ: May you be propitious or favorable.
    • kartanā: Refers to actions or deeds.
    • no: Us or our.
    • bhūta: Not angry or hostile.
    • viśve: All or universal.
    • 'vase: To be gracious or benevolent.
    • sajoṣāḥ: With joy or happiness.
    • sam: Together or in harmony.
    • asme: We are or may we be.
    • iṣaṁ: Wealth or prosperity.
    • vasavo: Referring to the Vasus, a group of deities associated with abundance.
    • dadīran: May you give or bestow.
    • yūyam: You (plural), referring to the divine beings.
    • pāta: Protect or guard.
    • svastibhiḥ: With blessings or auspiciousness.
    • sadā: Always or forever.
    • naḥ: Us or our.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a wedding ceremony, couples often invoke blessings from divine beings for a prosperous and harmonious life together, similar to the invocation in this mantra.
    • During festivals, people pray for the well-being of their families and communities, seeking the favor of the divine for peace and prosperity.
    • In business, entrepreneurs may recite this mantra to seek blessings for success and protection from challenges.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for divine blessings, protection, and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of harmony with the divine and the community. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as personal prayers, community gatherings, and significant life events where blessings and goodwill are sought. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the need for divine grace in achieving a fulfilling and prosperous life.
