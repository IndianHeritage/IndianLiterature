धियं पूषा जिंवतु विश्वमिन्वो रयिं सोमो रयिपतिर दधातु |अवतु देव्य अदितिर अनर्वा बृहद् वदेम विदथे सुवीराः ||
dhiyam pūṣā jinvatu viśvaminvo rayiṁ somo rayipatir dadhātu | avatu devy aditir anarvā bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ ||
  • dhiyam pūṣā jinvatu: This phrase can be translated as "May the divine nourishment of intellect thrive." Here, "dhiyam" refers to intellect or understanding, and "pūṣā" signifies nourishment or sustenance. In real life, this can be related to the importance of education and knowledge in personal and professional growth. For example, a student who seeks knowledge and understanding in their studies is nourishing their intellect.
  • viśvaminvo rayiṁ: This translates to "May we attain all forms of wealth." "Viśvaminvo" refers to all-encompassing or universal, while "rayiṁ" means wealth or prosperity. In a practical context, this can be seen in the pursuit of holistic success, where individuals strive for not just financial wealth but also emotional, spiritual, and social prosperity. For instance, a business that values ethical practices and community welfare alongside profit maximization embodies this principle.
  • somo rayipatir dadhātu: This means "May the moon, the lord of wealth, bestow upon us." "Somo" refers to the moon, often associated with calmness and prosperity, while "rayipatir" indicates the lord of wealth. This can be related to the idea of seeking blessings for abundance in life. For example, during festivals, people often pray for prosperity and well-being, reflecting this desire for divine blessings in their endeavors.
  • avatu devy aditir: This translates to "May the divine Aditi protect us." "Aditi" is a goddess representing the infinite and the mother of all gods. This phrase emphasizes the need for divine protection and guidance in our lives. In real life, this can be seen in the way people seek support from their families, communities, or spiritual beliefs during challenging times, highlighting the importance of a protective and nurturing environment.
  • anarvā bṛhad vadema: This means "May we speak of the unbounded and great." "Anarvā" signifies the limitless or unbounded, while "bṛhad" means great or vast. This can be related to the pursuit of lofty ideals and aspirations. For instance, individuals who aim to make significant contributions to society, such as scientists working on climate change solutions, embody this spirit of striving for greatness.
  • vidathe suvīrāḥ: This translates to "In the assembly of the brave." "Vidathe" refers to an assembly or gathering, while "suvīrāḥ" means the brave or the valiant. This highlights the importance of community and collaboration in achieving goals. In real life, this can be seen in teamwork, where individuals come together to support each other in overcoming challenges, such as in sports teams or community service projects.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the nourishment of intellect, the pursuit of wealth in all forms, the seeking of divine blessings, the aspiration for greatness, and the importance of community. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, from personal development and education to community collaboration and spiritual growth. By embodying these principles, individuals can strive for a balanced and fulfilling life, contributing positively to themselves and society.
