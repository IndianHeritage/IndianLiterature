मोग्हं अन्नं विन्दते अप्रचेताः सत्यं बृवीमि वध इति स तस्य |नार्यमणं पुष्यति नो सखायं केवलाघो भवति केवलादि ||
mogham annaṁ vindate apracetāḥ satyam bravīmi vadha it sa tasya | nāryamaṇam puṣyati no sakhāyaṁ kevalāgho bhavati kevalādī ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mogham: futile or useless
    • annaṁ: food
    • vindate: obtains
    • apracetāḥ: those who are unwise or ignorant
    • satyam: truth
    • bravīmi: I speak
    • vadha: killing or destruction
    • it: thus
    • sa: he or that
    • tasya: of him or that person
    • nāryamaṇam: the woman’s virtue or character
    • puṣyati: nourishes or flourishes
    • no: not
    • sakhāyaṁ: friendship
    • kevalāgho: only sin or evil
    • bhavati: becomes
    • kevalādī: only the beginning or essence
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the futility of obtaining food or sustenance without wisdom.
    • It emphasizes the importance of truth and the consequences of ignorance.
    • The phrase "satyam bravīmi" indicates the speaker's commitment to truth, suggesting that truth is essential for a meaningful life.
    • The mantra warns against the destruction that can arise from ignorance and the lack of virtuous character.
    • It highlights that true nourishment comes from virtuous actions and wise choices, rather than mere physical sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, individuals who act without wisdom may create a toxic environment, leading to conflicts and inefficiencies.
    • In personal relationships, those who do not value truth and virtue may find their connections shallow and unfulfilling.
    • In society, ignorance can lead to harmful decisions that affect the community, such as neglecting education or health.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of wisdom and truth in our lives.
    • It teaches that true nourishment comes not just from physical food but from virtuous living and wise choices.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to healthier relationships, better decision-making, and a more harmonious society.
