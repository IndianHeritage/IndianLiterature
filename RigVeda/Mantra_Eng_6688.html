असद् अत्र सुवीर्यम् उता त्यद् आश्वश्व्यम् | देवानां य इन् मनो यजमान इयक्षत्य अभीद् अयजनो भुवत् ||
asad atra suvīryam uta tyad āśvaśvyam | devānāṁ ya in mano yajamāna iyakṣaty abhīd ayajvano bhuvat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asad: non-existent, false, or unreal
    • atra: here, in this place
    • suvīryam: good strength, valor, or power
    • uta: and, also
    • tyad: that, which
    • āśvaśvyam: related to horses, or the qualities of horses
    • devānāṁ: of the gods
    • ya: who
    • in: in, within
    • mano: mind, thought
    • yajamāna: the one who performs the sacrifice
    • iyakṣaty: protects, guards
    • abhīd: may be, might be
    • ayajvano: of the non-performers of sacrifice
    • bhuvat: becomes, is
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the contrast between the unreal and the real, emphasizing the importance of strength and valor.
    • The mention of "horses" symbolizes speed, power, and nobility, which are qualities that are revered in many cultures.
    • The reference to the gods indicates a connection to divine protection and guidance, suggesting that those who are righteous and perform their duties with sincerity will be safeguarded.
    • The phrase "yajamāna" refers to the individual who performs rituals, highlighting the significance of intention and action in spiritual practices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the mantra can be related to the pursuit of truth and authenticity. For instance, in a corporate environment, a leader who values transparency and integrity can inspire their team to work with valor and strength.
    • In sports, athletes often embody the qualities of "suvīryam" as they train hard and compete, demonstrating the importance of mental and physical strength.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra can remind individuals to protect and nurture their connections, ensuring that they are built on genuine intentions and mutual respect.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of striving for authenticity and strength in our actions.
    • It encourages individuals to engage in righteous deeds and to seek divine guidance in their endeavors.
    • Ultimately, the mantra highlights that true power comes from within, and by aligning our thoughts and actions with noble intentions, we can achieve greatness and protect what is valuable in our lives.
