मथीद यद ईं विभृतो मातरिश्वा गृहे-गृहे श्येतो जेन्यो भूत |आद ईं राज्ञे न सहीयसे सचा सन्न आद दूत्यम् भृगवाणो विवाय ||
mathīd yad īṁ vibhṛto mātariśvā gṛhe-gṛhe śyeto jenyo bhūt | ād īṁ rājñe na sahīyase sacā sann ā dūtya1m bhṛgavāṇo vivāya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mathīd: May we think or contemplate.
    • yad: That which.
    • īṁ: Energy or power.
    • vibhṛto: Distributed or spread out.
    • mātariśvā: The wind or the divine force.
    • gṛhe-gṛhe: In every home or household.
    • śyeto: May it dwell or reside.
    • jenyo: The one who is born or created.
    • bhūt: Beings or entities.
    • ād: Therefore or thus.
    • īṁ: Energy or power (repeated).
    • rājñe: To the king or ruler.
    • na: Not.
    • sahīyase: You can endure or tolerate.
    • sacā: And also.
    • sann: Being or existing.
    • ādūtya: As a messenger or envoy.
    • bhṛgavāṇo: The one who is from the lineage of Bhrigu.
    • vivāya: In union or together.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of contemplation and the spread of divine energy in every household.
    • It suggests that the divine force, represented by Mātariśvā (the wind), should reside in every home, nurturing all beings.
    • The mantra also highlights the role of leadership, indicating that a ruler must be able to endure challenges and act as a messenger of peace and unity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, this mantra can be applied by encouraging open communication and the sharing of positive energy among family members, fostering a harmonious environment.
    • In leadership roles, such as in a workplace, a leader can embody the qualities of endurance and unity, ensuring that their team feels supported and motivated to work together towards common goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for collective well-being and the nurturing of positive energy in every aspect of life.
    • It serves as a reminder that both individuals and leaders have a role in creating a supportive and harmonious environment, whether at home or in society.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can contribute to a more peaceful and united world.
