मन्त्र: श्रुतं मे मित्रावरुणा हवेमोता श्रुतं सदने विश्वतः सीम | श्रोतु नः श्रोतुरातिः सुश्रोतुः सुखेत्रा सिंधुर अद्भिः ||
śrutam me mitrāvaruṇā havemota śrutaṁ sadane viśvataḥ sīm | śrotu naḥ śroturātiḥ suśrotuḥ sukṣetrā sindhur adbhiḥ ||
  • śrutam me mitrāvaruṇā: This phrase translates to "May I hear (the teachings) from Mitra and Varuna." Mitra and Varuna are deities representing friendship and cosmic order, respectively. This suggests the importance of learning from divine sources and the value of friendship in understanding the world.
  • havemota: This means "we offer." It signifies the act of giving or offering, which is a central theme in many spiritual practices. In real life, this can be seen in the act of sharing knowledge, resources, or support with others.
  • śrutaṁ sadane viśvataḥ sīm: This translates to "the knowledge that is heard in all directions." It emphasizes the universality of knowledge and the importance of being open to learning from various sources. In practical terms, this can relate to the idea of being receptive to diverse perspectives in our daily lives.
  • śrotu naḥ: This means "may we hear." It is a plea for the ability to listen and absorb knowledge. In a real-world context, this can be applied to the importance of active listening in communication, fostering better relationships and understanding.
  • śroturātiḥ suśrotuḥ: This translates to "the one who hears well." It highlights the value of being a good listener and the benefits that come from it. In everyday life, this can be seen in effective teamwork, where listening leads to better collaboration and problem-solving.
  • sukṣetrā sindhur adbhiḥ: This means "the river of knowledge flows with waters." It symbolizes the abundance of knowledge available to us. In real life, this can be likened to the vast resources of information we have access to today, such as books, the internet, and educational institutions.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of learning, listening, and the importance of community and divine guidance in our pursuit of knowledge. In real life, it encourages us to be open to various sources of wisdom, to actively listen to others, and to share our knowledge generously. This approach can lead to personal growth, stronger relationships, and a more harmonious society.
