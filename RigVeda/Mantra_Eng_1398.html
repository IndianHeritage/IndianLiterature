ऋतस्य रश्मिम अनुयच्छमानā भद्रम्-भद्रं क्रतुम् अस्मासु धेहि |उषो नो अद्य सुहवā व्य उच्छास्मासु रायो मगवत्सु च स्युः ||
ṛtasya raśmim anuyacchamānā bhadram-bhadraṁ kratum asmāsu dhehi | uṣo no adya suhavā vy ucchāsmāsu rāyo maghavatsu ca syuḥ ||
  • ṛtasya raśmim anuyacchamānā: This phrase can be translated as "Following the rays of truth." Here, "ṛta" refers to the cosmic order or truth, and "raśmi" means rays or beams. In real life, this can be understood as aligning oneself with honesty and integrity in all actions.
  • bhadram-bhadraṁ kratum asmāsu dhehi: This translates to "Bestow upon us auspicious thoughts and intentions." The repetition of "bhadra" emphasizes the importance of positivity and good intentions. In practical terms, this can be seen in how we approach our daily tasks, ensuring that our thoughts are constructive and beneficial.
  • uṣo no adya suhavā: This means "May the dawn bring us good fortune today." "Uṣa" refers to dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and opportunities. In everyday life, this can be related to starting each day with optimism and the belief that good things will come our way.
  • vy ucchāsmāsu rāyo maghavatsu ca syuḥ: This translates to "May we attain wealth and prosperity." "Rāyo" refers to wealth, and "maghavat" signifies abundance. This can be applied in real life by setting goals for financial stability and working diligently towards achieving them.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of aligning oneself with truth, fostering positive intentions, welcoming new beginnings, and striving for prosperity. In a real-life context, it serves as a reminder to approach each day with integrity, optimism, and a focus on personal and communal well-being. By embodying these principles, individuals can create a harmonious and prosperous life, contributing positively to society as a whole.
