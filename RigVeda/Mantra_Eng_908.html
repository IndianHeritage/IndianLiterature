युनज्मि ते ब्रह्मणा केशिना हरि उप प्र याहि दधिषे गभस्त्योः |उत त्वा सुतासो रभसां अमन्दिषुः पूषण्वान वज्रिन सम उपत्न्यामदः ||
yunajmi te brahmaṇā keśinā harī upa pra yāhi dadhiṣe gabhastyoḥ | ut tvā sutāso rabhasā amandiṣuḥ pūṣaṇvān vajrin sam u patnyāmadaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yunajmi te brahmaṇā keśinā harī upa pra yāhi dadhiṣe gabhastyoḥ | ut tvā sutāso rabhasā amandiṣuḥ pūṣaṇvān vajrin sam u patnyāmadaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yunajmi: I unite or connect
    • te: with you
    • brahmaṇā: through the divine or the ultimate reality
    • keśinā: with the one who has long hair (often referring to a deity)
    • harī: the divine or the one who removes obstacles
    • upa: near or towards
    • pra: forth or forward
    • yāhi: come or approach
    • dadhiṣe: you who are nourishing
    • gabhastyoḥ: of the light or brilliance
    • ut: and
    • tvā: you
    • sutāso: of the sons or offspring
    • rabhasā: with great force or energy
    • amandiṣuḥ: in the joyful or blissful
    • pūṣaṇvān: the nourisher or sustainer
    • vajrin: the one who wields the thunderbolt
    • sam: together or in harmony
    • u: and
    • patnyāmadaḥ: in the presence of the wife or partner
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine connection and the act of uniting with the ultimate reality.
    • The invocation of "harī" suggests a call to a deity who is known for removing obstacles and providing guidance.
    • The phrase "come forth" indicates a desire for divine presence and support in one's life.
    • The reference to "nourishing" and "light" symbolizes the importance of sustenance, both physical and spiritual.
    • The mention of "sons" and "joyful" suggests a focus on family, community, and the happiness that comes from unity.
    • The term "vajrin" refers to strength and power, indicating that the divine support sought is robust and protective.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek spiritual guidance, much like the invocation in this mantra, to find strength and clarity.
    • Families may come together in prayer or meditation, invoking divine support for harmony and nourishment in their relationships.
    • In community gatherings, the collective energy and joy can be seen as a manifestation of the "rabhasā amandiṣuḥ," where people unite for a common purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine connection, nourishment, and the strength found in unity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal challenges, family dynamics, and community support.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can seek guidance, strength, and a sense of belonging, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and with each other.
