अध प्लायोगिर अति दासद अन्यान आसङ्गो अग्ने दशभिः सहस्रैः |अधोक्षणो दश मह्यं रुशन्तो नलाऽ इव सरसो निर अतीष्टन् ||
adha plāyogir ati dāsad anyān āsaṅgo agne daśabhiḥ sahasraiḥ | adhokṣaṇo daśa mahyaṁ ruśanto naḻā iva saraso nir atiṣṭhan ||
  • Adha plāyogir ati dāsad anyān āsaṅgo agne daśabhiḥ sahasraiḥ
    • Adha: This word translates to "down" or "below," indicating a grounding or foundational aspect.
    • Plāyogir: This can be interpreted as "the one who is to be worshipped" or "the one who is invoked."
    • Ati: Meaning "beyond" or "exceeding," suggesting a sense of transcendence.
    • Dāsad: Refers to "servants" or "devotees," indicating a relationship of devotion.
    • Anyān: Meaning "others," which implies a distinction between the divine and the mundane.
    • Āsaṅgo: This translates to "connection" or "bond," emphasizing the relationship between the devotee and the divine.
    • Agne: Refers to "Agni," the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • Daśabhiḥ sahasraiḥ: This phrase means "with ten thousand," indicating abundance and the vastness of divine blessings.
  • Adhokṣaṇo daśa mahyaṁ ruśanto naḻā iva saraso nir atiṣṭhan
    • Adhokṣaṇo: This term means "the one who is beyond the reach of the senses," indicating the ineffable nature of the divine.
    • Daśa: Meaning "ten," which can symbolize completeness or wholeness.
    • Mahyaṁ: Translates to "to me," indicating a personal connection or appeal to the divine.
    • Ruśanto: This can be interpreted as "those who are angry" or "those who are fierce," suggesting the dual nature of divine energy.
    • Naḻā iva: Meaning "like the streams," which evokes imagery of flowing energy and life.
    • Saraso: Refers to "lakes" or "water bodies," symbolizing abundance and nourishment.
    • Nir atiṣṭhan: This means "not standing," indicating movement, dynamism, and the ever-changing nature of existence.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the relationship between the devotee and the divine, particularly through the figure of Agni, the fire god. It highlights themes of devotion, abundance, and the transcendent nature of the divine. In real life, this can be applied in various ways:

  • In personal rituals, invoking Agni can symbolize the transformation of one's inner self, encouraging individuals to seek purification and clarity in their lives.
  • The concept of abundance can inspire gratitude and recognition of the blessings one has, fostering a sense of community and connection with others.
  • The acknowledgment of the divine as beyond sensory perception encourages individuals to seek deeper understanding and connection through meditation and spiritual practices.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of devotion, the pursuit of spiritual growth, and the recognition of the divine presence in everyday life.
