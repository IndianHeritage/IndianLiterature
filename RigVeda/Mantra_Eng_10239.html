ये नः सपत्ना अप ते भवन्तु इन्द्राग्निभ्यां अव बाधामहे तान् | वसवो रुद्राः आदित्याः उपरिष्पृशं मोग्रं चेत्तारं अधिराजम् अकृन् ||
ye naḥ sapatnā apa te bhavantv indrāgnibhyām ava bādhāmahe tān | vasavo rudrā ādityā uparispṛśam mograṁ cettāram adhirājam akran ||
  • Translation: "May those who are our rivals be removed from us, O Indra and Agni; we do not wish to be hindered by them. O Vasus, Rudras, and Adityas, if you are pleased, grant us the strength to overcome our enemies."
  • Contextual Meaning: This mantra is a plea for protection and strength against adversaries. It invokes the deities Indra and Agni, who are often associated with power and fire, respectively. The speaker seeks to eliminate obstacles posed by rivals and to gain favor from the divine forces represented by the Vasus, Rudras, and Adityas.
  • Indra: In Vedic literature, Indra is the king of the gods and a warrior deity. He symbolizes strength and victory. For example, in a competitive environment like a business, one might invoke the spirit of Indra to overcome challenges posed by competitors.
  • Agni: Agni represents fire and is a symbol of transformation and purification. In real life, one might relate this to the process of overcoming personal challenges, akin to how fire transforms materials. For instance, someone facing a difficult situation may seek to channel their inner Agni to transform their circumstances.
  • Vasus, Rudras, and Adityas: These are groups of deities representing various aspects of nature and cosmic order. They symbolize abundance, strength, and the sun's life-giving energy. In daily life, one might call upon these energies when seeking harmony and balance in their personal or professional life.
  • Application in Real Life: This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:
    • In competitive sports, athletes may recite this mantra to gain mental strength and focus against their opponents.
    • In personal conflicts, individuals may use the essence of this mantra to seek resolution and protection from negativity.
    • In business, entrepreneurs might invoke the spirit of this mantra to overcome market competition and achieve success.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in overcoming obstacles and rivals. It emphasizes the importance of inner strength and the support of higher powers in achieving one's goals. By invoking these energies, individuals can find the courage and resilience needed to face challenges in various aspects of life.
