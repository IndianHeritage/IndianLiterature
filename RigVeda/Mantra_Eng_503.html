आ तत्ते दास्र मन्तुमः पूषन्न अवो वृणीमहे |येन पितृन् अचोदयः ||
ā tat te dasra mantumaḥ pūṣann avo vṛṇīmahe | yena pitṝn acodayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: From
    • tat: That
    • te: Your
    • dasra: Ten
    • mantumaḥ: Thoughts or intentions
    • pūṣann: O nourisher or sustainer
    • avo: May we attain
    • vṛṇīmahe: We choose or we invoke
    • yena: By which
    • pitṝn: Ancestors or forefathers
    • acodayaḥ: May we be inspired or guided
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer for guidance and nourishment from a higher power.
    • It emphasizes the importance of intentions and thoughts in achieving one's goals.
    • It also acknowledges the role of ancestors in providing wisdom and inspiration.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when making important decisions, one might reflect on the values and teachings of their predecessors, drawing inspiration from their experiences.
    • During personal challenges, individuals may seek guidance through meditation or prayer, invoking the support of their ancestors to find clarity and strength.
    • In community gatherings, people often share stories of their forefathers, reinforcing the idea that their wisdom can guide current and future generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking nourishment for the mind and spirit through positive thoughts and intentions.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness between generations, encouraging individuals to honor their roots while striving for personal growth.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as decision-making, personal development, and community bonding, fostering a sense of purpose and direction.
