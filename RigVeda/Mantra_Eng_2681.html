इळायास त्वा पदे वयं नाभा पृथिव्या अधि |जातवेदो न धिमहि अग्ने हव्या य वोल्हवे ||
iḻāyās tvā pade vayaṁ nābhā pṛthivyā adhi | jātavedo ni dhīmahy agne havyāya voḻhave ||
  • iḻāyās tvā pade vayaṁ nābhā pṛthivyā adhi
    • iḻāyās: This term can be interpreted as "to the one who is invoked" or "to the one who is worshipped." It signifies the act of calling upon a divine presence.
    • tvā: This means "you," referring to the deity being addressed, in this case, Agni, the fire god.
    • pade: This translates to "in your presence" or "in your abode," indicating a sacred space or a divine realm.
    • vayaṁ: This means "we" or "us," representing the collective group of worshippers or seekers.
    • nābhā: This word means "navel" or "center," symbolizing the core or essence of existence.
    • pṛthivyā: This translates to "of the earth," indicating the physical realm and its connection to the divine.
    • adhi: This means "above" or "upon," suggesting a higher state of being or consciousness.
  • jātavedo ni dhīmahy agne havyāya voḻhave
    • jātavedo: This term refers to Agni as the "born-knower" or "the one who knows all births," emphasizing his role as a witness to all actions and events.
    • ni dhīmahy: This means "we meditate upon" or "we contemplate," indicating a deep focus and reverence towards the deity.
    • agne: This is a direct address to Agni, the fire god, who is central to Vedic rituals and represents transformation and purification.
    • havyāya: This translates to "for the offerings," referring to the rituals and sacrifices made to the deity.
    • voḻhave: This means "to you," indicating the direction of the offerings and prayers towards Agni.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the connection between the worshippers and the divine, particularly through the element of fire, represented by Agni. It highlights the importance of invoking the divine presence in our lives, recognizing the earth as a sacred space, and meditating on the knowledge and wisdom that Agni embodies. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in rituals, meditation practices, and moments of reflection where individuals seek guidance, transformation, and a deeper understanding of their existence. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the physical and spiritual realms, encouraging individuals to honor both in their daily lives.
