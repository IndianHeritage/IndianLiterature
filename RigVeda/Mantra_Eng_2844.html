मातयः सोमपाम उरुं रिहन्ति शवसस पतिम् |इन्द्रं वत्सं न मातरः ||
matayaḥ somapām uruṁ rihanti śavasas patim | indraṁ vatsaṁ na mātaraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • matayaḥ: of the wise or intelligent
    • somapām: of those who drink soma (a sacred drink)
    • uruṁ: great or vast
    • rihanti: they bring forth or produce
    • śavasas: of the dead or the deceased
    • patim: lord or master
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods
    • vatsaṁ: calf or offspring
    • na: not
    • mātaraḥ: mothers
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the wise individuals who, through their understanding and knowledge, are able to produce great outcomes.
    • The reference to "somapām" indicates a connection to spiritual or divine experiences, suggesting that those who engage in spiritual practices can achieve significant results.
    • The phrase "śavasas patim" refers to the lord of the deceased, which can symbolize the respect and reverence for those who have passed away, acknowledging their wisdom and contributions.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents strength, power, and leadership, while "vatsaṁ" signifies the nurturing aspect, akin to a mother caring for her child.
    • The line "na mātaraḥ" implies that the wise do not rely solely on maternal figures for guidance, but rather seek knowledge and strength from within and from divine sources.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who embody wisdom and knowledge can inspire their teams to achieve great results, much like the wise individuals mentioned in the mantra.
    • Spiritual practices, such as meditation or yoga, can lead to profound personal growth and understanding, reflecting the idea of "somapām" and the benefits of engaging with the divine.
    • In honoring the deceased, communities often hold memorials or celebrations of life, recognizing the wisdom and impact of those who have passed, akin to the respect shown in the mantra.
    • Mentorship in professional environments can be seen as a modern interpretation of seeking guidance from wise figures, rather than solely relying on traditional sources of authority.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, spiritual engagement, and the respect for both the living and the deceased.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and strength from within, rather than solely depending on external figures, promoting self-reliance and personal growth.
    • In real life, the principles derived from this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, personal development, and community respect for heritage and wisdom.
