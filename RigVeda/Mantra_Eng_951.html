अस्य श्रोषन्त्वा भुवो विश्वा यश चर्षणीर अभि |सूरं चित सस्रुषीर इषः ||
asya śroṣantv ā bhuvo viśvā yaś carṣaṇīr abhi | sūraṁ cit sasruṣīr iṣaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asya: of this
    • śroṣantv: may they hear
    • ā: from
    • bhuvo: the worlds
    • viśvā: all
    • yaś: who
    • carṣaṇīr: the fields
    • abhi: towards
    • sūraṁ: the brave
    • cit: conscious
    • sasruṣīr: may they flow
    • iṣaḥ: the power
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the idea of listening and understanding the wisdom that comes from all realms of existence.
    • It emphasizes the importance of being receptive to knowledge and experiences from various sources, including nature and the universe.
    • The term "sūraṁ" refers to the brave or the strong, suggesting that true strength lies in the ability to absorb and act upon wisdom.
    • The phrase "may they flow" indicates a dynamic process of knowledge and power, encouraging a continuous exchange of ideas and insights.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who actively listen to their team members and consider diverse perspectives often make better decisions.
    • In education, teachers who encourage students to share their thoughts and experiences create a richer learning environment.
    • In personal relationships, being open to understanding each other's viewpoints fosters stronger connections and resolves conflicts.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of listening and learning from all aspects of life.
    • It teaches us that wisdom is not confined to a single source; rather, it is a collective experience that can empower us.
    • In today's fast-paced world, the ability to be receptive and adaptable is crucial for personal growth and societal progress.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can cultivate a more harmonious and enlightened existence.
