जज्ञानाḥ सोमं सहसे पपाथ प्र ते माता महिमानम् उवाच |इन्द्र पप्राथोर्व आन्तरिक्षं युधा देवैभ्यो वरिवश चकर्थ ||
jajñānaḥ somaṁ sahase papātha pra te mātā mahimānam uvāca | endra paprāthorv a1ntarikṣaṁ yudhā devebhyo varivaś cakartha ||
  • jajñānaḥ: This term refers to the act of birth or creation. In a broader sense, it signifies the emergence of knowledge or wisdom.
  • somaṁ: Soma is often associated with a sacred drink in Vedic texts, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. It represents the essence of life and consciousness.
  • sahase: This word means 'with strength' or 'powerfully'. It indicates the force or vigor behind an action.
  • papātha: This term can be translated as 'flowed' or 'poured forth', suggesting a release or dissemination of energy or knowledge.
  • pra te: This phrase means 'for you', indicating a direction or purpose towards a specific entity or deity.
  • mātā: Meaning 'mother', it signifies nurturing, care, and the source of life.
  • mahimānam: This term refers to 'greatness' or 'glory', often associated with divine attributes or the magnificence of the universe.
  • uvāca: This means 'spoke' or 'declared', indicating communication or the imparting of wisdom.
  • indra: Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and strength. He represents power and authority.
  • paprāthorv: This term can be understood as 'having poured forth', suggesting an action that has been completed or a state that has been achieved.
  • antarikṣaṁ: This word refers to the 'intermediate space' or 'atmosphere', symbolizing the realm between the earth and the heavens.
  • yudhā: Meaning 'in battle' or 'in conflict', it signifies struggle or competition, often in the context of achieving a goal.
  • devebhyo: This term means 'to the gods', indicating a reverence or offering made to divine beings.
  • varivaś: This word translates to 'with great power' or 'with authority', suggesting a strong influence or capability.
  • cakartha: This means 'did' or 'performed', indicating the completion of an action or task.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of creation, divine inspiration, and the interplay of strength and wisdom. It highlights the importance of nurturing knowledge and the role of divine forces in guiding human endeavors. In real life, this can be applied to various scenarios such as education, leadership, and personal growth, where the pursuit of knowledge and the acknowledgment of higher powers can lead to success and fulfillment. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek wisdom and strength in our actions, fostering a connection with the divine and the greater universe.
