कविम मृजन्ति मर्ज्यं धीभिर विप्राः अवस्यवः |वृषा कणिक्रद अर्षति ||
kavim mṛjanti marjyaṁ dhībhir viprā avasyavaḥ | vṛṣā kanikrad arṣati ||
  • kavim: This word translates to "the wise" or "the poet." In a broader sense, it refers to those who possess deep knowledge and insight.
  • mṛjanti: This means "to cleanse" or "to purify." It signifies the act of removing impurities or obstacles.
  • marjyaṁ: This term can be understood as "the one who is to be purified" or "the object of purification." It indicates something that requires cleansing or refinement.
  • dhībhir: This word translates to "by the intellects" or "through wisdom." It emphasizes the role of intelligence and understanding in the process of purification.
  • viprā: This refers to "the learned" or "the wise ones." It highlights the importance of knowledgeable individuals in guiding others.
  • avasyavaḥ: This means "they are destined" or "they are bound to." It suggests a sense of inevitability or purpose in the actions being described.
  • vṛṣā: This word translates to "the strong" or "the powerful." It can refer to strength in various forms, including physical, mental, or spiritual.
  • kanikrad: This term means "to roar" or "to make a loud sound." It can symbolize the expression of strength or authority.
  • arṣati: This means "to rise" or "to ascend." It indicates growth, elevation, or the attainment of a higher state.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for the wise and learned individuals to purify and elevate those who are in need of refinement. It emphasizes the importance of intellect and wisdom in the process of cleansing and growth. The strong and powerful are encouraged to express their strength and rise to a higher state of being.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Education: The role of teachers and mentors in guiding students towards knowledge and personal growth is crucial. Just as the wise purify the minds of the unlearned, educators help students cleanse their ignorance and rise to new levels of understanding.
  • Personal Development: Individuals seeking self-improvement can reflect on this mantra. By engaging in self-reflection and seeking wisdom, one can cleanse negative thoughts and behaviors, leading to personal growth and a stronger character.
  • Community Leadership: Leaders in society have the responsibility to guide their communities towards betterment. By embodying wisdom and strength, they can help others rise above challenges and create a more harmonious environment.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the transformative power of wisdom and strength in the process of purification and growth. It serves as a reminder of the importance of knowledgeable individuals in guiding others and the potential for personal and communal elevation through the pursuit of understanding and strength.
