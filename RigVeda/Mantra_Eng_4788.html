यो गृणतामिदासिथापिरूतिः शिवः सखा। स त्वं न इन्द्र मृळय।
yo gṛṇatām id āsithāpir ūtī śivaḥ sakhā | sa tvaṁ na indra mṛḻaya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • gṛṇatām: of those who sing
    • id: this
    • āsithā: you have sat
    • pir: together
    • ūtī: the auspiciousness
    • śivaḥ: auspicious, good
    • sakhā: friend
    • sa: he
    • tvaṁ: you
    • na: not
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • mṛḻaya: may you be pleased
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses the divine, invoking the presence of Indra, the king of gods, and emphasizes the importance of friendship and auspiciousness.
    • The phrase "yo gṛṇatām" suggests that those who sing or chant together create a bond that is sacred and powerful.
    • The term "śivaḥ sakhā" highlights the idea of friendship that is rooted in goodness and auspiciousness, indicating that true friends bring positivity into each other's lives.
    • By asking Indra to be pleased, the mantra reflects a desire for divine blessings and support in fostering these positive relationships.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, when people come together to celebrate festivals or events, they often sing and dance, reinforcing their bonds and creating a sense of unity.
    • In workplaces, team-building activities that encourage collaboration and camaraderie can lead to a more harmonious and productive environment, echoing the essence of "śivaḥ sakhā."
    • Friendship circles that support each other during tough times exemplify the idea of auspicious relationships, where individuals uplift one another.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of friendship and community in our lives.
    • It encourages us to foster relationships that are rooted in positivity and mutual support, invoking divine blessings for our connections.
    • In a world where individualism often prevails, this mantra calls us to recognize the power of togetherness and the importance of nurturing our bonds with others.
