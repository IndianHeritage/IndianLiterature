विभ्राजञ् ज्योतिषा स्वर्गगच्छो रोचनं दिवः |देवास त इन्द्र सख्याय येमिरे ||
vibhrājañ jyotiṣā sva1r agaccho rocanaṁ divaḥ | devās ta indra sakhyāya yemire ||
  • vibhrājañ: This word translates to "shining" or "illuminating." It signifies the brilliance and radiance of light.
  • jyotiṣā: Meaning "by the light," this term emphasizes the importance of light in understanding and enlightenment.
  • sva1r: This can be interpreted as "heaven" or "the celestial realm," indicating a divine or higher state of existence.
  • agaccho: This means "has gone" or "has reached," suggesting a journey or transition to a higher state.
  • rocanaṁ: Translated as "brightness" or "splendor," it refers to the beauty and allure of light.
  • divaḥ: This word means "of the sky" or "heavenly," reinforcing the connection to the divine and celestial.
  • devās: Meaning "gods," this term refers to divine beings or deities that embody various aspects of nature and existence.
  • ta: This is a pronoun meaning "those" or "they," referring to the gods mentioned earlier.
  • indra: Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and the king of gods.
  • sakhyāya: This translates to "for friendship" or "for companionship," indicating a bond or relationship.
  • yemire: This means "they have come" or "they have arrived," suggesting the presence or arrival of the gods.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine light and the gods, emphasizing the importance of illumination and enlightenment in our lives. It suggests that through the radiance of the celestial realm, we can achieve a higher understanding and connection with the divine.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Spiritual Growth: Just as light dispels darkness, seeking knowledge and wisdom can lead to personal growth and enlightenment.
  • Community and Friendship: The mention of Indra and companionship highlights the importance of relationships and support systems in our lives.
  • Celebration of Nature: The connection to the divine and natural elements reminds us to appreciate the beauty and splendor of the world around us.

Conclusion: This mantra signifies the pursuit of light, knowledge, and divine connection. It encourages individuals to seek enlightenment, foster relationships, and appreciate the beauty of existence. In a world often filled with challenges and darkness, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of light and companionship in our journey through life.
