एतानि धीरो निन्या चिकेत पृष्णिर यद ऊधो मही जब्हार ||
etāni dhīro niṇyā ciketa pṛśnir yad ūdho mahī jabhāra ||
  • etāni: This word translates to "these" or "these things." It indicates a focus on specific elements or concepts that are being discussed.
  • dhīro: This term means "wise" or "intelligent." It refers to individuals who possess wisdom and understanding, often implying a calm and composed demeanor.
  • niṇyā: This can be interpreted as "to know" or "to understand." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and awareness in one's life.
  • ciketa: This word means "to perceive" or "to realize." It suggests an active engagement with knowledge, leading to deeper insights.
  • pṛśnir: This term can be understood as "the one who is inquiring" or "the seeker." It highlights the quest for knowledge and truth.
  • yad: This translates to "that" or "which," serving as a connector to the following ideas.
  • ūdho: This word means "great" or "mighty." It often refers to something of significant importance or power.
  • mahī: This term translates to "earth" or "the great one," symbolizing stability, grounding, and the physical world.
  • jabhāra: This means "to bear" or "to carry." It implies the act of supporting or sustaining something significant.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to wise individuals to seek knowledge and understanding of the great and mighty aspects of existence, particularly those that the earth bears. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in perceiving the deeper truths of life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Education: It encourages students and educators to pursue knowledge with wisdom and understanding, recognizing the importance of what they learn and how it impacts the world.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to the earth signifies the need for individuals to understand and respect the environment, acknowledging the weight of our actions on the planet.
  • Personal Growth: It serves as a reminder for individuals to seek wisdom in their personal journeys, understanding that true knowledge comes from inquiry and reflection.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for wisdom and understanding in the pursuit of knowledge. It highlights the importance of being aware of the great responsibilities we carry as individuals in relation to the earth and our surroundings. By embodying the qualities of the wise and the seeker, we can contribute positively to our communities and the world at large.
