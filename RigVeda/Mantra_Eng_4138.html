मील्हुष्मतीव पृथिवी पराहता मदन्त्य एत्य अस्मद आ |ऋक्षो न वो मरुतः शिमिवाम् अमो दुध्रो गौर इव भीमयुः ||
mīḻhuṣmatīva pṛthivī parāhatā madanty ety asmad ā | ṛkṣo na vo marutaḥ śimīvām̐ amo dudhro gaur iva bhīmayuḥ ||
  • mīḻhuṣmatīva: This term can be interpreted as "like the one who is wise" or "possessing wisdom." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in our lives.
  • pṛthivī: Refers to the Earth or the physical world. It symbolizes our environment and the material aspects of life.
  • parāhatā: This word means "having been made strong" or "empowered." It suggests resilience and strength in facing challenges.
  • madanty: This can be understood as "those who are intoxicated" or "filled with joy." It reflects a state of happiness and fulfillment.
  • ety: Translates to "coming" or "arriving." It indicates the arrival of something significant or transformative.
  • asmad: This means "our" or "us," indicating a collective identity or community.
  • ṛkṣo: Refers to "bears" or "strong beings." It symbolizes strength and power in nature.
  • na vo: This phrase means "not for you," suggesting a distinction or separation from something.
  • marutaḥ: Refers to the "winds" or "storm gods," representing the forces of nature that can be both nurturing and destructive.
  • śimīvām̐: This term can be interpreted as "like a protector" or "guardian." It emphasizes the role of protection in our lives.
  • amo: Means "not" or "without," indicating a negation or absence of something.
  • dudhro: This can be understood as "like a bull" or "strong." It symbolizes strength and vigor.
  • gaur: Refers to "the cow" or "nourishment." It represents sustenance and the nurturing aspects of life.
  • iva: Means "like" or "as," indicating a comparison or similarity.
  • bhīmayuḥ: This term can be interpreted as "fearful" or "awe-inspiring." It reflects the dual nature of existence, where fear and reverence coexist.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of wisdom, strength, and the interconnectedness of life. It highlights the importance of resilience in the face of challenges and the joy that comes from collective identity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as community building, environmental conservation, and personal growth. By embracing wisdom and strength, we can navigate the complexities of life while fostering a sense of unity and purpose. The mantra serves as a reminder of the balance between nurturing and the awe-inspiring forces of nature, encouraging us to respect and protect our environment while celebrating the joy of existence.
