याभिः सूर्यं पर्याथः परावती मन्धातारं क्षैत्रपतयेऽवतम् |याभिर विप्रम प्र भरद्वाजम् आवतम् ताभिर ऊषु ऊतिभिर अश्विनां गतम् ||
yābhiḥ sūryam pariyāthaḥ parāvati mandhātāraṁ kṣaitrapatyeṣv āvatam | yābhir vipram pra bharadvājam āvataṁ tābhir ū ṣu ūtibhir aśvinā gatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yābhiḥ: By whom
    • sūryam: The sun
    • pariyāthaḥ: Surrounding
    • parāvati: The highest or supreme
    • mandhātāraṁ: The one who is like Mandhātā (a legendary king)
    • kṣaitrapatyeṣv: In the realms of the earth
    • āvatam: Protect
    • yābhir: By whom
    • vipram: The learned or wise
    • pra: For
    • bharadvājam: Bharadvaja (a sage or a lineage)
    • āvataṁ: Protect
    • tābhir: By those
    • ū: O
    • ṣu: Good
    • ūtibhir: With blessings
    • aśvinā: By the Ashvins (divine twin horsemen)
    • gatam: Gone or departed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the protective energies of the sun and the divine beings, particularly the Ashvins, who are known for their healing and benevolent qualities.
    • The reference to "Mandhātā" symbolizes a ruler or leader who embodies wisdom and strength, suggesting that the mantra seeks to establish a protective and nurturing environment for those who are wise and learned.
    • The repetition of "yābhiḥ" emphasizes the importance of collective support and protection, highlighting that wisdom and strength come from community and collaboration.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders can draw inspiration from this mantra by fostering a culture of collaboration and support among team members, ensuring that everyone feels protected and valued.
    • In personal life, individuals can apply the teachings of this mantra by surrounding themselves with wise and supportive friends and family, creating a nurturing environment that promotes growth and well-being.
    • In community service, this mantra can inspire initiatives that protect and uplift the vulnerable, ensuring that wisdom and resources are shared for the greater good.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for protection and support from divine forces, emphasizing the importance of wisdom, community, and collaboration.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, reminding us that we thrive best when we support one another and seek guidance from higher wisdom.
    • Ultimately, this mantra encourages us to create a harmonious and protective space for ourselves and others, fostering growth, healing, and collective well-being.
