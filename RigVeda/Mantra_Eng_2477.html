पितā यज्ञानाम् असुरो विपश्चितां विमानम् अग्निर वयुनं च वाघताम् |आ विवेश रोदसी भूर्वर्पसā पुरुप्रियो भण्डते धामभिः कविः ||
pitā yajñānām asuro vipaścitāṁ vimānam agnir vayunaṁ ca vāghatām | ā viveśa rodasī bhūrivarpasā purupriyo bhandate dhāmabhiḥ kaviḥ ||
  • pitā yajñānām: This phrase translates to "the father of sacrifices." In Vedic culture, sacrifices (yajñas) were central to spiritual and communal life, symbolizing the connection between humans and the divine. For example, in modern life, this can be seen in community gatherings where people come together to share food and celebrate, fostering a sense of unity and gratitude.
  • asuro vipaścitāṁ: This means "the wise among the powerful." It suggests that true wisdom comes from understanding and using power responsibly. In real life, this can be exemplified by leaders who use their influence to promote social justice and equality, such as activists who fight for the rights of marginalized communities.
  • vimānam agnir: This translates to "the fire that is the vehicle." Fire in Vedic texts often symbolizes transformation and energy. In contemporary terms, this can relate to the use of technology (the 'fire' of our age) as a vehicle for change, such as how renewable energy sources are transforming our approach to sustainability.
  • vayunaṁ ca vāghatām: This means "and the one who brings forth speech." It highlights the importance of communication and expression. In today's world, effective communication is crucial in various fields, from business negotiations to personal relationships, emphasizing the need for clarity and understanding.
  • ā viveśa rodasī: This translates to "he entered the two worlds." This can be interpreted as the connection between the physical and spiritual realms. In real life, this can be seen in practices like meditation or yoga, which aim to bridge the gap between our material existence and spiritual awareness.
  • bhūrivarpasā: This means "with abundant glory." It signifies the richness of life and the blessings that come from living in harmony with the universe. In practical terms, this can be reflected in the joy and fulfillment that comes from acts of kindness and generosity towards others.
  • purupriyo bhandate dhāmabhiḥ: This translates to "the beloved of many, who binds with his abode." It suggests the idea of community and belonging. In modern society, this can be seen in the importance of building strong relationships and networks, whether in personal life or professional settings.
  • kaviḥ: This means "the poet" or "the seer." It represents creativity and insight. In today's context, this can relate to artists, writers, and thinkers who inspire change and provoke thought through their work, highlighting the power of creativity in shaping society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, power, communication, and community. Its significance lies in the reminder that true strength comes from understanding and using our resources wisely, fostering connections, and embracing creativity. In real life, these principles can guide individuals and communities towards a more harmonious and fulfilling existence, encouraging us to act with intention and purpose in our daily lives.
