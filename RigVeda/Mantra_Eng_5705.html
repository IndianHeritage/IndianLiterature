नू मे हवम् आ श्रिणुतं युवाना यासिष्टं वर्तिर अश्विनाव इरावत् | धत्तं रत्नानि जरतं च सूरīn यūयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
nū me havam ā śṛṇutaṁ yuvānā yāsiṣṭaṁ vartir aśvināv irāvat | dhattaṁ ratnāni jarataṁ ca sūrīn yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Transliteration: "nū me havam ā śṛṇutaṁ yuvānā yāsiṣṭaṁ vartir aśvināv irāvat | dhattaṁ ratnāni jarataṁ ca sūrīn yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||"
  • Meaning:
    • nū me havam: "Now my invocation" - This indicates a call to the divine, expressing a desire for attention and assistance.
    • ā śṛṇutaṁ: "Listen to me" - A plea for the divine to hear the supplicant's words and intentions.
    • yuvānā: "young" - Referring to the youthful and vibrant energy of the deities, particularly the Ashvins.
    • yāsiṣṭaṁ: "most excellent" - Acknowledging the supreme qualities of the Ashvins, who are known for their benevolence and healing powers.
    • vartir: "presence" - Invoking the presence of the Ashvins in the life of the devotee.
    • aśvināv: "O Ashvins" - Directly addressing the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and well-being.
    • irāvat: "the one who brings prosperity" - A reference to the divine qualities that bring abundance and success.
    • dhattaṁ: "bestow" - A request for the deities to grant blessings.
    • ratnāni: "jewels" - Symbolizing valuable gifts, both material and spiritual.
    • jarataṁ: "aging" - Acknowledging the inevitability of aging and the need for divine support in facing it.
    • ca sūrīn: "and heroes" - Invoking the strength and valor of divine beings.
    • yūyam: "you" - Referring to the Ashvins, emphasizing their role in the invocation.
    • pāta: "protect" - A plea for safeguarding and guidance.
    • svastibhiḥ: "with auspiciousness" - Seeking blessings that lead to well-being and prosperity.
    • sadā: "always" - A desire for continuous support and protection.
    • naḥ: "for us" - A collective invocation for the benefit of the community or family.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be recited during significant life events such as weddings or childbirth, where blessings for health and prosperity are sought.
    • Healthcare practitioners may invoke the Ashvins for guidance and healing in their practice, reflecting the deities' association with medicine.
    • During community gatherings or festivals, this mantra can be chanted to foster unity and collective well-being, emphasizing the importance of divine support in communal life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine intervention in human life, particularly in matters of health, prosperity, and community well-being.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness between the divine and the material world, encouraging individuals to seek blessings and support from higher powers.
    • In essence, it teaches the importance of humility and the recognition of forces greater than oneself, fostering a sense of gratitude and reverence in daily life.
