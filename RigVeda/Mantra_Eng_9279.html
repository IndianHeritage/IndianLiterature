प्र जिह्वया भरते वेपो अग्निः प्र वयुनानि चेतसाऽ पृथिव्याः |तम आयवः शुचयन्तं पावकम् मन्द्रं होतारं दधिरे यजिष्टम् ||
pra jihvayā bharate vepo agniḥ pra vayunāni cetasā pṛthivyāḥ | tam āyavaḥ śucayantam pāvakam mandraṁ hotāraṁ dadhire yajiṣṭham ||
  • pra jihvayā bharate vepo agniḥ: This phrase can be translated as "May the fire, which is invoked with the tongue, be powerful." Here, "jihvayā" refers to the tongue, and "bharate" means to carry or to bring forth. The invocation of fire symbolizes the importance of communication and expression in our lives.
  • pra vayunāni cetasā pṛthivyāḥ: This translates to "May the thoughts of the earth be brought forth with intention." "Vayunāni" refers to intentions or thoughts, and "cetasā" means with the mind. This emphasizes the need for mindful thinking and intention in our actions, particularly in relation to the earth and nature.
  • tam āyavaḥ śucayantam pāvakam: This means "That pure and sacred fire, which is invoked." "Āyavaḥ" refers to the essence or the core, while "śucayantam" means pure or clean. This highlights the significance of purity in our thoughts and actions, suggesting that we should strive for clarity and sanctity in our endeavors.
  • mandraṁ hotāraṁ dadhire yajiṣṭham: This translates to "They who are the best of the priests, who perform the rituals." "Mandram" refers to the sacred chant or hymn, "hotāraṁ" means the one who invokes, and "yajiṣṭham" refers to the most worthy of worship. This signifies the importance of rituals and the role of those who guide us in spiritual practices.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of communication, intention, purity, and ritual in our lives. It serves as a reminder that our words and thoughts have power, and that we should approach our actions with mindfulness and clarity.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts. For instance, in personal relationships, clear and honest communication (pra jihvayā) is essential for understanding and connection. In environmental practices, being mindful of our intentions (pra vayunāni) can lead to more sustainable choices that honor the earth (pṛthivyāḥ). Additionally, engaging in rituals or practices that promote purity and clarity (tam āyavaḥ) can enhance our spiritual well-being.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a holistic guide for living a life that is intentional, pure, and connected to the greater universe. By embodying these principles, we can foster deeper connections with ourselves, others, and the world around us.
