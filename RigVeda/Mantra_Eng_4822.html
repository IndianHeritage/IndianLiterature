अयं स यो वरिमाणं पृथिव्या वर्ष्माणं दिवो अकृणोद अयं सः |अयम् पीयूषं तिसृषु प्रवत्सु सोमो दाधारोर्व अन्तरिक्षम् ||
ayaṁ sa yo varimāṇam pṛthivyā varṣmāṇaṁ divo akṛṇod ayaṁ saḥ | ayam pīyūṣaṁ tisṛṣu pravatsu somo dādhārorv a1ntarikṣam ||
  • ayaṁ sa yo varimāṇam: This phrase can be translated as "This is the one who has created the vastness." It refers to a divine force or entity that has the power to create and sustain the universe.
  • pṛthivyā varṣmāṇaṁ: This means "the one who has made the earth prosperous." It signifies the nurturing aspect of nature that provides sustenance and abundance to all living beings.
  • divo akṛṇod: Translated as "who has established the heavens," this part emphasizes the connection between the earth and the sky, suggesting a balance and harmony in creation.
  • ayam saḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "this is he," referring to the divine presence that is omnipresent and influential in all aspects of life.
  • ayam pīyūṣaṁ: Meaning "this is the nectar," it symbolizes the essence of life and the vital energy that sustains all beings.
  • tisṛṣu pravatsu: This translates to "in the three realms," indicating the interconnectedness of the physical, mental, and spiritual dimensions of existence.
  • somo dādhārorv: This means "the moon that nourishes the earth," representing the cyclical nature of life and the importance of celestial bodies in agriculture and human life.
  • antarikṣam: This refers to "the space between," symbolizing the vastness of the universe and the potential for growth and exploration.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, abundance, and the divine connection between the earth and the cosmos. It highlights the importance of nurturing and sustaining life through the balance of natural forces.
  • Real-life Application: In our daily lives, this mantra can inspire us to appreciate the resources provided by nature and to live in harmony with our environment. For example, sustainable farming practices reflect the nurturing aspect of the earth, while mindfulness and meditation can help us connect with the spiritual dimensions of our existence.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all life and the divine forces that sustain it. By recognizing and honoring these connections, we can cultivate a deeper appreciation for the world around us and strive for a more balanced and harmonious existence.
