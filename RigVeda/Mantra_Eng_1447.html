इन्द्रोता तुभ्यं तद् दिवे तद् रुद्राय स्वयशसे |मित्राय वोचं वरुणाय सप्रथः सुमृळीकाय सप्रथः ||
indrota tubhyaṁ tad dive tad rudrāya svayaśase | mitrāya vocaṁ varuṇāya saprathaḥ sumṛḻīkāya saprathaḥ ||
  • Indrota tubhyaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "O Indra, we call upon you." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength, power, and the force of nature. In real life, this can be seen as a call for help or guidance in times of need, much like how people seek inspiration from leaders or mentors.
  • Tad dive: This means "that which is in the heavens." It signifies the divine or celestial aspects of existence. In our daily lives, this can relate to aspirations and goals that seem lofty or beyond reach, encouraging individuals to strive for higher achievements.
  • Tad rudrāya svayaśase: This translates to "to Rudra, who is self-illuminated." Rudra is often associated with storms and the destructive aspects of nature, but also with healing and transformation. This duality can be reflected in life experiences where challenges lead to personal growth and enlightenment.
  • Mitrāya: This means "to Mitra," who represents friendship and harmony. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of building and maintaining relationships, emphasizing the value of community and support systems.
  • Vocaṁ varuṇāya: This translates to "we speak to Varuna." Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water, symbolizing the moral and ethical aspects of life. This can be related to the importance of integrity and justice in our actions and decisions.
  • Saprathaḥ sumṛḻīkāya: This phrase means "with all our strength, we seek the well-being." It emphasizes the collective effort and unity in pursuing well-being and prosperity. In real life, this can be seen in community initiatives and teamwork, where collaboration leads to greater success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound invocation to various deities, highlighting the interconnectedness of strength, harmony, moral order, and collective well-being. Its significance lies in the reminder that in our pursuit of goals, we should seek guidance from higher powers, foster relationships, uphold ethical standards, and work together for the common good. In contemporary life, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to strive for personal and collective growth, emphasizing the importance of spirituality, ethics, and collaboration in achieving a fulfilling life.
