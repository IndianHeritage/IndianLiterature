तस्मै तवस्यं अनु दायि सत्रेन्द्राय देवैभिर अर्णसातौ |प्रति यद अस्य वज्रम बाह्वोर धुर हत्वी दस्यून पुर आयसीर न तारीत ||
tasmai tavasya1m anu dāyi satrendrāya devebhir arṇasātau | prati yad asya vajram bāhvor dhur hatvī dasyūn pura āyasīr ni tārīt ||
  • Mantra Breakdown:
    • tasmai: To that (deity or force)
    • tavasya: Of your (divine) power
    • anu: Following or in accordance with
    • dāyi: Granting or bestowing
    • satrendrāya: To the lord of the sacrifice
    • devebhir: By the gods
    • arṇasātau: In the waters or in the depths
    • prati: For or towards
    • yad: Whatever
    • asya: Of this
    • vajram: Thunderbolt or weapon
    • bāhvor: Of the arms
    • dhur: Bearing or carrying
    • hatvī: Having struck down
    • dasyūn: The enemies or obstacles
    • pura: In the city or within
    • āyasīr: Strength or power
    • ni: Not
    • tārīt: To be saved or liberated
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes divine power to bestow strength and protection.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine assistance in overcoming obstacles and enemies.
    • The reference to "vajram" symbolizes the strength and power needed to face challenges.
    • It suggests that with divine support, one can strike down adversities and emerge victorious.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive work environment, individuals may face challenges from colleagues. This mantra can be a reminder to seek inner strength and divine guidance to overcome these challenges.
    • Students preparing for exams can recite this mantra to invoke focus and clarity, helping them to "strike down" distractions and perform well.
    • In personal conflicts, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek peace and resolution through divine support, rather than engaging in negativity.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine intervention and strength in the face of adversity.
    • It teaches the importance of seeking help from higher powers when confronted with challenges.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various situations where one feels overwhelmed, reminding individuals to harness their inner strength and seek guidance.
