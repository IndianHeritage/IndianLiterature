अग्निर्नो यज्ञमुप वेतु साधुयाग्निं नरो वि भरण्ते गृहगृहैः |अग्निर्दूतः अभवद् धव्यवाहनोऽग्निं वृणानां वृणते कविकृतुम् ||
agnir no yajñam upa vetu sādhuyāgniṁ naro vi bharante gṛhe-gṛhe | agnir dūto abhavad dhavyavāhano 'gniṁ vṛṇānā vṛṇate kavikratum ||
  • Mantra: "agnir no yajñam upa vetu sādhuyāgniṁ naro vi bharante gṛhe-gṛhe | agnir dūto abhavad dhavyavāhano 'gniṁ vṛṇānā vṛṇate kavikratum ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • agnir: Fire
    • no: our
    • yajñam: sacrifice or offering
    • upa: may approach
    • vetu: know or understand
    • sādhuyāgniṁ: the auspicious fire
    • naro: men or people
    • vi: indeed
    • bharante: carry or bear
    • gṛhe-gṛhe: in every home
    • dūto: messenger
    • abhavad: has become
    • dhavyavāhano: one who carries offerings
    • vṛṇānā: choosing or selecting
    • kavikratum: the wise or knowledgeable plan
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the sacred fire (Agni) as a central figure in rituals and daily life.
    • Agni is seen as a mediator between humans and the divine, representing purity and transformation.
    • The phrase "agnir no yajñam upa vetu" suggests that we seek the fire to guide our sacrifices, indicating the importance of intention and purity in our actions.
    • "Naro vi bharante gṛhe-gṛhe" emphasizes that every household should honor Agni, reflecting the idea that spirituality should be integrated into daily life.
    • Agni as a "dūto" or messenger signifies the role of fire in conveying our prayers and offerings to the divine.
    • The mantra highlights the importance of community and shared rituals, as it mentions "gṛhe-gṛhe," meaning in every home, suggesting that spirituality is a collective endeavor.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, lighting a fire during family gatherings or festivals symbolizes unity and the sharing of blessings.
    • In Hindu rituals, the Agni is often invoked during ceremonies like weddings and housewarming, where offerings are made to ensure prosperity and harmony.
    • Modern practices of mindfulness and intention-setting can be seen as a contemporary interpretation of the mantra's call for purity and focus in our actions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of Agni as a purifying force and a bridge between the material and spiritual worlds.
    • It teaches us to incorporate spirituality into our daily lives, making our homes sanctuaries of peace and intention.
    • By recognizing the role of Agni in our rituals and daily practices, we can foster a deeper connection with the divine and with each other, promoting harmony and collective well-being.
