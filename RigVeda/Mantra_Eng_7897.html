सं देवैः शोभते वृषा कविर योनाव अधि प्रियः |वृत्रहां देववीतमः ||
saṁ devaiḥ śobhate vṛṣā kavir yonāv adhi priyaḥ | vṛtrahā devavītamaḥ ||
  • saṁ devaiḥ: This phrase translates to "with the gods" or "together with the divine beings." It signifies the importance of divine presence and support in our lives.
  • śobhate: This means "shines" or "is radiant." It indicates a state of brilliance or excellence that comes from divine association.
  • vṛṣā: This word can be interpreted as "the strong" or "the powerful." It refers to those who possess strength, courage, and valor.
  • kavir: This translates to "the wise" or "the poet." It emphasizes the value of wisdom and creativity in understanding the world and expressing oneself.
  • yonāv: This means "in the womb" or "in the source." It suggests the origin or the place where something is nurtured and developed.
  • adhi priyaḥ: This translates to "dear above" or "beloved." It signifies the cherished nature of certain relationships or entities that hold a special place in our lives.
  • vṛtrahā: This term means "the slayer of Vritra," referring to the deity Indra, who is known for overcoming obstacles and challenges.
  • devavītamaḥ: This translates to "the best among the gods." It highlights the supreme nature of the divine and the ultimate power that governs the universe.

In summary, this mantra emphasizes the significance of divine support and wisdom in achieving strength and overcoming challenges. It reflects the idea that when one aligns with the divine and embraces wisdom, they can shine brightly and conquer obstacles in life. The mantra can be applied in real-life scenarios such as:


  • Seeking guidance and support from spiritual practices during difficult times.
  • Valuing wisdom and creativity in personal and professional endeavors.
  • Recognizing the importance of nurturing relationships that uplift and inspire.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, wisdom, and divine support in our journey through life.
