ऋतावरि दिवो अर्कैर अबोध्य आ रेवती रोधसी चित्रम् अस्तात् |आयतीं अग्न उषसं विभातीं वामम् एषि द्रविणं भिक्षमाणः ||
ṛtāvarī divo arkair abodhy ā revatī rodasī citram asthāt | āyatīm agna uṣasaṁ vibhātīṁ vāmam eṣi draviṇam bhikṣamāṇaḥ ||
  • ṛtāvarī: This term refers to the concept of truth or cosmic order. In real life, it can be likened to the principles of honesty and integrity that govern our actions and relationships.
  • divo: Meaning "of the heavens," this word signifies the divine or celestial realm. It can be associated with aspirations and goals that are lofty and noble.
  • arkair: This translates to "with rays" or "with light." In a practical sense, it can represent knowledge and enlightenment that illuminate our paths in life.
  • abodhy: This means "to awaken" or "to inspire." It reflects the importance of awakening one's consciousness and being aware of one's surroundings and actions.
  • ā: A preposition meaning "from" or "out of," indicating a source or origin.
  • revatī: This term can be interpreted as "prosperous" or "wealthy." It emphasizes the importance of abundance and well-being in our lives.
  • rodasī: Referring to "the earth" or "the world," it signifies our physical existence and the environment we inhabit.
  • citram: Meaning "colorful" or "varied," this word highlights the diversity of life experiences and the richness they bring.
  • asthāt: This translates to "has stood" or "has established." It suggests stability and permanence in the face of change.
  • āyatīm: Meaning "long-lasting" or "eternal," it reflects the desire for enduring values and principles in our lives.
  • agna: This term refers to "fire" or "the divine spark." It symbolizes energy, transformation, and the drive to achieve our goals.
  • uṣasaṁ: Meaning "of dawn," it signifies new beginnings and the hope that comes with each new day.
  • vibhātīṁ: This translates to "shining" or "illuminating," representing clarity and understanding in our thoughts and actions.
  • vāmam: Meaning "pleasant" or "agreeable," it emphasizes the importance of harmony and positivity in our interactions.
  • eṣi: This means "to go" or "to approach," indicating movement towards a goal or aspiration.
  • draviṇam: Referring to "wealth" or "resources," it highlights the significance of material and spiritual abundance.
  • bhikṣamāṇaḥ: This term means "seeking alms" or "requesting," which can symbolize humility and the recognition of our interdependence with others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking truth, prosperity, and enlightenment in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of awakening our consciousness, striving for noble goals, and recognizing the beauty and diversity of our experiences. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to live with integrity, pursue knowledge, and foster harmonious relationships, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and meaningful existence.
