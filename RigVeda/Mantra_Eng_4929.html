इमं नो अग्नि अध्वरं होतर वयुनशो यज |चिकित्वान दैव्यं जनम् ||
imaṁ no agne adhvaraṁ hotar vayunaśo yaja | cikitvān daivyaṁ janam ||
  • imaṁ - This word translates to "this" or "this one," indicating a focus on the subject at hand.
  • no - Meaning "our," it signifies possession or belonging.
  • agne - Refers to "Agni," the fire god, who is a central figure in Vedic rituals and symbolizes energy, transformation, and purity.
  • adhvaraṁ - This means "the sacrifice" or "the ritual," highlighting the importance of ceremonial acts in connecting with the divine.
  • hotar - Translates to "the priest" or "the one who invokes," emphasizing the role of the priest in performing rituals and invoking divine blessings.
  • vayunaśo - This can be interpreted as "of the wise" or "of the knowledgeable," suggesting the importance of wisdom in the performance of rituals.
  • yaja - Meaning "to worship" or "to offer," it indicates the act of devotion and the offering made to the divine.
  • cikitvān - This word means "having understood" or "having contemplated," pointing to the necessity of awareness and understanding in spiritual practices.
  • daivyaṁ - Translates to "divine," referring to the higher spiritual realm or the divine beings that are invoked during rituals.
  • janam - Meaning "people" or "community," it signifies the collective aspect of humanity and the importance of community in spiritual practices.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to Agni, the fire god, to bless and guide the community through the rituals performed by the priest, emphasizing the need for wisdom and understanding in these acts of devotion. It highlights the interconnectedness of the divine, the rituals, and the community.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Community Gatherings: Just as the mantra calls for collective worship, community events can foster unity and shared purpose, similar to how rituals bring people together.
  • Personal Reflection: The emphasis on understanding and contemplation can be applied in daily life through practices like meditation or mindfulness, encouraging individuals to seek deeper insights into their actions and beliefs.
  • Leadership and Guidance: The role of the priest as an invoker can be mirrored in leadership roles today, where wise and knowledgeable leaders guide their communities towards common goals.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of rituals, community, and wisdom in spiritual practices. It serves as a reminder that through understanding and collective effort, individuals can connect with the divine and foster a harmonious society. The principles embedded in this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, promoting unity, reflection, and guidance.
