त्वां ह त्यद इन्द्रार्णसातौ स्वर्मील्हे नर आजा हवन्ते |तव स्वधावा इयं आ समर्य ऊतिर वाजेष्व अतसाय्या भूत ||
tvāṁ ha tyad indrārṇasātau svarmīḻhe nara ājā havante | tava svadhāva iyam ā samarya ūtir vājeṣv atasāyyā bhūt ||
  • tvāṁ ha tyad indrārṇasātau svarmīḻhe nara ājā havante
    • tvāṁ - "You" or "to you": This refers to a divine entity, often interpreted as Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
    • ha - "Indeed": This word emphasizes the certainty or truth of the statement being made.
    • tyad - "That": Referring to something specific, often indicating a connection to the divine or a higher power.
    • indrārṇasātau - "In the realm of Indra": This phrase signifies the divine or celestial domain associated with Indra.
    • svarmīḻhe - "In the shining light": This can be interpreted as the brilliance or glory of the divine.
    • nara - "Man" or "human": This indicates the human aspect, suggesting a connection between the divine and humanity.
    • ājā - "Invoked" or "called upon": This indicates a plea or invocation to the divine.
    • havante - "Offerings": This refers to the offerings made to the divine, often in the form of prayers or rituals.
  • tava svadhāva iyam ā samarya ūtir vājeṣv atasāyyā bhūt
    • tava - "Your": This indicates possession, referring to the divine attributes or qualities.
    • svadhāva - "Self-essence" or "self-nature": This refers to the inherent qualities of the divine.
    • iyam - "This": Referring to the current invocation or offering being made.
    • ā - "From": This indicates a source or origin.
    • samarya - "Unity" or "togetherness": This signifies the importance of harmony and collective effort.
    • ūtir - "Elevation" or "upliftment": This refers to the spiritual or moral upliftment that comes from divine connection.
    • vājeṣv - "In the context of strength": This indicates the power and strength derived from the divine.
    • atasāyyā - "In the realm of abundance": This refers to the richness and prosperity that can be achieved through divine blessings.
    • bhūt - "Be" or "exist": This signifies the manifestation of the divine qualities in the material world.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the connection between the divine and humanity, highlighting the importance of invoking divine qualities for personal and communal upliftment. It signifies that through offerings and prayers, individuals can seek strength, unity, and abundance in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as community gatherings, rituals, or personal prayers where individuals seek to connect with a higher power for guidance and support. The mantra serves as a reminder of the potential for divine assistance in achieving harmony and prosperity in both personal and collective endeavors.
