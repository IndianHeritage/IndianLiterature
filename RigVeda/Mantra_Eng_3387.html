शतम् अश्मनमयीनाम् पुराम् इन्द्रो व्यास्यत् |दिवोदासाय दाशुषे ||
śatam aśmanmayīnām purām indro vy āsyat | divodāsāya dāśuṣe ||
  • śatam: This word translates to "hundred" or "many." It signifies abundance and prosperity.
  • aśmanmayīnām: This term refers to "made of stone" or "rock-like." It symbolizes strength, durability, and permanence.
  • purām: This means "city" or "fortress." It represents a place of safety, community, and civilization.
  • indro: This refers to "Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, associated with power, rain, and thunder. Indra symbolizes leadership and protection.
  • vy āsyat: This phrase can be interpreted as "may he protect" or "may he bestow." It indicates a plea for divine intervention and support.
  • divodāsāya: This term means "to the one who is a servant of the gods" or "to the divine." It emphasizes devotion and service to a higher power.
  • dāśuṣe: This translates to "for the one who gives" or "to the generous." It highlights the importance of generosity and sharing.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation for protection and abundance. It calls upon Indra to safeguard a strong and prosperous community, emphasizing the values of strength, generosity, and divine service.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Community Building: Just as the mantra speaks of a strong city, it can inspire communities to come together, fostering unity and resilience in the face of challenges.
  • Leadership: The invocation of Indra serves as a reminder for leaders to be protective and nurturing, ensuring the well-being of those they lead.
  • Generosity: The emphasis on giving encourages individuals to be generous in their actions, whether through charitable deeds or community service.

Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of strength, community, and divine support in achieving prosperity and safety. It serves as a reminder to cultivate these values in our lives, fostering a sense of responsibility towards others and the environment. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and supportive society.
