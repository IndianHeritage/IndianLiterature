यच चिद्धि वाम पुर ऋषयो जुहूरेऽवसे नराः |आ यातम अश्विनागतम उपेमां सुश्रुतिम मम ||
yac cid dhi vām pura ṛṣayo juhūre 'vase narā | ā yātam aśvinā gatam upemāṁ suṣṭutim mama ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yac - whatever
    • cid - indeed
    • dhi - thought or intellect
    • vām - your
    • pura - in the past
    • ṛṣayo - sages or seers
    • juhūre - have offered
    • 'vase - to the one who is in control
    • narā - men or people
    • ā yātam - may we attain
    • aśvinā - O Ashvins (the twin deities)
    • gatam - gone or departed
    • upemāṁ - my praise or commendation
    • suṣṭutim - good praise or eulogy
    • mama - my
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects a deep reverence for the wisdom of the sages of the past.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance and control from higher powers, represented by the Ashvins, who are known for their healing and benevolent qualities.
    • The invocation of the Ashvins suggests a desire for health, prosperity, and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be related to the practice of seeking mentorship or guidance from experienced individuals in various fields, such as business, education, or personal development.
    • For instance, a young entrepreneur may look up to seasoned business leaders for advice, paralleling the respect shown to the sages in the mantra.
    • Additionally, the call for the Ashvins can be likened to seeking support from healthcare professionals when facing health challenges, emphasizing the importance of community and expert assistance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the value of wisdom, guidance, and the interconnectedness of individuals seeking knowledge and support.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of the past and the importance of learning from those who have come before us.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek help, appreciate the wisdom of others, and strive for a harmonious existence through collaboration and respect.
