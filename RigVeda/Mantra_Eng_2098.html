मन्त्र: उक्तेष्व इन नु शूर येषु चाकन स्तोमेष्व इन्द्र रुद्रियेषु च |तुभ्येद एता यासु मन्दसानः प्र वायवे सिस्रते न शुभ्राः ||
uktheṣv in nu śūra yeṣu cākan stomeṣv indra rudriyeṣu ca | tubhyed etā yāsu mandasānaḥ pra vāyave sisrate na śubhrāḥ ||
  • uktheṣv in nu śūra yeṣu cākan stomeṣv indra rudriyeṣu ca
    • uktheṣv: This word refers to the hymns or praises that are sung. In real life, this can be likened to the way we celebrate achievements or express gratitude through songs and poetry.
    • in nu: This phrase can be interpreted as "indeed" or "truly," emphasizing the sincerity of the statement that follows.
    • śūra: This means "brave" or "valiant." In modern contexts, we can relate this to individuals who show courage in various fields, such as firefighters, soldiers, or activists.
    • yeṣu: This translates to "in whom" or "in those." It indicates a focus on specific individuals or entities.
    • cākan: This word means "to be present" or "to be found." It suggests the importance of presence and participation in communal activities.
    • stomeṣv: Referring to "in the praises" or "in the hymns," it highlights the significance of vocal expressions of reverence.
    • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength and power. In contemporary life, we can see Indra as a metaphor for leadership and authority figures who guide and protect.
    • rudriyeṣu: This refers to the "Rudras," a group of deities associated with storm and wind, representing the forces of nature. This can be related to environmental awareness and respect for nature's power.
  • tubhyed etā yāsu mandasānaḥ
    • tubhyed: This means "to you" or "for you," indicating a dedication or offering to a higher power.
    • etā: This translates to "these," referring to the offerings or praises being directed towards the divine.
    • yāsu: This means "in which" or "where," indicating a specific context or situation.
    • mandasānaḥ: This refers to "those who are gentle" or "calm." In real life, this can be associated with individuals who promote peace and harmony in society.
  • pra vāyave sisrate na śubhrāḥ
    • pra vāyave: This means "to the wind" or "to the air," symbolizing the vital force of life and communication.
    • sisrate: This translates to "to flow" or "to move," indicating the dynamic nature of life and energy.
    • na: This means "not," suggesting a negation or absence of something.
    • śubhrāḥ: This means "pure" or "auspicious," indicating a state of positivity and goodness.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of praising and recognizing the valor of individuals who contribute positively to society, akin to the way we honor leaders and protectors. It highlights the significance of gentle and peaceful individuals who promote harmony, while also acknowledging the vital forces of nature. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community gatherings, environmental initiatives, and leadership recognition events, where we celebrate courage, promote peace, and respect the natural world. Its significance lies in fostering a sense of unity and reverence for both human and natural forces, encouraging us to live harmoniously with one another and the environment.
