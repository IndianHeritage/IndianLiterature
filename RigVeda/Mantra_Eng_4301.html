यथा वातो यथा वनं यथा समुद्र एजति |एवा त्वं दशमास्य सहावेहि जरायुणा ||
yathā vāto yathā vanaṁ yathā samudra ejati | evā tvaṁ daśamāsya sahāvehi jarāyuṇā ||
  • yathā vāto: This phrase translates to "as the wind." It signifies the omnipresence and power of the wind, which can be gentle or fierce, much like the forces of nature in our lives.
  • yathā vanaṁ: Meaning "as the forest," this part emphasizes the richness and diversity of life found in nature. Forests are vital ecosystems that support countless species and provide resources for human survival.
  • yathā samudra ejati: This translates to "as the ocean moves." The ocean represents vastness, depth, and the ebb and flow of life. It can be calm or tumultuous, reflecting the emotional and physical challenges we face.
  • evā tvaṁ: This means "thus, you." It indicates a connection to the divine or a higher power, suggesting that the qualities of nature are mirrored in the divine essence.
  • daśamāsya: This term can be interpreted as "tenfold," symbolizing completeness or wholeness. It suggests that the divine encompasses all aspects of existence.
  • sahāvehi: This means "may you accompany." It expresses a desire for the divine presence to be with us, guiding and supporting us through life's journey.
  • jarāyuṇā: This translates to "with old age." It acknowledges the inevitability of aging and the wisdom that comes with it, reminding us to embrace the journey of life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the interconnectedness of nature, the divine, and human experience. It serves as a reminder of the forces that shape our lives—much like the wind, forest, and ocean. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In moments of stress, one can reflect on the calming presence of nature, finding solace in the wind or the ocean's waves.
  • In times of decision-making, invoking the wisdom of the forest can inspire thoughtful choices that consider the broader impact on the environment and community.
  • As we age, this mantra encourages us to embrace the journey, recognizing that wisdom and experience are valuable assets in navigating life's challenges.

Ultimately, this mantra encourages us to seek harmony with nature and the divine, fostering a sense of peace and purpose in our lives.
