तद् वो यामि द्रविणं सद्यौतयो येन स्वर्ण ततनाम नृमाभि | इदं सु मे मरुतो हर्यतां वचो यस्य तरेमा तरसा शतं हिमाः ||
tad vo yāmi draviṇaṁ sadyaūtayo yenā sva1r ṇa tatanāma nṝm̐r abhi | idaṁ su me maruto haryatā vaco yasya tarema tarasā śataṁ himāḥ ||
  • tad vo yāmi draviṇaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "I go to you for wealth." Here, "tad" means "that," "vo" means "to you," and "draviṇaṁ" refers to "wealth" or "resources." In real life, this can be understood as seeking support or resources from a higher power or community when in need.
  • sadyaūtayo: This term can be interpreted as "quickly" or "immediately." It emphasizes the urgency of the request for wealth or support. For example, in times of financial crisis, individuals often seek immediate assistance from friends, family, or community organizations.
  • yenā sva1r ṇa tatanāma nṝm̐r abhi: This part translates to "by whom we are named and recognized." It suggests that the source of wealth or support is also the source of identity and recognition. In a modern context, this can relate to how our professions or roles in society define us, such as being known as a teacher, doctor, or artist.
  • idaṁ su me maruto haryatā vaco: This phrase means "this is good for me, O Maruts, with your words." The Maruts are storm deities associated with strength and power. Here, it indicates that the speaker is seeking the blessings and guidance of these powerful forces. In everyday life, this can be likened to seeking advice from mentors or leaders who inspire and empower us.
  • yasya tarema tarasā śataṁ himāḥ: This translates to "by whom we may cross over with strength a hundred times." It signifies the ability to overcome challenges and obstacles with the help of divine or communal support. In real life, this can be seen in how teamwork and collaboration can help individuals achieve goals that seem insurmountable alone.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking support and resources from higher powers or the community, especially in times of need. It highlights the interconnectedness of identity, recognition, and the strength derived from collective efforts. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as seeking help during financial difficulties, relying on mentors for guidance, or collaborating with others to overcome challenges. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that we are not alone in our struggles and that support is available through both divine and communal channels.
