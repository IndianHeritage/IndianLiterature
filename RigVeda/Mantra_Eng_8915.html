यमाय सोमं सुनुत यमाय जुहुताहविः |यमं ह यज्ञो गच्छत्य अग्निदूतो अरंक्रितः ||
yamāya somaṁ sunuta yamāya juhutā haviḥ | yamaṁ ha yajño gacchaty agnidūto araṁkṛtaḥ ||
  • yamāya: This term refers to the concept of "Yama," which signifies control, restraint, or the moral code in life. In real life, this can be related to self-discipline and ethical living.
  • somaṁ: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. In modern terms, it can symbolize the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment.
  • sunuta: This word means "to be praised" or "to be invoked." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring the divine or the higher principles in our lives.
  • juhutā: This term means "to offer" or "to sacrifice." In everyday life, it can represent the act of giving, whether it be time, resources, or love, for the greater good.
  • haviḥ: This refers to offerings made during rituals. It signifies the importance of contributions and sacrifices we make in our lives, which can lead to personal and communal growth.
  • yajño: This word means "sacrifice" or "ritual." It highlights the significance of collective efforts and community gatherings in achieving common goals.
  • gacchaty: This means "to go" or "to reach." It signifies the journey or the process of achieving one's goals through dedication and effort.
  • agnidūto: This term refers to the "messenger of fire," symbolizing transformation and purification. In real life, it can represent the challenges we face that lead to personal growth.
  • araṁkṛtaḥ: This means "to be prepared" or "to be ready." It emphasizes the importance of being equipped and ready to face life's challenges and opportunities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of self-discipline, sacrifice, and the pursuit of higher knowledge. It teaches us the importance of honoring our commitments, making sacrifices for the greater good, and being prepared for the challenges that life presents. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, community service, and spiritual growth. By embodying the principles of Yama and making conscious offerings in our lives, we can foster a sense of purpose and connection with the world around us.
