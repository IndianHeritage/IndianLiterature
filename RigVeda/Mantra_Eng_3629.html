इन्द्रः सीतां न गृह्णातु ताम पूषानु यच्छतु |सा नः पयस्वती दूहाम उत्तराम्-उत्तराṁ समाम् ||
indraḥ sītāṁ ni gṛhṇātu tām pūṣānu yacchatu | sā naḥ payasvatī duhām uttarām-uttarāṁ samām ||
  • Indraḥ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Sītāṁ: Means 'the wealth' or 'prosperity'. In this context, it signifies the abundance that one seeks.
  • Ni gṛhṇātu: Translates to 'may he (Indra) accept' or 'may he take'. This is a call for acceptance and acknowledgment.
  • Tām: Refers to 'that' or 'her', indicating the wealth or prosperity being addressed.
  • Pūṣānu: Refers to Pūṣan, a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity, often invoked for blessings in agriculture and sustenance.
  • Yacchatu: Means 'may he grant' or 'may he provide'. This is a request for divine assistance.
  • Sā: Means 'that' or 'she', again referring to the wealth or prosperity.
  • Naḥ: Means 'for us', indicating a collective request or prayer.
  • Payasvatī: Translates to 'rich in milk' or 'nourishing', symbolizing abundance and fertility.
  • Duhām: Means 'of the cows' or 'of the nurturing', referring to the source of sustenance and nourishment.
  • Uttarām-uttarāṁ: Means 'the best of the best' or 'the highest', indicating a desire for the utmost quality in blessings.
  • Samām: Means 'equal' or 'similar', suggesting a balance or harmony in the blessings received.

Conclusion: This mantra is a prayer for the acceptance and granting of wealth and prosperity, invoking the blessings of Indra and Pūṣan. It emphasizes the importance of nourishment and abundance in life, both materially and spiritually. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts such as during agricultural rituals, business ventures, or personal endeavors where one seeks success and prosperity. It serves as a reminder to seek not just material wealth but also the nourishment of the mind and spirit, fostering a holistic approach to abundance in life.
