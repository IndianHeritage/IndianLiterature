प्रति घोराणाम एतानाम अयासाम मरुतां श्रिण्वा आयताम उपब्धिः |ये मार्त्यम पृतनायन्तं ऊमैर ऋणावानं न पतयन्त सर्गैः ||
prati ghorāṇām etānām ayāsām marutāṁ śṛṇva āyatām upabdiḥ | ye martyam pṛtanāyantam ūmair ṛṇāvānaṁ na patayanta sargaiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "prati ghorāṇām etānām ayāsām marutāṁ śṛṇva āyatām upabdiḥ | ye martyam pṛtanāyantam ūmair ṛṇāvānaṁ na patayanta sargaiḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Listen to the powerful Maruts, who are fierce and tireless. They do not let the mortal beings who are bound by fate fall into the cycle of creation."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • prati: towards or in relation to
    • ghorāṇām: fierce or terrible
    • etānām: these
    • ayāsām: tireless or without fatigue
    • marutāṁ: of the Maruts, the storm deities
    • śṛṇva: listen or hear
    • āyatām: powerful or strong
    • upabdiḥ: realization or attainment
    • ye: those who
    • martyam: mortal beings
    • pṛtanāyantam: bound by fate or destiny
    • ūmair: by the forces of nature
    • ṛṇāvānaṁ: do not let fall or descend
    • na: not
    • patayanta: allow to fall
    • sargaiḥ: into the cycle of creation

  • Contextual Significance:
    • The Maruts are often associated with storms and winds, symbolizing the forces of nature that are both fierce and protective.
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of listening to the powerful forces around us, which can guide and protect us from the cyclical nature of life and death.
    • In real life, this can be interpreted as a call to be aware of the challenges we face and to seek strength from the natural world and its rhythms.

  • Real-Life Application:
    • In times of adversity, individuals can draw inspiration from the Maruts, recognizing that challenges can be faced with strength and resilience.
    • For example, during a natural disaster, communities often come together, embodying the spirit of the Maruts, to support one another and rebuild.
    • This mantra can also serve as a reminder to remain grounded and not be overwhelmed by life's challenges, as the Maruts protect those who are aware and prepared.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of recognizing and harnessing the powerful forces of nature and the universe.
    • It encourages individuals to listen to the wisdom of these forces, which can help them navigate the complexities of life.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that while life may present challenges, there is strength and support available to those who seek it.
