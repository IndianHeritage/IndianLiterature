अग्निर हि वाजिनं विशे ददाति विश्वचर्षणिः | अग्नī राये स्वाभुवं स प्रीतो याति वार्यम इषं स्तोत्रिभ्य आ भर ||
agnir hi vājinaṁ viśe dadāti viśvacarṣaṇiḥ | agnī rāye svābhuvaṁ sa prīto yāti vāryam iṣaṁ stotṛbhya ā bhara ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Agnir: Fire
    • hi: indeed
    • vājinaṁ: the one who bestows strength or wealth
    • viśe: to the community or people
    • dadāti: gives
    • viśvacarṣaṇiḥ: the one who is all-pervading or universal
    • Agnī: O Fire
    • rāye: for prosperity or wealth
    • svābhuvaṁ: self-generated or inherent
    • sa: he
    • prīto: pleased
    • yāti: goes or moves
    • vāryam: to the offerings or prayers
    • iṣaṁ: the desire or wish
    • stotṛbhya: to the worshippers
    • ā: towards
    • bhara: bring or bestow
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the sacred fire, as a divine entity that provides strength and prosperity to the community.
    • Agni is seen as a universal force that is not only a physical element but also a spiritual guide that connects humans with the divine.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of fire in rituals and offerings, suggesting that when one offers prayers to Agni, it leads to blessings and fulfillment of desires.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is a symbol of purification and transformation. For instance, in Hindu weddings, the sacred fire (Agni) is a witness to the vows taken by the couple, symbolizing their commitment and the prosperity they seek together.
    • In modern contexts, the idea of 'fire' can be seen in motivational speeches where individuals are encouraged to ignite their passion and drive to achieve their goals, akin to how Agni bestows strength.
    • In environmental practices, the controlled use of fire in agriculture (like controlled burns) can lead to healthier ecosystems, reflecting the idea of Agni as a force that nurtures and sustains life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of Agni as a giver of strength and prosperity, reinforcing the idea that through devotion and offerings, one can attract positive energies and blessings into their life.
    • In real life, the principles embodied in this mantra can be applied in various aspects, such as personal growth, community welfare, and environmental sustainability, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing our spiritual and physical environments.
