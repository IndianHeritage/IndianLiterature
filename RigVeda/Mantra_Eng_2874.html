आ नस्तुजं रयिम्भरांशं न प्रतिजानते |वृक्षं पक्वं फलमङ्कीव धूनुहीन्द्र सम्पारणं वसु ||
ā nas tujaṁ rayim bharāṁśaṁ na pratijānate | vṛkṣam pakvam phalam aṅkīva dhūnuhīndra sampāraṇaṁ vasu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • nas: us
    • tujaṁ: your
    • rayim: wealth or prosperity
    • bharāṁśaṁ: abundance
    • na: not
    • pratijānate: to be denied or rejected
    • vṛkṣam: tree
    • pakvam: ripe
    • phalam: fruit
    • aṅkīva: like
    • dhūnuhī: to be shaken or stirred
    • indra: a deity, often associated with strength and power
    • sampāraṇaṁ: completion or fulfillment
    • vasu: wealth or good fortune
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for the abundance of wealth and prosperity to come to us without denial.
    • The imagery of a ripe fruit on a tree symbolizes the rewards of hard work and the fruition of efforts.
    • It suggests that just as a ripe fruit is ready to be harvested, so too should our efforts yield results.
    • The mention of Indra signifies the invocation of divine support in achieving these goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to work diligently and expect the fruits of one's labor, such as profits and growth.
    • In personal life, it can be applied to the pursuit of education or skills, where the 'ripe fruit' represents the knowledge and opportunities that come from hard work.
    • In community efforts, it can symbolize collective action towards a common goal, where the 'wealth' is the benefits that accrue to all members.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of striving for prosperity and the belief that such efforts will not be in vain.
    • It serves as a motivational reminder that with dedication and divine support, one can achieve abundance in various aspects of life.
    • In essence, it encourages individuals to remain hopeful and persistent in their pursuits, trusting that their hard work will lead to fruitful outcomes.
