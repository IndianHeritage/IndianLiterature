ऊर्ध्वासस त्वान्व इन्दवो bhuvan दसमं उप द्यवि |सं ते नमन्त कृत्स्तयः ||
ūrdhvāsas tvānv indavo bhuvan dasmam upa dyavi | saṁ te namanta kṛṣṭayaḥ ||
  • ūrdhvāsas: This term can be translated as "those who are elevated" or "those who rise above." It signifies a state of higher consciousness or spiritual elevation.
  • tvānv: This means "to you" or "to your essence," indicating a direction of reverence or devotion towards a higher power or divine entity.
  • indavo: This refers to "the Indus" or "the divine," often associated with the gods or celestial beings in Vedic literature.
  • bhuvan: This word translates to "world" or "universe," encompassing all realms of existence.
  • dasmam: This means "the tenth" or "the highest," suggesting a pinnacle or ultimate state of being.
  • upa dyavi: This phrase can be interpreted as "towards the heavens" or "up to the sky," indicating a spiritual ascent or connection with the divine.
  • saṁ: This means "together" or "with," emphasizing unity and collective consciousness.
  • te: This translates to "your," referring to the divine or higher power being addressed.
  • namanta: This means "to bow" or "to offer respect," indicating an act of reverence and humility.
  • kṛṣṭayaḥ: This term can be understood as "those who are devoted" or "the worshippers," referring to individuals who seek a connection with the divine.

  • Meaning: The mantra expresses a deep reverence for the divine, acknowledging the elevated state of being that comes from connecting with higher consciousness. It emphasizes the importance of unity and collective devotion towards the divine essence that governs the universe.
  • Real-life Example: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in meditation practices where individuals seek to elevate their consciousness and connect with a higher power. For instance, during group meditations or spiritual gatherings, participants often come together to express their devotion and seek a collective upliftment of spirit.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for spiritual elevation and collective devotion. It serves as a reminder of the importance of connecting with the divine and recognizing the unity of all beings in the pursuit of higher consciousness. In real-life scenarios, it can inspire individuals to engage in practices that foster spiritual growth, community bonding, and a deeper understanding of their place in the universe.
