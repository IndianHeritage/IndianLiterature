विचक्रमे पृथिवीं एष एतां क्षेत्राय विष्णुर मनुषे दशस्यन् |ध्रुवासो अस्य कीरयो जनासा उरुक्षितिं सुजनिमा चकार ||
vi cakrame pṛthivīm eṣa etāṁ kṣetrāya viṣṇur manuṣe daśasyan | dhruvāso asya kīrayo janāsa urukṣitiṁ sujanimā cakāra ||
  • Translation: "He (Vishnu) has traversed the earth; this is the field of action for man. The eternal one has created the vastness of the earth for the benefit of mankind."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • vi cakrame: He has traversed or moved around.
    • pṛthivīm: The earth or the physical world.
    • eṣa: This is.
    • etāṁ: This (referring to the earth).
    • kṣetrāya: For the field of action or work.
    • viṣṇur: Vishnu, the preserver and protector in Hindu mythology.
    • manuṣe: For mankind or human beings.
    • daśasyan: The eternal or everlasting one.
    • dhruvāso: The one who is stable or unchanging.
    • asya: Of this.
    • kīrayo: The vastness or greatness.
    • janāsa: For the people or mankind.
    • urukṣitiṁ: The vastness of the earth.
    • sujanimā: Created well or beneficially.
    • cakāra: Has created or made.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of the earth as a field for human action. In real life, this can be seen in agriculture, where farmers cultivate the land to produce food for society.
    • The concept of Vishnu as a protector can be related to environmental conservation efforts, where individuals and organizations work to preserve the earth for future generations.
    • In urban planning, the idea of creating a beneficial environment for mankind reflects the essence of this mantra, as cities are designed to enhance the quality of life for their inhabitants.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of the earth as a vital space for human activity and the role of divine protection in our endeavors. It encourages us to appreciate and care for our environment, reminding us that our actions have a direct impact on the world around us. In contemporary society, this mantra can inspire sustainable practices, community development, and a deeper connection to nature, ultimately guiding us towards a harmonious existence with our surroundings.
