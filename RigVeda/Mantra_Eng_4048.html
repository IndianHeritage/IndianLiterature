कदु प्रियाय धाम्ने मनामहे स्वकक्षत्राय स्वयशसे महे वयम् |आमेन्यस्य रजसो यद अभ्र आम् अपो वृणानां वितनोति मायिनी ||
kad u priyāya dhāmne manāmahe svakṣatrāya svayaśase mahe vayam | āmenyasya rajaso yad abhra ām̐ apo vṛṇānā vitanoti māyinī ||
  • kad u priyāya dhāmne manāmahe: This phrase can be translated as "When we meditate upon the beloved abode." Here, "priyāya" refers to something dear or beloved, and "dhāmne" signifies a place or abode. This suggests a focus on a cherished or sacred space, which can be interpreted as a spiritual or emotional sanctuary.
  • svakṣatrāya svayaśase mahe vayam: This translates to "We are great in our own right, in our own power." "Svayaśase" indicates self-illumination or inherent glory, while "mahe vayam" emphasizes our greatness. This can be related to self-empowerment and recognizing one's own strengths and capabilities in real life.
  • āmenyasya rajaso yad abhra: This means "As the clouds of the sky bring forth the rain." "Āmenyasya" refers to the clouds, and "rajaso" relates to the essence or nature of something. This metaphor illustrates the idea of nourishment and abundance, akin to how rain nourishes the earth.
  • ām̐ apo vṛṇānā vitanoti māyinī: This translates to "The magical waters that spread forth." "Apo" refers to water, and "māyinī" suggests something that is magical or enchanting. This can symbolize the flow of creativity and inspiration in our lives, akin to how water sustains life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of meditation on cherished spaces and recognizing our inherent greatness. It draws parallels between the nurturing aspects of nature, such as rain and water, and our own potential for growth and creativity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, spiritual practices, and fostering a sense of community. By meditating on our strengths and the nurturing aspects of our environment, we can cultivate a fulfilling and empowered life.
