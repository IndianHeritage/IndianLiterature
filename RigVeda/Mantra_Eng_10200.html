पश्यन्न अन्यस्य अतिथिं वयायाऽ ऋतस्य धाम विमि पुरूणि |शंसामि पितरे असुराय शेवम् अयज्ञियाद यज्ञियं भागम् एमी ||
paśyann anyasyā atithiṁ vayāyā ṛtasya dhāma vi mime purūṇi | śaṁsāmi pitre asurāya śevam ayajñiyād yajñiyam bhāgam emi ||
  • paśyann anyasyā atithiṁ: This phrase translates to "seeing the guest of another." In many cultures, hospitality is a revered value. For example, in Indian culture, the concept of "Atithi Devo Bhava" emphasizes treating guests as divine. This mantra highlights the importance of recognizing and honoring guests, which can be seen in practices like inviting friends and family for meals or celebrations.
  • vayāyā ṛtasya dhāma vi mime purūṇi: This part can be interpreted as "I seek the abode of truth." The term "ṛta" refers to cosmic order or truth. In real life, this can relate to the pursuit of honesty and integrity in our actions. For instance, in a workplace, maintaining transparency in communication fosters trust among colleagues and leads to a harmonious environment.
  • śaṁsāmi pitre asurāya: This translates to "I offer praise to the father and the divine." This can be seen as a call to honor our ancestors and the divine forces in our lives. In many families, rituals and prayers are performed to pay respect to ancestors, especially during festivals like Pitru Paksha in Hinduism, where offerings are made to honor deceased family members.
  • śevam ayajñiyād yajñiyam bhāgam emi: This means "I seek the share of the offerings." It emphasizes the importance of sharing and community. In modern society, this can be reflected in charitable acts, where individuals or organizations contribute to the welfare of others, such as donating to food banks or participating in community service projects.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of hospitality, truth, reverence for ancestors, and the spirit of sharing. Its significance lies in promoting values that foster community and respect for others. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide our interactions, encouraging us to be more hospitable, truthful, and generous. By embodying these values, we contribute to a more harmonious and compassionate society.
