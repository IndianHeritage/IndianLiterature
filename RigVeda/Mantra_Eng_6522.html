दामानं विश्वचर्षणे अग्निं विश्वमनो गिरा |उत स्तुषे विष्पर्धसो रथानाम् ||
dāmānaṁ viśvacarṣaṇe 'gniṁ viśvamano girā | uta stuṣe viṣpardhaso rathānām ||
  • Mantra: "dāmānaṁ viśvacarṣaṇe 'gniṁ viśvamano girā | uta stuṣe viṣpardhaso rathānām ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dāmānaṁ: of the one who is restrained or controlled
    • viśvacarṣaṇe: in the universal or all-encompassing
    • 'gniṁ: to Agni, the fire or divine energy
    • viśvamano: with a universal mind or consciousness
    • girā: through speech or words
    • uta: and
    • stuṣe: to the one who is praised
    • viṣpardhaso: of the one who is powerful or strong
    • rathānām: of the chariots or vehicles
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who represents energy, transformation, and the divine spark within all beings.
    • The phrase "dāmānaṁ viśvacarṣaṇe" suggests the importance of control and restraint in the pursuit of universal knowledge and understanding.
    • "viśvamano girā" emphasizes the power of speech and communication in shaping our thoughts and actions, highlighting the need for a universal mindset.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "uta stuṣe viṣpardhaso rathānām," acknowledges the strength and power of those who are praised, suggesting that recognition and appreciation can enhance one's capabilities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who practice restraint and control in their decision-making often foster a culture of respect and collaboration, leading to better outcomes.
    • Effective communication, as highlighted in the mantra, is crucial in personal relationships. Open and honest dialogue can resolve conflicts and strengthen bonds.
    • In community service, recognizing and praising the efforts of volunteers can motivate them and enhance their commitment to the cause.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of control, communication, and recognition in achieving success and harmony in life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, emphasizing that a balanced approach leads to positive outcomes.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a more harmonious and productive existence, both personally and collectively.
