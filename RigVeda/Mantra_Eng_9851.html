उत नो रुद्राः चिन् मृळतां अश्विनां विश्वे देवासो रथस्पतिर भगः |ऋभुर वाजा ऋभुक्षणः परिज्मा विश्ववेदसः ||
uta no rudrā cin mṛḻatām aśvinā viśve devāso rathaspatir bhagaḥ | ṛbhur vāja ṛbhukṣaṇaḥ parijmā viśvavedasaḥ ||
  • uta no rudrā: This phrase can be translated as "O Rudra, may you come to us." Rudra is a deity associated with storms and the hunt, often seen as a fierce protector. In real life, this can be understood as a call for divine protection and guidance in times of trouble or uncertainty.
  • cin mṛḻatām: This means "may you be pleased." It reflects the desire for the deity's favor and blessings. In everyday life, this can be likened to seeking approval or support from a mentor or leader, emphasizing the importance of relationships and goodwill.
  • aśvinā: Referring to the Ashvins, the twin gods of medicine and health. This highlights the significance of health and well-being in our lives. For example, in modern times, we often seek medical help or wellness practices to maintain our health.
  • viśve devāso: This translates to "all the gods." It signifies the collective power of divine beings. In a real-life context, this can be seen as a reminder of the interconnectedness of various forces in our lives, whether they be spiritual, social, or environmental.
  • rathaspatir bhagaḥ: "The lord of the chariot, the blessed one." This can symbolize the journey of life and the guidance we seek along the way. In practical terms, it can relate to the importance of having a clear direction and purpose in our endeavors, much like a charioteer guiding a chariot.
  • ṛbhur vāja: This means "the one who is rich in strength." It emphasizes the value of strength and resilience. In real life, this can be applied to personal development, where individuals strive to build their inner strength to overcome challenges.
  • ṛbhukṣaṇaḥ: This translates to "the one who provides nourishment." It highlights the importance of sustenance, both physical and spiritual. In modern life, this can be related to the need for balanced nutrition and emotional support from family and friends.
  • parijmā viśvavedasaḥ: This phrase means "the one who encompasses all knowledge." It signifies the pursuit of wisdom and understanding. In today's world, this can be seen in the value placed on education and lifelong learning, as individuals seek to expand their horizons and make informed decisions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for divine guidance, health, strength, and wisdom. Its significance lies in the holistic approach it advocates for life, emphasizing the need for protection, nourishment, and knowledge. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek balance in their lives, cultivate resilience, and foster connections with the divine and the community around them. By invoking these qualities, one can navigate the complexities of life with greater clarity and purpose.
