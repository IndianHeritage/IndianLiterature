अरं मे गन्तं हवनायास्मै गृणानां यथा पिबाथो अन्धः |परि ह त्यद् वर्तिर याथो ऋषो न यत् परो नान्तरस तुतुर्यāt ||
aram me gantaṁ havanāyāsmai gṛṇānā yathā pibātho andhaḥ | pari ha tyad vartir yātho riṣo na yat paro nāntaras tuturyāt ||
  • aram me gantaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "let my call be heard." It signifies the importance of communication and the desire for one's voice to reach the divine or the universe.
  • havanāyāsmai: This means "for the purpose of offering." In Vedic rituals, offerings are made to deities, symbolizing gratitude and the seeking of blessings.
  • gṛṇānā: This translates to "singing" or "chanting." It emphasizes the power of sound and vibration in spiritual practices, akin to how music can uplift the spirit in everyday life.
  • yathā pibātho andhaḥ: This phrase means "as the blind drink." It suggests that there are forces or energies that we may not perceive directly, yet they nourish and sustain us, similar to how we consume food and water without always being aware of their sources.
  • pari ha tyad vartir: This translates to "truly, that which is." It points to the essence of reality and existence, reminding us to recognize the truth in our lives.
  • yātho riṣo: This means "where the sage goes." It indicates the journey of wisdom and the pursuit of knowledge, akin to how individuals seek guidance from mentors or teachers in their personal and professional lives.
  • na yat paro: This translates to "not that which is beyond." It suggests that we should focus on what is within our reach and understanding, rather than getting lost in the complexities of the unknown.
  • nāntaras tuturyāt: This means "may we not be disturbed." It is a plea for peace and stability in our lives, reflecting the universal desire for tranquility amidst chaos.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking connection with the divine through communication, offerings, and the pursuit of wisdom. It highlights the importance of being aware of the unseen forces that nourish us and encourages us to focus on our journey towards knowledge and peace. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during meditation, rituals, or even in daily affirmations, reminding us to seek clarity, express gratitude, and maintain inner calm amidst the challenges we face. By embodying these principles, we can cultivate a more harmonious and fulfilling existence.
