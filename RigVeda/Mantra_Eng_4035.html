इन्द्राग्निमित्रावरुणादितिं स्वः पृथिवीं द्यां मरुतः पर्वतां अपः |हुवे विष्णुम्पूषणं ब्रह्मणस पतिं भगं नु शंसं सवितारमूतये ||
indrāgnī mitrāvaruṇāditiṁ svaḥ pṛthivīṁ dyām marutaḥ parvatām̐ apaḥ | huve viṣṇum pūṣaṇam brahmaṇas patim bhagaṁ nu śaṁsaṁ savitāram ūtaye ||
  • Indrāgnī: Refers to the deities Indra and Agni. Indra is the king of gods and represents strength and power, while Agni is the fire god, symbolizing energy and transformation.
  • Mitrāvaruṇā: These are the twin deities Mitra and Varuna. Mitra represents friendship and harmony, while Varuna embodies the cosmic order and moral law.
  • Aditiṁ: Aditi is the mother of the gods, representing infinity and the nurturing aspect of the universe.
  • Svaḥ: This term signifies the heavenly realm or the spiritual plane, indicating a connection to the divine.
  • Pṛthivīṁ: Refers to the Earth, symbolizing stability and grounding in the material world.
  • Dyām: Represents the sky or the heavens, indicating the vastness and the spiritual dimension of existence.
  • Marutaḥ: The Maruts are storm deities, representing the forces of nature and the dynamic aspects of life.
  • Parvatām̐: Refers to mountains, symbolizing strength, permanence, and the connection between the earth and the heavens.
  • Apaḥ: Means waters, representing life, nourishment, and the flow of energy in the universe.
  • Huve: This is a call to invoke or praise, indicating a desire to connect with the divine.
  • Viṣṇum: Refers to Lord Vishnu, the preserver and protector in the Hindu trinity, symbolizing sustenance and balance.
  • Pūṣaṇam: Pūṣaṇa is a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity, representing abundance in life.
  • Brahmaṇas patim: This phrase means the lord of the Brahman, indicating a connection to the ultimate reality or the source of all creation.
  • Bhagaṁ: Refers to wealth and prosperity, symbolizing the material and spiritual abundance in life.
  • Nu: A particle used for emphasis, often translated as "indeed" or "truly."
  • Śaṁsaṁ: Means to praise or glorify, indicating the importance of acknowledging the divine in our lives.
  • Savitāram: Refers to Savitar, the sun god, symbolizing illumination, energy, and the source of life.
  • Ūtaye: This term signifies the act of offering or invoking blessings, indicating a desire for divine grace.

Conclusion: This mantra is a profound invocation that brings together various aspects of the universe, including deities representing strength, harmony, nourishment, and cosmic order. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of the material and spiritual realms, urging individuals to seek balance and harmony in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in rituals, meditation, or daily affirmations to cultivate a sense of gratitude, connection to nature, and awareness of the divine presence in all aspects of life. By acknowledging and invoking these energies, individuals can foster a deeper understanding of their place in the universe and strive for a harmonious existence.
