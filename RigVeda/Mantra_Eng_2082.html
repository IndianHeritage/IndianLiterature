अत्रिम अनु स्वराज्यम अग्निम उक्तानि वावृधुः |विश्वा अधि श्रियो दधे ||
atrim anu svarājyam agnim ukthāni vāvṛdhuḥ | viśvā adhi śriyo dadhe ||
  • atrim: This word can be interpreted as "to the one who is pure" or "to the one who is free from impurities." In real life, this can refer to individuals who strive for purity in their thoughts, actions, and intentions.
  • anu: Meaning "following" or "after," this word signifies the importance of continuity and progression in life. For example, in a career, one must follow a path of learning and growth to achieve success.
  • svarājyam: This term translates to "sovereignty" or "self-rule." It emphasizes the idea of self-governance and autonomy. In a modern context, this can relate to personal empowerment and taking control of one’s own life decisions.
  • agnim: Referring to "fire," this symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. Fire is often used in rituals and represents the drive and passion one has in pursuing their goals.
  • ukthāni: This word means "utterances" or "sacrifices." It highlights the importance of words and intentions in manifesting desires. In everyday life, this can be seen in the power of positive affirmations and the impact of our spoken words on our reality.
  • vāvṛdhuḥ: This translates to "may they increase" or "may they grow." It signifies the aspiration for growth and abundance in various aspects of life, such as relationships, knowledge, and prosperity.
  • viśvā: Meaning "all" or "universal," this word encompasses the idea of inclusivity and interconnectedness. It reminds us that our actions affect not just ourselves but the larger community and environment.
  • adhi: This term means "upon" or "over," indicating a sense of elevation or enhancement. It suggests that we should strive to rise above challenges and limitations.
  • śriyo: This word translates to "prosperity" or "wealth." It represents not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional richness. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of a fulfilling life that encompasses various forms of abundance.
  • dadhe: Meaning "to place" or "to establish," this word emphasizes the act of creating a foundation for success and well-being. It encourages individuals to actively build their lives and futures.

Conclusion: The mantra "atrim anu svarājyam agnim ukthāni vāvṛdhuḥ | viśvā adhi śriyo dadhe ||" encapsulates the essence of personal empowerment, growth, and interconnectedness. It encourages individuals to pursue purity in their intentions, take control of their lives, and strive for prosperity in all forms. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, career advancement, and community engagement. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can create a more fulfilling and abundant life while positively impacting those around them.
