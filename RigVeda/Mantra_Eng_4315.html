एषा गोभिर अरुणैः युजानास्रेदन्ती रयिम अप्रायु चक्रे |पथो रदन्ती सुविताया देवी पुरुष्टुता विश्ववारा वि भाति ||
eṣā gobhir aruṇebhir yujānāsredhantī rayim aprāyu cakre | patho radantī suvitāya devī puruṣṭutā viśvavārā vi bhāti ||
  • eṣā - This word translates to "this" or "here," indicating a presence or a specific focus.
  • gobhir - Refers to "cows," which in Vedic literature symbolize wealth, nourishment, and prosperity.
  • aruṇebhir - Means "with the red ones," often associated with the dawn or the sun, symbolizing new beginnings and enlightenment.
  • yujānā - Translates to "joining" or "uniting," suggesting a coming together of forces or elements.
  • sredhantī - Means "to bring forth" or "to produce," indicating the act of generating or manifesting something valuable.
  • rayim - Refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," emphasizing the importance of abundance in life.
  • aprāyu - Means "not to be lost" or "everlasting," suggesting permanence and stability in wealth.
  • cakre - Translates to "to create" or "to establish," indicating an active role in the manifestation of wealth.
  • patho - Means "paths," symbolizing the various routes or choices one can take in life.
  • radantī - Refers to "roaring" or "resounding," suggesting a powerful presence or impact.
  • suvitāya - Means "well-nourished" or "well-fed," indicating a state of abundance and health.
  • devī - Refers to "goddess," symbolizing divine feminine energy and blessings.
  • puruṣṭutā - Means "praise of man," indicating the acknowledgment of human effort and devotion.
  • viśvavārā - Translates to "all-protecting," suggesting a universal safeguard or blessing.
  • vi bhāti - Means "shines" or "illuminates," indicating the presence of light and clarity.

This mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of wealth, nourishment, and divine blessings. It emphasizes the importance of unity and the active role individuals play in creating and sustaining prosperity. The imagery of cows and the dawn suggests that wealth is not just material but also spiritual and nourishing, akin to the sustenance provided by cows in agrarian societies.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In business, it encourages collaboration and unity among team members to create a prosperous environment.
  • In personal life, it highlights the importance of nurturing relationships and community ties to foster a sense of abundance.
  • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder to seek divine blessings and acknowledge the efforts of oneself and others in the pursuit of wealth and well-being.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to wealth and prosperity. It teaches that true abundance comes from unity, nourishment, and divine blessings. By applying these principles in our daily lives, we can create a more prosperous and harmonious existence, both individually and collectively.
