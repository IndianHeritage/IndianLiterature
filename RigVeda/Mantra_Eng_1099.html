स जामिभिर यत समजाति मीळहे 'जामिभिर वा पुरूहूत एवैः |अपां तोकस्य तनयस्य जेषे मरुत्वान नो भवत्व इन्द्र ऊती ||
sa jāmibhir yat samajāti mīḻhe 'jāmibhir vā puruhūta evaiḥ | apāṁ tokasya tanayasya jeṣe marutvān no bhavatv indra ūtī ||
  • sa jāmibhir yat samajāti mīḻhe: This phrase can be translated as "He who is united with the mothers." Here, "jāmibhir" refers to the nurturing aspects of nature or the divine feminine, symbolizing the supportive and protective qualities that foster growth and development.
  • 'jāmibhir vā puruhūta evaiḥ: This part translates to "or with the many who are invoked." It emphasizes the importance of community and collective effort in achieving goals. The term "puruhūta" refers to those who are called upon, suggesting that collaboration and unity are essential for success.
  • apāṁ tokasya tanayasya jeṣe: This translates to "may the son of the waters be victorious." Here, "apāṁ" refers to water, which is a vital element for life. The "son of the waters" symbolizes the potential for growth and prosperity that comes from nurturing environments.
  • marutvān no bhavatv indra ūtī: This means "may the powerful Indra grant us strength." Indra is a significant deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, storms, and strength. This line invokes the divine for support and empowerment in overcoming challenges.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, nurturing, and divine support in achieving success. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to various aspects such as teamwork in professional settings, community support during challenging times, and the importance of a nurturing environment for personal growth. By recognizing the interconnectedness of individuals and the need for collective effort, we can foster a more supportive and prosperous society. The invocation of divine strength serves as a reminder that seeking help and guidance from higher powers can empower us to overcome obstacles and achieve our goals.
