यश्चिद्धित्वा बहुभ्याऽसुतावां आविवासति |उग्रं तद्पत्यते शव इन्द्रोऽङ्ग ||
yaś cid dhi tvā bahubhya ā sutāvām̐ āvivāsati | ugraṁ tat patyate śava indro aṅga ||
  • yaś cid dhi tvā - This phrase can be translated as "Whatever is yours." It signifies possession or ownership, emphasizing the importance of recognizing what belongs to us in a broader sense, including our responsibilities and relationships.
  • bahubhya ā sutāvām̐ - This translates to "from many, the one who is born." It suggests the idea of unity arising from diversity, highlighting how individual identities come together to form a collective whole.
  • āvivāsati - This means "is established" or "is set forth." It indicates the process of bringing something into existence or making it manifest in the world.
  • ugraṁ tat patyate - This can be interpreted as "the fierce one is to be worshipped." It refers to the idea of reverence towards powerful forces or deities, acknowledging their strength and influence in our lives.
  • śava indro aṅga - This translates to "Indra, the lord of the dead." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, rain, and thunder. Here, it suggests a connection to the cycle of life and death, and the importance of honoring those who have passed.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of ownership, unity, reverence, and the cycle of life. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Recognizing our responsibilities towards our family and community, understanding that our actions impact the collective.
  • Embracing diversity in our relationships and workplaces, acknowledging that different perspectives contribute to a stronger whole.
  • Practicing gratitude and reverence towards the forces of nature and the universe, which can lead to a deeper appreciation of life.
  • Honoring the memory of those who have passed, understanding the importance of legacy and the continuity of life.

This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of respect and responsibility in our lives.
