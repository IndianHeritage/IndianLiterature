प्रत्नो हि कम īḍ्यो अध्वरेषु सना च होतā नव्यश च सत्त्सि |स्वां चाग्ने तन्वं पिप्रयस्वास्मभ्यं च सौभगम ā यजस्व ||
pratno hi kam īḍyo adhvareṣu sanāc ca hotā navyaś ca satsi | svāṁ cāgne tanvam piprayasvāsmabhyaṁ ca saubhagam ā yajasva ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pratno: May we be endowed
    • hi: indeed
    • kam: with desires
    • īḍyo: worthy of worship
    • adhvareṣu: in the sacrifices
    • sanāc: and from the ancient
    • ca: and
    • hotā: the priest
    • navyaś: new
    • ca: and
    • satsi: in the assembly
    • svāṁ: our
    • cāgne: O Agni (Fire)
    • tanvam: body
    • piprayas: may you nourish
    • vā: or
    • asmabhyaṁ: for us
    • ca: and
    • saubhagam: prosperity
    • ā: to
    • yajasva: may you perform
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • It expresses a desire for blessings and prosperity through rituals and sacrifices.
    • The invocation of Agni signifies the importance of fire in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity, transformation, and the divine presence.
    • The mantra emphasizes the need for both ancient wisdom and new beginnings, suggesting a balance between tradition and innovation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the importance of rituals in various cultures, such as lighting candles during festivals or performing ceremonies for significant life events.
    • It can also be seen in the context of seeking blessings for new ventures, such as starting a business or a new project, where individuals often perform rituals to invoke prosperity.
    • The balance between tradition and modernity can be observed in how people incorporate traditional practices into contemporary life, such as celebrating Diwali with both traditional and modern elements.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine blessings through rituals, emphasizing the role of Agni as a facilitator of prosperity and well-being.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of honoring traditions while embracing new opportunities, making it relevant in various aspects of life, from personal growth to community celebrations.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to cultivate a harmonious relationship with the divine and the world around them, fostering a sense of gratitude and purpose.
