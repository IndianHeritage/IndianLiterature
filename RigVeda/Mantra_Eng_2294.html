धृतव्रता आदित्याऽइषिरा आरे मत्कर्त रहसूर इवागः |श्रिण्वतो वो वरुण मित्र देवाः भद्रस्य विद्वांऽ अवसे हुवे वः ||
dhṛtavratā ādityā iṣirā āre mat karta rahasūr ivāgaḥ | śṛṇvato vo varuṇa mitra devā bhadrasya vidvām̐ avase huve vaḥ ||
  • dhṛtavratā: This term refers to those who are steadfast in their vows or commitments. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who remain dedicated to their goals, such as a student who studies diligently for exams despite distractions.
  • ādityā: This word signifies the solar deities or the sun. The sun is often associated with energy, vitality, and life. For example, the sun's rays are essential for photosynthesis in plants, which in turn supports life on Earth.
  • iṣirā: This term can be interpreted as 'with arrows' or 'with intent.' It suggests a focused approach to achieving one's objectives. In a modern context, this could relate to a person pursuing a career with determination and clear goals, akin to an archer aiming at a target.
  • āre mat: This phrase translates to 'may you be wise' or 'may you have understanding.' It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in decision-making. For instance, a wise leader considers the long-term effects of their policies on society.
  • karta: This means 'doer' or 'one who acts.' It highlights the significance of action in achieving results. In everyday life, this can be seen in entrepreneurs who take initiative to start businesses and create jobs.
  • rahasūr: This term can be interpreted as 'the hidden' or 'the secret.' It suggests that there are deeper truths or insights that may not be immediately visible. For example, a person may have hidden talents that only emerge when they are encouraged to explore their potential.
  • ivāgaḥ: This word means 'like a river' or 'flowing.' It symbolizes the continuous flow of life and experiences. In real life, this can be compared to the adaptability of individuals who navigate through challenges and changes gracefully.
  • śṛṇvato: This term means 'to those who listen.' It emphasizes the value of listening and being receptive to knowledge. For instance, effective communication in relationships often hinges on the ability to listen actively to one another.
  • vo: This means 'to you' or 'for you.' It indicates a sense of community and shared experience. In a workplace, collaboration and teamwork are essential for achieving common goals.
  • varuṇa mitra: Varuna and Mitra are deities associated with cosmic order and friendship. This highlights the importance of harmony and balance in relationships. In real life, maintaining healthy friendships and partnerships is crucial for personal and professional success.
  • devā: This term refers to the divine or celestial beings. It signifies the higher powers that guide and protect individuals. Many people find comfort in their faith or spirituality, believing that they are supported by a higher force.
  • bhadrasya: This word means 'of auspiciousness' or 'well-being.' It underscores the desire for positive outcomes in life. For example, people often seek blessings for health, prosperity, and happiness in their lives.
  • vidvām̐: This term translates to 'knowledgeable' or 'wise.' It emphasizes the importance of knowledge in navigating life's challenges. Lifelong learning and education are vital for personal growth and adaptability in a changing world.
  • avase huve vaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as 'we invoke you' or 'we call upon you.' It signifies a plea for guidance and support from the divine or from wise individuals in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of dedication, wisdom, and the importance of community and divine guidance in our lives. It serves as a reminder to remain steadfast in our commitments, to seek knowledge, and to act with intention. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, professional endeavors, and nurturing relationships. By embodying the values expressed in this mantra, individuals can navigate life's challenges with grace and purpose, ultimately contributing to their own well-being and that of others around them.
