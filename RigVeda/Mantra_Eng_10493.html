अभिवृत्य सपत्नान अभि या नो अरातयः |अभि पृतन्यन्तं तिष्ठाभि यो न इरस्यति ||
abhivṛtya sapatnān abhi yā no arātayaḥ | abhi pṛtanyantaṁ tiṣṭhābhi yo na irasyati ||
  • abhivṛtya: This term can be understood as "to overcome" or "to rise above." It signifies the act of transcending challenges or adversities.
  • sapatnān: This word refers to "rivals" or "enemies." In a broader sense, it can represent any obstacles or negative forces in life.
  • abhi: This prefix means "towards" or "in the direction of," indicating a proactive approach to overcoming challenges.
  • yā: This translates to "who" or "that," referring to the subject of the mantra.
  • no: This means "us" or "our," indicating a collective aspect, emphasizing community or shared experience.
  • arātayaḥ: This term means "those who oppose" or "adversaries," reinforcing the idea of facing challenges.
  • pṛtanyantaṁ: This can be interpreted as "to stand firm" or "to be steadfast," suggesting resilience in the face of difficulties.
  • tiṣṭhā: This means "to stand" or "to remain," emphasizing the importance of stability and perseverance.
  • abhi: Again, this prefix indicates a forward movement or action towards a goal.
  • yo: This translates to "who," referring to the individual or collective taking action.
  • na: This means "not," indicating a negation or refusal.
  • irasyati: This term can be understood as "to be defeated" or "to fall," suggesting the consequences of not standing firm against challenges.

The mantra essentially conveys a powerful message about overcoming adversities and standing firm against rivals or challenges. It encourages individuals to rise above their obstacles and remain steadfast in their pursuits. The repeated use of "abhi" emphasizes a proactive approach, urging one to take action towards overcoming difficulties.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing personal struggles, such as health issues or emotional turmoil, this mantra serves as a reminder to rise above these challenges and remain resilient.
  • Professional Obstacles: In a competitive work environment, individuals can draw inspiration from this mantra to overcome workplace rivalries and focus on their goals.
  • Social Conflicts: In situations of conflict or opposition within communities, this mantra encourages unity and collective strength to stand firm against adversities.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra emphasizes the significance of resilience, unity, and proactive action in overcoming challenges. It serves as a timeless reminder that with determination and steadfastness, one can rise above adversities and achieve their goals, making it applicable in various aspects of life.
