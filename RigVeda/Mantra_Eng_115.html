घृताहवन दीदिवः प्रति स्म ऋषतो दह |अग्नि त्वं रक्षस्विनः ||
ghṛtāhavana dīdivaḥ prati ṣma riṣato daha | agne tvaṁ rakṣasvinaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ghṛtāhavana dīdivaḥ prati ṣma riṣato daha | agne tvaṁ rakṣasvinaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ghṛtāhavana: Refers to offerings made with ghee (clarified butter), symbolizing purity and nourishment.
    • dīdivaḥ: Means "to the shining one," often referring to the divine light or energy.
    • prati: Translates to "towards" or "in the direction of."
    • ṣma: Indicates "peace" or "calmness."
    • riṣato: Refers to "the one who is angry" or "the fierce one."
    • daha: Means "burn" or "consume," often in the context of fire.
    • agne: Refers to "Agni," the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • tvaṁ: Means "you," addressing Agni directly.
    • rakṣasvinaḥ: Translates to "the protector" or "the one who safeguards."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, emphasizing the importance of fire in rituals and daily life.
    • The offering of ghee signifies the nourishment and purity that one seeks from the divine.
    • The plea for protection from Agni highlights the need for safety and guidance in the face of challenges.
    • In real life, this can be related to seeking clarity and direction in difficult situations, much like how fire provides light in darkness.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra is significant in rituals where fire is central, symbolizing transformation and purification.
    • It teaches the importance of seeking divine guidance and protection in our endeavors.
    • In modern life, it can be applied when facing challenges, reminding us to seek clarity and support from higher powers or our inner strength.
    • For example, during a stressful situation at work, one might reflect on this mantra to find calmness and focus, invoking the protective energy of Agni.
