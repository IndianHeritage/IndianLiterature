वि त्वद आपो न पर्वतस्य पृष्ठाद उक्तेभिर इन्द्रानयन्त यज्ञैः |तं त्वाभिः सुश्रुतिभिर वाजयन्त आजिं न जग्मुर गिर्वाहो अश्वाः ||
vi tvad āpo na parvatasya pṛṣṭhād ukthebhir indrānayanta yajñaiḥ | taṁ tvābhiḥ suṣṭutibhir vājayanta ājiṁ na jagmur girvāho aśvāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vi: through
    • tvad: your
    • āpo: waters
    • na: not
    • parvatasya: of the mountain
    • pṛṣṭhād: from the back
    • ukthebhir: by the utterances
    • indrānayanta: leading Indra
    • yajñaiḥ: with sacrifices
    • taṁ: him
    • tvābhiḥ: with your praises
    • suṣṭutibhir: with good praises
    • vājayanta: bringing forth strength
    • ājiṁ: the one who is invoked
    • na: not
    • jagmur: went
    • girvāho: the singers
    • aśvāḥ: horses
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine connection between the waters and the mountains, emphasizing the importance of nature in spiritual practices.
    • The phrase "leading Indra with sacrifices" suggests that through rituals and offerings, one can invoke divine blessings and strength.
    • The mention of "good praises" indicates the significance of positive affirmations and gratitude in our lives.
    • The reference to "singers" and "horses" symbolizes the collective effort and energy required to achieve spiritual goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to environmental conservation efforts, where the waters (rivers, lakes) and mountains (natural landscapes) are revered and protected.
    • In personal development, the idea of invoking strength through positive affirmations can be seen in practices like meditation and motivational speaking.
    • Community rituals, such as festivals or gatherings, reflect the collective effort to celebrate and honor nature and divinity, similar to the sacrifices mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of nature and spirituality, reminding us of our responsibility to honor and protect the environment.
    • It emphasizes the power of collective effort and positive expression in achieving our goals and invoking divine blessings.
    • In essence, it serves as a guide for living harmoniously with nature while fostering a spirit of gratitude and community.
