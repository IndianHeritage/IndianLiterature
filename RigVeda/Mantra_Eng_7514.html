त्वयेन्द्रो युजा वयं प्रति ब्रुवीमहि स्पृधः |त्वं अस्माकं तव स्मसि ||
tvayed indra yujā vayam prati bruvīmahi spṛdhaḥ | tvam asmākaṁ tava smasi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvayed: by you
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • yujā: with strength
    • vayam: we
    • prati: towards
    • bruvīmahi: we speak
    • spṛdhaḥ: with desire
    • tvam: you
    • asmākaṁ: of us
    • tava: your
    • smasi: we are
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Indra, expressing a collective desire for strength and support. The speakers (we) are addressing Indra, asking for his guidance and assistance in their endeavors, emphasizing a sense of unity and purpose.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Teamwork: In a corporate setting, a team may come together to seek guidance from a leader or mentor, similar to how the mantra seeks strength from Indra. For instance, during a challenging project, team members might gather to discuss their goals and seek collective support.
    • Community Support: In a community event, individuals may come together to invoke the spirit of cooperation and strength, much like the mantra's call for unity and strength from a higher power. For example, during a charity drive, community members might gather to express their shared commitment to helping others.
    • Sports Teams: Before a game, a sports team might huddle together to motivate each other and seek strength from their collective spirit, akin to the invocation of Indra for strength and support.
  • Conclusion and Significance: The mantra signifies the importance of seeking strength and guidance from a higher power or collective spirit. It highlights the value of unity, cooperation, and the desire for support in achieving common goals. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork, community efforts, and personal challenges, reminding individuals of the power of collective strength and the importance of seeking help when needed.
