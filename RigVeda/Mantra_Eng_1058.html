दशेमं तवष्टुर जनयन्त गर्भम् अतन्द्रासो युवतयो विभृत्रम् |तिग्मानीकं स्वयशसं जनेषु विरोचमानम् परि शीं नयन्ति ||
daśemaṁ tvaṣṭur janayanta garbham atandrāso yuvatayo vibhṛtram | tigmānīkaṁ svayaśasaṁ janeṣu virocamānam pari ṣīṁ nayanti ||
  • daśemaṁ: This word can be interpreted as "we may attain" or "we may achieve." It signifies a desire for accomplishment and success.
  • tvaṣṭur: Referring to "the divine craftsman" or "the creator," this term highlights the role of a higher power in the process of creation and manifestation.
  • janayanta: This means "giving birth" or "producing." It emphasizes the act of creation, whether it be in a physical, emotional, or spiritual sense.
  • garbham: Translated as "the womb" or "the embryo," it symbolizes potential and the beginnings of life.
  • atandrāso: This term refers to "those who are vigilant" or "those who are alert." It underscores the importance of awareness and attentiveness in the process of creation and nurturing.
  • yuvatayo: Meaning "young ones" or "youths," this word signifies the energy, vitality, and potential of the younger generation.
  • vibhṛtram: This can be interpreted as "the one who is endowed" or "the one who possesses." It suggests abundance and the richness of life.
  • tigmānīkaṁ: This term means "sharp" or "keen," indicating clarity of thought and purpose.
  • svayaśasaṁ: Translated as "self-glorious" or "renowned," it emphasizes the importance of self-worth and recognition in society.
  • janeṣu: This means "among people" or "in society," highlighting the social aspect of human existence.
  • virocamānam: This term refers to "shining" or "radiant," symbolizing success and the positive impact one can have on others.
  • pariṣīṁ: Meaning "to surround" or "to encompass," it suggests the idea of protection and support within a community.
  • nayanti: This translates to "they lead" or "they guide," indicating the role of leadership and direction in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, vigilance, and the nurturing of potential within individuals and society. It emphasizes the importance of being alert and aware in the process of bringing forth new life and ideas. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to parenting, education, and community building, where the focus is on fostering growth, supporting the younger generation, and leading with purpose. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the collective responsibility to nurture and guide one another towards success and fulfillment.
