यत् पूर्व्यम् मरुतो यच् च नूतनं यद् उद्यते वसवो यच् च शस्यते |विश्वस्य तस्य भवथाऽ नवेदसः शुभं याताम् अनु रथाऽ अवृत्तसत ||
yat pūrvyam maruto yac ca nūtanaṁ yad udyate vasavo yac ca śasyate | viśvasya tasya bhavathā navedasaḥ śubhaṁ yātām anu rathā avṛtsata ||
  • yat pūrvyam maruto: This phrase refers to the ancient forces of nature, particularly the Maruts, who are the storm deities in Vedic literature. They symbolize the power of the wind and storms, which can be seen as both destructive and life-giving.
  • yac ca nūtanaṁ: This means "and that which is new." It signifies the continuous cycle of renewal and change in nature, reflecting the idea that life is always evolving.
  • yad udyate vasavo: The Vasus are another group of deities associated with various natural elements, including earth, water, and fire. This part of the mantra highlights the emergence of these forces, suggesting that they are always present and active in the world around us.
  • yac ca śasyate: This translates to "and that which is cultivated." It refers to the agricultural aspect of life, emphasizing the importance of cultivation and growth in human existence.
  • viśvasya tasya bhavathā navedasaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "may you be the source of all that is auspicious." It calls for blessings and positive outcomes from the divine forces mentioned earlier.
  • śubhaṁ yātām anu rathā avṛtsata: This means "may auspiciousness follow you as you move forward." It suggests that as one progresses in life, they should be accompanied by good fortune and positive energy.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the interconnectedness of ancient natural forces, renewal, cultivation, and the pursuit of auspiciousness in life. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to various aspects such as environmental awareness, the importance of agriculture, and the need for positive intentions in our actions. By recognizing the power of nature and the cycles of life, individuals can strive for harmony and prosperity in their personal and communal lives. The mantra serves as a reminder to respect the forces of nature and to seek blessings for a fruitful and auspicious journey through life.
