कस्त उषः कधप्रिये भुजे मर्तो अमर्त्ये |कं नक्षसे विभावरि ||
kas ta uṣaḥ kadhapriye bhuje marto amartye | kaṁ nakṣase vibhāvari ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • kas: Who
    • ta: then, that
    • uṣaḥ: dawn, light
    • kadhapriye: when dear, beloved
    • bhuje: in the embrace, in the presence
    • marto: mortal, human
    • amartye: immortal, divine
    • kaṁ: whom
    • nakṣase: do you see, observe
    • vibhāvari: shining, illuminating
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra poses a question about the nature of existence and the relationship between the mortal and the immortal.
    • It reflects on the dawn (uṣaḥ) as a metaphor for new beginnings and enlightenment.
    • The phrase "when dear" suggests a moment of connection or intimacy, possibly with the divine or the universe.
    • It questions who is perceived in the light of dawn, emphasizing the importance of awareness and perception in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, the dawn can symbolize new opportunities. For instance, starting a new job or embarking on a new relationship can be seen as a 'dawn' in one's life.
    • The question of who we see in the light of dawn can relate to self-reflection. When we wake up each day, we have the chance to reflect on our goals and aspirations.
    • In a spiritual context, this mantra can encourage individuals to seek a deeper understanding of their existence and their connection to the universe.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of awareness and perception in our lives.
    • It encourages us to embrace new beginnings and to reflect on our existence as both mortal and immortal beings.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can inspire individuals to seek enlightenment and to recognize the beauty in each new day, fostering a sense of connection with the world around them.
