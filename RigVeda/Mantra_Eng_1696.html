यद अश्वाय वास उपस्तृणन्त्य अधीवासं या हिरण्यानि अस्मै |संदानम् अर्वन्तम् पद्बीशम् प्रियā देवेष्व ā यामयन्ति ||
yad aśvāya vāsa upastṛṇanty adhīvāsaṁ yā hiraṇyāny asmai | saṁdānam arvantam paḍbīśam priyā deveṣv ā yāmayanti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • aśvāya: to the horses
    • vāsa: dwelling or abode
    • upastṛṇanty: they spread or lay down
    • adhīvāsaṁ: the dwelling or abode of the divine
    • yā: who
    • hiraṇyāny: golden or precious
    • asmai: to him
    • saṁdānam: the offering or gift
    • arvantam: the one who is worthy
    • paḍbīśam: the one who is dear
    • priyā: beloved
    • deveṣv: among the gods
    • ā: towards
    • yāmayanti: they guide or lead
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the offerings made to the divine, symbolized by horses, which are often associated with strength and vitality in Vedic literature.
    • The act of spreading or laying down signifies preparation and reverence, indicating that one should prepare a sacred space for the divine presence.
    • The mention of "golden" signifies the value and purity of the offerings, suggesting that what we offer to the divine should be of the highest quality.
    • The phrase "the one who is worthy" emphasizes the importance of intention behind offerings, reminding us that sincerity and devotion are crucial in spiritual practices.
    • The concluding part of the mantra highlights the guidance provided by the divine, suggesting that through sincere offerings, one can receive divine blessings and direction in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be reflected in the way we prepare for important events, such as weddings or festivals, where we create a sacred atmosphere to invite blessings.
    • When we engage in acts of kindness or charity, we are metaphorically offering "golden" gifts to those in need, which can be seen as a form of spiritual offering.
    • In personal development, the idea of preparing a "sacred space" can translate to creating a conducive environment for meditation or reflection, allowing us to connect with our inner selves and the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of intention, preparation, and the quality of our offerings in both spiritual and everyday life.
    • It encourages us to recognize the divine in our actions and to seek guidance through sincere devotion and offerings.
    • Ultimately, it teaches that by honoring the divine and preparing ourselves with purity and sincerity, we can receive blessings and direction in our lives.
