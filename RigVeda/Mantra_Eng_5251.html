मन्त्र: नीत्वा नक्ष्य विश्वते द्युमन्तं देव धीमहि | सुवीरम् अग्न आहुत ||
ni tvā nakṣya viśpate dyumantaṁ deva dhīmahi | suvīram agna āhuta ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ni tvā: We meditate upon you
    • nakṣya: who is the protector
    • viśpate: O Lord of all beings
    • dyumantaṁ: who is radiant
    • deva: O divine one
    • dhīmahi: we contemplate
    • suvīram: the one who is powerful
    • agna: O Agni (Fire)
    • āhuta: who is invoked
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is considered the divine messenger and the one who connects humans with the divine. The mantra expresses a deep reverence and a desire to meditate upon Agni, who is seen as a protector and a source of strength and radiance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Spiritual Practices: In many cultures, fire is a symbol of purification and transformation. For instance, during rituals like Homa or Yajna, offerings are made to Agni, invoking his presence and blessings. This practice can be seen as a way to seek guidance and protection in daily life.
    • Personal Growth: The mantra encourages individuals to contemplate their inner strength and radiance. In real life, this can translate to self-reflection and personal development, where one seeks to harness their inner power to overcome challenges.
    • Community and Leadership: The invocation of Agni as the protector of all beings can be related to leadership roles in communities. Leaders are often seen as guiding lights, much like Agni, who brings people together and fosters unity and strength.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for meditation on the divine qualities of Agni, emphasizing protection, strength, and radiance. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to seek inner strength, engage in spiritual practices, and foster community leadership. By invoking these qualities, individuals can navigate life's challenges with resilience and purpose.
