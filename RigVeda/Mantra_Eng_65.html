इन्द्रं वयम् महाधन इन्द्रं अर्बhe हवामहे |युजं वृत्रेषु वज्रीणम् ||
indraṁ vayam mahādhana indram arbhe havāmahe | yujaṁ vṛtreṣu vajriṇam ||
  • Indraṁ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Vayam: Means "we" or "us," indicating a collective effort or intention.
  • Mahādhana: Translates to "great wealth" or "abundance," suggesting prosperity and richness in various forms.
  • Indram: Again refers to Indra, emphasizing the importance of invoking his presence and blessings.
  • Arbhe: Means "we invoke" or "we call upon," indicating a plea or request for divine assistance.
  • Havāmahe: Translates to "we offer" or "we worship," signifying an act of devotion and reverence.
  • Yujaṁ: Means "to unite" or "to join," suggesting a coming together for a common purpose.
  • Vṛtreṣu: Refers to "the obstacles" or "the enemies," symbolizing challenges or adversities in life.
  • Vajriṇam: Means "the one who wields the thunderbolt," a reference to Indra's power to overcome challenges and enemies.

This mantra can be understood as a collective invocation to Indra, seeking his blessings for strength, prosperity, and the ability to overcome obstacles. It emphasizes the importance of unity and collective effort in facing challenges, much like a team working together to achieve a common goal.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Teamwork in Organizations: Just as a team invokes the spirit of collaboration to achieve success, this mantra reminds us of the power of unity in overcoming challenges in the workplace.
  • Community Support: In times of crisis, communities come together to support one another, invoking the strength of collective action to overcome adversities.
  • Personal Challenges: Individuals facing personal struggles can draw inspiration from this mantra, reminding them to seek strength and support from their community or loved ones.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and the pursuit of prosperity. It serves as a reminder that by coming together and invoking the divine, we can overcome obstacles and achieve our goals. Whether in personal life, community efforts, or organizational settings, the principles encapsulated in this mantra are applicable and relevant, encouraging us to seek strength in unity and divine guidance in our endeavors.
