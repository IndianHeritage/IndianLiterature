प्र वः शर्धाय घृष्वये त्वेषद्युम्नाय शुष्मिणे |देवत्तम ब्रह्म गायत ||
pra vaḥ śardhāya ghṛṣvaye tveṣadyumnāya śuṣmiṇe | devattam brahma gāyata ||
  • pra vaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "let us speak" or "let us express." It signifies the importance of communication and sharing thoughts.
  • śardhāya: This word refers to "with faith" or "with devotion." It emphasizes the necessity of sincerity and belief in our expressions and actions.
  • ghṛṣvaye: This term means "to the one who is worthy" or "to the deserving." It suggests that our words and actions should be directed towards those who are deserving of respect and honor.
  • tveṣadyumnāya: This can be translated as "to the one who is victorious" or "to the one who has achieved." It highlights the importance of recognizing and honoring those who have succeeded through their efforts.
  • śuṣmiṇe: This word means "to the one who is radiant" or "to the one who shines." It signifies the qualities of brilliance and excellence in a person.
  • devattam: This term translates to "the highest deity" or "the supreme being." It indicates a connection to the divine and the ultimate source of wisdom and guidance.
  • brahma: This word refers to "the ultimate reality" or "the cosmic spirit." It represents the essence of existence and the universal truth.
  • gāyata: This means "to sing" or "to chant." It emphasizes the act of expressing reverence and devotion through vocalization.

In summary, this mantra encourages us to communicate with faith and devotion, directing our words towards those who are deserving and recognizing the achievements of others. It calls upon the highest divine presence and the ultimate reality, urging us to express our reverence through song or chant.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as in educational settings where teachers encourage students to express their thoughts and ideas with sincerity and respect.
  • In workplaces, it can serve as a reminder to acknowledge the contributions of colleagues and celebrate their successes, fostering a positive and supportive environment.
  • In personal relationships, it emphasizes the importance of open communication, faith, and respect, which are essential for building strong connections.
  • During spiritual practices, chanting this mantra can help individuals connect with the divine and reflect on their own actions and intentions.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a guide for living a life of integrity, respect, and devotion, encouraging us to uplift ourselves and those around us through our words and actions.
