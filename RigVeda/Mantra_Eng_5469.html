शं नो द्यावापृथिवी पूर्वहूतौ शम् अन्तरिक्षं दृष्टये नो अस्तु |शं न ओषधीर वनिनो भवन्तु शं नो रजसस् पतिर अस्तु जित्ष्णुः ||
śaṁ no dyāvāpṛthivī pūrvahūtau śam antarikṣaṁ dṛśaye no astu | śaṁ na oṣadhīr vanino bhavantu śaṁ no rajasas patir astu jiṣṇuḥ ||
  • śaṁ no dyāvāpṛthivī: This phrase translates to "May there be peace between the heavens and the earth." It emphasizes the importance of harmony in the universe. In real life, this can be seen in environmental conservation efforts where humans strive to maintain a balance with nature.
  • pūrvahūtau: This means "in the past." It suggests that the peace we seek is not just a present desire but a continuation of a historical legacy. For example, indigenous cultures often emphasize the wisdom of their ancestors in maintaining ecological balance.
  • śam antarikṣaṁ dṛśaye no astu: This translates to "May peace be seen in the atmosphere." It highlights the need for tranquility in the space around us. In modern contexts, this can relate to mental health awareness, where creating a peaceful environment is crucial for well-being.
  • śaṁ na oṣadhīr vanino bhavantu: This means "May the plants and the forests be peaceful." It underscores the significance of nature in our lives. For instance, urban gardening and reforestation projects aim to restore peace and balance in our ecosystems.
  • śaṁ no rajasas patir astu jiṣṇuḥ: This translates to "May the lord of the elements be victorious." It signifies the importance of overcoming chaos and striving for order. In practical terms, this can be seen in community efforts to resolve conflicts and promote social justice.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound desire for peace and harmony across various realms—between the heavens and the earth, in our surroundings, and within ourselves. Its significance lies in the universal aspiration for balance and tranquility, which is applicable in numerous real-life scenarios, from environmental conservation to mental health initiatives and social justice movements. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and communities can work towards a more harmonious existence, fostering peace in every aspect of life.
