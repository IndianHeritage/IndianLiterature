यस्मिन वृक्षे मध्वदः सुपर्णा निविशन्ते सुवते चाधि विश्वे |तस्येद आहुः पिप्पलं स्वाद्व अग्रे तन् नोन नशद यः पितरं न वेद ||
yasmin vṛkṣe madhvadaḥ suparṇā niviśante suvate cādhi viśve | tasyed āhuḥ pippalaṁ svādv agre tan non naśad yaḥ pitaraṁ na veda ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasmin: in which
    • vṛkṣe: tree
    • madhvadaḥ: sweet fruits
    • suparṇā: well-winged (often referring to birds)
    • niviśante: dwell or reside
    • suvate: they who are well nourished
    • cādhi: and above
    • viśve: all
    • tasyed: of that
    • āhuḥ: they say
    • pippalaṁ: pippala (a type of fruit)
    • svādv: sweet
    • agre: in the front or foremost
    • tan: that
    • non: not
    • naśad: perish
    • yaḥ: who
    • pitaraṁ: father
    • na: not
    • veda: know
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a tree that bears sweet fruits and is a dwelling place for well-winged birds.
    • The tree symbolizes nourishment and sustenance, while the birds represent freedom and joy.
    • The mention of "pippala" indicates a specific type of fruit that is valued for its sweetness and health benefits.
    • The phrase "who does not know the father" suggests a deeper philosophical meaning about the importance of knowledge and connection to one's roots.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, a tree can represent the support system that nourishes its members, much like how families provide emotional and physical sustenance.
    • Birds residing in the tree can symbolize the aspirations and dreams of individuals who thrive in a nurturing environment.
    • The pippala fruit can be likened to the rewards of hard work and dedication, which are sweet and fulfilling when one is connected to their roots.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of nurturing relationships and the importance of knowledge and connection to one's heritage.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek nourishment from their community and to appreciate the sweetness of life that comes from understanding one's origins.
    • It serves as a reminder that those who are disconnected from their roots may miss out on the richness of life and the wisdom passed down through generations.
