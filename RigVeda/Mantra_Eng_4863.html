तं व इन्द्रं न सुक्रतं वरुणं इव मायिनं |आर्यमणं न मन्द्रं सृप्रभोजसं विष्णुं न तुषा आदिशे ||
taṁ va indraṁ na sukratuṁ varuṇam iva māyinam | aryamaṇaṁ na mandraṁ sṛprabhojasaṁ viṣṇuṁ na stuṣa ādiśe ||
  • taṁ va indraṁ: This phrase refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength and power. In real life, this can be likened to leaders or influential figures who guide and protect their communities.
  • na sukratuṁ: This means "not the one with good intentions." It suggests a caution against those who may appear benevolent but have ulterior motives. In everyday life, this can be seen in situations where individuals or organizations may present themselves as helpful but are driven by self-interest.
  • varuṇam iva māyinam: Varuna is another deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority. The term "māyinam" implies illusion or deception. This can be interpreted as a reminder to be wary of those who may manipulate situations for their gain, similar to how some leaders may use propaganda to sway public opinion.
  • aryamaṇaṁ: This refers to a noble or virtuous being. In real life, this can represent individuals who act with integrity and uphold ethical standards, serving as role models in society.
  • na mandraṁ: This translates to "not the one who is weak or ineffective." It emphasizes the importance of strength and capability in leadership. In practical terms, this can be seen in the need for strong leaders in various sectors, such as politics or business, who can make decisive actions during crises.
  • sṛprabhojasaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "those who are nourished by the divine." It suggests the importance of spiritual or moral nourishment in one's life. In real life, this can be related to individuals who seek wisdom and guidance from spiritual practices or philosophies.
  • viṣṇuṁ na stuṣa ādiśe: Vishnu represents preservation and protection. The phrase implies that one should not praise or seek guidance from those who do not embody these qualities. In a contemporary context, this can be applied to the idea of supporting leaders or mentors who genuinely care for the welfare of others.

Conclusion: This mantra serves as a profound reminder to discern the true nature of leaders and influences in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of integrity, strength, and genuine intentions. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek out and support those who embody these qualities, whether in personal relationships, professional environments, or community leadership. By doing so, we contribute to a more ethical and harmonious society.
