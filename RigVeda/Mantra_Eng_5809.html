दाशराज्ञे पर्यत्ताय विश्वतः सुदास इन्द्रावरुणाव अशिक्षतम् |श्वित्यञ्चो यत्र नमसā कपर्दिनो धियā धīvन्तो असपन्त तृत्सवः ||
dāśarājñe pariyattāya viśvataḥ sudāsa indrāvaruṇāv aśikṣatam | śvityañco yatra namasā kapardino dhiyā dhīvanto asapanta tṛtsavaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "dāśarājñe pariyattāya viśvataḥ sudāsa indrāvaruṇāv aśikṣatam | śvityañco yatra namasā kapardino dhiyā dhīvanto asapanta tṛtsavaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dāśarājñe: Referring to the king of the Dasharajas, a term often associated with a ruler or leader.
    • pariyattāya: To be bound or constrained, indicating a sense of duty or responsibility.
    • viśvataḥ: From all directions or universally, suggesting inclusiveness.
    • sudāsa: A name that can refer to a wise or learned individual, often associated with prosperity.
    • indrāvaruṇāv: Referring to the divine forces of Indra and Varuna, symbolizing power and cosmic order.
    • aśikṣatam: To have taught or instructed, indicating the importance of knowledge and guidance.
    • śvityañco: A term that can refer to the essence or core of something, often linked to purity.
    • yatra: Where or in which, indicating a location or state of being.
    • namasā: With reverence or respect, highlighting the importance of humility.
    • kapardino: Referring to one who is wise or knowledgeable, often associated with a sage.
    • dhiyā: With intellect or understanding, emphasizing the role of wisdom.
    • dhīvanto: Those who are wise or thoughtful, indicating a community of learned individuals.
    • asapanta: To move forward or progress, suggesting action and growth.
    • tṛtsavaḥ: Those who are thirsty or eager, often symbolizing a desire for knowledge or fulfillment.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of leadership, knowledge, and the guidance of divine forces in achieving prosperity and harmony.
    • It highlights the role of wise individuals in society who, through their intellect and understanding, can lead others towards growth and fulfillment.
    • The invocation of Indra and Varuna signifies the need for balance and order in both personal and communal life.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied to the importance of ethical leadership and the need for leaders to guide their teams with wisdom and integrity.
    • In educational institutions, it underscores the role of teachers and mentors in shaping the minds of students, fostering a thirst for knowledge and understanding.
    • In community building, it serves as a reminder of the collective responsibility to support one another in the pursuit of growth and harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of leadership, knowledge, and divine guidance in our lives.
    • By embodying the principles of wisdom, respect, and collective progress, individuals and communities can strive towards a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
