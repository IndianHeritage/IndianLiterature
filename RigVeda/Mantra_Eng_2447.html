अक्रो न बभ्रिः समिथे महीनां दिदृक्षेयः सूनवे भार्जीकः |उद उष्रियाऽ जनिता यो जजानापां गर्भो नृतमो यह्वो अग्निः ||
akro na babhriḥ samithe mahīnāṁ didṛkṣeyaḥ sūnave bhāṛjīkaḥ | ud usriyā janitā yo jajānāpāṁ garbho nṛtamo yahvo agniḥ ||
  • Mantra: "akro na babhriḥ samithe mahīnāṁ didṛkṣeyaḥ sūnave bhāṛjīkaḥ | ud usriyā janitā yo jajānāpāṁ garbho nṛtamo yahvo agniḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • akro: not crying or lamenting
    • na: not
    • babhriḥ: to bear or carry
    • samithe: in the assembly or gathering
    • mahīnāṁ: of the great ones or the mighty
    • didṛkṣeyaḥ: may I see or perceive
    • sūnave: to the son or offspring
    • bhāṛjīkaḥ: the one who is strong or powerful
    • ud: up or forth
    • usriyā: by the one who is born
    • janitā: the creator or giver of birth
    • yo: who
    • jajānāpāṁ: has given birth to the beings
    • garbho: the womb or the embryo
    • nṛtamo: the best among men
    • yahvo: who
    • agniḥ: fire
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the strength and resilience of individuals, particularly in the context of community and creation.
    • The phrase "not crying or lamenting" suggests a focus on positivity and strength in the face of challenges.
    • The reference to "the great ones" indicates a respect for those who have achieved greatness and the desire to learn from them.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of offspring and the legacy one leaves behind, highlighting the role of parents in nurturing future generations.
    • The mention of "fire" symbolizes transformation, energy, and the divine spark within individuals that drives them to achieve their goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who embody the qualities of strength and resilience inspire their teams to overcome obstacles and strive for success.
    • Parents who nurture their children with love and guidance contribute to the development of strong, capable individuals who can make a positive impact on society.
    • In community gatherings, individuals who share their experiences and wisdom can uplift others, fostering a sense of unity and strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of strength, resilience, and community in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to focus on positivity and to learn from the great ones around them.
    • By nurturing future generations and embracing the transformative power of fire, we can create a legacy of strength and wisdom that benefits society as a whole.
