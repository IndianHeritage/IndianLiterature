इयं इन्द्रं वरुणं अष्ट मे गीः प्रावत तोके तनये तूतुजानाः |सुरत्नासो देववीतिं गमेमा यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
iyam indraṁ varuṇam aṣṭa me gīḥ prāvat toke tanaye tūtujānā | suratnāso devavītiṁ gamema yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • iyam indraṁ varuṇam: This phrase refers to the invocation of two significant deities in Vedic tradition, Indra and Varuna. Indra is often associated with rain, thunder, and war, while Varuna represents the cosmic order and moral authority.
  • aṣṭa me gīḥ: This translates to "eight hymns for me." The number eight can symbolize completeness and balance, suggesting a holistic approach to life and spirituality.
  • prāvat toke tanaye tūtujānā: This part can be interpreted as a request for blessings and protection for one's offspring or descendants. It emphasizes the importance of family and the continuity of life.
  • suratnāso devavītiṁ gamema: This phrase expresses a desire to attain divine knowledge or wisdom. It highlights the pursuit of spiritual growth and understanding.
  • yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: This is a plea for the deities to protect and bless the speaker and their community with well-being and prosperity. The term "svasti" signifies auspiciousness and good fortune.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for divine guidance, protection, and the pursuit of wisdom. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as family rituals, community gatherings, or personal prayers where individuals seek blessings for their loved ones and strive for a harmonious life. The invocation of Indra and Varuna serves as a reminder of the balance between material and spiritual pursuits, urging individuals to seek completeness in their lives while fostering a sense of responsibility towards their family and society. The mantra's significance lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of personal well-being, familial bonds, and spiritual growth.
