परी हि ष्मा पुरुहूतो जनानां विश्वासरद भोजना पूयमानः |अथा भर श्येनभृत प्रयांसी रयिं तुञ्जानो अभि वाजम् अर्ष ||
pari hi ṣmā puruhūto janānāṁ viśvāsarad bhojanā pūyamānaḥ | athā bhara śyenabhṛta prayāṁsi rayiṁ tuñjāno abhi vājam arṣa ||
  • Mantra: "pari hi ṣmā puruhūto janānāṁ viśvāsarad bhojanā pūyamānaḥ | athā bhara śyenabhṛta prayāṁsi rayiṁ tuñjāno abhi vājam arṣa ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pari hi: indeed, all around
    • ṣmā: protection, safety
    • puruhūto: invoked by many, widely worshipped
    • janānāṁ: of the people
    • viśvāsarad: trustworthy, reliable
    • bhojanā: nourishment, food
    • pūyamānaḥ: being filled, satisfied
    • athā: therefore, thus
    • bhara: bring, provide
    • śyenabhṛta: like the eagle, powerful
    • prayāṁsi: offerings, sacrifices
    • rayiṁ: wealth, prosperity
    • tuñjāno: bestowing, granting
    • abhi: towards, in favor of
    • vājam: strength, vigor
    • arṣa: divine, sacred
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of community and collective worship. The phrase "puruhūto janānāṁ" suggests that the divine is invoked by many, highlighting the power of unity in spiritual practices.
    • The term "viśvāsarad bhojanā pūyamānaḥ" indicates that when people come together in trust and faith, they are nourished both physically and spiritually. For example, community meals or potlucks can serve as a real-life representation of this idea, where sharing food fosters bonds and trust among individuals.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "athā bhara śyenabhṛta prayāṁsi rayiṁ tuñjāno," calls for the divine to provide strength and prosperity, akin to the powerful eagle. This can be seen in modern contexts where individuals or communities seek blessings for success in endeavors, such as starting a new business or undertaking a significant project.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of collective faith and the nourishment that comes from unity. It teaches that when people come together with trust, they can achieve greater strength and prosperity.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various communal activities, such as festivals, group prayers, or community service, where the collective effort leads to shared benefits and a sense of belonging.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the power of community and the divine blessings that come from collective trust and action.
