अन्तरिक्षेण पतति विश्वा रूपावचाकशत् |मुनिर देवस्य-देवस्य सौकृत्याय सखा हितः ||
antarikṣeṇa patati viśvā rūpāvacākaśat | munir devasya-devasya saukṛtyāya sakhā hitaḥ ||
  • antarikṣeṇa: This word refers to the "interspace" or "sky," symbolizing the vastness of the universe and the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • patati: Meaning "to fly" or "to move," this term suggests a sense of freedom and transcendence, indicating the ability to rise above worldly limitations.
  • viśvā: This translates to "all" or "universal," emphasizing inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings and elements in the cosmos.
  • rūpāvacākaśat: This compound word can be broken down into "rūpa" (form) and "vacāka" (that which indicates). It suggests the myriad forms and manifestations of existence, highlighting the diversity of life.
  • munir: Referring to a "sage" or "wise person," this term signifies the importance of wisdom and introspection in understanding the universe.
  • devasya-devasya: This phrase means "of the divine," indicating a connection to higher powers or deities, and the spiritual aspect of existence.
  • saukṛtyāya: This word means "for good deeds" or "for virtuous actions," emphasizing the significance of righteousness and ethical conduct in life.
  • sakhā: Meaning "friend," this term highlights the importance of companionship and support in the journey of life.
  • hitaḥ: This translates to "beneficial" or "auspicious," suggesting that the actions and relationships we cultivate should lead to positive outcomes.

The mantra encapsulates a profound understanding of the universe and our place within it. It speaks to the idea that through wisdom and virtuous actions, we can transcend our limitations and connect with the divine. The imagery of flying in the vast sky symbolizes liberation and the pursuit of higher knowledge.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek wisdom and engage in good deeds, fostering a sense of purpose and fulfillment.
  • In relationships, it highlights the importance of friendship and support, reminding us that our connections with others can lead to mutual growth and well-being.
  • In a broader societal context, it calls for collective efforts towards virtuous actions that benefit the community and the environment, promoting harmony and sustainability.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of wisdom, virtue, and friendship in navigating the complexities of life. By embodying these principles, we can aspire to create a more harmonious and meaningful existence.
