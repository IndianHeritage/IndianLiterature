प्र त आष्विनीयः पवमान धीजुवो दिव्याः असृग्न पयसा धरिमणि | प्रान्तर ऋषयः स्थाविरीर असृक्षत ये त्वां मृजन्त्य ऋषिषाण वेधसः ||
pra ta āśvinīḥ pavamāna dhījuvo divyā asṛgran payasā dharīmaṇi | prāntar ṛṣayaḥ sthāvirīr asṛkṣata ye tvā mṛjanty ṛṣiṣāṇa vedhasaḥ ||
  • pra ta āśvinīḥ: This phrase refers to the twin deities, the Ashvins, who are considered the divine physicians in Vedic literature. They symbolize health, vitality, and the duality of nature.
  • pavamāna: This term means "the purifying one" or "the one who brings purity." It signifies the importance of cleanliness and purity in both physical and spiritual contexts.
  • dhījuvo: This word can be interpreted as "the one who brings wisdom" or "the one who enlightens." It emphasizes the pursuit of knowledge and understanding.
  • divyā asṛgran: This phrase translates to "the divine nectar" or "the celestial essence." It represents the idea of receiving blessings and nourishment from the divine.
  • payasā: This means "with milk" or "nourishment." In many cultures, milk symbolizes sustenance and nurturing, reflecting the importance of care and support in life.
  • dharīmaṇi: This term refers to "the one who holds" or "the one who supports." It signifies stability and strength, essential qualities for both individuals and communities.
  • prāntar ṛṣayaḥ: This phrase means "the seers from afar" or "the wise ones." It highlights the value of wisdom and insight gained through experience and observation.
  • sthāvirīr: This word translates to "the ancient ones" or "the elders." It emphasizes the respect for tradition and the knowledge passed down through generations.
  • asṛkṣata: This term means "they observed" or "they watched." It signifies the importance of awareness and mindfulness in our actions and decisions.
  • ye tvā mṛjanty: This phrase translates to "those who seek you" or "those who worship you." It reflects the idea of devotion and the pursuit of a higher purpose.
  • ṛṣiṣāṇa vedhasaḥ: This means "the sages who possess knowledge." It emphasizes the significance of learning and the quest for truth in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking wisdom, purity, and divine nourishment in our lives. It highlights the importance of health, knowledge, and the support of the community. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in health practices, educational pursuits, and community building. By embodying the principles of the Ashvins and striving for purity and wisdom, individuals can lead more fulfilling and meaningful lives. The mantra serves as a reminder to honor tradition, seek knowledge, and support one another in our journeys.
