वाश्रेवा विद्युः मिमाति वत्सं न माताऽसिषक्ति। यद एषां वृष्टिः असर्जि॥
vāśreva vidyun mimāti vatsaṁ na mātā siṣakti | yad eṣāṁ vṛṣṭir asarji ||
  • vāśreva: This term can be interpreted as "like a powerful force" or "with great strength." It signifies the potency and influence of a divine or natural force.
  • vidyun: This word translates to "lightning" or "electricity," symbolizing sudden illumination, energy, and the power of nature.
  • mimāti: This means "to move" or "to flow," indicating a dynamic process or action.
  • vatsaṁ: This term refers to "calf" or "young one," often symbolizing innocence, growth, and nurturing.
  • na: This is a negation, meaning "not" or "no."
  • mātā: This translates to "mother," representing care, protection, and nurturing qualities.
  • siṣakti: This means "to have power" or "to be capable," indicating the ability to act or influence.
  • yad: This word means "that" or "which," referring to something specific.
  • eṣāṁ: This translates to "of these" or "of those," indicating a connection to a particular group or context.
  • vṛṣṭir: This term means "rain" or "shower," symbolizing nourishment, fertility, and abundance.
  • asarji: This means "to fall" or "to descend," often used in the context of rain or blessings coming down from the heavens.

The mantra can be interpreted as follows: "Like a powerful force, lightning moves; the calf does not have the power of the mother; that which is the rain of these does not fall." This suggests a relationship between strength, nurturing, and the natural cycles of life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied to various scenarios:

  • Parent-Child Relationships: The reference to the calf and mother highlights the nurturing role of a parent. Just as a mother provides for her young, individuals in society must support and guide the younger generation.
  • Natural Forces: The imagery of lightning and rain can be seen in the context of environmental awareness. Understanding the power of nature and its cycles can lead to better stewardship of the earth.
  • Personal Growth: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength and nurturing in personal development. Just as a calf relies on its mother for sustenance, individuals must seek guidance and support in their journeys.

Conclusion: This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of strength, nurturing, and the natural world. It serves as a reminder of the importance of support systems in our lives, whether in familial relationships, environmental stewardship, or personal growth. By recognizing these connections, we can foster a more harmonious existence with ourselves and the world around us.
