आ वो राजानम अध्वरस्य रुद्रं होतारं सत्ययजं रोडस्योः |अग्निम पुरा तनयित्नोर अचित्ताद धिरण्यरूपम अवसे कृणुध्वम् ||
ā vo rājānam adhvarasya rudraṁ hotāraṁ satyayajaṁ rodasyoḥ | agnim purā tanayitnor acittād dhiraṇyarūpam avase kṛṇudhvam ||
  • Mantra: "ā vo rājānam adhvarasya rudraṁ hotāraṁ satyayajaṁ rodasyoḥ | agnim purā tanayitnor acittād dhiraṇyarūpam avase kṛṇudhvam ||"
  • Translation: "O you who are the king of the sacrifice, Rudra, the hotar (priest) who is truthful, we invoke you from the heavens. May the fire, the son of the waters, bring forth the golden form of the divine."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ā vo: "to you" - a call or invocation directed towards a deity.
    • rājānam: "king" - signifies authority and leadership, often associated with divine power.
    • adhvarasya: "of the sacrifice" - refers to the ritualistic offerings made to the deities.
    • rudraṁ: "Rudra" - a form of Shiva, representing destruction and transformation.
    • hotāraṁ: "the priest" - the one who performs the rituals and invokes the divine.
    • satyayajaṁ: "truthful" - emphasizes the importance of truth in rituals and life.
    • rodasyoḥ: "from the heavens" - indicates the divine origin of the invocation.
    • agnim: "fire" - symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
    • purā: "from the waters" - signifies the source of life and creation.
    • tanayitnor: "son of the waters" - refers to the connection between fire and water, representing duality in creation.
    • acittād: "from the unthinking" - suggests invoking the divine from a state of ignorance or unawareness.
    • dhiraṇyarūpam: "golden form" - symbolizes prosperity, wealth, and divine blessings.
    • avase kṛṇudhvam: "may you bring forth" - a plea for the manifestation of divine qualities and blessings.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, this mantra can be recited during significant life events such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies, invoking divine blessings for prosperity and truth.
    • In leadership contexts, the invocation of "rājānam" can be seen as a reminder for leaders to embody truth and integrity in their governance.
    • The symbolism of fire and water can be reflected in environmental practices, emphasizing the balance between nature and human activities.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of truth, divine invocation, and the interconnectedness of elements in life.
    • Its significance lies in reminding individuals and communities to seek divine guidance and embody truthfulness in their actions.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in personal growth, leadership, and environmental stewardship, fostering a holistic approach to life and spirituality.
