स इम महीं धुनिम एतोर अरम्णात सो अस्नातृन अपारयत स्वस्ति |त उष्णाय रयिम अभि प्र तस्थुः सोमस्य ता मद इन्द्रश चकार ||
sa īm mahīṁ dhunim etor aramṇāt so asnātṝn apārayat svasti | ta utsnāya rayim abhi pra tasthuḥ somasya tā mada indraś cakāra ||
  • sa īm mahīṁ dhunim etor aramṇāt: This phrase can be translated as "He who moves the great earth with his power." Here, "sa" refers to a divine entity, possibly Indra, who is often associated with strength and power. The term "mahīṁ" signifies the earth, while "dhunim" implies movement or vibration, indicating the dynamic nature of the universe.
  • so asnātṝn apārayat svasti: This part translates to "He nourished the beings with abundance." The word "asnātṝn" refers to living beings, and "apārayat" means to provide or nourish. "Svasti" signifies well-being or prosperity, suggesting that the divine power not only sustains life but also ensures its flourishing.
  • ta utsnāya rayim abhi pra tasthuḥ: This can be interpreted as "They, through their warmth, established wealth." "Utsnāya" refers to warmth or energy, while "rayim" means wealth or resources. This line emphasizes the importance of energy and warmth in creating and sustaining prosperity.
  • somasya tā mada indraś cakāra: This translates to "Indra created that intoxication of the soma." "Soma" is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing divine inspiration and ecstasy. "Mada" refers to intoxication or bliss, indicating that the divine presence brings joy and fulfillment to life.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine power and its role in sustaining life and prosperity. It highlights the interconnectedness of the earth, living beings, and the divine, suggesting that true wealth comes from a harmonious relationship with nature and the divine.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can inspire individuals to recognize the importance of environmental stewardship and sustainable living. By understanding that our well-being is tied to the health of the earth, we can make conscious choices that promote ecological balance. For example, engaging in practices like planting trees, conserving water, and supporting local agriculture can be seen as modern expressions of this ancient wisdom.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the divine forces that nurture and sustain life. It encourages us to seek abundance not just for ourselves but for all living beings, fostering a sense of community and responsibility. By aligning our actions with the principles of harmony and sustainability, we can create a prosperous future that honors both the earth and the divine.
