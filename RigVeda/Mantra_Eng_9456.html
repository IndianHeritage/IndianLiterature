अरिष्टः स मार्तो विश्व एधते प्र प्रजाभिर जायते धर्मणस परी |यम् आदित्यासो नयथा सुनीतिभिर अति विश्वानि दुरिता स्वस्तये ||
ariṣṭaḥ sa marto viśva edhate pra prajābhir jāyate dharmaṇas pari | yam ādityāso nayathā sunītibhir ati viśvāni duritā svastaye ||
  • ariṣṭaḥ: This term refers to something that is free from obstacles or afflictions. In a real-life context, it can be related to a state of well-being or health, where an individual is not hindered by any negative influences.
  • sa marto: This phrase translates to "that mortal" or "the human being." It emphasizes the human condition and the shared experiences of mortality.
  • viśva edhate: This means "the whole universe thrives" or "all beings flourish." It signifies the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of harmony in the universe.
  • pra prajābhir: This translates to "with progeny" or "through offspring." It highlights the significance of family and the continuation of life through generations.
  • jāyate: This means "is born" or "comes into being." It reflects the cycle of life and the process of creation.
  • dharmaṇas: This term refers to "righteousness" or "duty." It underscores the importance of living a life aligned with moral and ethical principles.
  • pari: This means "around" or "in all directions." It suggests a holistic approach to life and the need for balance.
  • yam ādityāso: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which the sun (Āditya) supports." It symbolizes the life-giving energy of the sun and its role in sustaining life.
  • nayathā: This means "as directed" or "as guided." It implies the importance of following a path or guidance in life.
  • sunītibhir: This translates to "with good conduct" or "by following good principles." It emphasizes the value of ethical behavior and integrity.
  • ati viśvāni: This means "all beings" or "the entirety of existence." It reflects the universality of the message and its applicability to all life forms.
  • duritā: This term refers to "obstacles" or "difficulties." It acknowledges the challenges that individuals face in life.
  • svastaye: This translates to "for well-being" or "for prosperity." It signifies the ultimate goal of achieving a state of health and happiness.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of righteousness, interconnectedness, and the pursuit of well-being. It serves as a reminder that by living ethically and harmoniously, individuals can contribute to the flourishing of the universe. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as family life, community interactions, and personal development. By adhering to the principles of dharma and fostering good relationships, one can navigate the challenges of life and promote a sense of peace and prosperity for oneself and others.
