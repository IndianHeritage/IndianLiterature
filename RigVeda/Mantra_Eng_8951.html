पुषेमाऽआशा अनु वेद सर्वाः सोऽस्मां अभयतमेन नेषत् |स्वस्तिदा आगृह्णिः सर्ववीरोऽप्रयुच्छन् पुर एतु प्रजानन् ||
pūṣemā āśā anu veda sarvāḥ so asmām̐ abhayatamena neṣat | svastidā āghṛṇiḥ sarvavīro 'prayucchan pura etu prajānan ||
  • pūṣemā: This term refers to the deity Pūṣa, who is associated with nourishment and prosperity. In real life, this can be likened to the importance of sustenance in our daily lives, whether it be through food, knowledge, or emotional support.
  • āśā anu veda: This phrase translates to "may hope and knowledge guide us." It emphasizes the significance of hope and wisdom in navigating life's challenges. For example, in times of uncertainty, having hope can motivate individuals to pursue their goals and dreams.
  • sarvāḥ: Meaning "all" or "everything," this word signifies inclusivity. It reminds us that we should consider the well-being of all beings in our actions, promoting a sense of community and interconnectedness.
  • so asmām̐ abhayatamena neṣat: This can be interpreted as "may he lead us to safety and security." In practical terms, this reflects the human desire for safety in various aspects of life, such as physical security, emotional stability, and financial security.
  • svastidā: This word means "bestower of well-being." It highlights the importance of health and wellness in our lives. For instance, engaging in regular exercise and maintaining a balanced diet are ways to promote personal well-being.
  • āghṛṇiḥ: This term refers to the "one who is wise." Wisdom is crucial in making informed decisions. In real life, wise individuals often serve as mentors or guides, helping others navigate complex situations.
  • sarvavīro: Meaning "all heroes," this phrase acknowledges the strength and courage found in everyone. It encourages us to recognize and celebrate the heroism in everyday acts, such as standing up for justice or helping those in need.
  • 'prayucchan: This translates to "not withholding." It suggests the importance of generosity and sharing resources with others. In practice, this can be seen in charitable acts or community service, where individuals contribute to the welfare of others.
  • pura etu prajānan: This phrase means "may he come to us with knowledge." It emphasizes the value of learning and understanding. In real life, continuous education and the pursuit of knowledge are essential for personal and professional growth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of hope, wisdom, community, and well-being. Its significance lies in its call for individuals to seek nourishment in all forms, to act with inclusivity, and to strive for safety and wisdom. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire us to foster a supportive environment, promote health and wellness, and engage in lifelong learning. By embodying these principles, we contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
