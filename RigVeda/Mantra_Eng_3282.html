नू ष्टुत इन्द्र नू गृणान इषं जारित्रे नद्यो न पीपेः |अकारि ते हरिवो ब्रह्म नव्यं धियाऽस्याम रथ्यः सदासाः ||
nū ṣṭuta indra nū gṛṇāna iṣaṁ jaritre nadyo3 na pīpeḥ | akāri te harivo brahma navyaṁ dhiyā syāma rathyaḥ sadāsāḥ ||
  • nū ṣṭuta indra: This phrase can be translated as "O Indra, who is praised." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunder, and war. The invocation of Indra signifies the importance of divine support and guidance in human endeavors.
  • nū gṛṇāna iṣaṁ: This translates to "we sing your praises." It emphasizes the act of worship and the importance of expressing gratitude and reverence towards the divine. In real life, this can be likened to how people often express gratitude for the blessings they receive, whether through prayer, meditation, or acts of kindness.
  • jaritre nadyo3 na pīpeḥ: This can be interpreted as "the rivers that flow, do not dry up." Rivers are vital for sustenance and life, symbolizing abundance and continuity. In a real-life context, this can relate to the importance of preserving natural resources and ensuring that our environment remains sustainable for future generations.
  • akāri te harivo: This means "you have made the horses." Horses in Vedic culture symbolize strength, speed, and vitality. This phrase can be seen as a recognition of the divine's role in providing strength and resources necessary for success in various endeavors, akin to how athletes train and rely on their physical capabilities to achieve their goals.
  • brahma navyaṁ: This translates to "new and fresh knowledge." The pursuit of knowledge and wisdom is a recurring theme in many cultures. In modern life, this can be related to the continuous quest for education and self-improvement, highlighting the importance of staying updated and adaptable in a rapidly changing world.
  • dhiyā syāma rathyaḥ sadāsāḥ: This can be interpreted as "may we always have the wisdom to guide us." The term "rathyaḥ" refers to chariots, which symbolize the journey of life. This phrase emphasizes the need for wisdom and guidance in navigating life's challenges, similar to how individuals seek mentors or role models to help them make informed decisions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, expressing gratitude, and recognizing the importance of wisdom and sustainability in our lives. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature, knowledge, and the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate a sense of responsibility towards the environment, pursue continuous learning, and seek guidance in their personal and professional journeys. By embodying these principles, one can lead a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
