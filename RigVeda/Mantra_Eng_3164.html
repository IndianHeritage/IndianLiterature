स होतासेदुदूत्यं चिकीत्वां अन्तर ईयते |विद्वां āरोधनं दिवः ||
sa hotā sed u dūtyaṁ cikitvām̐ antar īyate | vidvām̐ ārodhanaṁ divaḥ ||
  • sa hotā: This phrase refers to the "hotar," a priest or a person who performs rituals. In a broader sense, it signifies the one who invokes or calls upon divine energies.
  • sed u dūtyaṁ: "sed" means "but," and "dūtyaṁ" refers to a messenger or an envoy. This part of the mantra suggests a contrast or a transition from the role of the hotar to that of a messenger.
  • cikitvām̐: This word means "thoughtful" or "wise." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in the actions of the hotar and the messenger.
  • antar īyate: "antar" means "within" or "inside," and "īyate" means "to move" or "to go." This phrase indicates an inward journey or exploration of the self.
  • vidvām̐: This term translates to "the knowledgeable" or "the wise one." It highlights the significance of knowledge and wisdom in spiritual practices.
  • ārodhanaṁ divaḥ: "ārodhanaṁ" means "ascent" or "upliftment," and "divaḥ" refers to "heaven" or "the divine." This part of the mantra speaks to the idea of rising towards a higher state of being or consciousness.

  • Meaning: The mantra emphasizes the roles of the hotar and the messenger, highlighting the importance of wisdom and inner exploration in spiritual practices. It suggests that through thoughtful actions and knowledge, one can ascend to a higher state of consciousness.
  • Real-life Example: In modern life, this mantra can be applied to leadership roles. A leader (hotar) must not only guide their team but also act as a messenger (dūtyaṁ) of ideas and values. They should be wise (cikitvām̐) in their decisions and encourage their team to explore their inner potential (antar īyate) to achieve collective goals (ārodhanaṁ divaḥ).

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for wisdom, inner exploration, and the pursuit of higher consciousness. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as leadership, education, and personal development, where the integration of knowledge and thoughtful action can lead to greater achievements and spiritual growth.
