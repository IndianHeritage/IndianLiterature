अग्नेमन्मानि तुभ्यं कं घृतं न जुह्व आसनि |स देवेषु प्र चिकीद्धि त्वं ह्यसि पूर्व्यः शिवो दूतो विवस्वतो नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
agne manmāni tubhyaṁ kaṁ ghṛtaṁ na juhva āsani | sa deveṣu pra cikiddhi tvaṁ hy asi pūrvyaḥ śivo dūto vivasvato nabhantām anyake same ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Manmāni: This term can be interpreted as "with a mind" or "with intention," indicating a conscious offering or prayer.
  • Tubhyaṁ: This means "to you," addressing Agni directly, showing reverence and devotion.
  • Kaṁ: This translates to "who" or "which," often used to inquire or express a desire.
  • Ghṛtaṁ: This refers to clarified butter, a sacred offering in Vedic rituals, symbolizing nourishment and purity.
  • Na juhva: This means "I do not offer," indicating a request or a plea rather than a direct offering.
  • Āsani: This can be interpreted as "to be present" or "to exist," emphasizing the importance of Agni's presence.
  • Sa deveṣu: This phrase means "among the gods," indicating the divine context of the prayer.
  • Pra cikiddhi: This translates to "you should think" or "you should consider," suggesting a call for Agni's attention and thoughtfulness.
  • Tvaṁ: This means "you," reinforcing the direct address to Agni.
  • Hy asi: This translates to "indeed you are," affirming Agni's significance and role.
  • Pūrvyaḥ: This means "ancient" or "previous," indicating Agni's timeless nature.
  • Śivo: This term means "auspicious" or "beneficial," highlighting the positive attributes of Agni.
  • Dūto: This translates to "messenger," suggesting that Agni serves as a bridge between humans and the divine.
  • Vivasvato: This refers to "of the sun," linking Agni to solar energy and light.
  • Nabhantām: This means "of the heavens," indicating a celestial connection.
  • Anyake: This translates to "other" or "different," suggesting a distinction from other entities.
  • Same: This means "in the same way" or "similarly," indicating a parallel or comparison.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a divine force that connects the earthly realm with the heavens. It reflects the importance of intention in offerings and the need for divine guidance in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in rituals, prayers, or moments of reflection where one seeks clarity, guidance, and the presence of positive energy. By invoking Agni, individuals can aspire to align their intentions with higher purposes, fostering a sense of connection with the divine and the universe.
