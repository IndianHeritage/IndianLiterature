सम्यक् श्रवन्ति सारितो न धेनाऽन्तर हृदाऽमनसā पूयमानाः |एते अर्षन्त्य ऊर्मयो घृतस्य मृगाऽइव क्षिपणोऽरीषमानाः ||
samyak sravanti sarito na dhenā antar hṛdā manasā pūyamānāḥ | ete arṣanty ūrmayo ghṛtasya mṛgā iva kṣipaṇor īṣamāṇāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samyak: properly, rightly
    • sravanti: flow, stream
    • sarito: rivers
    • na: not
    • dhenā: by the cow, or by the nurturing
    • antar: within
    • hṛdā: heart
    • manasā: mind
    • pūyamānāḥ: being filled
    • ete: these
    • arṣanty: are agitated
    • ūrmayo: waves
    • ghṛtasya: of ghee (clarified butter)
    • mṛgā: like animals
    • iva: like
    • kṣipaṇor: of the swift
    • īṣamāṇāḥ: being observed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the flow of rivers and the nurturing aspect of nature.
    • The rivers are described as flowing rightly and not being obstructed, symbolizing the importance of a free and unimpeded life.
    • The mention of the heart and mind being filled suggests a state of emotional and mental abundance.
    • The agitation of the waves of ghee indicates the dynamic nature of life and the need for balance amidst chaos.
    • Animals being observed in this context may represent the instinctual aspects of life that are often overlooked.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Consider a river that flows freely, providing water to the surrounding ecosystem. This can be likened to a person who lives authentically, nurturing their relationships and environment.
    • In a workplace, when communication flows smoothly (like the rivers), it leads to a productive and harmonious atmosphere, akin to the heart and mind being filled with positive energy.
    • In personal development, recognizing and observing our instincts (like the animals) can lead to greater self-awareness and emotional intelligence.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of living in harmony with nature and oneself.
    • It teaches us the importance of allowing our emotions and thoughts to flow freely, just as rivers do, while also being mindful of the chaos that life can bring.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more balanced, fulfilling, and nurturing existence, both personally and in our interactions with others.
