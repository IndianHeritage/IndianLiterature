अभ्रातृव्यो अनā त्वम् अनāpिर इन्द्र जनुषा सनाद असि | युधेद आपित्वम् इच्छसे ||
abhrātṛvyo anā tvam anāpir indra januṣā sanād asi | yudhed āpitvam icchase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhrātṛvyo: To the brothers (or kin) of the clouds.
    • anā: Without (or free from).
    • tvam: You (referring to Indra, the king of gods).
    • anāpir: Without any (or free from any).
    • indra: Indra (the deity, often associated with rain and thunderstorms).
    • januṣā: By birth (or by nature).
    • sanād: Everlasting (or eternal).
    • asi: You are.
    • yudhed: In battle (or in conflict).
    • āpitvam: The power (or strength).
    • icchase: You desire (or wish for).
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, invoking his strength and power.
    • It emphasizes the idea of being free from limitations and obstacles, akin to the clouds that bring rain without hindrance.
    • The mention of "brothers of the clouds" symbolizes unity and support among kin, suggesting that strength comes from collective effort.
    • By stating "you desire in battle," it highlights the importance of courage and the will to confront challenges head-on.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, the mantra can be applied to encourage collaboration and support among team members, much like the clouds that work together to bring rain.
    • In personal challenges, it serves as a reminder to seek inner strength and resilience, drawing on the idea of being free from fear and limitations.
    • In leadership, it can inspire leaders to embody the qualities of Indra, leading with courage and decisiveness in the face of adversity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of strength, unity, and the courage to face challenges.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal struggles to teamwork and leadership.
    • By invoking the qualities of Indra, individuals can find inspiration to overcome obstacles and strive for success, embodying the spirit of resilience and collaboration.
