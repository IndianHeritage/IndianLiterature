कायमानो वनां त्वं यन् मातृर् अजगन्न अपः |न तत्ते अग्ने प्रमृषे निवर्तनं यद् दूरे सन्न इहाभवः ||
kāyamāno vanā tvaṁ yan mātṝr ajagann apaḥ | na tat te agne pramṛṣe nivartanaṁ yad dūre sann ihābhavaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kāyamāno: One who is embodied or has a physical form.
    • vanā: In the forest or wilderness.
    • tvaṁ: You (referring to Agni, the fire god).
    • yan: That which.
    • mātṝr: Of the mother or maternal.
    • ajagann: Unborn or eternal.
    • apaḥ: Waters or the essence of life.
    • na: Not.
    • tat: That.
    • te: Your.
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god).
    • pramṛṣe: To be deceived or misled.
    • nivartanaṁ: To turn back or retreat.
    • yad: When.
    • dūre: Far away.
    • sann: Being or existing.
    • ihābhavaḥ: Here in this world.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Agni, the divine fire, emphasizing the connection between the physical and spiritual realms.
    • The phrase "kāyamāno vanā" suggests that even in the wilderness or amidst challenges, the divine presence is embodied.
    • The reference to "mātṝr ajagann apaḥ" indicates the nurturing aspect of the divine, akin to a mother's care, which is essential for life.
    • The mantra warns against being misled or deceived ("na tat te agne pramṛṣe") and encourages staying true to one's path.
    • It highlights the importance of remaining grounded and connected to the divine, even when faced with distant or challenging circumstances ("yad dūre sann ihābhavaḥ").
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek spiritual guidance, much like invoking Agni for clarity and strength.
    • Environmental challenges can be likened to the "wilderness," where one must find inner strength and divine support to navigate through difficulties.
    • The nurturing aspect of the divine can be seen in the care provided by mothers or caregivers, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on maternal qualities.
    • In professional settings, staying true to one's values and not being misled by external pressures resonates with the warning against deception in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives, urging us to seek guidance and strength from Agni.
    • It emphasizes the importance of nurturing relationships and staying true to oneself amidst challenges.
    • In a broader context, it encourages individuals to connect with their spiritual essence, fostering resilience and clarity in both personal and communal aspects of life.
