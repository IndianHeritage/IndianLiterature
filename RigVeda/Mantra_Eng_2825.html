नकिर एषां निन्दिता मार्त्येषु ये अस्माकं पितरो गोषु योद्धाः |इन्द्र एषां दृष्मिता माहिनावान उद गोत्राणि ससृजे दंशानवान् ||
nakir eṣāṁ ninditā martyeṣu ye asmākam pitaro goṣu yodhāḥ | indra eṣāṁ dṛṁhitā māhināvān ud gotrāṇi sasṛje daṁsanāvān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nakir: not (or no)
    • eṣāṁ: of these
    • ninditā: those who are criticized or condemned
    • martyeṣu: among mortals
    • ye: who
    • asmākam: our
    • pitaro: fathers or ancestors
    • goṣu: in the cows (or wealth)
    • yodhāḥ: warriors or fighters
    • indra: Indra (the king of gods)
    • dṛṁhitā: those who are strong or powerful
    • māhināvān: possessing great strength
    • ud: and
    • gotrāṇi: clans or lineages
    • sasṛje: created or produced
    • daṁsanāvān: possessing the ability to bite or strike
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the strength and valor of our ancestors, particularly in the context of their role as protectors and warriors.
    • It emphasizes that those who are criticized among mortals are not our true ancestors, suggesting a distinction between true valor and mere reputation.
    • Indra, as a symbol of power and strength, is invoked to highlight the qualities of our forefathers who were strong and capable of defending their lineage.
    • The mention of "cows" can be interpreted as a metaphor for wealth and prosperity, indicating that true warriors not only protect their lineage but also ensure the prosperity of their clan.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, this mantra can be related to the respect and honor given to military veterans who have fought for their country, symbolizing the strength and sacrifice of ancestors.
    • In family businesses, the legacy of ancestors is often celebrated, where the current generation strives to uphold the values and strength of their forebears.
    • In various cultures, the concept of honoring one's ancestors is prevalent, where rituals and traditions are followed to remember and respect those who came before.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of honoring our ancestors and recognizing the strength they have passed down to us.
    • It encourages individuals to embody the qualities of bravery and protection, not just for personal gain but for the welfare of their community and lineage.
    • In a broader sense, it highlights the significance of legacy, urging us to reflect on how we can contribute positively to our families and society, ensuring that we are remembered as warriors of virtue and strength.
