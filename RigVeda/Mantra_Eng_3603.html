न प्रमीये सवितुर्दैव्यस्य तद् यथा विश्वं भुवनं धारयिष्यति |यत् पृथिव्या वरिमन्ना स्वङ्गुरिर वर्ष्मन् दिवः सुवति सत्यं अस्य तत् ||
na pramiye savitur daivyasya tad yathā viśvam bhuvanaṁ dhārayiṣyati | yat pṛthivyā varimann ā svaṅgurir varṣman divaḥ suvati satyam asya tat ||
  • na pramiye savitur: This phrase can be translated as "I do not know the divine light of Savitar." Savitar is a solar deity in the Rigveda, representing the sun's life-giving energy. This line expresses a sense of humility and acknowledgment of the vastness of divine knowledge.
  • daivyasya tad yathā: This means "as that divine (light)". It emphasizes the connection between the divine and the natural world, suggesting that the divine light has a role in sustaining life.
  • viśvam bhuvanaṁ dhārayiṣyati: Translated, this means "that which sustains the entire universe." This line highlights the idea that the divine energy is responsible for maintaining the cosmos and all living beings within it.
  • yat pṛthivyā varimann: This translates to "that which is abundant on Earth." It refers to the richness and resources that the Earth provides, which are essential for life.
  • ā svaṅgurir varṣman divaḥ: This means "from the heavenly abode." It suggests that the blessings and resources we receive on Earth are derived from a higher, divine source.
  • suvati satyam asya tat: This translates to "this is the truth of that." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and acknowledging the truth behind the divine connection to the universe and our existence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the relationship between the divine, the universe, and the Earth. It teaches us humility in the face of divine knowledge and the importance of recognizing the interconnectedness of all life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to appreciate nature's resources, practice gratitude for the abundance provided by the Earth, and foster a sense of responsibility towards the environment. By acknowledging the divine source of our sustenance, we can cultivate a deeper respect for nature and strive for a harmonious existence with all living beings.
