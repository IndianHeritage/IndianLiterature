इन्द्रो दधिचो अस्तभिर वृत्राण्य अप्रतिष्कुतः |जघान नवतीर नवा ||
indro dadhīco asthabhir vṛtrāṇy apratiṣkutaḥ | jaghāna navatīr nava ||
  • Indro: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • dadhīco: This term can be interpreted as "one who is wise" or "one who possesses knowledge." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in leadership.
  • asthabhir: This word means "with strength" or "with might," indicating the necessity of physical and mental strength in overcoming challenges.
  • vṛtrāṇy: Refers to "the obstacles" or "the demons," symbolizing the challenges and adversities one faces in life.
  • apratiṣkutaḥ: This term means "unconquered" or "unstoppable," suggesting resilience and the ability to face challenges without being defeated.
  • jaghāna: Translates to "he struck down" or "he defeated," indicating the action of overcoming obstacles.
  • navatīr: This word means "the new" or "the fresh," symbolizing new beginnings or opportunities.
  • nava: Similar to navatīr, it also means "new," reinforcing the theme of renewal and transformation.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for strength and wisdom in overcoming obstacles and challenges in life. It emphasizes the importance of resilience and the ability to strike down adversities to embrace new opportunities.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties in personal life, such as health issues or relationship problems, one can invoke the qualities of Indra to find strength and wisdom to overcome these challenges.
  • Professional Obstacles: In a work environment, when encountering obstacles in projects or career advancement, this mantra serves as a reminder to remain resilient and seek innovative solutions.
  • Societal Issues: On a larger scale, communities facing social or economic challenges can draw inspiration from this mantra to unite and tackle issues collectively, fostering a sense of strength and renewal.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of strength, wisdom, and resilience in overcoming life's challenges. It encourages individuals to confront their obstacles head-on and embrace new beginnings, making it a powerful reminder of the potential for transformation in both personal and collective contexts.
