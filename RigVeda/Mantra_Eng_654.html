त्वं तं इन्द्र पर्वतं न भोजसे महो नृम्णस्य धर्माणाम् इराज्यसि | प्र वीर्येण देवताती चेकिते विश्वस्मा उग्रः कर्मणे पुरोहितः ||
tvaṁ tam indra parvataṁ na bhojase maho nṛmṇasya dharmaṇām irajyasi | pra vīryeṇa devatāti cekite viśvasmā ugraḥ karmaṇe purohitaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • tam: That
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • parvataṁ: Mountain
    • na: Not
    • bhojase: You enjoy
    • maho: Great
    • nṛmṇasya: Of men
    • dharmaṇām: Of righteousness
    • irajyasi: You are invoked
    • pra: With
    • vīryeṇa: Strength
    • devatāti: O divine one
    • cekite: You are aware
    • viśvasmā: From all
    • ugraḥ: Powerful
    • karmaṇe: In actions
    • purohitaḥ: The priest or guide
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, the chief deity in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength and power.
    • It emphasizes the importance of righteousness (dharma) in human actions and the divine guidance that comes from invoking Indra.
    • The mention of mountains (parvataṁ) signifies stability and strength, suggesting that one should be grounded in their values.
    • The phrase "you are aware" indicates that the divine is always cognizant of human actions and intentions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often invoke the principles of righteousness and strength when making ethical decisions, akin to invoking Indra for guidance.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek strength and clarity in their actions by reflecting on their values, much like the invocation of divine support in the mantra.
    • During challenging times, people may turn to spiritual practices that resonate with this mantra, seeking the strength to overcome obstacles while adhering to their moral compass.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of righteousness and strength in our actions.
    • It highlights the importance of divine guidance in navigating life's challenges, encouraging individuals to remain grounded in their values.
    • In contemporary life, the principles encapsulated in this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, from personal growth to ethical leadership, reinforcing the timeless relevance of Vedic wisdom.
