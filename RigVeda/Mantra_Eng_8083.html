त्वं सोम पवमानो विश्वानि दुरितातर | कविः सीद नि बर्हिषि ||
tvaṁ soma pavamāno viśvāni duritā tara | kaviḥ sīda ni barhiṣi ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity, often interpreted as the Soma or a deity associated with the sacred drink.
  • soma - Soma is a significant element in Vedic literature, representing a divine drink that grants immortality and enlightenment. It symbolizes the essence of life and spiritual awakening.
  • pavamāno - This term means "purifying" or "flowing," suggesting a cleansing or elevating quality of the Soma, which is essential for spiritual growth.
  • viśvāni - This word means "all" or "universal," indicating the inclusive nature of the blessings or benefits being sought.
  • duritā - Translated as "obstacles" or "sins," this term refers to the challenges and negative influences that hinder spiritual progress.
  • tara - This means "to cross over" or "to transcend," suggesting the act of overcoming difficulties and achieving a higher state of being.
  • kaviḥ - This word means "the wise one" or "the poet," often referring to a seer or sage who possesses deep insight and understanding.
  • sīda - Translated as "sit" or "reside," it implies a state of stability and presence, often in a meditative or contemplative manner.
  • ni - This is a prefix that can mean "down" or "in," indicating a grounding or settling aspect.
  • barhiṣi - This term refers to "the sacred grass" or "the altar," symbolizing a place of worship or a space where spiritual practices occur.

  • Meaning: The mantra invokes the divine essence of Soma, asking for purification and the ability to transcend obstacles. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and stability in spiritual practice.
  • Real-life Example: In daily life, individuals often face challenges that can be likened to "duritā." For instance, someone may struggle with stress or negative thoughts. By meditating and seeking clarity (as suggested by "kaviḥ sīda"), one can find peace and overcome these challenges, much like the purification of Soma.

  • Conclusion: This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine assistance in overcoming life's obstacles through wisdom and purification. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as during meditation, prayer, or moments of reflection, where individuals strive to connect with their higher selves and transcend difficulties.
