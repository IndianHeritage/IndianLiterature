एव नपातो मम तस्य धीभिर भरद्वाजाः अभ्यर्चन्त्यर्कैः |ज्ञा हुतासो वसवोऽधृष्टा विश्वे स्तुतासो भूताः यजत्राः ||
evā napāto mama tasya dhībhir bharadvājā abhy arcanty arkaiḥ | gnā hutāso vasavo 'dhṛṣṭā viśve stutāso bhūtā yajatrāḥ ||
  • evā napāto mama: This phrase can be interpreted as "O offspring of mine" or "O progeny of mine." It signifies a connection to one's lineage or descendants, emphasizing the importance of family and heritage.
  • tasya dhībhir bharadvājā: Here, "Bharadvāja" refers to a sage or a learned individual from the Bharadvāja clan. "Tasya dhībhir" means "with their intellect" or "with their wisdom." This suggests that wisdom and knowledge are essential in guiding one's progeny.
  • abhy arcanty arkaiḥ: This translates to "they worship with hymns." It indicates the act of reverence and devotion, highlighting the importance of spiritual practices and rituals in life.
  • gnā hutāso vasavo: "Vasavo" refers to the deities associated with prosperity and abundance. "Hutāso" means "those who are invoked." This part emphasizes the significance of invoking divine blessings for prosperity and well-being.
  • 'dhṛṣṭā viśve stutāso: "Dhṛṣṭā" means "those who are bold" or "those who are courageous." "Viśve stutāso" translates to "all beings who are praised." This suggests that courage and recognition of all beings are vital for a harmonious existence.
  • bhūtā yajatrāḥ: "Yajatrāḥ" refers to those who perform sacrifices or rituals. "Bhūtā" means "beings" or "entities." This indicates that all beings participate in the cosmic order through their actions and rituals.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of family, wisdom, spirituality, and the divine. It highlights the importance of nurturing one's lineage with knowledge and reverence while invoking blessings for prosperity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as family gatherings where wisdom is shared, rituals performed for well-being, and the recognition of the contributions of all individuals in a community. By embodying these principles, one can foster a harmonious and prosperous life, honoring both the past and the present.
