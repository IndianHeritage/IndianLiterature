क्षेमो न साधुः क्रतुर न भद्रो भवत स्वाधीर होताऽ हव्यवाट् ||
kṣemo na sādhuḥ kratur na bhadro bhuvat svādhīr hotā havyavāṭ ||
  • kṣemo: This word translates to "peace" or "well-being." It signifies a state of harmony and tranquility in life.
  • na sādhuḥ: This phrase means "not good" or "not beneficial." It implies that actions or thoughts that do not lead to peace are undesirable.
  • kratur: This term refers to "intellect" or "will." It represents the mental faculties that guide our decisions and actions.
  • na bhadro: This means "not auspicious" or "not favorable." It suggests that one should avoid paths that do not lead to positive outcomes.
  • bhuvat: This word translates to "become" or "manifest." It indicates the process of transformation or realization of potential.
  • svādhīr: This term means "self-controlled" or "self-possessed." It emphasizes the importance of self-discipline and mastery over one's desires.
  • hotā: This refers to "the priest" or "the one who invokes." It symbolizes the role of a mediator or facilitator in spiritual practices.
  • havyavāṭ: This word means "the one who offers oblations" or "the one who brings offerings." It signifies the act of giving and the importance of offerings in rituals.

The mantra as a whole can be interpreted as a call for peace and well-being, urging individuals to avoid unbeneficial actions and thoughts. It emphasizes the importance of intellect and self-control in achieving auspicious outcomes. The invocation of a priest or mediator highlights the significance of guidance in spiritual practices, while the act of offering symbolizes gratitude and connection to the divine.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In personal relationships, fostering peace and avoiding negative interactions can lead to healthier connections.
  • In decision-making, using intellect and self-control can help individuals choose paths that are beneficial and auspicious.
  • In spiritual practices, seeking guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders can enhance one's journey towards self-realization.
  • In community settings, the act of giving and sharing can strengthen bonds and promote collective well-being.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of peace, intellect, and self-control in our lives. It encourages individuals to seek positive outcomes and to engage in practices that foster harmony and connection with others and the divine. By embodying these principles, one can navigate life's challenges with grace and purpose.
