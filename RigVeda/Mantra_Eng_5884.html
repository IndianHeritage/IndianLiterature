गॊमद धीरण्यवद वसु यद वाम अश्वावद ईमहे |इन्द्राग्नि तद वनेमहि ||
gomad dhiraṇyavad vasu yad vām aśvāvad īmahe | indrāgnī tad vanemahi ||
  • gomad: This word refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," often associated with cattle in the Vedic context, symbolizing abundance and nourishment.
  • dhiraṇyavad: This term can be interpreted as "wise" or "intelligent," suggesting a thoughtful approach to acquiring wealth and prosperity.
  • vasu: This word means "good" or "well-being," indicating a state of harmony and positive energy in life.
  • yad: This translates to "that" or "which," serving as a connector in the mantra.
  • vām: This means "your" or "of you," indicating a personal connection to the wealth and prosperity being discussed.
  • aśvāvad: This term relates to "horses," which in Vedic culture symbolize strength, speed, and power, often associated with success in endeavors.
  • īmahe: This means "we meditate" or "we contemplate," suggesting a reflective approach to the concepts being presented.
  • indrāgnī: This compound word refers to "Indra" and "Agni," two significant deities in Vedic literature. Indra represents strength and leadership, while Agni symbolizes fire and transformation.
  • tad: This translates to "that," reinforcing the connection to the previous concepts.
  • vanemahi: This means "we invoke" or "we call upon," indicating a desire to connect with the divine or higher powers for assistance.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for wisdom in acquiring wealth and prosperity, emphasizing the importance of thoughtful contemplation and the invocation of divine forces for success. It highlights the interconnectedness of material wealth (symbolized by cattle and horses) and spiritual wisdom (represented by the deities Indra and Agni).


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In business, it encourages entrepreneurs to approach their ventures with intelligence and mindfulness, ensuring that their pursuits lead to genuine prosperity.
  • In personal finance, it serves as a reminder to reflect on one's financial decisions and to seek guidance from higher principles or values.
  • In community building, it emphasizes the importance of collective strength and support, invoking the idea that prosperity is best achieved when individuals work together harmoniously.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to wealth and prosperity, intertwining material success with spiritual wisdom. It encourages individuals to meditate on their goals, invoke divine support, and act with intelligence and integrity in their pursuits. By applying these principles in daily life, one can foster a balanced and prosperous existence.
