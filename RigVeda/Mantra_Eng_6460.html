वīḻुपविभिर मरुत ऋभुक्षण आ रुद्रासः सुदीतिभिः |इषा नो अद्य गता पुरुष्पृह यज्ञं आ सोभरियवः ||
vīḻupavibhir maruta ṛbhukṣaṇa ā rudrāsaḥ sudītibhiḥ | iṣā no adyā gatā puruspṛho yajñam ā sobharīyavaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "vīḻupavibhir maruta ṛbhukṣaṇa ā rudrāsaḥ sudītibhiḥ | iṣā no adyā gatā puruspṛho yajñam ā sobharīyavaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vīḻupavibhir: With the help of the winds or the Maruts.
    • maruta: The Maruts, deities of the storm and winds.
    • ṛbhukṣaṇa: Those who are strong and powerful.
    • ā: Come forth or arrive.
    • rudrāsaḥ: O Rudras, the fierce ones.
    • sudītibhiḥ: With good intentions or auspiciousness.
    • iṣā: May we have or may we attain.
    • no: Our.
    • adyā: Today.
    • gatā: Gone or reached.
    • puruspṛho: Desiring many things or having many desires.
    • yajñam: The sacrifice or offering.
    • ā: To or towards.
    • sobharīyavaḥ: Those who are glorious or radiant.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the Maruts, the storm deities, who are known for their strength and power. In real life, we can relate this to the forces of nature that can be both destructive and nurturing, such as storms that bring rain to nourish crops.
    • The mention of "Rudras" signifies the fierce aspects of nature, reminding us of the duality of creation and destruction. For example, a forest fire can destroy but also rejuvenate the ecosystem.
    • The phrase "may we attain" emphasizes the importance of aspiration and intention in our actions. In a modern context, this can be seen in goal-setting practices where individuals strive to achieve their dreams through focused efforts.
    • The term "yajñam" refers to sacrifice or offering, which can be interpreted as the dedication of one's efforts for a greater cause. This is evident in community service or charitable acts where individuals contribute their time and resources for the benefit of others.
    • The concluding part of the mantra, "those who are glorious," highlights the idea of achieving success and recognition through virtuous actions and intentions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature, our aspirations, and the importance of selfless actions. It encourages us to harness the power of nature and our inner strength to achieve our goals while being mindful of our responsibilities towards others.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as environmental conservation efforts, community building, and personal development, where we strive to create a positive impact while pursuing our desires.
