त्वां यज्ञेभिरुक्तैः उप हव्येभिरिमहे |शचीपते शचीनाṁ वि वो मदे श्रेष्ठं नो धेहि वार्यं विवक्षसे ||
tvāṁ yajñebhir ukthair upa havyebhir īmahe | śacīpate śacīnāṁ vi vo made śreṣṭhaṁ no dhehi vāryaṁ vivakṣase ||
  • tvāṁ - This word translates to "you" and signifies the divine presence or the deity being addressed in the mantra.
  • yajñebhir - This means "through sacrifices" or "by means of offerings," indicating the importance of rituals and offerings in Vedic traditions.
  • ukthair - This translates to "with hymns" or "with praises," emphasizing the role of vocal expressions of devotion in connecting with the divine.
  • upa - This means "near" or "approaching," suggesting a closeness or intimacy in the relationship between the devotee and the deity.
  • havyebhir - This refers to "with offerings" or "with oblations," highlighting the significance of material offerings in spiritual practices.
  • īmahe - This translates to "we invoke" or "we meditate upon," indicating an active engagement in worship and devotion.
  • śacīpate - This means "O Lord of Power" or "O Lord of Strength," addressing the deity as a source of strength and vitality.
  • śacīnāṁ - This translates to "of the powerful" or "of the strong," referring to those who possess strength and capability.
  • vi - This means "among" or "in," indicating a sense of community or collective worship.
  • vo - This translates to "your" or "to you," reinforcing the personal connection to the deity.
  • made - This means "joy" or "delight," suggesting that the worship is intended to bring happiness and fulfillment.
  • śreṣṭhaṁ - This translates to "the best" or "the greatest," indicating the highest form of worship or offering.
  • no - This means "to us," emphasizing the personal nature of the request being made in the mantra.
  • dhehi - This translates to "grant" or "bestow," indicating a plea for divine blessings.
  • vāryaṁ - This means "strength" or "power," referring to the qualities being sought from the deity.
  • vivakṣase - This translates to "you who are to be expressed" or "you who are to be revealed," suggesting a desire for the divine to manifest in the devotee's life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of devotion, offerings, and the invocation of divine strength and joy. It reflects the Vedic belief that through sincere worship and offerings, one can establish a close relationship with the divine, seeking blessings for strength and fulfillment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal prayers, community rituals, or significant life events where individuals seek divine guidance and support. It serves as a reminder of the power of collective worship and the joy that comes from connecting with a higher presence, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and empowered life.
