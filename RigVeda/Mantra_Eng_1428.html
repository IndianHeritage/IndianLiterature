वीलु चिद यस्य समृतौ श्रुवद वनेव यत स्थिरम् |निःषहमाणो यमते नायते धन्वासहा नायते ||
vīḻu cid yasya samṛtau śruvad vaneva yat sthiram | niḥṣahamāṇo yamate nāyate dhanvāsahā nāyate ||
  • vīḻu cid: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who is aware" or "the one who knows." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and awareness in life.
  • yasya samṛtau: This translates to "in whose prosperity" or "in whose abundance." It suggests that knowledge leads to prosperity and success.
  • śruvad vaneva: This means "like a tree that is firm." It symbolizes stability and strength, indicating that a knowledgeable person remains steadfast in their beliefs and actions.
  • yat sthiram: This translates to "that which is stable." It reinforces the idea of consistency and reliability in one's knowledge and actions.
  • niḥṣahamāṇo: This can be understood as "without any hesitation" or "without any doubt." It highlights the confidence that comes from true knowledge.
  • yamate: This means "one who controls" or "one who governs." It refers to the ability to manage oneself and one's circumstances through knowledge.
  • nāyate dhanvāsahā: This translates to "the one who is not led astray by wealth." It emphasizes that true knowledge transcends material wealth and possessions.
  • nāyate: This means "is not led" or "is not influenced." It suggests that a knowledgeable person remains unaffected by external distractions.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of knowledge and awareness in achieving stability and prosperity in life. It teaches that true strength comes from being well-informed and confident in one's understanding. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as education, personal development, and decision-making. For instance, a student who is aware of their strengths and weaknesses can make informed choices about their studies, leading to academic success. Similarly, a business leader who possesses knowledge about market trends can navigate challenges effectively, ensuring the growth and stability of their organization. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that knowledge is a powerful tool that can guide individuals towards a fulfilling and prosperous life.
