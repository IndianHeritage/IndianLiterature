यद् देवाऽऽदः सलिले सुसंरभ्धा अतिṣṭhता |अत्रा वो नृत्यताम् इव तीव्रो रेणुर अपायत ||
yad devā adaḥ salile susaṁrabdhā atiṣṭhata | atrā vo nṛtyatām iva tīvro reṇur apāyata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • devā: the gods
    • adaḥ: have placed
    • salile: in the waters
    • susaṁrabdhā: well-established
    • atiṣṭhata: have stood firm
    • atrā: here
    • vo: for you
    • nṛtyatām: may dance
    • iva: like
    • tīvro: intense
    • reṇur: the dust
    • apāyata: may rise
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine presence of the gods in the waters, suggesting a connection between the spiritual and the natural world.
    • The phrase "yad devā adaḥ salile susaṁrabdhā" indicates that the gods have established their presence firmly in the waters, symbolizing purity, life, and sustenance.
    • The latter part, "atrā vo nṛtyatām iva tīvro reṇur apāyata," evokes imagery of a vibrant dance, where the dust rises intensely, representing joy, celebration, and the dynamic nature of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, water is revered as a sacred element. For instance, in Hindu rituals, water is often used for purification and blessings, reflecting the idea of divine presence in natural elements.
    • Celebrations such as Holi, where colors and dust rise in the air, can be seen as a manifestation of the joyous dance mentioned in the mantra, symbolizing the vibrancy of life and community.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing the divine in nature, particularly in water, which is essential for life.
    • It encourages individuals to celebrate life and its vibrancy, reminding us of the interconnectedness of the spiritual and physical realms.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in practices of gratitude towards nature, environmental conservation, and community celebrations that honor life and its sources.
