मित्रं कृतध्वं खलु मृळतā नो मा नो घोरेण चरताभि धृष्णु |नि वो नु मन्युर विशताम अरातिर अन्यो बभ्रूणाम प्रसितौ न्व अस्तु ||
mitraṁ kṛṇudhvaṁ khalu mṛḻatā no mā no ghoreṇa caratābhi dhṛṣṇu | ni vo nu manyur viśatām arātir anyo babhrūṇām prasitau nv astu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mitraṁ: Friend or ally
    • kṛṇudhvaṁ: May you create or establish
    • khalu: Indeed
    • mṛḻatā: From the one who is gentle or soft
    • no: Our
    • mā: Not
    • no: Our
    • ghoreṇa: By the terrible or fierce
    • caratābhi: By those who are moving or acting
    • dhṛṣṇu: With courage or boldness
    • ni: Down or towards
    • vo: To you
    • nu: Indeed
    • manyur: Anger or wrath
    • viśatām: May it enter
    • arātir: Hostility or enmity
    • anyo: Another
    • babhrūṇām: Of those who are strong or powerful
    • prasitau: In the direction or path
    • nv: May it be
    • astu: Let it be
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of friendship and collaboration.
    • It calls for the establishment of bonds that are gentle and nurturing, rather than fierce and aggressive.
    • The plea is to avoid anger and hostility, promoting peace and understanding among individuals.
    • It suggests that strength should be directed towards constructive actions rather than destructive ones.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied to foster teamwork. Instead of competing against each other, colleagues can work together to achieve common goals.
    • In community service, individuals can come together to support those in need, creating a nurturing environment rather than one filled with conflict.
    • In personal relationships, this mantra encourages open communication and understanding, helping to resolve conflicts peacefully.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and cooperation.
    • It serves as a reminder that true strength is found in collaboration and kindness, rather than in aggression and division.
    • In today's world, where conflicts often arise, this mantra is applicable in promoting peace, understanding, and constructive relationships in various aspects of life.
