मन्त्र: नीत्वा होतारं ऋत्विजं दधिरे वसुवित्तमम् |श्रुतकर्णं सप्रथस्तमं विप्राः अग्ने दिविष्टिषु ||
ni tvā hotāram ṛtvijaṁ dadhire vasuvittamam | śrutkarṇaṁ saprathastamaṁ viprā agne diviṣṭiṣu ||
  • ni tvā hotāram: This phrase translates to "We invoke you, O Agni, the Hotar (the priest who offers the oblation)." Here, Agni represents the divine fire and is often seen as a mediator between humans and the gods.
  • ṛtvijaṁ dadhire: This means "who is the one that upholds the sacred order." The term "ṛtvija" refers to the priests or those who perform rituals, emphasizing the importance of maintaining cosmic order through proper rites.
  • vasuvittamam: This translates to "the best among the Vasus." The Vasus are a group of deities associated with various natural elements. Calling Agni the best among them highlights his supreme importance in rituals and offerings.
  • śrutkarṇaṁ: This means "having ears that hear." It signifies the ability to listen and respond to the prayers and offerings made by devotees, indicating Agni's role as a divine listener.
  • saprathastamaṁ: This translates to "the one who is most powerful among the powerful." It emphasizes Agni's strength and influence in the spiritual realm, suggesting that he can grant blessings and fulfill desires.
  • viprā agne: This means "O Agni, the wise ones." It acknowledges the role of the learned and wise individuals (vipras) who understand the significance of rituals and invoke Agni's presence.
  • diviṣṭiṣu: This translates to "in the heavenly realms." It indicates that the offerings made to Agni reach the divine realms, reinforcing the connection between the earthly and the celestial.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a divine mediator and the importance of rituals in maintaining cosmic order. In real life, this can be applied to the practice of mindfulness and intention in our actions. Just as the priests invoke Agni with respect and devotion, individuals can approach their daily tasks with a sense of purpose and reverence. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of listening, both to the divine and to one another, fostering harmony in our lives and communities.
