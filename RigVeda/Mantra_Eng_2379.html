यम् उ पूर्वम् अहुवे तं इदं हुवे सेद उ हव्यो ददिर यो नाम पत्यते |अध्वर्युभिः प्रस्थितं सोम्यम् मधु पोत्रात सोमं द्रविणोदः पिब ऋतुभिः ||
yam u pūrvam ahuve tam idaṁ huve sed u havyo dadir yo nāma patyate | adhvaryubhiḥ prasthitaṁ somyam madhu potrāt somaṁ draviṇodaḥ piba ṛtubhiḥ ||
  • yam u pūrvam ahuve: This phrase translates to "Whom we have called upon before." It signifies the invocation of a deity or a divine presence that has been acknowledged in previous rituals.
  • tam idaṁ huve: This means "We now call upon that." It indicates a continuation of the invocation, emphasizing the importance of the deity in the current context.
  • sed u havyo dadir: This translates to "He who is worthy of being invoked." It highlights the qualities of the deity, suggesting that the one being called upon is deserving of reverence and respect.
  • yo nāma patyate: This means "Whose name is known." It points to the significance of the name of the deity, which carries power and recognition among the worshippers.
  • adhvaryubhiḥ prasthitaṁ: This translates to "Set forth by the priests." It indicates the role of the priests in the ritual, guiding the invocation and ensuring that it is performed correctly.
  • somyam madhu potrāt: This means "From the sweet drink of Soma." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine connection. The phrase suggests the offering of this drink to the deity.
  • somaṁ draviṇodaḥ: This translates to "The Soma, the wealth." It emphasizes the importance of Soma not just as a drink but as a representation of abundance and prosperity.
  • piba ṛtubhiḥ: This means "Drink with the seasons." It signifies the cyclical nature of life and the importance of aligning rituals with the natural rhythms of the world.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of Vedic rituals, emphasizing the importance of invoking divine presence through established traditions. It highlights the role of priests, the significance of sacred offerings like Soma, and the connection between the divine and the natural world. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various rituals and ceremonies, reminding individuals of the importance of tradition, respect for the divine, and the interconnectedness of life. It serves as a guide for those seeking to deepen their spiritual practices and understand the significance of their actions within the broader context of existence.
