सुप्राव्यः प्राशुṣाल् एष वीरः सुष्वेः पक्तिं कृतुते केवलेंद्रः |नासुṣ्वर आपिर न सखा न जामिर दुष्प्राव्योऽवहन्तेद अवाचः ||
suprāvyaḥ prāśuṣāḻ eṣa vīraḥ suṣveḥ paktiṁ kṛṇute kevalendraḥ | nāsuṣver āpir na sakhā na jāmir duṣprāvyo 'vahanted avācaḥ ||
  • suprāvyaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "well-spoken" or "eloquent." In real life, effective communication is crucial in various fields such as business, education, and personal relationships. For example, a leader who communicates clearly can inspire and motivate their team.
  • prāśuṣāḻ: This word refers to "the one who is strong" or "powerful." In everyday life, strength can manifest in various forms, such as physical strength, mental resilience, or emotional stability. For instance, a person overcoming personal challenges demonstrates inner strength.
  • eṣa vīraḥ: Translated as "this hero," it signifies someone who embodies courage and valor. In contemporary society, heroes can be found in everyday life, such as healthcare workers during a crisis or activists fighting for social justice.
  • suṣveḥ: This term means "good" or "auspicious." In life, we often seek positive outcomes, whether in our careers, relationships, or personal endeavors. For example, a successful project at work can lead to promotions and recognition.
  • paktiṁ kṛṇute: This phrase translates to "creates strength" or "builds power." In real life, individuals and organizations build strength through collaboration, education, and experience. For instance, a team that works well together can achieve greater results than individuals working alone.
  • kevalendraḥ: This term can be interpreted as "the only lord" or "supreme being." In various cultures, this concept can relate to the idea of a higher power or ultimate truth that guides individuals in their moral and ethical decisions.
  • nāsuṣver: Meaning "not to be harmed," this word emphasizes protection and safety. In real life, ensuring safety in our environments—be it at home, work, or in society—is essential for well-being. For example, implementing safety protocols in workplaces protects employees.
  • āpir: This term refers to "friends" or "companions." The importance of friendship and community support is evident in our lives, as strong relationships contribute to emotional health and resilience. For instance, friends can provide support during difficult times.
  • na jāmir: This phrase means "not born" or "not created." It can signify the idea of timelessness or the eternal nature of certain truths or values. In life, some principles, such as love and compassion, are universally recognized and valued across cultures.
  • duṣprāvyo: This term translates to "difficult to achieve" or "hard to attain." It reflects the challenges we face in pursuing our goals. For example, achieving a high level of education or mastering a skill often requires significant effort and perseverance.
  • 'vahanted: This word means "they carry" or "they bear." In real life, individuals often carry burdens, whether emotional, financial, or social. Support systems, such as family and friends, can help lighten these burdens.
  • avācaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "words" or "speech." The power of words in shaping thoughts, actions, and relationships is profound. For instance, motivational speeches can inspire individuals to take action and pursue their dreams.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of eloquence, strength, heroism, auspiciousness, and the creation of power in our lives. It highlights the significance of protection, friendship, timeless values, and the challenges we face. In real life, these concepts are applicable in various scenarios, from personal development to community building. By embodying these qualities, individuals can navigate life's challenges and contribute positively to society, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and empowered existence.
