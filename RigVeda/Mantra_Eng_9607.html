मन्त्र: सुन्वन्ति सोमं रथिरासो अद्रयो निर अस्य रसं गविषो दूहन्ति ते | दूहन्त्य ऊधर उपसेचनाया कं नरो हव्याऽ न मरजयन्त āsभिः ||
sunvanti somaṁ rathirāso adrayo nir asya rasaṁ gaviṣo duhanti te | duhanty ūdhar upasecanāya kaṁ naro havyā na marjayanta āsabhiḥ ||
  • sunvanti: This word can be translated as "they who listen" or "they who hear." It signifies the importance of being receptive to knowledge and wisdom.
  • somaṁ: Refers to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition believed to confer immortality and divine insight. It symbolizes spiritual nourishment.
  • rathirāso: This term can be understood as "the charioteer of desires" or "the one who drives the chariot of aspirations." It represents the guiding force behind our ambitions.
  • adrayo: Meaning "the mountains" or "the heights," it signifies stability and strength, often associated with the challenges we face in life.
  • nir asya rasaṁ: Translates to "the essence of this is not." It suggests a deeper understanding that the material essence is transient and not the ultimate reality.
  • gaviṣo: This word means "the one who seeks" or "the seeker." It emphasizes the pursuit of knowledge and truth.
  • duhanti te: Translates to "they who extract" or "they who draw out." It refers to the act of deriving wisdom or nourishment from experiences.
  • duhanty ūdhar: This phrase means "they draw forth from the depths." It signifies the importance of introspection and understanding one's inner self.
  • upasecanāya: Meaning "for the purpose of offering," it highlights the act of giving and sharing knowledge or resources with others.
  • kaṁ naro: Translates to "who among men." It raises a rhetorical question about the nature of humanity and our collective responsibilities.
  • havyā: Refers to "the offerings" or "the sacred gifts." It symbolizes the importance of contributions to the divine or the community.
  • na marjayanta: This means "do not neglect" or "do not disregard." It emphasizes the need to pay attention to what is essential.
  • āsabhiḥ: Refers to "the strong" or "the powerful." It signifies the strength that comes from unity and collective effort.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of listening, seeking knowledge, and drawing wisdom from our experiences. It highlights the significance of spiritual nourishment and the collective responsibility of humanity to share and offer what they have learned. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as education, community service, and personal growth. By being receptive to knowledge and sharing it with others, we contribute to a more enlightened and harmonious society. The mantra serves as a reminder that true strength lies in unity and the pursuit of higher understanding.
