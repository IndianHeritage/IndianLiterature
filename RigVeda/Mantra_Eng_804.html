गोषु प्राशस्तिं वनेषु धिषे भरन्त विश्वे बलिं स्वर णः ||
goṣu praśastiṁ vaneṣu dhiṣe bharanta viśve baliṁ svar ṇaḥ ||
  • goṣu: This word translates to "in the cows" or "among the cattle." Cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and prosperity in Vedic literature, representing nourishment and sustenance.
  • praśastiṁ: This means "praise" or "glory." It signifies the act of honoring or recognizing the virtues of something or someone.
  • vaneṣu: This translates to "in the forests" or "in the woods." Forests are often associated with nature, tranquility, and the abundance of resources.
  • dhiṣe: This word means "with wisdom" or "with intelligence." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in our actions.
  • bharanta: This means "carrying" or "bearing." It suggests the act of taking responsibility or supporting something.
  • viśve: This translates to "all" or "universal." It indicates inclusivity and the idea that the message applies to everyone.
  • baliṁ: This word means "sacrifice" or "offering." It reflects the concept of giving back or dedicating something valuable for a higher purpose.
  • svar ṇaḥ: This translates to "of the golden" or "of the divine." It signifies something precious, often associated with divinity or higher ideals.

The mantra "goṣu praśastiṁ vaneṣu dhiṣe bharanta viśve baliṁ svar ṇaḥ" can be interpreted as a call to recognize and praise the abundance found in nature, particularly in the context of cattle and forests. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in our actions and the responsibility we carry towards the environment and all living beings. The mention of sacrifice suggests that we should be willing to give back to nature and the community, acknowledging the interconnectedness of all life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Agriculture and Animal Husbandry: Farmers can take inspiration from this mantra to honor the role of cattle in agriculture, recognizing their contribution to sustenance and livelihood.
  • Environmental Conservation: The reference to forests encourages individuals and communities to protect natural resources, promoting sustainable practices that benefit both nature and humanity.
  • Community Service: The idea of sacrifice can inspire people to engage in community service, giving back to society and supporting those in need.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of recognizing the abundance in our lives, the wisdom required to manage it, and the responsibility we have to give back to nature and our communities. It encourages a harmonious relationship with the environment and promotes values of gratitude, respect, and service.
