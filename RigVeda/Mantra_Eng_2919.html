पुषण्वते ते चक्रिमा करम्भं हरिवते हर्यश्वाय धनाः | अपूपमद्धि सगणो मरुद्भिः सोमं पिब वृत्रहां शूर विद्वान् ||
pūṣaṇvate te cakṛmā karambhaṁ harivate haryaśvāya dhānāḥ | apūpam addhi sagaṇo marudbhiḥ somam piba vṛtrahā śūra vidvān ||
  • pūṣaṇvate: This term refers to the divine aspect of nourishment and sustenance. In real life, it can be likened to the role of a caregiver or a provider, such as a parent or a mentor, who nurtures and supports others.
  • te: This means "your," indicating a personal connection to the divine or the higher power being addressed.
  • cakṛmā: This word signifies "to create" or "to make." In a practical sense, it can relate to the act of innovation or creativity in various fields, such as art, science, or technology.
  • karambhaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the actions" or "the deeds." It emphasizes the importance of actions in achieving goals, similar to how hard work and dedication lead to success in personal and professional life.
  • harivate: This refers to the quality of being joyful or bringing happiness. In everyday life, this can be seen in acts of kindness or community service that uplift others.
  • haryaśvāya: This term can be understood as "to the one who is swift" or "the one who brings prosperity." It can be related to individuals or entities that drive progress, such as entrepreneurs or leaders who inspire change.
  • dhānāḥ: This means "wealth" or "prosperity." It highlights the significance of abundance in life, which can be both material and spiritual, reflecting the importance of balance in our pursuits.
  • apūpam: This term signifies "the unblemished" or "the pure." It can be associated with integrity and ethical behavior in personal and professional relationships.
  • addhi: This means "indeed" or "certainly," reinforcing the certainty of the statements being made.
  • sagaṇo: This refers to "the group" or "the collective." It emphasizes the importance of community and collaboration in achieving common goals.
  • marudbhiḥ: This term relates to the "winds" or "forces of nature," symbolizing the dynamic and ever-changing aspects of life that we must navigate.
  • somam: This refers to "the nectar" or "the essence," often associated with spiritual or divine experiences that nourish the soul.
  • piba: This means "to drink," suggesting the act of consuming or embracing these divine qualities in our lives.
  • vṛtrahā: This term means "the slayer of Vritra," symbolizing the overcoming of obstacles and challenges in life.
  • śūra: This means "the brave" or "the courageous," highlighting the importance of bravery in facing life's challenges.
  • vidvān: This refers to "the wise" or "the knowledgeable," emphasizing the value of wisdom and understanding in our actions and decisions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine nourishment, creativity, and the importance of community and integrity in our actions. It serves as a reminder to embrace joy, pursue prosperity, and overcome challenges with courage and wisdom. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, community building, and professional endeavors, encouraging individuals to act with purpose and integrity while fostering a supportive environment for growth and success.
