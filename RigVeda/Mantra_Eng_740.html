विष्ववेदसो रयिभिः समोकसः सम्मिश्लासस तविषीभिर विरप्शिनः |अस्तारा इषुं दधिरे गभस्त्योः अनन्तशुष्मा वृषखादयो नरः ||
viśvavedaso rayibhiḥ samokasaḥ sammiślāsas taviṣībhir virapśinaḥ | astāra iṣuṁ dadhire gabhastyor anantaśuṣmā vṛṣakhādayo naraḥ ||
  • viśvavedaso: This term can be broken down into "viśva" meaning "universal" or "all" and "veda" meaning "knowledge" or "wisdom". Together, it refers to the universal knowledge or wisdom that encompasses all aspects of life.
  • rayibhiḥ: This word translates to "with wealth" or "with resources". It signifies the abundance and prosperity that one can attain through knowledge and wisdom.
  • samokasaḥ: This term means "together" or "in harmony". It emphasizes the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving goals.
  • sammiślāsas: This can be interpreted as "those who are mixed" or "those who are diverse". It highlights the value of diversity in thoughts, ideas, and approaches.
  • taviṣībhir: This word refers to "with strength" or "with power". It suggests that wisdom and knowledge should be accompanied by strength and determination.
  • virapśinaḥ: This term can be understood as "those who are wise" or "those who possess insight". It points to the importance of wisdom in guiding actions and decisions.
  • astāra: This means "to spread" or "to extend". It signifies the act of expanding one's horizons and knowledge.
  • iṣuṁ: This word translates to "arrows". In a metaphorical sense, it can represent goals or aspirations that one aims to achieve.
  • dadhire: This means "they have taken" or "they have held". It indicates the act of grasping or achieving something significant.
  • gabhastyor: This term refers to "of the heavens" or "of the sky". It symbolizes higher aspirations and the pursuit of lofty goals.
  • anantaśuṣmā: This can be interpreted as "infinite brilliance" or "endless radiance". It signifies the limitless potential of knowledge and wisdom.
  • vṛṣakhādayo: This term refers to "the strong" or "the powerful". It emphasizes the importance of strength in achieving one's goals.
  • naraḥ: This means "human" or "man". It signifies the role of humanity in the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of universal knowledge, collaboration, diversity, strength, and wisdom in achieving one's goals. It suggests that by coming together and utilizing our collective strengths and insights, we can aspire to reach higher aspirations and realize our full potential. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork in professional settings, community building, and personal development. The mantra serves as a reminder that true success is not just about individual achievements but also about the collective journey towards enlightenment and prosperity.
