युयं ह रत्नं मगवत्सु धत्त svardṛśa ऋभुक्षणो अमृतम् |सं यज्ञेषु स्वधावन्तः पिबध्वं वि नो राधांसी मतीभिर दयध्वम् ||
yūyaṁ ha ratnam maghavatsu dhattha svardṛśa ṛbhukṣaṇo amṛktam | saṁ yajñeṣu svadhāvantaḥ pibadhvaṁ vi no rādhāṁsi matibhir dayadhvam ||
  • yūyaṁ - This word translates to "you all" or "you together," indicating a collective group.
  • ha - A particle that emphasizes the statement, often translated as "indeed" or "certainly."
  • ratnam - Meaning "jewel" or "treasure," it symbolizes something precious or valuable.
  • maghavatsu - Refers to "the powerful" or "the mighty," often associated with deities or those with great strength.
  • dhattha - This means "you bestow" or "you give," indicating an act of generosity or sharing.
  • svardṛśa - Translates to "of the heavenly vision" or "of divine sight," suggesting a higher understanding or perspective.
  • ṛbhukṣaṇo - This word can mean "the one who is wise" or "the one who knows the truth," emphasizing wisdom and knowledge.
  • amṛktam - Meaning "immortal" or "eternal," it signifies something that transcends the physical realm.
  • saṁ - A prefix meaning "together" or "with," indicating unity.
  • yajñeṣu - Refers to "in sacrifices" or "in offerings," highlighting the importance of rituals and devotion.
  • svadhāvantaḥ - Meaning "those who are self-motivated" or "those who act with intention," it emphasizes personal agency.
  • pibadhvaṁ - Translates to "drink" or "consume," suggesting the act of taking in or absorbing something valuable.
  • vi - A particle that can mean "for us" or "among us," indicating a sense of community.
  • rādhāṁsi - Meaning "prosperity" or "wealth," it signifies abundance and well-being.
  • matibhir - Refers to "with thoughts" or "with intentions," emphasizing the role of mindset and consciousness.
  • dayadhvam - Meaning "show compassion" or "be kind," it calls for empathy and understanding among individuals.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of community, wisdom, and generosity. It encourages individuals to come together, share their treasures, and act with intention and compassion. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork in workplaces, community service, and family gatherings where collective efforts lead to shared prosperity. The mantra serves as a reminder that through unity and kindness, we can achieve greater heights and foster a supportive environment for all.
