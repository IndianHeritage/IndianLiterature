ते राया ते सुवीर्यैः ससवांसो वि श्रिण्विरे |ये अग्ना दधिरे दु्वः ||
te rāyā te suvīryaiḥ sasavāṁso vi śṛṇvire | ye agnā dadhire duvaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • te: you (plural)
    • rāyā: wealth, prosperity
    • te: your (plural)
    • suvīryaiḥ: with good strength, valor
    • sasavāṁso: those who have achieved, who have attained
    • vi: indeed, truly
    • śṛṇvire: they listen, they hear
    • ye: those who
    • agnā: fire, knowledge, wisdom
    • dadhire: they hold, they possess
    • duvaḥ: the two, the duality
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the collective strength and prosperity of a group.
    • It emphasizes the importance of listening and understanding, suggesting that true wealth comes from shared knowledge and wisdom.
    • The reference to "fire" symbolizes knowledge and enlightenment, indicating that those who possess wisdom are truly prosperous.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team that collaborates effectively and shares knowledge is more likely to succeed than one that works in silos.
    • In communities, those who come together to share resources and support each other often thrive, demonstrating the power of collective strength.
    • Educational institutions that foster an environment of open dialogue and shared learning create a more enriching experience for students, leading to greater overall success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity, shared knowledge, and collective strength in achieving prosperity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, where collaboration and understanding lead to success.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that true wealth is not just material but also lies in the strength of our connections and the wisdom we share.
