यद वृत्रं तव चाशनिं वज्रेण समयोधयः |अहिं इन्द्र जिघांसतो दिवि ते बद्बधि शवोऽर्चन्न अनु स्वराज्यम् ||
yad vṛtraṁ tava cāśaniṁ vajreṇa samayodhayaḥ | ahim indra jighāṁsato divi te badbadhe śavo 'rcann anu svarājyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad - that which
    • vṛtraṁ - Vritra (a serpent or dragon, symbolizing obstacles)
    • tava - your
    • cāśaniṁ - lightning or weapon
    • vajreṇa - with the thunderbolt
    • samayodhayaḥ - engage in battle
    • ahim - the serpent (Vritra)
    • indra - Indra (the king of gods)
    • jighāṁsato - desiring to kill
    • divi - in the heavens
    • te - your
    • badbadhe - to be struck
    • śavo - the dead
    • 'rcann - having been worshipped
    • anu - after
    • svarājyam - sovereignty or rule
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, to engage in battle against Vritra, who represents obstacles and challenges.
    • The mention of "vajreṇa" (thunderbolt) symbolizes the power and strength needed to overcome these challenges.
    • The phrase "jighāṁsato" indicates a desire to eliminate these obstacles, emphasizing the importance of determination in facing adversities.
    • The reference to "divi" suggests that this battle is not just physical but also spiritual, taking place in the heavens, representing higher consciousness.
    • The concluding part of the mantra speaks to the aftermath of worship and the attainment of sovereignty, indicating that overcoming challenges leads to success and authority.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal life, individuals often face challenges such as career obstacles or personal struggles. Just as Indra battles Vritra, one must confront these challenges head-on with determination and strength.
    • In a professional setting, a team may encounter a significant project hurdle. The mantra serves as a reminder to unite and tackle the problem collectively, using the "thunderbolt" of teamwork and collaboration.
    • In a broader societal context, communities facing natural disasters or economic hardships can draw inspiration from this mantra, rallying together to overcome adversity and restore stability.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of courage, strength, and unity in overcoming obstacles. It serves as a powerful reminder that challenges are a part of life, and with determination and collective effort, one can achieve sovereignty over their circumstances.
    • In real life, the principles embodied in this mantra can be applied in various situations, encouraging individuals and communities to confront their challenges with resilience and faith.
