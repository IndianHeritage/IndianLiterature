कुविद वृषाण्यन्तीभ्यः पुनानो गर्भं आदधत् |याः शुक्रं दुधते पयः ||
kuvid vṛṣaṇyantībhyaḥ punāno garbham ādadhat | yāḥ śukraṁ duhate payaḥ ||
  • kuvid: This term can be interpreted as "the one who knows" or "the wise one." It signifies knowledge and understanding, which are essential for making informed decisions in life.
  • vṛṣaṇyantībhyaḥ: This word refers to "the powerful" or "the strong." It highlights the importance of strength, both physical and mental, in overcoming challenges.
  • punāno: This means "purifying" or "renewing." It suggests the idea of cleansing oneself from impurities, whether they are physical, emotional, or spiritual.
  • garbham: This translates to "the womb" or "the seed." It symbolizes potential, growth, and the beginning of new life or ideas.
  • ādadhat: This means "to hold" or "to support." It indicates the act of nurturing and sustaining something valuable.
  • yāḥ: This word means "those who." It refers to a group or collective, emphasizing community and shared experiences.
  • śukraṁ: This translates to "pure" or "bright." It represents clarity, positivity, and the essence of goodness.
  • duhate: This means "to extract" or "to draw out." It signifies the act of bringing forth something valuable from within.
  • payaḥ: This translates to "milk." In many cultures, milk symbolizes nourishment, sustenance, and the nurturing aspect of life.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the wise and strong to purify and nurture the potential within, drawing out the pure essence of life and goodness. It emphasizes the importance of knowledge, strength, and community in fostering growth and renewal.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In education, it encourages students to seek knowledge and wisdom, nurturing their potential to become strong individuals who contribute positively to society.
  • In personal development, it serves as a reminder to cleanse oneself of negativity and to focus on nurturing positive qualities and relationships.
  • In community building, it highlights the importance of collective strength and support in fostering a nurturing environment for growth and development.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the interplay of knowledge, strength, and nurturing. It serves as a guiding principle for individuals and communities to purify themselves, support one another, and draw out the best qualities in life. By applying these teachings, one can foster a more harmonious and productive existence, both personally and collectively.
