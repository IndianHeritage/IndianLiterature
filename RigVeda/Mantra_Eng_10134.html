स इद्भोजो यो गृहे ददाति अन्नकामाय चरते कृशाय |अरम अस्मै भवति यामहूताः उतापरीषु कृणुते सखायम् ||
sa id bhojo yo gṛhave dadāty annakāmāya carate kṛśāya | aram asmai bhavati yāmahūtā utāparīṣu kṛṇute sakhāyam ||
  • sa id bhojo: This phrase can be translated as "he who is a benefactor" or "he who is generous." It emphasizes the importance of generosity and support in human relationships.
  • yo gṛhave dadāty: This means "who gives to the household." It highlights the act of providing for one's family or community, which is a fundamental aspect of social responsibility.
  • annakāmāya: This translates to "for the desire of food." It signifies the basic human need for sustenance and the importance of fulfilling this need for oneself and others.
  • carate kṛśāya: This can be interpreted as "acts for the benefit of the weak." It calls for compassion and support for those who are less fortunate or in need of assistance.
  • aram asmai bhavati: This means "to him, it becomes a blessing." It suggests that acts of kindness and generosity lead to positive outcomes and blessings in life.
  • yāmahūtā: This term refers to "the one who is invoked." It implies that there is a divine or spiritual aspect to the acts of giving and support.
  • utāparīṣu: This translates to "in the company of friends." It emphasizes the importance of community and friendship in the act of giving and receiving support.
  • kṛṇute sakhāyam: This means "he creates friendship." It highlights the idea that generosity fosters relationships and builds bonds among people.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of generosity, compassion, and community support. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Volunteering at local shelters or food banks to help those in need.
  • Supporting friends and family during difficult times, ensuring that their basic needs are met.
  • Engaging in community service projects that aim to uplift the less fortunate.
  • Creating a culture of sharing and support within neighborhoods or workplaces.

Ultimately, the mantra teaches that acts of kindness not only benefit those in need but also enrich the lives of the giver, fostering a sense of community and friendship that is essential for a harmonious society.
