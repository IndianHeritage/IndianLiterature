ददी रेक्णस तन्वे ददिर वसु ददिर वाजेषु पुरुहूत वाजिनम् |नूनम अथ ||
dadī rekṇas tanve dadir vasu dadir vājeṣu puruhūta vājinam | nūnam atha ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May the wealth (vasu) that is in the body (tanve) be bestowed upon us; may we be endowed with strength (vājeṣu) and may we be victorious (vājinam) in our endeavors." The term "puruhūta" refers to the many offerings or prayers made to the divine.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • dadī: to give or bestow.
    • rekṇas: wealth or riches.
    • tanve: in the body or physical form.
    • vasu: wealth or resources.
    • vājeṣu: in strength or power.
    • puruhūta: many offerings or prayers.
    • vājinam: victorious or strong.
    • nūnam: indeed or surely.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied when a team seeks to achieve success in a project. The "wealth" can represent the resources and skills of the team members, and the "strength" can symbolize their collective effort and determination.
    • In personal life, an individual may recite this mantra when embarking on a new venture, such as starting a business or pursuing higher education, seeking divine blessings for success and prosperity.
    • In community gatherings, this mantra can be invoked to foster unity and strength among members, encouraging them to work together towards common goals, thereby enhancing the collective wealth of the community.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine blessings for wealth, strength, and victory. It emphasizes the importance of both material and spiritual resources in achieving success. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal endeavors, professional projects, or community efforts, invoking this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek support and strength from the divine while also recognizing the value of collaboration and shared resources. It encourages individuals and groups to strive for success with the understanding that true wealth encompasses not just material gains but also the strength of character and unity.
