ऋतेन ऋतं नियतम् ईळा आ गोर आमā सच्चा मधुमत् पक्वम् अग्ने |कृष्णा सती रुशता धासिनैषा जामर्येण पयसा पीपाय ||
ṛtena ṛtaṁ niyatam īḻa ā gor āmā sacā madhumat pakvam agne | kṛṣṇā satī ruśatā dhāsinaiṣā jāmaryeṇa payasā pīpāya ||
  • ṛtena: This word translates to "by truth" or "through the principle of order." In real life, this can be understood as the importance of honesty and integrity in our actions and decisions.
  • ṛtaṁ: Refers to "the cosmic order" or "truth." It signifies the natural law that governs the universe, akin to the laws of nature that we observe in science.
  • niyatam: Means "fixed" or "constant." This suggests that truth and order are unchanging principles that we can rely on, much like the laws of physics that govern our world.
  • īḻa: Translates to "desire" or "intention." This highlights the importance of having a clear and positive intention behind our actions, similar to setting goals in our personal and professional lives.
  • ā: A preposition that can mean "towards" or "to." It indicates direction or purpose in our pursuits.
  • gor: Refers to "the cow," which is often a symbol of nourishment and abundance in Vedic literature. In modern terms, this can represent the resources we rely on for sustenance.
  • āmā: Means "mother" or "nurturer." This emphasizes the nurturing aspect of life, akin to the care and support we receive from our families and communities.
  • sacā: Translates to "with truth" or "with goodness." This reinforces the idea that our actions should be aligned with truth and virtue.
  • madhumat: Means "sweet" or "pleasant." This can be related to the joy and satisfaction that comes from living a life of integrity and purpose.
  • pakvam: Refers to "ripe" or "mature." This suggests the importance of growth and development in our lives, much like the process of maturing in our personal and professional journeys.
  • agne: This is a reference to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, this can be seen in the way we harness energy for progress and innovation.
  • kṛṣṇā: Refers to "black" or "dark," often symbolizing the unknown or the challenges we face. This can relate to the obstacles we encounter in our lives.
  • satī: Means "truthful" or "virtuous." This emphasizes the value of maintaining integrity in our character.
  • ruśatā: Translates to "with strength" or "power." This can be related to the resilience we need to overcome challenges.
  • dhāsinaiṣā: Refers to "this servant" or "this devotee." It signifies humility and the importance of serving others in our communities.
  • jāmaryeṇa: Means "with the essence of life" or "with vitality." This highlights the importance of living life to the fullest and embracing our experiences.
  • payasā: Refers to "milk," symbolizing nourishment and sustenance. This can be related to the care we provide to ourselves and others.
  • pīpāya: Means "to drink" or "to consume." This signifies the act of taking in nourishment, both physically and spiritually.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of living in accordance with truth and cosmic order, nurturing our intentions, and serving others with integrity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in our personal relationships, professional conduct, and community involvement. By embodying these principles, we can create a harmonious and fulfilling life, contributing positively to the world around us.
