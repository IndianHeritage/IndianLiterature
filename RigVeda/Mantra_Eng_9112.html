इयं सा भूयाऽउषसामिव क्षा यद्ध क्षुमन्तः शवसा समायन | अस्य स्तुतिं जारितुर भिक्षमाणाऽआ नः शग्मास उप यन्तु वाजाः ||
iyaṁ sā bhūyā uṣasām iva kṣā yad dha kṣumantaḥ śavasā samāyan | asya stutiṁ jaritur bhikṣamāṇā ā naḥ śagmāsa upa yantu vājāḥ ||
  • iyaṁ sā bhūyā uṣasām iva kṣā: This phrase can be translated to "This is like the dawn, may it be more abundant." Here, the dawn symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and enlightenment. In real life, this can be related to the start of a new day or a new phase in life, such as starting a new job or moving to a new city.
  • yad dha kṣumantaḥ śavasā samāyan: This translates to "Those who are hungry gather together." This line emphasizes the importance of community and support among individuals facing challenges. For example, in times of crisis, people often come together to support one another, such as during natural disasters or community service events.
  • asya stutiṁ jaritur bhikṣamāṇā: This means "Those who are seeking sustenance praise this." It highlights the act of gratitude and recognition for the resources and support one receives. In everyday life, this can be seen in practices of gratitude, such as giving thanks before meals or acknowledging the help of friends and family.
  • ā naḥ śagmāsa upa yantu vājāḥ: This translates to "May the strong ones come to us." This line calls for the arrival of strength and support in our lives. In practical terms, this can refer to seeking guidance from mentors, friends, or community leaders who can provide strength and encouragement during difficult times.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the themes of community, support, gratitude, and the hope for new beginnings. In real life, it serves as a reminder of the importance of coming together in times of need, appreciating the resources we have, and seeking strength from those around us. Whether in personal struggles or collective challenges, this mantra encourages us to foster connections and support one another, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and resilient society.
