तं गूर्तयो नेमान्निषः परीनसः समुद्रं न संचराणे सनीष्यवः |पतिं दक्षस्य विदथस्य नू सहो गिरिं न वेना अधि रोह तेजसा ||
taṁ gūrtayo nemanniṣaḥ parīṇasaḥ samudraṁ na saṁcaraṇe saniṣyavaḥ | patiṁ dakṣasya vidathasya nū saho giriṁ na venā adhi roha tejasā ||
  • taṁ gūrtayo nemanniṣaḥ parīṇasaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the divine for guidance and protection. The term "gūrtayo" refers to the wise or knowledgeable, while "nemanniṣaḥ" suggests a state of being free from obstacles. In real life, this can be seen as seeking wisdom and clarity in decision-making, especially in challenging situations.
  • samudraṁ na saṁcaraṇe saniṣyavaḥ: Here, "samudraṁ" refers to the ocean, symbolizing vastness and depth. "Na saṁcaraṇe" implies a steady path or journey. This can be related to navigating through life's challenges, much like a sailor who must understand the ocean's currents to reach their destination safely.
  • patiṁ dakṣasya vidathasya nū saho: "Patiṁ" means lord or master, while "dakṣasya" refers to skill or capability. This line emphasizes the importance of having a capable leader or guide in our lives. In a real-world context, this could relate to having mentors or leaders who inspire and guide us in our personal or professional journeys.
  • giriṁ na venā adhi roha tejasā: "Giriṁ" refers to a mountain, symbolizing strength and stability. "Adhi roha tejasā" suggests rising with brilliance or energy. This can be interpreted as striving to achieve greatness and overcoming obstacles with determination and vigor, much like climbing a mountain requires effort and resilience.

  • Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking wisdom, guidance, and strength in our lives. It highlights the importance of having knowledgeable mentors, navigating challenges with clarity, and striving for greatness with determination. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership, and overcoming life's obstacles. By embodying the principles expressed in this mantra, individuals can cultivate a more purposeful and fulfilling life.
