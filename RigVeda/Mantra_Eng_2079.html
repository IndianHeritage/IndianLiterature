यः सुनीतो ददाशुषे 'जुर्यो जारयन्न अरिम् |चारुप्रतीक आहुतः ||
yaḥ sunītho dadāśuṣe 'juryo jarayann arim | cārupratīka āhutaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaḥ: who
    • sunītho: well-behaved, disciplined
    • dadāśuṣe: to the one who gives, bestows
    • 'juryo: the one who is strong, powerful
    • jarayann: causing to grow old, aging
    • arim: enemy
    • cārupratīka: beautiful, pleasing appearance
    • āhutaḥ: invoked, called upon
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a disciplined individual who bestows strength and power while also being capable of aging or overcoming adversities.
    • The term "arim" refers to enemies or obstacles, suggesting that the well-behaved person can confront and overcome challenges.
    • The phrase "cārupratīka āhutaḥ" indicates that this individual is not only strong but also possesses a pleasing demeanor, making them admirable and worthy of invocation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a manager who is disciplined and fair can inspire their team to perform better, overcoming challenges together.
    • A community leader who is well-respected and invokes positive change can help address social issues, acting as a unifying force against adversities.
    • In personal relationships, a friend who is supportive and strong can help you face life's challenges, embodying the qualities described in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of discipline, strength, and a positive demeanor in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that invoking such qualities in ourselves can lead to personal growth and the ability to face adversities effectively.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can enhance our relationships, professional lives, and community interactions, fostering resilience and harmony.
