महत् तदुल्बं स्थविरं तदासीद येनाविष्टितः प्रविवेशिथापः |विश्वा अपश्यद बहुधा ते अग्ने जातवेदस तन् वो देव एकः ||
mahat tad ulbaṁ sthaviraṁ tad āsīd yenāviṣṭitaḥ praviveśithāpaḥ | viśvā apaśyad bahudhā te agne jātavedas tanvo deva ekaḥ ||
  • mahat tad ulbaṁ sthaviraṁ: This phrase can be translated to "that great, vast, and ancient one." Here, "mahat" signifies greatness, "ulbaṁ" refers to vastness or expansiveness, and "sthaviraṁ" indicates something that is ancient or enduring. In real life, this can be likened to the vastness of the universe or the enduring nature of wisdom that transcends time.
  • tad āsīd yenāviṣṭitaḥ praviveśithāpaḥ: This translates to "that which existed, into which the wise entered." This suggests a state of existence that is foundational and into which enlightened beings or wise individuals delve. An example could be the pursuit of knowledge and understanding in various fields, where scholars and thinkers explore the depths of their subjects.
  • viśvā apaśyad bahudhā: This means "the all-encompassing (or universal) was seen in many forms." It reflects the idea that the universal truth or reality can manifest in various ways. In everyday life, this can be observed in the diversity of cultures, beliefs, and perspectives that exist in the world, all pointing towards a common underlying truth.
  • te agne jātavedas: This translates to "O Agni, the one who knows all births." Agni, the fire god, symbolizes knowledge, transformation, and the divine spark within. In practical terms, this can relate to the idea of enlightenment and the pursuit of knowledge that leads to personal and collective growth.
  • tanvo deva ekaḥ: This means "the divine is one in many forms." It emphasizes the unity of the divine presence despite its diverse manifestations. In real life, this can be seen in the various religions and spiritual practices that, while different in form, seek the same ultimate truth or connection to the divine.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound philosophical concepts about existence, knowledge, and the divine. It signifies the importance of seeking wisdom and understanding the interconnectedness of all things. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue knowledge, embrace diversity, and recognize the unity underlying different beliefs and practices. It serves as a reminder that while we may perceive the world in various forms, there is a singular truth that binds us all together, encouraging harmony and respect among different cultures and ideologies.
