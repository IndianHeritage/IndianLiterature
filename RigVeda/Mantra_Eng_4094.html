मन्त्र: सप्त मे सप्त शाकिन एकम्-एकā शतā ददुः | यमुनायाम् अधि श्रुतम् उद राधो गव्यं मृजे निराधो अश्व्यम् मृजे ||
sapta me sapta śākina ekam-ekā śatā daduḥ | yamunāyām adhi śrutam ud rādho gavyam mṛje ni rādho aśvyam mṛje ||
  • Mantra: "sapta me sapta śākina ekam-ekā śatā daduḥ | yamunāyām adhi śrutam ud rādho gavyam mṛje ni rādho aśvyam mṛje ||"
  • Translation: "Seven (sapta) are my seven (sapta) powers; each one (ekam-ekā) gives a hundred (śatā). In the Yamuna (yamunāyām), I have heard (adhi śrutam) the call; may the cows (gavyam) be freed (mṛje) from the yoke (ni rādho) and may the horses (aśvyam) be freed (mṛje)."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • Sapta: Seven - symbolizes completeness and the totality of existence.
    • Śākina: Powers or energies - refers to the various forces that govern life.
    • Ekam-ekā: Each one - emphasizes individuality within the collective.
    • Śatā: Hundred - represents abundance and prosperity.
    • Yamunā: A sacred river in India - symbolizes purity and the flow of life.
    • Adhi śrutam: Heard or understood - signifies awareness and knowledge.
    • Gavyam: Cows - often represent wealth and nourishment in Vedic literature.
    • Mṛje: To free or liberate - indicates the act of releasing burdens.
    • Aśvyam: Horses - symbolize strength and power.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of "seven powers" can be related to the seven chakras in yoga, which represent different energies within the body that contribute to overall well-being.
    • The idea of freeing cows and horses can be seen in modern agricultural practices where animals are treated with care and respect, promoting ethical farming.
    • The Yamuna River serves as a reminder of the importance of environmental conservation, as it is a vital water source that supports millions of lives.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of individual powers and the collective abundance they can create.
    • It highlights the importance of awareness and the need to liberate ourselves from burdens, whether they be physical, emotional, or spiritual.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to recognize their unique strengths and contribute positively to their communities, while also advocating for the welfare of animals and the environment.
