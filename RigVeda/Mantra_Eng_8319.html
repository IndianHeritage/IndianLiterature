मन्त्र: एते सोमाः पवमानास इन्द्रं रथाऽ इव प्र ययुः सात्मिमच्छ || सुताः पवित्रमति यन्त्यव्यं हित्वī ववृं हरितो वृष्टिमच्छ ||
ete somāḥ pavamānāsa indraṁ rathā iva pra yayuḥ sātim accha | sutāḥ pavitram ati yanty avyaṁ hitvī vavriṁ harito vṛṣṭim accha ||
  • Translation: "These pressed juices (soma) flow towards Indra like chariots; they bring forth strength and purity, and they go beyond the limits, bestowing the rain of prosperity."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ete somāḥ: "these pressed juices" - Refers to the sacred drink, soma, which is believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration.
    • pavamānāsa: "flowing" - Indicates the movement or the act of the soma being offered to the deities.
    • indraṁ: "to Indra" - Indra is the king of the gods, associated with rain, thunder, and strength.
    • rathā iva: "like chariots" - Suggests the powerful and swift nature of the offerings, akin to chariots that carry warriors into battle.
    • pra yayuḥ: "they go forth" - Implies the act of sending forth the offerings to the divine.
    • sātim accha: "bring forth strength" - Refers to the empowerment and vitality that the offerings bestow.
    • sutāḥ: "the poured" - Refers to the soma that has been prepared and offered.
    • pavitram: "pure" - Highlights the purity of the offerings, essential in Vedic rituals.
    • ati yanty: "they go beyond" - Suggests transcending ordinary limits, reaching for the divine.
    • avyam: "without hindrance" - Indicates the unobstructed nature of the offerings reaching the divine.
    • hitvī: "having abandoned" - Refers to letting go of obstacles or impurities.
    • vavriṁ: "bestowing" - The act of giving or granting blessings.
    • harito: "of the green" - Symbolizes fertility, growth, and prosperity.
    • vṛṣṭim accha: "the rain of prosperity" - Refers to the blessings and abundance that come from the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the invocation of rain through rituals can be seen as a way to ensure a good harvest, much like how the mantra calls for the blessings of Indra for prosperity.
    • In personal development, the idea of "bringing forth strength" can be applied to individuals seeking motivation and empowerment in their lives, akin to how the soma is offered to gain divine support.
    • In community gatherings, the act of sharing food and drink can symbolize unity and the collective seeking of blessings, reflecting the communal aspect of the soma offerings.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of purity, strength, and the divine connection in seeking prosperity. It serves as a reminder that through collective efforts and offerings, individuals and communities can transcend their limitations and invite abundance into their lives. In modern contexts, this can be applied in various ways, from personal empowerment to community rituals aimed at fostering unity and growth.
