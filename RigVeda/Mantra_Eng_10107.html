एकः सुपर्णः स समुद्रम आविवेश स इदं विश्वं भुवनं वि चष्टे |तम पाकेन मनसापश्यं अन्तितस तम माता रेझि स उ रेझि मातरम् ||
ekaḥ suparṇaḥ sa samudram ā viveśa sa idaṁ viśvam bhuvanaṁ vi caṣṭe | tam pākena manasāpaśyam antitas tam mātā reḻhi sa u reḻhi mātaram ||
  • Translation: "One bird (soul) has entered the ocean; it surveys this entire world and the realms beyond. With a pure mind, it perceives the ultimate truth; it is the mother, the source of all."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ekaḥ: one
    • suparṇaḥ: bird (often interpreted as the soul or consciousness)
    • sa: that
    • samudram: ocean (symbolizing the vastness of existence)
    • ā viveśa: has entered
    • sa idaṁ: this
    • viśvam: entire world
    • bhuvanaṁ: realms or worlds
    • vi caṣṭe: surveys or observes
    • tam: that (referring to the ultimate truth)
    • pākena: with purity
    • manasā: mind
    • apaśyam: perceives
    • antitas: ultimate or highest
    • tam: that
    • mātā: mother
    • reḻhi: source or sustainer
    • sa: that
    • u: and
    • reḻhi: sustainer
    • mātaram: mother (again, emphasizing the nurturing aspect)
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of the "one bird" can be likened to the individual soul or consciousness that exists within each person, navigating through the vast ocean of life experiences.
    • In a practical sense, this mantra encourages individuals to seek a deeper understanding of their existence and the world around them, much like a person who reflects on their life choices and the impact they have on others.
    • The idea of "purity of mind" can be related to mindfulness practices, where individuals strive to maintain clarity and focus in their thoughts, leading to better decision-making and emotional well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking truth and understanding in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that, despite the vastness of the world, there is a singular essence that binds us all, encouraging us to nurture our minds and spirits.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal growth, spiritual exploration, and fostering compassion towards others, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
