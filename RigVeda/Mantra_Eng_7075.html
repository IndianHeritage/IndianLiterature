दश मह्यम पौत्रकृतः सहस्राद दस्यवे वृकः |नित्याद रायो अमंहता ||
daśa mahyam pautakrataḥ sahasrā dasyave vṛkaḥ | nityād rāyo amaṁhata ||
  • Mantra: "daśa mahyam pautakrataḥ sahasrā dasyave vṛkaḥ | nityād rāyo amaṁhata ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • daśa: ten
    • mahyam: to me
    • pautakrataḥ: of the descendants (or progeny)
    • sahasrā: thousands
    • dasyave: of the one who is powerful or strong
    • vṛkaḥ: wolf
    • nityād: always
    • rāyo: wealth or prosperity
    • amaṁhata: unassailable or invincible
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of receiving tenfold blessings or wealth from the descendants of a powerful entity, symbolized by the wolf.
    • The wolf can represent strength, cunning, and survival, qualities that are essential in both nature and human endeavors.
    • The phrase "nityād rāyo amaṁhata" suggests that this wealth or prosperity is not only abundant but also invulnerable, indicating a lasting and secure form of success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be interpreted as a call for entrepreneurs to seek out strong partnerships (the "wolf") that can lead to exponential growth (the "tenfold blessings").
    • In personal life, it can signify the importance of nurturing relationships with family and descendants, as they can provide support and prosperity in times of need.
    • In a broader societal context, it can be seen as a reminder of the strength found in community and collaboration, where collective efforts lead to greater achievements.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, prosperity, and the importance of lineage and community in achieving success.
    • It serves as a reminder that true wealth comes not just from individual efforts but also from the relationships and networks we build.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more fulfilling and prosperous existence, where one recognizes the value of collaboration and the strength found in unity.
