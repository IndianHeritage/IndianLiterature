अस्मे वत्सं परि षन्तं न विन्दन्न इच्छन्तो विश्वे अमृताः अमूराः |श्रमयुवः पदव्यो धियं धास तस्थुः पदे परमेषु आग्नेः ||
asme vatsam pari ṣantaṁ na vindann icchanto viśve amṛtā amūrāḥ | śramayuvaḥ padavyo dhiyaṁdhās tasthuḥ pade parame cārv agneḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asme: for us
    • vatsam: the calf or the young one
    • pari: around
    • ṣantaṁ: the peaceful or the tranquil
    • na vindann: not finding
    • icchanto: desiring
    • viśve: all
    • amṛtā: immortal
    • amūrāḥ: without form
    • śramayuvaḥ: those who are striving
    • padavyo: paths
    • dhiyaṁdhās: with wisdom
    • tasthuḥ: may they stand
    • pade: in the place
    • parame: the highest
    • cārv: of the fire
    • agneḥ: O Agni (the divine fire)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the quest for peace and immortality.
    • It emphasizes the importance of striving for wisdom and understanding in our lives.
    • The reference to Agni symbolizes the divine energy that fuels our aspirations and desires.
    • It suggests that while we may seek peace and immortality, we must also recognize the efforts and paths we take to achieve these goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, employees may strive for a peaceful work environment while seeking to achieve their career goals. This mantra reminds them to focus on collaboration and wisdom in their efforts.
    • In personal relationships, individuals may desire harmony and understanding. The mantra encourages them to engage in open communication and strive for emotional intelligence.
    • In spiritual practices, the pursuit of inner peace and enlightenment can be likened to the quest for immortality. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek wisdom and understanding on this journey.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for striving towards peace and wisdom in all aspects of life.
    • It highlights the importance of recognizing the paths we take and the efforts we make in our quest for higher understanding and fulfillment.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, professional development, and spiritual journeys, encouraging individuals to seek balance and enlightenment.
