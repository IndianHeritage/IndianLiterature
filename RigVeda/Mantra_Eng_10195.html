नाके सुपर्णम् उप यत पतन्तं हृदा वेनन्तो अभ्य अचक्षत त्वा |हिरण्यपक्षं वरुणस्य दूतं यमस्य योनौ शकुनं भुराण्यम् ||
nāke suparṇam upa yat patantaṁ hṛdā venanto abhy acakṣata tvā | hiraṇyapakṣaṁ varuṇasya dūtaṁ yamasya yonau śakunam bhuraṇyum ||
  • nāke suparṇam: This phrase refers to the "beautiful bird" or "eagle" in the sky. In many cultures, eagles symbolize strength, freedom, and a higher perspective. For example, in the United States, the bald eagle represents freedom and independence.
  • upa yat patantaṁ: This means "approaching as it descends." It signifies the act of coming down from a higher place, which can be interpreted as gaining wisdom or insight from a higher perspective. In real life, this can be likened to a leader who, after gaining knowledge, returns to guide their community.
  • hṛdā venanto abhy acakṣata tvā: This translates to "with the heart, they see you." It emphasizes the importance of perception and understanding that comes from the heart rather than just the mind. In practice, this can be seen in empathetic leaders who make decisions based on compassion and understanding of their people's needs.
  • hiraṇyapakṣaṁ: This means "golden wings." The imagery of golden wings can represent prosperity, success, and the ability to rise above challenges. In a real-life context, this could refer to individuals or organizations that achieve great success through hard work and perseverance.
  • varuṇasya dūtaṁ: This translates to "the messenger of Varuna." Varuna is the deity associated with cosmic order and water. This phrase can symbolize the importance of communication and the role of messengers in maintaining harmony. In modern life, this can be seen in diplomats or mediators who work to resolve conflicts and maintain peace.
  • yamasya yonau: This means "in the womb of Yama." Yama is the god of death and the underworld, representing the cycle of life and death. This phrase can remind us of the inevitability of death and the importance of living a meaningful life. In real life, it encourages individuals to reflect on their actions and their impact on others.
  • śakunam bhuraṇyum: This translates to "the auspicious omen." It signifies hope and positive signs that guide us in our journey. In everyday life, people often look for signs or omens when making significant decisions, such as starting a new job or moving to a new place.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of wisdom, perception, communication, and the cyclical nature of life. Its significance lies in the reminder to approach life with a heart-centered perspective, to seek higher knowledge, and to communicate effectively for the greater good. In real-life scenarios, these teachings can be applied in leadership, personal relationships, and decision-making processes, encouraging individuals to act with empathy, seek wisdom, and recognize the interconnectedness of all beings.
