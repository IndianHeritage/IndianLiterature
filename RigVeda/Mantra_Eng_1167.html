त आदित्याऽआ गतā सर्वतातये भूत देवā वृततूर्येषु शम्भुवः |रथं न दुर्गाद वसवः सुदानवो विश्वस्मान नो अंहसो निः पिपर्तन ||
ta ādityā ā gatā sarvatātaye bhūta devā vṛtratūryeṣu śambhuvaḥ | rathaṁ na durgād vasavaḥ sudānavo viśvasmān no aṁhaso niṣ pipartana ||
  • ta ādityā ā gatā sarvatātaye bhūta devā vṛtratūryeṣu śambhuvaḥ
    • ta ādityā: This phrase refers to the Adityas, the solar deities in Vedic tradition. They symbolize light, truth, and the life-giving force of the sun.
    • ā gatā: This means "have come" or "arrived," indicating the presence or manifestation of these divine beings.
    • sarvatātaye: This term translates to "in all directions" or "universally," suggesting the omnipresence of these deities.
    • bhūta devā: This refers to the "beings" or "gods" that exist in the natural world, emphasizing the connection between divinity and nature.
    • vṛtratūryeṣu: This can be interpreted as "in the battles against Vritra," where Vritra symbolizes obstacles or chaos that obstructs the flow of life.
    • śambhuvaḥ: This term means "those who are auspicious" or "blessed," indicating the positive influence of these deities in overcoming challenges.
  • rathaṁ na durgād vasavaḥ sudānavo viśvasmān no aṁhaso niṣ pipartana
    • rathaṁ: This word means "chariot," which symbolizes the journey of life and the means of moving forward.
    • na durgād: This translates to "not from difficulties," indicating that the path should not be obstructed by challenges.
    • vasavaḥ: Refers to the Vasus, a group of deities associated with abundance and prosperity.
    • sudānavo: This means "good nourishment" or "well-being," emphasizing the importance of sustenance in life.
    • viśvasmān: This term means "from all," suggesting inclusivity and the collective nature of existence.
    • no aṁhaso: This translates to "our strength," indicating the importance of inner strength and resilience.
    • niṣ pipartana: This means "do not let it be taken away," emphasizing the need to protect one's strength and resources.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine presence and support in overcoming life's challenges. It highlights the need for strength, nourishment, and the collective effort of all beings to navigate difficulties. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Facing personal challenges: Just as the deities support individuals in overcoming obstacles, one can seek inner strength and community support during tough times.
  • Promoting well-being: The mantra encourages a focus on nourishment and prosperity, which can be reflected in efforts to maintain physical and mental health.
  • Fostering unity: The idea of inclusivity and collective strength can inspire teamwork and collaboration in various aspects of life, from workplaces to community initiatives.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine support available to us and the importance of resilience, unity, and well-being in our journey through life.
