यथा गौरो अपाकृतं तृष्यन्न एत्य अवेरिणम् |आपित्वे नः प्रापित्वे तुयम् आ गहि कण्वेषु सु सचा पिब ||
yathā gauro apā kṛtaṁ tṛṣyann ety averiṇam | āpitve naḥ prapitve tūyam ā gahi kaṇveṣu su sacā piba ||
  • Mantra: "yathā gauro apā kṛtaṁ tṛṣyann ety averiṇam | āpitve naḥ prapitve tūyam ā gahi kaṇveṣu su sacā piba ||"
  • Translation: "As the cow, when thirsty, approaches the water, may the waters come to us; may they be full and abundant, and may we drink them with joy."

  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yathā: as
    • gauro: the cow (symbolizing nourishment and abundance)
    • apā: water
    • kṛtaṁ: done or created
    • tṛṣyann: thirsty
    • ety: approaches
    • averiṇam: to us (the waters)
    • āpitve: may they be full
    • naḥ: for us
    • prapitve: may they be abundant
    • tūyam: waters
    • ā: come
    • gahi: hold or grasp
    • kaṇveṣu: in the vessels
    • su: good
    • sacā: with joy
    • piba: drink

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra uses the metaphor of a thirsty cow approaching water to symbolize the human quest for sustenance and fulfillment.
    • The cow represents a natural and instinctive need for nourishment, which can be paralleled in human life where individuals seek resources, knowledge, and emotional support.
    • The act of the cow approaching water signifies the proactive nature of seeking what is necessary for survival and well-being.
    • In real life, this can be seen in various contexts, such as students seeking knowledge (water) to quench their thirst for learning, or individuals pursuing relationships and community (water) to fulfill their emotional needs.

  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking abundance and fulfillment in life.
    • It encourages individuals to be proactive in their pursuits, whether it be in education, career, or personal relationships.
    • The invocation of water as a source of joy and sustenance highlights the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of community and support.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in daily life by reminding individuals to seek out resources and connections that nourish their growth and happiness.
