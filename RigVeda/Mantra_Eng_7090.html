अवोचाम महते सौभगाय सत्यं त्वेषाभ्यां महिमानम् इन्द्रियम् |अस्मान् स्व इन्द्रावरुणा घृतश्चुतस् त्रिभिः सप्तेभिर अवतं शुभस पतिः ||
avocāma mahate saubhagāya satyaṁ tveṣābhyām mahimānam indriyam | asmān sv indrāvaruṇā ghṛtaścutas tribhiḥ sāptebhir avataṁ śubhas patī ||
  • avocāma: This word translates to "we have spoken" or "we have declared." It signifies the act of expressing or proclaiming something important.
  • mahate: Meaning "great" or "mighty," this word emphasizes the significance of what is being declared.
  • saubhagāya: This translates to "for auspiciousness" or "for good fortune," indicating that the declaration is aimed at bringing about positive outcomes.
  • satyaṁ: Meaning "truth," this word highlights the importance of honesty and integrity in the declaration.
  • tveṣābhyām: This can be interpreted as "by these two," referring to the dual aspects of truth and auspiciousness.
  • mahimānam: This means "greatness" or "glory," suggesting that the declaration is about recognizing and honoring greatness.
  • indriyam: This word refers to "senses" or "powers," indicating the faculties through which we perceive and interact with the world.
  • asmān: Meaning "us," it signifies the collective nature of the declaration, involving a community or group.
  • sv: This translates to "our," reinforcing the personal connection to the declaration.
  • indrāvaruṇā: This refers to the divine powers of Indra and Varuna, representing strength and cosmic order, respectively.
  • ghṛtaścutas: This means "the ghee that flows," symbolizing nourishment and abundance.
  • tribhiḥ: Meaning "by three," this indicates a triad of elements or forces at play.
  • sāptebhir: This translates to "by the seven," possibly referring to the seven sages or the seven directions, emphasizing completeness.
  • avataṁ: Meaning "to protect" or "to guard," indicating a plea for divine protection.
  • śubhas patī: This translates to "lord of auspiciousness," referring to a divine figure who brings good fortune and blessings.

The mantra essentially conveys a powerful declaration aimed at invoking auspiciousness and truth, recognizing the greatness of divine powers, and seeking protection and blessings for the community. It emphasizes the importance of collective intention and the role of divine forces in achieving prosperity and well-being.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Community Gatherings: When a community comes together for a festival or a significant event, this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings and ensure a successful and harmonious gathering.
  • Business Ventures: Entrepreneurs can use this mantra during the launch of a new business or project, seeking truth and auspiciousness in their endeavors.
  • Personal Reflection: Individuals can reflect on the values of truth and auspiciousness in their daily lives, striving to align their actions with these principles.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of collective intention, the importance of truth, and the need for divine guidance in our pursuits. It encourages individuals and communities to seek auspiciousness and protection in their endeavors, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
