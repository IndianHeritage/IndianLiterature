इन्द्र श्रुधि सु मे हवम् अस्मे सुतस्य गोमतः |वि पीतिं तृप्तिम अश्नुहि ||
indra śrudhi su me havam asme sutasya gomataḥ | vi pītiṁ tṛptim aśnuhi ||
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and strength. He represents power and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • Śrudhi: This term means "to hear" or "to listen." It signifies the importance of being receptive to divine guidance and wisdom.
  • Su: This word translates to "good" or "well." It emphasizes positivity and the beneficial aspects of the request being made.
  • Me: This means "my" or "to me," indicating a personal connection to the prayer or invocation.
  • Havam: This term refers to offerings or sacrifices made to the gods. It signifies the act of devotion and the desire to connect with the divine.
  • Asme: This means "we are" or "we exist," indicating a collective identity or community aspect in the prayer.
  • Sutasya: This translates to "of the son" or "of the offspring," which can refer to the fruits of one's labor or the results of one's actions.
  • Gomataḥ: This term means "of the cow," which is a symbol of wealth, nourishment, and sustenance in Vedic culture.
  • Vi: This word means "in many ways" or "variously," indicating diversity and abundance.
  • Pītiṁ: This translates to "nourishment" or "satisfaction," referring to the fulfillment of needs and desires.
  • Tṛptim: This means "contentment" or "satisfaction," emphasizing the importance of being fulfilled and at peace.
  • Aśnuhi: This term means "may you attain" or "may you achieve," expressing a wish or prayer for success and fulfillment.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to Indra, asking for his attention and blessings. The devotee seeks nourishment and satisfaction, both materially and spiritually, through offerings and devotion. The mention of the cow symbolizes the importance of sustenance and prosperity in life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Seeking Guidance: Just as the mantra calls for Indra's attention, individuals can seek guidance in their personal and professional lives, asking for clarity and strength to overcome challenges.
  • Community Support: The collective aspect of the mantra emphasizes the importance of community. In times of need, individuals can come together to support one another, much like the offerings made in the mantra.
  • Gratitude for Resources: The reference to the cow highlights the significance of appreciating and nurturing the resources we have, whether they are material possessions or relationships.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine guidance, fostering community, and being grateful for the resources that sustain us. It encourages individuals to strive for fulfillment and contentment in their lives, both materially and spiritually.
