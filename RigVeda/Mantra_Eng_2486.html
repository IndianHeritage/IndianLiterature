यं देवासस त्रिर अहन्न āयजन्ते दिवार्दिवे वरुणो मित्रो अग्निः |सेमं यज्ञं मधुमन्तं कृधī नस् तनूनपाद घृतयोनिं विधन्तम् ||
yaṁ devāsas trir ahann āyajante dive-dive varuṇo mitro agniḥ | semaṁ yajñam madhumantaṁ kṛdhī nas tanūnapād ghṛtayoniṁ vidhantam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: whom
    • devāsas: the gods
    • trir: thrice
    • ahann: have invoked
    • āyajante: they worship
    • dive-dive: day by day
    • varuṇo: Varuna (the god of cosmic order)
    • mitro: Mitra (the god of friendship and contracts)
    • agniḥ: Agni (the fire god)
    • semaṁ: the shelter
    • yajñam: the sacrifice
    • madhumantaṁ: sweet and nourishing
    • kṛdhī: may you create
    • nas: for us
    • tanūnapād: with bodies (or forms)
    • ghṛtayoniṁ: born of ghee (clarified butter)
    • vidhantam: may you bestow
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the gods who are invoked repeatedly, emphasizing the importance of regular worship and devotion.
    • It highlights the roles of Varuna, Mitra, and Agni, representing cosmic order, friendship, and the transformative power of fire, respectively.
    • The phrase "day by day" suggests a continuous practice of spirituality and connection with the divine.
    • The mantra calls for the creation of a nourishing and protective environment, symbolized by the "sweet sacrifice," which can be interpreted as the good deeds and positive actions we perform in life.
    • The mention of "bodies born of ghee" signifies the importance of purity and nourishment in our physical and spiritual existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in regular acts of kindness and service, akin to worshipping the divine through good deeds.
    • In a community setting, it can encourage collective efforts to create a harmonious environment, reflecting the roles of Varuna and Mitra in fostering relationships and order.
    • In personal development, the emphasis on nourishment can be applied to maintaining a healthy lifestyle, both physically and mentally, ensuring that one’s actions are beneficial to oneself and others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of regular spiritual practice and the interconnectedness of our actions with the divine.
    • Its significance lies in promoting a life of harmony, nourishment, and positive relationships, applicable in various aspects of daily living.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more balanced and fulfilling life, both personally and within their communities.
