अवितासी सुन्वतो वृक्तबरिषः पिबा सोमं मदाया कं शतक्रतो |यं ते भागम अधारयन विश्वाः सेहानः पृतना उरु ज्रयः सम अप्सुजिन मरुत्वां इन्द्र सत्पते ||
avitāsi sunvato vṛktabarhiṣaḥ pibā somam madāya kaṁ śatakrato | yaṁ te bhāgam adhārayan viśvāḥ sehānaḥ pṛtanā uru jrayaḥ sam apsujin marutvām̐ indra satpate ||
  • avitāsi: This word can be interpreted as "you have come" or "you have arrived." It signifies the presence of a divine force or energy.
  • sunvato: This means "of the one who listens" or "to the one who hears." It emphasizes the importance of being receptive and attentive to divine messages.
  • vṛktabarhiṣaḥ: This term refers to "the one who is adorned with the sacred grass." In Vedic rituals, sacred grass (like darbha) is used to purify and sanctify the space.
  • pibā: This translates to "drink." It suggests the act of consuming or absorbing something beneficial, in this case, the soma.
  • somam: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine ecstasy. It represents the essence of life and spiritual nourishment.
  • madāya: This means "for intoxication" or "for delight." It indicates the joy and bliss derived from the divine experience.
  • kaṁ: This word means "who" or "whom," indicating a question or inquiry about the divine presence.
  • śatakrato: Referring to "the one with a hundred powers," this term is often associated with Indra, the king of gods, symbolizing strength and capability.
  • yaṁ: This means "whom" or "which," referring to the divine being or energy being invoked.
  • te: This translates to "your," indicating possession or connection to the divine.
  • bhāgam: This means "portion" or "share," suggesting the distribution of divine blessings.
  • adhārayan: This means "who have supported" or "who have upheld," indicating the strength and support provided by the divine.
  • viśvāḥ: This translates to "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity and the presence of the divine in everything.
  • sehānaḥ: This means "those who are strong" or "powerful," referring to the divine forces at play.
  • pṛtanā: This translates to "the one who is praised" or "the one who is celebrated," indicating reverence towards the divine.
  • uru: This means "great" or "vast," emphasizing the magnitude of the divine presence.
  • jrayaḥ: This translates to "strength" or "power," indicating the potency of the divine energy.
  • sam: This means "together" or "with," indicating unity in the divine experience.
  • apsujin: This term refers to "the one who is born from the waters," symbolizing creation and life.
  • marutvām̐: This refers to "the storm gods" or "the winds," indicating the dynamic and powerful aspects of nature.
  • indra: This is the name of the king of gods, representing strength, courage, and leadership.
  • satpate: This means "the lord of truth" or "the master of reality," indicating the ultimate authority of the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra invokes the divine presence, emphasizing the importance of receptivity, strength, and the joy derived from spiritual experiences. It highlights the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine support that sustains life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of seeking guidance, strength in challenges, or during rituals that celebrate life and the divine. It serves as a reminder to remain open to divine messages and to appreciate the blessings that come from a higher power.
