त्वं अग्नि यज्ञानां होता विश्वेषां हितः |देवेभिर मानवसे जनः ||
tvam agne yajñānāṁ hotā viśveṣāṁ hitaḥ | devebhir mānuṣe jane ||
  • tvam - This word translates to "You" and signifies the divine presence or the deity being addressed.
  • agne - Refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic tradition, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine spark within all beings.
  • yajñānāṁ - This means "of the sacrifices" or "of the offerings," indicating the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine.
  • hotā - Translates to "the priest" or "the one who invokes," representing the role of a mediator between humans and the divine.
  • viśveṣāṁ - This means "of all beings" or "of the universe," emphasizing the inclusivity of the divine presence in all aspects of life.
  • hitaḥ - This translates to "beneficial" or "auspicious," indicating the positive outcomes of divine blessings and actions.
  • devebhir - Meaning "by the gods," it highlights the connection between the divine and the earthly realm.
  • mānuṣe - This means "human" or "mankind," emphasizing the importance of human actions and intentions in the cosmic order.
  • jane - Refers to "people" or "the community," indicating the collective aspect of human existence and the shared responsibility towards the divine.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Agni, the fire god, asking for his blessings and guidance in performing sacrifices that are beneficial for all beings. It emphasizes the role of humans in connecting with the divine through rituals and the importance of community in this process.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Community Gatherings: Just as the mantra emphasizes collective action, community gatherings for festivals or rituals can foster unity and shared purpose.
  • Environmental Awareness: The invocation of Agni can symbolize the importance of fire in nature, reminding us of our responsibility to protect the environment and use resources wisely.
  • Personal Growth: The transformative aspect of Agni can be seen in personal development, where individuals seek to ignite their inner potential through education, self-reflection, and spiritual practices.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the interconnectedness of the divine, humanity, and the universe. It serves as a reminder of our responsibilities towards each other and the environment, encouraging us to engage in actions that are beneficial for all beings. By invoking the divine presence in our lives, we can strive for harmony, growth, and positive transformation in both personal and communal spheres.
