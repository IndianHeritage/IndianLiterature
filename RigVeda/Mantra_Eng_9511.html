मन्त्र: अवो द्वाभ्यां पर एकया गा गुहा तिष्ठन्तीर अनृतस्य सेतौ | बृहस्पतिस तमसि ज्योतिर इच्छन्न उद उष्रा आकरि विहि तिस्रा आवः ||
avo dvābhyām para ekayā gā guhā tiṣṭhantīr anṛtasya setau | bṛhaspatis tamasi jyotir icchann ud usrā ākar vi hi tisra āvaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "avo dvābhyām para ekayā gā guhā tiṣṭhantīr anṛtasya setau | bṛhaspatis tamasi jyotir icchann ud usrā ākar vi hi tisra āvaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • avo: to invoke or call upon
    • dvābhyām: by two (often referring to duality or pairs)
    • para: beyond or higher
    • ekayā: by one or singular
    • gā: song or hymn
    • guhā: cave or hidden place
    • tiṣṭhantīr: standing or residing
    • anṛtasya: of untruth or falsehood
    • setau: bridge or connection
    • bṛhaspatis: the deity associated with wisdom and knowledge
    • tamasi: in darkness
    • jyotir: light or illumination
    • icchann: desiring or wishing
    • ud: upward or above
    • usrā: dawn or light
    • ākar: form or shape
    • vi: indeed or certainly
    • hi: for or because
    • tisra: three
    • āvaḥ: may we bring or may we attract
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence, calling upon the duality of existence and the singular truth that resides within.
    • The mention of "guhā" signifies the hidden aspects of life, suggesting that truth often lies beneath the surface, much like a cave that holds treasures.
    • The phrase "anṛtasya setau" indicates the need to bridge the gap between truth and falsehood, emphasizing the importance of discernment in our lives.
    • By invoking "bṛhaspatis," the mantra seeks wisdom and guidance to navigate through the darkness ("tamasi") towards enlightenment ("jyotir").
    • The desire for "usrā" or dawn symbolizes the aspiration for new beginnings and clarity, urging individuals to rise above ignorance.
    • The call to "bring" or "attract" the three aspects of life suggests a holistic approach to existence, integrating mind, body, and spirit.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, this mantra can be applied when one seeks to overcome challenges and false beliefs, striving for personal truth and authenticity.
    • In leadership, invoking wisdom and clarity can help guide teams through complex situations, ensuring decisions are made with integrity and insight.
    • In spiritual practices, this mantra can be used during meditation to focus on inner light and truth, helping individuals connect with their higher selves.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for wisdom, truth, and enlightenment amidst the dualities of life.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek deeper understanding and clarity, bridging the gap between illusion and reality.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to embrace their journey towards self-discovery and to illuminate the path for others.
