आ नो देवः सविता त्रायमाणो हिरण्यपाणिर यजतो जगम्यāt |यो दात्रवाम् उषसो न प्रतीकं व्यूर्णुते दाशुषे वार्याणि ||
ā no devaḥ savitā trāyamāṇo hiraṇyapāṇir yajato jagamyāt | yo datravām̐ uṣaso na pratīkaṁ vyūrṇute dāśuṣe vāryāṇi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā no: May he (Savitā) protect us.
    • devaḥ: God or divine being.
    • savitā: The Sun God, a source of life and energy.
    • trāyamāṇo: Protecting or safeguarding.
    • hiraṇyapāṇir: One who has golden hands, symbolizing prosperity and abundance.
    • yajato: For those who perform sacrifices or worship.
    • jagamyāt: May he come or may he be present.
    • yo: Who.
    • datravām̐: Bestowing or giving.
    • uṣaso: Of the dawn or morning light.
    • na: Not.
    • pratīkaṁ: A sign or symbol.
    • vyūrṇute: To remove or to dispel.
    • dāśuṣe: For the one who is devoted or worshipful.
    • vāryāṇi: The powers or strengths.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Savitā, the Sun God, asking for protection and blessings.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine guidance in our lives, especially for those who engage in spiritual practices.
    • The mention of "hiraṇyapāṇir" signifies the abundance and prosperity that comes from divine blessings.
    • The mantra also highlights the role of the dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and the dispelling of darkness or ignorance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek protection and guidance through prayers or rituals, similar to the essence of this mantra.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, people invoke divine blessings for success and prosperity.
    • The concept of starting anew with the dawn can be seen in practices like morning meditation or yoga, where individuals seek clarity and positivity for the day ahead.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine protection and guidance in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to engage in spiritual practices that foster a connection with the divine, leading to personal growth and prosperity.
    • In a broader sense, it highlights the significance of community and collective worship, as the blessings of the divine are often sought for the well-being of all.
