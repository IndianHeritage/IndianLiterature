आ यद वाम ईयचक्षसा मित्र वयं च सूरयः | व्यचिष्टे बहुपाय्ये यतेमहि स्वराज्ये ||
ā yad vām īyacakṣasā mitra vayaṁ ca sūrayaḥ | vyaciṣṭhe bahupāyye yatemahi svarājye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā - from
    • yad - whatever
    • vām - you (plural)
    • īyacakṣasā - with the eye of insight
    • mitra - O Friend (often referring to the divine or a higher power)
    • vayaṁ - we
    • ca - and
    • sūrayaḥ - the brave or the strong
    • vyaciṣṭhe - may we be endowed
    • bahupāyye - with many resources
    • yatemahi - may we strive
    • svarājye - for sovereignty or self-rule
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for insight and strength from a higher power.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and collective effort in achieving goals.
    • The term "sūrayaḥ" highlights the value of bravery and courage in facing challenges.
    • The phrase "bahupāyye" suggests the need for diverse resources and strategies to succeed.
    • Ultimately, it calls for striving towards self-governance and autonomy.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team may invoke this mantra to foster collaboration and innovation, seeking insights from all members to achieve a common goal.
    • In community organizing, groups may embody the spirit of this mantra by pooling resources and efforts to advocate for local issues, striving for self-determination.
    • In personal development, individuals may reflect on this mantra when facing challenges, reminding themselves to seek inner strength and diverse strategies to overcome obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of collective effort, insight, and courage in the pursuit of goals.
    • Its significance lies in its applicability across various aspects of life, from personal growth to community empowerment.
    • By embracing the principles of this mantra, individuals and groups can work towards achieving sovereignty in their respective domains.
