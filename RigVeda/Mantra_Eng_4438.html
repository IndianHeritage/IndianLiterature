नाहं तन्तुं न वि जानाम्य ओतुं न यं वयन्ति समरे 'तमानाः |कस्य स्वित पुत्र इह वक्त्वानि परो वदात्य अवरेण पित्रा ||
nāhaṁ tantuṁ na vi jānāmy otuṁ na yaṁ vayanti samare 'tamānāḥ | kasya svit putra iha vaktvāni paro vadāty avareṇa pitrā ||
  • nāhaṁ tantuṁ na vi jānāmy: This phrase translates to "I do not know the thread." In a metaphorical sense, the "thread" can represent the connections and relationships in life. Just as a thread weaves fabric together, our relationships and interactions weave the fabric of our existence.
  • otuṁ na yaṁ vayanti samare 'tamānāḥ: This part means "I do not know what they speak of in battle." Here, "battle" can symbolize the struggles and challenges we face in life. It suggests a lack of understanding of the conflicts that arise around us, whether they are personal, social, or existential.
  • kasya svit putra iha vaktvāni: This translates to "Whose son is it that speaks here?" This line raises questions about identity and lineage. It emphasizes the importance of understanding one's roots and the influence of ancestry on our lives.
  • paro vadāty avareṇa pitrā: This means "Another speaks of the father." It suggests that there are voices and influences from others, particularly from our forebears, that shape our understanding and perception of the world.

Conclusion: The mantra reflects on the themes of identity, relationships, and the struggles of life. It emphasizes the importance of understanding our connections to others and the influences of our heritage. In real life, this can be applicable in various scenarios such as family dynamics, social interactions, and personal growth. Recognizing the threads that connect us to our past and to each other can lead to a deeper understanding of ourselves and our place in the world. This mantra encourages introspection and awareness of the complexities of life, urging us to seek clarity amidst the chaos of our experiences.
