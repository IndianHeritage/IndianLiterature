एव न इन्द्रोतिभिर अव पाही गृणतः शूर कारुण |उत त्वचं ददतो वाजसातौ पिप्रीहि मध्वः सुषुतस्य चारोः ||
evā na indrotibhir ava pāhi gṛṇataḥ śūra kārūn | uta tvacaṁ dadato vājasātau piprīhi madhvaḥ suṣutasya cāroḥ ||
  • evā na indrotibhir: This phrase can be translated as "May we not be harmed by the powerful." Here, "Indra" refers to the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and power. In real life, this can be understood as a plea for protection from overwhelming forces, whether they be natural disasters, societal pressures, or personal challenges.
  • ava pāhi: This means "protect us." It emphasizes the need for divine or external protection in our lives. For example, in times of crisis, such as during a pandemic or economic downturn, people often seek protection through community support, healthcare, or spiritual practices.
  • gṛṇataḥ: This word translates to "those who sing" or "those who praise." It highlights the importance of gratitude and acknowledgment of the forces that support us. In everyday life, expressing gratitude can strengthen relationships and foster a positive environment, whether in personal interactions or in professional settings.
  • śūra kārūn: This phrase means "the brave and the strong." It signifies the qualities of courage and resilience. In real life, individuals who exhibit bravery in the face of adversity, such as healthcare workers during a crisis or activists fighting for justice, embody this principle.
  • uta tvacaṁ dadato: This translates to "or those who give us strength." It refers to the sources of strength in our lives, which can be family, friends, mentors, or even personal experiences. For instance, a supportive family can provide the emotional strength needed to overcome challenges.
  • vājasātau: This means "in the context of strength." It emphasizes the importance of being strong in various aspects of life, including mental, emotional, and physical strength. For example, athletes train rigorously to build physical strength, while individuals may engage in therapy or mindfulness practices to enhance mental resilience.
  • piprīhi madhvaḥ: This translates to "may you fill us with sweetness." It symbolizes the desire for positive experiences and joy in life. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of happiness through meaningful relationships, fulfilling careers, and personal passions.
  • suṣutasya cāroḥ: This means "of the well-being of the good." It emphasizes the importance of well-being and the collective good. In a community context, this can relate to initiatives that promote health, education, and welfare for all members, such as community health programs or educational outreach.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound plea for protection, strength, and positivity in life. It highlights the significance of community, gratitude, and resilience in overcoming challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as seeking support during difficult times, fostering relationships that provide strength, and promoting well-being within communities. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the importance of uplifting one another in the journey of life.
