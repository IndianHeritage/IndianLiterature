त्रीणि पदान्य अश्विनोर आविः सान्ति गुहा परः |कवी ऋतस्य पद्मभिर अर्वाग जीविभ्यस् परि ||
trīṇi padāny aśvinor āviḥ sānti guhā paraḥ | kavī ṛtasya patmabhir arvāg jīvebhyas pari ||
  • Trīṇi padāny: This phrase translates to "three steps" or "three feet." In many spiritual contexts, the number three symbolizes completeness or a holistic approach. For example, in real life, we often see the significance of three in various aspects, such as the three stages of life: birth, life, and death.
  • Aśvinor: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of existence and the balance between opposites, such as light and darkness. In modern life, we can relate this to the balance we seek between work and personal life.
  • Āviḥ: This term can be interpreted as "to come forth" or "to manifest." It signifies the emergence of potential into reality. In practical terms, this can be seen in the process of innovation, where ideas are transformed into tangible products or solutions.
  • Sānti: Meaning "peace" or "calmness." This concept is crucial in today's fast-paced world, where finding inner peace can lead to better mental health and well-being. Practices like meditation and mindfulness are modern examples of seeking sānti.
  • Guhā paraḥ: This translates to "the hidden cave" or "the ultimate secret." It represents the deeper truths of existence that are often obscured from our everyday perception. In real life, this can relate to the pursuit of knowledge and understanding beyond superficial appearances, such as the quest for self-awareness.
  • Kavī: This word means "poet" or "sage," indicating a person who possesses wisdom and insight. In contemporary society, we can see this in thought leaders and innovators who inspire others through their vision and creativity.
  • Ṛtasya: Referring to "truth" or "cosmic order." This concept emphasizes the importance of living in harmony with the natural laws of the universe. In real life, this can be applied to environmental sustainability and ethical living, where individuals strive to align their actions with the greater good.
  • Patmabhir: This term can be interpreted as "with wings" or "with the ability to rise." It symbolizes freedom and the potential to transcend limitations. In practical terms, this can be seen in personal growth and the pursuit of dreams, where individuals strive to overcome obstacles and achieve their goals.
  • Arvāg jīvebhyas pari: This phrase suggests "to live beyond" or "to transcend life." It speaks to the idea of seeking a higher purpose or understanding beyond mere existence. In modern life, this can be reflected in the pursuit of spirituality, community service, or contributing to a cause greater than oneself.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound themes of balance, peace, wisdom, and the pursuit of truth. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, encouraging individuals to seek harmony within themselves and with the world around them. By embodying the principles of the Aśvins, striving for inner peace, and pursuing deeper truths, we can navigate the complexities of modern existence with greater clarity and purpose.
