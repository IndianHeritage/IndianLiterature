प्र व उग्राय निष्टुरे षाल्हाय प्रसक्षिणे |देवत्तम ब्रह्म गायत ||
pra va ugrāya niṣṭure 'ṣāḻhāya prasakṣiṇe | devattam brahma gāyata ||
  • pra va ugrāya: This phrase can be interpreted as "to the powerful" or "to the strong." In real life, this can refer to individuals or forces that exhibit great strength or influence, such as leaders, athletes, or even natural phenomena like storms.
  • niṣṭure 'ṣāḻhāya: This part translates to "to the one who is steadfast" or "to the one who is firm." In everyday life, this can be associated with people who show resilience and determination, such as a student who perseveres through challenges or a community that stands together in times of crisis.
  • prasakṣiṇe: This word means "to the witness" or "to the observer." In a practical context, this can refer to those who observe and learn from experiences, such as mentors, teachers, or even individuals reflecting on their life choices.
  • devattam: This term translates to "the highest divine" or "the supreme deity." In real life, this can symbolize the pursuit of higher ideals, spirituality, or the quest for understanding the universe, akin to how people seek enlightenment or truth in various philosophies.
  • brahma: This word signifies "the ultimate reality" or "the cosmic spirit." In a contemporary context, it can represent the interconnectedness of all things, similar to how scientists explore the fundamental laws of nature that bind the universe together.
  • gāyata: This means "to sing" or "to chant." In real life, this can be seen in the practice of music, poetry, or any form of expression that brings people together, such as communal singing or cultural festivals that celebrate heritage.

Conclusion: The mantra "pra va ugrāya niṣṭure 'ṣāḻhāya prasakṣiṇe | devattam brahma gāyata ||" emphasizes the importance of strength, resilience, observation, and the pursuit of higher truths. It encourages individuals to recognize and honor the powerful and steadfast forces in their lives, whether they be personal, communal, or universal. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire people to strive for personal growth, support one another in challenges, and seek a deeper understanding of their existence and the world around them. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the significance of expressing gratitude and reverence through various forms of art and spirituality.
