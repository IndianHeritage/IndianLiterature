तां राजाना शुचिव्रतादित्यां मारुतं गणम् |वसो यक्षीह रोडसी ||
tā rājānā śucivratādityān mārutaṁ gaṇam | vaso yakṣīha rodasī ||
  • tā rājānā: This phrase translates to "those kings" or "those rulers." It signifies the leaders or authorities who govern and protect their people.
  • śucivratādityān: This can be broken down into "śuci" meaning "pure" or "sacred," "vrata" meaning "vow" or "commitment," and "ādityān" referring to the "sons of Aditi," often associated with the sun or divine beings. Together, it suggests rulers who are committed to purity and righteousness.
  • mārutaṁ gaṇam: "Māruta" refers to the winds or the forces of nature, and "gaṇam" means "group" or "assembly." This phrase can be interpreted as the collective forces of nature or the divine energies that support the rulers.
  • vaso yakṣīha rodasī: "Vaso" means "to dwell" or "to reside," "yakṣīha" can be understood as "may you grant" or "may you bestow," and "rodasī" refers to the "two worlds" or "the heavens and the earth." This part of the mantra expresses a plea for the divine to grant blessings and support in both realms.

In summary, this mantra invokes the blessings of divine rulers who are committed to purity and righteousness, along with the forces of nature, to bestow their grace upon the world. It emphasizes the importance of leadership that is grounded in moral values and the interconnectedness of the natural world.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra highlights the qualities of good leadership, which can be applied in various contexts, such as politics, business, and community service. Leaders who embody purity and righteousness can inspire trust and loyalty among their followers.
  • In a corporate setting, a manager who prioritizes ethical practices and the well-being of employees can create a positive work environment, leading to increased productivity and morale.
  • In community leadership, individuals who commit to serving their community with integrity can foster a sense of unity and cooperation among residents, leading to collective progress.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the responsibilities that come with leadership and the need for a harmonious relationship with nature and the divine. It encourages individuals in positions of power to act with integrity and to seek the support of higher forces in their endeavors.
