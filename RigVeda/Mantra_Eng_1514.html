ओ षु त्वा ववृतिमहि स्तोमेभिर दसम साधुभिः |नहि त्वा पूषन्न अतिमन्य आघृणे न ते सख्यम अपह्नवे ||
o ṣu tvā vavṛtīmahi stomebhir dasma sādhubhiḥ | nahi tvā pūṣann atimanya āghṛṇe na te sakhyam apahnuve ||
  • Mantra: "o ṣu tvā vavṛtīmahi stomebhir dasma sādhubhiḥ | nahi tvā pūṣann atimanya āghṛṇe na te sakhyam apahnuve ||"
  • Translation: "O Pūṣan, we invoke you with our hymns; may you come to us with your blessings. I do not wish to offend you, nor do I wish to lose your friendship."

  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • O: A call or invocation.
    • ṣu: A term of endearment or respect.
    • tvā: You (referring to the deity).
    • vavṛtīmahi: We invoke or call upon.
    • stomebhir: With hymns or praises.
    • dasma: May you come to us.
    • sādhubhiḥ: With blessings or goodness.
    • nahi: Not.
    • tvā: You.
    • pūṣann: O Pūṣan (a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity).
    • atimanya: I do not wish to offend.
    • āghṛṇe: I do not wish to lose your friendship.
    • na: Not.
    • te: Your.
    • sakhyam: Friendship.
    • apahnuve: I do not wish to sever or lose.

  • Context and Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Pūṣan, a deity who represents nourishment, prosperity, and the well-being of individuals and communities.
    • It emphasizes the importance of invoking divine blessings through hymns and praises, highlighting the relationship between humans and the divine.
    • The plea for friendship and the avoidance of offense signifies the need for harmony and respect in relationships, both with the divine and among individuals.

  • Real-Life Applications:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be a reminder to approach our goals and aspirations with humility and respect, acknowledging the support we receive from others.
    • It encourages individuals to maintain positive relationships, whether in personal or professional settings, by valuing cooperation and goodwill.
    • In times of need, invoking blessings through prayer or positive affirmations can serve as a source of strength and guidance.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support while fostering respectful and harmonious relationships.
    • Its teachings are applicable in various aspects of life, reminding us of the importance of gratitude, respect, and the pursuit of goodness.
    • By embodying these principles, individuals can cultivate a more fulfilling and prosperous life, both personally and within their communities.
