तवे ह यत् पितरश्चिन् न इन्द्र विश्वा वामाः जरीतारो असन्वन् |तवे गावाḥ सुदुग्धास तवे ह्य अश्वास त्वं वसु देवयते वनिष्ठः ||
tve ha yat pitaraś cin na indra viśvā vāmā jaritāro asanvan | tve gāvaḥ sudughās tve hy aśvās tvaṁ vasu devayate vaniṣṭhaḥ ||
  • tve ha yat pitaraś cin: This phrase can be translated as "You, O Indra, who are the father of all." Here, Indra is recognized as a paternal figure, symbolizing strength and protection.
  • na indra viśvā vāmā jaritāro asanvan: This part means "Indra, you are the one who brings all beings together." It emphasizes the unifying power of Indra, suggesting that he is responsible for harmony and balance in the universe.
  • tve gāvaḥ sudughās: This translates to "You are the one who provides us with excellent nourishment." The term "gāvaḥ" refers to cows, which are often seen as a source of sustenance and wealth in Vedic culture.
  • tve hy aśvās: This means "You are the one who gives us strength." The word "aśvās" relates to horses, which symbolize power and speed, indicating that Indra provides the energy and vigor needed for life.
  • tvaṁ vasu devayate vaniṣṭhaḥ: This can be interpreted as "You are the best among the divine beings." It highlights Indra's supreme status among the gods, reinforcing his role as a protector and provider.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the multifaceted role of Indra as a protector, provider, and unifier. In real life, we can draw parallels to the importance of leadership and community. Just as Indra is seen as a father figure who brings beings together and provides nourishment, effective leaders in our communities and families play a similar role. They foster unity, provide resources, and inspire strength among their members. This mantra serves as a reminder of the qualities we should seek in our leaders and the responsibilities we hold in our own lives to support and uplift those around us.
