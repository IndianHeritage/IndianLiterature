इन्द्रः स्पष् उता वृत्रहां परस्पा नो वरण्यः | स नो रक्षिषच् चरमं स मध्यं स पश्चात् पातु नः पुरः ||
indraḥ spaḻ uta vṛtrahā paraspā no vareṇyaḥ | sa no rakṣiṣac caramaṁ sa madhyamaṁ sa paścāt pātu naḥ puraḥ ||
  • Indraḥ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Spaḻ: Means to shine or to illuminate, indicating clarity and brilliance.
  • Uta: Translates to "and" or "also," connecting thoughts or ideas.
  • Vṛtrahā: Means "the slayer of Vritra," where Vritra represents obstacles or challenges. Indra is often depicted as overcoming these challenges.
  • Paraspā: Refers to "the one who protects from all sides," emphasizing the need for comprehensive protection.
  • No: Means "us," indicating a collective appeal for protection and guidance.
  • Vareṇyaḥ: Translates to "worthy of worship," suggesting that the divine presence is revered and respected.
  • Sa: Means "he" or "that," referring to the divine force or Indra.
  • Rakṣiṣac: Means "may protect," indicating a plea for safeguarding.
  • Caramaṁ: Means "the ultimate" or "the best," suggesting a desire for the highest form of protection.
  • Madhyamaṁ: Refers to "the middle" or "the balance," indicating the importance of stability and equilibrium.
  • Paścāt: Means "after" or "behind," suggesting protection from what comes after or follows.
  • Pātu: Means "may guard" or "may protect," reinforcing the plea for safety.
  • Naḥ: Means "us," again emphasizing the collective nature of the request.
  • Puraḥ: Means "in front" or "before," indicating the need for protection from what lies ahead.

Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation for protection and guidance from the divine, particularly from Indra, who symbolizes strength and the ability to overcome obstacles. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during challenging times when one seeks strength to face difficulties, or when embarking on new ventures that require courage and support. It emphasizes the importance of seeking protection from all directions—past, present, and future—highlighting the need for a holistic approach to safety and well-being. The mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate inner strength and resilience while also acknowledging the divine forces that guide and protect us in our journeys.
