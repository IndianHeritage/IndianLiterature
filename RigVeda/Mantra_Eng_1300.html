ऋषिं नराव अंहसः पाञ्चजन्यम ऋबीसाद अत्रीम मुंचथो गणेन |मिनन्ता दस्योर अशिवस्य मायां अनुपूर्वं वृषणा चोदयन्ता ||
ṛṣiṁ narāv aṁhasaḥ pāñcajanyam ṛbīsād atrim muñcatho gaṇena | minantā dasyor aśivasya māyā anupūrvaṁ vṛṣaṇā codayantā ||
  • ṛṣiṁ: This term refers to a sage or seer, someone who has attained a deep understanding of the universe through meditation and spiritual practice.
  • narāv: This word means "men" or "humans," indicating the collective human experience and the importance of human actions in the cosmic order.
  • aṁhasaḥ: This translates to "of the light" or "of the radiance," symbolizing knowledge, enlightenment, and the divine presence in the world.
  • pāñcajanyam: Referring to the conch shell, which is often associated with auspiciousness and the call to action in Hindu rituals.
  • ṛbīsād: This term can be interpreted as "through the hymns" or "by the verses," emphasizing the power of sacred words and prayers.
  • atrim: A reference to the sage Atri, who is known for his wisdom and contributions to Vedic literature.
  • muñcatho: This means "release" or "liberate," suggesting a call for freedom from ignorance or obstacles.
  • gaṇena: This translates to "by the group" or "with the community," highlighting the importance of collective effort and unity.
  • minantā: This word means "those who are falling" or "those who are in distress," indicating a plea for help or support.
  • dasyor: Referring to "the ten directions," symbolizing the entirety of the universe and the omnipresence of divine energy.
  • aśivasya: This translates to "of the auspicious," indicating a focus on positive outcomes and blessings.
  • māyā: This term refers to "illusion" or "the material world," suggesting the transient nature of life and the importance of seeking deeper truths.
  • anupūrvaṁ: Meaning "without precedent" or "unprecedented," indicating a unique or extraordinary situation.
  • vṛṣaṇā: This word can be interpreted as "of the strong" or "of the powerful," symbolizing strength and resilience.
  • codayantā: This means "inspiring" or "encouraging," suggesting a call to action or motivation for individuals and communities.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of collective human effort, the power of sacred words, and the pursuit of enlightenment. It calls for liberation from ignorance and encourages individuals to seek strength and inspiration from their community. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, spiritual gatherings, and personal development initiatives. It serves as a reminder that through unity, wisdom, and positive action, individuals can overcome challenges and contribute to a more harmonious world.
