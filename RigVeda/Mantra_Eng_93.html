युक्ष्वा हि केशिना हरī वृषणा कक्ष्यप्रा |अथा न इन्द्र सोमपा गिराम उपश्रुतिं चर ||
yukṣvā hi keśinā harī vṛṣaṇā kakṣyaprā | athā na indra somapā girām upaśrutiṁ cara ||
  • yukṣvā hi keśinā: This phrase can be translated as "with the one who has long hair." In Vedic literature, long hair often symbolizes strength, vitality, and a connection to the divine. For example, in many cultures, long hair is associated with spiritual leaders or warriors, representing their commitment and power.
  • harī vṛṣaṇā: "Harī" refers to the divine or the one who removes sins, while "vṛṣaṇā" can mean "the one who is strong" or "the bull." This can be interpreted as a call to the divine for strength and protection. In real life, this can be likened to seeking guidance from a mentor or a leader who embodies strength and wisdom during challenging times.
  • kakṣyaprā: This term can be understood as "the one who is in the assembly" or "the one who is present in the gathering." It emphasizes the importance of community and collective strength. In modern life, this can be seen in team sports or group projects where collaboration leads to success.
  • athā na indra somapā: "Athā" means "then," "na" means "not," "indra" refers to the king of gods, and "somapā" means "the drinker of soma." This part of the mantra suggests a plea or a statement regarding the divine's role in our lives. In practical terms, it can be related to the idea that even the most powerful beings need support and sustenance, much like how individuals rely on community and resources to thrive.
  • girām upaśrutiṁ cara: "Girām" means "of the voices" or "of the words," "upaśrutiṁ" refers to "the hearing" or "the understanding," and "cara" means "to move" or "to act." This can be interpreted as a call to listen to the wisdom of others and to act upon it. In everyday life, this highlights the importance of communication and understanding in relationships, whether personal or professional.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, community, divine guidance, and the importance of listening and acting upon wisdom. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to various aspects such as teamwork, leadership, and personal growth. By recognizing the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine, we can foster a supportive environment that encourages collaboration and strength in facing challenges. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek guidance, value community, and act with purpose in our lives.
