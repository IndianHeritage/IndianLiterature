दधुष् त्वा भृगवो मानवेषु आरयिं न चारुं सुहवं जनैभ्यः |होतारं अग्ने अतिथिं वरेण्यं मित्रं न शेवं दिव्याय जनमने ||
dadhuṣ ṭvā bhṛgavo mānuṣeṣv ā rayiṁ na cāruṁ suhavaṁ janebhyaḥ | hotāram agne atithiṁ vareṇyam mitraṁ na śevaṁ divyāya janmane ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O Agni, the Brighus (a family of sages) have given you a place among humans; may you grant us wealth that is not beautiful but is agreeable to all. You are the hotar (the priest) and the guest worthy of honor, O divine one, may you be our friend at birth."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • dadhuṣ: Given or bestowed.
    • ṭvā: You.
    • bhṛgavo: Brighus, referring to a lineage of sages known for their wisdom.
    • mānuṣeṣv: Among humans.
    • ā rayiṁ: Wealth or prosperity.
    • na: Not.
    • cāruṁ: Beautiful.
    • suhavaṁ: Agreeable or pleasant.
    • janebhyaḥ: To people or beings.
    • hotāram: The priest or the one who invokes.
    • agne: O Agni, the fire god.
    • atithiṁ: Guest.
    • vareṇyam: Worthy of honor or respect.
    • mitraṁ: Friend.
    • na: Not.
    • śevaṁ: Evil or harmful.
    • divyāya: Divine.
    • janmane: At birth.
  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of Agni, the fire god, as a central figure in Vedic rituals and human life. It acknowledges the blessings of wealth and prosperity that are agreeable to all, rather than merely beautiful or superficial wealth. The invocation of Agni as a friend and a divine guest highlights the significance of hospitality and respect in human relationships.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern society, this mantra can be applied to the concept of community and shared prosperity. It encourages individuals to seek wealth that benefits everyone rather than just personal gain.
    • The idea of treating guests with honor and respect is crucial in many cultures today, reflecting the importance of hospitality in building strong relationships.
    • In personal development, the mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate inner wealth—qualities like kindness, generosity, and wisdom—over material possessions.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking a harmonious and prosperous life through divine blessings, community spirit, and respectful relationships. It serves as a guiding principle for individuals and societies to foster unity, respect, and shared prosperity in their lives.
