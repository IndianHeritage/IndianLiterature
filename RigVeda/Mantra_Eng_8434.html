उभयतः पवमानस्य रश्मयो ध्रुवस्य सतः परि यान्ति केतवः |यदी पवित्रे अधि मृज्यते हरिः सत्तā नियोना कलशेषु सीदति ||
ubhayataḥ pavamānasya raśmayo dhruvasya sataḥ pari yanti ketavaḥ | yadī pavitre adhi mṛjyate hariḥ sattā ni yonā kalaśeṣu sīdati ||
  • Ubhayataḥ: This word translates to "from both sides" or "in all directions." It signifies the omnipresence of energy or divine influence.
  • Pavamānasya: Refers to the "purifying" or "sublime" aspect of the divine. It indicates a force that cleanses and elevates.
  • Raśmayo: This means "rays" or "light beams." It symbolizes knowledge, enlightenment, and the divine guidance that illuminates our path.
  • Dhruvasya: Translates to "constant" or "eternal." It represents the unchanging nature of truth and reality.
  • Sataḥ: Means "of the good" or "truthful." It emphasizes the importance of righteousness and virtue.
  • Pari yanti: This phrase means "they go around" or "they encompass." It suggests the idea of completeness and wholeness.
  • Ketavaḥ: Refers to "the wise" or "the enlightened ones." It denotes those who have attained knowledge and understanding.
  • Yadī: This means "if." It introduces a conditional aspect to the mantra.
  • Pavitre: Translates to "pure." It signifies the importance of purity in thought, action, and spirit.
  • Adhi: Means "upon" or "over." It indicates a position of elevation or superiority.
  • Mṛjyate: Refers to "is cleansed" or "is purified." It emphasizes the transformative power of purity.
  • Hariḥ: This is a name for the divine, often associated with Lord Vishnu. It represents the sustaining force of the universe.
  • Sattā: Means "existence" or "being." It refers to the essence of life and consciousness.
  • Ni yonā: Translates to "in the womb" or "in the source." It signifies the origin of creation and existence.
  • Kalaśeṣu: Refers to "in the vessels" or "in the containers." It symbolizes the physical forms that hold the essence of life.
  • Sīdati: Means "sits" or "resides." It indicates a state of being or existence within a particular context.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of purity, knowledge, and the eternal nature of truth. It suggests that divine energy purifies and elevates us, guiding us towards enlightenment. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal growth, spiritual practices, and ethical living. For instance, when we strive for purity in our thoughts and actions, we create a positive impact on ourselves and those around us. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek knowledge, embrace righteousness, and recognize the divine presence in all aspects of life.
