अत्रीणां स्तोमम् अद्रिवो महस् कृधि पिबा सोमम् मदाय कं शतक्रतो |यं ते भागम् अधारयन विश्वाः सेहानः पृतना उरु ज्रयः सम अप्सुजिन मरुत्वां इन्द्र सत्पते ||
atrīṇāṁ stomam adrivo mahas kṛdhi pibā somam madāya kaṁ śatakrato | yaṁ te bhāgam adhārayan viśvāḥ sehānaḥ pṛtanā uru jrayaḥ sam apsujin marutvām̐ indra satpate ||
  • atrīṇāṁ stomam: This phrase refers to the hymns or praises dedicated to the deities, particularly in the context of rituals. It emphasizes the importance of vocalizing gratitude and reverence.
  • adrivo mahas kṛdhi: This can be interpreted as a call to create greatness or abundance from the non-existent or the unseen. It suggests the power of intention and manifestation.
  • pibā somam madāya: Here, 'pibā' means to drink, and 'somam' refers to the sacred soma, a drink believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. This line encourages the consumption of knowledge and wisdom for spiritual elevation.
  • kaṁ śatakrato: 'Śatakrato' refers to Indra, the king of gods, known for his hundred powers. This line invokes Indra's strength and capability, suggesting reliance on divine support in endeavors.
  • yaṁ te bhāgam adhārayan viśvāḥ: This part speaks of the universal beings or forces that support and uphold the divine order. It highlights the interconnectedness of all entities in the universe.
  • sehānaḥ pṛtanā uru jrayaḥ: This can be interpreted as a call for strength and support from the divine, emphasizing the need for assistance in overcoming challenges.
  • sam apsujin marutvām̐: 'Marut' refers to the storm gods, symbolizing power and energy. This line suggests invoking the dynamic forces of nature to aid in one's pursuits.
  • indra satpate: This phrase acknowledges Indra as the lord of truth and righteousness, reinforcing the importance of integrity in one's actions.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support and strength in our endeavors. It emphasizes the importance of vocalizing gratitude, the power of intention, and the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, one can use the principles of intention and manifestation to achieve goals.
  • In community building, the mantra encourages collective support and strength in overcoming challenges.
  • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder to seek wisdom and guidance from higher powers.

Overall, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the divine support available to us and the importance of integrity and gratitude in our lives.
