त्यम् चिद इथ्था कत्वयं शयानम् असूर्ये तमसि वावृधानम् |तं चिन् मन्दानो वृषभः सुतस्योच्चैर इन्द्रो अपगूर्याः जग्हान ||
tyaṁ cid itthā katpayaṁ śayānam asūrye tamasi vāvṛdhānam | taṁ cin mandāno vṛṣabhaḥ sutasyoccair indro apagūryā jaghāna ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tyaṁ: that
    • cid: indeed
    • itthā: thus
    • katpayaṁ: what is the cause
    • śayānam: lying down
    • asūrye: in the absence of sunlight
    • tamasi: in darkness
    • vāvṛdhānam: that which is covered
    • taṁ: that
    • cin: indeed
    • mandāno: the one who is slow or dull
    • vṛṣabhaḥ: the bull, often symbolizing strength
    • sutasy: of the one who is born
    • occair: loudly
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • apagūryā: who is not to be ignored
    • jaghāna: struck or defeated
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nature of existence and the forces that govern it.
    • It reflects on the idea of what lies in darkness and how it is perceived.
    • The mention of Indra signifies the power and authority that can overcome obstacles.
    • In real life, this can be related to overcoming challenges and the importance of strength and resilience.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and confronting the darkness or ignorance in our lives.
    • It teaches us that even in the absence of light (knowledge or clarity), there is a force (like Indra) that can help us overcome difficulties.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in situations where one faces challenges, such as in personal struggles, professional obstacles, or societal issues.
    • It encourages individuals to seek strength and support from within and from their community to rise above adversities.
