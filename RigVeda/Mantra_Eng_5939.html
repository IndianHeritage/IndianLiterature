तस्मा इद आस्ये हविः जुहोताम् मधुमत्तमम् |इळां नः संयतम् करत् ||
tasmā id āsye havir juhotā madhumattamam | iḻāṁ naḥ saṁyataṁ karat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tasmā: Therefore
    • id: this
    • āsye: in this offering
    • havir: oblation or sacrifice
    • juhotā: one who offers
    • madhumattamam: the sweetest or most delightful
    • iḻāṁ: the divine essence or energy
    • naḥ: our
    • saṁyataṁ: control or restraint
    • karat: may it be done
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the act of offering and the importance of the oblation in spiritual practices.
    • The phrase "madhumattamam" suggests that the offerings should be of the highest quality, symbolizing the best of what we can give.
    • The invocation of "iḻāṁ" indicates a connection to divine energy, suggesting that our offerings should be made with intention and reverence.
    • The request for "saṁyataṁ" implies a desire for control over one's actions and thoughts, highlighting the need for discipline in spiritual pursuits.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be reflected in the way we approach our work and relationships. Just as one offers the best in a ritual, we should strive to give our best in our professional and personal lives.
    • For instance, a teacher offering their knowledge to students embodies the spirit of "havir juhotā," as they share their wisdom selflessly.
    • In a community setting, organizing a charity event can be seen as an offering to society, where the intention is to provide the best support to those in need.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of intention and quality in our offerings, whether they are material, emotional, or spiritual.
    • It encourages us to cultivate discipline and control in our actions, ensuring that we contribute positively to the world around us.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can enhance our spiritual practices and lead more fulfilling lives, ultimately connecting with the divine essence within and around us.
