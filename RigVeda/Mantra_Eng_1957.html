तव त्ये पीतो ददातस तव स्वादिष्ट ते पीतो |प्र स्वाद्मानो रसानां तुविग्रीवाऽवेरते ||
tava tye pito dadatas tava svādiṣṭha te pito | pra svādmāno rasānāṁ tuvigrīvā iverate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Tava: Your
    • Tye: Those
    • Pito: Who are nourished or fed
    • Dadatas: Bestowers or givers
    • Svādiṣṭha: Most delightful or pleasing
    • Te: Your
    • Pito: Who are nourished or fed
    • Pra: Forth or forward
    • Svādmāno: Enjoying sweetness or delight
    • Rasānāṁ: Of flavors or essences
    • Tuvigrīvā: With great strength or power
    • Iverate: To be praised or honored
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine qualities of nourishment and delight.
    • It emphasizes the importance of sharing and giving, as well as the joy that comes from enjoying the fruits of life.
    • The repetition of "pito" highlights the significance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual.
    • The phrase "svādiṣṭha" suggests that the essence of life is found in the delightful experiences we share with others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, sharing meals can be a source of joy and connection, embodying the essence of nourishment.
    • In community gatherings, the act of sharing food and experiences fosters a sense of belonging and happiness.
    • In professional environments, collaboration and sharing knowledge can lead to collective success and satisfaction.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of nourishment, both in a physical and spiritual sense.
    • It teaches us that sharing and giving are essential for creating joy and connection in our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in families, communities, or workplaces, the principles of nourishment and delight can lead to stronger bonds and a more fulfilling existence.
