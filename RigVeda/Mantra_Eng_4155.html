आ वो यन्तूदवाहासो अद्य वृष्टिं ये विश्वे मरुतो जुनेन्ति |अयं यो अग्निर मरुतः समिद्ध एतं जुषध्वं कवयो युवानः ||
ā vo yantūdavāhāso adya vṛṣṭiṁ ye viśve maruto junanti | ayaṁ yo agnir marutaḥ samiddha etaṁ juṣadhvaṁ kavayo yuvānaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vo: To you all
    • yantū: Who are moving or traveling
    • davāhāso: With great speed or force
    • adya: Today
    • vṛṣṭiṁ: Rain or abundance
    • ye: Who
    • viśve: All or universal
    • maruto: The Maruts, storm deities
    • junanti: Bring forth or produce
    • ayaṁ: This
    • yo: Who
    • agnir: Fire
    • marutaḥ: Of the Maruts
    • samiddha: Kindled or ignited
    • etaṁ: This
    • juṣadhvaṁ: May you enjoy or partake
    • kavayo: The wise or poets
    • yuvānaḥ: Young or youthful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Maruts, the storm deities, asking them to bring rain and abundance today.
    • It emphasizes the connection between natural elements (like rain and fire) and the divine forces that govern them.
    • The mention of "kavayo yuvānaḥ" suggests that wisdom and youthfulness are essential for understanding and enjoying the gifts of nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain to ensure a good harvest. This mantra reflects that sentiment, highlighting the importance of rain for sustenance.
    • In modern times, environmentalists may relate to this mantra as they advocate for the preservation of natural resources, recognizing the role of weather patterns in ecological balance.
    • During festivals, communities may perform rituals invoking rain and prosperity, similar to the invocation in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine forces in the natural world, particularly in relation to rain and fire.
    • It teaches us to respect and appreciate the elements that sustain life, encouraging a harmonious relationship with nature.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder of our dependence on natural phenomena and the need for gratitude and reverence towards the forces that shape our environment.
