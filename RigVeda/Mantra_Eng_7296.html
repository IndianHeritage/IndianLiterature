उप श्रक्वेषु बाप्सतः कृत्वते धरुणं दिवि |इन्द्रे अग्ना नमः स्वः ||
upa srakveṣu bapsataḥ kṛṇvate dharuṇaṁ divi | indre agnā namaḥ svaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • upa: towards, near
    • srakveṣu: in the offerings, in the sacrifices
    • bapsataḥ: of the waters, of the streams
    • kṛṇvate: they make, they create
    • dharuṇaṁ: strong, powerful
    • divi: in the heavens, in the sky
    • indre: to Indra, the king of gods
    • agnā: to the fire, the divine energy
    • namaḥ: homage, salutation
    • svaḥ: the realm of the heavens, the spiritual world
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine energies associated with Indra and Agni, emphasizing the importance of offerings and sacrifices.
    • The phrase "upa srakveṣu bapsataḥ" suggests a connection to the natural elements, particularly water, which is essential for life and sustenance.
    • "Kṛṇvate dharuṇaṁ divi" indicates the creation of strength and power in the heavens, symbolizing the divine support that comes from the universe when one engages in righteous actions.
    • The invocation to Indra and Agni signifies the importance of acknowledging and respecting the forces of nature and the divine, which can be seen in various cultural practices around the world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals and offerings are made to honor the elements, such as water and fire, which are seen as vital for life. For instance, in Hinduism, the practice of performing Homa (fire rituals) is a way to connect with Agni.
    • Environmental conservation efforts can be viewed as a modern-day interpretation of this mantra, where individuals and communities work to protect water sources and natural resources, acknowledging their divine significance.
    • In personal development, the idea of creating strength and power in one's life can be related to setting goals and making sacrifices to achieve them, much like the offerings made in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of nature, the divine, and human actions. It serves as a reminder of the importance of respect and gratitude towards the elements that sustain life.
    • In real life, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide individuals in their spiritual practices, environmental stewardship, and personal growth, encouraging a harmonious relationship with the world around them.
    • Ultimately, it signifies that through our actions and offerings, we can invoke divine support and strength, leading to a more fulfilling and purposeful life.
