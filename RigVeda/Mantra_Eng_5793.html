सम्राल् अन्यः स्वराल् अन्य उच्यते वाम महान्ताव इन्द्रावरुणा महावसू |विश्वे देवासः परम व्योमनि सं वाम ओजो वृषणा सम बलं दधुः ||
samrāḻ anyaḥ svarāḻ anya ucyate vām mahāntāv indrāvaruṇā mahāvasū | viśve devāsaḥ parame vyomani saṁ vām ojo vṛṣaṇā sam balaṁ dadhuḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra speaks of the divine powers of Indra and Varuna, highlighting their greatness and the blessings they bestow upon the world.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • samrāḻ: The supreme ruler or king.
    • anyaḥ: Another or different.
    • svarāḻ: The one who is self-illuminated or radiant.
    • ucyate: Is said or is declared.
    • vām: Your or you (plural).
    • mahāntāv: The great ones.
    • indrāvaruṇā: Referring to the deities Indra and Varuna.
    • mahāvasū: The great wealth or abundance.
    • viśve: All or universal.
    • devāsaḥ: The divine beings or gods.
    • parame: Supreme or highest.
    • vyomani: In the sky or celestial realm.
    • saṁ: Together or in union.
    • ojo: Vitality or energy.
    • vṛṣaṇā: Of the strong or powerful.
    • sam: Together or in harmony.
    • balaṁ: Strength or power.
    • dadhuḥ: They have given or bestowed.
  • Meaning: This mantra emphasizes the unity and strength of the divine forces represented by Indra and Varuna. It suggests that these deities, who embody different aspects of nature and existence, work together to provide strength and vitality to all beings. The invocation of their names signifies a call for their blessings and support in achieving harmony and abundance in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, when people come together to support each other during difficult times, they embody the spirit of this mantra by invoking the strength and unity of divine forces.
    • In personal development, individuals may seek the blessings of Indra and Varuna through meditation or prayer, aiming to harness their energy and vitality to overcome challenges and achieve their goals.
    • In environmental conservation efforts, the mantra can be seen as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of respecting and nurturing the natural world, which is often represented by the divine forces of Indra (rain, storms) and Varuna (water, cosmic order).
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and divine blessings. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking support from higher powers in our endeavors. In real life, it encourages individuals and communities to come together, harness their collective strength, and strive for harmony and abundance in all aspects of life.
