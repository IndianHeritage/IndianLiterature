विषां कविं विष्पतिम मानवीर इषः सं सीम अकृत्वान स्वधितिं न तेजसे |स उर्वतो निवतो याती वेविषत स गर्भं एषु भुवनेषु दीधरत ||
viśāṁ kaviṁ viśpatim mānuṣīr iṣaḥ saṁ sīm akṛṇvan svadhitiṁ na tejase | sa udvato nivato yāti veviṣat sa garbham eṣu bhuvaneṣu dīdharat ||
  • viśāṁ: This word refers to "the people" or "the community." It signifies the collective consciousness and unity among individuals.
  • kaviṁ: Translated as "the poet" or "the seer," this term represents wisdom, creativity, and the ability to perceive deeper truths.
  • viśpatim: Meaning "the lord of the universe," it denotes a higher power or divine authority that governs the cosmos.
  • mānuṣīr: This word means "human" or "of mankind," emphasizing the human experience and our connection to the divine.
  • iṣaḥ: Translated as "the will" or "the desire," it reflects the intentions and aspirations that drive individuals and communities.
  • saṁ sīm: This phrase can be understood as "to the limit" or "to the boundary," indicating the extent of human experience and understanding.
  • akṛṇvan: Meaning "to create" or "to establish," it signifies the act of bringing forth new ideas, structures, or realities.
  • svadhitiṁ: This term refers to "self-knowledge" or "self-awareness," highlighting the importance of understanding oneself in the broader context of existence.
  • na tejase: Translated as "not to the light" or "not to the brilliance," it suggests a caution against superficiality or mere appearances.
  • sa udvato: Meaning "he who rises" or "he who ascends," it symbolizes growth, elevation, and the pursuit of higher truths.
  • nivato: This word means "calm" or "peaceful," indicating the importance of tranquility in the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom.
  • yāti: Translated as "goes" or "moves," it signifies the journey of life and the continuous quest for understanding.
  • veviṣat: Meaning "to enter" or "to penetrate," it reflects the idea of delving deep into the mysteries of existence.
  • sa garbham: This phrase means "he who holds" or "he who contains," symbolizing the nurturing aspect of creation and the potential within.
  • eṣu bhuvaneṣu: Translated as "in these worlds," it emphasizes the interconnectedness of all realms of existence.
  • dīdharat: Meaning "he supports" or "he sustains," it signifies the role of the divine or higher powers in maintaining balance and harmony.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of human existence, creativity, and the pursuit of knowledge. It emphasizes the importance of community, self-awareness, and the divine guidance that supports our journey through life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in collaborative efforts within communities, the creative processes in art and literature, and the quest for personal growth and understanding. It serves as a reminder that while we strive for individual aspirations, we are also part of a larger tapestry of existence, where our actions and intentions resonate with the universe. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, wisdom, and the nurturing of our inner potential, guiding us towards a more harmonious and enlightened existence.
