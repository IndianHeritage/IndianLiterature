सजूर आदित्यैः वसुभिः सजूर इन्द्रेण वायुनā |आ याह्य अग्ने अतृवात सुते रण ||
sajūr ādityair vasubhiḥ sajūr indreṇa vāyunā | ā yāhy agne atrivat sute raṇa ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sajūr: Together, in harmony
    • ādityair: With the Adityas (solar deities)
    • vasubhiḥ: With the Vasus (elements or deities of abundance)
    • indreṇa: With Indra (the king of gods, associated with strength and rain)
    • vāyunā: With Vayu (the wind god)
    • ā: To, towards
    • yāhy: May we go, may we approach
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • atrivat: Like the one who is invoked
    • sute: In the offering, in the sacrifice
    • raṇa: Battle, conflict
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the collective powers of various deities, emphasizing the importance of unity and collaboration.
    • It highlights the significance of approaching Agni, the fire god, who symbolizes transformation and purification, especially in the context of offerings and sacrifices.
    • The mention of Indra and Vayu suggests a call for strength and support in overcoming challenges, akin to seeking help from friends or mentors in difficult times.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team project, individuals must work together harmoniously, much like the deities mentioned, to achieve a common goal.
    • When facing personal challenges, one might seek guidance from mentors (akin to Indra) and support from friends (like Vayu) to navigate through tough situations.
    • In rituals or ceremonies, invoking Agni can be seen as a metaphor for initiating change or transformation in one’s life, such as starting a new job or moving to a new place.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity, collaboration, and seeking divine support in our endeavors.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from teamwork in professional settings to personal growth and transformation.
    • By invoking the energies of Agni, Indra, and Vayu, individuals can find strength and guidance in their journeys, reminding us that we are not alone in our struggles.
