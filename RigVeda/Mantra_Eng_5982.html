प्रास्मै गायत्रं अर्चत वावातुर यः पुरंदरः |याभिः काण्वस्योप बरहिर आसदं यासद वज्री भिनत पुरः ||
prāsmai gāyatram arcata vāvātur yaḥ puraṁdaraḥ | yābhiḥ kāṇvasyopa barhir āsadaṁ yāsad vajrī bhinat puraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prāsmai: To the one who is worthy of praise
    • gāyatram: The Gayatri mantra, a sacred verse
    • arcata: We worship or honor
    • vāvātur: May he be favorable or auspicious
    • yaḥ: Who
    • puraṁdaraḥ: The destroyer of cities, often referring to Indra
    • yābhiḥ: With whom
    • kāṇvasy: Of the Kānva (a sage or lineage)
    • opa: Near or close
    • barhir: The covering or the skin
    • āsadaṁ: The seat or abode
    • yāsad: Who has come
    • vajrī: The one who wields the thunderbolt (Indra)
    • bhinat: Who breaks or shatters
    • puraḥ: In front or ahead
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of gods, who is often associated with strength, rain, and thunder.
    • It emphasizes the importance of worshiping the divine for blessings and protection.
    • The reference to the Gayatri mantra signifies the invocation of higher consciousness and enlightenment.
    • In real life, this can be seen as a call for divine intervention in times of struggle or adversity, much like how people pray for strength during challenging times.
    • The mention of "vajrī" symbolizes the power to overcome obstacles, akin to how individuals face and conquer their fears or challenges in life.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the relationship between humans and the divine, emphasizing the need for reverence and connection.
    • It serves as a reminder that seeking divine guidance can lead to strength and resilience in the face of difficulties.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be recited during rituals, meditation, or personal prayer to invoke protection and blessings.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of gratitude and respect towards the forces that govern life.
