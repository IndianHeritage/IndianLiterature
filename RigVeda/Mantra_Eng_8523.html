नū नो रयिम उप मास्व नृवंतम पुनानो वाताप्यं विश्वचन्द्रम् | प्र वन्दितुर इन्दो ताऱ्य आयुः प्रातर मक्षू धियावसुर जगम्यāt ||
nū no rayim upa māsva nṛvantam punāno vātāpyaṁ viśvaścandram | pra vanditur indo tāry āyuḥ prātar makṣū dhiyāvasur jagamyāt ||
  • nū no rayim upa māsva: This phrase can be translated as "May you bring us wealth." Here, "rayim" refers to wealth or prosperity, and "upa māsva" means to come near or to approach. In real life, this can be understood as a prayer or invocation for abundance in various forms, whether it be financial, emotional, or spiritual.
  • nṛvantam punāno: This translates to "reviving the living." "Nṛvantam" refers to the living beings, and "punāno" means to rejuvenate or restore. This can be seen in the context of environmental conservation, where efforts are made to revive ecosystems and support biodiversity, ensuring that life thrives on Earth.
  • vātāpyaṁ viśvaścandram: This can be interpreted as "the wind that brings the universal moon." "Vātāpyaṁ" refers to the wind or air, and "viśvaścandram" refers to the universal or all-encompassing moon. This symbolizes the interconnectedness of nature and the cosmos, reminding us of the importance of harmony between the elements and the universe. In real life, this can be reflected in practices like mindfulness and meditation, which help individuals connect with the larger universe.
  • pra vanditur indo: This means "may we praise the moon." "Pra vanditur" indicates the act of praising or honoring, while "indo" refers to the moon or lunar deity. This can be related to cultural practices where people celebrate the moon, such as during festivals like Karva Chauth or the Mid-Autumn Festival, where the moon is revered for its beauty and significance.
  • tāry āyuḥ: This translates to "long life." "Tāry" means to prolong or extend, and "āyuḥ" refers to life. This phrase emphasizes the desire for longevity and health, which is a universal aspiration. In real life, this can be seen in the emphasis on healthy living, nutrition, and wellness practices that aim to enhance the quality and duration of life.
  • prātar makṣū dhiyāvasur jagamyāt: This can be interpreted as "may we attain wisdom in the morning." "Prātar" means morning, "makṣū" refers to wisdom or intelligence, "dhiyā" means through understanding, and "avasur jagamyāt" implies the attainment or realization. This highlights the importance of starting the day with clarity and purpose, which can be applied in daily routines where individuals set intentions or engage in morning rituals to foster mindfulness and productivity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the pursuit of wealth, the revival of life, the interconnectedness of nature, the reverence for celestial bodies, the aspiration for longevity, and the importance of wisdom. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, encouraging individuals to seek prosperity, nurture the environment, honor traditions, promote health, and cultivate knowledge. In a real-life scenario, this mantra can serve as a guiding principle for personal development, community engagement, and environmental stewardship, fostering a balanced and fulfilling existence.
