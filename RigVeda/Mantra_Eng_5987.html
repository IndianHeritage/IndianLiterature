मां भूम निष्ठ्या इवेन्द्र त्वद अरणा इव |वनानि न प्रजहितानि अद्रिवो दुरोषासो अमन्महि ||
mā bhūma niṣṭyā ivendra tvad araṇā iva | vanāni na prajahitāny adrivo duroṣāso amanmahi ||
  • mā bhūma niṣṭyā ivendra tvad araṇā iva
    • mā bhūma: This phrase translates to "do not let us be deprived" or "may we not be abandoned." It expresses a plea for protection and support.
    • niṣṭyā: This word signifies "with firmness" or "steadfastness," indicating a strong desire for stability and security.
    • ivendra: Referring to "O Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and power.
    • tvad araṇā iva: This means "like your refuge," suggesting that the speaker seeks the same kind of shelter and protection that Indra provides.
  • vanāni na prajahitāny adrivo duroṣāso amanmahi
    • vanāni: This translates to "forests" or "natural surroundings," representing the abundance of nature.
    • na prajahitāny: Meaning "do not abandon," it emphasizes the importance of not forsaking the natural world.
    • adrivo: This term refers to "mountains," symbolizing strength and stability.
    • duroṣāso: This means "difficult to overcome," indicating challenges that may arise in life.
    • amanmahi: This translates to "we do not desire," suggesting a rejection of negativity or harm.

The mantra as a whole can be interpreted as a prayer for protection and stability, invoking the strength of Indra while emphasizing the importance of nature and the refusal to abandon it. It reflects a deep connection to the environment and a desire for harmony with the natural world.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity with nature and the divine. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to environmental conservation efforts, where individuals and communities strive to protect their natural surroundings. It serves as a reminder that just as we seek refuge and strength from higher powers, we must also cherish and safeguard the earth that sustains us. This mantra encourages a balanced relationship with nature, urging us to recognize the challenges we face and to remain steadfast in our commitment to preserving the environment for future generations.
