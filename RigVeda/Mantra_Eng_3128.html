अभ्रातरो न योषणो व्यन्तः पतिरिपो न जनयो दुरेवाः |पापासः सन्तो अनृताऽसत्याः इदं पदम् अजनतā गभीरम् ||
abhrātaro na yoṣaṇo vyantaḥ patiripo na janayo durevāḥ | pāpāsaḥ santo anṛtā asatyā idam padam ajanatā gabhīram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhrātaro: Those who are like clouds or mist.
    • na: Not.
    • yoṣaṇo: Women or feminine energy.
    • vyantaḥ: Those who are scattered or dispersed.
    • patiripo: The enemy or adversary.
    • na: Not.
    • janayo: The people or the community.
    • durevāḥ: Distant or far away.
    • pāpāsaḥ: Those who are sinful or wicked.
    • santo: The good or virtuous.
    • anṛtā: Falsehood or untruth.
    • asatyā: The unreal or deceptive.
    • idam: This.
    • padam: State or condition.
    • ajanatā: Created or manifested.
    • gabhīram: Deep or profound.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nature of reality and the dualities present in life.
    • The mention of "abhrātaro" and "yoṣaṇo" suggests a connection to the feminine aspect of existence, which can be seen as nurturing yet often overlooked.
    • The phrase "patiripo" indicates the presence of adversaries or challenges that one faces in life, which can be both external and internal.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community ("janayo") and how distance ("durevāḥ") can create barriers to understanding and connection.
    • The contrast between "pāpāsaḥ" (wicked) and "santo" (virtuous) highlights the moral choices individuals must make.
    • Furthermore, the terms "anṛtā" and "asatyā" remind us of the prevalence of falsehood in the world, urging us to seek truth and authenticity.
    • The mantra concludes with a reflection on the profound nature of existence ("gabhīram"), suggesting that understanding these concepts leads to deeper wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal relationships, recognizing the "feminine energy" can lead to more nurturing and supportive interactions.
    • In a workplace setting, understanding the challenges posed by adversaries can help in developing strategies to overcome them.
    • Community engagement can bridge the "distance" between people, fostering a sense of belonging and support.
    • Making ethical choices in business can reflect the struggle between "wicked" and "virtuous" paths, impacting society positively.
    • In a world filled with misinformation, seeking "truth" becomes essential for personal integrity and societal progress.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the complexities of life, urging individuals to navigate through challenges with wisdom and integrity.
    • Its significance lies in promoting awareness of the dualities present in our lives and the importance of community, truth, and ethical living.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a more harmonious society.
