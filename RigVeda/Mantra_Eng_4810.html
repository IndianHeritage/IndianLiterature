त्वां उग्रं अवसे चर्षणीसहं राजन् देवेषु हूमहे |विश्वा सु नो विथुराऽ पिब्दना वसोऽमित्रान सुषहान कृधि ||
tvām ugram avase carṣaṇīsahaṁ rājan deveṣu hūmahe | viśvā su no vithurā pibdanā vaso 'mitrān suṣahān kṛdhi ||
  • tvām ugram avase carṣaṇīsahaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke you, O powerful one, along with the forces of nature." Here, "tvām" refers to the divine or the powerful entity being addressed, while "ugram" signifies strength and intensity. "Avase" indicates the act of invoking or calling upon, and "carṣaṇī" refers to the natural forces or energies that surround us.
  • rājan deveṣu hūmahe: This translates to "O King, we call upon you among the gods." "Rājan" means king, suggesting a leader or a supreme being, while "deveṣu" refers to the divine or the gods. "Hūmahe" is a term used for invocation, indicating a plea or a call for assistance.
  • viśvā su no vithurā pibdanā: This can be interpreted as "May all good things come to us." "Viśvā" means all or everything, "su" signifies goodness, and "no" refers to us. "Vithurā" implies the act of bestowing or granting, while "pibdanā" suggests the idea of drinking or receiving abundance.
  • vaso 'mitrān suṣahān kṛdhi: This translates to "O Vasu, make us friends with the good." "Vaso" refers to the divine or the auspicious, "amitran" means friends or allies, and "suṣahān" indicates goodness or well-being. "Kṛdhi" is a call to action, asking for the establishment of friendly relations.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of invoking divine powers and natural forces for strength and abundance in life. It highlights the need for unity and friendship among individuals and the divine, suggesting that by calling upon these higher powers, one can receive blessings and prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as during rituals, prayers, or even in personal reflections where one seeks strength, support, and positive relationships. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking harmony and goodwill in our interactions.
