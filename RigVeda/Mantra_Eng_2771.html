इन्द्रः स्वर्षा जनयन्न अहानि जिगायोशिग्भिः पृतना अभिष्टिः |प्रारोचयन मनवे केतुम अह्नाम अविन्दज ज्योतिर बृहत् रणाय ||
indraḥ svarṣā janayann ahāni jigāyośigbhiḥ pṛtanā abhiṣṭiḥ | prārocayan manave ketum ahnām avindaj jyotir bṛhate raṇāya ||
  • Indraḥ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Svarṣā: This term can be interpreted as "the shining" or "the radiant," indicating the brilliance associated with divine beings.
  • Janayann: Means "giving birth" or "creating," suggesting the act of bringing forth or generating something new.
  • Ahāni: Translates to "days" or "time," emphasizing the cyclical nature of existence and the passage of time.
  • Jigāyośigbhiḥ: This term can be broken down to imply "victory" or "conquest," highlighting the triumph over challenges.
  • Pṛtanā: Refers to "praise" or "celebration," indicating the importance of acknowledging achievements and divine blessings.
  • Abhiṣṭiḥ: Means "blessings" or "wishes," suggesting the fulfillment of desires and aspirations.
  • Prārocayan: Translates to "inspiring" or "encouraging," indicating the role of divine influence in motivating individuals.
  • Manave: Refers to "Manu," the progenitor of humanity, symbolizing the human race and its responsibilities.
  • Ketum: Means "banner" or "flag," representing identity, purpose, and the goals one strives to achieve.
  • Ahnām: Refers to "day" or "light," symbolizing clarity, knowledge, and enlightenment.
  • Avindaj: Means "unobstructed" or "unhindered," indicating the removal of obstacles in the pursuit of goals.
  • Jyotir: Translates to "light" or "radiance," symbolizing knowledge, wisdom, and divine guidance.
  • Bṛhate: Means "great" or "vast," indicating the expansive nature of the universe and the divine.
  • Raṇāya: Refers to "battle" or "struggle," symbolizing the challenges faced in life and the need for courage.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine guidance, strength, and the cyclical nature of life. It emphasizes the importance of overcoming challenges, celebrating achievements, and seeking blessings. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, leadership, and community building. For instance, in a workplace setting, a leader can draw inspiration from this mantra to motivate their team, celebrate successes, and navigate challenges with resilience. The mantra serves as a reminder that with divine support and inner strength, one can achieve greatness and fulfill their aspirations.
