त्वं अग्नि प्रथमो अङ्गिरस्तमः कविः देवानाम् परिभूषसि व्रतम् |विभुर विश्वस्मै भुवनाय मेधिरो द्विमाता शयुः कतिधा चिद आयवे ||
tvam agne prathamo aṅgirastamaḥ kavir devānām pari bhūṣasi vratam | vibhur viśvasmai bhuvanāya medhiro dvimātā śayuḥ katidhā cid āyave ||
  • tvam agne - "You, O Agni" - This refers to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a vital force in Vedic rituals and symbolizes purity and transformation.
  • prathamo aṅgirastamaḥ - "the first among the Angiras" - Angiras refers to a group of sages or seers. This phrase highlights Agni's primary role among them, indicating his importance in spiritual and ritualistic contexts.
  • kavir devānām - "the wise one among the gods" - This emphasizes Agni's intelligence and insight, suggesting that he possesses knowledge that is crucial for the divine order.
  • pari bhūṣasi vratam - "you adorn the vow" - This indicates that Agni is not only a witness to vows taken during rituals but also enhances their significance, making them more powerful.
  • vibhur viśvasmai - "the all-pervading one" - This suggests that Agni is omnipresent, symbolizing the universal nature of fire and its essential role in life.
  • bhuvanāya medhiro - "for the well-being of the worlds" - This phrase indicates that Agni's presence and blessings are essential for the prosperity and health of all realms of existence.
  • dvimātā - "two mothers" - This could refer to the dual nature of Agni, representing both the nurturing and destructive aspects of fire.
  • śayuḥ katidhā cid āyave - "how many times have you been invoked?" - This reflects the idea of repeated invocations of Agni in various rituals, highlighting his significance in spiritual practices.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the multifaceted nature of Agni as a divine force that is central to Vedic rituals and spiritual practices. Agni represents transformation, purity, and the connection between the physical and spiritual realms. In real life, the significance of this mantra can be seen in various rituals where fire is used as a medium to connect with the divine, such as in weddings, homas (fire sacrifices), and other ceremonies. The invocation of Agni serves as a reminder of the importance of purity, commitment, and the transformative power of fire in our lives. By recognizing Agni's role, individuals can cultivate a deeper understanding of their own spiritual journeys and the vows they take in life.
