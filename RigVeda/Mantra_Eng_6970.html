दानासः पृथुश्रवसः कानीतस्य सुराधसः | रथं हिरण्ययं ददन मंहिष्ठः सूरीर अभूद वर्षिष्ठम अकृत श्रवः ||
dānāsaḥ pṛthuśravasaḥ kānītasya surādhasaḥ | rathaṁ hiraṇyayaṁ dadan maṁhiṣṭhaḥ sūrir abhūd varṣiṣṭham akṛta śravaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dānāsaḥ: Of the generous or those who give.
    • pṛthuśravasaḥ: Of great fame or renown.
    • kānītasya: Of the one who is skilled or adept.
    • surādhasaḥ: Of the divine or celestial beings.
    • rathaṁ: The chariot.
    • hiraṇyayaṁ: Made of gold or precious.
    • dadan: Giving or bestowing.
    • maṁhiṣṭhaḥ: The best or the most excellent.
    • sūrir: The wise or the learned.
    • abhūd: Has become or has manifested.
    • varṣiṣṭham: The most prosperous or abundant.
    • akṛta: Not done or unmade.
    • śravaḥ: Fame or glory.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the qualities of generosity, fame, skill, and divine attributes.
    • It emphasizes the importance of giving and the prosperity that comes from such actions.
    • The imagery of a golden chariot symbolizes wealth, success, and the journey of life.
    • The wise and learned individuals are recognized for their contributions and are seen as the most prosperous.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, philanthropists who donate to charitable causes exemplify the spirit of dānāsaḥ. For instance, individuals like Bill Gates and Warren Buffet have dedicated significant portions of their wealth to improve global health and education.
    • Famous leaders and innovators, such as Mahatma Gandhi and Nelson Mandela, are remembered for their great fame and renown, embodying pṛthuśravasaḥ through their impactful legacies.
    • In the business world, successful entrepreneurs who create jobs and contribute to the economy can be seen as kānītasya, showcasing their skills and expertise.
    • The concept of a golden chariot can be likened to achieving one's dreams and aspirations, where hard work and dedication lead to success and recognition.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of generosity, wisdom, and the pursuit of excellence in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that true prosperity comes not just from material wealth but from the impact one has on others and the legacy one leaves behind.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a fulfilling life, where individuals strive to be generous, wise, and skilled in their endeavors, ultimately contributing to a better society.
