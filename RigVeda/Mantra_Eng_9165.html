उषासनक्ता बृहती सुपेशसां dyावाक्षामां वरुणो मित्रो आर्यमाः | इन्द्रं हुवे मरुतः पर्वतां अप आदित्यां dyावापृथिवी अपः स्वः ||
uṣāsānaktā bṛhatī supeśasā dyāvākṣāmā varuṇo mitro aryamā | indraṁ huve marutaḥ parvatām̐ apa ādityān dyāvāpṛthivī apaḥ svaḥ ||
  • uṣāsānaktā: This term refers to the dawn or the goddess of dawn, Usha. In real life, dawn symbolizes new beginnings and hope. For example, every morning brings a fresh start, allowing individuals to set new goals and intentions.
  • bṛhatī: This word means vast or great. It signifies the expansive nature of the universe and life itself. In practical terms, it can relate to the vast opportunities available to us in life, encouraging us to explore and embrace them.
  • supeśasā: This translates to well-dressed or adorned. It can be interpreted as the beauty and richness of life. For instance, when we take care of our appearance or environment, it reflects our respect for ourselves and our surroundings.
  • dyāvākṣāmā: This term refers to the sky or heavens. It represents the infinite possibilities and aspirations we have. In everyday life, looking up at the sky can inspire us to dream big and reach for our goals.
  • varuṇo: Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water. This signifies the importance of balance and harmony in our lives. For example, maintaining a balance between work and personal life is crucial for overall well-being.
  • mitro: This means friend. It emphasizes the value of friendship and community. In real life, having supportive friends can significantly impact our mental health and happiness.
  • aryamā: This term refers to a noble or honorable being. It encourages us to strive for integrity and honor in our actions. For instance, being honest in our dealings fosters trust and respect in relationships.
  • indraṁ: Indra is the king of gods and represents strength and power. This can be related to personal empowerment, where individuals harness their inner strength to overcome challenges.
  • huve: This means to invoke or call upon. It signifies the importance of intention and prayer in our lives. For example, setting intentions at the beginning of a project can lead to greater focus and success.
  • marutaḥ: Referring to the storm gods, this symbolizes the forces of nature and change. In life, embracing change can lead to growth and new opportunities.
  • parvatām̐: This means mountains. Mountains symbolize challenges and obstacles. In real life, overcoming personal challenges can lead to personal growth and resilience.
  • apa: This term means water. Water is essential for life and symbolizes nourishment and purification. In practical terms, staying hydrated and maintaining a healthy lifestyle is crucial for well-being.
  • ādityān: Referring to the sun gods, this signifies light and knowledge. In life, seeking knowledge and enlightenment is vital for personal and professional development.
  • dyāvāpṛthivī: This term refers to the sky and earth, symbolizing the connection between the spiritual and material worlds. In real life, finding balance between our spiritual beliefs and material needs is essential for a fulfilling life.
  • apaḥ: This means waters again, emphasizing the importance of emotional and spiritual nourishment. For instance, engaging in activities that bring joy and peace can rejuvenate our spirits.
  • svaḥ: This refers to the heavens or the realm of the gods, symbolizing ultimate bliss and fulfillment. In life, striving for happiness and contentment is a universal goal.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of new beginnings, balance, friendship, strength, and the pursuit of knowledge. It serves as a reminder to embrace change, seek harmony, and strive for personal growth. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide individuals in their daily lives, encouraging them to cultivate positive relationships, overcome challenges, and pursue their aspirations with integrity and hope.
