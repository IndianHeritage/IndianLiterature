पावीरवी कन्या चित्रायुः सरस्वती वीरपत्नी धियं धात् |ज्ञानभिर अचिद्रम् शरणं सजोषा दुराधर्षं गृणते शर्मा यंषत् ||
pāvīravī kanyā citrāyuḥ sarasvatī vīrapatnī dhiyaṁ dhāt | gnābhir acchidraṁ śaraṇaṁ sajoṣā durādharṣaṁ gṛṇate śarma yaṁsat ||
  • pāvīravī: This term can be interpreted as "purifying" or "sacred." In real life, this can relate to the importance of maintaining purity in thoughts and actions, akin to how one might strive for cleanliness in their environment or personal conduct.
  • kanyā: This word means "daughter" or "virgin." It signifies the respect and value of women in society. For example, in many cultures, daughters are seen as bearers of tradition and values, highlighting the importance of nurturing and educating them.
  • citrāyuḥ: This translates to "having a colorful life" or "vibrant existence." It suggests living life to the fullest, embracing diversity and experiences. In modern life, this can be seen in the pursuit of varied interests, hobbies, and relationships that enrich one’s life.
  • sarasvatī: Referring to the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning. In practical terms, this emphasizes the value of education and creativity in personal and professional development, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and express themselves artistically.
  • vīrapatnī: This means "the wife of a hero" or "valiant partner." It highlights the importance of partnership and support in relationships. In real life, this can be seen in how partners uplift each other in their endeavors, whether in personal goals or professional achievements.
  • dhiyaṁ: This word means "intellect" or "thought." It underscores the significance of wisdom and thoughtful decision-making in life. For instance, making informed choices in career paths or personal relationships reflects the application of intellect.
  • dhāt: This can be interpreted as "to support" or "to uphold." It signifies the importance of support systems in life, such as family, friends, and community, which help individuals navigate challenges.
  • gnābhir: This term relates to "knowledge" or "understanding." It emphasizes the need for awareness and insight in various aspects of life, such as social issues or personal growth.
  • acchidraṁ: Meaning "without flaws" or "perfect." This suggests striving for excellence in one’s endeavors, whether in work, relationships, or personal development.
  • śaraṇaṁ: This translates to "refuge" or "shelter." It signifies the importance of having a safe space or support system where one can find solace during difficult times.
  • sajoṣā: This means "with joy" or "happiness." It highlights the importance of joy in life, encouraging individuals to seek happiness in their daily activities and relationships.
  • durādharṣaṁ: This term means "difficult to overcome" or "invincible." It signifies resilience and strength in facing challenges, akin to how individuals might overcome personal or professional obstacles.
  • gṛṇate: This translates to "to sing" or "to praise." It emphasizes the importance of expressing gratitude and appreciation in life, whether through words, actions, or art.
  • śarma: Meaning "comfort" or "peace." It signifies the ultimate goal of life, which is to achieve a state of tranquility and contentment.
  • yaṁsat: This can be interpreted as "that which is desired" or "what one seeks." It reflects the aspirations and goals individuals have in life, encouraging them to pursue their dreams.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of living a life filled with purity, knowledge, joy, and resilience. It emphasizes the importance of support systems, the pursuit of education, and the value of relationships. In real-life scenarios, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for individuals to strive for excellence, seek knowledge, and maintain a positive outlook, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and harmonious life.
