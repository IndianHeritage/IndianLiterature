मन्त्र: चन्द्रम् अग्निं चन्द्ररथं हरिव्रतं वैश्वानरम् अप्सुषदं स्वर्विदम् |विगाहं तूर्णिं तविषीभिर आवृतं भूर्णिं देवासा इह सुश्रियं दधुः ||
candram agniṁ candrarathaṁ harivrataṁ vaiśvānaram apsuṣadaṁ svarvidam | vigāhaṁ tūrṇiṁ taviṣībhir āvṛtam bhūrṇiṁ devāsa iha suśriyaṁ dadhuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • candram: Moon
    • agniṁ: Fire
    • candrarathaṁ: Chariot of the Moon
    • harivrataṁ: The one who is auspicious
    • vaiśvānaram: Universal or all-pervading
    • apsuṣadaṁ: One who is nourished by water
    • svarvidam: Knowledge of the heavens
    • vigāhaṁ: The one who is protected
    • tūrṇiṁ: Swift or quick
    • taviṣībhir: By the powerful
    • āvṛtam: Surrounded or enveloped
    • bhūrṇiṁ: Abundance or fullness
    • devāsa: The gods
    • iha: Here
    • suśriyaṁ: Good fortune or auspiciousness
    • dadhuḥ: They have given
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the celestial elements, particularly the Moon and Fire, symbolizing the balance of nature and the universe.
    • The "chariot of the Moon" can be seen as a metaphor for the journey of life, where the Moon represents emotions and intuition, while Fire symbolizes transformation and energy.
    • The phrase "nourished by water" signifies the importance of sustenance and the interconnectedness of all elements in nature.
    • The mention of "knowledge of the heavens" reflects the pursuit of wisdom and understanding of the cosmos, which is essential for personal and spiritual growth.
    • The idea of being "surrounded by abundance" suggests that divine blessings and prosperity are available to those who seek them with sincerity and devotion.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, one can relate to the balance of emotions (Moon) and actions (Fire) when making decisions, ensuring that both intuition and logic are considered.
    • The importance of nurturing relationships and environments, akin to being "nourished by water," can be seen in community efforts to support local ecosystems.
    • The pursuit of knowledge, whether through education or personal experiences, mirrors the quest for "knowledge of the heavens," encouraging individuals to seek understanding beyond the material world.
    • Practicing gratitude and recognizing the abundance in life can lead to a more fulfilling existence, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on divine blessings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, the importance of balance in life, and the pursuit of knowledge and abundance.
    • Its significance lies in reminding individuals to harmonize their emotional and rational aspects, seek wisdom, and appreciate the blessings they receive.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a deeper understanding of one's place in the universe.
