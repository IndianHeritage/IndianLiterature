व्युच्छन्ती हि रश्मिभिर विश्वं आभासि रोचनम् |तां त्वां उषर वसूयवो गीरभिः कण्वा अहूषत ||
vyucchantī hi raśmibhir viśvam ābhāsi rocanam | tāṁ tvām uṣar vasūyavo gīrbhiḥ kaṇvā ahūṣata ||
  • vyucchantī hi raśmibhir: This phrase can be translated as "the one who illuminates with rays." Here, "vyucchantī" refers to the act of illuminating or shining forth, while "raśmibhir" means "with rays" or "by the rays of light." This can be likened to the sun, which provides light and warmth, essential for life on Earth.
  • viśvam ābhāsi rocanam: This translates to "the entire universe shines beautifully." "Viśvam" means "the whole universe," "ābhāsi" means "shines," and "rocanam" refers to "beautifully" or "radiantly." This can be compared to the way the sun's light touches everything, making the world vibrant and full of life.
  • tāṁ tvām uṣar vasūyavo: This part means "to you, O dawn, we offer our praises." "Tāṁ" refers to "you," "tvām" is "to you," "uṣar" means "dawn," and "vasūyavo" translates to "we offer praises." This highlights the significance of dawn as a time of new beginnings and opportunities, much like how each day brings a fresh start.
  • gīrbhiḥ kaṇvā ahūṣata: This translates to "the sages have invoked you with hymns." "Gīrbhiḥ" means "with hymns," "kaṇvā" refers to "the sages," and "ahūṣata" means "have invoked." This emphasizes the importance of prayer and reverence in connecting with the divine, akin to how people today may seek guidance or inspiration through meditation or spiritual practices.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda celebrates the dawn as a powerful symbol of illumination, beauty, and new beginnings. It signifies the importance of light in our lives, both literally and metaphorically. In real life, this can be applied to the idea of starting fresh each day, embracing opportunities, and recognizing the beauty in our surroundings. The invocation of the dawn by sages reminds us of the value of gratitude and reverence in our daily lives, encouraging us to appreciate the light and guidance we receive. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek enlightenment and inspiration, fostering a deeper connection with the universe and ourselves.
