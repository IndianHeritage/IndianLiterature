पवस्व सोम देववीतये वृषेन्द्रस्य हार्दि सोमधानम आ विशा |पुरा नो बाधाद दुरिताति पारय क्षेत्रविद् हि दिशा आहाऽ विपृच्छते ||
pavasva soma devavītaye vṛṣendrasya hārdi somadhānam ā viśa | purā no bādhād duritāti pāraya kṣetravid dhi diśa āhā vipṛcchate ||
  • pavasva: This word translates to "purify" or "make pure." In a real-life context, this can refer to the act of cleansing oneself, both physically and spiritually, to achieve a state of clarity and focus.
  • soma: Soma is often associated with a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. In modern terms, it can represent anything that nourishes the soul, such as art, music, or meditation.
  • devavītaye: This means "for the sake of the divine." It emphasizes the importance of aligning one's actions and intentions with higher spiritual goals, akin to pursuing a life of purpose and meaning.
  • vṛṣendrasya: This refers to "the lord of strength," often associated with the deity Indra. In everyday life, this can symbolize the pursuit of strength and resilience in facing challenges.
  • hārdi: Meaning "in the heart," this word signifies the importance of inner feelings and emotions. It reminds us to connect with our true selves and act from a place of authenticity.
  • somadhānam: This can be interpreted as "the essence of soma." It suggests the importance of nurturing our inner vitality and well-being, much like how we cultivate our physical health through diet and exercise.
  • ā viśa: This phrase means "come forth" or "manifest." It encourages us to bring our intentions and desires into reality, akin to setting goals and working towards them.
  • purā: This translates to "before" or "in the past." It serves as a reminder to learn from our experiences and history to inform our present actions.
  • no bādhād: Meaning "from obstacles," this phrase highlights the need to overcome challenges. In real life, this can relate to personal struggles, such as overcoming fears or societal barriers.
  • duritāti: This means "difficulties" or "misfortunes." It acknowledges that life is filled with challenges, and we must navigate them with wisdom and strength.
  • pāraya: This translates to "to cross over" or "to overcome." It signifies the journey of overcoming obstacles, much like how individuals strive to achieve their dreams despite setbacks.
  • kṣetravid: This means "knower of the field," referring to someone who understands the environment or context they are in. In practical terms, it emphasizes the importance of awareness and adaptability in various situations.
  • hhi diśa: This translates to "in all directions." It signifies the need for a holistic approach to life, considering all aspects of our existence.
  • āhā: Meaning "to call" or "to invoke," this word emphasizes the power of intention and the importance of actively seeking what we desire.
  • vipṛcchate: This means "to inquire" or "to ask." It highlights the importance of curiosity and seeking knowledge, encouraging us to ask questions and learn from others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about purification, divine alignment, resilience, and the journey of overcoming obstacles. It serves as a reminder to connect with our inner selves, learn from our past, and actively pursue our goals while remaining aware of our surroundings. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, spiritual growth, and navigating life's challenges. By embodying the principles expressed in this mantra, individuals can cultivate a more meaningful and fulfilling life.
