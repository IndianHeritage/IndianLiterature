यो गर्भं ओषधीनां गवां कृत्नोति अर्वताम् |पर्जन्यः पुरुषीणाम् ||
yo garbham oṣadhīnāṁ gavāṁ kṛṇoty arvatām | parjanyaḥ puruṣīṇām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • garbham: the embryo or the essence
    • oṣadhīnāṁ: of the plants or herbs
    • gavāṁ: of the cows or cattle
    • kṛṇoty: creates or brings forth
    • arvatām: of the strong or powerful
    • parjanyaḥ: the rain or the divine force
    • puruṣīṇām: of the men or human beings
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of life and the divine forces that nurture it.
    • The phrase "yo garbham oṣadhīnāṁ" suggests that the essence of plants is created or nurtured by a divine force.
    • "gavāṁ kṛṇoty arvatām" indicates that this divine force also plays a role in the strength and vitality of cattle.
    • Finally, "parjanyaḥ puruṣīṇām" implies that this rain or divine energy is essential for the well-being of humanity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the health of crops (oṣadhīnāṁ) is directly influenced by rain (parjanyaḥ), which is essential for growth.
    • The strength of livestock (gavāṁ) is often a reflection of the quality of the environment and the resources available to them.
    • Human beings (puruṣīṇām) rely on both plants and animals for sustenance, highlighting the importance of a balanced ecosystem.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of nature and the divine forces that sustain life.
    • It serves as a reminder of our dependence on the environment and the need to respect and nurture it.
    • In modern life, this can be applied through sustainable practices in agriculture, conservation efforts, and a holistic approach to health and well-being.
