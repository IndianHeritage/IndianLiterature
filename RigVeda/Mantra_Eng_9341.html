अश्मन्वती रीयते सं रभध्वम् उत तिष्ठत प्र तरतां सखायः |अत्रा जहाम ये असन्न अशेवाः शिवान वयम् उत तरेमाभि वाजान ||
aśmanvatī rīyate saṁ rabhadhvam ut tiṣṭhata pra taratā sakhāyaḥ | atrā jahāma ye asann aśevāḥ śivān vayam ut taremābhi vājān ||
  • Mantra: "aśmanvatī rīyate saṁ rabhadhvam ut tiṣṭhata pra taratā sakhāyaḥ | atrā jahāma ye asann aśevāḥ śivān vayam ut taremābhi vājān ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśmanvatī: Related to stones or strength.
    • rīyate: To be prosperous or to flourish.
    • saṁ: Together or in unity.
    • rabhadhvam: To grasp or hold firmly.
    • ut: And, or also.
    • tiṣṭhata: To stand firm or to be steadfast.
    • pra: Forth or forward.
    • taratā: To cross over or to transcend.
    • sakhāyaḥ: Friends or companions.
    • atrā: Here or in this place.
    • jahāma: We abandon or let go.
    • ye: Those who.
    • asann: Not existing or absent.
    • aśevāḥ: Without service or duty.
    • śivān: Auspicious or beneficial.
    • vayam: We.
    • ut: And.
    • taremābhi: To cross over or to transcend.
    • vājān: Strength or power.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and strength among friends and companions.
    • It encourages individuals to stand firm and work together to overcome challenges.
    • The phrase "we abandon those who are absent" suggests letting go of negativity or those who do not contribute positively to our lives.
    • It highlights the significance of auspiciousness and beneficial actions in achieving success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, this mantra can be applied to foster collaboration and support among team members, ensuring everyone works towards a common goal.
    • In personal relationships, it encourages individuals to surround themselves with positive influences and to let go of toxic relationships that hinder growth.
    • In community initiatives, it can inspire collective action to address social issues, emphasizing the power of unity in achieving significant change.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the strength found in unity and the importance of surrounding ourselves with supportive individuals.
    • It encourages us to be proactive in our relationships and endeavors, letting go of negativity and focusing on what is beneficial.
    • In essence, this mantra is applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to community building, highlighting the significance of collaboration and positive action.
