अध्वर्यवोऽप इता समुद्रम अपां नपातं हविषा यजध्वम् |स वो ददद ऊर्मिम अद्या सुपूतं तस्मै सोमं मधुमन्तं सुनोत ||
adhvaryavo 'pa itā samudram apāṁ napātaṁ haviṣā yajadhvam | sa vo dadad ūrmim adyā supūtaṁ tasmai somam madhumantaṁ sunota ||
  • adhvaryavo: This term refers to the priests who perform the rituals, particularly the Adhvaryu, who is responsible for the physical aspects of the sacrifice.
  • 'pa: This is a call to action, meaning "to bring" or "to lead."
  • itā: This means "here" or "to this place," indicating a specific location for the ritual.
  • samudram: This translates to "ocean" or "sea," symbolizing vastness and abundance.
  • apāṁ: This means "of waters," referring to the life-giving properties of water.
  • napātaṁ: This term means "descendant" or "offspring," often used in the context of divine or sacred lineage.
  • haviṣā: This refers to offerings or oblations made during the ritual.
  • yajadhvam: This is a command meaning "perform the sacrifice" or "worship."
  • sa: This means "he" or "that," referring to a divine entity or force.
  • vo: This means "to you," indicating the audience or the deities being addressed.
  • dadad: This means "may give" or "may bestow," indicating a request for blessings.
  • ūrmim: This translates to "waves," symbolizing movement and energy.
  • adyā: This means "today," emphasizing the immediacy of the ritual.
  • supūtaṁ: This means "well-prepared" or "well-cooked," referring to the offerings made to the deities.
  • tasmai: This means "to him" or "to that," again referring to the divine entity.
  • somam: This refers to "soma," a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight.
  • madhumantaṁ: This means "sweet" or "honey-like," indicating the desirable qualities of the offerings.
  • sunota: This means "may he hear" or "may he listen," a plea for the divine to accept the offerings.

The mantra essentially calls upon the priests to perform a ritual offering to the divine, invoking the vastness of the ocean and the life-giving properties of water. It emphasizes the importance of well-prepared offerings and the desire for blessings from the divine. The mention of 'soma' highlights the significance of spiritual nourishment and the quest for higher consciousness.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: It can be recited during religious ceremonies to invoke blessings and ensure the success of the rituals.
  • Personal Growth: The call for divine blessings can be seen as a metaphor for seeking guidance and support in personal endeavors.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to water and the ocean can inspire individuals to appreciate and protect natural resources, emphasizing the interconnectedness of life.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine, the significance of well-prepared offerings, and the quest for spiritual nourishment. It encourages individuals to seek blessings in their lives while fostering a sense of responsibility towards nature and the community.
