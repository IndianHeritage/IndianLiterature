अग्निषोमा पिपृतम् अर्वतो न आप्यायन्ताम् उष्रियाः हव्यसूदः |अस्मे बलानि मगवत्सु धत्तं कृतुतं नो अध्वरं श्रुष्टिमन्तम् ||
agnīṣomā pipṛtam arvato na ā pyāyantām usriyā havyasūdaḥ | asme balāni maghavatsu dhattaṁ kṛṇutaṁ no adhvaraṁ śruṣṭimantam ||
  • Agnīṣomā: This term refers to the duality of Agni (fire) and Soma (the sacred drink). In Vedic tradition, Agni represents energy, transformation, and purification, while Soma symbolizes nourishment, vitality, and the divine essence of life.
  • Pipṛtam: This word means 'to be filled' or 'to be nourished.' It signifies the act of receiving blessings or abundance.
  • Arvato: This term can be interpreted as 'from all sides' or 'in all directions,' indicating a holistic approach to life and spirituality.
  • Na ā pyāyantām: This phrase translates to 'may they not be diminished' or 'may they not be depleted.' It emphasizes the importance of sustaining energy and resources.
  • Usriyā: This word refers to 'the offerings' or 'the sacrifices' made to the deities, highlighting the significance of rituals in Vedic culture.
  • Havyasūdaḥ: This term means 'the one who is worthy of offerings' or 'the divine being who receives the offerings.' It underscores the connection between humans and the divine through rituals.
  • Asme: This word means 'to us' or 'for us,' indicating a plea or request directed towards the divine.
  • Balāni: This term translates to 'strengths' or 'powers,' referring to the various forms of energy and support that one seeks from the divine.
  • Maghavatsu: This word means 'O Mighty One' or 'O Powerful One,' often used to address deities like Indra, who is associated with strength and abundance.
  • Dhattam: This term means 'may you grant' or 'may you bestow,' expressing a desire for divine blessings.
  • Kṛṇutaṁ: This word translates to 'may you create' or 'may you establish,' indicating a request for the manifestation of positive outcomes.
  • No adhvaraṁ: This phrase means 'our sacrifice' or 'our offering,' emphasizing the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • Śruṣṭimantam: This term refers to 'the one who has created' or 'the creator,' highlighting the divine aspect of creation and existence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound request for divine blessings, emphasizing the importance of energy, nourishment, and the sustainability of resources in our lives. It reflects the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine support, urging individuals to engage in rituals and offerings to maintain a harmonious relationship with the universe. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects, such as seeking strength in challenging times, fostering community through shared rituals, and recognizing the importance of sustainability in our actions. By invoking the energies of Agni and Soma, individuals can strive for balance, abundance, and a deeper connection to the divine, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and purposeful life.
