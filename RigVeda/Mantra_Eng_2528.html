इळाम अग्नि पुरुदंसं सनिं गोः शश्वत्तमं हवमानाय साधा |स्यान नः सूनुस तनयो विजावाग्नि सा ते सुमतिर भूत्व अस्मे ||
iḻām agne purudaṁsaṁ saniṁ goḥ śaśvattamaṁ havamānāya sādha | syān naḥ sūnus tanayo vijāvāgne sā te sumatir bhūtv asme ||
  • iḻām agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni," referring to the Vedic fire god, Agni, who symbolizes energy, light, and the divine messenger between humans and the gods.
  • purudaṁsaṁ: This means "of great glory" or "of great fame," highlighting Agni's revered status in Vedic literature and his importance in rituals.
  • saniṁ goḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is the protector of the cows," where cows symbolize wealth and prosperity in Vedic culture.
  • śaśvattamaṁ: This translates to "the eternal" or "the most enduring," emphasizing the timeless nature of Agni and his role in sustaining life.
  • havamānāya: This means "to the one who is invoked," indicating the act of calling upon Agni during rituals and sacrifices.
  • sādha: This word means "to fulfill" or "to accomplish," suggesting the desire for Agni to grant the wishes of the devotees.
  • syān naḥ sūnus: This translates to "may our son be," indicating a prayer for progeny and the continuation of lineage.
  • tanayo vijāvāgne: This means "the son who is wise," emphasizing the importance of wisdom and intelligence in offspring.
  • sā te sumatir: This translates to "may he have good thoughts," indicating a wish for the child to possess virtuous and noble qualities.
  • bhūtv asme: This means "may he become ours," expressing the desire for the blessings of Agni to manifest in the family.

  • Significance: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, seeking blessings for wisdom, prosperity, and a virtuous lineage. It reflects the values of the Vedic society, where the well-being of the family and the continuation of good qualities in descendants were of utmost importance.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be relevant in various contexts, such as during family rituals, naming ceremonies, or any occasion where blessings for children and family prosperity are sought. It serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing wisdom and good character in the younger generation.
