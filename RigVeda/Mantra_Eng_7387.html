विदद यत पूर्व्यं नष्टमुदीम ऋतायुमीरयत् | प्रेम आयुस्तारीदतीर्णम् ||
vidad yat pūrvyaṁ naṣṭam ud īm ṛtāyum īrayat | prem āyus tārīd atīrṇam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vidad: to give or to bestow
    • yat: that which
    • pūrvyaṁ: from the past or ancient
    • naṣṭam: lost or destroyed
    • ud: up or out
    • īm: to bring forth or to manifest
    • ṛtāyum: truth or righteousness
    • īrayat: to inspire or to lead
    • prem: love or affection
    • āyus: life or longevity
    • tārīd: to protect or to save
    • atīrṇam: beyond or transcending
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks about the restoration of what has been lost from the past.
    • It emphasizes the importance of truth and righteousness in inspiring and leading individuals.
    • Furthermore, it highlights the role of love in ensuring longevity and protection, transcending challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal relationships, when trust is lost, it can be restored through open communication and love, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In a community, when values are forgotten, leaders can inspire others by revisiting and upholding those values, akin to bringing forth righteousness.
    • In health and wellness, the mantra can be applied to the idea that love and support from family and friends can lead to a longer, healthier life.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call to restore lost values and truths, emphasizing the power of love and righteousness.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to community dynamics, and even in health contexts.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that through love and truth, we can overcome challenges and lead fulfilling lives.
