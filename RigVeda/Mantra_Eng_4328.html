विष्वानि देव सवितर दुरितानि परा सुव |यद भद्रं तन् न आ सुव ||
viśvāni deva savitar duritāni parā suva | yad bhadraṁ tan na ā suva ||
  • viśvāni: This word translates to "all" or "universal." It signifies the inclusiveness of all aspects of life and existence.
  • deva: Meaning "gods" or "divine beings," this term refers to the higher powers that govern the universe.
  • savitar: This is a name for the Sun God, representing illumination, energy, and life-giving force.
  • duritāni: This word means "difficulties" or "obstacles." It highlights the challenges and adversities that one may face in life.
  • parā: This translates to "beyond" or "transcendent," indicating a state that is higher than the ordinary.
  • suva: Meaning "good" or "auspicious," this term refers to positive outcomes and blessings.
  • yad: This word means "that" or "which," serving as a connector in the mantra.
  • bhadraṁ: This translates to "well-being" or "prosperity," emphasizing the importance of positive states in life.
  • tan: Meaning "that," it refers back to the good or auspicious things mentioned earlier.
  • na: This word means "not," indicating negation or absence.
  • ā suva: This phrase translates to "may it not be auspicious," suggesting a desire to avoid negative outcomes.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to the divine forces, particularly the Sun God, to remove all difficulties and obstacles from our lives and to bless us with well-being and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of seeking the divine's assistance in overcoming challenges and achieving a positive state of existence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • During challenging times, individuals can recite this mantra to seek divine support and guidance in overcoming their difficulties.
  • In community gatherings or rituals, this mantra can be chanted to invoke blessings for collective well-being and prosperity.
  • In personal reflection or meditation, one can use this mantra to focus on removing negative thoughts and attracting positive energy.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for divine intervention in human affairs. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking higher guidance in navigating life's challenges. By invoking the divine, individuals can cultivate a mindset of positivity and resilience, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and prosperous life.
