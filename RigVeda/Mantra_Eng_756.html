रयिर न चित्री सूरो न संदृग आयुर न प्राणो नित्यो न सूनुः ||
rayir na citrā sūro na saṁdṛg āyur na prāṇo nityo na sūnuḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "The rays (of light) are not varied, the hero (warrior) does not see (the end), life is not eternal, nor is the breath (of life) everlasting." This mantra reflects on the transient nature of life and existence.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Rayir: Refers to rays or light, symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
    • Na: Means "not," indicating negation or absence.
    • Citrā: Means "varied" or "different," suggesting diversity in perception or experience.
    • Sūro: Refers to a hero or warrior, representing strength and valor.
    • Na saṁdṛg: Translates to "does not see," implying a lack of awareness or understanding of the ultimate truth.
    • Āyur: Means "life" or "longevity," emphasizing the concept of existence.
    • Na prāṇo: Refers to "breath," which is often associated with life force.
    • Nityo: Means "eternal," indicating something that is everlasting or unchanging.
    • Na sūnuḥ: Translates to "nor is the son," which can imply the impermanence of lineage or legacy.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the context of personal growth, the mantra reminds us that while we may strive for success and recognition (the rays of light), these achievements are often fleeting and not the ultimate goal of life.
    • In relationships, understanding that life and connections are transient can help us appreciate the present moment rather than clinging to the past or worrying about the future.
    • In the face of challenges, recognizing that even the strongest individuals (heroes) may not have a clear vision of the end can foster humility and resilience.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its profound reminder of the impermanence of life and the importance of living in the present. It encourages individuals to seek deeper understanding and awareness beyond the superficial aspects of existence. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to cultivate mindfulness, acceptance of change, and a focus on what truly matters, such as relationships, experiences, and personal growth. By embracing the transient nature of life, we can find peace and fulfillment in the here and now.
