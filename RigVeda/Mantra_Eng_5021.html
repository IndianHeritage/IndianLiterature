तां भुज्युं विभिर अद्भ्यः समुद्रात तुग्रस्य सूनुम ऊहथू रजोभिः |अरेणुभिर योजनेभिर भुजन्तां पत्रत्रिभिर अर्णसो निर उपस्थात ||
tā bhujyuṁ vibhir adbhyaḥ samudrāt tugrasya sūnum ūhathū rajobhiḥ | areṇubhir yojanebhir bhujantā patatribhir arṇaso nir upasthāt ||
  • tā bhujyuṁ vibhir adbhyaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "those who are endowed with strength and vigor." It emphasizes the importance of physical and mental strength in overcoming challenges.
  • samudrāt tugrasya sūnum: This translates to "from the ocean, the son of the mighty." Here, the ocean symbolizes vastness and depth, while "the son of the mighty" refers to someone who inherits strength and power, suggesting that true strength comes from deep, rich sources.
  • ūhathū rajobhiḥ: This means "may they be adorned with the dust of the earth." Dust here symbolizes humility and grounding, indicating that even those with great strength should remain humble and connected to their roots.
  • areṇubhir yojanebhir: This translates to "with the sands and the distances." It suggests that one should navigate through life's challenges with patience and perseverance, much like traversing through sandy terrains.
  • bhujantā patatribhir: This means "those who are supported by wings." Wings symbolize freedom and the ability to rise above challenges, indicating that support systems are crucial for achieving one's goals.
  • arṇaso nir upasthāt: This translates to "may they come forth from the depths." It signifies the emergence of strength and wisdom from within, suggesting that true power often lies beneath the surface and must be drawn out.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of strength, humility, and support in overcoming life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to various situations such as personal growth, teamwork, and leadership. For instance, in a corporate environment, a leader who embodies strength and humility can inspire their team to navigate challenges effectively. Similarly, individuals facing personal struggles can draw upon their inner strength and seek support from their community, much like the metaphorical wings that help them rise above difficulties. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that true power is not just about physical strength but also about wisdom, humility, and the ability to connect with others.
