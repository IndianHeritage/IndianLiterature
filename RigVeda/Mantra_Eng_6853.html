उत त्वा नमसā वयं होतर वरेण्यकृतो |अग्नि समिद्भिर इमहे ||
uta tvā namasā vayaṁ hotar vareṇyakrato | agne samidbhir īmahe ||
  • Mantra: "uta tvā namasā vayaṁ hotar vareṇyakrato | agne samidbhir īmahe ||"
  • Translation: "We bow to you, O Agni, the divine priest, with our offerings; we invoke you with our sacred fires."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • uta: a term of reverence, often translated as "thus" or "therefore."
    • tvā: means "you," referring to Agni, the fire god.
    • namasā: translates to "with reverence" or "with bowing."
    • vayaṁ: means "we," indicating a collective invocation.
    • hotar: refers to "the priest" or "the one who offers," specifically in the context of rituals.
    • vareṇyakrato: means "of great wisdom" or "of excellent thought," highlighting Agni's divine qualities.
    • agne: directly addresses Agni, the fire god.
    • samidbhir: means "with offerings" or "with fuel," referring to the materials used in the fire ritual.
    • īmahe: translates to "we invoke" or "we call upon."

  • Context and Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, who is considered the mediator between humans and the divine in Vedic rituals.
    • Agni represents not just physical fire but also the transformative power of energy and light, symbolizing purity and the divine presence.
    • In real life, invoking Agni can be seen as a metaphor for seeking clarity, wisdom, and guidance in our actions and decisions.
    • For example, in a challenging situation at work, one might reflect on this mantra to seek inner strength and clarity, much like how fire transforms and purifies materials.

  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of reverence and intention in our actions, particularly in rituals and daily life.
    • It teaches us to approach our endeavors with respect and mindfulness, recognizing the divine presence in our efforts.
    • In contemporary life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal growth, community service, or professional integrity, reminding us to invoke our inner strength and wisdom.
