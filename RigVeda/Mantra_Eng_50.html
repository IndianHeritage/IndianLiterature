मां नो मर्ता अभि द्रुहान तनूनाम इन्द्र गिर्वणः |ईशानो यवया वधम् ||
mā no martā abhi druhan tanūnām indra girvaṇaḥ | īśāno yavayā vadham ||
  • mā no martā abhi druhan tanūnām indra girvaṇaḥ
    • mā no martā: This phrase translates to "may we not be harmed by mortals." It expresses a desire for protection from human adversities and challenges.
    • abhi druhan: This means "from those who wish to harm." It emphasizes the need for safeguarding against those who may have malicious intentions.
    • tanūnām: This refers to "the bodies" or "the physical forms." It signifies the importance of physical well-being and health.
    • indra girvaṇaḥ: "Indra" is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, often associated with strength and protection. "Girvaṇaḥ" refers to the divine utterances or hymns. Together, it invokes Indra's blessings through sacred chants.
  • īśāno yavayā vadham:
    • īśāno: This term means "the lord" or "the divine." It signifies a higher power or authority that governs and protects.
    • yavayā: This translates to "with the strength" or "with the might." It indicates the need for divine strength in overcoming challenges.
    • vadham: This means "to kill" or "to destroy." In this context, it refers to the destruction of negativity or harmful influences.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates a plea for divine protection and strength against adversities posed by mortals. In real life, this can be applicable in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal challenges, individuals may recite this mantra to seek strength and protection from negative influences in their lives.
  • In a community setting, it can be used to foster unity and resilience against external threats or conflicts.
  • In health-related issues, it serves as a reminder to seek divine support for physical well-being and to ward off ailments.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine intervention and strength, encouraging individuals and communities to seek protection and resilience in the face of life's challenges.
