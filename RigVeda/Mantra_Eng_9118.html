मन्त्र: उता कण्वं नृषदः पुत्रम् आहुर उता श्यावो धनम् आदत्त वाजी | प्र कृष्णाय रुशद अपिन्वतोधर ऋतम् अत्र नकीर अस्मा अपीपेत ||
uta kaṇvaṁ nṛṣadaḥ putram āhur uta śyāvo dhanam ādatta vājī | pra kṛṣṇāya ruśad apinvatodhar ṛtam atra nakir asmā apīpet ||
  • uta kaṇvaṁ nṛṣadaḥ putram āhur: This phrase can be translated to "the Kaṇva sages have called forth the son of Nṛṣada." Here, "Kaṇva" refers to a lineage of sages known for their wisdom, while "Nṛṣada" signifies a person or a group associated with strength and valor. This highlights the importance of lineage and the passing of knowledge through generations.
  • uta śyāvo dhanam ādatta vājī: This translates to "the dark one (or the one with dark features) has obtained wealth, like a horse." The term "śyāva" can refer to someone who is dark in complexion, and "vājī" relates to horses, which are often symbols of power and wealth in ancient texts. This suggests that wealth can come to those who are strong and capable, much like a horse that is vital for transportation and status.
  • pra kṛṣṇāya ruśad apinvatodhar: This means "to the one who is black (or dark), may we be uplifted." The term "kṛṣṇa" often symbolizes the divine or the ultimate reality in Hindu philosophy. This line emphasizes the aspiration for elevation and enlightenment through devotion to a higher power.
  • ṛtam atra nakir asmā apīpet: This translates to "truth is here; may we not be deprived of it." "Ritam" refers to cosmic order or truth, and this line expresses a desire to remain connected to this truth, suggesting that it is essential for a fulfilling life.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates themes of lineage, strength, wealth, devotion, and the pursuit of truth. It reflects the interconnectedness of these elements in achieving a balanced and prosperous life.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be applied in various ways:
    • In family and community settings, it emphasizes the importance of passing down wisdom and values from one generation to the next.
    • In personal development, it encourages individuals to recognize their strengths and capabilities, akin to the horse that symbolizes power and wealth.
    • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder to seek higher truths and remain grounded in one's beliefs, fostering a sense of purpose and direction.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a profound reminder of the values that guide human existence—heritage, strength, devotion, and truth. By integrating these principles into our daily lives, we can cultivate a sense of fulfillment and connection to the greater cosmos.
