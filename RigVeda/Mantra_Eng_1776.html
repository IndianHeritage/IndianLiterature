अनुत्तमाऽते मघवन नकीर न तवावाम् अस्ति देवता विदानः |न जायमानो नशते न जातो यानि करिष्याकृतुहि प्रवृद्ध ||
anuttam ā te maghavan nakir nu na tvāvām̐ asti devatā vidānaḥ | na jāyamāno naśate na jāto yāni kariṣyā kṛṇuhi pravṛddha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Anuttam: The supreme or the best.
    • Ā te: To you.
    • Maghavan: O Lord of wealth or abundance.
    • Nakir: Not at all.
    • Nu: Indeed.
    • Na: Not.
    • Tvā: You.
    • Vām: Your.
    • Astī: Is.
    • Devatā: Divine or deity.
    • Vidānaḥ: Knowledge or understanding.
    • Na: Not.
    • Jāyamāno: Being born.
    • Naśate: Is destroyed or perishes.
    • Na: Not.
    • Jātaḥ: Born.
    • Yāni: Whatever.
    • Kariṣyā: Will do or perform.
    • Kṛṇuhi: Make or create.
    • Pravṛddha: Increased or grown.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses the divine, specifically invoking the Lord of wealth, Maghavan.
    • It emphasizes the idea that true wealth and abundance come from a higher understanding and connection with the divine.
    • The phrase "not at all" suggests a rejection of superficial or materialistic views of wealth.
    • It highlights that even if something is born or created, it does not truly exist without the divine essence.
    • The mantra calls for the divine to create and increase what is truly valuable and meaningful in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a company may focus solely on profits (material wealth) but neglect the well-being of its employees. This mantra reminds us that true success comes from a holistic approach that values both profit and people.
    • In personal life, individuals may chase after material possessions, thinking they will bring happiness. This mantra encourages a deeper understanding that true fulfillment comes from spiritual growth and connections with others.
    • In community service, organizations that focus on uplifting the community rather than just providing aid exemplify the essence of this mantra, as they create lasting change and growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for a deeper understanding of wealth and existence.
    • It teaches that true abundance is not merely material but is rooted in spiritual and ethical values.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying this mantra can lead to more meaningful relationships, sustainable business practices, and a greater sense of community.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to seek the divine in all aspects of life, fostering growth that is both personal and collective.
