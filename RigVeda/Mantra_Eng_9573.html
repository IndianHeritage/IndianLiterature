अष्टौ पुत्रासो अदितेर ये जातास तन्वांस् परि |देवां उप प्रैत सप्तभिः परा मार्ताण्डम् आस्यत ||
aṣṭau putrāso aditer ye jātās tanva1s pari | devām̐ upa prait saptabhiḥ parā mārtāṇḍam āsyat ||
  • Mantra: "aṣṭau putrāso aditer ye jātās tanva1s pari | devām̐ upa prait saptabhiḥ parā mārtāṇḍam āsyat ||"
  • Translation: "The eight sons of Aditi, who are born from her body, approach the gods with seven (sacred) offerings, and they invoke the supreme light (Mārtāṇḍa)."

  • Key Terms:
    • Aditi: In Vedic literature, Aditi is the mother of the gods, representing the infinite and the boundless. She symbolizes the cosmic order and the nurturing aspect of the universe.
    • Eight Sons: The eight sons of Aditi are often identified as the eight Adityas, who are solar deities representing various aspects of life and nature.
    • Mārtāṇḍa: This term refers to the sun or the supreme light, symbolizing knowledge, enlightenment, and the source of life.
    • Seven Offerings: The number seven is significant in many cultures and often represents completeness or perfection. In this context, it refers to the offerings made to the gods, which can be seen as prayers or rituals.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Family and Nurturing: Just as Aditi represents motherhood and nurturing, in real life, families play a crucial role in providing support and guidance. For instance, parents often make sacrifices for their children, ensuring they have the best opportunities in life.
    • Community and Cooperation: The invocation of the gods with offerings can be likened to community gatherings where people come together to celebrate and support one another. For example, during festivals, communities unite to honor their traditions and share blessings.
    • Seeking Enlightenment: The reference to Mārtāṇḍa as the supreme light can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom in our lives. For instance, individuals often seek education and personal growth to illuminate their paths and make informed decisions.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of family, community, and the pursuit of knowledge. It signifies the interconnectedness of life, where nurturing relationships and collective efforts lead to enlightenment and fulfillment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to value their familial bonds, engage in community service, and strive for personal growth, ultimately contributing to a harmonious and enlightened society.
