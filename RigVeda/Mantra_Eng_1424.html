आगधिता परिगधिता या काशीकैव जङ्गहे |ददाति मह्यम् यादुरी याशूनाम् भोज्या शताः ||
āgadhitā parigadhitā yā kaśīkeva jaṅgahe | dadāti mahyaṁ yādurī yāśūnām bhojyā śatā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āgadhitā: That which is attained or achieved.
    • parigadhitā: That which is surrounded or encompassed.
    • yā: Which.
    • kaśīkeva: Like the city of Kashi (Varanasi), known for its spiritual significance.
    • jaṅgahe: In the context of the body or physical form.
    • dadāti: Gives or bestows.
    • mahyaṁ: To me.
    • yādurī: A term that can refer to a form of sustenance or nourishment.
    • yāśūnām: Of the successful or the prosperous.
    • bhojyā: That which is to be consumed or enjoyed.
    • śatā: Hundreds or multitude.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the attainment of spiritual and material wealth.
    • It draws a parallel between the blessings one receives and the sacredness of Kashi, a city revered for its spiritual significance.
    • The mantra suggests that just as Kashi is a center of spiritual nourishment, so too can one receive blessings that nourish the body and soul.
    • The phrase "dadāti mahyaṁ" emphasizes the act of giving, indicating that these blessings are bestowed upon the seeker.
    • The reference to "yāśūnām" highlights the importance of success and prosperity in life, suggesting that the blessings are meant for those who strive for achievement.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional context, this mantra can be applied to individuals seeking success in their careers. Just as Kashi is a place of pilgrimage, one can view their workplace as a place of growth and opportunity.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra can remind us of the importance of giving and sharing with others, fostering a sense of community and support.
    • In the pursuit of education, students can interpret this mantra as a call to seek knowledge and wisdom, which will ultimately lead to personal and professional success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of spiritual and material wealth.
    • It serves as a reminder that true success comes from both personal effort and the blessings we receive from the universe.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek balance between their spiritual pursuits and material aspirations, fostering a holistic approach to life.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us to be grateful for the blessings we receive and to share them generously with others, creating a cycle of abundance and prosperity.
