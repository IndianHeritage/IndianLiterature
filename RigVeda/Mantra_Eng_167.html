सेमं नः कामं ā पृण गोभिर अश्वैः शतक्रतो |स्तवाम तव स्वाध्यः ||
semaṁ naḥ kāmam ā pṛṇa gobhir aśvaiḥ śatakrato | stavāma tvā svādhyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • semaṁ: Abundance or prosperity
    • naḥ: Our
    • kāmam: Desires or wishes
    • ā pṛṇa: Fulfill or grant
    • gobhir: With the help of cows (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • aśvaiḥ: With horses (symbolizing strength and power)
    • śatakrato: O hundred-fold (a reference to Indra, the king of gods)
    • stavāma: We praise or invoke
    • tvā: You
    • svādhyaḥ: O self-sufficient one
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for abundance and fulfillment of desires.
    • The mention of "cows" and "horses" signifies the importance of wealth and strength in achieving one's goals.
    • By invoking "śatakrato," it acknowledges the divine power of Indra, who is believed to bestow prosperity and success.
    • The term "svādhyaḥ" emphasizes the self-sufficiency and independence of the divine, suggesting that true fulfillment comes from a higher power.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for a bountiful harvest, symbolizing the desire for abundance and prosperity.
    • In business, entrepreneurs seek strength and resources (akin to horses) to achieve their goals, reflecting the need for support and power in their endeavors.
    • Individuals may invoke this mantra during significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, to seek blessings for success and fulfillment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of desires, resources, and divine support in achieving one's goals.
    • It highlights the importance of seeking abundance not just for oneself but for the community, as represented by the collective "naḥ" (our).
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to recognize the role of both material and spiritual resources in fulfilling their aspirations.
    • Ultimately, it signifies that true prosperity comes from a harmonious balance of personal effort and divine grace.
