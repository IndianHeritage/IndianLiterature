वृषो अग्निः सम इध्यते अश्वो न देववाहनः |तं हविष्मन्त īḻते ||
vṛṣo agniḥ sam idhyate 'śvo na devavāhanaḥ | taṁ haviṣmanta īḻate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vṛṣo: The strong or powerful.
    • agniḥ: Fire, often symbolizing energy, purity, and transformation.
    • sam: Together or in harmony.
    • idhyate: Is invoked or is made manifest.
    • 'śvo: The horse, representing speed, strength, and nobility.
    • na: Not.
    • devavāhanaḥ: The one who carries the gods, often referring to divine beings or forces.
    • taṁ: That.
    • haviṣmanta: The offerings or sacrifices.
    • īḻate: Is worshipped or is honored.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the powerful fire (Agni) that is invoked in harmony.
    • The reference to the horse symbolizes strength and speed, suggesting that the energy of Agni is swift and potent.
    • The phrase "not the one who carries the gods" implies that this fire is not merely a vessel for divine energy but is itself a powerful force.
    • The mantra concludes with the idea that offerings are made to this fire, indicating a ritualistic aspect where worship and respect are given to this elemental force.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is a symbol of transformation. For instance, in cooking, raw ingredients are transformed into nourishing meals through the application of fire.
    • In modern rituals, such as weddings, fire is often present in the form of a sacred flame, symbolizing the union and the energy that binds the couple together.
    • In a metaphorical sense, the mantra can be applied to personal growth. Just as fire transforms materials, individuals can harness their inner strength (Agni) to overcome challenges and achieve their goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of harnessing inner strength and energy (Agni) in a harmonious manner.
    • It teaches us to respect and honor the transformative powers in our lives, whether they be in the form of personal challenges, relationships, or spiritual practices.
    • In real life, the principles of this mantra can guide individuals to invoke their inner power, work in harmony with others, and honor the energies that facilitate growth and transformation.
