तक्षन् नासत्याभ्यां परिज्मानं सुखं रथम् |तक्षन् धेनुं सबरदुग्धाम् ||
takṣan nāsatyābhyām parijmānaṁ sukhaṁ ratham | takṣan dhenuṁ sabardughām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • takṣan: "Crafting" or "making"
    • nāsatyābhyām: "by the Nāsatyas" (the twin deities, often associated with health and well-being)
    • parijmānaṁ: "a well-constructed" or "well-formed"
    • sukhaṁ: "happiness" or "comfort"
    • ratham: "chariot" or "vehicle"
    • dhenuṁ: "the cow" (symbolizing nourishment and wealth)
    • sabardughām: "with good qualities" or "well-nourished"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the act of crafting a chariot that brings happiness and comfort, created by the divine Nāsatyas.
    • The chariot symbolizes a vehicle of life, representing our journey and the means through which we achieve our goals.
    • The mention of the cow signifies abundance and sustenance, highlighting the importance of nourishment in our lives.
    • The Nāsatyas, as divine figures, suggest that our endeavors should be aligned with higher principles and values.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the "chariot" can be seen as our career or personal aspirations. Just as a chariot needs to be well-crafted to function effectively, our careers require planning, skill, and effort to succeed.
    • The concept of nourishment can be applied to both physical and emotional well-being. Just as a cow provides sustenance, we must ensure that we nurture our bodies and minds with healthy food and positive experiences.
    • The Nāsatyas remind us to seek divine guidance in our pursuits, whether through meditation, prayer, or ethical living, ensuring that our actions contribute to our happiness and the well-being of others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of crafting our lives with intention and care, seeking happiness and nourishment along the way.
    • It teaches us that our journey (the chariot) should be supported by divine principles (the Nāsatyas) and that we must cultivate both physical and emotional well-being (the cow).
    • In a broader sense, it encourages us to create a life that is not only fulfilling for ourselves but also beneficial to those around us, fostering a sense of community and shared happiness.
