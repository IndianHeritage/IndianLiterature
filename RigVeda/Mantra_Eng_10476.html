इदं श्रेष्टं ज्योतिषां ज्योतिर्म उत्तमं विश्वजिद धनजिद उच्यते बृहत् |विश्वभ्राड् भ्राजो महि सूर्यो दृष्ट उरु पप्रथे सह ओजो अच्युतम् ||
idaṁ śreṣṭhaṁ jyotiṣāṁ jyotir uttamaṁ viśvajid dhanajid ucyate bṛhat | viśvabhrāḍ bhrājo mahi sūryo dṛśa uru paprathe saha ojo acyutam ||
  • idaṁ: This word translates to "this" or "here." It signifies the present moment or the immediate context.
  • śreṣṭhaṁ: Meaning "the best" or "the most excellent," it emphasizes the quality of what is being referred to.
  • jyotiṣāṁ: This refers to "of lights" or "of luminaries," indicating a connection to celestial bodies or enlightenment.
  • jyotir: Translated as "light," it symbolizes knowledge, wisdom, and clarity.
  • uttamaṁ: Meaning "the highest" or "the supreme," it denotes the pinnacle of achievement or existence.
  • viśvajid: This term means "one who has conquered the universe," suggesting a sense of omnipotence or mastery over all.
  • dhanajid: Translated as "one who has acquired wealth," it signifies prosperity and abundance.
  • ucyate: This means "is said" or "is declared," indicating a statement or proclamation.
  • bṛhat: Meaning "great" or "vast," it refers to something of immense scale or significance.
  • viśvabhrāḍ: This term translates to "the one who supports the universe," indicating a nurturing or sustaining force.
  • bhrājo: Meaning "radiant" or "brilliant," it describes something that emits light or positivity.
  • mahi: This translates to "great" or "mighty," emphasizing strength and power.
  • sūryo: Referring to "the sun," it symbolizes vitality, energy, and life-giving force.
  • dṛśa: Meaning "of sight" or "to see," it relates to perception and understanding.
  • uru: This translates to "broad" or "vast," indicating expansiveness.
  • paprathe: Meaning "to spread" or "to extend," it suggests the dissemination of knowledge or influence.
  • saha: This means "together" or "with," indicating unity or collaboration.
  • ojo: Translated as "vigor" or "strength," it signifies energy and vitality.
  • acyutam: Meaning "imperishable" or "eternal," it denotes something that is everlasting and unchanging.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of light, knowledge, and the supreme power that governs the universe. It emphasizes the importance of enlightenment and prosperity, suggesting that true wealth comes from understanding and mastering the forces of nature. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, where one seeks knowledge and wisdom to achieve greatness, or in community building, where collaboration and unity lead to collective strength and prosperity. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of striving for excellence in all endeavors.
