एषा व्येनी भवति द्विभर्हा आविष्कृत्वानां तन्वं पुरस्तात् |ऋतस्य पन्थाम् अन्व एति साधु प्रजानतीव न दिशो मिनाति ||
eṣā vyenī bhavati dvibarhā āviṣkṛṇvānā tanvam purastāt | ṛtasya panthām anv eti sādhu prajānatīva na diśo mināti ||
  • eṣā vyenī bhavati: This phrase can be translated as "this path becomes a means." It signifies the importance of a chosen path or direction in life.
  • dvibarhā āviṣkṛṇvānā: This means "the two-fold (or dual) aspects reveal." It suggests that there are multiple dimensions or perspectives to consider in any situation.
  • tanvam purastāt: Translated as "the body (or essence) is in front." This indicates that our physical presence or essence is crucial in the journey we undertake.
  • ṛtasya panthām anv eti: This means "it follows the path of truth." It emphasizes the significance of adhering to truth and righteousness in our actions.
  • sādhu prajānatīva: This translates to "the wise (or good) person knows." It highlights the role of wisdom and understanding in navigating life's challenges.
  • na diśo mināti: This means "does not stray from the directions." It signifies the importance of staying true to one's path and not getting lost or distracted.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of choosing a righteous path in life, emphasizing the importance of wisdom, truth, and staying focused. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to various situations such as career choices, personal relationships, and moral dilemmas. For instance, when faced with a difficult decision, one can reflect on the dual aspects of the situation, seek wisdom, and ensure that their actions align with truth and integrity. This mantra serves as a guiding principle, encouraging individuals to remain steadfast on their chosen path while being aware of the complexities that life presents.
