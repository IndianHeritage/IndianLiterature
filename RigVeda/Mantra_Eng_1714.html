उप प्रागाच्छसनं वाज्यर्वा देवद्रीचामनसादीद्ध्यानः |अजः पुरो नीयते नाभिरस्यानुपश्चात्कवयो यान्ति रेब्हाः ||
upa prāgāc chasanaṁ vājy arvā devadrīcā manasā dīdhyānaḥ | ajaḥ puro nīyate nābhir asyānu paścāt kavayo yanti rebhāḥ ||
  • upa prāgāc: This phrase can be translated as "coming forward" or "approaching." It signifies the act of moving towards something with intention.
  • chasanaṁ: This word refers to "the light" or "radiance." It symbolizes knowledge, enlightenment, and clarity.
  • vājy: This term means "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is called upon." It represents the divine or higher powers that are sought for guidance.
  • arvā: This can be interpreted as "the one who is swift" or "the one who moves quickly." It denotes agility and the ability to adapt to changing circumstances.
  • devadrīcā: This word translates to "the divine vision" or "the insight of the gods." It emphasizes the importance of spiritual perception and understanding.
  • manasā: Meaning "with the mind," this term highlights the role of thought and intention in achieving one's goals.
  • dīdhyānaḥ: This word means "to contemplate" or "to meditate." It suggests the practice of deep reflection and focus on one's aspirations.
  • ajaḥ: This term refers to "the unborn" or "the eternal." It symbolizes the timeless aspect of existence and the unchanging nature of the soul.
  • puro: This word means "in front" or "before." It indicates the importance of foresight and planning in one's endeavors.
  • nīyate: This translates to "is led" or "is guided." It signifies the influence of higher powers or principles in directing one's path.
  • nābhir: This term means "the center" or "the core." It represents the essence of being and the source of one's strength.
  • asyānu: This can be interpreted as "after this" or "following this." It suggests the unfolding of events and the consequences of actions.
  • paścāt: Meaning "after" or "behind," this word emphasizes the importance of reflection on past actions and their outcomes.
  • kavayo: This term refers to "the wise" or "the seers." It highlights the value of wisdom and insight in navigating life's challenges.
  • yanti: This means "they go" or "they move." It signifies the journey of life and the progression towards one's goals.
  • rebhāḥ: This word translates to "the vibrations" or "the resonances." It symbolizes the impact of one's actions and thoughts on the surrounding environment.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of intention, knowledge, and spiritual insight in guiding one's life. It teaches that by approaching our goals with clarity and contemplation, we can navigate the complexities of existence. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, decision-making, and leadership. For instance, a leader who contemplates their actions and seeks divine guidance can inspire their team to achieve greater heights. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to align our thoughts and actions with higher principles, leading to a more fulfilling and purposeful life.
