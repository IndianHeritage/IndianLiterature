मन्त्र: सूर्यो देवीं उषसं रोचमानां मर्यो न योषाम अभ्य एति पश्चात् | यत्रा नरो देवयन्तो युगानि वितन्वते प्रति भद्राय भद्रम् ||
sūryo devīm uṣasaṁ rocamānām maryo na yoṣām abhy eti paścāt | yatrā naro devayanto yugāni vitanvate prati bhadrāya bhadram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sūryo: The Sun
    • devīm: Divine
    • uṣasaṁ: Dawn
    • rocamānām: Shining or Radiant
    • maryo: Mortal beings
    • na: Not
    • yoṣām: Of women
    • abhy eti: Approaches or Comes
    • paścāt: After
    • yatrā: Where
    • naro: Men
    • devayanto: Divine beings or Gods
    • yugāni: Ages or Eras
    • vitanvate: Spread or Extend
    • prati: Towards
    • bhadrāya: For auspiciousness or well-being
    • bhadram: Goodness or Auspiciousness
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the radiant dawn, symbolized by the Sun, which brings light and clarity to the world.
    • The phrase "maryo na yoṣām abhy eti paścāt" suggests that the divine light of the dawn does not approach women after it has already come, indicating a sense of equality and respect for all beings.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine guidance and the role of men and gods in spreading goodness and auspiciousness across different ages.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, the rising sun can be seen as a metaphor for new beginnings and opportunities. Just as the dawn brings light, individuals can seek to bring positivity and clarity into their lives and the lives of others.
    • The idea of divine beings guiding humanity can be reflected in various cultures where people seek blessings or guidance from higher powers in their endeavors, such as starting a new business or embarking on a journey.
    • In relationships, the mantra can remind us of the importance of mutual respect and equality, encouraging individuals to treat each other with dignity and kindness, regardless of gender.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the significance of light, guidance, and the pursuit of goodness in life.
    • Its teachings are applicable in various aspects of life, including personal growth, relationships, and community well-being.
    • By embodying the principles of respect, equality, and the pursuit of auspiciousness, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and enlightened society.
