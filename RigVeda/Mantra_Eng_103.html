इन्द्रं विश्वा अवीवृधन समुद्रव्यचसां गिरः | रथीतमं रथीनां वाजानां सत्पतिम पतिम ||
indraṁ viśvā avīvṛdhan samudravyacasaṁ giraḥ | rathītamaṁ rathīnāṁ vājānāṁ satpatim patim ||
  • Indraṁ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Viśvā: Means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusiveness and the idea of unity among all beings.
  • Avīvṛdhan: Translates to "having expanded" or "having spread out," suggesting growth and the flourishing of life.
  • Samudravyacasaṁ: This term can be broken down into "samudra" (ocean) and "vyacasaṁ" (to speak or to express), implying the vastness of knowledge and wisdom that can be drawn from the depths of experience.
  • Giraḥ: Refers to "words" or "speech," emphasizing the importance of communication and expression in human life.
  • Rathītamaṁ: Means "the best of chariots," symbolizing the best means of progress or advancement in life.
  • Rathīnāṁ: Refers to "of charioteers," indicating those who guide and lead others, akin to leaders or mentors in society.
  • Vājānāṁ: Translates to "of steeds" or "of horses," representing strength, speed, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • Satpatim: Means "the true path" or "the right way," suggesting the importance of righteousness and ethical conduct.
  • Patim: Refers to "lord" or "master," indicating a figure of authority or guidance.

This mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, unity, and the pursuit of knowledge. It emphasizes the role of Indra as a powerful leader who fosters growth and communication among all beings. The imagery of chariots and steeds symbolizes the journey of life, where guidance and strength are essential for navigating challenges.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of Indra can inspire their team to work together towards a common goal, fostering an environment of collaboration and growth.
  • Education: Teachers and mentors can take inspiration from the idea of guiding others on the "true path," helping students navigate their learning journeys with wisdom and support.
  • Community Building: In social initiatives, the mantra encourages inclusiveness and unity, reminding us to work together for the betterment of society.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of strong leadership, effective communication, and the pursuit of knowledge in our lives. It encourages us to embrace unity and righteousness as we navigate our personal and collective journeys.
