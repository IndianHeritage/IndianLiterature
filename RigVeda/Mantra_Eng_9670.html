आच्छद्विधानैर गुपितो बार्हतैः सोम रक्षितः |ग्राव्णामिच्छृण्वन् तिष्ठसि न ते अशनाति पार्थिवः ||
ācchadvidhānair gupito bārhataiḥ soma rakṣitaḥ | grāvṇām ic chṛṇvan tiṣṭhasi na te aśnāti pārthivaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ācchadvidhānair: By the means of covering or protection
    • gupito: Guarded or protected
    • bārhataiḥ: By the Brahmanas or wise ones
    • soma: The sacred drink or essence
    • rakṣitaḥ: Protected or preserved
    • grāvṇām: Of the people or community
    • ic: Desiring or wishing
    • chṛṇvan: Hearing or listening
    • tiṣṭhasi: You stand or remain
    • na: Not
    • te: You
    • aśnāti: Consume or eat
    • pārthivaḥ: Mortal or earthly beings
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the protection and preservation of the sacred essence, represented by 'soma'.
    • It emphasizes the importance of guarding wisdom and knowledge, as imparted by the wise (Brahmanas).
    • The phrase "na te aśnāti pārthivaḥ" suggests that those who are protected and wise are not consumed by earthly desires or troubles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate environment, a company that values and protects its intellectual property (akin to 'soma') ensures its competitive edge and longevity.
    • In personal life, individuals who seek knowledge and wisdom, and surround themselves with protective influences (mentors, supportive communities) are less likely to be overwhelmed by worldly distractions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of safeguarding wisdom and knowledge in our lives.
    • It teaches us that by protecting our inner essence and being guided by wise influences, we can navigate through life's challenges without being consumed by them.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to seek knowledge, surround ourselves with supportive individuals, and remain steadfast in our pursuits, thus leading to a more fulfilling and protected existence.
