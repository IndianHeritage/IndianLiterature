वायुर न यो नियुत्वां इष्टयामā नासत्येव हव ā शम्भविष्टः |विश्ववारो द्रविणोदā इव तमन पूषेवा धीजवनोऽसि सोम ||
vāyur na yo niyutvām̐ iṣṭayāmā nāsatyeva hava ā śambhaviṣṭhaḥ | viśvavāro draviṇodā iva tman pūṣeva dhījavano 'si soma ||
  • vāyur: This word refers to "air" or "wind," symbolizing the vital force or life energy that sustains all living beings.
  • na yo: This phrase translates to "who does not," indicating a negation or absence of something.
  • niyutvām̐: This term means "to be restrained" or "to be controlled," suggesting the importance of discipline and regulation in life.
  • iṣṭayāmā: This word can be interpreted as "to be desired" or "to be wished for," highlighting the aspirations and goals one seeks in life.
  • nāsatyeva: This phrase means "like the non-existent," implying a state of absence or void, which can relate to unfulfilled desires or goals.
  • hava: This term means "to invoke" or "to call upon," indicating the act of seeking blessings or support from higher powers.
  • ā: A preposition meaning "towards" or "to," suggesting direction or purpose.
  • śambhaviṣṭhaḥ: This word refers to "the one who is auspicious" or "the one who brings prosperity," often associated with divine blessings.
  • viśvavāro: This term means "the protector of all," symbolizing a universal guardian or force that safeguards life.
  • draviṇodā: This phrase translates to "the giver of wealth," indicating abundance and prosperity in life.
  • iva: This word means "like" or "as," used for comparison.
  • tman: This term refers to "self" or "soul," emphasizing the importance of self-awareness and inner strength.
  • pūṣeva: This phrase means "like the nourisher," indicating the nurturing aspect of life that sustains growth and development.
  • dhījavano: This term translates to "the wise" or "the intelligent," highlighting the value of wisdom and knowledge in making decisions.
  • 'si: A form of the verb "to be," indicating existence or presence.
  • soma: This word refers to the sacred drink or elixir, symbolizing spiritual nourishment and enlightenment.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of air as a vital force, the importance of discipline in achieving desires, and the need for divine blessings in our pursuits. It highlights the role of wisdom and self-awareness in navigating life's challenges while seeking prosperity and protection. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, goal-setting, and the pursuit of knowledge, reminding us to invoke higher powers for guidance and support in our endeavors.
