ये महो राजसो विदुर विश्वे देवासो अद्रुहः |मरुद्भिर अग्न आ गहि ||
ye maho rajaso vidur viśve devāso adruhaḥ | marudbhir agna ā gahi ||
  • Translation: "Those who know the great kings, the universal deities, who are free from enmity, may the Maruts bring Agni to us."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ye: Those who
    • maho: great
    • rajaso: kings or rulers
    • vidur: know or understand
    • viśve: universal or all
    • devāso: deities or divine beings
    • adruhaḥ: free from enmity or hostility
    • marudbhir: by the Maruts (the storm deities)
    • agna: Agni (the fire god)
    • ā gahi: may you come to us or bring to us

  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of understanding and recognizing the great rulers and divine beings who are benevolent and free from hostility.
    • The Maruts, representing the forces of nature, are invoked to bring Agni, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, it is crucial to recognize and support leaders who act with integrity and without malice. For instance, a community leader who promotes peace and cooperation among diverse groups embodies the essence of this mantra.
    • In environmental contexts, acknowledging the forces of nature (like the Maruts) can lead to sustainable practices. For example, communities that respect natural resources and work harmoniously with the environment reflect the teachings of this mantra.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of understanding and honoring those who lead with wisdom and compassion.
    • It encourages individuals and communities to seek harmony with nature and the divine, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in various aspects of life, from personal relationships to community leadership and environmental stewardship.
