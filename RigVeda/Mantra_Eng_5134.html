अग्निं नरो दीधितिभिर अरण्योः हस्तच्युतī जनयन्त प्रशस्तम् |दूरदृशं गृहपतिम अथार्युम् ||
agniṁ naro dīdhitibhir araṇyor hastacyutī janayanta praśastam | dūredṛśaṁ gṛhapatim atharyum ||
  • Agniṁ: Refers to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Naro: Means 'man' or 'human', indicating the connection between humanity and the divine.
  • Dīdhitibhir: Translates to 'with brilliance' or 'with light', suggesting enlightenment and knowledge.
  • Araṇyor: Refers to 'the two fires', which can symbolize duality in life, such as creation and destruction.
  • Hastacyutī: Means 'the hand that does not falter', representing strength and determination in action.
  • Janayanta: Translates to 'they generate' or 'they create', indicating the act of bringing forth or manifesting.
  • Praśastam: Means 'praise' or 'well-spoken', suggesting the importance of positive words and intentions.
  • Dūredṛśam: Refers to 'the one who is far-sighted', symbolizing wisdom and foresight in decision-making.
  • Gṛhapatim: Means 'the lord of the house', representing stability, security, and domestic harmony.
  • Atharyum: Translates to 'the one who is worthy', indicating the importance of virtue and righteousness.

The mantra emphasizes the relationship between humanity and the divine, particularly through the symbolism of fire (Agni). It highlights the qualities of strength, wisdom, and the importance of positive actions and intentions in life. The duality of existence is acknowledged, reminding us that creation and destruction coexist.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Just as Agni transforms and purifies, individuals can seek to transform their lives through knowledge and self-improvement.
  • Leadership: The qualities of foresight and determination are essential for effective leadership, guiding teams and communities towards success.
  • Conflict Resolution: The mantra encourages positive communication and intentions, which can help in resolving conflicts and fostering harmony.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine, urging individuals to embody strength, wisdom, and positive action in their lives. Its significance lies in its applicability to personal development, leadership, and the cultivation of harmonious relationships.
