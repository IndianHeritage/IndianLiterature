गन्धर्व इथ्था पदम् अस्य रक्षति पाति देवानां जनिमान्य अद्भुतः |गृह्णाति रिपुं निधया निधापतिः सुखृत्तमाः मधुनो भक्षम् आशत ||
gandharva itthā padam asya rakṣati pāti devānāṁ janimāny adbhutaḥ | gṛbhṇāti ripuṁ nidhayā nidhāpatiḥ sukṛttamā madhuno bhakṣam āśata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • gandharva: Refers to celestial beings or spirits associated with music and joy.
    • itthā: Thus or in this manner.
    • padam: The place or position.
    • asya: Of this.
    • rakṣati: Protects or guards.
    • pāti: Nourishes or sustains.
    • devānāṁ: Of the gods.
    • janimāny: The one who is born or created.
    • adbhutaḥ: Wonderful or extraordinary.
    • gṛbhṇāti: Seizes or takes.
    • ripuṁ: The enemy.
    • nidhayā: By the treasure or wealth.
    • nidhāpatiḥ: The lord of treasures.
    • sukṛttamā: The best of good deeds.
    • madhuno: Of the honey or sweetness.
    • bhakṣam: Food or sustenance.
    • āśata: May it be granted or bestowed.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the protective and nurturing qualities of divine beings, particularly in relation to the blessings they bestow upon individuals.
    • The mention of "gandharva" suggests a connection to the arts, beauty, and joy, indicating that these divine forces are not just protectors but also bringers of happiness.
    • The phrase "rakṣati pāti" emphasizes the dual role of the divine: to protect and to nourish, which can be seen in real life through the support we receive from our families, communities, and spiritual beliefs.
    • The idea of "nidhā" or treasure can be interpreted as the wealth of knowledge, love, and support that we gather throughout our lives, which helps us confront challenges or "ripuṁ" (enemies).
    • The reference to "sukṛttamā" highlights the importance of good deeds and positive actions, suggesting that our moral choices lead to divine favor and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often turn to their faith or community for support, reflecting the protective aspect of the divine.
    • Artists and musicians may find inspiration in the beauty of nature or divine presence, akin to the "gandharva" influence, which can lead to profound creative expressions.
    • Acts of kindness and charity can be seen as "sukṛttamā," where individuals contribute positively to society, thereby attracting blessings and support in return.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine protection, nourishment, and the importance of good deeds in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that we are supported by higher powers and that our actions can lead to positive outcomes, both for ourselves and for others.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to seek harmony, engage in good deeds, and appreciate the beauty and joy that life offers, fostering a sense of community and spiritual connection.
