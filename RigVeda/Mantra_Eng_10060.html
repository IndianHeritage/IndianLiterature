मन्त्र: ब्रह्मचारि चरति वेविषद विषः स देवानाम् भवत्य एकम् अङ्गम् |तेन जायाम् अन्व अविन्दद बृहस्पतिः सोमेन नीतां जुह्वं न देवाः ||
brahmacārī carati veviṣad viṣaḥ sa devānām bhavaty ekam aṅgam | tena jāyām anv avindad bṛhaspatiḥ somena nītāṁ juhva1ṁ na devāḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra speaks of the Brahmachari (a celibate student) who moves towards the divine, avoiding poison (negative influences) and becoming one with the gods. Through this path, he finds a bride, guided by Brihaspati (the deity of wisdom) and Soma (the deity of the moon and nectar).
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Brahmacārī: A student or seeker who practices celibacy and is dedicated to spiritual learning.
    • Carati: Moves or progresses.
    • Veviṣad: Avoids or shuns.
    • Viṣaḥ: Poison, representing negative influences or distractions.
    • Sa: He or that one.
    • Devānām: Of the gods.
    • Bhavaty: Becomes or is.
    • Ekam: One or unified.
    • Aṅgam: Limb or part, indicating unity with the divine.
    • Tena: By him or through this.
    • Jāyām: A bride or partner.
    • Anv avindad: He found or attained.
    • Bṛhaspatiḥ: The deity of wisdom and eloquence.
    • Somena: By the moon or nectar, symbolizing nourishment and enlightenment.
    • Nītāṁ: Guided or led.
    • Juhvaṁ: Offering or sacrifice.
    • Na: Not.
    • Devāḥ: The gods.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A student dedicating themselves to their studies, avoiding distractions such as social media or negative peer pressure, embodies the spirit of the Brahmachari.
    • In a professional setting, a person who focuses on their career goals while avoiding toxic work environments or relationships can be seen as following the path described in the mantra.
    • Individuals who seek spiritual growth through meditation and self-discipline, while steering clear of harmful habits, reflect the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the importance of discipline, focus, and the pursuit of higher knowledge. It teaches that by avoiding negative influences and distractions, one can achieve unity with the divine and find fulfillment in life. This principle is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as education, career development, and personal growth, where dedication and the right guidance lead to success and harmony.
