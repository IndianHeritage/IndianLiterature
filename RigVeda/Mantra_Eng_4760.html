अध्वर्यो वीर प्र महे सुतानाम इन्द्राय भरा स ह्य अस्य राजा |यः पूर्व्याभिर उता नूतनाभिर गीरभिर वावृधे गृणताम ऋषीणाम ||
adhvaryo vīra pra mahe sutānām indrāya bhara sa hy asya rājā | yaḥ pūrvyābhir uta nūtanābhir gīrbhir vāvṛdhe gṛṇatām ṛṣīṇām ||
  • adhvaryo: This term refers to the priest or the officiant in a Vedic ritual, particularly in the context of sacrifices. The adhvaryu plays a crucial role in performing the rituals and ensuring that they are conducted properly.
  • vīra: This word means "hero" or "brave one." It signifies strength, valor, and the qualities of a leader or a warrior.
  • pra mahe: This phrase translates to "we invoke" or "we praise." It indicates a call to the divine or a request for blessings.
  • sutānām: This means "of the sons" or "of the offspring." It often refers to the progeny or the results of one's actions.
  • indrāya: This term is directed towards Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, who represents strength, rain, and thunder. Indra is often invoked for protection and prosperity.
  • bhara: This means "to bring" or "to bestow." It is a request for the divine to grant blessings or abundance.
  • sa hy asya rājā: This translates to "for this king" or "for this ruler." It emphasizes the importance of leadership and governance in society.
  • yaḥ: This means "who" and introduces a relative clause.
  • pūrvyābhir: This term refers to "the ancient" or "the previous" ones, indicating a connection to tradition and the wisdom of the past.
  • uta: This means "or" and is used to present an alternative or additional idea.
  • nūtanābhir: This translates to "the new" or "the modern," suggesting a balance between tradition and innovation.
  • gīrbhir: This means "with hymns" or "with praises," indicating the importance of words and expressions in rituals.
  • vāvṛdhe: This term means "to increase" or "to grow," signifying the desire for prosperity and abundance.
  • gṛṇatām: This translates to "let them sing" or "let them praise," emphasizing the communal aspect of worship and celebration.
  • ṛṣīṇām: This means "of the sages" or "of the seers," highlighting the role of wise individuals in guiding society.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of invoking divine blessings for strength, prosperity, and leadership. It reflects the balance between honoring tradition and embracing new ideas, showcasing the role of community and wisdom in achieving success. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during important ceremonies, leadership initiatives, or community gatherings, where collective strength and divine guidance are sought for progress and harmony.
