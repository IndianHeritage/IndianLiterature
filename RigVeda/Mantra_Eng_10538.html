यो विश्वाभि विपश्यति भुवना सं च पश्यति |स नः पार्षद अति द्विषः ||
yo viśvābhi vipaśyati bhuvanā saṁ ca paśyati | sa naḥ parṣad ati dviṣaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • viśvābhi: with all beings
    • vipaśyati: perceives or sees
    • bhuvanā: the worlds or realms
    • saṁ: together
    • ca: and
    • paśyati: sees or observes
    • sa: he or that one
    • naḥ: our
    • parṣad: assembly or council
    • ati: beyond
    • dviṣaḥ: enemies or those who hate
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine being or consciousness that perceives all beings and the various realms of existence.
    • The phrase "who sees with all beings" suggests a universal awareness that transcends individual perspectives.
    • The second part emphasizes the importance of unity and collective vision, as it mentions seeing together.
    • Finally, it invokes a plea for protection from adversaries or negativity, highlighting the need for support from a higher power in overcoming challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, a leader who understands the perspectives of all team members and fosters collaboration can create a harmonious environment, akin to the universal awareness described in the mantra.
    • In community initiatives, when individuals come together to address social issues, they embody the collective vision of seeing and acting together for the greater good.
    • In personal life, seeking guidance from a mentor or spiritual leader can provide protection and wisdom in navigating conflicts or adversities, resonating with the plea for support against enemies.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of universal perception, collective action, and the need for protection from negativity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as leadership, community building, and personal growth.
    • By embodying the principles of unity and awareness, individuals can foster a more harmonious existence and navigate challenges effectively.
