यā त ūतिर अमित्रहन मक्षूजवस्तमासति |तयā नो हिनुही रथम ||
yā ta ūtir amitrahan makṣūjavastamāsati | tayā no hinuhī ratham ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yā: that which
    • ta: you
    • ūtir: the strength or power
    • amitrahan: of the enemies
    • makṣūjavastam: the one who is swift and powerful
    • āsati: resides or dwells
    • tayā: by that
    • no: for us
    • hinuhī: may we be protected or defended
    • ratham: the chariot or vehicle
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of invoking a powerful force that can protect us from our enemies.
    • The term "ūtir" signifies strength, suggesting that we seek a robust power to aid us.
    • "Makṣūjavastam" refers to a swift and potent force, indicating the need for quick and effective protection.
    • The phrase "tayā no hinuhī ratham" emphasizes the desire for this power to defend our journey or path, represented by the chariot.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive work environment, individuals often face challenges from colleagues. This mantra can be interpreted as a call for inner strength and resilience to overcome professional adversities.
    • In personal relationships, conflicts may arise that feel like battles. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek support and strength to navigate these challenges effectively.
    • In a broader societal context, communities facing external threats can draw inspiration from this mantra to unite and protect their collective interests.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of strength and protection against adversities.
    • It teaches us the importance of seeking support, whether from within ourselves or from external sources, to navigate life's challenges.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, from personal struggles to collective community efforts, emphasizing resilience and unity in the face of opposition.
