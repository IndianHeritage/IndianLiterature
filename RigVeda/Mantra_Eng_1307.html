एतानि वां श्रवस्यā सुदानु ब्रह्माङ्गूषं सदनं रोधस्योः |यद वाम पज्रासो अश्विनā हवन्ते यातम इषा च विदुषे च वाजम् ||
etāni vāṁ śravasyā sudānū brahmāṅgūṣaṁ sadanaṁ rodasyoḥ | yad vām pajrāso aśvinā havante yātam iṣā ca viduṣe ca vājam ||
  • etāni vāṁ: This phrase translates to "these are yours." It signifies possession or connection to something sacred or important.
  • śravasyā: This word means "of the glorious" or "of the renowned." It refers to the qualities of fame and honor.
  • sudānū: This term can be interpreted as "well-formed" or "well-structured," indicating something that is complete and perfect.
  • brahmāṅgūṣaṁ: This refers to "the essence of Brahman," which is the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit in Hindu philosophy.
  • sadanaṁ: This means "abode" or "dwelling place," suggesting a space that is sacred or significant.
  • rodasyoḥ: This translates to "of the two worlds" or "of the heavens and the earth," indicating a connection between different realms of existence.
  • yad: This means "that" or "which," pointing to something specific that is being referred to.
  • vām: This translates to "you," indicating a direct address to the divine or a higher power.
  • pajrāso: This term can be understood as "the offerings" or "the sacrifices," which are significant in Vedic rituals.
  • aśvinā: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn, symbolizing duality and balance.
  • havante: This means "they bring" or "they offer," indicating an action of giving or presenting something valuable.
  • yātam: This translates to "to you" or "towards you," suggesting a direction of devotion or prayer.
  • iṣā: This word means "power" or "authority," indicating strength and capability.
  • ca: This means "and," connecting ideas or elements together.
  • viduṣe: This translates to "to the wise" or "to the knowledgeable," indicating respect for wisdom and understanding.
  • ca vājam: This means "and the strength" or "and the vigor," referring to the energy or force behind actions.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the connection between the divine and the material world, highlighting the importance of offerings, wisdom, and the essence of Brahman. It signifies the relationship between the worshipper and the divine, encouraging individuals to recognize their place in the cosmos and the significance of their actions. In real life, this mantra can be applied in rituals, meditation, and daily practices that promote mindfulness, respect for nature, and the pursuit of knowledge. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of honoring the divine in every aspect of life.
