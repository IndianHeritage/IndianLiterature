इन्द्रवायू बृहस्पतिम मित्राग्निम पूषणं भगम् |आदित्यान मारुतं गणम् ||
indravāyū bṛhaspatim mitrāgnim pūṣaṇam bhagam | ādityān mārutaṁ gaṇam ||
  • Indravāyū: This term refers to the combined powers of Indra, the king of gods, and Vāyu, the god of wind. Indra symbolizes strength, power, and leadership, while Vāyu represents movement and the vital breath of life.
  • Bṛhaspatim: Bṛhaspati is the guru of the gods and represents wisdom, knowledge, and guidance. He is often associated with the planet Jupiter, which is considered a symbol of prosperity and good fortune.
  • Mitrāgnim: This term combines Mitra, the god of friendship and harmony, with Agni, the god of fire. Together, they symbolize the importance of relationships and the transformative power of fire, which can purify and energize.
  • Pūṣaṇam: Pūṣaṇa is a deity associated with nourishment and protection. He is often invoked for guidance in matters of health and well-being, representing the nurturing aspect of life.
  • Bhagam: This term refers to fortune or wealth. It signifies the blessings and abundance that one can receive in life.
  • Ādityān: This term refers to the Adityas, a group of solar deities representing various aspects of the sun, such as light, truth, and vitality.
  • Mārutaṁ: Mārutas are the storm gods, representing the forces of nature and the power of the elements. They embody the dynamic and sometimes unpredictable aspects of life.
  • Gaṇam: This term means a group or assembly, indicating the collective power and unity of the deities mentioned.

This mantra invokes a powerful assembly of deities, each representing different aspects of life, such as strength, wisdom, harmony, nourishment, and fortune. By calling upon these divine forces, the mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of various elements in life and the importance of seeking guidance and support from higher powers.


Real-Life Application: In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various life situations. For instance, when facing challenges, one might seek the strength of Indra and Vāyu to overcome obstacles. In times of decision-making, invoking Bṛhaspati can provide clarity and wisdom. The nurturing aspect of Pūṣaṇa can be called upon for health and well-being, while the blessings of Mitra and Agni can enhance relationships and personal growth.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, encouraging individuals to seek balance and support from various divine aspects. It serves as a reminder of the importance of community, wisdom, and the interconnectedness of all elements in our lives. By invoking these deities, one can cultivate a sense of harmony, strength, and abundance in their personal and communal endeavors.
