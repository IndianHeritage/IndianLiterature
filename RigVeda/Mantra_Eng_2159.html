सद्मेव प्राचो वि मिमाय मानैर वज्रेण खाण्य अतृणन नदीनाम् |वृथा­sृजत पथिभिर दीर्घयाथैः सोमस्य ता मद इन्द्रश चकार ||
sadmeva prāco vi mimāya mānair vajreṇa khāny atṛṇan nadīnām | vṛthāsṛjat pathibhir dīrghayāthaiḥ somasya tā mada indraś cakāra ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sadmeva: truly, indeed
    • prāco: from the east
    • vi: apart, separately
    • mimāya: he created, he manifested
    • mānair: with respect, with honor
    • vajreṇa: with the thunderbolt, with strength
    • khāny: to the void, to the emptiness
    • atṛṇan: he did not harm, he did not destroy
    • nadīnām: of the rivers
    • vṛthā: in vain, uselessly
    • sṛjat: he created, he produced
    • pathibhir: by the paths, by the ways
    • dīrghayāthaiḥ: with long-lasting, enduring
    • somasya: of the moon, of the soma
    • tā: that, those
    • mada: intoxication, joy
    • indraś: of Indra, the king of gods
    • cakāra: he made, he did
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the creation and manifestation of the universe, particularly focusing on the role of Indra, the king of gods, who is often associated with strength and power.
    • The phrase "sadmeva prāco vi mimāya" suggests that the creation is a true and significant act that comes from the east, symbolizing new beginnings or enlightenment.
    • The use of "vajreṇa" indicates that this creation is done with great strength, akin to how a thunderbolt strikes with force, representing the power of divine intervention.
    • "Khāny atṛṇan nadīnām" implies that this creation does not harm the rivers, which can be interpreted as a reminder of the importance of preserving nature and the environment.
    • The line "vṛthāsṛjat pathibhir dīrghayāthaiḥ" emphasizes that the paths created are enduring and significant, suggesting that the choices we make in life should lead to lasting impacts.
    • Finally, "somasya tā mada indraś cakāra" reflects the joy and intoxication that comes from the divine, indicating that the blessings of the gods can lead to fulfillment and happiness in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In our daily lives, we often face challenges that require strength and resilience. Just as Indra uses his thunderbolt to create and protect, we can draw on our inner strength to overcome obstacles.
    • The mantra reminds us of the importance of environmental conservation. For instance, when we engage in sustainable practices, we honor the rivers and nature, ensuring that our actions do not harm the ecosystem.
    • In decision-making, the idea of creating enduring paths can be applied. For example, when choosing a career, one should consider not just immediate benefits but also long-term fulfillment and impact on society.
    • The joy and intoxication mentioned can be related to moments of happiness in life, such as celebrating achievements or connecting with loved ones, which are often seen as blessings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, strength, and the importance of making choices that are beneficial and enduring.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to respect nature, draw on our inner strength, and seek joy in our lives, all while being mindful of the impact of our actions.
    • In a world where challenges abound, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for living a life that is both meaningful and harmonious with the universe.
