वनस्पते वीढ्वाङ्गो हि भूयाऽस्मत्सखा प्रतरणः सुवीरः |गोभिः संनद्धोऽसि वीळयस्वास्थाता ते जयतु जेत्वानि ||
vanaspate vīḍvaṅgo hi bhūyā asmatsakhā prataraṇaḥ suvīraḥ | gobhiḥ saṁnaddho asi vīḻayasvāsthātā te jayatu jetvāni ||
  • vanaspate: This term refers to the "lord of the plants" or "vegetation." In a real-life context, it can symbolize the importance of nature and the environment in sustaining life.
  • vīḍvaṅgo: This word can be interpreted as "one who is wise" or "knowledgeable." It emphasizes the value of wisdom and understanding in our actions and decisions.
  • hi: A conjunction meaning "indeed" or "certainly," reinforcing the importance of the preceding terms.
  • bhūyā: This means "may you become" or "may you grow." It suggests a desire for growth and development, both personally and collectively.
  • asmatsakhā: Translated as "our friend," it highlights the significance of friendship and community support in achieving goals.
  • prataraṇaḥ: This term means "overcoming" or "crossing over," indicating the ability to overcome obstacles in life.
  • suvīraḥ: Meaning "good hero" or "brave one," it signifies the qualities of courage and valor that are essential in facing challenges.
  • gobhiḥ: This word refers to "cows" or "nourishment," symbolizing abundance and the sustenance provided by nature.
  • saṁnaddho: Meaning "bound" or "connected," it suggests the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of unity.
  • asi: A form of the verb "to be," indicating existence or presence.
  • vīḻayasvāsthātā: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is established in victory," emphasizing the importance of stability and success.
  • te: Meaning "your," it personalizes the mantra, addressing the divine or the higher power.
  • jayatu: This means "may he/she/it be victorious," a wish for success and triumph.
  • jetvāni: This term refers to "having conquered," indicating the achievement of victory over challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of growth, wisdom, friendship, and overcoming obstacles. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the importance of courage and support from others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, teamwork, and environmental conservation. By embodying the qualities of a "good hero" and fostering strong relationships, individuals can navigate challenges effectively and contribute positively to their communities. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and resilience, encouraging us to strive for success while remaining grounded in our connections with nature and each other.
