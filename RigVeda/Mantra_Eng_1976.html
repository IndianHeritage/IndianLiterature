अग्नि त्वं पारयाण नव्यो अस्मान् स्वस्तिभिर् अति दुर्गाणि विश्वा |पूष च पृथ्वी बहुला न उर्वी भव तोकाय तनयाय शं योः ||
agne tvam pārayā navyo asmān svastibhir ati durgāṇi viśvā | pūś ca pṛthvī bahulā na urvī bhavā tokāya tanayāya śaṁ yoḥ ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Tvam: This means "you," addressing Agni directly, indicating a personal relationship with the divine.
  • Pārayā: This translates to "may you protect" or "may you lead us," expressing a plea for guidance and safety.
  • Navyo: This means "new" or "fresh," suggesting a desire for renewal and rejuvenation in life.
  • Asmān: This means "us," indicating a collective appeal for support and protection.
  • Svastibhir: This word means "with auspiciousness," emphasizing the importance of well-being and prosperity.
  • Ati: This translates to "beyond" or "exceeding," indicating a desire for surpassing challenges.
  • Durgāṇi: This means "difficulties" or "obstacles," acknowledging the presence of challenges in life.
  • Viśvā: This translates to "all" or "universal," indicating a holistic approach to life and challenges.
  • Pūś: This refers to "nourisher" or "provider," symbolizing sustenance and support.
  • Ca: This means "and," connecting ideas and emphasizing unity.
  • Pṛthvī: This means "earth," representing stability, grounding, and the physical world.
  • Bahulā: This translates to "abundant" or "plentiful," indicating a desire for richness in life.
  • Na: This means "not," indicating a negation or a desire to avoid something.
  • Urvi: This refers to "the vastness" or "the expanse," symbolizing the limitless nature of existence.
  • Bhavā: This means "may you become," indicating a wish for transformation or manifestation.
  • Tokāya: This translates to "for offspring" or "for progeny," emphasizing the importance of family and continuity.
  • Tanayāya: This means "for children," reinforcing the focus on future generations.
  • Śaṁ: This translates to "peace" or "well-being," indicating a desire for harmony.
  • Yoḥ: This means "of both," indicating a duality or partnership in the context of the mantra.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda is a profound invocation to Agni, seeking protection, nourishment, and the overcoming of obstacles. It emphasizes the importance of community, well-being, and the continuity of life through future generations. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during family gatherings, rituals for new beginnings, or when facing challenges in personal or professional life. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine support available to us in our journey. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of peace, purpose, and resilience in the face of life's difficulties.
