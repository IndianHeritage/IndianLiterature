न पूषणं मेथामसि सूक्तैर अभि गृणीमसि |वसूनि दसमं ईमहे ||
na pūṣaṇam methāmasi sūktair abhi gṛṇīmasi | vasūni dasmam īmahe ||
  • na pūṣaṇam methāmasi: This phrase can be translated as "We do not seek the nourishment of Pusha." Here, "Pusha" refers to a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity. The term "methāmasi" implies a sense of intention or desire. Thus, this part of the mantra suggests a conscious decision to not pursue materialistic or superficial forms of nourishment.
  • sūktair abhi gṛṇīmasi: This translates to "We sing praises with hymns." The word "sūktair" refers to hymns or verses that are composed to honor the divine. "Abhi gṛṇīmasi" indicates an act of offering or expressing reverence. This part emphasizes the importance of spiritual nourishment through devotion and the power of words in connecting with the divine.
  • vasūni dasmam īmahe: This can be interpreted as "We aspire for the wealth of the universe." "Vasūni" refers to wealth or abundance, while "dasmam" signifies a collective aspiration. "Īmahe" means to strive or seek. This phrase highlights the desire for a holistic and universal form of wealth that transcends material possessions, focusing instead on spiritual and communal prosperity.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates a profound philosophy of seeking spiritual nourishment over material wealth. It encourages individuals to focus on inner growth, community well-being, and the power of collective intention.
  • Real-life Application: In today's fast-paced world, where materialism often overshadows spiritual values, this mantra serves as a reminder to prioritize meaningful connections and spiritual practices. For instance, in a corporate setting, fostering a culture of collaboration and shared values can lead to a more fulfilling work environment, akin to the collective aspiration for universal wealth mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The mantra teaches us that true nourishment comes from spiritual practices and community bonds rather than mere material accumulation. By embracing this philosophy, individuals can cultivate a more balanced and fulfilling life, contributing to both personal growth and the welfare of society.
