प्र त इन्द्र पूर्व्याणि प्र नूनं वीर्याः वोचं प्रथमाः कृतानि |सतीनमन्‍युर अश्रथायो अद्रिं सुवेदनाम अकृणोर ब्रह्मणे गाम् ||
pra ta indra pūrvyāṇi pra nūnaṁ vīryā vocam prathamā kṛtāni | satīnamanyur aśrathāyo adriṁ suvedanām akṛṇor brahmaṇe gām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: forward, towards
    • ta: that, those
    • indra: the chief deity, often associated with strength and power
    • pūrvyāṇi: the ancient, the previous
    • pra nūnaṁ: indeed, truly
    • vīryā: strength, valor
    • vocam: I speak, I declare
    • prathamā: first, foremost
    • kṛtāni: done, accomplished
    • satīnam: of the truthful, the virtuous
    • anyur: anger, wrath
    • aśrathāyo: without disturbance, untroubled
    • adriṁ: the mountain, the stronghold
    • suvedanām: well-known, well-versed
    • akṛṇor: not to be harmed, protected
    • brahmaṇe: to the divine, the ultimate reality
    • gām: the goal, the path
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the king of gods, emphasizing the importance of strength and valor in achieving one's goals.
    • The phrase "pra ta indra pūrvyāṇi" suggests a call to the ancient powers and wisdom that guide us.
    • By stating "vīryā vocam prathamā kṛtāni," it highlights the significance of declaring one's intentions and actions with strength and clarity.
    • The mention of "satīnamanyur aśrathāyo" indicates the importance of maintaining a virtuous and calm demeanor, free from anger and disturbance.
    • Finally, "brahmaṇe gām" signifies the pursuit of a higher truth or ultimate reality, suggesting that our actions should align with a greater purpose.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when leading a team, invoking the qualities of Indra can inspire confidence and strength among team members, encouraging them to work towards a common goal.
    • In personal development, the mantra can serve as a reminder to approach challenges with valor and to communicate intentions clearly, fostering better relationships.
    • In conflict resolution, embodying the qualities of "satīnamanyur" can help in maintaining peace and understanding, allowing for constructive dialogue without anger.
    • In spiritual practices, the mantra can be used as a meditative focus, aligning one's actions with a higher purpose and seeking guidance from the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of strength, clarity of purpose, and virtuous conduct in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals to act with integrity and courage, while also seeking a connection to a higher truth.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in leadership, personal growth, conflict resolution, and spiritual practices, making it a timeless guide for navigating life's challenges.
