प्रतश्च यस्य सप्रतश्च नामानुष्टुभस्य हविषो हविर यत् |धातुर द्युतानात् सवितुश्च विष्णो रथंतरम् आ जभारा वसिष्ठः ||
prathaś ca yasya saprathaś ca nāmānuṣṭubhasya haviṣo havir yat | dhātur dyutānāt savituś ca viṣṇo rathaṁtaram ā jabhārā vasiṣṭhaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "prathaś ca yasya saprathaś ca nāmānuṣṭubhasya haviṣo havir yat | dhātur dyutānāt savituś ca viṣṇo rathaṁtaram ā jabhārā vasiṣṭhaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prathaś: in the morning
    • ca: and
    • yasya: whose
    • saprathaś: with great effort
    • ca: and
    • nāmā: names
    • anuṣṭubhasya: of the Anuṣṭubh meter
    • haviṣo: of offerings
    • havir: the offering
    • yat: which
    • dhātur: of the creator
    • dyutānāt: from the brilliance
    • savituh: of Savitar (the Sun)
    • ca: and
    • viṣṇo: of Vishnu
    • rathaṁtaram: the chariot
    • ā: towards
    • jabhārā: has brought
    • vasiṣṭhaḥ: Vasistha (a sage)
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the morning and the efforts associated with it, emphasizing the importance of the dawn in spiritual practices.
    • The mention of "haviṣo havir" refers to offerings made during rituals, highlighting the significance of devotion and gratitude.
    • The reference to "dhātur dyutānāt" connects the brilliance of the creator (often associated with the Sun) to the divine energy that sustains life.
    • By invoking "savituś ca viṣṇo," the mantra acknowledges the roles of both the Sun and Vishnu, representing the cycle of creation and preservation.
    • The term "rathaṁtaram" symbolizes the journey of life, where the chariot represents the soul's passage through various experiences.
    • Vasistha, a revered sage, signifies wisdom and guidance, suggesting that one should seek knowledge and enlightenment in their journey.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Morning rituals or practices, such as yoga or meditation, can be seen as a way to connect with the divine and set a positive tone for the day.
    • In many cultures, offerings are made to express gratitude, whether it be through food, flowers, or acts of kindness, reflecting the essence of "haviṣo havir."
    • The Sun's rising is a daily reminder of new beginnings, encouraging individuals to embrace change and growth.
    • Incorporating the teachings of wise figures, like Vasistha, into daily life can guide individuals in making informed decisions and fostering personal development.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of morning rituals, the importance of offerings, and the guidance of wisdom in navigating life's journey.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to honor the divine forces in our lives, seek knowledge, and embrace the new beginnings that each day brings.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to cultivate a spiritual practice, express gratitude, and seek wisdom, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and enlightened existence.
