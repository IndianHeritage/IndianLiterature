यत्र कामा निकामाश्च यत्र ब्रह्मनस्य विष्टपम् |स्वधा च यत्र तृप्तिः च तत्र मां अमृतं कृधीर्यन्द्रो परि श्रव ||
yatra kāmā nikāmāś ca yatra bradhnasya viṣṭapam | svadhā ca yatra tṛptiś ca tatra mām amṛtaṁ kṛdhīndrāyendo pari srava ||
  • yatra kāmā nikāmāś ca: This phrase translates to "where desires, both fulfilled and unfulfilled, exist." It signifies the dual nature of human desires, highlighting that both the attainment and the absence of desires play a crucial role in our lives.
  • yatra bradhnasya viṣṭapam: This means "where the abundance of wealth and prosperity resides." It emphasizes the importance of material and spiritual wealth in achieving a fulfilling life.
  • svadhā ca yatra tṛptiś ca: This translates to "where self-offering and satisfaction are present." It suggests that true contentment comes from selflessness and the act of giving, rather than merely receiving.
  • tatra mām amṛtaṁ kṛdhīndrāyendo pari srava: This can be interpreted as "there, may I be blessed with immortality, flowing like the nectar of the gods." It expresses a desire for eternal bliss and spiritual enlightenment, akin to the divine nectar that grants immortality.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of human existence, emphasizing the balance between desires, wealth, selflessness, and the pursuit of spiritual fulfillment. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek harmony in our desires, to appreciate both what we have and what we lack, and to find joy in giving and sharing with others. By embodying these principles, we can strive for a life that is not only materially prosperous but also spiritually enriching, ultimately leading us towards a state of bliss and contentment akin to immortality.
