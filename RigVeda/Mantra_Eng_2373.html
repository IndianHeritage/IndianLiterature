यज्ञैः सम्मिश्लाः पृथतीभिर ऋष्टिभिर यामञ्च चुभ्रासो अञ्जिषु प्रियाऽउत |आसद्याः बरहिर भरतस्य सूनवः पोत्राद आ सोमं पिबतā दिवो नरः ||
yajñaiḥ sammiślāḥ pṛṣatībhir ṛṣṭibhir yāmañ chubhrāso añjiṣu priyā uta | āsadyā barhir bharatasya sūnavaḥ potrād ā somam pibatā divo naraḥ ||
  • yajñaiḥ: This term refers to sacrifices or offerings made to the deities. In real life, this can be seen in various cultures where rituals and offerings are made to seek blessings or express gratitude.
  • sammiślāḥ: This word signifies the act of coming together or uniting. In a modern context, it can represent teamwork or collaboration in achieving a common goal.
  • pṛṣatībhir: This term relates to the essence or core of something. In everyday life, it can be associated with understanding the fundamental principles behind actions or beliefs.
  • ṛṣṭibhir: This refers to the results or outcomes of actions. In practical terms, it can be linked to the consequences of our decisions and how they shape our lives.
  • yāmañ: This word means 'those who are' or 'who are present'. It emphasizes the importance of being aware of one's surroundings and the people in one's life.
  • chubhrāso: This term can be interpreted as 'pure' or 'sacred'. In real life, it can relate to maintaining integrity and purity in thoughts and actions.
  • añjiṣu: This word signifies 'in the offerings' or 'in the rituals'. It highlights the importance of intention behind actions, especially in spiritual practices.
  • priyā uta: This phrase translates to 'beloved' or 'dear'. It reflects the significance of love and affection in relationships and community.
  • āsadyā: This term means 'seated' or 'established'. It can be related to finding stability and grounding in life.
  • barhir: This refers to the 'sacred grass' used in rituals. In a broader sense, it symbolizes the foundation of spiritual practices.
  • bharatasya: This term relates to 'of Bharata', which can be interpreted as a reference to the Indian subcontinent or its people. It signifies cultural identity and heritage.
  • sūnavaḥ: This means 'sons' or 'descendants'. It emphasizes the importance of lineage and the continuation of values through generations.
  • potrād: This term refers to 'from the pot' or 'from the vessel'. It can symbolize the source of nourishment and sustenance in life.
  • ā somam: This phrase translates to 'the soma', which is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals. It represents the pursuit of higher consciousness and spiritual awakening.
  • pibatā: This means 'drink' or 'consume'. In a metaphorical sense, it can relate to absorbing knowledge and experiences.
  • divo: This term means 'of the heavens' or 'divine'. It signifies the connection between the earthly and the spiritual realms.
  • naraḥ: This word means 'man' or 'human'. It emphasizes the human experience and the journey of life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of unity, intention, and the pursuit of spiritual growth through rituals and offerings. It highlights the importance of community, love, and the consequences of our actions. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in teamwork, family relationships, and spiritual practices. By understanding and embodying the principles expressed in this mantra, individuals can foster a sense of connection with others and the divine, leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
