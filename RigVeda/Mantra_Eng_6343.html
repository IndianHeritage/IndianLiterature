वृषा ग्रावा वृषा मदो वृषा सोमो अयं सुतः |वृषा यज्ञो यम् इन्बसि वृषा हवः ||
vṛṣā grāvā vṛṣā mado vṛṣā somo ayaṁ sutaḥ | vṛṣā yajño yam invasi vṛṣā havaḥ ||
  • vṛṣā: This word can be translated to mean "strong" or "powerful." It signifies strength and vigor, often associated with the divine or celestial forces.
  • grāvā: This term refers to "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is called upon." It indicates a connection to the divine or the act of worship.
  • mado: This word means "intoxication" or "ecstasy." It can refer to the blissful state achieved through spiritual practices or the joy derived from divine communion.
  • somo: This refers to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes the essence of life and spiritual awakening.
  • ayaṁ: This means "this" or "here," indicating presence and immediacy in the context of the mantra.
  • sutaḥ: This translates to "son" or "offspring," often used in a spiritual context to denote the results of rituals or the blessings received from the divine.
  • yajño: This word means "sacrifice" or "offering." It represents the act of giving and the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • yam: This means "whom" or "that which," referring to the object of invocation or worship.
  • invasi: This term means "to invoke" or "to call upon," emphasizing the act of reaching out to the divine.
  • havaḥ: This translates to "offering" or "sacrifice," reinforcing the theme of devotion and the importance of rituals in spiritual practice.

The mantra can be understood as a powerful invocation that calls upon the divine forces for strength, joy, and spiritual awakening. It emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine and receiving blessings. The mention of "Soma" highlights the significance of spiritual nourishment and the ecstatic experience that comes from divine communion.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can use this mantra during meditation or prayer to invoke strength and divine blessings, enhancing their spiritual journey.
  • Rituals and Offerings: In ceremonies, this mantra can be recited to emphasize the importance of offerings and the connection to the divine, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
  • Personal Growth: The themes of strength and ecstasy can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with vigor and joy, reminding them of the importance of spiritual nourishment in their lives.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with divine forces, emphasizing the importance of strength, joy, and spiritual awakening through rituals and offerings. It serves as a reminder of the power of devotion and the transformative effects of spiritual practices in everyday life.
