विष्पतिं यह्वम अतिथिं नरः सदा यन्तारं धीनाṁ उषिजं च वाघताम् |अध्वराणां चेतनं जातवेदसम् प्र शंसन्ति नमसā जूतिभिर वृधे ||
viśpatiṁ yahvam atithiṁ naraḥ sadā yantāraṁ dhīnām uśijaṁ ca vāghatām | adhvarāṇāṁ cetanaṁ jātavedasam pra śaṁsanti namasā jūtibhir vṛdhe ||
  • viśpatiṁ: This term refers to the "lord of all beings" or "universal lord." It signifies a higher power or divine presence that governs the universe.
  • yahvam: This word can be interpreted as "who" or "that which," indicating a connection to the divine or the universal lord mentioned earlier.
  • atithiṁ: This means "guest" or "visitor." In many cultures, guests are treated with great respect and honor, symbolizing hospitality and the importance of community.
  • naraḥ: This translates to "man" or "human." It emphasizes the human experience and the relationship between humans and the divine.
  • sadā: Meaning "always" or "forever," this word suggests a continuous state of being or action.
  • yantāraṁ: This term refers to "the one who moves" or "the one who guides." It can symbolize the guiding force in life, akin to a mentor or a spiritual guide.
  • dhīnām: This means "of the wise" or "of the learned," indicating a connection to knowledge and wisdom.
  • uśijaṁ: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is pure" or "the one who is virtuous," highlighting the importance of purity in thought and action.
  • ca: This simply means "and," connecting the various elements of the mantra.
  • vāghatām: This term refers to "the one who speaks" or "the one who communicates," emphasizing the power of words and communication in human relationships.
  • adhvarāṇāṁ: This means "of the rituals" or "of the sacrifices," indicating the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • cetanaṁ: This translates to "consciousness" or "awareness," highlighting the significance of being aware and conscious in our actions and thoughts.
  • jātavedasam: This term refers to "the one who is born of the fire" or "the divine fire," symbolizing transformation and the divine spark within us.
  • pra: This means "forth" or "out," indicating a movement towards something greater.
  • śaṁsanti: This translates to "they praise" or "they commend," emphasizing the act of honoring and recognizing the divine.
  • namasā: This means "with reverence" or "with respect," indicating the importance of humility in our approach to the divine.
  • jūtibhir: This term refers to "with offerings" or "with gifts," highlighting the significance of giving and sharing in our spiritual practices.
  • vṛdhe: This means "to increase" or "to grow," suggesting the idea of growth and expansion in our spiritual journey.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of humanity, the divine, and the importance of hospitality, wisdom, and communication. It teaches us to honor the divine presence in our lives and to approach it with reverence and humility. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in our daily interactions with others, promoting kindness, respect, and a sense of community. By recognizing the divine in ourselves and others, we can foster a more harmonious and compassionate world.
