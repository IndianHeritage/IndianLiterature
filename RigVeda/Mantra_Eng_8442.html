द्रापिं वसानो यजतो दिविष्पृशम् अन्तरिक्षप्रा भुवनेष्व अर्पितः |स्वर जज्ञानो नभसाभ्य अक्रमीत प्रत्नम् अस्य पितरम् आ विवासति ||
drāpiṁ vasāno yajato divispṛśam antarikṣaprā bhuvaneṣv arpitaḥ | svar jajñāno nabhasābhy akramīt pratnam asya pitaram ā vivāsati ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "The one who is adorned with the essence of the earth, who performs sacrifices, reaches the heavens, and is established in the space between the worlds. The one who is born from the sky does not transgress the boundaries of this world, and thus, the ancestors are invoked."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • drāpiṁ: Refers to the essence or the core of something, often associated with the earth or nature.
    • vasāno: One who is adorned or dressed, indicating a state of being or existence.
    • yajato: Pertaining to the act of sacrifice or worship, a central theme in Vedic rituals.
    • divispṛśam: Touching the heavens, symbolizing a connection to the divine or higher realms.
    • antarikṣaprā: The one who exists in the space between the worlds, representing the balance between the earthly and the celestial.
    • bhuvaneṣv: In the various realms or worlds, indicating the vastness of existence.
    • arpitaḥ: Offered or dedicated, often in the context of offerings made during rituals.
    • svar: The sky or heaven, a place of higher consciousness.
    • jajñāno: Born or created, suggesting a new beginning or emergence.
    • nabhasā: From the sky or space, indicating a divine origin.
    • akramīt: Does not transgress or violate, emphasizing adherence to cosmic laws.
    • pratnam: The ancestors or forefathers, representing lineage and heritage.
    • asya: Of this, referring to the individual or the context of the mantra.
    • pitaram: The paternal or ancestral figure, invoking respect and remembrance.
    • ā vivāsati: Invoked or called upon, suggesting a plea for guidance or blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of sacrifice can be seen in the dedication of individuals who work hard for their families, often putting their needs aside for the greater good.
    • The idea of reaching for higher goals, akin to "touching the heavens," can be reflected in personal aspirations, such as pursuing education or career advancements.
    • Invoking ancestors during family gatherings or rituals, such as during festivals or memorials, highlights the importance of lineage and respect for those who came before us.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the interconnectedness of the earthly and the divine. It teaches us the importance of sacrifice, respect for our ancestors, and the pursuit of higher ideals. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to strive for personal growth while honoring their heritage and the sacrifices made by their forebears. It serves as a reminder that our actions have consequences that resonate across generations, urging us to live with purpose and integrity.
