आ ययोस त्रिंशतं तनाः सहस्राणि च दद्महे |तरत स मन्दी धावति ||
ā yayos triṁśataṁ tanā sahasrāṇi ca dadmahe | tarat sa mandī dhāvati ||
  • Mantra: "ā yayos triṁśataṁ tanā sahasrāṇi ca dadmahe | tarat sa mandī dhāvati ||"
  • Translation: "We offer our prayers to the one who has thirty bodies and thousands of forms; may he swiftly grant us the strength to overcome obstacles."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yayos: to the one who has come forth
    • triṁśataṁ: thirty
    • tanā: bodies or forms
    • sahasrāṇi: thousands
    • ca: and
    • dadmahe: we offer or we pray
    • tarat: may he grant
    • sa: he
    • mandī: strength or power
    • dhāvati: to overcome or to run swiftly
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes a divine entity that is described as having multiple forms and bodies, symbolizing the omnipresence and omnipotence of the divine.
    • The mention of "thirty bodies" can be interpreted as the various aspects of life and existence that the divine encompasses, while "thousands of forms" signifies the diversity of creation.
    • In real life, this can be related to the idea that challenges and obstacles come in many forms, and the divine presence is always there to guide and support us through them.
    • The prayer for strength to overcome obstacles reflects a common human experience; for instance, when facing difficulties in personal relationships, career challenges, or health issues, one can seek inner strength and guidance.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine assistance in times of need, reminding us that we are not alone in our struggles.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize the multifaceted nature of challenges and to approach them with the understanding that there is a higher power that can provide support.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be chanted during meditation or prayer, especially when one is feeling overwhelmed or in need of guidance, fostering a sense of connection to the divine.
