प्र वाम अशनतु सुष्टुतिर इन्द्रावरुण यां हुवे |याम ऋधाति सधस्तुतिम ||
pra vām aśnotu suṣṭutir indrāvaruṇa yāṁ huve | yām ṛdhāthe sadhastutim ||
  • pra vām aśnotu: This phrase can be translated as "may we attain" or "may we receive." It signifies a desire for blessings and positive outcomes in life.
  • suṣṭutir: This word means "well-being" or "good fortune." It emphasizes the importance of prosperity and happiness in our lives.
  • indrāvaruṇa: This refers to the divine powers of Indra and Varuna, two significant deities in Vedic literature. Indra is often associated with rain and thunderstorms, while Varuna represents the cosmic order and moral authority.
  • yāṁ huve: This translates to "whom I invoke" or "to whom I call upon." It indicates a plea or invocation to the divine for assistance and guidance.
  • yām ṛdhāthe: This means "for which we strive" or "for which we seek." It reflects the human endeavor to achieve goals and aspirations.
  • sadhastutim: This can be interpreted as "the praise of success" or "the praise of accomplishment." It highlights the importance of recognizing and celebrating achievements.

In summary, this mantra is a heartfelt invocation for blessings, well-being, and success from the divine powers of Indra and Varuna. It reflects the human desire for prosperity and the pursuit of goals, while also acknowledging the importance of divine support in achieving these aspirations.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be recited during personal or communal prayers to seek blessings for health, prosperity, and success in various endeavors.
  • In a real-life scenario, individuals may use this mantra before starting a new project, business, or significant life event, invoking divine support for a favorable outcome.
  • It serves as a reminder to appreciate and celebrate achievements, fostering a sense of gratitude and positivity in daily life.
