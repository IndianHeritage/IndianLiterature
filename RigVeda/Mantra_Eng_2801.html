वārt्रहत्या य शवसे पृथनाशाह्याय च |इन्द्र त्वा वर्तयामसि ||
vārtrahatyāya śavase pṛtanāṣāhyāya ca | indra tvā vartayāmasi ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vārtrahatyāya: Refers to the act of killing or overcoming an enemy or obstacle.
    • śavase: Means 'to the dead' or 'to the deceased', often implying a connection to the afterlife or the spirit.
    • pṛtanāṣāhyāya: Indicates the destruction of barriers or limitations, suggesting a removal of hindrances.
    • indra: A prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, power, and leadership.
    • tvā: Means 'you', addressing Indra directly.
    • vartayāmasi: Translates to 'we are invoking' or 'we are calling upon', indicating a plea or request for assistance.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to Indra, seeking his strength and intervention in overcoming obstacles and challenges.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine support in facing adversities, whether they are physical, emotional, or spiritual.
    • The reference to 'the dead' suggests a connection to the past, possibly invoking the wisdom of ancestors or the lessons learned from previous struggles.
    • The phrase about destroying barriers highlights the need for personal growth and the removal of limitations that hinder progress.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual may face challenges such as competition or workplace politics. This mantra can serve as a reminder to seek guidance and strength from mentors or spiritual beliefs to navigate these obstacles.
    • In personal relationships, one might encounter misunderstandings or conflicts. The invocation of Indra can symbolize the need for open communication and the courage to address issues head-on.
    • In times of grief or loss, the reference to 'the dead' can encourage individuals to reflect on the teachings and memories of loved ones, using that wisdom to overcome current challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine assistance in overcoming life's challenges and barriers.
    • It serves as a reminder that strength can be found in both the divine and the wisdom of those who have come before us.
    • In real-life scenarios, invoking this mantra can inspire individuals to confront their fears, seek support, and strive for personal growth, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life.
