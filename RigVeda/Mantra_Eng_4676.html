श्रिये ते पादादु वामिमिक्षुर धृष्णुर वज्री शवसादक्षिणावान् |वासानो अटकं सुरभिं दृष्टे कं स्वर्ण नृतव इषिरो बभूथा ||
śriye te pādā duva ā mimikṣur dhṛṣṇur vajrī śavasā dakṣiṇāvān | vasāno atkaṁ surabhiṁ dṛśe kaṁ sva1r ṇa nṛtav iṣiro babhūtha ||
  • śriye te pādā: This phrase can be translated as "Your feet are for prosperity." Here, "śriye" refers to prosperity or wealth, and "pādā" means feet. In a real-life context, this can symbolize the foundation of success and abundance in one's life, suggesting that one should walk the path of righteousness and virtue to attain prosperity.
  • duva ā mimikṣur: This can be interpreted as "may we be blessed with abundance." The word "duva" signifies abundance or plenty, while "mimikṣur" implies a desire for blessings. In practical terms, this can relate to the aspiration for a fulfilling life, where one seeks not just material wealth but also emotional and spiritual richness.
  • dhṛṣṇur vajrī: This phrase translates to "the one who is strong and powerful." "Dhṛṣṇur" refers to strength or courage, and "vajrī" means one who possesses the qualities of a thunderbolt, symbolizing invincibility. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who face challenges with resilience and determination, embodying the spirit of overcoming obstacles.
  • śavasā dakṣiṇāvān: This can be understood as "the one who is skilled and adept." "Śavasā" refers to skill or expertise, while "dakṣiṇāvān" implies being proficient or capable. This highlights the importance of honing one's skills and talents in various fields, whether in professional careers or personal endeavors, to achieve success.
  • vasāno atkaṁ surabhiṁ: This phrase means "may we dwell in the essence of fragrance." "Vasāno" refers to dwelling or residing, "atkaṁ" signifies essence, and "surabhiṁ" means fragrance or sweetness. This can be related to creating a positive environment around oneself, akin to cultivating relationships and experiences that are uplifting and enriching.
  • dṛśe kaṁ sva1r ṇa nṛtav: This translates to "may we see the beauty of life." "Dṛśe" means to see, "kaṁ" refers to beauty, and "sva1r ṇa nṛtav" implies the dance of life. This encourages individuals to appreciate the beauty in their surroundings and the joy of living, reminding us to find happiness in the little things.
  • iṣiro babhūtha: This can be interpreted as "may we be blessed with wisdom." "Iṣiro" refers to wisdom or knowledge, and "babhūtha" means to be blessed or to become. This emphasizes the significance of seeking knowledge and understanding in our lives, which can guide us in making informed decisions and leading a fulfilling life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the importance of prosperity, strength, skill, positivity, beauty, and wisdom. Its significance lies in the reminder that true success is not merely about material wealth but encompasses emotional and spiritual well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various aspects, such as personal development, professional growth, and fostering positive relationships. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can strive for a balanced and fulfilling life, ultimately leading to a harmonious existence.
