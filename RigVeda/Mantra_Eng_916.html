इन्द्रं इद धरी वहतोऽप्रतिधृष्टशवसम् |ऋषीणां च स्तुतीर उप यज्ञं च मानवाणाम् ||
indram id dharī vahato 'pratidhṛṣṭaśavasam | ṛṣīṇāṁ ca stutīr upa yajñaṁ ca mānuṣāṇām ||
  • Indram: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
  • id: This word can be translated as "this" or "here," indicating a focus on the present moment or context.
  • dharī: Means "to hold" or "to bear," suggesting the act of supporting or sustaining something.
  • vahato: Translates to "of those who carry" or "of those who bear," referring to individuals or entities that take on responsibilities.
  • 'pratidhṛṣṭaśavasam: This compound word can be broken down into "pratidhṛṣṭa" (to be steadfast or resolute) and "śavasam" (related to vitality or life force), indicating a state of being strong and full of life.
  • ṛṣīṇāṁ: Refers to the sages or seers, who are revered for their wisdom and insight.
  • ca: Means "and," connecting different elements of the mantra.
  • stutīr: Translates to "praise" or "worship," indicating the act of honoring or acknowledging the divine.
  • upa: Means "near" or "towards," suggesting a movement towards something sacred or important.
  • yajñaṁ: Refers to a ritual or sacrifice, often performed to invoke blessings or favor from the divine.
  • ca: Again, means "and," linking the concepts together.
  • mānuṣāṇām: Translates to "of humans," indicating the focus on human beings and their actions.

The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, leadership, and the role of humans in performing rituals and praises. It highlights the connection between divine forces and human actions, suggesting that through steadfastness and vitality, individuals can invoke blessings and support from the divine, particularly from Indra.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra represents strength and leadership, individuals in positions of authority can draw inspiration from this mantra to lead with courage and responsibility.
  • Community Support: The idea of bearing responsibilities resonates in community service, where individuals come together to support one another, reflecting the essence of "vahato."
  • Rituals and Celebrations: The mention of yajña highlights the significance of rituals in human life, reminding us to honor traditions and seek blessings through collective efforts.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of divine and human actions. It encourages individuals to embody strength, engage in community support, and participate in rituals that honor the divine, ultimately leading to a harmonious and prosperous life.
