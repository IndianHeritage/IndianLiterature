पूर्वीष ṭा इन्द्रोपमातयः पूर्वीर उता प्रशस्तयः सूनो हिन्वस्य हरिवः |वास्वो वीरस्यापृचो या नु साधन्त नो धियो नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
pūrvīṣ ṭa indropamātayaḥ pūrvīr uta praśastayaḥ sūno hinvasya harivaḥ | vasvo vīrasyāpṛco yā nu sādhanta no dhiyo nabhantām anyake same ||
  • pūrvīṣ ṭa indropamātayaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the ancient powers or deities, particularly Indra, who is often associated with strength and leadership. In real life, this can be seen as a reminder to seek guidance from those who have wisdom and experience, much like consulting a mentor or a leader in a community.
  • pūrvīr uta praśastayaḥ: This translates to the praise of the ancestors or those who have come before us. It emphasizes the importance of honoring our heritage and learning from the past. For example, in many cultures, family traditions and stories are passed down through generations, shaping our identities and values.
  • sūno hinvasya harivaḥ: This part refers to the offspring or descendants, highlighting the continuity of life and the responsibilities we have towards future generations. In a practical sense, this can relate to how we raise our children and the values we instill in them, ensuring they are prepared to face the world.
  • vasvo vīrasyāpṛco: This phrase can be understood as a call to the brave and noble, suggesting that courage and valor are essential qualities. In everyday life, this can be seen in acts of bravery, whether in personal challenges or in standing up for what is right in society.
  • yā nu sādhanta no dhiyo: This can be interpreted as a plea for wisdom and understanding. It emphasizes the need for clarity of thought and purpose. In real life, this is applicable in decision-making processes, where seeking knowledge and insight leads to better outcomes.
  • nabhantām anyake same: This final part suggests a call for unity and harmony among different beings or entities. It reflects the importance of collaboration and coexistence in our diverse world. For instance, in community projects, working together towards a common goal can lead to significant positive changes.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of reverence for the past, the importance of wisdom, courage, and the need for unity. Its significance lies in its applicability to our daily lives, reminding us to honor our roots, seek guidance, and work together for a better future. By embodying these principles, we can foster a more harmonious and enlightened society.
