अध यद राजानां गविष्टौ सरत सरण्युः कारवे जरण्युः |विप्रः प्रेष्ठः स ह्य एषां बभूव परा च वक्षद उता पर्षद एनान ||
adha yad rājānā gaviṣṭau sarat saraṇyuḥ kārave jaraṇyuḥ | vipraḥ preṣṭhaḥ sa hy eṣām babhūva parā ca vakṣad uta parṣad enān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • adha: below or beneath
    • yad: that which
    • rājānā: of the kings or rulers
    • gaviṣṭau: in the search or pursuit
    • sarat: flowing or moving
    • saraṇyuḥ: in the streams or rivers
    • kārave: in the act of doing or creating
    • jaraṇyuḥ: in the process of aging or decay
    • vipraḥ: the wise or learned
    • preṣṭhaḥ: the most esteemed or beloved
    • sa: he or that
    • hy: indeed
    • eṣām: of these
    • babhūva: became or was
    • parā: supreme or beyond
    • ca: and
    • vakṣad: in the heart or mind
    • uta: or
    • parṣad: assembly or gathering
    • enān: these
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, particularly in the context of leadership and governance.
    • The phrase "adha yad rājānā gaviṣṭau" suggests that even the kings, in their quest for understanding, must look beneath the surface of things.
    • The mention of "vipraḥ preṣṭhaḥ" indicates that the wise individuals are held in high regard, and their insights are crucial for the well-being of society.
    • The idea of "babhūva parā ca vakṣad" implies that true wisdom resides in the heart and mind, guiding actions and decisions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern governance, leaders often consult experts and advisors (the "vipraḥ") to make informed decisions that benefit the community.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek deeper understanding in relationships, recognizing that true connection requires looking beyond superficial appearances.
    • In education, students are encouraged to think critically and explore subjects deeply, embodying the pursuit of knowledge emphasized in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of wisdom and understanding in leadership and personal growth.
    • It serves as a reminder that true insight often requires looking beyond the obvious and valuing the contributions of knowledgeable individuals.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to better decision-making, stronger relationships, and a more informed society.
