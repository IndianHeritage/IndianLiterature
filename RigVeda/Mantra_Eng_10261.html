ईजानम् इद द्यौर गूर्तावसुर ईजानम् भूमिर अभि प्रभूषणि |ईजानं देवाव अश्विनाव अभि सुम्नैर अवर्धताम् ||
ījānam id dyaur gūrtāvasur ījānam bhūmir abhi prabhūṣaṇi | ījānaṁ devāv aśvināv abhi sumnair avardhatām ||
  • ījānam: This term refers to the act of worship or the offerings made to the divine. In real life, this can be likened to the rituals and ceremonies performed in various cultures to honor deities or spiritual entities.
  • id: This word means "this" or "here," indicating a focus on the present moment or the immediate surroundings. It emphasizes the importance of being aware of one's current environment.
  • dyaur: Translated as "heaven" or "sky," it represents the celestial realm. In everyday life, this can symbolize aspirations, dreams, and the pursuit of higher goals.
  • gūrtāvasur: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is worthy of praise." It highlights the significance of recognizing and honoring those who contribute positively to society, such as leaders, mentors, or even family members.
  • bhūmir: Meaning "earth," it signifies the physical world and our connection to it. This can relate to environmental consciousness and the importance of caring for our planet.
  • abhi prabhūṣaṇi: This phrase translates to "may it be well-ordered" or "may it be auspicious." It reflects the desire for harmony and balance in life, akin to striving for peace and stability in personal and communal relationships.
  • devāv: This word means "gods" or "divine beings," representing the higher powers that guide and protect us. In real life, this can be seen in the way people seek guidance from spiritual leaders or through personal faith.
  • aśvināv: Referring to the twin deities associated with health and medicine, it symbolizes the importance of well-being and healing in our lives. This can be related to the modern emphasis on health care and wellness practices.
  • abhi sumnair: This phrase means "with good fortune" or "with auspiciousness." It emphasizes the importance of positive outcomes and blessings in our endeavors.
  • avardhatām: This term translates to "may it increase" or "may it prosper," indicating a desire for growth and abundance in various aspects of life, such as personal development, relationships, and community welfare.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic view of life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of the divine, the earth, and human endeavors. It encourages individuals to engage in worship and rituals that foster harmony, well-being, and prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied through community service, environmental stewardship, and personal growth initiatives. By recognizing the divine in our daily lives and striving for balance and positivity, we can create a more harmonious existence for ourselves and those around us.
