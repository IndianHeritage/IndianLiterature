राज्ञो नु ते वरुणस्य व्रतानि बृहद् गभीरं तव सोम धाम | शुचिष् त्वमसि प्रियो न मित्रो दक्षाय्यो आर्यमेवासी सोम ||
rājño nu te varuṇasya vratāni bṛhad gabhīraṁ tava soma dhāma | śuciṣ ṭvam asi priyo na mitro dakṣāyyo aryamevāsi soma ||
  • rājño nu te varuṇasya vratāni: This phrase translates to "O Varuna, the king, your vows are great and profound." Here, Varuna is the Vedic deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority. The term "vows" refers to the principles and laws that govern the universe.
  • bṛhad gabhīraṁ tava soma dhāma: This means "Your abode of Soma is vast and deep." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. The "vast and deep" aspect signifies the profound nature of divine wisdom and the richness of spiritual experiences.
  • śuciṣ ṭvam asi priyo: This translates to "You are pure and dear." Purity here refers to the moral and ethical clarity that Varuna embodies. In real life, this can be related to the importance of integrity and honesty in our actions and relationships.
  • na mitro dakṣāyyo: This means "You are not a friend to the unskilled." This highlights the idea that true friendship and support come to those who are capable and deserving. In practical terms, it suggests that we should strive for competence and skill in our endeavors to earn respect and support from others.
  • aryamevāsi soma: This translates to "You are indeed Aryama, O Soma." Aryama is another deity associated with the afterlife and moral order. This line emphasizes the connection between Soma and the divine order, suggesting that spiritual practices lead to a higher understanding of life and death.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine order, purity, and the importance of moral integrity. In real life, it serves as a reminder to uphold ethical values, seek wisdom, and strive for personal growth. By embodying these principles, individuals can foster deeper connections with themselves and others, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence. The teachings of this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, professional conduct, and spiritual practices, guiding individuals towards a path of righteousness and fulfillment.
