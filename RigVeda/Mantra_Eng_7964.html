शुम्भमाना ऋतायुभिर मृज्यमानो गभस्त्योः |पवते वारे अव्यये ||
śumbhamāna ṛtāyubhir mṛjyamāno gabhastyoḥ | pavate vāre avyaye ||
  • śumbhamāna: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is praised" or "the one who is glorified." In real life, this can refer to individuals or entities that are recognized for their achievements or virtues, such as leaders, teachers, or even cultural icons.
  • ṛtāyubhir: This word relates to "truth" or "righteousness." It emphasizes the importance of living in accordance with moral and ethical principles. For example, a person who practices honesty in their business dealings embodies this concept.
  • mṛjyamāno: This term means "being purified" or "cleansed." In a practical sense, it can refer to the process of self-improvement or personal growth, such as someone who seeks to overcome their flaws and become a better version of themselves.
  • gabhastyoḥ: This word translates to "of the luminous" or "of the radiant." It can symbolize knowledge, enlightenment, or wisdom. For instance, a scholar or a mentor who illuminates the path for others through their knowledge can be seen as embodying this quality.
  • pavate: This term means "to blow" or "to move." It can be associated with the forces of nature, such as the wind, which can be a metaphor for change or transformation in life. For example, the wind can represent new opportunities that come our way.
  • vāre: This word means "in the atmosphere" or "in the space." It signifies the environment or context in which actions take place. In real life, this can refer to the social or cultural environment that influences our decisions and behaviors.
  • avyaye: This term means "imperishable" or "eternal." It suggests the idea of something that transcends time and remains constant. For example, values like love, compassion, and integrity are often considered timeless and enduring qualities.

Conclusion: The mantra "śumbhamāna ṛtāyubhir mṛjyamāno gabhastyoḥ | pavate vāre avyaye ||" encapsulates the essence of living a life of righteousness, purity, and enlightenment. It encourages individuals to strive for personal growth while being aware of their environment and the forces of change around them. In real life, this mantra can serve as a guiding principle for those seeking to lead a meaningful and impactful life, reminding us of the importance of integrity, wisdom, and the pursuit of truth in our daily actions and interactions.
