आ जामिर अतके अव्यत भुजे न पुत्र ओण्योः |सरज जारो न योषणाṁ वर न योनिम आसदं ||
ā jāmir atke avyata bhuje na putra oṇyoḥ | saraj jāro na yoṣaṇāṁ varo na yonim āsadam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • jāmir: birth
    • atke: not hindered
    • avyata: unrestrained
    • bhuje: in the body
    • na: not
    • putra: son
    • oṇyoḥ: of others
    • saraj: flowing
    • jāro: the one who is born
    • na: not
    • yoṣaṇāṁ: of women
    • varo: the best
    • na: not
    • yonim: womb
    • āsadam: seated
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nature of existence and the essence of life.
    • It suggests that one should not be hindered by the circumstances of birth or the limitations imposed by others.
    • The phrase "not hindered" emphasizes the importance of freedom and the ability to pursue one's own path.
    • It also reflects on the idea that the essence of life is not confined to familial ties or societal expectations.
    • The mention of "the best of women" and "the womb" indicates a respect for the feminine aspect of creation, acknowledging the role of women in the cycle of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, individuals often face pressure to conform to family expectations regarding career choices or lifestyle. This mantra encourages breaking free from such constraints.
    • Women in various cultures have fought against societal norms that limit their roles. This mantra can serve as a reminder of their strength and the importance of their contributions.
    • In personal development, the idea of not being hindered by one's past or by others' opinions can inspire individuals to pursue their dreams and aspirations without fear.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its universal message of empowerment and self-determination.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize their own potential and to rise above societal limitations.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal growth, gender equality movements, and the pursuit of one's passions, reminding us that our worth is not defined by our origins or the expectations of others.
