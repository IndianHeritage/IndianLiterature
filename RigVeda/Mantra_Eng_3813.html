अग्निर्देवेषु राजत्य अग्निर्मर्तेष्वाविशन् |अग्निर्नो हव्यवाहनोऽग्निं धीभिः सपर्त्यत ||
agnir deveṣu rājaty agnir marteṣv āviśan | agnir no havyavāhano 'gniṁ dhībhiḥ saparyata ||
  • Agnir: The term "Agnir" refers to fire, which is not just a physical element but also symbolizes energy, transformation, and purity in Vedic literature.
  • Deveṣu: This word means "among the gods," indicating the divine aspect of fire and its connection to the celestial realm.
  • Rājaty: Translated as "rules" or "governs," it suggests that fire has a commanding presence and authority in both the material and spiritual worlds.
  • Marteṣv: This refers to "among mortals," highlighting the relationship between fire and human beings, emphasizing its importance in daily life.
  • Āviśan: Meaning "enters" or "pervades," it indicates that fire is an omnipresent force that influences various aspects of existence.
  • No: This translates to "our," indicating a personal connection and invocation to fire.
  • Havyavāhano: This term means "the one who carries offerings," referring to fire's role in rituals and sacrifices, where it acts as a mediator between humans and the divine.
  • Agniṁ: A repetition of "fire," reinforcing its significance in the mantra.
  • Dhībhiḥ: This word means "with wisdom" or "intellect," suggesting that the worship of fire should be accompanied by understanding and reverence.
  • Saparyata: Meaning "be worshipped" or "be honored," it emphasizes the need to respect and venerate fire as a sacred element.

In summary, this mantra highlights the multifaceted nature of fire as a divine entity that governs both the celestial and earthly realms. It emphasizes the importance of fire in rituals, its role as a mediator between humans and the divine, and the necessity of approaching it with wisdom and respect. In real life, this can be seen in various cultural practices where fire is central, such as in Hindu rituals (yajnas) where offerings are made to Agni, symbolizing the connection between the material and spiritual worlds. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the sacredness of fire and its vital role in our lives, urging us to honor and understand the forces that govern our existence.
