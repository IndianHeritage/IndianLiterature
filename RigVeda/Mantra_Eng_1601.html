स यो वृषा नरां न रोदस्योः श्रवोभिर अस्ति जीवपीतसर्गः |प्र यः सस्राणः शिश्रित योनौ ||
sa yo vṛṣā narāṁ na rodasyoḥ śravobhir asti jīvapītasargaḥ | pra yaḥ sasrāṇaḥ śiśrīta yonau ||
  • sa yo vṛṣā: This phrase can be translated as "he who is powerful" or "the one who is strong." In real life, this can refer to individuals who exhibit strength in various forms, such as physical strength, mental resilience, or moral courage. For example, a leader who stands firm in the face of adversity embodies this strength.
  • narāṁ: This word means "of men" or "of humans." It emphasizes the human aspect of the mantra, suggesting that the qualities being discussed are relevant to humanity as a whole. In a practical sense, this can relate to the collective strength and potential of communities working together for a common goal.
  • na rodasyoḥ: This translates to "not of the obstructed" or "not of the hindered." It implies a sense of freedom and the ability to overcome obstacles. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who pursue their dreams despite facing challenges, such as an entrepreneur who perseveres through financial difficulties to establish a successful business.
  • śravobhir asti: This means "there is fame" or "there is renown." It suggests that the qualities of strength and resilience lead to recognition and respect. For instance, a scientist whose research leads to significant breakthroughs may gain fame and acknowledgment in their field, inspiring others to pursue knowledge and innovation.
  • jīvapītasargaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the source of life" or "the essence of existence." It highlights the importance of vitality and the fundamental aspects of life. In real life, this can relate to the pursuit of health and well-being, as individuals strive to maintain a balanced lifestyle that nurtures both body and mind.
  • pra yaḥ: This translates to "the one who is wise" or "the one who knows." It emphasizes the value of wisdom and knowledge in navigating life's challenges. For example, a mentor who guides young professionals in their careers embodies this wisdom, helping them make informed decisions.
  • sasrāṇaḥ: This means "who has spread" or "who has expanded." It suggests growth and the sharing of knowledge or resources. In a practical context, this can refer to community initiatives that aim to spread awareness about social issues, such as environmental conservation efforts that educate the public on sustainability.
  • śiśrīta yonau: This phrase can be interpreted as "in the womb of creation" or "in the source of birth." It signifies the potential for new beginnings and the nurturing aspect of life. In real life, this can relate to the importance of education and nurturing young minds, as they represent the future and the potential for positive change in society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, resilience, wisdom, and the nurturing of life. It emphasizes the importance of overcoming obstacles and striving for recognition through virtuous actions. In real-life scenarios, these qualities are applicable in various contexts, such as leadership, community building, personal growth, and education. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and progressive society, fostering an environment where strength and wisdom lead to collective success and well-being.
