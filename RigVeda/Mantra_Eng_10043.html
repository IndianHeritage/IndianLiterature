भोजायाश्वं सम मृjanty āśum भोजायास्ते कन्याः शुम्भमानाः |भोजस्येदं पुष्करिणीव वेष्म परिस्कृतं देवमानेव चित्रम् ||
bhojāyāśvaṁ sam mṛjanty āśum bhojāyāste kanyā3 śumbhamānā | bhojasyedam puṣkariṇīva veśma pariṣkṛtaṁ devamāneva citram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bhojāyāśvaṁ: The horses that are meant for enjoyment or sustenance.
    • sam mṛjanty: They are being well-groomed or taken care of.
    • āśum: Quickly or with speed.
    • bhojāyāste: Those who are meant for enjoyment.
    • kanyā: The maiden or daughter.
    • śumbhamānā: Being adorned or decorated.
    • bhojasyedam: This is for the enjoyment.
    • puṣkariṇīva: Like a beautiful pond or lake.
    • veśma: The abode or home.
    • pariṣkṛtaṁ: Well-prepared or well-decorated.
    • devamāneva: Like a divine being or deity.
    • citram: Colorful or splendid.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the care and grooming of horses, which can be seen as a metaphor for nurturing and preparing something valuable in life.
    • The mention of a maiden being adorned suggests the importance of beauty and preparation in life, akin to how we prepare for significant events or milestones.
    • The comparison of a well-prepared home to a beautiful pond emphasizes the significance of creating a welcoming and nurturing environment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, grooming and preparing for a presentation can be likened to the care given to the horses. Just as horses need to be well-groomed to perform well, individuals must prepare thoroughly to succeed.
    • In personal life, preparing for a wedding or a significant celebration involves adorning oneself and creating a beautiful environment, similar to the maiden being decorated.
    • In community settings, creating a welcoming home for guests reflects the idea of a well-prepared abode, akin to the beautiful pond that attracts and nurtures life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of preparation, care, and beauty in various aspects of life.
    • It teaches us that just as horses need grooming and a home needs decoration, our endeavors and relationships require nurturing and attention to flourish.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in personal development, community building, and professional growth, reminding us to invest time and effort into what we value.
