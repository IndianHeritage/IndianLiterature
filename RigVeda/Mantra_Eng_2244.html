अश्मास्यं अवतं ब्रह्मणस् पतिर् मधुधारम् अभि यम् ओजसातृणत् |तम एव विश्वे पपिरे स्वर्दृशो बहु साकं सिसिचुर् उत्सम् उद्रिणम् ||
aśmāsyam avatam brahmaṇas patir madhudhāram abhi yam ojasātṛṇat | tam eva viśve papire svardṛśo bahu sākaṁ sisicur utsam udriṇam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśmāsyam: Of stones or rocks, symbolizing strength and stability.
    • avatam: To protect or to shelter.
    • brahmaṇas: Of the divine or the ultimate reality.
    • patir: Lord or master.
    • madhudhāram: One who holds or carries sweetness, often referring to nourishment or sustenance.
    • abhi: Towards or in the direction of.
    • yam: Whom or that which.
    • ojasātṛṇat: With strength or energy.
    • tam: That or him.
    • eva: Indeed or certainly.
    • viśve: All or universal.
    • papire: They have taken or accepted.
    • svardṛśo: Of the radiant or luminous.
    • bahu: Many or abundant.
    • sākaṁ: Together or in unison.
    • sisicur: They have nourished or fed.
    • utsam: The best or the highest.
    • udriṇam: Of the great or the powerful.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine protection and nourishment provided by the ultimate reality, often personified as a lord or master.
    • The imagery of stones suggests a foundation of strength, indicating that true protection comes from a solid base of spiritual or moral values.
    • The reference to sweetness (madhudhāram) implies that the sustenance provided is not just physical but also emotional and spiritual, enriching our lives.
    • The collective aspect (sākaṁ) emphasizes the importance of community and unity in receiving and sharing this divine nourishment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of crisis, individuals often seek strength from their community, much like the mantra suggests that protection and nourishment come from a united front.
    • In personal development, one may find that grounding oneself in strong values (the stones) provides the necessary support to navigate life's challenges.
    • Acts of kindness and support within a community can be seen as manifestations of the sweetness mentioned, where individuals uplift each other.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine protection and nourishment in our lives, emphasizing the importance of community and strong values.
    • It serves as a reminder that true strength comes from unity and shared support, which is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to community efforts.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can foster a nurturing environment that promotes growth, resilience, and harmony.
