एवां ह्यस्य काम्या स्तोमा उक्तं च शंस्या |इन्द्राय सोमपीतये ||
evā hy asya kāmyā stoma ukthaṁ ca śaṁsyā | indrāya somapītaye ||
  • evā hy asya: This phrase translates to "Indeed, this is" or "This is certainly." It emphasizes the importance and certainty of what follows.
  • kāmyā stoma: "Kāmyā" means "desired" or "wishful," and "stoma" refers to a hymn or praise. Together, it signifies a hymn that is aimed at fulfilling desires.
  • ukthaṁ ca: "Ukthaṁ" means "utterance" or "saying," and "ca" means "and." This indicates that the hymn is not just a mere chant but carries significant meaning and intention.
  • śaṁsyā: This word means "to be praised" or "worthy of praise." It suggests that the hymn is directed towards a deity or a higher power that deserves reverence.
  • indrāya: This refers to "Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, associated with rain, thunder, and strength. It indicates that the hymn is dedicated to Indra.
  • somapītaye: "Somapītaye" translates to "for the drinker of Soma." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. This phrase indicates that the hymn is meant for Indra, who is often associated with the consumption of Soma.

In summary, the mantra "evā hy asya kāmyā stoma ukthaṁ ca śaṁsyā | indrāya somapītaye ||" is a powerful invocation that emphasizes the significance of a desired hymn dedicated to Indra, the god of strength and rain, who is worthy of praise and associated with the sacred drink Soma. This mantra highlights the importance of intention in spiritual practices, where the utterance of hymns is not just a ritualistic act but a means to connect with divine energies and fulfill one's desires.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, particularly in the context of seeking strength and support during challenging times. For instance, when facing difficulties in personal or professional life, one can recite this mantra to invoke the strength of Indra and seek guidance.
  • In a broader sense, it serves as a reminder of the power of intention in our actions. Just as the hymn is aimed at fulfilling desires, individuals can focus their intentions on their goals, whether in career, relationships, or personal growth.
  • The concept of "Soma" can also be related to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment. In modern life, this can translate to the importance of seeking wisdom and inspiration through education, meditation, or spiritual practices.

Ultimately, this mantra encourages individuals to recognize the divine forces at play in their lives and to actively engage with them through intention, praise, and the pursuit of their desires.
