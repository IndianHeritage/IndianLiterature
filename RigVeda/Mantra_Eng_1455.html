हन्ता पापस्य रक्षस त्राता विप्रस्य मावतः |अधाऽहि त्वा जनिता जीजनद वसो रक्षोहनं त्वा जीजनद वसो ||
hantā pāpasya rakṣasas trātā viprasya māvataḥ | adhā hi tvā janitā jījanad vaso rakṣohaṇaṁ tvā jījanad vaso ||
  • Mantra: "hantā pāpasya rakṣasas trātā viprasya māvataḥ | adhā hi tvā janitā jījanad vaso rakṣohaṇaṁ tvā jījanad vaso ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be interpreted as a plea for protection and guidance. It speaks of the destruction of evil (pāpasya) and the safeguarding of the wise (viprasya). The invocation of a protector (trātā) suggests a desire for divine intervention against malevolent forces (rakṣasas).
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Hantā: Destroyer or killer, often referring to the removal of negativity or evil.
    • Pāpasya: Of sin or evil, indicating the negative forces that one wishes to overcome.
    • Rakṣasas: Demonic beings or malevolent forces that threaten peace and harmony.
    • Trātā: Protector or savior, someone who provides safety and security.
    • Viprasya: The wise or learned, representing those who seek knowledge and truth.
    • Māvataḥ: A call for protection, invoking a higher power for assistance.
    • Jījanad: To give life or to create, suggesting the nurturing aspect of the divine.
    • Vaso: A term that can refer to dwelling or residing, indicating a sense of belonging or safety.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek protection from negative influences, whether they be emotional, psychological, or spiritual. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek help from higher powers or wise individuals in our lives.
    • In a community facing challenges such as crime or social unrest, invoking the spirit of protection and wisdom can unite people in their efforts to create a safer environment.
    • During moments of moral dilemmas, individuals may reflect on the mantra to guide their decisions, seeking to overcome evil with wisdom and righteousness.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine protection against evil and the nurturing of wisdom. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal struggles to community challenges. By invoking this mantra, individuals can find strength and guidance in their pursuit of truth and safety, reminding them that they are not alone in their battles against negativity.
