मन्त्र: सनā ज्योतिः सनā स्वार विश्वा च सोम सौभगā | अथा नो वस्यसस कृधि ||
sanā jyotiḥ sanā sva1r viśvā ca soma saubhagā | athā no vasyasas kṛdhi ||
  • Sanā Jyotiḥ: This phrase translates to "everlasting light." It signifies the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment that is eternal and unchanging. In real life, this can be related to the quest for wisdom and understanding that guides individuals throughout their lives.
  • Sanā Svar: Meaning "everlasting sound" or "eternal voice," this part emphasizes the importance of communication and expression. In practical terms, it can be seen in the way we share ideas and connect with others, highlighting the significance of dialogue in fostering relationships and community.
  • Viśvā ca Soma Saubhagā: This translates to "all beings and the auspiciousness of Soma." Soma is often associated with a divine nectar that brings joy and prosperity. This phrase suggests a universal connection among all beings and the blessings that come from harmony and well-being. In everyday life, this can be reflected in the importance of community support and the shared joy that comes from collective achievements.
  • Athā no Vasyasas Kṛdhi: This means "therefore, grant us prosperity." It is a plea for abundance and well-being. In a real-world context, this can be interpreted as a call for the resources and opportunities necessary for growth and success, whether in personal endeavors, community projects, or societal advancements.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the pursuit of eternal knowledge, the importance of communication, the interconnectedness of all beings, and the desire for prosperity. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, such as education, relationships, community building, and personal growth. By embodying the principles expressed in this mantra, individuals and societies can strive for enlightenment, harmony, and abundance, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and prosperous existence.
