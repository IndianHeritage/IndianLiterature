अरवाँचां वां सप्तयोऽध्वरश्रियो वहन्तु सवनेद उप |इषं पृñचन्ता सुखृत सुदानव आ बरहिः सीदतं नराः ||
arvāñcā vāṁ saptayo 'dhvaraśriyo vahantu savaned upa | iṣam pṛñcantā sukṛte sudānava ā barhiḥ sīdataṁ narā ||
  • arvāñcā vāṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "may the seven (saptayo) directions" or "may the seven realms" be favorable to us. In a real-life context, this can be understood as a prayer for guidance and support from all directions, symbolizing the importance of community and the interconnectedness of life.
  • saptayo 'dhvaraśriyo: Here, "saptayo" refers to the seven realms or directions, while "dhvaraśriyo" can be translated as "the auspiciousness of the rituals." This emphasizes the significance of rituals in bringing harmony and auspiciousness into our lives. For example, in modern life, rituals such as weddings, festivals, and even daily prayers can create a sense of belonging and positivity.
  • vahantu savaned upa: This phrase means "may they bring forth abundance." It reflects the desire for prosperity and well-being. In practical terms, this can relate to the pursuit of success in various aspects of life, such as career, relationships, and personal growth. For instance, a business that fosters teamwork and collaboration often sees greater success and abundance.
  • iṣam pṛñcantā: "Iṣam" refers to "desire" or "wish," while "pṛñcantā" can be interpreted as "those who are engaged in good deeds." This highlights the importance of aligning our desires with positive actions. In real life, this can be seen in community service or charitable work, where individuals strive to fulfill their desires for a better world through their actions.
  • sukṛte sudānava: "Sukṛte" means "good deeds," and "sudānava" refers to "those who are virtuous." This reinforces the idea that virtuous actions lead to positive outcomes. For example, a person who consistently acts with kindness and integrity often finds themselves surrounded by supportive relationships and opportunities.
  • ā barhiḥ sīdataṁ narā: This can be translated as "may the wise ones (narā) be blessed." It suggests a prayer for wisdom and enlightenment. In everyday life, this can be applied to the pursuit of knowledge and understanding, whether through education, mentorship, or personal reflection. For instance, seeking guidance from experienced individuals can lead to better decision-making and personal growth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound prayer for guidance, prosperity, and the pursuit of virtuous actions. Its significance lies in the interconnectedness of our desires, actions, and the outcomes we seek in life. By embracing the principles of community, ritual, and virtue, we can create a harmonious existence that benefits not only ourselves but also those around us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages us to engage in positive actions, seek wisdom, and foster a sense of abundance in all aspects of our lives.
