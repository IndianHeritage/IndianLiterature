गृहो याम्य अरंक्रतो देवभ्यो हव्यवाहनः |कुवित सोमस्यापाम इति ||
gṛho yāmy araṁkṛto devebhyo havyavāhanaḥ | kuvit somasyāpām iti ||
  • gṛho: This word translates to "house" or "home." In a broader sense, it signifies a place of shelter, comfort, and family.
  • yāmy: This term means "to go" or "to lead." It implies movement or direction, often associated with guidance.
  • araṁkṛto: This can be interpreted as "made ready" or "prepared." It suggests a state of readiness or being equipped for something.
  • devebhyo: This word means "to the gods" or "divine beings." It indicates a connection to the spiritual or divine realm.
  • havyavāhanaḥ: This term refers to "the one who carries offerings." It signifies a being or entity that transports or delivers offerings to the divine.
  • kuvit: This word can be understood as "good" or "well." It denotes positivity and favorable conditions.
  • somasyāpām: This phrase relates to "the essence of Soma," which is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
  • iti: This is a concluding particle, often used to indicate the end of a statement or mantra.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation that emphasizes the importance of a well-prepared home that is conducive to spiritual offerings and connections with the divine. It suggests that a home should be a place where one can lead a life that is aligned with divine principles and where offerings to the gods are made with sincerity and positivity.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In family life, it encourages creating a nurturing environment where family members feel safe and supported.
  • In community settings, it highlights the importance of preparing spaces for gatherings, rituals, or celebrations that honor spiritual traditions.
  • In personal development, it serves as a reminder to cultivate inner readiness and positivity before engaging in spiritual practices or community service.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for a harmonious balance between the material and spiritual aspects of life. It emphasizes the importance of preparing our homes and hearts for divine connection and the act of giving. By embodying the principles of readiness, positivity, and devotion, individuals can create a life that resonates with higher values and fosters a deeper connection with the divine.
