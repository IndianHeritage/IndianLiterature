न दुष्टुतī मर्त्यो विन्दते वसु न श्रेधन्तं रयिर नशत् |सुशक्तिर इन मगहवन तुभ्यम मावते देश्णं यत् पारेय दिवि ||
na duṣṭutī martyo vindate vasu na sredhantaṁ rayir naśat | suśaktir in maghavan tubhyam māvate deṣṇaṁ yat pārye divi ||
  • na duṣṭutī martyo vindate vasu: This phrase translates to "a mortal does not attain wealth through wickedness." It emphasizes the idea that unethical means do not lead to true prosperity. In real life, this can be seen in the context of business practices; companies that engage in dishonest practices may achieve short-term gains but often face long-term consequences, such as loss of reputation or legal issues.
  • na sredhantaṁ rayir naśat: This means "wealth does not come to those who are unworthy." It suggests that true wealth is reserved for those who are deserving and act with integrity. For example, in a community, individuals who contribute positively, such as through volunteer work or ethical business practices, often find themselves rewarded with respect and support from others.
  • suśaktir in maghavan tubhyam: This translates to "O Maghavan (a name for the deity Indra), you are the one of great power." It acknowledges the divine force that supports those who act righteously. In practical terms, this can be related to the idea of divine justice or karma, where good deeds lead to positive outcomes, often seen in stories of individuals who have overcome adversity through their virtuous actions.
  • māvate deṣṇaṁ yat pārye divi: This means "may you grant us the vision to see the truth in the heavens." It is a plea for wisdom and clarity in understanding the right path. In everyday life, this can be applied to decision-making processes, where individuals seek guidance through reflection, meditation, or consultation with wise mentors to ensure they are making choices aligned with their values.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of integrity, ethical behavior, and the pursuit of true wealth through righteous actions. It serves as a reminder that success achieved through dishonest means is fleeting and ultimately unfulfilling. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can guide individuals in their personal and professional lives, encouraging them to act with virtue and seek wisdom in their decisions. By adhering to these principles, one can cultivate a life of true prosperity and fulfillment, both materially and spiritually.
