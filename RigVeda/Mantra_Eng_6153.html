इमास त इन्द्र पृष्णयो घृतं दूहता आशिरम् |एनाम ऋतस्य पिप्युषीः ||
imās ta indra pṛśnayo ghṛtaṁ duhata āśiram | enām ṛtasya pipyuṣīḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imās: these
    • ta: you (Indra)
    • indra: O Indra, the king of gods
    • pṛśnayo: of the questions or inquiries
    • ghṛtaṁ: clarified butter, symbolizing nourishment and purity
    • duhata: from which is extracted or drawn
    • āśiram: the abode or dwelling
    • enām: this (feminine form)
    • ṛtasya: of truth or cosmic order
    • pipyuṣīḥ: the essence or nectar
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, invoking him to provide nourishment and wisdom.
    • The reference to "ghṛtaṁ" (clarified butter) symbolizes the purity and richness of knowledge and sustenance.
    • The phrase "pṛśnayo" suggests a quest for understanding and answers to life's profound questions.
    • By seeking the essence of truth ("ṛtasya pipyuṣīḥ"), the mantra emphasizes the importance of aligning oneself with cosmic order and righteousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, when faced with difficult decisions, individuals often seek guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders, akin to invoking Indra for wisdom.
    • In a community setting, rituals involving offerings of ghee (ghṛta) during festivals symbolize the collective pursuit of truth and harmony.
    • In educational settings, the quest for knowledge mirrors the inquiries made in the mantra, where students seek clarity and understanding from teachers.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and nourishment in our quest for truth and understanding.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal growth, community harmony, and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • By invoking the essence of this mantra, individuals can align themselves with higher principles and foster a deeper connection with the universe.
