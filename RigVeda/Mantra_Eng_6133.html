यो मे हिरण्यसंदृशो दश राज्ञो अमंहता |अधस्पदाऽइ च चिद्यस्य कृष्टयश्चर्मम्ना अभितो जनाः ||
yo me hiraṇyasaṁdṛśo daśa rājño amaṁhata | adhaspadā ic caidyasya kṛṣṭayaś carmamnā abhito janāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • me: for me
    • hiraṇya: golden
    • saṁdṛśo: of the one who is seen
    • daśa: ten
    • rājño: of kings
    • amaṁhata: unassailable
    • adhaspadā: from below
    • ic: and
    • caidyasya: of the one who is strong
    • kṛṣṭayaś: of the cultivated
    • carmamnā: by the skin
    • abhito: all around
    • janāḥ: people
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence that is golden and powerful, associated with kings and strength.
    • The phrase "yo me hiraṇyasaṁdṛśo" suggests a connection to a higher power that is both visible and influential in one's life.
    • The mention of "daśa rājño" indicates the authority and governance of ten kings, symbolizing a collective strength or leadership.
    • "amaṁhata" implies that this power is unassailable, suggesting that it cannot be easily challenged or defeated.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "adhaspadā ic caidyasya," emphasizes the strength that comes from the ground up, indicating that true power is rooted in solid foundations.
    • The reference to "carmamnā abhito janāḥ" suggests that the people are surrounded by this strength, indicating a community or society that is fortified by this divine influence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, a strong and wise leader can be seen as a "hiraṇyasaṁdṛśo" figure, guiding their people with authority and vision.
    • In a community, the collective strength of individuals working together can be likened to the "daśa rājño," where each person contributes to the overall stability and resilience of the group.
    • In personal development, one can draw strength from their roots and upbringing, embodying the idea of "adhaspadā ic caidyasya," where one's foundation supports their growth and success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine strength and leadership in our lives.
    • It teaches us that true power comes from a solid foundation and that we are surrounded by influences that can uplift and empower us.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal growth, community building, or leadership, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide us towards resilience and strength.
