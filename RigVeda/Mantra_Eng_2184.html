नूनं सा ते प्रति वरं जरीत्रे दूहीयद इन्द्र दक्षिणा मगहोनी |शिक्षा स्तोत्रिभ्यो माती धग भगो नो बृहद वदेम विदथे सुवीराः ||
nūnaṁ sā te prati varaṁ jaritre duhīyad indra dakṣiṇā maghonī | śikṣā stotṛbhyo māti dhag bhago no bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ ||
  • nūnaṁ: This word can be translated as "indeed" or "certainly," indicating a strong affirmation.
  • sā: This means "that" or "she," referring to a divine or spiritual presence.
  • te: This translates to "your," indicating possession or connection.
  • prati varaṁ: This phrase means "the best" or "the most excellent," suggesting a pursuit of the highest quality or virtue.
  • jaritre: This word refers to "the one who supports" or "the sustainer," often associated with a divine force or energy.
  • duhīyad: This can be interpreted as "may it be given" or "may it be bestowed," indicating a request or prayer for blessings.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • dakṣiṇā: This word means "offering" or "gift," often associated with rituals and sacrifices.
  • maghonī: This translates to "of wealth" or "of abundance," indicating a desire for prosperity.
  • śikṣā: This means "instruction" or "teaching," emphasizing the importance of knowledge and wisdom.
  • stotṛbhyo: This refers to "those who praise" or "the worshippers," highlighting the role of devotion.
  • māti: This word means "intellect" or "understanding," suggesting the need for wisdom in actions.
  • dhag: This can be interpreted as "may it shine" or "may it illuminate," indicating a desire for enlightenment.
  • bhago: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity," reinforcing the theme of abundance.
  • no: This means "our," indicating a collective aspiration.
  • bṛhad: This word means "great" or "vast," suggesting the pursuit of something significant.
  • vadema: This translates to "we may speak" or "we may declare," indicating a call to express or proclaim.
  • vidathe: This refers to "in the assembly" or "in the gathering," emphasizing community and collaboration.
  • suvīrāḥ: This means "the brave" or "the valiant," suggesting a call for courage and strength in endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a prayer for guidance, strength, and prosperity, invoking the divine presence of Indra. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom, community, and the pursuit of excellence. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during significant life events, rituals, or gatherings where collective aspirations for success and abundance are shared. It serves as a reminder to seek knowledge, support one another, and strive for greatness in all endeavors.
