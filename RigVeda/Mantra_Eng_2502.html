आ योनिम अग्निर घृतवन्तम् अस्तात पृथुप्रगाणम् उशन्तम् उशानः |दीद्यानाḥ शुचिर ऋष्वः पावकः पुनः-पुनर मातरा नव्यसी कः ||
ā yonim agnir ghṛtavantam asthāt pṛthupragāṇam uśantam uśānaḥ | dīdyānaḥ śucir ṛṣvaḥ pāvakaḥ punaḥ-punar mātarā navyasī kaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā - from
    • yonim - the source or womb
    • agnir - fire
    • ghṛtavantam - endowed with ghee (clarified butter)
    • asthāt - has established
    • pṛthupragāṇam - vast and expansive
    • uśantam - shining or radiant
    • uśānaḥ - the dawn
    • dīdyānaḥ - illuminating
    • śucir - pure
    • ṛṣvaḥ - the one who is strong
    • pāvakaḥ - the purifier
    • punaḥ-punar - again and again
    • mātarā - by the mother
    • navyasī - the new or fresh
    • kaḥ - who
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine fire (Agni) that is born from the source, which is pure and radiant.
    • Agni is often associated with transformation and purification, much like how fire transforms materials and purifies them.
    • The mention of "mātarā" (by the mother) signifies the nurturing aspect of nature, suggesting that this fire is not just destructive but also a source of life and sustenance.
    • The phrase "punaḥ-punar" indicates the cyclical nature of life, where creation and destruction are part of a continuous process.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we can see the role of fire in cooking, where it transforms raw ingredients into nourishing meals, symbolizing the nurturing aspect of Agni.
    • In a broader sense, the cycles of nature, such as the changing seasons, reflect the mantra's theme of renewal and transformation.
    • In spiritual practices, fire rituals (Homa) are performed to invoke purity and transformation, aligning with the mantra's emphasis on Agni as a purifier.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of fire as a transformative and purifying force in both the physical and spiritual realms.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the cycles of life and the nurturing aspects of nature, reminding us that destruction can lead to new beginnings.
    • In real life, we can apply the teachings of this mantra by embracing change, seeking purification in our actions, and recognizing the interconnectedness of all life.
