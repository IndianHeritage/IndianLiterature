मन्तर: नराशंसम् इह प्रियं अस्मिन यज्ञ उप हवये | मधुजिह्वं हविष्कृतम् ||
narāśaṁsam iha priyam asmin yajña upa hvaye | madhujihvaṁ haviṣkṛtam ||
  • Mantra: "narāśaṁsam iha priyam asmin yajña upa hvaye | madhujihvaṁ haviṣkṛtam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • narāśaṁsam: Referring to the divine or the supreme being, often interpreted as a form of Lord Vishnu or a representation of the universal spirit.
    • iha: Here, in this world or in this context.
    • priyam: Beloved, dear, or something that is cherished.
    • asmin: In this, or in this situation.
    • yajña: Sacrifice, offering, or a ritual performed for a higher purpose.
    • upa: Near, close to, or in the presence of.
    • hvaye: I invoke, I call upon, or I offer.
    • madhujihvaṁ: Sweet-tongued, often referring to the divine or a deity that speaks sweetly or kindly.
    • haviṣkṛtam: Prepared offerings or that which is made ready for the sacrifice.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence, emphasizing the importance of offerings and rituals in connecting with the higher powers.
    • The phrase "madhujihvaṁ" suggests that the divine being is approachable and benevolent, encouraging devotees to seek a relationship with the divine through sweet words and sincere offerings.
    • In practical terms, this can be seen in various cultural rituals where offerings are made to deities, symbolizing gratitude and the desire for blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu weddings, offerings are made to the fire (Agni) as a witness to the vows taken by the couple, symbolizing the sacredness of their union.
    • During festivals like Diwali, people prepare sweets and offer them to deities, reflecting the essence of "madhujihvaṁ" by presenting something sweet and cherished.
    • In daily prayers, individuals may offer flowers, fruits, or incense, embodying the spirit of yajña and the act of invoking divine blessings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of devotion, offerings, and the connection between the divine and the devotee.
    • It teaches that through sincere offerings and sweet words, one can cultivate a relationship with the divine, leading to spiritual growth and fulfillment.
    • In contemporary life, the principles of this mantra can be applied in various forms of worship, community service, and acts of kindness, reinforcing the idea that our actions and intentions can invoke positive energies and blessings in our lives.
