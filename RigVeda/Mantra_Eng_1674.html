इदं उदकम् पिबतेत्य अब्रवीतानेदं वा घा पिबता मुंजनेजनम् |सौधन्वना यदि तन् नेव हर्यथा तृतीये घा सवने मादयाध्वै ||
idam udakam pibatety abravītanedaṁ vā ghā pibatā muñjanejanam | saudhanvanā yadi tan neva haryatha tṛtīye ghā savane mādayādhvai ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • idam: this
    • udakam: water
    • pibat: drink
    • etey: thus
    • abravīt: he said
    • edaṁ: this
    • vā: or
    • ghā: drink
    • pibatā: of the drinker
    • muñjanejanam: of the one who is offering
    • saudhanvanā: with wealth
    • yadi: if
    • tan: that
    • neva: not
    • haryatha: be happy
    • tṛtīye: in the third
    • ghā: drink
    • savane: in the offering
    • mādayādhvai: may you be delighted
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the act of drinking water, which is often seen as a sacred and purifying act in many cultures.
    • The phrase "idam udakam pibate" emphasizes the importance of water as a life-giving force, suggesting that one should drink it with reverence.
    • The mention of "saudhanvanā" indicates that wealth or prosperity is associated with the act of sharing or offering water, highlighting the communal aspect of sustenance.
    • The line "yadi tan neva haryatha" suggests that if one does not partake in this offering, they may not find happiness or fulfillment, indicating the importance of community and sharing in achieving joy.
    • The concluding part "mādayādhvai" implies that through this act of sharing and drinking, one may find delight and satisfaction.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, offering water to guests is a sign of hospitality and respect, reflecting the communal spirit emphasized in the mantra.
    • During festivals or communal gatherings, sharing food and drink symbolizes unity and joy, resonating with the idea of collective happiness found in the mantra.
    • In environmental discussions, the importance of water as a precious resource is highlighted, reminding us to treat it with reverence and care, akin to the sacredness expressed in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra underscores the significance of water as a vital element for life and community.
    • It teaches us the value of sharing resources and the joy that comes from communal experiences.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to appreciate and respect the natural resources we have, fostering a sense of gratitude and interconnectedness with others.
