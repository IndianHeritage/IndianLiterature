यः सुषव्यः सुदक्षिण इनो यः सुक्रतुर गृणे |य आकरः सहस्राः यः शतामघ इन्द्रो यः पूर्वभिद आरितः ||
yaḥ suṣavyaḥ sudakṣiṇa ino yaḥ sukratur gṛṇe | ya ākaraḥ sahasrā yaḥ śatāmagha indro yaḥ pūrbhid āritaḥ ||
  • yaḥ suṣavyaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "He who is well-versed" or "He who is knowledgeable." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in one's actions and decisions.
  • sudakṣiṇa: This means "skillful" or "dexterous." It highlights the value of skill and expertise in achieving success in various endeavors.
  • ino yaḥ sukratur gṛṇe: This translates to "He who performs good deeds." It signifies the importance of righteousness and ethical behavior in life.
  • ya ākaraḥ sahasrā: This means "He who creates thousands." It can refer to the ability to generate abundance and prosperity, whether in terms of wealth, knowledge, or relationships.
  • yaḥ śatāmagha indro: This phrase translates to "He who is the lord of a hundred powers." It signifies strength, authority, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • yaḥ pūrbhid āritaḥ: This means "He who is invoked for protection." It emphasizes the need for divine guidance and support in times of difficulty.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, skill, righteousness, abundance, strength, and divine protection. In real life, these qualities are essential for personal and professional success. For instance, a successful entrepreneur embodies these traits by being knowledgeable about their industry (yaḥ suṣavyaḥ), skillful in their craft (sudakṣiṇa), and ethical in their business practices (ino yaḥ sukratur gṛṇe). Furthermore, the ability to create opportunities (ya ākaraḥ sahasrā) and to lead with authority (yaḥ śatāmagha indro) is crucial in leadership roles. Finally, seeking divine guidance (yaḥ pūrbhid āritaḥ) can provide comfort and strength during challenging times. Thus, this mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate these virtues in our daily lives for holistic growth and fulfillment.
