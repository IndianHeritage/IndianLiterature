आ मित्रे वरुणे वयं गीरभिर जुहुमो अत्रिवत् |नि बरहिषि सदतं सोमपीतये ||
ā mitre varuṇe vayaṁ gīrbhir juhumo atrivat | ni barhiṣi sadataṁ somapītaye ||
  • Mantra: "ā mitre varuṇe vayaṁ gīrbhir juhumo atrivat | ni barhiṣi sadataṁ somapītaye ||"
  • Translation: "To Mitra and Varuna, we offer our hymns; may they be pleased with our offerings, as we continually strive for the essence of the Soma."
  • Explanation:
    • Mitra: In Vedic literature, Mitra is the deity associated with friendship, harmony, and the natural order. He represents the light of day and is often invoked for protection and goodwill.
    • Varuna: Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water. He embodies the moral and ethical aspects of the universe, overseeing the laws of nature and human conduct.
    • Gīrbhir juhumo: This phrase means "we offer our hymns." It signifies the act of devotion and the importance of vocalizing one's prayers and intentions to the deities.
    • Atrivat: This term suggests a sense of continuity and tradition in worship, indicating that the offerings are made in a manner consistent with established practices.
    • Somapītaye: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. The phrase indicates the desire for spiritual nourishment and enlightenment through the offerings made to the deities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the principles of friendship and harmony represented by Mitra can be applied in personal relationships and community building. For instance, fostering a spirit of cooperation in a workplace can lead to a more productive and positive environment.
    • The ethical and moral guidance of Varuna can be reflected in our daily decisions, such as choosing to act with integrity in business dealings or advocating for environmental conservation, which aligns with his association with water and nature.
    • The act of offering hymns can be seen in various forms of expression, such as poetry, music, or even social media posts that convey gratitude and appreciation for the beauty of life and the universe.
    • Seeking spiritual nourishment, akin to the essence of Soma, can be pursued through practices like meditation, yoga, or engaging in community service, which enriches one's life and fosters a sense of connection with others.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of devotion, ethical living, and the pursuit of harmony in our lives. By invoking the qualities of Mitra and Varuna, we are reminded to cultivate friendships, uphold moral values, and seek spiritual growth. In a world often filled with discord, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for fostering peace, integrity, and a deeper connection with the divine and the community around us. Its significance lies in its timeless relevance, encouraging individuals to live harmoniously and ethically in their daily lives.
