अहस्ता यद अपदी वर्धत क्षाः शचीभिर वेद्यानाम् |शुष्णं परि प्रदक्षिणिद विश्वायवे न शिश्नथः ||
ahastā yad apadī vardhata kṣāḥ śacībhir vedyānām | śuṣṇam pari pradakṣiṇid viśvāyave ni śiśnathaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ahastā: Without hands
    • yad: That which
    • apadī: In the absence of obstacles
    • vardhata: Grows or increases
    • kṣāḥ: The earth or the ground
    • śacībhir: By the powerful or the capable
    • vedyānām: Of the wise or the learned
    • śuṣṇam: The dry or the barren
    • pari: All around
    • pradakṣiṇid: Circumambulating or going around
    • viśvāyave: To all beings or to the universe
    • ni: Not
    • śiśnathaḥ: To be disturbed or shaken
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the growth and flourishing of life and nature, even in the absence of direct intervention (represented by "ahastā").
    • The phrase "yad apadī vardhata" suggests that growth occurs when there are no obstacles, indicating that a conducive environment is essential for progress.
    • The term "kṣāḥ" refers to the earth, symbolizing stability and nourishment, which is vital for any form of life.
    • "śacībhir vedyānām" implies that those who are wise and capable can harness this growth, suggesting the importance of knowledge and wisdom in nurturing life.
    • The mention of "śuṣṇam" indicates that even barren or dry conditions can be transformed through the right actions and intentions.
    • "pari pradakṣiṇid" emphasizes the importance of holistic approaches, where one must consider the entire environment and community.
    • Finally, "viśvāyave ni śiśnathaḥ" conveys a message of stability and peace, indicating that true growth should not disturb the balance of nature or society.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, sustainable practices that respect the environment lead to better yields, demonstrating how growth can occur without harming the ecosystem.
    • In personal development, individuals often flourish when they are in supportive environments free from negativity or obstacles.
    • Community initiatives that involve collective efforts (like community gardens) show how collaboration can lead to growth and nourishment for all involved.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of creating an environment conducive to growth, emphasizing the roles of wisdom, community, and respect for nature.
    • It teaches us that growth is possible even in challenging conditions, provided there is a collective effort and a mindful approach.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various fields such as education, community building, and environmental conservation, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing our surroundings.
