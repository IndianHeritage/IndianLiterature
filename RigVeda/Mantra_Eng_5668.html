आ राजाना मह ऋतस्य गोपा सिंधुपती क्षत्रियाऽयातम् अर्वाक् |इळां नो मित्रावरुणोत वृष्टिम अव दिवार्वतं जीरदाणु ||
ā rājānā maha ṛtasya gopā sindhupatī kṣatriyā yātam arvāk | iḻāṁ no mitrāvaruṇota vṛṣṭim ava diva invataṁ jīradānū ||
  • Mantra: "ā rājānā maha ṛtasya gopā sindhupatī kṣatriyā yātam arvāk | iḻāṁ no mitrāvaruṇota vṛṣṭim ava diva invataṁ jīradānū ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā rājānā: O kings
    • maha: great
    • ṛtasya: of truth/order
    • gopā: protectors
    • sindhupatī: lord of the river
    • kṣatriyā: warriors
    • yātam: may we go
    • arvāk: forward
    • iḻāṁ: prosperity
    • no: our
    • mitrāvaruṇota: may Mitra and Varuna
    • vṛṣṭim: rain
    • ava: down
    • diva: from the heavens
    • invataṁ: may we invoke
    • jīradānū: the giver of life
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra addresses the kings and warriors, invoking their role as protectors of truth and order.
    • The term "sindhupatī" refers to the lord of the river, symbolizing abundance and the flow of life.
    • The invocation of "Mitra and Varuna" signifies the duality of nature, where Mitra represents friendship and harmony, while Varuna embodies the cosmic order and moral law.
    • The request for rain ("vṛṣṭim") from the heavens emphasizes the importance of nature's blessings for prosperity and sustenance.
    • In a real-life context, this mantra can be seen as a call for responsible leadership, where rulers and protectors are urged to uphold truth and ensure the welfare of their people.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to governance and community well-being.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of leadership, nature, and societal harmony.
    • In modern scenarios, this mantra can inspire leaders to prioritize ethical governance, environmental sustainability, and the welfare of their citizens.
    • By invoking the blessings of nature and the moral order, individuals and communities can strive for a balanced and prosperous existence.
