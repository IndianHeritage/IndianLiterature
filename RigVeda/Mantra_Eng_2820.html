युवं प्रात्नस्य साधथो महो यद् दैवी स्वस्तिः परि णः स्यातम् |गोपालजिह्वस्य तस्थुषो विरूपा विश्वे पश्यन्ति मायिनः कृतानि ||
yuvam pratnasya sādhatho maho yad daivī svastiḥ pari ṇaḥ syātam | gopājihvasya tasthuṣo virūpā viśve paśyanti māyinaḥ kṛtāni ||
  • yuvam pratnasya sādhatho maho: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the divine for assistance and support in our endeavors. The word "yuvam" refers to the youthful energy and potential, while "pratnasya" signifies the pursuit of goals or aspirations. "Sādhatho" implies the act of achieving or accomplishing something significant.
  • yad daivī svastiḥ pari ṇaḥ syātam: Here, "yad" means "that which," and "daivī svastiḥ" refers to divine well-being or auspiciousness. "Pari ṇaḥ" suggests a comprehensive or all-encompassing nature, while "syātam" indicates a wish or desire. Together, this line expresses a longing for divine blessings and prosperity in all aspects of life.
  • gopājihvasya tasthuṣo virūpā: "Gopājihvasya" can be understood as the essence of the protector or caretaker, often associated with the divine cowherd (like Lord Krishna). "Tasthuṣo" refers to the one who is revered or honored, and "virūpā" signifies the multifaceted nature of existence. This line emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring the divine in its various forms.
  • viśve paśyanti māyinaḥ kṛtāni: "Viśve" means "all" or "universal," while "paśyanti" translates to "they see" or "they perceive." "Māyinaḥ" refers to the illusionary aspects of life, and "kṛtāni" means "actions" or "deeds." This line suggests that all beings perceive the actions of the divine, which may often be veiled in illusion, and encourages awareness of the deeper truths behind our experiences.

  • Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a profound understanding of the relationship between the divine and human endeavors. It emphasizes the importance of seeking divine blessings for success and well-being while recognizing the multifaceted nature of existence. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as during significant life events like weddings, new ventures, or personal challenges, where invoking divine support can provide strength and guidance. The mantra serves as a reminder to remain aware of the divine presence in our lives and to honor it through our actions and intentions.
