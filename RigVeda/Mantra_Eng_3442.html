ऋभुर विभ्वा वाज इन्द्रो नो अचेमं यज्ञं रत्नधेयोप याता |इदा हि वो धिषणा देवी अह्नाम अधात पीतिं सम मदा अग्मता वः ||
ṛbhur vibhvā vāja indro no acchemaṁ yajñaṁ ratnadheyopa yāta | idā hi vo dhiṣaṇā devy ahnām adhāt pītiṁ sam madā agmatā vaḥ ||
  • ṛbhur: This word can be translated as "the one who is rich" or "the one who is prosperous." It signifies abundance and wealth.
  • vibhvā: This term means "spreading" or "expanding." It indicates the idea of growth and the dissemination of prosperity.
  • vāja: This word translates to "strength" or "power." It often refers to the vigor and energy that one possesses.
  • indro: This refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing leadership, authority, and protection.
  • no: This is a pronoun meaning "our," indicating a collective appeal or request.
  • acchemaṁ: This word means "to not be harmed" or "to be free from obstacles." It conveys a sense of safety and security.
  • yajñaṁ: This term refers to a ritual or sacrifice, often performed to please the deities and seek their blessings.
  • ratnadheyopa: This can be interpreted as "bestowing treasures" or "granting valuable gifts." It emphasizes the idea of receiving blessings in the form of wealth or resources.
  • yāta: This word means "to go" or "to proceed," suggesting movement towards a goal or aspiration.
  • idā: This means "now" or "at this moment," indicating immediacy and relevance.
  • hi: This is an emphatic particle, often used for emphasis in a statement.
  • vo: This means "your," indicating a direct address to the deities or divine forces.
  • dhiṣaṇā: This term refers to "intelligence" or "wisdom," highlighting the importance of knowledge in achieving success.
  • devy: This means "goddess" or "divine," referring to the feminine aspect of divinity.
  • ahnām: This translates to "of the day," indicating the time frame of the blessings being sought.
  • adhāt: This means "to place" or "to establish," suggesting the act of setting something in motion.
  • pītiṁ: This word means "nourishment" or "satisfaction," indicating the fulfillment of desires.
  • sam: This means "together" or "in harmony," emphasizing unity in purpose.
  • madā: This translates to "joy" or "delight," indicating the emotional aspect of the blessings received.
  • agmatā: This means "to come" or "to arrive," suggesting the arrival of blessings or good fortune.
  • vaḥ: This is a pronoun meaning "to you," indicating the recipients of the blessings.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a prayer for prosperity, strength, and protection, invoking the blessings of the divine. It emphasizes the importance of rituals (yajña) in seeking abundance and wisdom in life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during significant life events like weddings, new ventures, or any occasion where blessings for success and safety are desired. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset of abundance and gratitude, fostering a harmonious relationship with the divine and the universe.
