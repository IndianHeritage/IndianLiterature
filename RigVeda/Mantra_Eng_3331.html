आद इद ध नेमा इन्द्रियं यजन्त आद इत पक्तिः पुरोळाशं रिरिच्यात् |आद इत सोमो वि पपृच्याद असुष्वीन आद इज जुजोष वृषभं यजध्यै ||
ād id dha nema indriyaṁ yajanta ād it paktiḥ puroḻāśaṁ riricyāt | ād it somo vi papṛcyād asuṣvīn ād ij jujoṣa vṛṣabhaṁ yajadhyai ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ād: This word signifies "from" or "out of," indicating a source or origin.
    • id: Translates to "this," referring to the present moment or situation.
    • dha: Means "to place" or "to establish," suggesting the act of setting something in order.
    • nema: Refers to "the path" or "the way," indicating a direction or guidance.
    • indriyaṁ: Means "senses" or "organs," referring to the faculties through which we perceive the world.
    • yajanta: Translates to "they worship" or "they perform rituals," indicating an act of devotion.
    • it: Means "that," pointing to something beyond the immediate context.
    • paktiḥ: Refers to "strength" or "power," indicating a force or energy.
    • puroḻāśaṁ: Means "the one who is worthy of offerings," referring to a deity or divine presence.
    • riricyāt: Translates to "may he be pleased," indicating a desire for divine favor.
    • somo: Refers to "the moon" or "soma," often associated with nourishment and immortality.
    • vi: Means "in a special way," indicating uniqueness or distinction.
    • papṛcyād: Translates to "may he inquire," suggesting a quest for knowledge or understanding.
    • asuṣvīn: Refers to "the vital energy" or "life force," indicating the essence of life.
    • ij: Means "to invoke" or "to call upon," indicating a plea for divine attention.
    • jujoṣa: Translates to "may he unite," indicating a desire for harmony and connection.
    • vṛṣabhaṁ: Refers to "the bull," symbolizing strength and virility.
    • yajadhyai: Means "for the worship," indicating the purpose of the rituals.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of worship and the invocation of divine energies.
    • It highlights the connection between the senses, the divine, and the strength that comes from such worship.
    • The mantra calls for the divine to be pleased and to grant knowledge and unity, symbolized by the moon and the bull.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek guidance and strength through meditation or prayer, similar to the worship described in the mantra.
    • Communities come together in rituals to invoke blessings, reflecting the collective desire for divine favor and unity.
    • In moments of decision-making, people may reflect on their inner senses and seek clarity, akin to the inquiry for knowledge mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of spiritual practices in fostering connection with the divine.
    • It emphasizes the role of worship in gaining strength, knowledge, and unity, which are essential in navigating life's challenges.
    • In a broader context, the mantra encourages individuals and communities to seek harmony and divine guidance in their endeavors.
