देवो भागः सविता रयो अंश इन्द्रो वृत्रस्य संजितो धनानाम् |ऋभुक्षा वाजा उता वपुरन्धिर अवन्तु नो अमृतासस तुरासः ||
devo bhagaḥ savitā rāyo aṁśa indro vṛtrasya saṁjito dhanānām | ṛbhukṣā vāja uta vā puraṁdhir avantu no amṛtāsas turāsaḥ ||
  • Devo bhagaḥ savitā: This phrase refers to the divine blessings of Savitā, the Sun God, who is often associated with prosperity and enlightenment. In real life, this can be likened to seeking guidance and inspiration from a higher power or the universe to achieve one's goals.
  • Rāyo aṁśa indro: Here, "Rāyo" signifies wealth or prosperity, and "Indro" refers to Indra, the king of gods, who is known for his strength and power. This can be interpreted as a call for divine intervention to attain success and abundance in life, similar to how individuals seek mentors or leaders for support in their endeavors.
  • Vṛtrasya saṁjito dhanānām: "Vṛtra" symbolizes obstacles or challenges, and "saṁjito" means to overcome. This part of the mantra emphasizes the importance of overcoming difficulties to achieve wealth and success. In everyday life, this can be seen in the stories of entrepreneurs who face numerous challenges but persist to achieve their dreams.
  • Ṛbhukṣā vāja uta: "Ṛbhukṣā" refers to nourishment or sustenance, while "vāja" means strength or vigor. This suggests that true prosperity comes from a balanced life that nourishes both the body and the spirit. For example, individuals who maintain a healthy lifestyle often find greater success in their personal and professional lives.
  • Vā puraṁdhir avantu: "Puraṁdhir" can be interpreted as the one who creates or builds. This part of the mantra calls for the divine to assist in the creation of a prosperous and fulfilling life. In real life, this can be related to teamwork and collaboration, where collective efforts lead to greater achievements.
  • No amṛtāsas turāsaḥ: "Amṛtāsas" refers to immortality or eternal bliss, and "turāsaḥ" signifies strength or power. This closing line emphasizes the desire for lasting success and happiness. In practical terms, this can be seen in the pursuit of meaningful relationships and legacies that endure beyond one's lifetime.

  • Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and support in overcoming challenges to achieve prosperity and fulfillment in life. Its significance lies in the reminder that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses spiritual and emotional well-being. In real-life scenarios, individuals can apply the teachings of this mantra by setting clear goals, seeking mentorship, maintaining a balanced lifestyle, and fostering collaboration with others to create a prosperous future.
