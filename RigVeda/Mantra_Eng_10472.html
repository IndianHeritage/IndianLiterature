यā देवेषु तन्‍वम् ऐरयन्त यासां सोमो विश्वा रूपाणि वेद |तā अस्मभ्यम् पयसा पिन्वमानाः प्रजावतीर इन्द्र गोष्ठे रिरिहि ||
yā deveṣu tanva1m airayanta yāsāṁ somo viśvā rūpāṇi veda | tā asmabhyam payasā pinvamānāḥ prajāvatīr indra goṣṭhe rirīhi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yā: who
    • deveṣu: among the gods
    • tanvā: with bodies
    • airayanta: who are nourishing
    • yāsāṁ: of whom
    • somo: the moon (or soma, the divine nectar)
    • viśvā: all
    • rūpāṇi: forms
    • veda: knows
    • tā: those
    • asmabhyam: to us
    • payasā: with nourishment
    • pinvamānāḥ: being filled
    • prajāvatīr: with progeny
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • goṣṭhe: in the herd
    • rirīhi: may you be pleased
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine beings, asking for their nourishing presence.
    • It highlights the connection between the divine (represented by the gods) and the earthly realm (represented by nourishment and progeny).
    • The mention of "soma" signifies the importance of divine sustenance, which can be interpreted as both physical nourishment and spiritual enlightenment.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, symbolizes strength and abundance, and the mantra seeks his blessings for prosperity and fertility.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, this mantra can be recited during harvest festivals to invoke blessings for a bountiful crop.
    • In family settings, it can be used during rituals for childbirth, seeking blessings for the health and prosperity of the newborn.
    • In community gatherings, it can serve as a reminder of the importance of unity and collective nourishment, both physically and spiritually.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine nourishment and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek blessings for abundance and prosperity in various aspects of life.
    • In modern contexts, it can inspire individuals and communities to foster a sense of gratitude and responsibility towards nurturing life and relationships.
