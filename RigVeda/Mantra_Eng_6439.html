त इद वेदिं शुभग त आहुतिं ते सोतुं चक्रिरे दिवि |त इद वाजेभिर जिग्युर महद धनं ये त्वे कामं न्येरिरे ||
ta id vediṁ subhaga ta āhutiṁ te sotuṁ cakrire divi | ta id vājebhir jigyur mahad dhanaṁ ye tve kāmaṁ nyerire ||
  • ta id vediṁ subhaga: This phrase can be translated as "May this knowledge be auspicious." The term "subhaga" signifies something that is beneficial or fortunate. In real life, this can relate to the pursuit of knowledge and education, which is often seen as a path to success and prosperity.
  • ta āhutiṁ te sotuṁ cakrire divi: This translates to "May our offerings reach you in the heavens." Here, "āhutiṁ" refers to offerings or sacrifices made to deities. This can be likened to the act of giving back to the community or contributing to a cause, which is a common practice in many cultures, such as charity work or volunteering.
  • ta id vājebhir jigyur: This means "May they strive for wealth." The word "vājebhir" relates to wealth or resources. In a modern context, this can be interpreted as the pursuit of financial stability and success, emphasizing the importance of hard work and ambition in achieving one's goals.
  • mahad dhanaṁ ye tve kāmaṁ nyerire: This translates to "Those who desire great wealth, may they attain it." The term "mahad dhanaṁ" refers to great wealth, while "kāmaṁ" signifies desire or aspiration. This reflects the universal human aspiration for prosperity and fulfillment, encouraging individuals to pursue their dreams and ambitions.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of knowledge, offerings, ambition, and the pursuit of wealth. It highlights the interconnectedness of these elements in achieving a prosperous and fulfilling life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to various aspects such as education, community service, personal ambition, and financial planning. By striving for knowledge and contributing positively to society, individuals can create a foundation for success and well-being, ultimately leading to the attainment of their desires and aspirations.
