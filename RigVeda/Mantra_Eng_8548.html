एष स्य सोमो मतीभिः पुनानो 'त्यो न वाजी तरतीद अरातिः |पयो न दुग्धम अदितेर इषिरम् उर्व इव गातुः सुयमो न वोल्हा ||
eṣa sya somo matibhiḥ punāno 'tyo na vājī taratīd arātīḥ | payo na dugdham aditer iṣiram urv iva gātuḥ suyamo na voḻhā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣa: this
    • sya: of him
    • somo: the soma (a sacred drink)
    • matibhiḥ: with thoughts or intellect
    • punāno: purifying or refreshing
    • 'tyo: not (in the sense of negation)
    • na: not
    • vājī: the one who is strong or powerful
    • taratīd: swiftly or quickly
    • arātīḥ: obstacles or hindrances
    • payo: milk
    • na: not
    • dugdham: the one that is milked
    • aditer: of Aditi (the mother of gods)
    • iṣiram: the essence or the best part
    • urv: wide or vast
    • iva: like or as
    • gātuḥ: of the earth
    • suyamo: well-directed or auspicious
    • na: not
    • voḻhā: to you (plural)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the sacred soma, which is often associated with divine inspiration and enlightenment.
    • The phrase "with thoughts or intellect" suggests that the consumption of soma is not just a physical act but also a mental and spiritual one.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of overcoming obstacles swiftly, indicating that with the right mindset and divine support, challenges can be surmounted.
    • The reference to milk symbolizes nourishment and purity, suggesting that divine blessings are essential for growth and sustenance.
    • The mention of Aditi, the mother of gods, highlights the connection to the divine and the importance of seeking higher wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, the mantra can be applied to teamwork. Just as the soma purifies and refreshes, effective collaboration can lead to innovative solutions and overcoming challenges.
    • In personal development, the idea of purifying thoughts can be related to mindfulness practices, where individuals strive to clear their minds of negativity and focus on positive outcomes.
    • In education, the pursuit of knowledge can be likened to the consumption of soma, where students are encouraged to engage intellectually and spiritually with their studies.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine inspiration, intellectual engagement, and the overcoming of obstacles in life.
    • It teaches us that with the right mindset and support, we can navigate challenges effectively and achieve our goals.
    • In real life, this mantra can be a reminder to seek purity in our thoughts and actions, to nourish ourselves with knowledge, and to remain connected to the divine for guidance and strength.
