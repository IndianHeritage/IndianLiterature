सुṣ्वाणासो व्य अद्रिभिः चितानां गोः अधि त्वचि |इषं अस्मभ्यम् अभितः सम अस्वरन् वसुविदः ||
suṣvāṇāso vy adribhiś citānā gor adhi tvaci | iṣam asmabhyam abhitaḥ sam asvaran vasuvidaḥ ||
  • suṣvāṇāso: This term can be broken down into "su" meaning good or auspicious, and "vāṇāso" which refers to those who are well-spoken or articulate. In a real-life context, this can relate to individuals who communicate effectively and positively, fostering good relationships.
  • vy adribhiś: "Vy" means to be surrounded or encompassed, while "adribhiś" refers to mountains or obstacles. This phrase suggests being surrounded by challenges or difficulties, akin to facing life's hurdles. For example, a student preparing for exams may feel overwhelmed by the vast syllabus, representing the 'mountains' in their path.
  • citānā: This word translates to 'awakened' or 'enlightened'. It signifies a state of awareness and consciousness. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who seek knowledge and understanding, striving to be more aware of their surroundings and the impact of their actions.
  • gor adhi tvaci: "Gor" refers to strength or power, while "adhi tvaci" means 'upon your body' or 'in your presence'. This can be interpreted as drawing strength from one's surroundings or community. For instance, a team working together in a corporate environment can draw strength from each other to achieve common goals.
  • iṣam asmabhyam: "Iṣam" means 'desire' or 'wish', and "asmabhyam" translates to 'for us'. This indicates a collective aspiration or goal. In a practical scenario, this could relate to a community coming together to achieve a common objective, such as environmental conservation efforts.
  • abhitaḥ: This term means 'from all sides' or 'surrounding'. It suggests a holistic approach to challenges, where one considers all aspects of a situation. For example, a business leader might evaluate market trends, customer feedback, and employee satisfaction to make informed decisions.
  • sam asvaran: "Sam" means 'together' or 'united', and "asvaran" refers to 'sound' or 'voice'. This phrase emphasizes the importance of unity and collective voice. In real life, this can be seen in social movements where individuals unite to raise awareness about important issues, amplifying their message.
  • vasuvidaḥ: This term translates to 'those who know the wealth' or 'those who are knowledgeable about resources'. It signifies the importance of wisdom and understanding in utilizing resources effectively. For instance, sustainable farming practices require knowledge of the land and its resources to ensure long-term productivity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of overcoming challenges through collective strength, awareness, and effective communication. It highlights the significance of unity in achieving common goals and the importance of knowledge in utilizing resources wisely. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork in professional settings, community initiatives, and personal growth endeavors. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals and groups can navigate obstacles and work towards a prosperous and harmonious existence.
