तं गोभिर वृषणं रसं मदाय देववीतये |सुतं भराय सं सृजा ||
taṁ gobhir vṛṣaṇaṁ rasam madāya devavītaye | sutam bharāya saṁ sṛja ||
  • taṁ - This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • gobhir - This means "with the cows" or "through the cattle," symbolizing wealth, nourishment, and prosperity.
  • vṛṣaṇaṁ - This term can be interpreted as "the strong" or "the powerful," often associated with strength and vitality.
  • rasam - This word means "essence" or "juice," indicating the vital energy or life force that sustains existence.
  • madāya - This translates to "for the sake of joy" or "for intoxication," suggesting a celebration of life and abundance.
  • devavītaye - This means "for the divine purpose" or "for the sake of the gods," indicating a connection to spirituality and higher ideals.
  • sutam - This word means "the son" or "the offspring," often referring to progeny or the continuation of lineage.
  • bharāya - This translates to "to fill" or "to nourish," indicating the act of providing sustenance or support.
  • saṁ sṛja - This means "to create" or "to bring forth," suggesting the act of manifestation or bringing ideas into reality.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to a divine power, asking for strength, nourishment, and the joy of life. It emphasizes the importance of prosperity, vitality, and the divine purpose behind creation.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Farming and Agriculture: The reference to "cows" and "nourishment" can be linked to agricultural practices. Farmers can chant this mantra to invoke blessings for a bountiful harvest and the health of their livestock.
  • Personal Growth: The concepts of strength and vitality can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with vigor. It encourages people to seek joy in their endeavors and to create a fulfilling life.
  • Spiritual Practices: The mantra can be used in meditation or spiritual rituals to connect with higher powers, seeking guidance and support in one's spiritual journey.

Conclusion: This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of life, prosperity, and spirituality. It serves as a reminder to seek strength and joy in our pursuits while acknowledging the divine purpose behind our actions. By incorporating its teachings into daily life, individuals can foster a sense of abundance and fulfillment, both materially and spiritually.
