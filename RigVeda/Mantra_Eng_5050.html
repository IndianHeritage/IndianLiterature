वपुर नु तच चिकीषे चिद अस्तु समानं नाम धेनु पत्यमानम् |मार्तेष्व अन्यद दोहसे पीपाय सकृत् शुक्रं दुदुहे पृथ्निर ऊधः ||
vapur nu tac cikituṣe cid astu samānaṁ nāma dhenu patyamānam | marteṣv anyad dohase pīpāya sakṛc chukraṁ duduhe pṛśnir ūdhaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vapur: form or body
    • nu: indeed or certainly
    • tac: that
    • cikituṣe: may it be known or understood
    • cid: consciousness or awareness
    • astu: may it be
    • samānaṁ: equal or similar
    • nāma: name or identity
    • dhenu: cow or nurturing entity
    • patyamānam: being milked or drawn out
    • marteṣv: among mortals
    • anyad: other or different
    • dohase: milking or nurturing
    • pīpāya: may it nourish or sustain
    • sakṛc: once or at least
    • chukraṁ: bright or pure
    • duduhe: may it be drawn out or extracted
    • pṛśnir: the one who is bountiful or generous
    • ūdhaḥ: from above or high
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the essence of nurturing and the importance of sustenance in life.
    • The reference to "vapur" and "dhenu" symbolizes the physical form and nurturing aspects of life, akin to how a cow provides milk, which is essential for growth and sustenance.
    • The phrase "marteṣv anyad" suggests that among mortals, there are various forms of nourishment and support that can be sought.
    • The invocation of "pīpāya" emphasizes the need for nourishment, not just physically but also spiritually and emotionally.
    • In a real-life context, this can be related to how we seek support from family, friends, and community, much like how a cow provides milk to sustain life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of nurturing and the interconnectedness of life.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek and provide nourishment in various forms—be it physical, emotional, or spiritual.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to foster relationships that provide support and sustenance, much like the nurturing role of a mother or caregiver.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us to appreciate the sources of nourishment in our lives and to be mindful of how we can contribute to the well-being of others.
