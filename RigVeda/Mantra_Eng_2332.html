उं मां ममन्द वृषभो मरुत्वान त्वक्षीयसां वयसा नाधमानम् |घृणीव च्छायाम अरपा अशीयां विवासेयं रुद्रस्य सुम्नम् ||
un mā mamanda vṛṣabho marutvān tvakṣīyasā vayasā nādhamānam | ghṛṇīva cchāyām arapā aśīyā vivāseyaṁ rudrasya sumnam ||
  • un mā mamanda: This phrase can be interpreted as a call for protection and guidance. "Un" signifies a plea or invocation, while "mā" means "not" or "do not," suggesting a desire to avoid negativity or harm.
  • vṛṣabho marutvān: "Vṛṣabho" refers to a bull, symbolizing strength and power, while "marutvān" relates to the Maruts, the storm deities associated with wind and vitality. Together, they represent a force of nature that is both protective and powerful.
  • tvakṣīyasā vayasā nādhamānam: This line speaks to the idea of being enveloped in a protective layer. "Tvakṣīyasā" suggests a covering or shield, while "vayasā" refers to age or life force. "Nādhamānam" implies a state of being unblemished or untouched, indicating a desire for purity and safety.
  • ghṛṇīva cchāyām: "Ghṛṇīva" means to be compassionate or nurturing, while "cchāyām" refers to shade or shelter. This suggests a nurturing presence that provides comfort and protection, akin to a mother shielding her child from harm.
  • arapā aśīyā: "Arapā" can be interpreted as a call for freedom from obstacles, while "aśīyā" refers to blessings or auspiciousness. This line emphasizes the importance of removing barriers to well-being and success.
  • vivāseyaṁ rudrasya sumnam: "Vivāseyaṁ" means to invoke or call upon, while "rudrasya" refers to Rudra, a form of Shiva associated with transformation and healing. "Sumnam" signifies auspiciousness or good fortune, indicating a desire to attract positive energies and blessings.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a profound plea for protection, strength, and auspiciousness. It emphasizes the importance of nurturing and shielding oneself from negativity while invoking the powerful energies of nature and divinity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during times of uncertainty or challenge, such as during personal crises, health issues, or significant life changes. By reciting this mantra, individuals can seek solace, strength, and guidance, fostering a sense of security and positivity in their lives. It serves as a reminder of the protective forces available to us and the importance of maintaining a nurturing environment for ourselves and others.
