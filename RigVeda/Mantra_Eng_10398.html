सहस्रणीथाः कवयो ये गोपायन्ति सूर्यं |ऋषीन तपस्वतो यम तपोजाम् अपि गच्छतात् ||
sahasraṇīthāḥ kavayo ye gopāyanti sūryam | ṛṣīn tapasvato yama tapojām̐ api gacchatāt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahasraṇīthāḥ: Those who have a thousand paths or ways.
    • kavayo: The wise or the seers.
    • ye: Who.
    • gopāyanti: Protect or guide.
    • sūryam: The sun.
    • ṛṣīn: The sages or seers.
    • tapasvato: Of the ascetics or those who practice austerity.
    • yama: The control or restraint.
    • tapojām̐: Born of austerity.
    • api: Even.
    • gacchatāt: May go or reach.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the wise seers who have multiple paths to knowledge and understanding.
    • It emphasizes the importance of guidance and protection, particularly in relation to the sun, which symbolizes enlightenment and truth.
    • The mention of sages and ascetics highlights the value of discipline and self-control in the pursuit of wisdom.
    • The phrase "may go" suggests that through dedication and austerity, one can attain higher states of consciousness or understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the various paths individuals take in their careers. For instance, a person may choose to pursue multiple avenues of learning—such as formal education, self-study, and mentorship—to gain a comprehensive understanding of their field.
    • The concept of guidance can be seen in the role of mentors or coaches who help individuals navigate their personal and professional journeys, much like the seers guiding others towards enlightenment.
    • Practices such as meditation and yoga reflect the idea of austerity and self-control, where individuals seek to attain a higher state of awareness and understanding through disciplined practice.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of wisdom, guidance, and the pursuit of knowledge through various paths.
    • It teaches that through discipline and self-control, one can achieve enlightenment and deeper understanding in life.
    • In a world filled with distractions, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek guidance, embrace multiple learning paths, and practice self-discipline to reach our goals.
