ब्रह्माणस्त्वा वयं युजा सोमपाम इन्द्र सोमिनः |सुतावन्तो हवामहे ||
brahmāṇas tvā vayaṁ yujā somapām indra sominaḥ | sutāvanto havāmahe ||
  • Mantra: "brahmāṇas tvā vayaṁ yujā somapām indra sominaḥ | sutāvanto havāmahe ||"
  • Translation: "O Indra, we invoke you, the one who is connected with the Brahmanas (the learned or wise), the one who enjoys the nectar of the Soma. We offer our praises to you, the one who is rich in strength."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Brahmāṇas: Refers to the wise or learned individuals, often associated with spiritual knowledge and wisdom.
    • Tvā: Means "you," addressing Indra directly.
    • Vayaṁ: Translates to "we," indicating a collective invocation.
    • Yujā: Means "connected" or "united," suggesting a bond or relationship.
    • Somapām: Refers to "the one who enjoys Soma," a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine qualities.
    • Indra: The king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and the force of nature.
    • Sominaḥ: Means "of the Soma," emphasizing the divine connection with the sacred drink.
    • Sutāvanto: Translates to "rich in strength" or "possessing great power."
    • Havāmahe: Means "we offer praise" or "we invoke," indicating a call to the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to seek guidance or blessings from a leader or elder, they often invoke their wisdom and strength, similar to how this mantra invokes Indra.
    • During a celebration, when participants share a drink or food that symbolizes unity and joy, it reflects the essence of "Soma," which is about sharing and enjoying life together.
    • In modern contexts, when individuals seek inspiration from mentors or leaders who embody strength and wisdom, they are essentially invoking the qualities represented by Indra in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, strength, and community in our lives.
    • It signifies the need to connect with knowledgeable individuals and seek their guidance, much like how the Brahmanas are invoked for their wisdom.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as seeking mentorship, celebrating communal bonds, and recognizing the strength that comes from unity and shared experiences.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the divine qualities we can aspire to embody and the importance of invoking strength and wisdom in our daily lives.
