मन्त्र: परि प्रासिष्यदत कविः सिन्धोर ऊर्माव अधि श्रितः | कारम् बिभ्रत पुरुष्पृहम् ||
pari prāsiṣyadat kaviḥ sindhor ūrmāv adhi śritaḥ | kāram bibhrat puruspṛham ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pari: around, in all directions
    • prāsiṣyadat: having spread out, having expanded
    • kaviḥ: the poet, the seer, the wise one
    • sindhor: of the ocean, of the sea
    • ūrmāv: waves, currents
    • adhi: upon, over
    • śritaḥ: resting, supported
    • kāram: the act, the deed
    • bibhrat: bearing, holding
    • puruspṛham: the great being, the universal spirit
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a wise seer who is surrounded by the vastness of the ocean and its waves.
    • The seer is depicted as someone who holds the essence of creation and existence, symbolized by the great being.
    • The imagery of the ocean and its waves can represent the vastness of knowledge and the depths of understanding that one can achieve.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the context of personal growth, one can relate to the seer as an individual who seeks knowledge and wisdom amidst the chaos of life, much like navigating through turbulent waters.
    • In a professional setting, a leader can be seen as a kaviḥ, guiding their team through challenges, drawing from a deep well of experience and insight.
    • In creative pursuits, artists and writers often draw inspiration from the vastness of their surroundings, akin to the ocean's waves, to create meaningful works.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in navigating life's complexities.
    • It encourages individuals to seek deeper understanding and to embrace the vastness of their experiences.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder that, like the ocean, knowledge is boundless, and those who seek it can find strength and purpose in their journey.
