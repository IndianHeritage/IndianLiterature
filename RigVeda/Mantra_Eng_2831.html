इन्द्र त्वा वृषभं वयं सुतै सोम हेवामहे |स पाही मध्वो अन्धसः ||
indra tvā vṛṣabhaṁ vayaṁ sute some havāmahe | sa pāhi madhvo andhasaḥ ||
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • Tvā: This means "you," referring directly to Indra, indicating a personal appeal or invocation.
  • Vṛṣabhaṁ: This translates to "the bull," symbolizing strength and virility. In this context, it refers to Indra's might and prowess.
  • Vayaṁ: This means "we," indicating a collective voice or community calling upon Indra.
  • Sute: This translates to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration.
  • Some: This is another reference to "soma," emphasizing its importance in the ritualistic context.
  • Havāmahe: This means "we invoke" or "we call upon," indicating a prayer or supplication.
  • Sa: This means "he," referring back to Indra.
  • Pāhi: This translates to "protect" or "safeguard," indicating a request for divine protection.
  • Madhvo: This means "of sweetness," referring to the pleasant and beneficial aspects of life.
  • Andhasaḥ: This translates to "from darkness," symbolizing ignorance or adversity.

The mantra can be understood as a collective invocation to Indra, asking for his protection and blessings through the sacred soma. The community seeks strength and guidance from Indra, the powerful deity, to safeguard them from darkness and adversity while bestowing sweetness and prosperity in their lives.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Community Gatherings: When people come together for a common purpose, invoking strength and protection can foster unity and resilience.
  • Rituals and Celebrations: During festivals or significant life events, such as weddings or harvests, this mantra can be recited to seek blessings for prosperity and happiness.
  • Personal Challenges: Individuals facing difficulties can use this mantra as a form of meditation or prayer, seeking strength and guidance to overcome obstacles.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine intervention and support in the face of challenges. It emphasizes the importance of community, strength, and the pursuit of sweetness in life. By invoking Indra, individuals and communities can find solace and empowerment, making it relevant in various aspects of life, from personal struggles to collective celebrations.
