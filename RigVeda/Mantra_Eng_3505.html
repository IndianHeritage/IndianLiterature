तोक हिते तनय उर्वरासु सूरो दृष्टिके वृषणश्च पौंस्ये |इन्द्रा नो अत्र वरुणा स्याताम अवोभिर दसमां परितकम्यायाम् ||
toke hite tanaya urvarāsu sūro dṛśīke vṛṣaṇaś ca pauṁsye | indrā no atra varuṇā syātām avobhir dasmā paritakmyāyām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • toke: for the sake of the offspring
    • hite: for the welfare
    • tanaya: progeny or children
    • urvarāsu: in the fertile or prosperous
    • sūro: the brave or strong
    • dṛśīke: with vision or insight
    • vṛṣaṇaś: the one who is powerful
    • ca: and
    • pauṁsye: in manliness or strength
    • indrā: may Indra (the king of gods) be
    • no: for us
    • atra: here
    • varuṇā: may Varuna (the god of cosmic order) be
    • syātām: may he be
    • avobhir: with the help of
    • dasmā: from this
    • paritakmyāyām: in the surrounding or encompassing
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking blessings for the welfare and prosperity of one's offspring.
    • It invokes the strength and insight of divine beings, specifically Indra and Varuna, to ensure the well-being of the family.
    • The mention of "urvarāsu" suggests a desire for abundance and fertility, which can be interpreted in both a literal and metaphorical sense.
    • In real life, this can relate to the aspirations of parents who wish for their children to be strong, wise, and prosperous.
    • The invocation of Indra and Varuna symbolizes the need for divine support in achieving these goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Parents often pray for their children's success in education and career, reflecting the desire for their offspring to thrive.
    • Communities may come together to perform rituals or ceremonies that seek blessings for prosperity and harmony, akin to the invocation of divine forces in this mantra.
    • In times of difficulty, individuals may turn to spiritual practices, seeking guidance and strength from higher powers, similar to the appeal made in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of family, community, and the divine.
    • It highlights the significance of seeking blessings for the next generation, ensuring their strength and prosperity.
    • In contemporary life, the principles of this mantra can be applied in parenting, community support, and spiritual practices, fostering a sense of responsibility towards future generations.
