तं उ नः पूर्वे पितरो नवग्वाः सप्त विप्रासो अभि वाजयन्तः |नक्षद्दाभं ततुरिम पर्वतेष्ठाम अद्रोघवाचं मतीभिः शविष्टम् ||
tam u naḥ pūrve pitaro navagvāḥ sapta viprāso abhi vājayantaḥ | nakṣaddābhaṁ taturim parvateṣṭhām adroghavācam matibhiḥ śaviṣṭham ||
  • tam u naḥ pūrve pitaro: This phrase translates to "may our forefathers" or "may our ancestors." It emphasizes the importance of honoring and remembering those who came before us, acknowledging their contributions and wisdom.
  • navagvāḥ sapta viprāso abhi vājayantaḥ: This part means "the seven wise ones who are like the new cows." Here, "cows" symbolize nourishment and wealth, while "wise ones" refers to learned individuals or sages. This suggests that wisdom and knowledge are as valuable as material wealth.
  • nakṣaddābhaṁ taturim: This translates to "the shining light of the stars." It signifies guidance and inspiration, suggesting that wisdom and knowledge illuminate our path, much like stars guide travelers at night.
  • parvateṣṭhām: This means "established in the mountains." Mountains often symbolize stability, strength, and permanence. This phrase implies that wisdom should be grounded in strong, unshakeable principles.
  • adroghavācam matibhiḥ śaviṣṭham: This translates to "the speech that is free from deceit, with the best intentions." It emphasizes the importance of honesty and integrity in communication, suggesting that true wisdom is accompanied by truthful expression.

  • Significance: This mantra highlights the value of wisdom, integrity, and the importance of honoring our ancestors. It teaches us that true wealth lies not just in material possessions but in the knowledge and values passed down through generations.
  • Real-life Application: In today's world, this mantra can be applied in various ways:
    • In family gatherings, we can honor our ancestors by sharing stories and lessons learned from them, fostering a sense of connection and continuity.
    • In educational settings, valuing the wisdom of teachers and mentors can guide students toward a more enlightened path.
    • In professional environments, promoting honesty and integrity in communication can lead to a more trustworthy and productive workplace.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, integrity, and the legacy of our ancestors. By embodying these values in our daily lives, we can create a more harmonious and enlightened society, ensuring that the light of knowledge continues to shine brightly for future generations.
