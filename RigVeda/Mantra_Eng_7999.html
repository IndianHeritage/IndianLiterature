वावृद्धानाय तूर्वये पवन्ते वाजसातये |सोमाḥ सहस्रपाजसः ||
vāvṛdhānāya tūrvaye pavante vājasātaye | somāḥ sahasrapājasaḥ ||
  • vāvṛdhānāya: This term can be broken down into two parts: "vā" meaning "to be" or "to become," and "vṛdhā" meaning "to grow" or "to increase." Thus, "vāvṛdhānāya" signifies the act of fostering growth or enhancement.
  • tūrvaye: This word is derived from "tūrvā," which refers to strength or power. It implies a call for strength or the invocation of powerful forces.
  • pavante: This term translates to "the winds" or "the forces of nature." In a broader sense, it can also refer to the vital energies that drive life and existence.
  • vājasātaye: "Vāja" means "food" or "nourishment," and "sātaye" refers to the act of providing or bestowing. Therefore, "vājasātaye" can be understood as the provision of sustenance or nourishment.
  • somāḥ: This word refers to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition that symbolizes immortality and divine nourishment. It represents the essence of life and vitality.
  • sahasrapājasaḥ: This term can be broken down into "sahasra," meaning "thousand," and "pājasaḥ," which refers to "the one who is powerful" or "the one who has strength." Together, it signifies an abundance of strength or a multitude of powerful forces.

In summary, the mantra "vāvṛdhānāya tūrvaye pavante vājasātaye | somāḥ sahasrapājasaḥ ||" is a powerful invocation for growth, strength, and nourishment. It calls upon the forces of nature to enhance life and provide sustenance, while also invoking the essence of vitality represented by soma.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra emphasizes the importance of growth and strength in our lives. In a real-life scenario, it can be applied to personal development, where individuals seek to enhance their skills and knowledge.
  • The invocation of natural forces reminds us of our connection to the environment. For instance, in agriculture, farmers may recite this mantra to seek favorable conditions for their crops, symbolizing the need for harmony with nature.
  • The concept of nourishment extends beyond physical food to include mental and spiritual sustenance. In modern life, this can translate to the importance of education, healthy relationships, and self-care practices that foster overall well-being.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of growth, strength, and nourishment in all aspects of life, encouraging us to seek balance and vitality in our pursuits.
