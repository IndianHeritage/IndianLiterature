वाय उक्तेभिर जarantे त्वाम अच्‍छा जरतारः |सुतसोमा अहर्विदः ||
vāya ukthebhir jarante tvām acchā jaritāraḥ | sutasomā aharvidaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "vāya ukthebhir jarante tvām acchā jaritāraḥ | sutasomā aharvidaḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "O Vayu (Wind), you who are invoked by the wise, may you come to us, bringing the essence of life, like the Soma (sacred drink) that nourishes us daily."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vāya: Refers to the Wind or Vayu, a vital force in nature, symbolizing movement and life.
    • ukthebhir: Means "by the utterances" or "by the invocations," indicating the power of words and prayers.
    • jarante: Translates to "may they come" or "may they flow," suggesting a call for the arrival of blessings.
    • tvām: Means "you," referring directly to Vayu.
    • acchā: Means "good" or "auspicious," indicating a positive intention behind the invocation.
    • jaritāraḥ: Refers to "those who are wise" or "the learned," emphasizing the importance of knowledge in invoking divine forces.
    • sutasomā: Refers to "the Soma," a sacred drink that symbolizes nourishment and vitality.
    • aharvidaḥ: Means "daily" or "every day," indicating the continuous need for sustenance and blessings.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we often seek the support of nature, much like how we invoke Vayu. For instance, during meditation or yoga, practitioners may focus on their breath, which is akin to inviting the essence of Vayu into their being.
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to share their thoughts and prayers, they are invoking the collective wisdom (jaritāraḥ) to bring about positive change, similar to the invocation of Vayu in this mantra.
    • In modern times, the concept of sustainability and environmental consciousness can be seen as a way of honoring Vayu, as we strive to maintain the balance of nature and ensure that our actions are beneficial for future generations.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of invoking natural forces and the wisdom of the learned to bring about nourishment and vitality in our lives.
    • It teaches us the importance of words and intentions in our prayers and actions, reminding us that our thoughts can manifest into reality.
    • In a broader context, it encourages us to respect and connect with nature, recognizing that our well-being is intertwined with the environment around us.
    • Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek harmony with the forces of nature and to cultivate wisdom in our daily lives for a fulfilling existence.
