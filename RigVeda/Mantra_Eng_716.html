सनााद् दिवं परि भूमा विरूपे पुनर्भुवां युवती स्वेभिर एविः |कृष्णेभिरक्तोषा रुशद्भिर वपुर्भिरा चरतो अन्यान्याः ||
sanād divam pari bhūmā virūpe punarbhuvā yuvatī svebhir evaiḥ | kṛṣṇebhir aktoṣā ruśadbhir vapurbhir ā carato anyānyā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sanād: From the beginning, or from the very first.
    • divam: Heaven or the celestial realm.
    • pari: Around or surrounding.
    • bhūmā: The earth or the physical world.
    • virūpe: In diverse forms or shapes.
    • punarbhuvā: Reborn or renewed.
    • yuvatī: Young women or youthful beings.
    • svebhir: By their own.
    • evaiḥ: Indeed or certainly.
    • kṛṣṇebhir: By the black or dark ones (often referring to the divine or powerful).
    • aktoṣā: Not being satisfied or unfulfilled.
    • ruśadbhir: By those who are fierce or intense.
    • vapurbhir: By forms or bodies.
    • ā carato: While moving or wandering.
    • anyānyā: Other or different.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the interconnectedness of the celestial and earthly realms, emphasizing the diversity of forms and the renewal of life.
    • The mention of "yuvatī" (young women) can symbolize vitality, creativity, and the nurturing aspects of life.
    • The phrase "kṛṣṇebhir aktoṣā" suggests a sense of dissatisfaction that can arise even amidst abundance, reflecting the human condition of seeking fulfillment.
    • The idea of "moving" or "wandering" indicates the journey of life, where one encounters various forms and experiences.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we see the cycle of life where plants and animals are reborn in different forms, illustrating the concept of renewal.
    • In human relationships, the youthful energy of individuals can lead to creativity and new beginnings, but there can also be feelings of dissatisfaction that drive personal growth.
    • In a broader societal context, the diversity of cultures and ideas can lead to a richer understanding of life, yet the quest for fulfillment remains a common thread among all people.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of recognizing the interplay between the celestial and earthly realms, the diversity of life, and the continuous journey of renewal.
    • It encourages individuals to embrace their experiences, seek fulfillment, and appreciate the beauty in diversity.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in personal development, relationships, and understanding the world around us, reminding us that every experience contributes to our growth and understanding.
