अग्निषोमा यो अद्य वाम इदं वचः सपण्यति |तस्मै धत्तं सुवीर्यं गवाम पोषं स्वस्व्यम् ||
agnīṣomā yo adya vām idaṁ vacaḥ saparyati | tasmai dhattaṁ suvīryaṁ gavām poṣaṁ svaśvyam ||
  • Agnīṣomā: This term refers to the duality of Agni (fire) and Soma (the moon). In Vedic philosophy, Agni represents energy, transformation, and the physical realm, while Soma symbolizes nourishment, tranquility, and the spiritual realm.
  • Yo adya vām idaṁ vacaḥ saparyati: This phrase translates to "who today worships you with these words." It emphasizes the act of devotion and the importance of verbal offerings in rituals, highlighting the connection between speech and divine energy.
  • Tasmai dhattaṁ: This means "to him, grant." It is a plea for blessings and strength, indicating a desire for divine support in one's endeavors.
  • Suvīryaṁ: This word translates to "great strength" or "valour." It signifies the importance of courage and resilience in facing life's challenges.
  • Gavām poṣaṁ: This phrase means "the nourishment of cattle." In ancient Vedic society, cattle were a symbol of wealth and prosperity, representing sustenance and abundance.
  • Svaśvyam: This term refers to "one's own strength" or "self-reliance." It emphasizes the importance of personal empowerment and the ability to rely on oneself.

Conclusion: The mantra "agnīṣomā yo adya vām idaṁ vacaḥ saparyati | tasmai dhattaṁ suvīryaṁ gavām poṣaṁ svaśvyam ||" encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for strength, nourishment, and self-reliance. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during challenging times when one seeks courage to overcome obstacles, or when striving for personal growth and empowerment. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the physical and spiritual realms, urging individuals to cultivate both inner strength and external support. By invoking this mantra, one can foster a sense of resilience and abundance in their life, aligning their actions with divine energy and purpose.
