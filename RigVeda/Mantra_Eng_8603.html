एष स्य ते पवता इन्द्र सोमश्चमूषु धीर उषते तवस्वान् |स्वरचक्षाः रथिरः सत्यशुष्मः कामो न यो देवयताम् असर्जि ||
eṣa sya te pavata indra somaś camūṣu dhīra uśate tavasvān | svarcakṣā rathiraḥ satyaśuṣmaḥ kāmo na yo devayatām asarji ||
  • eṣa sya te pavata: This phrase can be translated as "This is your breath, O Indra." Here, "Indra" refers to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength and power. The term "pavata" signifies the vital breath or life force, indicating the importance of breath in sustaining life.
  • indra somaś camūṣu dhīra: This translates to "Indra, the soma, is strong among the armies." "Soma" refers to a sacred drink that was believed to confer immortality and strength. "Camūṣu" means "among the armies," suggesting that Indra's strength is paramount in battles, symbolizing the idea of leadership and valor in challenging situations.
  • uśate tavasvān: This means "He who is powerful resides." It emphasizes the presence of strength and power in one's life. In real life, this can be interpreted as the importance of inner strength and resilience in overcoming obstacles.
  • svarcakṣā rathiraḥ: This translates to "The one with shining eyes, the charioteer." "Svarcakṣā" refers to someone with radiant vision or insight, while "rathiraḥ" indicates a charioteer, symbolizing guidance and direction. This can be related to leaders or mentors in our lives who provide clarity and direction.
  • satyaśuṣmaḥ: This means "the one who is true and radiant." It signifies the importance of truthfulness and integrity. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who uphold their values and principles, shining as examples for others.
  • kāmo na yo devayatām asarji: This translates to "Desire is not what the gods bestow." It suggests that true fulfillment does not come from mere desires or materialistic pursuits but rather from spiritual growth and understanding. In practical terms, this can be seen in the pursuit of meaningful relationships and experiences rather than superficial gains.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of inner strength, leadership, truthfulness, and the understanding that true fulfillment comes from spiritual rather than material pursuits. In real life, it encourages individuals to cultivate resilience, seek guidance from wise mentors, and prioritize integrity in their actions. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, leadership roles, and in fostering meaningful relationships, ultimately guiding individuals towards a more fulfilling and purposeful life.
