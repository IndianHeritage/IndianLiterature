शुनं अन्धाय भरम् अह्वयत् सा वृकीर अश्विनां वृषणा नरेति |जारः कनीन इव चक्षदाना ऋज्राश्वः शतम् एकं च मेषान् ||
śunam andhāya bharam ahvayat sā vṛkīr aśvinā vṛṣaṇā nareti | jāraḥ kanīna iva cakṣadāna ṛjrāśvaḥ śatam ekaṁ ca meṣān ||
  • śunam: This word refers to a "good" or "auspicious" state. In real life, it can be associated with positive actions or intentions that lead to beneficial outcomes.
  • andhāya: Meaning "to the blind" or "to those who cannot see." This can symbolize ignorance or lack of awareness in our lives, highlighting the importance of knowledge and enlightenment.
  • bharam: Translates to "bear" or "carry." This can be related to the burdens we carry in life, whether they are emotional, physical, or social.
  • ahvayat: This means "I call" or "I invoke." It signifies the act of seeking help or guidance, which is a common practice in various cultures when facing challenges.
  • sā: This word means "that" or "she," often referring to a divine entity or force. In a real-life context, it can represent the higher powers we believe in or look up to for support.
  • vṛkīr: This term can be interpreted as "to be strong" or "to be powerful." It emphasizes the importance of strength in overcoming obstacles in life.
  • aśvinā: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities associated with health and medicine. This highlights the significance of well-being and the pursuit of health in our daily lives.
  • vṛṣaṇā: This word means "the one who brings prosperity." It can be related to the idea of abundance and success in various endeavors.
  • nareti: Meaning "to the man" or "to the human." This emphasizes the human experience and the journey we all undertake in life.
  • jāraḥ: This translates to "the one who is wise" or "the sage." It signifies the value of wisdom and knowledge in navigating life's complexities.
  • kanīna: Meaning "the one who sees" or "the seer." This can represent the importance of perception and insight in making informed decisions.
  • iva: This word means "like" or "as." It is often used to draw comparisons, which can help us understand concepts better.
  • cakṣadāna: This translates to "the one who gives sight." It symbolizes enlightenment and the ability to see beyond the surface, which is crucial in personal growth.
  • ṛjrāśvaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is swift" or "the one who moves quickly." It emphasizes the need for agility and adaptability in life.
  • śatam: Meaning "hundred," it can symbolize abundance or a multitude of opportunities available to us.
  • ekaṁ: This translates to "one." It signifies unity and the importance of focusing on singular goals or aspirations.
  • ca: Meaning "and," it connects ideas, emphasizing the interconnectedness of various aspects of life.
  • meṣān: This word refers to "sheep," which can symbolize innocence, purity, and the need for guidance in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a call for strength, wisdom, and guidance in overcoming ignorance and challenges in life. It emphasizes the importance of seeking help from higher powers and the value of insight and perception. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied when facing difficulties, reminding us to invoke our inner strength and seek wisdom from those who have experienced similar challenges. It encourages us to remain aware and enlightened, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in our endeavors.
