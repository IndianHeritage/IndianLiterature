महान्तं त्वा महीर अन्व आपो अर्षन्ति सिन्धवः |यद गोभिर वासयिष्यसे ||
mahāntaṁ tvā mahīr anv āpo arṣanti sindhavaḥ | yad gobhir vāsayiṣyase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mahāntaṁ: Great or Magnificent
    • tvā: You
    • mahīr: Of the Earth
    • anv: Following or Pursuing
    • āpo: Waters
    • arṣanti: Flow or Move
    • sindhavaḥ: Rivers or Oceans
    • yad: Which
    • gobhir: By the Cows or Livestock
    • vāsayiṣyase: You will be nourished or Sustained
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the greatness of a divine entity, often interpreted as a reference to a deity or the universal spirit.
    • The phrase "mahāntaṁ tvā" emphasizes the magnificence and vastness of this entity, suggesting that it encompasses all that is great in the universe.
    • The mention of "mahīr" indicates a connection to the Earth, suggesting that this greatness is not just abstract but is also grounded in the physical world.
    • The waters ("āpo") and rivers ("sindhavaḥ") symbolize the flow of life and the sustenance that comes from nature, highlighting the importance of natural resources.
    • The line "yad gobhir vāsayiṣyase" suggests that this greatness is nourished by the very essence of life, represented by cows or livestock, which are vital for agriculture and sustenance in many cultures.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the health of the land and the rivers is crucial for the growth of crops. Just as the mantra suggests, the greatness of the Earth is sustained by the waters that nourish it.
    • In many cultures, cows are considered sacred and are a source of milk, labor, and companionship. This reflects the idea that the divine or greatness is sustained by the nurturing aspects of life.
    • Environmental conservation efforts can be seen as a modern application of this mantra, where protecting rivers and natural resources is essential for sustaining life on Earth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the interconnectedness of the divine, nature, and human sustenance.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of respecting and nurturing our environment, as it is through these natural elements that we find sustenance and support.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages us to recognize the greatness in the world around us and to live in harmony with it, ensuring that we honor the sources of our nourishment and life.
