हिङ्कृत्वती वसुपत्नī वसूनां वत्सम् इच्छन्ती मनसाभ्य आगात् |दुहाम अश्विभ्यां पयो अग्न्येयं सा वर्धताम महते सौभागाय ||
hiṅkṛṇvatī vasupatnī vasūnāṁ vatsam icchantī manasābhy āgāt | duhām aśvibhyām payo aghnyeyaṁ sā vardhatām mahate saubhagāya ||
  • hiṅkṛṇvatī: This term can be interpreted as "the one who brings forth" or "the one who creates." It signifies the act of generating or producing something valuable.
  • vasupatnī: This translates to "the mistress of wealth" or "the goddess of prosperity." It represents a divine feminine energy associated with abundance and material well-being.
  • vasūnāṁ: This word means "of the wealth" or "of the riches." It emphasizes the connection to material prosperity and the blessings that come with it.
  • vatsam icchantī: This phrase means "desiring a calf" or "yearning for offspring." In a broader sense, it can symbolize the desire for growth, nurturing, and the continuation of prosperity.
  • manasābhy āgāt: This translates to "came with the mind" or "arrived through thought." It highlights the importance of intention and mental focus in manifesting desires.
  • duhām: This word means "to milk" or "to extract." It signifies the act of drawing out resources or benefits from a source.
  • aśvibhyām: This term refers to "the two Ashvins," who are divine twin horsemen in Vedic mythology, symbolizing health, vitality, and the swift delivery of blessings.
  • payo: This means "milk," which is often associated with nourishment, sustenance, and the essence of life.
  • aghnīyaṁ: This translates to "that which is to be enjoyed" or "that which is beneficial." It emphasizes the positive outcomes of the blessings being sought.
  • sā: This means "she" or "that," referring to the goddess or divine energy being invoked.
  • vardhatām: This word means "may it grow" or "may it increase." It expresses a desire for expansion and enhancement of blessings.
  • mahate: This translates to "great" or "mighty," indicating the significance and power of the blessings being sought.
  • saubhagāya: This means "for good fortune" or "for auspiciousness." It signifies the ultimate goal of seeking prosperity and well-being.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of invoking divine blessings for prosperity, growth, and well-being. It emphasizes the importance of intention and mental focus in manifesting desires. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In business, individuals can recite this mantra to attract wealth and success, focusing their intentions on growth and prosperity.
  • In personal life, it can be used to nurture relationships and family, symbolizing the desire for growth and abundance in one's personal sphere.
  • During rituals or ceremonies, this mantra can be chanted to invoke blessings for health, vitality, and overall well-being.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to focus their thoughts and intentions on attracting prosperity and abundance in various aspects of life.
