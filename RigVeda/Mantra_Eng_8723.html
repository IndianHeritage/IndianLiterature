पवस्व वाजसातयेऽभि विश्वानि काव्याः |त्वं समुद्रम प्रथमो विथारयो देवभ्यः सोम मत्सरः ||
pavasva vājasātaye 'bhi viśvāni kāvyā | tvaṁ samudram prathamo vi dhārayo devebhyaḥ soma matsaraḥ ||
  • pavasva: This word can be interpreted as "to purify" or "to cleanse." In a real-life context, this can relate to the importance of mental and spiritual purification, akin to practices like meditation or yoga that help individuals clear their minds and focus on positive thoughts.
  • vājasātaye: This term refers to "for the sake of strength" or "for the purpose of vigor." In everyday life, this can be associated with the pursuit of physical fitness and mental resilience, emphasizing the need for strength in overcoming challenges.
  • 'bhi: This is a particle that can mean "to" or "for." It indicates direction or purpose, suggesting that the actions taken are aimed at achieving something significant.
  • viśvāni: This word translates to "all" or "universal." It signifies inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings. In a practical sense, this can be seen in community efforts where collective action leads to greater good, such as environmental conservation initiatives.
  • kāvyā: This term means "poetic" or "creative." It highlights the importance of creativity in life. Real-life examples include the arts, literature, and music, which enrich human experience and foster emotional connections.
  • tvaṁ: This means "you," indicating a direct address to a divine or higher power. In a personal context, it can represent the individual's relationship with the divine or the universe, encouraging introspection and personal growth.
  • samudram: This translates to "ocean" or "sea." The ocean symbolizes vastness and depth, representing the infinite possibilities in life. It can also relate to the idea of emotional depth and the need to navigate through life's challenges, much like sailing through the sea.
  • prathamo: This means "first" or "foremost." It emphasizes the importance of prioritizing certain values or actions in life, such as love, compassion, and integrity, which should come first in our interactions with others.
  • vi dhārayo: This phrase can be interpreted as "you hold" or "you support." It suggests the idea of support and stability, akin to the role of family and friends in providing emotional and practical support during difficult times.
  • devebhyaḥ: This means "to the gods" or "to the divine." It reflects the importance of acknowledging and respecting higher powers or forces in life, which can be seen in various cultural practices, such as rituals and prayers.
  • soma: This term refers to a sacred drink or elixir, symbolizing nourishment and vitality. In modern life, this can relate to the importance of healthy living and the consumption of nourishing foods that promote well-being.
  • matsaraḥ: This word means "jealousy" or "envy." It serves as a reminder to avoid negative emotions that can hinder personal growth and relationships. In real life, fostering a mindset of gratitude and appreciation can help combat feelings of jealousy.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of purification, strength, creativity, and support from the divine while warning against negative emotions like jealousy. Its significance lies in its holistic approach to life, encouraging individuals to seek balance and harmony in their personal and communal lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire practices that promote mental clarity, physical health, and emotional well-being, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and interconnected existence.
