मन्त्र: स्तुषे जनं सुव्रतं नव्यसीभिर गीरभिर मित्रावरुणा सुम्नयन्ता |त आ गमन्तु त इह श्रुवन्तु सुख्षत्रासो वरुणो मित्रो अग्निः ||
stuṣe janaṁ suvrataṁ navyasībhir gīrbhir mitrāvaruṇā sumnayantā | ta ā gamantu ta iha śruvantu sukṣatrāso varuṇo mitro agniḥ ||
  • stuṣe janaṁ suvrataṁ: This phrase can be translated as "to the praised people, the ones with good vows." Here, "stuṣe" refers to praise or commendation, while "janaṁ" means people or community. "Suvrataṁ" signifies those who are committed to righteous conduct or good vows.
  • navyasībhir gīrbhir: This translates to "with new and fresh hymns." "Navyasībhir" indicates something that is new or innovative, and "gīrbhir" refers to hymns or songs. This suggests the importance of fresh expressions of devotion and creativity in spiritual practices.
  • mitrāvaruṇā sumnayantā: This means "may the friends Mitra and Varuna bring auspiciousness." "Mitra" represents friendship and harmony, while "Varuna" is associated with cosmic order and moral authority. Together, they symbolize the balance of social and cosmic laws.
  • ta ā gamantu: This translates to "may they come here." It is an invocation for the divine presence to manifest in the physical realm, emphasizing the connection between the spiritual and material worlds.
  • ta iha śruvantu: This means "may they listen here." It signifies the importance of being receptive to divine guidance and wisdom, encouraging individuals to be attentive to spiritual teachings.
  • sukṣatrāso varuṇo mitro agniḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the auspicious ones, Varuna, Mitra, and Agni." "Sukṣatrāso" refers to those who are auspicious or favorable, while "Agni" represents fire, which is a symbol of transformation and purification. This highlights the significance of these deities in bringing about positive change and enlightenment.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of community, righteousness, and divine presence in our lives. It encourages individuals to engage in praise and devotion, fostering a sense of harmony and auspiciousness. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as community gatherings, rituals, or personal reflections where individuals seek to connect with higher principles and values. By invoking the qualities of Mitra, Varuna, and Agni, one can aspire to create a balanced and harmonious life, promoting both personal growth and collective well-being.
