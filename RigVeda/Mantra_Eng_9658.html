आयं ते अस्म्य उप मेह्य अरवाङ् प्रतिचीनाḥ सहुरे विश्वधायः |मन्यो वज्रिन्न अभि मां आ ववृत्स्व हनावा दस्यूम् उता बोध्य आपेः ||
ayaṁ te asmy upa mehy arvāṅ pratīcīnaḥ sahure viśvadhāyaḥ | manyo vajrinn abhi mām ā vavṛtsva hanāva dasyūm̐r uta bodhy āpeḥ ||
  • ayaṁ te asmy - This phrase translates to "I am yours." It signifies a sense of belonging and devotion. In real life, this can be seen in relationships where individuals express their commitment to one another, such as in marriages or close friendships.
  • upa mehy - This means "come near to me." It reflects a desire for closeness and connection. For example, in a professional setting, a leader may seek to connect with their team to foster collaboration and unity.
  • arvāṅ pratīcīnaḥ - This can be interpreted as "the one who is ancient and wise." It suggests the importance of learning from the past. In real life, this is akin to seeking guidance from elders or mentors who have valuable life experiences to share.
  • sahure viśvadhāyaḥ - This phrase means "the one who is strong and supports all." It emphasizes the idea of strength in community. For instance, in a neighborhood, residents may come together to support each other during difficult times, showcasing collective strength.
  • manyo vajrinn - This translates to "with a mind like a thunderbolt." It symbolizes clarity of thought and decisiveness. In real life, this can be seen in leaders who make bold decisions that lead to significant changes in their organizations or communities.
  • abhi mām ā vavṛtsva - This means "protect me." It expresses a plea for safety and security. In everyday life, this can be reflected in the way parents protect their children or how communities come together to ensure the safety of their members.
  • hanāva dasyūm̐r - This translates to "to defeat the enemies." It signifies overcoming obstacles and challenges. In a real-world context, this can relate to individuals or groups fighting against injustice or adversity, such as activists working for social change.
  • uta bodhy āpeḥ - This means "and make me aware." It highlights the importance of knowledge and awareness. In practical terms, this can be seen in educational initiatives that aim to enlighten individuals about their rights and responsibilities.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of devotion, connection, wisdom, strength, decisiveness, protection, overcoming challenges, and the pursuit of knowledge. Its significance lies in the holistic approach it advocates for personal and communal growth. In real life, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as fostering strong relationships, building supportive communities, making informed decisions, and striving for justice and awareness. By embodying these values, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and enlightened society.
