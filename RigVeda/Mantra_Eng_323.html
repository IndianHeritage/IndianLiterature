शिप्रिन वाजानाम् पते शचिवस तव दंशना |आ तु न इन्द्र शंसय गोष्व अश्वेषु शुभ्रिषु सहस्रेṣu तुवीमघ ||
śiprin vājānām pate śacīvas tava daṁsanā | ā tū na indra śaṁsaya goṣv aśveṣu śubhriṣu sahasreṣu tuvīmagha ||
  • śiprin vājānām pate: This phrase can be translated as "O Lord of the steeds" or "O Master of the horses." In ancient Vedic society, horses were symbols of power, wealth, and speed. They were essential for transportation and warfare. The invocation of the "Lord of the steeds" signifies a request for strength and support in endeavors that require speed and agility.
  • śacīvas tava daṁsanā: This translates to "by your powerful bite." Here, "bite" can be interpreted as a metaphor for the strength and impact of the divine. It suggests that the blessings or actions of the deity can have a profound effect on the world, much like a powerful bite can change the course of events.
  • ā tū na indra śaṁsaya: This means "do not hesitate, O Indra." Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and war. The phrase expresses a plea for the deity to act decisively and without delay, highlighting the importance of timely intervention in critical situations.
  • goṣv aśveṣu: This translates to "among the cows and horses." Cows and horses were vital to the agrarian society of the Vedic period, representing sustenance and strength. This line emphasizes the interconnectedness of life and the need for divine blessings in all aspects of existence.
  • śubhriṣu sahasreṣu: This means "in the thousands of auspicious ones." The term "auspicious" refers to favorable conditions or outcomes. This line suggests that the blessings sought are not just for the individual but extend to the community, ensuring prosperity and well-being for many.
  • tuvīmagha: This can be interpreted as "we seek your favor." It is a direct appeal to the deity for blessings, guidance, and support in various endeavors, whether personal or communal.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for divine intervention and support in various aspects of life, particularly in endeavors that require strength, speed, and auspiciousness. In real-life scenarios, this can be applicable in situations such as starting a new venture, seeking success in competitions, or even during significant life events like weddings or festivals. The invocation of divine blessings serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking guidance from higher powers in our pursuits. This mantra encourages individuals to act with confidence and to trust in the support of the divine in their journeys.
