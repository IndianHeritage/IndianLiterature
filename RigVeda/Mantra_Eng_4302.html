दश मासाञ्छ चशयानः कुमारो अधि मातरि। निर्इतु जीवो अक्षतो जीवो जीवन्त्याऽधि॥
daśa māsāñ chaśayānaḥ kumāro adhi mātari | niraitu jīvo akṣato jīvo jīvantyā adhi ||
  • Mantra: "daśa māsāñ chaśayānaḥ kumāro adhi mātari | niraitu jīvo akṣato jīvo jīvantyā adhi ||"
  • Translation: "The child, resting for ten months in the womb, may he come forth unharmed, may he live long and thrive."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • daśa: ten
    • māsāñ: months
    • chaśayānaḥ: resting or lying down
    • kumāro: child or infant
    • adhi: upon or in
    • mātari: womb or mother
    • niraitu: may he come forth or emerge
    • jīvo: living or alive
    • akṣato: unharmed or uninjured
    • jīvantyā: may he live long

  • Context and Significance:
    • This mantra is often recited during pregnancy and childbirth, emphasizing the importance of a safe delivery and the well-being of the child.
    • It reflects the deep-rooted cultural belief in the sanctity of life and the protective wishes for the newborn.
    • In real life, this mantra can be seen as a prayer for health and safety, resonating with the hopes of parents for their child's future.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Expectant parents may recite this mantra during prenatal classes or rituals, seeking blessings for a healthy pregnancy.
    • In hospitals, healthcare providers may encourage families to engage in positive affirmations and prayers, similar to this mantra, to foster a supportive environment for childbirth.
    • Communities may hold ceremonies where this mantra is chanted, reinforcing the collective hope for the safety and prosperity of new life.

  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the fragility of life and the collective responsibility to nurture and protect the young.
    • It highlights the cultural significance of childbirth and the universal desire for health and longevity, making it relevant across various cultures and societies.
    • Ultimately, this mantra encapsulates the essence of hope, love, and the sacred bond between a mother and her child, applicable in both spiritual and practical realms of life.
