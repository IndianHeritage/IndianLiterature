आ नः सोम संयतम पिप्युषीम इषम् इन्दो पवस्व पवमानो अस्रिधम् |या नो दोहते त्रिर अहन्न असश्चुषी क्षुमद वाजवन मधुमत सुवीर्यम् ||
ā naḥ soma saṁyatam pipyuṣīm iṣam indo pavasva pavamāno asridham | yā no dohate trir ahann asaścuṣī kṣumad vājavan madhumat suvīryam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā naḥ: "to us" or "for us"
    • soma: "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration
    • saṁyatam: "well-prepared" or "well-controlled"
    • pipyuṣīm: "nectar-like" or "sweet"
    • iṣam: "power" or "strength"
    • indo: "O Indra," referring to the king of gods in Vedic mythology
    • pavasva: "may you purify" or "may you cleanse"
    • pavamāno: "as you flow" or "as you move"
    • asridham: "to the one who is worthy" or "to the deserving"
    • yā: "who" or "that which"
    • no: "our"
    • dohate: "nourishes" or "feeds"
    • trir: "three times"
    • ahann: "strikes" or "defeats"
    • asaścuṣī: "the one who is without fear" or "the fearless one"
    • kṣumad: "the one who is hungry" or "the needy"
    • vājavan: "the one who is strong" or "the powerful one"
    • madhumat: "sweet" or "pleasant"
    • suvīryam: "of great strength" or "of excellent power"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine essence of Soma, asking for its purifying and nourishing qualities.
    • It emphasizes the importance of strength and power, both in a physical and spiritual sense.
    • The reference to Indra highlights the need for divine support in overcoming challenges and fears.
    • The mantra suggests that through divine blessings, one can achieve success and nourishment in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as preparing for an important exam or presentation, individuals often seek inspiration and strength, akin to the blessings of Soma.
    • In times of need, people may turn to their faith or community for support, reflecting the mantra's call for nourishment and strength.
    • In personal development, individuals may strive to overcome fears and insecurities, drawing on inner strength and divine guidance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine support and nourishment in our lives.
    • It teaches us to cultivate strength and resilience, both in facing external challenges and in our internal struggles.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles of this mantra can be applied in personal growth, community support, and spiritual practices, reminding us of the interconnectedness of strength, nourishment, and divine grace.
