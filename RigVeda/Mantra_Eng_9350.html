यो अदधाज् ज्योतिषि ज्योतिरन्तर् यो असृजन् मधुनā सम् मधूनि |अध प्रियं शूषम् इन्द्राय मनमा ब्रह्मकृतो बृहत् उक्थाद् अवाचि ||
yo adadhāj jyotiṣi jyotir antar yo asṛjan madhunā sam madhūni | adha priyaṁ śūṣam indrāya manma brahmakṛto bṛhadukthād avāci ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo - who
    • adadhāj - has placed
    • jyotiṣi - in the lights
    • jyotir - of light
    • antar - within
    • yo - who
    • asṛjan - has created
    • madhunā - with sweetness
    • sam - together
    • madhūni - the sweet things
    • adha - below
    • priyaṁ - dear
    • śūṣam - the dry
    • indrāya - to Indra
    • manma - my mind
    • brahmakṛto - created by Brahman
    • bṛhadukthād - from the great utterance
    • avāci - has spoken
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine light and creation, emphasizing the connection between the creator (Brahman) and the created world.
    • The phrase "who has placed in the lights" suggests the presence of a higher power that illuminates and guides us.
    • The reference to sweetness indicates the beauty and joy found in creation, which can be seen in nature, relationships, and experiences.
    • Indra, often regarded as the king of gods, symbolizes strength and power, and the mantra calls upon him to bless the speaker's mind with wisdom and clarity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we often seek guidance and clarity in our decisions. This mantra can be a reminder to look for the 'light' or wisdom in challenging situations.
    • The sweetness mentioned can be reflected in moments of joy, such as spending time with loved ones or enjoying nature, reminding us to appreciate the beauty around us.
    • In times of struggle, invoking the strength of Indra can inspire us to overcome obstacles and find resilience within ourselves.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine guidance, the beauty of creation, and the strength we can draw from higher powers.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where we seek clarity, joy, and resilience, serving as a reminder to connect with the divine and appreciate the sweetness of life.
    • By reflecting on this mantra, individuals can cultivate a deeper understanding of their place in the universe and the interconnectedness of all beings.
