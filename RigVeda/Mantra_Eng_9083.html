तेभ्यो गोधाऽयथं कर्षद एतद् ये ब्रह्मणाḥ प्रतिपीयन्त्यन्नैः |सिमा उक्ष्णोऽवसृष्टाम् अदन्ति स्वयम् बलानि तन् वः श्रिणानाः ||
tebhyo godhā ayathaṁ karṣad etad ye brahmaṇaḥ pratipīyanty annaiḥ | sima ukṣṇo 'vasṛṣṭām̐ adanti svayam balāni tanvaḥ śṛṇānāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tebhyo: from them
    • godhā: the cows
    • ayathaṁ: as they are
    • karṣad: he who cultivates
    • etad: this
    • ye: those who
    • brahmaṇaḥ: of the wise or learned
    • pratipīyanty: who partake
    • annaiḥ: with food
    • sima: the boundary
    • ukṣṇo: of the strong
    • 'avasṛṣṭām̐: that which is not released
    • adanti: they consume
    • svayam: by themselves
    • balāni: the strengths
    • tanvaḥ: of the body
    • śṛṇānāḥ: those who listen
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the relationship between nature, nourishment, and the wise.
    • The "cows" symbolize abundance and the nurturing aspect of nature, which provides sustenance.
    • The phrase "those who partake with food" emphasizes the importance of consuming what is provided by nature wisely.
    • The "boundary of the strong" suggests the limits within which one should operate, respecting the natural order.
    • The mantra encourages individuals to listen and learn from the strengths of their surroundings and the wisdom of the learned.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers who respect the land and its resources often yield better crops, reflecting the idea of "cultivating" wisely.
    • In modern society, sustainable practices in food production and consumption echo the mantra's call for wise partaking of nature's offerings.
    • Listening to elders and knowledgeable individuals in a community can lead to better decision-making, akin to "those who listen" gaining strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of harmony with nature and the wisdom of learned individuals.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from sustainable agriculture to community learning.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us to respect the resources we have, listen to wisdom, and cultivate our lives in a balanced manner.
