भूरिद इन्द्रस्य वीर्यं व्याख्यं अभ्यायति |राधस ते दस्यवे वृक ||
bhūrīd indrasya vīrya1ṁ vy akhyam abhy āyati | rādhas te dasyave vṛka ||
  • Mantra: "bhūrīd indrasya vīryaṁ vy akhyam abhy āyati | rādhas te dasyave vṛka ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bhūrīd: Abundant or rich
    • indrasya: Of Indra (the king of gods in Vedic tradition)
    • vīryaṁ: Strength or power
    • vy akhyam: That which is known or revealed
    • abhy āyati: Comes forth or approaches
    • rādhas: Prosperity or wealth
    • te: Your
    • dasyave: To the one who is devoted or dedicated
    • vṛka: Wolf or a metaphor for a powerful being
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the abundant strength of Indra, which is revealed and comes forth.
    • It emphasizes the importance of prosperity and dedication, suggesting that those who are devoted will receive the strength and support of Indra.
    • The reference to "vṛka" (wolf) can symbolize a powerful ally or protector, indicating that strength can come from unexpected sources.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team that works diligently and is dedicated to their goals may find that they receive support from unexpected allies, such as mentors or sponsors, much like the strength of Indra.
    • In personal life, individuals who show dedication to their community or family often find that they are supported by those around them, reflecting the abundance of strength and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of strength, dedication, and the support that comes from being devoted to a cause or community.
    • It teaches us that by being dedicated and working towards our goals, we can attract strength and prosperity into our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, professional endeavors, or community involvement, the principles of this mantra can guide individuals to seek and recognize the support that surrounds them.
