प्र यद वस त्रिष्टुभम् इषम् मरुतो विप्रो अक्षरत् |वि पर्वतेषु राजथ ||
pra yad vas triṣṭubham iṣam maruto vipro akṣarat | vi parvateṣu rājatha ||
  • Mantra: "pra yad vas triṣṭubham iṣam maruto vipro akṣarat | vi parvateṣu rājatha ||"
  • Translation: "Wherever the Maruts (storm deities) are, may the wise (vipra) be endowed with the power of the Triṣṭubh (a meter of Vedic poetry) and may they rule over the mountains."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra: A prefix meaning 'before' or 'in front of', indicating a sense of priority or importance.
    • yad: Meaning 'where' or 'that', referring to a specific location or condition.
    • vas: Meaning 'to dwell' or 'to reside', indicating a state of being or existence.
    • triṣṭubham: Referring to a specific meter in Vedic poetry, symbolizing structure and harmony.
    • iṣam: Meaning 'power' or 'strength', indicating the ability to influence or control.
    • maruto: Referring to the Maruts, the storm deities associated with wind and storms, representing dynamic energy.
    • vipro: Meaning 'wise' or 'intelligent', referring to those who possess knowledge and insight.
    • akṣarat: Meaning 'imperishable' or 'eternal', indicating something that cannot be destroyed.
    • vi: A prefix meaning 'in' or 'among', indicating presence within a certain context.
    • parvateṣu: Meaning 'in the mountains', referring to elevated places or challenges.
    • rājatha: Meaning 'may you rule' or 'may you govern', indicating authority and leadership.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The Maruts can be seen as a metaphor for the challenges and dynamic forces in life, such as natural disasters or personal struggles. Just as the Maruts bring storms, life brings challenges that require wisdom and strength to navigate.
    • The concept of 'vipra' emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in overcoming obstacles. For instance, a leader who is well-informed and wise can guide their community through difficult times, much like a wise elder in a village.
    • The reference to 'mountains' symbolizes the challenges we face in life. For example, climbing a mountain can represent achieving personal goals or overcoming significant hurdles, requiring both physical and mental strength.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of wisdom and strength in the face of challenges. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and to harness their inner power to navigate life's storms.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in leadership, personal development, and community resilience. It serves as a reminder that with wisdom and strength, one can overcome any obstacle, much like a wise leader guiding their people through turbulent times.
    • Ultimately, the mantra inspires us to embrace challenges as opportunities for growth and to lead with wisdom in our personal and communal lives.
