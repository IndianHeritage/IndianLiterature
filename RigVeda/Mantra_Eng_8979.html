मन्त्र: य उदानड व्ययनं य उदानट परायणम् |आवर्तनं निवर्तनम् अपि गोपा निवारताम ||
ya udānaḍ vyayanaṁ ya udānaṭ parāyaṇam | āvartanaṁ nivartanam api gopā ni vartatām ||
  • Mantra: "ya udānaḍ vyayanaṁ ya udānaṭ parāyaṇam | āvartanaṁ nivartanam api gopā ni vartatām ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who/that
    • udānaḍ: upward movement or elevation
    • vyayanaṁ: downward movement or descent
    • udānaṭ: that which elevates
    • parāyaṇam: the ultimate goal or destination
    • āvartanaṁ: turning back or returning
    • nivartanam: cessation or stopping
    • api: also
    • gopā: the protectors or guardians
    • ni: not
    • vartatām: let it happen or let it be
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the duality of existence, represented by the upward and downward movements. It reflects the journey of life, where one seeks elevation (spiritual or material) while also experiencing descent (challenges or failures).
    • The phrase "udānaḍ vyayanaṁ" can be understood as the balance between aspirations and the realities of life. For example, in a career, one may strive for promotion (upward movement) while facing setbacks (downward movement).
    • "Parāyaṇam" signifies the ultimate goal, which can be interpreted as personal fulfillment or enlightenment. In real life, this could relate to achieving one's dreams or finding inner peace.
    • The terms "āvartanaṁ" and "nivartanam" suggest the importance of reflection and the ability to return to one's roots or values. For instance, after a period of struggle, one might return to their core beliefs or family for support.
    • The mention of "gopā" indicates the role of guardians or protectors in our lives, such as mentors, family, or friends who guide us through challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the cyclical nature of life, where one must navigate between aspirations and challenges, always seeking guidance and returning to one's core values.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us to maintain balance in our pursuits, to reflect on our journeys, and to appreciate the support systems around us.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal development, career planning, and even in spiritual practices, encouraging individuals to embrace both their highs and lows while striving for their ultimate goals.
