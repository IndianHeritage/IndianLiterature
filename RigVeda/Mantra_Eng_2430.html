कनिक्रदज जनुषम् प्रब्रुवाण इयर्ति वाचम् अरितेव नावम् |सुमङ्गलश च शकुने भवासि मा त्वा का चिद अभिभा विश्व्या विदत ||
kanikradaj januṣam prabruvāṇa iyarti vācam ariteva nāvam | sumaṅgalaś ca śakune bhavāsi mā tvā kā cid abhibhā viśvyā vidat ||
  • kanikradaj: This term can be interpreted as "one who is born from the seed" or "the one who is created." It signifies the origin of life and the potential that lies within every being.
  • januṣam: This word refers to "the limbs" or "the body." It emphasizes the physical aspect of existence and the importance of our physical form in the world.
  • prabruvāṇa: This means "speaking" or "expressing." It highlights the power of communication and the ability to convey thoughts and emotions.
  • iyarti: This term can be understood as "to move" or "to progress." It signifies the journey of life and the continuous movement towards goals and aspirations.
  • vācam: This translates to "speech" or "voice." It underscores the significance of words and their impact on our lives and relationships.
  • ariteva: This means "like a boat." It symbolizes the journey of life, where we navigate through challenges and opportunities, much like a boat sailing through water.
  • nāvam: This word means "boat" or "vessel." It represents the means by which we traverse the waters of life, emphasizing the importance of guidance and support.
  • sumaṅgalaś: This term refers to "auspiciousness" or "good fortune." It signifies the positive outcomes and blessings that come from righteous actions.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," connecting ideas and concepts.
  • śakune: This word means "omen" or "auspicious sign." It highlights the importance of recognizing signs and signals in our lives that guide us towards the right path.
  • bhavāsi: This translates to "you become" or "you are." It emphasizes the transformative power of our actions and thoughts.
  • mā: This means "not" or "do not." It serves as a negation, indicating a desire to avoid certain outcomes.
  • tvā: This means "you," referring to the individual or the subject of the mantra.
  • kā cid: This translates to "anyone" or "any." It signifies the universality of the message.
  • abhibhā: This means "to overcome" or "to conquer." It emphasizes the strength and resilience needed to face challenges.
  • viśvyā: This term means "all" or "universal." It signifies inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • vidat: This translates to "knowledge" or "wisdom." It highlights the importance of understanding and awareness in navigating life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of communication, progress, and the recognition of auspicious signs. It serves as a reminder that we are all on a journey, navigating through the waters of existence with the potential for growth and transformation. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal development, decision-making, and the pursuit of goals. By being aware of the signs around us and harnessing the power of our words and actions, we can create a life filled with auspiciousness and wisdom.
