सुशिल्पे बृहती मही पवमानो वृषण्यति |नक्तोषासां न दर्शते ||
suśilpe bṛhatī mahī pavamāno vṛṣaṇyati | naktoṣāsā na darśate ||
  • suśilpe: This word can be broken down into "su" meaning good or well, and "śilpe" meaning craft or art. Together, it refers to the idea of skilled craftsmanship or artistry.
  • bṛhatī: This term means vast or great. It signifies something that is expansive, both in physical and metaphorical senses.
  • mahī: This word translates to earth or the great expanse. It represents the physical world and the nurturing aspect of nature.
  • pavamāno: This term means the purifying or the one that brings purity. It can refer to the wind or air, which is often seen as a cleansing force.
  • vṛṣaṇyati: This word can be interpreted as the one who brings forth or produces. It is often associated with fertility and abundance.
  • naktoṣāsā: This term refers to the night or darkness. It can symbolize ignorance or the unknown.
  • na darśate: This phrase means "does not reveal" or "does not show." It indicates a lack of visibility or understanding.

The mantra can be interpreted as a reflection on the relationship between skilled craftsmanship, the vastness of the earth, and the purifying forces of nature. It suggests that while the earth is abundant and nurturing, there are aspects of life (symbolized by night) that remain hidden or unrecognized.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Art and Craftsmanship: Just as "suśilpe" emphasizes the importance of skilled craftsmanship, it can be seen in the work of artisans who create beautiful and functional objects, reminding us of the value of skill and creativity in our daily lives.
  • Environmental Awareness: The mention of "mahī" and "pavamāno" highlights the significance of nature and the environment. In today's world, this can translate into efforts to protect our planet and promote sustainability.
  • Personal Growth: The idea of "naktoṣāsā na darśate" can be related to personal challenges and the journey of self-discovery. It reminds us that there are aspects of ourselves and our lives that may not be immediately visible, encouraging introspection and growth.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of craftsmanship, nature, and the hidden aspects of life. It encourages us to appreciate the skills we possess, to respect and protect our environment, and to seek understanding in the areas of our lives that may not be immediately clear. By embodying these principles, we can lead more fulfilling and meaningful lives.
