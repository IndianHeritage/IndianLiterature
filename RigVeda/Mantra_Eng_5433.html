मन्त्र: सूर्यस्येव वक्षथो ज्योतिर एषां समुद्रस्येव महिमा गभीरः | वातस्येव प्रजवो नान्येन स्तोमो वसिष्ठा अन्वेतवे वः ||
sūryasyeva vakṣatho jyotir eṣāṁ samudrasyeva mahimā gabhīraḥ | vātasyeva prajavo nānyena stomo vasiṣṭhā anvetave vaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sūryasya - of the sun
    • eva - indeed
    • vakṣatho - in the chest
    • jyotir - light
    • eṣāṁ - of these
    • samudrasya - of the ocean
    • mahimā - greatness
    • gabhīraḥ - profound
    • vātasya - of the wind
    • prajavo - progeny
    • nānyena - by none other
    • stomo - praise
    • vasiṣṭhā - the best
    • anvetave - may follow
    • vaḥ - you
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra draws a parallel between the light of the sun and the profound greatness of the ocean, suggesting that just as the sun radiates light, there is a deep and vast greatness in the universe.
    • The mention of the wind and progeny indicates the importance of life and creation, emphasizing that true praise and recognition come from the essence of nature itself.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In our daily lives, we often seek inspiration from nature. For instance, the sun's light can symbolize hope and positivity, encouraging us to pursue our goals with vigor.
    • The ocean's depth can remind us of the vastness of knowledge and experiences that we can explore throughout our lives.
    • Wind, often associated with change, can represent the dynamic nature of life and the importance of adaptability in our personal and professional journeys.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of nature and the human experience, highlighting that we are part of a larger cosmic order.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the light, depth, and change in our lives, encouraging us to seek wisdom and growth.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in moments of reflection, meditation, or when seeking guidance in challenging situations, reminding us of the profound forces at play in our existence.
