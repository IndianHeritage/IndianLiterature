वीतिहोत्रा कृतद्वसू दशस्यन्तामृताया कम् |सम ऊधो रोमा षं हतो देवेषु कृणुतो दु्वः ||
vītihotrā kṛtadvasū daśasyantāmṛtāya kam | sam ūdho romaśaṁ hato deveṣu kṛṇuto duvaḥ ||
  • vītihotrā: This term refers to the one who performs the Vedic rituals, particularly the offerings made to the deities. In real life, this can be likened to individuals who take on the role of leaders or facilitators in various ceremonies or important events, ensuring that traditions are upheld.
  • kṛtadvasū: This word signifies those who have achieved success or prosperity through their actions. In a modern context, this can relate to entrepreneurs or individuals who have worked hard to attain their goals, embodying the spirit of perseverance and dedication.
  • daśasyantāmṛtāya: This phrase can be interpreted as a call for the blessings of immortality or eternal life. In everyday life, this can be associated with the pursuit of knowledge, wisdom, and experiences that leave a lasting impact, much like how influential figures in history are remembered for their contributions.
  • kam: This word translates to "who" or "what," indicating a quest for understanding or seeking answers. In real life, this reflects the human desire to seek purpose and meaning in our actions and existence.
  • sam ūdho: This can be understood as a collective or unified effort. In practical terms, this is akin to teamwork in organizations or communities where individuals come together to achieve a common goal, emphasizing the importance of collaboration.
  • romaśaṁ: This term refers to the hair or the physical form, symbolizing the material aspect of existence. In contemporary life, this can represent our physical bodies and the importance of taking care of our health and well-being.
  • hato: This means "struck" or "defeated," which can signify overcoming challenges or adversities. In real life, this can be seen in stories of resilience where individuals face hardships but emerge stronger, much like athletes who overcome injuries to achieve greatness.
  • deveṣu: This word refers to the deities or divine beings. In a modern context, this can symbolize the higher ideals or values that guide our actions, such as integrity, compassion, and justice.
  • kṛṇuto: This means "to create" or "to make." In real life, this can relate to innovation and creativity, where individuals or groups bring new ideas to life, contributing to societal progress.
  • duvaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "to be fulfilled" or "to achieve." In practical terms, this reflects the human aspiration for success and fulfillment in various aspects of life, whether personal, professional, or spiritual.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of human endeavor, the pursuit of success, and the quest for understanding and fulfillment. It emphasizes the importance of collective effort, resilience in the face of challenges, and the aspiration for higher ideals. In real life, this mantra serves as a reminder to strive for excellence, work together towards common goals, and seek a deeper understanding of our existence and purpose. It is applicable in various scenarios, from personal growth to community development, encouraging individuals to embrace their roles as creators and leaders in their respective fields.
