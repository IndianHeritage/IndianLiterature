इमा रुद्राय स्थिरधन्वने गिरः क्षिप्रेषवे देवाय स्वधावने |अशाल्हाय सहमानाय वेधसे तिग्मायुधाय भरता श्रिणोतु नः ||
imā rudrāya sthiradhanvane giraḥ kṣipreṣave devāya svadhāvne | aṣāḻhāya sahamānāya vedhase tigmāyudhāya bharatā śṛṇotu naḥ ||
  • imā rudrāya: This phrase refers to invoking Rudra, a deity associated with storms, wind, and the hunt. Rudra is often seen as a fierce and protective force.
  • sthiradhanvane: This translates to "the one with a steady bow." It symbolizes strength and stability, suggesting that Rudra is a protector who is always ready to defend.
  • giraḥ: This means "words" or "speech." In this context, it indicates the power of communication and the importance of invoking divine support through prayer or mantra.
  • kṣipreṣave: This can be interpreted as "swift" or "quick." It emphasizes the immediacy of divine assistance and the need for rapid response in times of trouble.
  • devāya: This means "to the divine" or "to the god." It highlights the reverence and respect towards the divine forces that govern the universe.
  • svadhāvne: This translates to "self-moving" or "self-acting." It suggests that the divine energy is dynamic and can act on its own, reflecting the idea of natural forces at work.
  • aṣāḻhāya: This means "to the one who is unyielding" or "firm." It signifies the strength and resilience of the divine, which can inspire humans to be steadfast in their own lives.
  • sahamānāya: This translates to "to the one who bears with us." It indicates a sense of companionship and support from the divine, suggesting that we are not alone in our struggles.
  • vedhase: This means "to the one who knows" or "to the wise." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in navigating life's challenges.
  • tigmāyudhāya: This translates to "to the one with sharp weapons." It symbolizes the power to cut through ignorance and obstacles, representing the divine's ability to protect and guide.
  • bharatā: This can be interpreted as "to the one who is revered" or "to the one who is worthy of respect." It signifies the importance of honoring the divine in our lives.
  • śṛṇotu naḥ: This means "may he listen to us." It is a plea for divine attention and support, highlighting the human desire for connection with the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation to Rudra, seeking protection, guidance, and support in times of need. It emphasizes the importance of divine assistance in overcoming challenges and the need for resilience and wisdom in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during difficult times, such as facing personal crises, health issues, or challenges in relationships. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of connection with the divine, fostering hope and strength to navigate their struggles. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to remind us of the protective and guiding forces in the universe, encouraging us to seek support and remain steadfast in our endeavors.
