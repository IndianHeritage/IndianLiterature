यस् ते द्रप्सः स्कन्दति यस् ते अंशुर बाहुच्युतो धिषणायाः उपस्थात् |अध्वर्योः वा परि वा यः पवित्रात् तं ते जुहोमि मनसा वषट्कृतम् ||
yas te drapsaḥ skandati yas te aṁśur bāhucyuto dhiṣaṇāyā upasthāt | adhvaryor vā pari vā yaḥ pavitrāt taṁ te juhomi manasā vaṣaṭkṛtam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • drapsaḥ: desires
    • skandati: fulfills
    • aṁśur: the light
    • bāhucyuto: from the arms
    • dhiṣaṇāyā: of the one who is wise
    • upasthāt: approaches
    • adhvaryor: of the priests
    • vā: or
    • pari: around
    • yaḥ: who
    • pavitrāt: from the pure
    • taṁ: him
    • te: your
    • juhomi: I offer
    • manasā: with my mind
    • vaṣaṭkṛtam: invoked
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine light and wisdom that fulfills desires.
    • It emphasizes the importance of purity and intention in offerings.
    • The phrase "adhvaryor vā" suggests the role of priests in rituals, highlighting the communal aspect of spiritual practices.
    • The invocation "vaṣaṭkṛtam" indicates a call to the divine, showing the significance of mental focus in spiritual offerings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, when someone sets a goal (like pursuing a career), they often visualize their success and maintain a positive mindset, akin to invoking the divine light for guidance.
    • In community rituals, such as weddings or festivals, the role of priests and the collective intention of the participants reflect the essence of this mantra, where purity and focus are essential for the success of the event.
    • In meditation practices, individuals often focus their minds on a single intention or desire, paralleling the invocation of "vaṣaṭkṛtam" to manifest their aspirations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the power of intention, purity, and communal effort in achieving desires and spiritual fulfillment.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal aspirations to communal rituals, emphasizing the importance of mental focus and purity in our actions.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that our thoughts and intentions can invoke divine support in our endeavors, guiding us towards fulfillment and wisdom.
