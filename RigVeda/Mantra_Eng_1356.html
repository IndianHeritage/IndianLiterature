मन्त्र: कद इत्तः नृंहः पात्रं देवयतां श्रवद गीरः अंगिरसां तुरण्यन | प्र यद आनड विषा आ हरम्यस्योऽरु क्रंसते अध्वरे यजत्रः ||
kad itthā nṝm̐ḥ pātraṁ devayatāṁ śravad giro aṅgirasāṁ turaṇyan | pra yad ānaḍ viśa ā harmyasyoru kraṁsate adhvare yajatraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kad: when
    • itthā: here
    • nṝm̐ḥ: men or beings
    • pātraṁ: vessel or container
    • devayatāṁ: of the divine or godly
    • śravad: flowing or abundant
    • giro: speech or words
    • aṅgirasāṁ: of the Angirasas (a group of sages)
    • turaṇyan: swift or quick
    • pra: towards
    • yad: that which
    • ānaḍ: bliss or joy
    • viśa: of the universe or all
    • ā: in
    • harmyasy: of the abode or home
    • oru: of the thighs or strength
    • kraṁsate: is drawn or attracted
    • adhvare: in the sacrifice or ritual
    • yajatraḥ: of the worshippers or those who perform the sacrifice
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the connection between divine beings and humans, emphasizing the importance of communication and offerings in rituals.
    • The term "pātraṁ" suggests that humans are vessels for divine energy, and through their actions and words, they can channel this energy.
    • The reference to "aṅgirasāṁ" indicates the wisdom of ancient sages, suggesting that their teachings guide the way for modern practitioners.
    • The phrase "ānaḍ viśa" implies that the joy and bliss of the universe can be accessed through proper rituals and offerings.
    • The mantra encourages individuals to be swift and proactive in their spiritual practices, as indicated by "turaṇyan."
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various spiritual practices, such as meditation, where individuals seek to connect with the divine and channel positive energy.
    • During rituals or ceremonies, participants can reflect on their role as vessels for divine energy, enhancing their focus and intention.
    • In community gatherings, the teachings of ancient sages can inspire collective actions that promote harmony and joy, aligning with the mantra's message.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of human-divine interaction through rituals and the importance of being a vessel for divine energy.
    • It serves as a reminder that through our words and actions, we can attract joy and bliss into our lives and the lives of others.
    • By understanding and applying the teachings of ancient wisdom, individuals can enhance their spiritual practices and contribute positively to their communities.
