अभि वहनाय ऊतये नूषत प्रशस्तये |न देव विव्रता हरि ऋतस्य यत ||
abhi vahnaya ūtaye 'nūṣata praśastaye | na deva vivratā harī ṛtasya yat ||
  • Mantra: "abhi vahnaya ūtaye 'nūṣata praśastaye | na deva vivratā harī ṛtasya yat ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, for
    • vahnaya: fire, light
    • ūtaye: for the purpose of, for the benefit of
    • 'nūṣata: may we attain, may we achieve
    • praśastaye: for praise, for glory
    • na: not
    • deva: gods, divine beings
    • vivratā: opened, revealed
    • harī: the one who is auspicious, often referring to Vishnu
    • ṛtasya: of truth, of order
    • yat: which, that
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine light and fire, seeking its blessings for achieving glory and praise.
    • It emphasizes the importance of truth and order in our lives, suggesting that divine beings reveal the path of righteousness.
    • The phrase "na deva vivratā harī ṛtasya yat" indicates that the divine does not reveal the truth to those who are not aligned with righteousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied to the pursuit of ethical practices. For instance, a company that prioritizes transparency and integrity in its operations is more likely to gain respect and recognition.
    • In personal relationships, seeking the truth and maintaining honesty can lead to stronger bonds and mutual respect, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In education, students who strive for knowledge and truth, rather than shortcuts, often achieve greater success and fulfillment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of seeking truth and righteousness in all aspects of life.
    • It encourages individuals to align their actions with ethical principles, thereby attracting divine blessings and recognition.
    • By embodying the values of honesty and integrity, one can navigate life's challenges more effectively and achieve lasting success.
