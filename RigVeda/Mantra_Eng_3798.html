प्र विश्वसामन्न अतिवद आर्चा पावकशोचिषे |यो अध्वरिषु ईड्यो होता मन्द्रतमो विशि ||
pra viśvasāmann atrivad arcā pāvakaśociṣe | yo adhvareṣv īḍyo hotā mandratamo viśi ||
  • pra viśvasāmann atrivad arcā pāvakaśociṣe
    • pra - This prefix often means "forth" or "before," indicating a sense of initiation or beginning.
    • viśvasāmann - This term can be broken down into "viśva," meaning "all" or "universal," and "sāmann," which refers to "harmony" or "agreement." Together, it suggests a universal harmony or collective agreement.
    • atrivad - This word can be interpreted as "like this" or "in this manner," indicating a way of doing things or a method.
    • arcā - This refers to "worship" or "adoration," often in the context of rituals or offerings to deities.
    • pāvakaśociṣe - This compound word combines "pāvaka," meaning "purifier" or "fire," and "śociṣe," which means "shining" or "illuminating." It signifies the purifying and illuminating nature of fire.
  • yo adhvareṣv īḍyo hotā mandratamo viśi
    • yo - This means "who" or "that," referring to a person or entity.
    • adhvareṣv - This term relates to "sacrificial rites" or "rituals," indicating the context of worship and offerings.
    • īḍyo - This means "worthy of praise" or "adorable," referring to the qualities of the deity or the essence being worshipped.
    • hotā - This refers to the "priest" or "invoker," the one who performs the rituals and calls upon the divine.
    • mandratamo - This term can be understood as "the most excellent" or "the best," indicating a high status or quality.
    • viśi - This means "to distinguish" or "to separate," suggesting a sense of uniqueness or specialness.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of universal harmony and the purifying power of fire in rituals. It highlights the role of the priest as a mediator between the divine and the worshippers, underscoring the significance of rituals in connecting with the spiritual realm. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as community gatherings, celebrations, and personal spiritual practices where individuals seek to create harmony and purification in their lives. The mantra serves as a reminder of the sacredness of rituals and the importance of intention in worship, encouraging individuals to approach their spiritual practices with reverence and mindfulness.
