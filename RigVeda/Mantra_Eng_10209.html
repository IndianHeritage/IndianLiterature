अहं राष्ट्री संगमनी वसूनां चिकीतुशी प्रथमायज्ञियानाम् |तां मा देवाः व्य अदधुः पुरुत्रा भूरिष्ठात्राम् भूय आवेशयन्तीम् ||
ahaṁ rāṣṭrī saṁgamanī vasūnāṁ cikituṣī prathamā yajñiyānām | tām mā devā vy adadhuḥ purutrā bhūristhātrām bhūry āveśayantīm ||
  • ahaṁ rāṣṭrī: This phrase translates to "I am the ruler" or "I am the sovereign." It signifies the importance of leadership and governance in society.
  • saṁgamanī: This means "the one who brings together" or "the unifier." It emphasizes the role of a leader in uniting people for a common purpose.
  • vasūnāṁ: This refers to "the wealth" or "the resources." It highlights the significance of material and spiritual wealth in sustaining a community.
  • cikituṣī: This word means "the one who is wise" or "the one who knows." It points to the necessity of wisdom in leadership and decision-making.
  • prathamā: This translates to "the first" or "the foremost." It indicates the priority of certain values or actions in the context of governance and society.
  • yajñiyānām: This refers to "the sacrificial" or "the ritualistic." It signifies the importance of rituals and sacrifices in maintaining harmony and balance in society.
  • tām mā devā: This means "that which the gods have given." It suggests a divine blessing or guidance in leadership and governance.
  • vy adadhuḥ: This translates to "they have bestowed" or "they have granted." It indicates the act of giving or providing support to the leader.
  • purutrā: This means "in all directions" or "everywhere." It signifies the universal application of the principles being discussed.
  • bhūristhātrām: This translates to "the one who is abundant" or "the one who has plenty." It emphasizes the importance of abundance and prosperity in a community.
  • bhūry: This means "much" or "many." It reinforces the idea of abundance and the need for resources in governance.
  • āveśayantīm: This translates to "the one who inspires" or "the one who fills with energy." It highlights the role of a leader in motivating and energizing the community.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of effective leadership and governance. It emphasizes the qualities of unity, wisdom, and abundance that a leader must embody to guide their community successfully. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to various contexts, such as political leadership, community organization, or even in corporate environments where leaders are expected to inspire and unify their teams. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that true leadership is not just about authority but also about nurturing and uplifting those one leads, ensuring that resources are wisely managed and that the community thrives as a whole.
