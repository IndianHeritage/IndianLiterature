अध द्युतानः पित्रोः सच्चासामनुत गुह्यं चारु पृष्णेः | मातुष पादे परम एण्ति षड् गोर वृष्णः शोचिषः प्रयतस्य जिव्हा ||
adha dyutānaḥ pitroḥ sacāsāmanuta guhyaṁ cāru pṛśneḥ | mātuṣ pade parame anti ṣad gor vṛṣṇaḥ śociṣaḥ prayatasya jihvā ||
  • Adha dyutānaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "downward shining" or "those who shine below." It suggests a connection to the earth and the grounding aspects of life.
  • Pitroḥ: This word means "of the ancestors" or "of the fathers." It emphasizes the importance of lineage and the wisdom passed down through generations.
  • Sacāsāmanuta: This can be translated as "with truth and harmony." It highlights the significance of living a life aligned with truth and balance.
  • Guhyaṁ: Meaning "hidden" or "secret," this term points to the deeper, often unspoken truths of existence that require introspection to uncover.
  • Cāru pṛśneḥ: This phrase translates to "beautifully radiant." It signifies the beauty found in knowledge and understanding, as well as the illumination that comes from wisdom.
  • Mātuṣ pade: Meaning "in the mother's abode," this emphasizes the nurturing and protective aspects of motherhood, symbolizing safety and comfort.
  • Parame: This word means "supreme" or "ultimate," indicating the highest state of being or consciousness.
  • Anti: This can be interpreted as "to reach" or "to attain," suggesting a journey towards a higher understanding or state of existence.
  • Ṣad gor vṛṣṇaḥ: This phrase translates to "six strong bulls," which can symbolize strength, vitality, and the power of nature.
  • Śociṣaḥ: Meaning "of the shining," this term refers to the brilliance and clarity that comes from knowledge and enlightenment.
  • Prayatasya: This word means "of the one who strives" or "of the diligent," emphasizing the importance of effort and perseverance in achieving one's goals.
  • Jihvā: This translates to "tongue," symbolizing communication, expression, and the power of words in shaping reality.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking truth, wisdom, and strength through the guidance of ancestors and the nurturing aspects of life. It emphasizes the importance of striving for knowledge and understanding while remaining grounded in one's roots. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, family values, and the pursuit of enlightenment. It serves as a reminder to honor our heritage, communicate effectively, and strive for a harmonious existence, ultimately leading to a more fulfilled and meaningful life.
