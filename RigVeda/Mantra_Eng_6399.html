पृदाकुसानुर यजतो गवेक्षण एकः सन्न अभि भूयसः | भूर्णिम अश्वं नयत तुजा पुरो गृभेन्द्रं सोमस्य पीतये ||
pṛdākusānur yajato gaveṣaṇa ekaḥ sann abhi bhūyasaḥ | bhūrṇim aśvaṁ nayat tujā puro gṛbhendraṁ somasya pītaye ||
  • pṛdākusānur: This term can be broken down into "pṛdā" (to be strong or powerful) and "kusānur" (the one who is like a sun). It signifies a powerful entity or force, akin to the sun that provides energy and sustenance.
  • yajato: This word refers to the act of worship or sacrifice. In ancient Vedic culture, yajna (sacrifice) was a central practice, symbolizing the connection between humans and the divine.
  • gaveṣaṇa: This term can be interpreted as the search for or the pursuit of something valuable, often associated with the quest for knowledge or truth.
  • ekaḥ: Meaning "one" or "singular," this word emphasizes unity and the importance of focusing on a singular goal or purpose.
  • sann abhi bhūyasaḥ: This phrase translates to "being present and powerful." It suggests the importance of being grounded and strong in one's actions and intentions.
  • bhūrṇim: This term refers to abundance or prosperity. It signifies the richness of life and the blessings that come from devotion and sacrifice.
  • aśvaṁ: Meaning "horse," this symbolizes strength, speed, and vitality. In Vedic rituals, horses were often used as offerings, representing the power of the sun and the energy of life.
  • nayat: This word means "to lead" or "to guide." It emphasizes the role of leadership and direction in achieving one's goals.
  • tujā: This term can be interpreted as "with your strength" or "by your power," indicating the importance of personal strength and will in the pursuit of goals.
  • puro gṛbhendraṁ: This phrase translates to "the lord of the house" or "the protector of the home." It signifies the importance of safeguarding one's family and community.
  • somasya: Referring to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, it symbolizes divine nourishment and the connection to the spiritual realm.
  • pītaye: This term means "for drinking" or "to consume," indicating the act of receiving blessings or nourishment from the divine.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, unity, and devotion in the pursuit of prosperity and spiritual fulfillment. It highlights the importance of leadership and guidance in achieving one's goals while remaining grounded in one's values. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal development, leadership roles, and community building, reminding individuals to harness their inner strength and work towards a common purpose for the greater good. By engaging in acts of devotion and sacrifice, one can cultivate abundance and spiritual nourishment in their life.
