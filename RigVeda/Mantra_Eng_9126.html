अद्येदुप्राणीदममननिमाहापीवृतोऽधयनमातुरूधः। एमेनमापजरीमायुवानमहेळनवसुःसुमना बभूव।
adyed u prāṇīd amamann imāhāpīvṛto adhayan mātur ūdhaḥ | em enam āpa jarimā yuvānam aheḻan vasuḥ sumanā babhūva ||
  • adyed: This word translates to "today" or "in this moment." It emphasizes the importance of the present time.
  • u: A conjunction meaning "and," linking thoughts together.
  • prāṇīd: Refers to "life" or "breath," symbolizing vitality and existence.
  • amamann: This can be interpreted as "nourishment" or "food," indicating sustenance for life.
  • imāhāpīvṛto: This phrase suggests "surrounded by these," referring to the elements or forces that support life.
  • adhayan: Meaning "to rise" or "to elevate," it signifies growth and progress.
  • mātur: Translates to "of the mother," symbolizing nurturing and care.
  • ūdhaḥ: This word means "upward" or "higher," indicating aspiration and elevation.
  • em: A term that can mean "this" or "that," pointing to a specific focus.
  • enam: Refers to "this" or "him," often used to denote a subject of importance.
  • āpa: Meaning "water," which is essential for life and sustenance.
  • jarimā: This word can be interpreted as "old age" or "decay," representing the natural cycle of life.
  • yuvānam: Translates to "youth" or "young," symbolizing vitality and potential.
  • aheḻan: This can mean "to be strong" or "to be powerful," indicating resilience.
  • vasuḥ: Refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," often associated with abundance.
  • sumanā: Meaning "good mind" or "intellect," it signifies wisdom and clarity of thought.
  • babhūva: This word means "became" or "was," indicating transformation or realization.

The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, nourishment, and the nurturing aspects of existence. It highlights the importance of the present moment and the need for growth and elevation in life. The reference to water signifies the essential nature of sustenance, while the contrast between youth and old age reflects the natural cycle of life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects of personal development and well-being. For instance:

  • In health and nutrition, it reminds us to focus on nourishing our bodies with wholesome food and water, essential for maintaining vitality.
  • In personal growth, it encourages individuals to rise above challenges and aspire for higher goals, much like a plant reaching for sunlight.
  • In relationships, the nurturing aspect of "mother" can be seen in the care and support we provide to loved ones, fostering a sense of community and belonging.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of the present moment, the importance of nourishment—both physical and emotional—and the aspiration for growth and prosperity in life. It encourages us to embrace our journey, recognizing the cycles of youth and age, and to cultivate a good mind for a fulfilling existence.
