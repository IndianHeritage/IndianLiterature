आ याहि वनसा सह गावः सचन्त वर्तनिं यद ऊधभिः ||
ā yāhi vanasā saha gāvaḥ sacanta vartaniṁ yad ūdhabhiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ā yāhi vanasā saha gāvaḥ sacanta vartaniṁ yad ūdhabhiḥ"
  • Translation: "Come here, O cows, together with the forest; may you bring forth the essence that is present in the waters."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā yāhi: "Come here" - A call to gather or unite.
    • vanasā: "with the forest" - Symbolizes nature, wilderness, and the interconnectedness of life.
    • saha: "together" - Emphasizes unity and collaboration.
    • gāvaḥ: "cows" - Represents nourishment, wealth, and sustenance in Vedic culture.
    • sacanta: "bring forth" - A request for manifestation or production.
    • vartaniṁ: "the essence" - Refers to the vital energy or life force.
    • yad: "that" - Indicates a specific quality or element.
    • ūdhabhiḥ: "in the waters" - Suggests purity, life, and the source of sustenance.
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra reflects the deep connection between humans, nature, and animals, particularly cows, which are revered in Vedic culture for their role in agriculture and sustenance.
    • The invocation of cows alongside the forest highlights the importance of biodiversity and the need for harmony between different elements of nature.
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be seen as a call for environmental awareness and sustainable practices, urging people to respect and protect natural resources.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the integration of livestock (like cows) with crop farming can lead to sustainable practices that enhance soil fertility and reduce waste.
    • Community initiatives that promote reforestation and the protection of natural habitats resonate with the essence of this mantra, as they seek to restore balance in ecosystems.
    • In personal life, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate a sense of gratitude for the resources provided by nature and to engage in practices that support ecological health.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all life forms and the importance of nurturing our environment.
    • It encourages a holistic approach to living, where humans, animals, and nature coexist harmoniously.
    • By embracing the principles embodied in this mantra, individuals and communities can work towards a sustainable future that honors the gifts of nature.
