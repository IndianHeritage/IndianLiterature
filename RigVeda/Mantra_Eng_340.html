तथा तद अस्तु सोमपाः सखे वज्रिन तथा कृणु |यथा त उष्मसीष्टये ||
tathā tad astu somapāḥ sakhe vajrin tathā kṛṇu | yathā ta uśmasīṣṭaye ||
  • tathā - This word translates to "thus" or "so," indicating a confirmation or affirmation of what follows.
  • tad - Meaning "that," it refers to something specific that has been previously mentioned or is understood in context.
  • astu - This means "let it be" or "may it be," expressing a wish or desire for something to come to fruition.
  • somapāḥ - This term refers to "those who drink soma," which is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition believed to confer immortality and divine insight.
  • sakhe - Translated as "O friend," this word indicates a sense of camaraderie and connection, suggesting that the speaker is addressing a close companion.
  • vajrin - This word means "the one who wields the thunderbolt," often associated with Indra, the king of gods, symbolizing strength and power.
  • tathā - Again, this word is used to reinforce the previous statement, emphasizing continuity in thought.
  • kṛṇu - Meaning "do" or "make," it is a call to action, urging the listener to take initiative.
  • yathā - This translates to "as" or "in the way that," indicating a comparison or a manner in which something should be done.
  • ta - Meaning "that," it refers back to the subject of the mantra.
  • uśmasīṣṭaye - This term can be broken down into "uśma," meaning "warmth" or "heat," and "īṣṭaye," which refers to "for the purpose of worship" or "for the sake of." Together, it suggests a focus on warmth or energy in a spiritual context.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation, where the speaker is affirming a desire for the blessings of the divine, particularly those associated with strength, friendship, and spiritual insight. The reference to "soma" highlights the importance of divine nourishment and the pursuit of higher knowledge.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Friendship and Support: The invocation of "sakhe" emphasizes the importance of friendship and support in our lives. In times of difficulty, reaching out to friends for help can be a source of strength.
  • Seeking Strength: The reference to "vajrin" can inspire individuals to seek inner strength and resilience when facing challenges, much like how athletes prepare mentally and physically for competitions.
  • Spiritual Growth: The mention of "soma" encourages individuals to seek spiritual nourishment through practices such as meditation, yoga, or community service, which can lead to personal growth and enlightenment.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of friendship, strength, and spiritual pursuit. It encourages individuals to affirm their desires for growth and support, fostering a sense of community and resilience in the face of life's challenges.
