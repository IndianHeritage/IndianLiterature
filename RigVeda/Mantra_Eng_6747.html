आ याह्य आर्य आ परि स्वाहा सोमस्य पीतये |दिवो अमुष्य शासतो दिवं यय दिवावसो ||
ā yāhy arya ā pari svāhā somasya pītaye | divo amuṣya śāsato divaṁ yaya divāvaso ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yāhy: "Come forth" or "Let us go."
    • arya: "Noble" or "worthy."
    • ā pari: "Around" or "from all sides."
    • svāhā: A term used in Vedic rituals, often translated as "Hail!" or "Let it be so."
    • somasya: "Of the Soma," referring to the sacred drink in Vedic rituals.
    • pītaye: "For the drinker" or "for the one who consumes."
    • divo: "Of the sky" or "heaven."
    • amuṣya: "Of this" or "of that."
    • śāsato: "By the command" or "by the rule."
    • divaṁ: "Heaven" or "the celestial realm."
    • yaya: "By which" or "through which."
    • divāvaso: "Living in the day" or "day-dwelling."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine essence of Soma, a symbol of immortality and spiritual enlightenment.
    • The phrase "Come forth, noble one" suggests a call to the divine or higher consciousness to manifest in our lives.
    • The reference to "the drinker" emphasizes the importance of consuming or integrating this divine essence into our daily existence.
    • By mentioning "the sky" and "heaven," the mantra connects earthly experiences with celestial aspirations, highlighting the relationship between the material and spiritual realms.
    • The idea of "living in the day" suggests a focus on awareness and presence in our daily lives, encouraging mindfulness and conscious living.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in practices such as meditation, where individuals seek to connect with their higher selves and the universe.
    • Mindfulness practices, such as yoga or conscious breathing, can be seen as ways to "consume" the divine essence and live in alignment with higher principles.
    • In community gatherings or rituals, invoking the spirit of Soma can symbolize the collective aspiration for harmony, unity, and spiritual growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, urging us to seek the divine in our everyday actions.
    • Its significance lies in the call for spiritual awakening and the integration of higher consciousness into our daily lives.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a deeper sense of purpose, connection, and mindfulness in their journey through life.
