रपत कविर इन्द्रार्कसातौ क्षां दासायोपबरहणीं कः |करत तिस्रो मगवां दानुचित्रा न दुर्योन कुयवाचं मृधि श्रेत ||
rapat kavir indrārkasātau kṣāṁ dāsāyopabarhaṇīṁ kaḥ | karat tisro maghavā dānucitrā ni duryoṇe kuyavācam mṛdhi śret ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • rapat: to flow or to move
    • kavir: the poet or the wise one
    • indrārkasātau: Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, and the sun
    • kṣāṁ: to be nourished or to be sustained
    • dāsāyopabarhaṇīṁ: the one who is devoted or a servant
    • kaḥ: who
    • karat: to do or to act
    • tisro: three
    • maghavā: the powerful or the great one
    • dānucitrā: the one who gives wealth or prosperity
    • ni: not
    • duryoṇe: in difficult situations
    • kuyavācam: with false words or deceitful speech
    • mṛdhi: in the mind or in thought
    • śret: to shine or to illuminate
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the flow of wisdom and nourishment that comes from the divine, represented by Indra and the sun.
    • It emphasizes the importance of being devoted and acting with integrity, especially in challenging situations.
    • The mantra suggests that true power lies in the ability to give and share wealth, both materially and spiritually.
    • It warns against the use of deceitful words and encourages clarity of thought and intention.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a leader who embodies the qualities of Indra by providing guidance and support can help their team thrive, especially during tough projects.
    • In personal relationships, being devoted and honest fosters trust and strengthens bonds, while deceit can lead to misunderstandings and conflicts.
    • Philanthropy is a practical application of the mantra, where individuals or organizations share their wealth to uplift others, reflecting the spirit of giving.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, integrity, and generosity in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to act with purpose and clarity, especially when faced with challenges.
    • By embodying these principles, one can create a positive impact in their community and foster a harmonious environment.
