आद इति ते विश्वे क्रतुः जुषन्त श्रुष्काद यद देव जीवो जनिष्ठाः ||
ād it te viśve kratuṁ juṣanta śuṣkād yad deva jīvo janiṣṭhāḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ād: This can be interpreted as "from" or "out of," indicating a source or origin.
    • it: This word means "that" or "there," often used to refer to something specific.
    • te: This translates to "your," indicating possession or belonging.
    • viśve: Meaning "all" or "universal," it signifies inclusiveness.
    • kratuṁ: This word refers to "intellect" or "will," often associated with mental strength or determination.
    • juṣanta: This means "enjoying" or "delighting in," suggesting a sense of fulfillment or satisfaction.
    • śuṣkād: This can be interpreted as "from the dry" or "from the barren," indicating a state of lack or emptiness.
    • yad: This translates to "that which," often used to introduce a clause or idea.
    • deva: Meaning "god" or "divine," it refers to a higher power or spiritual being.
    • jīvo: This word means "living" or "life," emphasizing vitality and existence.
    • janiṣṭhāḥ: This translates to "born" or "created," indicating the act of coming into being.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • The mantra speaks to the idea of drawing from a universal source of intellect and will.
    • It suggests that all beings, through their divine essence, can find joy and fulfillment even from barren or challenging circumstances.
    • In essence, it emphasizes the connection between the divine and the living, highlighting the potential for creation and growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Consider a student facing academic challenges. By tapping into their inner strength and seeking knowledge (kratuṁ), they can transform their struggles (śuṣkād) into success (jīvo).
    • A community facing drought may find innovative solutions to water scarcity by uniting (viśve) and drawing on collective wisdom (te). This can lead to sustainable practices that rejuvenate their environment (janiṣṭhāḥ).
    • In personal development, individuals often face periods of stagnation. By recognizing their divine potential (deva) and embracing change (yad), they can emerge stronger and more fulfilled.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine.
    • It encourages individuals to seek joy and fulfillment through their inherent strengths, even in difficult times.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can inspire resilience, creativity, and a sense of community, fostering growth and transformation.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the importance of recognizing our divine essence and the potential for renewal and creation in every aspect of life.
