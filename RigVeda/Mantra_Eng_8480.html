एष स्य ते मधुमां इन्द्र सोमो वृषा वृष्णे परि पवित्रे अक्षाः |सहस्रसाः शतसाः भूридावा शाश्वत्तमम् बर्हिर आ वाज्य अस्तात् ||
eṣa sya te madhumām̐ indra somo vṛṣā vṛṣṇe pari pavitre akṣāḥ | sahasrasāḥ śatasā bhūridāvā śaśvattamam barhir ā vājy asthāt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣa: this
    • sya: of him
    • te: your
    • madhumām̐: sweet, honey-like
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • somo: soma (a sacred drink)
    • vṛṣā: strong, powerful
    • vṛṣṇe: O Vṛṣhṇa (another name for Indra)
    • pari: around
    • pavitre: pure
    • akṣāḥ: eyes, visions
    • sahasrasāḥ: thousands
    • śatasā: hundreds
    • bhūridāvā: very generous
    • śaśvattamam: the most eternal
    • barhir: the sacred grass
    • ā: to
    • vājy: the one who is invoked
    • asthāt: has stood
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the king of gods, and refers to the sweet and powerful essence of the sacred soma drink.
    • It emphasizes the purity and abundance of blessings that surround the devotee, symbolized by the "thousands" and "hundreds" of visions or insights.
    • The mention of "bhūridāvā" highlights the generosity of the divine, suggesting that the blessings are not just plentiful but also of great quality.
    • The term "śaśvattamam" indicates the eternal nature of these blessings, suggesting that they are everlasting and reliable.
    • The "sacred grass" symbolizes the connection between the earthly and the divine, serving as a medium for invoking spiritual energies.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to seek purity in thoughts and actions, much like how one would strive for clarity in decision-making.
    • When facing challenges, invoking the essence of this mantra can inspire individuals to tap into their inner strength and seek divine guidance, similar to how athletes prepare mentally before a competition.
    • The concept of generosity can be applied in community service, where individuals contribute their time and resources to help others, reflecting the "bhūridāvā" aspect of the mantra.
    • In moments of reflection or meditation, one can visualize the "thousands" of blessings in their life, fostering gratitude and a positive mindset.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful invocation for strength, purity, and abundance in life.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder of the eternal blessings that surround us and the importance of maintaining a pure heart and mind.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek divine guidance, practice generosity, and cultivate gratitude, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
