ये ते सरस्व उर्मयो मधुमन्तो घृताचुतः |तेभिर नोऽविताभव ||
ye te sarasva ūrmayo madhumanto ghṛtaścutaḥ | tebhir no 'vitā bhava ||
  • Translation: "Those who are endowed with sweet and nourishing qualities, may they not be deprived of us."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: Those who
    • te: You (referring to the divine or celestial beings)
    • sarasva: Endowed with sweetness or nourishment
    • ūrmayo: Waves or qualities
    • madhumanto: Sweet or pleasant
    • ghṛtaścutaḥ: Those who are rich in ghee or nourishment
    • tebhir: By them
    • no: Us
    • avitā: Deprived or taken away
    • bhava: May it be

  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes the divine qualities of nourishment and sweetness, suggesting a plea for abundance and sustenance in life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of positive qualities and the need for their presence in our lives.
    • In a broader sense, it can be interpreted as a call for the blessings of prosperity, health, and well-being.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be relevant during harvest festivals where people express gratitude for the abundance of crops and seek blessings for future prosperity.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during times of need, such as during illness or financial hardship, to invoke support and nourishment from the universe.
    • In a corporate environment, teams may use this mantra to foster a culture of collaboration and support, ensuring that everyone's contributions are valued and that no one is left behind.

  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for nourishment, support, and the presence of positive qualities in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to appreciate the abundance around us and to seek the blessings of those qualities that enrich our existence.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals and communities to cultivate an environment of support, gratitude, and positivity, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal well-being to communal harmony.
