त्रीणि पदा विचक्रमे विष्णुर गोपा अदाभ्यः |अतो धर्माणि धारयन् ||
trīṇi padā vi cakrame viṣṇur gopā adābhyaḥ | ato dharmāṇi dhārayan ||
  • Trīṇi padā: This phrase translates to "three steps" or "three feet." In a broader context, it can symbolize the three realms of existence: the physical, the mental, and the spiritual. These realms are interconnected and represent the holistic nature of life.
  • Vi cakrame: This means "he revolves" or "he moves." It signifies the dynamic nature of the universe and the continuous cycle of creation, preservation, and dissolution. This can be likened to the cycles of nature, such as the changing seasons or the life cycle of living beings.
  • Viṣṇur: Referring to Lord Vishnu, the preserver in Hindu mythology, this term embodies the qualities of protection, sustenance, and balance. In real life, this can be seen in the role of guardians or caretakers in various forms, such as parents, teachers, or leaders who strive to maintain harmony and support growth.
  • Gopā: This translates to "the protector of cows" or "the shepherd." It symbolizes nurturing and guidance. In contemporary terms, this can relate to individuals or organizations that protect and nurture the environment, animals, or communities, emphasizing the importance of stewardship.
  • Adābhyaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "from the unmanifest" or "from the unseen." It suggests the source of all creation and the potential that lies within the unknown. In real life, this can be compared to the potential of ideas before they are realized, such as innovations that emerge from creativity and imagination.
  • Ato dharmāṇi dhārayan: This phrase means "therefore, he upholds the principles of righteousness." It emphasizes the importance of dharma (righteousness or duty) in maintaining order and balance in the universe. In practical terms, this can be seen in the actions of individuals who uphold ethical standards in their personal and professional lives, contributing to a just society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of balance, protection, and the cyclical nature of existence. It highlights the significance of upholding righteousness and nurturing the interconnected realms of life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as environmental conservation, ethical leadership, and personal growth. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a harmonious and sustainable world, reflecting the divine qualities of preservation and stewardship represented by Lord Vishnu.
