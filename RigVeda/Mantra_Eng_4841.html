दशाश्वान दश कोशान दश वस्त्राधिभोजनाः |दशो हिरण्यपिण्डान दिवोदासाद असानिषम् ||
daśāśvān daśa kośān daśa vastrādhibhojanā | daśo hiraṇyapiṇḍān divodāsād asāniṣam ||
  • daśāśvān: This term refers to the "ten horses." In Vedic literature, horses symbolize strength, speed, and the ability to overcome obstacles. They are often associated with the sun and the journey of life.
  • daśa kośān: This translates to "ten sheaths" or "ten layers." In the context of human existence, it can represent the various layers of the self, including physical, emotional, mental, and spiritual aspects.
  • daśa vastrādhibhojanā: This phrase means "ten garments that provide sustenance." Garments can symbolize protection and identity, while sustenance refers to the nourishment needed for life. This can be interpreted as the various means through which we sustain ourselves in life.
  • daśo hiraṇyapiṇḍān: This means "ten golden balls." The golden balls can symbolize wealth, prosperity, and the rewards of life. They represent the fruits of our labor and the abundance that comes from living a righteous life.
  • divodāsād: This term refers to "from the divine." It emphasizes the connection between the material and spiritual realms, suggesting that our sustenance and prosperity come from a higher power.
  • asāniṣam: This translates to "without interruption" or "continuously." It signifies the ongoing nature of life and the perpetual cycle of giving and receiving.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of strength, identity, sustenance, and prosperity. It teaches us that our journey is supported by both material and spiritual resources. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal development, recognizing the different layers of our identity can help us understand ourselves better and work towards holistic growth.
  • In professional life, the pursuit of wealth and success should be balanced with ethical practices and a sense of purpose.
  • In relationships, the idea of continuous giving and receiving fosters a sense of community and support.

This mantra serves as a reminder that our lives are interconnected with the divine and that true prosperity comes from a harmonious balance of all aspects of existence.
