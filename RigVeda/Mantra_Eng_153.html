द्रविणोदाऽ द्रविणसो ग्रावहस्तासो अध्वरे |यज्ञेषु देवम् ईळते ||
draviṇodā draviṇaso grāvahastāso adhvare | yajñeṣu devam īḻate ||
  • Mantra: "draviṇodā draviṇaso grāvahastāso adhvare | yajñeṣu devam īḻate ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • draviṇodā: Refers to wealth or resources.
    • draviṇaso: Indicates the essence or nature of wealth.
    • grāvahastāso: Pertains to those who are engaged in rituals or sacrifices.
    • adhvare: Refers to the act of performing a sacrifice or ritual.
    • yajñeṣu: In the context of sacrifices or offerings.
    • devam: Refers to the divine or deities.
    • īḻate: Means to be invoked or to shine forth.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wealth and resources in the context of rituals and sacrifices.
    • It suggests that those who possess wealth and engage in rituals are able to invoke the divine presence effectively.
    • The connection between material resources and spiritual practices is highlighted, indicating that wealth can facilitate the performance of rituals that honor the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, including Hinduism, offerings made during religious ceremonies often involve material goods such as food, flowers, and money. This reflects the idea that resources are necessary to honor the divine.
    • In modern society, charitable donations can be seen as a form of 'sacrifice' where wealth is used to support community welfare, thereby invoking a sense of divine blessing and goodwill.
    • Business sponsorships of religious events or community services can also be viewed as a way to use wealth to create a positive impact, aligning with the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of material wealth and spiritual practices.
    • It teaches that while spiritual pursuits are essential, the resources we possess can enhance our ability to connect with the divine.
    • In real life, this can be applied by recognizing the role of our resources in supporting spiritual and community endeavors, thus fostering a holistic approach to life that values both material and spiritual wealth.
