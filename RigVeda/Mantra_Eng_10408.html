अग्नि केतुर्विशाम् असि प्रेष्ठः श्रेष्ठ उपस्थसत् |बोधा स्तोत्रे वयो दधत् ||
agne ketur viśām asi preṣṭhaḥ śreṣṭha upasthasat | bodhā stotre vayo dadhat ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • Ketur: This term means "the one who leads" or "the one who guides," indicating a source of direction and support.
  • Viśām: This translates to "of the people" or "of the community," emphasizing the collective aspect of society.
  • Asi: This means "you are," affirming the presence and essence of Agni in the context of the mantra.
  • Preṣṭhaḥ: This word means "the most beloved" or "the dearest," highlighting the importance of Agni in rituals and daily life.
  • Śreṣṭha: This translates to "the best" or "the highest," indicating the supreme status of Agni among the deities.
  • Upasthasat: This means "may you be present" or "may you come forth," invoking Agni's presence in rituals.
  • Bodhā: This word means "knowledge" or "awareness," suggesting the importance of enlightenment and understanding.
  • Stotre: This translates to "in praise" or "in worship," indicating the act of reverence towards Agni.
  • Vayo: This means "of the ages" or "of life," referring to the continuity and longevity of existence.
  • Dhavat: This means "may you grant" or "may you bestow," expressing a desire for blessings and support.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for his guidance, presence, and blessings for the community. It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and awareness in life, suggesting that through the worship of Agni, one can attain enlightenment and support in their endeavors.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: The invocation of Agni during rituals signifies the importance of fire as a purifying element and a witness to vows and commitments.
  • Community Gatherings: The emphasis on the collective aspect (viśām) highlights the importance of community support and unity in achieving common goals.
  • Personal Growth: The call for knowledge (bodhā) encourages individuals to seek wisdom and understanding in their personal and professional lives.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of Agni in our lives, symbolizing guidance, purity, and the pursuit of knowledge. It encourages individuals and communities to come together in worship and support, fostering a sense of unity and enlightenment in their journeys.
