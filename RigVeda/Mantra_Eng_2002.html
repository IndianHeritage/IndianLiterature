त्रिः सप्त विष्पुलिङ्गकाः विषस्य पुष्यम अक्षन् |ताश्चिन नु न मारन्ति नो वयं मारामारे अस्य योजनं हरिष्टा मधु त्वा मधुला चकार ||
triḥ sapta viṣpuliṅgakā viṣasya puṣyam akṣan | tāś cin nu na maranti no vayam marāmāre asya yojanaṁ hariṣṭhā madhu tvā madhulā cakāra ||
  • Mantra Breakdown:
    • triḥ sapta: The term "triḥ" means three, and "sapta" means seven. Together, they signify a total of ten, which can represent completeness or wholeness in various contexts.
    • viṣpuliṅgakā: This word can be interpreted as a reference to the various forms or manifestations of life and energy. It suggests diversity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • viṣasya puṣyam: "Viṣasya" refers to the essence or nature of life, while "puṣyam" means nourishment or growth. This phrase emphasizes the importance of nurturing life and its various forms.
    • akṣan: This term means "eyes" or "vision," symbolizing awareness and perception. It suggests the need for clarity and insight in understanding life.
    • tāś cin nu na maranti: This phrase can be interpreted as a plea or affirmation that those who are aware and enlightened do not perish. It highlights the idea of immortality through knowledge and wisdom.
    • no vayam marāmāre: "No vayam" means "we do not," and "marāmāre" refers to death or destruction. This reinforces the previous idea that wisdom and awareness protect one from the cycle of death.
    • asya yojanaṁ: This term means "this plan" or "this arrangement," indicating a divine or cosmic order that governs existence.
    • hariṣṭhā: This word can be interpreted as "the best" or "the most excellent," suggesting a pursuit of the highest ideals in life.
    • madhu tvā madhulā cakāra: "Madhu" means honey, symbolizing sweetness and abundance. This phrase suggests that the divine or the ultimate reality creates sweetness and joy in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of "triḥ sapta" can be seen in various aspects of life, such as the tenets of teamwork, where diverse individuals come together to create a harmonious whole.
    • In nature, the interconnectedness of ecosystems reflects the idea of "viṣpuliṅgakā," where every organism plays a role in maintaining balance.
    • The importance of nurturing relationships and communities can be linked to "viṣasya puṣyam," emphasizing the need for support and growth in our social lives.
    • In personal development, "akṣan" represents the pursuit of knowledge and self-awareness, which are crucial for making informed decisions.
    • The idea that wisdom leads to a form of immortality can be seen in the legacies of great thinkers and leaders whose ideas continue to influence generations.
    • In a spiritual context, "asya yojanaṁ" can be related to the belief in a higher purpose or plan that guides individuals through life's challenges.
    • The pursuit of excellence in various fields, such as art, science, and ethics, aligns with "hariṣṭhā," encouraging individuals to strive for their best selves.
    • The metaphor of "madhu" can be found in the joy and fulfillment that comes from meaningful experiences and relationships in life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates profound wisdom about life, interconnectedness, and the pursuit of knowledge and excellence.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that awareness and nurturing lead to a fulfilling and meaningful existence.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can enhance personal growth, strengthen relationships, and foster a sense of community.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to seek sweetness and joy in their lives while recognizing the divine order that governs all existence.
