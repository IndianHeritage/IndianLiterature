यद इन्द्र हन्तवे मृधो वृषा वज्रीं चिकेतसि |आ मे अस्य वेधसो नवीयसो मनमा श्रुधि नवीयसः ||
yad indra hantave mṛdho vṛṣā vajriñ ciketasi | ā me asya vedhaso navīyaso manma śrudhi navīyasaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • hantave: to be killed or defeated
    • mṛdho: in the battle or conflict
    • vṛṣā: the strong or powerful
    • vajriñ: the one who wields the thunderbolt
    • ciketasi: you are aware or you know
    • ā: to or towards
    • me: my
    • asya: of this
    • vedhaso: of the sacrifice or offering
    • navīyaso: new or fresh
    • manma: my mind
    • śrudhi: listen or hear
    • navīyasaḥ: of the new or fresh
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, who is often associated with strength and power.
    • The phrase "yad indra hantave" suggests a plea for assistance in overcoming obstacles or enemies, particularly in challenging situations.
    • The reference to "vajriñ" highlights Indra's might, as he wields the thunderbolt, symbolizing both power and the ability to overcome challenges.
    • The speaker seeks to align their mind ("manma") with the divine will, asking Indra to listen to their thoughts and intentions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal struggle, such as facing a difficult decision or conflict, individuals may turn to spiritual practices or prayers, similar to invoking Indra for strength and guidance.
    • In a professional setting, when facing competition or challenges, one might seek inspiration from this mantra to remain focused and resilient, much like how Indra is called upon for support in battles.
    • During community gatherings or rituals, this mantra can be recited to invoke collective strength and unity, encouraging participants to overcome shared challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that strength, both internal and external, can be harnessed through faith and intention.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether personal or communal, invoking such divine energies can foster resilience, unity, and a sense of purpose.
