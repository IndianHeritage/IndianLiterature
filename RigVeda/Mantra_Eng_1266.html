अवोचाम नमो अस्मा अवस्यवः श्रिणोतु नो हवं रुद्रो मरुत्वान् |तन नो मित्रो वरुणो मामहन्ताम् अदितिः सिन्धुः पृथिवी उता द्यौः ||
avocāma namo asmā avasyavaḥ śṛṇotu no havaṁ rudro marutvān | tan no mitro varuṇo māmahantām aditiḥ sindhuḥ pṛthivī uta dyauḥ ||
  • Translation: "We invoke (or call upon) the one who is worthy of our reverence, may Rudra and the Maruts hear our call. May Mitra and Varuna, the great ones, along with Aditi, the ocean, the earth, and the sky, listen to us."
  • Breakdown of Key Terms:
    • Avocāma: We invoke or call upon.
    • Namo: Salutations or reverence.
    • Rudra: A deity associated with storms and the hunt, often seen as a fierce protector.
    • Marutvān: The Maruts are storm deities, representing the forces of nature.
    • Mitra and Varuna: Deities representing cosmic order and moral law, often associated with friendship and truth.
    • Aditi: The mother of the gods, representing infinity and the boundless.
    • Sindhu: Refers to the ocean or river, symbolizing abundance and life.
    • Pṛthivī: The earth, representing stability and nourishment.
    • Dyauḥ: The sky, symbolizing the vastness and the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of natural calamities, people often invoke deities like Rudra for protection and strength, similar to how communities come together to support each other during crises.
    • The concept of Mitra and Varuna can be seen in modern friendships and partnerships, where trust and moral integrity are foundational to relationships.
    • Aditi's representation of infinity can be related to the limitless potential of human creativity and innovation, as seen in advancements in technology and science.
    • The elements of nature—ocean, earth, and sky—remind us of our responsibility towards environmental conservation, as we rely on these for our survival.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of humanity with the divine and the natural world. It serves as a reminder to seek guidance and protection from higher powers while acknowledging the importance of moral values and the environment. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to foster relationships based on trust, to respect nature, and to call upon inner strength during challenging times. Its significance lies in promoting harmony between the individual, society, and the cosmos, encouraging a holistic approach to life.
