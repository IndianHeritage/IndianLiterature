आ वां रथो रोधसी बद्बधानो हिरण्ययो वृषभिर यात्व अश्वैः |घृतवर्तिनिः पविभी रुचाना इषां वोळ्हा नृपतिर वाजिनीवान ||
ā vāṁ ratho rodasī badbadhāno hiraṇyayo vṛṣabhir yātv aśvaiḥ | ghṛtavartaniḥ pavibhī rucāna iṣāṁ voḻhā nṛpatir vājinīvān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vāṁ: "to you" or "for you"
    • ratho: "chariot"
    • rodasī: "the two worlds" or "the heavens and the earth"
    • badbadhāno: "spreading" or "expanding"
    • hiraṇyayo: "golden" or "wealth"
    • vṛṣabhir: "with the strong" or "with the powerful"
    • yātv: "may you go" or "may you travel"
    • aśvaiḥ: "with horses"
    • ghṛtavartaniḥ: "with clarified butter" or "with nourishment"
    • pavibhī: "with the wind" or "with the breath"
    • rucāna: "of brilliance" or "of light"
    • iṣāṁ: "of the desires" or "of the wishes"
    • voḻhā: "to you" or "for you"
    • nṛpatir: "the king" or "the ruler"
    • vājinīvān: "the one who is swift" or "the one who is powerful"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of prosperity and strength.
    • The chariot symbolizes the journey of life, where the two worlds represent the physical and spiritual realms.
    • The mention of "golden" signifies wealth and abundance, which can be interpreted as both material and spiritual richness.
    • The phrase "may you go with horses" suggests the importance of speed and agility in achieving one's goals.
    • The reference to "clarified butter" indicates nourishment and the sustenance needed for growth and success.
    • The "king" represents leadership and authority, emphasizing the need for wise governance in any endeavor.
    • The overall essence of the mantra is a prayer for guidance, strength, and prosperity in one's pursuits.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied when launching a new venture, seeking blessings for success and prosperity.
    • In personal life, it can be recited during significant life transitions, such as marriage or starting a family, to invoke strength and abundance.
    • In leadership roles, it serves as a reminder for leaders to govern wisely and ensure the well-being of their followers.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the balance between material success and spiritual growth.
    • It encourages individuals to seek strength and prosperity while remaining grounded and mindful of their responsibilities.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be a source of inspiration and motivation, guiding individuals towards their goals with clarity and purpose.
