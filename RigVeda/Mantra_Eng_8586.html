शतं धाराऽदेवजाताऽसृग्नं सहस्रं एनाः कवयो मृजन्ति |इन्दो सन्त्रं दिव आ पवस्व पुरेतासि महतो धनस्य ||
śataṁ dhārā devajātā asṛgran sahasram enāḥ kavayo mṛjanti | indo sanitraṁ diva ā pavasva puraetāsi mahato dhanasya ||
  • śataṁ dhārā: This phrase translates to "a hundred streams" or "hundreds of flows." It symbolizes abundance and the various paths of life that one can take. In real life, this can be likened to the numerous opportunities and choices we encounter daily, such as career paths, relationships, and personal growth.
  • devajātā: Meaning "born of the gods," this term signifies divine origin or inspiration. It suggests that certain ideas or actions are guided by a higher power. For example, when individuals feel a calling to serve others or pursue a noble cause, they may interpret this as a divine inspiration.
  • asṛgran: This word means "to gather" or "to collect." It implies the act of bringing together various elements. In a practical sense, this can be seen in community efforts where people unite for a common goal, such as environmental conservation or social justice initiatives.
  • sahasram enāḥ: This translates to "thousands of them." It emphasizes the vastness and multitude of resources or ideas available. In modern contexts, this can refer to the wealth of information accessible through the internet, where countless perspectives and solutions can be found for any given problem.
  • kavayo mṛjanti: This phrase means "the wise ones cleanse" or "the poets purify." It suggests that those with wisdom or creativity have the ability to refine thoughts and ideas. In real life, this can be seen in the role of artists, writers, and thinkers who challenge societal norms and inspire change through their work.
  • indo sanitraṁ: This translates to "the moon, the source of light." The moon often symbolizes calmness and reflection. In everyday life, this can be related to moments of introspection and the importance of taking time to reflect on one’s actions and decisions.
  • diva ā pavasva: Meaning "come to the sky," this phrase can be interpreted as a call to elevate oneself or aspire to higher ideals. In practical terms, this can be seen in personal development efforts, where individuals strive to improve themselves and reach their full potential.
  • puraetāsi mahato dhanasya: This means "you are the protector of great wealth." It signifies the importance of safeguarding valuable resources, whether they be material wealth, knowledge, or relationships. In real life, this can be applied to financial literacy, where individuals learn to manage their resources wisely to ensure long-term security.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of abundance, divine inspiration, unity, wisdom, reflection, aspiration, and the protection of wealth. Its significance lies in the reminder that life is filled with opportunities and choices, and that we are guided by higher ideals and wisdom. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to seek out their paths, collaborate with others, reflect on their actions, and strive for personal growth while safeguarding their resources. It serves as a guiding principle for living a fulfilling and purposeful life.
