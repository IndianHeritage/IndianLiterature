आपश्चिद्धि स्वयशसः सदःसु देवीर इन्द्रं वरुणं देवता धुः |कृष्टीर अन्यो धारयति प्रविक्तां वृत्राण्य अन्यो अप्रतीनी हन्ति ||
āpaś cid dhi svayaśasaḥ sadaḥsu devīr indraṁ varuṇaṁ devatā dhuḥ | kṛṣṭīr anyo dhārayati praviktā vṛtrāṇy anyo apratīni hanti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āpaś: waters
    • cid: indeed
    • dhi: mind or intellect
    • svayaśasaḥ: of self-luminous or self-illuminated
    • sadaḥsu: in the assemblies or gatherings
    • devīr: by the goddesses
    • indraṁ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • varuṇaṁ: Varuna (the god of cosmic order)
    • devatā: deities
    • dhuḥ: they bring forth
    • kṛṣṭīr: the one who cultivates or nurtures
    • anyo: another
    • dhārayati: sustains or supports
    • praviktā: well-known or established
    • vṛtrāṇy: the obstacles or enemies
    • anyo: another
    • apratīni: unyielding or unbreakable
    • hanti: destroys
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine powers of the waters, which are personified as goddesses. They are described as self-illuminated and capable of bringing forth life and sustenance.
    • Indra and Varuna are invoked, representing strength and cosmic order, respectively. This highlights the importance of harmony between strength and order in the universe.
    • The mantra emphasizes the role of nurturing and cultivation, suggesting that while one may face obstacles (vṛtrāṇy), there are forces (the goddesses) that support and sustain life.
    • The mention of "apratīni" indicates that some challenges are formidable, yet they can be overcome with the right support and divine intervention.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the nurturing aspect of the waters can be likened to the importance of irrigation and rainfall in sustaining crops. Farmers often pray for rain, recognizing its divine significance in their livelihood.
    • In personal challenges, individuals may seek strength and guidance from spiritual practices, invoking the support of higher powers to overcome obstacles in their lives.
    • In community settings, the assembly of people working together can be seen as a reflection of the divine gathering mentioned in the mantra, where collective strength and support can lead to overcoming societal challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of divine forces, nature, and human endeavors. It teaches us that while we may face challenges, there are supportive energies in the universe that can help us navigate through them.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek harmony between their inner strength and the cosmic order, fostering a sense of community and collaboration in overcoming obstacles.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing, both in nature and in our relationships, to cultivate a thriving existence.
