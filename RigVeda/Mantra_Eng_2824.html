यमाऽ cid atra यमसूर asūta जिह्वायाऽ agram पतद ā ह्य asthāt |वपूंषि जाताऽ मिथुनā सचेते तमोहनā तपुषो बुध्न etā ||
yamā cid atra yamasūr asūta jihvāyā agram patad ā hy asthāt | vapūṁṣi jātā mithunā sacete tamohanā tapuṣo budhna etā ||
  • yamā cid atra yamasūr asūta: This phrase can be interpreted as "those who are under the control of Yama, the god of death, are here." Yama represents the inevitability of death and the moral order of the universe.
  • jihvāyā agram patad ā hy asthāt: This translates to "the tongue, which is the foremost of the senses, has been established." The tongue symbolizes speech and communication, highlighting the importance of words and their impact on our lives.
  • vapūṁṣi jātā mithunā sacete: This means "the bodies that have been born are united." It signifies the interconnectedness of all living beings and the relationships we form throughout our lives.
  • tamohanā tapuṣo budhna etā: This can be understood as "may we awaken from ignorance and attain enlightenment." It emphasizes the pursuit of knowledge and understanding, urging individuals to seek wisdom and clarity.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates profound themes of mortality, communication, interconnectedness, and the quest for enlightenment. In real life, it serves as a reminder of the transient nature of existence and the importance of our words and relationships. By recognizing the inevitability of death, we can appreciate life more fully and strive to communicate thoughtfully. The call to awaken from ignorance encourages us to pursue knowledge and understanding, fostering personal growth and a deeper connection with others. This mantra is applicable in various life scenarios, such as in personal relationships, professional communication, and spiritual practices, guiding us towards a more meaningful existence.
