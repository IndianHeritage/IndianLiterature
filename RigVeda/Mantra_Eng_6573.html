एव नूनमुप स्तुहि वैयश्व दशमं नवम् |सुविद्वांसं चर्कृत्यं चरणीनाम् ||
evā nūnam upa stuhi vaiyaśva daśamaṁ navam | suvidvāṁsaṁ carkṛtyaṁ caraṇīnām ||
  • evā nūnam upa stuhi: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, let us praise." It signifies the importance of offering respect and gratitude, which is a fundamental aspect of many cultures and religions. For example, in daily life, we often express gratitude towards our parents, teachers, or mentors for their guidance and support.
  • vaiyaśva daśamaṁ navam: This part refers to "the tenth and the new." It can symbolize the idea of renewal and the importance of new beginnings. In real life, this can be seen in various contexts, such as starting a new job, moving to a new city, or embarking on a new phase in life, like marriage or parenthood.
  • suvidvāṁsaṁ carkṛtyaṁ: This translates to "the well-versed and skilled." It emphasizes the value of knowledge and expertise. In today's world, this can be related to the importance of education and continuous learning. For instance, professionals often seek further training or certifications to enhance their skills and stay relevant in their fields.
  • caraṇīnām: This word means "of the feet" or "of those who walk." It can symbolize the journey of life and the paths we choose. In a practical sense, this can relate to the choices we make in our careers, relationships, and personal growth. Each step we take shapes our future and defines our experiences.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of gratitude, renewal, knowledge, and the journey of life. Its significance lies in the reminder to appreciate our roots and the people who guide us, embrace new beginnings, value education, and be mindful of the paths we choose. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate a mindset of gratitude, pursue lifelong learning, and make conscious choices that lead to personal and professional fulfillment.
