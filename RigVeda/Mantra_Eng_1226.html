याभिर नराः शयवे याभिर अतृये याभिः पुरा मनवे गातुम ईषतुः |याभिः शारीर आजतं स्यूमरश्मये ताभिर ऊषु ऊतिभिर अश्विनाः गतम् ||
yābhir narā śayave yābhir atraye yābhiḥ purā manave gātum īṣathuḥ | yābhiḥ śārīr ājataṁ syūmaraśmaye tābhir ū ṣu ūtibhir aśvinā gatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yābhir: By whom
    • narā: Men
    • śayave: To the bed (or resting place)
    • yābhir: By whom
    • atraye: To the one who is in need
    • yābhiḥ: By whom
    • purā: In the past
    • manave: To Manu (the progenitor of mankind)
    • gātum: To go
    • īṣathuḥ: To be able
    • yābhiḥ: By whom
    • śārīr: Of the body
    • ājataṁ: Born
    • syūmaraśmaye: To the one who is radiant
    • tābhir: By them
    • ū: O
    • ṣu: Good
    • ūtibhir: With strength
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin deities)
    • gatam: Gone
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine powers of the Ashvins, who are known as the twin gods of health and medicine in Vedic literature.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking help and guidance from divine forces in times of need, particularly in relation to health and well-being.
    • It reflects the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine, suggesting that through devotion and prayer, individuals can attain strength and vitality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, individuals often turn to meditation, prayer, or spiritual practices when facing health challenges, seeking a connection to a higher power for support.
    • Healthcare professionals may invoke the blessings of deities or spiritual figures in their practice, believing that divine guidance can enhance their ability to heal.
    • Communities may come together in rituals or ceremonies to pray for the health and well-being of their members, reflecting the collective belief in the power of divine intervention.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the importance of seeking divine assistance in our lives, particularly in matters of health and well-being.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize their vulnerabilities and to reach out for support, whether through spiritual practices or community solidarity.
    • In a world where stress and health issues are prevalent, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the potential for divine grace and healing in our lives.
