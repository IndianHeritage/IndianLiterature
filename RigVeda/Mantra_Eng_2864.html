इन्द्र पिब वृषधूतस्य वृष्ण आ यं ते श्येन उषते जभारा |यस्य मदे च्यावयसि प्र कृष्टीर यस्य मदे अप गोत्रा ववर्थ ||
indra piba vṛṣadhūtasya vṛṣṇa ā yaṁ te śyena uśate jabhāra | yasya made cyāvayasi pra kṛṣṭīr yasya made apa gotrā vavartha ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Piba: Means "to drink," often associated with the consumption of soma, a sacred ritual drink that grants immortality and divine insight.
  • Vṛṣadhūtasya: Translates to "the one who is the bearer of strength," indicating a powerful entity or force.
  • Vṛṣṇa: Refers to the divine or the one who is strong and capable, often associated with prosperity and abundance.
  • Ā yaṁ: Means "to you," indicating a direction or offering towards the divine.
  • Te: Means "your," signifying possession or belonging to the divine.
  • Śyena: Refers to a falcon or eagle, symbolizing speed, vision, and the ability to rise above challenges.
  • Uśate: Means "to dwell" or "to reside," indicating a state of being or existence.
  • Jabhāra: Translates to "to bear" or "to carry," suggesting the act of supporting or sustaining.
  • Yasya: Means "whose," indicating possession or relation.
  • Made: Refers to "joy" or "intoxication," often linked to spiritual ecstasy or divine bliss.
  • Cyāvayasi: Means "to remove" or "to drive away," indicating the act of dispelling negativity or obstacles.
  • Pra kṛṣṭīr: Refers to "the best" or "the highest," indicating excellence or superiority.
  • Apā: Means "water," symbolizing purity, life, and sustenance.
  • Gotrā: Refers to "clan" or "lineage," indicating a sense of belonging and identity.
  • Vavartha: Means "to flourish" or "to grow," suggesting prosperity and abundance.

This mantra invokes the divine presence of Indra, asking for strength, prosperity, and the removal of obstacles. It emphasizes the importance of divine support in achieving success and well-being. The imagery of the falcon signifies the ability to rise above challenges and gain a broader perspective in life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra is a symbol of leadership, individuals in positions of authority can draw inspiration from this mantra to lead with strength and wisdom.
  • Personal Growth: The call for strength and prosperity can motivate individuals to pursue their goals with determination and resilience.
  • Community Support: The reference to lineage and clan highlights the importance of community and support systems in overcoming challenges.
  • Spiritual Practices: The act of drinking soma symbolizes the pursuit of spiritual knowledge and enlightenment, encouraging individuals to seek deeper understanding and connection with the divine.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the strength and support available from the divine. It encourages individuals to seek prosperity, overcome obstacles, and embrace their identity within a community. By invoking these qualities, one can navigate life's challenges with confidence and purpose.
