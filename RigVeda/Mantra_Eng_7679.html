यस्माद् रेजन्त कृष्टयश्चर्कृत्यानि कृत्वतः | सहस्रसाम् मेधसाताव् इव तमनाग्निं धीभिः सपर्यत ||
yasmād rejanta kṛṣṭayaś carkṛtyāni kṛṇvataḥ | sahasrasām medhasātāv iva tmanāgniṁ dhībhiḥ saparyata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasmād: From whom
    • rejanta: The one who shines or radiates
    • kṛṣṭayaḥ: The fields or the cultivated lands
    • carkṛtyāni: The actions or deeds
    • kṛṇvataḥ: Of those who perform
    • sahasrasām: Of thousands
    • medhasātāv: Of intelligence or wisdom
    • iva: Like
    • tmanā: With the self
    • agniṁ: The fire
    • dhībhiḥ: With intellect or understanding
    • saparyata: To be worshipped or revered
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine source from which radiance and prosperity emanate.
    • The reference to "kṛṣṭayaḥ" indicates the importance of cultivation, both in agriculture and in personal development.
    • The phrase "sahasrasām medhasātāv" suggests that true wisdom is abundant and can be likened to a fire that illuminates the path of knowledge.
    • It emphasizes the need for intellect and understanding in our actions, akin to how fire is revered in various cultures as a source of life and transformation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers who cultivate their fields with care and knowledge often reap abundant harvests, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on the importance of diligent actions.
    • In personal development, individuals who seek knowledge and understanding, much like the fire that provides warmth and light, can illuminate their lives and the lives of others.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who inspire their teams with wisdom and clarity can create a thriving work environment, similar to how fire brings people together.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of divine radiance, human effort, and the pursuit of wisdom.
    • It teaches us that through our actions, guided by intellect and understanding, we can cultivate not only our fields but also our lives.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as agriculture, education, and leadership, reminding us to honor the divine source of knowledge and to act with purpose and clarity.
