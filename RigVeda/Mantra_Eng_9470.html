उत मातृ बृहद्विवा श्रिणोतु नस् त्वष्टा देवैभिर जनिभिः पिता वचः |ऋभुक्षा वाजो रथस्पतिर भगो रण्वः शंसः शशमानस्य पातु नः ||
uta mātā bṛhaddivā śṛṇotu nas tvaṣṭā devebhir janibhiḥ pitā vacaḥ | ṛbhukṣā vājo rathaspatir bhago raṇvaḥ śaṁsaḥ śaśamānasya pātu naḥ ||
  • uta mātā: This phrase translates to "O Mother." In many cultures, the concept of a mother symbolizes nurturing, protection, and unconditional love. For example, in real life, a mother often sacrifices her own needs for the well-being of her children.
  • bṛhaddivā: This means "the great sky" or "the vast heavens." It signifies the expansive nature of the universe. In our daily lives, we often look up at the sky and feel a sense of wonder and connection to something greater than ourselves.
  • śṛṇotu nas: This translates to "may he listen to us." It reflects the human desire for communication and understanding, whether it be with the divine or among ourselves. For instance, in relationships, active listening is crucial for building trust and empathy.
  • tvaṣṭā: This refers to "the creator" or "the divine craftsman." In real life, we see creators in various forms, such as artists, engineers, and innovators who shape the world around us through their creativity and skills.
  • devebhir: This means "by the gods." It emphasizes the divine influence in our lives. Many people turn to spirituality or religion for guidance and support during challenging times, believing that a higher power is watching over them.
  • janibhiḥ: This translates to "by the born ones" or "by the beings." It signifies the interconnectedness of all living beings. In our daily lives, we often rely on community and relationships to thrive, highlighting the importance of social bonds.
  • pitā vacaḥ: This means "the words of the father." It symbolizes wisdom and guidance passed down through generations. For example, many people cherish the advice of their parents or elders, which shapes their values and decisions.
  • ṛbhukṣā: This translates to "the one who provides." It signifies abundance and sustenance. In real life, this can be seen in the way nature provides for us through food, water, and resources, reminding us to be grateful for what we have.
  • vājo: This means "strength" or "energy." It reflects the importance of vitality in our lives. For instance, maintaining physical health through exercise and nutrition is essential for living a fulfilling life.
  • rathaspatir: This translates to "the lord of the chariot." It symbolizes guidance and direction. In our lives, we often seek mentors or leaders who can help steer us in the right direction, whether in our careers or personal growth.
  • bhago: This means "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the abundance we seek in life. Many people strive for financial stability and success, which can lead to a sense of security and happiness.
  • raṇvaḥ: This translates to "the one who brings joy." It emphasizes the importance of happiness and positivity in our lives. For example, engaging in activities that bring us joy, such as hobbies or spending time with loved ones, is essential for our well-being.
  • śaṁsaḥ: This means "the one who is praised." It reflects the human desire for recognition and appreciation. In real life, receiving praise for our efforts can motivate us to continue striving for excellence.
  • śaśamānasya: This translates to "of the one who is peaceful." It signifies the importance of tranquility and calmness in our lives. Practicing mindfulness or meditation can help us achieve a state of peace amidst the chaos of daily life.
  • pātu naḥ: This means "may he protect us." It reflects the universal desire for safety and security. In real life, people often seek protection through community, law, and personal relationships to ensure their well-being.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, protection, and abundance in our lives. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing relationships with both the divine and each other. In real-life scenarios, the themes of love, wisdom, strength, and prosperity resonate deeply, reminding us to appreciate the support we receive from our families, communities, and the universe. By embodying these values, we can lead more fulfilling and harmonious lives.
