इन्द्र सोमं पिब ऋतुनā त्वा विशन्तु इन्दवः |मत्सरासस तदोकसः ||
indra somam piba ṛtunā tvā viśantv indavaḥ | matsarāsas tadokasaḥ ||
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • Somam: Soma refers to a ritual drink, believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes the essence of life and vitality.
  • Piba: This word means "to drink." It signifies the act of consuming or absorbing something beneficial.
  • Ṛtunā: This term translates to "with the seasons" or "in accordance with the cycles of nature." It emphasizes harmony with the natural world.
  • Tvā: This means "you," referring to Indra, indicating a direct address to the deity.
  • Viśantv: This word means "may they enter" or "may they come." It expresses a desire for divine presence or blessings.
  • Indavaḥ: This term refers to the "soma" or "soma-like" beings, often associated with celestial or divine entities that bring joy and prosperity.
  • Matsarāsas: This word can be interpreted as "those who are envious" or "those who harbor jealousy." It highlights the negative emotions that can hinder progress.
  • Tadokasaḥ: This term means "those who are free from envy" or "those who are generous." It signifies the importance of positive qualities in achieving success.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation to Indra, asking him to partake of the soma and bless the worshippers with prosperity and abundance. It emphasizes the importance of aligning oneself with the natural cycles and seeking the divine presence to overcome negative emotions like envy.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra calls for the removal of envy, individuals can strive to cultivate generosity and positivity in their lives, fostering a supportive environment.
  • Community Building: In a community setting, invoking the spirit of Indra can inspire collective efforts towards prosperity, encouraging collaboration over competition.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to "with the seasons" reminds us of the importance of living in harmony with nature, promoting sustainable practices.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of divine blessings, the importance of positive emotions, and the need for harmony with nature. By embodying these principles, individuals and communities can work towards a more prosperous and fulfilling life.
