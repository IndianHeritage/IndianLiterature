स मातरिश्वा पूर्वारपुष्टिर विदद गातुं तनयाय स्वरवित् |विशां गोपा जनिता रोदस्योर देवाः अग्निं धारयन द्रविणोदाम् ||
sa mātariśvā puruvārapuṣṭir vidad gātuṁ tanayāya svarvit | viśāṁ gopā janitā rodasyor devā agniṁ dhārayan draviṇodām ||
  • sa mātariśvā: This phrase refers to the deity Mātariśvā, who is often associated with the winds and the atmosphere. In a real-life context, we can think of Mātariśvā as a representation of the natural forces that govern our environment, such as the wind that carries seeds for growth or the air we breathe.
  • puruvārapuṣṭir: This term can be broken down into "puruvāra" (the one who has abundant resources) and "puṣṭir" (nourishment or prosperity). It signifies the abundance and nourishment that comes from nature. For example, a farmer who cultivates a bountiful harvest is experiencing the blessings of puruvārapuṣṭir.
  • vidad gātuṁ tanayāya svarvit: This line suggests the act of bestowing or granting something to the offspring or progeny. It emphasizes the importance of nurturing the next generation. In modern life, this can be seen in the efforts of parents and educators who strive to provide knowledge and resources to children, ensuring their growth and success.
  • viśāṁ gopā: This phrase translates to "the protector of the community." It highlights the role of guardianship and leadership within society. For instance, community leaders or local governments work to protect and serve the interests of their citizens, ensuring safety and well-being.
  • janitā rodasyor: This refers to the creator or originator of the heavens and the earth. It signifies the divine force behind creation. In a real-life scenario, this can be likened to scientists and innovators who explore and understand the universe, contributing to our knowledge of existence.
  • devā agniṁ dhārayan draviṇodām: This line speaks of the divine beings (devas) who carry or uphold Agni, the fire. Agni symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. In everyday life, we can relate this to the importance of fire in cooking, warmth, and rituals, as well as the metaphorical fire of passion and ambition that drives individuals to achieve their goals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of nature, abundance, protection, creation, and transformation. Its significance lies in the interconnectedness of these elements in our lives. By recognizing the forces of nature and the importance of nurturing future generations, we can foster a sense of responsibility towards our environment and community. This mantra encourages us to appreciate the resources we have, protect our communities, and strive for personal and collective growth, making it applicable in various real-life scenarios such as parenting, community service, and environmental stewardship.
