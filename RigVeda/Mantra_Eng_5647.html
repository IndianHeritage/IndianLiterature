इयं देव पुरोहितिर युवाभ्यां यज्ञेषु मित्रावरुणाव अकारी |विश्वानि दुर्गा पिपृतं तिरो नो यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
iyaṁ deva purohitir yuvabhyāṁ yajñeṣu mitrāvaruṇāv akāri | viśvāni durgā pipṛtaṁ tiro no yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "This divine priest, with the two youthful ones, has performed the sacrifices for Mitra and Varuna. May they remove all obstacles from us; may you always protect us with your auspiciousness."
  • Deities Mentioned:
    • Mitra: The god of friendship, contracts, and the sun, representing the light and warmth that fosters relationships.
    • Varuna: The god of cosmic order and water, symbolizing the moral and ethical aspects of the universe.
  • Significance of the Mantra:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of rituals and the role of a priest (purohita) in connecting the divine with the human realm.
    • The invocation of Mitra and Varuna signifies the need for harmony and balance in life, as well as the importance of ethical conduct.
    • The plea for protection and removal of obstacles reflects a universal human desire for safety and well-being.
  • Real-Life Applications:
    • In personal relationships, invoking the spirit of Mitra can encourage trust and cooperation among friends and family.
    • In professional settings, the principles of Varuna can guide ethical decision-making and promote a sense of responsibility.
    • During challenging times, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek divine support and maintain a positive outlook.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of human actions and divine influence. It highlights the importance of rituals in fostering relationships and maintaining ethical standards. In real life, it encourages individuals to seek harmony, support, and protection from the divine, making it relevant in both personal and communal contexts.
