कण्वासा इन्द्र ते मतीं विश्वे वर्धन्ति पौंस्यम् |उतो शविष्ट वृष्ण्यम् ||
kaṇvāsa indra te matiṁ viśve vardhanti pauṁsyam | uto śaviṣṭha vṛṣṇyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kaṇvāsa: Referring to the wise or learned ones, often associated with the sage Kanva.
    • indra: The king of gods, representing strength, power, and leadership.
    • te: Your, indicating possession or belonging.
    • matiṁ: Intellect or understanding, often related to wisdom and insight.
    • viśve: All or universal, indicating inclusiveness.
    • vardhanti: They increase or promote, suggesting growth or enhancement.
    • pauṁsyam: Related to manliness or strength, often associated with vitality.
    • uto: Also or furthermore, indicating addition.
    • śaviṣṭha: The best or most excellent, denoting superiority.
    • vṛṣṇyam: Related to the Vrishni clan, often associated with valor and heroism.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the god of strength and leadership, asking for the enhancement of intellect and strength.
    • The mention of "kaṇvāsa" suggests that wisdom and learning are essential for growth and success.
    • By addressing "viśve," it emphasizes the importance of collective growth and the contribution of all individuals.
    • The terms "pauṁsyam" and "vṛṣṇyam" highlight the significance of manliness and valor, suggesting that true strength comes from both physical and intellectual prowess.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of Indra can inspire their team to work together, enhancing collective intelligence and productivity.
    • In education, teachers who promote wisdom and understanding among students foster an environment where everyone can grow and succeed.
    • In personal development, individuals who seek to enhance their intellect and strength can achieve greater success in their careers and personal lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of wisdom, collective growth, and the balance of strength and intellect in achieving success.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, including leadership, education, and personal development, reminding us that true strength lies in the enhancement of both mind and body.
    • By invoking these qualities, individuals and communities can strive for excellence and harmony in their endeavors.
