उतादः पुरुषे गवि सूरश चक्रं हिरण्ययम् |न्य ऐरयद रथीतमः ||
utādaḥ paruṣe gavi sūraś cakraṁ hiraṇyayam | ny airayad rathītamaḥ ||
  • utādaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who rises" or "the one who elevates." It signifies the act of rising above challenges or obstacles.
  • paruṣe: This word means "in strength" or "with vigor." It emphasizes the importance of strength and resilience in overcoming difficulties.
  • gavi: This refers to "the cow," which in Vedic literature symbolizes wealth, nourishment, and abundance. Cows are often associated with prosperity and the sustenance of life.
  • sūraś: This term translates to "heroic" or "valiant." It denotes bravery and the qualities of a warrior, suggesting that one must embody courage in their endeavors.
  • cakraṁ: This means "wheel" or "circle." In a broader sense, it can represent the cycle of life, the universe, or the continuous movement of time and events.
  • hiraṇyayam: This translates to "golden" or "radiant." It symbolizes purity, wealth, and the highest form of achievement or success.
  • ny airayad: This phrase can be interpreted as "he who has attained" or "he who has achieved." It signifies accomplishment and the rewards of hard work.
  • rathītamaḥ: This term means "the best charioteer" or "the supreme driver." It symbolizes guidance, leadership, and the ability to navigate through life's challenges effectively.

The mantra as a whole can be understood as a call to rise with strength and courage, embodying the qualities of a hero while navigating the cycles of life towards achieving golden success. It emphasizes the importance of resilience, guidance, and the pursuit of prosperity.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: When facing challenges in personal or professional life, one can draw inspiration from this mantra to rise above difficulties with strength and courage.
  • Leadership: In leadership roles, embodying the qualities of a "rathītamaḥ" can help guide teams effectively through challenges, ensuring collective success.
  • Financial Prosperity: The symbolism of the cow and gold can inspire individuals to seek abundance and prosperity through hard work and ethical means.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the virtues of strength, courage, and guidance in the pursuit of success and prosperity. It encourages individuals to rise above challenges and strive for excellence in all aspects of life.
