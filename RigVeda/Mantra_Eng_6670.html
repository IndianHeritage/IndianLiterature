ये देवासा इह स्थान विश्वे वैश्वानरा उता |अस्मभ्यं शर्म सप्रथो गवे 'श्वाय यच्छत ||
ye devāsa iha sthana viśve vaiśvānarā uta | asmabhyaṁ śarma sapratho gave 'śvāya yacchata ||
  • Translation: "The gods who are here, all-pervading, grant us protection and well-being, just as they bestow upon the horse."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: those who
    • devāsa: gods
    • iha: here
    • sthāna: place or position
    • viśve: all or universal
    • vaiśvānarā: all-pervading or universal
    • uta: and
    • asmabhyaṁ: for us
    • śarma: protection or well-being
    • sapratho: with abundance
    • gave: horse
    • 'śvāya: to the horse
    • yacchata: grant or bestow
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes the presence of the gods, acknowledging their omnipresence and power.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine protection and blessings in our lives.
    • The reference to the horse symbolizes strength, vitality, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of uncertainty, individuals often seek divine guidance and protection, similar to how this mantra calls upon the gods for well-being.
    • In various cultures, horses are seen as symbols of power and freedom; thus, the mantra can be related to seeking strength in personal endeavors.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, people often recite prayers or mantras for blessings, reflecting the essence of this invocation.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine support in our daily lives.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of protection and abundance.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of gratitude and reliance on higher powers, fostering a positive outlook in challenging situations.
