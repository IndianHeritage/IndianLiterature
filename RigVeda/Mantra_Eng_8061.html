अस्य प्रात्नाम् अनु द्युतं शुक्रं दुदुहरे अह्रयः |पयः सहस्रसाम ऋषिम ||
asya pratnām anu dyutaṁ śukraṁ duduhre ahrayaḥ | payaḥ sahasrasām ṛṣim ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asya: of this
    • pratnām: of the offerings
    • anu: following
    • dyutaṁ: brilliance or light
    • śukraṁ: pure or bright
    • duduhre: they have drawn forth
    • ahrayaḥ: the great ones or the powerful
    • payaḥ: milk or nourishment
    • sahasrasām: of the thousand
    • ṛṣim: sage or seer
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the offerings that are made to the divine, emphasizing the purity and brilliance of these offerings.
    • The term "dyutaṁ" signifies the light or brilliance that is associated with divine blessings, suggesting that the offerings are not just material but also imbued with spiritual significance.
    • "Ahrayaḥ" refers to the powerful beings or deities who are the recipients of these offerings, indicating that they are worthy of reverence and respect.
    • The mention of "payaḥ" (milk) symbolizes nourishment, both physical and spiritual, suggesting that the offerings provide sustenance to the divine and, in turn, to the devotees.
    • The phrase "sahasrasām ṛṣim" highlights the wisdom of the sages, indicating that the knowledge and insights gained from these offerings can be vast and profound.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be related to the practice of gratitude and offering in daily life. For instance, when we express gratitude for the food we eat, we acknowledge the effort that went into its production and the nourishment it provides.
    • In rituals and ceremonies, offerings of flowers, fruits, or other items can be seen as a way to connect with the divine, reflecting the purity and intention behind the act.
    • In a broader sense, the mantra encourages individuals to seek brilliance and purity in their actions, striving to contribute positively to the world around them.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of purity, intention, and reverence in our offerings, whether they are material or spiritual.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of nourishment, wisdom, and divine blessings, encouraging individuals to cultivate a sense of gratitude and mindfulness in their daily lives.
    • By embodying the principles expressed in this mantra, individuals can enhance their spiritual practice and contribute to a more harmonious existence.
