युयं न उग्रा मरुतः सुचेतुनारीष्टग्रामाः सुमतिम पिपर्तन |यत्रा वो दिद्युद रदति कृविर्दती रिणाति पश्वः सुधितेव बरहणा ||
yūyaṁ na ugrā marutaḥ sucetunāriṣṭagrāmāḥ sumatim pipartana | yatrā vo didyud radati krivirdatī riṇāti paśvaḥ sudhiteva barhaṇā ||
  • yūyaṁ - You (plural), referring to a group or community.
  • na - Not, indicating negation.
  • ugrā - Fierce or violent, often associated with strong forces of nature.
  • marutaḥ - The Maruts, a group of storm deities in Vedic literature, representing the power of storms and winds.
  • sucetunā - With good intellect or wisdom, suggesting clarity of thought.
  • ariṣṭagrāmāḥ - Those who are free from obstacles or misfortunes, indicating a state of well-being.
  • sumatim - Good understanding or wisdom, emphasizing the importance of knowledge.
  • pipartana - To bestow or grant, indicating the act of giving or sharing.
  • yatrā - Where, indicating a location or situation.
  • vo - To you, again addressing the group.
  • didyud - Shining or illuminating, often associated with enlightenment.
  • radati - Roaring or making a sound, often used to describe thunder.
  • krivirdatī - Giving or providing, indicating generosity.
  • riṇāti - To call or invoke, suggesting a plea or request.
  • paśvaḥ - Animals or creatures, often referring to livestock or nature.
  • sudhiteva - Like the essence of good, indicating purity and positivity.
  • barhaṇā - With feathers or in a decorated manner, often symbolizing beauty and grace.

This mantra speaks to the collective strength and wisdom of a community, invoking the Maruts to bestow their blessings. It emphasizes the importance of good understanding and the absence of obstacles in achieving a harmonious existence. The imagery of thunder and illumination suggests a powerful force that brings clarity and prosperity.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Community Building: Encouraging collaboration and unity among members of a community to overcome challenges.
  • Education: Promoting the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom as a means to achieve personal and collective growth.
  • Environmental Awareness: Recognizing the power of nature and the importance of living in harmony with it.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for collective wisdom and strength, urging individuals to seek understanding and work together for a prosperous future. It serves as a reminder of the power of community and the importance of nurturing both intellect and relationships in our daily lives.
