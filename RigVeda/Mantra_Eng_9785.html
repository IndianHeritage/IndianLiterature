आपान्तमञ्जुस त्रिपलप्रभर्मा धुनिः शिमिवाञ्छ चरुमाम् ऋजीषी |सोमो विश्वानि अतसां वनानि नर्वाग इन्द्रं प्रतिमानानि देभुः ||
āpāntamanyus tṛpalaprabharmā dhuniḥ śimīvāñ charumām̐ ṛjīṣī | somo viśvāny atasā vanāni nārvāg indram pratimānāni debhuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āpāntamanyus: Refers to the one who is beyond anger or wrath.
    • tṛpalaprabharmā: The one who shines with threefold brilliance.
    • dhuniḥ: The one who is a source of sound or vibration.
    • śimīvāñ: The one who is auspicious or brings good fortune.
    • charumām̐: The one who is beautiful or charming.
    • ṛjīṣī: The one who is righteous or follows the path of truth.
    • somo: Refers to the moon or the divine nectar.
    • viśvāny: All-encompassing or universal.
    • atasā: With great strength or power.
    • vanāni: Refers to forests or nature.
    • nārvāg: The one who is not to be harmed or is invincible.
    • indram: Refers to Indra, the king of gods.
    • pratimānāni: The one who is worthy of worship or reverence.
    • debhūḥ: The divine or celestial beings.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine entity that embodies qualities such as righteousness, beauty, and strength.
    • The reference to being beyond anger suggests a state of peace and tranquility, which is essential in today's fast-paced world where stress and anger can dominate.
    • The threefold brilliance can be interpreted as the integration of mind, body, and spirit, emphasizing the importance of holistic well-being.
    • The mention of nature (vanāni) highlights the connection between the divine and the natural world, reminding us of the importance of environmental conservation.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents leadership and authority, suggesting that true leadership is characterized by righteousness and the ability to inspire others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of this mantra can create a harmonious work environment, fostering collaboration and innovation.
    • In personal life, practicing patience and understanding, as suggested by being beyond anger, can lead to healthier relationships.
    • Engaging in environmental initiatives reflects the reverence for nature mentioned in the mantra, promoting sustainability and ecological balance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the qualities we should aspire to embody in our lives: peace, righteousness, and a deep connection to nature.
    • Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, from personal relationships to leadership roles and environmental stewardship.
    • By integrating these values into our daily lives, we can contribute to a more harmonious and sustainable world.
