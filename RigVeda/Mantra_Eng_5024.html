या ईं राजानाव ऋतुथा विदधद् रजसो मित्रो वरुणश्चिकेतत् |गम्भीराय रक्षसे हेतिमस्य द्रोघाय चिद्वचसा आनवाय ||
ya īṁ rājānāv ṛtuthā vidadhad rajaso mitro varuṇaś ciketat | gambhīrāya rakṣase hetim asya droghāya cid vacasa ānavāya ||
  • ya īṁ rājānāv: This phrase can be translated as "who gives strength to the kings." It signifies the divine support and guidance provided to rulers, emphasizing the importance of righteous leadership.
  • ṛtuthā vidadhad: This means "who establishes the order." It refers to the maintenance of cosmic and social order (ṛta), which is essential for harmony in society.
  • rajaso mitro: Translated as "the friend of the realm," this phrase highlights the role of divine forces in protecting and nurturing the land and its people.
  • varuṇaś ciketat: This means "Varuna, the all-seeing." Varuna is a deity associated with water and cosmic order, symbolizing the omnipresence of divine justice and moral authority.
  • gambhīrāya rakṣase: This translates to "for the deep protector." It indicates the need for a profound and unwavering protection against chaos and disorder.
  • hetim asya: This means "the cause of this." It refers to the underlying reasons or principles that govern the universe and human actions.
  • drogāya cid vacasa: Translated as "to the deceitful with wisdom," this phrase warns against those who act with malice or deceit, emphasizing the need for discernment and wisdom in dealing with such individuals.
  • ānavāya: This means "to the one who is free from deceit." It signifies the importance of truthfulness and integrity in both personal and societal contexts.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of divine guidance in leadership and the maintenance of cosmic order. It serves as a reminder of the responsibilities of rulers to act justly and protect their realms. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to modern governance, where leaders are encouraged to uphold ethical standards, promote justice, and ensure the welfare of their citizens. The mantra also highlights the importance of wisdom in recognizing deceit and the value of integrity in personal conduct. Overall, it calls for a harmonious society where truth and righteousness prevail, reflecting the timeless relevance of these ancient teachings.
