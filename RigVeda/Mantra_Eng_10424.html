सम अजैषम् इमा अहं सपत्नीर अभिभूवरि |यथाहम अस्य वीरस्य विराजानि जनस्य च ||
sam ajaiṣam imā ahaṁ sapatnīr abhibhūvarī | yathāham asya vīrasya virājāni janasya ca ||
  • Translation: "I have conquered these rivals; I have become the supreme one. Just as I have the glory of this hero and the people."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • sam ajaiṣam: "I have conquered" - This indicates a sense of victory and achievement over challenges or adversaries.
    • imā: "these" - Refers to the rivals or obstacles that one faces in life.
    • ahaṁ: "I" - The individual asserting their identity and agency.
    • sapatnīr: "rivals" - Represents competitors or challenges that one must overcome.
    • abhibhūvarī: "I have become the supreme one" - Suggests attaining a position of power or superiority after overcoming challenges.
    • yathāham: "just as I" - Indicates a comparison or a way of illustrating a point.
    • asya: "of this" - Refers to the hero or the person of significance in the context.
    • vīrasya: "of the hero" - Highlights the qualities of bravery and valor.
    • virājāni: "glory" - The honor and recognition that comes from achievements.
    • janasya: "of the people" - Indicates the collective aspect of society and the importance of community.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive workplace, an employee may face rivals for a promotion. By working hard and demonstrating their skills, they can "conquer" these challenges and achieve recognition, similar to the mantra's assertion of overcoming rivals.
    • A student preparing for exams may encounter various obstacles, such as difficult subjects or distractions. By focusing and persevering, they can achieve academic success, embodying the spirit of the mantra.
    • In sports, an athlete may face tough competitors. Through training and determination, they can rise to the top, reflecting the idea of becoming "the supreme one" in their field.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of overcoming challenges and rivals to achieve greatness. It serves as a reminder that through perseverance and determination, one can attain glory and recognition, not just for oneself but also for the community. This principle is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal development, professional growth, and social interactions. By embodying the spirit of this mantra, individuals can inspire themselves and others to strive for excellence and triumph over adversity.
