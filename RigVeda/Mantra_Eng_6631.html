आ पशुं गासि पृथिवीं वनस्पतिन उषासा नक्तम ओषधिः |विश्वे च नो वसवो विश्ववेदसो धीनाम भूता प्रावितारः ||
ā paśuṁ gāsi pṛthivīṁ vanaspatīn uṣāsā naktam oṣadhīḥ | viśve ca no vasavo viśvavedaso dhīnām bhūta prāvitāraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • paśuṁ: the animals
    • gāsi: you (feminine) sing
    • pṛthivīṁ: the earth
    • vanaspatīn: the plants/trees
    • uṣāsā: the dawn
    • naktam: the night
    • oṣadhīḥ: the herbs
    • viśve: all
    • ca: and
    • no: for us
    • vasavo: the deities
    • viśvavedaso: all-knowing
    • dhīnām: of the wise
    • bhūta: beings
    • prāvitāraḥ: the one who brings forth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the natural elements and deities, emphasizing the interconnectedness of life.
    • It highlights the importance of animals, plants, and herbs, suggesting a harmonious relationship with nature.
    • The mention of dawn and night signifies the cycles of life and the balance between different states of existence.
    • It calls upon the deities to bless and guide the wise, indicating a reliance on divine wisdom for prosperity and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to respect and nurture the land and its resources, promoting sustainable farming practices.
    • In personal life, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom, fostering a deeper understanding of their environment and relationships.
    • In community settings, it can inspire collective efforts to protect nature, such as tree planting initiatives or wildlife conservation programs.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic view of life, urging us to recognize our dependence on nature and the divine.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from environmental conservation to personal growth and community building.
    • By embracing the teachings of this mantra, individuals and societies can work towards a more harmonious and sustainable existence.
