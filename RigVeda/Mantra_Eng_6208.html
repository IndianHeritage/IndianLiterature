उशनā यत परावत उक्ष्णो रन्ध्रम अयातन | द्यौर न चक्रदद भीया ||
uśanā yat parāvata ukṣṇo randhram ayātana | dyaur na cakradad bhiyā ||
  • uśanā: This term refers to the dawn or the light of the morning. In a broader sense, it symbolizes new beginnings and enlightenment.
  • yat: This word means "that" or "which," indicating a connection or a reference to something significant.
  • parāvata: This term can be interpreted as "the highest" or "the supreme," often referring to the divine or celestial realms.
  • ukṣṇo: This word means "of the sun" or "radiant," emphasizing the power and energy associated with sunlight.
  • randhram: This term translates to "the opening" or "the gap," which can signify opportunities or pathways in life.
  • ayātana: This word means "the abode" or "the dwelling," suggesting a place of stability or permanence.
  • dyaur: This term refers to "the sky" or "heaven," representing the vastness and the divine nature of the universe.
  • na: This word means "not," indicating negation or absence.
  • cakradad: This term can be interpreted as "the wheel" or "the cycle," often symbolizing the cyclical nature of life and time.
  • bhiyā: This word means "from fear" or "due to fear," indicating a sense of caution or apprehension.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation that highlights the importance of light, opportunities, and the divine presence in overcoming fear and challenges. It emphasizes the connection between the dawn (uśanā) and the divine (parāvata), suggesting that enlightenment and new beginnings are always available to us, provided we are open to them.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as dawn brings light, individuals can seek new beginnings in their personal and professional lives. For example, someone starting a new job or moving to a new city can embrace the opportunities that come with change.
  • Overcoming Fear: The mention of "bhiyā" reminds us that fear can often hold us back. By recognizing our fears and seeking the light (uśanā), we can find the courage to face challenges head-on.
  • Spiritual Awakening: The connection to the divine (parāvata) encourages individuals to seek spiritual enlightenment. This can be through meditation, prayer, or other spiritual practices that help one connect with a higher power.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to seek light and opportunities in their lives while overcoming fear. It serves as a reminder that new beginnings are always possible, and by connecting with the divine, we can navigate the challenges of life with courage and clarity.
