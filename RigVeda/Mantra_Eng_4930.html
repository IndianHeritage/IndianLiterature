विष्वे देवाः श्रिणुतेमं हवम् मे ये अन्तरिक्षे य उप द्यवि ष्ठा |ये अग्निजिह्वा उता वा यजत्रा आसद्यास्मिन बरहिषि मादयध्वम् ||
viśve devāḥ śṛṇutemaṁ havam me ye antarikṣe ya upa dyavi ṣṭha | ye agnijihvā uta vā yajatrā āsadyāsmin barhiṣi mādayadhvam ||
  • Translation: "O all gods, listen to this my invocation; those who dwell in the sky and those who are in the atmosphere; those with fiery tongues, or those who are the offerings of the worshippers, come and enjoy this sacrifice."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • viśve devāḥ: All gods
    • śṛṇutemaṁ: Listen to this
    • havam me: My invocation/sacrifice
    • ye antarikṣe: Those who dwell in the atmosphere
    • upa dyavi ṣṭha: Those who are in the sky
    • ye agnijihvā: Those with fiery tongues
    • uta vā: Or those who
    • yajatrā: The offerings of the worshippers
    • āsadyāsmin: Come and enjoy this
    • barhiṣi: In this sacred place
    • mādayadhvam: Be pleased/enjoy
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra is a call to all divine entities, inviting them to partake in the offerings made during a ritual.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and connection with the divine, suggesting that the act of worship is not solitary but a collective experience.
    • In real life, this can be likened to community gatherings where people come together to celebrate, share, and invoke blessings, such as festivals or communal prayers.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of acknowledging and inviting divine presence in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions, whether in worship or daily life, should be inclusive and respectful of the greater universe.
    • In modern contexts, this can be applied in various settings, such as environmental movements where collective action is taken to honor and protect nature, which is often seen as a manifestation of the divine.
