युवं श्वेतम् पेदव इन्द्रजूतम् अहिहनम् अश्विनादत्तम् अश्वम् |जोहूत्रम् आर्यो अभिभूतिम उग्रं सहस्रसां वृषणं वीढ्वङ्गम् ||
yuvaṁ śvetam pedava indrajūtam ahihanam aśvinādattam aśvam | johūtram aryo abhibhūtim ugraṁ sahasrasāṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ vīḍvaṅgam ||
  • yuvaṁ: This word translates to "young" or "youthful." It signifies vitality and energy, often associated with new beginnings and potential.
  • śvetam: Meaning "white," this term symbolizes purity, clarity, and auspiciousness. In many cultures, white is associated with peace and new beginnings.
  • pedava: This can be interpreted as "the one who is strong" or "powerful." It denotes strength and resilience, qualities that are essential in overcoming challenges.
  • indrajūtam: Referring to "the one who is protected by Indra," this term emphasizes divine protection and guidance. Indra is often seen as a king of gods, representing power and authority.
  • ahihanam: This word means "the one who is fearless" or "the one who conquers." It suggests bravery and the ability to face adversities without fear.
  • aśvinādattam: This term refers to "the one who is bestowed by the Ashvins," the twin gods of medicine and health. It signifies blessings of health and well-being.
  • aśvam: Meaning "horse," this symbolizes speed, strength, and the ability to overcome obstacles. In ancient cultures, horses were often seen as symbols of power and freedom.
  • johūtram: This can be interpreted as "the one who is a protector" or "the one who offers shelter." It emphasizes the importance of safety and security in life.
  • aryo: This term means "noble" or "honorable." It signifies the importance of integrity and moral values in one's life.
  • abhibhūtim: This word translates to "the one who is victorious" or "the one who overcomes." It emphasizes the importance of triumph over challenges.
  • ugraṁ: Meaning "fierce" or "powerful," this term denotes strength and intensity, often associated with determination and focus.
  • sahasrasāṁ: This translates to "thousands" or "many," indicating abundance and prosperity in various aspects of life.
  • vṛṣaṇaṁ: This word means "the one who is strong" or "the one who is capable." It emphasizes the importance of capability and strength in achieving goals.
  • vīḍvaṅgam: This term refers to "the one who is wise" or "the one who possesses knowledge." It signifies the importance of wisdom and understanding in navigating life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a powerful message about youth, strength, protection, and wisdom. It emphasizes the importance of being fearless and noble while striving for victory and abundance in life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal development, encouraging individuals to embrace their youthful energy, seek divine protection, and cultivate wisdom. It serves as a reminder to face challenges with courage and integrity, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
