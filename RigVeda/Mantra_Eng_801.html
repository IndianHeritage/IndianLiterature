एता चिकीर्त्वो भूमा न पाही देवानां जन्म मार्तांश्च विद्वान् ||
etā cikitvo bhūmā ni pāhi devānāṁ janma martām̐ś ca vidvān ||
  • etā - This word translates to "this" or "these," indicating a focus on the present or immediate context.
  • cikitvo - This term can be understood as "knowledge" or "awareness," suggesting a deep understanding or insight.
  • bhūmā - Meaning "earth" or "ground," it symbolizes the physical world and our existence within it.
  • ni - A prefix that can imply "down" or "in," often used to denote a sense of grounding or stability.
  • pāhi - This word means "protect" or "guard," indicating a plea for safeguarding or preservation.
  • devānāṁ - Translated as "of the gods," it refers to divine beings or higher powers that oversee the universe.
  • janma - This means "birth" or "life," highlighting the cycle of existence and the importance of life experiences.
  • martām̐ś - Referring to "mortals" or "human beings," it emphasizes the human condition and our vulnerabilities.
  • ca - A conjunction meaning "and," linking the concepts together.
  • vidvān - This term means "wise" or "knowledgeable," suggesting the importance of wisdom in understanding life.

  • Meaning: The mantra expresses a plea for protection and guidance from the divine, emphasizing the importance of knowledge and awareness in navigating the earthly existence of mortals. It highlights the relationship between the divine and human beings, suggesting that wisdom is essential for understanding and thriving in life.

  • Real-life Example: In everyday life, individuals often seek guidance and protection in various forms, such as through prayer, meditation, or seeking advice from mentors. For instance, a student preparing for exams may pray for divine support and wisdom to succeed, reflecting the essence of this mantra.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine protection and the pursuit of wisdom. It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals face challenges, uncertainties, or transitions. By invoking the divine and seeking knowledge, one can navigate life's complexities with greater clarity and strength, fostering a deeper connection with both the spiritual and physical realms.
