अदित्युतत sv अपाको विभावाग्नि यजस्व रोडसी उरूचि |आयुः न यं नमसाऽ रात्रहव्याः अञ्जन्ति सुप्रयसम् पञ्च जनाः ||
adidyutat sv apāko vibhāvāgne yajasva rodasī urūcī | āyuṁ na yaṁ namasā rātahavyā añjanti suprayasam pañca janāḥ ||
  • Adidyutat: This term can be interpreted as "shining" or "radiant." It signifies the brilliance of the divine or the cosmic energy that illuminates the universe.
  • Sv apāko: This phrase means "the one who is pure" or "the one who is free from impurities." It emphasizes the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions.
  • Vibhāvāgne: This refers to the "manifesting fire" or "the fire that brings forth." Fire is often seen as a symbol of transformation and energy in various cultures.
  • Yajasva: This means "perform the sacrifice" or "engage in worship." It highlights the significance of rituals and devotion in connecting with the divine.
  • Rodasī: This term refers to the "two worlds" or "the heavens and the earth." It symbolizes the connection between the spiritual and the material realms.
  • Urūcī: This means "the vastness" or "the expanse." It signifies the limitless nature of the universe and the divine.
  • Āyuṁ: This translates to "life" or "longevity." It emphasizes the value of a long and fulfilling life.
  • Na yaṁ: This means "not that" or "not the one." It suggests a distinction or a negation of something undesirable.
  • Namasā: This term means "with reverence" or "with respect." It indicates the importance of humility and devotion in one's approach to the divine.
  • Rātahavyā: This refers to "the offerings made during the night" or "the sacrifices of the night." It signifies the continuous nature of devotion and the importance of nighttime rituals.
  • Añjanti: This means "they who are engaged" or "they who perform." It highlights the active participation of individuals in spiritual practices.
  • Suprayasam: This translates to "great effort" or "excellent endeavor." It emphasizes the need for dedication and hard work in achieving one's goals.
  • Pañca janāḥ: This means "the five people" or "the five beings." It can symbolize the collective effort of individuals in a community or society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, purity, and the interconnectedness of life and the universe. It emphasizes the importance of engaging in spiritual practices with reverence and dedication. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects, such as personal growth, community service, and environmental stewardship. By embodying the principles of purity, effort, and reverence, individuals can contribute to a harmonious and fulfilling life, both for themselves and for those around them. The mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in everyday life and the significance of our actions in the broader cosmic context.
