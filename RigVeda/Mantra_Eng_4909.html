नम इद उग्रं नम आविवासे नमो दाधार पृथिवीं उता द्यां |नमो देवेषु नम ईश एषां कृतं चिद एनो नमसां आविवासे ||
nama id ugraṁ nama ā vivāse namo dādhāra pṛthivīm uta dyām | namo devebhyo nama īśa eṣāṁ kṛtaṁ cid eno namasā vivāse ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nama: Salutation or bowing down
    • id: This
    • ugraṁ: Mighty or powerful
    • ā: To
    • vivāse: To the one who resides or dwells
    • dādhāra: He who supports or sustains
    • pṛthivīm: The Earth
    • uta: And
    • dyām: The sky or heavens
    • devebhyo: To the gods
    • īśa: Lord or master
    • eṣāṁ: Of these
    • kṛtaṁ: Done or created
    • cid: Consciousness or awareness
    • eno: To that
    • namasā: With reverence or salutation
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a profound expression of reverence and acknowledgment of the powerful forces of nature and the divine. It begins with a salutation to the mighty and powerful, recognizing the presence of the divine in both the Earth and the heavens. The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all elements of existence, acknowledging the support and sustenance provided by these forces.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we often take for granted the natural elements around us. This mantra reminds us to appreciate the Earth (pṛthivīm) for its resources and the sky (dyām) for its life-giving properties, such as sunlight and rain.
    • In moments of gratitude, such as during a harvest festival, people may recite similar mantras to honor the Earth and the divine forces that contribute to their sustenance.
    • In environmental conservation efforts, this mantra can serve as a reminder of our responsibility to protect the Earth and its resources, recognizing that we are all interconnected.
  • Conclusion and Significance: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for reverence towards the natural world and the divine. It teaches us to acknowledge the power and support that the Earth and heavens provide. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in practices of gratitude, environmental awareness, and spiritual reflection. By recognizing the interconnectedness of all life, we can foster a deeper respect for nature and the divine forces that sustain us.
