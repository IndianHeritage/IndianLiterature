आ यातं मित्रावरुणा सुशस्त्य उप प्रियā नमसā हूयमानā |सं याव अप्नःस्थो अपसेव जनाञ छ्रुधीयतश चिद यतथो महित्वा ||
ā yātam mitrāvaruṇā suśasty upa priyā namasā hūyamānā | saṁ yāv apnaḥstho apaseva janāñ chrudhīyataś cid yatatho mahitvā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yātam: "May we be guided" or "Let us go forth."
    • mitrāvaruṇā: "By the divine forces of Mitra and Varuna," representing friendship and cosmic order.
    • suśasty: "With auspiciousness" or "with goodness."
    • upa priyā: "To that which is dear" or "to what is cherished."
    • namasā: "With reverence" or "with respect."
    • hūyamānā: "Being invoked" or "being called upon."
    • saṁ yāv: "As long as" or "until."
    • apnaḥstho: "Those who are steadfast" or "those who are firmly established."
    • apaseva: "In service" or "in devotion."
    • janāñ: "People" or "the community."
    • chrudhīyataś: "With wisdom" or "with understanding."
    • cid: "That which is consciousness" or "that which is awareness."
    • yatatho: "As it is" or "in its true form."
    • mahitvā: "Greatness" or "majesty."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance from divine forces, specifically Mitra and Varuna, who symbolize friendship and the moral order of the universe.
    • It calls for auspiciousness and reverence in our actions and thoughts, suggesting that we should approach our endeavors with respect and a sense of duty.
    • The mantra highlights the significance of community and collective wisdom, urging individuals to remain steadfast in their service to others.
    • It also points to the importance of consciousness and awareness in understanding the true nature of existence and the greatness that lies within it.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied by fostering a culture of teamwork and respect, where colleagues support each other and work towards common goals with a sense of duty.
    • In community service, individuals can embody the principles of this mantra by engaging in selfless acts that benefit others, thereby promoting collective well-being.
    • In personal relationships, approaching interactions with reverence and understanding can lead to stronger bonds and a more harmonious environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of approaching life with respect, wisdom, and a sense of community.
    • Its significance lies in its call for individuals to be aware of their actions and their impact on others, promoting a life of service and understanding.
    • In today's fast-paced world, the teachings of this mantra can guide us towards a more meaningful and harmonious existence, both personally and collectively.
