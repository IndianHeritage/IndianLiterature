वाजिन्तमाया सह्यसे सुपित्र्य तृषु च्यवानो अनु जातवेदसे |अनुद्रे चिद यो धृतसता वरं सते महिन्तमाया धन्वनेद अविष्यते ||
vājintamāya sahyase supitrya tṛṣu cyavāno anu jātavedase | anudre cid yo dhṛṣatā varaṁ sate mahintamāya dhanvaned aviṣyate ||
  • vājintamāya: This term can be broken down into "vāj" meaning strength or vigor, and "intama" which implies the best or the most excellent. Thus, it refers to the one who possesses supreme strength or excellence.
  • sahyase: This word means "to assist" or "to support." It indicates a sense of collaboration or partnership.
  • supitrya: This can be interpreted as "well-nourished" or "prosperous." It suggests a state of abundance and well-being.
  • tṛṣu: This word means "in the three worlds" or "in all realms," indicating a universal presence or influence.
  • cyavāno: This term means "one who has fallen" or "one who has deviated." It can refer to someone who has strayed from their path or purpose.
  • anu: This means "following" or "after," suggesting a sequence or continuity.
  • jātavedase: This refers to "the one who knows all births" or "the all-knowing." It is often associated with Agni, the fire god, who is a witness to all actions.
  • anudre: This means "to be free from obstacles" or "to overcome challenges." It implies a sense of liberation.
  • cid: This word means "consciousness" or "awareness." It signifies a state of being aware or enlightened.
  • yo: This means "who" or "that one," referring to a person or entity.
  • dhṛṣatā: This term means "with courage" or "with determination." It emphasizes the importance of bravery in facing challenges.
  • varaṁ: This means "boon" or "blessing." It signifies a gift or favor that is sought after.
  • sate: This word means "truth" or "reality." It indicates a connection to what is genuine and authentic.
  • mahintamāya: This can be interpreted as "to the great one" or "to the supreme being," indicating reverence towards a higher power.
  • dhanvaned: This term means "the one who wields a bow" or "the archer." It symbolizes strength and the ability to protect.
  • aviṣyate: This means "is not obstructed" or "is not hindered." It suggests a state of freedom and unobstructed progress.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, support, and prosperity in overcoming challenges. It highlights the role of courage and awareness in seeking blessings and truth. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, teamwork, and leadership. For instance, in a workplace setting, individuals can draw upon their strengths and collaborate effectively to achieve common goals, while remaining aware of their challenges and striving for excellence. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek support from others and to be courageous in the face of adversity, ultimately leading to a prosperous and fulfilling life.
