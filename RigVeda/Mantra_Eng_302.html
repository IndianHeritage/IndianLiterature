स नो दूराच्चासाच्च निमर्त्यादघायोः |पाहि सदमिद्विश्वायुः ||
sa no dūrāc cāsāc ca ni martyād aghāyoḥ | pāhi sadam id viśvāyuḥ ||
  • sa no dūrāc: This phrase translates to "may he protect us from afar." It signifies a plea for divine protection and guidance from a distance, suggesting that the divine presence is always watching over us, even when we are not directly aware of it.
  • cāsāc ca ni martyād: This part means "and from the mortal dangers." It acknowledges the existence of threats and challenges in the human experience, emphasizing the need for protection from these dangers that can affect our lives.
  • aghāyoḥ: This word refers to "the evils" or "the harmful forces." It highlights the presence of negativity or malevolent influences in the world that can lead to suffering or harm.
  • pāhi: This translates to "protect" or "guard." It is a direct request for safeguarding from the aforementioned dangers and evils.
  • sadam id viśvāyuḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we attain the abode of the all-pervading." It expresses a desire to reach a state of universal well-being and harmony, where one is connected to the greater cosmos and its energies.

  • Real-Life Example: In everyday life, individuals often face various challenges, such as health issues, financial difficulties, or emotional struggles. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek protection and guidance from higher powers or the universe, encouraging individuals to remain hopeful and resilient in the face of adversity.
  • Application in Daily Life: People can recite this mantra during meditation or prayer, especially when feeling overwhelmed by life's challenges. It can also be used in rituals or gatherings to invoke a sense of collective protection and unity among participants.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for protection and guidance against the evils and dangers of life. It serves as a reminder that, despite the challenges we face, there is a higher power that watches over us. By incorporating this mantra into our daily practices, we can cultivate a sense of security and connection to the universe, fostering resilience and hope in our lives.
