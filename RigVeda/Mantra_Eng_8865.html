आपो अद्यांन्व आचारिषं रसना सम अगस्महि |पयस्वान अग्न आ गहि तं मा सं सृजा वर्चसा ||
āpo adyānv acāriṣaṁ rasena sam agasmahi | payasvān agna ā gahi tam mā saṁ sṛja varcasā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āpo: Waters
    • adyānv: Today
    • acāriṣaṁ: I invoke
    • rasena: With essence
    • sam: Together
    • agasmahi: We have come
    • payasvān: O nourisher
    • agna: O Fire
    • ā gahi: Come to us
    • tam: That
    • mā: Do not
    • saṁ: Together
    • sṛja: Create
    • varcasā: With brilliance
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer invoking the waters and fire, elements that are essential for life and sustenance.
    • The phrase "āpo adyānv acāriṣaṁ" signifies a call to the waters to come forth today, highlighting the importance of water in our lives.
    • "rasena sam agasmahi" emphasizes the unity and essence of life that water brings, suggesting that we are all interconnected through this vital resource.
    • "payasvān agna ā gahi" is a plea to the nourishing fire to come to us, symbolizing the warmth and energy that fire provides, essential for cooking and warmth.
    • The concluding part "tam mā saṁ sṛja varcasā" expresses a desire for the fire not to create negativity or harm, but to bring forth brilliance and positivity in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the importance of water is paramount. Farmers pray for rain, recognizing that without water, crops cannot grow, which directly affects food supply.
    • In daily life, we rely on fire for cooking and heating. The invocation of fire in this mantra can be seen as a reminder to respect and harness fire's power responsibly.
    • In spiritual practices, water is often used for purification rituals, symbolizing the cleansing of the mind and body, while fire is used in ceremonies to symbolize transformation and energy.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of water and fire in our lives, reminding us of their essential roles in sustenance, energy, and spiritual practices.
    • It teaches us to respect these elements and to seek their blessings for a harmonious and prosperous life.
    • In a broader context, it encourages us to recognize our interconnectedness with nature and the importance of maintaining balance and harmony with the environment.
