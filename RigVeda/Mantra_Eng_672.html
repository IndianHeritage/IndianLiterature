नू चित सहोजा अमृतो न तुन्दते होता यद दूतो अभवद विवस्वतः |वि साधिष्ठेभिः पथिभी रजो मम आ देवताता हविषा विवासति ||
nū cit sahojā amṛto ni tundate hotā yad dūto abhavad vivasvataḥ | vi sādhiṣṭhebhiḥ pathibhī rajo mama ā devatātā haviṣā vivāsati ||
  • nū cit sahojā amṛto ni tundate: This phrase can be translated to "Indeed, the immortal (amṛta) does not perish (ni tundate) when it is born (sahojā)." It emphasizes the idea of eternal existence and the unchanging nature of the soul or spirit, which transcends physical death.
  • hotā yad dūto abhavad vivasvataḥ: This part means "the one who invokes (hotā) is like a messenger (dūto) of the radiant one (vivasvataḥ)." It suggests that those who call upon the divine or higher powers act as intermediaries, channeling spiritual energy and wisdom into the world.
  • vi sādhiṣṭhebhiḥ pathibhī rajo mama: This translates to "with the support of the established (sādhiṣṭhebhiḥ) paths (pathibhī), my desires (rajo) are fulfilled." It indicates that by following the right paths and principles, one can achieve their goals and aspirations.
  • ā devatātā haviṣā vivāsati: This means "the divine essence (devatātā) is nourished (vivāsati) by the offerings (haviṣā)." It highlights the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine and receiving blessings in return.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates profound philosophical concepts about immortality, the role of divine messengers, the importance of following righteous paths, and the significance of offerings in spiritual practices. In real life, it can be applied in various ways:

  • Understanding the eternal nature of the soul can provide comfort in times of loss, reminding us that life continues beyond physical existence.
  • Recognizing the role of mentors and guides in our lives as messengers of wisdom can inspire us to seek knowledge and support from others.
  • Following ethical and moral paths in our personal and professional lives can lead to the fulfillment of our desires and aspirations.
  • Engaging in rituals and acts of gratitude can strengthen our connection to the divine and enhance our spiritual well-being.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of spiritual practices, and the pursuit of higher truths in our everyday existence.
