सं वत्स इव मातृभिर इन्दुर हिन्वानो अज्यते |देववीर मदो मतीभिः परिष्कृतः ||
saṁ vatsa iva mātṛbhir indur hinvāno ajyate | devāvīr mado matibhiḥ pariṣkṛtaḥ ||
  • saṁ vatsa iva mātṛbhir: This phrase can be translated as "like a calf with its mothers." It signifies the nurturing and protective relationship between a child and its mother. In real life, this can be seen in the bond between parents and their children, where the parents provide guidance, support, and love.
  • indur hinvāno ajyate: Here, "indur" refers to the moon, and "hinvāno ajyate" means "is being invoked or called forth." The moon is often associated with calmness, reflection, and the cyclical nature of life. In practical terms, this can relate to how we seek tranquility and guidance in our lives, especially during challenging times.
  • devāvīr mado matibhiḥ: This translates to "the divine heroes are filled with joy." It emphasizes the importance of joy and positivity in our lives. In everyday scenarios, this can be observed in how teamwork and collaboration can lead to success and happiness, much like how a group of people working together can achieve great things.
  • pariṣkṛtaḥ: This word means "refined" or "purified." It suggests the idea of improvement and growth. In real life, this can be seen in personal development, where individuals strive to better themselves through education, self-reflection, and experiences.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of nurturing relationships, the pursuit of tranquility, the joy of collaboration, and the journey of personal growth. Its significance lies in reminding us of the interconnectedness of life and the importance of community and self-improvement. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to foster strong familial bonds, seek inner peace, work harmoniously with others, and continuously strive for personal excellence. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
