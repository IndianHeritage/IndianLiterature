यो धार्ता भुवनानां य उष्राणाम अपिच्याऽ वेद नामानि गुह्याः |स कविः काव्यां पुरु रूपं द्यौर इव पुष्यति नभन्तां अन्यके समे ||
yo dhartā bhuvanānāṁ ya usrāṇām apīcyā3 veda nāmāni guhyā | sa kaviḥ kāvyā puru rūpaṁ dyaur iva puṣyati nabhantām anyake same ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • dhartā: sustainer
    • bhuvanānāṁ: of the worlds
    • ya: who
    • usrāṇām: of the cows (or beings)
    • apīcyā: even
    • veda: knows
    • nāmāni: names
    • guhyā: secret
    • sa: he
    • kaviḥ: poet or seer
    • kāvyā: of poetry
    • puru: many
    • rūpaṁ: forms
    • dyaur: sky
    • iva: like
    • puṣyati: nourishes
    • nabhantām: of the heavens
    • anyake: others
    • same: in the same way
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine figure who sustains the worlds and knows the secret names of all beings.
    • The reference to "cows" can symbolize nourishment and abundance, as cows are often seen as a source of sustenance in many cultures.
    • The term "kaviḥ" indicates a seer or poet, suggesting that this figure possesses profound wisdom and creativity.
    • The imagery of the sky and heavens implies a connection to the vastness of existence and the nurturing aspect of the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In our daily lives, we often rely on individuals who sustain us, whether they are family members, friends, or mentors. They provide emotional and spiritual nourishment, much like the sustainer mentioned in the mantra.
    • In the context of creativity, artists, writers, and thinkers can be seen as modern-day "kavis," who bring forth new ideas and forms, enriching our cultural landscape.
    • The concept of knowing the "secret names" can be likened to understanding the deeper truths of life, which can lead to personal growth and enlightenment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of sustenance, wisdom, and creativity in our lives.
    • It encourages us to recognize and appreciate those who nourish us, both physically and spiritually.
    • In a broader sense, it reminds us of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine presence that nurtures the universe.
    • By embodying the qualities of the "kavi," we can contribute to the world through our own unique expressions and insights.
