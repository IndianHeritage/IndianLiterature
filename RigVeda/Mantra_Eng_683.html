आ सूर्ये न रश्मयो ध्रुवासो वैश्वानरे दधिरे अग्ना वसूनि |या पर्वतेष्वोषधिष्वाप्सु या मानवेष्वअसि तस्य राजा ||
ā sūrye na raśmayo dhruvāso vaiśvānare dadhire 'gnā vasūni | yā parvateṣv oṣadhīṣv apsu yā mānuṣeṣv asi tasya rājā ||
  • Transliteration: ā sūrye na raśmayo dhruvāso vaiśvānare dadhire 'gnā vasūni | yā parvateṣv oṣadhīṣv apsu yā mānuṣeṣv asi tasya rājā ||
  • Meaning:
    • ā sūrye: "In the sun" - This refers to the source of light and energy, symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
    • na raśmayo: "not the rays" - This indicates a deeper understanding beyond superficial appearances.
    • dhruvāso: "the fixed" - Suggests stability and permanence, representing eternal truths.
    • vaiśvānare: "in the universal fire" - This symbolizes the universal energy that sustains life.
    • dadhire: "they hold" - Implies possession or control over something significant.
    • 'gnā vasūni: "the treasures of fire" - Refers to the valuable aspects of life, such as knowledge, wisdom, and resources.
    • yā parvateṣv: "that which is in the mountains" - Represents the natural world and its resources.
    • oṣadhīṣv: "in the herbs" - Symbolizes healing and nourishment, both physical and spiritual.
    • apsu: "in the waters" - Represents purity, life, and the flow of energy.
    • yā mānuṣeṣv: "that which is in humans" - Refers to human qualities, consciousness, and the potential for growth.
    • asi tasya rājā: "is the king of that" - Indicates the supreme authority or essence that governs all these elements.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The sun is often seen as a source of life and energy, much like how knowledge and education illuminate our paths in life.
    • In a corporate setting, understanding the deeper truths of a business (beyond just profits) can lead to sustainable success.
    • Natural resources, such as herbs and water, are essential for health and well-being, emphasizing the importance of environmental conservation.
    • Human qualities like empathy and wisdom can lead to better relationships and community building.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all elements of life—nature, knowledge, and humanity.
    • Its significance lies in recognizing the deeper truths that govern our existence and the importance of nurturing both our inner and outer worlds.
    • In real life, applying the wisdom of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious and balanced life, where we respect nature, seek knowledge, and cultivate our human qualities.
