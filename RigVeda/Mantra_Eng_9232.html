यस्मिन वयं दधिमाः शंसम् इन्द्रे यः शिश्राय मगवाः कामम् अस्मे |आराच् चित् सन् भयताम् अस्य शत्रुर् न्यस्मै द्युम्ना जन्या नमन्ताम् ||
yasmin vayaṁ dadhimā śaṁsam indre yaḥ śiśrāya maghavā kāmam asme | ārāc cit san bhayatām asya śatrur ny asmai dyumnā janyā namantām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasmin: in whom
    • vayaṁ: we
    • dadhimā: are endowed
    • śaṁsam: with peace
    • indre: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • yaḥ: who
    • śiśrāya: grants
    • maghavā: the powerful
    • kāmam: desires
    • asme: for us
    • ārāc: from afar
    • cit: with wisdom
    • san: being
    • bhayatām: from fear
    • asya: of this
    • śatrur: enemy
    • ny: not
    • asmai: to him
    • dyumnā: with glory
    • janyā: born
    • namantām: may bow down
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who is often associated with strength and power.
    • It emphasizes the importance of peace and the fulfillment of desires, suggesting that true strength lies in the ability to provide for the needs of others.
    • The phrase "from afar" indicates that divine help can come from unexpected places, and wisdom is essential in recognizing and receiving it.
    • The mantra also addresses the concept of overcoming fear, particularly in the face of adversaries or challenges.
    • It suggests that glory and respect should be given to those who embody these qualities, promoting a sense of community and support.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of Indra—strength, wisdom, and the ability to fulfill the needs of their team—can create a harmonious and productive environment.
    • In personal relationships, being able to provide emotional support and understanding can help overcome fears and challenges, fostering deeper connections.
    • In community service, individuals who work to uplift others and provide for their needs can inspire respect and admiration, creating a cycle of positivity and support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of strength, wisdom, and the fulfillment of desires in our lives.
    • It encourages us to seek peace and support from the divine while also recognizing the power of community and mutual respect.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, stronger relationships, and a more harmonious society.
