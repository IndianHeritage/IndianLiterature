अभि त्यं मेषम् पुरुहूतम् ऋग्मियम इन्द्रं गीरभिर मदता वस्वो अर्णवम् |यस्य dyāvo न विचरन्ति मानवः भुजे मंहिष्ठम् अभि विप्रम अर्चत ||
abhi tyam meṣam puruhūtam ṛgmiyam indraṁ gīrbhir madatā vasvo arṇavam | yasya dyāvo na vicaranti mānuṣā bhuje maṁhiṣṭham abhi vipram arcata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards
    • tyam: that
    • meṣam: sheep or ram (symbolizing strength and sacrifice)
    • puruhūtam: invoked by many (referring to the divine)
    • ṛgmiyam: related to hymns or praises
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods
    • gīrbhir: with songs or hymns
    • madatā: intoxicated or filled with joy
    • vasvo: of the wealth or prosperity
    • arṇavam: ocean or vastness
    • yasya: whose
    • dyāvo: heavens
    • na: not
    • vicaranti: wander or stray
    • mānuṣā: humans
    • bhuje: in the presence of
    • maṁhiṣṭham: the most excellent or supreme
    • abhi: towards
    • vipram: the learned or wise
    • arcata: worship or honor
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the king of gods, emphasizing the importance of divine blessings in achieving prosperity and strength.
    • The reference to "meṣam" (sheep or ram) symbolizes sacrifice and the idea that strength comes from giving and sharing.
    • The phrase "puruhūtam" indicates that many people are calling upon the divine, highlighting the communal aspect of worship.
    • By mentioning "gīrbhir madatā," it suggests that joy and intoxication come from the divine presence, which can be experienced through hymns and praises.
    • The line about "dyāvo na vicaranti mānuṣā" implies that when humans are in the presence of the divine, they are grounded and do not stray from their path.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering or festival, people come together to sing hymns and praise the divine, creating a sense of unity and shared joy.
    • In personal life, individuals may find strength and guidance through prayer or meditation, feeling a connection to something greater that helps them navigate challenges.
    • Acts of charity and sacrifice, such as donating to those in need, reflect the essence of "meṣam," where strength is derived from giving.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine connection, communal worship, and the strength found in sacrifice and joy.
    • It teaches that by honoring the divine and engaging in collective praise, individuals can find guidance and stability in their lives.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can inspire community service, collective celebrations, and personal spiritual practices that foster a sense of belonging and purpose.
