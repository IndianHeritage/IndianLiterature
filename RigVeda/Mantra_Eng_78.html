एवाह्यस्य सूनृतā विरप्शी गोमती मही |पक्वा शाखा न दाशुषे ||
evā hy asya sūnṛtā virapśī gomatī mahī | pakvā śākhā na dāśuṣe ||
  • evā hy asya: This phrase can be translated as "Indeed, this is." It emphasizes the certainty and importance of what follows.
  • sūnṛtā: This word means "truth" or "righteousness." It signifies the value of honesty and integrity in one's actions.
  • virapśī: This term refers to "abundance" or "prosperity." It suggests a state of richness, not just in material wealth but also in spiritual and emotional fulfillment.
  • gomatī: This word can be interpreted as "the one who possesses cows" or "wealth." In ancient times, cows were a symbol of wealth and prosperity, representing sustenance and livelihood.
  • mahī: This translates to "earth" or "greatness." It signifies the vastness and nurturing aspect of the earth, which provides for all living beings.
  • pakvā: This word means "mature" or "ripe." It indicates the importance of growth and development, whether in personal character or in the fruits of one's labor.
  • śākhā: This translates to "branch" or "offshoot." It symbolizes the various paths and opportunities that arise from a single source, much like branches from a tree.
  • na dāśuṣe: This phrase means "not for the unworthy" or "not for the deceitful." It emphasizes that the rewards of righteousness and abundance are reserved for those who act with integrity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of living a life rooted in truth and righteousness, leading to prosperity and growth. It highlights the significance of integrity in one's actions and the rewards that come from being virtuous. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in business ethics, personal relationships, and community interactions. By adhering to these principles, individuals can cultivate a life of abundance and fulfillment, ensuring that their efforts yield positive outcomes not just for themselves, but for society as a whole. The mantra serves as a reminder that true wealth is not merely material but is deeply connected to one's character and the impact one has on the world around them.
