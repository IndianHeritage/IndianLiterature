नृचक्षाऽएष दिवो मध्य आस्त आपप्रिवान रोडसी अन्तरिक्षम् |स विश्वाचीर अभि चष्टे घृताचीर अन्तरā पूर्वम् अपरं च केतुम् ||
nṛcakṣā eṣa divo madhya āsta āpaprivān rodasī antarikṣam | sa viśvācīr abhi caṣṭe ghṛtācīr antarā pūrvam aparaṁ ca ketum ||
  • nṛcakṣā: This term can be broken down into "nṛ" meaning "man" or "human" and "cakṣā" meaning "eye" or "sight". Together, it refers to the "eye of man" or the perception of humanity.
  • eṣa: This means "this" or "he\ indicating a presence or a specific entity.
  • divo: This translates to "of the sky" or "heaven\ suggesting a divine or celestial context.
  • madhya: Meaning "middle" or "center\ it indicates a central position or balance.
  • āsta: This means "is established" or "sits\ indicating a state of being or presence.
  • āpaprivān: This term can be interpreted as "one who is free from sin" or "pure\ suggesting a state of moral or spiritual cleanliness.
  • rodasī: This refers to "the two worlds" or "the earth and sky\ indicating a connection between different realms.
  • antarikṣam: This means "the space between" or "the atmosphere\ representing the area that connects the earth and the heavens.
  • sa: This translates to "he" or "that\ referring to the subject of the mantra.
  • viśvācīr: This term means "all-seeing" or "omniscient\ indicating a comprehensive awareness or understanding.
  • abhi: This means "towards" or "in the direction of\ suggesting movement or intention.
  • caṣṭe: This translates to "acts" or "performs\ indicating action or behavior.
  • ghṛtācīr: This term can be interpreted as "one who is rich in ghee" or "nourishing\ suggesting abundance and sustenance.
  • antarā: Meaning "between" or "within\ indicating an inner or intermediary state.
  • pūrvam: This translates to "before" or "previous\ indicating a sense of time or precedence.
  • aparaṁ: Meaning "the latter" or "the next\ suggesting a continuation or subsequent state.
  • ca: This means "and\ indicating a connection or addition.
  • ketum: This term can be interpreted as "the banner" or "the sign\ suggesting a symbol or representation.

Conclusion: The mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of humanity, the divine, and the cosmos. It emphasizes the importance of perception, moral purity, and the balance between different realms of existence. In real life, this can be applied to the idea of being aware of our actions and their impact on the world around us. It encourages individuals to strive for a higher understanding and to act with intention and purity, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in our lives. This mantra can serve as a reminder to seek balance and clarity in our thoughts and actions, ultimately guiding us towards a more harmonious existence.
