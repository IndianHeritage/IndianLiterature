सहस्रणीथः शतधारो अद्भुत इन्द्रायन्दुः पवते काम्यम् मधु |जयन् क्षेत्रम् अभ्य अर्षा जयन्न अप उरुं नो गातुं कृणु सोम मीढ्वः ||
sahasraṇīthaḥ śatadhāro adbhuta indrāyenduḥ pavate kāmyam madhu | jayan kṣetram abhy arṣā jayann apa uruṁ no gātuṁ kṛṇu soma mīḍhvaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahasraṇīthaḥ: One who has a thousand paths or ways.
    • śatadhāro: One who holds a hundred (or many) things.
    • adbhuta: Wonderful or extraordinary.
    • indrāyenduḥ: To Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms.
    • pavate: He who blows or moves (referring to the wind).
    • kāmyam: Desired or wished for.
    • madhu: Sweetness or nectar.
    • jayan: Victorious or triumphant.
    • kṣetram: Field or area (often referring to a battlefield or a place of action).
    • abhy arṣā: Having achieved or attained.
    • jayann: Winning or conquering.
    • apa: Away or from.
    • uruṁ: Broad or vast.
    • no: Our.
    • gātuṁ: To go or to move.
    • kṛṇu: Make or create.
    • soma: Referring to the moon or a divine drink.
    • mīḍhvaḥ: To be made or to be created.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine forces, particularly Indra, who is associated with strength, rain, and prosperity.
    • The mention of "sahasraṇīthaḥ" suggests the multitude of paths and opportunities available to us, emphasizing the importance of choice and direction in life.
    • "Adbhuta" highlights the extraordinary nature of the divine, reminding us to recognize and appreciate the wonders around us.
    • The reference to "madhu" symbolizes the sweetness of life and the rewards of our efforts, encouraging us to seek joy and fulfillment.
    • By calling upon Indra and the wind ("pavate"), the mantra suggests a connection to nature and the elements, which can be seen as a metaphor for the forces that drive our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, one might face multiple paths in their career. The mantra encourages individuals to choose wisely and pursue the path that brings them fulfillment and success.
    • In personal relationships, recognizing the "adbhuta" or extraordinary aspects of our loved ones can deepen connections and foster appreciation.
    • During challenging times, invoking the strength of Indra can serve as a reminder to seek support from the community and the natural world, finding resilience in unity.
    • In moments of celebration, the sweetness of "madhu" can be reflected in the joy of achievements, whether personal or communal, reminding us to savor our victories.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the divine forces at play in our lives, encouraging us to recognize the opportunities available to us and to seek the extraordinary in our everyday experiences.
    • Its significance lies in the call for action, urging us to pursue our desires with determination while remaining connected to the natural world and the community around us.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to embrace their journeys, celebrate their victories, and appreciate the sweetness of life, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
