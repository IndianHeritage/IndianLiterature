यां वा ते सन्ति दाशुषे अधृष्टा गिरो वा याभिर नृवतीर उरुष्याः |ताभिर नः सूनो सहसो न पाही स्मत सूरीञ जारित्रीñ जातवेदः ||
yā vā te santi dāśuṣe adhṛṣṭā giro vā yābhir nṛvatīr uruṣyāḥ | tābhir naḥ sūno sahaso ni pāhi smat sūrīñ jaritṝñ jātavedaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yā vā te: Whatever is yours
    • santi: Exists
    • dāśuṣe: For the one who is devoted
    • adhṛṣṭā: Unseen or hidden
    • giro: Words or utterances
    • vā: Or
    • yābhir: By which
    • nṛvatīr: The human beings
    • uruṣyāḥ: Are made strong
    • tābhir: By those
    • naḥ: Our
    • sūno: Sons or progeny
    • sahaso: With strength
    • ni pāhi: Protect
    • smat: From us
    • sūrīñ: The brave ones
    • jaritṝñ: The wise or learned
    • jātavedaḥ: The one who knows all
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for protection and strength.
    • It acknowledges the unseen forces and words that contribute to human strength and resilience.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and bravery in guiding and protecting one's progeny.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, parents often seek blessings for their children to be strong and wise, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In a community, leaders may invoke such prayers to ensure the safety and strength of their people during challenging times.
    • In personal development, individuals may meditate on this mantra to cultivate inner strength and wisdom, seeking guidance from unseen forces.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking protection and strength from higher powers.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as family prayers, community gatherings, and personal reflections.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of wisdom, strength, and the unseen forces that guide our lives.
