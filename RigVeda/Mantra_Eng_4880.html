प्रथमभाजं यशसं वयोधां सुपाणिं देवं सुगभस्तिम ऋभ्वम् |होतā यक्षद यजतम पस्त्यानाम अग्निस त्वष्टारं सुहवं विभावā ||
prathamabhājaṁ yaśasaṁ vayodhāṁ supāṇiṁ devaṁ sugabhastim ṛbhvam | hotā yakṣad yajatam pastyānām agnis tvaṣṭāraṁ suhavaṁ vibhāvā ||
  • prathamabhājaṁ: This term translates to "the first share" or "the foremost part." It signifies the importance of prioritizing the divine or spiritual aspects in our lives.
  • yaśasaṁ: Meaning "of fame" or "glory," this word emphasizes the pursuit of honor and recognition through virtuous actions.
  • vayodhāṁ: This refers to "the strength of youth" or "vigor." It highlights the vitality and energy that comes with youth, which can be harnessed for positive endeavors.
  • supāṇiṁ: Translated as "well-formed hands," this suggests the importance of skillful action and the ability to perform tasks effectively.
  • devaṁ: This means "the divine" or "god." It represents the higher powers or deities that guide and protect us.
  • sugabhastim: This term means "well-shining" or "brilliant." It signifies the radiance of knowledge and wisdom that illuminates our path.
  • ṛbhvam: This translates to "the one who is worthy" or "the noble." It emphasizes the importance of being virtuous and deserving of respect.
  • hotā: Meaning "the priest" or "the one who invokes," this word refers to the role of a mediator between the divine and the earthly realms.
  • yakṣad: This term means "to invoke" or "to call upon." It signifies the act of seeking divine assistance or blessings.
  • yajatam: Translated as "the one who is worshipped," this refers to the divine being that is the focus of devotion and reverence.
  • pastyānām: This means "of the past" or "ancient." It connects us to the wisdom and traditions of our ancestors.
  • agnis: Referring to "fire," this symbolizes transformation, purification, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • tvaṣṭāraṁ: This translates to "the creator" or "the one who shapes." It signifies the creative force in the universe.
  • suhavaṁ: Meaning "auspicious" or "beneficial," this term emphasizes the positive outcomes of divine blessings.
  • vibhāvā: This means "manifestation" or "expression." It refers to the ways in which the divine is revealed in the world.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and embodying virtues such as skill, glory, and nobility. It encourages individuals to prioritize spiritual pursuits and to act with intention and purpose. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, community service, and professional endeavors. By invoking the divine and striving for excellence, individuals can create a positive impact in their lives and the lives of others, fostering a sense of connection to the greater universe.
