स śvitānas tanyatū रोचनस्थā अजरेभिर नानदद्भिर यविष्टः |यः पावकः पुरुतमः पुरूणि पृथून्य अग्निर अनुयाति भरवन ||
sa śvitānas tanyatū rocanasthā ajarebhir nānadadbhir yaviṣṭhaḥ | yaḥ pāvakaḥ purutamaḥ purūṇi pṛthūny agnir anuyāti bharvan ||
  • sa śvitānas tanyatū rocanasthā ajarebhir nānadadbhir yaviṣṭhaḥ
  • yaḥ pāvakaḥ purutamaḥ purūṇi pṛthūny agnir anuyāti bharvan

  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sa: he, that
    • śvitānas: shining, radiant
    • tanyatū: having a body, embodied
    • rocanasthā: residing in brightness
    • ajarebhir: imperishable, eternal
    • nānadadbhir: with joyful sounds
    • yaviṣṭhaḥ: the most excellent
  • yaḥ: who
  • pāvakaḥ: the purifier, fire
  • purutamaḥ: the oldest, the primordial
  • purūṇi: many, various
  • pṛthūny: vast, extensive
  • agnir: fire
  • anuyāti: follows, leads
  • bharvan: carrying, bearing

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of a radiant entity that embodies eternal qualities and resides in brightness. It emphasizes the idea of purity and the joy that comes from it.
    • The reference to "pāvakaḥ" (the purifier) signifies the transformative power of fire, which is not only a physical element but also a metaphor for spiritual purification and enlightenment.
    • In real life, this can be likened to the process of personal growth and self-improvement, where one sheds negativity and embraces positivity, much like how fire transforms raw materials into something valuable.
    • The mantra also highlights the vastness and ancient nature of this purifying force, suggesting that it has been a guiding principle throughout human history.
    • For example, in many cultures, fire is used in rituals to symbolize purification and renewal, such as in the practice of lighting candles or bonfires during festivals.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of purity, transformation, and the eternal nature of spiritual truths. It encourages individuals to seek enlightenment and embrace the joy that comes from living a life aligned with these principles.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in daily life through practices such as meditation, self-reflection, and engaging in activities that promote personal and communal well-being.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the enduring power of the spirit and the continuous journey towards self-improvement and enlightenment.
