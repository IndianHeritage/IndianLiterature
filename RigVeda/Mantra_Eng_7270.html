न तं अग्नि अरातयो मर्तं युवन्त रायः |यं त्रायसे दाश्वांसम् ||
na tam agne arātayo martaṁ yuvanta rāyaḥ | yaṁ trāyase dāśvāṁsam ||
  • na tam agne arātayo martaṁ yuvanta rāyaḥ
    • na - not
    • tam - that
    • agne - O Agni (the fire god)
    • arātayo - enemies or adversaries
    • martaṁ - mortal or human
    • yuvanta - may they not harm
    • rāyaḥ - the rulers or kings
  • yaṁ trāyase dāśvāṁsam
    • yaṁ - whom
    • trāyase - you protect
    • dāśvāṁsam - the one who is like a horse (symbolizing strength and speed)

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for protection against adversaries and ensuring the safety of rulers or leaders. The phrase "may they not harm" emphasizes a plea for safeguarding those in power from threats, while "whom you protect" highlights the divine guardianship that Agni provides, particularly to those who embody strength and valor, akin to a horse.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Leadership and Governance: Leaders often face challenges and adversaries. This mantra serves as a reminder for leaders to seek divine guidance and protection in their endeavors, ensuring they remain steadfast and unyielding against opposition.
  • Personal Protection: Individuals can recite this mantra to invoke a sense of security in their lives, especially when facing difficult situations or adversaries. It encourages the belief that divine forces are at play, safeguarding them from harm.
  • Community and Society: In a broader sense, this mantra can be a call for unity and strength within communities. By invoking the protection of Agni, communities can come together to face challenges collectively, ensuring the well-being of all members.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine protection and strength. It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking guidance from higher powers in times of adversity. Whether in leadership, personal challenges, or community struggles, the essence of this mantra encourages resilience and the belief that one is not alone in their battles. By embracing this mantra, individuals and leaders alike can find solace and strength in the face of challenges, fostering a sense of security and unity.
