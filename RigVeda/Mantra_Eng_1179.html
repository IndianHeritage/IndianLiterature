समिद्धेष्व अग्निष्व आनजानां यतस्रुचā बर्हिरु तिस्तिराणां | तीव्रैः सोमैः परिषिक्तैः अर्वाग इन्द्राग्नī सुमनसाया यातम् ||
samiddheṣv agniṣv ānajānā yatasrucā barhir u tistirāṇā | tīvraiḥ somaiḥ pariṣiktebhir arvāg endrāgnī saumanasāya yātam ||
  • Mantra: "samiddheṣv agniṣv ānajānā yatasrucā barhir u tistirāṇā | tīvraiḥ somaiḥ pariṣiktebhir arvāg endrāgnī saumanasāya yātam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samiddheṣv: in the kindled (fires)
    • agniṣv: in the fires
    • ānajānā: those who are nourished
    • yatasrucā: with the offering of the sacred grass
    • barhir: with the sacred grass
    • u: and
    • tistirāṇā: those who are established
    • tīvraiḥ: with intense
    • somaiḥ: with the offerings of Soma
    • pariṣiktebhir: sprinkled
    • arvāg: towards the east
    • endrāgnī: the fire of Indra
    • saumanasāya: for the well-being
    • yātam: may it go
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the sacred fire (Agni) and emphasizes the importance of offerings made with devotion.
    • The phrase "samiddheṣv agniṣv" refers to the kindled fires, symbolizing the presence of divine energy and purity.
    • "ānajānā" indicates those who are nourished by these offerings, suggesting a reciprocal relationship between humans and the divine.
    • The mention of "yatasrucā barhir" highlights the significance of sacred grass in rituals, which is often used to purify and sanctify the space.
    • "tīvraiḥ somaiḥ" refers to the intense offerings of Soma, a sacred drink that represents vitality and divine inspiration.
    • The invocation of "endrāgnī" connects the fire to Indra, the king of gods, symbolizing strength and protection.
    • Overall, the mantra emphasizes the importance of ritualistic offerings and the connection between the physical and spiritual realms.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern Hindu rituals, offerings made to Agni during ceremonies like weddings or housewarming are seen as a way to invite blessings and prosperity.
    • Just as the mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings, in daily life, acts of kindness and generosity can be viewed as offerings to the universe, fostering goodwill and positive energy.
    • The concept of nourishing the divine through rituals can be paralleled with self-care practices, where individuals nourish their own spirit through meditation, yoga, or community service.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of rituals, and the need for offerings to maintain harmony with the divine.
    • Its significance lies in the understanding that our actions, whether in rituals or daily life, have the power to create positive energy and foster well-being.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to engage in practices that promote peace, prosperity, and a sense of community, reflecting the essence of the mantra in contemporary life.
