तत् त्वा यामि सुवीर्यं तद् ब्रह्म पूर्वचित्तये |येना यतिभ्यो भृगवे धन हिते येन प्रस्कण्वम आविथ ||
tat tvā yāmi suvīryaṁ tad brahma pūrvacittaye | yenā yatibhyo bhṛgave dhane hite yena praskaṇvam āvitha ||
  • tat tvā yāmi suvīryaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "I go to you, O strong one." Here, "tat" means "that," "tvā" means "you," "yāmi" means "I go," and "suvīryaṁ" refers to "the strong" or "the powerful." This can be understood as a call to a higher power or divine strength, seeking guidance and support.
  • tad brahma pūrvacittaye: This translates to "that is the Brahman, for the sake of the past." "Brahma" refers to the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit in Hindu philosophy, while "pūrvacittaye" indicates a connection to past actions or consciousness. This suggests that the strength sought is rooted in a deeper, universal truth that transcends time.
  • yenā yatibhyo bhṛgave: This means "by which the ascetics are empowered." "Yena" translates to "by which," "yatibhyo" refers to "ascetics" or those who practice self-discipline, and "bhṛgave" can be interpreted as "to be empowered" or "to be enriched." This highlights the idea that true strength and wisdom come from disciplined practice and spiritual pursuit.
  • dhane hite yena praskaṇvam āvitha: This translates to "by which wealth and well-being are attained." "Dhane" means "wealth," "hite" means "well-being," "yena" again means "by which," and "praskaṇvam" refers to "attainment" or "prosperity." This part of the mantra emphasizes that the divine strength sought leads to both material and spiritual prosperity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound spiritual journey where the seeker acknowledges a higher power and seeks strength and wisdom from it. It emphasizes the importance of past actions and disciplined practice in achieving both material wealth and spiritual well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during meditation, in moments of seeking guidance, or when striving for personal growth. It serves as a reminder that true strength comes from a connection to the divine and a commitment to self-discipline, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
