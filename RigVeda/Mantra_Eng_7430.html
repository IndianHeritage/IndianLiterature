त्वं यविष्ट दाशुषो नृंह् पाहि श्रिणुधी गिरः |रक्षा तोकमुत तमनः ||
tvaṁ yaviṣṭha dāśuṣo nṝm̐ḥ pāhi śṛṇudhī giraḥ | rakṣā tokam uta tmanā ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "You" and signifies a divine presence or a higher power that is being addressed in the mantra.
  • yaviṣṭha - Meaning "most excellent" or "most revered," this term emphasizes the greatness of the entity being invoked.
  • dāśuṣo - This can be interpreted as "of the worshippers" or "of the devotees," indicating a connection between the divine and those who seek its blessings.
  • nṝm̐ḥ - This word means "men" or "humans," highlighting the human aspect of the invocation and the need for divine protection.
  • pāhi - Translated as "protect," this term expresses a plea for safeguarding and support from the divine.
  • śṛṇudhī - Meaning "listen" or "hear," this word indicates a request for the divine to pay attention to the prayers and needs of the devotees.
  • giraḥ - This translates to "words" or "speech," suggesting the importance of communication and expression in the relationship between humans and the divine.
  • rakṣā - Meaning "protection," this term reinforces the theme of safeguarding and the need for divine intervention in times of trouble.
  • tokam - This word can be interpreted as "offspring" or "children," indicating a concern for future generations and the well-being of one's family.
  • uta - Translated as "or," this word presents an alternative or additional plea, emphasizing the multifaceted nature of the request.
  • tmanā - Meaning "with the mind" or "with the self," this term suggests that the invocation is not just a verbal plea but also a heartfelt request from within.

In summary, this mantra is a heartfelt invocation for divine protection and guidance. It emphasizes the relationship between the divine and humanity, highlighting the need for safeguarding not just individuals but also their families and future generations. The plea for the divine to listen to the words of the devotees signifies the importance of communication in spiritual practice.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be recited during personal prayers or family gatherings to seek blessings and protection for loved ones.
  • In times of uncertainty or crisis, such as during health issues or financial difficulties, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek divine support and guidance.
  • It can also be used in educational settings, where teachers and parents invoke blessings for the well-being and success of children.

Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking divine assistance in navigating life's challenges.
