पुनानश्चमू जनयनमतिं कविः सोमो देवेषु रण्यति |अपो वसानाः परि गोभिरुत्तरः सीदन वनेष्वव्यत ||
punānaś camū janayan matiṁ kaviḥ somo deveṣu raṇyati | apo vasānaḥ pari gobhir uttaraḥ sīdan vaneṣv avyata ||
  • punānaś: This word can be interpreted as "purifying" or "refreshing." In a real-life context, this can relate to the idea of cleansing oneself from negative thoughts or influences, similar to how one might seek mental clarity through meditation or mindfulness practices.
  • camū: This term refers to "armies" or "forces." In modern terms, it can symbolize the collective strength of a community or group working towards a common goal, such as a team in a workplace or a community coming together for a social cause.
  • janayan: Meaning "creating" or "generating," this word emphasizes the act of bringing something into existence. For example, this can be seen in the context of innovation, where new ideas are generated to solve problems or improve lives.
  • matiṁ: This translates to "intellect" or "understanding." In everyday life, this can relate to the importance of wisdom in decision-making, such as how individuals use their knowledge and experiences to navigate challenges.
  • kaviḥ: This word means "poet" or "sage," representing creativity and insight. In a contemporary setting, this can be likened to artists, writers, or thinkers who inspire others through their work.
  • somo: Referring to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, it symbolizes nourishment and vitality. In modern life, this can be compared to the importance of nutrition and wellness in maintaining physical and mental health.
  • deveṣu: This means "among the gods" or "divine beings." It signifies the connection to higher powers or ideals, which can be reflected in how people seek guidance or inspiration from spiritual beliefs or philosophies.
  • raṇyati: This translates to "shining" or "radiating." In real life, this can be seen in how individuals express their inner light or positivity, influencing those around them through acts of kindness or leadership.
  • apo: Meaning "water," it symbolizes life and sustenance. In practical terms, this can relate to the essential role of water in our daily lives, from hydration to agriculture, highlighting the importance of environmental conservation.
  • vasānaḥ: This word means "dwelling" or "residing." It can represent the idea of finding a place of comfort and safety, such as a home or community where one feels accepted and supported.
  • pari: This translates to "around" or "surrounding." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, akin to how individuals are influenced by their surroundings, including family, friends, and society.
  • gobhir: Meaning "cows," it symbolizes wealth and nourishment in Vedic culture. In modern terms, this can relate to the importance of sustainable agriculture and ethical treatment of animals in food production.
  • uttaraḥ: This word means "northern" or "higher." It can symbolize aspirations and goals, representing the pursuit of higher knowledge or achievements in life.
  • sīdan: This translates to "sitting" or "settling." In a real-life context, it can refer to the importance of taking time to reflect and find peace in a busy world, such as through practices like yoga or quiet contemplation.
  • vaneṣv: Meaning "in the forests," it symbolizes nature and the environment. This can relate to the modern movement towards environmental awareness and the need to protect natural habitats.
  • avyata: This word means "unharmed" or "untouched." It signifies purity and the idea of maintaining integrity in one's actions, akin to ethical living and making choices that do not harm others or the environment.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of purification, collective strength, creativity, and the pursuit of wisdom. It highlights the interconnectedness of life and the significance of nurturing both the mind and body. In real-life scenarios, these themes can be applied to personal development, community building, and environmental stewardship. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can strive for a balanced and harmonious life, contributing positively to society and the world around them.
