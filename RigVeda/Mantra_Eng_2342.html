धारावराः मरुतो धृष्ण्वोजसो मृगाः न भीमास तविषीभिरर्चिनः |अग्नयो न शुशुचाना ऋजीषिणो भ्रिमिं धामन्तो अप गा अवृण्वत ||
dhārāvarā maruto dhṛṣṇvojaso mṛgā na bhīmās taviṣībhir arcinaḥ | agnayo na śuśucānā ṛjīṣiṇo bhṛmiṁ dhamanto apa gā avṛṇvata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dhārāvarā: Those who are strong and powerful.
    • maruto: The Maruts, the storm deities or winds.
    • dhṛṣṇvojaso: With great energy and strength.
    • mṛgā: The creatures or beings.
    • na: Not.
    • bhīmās: Fearful or terrifying.
    • taviṣībhir: With great power.
    • arcinaḥ: Those who are worshipped or honored.
    • agnayo: The Agni, the fire or divine energy.
    • na: Not.
    • śuśucānā: Those who are in distress or suffering.
    • ṛjīṣiṇo: Those who are righteous or just.
    • bhṛmiṁ: The earth or the ground.
    • dhamanto: Those who are powerful or strong.
    • apa: Away or from.
    • gā: The cows or the wealth.
    • avṛṇvata: To protect or to cover.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Maruts, the storm deities, who are known for their strength and energy.
    • It emphasizes the idea that these powerful beings are not to be feared, as they are worshipped and honored.
    • The mention of Agni signifies the divine energy that is present in all forms of life and nature.
    • The mantra suggests that those who are righteous and just will not suffer, and they will be protected from harm.
    • It highlights the importance of strength, righteousness, and the divine protection that comes from honoring the forces of nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of natural disasters, such as storms or floods, communities often come together to honor and seek protection from the forces of nature, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal challenges, individuals may find strength in their beliefs and values, drawing on the idea of righteousness to overcome fear and adversity.
    • Environmental movements often invoke the power of nature, recognizing the importance of respecting and honoring the earth, akin to the reverence expressed in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the strength and power inherent in nature and the divine.
    • It encourages individuals to embrace righteousness and seek protection through honor and respect for the forces that govern life.
    • In real life, it is applicable in various scenarios, from personal struggles to collective efforts in environmental conservation, emphasizing the interconnectedness of strength, righteousness, and divine protection.
