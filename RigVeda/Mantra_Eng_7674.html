यद अग्ने काणि काणि चिद आ ते दारुणि दध्मसि |तां जुषस्व यविष्ठ्य ||
yad agne kāni kāni cid ā te dārūṇi dadhmasi | tā juṣasva yaviṣṭhya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • kāni: which
    • kāni: which (repeated for emphasis)
    • cid: indeed
    • ā: you
    • te: your
    • dārūṇi: wood (or fuel)
    • dadhmasi: we prepare or we offer
    • tā: those
    • juṣasva: may you enjoy
    • yaviṣṭhya: with great delight
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, expressing the offering of wood and fuel to the divine fire. It emphasizes the importance of the offerings made to Agni and seeks his blessings to enjoy the fruits of these offerings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, Agni is often invoked during ceremonies such as weddings and yajnas (sacrificial rituals). The offerings made to Agni symbolize the connection between the material and spiritual worlds.
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be related to the idea of dedication and commitment in our daily lives. Just as we offer wood to Agni, we can offer our efforts and hard work towards our goals, seeking fulfillment and success.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of offerings and the act of giving in various forms, whether it be through rituals, service to others, or personal dedication to one's goals.
    • In a broader sense, it teaches us to appreciate the resources we have and to use them wisely, ensuring that our actions are aligned with our intentions.
    • In everyday life, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate gratitude and mindfulness, recognizing the value of their contributions and the impact they have on their surroundings.
