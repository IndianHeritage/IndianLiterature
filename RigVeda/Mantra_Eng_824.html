स्वाध्यो दिव ā सप्त यह्वी रायो दुरो व्य ऋतज्ञा अजानन |विदद गव्यं सरमा दृढम् ऊर्वं येनाऽनु कम मानवशी भोजते विट् ||
svādhyo diva ā sapta yahvī rāyo duro vy ṛtajñā ajānan | vidad gavyaṁ saramā dṛḻham ūrvaṁ yenā nu kam mānuṣī bhojate viṭ ||
  • svādhyo: This term refers to self-knowledge or self-awareness. It emphasizes the importance of understanding oneself in order to navigate life effectively.
  • diva: Meaning "heaven" or "sky," this word signifies the higher realms of existence and consciousness.
  • ā: A preposition that can mean "towards" or "in the direction of," indicating movement or aspiration.
  • sapta: This means "seven," which often symbolizes completeness or a holistic approach in various cultures.
  • yahvī: Refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," highlighting the importance of abundance in life.
  • rāyo: This term is related to "riches" or "prosperity," reinforcing the idea of seeking material and spiritual wealth.
  • duro: Meaning "difficult" or "hard," it suggests that the path to achieving wealth and prosperity may not be easy.
  • vy: A prefix that can denote "through" or "by means of," indicating a method or approach.
  • ṛtajñā: This term means "knowledge of truth" or "wisdom," emphasizing the importance of understanding the deeper truths of life.
  • ajānan: Refers to "not knowing" or "ignorance," highlighting the need to overcome ignorance to achieve true understanding.
  • vidad: This means "to give" or "to provide," indicating the act of sharing or bestowing knowledge and wealth.
  • gavyaṁ: Refers to "the essence of cows," which in Vedic culture symbolizes nourishment and sustenance.
  • saramā: This term can mean "the one who brings," often associated with the idea of guidance or support.
  • dṛḻham: Meaning "strong" or "firm," it emphasizes the need for resilience and stability in one's pursuits.
  • ūrvaṁ: Refers to "earth" or "ground," symbolizing the material world and our connection to it.
  • yenā: Meaning "by which," it indicates the means or method through which something is achieved.
  • nu: A particle that can mean "indeed" or "certainly," adding emphasis to the statement.
  • kam: Refers to "who" or "what," often used in questioning or seeking understanding.
  • mānuṣī: Meaning "human" or "mankind," it emphasizes the human experience and our shared journey.
  • bhojate: This means "to enjoy" or "to partake," indicating the act of experiencing life and its offerings.
  • viṭ: Refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," reinforcing the theme of abundance in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of self-awareness, the pursuit of knowledge, and the quest for prosperity in both material and spiritual realms. It acknowledges that the path to wealth and understanding may be challenging, but through resilience and wisdom, one can navigate life's complexities. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal development, financial planning, and the pursuit of knowledge, encouraging individuals to seek a balanced approach to life that encompasses both material success and spiritual growth.
