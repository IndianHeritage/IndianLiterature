यमाय घृतवत् धविर जुहोत प्र च तिष्ठत |स नो देवेष्व ā यमद दीर्घम आयुः प्र जीवसे ||
yamāya ghṛtavad dhavir juhota pra ca tiṣṭhata | sa no deveṣv ā yamad dīrgham āyuḥ pra jīvase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yamāya: To the one who is worthy or deserving.
    • ghṛtavad: Like ghee or clarified butter, symbolizing richness and nourishment.
    • dhavir: With strength or power.
    • juhota: May he offer or invoke.
    • pra: Forth or forward.
    • ca: And.
    • tiṣṭhata: May he stand or remain.
    • sa: He or that one.
    • no: Our.
    • deveṣv: Among the gods.
    • ā: From or out of.
    • yamad: The one who is worthy of respect.
    • dīrgham: Long or extended.
    • āyuḥ: Life or lifespan.
    • pra: Forth or forward.
    • jīvase: For living or to live.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for longevity and strength, invoking the blessings of the divine.
    • The reference to "ghṛtavad" suggests that just as ghee nourishes and enriches food, the blessings sought are meant to enrich life.
    • By addressing the divine, the mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking higher powers for guidance and support in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, parents often pray for the health and longevity of their children, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In various cultures, rituals are performed to invoke blessings for a long and prosperous life, similar to the invocation in this mantra.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or births, people often seek divine blessings for a fruitful and long life, resonating with the themes of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine blessings in achieving a long and fulfilling life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as family prayers, rituals for health, and during significant life events.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the divine, and the pursuit of longevity and strength.
