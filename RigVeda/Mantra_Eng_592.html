येनापावकचक्षसा भुरण्यन्तं जनांऽ अनु |त्वं वरुण पश्यसि ||
yenā pāvaka cakṣasā bhuraṇyantaṁ janām̐ anu | tvaṁ varuṇa paśyasi ||
  • Mantra: "yenā pāvaka cakṣasā bhuraṇyantaṁ janām̐ anu | tvaṁ varuṇa paśyasi ||"
  • Translation: "By whom, with the eye of the fire, the people are illuminated; you, Varuna, see them."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • yenā: By whom
    • pāvaka: Fire, often symbolizing purity and illumination
    • cakṣasā: With the eye, referring to perception or vision
    • bhuraṇyantaṁ: Illuminated or made bright
    • janām̐: People or beings
    • anu: Following or in accordance with
    • tvaṁ: You
    • varuṇa: The deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority
    • paśyasi: You see

  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Varuna, the god of water and cosmic order, emphasizing his role in overseeing the moral and ethical conduct of humanity.
    • The phrase "by whom, with the eye of the fire" suggests that fire symbolizes knowledge and enlightenment, illuminating the path for people.
    • In a real-life context, this can be likened to the role of education and wisdom in guiding individuals and societies towards ethical living.
    • Just as fire provides light in darkness, knowledge and awareness help individuals navigate through life's challenges and moral dilemmas.

  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of divine oversight in human affairs, reminding us that our actions are observed and judged by higher powers.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and enlightenment, fostering a sense of responsibility towards oneself and society.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as in education, leadership, and personal development, where the pursuit of knowledge and ethical conduct is paramount.
