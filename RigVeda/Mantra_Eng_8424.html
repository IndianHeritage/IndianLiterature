पवमानो अभ्यर्षा सुवीर्यं उर्वीं गव्यूतिम महि शर्म सप्रथः |माकिर नो अस्य परिषूतिर ईशतेन्दो जयेम तवया धनं-धनम् ||
pavamāno abhy arṣā suvīryam urvīṁ gavyūtim mahi śarma saprathaḥ | mākir no asya pariṣūtir īśatendo jayema tvayā dhanaṁ-dhanam ||
  • pavamāno: This word translates to "purifying" or "the one who is being purified." In real life, this can relate to the process of self-improvement and personal growth, where individuals strive to cleanse their thoughts and actions.
  • abhy arṣā: This means "through the heavens" or "in the celestial realm." It signifies the connection between the earthly and the divine, reminding us of the importance of spirituality in our lives.
  • suvīryam: This translates to "great strength" or "valour." In practical terms, it can refer to the inner strength we draw upon during challenging times, such as overcoming obstacles in our careers or personal lives.
  • urvīṁ: This means "the vastness" or "the expanse." It can symbolize the limitless opportunities available to us in life, encouraging us to explore and expand our horizons.
  • gavyūtim: This translates to "the wealth of cows," which in ancient times symbolized prosperity and abundance. In modern contexts, it can represent the various forms of wealth we seek, whether material or spiritual.
  • mahi: This means "great" or "mighty." It emphasizes the importance of greatness in our endeavors, inspiring us to aim high and achieve our goals.
  • śarma: This translates to "comfort" or "shelter." It highlights the need for security and peace in our lives, akin to the comfort of a loving home or a supportive community.
  • saprathaḥ: This means "together" or "in unity." It underscores the significance of collaboration and teamwork in achieving success, whether in personal relationships or professional settings.
  • mākir: This translates to "may we be blessed" or "may we attain." It reflects the desire for divine blessings in our pursuits, reminding us to seek guidance and support from higher powers.
  • no asya: This means "for us" or "to us." It emphasizes the personal nature of the mantra, indicating that the blessings and strength sought are intended for the individual or community.
  • pariṣūtir: This translates to "protection" or "safeguarding." It signifies the importance of safety and security in our lives, whether it be physical, emotional, or financial.
  • īśatendo: This means "by the divine" or "through the lord." It highlights the role of divine intervention in our lives, suggesting that we are not alone in our struggles and that higher powers are at work.
  • jayema: This translates to "may we conquer" or "may we triumph." It reflects the aspiration for success and victory in our endeavors, encouraging a mindset of resilience and determination.
  • tvayā: This means "by you" or "through you." It indicates reliance on divine support and guidance in our journey, reminding us to remain humble and open to assistance.
  • dhanam-dhanam: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." It emphasizes the pursuit of abundance in various forms, whether it be financial, emotional, or spiritual wealth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message of purification, strength, unity, and the pursuit of prosperity. It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in our endeavors. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as striving for personal growth, fostering teamwork in professional settings, and seeking comfort and security in our lives. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can navigate challenges with resilience and aim for greatness in their pursuits.
