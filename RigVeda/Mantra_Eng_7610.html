मन्त्र: इन्द्र नो गधि प्रियः सत्राजिद अगोह्यः |गिरिर न विश्वतस् पृथुः पतिर दिवः ||
endra no gadhi priyaḥ satrājid agohyaḥ | girir na viśvatas pṛthuḥ patir divaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Endra: Refers to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing power and authority.
    • No: Means "our," indicating a sense of possession or belonging.
    • Gadhi: Can be interpreted as "strength" or "power."
    • Priyaḥ: Means "beloved" or "dear," suggesting affection or importance.
    • Satrājid: Refers to "the conqueror of enemies," highlighting victory and triumph.
    • Agohyaḥ: Means "unseen" or "invisible," indicating something that is beyond perception.
    • Girir: Refers to "mountain," symbolizing stability and strength.
    • Na: Means "not," indicating negation.
    • Viśvatas: Means "from all sides" or "everywhere," suggesting omnipresence.
    • Pṛthuḥ: Means "broad" or "vast," indicating expansiveness.
    • Patir: Means "lord" or "master," denoting authority.
    • Divaḥ: Refers to "heaven" or "sky," symbolizing the divine realm.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, asking for his strength and support in overcoming challenges.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine assistance in achieving victory and stability in life.
    • The imagery of mountains and the vastness of the sky signifies the strength and omnipresence of divine power.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal struggle, individuals often seek strength from a higher power, much like invoking Indra for support.
    • In leadership, the qualities of strength, authority, and the ability to overcome challenges are essential, paralleling the attributes of Indra.
    • In nature, mountains symbolize resilience and stability, reminding us to remain steadfast in our pursuits.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine strength and support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of resilience, authority, and the quest for victory in various aspects of life.
    • Applicable in personal, professional, and spiritual contexts, it encourages individuals to connect with their inner strength and seek guidance from the divine.
