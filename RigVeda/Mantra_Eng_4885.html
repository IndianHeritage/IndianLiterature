तन्नो हिर बुद्ध्यः अद्भिरर्कैः तत्पर्वतस्तत्सविता चनो धात् |तदोषधिभिरभि रातिषाचो भगः पुरन्धिः जिन्वतु प्र राये ||
tan no 'hir budhnyo adbhir arkais tat parvatas tat savitā cano dhāt | tad oṣadhībhir abhi rātiṣāco bhagaḥ puraṁdhir jinvatu pra rāye ||
  • tan no 'hir budhnyo: This phrase can be translated as "may that divine light enlighten us." Here, "tan" refers to "that," "no" means "us," and "hir budhnyo" signifies "the divine light." In real life, this can be understood as a call for wisdom and understanding in our actions and thoughts.
  • adbhir arkais: This translates to "with the rays of the sun." "Adbhir" means "with," and "arkais" refers to "rays" or "light." This can be related to how sunlight is essential for life on Earth, symbolizing knowledge and clarity. For example, just as sunlight helps plants grow, knowledge helps individuals grow intellectually and spiritually.
  • tat parvatas: This means "that mountain." Mountains often symbolize stability and strength. In our lives, we can think of our personal challenges as mountains that we must climb. Overcoming these challenges leads to personal growth and resilience.
  • tat savitā cano dhāt: This translates to "may that sun, Savita, bestow upon us." "Savitā" refers to the sun, which is often associated with life-giving energy. In a practical sense, this can be seen in how we seek inspiration and motivation from various sources, much like how we rely on the sun for energy.
  • tad oṣadhībhir abhi rātiṣāco: This means "may that be fulfilled by the herbs." "Oṣadhībhir" refers to "herbs" or "plants," and "rātiṣāco" signifies "fulfillment." This can be related to the importance of nature in our lives, as herbs and plants provide nourishment and healing. For instance, herbal medicine is a practical application of this idea, where we use nature's resources for our well-being.
  • bhagaḥ puraṁdhir: This translates to "may the divine wealth and prosperity." "Bhagaḥ" refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," and "puraṁdhir" signifies "the one who brings." This can be understood in the context of seeking abundance in our lives, whether it be financial, emotional, or spiritual. For example, cultivating a mindset of gratitude can attract more positivity and abundance.
  • jinvatu pra rāye: This means "may it bring us prosperity." "Jinvatu" signifies "may it bring," and "pra rāye" refers to "prosperity." This is a hopeful conclusion, emphasizing the desire for a fruitful and prosperous life. In real life, this can be seen in our aspirations for success in various endeavors, whether in career, relationships, or personal growth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound desire for enlightenment, strength, and prosperity through the divine light of knowledge and nature's resources. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, where wisdom, nature, and divine blessings play crucial roles in our journey. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek knowledge, overcome challenges, appreciate nature, and cultivate a mindset of abundance, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
