मन्त्र: भद्रā अग्नेर वध्र्यश्वस्य संदृशो वामī प्रणीतिः सुरणā उपेतयः |यद īं सुमित्रा विषो अग्र इन्धते घृतेनाहुतो जरते दविद्युतत् ||
bhadrā agner vadhryaśvasya saṁdṛśo vāmī praṇītiḥ suraṇā upetayaḥ | yad īṁ sumitrā viśo agra indhate ghṛtenāhuto jarate davidyutat ||
  • bhadrā agner: This phrase translates to "auspiciousness of the fire." Fire is often seen as a purifying and transformative element in many cultures. In real life, this can be related to the importance of fire in rituals, cooking, and even in industrial processes where it symbolizes energy and transformation.
  • vadhryaśvasya: This term refers to "the strong and powerful." It signifies strength and resilience. In a real-life context, this can be associated with individuals or communities that exhibit strength in overcoming challenges, such as athletes or leaders who inspire others through their determination.
  • saṁdṛśo vāmī praṇītiḥ: This can be interpreted as "the beautiful and harmonious guidance." It emphasizes the importance of wise leadership and guidance in achieving harmony. For example, effective leaders in organizations or communities who promote collaboration and understanding can be seen as embodying this principle.
  • suraṇā upetayaḥ: This phrase means "approaching the divine." It suggests a connection to higher powers or spiritual realms. In everyday life, this can be reflected in practices such as meditation, prayer, or community service, where individuals seek to connect with something greater than themselves.
  • yad īṁ sumitrā: This translates to "when the good friend." It highlights the value of friendship and support in our lives. Real-life examples include the importance of having a supportive network of friends and family during difficult times, which can significantly impact one's mental and emotional well-being.
  • viśo agra indhate: This means "the community leads." It emphasizes the role of community in guiding and supporting its members. In practice, this can be seen in community initiatives, local organizations, or social movements that work together for a common cause, showcasing the power of collective action.
  • ghṛtenāhuto: This term refers to "offered with ghee." Ghee is a sacred substance in many rituals, symbolizing purity and nourishment. In real life, this can be related to the importance of offering and sharing resources, whether in a family setting or in charitable acts that nourish the community.
  • jarate davidyutat: This translates to "the one who ages with brilliance." It signifies the wisdom that comes with age and experience. In real life, this can be seen in elders who share their knowledge and life lessons with younger generations, fostering a culture of respect and learning.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of auspiciousness, strength, guidance, community, friendship, and the wisdom of age. Its significance lies in the reminder of the interconnectedness of these elements in our lives. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek harmony, support one another, and recognize the value of community and shared experiences. By embodying these principles, we can create a more compassionate and resilient society.
