आउच्छत सा रात्रि परितकम्यां यां ऋणं चये राजनि रुशमानाम् |अत्यो न वाजी रघुर अज्यमानो बभ्रुश चत्वार्य असनत सहस्राः ||
aucchat sā rātrī paritakmyā yām̐ ṛṇaṁcaye rājani ruśamānām | atyo na vājī raghur ajyamāno babhruś catvāry asanat sahasrā ||
  • aucchat: This word can be interpreted as "purity" or "clarity." It signifies the importance of maintaining a pure and clear state of mind and spirit.
  • sā rātrī: Translated as "that night," it refers to the night as a metaphor for introspection and the subconscious mind. Nights are often associated with rest, reflection, and the unknown.
  • paritakmyā: This term suggests "surrounding" or "encompassing." It indicates the idea of being enveloped by certain energies or influences.
  • yām̐ ṛṇaṁcaye: This phrase can be understood as "to whom we offer our prayers." It emphasizes the act of devotion and the importance of seeking blessings from higher powers.
  • rājani: Meaning "in the realm of kings" or "royalty," it symbolizes authority, governance, and the responsibilities that come with power.
  • ruśamānām: This word translates to "those who are angry" or "those who are in conflict." It highlights the challenges and adversities one may face in life.
  • atyō na vājī: This can be interpreted as "not being defeated" or "not being overpowered." It speaks to resilience and strength in the face of difficulties.
  • raghur: Referring to "the one who is swift" or "the one who moves quickly," it symbolizes agility and the ability to adapt to changing circumstances.
  • ajyamāno: This term means "unharmed" or "untouched," suggesting a state of protection and safety.
  • babhruś: Translated as "the one who is strong" or "the one who is powerful," it emphasizes the importance of inner strength and fortitude.
  • catvāry: Meaning "four," it can symbolize stability and balance, as four is often associated with the four directions or elements.
  • asanat: This word means "seats" or "places," indicating the importance of having a stable foundation or support system.
  • sahasrā: Translated as "thousands," it signifies abundance and the vastness of possibilities available to us.

  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of purity, resilience, and strength in navigating life's challenges. It encourages individuals to seek clarity and protection while remaining adaptable and strong in the face of adversity. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal struggles, professional challenges, or any situation where one must maintain integrity and strength. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of inner peace and empowerment, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life.
