मां नोऽहिर बुद्ध्योरिषे धान मां यज्ञोऽस्य श्रीधदृतायोः ||
mā no 'hir budhnyo riṣe dhān mā yajño asya sridhad ṛtāyoḥ ||
  • mā no: This phrase translates to "may we not" or "let us not." It expresses a desire or intention to avoid something negative.
  • 'hir: This word can be interpreted as "to be enlightened" or "to gain knowledge." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding.
  • budhnyo: This term relates to "intellect" or "understanding." It signifies the need for clarity of thought and perception.
  • riṣe: This word means "to the sage" or "to the wise one." It acknowledges the value of wisdom and guidance from those who possess deep knowledge.
  • dhān: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." It indicates a desire for abundance, not just in material terms but also in knowledge and spiritual wealth.
  • mā yajño: This phrase means "let not the sacrifice" or "may the offering not be." It reflects the importance of intention behind actions, particularly in rituals or sacrifices.
  • asya: This word means "of this" or "of that," referring to the context of the sacrifice or offering being discussed.
  • sridhad: This term can be interpreted as "to be fulfilled" or "to be successful." It emphasizes the outcome of actions and the fulfillment of desires.
  • ṛtāyoḥ: This word relates to "truth" or "righteousness." It signifies the importance of aligning actions with truth and moral values.

The mantra essentially conveys a prayer for wisdom and understanding, urging individuals to avoid ignorance and to seek guidance from the wise. It emphasizes the significance of intention behind actions, particularly in the context of sacrifices or offerings, and highlights the importance of aligning one's actions with truth and righteousness.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Education: In the pursuit of knowledge, students can reflect on this mantra to seek wisdom and clarity, ensuring they approach their studies with the right intentions.
  • Decision Making: When faced with important decisions, individuals can invoke the essence of this mantra to avoid hasty choices and instead seek guidance from experienced mentors.
  • Spiritual Practices: In rituals or spiritual offerings, practitioners can focus on the intention behind their actions, ensuring they align with truth and righteousness.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, intention, and alignment with truth in all aspects of life. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate their paths with clarity and purpose, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and prosperous existence.
