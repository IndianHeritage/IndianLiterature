एते धामान्य आर्याः शुक्रा ऋतस्य धारया |वाजं गोमन्तम् अक्षरम् ||
ete dhāmāny āryā śukrā ṛtasya dhārayā | vājaṁ gomantam akṣaran ||
  • Translation: "These are the abodes of the noble, the pure, which uphold the truth; the wealth that is full of strength and is imperishable."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • ete: These
    • dhāmāny: Abodes or realms
    • āryā: Noble or honorable
    • śukrā: Pure or bright
    • ṛtasya: Of truth or righteousness
    • dhārayā: Upholding or supporting
    • vājaṁ: Wealth or strength
    • gomantam: Full of strength (often associated with cattle, a symbol of wealth)
    • akṣaran: Imperishable or eternal

  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks to the essence of noble qualities and the importance of truth and purity in our lives.
    • The term "abodes of the noble" suggests that there are places or states of being that embody these virtues, which can be interpreted as both physical and spiritual realms.
    • The reference to "wealth that is full of strength" can be understood in a modern context as the importance of inner strength and moral wealth over material possessions.
    • For example, in a corporate setting, a company that values integrity and ethical practices can be seen as embodying the principles of this mantra, as it upholds truth and purity in its dealings.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who prioritize honesty and support each other can create a "noble abode" that fosters growth and resilience.

  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call to embody noble qualities and uphold truth in all aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder that true wealth is not merely material but is rooted in strength of character and moral integrity.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to healthier relationships, ethical business practices, and a more harmonious society.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to strive for purity and truth, creating a lasting impact on themselves and their communities.
