कुवित्सस्य प्र हि व्रजं गोमन्तं दस्युहा गमत् |शचीभिर अप नो वरत ||
kuvitsasya pra hi vrajaṁ gomantaṁ dasyuhā gamat | śacībhir apa no varat ||
  • kuvitsasya: This term can be interpreted as "of the one who is wise" or "of the one who has knowledge." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in guiding actions and decisions.
  • pra hi vrajaṁ: This phrase translates to "indeed, go forth." It suggests a call to action, encouraging individuals to move forward with purpose and determination.
  • gomantaṁ: This word refers to "the one who possesses cattle" or "wealth." In ancient times, cattle were a symbol of prosperity and abundance, representing not just material wealth but also the sustenance of life.
  • dasyuhā: This term can be understood as "the one who is a thief" or "the one who takes away." It implies a warning against those who may seek to disrupt or steal from the wise and prosperous.
  • gamat: This word means "to go" or "to move." It reinforces the idea of action and the necessity of taking steps towards one's goals.
  • śacībhir: This term refers to "with the help of the wise" or "with the support of the knowledgeable." It highlights the importance of community and collaboration in achieving success.
  • apa no varat: This phrase translates to "may we not be harmed" or "may we be protected." It expresses a desire for safety and security in the face of challenges.

In summary, this mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom, action, and community support in overcoming obstacles and achieving prosperity. It serves as a reminder to be vigilant against those who may seek to disrupt our progress and to seek guidance from knowledgeable individuals. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:


  • In personal development, individuals can reflect on the importance of seeking knowledge and wisdom before making significant life decisions.
  • In business, the mantra encourages collaboration and teamwork, highlighting the need for collective effort to achieve success while being aware of potential threats from competitors.
  • In community building, it serves as a reminder to support one another and protect the interests of the group against external challenges.

Ultimately, the mantra teaches us that wisdom, action, and community are essential components in navigating life's challenges and achieving our goals.
