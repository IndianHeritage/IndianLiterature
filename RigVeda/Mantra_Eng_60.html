इतो वा सातिम ईमहे दिवो वा पार्थिवाद अधि |इन्द्रं महो वा रजसः ||
ito vā sātim īmahe divo vā pārthivād adhi | indram maho vā rajasaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ito: from here, from this place
    • vā: or
    • sātim: wisdom, intelligence
    • īmahe: we invoke, we seek
    • divo: of the heavens, celestial
    • vā: or
    • pārthivād: of the earthly, terrestrial
    • adhi: above, over
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods
    • maho: great, powerful
    • vā: or
    • rajasaḥ: of the storm, of the atmosphere
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra expresses a call to invoke wisdom and intelligence from both the celestial and earthly realms.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance from Indra, who represents strength and power, particularly in the context of overcoming challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often seek wisdom from both experienced mentors (earthly) and innovative thinkers (celestial) to make informed decisions.
    • During challenging times, such as natural disasters, communities come together to invoke strength and support from both local leaders and external aid organizations.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the duality of seeking wisdom from both the material and spiritual worlds, reminding us that knowledge can come from various sources.
    • It encourages individuals to look beyond their immediate surroundings and consider broader perspectives when facing difficulties.
    • In personal development, it serves as a reminder to balance practical knowledge with spiritual insight, fostering holistic growth.
