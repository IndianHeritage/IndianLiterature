आ सूर्यो यातु सप्ताश्वः क्षेत्रं यद अस्योरविय दीरघयाथे |रघुः श्येनः पतयद अन्धो अच्छा युवा कविर दीदायद गोषु गच्छन् ||
ā sūryo yātu saptāśvaḥ kṣetraṁ yad asyorviyā dīrghayāthe | raghuḥ śyenaḥ patayad andho acchā yuvā kavir dīdayad goṣu gacchan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • sūryo: the sun
    • yātu: may it go
    • saptāśvaḥ: with seven horses
    • kṣetraṁ: the field or realm
    • yad: which
    • asyorviyā: of the one who is powerful
    • dīrghayāthe: may it be long-lasting
    • raghuḥ: the swift one
    • śyenaḥ: the eagle
    • patayad: may it fly
    • andho: blind
    • acchā: good
    • yuvā: young
    • kavir: the wise one
    • dīdayad: may it shine
    • goṣu: among the cows
    • gacchan: going
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the sun, symbolizing energy, vitality, and enlightenment.
    • The phrase "saptāśvaḥ" refers to the seven horses of the sun, representing the various aspects of life and the universe.
    • "Kṣetraṁ" signifies the field or realm of existence, indicating the importance of our environment and surroundings.
    • The mantra emphasizes the need for longevity and prosperity in life, as indicated by "dīrghayāthe."
    • The imagery of "raghuḥ" and "śyenaḥ" suggests swiftness and wisdom, qualities that are essential for success.
    • The mention of "andho" and "acchā" highlights the contrast between ignorance and knowledge, urging us to seek clarity and understanding.
    • "Yuvā" and "kavir" point towards youthfulness and wisdom, suggesting that one should maintain a balance between energy and knowledge.
    • The reference to "goṣu" indicates the nurturing aspect of life, as cows are often seen as symbols of abundance and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, encouraging individuals to seek enlightenment in their personal and professional endeavors.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders can embody the qualities of "raghuḥ" and "śyenaḥ" by being swift in decision-making while also being wise and considerate of their team's needs.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra serves as a reminder to nurture connections, much like caring for "goṣu," ensuring that relationships are both fruitful and supportive.
    • In health and wellness, the emphasis on longevity ("dīrghayāthe") can inspire individuals to adopt healthy lifestyles that promote physical and mental well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking knowledge, wisdom, and prosperity in life.
    • Its significance lies in the holistic approach it advocates, urging individuals to balance energy, knowledge, and nurturing aspects of life.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a guiding principle for personal growth, professional success, and harmonious relationships.
    • By embodying the qualities represented in this mantra, individuals can strive for a fulfilling and meaningful existence.
