अग्नि त्वं यशाऽस्यामित्रावरुणा वह | ऋतावानां सम्राजाऽपूतदक्षसः ||
agne tvaṁ yaśā asy ā mitrāvaruṇā vaha | ṛtāvānā samrājā pūtadakṣasā ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying force and a symbol of energy and transformation.
  • Tvaṁ: This means "you," addressing Agni directly, indicating a personal relationship and reverence towards the divine.
  • Yaśā: This translates to "glory" or "fame." It signifies the importance of recognition and honor in one's life.
  • Asy: This means "of this," referring to the offerings or the context in which the mantra is being recited.
  • Ā: This is a particle that can mean "to" or "towards," indicating direction or intention.
  • Mitrāvaruṇā: This compound word refers to the deities Mitra and Varuna, who represent friendship, harmony, and cosmic order. They embody the principles of truth and righteousness.
  • Vaha: This means "carry" or "bring," suggesting a request for the divine to bring forth blessings or benefits.
  • Ṛtāvānā: This term means "one who is established in truth" or "the one who follows the cosmic order." It emphasizes the importance of living in accordance with dharma (righteousness).
  • Samrājā: This translates to "the great ruler" or "sovereign," indicating a position of authority and power, often associated with leadership and governance.
  • Pūtadakṣasā: This means "one who is pure and skilled," highlighting the qualities of purity and competence that are essential for effective leadership.

Conclusion: The mantra "agne tvaṁ yaśā asy ā mitrāvaruṇā vaha | ṛtāvānā samrājā pūtadakṣasā ||" is a prayer invoking the fire god Agni to bestow glory and blessings, while also calling upon the deities Mitra and Varuna to ensure harmony and truth. It emphasizes the significance of purity, skill, and adherence to cosmic order in leadership. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during important ceremonies, leadership roles, or personal endeavors where one seeks guidance, strength, and recognition. It serves as a reminder to uphold values of truth and righteousness in all aspects of life, fostering a sense of responsibility and integrity in one's actions.
