प्रजापतिर मह्यम् एता रराणो विश्वैर देवैः पितृभिः संविदानः | शिवाः सतीर उप नो गोष्ठम् आकाश तासां वयम् प्रजया सं सदेम ||
prajāpatir mahyam etā rarāṇo viśvair devaiḥ pitṛbhiḥ saṁvidānaḥ | śivāḥ satīr upa no goṣṭham ākas tāsāṁ vayam prajayā saṁ sadema ||
  • prajāpatir: This term refers to the "Lord of Creatures" or "Creator," symbolizing the divine force responsible for creation and the sustenance of life.
  • mahyam: Meaning "to me," this word indicates a personal connection or appeal to the divine.
  • etā: Translated as "these," it refers to the various aspects or entities that are being addressed in the mantra.
  • rarāṇo: This word means "to be nourished" or "to be sustained," emphasizing the need for support and sustenance from the divine.
  • viśvair: Meaning "by all," it signifies the collective support from all divine beings and ancestors.
  • devaiḥ: This translates to "by the gods," indicating the involvement of divine forces in the sustenance of life.
  • pitṛbhiḥ: Referring to "by the ancestors," this word highlights the importance of ancestral blessings and guidance.
  • saṁvidānaḥ: Meaning "bestowing knowledge" or "providing wisdom," it emphasizes the role of divine and ancestral guidance in life.
  • śivāḥ: This term means "auspicious" or "peaceful," representing the positive and beneficial aspects of life.
  • satīr: Translated as "truthful" or "virtuous," it signifies the importance of integrity and righteousness.
  • upa: Meaning "near" or "close," it indicates a desire for closeness to the divine and the community.
  • no: This word means "us," emphasizing the collective aspect of the prayer.
  • goṣṭham: Referring to "the community" or "the gathering," it highlights the importance of social bonds and unity.
  • ākas: Meaning "in the sky" or "heavens," it symbolizes the divine realm and higher consciousness.
  • tāsāṁ: This translates to "of them," referring to the divine and ancestral beings.
  • vayam: Meaning "we," it emphasizes the collective identity of the community.
  • prajayā: This word means "by progeny" or "through offspring," highlighting the importance of continuity and legacy.
  • saṁ: Meaning "together," it signifies unity and collective effort.
  • sadema: Translated as "may we attain," it expresses a desire for achievement and fulfillment.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of creation, community, and divine guidance. It calls upon the Creator, gods, and ancestors for nourishment, wisdom, and support, highlighting the importance of living a virtuous life in harmony with others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in community gatherings, rituals, or personal reflections where individuals seek blessings for their families and communities. It serves as a reminder of the significance of unity, integrity, and the pursuit of a meaningful life, fostering a sense of belonging and purpose in the collective journey of existence.
