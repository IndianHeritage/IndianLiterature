उता स्तुतासो मरुतो व्यन्तु विश्वेभिर नामभिर नरो हविष्णि |ददात नो अमृतस्य प्रजायै जिगृता रायः सूनृताः मग्हानि ||
uta stutāso maruto vyantu viśvebhir nāmabhir naro havīṁṣi | dadāta no amṛtasya prajāyai jigṛta rāyaḥ sūnṛtā maghāni ||
  • uta stutāso maruto: This phrase can be translated as "may the Maruts, the storm deities, be praised." The Maruts are often associated with strength, power, and the forces of nature. In real life, this can be likened to the respect we give to natural phenomena, such as storms and winds, which can be both destructive and life-giving.
  • vyantu viśvebhir nāmabhir: This means "may they come with all their names." Here, the emphasis is on the various aspects and manifestations of the Maruts. In our lives, this can be seen in how we recognize and appreciate the diversity in nature and the different roles that various elements play in our ecosystem.
  • naro havīṁṣi: Translated, this means "may men offer their oblations." This reflects the human practice of giving thanks and offerings to the divine forces. In modern life, this can be compared to acts of gratitude, whether through rituals, community service, or simply appreciating the resources we have.
  • dadāta no amṛtasya prajāyai: This phrase translates to "may they grant us the immortality of progeny." It speaks to the desire for longevity and the continuation of life through future generations. In contemporary terms, this can relate to our efforts in ensuring a sustainable future for our children, such as environmental conservation and education.
  • jigṛta rāyaḥ sūnṛtā maghāni: This can be interpreted as "may they bestow upon us wealth and prosperity." This reflects the human aspiration for success and abundance. In real life, this can be seen in our pursuit of career goals, financial stability, and the importance of community support in achieving these aspirations.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep reverence for nature and the divine forces that govern it. It emphasizes the importance of gratitude, the desire for prosperity, and the continuity of life. In today's world, this mantra serves as a reminder of our interconnectedness with nature and the responsibilities we hold towards future generations. By recognizing the power of nature and expressing gratitude, we can foster a more harmonious existence, ensuring that we not only thrive but also contribute positively to the world around us.
