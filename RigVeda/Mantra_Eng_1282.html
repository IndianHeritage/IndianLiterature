जुजुरुषो नासत्योता वव्रिम प्रामुñचतं द्रापिम इव च्यवानात् |प्रातिरतं जहितस्यायुर दास्राद इत पतिम अकृतुं कनीनाम् ||
jujuruṣo nāsatyota vavrim prāmuñcataṁ drāpim iva cyavānāt | prātirataṁ jahitasyāyur dasrād it patim akṛṇutaṁ kanīnām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • jujuruṣo: One who is engaged in the act of seeking or striving.
    • nāsatyota: Referring to the Asvins, the twin deities associated with health and medicine.
    • vavrim: To release or to let go.
    • prāmuñcataṁ: To free or to liberate.
    • drāpim: A term that can refer to a bond or a tie.
    • iva: Like or as.
    • cyavānāt: From falling or from being lost.
    • prātirataṁ: The dawn or the beginning of a new day.
    • jahitasyāyur: The life that is relinquished or given up.
    • dasrād: From the one who is bound or tied.
    • it: Here or in this context.
    • patim: The lord or master.
    • akṛṇutaṁ: To create or to establish.
    • kanīnām: Of the young or the new.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the act of seeking liberation from the bonds that tie us down, much like how the Asvins, as divine figures, assist in healing and restoring balance.
    • The phrase "release the bonds" can be seen as a metaphor for overcoming obstacles in life, whether they be emotional, physical, or spiritual.
    • It emphasizes the importance of letting go of what no longer serves us, akin to how one might release negative thoughts or toxic relationships to embrace a healthier lifestyle.
    • The reference to the dawn signifies new beginnings, suggesting that by freeing ourselves from our limitations, we can welcome new opportunities and experiences.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, someone might find themselves in a job that is unfulfilling. By "releasing the bonds," they may choose to pursue a new career path that aligns with their passions.
    • In relationships, letting go of toxic friendships can lead to healthier connections and a more supportive social circle.
    • On a broader scale, communities can apply this mantra by working together to overcome societal issues, such as poverty or discrimination, by freeing themselves from outdated beliefs and practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of liberation and renewal in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement to seek out and embrace change, allowing for personal growth and the pursuit of a fulfilling life.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can inspire individuals and communities to break free from limitations, fostering resilience and hope for a brighter future.
