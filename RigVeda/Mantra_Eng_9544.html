इमां मे अग्नि समिधं जुषस्वेऽलस पदे प्रति हर्याः घृताचिम् |वर्ष्मन् पृथिव्याः सुदिनत्वे अह्नाम् ऊर्ध्वो भव सुक्रतो देवयज्ञा ||
imām me agne samidhaṁ juṣasveḻas pade prati haryā ghṛtācīm | varṣman pṛthivyāḥ sudinatve ahnām ūrdhvo bhava sukrato devayajyā ||
  • imām me agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni, this is for you." Agni is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, we can relate this to the importance of fire in rituals, cooking, and even in our daily lives as a source of warmth and light.
  • samidhaṁ juṣasve: This means "may you enjoy this offering." The term "samidhaṁ" refers to the fuel or wood used in the fire, which is essential for the sacrificial offerings. In a practical sense, this can be seen in how we prepare for important events or gatherings, ensuring that we have the right resources to create a welcoming environment.
  • las pade prati haryā ghṛtācīm: This translates to "in the place where the offerings are made, may you be pleased with the clarified butter." Clarified butter (ghee) is a significant offering in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and nourishment. In modern life, this can be likened to the importance of quality ingredients in cooking, which enhance the flavor and health benefits of our meals.
  • varṣman pṛthivyāḥ: This means "may you bring prosperity to the earth." It emphasizes the connection between divine blessings and the fertility of the land. In real life, this can be related to sustainable farming practices that ensure the earth remains fertile and productive for future generations.
  • sudinatve ahnām: This translates to "in the brilliance of the day." It signifies the importance of light and clarity in our lives. In a practical sense, this can be seen in how we seek knowledge and wisdom to illuminate our paths, much like the sun brightens the day.
  • ūrdhvo bhava sukrato: This means "may you rise high, O virtuous one." It speaks to the aspiration for growth and elevation in life through good deeds. In real life, this can be related to personal development and striving for excellence in our endeavors, whether in our careers or personal lives.
  • devayajyā: This translates to "through divine worship." It highlights the importance of spirituality and connection with the divine in our lives. In modern contexts, this can be seen in various forms of meditation, prayer, or community service that foster a sense of connection and purpose.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for prosperity, purity, and growth in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of offerings, both literal and metaphorical, that we make in our daily lives to foster a connection with the divine. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied through acts of kindness, sustainable practices, and the pursuit of knowledge and virtue. By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can create a harmonious and prosperous existence, both for ourselves and for the world around us.
