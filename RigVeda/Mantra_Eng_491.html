यम् बाहुतेव पिप्रति पान्ति मर्त्यं ऋषः |अरीष्टः सर्व एधते ||
yam bāhuteva piprati pānti martyaṁ riṣaḥ | ariṣṭaḥ sarva edhate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yam: who
    • bāhuteva: like a strong arm
    • piprati: nourishes or sustains
    • pānti: protects or guards
    • martyaṁ: mortal beings
    • riṣaḥ: the sage or seer
    • ariṣṭaḥ: free from obstacles or misfortunes
    • sarva: all
    • edhate: thrive or flourish
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine force or energy that acts like a strong arm, providing nourishment and protection to all mortal beings.
    • The term "riṣaḥ" refers to a sage, indicating that wisdom and insight are essential for understanding and harnessing this divine energy.
    • The phrase "ariṣṭaḥ sarva edhate" suggests that when one is aligned with this divine force, they can overcome obstacles and thrive in all aspects of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a mentor can be seen as a "strong arm" who nourishes and protects their mentees, guiding them through challenges and helping them to flourish in their careers.
    • In personal relationships, a supportive family member or friend can provide the necessary encouragement and protection that allows an individual to thrive, especially during difficult times.
    • In a broader societal context, community leaders who work to uplift and protect their communities embody the essence of this mantra, ensuring that all members can thrive without fear of misfortune.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the importance of support, wisdom, and protection in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek out and embody the qualities of nourishment and protection, both for themselves and for others.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as mentorship, community support, and personal relationships, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of collective flourishing.
