या इमे उभे अहनी पुर एत्य अप्रयुच्छन् |स्वाधीर देवः सविता ||
ya ime ubhe ahanī pura ety aprayucchan | svādhīr devaḥ savitā ||
  • Mantra: "ya ime ubhe ahanī pura ety aprayucchan | svādhīr devaḥ savitā ||"
  • Translation: "Those who have come to this world, the two (aspects of time) that do not hinder (the progress), may the self-controlled deity Savitā (the Sun) guide us."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ya: who/that
    • ime: these
    • ubhe: both
    • ahanī: aspects of time (day and night)
    • pura: having come
    • ety: having arrived
    • aprayucchan: do not hinder
    • svādhīr: self-controlled
    • devaḥ: deity
    • savitā: the Sun
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra invokes the deity Savitā, representing the Sun, which is a symbol of light, knowledge, and enlightenment.
    • The two aspects of time, day and night, are acknowledged as essential parts of life that should not hinder our progress.
    • It emphasizes the importance of self-control and guidance from a higher power in navigating life's challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often face challenges that can be likened to the cycles of day and night. For instance, during difficult times (night), one must remember that brighter days (day) will come.
    • Self-control is crucial in various aspects, such as managing stress, making decisions, and maintaining relationships. For example, a student preparing for exams must exercise self-discipline to study effectively.
    • Seeking guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders can be compared to invoking the deity Savitā, as they provide wisdom and direction in times of uncertainty.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the cyclical nature of life and the importance of self-control and divine guidance.
    • It encourages individuals to embrace both the challenges and opportunities presented by the passage of time.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, resilience, and a deeper understanding of one's journey.
