येब्यः माता मधुमत् पिन्वते पयः पीयूषं द्यौर अदितिः अद्रिबर्हाः |उक्तशुष्मान वृषभरान स्वप्नसस तां आदित्यां अनु मदा स्वस्तये ||
yebhyo mātā madhumat pinvate payaḥ pīyūṣaṁ dyaur aditir adribarhāḥ | ukthaśuṣmān vṛṣabharān svapnasas tām̐ ādityām̐ anu madā svastaye ||
  • yebhyo mātā madhumat pinvate payaḥ: This phrase translates to "From whom the nourishing mother (the Earth) pours forth sweet milk." Here, "mātā" refers to the Earth or nature, and "madhumat" signifies something that is sweet or nourishing. In real life, this can be likened to how we depend on nature for sustenance, much like a child depends on its mother for milk.
  • pīyūṣaṁ dyaur aditir adribarhāḥ: This part means "the sky, the limitless, and the bounteous." "Pīyūṣaṁ" refers to something that is nourishing, while "dyaur" signifies the sky. This can be related to how the sky provides rain, which is essential for agriculture and life. For instance, farmers rely on the sky's bounty to grow crops, which in turn sustains communities.
  • ukthaśuṣmān vṛṣabharān svapnasas: This translates to "the one who is rich in hymns and who brings forth dreams." "Uktha" refers to hymns or praises, and "svapnasas" relates to dreams or aspirations. This can be seen in how individuals and societies create aspirations through culture, art, and spirituality. For example, a community may come together to celebrate their heritage through music and dance, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
  • tām̐ ādityām̐ anu madā svastaye: This means "to that Aditya (the Sun), we offer our praise for well-being." "Aditya" refers to the Sun, which is often seen as a source of life and energy. In real life, the Sun is crucial for photosynthesis, which sustains plant life and, consequently, all life on Earth. For instance, solar energy is harnessed today as a sustainable energy source, reflecting our reverence for the Sun's power.

  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, the importance of nourishment, and the role of aspirations in human life. It highlights how we rely on the Earth and the sky for sustenance and how cultural expressions shape our dreams and aspirations. In a modern context, this mantra can inspire us to respect and protect our environment, recognizing that our well-being is tied to the health of the planet. It also encourages us to pursue our dreams and aspirations, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
