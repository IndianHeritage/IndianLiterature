जनीयन्तो न्व अग्रवः पुत्रीयन्तः सुदानवः |सरस्वन्तं हवामहे ||
janīyanto nv agravaḥ putrīyantaḥ sudānavaḥ | sarasvantaṁ havāmahe ||
  • Mantra: "janīyanto nv agravaḥ putrīyantaḥ sudānavaḥ | sarasvantaṁ havāmahe ||"
  • Translation: "We invoke the Sarasvatī, the one who brings forth prosperity and knowledge, who is the source of all that is good and auspicious."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • janīyanto: Those who give birth or create.
    • nv: Indeed or certainly.
    • agravaḥ: The foremost or the best.
    • putrīyantaḥ: Those who are worthy of being called children or descendants.
    • sudānavaḥ: Those who are good and auspicious.
    • sarasvantaṁ: The one who is endowed with knowledge and wisdom.
    • havāmahe: We invoke or we call upon.
  • Context and Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Sarasvatī, the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning in Hindu tradition.
    • It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and the role of education in personal and societal development.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in educational settings, where students and teachers invoke the blessings of Sarasvatī for wisdom and success in their endeavors.
    • For example, students often recite this mantra before exams or important academic pursuits to seek clarity and understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra highlights the significance of knowledge and the pursuit of wisdom as essential components of a fulfilling life.
    • It serves as a reminder to value education and the continuous quest for learning in various aspects of life.
    • By invoking Sarasvatī, individuals can cultivate a mindset that appreciates the importance of knowledge, creativity, and the arts, leading to personal growth and societal advancement.
