यथा नो अदितिः करत पश्वे नृभ्यो यथा गवे |यथा तोकाय रुद्रियम ||
yathā no aditiḥ karat paśve nṛbhyo yathā gave | yathā tokāya rudriyam ||
  • Mantra: "yathā no aditiḥ karat paśve nṛbhyo yathā gave | yathā tokāya rudriyam ||"
  • Translation: "As Aditi (the infinite) acts towards us, as she does towards the cattle and men, as she does towards the offspring."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yathā: as, in the manner of
    • no: towards us
    • aditiḥ: Aditi, the infinite or boundless
    • karat: acts, performs
    • paśve: towards cattle
    • nṛbhyo: towards men
    • gavaḥ: towards the cow (symbol of wealth and nourishment)
    • tokāya: towards the offspring, progeny
    • rudriyam: Rudra, often associated with the storm and the fierce aspect of divinity
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the nurturing and protective qualities of Aditi, who represents the infinite and the cosmic mother.
    • It draws a parallel between the divine care provided by Aditi and the care that should be extended towards all living beings, including cattle and humans.
    • The mention of "offspring" signifies the importance of nurturing future generations, ensuring their well-being and prosperity.
    • Rudra, associated with transformation and change, suggests that while nurturing is essential, it is also important to embrace the changes that life brings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for the well-being of their cattle, recognizing their role in sustaining human life through milk and labor. This reflects the mantra's call for care towards animals.
    • In family life, parents strive to provide a nurturing environment for their children, ensuring they grow up healthy and strong, akin to the divine care of Aditi.
    • In community settings, initiatives that focus on the welfare of all members, including the vulnerable, resonate with the mantra's message of universal care.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all life and the responsibility we have towards each other and the environment.
    • It encourages us to act with compassion and care, reflecting the nurturing qualities of the divine in our daily lives.
    • By embodying these principles, we contribute to a harmonious society where all beings can thrive, much like the nurturing aspect of Aditi.
