मां नः समस्या दूद्यः परिद्वेषसो अंहतिḥ |ऊर्मिर न नावम् आवधीत ||
mā naḥ samasya dūḍhya1ḥ paridveṣaso aṁhatiḥ | ūrmir na nāvam ā vadhīt ||
  • mā naḥ - "Do not" or "Let not us" - This phrase serves as a plea or a request, indicating a desire for protection or avoidance of harm.
  • samasya - "Problems" or "difficulties" - This word refers to the challenges or obstacles that one may face in life.
  • dūḍhyaḥ - "To be overcome" or "to be defeated" - This suggests the act of overcoming or conquering difficulties.
  • paridveṣaso - "Of enmity" or "of hatred" - This term highlights the negative emotions that can arise in human interactions, such as hostility or animosity.
  • aṁhatiḥ - "Destruction" or "death" - This word signifies the ultimate consequence of unresolved conflicts or enmity.
  • ūrmir - "Wave" - This can symbolize the flow of life, emotions, or experiences that come and go.
  • na - "Not" - A negation, reinforcing the desire to avoid certain outcomes.
  • nāvam - "Boat" - A metaphor for a vessel that carries one through the challenges of life.
  • ā vadhīt - "Be destroyed" - This phrase expresses a wish for the boat (symbolizing life or one's journey) to not be harmed or sunk.

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for protection against the difficulties and enmities that life may present. It emphasizes the importance of overcoming challenges without succumbing to hatred or destruction. The imagery of a boat suggests that life is a journey, and one must navigate through waves (both good and bad) without allowing negativity to sink their vessel.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal relationships, it serves as a reminder to resolve conflicts peacefully and avoid harboring resentment.
  • In professional settings, it encourages teamwork and collaboration, urging individuals to overcome challenges together rather than allowing competition to breed hostility.
  • In mental health, it highlights the importance of addressing emotional struggles and seeking support to navigate through life's turbulent waves.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for harmony and resilience. It teaches us to face life's challenges with courage and compassion, ensuring that we do not let negativity or enmity lead to our downfall. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can foster a more peaceful and supportive environment in their personal and communal lives.
