अद्रिणा ते मन्दिन इन्द्र तुयांन सुन्वन्ति सोमान पिबसि त्वम् एषाम् |पचन्ति ते वृषभाम् अति तेषाम् पृथ्क्षेण यन् मग्धवन हूयमानः ||
adriṇā te mandina indra tūyān sunvanti somān pibasi tvam eṣām | pacanti te vṛṣabhām̐ atsi teṣām pṛkṣeṇa yan maghavan hūyamānaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • adriṇā: By the mountains
    • te: Your
    • mandina: With the wise
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • tūyān: You who are praised
    • sunvanti: They who are offering
    • somān: The Soma (sacred drink)
    • pibasi: You drink
    • tvam: You
    • eṣām: Of these
    • pacanti: They cook
    • te: Your
    • vṛṣabhām̐: The strong one
    • atsi: You are
    • teṣām: Of them
    • pṛkṣeṇa: With the offering
    • yan: That which
    • maghavan: O bounteous one
    • hūyamānaḥ: Being invoked
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of gods, who is often associated with strength, rain, and the nourishment of life.
    • The phrase "adriṇā te mandina" suggests that the mountains, which are often seen as symbols of stability and strength, are praising Indra.
    • "Sunvanti somān pibasi tvam eṣām" indicates that the wise ones are offering Soma, a sacred drink, to Indra, who is invited to partake in it.
    • The line "pacanti te vṛṣabhām̐" refers to the strong ones who prepare offerings for Indra, emphasizing the importance of community and collaboration in worship.
    • "Pṛkṣeṇa yan maghavan hūyamānaḥ" highlights the act of invoking Indra, suggesting that he is called upon for blessings and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, invoking Indra through rituals and offerings is a way to seek rain and ensure a good harvest, reflecting the community's reliance on nature.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can symbolize the importance of collaboration and collective effort in achieving goals, much like how teams work together to succeed in various fields.
    • The act of sharing and offering, as depicted in the mantra, can be seen in community gatherings where food and resources are shared, fostering unity and support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine blessings in human endeavors, particularly in agriculture and community life.
    • It teaches us the value of collaboration, respect for nature, and the need to invoke higher powers for guidance and support.
    • In contemporary life, the principles of this mantra can be applied in teamwork, community service, and environmental stewardship, reminding us of our interconnectedness and the need for collective action.
