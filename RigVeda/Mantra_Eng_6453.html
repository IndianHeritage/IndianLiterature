तं आगन्म सोभरयः सहस्रमुष्कं स्वभिष्टिम अवसे |सम्राजं त्रासदस्यवम् ||
tam āganma sobharayaḥ sahasramuṣkaṁ svabhiṣṭim avase | samrājaṁ trāsadasyavam ||
  • tam āganma: This phrase can be translated as "we have come to that" or "we have approached that." It signifies a journey or a quest towards a specific goal or state of being.
  • sobharayaḥ: This word means "illuminated" or "radiant." It suggests a state of enlightenment or clarity, often associated with knowledge and wisdom.
  • sahasramuṣkaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "thousand-fold" or "abundant." It indicates a vastness or richness, often referring to resources, blessings, or opportunities.
  • svabhiṣṭim avase: This phrase translates to "we seek our own desires" or "we attain our own wishes." It emphasizes the importance of personal aspirations and the pursuit of one's goals.
  • samrājaṁ: This word means "sovereign" or "king." It represents authority, power, and the ability to govern one's life and circumstances.
  • trāsadasyavam: This term can be understood as "the one who brings fear" or "the one who protects from fear." It highlights the duality of power—both the ability to instill fear and the capacity to provide safety and security.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a journey towards enlightenment and the pursuit of personal desires while acknowledging the dual nature of power. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as leadership, personal development, and the quest for knowledge. For instance, a leader who seeks to illuminate their path and the path of others can create a thriving environment where individuals pursue their aspirations. The mantra serves as a reminder that while we strive for our goals, we must also be aware of the responsibilities and impacts of our power on others. It encourages us to seek wisdom, embrace abundance, and govern our lives with a sense of purpose and responsibility.
