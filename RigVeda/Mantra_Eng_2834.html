इन्द्र सोमाः सुताः इमे तव प्र यन्ति सत्पते |क्षयम् चन्द्रासा इन्दवः ||
indra somāḥ sutā ime tava pra yanti satpate | kṣayaṁ candrāsa indavaḥ ||
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • Somāḥ: This refers to the soma, a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine insight. It symbolizes the essence of life and vitality.
  • Sutā: This term means 'sons' or 'offspring,' indicating the progeny or the results of divine actions.
  • Ime: This word translates to 'these,' referring to the offerings or the entities being addressed in the mantra.
  • Tava: Meaning 'your,' it signifies possession or connection to the divine.
  • Pra yanti: This phrase means 'they go forth' or 'they proceed,' indicating movement towards a goal or destination.
  • Satpate: This term refers to the 'lord of truth' or 'the one who leads to truth,' often associated with the ultimate reality or the divine order.
  • Kṣayaṁ: This means 'destruction' or 'diminution,' indicating a loss or a decrease in something.
  • Candrāsa: This term relates to the moon, often symbolizing calmness, beauty, and the cyclical nature of life.
  • Indavaḥ: This refers to the 'soma' or 'nectar' that is derived from the moon, symbolizing nourishment and the divine essence.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Indra, asking for the blessings of vitality and strength through the sacred soma. It emphasizes the connection between the divine and the earthly, where the offerings made to Indra are intended to bring forth prosperity and truth.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Spiritual Growth: Just as the mantra seeks blessings from Indra for strength and vitality, individuals can invoke similar energies in their lives through meditation and prayer, seeking clarity and purpose.
  • Community and Leadership: The reference to 'satpate' highlights the importance of truth and leadership. In a community setting, leaders can embody these qualities to inspire and guide others towards a common goal.
  • Environmental Awareness: Indra's association with rain and storms can remind us of the importance of nature and the need to respect and protect our environment, ensuring that we live in harmony with the earth.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in achieving strength, truth, and vitality. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, urging individuals to seek balance and harmony in their pursuits. By embodying the qualities represented in the mantra, one can navigate life's challenges with resilience and purpose.
