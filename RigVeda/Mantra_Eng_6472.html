तान वन्दस्व मरुतस् तां उप स्तुहि तेषां हि धुनीनाम् |अराणां न चरमस् तद एषां दानां मह्ना तद एषाम् ||
tān vandasva marutas tām̐ upa stuhi teṣāṁ hi dhunīnām | arāṇāṁ na caramas tad eṣāṁ dānā mahnā tad eṣām ||
  • tān vandasva marutas: This phrase can be translated as "I bow to the Maruts." The Maruts are a group of storm deities in Vedic literature, often associated with wind, storms, and the power of nature. In real life, this can be seen as a recognition of the forces of nature and the importance of respecting them.
  • tām̐ upa stuhi teṣāṁ hi dhunīnām: This translates to "I praise them, for they are the movers." Here, the term "movers" refers to the dynamic and transformative aspects of the Maruts. In our daily lives, this can be related to the idea of change and progress, emphasizing the need to adapt and move forward in life.
  • arāṇāṁ na caramas tad: This means "the ultimate is not for the unworthy." It suggests that the highest rewards or achievements are reserved for those who are deserving. In practical terms, this can be applied to the concept of meritocracy in society, where hard work and dedication lead to success.
  • eṣāṁ dānā mahnā tad eṣām: This translates to "this is the great gift of theirs." It highlights the blessings and gifts that the Maruts bestow upon humanity. In real life, this can be interpreted as the abundance of resources and opportunities that nature provides, which we should cherish and utilize wisely.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and respecting the forces of nature, the value of hard work and merit, and the blessings that come from these natural elements. In a real-life scenario, it serves as a reminder to appreciate the environment, strive for personal growth, and acknowledge that true rewards come from deserving efforts. This mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as environmental conservation, personal development, and community service, encouraging individuals to act responsibly and honorably in their pursuits.
