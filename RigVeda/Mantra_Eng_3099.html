मन्त्र: कथामहे पुष्टिम्भराय पूष्णे कद रुद्राय सुमखाय हविर्दे |कद विष्णव उरुगायाय रेतो ब्रवः कद अग्ने शरवे बृहत्यै ||
kathā mahe puṣṭimbharāya pūṣṇe kad rudrāya sumakhāya havirde | kad viṣṇava urugāyāya reto bravaḥ kad agne śarave bṛhatyai ||
  • kathā mahe puṣṭimbharāya pūṣṇe: This phrase can be translated as "How do we invoke the great nourisher, the one who provides sustenance?" Here, "puṣṭimbharāya" refers to the divine source of nourishment, which can be interpreted as the forces of nature or the universe that sustain life. In real life, this can relate to the importance of agriculture, food production, and the natural resources that provide for humanity.
  • kad rudrāya sumakhāya havirde: This translates to "When do we offer our prayers to Rudra, the auspicious one?" Rudra is often associated with storms and the wild aspects of nature, but also with healing and protection. In a modern context, this can be seen in how we seek balance in our lives, turning to nature for both its beauty and its ferocity, and recognizing the need for respect and reverence towards it.
  • kad viṣṇava urugāyāya reto bravaḥ: This means "When do we call upon Vishnu, the one who pervades all, for the essence of life?" Vishnu represents preservation and protection. In everyday life, this can be reflected in our efforts to maintain harmony in our communities and environments, emphasizing sustainability and the protection of our planet.
  • kad agne śarave bṛhatyai: This translates to "When do we invoke Agni, the great one, for our offerings?" Agni symbolizes fire, transformation, and the divine messenger. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of rituals, traditions, and the transformative power of fire in various cultures, such as cooking, warmth, and purification.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life and the divine forces that sustain and protect us. It calls for a recognition of the natural world and the deities that represent various aspects of existence. In real life, this can be applied through sustainable practices, respect for nature, and the importance of community and tradition. By invoking these divine energies, we acknowledge our dependence on the universe and our responsibility to care for it, fostering a sense of gratitude and reverence in our daily lives.
