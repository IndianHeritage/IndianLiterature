यच्छुश्रूयाऽइमं हवं दुरमार्षं चक्रियाऽउत |भवेर्वापिर नो अन्तमः ||
yac chuśrūyā imaṁ havaṁ durmarṣaṁ cakriyā uta | bhaver āpir no antamaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yac: that which
    • chuśrūyā: is heard or listened to
    • imaṁ: this
    • havaṁ: invocation or offering
    • durmarṣaṁ: difficult to overcome or resist
    • cakriyā: through action or effort
    • uta: or
    • bhaver: may it become
    • āpir: abundant or plentiful
    • no: for us
    • antamaḥ: the ultimate or the best
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the power of invocation and the importance of listening to divine guidance.
    • It emphasizes the need for effort in overcoming challenges and achieving abundance.
    • The phrase "may it become abundant for us" suggests a desire for prosperity and fulfillment in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied when launching a new product. The team must listen to market feedback (chuśrūyā) and take action (cakriyā) to overcome competition (durmarṣaṁ) to achieve success (bhaver āpir).
    • In personal development, individuals may face challenges in their journey. By invoking positive affirmations (havaṁ) and actively working towards their goals (cakriyā), they can manifest abundance (bhaver āpir) in their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of listening, taking action, and striving for abundance in various aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder that challenges can be overcome through dedicated effort and a positive mindset.
    • In essence, it encourages individuals to seek divine guidance and work diligently towards their aspirations, ensuring that they remain open to the opportunities that life presents.
