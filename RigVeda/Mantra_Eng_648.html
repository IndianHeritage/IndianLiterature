असमाṁ क्षत्रम् असमा मनिषा प्र सोमपा अपसां सन्तु नेमे |ये त इन्द्र ददुषो वर्धयन्ति महि क्षत्रं स्थविरं वृष्ण्यं च ||
asamaṁ kṣatram asamā manīṣā pra somapā apasā santu neme | ye ta indra daduṣo vardhayanti mahi kṣatraṁ sthaviraṁ vṛṣṇyaṁ ca ||
  • asamaṁ kṣatram: This phrase translates to "unequal power" or "disparate strength." It suggests that power and strength are not uniform and can vary greatly among individuals or groups.
  • asamā manīṣā: This means "unequal wisdom" or "different levels of understanding." It highlights that wisdom and intelligence are not distributed equally among people.
  • pra somapā: This refers to "those who drink soma," which symbolizes those who partake in divine knowledge or enlightenment. Soma is often associated with immortality and spiritual awakening.
  • apasā santu neme: This can be interpreted as "may they not be hindered." It expresses a desire for those who seek knowledge and power to be free from obstacles.
  • ye ta indra daduṣo: This translates to "those who, like Indra, are powerful." Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, representing strength, leadership, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • vardhayanti mahi kṣatraṁ: This means "who enhance the great power." It emphasizes the importance of nurturing and increasing strength and capability in society.
  • sthaviraṁ vṛṣṇyaṁ ca: This refers to "the ancient and the strong." It acknowledges the value of tradition and the wisdom that comes with age and experience.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the diversity of power and wisdom among individuals and the importance of nurturing strength and knowledge in society. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, education, and community building. For instance, in a workplace, recognizing that team members have different strengths and areas of expertise can lead to more effective collaboration and innovation. Similarly, in educational settings, understanding that students learn at different paces can help create a more inclusive and supportive learning environment. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to value diversity and to support one another in the pursuit of knowledge and strength.
