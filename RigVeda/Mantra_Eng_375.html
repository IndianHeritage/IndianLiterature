अपाद अहस्तो अपृतन्यद इन्द्रं आस्य वज्रम अधि सानौ जग्हान | वृष्णो वध्रिः प्रतिमानं बुभूषण पुरुत्रा वृत्रो अशयद व्यस्तः ||
apād ahasto apṛtanyad indram āsya vajram adhi sānau jaghāna | vṛṣṇo vadhriḥ pratimānam bubhūṣan purutrā vṛtro aśayad vyastaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apād: without feet
    • ahasto: without hands
    • apṛtanyad: not having any support
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods
    • āsya: of this
    • vajram: thunderbolt or weapon
    • adhi: upon
    • sānau: the two
    • jaghāna: struck down
    • vṛṣṇo: of the Vṛṣhṇi clan
    • vadhriḥ: the strong one
    • pratimānam: in the form
    • bubhūṣan: desiring
    • purutrā: everywhere
    • vṛtro: the demon Vritra
    • aśayad: who has been slain
    • vyastaḥ: scattered
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power of Indra, who is depicted as a mighty warrior capable of overcoming obstacles.
    • The phrase "apād ahasto" suggests a state of being without physical means, yet still capable of achieving great feats, symbolizing the idea that true strength comes from within.
    • Indra's "vajram" represents the divine weapon that can conquer evil, akin to how individuals can overcome personal challenges with inner strength and determination.
    • The mention of "vṛtro" refers to the demon of drought and obstacles, which can be likened to the challenges we face in life, such as financial difficulties or emotional struggles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an employee may face a challenging project (vṛtro) that seems insurmountable. By channeling their inner strength (vajram) and seeking support from colleagues (Indra), they can overcome these challenges.
    • In personal life, someone dealing with mental health issues may feel "apād ahasto," yet through self-reflection and seeking help, they can find the strength to heal and thrive.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of inner strength and resilience in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It teaches us that even when we feel powerless or unsupported, we can draw upon our inner resources to confront and conquer our obstacles.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to face their fears, seek help when needed, and ultimately emerge victorious in their personal and professional endeavors.
