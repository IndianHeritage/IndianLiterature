यस्य श्वेताऽविचक्षणा तिस्रो भूमीरधिक्षितः |त्रिरुत्तराṇि पप्रतुर्वरुणस्य ध्रुवं सदा स सप्तानामिरज्यति नभन्तामन्यके समे ||
yasya śvetā vicakṣaṇā tisro bhūmīr adhikṣitaḥ | trir uttarāṇi papratur varuṇasya dhruvaṁ sadaḥ sa saptānām irajyati nabhantām anyake same ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • śvetā: white or pure
    • vicakṣaṇā: wise or perceptive
    • tisro: three
    • bhūmīr: the earth
    • adhikṣitaḥ: governed or controlled
    • trir: three times
    • uttarāṇi: the higher or upper
    • papratur: they have attained
    • varuṇasya: of Varuna (the god of water and cosmic order)
    • dhruvaṁ: steadfast or eternal
    • sadaḥ: always
    • sa: he or that
    • saptānām: of the seven
    • irajyati: is established
    • nabhantām: in the heavens
    • anyake: other
    • same: in the same manner
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine being who is wise and pure, governing the earth and the heavens.
    • The reference to "three times" suggests a cyclical nature of existence, akin to the cycles of life, death, and rebirth.
    • Varuna, as the god of cosmic order, signifies the importance of maintaining harmony and balance in the universe.
    • The phrase "dhruvaṁ sadaḥ" emphasizes the eternal nature of this divine order, suggesting that it is unchanging and reliable.
    • The mention of "saptānām" indicates a connection to the seven realms or aspects of existence, which can be interpreted in various ways, such as the seven continents, seven colors of the rainbow, or seven chakras in spiritual practices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In environmental conservation, the idea of governing the earth can be seen in the efforts to maintain ecological balance, where wise management of resources is crucial.
    • The cyclical nature of life can be observed in agricultural practices, where farmers plant, harvest, and prepare for the next season, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on cycles.
    • In personal development, the concept of striving for balance and harmony in one's life mirrors the teachings of Varuna, encouraging individuals to seek stability in their emotional and spiritual lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom, purity, and the importance of maintaining cosmic order.
    • It serves as a reminder of our responsibility to govern our actions and the environment wisely, ensuring harmony in our lives and the world around us.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to seek balance and understanding in their personal and communal lives, reflecting the eternal truths that govern existence.
