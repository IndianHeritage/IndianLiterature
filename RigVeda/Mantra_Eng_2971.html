आक्षित पूर्वास्व अपरानूरुत सद्यो जातासु तरुणीष्व अन्तः |अन्तर्वतीः सुवते अप्रवीता महद देवानाम असुरत्वम् एकम् ||
ākṣit pūrvāsv aparā anūrut sadyo jātāsu taruṇīṣv antaḥ | antarvatīḥ suvate apravītā mahad devānām asuratvam ekam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ākṣit: Seen or perceived
    • pūrvāsv: In the earlier or previous
    • aparā: Later or subsequent
    • anūrut: Following or in accordance with
    • sadyo: Immediately or instantly
    • jātāsu: Born or manifested
    • taruṇīṣv: In the young or youthful
    • antaḥ: Within or inside
    • antarvatīḥ: Those that are within
    • suvate: They give birth or produce
    • apravītā: Not obstructed or unimpeded
    • mahad: Great or significant
    • devānām: Of the gods
    • asuratvam: The quality of being divine or celestial
    • ekam: One or singular
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the perception of the divine and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It emphasizes the idea that the divine essence is present in all forms of life, especially in the youthful and vibrant aspects of existence.
    • The phrase "antarvatīḥ suvate" suggests that those who are within (the divine) give birth to new life, indicating a cycle of creation and rebirth.
    • The mantra also highlights the unimpeded nature of this divine essence, suggesting that it flows freely and is accessible to all.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we can observe the cycle of life where young plants (taruṇīṣv) grow and flourish, embodying the divine essence of creation.
    • In human relationships, the nurturing aspect of love and care can be seen as a manifestation of this divine quality, where individuals support and uplift each other.
    • In moments of inspiration or creativity, individuals often feel a connection to something greater, reflecting the idea of the divine being present within them.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the omnipresence of the divine in all aspects of life, encouraging individuals to recognize and honor this connection.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios such as personal growth, relationships, and creative endeavors, reminding us that we are all part of a larger, interconnected whole.
    • By acknowledging the divine within ourselves and others, we can foster a sense of unity and purpose in our lives.
