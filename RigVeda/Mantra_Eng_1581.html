तं इति पृच्छन्ति न सिमो वि पृच्छति स्वेनैव धीरः मनसा यद अग्रभीत् |न मृष्यते प्रथमं नापरं वचोऽस्य क्रत्वा साचते अप्रदृपितः ||
tam it pṛcchanti na simo vi pṛcchati sveneva dhīro manasā yad agrabhīt | na mṛṣyate prathamaṁ nāparaṁ vaco 'sya kratvā sacate apradṛpitaḥ ||
  • tam it pṛcchanti: This phrase translates to "they inquire about him." It signifies the importance of seeking knowledge and understanding from wise individuals.
  • na simo vi pṛcchati: This means "not even a fool inquires." It highlights that ignorance often leads to a lack of curiosity or the desire to learn.
  • sveneva dhīro manasā: This translates to "the wise one, with his own mind." It emphasizes the idea that true wisdom comes from within and is cultivated through introspection and self-awareness.
  • yad agrabhīt: This means "what is most feared." It suggests that the pursuit of knowledge often involves confronting fears and uncertainties.
  • na mṛṣyate prathamaṁ nāparaṁ vaco 'sya: This translates to "his words do not deceive, neither the first nor the latter." It underscores the reliability and truthfulness of wise counsel.
  • kratvā sacate apradṛpitaḥ: This means "by action, he achieves without being disturbed." It indicates that wisdom is not just theoretical but is manifested through actions and decisions that are calm and composed.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of seeking knowledge and wisdom. It teaches us that true understanding comes from within and requires courage to confront our fears. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in education, personal development, and decision-making processes. For instance, a student who actively seeks help from teachers and peers demonstrates the essence of "tam it pṛcchanti," while a leader who makes informed decisions based on reliable information embodies "kratvā sacate apradṛpitaḥ." Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the value of wisdom and the importance of being inquisitive and truthful in our pursuits.
