स चेतयन् मनुष्यो यज्ञबन्धुः प्र तं मह्यां रशनया नयन्ति |स क्षेत्य अस्य दुर्यासु साधन देवो मर्तस्य साधनित्वम् आप ||
sa cetayan manuṣo yajñabandhuḥ pra tam mahyā raśanayā nayanti | sa kṣety asya duryāsu sādhan devo martasya sadhanitvam āpa ||
  • sa cetayan manuṣo yajñabandhuḥ: This phrase can be translated to "the one who is aware, a human connected to the sacrifice." Here, "sa" refers to "he" or "that person," "cetayan" means "aware" or "conscious," "manuṣo" means "human," and "yajñabandhuḥ" refers to "connected to the sacrifice." This highlights the importance of consciousness and awareness in human actions, particularly in the context of rituals and sacrifices.
  • pra tam mahyā raśanayā nayanti: This translates to "they lead him with great rays." "Pra" means "forth" or "forward," "tam" means "him," "mahyā" means "great," "raśanayā" refers to "rays" or "light," and "nayanti" means "they lead." This suggests that those who are aware and connected to the divine or the greater purpose are guided by a higher light or wisdom.
  • sa kṣety asya duryāsu sādhan: This can be interpreted as "he who is capable in difficult situations." "Sa" again refers to "he," "kṣety" means "capable" or "able," "asya" means "of this," "duryāsu" refers to "difficult situations," and "sādhan" means "to achieve" or "to accomplish." This emphasizes the ability to navigate through challenges and adversities.
  • devo martasya sadhanitvam āpa: This translates to "the divine grants the ability to achieve in the mortal realm." "Devo" means "the divine," "martasya" refers to "of mortals," "sadhanitvam" means "ability to achieve," and "āpa" means "grants" or "bestows." This signifies that divine support is essential for humans to attain their goals and overcome obstacles.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of awareness, connection to a higher purpose, and the divine guidance that helps individuals navigate through life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, leadership, and spiritual growth. For instance, a leader who is aware of their responsibilities and connected to their team's goals can inspire and guide their team effectively, much like the rays of light leading the way. Additionally, individuals facing difficulties in their personal or professional lives can find solace in the belief that divine support is available to help them achieve their aspirations. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interplay between human effort and divine assistance in the journey of life.
