सोमो राजा प्रथमो ब्रह्मजायाम पुनः प्रायच्छद अहृणीयमानः |अन्वर्तिता वरुणो मित्र आसीद अग्निर होता हस्तगृह्या निनाय ||
somo rājā prathamo brahmajāyām punaḥ prāyacchad ahṛṇīyamānaḥ | anvartitā varuṇo mitra āsīd agnir hotā hastagṛhyā nināya ||
  • Mantra: "somo rājā prathamo brahmajāyām punaḥ prāyacchad ahṛṇīyamānaḥ | anvartitā varuṇo mitra āsīd agnir hotā hastagṛhyā nināya ||"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • somo: Refers to 'Soma', a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing vitality and immortality.
    • rājā: Means 'king' or 'ruler', indicating authority and leadership.
    • prathamo: Translates to 'first', suggesting primacy or importance.
    • brahmajāyām: Refers to 'in the realm of Brahman', the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit.
    • punaḥ: Means 'again' or 'once more', indicating repetition or renewal.
    • prāyacchad: Means 'bestowed' or 'given', suggesting generosity or sharing.
    • ahṛṇīyamānaḥ: Refers to 'not being taken away', indicating permanence or stability.
    • anvartitā: Means 'having been followed' or 'adhered to', suggesting adherence to a path or principle.
    • varuṇo: Refers to 'Varuna', the god of cosmic order and water, symbolizing law and morality.
    • mitra: Means 'friend', indicating companionship and support.
    • āsīd: Means 'was' or 'existed', indicating a state of being.
    • agnir: Refers to 'Agni', the fire god, symbolizing transformation and energy.
    • hotā: Means 'the one who offers', indicating a role of a priest or mediator.
    • hastagṛhyā: Refers to 'having been taken by hand', indicating direct involvement or action.
    • nināya: Means 'led' or 'guided', suggesting direction or leadership.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of leadership and guidance in both spiritual and worldly matters.
    • The reference to 'Soma' and 'Agni' highlights the balance between nourishment (Soma) and transformation (Agni), which can be seen in our daily lives where we seek both sustenance and growth.
    • The invocation of Varuna and Mitra signifies the need for moral integrity and friendship in our interactions, reminding us to uphold ethical values in our relationships.
    • In a practical sense, this mantra can be applied in leadership roles, where one must embody the qualities of a ruler who is generous, stable, and adheres to a moral code.
    • In community settings, it encourages collaboration and support among individuals, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of leadership, morality, and community. It encourages individuals to embody these qualities in their personal and professional lives, promoting a harmonious and balanced existence. By reflecting on these principles, we can strive to create a more just and supportive society.
