शतक्रतुम अर्णवं शाकिनं नरं गिरो मा इन्द्रं उप यन्ति विश्वतः |वाजसनिम पूर्वभिदं तूर्णिम अप्तुरं धामसाचम् अभिषाचं स्वरविदम् ||
śatakratum arṇavaṁ śākinaṁ naraṁ giro ma indram upa yanti viśvataḥ | vājasanim pūrbhidaṁ tūrṇim apturaṁ dhāmasācam abhiṣācaṁ svarvidam ||
  • śatakratum: This term refers to "the one who has a hundred powers" or "the one who has achieved a hundred victories." In real life, this can symbolize a person who has overcome numerous challenges and has a wealth of experience.
  • arṇavaṁ: This word means "ocean" or "vastness." It can represent the vastness of knowledge or the depth of emotions that one experiences in life.
  • śākinaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is capable" or "the one who has the ability." It signifies competence and skill, which are essential in various aspects of life, such as career and personal development.
  • naraṁ: This means "man" or "human." It emphasizes the importance of humanity and the human experience in the context of the mantra.
  • giro: This word translates to "speech" or "words." It highlights the power of communication and expression in shaping our realities and relationships.
  • ma: This is a particle that can mean "not" or "may." It serves as a call for attention or a request for divine assistance.
  • indram: Referring to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature, this term symbolizes strength, leadership, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • upa yanti: This phrase means "approach" or "come near." It signifies the act of seeking guidance or support from a higher power or authority.
  • viśvataḥ: This term means "from all directions" or "universally." It indicates the all-encompassing nature of the divine or the universal principles that govern life.
  • vājasanim: This word can be interpreted as "the one who brings victory" or "the one who provides strength." It represents the qualities of resilience and determination.
  • pūrbhidaṁ: This means "the one who breaks through" or "the one who overcomes." It signifies the ability to break barriers and achieve success against all odds.
  • tūrṇim: This term translates to "swift" or "quick." It emphasizes the importance of timely action and responsiveness in life.
  • apturaṁ: This word means "the one who is fulfilled" or "the one who has attained." It represents the achievement of goals and the realization of potential.
  • dhāmasācam: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is radiant" or "the one who shines." It signifies the qualities of brilliance and excellence in one's endeavors.
  • abhiṣācaṁ: This means "the one who is blessed" or "the one who is anointed." It represents the idea of divine favor and support in one's pursuits.
  • svarvidam: This term translates to "the one who knows the essence of sound" or "the one who understands the nature of reality." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in navigating life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of human potential, divine support, and the pursuit of excellence. It serves as a reminder that individuals possess the ability to overcome challenges, seek guidance, and achieve their goals. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, leadership, and communication. By embodying the qualities represented in the mantra, individuals can strive for success and fulfillment in their lives, drawing strength from both their inner capabilities and the support of the universe.
