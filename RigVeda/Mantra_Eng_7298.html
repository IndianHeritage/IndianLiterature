सोमस्य मित्रावरुणोदितā सूर ā ददē |तद आतुरस्य भेषजम् ||
somasya mitrāvaruṇoditā sūra ā dade | tad āturasya bheṣajam ||
  • Mantra: "somasya mitrāvaruṇoditā sūra ā dade | tad āturasya bheṣajam ||"
  • Translation: "The one who is born from the Soma, may Mitra and Varuna grant strength; may they provide the remedy for the afflicted."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • Soma: In Vedic literature, Soma refers to a sacred drink that is believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes the essence of life and vitality.
    • Mitra: Mitra is a deity associated with friendship, harmony, and the natural order. He represents the positive aspects of relationships and social bonds.
    • Varuna: Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water. He embodies the moral and ethical aspects of the universe, ensuring that natural laws are upheld.
    • Sūra: This term refers to strength or vigor, often associated with bravery and the ability to overcome challenges.
    • Āturasya: This word means 'afflicted' or 'sick', indicating a state of distress or suffering.
    • Bheṣajam: This translates to 'remedy' or 'healing', suggesting a solution or cure for ailments.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern medicine, the concept of seeking strength and healing can be seen in the way people turn to holistic practices, combining traditional medicine with spiritual and emotional support.
    • In personal relationships, the invocation of Mitra can be reflected in how friends support each other during tough times, emphasizing the importance of community and connection.
    • Varuna's aspect of maintaining order can be related to environmental conservation efforts, where individuals and communities work together to uphold the balance of nature.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of strength, healing, and the divine forces of friendship and order. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek support from our relationships and the natural world when facing challenges. It encourages us to cultivate strength not just for ourselves but also for the well-being of others, highlighting the importance of community and ethical living. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during personal struggles, in health and wellness practices, and in fostering harmonious relationships within society.
