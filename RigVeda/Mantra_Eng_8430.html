प्र ते मदासो मदिरासा आशवो 'सृक्षत रथ्यासो यथा पृथक् |धेनुर न वत्सम् पयसााभि वज्रीणम् इन्द्रम् इन्दवो मधुमन्त उर्मयः ||
pra te madāso madirāsa āśavo 'sṛkṣata rathyāso yathā pṛthak | dhenur na vatsam payasābhi vajriṇam indram indavo madhumanta ūrmayaḥ ||
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "May the intoxication of your essence, O Indra, flow forth like a river, as the cows yield their milk to the calf, and may you, the mighty one, grant us the sweetness of life."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra te: For you
    • madāso: intoxication or essence
    • madirāsa: sweet or delightful
    • āśavo: flowing or abundant
    • 'sṛkṣata: may flow forth
    • rathyāso: like a river
    • yathā: as
    • pṛthak: separate or distinct
    • dhenur: cow
    • na: not
    • vatsam: calf
    • payasābhi: with milk
    • vajriṇam: mighty or powerful
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods
    • indavo: of the Indras
    • madhumanta: sweet or delightful
    • ūrmayaḥ: waves or abundance
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the god of rain and thunder, symbolizing strength and abundance.
    • The imagery of cows yielding milk to their calves represents nourishment and the cycle of life, emphasizing the importance of sustenance and care.
    • The flow of intoxication signifies joy and the richness of life experiences, suggesting that one should embrace the sweetness of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, the blessings of rain (associated with Indra) are crucial for crop growth, symbolizing the importance of divine favor in daily life.
    • The nurturing relationship between a mother cow and her calf can be seen in dairy farming, where the health of livestock directly impacts the livelihood of farmers.
    • In personal life, the mantra encourages individuals to seek joy and abundance, reminding them to appreciate the small pleasures that life offers.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine blessings in achieving abundance and joy in life.
    • It serves as a reminder to appreciate the nurturing aspects of life, whether through relationships, nature, or personal experiences.
    • In modern contexts, it can inspire individuals to cultivate gratitude and seek fulfillment in their daily lives, recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
