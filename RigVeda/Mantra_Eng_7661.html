अग्निं वो वृद्धन्तम अध्वराणाम् पुरूतमम् |अच्छा नप्त्रे सहस्वते ||
agniṁ vo vṛdhantam adhvarāṇām purūtamam | acchā naptre sahasvate ||
  • Mantra: "agniṁ vo vṛdhantam adhvarāṇām purūtamam | acchā naptre sahasvate ||"
  • Translation: "O Agni, the most excellent among the offerings, may you be our strength and support in all our rituals. May you grant us abundance and prosperity."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Agni: Refers to fire, which is a central element in Vedic rituals and symbolizes purity, transformation, and energy.
    • Vṛdhantam: Means "increasing" or "growing," indicating the desire for growth and prosperity.
    • Adhvarāṇām: Refers to "rituals" or "sacrifices," emphasizing the importance of performing sacred rites.
    • Purūtamam: Means "the most excellent" or "the best," highlighting Agni's supreme status among the deities.
    • Acchā: Translates to "may you grant" or "may you provide," expressing a plea for blessings.
    • Naptre: Refers to "descendants" or "offspring," indicating a focus on family and future generations.
    • Sahasvate: Means "abundant" or "prosperous," signifying a wish for wealth and success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a wedding ceremony, the couple may invoke Agni as a witness and a source of strength for their union, seeking blessings for a prosperous life together.
    • During a housewarming ceremony, families often light a fire to symbolize the welcoming of Agni into their new home, asking for abundance and protection.
    • In agricultural communities, rituals may be performed to honor Agni, seeking favorable conditions for crops and prosperity for the family.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a vital force in rituals and daily life. It reflects the human desire for growth, prosperity, and the well-being of future generations. By invoking Agni, individuals and communities seek to align themselves with divine energies that promote abundance and success. This mantra is applicable in various life scenarios, including personal milestones, family gatherings, and community celebrations, reinforcing the connection between the sacred and the everyday.
