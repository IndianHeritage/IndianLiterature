दीर्घतमā मामतेयो जुजुर्वान दशमे युगे | अपाम अर्थं यतीनां ब्रह्मा भवति सारथिḥ ||
dīrghatamā māmateyo jujurvān daśame yuge | apām arthaṁ yatīnām brahmā bhavati sārathiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dīrghatamā: Long-lasting or eternal
    • māmateyo: Of my mind or thoughts
    • jujurvān: One who is engaged or involved
    • daśame: In the tenth
    • yuge: Age or era
    • apām: Of the waters
    • arthaṁ: Purpose or meaning
    • yatīnām: Of the ascetics or sages
    • brahmā: The ultimate reality or cosmic spirit
    • bhavati: Becomes or manifests
    • sārathiḥ: Charioteer or guide
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the eternal nature of thoughts and the importance of being engaged in the right pursuits during the significant era of the tenth age.
    • The reference to "waters" symbolizes the flow of life and knowledge, suggesting that the purpose of sages is to guide others through this flow.
    • In a metaphorical sense, the "charioteer" represents a guiding force, akin to a mentor or teacher who helps navigate the complexities of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern education, teachers often act as charioteers, guiding students through the vast ocean of knowledge, helping them to focus their thoughts and efforts on meaningful pursuits.
    • In personal development, individuals who seek wisdom and guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders embody the essence of the sages mentioned in the mantra.
    • In leadership, effective leaders serve as charioteers for their teams, steering them towards a common goal while ensuring that their thoughts and actions are aligned with the organization's vision.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of having a long-lasting and focused mindset, especially during critical times.
    • It highlights the role of guidance in achieving one's purpose, whether through education, mentorship, or leadership.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, effective teamwork, and a deeper understanding of one's purpose in the grand scheme of life.
