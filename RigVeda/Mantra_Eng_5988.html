अमनमहीद अनाशवोऽनुग्रासश्च वृत्रहन् |सकृत्सु ते महता शूर राधसा अनु स्तोमं मुदीमहि ||
amanmahīd anāśavo 'nugrāsaś ca vṛtrahan | sakṛt su te mahatā śūra rādhasā anu stomam mudīmahi ||
  • Amanmahīd: This term can be broken down into "aman" meaning "not" and "mahīd" which refers to "earth" or "world." Together, it suggests a state of being that transcends the earthly realm.
  • Anāśavo: This word means "without decay" or "immortal." It signifies a state of eternal existence, free from the limitations of time and physical deterioration.
  • 'Anugrāsaś: This term can be interpreted as "one who is not consumed" or "not devoured." It implies a sense of protection or preservation from harm.
  • Vṛtrahan: This is a compound word where "Vṛtra" refers to a demon or obstacle, and "han" means "to slay" or "to overcome." Thus, it signifies the act of conquering challenges or adversities.
  • Sakṛt: This word means "once" or "at once," indicating immediacy or a singular moment of action.
  • Su te: This phrase translates to "good for you" or "well for you," expressing a positive sentiment or blessing.
  • Mahātā: This term means "great" or "mighty," often used to describe something of significant power or importance.
  • Śūra: This word translates to "brave" or "heroic," referring to qualities of courage and valor.
  • Rādhasā: This term can be associated with "Rādhā," symbolizing devotion and love, particularly in the context of divine relationships.
  • Anu: This word means "following" or "after," indicating a sense of continuity or progression.
  • Stomam: This refers to "praise" or "hymn," often used in the context of chanting or reciting verses in reverence.
  • Mudīmahi: This term means "we celebrate" or "we rejoice," indicating a collective expression of joy or gratitude.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of transcendence, immortality, protection, and the triumph over obstacles. It emphasizes the importance of courage and devotion in overcoming life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to moments of struggle, where individuals seek strength and resilience. For instance, during difficult times such as illness or personal loss, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder of one's inner strength and the support of the divine. It encourages individuals to celebrate their victories, no matter how small, and to remain steadfast in their pursuits, embodying the qualities of bravery and devotion in their daily lives.
