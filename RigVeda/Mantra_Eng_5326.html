प्र यन्ति यज्ञं विपयन्ति बर्हिः सोममादो विदथे दुध्रवाचः |न्य उ भ्रियन्ते यशसो गृभाद आ दूरत्पब्दो वृषणो नृषाचः ||
pra yanti yajñaṁ vipayanti barhiḥ somamādo vidathe dudhravācaḥ | ny u bhriyante yaśaso gṛbhād ā dūraüpabdo vṛṣaṇo nṛṣācaḥ ||
  • pra yanti yajñaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "they go towards the sacrifice." In the context of the Rigveda, yajña refers to a ritual or sacrifice performed to please the deities. In real life, this can be seen in various cultural practices where offerings are made to seek blessings, such as during festivals or important life events.
  • vipayanti barhiḥ: This means "the sacred grass is spread." Barhiḥ refers to the grass used in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and sanctity. In modern life, this can be likened to the preparation of a sacred space for important events, such as weddings or religious ceremonies, where specific items are used to create a holy atmosphere.
  • somamādo vidathe dudhravācaḥ: This translates to "the joy of the soma is proclaimed in the assembly." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, representing divine ecstasy and inspiration. In contemporary terms, this can be compared to the joy and celebration found in communal gatherings, such as family reunions or community festivals, where shared experiences bring people together.
  • ny u bhriyante yaśaso gṛbhād: This means "they do not hold back from glory." It emphasizes the importance of striving for honor and recognition. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of achievements, whether in personal goals, professional success, or community service, where individuals seek to make a positive impact and gain respect.
  • ā dūraüpabdo vṛṣaṇo nṛṣācaḥ: This translates to "from afar, the strong ones are called." It suggests that those who are powerful or influential are invited to participate. In modern contexts, this can relate to leadership and the importance of bringing together strong, capable individuals to work towards a common goal, such as in team projects or community initiatives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of communal effort, the significance of rituals, and the pursuit of glory and strength. It highlights how ancient practices can still resonate in today's world, where rituals and gatherings foster community spirit, and the pursuit of honor drives individuals to achieve greatness. This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of collective participation in both spiritual and worldly endeavors, encouraging us to come together for a greater purpose.
