त्रिधा हितं पाणिभिर गुह्यमानं गवि देवासो घृतम् अन्व अविन्दन् |इन्द्र एकं सूर्य एकं जजाना वेनाद एकं स्वधया निष्टतक्षुः ||
tridhā hitam paṇibhir guhyamānaṁ gavi devāso ghṛtam anv avindan | indra ekaṁ sūrya ekaṁ jajāna venād ekaṁ svadhayā niṣ ṭatakṣuḥ ||
  • Mantra: "tridhā hitam paṇibhir guhyamānaṁ gavi devāso ghṛtam anv avindan | indra ekaṁ sūrya ekaṁ jajāna venād ekaṁ svadhayā niṣ ṭatakṣuḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tridhā: in three ways
    • hitam: beneficial or good
    • paṇibhir: by hands
    • guhyamānaṁ: hidden or secret
    • gavi: in the cow (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • devāso: the gods
    • ghṛtam: clarified butter (symbolizing purity and nourishment)
    • anv avindan: found or discovered
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • ekaṁ: one
    • sūrya: the sun
    • jajāna: was born or created
    • venād: from the sound or vibration
    • svadhayā: by one's own will or desire
    • niṣ ṭatakṣuḥ: having established or set
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine and the interconnectedness of various elements in the universe.
    • The phrase "tridhā hitam" suggests that there are three beneficial aspects or ways to approach a situation, which can be interpreted as physical, mental, and spiritual dimensions.
    • The mention of "guhyamānaṁ" indicates that there are hidden truths or knowledge that can be discovered through effort and devotion.
    • The cow, represented by "gavi," symbolizes wealth and nourishment, emphasizing the importance of sustenance in life.
    • The gods, referred to as "devāso," are seen as guiding forces that help individuals find clarity and purpose.
    • "ghṛtam" signifies purity and the essence of life, suggesting that one should seek purity in thoughts and actions.
    • The invocation of Indra and the sun highlights the significance of strength and illumination in one's journey.
    • The phrase "by one's own will" emphasizes personal agency and the power of intention in manifesting desires.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, one might approach a project with a "tridhā hitam" mindset by considering the technical, interpersonal, and strategic aspects to ensure success.
    • When facing challenges, individuals can reflect on the "guhyamānaṁ" aspect by seeking hidden opportunities or lessons within difficulties.
    • The importance of nourishment can be seen in the growing trend of organic farming, where the focus is on pure and sustainable food sources.
    • In personal development, individuals often invoke the strength of "Indra" and the clarity of "sūrya" to overcome obstacles and illuminate their paths.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking knowledge, strength, and purity in life.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that through intention, effort, and a holistic approach, one can uncover hidden truths and achieve personal growth.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more balanced and fulfilling existence, guiding individuals in their personal and professional journeys.
