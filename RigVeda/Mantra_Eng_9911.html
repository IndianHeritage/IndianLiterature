अश्वावतीं सोमावतīm ऊर्जयन्तीं उदोजसम् |आवित्सि सर्वा ओषधीर अस्मा अरिष्टतातये ||
aśvāvatīṁ somāvatīm ūrjayantīm udojasam | āvitsi sarvā oṣadhīr asmā ariṣṭatātaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvāvatīṁ: One who is like a horse, symbolizing strength and vitality.
    • somāvatīm: One who is like Soma, representing nourishment and rejuvenation.
    • ūrjayantīm: One who is powerful and energetic.
    • udojasam: One who rises or ascends, indicating growth and elevation.
    • āvitsi: May it be present or manifest.
    • sarvā: All or every.
    • oṣadhīr: Herbs or plants, often associated with healing and sustenance.
    • asmā: Us or our.
    • ariṣṭatātaye: For the purpose of removing obstacles or difficulties.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the qualities of strength, nourishment, and vitality, akin to a horse and the divine Soma.
    • It emphasizes the importance of herbs and plants in providing sustenance and healing.
    • The phrase "for the purpose of removing obstacles" suggests a plea for support in overcoming challenges in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the mantra can be seen as a call for healthy crops and the vitality of plants, which are essential for food security.
    • In personal health, it can be interpreted as a reminder to seek natural remedies and nourishment from plants to maintain well-being.
    • In a broader sense, it can be applied to any endeavor where strength and support are needed to overcome challenges, such as in business or personal relationships.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of strength, nourishment, and the natural world.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek balance and support from nature in our lives.
    • By invoking these qualities, we can aspire to overcome obstacles and achieve our goals, whether in health, agriculture, or personal growth.
