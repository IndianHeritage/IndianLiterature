सत्यं इत्त्था वृषेद असि वृषजूतिर नोऽवृतः |वृषा ह्युग्र श्रिण्विषे परावती वृषो अर्वावती श्रुतः ||
satyam itthā vṛṣed asi vṛṣajūtir no 'vṛtaḥ | vṛṣā hy ugra śṛṇviṣe parāvati vṛṣo arvāvati śrutaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • satyam: Truth
    • itthā: Thus
    • vṛṣed: You may be strong
    • asi: You are
    • vṛṣajūtir: The one who brings strength
    • no: Our
    • 'vṛtaḥ: Not covered
    • vṛṣā: Strength
    • hy: Indeed
    • ugra: Fierce
    • śṛṇviṣe: You hear
    • parāvati: Supreme
    • vṛṣo: The strong one
    • arvāvati: One who is powerful
    • śrutaḥ: Heard
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of truth and strength in life.
    • The phrase "satyam itthā" suggests that truth is a foundation upon which strength is built.
    • In real life, this can be seen in leaders who are honest and transparent, gaining the trust and strength of their followers.
    • The term "vṛṣed asi" indicates that one can embody strength through truthfulness.
    • For example, a person who stands by their principles, even in challenging situations, demonstrates true strength.
    • The mantra also highlights the idea that strength should not be hidden or covered ("no 'vṛtaḥ"), suggesting that authenticity is crucial.
    • In a workplace, employees who are open and honest about their capabilities and challenges often foster a more collaborative and productive environment.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder that truth and strength are interconnected.
    • In personal relationships, being truthful can lead to stronger bonds and mutual respect.
    • In society, leaders who prioritize honesty and integrity can inspire others and create a more just community.
    • In conclusion, the mantra encourages individuals to embrace truth as a source of strength, applicable in various aspects of life, from personal integrity to leadership and community building.
