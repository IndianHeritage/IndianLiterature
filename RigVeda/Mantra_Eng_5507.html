जमयाऽत्र वसवो रन्त देवाऽ उराव् अन्तरिक्षे मर्जयन्त शुभराः |अर्वाक् पथ उरुज्रयः कृत्वं श्रोताः दूतस्य जग्मुषो नोऽस्य ||
jmayā atra vasavo ranta devā urāv antarikṣe marjayanta śubhrāḥ | arvāk patha urujrayaḥ kṛṇudhvaṁ śrotā dūtasya jagmuṣo no asya ||
  • jmayā atra vasavo ranta devā urāv antarikṣe marjayanta śubhrāḥ
    • jmayā: This word can be interpreted as "by birth" or "from the womb." It signifies the origin or the beginning of existence.
    • atra: Meaning "here" or "in this place," it indicates a specific context or situation.
    • vasavo: Refers to the "Vasus," a group of deities associated with various natural elements and prosperity.
    • ranta: This can mean "delight" or "joy," suggesting a state of happiness or fulfillment.
    • devā: Meaning "gods," it refers to divine beings or higher powers.
    • urāv: This word means "great" or "vast," indicating something of significant magnitude.
    • antarikṣe: Refers to the "intermediate space" or "atmosphere," symbolizing the realm between earth and heaven.
    • marjayanta: Meaning "purifying" or "cleansing," it suggests a process of making something pure or sacred.
    • śubhrāḥ: This translates to "bright" or "auspicious," indicating positivity and goodness.
  • arvāk patha urujrayaḥ kṛṇudhvaṁ śrotā dūtasya jagmuṣo no asya
    • arvāk: Meaning "towards the front" or "forward," it suggests a direction or intention.
    • patha: Refers to "path" or "way," indicating a journey or course of action.
    • urujrayaḥ: This can be interpreted as "those who are strong" or "powerful," referring to individuals with strength or capability.
    • kṛṇudhvaṁ: Meaning "make" or "create," it implies an action of bringing something into existence.
    • śrotā: Refers to "listeners" or "hearers," indicating those who are receptive to knowledge or messages.
    • dūtasya: Meaning "of the messenger," it refers to a representative or envoy.
    • jagmuṣo: This translates to "who have gone" or "who have departed," indicating movement or transition.
    • no asya: Meaning "of this," it refers to something specific that has been mentioned or is being discussed.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine presence and the purification of one's path in life. It suggests that the Vasus, as embodiments of natural elements, bring joy and prosperity when invoked. The call to action for the strong and capable to create a path forward highlights the significance of taking initiative and being proactive in one's life journey. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, community building, and environmental stewardship, encouraging individuals to purify their intentions and actions for a brighter future. It serves as a reminder that divine support is available when one aligns their actions with purity and positivity.
