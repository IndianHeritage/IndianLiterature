इन्द्रवायू अयं सुतस् तं देवैभिः सजोषसा |पिबतं दाशुषो गृहे ||
indravāyū ayaṁ sutas taṁ devebhiḥ sajoṣasā | pibataṁ dāśuṣo gṛhe ||
  • Indravāyū: This term refers to the combined forces of Indra, the king of gods, and Vayu, the god of wind. In a broader sense, it symbolizes the powerful and dynamic forces of nature that govern the universe.
  • ayaṁ: This word translates to "this" or "here," indicating a presence or a specific focus on the subject at hand.
  • sutas: This means "the son" or "offspring," often referring to the offerings or the results of one's actions.
  • taṁ: This translates to "that," pointing towards something significant or worthy of attention.
  • devebhiḥ: This word means "by the gods," indicating divine involvement or blessings in the context of the mantra.
  • sajoṣasā: This term can be interpreted as "with joy" or "with enthusiasm," suggesting a positive and celebratory approach to the offerings or actions being performed.
  • pibataṁ: This means "drink" or "consume," often used in the context of consuming offerings or blessings from the divine.
  • dāśuṣo: This word refers to "the offerings" or "the gifts," emphasizing the importance of giving and sharing in the context of rituals.
  • gṛhe: This translates to "in the home," indicating the significance of domestic life and the importance of rituals within the household.

The mantra can be understood as a call to the divine forces of Indra and Vayu to bless the household with their presence and to partake in the offerings made with joy and enthusiasm. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring the divine in our daily lives, particularly within the sanctity of our homes.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during family gatherings, rituals, or celebrations where the focus is on unity, joy, and the sharing of blessings. For instance, during a festival like Diwali, families perform rituals to invite divine blessings into their homes, echoing the sentiments expressed in this mantra.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine and the domestic sphere. It encourages individuals to celebrate life, acknowledge the forces that sustain them, and create a joyful atmosphere in their homes. By invoking the blessings of the gods, one can foster a sense of gratitude and harmony within the family, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from daily rituals to significant life events.
