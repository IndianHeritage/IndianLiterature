ऊर्ध्व ऊ शु णो अध्वरस्य होतर अग्नि तिष्ठ देवताता यजीयान् |त्वं हि विश्वम् अभ्य असि मनमा प्र वेधसश् चित तिरसि मनिषाम् ||
ūrdhva ū ṣu ṇo adhvarasya hotar agne tiṣṭha devatātā yajīyān | tvaṁ hi viśvam abhy asi manma pra vedhasaś cit tirasi manīṣām ||
  • ūrdhva ū: This phrase can be interpreted as "upward" or "higher." It signifies the aspiration to rise above the mundane and reach a higher state of consciousness or existence.
  • ṣu ṇo adhvarasya: "Adhvara" refers to a ritual or sacrifice. This part of the mantra suggests the importance of purity and intention in rituals, emphasizing that the offerings made should be sincere and meaningful.
  • hotar: This term refers to the priest or the one who performs the ritual. It highlights the role of the individual who conducts the ceremonies, indicating that their mindset and purity are crucial for the success of the ritual.
  • agne tiṣṭha: "Agni" means fire, which is a central element in Vedic rituals. "Tiṣṭha" means "to stand" or "to be present." This phrase calls upon Agni to be present and active in the ritual, symbolizing the divine energy that transforms offerings into blessings.
  • devatātā yajīyān: This refers to the divine essence of the offerings. It emphasizes that the offerings should be made with devotion and respect towards the deities, acknowledging their presence and power.
  • tvaṁ hi viśvam abhy asi: This translates to "You are indeed the universe." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine, suggesting that the divine essence permeates everything in existence.
  • manma pra vedhasaś: "Manma" refers to the mind or consciousness. This part of the mantra indicates that the divine should guide our thoughts and intentions, leading us towards wisdom and understanding.
  • cit tirasi manīṣām: "Cit" means consciousness or awareness, while "manīṣām" refers to intellect or wisdom. This phrase calls for the divine to illuminate our intellect and guide our understanding, helping us to discern truth from illusion.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of spiritual practice and the importance of intention in rituals. It emphasizes the need for purity of mind and heart when engaging in spiritual activities. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal meditation practices, community rituals, or even in daily decision-making. By invoking higher consciousness and divine guidance, individuals can strive to align their actions with their higher purpose, fostering a sense of unity with the universe and promoting a deeper understanding of their place within it. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for mindfulness, devotion, and the pursuit of wisdom, which are essential for personal growth and spiritual development.
