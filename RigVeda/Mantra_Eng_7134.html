आ याहि कृणवाम त इन्द्र ब्रह्माणि वर्धना | येभिः शविष्ट चाकनो भद्रम इह श्रवस्यते भद्राः इन्द्रस्य रातयः ||
ā yāhi kṛṇavāma ta indra brahmāṇi vardhanā | yebhiḥ śaviṣṭha cākano bhadram iha śravasyate bhadrā indrasya rātayaḥ ||
  • Transliteration: ā yāhi kṛṇavāma ta indra brahmāṇi vardhanā
  • Meaning: "Come, we invoke you, O Indra, who increases the strength of the Brahman."
  • Explanation: This line is a call to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, to come forth and bless the devotees with strength and prosperity. The term "Brahman" refers to the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit in Hindu philosophy, indicating that the strength being sought is not just physical but also spiritual and universal.
  • Real-life Example: In a modern context, this can be likened to individuals seeking motivation and strength in their personal or professional lives. For instance, a student preparing for exams may invoke the spirit of determination and focus, akin to calling upon Indra for strength.
  • Transliteration: yebhiḥ śaviṣṭha cākano bhadram iha śravasyate bhadrā indrasya rātayaḥ
  • Meaning: "By whom the best of the strong are made to prosper; may good fortune here be praised, the good fortunes of Indra's chariots."
  • Explanation: This part emphasizes the blessings that come from the divine, particularly through Indra's chariots, which symbolize power and success. The "best of the strong" refers to those who are virtuous and righteous, suggesting that true strength comes from good deeds and intentions.
  • Real-life Example: In everyday life, this can be seen in the success of leaders or individuals who act with integrity and purpose. For example, a community leader who works tirelessly for the welfare of others may be seen as receiving blessings akin to those of Indra, leading to the prosperity of their initiatives.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful invocation for strength, prosperity, and divine blessings. It highlights the importance of seeking spiritual and moral strength in our endeavors. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as during challenging times when individuals or communities seek guidance and support. By invoking the qualities of Indra, one can aspire to achieve success through righteousness and strength, ultimately leading to a harmonious and prosperous life.
