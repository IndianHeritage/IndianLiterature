क्षेत्रस्य पते मधुमन्तमूर्मिं धेनुरिव पयो अस्मासु धुक्ष्वा |मधुश्चुतं घृतमिव सुपूतमृतस्य नः पतयो मृळयन्तु ||
kṣetrasya pate madhumantam ūrmiṁ dhenur iva payo asmāsu dhukṣva | madhuścutaṁ ghṛtam iva supūtam ṛtasya naḥ patayo mṛḻayantu ||
  • kṣetrasya pate: This phrase translates to "O Lord of the field." In this context, "field" refers to the physical and spiritual realms where life unfolds. It signifies the divine presence that governs and nurtures all aspects of existence.
  • madhumantam: This means "sweet" or "honeyed." It symbolizes the essence of life that is nourishing and fulfilling, akin to the sweetness found in honey, which is often associated with prosperity and abundance.
  • ūrmiṁ: This word means "wave" or "current." It represents the dynamic and ever-changing nature of life, akin to the waves of the ocean that ebb and flow, symbolizing the cycles of existence.
  • dhenur iva payo: This translates to "like a cow giving milk." Cows are revered in many cultures for their nurturing qualities, providing sustenance and nourishment. This phrase emphasizes the importance of nurturing and providing for others.
  • asmāsu dhukṣva: This means "may you pour forth upon us." It is a plea for blessings and abundance, inviting the divine to shower its grace upon the devotees.
  • madhuścutaṁ: This translates to "the sweet essence." It signifies the ultimate goal of life, which is to attain joy and fulfillment, akin to the sweetness of honey.
  • ghṛtam iva supūtam: This means "like well-prepared ghee." Ghee is a symbol of purity and is often used in rituals. This phrase emphasizes the importance of purity and quality in our actions and offerings.
  • ṛtasya naḥ patayo: This translates to "O Lord of Truth." It signifies the pursuit of righteousness and truth in life, urging individuals to align their actions with moral and ethical principles.
  • mṛḻayantu: This means "may they bless." It is a call for divine blessings and guidance in our endeavors, seeking support from the higher powers.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for nourishment, abundance, and truth in life. It emphasizes the importance of nurturing relationships, both with the divine and with others, and encourages individuals to pursue righteousness. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, prayers for prosperity, or moments of reflection on one's actions and their alignment with truth. By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of gratitude and awareness of the interconnectedness of life, fostering a harmonious existence.
