अद्रिभिः सुतो मतिभिः चनोहितः प्ररोचयन rodasī मातराऽ शुचिः |रोमाण्य अव्यां समया विघावति मधोः धाराऽ पिन्वमानā dive-dive ||
adribhiḥ suto matibhiś canohitaḥ prarocayan rodasī mātarā śuciḥ | romāṇy avyā samayā vi dhāvati madhor dhārā pinvamānā dive-dive ||
  • adribhiḥ: This word translates to "from the mountains" or "by the mountains." In a real-life context, mountains can symbolize stability, strength, and the natural world that provides resources and inspiration.
  • suto: Meaning "son" or "offspring," this term emphasizes the importance of lineage and heritage. In modern life, this can relate to family values and the transmission of knowledge and culture from one generation to the next.
  • matibhiś: This translates to "with thoughts" or "with intellect." It highlights the significance of wisdom and understanding in decision-making. For example, in business, making informed choices based on careful consideration can lead to success.
  • canohitaḥ: Meaning "well-wisher" or "benefactor," this term reflects the idea of support and goodwill. In everyday life, having mentors or friends who genuinely wish for our success can be invaluable.
  • prarocayan: This means "illuminating" or "enlightening." It suggests the act of bringing clarity or understanding to a situation. For instance, educators illuminate the path of knowledge for their students.
  • rodasī: This word refers to "the two worlds" or "the sky and the earth." It symbolizes the connection between the spiritual and the material realms, reminding us of the balance needed in life.
  • mātarā: Meaning "mothers," this term signifies nurturing and care. In real life, mothers or maternal figures play a crucial role in shaping individuals and communities through love and support.
  • śuciḥ: This translates to "pure" or "sacred." It emphasizes the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions. For example, practicing mindfulness can lead to a more fulfilling life.
  • romāṇy: Meaning "hairs" or "fibers," this term can symbolize the interconnectedness of life. Just as hair connects to the body, our relationships and experiences connect us to the world.
  • avyā: This translates to "unwavering" or "constant." It reflects the idea of steadfastness in our beliefs and actions, which is essential for personal growth and resilience.
  • samayā: Meaning "time," this term highlights the importance of timing in our actions. In real life, recognizing the right moment to act can lead to success in various endeavors.
  • vi dhāvati: This means "runs" or "flows." It can symbolize the dynamic nature of life and the need to adapt to changing circumstances, much like a river flows around obstacles.
  • madhor: Meaning "of the sweet," this term can represent the joys and pleasures of life. It reminds us to appreciate the sweetness in our experiences.
  • dhārā: This translates to "stream" or "flow." It signifies continuity and the importance of nurturing our passions and pursuits over time.
  • pinvamānā: Meaning "ripening" or "maturing," this term reflects the process of growth and development. In life, we all go through stages of maturation, learning from experiences.
  • dive-dive: This translates to "day by day," emphasizing the importance of daily efforts and consistency in achieving our goals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of nature, family, wisdom, and purity. It serves as a reminder to seek enlightenment, nurture relationships, and appreciate the journey of growth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate a balanced life, where they honor their roots, make informed decisions, and strive for personal and communal well-being. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, one can navigate the complexities of life with grace and purpose.
