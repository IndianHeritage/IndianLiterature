ये अग्नि चन्द्र ते गिरः शुम्भन्त्य अश्वराद्हसः | शुष्मेभिः शुष्मिणो नरो दिवश चिद येषाम् बृहत् सुकीर्तिर बोधति तमनः ||
ye agne candra te giraḥ śumbhanty aśvarādhasaḥ | śuṣmebhiḥ śuṣmiṇo naro divaś cid yeṣām bṛhat sukīrtir bodhati tmanā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ye: those who
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • candra: moon
    • te: your
    • giraḥ: words or hymns
    • śumbhanty: praise or glorify
    • aśvarādhasaḥ: those who are below the horses (symbolizing strength and power)
    • śuṣmebhiḥ: with those who are wise
    • śuṣmiṇo: those who are intelligent
    • naro: men or people
    • divaś: of the heavens
    • cid: who
    • yeṣām: whose
    • bṛhat: great
    • sukīrtir: good fame or reputation
    • bodhati: awakens or becomes aware
    • tmanā: with their mind or self
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, and the moon, symbolizing illumination and wisdom.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wise and intelligent individuals who praise and glorify the divine.
    • The phrase "those who are below the horses" can be interpreted as those who are humble yet strong, acknowledging their place in the cosmic order.
    • The mantra suggests that those who possess great fame and reputation do so through their awareness and understanding, which is cultivated through wisdom and devotion.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who recognize and praise the contributions of their team members often foster a culture of respect and collaboration, leading to greater success.
    • In education, teachers who inspire their students to seek knowledge and wisdom create an environment where students thrive and develop a good reputation in their community.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who practice humility and acknowledge the strengths of others often build stronger, more meaningful connections.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom, humility, and the power of praise in achieving greatness and good reputation.
    • It serves as a reminder that true strength lies not just in power but in the ability to uplift others and cultivate awareness.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a positive impact on society.
