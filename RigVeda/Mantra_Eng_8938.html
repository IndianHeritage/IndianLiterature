यत ते कृष्णः शकुन आतुतोद पिपीलः सर्प उता वा श्वापदः |अग्निष टद विश्वाद अगदं कृतोतु सोमश च यो ब्राह्मणां आविवेश ||
yat te kṛṣṇaḥ śakuna ātutoda pipīlaḥ sarpa uta vā śvāpadaḥ | agniṣ ṭad viśvād agadaṁ kṛṇotu somaś ca yo brāhmaṇām̐ āviveśa ||
  • yat te kṛṣṇaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "what is yours, O Krishna." It signifies a connection to the divine and the acknowledgment of Krishna's presence in our lives.
  • śakuna ātutoda: "śakuna" refers to auspicious signs or omens, while "ātutoda" can be interpreted as "that which is beneficial." This suggests that we should be aware of the positive signs around us that guide our actions.
  • pipīlaḥ: This word means "ant." In a broader sense, it can symbolize diligence and hard work, as ants are known for their industrious nature.
  • sarpa uta vā śvāpadaḥ: "sarpa" means "snake," and "śvāpadaḥ" refers to "creatures." This part of the mantra acknowledges the presence of all beings, both beneficial and harmful, in our environment.
  • agniṣ ṭad: "agni" means "fire," which is often associated with purification and transformation. "ṭad" can be interpreted as "that," indicating the transformative power of fire in our lives.
  • viśvād agadaṁ: "viśvād" means "from all," and "agadaṁ" refers to "remedy" or "cure." This suggests that there is a universal remedy available to us, transcending individual circumstances.
  • kṛṇotu: This word means "may it be done" or "let it happen," indicating a desire for positive outcomes in our lives.
  • somaś ca yo brāhmaṇām̐ āviveśa: "soma" refers to the sacred drink that grants immortality, and "brāhmaṇām̐" refers to the learned or wise individuals. This part emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in achieving a higher state of being.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking guidance from the divine (Krishna) while being aware of the signs and symbols in our lives. It encourages diligence (like the ant) and acknowledges the presence of both beneficial and harmful forces. The transformative power of fire signifies the potential for change and growth, while the universal remedy suggests that wisdom and knowledge can lead us to a better understanding of our circumstances. In real life, this mantra can be applied in decision-making processes, encouraging individuals to remain observant, work hard, and seek wisdom in their actions. It serves as a reminder that with the right mindset and awareness, we can navigate life's challenges and achieve positive outcomes.
