आ नो मखस्य दावने 'श्वैर हिरण्यपाणिभिः | देवासा उप गन्तना ||
ā no makhasya dāvane 'śvair hiraṇyapāṇibhiḥ | devāsa upa gantana ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: towards
    • no: us
    • makhasya: of the sacrifice
    • dāvane: in the fire
    • śvair: with our own
    • hiraṇyapāṇibhiḥ: with golden hands
    • devāsa: the gods
    • upa: near
    • gantana: to come
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine forces, inviting them to come closer to us.
    • The phrase "ā no makhasya dāvane" suggests a desire for the blessings that arise from our sacrifices, particularly those performed with sincerity and devotion.
    • The term "hiraṇyapāṇibhiḥ" refers to the gods who are depicted as having golden hands, symbolizing their ability to bestow wealth, prosperity, and blessings upon us.
    • Overall, the mantra emphasizes the importance of our actions (sacrifices) and the divine response to those actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be related to the idea of hard work and dedication leading to success. Just as sacrifices in the Vedic tradition were made to please the gods, individuals today must put in effort and dedication to achieve their goals.
    • For instance, a student who studies diligently for exams is making a sacrifice of time and leisure, hoping to receive the 'blessings' of good grades and future opportunities.
    • Similarly, in a business context, entrepreneurs often invest significant resources and effort into their ventures, hoping that their sacrifices will lead to prosperity and success.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the reciprocal relationship between humans and the divine. It teaches us that our efforts and sacrifices are acknowledged and rewarded by higher powers.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages individuals to engage in meaningful actions, whether in personal, academic, or professional spheres, with the understanding that such efforts can lead to positive outcomes.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a motivational reminder that our dedication and hard work can attract blessings and support from the universe.
