एता उ त्या उषसः केतुम अकृत पूर्वे अर्धे रजसो भानुम अञ्जते |निष्कृत्वाना आयुधानीव धृष्णवः प्रति गावो 'रुषीर यन्ति मातरः ||
etā u tyā uṣasaḥ ketum akrata pūrve ardhe rajaso bhānum añjate | niṣkṛṇvānā āyudhānīva dhṛṣṇavaḥ prati gāvo 'ruṣīr yanti mātaraḥ ||
  • etā u tyā uṣasaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "These and those, the dawns." It signifies the duality of dawns, representing both the physical and metaphorical beginnings in life.
  • ketum akrata: This means "having made a connection." It suggests the importance of establishing links or relationships, whether with nature, people, or the divine.
  • pūrve ardhe rajaso bhānum añjate: This translates to "the sun, the light of the dawn, is invoked in the earlier part of the day." It emphasizes the significance of the sun and light as symbols of knowledge, clarity, and enlightenment.
  • niṣkṛṇvānā āyudhānīva: This phrase means "like those who wield weapons." It implies strength and readiness to face challenges, akin to warriors preparing for battle.
  • dhṛṣṇavaḥ prati gāvo 'ruṣīr yanti mātaraḥ: This translates to "the mothers, like the cows, go forth without anger." It highlights the nurturing aspect of motherhood and the importance of compassion and care in society.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of beginnings, relationships, strength, and nurturing. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Embracing new opportunities at the start of a day or a new phase in life, much like the dawn symbolizes new beginnings.
  • Building connections with others, whether in personal relationships or professional networks, to foster growth and understanding.
  • Drawing strength from challenges, akin to a warrior preparing for battle, which can be relevant in facing life's adversities.
  • Recognizing the importance of nurturing and compassion in our interactions, reflecting the qualities of motherhood and care in our communities.

This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of beginnings, and the strength found in compassion and relationships.
