ऋजीत्य एनī रुशती महित्वा परि ज्रयांसी भारते राजांसी | अदब्धा सिंधुर अपसाम अपस्तमाश्वा न चित्रा वपुषीव दर्षता ||
ṛjīty enī ruśatī mahitvā pari jrayāṁsi bharate rajāṁsi | adabdhā sindhur apasām apastamāśvā na citrā vapuṣīva darśatā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ṛjīty: To be righteous or to be in harmony.
    • enī: This refers to the divine or celestial beings.
    • ruśatī: To shine or to be radiant.
    • mahitvā: Greatness or magnificence.
    • pari: All around or in every direction.
    • jrayāṁsi: To be strong or powerful.
    • bharate: To bear or to carry.
    • rajāṁsi: Kings or rulers.
    • adabdhā: Unharmed or unbroken.
    • sindhur: River or ocean.
    • apasām: Of the waters or of the flow.
    • apastamāśvā: The swift horses or the powerful forces.
    • na: Not.
    • citrā: Colorful or diverse.
    • vapuṣīva: Like a form or body.
    • darśatā: To show or to manifest.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the harmony and radiance of divine beings, emphasizing the importance of righteousness and strength.
    • The mention of "kings" suggests leadership and governance, highlighting the qualities that make a ruler great.
    • The imagery of rivers and horses symbolizes the flow of life and the power of movement, indicating that true strength lies in adaptability and resilience.
    • The phrase "not colorful" suggests a focus on the essence rather than superficial appearances, urging individuals to look beyond the surface.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, a great ruler embodies righteousness and strength, much like Mahatma Gandhi, who led with non-violence and integrity.
    • In nature, rivers symbolize the flow of life, teaching us to adapt and navigate through challenges, similar to how the Ganges flows through diverse terrains.
    • In personal development, focusing on inner qualities rather than external appearances can lead to a more fulfilling life, akin to the philosophy of minimalism.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of righteousness, strength, and the essence of true leadership.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to effective governance, reminding us to prioritize inner qualities over superficial attributes.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to strive for greatness through harmony and resilience, fostering a deeper understanding of their roles in society.
