इमा अग्ने मतयस् तुभ्यं जाताऽ गोभिर अश्वैर अभि गृणन्ति राधः |यदा ते मर्तो अनु भोगम् आनड वसो दधानो मतिभिः सुजात ||
imā agne matayas tubhyaṁ jātā gobhir aśvair abhi gṛṇanti rādhaḥ | yadā te marto anu bhogam ānaḍ vaso dadhāno matibhiḥ sujāta ||
  • imā agne matayas tubhyaṁ jātā: This phrase translates to "These thoughts have been born for you, O Agni." Here, Agni represents the divine fire and is often seen as a mediator between humans and the gods. In real life, this can be likened to the way we channel our thoughts and intentions towards a higher purpose or goal.
  • gobhir aśvair abhi gṛṇanti rādhaḥ: This means "The wise ones sing praises to you with their thoughts and actions." This highlights the importance of expressing gratitude and reverence through our deeds. For example, in a community, when people come together to support a cause, they are essentially singing praises to the greater good.
  • yadā te marto anu bhogam ānaḍ: This translates to "When a mortal enjoys the benefits of your presence." It emphasizes the blessings and benefits that come from divine connection. In everyday life, this can be seen when individuals feel a sense of peace or fulfillment after engaging in spiritual practices or community service.
  • vaso dadhāno matibhiḥ sujāta: This means "Those who are endowed with good thoughts and intentions." It suggests that having a positive mindset and good intentions leads to a fruitful life. For instance, in a workplace, employees who maintain a positive attitude often contribute to a more harmonious and productive environment.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of channeling our thoughts and actions towards a higher purpose, expressing gratitude, and maintaining a positive mindset. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community service, workplace dynamics, and personal spiritual practices. By embodying these principles, individuals can foster a sense of connection with the divine and enhance their overall well-being.
