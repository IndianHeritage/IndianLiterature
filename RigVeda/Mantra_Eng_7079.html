युवं देवाः क्रतुनā पूर्व्येण युक्ता रथेन तविषं यजत्रा |आगच्छतं नासत्याः शचीभिर इदं तृतीयं सवनं पिबाथः ||
yuvaṁ devā kratunā pūrvyeṇa yuktā rathena taviṣaṁ yajatrā | āgacchataṁ nāsatyā śacībhir idaṁ tṛtīyaṁ savanam pibāthaḥ ||
  • yuvaṁ devā: This phrase translates to "young gods" or "youthful deities." It signifies the energy, vitality, and potential of youth. In real life, this can be related to the enthusiasm and innovative spirit found in young people who are often the driving force behind change and progress in society.
  • kratunā pūrvyeṇa: This means "by the power of intention or resolve." It emphasizes the importance of determination and willpower in achieving goals. For example, a student who sets a clear intention to excel in their studies and works diligently towards that goal embodies this principle.
  • yuktā rathena: This translates to "united with the chariot." The chariot symbolizes a vehicle of progress or a means to achieve one's goals. In a modern context, this could represent the tools and resources we use, such as technology or education, to move forward in life.
  • taviṣaṁ yajatrā: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who performs the sacrifice with strength." It highlights the idea of making sacrifices for a greater good. In everyday life, this could refer to parents who work hard to provide for their children or individuals who volunteer their time for community service.
  • āgacchataṁ nāsatyā: This means "come here, O Nāsatyas." Nāsatyas are twin deities associated with healing and truth. This invocation suggests a call for assistance and guidance from divine forces. In real life, this can be seen in how people seek help from mentors, spiritual leaders, or supportive communities during challenging times.
  • śacībhir idaṁ: This translates to "with the powers of the wise." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in our endeavors. For instance, a business leader who relies on data and insights to make informed decisions exemplifies this principle.
  • tṛtīyaṁ savanam pibāthaḥ: This means "drink the third offering." The "third offering" can symbolize the culmination of efforts and the rewards that come from hard work and dedication. In practical terms, this could refer to the success one achieves after years of perseverance and commitment to their goals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of youthful energy, determination, and the importance of seeking guidance and wisdom in our pursuits. It serves as a reminder that success often requires sacrifice and the right tools, and that we should remain open to divine assistance and the support of our communities. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to harness their youthful vigor, set clear intentions, and work diligently towards their goals while remaining connected to their roots and seeking wisdom along the way.
