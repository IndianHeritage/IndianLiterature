यā दसरā सिन्धुमातरā मनोतरā रयीणाम् |धियā देवā वसुविदā ||
yā dasrā sindhumātarā manotarā rayīṇām | dhiyā devā vasuvidā ||
  • yā dasrā: This phrase can be interpreted as "who is the giver" or "who provides." It signifies a source of abundance and nourishment.
  • sindhumātarā: This term translates to "mother of the rivers." In a broader sense, it represents the nurturing aspect of nature, akin to how rivers sustain life.
  • manotarā: This word means "the one who fulfills desires." It emphasizes the idea of a divine force that grants wishes and aspirations.
  • rayīṇām: This refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." It highlights the importance of material and spiritual abundance in life.
  • dhiyā: This term means "with intellect" or "with understanding." It suggests that wisdom and discernment are essential in the pursuit of wealth and fulfillment.
  • devā: This translates to "gods" or "divine beings." It indicates the presence of higher powers that oversee and guide human endeavors.
  • vasuvidā: This phrase means "the one who knows the essence of wealth." It implies a deep understanding of what true prosperity entails, beyond mere material possessions.

In summary, this mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the relationship between nature, divine forces, and human aspirations. It emphasizes the importance of seeking abundance and fulfillment through wisdom and understanding. The imagery of rivers as nurturing mothers serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the resources that sustain us.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as personal growth, financial success, and spiritual development.
  • In a practical sense, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom in their pursuits, ensuring that their desires align with a greater understanding of what constitutes true wealth.
  • For example, in business, one might interpret this mantra as a call to create value not just for profit but for the well-being of the community, thereby fostering sustainable growth.
  • In personal life, it serves as a reminder to appreciate the resources we have, like nature and relationships, and to cultivate them wisely.

Ultimately, this mantra encourages a holistic approach to life, where material wealth is balanced with spiritual and intellectual growth, leading to a more fulfilling existence.
