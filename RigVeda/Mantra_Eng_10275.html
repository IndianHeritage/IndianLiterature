उभे यद इन्द्र रोदसी आपप्राथोषा इव |महान्तं त्वा महीनां सम्राजं चर्षणीनां देवी जनित्री अजीजनद भद्रा जनित्री अजीजनत ||
ubhe yad indra rodasī āpaprāthoṣā iva | mahāntaṁ tvā mahīnāṁ samrājaṁ carṣaṇīnāṁ devī janitry ajījanad bhadrā janitry ajījanat ||
  • Translation: "Both, O Indra, the two worlds (heaven and earth) have been created by you, like the two birds that are born from the same nest. The great one, the ruler of the vastness, has been born from the divine mother, the auspicious one, who has given birth to the good."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ubhe: both
    • yad: that
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • rodasī: worlds or realms
    • āpaprāthoṣā: like the two birds
    • iva: like
    • mahāntaṁ: the great one
    • tvā: you
    • mahīnāṁ: of the vastness
    • samrājaṁ: ruler
    • carṣaṇīnāṁ: of the divine mother
    • devī: goddess
    • janitry: mother or creator
    • ajījanad: has given birth
    • bhadrā: auspicious
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra emphasizes the duality of existence, represented by the two worlds—heaven and earth. It suggests that both realms are interconnected and created by a higher power, Indra.
    • The metaphor of two birds signifies the unity and interdependence of life and the cosmos, highlighting that all beings share a common origin.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, symbolizes strength, power, and the ability to govern the vastness of existence.
    • The mention of the divine mother reflects the nurturing aspect of creation, suggesting that life is born from a source of goodness and auspiciousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we can observe the interdependence of ecosystems, where various species coexist and rely on each other for survival, much like the two worlds in the mantra.
    • In human society, the concept of community reflects the idea of unity and shared origins, where individuals support one another, akin to the nurturing aspect of the divine mother.
    • In leadership, the qualities of Indra can be seen in effective leaders who govern with strength and wisdom, ensuring the well-being of their communities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of recognizing our shared origins.
    • It encourages us to appreciate the nurturing aspects of creation and the role of leadership in fostering unity and strength within communities.
    • In a world that often emphasizes division, this mantra calls for a return to understanding and harmony, applicable in personal relationships, community building, and environmental stewardship.
