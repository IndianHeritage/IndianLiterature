एष प्र पूर्वीर अव तस्य चामृषो 'त्यो न योषाम उद अयंस्त भुवणिः |Dakṣam महे पाययते हिरण्ययं रथम आवृत्या हरियोगम् ऋभ्वसम् ||
eṣa pra pūrvīr ava tasya camriṣo 'tyo na yoṣām ud ayaṁsta bhurvaṇiḥ | dakṣam mahe pāyayate hiraṇyayaṁ ratham āvṛtyā hariyogam ṛbhvasam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣa: this
    • pra: before, in front of
    • pūrvīr: of the ancients, the wise
    • ava: down, below
    • tasya: of that
    • camriṣo: of the wise, the learned
    • 'tyo: beyond
    • na: not
    • yoṣām: of women
    • ud: up, above
    • ayaṁsta: this is, this becomes
    • bhurvaṇiḥ: the one who is nourished, the sustainer
    • dakṣam: skilled, capable
    • mahe: great
    • pāyayate: nourishes, sustains
    • hiraṇyayaṁ: golden, wealth
    • ratham: chariot, vehicle
    • āvṛtyā: by covering, enveloping
    • hariyogam: the union of the divine
    • ṛbhvasam: the one who is rich, prosperous
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the wisdom and nourishment provided by the ancients and the learned.
    • It emphasizes the importance of sustaining and nurturing not just material wealth but also spiritual and intellectual growth.
    • The reference to women suggests a recognition of their role in society and the importance of their contributions.
    • The idea of a chariot symbolizes the journey of life, which is guided by wisdom and divine union.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern education, the wisdom of past scholars is crucial for shaping future generations. For instance, teaching history helps students learn from past mistakes.
    • In family structures, the nurturing role of women is vital for the emotional and intellectual development of children, highlighting the importance of their contributions.
    • In business, companies that prioritize ethical practices and social responsibility often find greater success, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on nourishing the community.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of wisdom, nourishment, and the journey of life.
    • It teaches us to value the contributions of the past while recognizing the importance of nurturing future generations.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to a more harmonious and prosperous society, where both material and spiritual wealth are prioritized.
