यस्ते यज्ञेन समिधा य उक्थैर अर्कैभिः सूनो सहसो ददाशत |स मार्त्येष्व अमृत प्रचेताऽ राया द्युम्नेन श्रवसा वि भाति ||
yas te yajñena samidhā ya ukthair arkebhiḥ sūno sahaso dadāśat | sa martyeṣv amṛta pracetā rāyā dyumnena śravasā vi bhāti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • te: you
    • yajñena: through sacrifice
    • samidhā: kindled
    • ya: who
    • ukthair: with hymns
    • arkebhiḥ: with praises
    • sūno: of the sun
    • sahaso: with strength
    • dadāśat: has given
    • sa: he
    • martyeṣv: among mortals
    • amṛta: immortal
    • pracetā: with wisdom
    • rāyā: wealth
    • dyumnena: with glory
    • śravasā: with fame
    • vi: indeed
    • bhāti: shines
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of sacrifice (yajña) and the divine blessings that come from it.
    • The phrase "who through sacrifice has kindled" suggests that through acts of devotion and selflessness, one can invoke divine favor.
    • The reference to "the sun" symbolizes enlightenment, energy, and life, indicating that these blessings are vital for existence.
    • It emphasizes that those who engage in such sacrifices are granted wisdom, wealth, and immortality among mortals.
    • The concluding part highlights that such individuals shine with glory and fame, suggesting that their actions lead to a lasting legacy.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, acts of charity and community service can be seen as forms of yajña. For instance, a community coming together to build a school or provide food for the needy reflects the essence of this mantra.
    • Individuals who dedicate their lives to helping others, such as social workers or volunteers, often find a sense of fulfillment and recognition, embodying the idea of shining with glory and fame.
    • In the corporate world, companies that prioritize corporate social responsibility (CSR) and engage in sustainable practices can be viewed as performing a modern-day yajña, contributing to the welfare of society while also enhancing their reputation.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of selfless actions and sacrifices in achieving a meaningful and prosperous life.
    • It teaches that through dedication to the greater good, individuals can attain wisdom, wealth, and a lasting legacy.
    • In real life, the principles of this mantra can be applied in various contexts, from personal relationships to community engagement and corporate ethics, emphasizing that true success is measured not just by material gain but by the positive impact one has on others.
