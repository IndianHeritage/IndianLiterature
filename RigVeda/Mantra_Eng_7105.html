मन्त्र: शिषानो वृषभो यथाग्निः श्रिङ्गे दविध्वत् | तिग्मा अस्य हनवो न प्रतिधृषे सुजम्भः सहसो यहुः ||
śiśāno vṛṣabho yathāgniḥ śṛṅge davidhvat | tigmā asya hanavo na pratidhṛṣe sujambhaḥ sahaso yahuḥ ||
  • śiśāno vṛṣabho yathāgniḥ: This phrase can be translated as "like the bull (vṛṣabha) that is strong (śiśāno) and powerful, like fire (agni)." Here, the bull symbolizes strength and vitality, while fire represents energy and transformation.
  • śṛṅge davidhvat: This means "with horns (śṛṅge) that are sharp (davidhvat)." The horns of the bull can be seen as a metaphor for protection and assertiveness, indicating the need to be prepared and strong in the face of challenges.
  • tigmā asya hanavo: This translates to "the sharp (tigmā) weapons (hanavo) of this." It suggests the idea of being equipped with the right tools or skills to overcome obstacles, much like a warrior preparing for battle.
  • na pratidhṛṣe sujambhaḥ: This means "do not be deterred (na pratidhṛṣe) by the powerful (sujambhaḥ)." It emphasizes the importance of resilience and courage, encouraging individuals to face difficulties head-on without fear.
  • sahaso yahuḥ: This can be interpreted as "with strength (sahaso), they (yahuḥ) will prevail." It reinforces the idea that with determination and strength, one can achieve success and overcome challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, resilience, and preparedness in life. It serves as a reminder that, like the bull and fire, we must harness our inner strength and be equipped to face challenges. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, professional challenges, or even in overcoming societal obstacles. By embodying the qualities of strength and courage, individuals can navigate through life's difficulties and emerge victorious.
