सोमापूषणā रजसो विमानं सप्तचक्रं रथम अविश्वमिन्वम् |विषुवृतं मनसा युज्यमानं तं जिन्वथो वृषणा पंचरश्मिम् ||
somāpūṣaṇā rajaso vimānaṁ saptacakraṁ ratham aviśvaminvam | viṣūvṛtam manasā yujyamānaṁ taṁ jinvatho vṛṣaṇā pañcaraśmim ||
  • Mantra: "somāpūṣaṇā rajaso vimānaṁ saptacakraṁ ratham aviśvaminvam | viṣūvṛtam manasā yujyamānaṁ taṁ jinvatho vṛṣaṇā pañcaraśmim ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • somāpūṣaṇā: Referring to the nourishing aspect of Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic rituals.
    • rajaso: Related to the quality of passion or activity.
    • vimānaṁ: A vehicle or chariot, often symbolizing a means of transport or progress.
    • saptacakraṁ: The seven chakras, representing energy centers in the body.
    • ratham: Chariot, symbolizing movement and journey.
    • aviśvaminvam: Invoking the universal or all-encompassing aspect.
    • viṣūvṛtam: Surrounded or enveloped by the universe.
    • manasā: With the mind or through mental focus.
    • yujyamānaṁ: Being united or connected.
    • taṁ: That (referring to the divine or universal energy).
    • jinvatho: Invoking or calling upon.
    • vṛṣaṇā: The powerful or strong aspect.
    • pañcaraśmim: The five rays or aspects, possibly referring to the five elements or senses.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the nourishing qualities of Soma, which can be likened to the importance of nourishment in our daily lives, both physically and spiritually.
    • The reference to "rajaso" highlights the dynamic and passionate aspects of life, reminding us to engage actively with our surroundings.
    • The "vimānaṁ" and "ratham" symbolize our journey through life, suggesting that we must navigate our paths with intention and purpose.
    • The "saptacakraṁ" emphasizes the significance of energy centers within us, akin to how we must maintain balance in our physical, emotional, and spiritual well-being.
    • "aviśvaminvam" and "viṣūvṛtam" remind us of our connection to the universe, encouraging us to recognize our place within the larger cosmos.
    • The phrase "manasā yujyamānaṁ" suggests the importance of mental focus and intention in achieving our goals, similar to how athletes visualize success before a competition.
    • Finally, "pañcaraśmim" can be interpreted as a call to harness the five elements or senses, which is essential in practices like yoga and meditation for holistic well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of nourishment, and the need for balance in our physical and spiritual journeys.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in practices such as meditation, yoga, and personal development, where individuals seek to align their energies and intentions with their goals.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to embrace our passions, focus our minds, and recognize our place in the universe, leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
