शुनं हुवेम मगवाणं इन्द्रं अस्मिन भरे नृतमं वाजसातौ | श्रिण्वन्तं उग्रं ऊतये समत्सु घ्नन्तं वृत्राणि संजितं धनानाम् ||
śunaṁ huvema maghavānam indram asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau | śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi saṁjitaṁ dhanānām ||
  • śunaṁ huvema: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke the powerful." The term "śunaṁ" refers to something that is auspicious or beneficial, while "huvema" means to invoke or call upon. In real life, this can be seen as a call to seek help or guidance from a higher power or a source of strength when facing challenges.
  • maghavānam indram: Here, "maghavānam" refers to the mighty or powerful, and "indram" is a reference to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition. This signifies the importance of seeking the support of powerful forces or leaders in our endeavors. For example, in a corporate setting, one might seek the guidance of a mentor or a leader to navigate complex situations.
  • asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau: This translates to "in this burden, we seek the best." "Asmin bhare" refers to the burdens or challenges we face, while "nṛtamaṁ" means the best or the most excellent. "Vājasātau" can be interpreted as the source of strength or energy. This part of the mantra emphasizes the importance of striving for excellence and seeking the best solutions in difficult times, akin to a student aiming for top grades despite challenges.
  • śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye: "śṛṇvantam" means "listening" or "hearing," while "ugram" refers to something fierce or powerful. "Ūtaye" means for the purpose of upliftment or elevation. This suggests that by listening to powerful teachings or guidance, one can elevate themselves. In real life, this can be related to attending motivational talks or reading inspiring literature that encourages personal growth.
  • samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi: "samatsu" means "in the assembly" or "among the people," while "ghnantaṁ" refers to "destroying" or "overcoming." "Vṛtrāṇi" refers to obstacles or enemies. This part of the mantra highlights the collective effort to overcome challenges together. For instance, in a community project, individuals come together to tackle social issues, symbolizing unity in overcoming obstacles.
  • saṁjitaṁ dhanānām: "saṁjitaṁ" means "gathered" or "assembled," and "dhanānām" refers to wealth or resources. This signifies the importance of gathering resources and support to achieve common goals. In a business context, this could relate to pooling resources for a startup or a community initiative aimed at improving local infrastructure.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of invoking powerful forces, seeking excellence in the face of challenges, listening to uplifting teachings, and working collectively to overcome obstacles. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, community building, and professional growth. By embodying these values, individuals and groups can navigate difficulties more effectively and achieve their goals with strength and unity.
