भूरि त इन्द्र वीर्यं तव स्मस्य अस्य स्तोतुर मघवन कामम् आ पृण |अनु ते द्यौः बृहती वीर्यम मम इयं च ते पृथिवी नेमा ओजसे ||
bhūri ta indra vīrya1ṁ tava smasy asya stotur maghavan kāmam ā pṛṇa | anu te dyaur bṛhatī vīryam mama iyaṁ ca te pṛthivī nema ojase ||
  • bhūri ta indra vīryaṁ: This phrase translates to "O Indra, grant us abundant strength." Here, Indra is the king of the gods and represents power and might. In real life, this can be seen as a plea for strength in overcoming challenges, much like athletes seeking strength and resilience in their training.
  • tava smasy asya stotur maghavan kāmam ā pṛṇa: This means "May we be fulfilled with your blessings, O Maghavan." Maghavan is another name for Indra, emphasizing his role as a benefactor. In everyday life, this can relate to seeking support from mentors or leaders who can provide guidance and resources to achieve our goals.
  • anu te dyaur bṛhatī vīryam mama: This translates to "May the vast sky grant me strength." The sky symbolizes limitless potential and opportunities. In practical terms, this can be likened to the vast possibilities in life, encouraging individuals to reach for their dreams and aspirations, much like entrepreneurs who look to the sky as the limit for their ventures.
  • iyaṁ ca te pṛthivī nema ojase: This means "And this earth may also provide me with energy." The earth represents stability and grounding. In real life, this can be interpreted as the importance of having a solid foundation, whether it be in relationships, finances, or personal well-being, to harness energy and drive towards success.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of seeking strength, support, and resources from both divine and earthly sources. It highlights the interconnectedness of aspiration and the need for a solid foundation in achieving one's goals. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, teamwork, and community building, where individuals seek to harness their inner strength while also relying on the support of others and the environment around them. The mantra serves as a reminder that true power comes from a combination of personal effort and external support, encouraging a holistic approach to success.
