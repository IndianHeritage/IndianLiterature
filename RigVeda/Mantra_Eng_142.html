ये यजत्रा य ईड्याः ते ते पिबन्तु जिव्हया |मधोर अग्नि वषट्कृतिः ||
ye yajatrā ya īḍyās te te pibantu jihvayā | madhor agne vaṣaṭkṛti ||
  • Translation: "Those who perform the sacrifice, may they drink (the offerings) through the tongue; O Agni, the sweet one, we call upon you."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: Those who
    • yajatrā: Perform the sacrifice
    • ya: Who
    • īḍyās: Are worthy of worship
    • te: May they
    • pibantu: Drink
    • jihvayā: Through the tongue
    • madhor: Of the sweet
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • vaṣaṭkṛti: We call upon you
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of sacrifice (yajna) in Vedic culture, where offerings are made to deities, particularly Agni, the fire god.
    • The act of drinking through the tongue symbolizes the acceptance and enjoyment of the offerings made to the divine.
    • Agni represents the divine intermediary between humans and gods, facilitating communication and the transfer of offerings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern Hindu rituals, offerings of food and flowers are made to deities, symbolizing gratitude and devotion. The act of consuming prasad (blessed food) can be seen as a way of 'drinking' the divine blessings.
    • In community gatherings, such as weddings or festivals, the act of sharing food after a ritual can be likened to the collective 'drinking' of blessings, fostering unity and connection among participants.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine, emphasizing the role of Agni as a facilitator of these connections.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder of the importance of gratitude, community, and the sacredness of shared experiences.
    • By engaging in acts of devotion and sacrifice, individuals can cultivate a sense of purpose and connection to something greater than themselves.
