प्र यद वहध्वे मरुतः पराकाद यूयम् महः संवरणस्य वस्वः | विदानासो वसवो राध्यस्याराच चिद द्वेषः सन्तर युयोत ||
pra yad vahadhve marutaḥ parākād yūyam mahaḥ saṁvaraṇasya vasvaḥ | vidānāso vasavo rādhyasyārāc cid dveṣaḥ sanutar yuyota ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O Maruts, who are powerful and mighty, may you protect us from all adversities. The Vasus, who are knowledgeable, may they help us in overcoming our enemies and obstacles."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Pra: A prefix meaning "forth" or "outward," indicating a call or invocation.
    • Yad: Meaning "that" or "which," referring to the qualities or actions of the Maruts.
    • Vahadhve: Derived from "vah," meaning "to carry" or "to bear," suggesting the Maruts' role in carrying forth strength and protection.
    • Marutaḥ: Refers to the Maruts, the storm deities associated with wind and storms, symbolizing power and energy.
    • Parākād: Meaning "from all sides" or "in every direction," indicating the comprehensive nature of protection sought.
    • Yūyam: Meaning "you" (plural), addressing the Maruts collectively.
    • Mahaḥ: Meaning "great" or "mighty," emphasizing the strength of the Maruts.
    • Saṁvaraṇasya: Meaning "of protection" or "of defense," indicating the purpose of the invocation.
    • Vasvaḥ: Meaning "all" or "everything," suggesting a holistic approach to protection.
    • Vidānāso: Referring to the Vasus, who are deities of abundance and prosperity, known for their wisdom.
    • Rādhyasyārāc: Meaning "to overcome" or "to conquer," indicating the desire to triumph over challenges.
    • Cid: Meaning "that which is," referring to the essence of existence or consciousness.
    • Dveṣaḥ: Meaning "hatred" or "enmity," referring to the negative forces or adversaries.
    • Sanutar: Meaning "quickly" or "swiftly," indicating the urgency of the request for protection.
    • Yuyota: Meaning "may you fight" or "may you engage," calling upon the deities to take action against adversities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, such as facing a health issue or financial difficulty, individuals may invoke the protective qualities of the Maruts, seeking strength and support to overcome these challenges.
    • In a community setting, during natural disasters like storms or floods, people may come together to pray for the protection of their homes and families, embodying the essence of this mantra.
    • In competitive environments, such as sports or business, individuals may seek the wisdom and guidance of the Vasus to navigate challenges and emerge victorious.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine protection and strength against adversities. It emphasizes the importance of community, wisdom, and the collective power of deities in overcoming challenges. In real-life scenarios, whether facing personal struggles, natural calamities, or competitive situations, this mantra serves as a reminder of the support available from higher powers and the strength that can be drawn from unity and wisdom. It encourages individuals to seek help and to be proactive in overcoming obstacles, fostering resilience and hope in the face of difficulties.
