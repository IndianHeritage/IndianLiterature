युयं धूर्षु प्रयुजो न रश्मिभिर ज्योतिṣ्मन्तो न भासा व्युष्टिषु |श्येनासो न स्वयशसो ऋशादसः प्रवासो न प्रसितासः परिप्रुषः ||
yūyaṁ dhūrṣu prayujo na raśmibhir jyotiṣmanto na bhāsā vyuṣṭiṣu | śyenāso na svayaśaso riśādasaḥ pravāso na prasitāsaḥ paripruṣaḥ ||
  • yūyaṁ - This word translates to "you" or "your," indicating a collective address to a group.
  • dhūrṣu - This means "in the midst of," suggesting a state of being surrounded or engaged in something.
  • prayujo - This can be interpreted as "being engaged" or "being involved," indicating active participation.
  • na - This is a negation, meaning "not."
  • raśmibhir - This refers to "rays" or "light," often symbolizing knowledge or enlightenment.
  • jyotiṣmanto - This means "those who possess light" or "illuminated beings," referring to wise or enlightened individuals.
  • na bhāsā - This translates to "not by light," indicating a lack of clarity or understanding.
  • vyuṣṭiṣu - This word means "in the dawn" or "in the early light," symbolizing new beginnings or opportunities.
  • śyenāso - This refers to "eagles," which are often symbols of strength, vision, and freedom.
  • na svayaśaso - This means "not by one's own glory," suggesting humility or the idea that true worth is not self-proclaimed.
  • riśādasaḥ - This can be interpreted as "those who are strong" or "powerful beings," indicating a sense of strength and capability.
  • pravāso - This means "wanderers" or "travelers," symbolizing the journey of life.
  • na prasitāsaḥ - This translates to "not by those who are restrained," indicating freedom and the ability to act without limitations.
  • paripruṣaḥ - This means "those who are well-versed" or "knowledgeable," suggesting wisdom and understanding.

The mantra speaks to the collective journey of individuals who are engaged in the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment. It emphasizes the importance of community and shared experiences while also highlighting the need for humility and the recognition that true strength comes from within and is not merely a display of power or glory.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In educational settings, where students learn together, supporting each other in their quest for knowledge.
  • In workplaces, where collaboration and teamwork lead to greater innovation and success.
  • In personal relationships, where mutual respect and understanding foster deeper connections.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, humility, and the pursuit of collective wisdom. It reminds us that while individual journeys are important, the strength of a community and the shared pursuit of enlightenment can lead to greater achievements. In a world that often emphasizes individual success, this mantra encourages us to recognize the value of collaboration and the importance of supporting one another in our endeavors.
