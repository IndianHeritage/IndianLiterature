इदं ते पात्रं सनवित्तम इन्द्र पिबा सोमं एनā शतक्रतो |पूर्ण आहावो मदिरस्य मध्वो यं विश्व इद अभिहर्यन्ति देवाः ||
idaṁ te pātraṁ sanavittam indra pibā somam enā śatakrato | pūrṇa āhāvo madirasya madhvo yaṁ viśva id abhiharyanti devāḥ ||
  • idaṁ te pātraṁ - "This is your vessel": The mantra begins by presenting a vessel, symbolizing readiness to receive blessings or divine grace. In real life, this can be likened to being open and prepared to accept opportunities or wisdom that come our way.
  • sanavittam indra - "O Indra, the one who possesses wealth": Indra is often regarded as the king of gods and a deity of rain and thunderstorms. This phrase acknowledges his power and abundance. In a modern context, it can represent leaders or influential figures who have the ability to provide resources or support.
  • pibā somam - "Drink the soma": Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine ecstasy. This can be interpreted as embracing experiences that elevate our consciousness or bring joy, akin to pursuing passions that enrich our lives.
  • enā śatakrato - "With this, O hundred-folded one": "Śatakratu" refers to Indra's hundred powers or capabilities. This line emphasizes the multifaceted nature of divine blessings. In everyday life, it can remind us to recognize and utilize our diverse skills and strengths.
  • pūrṇa āhāvo - "Full of offerings": This suggests a state of abundance and generosity. In practical terms, it encourages us to contribute positively to our communities, whether through time, resources, or kindness.
  • madirasya madhvo - "The essence of intoxication": This phrase refers to the blissful state that comes from divine experiences. It can be related to moments of joy and fulfillment in life, such as achieving personal goals or experiencing love and connection.
  • yaṁ viśva id abhiharyanti devāḥ - "Which all the gods desire": This line indicates that the blessings and experiences sought are universally valued by the divine. In real life, it can be seen as a reminder that our aspirations should align with higher values and the greater good.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings and being open to receiving abundance in various forms. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing our strengths, contributing to the community, and pursuing experiences that elevate our spirit. In real life, it serves as a guiding principle to live with intention, gratitude, and a sense of connection to the divine and to each other.
