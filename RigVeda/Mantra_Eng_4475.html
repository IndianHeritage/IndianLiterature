इमं ऊषु वो अतिथिम उषर्बुधं विश्वासां विश्वाम् पतिम ऋञ्जसे गिरा |वेतीद दिवो जनुषा कच चिद आ शुचिर ज्योक चिद अत्ति गर्भो यद अच्युतम् ||
imam ū ṣu vo atithim uṣarbudhaṁ viśvāsāṁ viśām patim ṛñjase girā | vetīd divo januṣā kac cid ā śucir jyok cid atti garbho yad acyutam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imam: this
    • ū: indeed
    • ṣu: good
    • vo: for you
    • atithim: guest
    • uṣarbudhaṁ: who is awakened
    • viśvāsāṁ: of all beings
    • viśām: of the world
    • patim: lord
    • ṛñjase: to be pleased
    • girā: with speech
    • vetīd: may he know
    • divo: of the heavens
    • januṣā: by birth
    • kac: whether
    • cid: at all
    • ā: pure
    • śucir: clean
    • jyok: light
    • cid: at all
    • atti: he attains
    • garbho: the womb
    • yad: that
    • acyutam: which is imperishable
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of welcoming and honoring guests, symbolizing the universal principle of hospitality.
    • The phrase "atithim uṣarbudhaṁ" emphasizes the idea of a guest who is awakened, suggesting that guests bring new perspectives and insights.
    • It acknowledges the interconnectedness of all beings, as indicated by "viśvāsāṁ viśām patim," highlighting the need for unity and respect among all individuals.
    • The mantra also touches upon purity and the attainment of higher knowledge, as seen in "ā śucir jyok cid," which can be interpreted as the pursuit of enlightenment and truth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, hospitality is a cherished value. For instance, in Indian culture, guests are often treated with great respect, and the saying "Atithi Devo Bhava" (The guest is God) reflects this sentiment.
    • In a workplace setting, welcoming new employees or clients with warmth and openness can foster a positive environment and encourage collaboration.
    • In community gatherings, the act of inviting and honoring diverse voices can lead to richer discussions and a sense of belonging.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of hospitality, respect, and unity among all beings.
    • It encourages individuals to embrace purity of thought and action, leading to personal growth and enlightenment.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can enhance relationships, foster community spirit, and promote a culture of inclusivity and understanding.
