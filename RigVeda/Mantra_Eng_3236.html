एवेन्द्राय वृषभाय वृष्णे ब्रह्माकर्म भृगवो न रथम् |नू चिद यथा नः सख्या वियोषद असन न उग्रो 'वितातनूपाः ||
eved indrāya vṛṣabhāya vṛṣṇe brahmākarma bhṛgavo na ratham | nū cid yathā naḥ sakhyā viyoṣad asan na ugro 'vitā tanūpāḥ ||
  • eved indrāya: This phrase can be translated as "this is for Indra." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, thunder, and war. In real life, this can symbolize the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in our endeavors.
  • vṛṣabhāya vṛṣṇe: "For the bull, for Vṛṣhṇa." Vṛṣhṇa refers to the divine aspect of strength and vitality. The bull is often a symbol of power and fertility. In practical terms, this can relate to the strength we need in our personal and professional lives to overcome challenges.
  • brahmākarma bhṛgavo: "The actions of Brahman, the Bhṛgus." Brahman represents the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit, while the Bhṛgus are a family of sages known for their wisdom. This suggests that our actions should align with higher principles and wisdom. In everyday life, this can be seen in ethical decision-making and striving for a higher purpose.
  • na ratham: "Not the chariot." The chariot symbolizes movement and progress. This phrase may imply that we should not rely solely on material means for our journey. In real life, it can remind us to focus on inner growth rather than just external achievements.
  • nū cid yathā naḥ sakhyā: "Indeed, as our friendship." This emphasizes the importance of relationships and community support. In practical terms, it highlights how collaboration and friendship can lead to success in various aspects of life.
  • viyošad asan: "May we not be separated." This phrase underscores the desire for unity and togetherness. In real life, it can be applied to maintaining strong bonds with family, friends, and colleagues, fostering a sense of belonging.
  • na ugro 'vitā tanūpāḥ: "May we not be fierce, may we not be weak." This line suggests a balance between strength and gentleness. In practical terms, it can be interpreted as the need for resilience without aggression, promoting a harmonious approach to challenges.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of divine guidance, strength, ethical actions, community support, unity, and balance in our lives. Its significance lies in reminding us to seek higher principles in our actions, value our relationships, and maintain a harmonious approach to life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in personal development, professional ethics, and fostering strong community ties, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and balanced life.
