उता नो नक्तम् अपां वृषण्वसू सूर्यामासा सदनाय सधन्या |सच यत साद्य एषाम अहिर बुध्नेषु बुध्न्यः ||
uta no naktam apāṁ vṛṣaṇvasū sūryāmāsā sadanāya sadhanyā | sacā yat sādy eṣām ahir budhneṣu budhnyaḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May we attain the night that is full of strength, the sun that brings prosperity, and the abode of happiness. Whatever is beneficial for us, may it come to us from the depths of the ocean."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: may we attain
    • no: our
    • naktam: night
    • apāṁ: of the waters
    • vṛṣaṇvasū: full of strength
    • sūryāmāsā: the sun that brings prosperity
    • sadanāya: to the abode
    • sadhanyā: of happiness
    • sacā: and whatever
    • yat: that
    • sādy: beneficial
    • eṣām: for us
    • ahir: from the depths
    • budhneṣu: of the ocean
    • budhnyaḥ: may it come
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking strength and prosperity in life.
    • It reflects a desire for a fulfilling and happy existence, symbolized by the night and the sun.
    • The reference to the ocean suggests that valuable resources and blessings can come from deep and unexpected places.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In personal development, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek strength during challenging times, much like how the night can be a time of rest and rejuvenation.
    • In business, it can be a reminder to pursue opportunities that lead to prosperity, akin to how the sun nurtures growth.
    • In relationships, it encourages the pursuit of happiness and fulfillment, suggesting that joy can be found in the depths of our connections with others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, prosperity, and happiness.
    • It encourages individuals to seek out and embrace the blessings that life offers, even from the most unexpected sources.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can cultivate a life filled with purpose and joy.
