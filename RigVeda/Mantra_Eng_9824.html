तवाग्ने होत्रं तव पोत्रं ऋत्वियं तव नेष्ट्रं त्वं अग्निद ऋतायतः |तव प्रशास्त्रं त्वं अध्वरियसि ब्रह्मा चासि गृहपतिश च नो दमे ||
tavāgne hotraṁ tava potram ṛtviyaṁ tava neṣṭraṁ tvam agnid ṛtāyataḥ | tava praśāstraṁ tvam adhvarīyasi brahmā cāsi gṛhapatiś ca no dame ||
  • tavāgne hotraṁ: This phrase refers to the fire (Agni) that is invoked in rituals. In real life, fire symbolizes purification and transformation. For example, in many cultures, lighting a candle or a fire during a ceremony signifies the presence of divine energy.
  • tava potram ṛtviyaṁ: "Potram" signifies the vessel or container, and "ṛtviyaṁ" refers to the truth or righteousness. This can be understood as the idea that our actions should be guided by truth and integrity. In everyday life, this can be seen in ethical business practices where companies strive to be transparent and honest with their customers.
  • tava neṣṭraṁ: This term can be interpreted as the guiding principle or the path one should follow. In a real-life context, this could relate to personal values or moral compasses that guide individuals in making decisions, such as choosing to help others in need.
  • tvam agnid ṛtāyataḥ: Here, "tvam" means "you," and "agnid" refers to the divine fire. "Ṛtāyataḥ" suggests stability or permanence. This can be related to the idea of being a constant source of inspiration and strength in one’s community, much like a leader who remains steadfast in their values during challenging times.
  • tava praśāstraṁ: This means "your command" or "your authority." In practical terms, this can be seen in leadership roles where a leader’s vision and directives shape the actions of their team, such as a coach guiding a sports team to victory.
  • tvam adhvarīyasi: This phrase indicates the act of performing rituals or sacrifices. In modern life, this can be likened to the dedication and effort put into achieving goals, such as a student studying diligently for exams.
  • brahmā cāsi gṛhapatiś ca no dame: "Brahmā" refers to the creator, and "gṛhapatiś" means the lord of the house. This signifies the importance of creation and stewardship in our lives. In a real-life scenario, this can be reflected in how families nurture their homes and communities, fostering growth and harmony.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of fire as a purifying and transformative force, the importance of truth and integrity in our actions, and the role of leadership and guidance in our lives. It teaches us to be steadfast in our values, to lead with authority, and to nurture our communities. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in personal development, ethical practices in business, and community leadership, ultimately guiding us towards a more harmonious and purposeful existence.
