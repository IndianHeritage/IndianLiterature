आ यं पृणन्ति दिवि सद्मबरिषः समुद्रं न सुभ्वः स्वा अभिष्टयः |तं वृत्रहत्ये अनु तस्थुर ऊतयः शुष्मा इन्द्रं अवाताऽहृतत्सवः ||
ā yam pṛṇanti divi sadmabarhiṣaḥ samudraṁ na subhva1ḥ svā abhiṣṭayaḥ | taṁ vṛtrahatye anu tasthur ūtayaḥ śuṣmā indram avātā ahrutapsavaḥ ||
  • Mantra: ā yam pṛṇanti divi sadmabarhiṣaḥ samudraṁ na subhvaḥ svā abhiṣṭayaḥ | taṁ vṛtrahatye anu tasthur ūtayaḥ śuṣmā indram avātā ahrutapsavaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yam: This refers to "that which" or "that which is present."
    • pṛṇanti: Means "they fill" or "they nourish."
    • divi: Translates to "in the heavens" or "in the divine realm."
    • sadmabarhiṣaḥ: Refers to "the abode of the divine" or "the sacred place."
    • samudraṁ: Means "the ocean" or "the vast expanse."
    • na subhvaḥ: Translates to "not to be obstructed" or "not to be hindered."
    • svā: Refers to "the self" or "the divine essence."
    • abhiṣṭayaḥ: Means "the blessings" or "the desired outcomes."
    • taṁ: Refers to "that" or "him."
    • vṛtrahatye: Means "the slayer of Vritra," referring to Indra, the deity who conquers obstacles.
    • anu: Translates to "after" or "following."
    • tasthur: Means "they have come" or "they have approached."
    • ūtayaḥ: Refers to "the powers" or "the energies."
    • śuṣmā: Means "the radiant" or "the brilliant."
    • indram: Refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature.
    • avātā: Means "they have been protected" or "they have been sheltered."
    • ahrutapsavaḥ: Refers to "those who are devoted" or "those who are engaged in spiritual practices."
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine nourishment and blessings that come from the heavens, symbolized by the ocean, which is vast and abundant.
    • The phrase "they fill" suggests that the divine energies are constantly providing sustenance and support to those who seek it.
    • Indra, as the slayer of Vritra, represents the overcoming of obstacles and challenges in life. This can be likened to personal struggles where one seeks strength and guidance to overcome difficulties.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine blessings and the need for protection and guidance in one's spiritual journey.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek spiritual guidance through prayer or meditation, similar to invoking the blessings mentioned in the mantra.
    • Communities coming together to support one another during natural disasters can be seen as a manifestation of the divine energies that "fill" and "nourish" the collective spirit.
    • In professional settings, overcoming challenges and achieving success can be viewed as receiving blessings from a higher power, akin to the protection and guidance sought in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine support and the importance of seeking blessings in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that, like Indra, we can overcome obstacles with the right mindset and spiritual connection.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal struggles or communal efforts, the essence of this mantra encourages individuals to seek and acknowledge the divine forces that aid in their journey.
