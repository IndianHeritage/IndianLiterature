द्यावापृथिवी जनयन्न अभि व्रताप ओषधीर वनिनानि यज्ञियाः |अन्तरिक्षं स्वार अपप्रुर ऊतये वशं देवासस तन्वीं न मामृजुः ||
dyāvāpṛthivī janayann abhi vratāpa oṣadhīr vanināni yajñiyā | antarikṣaṁ sva1r ā paprur ūtaye vaśaṁ devāsas tanvī3 ni māmṛjuḥ ||
  • dyāvāpṛthivī: This term refers to the duality of the sky (dyau) and the earth (pṛthivī). It symbolizes the vastness of the universe and the grounding nature of our planet.
  • janayann: This means "giving birth" or "creating." It signifies the act of creation and the nurturing aspect of nature.
  • abhi vratāpa: This phrase can be interpreted as "with a vow" or "with determination." It emphasizes the importance of commitment and purpose in our actions.
  • oṣadhīr: This refers to "herbs" or "plants," which are essential for sustenance and healing. It highlights the significance of nature in our lives.
  • vanināni: This term means "forests" or "woodlands," representing the natural world and its resources.
  • yajñiyā: This word relates to "sacrifice" or "offering," indicating the importance of giving back to nature and the divine.
  • antarikṣaṁ: This refers to the "intermediate space" or "atmosphere," symbolizing the connection between the earth and the heavens.
  • sva1r: This term means "heaven" or "sky," representing the spiritual realm and higher consciousness.
  • āpāprur: This means "to reach" or "to attain," indicating the pursuit of goals and aspirations.
  • ūtaye: This term signifies "for prosperity" or "for well-being," emphasizing the desire for abundance in life.
  • vaśaṁ: This means "control" or "influence," suggesting the power of intention and will in shaping our reality.
  • devāsas: This refers to "gods" or "divine beings," symbolizing the spiritual forces that guide and protect us.
  • tanvī3: This term means "body" or "form," representing the physical manifestation of our existence.
  • ni māmṛjuḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "do not lead me astray," indicating a plea for guidance and clarity in our journey.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the interconnectedness of nature, spirituality, and human intention. It emphasizes the importance of commitment to our actions, respect for the natural world, and the pursuit of prosperity through divine guidance. In real life, this can be applied in various ways, such as sustainable living, environmental conservation, and fostering a sense of community through shared values and offerings. By recognizing our place within the larger cosmos and honoring the resources we have, we can cultivate a harmonious existence that benefits both ourselves and the world around us.
