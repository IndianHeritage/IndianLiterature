अनु त्वा रोदसी उभे क्रक्षमानम् अकृपेताम् |इन्द्र यद दस्युहाभवः ||
anu tvā rodasī ubhe krakṣamāṇam akṛpetām | indra yad dasyuhābhavaḥ ||
  • anu tvā rodasī: This phrase translates to "I follow you, O Earth." Here, "anu" means "following" or "after," and "tvā" means "you." "Rodasī" refers to the Earth or the ground. This signifies a deep connection and respect for the Earth, acknowledging its importance in our lives.
  • ubhe krakṣamāṇam: This part means "both the worlds are being protected." "Ubhe" translates to "both," and "krakṣamāṇam" means "being protected" or "guarded." This indicates a duality in existence, where both the physical and spiritual realms are safeguarded.
  • akṛpetām: This word means "without any harm" or "unharmed." It emphasizes the idea of safety and preservation, suggesting that the Earth and its inhabitants should remain untouched by negativity or destruction.
  • indra yad dasyuhābhavaḥ: This translates to "O Indra, may you protect us from harm." "Indra" is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength and protection. "Yad" means "that," and "dasyuhābhavaḥ" refers to "the one who brings harm." This line is a plea for divine protection against malevolent forces.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of humanity with the Earth and the divine. It serves as a reminder of our responsibility to protect our environment and seek protection from negative influences. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in environmental conservation efforts, where individuals and communities work together to safeguard nature. It also highlights the importance of spiritual practices that foster a sense of safety and well-being, encouraging people to seek harmony in their lives and surroundings. By invoking the essence of this mantra, we can cultivate a deeper respect for our planet and strive for a balanced existence, both physically and spiritually.
