मन्त्र: पुरो वो मन्त्रं दिव्यं सुवृक्तिम प्रयति यज्ञे अग्निम अध्वरे दधिध्वम् |पुर उक्तेभिः स हि नो विभावाऽ स्वध्वरा करोति जातवेदाः ||
puro vo mandraṁ divyaṁ suvṛktim prayati yajñe agnim adhvare dadhidhvam | pura ukthebhiḥ sa hi no vibhāvā svadhvarā karati jātavedāḥ ||
  • puro vo mandraṁ divyaṁ: This phrase translates to "the divine mantra for you." Here, "puro" means "for you," "mandraṁ" refers to "mantra" or "sacred sound," and "divyaṁ" means "divine." This suggests the importance of sacred sounds or chants in spiritual practices.
  • suvṛktim prayati: This means "may it lead to auspiciousness." "Suvṛktim" refers to "auspiciousness" or "well-being," and "prayati" means "may it lead." This emphasizes the intention behind the mantra, which is to invoke positive outcomes.
  • yajñe agnim adhvare dadhidhvam: This translates to "offer the fire in the sacrifice." "Yajñe" refers to "sacrifice" or "ritual," "agnim" means "fire," and "adhvare" relates to "the act of sacrifice." This highlights the significance of fire in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purification and transformation.
  • pura ukthebhiḥ: This means "with the words spoken before." "Pura" means "before," and "ukthebhiḥ" refers to "words spoken." This indicates the continuity of tradition and the importance of prior knowledge in spiritual practices.
  • sa hi no vibhāvā: This translates to "for he indeed is our illuminator." "Sa" means "he," "hi" means "indeed," and "vibhāvā" refers to "illuminator" or "one who brings light." This suggests that the divine presence is a source of guidance and enlightenment.
  • svadhvarā karati jātavedāḥ: This means "the one who creates through his own power." "Svadhvarā" refers to "one's own power," and "jātavedāḥ" means "the one who knows all births." This emphasizes the idea of self-creation and the power of knowledge in shaping one's destiny.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of Vedic rituals, emphasizing the importance of divine sounds, auspiciousness, and the transformative power of fire in sacrifices. It highlights the continuity of tradition and the role of divine guidance in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal rituals, ceremonies, or even in daily affirmations where one seeks to invoke positivity and transformation. By understanding and reciting this mantra, individuals can connect with their spiritual roots, seek guidance, and foster a sense of well-being and purpose in their lives.
