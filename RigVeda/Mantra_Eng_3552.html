यां वां सन्ति पुरुष्पृहो नियुतो दाशुषे नराः |अस्मे ता यज्ञवाहसेन्द्रवायू न यच्छतम् ||
yā vāṁ santi puruspṛho niyuto dāśuṣe narā | asme tā yajñavāhasendravāyū ni yacchatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yā: who
    • vāṁ: you
    • santi: are
    • puruspṛho: with great desires
    • niyuto: directed
    • dāśuṣe: to the giver
    • narā: men
    • asme: for us
    • tā: those
    • yajñavāhas: who carry the offerings
    • endravāyū: like the powerful wind
    • ni: not
    • yacchatam: may they give
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the divine forces that are present in our lives, particularly those who are driven by strong desires and are directed towards giving.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and the collective effort of individuals (men) who are engaged in acts of giving and service.
    • The reference to "yajñavāhas" suggests that those who carry out sacrifices or offerings are akin to the powerful forces of nature, like the wind, which can be both nurturing and destructive.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, individuals who volunteer their time and resources for charitable causes embody the spirit of this mantra. For instance, a group of people coming together to feed the homeless demonstrates the collective effort of giving.
    • In a corporate environment, companies that engage in corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives reflect the essence of this mantra by directing their resources towards societal betterment.
    • On a personal level, individuals who mentor others or support friends in need are also living out the principles of this mantra, as they are driven by a desire to give and uplift others.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for collective action and the importance of giving in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that our desires should be directed towards the welfare of others, and that true fulfillment comes from acts of service and generosity.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in community service, corporate responsibility, or personal relationships, embodying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious and supportive society.
