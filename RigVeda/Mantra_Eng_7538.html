इष्टा होत्रा असृक्षतेन्द्रं वृद्धासो अध्वरे |अच्छावभृतं ओजसा ||
iṣṭā hotrā asṛkṣatendraṁ vṛdhāso adhvare | acchāvabhṛtham ojasā ||
  • iṣṭā hotrā: This phrase can be translated as "the offerings made by the priest." In Vedic rituals, the hotra refers to the priest who performs the sacrificial rites. The term "iṣṭā" signifies the offerings that are pleasing or desirable.
  • asṛkṣatendraṁ: This can be interpreted as "the one who protects or guards." The word "asṛkṣat" implies protection, while "indraṁ" refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, who is often associated with strength and power.
  • vṛdhāso adhvare: Here, "vṛdhāso" means "increasing" or "growing," and "adhvare" refers to the sacrificial ritual itself. This suggests that the offerings made during the ritual lead to growth and prosperity.
  • acchāvabhṛtham ojasā: This phrase can be translated as "with great energy or strength." "Acchāvabhṛtham" implies something that is well-covered or protected, while "ojasā" refers to vitality or energy. Together, they suggest that the offerings are made with great vigor and intention.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of ritual offerings and the role of the priest in invoking divine protection and prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to various aspects of life where intention and effort lead to positive outcomes. For example, in a business setting, making thoughtful decisions and putting in dedicated work can lead to growth and success, much like the offerings in a Vedic ritual. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that our actions, when performed with sincerity and energy, can attract blessings and protection in our endeavors.
