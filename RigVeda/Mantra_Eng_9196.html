यो वाम् परिज्मा सुवृद् अश्विना रथो दोषाम् उषासो हव्यो हविष्मता |शश्वत्तमासस तं उ वाम् इदं वयम् पितुर न नाम सुहवम् हवामहे ||
yo vām parijmā suvṛd aśvinā ratho doṣām uṣāso havyo haviṣmatā | śaśvattamāsas tam u vām idaṁ vayam pitur na nāma suhavaṁ havāmahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • vām: you
    • parijmā: the one who is surrounded or encompassed
    • suvṛd: well-formed or well-structured
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin deities)
    • ratho: chariot
    • doṣām: faults or imperfections
    • uṣāso: of the dawn
    • havyo: worthy of being invoked
    • haviṣmatā: with offerings
    • śaśvattamāsas: eternal or everlasting
    • tam: that
    • u: and
    • vām: you
    • idaṁ: this
    • vayam: we
    • pitur: of the father
    • na: not
    • nāma: name
    • suhavaṁ: auspicious
    • havāmahe: we invoke
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Ashvins, the divine twin horsemen associated with health, medicine, and the dawn.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking the blessings of the Ashvins to remove faults and imperfections in life.
    • The mention of "dawn" symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the dispelling of darkness, both literally and metaphorically.
    • By calling upon the Ashvins, the mantra suggests a desire for auspiciousness and well-being, not just for oneself but for the collective ("we invoke").
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek blessings for new ventures, such as starting a business or a new job. This mantra can be recited to invoke positive energy and remove obstacles.
    • During health challenges, people may chant this mantra to seek divine intervention for healing and restoration, reflecting the Ashvins' association with medicine.
    • In family settings, invoking this mantra can foster a sense of unity and collective well-being, encouraging family members to support each other in overcoming difficulties.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in overcoming life's challenges and imperfections.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of community and collective well-being, urging individuals to seek blessings not just for themselves but for their families and society.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be a source of inspiration and hope, encouraging individuals to embrace new beginnings with positivity and faith.
