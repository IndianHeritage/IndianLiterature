आ नो याहि सुतावतोऽस्माकं सुश्रुतीर उप |पिबा सु शिप्रिन्न अन्धसः ||
ā no yāhi sutāvato 'smākaṁ suṣṭutīr upa | pibā su śiprinn andhasaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ā no yāhi sutāvato 'smākaṁ suṣṭutīr upa | pibā su śiprinn andhasaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Come to us, O well-spoken ones; let us drink of the sweet nectar, free from darkness."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ā: Come
    • no: to us
    • yāhi: go or come
    • sutāvato: of the well-spoken or eloquent ones
    • 'smākaṁ: our
    • suṣṭutīr: sweet nectar or praise
    • upa: near or close
    • pibā: drink
    • su: good or sweet
    • śiprinn: free from darkness
    • andhasaḥ: of darkness

  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra is a call for divine guidance and wisdom. It emphasizes the importance of eloquence and clarity in communication.
    • The "well-spoken ones" can be interpreted as wise individuals or deities who possess knowledge and understanding.
    • The "sweet nectar" symbolizes the essence of knowledge, truth, and enlightenment, which is essential for personal and spiritual growth.
    • The plea to "drink" signifies the desire to absorb this wisdom and clarity, moving away from ignorance and darkness.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, seeking advice from a mentor or a knowledgeable colleague can be seen as a way of inviting the "well-spoken ones" into your life to gain insights and clarity.
    • In educational contexts, students often seek out teachers or resources that provide clear and effective communication, akin to drinking the "sweet nectar" of knowledge.
    • In personal relationships, open and honest communication can help dispel misunderstandings and darkness, leading to healthier interactions.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking wisdom and clarity in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to surround themselves with those who can provide guidance and insight, fostering an environment of learning and growth.
    • By embracing knowledge and moving away from ignorance, we can lead more fulfilling and enlightened lives.
