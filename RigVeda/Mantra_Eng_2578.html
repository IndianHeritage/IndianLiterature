स यन्ता विप्र एषां स यज्ञानाम् अथ हि षः |अग्निं तं वो दुवस्यत दाता यो वनिता मगहम् ||
sa yantā vipra eṣāṁ sa yajñānām athā hi ṣaḥ | agniṁ taṁ vo duvasyata dātā yo vanitā magham ||
  • sa yantā vipra eṣāṁ: This phrase can be translated as "those who are wise and knowledgeable." The term "vipra" refers to a learned person or sage, someone who possesses wisdom and insight.
  • sa yajñānām athā hi ṣaḥ: This translates to "those who are engaged in sacrifices." The word "yajña" refers to a ritual of offerings, often performed to please the deities and seek their blessings.
  • agniṁ taṁ vo duvasyata: This means "may that Agni (fire) be favorable to you." Agni is a significant deity in Vedic literature, representing fire, energy, and transformation. Fire is often seen as a purifying force.
  • dātā yo vanitā magham: This translates to "the giver who bestows wealth." Here, "vanitā" refers to prosperity or wealth, and "magham" signifies abundance or blessings.

The mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in performing rituals and sacrifices. It highlights the role of Agni as a mediator between humans and the divine, suggesting that through proper rituals, one can attain blessings and prosperity.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Education and Knowledge: Just as the mantra speaks of wise individuals, it underscores the value of education and continuous learning in our lives. For example, a student who dedicates time to study and understand their subjects is more likely to succeed in their exams and future endeavors.
  • Rituals and Traditions: The reference to yajñas can be seen in modern practices where people perform rituals for various occasions, such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies, to invoke blessings and prosperity.
  • Seeking Blessings: The invocation of Agni as a favorable force can be related to how individuals seek positive energy and blessings in their lives, whether through meditation, prayer, or other spiritual practices.

Conclusion: This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, ritual, and the divine. It signifies that wisdom and proper actions lead to blessings and prosperity in life. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate their lives with purpose and intention, fostering a harmonious relationship with the world around them.
