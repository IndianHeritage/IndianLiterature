अग्निषोमा य आहुतिं यो वां दाशाद धविष्कृतिम् |स प्रजया सुवीर्यं विश्वम आयुर व्य अश्नवत् ||
agnīṣomā ya āhutiṁ yo vāṁ dāśād dhaviṣkṛtim | sa prajayā suvīryaṁ viśvam āyur vy aśnavat ||
  • Agnīṣomā: This term refers to the divine forces of fire (Agni) and the moon (Soma). In Vedic philosophy, Agni symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification, while Soma represents nourishment, vitality, and the essence of life.
  • Ya āhutiṁ: This phrase translates to "who offers the oblation." It signifies the act of making offerings or sacrifices, which is a central theme in Vedic rituals. The act of offering is seen as a way to connect with the divine and seek blessings.
  • Yo vāṁ dāśād dhaviṣkṛtim: This part means "who brings forth your (divine) creation." It emphasizes the role of the divine in manifesting life and creation. The term "dāśād" can be interpreted as the act of giving or bestowing, highlighting the importance of generosity and abundance.
  • Sa prajayā: This translates to "he (or she) with progeny." It signifies the importance of family, lineage, and the continuation of life through generations. In many cultures, including Vedic traditions, having children is seen as a blessing and a duty.
  • Suvīryaṁ: This word means "with great strength" or "valiant." It reflects the qualities of courage, resilience, and the ability to overcome challenges. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who face adversities with determination and strength.
  • Viśvam āyur: This phrase translates to "all life" or "universal life." It signifies the interconnectedness of all living beings and the importance of health and longevity. In practical terms, it can relate to holistic health practices that promote well-being for individuals and communities.
  • Vy aśnavat: This means "may you be free from disease" or "may you be healthy." It emphasizes the desire for good health and well-being, which is a universal aspiration. In modern contexts, this can relate to the importance of healthcare, nutrition, and mental well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra "agnīṣomā ya āhutiṁ yo vāṁ dāśād dhaviṣkṛtim | sa prajayā suvīryaṁ viśvam āyur vy aśnavat ||" encapsulates profound themes of creation, health, strength, and the interconnectedness of life. Its significance lies in the recognition of the divine forces that govern existence and the importance of making offerings to these forces. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate strength and resilience, prioritize health and well-being, and foster a sense of community and family. It serves as a reminder of the blessings of life and the importance of nurturing both ourselves and our relationships with others.
