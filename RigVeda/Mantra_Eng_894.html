इन्द्रो मदाय वावृधे शवसे वृत्रहां नृभिः |तम इन महत्स्व आजिषूतेम अर्बे हवामहे स वाजेषु प्र नो 'विषत ||
indro madāya vāvṛdhe śavase vṛtrahā nṛbhiḥ | tam in mahatsv ājiṣūtem arbhe havāmahe sa vājeṣu pra no 'viṣat ||
  • Indro: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Madāya: Means "to intoxicate" or "to inspire," suggesting a state of heightened awareness or divine influence.
  • Vāvṛdhe: Translates to "he has increased" or "he has grown," indicating growth or enhancement in strength or resources.
  • Śavase: Means "in the strength" or "in the vigor," emphasizing the importance of vitality and energy.
  • Vṛtrahā: Refers to "the slayer of Vritra," a demon representing obstacles or challenges, symbolizing the overcoming of difficulties.
  • Nṛbhiḥ: Means "by men" or "with people," indicating the collective effort of humanity in achieving goals.
  • Tam: Means "that" or "him," referring to the divine presence or energy being invoked.
  • In: A preposition meaning "in" or "within," suggesting an internal or intrinsic quality.
  • Mahatsv: Means "great" or "mighty," indicating the significance of the divine force being called upon.
  • Ājiṣūtem: Refers to "the one who is to be praised" or "the one who is worthy of worship," emphasizing reverence.
  • Arbhe: Means "in the young" or "in the new," suggesting the potential for growth and new beginnings.
  • Havāmahe: Translates to "we invoke" or "we call upon," indicating a collective prayer or request for divine assistance.
  • Sa: Means "he" or "that," again referring to the divine energy being addressed.
  • Vājeṣu: Means "in the offerings" or "in the sacrifices," indicating the importance of rituals and offerings in Vedic tradition.
  • Pra: A prefix meaning "forth" or "forward," suggesting movement or progress.
  • No: Means "our," indicating a personal connection to the divine.
  • 'Viṣat: Means "may he enter" or "may he come," expressing a desire for the divine presence to be with us.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the invocation of divine strength and guidance, particularly through the figure of Indra, who represents overcoming obstacles and achieving greatness. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as leadership, teamwork, and personal challenges. For instance, in a corporate setting, a leader may draw inspiration from this mantra to motivate their team to overcome challenges and strive for success. The collective effort of individuals, as highlighted in the mantra, is crucial in achieving common goals, whether in personal endeavors, community projects, or organizational objectives. Thus, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in the pursuit of strength, unity, and success in all aspects of life.
