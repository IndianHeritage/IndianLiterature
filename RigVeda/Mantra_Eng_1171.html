इन्द्रं कुत्सो वृत्रहणं शचीपतिं काते निबाल्ह ऋषिर अह्वद ऊतये | रथं न दुर्गाद वसवः सुदानवो विश्वस्मान नो अंहसो निष पिपर्तन ||
indraṁ kutso vṛtrahaṇaṁ śacīpatiṁ kāṭe nibāḻha ṛṣir ahvad ūtaye | rathaṁ na durgād vasavaḥ sudānavo viśvasmān no aṁhaso niṣ pipartana ||
  • Indraṁ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength and power.
  • Kutso: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is strong" or "the powerful one," emphasizing Indra's might.
  • Vṛtrahaṇaṁ: Meaning "the slayer of Vritra," Vritra is a serpent or dragon that represents chaos and obstacles. Indra's victory over Vritra symbolizes the triumph of order over chaos.
  • Śacīpatiṁ: Refers to Indra as the lord of Śacī, his consort, representing the balance of power and support in relationships.
  • Kāṭe: This word can mean "in the battle" or "in the conflict," indicating the context of struggle and challenge.
  • Nibāḻha: Translates to "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is called upon," suggesting the act of seeking divine assistance.
  • Ṛṣir: Refers to a sage or seer, indicating wisdom and insight, often sought for guidance.
  • Ahvad: Meaning "to call upon" or "to invoke," reinforcing the theme of seeking help from the divine.
  • Ūtaye: This term means "for prosperity" or "for well-being," indicating the purpose of the invocation.
  • Rathaṁ: Refers to a chariot, symbolizing movement, progress, and the journey of life.
  • Na durgād: Meaning "not from difficulties," suggesting a desire to overcome challenges.
  • Vasavaḥ: Refers to the Vasus, a group of deities associated with abundance and prosperity.
  • Sudānavo: Meaning "the good givers," indicating generosity and the sharing of resources.
  • Viśvasmān: Translates to "the all-pervading," suggesting a universal presence and support.
  • No: Meaning "our," indicating a collective invocation.
  • Aṁhaso: Refers to "strength" or "power," emphasizing the importance of resilience.
  • Niṣ pipartana: Meaning "may not be taken away," indicating a desire for protection and preservation.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine strength and support in overcoming obstacles and challenges in life. It highlights the relationship between the divine and humanity, where individuals seek guidance and assistance from higher powers.
  • Real-Life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be applied when facing difficulties, whether in personal relationships, career challenges, or health issues. By invoking strength and support, individuals can find the courage to confront their problems and seek solutions.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of faith and the importance of seeking help from the divine. It encourages individuals to recognize their inner strength while also acknowledging the support available from higher powers, fostering resilience and hope in the face of adversity.
