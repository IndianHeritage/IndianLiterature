अषाल्हं युत्सु पृथनासु पप्रिं स्वर्षाम अप्सां वृजनस्य गोपाम् |भरेषुजां सुख्षितिं सुश्रवसं जयन्तं त्वाम् अनु मदेम सोम ||
aṣāḻhaṁ yutsu pṛtanāsu papriṁ svarṣām apsāṁ vṛjanasya gopām | bhareṣujāṁ sukṣitiṁ suśravasaṁ jayantaṁ tvām anu madema soma ||
  • Mantra: "aṣāḻhaṁ yutsu pṛtanāsu papriṁ svarṣām apsāṁ vṛjanasya gopām | bhareṣujāṁ sukṣitiṁ suśravasaṁ jayantaṁ tvām anu madema soma ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aṣāḻhaṁ: Unwavering, steadfast
    • yutsu: In the battles or struggles
    • pṛtanāsu: In the paths or ways
    • papriṁ: To be praised or honored
    • svarṣām: Of the celestial or divine
    • apsāṁ: Of the waters or the divine essence
    • vṛjanasya: Of the protector or guardian
    • gopām: The one who protects or nurtures
    • bhareṣujāṁ: Of the one who brings forth or nourishes
    • sukṣitiṁ: Well-being or prosperity
    • suśravasaṁ: Of good fame or reputation
    • jayantaṁ: Victorious or triumphant
    • tvām: You (referring to Soma)
    • anu: Following or in accordance with
    • madema: We may enjoy or partake
    • soma: The divine nectar or essence
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of Soma, a significant deity in Vedic literature, representing the divine nectar that brings joy, health, and immortality.
    • The phrase "aṣāḻhaṁ yutsu" emphasizes the importance of steadfastness in the face of challenges, akin to how individuals must remain resolute in their personal and professional lives when facing adversities.
    • “pṛtanāsu papriṁ” suggests that one should be honored and praised for their efforts, similar to how employees are recognized for their hard work in a corporate environment, fostering motivation and morale.
    • The reference to “svarṣām apsāṁ” highlights the connection to the divine and the natural world, reminding us of the importance of nurturing our environment, much like sustainable practices in agriculture or conservation efforts.
    • “vṛjanasya gopām” speaks to the role of protectors, akin to guardians in our lives, such as parents or mentors who guide us through challenges.
    • “bhareṣujāṁ sukṣitiṁ” emphasizes the importance of well-being and prosperity, which can be seen in community efforts to support local businesses or health initiatives that promote wellness.
    • “suśravasaṁ jayantaṁ” reflects the aspiration for a good reputation and success, similar to how individuals strive for excellence in their careers or personal endeavors.
    • Finally, “tvām anu madema soma” is a call to partake in the divine essence, symbolizing the pursuit of joy and fulfillment in life, much like how people seek happiness through relationships, achievements, and experiences.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of resilience, protection, and the pursuit of well-being, making it applicable in various real-life scenarios.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to remain steadfast in our endeavors, honor those who support us, and strive for a life filled with joy and prosperity.
    • In contemporary life, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate a balanced approach to challenges, emphasizing the importance of community, sustainability, and personal growth.
