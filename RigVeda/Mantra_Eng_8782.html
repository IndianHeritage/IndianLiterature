मन्त्र: कारुर अहं ततो भिषग उपलप्रक्षिणी नना | नानाधियो वसूयवो 'नु गा इव तस्थिमेन्द्रायन्दो परि श्रव ||
kārur ahaṁ tato bhiṣag upalaprakṣiṇī nanā | nānādhiyo vasūyavo 'nu gā iva tasthimendrāyendo pari srava ||
  • kārur ahaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "I am the one who is capable" or "I am the one who can act." It signifies a sense of empowerment and readiness to take action.
  • tato bhiṣag: This translates to "then the healer" or "the one who brings healing." It emphasizes the importance of healing and wellness in life.
  • upalaprakṣiṇī: This term refers to "the one who is aware" or "the one who perceives." It highlights the significance of awareness and perception in understanding our surroundings and ourselves.
  • nanā: This word can mean "various" or "many." It suggests diversity and the multitude of experiences and perspectives in life.
  • nānādhiyo: This translates to "those with various intellects" or "those with diverse understandings." It points to the richness of human thought and the importance of different viewpoints.
  • vasūyavo: This can be interpreted as "those who are generous" or "those who give." It emphasizes the value of generosity and kindness in human interactions.
  • 'nu gā iva: This phrase means "like the song" or "as if singing." It suggests harmony and the beauty of expression, akin to music.
  • tasthimendrāyendo: This can be understood as "standing firm like Indra" or "with the strength of Indra." Indra is a deity associated with power and authority, symbolizing strength and resilience.
  • pari srava: This translates to "flowing all around" or "spreading everywhere." It signifies the idea of abundance and the interconnectedness of all things.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of empowerment, healing, awareness, diversity, generosity, harmony, strength, and interconnectedness. In real life, these concepts can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, community building, and fostering relationships. For instance, when facing challenges, one can draw upon their inner strength (kārur ahaṁ) and seek healing (tato bhiṣag) through awareness (upalaprakṣiṇī) and the support of diverse perspectives (nānādhiyo). The mantra encourages individuals to be generous (vasūyavo) and to express themselves harmoniously (nu gā iva), ultimately leading to a more connected and resilient community (tasthimendrāyendo pari srava). Its significance lies in promoting a holistic approach to life, where each individual contributes to the collective well-being, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
