अग्निं सूनुं सनश्रुतं सहसो जातवेदसम् |वह्निं देवाऽकृत्वता ||
agniṁ sūnuṁ sanaśrutaṁ sahaso jātavedasam | vahniṁ devā akṛṇvata ||
  • Agniṁ: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Sūnuṁ: Meaning "son," it signifies the relationship of Agni as a progenitor or a source of light and warmth.
  • Sanaśrutaṁ: This term translates to "well-heard" or "well-known," indicating the importance of Agni in rituals and daily life.
  • Sahaso: Meaning "with strength" or "powerful," it emphasizes the potency of Agni in various forms.
  • Jātavedasam: This word means "the one who knows all births," referring to Agni's role as a witness to all actions and events.
  • Vahniṁ: This term means "fire" or "flame," representing the physical manifestation of Agni.
  • Devā: Meaning "gods," it signifies the divine aspect of Agni and his connection to other deities.
  • Akṛṇvata: This word translates to "they created" or "they established," indicating the collaborative effort of the gods in invoking Agni.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation to Agni, acknowledging his vital role in the universe and in human life. It highlights the reverence for fire as a purifying and transformative force, essential for rituals, cooking, and warmth.


In real life, the significance of this mantra can be seen in various contexts:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: Agni is central to many Hindu rituals, where offerings are made into the fire, symbolizing the connection between the material and spiritual worlds.
  • Transformation: Just as fire transforms materials, individuals often undergo personal transformations through challenges, akin to the purifying nature of Agni.
  • Community and Togetherness: Gathering around a fire fosters community bonding, reflecting the collective effort of the gods in the mantra.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of Agni as a powerful and transformative force in both the cosmos and human life. Its application in rituals, personal growth, and community gatherings underscores the timeless relevance of Agni in our daily existence, reminding us of the sacredness of fire and its role in fostering connection and transformation.
