उप याम एति युवतिः सुदक्षं दोषा वस्तोर हविष्मती घृताचि |उप स्वैन्म अरमतिर वसूयुः ||
upa yam eti yuvatiḥ sudakṣaṁ doṣā vastor haviṣmatī ghṛtācī | upa svainam aramatir vasūyuḥ ||
  • upa yam eti yuvatiḥ: This phrase can be translated as "the young woman approaches." Here, "upa" means "towards," "yam" refers to "that which," and "yuvatiḥ" means "young woman." This can symbolize the pursuit of knowledge, wisdom, or a goal in life.
  • sudakṣaṁ doṣā vastor haviṣmatī: This part translates to "the one who is skilled in overcoming the faults of the object." "Sudakṣaṁ" means "skilled," "doṣā" refers to "faults," "vastor" means "of the object," and "haviṣmatī" can be interpreted as "one who is knowledgeable or wise." This suggests the importance of skill and wisdom in addressing challenges or imperfections in life.
  • ghṛtācī: This term refers to "clarity" or "purity," often associated with ghee (clarified butter) in Vedic literature. It symbolizes nourishment and the essence of life, indicating that clarity and purity are essential for growth and understanding.
  • upa svainam aramatir vasūyuḥ: This can be translated as "the one who is devoted to the pursuit of wealth and prosperity." "Svainam" means "one's own," "aramatir" refers to "devotion," and "vasūyuḥ" means "wealth." This emphasizes the importance of dedication and effort in achieving prosperity and success in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the journey of a young woman who approaches her goals with skill and wisdom, overcoming challenges and striving for clarity and purity in her pursuits. It highlights the significance of dedication and effort in achieving prosperity. In real life, this mantra can be applied to various scenarios, such as personal development, career advancement, and the pursuit of knowledge. It serves as a reminder that with the right mindset and approach, one can navigate the complexities of life and achieve their aspirations.
