स नो विश्वानि आ भर सुवितानि शतक्रतो |यद इन्द्र मृळयासि नः ||
sa no viśvāny ā bhara suvitāni śatakrato | yad indra mṛḻayāsi naḥ ||
  • sa no viśvāny ā bhara: This phrase translates to "May you bring us all that is good." Here, "sa" means "may," "no" means "us," "viśvāny" means "all," and "ā bhara" means "bring." This part of the mantra emphasizes the desire for abundance and positivity in life.
  • suvitāni: This word means "good things" or "well-being." It signifies the aspiration for prosperity, health, and happiness. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of a fulfilling career, nurturing relationships, and overall well-being.
  • śatakrato: This term refers to "the one with a hundred powers" or "the powerful one." It is often associated with Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and capability. In a modern context, this can represent individuals or leaders who possess the ability to effect change and inspire others.
  • yad indra mṛḻayāsi naḥ: This translates to "Whatever you, Indra, bestow upon us." "Yad" means "whatever," "indra" refers to the deity Indra, "mṛḻayāsi" means "you bestow," and "naḥ" means "upon us." This part of the mantra expresses gratitude and recognition of divine blessings, acknowledging that the good things in life come from a higher power.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates a prayer for abundance, well-being, and divine support. It reflects a holistic approach to life, where one seeks not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional fulfillment.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during personal challenges, community gatherings, or even in corporate settings where teamwork and collective success are emphasized. It serves as a reminder to focus on the positive aspects of life and to be grateful for the support we receive from others and the universe.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encourages individuals to seek goodness and abundance while recognizing the power of divine influence in their lives. By embodying the principles of gratitude, positivity, and collective well-being, one can foster a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
