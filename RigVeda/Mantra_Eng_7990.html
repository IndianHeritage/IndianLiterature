पुनान इन्दव आ भर सोम द्विबर्हसँ रयिम् |वृषन्न इन्दो न उक्त्यम् ||
punāna indav ā bhara soma dvibarhasaṁ rayim | vṛṣann indo na ukthyam ||
  • Mantra: "punāna indav ā bhara soma dvibarhasaṁ rayim | vṛṣann indo na ukthyam ||"
  • Translation: "O Indra, may you fill us with the invigorating Soma, bestowing upon us double the wealth; may the strong Indra not deny us our offerings."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Punāna: Invigorating or purifying, suggesting a renewal or enhancement.
    • Indav: Referring to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic tradition, associated with strength and rain.
    • Ā bhara: May you fill or bestow, indicating a request for abundance.
    • Soma: A sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
    • Dvibarhasaṁ: Double wealth or abundance, indicating a desire for prosperity.
    • Rayim: Wealth or riches, often associated with material and spiritual prosperity.
    • Vṛṣann: Strong or powerful, emphasizing the might of Indra.
    • Indo: Another reference to Indra, reinforcing his importance in the mantra.
    • Na ukthyam: Not deny or refuse, expressing a plea for acceptance of offerings.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer for abundance and prosperity, invoking the blessings of Indra, who is seen as a provider of wealth and strength.
    • The mention of Soma highlights the importance of spiritual nourishment and the quest for enlightenment, suggesting that material wealth should be accompanied by spiritual growth.
    • The plea for Indra not to deny offerings reflects the human desire for recognition and acceptance in the divine realm, emphasizing the relationship between humans and the divine.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in contexts such as business and personal growth, where individuals seek to enhance their resources and capabilities.
    • It can serve as a reminder to balance material pursuits with spiritual well-being, encouraging individuals to seek not just wealth but also wisdom and fulfillment.
    • During rituals or personal meditations, reciting this mantra can help individuals focus on their goals, inviting abundance and strength into their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking both material and spiritual wealth, highlighting the interconnectedness of the two.
    • By invoking Indra and the invigorating Soma, practitioners are reminded of the importance of divine blessings in achieving their aspirations.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a holistic approach to life, where prosperity is not just measured in material terms but also in spiritual fulfillment and growth.
