आ भण्डमाने उषसा उपाके उता स्मयते तंवा3 विरूपे |यथा नो मित्रो वरुणो जुजोषद इन्द्रो मरुत्वां उता वा महोभिः ||
ā bhandamāne uṣasā upāke uta smayete tanvā3 virūpe | yathā no mitro varuṇo jujoṣad indro marutvām̐ uta vā mahobhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • bhandamāne: when bound or tied
    • uṣasā: by the dawn
    • upāke: to the one who is near
    • uta: and
    • smayete: smiles or rejoices
    • tanvā: in the body
    • virūpe: in various forms
    • yathā: as
    • no: for us
    • mitro: the friend
    • varuṇo: Varuna, the god of cosmic order
    • jujoṣad: may he protect
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • marutvām: with the Maruts, the storm gods
    • uta: and
    • vā: or
    • mahobhiḥ: with great powers
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and hope.
    • The phrase "when bound or tied" suggests overcoming obstacles or limitations.
    • The mention of gods like Varuna and Indra emphasizes the importance of divine protection and guidance.
    • The idea of "smiling in various forms" reflects the diversity of life and experiences, encouraging acceptance and adaptability.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, this mantra can inspire individuals to embrace change and seek new opportunities, much like the dawn brings light after darkness.
    • In a community setting, it can be a reminder of the importance of unity and support, as seen in teamwork where diverse skills come together for a common goal.
    • In times of adversity, invoking the protective qualities of deities like Varuna and Indra can provide comfort and strength, similar to seeking guidance from mentors or leaders in challenging situations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of resilience, adaptability, and divine support in our lives.
    • It encourages us to embrace new beginnings and face challenges with a positive outlook.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to seek help from others and to recognize the strength found in community and divine guidance.
