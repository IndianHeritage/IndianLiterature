वृष्टिं दिवः शतधारः पवस्व सहस्रसां वाजयुर देववीतौ |सं सिंधुभिः कलशे वावशानः सम उष्रियाभिः प्रतिरन् न आयुः ||
vṛṣṭiṁ divaḥ śatadhāraḥ pavasva sahasrasā vājayur devavītau | saṁ sindhubhiḥ kalaśe vāvaśānaḥ sam usriyābhiḥ pratiran na āyuḥ ||
  • vṛṣṭiṁ divaḥ: This phrase translates to "the rain from the heavens." It signifies the essential role of rain in sustaining life on Earth. In real life, we can observe how rain is crucial for agriculture, replenishing water sources, and maintaining ecosystems.
  • śatadhāraḥ: This means "with a hundred streams." It emphasizes abundance and the idea that rain brings forth multiple sources of water. For example, a single rainstorm can fill rivers, lakes, and reservoirs, showcasing nature's generosity.
  • pavasva: This word translates to "flow" or "move." It suggests the dynamic nature of rainwater as it travels through the environment, nourishing the soil and supporting plant growth. In urban areas, we see how rainwater management systems are designed to channel water effectively to prevent flooding.
  • sahasrasā: This means "with a thousand streams." It reinforces the idea of abundance, indicating that the blessings of rain are manifold. In agriculture, farmers often rely on seasonal rains to ensure a bountiful harvest, which can be seen in regions that celebrate monsoon festivals.
  • vājayur devavītau: This phrase can be interpreted as "the divine energy of the rain." It highlights the spiritual significance of rain, often seen as a blessing from the gods. In many cultures, rituals and prayers are performed to invoke rain, especially in drought-prone areas.
  • saṁ sindhubhiḥ: This translates to "with the oceans." It connects the concept of rain to larger bodies of water, emphasizing the cycle of water in nature. For instance, the water cycle illustrates how evaporation from oceans leads to cloud formation and eventually rain, which is vital for life.
  • kalaśe vāvaśānaḥ: This means "in the vessel." It symbolizes the idea of storing and utilizing the rainwater effectively. In many communities, rainwater harvesting systems are implemented to collect and store rainwater for later use, demonstrating sustainable practices.
  • sam usriyābhiḥ: This phrase translates to "with the streams." It signifies the interconnectedness of water sources and their collective importance. In real life, rivers and streams often serve as lifelines for communities, providing water for drinking, irrigation, and recreation.
  • pratiran na āyuḥ: This means "may we have long life." It expresses a prayer for longevity and prosperity, which is often linked to the availability of water and food. In many cultures, a good harvest, facilitated by adequate rainfall, is directly associated with the well-being and longevity of the community.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of rain as a life-giving force, highlighting its abundance and divine nature. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of water sources and the importance of sustainable practices in managing water. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to appreciate the blessings of rain, engage in water conservation efforts, and perform rituals that honor the natural cycles of life. By recognizing the significance of rain, we can foster a deeper connection with nature and promote environmental stewardship.
