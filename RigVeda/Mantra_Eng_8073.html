प्र ते धाराऽसश्चतो दिवो न यन्ति वृष्टयः |अच्छा वाजं सहस्रिणम् ||
pra te dhārā asaścato divo na yanti vṛṣṭayaḥ | acchā vājaṁ sahasriṇam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: for you
    • te: your
    • dhārā: flow or stream
    • asaścato: of the many
    • dive: of the heavens
    • na: not
    • yanti: go or move
    • vṛṣṭayaḥ: rains or showers
    • acchā: good or auspicious
    • vājaṁ: strength or vigor
    • sahasriṇam: of a thousand
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the flow of divine blessings and abundance.
    • The phrase "for you, the flow of the many from the heavens does not go" suggests that the divine blessings are constant and ever-present.
    • The latter part, "good strength of a thousand," emphasizes the power and potential that comes from these blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain, which is essential for their crops. This mantra can be seen as a prayer for consistent and abundant rainfall, ensuring a good harvest.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek strength and support during challenging times. This mantra can serve as a reminder that divine support is always available, helping them to overcome obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its affirmation of divine support and abundance in life.
    • It teaches us to recognize and appreciate the blessings we receive, encouraging a mindset of gratitude.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as seeking strength in adversity, praying for prosperity, or fostering a sense of community and support among individuals.
