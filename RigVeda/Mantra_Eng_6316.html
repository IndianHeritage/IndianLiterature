नूनं तद इन्द्र दद्धि नो यत त्वा सुन्वन्त इमहे |रयिं नश् चित्रम् आ भरा स्वर्विदम् ||
nūnaṁ tad indra daddhi no yat tvā sunvanta īmahe | rayiṁ naś citram ā bharā svarvidam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nūnaṁ: Indeed
    • tad: that
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • daddhi: grant or bestow
    • no: to us
    • yat: which
    • tvā: you
    • sunvanta: we invoke or praise
    • īmahe: we meditate or contemplate
    • rayiṁ: wealth or prosperity
    • naś: our
    • citram: splendid or diverse
    • ā bharā: may you bring or bestow
    • svarvidam: knowledge of the heavens or celestial wisdom
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Indra, asking him to bestow wealth and prosperity upon us, as we invoke and meditate upon him. It emphasizes the importance of divine blessings in achieving a prosperous and fulfilling life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, entrepreneurs often seek divine blessings for success and prosperity. This mantra can be recited during business meetings or new ventures to invoke positive energy and support.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during times of financial difficulty, seeking guidance and abundance from a higher power.
    • During festivals or rituals, families may chant this mantra to invite prosperity and well-being into their homes, reinforcing the belief in divine intervention in daily life.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine support for wealth and prosperity. It teaches us the importance of seeking higher wisdom and blessings in our endeavors. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts, such as business, personal challenges, and family rituals, reminding us that our efforts are often complemented by spiritual guidance and support.
