तिस्रो वाचः प्र वद ज्योतिरग्रा या एतद दुहरे मधुदोघम ऊधः |स वत्सं कृत्वान गर्भं ओषधीनां सद्यो जातो वृषभो रोरवीति ||
tisro vācaḥ pra vada jyotiragrā yā etad duhre madhudogham ūdhaḥ | sa vatsaṁ kṛṇvan garbham oṣadhīnāṁ sadyo jāto vṛṣabho roravīti ||
  • tisro vācaḥ pra vada: This phrase translates to "Speak the three words." In this context, "three words" can symbolize the essential truths or principles that guide our lives. For example, in a real-life scenario, these could be the values of honesty, compassion, and integrity that one should uphold in their daily interactions.
  • jyotiragrā yā etad duhre: This means "the light that is born from this." Here, "light" represents knowledge, wisdom, or enlightenment. In practical terms, this can be related to the pursuit of education and understanding in our lives, where gaining knowledge illuminates our path and helps us make informed decisions.
  • madhudogham ūdhaḥ: This translates to "the sweet nectar that is obtained." The "nectar" symbolizes the rewards or benefits that come from following the right path. For instance, when one practices kindness and generosity, the "nectar" could be the joy and fulfillment that comes from helping others.
  • sa vatsaṁ kṛṇvan garbham oṣadhīnāṁ: This phrase means "he who creates the embryo of the plants." It signifies the nurturing aspect of life, where one cultivates growth and development. In real life, this can be seen in parenting, mentorship, or any role where one supports the growth of others, such as teachers guiding students.
  • sadyo jāto vṛṣabho roravīti: This translates to "the bull that is born immediately roars." The "bull" symbolizes strength and vitality, and its roar represents the expression of power and presence. In a real-life context, this can be related to individuals who, upon achieving success or recognition, assert their presence confidently in their respective fields.

  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of speaking truth, seeking knowledge, nurturing growth, and expressing strength. In our daily lives, we can apply these principles by being honest in our communication, pursuing education, supporting the development of others, and confidently asserting our achievements. The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to personal and communal growth, encouraging individuals to embody these values for a harmonious and prosperous life.
