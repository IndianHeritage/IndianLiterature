तपोष पवित्रं विततं दिवस पदे शोचन्तो अस्य तन्तवो व्य अस्थिरन् |अवन्त्य अस्य पवितारम् आशवो दिवस पृष्ठम् अधि तिष्ठन्ति चेतसा ||
tapoṣ pavitraṁ vitataṁ divas pade śocanto asya tantavo vy asthiran | avanty asya pavītāram āśavo divas pṛṣṭham adhi tiṣṭhanti cetasā ||
  • tapoṣ: This word refers to "austerity" or "penance." It signifies the practice of self-discipline and spiritual effort. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who dedicate themselves to meditation, yoga, or other forms of spiritual practice to attain higher consciousness.
  • pavitraṁ: Meaning "pure" or "sacred," this term emphasizes the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and surroundings. For example, maintaining a clean environment or practicing ethical living can be seen as a way to embody this purity.
  • vitataṁ: This translates to "spread out" or "expanded." It can refer to the vastness of the universe or the expansion of consciousness. In practical terms, this can relate to the idea of broadening one's horizons through education and experiences.
  • divas pade: Meaning "in the realm of the heavens," this phrase suggests a connection to the divine or higher realms of existence. It can be likened to seeking inspiration from nature or the cosmos, which many people do through stargazing or spending time in nature.
  • śocanto: This word means "lamenting" or "sorrowing." It reflects the human experience of grief and the importance of acknowledging one's emotions. For instance, people often gather to mourn the loss of a loved one, which is a natural part of life.
  • asya tantavo: This phrase can be interpreted as "the threads of this" or "the connections of this." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the universe. In real life, this can be seen in the way communities come together to support one another during difficult times.
  • vy asthiran: Meaning "they are not stable" or "they are transient," this term highlights the impermanence of life. This is evident in the changing seasons, the aging process, and the fleeting nature of moments, reminding us to cherish the present.
  • avanty: This word means "to protect" or "to preserve." It emphasizes the importance of safeguarding what is sacred and valuable. In real life, this can be seen in environmental conservation efforts aimed at protecting nature and wildlife.
  • asya pavītāram: This translates to "the purest of this." It suggests striving for the highest form of purity and goodness. In practice, this can be reflected in acts of kindness and compassion towards others.
  • āśavo: Meaning "the desires" or "the aspirations," this term refers to the hopes and dreams that drive individuals. In real life, this can be seen in people's ambitions and goals, whether personal or professional.
  • divas pṛṣṭham: This phrase means "the back of the heavens" or "the higher realms." It signifies a connection to the divine or spiritual guidance. Many people seek this connection through prayer, meditation, or rituals.
  • adhi tiṣṭhanti: Meaning "they stand above" or "they reside above," this term suggests a higher state of being or consciousness. In real life, this can be related to individuals who strive for personal growth and enlightenment.
  • cetasā: This word means "with the mind" or "with consciousness." It emphasizes the role of awareness and intention in our actions. For example, mindfulness practices encourage individuals to be present and aware in their daily lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of spiritual practice, emphasizing purity, interconnectedness, and the transient nature of life. It serves as a reminder to engage in self-discipline, seek higher consciousness, and acknowledge the emotions that arise in our journey. In real life, these teachings can be applied through mindfulness, community support, and a commitment to personal growth, ultimately guiding individuals towards a more meaningful and harmonious existence.
