अविता नो अजाश्वः पूषा यामनि-यामनि |आ भक्षत कन्यासु नः ||
avitā no ajāśvaḥ pūṣā yāmani-yāmani | ā bhakṣat kanyāsu naḥ ||
  • avitā: This word translates to "may he protect" or "may he guard." It signifies a plea for protection and safety.
  • no: This means "us" or "our," indicating a collective request or desire.
  • ajāśvaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is unassailable" or "the invincible." It refers to a powerful entity or force that cannot be easily overcome.
  • pūṣā: This is a name of a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity. Pūṣā is often invoked for blessings related to growth and sustenance.
  • yāmani-yāmani: This phrase suggests a sense of movement or progression, often interpreted as "moving forward" or "advancing." It can also imply a journey or path.
  • ā: This word means "to" or "towards," indicating direction or intention.
  • bhakṣat: This translates to "may he consume" or "may he take." It implies a request for the deity to partake in something beneficial.
  • kanyāsu: This term refers to "young girls" or "daughters," symbolizing purity, potential, and the future generation.
  • naḥ: This means "for us," reinforcing the collective nature of the request.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer for protection and nourishment, particularly for the younger generation. It invokes the deity Pūṣā to safeguard and bless the youth, ensuring their growth and well-being as they navigate through life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Family Blessings: Parents may recite this mantra for the safety and prosperity of their children, especially daughters, as they embark on their life journeys.
  • Community Gatherings: During community events or rituals, this mantra can be chanted to invoke blessings for the collective well-being of the community, particularly focusing on the younger members.
  • Educational Institutions: Schools and colleges may use this mantra during ceremonies to seek divine protection and guidance for students as they pursue knowledge and personal growth.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on protection, nourishment, and the importance of the younger generation. It serves as a reminder of the collective responsibility to nurture and safeguard the future, ensuring that the values of care and support are passed down. By invoking the blessings of Pūṣā, individuals and communities can foster an environment conducive to growth, safety, and prosperity.
