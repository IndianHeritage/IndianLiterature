उप नो हरिभिः सुतं याहि मदानां पते | उप नो हरिभिः सुतम् ||
upa no haribhiḥ sutaṁ yāhi madānām pate | upa no haribhiḥ sutam ||
  • Mantra: "upa no haribhiḥ sutaṁ yāhi madānām pate | upa no haribhiḥ sutam ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as "Come to us, O Lord of the Madanas, with your sons, the horses." The repetition emphasizes the call to the divine for assistance and blessings.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • upa: Come closer or approach.
    • no: To us or for us.
    • haribhiḥ: Referring to the divine or the gods, often associated with horses in Vedic literature.
    • sutaṁ: Sons or offspring, symbolizing strength and vitality.
    • yāhi: Go or come.
    • madānām: Of the Madanas, which can refer to a group or a specific divine aspect.
    • pate: Lord or master, indicating a higher power.
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra is a call for divine intervention and support, particularly in times of need or challenge.
    • In Vedic culture, horses were symbols of power, speed, and nobility. Invoking the divine with horses signifies a request for strength and support in endeavors.
    • The repetition of the mantra emphasizes the urgency and sincerity of the plea, highlighting the importance of divine assistance in achieving goals.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in situations where individuals seek guidance or support, such as during important decisions, challenges in career, or personal struggles.
    • For example, a student preparing for an important exam might recite this mantra to invoke focus and clarity, seeking the strength to perform well.
    • In a business context, a leader might use this mantra to inspire their team, calling upon collective strength and divine guidance to achieve success in a project.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking support from higher powers, whether divine or communal, in our endeavors.
    • It emphasizes the interconnectedness of strength, support, and success, encouraging individuals to reach out for help when needed.
    • Ultimately, this mantra highlights the significance of faith and the pursuit of collective strength in overcoming life's challenges.
