तत् सूर्यं रोधसी उभे दोषा वस्तोर उप ब्रुवे |भोजेष्व अस्मां अभ्य उच चरā सदā ||
tat sūryaṁ rodasī ubhe doṣā vastor upa bruve | bhojeṣv asmām̐ abhy uc carā sadā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tat: that
    • sūryaṁ: sun
    • rodasī: in the sky or the heavens
    • ubhe: both
    • doṣā: faults or imperfections
    • vastor: of the things or objects
    • upa: near or close
    • bruve: I speak or I declare
    • bhojeṣv: in the offerings or sacrifices
    • asmām̐: to us
    • abhy: towards
    • uc: utter or speak
    • carā: moving or wandering
    • sadā: always or forever
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the sun, representing light, knowledge, and divinity.
    • The phrase "tat sūryaṁ" emphasizes the importance of the sun as a source of life and energy.
    • "Rodasi ubhe" suggests a connection between the heavens and the earth, indicating a holistic view of existence.
    • The mention of "doṣā" refers to the imperfections or faults in our lives, acknowledging that they exist.
    • By stating "vastor upa bruve," the speaker is declaring their intention to address these imperfections.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "bhojeṣv asmām̐ abhy uc carā sadā," signifies a desire for continuous movement towards improvement and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied to personal growth. Just as the sun rises every day, we too can strive to rise above our faults and imperfections.
    • In a professional setting, acknowledging the "doṣā" in a project can lead to constructive feedback and improvement, much like how the sun illuminates the path forward.
    • In relationships, recognizing and addressing imperfections can foster deeper connections, akin to the sun nurturing life on earth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of self-awareness and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • It encourages individuals to acknowledge their flaws while striving for continuous improvement.
    • In essence, it highlights the significance of the sun as a metaphor for enlightenment and the journey towards a better self.
