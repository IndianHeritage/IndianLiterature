रामध्वं मे वचसे सोम्याय ऋतावरीर उप मुहूर्तम् एविः |प्र सिंधुम अच्‍छा बृहतī मनिषावस्युर अह्वे कुशिकस्य सूनुः ||
ramadhvam me vacase somyāya ṛtāvarīr upa muhūrtam evaiḥ | pra sindhum acchā bṛhatī manīṣāvasyur ahve kuśikasya sūnuḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ramadhvam me vacase somyāya ṛtāvarīr upa muhūrtam evaiḥ | pra sindhum acchā bṛhatī manīṣāvasyur ahve kuśikasya sūnuḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ramadhvam: This can be interpreted as "to be joyful" or "to rejoice." It signifies a state of happiness and contentment.
    • me: Means "my" or "to me," indicating a personal connection or request.
    • vacase: Refers to "speech" or "words," emphasizing the importance of communication and expression.
    • somyāya: This term relates to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing nourishment and vitality.
    • ṛtāvarīr: This can be understood as "truth" or "righteousness," highlighting the significance of living in accordance with cosmic order.
    • upa: Means "near" or "towards," suggesting a direction or approach.
    • muhūrtam: Refers to a "moment" or "auspicious time," indicating the importance of timing in actions.
    • evaiḥ: This can be translated as "with these" or "by these," suggesting a collective effort or support.
    • pra: Means "forth" or "forward," indicating movement or progression.
    • sindhum: Refers to "ocean" or "river," symbolizing vastness and abundance.
    • acchā: Means "to attain" or "to reach," indicating achievement or success.
    • bṛhatī: This term signifies "greatness" or "expansiveness," emphasizing the importance of grand aspirations.
    • manīṣāvasyur: Refers to "wisdom" or "intellect," highlighting the value of knowledge and understanding.
    • ahve: Means "to call" or "to invoke," indicating a request or appeal.
    • kuśikasya: Refers to "of the Kusi," which may denote a lineage or a specific group.
    • sūnuḥ: Means "son," indicating a familial or generational connection.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of joy, communication, and righteousness in our lives. It encourages individuals to seek happiness and express themselves positively.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal relationships where open communication fosters understanding and joy.
    • The reference to "soma" suggests the need for nourishment, not just physically but also emotionally and spiritually, reminding us to take care of our holistic well-being.
    • The idea of "auspicious time" encourages us to recognize the importance of timing in our decisions, whether in business, personal life, or spiritual practices.
    • Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder to strive for greatness, wisdom, and connection with others, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
