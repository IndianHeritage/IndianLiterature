ऋशादसः सत्पतīm̐र अदब्धान महो राजाḥ सुवसनस्य दातृन् |यूनः सुख्षत्रान क्षयतो दिवो नृन आदित्यान याम्य अदितिं दुवोयु ||
riśādasaḥ satpatīm̐r adabdhān maho rājñaḥ suvasanasya dātṝn | yūnaḥ sukṣatrān kṣayato divo nṝn ādityān yāmy aditiṁ duvoyu ||
  • riśādasaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who are swift" or "the quick ones." In real life, this can refer to individuals or entities that are agile and responsive, such as successful businesses that adapt quickly to market changes.
  • satpatīm̐r: This means "the path of truth" or "the righteous path." In everyday life, this can be seen in ethical decision-making, where individuals choose honesty and integrity over deceit.
  • adabdhān: This word translates to "unharmed" or "untouched." It signifies resilience and the ability to withstand challenges, much like a person who remains unscathed through adversity.
  • maho rājñaḥ: This phrase means "of the great king." It can symbolize leadership and authority, akin to a CEO or a leader who inspires and guides their team towards success.
  • suvasanasya: This translates to "of the well-clad" or "well-dressed." It can represent the importance of presentation and how first impressions matter in social and professional settings.
  • dātṝn: This means "givers" or "donors." In real life, this can refer to philanthropists or individuals who contribute to society, emphasizing the value of generosity.
  • yūnaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who are united" or "together." It highlights the significance of teamwork and collaboration in achieving common goals.
  • sukṣatrān: This translates to "those who are well-armed" or "prepared." In a modern context, this can refer to individuals or organizations that are well-equipped to face challenges, such as having the right skills or resources.
  • kṣayato: This means "to destroy" or "to eliminate." It can symbolize the need to overcome obstacles or negative influences in life, similar to how a person might work to eliminate bad habits.
  • divo: This translates to "of the sky" or "heavenly." It can represent aspirations and high goals, akin to someone striving for excellence in their career or personal life.
  • nṝn: This means "of men" or "human beings." It emphasizes the human experience and the collective journey we share.
  • ādityān: This term refers to "the sons of Aditi," often associated with the sun and divine beings. It can symbolize enlightenment and knowledge, much like how education illuminates our understanding.
  • yāmy: This translates to "to the one who leads" or "the guide." It signifies the importance of mentorship and guidance in personal and professional development.
  • aditiṁ: This means "the boundless" or "infinite." It can represent limitless possibilities and the potential for growth and expansion in life.
  • duvoyu: This term can be interpreted as "to be united" or "to come together." It emphasizes the power of unity and collaboration in achieving success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of agility, righteousness, resilience, leadership, generosity, teamwork, preparedness, and the pursuit of high aspirations. Its significance lies in the reminder that in our personal and professional lives, we should strive to be quick in our actions, truthful in our dealings, resilient in the face of challenges, and united in our efforts. By embodying these principles, we can navigate the complexities of life more effectively and contribute positively to our communities.
