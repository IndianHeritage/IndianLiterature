न तस्य मायया चन रिपुर ईशीत मर्त्यः |यो अग्नये ददाश हव्यदातिभिः ||
na tasya māyayā cana ripur īśīta martyaḥ | yo agnaye dadāśa havyadātibhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na: not
    • tasya: of him
    • māyayā: by illusion
    • cana: at all
    • ripur: enemy
    • īśīta: can rule
    • martyaḥ: mortal
    • yo: who
    • agnaye: to Agni (the fire god)
    • dadāśa: has given
    • havyadātibhiḥ: with offerings
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes that no mortal can be ruled by an enemy through illusion.
    • It highlights the importance of divine connection, particularly through offerings to Agni, the fire god, who represents purity and transformation.
    • The phrase suggests that true power and protection come from spiritual practices and devotion rather than worldly fears or enemies.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace scenario, an employee may feel threatened by a rival. By focusing on their skills and maintaining integrity (akin to offering to Agni), they can rise above the competition without succumbing to negativity.
    • In personal relationships, individuals may face conflicts or misunderstandings. By practicing forgiveness and understanding (symbolic offerings), they can overcome these challenges and foster harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder that spiritual strength and integrity can protect us from external negativity and challenges.
    • It encourages individuals to seek a deeper connection with the divine through acts of devotion, which can lead to personal empowerment and resilience.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can help individuals navigate conflicts, fears, and challenges with grace and strength.
