वासोः इन्द्रं वसुपतिं गीरभिः गृणन्त ऋग्मियम् |होम गन्तारम् ऊतये ||
vasor indraṁ vasupatiṁ gīrbhir gṛṇanta ṛgmiyam | homa gantāram ūtaye ||
  • Vasor Indram: This phrase refers to the "Lord of Wealth" (Vasu) and "Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic tradition. Indra is often associated with rain, thunder, and the welfare of the community.
  • Vasupatiṁ: This term translates to "the lord of all beings" or "the master of wealth." It signifies a divine presence that governs prosperity and abundance.
  • Gīrbhir Gṛṇanta: This means "by singing praises" or "through hymns." It emphasizes the importance of devotion and the power of words in invoking divine blessings.
  • Ṛgmiyam: This refers to the "sacred verses" or "mantras" that are recited in worship. It highlights the significance of sacred texts in spiritual practices.
  • Homa Gantāram: This phrase indicates the act of offering (Homa) to the divine. It represents the ritualistic aspect of Vedic worship where offerings are made to please the gods.
  • Ūtaye: This translates to "for the purpose of prosperity" or "for the attainment of wealth." It signifies the ultimate goal of the rituals and prayers being performed.

Conclusion: The mantra "vasor indraṁ vasupatiṁ gīrbhir gṛṇanta ṛgmiyam | homa gantāram ūtaye ||" encapsulates the essence of invoking divine blessings for prosperity and abundance through hymns and rituals. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts such as during religious ceremonies, personal prayers for success, or community gatherings aimed at fostering unity and well-being. The significance lies in the belief that through sincere devotion and the recitation of sacred verses, individuals and communities can attract positive energies and blessings that lead to a prosperous life. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of spirituality, community, and the pursuit of wealth in a holistic manner.
