इषम् ऊर्जम् पवमानाभ्य अर्षसि श्येनो न वंसु कलशेषु सीदसि |इन्द्राय मद्वा मद्यो मदः सुतो दिवो विष्टम्भ उपमो विचक्षणः ||
iṣam ūrjam pavamānābhy arṣasi śyeno na vaṁsu kalaśeṣu sīdasi | indrāya madvā madyo madaḥ suto divo viṣṭambha upamo vicakṣaṇaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • iṣam: strength or power
    • ūrjam: energy or vigor
    • pavamānābhy: flowing or moving (often associated with wind or air)
    • arṣasi: you (Indra) are present or manifest
    • śyeno: like a falcon or eagle, symbolizing speed and sharpness
    • na: not
    • vaṁsu: in the waters or in the flow
    • kalaśeṣu: in the vessels or containers
    • sīdasi: you settle or reside
    • indrāya: to Indra, the king of gods
    • madvā: intoxication or bliss
    • madyo: the one who brings joy
    • madaḥ: ecstasy or exhilaration
    • suto: son or offspring
    • divo: of the sky or heaven
    • viṣṭambha: support or foundation
    • upamo: comparable or similar
    • vicakṣaṇaḥ: wise or perceptive
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the strength and energy of Indra, the king of gods, who is often associated with thunder, rain, and the power of nature.
    • The imagery of the falcon suggests swiftness and sharp perception, qualities that are essential for overcoming challenges.
    • The reference to settling in vessels indicates the importance of stability and nourishment, both physically and spiritually.
    • The mantra emphasizes the bliss and joy that come from divine connection and the support that the heavens provide.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal struggle, one might recite this mantra to invoke strength and clarity, much like a leader who draws upon their inner resources to guide their team through challenges.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can symbolize the collective strength and support that individuals provide to one another, akin to a team working together to achieve a common goal.
    • In nature, the power of wind and rain can be seen as a reminder of the forces that shape our environment, encouraging us to respect and harness these energies wisely.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, wisdom, and divine support in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek inner strength and clarity, while also recognizing the importance of community and divine guidance.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in moments of decision-making, leadership, and personal growth, reminding us to draw upon our inner resources and the support of those around us.
