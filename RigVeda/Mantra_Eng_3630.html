शुनं नः फालाः विगृहन्तु भूमिं शुनं कीनाशा अभि यन्तु वाहैः |शुनं पर्जन्यो मधुनā पयोभिः शुनासीराः शुनं अस्मासु धत्तम् ||
śunaṁ naḥ phālā vi kṛṣantu bhūmiṁ śunaṁ kīnāśā abhi yantu vāhaiḥ | śunam parjanyo madhunā payobhiḥ śunāsīrā śunam asmāsu dhattam ||
  • śunaṁ naḥ phālā vi kṛṣantu bhūmiṁ: This line can be translated to "May the fruits of the earth be favorable to us." Here, "śunaṁ" refers to something auspicious or good, "phālā" means fruits or results, and "bhūmiṁ" refers to the earth. This invokes a desire for prosperity and abundance from the land.
  • śunaṁ kīnāśā abhi yantu vāhaiḥ: This translates to "May the good things come to us through our efforts." "Kīnāśā" refers to efforts or endeavors, and "vāhaiḥ" means to carry or bring. This emphasizes the importance of hard work and the belief that good outcomes are a result of our actions.
  • śunam parjanyo madhunā payobhiḥ: This line means "May the rain bring us sweetness and nourishment." "Parjanyo" refers to rain or the rain god, "madhunā" means sweetness, and "payobhiḥ" refers to nourishment or sustenance. This highlights the significance of rain in agriculture and the sustenance it provides.
  • śunāsīrā śunam asmāsu dhattam: This translates to "May the good things be established in our lives." "Asmāsu" refers to our lives or existence, and "dhattam" means to establish or support. This line expresses a wish for stability and prosperity in one's life.

  • Real-Life Example: In agriculture, farmers often pray for good weather and fruitful harvests. This mantra reflects their hopes for a bountiful yield, emphasizing the connection between nature and human effort.
  • Significance: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking blessings for prosperity, hard work, and the importance of nature in our lives. It serves as a reminder that while we strive for success, we must also respect and nurture the environment that sustains us.
  • Application: This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as during agricultural rituals, community gatherings focused on sustainability, or personal reflections on the importance of hard work and gratitude for nature's bounty.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of human effort, nature, and the pursuit of prosperity. It serves as a guiding principle for individuals and communities to work diligently while remaining grateful for the resources provided by the earth. By embodying these values, we can foster a harmonious relationship with our environment and ensure a sustainable future.
