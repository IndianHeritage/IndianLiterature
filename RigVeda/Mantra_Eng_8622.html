परी त्यं हर्यतं हरिम बभ्रम पुनन्ति वारेण |यो देवान विश्वाम् इति परी मदेन सह गच्छति ||
pari tyaṁ haryataṁ harim babhrum punanti vāreṇa | yo devān viśvām̐ it pari madena saha gacchati ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May the one who is adorned with the golden hue, who purifies with the waters, and who moves with the divine beings, grant us strength and prosperity."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Pari tyaṁ: "May we be surrounded" or "May we be encompassed."
    • Haryataṁ: "The one who is golden" or "the one who shines."
    • Harim: "The divine" or "the one who is auspicious."
    • Babhrum: "Who is adorned" or "who is rich."
    • Punanti: "Purifies" or "cleanses."
    • Vāreṇa: "With the waters" or "through the medium of water."
    • Yo: "Who" or "the one who."
    • Devān: "Divine beings" or "gods."
    • Viśvām̐: "All" or "the universe."
    • It: "This" or "that."
    • Pari: "Around" or "surrounding."
    • Madena: "With joy" or "with delight."
    • Saha: "Together" or "along with."
    • Gacchati: "Moves" or "goes."
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra can be invoked during rituals for prosperity and well-being, such as during a housewarming ceremony where one seeks blessings for a new home.
    • In daily life, individuals can recite this mantra while meditating near water bodies, symbolizing purification and the flow of positive energy.
    • During community gatherings or festivals, this mantra can be chanted to invoke collective strength and harmony among participants.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine blessings in achieving prosperity and purity in life.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of individuals with the divine and the natural elements, such as water, which is essential for life.
    • The mantra serves as a reminder to seek strength and support from the universe and the divine beings around us.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal rituals to community events, where the focus is on seeking divine blessings for prosperity and purity.
    • By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of connection with the divine and the natural world, fostering a harmonious and prosperous life.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a mindset of gratitude and reverence for the forces that sustain and enrich our lives.
