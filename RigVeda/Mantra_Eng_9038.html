आयं घा स तुरो मद इन्द्रस्य वर्धत प्रियः |आयं कक्षीवत महो वि वो मदे मतीं विप्रस्य वर्धयद विवक्षसे ||
ayaṁ gha sa turo mada indrasya vardhata priyaḥ | ayaṁ kakṣīvato maho vi vo made matiṁ viprasya vardhayad vivakṣase ||
  • ayaṁ gha sa turo mada indrasya vardhata priyaḥ
    • ayaṁ - This word translates to "this" or "here." It indicates a presence or a focus on the current moment.
    • gha - This can mean "to bring" or "to lead." It suggests an action of bringing forth or leading something important.
    • sa - Meaning "he" or "that," it refers to a specific entity, in this case, likely Indra.
    • turo - This word means "swift" or "quick," indicating speed and efficiency.
    • mada - Translates to "joy" or "intoxication," often associated with divine ecstasy or bliss.
    • indrasya - Referring to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power and authority.
    • vardhata - This means "grows" or "increases," suggesting a sense of expansion or enhancement.
    • priyaḥ - Meaning "beloved" or "dear," it indicates something cherished or valued.
  • ayaṁ kakṣīvato maho vi vo made matiṁ viprasya vardhayad vivakṣase
    • ayaṁ - Again, "this" or "here," emphasizing the immediacy of the message.
    • kakṣīvato - This word can be interpreted as "of the wise" or "of the learned," referring to those who possess knowledge.
    • maho - Meaning "great" or "mighty," it signifies something of significant importance.
    • vi - This can mean "in" or "within," indicating a sense of inclusion or presence.
    • vo - Meaning "your," it personalizes the message, addressing the audience directly.
    • made - Referring to "joy" or "intoxication," similar to its earlier usage, emphasizing a state of bliss.
    • matiṁ - This translates to "intellect" or "thought," indicating the importance of wisdom and understanding.
    • viprasya - Meaning "of the wise" or "of the learned," reinforcing the theme of knowledge.
    • vardhayad - This means "increases" or "enhances," suggesting growth in wisdom or understanding.
    • vivakṣase - Meaning "you express" or "you articulate," indicating the act of communication or sharing knowledge.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of joy, wisdom, and the growth of intellect. It highlights the role of Indra as a symbol of power and authority, suggesting that true strength comes from knowledge and understanding. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In educational settings, where the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom is paramount.
  • In leadership, where effective communication and the ability to inspire others are crucial.
  • In personal development, where cultivating joy and a positive mindset can lead to greater achievements.

Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder to cherish knowledge, seek joy in our endeavors, and strive for personal and collective growth.
