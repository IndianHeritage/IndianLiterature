नकीं इन्द्रो निकर्तवे न शक्रः परिशक्तवे |विश्वं श्रिणोति पश्यति ||
nakīm indro nikartave na śakraḥ pariśaktave | viśvaṁ śṛṇoti paśyati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Nakīm: Not any
    • Indro: Indra (the king of gods)
    • Nikartave: To be made or created
    • Na: Not
    • Śakraḥ: Powerful or capable
    • Pariśaktave: To be fully capable
    • Viśvaṁ: All or the universe
    • Śṛṇoti: Hears or listens
    • Paśyati: Sees or observes
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects the omnipotence and omnipresence of Indra, the deity representing strength and power.
    • It suggests that there is nothing that Indra cannot create or accomplish, emphasizing his supreme capabilities.
    • The mantra also highlights the idea that Indra is aware of everything in the universe, as he hears and sees all.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a leadership context, a strong leader embodies the qualities of Indra, being capable of making significant decisions and being aware of the needs of their team.
    • In nature, the forces of nature can be seen as manifestations of Indra's power, where storms and rains are reminders of the strength and control that nature holds.
    • In personal development, individuals can strive to emulate Indra's qualities by being proactive, aware, and capable in their own lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the potential within each individual to harness their own power and capabilities.
    • It encourages us to be observant and aware of our surroundings, just as Indra is depicted to be.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to take charge of their lives, make impactful decisions, and remain mindful of the world around them.
