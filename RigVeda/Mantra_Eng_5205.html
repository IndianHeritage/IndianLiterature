आ देवो ददाति बुद्ध्यै वासूनि वैश्वानर उदीत सूर्यस्य |आ समुद्राद अवराद आ परस्माद आग्निर ददाति दिव आ पृथिव्याः ||
ā devo dade budhnyā3 vasūni vaiśvānara uditā sūryasya | ā samudrād avarād ā parasmād āgnir dade diva ā pṛthivyāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • devo: the divine
    • dade: gives
    • budhnyā: understanding or wisdom
    • vasūni: wealth or resources
    • vaiśvānara: universal or cosmic
    • uditā: arisen or emerged
    • sūryasya: of the sun
    • samudrād: from the ocean
    • avarād: from below
    • ā: from
    • parasmād: from beyond
    • āgnir: fire
    • dade: gives
    • diva: to the sky
    • ā: to
    • pṛthivyāḥ: of the earth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine giving wisdom and resources.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the cosmic elements (like the sun and fire) and the earthly realm.
    • The mention of the ocean signifies depth and the source of life, while the sun represents illumination and energy.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be seen as a reminder that success comes from understanding and utilizing resources wisely.
    • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek wisdom and enlightenment from various sources, including nature and the universe.
    • In environmental discussions, the reference to the ocean and earth highlights the importance of respecting and nurturing our natural resources.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic view of life, where divine wisdom and earthly resources are interconnected.
    • It teaches us to seek knowledge and understanding from the universe while being mindful of our responsibilities towards the earth.
    • This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, including personal growth, environmental stewardship, and ethical business practices.
