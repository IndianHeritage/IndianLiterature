अग्निः शुचिव्रततमः शुचिर् विप्रः शुचिः कविः |शुचī रोचत आहुतः ||
agniḥ śucivratatamaḥ śucir vipraḥ śuciḥ kaviḥ | śucī rocata āhutaḥ ||
  • Agniḥ: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • Śucivratatamaḥ: This term can be broken down into "śuci" meaning pure, and "vrata" meaning vow or commitment. Thus, it signifies the highest commitment to purity and righteousness.
  • Śucir: This word means "pure" or "clean." It emphasizes the importance of maintaining purity in thoughts, actions, and surroundings.
  • Vipraḥ: This refers to a learned or wise person, often a sage or priest. It highlights the value of knowledge and wisdom in guiding others.
  • Śuciḥ: Similar to "śucir," it reinforces the idea of purity, particularly in a spiritual or moral context.
  • Kaviḥ: This term means "poet" or "seer," indicating someone who has the ability to perceive deeper truths and express them creatively.
  • Śucī: Again, this word emphasizes purity, suggesting that purity is a recurring theme in this mantra.
  • Rocata: This means "to shine" or "to be radiant," indicating the positive outcomes of purity and wisdom.
  • Āhutaḥ: This term refers to something that is invoked or offered, often in the context of a ritual or prayer.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine fire (Agni) to bless us with purity, wisdom, and the ability to shine brightly in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of maintaining purity in our thoughts and actions, and the role of wise individuals in guiding society towards righteousness.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Development: Individuals can strive to embody the qualities of purity and wisdom in their daily lives, making conscious choices that reflect these values.
  • Community Leadership: Leaders and educators can take inspiration from the "vipraḥ" aspect, using their knowledge to uplift and guide others towards a more ethical and harmonious society.
  • Spiritual Practices: The mantra can be recited during meditation or rituals to invoke a sense of purity and clarity, helping individuals connect with their higher selves.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of purity, wisdom, and the transformative power of fire in our lives. By embodying these principles, we can create a positive impact on ourselves and those around us, fostering a more enlightened and harmonious existence.
