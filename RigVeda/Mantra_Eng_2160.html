स प्रवोळ्हृन् परिगत्यā दभीतेर विश्वम् अधाग आयुधम् इद्धे अग्नौ |सं गोभिर अश्वैर असृजद रथेभिः सोमस्य ता मद इन्द्रश चकार ||
sa pravoḻhṝn parigatyā dabhīter viśvam adhāg āyudham iddhe agnau | saṁ gobhir aśvair asṛjad rathebhiḥ somasya tā mada indraś cakāra ||
  • sa pravoḻhṝn parigatyā: This phrase can be interpreted as "he who is endowed with strength and vigor." In real life, this can refer to individuals who possess resilience and determination, such as athletes training for a competition or leaders guiding their teams through challenges.
  • dabhīter viśvam adhāg āyudham iddhe agnau: This translates to "the fearlessness that pervades the universe, igniting the weapons in the fire." This can be likened to the courage shown by firefighters or first responders who face danger head-on to protect others, embodying the spirit of fearlessness in the face of adversity.
  • saṁ gobhir aśvair asṛjad rathebhiḥ: This means "he created the world with the help of the cows and the horses." In a modern context, this can symbolize the importance of agriculture and transportation in building civilizations, where farmers and transporters work together to sustain communities.
  • somasya tā mada indraś cakāra: This translates to "Indra, the lord of the heavens, created the intoxication of the soma." Soma is often associated with vitality and divine inspiration. In contemporary life, this can be seen in the pursuit of knowledge and creativity, where individuals seek inspiration through education, art, and culture.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the themes of strength, fearlessness, collaboration, and divine inspiration. Its significance lies in the encouragement of individuals to embody these qualities in their daily lives. Whether it is through personal resilience, community support, or the pursuit of knowledge, the teachings of this mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, inspiring people to face challenges with courage and to work together for the greater good.
