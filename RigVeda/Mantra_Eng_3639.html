कन्या इव वहतुम एतवा उ अञ्ज्य अञ्जानां अभि चाकशीमि |यत्र सोमः सूयते यत्र यज्ञो घृतस्य धाराः अभि तत् पवन्ते ||
kanyā iva vahatum etavā u añjy añjānā abhi cākaśīmi | yatra somaḥ sūyate yatra yajño ghṛtasya dhārā abhi tat pavante ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kanyā: maiden or young girl
    • iva: like or as
    • vahatum: to carry or to bear
    • etavā: this much or to this extent
    • u: indeed or certainly
    • añjy: to anoint or to bless
    • añjānā: those who are blessed or anointed
    • abhi: towards or in the direction of
    • cākaśīmi: I shine or I illuminate
    • yatra: where
    • somaḥ: the moon or soma (a sacred drink)
    • sūyate: is produced or is generated
    • yatra: where
    • yajño: sacrifice or worship
    • ghṛtasya: of ghee or clarified butter
    • dhārā: flow or stream
    • abhi: towards
    • tat: that
    • pavante: flows or moves
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra draws a beautiful analogy between a maiden and the divine forces at play in the universe.
    • The maiden symbolizes purity, grace, and the potential for new beginnings, much like the fresh energy that comes with the moon's phases.
    • The act of carrying or bearing signifies the nurturing aspect of nature, where the divine supports and sustains life.
    • The mention of "soma" refers to the sacred drink that is believed to bestow immortality and divine inspiration, highlighting the importance of spiritual nourishment.
    • The flow of ghee represents the essence of offerings made during rituals, which are believed to attract divine blessings and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the maiden is often associated with new beginnings, such as the start of a new venture or the birth of a child, symbolizing hope and potential.
    • In modern rituals, the act of offering food or ghee in ceremonies can be seen as a way to connect with the divine and seek blessings for health, wealth, and happiness.
    • The concept of "soma" can be likened to the importance of spiritual practices in our lives, such as meditation or prayer, which nourish our inner selves and promote well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, nature, and the divine.
    • It serves as a reminder to honor the purity and grace within ourselves and the world around us.
    • By recognizing the significance of our actions and offerings, we can cultivate a deeper connection with the divine and manifest positive energies in our lives.
    • In essence, it encourages us to embrace our roles as nurturers and creators, fostering a harmonious existence with the universe.
