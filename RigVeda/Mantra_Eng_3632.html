वयं नाम प्र ब्रवामā घृतस्यास्मिन यज्ञे धारण्यामā नमोभिः |उप ब्रह्मा श्रिणवच शस्यमानं चतुःशृङ्गो 'वमीद गौर एतत् ||
vayaṁ nāma pra bravāmā ghṛtasyāsmin yajñe dhārayāmā namobhiḥ | upa brahmā śṛṇavac chasyamānaṁ catuḥśṛṅgo 'vamīd gaura etat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vayaṁ: We
    • nāma: name
    • pra: forth
    • bravāmā: we speak
    • ghṛtasyāsmin: in this ghee
    • yajñe: in the sacrifice
    • dhārayāmā: we hold
    • namobhiḥ: with reverence
    • upa: near
    • brahmā: the ultimate reality
    • śṛṇavac: listen
    • shasyamānaṁ: being cultivated
    • catuḥśṛṅgo: four-horned
    • 'vamīd: it has come forth
    • gaura: the golden
    • etat: this
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the collective identity and purpose of a community or group.
    • The reference to "ghee" signifies purity and nourishment, often used in rituals to invoke divine blessings.
    • The act of speaking forth a name in the context of a sacrifice indicates the importance of intention and reverence in spiritual practices.
    • The mention of "four-horned" could symbolize strength and stability, representing a powerful entity or force in nature.
    • The mantra encourages listening to the ultimate reality, suggesting that understanding and awareness are key to spiritual growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In community gatherings, such as weddings or festivals, people often come together to share their names and intentions, creating a sense of unity and purpose.
    • In cooking, the use of ghee is prevalent in many cultures, symbolizing the importance of purity and quality in what we consume, both physically and spiritually.
    • In modern spiritual practices, the act of setting intentions before rituals or meditations reflects the essence of this mantra, emphasizing the power of words and thoughts.
    • The concept of "four-horned" can be seen in various cultural symbols representing strength, such as the bull in Hinduism, which is revered for its power and stability.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of community, intention, and reverence in spiritual practices.
    • It serves as a reminder that our words and actions, especially in a collective setting, hold power and can invoke divine blessings.
    • In real life, it encourages us to approach our rituals, gatherings, and even daily interactions with mindfulness and respect, fostering a deeper connection with ourselves and the universe.
