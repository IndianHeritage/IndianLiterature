भोजं त्वां इन्द्र वयं हुवेम ददिष त्वं इन्द्रापांसि वाजान् | अविढ्धीन्द्र चित्रया न ऊती कृधि वृषन्न इन्द्र वस्यसो नः ||
bhojaṁ tvām indra vayaṁ huvema dadiṣ ṭvam indrāpāṁsi vājān | aviḍḍhīndra citrayā na ūtī kṛdhi vṛṣann indra vasyaso naḥ ||
  • Mantra: "bhojaṁ tvām indra vayaṁ huvema dadiṣ ṭvam indrāpāṁsi vājān | aviḍḍhīndra citrayā na ūtī kṛdhi vṛṣann indra vasyaso naḥ ||"
  • Translation: "We invoke you, Indra, to partake in our offerings; grant us your bountiful blessings. O Indra, with your diverse powers, may you bestow upon us the strength and prosperity we seek."

  • Explanation of Key Terms:
    • Bhojaṁ: Refers to offerings or food, symbolizing sustenance and nourishment.
    • Indra: The king of gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength, power, and the forces of nature.
    • Huvema: A call to invoke or summon, indicating a desire for connection and communication with the divine.
    • Dadiṣ: To grant or bestow, emphasizing the act of giving blessings or favors.
    • Vājān: Refers to strength or vigor, often associated with wealth and prosperity.
    • Aviḍḍhīndra: Suggests the diverse or multifaceted nature of Indra's powers.
    • Citrayā: Meaning 'varied' or 'colorful', indicating the richness of life and experiences.
    • Vṛṣann: Signifying abundance or fertility, often linked to agricultural prosperity.
    • Vasyaso: Refers to being under the protection or favor of Indra.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, invoking Indra is common during rituals for rain and fertility, as he is associated with thunderstorms and rain, essential for crops.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during times of need, seeking strength and support in overcoming challenges, much like how one might turn to a mentor or leader for guidance.
    • Businesses may use the essence of this mantra in their mission statements, emphasizing the importance of community support and the pursuit of prosperity through collective effort.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support and blessings in various aspects of life. It highlights the importance of community, strength, and the pursuit of prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in agricultural practices, personal challenges, and even in business endeavors, reminding us of the interconnectedness of our efforts and the divine forces that support us. By invoking Indra, we acknowledge the need for strength and abundance in our lives, fostering a sense of gratitude and purpose in our actions.
