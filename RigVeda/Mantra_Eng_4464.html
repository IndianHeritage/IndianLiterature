त्वं भागो न आ हि रत्नम् इषे परिज्मेव क्षयसि दसमवर्चाः | अग्नि मित्रो न बृहता ऋतस्यासि क्षत्तā वामस्य देव भूरेः ||
tvam bhago na ā hi ratnam iṣe parijmeva kṣayasi dasmavarcāḥ | agne mitro na bṛhata ṛtasyāsi kṣattā vāmasya deva bhūreḥ ||
  • tvam bhago na ā hi ratnam iṣe parijmeva kṣayasi dasmavarcāḥ
    • tvam bhago: "You are the divine wealth." This phrase acknowledges the presence of divine abundance and prosperity in our lives.
    • na ā hi: "Do not indeed." This suggests a plea or a request for the divine to not withhold blessings.
    • ratnam: "jewel" or "precious thing." This symbolizes the valuable aspects of life, such as knowledge, health, and relationships.
    • iṣe: "in the ruler" or "in the lord." This indicates a connection to a higher power or authority.
    • parijmeva: "as if you are." This phrase implies a sense of identification or unity with the divine.
    • kṣayasi: "you destroy" or "you remove." This refers to the divine's ability to eliminate obstacles or negativity.
    • dasmavarcāḥ: "of the tenfold radiance." This can be interpreted as the multifaceted blessings that come from the divine.
  • agne mitro na bṛhata ṛtasyāsi kṣattā vāmasya deva bhūreḥ
    • agne: "O Agni," referring to the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • mitro: "friend." This indicates a relationship of trust and support with the divine.
    • na bṛhata: "not great." This suggests humility and the recognition that greatness comes from the divine.
    • ṛtasyāsi: "you are of truth." This emphasizes the importance of truth and righteousness in life.
    • kṣattā: "you are the protector." This highlights the divine's role as a guardian and supporter.
    • vāmasya: "of the left." This can symbolize the nurturing and protective aspects of the divine.
    • deva bhūreḥ: "O abundant god." This acknowledges the richness and generosity of the divine.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the relationship between humans and the divine, highlighting themes of abundance, protection, and truth. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Seeking guidance and support during challenging times, trusting that the divine will provide the necessary resources.
  • Recognizing the importance of truth and righteousness in personal and professional relationships, fostering a sense of integrity.
  • Understanding that true wealth is not just material but encompasses health, knowledge, and meaningful connections.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate a relationship with the divine, embrace abundance, and live a life rooted in truth and righteousness.
