यः समिधा य आहुतī यो वेदेन ददाश मार्तो अग्नये |यो नमसā स्वध्वरः ||
yaḥ samidhā ya āhutī yo vedena dadāśa marto agnaye | yo namasā svadhvaraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaḥ: who
    • samidhā: with the offerings
    • ya: who
    • āhutī: the invocation or offering
    • yo: who
    • vedena: by the knowledge (Veda)
    • dadāśa: has given
    • marto: the mortal
    • agnaye: to the fire (Agni)
    • yo: who
    • namasā: with reverence
    • svadhvaraḥ: the one who is self-illuminated or self-manifested
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine fire, Agni, which is central to Vedic rituals.
    • It emphasizes the importance of offerings and invocations made with reverence.
    • The phrase "who has given by the knowledge" suggests that true understanding and wisdom come from the Vedas, which guide human actions and offerings.
    • The term "mortal" indicates the human condition, highlighting the relationship between humans and the divine.
    • Agni is not just a physical fire but symbolizes transformation, purification, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of offering can be seen in various forms, such as charity, community service, or even simple acts of kindness.
    • The invocation of Agni can be likened to the way we seek guidance and inspiration in our daily lives, whether through spiritual practices, meditation, or philosophical study.
    • Understanding the importance of knowledge (Veda) can be applied in education, where learning is seen as a sacred offering to the self and society.
    • In personal development, the idea of being "self-illuminated" encourages individuals to seek their inner truth and wisdom, fostering self-awareness and growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the profound connection between the divine and the mortal, emphasizing the importance of reverence, knowledge, and offerings in our lives.
    • It teaches us that through our actions, guided by wisdom and respect, we can connect with the divine and transform our lives.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages us to recognize the sacredness in our daily actions and the importance of seeking knowledge and understanding in our journey through life.
