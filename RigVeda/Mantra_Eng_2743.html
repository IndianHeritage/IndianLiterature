त्वं अपो यद ध वृत्रं जगहन्वां अति्याम् इव प्रासृजः सर्तवाजौ | शयानम् इन्द्र चरता वधेन वव्रिवांसम् परि देवीर अदेवम् ||
tvam apo yad dha vṛtraṁ jaghanvām̐ atyām̐ iva prāsṛjaḥ sartavājau | śayānam indra caratā vadhena vavrivāṁsam pari devīr adevam ||
  • tvam - This word translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity, often interpreted as Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
  • apo - Meaning "from the waters," this word signifies the importance of water in life and its purifying qualities.
  • yad - Translates to "that which," referring to a specific action or quality that is being highlighted.
  • dha - This means "to place" or "to set," indicating the act of establishing something significant.
  • vṛtraṁ - Refers to "Vritra," a serpent or dragon-like demon representing drought and obstruction, symbolizing challenges or obstacles in life.
  • jaghanvām̐ - This can be interpreted as "having defeated" or "overcoming," suggesting the triumph over difficulties.
  • atyām̐ - Meaning "beyond," it implies transcending limitations or boundaries.
  • iva - Translates to "like" or "as," drawing a comparison to something else.
  • prāsṛjaḥ - Meaning "to release" or "to let go," indicating the act of freeing oneself from burdens.
  • sartavājau - Refers to "the two who are moving," possibly indicating the duality of existence or the balance of forces in life.
  • śayānam - Meaning "lying down," which can symbolize rest, contemplation, or a state of being at peace.
  • indra - The name of the deity, representing strength, power, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • caratā - Meaning "moving" or "acting," indicating the dynamic nature of life and the need for action.
  • vadhena - Translates to "by killing" or "through destruction," suggesting the necessity of removing obstacles to achieve success.
  • vavrivāṁsam - Meaning "having taken," indicating the act of seizing opportunities or resources.
  • pari - Meaning "around" or "surrounding," suggesting a holistic approach to life and challenges.
  • devīr - Refers to "goddesses," symbolizing divine feminine energy and support.
  • adevam - Meaning "not divine" or "mundane," indicating the contrast between the divine and the earthly realm.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of overcoming obstacles and challenges in life, much like how water nourishes and sustains life. It emphasizes the importance of divine support in our endeavors and the need to act decisively to remove barriers. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal struggles, professional challenges, or any situation where one feels hindered. By invoking the strength of Indra and the purifying qualities of water, individuals can find the courage to confront their Vritra-like challenges, ultimately leading to growth and success.
