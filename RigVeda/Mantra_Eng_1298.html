मध्वः सोमस्याश्विनां मदाया प्रात्नो होता विवासते वाम् |बरहिष्मती रातिर विश्रिता गीर इषा यातं नासत्योप वाजैः ||
madhvaḥ somasyāśvinā madāya pratno hotā vivāsate vām | barhiṣmatī rātir viśritā gīr iṣā yātaṁ nāsatyopa vājaiḥ ||
  • madhvaḥ: This word can be interpreted as "sweet" or "pleasant." In a real-life context, it can refer to the sweetness of relationships, experiences, or even the joy found in nature.
  • somasyāśvinā: This refers to the "two Ashvins," who are divine twin horsemen in Vedic mythology, symbolizing health and vitality. They represent the duality of life, such as day and night, joy and sorrow, and can be seen in real life as the balance we seek in our own lives.
  • madāya: This word means "for the intoxication" or "for the delight." It signifies the pursuit of joy and fulfillment in life, akin to seeking happiness through various experiences.
  • pratno: This translates to "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is called upon." In practical terms, it can refer to the importance of seeking guidance or support from mentors, friends, or spiritual figures in our lives.
  • hotā: This means "the priest" or "the one who offers." It emphasizes the role of individuals who facilitate connections, whether in spiritual rituals or in everyday life, such as community leaders or family members who bring people together.
  • vivāsate: This word means "to shine" or "to illuminate." It can be related to the idea of enlightenment or gaining knowledge, which is essential in our personal and professional growth.
  • vām: This translates to "your" or "of you," indicating a personal connection or relationship. It highlights the importance of interpersonal relationships in our lives.
  • barhiṣmatī: This word refers to "the one who is adorned with grass," often associated with sacred rituals. It symbolizes purity and the sanctity of the environment, reminding us to respect nature and our surroundings.
  • rātir: This means "delight" or "joy." It signifies the importance of finding happiness in our daily lives, whether through simple pleasures or significant achievements.
  • viśritā: This translates to "well-established" or "settled." It can refer to the stability we seek in our lives, whether in our careers, relationships, or personal goals.
  • gīr: This word means "speech" or "song." It emphasizes the power of communication and expression in our lives, highlighting how sharing our thoughts and feelings can strengthen bonds with others.
  • iṣā: This translates to "power" or "authority." It signifies the strength we derive from our beliefs, values, and the support of our community.
  • yātaṁ: This means "to go" or "to move." It can symbolize the journey of life, emphasizing the importance of progress and evolution in our personal and professional paths.
  • nāsatyopa: This refers to "the Nāsatyas," the twin deities associated with truth and health. They remind us of the importance of honesty and integrity in our lives.
  • vājaiḥ: This word means "with strength" or "with vigor." It signifies the importance of resilience and determination in overcoming challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking joy, balance, and enlightenment in life. It emphasizes the importance of relationships, communication, and the pursuit of happiness while reminding us to respect nature and uphold integrity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, community building, and the pursuit of a fulfilling life, encouraging individuals to seek support, express themselves, and strive for a harmonious existence.
