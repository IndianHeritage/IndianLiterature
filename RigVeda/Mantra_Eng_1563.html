पुषण्वते मरुत्वते विश्वदेवाय वायवे |स्वाहा गायत्रवेपसे हव्यम इन्द्राय कर्तन ||
pūṣaṇvate marutvate viśvadevāya vāyave | svāhā gāyatravepase havyam indrāya kartana ||
  • pūṣaṇvate: This term refers to the divine aspect of nourishment and sustenance. In real life, it can be related to the importance of food and resources that sustain life. For example, farmers who cultivate crops are embodying the essence of Pūṣaṇ, as they provide nourishment to society.
  • marutvate: This word signifies the power of the Maruts, the storm deities associated with wind and strength. In a contemporary context, this can be likened to the forces of nature that can both create and destroy. For instance, strong winds can lead to the growth of plants by dispersing seeds, but they can also cause destruction during storms.
  • viśvadevāya: This term translates to "to all the gods" or "universal deities." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all divine forces. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way people from different cultures and religions come together to celebrate common values, such as peace and harmony.
  • vāyave: This refers to the wind or air, which is essential for life. In practical terms, it can be related to the importance of clean air and environmental conservation. For example, initiatives to reduce pollution and promote clean energy sources reflect the reverence for Vāyu, the wind deity.
  • svāhā: This is an invocation often used in Vedic rituals, signifying the offering made to the divine. In modern life, it can be seen in the act of gratitude, where individuals express thanks for the blessings they receive, whether through prayer, meditation, or simple acknowledgment of life's gifts.
  • gāyatravepase: This term relates to the Gayatri mantra, which is a powerful invocation for enlightenment and wisdom. In real life, it can be associated with the pursuit of knowledge and understanding, such as students striving for academic excellence or individuals seeking personal growth through education.
  • havyam: This refers to offerings made to the gods, symbolizing the act of giving and sharing. In contemporary society, this can be reflected in charitable acts, where individuals or organizations contribute to the welfare of others, embodying the spirit of Havyam.
  • indrāya: This term is associated with Indra, the king of the gods and the deity of rain and thunderstorms. In real life, it can symbolize leadership and the importance of strong governance. For example, effective leaders who bring prosperity and stability to their communities can be seen as embodying the qualities of Indra.
  • kartana: This word means "to do" or "to act." It emphasizes the importance of action in achieving goals. In everyday life, this can be related to the idea that success comes from hard work and determination, such as athletes training rigorously to achieve their dreams.

Conclusion: The mantra "pūṣaṇvate marutvate viśvadevāya vāyave | svāhā gāyatravepase havyam indrāya kartana ||" encapsulates the essence of nourishment, strength, interconnectedness, and the importance of action in life. Its significance lies in the recognition of the divine forces that sustain and guide us, encouraging individuals to act with purpose and gratitude. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire people to appreciate the resources they have, work towards environmental sustainability, foster community spirit, and strive for personal and collective growth. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a harmonious and prosperous society.
