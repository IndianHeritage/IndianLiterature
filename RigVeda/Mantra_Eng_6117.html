मन्त्र: कदā वां तौग्र्यो विधत समुद्रे जहितो नराः | यद वां रथो विभिष पतात ||
kadā vāṁ taugryo vidhat samudre jahito narā | yad vāṁ ratho vibhiṣ patāt ||
  • Mantra: "kadā vāṁ taugryo vidhat samudre jahito narā | yad vāṁ ratho vibhiṣ patāt ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kadā: When
    • vāṁ: you (plural)
    • taugryo: fierce or powerful
    • vidhat: to create or establish
    • samudre: in the ocean or sea
    • jahito: having been released or set free
    • narā: men or people
    • yad: when
    • vāṁ: you (plural)
    • ratho: chariot
    • vibhiṣ: fear or danger
    • patāt: falls or descends
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a question about the time when powerful forces are established in the ocean, and when men are released from their fears as they face challenges.
    • The imagery of the ocean can symbolize the vast and unpredictable nature of life, while the chariot represents the journey we undertake.
    • The mention of fear suggests that there are obstacles and dangers that we must confront in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when a team faces a challenging project (the ocean), they must harness their collective strength (the powerful forces) to overcome obstacles (the fears) and achieve success (the journey in the chariot).
    • In personal life, individuals may encounter fears related to health, relationships, or career changes. The mantra encourages them to confront these fears and establish a path forward.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of courage and collective strength in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that while fears may arise, it is essential to confront them and establish a path forward, much like navigating through the vast ocean.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals and teams to work together, face their fears, and create a successful journey, whether in personal or professional contexts.
