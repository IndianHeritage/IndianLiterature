तं इति सखित्वा इमहे तं राये तं सुवीर्ये |स शक्र उता नः शकद इन्द्रो वसु दयमानः ||
tam it sakhitva īmahe taṁ rāye taṁ suvīrye | sa śakra uta naḥ śakad indro vasu dayamānaḥ ||
  • tam it sakhitva īmahe: This phrase can be translated as "We meditate upon that (divine) together." The word "sakhitva" implies a sense of companionship or unity in the act of meditation. In real life, this can be likened to the importance of community and collective effort in achieving goals, such as team sports or group projects where collaboration leads to success.
  • taṁ rāye: This translates to "that (divine) for wealth." Here, "rāye" refers to prosperity or wealth. In a modern context, this can be seen in the pursuit of financial stability and abundance, where individuals or communities strive for economic growth through hard work and ethical practices.
  • taṁ suvīrye: This means "that (divine) for strength." "Suvīrye" refers to valor or strength, both physical and moral. In everyday life, this can be exemplified by individuals who demonstrate courage in the face of adversity, such as activists fighting for social justice or healthcare workers during a crisis.
  • sa śakra uta naḥ: This translates to "may that (divine) be powerful for us." The term "śakra" denotes power or might. This can be related to the idea of seeking divine support or inspiration in our endeavors, similar to how people often pray or seek guidance in challenging situations, such as during exams or important life decisions.
  • śakad indro vasu dayamānaḥ: This phrase means "Indra, the giver of wealth, may he grant us." "Indra" is a deity associated with rain and thunder, symbolizing abundance and prosperity. In real life, this can be seen in the way communities come together to support each other during difficult times, such as fundraising for those in need or collective efforts to improve local infrastructure.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, prosperity, strength, and divine support in our lives. It encourages individuals and communities to work together towards common goals, seek abundance, and draw strength from one another. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork in professional settings, community service, and personal growth through collective efforts. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that we are stronger together and that seeking divine guidance can lead to greater achievements and fulfillment in life.
