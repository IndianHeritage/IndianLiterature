दासपत्नीः अहिगोपा अतिष्ठन् निरुद्धा आपः पाणिनेव गावाḥ |अपाम बिलं अपिहितं यद आसीद वृतं जगहन्वाम् अप तद ववारा ||
dāsapatnīr ahigopā atiṣṭhan niruddhā āpaḥ paṇineva gāvaḥ | apām bilam apihitaṁ yad āsīd vṛtraṁ jaghanvām̐ apa tad vavāra ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dāsapatnīr: The wives of the servants or the maidens.
    • ahigopā: The serpent or the protector of the cows.
    • atiṣṭhan: Stood firm or remained steadfast.
    • niruddhā: Restrained or held back.
    • āpaḥ: Waters or the essence of life.
    • paṇineva: Like the hands or with the hands.
    • gāvaḥ: Cows or the symbols of wealth and nourishment.
    • apām: Of the waters.
    • bilam: The hole or the hiding place.
    • apihitaṁ: Hidden or concealed.
    • yad: That which.
    • āsīd: Was or existed.
    • vṛtraṁ: The obstacle or the demon.
    • jaghanvām̐: We shall strike down or defeat.
    • apa: Away or from.
    • tad: That.
    • vavāra: Shone forth or revealed.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the strength and resilience of the divine forces that protect and nurture life.
    • The "wives of the servants" can symbolize the nurturing aspects of nature, while "ahigopā" represents the protective forces that guard these aspects.
    • The phrase "atiṣṭhan niruddhā" indicates a steadfastness in the face of challenges, suggesting that even when faced with obstacles, there is a divine presence that remains firm.
    • The waters mentioned symbolize the essence of life, which is often hidden or concealed, much like the potential within each individual or situation.
    • The reference to "vṛtraṁ" signifies the obstacles or demons that one must confront in life, and the mantra emphasizes the importance of overcoming these challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal struggles, such as overcoming addiction or mental health issues, individuals often find strength in their support systems, akin to the "wives of the servants" providing nurturing support.
    • In environmental conservation, the protective forces of nature can be likened to "ahigopā," where efforts to safeguard ecosystems reflect the divine guardianship over life.
    • In professional settings, facing competition or challenges can be seen as confronting "vṛtraṁ," where resilience and teamwork help in overcoming these obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of resilience, protection, and the nurturing aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder that while obstacles may arise, there are divine forces and support systems that help individuals navigate through challenges.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek strength in community, nature, and their inner resilience, ultimately leading to personal growth and overcoming adversities.
