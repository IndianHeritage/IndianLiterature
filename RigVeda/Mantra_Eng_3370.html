मन्त्र: विश्वे चनेद अनā त्वा देवास इन्द्र युयुधुः |यद अहा नक्तम् आतिरः ||
viśve caned anā tvā devāsa indra yuyudhuḥ | yad ahā naktam ātiraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viśve: all, universal
    • caned: may they be pleased
    • anā: without
    • tvā: you
    • devāsa: O gods
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • yuyudhuḥ: fought, engaged in battle
    • yad: whatever
    • ahā: we have
    • naktam: night
    • ātiraḥ: beyond, transcending
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to the universal gods, asking them to be pleased and to engage in battle on behalf of the devotee. It reflects a desire for divine support and protection, especially during challenging times, such as the night, which symbolizes uncertainty and fear.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Seeking Guidance: Just as a student may pray for guidance before an important exam, this mantra can be recited to seek divine assistance in overcoming challenges.
    • Facing Difficulties: When someone is going through a tough phase in life, such as a job loss or personal crisis, invoking the support of the divine can provide comfort and strength.
    • Community Support: In times of natural disasters, communities often come together to pray for safety and recovery, reflecting the collective invocation of divine powers for protection.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine intervention and support in times of need. It emphasizes the importance of faith and the belief that higher powers can assist individuals in overcoming obstacles. In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as personal struggles, community challenges, or even during significant life events, reminding us of the universal support available to us when we seek it.
