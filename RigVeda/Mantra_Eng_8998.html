त्वां यज्ञेषु ईळतेऽग्ने प्रयत्य अध्वरे |त्वं वसूनि काम्या वि वो मदे विश्व दधासि दाशुषे विवक्षसे ||
tvāṁ yajñeṣv īḻate 'gne prayaty adhvare | tvaṁ vasūni kāmyā vi vo made viśvā dadhāsi dāśuṣe vivakṣase ||
  • tvāṁ - This word translates to "you," referring to the divine or the sacred fire (Agni) in this context.
  • yajñeṣv - This means "in the sacrifices," indicating the rituals and offerings made to the deities.
  • īḻate - This translates to "is invoked" or "is worshipped," suggesting the act of calling upon the divine presence.
  • 'gne - This is a direct reference to "Agni," the fire god, who is central to Vedic rituals.
  • prayaty - This means "strives" or "makes an effort," indicating the dedication and intention behind the offerings.
  • adhvare - This refers to "the sacrifice" or "the ritual," emphasizing the importance of the ceremonial act.
  • tvaṁ - Again, this refers to "you," reinforcing the connection to Agni.
  • vasūni - This translates to "the good things" or "prosperities," indicating the blessings sought from the divine.
  • kāmyā - This means "desired" or "wishful," highlighting the intentions behind the offerings.
  • vi - This is a preposition meaning "in" or "among," used to connect ideas.
  • vo - This translates to "your," referring to the collective audience or worshippers.
  • made - This means "joy" or "delight," indicating the emotional aspect of the worship.
  • viśvā - This translates to "all" or "universal," suggesting inclusivity in the blessings sought.
  • dadhāsi - This means "you bestow" or "you give," indicating the act of granting blessings.
  • dāśuṣe - This translates to "to the worshipper" or "to the devotee," emphasizing the relationship between the divine and the devotee.
  • vivakṣase - This means "you express" or "you declare," indicating the communication of divine will or blessings.

The mantra essentially invokes Agni, the fire god, as a central figure in Vedic rituals. It emphasizes the importance of sacrifices and the intention behind them, highlighting the desire for prosperity and joy. The worshippers express their devotion and seek blessings for their efforts in the rituals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: During significant life events like weddings, housewarming, or festivals, people perform rituals to invoke blessings for prosperity and happiness.
  • Personal Goals: Individuals can use the essence of this mantra to focus their intentions when striving for personal or professional goals, recognizing the effort and dedication required.
  • Community Gatherings: In communal events, the collective invocation of blessings can foster a sense of unity and shared purpose among participants.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the deep connection between the divine and the devotee, emphasizing the importance of intention, effort, and the desire for blessings in both personal and communal aspects of life. It serves as a reminder of the sacredness of rituals and the universal quest for prosperity and joy.
