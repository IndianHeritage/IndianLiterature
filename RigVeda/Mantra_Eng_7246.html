यो व्यतींर अपहानयत सुयुक्तां उप दाशुषे |तक्वो नेताऽतिद वपुर उपमा यो अमुच्यत ||
yo vyatīm̐r aphāṇayat suyuktām̐ upa dāśuṣe | takvo netā tad id vapur upamā yo amucyata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • vyatīm̐r: has transcended
    • aphāṇayat: has spoken or declared
    • suyuktām̐: well-joined or well-connected
    • upa: towards
    • dāśuṣe: the one who is worthy or deserving
    • takvo: the one who has given up
    • netā: leader or guide
    • tad: that
    • id: this
    • vapur: body or form
    • upamā: comparison or analogy
    • yo: who
    • amucyata: has been freed or liberated
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a person who has transcended worldly limitations and has declared their understanding of the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • The phrase "well-joined" suggests harmony and unity, indicating that the individual recognizes the importance of relationships and connections in life.
    • By addressing the "one who is worthy," it emphasizes the value of deserving respect and recognition through one's actions and character.
    • The leader mentioned here is someone who has given up personal desires for the greater good, embodying selflessness and altruism.
    • The reference to "this body" and "comparison" implies that the physical form is transient, and true liberation comes from understanding one's essence beyond the material.
    • Ultimately, the mantra highlights the journey of self-realization and the importance of leading a life that is in harmony with others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A community leader who prioritizes the needs of the community over personal gain exemplifies the qualities of the "worthy one" in this mantra.
    • In a workplace, a manager who fosters teamwork and collaboration, ensuring that all voices are heard, embodies the idea of being "well-joined."
    • Individuals who practice mindfulness and selflessness, such as volunteers in humanitarian efforts, reflect the essence of transcending personal desires for the benefit of others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of selflessness, interconnectedness, and the pursuit of higher understanding in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to strive for personal growth while recognizing their role in the larger community.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more harmonious relationships, effective leadership, and a deeper sense of purpose.
