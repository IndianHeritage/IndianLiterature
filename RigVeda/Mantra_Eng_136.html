आ त्वा कण्वा अहूषत गृणन्ति विप्र ते धियः |देवेभिर अग्न आ गहि ||
ā tvā kaṇvā ahūṣata gṛṇanti vipra te dhiyaḥ | devebhir agna ā gahi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from, out of
    • tvā: you
    • kaṇvā: the wise ones, the learned
    • ahūṣata: have called, have invoked
    • gṛṇanti: sing, chant
    • vipra: the learned, the wise
    • te: your
    • dhiyaḥ: thoughts, intellects
    • devebhir: by the gods
    • agna: O Agni (the fire god)
    • ā: to, towards
    • gahi: take, accept
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, expressing the invocation of wisdom and intellect. The learned individuals (vipra) call upon Agni, asking him to accept their thoughts and intentions. It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and the divine guidance that comes from the gods.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be related to the pursuit of knowledge in educational institutions. Just as the wise invoke Agni for guidance, students seek the wisdom of their teachers and mentors to illuminate their paths.
    • In corporate settings, leaders often invoke the spirit of collaboration and innovation, akin to calling upon Agni, to inspire their teams to think creatively and work towards common goals.
    • During rituals and ceremonies, individuals may chant this mantra to seek blessings for clarity of thought and purpose, similar to how people today pray for guidance in making important life decisions.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine wisdom and the importance of intellectual pursuits. It serves as a reminder that in our quest for knowledge and understanding, we should seek guidance from higher powers, whether through prayer, meditation, or the wisdom of those around us. In real life, this mantra encourages individuals to value learning, seek enlightenment, and foster a connection with the divine to enhance their intellectual and spiritual journeys.
