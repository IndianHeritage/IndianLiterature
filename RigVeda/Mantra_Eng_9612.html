युṣ्माकं बुद्धने अपां न यामनि विथुर्यति न मही श्रथर्यति |विश्वाप्सुर यज्ञो अर्वाग अयं सु वः प्रयस्वन्तो न सत्राच आगत ||
yuṣmākam budhne apāṁ na yāmani vithuryati na mahī śratharyati | viśvapsur yajño arvāg ayaṁ su vaḥ prayasvanto na satrāca ā gata ||
  • yuṣmākam - This word translates to "your" or "of you," indicating a connection or relationship with the audience or the divine.
  • budhne - This means "in the ocean" or "in the depths," symbolizing vastness and depth, often associated with knowledge or consciousness.
  • apāṁ - This translates to "of waters," referring to the life-giving and purifying qualities of water.
  • na yāmani - This phrase means "do not wander" or "do not stray," suggesting a call for focus and direction.
  • vithuryati - This can be interpreted as "to be prosperous" or "to thrive," indicating a desire for abundance and success.
  • na mahī - This means "not the earth," which can imply a distinction between material and spiritual realms.
  • śratharyati - This translates to "to be strong" or "to be firm," emphasizing resilience and stability.
  • viśvapsur - This word means "universal" or "all-encompassing," suggesting a connection to the greater cosmos.
  • yajño - This refers to "sacrifice" or "offering," a central theme in Vedic rituals that signifies devotion and the act of giving.
  • arvāg - This translates to "from below" or "from the depths," indicating a source of strength or wisdom that comes from within.
  • ayaṁ - This means "this," pointing to the present moment or the current situation.
  • su vaḥ - This translates to "good for you," indicating a positive outcome or blessing.
  • prayasvanto - This means "those who strive" or "the diligent," emphasizing the importance of effort and hard work.
  • na satrāca - This translates to "not from the enemies," suggesting a focus on positive relationships and alliances.
  • ā gata - This means "has come" or "arrived," indicating the manifestation of desires or goals.

The mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of individuals with the universe, emphasizing the importance of focus, effort, and positive relationships. It highlights the significance of striving for prosperity and strength while remaining grounded in one's values and purpose.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to stay focused on their goals and work diligently towards them, much like a student preparing for exams.
  • In professional settings, it can serve as a reminder to build positive relationships with colleagues and to strive for collective success rather than competition.
  • In spiritual practices, it emphasizes the importance of inner strength and the pursuit of knowledge, akin to a seeker on a path of enlightenment.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of focus, effort, and positive relationships in achieving prosperity and strength in life. It encourages individuals to connect with the deeper aspects of their existence while striving for success in their endeavors.
