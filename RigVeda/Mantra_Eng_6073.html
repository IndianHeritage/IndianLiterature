यसमाऽन्ये दश प्रति धुरं वहन्ति वहनयः |अस्तं वयो न तुग्र्यम् ||
yasmā anye daśa prati dhuraṁ vahanti vahnayaḥ | astaṁ vayo na tugryam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasmā: Because of which
    • anye: Others
    • daśa: Ten
    • prati: Against
    • dhuraṁ: Burden
    • vahanti: Carry
    • vahnayaḥ: Fire (or flames)
    • astaṁ: Setting (or going down)
    • vayo: Age
    • na: Not
    • tugryam: To be overcome
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the strength and resilience of fire, symbolizing energy and transformation.
    • The phrase "yasmā anye daśa prati dhuraṁ vahanti vahnayaḥ" suggests that while others may carry burdens, the fire remains unyielding and powerful.
    • The latter part, "astaṁ vayo na tugryam," implies that even as time passes and ages change, the essence of fire does not diminish or succumb to challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader may face numerous challenges (the burdens) while guiding their team (the fire) through difficult times. Just as fire transforms materials, a good leader transforms challenges into opportunities.
    • In personal life, individuals often face various burdens such as stress, responsibilities, and societal expectations. However, like fire, one can harness their inner strength to overcome these challenges and emerge stronger.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of resilience and strength in the face of adversity.
    • It serves as a reminder that, like fire, we can carry our burdens and not be overcome by the passage of time or challenges.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to embrace their inner strength, face challenges head-on, and transform obstacles into opportunities for growth.
