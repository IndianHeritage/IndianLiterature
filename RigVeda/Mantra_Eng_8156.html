आयुक्त सूर एतशम् पवमानो मनाव अधि |अन्तरिक्षेण यातवे ||
ayukta sūra etaśam pavamāno manāv adhi | antarikṣeṇa yātave ||
  • Mantra: "ayukta sūra etaśam pavamāno manāv adhi | antarikṣeṇa yātave ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ayukta: unrestrained or uncontrolled
    • sūra: hero or strong one
    • etaśam: this one or this being
    • pavamāno: moving or flowing
    • manāv: mind or intellect
    • adhi: above or over
    • antarikṣeṇa: in the atmosphere or space
    • yātave: to the one who travels or moves
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful force that is unrestrained and flows through the atmosphere.
    • The term "sūra" indicates strength and valor, suggesting that this force is not just physical but also mental and spiritual.
    • The phrase "pavamāno manāv adhi" implies that this force influences the mind, guiding thoughts and actions.
    • In a real-life context, this can be likened to the influence of inspiration or motivation that propels individuals to achieve their goals.
    • For example, an athlete preparing for a competition may feel a surge of energy and focus, akin to the "pavamāno" that drives them to perform at their best.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of harnessing inner strength and mental clarity.
    • It serves as a reminder that our thoughts and intentions can shape our reality, much like the unrestrained force described.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership, where a leader's vision can inspire and mobilize a team.
    • Moreover, in personal development, understanding and channeling this "pavamāno" can lead to greater achievements and fulfillment.
