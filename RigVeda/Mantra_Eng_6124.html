मन्त्र: हिरण्ययी वां रभिर ईषा अक्षो हिरण्ययः | उभा चक्रा हिरण्यया ||
hiraṇyayī vāṁ rabhir īṣā akṣo hiraṇyayaḥ | ubhā cakrā hiraṇyayā ||
  • Mantra: "hiraṇyayī vāṁ rabhir īṣā akṣo hiraṇyayaḥ | ubhā cakrā hiraṇyayā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • hiraṇyayī: Golden or radiant
    • vāṁ: Your (referring to the divine or the universe)
    • rabhi: Power or strength
    • īṣā: Command or authority
    • akṣo: Eye or vision
    • hiraṇyayaḥ: Golden or radiant (again)
    • ubhā: Both or dual
    • cakrā: Wheel or cycle
    • hiraṇyayā: With gold or radiance
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine radiance and power that governs the universe.
    • The repetition of "hiraṇyayā" emphasizes the importance of gold or light, symbolizing purity, wealth, and spiritual enlightenment.
    • The phrase "ubhā cakrā" suggests the dual nature of existence, where both aspects are interconnected and essential for balance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, the mantra can be seen as a reminder to harness one's inner strength and vision to achieve goals, much like how a leader inspires their team with a clear vision.
    • In nature, the cycle of day and night can be viewed as a representation of "ubhā cakrā," where both light and darkness play crucial roles in maintaining ecological balance.
    • In financial contexts, the idea of "hiraṇyayī" can relate to the pursuit of wealth and prosperity, emphasizing the importance of ethical practices in achieving success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and harnessing the divine power and radiance within ourselves and the universe.
    • It teaches us to balance the dualities of life, such as light and dark, strength and vulnerability, to achieve harmony.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with clarity and purpose, while also respecting the interconnectedness of all aspects of existence.
