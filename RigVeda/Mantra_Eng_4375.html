तं त्वा वयं सुध्योऽनव्यं अग्ने सुम्नायव इमहे देवयन्तः |त्वं विश्वो अनयो दीद्यानो दिवो अग्ने बृहता रोचनेन ||
taṁ tvā vayaṁ sudhyo3 navyam agne sumnāyava īmahe devayantaḥ | tvaṁ viśo anayo dīdyāno divo agne bṛhatā rocanena ||
  • taṁ tvā vayaṁ sudhyo3 navyam agne sumnāyava īmahe devayantaḥ
    • taṁ tvā - "to you" or "that you": This phrase indicates a direct address to the divine, acknowledging the presence of a higher power.
    • vayaṁ - "we": This signifies a collective identity, emphasizing community and togetherness in worship.
    • sudhyo3 - "pure" or "sublime": This word highlights the importance of purity in thoughts and actions, suggesting that the divine is associated with purity.
    • navyam - "new" or "fresh": This implies renewal and the idea of seeking new beginnings or insights.
    • agne - "O Agni": Agni is the fire god, representing energy, transformation, and the divine spark within all beings.
    • sumnāyava - "with good intentions": This phrase emphasizes the importance of approaching the divine with positive thoughts and intentions.
    • īmahe - "we invoke": This indicates a call to the divine, a plea for connection and guidance.
    • devayantaḥ - "the divine beings": This refers to the various deities and forces of nature that are invoked in the Vedic tradition.
  • tvaṁ viśo anayo dīdyāno divo agne bṛhatā rocanena
    • tvaṁ - "you": Again, a direct address to Agni, reinforcing the personal connection with the divine.
    • viśo - "of the people" or "community": This emphasizes the collective aspect of worship and the role of the divine in the lives of the community.
    • anayo - "who leads": This suggests that Agni is a guiding force, leading the way for individuals and communities.
    • dīdyāno - "shining" or "radiant": This highlights the brilliance and illuminating nature of Agni, symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
    • divo - "of the heavens": This connects Agni to the celestial realm, indicating a divine presence that transcends the earthly plane.
    • bṛhatā - "great" or "vast": This signifies the immense power and significance of Agni in the universe.
    • rocanena - "with radiance": This reinforces the idea of light and illumination, suggesting that the divine brings clarity and understanding.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking the divine presence of Agni with purity, good intentions, and a sense of community. It highlights the transformative power of the divine, encouraging individuals to seek enlightenment and guidance in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In rituals and ceremonies, where individuals come together to seek blessings and guidance from the divine.
  • In personal reflection and meditation, where one can invoke the qualities of Agni—purity, radiance, and transformation—in their own lives.
  • In community gatherings, fostering a sense of unity and collective purpose, reminding individuals of their interconnectedness.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine's role in guiding and illuminating the path of individuals and communities alike.
