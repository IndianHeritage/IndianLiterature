मन्त्र: स्तरीर यत सूत सद्योज्यमाना व्यथिर व्यथीः कृतुता स्वगोपाः |पुत्रो यत पूर्वः पितरो जनिष्ट शम्यां गौर जगारा यद ध पृच्छान ||
starīr yat sūta sadyo ajyamānā vyathir avyathīḥ kṛṇuta svagopā | putro yat pūrvaḥ pitror janiṣṭa śamyāṁ gaur jagāra yad dha pṛcchān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • starīr: a star or celestial body
    • yat: that which
    • sūta: born or produced
    • sadyo: immediately or at once
    • ajyamānā: not being harmed or affected
    • vyathir: distress or suffering
    • avyathīḥ: without distress or suffering
    • kṛṇuta: may you create or establish
    • svagopā: your own protector or guardian
    • putro: son
    • yat: that which
    • pūrvaḥ: previous or earlier
    • pitror: of the parents
    • janiṣṭa: born or produced
    • śamyāṁ: in the peaceful or tranquil state
    • gaur: the cow or the nurturing aspect
    • jagāra: has awakened or arisen
    • yad: that which
    • dha: indeed
    • pṛcchān: inquiring or questioning
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the creation of a protective and nurturing environment.
    • The reference to "starīr" and "sūta" suggests the importance of celestial guidance and the immediate birth of positive energies.
    • It emphasizes the need to be free from distress ("vyathir" and "avyathīḥ") and to establish a sense of security ("kṛṇuta svagopā").
    • The mention of "putro" and "pitror" highlights the significance of familial bonds and the legacy passed from parents to children.
    • It also reflects the nurturing aspect of life, akin to the cow ("gaur"), which is often seen as a symbol of abundance and care.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, this mantra can be applied to foster a nurturing environment where children feel secure and loved.
    • In a community, it can inspire collective efforts to create a safe space free from distress, where everyone looks out for one another.
    • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek guidance from higher principles (like stars) and to cultivate resilience against life's challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of creating a protective and nurturing environment, both in familial and communal contexts.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek guidance and to establish a life free from distress, fostering growth and security.
    • In real life, it encourages us to be guardians of our loved ones and to create spaces where everyone can thrive peacefully.
