तुझे नस् ताने पर्वताः सन्तु स्वैतवो ये वसवो न वीराः |पणित आप्त्यो यजतः सदा नो वर्धान नः शंसं नार्यो अभिष्टौ ||
tuje nas tane parvatāḥ santu svaitavo ye vasavo na vīrāḥ | panita āptyo yajataḥ sadā no vardhān naḥ śaṁsaṁ naryo abhiṣṭau ||
  • Translation: "May the mountains be your support, and may the Vasus, who are not heroes, always be with you. May we, who are engaged in worship, always receive abundance and blessings from the divine."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • tujhe: You
    • nas: Us
    • tane: Support
    • parvatāḥ: Mountains
    • santu: May they be
    • svaitavo: With you
    • ye: Who
    • vasavo: Vasus (a group of deities)
    • na: Not
    • vīrāḥ: Heroes
    • panita: Abundance
    • āptyo: Blessings
    • yajataḥ: Worship
    • sadā: Always
    • no: Our
    • vardhān: Growth
    • naḥ: Us
    • śaṁsaṁ: Blessings
    • naryo: Women
    • abhiṣṭau: Wishes
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of support systems in our lives, akin to how mountains provide stability and strength. For instance, family and friends can be seen as our 'mountains' that support us during challenging times.
    • The mention of Vasus, who are not heroes, suggests that even ordinary beings can play a significant role in our lives. This can be reflected in everyday interactions, where kindness from strangers or acquaintances can uplift our spirits.
    • The idea of engaging in worship and receiving blessings can be related to practices like meditation or community service, where individuals seek to connect with something greater than themselves and often find fulfillment and abundance in giving back.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for support, abundance, and blessings in our lives. It teaches us to appreciate the strength we derive from our surroundings and relationships, and the importance of gratitude and worship in fostering a sense of community and personal growth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek support from their 'mountains' and recognize the value of both extraordinary and ordinary contributions to their well-being.
