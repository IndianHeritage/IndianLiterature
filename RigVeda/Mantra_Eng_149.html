अभि यज्ञं गृणीहि नो ग्नावो नेष्टः पिब ऋतुनाः |त्वं हि रत्नधा असि ||
abhi yajñaṁ gṛṇīhi no gnāvo neṣṭaḥ piba ṛtunā | tvaṁ hi ratnadhā asi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, for
    • yajñaṁ: the sacrifice, the offering
    • gṛṇīhi: you sing, you praise
    • no: our
    • gnāvo: wise, knowledgeable
    • neṣṭaḥ: the best, the most excellent
    • piba: drink, consume
    • ṛtunā: with the seasons, with the cycles of nature
    • tvaṁ: you
    • hi: indeed
    • ratnadhā: the one who possesses jewels, treasures
    • asi: you are
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine to participate in the ritual of sacrifice.
    • It emphasizes the importance of offering praises and acknowledging the wisdom of the divine.
    • The phrase "piba ṛtunā" suggests that the divine should partake in the offerings in harmony with the natural cycles.
    • By referring to the divine as "ratnadhā," it highlights the idea that the divine is a source of wealth and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the practice of gratitude. Just as one offers praises to the divine, individuals can express gratitude for the abundance in their lives.
    • In environmental practices, the idea of "piba ṛtunā" can be seen in sustainable living, where people consume resources in harmony with nature's cycles.
    • In community gatherings, such as festivals or rituals, the collective offering and praise can strengthen social bonds and foster a sense of unity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and honoring the divine presence in our lives.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the wisdom and abundance that comes from nature and the universe.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages practices of gratitude, sustainability, and community bonding, reminding us that we are part of a larger cycle of existence.
