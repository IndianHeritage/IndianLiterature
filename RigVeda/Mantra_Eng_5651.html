शंसा मित्रस्य वरुणस्य धाम शुष्मो रोधसी बद्बधे महित्वा |अयन मासा अयज्वनाम अवीराः प्र यज्ञमनमा वृजनं तिराते ||
śaṁsā mitrasya varuṇasya dhāma śuṣmo rodasī badbadhe mahitvā | ayan māsā ayajvanām avīrāḥ pra yajñamanmā vṛjanaṁ tirāte ||
  • śaṁsā: This term can be interpreted as "praise" or "glory." It signifies the act of acknowledging the divine qualities of the gods.
  • mitrasya: Refers to "Mitra," the god of friendship and harmony. In real life, this can be related to the importance of maintaining good relationships and fostering unity among people.
  • varuṇasya: This refers to "Varuna," the god of cosmic order and water. Varuna represents the natural laws and the moral order of the universe, which can be seen in the way we respect nature and its resources.
  • dhāma: Meaning "abode" or "realm," it signifies the place where the divine resides. This can be likened to the idea of a sacred space in our lives, such as a temple or a peaceful environment where one can connect with the divine.
  • šuṣmo: This word means "radiant" or "brilliant," indicating the illuminating qualities of the divine presence.
  • rodasī: Refers to the "two worlds" or "heavens and earth," symbolizing the connection between the spiritual and the material realms.
  • badbadhe: This term can be understood as "to be established" or "to be firm," suggesting the stability and permanence of divine principles.
  • mahitvā: Meaning "greatness" or "majesty," it emphasizes the grandeur of the divine forces at play in the universe.
  • ayan: This word means "path" or "way," indicating the journey one takes in life, guided by divine principles.
  • māsā: Refers to "months," symbolizing the passage of time and the cycles of life.
  • ayajvanām: This term means "of the worshippers," highlighting the importance of devotion and ritual in connecting with the divine.
  • avīrāḥ: Meaning "heroes" or "valiant ones," it signifies the strength and courage that comes from divine blessings.
  • pra: This prefix means "forth" or "forward," indicating progress and movement towards a goal.
  • yajñamanmā: Refers to "the sacrifice" or "the offering," which is a central theme in Vedic rituals, symbolizing the act of giving and devotion.
  • vṛjanaṁ: This term means "protection" or "shelter," indicating the safety that comes from divine guidance.
  • tirāte: Meaning "to cross over" or "to transcend," it signifies the ability to overcome obstacles with divine support.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine connection, emphasizing the importance of harmony, respect for nature, and the pursuit of spiritual greatness. In real life, it serves as a reminder to foster good relationships, honor the natural world, and engage in acts of devotion and sacrifice. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate life's challenges with courage and find a path that leads to spiritual fulfillment and protection. The mantra encourages us to recognize the divine presence in our lives and to strive for a harmonious existence, both within ourselves and in our communities.
