युवां सुवासाः परिवीत आगात स उ श्रेयान भवति जायमानः |तं धीरासः कवय उन्नयन्ति स्वाध्यो मनसा देवयन्तः ||
yuvā suvāsāḥ parivīta āgāt sa u śreyān bhavati jāyamānaḥ | taṁ dhīrāsaḥ kavaya un nayanti svādhyo3 manasā devayantaḥ ||
  • yuvā suvāsāḥ parivīta āgāt: This phrase can be translated to "the young and well-dressed one has arrived." Here, "yuvā" refers to youth, symbolizing energy and potential. "Suvāsāḥ" indicates someone who is well-dressed or adorned, suggesting a person of good character or virtue. In real life, this can be likened to a young leader or an individual who embodies hope and promise in a community.
  • sa u śreyān bhavati jāyamānaḥ: This translates to "he who is born becomes the best." The term "śreyān" signifies excellence or superiority. This part of the mantra emphasizes that with the right qualities and virtues, one can achieve greatness. For example, a student who works hard and maintains integrity is likely to succeed in their career and life.
  • taṁ dhīrāsaḥ kavaya un nayanti: This means "the wise and learned guide him." "Dhīrāsaḥ" refers to the wise or intelligent individuals, while "kavaya" means learned or knowledgeable. This suggests that those who are wise will guide the young and virtuous person towards success. In a real-life scenario, mentors in a workplace or educational setting play a crucial role in guiding the youth towards their goals.
  • svādhyo3 manasā devayantaḥ: This translates to "those who are self-controlled and divine in their minds." "Svādhyo" refers to self-control or discipline, and "devayantaḥ" indicates a divine or elevated mindset. This part of the mantra highlights the importance of self-discipline and a higher purpose in achieving success. For instance, athletes who practice self-discipline in their training often achieve remarkable results in competitions.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of youth, virtue, wisdom, and self-discipline in achieving greatness. It serves as a reminder that with the right guidance and qualities, individuals can reach their full potential. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as education, leadership, and personal development. It encourages young individuals to seek wisdom, maintain discipline, and strive for excellence, ultimately contributing to their success and the betterment of society.
