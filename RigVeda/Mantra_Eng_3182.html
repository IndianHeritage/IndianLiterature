घृतं न पूतं तनूर अरेपाḥ शुचि हिरण्यं |तत ते रुक्मो न रोचत स्वधावः ||
ghṛtaṁ na pūtaṁ tanūr arepāḥ śuci hiraṇyam | tat te rukmo na rocata svadhāvaḥ ||
  • ghṛtaṁ: This word refers to ghee, a clarified butter that is considered sacred in Vedic rituals. It symbolizes purity and nourishment.
  • na pūtaṁ: "na" means "not," and "pūtaṁ" means "impure." Together, this phrase emphasizes the importance of purity in offerings and actions.
  • tanūr: This term means "body" or "form." It signifies the physical aspect of existence.
  • arepāḥ: This word can be interpreted as "those who are pure" or "the pure ones." It refers to individuals who embody purity in their thoughts and actions.
  • śuci: Meaning "clean" or "pure," this word reinforces the theme of purity in both physical and spiritual contexts.
  • hiraṇyam: This translates to "gold" and symbolizes wealth, prosperity, and the divine. Gold is often associated with the sun and is considered auspicious.
  • tat te: This phrase means "that to you," indicating a connection or offering to a higher power or deity.
  • rukmo: This word means "shining" or "radiant," suggesting brilliance and excellence.
  • na rocata: "na" means "not," and "rocata" means "to shine" or "to be pleasing." This phrase implies that something is not appealing or does not shine.
  • svadhāvaḥ: This term refers to "self-offering" or "self-sacrifice," highlighting the importance of giving oneself in devotion or service.

The mantra emphasizes the significance of purity, both in physical offerings and in one's character. It suggests that true wealth and radiance come from a pure heart and selfless actions. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal relationships, maintaining purity of intention and honesty fosters trust and strengthens bonds.
  • In professional settings, ethical practices and integrity lead to a positive reputation and long-term success.
  • In spiritual practices, offering oneself selflessly to a cause or community can lead to personal fulfillment and collective growth.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of purity and selflessness in our lives. It encourages individuals to strive for inner cleanliness and to offer their best selves to the world, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
