अभि न इळा यूथस्य माता स्मन नदिभिर उर्वशी वा गृणातु |उर्वशी वा बृहद्दिवा गृणानाभ्यूर्णवानां प्रभृतस्यायोः ||
abhi na iḻā yūthasya mātā sman nadībhir urvaśī vā gṛṇātu | urvaśī vā bṛhaddivā gṛṇānābhyūrṇvānā prabhṛthasyāyoḥ ||
  • Mantra: "abhi na iḻā yūthasya mātā sman nadībhir urvaśī vā gṛṇātu | urvaśī vā bṛhaddivā gṛṇānābhyūrṇvānā prabhṛthasyāyoḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, for
    • na: not
    • iḻā: the goddess of speech or eloquence
    • yūthasya: of the group or community
    • mātā: mother
    • sman: with joy or happiness
    • nadībhir: by the rivers
    • urvaśī: a celestial nymph or a symbol of beauty
    • vā: or
    • gṛṇātu: may sing or praise
    • bṛhad: great or vast
    • divā: during the day
    • gṛṇānā: singing
    • abhyūrṇvānā: may be filled or abundant
    • prabhṛthasyāyoḥ: of the great or vast
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the goddess of speech, iḻā, asking for her blessings for the community.
    • It emphasizes the importance of joy and happiness in communal gatherings, symbolized by the rivers, which are often seen as sources of life and nourishment.
    • Urvaśī represents beauty and grace, suggesting that the community should aspire to these qualities in their interactions.
    • The mention of singing during the day highlights the significance of expressing joy and gratitude openly.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community festival, people come together to celebrate, share stories, and sing songs, embodying the spirit of joy and togetherness.
    • In workplaces, fostering a culture of open communication and appreciation can lead to a more harmonious and productive environment.
    • In educational settings, encouraging students to express themselves through art, music, or speech can enhance their confidence and creativity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of community, joy, and eloquence in our lives.
    • It teaches us to appreciate beauty and grace in our interactions and to express our feelings openly.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger relationships, enhanced communication, and a more vibrant community spirit.
