यजध्वै नम प्रियमेधा इन्द्रं सत्राच मनसा |यो भूत्व सोमैः सत्यमद्वा ||
yajadhvainam priyamedhā indraṁ satrācā manasā | yo bhūt somaiḥ satyamadvā ||
  • yajadhvainam: This term can be broken down into "yaja," which means to worship or to offer, and "dhvainam," which refers to the divine or the gods. Thus, it signifies the act of worshipping the divine.
  • priyamedhā: This word combines "priya," meaning dear or beloved, and "medhā," which refers to intelligence or wisdom. Together, it suggests the idea of a beloved wisdom or intelligence that is cherished.
  • indraṁ: Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and the king of the gods. He represents strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • satrācā: This term can be understood as "satra," which refers to a sacrificial gathering or a ritual, and "cā," meaning and. It implies the importance of community and collective worship.
  • manasā: This word means "with the mind" or "mentally." It emphasizes the importance of intention and thought in the act of worship.
  • yo: This is a pronoun meaning "who" or "that." It introduces a conditional or relative clause.
  • bhūt: This term means "being" or "existence." It refers to the essence of life and the universe.
  • somaiḥ: "Soma" is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. The term here suggests offerings made with this divine substance.
  • satyamadvā: This can be broken down into "satyam," meaning truth, and "advā," which can be interpreted as "not" or "without." Together, it conveys the idea of being true or truthful.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to worship the divine with a beloved wisdom, invoking Indra during communal rituals, and emphasizing the importance of mental intention. It suggests that those who exist and offer with the essence of life, through the sacred drink of Soma, are aligned with truth.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Community Gatherings: Just as the mantra emphasizes collective worship, it highlights the importance of community in spiritual practices. For example, modern-day festivals or community service events can embody this spirit of togetherness.
  • Mindfulness in Worship: The emphasis on "manasā" encourages individuals to approach their spiritual practices with intention and mindfulness, similar to how people today practice meditation or yoga to connect with their inner selves.
  • Truthfulness in Actions: The mantra's focus on truth can inspire individuals to lead lives of integrity, promoting honesty in personal and professional relationships.

In conclusion, this mantra from the Rigveda serves as a timeless reminder of the significance of worship, community, intention, and truth in our lives. It encourages us to engage with the divine and each other meaningfully, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in our spiritual journeys.
