यद इन्द्रो अनयद ऋतो महीर अपो वृषन्तमः |तत्र पूषाभवत सचा ||
yad indro anayad rito mahīr apo vṛṣantamaḥ | tatra pūṣābhavat sacā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • anayad: has brought forth
    • rito: truth or righteousness
    • mahīr: of the great or vast
    • apo: waters
    • vṛṣantamaḥ: the most nourishing or bountiful
    • tatra: there
    • pūṣā: Pūṣa, the deity associated with nourishment
    • abhavat: became or manifested
    • sacā: and with that
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine forces that bring forth truth and nourishment.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, symbolizes strength and leadership, suggesting that true leadership is rooted in righteousness.
    • The mention of "waters" signifies the essential element for life, representing abundance and sustenance.
    • Pūṣa, the deity of nourishment, indicates that the blessings of sustenance come from divine sources.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the reliance on seasonal rains (symbolized by waters) is crucial for a bountiful harvest. Farmers pray for the right conditions, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In leadership roles, individuals who embody truth and righteousness, like Mahatma Gandhi or Nelson Mandela, can be seen as modern-day embodiments of Indra, bringing forth positive change.
    • In personal life, individuals often seek nourishment not just in food but in relationships and emotional support, which can be seen as the manifestation of Pūṣa in their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of truth and nourishment in our lives.
    • It teaches us that true abundance comes from righteous actions and divine blessings.
    • In a world where challenges abound, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek truth and nourishment, both physically and spiritually, to lead a fulfilling life.
