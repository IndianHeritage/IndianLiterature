शं नो भगः शम् उ नः शंसो अस्तु शं नः पुरन्धिः शम् उ सन्तु रायः |शं नः सत्यस्य सुयमस्य शंसः शं नो आर्यमा पुरुजातो अस्तु ||
śaṁ no bhagaḥ śam u naḥ śaṁso astu śaṁ naḥ puraṁdhiḥ śam u santu rāyaḥ | śaṁ naḥ satyasya suyamasya śaṁsaḥ śaṁ no aryamā purujāto astu ||
  • śaṁ no bhagaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "May auspiciousness come to us." The term "bhaga" refers to prosperity and good fortune. In real life, this can be seen in the context of seeking blessings for success in endeavors, such as starting a new business or embarking on a significant life change.
  • śam u naḥ: This means "May peace be upon us." Peace is essential for a harmonious life, and in practical terms, it can relate to maintaining calm in stressful situations, such as during conflicts at work or in personal relationships.
  • śaṁso astu: This translates to "Let there be auspiciousness." It emphasizes the importance of positive outcomes in our actions. For example, when preparing for an exam or a presentation, one hopes for a successful result, which can be seen as invoking this mantra.
  • śaṁ naḥ puraṁdhiḥ: This phrase means "May the divine intelligence be with us." It suggests the need for wisdom and guidance in our decisions. In real life, this can be applied when making important life choices, such as career paths or personal relationships, where wise counsel is sought.
  • śam u santu rāyaḥ: This translates to "May peace bring us wealth." It highlights the connection between peace and prosperity. In practical terms, it can be observed in communities that thrive when there is social harmony, leading to economic growth and stability.
  • śaṁ naḥ satyasya suyamasya: This means "May we be blessed with the truth and good deeds." It emphasizes the importance of integrity and righteousness in our actions. In everyday life, this can be seen in the value placed on honesty in business dealings or personal relationships.
  • śaṁsaḥ: This translates to "Let there be blessings." It is a call for divine favor in all aspects of life. For instance, during significant life events like weddings or childbirth, people often seek blessings for a prosperous future.
  • śaṁ no aryamā purujāto astu: This means "May Aryama, the lord of the righteous, be with us." Aryama represents the principles of justice and fairness. In real life, this can be applied in legal matters or situations where fairness and justice are sought, such as in community disputes or negotiations.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the importance of auspiciousness, peace, wisdom, truth, and justice. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of daily life, from personal endeavors to community interactions. By invoking these principles, individuals and communities can strive for a balanced and prosperous existence, fostering an environment where positive outcomes are more likely to manifest. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek blessings and maintain integrity in all actions, ultimately contributing to a harmonious society.
