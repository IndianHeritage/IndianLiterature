यद इन्द्राहं यथा त्वम् ईशीय वस्व एक इत् |स्तोता मे गोषखā स्यात् ||
yad indrāhaṁ yathā tvam īśīya vasva eka it | stotā me goṣakhā syāt ||
  • Mantra: "yad indrāhaṁ yathā tvam īśīya vasva eka it | stotā me goṣakhā syāt ||"
  • Translation: "As Indra, you are the one who rules over everything; may my praise be like a cow that gives milk."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • yad: "that" or "which"
    • indrāhaṁ: "Indra" (the king of gods in Vedic tradition) + "aham" (I or me)
    • yathā: "as" or "like"
    • tvam: "you"
    • īśīya: "ruler" or "lord"
    • vasva: "everything" or "all"
    • eka: "one"
    • it: "here" or "in this place"
    • stotā: "praise" or "worship"
    • me: "my"
    • goṣakhā: "like a cow" (goṣa means cow, and khā implies a source of nourishment)
    • syāt: "may it be" or "let it be"

  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the deity associated with rain, thunder, and strength, symbolizing the power of nature and the universe.
    • The phrase "yathā tvam īśīya vasva eka it" emphasizes the omnipotence of Indra, suggesting that he is the singular ruler of all that exists.
    • The metaphor of "stotā me goṣakhā syāt" likens the speaker's praise to a cow, which is a vital source of sustenance in many cultures, particularly in agrarian societies. Just as a cow provides milk, the speaker's praise aims to nourish and sustain the divine connection with Indra.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, the cow is revered for its role in providing milk and other resources. This mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of gratitude and respect towards nature and its bounties.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be applied to the idea of leadership and responsibility. Just as Indra is seen as a ruler, individuals in positions of power should strive to be benevolent and nurturing, providing for those they lead.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of recognizing the divine in the natural world and the importance of expressing gratitude through praise.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the responsibility of individuals to honor and nurture their environment and communities.
    • In daily life, this can manifest as acts of kindness, stewardship of nature, and a commitment to leadership that prioritizes the well-being of others.
