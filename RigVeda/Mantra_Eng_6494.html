हर्यश्वं सत्पतिं चर्षणीसहं स हि स्मा यो अमन्दत |आ तु नः स वयति गव्यं अश्व्यं स्तोत्रिभ्यो मगवाः शतम् ||
haryaśvaṁ satpatiṁ carṣaṇīsahaṁ sa hi ṣmā yo amandata | ā tu naḥ sa vayati gavyam aśvyaṁ stotṛbhyo maghavā śatam ||
  • haryaśvaṁ: This term can be broken down into "harya" meaning "one who brings joy" and "aśva" meaning "horse." In ancient times, horses were symbols of wealth and power. Thus, "haryaśvaṁ" refers to a joyful or auspicious horse, representing prosperity and success.
  • satpatiṁ: This word translates to "the true lord" or "the rightful ruler." It signifies a leader who is just, wise, and benevolent, embodying the qualities of a good ruler in society.
  • carṣaṇīsahaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is accompanied by the earth" or "the one who is grounded." It emphasizes the connection between the divine and the earthly realm, suggesting that true power comes from being rooted in reality.
  • sa hi ṣmā yo amandata: This phrase can be understood as "for he who is the source of joy." It highlights the importance of joy and happiness as fundamental aspects of existence, suggesting that true fulfillment comes from within.
  • ā tu naḥ: This translates to "may he grant us." It is a plea for blessings and support from the divine, indicating a desire for guidance and assistance in our endeavors.
  • sa vayati gavyam: This phrase means "he who leads us to abundance." It emphasizes the importance of guidance in achieving prosperity and success, suggesting that we should seek wisdom from those who have attained it.
  • aśvyaṁ: This term refers to "the horse," symbolizing strength, speed, and vitality. In a broader sense, it represents the energy and drive needed to pursue our goals.
  • stotṛbhyo maghavā śatam: This can be interpreted as "may the powerful grant us a hundred praises." It signifies the importance of gratitude and recognition, suggesting that we should acknowledge the contributions of others in our journey towards success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking joy, prosperity, and guidance in life. It emphasizes the importance of being grounded, recognizing the contributions of others, and striving for a just and benevolent leadership. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership roles in organizations, personal development, and community building. By embodying the qualities of joy, wisdom, and gratitude, individuals can create a positive impact in their lives and the lives of those around them. The mantra serves as a reminder that true success is not just about personal achievement but also about uplifting others and fostering a harmonious environment.
