जुष्ट्वी न इन्दो सुपथा सुगाण्य उरौ पवस्व वरिवांसि कृत्वान् |घनेव विश्वग् दुरितानि विघ्नन् अधि ष्णुना धन्वा सानो अव्ये ||
juṣṭvī na indo supathā sugāny urau pavasva varivāṁsi kṛṇvan | ghaneva viṣvag duritāni vighnann adhi ṣṇunā dhanva sāno avye ||
  • juṣṭvī na indo: This phrase can be interpreted as "May we be blessed by the divine." Here, "Indo" refers to the divine or the god, often associated with Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
  • supathā sugāny urau: This translates to "May we walk on the right path." "Supathā" means the good or right path, while "sugāny urau" refers to the auspicious or beneficial ways we should tread upon in life.
  • pavasva varivāṁsi kṛṇvan: This can be understood as "May the winds carry us forward." "Pavasva" means to blow or move, and "varivāṁsi" refers to the beneficial or favorable aspects of life that we seek to embrace.
  • ghaneva viṣvag duritāni vighnann: This means "Like a cloud, may we dispel all difficulties." "Ghaneva" refers to something dense or thick, like a cloud, while "viṣvag duritāni" means all kinds of difficulties or obstacles.
  • adhi ṣṇunā dhanva sāno avye: This translates to "With strength, may we overcome challenges." "Adhi" means above or over, "ṣṇunā" refers to strength, and "dhanva" signifies overcoming or conquering.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of divine blessings, the pursuit of righteousness, and the strength to overcome obstacles in life. It serves as a reminder to seek guidance from higher powers while navigating the complexities of existence. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during challenging times, such as when facing personal or professional difficulties. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset focused on positivity, resilience, and the pursuit of a virtuous path, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life.
