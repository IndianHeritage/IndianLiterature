त्वं अग्नि वाघते सुप्रणीतिः सुतसोमाय विधते यविष्टः |रत्नं भरा शशमानाय घृष्वे पृथु श्चन्द्रम अवसे चरषणिप्राः ||
tvam agne vāghate supraṇītiḥ sutasomāya vidhate yaviṣṭha | ratnam bhara śaśamānāya ghṛṣve pṛthu ścandram avase carṣaṇiprāḥ ||
  • tvam agne: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni." Agni is the Vedic god of fire, representing energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, fire symbolizes both destruction and renewal, much like challenges we face that lead to personal growth.
  • vāghate: This means "who brings forth." It signifies the ability to manifest or create. In our lives, this can relate to individuals who inspire or lead others, such as teachers or mentors who help us realize our potential.
  • supraṇītiḥ: This translates to "excellent guidance." It emphasizes the importance of wise counsel in our decisions. For example, seeking advice from experienced professionals when making career choices can lead to better outcomes.
  • sutasomāya: This means "to the one who is full of joy." It refers to the joy that comes from success and fulfillment. In everyday life, this can be seen in moments of achievement, such as graduating from school or completing a significant project.
  • vidhate: This translates to "who arranges" or "who establishes." It highlights the role of planning and organization in achieving goals. For instance, a well-structured business plan can lead to a successful startup.
  • yaviṣṭha: This means "the best" or "the most excellent." It signifies striving for excellence in all endeavors. In real life, this can be applied to athletes who train rigorously to achieve peak performance in their sport.
  • ratnam bhara: This translates to "bring forth the jewel." It symbolizes the pursuit of valuable knowledge or wisdom. In practical terms, this can relate to lifelong learning and the importance of education in personal and professional development.
  • śaśamānāya: This means "to the one who is prosperous." It reflects the idea of abundance and success. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who achieve financial stability through hard work and smart investments.
  • ghṛṣve: This translates to "to the one who is nourished." It signifies the importance of sustenance and support in our lives. For example, a supportive family can provide the emotional nourishment needed to pursue one's dreams.
  • pṛthu: This means "broad" or "vast." It represents the idea of expansiveness and growth. In real life, this can be related to personal development, where individuals seek to broaden their horizons through travel or new experiences.
  • ścandram: This translates to "the moon." The moon symbolizes calmness and reflection. In our lives, it can represent the importance of taking time for introspection and self-care amidst our busy schedules.
  • avase: This means "to dwell" or "to reside." It signifies the importance of finding a place of comfort and stability. In real life, this can relate to creating a safe and nurturing home environment.
  • carṣaṇiprāḥ: This translates to "the one who is surrounded by the earth." It symbolizes grounding and connection to nature. In practical terms, this can be seen in the importance of environmental conservation and our relationship with the earth.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of Agni as a source of guidance, joy, and prosperity. It highlights the importance of striving for excellence, seeking wise counsel, and nurturing our connections with others and the environment. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, career planning, and fostering supportive relationships. By embodying the qualities represented in this mantra, individuals can lead more fulfilling and purposeful lives.
