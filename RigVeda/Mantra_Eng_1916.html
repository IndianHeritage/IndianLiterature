अवविद्धं तौग्र्यं अप्स्व अंतर अनारम्भणे तमसि प्रविद्धम् |चतस्रो नावो जठलस्य जुष्टा उद अश्विभ्यां इषिताः पारयन्ति ||
avaviddhaṁ taugryam apsv a1ntar anārambhaṇe tamasi praviddham | catasro nāvo jaṭhalasya juṣṭā ud aśvibhyām iṣitāḥ pārayanti ||
  • Mantra: "avaviddhaṁ taugryam apsv a1ntar anārambhaṇe tamasi praviddham | catasro nāvo jaṭhalasya juṣṭā ud aśvibhyām iṣitāḥ pārayanti ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • avaviddhaṁ: that which is not bound or restricted
    • taugryam: strength or power
    • apsv: in the waters or in the depths
    • a1ntar: within or inside
    • anārambhaṇe: without initiation or beginning
    • tamasi: in darkness or ignorance
    • praviddham: that which is illuminated or made clear
    • catasro: four
    • nāvo: ships or vessels
    • jaṭhalasya: of the body or form
    • juṣṭā: filled or equipped
    • ud: upward or above
    • aśvibhyām: by the two horses (often symbolizing speed or power)
    • iṣitāḥ: controlled or directed
    • pārayanti: they carry or transport
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a state of being that is unbound and powerful, existing within the depths of water, yet without any initiation or beginning.
    • The reference to "darkness" suggests ignorance or a lack of awareness, while "praviddham" indicates the process of enlightenment or gaining clarity.
    • The mention of "four ships" can symbolize various aspects of life or different paths one can take, all equipped to navigate through the challenges of existence.
    • The "two horses" may represent dual forces or energies that propel us forward, suggesting that with the right direction and control, we can overcome obstacles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to the journey of an entrepreneur who starts with a vision (unbound power) but must navigate through uncertainties (darkness) to achieve success (enlightenment).
    • In personal development, it reflects the journey of self-discovery where one must confront their fears (darkness) to find their true potential (light).
    • In relationships, it can symbolize the importance of communication and understanding (the ships) to navigate through challenges together, guided by mutual respect and love (the horses).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking clarity and strength within oneself while navigating through life's challenges.
    • It teaches us that even in moments of confusion or darkness, there is potential for enlightenment and growth.
    • By recognizing the dual forces in our lives and directing them wisely, we can achieve our goals and overcome obstacles, making this mantra relevant in various aspects of life, including personal growth, relationships, and professional endeavors.
