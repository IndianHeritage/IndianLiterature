इन्द्र पिब तुभ्यं सुतो मदायाव स्य हरī वि मुचā सखायā | उत प्र गाया गण आ निषद्याथा यज्ञाय गृणते वयो धाः ||
indra piba tubhyaṁ suto madāyāva sya harī vi mucā sakhāyā | uta pra gāya gaṇa ā niṣadyāthā yajñāya gṛṇate vayo dhāḥ ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Piba: Means "drink," indicating the act of consuming or absorbing something beneficial.
  • Tubhyaṁ: Translates to "for you," suggesting a dedication or offering to Indra.
  • Suto: Means "son" or "offspring," which can symbolize the fruits of one's labor or efforts.
  • Madāyāva: Refers to intoxication or bliss, often associated with the joy of success or achievement.
  • Harī: Means "the one who removes obstacles," often associated with prosperity and well-being.
  • Vi: Translates to "with," indicating a connection or relationship.
  • Mucā: Means "to free" or "to liberate," suggesting the removal of hindrances.
  • Sakhāyā: Refers to "friends" or "companions," emphasizing the importance of community and support.
  • Uta: Means "and" or "also," indicating an addition or continuation of thought.
  • Pra: Means "forth" or "forward," suggesting progress or movement.
  • Gāya: Means "to sing," symbolizing celebration and expression of joy.
  • Gaṇa: Refers to "group" or "community," highlighting the collective aspect of human experience.
  • Ā: Means "towards," indicating direction or purpose.
  • Niṣadyāthā: Refers to "as established" or "as settled," suggesting stability and foundation.
  • Yajñāya: Means "for the sacrifice" or "for the offering," indicating the importance of giving and sharing.
  • Gṛṇate: Means "to praise" or "to celebrate," emphasizing the act of recognition and gratitude.
  • Vayo: Refers to "age" or "time," indicating the passage of time and its significance.
  • Dhāḥ: Means "to hold" or "to support," suggesting the importance of stability and strength.

This mantra can be interpreted as a call to Indra, asking him to partake in the offerings made, to bless the community with joy and prosperity, and to remove obstacles that hinder progress. It emphasizes the importance of friendship, community, and the collective effort in achieving success.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Community Gatherings: When people come together for celebrations or festivals, invoking the spirit of unity and joy, similar to the essence of this mantra.
  • Teamwork in Workplaces: Encouraging collaboration among colleagues to achieve common goals, reflecting the importance of friendship and support.
  • Personal Growth: Seeking guidance and strength from mentors or leaders in overcoming challenges, akin to invoking Indra's blessings.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on community, support, and the pursuit of joy and prosperity. It serves as a reminder of the importance of collective efforts and the need for divine guidance in overcoming obstacles. In our daily lives, we can apply its teachings by fostering relationships, celebrating achievements, and working together towards common goals.
