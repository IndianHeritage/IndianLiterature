इयं वाम अस्य मनमन इन्द्राग्नि पूर्व्यस्तुतिः |अभ्राद वृष्टिर इवाजनि ||
iyaṁ vām asya manmana indrāgnī pūrvyastutiḥ | abhrād vṛṣṭir ivājani ||
  • iyaṁ vām asya manmana: This phrase can be translated as "This is your thought, O Indra and Agni." Here, "Indra" represents the god of rain and thunderstorms, while "Agni" symbolizes fire and is often associated with sacrifice and transformation. This invocation suggests a deep connection between human thoughts and divine powers.
  • indrāgnī: The combination of Indra and Agni signifies the dual forces of nature—rain and fire. In real life, these elements are crucial for agriculture. For instance, in farming communities, the arrival of rain (Indra) is essential for crop growth, while fire (Agni) is used in rituals and for clearing land.
  • pūrvyastutiḥ: This term means "the praise of the ancients" or "the ancient hymn." It reflects the tradition of honoring past wisdom and practices. In modern life, this can be seen in how cultures preserve their heritage through storytelling, rituals, and festivals that celebrate their history.
  • abhrād vṛṣṭir ivājani: This translates to "like the rain that comes from the clouds." It emphasizes the idea of abundance and nourishment that rain brings. In a practical sense, this can be related to the importance of water conservation and sustainable practices in agriculture to ensure food security.
  • vṛṣṭir: The word "vṛṣṭi" means "rain" or "shower." It symbolizes blessings and prosperity. In everyday life, this can be likened to the blessings we receive from nature, such as the fruits of our labor or the support from our community.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the relationship between human consciousness and the divine forces of nature. It highlights the importance of reverence for the elements that sustain life—rain and fire—and the wisdom of our ancestors. In real life, this mantra serves as a reminder to respect and honor nature, to seek balance in our lives, and to appreciate the interconnectedness of all things. By acknowledging these forces, we can cultivate gratitude and mindfulness, leading to a more harmonious existence.
