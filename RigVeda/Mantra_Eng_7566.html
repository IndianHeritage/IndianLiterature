इन्द्र यस् ते नवीयसीं गिरं मन्द्राम अजीजनत् |चिकित्विन्मनसं धियम् प्रत्नाम ऋतस्य पिप्युषीम् ||
indra yas te navīyasīṁ giram mandrām ajījanat | cikitvinmanasaṁ dhiyam pratnām ṛtasya pipyuṣīm ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Yas: Means "who" or "that," indicating a connection or relationship.
  • Te: Translates to "your," suggesting possession or belonging.
  • Navīyasīṁ: Means "new" or "fresh," indicating innovation or renewal.
  • Giram: Refers to "speech" or "words," emphasizing communication and expression.
  • Mandrām: Means "sweet" or "pleasant," suggesting the quality of the speech or words.
  • Ajījanat: Translates to "produced" or "created," indicating the act of bringing forth.
  • Cikitvin: Refers to "thought" or "intellect," emphasizing the role of the mind in understanding.
  • Manasaṁ: Means "of the mind," reinforcing the connection between thought and consciousness.
  • Dhiyam: Refers to "intellect" or "wisdom," highlighting the importance of knowledge.
  • Pratnām: Means "of the truth" or "of the reality," indicating a quest for understanding.
  • Ṛtasya: Refers to "of the cosmic order" or "truth," emphasizing the universal principles that govern existence.
  • Pipyuṣīm: Means "nourishing" or "sustaining," suggesting the life-giving aspect of truth and knowledge.

This mantra speaks to the divine qualities of Indra, who is invoked for the creation of new and pleasant words that arise from a thoughtful and wise mind. It emphasizes the importance of communication, intellect, and the pursuit of truth in our lives. The mantra suggests that through the power of speech and thought, one can connect with the cosmic order and gain nourishment for the soul.


In real life, this can be seen in various scenarios such as:

  • Leadership: A leader who communicates effectively and inspires others embodies the qualities of Indra. For example, a motivational speaker who uses their words to uplift and encourage can create a positive impact on their audience.
  • Education: Teachers who impart knowledge with clarity and wisdom help shape the minds of their students, fostering a new generation of thinkers and innovators.
  • Conflict Resolution: In situations of disagreement, using thoughtful communication can lead to understanding and harmony, reflecting the nourishing aspect of truth.

In conclusion, this mantra highlights the significance of effective communication, the pursuit of knowledge, and the importance of aligning oneself with universal truths. It serves as a reminder that our words and thoughts have the power to create, inspire, and sustain life, making it applicable in various aspects of our daily lives.
