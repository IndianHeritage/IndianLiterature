प्र सावा वाचि सुष्टुतिर मघोनाम इदं सूक्तं मरुतो जुषन्त |आराच चिद द्वेषो वृषणो युयोत यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
pra sā vāci suṣṭutir maghonām idaṁ sūktam maruto juṣanta | ārāc cid dveṣo vṛṣaṇo yuyota yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • pra sā vāci suṣṭutir: This phrase can be translated as "May the well-spoken words be auspicious." It emphasizes the importance of communication and the power of words in shaping our reality. In real life, effective communication can lead to better relationships and understanding among individuals.
  • maghonām idaṁ sūktam: This means "This hymn is for the mighty ones." It acknowledges the strength and influence of powerful beings or forces, which can be interpreted as both divine entities and influential people in our lives. For example, in a workplace, leaders who inspire and motivate their teams can be seen as 'mighty ones.'
  • maruto juṣanta: Translated as "the Maruts (storm deities) enjoy," this line refers to the natural forces that bring change and transformation. In life, we often face challenges that can be likened to storms; embracing these changes can lead to personal growth and resilience.
  • ārāc cid dveṣo vṛṣaṇo yuyota: This can be interpreted as "May the forces of enmity be dispelled." It highlights the need for harmony and the resolution of conflicts. In everyday life, this can relate to resolving disputes in families or communities, promoting peace and understanding.
  • yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: This translates to "May you protect us always with auspiciousness." It is a plea for protection and well-being. In practical terms, this can be seen in the way we seek safety and security in our lives, whether through community support, family bonds, or spiritual practices.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of communication, strength, transformation, conflict resolution, and protection. Its significance lies in its call for auspiciousness in our words and actions, urging us to foster positive relationships and navigate life's challenges with resilience. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal relationships, community interactions, and even in professional environments, where effective communication and harmony are essential for success and well-being.
