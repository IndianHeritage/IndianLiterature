या एनं आदिदेशति करम्भाद् इति पूषणम् |न तेन देव आदिशे ||
ya enam ādideśati karambhād iti pūṣaṇam | na tena deva ādiśe ||
  • Mantra: "ya enam ādideśati karambhād iti pūṣaṇam | na tena deva ādiśe ||"
  • Translation: "He who directs this (sacrifice) through action, O Pūṣaṇa, may the gods not direct him."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ya: who
    • enam: this
    • ādideśati: directs or commands
    • karambhād: through action
    • iti: thus
    • pūṣaṇam: O Pūṣaṇa (a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity)
    • na: not
    • tena: by him
    • deva: gods
    • ādiśe: direct or command
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of intention and action in spiritual practices.
    • Pūṣaṇa, as a deity, represents nourishment and the fulfillment of desires, suggesting that actions taken with the right intention can lead to divine blessings.
    • The phrase "may the gods not direct him" implies a caution against misguided actions that do not align with divine will or purpose.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who directs their team with clear intentions and ethical actions is more likely to achieve success and earn respect, akin to the blessings of Pūṣaṇa.
    • In personal relationships, acting with genuine care and intention can lead to stronger bonds, while selfish or misguided actions may lead to conflict and misunderstanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of aligning our actions with our intentions and the divine will.
    • It encourages individuals to reflect on their motivations and the potential consequences of their actions in various aspects of life.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can cultivate a life that is harmonious, prosperous, and aligned with higher values.
