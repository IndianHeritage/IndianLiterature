तं ते मदं गृणीमसि वृषणं पृथ्सु सासहिम् |उ लोककृत्नुम अद्रिवो हरिश्रियं ||
taṁ te madaṁ gṛṇīmasi vṛṣaṇam pṛtsu sāsahim | u lokakṛtnum adrivo hariśriyam ||
  • taṁ te madaṁ gṛṇīmasi: This phrase can be translated as "We sing of that intoxication of yours." Here, "mada" refers to a state of bliss or ecstasy, often associated with divine experiences. In real life, this can be likened to moments of joy and fulfillment, such as achieving a long-desired goal or experiencing love.
  • vṛṣaṇam pṛtsu sāsahim: This part means "the strong one who is present in the world." "Vṛṣaṇa" signifies strength and power, while "pṛtsu" refers to the earth or the world. This can be related to individuals who embody strength and resilience, such as leaders or mentors who inspire others through their actions.
  • u lokakṛtnum adrivo: Translated, this means "the one who creates the world." "Lokakṛtnum" refers to the creator or the one who shapes existence. In a real-life context, this can be seen in innovators and visionaries who change the world through their ideas and inventions, like scientists or artists.
  • hariśriyam: This term can be interpreted as "the glory of the divine." "Hari" often refers to a divine being, and "śriyam" signifies prosperity or beauty. This can be related to the appreciation of nature's beauty or the pursuit of spiritual wealth, such as kindness and compassion.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine intoxication, strength, creativity, and glory. It signifies the importance of recognizing and celebrating the divine presence in our lives, which can manifest through joy, resilience, innovation, and appreciation of beauty. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to seek fulfillment in their passions, to draw strength from their experiences, to innovate for the betterment of society, and to appreciate the beauty around them. By embodying these principles, one can lead a more meaningful and enriched life.
