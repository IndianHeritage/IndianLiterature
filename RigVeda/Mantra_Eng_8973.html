उत ते स्तभ्नामि पृथिवीं त्वत् परीमं लोकं निदधन मो अहं ऋषम् |एतां स्थूणाम् पितरो धारयन्तु ते 'त्रा यमः साधना ते मिनोतु ||
ut te stabhnāmi pṛthivīṁ tvat parīmaṁ logaṁ nidadhan mo ahaṁ riṣam | etāṁ sthūṇām pitaro dhārayantu te 'trā yamaḥ sādanā te minotu ||
  • ut te stabhnāmi: This phrase can be translated as "I establish you" or "I support you." It signifies the act of providing stability and strength.
  • pṛthivīṁ: This word means "the Earth." It represents the physical world and our connection to it.
  • tvat parīmaṁ: This translates to "your vastness" or "your expanse," indicating the limitless nature of the divine or the universe.
  • logaṁ nidadhan: This means "the worlds are established" or "the realms are created." It reflects the idea of creation and the existence of various realms of life.
  • mo ahaṁ riṣam: This can be interpreted as "I am the one who is in awe" or "I am filled with reverence." It expresses a sense of humility and respect towards the divine.
  • etāṁ sthūṇām: This phrase means "these great ones" or "these mighty beings," referring to the powerful forces of nature or deities.
  • pitaro dhārayantu: This translates to "may the ancestors support" or "may the forefathers uphold." It emphasizes the importance of ancestral blessings and guidance.
  • te 'trā yamaḥ: This means "may Yama (the god of death) be here" or "may Yama be present." It acknowledges the inevitability of death and the importance of living a righteous life.
  • sādanā te minotu: This can be interpreted as "may your abode be protected" or "may your dwelling be safe." It signifies the desire for safety and security in one's life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of stability, reverence, and the interconnectedness of life. It highlights the importance of grounding oneself in the physical world while acknowledging the vastness of the universe and the support of ancestors. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in rituals for seeking blessings for stability in one's home, family, and community. It serves as a reminder to respect the forces of nature and the wisdom of our forebears, encouraging us to live with humility and awareness of our place in the cosmos. By invoking these principles, individuals can foster a sense of security and purpose in their lives, ultimately leading to a harmonious existence.
