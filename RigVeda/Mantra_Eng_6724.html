यो धृतितो यो अवृतो यो अस्ति श्मश्रुषु श्रितः |विभूतद्युम्नश्च्यवनः पुरुष्टुतः क्रत्वा गौर इव शाकिनः ||
yo dhṛṣito yo 'vṛto yo asti śmaśruṣu śritaḥ | vibhūtadyumnaś cyavanaḥ puruṣṭutaḥ kratvā gaur iva śākinaḥ ||
  • yo dhṛṣito: This phrase can be translated as "who is courageous" or "who is determined." It emphasizes the importance of bravery and resolve in facing challenges. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who take risks to pursue their dreams, such as entrepreneurs who start their own businesses despite uncertainties.
  • yo 'vṛto: This means "who is protected" or "who is shielded." It signifies the idea of safety and security. For example, a person who has a strong support system of family and friends often feels more secure in their endeavors, allowing them to take on greater challenges.
  • yo asti śmaśruṣu śritaḥ: This translates to "who is established in the realm of the dead" or "who is grounded in reality." It suggests a connection to the past and an understanding of mortality. In practical terms, this can relate to individuals who honor their ancestors and learn from history, using those lessons to guide their present actions.
  • vibhūtadyumnaś: This term refers to "one who is endowed with glory and strength." It highlights the qualities of power and excellence. In everyday life, this can be seen in leaders who inspire others through their vision and strength, such as community leaders who advocate for social change.
  • cyavanaḥ: This word can be interpreted as "one who is rejuvenated" or "one who has regained vitality." It symbolizes renewal and the ability to overcome adversity. A real-life example could be someone who has faced health challenges but has worked hard to regain their strength and vitality through perseverance and determination.
  • puruṣṭutaḥ: This means "one who is praised by men" or "one who is respected." It signifies the importance of earning respect through actions and character. In a modern context, this can be seen in individuals who are recognized for their contributions to society, such as activists or philanthropists who work tirelessly for the betterment of others.
  • kratvā: This term refers to "through action" or "by means of effort." It emphasizes the significance of taking action to achieve goals. For instance, a student who diligently studies and works hard to excel in their exams embodies this principle.
  • gaur iva śākinaḥ: This phrase translates to "like a cow that is strong and nourished." It symbolizes abundance and the nurturing aspect of life. In real life, this can be related to the importance of nurturing relationships and communities, much like how a healthy environment fosters growth and prosperity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of courage, protection, grounding in reality, glory, rejuvenation, respect, action, and nurturing. Its significance lies in the holistic approach it advocates for living a fulfilling life. By embodying these qualities, individuals can navigate challenges, foster strong relationships, and contribute positively to society. In real-life scenarios, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for personal development, community engagement, and the pursuit of excellence in various endeavors.
