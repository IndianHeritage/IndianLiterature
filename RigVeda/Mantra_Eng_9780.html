  • yāvanmātram: This term can be translated as "as long as" or "for the duration of." It signifies a temporal aspect, indicating a limit or a measure of time.
  • uṣaso: This word means "of the dawn" or "of the morning." It symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the arrival of light after darkness.
  • na pratīkaṁ: This phrase translates to "not a sign" or "not an indication." It suggests the absence of something that would typically signify a change or event.
  • suparṇyo: This term refers to "the beautiful or auspicious birds," often associated with the divine or celestial beings. In many cultures, birds symbolize freedom and transcendence.
  • vasate: This means "dwells" or "resides." It indicates a state of being or existence in a particular place.
  • mātariśvaḥ: This word refers to "the wind" or "the atmosphere." It embodies the idea of movement, change, and the unseen forces that connect all living beings.
  • tāvad: This means "as long as" or "until." It emphasizes continuity and the ongoing nature of existence.
  • dadhāty: This translates to "provides" or "gives." It indicates an act of offering or bestowing something valuable.
  • upa yajñam: This phrase means "to the sacrifice" or "to the offering." It signifies the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine.
  • āyan: This term means "arrival" or "coming." It suggests the idea of welcoming or receiving something significant.
  • brāhmaṇo: This refers to "the priest" or "the learned one." It symbolizes knowledge, wisdom, and the role of guiding others in spiritual matters.
  • hotur: This word means "the one who invokes" or "the one who offers prayers." It emphasizes the act of communication with the divine.
  • avaro: This translates to "the lower" or "the humble." It signifies humility and the importance of being grounded.
  • niṣīdan: This means "sitting" or "residing." It indicates a state of stability and presence.

Conclusion: The mantra reflects the interconnectedness of time, nature, and the divine. It emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings in maintaining harmony with the universe. In real life, this can be applied through practices such as meditation, mindfulness, and gratitude, which help individuals connect with their surroundings and foster a sense of peace and purpose. The mantra serves as a reminder to appreciate the present moment and the beauty of existence, encouraging individuals to live with intention and humility.
