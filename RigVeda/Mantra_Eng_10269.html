त्वं सिन्धूम् रवासृजो 'धरा चो अहन्न अहिम् |अशत्रुर इन्द्र जजñिषे विश्वं पुष्यसि वार्यं तं त्वा परि स्वजामहे नभन्ताम् अन्यकेषां ज्याकाद् अधि धन्वसु ||
tvaṁ sindhūm̐r avāsṛjo 'dharāco ahann ahim | aśatrur indra jajñiṣe viśvam puṣyasi vāryaṁ taṁ tvā pari ṣvajāmahe nabhantām anyakeṣāṁ jyākā adhi dhanvasu ||
  • tvaṁ - You
  • sindhūm̐r - Ocean or river, symbolizing vastness and abundance
  • avāsṛjo - Have created or brought forth
  • 'dharāco - Earth or the ground, representing stability
  • ahann - I have struck or defeated
  • ahim - The serpent or enemy, often symbolizing obstacles or negativity
  • aśatrur - Without enemies, indicating peace and harmony
  • indra - The king of gods, representing strength and power
  • jajñiṣe - You have been born or manifested
  • viśvam - The universe or all beings, indicating inclusiveness
  • puṣyasi - You nourish or support
  • vāryaṁ - The best or the most excellent
  • taṁ - That
  • tvā - You
  • pari - Around or in all directions
  • ṣvajāmahe - We embrace or honor
  • nabhantām - The heavens or sky, representing the divine
  • anyakeṣāṁ - Other beings or entities
  • jyākā - The light or brilliance
  • adhi - Above or over
  • dhanvasu - In the realms of wealth or prosperity

This mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of the divine, nature, and humanity. It emphasizes the creation of abundance and stability in life, while also highlighting the importance of overcoming obstacles and fostering peace. The invocation of Indra, the king of gods, signifies the strength and support that comes from the divine in our endeavors.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the ocean represents vastness, individuals can strive for personal growth and abundance in their lives by overcoming challenges (ahim) and embracing peace (aśatrur).
  • Community Harmony: The idea of nourishing (puṣyasi) the universe (viśvam) can be reflected in community service, where individuals work together to support and uplift one another.
  • Spiritual Connection: The reference to the heavens (nabhantām) encourages individuals to seek a deeper spiritual connection, recognizing the divine presence in all aspects of life.

Conclusion: This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of embracing abundance, overcoming obstacles, and fostering harmony in our lives. By recognizing the divine support available to us and the interconnectedness of all beings, we can cultivate a life filled with purpose, peace, and prosperity.
