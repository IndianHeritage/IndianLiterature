तुभ्येद एते मरुतः सुशेवा अर्चन्त्य अर्कं सुन्वन्त्य अन्धः |अहिम ओहानम् अप आश्यानम् प्र मायाभिर मायिनं साक्षद इन्द्रः ||
tubhyed ete marutaḥ suśevā arcanty arkaṁ sunvanty andhaḥ | ahim ohānam apa āśayānam pra māyābhir māyinaṁ sakṣad indraḥ ||
  • tubhyed: This word translates to "to you" or "for you," indicating a sense of devotion or offering.
  • ete marutaḥ: "These Maruts" refers to the storm deities or winds, symbolizing power and energy in nature.
  • suśevā: This means "well-formed" or "beautiful," suggesting that the offerings or actions are of high quality.
  • arcanty: This translates to "worship" or "offer," indicating a ritualistic aspect of devotion.
  • arkaṁ: Refers to "light" or "radiance," often associated with the sun or divine illumination.
  • sunvanty: This means "to bring forth" or "to produce," indicating the act of creation or manifestation.
  • andhaḥ: Translates to "blind" or "ignorant," representing a state of unawareness or lack of knowledge.
  • ahim: This word means "non-violence" or "peace," emphasizing the importance of harmony.
  • ohānam: Refers to "removal" or "dispelling," suggesting the act of overcoming obstacles or negativity.
  • apa āśayānam: This translates to "removing desires" or "overcoming attachments," indicating a spiritual journey towards liberation.
  • pra: This means "forward" or "towards," indicating a direction or purpose.
  • māyābhir: Refers to "illusions" or "deceptions," highlighting the challenges faced in life.
  • māyinaṁ: This means "the one who creates illusions," often referring to the divine or cosmic forces.
  • sakṣad: Translates to "directly" or "immediately," indicating a sense of immediacy or presence.
  • indraḥ: Refers to "Indra," the king of gods, symbolizing strength, power, and authority.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of devotion and the act of worship towards the divine forces represented by the Maruts and Indra. It highlights the need to overcome ignorance and desires, promoting a path towards peace and enlightenment. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, spiritual practices, and community rituals where individuals seek to connect with higher powers, dispel negativity, and cultivate harmony in their lives. The mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives and the importance of aligning our actions with higher ideals for a fulfilling existence.
