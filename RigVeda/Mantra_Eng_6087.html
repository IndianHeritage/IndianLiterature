रत्हेष्ठायाध्वर्यवः सोमं इन्द्राय सोतन | अधि ब्रध्नस्याद्रयो वि चक्षते सुन्वन्तो दाश्वध्वरम् ||
ratheṣṭhāyādhvaryavaḥ somam indrāya sotana | adhi bradhnasyādrayo vi cakṣate sunvanto dāśvadhvaram ||
  • ratheṣṭhāyādhvaryavaḥ: This term can be broken down into two parts: "ratheṣṭhā" meaning "in the chariot" and "adhvaryavaḥ" referring to the priests or those who perform rituals. This suggests a connection between the divine and the earthly, where the chariot symbolizes the journey of life and the priests represent the guiding forces.
  • somam indrāya sotana: "Somam" refers to the sacred drink, "Indrāya" indicates the god Indra, and "sotana" means "for the son." This line emphasizes the offering of soma to Indra, who is often associated with strength and power. In real life, this can be seen as a metaphor for seeking strength and support from higher powers or deities in our endeavors.
  • adhi bradhnasyādrayo: "Adhi" means "upon," "bradhnasyā" refers to the strong or powerful, and "adrayo" means "mountains." This line suggests that the offerings are made upon the strong mountains, symbolizing stability and endurance. In life, this can be interpreted as the importance of having a strong foundation or support system when facing challenges.
  • vi cakṣate sunvanto dāśvadhvaram: "Vi" means "through," "cakṣate" refers to "seeing," "sunvanto" means "those who are good," and "dāśvadhvaram" refers to the "tenfold offerings." This line highlights the act of perceiving or recognizing the good in the world through the offerings made. It suggests that by acknowledging and appreciating the positive aspects of life, we can enhance our spiritual journey.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of rituals, offerings, and the connection between the divine and the earthly. It teaches us the importance of seeking strength from higher powers, having a solid foundation in life, and recognizing the goodness around us. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal growth, community support, and spiritual practices. By understanding and embodying these principles, individuals can navigate their life journeys with purpose and resilience.
