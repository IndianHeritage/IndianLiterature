अयम् उ ते सम अतसि कपोत इव गर्भाधिम् |वचस तच चिन् न ओहसे ||
ayam u te sam atasi kapota iva garbhadhim | vacas tac cin na ohase ||
  • Mantra: "ayam u te sam atasi kapota iva garbhadhim | vacas tac cin na ohase ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "This is your essence, like a dove in the womb; may your words not be in vain."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ayam: This (referring to the essence or being)
    • u: indeed, or truly
    • te: your
    • sam: essence or being
    • atasi: in the womb
    • kapota: dove (symbolizing peace and purity)
    • iva: like
    • garbhadhim: womb or the state of being within
    • vacas: words or speech
    • tac: that
    • cin: not
    • na: not
    • ohase: be in vain or be wasted
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The dove is often seen as a symbol of peace. In real life, when we strive for peace in our relationships, we embody the essence of this mantra.
    • In communication, ensuring that our words carry meaning and intention is crucial. For instance, in a workplace setting, clear and purposeful communication can lead to better teamwork and project outcomes.
    • In personal development, understanding one's essence and purpose can guide individuals in making choices that align with their true selves, much like the dove that represents purity and clarity.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on recognizing one's true essence and the importance of meaningful communication. In real life, it serves as a reminder to be mindful of our words and actions, ensuring they reflect our true selves and contribute positively to our surroundings. Whether in personal relationships, professional environments, or self-reflection, this mantra encourages us to live authentically and communicate with purpose, ultimately fostering peace and understanding in our lives.
