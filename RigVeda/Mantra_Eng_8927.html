ये तातृषुर देवत्रा जेहमाना होत्राविदः स्तोमतष्टासो अर्कैः |आग्ने याहि सुविदात्रेभिर अर्वाङ् सत्यैः कव्यैः पितृभिर घर्मसद्भिः ||
ye tātṛṣur devatrā jehamānā hotrāvidaḥ stomataṣṭāso arkaiḥ | āgne yāhi suvidatrebhir arvāṅ satyaiḥ kavyaiḥ pitṛbhir gharmasadbhiḥ ||
  • Translation: "Those who are thirsty, the divine beings, who are invoked by the priests, who are knowledgeable in the hymns, may they come to us with their blessings, O Agni, with the well-prepared offerings, with the truthful and wise ones, with the ancestral spirits who dwell in the warmth."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye tātṛṣur: "Those who are thirsty" - This refers to beings or individuals who are in need or seeking something, often interpreted as spiritual or material fulfillment.
    • devatrā: "divine beings" - This signifies the gods or celestial entities that are invoked in rituals.
    • jehamānā: "who are invoked" - This indicates the act of calling upon these divine beings through prayers or rituals.
    • hotrāvidaḥ: "knowledgeable priests" - Refers to the priests who possess the knowledge of the Vedic hymns and rituals.
    • stomataṣṭāso: "with hymns" - This emphasizes the importance of hymns in invoking the divine.
    • arkaiḥ: "with offerings" - Refers to the offerings made to the deities, often in the form of fire sacrifices.
    • āgne: "O Agni" - Agni is the fire god, a central figure in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and the divine messenger.
    • yāhi: "may you come" - A call for the divine presence to manifest.
    • suvidatrebhir: "with well-prepared offerings" - This highlights the importance of preparation and intention in rituals.
    • arvāṅ: "truthful" - Signifying honesty and integrity in the offerings and intentions.
    • satyaiḥ: "with truth" - Emphasizing the value of truthfulness in spiritual practices.
    • kavyaiḥ: "wise ones" - Referring to those who possess wisdom and understanding.
    • pitṛbhir: "with ancestral spirits" - Acknowledging the presence and blessings of ancestors in rituals.
    • gharmasadbhiḥ: "who dwell in warmth" - Referring to the spirits or deities that reside in the sacred fire, symbolizing warmth and protection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern spiritual practices, individuals often seek guidance and blessings from higher powers during significant life events such as weddings, births, or new ventures. This mantra reflects the universal human desire for divine support in these moments.
    • In community gatherings or rituals, the preparation of offerings and the invocation of divine beings can be seen in various cultures, such as during Thanksgiving in the United States, where gratitude is expressed through shared meals and prayers.
    • In personal development, the emphasis on truthfulness and wisdom can be applied in daily life by fostering honesty in relationships and seeking knowledge through education and experience.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings through knowledge, preparation, and truthfulness. It highlights the interconnectedness of human beings with the divine and ancestral spirits, emphasizing the importance of intention in spiritual practices. In real life, this mantra serves as a reminder to approach our desires and needs with sincerity, wisdom, and respect for the traditions that connect us to our roots and the greater universe. Whether in personal rituals or communal celebrations, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide individuals towards a more fulfilling and spiritually enriched life.
