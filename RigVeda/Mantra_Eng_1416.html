नाकस्य पृष्ठे अधि तिष्ठति श्रितो यः पृणाति स ह देवेषु गच्छति |तस्मा आपो घृतम् अर्षन्ति सिन्धवस् तस्मा इयं दक्षिणा पिन्वते सदा ||
nākasya pṛṣṭhe adhi tiṣṭhati śrito yaḥ pṛṇāti sa ha deveṣu gacchati | tasmā āpo ghṛtam arṣanti sindhavas tasmā iyaṁ dakṣiṇā pinvate sadā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nākasya: of the sky
    • pṛṣṭhe: on the back
    • adhi: above
    • tiṣṭhati: stands
    • śrito: who is nourished
    • yaḥ: who
    • pṛṇāti: fills
    • sa: he
    • ha: indeed
    • deveṣu: among the gods
    • gacchati: goes
    • tasmā: therefore
    • āpo: waters
    • ghṛtam: ghee
    • arṣanti: flow
    • sindhavas: rivers
    • tasmā: therefore
    • iyaṁ: this
    • dakṣiṇā: southern
    • pinvate: bestows
    • sadā: always
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the connection between the sky and the earth, emphasizing the importance of nourishment and abundance.
    • The phrase "nākasya pṛṣṭhe adhi tiṣṭhati" suggests that something significant stands above the sky, indicating a higher power or divine presence.
    • The idea of being nourished and filled ("śrito yaḥ pṛṇāti") reflects the essential role of nature and the divine in providing sustenance to life.
    • The mention of "deveṣu gacchati" implies that those who are nourished by this divine presence are elevated to a higher spiritual state.
    • The reference to "āpo ghṛtam arṣanti sindhavas" highlights the flowing waters (rivers) that bring nourishment, akin to how rivers provide water for agriculture and life.
    • The concluding part "iyaṁ dakṣiṇā pinvate sadā" suggests that the southern direction is always bestowing blessings, which can be interpreted as a metaphor for prosperity and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the reliance on rivers for irrigation exemplifies the nourishment that nature provides, akin to the divine blessings mentioned in the mantra.
    • In spiritual practices, individuals often seek connection with higher powers through meditation or prayer, reflecting the idea of being nourished by divine energy.
    • In cultural rituals, offerings of ghee (ghṛtam) to deities symbolize gratitude for the sustenance provided by nature, aligning with the mantra's themes.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, divinity, and human existence, highlighting the importance of seeking nourishment from both the earth and the divine.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition of the blessings that come from nature and the divine, encouraging individuals to live in harmony with their surroundings.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire practices of sustainability, gratitude, and spiritual connection, fostering a deeper appreciation for the resources and blessings we receive.
