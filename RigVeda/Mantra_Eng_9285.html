सनद्वाजं विप्रवीरं तरुत्रं धनस्पृतं शूशुवांसं सुदक्षम् |दस्युहनं पूर्वभिदं इन्द्र सत्यं अस्मभ्यं चित्रं वृषणं रयिं दाह ||
sanadvājaṁ vipravīraṁ tarutraṁ dhanaspṛtaṁ śūśuvāṁsaṁ sudakṣam | dasyuhanam pūrbhidam indra satyam asmabhyaṁ citraṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ rayiṁ dāḥ ||
  • Sanadvājaṁ: This term can be broken down into "sana" meaning "good" or "auspicious" and "dvāja" meaning "flag" or "banner." It symbolizes the auspiciousness and victory that one should aspire to in life.
  • Vipravīraṁ: "Vipra" refers to a learned or wise person, and "vīra" means "hero" or "brave." Together, it signifies the importance of wisdom and bravery in overcoming challenges.
  • Tarutraṁ: This word means "the one who is strong" or "the one who can withstand." It emphasizes resilience and the ability to endure hardships.
  • Dhanaspṛtaṁ: "Dhana" means "wealth" and "spṛta" means "touched" or "influenced." This suggests the blessings of wealth and prosperity that one should seek in life.
  • Śūśuvāṁsaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is well-versed" or "the one who has good lineage." It highlights the importance of knowledge and heritage in shaping one's character.
  • Sudakṣam: "Su" means "good" and "dakṣam" means "skilled" or "capable." This emphasizes the value of skillfulness and competence in achieving success.
  • Dasyuhanam: This term refers to "the one who conquers enemies" or "the one who overcomes obstacles." It signifies the need for strength and determination in facing adversities.
  • Pūrbhidam: "Pūrbhi" means "from the east" or "the direction of the rising sun," symbolizing new beginnings and opportunities.
  • Indra: Indra is the king of gods in Vedic literature, representing power, authority, and the ability to protect and provide. Invoking Indra signifies seeking divine support in one's endeavors.
  • Satyam: This word means "truth." It emphasizes the importance of honesty and integrity in all actions.
  • Asmabhyaṁ: This term means "for us" or "to us," indicating a collective aspiration or prayer for blessings.
  • Citraṁ: "Citra" means "beautiful" or "varied." It signifies the diversity and richness of life experiences.
  • Vṛṣaṇaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who brings strength" or "the one who is powerful." It emphasizes the need for strength in various aspects of life.
  • Rayiṁ: "Rayi" refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the aspiration for abundance in life.
  • Dāḥ: This means "to give" or "to bestow." It emphasizes the importance of generosity and sharing one's blessings with others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the importance of wisdom, bravery, resilience, skillfulness, and truthfulness. It serves as a reminder to seek divine blessings for prosperity and strength while also encouraging individuals to be generous and share their wealth with others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal development, leadership, and community service, where one strives to embody these qualities and contribute positively to society.
