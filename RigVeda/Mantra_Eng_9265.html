विश्वस्य केतुर्भुवनस्य गर्भ आ रोधसी अपृणाज्जायमानः |वीळुं चिदाद्रिमभिनत्परायण जना यदाग्निमयजन्तपञ्च ||
viśvasya ketur bhuvanasya garbha ā rodasī apṛṇāj jāyamānaḥ | vīḻuṁ cid adrim abhinat parāyañ janā yad agnim ayajanta pañca ||
  • viśvasya ketur: This phrase can be interpreted as "the banner of the universe." It signifies a guiding principle or a source of inspiration that unites all beings and elements in the cosmos.
  • bhuvanasya garbha: This translates to "the womb of the world." It represents the nurturing aspect of the universe, where all creation is conceived and developed.
  • ā rodasī apṛṇāj: This means "the two worlds (heaven and earth) are connected." It emphasizes the relationship between the spiritual and the material realms, suggesting that both are interdependent.
  • jāyamānaḥ: This word means "being born" or "coming into existence." It highlights the continuous cycle of birth and rebirth in the universe.
  • vīḻuṁ cid adrim: This can be interpreted as "the wise one who breaks the mountain." It symbolizes overcoming obstacles and challenges through wisdom and knowledge.
  • abhinat parāyañ: This phrase means "the one who is revered." It indicates the importance of respect and reverence in our lives, particularly towards knowledge and wisdom.
  • janā yad agnim: This translates to "the people who worship fire." Fire is often seen as a symbol of transformation and purification, representing the energy that fuels life.
  • ayajanta pañca: This means "they who perform the five sacrifices." It refers to the various offerings made to maintain harmony in the universe, including offerings to nature, ancestors, and the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the interconnectedness of the universe, the importance of wisdom, and the reverence for the forces of nature. In real life, it serves as a reminder of our responsibility to honor and respect the world around us. By recognizing the cycles of life and the significance of our actions, we can strive to live in harmony with the universe. This mantra is applicable in various scenarios, such as environmental conservation, community building, and personal growth, where the principles of respect, wisdom, and interconnectedness can guide our actions and decisions.
