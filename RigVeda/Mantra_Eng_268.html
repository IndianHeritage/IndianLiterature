उद उत्तमं वरुण पाशम अस्मद अवाधमं विथ मध्यं श्रथाय |अथा वयं आदित्य व्रते तवानागसो अदितये स्याम ||
ud uttamaṁ varuṇa pāśam asmad avādhamaṁ vi madhyamaṁ śrathāya | athā vayam āditya vrate tavānāgaso aditaye syāma ||
  • ud uttamaṁ: This phrase translates to "upward, the best" or "the highest." It signifies the aspiration towards the highest ideals or goals in life.
  • varuṇa: Varuṇa is a deity associated with the cosmic order and moral authority. He represents the principles of truth and righteousness.
  • pāśam: This means "bond" or "noose." In a metaphorical sense, it can refer to the constraints or limitations that bind individuals.
  • asmad avādhamaṁ: This translates to "our lowest" or "our base." It indicates the recognition of one's own limitations or shortcomings.
  • vi madhyamaṁ: This means "in the middle" or "the average." It suggests a balance between extremes, advocating for moderation in life.
  • śrathāya: This term can be interpreted as "for the sake of trust" or "for the sake of faith." It emphasizes the importance of trust in relationships and actions.
  • athā vayam: This translates to "therefore we." It indicates a collective intention or action, suggesting unity in purpose.
  • āditya: Referring to the sun or solar deities, it symbolizes enlightenment, energy, and life-giving force.
  • vrate: This means "in the vow" or "in the commitment." It signifies dedication to a cause or principle.
  • tavānāgaso: This can be interpreted as "your non-violent" or "your peaceful." It emphasizes the importance of non-violence and harmony.
  • aditaye: This translates to "to the infinite" or "to the boundless." It signifies the limitless nature of existence and the universe.
  • syāma: This means "may we be" or "let us be." It expresses a desire for a certain state of being or existence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about striving for the highest ideals while acknowledging our limitations. It encourages individuals to seek balance and trust in their relationships and commitments. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, where one aims for self-improvement while being aware of their flaws. In community settings, it promotes unity and collective action towards common goals, fostering a sense of harmony and non-violence. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of aspiration, balance, and trust in our journey through life.
