अपाद धोत्राद उता पोत्राद अमत्तोत नेष्ट्राद अजुषत प्रयो हितम् |तुरीयम् पात्रम् अमृत्कम् अमर्त्यं द्रविणोदाः पिबतु द्राविणोदसः ||
apād dhotrād uta potrād amattota neṣṭrād ajuṣata prayo hitam | turīyam pātram amṛktam amartyaṁ draviṇodāḥ pibatu drāviṇodasaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apād: from the foot or base
    • dhotrād: from the one who pours (the libation)
    • uta: and
    • potrād: from the one who receives (the offering)
    • amattota: the one who is not intoxicated
    • neṣṭrād: from the one who is not a thief
    • ajuṣata: may they drink
    • prayo: beneficial
    • hitam: good or auspicious
    • turīyam: the fourth (or the ultimate)
    • pātram: vessel or container
    • amṛktam: not mixed (pure)
    • amartyaṁ: immortal
    • draviṇodāḥ: those who have wealth
    • pibatu: may they drink
    • drāviṇodasaḥ: those who are wealthy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the act of offering and receiving, emphasizing purity and the importance of good intentions.
    • The mention of "not intoxicated" and "not a thief" suggests a call for integrity and clarity in actions.
    • The phrase "may they drink" indicates a wish for the recipients to partake in the offerings, which can be seen as a metaphor for sharing knowledge, wealth, and prosperity.
    • The reference to "the fourth" can symbolize the ultimate goal or the highest state of being, which is often associated with spiritual enlightenment.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in contexts such as community gatherings, where offerings are made with pure intentions, and the focus is on sharing and uplifting one another.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on purity, integrity, and the sharing of resources for the greater good.
    • It serves as a reminder to approach life with a clear mind and good intentions, fostering a sense of community and mutual support.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be invoked during rituals, community service, or any gathering where the focus is on collective well-being and prosperity.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to contribute positively to society, ensuring that their actions are beneficial and uplifting for all.
