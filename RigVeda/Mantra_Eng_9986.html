परिवृक्तेव पतिविद्यम आनाṭ पीप्याना कूचकरेणेव सिञ्चन |एषैष्याऽचिद रथ्या जयेम सुमङ्गलं सिन्‍वद अस्तु सात्म ||
parivṛkteva patividyam ānaṭ pīpyānā kūcakreṇeva siñcan | eṣaiṣyā cid rathyā jayema sumaṅgalaṁ sinavad astu sātam ||
  • Mantra Breakdown:
    • parivṛkteva: This term can be understood as "like a well-cultivated field" or "well-prepared." It signifies the importance of preparation and readiness in life.
    • patividyam: This refers to "knowledge of the path" or "understanding of the way." It emphasizes the significance of having direction and wisdom in one's journey.
    • ānaṭ: This word can be interpreted as "to be nourished" or "to be sustained." It highlights the need for sustenance, both physically and spiritually.
    • pīpyānā: This term means "thirsty" or "yearning." It reflects the human condition of longing for knowledge, fulfillment, and connection.
    • kūcakreṇeva: This phrase can be translated as "like a stream of water." It symbolizes the flow of life and the importance of adaptability and resilience.
    • siñcan: This means "to sprinkle" or "to nourish." It suggests the act of nurturing oneself and others, fostering growth and well-being.
    • eṣaiṣyā: This can be understood as "may we attain" or "let us achieve." It expresses a desire for success and accomplishment.
    • cid rathyā: This phrase translates to "through the path of consciousness." It emphasizes the importance of awareness and mindfulness in our actions.
    • jayema: This means "may we conquer" or "may we triumph." It reflects the aspiration for victory over challenges and obstacles.
    • sumaṅgalaṁ: This term signifies "auspiciousness" or "good fortune." It highlights the desire for positive outcomes in life.
    • sinavad: This can be interpreted as "may it be so" or "let it be." It conveys a sense of affirmation and hope.
    • astu sātam: This phrase means "let it be a hundredfold" or "may it multiply." It signifies the wish for abundance and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, being "parivṛkteva" can relate to thorough preparation before a presentation or project, ensuring that one is well-equipped to succeed.
    • The concept of "patividyam" can be seen in individuals who seek mentorship or guidance, understanding that having a clear path is crucial for personal and professional growth.
    • "ānaṭ" reflects the importance of self-care, where individuals nourish their bodies and minds through healthy habits, such as balanced diets and mindfulness practices.
    • The feeling of "pīpyānā" can be observed in students who are eager to learn and grow, demonstrating a thirst for knowledge that drives them to seek out new experiences.
    • "kūcakreṇeva" can be likened to adapting to life's changes, much like a river that flows around obstacles, illustrating resilience in the face of adversity.
    • The act of "siñcan" can be seen in community service, where individuals come together to support and nurture those in need, fostering a sense of belonging and care.
    • "eṣaiṣyā" resonates with goal-setting, where individuals visualize their aspirations and work diligently towards achieving them.
    • "cid rathyā" emphasizes the importance of mindfulness in daily activities, encouraging individuals to be present and aware of their surroundings and actions.
    • "jayema" can be reflected in sports, where athletes strive to overcome their limits and achieve victory through hard work and determination.
    • "sumaṅgalaṁ" is often invoked in rituals and prayers, where individuals seek blessings for good fortune and success in their endeavors.
    • "sinavad" and "astu sātam" can be seen in affirmations and positive thinking, where individuals express their hopes for abundance and prosperity in their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of preparation, knowledge, nourishment, and the pursuit of auspiciousness in life.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to cultivate our paths with awareness and resilience, nurturing ourselves and others along the way
