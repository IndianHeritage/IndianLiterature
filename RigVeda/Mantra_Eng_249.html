अप्सु मे सोमो अब्रवीद अन्तर विश्वानि भेषजाः | अग्निं च विश्वशम्भुवम् आपश्च विश्वभेषजीः ||
apsu me somo abravīd antar viśvāni bheṣajā | agniṁ ca viśvaśambhuvam āpaś ca viśvabheṣajīḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apsu: in the waters
    • me: to me
    • somo: the soma (a sacred drink)
    • abravīd: spoke or declared
    • antar: within
    • viśvāni: all or every
    • bheṣajā: healing or medicinal
    • agniṁ: to Agni (the fire god)
    • ca: and
    • viśvaśambhuvam: all-pervading or universal
    • āpaś: waters
    • ca: and
    • viśvabheṣajīḥ: all-healing or universal medicine
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the connection between water, the sacred soma, and Agni, the fire god.
    • It emphasizes the healing properties of nature, suggesting that all elements of the universe contribute to health and well-being.
    • The invocation of Agni signifies the transformative power of fire, which is essential for purification and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Ayurveda, the ancient Indian system of medicine, water is considered vital for health, and herbal remedies often utilize water as a base for infusions and decoctions.
    • The concept of soma can be likened to modern health drinks that are designed to rejuvenate and heal the body.
    • Agni represents the digestive fire in Ayurveda, which is crucial for the assimilation of nutrients and overall health.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of natural elements in promoting health and healing.
    • It serves as a reminder to respect and utilize the resources provided by nature, such as water and fire, for our well-being.
    • In contemporary life, this can be applied by embracing holistic health practices that consider the balance of physical, mental, and spiritual well-being.
