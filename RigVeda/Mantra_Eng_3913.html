सहस्रसाम् आग्निवेशिं गृणीषे शत्रिम अग्न उपमाṁ केतुम आर्यः |तस्मा आपः संयतः पीपयन्त तस्मिन क्षत्रम् अमवत त्वेषम् अस्तु ||
sahasrasām āgniveśiṁ gṛṇīṣe śatrim agna upamāṁ ketum aryaḥ | tasmā āpaḥ saṁyataḥ pīpayanta tasmin kṣatram amavat tveṣam astu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahasrasām: of thousands
    • āgniveśiṁ: the one who is established in fire
    • gṛṇīṣe: I sing or praise
    • śatrim: the enemy or adversary
    • agna: O Agni (the fire god)
    • upamāṁ: the best or the highest
    • ketum: the banner or the standard
    • aryaḥ: noble or honorable
    • tasmā: therefore
    • āpaḥ: waters
    • saṁyataḥ: controlled or restrained
    • pīpayanta: nourishing or providing sustenance
    • tasmin: in that
    • kṣatram: power or dominion
    • amavat: may it protect
    • tveṣam: this
    • astu: let it be
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The phrase "sahasrasām āgniveśiṁ" suggests the vastness and omnipresence of Agni, symbolizing the countless forms of energy and transformation in the universe.
    • By praising Agni, the speaker seeks protection from adversaries ("śatrim") and aims to establish a noble standard ("ketum aryaḥ").
    • The mention of "āpaḥ" (waters) signifies the importance of balance and nourishment in life, as water is essential for sustenance.
    • The mantra concludes with a plea for protection and strength in one's endeavors, emphasizing the need for divine support in overcoming challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often invoke the spirit of teamwork and collaboration (akin to Agni) to overcome competition (the adversary) and achieve their goals.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek guidance and strength from spiritual practices or rituals that invoke a higher power for protection and support during difficult times.
    • Environmental initiatives can be seen as a modern interpretation of the balance between fire (energy) and water (sustenance), where sustainable practices nourish the earth while respecting its resources.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of invoking divine energy for protection and guidance in the face of challenges.
    • It teaches the importance of balance in life, where the elements of fire and water symbolize energy and sustenance, respectively.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles of this mantra can be applied in personal growth, leadership, and environmental stewardship, reminding us to seek divine support and maintain harmony in our endeavors.
