सव्यां अनु स्फिग्यं वावसे वृषा न दानो अस्य रोषति |मध्वा संप्रक्ताः सारघेण धेनवस तुयम एहि द्रवा पिब ||
savyām anu sphigyaṁ vāvase vṛṣā na dāno asya roṣati | madhvā sampṛktāḥ sāragheṇa dhenavas tūyam ehi dravā piba ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • savyām: In the right way, auspiciously
    • anu: Following, in accordance with
    • sphigyaṁ: Abundance, richness
    • vāvase: You may drink, you may enjoy
    • vṛṣā: Strength, power
    • na: Not
    • dāno: Generosity, giving
    • asya: Of this, of it
    • roṣati: Anger, wrath
    • madhvā: Sweetness, honey
    • sampṛktāḥ: Mixed, combined
    • sāragheṇa: With the essence, with the core
    • dhenavas: Of the cows, of the nurturing
    • tūyam: Water, essence
    • ehi: Come, approach
    • dravā: Fluid, liquid
    • piba: Drink
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of approaching abundance and richness in a righteous manner.
    • The phrase "savyām anu sphigyaṁ" suggests that one should follow the path of righteousness to attain prosperity.
    • The mention of "vāvase vṛṣā" indicates that strength and power should not be accompanied by anger or wrath.
    • It highlights the significance of generosity and the idea that true abundance does not come from selfishness.
    • The reference to "madhvā sampṛktāḥ" implies that sweetness and joy come from a harmonious blend of elements, much like how honey is a mixture of various floral essences.
    • The call to "ehi dravā piba" invites one to partake in the nourishing essence of life, symbolizing the importance of embracing the good and the sweet in our experiences.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, approaching projects with a positive and collaborative attitude can lead to greater success and satisfaction among team members.
    • In personal relationships, practicing generosity and kindness fosters deeper connections and mutual respect.
    • In community service, engaging in acts of giving without expecting anything in return can create a more harmonious and supportive environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of approaching life with righteousness, generosity, and a positive spirit.
    • It teaches that true abundance is achieved not through selfishness or anger, but through kindness and collaboration.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying these principles can lead to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence, both personally and within the community.
