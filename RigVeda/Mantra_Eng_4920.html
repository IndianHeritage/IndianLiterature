किम अङ्ग त्वा ब्रह्मणः सोम गोपां किम अङ्ग त्वाहुर अभिशस्तिपां नः |किम अङ्ग नः पश्यसि निद्यमानान ब्रह्मद्विषे तपुषिं हेतिम अस्य ||
kim aṅga tvā brahmaṇaḥ soma gopāṁ kim aṅga tvāhur abhiśastipāṁ naḥ | kim aṅga naḥ paśyasi nidyamānān brahmadviṣe tapuṣiṁ hetim asya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kim aṅga: What is this?
    • tvā: You
    • brahmaṇaḥ: Of the Brahman (the ultimate reality)
    • soma: Soma (a sacred drink, often associated with immortality)
    • gopāṁ: The protector or guardian
    • abhiśastipāṁ: Those who are blessed or endowed
    • naḥ: Our
    • paśyasi: You see
    • nidyamānān: Those who are being offered
    • brahmadviṣe: To the one who is hostile to Brahman
    • tapuṣiṁ: The one who is engaged in austerity
    • hetim: The cause or reason
    • asya: Of this
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a series of questions, seeking to understand the nature of the divine and its relationship with the individual.
    • The reference to "soma" indicates a connection to spiritual nourishment and the quest for higher consciousness.
    • The term "gopāṁ" suggests the idea of guardianship, implying that the divine protects those who seek truth.
    • By asking "What is this?" the mantra invites introspection and contemplation about one's own existence and purpose.
    • The mention of "brahmadviṣe" highlights the challenges faced by those who pursue spiritual paths, as they may encounter opposition or negativity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, an employee may feel challenged by a colleague who is resistant to change. The mantra encourages the individual to seek understanding and maintain focus on their goals, much like the pursuit of Brahman.
    • In personal relationships, one may encounter negativity or hostility from others. This mantra serves as a reminder to remain steadfast in one's values and to seek the support of those who uplift and protect.
    • During times of personal crisis, individuals often turn to spiritual practices for guidance. The reference to "soma" can be likened to seeking solace in meditation or prayer, which nourishes the soul.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking truth and understanding in the face of challenges.
    • It encourages individuals to reflect on their spiritual journey and the guardianship provided by the divine.
    • In real life, the teachings of this mantra can be applied to various situations, reminding us to remain focused on our higher purpose and to seek support from those who share our values.
    • Ultimately, it signifies the quest for spiritual enlightenment and the resilience required to overcome obstacles on that path.
