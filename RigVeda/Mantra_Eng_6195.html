आ नो रयिम मदच्युतं पुरुक्षुं विश्वधायसम् |इयर्ता मरुतो दिवः ||
ā no rayim madacyutam purukṣuṁ viśvadhāyasam | iyartā maruto divaḥ ||
  • ā no rayim: This phrase can be translated to "may we attain wealth" or "may we receive prosperity." In real life, this can be understood as a prayer for abundance in various forms, such as financial stability, health, and happiness.
  • madacyutam: This term refers to "the one who is not subject to decay" or "immortal." It signifies the eternal nature of certain truths or values. For example, love and knowledge are often seen as timeless and enduring, transcending material possessions.
  • purukṣuṁ: This word means "the great being" or "the vast person." It can be interpreted as a reference to the universal spirit or consciousness that connects all living beings. In a practical sense, this can relate to the idea of community and interconnectedness among people.
  • viśvadhāyasam: This term translates to "the one who supports all" or "the all-encompassing." It emphasizes the idea of unity and support within the universe. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way communities come together to support one another during times of need.
  • iyartā: This word can be understood as "may we be inspired" or "may we be guided." It reflects the desire for divine guidance in our actions and decisions. For instance, individuals often seek inspiration from mentors or spiritual leaders to navigate life's challenges.
  • maruto: This term refers to "the winds" or "the forces of nature." It symbolizes the dynamic and ever-changing aspects of life. In real life, this can be likened to the unpredictability of circumstances and the need to adapt to change.
  • divaḥ: This word means "of the heavens" or "divine." It signifies a connection to the higher realms or spiritual dimensions. In practical terms, this can relate to the pursuit of higher ideals and values in our lives, such as compassion, integrity, and wisdom.

Conclusion: The mantra "ā no rayim madacyutam purukṣuṁ viśvadhāyasam | iyartā maruto divaḥ ||" encapsulates a profound prayer for prosperity, guidance, and unity. Its significance lies in the recognition of the interconnectedness of all beings and the eternal values that transcend material existence. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek abundance not just in material wealth but in relationships, knowledge, and spiritual growth. It encourages us to support one another and to remain adaptable in the face of life's challenges, ultimately guiding us towards a more harmonious and fulfilling existence.
