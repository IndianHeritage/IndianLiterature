अस्मै वयं यद वावान तद विविष्म इन्द्राय यो नः प्रदिवो अपस कः |सुते सोमे स्तुमसि शंसद उक्तेन्द्राय ब्रह्म वर्धनं यथासत ||
asmai vayaṁ yad vāvāna tad viviṣma indrāya yo naḥ pradivo apas kaḥ | sute some stumasi śaṁsad ukthendrāya brahma vardhanaṁ yathāsat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmai: to him
    • vayaṁ: our
    • yad: whatever
    • vāvāna: is offered
    • tad: that
    • viviṣma: we may enter
    • indrāya: to Indra
    • yo: who
    • naḥ: for us
    • pradivo: from the heavens
    • apas: away
    • kaḥ: who
    • sute: in the soma
    • some: of the soma
    • stumasi: in the praise
    • śaṁsad: may we be blessed
    • ukthendrāya: to the one who is the lord of hymns
    • brahma: the ultimate reality
    • vardhanaṁ: may it grow
    • yathāsat: as it is true
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, seeking his blessings and guidance.
    • The phrase "asmai vayaṁ yad vāvāna" indicates a sense of offering and devotion, suggesting that whatever is offered should be accepted by Indra.
    • "tad viviṣma indrāya" expresses the desire to enter into a state of divine connection with Indra, highlighting the importance of spiritual communion.
    • The mention of "sute some" refers to the sacred soma, which is often associated with divine inspiration and ecstasy, emphasizing the significance of spiritual experiences.
    • "ukthendrāya brahma vardhanaṁ" signifies the aspiration for growth in spiritual wisdom and understanding, aligning oneself with the ultimate reality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied during rituals or personal prayers where individuals seek guidance and blessings from a higher power.
    • In moments of decision-making or challenges, one might reflect on this mantra to invoke clarity and strength, similar to how leaders seek counsel from wise advisors.
    • During celebrations or significant life events, such as weddings or births, this mantra can be recited to bless the occasion and invite divine presence.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, the pursuit of spiritual growth, and the importance of divine connection in one's life.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to seek blessings and wisdom from higher powers, fostering a sense of humility and reverence.
    • In a world filled with distractions, this mantra serves as a guiding light, encouraging individuals to cultivate a deeper understanding of themselves and their place in the universe.
