उग्रो जज्ञे वीर्याय स्वधावाँ चक्रिर अपो नार्यो यत करिष्यन् |जग्मिर युवा नृषदानम अवोभिस त्राता न इन्द्र एनसो महश्चित ||
ugro jajñe vīryāya svadhāvāñ cakrir apo naryo yat kariṣyan | jagmir yuvā nṛṣadanam avobhis trātā na indra enaso mahaś cit ||
  • ugro jajñe: This phrase can be translated as "the powerful one is born." It signifies the emergence of strength and vigor. In real life, this can be related to the birth of a leader or a significant figure who brings change and empowerment to a community.
  • vīryāya: This word means "for strength" or "for valor." It emphasizes the importance of courage and bravery in facing challenges. For example, a soldier displaying valor in battle or an activist standing up for justice embodies this concept.
  • svadhāvāñ: This term refers to "self-motivated" or "self-driven." It highlights the significance of personal initiative and determination. In everyday life, this can be seen in entrepreneurs who take risks to start their own businesses, driven by their vision and passion.
  • cakrir apo naryo: This phrase translates to "the waters (or resources) are made to flow." It symbolizes the idea of harnessing resources for growth and sustenance. In a practical sense, this can relate to sustainable water management practices that ensure communities have access to clean water.
  • yat kariṣyan: This means "what is to be done" or "what is intended." It reflects the importance of purpose and intention in actions. For instance, setting clear goals in personal or professional life aligns with this idea, as it guides individuals toward their desired outcomes.
  • jagmir yuvā nṛṣadanam: This phrase can be interpreted as "the young ones (or youth) are to be protected." It emphasizes the responsibility of society to nurture and safeguard its future generations. In real life, this can be seen in educational initiatives aimed at empowering youth through knowledge and skills.
  • avobhis trātā: This translates to "the protector from all sides." It signifies the need for comprehensive protection and support. In a modern context, this can relate to community support systems that provide safety nets for vulnerable populations.
  • na indra enaso mahaś cit: This phrase means "may the great Indra (the deity of strength and rain) grant us strength." It invokes divine support for strength and resilience. In real life, this can be reflected in rituals or practices where individuals seek blessings for strength in overcoming obstacles.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, initiative, protection, and purpose. Its significance lies in the encouragement of individuals and communities to harness their inner strength, take initiative, and protect future generations. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as leadership, community service, and personal development, fostering a society that values courage, responsibility, and growth.
