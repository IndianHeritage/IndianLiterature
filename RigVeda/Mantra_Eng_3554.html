निर्युवाणो अशस्तीर नियुत्वाम् इन्द्रसारथिः |वायव आ चन्द्रेण रथेन याहि सुतस्य पीतये ||
niryuvāṇo aśastīr niyutvām̐ indrasārathiḥ | vāyav ā candreṇa rathena yāhi sutasya pītaye ||
  • niryuvāṇo aśastīr: This phrase can be translated as "the one who is moving forward, the one who is strong." In real life, this can be related to individuals or leaders who are determined and resilient in their pursuits, much like a successful entrepreneur who overcomes obstacles to achieve their goals.
  • niyutvām̐ indrasārathiḥ: This means "the charioteer of Indra." Indra is often seen as a king of the gods and a symbol of power and authority. In a modern context, this could refer to someone who guides or leads others, such as a mentor or a coach who helps individuals navigate their paths in life.
  • vāyav ā candreṇa: This translates to "with the wind and the moon." The wind symbolizes change and movement, while the moon represents calmness and stability. Together, they can signify the balance between embracing change and maintaining inner peace, much like how a person adapts to life's challenges while staying grounded.
  • rathena yāhi sutasya pītaye: This phrase means "go forth with the chariot for the sake of the son." This can be interpreted as taking action or making efforts for the benefit of future generations, similar to how parents work hard to provide a better life for their children.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, guidance, balance, and action for the benefit of others. In real life, it serves as a reminder to be resilient in the face of challenges, to lead and support those around us, to adapt to changes while maintaining our core values, and to work towards a better future for the next generation. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as in leadership roles, parenting, and personal development, encouraging individuals to embody these qualities in their daily lives.
