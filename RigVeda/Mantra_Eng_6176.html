अस्माकं त्वां सुतामुप वीतपृष्ठा अभि प्रयः |शतम् वहन्तु हरयः ||
asmākaṁ tvā sutām̐ upa vītapṛṣṭhā abhi prayaḥ | śataṁ vahantu harayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asmākaṁ: Our
    • tvā: You
    • sutām̐: Offspring or progeny
    • upa: Near or close
    • vītapṛṣṭhā: With a strong back or support
    • abhi: Towards or for
    • prayaḥ: Efforts or endeavors
    • śataṁ: A hundred
    • vahantu: May carry or bring
    • harayaḥ: The divine or auspicious beings
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra expresses a prayer for the divine beings to support and carry forth our efforts, particularly in relation to our progeny or offspring. It emphasizes the importance of divine assistance in our endeavors, especially when it comes to nurturing and guiding the next generation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In parenting, this mantra can be a reminder for parents to seek divine guidance in raising their children, ensuring they grow up with strong values and support.
    • In education, teachers can invoke this mantra to inspire their students, hoping that their efforts in teaching will be supported by higher powers, leading to successful outcomes.
    • In community service, volunteers may use this mantra to seek blessings for their initiatives aimed at helping others, ensuring that their hard work is fruitful and impactful.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in our endeavors, particularly in relation to the future generations. It serves as a reminder that while we put in our efforts, seeking blessings and guidance from higher powers can enhance our endeavors. This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as parenting, education, and community service, where the nurturing of the next generation and the success of our efforts are paramount.
