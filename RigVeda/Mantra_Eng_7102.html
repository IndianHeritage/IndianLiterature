पाहि विश्वस्माद् रक्षसो अराव्णः प्र स्म वाजेषु नोऽव || त्वामिद्धि नेदिष्टं देवतातय आपिं नक्षामहे वृधे ||
pāhi viśvasmād rakṣaso arāvṇaḥ pra sma vājeṣu no 'va | tvām id dhi nediṣṭhaṁ devatātaya āpiṁ nakṣāmahe vṛdhe ||
  • pāhi - This word translates to "protect" or "guard." It signifies the need for divine protection in our lives.
  • viśvasmād - Meaning "from all directions" or "from everywhere," it emphasizes the comprehensive nature of the protection sought.
  • rakṣaso - This refers to "demons" or "evil forces." It symbolizes the negative influences or challenges we face in life.
  • arāvṇaḥ - This term can be interpreted as "unseen" or "hidden." It suggests that some threats may not be immediately visible.
  • pra sma - This phrase means "may we not be harmed." It expresses a plea for safety and well-being.
  • vājeṣu - Translated as "in the battles" or "in the struggles," it indicates the challenges we encounter in our daily lives.
  • no 'va - This means "not for us," reinforcing the desire to be shielded from harm.
  • tvām - This word means "you," referring to the divine or a higher power that is being addressed for protection.
  • id dhi - This translates to "indeed" or "certainly," emphasizing the sincerity of the plea.
  • nediṣṭhaṁ - Meaning "the best" or "the most excellent," it signifies the high regard for the divine being invoked.
  • devatātaya - This term refers to "divine nature" or "godliness," highlighting the sacred aspect of the entity being addressed.
  • āpiṁ - This can be interpreted as "to us" or "for us," indicating a personal connection to the divine.
  • nakṣāmahe - Meaning "we do not fail," it expresses confidence in the divine's ability to protect.
  • vṛdhe - This word translates to "in growth" or "in prosperity," suggesting that protection leads to flourishing and success.

In summary, this mantra is a heartfelt invocation for protection from all forms of negativity and challenges in life. It acknowledges the unseen threats that may arise and seeks the divine's assistance in overcoming them. The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine support in our struggles, reinforcing the belief that with such protection, we can thrive and grow.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various situations, such as:

  • During times of personal crisis, individuals may recite this mantra to seek strength and protection from negative influences.
  • In professional settings, it can be a reminder to stay focused and shield oneself from workplace politics or competition.
  • Parents may use this mantra to invoke safety and well-being for their children, especially in challenging environments.

Ultimately, the significance of this mantra lies in its ability to instill a sense of security and confidence, encouraging individuals to face life's challenges with the assurance that they are supported by a higher power.
