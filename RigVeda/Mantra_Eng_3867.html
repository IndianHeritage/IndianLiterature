सुपेशसम् माव सृजन्त्य अस्तं गवां सहस्रै रुशमासो अग्ने |तीव्राः इन्द्रम् अममन्दुः सुतासो 'क्तोर व्युष्टौ परितकम्यायाः ||
supeśasam māva sṛjanty astaṁ gavāṁ sahasrai ruśamāso agne | tīvrā indram amamanduḥ sutāso 'ktor vyuṣṭau paritakmyāyāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • supeśasam: Well-formed or auspicious.
    • māva: May we not be deprived.
    • sṛjanty: They create or produce.
    • astaṁ: The setting or going down (often referring to the sun).
    • gavāṁ: Of the cows or cattle.
    • sahasrai: Thousand.
    • ruśamāso: With great strength or vigor.
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god).
    • tīvrā: Intense or powerful.
    • indram: O Indra (the king of gods).
    • amamanduḥ: Who is the source of abundance.
    • sutāso: Of the sons or offspring.
    • 'ktor: Who is the protector.
    • vyuṣṭau: In the dawn or morning.
    • paritakmyāyāḥ: Surrounding or encompassing.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the auspiciousness and abundance associated with Agni and Indra.
    • It emphasizes the importance of creation and sustenance, particularly in relation to nature and agriculture.
    • The reference to cows symbolizes wealth and nourishment, highlighting the significance of livestock in agrarian societies.
    • The mention of dawn signifies new beginnings and the hope that comes with each day.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, the invocation of Agni and Indra can be seen during rituals for a good harvest, where farmers pray for favorable weather and abundant crops.
    • The importance of livestock, such as cows, is evident in many cultures where they are considered a source of wealth and sustenance.
    • Morning rituals and prayers are common in various traditions, symbolizing the hope and opportunities that each new day brings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of nature, divinity, and human existence.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of gratitude for the resources provided by nature and the divine.
    • In modern life, the principles of sustainability, respect for nature, and the importance of community can be derived from this ancient wisdom.
    • By recognizing the significance of these elements, individuals can foster a deeper connection with their environment and promote a harmonious existence.
