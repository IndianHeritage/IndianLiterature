मन्त्र: पूर्वीर इन्द्रस्य रातयो न विदस्यन्त्य ऊतयः | यदि वाजस्य गोमतः स्तोत्रिभ्यो मंहते मगहम् ||
pūrvīr indrasya rātayo na vi dasyanty ūtayaḥ | yadī vājasya gomataḥ stotṛbhyo maṁhate magham ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pūrvīr: of the early or previous
    • indrasya: of Indra (the king of gods)
    • rātayo: the chariots or vehicles
    • na: not
    • vi: apart or away
    • dasyanty: they are separated or divided
    • ūtayaḥ: the ones who are united or connected
    • yadī: if
    • vājasya: of the horse or strength
    • gomataḥ: of the cow or wealth
    • stotṛbhyo: to the worshippers or those who praise
    • maṁhate: they are honored or revered
    • magham: the great wealth or abundance
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the unity and strength that comes from the blessings of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who is often associated with power and prosperity.
    • The phrase "pūrvīr indrasya rātayo" suggests that the previous blessings or actions of Indra are crucial for the success of endeavors.
    • The idea that "na vi dasyanty ūtayaḥ" implies that those who are united in purpose will not be separated or divided, emphasizing the importance of community and collaboration.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "yadī vājasya gomataḥ stotṛbhyo maṁhate magham," indicates that if one honors and praises the sources of strength (symbolized by the horse) and wealth (symbolized by the cow), they will receive great abundance in return.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to teamwork. When team members work together harmoniously, they can achieve greater success than when they work in isolation.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra highlights the importance of unity and support. Friends and family who uplift each other can create a nurturing environment that fosters growth and prosperity.
    • In agriculture, honoring the natural elements (like rain, represented by Indra) and the resources (like cattle) can lead to a bountiful harvest, reflecting the mantra's message of reverence leading to abundance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, collaboration, and reverence for the sources of strength and wealth in our lives.
    • Its application in real life can be seen in various domains, including personal relationships, teamwork, and respect for nature.
    • By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals and communities can cultivate an environment of prosperity and abundance.
