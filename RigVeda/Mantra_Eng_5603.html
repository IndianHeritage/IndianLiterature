आ वो होतā जोहवीति सत्तः सत्राचीं रातिम मरुतो गृणानः |य इवतो वृषणो अस्ति गोपाः सो अद्वयावी हवते व उक्थैः ||
ā vo hotā johavīti sattaḥ satrācīṁ rātim maruto gṛṇānaḥ | ya īvato vṛṣaṇo asti gopāḥ so advayāvī havate va ukthaiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vo: To you all
    • hotā: The priest or the one who offers
    • johavīti: Invoking or calling upon
    • sattaḥ: Truth or reality
    • satrācīṁ: From the assembly or gathering
    • rātim: Joy or delight
    • maruto: The Maruts, storm deities
    • gṛṇānaḥ: Singing or praising
    • ya: Who
    • īvato: Of the strong or powerful
    • vṛṣaṇo: The one who is capable or strong
    • asti: Is
    • gopāḥ: The protectors or guardians
    • so: He
    • advayāvī: Without a second or unique
    • havate: Offers or sacrifices
    • va: Indeed
    • ukthaiḥ: With hymns or praises
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine forces, particularly the Maruts, who are associated with storms and the vitality of nature.
    • It emphasizes the importance of truth and joy in gatherings, suggesting that the presence of divine beings enhances the experience of community and celebration.
    • The mention of "gopāḥ" indicates the guardianship and protection provided by these divine forces, which can be interpreted as a metaphor for the protective aspects of nature and community.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, such as a festival, invoking the spirit of joy and truth can create a harmonious atmosphere, much like the Maruts bringing vitality to nature.
    • In personal life, when one seeks guidance or protection, acknowledging the strength and support of community can lead to a sense of security and belonging.
    • In environmental contexts, recognizing the guardianship of nature can inspire actions that protect the environment, reflecting the mantra's call for respect and reverence towards the natural world.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of community, truth, and the divine presence in our lives.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the joy of togetherness and the protective forces that surround us, both in nature and in our social structures.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to foster a sense of unity, respect for nature, and the celebration of truth, which can lead to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
