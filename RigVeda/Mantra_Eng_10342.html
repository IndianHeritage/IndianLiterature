आयम् अस्मासु काव्य ऋभर वज्रो दास्वते |आयम् बिभर्त्य ऊर्ध्वकृषणं मदं ऋभर न कृत्व्यम् मदं ||
ayam asmāsu kāvya ṛbhur vajro dāsvate | ayam bibharty ūrdhvakṛśanam madam ṛbhur na kṛtvyam madam ||
  • ayam asmāsu kāvya ṛbhur vajro dāsvate
    • ayam - This means "this" or "this one," indicating a specific focus.
    • asmāsu - Translates to "in us" or "among us," suggesting a collective experience or presence.
    • kāvya - Refers to "poetry" or "art," symbolizing creativity and expression.
    • ṛbhur - Means "the one who is strong" or "the powerful," indicating strength and resilience.
    • vajro - Translates to "thunderbolt" or "diamond," representing indestructibility and power.
    • dāsvate - Means "to be given" or "to be bestowed," implying a gift or blessing.
  • ayam bibharty ūrdhvakṛśanam madam ṛbhur na kṛtvyam madam
    • bibharty - Means "carries" or "supports," indicating a nurturing or sustaining force.
    • ūrdhvakṛśanam - Translates to "the one who is elevated" or "the one who rises high," symbolizing aspiration and growth.
    • madam - Refers to "intoxication" or "ecstasy," suggesting a state of bliss or heightened awareness.
    • na - Means "not," indicating negation or absence.
    • kṛtvyam - Translates to "action" or "deed," referring to the importance of doing or achieving.

The mantra speaks to the inherent strength and creativity within us, suggesting that we possess a powerful essence that can elevate us. The imagery of the "thunderbolt" signifies that this strength is not only formidable but also a gift that can be harnessed for positive action. The second part emphasizes the importance of nurturing this strength and creativity, suggesting that while we may experience moments of bliss or intoxication, we must also engage in meaningful actions to realize our potential.


Conclusion: This mantra is significant as it encourages individuals to recognize and cultivate their inner strength and creativity. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, artistic expression, and leadership. For instance, an artist may draw inspiration from this mantra to create powerful works that resonate with others, while a leader may embody the qualities of strength and elevation to inspire their team. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that our inner power, when nurtured and acted upon, can lead to profound transformations in ourselves and the world around us.
