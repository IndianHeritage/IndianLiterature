सत्तो होता मनुष्वद आ देवां अच् छा विदुष्टारः |अग्निर हव्याऽ सुषूदति देवो देवेषु मेधिरो वित्तं मे अस्य रोदसी ||
satto hotā manuṣvad ā devām̐ acchā viduṣṭaraḥ | agnir havyā suṣūdati devo deveṣu medhiro vittam me asya rodasī ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • satto: Of the truth or the truthful
    • hotā: The one who invokes or calls upon
    • manuṣvad: Of human beings or mankind
    • ā: To or towards
    • devām̐: The divine or the gods
    • acchā: Good or auspicious
    • viduṣṭaraḥ: Knowledgeable or wise
    • agnir: Fire
    • havyā: Offerings or sacrifices
    • suṣūdati: Satisfies or fulfills
    • devo: The god
    • deveṣu: Among the gods
    • medhiro: Wise or intelligent
    • vittam: Wealth or resources
    • me: My
    • asya: This
    • rodasī: The two worlds or realms
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of truthfulness and the invocation of divine forces.
    • It suggests that when humans call upon the divine with sincerity and truth, they receive blessings and wisdom.
    • The mention of fire (Agni) symbolizes purification and the transformative power of offerings made with devotion.
    • The phrase "wealth of the two worlds" indicates the holistic prosperity that comes from aligning oneself with divine principles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies truthfulness and integrity can inspire their team, leading to a more productive and harmonious work environment.
    • In personal relationships, being truthful fosters trust and strengthens bonds, much like the invocation of divine blessings in the mantra.
    • During rituals or ceremonies, the act of making offerings with a pure heart can be seen as a way to connect with higher powers, similar to the fire's role in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of truth and sincerity in our interactions with the divine and with each other.
    • It highlights the idea that true wealth and prosperity come from a deep connection with spiritual principles and the wisdom that arises from them.
    • In everyday life, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a more fulfilling existence.
