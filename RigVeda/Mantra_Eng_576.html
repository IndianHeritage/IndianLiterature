विष्वस्य हि प्राणनं जीवने त्वे वियद उच्छसि सूनरि |सां नो रथेन बृहता विभावरी श्रुधि चित्रामघे हवम् ||
viśvasya hi prāṇanaṁ jīvanaṁ tve vi yad ucchasi sūnari | sā no rathena bṛhatā vibhāvari śrudhi citrāmaghe havam ||
  • viśvasya hi prāṇanaṁ jīvanaṁ: This phrase translates to "the life force of all beings." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all living entities and the universal breath that sustains life.
  • tve vi yad ucchasi sūnari: This means "O Sun, you who rise high." Here, the Sun is personified as a powerful entity that brings light and energy, essential for life on Earth.
  • sā no rathena bṛhatā: This translates to "may she, with her great chariot." The 'chariot' symbolizes the Sun's journey across the sky, representing the passage of time and the cycles of nature.
  • vibhāvari: This word means "illuminating" or "enlightening." It signifies the role of the Sun in providing clarity and understanding in life.
  • śrudhi citrāmaghe havam: This can be interpreted as "we offer our prayers to you, O magnificent one." It reflects the act of devotion and the importance of seeking blessings from the divine.

In conclusion, this mantra from the Rigveda highlights the significance of the Sun as a life-giving force and a source of enlightenment. It teaches us to recognize the interconnectedness of all life and the importance of seeking guidance and blessings from higher powers. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in practices such as meditation, where individuals seek to connect with the universal life force, or in rituals that honor nature and its cycles. By acknowledging the Sun's role in sustaining life, we cultivate gratitude and respect for the environment, promoting a harmonious existence with all living beings.
