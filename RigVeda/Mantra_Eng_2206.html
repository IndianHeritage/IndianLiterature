तं उ स्तुष इन्द्रं तं गृणीषे यस्मिन पुरा वावृधुः शाशदुश्च |स वस्वः कामं पीपरद इयानो ब्रह्मण्यतो नूतनस्यायोः ||
tam u stuṣa indraṁ taṁ gṛṇīṣe yasmin purā vāvṛdhuḥ śāśaduś ca | sa vasvaḥ kāmam pīparad iyāno brahmaṇyato nūtanasyāyoḥ ||
  • tam u stuṣa indraṁ: This phrase translates to "I praise that Indra." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. In real life, this can be likened to acknowledging and appreciating the forces of nature that provide sustenance and life, such as rain for crops.
  • taṁ gṛṇīṣe: This means "I sing of him." Singing or praising signifies a deep respect and recognition of the qualities of Indra. In modern terms, this can be compared to celebrating achievements or virtues in individuals or communities, fostering a sense of gratitude and acknowledgment.
  • yasmin purā vāvṛdhuḥ: This translates to "in whom the ancient ones have grown." It suggests a connection to tradition and the wisdom of ancestors. In contemporary life, this can be seen in how we value and learn from our heritage, whether through family traditions or cultural practices.
  • śāśaduś ca: This means "and the eternal." It emphasizes the timeless nature of the divine and the principles that govern existence. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of eternal truths, such as love, justice, and compassion, which transcend time and culture.
  • sa vasvaḥ kāmam pīparad: This translates to "he who fulfills all desires." It reflects the idea that the divine can provide for all needs and aspirations. In practical terms, this can be interpreted as the importance of seeking fulfillment in life through meaningful goals and aspirations, whether personal or communal.
  • iyāno brahmaṇyato: This means "moving towards the divine." It signifies a journey or aspiration towards higher consciousness or spiritual enlightenment. In everyday life, this can be seen in the pursuit of knowledge, self-improvement, and spiritual practices that elevate one's existence.
  • nūtanasyāyoḥ: This translates to "of the new." It suggests the importance of innovation and renewal. In a real-world context, this can be related to the need for adaptation and growth in various aspects of life, such as technology, relationships, and personal development.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep reverence for the divine, the importance of tradition, and the pursuit of fulfillment and enlightenment. Its significance lies in the recognition of the interconnectedness of all aspects of life—nature, heritage, desires, and the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to appreciate the forces that sustain them, learn from their past, strive for their aspirations, and embrace change and growth. It serves as a reminder to seek balance and harmony in life, fostering a sense of gratitude and purpose in our daily endeavors.
