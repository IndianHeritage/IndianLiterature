सुभगः स व ऊतिष्व आस पूर्वासु मरुतो व्युष्टिषु |यो वा नूनमुतासति ||
subhagaḥ sa va ūtiṣv āsa pūrvāsu maruto vyuṣṭiṣu | yo vā nūnam utāsati ||
  • Subhagaḥ: This word translates to "the auspicious one" or "the fortunate one." It signifies a being or entity that brings good fortune and positivity.
  • Sa va: This phrase means "he indeed" or "that one." It emphasizes the subject being discussed, indicating a specific focus on the auspicious one.
  • ūtiṣv: This term can be interpreted as "to rise" or "to elevate." It suggests an action of uplifting or enhancing one's state or condition.
  • āsa: This word means "to dwell" or "to reside." It indicates a sense of stability or permanence in a particular state or condition.
  • Pūrvāsu: This term refers to "the earlier ones" or "the predecessors." It connects the current subject to a lineage or tradition that has come before.
  • Maruto: This word is associated with the "Maruts," the storm deities in Vedic literature. They represent the forces of nature, particularly storms and winds, symbolizing power and dynamism.
  • Vyuṣṭiṣu: This term means "in the manifestations" or "in the expressions." It refers to the various forms or appearances that the subject may take.
  • Yo vā: This phrase translates to "who indeed" or "whoever." It introduces a conditional aspect, indicating that the following statement applies to anyone who meets the criteria.
  • Nūnam: This word means "certainly" or "indeed." It adds emphasis to the statement that follows, reinforcing its truthfulness.
  • Utāsati: This term means "to rise" or "to ascend." It suggests an upward movement, both physically and metaphorically, indicating growth or improvement.

Conclusion: The mantra speaks to the idea of auspiciousness and elevation, emphasizing the importance of rising to a higher state of being while acknowledging the influences of nature and tradition. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, spiritual development, and the pursuit of success. For instance, when individuals strive to improve their circumstances or uplift their communities, they embody the essence of this mantra. It encourages us to seek positivity and elevation in our lives, drawing inspiration from the past while being mindful of the natural forces that shape our journey.
