क्षत्रं जिन्वतम उता जिन्वतां नृन हतं रक्षांसी सेधतम अमीवाः |सजोषसां उषसा सूर्येण च सोमं सुन्वतो अश्विनाः ||
kṣatraṁ jinvatam uta jinvataṁ nṝn hataṁ rakṣāṁsi sedhatam amīvāḥ | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa ca somaṁ sunvato aśvinā ||
  • kṣatraṁ: This term refers to "power" or "sovereignty," often associated with the warrior class or those who protect and govern.
  • jinvatam: This means "victorious" or "conquering," indicating success in endeavors.
  • uta: This translates to "and" or "also," connecting ideas within the mantra.
  • jinvataṁ: Similar to "jinvatam," it emphasizes the act of winning or achieving victory.
  • nṝn: This word means "men" or "humans," referring to the people who are part of the society.
  • hataṁ: This means "defeated" or "killed," indicating the challenges or adversities faced.
  • rakṣāṁsi: This translates to "protections" or "guardians," referring to the forces that safeguard individuals or communities.
  • sedhatam: This means "to be established" or "to be firm," suggesting stability and resilience.
  • amīvāḥ: This term refers to "the enemies" or "those who oppose," indicating the presence of challenges or adversaries.
  • sajoṣasā: This means "with joy" or "with enthusiasm," suggesting a positive approach to challenges.
  • uṣasā: This translates to "dawn," symbolizing new beginnings and hope.
  • sūryeṇa: This means "with the sun," representing energy, vitality, and enlightenment.
  • ca: This translates to "and," further connecting the ideas.
  • somaṁ: This refers to "the moon," symbolizing calmness, reflection, and nourishment.
  • sunvato: This means "to those who are prosperous" or "to those who are successful."
  • aśvinā: This refers to "the Ashvins," the twin deities associated with health, medicine, and well-being.

The mantra speaks to the themes of victory, protection, and the duality of challenges and support in life. It emphasizes the importance of resilience and the need for both strength (kṣatraṁ) and support (rakṣāṁsi) in overcoming adversities (hataṁ). The invocation of the sun (sūryeṇa) and moon (somaṁ) symbolizes the balance of energy and calmness required to navigate life's challenges.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Leadership: Leaders can draw inspiration from this mantra to embody strength and resilience while ensuring the protection and well-being of their teams.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals facing challenges can use the mantra as a reminder to remain victorious and seek support from their community.
  • Conflict Resolution: The mantra encourages a positive approach to adversities, promoting collaboration and understanding in resolving conflicts.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interplay between strength and support, victory and challenges. It encourages individuals and communities to embrace resilience, seek protection, and approach life with joy and enthusiasm, ultimately leading to prosperity and well-being.
