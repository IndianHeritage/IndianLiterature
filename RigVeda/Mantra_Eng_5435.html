विद्युतो ज्योतिः परि संजीहाणं मित्रावरुणा यद अपश्यतां त्वा |तत ते जन्मोतैकम् वसिष्ठागस्त्यो यत त्वा विश आजब्हार ||
vidyuto jyotiḥ pari saṁjihānam mitrāvaruṇā yad apaśyatāṁ tvā | tat te janmotaikaṁ vasiṣṭhāgastyo yat tvā viśa ājabhāra ||
  • Vidyuto: This word translates to "lightning" or "brilliance." It symbolizes sudden illumination or insight, akin to moments of clarity in our lives.
  • Jyotiḥ: Meaning "light" or "radiance," it represents knowledge and wisdom. In real life, this can be compared to the enlightenment one feels when learning something new.
  • Pari saṁjihānam: This phrase can be interpreted as "surrounding or encompassing." It suggests a holistic approach to understanding, where knowledge envelops us from all sides.
  • Mitrāvaruṇā: Referring to the deities Mitra and Varuna, who symbolize friendship and cosmic order, respectively. This can be related to the importance of harmony and balance in our relationships and the universe.
  • Yad apaśyatāṁ: This means "those who have seen" or "those who perceive." It emphasizes the value of perception and awareness in our lives.
  • Tvā: Translates to "you," indicating a personal connection to the divine or the knowledge being sought.
  • Tat te: Meaning "that which is yours," it signifies ownership of knowledge and wisdom.
  • Janmotaikaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the one birth" or "the singular essence of life." It highlights the idea of unity in existence.
  • Vasiṣṭhāgastyo: Referring to the sage Vasiṣṭha, known for his wisdom and knowledge. This suggests the importance of learning from wise figures in our lives.
  • Yat tvā viśa ājabhāra: This phrase can be understood as "what you have brought forth." It indicates the manifestation of knowledge and wisdom in the world.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of knowledge, wisdom, and perception in our lives. It encourages us to seek enlightenment and to understand the interconnectedness of all beings. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways, such as in education, personal growth, and fostering harmonious relationships. By embracing the principles of this mantra, we can strive for a more enlightened and balanced existence, recognizing the importance of learning from wise individuals and the cosmic order that governs our lives.
