विभूषण्न अग्न उभयाम् अनु व्रता दूतो देवानां रजसी सम ईयसे |यत ते धीतिं सुमतिम आवृणीमहे 'ध स्मा नस् त्रिवरूथः शिवो भव ||
vibhūṣann agna ubhayām̐ anu vratā dūto devānāṁ rajasī sam īyase | yat te dhītiṁ sumatim āvṛṇīmahe 'dha smā nas trivarūthaḥ śivo bhava ||
  • vibhūṣann: This word can be translated as "shining" or "illuminated." It signifies the brilliance and radiance of the divine.
  • agna: Referring to "Agni," the fire god, who is a central figure in Vedic rituals. Agni represents energy, transformation, and the divine messenger.
  • ubhayaṁ: Meaning "both" or "twofold," indicating the dual nature of existence or the balance between different forces.
  • anu vratā: This phrase translates to "following the vow" or "adhering to the path," suggesting a commitment to righteousness and duty.
  • dūto: Meaning "messenger," this term emphasizes the role of Agni as a communicator between the divine and human realms.
  • devānāṁ: This means "of the gods," indicating the divine context in which the mantra is situated.
  • rajasī: Referring to "passion" or "activity," this word highlights the dynamic aspect of existence.
  • sam īyase: This translates to "you come together" or "you unite," suggesting the gathering of energies or forces.
  • yat te: Meaning "that which is yours," this phrase indicates a connection to the divine will or intention.
  • dhītiṁ: This word means "intellect" or "understanding," emphasizing the importance of wisdom in our actions.
  • sumatim: Translated as "good thought" or "auspiciousness," it signifies the pursuit of positive and constructive ideas.
  • āvṛṇīmahe: Meaning "we invoke" or "we seek," this term indicates a call to the divine for guidance and support.
  • 'dha: This word can be interpreted as "here" or "in this place," grounding the invocation in the present moment.
  • smā: Meaning "may we," this phrase expresses a collective desire or aspiration.
  • nas: This translates to "us," indicating a communal aspect to the invocation.
  • trivarūthaḥ: This term means "threefold," suggesting a holistic approach to understanding existence, encompassing body, mind, and spirit.
  • śivo: Referring to "auspiciousness" or "well-being," this word emphasizes the positive outcomes of the invocation.
  • bhava: Meaning "become" or "may you become," this term expresses a desire for transformation and growth.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and wisdom while adhering to a righteous path. It emphasizes the importance of illumination, understanding, and the interconnectedness of all beings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of decision-making, where individuals seek clarity and positive outcomes in their lives. By invoking the divine and committing to good thoughts and actions, one can navigate challenges with a sense of purpose and harmony. The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of intention and the significance of aligning oneself with higher principles for personal and communal well-being.
