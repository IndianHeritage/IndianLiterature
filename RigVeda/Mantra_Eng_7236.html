तां अस्य सूददोहसः सोमं श्रीणन्ति पृष्णयः |जनमन देवानां विशस त्रिष्वा रोचने दिवः ||
tā asya sūdadohasaḥ somaṁ śrīṇanti pṛśnayaḥ | janman devānāṁ viśas triṣv ā rocane divaḥ ||
  • tā asya sūdadohasaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "those who are endowed with strength." Here, "tā" refers to "those," "asya" means "of this," and "sūdadohasaḥ" signifies "endowed with strength." In real life, this can refer to individuals or groups who possess the strength or capability to achieve their goals, such as athletes or leaders who inspire others.
  • somaṁ śrīṇanti pṛśnayaḥ: This translates to "the seekers of Soma." "Soma" is often associated with a divine drink that grants immortality and enlightenment. "Śrīṇanti" means "they seek," and "pṛśnayaḥ" refers to "the seekers." In contemporary terms, this can be likened to individuals pursuing knowledge, wisdom, or spiritual enlightenment, much like students or researchers striving for breakthroughs in their fields.
  • janman devānāṁ: This means "the birth of the gods." "Janman" refers to "birth," and "devānāṁ" means "of the gods." This can be interpreted as the emergence of divine qualities or virtues in people, akin to how leaders or visionaries can inspire others to rise to greatness.
  • viśas triṣv ā rocane divaḥ: This translates to "in the three realms of the heavens." "Viśas" means "in the realms," "triṣv" refers to "three," and "ā rocane divaḥ" means "in the shining heavens." This can symbolize the interconnectedness of the physical, mental, and spiritual realms, suggesting that true strength and enlightenment come from a balance of all three aspects in life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, the pursuit of knowledge, and the emergence of divine qualities in individuals. It highlights the interconnectedness of different realms of existence, suggesting that true enlightenment and strength come from a holistic approach to life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek personal growth, strive for excellence, and cultivate virtues that benefit not only themselves but also their communities. It serves as a reminder that the journey towards enlightenment is a collective endeavor, where the strength of one can uplift many.
