तं ओषधीर दधिरे गर्भं ऋत्वियं तं आपो अग्निं जनयन्त मातरः |तं इति समानं वनिनश्च वीरुधोऽन्तर्वतीश्च सुवते च विश्वहा ||
tam oṣadhīr dadhire garbham ṛtviyaṁ tam āpo agniṁ janayanta mātaraḥ | tam it samānaṁ vaninaś ca vīrudho 'ntarvatīś ca suvate ca viśvahā ||
  • tam oṣadhīr dadhire garbham ṛtviyaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "the herbs (oṣadhīr) have nurtured (dadhire) the embryo (garbham) of truth (ṛtviyaṁ)." Here, the herbs symbolize the natural world and its nurturing qualities. In real life, this can be seen in how plants and nature provide sustenance and healing to humans. For example, herbal medicine is widely used today to promote health and well-being.
  • tam āpo agniṁ janayanta mātaraḥ: This translates to "the waters (āpo) give birth (janayanta) to fire (agniṁ), the mothers (mātaraḥ)." This line emphasizes the interconnectedness of elements in nature. Water is essential for life and can also create steam and energy, symbolizing the birth of fire. In practical terms, this can be observed in how water is used in cooking, generating steam, or even in hydroelectric power generation, where water is transformed into energy.
  • tam it samānaṁ vaninaś ca vīrudho: This means "that which is equal (samānaṁ) is also the one that grows (vīrudho) in the forest (vaninaś)." This line suggests that equality and growth are fundamental principles in nature. In a real-life context, this can be related to the idea of diversity in ecosystems, where different species coexist and thrive together, contributing to a balanced environment.
  • 'ntarvatīś ca suvate ca viśvahā: This translates to "the one who is within (antarvatīś) and the one who brings forth (suvate) is the universal (viśvahā)." This highlights the idea of an inner essence that connects all beings. In everyday life, this can be reflected in the concept of mindfulness and self-awareness, where understanding oneself leads to a deeper connection with the world and others.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, the nurturing qualities of the earth, and the importance of balance and equality in life. It teaches us to appreciate the natural world and recognize our place within it. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in environmental conservation, sustainable living, and holistic health practices. By understanding and respecting the relationships between different elements of nature, we can foster a more harmonious existence for ourselves and future generations.
