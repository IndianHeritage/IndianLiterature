यो रायोऽवनिर्महान् सुपारः सुन्वतः सखा। तस्मा इन्द्राय गायत।
yo rāyo3 'vanir mahān supāraḥ sunvataḥ sakhā | tasmā indrāya gāyata ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yo: who
    • rāyaḥ: wealth or prosperity
    • avanir: the one who brings or bestows
    • mahān: great
    • supāraḥ: well-formed or well-structured
    • sunvataḥ: of those who are worthy or deserving
    • sakhā: friend or ally
    • tasmā: therefore
    • indrāya: to Indra, the king of gods
    • gāyata: sing or chant
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of a great wealth or prosperity that is bestowed by a worthy friend or ally.
    • It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring the source of this wealth, which is directed towards Indra, the king of gods.
    • The act of singing or chanting to Indra signifies gratitude and reverence for the blessings received.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be related to the importance of partnerships and alliances. A successful business often relies on strong relationships with partners who contribute to its prosperity.
    • In personal life, it can reflect the significance of friendships that bring joy and support, highlighting how these relationships can lead to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
    • In community settings, this mantra can be applied to the collective efforts of individuals working together for a common goal, such as community service or social initiatives that enhance the well-being of all.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra underscores the value of recognizing and appreciating the sources of our prosperity, whether they be friends, allies, or divine forces.
    • It teaches us to express gratitude and to honor those who contribute to our success, fostering a sense of community and interconnectedness.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger relationships, greater collaboration, and a more profound sense of fulfillment in both personal and professional realms.
