मन्त्र: असाव् अन्यो असुर सूयत द्यौस् त्वं विश्वेषां वरुणासी राजा | मूर्धा रथस्य चाकन् नैतावतैनेसान्तकध्रुक ||
asāv anyo asura sūyata dyaus tvaṁ viśveṣāṁ varuṇāsi rājā | mūrdhā rathasya cākan naitāvatainasāntakadhruk ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asāv: that (which is)
    • anyo: another
    • asura: divine or powerful being
    • sūyata: born or created
    • dyaus: sky or heaven
    • tvaṁ: you
    • viśveṣāṁ: of all beings
    • varuṇāsi: you are Varuna (the god of cosmic order)
    • rājā: king
    • mūrdhā: head or top
    • rathasya: of the chariot
    • cākan: you are the one who holds
    • naitāvat: not to this extent
    • ainasāntakadhruk: the one who holds the reins of the chariot
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine nature of the universe and the powerful beings that govern it.
    • It emphasizes the omnipresence of the divine, represented by Varuna, who is the king of all beings.
    • The reference to the chariot symbolizes the journey of life, where the divine guides and controls the direction.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In our daily lives, we often seek guidance and support from higher powers or principles, much like how the mantra invokes Varuna.
    • When making important decisions, individuals may reflect on their values and the greater cosmic order, akin to the divine guidance mentioned in the mantra.
    • The metaphor of the chariot can be seen in the context of personal goals; we are the charioteers of our lives, and we must navigate our paths with wisdom and awareness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine order that governs the universe.
    • It reminds us of the importance of seeking guidance from higher principles in our lives.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to be mindful of our actions and decisions, recognizing that we are part of a larger cosmic journey.
