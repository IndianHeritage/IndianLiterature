अश्वम् इद गां रथप्रां त्वेषम् इन्द्रं न सत्पतिम् |यस्य श्रवांसि तूर्वथा पण्यं-पण्यं च कृष्टयः ||
aśvam id gāṁ rathaprāṁ tveṣam indraṁ na satpatim | yasya śravāṁsi tūrvatha panyam-panyaṁ ca kṛṣṭayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvam: horse
    • id: this
    • gāṁ: cow
    • rathaprāṁ: chariot
    • tveṣam: your
    • indraṁ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • na: not
    • satpatim: the good path
    • yasya: whose
    • śravāṁsi: fame
    • tūrvatha: in various ways
    • panyam-panyaṁ: wealth and riches
    • ca: and
    • kṛṣṭayaḥ: cultivated lands
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who is often associated with strength and prosperity.
    • The mention of "horse," "cow," and "chariot" symbolizes wealth, sustenance, and the means of transport, respectively, which are essential for a prosperous life.
    • The phrase "not the good path" suggests a plea for guidance away from negative influences or paths that do not lead to prosperity.
    • The reference to "wealth and riches" indicates a desire for abundance in various forms, including material wealth and agricultural success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers may recite this mantra before the sowing season, seeking Indra's blessings for rain and a bountiful harvest.
    • Business owners might use this mantra during rituals to attract prosperity and success in their ventures, symbolized by the "wealth and riches."
    • Individuals may reflect on the mantra when making important life decisions, ensuring they choose paths that lead to growth and fulfillment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and blessings for prosperity and success in various aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder to stay on the right path and to cultivate not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional richness.
    • In contemporary life, the principles of this mantra can be applied in personal development, business ethics, and community welfare, promoting a holistic approach to prosperity.
