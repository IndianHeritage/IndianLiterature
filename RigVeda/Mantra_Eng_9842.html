प्र नः पूषा चरथं विश्वदेव्योऽपां नपाद अवतु वायुरिष्टये |आत्मानं वस्यो अभि वातमर्चत तद अश्विनाः सुहवा यामनि श्रुतम् ||
pra naḥ pūṣā carathaṁ viśvadevyo 'pāṁ napād avatu vāyur iṣṭaye | ātmānaṁ vasyo abhi vātam arcata tad aśvinā suhavā yāmani śrutam ||
  • pra naḥ pūṣā carathaṁ viśvadevyo 'pāṁ napād avatu vāyur iṣṭaye
    • pra naḥ: "For us" - This phrase indicates a collective appeal or prayer for the well-being of a group.
    • pūṣā: "O Nourisher" - Refers to the divine force that sustains and nourishes life.
    • carathaṁ: "May you guide" - A request for guidance and direction in life.
    • viśvadevyo: "All deities" - Acknowledges the presence and power of all divine beings.
    • 'pāṁ napād: "May we be protected" - A plea for safety and protection from harm.
    • avatu: "May protect" - A reiteration of the desire for divine protection.
    • vāyur: "O Wind" - Symbolizes the life force and the breath of life.
    • iṣṭaye: "For our desires" - Indicates the fulfillment of our aspirations and needs.
  • ātmānaṁ vasyo abhi vātam arcata tad aśvinā suhavā yāmani śrutam
    • ātmānaṁ: "The self" - Refers to the individual soul or essence.
    • vasyo: "May be controlled" - A wish for mastery over one's own self and desires.
    • abhi: "Towards" - Indicates direction or focus.
    • vātam: "The wind" - Again symbolizes the life force and the dynamic energy of existence.
    • arcata: "Worship" - The act of reverence and devotion.
    • tad: "That" - Refers to the divine or the higher power.
    • aśvinā: "O Aśvins" - Invokes the twin deities associated with health and medicine.
    • suhavā: "Pleasant" - Describes the nature of the blessings sought.
    • yāmani: "That which is heard" - Refers to the wisdom or teachings that are received.
    • śrutam: "Heard" - Emphasizes the importance of listening and understanding divine guidance.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of divine guidance, protection, and the mastery of the self. It reflects a holistic approach to life, where one seeks nourishment from the divine while also striving for personal growth and control over one's desires. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • In times of uncertainty or challenge, individuals can recite this mantra to seek guidance and protection from higher powers.
  • In personal development, it serves as a reminder to cultivate self-control and mastery over one's thoughts and actions.
  • In communal settings, it fosters a sense of unity and collective well-being, encouraging individuals to support one another in their spiritual and personal journeys.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in navigating life's challenges while promoting self-awareness and communal harmony.
