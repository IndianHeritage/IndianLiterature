अच्छिद्रां सूनो सहसो नो अद्य स्तोत्रिभ्यो मित्रमहः शर्म यच्छ |अग्नि गृणन्तम अम्हस उरुश्योर्जो नपात पूर्विर आयसीभिः ||
acchidrā sūno sahaso no adya stotṛbhyo mitramahaḥ śarma yaccha | agne gṛṇantam aṁhasa uruṣyorjo napāt pūrbhir āyasībhiḥ ||
  • acchidrā sūno sahaso no adya stotṛbhyo mitramahaḥ śarma yaccha
    • acchidrā: This word means "unbroken" or "whole." It signifies completeness and integrity.
    • sūno: This translates to "of the son" or "of the offspring," indicating a connection to lineage and heritage.
    • sahaso: Meaning "with strength" or "power," it emphasizes the importance of vigor and resilience.
    • no: This is a possessive pronoun meaning "our," indicating a collective aspect.
    • adya: This means "today," emphasizing the immediacy and relevance of the request.
    • stotṛbhyo: This refers to "those who praise" or "worshippers," indicating a community of devotees.
    • mitramahaḥ: This translates to "great friend," suggesting a divine connection or allyship.
    • śarma: Meaning "protection" or "shelter," it signifies the need for safety and security.
    • yaccha: This means "grant" or "bestow," indicating a request for blessings.
  • agne gṛṇantam aṁhasa uruṣyorjo napāt pūrbhir āyasībhiḥ
    • agne: Referring to "Agni," the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • gṛṇantam: This means "to those who sing" or "to those who praise," indicating the act of worship and reverence.
    • aṁhasa: This translates to "of the light" or "radiance," symbolizing enlightenment and knowledge.
    • uruṣyorjo: Meaning "of great strength," it emphasizes the power and might of the divine.
    • napāt: This means "descendant" or "offspring," indicating a lineage or continuation of blessings.
    • pūrbhir: This translates to "from the past" or "ancient," connecting the present with historical wisdom.
    • āyasībhiḥ: Meaning "with metals" or "with resources," it signifies abundance and prosperity.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of seeking protection, strength, and blessings from the divine, particularly through the worship of Agni, the fire god. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In times of personal or communal challenges, individuals can invoke this mantra to seek strength and resilience.
  • During rituals or ceremonies, it can be recited to invite blessings for safety and prosperity.
  • In family settings, it can serve as a reminder of the importance of lineage, heritage, and the collective strength of the family unit.

The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with divine energy, fostering a sense of community, protection, and empowerment in their daily lives.
