मन्त्र: भर्गो ह नामोता यस्य देवाः स्वर्ण न ये त्रिषधस्थे निषेदुः | अग्निर ह नामोता जातवेदाः श्रुधी नो होतर ऋतस्य होताध्रुक ||
bhargo ha nāmota yasya devāḥ sva1r ṇa ye triṣadhasthe niṣeduḥ | agnir ha nāmota jātavedāḥ śrudhī no hotar ṛtasya hotādhruk ||
  • bhargo ha nāmota yasya devāḥ: This phrase refers to the divine light or radiance of the Supreme Being. The term "bhargo" signifies the brilliance or the illuminating power of the divine. In real life, this can be likened to the way sunlight brightens our surroundings, symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
  • sva1r ṇa ye triṣadhasthe niṣeduḥ: This part speaks of the deities who reside in the three realms (heaven, earth, and the atmosphere). It emphasizes the omnipresence of divine forces in our lives. For example, one can observe this in nature, where the elements of earth, water, and air coexist harmoniously, reflecting the divine order.
  • agnir ha nāmota jātavedāḥ: Here, "Agnir" refers to Agni, the fire god, who is considered the mediator between humans and the divine. "Jātavedāḥ" means the one who knows all that is born. In practical terms, this can be seen in rituals where fire is used as a purifying agent, symbolizing transformation and the connection between the material and spiritual worlds.
  • śrudhī no hotar ṛtasya hotādhruk: This line calls upon the divine to listen to our prayers and to guide us in accordance with the cosmic order (ṛta). In everyday life, this can be related to the importance of ethical living and making choices that align with universal truths, such as honesty and integrity.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and enlightenment. It highlights the interconnectedness of the divine with the natural world and the importance of living in harmony with cosmic principles. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue knowledge, ethical living, and a deeper connection with the universe, fostering a sense of purpose and direction in their lives.
