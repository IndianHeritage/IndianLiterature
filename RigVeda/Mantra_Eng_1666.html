ते नो गृणाने महिनी महि श्रवः क्षत्रं द्यावापृथिवी धासथो बृहत् |येनााभि कृष्टीस ततनाम विश्वहा पनाय्यम ओजो अस्मे सम इन्बतम् ||
te no gṛṇāne mahinī mahi śravaḥ kṣatraṁ dyāvāpṛthivī dhāsatho bṛhat | yenābhi kṛṣṭīs tatanāma viśvahā panāyyam ojo asme sam invatam ||
  • te no gṛṇāne: This phrase can be translated as "may they sing for us." It signifies the importance of collective praise and acknowledgment, suggesting that when people come together to celebrate or honor something, it creates a powerful energy.
  • mahinī mahi śravaḥ: This translates to "the great glory of the earth." It emphasizes the vastness and richness of the earth, reminding us of the abundance that nature provides. For example, when we appreciate the beauty of a sunset or the bounty of a harvest, we connect with this glory.
  • kṣatraṁ dyāvāpṛthivī: This means "the power of heaven and earth." It reflects the duality and balance between the celestial and terrestrial realms. In real life, this can be seen in the way we seek harmony between our spiritual aspirations (heaven) and our earthly responsibilities (earth).
  • dhāsatho bṛhat: This translates to "may you be great." It is a blessing for greatness and success. In practical terms, this can be applied in our personal and professional lives, where we strive for excellence and encourage others to do the same.
  • yenābhi kṛṣṭīs: This means "by which we are nourished." It highlights the importance of sustenance, both physical and spiritual. For instance, the food we eat nourishes our bodies, while knowledge and wisdom nourish our minds.
  • tatanāma viśvahā: This translates to "may all beings be united." It speaks to the interconnectedness of all life. In today's world, this can be seen in movements for global unity and cooperation, such as climate action or humanitarian efforts.
  • panāyyam ojo asme sam invatam: This means "may we be endowed with strength." It is a call for empowerment and resilience. In real life, this can be applied in challenging situations where we seek inner strength to overcome obstacles, such as during personal crises or societal challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of unity, nourishment, empowerment, and the celebration of the earth's glory. Its significance lies in its call for collective action and recognition of the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, it encourages us to appreciate nature, strive for greatness, support one another, and seek strength in unity. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, from community gatherings to personal reflections, reminding us of our shared responsibilities and the power of collective praise and action.
