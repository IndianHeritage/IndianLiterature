आ धावताऽ सुहस्त्यः शुक्रा गृभ्णीत मन्थिना |गोभिः श्रीणीत मत्सरम् ||
ā dhāvatā suhastyaḥ śukrā gṛbhṇīta manthinā | gobhiḥ śrīṇīta matsaram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā dhāvatā: From the one who is running or moving.
    • suhastyaḥ: With good hands or skillful.
    • śukrā: Bright, pure, or auspicious.
    • gṛbhṇīta: May he/she/they receive or grasp.
    • manthinā: By the one who churns or stirs.
    • gobhiḥ: With the cows or through the means of cattle.
    • śrīṇīta: May it be endowed or blessed.
    • matsaram: Envy or jealousy.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the importance of skillful action and the pursuit of purity and auspiciousness in our endeavors.
    • The phrase "ā dhāvatā suhastyaḥ" suggests that one should act with skill and intention, much like a skilled artisan or a dedicated athlete who practices diligently to achieve excellence.
    • "śukrā gṛbhṇīta" emphasizes the need to receive or embrace purity and auspiciousness, akin to how one might seek out positive experiences and relationships in life.
    • The mention of "manthinā" indicates the process of churning, which can be likened to the effort required to extract the best outcomes from our actions, similar to how one might churn milk to make butter.
    • "gobhiḥ" refers to the significance of cattle, which in ancient times symbolized wealth and prosperity. In modern terms, this can represent the resources and support systems we rely on to achieve our goals.
    • Finally, "matsaram" warns against the destructive nature of envy, reminding us to focus on our own paths rather than comparing ourselves to others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can inspire individuals to collaborate skillfully, ensuring that their contributions are pure and beneficial to the team, while avoiding jealousy towards colleagues' successes.
    • In personal relationships, it encourages nurturing connections that are based on mutual respect and support, rather than competition or envy.
    • In education, students can apply this mantra by diligently working on their studies, embracing knowledge with purity, and supporting one another rather than competing destructively.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of skillful action, the pursuit of purity, and the need to avoid envy in all aspects of life.
    • Its significance lies in promoting a harmonious and prosperous existence, where individuals work together towards common goals, uplift each other, and focus on their own growth.
    • By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, one can cultivate a fulfilling and successful life, both personally and professionally.
