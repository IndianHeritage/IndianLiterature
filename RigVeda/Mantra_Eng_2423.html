इन्द्रज्येष्ठा मरुद्गणा देवासः पूषरातयः |विश्वे मम श्रुताः हवम् ||
indrajyeṣṭhā marudgaṇā devāsaḥ pūṣarātayaḥ | viśve mama śrutā havam ||
  • Indrajyeṣṭhā: This term refers to the "greatest among the Indras" or "the most powerful." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and authority.
  • Marudgaṇā: This translates to "the group of Maruts," who are storm deities associated with wind and storms. They represent the forces of nature and the dynamic aspects of life.
  • Devāsaḥ: This means "the divine beings" or "gods." It emphasizes the presence of divine energy and guidance in our lives.
  • Pūṣarātayaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who nourish" or "the providers of sustenance." It signifies the nurturing aspect of the divine, providing for the needs of all beings.
  • Viśve: This means "all" or "universal." It indicates inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings and elements in the universe.
  • Mama: This translates to "my" or "mine," indicating a personal connection to the divine and the universe.
  • Śrutā: This means "heard" or "listened to," suggesting the importance of communication and the transmission of knowledge.
  • Havam: This refers to "the offering" or "sacrifice," which is a central theme in Vedic rituals, symbolizing devotion and the act of giving.

The mantra can be understood as a call to the powerful deities, particularly Indra and the Maruts, to listen to the offerings made by the devotee. It emphasizes the importance of divine support and nourishment in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be seen as a reminder to seek strength and guidance from higher powers when facing challenges, much like how individuals turn to mentors or leaders for support in difficult times.


In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the divine forces in our lives that provide strength, nourishment, and guidance. It encourages individuals to make offerings, whether they be in the form of prayers, actions, or contributions to the community, fostering a sense of connection with the universe and the divine. This principle can be applied in various aspects of life, such as in personal growth, community service, and spiritual practices, reminding us of our interconnectedness and the support available to us from the divine and nature.
