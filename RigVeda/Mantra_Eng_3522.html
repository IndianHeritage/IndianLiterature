को मृळाति कतम आगमिष्टो देवानाम् उ कतमः शम्भविष्टः | रथं कम आहुर द्रवदश्वम् आशुं यं सूर्यस्य दुहितावृणीत ||
ko mṛḻāti katama āgamiṣṭho devānām u katamaḥ śambhaviṣṭhaḥ | rathaṁ kam āhur dravadaśvam āśuṁ yaṁ sūryasya duhitāvṛṇīta ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ko: who
    • mṛḻāti: is the one who brings forth
    • katama: which
    • āgamiṣṭho: is the most desirable
    • devānām: of the gods
    • u: and
    • katamaḥ: which
    • śambhaviṣṭhaḥ: is the most auspicious
    • rathaṁ: chariot
    • kam: whom
    • āhur: do they call
    • dravadaśvam: the swift horse
    • āśuṁ: quickly
    • yaṁ: whom
    • sūryasya: of the sun
    • duhitā: daughter
    • vṛṇīta: may choose
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a series of questions about the nature of divinity and the qualities of the gods.
    • It inquires about the most desirable aspects of the divine and the auspiciousness associated with them.
    • The mention of a chariot and a swift horse symbolizes speed, power, and the journey of life.
    • The reference to the sun's daughter suggests a connection to light, knowledge, and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive environment, such as a workplace, individuals often seek to embody the qualities of the "most desirable" traits, such as leadership and innovation.
    • The metaphor of a chariot can be likened to the journey of personal growth, where one must choose the right path and companions to achieve success.
    • The swift horse represents the need for agility and quick decision-making in today's fast-paced world.
    • Just as the sun's daughter symbolizes enlightenment, individuals often strive for knowledge and wisdom in their personal and professional lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of understanding the qualities that lead to success and auspiciousness in life.
    • It encourages individuals to seek out and embody these qualities, whether in personal endeavors or in their interactions with others.
    • In a broader sense, it serves as a reminder to align oneself with divine qualities and to make choices that lead to enlightenment and fulfillment.
    • Ultimately, the mantra inspires a quest for knowledge, speed, and auspiciousness in all aspects of life.
