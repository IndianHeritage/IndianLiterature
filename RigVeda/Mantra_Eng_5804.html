सम भूम्या अन्ता ध्वसिरा अदृक्षतेन्द्रावरुणा दिवि घोष आरुहत् |अस्थुर जनानाम् उप माम् अरातयो 'र्वाग अवसाः हवनश्रुताः गतम् ||
sam bhūmyā antā dhvasirā adṛkṣatendrāvaruṇā divi ghoṣa āruhat | asthur janānām upa mām arātayo 'rvāg avasā havanaśrutā gatam ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "The Earth, having been made firm, is the support of all beings. The divine Indra and Varuna, who are the guardians of the heavens, have observed this. May they grant us strength and protect us from the enemies who are hostile to us."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • sam bhūmyā: "sam" means together or in harmony, and "bhūmyā" refers to the Earth. This signifies the unity and stability of the Earth.
    • antā dhvasirā: "antā" means end or boundary, and "dhvasirā" refers to the firm or stable. This suggests that the Earth provides a boundary and stability for all life.
    • adṛkṣatendrāvaruṇā: "adṛkṣat" means to observe, "Indra" and "Varuna" are deities representing strength and cosmic order, respectively. This indicates that these divine beings are watching over the Earth and its inhabitants.
    • divi ghoṣa āruhat: "divi" means in the heavens, "ghoṣa" refers to sound or proclamation, and "āruhat" means to ascend. This implies that there is a divine proclamation or support from the heavens.
    • asthur janānām: "asthur" means strength or support, and "janānām" refers to people or beings. This emphasizes the need for strength among all beings.
    • upa mām arātayo: "upa" means towards, "mām" means me, and "arātayo" refers to enemies or those who oppose. This is a plea for protection from adversaries.
    • 'rvāg avasā: "rvāg" means to speak or declare, and "avasā" means to be under control or protection. This suggests a desire for guidance and protection in speech and action.
    • havanaśrutā gatam: "havanaśrutā" refers to the act of invocation or offering, and "gatam" means to go or to reach. This indicates the importance of rituals and offerings in seeking divine favor.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for the stability of the Earth and favorable weather conditions, reflecting the idea of seeking divine support for their livelihoods.
    • In community gatherings, people come together to celebrate and strengthen their bonds, symbolizing the unity and support that the Earth provides to all beings.
    • During times of conflict, individuals and communities may invoke the protection of higher powers, similar to the plea for protection from enemies in the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the stability and support provided by the Earth, as well as the divine protection from adversaries. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking strength and unity in the face of challenges. In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as community support, agricultural practices, and during conflicts, where individuals seek divine guidance and protection.
