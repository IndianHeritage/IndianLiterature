एष एतानि चकारेन्द्रो विश्वा योऽति श्रिण्वे |वाजदावाऽमघोनाम् ||
eṣa etāni cakārendro viśvā yo 'ti śṛṇve | vājadāvā maghonām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣa: this
    • etāni: these
    • cakār: has created
    • indro: Indra (the king of gods)
    • viśvā: all, universal
    • yo: who
    • 'ati: surpasses
    • śṛṇve: hears
    • vājadāvā: like a thunderbolt
    • maghonām: of the mighty
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the universal power of Indra, who is often associated with strength, rain, and thunder.
    • It emphasizes the idea that Indra has created everything and that he is capable of hearing all that happens in the universe.
    • The comparison to a thunderbolt signifies immense power and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a leadership context, a leader who embodies the qualities of Indra can be seen as someone who listens to their team and creates an environment where everyone feels valued.
    • In nature, the power of a thunderstorm can be likened to the strength of Indra, reminding us of the forces that shape our environment.
    • In personal development, the mantra can inspire individuals to harness their inner strength and listen to their intuition, much like Indra listens to the universe.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of universal awareness and the power of creation.
    • It teaches us to be attentive to the world around us and to recognize the strength within ourselves.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to lead with strength and compassion, to listen actively, and to create a positive impact in our communities.
