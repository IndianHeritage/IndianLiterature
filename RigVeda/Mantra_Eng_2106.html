पिबा-पिबेद इन्द्र शूर सोमं मन्दन्तु त्वा मन्दिनः सुतासः |पृणन्तस ते कुक्षी वर्धयन्त्व इथ्था सुतः पौरा इन्द्रं आव ||
pibā-pibed indra śūra somam mandantu tvā mandinaḥ sutāsaḥ | pṛṇantas te kukṣī vardhayantv itthā sutaḥ paura indram āva ||
  • pibā-pibed: This term translates to "drink" or "consume." It signifies the act of taking in something beneficial, often associated with the consumption of soma, a sacred drink in Vedic rituals.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, representing strength, power, and the force of nature. He is often invoked for protection and victory.
  • śūra: This word means "brave" or "valiant." It emphasizes the qualities of courage and strength, which are essential for overcoming challenges.
  • somam: Soma refers to a divine drink that is believed to confer immortality and strength. It symbolizes spiritual nourishment and enlightenment.
  • mandantu: This term can be interpreted as "may they be pleased" or "may they be joyful." It reflects the desire for harmony and positivity among those who partake in the ritual.
  • tvā: This means "you," referring to Indra, the deity being addressed in the mantra.
  • mandinaḥ: This word means "wise" or "intelligent," indicating the importance of wisdom in the pursuit of strength and success.
  • sutāsaḥ: This translates to "sons" or "offspring," referring to the followers or devotees of Indra who seek his blessings.
  • pṛṇantas: This means "filling" or "satisfying," suggesting the act of providing abundance and fulfillment.
  • te: This means "your," indicating possession or connection to Indra.
  • kukṣī: This term refers to the "womb" or "belly," symbolizing the source of life and nourishment.
  • vardhayantv: This means "may they increase" or "may they grow," reflecting the desire for prosperity and growth.
  • itthā: This translates to "thus" or "in this way," indicating a manner of action or intention.
  • sutaḥ: This means "son" or "child," reinforcing the connection to lineage and heritage.
  • paura: This term refers to "the people" or "the community," emphasizing the collective aspect of worship and support.
  • indram: This is a repetition of the name Indra, reinforcing the focus on the deity.
  • āva: This means "to come" or "to approach," indicating a call for Indra's presence and blessings.

Conclusion: This mantra is a call to Indra, urging him to bless his devotees with strength, wisdom, and abundance. It emphasizes the importance of community and the collective pursuit of prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals for success in endeavors, seeking protection in challenging situations, or fostering unity among people. The essence of the mantra lies in the recognition of divine support in achieving personal and communal goals, highlighting the interconnectedness of strength, wisdom, and abundance in our lives.
