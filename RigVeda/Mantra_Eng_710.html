प्र वो महे महि नमो भरध्वम् आङ्गूष्यं शवसानाय साम |येना नः पूर्वे पितरः पदज्ञा अर्चन्तो अङ्गिरसो गा अविन्दन ||
pra vo mahe mahi namo bharadhvam āṅgūṣyaṁ śavasānāya sāma | yenā naḥ pūrve pitaraḥ padajñā arcanto aṅgiraso gā avindan ||
  • pra vo mahe mahi: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke you, O great Earth." Here, "mahe" signifies greatness, and "mahi" refers to the Earth. This invocation emphasizes the importance of the Earth in sustaining life.
  • namo bharadhvam: This translates to "We bow to you." The term "bharadhvam" can be understood as a call to the forces of nature or the divine to bestow their blessings upon us. It signifies respect and reverence.
  • āṅgūṣyaṁ śavasānāya sāma: This part can be interpreted as "to the one who is the essence of life." "Āṅgūṣyaṁ" refers to the essence or the core, while "śavasānāya" relates to life or vitality. "Sāma" indicates harmony or song, suggesting a connection to the rhythm of life.
  • yenā naḥ pūrve pitaraḥ: This translates to "By whom our ancestors." "Pūrve" means "ancient" or "forefathers," and "pitaraḥ" refers to fathers or ancestors. This line acknowledges the wisdom and guidance of those who came before us.
  • padajñā arcanto: This means "who knew the paths." "Padajñā" refers to knowledge of the paths or ways, and "arcanto" indicates worship or reverence. This line emphasizes the importance of following the wisdom of our ancestors in our journey through life.
  • aṅgiraso gā avindan: This translates to "the Angirasas found." The Angirasas are a group of sages in Vedic literature known for their wisdom and knowledge. "Gā avindan" means "they discovered" or "they attained," indicating the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, the reverence for nature, and the wisdom of our ancestors. It serves as a reminder to honor the Earth and seek guidance from those who have come before us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in practices such as environmental conservation, where we recognize the importance of the Earth in our lives, and in family traditions, where we honor our ancestors and their teachings. By invoking the essence of life and the wisdom of our forefathers, we can navigate our paths with greater awareness and respect for the world around us.
