देवाव्यो नः परिषिच्यमानाः क्षयं सुवीरं धन्वन्तु सोमाः |आयज्यवः सुमतिं विश्ववारा होतारो न दिवियजो मंद्रतमाः ||
devāvyo naḥ pariṣicyamānāḥ kṣayaṁ suvīraṁ dhanvantu somāḥ | āyajyavaḥ sumatiṁ viśvavārā hotāro na diviyajo mandratamāḥ ||
  • devāvyo naḥ pariṣicyamānāḥ: This phrase can be translated as "May the divine beings, who are being invoked, shower us." Here, "devāvyo" refers to the divine entities or gods, and "pariṣicyamānāḥ" implies the act of showering or bestowing blessings.
  • kṣayaṁ suvīraṁ dhanvantu somāḥ: This part means "May the potent Soma grant us wealth and strength." "Kṣayaṁ" refers to destruction or loss, while "suvīraṁ" signifies strength or valor. "Dhanvantu" indicates the granting of wealth, and "somāḥ" refers to the sacred drink Soma, which symbolizes vitality and divine inspiration.
  • āyajyavaḥ sumatiṁ viśvavārā: This translates to "May those who are worthy of worship bring us good thoughts." "Āyajyavaḥ" refers to those who are worthy of worship, while "sumatiṁ" means good thoughts or wisdom. "Viśvavārā" implies universal protection or safeguarding.
  • hotāro na diviyajo mandratamāḥ: This phrase means "The priests who are divine should not be weak." "Hotāro" refers to the priests or those who perform rituals, "diviyajo" means divine or celestial, and "mandratamāḥ" implies weakness or ineffectiveness.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine blessings, strength, wisdom, and the effectiveness of rituals. It reflects a deep connection between the divine and human realms, highlighting the need for spiritual support in achieving prosperity and well-being.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as:
    • Personal Growth: Seeking divine guidance and blessings in personal endeavors, such as career advancements or personal relationships.
    • Community Well-being: Invoking collective strength and wisdom for community projects or initiatives aimed at improving societal conditions.
    • Spiritual Practices: Incorporating this mantra in daily prayers or meditation to foster a sense of connection with the divine and to cultivate positive thoughts.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine and human experiences. It encourages individuals to seek divine blessings for strength, wisdom, and prosperity, while also emphasizing the importance of effective spiritual practices in achieving these goals. By invoking the divine, individuals can foster a sense of purpose and direction in their lives.
