मन्त्र: उता सखास्य अश्विनोर उता माता गावाम् असि | उतोषो वस्व ईशिषे ||
uta sakhāsy aśvinor uta mātā gavām asi | utoṣo vasva īśiṣe ||
  • uta - This word can be translated as "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition.
  • sakhāsy - This means "of friends" or "of companions," suggesting a sense of camaraderie and support.
  • aśvinor - Refers to the "Aśvins," the twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing and benevolent qualities.
  • uta - Again, this word signifies connection, reinforcing the idea of unity.
  • mātā - This translates to "mother," symbolizing nurturing, care, and protection.
  • gavām - Meaning "of cows," which in Vedic culture represents wealth, sustenance, and prosperity.
  • asi - This means "you are," indicating existence or presence.
  • utoṣo - This can be interpreted as "joy" or "happiness," reflecting a positive emotional state.
  • vasva - This word means "all" or "every," suggesting inclusivity and universality.
  • īśiṣe - This translates to "in the lordship" or "in the power," indicating authority or control.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation that emphasizes the importance of friendship, maternal care, and the blessings of prosperity and joy. It acknowledges the divine presence of the Aśvins, who are often called upon for healing and support.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Friendship: It highlights the value of companionship and support among friends. For example, in times of difficulty, having friends who stand by you can provide emotional strength.
  • Maternal Care: The reference to "mother" signifies the nurturing aspect of life. In a family setting, a mother's love and care are crucial for the well-being of children, fostering a sense of security and growth.
  • Prosperity: The mention of cows symbolizes wealth and sustenance. In agricultural societies, cows are vital for livelihood, and this mantra can be a reminder to appreciate and seek prosperity in our endeavors.
  • Joy and Inclusivity: The emphasis on joy and the idea of "all" encourages a positive outlook on life and the importance of inclusivity in communities, promoting harmony and collective well-being.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of friendship, maternal love, and the pursuit of joy and prosperity. It encourages individuals to cherish their relationships, seek support in times of need, and foster a sense of community that uplifts everyone. By embodying these principles, one can navigate life's challenges with resilience and positivity.
