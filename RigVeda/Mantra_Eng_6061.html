शग्धी न इन्द्र यत् त्वा रयिं यामि सुवीर्यम् |शग्धि वाजाय प्रथमं सिषासते शग्धि स्तोमाय पूर्व्य ||
śagdhī na indra yat tvā rayiṁ yāmi suvīryam | śagdhi vājāya prathamaṁ siṣāsate śagdhi stomāya pūrvya ||
  • śagdhī: This term can be interpreted as "to be strong" or "to be powerful." It signifies the importance of strength in various aspects of life.
  • na: This is a negation, meaning "not." It emphasizes the absence of something, often used to highlight a contrast.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, representing power, authority, and leadership. He is often associated with rain and thunderstorms, symbolizing abundance and prosperity.
  • yat: This means "that" or "which," referring to something specific that is being discussed.
  • tvā: This translates to "you," indicating a direct address to Indra, invoking his presence and blessings.
  • rayiṁ: This word means "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the material and spiritual abundance one seeks in life.
  • yāmi: This means "I go" or "I seek," indicating a journey or pursuit towards something desirable.
  • suvīryam: This translates to "great strength" or "valiant." It emphasizes the qualities of bravery and valor that are essential for achieving goals.
  • vājāya: This term means "for the victor" or "for the one who wins." It highlights the importance of success and triumph in endeavors.
  • prathamaṁ: This means "first" or "foremost," indicating priority or the primary focus in one's pursuits.
  • siṣāsate: This can be interpreted as "to be established" or "to be firm," suggesting stability and permanence in one's achievements.
  • stomāya: This refers to "praise" or "hymn," indicating the importance of expressing gratitude and reverence.
  • pūrvya: This means "ancient" or "previous," suggesting a connection to tradition and the wisdom of the past.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the pursuit of strength, wealth, and success through the invocation of divine blessings, particularly from Indra. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as striving for personal goals, seeking prosperity in business, or fostering leadership qualities. By recognizing the importance of strength and valor, individuals can navigate challenges and achieve their aspirations. The mantra serves as a reminder to honor the traditions and wisdom of the past while actively seeking to establish a prosperous and successful future.
