मन्त्र: क उ नु ते महिमनाḥ समस्यास्मत पूर्व ऋषयोऽन्तमापुः |यन् मातरं च पितरं च साकं जनयथास तन् वः स्वायाः ||
ka u nu te mahimanaḥ samasyāsmat pūrva ṛṣayo 'ntam āpuḥ | yan mātaraṁ ca pitaraṁ ca sākam ajanayathās tanva1ḥ svāyāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ka u nu: Who indeed?
    • te: Your
    • mahimanaḥ: Greatness
    • samasyāsmat: Our difficulties
    • pūrva: Previous
    • ṛṣayo: Sages
    • antam: End
    • āpuḥ: They attained
    • yan: What
    • mātaraṁ: Mother
    • ca: And
    • pitaraṁ: Father
    • ca: And
    • sākam: Together
    • ajanayathās: You gave birth
    • tanvaḥ: Bodies
    • svāyāḥ: Of your own
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra begins with a question, "Who indeed is your greatness?" It reflects a quest for understanding the divine or the higher power's attributes.
    • The mention of "our difficulties" suggests that the speaker acknowledges the challenges faced in life and seeks guidance or support from the divine.
    • The reference to "previous sages" indicates that wisdom and knowledge have been passed down through generations, and the speaker is looking to connect with that lineage.
    • The phrase "what mother and father together gave birth to your own bodies" emphasizes the importance of familial bonds and the divine role in creation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek solace in spirituality, asking for guidance from a higher power, much like the inquiry in this mantra.
    • Parents often reflect on the teachings of their own parents (the sages) when raising their children, emphasizing the continuity of wisdom across generations.
    • In many cultures, the concept of family is sacred, and the acknowledgment of both maternal and paternal influences in one's life is a common theme in literature and personal narratives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking understanding and support from the divine while recognizing the importance of familial and ancestral wisdom.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that we are not alone in our struggles; we are part of a larger continuum of knowledge and support.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to reflect on their challenges, seek guidance, and appreciate the wisdom of their ancestors, fostering a sense of connection and purpose.
