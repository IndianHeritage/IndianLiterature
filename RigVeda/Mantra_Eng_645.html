नि यद वृणक्षि श्वसनस्य मूर्धनि शुष्णस्य चिद व्रन्दिनो रोर्वद वनाः | प्राचीनैना मनसा बरहणावतां यद अद्याऽचित कृत्नवः कस् त्वा परी ||
ni yad vṛṇakṣi śvasanasya mūrdhani śuṣṇasya cid vrandino roruvad vanā | prācīnena manasā barhaṇāvatā yad adyā cit kṛṇavaḥ kas tvā pari ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ni: not
    • yad: what
    • vṛṇakṣi: you choose
    • śvasanasya: of the breath
    • mūrdhani: on the head
    • śuṣṇasya: of the warm
    • cid: consciousness
    • vrandino: of the group
    • roruvad: crying out
    • vanā: in the forest
    • prācīnena: with the ancient
    • manasā: with the mind
    • barhaṇāvatā: with the feathers
    • yad: what
    • adyā: today
    • cit: consciousness
    • kṛṇavaḥ: we create
    • kas: who
    • tvā: you
    • pari: around
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the act of choosing and the significance of breath and consciousness.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the mind and the ancient wisdom, suggesting that our thoughts and choices are influenced by a deeper, collective consciousness.
    • The imagery of crying out in the forest symbolizes a search for truth and understanding amidst the chaos of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In moments of decision-making, we often reflect on our past experiences and the wisdom of those who came before us, much like the ancient mind referenced in the mantra.
    • When faced with challenges, individuals may find solace in nature (the forest) and seek clarity through meditation or deep breathing, aligning with the mantra's focus on breath and consciousness.
    • In group settings, such as team projects or community efforts, the collective consciousness can lead to innovative solutions, echoing the idea of the 'group' in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of conscious choice and the interconnectedness of our thoughts, actions, and the wisdom of the past.
    • It serves as a reminder to be mindful of our decisions and to seek deeper understanding through reflection and connection with nature.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more intentional living, fostering a sense of community and awareness in our daily interactions.
