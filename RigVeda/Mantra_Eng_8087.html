अति वाराण पवमानो अष्यदत कलशां अभि धावति |इन्द्रस्य हार्द्य आविशन् ||
ati vārān pavamāno asiṣyadat kalaśām̐ abhi dhāvati | indrasya hārdy āviśan ||
  • ati vārān: This phrase can be translated as "beyond the limits" or "exceeding the boundaries." It suggests a state of transcendence or surpassing ordinary experiences.
  • pavamāno: This term refers to something that is pure or sacred. It is often associated with the concept of purification, both physically and spiritually.
  • asiṣyadat: This word can be interpreted as "to bestow" or "to grant." It implies an act of giving or sharing something valuable.
  • kalaśām̐: A "kalaśa" is a pot or vessel, often used in rituals to hold sacred water or offerings. It symbolizes abundance and the nurturing aspect of life.
  • abhi dhāvati: This phrase means "runs towards" or "approaches." It indicates movement or progression towards a goal or destination.
  • indrasya: Referring to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, who represents strength, power, and the forces of nature.
  • hārdy: This term can be understood as "joyful" or "delightful," suggesting a state of happiness or fulfillment.
  • āviśan: This word means "to enter" or "to dwell." It indicates a sense of presence or being within a space or state.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to transcend ordinary limitations through purification and the bestowal of abundance, leading to a joyful state of being that aligns with divine forces. It emphasizes the importance of moving towards higher states of consciousness and fulfillment.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to strive for personal excellence and to seek purification of thoughts and actions, leading to a more fulfilling life.
  • Community Well-being: In a community context, it can inspire collective efforts towards abundance and joy, encouraging people to share resources and support one another.
  • Spiritual Practices: During meditation or rituals, reciting this mantra can help individuals focus on their intentions to transcend limitations and connect with higher energies.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to seek purity, abundance, and joy in their lives. By transcending limitations and aligning with divine energies, one can cultivate a deeper sense of fulfillment and connection with the world around them. It serves as a powerful reminder of the potential for growth and transformation in both personal and communal contexts.
