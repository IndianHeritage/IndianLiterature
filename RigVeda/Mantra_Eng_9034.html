पशुं नः सोम रक्षसि पुरुत्रा विष्ठितं जगत् |समाकृत्नोषि जीवसे वि वो मदे विश्वं सम्पश्यन् भुवना विवक्षसे ||
paśuṁ naḥ soma rakṣasi purutrā viṣṭhitaṁ jagat | samākṛṇoṣi jīvase vi vo made viśvā sampaśyan bhuvanā vivakṣase ||
  • paśuṁ: This word translates to "the animal" or "the living being." In a broader sense, it can refer to all creatures that inhabit the earth.
  • naḥ: This means "our," indicating a sense of possession or belonging.
  • soma: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
  • rakṣasi: This translates to "protect" or "guard," suggesting a plea for safety and preservation.
  • purutrā: This means "in all directions" or "everywhere," indicating a universal presence.
  • viṣṭhitaṁ: This word means "established" or "situated," referring to the presence of life in the world.
  • jagat: This translates to "the world" or "the universe," encompassing all of creation.
  • samākṛṇoṣi: This means "you create" or "you bring together," referring to the divine force that unites all beings.
  • jīvase: This translates to "for life," emphasizing the importance of existence and vitality.
  • vi vo: This means "for you," indicating a direct address to the divine or the cosmic force.
  • made: This translates to "joy" or "bliss," suggesting a state of happiness and fulfillment.
  • viśvā: This means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity and wholeness.
  • sampaśyan: This translates to "seeing" or "perceiving," referring to awareness and understanding.
  • bhuvanā: This means "worlds" or "realms," indicating the various dimensions of existence.
  • vivakṣase: This translates to "you express" or "you reveal," suggesting the unfolding of truth and knowledge.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for the protection and nurturing of all living beings in the universe. It acknowledges the divine presence (Soma) that sustains life and calls for a universal awareness that connects all creatures. The plea for protection is not just for individuals but for the collective existence of all beings, emphasizing the interconnectedness of life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Environmental Conservation: The plea for protection can inspire efforts to safeguard wildlife and natural habitats, recognizing the importance of every creature in maintaining ecological balance.
  • Community Well-being: The mantra's emphasis on collective existence can encourage communities to work together for the welfare of all, fostering a sense of unity and shared responsibility.
  • Personal Growth: The call for joy and awareness can motivate individuals to seek fulfillment and happiness in their lives, promoting mental and emotional well-being.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the sacredness of life and the importance of protecting and nurturing all beings. Its significance lies in fostering a sense of unity, awareness, and joy in our interactions with the world around us. By embodying these principles, we can contribute to a more harmonious and sustainable existence for all.
