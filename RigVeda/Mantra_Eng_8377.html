एष प्र कोषे मधुमाम् अचिक्रदद इन्द्रस्य वज्रो वपुषो वपुष्टरः |अभीम ऋतस्य सुदुघा घृतश्चुतो वाश्राः अर्षन्ति पयसेव दhenवः ||
eṣa pra kośe madhumām̐ acikradad indrasya vajro vapuṣo vapuṣṭaraḥ | abhīm ṛtasya sudughā ghṛtaścuto vāśrā arṣanti payaseva dhenavaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣa: this
    • pra: for, in favor of
    • kośe: in the treasure, in the storehouse
    • madhumām̐: sweet, honey-like
    • acikradad: has cried out, has called
    • indrasya: of Indra, the king of gods
    • vajro: thunderbolt, strength
    • vapuṣo: of the body, form
    • vapuṣṭaraḥ: more powerful, stronger
    • abhīm: fearless, bold
    • ṛtasya: of truth, of order
    • sudughā: well-nourished, rich
    • ghṛta: clarified butter, ghee
    • cuto: having fallen, descended
    • vāśrā: those who are nourished
    • arṣanti: they are filled, they are satisfied
    • payaseva: like milk
    • dhenavaḥ: cows, the givers of nourishment
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine qualities associated with Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who is often seen as a provider of strength and nourishment.
    • The reference to "madhumām̐" suggests the sweetness of life and abundance, akin to the blessings we receive in our daily lives.
    • The mention of "vajro" and "vapuṣo" emphasizes the strength and form of Indra, symbolizing the power we can harness in our own lives.
    • "Abhīm ṛtasya" indicates the importance of being fearless in the pursuit of truth and righteousness, a quality that is essential in overcoming challenges.
    • The imagery of "ghṛta" and "payaseva dhenavaḥ" evokes the idea of nourishment and sustenance, highlighting the importance of nurturing ourselves and others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, embodying the qualities of Indra can mean leading with strength and confidence, inspiring your team to achieve their goals.
    • In personal relationships, being "abhīm" or fearless can help in addressing conflicts openly and honestly, fostering deeper connections.
    • Just as cows provide milk, we can think of ways to nourish our communities, whether through volunteering, sharing knowledge, or providing emotional support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the divine qualities we can aspire to embody in our lives.
    • It encourages us to seek strength, truth, and nourishment, both for ourselves and for those around us.
    • By integrating these principles into our daily actions, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling life, reflecting the abundance and sweetness that the mantra speaks of.
