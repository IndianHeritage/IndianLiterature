शुचिर अपः सूयवसाऽ अदब्ध उप क्षेति वृद्धवयाः सुवीरः |नकीषṭं घ्नन्त्य अन्तितो न दूराद य आदित्याणां भवति प्रणीतौ ||
śucir apaḥ sūyavasā adabdha upa kṣeti vṛddhavayāḥ suvīraḥ | nakiṣ ṭaṁ ghnanty antito na dūrād ya ādityānām bhavati praṇītau ||
  • śucir apaḥ: This phrase translates to "pure waters." In many cultures, water is seen as a symbol of purity and life. For example, in real life, clean drinking water is essential for health and well-being.
  • sūyavasā: This term can be interpreted as "those who are well-prepared" or "those who are equipped." In a practical sense, this can relate to individuals or communities that are ready to face challenges, such as emergency preparedness in natural disasters.
  • adabdha: This word means "untouched" or "unharmed." In a real-life context, it can refer to the importance of maintaining the integrity of our environment, such as protecting untouched natural reserves from pollution and exploitation.
  • upa kṣeti: This phrase means "approaches" or "comes near." It signifies the act of seeking or striving towards something valuable, like pursuing education or personal growth.
  • vṛddhavayāḥ: This term refers to "the wise" or "the learned." In real life, this can be associated with the value of wisdom and knowledge, such as seeking guidance from mentors or educators.
  • suvīraḥ: This translates to "the strong" or "the valiant." In practical terms, it can represent individuals or groups who show courage in the face of adversity, like healthcare workers during a pandemic.
  • nakiṣ ṭaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "they do not harm." It emphasizes the importance of non-violence and compassion in our actions, akin to practicing kindness in our daily interactions.
  • ghnanty antito na dūrād: This means "they do not strike from afar." It suggests that true strength and impact come from direct engagement rather than distant actions, similar to community service where individuals work closely with those in need.
  • ya ādityānām bhavati praṇītau: This translates to "what is established by the sun." The sun symbolizes life, energy, and growth. In real life, this can relate to the importance of sustainable practices that harness solar energy for a cleaner environment.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of purity, preparedness, wisdom, strength, and non-violence in our lives. It encourages individuals to strive for personal growth while maintaining a harmonious relationship with nature and society. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as environmental conservation, community service, and personal development. By embodying these values, we contribute to a more sustainable and compassionate world.
