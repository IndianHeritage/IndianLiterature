नū इन्द्र शूर स्तवमान ऊती ब्रह्मजूतस् तन्vā वावृधस्व |उप नो वाजान मिमीहि उप स्तीन युयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
nū indra śūra stavamāna ūtī brahmajūtas tanvā vāvṛdhasva | upa no vājān mimīhy upa stīn yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • nū indra: This phrase refers to "O Indra," invoking the deity Indra, who is often associated with strength, power, and rain in Vedic literature.
  • śūra: This word means "brave" or "heroic," highlighting the qualities of valor and courage.
  • stavamāna: This term translates to "praising" or "worshiping," indicating an act of devotion or reverence towards Indra.
  • ūtī: This means "wealth" or "prosperity," suggesting a request for abundance and blessings.
  • brahmajūtas: This can be interpreted as "born of the divine" or "spiritual essence," emphasizing the connection to higher consciousness or divine knowledge.
  • tanvā: This word means "body" or "form," referring to the physical manifestation of the divine or the self.
  • vāvṛdhasva: This phrase means "may you grow" or "may you increase," expressing a desire for growth and enhancement.
  • upa no: This translates to "towards us," indicating a plea for the divine presence to come closer or to assist.
  • vājān: This word means "strength" or "power," often associated with the idea of energy or vigor.
  • mimīhy: This means "may you inspire" or "may you motivate," suggesting a call for divine guidance and inspiration.
  • upa stīn: This translates to "towards us," reinforcing the idea of seeking the divine's attention and support.
  • yūyam: This word means "you," referring to the divine entities being addressed.
  • pāta: This means "protect" or "guard," indicating a request for safety and security.
  • svastibhiḥ: This translates to "with auspiciousness," suggesting a desire for well-being and good fortune.
  • sadā: This means "always," indicating a continuous or eternal aspect.
  • naḥ: This word means "us," referring to the collective group seeking blessings.

Conclusion: The mantra is a heartfelt invocation to Indra, seeking his blessings for strength, prosperity, and protection. It emphasizes the importance of courage and divine support in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable during times of challenge or uncertainty, where individuals or communities seek strength and guidance. For example, during a difficult project at work or a personal crisis, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to invoke inner strength and seek support from the divine. Its significance lies in fostering a sense of connection to higher powers and encouraging individuals to strive for growth and well-being in their lives.
