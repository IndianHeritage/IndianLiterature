मन्त्र: का ईषते तुज्यते को बिभाय को मंसते संतं इन्द्रं को अन्ति |कस तोकाय का इभायोता राये 'धि ब्रवत तंवे3 को जनाय ||
ka īṣate tujyate ko bibhāya ko maṁsate santam indraṁ ko anti | kas tokāya ka ibhāyota rāye 'dhi bravat tanve3 ko janāya ||
  • Meaning of the Mantra:
    • ka īṣate: Who is the one that sees or perceives?
    • tujyate: Who is the one that is praised or worshipped?
    • ko bibhāya: Who is the one that fears?
    • ko maṁsate: Who is the one that is nourished or sustained?
    • santam indraṁ: The peaceful or benevolent Indra.
    • ko anti: Who is the one that speaks or declares?
    • kas tokāya: Who is the one that is worthy of offerings?
    • ka ibhāyota: Who is the one that is revered or honored?
    • rāye 'dhi: For wealth or prosperity.
    • bravat: Speak or declare.
    • tanve3: In this body or form.
    • ko janāya: Who is the one that gives birth or creates?
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a series of questions that reflect on the nature of existence, divinity, and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It invites contemplation on who perceives reality, who is worthy of worship, and who sustains life.
    • Indra, often seen as a deity of rain and thunder, symbolizes strength and benevolence, suggesting that true power lies in peace and sustenance rather than fear and domination.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, the leader who listens to the needs of the people and acts with compassion embodies the qualities of Indra, fostering a sense of unity and support.
    • In personal relationships, the one who nurtures and sustains connections through love and understanding is revered, much like the divine qualities described in the mantra.
    • In environmental stewardship, those who protect and sustain nature can be seen as embodying the essence of this mantra, as they ensure the well-being of future generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of introspection and understanding our roles in the larger tapestry of life.
    • It encourages individuals to seek wisdom, act with compassion, and recognize the divine in everyday interactions.
    • In a world often driven by competition and fear, the teachings of this mantra remind us to cultivate peace, nurture relationships, and honor the interconnectedness of all beings.
