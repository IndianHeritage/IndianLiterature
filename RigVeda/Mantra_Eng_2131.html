यः सुन्वते पचते दुध्र आ चिद वाजं दर्दर्षि स किलासि सत्यः |वयं त इन्द्र विश्वहा प्रियासः सुवीरासो विदथम आ वदेम ||
yaḥ sunvate pacate dudhra ā cid vājaṁ dardarṣi sa kilāsi satyaḥ | vayaṁ ta indra viśvaha priyāsaḥ suvīrāso vidatham ā vadema ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaḥ: who
    • sunvate: listens or hears
    • pacate: cooks or prepares
    • dudhra: with great effort
    • ā: from
    • cid: if
    • vājaṁ: the prize or reward
    • dardarṣi: has seen or perceived
    • sa: he or that
    • kilāsi: indeed or truly
    • satyaḥ: the truth
    • vayaṁ: we
    • ta: that
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • viśvaha: all or universal
    • priyāsaḥ: dear or beloved
    • suvīrāso: with great strength
    • vidatham: the arrangement or plan
    • ā: to
    • vadema: we speak or declare
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of listening and understanding.
    • It emphasizes the value of truth and the efforts made to achieve it.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies seeking divine support in our endeavors.
    • It suggests that through collective strength and unity, we can achieve our goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, listening to each member's ideas and contributions can lead to a more successful project outcome.
    • In personal relationships, understanding and valuing the truth fosters trust and strengthens bonds.
    • In community initiatives, coming together with a shared vision can lead to impactful changes.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of communication, truth, and collaboration.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, professional environments, and community efforts.
    • By embodying the principles of listening, understanding, and working together, we can navigate challenges and achieve our aspirations.
