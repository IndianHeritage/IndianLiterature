इच्छन्त रेतो मिथस तनूषु सं जानता स्वैर दक्षैर अमूराः ||
icchanta reto mithas tanūṣu saṁ jānata svair dakṣair amūrāḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • icchanta: Desiring or wishing for.
    • reto: Referring to vitality or energy, often associated with reproductive energy.
    • mithas: Together or in harmony.
    • tanūṣu: In bodies or forms.
    • saṁ: Together or in union.
    • jānata: Knowing or understanding.
    • svair: Freely or independently.
    • dakṣair: Skilled or capable.
    • amūrāḥ: Those who are not bound or limited.
  • Meaning: This mantra speaks about the desire for vitality and energy that exists in harmony among beings. It emphasizes the importance of understanding and skill in achieving a state of freedom and capability. The essence of the mantra suggests that when individuals come together with a shared desire for growth and vitality, they can achieve great things through mutual understanding and skillful action.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Teamwork in Organizations: In a workplace, when team members share a common goal (icchanta) and work together harmoniously (mithas), they can achieve greater productivity and innovation. For instance, a project team that collaborates effectively can produce results that exceed individual contributions.
    • Community Initiatives: Community projects, such as environmental clean-up drives, exemplify this mantra. When individuals come together (saṁ) with a shared understanding (jānata) of the importance of a clean environment, they can create a significant impact.
    • Personal Relationships: In personal relationships, mutual understanding and support (svair dakṣair) can lead to stronger bonds. Couples who communicate openly and work together towards common goals often experience deeper connections and fulfillment.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the power of collective desire and understanding. It teaches us that when individuals unite with a common purpose and harness their skills, they can transcend limitations (amūrāḥ) and achieve remarkable outcomes. This principle is applicable in various aspects of life, including professional settings, community efforts, and personal relationships, highlighting the importance of collaboration and shared aspirations in fostering growth and success.
