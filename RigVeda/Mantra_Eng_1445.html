सद्यश्चित्तमभिष्टये करो वशश्च वाजिनम् |सास्माकमअनवद्यतूतुजान वेधसामिमां वाचं न वेधसाम् ||
sadyaś cit tam abhiṣṭaye karo vaśaś ca vājinam | sāsmākam anavadya tūtujāna vedhasām imāṁ vācaṁ na vedhasām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sadyaś: immediately, right now
    • cit: consciousness, awareness
    • tam: that, referring to the divine or the ultimate truth
    • abhiṣṭaye: for the purpose of achieving, for the attainment
    • karo: do, act
    • vaśaś: under control, in command
    • ca: and
    • vājinam: the strong, powerful one
    • sāsmākam: our, belonging to us
    • anavadya: without fault, impeccable
    • tūtujāna: to be known, to be recognized
    • vedhasām: of the seers, of the wise
    • imāṁ: this, referring to the speech or utterance
    • vācaṁ: speech, word
    • na: not
    • vedhasām: of the seers, of the wise
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of immediate action and awareness in the pursuit of truth and divine connection.
    • It calls for the individual to act with intention and control, aligning oneself with the powerful forces of the universe.
    • The phrase "anavadya tūtujāna" suggests that our actions and words should be impeccable and worthy of recognition by the wise.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied by encouraging individuals to take immediate and conscious actions towards their goals, ensuring that their efforts are aligned with their values.
    • In personal relationships, it serves as a reminder to communicate thoughtfully and with integrity, fostering deeper connections based on mutual respect.
    • In spiritual practices, it can inspire practitioners to engage in meditation or prayer with a focused mind, seeking to connect with the divine presence in their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of conscious action and impeccable communication in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to strive for excellence in their thoughts, words, and deeds, fostering a harmonious relationship with themselves and the world around them.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can cultivate a life of purpose, integrity, and connection to the divine.
