जिघ्मं नुनुद्रे 'वतं तया दिशासिञ्चन्न उत्सं गोतमाय तृष्णजे |आ गच्छन्तीम अवसā चित्रभानवः कामं विप्रस्य तर्पयन्त धामभिः ||
jihmaṁ nunudre 'vataṁ tayā diśāsiñcann utsaṁ gotamāya tṛṣṇaje | ā gacchantīm avasā citrabhānavaḥ kāmaṁ viprasya tarpayanta dhāmabhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • jihmaṁ: crooked or twisted
    • nunudre: to be driven away or dispelled
    • 'vataṁ: wind or air
    • tayā: by you
    • diśāsiñcann: having poured forth in directions
    • utsaṁ: the essence or the best part
    • gotamāya: for the sage Gotama
    • tṛṣṇaje: born of thirst or desire
    • ā gacchantīm: going towards
    • avasā: having settled or established
    • citrabhānavaḥ: of the bright sun
    • kāmaṁ: desire or wish
    • viprasya: of the wise or learned
    • tarpayanta: satisfying or fulfilling
    • dhāmabhiḥ: with the realms or abodes
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the dispelling of desires and the fulfillment of the wise.
    • The "crooked" desires are likened to the wind, which can be unpredictable and difficult to control.
    • By invoking the essence of the sage Gotama, the mantra suggests a path to clarity and satisfaction.
    • The "bright sun" symbolizes enlightenment and the realization of true desires, which are not merely physical but spiritual and intellectual.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, individuals often face distractions and desires that lead them away from their true goals. This mantra encourages one to focus on what is essential and to dispel the unnecessary.
    • For instance, a student may feel overwhelmed by the desire to achieve high grades (a crooked desire) but can find clarity by focusing on learning and understanding the material (the essence).
    • In a corporate setting, employees may be driven by competition and ambition (desires) but can achieve greater satisfaction by collaborating and sharing knowledge (the fulfillment of the wise).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and dispelling misguided desires while seeking true fulfillment through wisdom and enlightenment.
    • It serves as a reminder to focus on what truly matters in life, whether in personal aspirations or professional endeavors.
    • By applying the teachings of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a more meaningful and satisfying existence, aligning their actions with their true purpose.
