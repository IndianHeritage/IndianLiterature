इन्द्राकुत्सा वहमान रथेन वाम अत्यापि कर्णे वहन्तु |निः शīm अद्भ्यो धमतो निः षदस्थाण मगहोनो हृदो वरथस तमांसि ||
indrākutsā vahamānā rathenā vām atyā api karṇe vahantu | niḥ ṣīm adbhyo dhamatho niḥ ṣadhasthān maghono hṛdo varathas tamāṁsi ||
  • Indrākutsā vahamānā rathenā vām atyā api karṇe vahantu:
    • Indrākutsā: Refers to the powerful and divine aspect of Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
    • Vahamānā: Means 'carrying' or 'bringing forth', indicating the action of moving forward with strength.
    • Rathenā: Translates to 'by the chariot', symbolizing the journey of life and the means of progress.
    • Vām atyā: Means 'to you, O Indra', indicating a direct appeal or invocation to the deity.
    • Api karṇe vahantu: Suggests 'may they carry (us) to the ears', implying the importance of listening and receiving wisdom.
  • Niḥ ṣīm adbhyo dhamatho niḥ ṣadhasthān maghono hṛdo varathas tamāṁsi:
    • Niḥ ṣīm: Means 'without obstacles' or 'free from hindrances', indicating a desire for a smooth path.
    • Adbhyo dhamatho: Refers to 'from the waters', symbolizing the source of life and nourishment.
    • Niḥ ṣadhasthān: Means 'without boundaries', suggesting a limitless potential.
    • Maghono: Refers to 'of the generous', indicating the importance of generosity and abundance.
    • Hṛdo: Means 'of the heart', emphasizing the emotional and spiritual aspects of life.
    • Varathas: Suggests 'may they be strong', indicating a plea for strength and resilience.
    • Tamāṁsi: Refers to 'darkness', symbolizing ignorance or obstacles that need to be overcome.

Conclusion: This mantra invokes the divine strength of Indra, seeking guidance and support in overcoming obstacles in life. It emphasizes the importance of listening to wisdom, the need for a smooth journey, and the value of generosity and strength. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during challenging times, such as when facing personal or professional hurdles, reminding individuals to seek divine support, remain generous, and strive for resilience. It serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of community, and the strength that comes from unity and divine guidance.
