ते हि प्रजायाः अभरण्त विष्रवो बृहस्पतिः वृषभः सोमजामयः |यज्ञैर अथर्वा प्रथमः विद्धारयद देवाः दक्षैर भृगवः सं चिकीर्तिरे ||
te hi prajāyā abharanta vi śravo bṛhaspatir vṛṣabhaḥ somajāmayaḥ | yajñair atharvā prathamo vi dhārayad devā dakṣair bhṛgavaḥ saṁ cikitrire ||
  • te hi prajāyā abharanta: This phrase can be translated as "they who bring forth progeny." It signifies the importance of creation and the role of divine forces in the process of birth and nurturing life. In real life, this can be seen in the context of parents who nurture their children, ensuring their growth and development.
  • vi śravo bṛhaspatir vṛṣabhaḥ: Here, "Bṛhaspati" refers to the deity of wisdom and knowledge, often associated with Jupiter. "Vṛṣabhaḥ" means the bull, symbolizing strength and fertility. This part emphasizes the need for wisdom and strength in guiding progeny. For example, a wise leader in a community or family who provides guidance and support can be seen as embodying these qualities.
  • somajāmayaḥ: This term refers to the "son of the moon," which can symbolize the nurturing and gentle aspects of life. In real life, this can be related to the nurturing qualities of caregivers, such as mothers, who provide emotional and physical support to their children.
  • yajñair atharvā prathamo vi dhārayad: "Yajñair" refers to sacrifices or offerings, and "Atharvā" relates to the Atharva Veda, which emphasizes rituals and the importance of maintaining harmony in the universe. This line suggests that through rituals and offerings, one can sustain and support life. In modern life, this can be seen in community gatherings or family traditions that strengthen bonds and promote well-being.
  • devā dakṣair bhṛgavaḥ saṁ cikitrire: This part speaks of the gods (devā) who are skilled (dakṣair) and who take care (cikitrire) of the world. It highlights the role of divine intervention in maintaining balance and order. In real life, this can be reflected in the efforts of community leaders or organizations that work towards social welfare and harmony.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of creation, wisdom, nurturing, and the importance of rituals in sustaining life. It signifies that through divine guidance and human effort, we can foster growth and harmony in our lives and communities. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in family dynamics, community leadership, and social responsibilities, reminding us of our roles in nurturing and supporting one another for a balanced and harmonious existence.
