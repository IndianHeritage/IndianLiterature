क्रव्यादम् अग्निम प्र हिणोमि दूरं यमराज्ञो गच्छतु रिप्रवाहः |इहैवायम् इतरः जातवेदाः देवभ्यः हव्यम् वहतु प्रजानन् ||
kravyādam agnim pra hiṇomi dūraṁ yamarājño gacchatu ripravāhaḥ | ihaivāyam itaro jātavedā devebhyo havyaṁ vahatu prajānan ||
  • kravyādam agnim pra hiṇomi: This phrase can be translated as "I invoke the fire (Agni) that consumes the offerings." Here, Agni represents the divine fire, which is a symbol of transformation and purification. In real life, this can be likened to the process of change we undergo when we face challenges, much like how fire transforms materials into something new.
  • dūraṁ yamarājño gacchatu ripravāhaḥ: This translates to "May the flow of the enemies go far away from the realm of Yama (the god of death)." Yama symbolizes the inevitability of death and the journey of the soul. In practical terms, this can be understood as a prayer for protection against negative influences or adversities in life, encouraging individuals to focus on positivity and resilience.
  • ihaivāyam itaro jātavedā: This means "Here is the other one, the all-knowing." Jātavedā refers to the one who knows all births and is often associated with Agni. This can be related to the idea of wisdom and knowledge in our lives, emphasizing the importance of learning from experiences and understanding the world around us.
  • devebhyo havyaṁ vahatu prajānan: This translates to "May the offerings reach the gods, who are the creators." This line highlights the significance of devotion and the act of giving. In real life, this can be seen in the practice of gratitude, where individuals acknowledge and appreciate the contributions of others in their lives, fostering a sense of community and connection.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of transformation, protection, wisdom, and devotion. Its significance lies in the reminder to seek positive change in our lives, to protect ourselves from negativity, to value knowledge, and to express gratitude. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide individuals in their personal growth, relationships, and spiritual practices, encouraging a holistic approach to living harmoniously with oneself and the universe.
