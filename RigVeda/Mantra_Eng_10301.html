आप इद वाऽउ भेषजीर आपो अमीवचातनीः | आपः सर्वस्य भेषजीस तास ते कृत्वन्तु भेषजम् ||
āpa id vā u bheṣajīr āpo amīvacātanīḥ | āpaḥ sarvasya bheṣajīs tās te kṛṇvantu bheṣajam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āpa: Waters
    • id: Here
    • vā: Or
    • u: And
    • bheṣajīr: Healing
    • āpo: Waters (again)
    • amīvacātanīḥ: Those that are full of healing properties
    • sarvasya: Of all
    • bheṣajīs: Healers
    • tās: Those
    • te: You
    • kṛṇvantu: May they create
    • bheṣajam: Healing (or medicine)
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes the healing powers of water, suggesting that water has the ability to heal and restore health.
    • It emphasizes the importance of water as a universal healer, capable of providing remedies for all ailments.
    • The repetition of "āpa" highlights the significance of water in various forms, whether it be physical water or the metaphorical essence of healing.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Hydration: Just as water is essential for physical health, staying hydrated is crucial for maintaining bodily functions. Dehydration can lead to various health issues, emphasizing the healing aspect of water.
    • Aromatherapy: In holistic healing practices, water is often used to diffuse essential oils, creating a healing environment that promotes relaxation and well-being.
    • Natural Springs: Many cultures believe in the healing properties of natural springs and mineral waters, where people visit to rejuvenate and heal their ailments.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the vital role of water in healing and health, reminding us of its importance in our daily lives.
    • It encourages us to recognize and utilize the healing properties of water, whether through drinking, bathing, or engaging in therapeutic practices.
    • In a broader sense, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature and health, urging us to respect and preserve our natural resources for the well-being of all.
