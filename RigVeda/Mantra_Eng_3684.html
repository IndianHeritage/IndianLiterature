वयं ते अग्न उक्थैर विद्हेम वयं हव्यैः पावक भद्रशोचे |अस्मे रयिं विश्ववारं सम इन्वास्मे विश्वानि द्रविणानि धेहि ||
vayaṁ te agna ukthair vidhema vayaṁ havyaiḥ pāvaka bhadraśoce | asme rayiṁ viśvavāraṁ sam invāsme viśvāni draviṇāni dhehi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vayaṁ: We
    • te: Your
    • agna: O Fire
    • ukthair: With our words
    • vidhema: We meditate
    • havyaiḥ: With offerings
    • pāvaka: O Purifier
    • bhadraśoce: O Auspicious One
    • asme: For us
    • rayiṁ: Wealth
    • viśvavāraṁ: All-encompassing
    • sam: Together
    • invāsme: May we be invoked
    • viśvāni: All
    • draviṇāni: Wealthy things
    • dhehi: Bestow
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the sacred fire, asking for blessings and wealth. It emphasizes the importance of offerings and the purification that fire represents. The mantra seeks to invoke the divine presence of Agni to bestow prosperity and abundance upon the devotees.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, Agni is often invoked during ceremonies such as weddings and yajnas (sacrificial offerings) where offerings are made to the fire, symbolizing the connection between the material and spiritual worlds.
    • In modern life, the concept of 'fire' can be seen as a metaphor for passion and energy. For instance, individuals pursuing their goals with fervor can be seen as invoking their inner 'Agni' to achieve success.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of intention and devotion in seeking blessings. It teaches that through sincere offerings and meditation, one can attract prosperity and well-being.
    • In a broader context, it encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of gratitude and to recognize the abundance in their lives, fostering a mindset that attracts further blessings.
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied by setting intentions for personal and professional growth, engaging in acts of kindness, and being open to receiving the wealth of experiences and opportunities that come one's way.
