यज्ञेन वाचः पदवीयं आयन ताम अन्व अविन्दन्न ऋषिषु प्रवेश्टाम् |ताम आभृत्या व्य अदधुः पुरुत्रा तां सप्त रेब्हा अभि सं नवन्ते ||
yajñena vācaḥ padavīyam āyan tām anv avindann ṛṣiṣu praviṣṭām | tām ābhṛtyā vy adadhuḥ purutrā tāṁ sapta rebhā abhi saṁ navante ||
  • yajñena: This word refers to the concept of sacrifice or offering, which is central to Vedic rituals. In real life, this can be seen in various forms of selfless service or contributions to society, such as volunteering or charitable donations.
  • vācaḥ: This translates to "speech" or "words." It emphasizes the power of communication. In everyday life, effective communication is crucial in personal relationships, workplaces, and community interactions.
  • padavīyam: This means "path" or "way." It signifies the journey one takes in life. For example, choosing a career path or a personal development journey reflects this concept.
  • āyan: This word means "arrival" or "coming." It can be related to the idea of achieving goals or milestones in life, such as graduating from school or reaching a personal goal.
  • tām: This is a demonstrative pronoun meaning "that." It refers to the subject being discussed, which in this context relates to the essence of the mantra.
  • anv avindann: This phrase means "having found" or "having discovered." It signifies the pursuit of knowledge and understanding, akin to the journey of learning through education or life experiences.
  • ṛṣiṣu: This refers to "sages" or "seers." In real life, this can be likened to mentors or wise individuals who guide us through our journeys, such as teachers or spiritual leaders.
  • praviṣṭām: This means "entered" or "penetrated." It can symbolize the deep understanding or insight gained through experience, similar to how one might gain wisdom through life challenges.
  • ābhṛtyā: This translates to "by the support" or "by the help." It highlights the importance of support systems in our lives, such as family, friends, or community networks.
  • vy adadhuḥ: This means "they gave" or "they provided." It reflects the act of sharing resources or knowledge, which is essential in collaborative environments like workplaces or community projects.
  • purutrā: This word means "everywhere" or "in all places." It signifies the universality of the principles discussed in the mantra, applicable in various contexts of life.
  • tām: Again, this refers to "that," emphasizing the focus on the subject of the mantra.
  • sapta: This means "seven." In many cultures, the number seven holds significance, often representing completeness or perfection, such as the seven wonders of the world or the seven days of the week.
  • rebhā: This translates to "pulses" or "vibrations." It can symbolize the energy and dynamism present in life, akin to the rhythms of nature or human emotions.
  • abhi: This means "towards" or "in the direction of." It indicates movement or progress, similar to striving towards goals or aspirations.
  • saṁ navante: This phrase means "they rejoice" or "they celebrate." It reflects the joy that comes from achievements, community gatherings, or personal milestones.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of sacrifice, communication, and the journey of life. It emphasizes the importance of support, knowledge, and the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, community service, and the pursuit of wisdom. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that through collective efforts and shared knowledge, we can achieve greater understanding and joy in our lives.
