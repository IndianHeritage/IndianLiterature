माहं मघोनो वरुण प्रियस्य भूридाव्न आ विदं शूनम् आपेः |मा रायो राजन सुमाद अव स्थाम बृहद वदेम विदथे सुवीराः ||
māham maghono varuṇa priyasya bhūridāvna ā vidaṁ śūnam āpeḥ | mā rāyo rājan suyamād ava sthām bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ ||
  • māham maghono varuṇa priyasya bhūridāvna ā vidaṁ śūnam āpeḥ
    • māham: This translates to "I" or "myself," indicating a personal invocation or plea.
    • maghono: Refers to "of the bountiful" or "of abundance," often associated with prosperity and wealth.
    • varuṇa: Varuṇa is the deity of cosmic order and water, symbolizing the moral and natural laws of the universe.
    • priyasya: Means "of the beloved," suggesting a connection to something cherished or valued.
    • bhūridāvna: This term can be interpreted as "the one who gives abundantly," emphasizing generosity and the act of giving.
    • ā vidaṁ: Translates to "may I know" or "let me understand," indicating a quest for knowledge or insight.
    • śūnam: Refers to "emptiness" or "void," which can symbolize a lack of resources or understanding.
    • āpeḥ: Means "of the waters," connecting to the life-giving and purifying aspects of water.
  • mā rāyo rājan suyamād ava sthām bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ
    • mā: Again means "not" or "do not," indicating a negation or a plea for avoidance of negativity.
    • rāyo: Refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," emphasizing the importance of abundance in life.
    • rājan: Means "O King," addressing a figure of authority or power, which can be interpreted as a metaphor for leadership.
    • suyamād: Translates to "by good deeds" or "through virtuous actions," highlighting the significance of morality.
    • ava sthām: Means "may I attain" or "let me achieve," indicating a desire for success or fulfillment.
    • bṛhad: Refers to "great" or "vast," suggesting the pursuit of grand goals or aspirations.
    • vadema: Means "we speak" or "we declare," indicating a collective voice or intention.
    • vidathe: Refers to "in the assembly" or "in the gathering," emphasizing community and collaboration.
    • suvīrāḥ: Means "the brave" or "the valiant," celebrating courage and strength in the face of challenges.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a profound plea for understanding, abundance, and moral integrity. It emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and the value of generosity, both from the divine and within ourselves. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal development, where individuals strive for self-awareness and understanding of their purpose.
  • In community initiatives, where collective efforts are made to promote prosperity and support one another through virtuous actions.
  • In leadership, where leaders are encouraged to act with integrity and generosity, fostering an environment of trust and collaboration.

Ultimately, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for a harmonious balance between personal aspirations and communal well-being, urging us to act with courage and wisdom in our pursuits.
