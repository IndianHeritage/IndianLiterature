प्र सīm आदित्यः असृजद् विधर्ताम् ऋतं सिन्धवो वरुणस्य यन्ति |न श्राम्यन्ति न वि मुचन्त्य एते वयो न पप्तु रघुयाः परिज्मन् ||
pra sīm ādityo asṛjad vidhartām̐ ṛtaṁ sindhavo varuṇasya yanti | na śrāmyanti na vi mucanty ete vayo na paptū raghuyā parijman ||
  • pra sīm ādityo asṛjad: This phrase can be translated as "the Sun (Aditya) has created (asṛjad) the path (pra sīm)." Here, Aditya symbolizes the source of light and life, representing knowledge and enlightenment.
  • vidhartām̐ ṛtaṁ: This means "to uphold (vidhartām̐) the cosmic order (ṛtaṁ)." In Vedic philosophy, ṛta represents the natural order and truth that governs the universe, akin to the laws of nature.
  • sindhavo varuṇasya yanti: This translates to "the rivers (sindhavo) flow towards Varuna." Varuna is the deity associated with water and cosmic order, symbolizing the connection between nature and divine principles.
  • na śrāmyanti na vi mucanty: This means "they do not tire (na śrāmyanti) nor do they release (na vi mucanty)." This suggests a sense of perseverance and continuity in the natural order.
  • ete vayo na paptū raghuyā parijman: This can be interpreted as "these (ete) beings (vayo) do not fall (na paptū) from the path of righteousness (raghuyā parijman)." It emphasizes the importance of adhering to the path of truth and righteousness in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of the Sun as a source of enlightenment and the importance of maintaining the cosmic order (ṛta) in our lives. It teaches us about the interconnectedness of nature and divinity, urging us to remain steadfast and committed to the path of truth and righteousness. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to our daily actions, encouraging us to act with integrity, respect the natural world, and strive for knowledge and enlightenment in our personal and communal lives. By embodying these principles, we contribute to a harmonious existence that aligns with the cosmic order.
