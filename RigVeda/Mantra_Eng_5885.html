यत सोम ā सुत नरा इन्द्राग्नī अजोहवुः |सप्तीवन्तā सपर्व्यवः ||
yat soma ā sute nara indrāgnī ajohavuḥ | saptīvantā saparyavaḥ ||
  • yat - This word translates to "that" or "which," indicating a reference to something significant.
  • soma - Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
  • ā sute - This phrase means "having been poured" or "having been offered," referring to the act of offering Soma to the deities.
  • nara - This word means "man" or "human," emphasizing the connection between humanity and the divine.
  • indrāgnī - This compound refers to the deities Indra and Agni, representing strength and fire, respectively. Indra is the king of gods, while Agni is the god of fire and sacrifice.
  • ajohavuḥ - This term can be interpreted as "they have not been defeated" or "they have not been overcome," suggesting resilience and strength.
  • saptīvantā - This word means "having seven," which can symbolize completeness or wholeness, often associated with the seven sages or the seven sacred rivers.
  • saparyavaḥ - This term refers to "those who are worshipped" or "those who are honored," indicating the reverence given to the deities and the sacred.

The mantra speaks to the offering of Soma to the deities Indra and Agni, highlighting the relationship between humans and divine forces. It emphasizes the importance of resilience and strength in the face of challenges, as well as the completeness and reverence found in spiritual practices.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can use this mantra during rituals or meditation to invoke strength and resilience in their lives.
  • Community Gatherings: In communal settings, this mantra can be recited to foster unity and collective strength, much like how communities come together to support one another in times of need.
  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties, recalling the essence of this mantra can inspire individuals to remain steadfast and not be overcome by adversity.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, resilience, and reverence towards the divine. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine, encouraging individuals to seek support from spiritual practices and community in their journey through life.
