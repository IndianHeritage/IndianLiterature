शग्ध्य ū3 षु शचीपात इन्द्र विश्वाभिर ऊतिभिः | भगं न हि त्वा यशसां वसुविदम् अनु शूर चरामसि ||
śagdhy ū3 ṣu śacīpata indra viśvābhir ūtibhiḥ | bhagaṁ na hi tvā yaśasaṁ vasuvidam anu śūra carāmasi ||
  • śagdhy ū3: This phrase can be interpreted as a call for strength and vitality. In real life, this can relate to the importance of physical and mental health, emphasizing the need for individuals to maintain their well-being to face life's challenges.
  • ṣu śacīpata: This refers to the divine qualities of Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature. Indra symbolizes power, courage, and leadership. In a modern context, this can be seen in leaders who inspire and guide others, showcasing the importance of strong leadership in communities and organizations.
  • indra viśvābhir ūtibhiḥ: This part highlights the universal blessings and abundance that Indra provides. It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the shared prosperity that can be achieved through cooperation. In everyday life, this can be reflected in community efforts, where collective action leads to greater benefits for all.
  • bhagaṁ na hi tvā yaśasaṁ: This phrase speaks to the idea of fortune and glory. It suggests that true success is not just about personal gain but also about the recognition and respect one earns in society. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who contribute to their communities, gaining respect and admiration for their selfless acts.
  • vasuvidam anu: This can be interpreted as the pursuit of wealth and resources. It emphasizes the importance of responsible stewardship of resources for the benefit of all. In contemporary society, this is relevant in discussions about sustainability and ethical consumption, where individuals and organizations are encouraged to use resources wisely.
  • śūra carāmasi: This phrase refers to the idea of moving forward with courage and determination. It encourages individuals to take action and strive for their goals despite obstacles. In real life, this can be seen in stories of perseverance, where individuals overcome challenges to achieve their dreams.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, leadership, community, respect, responsible resource management, and perseverance. Its significance lies in its call for individuals to embody these qualities in their lives, fostering a sense of unity and purpose. In today's world, applying these principles can lead to personal growth and a more harmonious society, where collective efforts result in shared prosperity and well-being.
