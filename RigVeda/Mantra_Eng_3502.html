इन्द्रा ह रत्नं वरुणा धेष्ठेत्था नृभ्यः शशमानेब्यस ता |यदी सखायाः सख्याय सोमैः सुतैः सुप्रयसा मादयैते ||
indrā ha ratnaṁ varuṇā dheṣṭhetthā nṛbhyaḥ śaśamānebhyas tā | yadī sakhāyā sakhyāya somaiḥ sutebhiḥ suprayasā mādayaite ||
  • Indrā ha ratnaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "Indra, the lord of treasures." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunder, and the heavens. In real life, this can symbolize the pursuit of wealth and prosperity, not just in material terms but also in knowledge and relationships.
  • Varuṇā dheṣṭhetthā nṛbhyaḥ: This translates to "May Varuna bestow upon us." Varuna is another significant deity, representing cosmic order and truth. This line emphasizes the importance of divine blessings in our endeavors. In practical terms, it suggests that success often requires a moral foundation and alignment with universal principles.
  • Śaśamānebhyas tā: This means "from those who are prosperous." It highlights the idea of learning from those who have achieved success. In everyday life, this can be seen in mentorship, where individuals seek guidance from those who have navigated similar paths successfully.
  • Yadī sakhāyā sakhyāya: This translates to "if there is friendship." The emphasis on friendship indicates the value of relationships and alliances in achieving goals. In real life, this can be reflected in teamwork and collaboration, where mutual support leads to greater achievements.
  • Somaiḥ sutebhiḥ: This means "with the sons of the moon." The moon is often associated with nourishment and fertility. This line suggests that prosperity can be enhanced through nurturing relationships and connections. In practical terms, it can refer to family ties or community support that fosters growth.
  • Suprayasā mādayaite: This translates to "may they bring joy." The conclusion of the mantra emphasizes the ultimate goal of all endeavors: happiness and fulfillment. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of careers, hobbies, and relationships that bring joy and satisfaction.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings, learning from the prosperous, valuing friendships, and nurturing relationships to achieve joy and fulfillment. Its significance lies in the reminder that success is not solely an individual pursuit but is deeply intertwined with community, morality, and divine grace. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in professional settings where collaboration and ethical practices lead to sustainable success, or in personal relationships where support and love foster happiness.
