इन्द्रस्य सोम राधसे शम् पवस्व विचर्षणे |प्रजावत् रेत आ भर ||
indrasya soma rādhase śam pavasva vicarṣaṇe | prajāvad reta ā bhara ||
  • Indrasya: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Soma: A sacred drink in Vedic rituals, representing divine inspiration and the essence of life.
  • Rādhase: Pertains to the essence or the qualities associated with Indra, often linked to prosperity and abundance.
  • Śam: Means peace, tranquility, or well-being, indicating a state of harmony.
  • Pavasva: A call for purification or cleansing, suggesting the removal of negativity or obstacles.
  • Vicarṣaṇe: Refers to the act of moving or wandering, symbolizing the journey of life and the pursuit of goals.
  • Prajāvad: Means to be fruitful or to generate progeny, emphasizing growth and continuity.
  • Retā: Refers to vitality or energy, often associated with fertility and creative power.
  • Ā bhara: A plea to fill or bestow, indicating a desire for abundance and fulfillment.

  • Meaning: This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra and the divine essence of Soma to bring peace, prosperity, and vitality into one’s life. It emphasizes the importance of purification and the pursuit of growth and abundance.
  • Real-life Example: In a modern context, this mantra can be related to the pursuit of personal and professional goals. For instance, an entrepreneur may recite this mantra to seek inspiration and strength while navigating the challenges of starting a new business. The call for purification can be seen as a reminder to clear negative thoughts and focus on positive actions.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, encouraging individuals to seek strength, peace, and abundance. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as during personal challenges, professional endeavors, or even in family planning. By invoking these qualities, one can strive for a balanced and fulfilling life, aligning with the divine energies represented by Indra and Soma.
