आकेनिपासो अहभिर दविद्वतः स्वर्ण न शुक्रं तन्वन्त आ रजः | सूरश्चिद अश्वान युयुजाना ईयते विश्वांऽ अनु स्वधया चेतथस् पथः ||
ākenipāso ahabhir davidhvataḥ sva1r ṇa śukraṁ tanvanta ā rajaḥ | sūraś cid aśvān yuyujāna īyate viśvām̐ anu svadhayā cetathas pathaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ākenipāso: Those who are bound by the sky or the heavens.
    • ahabhir: By the day or the light.
    • davidhvataḥ: Those who are separated or divided.
    • sva1r: The self or one's own.
    • ṇa: Not or without.
    • śukraṁ: Brightness or purity.
    • tanvanta: Those who are endowed with form or body.
    • ā rajaḥ: The essence or the quality of being.
    • sūraś: The brave or the strong.
    • cid: Consciousness or awareness.
    • aśvān: Horses, often symbolizing speed and power.
    • yuyujāna: To unite or to bring together.
    • īyate: Is being done or is happening.
    • viśvām̐: All or the universe.
    • anu: Following or in accordance with.
    • svadhayā: By one's own will or intention.
    • cetathas: With thought or consideration.
    • pathaḥ: The path or the way.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the connection between the divine and the earthly realm, emphasizing the importance of unity and consciousness.
    • The phrase "ākenipāso" suggests that we are all interconnected through the vastness of the sky, symbolizing the universal bond that exists among all beings.
    • "By the day" indicates the importance of light, knowledge, and awareness in our lives, guiding us towards truth and understanding.
    • The mantra encourages us to embrace our individuality ("sva1r") while recognizing that we are part of a larger whole ("viśvām̐").
    • The mention of "sūraś" and "aśvān" symbolizes strength and the power of unity, suggesting that when we come together with a shared purpose, we can achieve great things.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, whether in sports or work, individuals must recognize their unique strengths while working towards a common goal, much like the "sūraś cid aśvān" coming together to achieve success.
    • In community service, people from diverse backgrounds unite to address social issues, embodying the essence of "anu svadhayā cetathas pathaḥ" by following a path of collective intention and action.
    • In personal growth, individuals can reflect on their own consciousness and intentions, striving for clarity and purity in their actions, akin to the "śukraṁ tanvanta" aspect of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of unity, consciousness, and intention in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to recognize their role within the larger universe and to act with awareness and purpose.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger relationships, effective teamwork, and a deeper understanding of our shared existence.
