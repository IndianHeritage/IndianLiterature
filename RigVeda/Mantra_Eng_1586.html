उक्षां महां अभि ववक्ष एने अजरस तस्थाव इतौति ऋष्वः | उर्व्याः पदो नि दधाति सानौ रिहन्त्य ऊधो अरुषासो अस्य ||
ukṣā mahām̐ abhi vavakṣa ene ajaras tasthāv itaūtir ṛṣvaḥ | urvyāḥ pado ni dadhāti sānau rihanty ūdho aruṣāso asya ||
  • ukṣā: This word can be interpreted as "awakening" or "arising." It signifies the beginning of something new, much like the dawn of a new day.
  • mahām̐: This term means "great" or "mighty," indicating the significance and power of the subject being discussed.
  • abhi vavakṣa: This phrase translates to "has come forth" or "has manifested," suggesting the emergence of a profound truth or reality.
  • ene: This word means "that" or "which," referring to the subject of the mantra.
  • ajaras: This term means "immortal" or "eternal," emphasizing the timeless nature of the truth being revealed.
  • tasthāv: This means "stands" or "remains," indicating stability and permanence.
  • itaūtir: This word can be interpreted as "beyond" or "transcending," suggesting a state that surpasses ordinary existence.
  • ṛṣvaḥ: This term refers to "the strong" or "the powerful," highlighting the strength inherent in the subject.
  • urvyāḥ: This means "of the earth" or "of the world," connecting the mantra to the physical realm.
  • pado: This word translates to "foot" or "path," symbolizing the journey or the way forward.
  • ni dadhāti: This phrase means "establishes" or "supports," indicating a foundation or grounding.
  • sānau: This term can be interpreted as "in harmony" or "together," suggesting unity and cooperation.
  • rihanty: This means "to remove" or "to eliminate," indicating the act of clearing obstacles.
  • ūdho: This word refers to "the bright" or "the radiant," symbolizing enlightenment and clarity.
  • aruṣāso: This term means "of the red dawn," representing new beginnings and hope.
  • asya: This means "of this" or "of it," referring back to the subject of the mantra.

Conclusion: The mantra speaks to the emergence of a great and eternal truth that stands firm and transcends ordinary existence. It emphasizes the importance of establishing a path that is grounded in harmony and unity, while also removing obstacles to enlightenment. In real life, this can be applied to personal growth, where individuals seek to awaken their true potential, establish a strong foundation in their values, and work together with others to create a brighter future. The imagery of the "red dawn" serves as a powerful reminder that every day brings new opportunities for renewal and transformation.
