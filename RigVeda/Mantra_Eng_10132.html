न वा उ देवाः क्षुधम् इद वधं ददुर उताशितम् उप गच्छन्ति मृत्यवः |उतो रयिः पृतनतो नोप दस्यत्य उतापृतन मार्डितारं न विन्दते ||
na vā u devāḥ kṣudham id vadhaṁ dadur utāśitam upa gacchanti mṛtyavaḥ | uto rayiḥ pṛṇato nopa dasyaty utāpṛṇan marḍitāraṁ na vindate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na vā: not indeed
    • u: and
    • devāḥ: the gods
    • kṣudham: hunger
    • id: this
    • vadhaṁ: killing
    • dadur: they have given
    • utāśitam: the food
    • upa: towards
    • gacchanti: they go
    • mṛtyavaḥ: those who are mortal
    • uto: and
    • rayiḥ: wealth
    • pṛṇato: of the one who is full
    • nopa: not
    • dasyaty: will be given
    • utāpṛṇan: the one who is not full
    • marḍitāraṁ: the one who is oppressed
    • na: not
    • vindate: finds
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nature of the gods and their relationship with mortals.
    • It emphasizes that the gods do not provide sustenance to those who are hungry through killing or destruction.
    • It suggests that wealth and abundance are not given to those who are not fulfilled or who oppress others.
    • In essence, it highlights the importance of righteousness and fulfillment in receiving blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a manager who supports and nurtures their team is more likely to see productivity and success, as opposed to one who rules through fear and oppression.
    • In personal relationships, those who give love and support receive love in return, while those who act selfishly often find themselves isolated.
    • Communities that work together for the common good often thrive, while those that exploit or harm their members tend to struggle.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the principles of reciprocity and moral integrity.
    • It teaches that true sustenance and wealth come from a place of fulfillment and righteousness, rather than through harmful actions.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to healthier relationships, successful endeavors, and a more harmonious society.
