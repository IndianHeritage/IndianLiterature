मन्त्र: नि शुष्ण इन्द्र धार्णसिं वज्रं जगहन्त दस्यवि | वृषा ह्य उग्र श्रिण्विषे ||
ni śuṣṇa indra dharṇasiṁ vajraṁ jaghantha dasyavi | vṛṣā hy ugra śṛṇviṣe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ni: not
    • śuṣṇa: dry or barren
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • dharṇasiṁ: you hold or support
    • vajraṁ: thunderbolt or weapon
    • jaghantha: you strike or defeat
    • dasyavi: the enemies or the wicked
    • vṛṣā: strong or powerful
    • hy: indeed
    • ugra: fierce or terrible
    • śṛṇviṣe: you hear or listen
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the powerful deity known for his strength and ability to conquer enemies.
    • It emphasizes that Indra does not allow the barren or dry aspects of life to prevail.
    • Indra is depicted as holding a thunderbolt, symbolizing his power to strike down evil and protect the righteous.
    • The mantra acknowledges Indra's fierce nature and his ability to listen to the prayers of his devotees.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal struggle, individuals often seek strength and support from a higher power, much like invoking Indra for assistance.
    • In a community facing challenges, leaders may embody the qualities of Indra by taking decisive action to protect and uplift their people.
    • In environmental contexts, the mantra can symbolize the fight against drought and the need for rain, invoking the divine for sustenance and growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine support in overcoming adversities and challenges in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that strength and resilience can be drawn from faith and the belief in a higher power.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals and communities to seek help and to act with courage in the face of difficulties.
