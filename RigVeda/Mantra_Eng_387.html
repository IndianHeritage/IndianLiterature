वधीर्हि दस्युं धनिनं घनेनमं एकश्चरन्न उपशाकेबHIR इन्द्र |धनोर अधि विषुणक ते व्यायन्न अयज्वानः सनकाः प्रेतिम ईयुः ||
vadhīr hi dasyuṁ dhaninaṁ ghanenam̐ ekaś carann upaśākebhir indra | dhanor adhi viṣuṇak te vy āyann ayajvānaḥ sanakāḥ pretim īyuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vadhīr: to kill or destroy
    • hi: indeed
    • dasyuṁ: the enemy or the wicked
    • dhaninaṁ: the wealthy or the rich
    • ghanenam̐: with strength or power
    • ekaś: alone or single
    • carann: moving or wandering
    • upaśākebhir: with branches or supports
    • indra: the king of gods, representing power and authority
    • dhanor: of wealth
    • adhi: above or over
    • viṣuṇak: the one who is powerful or strong
    • te: your
    • vy: to you
    • āyann: coming or arriving
    • ayajvānaḥ: the one who performs sacrifices
    • sanakāḥ: the wise or learned
    • pretim: death or the dead
    • īyuḥ: they have gone or departed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the destruction of the wicked and the powerful, emphasizing the importance of righteousness and justice.
    • It highlights the role of Indra, the king of gods, who is invoked to protect the righteous and to ensure that the wealthy do not misuse their power.
    • The mantra suggests that even the strongest can be brought down by the collective strength of the righteous, symbolized by the branches that support the tree of justice.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, this mantra can be related to social justice movements where the collective voice of the people rises against corruption and the misuse of power by the wealthy.
    • For instance, movements like Black Lives Matter or anti-corruption protests demonstrate how the collective strength of the community can challenge and dismantle oppressive systems.
    • In personal life, standing up against bullying or injustice in the workplace reflects the essence of this mantra, where individuals unite to confront and overcome the wrongdoings of the powerful.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for justice and the protection of the righteous against the wicked.
    • It serves as a reminder that strength is not just in wealth or power, but in unity and righteousness.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to stand together against injustice, ensuring that the powerful are held accountable for their actions.
