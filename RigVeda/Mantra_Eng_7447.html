कथा नूनं वां विमना उप स्तवद युवं धियं ददथुर वस्यैष्टये |तां वां विश्वको हवते तनूकृतहे मा नो वियौष्टं सख्या मुमोचतम् ||
kathā nūnaṁ vāṁ vimanā upa stavad yuvaṁ dhiyaṁ dadathur vasyaïṣṭaye | tā vāṁ viśvako havate tanūkṛthe mā no vi yauṣṭaṁ sakhyā mumocatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Kathā: How
    • Nūnaṁ: Indeed
    • Vāṁ: You
    • Vimanā: In the mind
    • Upa: Close to
    • Stavad: Praise
    • Yuvaṁ: Young
    • Dhiyaṁ: Intellect
    • Dadathur: They have given
    • Vasyaïṣṭaye: For control
    • Tā: Those
    • Viśvako: All-encompassing
    • Havate: Invoke
    • Tanūkṛthe: In the body
    • Mā: May not
    • Vi: From
    • Yauṣṭaṁ: Strength
    • Sakhyā: Friendship
    • Mumocatam: Release
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects a deep inquiry into the nature of the mind and intellect.
    • It emphasizes the importance of youth and the power of intellect in achieving control over one's life.
    • The invocation of friendship and the plea for release from limitations highlight the significance of relationships and support systems.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, young leaders often rely on their intellect and the support of their peers to navigate challenges.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of nurturing friendships that help us grow and overcome obstacles.
    • In educational environments, students are encouraged to use their intellect and collaborate with others to achieve success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of intellect, youth, and the power of relationships in achieving control over one's life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as personal growth, professional development, and the cultivation of meaningful relationships.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to seek support and wisdom from others while striving for personal mastery and understanding.
