ते त्वा मदा बृहद् इन्द्र स्वधाव इमे पीताः उक्षयन्तं द्युमन्तम् |महाम् अनूनं तवसं विभूतिम् मत्सरासो जर्हृषन्त प्रसाहम् ||
te tvā madā bṛhad indra svadhāva ime pītā ukṣayanta dyumantam | mahām anūnaṁ tavasaṁ vibhūtim matsarāso jarhṛṣanta prasāham ||
  • te tvā madā bṛhad indra svadhāva ime pītā ukṣayanta dyumantam
    • te - "to you" or "for you": This indicates a direction of reverence or offering towards a divine entity.
    • tvā - "you": Referring to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
    • madā - "intoxication" or "delight": This suggests a state of bliss or ecstasy, often associated with divine experiences.
    • bṛhad - "great" or "vast": This emphasizes the grandeur and magnitude of Indra's presence and power.
    • indra - "Indra": The chief deity in the Rigveda, symbolizing strength, rain, and thunder.
    • svadhāva - "self-existent" or "self-sustaining": This highlights the inherent nature of the divine, existing independently.
    • ime - "these": Referring to the offerings or the worshippers present.
    • pītā - "the fathers" or "ancestors": This connects the worshippers to their lineage and the importance of honoring them.
    • ukṣayanta - "nourishing" or "supporting": This indicates the act of providing sustenance or blessings.
    • dyumantam - "radiant" or "brilliant": Describing the divine light or glory of Indra.
  • mahām anūnaṁ tavasaṁ vibhūtim matsarāso jarhṛṣanta prasāham
    • mahām - "great" or "mighty": Again emphasizing the power and significance of the divine.
    • anūnaṁ - "not less" or "without decrease": Suggesting that the divine power is ever-present and unchanging.
    • tavasaṁ - "of your strength": Referring to the might and capabilities of Indra.
    • vibhūtim - "glory" or "wealth": This indicates the abundance and richness of divine blessings.
    • matsarāso - "those who are envious": This refers to negative emotions that can hinder spiritual progress.
    • jarhṛṣanta - "overcoming" or "defeating": This suggests the triumph over obstacles and negativity.
    • prasāham - "strength" or "power": This reinforces the idea of divine support and empowerment.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda is a powerful invocation to Indra, celebrating his greatness and seeking his blessings. It emphasizes the importance of divine support in overcoming challenges and the need to honor one's ancestors. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In times of difficulty, individuals can seek strength and guidance from higher powers, much like how people pray for help during crises.
  • Recognizing the contributions of ancestors and honoring them can foster a sense of belonging and continuity in families.
  • The mantra serves as a reminder to overcome negative emotions like envy, promoting a more harmonious and supportive community.

Overall, this mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine intervention, recognizing one's roots, and striving for personal and communal growth.
