मां त्वा मूरा अविष्यवो मोपहस्वान आ दभन |माकीम ब्रह्मद्विषो वनः ||
mā tvā mūrā aviṣyavo mopahasvāna ā dabhan | mākīm brahmadviṣo vanaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mā: not
    • tvā: you
    • mūrā: foolish, ignorant
    • aviṣyavo: may I not be harmed
    • mopahasvāna: may I not be insulted
    • ā: towards
    • dabhan: to harm, to injure
    • mākīm: what
    • brahmadviṣo: those who hate the divine
    • vanaḥ: forest, wilderness
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for protection against ignorance and harm.
    • The speaker seeks to avoid being insulted or harmed by those who are foolish or who harbor ill will.
    • It reflects a desire for peace and safety in a world that can often be hostile.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, an employee may feel threatened by a colleague who undermines their efforts. This mantra can serve as a reminder to seek protection from negativity and to focus on personal growth.
    • In personal relationships, one might encounter friends or family members who do not support their aspirations. This mantra encourages individuals to distance themselves from such negativity and to seek supportive environments.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for self-protection and the pursuit of a harmonious existence.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios where individuals face challenges from ignorance or hostility.
    • By reciting this mantra, one can cultivate a mindset of resilience and focus on positive interactions, ultimately leading to personal and communal well-being.
