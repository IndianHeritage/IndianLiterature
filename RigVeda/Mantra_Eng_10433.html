शतं जीव शरदो वर्धमानः शतं हेमन्ताञ्छतमु वसन्तान् |शतं इन्द्राग्नि सविता बृहस्पतिः शतायुषा हविषेमं पुनर दुः ||
śataṁ jīva śarado vardhamānaḥ śataṁ hemantāñ chatam u vasantān | śatam indrāgnī savitā bṛhaspatiḥ śatāyuṣā haviṣemam punar duḥ ||
  • śataṁ jīva: This phrase translates to "may we live a hundred years." It emphasizes the desire for a long and prosperous life. In real life, this can be related to the importance of health and wellness practices that contribute to longevity, such as a balanced diet, regular exercise, and mental well-being.
  • śarado vardhamānaḥ: This means "growing in the autumn." Autumn is often associated with harvest and abundance. In a practical sense, this can be seen in the way communities come together to celebrate the harvest season, reflecting gratitude for the fruits of their labor.
  • śataṁ hemantāñ: This translates to "a hundred winters." It signifies endurance through the harshness of winter, symbolizing resilience. In real life, this can be likened to overcoming challenges and adversities, much like how people prepare for and endure difficult times in their lives.
  • chatam u vasantān: This means "a hundred springs." Springs symbolize renewal and new beginnings. In everyday life, this can be related to personal growth and transformation, such as starting a new job, moving to a new place, or embarking on a new relationship.
  • śatam indrāgnī savitā bṛhaspatiḥ: This phrase invokes various deities, including Indra (the god of rain and thunder), Agni (the fire god), Savitā (the sun god), and Bṛhaspati (the god of wisdom). It signifies the importance of divine blessings in achieving success and prosperity. In real life, this can be seen in the way people seek guidance and support from mentors, spiritual leaders, or community figures during significant life events.
  • śatāyuṣā: This translates to "with a hundred years of life." It reiterates the theme of longevity and vitality. In practical terms, this can be connected to the modern emphasis on healthy living and preventive healthcare to ensure a long life.
  • haviṣemam punar duḥ: This means "may we have offerings and be free from sorrow." It emphasizes the importance of abundance and the desire to live a life free from suffering. In real life, this can be reflected in the pursuit of happiness, fulfillment, and the importance of community support in times of need.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic vision of life, emphasizing longevity, resilience, renewal, divine blessings, and the pursuit of happiness. Its significance lies in its call for a balanced life, where one seeks not only personal well-being but also the well-being of the community. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to focus on health, cultivate resilience in the face of challenges, embrace new beginnings, and seek support from others, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
