स त्वं न इन्द्राकवाभिर ऊती सखा विश्वायुर अवितā वृधे भूः |स्वरṣातā यद ध्वयामसि त्वा युध्यन्तो नेमधितā पृत्सु शूर ||
sa tvaṁ na indrākavābhir ūtī sakhā viśvāyur avitā vṛdhe bhūḥ | svarṣātā yad dhvayāmasi tvā yudhyanto nemadhitā pṛtsu śūra ||
  • sa tvaṁ - "You are" - This phrase addresses a divine entity, emphasizing the connection between the speaker and the divine.
  • na indrākavābhir ūtī - "not with the strength of Indra" - This suggests that the speaker does not rely solely on the power of Indra, the king of gods, but seeks a more profound connection.
  • sakhā - "friend" - This term indicates a relationship of camaraderie and support, highlighting the importance of friendship in spiritual and worldly matters.
  • viśvāyur - "universal life" - This word signifies the interconnectedness of all living beings and the essence of life that binds them together.
  • avitā - "protector" - This term refers to the divine as a guardian, emphasizing the need for protection in life’s journey.
  • vṛdhe bhūḥ - "in the growth of the earth" - This phrase connects the divine to the natural world, suggesting that spiritual growth is intertwined with the physical world.
  • svarṣātā - "from the heavens" - This indicates a divine influence or blessing that comes from a higher realm.
  • yad dhvayāmasi - "when we call upon you" - This emphasizes the act of invocation, showing the importance of prayer and connection with the divine.
  • tvā - "you" - Again, this personal address reinforces the relationship between the speaker and the divine.
  • yudhyanto - "fighting" - This term suggests the struggles and challenges faced in life, indicating that life is a battle that requires strength and support.
  • nemadhitā - "not to be defeated" - This phrase conveys the idea of resilience and the importance of overcoming obstacles.
  • pṛtsu - "in the battles" - This reinforces the theme of conflict and the need for divine assistance in overcoming life's challenges.
  • śūra - "brave" - This term highlights the qualities of courage and valor that are essential in facing life's adversities.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of divine connection, friendship, and resilience in the face of life's challenges. It teaches us that while we may face struggles and conflicts, invoking the divine and recognizing our interconnectedness with all life can provide strength and protection. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of difficulty, encouraging individuals to seek support from friends and the divine, fostering a sense of community and shared strength. It serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our battles and that spiritual growth is essential for overcoming life's obstacles.
