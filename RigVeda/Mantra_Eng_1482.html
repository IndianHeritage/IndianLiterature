यासां तिस्रः पञ्चाशतोऽभिव्लङ्गैर अपावपः |तत्सु ते मनायति तकट्सु ते मनायति ||
yāsāṁ tisraḥ pañcāśato 'bhivlaṅgair apāvapaḥ | tat su te manāyati takat su te manāyati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yāsāṁ: of whom
    • tisraḥ: three
    • pañcāśato: fifty
    • 'abhivlaṅgair: with the powerful
    • apāvapaḥ: those who are unrestrained
    • tat: that
    • su: good
    • te: for you
    • manāyati: may it be thought
    • takat: may it be done
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of collective strength and the importance of unity.
    • The mention of "three" and "fifty" can symbolize different groups or aspects of life that come together to create a stronger whole.
    • The phrase "with the powerful" suggests that those who are strong and unrestrained can achieve great things when they work together.
    • The repetition of "may it be thought" and "may it be done" emphasizes the importance of intention and action in achieving goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a team of three individuals may collaborate with fifty others to complete a project. Their combined efforts can lead to innovative solutions and successful outcomes.
    • In community service, a small group of volunteers (three) can inspire a larger community (fifty) to come together for a common cause, demonstrating the power of collective action.
    • In sports, a few key players (three) can lead a team of many (fifty) to victory, showcasing how leadership and unity can drive success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of collaboration and the strength that comes from unity.
    • It teaches us that when individuals come together with a common purpose, they can achieve remarkable results.
    • In real life, this principle can be applied in various scenarios, such as teamwork in professional settings, community initiatives, and even personal relationships.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that collective effort, guided by good intentions, can lead to meaningful accomplishments.
