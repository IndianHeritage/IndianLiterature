ज्योतिर्यज्ञाय रोदसी अनु ष्यादारे स्याम दुर्तस्य भूरेः |भूरी चिद्धि तुजतो मार्त्यस्य सुपारासो वसवो बरहणावत ||
jyotir yajñāya rodasī anu ṣyād āre syāma duritasya bhūreḥ | bhūri cid dhi tujato martyasya supārāso vasavo barhaṇāvat ||
  • jyotir yajñāya: This phrase translates to "for the light of the sacrifice." In this context, "jyotir" refers to light or illumination, while "yajñāya" pertains to sacrifice or offering. It signifies the importance of enlightenment and knowledge that comes from selfless actions.
  • rodasī anu ṣyād: This means "may it shine in the heavens." "Rodasi" refers to the heavens or the sky, and "anu ṣyād" implies a desire for something to manifest or shine forth. This part of the mantra emphasizes the aspiration for divine blessings and guidance from the cosmos.
  • āre syāma duritasya bhūreḥ: This translates to "may we be free from the great difficulties." "Āre" means to be free or liberated, "syāma" refers to darkness or difficulties, and "duritasya bhūreḥ" indicates the vastness of these challenges. This line expresses a plea for liberation from the burdens and troubles of life.
  • bhūri cid dhi tujato: This means "may we attain abundant wisdom." "Bhūri" signifies abundance, "cid" refers to consciousness or awareness, and "dhi" means intellect or wisdom. This part of the mantra highlights the importance of gaining knowledge and understanding in our lives.
  • martyasya supārāso: This translates to "for the well-being of mortals." "Martyasya" refers to mortals or human beings, and "supārāso" means well-being or prosperity. This line emphasizes the need for the welfare and prosperity of humanity.
  • vasavo barhaṇāvat: This means "may the divine forces protect us." "Vasavo" refers to the divine beings or forces, and "barhaṇāvat" implies protection or safeguarding. This part of the mantra calls for the protection and guidance of divine energies in our lives.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a profound message about the pursuit of knowledge, the importance of selfless actions, and the aspiration for divine guidance and protection. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can reflect on the importance of selfless service and the pursuit of wisdom to overcome life's challenges.
  • In community settings, this mantra can inspire collective efforts towards enlightenment and well-being, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
  • In spiritual practices, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek divine guidance and protection in times of difficulty.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for enlightenment, liberation from difficulties, and the pursuit of collective well-being, making it relevant in both personal and communal aspects of life.
