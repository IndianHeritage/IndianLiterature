उर्व्यचां नो महिषः शर्म यं साद अस्मिन हवें पुरुहूतः पुरुक्षुः |स नः प्रजायै हर्यश्व मृळयेंद्र मा नो रीरीषो मा परा दाह् ||
uruvyacā no mahiṣaḥ śarma yaṁsad asmin have puruhūtaḥ purukṣuḥ | sa naḥ prajāyai haryaśva mṛḻayendra mā no rīriṣo mā parā dāḥ ||
  • Uruvyacā: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is vast" or "the one who is expansive." It signifies the boundless nature of the divine or the universe.
  • No mahiṣaḥ: "No" means "our," and "mahiṣaḥ" refers to "the great one" or "the powerful." This phrase can be understood as invoking the great power or energy that surrounds us.
  • Śarma: This word translates to "shelter" or "protection." It emphasizes the need for safety and security in our lives.
  • Yaṁsad: This can be interpreted as "that which is desired" or "that which is sought after." It reflects our aspirations and goals.
  • Asmin: Meaning "in this," it indicates the present moment or the current situation we find ourselves in.
  • Have: This is a call to action, often translated as "we offer" or "we invoke." It signifies a request or a prayer.
  • Puruhūtaḥ: This term refers to "the one who is invoked frequently" or "the one who is called upon." It highlights the importance of divine presence in our lives.
  • Purukṣuḥ: This means "the one who is strong" or "the one who is powerful." It represents strength and resilience.
  • Sa naḥ: "Sa" means "he" or "that," and "naḥ" means "to us." Together, it signifies a connection to the divine or the great power.
  • Prajāyai: This translates to "for progeny" or "for offspring." It emphasizes the importance of family and continuity.
  • Haryaśva: This term can be interpreted as "the one who brings joy" or "the one who is auspicious." It signifies positivity and good fortune.
  • Mṛḻayendra: This can be understood as "the lord of the gentle" or "the one who is kind." It reflects compassion and benevolence.
  • Mā no rīriṣo: "Mā" means "may not," "no" means "us," and "rīriṣo" refers to "destruction" or "harm." This phrase is a plea for protection from negativity.
  • Mā parā dāḥ: "Mā" again means "may not," "parā" means "other," and "dāḥ" refers to "give" or "bestow." It signifies a request to avoid giving us anything harmful or detrimental.

  • Conclusion: This mantra is a profound invocation for protection, strength, and positivity. It emphasizes the importance of divine support in our lives, particularly in the context of family and community. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during times of uncertainty or challenge, such as during a family crisis, a health issue, or when embarking on a new venture. By reciting this mantra, individuals can seek the blessings of strength, protection, and auspiciousness, fostering a sense of security and hope in their lives.
