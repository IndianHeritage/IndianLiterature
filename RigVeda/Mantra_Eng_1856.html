अजा वृता इन्द्र शूरपत्नीः द्यां च येभिः पुरुहूत नूनम् |रक्षो अग्निम अशुषं तूर्वयाणं सिंहो न दमे अपांसी वस्तोः ||
ajā vṛta indra śūrapatnīr dyāṁ ca yebhiḥ puruhūta nūnam | rakṣo agnim aśuṣaṁ tūrvayāṇaṁ siṁho na dame apāṁsi vastoḥ ||
  • ajā - This word translates to "unborn" or "eternal." It signifies the timeless nature of the divine or the ultimate reality.
  • vṛta - Meaning "enveloped" or "surrounded," it suggests the idea of protection or being shielded by a higher power.
  • indra - Refers to the king of gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • śūrapatnīr - This term can be interpreted as "the wife of the hero," indicating the importance of partnership and support in achieving greatness.
  • dyāṁ - Meaning "heaven" or "sky," it represents the spiritual realm or higher consciousness.
  • ca - A conjunction meaning "and," linking ideas together.
  • yebhiḥ - Translates to "by whom," indicating the agency or action taken by divine forces.
  • puruhūta - This term means "many names" or "many forms," suggesting the diversity of existence and the multifaceted nature of the divine.
  • nūnam - Meaning "certainly" or "indeed," it emphasizes certainty and assurance in the context of the mantra.
  • rakṣo - Refers to "protection" or "guardianship," highlighting the need for safeguarding oneself from negative influences.
  • agnim - Meaning "fire," it symbolizes transformation, purification, and energy.
  • aśuṣaṁ - This word can be interpreted as "swift" or "quick," indicating the urgency of action or response.
  • tūrvayāṇaṁ - Refers to "the one who brings forth," suggesting the act of creation or manifestation.
  • siṁho - Meaning "lion," it symbolizes strength, courage, and leadership.
  • na - A negation meaning "not," indicating the absence of something.
  • dame - Meaning "in restraint" or "in control," it emphasizes the importance of discipline.
  • apāṁsi - Refers to "waters," symbolizing emotions, intuition, and the subconscious.
  • vastōḥ - Meaning "of the dwelling" or "of the home," it signifies stability and security.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine protection, strength, and the importance of partnership in the journey of life. It emphasizes the need for safeguarding oneself from negative influences while embracing transformation and courage. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as seeking guidance during challenging times, fostering strong relationships, and maintaining discipline in personal and professional endeavors. By invoking the qualities represented in this mantra, individuals can cultivate resilience and navigate life's complexities with confidence and grace.
