अमी य ऋक्षाः निहितास उच्छा नक्तं ददृश्रे कुहा चिद् दिवेयुः |अदब्धानि वरुणस्य व्रतानि विचाकशच चन्द्रमा नक्तं एति ||
amī ya ṛkṣā nihitāsa uccā naktaṁ dadṛśre kuha cid diveyuḥ | adabdhāni varuṇasya vratāni vicākaśac candramā naktam eti ||
  • amī ya ṛkṣā: This phrase can be translated as "these are the creatures" or "these beings." It refers to the various entities in the universe, highlighting the interconnectedness of all life forms.
  • nihitāsa uccā: This means "placed high" or "elevated." It suggests a sense of importance or reverence for these beings, indicating that they hold a significant place in the cosmic order.
  • naktaṁ dadṛśre: Translated as "they have seen the night," this part emphasizes the experience of darkness or challenges that these beings encounter, which can be likened to the struggles faced in life.
  • kuha cid diveyuḥ: This phrase means "somewhere in the day." It implies that amidst the challenges of life (the night), there is also the promise of light and hope (the day).
  • adabdhāni varuṇasya vratāni: This translates to "the unbroken vows of Varuna." Varuna is the deity associated with cosmic order and moral law. This part signifies the importance of adhering to one's principles and commitments, even in difficult times.
  • vicākaśac: This means "having discernment" or "wisdom." It suggests that one should navigate through life's challenges with clarity and understanding.
  • candramā naktam eti: Translated as "the moon approaches the night," this symbolizes the cyclical nature of life, where darkness (night) is followed by light (day), and the importance of patience and resilience.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of life’s journey, emphasizing the importance of recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings, facing challenges with wisdom, and adhering to one’s principles. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal struggles, ethical dilemmas, and the pursuit of knowledge. It encourages individuals to remain steadfast in their values and to seek understanding in the face of adversity, ultimately leading to growth and enlightenment. The cyclical nature of life reminds us that after every dark phase, there is a promise of light, urging us to remain hopeful and resilient.
