तं इद् वाचेमā विदथेṣu शम्भुवम् मन्त्रं देवā अनहसम् |इमां च वाचं प्रतिहर्यथā नरो विश्वेद् वामā वो अश्नवत् ||
tam id vocemā vidatheṣu śambhuvam mantraṁ devā anehasam | imāṁ ca vācam pratiharyathā naro viśved vāmā vo aśnavat ||
  • tam id vocemā vidatheṣu śambhuvam mantraṁ devā anehasam
    • tam id vocemā: This phrase can be translated as "this voice" or "this sound." It signifies the importance of communication and expression in the spiritual and material realms.
    • vidatheṣu: Refers to "in the assemblies" or "in gatherings." This highlights the significance of community and collective efforts in spiritual practices.
    • śambhuvam: This term relates to "auspicious" or "blessed." It indicates that the mantra is considered sacred and powerful.
    • mantraṁ devā anehasam: This translates to "the mantra of the gods, which is radiant." It emphasizes the divine nature of the mantra and its ability to bring light and clarity to the practitioner.
  • imāṁ ca vācam pratiharyathā naro viśved vāmā vo aśnavat
    • imāṁ ca vācam: This means "this speech" or "this utterance." It suggests the power of words and their impact on reality.
    • pratiharyathā: This can be interpreted as "may you protect" or "may you uphold." It indicates a plea for safeguarding the spoken word and its intentions.
    • naro viśved: Refers to "all men" or "all people." This signifies inclusivity and the universal application of the mantra.
    • vāmā vo aśnavat: This translates to "may you attain your desires." It expresses the hope that through the power of this mantra, individuals can achieve their aspirations and goals.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the significance of communication, community, and the divine nature of words. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • Public Speaking: The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of speech and the importance of delivering messages with clarity and intention.
  • Team Collaboration: In group settings, the mantra highlights the need for collective efforts and the significance of each individual's voice in achieving common goals.
  • Spiritual Practices: The mantra can be used in meditation or prayer, focusing on the divine qualities of words and their ability to manifest desires.

Overall, this RigVeda mantra encourages individuals to recognize the power of their words and the importance of community in spiritual and material pursuits.
