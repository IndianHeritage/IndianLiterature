यद् वो देवाश् चक्रिम जिह्वया गुरु मनसो वा प्रयुती देवहेळनम् |अरावा यो नो अभि दुष्चुनायते तस्मिन् तद् एनो वसवो नि धेतन ||
yad vo devāś cakṛma jihvayā guru manaso vā prayutī devaheḻanam | arāvā yo no abhi ducchunāyate tasmin tad eno vasavo ni dhetana ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • vo: you (plural)
    • devāś: gods
    • cakṛma: we have done
    • jihvayā: with the tongue
    • guru: great or wise
    • manaso: of the mind
    • vā: or
    • prayutī: with intention
    • devaheḻanam: divine offering
    • arāvā: who is not obstructed
    • yo: who
    • no: us
    • abhi: towards
    • ducchunāyate: does not harm
    • tasmin: in that
    • tad: that
    • eno: may it bring
    • vasavo: the divine powers
    • ni: down
    • dhetana: to support or uphold
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the offerings made to the gods through our words and intentions.
    • It emphasizes the importance of purity in speech and thought, suggesting that what we express should be aligned with our higher intentions.
    • The phrase "who does not harm us" indicates a plea for protection from negative influences or energies.
    • The concluding part, "may it bring the divine powers to support us," reflects a desire for divine assistance in our endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied in situations where clear communication is essential, such as in negotiations or conflict resolution. By speaking with intention and clarity, we can foster better understanding and cooperation.
    • In a professional setting, when giving presentations or leading teams, focusing on the purity of our message and the intention behind it can lead to more effective leadership and collaboration.
    • In personal relationships, being mindful of our words and thoughts can help in nurturing trust and respect, thereby preventing misunderstandings and conflicts.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of words and intentions in our lives.
    • It encourages us to align our speech and thoughts with our higher purpose, ensuring that we contribute positively to ourselves and those around us.
    • By invoking divine support, we acknowledge the interconnectedness of our actions and the greater cosmic order, fostering a sense of responsibility and mindfulness in our daily interactions.
