पितुश्च गर्भं जनितुश्च बभ्रे पूर्वीर एको अधयत पीप्याना: |वृष्णे sapatnī शुचये सबन्धू उभे अस्मै मनुष्ये न पाही ||
pituś ca garbhaṁ janituś ca babhre pūrvīr eko adhayat pīpyānāḥ | vṛṣṇe sapatnī śucaye sabandhū ubhe asmai manuṣye3 ni pāhi ||
  • pituś ca garbhaṁ - This phrase translates to "the womb of the father." It signifies the origin or source of life, emphasizing the paternal role in creation.
  • janituś ca babhre - This means "and the one who gives birth." It highlights the maternal aspect, acknowledging the mother's role in nurturing and bringing forth life.
  • pūrvīr eko adhayat - This can be interpreted as "the one who was the first to unite." It suggests the importance of unity and connection, possibly referring to the primordial forces that brought existence into being.
  • pīpyānāḥ - This word means "thirsting" or "desiring." It reflects the innate human longing for knowledge, connection, and fulfillment.
  • vṛṣṇe sapatnī - This phrase translates to "to the one who is worthy." It indicates reverence towards a divine or noble figure, possibly referring to Lord Vishnu or a similar deity.
  • śucaye sabandhū - This means "to the pure and the connected." It emphasizes purity in relationships and the importance of bonds among individuals.
  • ubhe asmai manuṣye - This translates to "both to him and to humanity." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the shared experiences of life.
  • ni pāhi - This means "protect." It is a plea for safeguarding and nurturing, highlighting the need for protection in the journey of life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, the roles of parents, and the importance of unity and protection in life. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine forces that guide and protect us. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in family dynamics, emphasizing the roles of both parents in nurturing children, and in community relationships, where purity and connection among individuals are vital for harmony. It encourages us to seek protection and guidance from higher powers while fostering strong bonds with one another.
