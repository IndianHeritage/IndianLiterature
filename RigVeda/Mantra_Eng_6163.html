अतः समुद्रमुद्वतश्चिकित्वाम् अव पश्यति |यतो विपान एजति ||
ataḥ samudram udvataś cikitvām̐ ava paśyati | yato vipāna ejati ||
  • ataḥ - This word translates to "therefore" or "hence," indicating a conclusion or a result derived from previous statements.
  • samudram - This means "ocean" or "sea," symbolizing vastness, depth, and the collective consciousness of existence.
  • udvataś - This term can be interpreted as "rising" or "elevating," suggesting an upward movement or ascension.
  • cikitvām̐ - This word refers to "awareness" or "knowledge," emphasizing the importance of understanding and perception.
  • ava - This means "down" or "below," indicating a grounding or foundational aspect.
  • paśyati - This translates to "sees" or "perceives," highlighting the act of observation and insight.
  • yato - This word means "from where" or "from which," pointing to a source or origin.
  • vipāna - This can be understood as "the one who moves" or "the one who acts," suggesting dynamism and activity.
  • ejati - This means "shakes" or "vibrates," indicating a forceful movement or energy.

The mantra can be interpreted as follows: "Therefore, the ocean rises with awareness; it sees from where the one who acts vibrates." This suggests a profound connection between the vastness of the ocean and the awareness that comes from understanding one's surroundings and actions.


In real life, this mantra can be applied to various scenarios. For instance, consider a leader in a corporate environment. Just as the ocean represents vast knowledge and depth, a leader must rise above challenges with awareness and insight. They must perceive the dynamics of their team (the "one who acts") and understand how their decisions impact the organization. This awareness allows them to navigate through turbulent waters, much like the ocean's waves, leading their team effectively.


In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the significance of awareness and perception in navigating life's complexities. It teaches us that by elevating our understanding and observing our surroundings, we can effectively respond to the challenges we face. Whether in leadership, personal relationships, or self-awareness, the principles derived from this mantra encourage us to rise with knowledge and act with intention, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
