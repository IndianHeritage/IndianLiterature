विष्वेषाम् इराज्यन्तं वसूनां सासह्वांसं चिदस्य वर्पसः |कृपयतो नूनम् अत्य अथ ||
viśveṣām irajyantaṁ vasūnāṁ sāsahvāṁsaṁ cid asya varpasaḥ | kṛpayato nūnam aty atha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viśveṣām: of all beings
    • irajyantaṁ: who are born or created
    • vasūnāṁ: of the wealth or the good things
    • sāsahvāṁsaṁ: with the essence or the nature
    • cid: consciousness or awareness
    • asya: of this
    • varpasaḥ: of the body or form
    • kṛpayato: with compassion or mercy
    • nūnam: indeed or surely
    • aty: beyond or surpassing
    • atha: then or thus
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of all beings and the essence of wealth and goodness that exists within them.
    • It emphasizes the importance of compassion and awareness in recognizing the divine nature present in every individual.
    • The phrase "with compassion" suggests that true understanding and connection come from a place of empathy and kindness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community service setting, when individuals come together to help those in need, they embody the essence of this mantra by recognizing the shared humanity and the wealth of compassion that exists within each person.
    • In a workplace, fostering a culture of empathy and support can lead to a more harmonious environment, where everyone feels valued and connected, reflecting the principles of this mantra.
    • In personal relationships, practicing compassion and understanding can strengthen bonds and create a deeper awareness of each other's experiences and emotions.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for compassion and awareness of the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It serves as a reminder to cultivate empathy in our daily lives, whether in personal relationships, community interactions, or professional environments.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can contribute to a more compassionate and understanding world, recognizing the divine essence in everyone we encounter.
