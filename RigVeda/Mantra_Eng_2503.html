सद्योजातोऽषधिभिरववक्षे यदी वर्धन्ति प्रस्वो घृतेन |आप इव प्रवता शुम्भमान उरुष्यद अग्निः पितरोर उपस्थे ||
sadyo jāta oṣadhībhir vavakṣe yadī vardhanti prasvo ghṛtena | āpa iva pravatā śumbhamānā uruṣyad agniḥ pitror upasthe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sadyo: immediately, at once
    • jāta: born, created
    • oṣadhībhir: by the herbs, plants
    • vavakṣe: may you nourish, may you support
    • yadī: if
    • vardhanti: they grow, they increase
    • prasvo: with ghee, clarified butter
    • ghṛtena: with ghee
    • āpa: like water
    • iva: as, like
    • pravatā: flowing, moving
    • śumbhamānā: being praised, being honored
    • uruṣyad: great, vast
    • agniḥ: fire
    • pitror: of the ancestors, of the fathers
    • upasthe: in the presence, in the lap
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the immediate birth and nourishment provided by herbs and plants.
    • It emphasizes the importance of ghee (clarified butter) in enhancing the growth and vitality of these plants.
    • The comparison of the nourishing qualities of plants to the flowing nature of water signifies abundance and sustenance.
    • It also highlights the reverence for fire (Agni) and its connection to the ancestors, suggesting a spiritual and ancestral blessing in the process of growth and nourishment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the use of organic fertilizers, such as ghee or compost, can significantly enhance the growth of crops, similar to the mantra's emphasis on nourishment.
    • In traditional Indian cooking, ghee is often used to prepare dishes that are not only flavorful but also considered to have health benefits, reflecting the mantra's idea of nourishment.
    • The practice of honoring ancestors during festivals, such as Pitru Paksha, connects to the reverence for Agni and the blessings of the past generations, as mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of nature, nourishment, and spirituality.
    • It teaches us the importance of respecting and utilizing natural resources wisely for our sustenance.
    • In real life, it encourages us to honor our traditions and ancestors while fostering growth and abundance in our lives.
    • Overall, it serves as a reminder of the balance between material and spiritual nourishment, urging us to cultivate both in our daily lives.
