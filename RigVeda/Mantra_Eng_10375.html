इमं यज्ञं इदं वचो जुजुषाण उपागहि |मर्तासस त्वा समिधाना हवामहे मृळीकाय हवामहे ||
imaṁ yajñam idaṁ vaco jujuṣāṇa upāgahi | martāsas tvā samidhāna havāmahe mṛḻīkāya havāmahe ||
  • imaṁ yajñam: This phrase translates to "this sacrifice" or "this offering." In the context of the Rigveda, yajna refers to a ritual of offerings made to the deities, symbolizing the connection between humans and the divine.
  • idaṁ vaco: This means "this speech" or "this word." It emphasizes the importance of words and communication in rituals and life, suggesting that the spoken word has power and significance.
  • jujuṣāṇa upāgahi: This can be interpreted as "may you accept" or "may you be pleased." It is an invocation for the deities to accept the offerings and the intentions behind them, highlighting the relationship between the worshipper and the divine.
  • martāsas: This term refers to "mortals" or "human beings." It acknowledges the human condition and the need for divine support and guidance in life.
  • tvā samidhāna: This translates to "we kindle you" or "we offer to you." It signifies the act of making offerings, which is central to many spiritual practices, symbolizing devotion and respect towards the divine.
  • havāmahe: This means "we invoke" or "we call upon." It is a declaration of intent to engage with the divine, reinforcing the idea of active participation in spiritual practices.
  • mṛḻīkāya: This term can be interpreted as "to the one who is worthy" or "to the one who is deserving." It emphasizes the qualities of the divine that are being honored through the offerings.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of ritualistic offerings and the importance of communication with the divine. It signifies the human desire to connect with higher powers through acts of devotion and the spoken word. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various forms of worship, ceremonies, and even in daily life where individuals seek to express gratitude, seek blessings, or find guidance. The significance lies in the acknowledgment of the divine presence in everyday actions and the importance of intention behind our words and deeds. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of maintaining a respectful and humble approach towards the divine and the universe.
