त्वेषासो अग्नेर अमवान्तो अर्चयो भीमासो न प्रतीतये |रक्षस्विनः सदमिद् यातुमावतो विश्वं समात्रिणं दह ||
tveṣāso agner amavanto arcayo bhīmāso na pratītaye | rakṣasvinaḥ sadam id yātumāvato viśvaṁ sam atriṇaṁ daha ||
  • tveṣāso: This word can be interpreted as "those who are powerful" or "those who have strength." It signifies the might and energy that one possesses.
  • agner: This translates to "of the fire." Fire is often seen as a symbol of transformation, energy, and purification in various cultures.
  • amavanto: This means "immortal" or "eternal." It suggests a connection to the divine or the everlasting nature of certain truths.
  • arcayo: This word refers to "worship" or "adoration." It emphasizes the importance of reverence and devotion in spiritual practices.
  • bhīmāso: This can be understood as "those who are formidable" or "terrifying." It highlights the awe-inspiring aspects of nature and the divine.
  • na pratītaye: This phrase means "not for the sake of recognition" or "not for the purpose of being seen." It suggests that true devotion is selfless and not motivated by external validation.
  • rakṣasvinaḥ: This translates to "of the protectors" or "those who safeguard." It indicates the presence of guardianship and protection in the spiritual realm.
  • sadam: This means "to the dwelling" or "to the abode." It refers to a place of safety or sanctuary.
  • id: This word means "this" or "here." It emphasizes the immediacy and presence of the divine in our lives.
  • yātumāvato: This can be interpreted as "to go forth" or "to move forward." It signifies progress and the journey of life.
  • viśvaṁ: This translates to "the universe" or "all that exists." It encompasses the entirety of creation and existence.
  • sam atriṇaṁ: This means "together with the protectors" or "in harmony with the guardians." It emphasizes the importance of community and collective strength.
  • daha: This translates to "burn" or "consume." It signifies the transformative power of fire, which can purify and renew.

Conclusion: The mantra speaks to the themes of strength, protection, and the divine presence in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of selfless devotion and the transformative power of fire, both literally and metaphorically. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to situations where individuals seek strength and protection, whether in personal challenges, community efforts, or spiritual journeys. It reminds us that true power lies not in recognition but in the collective strength of those who protect and uplift one another. The significance of this mantra is profound, as it encourages us to embrace our inner strength, seek protection from the divine, and move forward in harmony with the universe.
