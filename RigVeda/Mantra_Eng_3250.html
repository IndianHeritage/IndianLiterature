क्षियन्तं त्वम् अक्षियन्तं कृणोतीयर्ति रेणुम् मगवाऽसमोहम् |विभञ्जनुर अशिनिमां इव द्यौर उता स्तोतारम् मगवाऽवसौ धात् ||
kṣiyantaṁ tvam akṣiyantaṁ kṛṇotīyarti reṇum maghavā samoham | vibhañjanur aśanimām̐ iva dyaur uta stotāram maghavā vasau dhāt ||
  • kṣiyantaṁ tvam akṣiyantaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "You who are diminishing, yet you do not diminish." It suggests a paradox of existence where something may appear to be fading or reducing, yet its essence remains intact. In real life, this can be likened to the aging process; while our physical bodies may decline, our wisdom and experiences often grow richer.
  • kṛṇotīyarti reṇum: This translates to "You create the essence of the earth." It emphasizes the role of a divine force in shaping the material world. For example, consider how nature creates fertile soil from decaying matter, illustrating the cycle of life and death that sustains ecosystems.
  • maghavā samoham: Here, "maghavā" refers to a powerful deity or force, and "samoham" means "togetherness" or "unity." This can be seen in community efforts where individuals come together to achieve a common goal, such as environmental conservation projects that unite people for a sustainable future.
  • vibhañjanur aśanimām̐ iva dyaur: This phrase can be interpreted as "like the sky that splits with lightning." It symbolizes sudden illumination or revelation. In real life, moments of insight can strike unexpectedly, much like a flash of lightning, leading to breakthroughs in understanding or creativity.
  • uta stotāram maghavā vasau dhāt: This translates to "and the powerful one, the giver of wealth." It highlights the idea of abundance and the blessings that come from divine sources. In practical terms, this can relate to the prosperity that comes from hard work and the support of a community, as well as the importance of gratitude for what we receive.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of paradox, creation, unity, revelation, and abundance. Its significance lies in the understanding that while life may present challenges and changes, there is a divine force that sustains and enriches our existence. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to embrace the cycles of life, work collaboratively for common goals, seek moments of insight, and remain grateful for the abundance in their lives. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all things and the divine presence that guides us through our journeys.
