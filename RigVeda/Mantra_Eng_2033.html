स नो बोधि सहस्य प्रशंस्यो यस्मिन सुजाताः इषयन्त सूरयः |यम् अग्निः यज्ञं उपयन्ति वाजिनो नित्ये तोके दीदिवांसं स्वे दामे ||
sa no bodhi sahasya praśaṁsyo yasmin sujātā iṣayanta sūrayaḥ | yam agne yajñam upayanti vājino nitye toke dīdivāṁsaṁ sve dame ||
  • sa no bodhi: This phrase translates to "may we attain wisdom." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in our lives. For example, in a real-life scenario, pursuing education and lifelong learning can lead to personal and professional growth.
  • sahasya praśaṁsyo: This means "may we be praised for our strength." It highlights the value of courage and resilience. In everyday life, individuals who face challenges with determination, such as athletes training for competitions or entrepreneurs overcoming obstacles, embody this strength.
  • yasmin sujātā iṣayanta sūrayaḥ: This translates to "in whom the noble ones are empowered." It refers to the idea that those who are virtuous and noble can inspire and lead others. For instance, community leaders or mentors who guide and uplift others exemplify this principle.
  • yam agne yajñam upayanti: This means "whom the fire (Agni) leads to the sacrifice." Agni symbolizes transformation and purification. In real life, this can be seen in the process of personal development, where individuals undergo challenges (sacrifices) to achieve their goals, much like a student sacrificing leisure time for studies.
  • vājino nitye toke dīdivāṁsaṁ sve dame: This translates to "the one who brings forth abundance in our own home." It signifies the importance of prosperity and well-being in our personal lives. For example, cultivating a positive environment at home through love, respect, and support can lead to a flourishing family life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the pursuit of wisdom, strength, and virtue, while also highlighting the importance of personal sacrifice for growth and the creation of abundance in our lives. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, such as education, personal development, community leadership, and family well-being. By embodying these principles, individuals can lead fulfilling lives and contribute positively to society.
