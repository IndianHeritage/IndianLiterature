स त्वं नो रायः शिशीहि मीढ्वो अग्ने सुवीर्यस्य |तुविद्युम्न वर्षिष्ठस्य प्रजावतोऽनमीवस्य शुष्मिणः ||
sa tvaṁ no rāyaḥ śiśīhi mīḍhvo agne suvīryasya | tuvidyumna varṣiṣṭhasya prajāvato 'namīvasya śuṣmiṇaḥ ||
  • sa tvaṁ no rāyaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "May you, O Agni, bestow upon us prosperity." Here, "Agni" refers to the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine. In real life, this can be likened to seeking blessings for success in endeavors, much like how people pray for good fortune before starting a new business or project.
  • śiśīhi: This word means "nourish" or "sustain." It emphasizes the need for continuous support and growth. For example, in a family setting, parents nourish their children not just physically but also emotionally and intellectually, ensuring their overall development.
  • mīḍhvo agne: "Mīḍhvo" refers to "the one who is worthy of praise." This highlights the reverence for Agni, acknowledging his importance in rituals and daily life. In modern contexts, this can be compared to recognizing and appreciating mentors or leaders who guide us in our personal and professional lives.
  • suvīryasya: This term translates to "of great strength." It signifies the power and vigor that one seeks in life. In practical terms, this can relate to physical strength, mental resilience, or even the strength of character that individuals strive to embody in challenging situations.
  • tuvidyumna: This word means "one who is knowledgeable." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and learning. In today's world, this can be seen in the value placed on education and continuous self-improvement, where individuals seek knowledge to navigate life's complexities.
  • varṣiṣṭhasya: This translates to "the most excellent." It suggests striving for the highest standards in all endeavors. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of excellence in various fields, such as sports, arts, or academics, where individuals aim to be the best versions of themselves.
  • prajāvato: This means "of the one who creates life." It signifies the importance of creation and procreation, reflecting the value of family and community. In contemporary society, this can be linked to the significance of nurturing relationships and contributing to the growth of society through family and community involvement.
  • 'namīvasya: This term refers to "the one who is humble." It highlights the virtue of humility, which is essential in personal and professional relationships. In real life, humility can be seen in leaders who listen to their teams and value input from others, fostering a collaborative environment.
  • śuṣmiṇaḥ: This word means "the one who is radiant." It symbolizes the brightness and positivity that one should embody. In everyday life, this can be reflected in individuals who spread positivity and inspire others through their actions and words.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a prayer for prosperity, nourishment, strength, knowledge, excellence, creation, humility, and radiance. Its significance lies in the holistic approach to life, encouraging individuals to seek balance in material and spiritual pursuits. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, family values, community involvement, and professional excellence. By embodying the qualities represented in this mantra, individuals can lead fulfilling lives and contribute positively to society.
