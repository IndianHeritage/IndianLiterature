मन्त्र: शूरो न धत्त आयुधा गभस्त्योः स्वः सिषासन रथिरो गविष्टिषु | इन्द्रस्य शुष्मं ईरयन्न अपस्युभिर इन्दुर हिन्वानो अज्यते मनिषिभिः ||
śūro na dhatta āyudhā gabhastyoḥ sva1ḥ siṣāsan rathiro gaviṣṭiṣu | indrasya śuṣmam īrayann apasyubhir indur hinvāno ajyate manīṣibhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • śūro: Brave or heroic
    • na: Not
    • dhatta: He holds or carries
    • āyudhā: Weapons
    • gabhastyoḥ: Of the shining ones
    • svaḥ: His own
    • siṣāsan: He who is seated
    • rathiro: In the chariot
    • gaviṣṭiṣu: In the cows or in the wealth
    • indrasya: Of Indra (the king of gods)
    • śuṣmam: The dry or the barren
    • īrayann: Making to flow or to move
    • apasyubhir: With the seers or wise ones
    • indur: The moon
    • hinvāno: Driving away
    • ajyate: Is invoked or is called upon
    • manīṣibhiḥ: By the wise or the intelligent
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a brave individual who does not carry weapons but is seated in a chariot, symbolizing strength and readiness.
    • The reference to "gabhastyoḥ" suggests a connection to the divine or the shining ones, indicating that this bravery is not just physical but also spiritual.
    • Indra, the king of gods, is invoked here, emphasizing the importance of divine support in overcoming challenges.
    • The mention of the moon signifies illumination and guidance, suggesting that wisdom and insight are crucial in navigating life's difficulties.
    • The mantra highlights the role of wise individuals in invoking strength and support from the divine, illustrating the importance of community and shared wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader may not always wield authority (weapons) but can guide their team (chariot) with wisdom and vision, much like the brave individual in the mantra.
    • In personal challenges, individuals often rely on their inner strength and the support of friends (the wise) to overcome obstacles, akin to invoking divine assistance.
    • In community service, volunteers may not have physical power but can bring about significant change through collective wisdom and action, reflecting the essence of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of inner strength, wisdom, and divine support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It teaches that true bravery does not always manifest in physical might but in the ability to lead, inspire, and invoke support from the community and the divine.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, from leadership roles to personal struggles, emphasizing the need for wisdom and collective strength in achieving success.
