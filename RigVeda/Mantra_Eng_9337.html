तदाद्य वाचः प्रथमं मासीय येनासुराम् अभि देवाः असाम |ऊर्जादुत यज्ञियासः पञ्च जनाः मम होत्रं जुषध्वम् ||
tad adya vācaḥ prathamam masīya yenāsurām̐ abhi devā asāma | ūrjāda uta yajñiyāsaḥ pañca janā mama hotraṁ juṣadhvam ||
  • tad adya vācaḥ prathamam masīya: This phrase translates to "Today, I speak the first words of the month." It signifies the importance of communication and the initiation of new beginnings. In real life, this can be likened to starting a new project or a new phase in life, where the first step is crucial.
  • yenāsurām̐ abhi devā asāma: This means "by which the divine beings are invoked." It emphasizes the role of divine support in human endeavors. For example, when people pray or seek blessings before starting a significant task, they are invoking divine assistance, much like athletes who pray before a competition.
  • ūrjāda uta yajñiyāsaḥ: This translates to "with energy and through sacrifice." It highlights the importance of effort and dedication in achieving goals. In everyday life, this can be seen in the context of hard work leading to success, such as students studying diligently for exams or professionals putting in extra hours to meet deadlines.
  • pañca janā mama hotraṁ juṣadhvam: This means "may the five people (or elements) enjoy my offerings." It suggests a sense of community and sharing. In real life, this can be related to teamwork and collaboration, where the success of one is shared among many, such as in a family or a workplace where collective efforts lead to achievements.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of communication, divine invocation, hard work, and community. Its significance lies in the reminder that every new beginning requires intention and effort, and that success is often a collective endeavor. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as starting a new business, embarking on a personal journey, or engaging in community service, where the principles of dedication, divine support, and collaboration are essential for success.
