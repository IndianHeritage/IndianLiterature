यो नो मरुतो वृष्कताति मर्त्यो रिपुर दधे वसवो रक्षता ऋषः |वर्तयत तपुषा चक्रियाभि तम अव रुद्राऽशसो हन्तनां वधः ||
yo no maruto vṛkatāti martyo ripur dadhe vasavo rakṣatā riṣaḥ | vartayata tapuṣā cakriyābhi tam ava rudrā aśaso hantanā vadhaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • no: our
    • maruto: of the Maruts (storm deities)
    • vṛkatāti: who is fierce
    • martyo: mortal
    • ripur: enemy
    • dadhe: places
    • vasavo: the Vasus (deities of abundance)
    • rakṣatā: protect
    • riṣaḥ: from harm
    • vartayata: may they maintain
    • tapuṣā: through their power
    • cakriyābhi: with their actions
    • tam: that
    • ava: down
    • rudrā: O Rudra (the fierce god)
    • aśaso: of the world
    • hantanā: of destruction
    • vadhaḥ: death
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the fierce Maruts and the benevolent Vasus, asking for protection against enemies and harm.
    • The mention of Rudra signifies a plea for strength and resilience in the face of adversity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine support in overcoming challenges and maintaining peace.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of conflict, individuals or communities may recite this mantra to seek protection and strength from higher powers.
    • During natural disasters, people often turn to spiritual practices for solace and safety, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal struggles, such as health issues or financial difficulties, individuals may find comfort in the belief that divine forces are protecting them.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine intervention in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of human struggles and the support available from the divine.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to maintain faith and resilience, knowing that they are not alone in their battles.
