हरि नु त इन्द्र वाजयन्ता घृतचुतं स्वारम अस्वार्ष्टाम् |वि समना भूमिर अप्रथिष्ठारंस्त पर्वतश्चित सरीष्यन् ||
harī nu ta indra vājayantā ghṛtaścutaṁ svāram asvārṣṭām | vi samanā bhūmir aprathiṣṭāraṁsta parvataś cit sariṣyan ||
  • harī nu ta indra vājayantā: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to Indra, the king of the gods, to bestow his blessings. "Harī" refers to the divine, while "Indra" signifies strength and power. In real life, this can be likened to seeking guidance and support from a higher power or authority when facing challenges.
  • ghṛtaścutaṁ svāram asvārṣṭām: "Ghṛta" means clarified butter, often used in rituals, symbolizing purity and nourishment. "Svāram" refers to the essence or spirit. This part of the mantra emphasizes the importance of purity and spiritual nourishment in our lives. For example, in a modern context, this could relate to the need for mental and emotional well-being through practices like meditation or healthy living.
  • vi samanā bhūmir aprathiṣṭāraṁ: "Vi samanā" suggests harmony and balance, while "bhūmir" refers to the earth or foundation. This line speaks to the need for stability and balance in our lives. In practical terms, this can be seen in the importance of maintaining a work-life balance or ensuring that our relationships are healthy and supportive.
  • sta parvataś cit sariṣyan: "Parvata" means mountain, symbolizing strength and stability. "Sariṣyan" can be interpreted as the essence of life or vitality. This part of the mantra encourages us to seek strength and resilience in our endeavors. For instance, when facing obstacles, one might draw inspiration from nature, such as mountains, which stand tall and unwavering against the elements.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a call for divine support, the importance of purity and nourishment, the need for balance and stability, and the pursuit of strength and resilience. In real life, these themes are applicable in various scenarios, such as personal growth, professional challenges, and maintaining healthy relationships. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can navigate life's complexities with grace and strength, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
