अग्नि जुषस्व नो हविः पुरोळाशं जातवेदः |प्रातःसावे धियावसो ||
agne juṣasva no haviḥ puroḻāśaṁ jātavedaḥ | prātaḥsāve dhiyāvaso ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying and transformative force.
  • Juṣasva: This means "may you enjoy" or "may you be pleased." It indicates a desire for the divine presence to be favorable and beneficial.
  • No: This translates to "our," indicating a personal connection and a plea for the divine to be involved in human affairs.
  • Haviḥ: This refers to offerings or sacrifices made to the gods, symbolizing devotion and the act of giving.
  • Puroḻāśaṁ: This term can be understood as "the one who is the best among the offerings" or "the most excellent." It emphasizes the quality of the offerings made to Agni.
  • Jātavedaḥ: This means "the one who knows all births" or "the all-knowing." It signifies Agni's role as a mediator between the divine and the earthly realms.
  • Prātaḥsāve: This translates to "in the morning," indicating a time of renewal and new beginnings.
  • Dhiyāvaso: This means "may you inspire our intellect" or "may you grant us wisdom." It reflects a desire for divine guidance in thought and action.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer to Agni, asking for his blessings and guidance. It expresses a wish for the divine to be pleased with our offerings and to inspire our intellect, especially in the morning, a time associated with new beginnings and clarity of thought.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Morning Rituals: Many people start their day with rituals or prayers, seeking clarity and inspiration for the day ahead. This mantra can be recited to invoke positive energy and guidance.
  • Decision Making: When faced with important decisions, individuals can reflect on this mantra, asking for wisdom and insight to make the right choices.
  • Offerings and Gratitude: The act of giving, whether through charity or simple acts of kindness, can be seen as a modern-day offering. This mantra reminds us to approach such acts with sincerity and a desire for the greater good.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance and the importance of offering our best to the universe. It teaches us to seek wisdom and clarity, especially at the start of new endeavors, and to approach life with a spirit of gratitude and devotion. By incorporating these principles into our daily lives, we can foster a deeper connection with the divine and enhance our personal growth.
