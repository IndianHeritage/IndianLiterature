इन्द्र स्थातार हरिणां नकीष्टे पूर्व्यस्तुतिम् |उदानंष शवासां न बन्धनाः ||
indra sthātar harīṇāṁ nakiṣ ṭe pūrvyastutim | ud ānaṁśa śavasā na bhandanā ||
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • Sthātar: This term refers to the one who supports or upholds. It signifies stability and the ability to provide strength to others.
  • Harīṇāṁ: This word means 'of the horses' or 'of the swift ones.' In the Vedic context, horses symbolize speed, agility, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • Nakiṣ ṭe: This phrase can be interpreted as 'to you' or 'for you,' indicating a sense of devotion or offering to the divine.
  • Pūrvyastutim: This term means 'the praise of the ancients' or 'the praise that comes from the past.' It emphasizes the importance of tradition and the wisdom of previous generations.
  • Ud ānaṁśa: This can be understood as 'the one who rises up' or 'the one who elevates.' It signifies progress, ascension, and the pursuit of higher goals.
  • Śavasā: This word refers to 'the dead' or 'the lifeless.' In a broader sense, it can symbolize challenges or obstacles that one must overcome.
  • Na bhandanā: This phrase means 'not bound' or 'not restricted.' It conveys a sense of freedom and liberation from limitations.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to Indra, the powerful deity, to support and uplift those who are swift and agile, while also acknowledging the wisdom of the past. It emphasizes the importance of overcoming obstacles and achieving freedom from limitations.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as Indra is called upon for strength, individuals can seek inner strength and support from their experiences and the wisdom of their ancestors to overcome personal challenges.
  • Teamwork: In a team setting, the mantra highlights the importance of supporting one another, much like Indra supports the swift horses, to achieve common goals.
  • Overcoming Obstacles: The reference to rising above challenges can inspire individuals to face difficulties head-on, seeking liberation from fears and limitations.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of support, the importance of tradition, and the potential for personal and collective growth. It encourages individuals to seek strength from their heritage while striving for progress and freedom in their lives.
