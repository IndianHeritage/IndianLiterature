अग्निम् अस्तोष्य ऋग्मियम् अग्निम् ईळा यजधैयै |अग्निः देवाम् अनक्तु न उभे हि विदथे कविः अन्तश् चरति दूत्यं नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
agnim astoṣy ṛgmiyam agnim īḻā yajadhyai | agnir devām̐ anaktu na ubhe hi vidathe kavir antaś carati dūtya1ṁ nabhantām anyake same ||
  • Agnim: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purity.
  • astoṣy: This term means "to be praised" or "to be worshipped," indicating the reverence towards Agni.
  • ṛgmiyam: This word relates to "the hymns" or "the verses," suggesting the importance of sacred chants in invoking Agni.
  • īḻā: This term can be interpreted as "the essence" or "the spirit," emphasizing the spiritual aspect of fire.
  • yajadhyai: This means "for the sacrifice," highlighting the role of Agni in Vedic rituals and offerings.
  • devām̐: This word translates to "the divine," reinforcing the sacred nature of Agni.
  • anaktu: This means "to be invoked" or "to be called upon," indicating the active role of Agni in rituals.
  • na ubhe: This phrase means "not both," suggesting a distinction or choice in the context of offerings.
  • vidathe: This term refers to "the assembly" or "the gathering," often related to communal rituals.
  • kavir: This word means "the wise" or "the poet," indicating the importance of knowledge and wisdom in understanding Agni.
  • antaś: This means "within" or "inside," suggesting an inner connection to the divine.
  • carati: This term means "to move" or "to travel," indicating the dynamic nature of Agni.
  • dūtyaṁ: This word translates to "the messenger," symbolizing Agni's role in communication between the divine and humans.
  • nabhantām: This term refers to "the heavens" or "the sky," indicating the celestial aspect of Agni.
  • anyake: This means "other" or "different," suggesting the diversity of offerings and paths to the divine.
  • same: This word means "in harmony" or "together," emphasizing unity in worship.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the significance of Agni as a central figure in Vedic rituals, representing transformation, purity, and divine communication. In real-life scenarios, the essence of this mantra can be applied in various ways, such as:

  • In personal rituals, invoking Agni can symbolize the purification of one's intentions and actions.
  • In communal gatherings, the mantra can serve as a reminder of unity and collective purpose in spiritual practices.
  • In modern contexts, the principles of transformation and energy represented by Agni can inspire individuals to embrace change and seek enlightenment in their lives.

Overall, the mantra serves as a profound reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine, nature, and human experience, encouraging individuals to engage in practices that honor this relationship.
