आयं वाम् परि शिच्यते सोम इन्द्राबृहस्पति |चारुर मदाय पीतये ||
ayaṁ vām pari ṣicyate soma indrābṛhaspatī | cārur madāya pītaye ||
  • ayaṁ - This word translates to "this" or "here." It signifies the presence of something significant in the moment.
  • vām - Meaning "your" or "of you," it indicates a connection or relationship with the divine or the audience.
  • pari - This means "around" or "surrounding," suggesting a sense of encompassing or enveloping.
  • ṣicyate - Translated as "is poured" or "is sprinkled," it refers to the act of offering or sharing something sacred.
  • soma - Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes the essence of life and vitality.
  • indrābṛhaspatī - This compound word refers to Indra, the king of gods, and Brihaspati, the god of wisdom and eloquence. Together, they represent strength, knowledge, and divine guidance.
  • cārur - Meaning "beautiful" or "charming," it denotes something that is pleasing and delightful.
  • madāya - This translates to "for intoxication" or "for joy," indicating a state of bliss or ecstasy that comes from divine blessings.
  • pītaye - Meaning "to drink" or "for drinking," it emphasizes the act of consuming or partaking in something sacred or joyful.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine forces, inviting them to bless the offerings made with the sacred Soma. It emphasizes the importance of connection with the divine, the beauty of life, and the joy that comes from divine blessings. The invocation of Indra and Brihaspati highlights the need for strength and wisdom in our lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Rituals and Offerings: During religious ceremonies, this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings and divine presence, enhancing the spiritual atmosphere.
  • Personal Reflection: Individuals can use this mantra for meditation, focusing on the beauty and joy in their lives, and seeking guidance from higher powers.
  • Community Gatherings: In communal events, reciting this mantra can foster a sense of unity and shared joy, celebrating the collective strength and wisdom of the group.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of divine blessings, and the joy that comes from embracing beauty and wisdom. It encourages individuals to seek strength and guidance from the divine, making it relevant in both personal and communal aspects of life.
