माध्यंदिनि सवने जातवेदः पुरोळाशम् इह कवे जुषस्व |अग्ने यह्वस्य तव भागधेयं न प्र मिनन्ति विदथेṣu धीराः ||
mādhyaṁdine savane jātavedaḥ puroḻāśam iha kave juṣasva | agne yahvasya tava bhāgadheyaṁ na pra minanti vidatheṣu dhīrāḥ ||
  • mādhyaṁdine: This word refers to the midday or the time around noon. In many cultures, noon is considered a time of peak energy and activity.
  • savane: This term is associated with the act of invoking or calling upon divine forces. It signifies a moment of connection with the spiritual realm.
  • jātavedaḥ: This is a name for Agni, the fire god, who represents knowledge, transformation, and the divine spark within all beings. Agni is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
  • puroḻāśam: This word can be interpreted as the one who is the best among the offerings or the one who is worthy of worship. It emphasizes the importance of quality in offerings made to the divine.
  • iha: Meaning "here" or "in this place," it indicates the immediacy of the invocation and the presence of the divine in the current moment.
  • kave: This term refers to the wise or the seer, someone who possesses deep insight and understanding. It highlights the importance of wisdom in spiritual practices.
  • juṣasva: This is an invitation or a plea for the divine to accept the offerings and blessings being presented. It reflects a desire for connection and favor from the divine.
  • agne: Again referring to Agni, it emphasizes the central role of fire and transformation in spiritual practices.
  • yahvasya: This term can be understood as "of your greatness" or "of your power," acknowledging the vastness and strength of the divine.
  • tava: Meaning "your," it personalizes the relationship between the devotee and the divine.
  • bhāgadheyaṁ: This refers to the share or portion of blessings that one receives. It signifies the importance of receiving divine grace in one's life.
  • na pra minanti: This phrase means "do not diminish" or "do not reduce," indicating a plea for the divine blessings to remain abundant and unyielding.
  • vidatheṣu: This term refers to gatherings or assemblies, often in a spiritual or communal context, highlighting the importance of community in spiritual practices.
  • dhīrāḥ: This word means "the wise" or "the courageous," emphasizing the qualities of strength and wisdom that are essential in navigating life's challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep invocation to Agni, the fire god, asking for blessings, wisdom, and strength during the midday, a time of heightened energy. It signifies the importance of quality offerings and the connection between the devotee and the divine. In real life, this mantra can be applied during rituals, prayers, or moments of reflection, especially when seeking guidance or strength in challenging situations. It serves as a reminder to remain wise and courageous, fostering a sense of community and shared spiritual experience.
