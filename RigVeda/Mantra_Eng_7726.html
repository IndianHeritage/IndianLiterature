तव क्रत्वा तवोतिभिर ज्योक पश्येम सूर्यं |अथा नो वस्यसस कृधि ||
tava kratvā tavotibhir jyok paśyema sūryam | athā no vasyasas kṛdhi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Tava: Your
    • kratvā: Power or strength
    • tavotibhir: With your great powers
    • jyok: May we see
    • paśyema: Let us behold
    • sūryam: The sun
    • athā: Therefore
    • no: Our
    • vasyasas: Of the auspicious
    • kṛdhi: Do or create
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra expresses a prayer to the divine, asking for the ability to perceive the sun's brilliance through the power of the divine. It emphasizes the importance of divine strength in achieving clarity and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom. Just as the sun illuminates the world, knowledge enlightens our minds. For instance, students often seek guidance from teachers (representing divine power) to gain clarity in their studies.
    • In a broader sense, this mantra can be applied to personal growth. Individuals may seek inspiration from mentors or spiritual leaders to help them "see" their path clearly, much like the sun guiding travelers.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine guidance in our lives. It reminds us that with the right support and strength, we can achieve clarity and understanding.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various scenarios such as decision-making, where one seeks wisdom and insight before taking action. For example, before making a significant life choice, individuals may reflect on their values and seek advice from trusted sources.
