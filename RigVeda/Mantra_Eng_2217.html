इन्द्र श्रेष्ठानि द्रविणानि धेहि चित्तिं दक्षस्य सुभगत्वम् अस्मे |पोषं रयीणाम् अरिष्टिं तनूनां स्वाद्मानं वाचः सुदिनत्वम् अह्नाम् ||
indra śreṣṭhāni draviṇāni dhehi cittiṁ dakṣasya subhagatvam asme | poṣaṁ rayīṇām ariṣṭiṁ tanūnāṁ svādmānaṁ vācaḥ sudinatvam ahnām ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
  • Śreṣṭhāni: Means the best or the most excellent, indicating a request for the highest quality or the best blessings.
  • Draviṇāni: Refers to wealth or resources, suggesting a desire for abundance and prosperity.
  • Dhehi: A plea or invocation, meaning "please grant" or "bestow upon us."
  • Cittiṁ: Refers to intelligence or wisdom, highlighting the importance of mental clarity and understanding.
  • Dakṣasya: Means skillful or capable, emphasizing the need for competence in actions.
  • Subhagatvam: Refers to auspiciousness or good fortune, indicating a desire for favorable outcomes.
  • Asme: Means "to us," indicating the recipients of the blessings.
  • Poṣaṁ: Refers to nourishment or sustenance, suggesting the need for support and growth.
  • Rayīṇām: Refers to wealth or resources, reinforcing the theme of abundance.
  • Ariṣṭiṁ: Means obstacles or difficulties, indicating a desire to overcome challenges.
  • Tanūnāṁ: Refers to bodies or forms, suggesting a connection to physical existence.
  • Svādmānaṁ: Means sweetness or pleasure, indicating a desire for joy and satisfaction.
  • Vācaḥ: Refers to speech or words, highlighting the importance of communication.
  • Sudinavatvam: Means auspiciousness or good fortune, emphasizing the need for positive outcomes.
  • Ahnām: Refers to days or time, indicating the ongoing nature of these blessings.

This mantra is a prayer to Indra, seeking blessings of wealth, wisdom, and auspiciousness. It emphasizes the importance of intelligence and skill in achieving success and overcoming obstacles. The invocation reflects a holistic approach to life, where material wealth, mental clarity, and good fortune are interconnected.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In business, seeking prosperity and success through wise decision-making and effective communication.
  • In personal development, striving for mental clarity and skillfulness in one's endeavors.
  • In community efforts, working together to overcome challenges and support each other's growth.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the need for wealth, wisdom, and auspiciousness. It serves as a reminder that success is not just about material gain but also about mental clarity, effective communication, and overcoming obstacles. By invoking these qualities, individuals can strive for a balanced and fulfilling life.
