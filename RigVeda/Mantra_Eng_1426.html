या ऊर्ध्वया स्वध्वरो देवो देवाच्याऽकृपा |घृतस्य विभ्राश्टिम अनु वष्टि शोचिषाजुह्वानस्य सर्पिषः ||
ya ūrdhvayā svadhvaro devo devācyā kṛpā | ghṛtasya vibhrāṣṭim anu vaṣṭi śociṣājuhvānasya sarpiṣaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who
    • ūrdhvayā: by the upward (or higher)
    • svadhvaro: self-possessed or self-empowered
    • devo: the divine or god
    • devācyā: the divine speech or utterance
    • kṛpā: grace or compassion
    • ghṛtasya: of ghee (clarified butter)
    • vibhrāṣṭim: the brilliance or radiance
    • anu: following
    • vaṣṭi: to be established or to dwell
    • śociṣaḥ: of the shining or illuminating
    • juhvānasya: of the one who offers
    • sarpiṣaḥ: of the ghee or essence
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine being who is self-empowered and embodies grace.
    • The reference to "ghṛtasya vibhrāṣṭim" suggests a connection to the purity and brilliance of ghee, which is often used in rituals and symbolizes nourishment and sustenance.
    • The phrase "anu vaṣṭi" indicates the establishment of this divine presence in the lives of those who seek it, emphasizing the importance of divine grace in achieving higher states of being.
    • The mantra highlights the significance of offerings ("juhvānasya") and the essence of life ("sarpiṣaḥ"), suggesting that through our actions and offerings, we can connect with the divine and receive blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek divine guidance and support through prayer and offerings, similar to the essence of this mantra.
    • During festivals, offerings of ghee and other pure substances are made to invoke divine blessings, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on the importance of offerings.
    • In moments of challenge, people may turn to their faith, seeking the grace and compassion of a higher power, resonating with the self-empowerment and divine connection described in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of divine grace and self-empowerment in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to make offerings and seek a connection with the divine, which can lead to personal growth and enlightenment.
    • In a broader sense, it highlights the significance of compassion and the pursuit of higher ideals, applicable in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, community service, and spiritual practices.
