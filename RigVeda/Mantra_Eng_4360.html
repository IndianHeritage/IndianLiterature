प्र वो महे मातयो यन्तु विष्णवे मरुत्वते गिरिजा एवयामरुत् |प्र शर्धाय प्रयज्यवे सुखादाय तवसे भण्डदिष्टये धुनिव्रताय शवसे ||
pra vo mahe matayo yantu viṣṇave marutvate girijā evayāmarut | pra śardhāya prayajyave sukhādaye tavase bhandadiṣṭaye dhunivratāya śavase ||
  • pra vo mahe matayo: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke your great thoughts." Here, the speaker is calling upon the divine for wisdom and guidance. In real life, this can be seen in situations where individuals seek inspiration or clarity in decision-making, such as during important life choices or when facing challenges.
  • yantu viṣṇave marutvate: This means "may they go towards the divine, the wind gods." Vishnu represents preservation and protection, while Maruts are storm deities associated with strength and power. This can be related to the idea of harnessing natural forces for positive outcomes, like using renewable energy sources (wind, solar) to create a sustainable future.
  • girijā evayāmarut: Translated as "the mountains and the winds," this phrase signifies the connection between nature and the divine. It reminds us of the importance of respecting and preserving our environment, as seen in movements advocating for climate change awareness and conservation efforts.
  • pra śardhāya prayajyave: This translates to "for the purpose of strength and prosperity." It emphasizes the pursuit of well-being and success. In everyday life, this can be reflected in personal goals, such as striving for career advancement or financial stability through hard work and dedication.
  • sukhādaye tavase: Meaning "for the bestowal of happiness," this phrase highlights the importance of seeking joy and contentment. In real life, this can be seen in practices like mindfulness and gratitude, where individuals focus on appreciating the present moment and fostering positive relationships.
  • bhandadiṣṭaye dhunivratāya: This can be interpreted as "for the fulfillment of desires and commitments." It speaks to the importance of setting intentions and following through with them. In practical terms, this can relate to goal-setting in personal and professional life, where individuals work diligently to achieve their aspirations.
  • śavase: This word means "to the swift." It suggests the need for agility and responsiveness in life. In a fast-paced world, being adaptable and open to change is crucial, whether in business, technology, or personal relationships.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound invocation for wisdom, strength, happiness, and the fulfillment of desires. Its significance lies in the holistic approach it advocates—encouraging individuals to seek divine guidance while actively engaging with the natural world and pursuing personal goals. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate a balanced life, where spiritual, environmental, and personal aspirations coexist harmoniously. By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can navigate life's challenges with resilience and purpose.
