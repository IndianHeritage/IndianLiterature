हस्ताभ्यां दशशाखाभ्यां जिव्हा वाचः पुरोगवी |अनामयित्नुभ्यां त्वा ताभ्यां त्वोप स्पृशामसि ||
hastābhyāṁ daśaśākhābhyāṁ jihvā vācaḥ purogavī | anāmayitnubhyāṁ tvā tābhyāṁ tvopa spṛśāmasi ||
  • hastābhyāṁ: This word translates to "by the hands." In a practical sense, hands are essential tools for action and creation. For example, when we create art or build something, we use our hands to manifest our ideas into reality.
  • daśaśākhābhyāṁ: This means "by the ten branches." The ten branches can symbolize various aspects of life or knowledge. For instance, in a family, the ten branches could represent the different members and their roles, emphasizing the importance of unity and collaboration.
  • jihvā: This translates to "the tongue." The tongue is crucial for communication, expressing thoughts, and sharing knowledge. In real life, effective communication can lead to better relationships and understanding among people.
  • vācaḥ: This means "speech" or "words." Words have the power to inspire, heal, or harm. For example, a kind word can uplift someone's spirits, while harsh words can cause pain. This highlights the importance of mindful communication.
  • purogavī: This term can be interpreted as "the foremost" or "the leader." In a leadership context, it signifies the importance of guiding others with wisdom and integrity. A good leader inspires their team to achieve common goals.
  • anāmayitnubhyāṁ: This phrase means "by the unblemished." It suggests purity and integrity in actions and intentions. In real life, maintaining integrity in our actions fosters trust and respect in our relationships.
  • tvā: This translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a higher power or the divine. It emphasizes the connection between the individual and the divine, reminding us to seek guidance and support in our endeavors.
  • tābhyāṁ: This means "by them," referring to the hands, branches, tongue, and speech mentioned earlier. It signifies the interconnectedness of our actions and words in shaping our reality.
  • tvopa: This translates to "you also." It reinforces the idea that the divine is involved in our actions and intentions, encouraging us to align our efforts with higher principles.
  • spṛśāmasi: This means "we touch" or "we connect." It signifies the act of reaching out, whether physically or metaphorically, to connect with others and the divine.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of our actions, words, and intentions in shaping our lives and the world around us. It teaches us the importance of integrity, communication, and connection with the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in leadership roles, personal relationships, and creative endeavors. By being mindful of how we use our hands, words, and intentions, we can create a positive impact in our lives and the lives of others.
