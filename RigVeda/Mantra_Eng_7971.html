स वृत्रहा वृषा सुतो वरिवोविद अदाभ्यः |सोमो वाजम् इवासरत ||
sa vṛtrahā vṛṣā suto varivovid adābhyaḥ | somo vājam ivāsarat ||
  • sa - This word translates to "he" or "that," indicating a subject or a person being referred to.
  • vṛtrahā - This term means "the slayer of Vritra," where Vritra is a serpent or dragon-like figure representing chaos and obstacles. The act of slaying Vritra symbolizes overcoming challenges.
  • vṛṣā - This word can mean "the strong" or "the powerful," often referring to a deity or a force that embodies strength and resilience.
  • suto - This translates to "the son" or "the offspring," indicating lineage or the result of a powerful union.
  • varivovid - This term means "one who knows the best," suggesting wisdom and knowledge in making choices or decisions.
  • adābhyaḥ - This word means "from the unblemished" or "from the pure," indicating a source that is free from impurities or negativity.
  • somo - This refers to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition that symbolizes nourishment, vitality, and divine inspiration.
  • vājam - This translates to "the strength" or "the power," often associated with the energy that drives success and achievement.
  • ivāsarat - This means "like a chariot" or "as if it were a vehicle," suggesting movement, progress, and the journey towards a goal.

The mantra can be interpreted as a powerful invocation of strength, wisdom, and the overcoming of obstacles. It emphasizes the importance of divine support in achieving one's goals and the necessity of purity and knowledge in the pursuit of success.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Overcoming Challenges: Just as the slayer of Vritra represents the triumph over chaos, individuals facing personal or professional challenges can draw inspiration from this mantra to find the strength to overcome their obstacles.
  • Seeking Wisdom: The reference to "one who knows the best" encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom in their decisions, whether in business, relationships, or personal growth.
  • Embracing Purity: The idea of drawing from the "unblemished" can be a reminder to maintain integrity and purity in one's actions and intentions, fostering a positive environment.
  • Harnessing Vitality: The mention of Soma as a source of nourishment can be related to the importance of mental and physical well-being, encouraging practices that enhance vitality and inspiration.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of resilience, the importance of wisdom, and the necessity of purity in our endeavors. It encourages individuals to harness their inner strength and seek divine guidance in their journey, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal challenges to professional pursuits.
