त्वां अग्ने मानुषीर ईळते विश्वो होत्राविदं विविचिं रत्नधातमम् |गुहा सन्तं शुभग विश्वदर्शतं तुविष्वणसं सुयजं घृतश्रियम् ||
tvām agne mānuṣīr īḻate viśo hotrāvidaṁ viviciṁ ratnadhātamam | guhā santaṁ subhaga viśvadarśataṁ tuviṣvaṇasaṁ suyajaṁ ghṛtaśriyam ||
  • tvām agne - "O Agni" - This refers to the divine fire, Agni, which is a significant deity in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • mānuṣīr - "human" - This term indicates the connection between the divine and human realms, suggesting that Agni is accessible and relevant to human life.
  • īḻate - "is invoked" - This implies the act of calling upon Agni, highlighting the importance of rituals and prayers in Vedic traditions.
  • viśo - "of the people" - This signifies the collective aspect of humanity, emphasizing that the invocation is for the benefit of all.
  • hotrā - "the priest" - Refers to the one who performs the rituals, indicating the role of intermediaries in spiritual practices.
  • vidaṁ - "knowledge" - This suggests the importance of wisdom and understanding in the rituals performed.
  • viviciṁ - "distinguished" - This indicates the need to discern and choose wisely in life, reflecting the importance of making informed decisions.
  • ratnadhātamam - "the best of treasures" - This phrase highlights the value of spiritual wealth and the pursuit of higher knowledge.
  • guhā - "cave" - Symbolizes the hidden aspects of existence, suggesting that deeper truths lie beneath the surface.
  • santaṁ - "peaceful" - This indicates the importance of tranquility and calmness in life.
  • subhaga - "auspicious" - Refers to the favorable and beneficial aspects of life, encouraging positivity.
  • viśvadarśataṁ - "all-seeing" - This suggests a broader perspective, encouraging individuals to be aware of the larger picture in life.
  • tuviṣvaṇasaṁ - "of all beings" - This emphasizes the interconnectedness of all life forms, promoting compassion and empathy.
  • suyajaṁ - "well-directed" - This indicates the importance of having a clear purpose and direction in life.
  • ghṛtaśriyam - "the glory of ghee" - Ghee is a symbol of nourishment and purity, representing the sustenance of both body and spirit.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of invoking divine energy for the benefit of humanity. It emphasizes the importance of knowledge, discernment, and the interconnectedness of all beings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, personal reflections, or community gatherings, where individuals seek guidance, peace, and a deeper understanding of their purpose. By recognizing the divine presence in everyday life and striving for spiritual wealth, individuals can cultivate a harmonious existence that benefits not only themselves but also the larger community.
