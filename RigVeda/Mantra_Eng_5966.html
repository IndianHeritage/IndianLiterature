प्र यā जिगाति खरगलेव नक्तम् अप द्रुहा तन्बं गूहमानā | वव्रां अणन्तां अव सā पदिष्ट ग्रावाणो घ्नन्तु रक्षस उपब्दैः ||
pra yā jigāti khargaleva naktam apa druhā tanva1ṁ gūhamānā | vavrām̐ anantām̐ ava sā padīṣṭa grāvāṇo ghnantu rakṣasa upabdaiḥ ||
  • pra yā jigāti: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which moves forward" or "that which progresses." It signifies the importance of progress and movement in life.
  • khargaleva naktam: This translates to "like the night that is fierce." Here, the night symbolizes challenges or obstacles that one may face. Just as the night can be daunting, so can the difficulties in life.
  • apa druhā tanvaṁ gūhamānā: This means "without any malice or harm." It emphasizes the need for purity of intention and action, suggesting that one should act without causing harm to others.
  • vavrām̐ anantām̐: This can be understood as "I have taken refuge in the infinite." It reflects the idea of seeking shelter in something eternal and unchanging, which can be a source of strength and stability.
  • ava sā padīṣṭa: This translates to "may that be established." It indicates a desire for stability and permanence in one's endeavors.
  • grāvāṇo ghnantu rakṣasa upabdaiḥ: This phrase means "may the evil ones be vanquished." It expresses a wish for the removal of negativity and harmful influences from one's life.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the journey of life, emphasizing the importance of progress, purity of intention, and the quest for stability amidst challenges. It serves as a reminder to seek refuge in the eternal and to strive for the eradication of negativity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal growth, conflict resolution, and the pursuit of peace. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can navigate their lives with a sense of purpose and resilience, ultimately contributing to a more harmonious existence.
