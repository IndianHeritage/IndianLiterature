त्वं तान वृत्रहत्ये चोदयो नृन् कार्पाणे शूर वज्रिवः |गुहा यदī कवीनां विश्वां नक्षत्रशवासाम ||
tvaṁ tān vṛtrahatye codayo nṝn kārpāṇe śūra vajrivaḥ | guhā yadī kavīnāṁ viśāṁ nakṣatraśavasām ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or force.
  • tān - Meaning "those," it refers to the subjects or beings that are being addressed or invoked.
  • vṛtrahatye - This term means "in the slaying of Vritra," where Vritra symbolizes obstacles or adversities that hinder progress.
  • codayo - Translates to "inspire" or "motivate," suggesting a call for action or encouragement.
  • nṝn - Meaning "men" or "humans," it emphasizes the focus on human beings and their struggles.
  • kārpāṇe - This word means "in poverty" or "in need," highlighting a state of lack or desperation.
  • śūra - Translates to "brave" or "valiant," indicating the qualities of courage and strength.
  • vajrivaḥ - Meaning "like the thunderbolt," it symbolizes power and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • guhā - This word means "cave" or "hidden place," suggesting a depth of knowledge or wisdom that is not immediately visible.
  • yadī - Translates to "if," indicating a conditional statement that opens up possibilities.
  • kavīnāṁ - Meaning "of the wise" or "of the poets," it refers to those who possess insight and understanding.
  • viśāṁ - This term means "of the people" or "of the community," emphasizing collective existence.
  • nakṣatraśavasām - Translates to "of the starry beings," indicating a connection to celestial or divine influences.

The mantra essentially calls upon a divine force to inspire and empower humans, especially in times of need and adversity. It acknowledges the struggles faced by individuals and communities, invoking the strength and bravery akin to that of a thunderbolt to overcome obstacles. The reference to wisdom and the hidden depths of knowledge suggests that true understanding and insight can guide individuals through their challenges.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties in life, such as financial struggles or emotional distress, individuals can recite this mantra to seek inspiration and courage to overcome their circumstances.
  • Community Support: In times of collective hardship, such as during natural disasters or economic downturns, communities can come together, invoking this mantra to foster unity and strength among members.
  • Leadership and Motivation: Leaders can use the essence of this mantra to inspire their teams, encouraging them to face challenges with bravery and resilience.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the strength that lies within individuals and communities to overcome adversity. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom, courage, and collective support in navigating life's challenges, making it relevant in various aspects of personal and communal life.
