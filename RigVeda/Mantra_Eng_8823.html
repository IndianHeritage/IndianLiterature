मूरा अमूर न वयं चिकीर्त्वो महित्वम् अग्ने त्वम् अङ्ग विटसे |शये ववृष चरति जिह्वयादन रेरिह्यते युवतिं विश्वपतिः सन् ||
mūrā amūra na vayaṁ cikitvo mahitvam agne tvam aṅga vitse | śaye vavriś carati jihvayādan rerihyate yuvatiṁ viśpatiḥ san ||
  • mūrā amūra: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who is not bound" or "the one who is free." In a real-life context, this can refer to individuals who break free from societal constraints or limitations, much like innovators or leaders who challenge the status quo.
  • na vayaṁ cikitvo: This translates to "we do not know." It signifies a sense of humility and acknowledgment of our limitations in understanding the vastness of existence. For example, scientists often admit that there is much about the universe that remains a mystery, encouraging continuous exploration and learning.
  • mahitvam agne: "O Agni, the great one." Agni, the fire god, symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. In everyday life, this can be likened to the transformative power of education or personal growth, where individuals harness their inner fire to achieve greatness.
  • tvam aṅga vitse: This means "you, O Agni, are the one who brings forth." It highlights the role of Agni in creation and sustenance. In a practical sense, this can be seen in how leaders or mentors nurture and guide others, fostering growth and development in their communities.
  • śaye vavriś: This phrase can be interpreted as "you who dwell in the abode." It suggests a connection to a higher power or divine presence. In real life, this can be related to the idea of finding peace and solace in spirituality or nature, where individuals feel a sense of belonging and connection.
  • carati jihvayādan: This translates to "moving with the tongue." It can symbolize communication and expression. In today's world, effective communication is crucial in personal and professional relationships, emphasizing the importance of dialogue and understanding.
  • rerihyate yuvatiṁ: This means "the young one is nourished." It signifies the importance of nurturing the youth, who are the future of society. In real life, this can be seen in educational initiatives and mentorship programs that aim to empower young individuals to reach their potential.
  • viśpatiḥ san: "As the lord of the universe." This phrase acknowledges the omnipresence and authority of a higher power. In practical terms, it can be related to the idea of stewardship, where individuals take responsibility for their actions and their impact on the world around them.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of freedom, humility, transformation, nurturing, and responsibility. Its significance lies in the reminder that while we may not fully understand the universe, we have the power to transform ourselves and others through knowledge, communication, and care. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to embrace their potential, support the growth of others, and act as responsible stewards of the world, fostering a sense of community and interconnectedness.
