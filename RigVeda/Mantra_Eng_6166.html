इमां मा इन्द्र सुष्टुतिं जुषस्व प्र सु मां अव |उता प्र वर्धयामति ||
imām ma indra suṣṭutiṁ juṣasva pra su mām ava | uta pra vardhayā matim ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imām: this
    • ma: my
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • suṣṭutim: good praise or commendation
    • juṣasva: enjoy or accept
    • pra: well or fully
    • su: good
    • mām: me
    • ava: protect or support
    • uta: and
    • pra: well or fully
    • vardhayā: increase or enhance
    • matim: intellect or understanding
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Indra, asking him to accept the good praise offered and to protect the speaker while enhancing their intellect and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an employee may seek the support of their manager (akin to Indra) to recognize their hard work and provide guidance for career growth.
    • Students often pray for wisdom and understanding before exams, hoping for divine assistance in enhancing their intellect.
    • In personal relationships, individuals may seek the support of friends or family to acknowledge their efforts and provide encouragement during challenging times.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognition and support in personal and professional growth.
    • It highlights the need for divine or external assistance in enhancing one's capabilities and understanding.
    • In daily life, individuals can apply this mantra by seeking constructive feedback and support from mentors, peers, and loved ones to foster their development.
