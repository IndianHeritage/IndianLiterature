ये चाकनन्त चाकनन्त नू ते मर्ता अमृत मो ते अंहा आरन |वावन्धि यज्यूंर उता तेषु धेह्य ओजो जनेषु येषु ते स्याम ||
ye cākananta cākananta nū te martā amṛta mo te aṁha āran | vāvandhi yajyūm̐r uta teṣu dhehy ojo janeṣu yeṣu te syāma ||
  • Translation: "Those who are infinite, those who are infinite, may you, O mortals, attain immortality. May we be endowed with strength in those who are engaged in sacrifice, in those who are worthy of worship."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: Those who
    • cākananta: are infinite or boundless
    • nū: indeed
    • te: you
    • martā: mortals
    • amṛta: immortality
    • mo: may you
    • aṁha: strength or power
    • āran: engaged in
    • vāvandhi: may we be endowed
    • yajyūm̐r: in sacrifice
    • uta: and
    • teṣu: in those
    • dhehy: who are worthy
    • ojo: strength
    • janeṣu: in people
    • yeṣu: in whom
    • te: you
    • syāma: may we be
  • Meaning and Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the concept of infinity and immortality, suggesting that while we are mortal beings, we can aspire to attain a higher state of existence.
    • The repetition of "cākananta" highlights the boundless nature of the divine or the ultimate reality, which is beyond human comprehension.
    • The phrase "may you, O mortals, attain immortality" serves as a reminder that through our actions, particularly those that are selfless and sacrificial, we can transcend our mortal limitations.
    • In real life, this can be seen in the actions of individuals who dedicate their lives to serving others, such as humanitarian workers, volunteers, or even parents who sacrifice their own needs for the well-being of their children.
    • The call for strength in those engaged in sacrifice reflects the importance of community and support in achieving collective goals, much like how teams in sports or organizations work together towards a common purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the aspiration for immortality through selfless actions and the pursuit of higher ideals.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as in leadership, community service, and personal relationships, where individuals strive to make a positive impact beyond their own existence.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to recognize the infinite potential within ourselves and others, motivating us to act with purpose and compassion.
