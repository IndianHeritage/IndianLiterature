शुनं हुवेम मगवाणं इन्द्रं अस्मिन भरे नृतमं वाजसातौ | श्रिण्वन्तं उग्रं ऊतये समत्सु घ्नन्तं वृत्राणि संजितं धनानाम् ||
śunaṁ huvema maghavānam indram asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau | śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi saṁjitaṁ dhanānām ||
  • śunaṁ huvema: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke the powerful." Here, "śunaṁ" refers to the act of invoking or calling upon, while "huvema" signifies a strong desire or intention to connect with a higher power.
  • maghavānam indram: This refers to "Indra, the mighty one." Indra is often seen as the king of the gods in Vedic literature, representing strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau: This can be interpreted as "in this place, we offer our best." "Nṛtamaṁ" signifies the best or the highest, while "vājasātau" refers to offerings or sacrifices made to the divine.
  • śṛṇvantam ugram: This means "listening to the fierce." "Śṛṇvantam" indicates the act of listening or paying attention, while "ugram" refers to something fierce or powerful, suggesting the importance of being receptive to strong energies or messages.
  • ūtaye samatsu: This translates to "for the purpose of prosperity." "Ūtaye" signifies the aim or goal, while "samatsu" refers to harmony or well-being, indicating a desire for collective prosperity.
  • ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi: This phrase means "destroying the obstacles." "Ghnantaṁ" refers to the act of striking down or overcoming, while "vṛtrāṇi" signifies the obstacles or challenges that one faces in life.
  • saṁjitaṁ dhanānām: This can be interpreted as "the gathering of wealth." "Saṁjitaṁ" refers to the collection or accumulation, while "dhanānām" signifies wealth or resources, emphasizing the importance of abundance in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of invoking divine strength and guidance to overcome obstacles and achieve prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of intention, receptivity, and collective well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during challenging times when one seeks strength and support, or in rituals aimed at attracting abundance and harmony in personal and communal life. By invoking the energies represented in this mantra, individuals can foster a mindset of resilience and openness to opportunities, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and prosperous existence.
