प्रेन्द्रस्य वाचं प्रथमां कृतानि प्र नूतना मगवाऽया चकार |यदेद अदेवीर असहिष्टा मायाऽथाभवत् केवलः सोमोऽस्य ||
prendrasya vocam prathamā kṛtāni pra nūtanā maghavā yā cakāra | yaded adevīr asahiṣṭa māyā athābhavat kevalaḥ somo asya ||
  • Mantra: "prendrasya vocam prathamā kṛtāni pra nūtanā maghavā yā cakāra | yaded adevīr asahiṣṭa māyā athābhavat kevalaḥ somo asya ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prendrasya: of the supreme being or the divine
    • vocam: voice or utterance
    • prathamā: the first or foremost
    • kṛtāni: actions or deeds
    • pra: towards or in favor of
    • nūtanā: new or innovative
    • maghavā: the powerful or mighty one
    • yā: which
    • cakāra: created or made
    • yaded: whatever
    • adevīr: without the divine
    • asahiṣṭa: unendurable or intolerable
    • māyā: illusion or magic
    • athābhavat: then became
    • kevalaḥ: only or solely
    • somo: the moon or soma (nectar)
    • asya: of this
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine voice that initiates the first actions, emphasizing the importance of new and powerful creations.
    • The reference to "maghavā" suggests a connection to strength and abundance, indicating that the actions taken are not just ordinary but are imbued with divine power.
    • The phrase "yā cakāra" highlights the creative aspect of the divine, suggesting that the universe and its phenomena are manifestations of this divine will.
    • Furthermore, the mantra touches upon the concept of "māyā," which refers to the illusions of the material world. It suggests that without the divine, these illusions can become intolerable.
    • The mention of "somo" symbolizes nourishment and rejuvenation, akin to how the moon nourishes life on Earth, indicating that divine actions lead to sustenance and growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of "maghavā" can inspire innovation and creativity among team members, leading to groundbreaking projects.
    • In personal life, individuals may experience moments of clarity or inspiration that feel divinely guided, leading them to make significant life changes or pursue new paths.
    • In nature, the cycles of the moon can be seen as a representation of the "somo," where its phases influence tides and agricultural practices, reminding us of the interconnectedness of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the divine influence in our actions and the importance of recognizing the power of creativity and innovation in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek the divine in their endeavors, understanding that true strength and sustenance come from aligning with higher principles.
    • In a broader context, it highlights the significance of spirituality in navigating the challenges of life, urging us to transcend illusions and embrace the nourishing aspects of existence.
