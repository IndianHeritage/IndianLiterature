आर्ष्टिषेणो होत्रं ऋषिर निषीदन देवापिर देवसुमतिं चिकीत्वान् |स उttarasmād अधरं समुद्रम अपो दिव्याऽसृजद वर्ष्या अभि ||
ārṣṭiṣeṇo hotram ṛṣir niṣīdan devāpir devasumatiṁ cikitvān | sa uttarasmād adharaṁ samudram apo divyā asṛjad varṣyā abhi ||
  • ārṣṭiṣeṇo hotram: This phrase refers to the "sacred fire" or "sacrificial offering" that is performed by a priest (hotri). In real life, this can be likened to the importance of rituals and ceremonies in various cultures, where offerings are made to seek blessings or express gratitude.
  • ṛṣir niṣīdan: Here, "ṛṣir" means sage or seer, and "niṣīdan" implies sitting or meditating. This suggests the importance of wisdom and contemplation. In modern life, this can be related to practices like meditation or mindfulness, where individuals seek inner peace and understanding.
  • devāpir devasumatiṁ cikitvān: This translates to "the gods, having understood the divine intention." It emphasizes the connection between human actions and divine will. In contemporary terms, this can be seen in how people often seek guidance from a higher power or the universe when making significant life decisions.
  • sa uttarasmād adharaṁ samudram: This phrase means "from that, he created the lower ocean." It symbolizes creation and the emergence of life from the depths. In real life, this can be compared to the idea of innovation and creativity, where new ideas emerge from foundational knowledge or experiences.
  • apo divyā asṛjad varṣyā abhi: This translates to "from the waters, he created the divine rain." It signifies the importance of water as a life-giving force. In today's context, this can relate to environmental awareness and the significance of water conservation for sustaining life on Earth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of creation, divine connection, and the significance of rituals. It highlights the interplay between human actions and the divine, emphasizing the importance of wisdom, contemplation, and the natural elements that sustain life. In real-life scenarios, these themes are applicable in various contexts, such as environmental conservation, the importance of rituals in cultural practices, and the pursuit of inner peace through mindfulness. Understanding this mantra encourages individuals to reflect on their actions and their impact on the world around them, fostering a deeper connection with nature and the divine.
