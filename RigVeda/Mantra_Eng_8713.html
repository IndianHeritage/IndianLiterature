आ हर्यतो अर्जुने अतके अव्यत प्रियः सूनुर न मर्ज्यः |तम ईं हिन्वन्त्य अपसो यथा रथं नदिष्व आ गभस्त्योः ||
ā haryato arjune atke avyata priyaḥ sūnur na marjyaḥ | tam īṁ hinvanty apaso yathā rathaṁ nadīṣv ā gabhastyoḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā haryato: "from the one who is swift" - refers to a force or energy that is quick and powerful.
    • arjune: "O Arjuna" - a direct address, invoking the spirit of Arjuna, a key figure in the Mahabharata, symbolizing valor and righteousness.
    • atke: "not hindered" - signifies the idea of being unimpeded or free from obstacles.
    • avyata: "unwavering" - denotes stability and consistency in purpose or action.
    • priyaḥ: "beloved" - something that is cherished or dear.
    • sūnur: "son" - can refer to progeny or something that is born from a source.
    • na marjyaḥ: "not to be killed" - implies immortality or the eternal nature of the spirit.
    • tam: "that" - refers to the subject of the mantra, which is a divine or powerful entity.
    • īṁ: "may we attain" - a wish or prayer for attainment or realization.
    • hinvanty: "to drive away" - signifies the act of removing obstacles or negativity.
    • apasō: "from the waters" - can symbolize the source of life or the flow of energy.
    • yathā: "as" - a comparative term that connects two ideas.
    • rathaṁ: "chariot" - symbolizes the vehicle of progress or movement in life.
    • nadīṣv: "in the rivers" - represents the flow of life and the interconnectedness of all things.
    • ā gabhastyoḥ: "from the depths" - indicates a source that is profound and rich.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of harnessing swift and powerful energies in life, much like how Arjuna, a warrior, harnessed his skills in battle.
    • It emphasizes the importance of being unimpeded and unwavering in one's pursuits, akin to a river that flows freely without obstruction.
    • The reference to "son" and "not to be killed" suggests the eternal nature of the spirit and the legacy we leave behind, much like the teachings of great leaders that continue to inspire generations.
    • The metaphor of the chariot represents our journey through life, where we must navigate through various challenges, similar to how a charioteer skillfully maneuvers through obstacles on the battlefield.
    • The connection to water signifies the nurturing aspect of life, reminding us of the importance of adaptability and flow in our endeavors.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of determination and the importance of remaining steadfast in our goals.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to personal growth, career aspirations, and overcoming challenges, encouraging individuals to embrace their inner strength and navigate through life's complexities.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us to cherish our journey, remain connected to our roots, and strive for a legacy that transcends time.
