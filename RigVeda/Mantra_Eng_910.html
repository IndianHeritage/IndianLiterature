आपो न देवीर उप यन्ति होत्रियम अवः पश्यन्ति विततं यथा रजः |प्राचैर देवासः प्र णयन्ति देवयुम ब्रह्मप्रियं जोषयन्ते वराऽ इव ||
āpo na devīr upa yanti hotriyam avaḥ paśyanti vitataṁ yathā rajaḥ | prācair devāsaḥ pra ṇayanti devayum brahmapriyaṁ joṣayante varā iva ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āpo: Waters
    • na: Not
    • devīr: Goddesses
    • upa: Near
    • yanti: Go
    • hotriyam: The act of invocation or prayer
    • avaḥ: May they come
    • paśyanti: They see
    • vitataṁ: Spread out
    • yathā: As
    • rajaḥ: Dust or particles
    • prācair: From the east
    • devāsaḥ: The gods
    • pra ṇayanti: Lead or guide
    • devayum: Divine or godly
    • brahmapriyaṁ: Beloved of Brahman
    • joṣayante: They delight
    • varā: Excellent or best
    • iva: Like
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the waters as divine entities that are invoked through prayer.
    • The waters are seen as a source of life and nourishment, akin to how we rely on water for sustenance in our daily lives.
    • The phrase "may they come" suggests a plea for the blessings of these divine waters to be present in our lives.
    • The reference to the gods leading us indicates a desire for guidance and support from higher powers.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of connection with nature and the divine, highlighting how they work together to sustain life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers pray for rain, recognizing the vital role of water in crop growth, similar to the invocation of divine waters in the mantra.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek guidance through meditation or prayer, hoping for clarity and direction, akin to the gods leading us.
    • Environmental conservation efforts can be seen as a modern application of this mantra, where the focus is on preserving water bodies and recognizing their sacredness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of nature, divinity, and human existence.
    • It teaches us to respect and honor the natural elements, particularly water, as essential for life.
    • In real life, it encourages us to seek guidance from higher powers and to be mindful of our relationship with the environment.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the blessings that come from acknowledging and nurturing our connection to the divine and the natural world.
