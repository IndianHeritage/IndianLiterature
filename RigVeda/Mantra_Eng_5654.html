इयं देव पुरोहितिर युवाभ्यां यज्ञेषु मित्रावरुणाव अकारी |विश्वानि दुर्गा पिपृतं तिरो नो यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
iyaṁ deva purohitir yuvabhyāṁ yajñeṣu mitrāvaruṇāv akāri | viśvāni durgā pipṛtaṁ tiro no yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • iyaṁ deva purohitir - This phrase translates to "this divine priest." In the context of rituals, the purohit (priest) plays a crucial role in mediating between the divine and the devotees. For example, in modern weddings, a priest conducts the ceremony, invoking blessings for the couple.
  • yuvabhyāṁ yajñeṣu - This means "with the young ones in the sacrifices." It emphasizes the importance of youth and vitality in spiritual practices. In real life, this can be seen in youth-led initiatives in communities, where young people take charge of social and spiritual activities.
  • mitrāvaruṇāv akāri - This translates to "Mitra and Varuna have made." Mitra and Varuna are deities representing friendship and cosmic order, respectively. Their invocation signifies the importance of harmony and balance in our lives. For instance, in teamwork, fostering good relationships (Mitra) and maintaining ethical standards (Varuna) are essential for success.
  • viśvāni durgā pipṛtaṁ - This means "may all obstacles be removed." It is a prayer for the removal of difficulties. In everyday life, this can relate to overcoming challenges in personal or professional endeavors, such as studying for exams or starting a new business.
  • tiro no yūyam pāta - This translates to "may you protect us." It is a plea for divine protection and guidance. In real life, this can be seen in the way people seek blessings before embarking on significant journeys or new ventures, such as moving to a new city or starting a new job.
  • svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ - This means "may you always grant us well-being." It emphasizes the desire for continuous prosperity and health. In practical terms, this can relate to maintaining a healthy lifestyle through balanced nutrition and regular exercise, as well as fostering positive relationships.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound spiritual message that emphasizes the importance of divine guidance, the role of youth in spiritual practices, and the need for harmony and well-being in our lives. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, from personal growth to community involvement. By invoking these principles, individuals can navigate challenges, foster relationships, and seek continuous improvement in their lives, making it a timeless guide for both spiritual and practical living.
