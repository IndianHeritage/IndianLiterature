तत् तु ते दंशो यद् अहन् समानैर नृभिर यद् युक्तो विवे रपांसि ||
tat tu te daṁso yad ahan samānair nṛbhir yad yukto vive rapāṁsi ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • tat: that
    • tu: but
    • te: your
    • daṁso: teeth or sharpness
    • yad: which
    • ahan: day or light
    • samānair: equal or similar
    • nṛbhir: by men or humans
    • yad: which
    • yukto: connected or united
    • vive: to separate or discern
    • rapāṁsi: the waters or the essence
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks about the essence of connection and discernment among humans.
    • It emphasizes the importance of understanding and recognizing the sharpness or clarity in our interactions.
    • The phrase "that but your sharpness" suggests that while we may be similar, it is our unique qualities that define us.
    • The reference to "waters" can symbolize the flow of life and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, individuals may have similar roles, but their unique skills and perspectives contribute to the overall success of the team.
    • In friendships, while people may share common interests, it is their distinct personalities that enrich the relationship.
    • In nature, different species coexist in an ecosystem, each playing a vital role, showcasing the importance of diversity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of recognizing and valuing individual differences while fostering connections among people.
    • It encourages us to appreciate the unique qualities that each person brings to the table, promoting harmony and understanding.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying this wisdom can lead to more effective teamwork, deeper relationships, and a greater appreciation for diversity in all aspects of life.
