यो अस्मै हव्यदातिभिराहुतिमर्तोऽविदत् |भूरि पोषं स धत्ते वीरवत् यशः ||
yo asmai havyadātibhir āhutim marto 'vidhat | bhūri poṣaṁ sa dhatte vīravad yaśaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • asmai: to him
    • havyadātibhir: with offerings
    • āhutim: the invocation or call
    • marto: a mortal or human
    • 'vidhat: does not create or establish
    • bhūri: abundant or plentiful
    • poṣaṁ: nourishment or sustenance
    • sa: he
    • dhatte: holds or possesses
    • vīravad: like a hero
    • yaśaḥ: fame or glory
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the relationship between offerings and the sustenance of a mortal.
    • It emphasizes that a person who does not make offerings or invoke the divine does not receive the abundant nourishment and glory that comes from such actions.
    • The phrase "vīravad yaśaḥ" suggests that true fame and glory are akin to the qualities of a hero, implying that one must act with valor and purpose to achieve recognition.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals and offerings are made to honor deities or ancestors, reflecting the belief that such acts bring blessings and prosperity.
    • For instance, in modern life, people often engage in charitable acts or community service, which can be seen as a form of offering that brings personal fulfillment and societal recognition.
    • In professional settings, individuals who contribute positively to their organizations often gain respect and recognition, akin to the "yaśaḥ" mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of making offerings and engaging in actions that contribute to the greater good.
    • It serves as a reminder that nourishment—both physical and spiritual—comes from our willingness to give and contribute.
    • In real life, this can be applied by actively participating in community service, fostering relationships, and striving for personal growth, which ultimately leads to a fulfilling and recognized life.
