कां राधद धोत्राश्विनां वां को वां जोष उभयोः |कथा विधाति अप्रचेताः ||
kā rādhad dhotrāśvinā vāṁ ko vāṁ joṣa ubhayoḥ | kathā vidhāty apracetāḥ ||
  • kā rādhad: This phrase can be interpreted as "who has brought" or "who has given." It signifies the act of providing or bestowing something valuable.
  • dhotrāśvinā: Referring to the Ashvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing powers and ability to bring prosperity. They symbolize duality and balance in life.
  • vāṁ: This word translates to "you" or "your," indicating a personal connection or address to the listener or the divine.
  • ko vāṁ: This can be understood as "who are you" or "who is it that you are," prompting introspection and self-awareness.
  • joṣa: Meaning "enjoyment" or "delight," it emphasizes the importance of experiencing joy and fulfillment in life.
  • ubhayoḥ: This translates to "of both," suggesting a balance between two aspects, possibly the material and spiritual realms.
  • kathā: Meaning "how" or "in what way," it invites inquiry into the processes and methods of life.
  • vidhāty: This word means "to create" or "to establish," indicating the act of bringing something into existence.
  • apracetāḥ: Translated as "without understanding" or "unwise," it serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom and awareness in our actions.

In conclusion, this mantra reflects the interplay between divine assistance, self-awareness, and the pursuit of joy. It encourages individuals to seek understanding and balance in their lives, recognizing the dualities that exist. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • In personal relationships, understanding the balance between giving and receiving can lead to healthier connections.
  • In professional settings, recognizing the importance of both material success and personal fulfillment can guide career choices.
  • In spiritual practices, the mantra serves as a reminder to seek wisdom and understanding in one's journey, avoiding actions taken without thought.

Ultimately, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for introspection, balance, and the pursuit of joy through understanding, making it relevant in various aspects of life.
