तद् वो दिवो दुहितरो विभातीर उप ब्रुव उषसो यज्ञकेतुः |वयं स्याम यशसो जनेषु तद् dyauś च धत्ताम पृथिवी च देवी ||
tad vo divo duhitaro vibhātīr upa bruva uṣaso yajñaketuḥ | vayaṁ syāma yaśaso janeṣu tad dyauś ca dhattām pṛthivī ca devī ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • vo: for you
    • divo: of the sky
    • duhitaro: daughters
    • vibhātīr: shining
    • upa: near
    • bruva: speak
    • uṣaso: of the dawn
    • yajñaketuḥ: the banner of sacrifice
    • vayaṁ: we
    • syāma: may become
    • yaśaso: of fame
    • janeṣu: among people
    • tad: that
    • dyauś: the sky
    • ca: and
    • dhattām: may hold
    • pṛthivī: the earth
    • ca: and
    • devī: the goddess
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine qualities of the daughters of the sky, symbolizing purity, brightness, and new beginnings.
    • The phrase "yajñaketuḥ" refers to the importance of sacrifice and dedication in achieving success and fame.
    • It emphasizes the collective aspiration of people to attain glory and recognition in society.
    • The invocation of the sky and earth signifies the connection between the divine and the material world, highlighting the balance needed in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be reflected in the efforts of individuals working together for a common goal, such as organizing a festival or a charitable event.
    • In personal life, it can inspire individuals to pursue their dreams and ambitions while remaining grounded and connected to their roots.
    • In professional environments, it can be seen in teamwork where members support each other to achieve collective success.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, sacrifice, and the pursuit of excellence.
    • It serves as a reminder that individual success is intertwined with the well-being of the community and the environment.
    • In real life, it encourages us to strive for personal and collective achievements while honoring our connections to the divine and the earth.
