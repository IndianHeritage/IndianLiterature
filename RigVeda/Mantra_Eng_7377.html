स मण्युम् मार्त्याणाम् अदब्धो नि चिकीषते | पुरा निदश चिकीषते ||
sa manyum martyānām adabdho ni cikīṣate | purā nidaś cikīṣate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sa: he, that
    • manyum: anger, wrath
    • martyānām: of mortals, of human beings
    • adabdho: unrestrained, uncontrolled
    • ni: down, towards
    • cikīṣate: seeks, desires
    • purā: before, in the past
    • nidaś: to be known, to be revealed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the nature of human emotions, particularly anger.
    • It suggests that there is a force or entity that seeks to understand or control the unrestrained anger of mortals.
    • The phrase "purā nidaś" implies that this understanding or revelation has been sought since ancient times.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern psychology, understanding and managing anger is crucial for mental health. Therapists often help individuals recognize their anger triggers and develop coping strategies.
    • In conflict resolution, mediators work to address the underlying emotions, including anger, to facilitate understanding and reconciliation between parties.
    • In personal relationships, open communication about feelings can prevent the escalation of anger and promote healthier interactions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of recognizing and managing human emotions, particularly anger.
    • Its significance lies in the timeless pursuit of understanding our emotional responses, which is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, mental health, and conflict resolution.
    • By acknowledging and addressing our emotions, we can foster better communication, enhance our well-being, and create a more harmonious society.
