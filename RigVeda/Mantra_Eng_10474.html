विभ्राड् बृहद् पिबतु सोम्यम् मध्व आयुर् दधद् यज्ञपताव् अविहृतम् | वातजूतः यो अभिरक्षति तमनाऽ प्रजाः पूपोष पुरुधा वि राजति ||
vibhrāḍ bṛhat pibatu somyam madhv āyur dadhad yajñapatāv avihrutam | vātajūto yo abhirakṣati tmanā prajāḥ pupoṣa purudhā vi rājati ||
  • vibhrāḍ: This word can be interpreted as "spreading" or "expanding." It signifies the vastness of the universe and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • bṛhat: Meaning "great" or "large," this term emphasizes the grandeur of existence and the importance of recognizing the larger picture in life.
  • pibatu: Translated as "may drink," it refers to the act of consuming or absorbing knowledge, wisdom, or experiences.
  • somyam: This word relates to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
  • madhv: Meaning "sweet," it represents the pleasant and fulfilling aspects of life, such as joy and happiness.
  • āyur: Translated as "life" or "longevity," it emphasizes the importance of health and well-being.
  • dadhad: This term means "to bestow" or "to grant," indicating the act of giving or sharing blessings.
  • yajñapatāv: Referring to "the lord of sacrifices," it highlights the significance of rituals and offerings in maintaining harmony in the universe.
  • avihrutam: This word means "unobstructed" or "free," suggesting the importance of a clear and open path in life.
  • vātajūto: Meaning "one who is connected to the wind," it symbolizes the dynamic and ever-changing nature of life.
  • yo: Translated as "who," it introduces the subject of the mantra.
  • abhirakṣati: This term means "protects" or "safeguards," emphasizing the importance of protection and care in our lives.
  • tmanā: Meaning "with the mind," it indicates the role of consciousness and intention in our actions.
  • prajāḥ: Translated as "creatures" or "beings," it refers to all living entities in the universe.
  • pupoṣa: This word means "nourishes" or "sustains," highlighting the importance of nurturing and supporting life.
  • purudhā: Meaning "in many ways," it signifies the diversity and richness of life experiences.
  • vi: A prefix meaning "in" or "with," indicating a connection or relationship.
  • rājati: Translated as "shines" or "rules," it emphasizes the idea of leadership and excellence in one's endeavors.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of wisdom, nourishment, protection, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It serves as a reminder to seek knowledge, embrace joy, and maintain harmony through rituals and conscious living. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate a holistic approach to life, focusing on personal growth, community well-being, and environmental sustainability. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, one can lead a fulfilling and meaningful life, contributing positively to the world around them.
