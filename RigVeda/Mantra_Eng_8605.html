नū नस् त्वं रथिरो देव सोम परि श्रव चाम्वोः पूयमानः |अप्सु स्वादिष्टो मधुमाम् ऋतावा देवो न यः सविता सत्यमनम् ||
nū nas tvaṁ rathiro deva soma pari srava camvoḥ pūyamānaḥ | apsu svādiṣṭho madhumām̐ ṛtāvā devo na yaḥ savitā satyamanmā ||
  • nū nas tvaṁ rathiro deva soma pari srava camvoḥ pūyamānaḥ
    • nū - This word can be interpreted as "indeed" or "truly," emphasizing the sincerity of the invocation.
    • nas - Meaning "to us," indicating a collective appeal or prayer.
    • tvaṁ - Translates to "you," referring to the divine or the deity being addressed.
    • rathiro - This term relates to "the chariot," symbolizing movement, progress, or the journey of life.
    • deva - Meaning "god" or "divine being," acknowledging the higher power.
    • soma - Refers to the sacred drink, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • pari srava - This phrase means "flowing around," suggesting abundance and the spreading of blessings.
    • camvoḥ - Refers to "the offerings," indicating the act of giving or sharing with the divine.
    • pūyamānaḥ - Meaning "being filled," which signifies the fulfillment of desires or needs.
  • apsu svādiṣṭho madhumām̐
    • apsu - Meaning "in the waters," symbolizing purity, life, and sustenance.
    • svādiṣṭho - Translates to "well-chosen" or "well-placed," indicating the importance of right choices.
    • madhumām̐ - Refers to "sweetness" or "honey," symbolizing nourishment, joy, and the sweetness of life.
  • ṛtāvā devo na yaḥ savitā satyamanmā
    • ṛtāvā - Meaning "the one who is true" or "the one who upholds truth," emphasizing the importance of righteousness.
    • devo - Again refers to "god," reinforcing the divine connection.
    • na yaḥ - Translates to "who is not," indicating a negation or a distinction from falsehood.
    • savitā - Refers to "the sun," symbolizing light, knowledge, and enlightenment.
    • satyamanmā - Meaning "truthful mind," emphasizing the importance of integrity and honesty in thoughts.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of divine connection, the flow of blessings, and the pursuit of truth and righteousness. It serves as a reminder to seek abundance and sweetness in life while making well-informed choices. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek truth and integrity in their actions and thoughts.
  • In community settings, it promotes the idea of sharing and giving, fostering a sense of unity and abundance.
  • In spiritual practices, it serves as a prayer for divine guidance and blessings in one's journey.

Overall, this mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support while living a life grounded in truth and righteousness, making it relevant in both personal and communal aspects of life.
