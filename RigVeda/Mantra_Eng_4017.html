श्येन आसाम् अदितिः कक्ष्योऽ मदो विश्ववारस्य यजतस्य मायिनः |सम् अन्यम्-अन्यं अर्थयन्त्य एतवे विदुर विषाणम् परिपानम् अन्ति ते ||
śyena āsām aditiḥ kakṣyo3 mado viśvavārasya yajatasya māyinaḥ | sam anyam-anyam arthayanty etave vidur viṣāṇam paripānam anti te ||
  • śyena: This term refers to a "hawk" or "eagle," symbolizing strength, vision, and the ability to rise above challenges.
  • āsām: This means "of those" or "of the ones," indicating a collective or a group.
  • aditiḥ: Aditi is a goddess representing infinity, the mother of gods, and the concept of boundlessness.
  • kakṣyo: This word can be interpreted as "of the womb" or "of the space," suggesting a nurturing aspect or a source of creation.
  • mado: This translates to "joy" or "intoxication," often associated with the bliss of spiritual experiences.
  • viśvavārasya: This means "of the universal protector," indicating a force or entity that safeguards all beings.
  • yajatasya: This refers to "of the one who performs sacrifice," highlighting the importance of rituals and offerings in spiritual practices.
  • māyinaḥ: This term means "of the illusionist" or "the one who creates illusions," pointing to the transient nature of reality.
  • sam: This means "together" or "in harmony," emphasizing unity and collaboration.
  • anyam-anyam: This phrase translates to "each other," indicating mutual support and interaction among individuals.
  • arthayanty: This means "they seek" or "they strive for," suggesting a quest for meaning or purpose.
  • etave: This translates to "for this," indicating a specific focus or intention.
  • vidur: This means "wise" or "knowledgeable," referring to those who possess understanding and insight.
  • viṣāṇam: This term can be interpreted as "horns" or "instruments," symbolizing power and the ability to manifest intentions.
  • paripānam: This means "to nourish" or "to sustain," indicating the importance of support and care.
  • anti te: This translates to "they are," suggesting a state of being or existence.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine, highlighting the importance of unity, support, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in professional settings, community support during challenging times, and the importance of spiritual practices that foster a sense of belonging and purpose. The mantra serves as a reminder that through collective efforts and shared wisdom, we can navigate the complexities of life and achieve greater harmony and fulfillment.
