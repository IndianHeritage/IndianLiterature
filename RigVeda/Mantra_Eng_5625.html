युṣ्माकं देवाऽवसाहनि प्रिय ईजानस तरति द्विषः |प्र स क्षयं तिरते वि महीर इषो यो वो वराय दाशति ||
yuṣmākaṁ devā avasāhani priya ījānas tarati dviṣaḥ | pra sa kṣayaṁ tirate vi mahīr iṣo yo vo varāya dāśati ||
  • yuṣmākaṁ: This word translates to "your" or "yours," indicating a sense of possession or belonging.
  • devā: Refers to "gods" or "divine beings," symbolizing higher powers or spiritual entities that govern the universe.
  • avasāhani: This term means "to protect" or "to safeguard," suggesting a plea for divine protection.
  • pri: This root means "beloved" or "dear," indicating affection or a close relationship.
  • ījānas: Translates to "worship" or "adoration," emphasizing the importance of devotion in spiritual practices.
  • tarati: Means "to overcome" or "to cross," often used in the context of overcoming obstacles or challenges.
  • dviṣaḥ: Refers to "enemies" or "haters," indicating the presence of negativity or opposition in life.
  • pra: A prefix meaning "forth" or "forward," often used to denote movement or progression.
  • kṣayaṁ: This word means "destruction" or "loss," referring to the removal of negativity or harmful influences.
  • tirate: Means "to flow" or "to move," suggesting a dynamic process of change or transformation.
  • vi: A prefix meaning "apart" or "separately," indicating distinction or separation from negativity.
  • mahīr: Refers to "great" or "mighty," often used to describe powerful forces or entities.
  • iṣo: Means "desire" or "wish," indicating a longing for something positive or beneficial.
  • yo: Translates to "who," referring to a person or entity that is the subject of the mantra.
  • vo: Means "to you," indicating a direct address to the divine or the audience.
  • varāya: This term means "to grant" or "to bestow," suggesting a request for blessings or favors.
  • dāśati: Refers to "to give" or "to offer," emphasizing the act of providing or sharing.

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for divine protection and guidance. It expresses a desire for the gods to safeguard the devotees from their enemies and negative influences, allowing them to overcome challenges and achieve their goals. The invocation of divine beings highlights the importance of faith and devotion in navigating life's difficulties.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing obstacles in personal or professional life, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek divine support and protection.
  • Conflict Resolution: In situations of conflict or rivalry, this mantra encourages individuals to focus on overcoming negativity and fostering positive relationships.
  • Spiritual Growth: The emphasis on worship and devotion in the mantra can inspire individuals to deepen their spiritual practices and connect with higher powers.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine intervention in overcoming adversities and fostering a sense of security. It serves as a reminder of the power of faith and the importance of seeking protection from higher forces in our daily lives. By incorporating this mantra into personal rituals or meditative practices, individuals can cultivate resilience and a positive outlook amidst challenges.
