महाम् आदित्यो नमसोपसद्य यातयज्जनो गृणते सुशेवः |तस्मा एतत पण्यतमाया जुष्टम् अग्नौ मित्राय हविर आ जुहोत ||
mahām̐ ādityo namasopasadyo yātayajjano gṛṇate suśevaḥ | tasmā etat panyatamāya juṣṭam agnau mitrāya havir ā juhota ||
  • Translation: "Great Aditya, to you I bow, who is the one that all beings praise with good deeds. Therefore, this offering is to be made to the fire, for the sake of Mitra."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • mahām̐ ādityo: Refers to the "Great Aditya," a representation of the sun and a symbol of divine light and knowledge.
    • namasopasadyo: "To you I bow," indicating reverence and respect towards the divine.
    • yātayajjano: "Who is praised by all beings," suggesting that the sun is universally acknowledged and revered.
    • gṛṇate suśevaḥ: "With good deeds," emphasizing the importance of righteous actions and their connection to divine favor.
    • tasmā etat panyatamāya: "Therefore, this offering is to be made," indicating the act of giving and the significance of offerings in spiritual practices.
    • juṣṭam agnau: "To the fire," highlighting the sacredness of fire in Vedic rituals as a medium to connect with the divine.
    • mitrāya: "For the sake of Mitra," referring to the deity associated with friendship, harmony, and the sun, reinforcing the theme of unity and goodwill.
    • havir ā juhota: "Let the offering be made," a call to action for devotees to engage in rituals and offerings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, people often express gratitude and reverence towards nature, akin to bowing to the "Great Aditya." For instance, farmers may pray for sunlight and favorable weather conditions for their crops, recognizing the sun's vital role in agriculture.
    • Acts of kindness and good deeds in communities can be seen as a way of "praising" the divine. Volunteering, helping neighbors, or participating in community service reflects the essence of "gṛṇate suśevaḥ."
    • In many cultures, fire is a central element in rituals, such as lighting candles during festivals or using fire in ceremonies to symbolize purification and connection to the divine.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of reverence towards the divine, the importance of good deeds, and the role of offerings in spiritual practices. In real life, it encourages individuals to acknowledge the interconnectedness of all beings, to act with kindness, and to engage in rituals that honor the divine presence in nature. This mantra serves as a reminder of the harmony between humanity and the cosmos, urging us to live with gratitude and purpose.
