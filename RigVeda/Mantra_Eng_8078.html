उष्रा वेद वसूनां मर्तस्य देव्य अवसः |तरत स मन्दी धावति ||
usrā veda vasūnām martasya devy avasaḥ | tarat sa mandī dhāvati ||
  • usrā: This word translates to "the dawn" or "the light." In a metaphorical sense, it represents knowledge, enlightenment, and the beginning of new opportunities.
  • veda: This term means "to know" or "knowledge." It signifies the importance of wisdom and understanding in life.
  • vasūnām: This refers to "the wealth" or "the treasures." It can be interpreted as the various blessings and resources that life offers.
  • martasya: This word means "of mortals" or "human beings." It emphasizes the human experience and the challenges faced in life.
  • devy: This translates to "divine" or "goddess." It signifies the presence of higher powers or spiritual guidance in our lives.
  • avasaḥ: This means "to dwell" or "to reside." It indicates the idea of being grounded or connected to one's surroundings.
  • tarat: This word means "to cross over" or "to transcend." It represents the journey of overcoming obstacles and achieving higher states of being.
  • sa: This translates to "that" or "he/she/it." It refers to the subject of the mantra, which can be interpreted as the seeker or the divine.
  • mandī: This word means "the one who moves" or "the one who flows." It symbolizes the dynamic nature of life and the constant changes we experience.
  • dhāvati: This translates to "runs" or "flows." It signifies the active pursuit of goals and the movement towards enlightenment.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to seek knowledge and enlightenment (usrā veda) while recognizing the wealth and blessings (vasūnām) that life offers to mortals (martasya). It acknowledges the divine presence (devy) that guides us as we dwell (avasaḥ) in our experiences. The mantra encourages us to transcend (tarat) our limitations and actively pursue (dhāvati) our goals, embracing the dynamic flow of life (mandī).


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios. For instance, when facing challenges in personal or professional life, one can reflect on the importance of seeking knowledge and wisdom to navigate through difficulties. It serves as a reminder to appreciate the resources and blessings available to us, fostering gratitude and a positive mindset.


In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the significance of knowledge, divine guidance, and the pursuit of personal growth. It encourages individuals to embrace change and actively seek enlightenment, making it applicable in everyday life as a source of inspiration and motivation.
