तं आरवन्तं न सानसिं अरुशं न दिवः शिशुम् |मार्मृज्यन्ते दिवे-दिवे ||
tam arvantaṁ na sānasim aruṣaṁ na divaḥ śiśum | marmṛjyante dive-dive ||
  • tam arvantaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "that which is to be attained" or "that which is desired." It signifies the ultimate goal or aspiration in life.
  • na sānasim: This means "not to be harmed" or "not to be destroyed." It emphasizes the importance of preservation and protection of what is valuable.
  • aruṣaṁ: This word refers to "the red one" or "the radiant one," often symbolizing vitality, energy, or the life force.
  • na divaḥ: This translates to "not of the heavens" or "not celestial," indicating a focus on earthly or practical matters rather than purely spiritual or heavenly pursuits.
  • śiśum: This means "the child" or "the young one," representing innocence, potential, and new beginnings.
  • marmṛjyante: This can be interpreted as "they are wiped away" or "they are removed," suggesting the idea of overcoming obstacles or challenges.
  • dive-dive: This phrase means "day by day" or "each day," indicating a continuous process or the importance of daily efforts.

In summary, this mantra speaks to the pursuit of a desired goal that is vital and radiant, while emphasizing the need for protection and preservation of what is important. It acknowledges the challenges that may arise but encourages perseverance and daily effort to overcome them. The reference to "the child" symbolizes the potential for growth and new beginnings, reminding us that every day is an opportunity to strive towards our aspirations.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in personal development, where individuals strive to achieve their goals while safeguarding their values and well-being.
  • In a professional context, it encourages employees to pursue their career aspirations with dedication, while also fostering a supportive environment that protects their interests.
  • In education, it highlights the importance of nurturing young minds, allowing them to grow and overcome challenges day by day.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder that the journey towards our goals requires consistent effort, protection of our values, and an acknowledgment of the potential within us, much like the nurturing of a child into adulthood.
