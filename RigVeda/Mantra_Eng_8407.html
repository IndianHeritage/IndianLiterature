पवित्रं ते विततम् ब्रह्मणस् पते प्रभुर् गात्राणि पर्येक्षि विश्वतः |अतप्ततनूर् न तद् आमो अशनुते श्रुतास इद् वहन्तस् तत् सम् आशत ||
pavitraṁ te vitatam brahmaṇas pate prabhur gātrāṇi pary eṣi viśvataḥ | ataptatanūr na tad āmo aśnute śṛtāsa id vahantas tat sam āśata ||
  • pavitraṁ te vitatam: This phrase translates to "Your purity is vast." Here, "pavitraṁ" signifies purity, which is a central theme in many spiritual and religious practices. In real life, purity can be seen in actions, thoughts, and intentions. For example, a person who practices honesty and integrity in their dealings embodies this purity.
  • brahmaṇas pate: This means "O Lord of the Universe." It acknowledges a higher power or divine presence that governs the cosmos. In everyday life, this can be related to the respect and reverence we show towards nature and the universe, recognizing that we are part of something much larger than ourselves.
  • prabhur gātrāṇi pary eṣi viśvataḥ: This translates to "The Lord pervades all bodies everywhere." It emphasizes the omnipresence of the divine. In practical terms, this can be understood through the interconnectedness of all living beings. For instance, the idea that our actions affect others can be seen in environmental conservation efforts, where protecting nature benefits all life forms.
  • ataptatanūr: This means "the body that is not heated." It symbolizes a state of calmness and tranquility. In real life, this can be related to maintaining composure in stressful situations, such as during a conflict at work or in personal relationships, where a calm demeanor can lead to better resolutions.
  • na tad āmo aśnute: This translates to "that does not consume." It suggests a state of being that is not driven by greed or desire. In modern life, this can be reflected in minimalism or sustainable living, where individuals choose to live with less and focus on what truly matters, rather than accumulating material possessions.
  • śṛtāsa id vahantas: This means "those who have heard and understood." It highlights the importance of knowledge and wisdom. In real life, this can be seen in the value of education and learning from experiences, where individuals who seek knowledge can make informed decisions that positively impact their lives and communities.
  • tat sam āśata: This translates to "may they attain that." It is a prayer for the attainment of higher consciousness and understanding. In practical terms, this can be related to personal growth and self-improvement, where individuals strive to reach their full potential through self-reflection and continuous learning.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of purity, divine presence, interconnectedness, calmness, non-attachment, wisdom, and the pursuit of higher consciousness. Its significance lies in guiding individuals towards a more harmonious and meaningful life. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various aspects, such as personal relationships, environmental stewardship, and self-development, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence aligned with the greater good of all beings.
