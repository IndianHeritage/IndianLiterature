इन्द्रावरुणा सौमनसम् अदृप्तं रायस पोषं यजमानेषु धत्तम् | प्रजां पुष्टिम भूतिं अस्मासु धत्तं दीर्घायुत्वाय प्र तिरतम् न आयुः ||
indrāvaruṇā saumanasam adṛptaṁ rāyas poṣaṁ yajamāneṣu dhattam | prajām puṣṭim bhūtim asmāsu dhattaṁ dīrghāyutvāya pra tirataṁ na āyuḥ ||
  • Indrāvaruṇā: This refers to the divine pair of Indra and Varuna, representing the forces of nature and cosmic order. Indra is often associated with rain and thunderstorms, while Varuna embodies the celestial ocean and moral law.
  • Saumanasam: This term signifies a state of pleasantness or harmony. It suggests a mindset that is conducive to positive outcomes and well-being.
  • Adṛptaṁ: This word means 'ungrasped' or 'unattained.' It implies that the blessings or resources being sought are not yet fully realized or possessed.
  • Rāyas: This refers to wealth or prosperity. In a modern context, it can be related to both material wealth and spiritual richness.
  • Poṣaṁ: This means nourishment or sustenance. It emphasizes the importance of nurturing both physical and spiritual aspects of life.
  • Yajamāneṣu: This term refers to those who are performing rituals or sacrifices. It highlights the significance of community and collective efforts in spiritual practices.
  • Dhattam: This means 'may it be bestowed' or 'may it be granted.' It is a plea for divine blessings and support.
  • Prajām: This refers to progeny or offspring. It signifies the importance of family and the continuation of life.
  • Puṣṭim: This means growth or prosperity. It emphasizes the need for development in various aspects of life.
  • Bhūtim: This term signifies well-being or fortune. It is a call for overall health and success.
  • Asmāsu: This means 'in us' or 'within us.' It indicates a focus on internal growth and self-awareness.
  • Dīrghāyutvāya: This term means 'for long life' or 'for longevity.' It emphasizes the desire for a healthy and extended life.
  • Pra tirataṁ: This means 'may it be directed' or 'may it be guided.' It suggests the need for purposeful living and guidance in one's actions.
  • Na āyuḥ: This translates to 'not life' or 'not lifespan.' It implies a desire to transcend mere existence and seek a meaningful life.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a profound prayer for prosperity, well-being, and longevity, invoking the blessings of Indra and Varuna. It emphasizes the importance of harmony, community, and nurturing both material and spiritual aspects of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during family gatherings, community rituals, or personal reflections, where individuals seek to cultivate a harmonious and prosperous life. By focusing on collective efforts and internal growth, one can strive for a fulfilling existence that transcends mere survival, aiming instead for a life rich in purpose and connection.
