उत नः सुभगाम् अरिर वोच्युर दसम कृष्टयः |स्यामेद इन्द्रस्य शर्मणि ||
uta naḥ subhagām̐ arir voceyur dasma kṛṣṭayaḥ | syāmed indrasya śarmaṇi ||
  • Translation: The mantra translates to "May the auspicious ones speak to us; may we be protected by the powerful Indra." This invocation seeks blessings and protection from the divine.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • uta: A word that signifies "may" or "let us."
    • naḥ: Means "us," indicating a collective appeal.
    • subhagām̐: Refers to "auspicious" or "blessed," suggesting a desire for positive influences.
    • arir: Translates to "enemies" or "adversaries," indicating a wish to overcome challenges.
    • voceyur: Means "speak" or "communicate," implying a call for guidance or wisdom.
    • dasma: Means "may we be protected," emphasizing the need for safety.
    • kṛṣṭayaḥ: Refers to "powerful" or "strong," often associated with divine strength.
    • syāmed: Means "may we be," indicating a hopeful aspiration.
    • indrasya: Refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength and protection.
    • śarmaṇi: Means "in safety" or "in peace," highlighting the desire for a secure environment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be invoked during team meetings to foster a positive and collaborative environment, encouraging team members to communicate openly and support one another.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during challenging times, such as during conflicts or when facing adversities, to seek strength and protection from negative influences.
    • During community gatherings or festivals, this mantra can be chanted to invoke blessings for collective well-being and harmony among participants.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for auspiciousness and protection from adversities. It emphasizes the importance of communication, collective strength, and divine guidance in overcoming challenges. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal, professional, or communal contexts, invoking this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek positivity, support, and safety, fostering an environment conducive to growth and harmony.
