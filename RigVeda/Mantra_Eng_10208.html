अहं सोमं आहनसंबिभर्म्य अहं त्वष्टारमुत पूषणं भागम् |अहं दधामि द्रविणं हविष्मते सुप्राव्ये यजमानाय सुन्वते ||
ahaṁ somam āhanasam bibharmy ahaṁ tvaṣṭāram uta pūṣaṇam bhagam | ahaṁ dadhāmi draviṇaṁ haviṣmate suprāvye3 yajamānāya sunvate ||
  • ahaṁ somam āhanasam bibharmy: This phrase translates to "I carry the Soma, the invigorating drink." Soma is often associated with divine inspiration and immortality in Vedic literature. In real life, this can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and experiences that invigorate our spirit and mind, much like how people seek out education, travel, or art to enrich their lives.
  • ahaṁ tvaṣṭāram uta pūṣaṇam bhagam: Here, "I am the creator (Tvaṣṭā) and the nourisher (Pūṣaṇa)." Tvaṣṭā is the divine craftsman, while Pūṣaṇa represents nourishment and prosperity. This can be related to individuals who take on roles of creation and nurturing in their communities, such as teachers, parents, or mentors who shape the future and provide support to others.
  • ahaṁ dadhāmi draviṇaṁ haviṣmate: This means "I bestow wealth (draviṇa) upon those who offer (haviṣmate)." It emphasizes the importance of generosity and the reciprocal nature of giving and receiving. In modern life, this can be seen in charitable acts, where individuals or organizations provide resources to those in need, fostering a sense of community and support.
  • suprāvye yajamānāya sunvate: This translates to "I grant blessings to the worshippers (yajamānā) who are devoted." It highlights the significance of devotion and sincerity in one's actions. In everyday life, this can be observed in the dedication people show towards their work, family, or community service, where their efforts are often rewarded with success and fulfillment.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, nourishment, and the reciprocal relationship between giving and receiving. It signifies the importance of being a source of inspiration and support in the lives of others. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in leadership roles, community service, and personal relationships, where individuals strive to uplift and empower those around them. By embodying the principles of creation, nourishment, and devotion, we contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
