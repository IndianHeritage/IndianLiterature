परā श्रिणीहि तपसा यातुधानान पराग्ने रक्षो हरसा श्रिणीहि | परार्चिषा मूरेदेवान् श्रिणीहि परासुतृपो अभि शोशुचानाḥ ||
parā śṛṇīhi tapasā yātudhānān parāgne rakṣo harasā śṛṇīhi | parārciṣā mūradevāñ chṛṇīhi parāsutṛpo abhi śośucānaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "parā śṛṇīhi tapasā yātudhānān parāgne rakṣo harasā śṛṇīhi | parārciṣā mūradevāñ chṛṇīhi parāsutṛpo abhi śośucānaḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be interpreted as a call for protection and guidance from higher powers against malevolent forces. It invokes the divine to listen and act against those who wish harm.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • parā: beyond, higher, supreme
    • śṛṇīhi: listen, hear
    • tapasā: through austerity, penance
    • yātudhānān: against the evil spirits or malevolent beings
    • parāgne: supreme fire, representing divine energy
    • rakṣo: demons, evil forces
    • harasā: to remove, to dispel
    • parārciṣā: with supreme light or radiance
    • mūradevāñ: the divine beings or deities
    • chṛṇīhi: listen, pay attention
    • parāsutṛpo: with supreme strength
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • śośucānaḥ: those who are harmful or destructive
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek spiritual guidance or protection, similar to how this mantra calls for divine intervention against negativity.
    • In various cultures, rituals are performed to ward off evil spirits, reflecting the essence of this mantra's plea for protection.
    • People may recite this mantra during challenging times, such as illness or conflict, to invoke a sense of safety and divine support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking higher powers for protection against malevolent forces in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder of the strength found in spirituality and the belief in a protective divine presence.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to turn to their faith or spiritual practices during difficult times, fostering resilience and hope.
