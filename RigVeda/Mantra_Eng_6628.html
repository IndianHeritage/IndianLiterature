त्वां हि सुप्सरस्तमं नृषदानेषु हूमहे |ग्रावाणं नाश्वपृष्ठं मंहनाः ||
tvāṁ hi supsarastamaṁ nṛṣadaneṣu hūmahe | grāvāṇaṁ nāśvapṛṣṭham maṁhanā ||
  • tvāṁ - This word translates to "you" or "to you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • hi - This is an emphatic particle, often used to emphasize the preceding word, suggesting certainty or affirmation.
  • supsarastamaṁ - This term can be broken down into "su" (good or well) and "psarastamaṁ" (the best or the most excellent), referring to something that is of the highest quality or excellence.
  • nṛṣadaneṣu - This word refers to "in the gatherings of men" or "among people," indicating a social or communal context.
  • hūmahe - This translates to "we invoke" or "we call upon," suggesting a plea or a prayer directed towards the divine.
  • grāvāṇaṁ - This term can be understood as "the one who brings together" or "the gatherer," often referring to a leader or a guiding force.
  • nāśvapṛṣṭham - This can be interpreted as "the one who is at the back of the horses," which may symbolize strength, support, or protection.
  • maṁhanā - This word means "to be strong" or "to be powerful," indicating a call for strength or empowerment.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation directed towards a divine entity, asking for guidance and strength in communal gatherings. It emphasizes the importance of invoking the divine presence in social contexts, seeking excellence and support in our endeavors.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Community Gatherings: When people come together for events, rituals, or meetings, invoking a higher power can foster unity and purpose.
  • Leadership: Leaders can draw upon this mantra to seek strength and wisdom in guiding their communities, ensuring that they act in the best interest of all.
  • Personal Empowerment: Individuals can use this invocation in their personal lives to seek strength and clarity when facing challenges or making important decisions.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of divine guidance and strength in communal and personal contexts. It serves as a reminder to seek excellence and support from a higher power, fostering unity and empowerment in our lives.
