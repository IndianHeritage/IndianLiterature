परā पूर्वेषां सख्यā वृणक्ति वितर्तुराणो अपरेभिर एति | अनानुभूतीर अवधून्वानः पूर्वीर इन्द्रः शरदस् तर्तरिति ||
parā pūrveṣāṁ sakhyā vṛṇakti vitarturāṇo aparebhir eti | anānubhūtīr avadhūnvānaḥ pūrvīr indraḥ śaradas tartarīti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • parā: beyond, superior
    • pūrveṣāṁ: of the ancients, the earlier ones
    • sakhyā: friendship, companionship
    • vṛṇakti: chooses, selects
    • vitarturāṇo: those who are striving, those who are making efforts
    • aparebhir: by others, by those who come later
    • eti: goes, approaches
    • anānubhūtīr: without experience, unexperienced
    • avadhūnvānaḥ: one who is aware, one who knows
    • pūrvīr: of the ancients, the earlier ones
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods
    • śaradas: autumn, the season of harvest
    • tartarīti: moves swiftly, progresses
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the choices made by those who are striving for friendship and companionship, emphasizing the importance of selecting the right associations.
    • The reference to "the ancients" suggests that wisdom and experience from the past play a crucial role in guiding present actions.
    • Indra, as a symbol of strength and leadership, represents the qualities needed to navigate through life's challenges, especially during times of change, like the transition of seasons.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, choosing the right colleagues and mentors can significantly impact one's career trajectory. Just as the mantra suggests, selecting companions wisely can lead to greater success.
    • In personal relationships, understanding the lessons from past friendships can help individuals make better choices in their current relationships, fostering deeper connections.
    • During times of change, such as moving to a new city or starting a new job, the wisdom of those who have experienced similar transitions can provide valuable insights and support.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the importance of wise choices in relationships and the value of learning from the past.
    • It encourages individuals to seek out meaningful connections and to be mindful of the influences they allow into their lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to more fulfilling relationships, better decision-making, and a deeper understanding of one's journey through life.
