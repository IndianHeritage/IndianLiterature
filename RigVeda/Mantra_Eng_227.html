विष्णोः कर्माणि पश्यत यतो व्रतानि पस्पशे |इन्द्रस्य युज्यः सखा ||
viṣṇoḥ karmāṇi paśyata yato vratāni paspaśe | indrasya yujyaḥ sakhā ||
  • viṣṇoḥ: Refers to Lord Vishnu, the preserver and protector in Hindu mythology. He represents the aspect of divinity that maintains cosmic order.
  • karmāṇi: This word means "actions" or "deeds." It emphasizes the importance of one's actions in the world.
  • paśyata: Translates to "see" or "observe." It encourages individuals to be aware and mindful of their surroundings and actions.
  • yato: Means "from where" or "from which." It indicates a source or origin.
  • vratāni: Refers to "vows" or "resolutions." This highlights the significance of commitment and discipline in one's life.
  • paspaśe: This means "to be fulfilled" or "to be accomplished." It suggests the realization of one's vows through actions.
  • indrasya: Refers to Lord Indra, the king of the gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms. He symbolizes strength and power.
  • yujyaḥ: Means "connected" or "united." It signifies the importance of relationships and alliances.
  • sakhā: Translates to "friend" or "companion." It emphasizes the value of friendship and support in achieving goals.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to observe the actions of Lord Vishnu, from which one can understand the importance of fulfilling vows and commitments. It also highlights the connection between individuals, like friends, and the strength that comes from unity, as exemplified by Lord Indra.


In real life, this mantra encourages individuals to:

  • Be mindful of their actions and the impact they have on themselves and others.
  • Commit to their goals and resolutions, understanding that fulfillment comes through dedicated effort.
  • Value relationships and friendships, recognizing that support from others can help in overcoming challenges.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on awareness, commitment, and the power of relationships. In today's fast-paced world, where distractions are abundant, this mantra serves as a reminder to stay focused on our actions, fulfill our promises, and cherish the bonds we share with others. By embodying these principles, individuals can lead more meaningful and fulfilling lives.
