इमां अग्नेशरणिं मीमृषो न इमं अध्वानं यं अगाम दूरात् |आपिः पिता प्रमतिः सोम्यानां भृमिर अस्य ऋषिकृन् मार्त्यानां ||
imām agne śaraṇim mīmṛṣo na imam adhvānaṁ yam agāma dūrāt | āpiḥ pitā pramatiḥ somyānām bhṛmir asy ṛṣikṛn martyānām ||
  • imām agne: This phrase translates to "this Agni" or "this fire." In Vedic literature, Agni represents the divine fire, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the connection between the physical and spiritual realms.
  • śaraṇim: This means "shelter" or "refuge." It indicates seeking protection or guidance from the divine.
  • mīmṛṣo: This word can be interpreted as "not false" or "truthful." It emphasizes the importance of sincerity and authenticity in one's actions and intentions.
  • na imam adhvānaṁ: This translates to "not this path." It suggests a rejection of a certain way or approach, possibly indicating the need to choose a different path in life.
  • yam agāma dūrāt: This means "which has come from afar." It signifies the idea of distant goals or aspirations that one seeks to achieve.
  • āpiḥ: This word means "also" or "too," indicating inclusivity or additional aspects.
  • pitā: This translates to "father," symbolizing authority, guidance, and protection.
  • pramatiḥ: This means "wisdom" or "intellect." It highlights the importance of knowledge and understanding in navigating life's challenges.
  • somyānām: This refers to "the gentle ones" or "the wise." It emphasizes the qualities of compassion and wisdom in individuals.
  • bhṛmir asy: This translates to "the earth of this." It signifies the grounding aspect of existence and the material world.
  • ṛṣikṛn: This means "the seer" or "the sage." It represents those who possess deep insight and understanding.
  • martyānām: This translates to "of mortals." It indicates the human condition and the struggles faced by individuals in life.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking refuge in the divine fire (Agni) while navigating the complexities of life. It emphasizes the importance of truthfulness, wisdom, and compassion in our journey.
  • Real-life Application: In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as decision-making, personal growth, and relationships. For instance, when faced with a difficult choice, one might reflect on the sincerity of their intentions (mīmṛṣo) and seek guidance (śaraṇim) from a trusted mentor (pitā) or wise friend (somyānām).
  • Conclusion: Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek truth and wisdom while navigating life's paths. It encourages individuals to connect with their inner selves and the divine, fostering a sense of purpose and direction in their lives.
