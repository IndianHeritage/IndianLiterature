यā ते काकुत सुखृतā यā वरिष्टā यया शाश्वत पिबसि मध्व ūर्मिम् |तया पाहि प्र ते अध्वर्युर अस्तात सं ते वज्रो वर्तताम इन्द्र गव्युः ||
yā te kākut sukṛtā yā variṣṭhā yayā śaśvat pibasi madhva ūrmim | tayā pāhi pra te adhvaryur asthāt saṁ te vajro vartatām indra gavyuḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yā te kākut sukṛtā yā variṣṭhā yayā śaśvat pibasi madhva ūrmim | tayā pāhi pra te adhvaryur asthāt saṁ te vajro vartatām indra gavyuḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yā: who/that
    • te: your
    • kākut: of the Kākutstha (a lineage of kings)
    • sukṛtā: well-done, virtuous
    • yā: who/that
    • variṣṭhā: the best, the most excellent
    • yayā: by which
    • śaśvat: eternal, everlasting
    • pibasi: you drink
    • madhva: sweet, nectar
    • ūrmim: waves
    • tayā: by that
    • pāhi: protect
    • pra: for
    • te: your
    • adhvaryur: the priest (performer of rituals)
    • asthāt: may it stand
    • saṁ: together
    • te: your
    • vajro: thunderbolt, strength
    • vartatām: may it be present
    • indra: the king of gods
    • gavyuḥ: of the cows, wealth
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine qualities associated with Indra, the king of gods, who is often seen as a protector and a provider of strength and prosperity.
    • The phrase "yā te kākut sukṛtā" refers to the virtuous actions that lead to success and well-being, emphasizing the importance of righteousness in one's life.
    • "yayā śaśvat pibasi madhva ūrmim" suggests that through these virtuous actions, one can partake in the eternal sweetness of life, akin to enjoying the waves of nectar, which can be likened to the joy derived from fulfilling relationships and achievements.
    • The call for protection "tayā pāhi pra te adhvaryur asthāt" signifies the need for divine support in our endeavors, especially in rituals and important life events, much like seeking guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders in real life.
    • Finally, "saṁ te vajro vartatām indra gavyuḥ" emphasizes the desire for strength and abundance, akin to the blessings of wealth and prosperity that one seeks in their personal and professional life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of virtuous living, the pursuit of excellence, and the importance of divine support in achieving one's goals.
    • In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as during important decisions, rituals, or when seeking guidance in challenging times.
    • By embodying the principles of righteousness and seeking divine blessings, individuals can navigate their lives with strength and purpose, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and prosperous existence.
