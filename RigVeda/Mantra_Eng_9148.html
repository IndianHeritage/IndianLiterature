यो वः सेनानीर महतो गणस्य राजा व्रातस्य प्रथमो बभूव |तस्मै कृणोमि न धनाऽरुणध्मि दशाहं प्राचीस तद ऋतं वदामि ||
yo vaḥ senānīr mahato gaṇasya rājā vrātasya prathamo babhūva | tasmai kṛṇomi na dhanā ruṇadhmi daśāham prācīs tad ṛtaṁ vadāmi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • vaḥ: your
    • senānīr: leader of the army
    • mahato: of the great
    • gaṇasya: of the multitude
    • rājā: king
    • vrātasya: of the assembly
    • prathamo: the first
    • babhūva: became
    • tasmai: to him
    • kṛṇomi: I offer
    • na: not
    • dhanā: wealth
    • ruṇadhmi: I cry out
    • daśāham: tenfold
    • prācīs: of the east
    • tad: that
    • ṛtaṁ: truth
    • vadāmi: I speak
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the leader of a great assembly or army, emphasizing the importance of leadership and the qualities that make a good leader.
    • The phrase "the first became" suggests that the leader is not just a title but a role that is earned through actions and virtues.
    • The offering of "not wealth" indicates that true leadership is not about material riches but about wisdom, guidance, and moral integrity.
    • The mention of "tenfold" signifies the amplification of virtues or qualities that a leader should possess, suggesting that a good leader should strive to enhance their capabilities continuously.
    • The reference to "truth" highlights the importance of honesty and integrity in leadership, which is essential for gaining the trust of followers.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern organizations, effective leaders are often those who prioritize the well-being of their team over personal gain, embodying the essence of this mantra.
    • Political leaders who focus on transparency and truthfulness, rather than wealth and power, can inspire trust and loyalty among their constituents.
    • In community settings, leaders who listen to their members and act in their best interests, rather than seeking personal glory, exemplify the qualities described in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a timeless reminder of the qualities that define true leadership: integrity, selflessness, and a commitment to truth.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from corporate leadership to community organizing, emphasizing that the essence of leadership lies in serving others rather than seeking personal gain.
    • By embodying these principles, individuals can foster environments of trust and cooperation, ultimately leading to greater success and harmony in any collective endeavor.
