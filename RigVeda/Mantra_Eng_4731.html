अस्य पिब यस्य जज्ञाना इन्द्र मदाय क्रत्वे अपिबो विरप्शिन् |तम उ ते गावो नर आपो अद्रिर इन्दुं सम अह्यन् पीतये सम अस्मै ||
asya piba yasya jajñāna indra madāya kratve apibo virapśin | tam u te gāvo nara āpo adrir induṁ sam ahyan pītaye sam asmai ||
  • Mantra: "asya piba yasya jajñāna indra madāya kratve apibo virapśin | tam u te gāvo nara āpo adrir induṁ sam ahyan pītaye sam asmai ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as a call to Indra, the king of gods, to partake in the knowledge and wisdom that is offered. It emphasizes the importance of strength and vitality in achieving one's goals.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • asya: of this
    • piba: drink or partake
    • yasya: whose
    • jajñāna: knowledge or wisdom
    • indra: the king of gods
    • madāya: for the purpose of intoxication or inspiration
    • kratve: for the sacrifice or effort
    • apibo: to partake
    • virapśin: strong or powerful
    • tam: that
    • u: and
    • te: your
    • gāvo: cows (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • nara: man or human
    • āpo: waters
    • adrir: mountain or strength
    • induṁ: moon or nectar
    • sam: together
    • ahyan: to be made manifest
    • pītaye: for drinking
    • sam: together
    • asmai: to him
  • Significance: This mantra highlights the importance of seeking knowledge and strength from divine sources. It reflects the idea that wisdom and vitality are essential for success in life. Just as Indra is invoked for his power and knowledge, individuals can seek inspiration and strength from their experiences and the world around them.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their experiences and seek knowledge from mentors or books to enhance their understanding and capabilities.
    • In a professional setting, teams can invoke collective wisdom and strength to overcome challenges, much like how Indra is called upon for support.
    • In times of crisis, individuals can look to their inner strength and the support of their community, akin to the invocation of divine strength in the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, strength, and community. By invoking these elements, individuals can navigate life's challenges more effectively, drawing on both internal and external resources for support and inspiration.
