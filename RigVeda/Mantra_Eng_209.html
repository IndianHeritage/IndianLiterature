प्रातर्युजां वि बोधयाश्विनाव् एष गच्छताम् |अस्य सोमस्य पीतये ||
prātaryujā vi bodhayāśvināv eha gacchatām | asya somasya pītaye ||
  • Mantra: "prātaryujā vi bodhayāśvināv eha gacchatām | asya somasya pītaye ||"
  • Translation: "Awaken, O Ashvins, and come here; for the sake of this Soma, let us drink."
  • Explanation:
    • Prātaryujā: This term can be understood as "awaken" or "arise." It signifies a call to action, urging the divine beings to come forth.
    • Vi Bodhayā: "Vi" means "well" or "thoroughly," and "Bodhayā" means "to awaken" or "to enlighten." This phrase emphasizes the need for a complete awakening, not just a superficial one.
    • Ashvināv: The Ashvins are twin deities in Vedic mythology, often associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between opposites.
    • Eha Gacchatām: "Eha" means "here" or "come," and "Gacchatām" means "to go" or "to arrive." This phrase is an invitation for the Ashvins to come to the ritual or the devotee's presence.
    • Asya Somasya: "Asya" means "of this," and "Somasya" refers to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine insight.
    • Pītaye: This term means "for drinking" or "to drink." It indicates the purpose of the invocation, which is to partake in the divine essence of Soma.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the call to "awaken" can be seen in various contexts, such as personal development workshops where individuals are encouraged to realize their potential and take action towards their goals.
    • The Ashvins symbolize the importance of balance in life. For instance, in healthcare, the integration of traditional and modern medicine reflects the duality represented by the Ashvins, promoting holistic well-being.
    • The concept of "Soma" can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment in contemporary society, where individuals seek wisdom through education, meditation, or spiritual practices.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of awakening and enlightenment, calling upon divine forces to assist in the pursuit of knowledge and well-being. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek balance, embrace personal growth, and strive for a deeper understanding of oneself and the universe. The invocation of the Ashvins highlights the significance of divine support in our endeavors, encouraging us to partake in the 'Soma' of life—knowledge, health, and spiritual fulfillment.
