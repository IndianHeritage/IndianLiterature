यं त्वा गोपवनो गिरा चनिष्ठद अग्नि अङ्गिरः |स पावक श्रुधी हवम् ||
yaṁ tvā gopavano girā caniṣṭhad agne aṅgiraḥ | sa pāvaka śrudhī havam ||
  • Mantra: "yaṁ tvā gopavano girā caniṣṭhad agne aṅgiraḥ | sa pāvaka śrudhī havam ||"
  • Translation: "O Agni, who is invoked by the voice of the cowherd, may you, O divine one, be present and accept our offerings."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • yaṁ: "whom" - Referring to Agni, the fire god.
    • tvā: "you" - Directly addressing Agni.
    • gopavano: "the voice of the cowherd" - Symbolizing the pastoral and nurturing aspects of life.
    • girā: "by the voice" - The act of invocation through sound or prayer.
    • caniṣṭhad: "may you be present" - A request for the divine presence.
    • agne: "O Agni" - Directly calling upon the fire god.
    • aṅgiraḥ: "of the Angiras" - Referring to the ancient sages associated with fire and knowledge.
    • sa: "he" - Referring back to Agni.
    • pāvaka: "pure" - Signifying the purifying nature of fire.
    • śrudhī: "may you hear" - A plea for attentiveness from the divine.
    • havam: "our offerings" - The offerings made to Agni.

  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of Agni as a divine entity that purifies and accepts offerings.
    • It reflects the relationship between humans and the divine, emphasizing the need for communication through prayer and invocation.
    • The mention of "gopavano" connects to the pastoral lifestyle, symbolizing care, nurturing, and the sustenance provided by nature.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, lighting a fire during ceremonies symbolizes the presence of Agni, representing purification and the divine acceptance of offerings.
    • In agricultural communities, invoking Agni can be seen as a way to seek blessings for a good harvest, reflecting the nurturing aspect of the cowherd.
    • In personal life, the mantra can inspire individuals to seek purity in their actions and thoughts, fostering a connection with the divine through mindfulness and intention.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of Agni in Vedic traditions, symbolizing purity, acceptance, and the nurturing aspects of life.
    • It encourages individuals to engage in prayer and offerings, fostering a deeper connection with the divine and the natural world.
    • In contemporary life, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide individuals towards a more mindful and respectful approach to their environment and relationships.
