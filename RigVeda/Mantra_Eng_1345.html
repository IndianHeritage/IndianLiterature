विद्वांसाव इद दुरः पृच्छेद अविद्वान इथापरो अचेताः |नू चिन् नु मार्ते अकrau ||
vidvāṁsāv id duraḥ pṛcched avidvān itthāparo acetāḥ | nū cin nu marte akrau ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vidvāṁsāv: the wise or knowledgeable ones
    • id: this
    • duraḥ: difficult or hard
    • pṛcched: to inquire or ask
    • avidvān: the ignorant or unwise
    • itthāparo: in this manner or thus
    • acetāḥ: without understanding or awareness
    • nū: indeed or surely
    • cin: to know or to perceive
    • nu: whether or not
    • marte: in death or mortality
    • akrau: without harm or injury
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the contrast between the wise and the ignorant.
    • The wise (vidvāṁsāv) are capable of asking difficult questions and seeking deeper truths.
    • In contrast, the ignorant (avidvān) may remain unaware and uncurious about the complexities of life.
    • The phrase "nū cin nu marte akrau" suggests a contemplation on mortality and the nature of existence, questioning whether one can truly understand life and death without wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a wise leader seeks feedback and asks challenging questions to foster growth, while an unwise leader may avoid difficult conversations, leading to stagnation.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who are open to understanding and discussing their feelings (the wise) can navigate conflicts better than those who ignore or dismiss issues (the ignorant).
    • In education, students who ask questions and seek knowledge (the wise) are more likely to succeed than those who passively accept information without inquiry (the ignorant).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom and inquiry in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding, especially regarding the profound questions of life and death.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a deeper understanding of the world around us.
    • Ultimately, it highlights that true awareness and understanding come from a willingness to ask difficult questions and engage with the complexities of existence.
