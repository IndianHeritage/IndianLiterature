उदितā यो निदितā वेदितā वस्व ā यज्ञियो ववर्तति |दुष्टरā यस्य प्रवणे नॉर्मयो धियā वाजं सिषासतः ||
uditā yo niditā veditā vasv ā yajñiyo vavartati | duṣṭarā yasya pravaṇe normayo dhiyā vājaṁ siṣāsataḥ ||
  • uditā: This word translates to "arisen" or "awakened." It signifies the beginning of knowledge or consciousness.
  • yo: This means "who" and refers to the subject of the mantra, indicating a person or entity that possesses certain qualities.
  • niditā: This term means "hidden" or "concealed." It suggests that there are deeper truths or knowledge that are not immediately visible.
  • veditā: This translates to "known" or "understood." It implies a level of awareness or comprehension that goes beyond surface knowledge.
  • vasv: This word means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all things.
  • ā yajñiyo: This phrase refers to "sacrificial" or "devotional" acts, emphasizing the importance of selfless actions and offerings in life.
  • vavartati: This means "moves" or "operates," suggesting a dynamic process of action and interaction.
  • duṣṭarā: This translates to "difficult" or "challenging," indicating obstacles or complexities in life.
  • yasya: This means "whose," referring back to the subject of the mantra.
  • pravaṇe: This term means "in the pursuit" or "in the endeavor," suggesting an active engagement in seeking knowledge or truth.
  • normayo: This translates to "not ordinary" or "extraordinary," indicating a higher state of being or understanding.
  • dhiyā: This means "intellect" or "understanding," emphasizing the role of wisdom in navigating life's challenges.
  • vājaṁ: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating the rewards of knowledge and devotion.
  • siṣāsataḥ: This means "to those who strive" or "to those who seek," highlighting the importance of effort and dedication in achieving one's goals.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the journey of awakening to deeper truths and the importance of selfless actions in navigating life's challenges. It emphasizes that true understanding comes from a combination of awareness, devotion, and intellectual engagement. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal growth, professional endeavors, and community service. By striving for knowledge and acting selflessly, individuals can overcome difficulties and achieve prosperity, both materially and spiritually. The mantra serves as a reminder that the pursuit of wisdom and the practice of devotion are essential for a fulfilling and meaningful life.
