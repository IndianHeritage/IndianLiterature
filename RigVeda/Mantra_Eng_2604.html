यथायजो होत्रम् अग्ने पृथिव्या यथा दिवो जातवेदश्चिकीत्वान् | एवानेन हविषा यक्षि देवान् मनुष्वद यज्ञं प्र तिरेमम अद्य ||
yathāyajo hotram agne pṛthivyā yathā divo jātavedaś cikitvān | evānena haviṣā yakṣi devān manuṣvad yajñam pra tiremam adya ||
  • yathāyajo hotram agne pṛthivyā: This phrase can be translated as "As the offerings are made, O Agni, on the earth." Here, Agni represents the divine fire and is a significant deity in Vedic rituals. The mention of "pṛthivyā" (earth) emphasizes the connection between the spiritual and the material world.
  • yathā divo jātavedaś cikitvān: This translates to "As the one who knows all, O Jātaveda, in the heavens." Jātaveda refers to the all-knowing aspect of Agni, who is believed to connect the earthly realm with the celestial. This line highlights the importance of knowledge and awareness in our actions.
  • evānena haviṣā yakṣi devān: This means "With this offering, may we please the gods." The term "haviṣā" refers to the offerings made during rituals. This line signifies the importance of devotion and the act of giving in order to receive blessings from the divine.
  • manuṣvad yajñam pra tiremam adya: This translates to "May we attain the benefits of the sacrifice today." The word "yajñam" refers to the sacrificial rituals that are performed to honor the gods. This line emphasizes the desire for the fruits of one's actions and the importance of performing rituals with sincerity.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of Vedic rituals, emphasizing the relationship between humans and the divine. It highlights the importance of offerings, knowledge, and the desire for blessings.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:
    • In personal development, the idea of making offerings can be seen as contributing positively to society, whether through charity, volunteering, or acts of kindness.
    • In professional settings, the emphasis on knowledge and awareness can be related to continuous learning and adapting to new challenges.
    • In spiritual practices, the act of performing rituals or meditative practices can be a way to connect with the divine and seek guidance.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of our actions. By recognizing the divine in our daily lives and performing our duties with sincerity, we can cultivate a harmonious existence and attract positive outcomes.
