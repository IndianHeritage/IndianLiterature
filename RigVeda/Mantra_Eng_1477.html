तस्मा आयुः प्रजावद इद बाधे आर्चन्त्य ओजसा |इन्द्र ओक्यं दिधिषन्त धीतयो देवाम् अच् छा न धीतयः ||
tasmā āyuḥ prajāvad id bādhe arcanty ojasā | indra okyaṁ didhiṣanta dhītayo devām̐ acchā na dhītayaḥ ||
  • tasmā āyuḥ prajāvad: This phrase can be translated as "therefore, life is to be cherished." It emphasizes the importance of life and the vitality that comes with it. In real life, this can be seen in how people celebrate milestones such as birthdays or anniversaries, recognizing the value of life and the experiences that come with it.
  • id bādhe arcanty ojasā: This part means "they who worship with strength." It suggests that those who dedicate themselves to their pursuits with vigor and devotion will be rewarded. For example, athletes who train rigorously for competitions often achieve great success, demonstrating the power of dedication and hard work.
  • indra okyaṁ didhiṣanta: This translates to "Indra, the lord of strength, grants." Indra is often seen as a deity who provides strength and support. In a modern context, this can relate to how individuals seek guidance and support from mentors or leaders in their fields, drawing strength from their wisdom and experience.
  • dhītayo devām̐ acchā na dhītayaḥ: This phrase means "the wise ones do not forsake the divine." It highlights the importance of wisdom and the connection to the divine or higher principles. In everyday life, this can be reflected in how individuals make ethical decisions, often guided by their values and beliefs, ensuring they do not stray from their moral compass.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of cherishing life, dedicating oneself to pursuits with strength, seeking guidance from powerful figures, and maintaining wisdom in alignment with divine principles. In real-life scenarios, these teachings can be applied in various aspects such as personal growth, professional development, and ethical decision-making. By embodying these principles, individuals can lead fulfilling lives that honor both their personal aspirations and the greater good.
