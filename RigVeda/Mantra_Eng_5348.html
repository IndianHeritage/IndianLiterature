ते त्वा मदा इन्द्र मदायन्तु शुष्मिणं तुविराधसं जरीत्रे |एको देवत्रा दयसे हि मर्तान अस्मिञ्छूर सवने मदायस्व ||
te tvā madā indra mādayantu śuṣmiṇaṁ tuvirādhasaṁ jaritre | eko devatrā dayase hi martān asmiñ chūra savane mādayasva ||
  • te tvā madā indra mādayantu: This phrase can be translated as "May the intoxication of the divine Indra inspire you." Here, "Indra" represents the king of the gods, often associated with strength, power, and the forces of nature. The term "madā" refers to a state of bliss or intoxication, suggesting a divine inspiration that elevates one's spirit.
  • śuṣmiṇaṁ tuvirādhasaṁ jaritre: This part can be interpreted as "In the one who is strong and powerful, may the divine energy flow." "Tuvirādhasaṁ" indicates strength and might, while "jaritre" refers to the act of bringing forth or manifesting. This suggests that true power comes from a divine source and is meant to be expressed in the world.
  • eko devatrā dayase hi martān: This translates to "One deity, through compassion, brings life to mortals." The word "eko" emphasizes the oneness of the divine, while "dayase" refers to compassion or kindness. This highlights the idea that the divine is not distant but actively involved in the lives of humans, providing support and sustenance.
  • asmiñ chūra savane mādayasva: This can be understood as "In this gathering, may you be inspired." "Chūra" refers to a gathering or assembly, and "savane" relates to offerings or sacrifices. This line emphasizes the importance of community and collective worship, suggesting that divine inspiration is amplified in the presence of others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine inspiration, strength, and community. It signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in our lives, especially in moments of challenge or uncertainty. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as during communal gatherings, rituals, or even personal moments of reflection. It encourages individuals to connect with the divine, draw strength from it, and share that inspiration with others. By invoking this mantra, one can foster a sense of unity, compassion, and empowerment within themselves and their community.
