क्व वः सुम्ना नव्यांसी मरुतः क्व सुवीता |क्वोऽऽ विश्वानि सौभगा ||
kva vaḥ sumnā navyāṁsi marutaḥ kva suvitā | kvo3 viśvāni saubhagā ||
  • Mantra: "kva vaḥ sumnā navyāṁsi marutaḥ kva suvitā | kvo3 viśvāni saubhagā ||"
  • Translation: "Where are your auspicious and new (blessings), O Maruts? Where are your well-formed (gifts)? Where are all the good fortunes?"

  • Word Breakdown:
    • kva: Where
    • vaḥ: Your
    • sumnā: Auspicious, good fortune
    • navyāṁsi: New, fresh
    • marutaḥ: O Maruts (the storm deities)
    • suvitā: Well-formed, well-made
    • viśvāni: All, every
    • saubhagā: Good fortune, prosperity

  • Contextual Explanation:
    • The Maruts are a group of storm deities in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, vitality, and the forces of nature.
    • This mantra expresses a yearning for blessings and prosperity, invoking the Maruts to bestow their gifts upon the seeker.
    • In real life, this can be seen as a call for positive energy and good fortune in various aspects of life, such as health, relationships, and career.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, an entrepreneur might recite this mantra before launching a new product, seeking the blessings of the Maruts for success and prosperity.
    • Individuals facing challenges in their personal lives may use this mantra as a form of meditation or prayer, asking for new beginnings and auspicious opportunities.
    • During festivals or significant life events, people may chant this mantra to invoke blessings for the well-being of their families and communities.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking blessings and good fortune in our lives.
    • It emphasizes the need for positivity and auspiciousness, encouraging individuals to remain open to new opportunities and blessings.
    • In a broader sense, it highlights the interconnectedness of nature and human life, urging us to acknowledge and appreciate the forces that contribute to our well-being.
