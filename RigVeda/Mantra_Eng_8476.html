पवस्व सोम क्रतुविन न उक्त्योऽव्यो वारे परि धावा मधु प्रियम् |जहि विश्वान रक्षस इन्दो अतृणो बृहद् वदेम विदथे सुवीराः ||
pavasva soma kratuvin na ukthyo 'vyo vāre pari dhāva madhu priyam | jahi viśvān rakṣasa indo atriṇo bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ ||
  • pavasva: This word can be interpreted as "purify" or "make pure." In a real-life context, this can relate to the idea of cleansing oneself of negative thoughts or actions, similar to how one might engage in meditation or mindfulness practices to achieve mental clarity.
  • soma: Soma refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. In modern terms, it can symbolize the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, akin to how people seek wisdom through education and personal growth.
  • kratuvin: This term means "one who has the power of will" or "intelligent." It emphasizes the importance of intention and determination in achieving goals, much like how successful individuals often set clear objectives and work diligently towards them.
  • na ukthyo 'vyo: This phrase can be translated as "not to be neglected." It suggests the importance of valuing and honoring one's commitments and responsibilities, similar to how individuals must prioritize their duties in both personal and professional life.
  • vāre: This word means "to protect" or "to guard." In everyday life, this can relate to the idea of safeguarding one's values, relationships, and well-being, much like how people take measures to ensure their safety and security.
  • pari dhāva: This phrase translates to "surrounding" or "encompassing." It can symbolize the idea of community and support, reflecting how individuals often rely on their social networks for encouragement and assistance in times of need.
  • madhu priyam: This means "sweet nectar" or "beloved." It signifies the pursuit of joy and happiness in life, akin to how people seek fulfilling experiences and relationships that bring them joy.
  • jahi viśvān rakṣasa: This can be interpreted as "overcome all obstacles." It emphasizes resilience and the ability to face challenges head-on, similar to how individuals often confront difficulties in their careers or personal lives with determination.
  • indo atriṇo: This phrase refers to "the divine" or "the celestial." It highlights the connection to higher powers or spiritual guidance, much like how people often seek inspiration from their beliefs or philosophies during tough times.
  • bṛhad vadema: This means "we speak of greatness." It encourages the expression of noble thoughts and actions, similar to how leaders inspire others through their vision and values.
  • vidathe suvīrāḥ: This phrase translates to "in the assembly of the brave." It signifies the importance of courage and collaboration, reflecting how teamwork and collective effort are essential in achieving significant goals.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of purification, intention, protection, community, joy, resilience, spiritual connection, greatness, and courage. In real-life scenarios, these themes are applicable in various aspects, such as personal development, professional endeavors, and social relationships. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate life's challenges more effectively and foster a sense of fulfillment and purpose. The mantra serves as a reminder to strive for purity of thought and action, to support one another, and to pursue greatness in all endeavors.
