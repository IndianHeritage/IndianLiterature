वृषा त्वा वृषणं हुवे वज्रीं चित्राभिर ऊतिभिः |वृषन्न इन्द्र वृषभिर वृत्रहन्तम ||
vṛṣā tvā vṛṣaṇaṁ huve vajriñ citrābhir ūtibhiḥ | vṛṣann indra vṛṣabhir vṛtrahantama ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vṛṣā: This word can be interpreted as "the strong" or "the powerful."
    • tvā: This means "you," referring to a deity or a powerful entity.
    • vṛṣaṇaṁ: This can be understood as "the one who is worthy of praise" or "the one who brings strength."
    • huve: This translates to "I invoke" or "I call upon."
    • vajriñ: This refers to "the one who wields the thunderbolt," often associated with Indra, the king of gods.
    • citrābhir: This means "with various" or "with colorful" offerings.
    • ūtibhiḥ: This translates to "with praises" or "with hymns."
    • vṛṣann: This can be interpreted as "the strong one" or "the powerful one."
    • indra: Refers to Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, symbolizing strength and power.
    • vṛṣabhir: This means "with strength" or "with powerful beings."
    • vṛtrahantama: This translates to "the one who slays Vritra," where Vritra symbolizes obstacles or evil.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to Indra, invoking his strength and power.
    • The speaker expresses a desire for Indra's assistance in overcoming challenges, symbolized by Vritra.
    • The use of colorful offerings and praises signifies the importance of devotion and respect in seeking divine help.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal struggle, individuals often seek strength from a higher power, much like invoking Indra for support.
    • In a community facing natural disasters, people may come together in prayer, similar to how this mantra calls upon Indra for protection and strength.
    • In sports, athletes often invoke a sense of inner strength and resilience, akin to calling upon divine support to overcome their challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of invoking divine strength in overcoming obstacles.
    • It highlights the importance of faith, devotion, and the collective effort in seeking help from a higher power.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder that we can call upon our inner strength and the support of our community when faced with challenges.
