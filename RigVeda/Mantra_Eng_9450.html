येब्यो होत्राम् प्रथमाम् आयेजे मनुः समिद्धाग्निर् मनसा सप्त होत्रिभिः |त आदित्याः अभयं शर्म यच्छत सुगा नः कर्त सुपथा स्वस्तये ||
yebhyo hotrām prathamām āyeje manuḥ samiddhāgnir manasā sapta hotṛbhiḥ | ta ādityā abhayaṁ śarma yacchata sugā naḥ karta supathā svastaye ||
  • yebhyo hotrām prathamām āyeje manuḥ: This phrase refers to the invocation of the first among the priests (hotrām) who perform the rituals. It emphasizes the importance of the priestly role in Vedic rituals, where the priest acts as a mediator between the divine and the human.
  • samiddhāgnir manasā sapta hotṛbhiḥ: Here, 'samiddhāgnir' refers to the fire that is kindled and ready for the ritual. 'Manasā' indicates the mental focus and intention behind the rituals. 'Sapta hotṛbhiḥ' signifies the seven priests who are involved in the ceremony, highlighting the collective effort in spiritual practices.
  • ta ādityā abhayaṁ śarma yacchata: This line calls upon the Adityas, the solar deities, to grant safety (abhayaṁ) and protection (śarma). It reflects the belief in divine guardianship and the need for spiritual and physical safety in life.
  • sugā naḥ karta supathā svastaye: This part expresses a desire for good actions (sugā) and the right path (supathā) leading to well-being (svastaye). It emphasizes the importance of righteous living and the pursuit of a prosperous life.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of Vedic rituals, the role of priests, and the invocation of divine protection and guidance. It highlights the interconnectedness of human efforts, spiritual practices, and divine blessings.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as:
    • Rituals and Ceremonies: It can be recited during important life events like weddings, housewarming ceremonies, or any significant undertaking to invoke blessings and ensure safety.
    • Mental Focus: The emphasis on mental intention can be related to modern practices like meditation and mindfulness, where focused intention is crucial for achieving goals.
    • Community and Collaboration: The mention of collective efforts by priests can be mirrored in community service and teamwork, where collaboration leads to greater outcomes.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of spiritual practices, community involvement, and the pursuit of a righteous path in life. It encourages individuals to seek divine guidance and protection while engaging in meaningful actions that contribute to personal and communal well-being.
