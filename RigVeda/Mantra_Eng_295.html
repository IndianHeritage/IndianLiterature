यच चिद्धि शाश्वता तना देवं-देवं यजामहे |त्वे इद धूयते हविः ||
yac cid dhi śaśvatā tanā devaṁ-devaṁ yajāmahe | tve id dhūyate haviḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yac: that which
    • cid: indeed
    • dhi: intellect or understanding
    • śaśvatā: eternal or everlasting
    • tanā: body or form
    • devaṁ-devaṁ: the divine, the divine one
    • yajāmahe: we worship or we offer
    • tve: to you
    • id: this
    • dhūyate: is offered or is invoked
    • haviḥ: the offering or sacrifice
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep reverence for the divine, acknowledging the eternal nature of the divine form.
    • It emphasizes the importance of worship and offerings, suggesting that through our understanding and devotion, we connect with the divine essence.
    • The phrase "we worship the divine" indicates a collective effort in spiritual practice, highlighting community and shared beliefs.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals and offerings are made to honor deities, such as lighting candles in churches or offering flowers at temples, reflecting the same spirit of devotion.
    • In personal life, individuals may engage in meditation or prayer, seeking to connect with a higher power, which aligns with the essence of this mantra.
    • Community gatherings, such as festivals or religious ceremonies, serve as a modern-day manifestation of collective worship, reinforcing the idea of unity in spirituality.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the divine presence in our lives and the eternal nature of that connection.
    • It teaches us that through worship and offerings, we can cultivate a deeper understanding of ourselves and our relationship with the universe.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals and communities to engage in practices that foster spiritual growth, unity, and a sense of purpose.
