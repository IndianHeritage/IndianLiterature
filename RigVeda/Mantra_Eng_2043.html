सरस्वती साधयन्ती धियं न इळा देवी भारती विश्वतूर्तिः |तिस्रो देविः स्वधया बर्हिर एदं अचिद्रम पान्तु शरणं निषद्य ||
sarasvatī sādhayantī dhiyaṁ na iḻā devī bhāratī viśvatūrtiḥ | tisro devīḥ svadhayā barhir edam acchidram pāntu śaraṇaṁ niṣadya ||
  • Sarasvatī: This refers to the goddess of knowledge, music, art, wisdom, and learning. In real life, Sarasvatī symbolizes the pursuit of education and the importance of knowledge in our lives. For example, students often pray to her for success in their studies.
  • Sādhayantī: This term means 'to accomplish' or 'to achieve.' It emphasizes the active pursuit of goals. In a practical sense, this can relate to individuals striving to achieve their dreams, whether in academics, career, or personal development.
  • Dhiyaṁ: This word translates to 'intellect' or 'understanding.' It highlights the importance of wisdom and discernment in decision-making. For instance, in a business context, having a clear understanding of market trends can lead to successful strategies.
  • Na iḻā: This phrase can be interpreted as 'not to be neglected' or 'not to be overlooked.' It serves as a reminder to value and respect knowledge and wisdom in our lives. For example, ignoring the advice of experienced mentors can lead to poor choices.
  • Devī: This term means 'goddess' and signifies the divine feminine energy. It represents the nurturing aspect of knowledge and creativity. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way educators and mentors guide and support their students.
  • Bhāratī: This refers to the goddess of speech and eloquence. It signifies the power of communication and expression. In real life, effective communication is crucial in various fields, such as public speaking, negotiations, and interpersonal relationships.
  • Viśvatūrtiḥ: This term means 'the one who encompasses all.' It suggests the universality of knowledge and its application across different domains. For example, interdisciplinary studies combine knowledge from various fields to solve complex problems.
  • Tisro devīḥ: This phrase indicates 'three goddesses,' which can symbolize the different aspects of knowledge: theoretical, practical, and creative. In real life, a well-rounded education incorporates all these aspects, preparing individuals for diverse challenges.
  • Svadhayā: This means 'by one's own effort.' It emphasizes the importance of self-initiative and personal responsibility in the pursuit of knowledge. For instance, self-study and continuous learning are essential for professional growth.
  • Barhir: This term refers to 'the outer world' or 'external reality.' It signifies the application of knowledge in real-life situations. For example, applying theoretical knowledge in practical scenarios, such as internships or projects, enhances learning.
  • Edam acchidram: This phrase translates to 'this is unbroken' or 'this is whole.' It suggests the completeness and integrity of knowledge. In real life, a holistic approach to education ensures that individuals are well-prepared for life's challenges.
  • Pāntu: This means 'may protect' or 'may nurture.' It signifies the protective aspect of knowledge, guiding individuals through life's uncertainties. For example, knowledge can empower individuals to make informed decisions and avoid pitfalls.
  • Śaraṇaṁ: This term means 'refuge' or 'shelter.' It indicates that knowledge serves as a safe haven in times of confusion or difficulty. In real life, turning to books, mentors, or educational resources can provide clarity and direction.
  • Niṣadya: This means 'to sit' or 'to settle.' It suggests a state of contemplation and reflection. In practice, taking time to reflect on one's learning and experiences can lead to deeper understanding and personal growth.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of knowledge, wisdom, and the pursuit of learning in our lives. It highlights the importance of self-initiative, effective communication, and the application of knowledge in real-world scenarios. In today's fast-paced world, this mantra serves as a reminder to value education, seek knowledge actively, and apply it thoughtfully to navigate life's challenges. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can foster personal growth, contribute positively to society, and achieve their aspirations.
