अव चष्ट ऋचिषमो 'वताम् इव मानवः | जुष्ट्वी दक्षस्य सोमिनः सखायं कृतुते युजम् भद्रा इन्द्रस्य रातयः ||
ava caṣṭa ṛcīṣamo 'vatām̐ iva mānuṣaḥ | juṣṭvī dakṣasya sominaḥ sakhāyaṁ kṛṇute yujam bhadrā indrasya rātayaḥ ||
  • ava caṣṭa: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we be protected" or "may we be shielded." It emphasizes the need for safety and security in our lives.
  • ṛcīṣamo 'vatām̐: This translates to "like the human beings." It suggests a connection to humanity and the shared experiences of people.
  • iva mānuṣaḥ: This means "like humans." It reinforces the idea of human qualities and the importance of human relationships.
  • juṣṭvī dakṣasya sominaḥ: This can be understood as "the one who is skilled and wise." It highlights the value of wisdom and skill in achieving success.
  • sakhāyaṁ kṛṇute: This translates to "creates friendships." It emphasizes the importance of building relationships and connections with others.
  • yujam bhadrā indrasya: This means "the auspicious union of Indra." Indra is often associated with strength and power, suggesting that auspiciousness comes from strong alliances.
  • rātayaḥ: This refers to "nights" or "journeys." It symbolizes the passage of time and the experiences we gather along the way.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of protection, human connection, wisdom, and the creation of friendships. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as fostering teamwork in a workplace, building strong family bonds, or creating supportive communities. The mantra serves as a reminder that our strength lies in our relationships and the wisdom we share, guiding us through the journeys of life with auspiciousness and safety.
