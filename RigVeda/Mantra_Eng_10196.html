ऊर्ध्वो गन्धर्वो अधि नाके अस्तात प्रत्यङ् चित्रा बिभ्रद अस्यायुधानि | वसानो अटकं सुरभिं दृष्टे कं स्वर णा नाम जनता प्रियाणि ||
ūrdhvo gandharvo adhi nāke asthāt pratyaṅ citrā bibhrad asyāyudhāni | vasāno atkaṁ surabhiṁ dṛśe kaṁ sva1r ṇa nāma janata priyāṇi ||
  • ūrdhvo: This word translates to "above" or "upward." It signifies elevation, both in a physical and spiritual sense.
  • gandharvo: Refers to "Gandharvas," celestial musicians in Hindu mythology, symbolizing beauty, art, and the divine connection through music.
  • adhi: Means "over" or "upon," indicating a position of superiority or dominance.
  • nāke: Translates to "in the sky" or "heavens," representing the spiritual realm or higher consciousness.
  • asthāt: Means "stood" or "established," indicating a firm presence or existence.
  • pratyaṅ: This word means "in front" or "before," suggesting a sense of direction or purpose.
  • citrā: Translates to "colorful" or "variegated," symbolizing diversity and richness in life.
  • bibhrad: Means "holding" or "bearing," indicating possession or control over something.
  • asyāyudhāni: Refers to "his weapons," symbolizing strength, protection, and the tools necessary for overcoming challenges.
  • vasāno: Means "dwelling" or "residing," indicating a place of comfort or belonging.
  • atkaṁ: Translates to "the best" or "the finest," suggesting excellence in quality.
  • surabhiṁ: Refers to "fragrance" or "sweetness," symbolizing positivity and pleasant experiences.
  • dṛśe: Means "to the sight" or "visible," indicating perception and awareness.
  • kaṁ: Translates to "who" or "whom," suggesting inquiry or seeking understanding.
  • sva1r: Refers to "sound" or "voice," symbolizing communication and expression.
  • ṇa: A negation, often used to indicate absence or denial.
  • nāma: Means "name," representing identity and recognition.
  • janata: Refers to "people" or "mankind," indicating the collective human experience.
  • priyāṇi: Means "beloved" or "dear," symbolizing affection and connection.

This mantra speaks to the elevation of the spirit and the beauty of existence. It emphasizes the importance of art, music, and the divine connection that uplifts humanity. The imagery of Gandharvas in the heavens suggests that there is a higher purpose and beauty in life that we can aspire to reach. The mention of weapons symbolizes the strength we need to face life's challenges, while the fragrance and sweetness represent the positive experiences that enrich our lives.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In artistic endeavors, where creativity and beauty are celebrated, reminding us to seek inspiration from the divine.
  • In personal development, where we strive to elevate our consciousness and overcome challenges with strength and resilience.
  • In community building, where we recognize the importance of connection and affection among people, fostering a sense of belonging.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call to elevate our lives through beauty, strength, and connection. It encourages us to seek higher purposes, embrace diversity, and cultivate positive experiences in our daily lives. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling existence.
