इन्द्रस्य सोम पवमान उर्मिणा तविष्यमाणो जठरेष्वा विशा |प्राणः पिन्व विद्युदभ्रेव रोधसी धिया न वाजाम् उप मासि शाश्वतः ||
indrasya soma pavamāna ūrmiṇā taviṣyamāṇo jaṭhareṣv ā viśa | pra ṇaḥ pinva vidyud abhreva rodasī dhiyā na vājām̐ upa māsi śaśvataḥ ||
  • Indrasya: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength and power.
  • Soma: A sacred drink in Vedic rituals, representing divine inspiration and immortality.
  • Pavamāna: Means "purifying" or "sublime," indicating the cleansing and elevating nature of Soma.
  • Ūrmiṇā: Translates to "with waves," suggesting the dynamic and flowing nature of life and energy.
  • Taviṣyamāṇo: Means "being energized" or "being empowered," indicating the activation of potential.
  • Jaṭhareṣv: Refers to "in the bellies," symbolizing the inner self or core of being.
  • Ā viśa: Means "enter," suggesting the idea of internalizing or assimilating knowledge and energy.
  • Pra ṇaḥ: Refers to "life force" or "vital energy," essential for existence.
  • Pinva: Means "to fill" or "to nourish," indicating the act of providing sustenance.
  • Vidyud: Translates to "lightning," symbolizing sudden illumination or insight.
  • Abhreva: Means "like a cloud," suggesting the vastness and potential of the mind.
  • Rodasī: Refers to "the two worlds" or "the heavens and the earth," indicating the connection between the material and spiritual realms.
  • Dhiyā: Means "with intellect" or "with understanding," emphasizing the importance of wisdom.
  • Na: Means "not," indicating negation or absence.
  • Vājām̐: Refers to "the best," symbolizing excellence or superiority.
  • Upa: Means "towards" or "near," indicating proximity or approach.
  • Māsi: Means "to be present" or "to exist," suggesting continuity.
  • Śaśvataḥ: Means "eternal" or "everlasting," indicating the timeless nature of the essence being referred to.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine energy and the life force that nourishes and empowers us. It highlights the connection between the physical and spiritual realms, urging individuals to internalize wisdom and seek enlightenment. In real life, this can be applied through practices such as meditation, yoga, and self-reflection, which help individuals tap into their inner strength and potential. The mantra serves as a reminder of the eternal nature of knowledge and the importance of nurturing our intellect and spirit for a fulfilling life.
