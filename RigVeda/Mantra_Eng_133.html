अव सृजा वनस्पते देव देवभ्यो हविः |प्र दातुर अस्तु चेतनम् ||
ava sṛjā vanaspate deva devebhyo haviḥ | pra dātur astu cetanam ||
  • ava sṛjā vanaspate: This phrase can be translated as "O Vanaspati (Lord of the Forest), create or manifest." Here, "ava" means to create or bring forth, and "vanaspate" refers to the deity associated with vegetation and plant life. In real life, this can be seen as a call for the growth and flourishing of nature, emphasizing the importance of trees and plants in our ecosystem.
  • deva devebhyo haviḥ: This translates to "offerings to the gods." "Deva" means god, and "haviḥ" refers to offerings or sacrifices. This part of the mantra highlights the significance of gratitude and reverence towards divine forces. In practical terms, it can be related to the act of giving back to nature and the community, such as through environmental conservation efforts or charitable acts.
  • pra dātur astu cetanam: This means "may there be consciousness or awareness." "Pra" signifies abundance or fullness, "dātur" refers to the giver, and "cetanam" means consciousness or awareness. This part of the mantra emphasizes the importance of being aware and conscious of our actions and their impact on the world around us. In everyday life, this can be applied to mindfulness practices, encouraging individuals to be present and considerate in their interactions with others and the environment.

  • Significance of the Mantra: This mantra encapsulates a deep respect for nature and the divine, urging individuals to recognize the interconnectedness of all life. It serves as a reminder to nurture our environment and to be conscious of our contributions to the world.
  • Real-Life Application: The principles found in this mantra can be applied in various ways, such as:
    • Engaging in sustainable practices, like planting trees and supporting biodiversity.
    • Participating in community service or charitable activities that benefit others.
    • Practicing mindfulness and awareness in daily life, fostering a deeper connection with oneself and the environment.
