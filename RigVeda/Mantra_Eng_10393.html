त्वं इन्द्राभिभूर् असि विश्वा जातानि ओजसा |स विश्वा भुव आभवः ||
tvam indrābhibhūr asi viśvā jātāny ojasā | sa viśvā bhuva ābhavaḥ ||
  • tvam - You
  • indrābhibhūr - are the one who is powerful like Indra
  • asi - are
  • viśvā - all
  • jātāny - beings or creatures
  • ojasā - with energy or strength
  • sa - that
  • viśvā - all
  • bhuva - existence or being
  • ābhavaḥ - may you manifest or become

The mantra "tvam indrābhibhūr asi viśvā jātāny ojasā | sa viśvā bhuva ābhavaḥ ||" can be translated to mean: "You are powerful like Indra, the one who energizes all beings; may you manifest in all existence." This mantra invokes the strength and energy associated with the deity Indra, who is often regarded as the king of the gods and a symbol of power and vitality.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Empowerment: Just as Indra represents strength and power, individuals can draw inspiration from this mantra to harness their inner strength and overcome challenges. For example, someone facing a difficult situation at work can recite this mantra to remind themselves of their capabilities and to approach the challenge with confidence.
  • Collective Strength: The mention of "all beings" emphasizes the importance of community and collective energy. In a team setting, this mantra can serve as a reminder that when individuals come together, they can achieve greater things. For instance, a sports team might use this mantra to foster unity and motivation before a big game.
  • Manifestation of Potential: The phrase "may you manifest in all existence" encourages individuals to realize their potential and contribute positively to the world around them. This can be seen in social initiatives where individuals work together to create change, embodying the spirit of the mantra.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of strength, unity, and the potential for manifestation in both personal and collective contexts. It serves as a powerful reminder that we all possess the energy to overcome obstacles and contribute to the greater good, much like the deity Indra. By embodying these qualities, individuals can navigate life's challenges with resilience and purpose.
