यो न इन्द्राभिदासति सनाभिर्यश्च निष्ट्यः |अव तस्य बलं तिर महीव द्यौर अध तमनā नभन्ताम् अन्यकेषां ज्याकाद अधि धन्वसु ||
yo na indrābhidāsati sanābhir yaś ca niṣṭyaḥ | ava tasya balaṁ tira mahīva dyaur adha tmanā nabhantām anyakeṣāṁ jyākā adhi dhanvasu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • na: not
    • indrābhidāsati: is not overcome by Indra
    • sanābhir: with the ancient ones
    • yaś: who
    • ca: and
    • niṣṭyaḥ: steadfast
    • ava: may he
    • tasya: of him
    • balaṁ: strength
    • tira: may it flow
    • mahīva: like the earth
    • dyaur: heaven
    • adha: below
    • tmanā: with the self
    • nabhantām: may it not be hindered
    • anyakeṣāṁ: of others
    • jyākā: may it shine
    • adhi: upon
    • dhanvasu: the bow (or strength)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a person who is not overcome by the challenges posed by Indra, the king of gods, and remains steadfast like the ancient ones.
    • It emphasizes the importance of inner strength and resilience, suggesting that true power comes from within and is not easily swayed by external forces.
    • The imagery of strength flowing like the earth and shining like the heavens indicates a balance between groundedness and aspiration.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, an employee who remains steadfast in their values and work ethic, despite pressures from management or competition, embodies the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal life, someone facing adversity, such as illness or loss, who draws upon their inner strength and support from loved ones, reflects the resilience described in the mantra.
    • In sports, an athlete who maintains focus and determination in the face of challenges, not allowing external distractions to affect their performance, exemplifies the steadfastness highlighted in this verse.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for inner strength and resilience in the face of challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that true power is derived from within and that one should remain steadfast in their beliefs and values.
    • This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, encouraging individuals to cultivate inner strength and remain undeterred by external pressures.
