त्वं तान सं च प्रति चासि मजनानाग्ने सुजात प्र च देव रिच्यसे |पृक्षो यद अत्र महिना वि ते भुवद अनु dyावापृथिवी रोडसी उभे ||
tvaṁ tān saṁ ca prati cāsi majmanāgne sujāta pra ca deva ricyase | pṛkṣo yad atra mahinā vi te bhuvad anu dyāvāpṛthivī rodasī ubhe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • tān: Those
    • saṁ: Together
    • ca: And
    • prati: Towards
    • cāsi: You are
    • majmanā: Of the offerings
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • su-jāta: Well-born
    • pra: For
    • ca: And
    • deva: O divine
    • ricyase: You are praised
    • pṛkṣo: As you are seen
    • yad: That
    • atra: Here
    • mahinā: With greatness
    • vi: In
    • te: Your
    • bhuvad: Being
    • anu: Following
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: Heaven and Earth
    • rodasī: The two realms
    • ubhe: Both
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Agni, the fire god, acknowledging his role in the universe.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the divine and the earthly realms, highlighting the importance of offerings and rituals.
    • The phrase "you are praised" signifies the reverence and respect given to Agni, who is seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The mention of "Heaven and Earth" reflects the duality of existence and the balance that must be maintained in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, lighting a fire during ceremonies symbolizes the presence of Agni, representing purification and the divine connection.
    • In many cultures, fire is used in offerings to seek blessings, similar to how Agni is invoked in the RigVeda.
    • The concept of balance between heaven and earth can be seen in environmental practices, where respecting nature is crucial for sustainability.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the divine in everyday life and the need for balance between the spiritual and material worlds.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the interconnectedness of all beings and the significance of our actions in maintaining harmony.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in practices that honor both the divine and the natural world, fostering a sense of responsibility and reverence.
