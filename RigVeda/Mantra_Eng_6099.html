पुरुप्रियā ण ūtaye पुरुमन्द्रā पुरूवासū |स्तुषे कण्वासो अश्विनā ||
purupriyā ṇa ūtaye purumandrā purūvasū | stuṣe kaṇvāso aśvinā ||
  • Purupriyā: This term can be broken down into "puru" meaning "man" or "human" and "priyā" meaning "beloved" or "dear." It signifies the idea of a beloved person or entity, often referring to the divine or a cherished aspect of life.
  • Ṇa ūtaye: "Ṇa" can be interpreted as "not" or "without," while "ūtaye" refers to "to be" or "to exist." Together, this phrase suggests a state of being that is devoid of something, possibly indicating a longing or a desire for connection.
  • Purumandrā: "Purumandrā" can be understood as "the great one" or "the supreme being." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing a higher power or a greater truth in our lives.
  • Purūvasū: This term can be translated to mean "the one who is rich in resources" or "abundant." It reflects the idea of prosperity and the blessings that come from the divine.
  • Stuṣe: This word means "to praise" or "to worship." It highlights the significance of devotion and the act of honoring the divine or the beloved.
  • Kaṇvāso: "Kaṇvāso" refers to "the wise" or "the learned." It suggests that wisdom and knowledge are essential in understanding and connecting with the divine.
  • Aśvinā: The Aśvins are twin deities in Vedic mythology, often associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between opposites.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, wisdom, and the pursuit of a deeper connection with the divine. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing the beloved aspects of life, whether they be people, experiences, or the divine itself. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of reflection, prayer, or meditation, encouraging individuals to seek wisdom and abundance while fostering a sense of gratitude and reverence for the blessings they have. By acknowledging the divine and the beloved in our lives, we cultivate a deeper understanding of our purpose and the interconnectedness of all beings.
