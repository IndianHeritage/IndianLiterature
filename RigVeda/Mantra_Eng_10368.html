श्रुधी हवम् इन्द्र शूर पृथ्या उता स्तवसे वेन्यस्यार्कैः |आ यस ते योनिं घृतवन्तम् अस्वार ऊर्मिर न निम्नैर द्रवयन्त वक्वाः ||
śrudhī havam indra śūra pṛthyā uta stavase venyasyārkaiḥ | ā yas te yoniṁ ghṛtavantam asvār ūrmir na nimnair dravayanta vakvāḥ ||
  • śrudhī havam indra: This phrase can be translated as "We call upon you, Indra, with our offerings." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. In real life, this can be likened to how people seek divine intervention or blessings in times of need, such as during a drought when farmers pray for rain.
  • śūra pṛthyā uta: This means "the brave one, the protector of the earth." It emphasizes the qualities of bravery and protection. In contemporary terms, this can relate to leaders or individuals who stand up for their communities, much like activists who fight for environmental protection or social justice.
  • stavase venyasyārkaiḥ: This translates to "we praise you with our hymns." It highlights the importance of worship and reverence. In modern life, this can be seen in how people express gratitude and appreciation, whether through rituals, music, or art, to honor those who have made significant contributions to society.
  • ā yas te yoniṁ ghṛtavantam: This means "may your source be rich in ghee." Ghee is a symbol of purity and nourishment in Vedic culture. In today's context, this can represent the importance of nurturing our roots and traditions while also ensuring that we have the resources needed for growth and prosperity, similar to how families invest in education and health for future generations.
  • asvār ūrmir na nimnair: This translates to "the waves of the sea do not diminish." It signifies resilience and strength. In real life, this can be compared to individuals or communities that face challenges but continue to thrive, much like how certain ecosystems adapt and flourish despite environmental changes.
  • dravayanta vakvāḥ: This means "the words that flow." It suggests the power of communication and expression. In today's world, this can relate to how social media and other platforms allow voices to be heard, enabling movements for change and awareness on various issues.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of divine invocation, bravery, praise, nourishment, resilience, and the power of words. Its significance lies in the reminder of the interconnectedness of these elements in our lives. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek strength and support from higher powers, to stand up for what is right, to express gratitude, and to remain resilient in the face of adversity. This mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as during community gatherings, environmental campaigns, or personal reflections, serving as a source of inspiration and guidance.
