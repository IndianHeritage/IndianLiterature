नैतावद अन्ये मरुतो यथेमा भ्राजन्ते रुक्मैर आयुधैस तनुभिः |आ रोडसी विश्वपिशः पिशानाः समानम अञ्ज्य अञ्जते शुभे कम ||
naitāvad anye maruto yatheme bhrājante rukmair āyudhais tanūbhiḥ | ā rodasī viśvapiśaḥ piśānāḥ samānam añjy añjate śubhe kam ||
  • Translation: "Not by these (weapons) do the Maruts shine forth, with their golden armaments, in the battle. The two worlds, the all-pervading, are filled with the auspicious ones who are united in harmony."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Naitāvad: Not by these
    • anye: others
    • maruto: the Maruts (storm deities)
    • yatheme: where they shine forth
    • bhrājante: shine or radiate
    • rukmair: golden
    • āyudhais: with weapons
    • tanūbhiḥ: with their bodies
    • ā rodasī: the two worlds
    • viśvapiśaḥ: all-pervading
    • piśānāḥ: auspicious ones
    • samānam: united
    • añjy: in harmony
    • añjate: are united
    • śubhe: auspicious
    • kam: who
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the idea that true strength and radiance do not come from physical weapons or material possessions, but rather from unity and harmony among individuals.
    • The Maruts, representing the forces of nature, are depicted as powerful yet not reliant on weapons, suggesting that their might comes from their collective energy and purpose.
    • In real life, this can be seen in various scenarios such as teamwork in a corporate environment, where collaboration and mutual support lead to success rather than individual competition.
    • Another example can be found in community efforts during crises, such as natural disasters, where people come together to support one another, showcasing the power of unity over individual strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra teaches us that the essence of strength lies in cooperation and shared goals rather than in materialistic pursuits or individual accolades.
    • It encourages us to foster relationships and build communities based on mutual respect and harmony, which can lead to a more prosperous and peaceful existence.
    • In a world often divided by competition and conflict, this message is particularly relevant, reminding us to seek unity and collaboration in our personal and professional lives.
