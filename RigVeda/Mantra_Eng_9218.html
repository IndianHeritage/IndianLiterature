जनीष्टा योषा पतयत कनिनको वि चाऽरुहन वीरुधो दंशना अनु |आस्मै रीयन्ते निवनेव सिन्धवोऽस्मा अह्ने भवति तत् पतित्वनम् ||
janiṣṭa yoṣā patayat kanīnako vi cāruhan vīrudho daṁsanā anu | āsmai rīyante nivaneva sindhavo 'smā ahne bhavati tat patitvanam ||
  • janiṣṭa: This word translates to "born" or "created." It signifies the beginning of life or existence.
  • yoṣā: Refers to "woman" or "female." In the context of the mantra, it highlights the importance of femininity and the role of women in creation and sustenance.
  • patayat: This means "to fly" or "to soar." It can symbolize freedom, aspiration, and the ability to rise above challenges.
  • kanīnako: This term can be interpreted as "young" or "youthful." It emphasizes vitality and the potential that comes with youth.
  • vi cāruhan: This phrase can be understood as "beautifully adorned" or "graceful." It suggests the aesthetic and spiritual beauty that surrounds us.
  • vīrudho: This word means "strong" or "powerful." It represents resilience and the strength needed to overcome obstacles.
  • daṁsanā: This translates to "with teeth" or "biting." It can symbolize the challenges or struggles that one must face in life.
  • anu: This word means "after" or "following." It indicates a sequence or a journey that one undertakes.
  • āsmai: This means "to him" or "to that one." It signifies a direction or a focus towards a particular goal or entity.
  • rīyante: This translates to "they flow" or "they move." It can represent the dynamic nature of life and experiences.
  • nivaneva: This means "like a home" or "as in a dwelling." It emphasizes comfort, safety, and belonging.
  • sindhavo: This word refers to "rivers" or "ocean." It symbolizes abundance, nourishment, and the flow of life.
  • 'smā: This means "we" or "us." It indicates a collective experience or shared journey.
  • ahne: This translates to "in the day" or "during the daytime." It signifies clarity, awareness, and the active phase of life.
  • bhavati: This means "becomes" or "is." It indicates transformation and the process of becoming.
  • tat: This translates to "that." It signifies a connection to something greater or a higher purpose.
  • patitvanam: This word means "falling" or "descending." It can symbolize challenges, setbacks, or the cyclical nature of life.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the journey of life, emphasizing the roles of femininity, strength, and beauty in overcoming challenges. It reflects the dynamic flow of experiences, akin to rivers nourishing the land. In real life, this can be seen in the resilience of women who rise above societal challenges, the beauty found in diversity, and the strength required to navigate life's obstacles. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of embracing both the struggles and the joys of existence. It encourages us to recognize our potential and the beauty in our journeys, ultimately guiding us towards a deeper understanding of ourselves and our place in the world.
