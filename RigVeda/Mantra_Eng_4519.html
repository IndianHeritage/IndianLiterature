मन्त्र: क्रत्वा दा अस्तु श्रेष्ठो 'द्या त्वा वंवन सुरेक्षणाः | मर्त आनााश सुवृक्तिम ||
kratvā dā astu śreṣṭho 'dya tvā vanvan surekṇāḥ | marta ānāśa suvṛktim ||
  • kratvā: This word can be understood as "by action" or "through effort." It emphasizes the importance of taking initiative and being proactive in one's endeavors.
  • dā: This translates to "gift" or "offering." It signifies the act of giving, which is a fundamental aspect of many cultures and religions, highlighting generosity and selflessness.
  • astu: Meaning "let it be" or "may it be," this word conveys a sense of acceptance and willingness to embrace outcomes, whether favorable or not.
  • śreṣṭho: This means "the best" or "the highest." It denotes the pursuit of excellence and the aspiration to achieve the utmost in one's actions and intentions.
  • 'dya: This word translates to "today," indicating the immediacy of the actions and intentions being discussed. It emphasizes the importance of the present moment.
  • tvā: Meaning "you," this word personalizes the mantra, directing the intention towards an individual or a deity, making it a personal invocation.
  • vanvan: This can be interpreted as "to the one who is worthy" or "to the one who is deserving," suggesting a recognition of merit and virtue.
  • surekṇāḥ: This term refers to "the divine" or "the celestial," indicating a connection to higher powers or spiritual beings that guide and protect.
  • marta: This word means "mortal" or "human," reminding us of our human condition and the transient nature of life.
  • ānāśa: This translates to "immortal" or "eternal," contrasting with the previous term and highlighting the quest for lasting significance beyond physical existence.
  • suvṛktim: This means "good conduct" or "virtuous behavior," emphasizing the importance of ethical living and moral integrity in one's actions.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates a profound philosophy of life that encourages individuals to take action with the intention of giving and striving for excellence. It highlights the importance of living in the present while recognizing the duality of human existence—our mortal nature and the pursuit of immortality through virtuous actions. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal development, community service, and spiritual practices. By embodying the principles of generosity, excellence, and ethical conduct, individuals can contribute positively to society and foster a sense of connection with the divine. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to live purposefully and with integrity, making each action count towards a greater good.
