सप्तार्धगर्भा भुवनस्य रेतो विष्णोः तिष्ठन्ति प्रदिशा विधर्मणि |ते धीतिभिर मनसा ते विपश्चितः परिभुवः परि भवन्ति विश्वतः ||
saptārdhagarbhā bhuvanasya reto viṣṇos tiṣṭhanti pradiśā vidharmaṇi | te dhītibhir manasā te vipaścitaḥ paribhuvaḥ pari bhavanti viśvataḥ ||
  • Mantra: "saptārdhagarbhā bhuvanasya reto viṣṇos tiṣṭhanti pradiśā vidharmaṇi | te dhītibhir manasā te vipaścitaḥ paribhuvaḥ pari bhavanti viśvataḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • saptārdhagarbhā: Refers to the sevenfold essence or the seven layers of existence.
    • bhuvanasya: Pertaining to the universe or the world.
    • reto: The creative energy or seed.
    • viṣṇos: Of Lord Vishnu, the preserver in Hindu mythology.
    • tiṣṭhanti: They stand or exist.
    • pradiśā: Directions or realms.
    • vidharmaṇi: In accordance with righteousness or dharma.
    • te: They.
    • dhītibhir: By wisdom or intellect.
    • manasā: With the mind.
    • vipaścitaḥ: The wise or the enlightened ones.
    • paribhuvaḥ: They encompass or embrace.
    • pari: All around or completely.
    • bhavanti: They become or manifest.
    • viśvataḥ: From all sides or universally.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the fundamental aspects of existence and the divine order that governs the universe.
    • The phrase "saptārdhagarbhā" suggests that there are multiple layers or dimensions to reality, much like how we perceive different aspects of life—physical, emotional, intellectual, and spiritual.
    • The reference to "bhuvanasya reto" indicates that the creative energy of the universe is rooted in a divine source, akin to how nature operates through cycles of creation and destruction.
    • When it mentions "viṣṇos tiṣṭhanti," it emphasizes the role of divine preservation, suggesting that all forms of life and existence are sustained by a higher power.
    • The directions or realms ("pradiśā") signify the various paths one can take in life, each governed by the principles of dharma, or righteousness.
    • The wise ("vipaścitaḥ") are those who understand these principles and navigate life with insight and awareness, leading to a more harmonious existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, understanding the "seven layers" can relate to the different departments working together—finance, marketing, operations, etc.—to create a successful business.
    • In personal relationships, recognizing the various dimensions of communication and emotional intelligence can lead to healthier interactions.
    • In environmental conservation, the mantra can inspire individuals to respect the interconnectedness of ecosystems and the importance of sustainable practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of understanding the multifaceted nature of existence and the importance of aligning oneself with divine principles.
    • It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and act in accordance with dharma, leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
    • In essence, it serves as a reminder of our interconnectedness and the need to embrace a holistic approach to life, applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to societal development.
