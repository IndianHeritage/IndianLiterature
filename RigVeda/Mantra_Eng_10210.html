मयā सो अन्नम् अत्ति यो विपश्यति यः प्राणिति य ईं श्रिणोति उक्तम् |अमन्तवो मां त उप क्षियन्ति श्रुधि श्रुत श्रद्दिवं ते वदामि ||
mayā so annam atti yo vipaśyati yaḥ prāṇiti ya īṁ śṛṇoty uktam | amantavo māṁ ta upa kṣiyanti śrudhi śruta śraddhivaṁ te vadāmi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mayā: by me
    • so: that
    • annam: food
    • atti: eats
    • yo: who
    • vipaśyati: sees or understands
    • yaḥ: who
    • prāṇiti: breathes or lives
    • ya: who
    • īṁ: this
    • śṛṇoty: hears
    • uktam: spoken or said
    • amantavo: those who are wise
    • māṁ: me
    • ta: they
    • upa: near
    • kṣiyanti: diminish or reduce
    • śrudhi: listen
    • śruta: heard
    • śraddhivaṁ: with faith
    • te: you
    • vadāmi: I say
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of understanding and appreciating the sustenance we receive.
    • It suggests that those who truly see, hear, and understand the essence of food and life are the ones who truly live.
    • The wise individuals who listen and have faith in the teachings will not diminish the value of what they receive.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be related to mindful eating, where individuals are encouraged to appreciate the food they consume, understanding its source and nutritional value.
    • In a broader sense, it can apply to how we approach life and relationships, emphasizing the importance of being present and aware of our surroundings and interactions.
    • For instance, in a workplace, employees who actively listen and engage with their tasks and colleagues tend to perform better and foster a positive environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for awareness and appreciation of life’s essentials.
    • It teaches us that true nourishment comes not just from food but from understanding and valuing our experiences.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more fulfilling and enriched existence, encouraging us to live mindfully and with gratitude.
