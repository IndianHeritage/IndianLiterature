मन्त्र: चत्वारि ते असुर्याणि नामादाभ्याणि महिषस्य सन्ति |त्वम् अङ्ग तानि विश्वानि विट्से येभिः कर्माणि मगवञ्च चकर्थ ||
catvāri te asuryāṇi nāmādābhyāni mahiṣasya santi | tvam aṅga tāni viśvāni vitse yebhiḥ karmāṇi maghavañ cakartha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • catvāri: four
    • te: your
    • asuryāṇi: demonic or negative forces
    • nāmādābhyāni: by names or titles
    • mahiṣasya: of the great one (often referring to a powerful entity)
    • santi: exist
    • tvam: you
    • aṅga: O part or aspect
    • tāni: those
    • viśvāni: all or universal
    • vitse: you conquer
    • yebhiḥ: by whom
    • karmāṇi: actions or deeds
    • maghavañ: O mighty one
    • cakartha: you have accomplished
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the existence of four negative forces that can hinder progress and success.
    • It emphasizes the power of the individual (often referred to as a divine or mighty being) to overcome these forces through their actions.
    • The term "mahiṣasya" can be interpreted as referring to a great entity or the self, indicating that the strength to conquer lies within.
    • In real life, this can be related to personal challenges such as fear, doubt, procrastination, and negativity that can prevent one from achieving their goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Consider a student preparing for exams. They may face internal doubts (the four negative forces) that can hinder their performance. By recognizing these doubts and actively working to overcome them through study and practice, they embody the essence of this mantra.
    • A professional facing workplace challenges may encounter negativity from colleagues or self-doubt. By focusing on their skills and taking decisive actions, they can conquer these obstacles, reflecting the mantra's message.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its encouragement to recognize and confront the negative forces in our lives.
    • It teaches that through conscious actions and determination, one can overcome challenges and achieve success.
    • This mantra is applicable in various life scenarios, including education, career, and personal development, serving as a reminder of the inner strength we possess to conquer adversity.
