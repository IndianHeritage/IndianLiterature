उद आगाद अयम् आदित्यो विश्वेन सहसा सह |द्विषन्तम् मह्यं रन्धयन मो अहं द्विषते रधम् ||
ud agād ayam ādityo viśvena sahasā saha | dviṣantam mahyaṁ randhayan mo ahaṁ dviṣate radham ||
  • ud agād: This phrase can be translated as "has risen" or "has emerged." It signifies the arrival of light or enlightenment, much like the dawn breaking after a long night.
  • ayam ādityo: Referring to "this sun" or "the sun," it symbolizes vitality, energy, and the source of life. The sun is often seen as a powerful force that sustains life on Earth.
  • viśvena sahasā saha: This can be interpreted as "with all its might" or "with universal strength." It emphasizes the idea of collective power and the strength that comes from unity.
  • dviṣantam: Meaning "to the one who hates" or "to the adversary," this word introduces the concept of opposition or conflict, which is a natural part of life.
  • mahyaṁ randhayan: This translates to "wounding me" or "attacking me." It reflects the challenges and adversities one may face in life.
  • mo ahaṁ dviṣate radham: This phrase can be understood as "I do not desire the chariot of the one who hates." It suggests a rejection of negativity and harmful influences.

In essence, this mantra speaks to the triumph of light over darkness, the power of unity, and the importance of rejecting negativity. It encourages individuals to embrace positivity and strength while acknowledging the existence of adversities.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its message of resilience and empowerment. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied when facing challenges, whether in personal relationships, professional environments, or societal issues. By focusing on collective strength and rejecting negativity, individuals can foster a more positive and harmonious existence. This mantra serves as a reminder to rise above conflicts and embrace the light of unity and strength in our daily lives.
