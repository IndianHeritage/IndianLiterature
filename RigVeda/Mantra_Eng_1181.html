यद अब्रवम् प्रथमं वां वृणानोऽयं सोमो असुरैर नो विहव्यः |तां सत्यां श्रद्धाम अभ्याहि यातम अथ सोमस्य पिबतं सुतस्य ||
yad abravam prathamaṁ vāṁ vṛṇāno3 'yaṁ somo asurair no vihavyaḥ | tāṁ satyāṁ śraddhām abhy ā hi yātam athā somasya pibataṁ sutasya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • abravam: I have spoken
    • prathamaṁ: first
    • vāṁ: to you
    • vṛṇāno: choosing
    • yaṁ: which
    • somo: the soma (a sacred drink)
    • asurair: by the demons
    • no: not
    • vihavyaḥ: to be disturbed
    • tāṁ: that
    • satyāṁ: true
    • śraddhām: faith
    • abhy: towards
    • ā hi: indeed
    • yātam: let us go
    • athā: then
    • somasya: of the soma
    • pibataṁ: let us drink
    • sutasya: of the pressed (soma)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the importance of the sacred drink, soma, which is often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • The speaker expresses a desire to share this experience with others, emphasizing the communal aspect of spiritual practices.
    • The reference to "asurair" (demons) suggests a struggle against negative forces that may disrupt spiritual pursuits.
    • The call to "let us drink" signifies a collective journey towards truth and faith, reinforcing the idea that spiritual growth is often a shared experience.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern contexts, the concept of "soma" can be likened to any form of spiritual or communal practice that brings people together, such as meditation groups or religious ceremonies.
    • The idea of overcoming "asurair" can be seen in personal struggles against negativity, whether it be through mental health challenges or societal pressures, where individuals seek support from their community.
    • Faith and truth can be applied in various life situations, such as making ethical decisions in business or personal relationships, where one must choose integrity over convenience.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of community, faith, and the pursuit of truth in spiritual practices.
    • It serves as a reminder that while individual journeys are important, the support and shared experiences of a community can enhance spiritual growth.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek truth and maintain faith in the face of challenges, fostering resilience and unity among people.
