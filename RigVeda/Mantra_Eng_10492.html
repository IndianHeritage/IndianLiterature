अभīvर्तेन हविषा येनेन्द्रो अभिवावृते |तेनास्मान् ब्रह्मणस पते 'भि राष्ट्राय वर्तय ||
abhīvartena haviṣā yenendro abhivāvṛte | tenāsmān brahmaṇas pate 'bhi rāṣṭrāya vartaya ||
  • abhīvartena: This term can be broken down into "abhi" meaning "towards" and "vartena" meaning "movement" or "progress." It signifies a direction or a path that is being taken.
  • haviṣā: This word refers to offerings or sacrifices, often associated with rituals in Vedic traditions. It symbolizes the act of giving or contributing to a higher cause.
  • yenendro: "Yena" means "by whom" and "Indra" refers to the king of gods in Vedic mythology, representing power, strength, and leadership.
  • abhivāvṛte: This term can be understood as "enveloped" or "surrounded." It indicates a protective or encompassing force.
  • tena: Meaning "by that" or "by whom," it connects the previous ideas to the action or influence that follows.
  • asmān: This translates to "us," indicating a collective group or community.
  • brahmaṇas: Referring to "Brahman," the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit in Vedic philosophy, and "pate" meaning "lord" or "master." Together, they signify a divine authority or guiding force.
  • 'bhi: This is an emphatic particle, often used to strengthen the preceding word.
  • rāṣṭrāya: This term means "for the nation" or "for the realm," indicating a focus on collective well-being and governance.
  • vartaya: Meaning "to lead" or "to guide," it emphasizes the action of directing or influencing.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for divine guidance and protection in the pursuit of progress and well-being for the community or nation. It invokes the strength of Indra, asking for his blessings to envelop and support the collective efforts of the people.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Leadership: Leaders can use this mantra as a reminder to seek divine guidance in their decision-making processes, ensuring that their actions benefit the community.
  • Community Initiatives: When starting community projects, invoking this mantra can help unite people towards a common goal, fostering a sense of collective responsibility.
  • National Progress: Governments and policymakers can reflect on this mantra to ensure that their policies are aimed at the welfare of the nation, guided by higher principles.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on collective progress, divine guidance, and the importance of leadership in achieving the greater good. It serves as a reminder that individual efforts, when aligned with a higher purpose and supported by divine strength, can lead to the flourishing of society as a whole.
