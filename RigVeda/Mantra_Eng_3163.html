सा वेद देव आनमं देवां ऋतायते दमे |दाति प्रियाणि चिद वसु ||
sa veda deva ānamaṁ devām̐ ṛtāyate dame | dāti priyāṇi cid vasu ||
  • sa veda: This phrase translates to "he knows" or "he understands." It signifies the importance of knowledge and wisdom in life.
  • deva: This word means "god" or "divine." It represents the higher powers or forces that govern the universe.
  • ānamaṁ: This can be interpreted as "to the divine" or "to the gods." It emphasizes the connection between humans and the divine realm.
  • devām̐: This is a form of "deva," indicating the presence of multiple divine entities or aspects of divinity.
  • ṛtāyate: This term refers to "truth" or "righteousness." It highlights the significance of living a life aligned with truth and moral values.
  • dame: This word means "in the home" or "in the household." It signifies the importance of family and domestic life.
  • dāti: This translates to "gives" or "bestows." It indicates the act of sharing or providing for others.
  • priyāṇi: This means "beloved" or "dear." It emphasizes the value of love and affection in relationships.
  • cid: This term can be interpreted as "consciousness" or "awareness." It signifies the importance of being aware and mindful in our actions.
  • vasu: This word means "wealth" or "prosperity." It represents the material and spiritual abundance in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of knowledge, divine connection, truth, and the importance of family and relationships. It teaches us that by understanding and aligning ourselves with divine principles, we can create a harmonious and prosperous life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as fostering love and respect within families, seeking knowledge for personal growth, and striving for truth in our actions. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to live a life that honors both the divine and the values that promote well-being and prosperity in our communities.
