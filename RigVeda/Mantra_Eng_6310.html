यद अस्य धामनि प्रिय समीचीनासो अस्वरन् |नाभा यज्ञस्य दोहनā प्राध्वरे ||
yad asya dhāmani priye samīcīnāso asvaran | nābhā yajñasya dohanā prādhvare ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • asya: of this
    • dhāmani: in the support or foundation
    • priye: dear or beloved
    • samīcīnāso: those who are well-placed or well-situated
    • asvaran: have not spoken or have not made a sound
    • nābhā: the navel or center
    • yajñasya: of the sacrifice or offering
    • dohānā: of the milking or extraction
    • prādhvare: in the highest or most excellent manner
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the essence of support and the importance of maintaining harmony in relationships.
    • The phrase "that which is dear" emphasizes the value of cherished connections, whether they be familial, social, or spiritual.
    • The mention of "those who are well-placed" suggests that individuals who are grounded and stable contribute positively to the collective well-being.
    • The reference to "the navel of the sacrifice" symbolizes the core or center of spiritual practices, indicating that true offerings come from a place of sincerity and love.
    • The idea of "milking" in the context of sacrifice can be seen as extracting the essence or benefits of our actions, which should be done with care and respect.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, nurturing relationships and ensuring that each member feels valued can lead to a harmonious environment, akin to the "dear" connections mentioned in the mantra.
    • In a workplace, having well-placed individuals who contribute positively can enhance teamwork and productivity, reflecting the idea of being "well-situated."
    • In spiritual practices, approaching rituals with sincerity and love, rather than mere obligation, can lead to a more profound experience, similar to the essence of "sacrifice."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of relationships, stability, and sincerity in our actions.
    • It serves as a reminder to cherish our connections and approach our duties with love and respect.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence, both personally and collectively.
