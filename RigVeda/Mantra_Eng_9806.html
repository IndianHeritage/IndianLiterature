तस्माद्यज्ञात्सर्वहुतः संभरतं पृषदाज्यम् |पशून्तांश्चक्रे वायव्यांआरण्यां ग्राम्याश्च ये ||
tasmād yajñāt sarvahutaḥ sambhṛtam pṛṣadājyam | paśūn tām̐ś cakre vāyavyān āraṇyān grāmyāś ca ye ||
  • Mantra: "tasmād yajñāt sarvahutaḥ sambhṛtam pṛṣadājyam | paśūn tām̐ś cakre vāyavyān āraṇyān grāmyāś ca ye ||"
  • Translation: "From that sacrifice, all offerings are prepared; the clarified butter (ghee) is made. The animals (sacrificed) are those that are from the forest and the village."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Tasmād: From that
    • Yajñāt: From the sacrifice
    • Sarvahutaḥ: All offerings
    • Sambhṛtam: Prepared or gathered
    • Pṛṣadājyam: Clarified butter (ghee)
    • Paśūn: Animals
    • Tām̐ś: Those
    • Cakre: Made or created
    • Vāyavyān: From the forest
    • Āraṇyān: Wild or forest-dwelling
    • Grāmyāś: From the village
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of yajña (sacrifice) in the Vedic tradition, highlighting how it is the source of all offerings.
    • The term "sambhṛtam pṛṣadājyam" refers to the preparation of ghee, which is a vital component in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and nourishment.
    • The mention of "paśūn" indicates the animals that are offered in sacrifice, which are sourced from both the wild (vāyavyān) and the village (grāmyāś), illustrating the interconnectedness of nature and human society.
    • In real life, this can be seen in agricultural practices where farmers use local resources (animals and plants) to sustain their livelihoods, reflecting a balance between nature and human needs.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra signifies the holistic approach of Vedic rituals, where every element is interconnected and serves a purpose in the cycle of life.
    • It teaches us the value of gratitude towards nature and the importance of sustainable practices in our daily lives.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can inspire practices such as organic farming, where the focus is on using local resources responsibly and maintaining ecological balance.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of our responsibilities towards nature and the importance of rituals that honor the interconnectedness of all living beings.
