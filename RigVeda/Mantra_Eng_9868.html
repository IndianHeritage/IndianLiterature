ते सोमादो हरिः इन्द्रस्य निंसते अंशुं दूहन्तो अध्य आसते गवि |तेभिर दुग्धं पपीवान सोम्यम् मध्व इन्द्रो वर्धते प्रथते वृषायते ||
te somādo harī indrasya niṁsate 'ṁśuṁ duhanto adhy āsate gavi | tebhir dugdham papivān somyam madhv indro vardhate prathate vṛṣāyate ||
  • Translation: This mantra from the Rigveda speaks of the divine drink, Soma, which is associated with the god Indra. It describes how the Soma is extracted and enjoyed, leading to the enhancement of Indra's strength and vitality.
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • te: those
    • somādo: who partake of Soma
    • harī: the divine or the powerful
    • indrasya: of Indra
    • niṁsate: who flow or emerge
    • 'ṁśuṁ: the essence or the strength
    • duhanto: who extract or draw out
    • adhy: upon or in the presence of
    • āsate: they sit or reside
    • gavi: in the cow or in the source
    • tebhir: with those
    • dugdham: milk or nourishment
    • papivān: having drunk
    • somyam: the Soma-like or divine
    • madhv: sweet or pleasant
    • indro: Indra
    • vardhate: grows or increases
    • prathate: spreads or shines
    • vṛṣāyate: becomes strong or powerful
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • The mantra highlights the ritualistic importance of Soma, a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine qualities.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, symbolizes strength, courage, and the ability to overcome obstacles. The consumption of Soma is linked to these attributes.
    • In real life, this can be likened to how certain practices or rituals (like meditation, yoga, or even communal gatherings) can enhance one's mental and spiritual strength, much like Soma enhances Indra's power.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of nourishment—both physical and spiritual. Just as Soma nourishes Indra, we must seek out experiences and practices that nourish our own lives.
    • In modern contexts, this can be applied to the idea of self-care, where individuals engage in activities that promote well-being, such as healthy eating, exercise, and mindfulness.
    • The mantra encourages us to recognize the sources of strength in our lives, whether they be relationships, community, or personal practices, and to cultivate them for personal growth and empowerment.
