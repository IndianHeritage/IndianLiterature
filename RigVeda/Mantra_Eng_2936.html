ससर्परीर अभरत तुयम् एभ्योऽधि श्रवः पाञ्चजन्यासु कृष्टिषु |सां पक्ष्याः नव्यम आयुर दधानाः याम मे पलस्तिजमदग्नयो ददुः ||
sasarparīr abharat tūyam ebhyo 'dhi śravaḥ pāñcajanyāsu kṛṣṭiṣu | sā pakṣyā3 navyam āyur dadhānā yām me palastijamadagnayo daduḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sasarparīr: The one who brings forth or carries.
    • abharat: To bring or carry.
    • tūyam: Water or nourishment.
    • ebhyo: To these or from these.
    • 'dhi: Indeed or certainly.
    • śravaḥ: Fame or glory.
    • pāñcajanyāsu: In the five-fold or among the five.
    • kṛṣṭiṣu: In the fields or in the cultivated lands.
    • sā: That or she.
    • pakṣyā3: The wings or the aspects.
    • navyam: New or fresh.
    • āyur: Life or longevity.
    • dadhānā: Holding or possessing.
    • yām: Which or that.
    • me: My or mine.
    • palastijamadagnayo: The fire of the palas tree or the divine fire.
    • daduḥ: They have given or bestowed.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine forces that bring nourishment and sustenance to life.
    • The reference to "water" signifies the essential element for growth and vitality, much like how water is crucial for agriculture and human survival.
    • The "five-fold" aspect can be interpreted as the five elements (earth, water, fire, air, and space) that are fundamental to existence.
    • The mention of "new life" and "longevity" emphasizes the importance of renewal and the sustenance of life, akin to the cycles of nature where new plants grow and old ones decay.
    • The "fire of the palas tree" symbolizes transformation and the divine energy that fuels life, similar to how fire is used in rituals to invoke blessings and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers rely on water and the right conditions to cultivate crops, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on nourishment and sustenance.
    • In personal life, individuals seek new opportunities and experiences that contribute to their growth and longevity, mirroring the idea of "new life" and "holding onto life."
    • In spiritual practices, rituals involving fire are common, symbolizing purification and the invocation of divine blessings, akin to the "fire of the palas tree."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of life, nature, and the divine forces that sustain us.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to appreciate the resources we have, to seek renewal, and to honor the cycles of life.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to cultivate our surroundings, nurture relationships, and engage in practices that promote well-being and longevity.
