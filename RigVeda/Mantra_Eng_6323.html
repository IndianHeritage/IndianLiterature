इन्द्र शविष्ट सत्पते रयिं गृणत्सु धारय |श्रवः सूरिभ्यो अमृतं वसुत्वनम् ||
indra śaviṣṭha satpate rayiṁ gṛṇatsu dhāraya | śravaḥ sūribhyo amṛtaṁ vasutvanam ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Śaviṣṭha: Means the best or the most excellent, indicating superiority in qualities or actions.
  • Satpate: Translates to the lord of the seven, which can refer to the seven realms or aspects of existence.
  • Rayiṁ: Means wealth or prosperity, often associated with material and spiritual abundance.
  • Gṛṇatsu: Refers to the act of praising or singing, highlighting the importance of expression and acknowledgment.
  • Dhāraya: Means to hold or sustain, indicating the need for stability and support in life.
  • Śravaḥ: Refers to fame or glory, emphasizing the value of reputation and recognition.
  • Sūribhyo: Means to the wise or the learned, suggesting that wisdom is a guiding force in life.
  • Amṛtaṁ: Translates to immortality or eternal truth, representing the quest for lasting values and principles.
  • Vasutvanam: Refers to the abode of wealth or prosperity, indicating a place or state of abundance.

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Indra, asking for the sustenance of wealth and prosperity while recognizing the importance of wisdom and glory. It emphasizes the need for leadership and excellence in all endeavors, encouraging individuals to seek both material and spiritual wealth.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: In a corporate setting, leaders can embody the qualities of Indra by being strong, wise, and supportive, fostering an environment where employees feel valued and motivated.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can strive for excellence in their personal and professional lives, seeking knowledge and wisdom to make informed decisions.
  • Community Building: By recognizing and praising the contributions of others, communities can create a culture of appreciation and support, leading to collective prosperity.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the balance between material wealth and spiritual wisdom. It serves as a reminder to seek excellence, support one another, and strive for a life that is rich in both resources and values. By applying these principles, individuals and communities can work towards a prosperous and fulfilling existence.
