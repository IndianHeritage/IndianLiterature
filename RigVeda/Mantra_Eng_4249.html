आ नो गन्तं ऋषादसां वरुण मित्र बर्हणा |उपेमं चारुम अध्वरम् ||
ā no gantaṁ riśādasā varuṇa mitra barhaṇā | upemaṁ cārum adhvaram ||
  • Mantra: "ā no gantaṁ riśādasā varuṇa mitra barhaṇā | upemaṁ cārum adhvaram ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "O Varuna and Mitra, may we be free from the bonds of sin; may we attain the beautiful and auspicious offerings."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā no: "May we" - a plea for blessings and guidance.
    • gantaṁ: "free from" - signifies liberation from negativity or sin.
    • riśādasā: "bonds of sin" - refers to the constraints that hold us back from spiritual growth.
    • varuṇa: "Varuna" - the deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority.
    • mitra: "Mitra" - the deity representing friendship and harmony.
    • barhaṇā: "auspicious offerings" - signifies the positive actions and rituals that lead to spiritual elevation.
    • upemaṁ: "beautiful" - refers to the aesthetic and spiritual beauty of life.
    • cārum: "auspicious" - denotes good fortune and positive outcomes.
    • adhvaram: "offerings" - represents the rituals and actions performed to honor the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we often find ourselves bound by negative thoughts or actions. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek liberation from these constraints, much like how one might seek therapy or counseling to overcome personal challenges.
    • The invocation of Varuna and Mitra emphasizes the importance of maintaining harmony in relationships. For instance, in a workplace setting, fostering a spirit of cooperation and friendship can lead to a more productive environment.
    • The concept of "auspicious offerings" can be seen in various cultural practices, such as celebrating festivals where people come together to share food and joy, symbolizing unity and gratitude.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for spiritual liberation and the pursuit of harmony and beauty in life. It encourages individuals to reflect on their actions and relationships, promoting a life that is not only free from negativity but also rich in positive experiences. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal development, relationship building, and community engagement, reminding us to strive for a life filled with auspiciousness and beauty.
