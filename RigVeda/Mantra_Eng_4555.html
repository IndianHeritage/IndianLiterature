स नो वाजाय श्रवसा इषे च राये धेहि द्युमत इन्द्र विप्रान् |भरद्वाजे नृवत इन्द्र सूरीन् दिवि च स्मैधि पार्ये न इन्द्र ||
sa no vājāya śravasa iṣe ca rāye dhehi dyumata indra viprān | bharadvāje nṛvata indra sūrīn divi ca smaidhi pārye na indra ||
  • sa no vājāya: This phrase can be translated as "may He grant us strength." The term "vājāya" refers to strength or vigor, often associated with the power to overcome challenges.
  • śravasa: This word means "of fame" or "renown." It signifies the importance of being recognized for one's deeds and virtues.
  • iṣe ca rāye: This translates to "and for wealth." Here, "iṣe" refers to the ability to achieve or attain, while "rāye" signifies prosperity or wealth.
  • dhehi: This means "bestow" or "grant." It is a plea for divine assistance in achieving the aforementioned qualities.
  • dyumata: This term means "of the shining" or "radiant." It often refers to the brilliance associated with success and enlightenment.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
  • viprān: This word means "the wise" or "the learned." It emphasizes the value of knowledge and wisdom in achieving success.
  • bharadvāje: This refers to a sage or a learned person, often associated with wisdom and guidance.
  • nṛvata: This means "of men" or "human beings," indicating the mantra's relevance to humanity.
  • sūrīn: This translates to "the brave" or "the valiant," highlighting the importance of courage in life.
  • divi ca: This means "and in the heavens," suggesting a connection between earthly endeavors and divine support.
  • smaidhi: This term means "with a smile" or "with joy," indicating a positive and optimistic approach to life.
  • pārye: This can be interpreted as "to the one who is worthy" or "to the deserving," emphasizing merit and righteousness.
  • na indra: This phrase means "O Indra," addressing the divine power directly, seeking blessings and guidance.

Conclusion: This mantra is a prayer for strength, wisdom, prosperity, and divine guidance. It emphasizes the importance of courage, knowledge, and a positive attitude in achieving success. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during challenging times when one seeks motivation and support, or when pursuing goals that require both mental and physical strength. It serves as a reminder that with divine assistance and personal effort, one can attain greatness and recognition in their endeavors.
