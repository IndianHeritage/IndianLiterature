आ विद्युन्मद्भिर मरुतः स्वर्कै रथैभिर यात ऋष्टिमद्भिर अश्वपर्णैः |आ वर्षिष्ठया न इषा वयो न पप्तता सुमायाः ||
ā vidyunmadbhir marutaḥ svarkai rathebhir yāta ṛṣṭimadbhir aśvaparṇaiḥ | ā varṣiṣṭhayā na iṣā vayo na paptatā sumāyāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • vidyunmadbhir: with the lightning-like (or powerful) ones
    • marutaḥ: of the Maruts (storm deities)
    • svarkai: with the heavenly (or celestial) ones
    • rathebhir: in chariots
    • yāta: having gone
    • ṛṣṭimadbhir: with the ones who have achieved success
    • aśvaparṇaiḥ: with horse-like wings
    • ā: from
    • varṣiṣṭhayā: with the best of rains
    • na: not
    • iṣā: power
    • vayo: age
    • na: not
    • paptatā: falling
    • sumāyāḥ: of the good mind
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Maruts, the storm deities, who are often associated with power, strength, and the forces of nature.
    • The phrase "with the lightning-like ones" suggests a connection to the dynamic and energetic aspects of life, akin to how we might view powerful leaders or innovators in society.
    • The mention of "chariots" symbolizes movement and progress, reflecting how we strive to advance in our personal and professional lives.
    • The reference to "the best of rains" can be seen as a metaphor for abundance and prosperity, similar to how we seek fruitful outcomes in our endeavors.
    • The concluding lines emphasize resilience and the importance of a good mindset, indicating that despite challenges (age, power, or setbacks), one should maintain a positive and constructive attitude.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who embody the qualities of the Maruts can inspire their teams to achieve great success, much like how the mantra calls for strength and progress.
    • In personal development, individuals can draw inspiration from the mantra to overcome obstacles, reminding themselves to maintain a positive mindset even in the face of adversity.
    • In environmental discussions, the reference to rain can be linked to the importance of sustainable practices that ensure abundance for future generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of resilience, the importance of a positive mindset, and the pursuit of progress in various aspects of life.
    • Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to harness their inner strength and strive for success while remaining grounded and optimistic.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in leadership, personal growth, or environmental stewardship, the teachings of this mantra can guide us toward a more fulfilling and impactful existence.
