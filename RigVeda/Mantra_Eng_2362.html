अश्वस्यात्र जनिमास्य च स्वर द्रुहो ऋषः सम्पृचः पाहि सूरिन् |आमासु पूर्षु परो अप्रमृष्यं नारातयो वि नशन नानृतानि ||
aśvasyātra janimāsya ca svar druho riṣaḥ sampṛcaḥ pāhi sūrīn | āmāsu pūrṣu paro apramṛṣyaṁ nārātayo vi naśan nānṛtāni ||
  • Mantra: "aśvasyātra janimāsya ca svar druho riṣaḥ sampṛcaḥ pāhi sūrīn | āmāsu pūrṣu paro apramṛṣyaṁ nārātayo vi naśan nānṛtāni ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvasyātra: pertaining to the horse, symbolizing strength and vitality.
    • janimāsya: the birth or origin, indicating the beginning of life or creation.
    • svar: the sky or heaven, representing the divine or spiritual realm.
    • druho: deceit or treachery, warning against falsehood.
    • riṣaḥ: the sages or seers, those who possess wisdom and insight.
    • sampṛcaḥ: to gather or unite, emphasizing the importance of community and togetherness.
    • pāhi: protect or safeguard, a call for divine protection.
    • sūrīn: the heroes or divine beings, representing strength and valor.
    • āmāsu: in the bodies or forms, referring to the physical existence.
    • pūrṣu: the supreme being or the ultimate reality.
    • paro: beyond or transcendent, indicating something that surpasses ordinary understanding.
    • apramṛṣyaṁ: unassailable or inviolable, suggesting something that cannot be harmed.
    • nārātayo: the beings of the earth, referring to humanity.
    • vi: indeed or certainly, reinforcing the statement.
    • naśan: to perish or be destroyed, indicating the fragility of life.
    • nānṛtāni: falsehoods or lies, emphasizing the importance of truth.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, unity, and truth in life.
    • The reference to "aśvasyātra" (the horse) symbolizes vitality and the energy needed to navigate life's challenges.
    • It calls for protection from deceit ("druho") and encourages the gathering of wise individuals ("riṣaḥ") to foster a supportive community.
    • The mantra also highlights the significance of the physical existence ("āmāsu") while recognizing the transcendent nature of the ultimate reality ("pūrṣu").
    • It serves as a reminder that while life is fragile ("naśan"), one must strive to uphold truth and integrity ("nānṛtāni").
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra is significant as it encapsulates the values of strength, community, and truthfulness, which are essential in both personal and societal contexts.
    • In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in professional settings, fostering honesty in relationships, and promoting unity in communities.
    • By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and truthful society.
