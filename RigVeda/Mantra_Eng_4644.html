मन्त्र: शूरो वा शूरं वनते शरीरैस तनूरुचां तरुषि यत्कृत्वैते |तोकं वा गोषु तनये यदप्सु विखन्दसी उर्वरासु ब्रवैत ||
śūro vā śūraṁ vanate śarīrais tanūrucā taruṣi yat kṛṇvaite | toke vā goṣu tanaye yad apsu vi krandasī urvarāsu bravaite ||
  • śūro vā śūraṁ vanate: This phrase can be translated to "the brave one, or the one who is courageous." It emphasizes the importance of bravery in facing challenges. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who stand up for justice, such as activists fighting for human rights.
  • śarīrais tanūrucā: This means "with bodies that are strong and healthy." It highlights the significance of physical well-being. For example, athletes train rigorously to maintain their health and strength, which is essential for their performance in sports.
  • taruṣi yat kṛṇvaite: This translates to "those who create or build." It signifies the act of creation and contribution to society. In a real-world context, this can refer to entrepreneurs who innovate and create businesses that provide jobs and services to the community.
  • toke vā goṣu tanaye: This phrase can be interpreted as "in the offspring of cows." Cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and prosperity in many cultures. This can relate to farmers who raise cattle, contributing to the economy and food supply.
  • yad apsu vi krandasī: This means "where the waters flow." Water is essential for life and symbolizes nourishment and sustenance. In real life, this can be related to communities that rely on rivers and lakes for their livelihoods, such as fishing or agriculture.
  • urvarāsu bravaite: This translates to "they who thrive in abundance." It signifies prosperity and success. In a modern context, this can be seen in businesses that flourish and contribute positively to the economy, creating wealth and opportunities for others.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the virtues of bravery, health, creativity, prosperity, and the importance of natural resources. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of these elements in achieving a fulfilling and successful life. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to be courageous in their pursuits, maintain their health, contribute positively to society, and recognize the value of natural resources in sustaining life and prosperity. This mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, including personal development, community service, and environmental stewardship.
