यस्ते मदो युज्यश्चारुरस्ति येन वृत्राणि हर्यश्व हंसि |स त्वां इन्द्र प्रभूवसो ममत्तु ||
yas te mado yujyaś cārur asti yena vṛtrāṇi haryaśva haṁsi | sa tvām indra prabhūvaso mamattu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • mado: joy or delight
    • yujyaś: connected or united
    • cārur: beautiful or charming
    • asti: exists or is
    • yena: by whom
    • vṛtrāṇi: the obstacles or enemies
    • haryaśva: the swift horses
    • haṁsi: you have killed
    • sa: he
    • tvām: you
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • prabhūvaso: of great power
    • mamattu: may you grant me
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, the Vedic deity associated with rain, thunder, and strength.
    • The speaker expresses a desire for the joy and strength that Indra embodies, highlighting the connection between divine power and human experience.
    • The mention of "vṛtrāṇi" refers to overcoming obstacles, which can be interpreted as challenges in life.
    • The phrase "haryaśva haṁsi" signifies the triumph over these challenges, akin to achieving success in personal or professional endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive work environment, one may invoke the spirit of this mantra to seek strength and clarity in overcoming challenges, much like a leader who inspires their team to achieve a common goal.
    • During difficult times, such as illness or personal loss, individuals may find solace in the idea of divine support, drawing strength from the belief that they can overcome their struggles.
    • In sports, athletes often seek motivation and strength, paralleling the invocation of Indra's power to conquer their opponents and achieve victory.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It emphasizes the connection between joy, strength, and the ability to conquer obstacles, which is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal growth, professional success, and spiritual well-being.
    • By invoking the qualities of Indra, individuals can find inspiration and motivation to face their challenges with courage and resilience.
