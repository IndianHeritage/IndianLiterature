मन्त्र: ब्राह्मणासः सोमिनो वाचम् अक्रत ब्रह्म कृत्वन्तः परिवत्सरीणम् | अध्वर्यवो घर्मिणः सिष्विदाना आवीर भवन्ति गुह्याः न के चित् ||
brāhmaṇāsaḥ somino vācam akrata brahma kṛṇvantaḥ parivatsarīṇam | adhvaryavo gharmiṇaḥ siṣvidānā āvir bhavanti guhyā na ke cit ||
  • brāhmaṇāsaḥ: This term refers to the Brahmins, the priestly class in ancient Indian society, who were responsible for performing rituals and preserving sacred knowledge.
  • somino: This word relates to 'Soma', a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
  • vācam: Meaning 'speech' or 'words', it emphasizes the power of spoken words in rituals and their ability to manifest intentions.
  • akrata: This translates to 'created' or 'formed', indicating the act of bringing something into existence through ritualistic practices.
  • brahma: Referring to the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit, it signifies the highest truth in Vedic philosophy.
  • kṛṇvantaḥ: This means 'those who create' or 'make', highlighting the active role of the priests in the ritual process.
  • parivatsarīṇam: This term can be understood as 'the cycle of years', indicating the cyclical nature of time and the importance of rituals in maintaining cosmic order.
  • adhvaryavo: Referring to the priests who perform the rituals, particularly the ones who offer sacrifices.
  • gharmiṇaḥ: This word relates to the 'sacrificial fire', which is central to Vedic rituals, symbolizing transformation and purification.
  • siṣvidānā: Meaning 'those who know', it refers to the knowledgeable individuals who understand the significance of the rituals.
  • āvir: This translates to 'manifest' or 'appear', indicating the emergence of divine presence through rituals.
  • bhavanti: Meaning 'become', it signifies the transformation that occurs through the performance of rituals.
  • guhyā: This term means 'secret' or 'mystical', referring to the esoteric knowledge that is often hidden from the uninitiated.
  • na ke cit: This translates to 'no one', indicating that the knowledge and results of these rituals are not accessible to everyone.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of rituals and the role of the Brahmins in creating a connection between the divine and the material world. It highlights the power of words and the importance of knowledge in performing rituals that maintain cosmic order. In real life, this can be seen in various cultural practices where rituals are performed to invoke blessings, seek guidance, or mark significant life events. The mantra serves as a reminder of the sacredness of words and actions, encouraging individuals to engage in practices that foster spiritual growth and understanding.
