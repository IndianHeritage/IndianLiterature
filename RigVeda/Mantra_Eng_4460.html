सास्माकेबHIR एतरī न शूषैर अग्निः ष्टवे दमा आ जातवेदाः |द्र्वन्नो वन्बन क्रत्वा नर्वोश्रः पितेव जारयायि यज्ञैः ||
sāsmākebhir etarī na śūṣair agniḥ ṣṭave dama ā jātavedāḥ | drvanno vanvan kratvā nārvosraḥ piteva jārayāyi yajñaiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sāsmākebhir: With our offerings
    • etarī: To the other (gods)
    • na: Not
    • śūṣair: With dry (offerings)
    • agniḥ: O Agni (the Fire God)
    • ṣṭave: We praise
    • dama: Restraint or control
    • ā: To
    • jātavedāḥ: O Knower of all births
    • drvanno: From the forest
    • vanvan: With the trees
    • kratvā: With the sacrifice
    • nārvosraḥ: Not to be neglected
    • piteva: Like a father
    • jārayāyi: Nourishing
    • yajñaiḥ: With sacrifices
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The phrase "sāsmākebhir etarī" emphasizes the importance of collective offerings, suggesting that unity in worship is vital.
    • The term "na śūṣair" indicates that offerings should not be dry or lifeless, symbolizing the need for genuine devotion and vitality in rituals.
    • "Agniḥ ṣṭave" reflects the act of praising Agni, highlighting the significance of gratitude and reverence in spiritual practices.
    • The reference to "dama" signifies the importance of self-control and discipline in one's life, which is essential for spiritual growth.
    • "Jātavedāḥ" refers to Agni as the knower of all births, symbolizing the omnipresence of divine knowledge and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • The imagery of "drvanno vanvan" evokes the natural world, reminding us of our connection to nature and the importance of respecting it.
    • "Krattvā nārvosraḥ" suggests that sacrifices should not be neglected, emphasizing the need for consistent spiritual practice.
    • The phrase "piteva jārayāyi" likens Agni to a nurturing father, indicating that the divine provides sustenance and support to devotees.
    • "Yajñaiḥ" reinforces the idea that sacrifices and rituals are essential for maintaining harmony in life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of collective worship, genuine devotion, and the nurturing aspect of the divine in our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to engage in community rituals, practice self-discipline, and maintain a connection with nature.
    • The teachings of this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as fostering teamwork in professional settings, nurturing relationships, and respecting the environment.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that spiritual practices and community involvement are essential for personal and collective well-being.
