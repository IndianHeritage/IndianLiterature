मन्त्र: चतुस्त्रिंशद् वाजिनो देवबन्धोः वङ्क्रीर अश्वस्य स्वधितिः सम एति | अच्छिद्रा गात्रा वयुनā कृत्नोति परुष-परुर अनुगुष्यā वि शस्त ||
catustriṁśad vājino devabandhor vaṅkrīr aśvasya svadhitiḥ sam eti | acchidrā gātrā vayunā kṛṇota paruṣ-parur anughuṣyā vi śasta ||
  • catustriṁśad: This term refers to the number thirty-four, which can symbolize completeness or a full cycle in various contexts.
  • vājino: This word means "of the swift" or "of the strong," often associated with horses or chariots, symbolizing power and speed.
  • devabandhor: This translates to "of the divine connection," indicating a relationship with the divine or higher powers.
  • vaṅkrīr: This term can be interpreted as "to bend" or "to curve," suggesting adaptability or flexibility in approach.
  • aśvasya: Meaning "of the horse," it symbolizes strength, vitality, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • svadhitiḥ: This word refers to "self-control" or "self-discipline," emphasizing the importance of inner strength and governance.
  • sam eti: This phrase means "comes together" or "unites," indicating harmony and collaboration.
  • acchidrā: This term means "without division" or "whole," suggesting unity and integrity.
  • gātrā: Referring to "limbs" or "parts," it symbolizes the various aspects of life and their interconnectedness.
  • vayunā: This word means "with strength" or "with vigor," highlighting the importance of energy and enthusiasm in actions.
  • kṛṇota: Meaning "to create" or "to make," it emphasizes the act of bringing forth or manifesting ideas into reality.
  • paruṣ-parur: This phrase can be interpreted as "harsh and strong," indicating resilience in the face of challenges.
  • anughuṣyā: This term means "to be praised" or "to be honored," suggesting the value of recognition and appreciation.
  • vi śasta: This translates to "well-ordered" or "well-structured," indicating the importance of organization and clarity in life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, unity, and self-discipline in achieving success and harmony in life. It teaches us that by harnessing our inner power and maintaining a connection with the divine, we can navigate challenges effectively. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork in professional settings, personal development through self-discipline, and fostering relationships built on mutual respect and understanding. The mantra serves as a reminder that true strength lies not just in individual prowess but in the ability to work together harmoniously towards a common goal.
