यज्ञस्य केतुम् प्रथमम् पुरोहितम् अग्निं नरस् त्रिषधस्थे सम ईधिरे |इन्द्रेण देवैः सारथं स बरहिषि सीदन् निहोतā यजथाय सुक्रतुः ||
yajñasya ketum prathamam purohitam agniṁ naras triṣadhasthe sam īdhire | indreṇa devaiḥ sarathaṁ sa barhiṣi sīdan ni hotā yajathāya sukratuḥ ||
  • yajñasya ketum prathamam purohitam: This phrase refers to the "first priest of the sacrifice." In Vedic rituals, the purohit (priest) plays a crucial role in conducting the yajna (sacred fire sacrifice). The purohit is seen as the mediator between the divine and the human realms.
  • agniṁ naras triṣadhasthe sam īdhire: Here, "Agni" represents the fire god, who is central to Vedic rituals. "Naras" refers to humans, and "triṣadhasthe" suggests the three realms (heaven, earth, and the underworld). This line emphasizes the importance of Agni in connecting humans with the divine through the act of sacrifice.
  • indreṇa devaiḥ sarathaṁ sa barhiṣi: "Indra" is the king of the gods, and "devaiḥ" refers to the other deities. "Sarathaṁ" means to be in harmony or in a chariot, symbolizing the unity of divine forces. "Barhiṣi" refers to the sacred grass used in rituals, indicating the sanctity of the space where the yajna is performed.
  • sīdan ni hotā yajathāya sukratuḥ: This line speaks of the "hotā," the priest who invokes the deities during the yajna. "Sīdan" means to sit or reside, indicating the presence of the divine during the ritual. "Yajathāya sukratuḥ" suggests that the purpose of the yajna is to achieve good intentions and favorable outcomes.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of Vedic rituals, highlighting the roles of the priest, the fire god, and the deities in the sacrificial process. It emphasizes the importance of intention and harmony in spiritual practices.
  • Real-life Application: In modern contexts, this mantra can be applied to various aspects of life where intention and harmony are crucial. For example, in team projects, the role of a leader (akin to the purohit) is vital in guiding the group towards a common goal (the yajna). The collective effort and positive intentions of all members can lead to successful outcomes, much like the divine blessings invoked in a yajna.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of human actions and divine support. It teaches us that through sincere efforts, collaboration, and positive intentions, we can achieve our goals and foster harmony in our lives and communities.
