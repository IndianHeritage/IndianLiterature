उतालब्धं स्पृणुहि जातवेद आलिभानाद ऋष्टिभिर यातुधानात् |अग्ने पूर्वो न जहि शोशुचाना आमादः क्ष्विङ्कास तं अदन्त्व एनिः ||
utālabdhaṁ spṛṇuhi jātaveda ālebhānād ṛṣṭibhir yātudhānāt | agne pūrvo ni jahi śośucāna āmādaḥ kṣviṅkās tam adantv enīḥ ||
  • Mantra: "utālabdhaṁ spṛṇuhi jātaveda ālebhānād ṛṣṭibhir yātudhānāt | agne pūrvo ni jahi śośucāna āmādaḥ kṣviṅkās tam adantv enīḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • utālabdhaṁ: attained or achieved
    • spṛṇuhi: touch or connect
    • jātaveda: O Agni, the one who knows all births
    • ālebhānād: from the grasp or hold
    • ṛṣṭibhir: with the blessings or offerings
    • yātudhānāt: from the evil spirits or obstacles
    • agne: O Agni, the divine fire
    • pūrvo: the ancient or primordial
    • ni jahi: do not let go or destroy
    • śośucāna: of the one who is pure or cleansed
    • āmādaḥ: the one who is full or complete
    • kṣviṅkās: the one who is powerful or strong
    • tam: that
    • adantv: may not be harmed
    • enīḥ: may you lead or guide
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The invocation of "utālabdhaṁ" suggests a desire to achieve or attain something significant, which can be interpreted as spiritual enlightenment or material success.
    • "spṛṇuhi" indicates a plea for connection, emphasizing the importance of divine guidance in our lives.
    • By addressing "jātaveda," the mantra acknowledges Agni's omniscience, recognizing that he knows all births and the essence of existence.
    • The phrase "ālebhānād ṛṣṭibhir yātudhānāt" signifies a request for protection from negative influences or obstacles, which can be likened to the challenges we face in our daily lives, such as stress, anxiety, or external negativity.
    • In the line "agne pūrvo ni jahi," there is a call to Agni to preserve purity and goodness, which can be related to maintaining integrity and moral values in our actions.
    • The concluding part of the mantra, "tam adantv enīḥ," expresses a wish for guidance and protection, highlighting the need for support in navigating life's complexities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, one might recite this mantra before a crucial meeting or presentation, seeking clarity and protection from negative outcomes.
    • During personal challenges, such as health issues or relationship troubles, individuals may invoke this mantra to seek divine assistance and strength.
    • In rituals or ceremonies, this mantra can be used to purify the space and invite positive energies, ensuring a conducive environment for spiritual practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine guidance and protection in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek purity, integrity, and strength while navigating the challenges we encounter.
    • By invoking Agni, we acknowledge the need for a connection with the divine, which can lead to personal growth and fulfillment.
    • Overall, the mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, encouraging individuals to seek support and maintain a positive outlook amidst adversity.
