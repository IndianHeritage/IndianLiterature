आ याहि सुशुमाहि त इन्द्र सोमं पिबा इमम् |एदं बर्हिः सदो मम ||
ā yāhi suṣumā hi ta indra somam pibā imam | edam barhiḥ sado mama ||
  • Transliteration: ā yāhi suṣumā hi ta indra somam pibā imam | edam barhiḥ sado mama ||
  • Meaning:
    • ā yāhi: "Come here" or "approach." This phrase invites a divine presence or energy to manifest.
    • suṣumā: Refers to the "good" or "auspicious" energy, often associated with the flow of life and consciousness.
    • hi: A particle that emphasizes the preceding word, often translated as "indeed" or "certainly."
    • ta: "That" or "those," indicating a specific divine or spiritual entity.
    • indra: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • somam: "Soma," a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration, often associated with spiritual awakening.
    • pibā: "Drink" or "consume," suggesting the act of taking in or absorbing something beneficial.
    • imam: "This," referring to the specific offering or experience being presented.
    • edam: "This" or "here," reinforcing the immediacy of the offering.
    • barhiḥ: "Sacred grass" or "offering," symbolizing purity and the connection between the earthly and the divine.
    • sado: "Joy" or "happiness," indicating the emotional state that the offering aims to invoke.
    • mama: "My," personalizing the experience and connection to the divine.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to the divine, inviting Indra to partake in the sacred offering of Soma.
    • It emphasizes the importance of connecting with higher energies and seeking divine blessings for strength and joy.
    • The act of drinking Soma symbolizes the absorption of divine wisdom and inspiration, which can lead to personal transformation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be likened to seeking guidance and support during challenging times, such as during a personal crisis or when facing significant life decisions.
    • Just as one might turn to meditation or prayer to connect with a higher power, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of spiritual practices in daily life.
    • In communal settings, such as rituals or ceremonies, invoking the divine presence can foster a sense of unity and shared purpose among participants.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of inviting divine energies into our lives, emphasizing the importance of spiritual nourishment and connection.
    • It serves as a reminder that seeking strength and joy through divine connection can lead to personal growth and transformation.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to engage in practices that foster spiritual awareness and community, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
