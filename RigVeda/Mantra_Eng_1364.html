त्वम āयसम प्रति वर्तयो गोर दिवो अश्मानम् उपनीतम ऋभ्वा |कुत्साय यत्र पुरुहूत वन्बञ्छुष्णम् अनन्तैः पर्यासि वधैः ||
tvam āyasam prati vartayo gor divo aśmānam upanītam ṛbhvā | kutsāya yatra puruhūta vanvañ chuṣṇam anantaiḥ pariyāsi vadhaiḥ ||
  • tvam - You
  • āyasam - Iron or strength
  • prati vartayo - Moving towards or directed towards
  • gor - Of the cow, symbolizing nourishment and wealth
  • dive - In the sky or divine
  • aśmānam - Stone or solid foundation
  • upanītam - Brought near or presented
  • ṛbhvā - With strength or power
  • kutsāya - To the one who is worthy or deserving
  • yatra - Where
  • puruhūta - Many offerings or sacrifices
  • vanvañ - To the one who is revered
  • chuṣṇam - Sweetness or pleasantness
  • anantaiḥ - With endless or infinite
  • pariyāsi - Surrounding or encompassing
  • vadhaiḥ - With strength or power

This mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine, recognizing the strength and nourishment that comes from the earth and the heavens. It emphasizes the importance of solid foundations (symbolized by stone) and the power of offerings made with sincerity and reverence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as iron symbolizes strength, individuals can strive to develop their inner strength and resilience. For example, someone facing challenges in their career can draw inspiration from this mantra to persevere and seek support from their community.
  • Community and Relationships: The reference to the cow signifies nourishment and wealth, which can be interpreted as the importance of nurturing relationships. In a community setting, this mantra encourages individuals to support one another, creating a strong and cohesive environment.
  • Spiritual Practices: The mantra can be used in meditation or prayer, focusing on the connection between the earthly and the divine. It serves as a reminder to seek strength and guidance from higher powers while being grounded in reality.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, nourishment, and reverence. It teaches us to recognize the power of our actions and the importance of community and spirituality in our lives. By applying its principles, we can foster personal growth, strengthen our relationships, and deepen our spiritual practices.
