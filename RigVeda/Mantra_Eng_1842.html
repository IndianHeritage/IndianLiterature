अर्चद वृषा वृषभिः स्वेदुहव्यैर मृगो नाश्नो अति यज जुगुर्यात् |प्र मन्दायुर मनां गूर्त हुताऽभरते मर्यो मिथुनाऽयजत्रः ||
arcad vṛṣā vṛṣabhiḥ sveduhavyair mṛgo nāśno ati yaj juguryāt | pra mandayur manāṁ gūrta hotā bharate maryo mithunā yajatraḥ ||
  • arcad: This word can be interpreted as "to be invoked" or "to be called upon." It signifies the act of seeking divine assistance or blessings.
  • vṛṣā: This term refers to "strength" or "power." It embodies the idea of vitality and vigor, often associated with the masculine principle.
  • vṛṣabhiḥ: This word means "with the strong" or "with the powerful." It emphasizes the importance of community and collective strength.
  • sveduhavyair: This can be broken down into "sveda," meaning "sweat" or "effort," and "havyai," which refers to offerings. Together, it suggests that through hard work and effort, one can achieve success and make offerings to the divine.
  • mṛgo: This word means "deer" or "animal," often symbolizing grace and agility. It can also represent the pursuit of goals or desires.
  • nāśno: This term means "not consuming" or "not taking." It implies restraint and the importance of moderation in life.
  • ati: This word means "beyond" or "exceeding." It suggests going beyond the ordinary limits.
  • yaj: This refers to "sacrifice" or "worship." It highlights the importance of devotion and the act of giving back to the divine.
  • juguryāt: This can be interpreted as "may it be fulfilled" or "may it come to fruition." It expresses a desire for the successful outcome of one's efforts.
  • pra: This prefix means "forth" or "forward," indicating movement or progress.
  • mandayur: This term can be understood as "long-lived" or "having a long life." It signifies the importance of longevity and sustainability in life.
  • manāṁ: This word means "mind" or "intellect." It emphasizes the role of thought and intention in shaping one's reality.
  • gūrta: This can be interpreted as "to be invoked" or "to be called upon," similar to "arcad," reinforcing the theme of seeking divine guidance.
  • hotā: This term refers to "the priest" or "the one who offers." It signifies the role of the mediator between the divine and the earthly realm.
  • bharate: This word means "to bear" or "to carry." It emphasizes the responsibility of individuals to uphold their duties.
  • maryo: This term can be interpreted as "the mortal" or "the human." It highlights the human condition and the challenges faced in life.
  • mithunā: This word means "pair" or "couple." It signifies the importance of relationships and partnerships in life.
  • yajatraḥ: This can be understood as "the one who performs the sacrifice." It emphasizes the active participation in rituals and the importance of community.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, community, and divine guidance through effort and sacrifice. It highlights the importance of moderation, the pursuit of goals, and the role of relationships in achieving success. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in teamwork, where collective strength and effort lead to success, or in personal endeavors where one seeks divine blessings through hard work and dedication. It serves as a reminder that through devotion and community, one can navigate the challenges of life and achieve fulfillment.
