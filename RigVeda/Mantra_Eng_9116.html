स्तेगो न क्षाम् अति एति पृथ्वीं मिहं न वातो विह वाति भूमः |मित्रो यत्र वरुणो अज्यमानो 'ग्निर वने न व्य असृष्ट शोकम् ||
stego na kṣām aty eti pṛthvīm mihaṁ na vāto vi ha vāti bhūma | mitro yatra varuṇo ajyamāno 'gnir vane na vy asṛṣṭa śokam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • stego: protection, covering
    • na: not
    • kṣām: the earth, the world
    • aty: beyond, surpassing
    • eti: goes, moves
    • pṛthvīm: the earth
    • mihaṁ: here, in this place
    • na vāto: not the wind
    • vi ha: indeed
    • vāti: blows
    • bhūma: the ground, the earth
    • mitro: friend
    • yatra: where
    • varuṇo: Varuna, the god of water and cosmic order
    • ajyamāno: unrestrained, unbound
    • 'gnir: fire
    • vane: in the forest
    • na vy asṛṣṭa: not released, not let go
    • śokam: sorrow, grief
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the protective nature of the earth and the divine forces that govern it.
    • It emphasizes that the earth is not disturbed by the winds, symbolizing stability and peace.
    • The mention of Varuna and fire in the forest suggests a harmonious balance between natural elements and divine presence.
    • It conveys a message of friendship and support from the divine, indicating that in times of distress, one can find solace in nature and the divine order.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of natural disasters, such as hurricanes or earthquakes, people often seek refuge in the earth's stability and the support of their community, reflecting the mantra's theme of protection.
    • Environmental conservation efforts can be seen as a way to honor the earth and maintain the balance that this mantra speaks of, ensuring that nature remains undisturbed.
    • In personal struggles, individuals may find comfort in nature, using it as a source of strength and healing, akin to the protective qualities described in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of the earth as a protective entity and the importance of divine support in maintaining harmony.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek balance in our lives, to respect nature, and to find solace in the divine presence during challenging times.
    • In a broader context, it encourages us to foster friendships and community ties, reinforcing the idea that we are not alone in our struggles.
