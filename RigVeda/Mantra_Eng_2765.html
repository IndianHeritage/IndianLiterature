यद अङ्ग त्वा भरताः संतर्युर गव्यन् ग्राम इषित इन्द्रजूतः |अर्षाद अह प्रस्वः सर्गतक्त आ वो वृणे सुमतिं यज्ञियानाम् ||
yad aṅga tvā bharatāḥ saṁtareyur gavyan grāma iṣita indrajūtaḥ | arṣād aha prasavaḥ sargatakta ā vo vṛṇe sumatiṁ yajñiyānām ||
  • yad aṅga tvā bharatāḥ saṁtareyur: This phrase can be interpreted as "O Indra, may the Bharatas (a tribe in ancient India) be united and may they attain strength through you." Here, Indra represents a divine force or energy that brings people together and empowers them.
  • gavyan grāma iṣita indrajūtaḥ: This translates to "the village that is ruled by the one who is strong like Indra." It emphasizes the importance of leadership and strength in a community, suggesting that a strong leader can guide the people towards prosperity.
  • arṣād aha prasavaḥ sargatakta: This can be understood as "from the heavens, I invoke the creative force." It signifies the connection between the divine and the earthly, highlighting the belief that divine blessings are essential for creation and sustenance.
  • ā vo vṛṇe sumatiṁ yajñiyānām: This means "I choose for you, O worshippers, the path of good understanding." It suggests that through rituals and worship, individuals can attain wisdom and clarity in their lives.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, strength, divine blessings, and wisdom in a community. It reflects the values of cooperation and the need for strong leadership to guide people towards a prosperous life.
  • Real-life Application: In modern society, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as community building, leadership development, and spiritual practices. For instance, in a workplace, a strong leader can unite the team and drive them towards common goals, much like the Bharatas seeking strength through Indra. Additionally, community gatherings and rituals can foster a sense of belonging and shared purpose, leading to collective growth and understanding.
