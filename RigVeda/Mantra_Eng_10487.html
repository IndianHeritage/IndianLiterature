इहैविधि माप च्योष्ठाः पर्वत इवाविचाचलिः | इन्द्र इवेह ध्रुवस तिष्ठेह राष्ट्रं उ धारय ||
ihaivaidhi māpa cyoṣṭhāḥ parvata ivāvicācaliḥ | indra iveha dhruvas tiṣṭheha rāṣṭram u dhāraya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ihaivaidhi: Here, indeed, may you be.
    • māpa: May you not.
    • cyoṣṭhāḥ: Be disturbed or shaken.
    • parvata: Like a mountain.
    • ivāvicācaliḥ: Unmoved, unshaken.
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods).
    • iveha: Here, indeed.
    • dhruvas: Steadfast, firm.
    • tiṣṭheha: May you stand.
    • rāṣṭram: The realm or kingdom.
    • u: And.
    • dhāraya: May you uphold.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the strength and stability of Indra, asking for a steadfast presence in the face of challenges.
    • It emphasizes the importance of being unshaken like a mountain, suggesting resilience in difficult times.
    • The call to Indra signifies a plea for divine support in maintaining order and stability in one's life or community.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader may refer to this mantra when facing market fluctuations, emphasizing the need for a steady approach to maintain the company's integrity.
    • During personal crises, individuals may draw inspiration from this mantra to remain calm and composed, much like a mountain that stands firm against the elements.
    • In community leadership, this mantra can serve as a reminder to uphold the values and stability of the community, ensuring that it remains united and strong.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for resilience and steadfastness in the face of adversity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal challenges to leadership roles, reminding individuals and communities to remain grounded and strong.
    • By invoking the qualities of Indra and the metaphor of the mountain, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the strength that can be found within oneself and the support that can be sought from the divine.
