मंसीमहि त्वा वयम् अस्माकं देव पूषण |मतीनां च साधनं विप्राणां चाधवम् ||
maṁsīmahi tvā vayam asmākaṁ deva pūṣan | matīnāṁ ca sādhanaṁ viprāṇāṁ cādhavam ||
  • maṁsīmahi: This term can be interpreted as "we meditate upon" or "we invoke." It signifies the act of focusing one's thoughts and intentions towards a higher power or divine entity.
  • tvā: This means "you," referring to the divine being being addressed, in this case, Pūṣan, who is a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity.
  • vayam: This translates to "we," indicating a collective group, often representing the community or society that is invoking the deity.
  • asmākaṁ: This means "our," emphasizing a sense of belonging and connection to the divine being being invoked.
  • deva: This word means "god" or "deity," highlighting the divine nature of Pūṣan.
  • pūṣan: Pūṣan is a specific deity in the Vedic tradition, associated with nourishment, prosperity, and the protection of cattle, which were vital for sustenance in ancient times.
  • matīnāṁ: This translates to "of our minds" or "of our thoughts," indicating the importance of mental focus and intention in the invocation.
  • ca: This means "and," connecting the ideas presented in the mantra.
  • sādhanaṁ: This term refers to "the means" or "the practice," suggesting that there are specific actions or rituals that can be undertaken to achieve a desired outcome.
  • viprāṇāṁ: This means "of the wise" or "of the learned," referring to those who possess knowledge and understanding, often the priests or sages in Vedic society.
  • cādhavam: This can be interpreted as "we seek" or "we strive for," indicating a desire to attain something through the invocation of the deity.

Conclusion: The mantra "maṁsīmahi tvā vayam asmākaṁ deva pūṣan | matīnāṁ ca sādhanaṁ viprāṇāṁ cādhavam ||" emphasizes the collective invocation of the deity Pūṣan for nourishment and prosperity. It highlights the importance of focused intention and the practices that can lead to spiritual and material fulfillment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as during agricultural rituals, community gatherings, or personal meditative practices aimed at seeking abundance and well-being. By invoking the divine with a collective mindset, individuals can foster a sense of unity and purpose, ultimately leading to a more prosperous and harmonious life.
