दधिक्राम अग्निम उषसं च देवीं बृहस्पतिं सवितारं च देवम् | अश्विनां मित्रावरुणा भागं च वसून रुद्रां अादित्यां इह हुवे ||
dadhikrām agnim uṣasaṁ ca devīm bṛhaspatiṁ savitāraṁ ca devam | aśvinā mitrāvaruṇā bhagaṁ ca vasūn rudrām̐ ādityām̐ iha huve ||
  • Translation: "I invoke the divine Agni, the goddess of dawn, the great sage Bṛhaspati, and the sun Savitar. I call upon the Aśvins, Mitra, Varuṇa, Bhaga, the Vasus, and Rudra, the Adityas, here."
  • Explanation of Key Terms:
    • Agni: The fire god, representing energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, fire is essential for cooking, warmth, and rituals.
    • Uṣasa: The goddess of dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and hope. Just as dawn brings light after darkness, it signifies opportunities and fresh starts in our lives.
    • Bṛhaspati: The guru of the gods, representing wisdom and knowledge. In our lives, we seek guidance from mentors and educators who help us navigate challenges.
    • Savitā: The sun god, embodying life and vitality. The sun is crucial for agriculture, health, and energy, reminding us of the importance of nature in our existence.
    • Aśvins: Twin gods associated with health and medicine. They symbolize the importance of well-being and the pursuit of a healthy lifestyle.
    • Mitra and Varuṇa: Deities representing friendship and cosmic order. They remind us of the significance of relationships and harmony in society.
    • Bhaga: The god of wealth and prosperity. This highlights the importance of abundance and the pursuit of a fulfilling life.
    • Vasūs: A group of deities representing various aspects of material wealth and prosperity. They signify the importance of resources in our daily lives.
    • Rudra: The storm god, representing transformation and the power of nature. He teaches us about resilience and the need to adapt to change.
    • Adityas: A group of solar deities, symbolizing the various aspects of life and the universe. They remind us of the interconnectedness of all things.
  • Significance: This mantra is a powerful invocation that calls upon various deities to seek their blessings for wisdom, prosperity, health, and harmony. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of different aspects of life and the divine forces that guide us.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In daily life, we can reflect on the qualities of these deities to inspire our actions. For instance, invoking Agni can remind us to pursue our passions with energy and dedication.
    • As we face challenges, we can look to Bṛhaspati for wisdom and guidance, seeking knowledge to make informed decisions.
    • In moments of uncertainty, recalling Uṣasa can inspire hope and the courage to embrace new beginnings.
    • In our relationships, we can embody the principles of Mitra and Varuṇa, fostering friendship and harmony in our communities.
