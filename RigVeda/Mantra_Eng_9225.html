प्रातर्युजं नासत्याधि तिष्ठतः प्रातर्यावाणं मधुवाहनं रथम् |विशो येन गच्छथो यज्वरीर नराः कीरेश चिद यज्ञं होत्रिमन्तम् अश्विनाः ||
prātaryujaṁ nāsatyādhi tiṣṭhathaḥ prātaryāvāṇam madhuvāhanaṁ ratham | viśo yena gacchatho yajvarīr narā kīreś cid yajñaṁ hotṛmantam aśvinā ||
  • prātaryujaṁ: This term can be broken down into "prātara," meaning "in the morning," and "yuj," which means "to unite" or "to harness." It signifies the beginning of a new day and the potential it brings.
  • nāsatyādhi: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities of health and medicine in Vedic literature. "Nāsatyā" can be interpreted as "the one who is truthful" or "the one who brings healing."
  • tiṣṭhathaḥ: This means "to stand" or "to remain." It suggests stability and presence, urging the listener to be steadfast.
  • prātaryāvāṇam: This can be understood as "the one who brings forth in the morning." It emphasizes the importance of new beginnings and the opportunities that arise with each dawn.
  • madhuvāhanaṁ: "Madhu" means "honey" or "sweetness," and "vāhana" refers to "that which carries." This phrase symbolizes the sweetness of life and the blessings that come with it.
  • ratham: Meaning "chariot," it represents the vehicle of life, which carries us through our journeys and experiences.
  • viśo: This term means "of the people" or "community," highlighting the collective aspect of human experience.
  • yena gacchatho: Translated as "by which you go," it emphasizes the path or journey one takes in life.
  • yajvarīr: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is devoted to sacrifice," indicating the importance of selflessness and contribution to the greater good.
  • narā: Meaning "men" or "humans," it signifies the human experience and the shared journey of life.
  • kīreś: This can be understood as "the one who is praised," suggesting the importance of recognition and honor in our endeavors.
  • cid: Meaning "consciousness" or "awareness," it emphasizes the need for mindfulness in our actions.
  • yajñaṁ: Referring to "sacrifice" or "offering," it highlights the significance of giving and sharing in life.
  • hotṛmantam: This term refers to "the one who invokes," indicating the role of a leader or guide in the community.
  • aśvinā: Again referring to the Aśvins, symbolizing health, well-being, and the divine assistance available to us.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of new beginnings, community, and the importance of mindfulness in our actions. It encourages individuals to embrace the opportunities that each day brings, to remain steadfast in their pursuits, and to contribute selflessly to the collective well-being. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as starting a new project, engaging in community service, or simply approaching each day with a positive mindset. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that we are all part of a larger journey, and our actions, guided by awareness and devotion, can lead to a harmonious and fulfilling life.
