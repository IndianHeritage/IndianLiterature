यत्र ग्रावा पृथुबुध्न ऊर्ध्वो भवति सोतवे |उलूकहालसुतानाम् अवेद व इन्द्र जलगुलः ||
yatra grāvā pṛthubudhna ūrdhvo bhavati sotave | ulūkhalasutānām aved v indra jalgulaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yatra grāvā pṛthubudhna ūrdhvo bhavati sotave | ulūkhalasutānām aved v indra jalgulaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yatra: where
    • grāvā: the strong or powerful
    • pṛthubudhna: the one who is broad-minded or wise
    • ūrdhvo: upward or elevated
    • bhavati: becomes or is
    • sotave: in the context of strength or power
    • ulūkhalasutānām: of those who are like owls (symbolizing wisdom or night creatures)
    • aved: knowledge or understanding
    • v: and
    • indra: the king of gods, representing power and authority
    • jalgulaḥ: the one who is fierce or strong
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about a place or state where the powerful and wise rise above challenges.
    • The reference to "ulūkhalasutānām" suggests a connection to wisdom that may come from those who are often overlooked or misunderstood, much like owls that are active at night.
    • Indra, as a symbol of strength and authority, indicates that this wisdom and power can lead to significant achievements and victories.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who is both powerful and wise can navigate challenges effectively, leading their team to success.
    • In personal life, individuals who rise above their circumstances, using their wisdom and strength, often achieve their goals despite obstacles.
    • In education, students who embrace knowledge and wisdom can elevate themselves and their peers, creating a positive environment for growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and strength in overcoming challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that true power comes from understanding and elevating oneself and others.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, effective leadership, and the ability to inspire others.
