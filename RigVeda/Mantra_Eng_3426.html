भूरिदा भूरी देहि नो मा दभ्रम भूरी आ भर |भूरी घेद इन्द्र दित्ससि ||
bhūridā bhūri dehi no mā dabhram bhūry ā bhara | bhūri ghed indra ditsasi ||
  • bhūridā: This term can be translated as "one who gives abundantly" or "generous." It signifies the importance of generosity in human interactions and relationships.
  • bhūri: This word means "much" or "many." It emphasizes the idea of abundance and the vastness of resources or blessings that one can receive or share.
  • dehi: This translates to "give" or "bestow." It is a call to action, urging the divine or others to provide what is needed.
  • no: This means "to us," indicating a personal plea or request for assistance or blessings.
  • mā: This word means "not," serving as a negation in the context of the mantra.
  • dabhram: This can be interpreted as "obstruction" or "hindrance." It suggests a desire to be free from obstacles that may prevent the flow of abundance.
  • bhūry: Again, this term signifies "much" or "many," reinforcing the theme of abundance.
  • ā: This is a preposition that can mean "towards" or "to," indicating direction or intention.
  • bhara: This means "to fill" or "to bring." It is a request for fulfillment and the provision of needs.
  • bhūri: Reiterating the concept of abundance, this word emphasizes the importance of receiving plentiful blessings.
  • ghed: This term can be understood as "to be strong" or "to be powerful," indicating a desire for strength and support.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, often associated with rain, storms, and strength. Invoking Indra signifies a request for divine intervention and support.
  • ditsasi: This translates to "you desire" or "you wish," indicating the intention of the divine to grant the requests made in the mantra.

Conclusion: The mantra "bhūridā bhūri dehi no mā dabhram bhūry ā bhara | bhūri ghed indra ditsasi ||" encapsulates a profound plea for abundance, generosity, and the removal of obstacles. It highlights the importance of seeking divine support in our endeavors and the need for strength in facing challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during times of need, when seeking help from others, or when striving for personal growth and fulfillment. It serves as a reminder to be generous and to seek abundance not just for oneself but for the community as a whole, fostering a spirit of cooperation and mutual support.
