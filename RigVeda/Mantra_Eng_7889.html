अभि गावो अधन्विषुर आपो न प्रवता यतीः |पुनाना इन्द्रं आशत ||
abhi gāvo adhanviṣur āpo na pravatā yatīḥ | punānā indram āśata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • gāvo: cows, representing wealth and nourishment
    • adhanviṣur: those who are skilled in the art of archery, symbolizing strength and skill
    • āpo: waters, often representing purity and life
    • na: not
    • pravatā: flowing, moving, or going
    • yatīḥ: those who are disciplined or ascetic
    • punānā: purifying or cleansing
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods, representing power and authority
    • āśata: may attain or achieve
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the pursuit of wealth and strength, symbolized by cows and skilled archers.
    • The mention of waters suggests a need for purity and the flow of life, indicating that one should not be stagnant but rather dynamic in their pursuits.
    • The term "yatīḥ" refers to those who practice discipline, suggesting that through discipline and purity, one can attain the blessings of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who is often associated with prosperity and success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the importance of water (āpo) is paramount. Farmers must ensure that their fields are irrigated and that they practice sustainable farming to maintain the flow of resources.
    • In personal development, individuals often seek to cultivate skills (adhanviṣur) through discipline and practice, much like an archer honing their craft to hit the target.
    • In business, the pursuit of wealth (gāvo) can be likened to a company striving for market leadership while ensuring ethical practices and sustainability.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of pursuing wealth and strength through discipline and purity.
    • It teaches that by aligning oneself with natural resources and maintaining a disciplined approach, one can achieve success and prosperity.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various fields such as agriculture, personal development, and business, where the balance of resources, skills, and ethical practices leads to holistic success.
