अभीम अवन्वन स्वभिष्टिम ऊतयोऽन्तरिक्षप्रां तविषीभिर आवृतम् |इन्द्रं दक्षास ऋभवो मदाच्युतं शतक्रतुं जवनī सूनृतारुहत ||
abhīm avanvan svabhiṣṭim ūtayo 'ntarikṣaprāṁ taviṣībhir āvṛtam | indraṁ dakṣāsa ṛbhavo madacyutaṁ śatakratuṁ javanī sūnṛtāruhat ||
  • abhīm: This word can be interpreted as "fearless" or "brave." It signifies the quality of courage and strength in facing challenges.
  • avanvan: This term refers to "protection" or "safeguarding." It emphasizes the importance of being shielded from harm.
  • svabhiṣṭim: This means "one's own desires" or "wishes." It highlights the significance of personal aspirations and goals.
  • ūtayo: This word translates to "with abundance" or "prosperity." It suggests a state of richness and fulfillment in life.
  • 'ntarikṣaprāṁ: This refers to "the celestial realm" or "the sky." It symbolizes the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • taviṣībhir: This means "with strength" or "powerful." It denotes the importance of vigor and energy in achieving one's goals.
  • āvṛtam: This translates to "surrounded" or "enveloped." It signifies being encompassed by positive influences or energies.
  • indraṁ: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, representing power, authority, and leadership.
  • dakṣāsa: This term means "skilled" or "capable." It emphasizes the value of competence and expertise.
  • ṛbhavo: This refers to "the wise" or "the learned." It highlights the importance of knowledge and wisdom in life.
  • madacyutaṁ: This translates to "the one who is intoxicated with joy." It signifies the blissful state of being that comes from achieving one's desires.
  • śatakratuṁ: This means "the one with a hundred powers" or "the omnipotent." It symbolizes limitless potential and capability.
  • javanī: This term refers to "speed" or "swiftness." It emphasizes the importance of quick action and responsiveness.
  • sūnṛtāruhat: This translates to "the one who upholds truth." It signifies the value of honesty and integrity in one's actions.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of strength, protection, and the pursuit of one's desires while being surrounded by positive energies. It emphasizes the importance of courage, skill, wisdom, and integrity in achieving success. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, leadership, and teamwork. For instance, a leader who embodies these qualities can inspire their team to work towards common goals with confidence and integrity. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate these virtues in our lives to navigate challenges and achieve our aspirations effectively.
