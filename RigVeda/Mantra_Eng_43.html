स घा नो योग आ भुवत स राय स पुरन्द्याम् |गमद वाजेभिर आ स नः ||
sa ghā no yoga ā bhuvat sa rāye sa puraṁdhyām | gamad vājebhir ā sa naḥ ||
  • sa ghā no yoga ā bhuvat: This phrase can be translated as "May that which is beneficial come to us." Here, "sa" refers to "that," "ghā" means "beneficial," "no" means "to us," "yoga" refers to "union" or "connection," and "ā bhuvat" means "may it come." This part of the mantra emphasizes the desire for positive connections and beneficial outcomes in life.
  • sa rāye: This translates to "that which brings prosperity." "Rāye" signifies wealth or prosperity. This part of the mantra expresses a wish for abundance and success in various aspects of life, whether material, emotional, or spiritual.
  • sa puraṁdhyām: This can be interpreted as "that which is the source of strength." "Puraṁdhyām" refers to a place of strength or a stronghold. This phrase highlights the importance of seeking strength and stability in our lives, which can be derived from various sources such as family, community, or personal resilience.
  • gamad vājebhir ā: This translates to "may we be led by the best of energies." "Gamad" means "to lead," "vājebhir" refers to "energies" or "forces," and "ā" means "towards." This part of the mantra encourages individuals to be guided by positive energies and forces in their lives, which can manifest as inspiration, motivation, or support from others.
  • sa naḥ: This means "may it be ours." "Naḥ" refers to "us" or "ours." This concluding phrase encapsulates the desire for the blessings and benefits mentioned in the mantra to be received by the individual or community.

Conclusion:

The mantra "sa ghā no yoga ā bhuvat sa rāye sa puraṁdhyām | gamad vājebhir ā sa naḥ ||" serves as a powerful invocation for prosperity, strength, and positive connections in life. Its significance lies in the holistic approach it promotes—seeking not just material wealth but also emotional and spiritual well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal challenges, community gatherings, or even in business settings where collaboration and mutual support are essential. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset focused on attracting positive energies and fostering beneficial relationships, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and prosperous life.
