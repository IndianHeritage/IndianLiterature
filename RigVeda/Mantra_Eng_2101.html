मन्त्र: स्तवाऽनु त इन्द्र पूर्व्याः महान्युत स्तवाम नूतनाः कृतानि |स्तवाऽ वज्रम बाह्वोर उशन्तं स्तवाऽ हरिः सूर्यस्य केतुः ||
stavā nu ta indra pūrvyā mahāny uta stavāma nūtanā kṛtāni | stavā vajram bāhvor uśantaṁ stavā harī sūryasya ketū ||
  • stavā nu ta indra pūrvyā mahāny uta stavāma nūtanā kṛtāni
    • stavā: This word means "praise" or "worship." It signifies the act of honoring a deity or a powerful force.
    • nu: This is an expression of emphasis, often translated as "indeed" or "truly."
    • ta: Refers to "you," addressing the deity directly.
    • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, associated with rain, thunder, and strength.
    • pūrvyā: This means "from the east," indicating a direction that is often associated with new beginnings and the rising sun.
    • mahāny: Translates to "great" or "mighty," emphasizing the power of Indra.
    • uta: This word means "or" and is used to present an alternative or additional thought.
    • stavāma: This is a form of the verb "to praise," indicating a collective act of worship.
    • nūtanā: Meaning "new" or "fresh," suggesting the renewal of strength or blessings.
    • kṛtāni: This refers to "deeds" or "actions," indicating the importance of actions in the context of worship.
  • stavā vajram bāhvor uśantaṁ
    • vajram: This means "thunderbolt" or "weapon," symbolizing strength and power.
    • bāhvor: Refers to "of the arms," indicating the physical strength and might of Indra.
    • uśantaṁ: This means "the one who is awake" or "alert," suggesting the readiness and vigilance of the deity.
  • stavā harī sūryasya ketū
    • harī: This term can refer to "the divine" or "the one who removes obstacles," often associated with Vishnu.
    • sūryasya: Meaning "of the sun," indicating the source of light, energy, and life.
    • ketū: This translates to "banner" or "flag," symbolizing victory and the presence of divine protection.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of praising and invoking the divine forces, particularly Indra, who represents strength, power, and renewal. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In times of challenge or adversity, individuals can seek strength and guidance through prayer and positive affirmations, much like the worship of Indra for protection and support.
  • The concept of renewal can be seen in personal growth, where one acknowledges past achievements and seeks new beginnings, akin to the "nūtanā kṛtāni" aspect of the mantra.
  • In community settings, collective worship and praise can foster unity and strength, similar to the collective invocation of divine blessings in the mantra.

This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of faith, the importance of collective strength, and the potential for renewal in our lives.
