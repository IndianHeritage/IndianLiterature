उद अपप्तन्न अरुणा भानवो वृत्था स्वायुजो अरुषीर गा आयुक्षत |अक्रन्न उषासो वयुनानि पूर्वथा रुशन्तं भानुम अरुषीर अशिश्रयुः ||
ud apaptann aruṇā bhānavo vṛthā svāyujo aruṣīr gā ayukṣata | akrann uṣāso vayunāni pūrvathā ruśantam bhānum aruṣīr aśiśrayuḥ ||
  • ud apaptann: This phrase can be interpreted as "rising up" or "emerging." It signifies the dawn or the beginning of a new day, symbolizing hope and new opportunities.
  • aruṇā bhānavo: "Arunā" refers to the reddish hue of the dawn, while "bhānavo" means "of the sun." Together, they evoke the image of the sun rising, bringing light and warmth to the world.
  • vṛthā svāyujo: "Vṛthā" means "in vain" or "without purpose," and "svāyujo" can be interpreted as "those who are self-sufficient." This suggests that the dawn and the sun are not just beautiful but serve a significant purpose in sustaining life.
  • aruṣīr gā ayukṣata: "Aruṣīr" refers to the red or glowing, while "gā ayukṣata" can be understood as "they have come forth." This line emphasizes the arrival of the dawn and the sun, which are essential for life on Earth.
  • akrann uṣāso: "Akrann" means "they have not harmed," and "uṣāso" refers to the dawn. This suggests that the dawn brings peace and tranquility, without causing harm to the world.
  • vayunāni pūrvathā: "Vayunāni" means "the forces" or "the powers," and "pūrvathā" refers to "from the east." This indicates that the powers of nature, particularly those associated with the dawn, originate from the east.
  • ruśantam bhānum: "Ruśantam" means "shining" or "radiant," and "bhānum" refers to the sun. This line highlights the brilliance of the sun as it rises, illuminating the world.
  • aruṣīr aśiśrayuḥ: "Aruṣīr" again refers to the red or glowing, while "aśiśrayuḥ" can be interpreted as "they have taken shelter." This suggests that all beings find refuge in the light and warmth of the sun.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda celebrates the arrival of dawn and the sun, symbolizing hope, renewal, and the sustenance of life. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to moments of new beginnings, such as starting a new project, entering a new phase in life, or overcoming challenges. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that each day brings new opportunities and that the forces of nature, represented by the sun, provide the necessary energy and light for growth and prosperity. It encourages individuals to embrace the dawn of each day with optimism and gratitude, recognizing the vital role that light plays in our lives.
