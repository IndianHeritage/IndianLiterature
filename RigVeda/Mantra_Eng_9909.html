अश्वत्ते वो निषादनं पर्णे वो वसतिष कृताः |गोभाजा इति किलासथ यत सनवथ पूरुषम् ||
aśvatthe vo niṣadanam parṇe vo vasatiṣ kṛtā | gobhāja it kilāsatha yat sanavatha pūruṣam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvatthe: In the sacred fig tree (Ficus religiosa), often considered a symbol of enlightenment and wisdom.
    • vo: For you (plural), addressing a group or community.
    • niṣadanam: A place to sit or dwell, indicating a space of comfort or refuge.
    • parṇe: On the leaves, which can symbolize nourishment and sustenance.
    • vasatiṣ: Residing or dwelling, suggesting a sense of permanence or stability.
    • kṛtā: Made or created, indicating an action that has been completed.
    • gobhāja: The one who is nourished by the cows, often referring to a person who is virtuous or righteous.
    • it: Indeed, emphasizing the statement that follows.
    • kilāsatha: You are indeed seated or established, reinforcing the idea of stability.
    • yat: That which, introducing a clause that provides further explanation.
    • sanavatha: The one who is endowed with strength or power.
    • pūruṣam: The person or human being, often referring to the ideal individual or the self.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of finding a place of refuge and stability under the sacred fig tree, which is a symbol of wisdom and enlightenment.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and support, as it addresses a group and suggests that they are nourished and strengthened together.
    • The reference to being nourished by cows can symbolize the sustenance provided by nature and the importance of living in harmony with the environment.
    • Overall, the mantra conveys a message of unity, strength, and the importance of a supportive community in achieving personal and collective growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community garden, individuals come together to cultivate plants, sharing resources and knowledge, much like the nourishment provided by the sacred fig tree.
    • Support groups for mental health emphasize the importance of community and shared experiences, reflecting the idea of finding strength in unity.
    • Environmental movements often highlight the need to live in harmony with nature, akin to the nourishment derived from the cows in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of community, support, and the connection to nature in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as community building, environmental sustainability, and personal growth.
    • By recognizing the value of unity and nourishment from our surroundings, we can foster a more harmonious and fulfilling existence.
