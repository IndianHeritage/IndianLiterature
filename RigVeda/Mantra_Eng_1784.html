नित्यं न सूनुम् मधु बिभ्रता उप क्रीलन्ति क्रीला विदथेṣu घृष्वयः |नक्षन्ति रुद्राः अवसाऽनमस्विनं न मर्धन्ति स्वतवसो हविष्कृतम् ||
nityaṁ na sūnum madhu bibhrata upa krīḻanti krīḻā vidatheṣu ghṛṣvayaḥ | nakṣanti rudrā avasā namasvinaṁ na mardhanti svatavaso haviṣkṛtam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nityaṁ: eternal, constant
    • na: not
    • sūnum: son, offspring
    • madhu: honey, sweetness
    • bibhrata: holding, possessing
    • upa: towards, near
    • krīḻanti: they play, they engage
    • krīḻā: play, sport
    • vidatheṣu: in the gatherings, assemblies
    • ghṛṣvayaḥ: those who are wise, learned
    • nakṣanti: they do not harm
    • rudrā: the fierce ones, often referring to deities
    • avasā: in a state of surrender
    • namasvinaṁ: to the respectful ones
    • na: not
    • mardhanti: they crush, they destroy
    • svatavaso: those who are self-controlled
    • haviṣkṛtam: offerings, sacrifices
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the eternal nature of existence and the importance of engaging in joyful activities without causing harm.
    • The phrase "nityaṁ na sūnum madhu bibhrata" suggests that true sweetness and joy are found in eternal values rather than transient pleasures.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and gathering ("vidatheṣu") where wise individuals ("ghṛṣvayaḥ") come together to share knowledge and joy.
    • The mantra also highlights the idea that those who are self-controlled and respectful ("namasvinaṁ") do not engage in destructive behaviors, even in the presence of fierce energies ("rudrā").
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, fostering a culture of collaboration and joy can lead to greater productivity and satisfaction among employees. This aligns with the idea of engaging in joyful activities within a community.
    • In personal relationships, prioritizing respect and self-control can prevent conflicts and promote harmony, reflecting the essence of the mantra.
    • In educational settings, creating an environment where students can learn and play together encourages wisdom and growth, resonating with the gathering of wise individuals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of joy, community, and respect in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek eternal values and engage in positive interactions, avoiding harm to others.
    • In a world often filled with conflict and competition, the teachings of this mantra can guide us towards a more harmonious and fulfilling existence.
