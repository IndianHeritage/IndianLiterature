उच्छ्वञ्चमानā पृथिवी सु तिष्ठतु सहस्रं मित उप हि श्रयन्ताम् |ते गृहासो घृतश्चुतो भवन्तु विश्वाहास्मै शरणाः सन्त्व् अत्र ||
ucchvañcamānā pṛthivī su tiṣṭhatu sahasram mita upa hi śrayantām | te gṛhāso ghṛtaścuto bhavantu viśvāhāsmai śaraṇāḥ santv atra ||
  • ucchvañcamānā: This term can be broken down into "ucchvañca," which means "to rise" or "to elevate." It signifies the aspiration for growth and elevation in life.
  • pṛthivī: This word refers to "the Earth" or "the world." It symbolizes the physical realm we inhabit and the resources it provides.
  • su tiṣṭhatu: "Su" means "well" or "good," and "tiṣṭhatu" means "to stand" or "to be stable." Together, they convey the idea of stability and prosperity in life.
  • sahasram: This translates to "a thousand," indicating abundance and the vastness of opportunities available to us.
  • mita: This means "measured" or "moderate," suggesting the importance of balance in our pursuits and desires.
  • upa hi śrayantām: "Upa" means "near" or "towards," "hi" is an emphatic particle, and "śrayantām" means "to take shelter." This phrase emphasizes seeking refuge or support in times of need.
  • te gṛhāso: "Te" means "your," and "gṛhāso" refers to "householders" or "those who dwell." It signifies the importance of community and family in our lives.
  • ghṛtaścuto: "Ghṛta" means "clarity" or "purity," and "cuto" implies "to be free from." This suggests the aspiration for a life free from negativity and filled with clarity.
  • bhavantu: This means "may they become" or "may they be." It expresses a wish or blessing for the future.
  • viśvāhāsmai: "Viśvā" means "all" or "universal," and "hāsmai" refers to "to him" or "to that." This indicates a universal appeal or blessing.
  • śaraṇāḥ: This translates to "shelters" or "refuges," emphasizing the need for safety and support in our lives.
  • santv atra: "Santv" means "may there be" or "let there be," and "atra" means "here." This phrase calls for the presence of blessings in our current situation.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the importance of growth, stability, community, and seeking refuge in times of need. It encourages individuals to aspire for abundance while maintaining balance and clarity in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, family dynamics, and community building. For instance, in a workplace setting, fostering a supportive environment where colleagues uplift each other can lead to collective growth and success. Similarly, in personal relationships, seeking clarity and stability can enhance the quality of interactions and create a nurturing atmosphere. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our aspirations and the importance of a supportive community in achieving them.
