विचेदुच्छन्त्यश्विनौ उषासः प्र वाम ब्रह्माणि कारवो भरण्ते |ऊर्ध्वं भानुं सविता देवो अश्रेद्बृहदग्नयः समिधा जारन्ते ||
vi ced ucchanty aśvinā uṣāsaḥ pra vām brahmāṇi kāravo bharante | ūrdhvam bhānuṁ savitā devo aśred bṛhad agnayaḥ samidhā jarante ||
  • Translation: "When the Aśvins (the twin gods) call out, the dawns (Uṣāsa) come forth; may the divine powers (Brahmāṇi) bring forth the actions (kāravo) for you. The sun (Bhānu) rises high, and the divine Savitā (the sun god) ignites the great fires (Agnaya) that are kindled."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Vi ced: When (indicating a condition or time)
    • Ucchanty: Call out or proclaim
    • Aśvinā: The Aśvins, twin deities associated with health and medicine
    • Uṣāsaḥ: The dawns, symbolizing new beginnings and hope
    • Pra vām: For you, indicating a personal connection
    • Brahmāṇi: Divine powers or energies
    • Kāravo: Actions or deeds
    • Ūrdhvam: Upward or high
    • Bhānuṁ: The sun, representing light and knowledge
    • Savitā: The sun god, associated with creation and vitality
    • Ašred: Ignites or kindles
    • Bṛhad: Great or vast
    • Agnayaḥ: Fires, symbolizing energy and transformation
    • Samidhā: Kindled or lit
    • Jarante: They are kindled or burn
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The Aśvins represent the duality of life, akin to how we often face challenges and opportunities simultaneously. For instance, in a medical emergency, one might call for help (like the Aśvins) while also seeking hope (the dawn).
    • The concept of dawn (Uṣāsa) can be related to new beginnings in our lives, such as starting a new job or moving to a new city, symbolizing hope and fresh opportunities.
    • The sun (Bhānu) rising can be compared to gaining knowledge and enlightenment, much like how education opens new horizons for individuals.
    • The act of kindling fires (Agnaya) can be seen in rituals or gatherings where fire symbolizes transformation, such as weddings or festivals, where new relationships and communities are formed.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine intervention and the cyclical nature of life. It signifies that with every dawn, there is a chance for renewal and action. In real life, this can be applied to personal growth, where one seeks guidance and support during transitions. The invocation of the Aśvins and the sun god serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our endeavors; divine energies are always at play, encouraging us to rise, act, and transform our lives for the better.
