विषू चर स्वधा अनु कृष्टीनाम् अन्वाहुवः |इन्द्र पिब सुतानाम् ||
vi ṣū cara svadhā anu kṛṣṭīnām anv āhuvaḥ | indra piba sutānām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viṣū: This can be interpreted as "in the midst of" or "among." It signifies being surrounded by or existing within a certain context.
    • cara: This means "to move" or "to go." It implies action, progress, or the act of navigating through life.
    • svadhā: This term refers to "self" or "one's own." It is often associated with personal identity and the essence of being.
    • anu: This means "following" or "in accordance with." It suggests alignment or adherence to a principle or path.
    • kṛṣṭīnām: This translates to "of the cultivated" or "of the plowed fields." It symbolizes growth, development, and the fruits of labor.
    • anv āhuvaḥ: This can be understood as "we invoke" or "we call upon." It indicates a plea or request for divine assistance or blessings.
    • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, often associated with strength, power, and rain. He represents authority and protection.
    • piba: This means "drink." It signifies the act of receiving or partaking in something beneficial.
    • sutānām: This translates to "of the offerings" or "of the libations." It refers to the sacred offerings made to the deities.
  • Overall Meaning: The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine, specifically to Indra, to partake in the offerings made by the devotees. It emphasizes the importance of personal identity and growth while navigating through life, invoking divine blessings for prosperity and success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often invoke the blessings of deities like Indra for rain and good harvests. This mantra can be recited during rituals to seek divine favor for their cultivated fields.
    • In personal development, individuals may reflect on their identity and the paths they choose. This mantra serves as a reminder to align one's actions with their true self and to seek guidance in their endeavors.
    • During community gatherings or festivals, this mantra can be recited to foster a sense of unity and collective growth, invoking blessings for the well-being of all members.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine intervention in the journey of life. It highlights the importance of self-awareness, growth, and the need for support from higher powers. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in agricultural practices, personal development, and community bonding, reminding individuals to seek blessings and align their actions with their true purpose.
