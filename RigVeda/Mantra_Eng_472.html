प्र यद इत्त्था परावतः शोचिर न मानम् अस्यथ |कस्य क्रत्वा मरुतः कस्य वर्पसां कं यातः कं ह धूतयः ||
pra yad itthā parāvataḥ śocir na mānam asyatha | kasya kratvā marutaḥ kasya varpasā kaṁ yātha kaṁ ha dhūtayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: for, towards
    • yad: what, which
    • itthā: thus, in this way
    • parāvataḥ: of the great wind, the powerful
    • śocir: shining, illuminating
    • na: not
    • mānam: pride, honor
    • asyatha: you should not have
    • kasya: whose
    • kratvā: by the power, strength
    • marutaḥ: of the winds, the storm gods
    • kasya: whose
    • varpasā: by the brilliance, radiance
    • kaṁ: whom
    • yātha: you should go, you should approach
    • kaṁ: whom
    • ha: indeed
    • dhūtayaḥ: the purifiers, the cleansers
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the relationship between the individual and the forces of nature, particularly the winds (Maruts).
    • It emphasizes humility and the importance of not being prideful in the face of the vastness of the universe.
    • The phrase "you should not have pride" suggests that one should remain grounded and aware of their place in the cosmos.
    • The reference to the winds and their power indicates that nature is a force to be respected and revered.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in environmental awareness. For instance, recognizing the impact of climate change and the power of natural forces can lead to more sustainable living practices.
    • In personal relationships, the idea of humility can foster better communication and understanding. For example, in a team setting, acknowledging the contributions of others can lead to a more harmonious work environment.
    • In spiritual practices, this mantra can serve as a reminder to approach life with reverence and gratitude, recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for humility and respect towards the natural world and its forces.
    • It encourages individuals to reflect on their actions and their impact on the environment and society.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can cultivate a sense of balance and harmony in their life, leading to personal growth and a deeper connection with the world around them.
