ममच चन तव युवतिः परास ममच चन तव कुषवाः जगारा |ममच चिद आपः शिशवे ममृड्युर ममच चिद इन्द्रः सहसोद अतिष्ठत ||
mamac cana tvā yuvatiḥ parāsa mamac cana tvā kuṣavā jagāra | mamac cid āpaḥ śiśave mamṛḍyur mamac cid indraḥ sahasod atiṣṭhat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mamac: My
    • cana: Also
    • tvā: You
    • yuvatiḥ: Young woman or maiden
    • parāsa: To be protected or cherished
    • kuṣavā: Good or auspicious
    • jagāra: Awakened or enlightened
    • cid: Consciousness or awareness
    • āpaḥ: Waters or life-giving forces
    • śiśave: To be born or generated
    • mamṛḍyur: My life or vitality
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods
    • sahasod: With great power or strength
    • atiṣṭhat: Stood or established
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep connection between the speaker and a young woman, emphasizing the importance of protection and nurturing.
    • The phrase "mamac cana tvā yuvatiḥ parāsa" suggests a desire for the young woman to be cherished and safeguarded, reflecting the universal human need for love and care.
    • The mention of "kuṣavā jagāra" indicates a state of enlightenment or awakening, which can be related to personal growth and self-awareness in real life.
    • The reference to "cid āpaḥ" symbolizes the life-giving forces, akin to the nurturing aspects of nature that sustain us, such as water and nourishment.
    • Furthermore, "mamṛḍyur" signifies the vitality of life, which can be interpreted as the importance of maintaining one's health and well-being.
    • Lastly, invoking "indraḥ sahasod atiṣṭhat" connects the speaker to divine strength and support, suggesting that one can draw upon greater powers in times of need.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In relationships, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to cherish and protect loved ones, fostering a nurturing environment.
    • In personal development, the idea of awakening and enlightenment can be applied to practices such as meditation or self-reflection, leading to greater self-awareness.
    • The emphasis on life-giving forces can relate to the importance of clean water and nutrition in our daily lives, highlighting the need for a healthy lifestyle.
    • In challenging situations, invoking strength and support can be likened to seeking help from friends, family, or even spiritual practices to overcome obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of love, protection, and the pursuit of enlightenment, making it relevant in various aspects of life.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder to nurture relationships, seek personal growth, and draw upon inner strength and external support.
    • By applying the teachings of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a more fulfilling and harmonious life, both personally and in their interactions with others.
