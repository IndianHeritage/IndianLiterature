यम ई गर्भं ऋतावृधो दृष्टे चारुम अजीजनन |कविम मंहिष्ठम अध्वरे पुरुष्पृहं ||
yam ī garbham ṛtāvṛdho dṛśe cārum ajījanan | kavim maṁhiṣṭham adhvare puruspṛham ||
  • yam: This word translates to "whom" or "that which." It indicates a subject or an entity that is being referred to.
  • ī: This is a prefix that can denote possession or connection, often used to emphasize the subject.
  • garbham: This means "womb" or "embryo," symbolizing the source of life and creation.
  • ṛtāvṛdho: This term can be broken down into "ṛta," meaning "truth" or "cosmic order," and "vṛdho," which means "nurtured" or "supported." Together, it suggests that the womb is nurtured by the cosmic order.
  • dṛśe: This translates to "sight" or "vision," indicating perception or awareness.
  • cārum: This means "beautiful" or "charming," often used to describe something that is aesthetically pleasing.
  • ajījanan: This word means "gave birth" or "produced," referring to the act of creation or manifestation.
  • kavim: This translates to "the wise one" or "the poet," often used to denote a creator or a divine figure with profound knowledge.
  • maṁhiṣṭham: This means "the most excellent" or "the best," indicating superiority or high status.
  • adhvare: This term refers to "sacrifice" or "ritual," often associated with Vedic ceremonies that connect the material and spiritual worlds.
  • puruspṛham: This can be interpreted as "the one who is to be worshipped" or "the one who is revered," indicating a divine presence worthy of respect and devotion.

The mantra can be understood as a profound reflection on the process of creation and the divine forces that govern it. It speaks of the womb that is nurtured by cosmic truth, giving birth to beauty and wisdom. The wise creator, who is revered in rituals, embodies the essence of excellence and is central to the act of creation.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Parenthood: The concept of nurturing life in the womb can be related to the responsibilities of parents in providing a loving and supportive environment for their children.
  • Creativity: Artists and creators can draw inspiration from the idea of beauty and wisdom emerging from the creative process, emphasizing the importance of nurturing one's talents.
  • Spiritual Practices: The reference to rituals highlights the significance of spiritual practices in connecting with the divine and understanding the cosmic order.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, creation, and the divine. It serves as a reminder of the beauty and wisdom inherent in the process of nurturing and creation, encouraging individuals to recognize and honor these aspects in their own lives. By understanding and applying the principles encapsulated in this mantra, one can foster a deeper appreciation for the cycles of life and the importance of nurturing both oneself and others.
