न घा राजेन्द्र आदभन नो या नु स्वसारा कृणवन्त योनौ |आपश्चिदस्मै सुतुका अवेषन गमन न इन्द्रः सख्या वयश्च ||
na ghā rājendra ā dabhan no yā nu svasārā kṛṇavanta yonau | āpaś cid asmai sutukā aveṣan gaman na indraḥ sakhyā vayaś ca ||
  • na ghā rājendra ā dabhan: This phrase can be translated as "O King, do not be angry." Here, "rājendra" refers to a king or a ruler, and "ghā" implies anger or wrath. In real life, this can be seen in situations where leaders or authority figures are urged to maintain composure and not let anger dictate their actions, such as in political negotiations or conflict resolution.
  • no yā nu svasārā kṛṇavanta yonau: This translates to "Do not let the sisters of the family be harmed." The term "svasārā" refers to sisters, and "kṛṇavanta" implies causing harm. This can be related to the importance of protecting family members and ensuring their safety, which is a universal value across cultures. For example, in many societies, there are laws and social norms that protect women and children from violence and abuse.
  • āpaś cid asmai sutukā aveṣan: This can be interpreted as "May the waters bring forth prosperity." "Āpaś" refers to water, which is often associated with life and sustenance. In real life, this can be connected to the significance of water in agriculture, where irrigation is crucial for crop production. For instance, in regions dependent on monsoon rains, the timely arrival of water can determine the success of the harvest.
  • gaman na indraḥ sakhyā vayaś ca: This translates to "May Indra, the god of rain, grant us friendship and strength." "Indra" is a prominent deity in Vedic literature associated with rain and storms. This phrase emphasizes the importance of divine blessings in fostering community and strength. In modern contexts, this can be seen in collaborative efforts among communities to achieve common goals, such as disaster relief or environmental conservation.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of maintaining composure, protecting loved ones, seeking prosperity through natural resources, and fostering community strength through divine blessings. In real life, these themes are applicable in various scenarios, such as family dynamics, community safety, agricultural practices, and collaborative efforts for social good. The significance of this mantra lies in its timeless wisdom, reminding us of the values that sustain harmonious living and the importance of seeking balance in our actions and relationships.
