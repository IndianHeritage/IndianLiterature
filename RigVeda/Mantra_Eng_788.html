वेद्हा अदृप्तो अग्निर विजानन्न ऊधर न गोनाṁ स्वाद्मा पितूनाम् ||
vedhā adṛpto agnir vijānann ūdhar na gonāṁ svādmā pitūnām ||
  • vedhā: This term can be understood as "knowledge" or "wisdom." It signifies the importance of understanding and insight in our lives.
  • adṛpto: This word means "unseen" or "invisible." It suggests that there are aspects of knowledge and existence that are not immediately apparent to us.
  • agnir: Translated as "fire," it symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine spark of life. Fire is often associated with purification and the ability to bring about change.
  • vijānann: This means "knowing" or "understanding." It emphasizes the importance of awareness and comprehension in our actions and decisions.
  • ūdhar: This term can be interpreted as "to uplift" or "to elevate." It signifies the act of raising oneself or others to a higher state of being.
  • na gonāṁ: This phrase translates to "of the cows" or "of the beings." In a broader sense, it can refer to the nurturing and sustaining aspects of life.
  • svādmā: This means "sweet" or "pleasant." It indicates the joy and satisfaction that comes from understanding and experiencing life fully.
  • pitūnām: This word translates to "of the fathers" or "of the ancestors." It highlights the connection to lineage, heritage, and the wisdom passed down through generations.

The mantra "vedhā adṛpto agnir vijānann ūdhar na gonāṁ svādmā pitūnām" encapsulates the essence of knowledge, transformation, and the importance of understanding our roots. It suggests that true wisdom is often hidden and requires effort to uncover. The fire symbolizes the transformative power of knowledge, which can uplift us and those around us.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Education: Just as knowledge is essential for personal growth, this mantra encourages individuals to seek deeper understanding in their studies and professions.
  • Personal Development: The idea of uplifting oneself resonates with the journey of self-improvement, where one strives to elevate their thoughts and actions.
  • Community and Family: Recognizing the wisdom of our ancestors and the nurturing aspects of life can foster stronger family bonds and community ties.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of knowledge and understanding in our lives. It encourages us to seek wisdom, embrace transformation, and honor our heritage, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and enriched existence.
