मन्त्र: वैश्वानराय धिषणाम ऋतावृधे घृतं न पूतम अग्नये जनामसि | द्वितā होतारं मनुषश्च वाघतो धियā रथं न कुलिशः सम ऋण्वति ||
vaiśvānarāya dhiṣaṇām ṛtāvṛdhe ghṛtaṁ na pūtam agnaye janāmasi | dvitā hotāram manuṣaś ca vāghato dhiyā rathaṁ na kuliśaḥ sam ṛṇvati ||
  • vaiśvānarāya: This term refers to the universal aspect of the divine, often associated with the fire god Agni. It signifies the all-pervading nature of divinity that connects all beings.
  • dhiṣaṇām: This word means 'of the mind' or 'of the intellect'. It emphasizes the importance of mental clarity and wisdom in our actions and decisions.
  • ṛtāvṛdhe: This term relates to the concept of 'ṛta', which signifies cosmic order and truth. It suggests that our actions should align with the universal laws and principles.
  • ghṛtaṁ: This means 'ghee' or 'clarified butter', which is often used in Vedic rituals. It symbolizes purity and nourishment, both physically and spiritually.
  • na pūtam: This phrase translates to 'not spoiled' or 'not contaminated'. It indicates the importance of maintaining purity in our thoughts and actions.
  • agnaye: Referring to Agni, the fire god, this term highlights the significance of fire in rituals and as a symbol of transformation and energy.
  • janāmasi: This means 'we are born' or 'we come into being'. It signifies the act of creation and the importance of our existence in the universe.
  • dvitā: This term means 'second' or 'dual'. It can refer to the duality of existence, such as the physical and spiritual realms.
  • hotāram: This refers to the 'priest' or 'one who offers', emphasizing the role of individuals who perform rituals and connect with the divine.
  • manuṣaś: This means 'human beings'. It highlights the importance of humanity in the cosmic order and the responsibilities that come with it.
  • vāghato: This term relates to 'speech' or 'words', indicating the power of communication and expression in shaping our reality.
  • dhiya: This means 'intellect' or 'understanding', emphasizing the need for wisdom in our actions and decisions.
  • rathaṁ: This translates to 'chariot', symbolizing the journey of life and the direction we choose to take.
  • na kuliśaḥ: This phrase means 'not a thunderbolt' or 'not destructive', indicating the importance of non-violence and harmony in our actions.
  • sam ṛṇvati: This means 'to be in harmony' or 'to unite', emphasizing the significance of unity and cooperation among all beings.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of universal connection, the importance of purity in thought and action, and the need for wisdom in navigating life's journey. It serves as a reminder that our existence is intertwined with the cosmic order, and our actions should reflect harmony and understanding. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal relationships, community interactions, and even in professional settings, where collaboration and respect for one another's existence are crucial for a balanced and fulfilling life.
