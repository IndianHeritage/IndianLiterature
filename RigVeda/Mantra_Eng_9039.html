आयं विप्राय दाशुषे वाजाम् इयर्ति गोमता: |आयं सप्तभ्य आ वरं वि वो मदे प्राण्डं श्रोणं च तारीषद विवक्षसे ||
ayaṁ viprāya dāśuṣe vājām̐ iyarti gomataḥ | ayaṁ saptabhya ā varaṁ vi vo made prāndhaṁ śroṇaṁ ca tāriṣad vivakṣase ||
  • ayaṁ viprāya dāśuṣe vājām̐ iyarti gomataḥ: This line can be translated to "This (offering) is for the wise (vipra) and for the one who serves (dāśuṣe), may it bring forth the strength (vājām̐) of the cows (gomataḥ)."
    • ayaṁ: This refers to "this," indicating the offering or the action being performed.
    • viprāya: Refers to the wise or learned individuals, often priests or sages who possess knowledge.
    • dāśuṣe: Refers to those who serve, indicating a sense of duty and dedication.
    • vājām̐: Means strength or vigor, often associated with the power of horses or cattle.
    • gomataḥ: Refers to cows, which are considered sacred and valuable in Vedic culture.
  • ayaṁ saptabhya ā varaṁ vi vo made prāndhaṁ śroṇaṁ ca tāriṣad vivakṣase: This line translates to "This (offering) is for the seven (saptabhya), may it bring you the best (varaṁ), and may you be filled with joy (made) and may you attain the desired (tāriṣad) through your intentions (vivakṣase)."
    • saptabhya: Refers to the seven, which could symbolize completeness or a group of important entities.
    • ā varaṁ: Means "the best" or "the most excellent," indicating the quality of the offering.
    • vi vo made: Refers to being filled with joy or happiness.
    • prāndhaṁ: Indicates vitality or life force.
    • śroṇaṁ: Refers to the essence or core of something.
    • tāriṣad: Means to attain or achieve.
    • vivakṣase: Refers to intentions or desires, indicating the importance of clarity in one's goals.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings made with intention and respect towards the wise and those who serve. It highlights the significance of strength, joy, and the pursuit of excellence in life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as community service, where individuals come together to support the wise and the needy, or in personal endeavors where one seeks to achieve their goals with clarity and purpose. The mantra serves as a reminder to approach life with dedication, to value knowledge, and to strive for the best outcomes while fostering joy and vitality in our actions.
