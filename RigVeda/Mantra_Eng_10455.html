यद उलूकः वदति मोघम् एतद यत कपोतः पदम् अग्नौ कृत्यति |यस्य दूतः प्रहित एष एतत तस्मै यमाय नमो अस्तु मृत्यवे ||
yad ulūko vadati mogham etad yat kapotaḥ padam agnau kṛṇoti | yasya dūtaḥ prahita eṣa etat tasmai yamāya namo astu mṛtyave ||
  • Mantra: "yad ulūko vadati mogham etad yat kapotaḥ padam agnau kṛṇoti |
  • Translation: "What the owl speaks is futile; what the pigeon offers is to the fire."
  • Meaning: This mantra reflects on the nature of communication and offerings. The owl, often associated with wisdom, is said to speak words that are ultimately meaningless or without purpose. In contrast, the pigeon, which is often seen as a symbol of peace and sacrifice, offers something valuable to the fire, representing transformation and purification.
  • Real-life Example: In our daily lives, we often encounter situations where people may speak a lot but say very little of substance. For instance, in a corporate meeting, a person may dominate the conversation with jargon and buzzwords, but their contributions may not lead to actionable outcomes. On the other hand, a colleague who may not speak as much but offers a well-thought-out solution can be likened to the pigeon offering something meaningful to the fire.
  • Continuing the Mantra: "yasya dūtaḥ prahita eṣa etat tasmai yamāya namo astu mṛtyave ||"
  • Translation: "To that messenger who is sent forth, we offer our salutations to Yama, the god of death."
  • Meaning: This part of the mantra acknowledges the inevitability of death and the role of Yama as the guide to the afterlife. It emphasizes respect for the cycle of life and death, recognizing that every being has a messenger that leads them to their ultimate fate.
  • Real-life Example: In many cultures, there are rituals and practices that honor the deceased and acknowledge the role of death in life. For instance, during funerals, people often share stories and memories of the departed, celebrating their life while also recognizing the transition they have made. This aligns with the respect shown to Yama in the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its profound commentary on communication, the value of meaningful contributions, and the acceptance of mortality. In real life, it serves as a reminder to focus on the quality of our words and actions, ensuring they lead to constructive outcomes rather than empty rhetoric. Additionally, it encourages us to respect the natural cycle of life and death, fostering a deeper understanding of our existence and the connections we share with others.
