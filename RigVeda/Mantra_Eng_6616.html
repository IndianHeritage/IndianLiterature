युवादत्तस्य धिष्ण्या युवानीतस्य सूरिभिः |अहर-अहर वृषण मह्यम् शिक्षतम् ||
yuvādattasya dhiṣṇyā yuvānītasya sūribhiḥ | ahar-ahar vṛṣaṇa mahyaṁ śikṣatam ||
  • yuvādattasya dhiṣṇyā: This phrase can be broken down into two parts. "Yuvādattasya" refers to the youthful or fresh energy, while "dhiṣṇyā" signifies a place of stability or a foundation. Together, it suggests the importance of a strong foundation for the youthful energy to thrive.
  • yuvānītasya sūribhiḥ: Here, "yuvānītasya" again refers to the youth or the young, and "sūribhiḥ" means those who are wise or knowledgeable. This part emphasizes the need for guidance from wise individuals to channel youthful energy effectively.
  • ahar-ahar vṛṣaṇa mahyaṁ: "Ahar-ahar" translates to "day by day," indicating a continuous process. "Vṛṣaṇa" refers to strength or vigor, and "mahyaṁ" means "to me." This suggests that strength and vigor are to be cultivated daily.
  • śikṣatam: This word means "to be taught" or "to be trained." It emphasizes the importance of learning and development in harnessing one's potential.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of youth, guidance, and continuous growth. It signifies that for the youthful energy to be effective, it must be grounded in stability and guided by wisdom. In real life, this can be seen in various scenarios such as education, mentorship, and personal development. For instance, young professionals often benefit from mentorship programs where experienced individuals guide them in their careers. The mantra serves as a reminder that daily efforts in learning and self-improvement are crucial for harnessing one's potential and achieving success.
