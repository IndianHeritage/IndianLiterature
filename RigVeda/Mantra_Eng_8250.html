अच्छा समुद्रम इन्दवोऽस्तं गावो न धेनवः |अग्मन्न ऋतस्य योनिṁ आ ||
acchā samudram indavo 'staṁ gāvo na dhenavaḥ | agmann ṛtasya yonim ā ||
  • Mantra: "acchā samudram indavo 'staṁ gāvo na dhenavaḥ | agmann ṛtasya yonim ā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • acchā: good, auspicious
    • samudram: ocean, sea
    • indavo: the divine beings, often referring to the gods
    • 'staṁ: have risen, have appeared
    • gāvo: cows, representing wealth and nourishment
    • na: not
    • dhenavaḥ: the milking cows, symbolizing abundance
    • agmann: we have come, we have arrived
    • ṛtasya: of truth, order, or cosmic law
    • yonim: womb, source, or origin
    • ā: to, towards
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the auspiciousness of the ocean and the divine beings that have risen, symbolizing a connection between the divine and the earthly.
    • The mention of cows signifies wealth and nourishment, which are essential for sustenance and prosperity in life.
    • The phrase "we have come to the womb of truth" suggests a journey towards understanding and embracing the fundamental truths of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the importance of cows can be seen in dairy farming, where they provide milk, a vital source of nutrition for many communities.
    • The ocean, as a source of life, can be related to fishing communities that rely on it for their livelihood, emphasizing the interconnectedness of nature and human existence.
    • In personal development, seeking the "womb of truth" can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, where individuals strive to understand their purpose and the laws that govern their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of harmony between the divine, nature, and human life.
    • It encourages individuals to seek abundance and nourishment while remaining grounded in truth and cosmic order.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to appreciate the resources around us, cultivate gratitude, and strive for a deeper understanding of our existence and responsibilities towards nature and society.
