प्राचीनो यज्ञः सुधितं हि बर्हिः प्रीणीत अग्निर ईळितो न होतā |आ मातरā विश्ववारे हुवानो यतो यविष्टha जजñिषे सुशेवं ||
prācīno yajñaḥ sudhitaṁ hi barhiḥ prīṇīte agnir īḻito na hotā | ā mātarā viśvavāre huvāno yato yaviṣṭha jajñiṣe suśevaḥ ||
  • Translation: The ancient sacrifice, well-prepared, indeed pleases the fire, the invoked priest.
    • prācīno yajñaḥ: "ancient sacrifice" - This refers to the traditional rituals and offerings made to the deities, which have been practiced since ancient times.
    • sudhitaṁ hi barhiḥ: "well-prepared" - This emphasizes the importance of preparation and intention behind the rituals.
    • prīṇīte agnir: "pleases the fire" - Fire is a central element in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and the divine presence.
    • īḻito na hotā: "the invoked priest" - The priest, or hota, plays a crucial role in performing the rituals and invoking the deities.
  • Continuing the mantra:
    • ā mātarā viśvavāre: "O universal mother" - This phrase addresses the divine feminine, representing nurturing and protection.
    • huvāno: "invoking" - The act of calling upon the divine for blessings and guidance.
    • yato yaviṣṭha: "where the best is born" - This suggests that from the divine invocation, the highest good or prosperity emerges.
    • jajñiṣe suśevaḥ: "you who are born of good" - This highlights the idea that the results of the rituals are inherently good and beneficial.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of rituals and the role of the priest in connecting the material world with the divine.
    • It highlights the need for preparation and intention in any endeavor, suggesting that success comes from careful planning and respect for tradition.
    • The invocation of the divine feminine reflects the nurturing aspect of creation, reminding us of the importance of balance in our lives.
  • Real-life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied to various aspects such as personal projects, community events, or family gatherings where intention and preparation are key to success.
    • It serves as a reminder to honor our roots and traditions while engaging in contemporary practices, ensuring that we remain connected to our heritage.
    • The invocation of nurturing qualities can be reflected in our relationships, emphasizing the importance of care and support in our interactions with others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of Vedic rituals, emphasizing preparation, intention, and the invocation of divine blessings.
    • Its teachings are applicable in various real-life scenarios, encouraging individuals to approach their endeavors with respect for tradition and a focus on nurturing relationships.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that through our actions and intentions, we can invoke the highest good in our lives and the lives of others.
