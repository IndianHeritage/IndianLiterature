शविष्टं न आ भरा शूर शव ओजिष्टम ओजो अभिभूत उग्रं |विश्वा द्युम्ना वृष्ण्या मानवाणाम अस्मभ्यं दा हरिवो माधयधै ||
śaviṣṭhaṁ na ā bhara śūra śava ojiṣṭham ojo abhibhūta ugram | viśvā dyumnā vṛṣṇyā mānuṣāṇām asmabhyaṁ dā harivo mādayadhyai ||
  • śaviṣṭhaṁ: This term refers to the best or the most excellent. It signifies the highest quality or the peak of something.
  • na ā bhara: This phrase translates to "do not bring" or "do not carry." It implies a request or a command to refrain from bringing negativity or obstacles.
  • śūra: This word means "brave" or "valiant." It denotes courage and strength in facing challenges.
  • śava: This term can mean "body" or "form." In a broader sense, it can refer to the physical aspect of existence.
  • ojiṣṭham: This means "the most powerful" or "the strongest." It emphasizes the importance of strength and vitality.
  • ojo: This word refers to "energy" or "vitality." It signifies the life force that drives action and purpose.
  • abhibhūta: This term means "overcome" or "conquered." It suggests the idea of triumphing over difficulties.
  • ugram: This translates to "fierce" or "intense." It indicates a strong and forceful nature.
  • viśvā: This word means "all" or "universal." It signifies inclusiveness and the entirety of existence.
  • dyumnā: This term refers to "glory" or "fame." It emphasizes the importance of recognition and honor.
  • vṛṣṇyā: This word relates to the "Vṛṣṇi" clan, known for their valor and strength. It symbolizes a lineage of bravery.
  • mānuṣāṇām: This means "of humans." It connects the divine or cosmic aspects to human existence.
  • asmabhyaṁ: This translates to "to us." It indicates a personal or collective appeal.
  • dā: This word means "give." It is a request for bestowal or granting something valuable.
  • harivo: This term can be interpreted as "O Lord" or "O God." It invokes a divine presence.
  • mādayadhyai: This means "may you delight us." It expresses a desire for joy and fulfillment.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, courage, and overcoming obstacles in life. It calls upon divine energy to bestow glory and vitality upon humanity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of challenge, encouraging individuals to seek inner strength and resilience. For example, during difficult times such as personal loss or professional setbacks, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to harness one's inner power and strive for success. Its significance lies in the universal appeal for divine support in achieving greatness and overcoming life's adversities.
