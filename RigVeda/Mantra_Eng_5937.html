पर्जन्याय प्र गायत दिवस पुत्राय मीळ्हुषे |स नो यवसम इच्छतु ||
parjanyāya pra gāyata divas putrāya mīḻhuṣe | sa no yavasam icchatu ||
  • Mantra: "parjanyāya pra gāyata divas putrāya mīḻhuṣe | sa no yavasam icchatu ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Parjanyāya: To the rain or the rain-giver.
    • Pra gāyata: May we sing forth or may we praise.
    • Divas: Of the heavens or the sky.
    • Putrāya: For the offspring or for the children.
    • Mīḻhuṣe: To the one who is nourishing or to the one who provides sustenance.
    • Sa: May he or that.
    • No: For us.
    • Yavasam: The grains or the harvest.
    • Icchatu: May he desire or may he wish.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards the rain, acknowledging its vital role in sustaining life.
    • It expresses a desire for the rain to bless the earth with nourishment, particularly for the benefit of future generations.
    • The invocation of the heavens signifies a connection to the divine, recognizing that the forces of nature are beyond human control.
    • By asking for grains, it emphasizes the importance of agriculture and food security in human life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Farmers often pray for rain during droughts, understanding that their livelihoods depend on the timely arrival of rain for their crops.
    • In many cultures, rituals are performed to honor the rain gods, reflecting the deep-seated belief in the interconnectedness of nature and human existence.
    • Environmental movements advocate for sustainable practices to ensure that future generations inherit a healthy planet, echoing the mantra's concern for offspring.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of rain as a life-giving force and the importance of nurturing the earth for future generations.
    • It serves as a reminder of our responsibility to care for the environment and to seek harmony with nature.
    • In contemporary society, this mantra can inspire actions towards sustainable agriculture, conservation efforts, and a deeper appreciation for the natural world.
