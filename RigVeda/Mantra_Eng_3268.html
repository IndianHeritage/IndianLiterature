गृष्टिः ससूव स्थविरं तवागाम अनाधृष्यं वृषभं तुम्रम इन्द्रम् |अरिळ्हं वत्सं चरथाया माता स्वयम् गातुं तन्वा इच्छमानम् ||
gṛṣṭiḥ sasūva sthaviraṁ tavāgām anādhṛṣyaṁ vṛṣabhaṁ tumram indram | arīḻhaṁ vatsaṁ carathāya mātā svayaṁ gātuṁ tanva icchamānam ||
  • gṛṣṭiḥ sasūva: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who has been nourished or supported." In real life, this can relate to the importance of community and family support in achieving personal goals. For example, a student who receives encouragement from family and friends is more likely to succeed in their studies.
  • sthaviraṁ tavāgām: This translates to "the ancient or wise one who has come to you." It signifies the value of wisdom and experience. In a workplace, for instance, a mentor who has years of experience can guide a younger employee, helping them navigate challenges and make informed decisions.
  • anādhṛṣyaṁ vṛṣabhaṁ tumram indram: This can be understood as "the invincible bull, the powerful Indra." Indra is often associated with strength and leadership. In a modern context, this could represent a leader who is resilient and capable of overcoming obstacles, much like a CEO who leads a company through tough economic times.
  • arīḻhaṁ vatsaṁ: This phrase means "the well-nourished calf." It symbolizes growth and potential. In agriculture, a well-cared-for calf can grow into a strong cow, contributing to the farm's productivity. This reflects the idea that nurturing potential leads to future success.
  • carathāya mātā: This translates to "the mother who guides." It emphasizes the nurturing aspect of leadership. In a family setting, a mother often plays a crucial role in guiding her children, teaching them values and life skills that shape their future.
  • svayaṁ gātuṁ tanva icchamānam: This means "desiring to go forth with a healthy body." It highlights the importance of health and well-being in pursuing one's goals. For example, an athlete must maintain physical fitness to compete effectively, illustrating the connection between health and achievement.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of support, wisdom, strength, nurturing, and health. Its significance lies in the interconnectedness of these elements in achieving success in various aspects of life. Whether in personal relationships, professional environments, or individual pursuits, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide individuals toward a fulfilling and prosperous life. By recognizing the importance of community support, seeking wisdom from experienced individuals, embodying strength in leadership, nurturing potential, and maintaining health, one can navigate life's challenges effectively and achieve their aspirations.
