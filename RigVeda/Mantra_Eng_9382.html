यत ते पराः परावतो मनो जगाम दूरकम् |तत त आ वर्तयामसिह क्षयाय जिवसे ||
yat te parāḥ parāvato mano jagāma dūrakam | tat ta ā vartayāmasīha kṣayāya jīvase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yat: that which
    • te: of you
    • parāḥ: the supreme
    • parāvato: of the highest
    • mano: mind
    • jagāma: has gone
    • dūrakam: far away
    • tat: that
    • ta: that
    • ā: indeed
    • vartayāmasi: we are engaged
    • ha: indeed
    • kṣayāya: for destruction
    • jīvase: for life
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the relationship between the supreme mind and the individual. It suggests that the higher consciousness or supreme intelligence has moved far away from the ordinary mind.
    • The phrase "we are engaged for life" indicates a commitment to align oneself with this higher consciousness, suggesting that our actions should be directed towards life-affirming purposes rather than destruction.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often strive to align their vision with the greater good of society. This reflects the idea of engaging with a higher purpose rather than merely focusing on profit.
    • In personal development, individuals may seek mindfulness practices to connect with their higher self, moving away from distractions and negativity, akin to the "supreme mind" mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call to elevate our consciousness and actions towards a higher purpose. It encourages individuals to seek alignment with a greater intelligence, promoting life and constructive outcomes.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, personal growth, and community service, where the focus is on contributing positively to the world rather than engaging in destructive behaviors.
