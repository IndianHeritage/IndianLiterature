अभि प्रवन्त समानेव योषाः कल्याण्यः स्मयमानासो अग्निम् |घृतस्य धाराः समिधो नसन्त ता जुषाणो हर्यति जातवेदाः ||
abhi pravanta samaneva yoṣāḥ kalyāṇya1ḥ smayamānāso agnim | ghṛtasya dhārāḥ samidho nasanta tā juṣāṇo haryati jātavedāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "abhi pravanta samaneva yoṣāḥ kalyāṇyaḥ smayamānāso agnim | ghṛtasya dhārāḥ samidho nasanta tā juṣāṇo haryati jātavedāḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • pravanta: flowing, moving forward
    • samaneva: like the same, similar
    • yoṣāḥ: women, feminine energy
    • kalyāṇyaḥ: auspicious, beneficial
    • smayamānāso: smiling, joyful
    • agnim: fire, representing energy and transformation
    • ghṛtasya: of ghee, clarified butter, symbolizing nourishment
    • dhārāḥ: streams, flows
    • samidho: of the fuel, the wood used in the fire
    • nasanta: not diminishing, ever-increasing
    • tā: those
    • juṣāṇo: enjoying, partaking
    • haryati: brings forth, produces
    • jātavedāḥ: the all-knowing, the one who knows all births (a name for Agni)
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the energy of Agni, the fire god, who symbolizes transformation and purification.
    • The phrase "abhi pravanta samaneva yoṣāḥ" suggests a flow of energy that is harmonious and auspicious, akin to the nurturing qualities of women in society.
    • "Kalyāṇyaḥ smayamānāso agnim" emphasizes the joy and positivity that comes from this energy, indicating that when we align with this auspicious flow, we can invoke the transformative power of Agni.
    • The reference to "ghṛtasya dhārāḥ" signifies the nourishing aspect of ghee, which is often used in rituals to enhance the offerings to Agni, symbolizing the importance of sustenance in our spiritual practices.
    • "Samidho nasanta" indicates that the energy we cultivate through our actions and intentions is ever-increasing, suggesting that positive actions lead to greater rewards.
    • "Tā juṣāṇo haryati jātavedāḥ" highlights that those who partake in this energy and nourishment will be blessed by Agni, who is all-knowing and brings forth abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied to the nurturing roles of women, who often act as the backbone of families and communities, fostering growth and positivity.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders who embody the qualities of Agni—transformative, nurturing, and joyful—can inspire their teams to achieve greater success.
    • During festivals or rituals, the use of ghee in offerings symbolizes the importance of nourishment and gratitude, reminding us to appreciate the abundance in our lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of energy, nourishment, and transformation in our lives.
    • By aligning ourselves with the auspicious flow of energy represented by Agni, we can cultivate joy, abundance, and positive transformation in our personal and communal lives.
    • It encourages us to recognize the importance of nurturing relationships and the power of positive actions, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
