चक्षुषः पिता मनसा हि धीरो घृतम् एनं जनन नन्नमाने |यदेदन्ता अददृहन्त पूर्व आद इद dyावापृथिवी अप्रत्थेताम् ||
cakṣuṣaḥ pitā manasā hi dhīro ghṛtam ene ajanan nannamāne | yaded antā adadṛhanta pūrva ād id dyāvāpṛthivī aprathetām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • cakṣuṣaḥ: of the eyes
    • pitā: father
    • manasā: with the mind
    • hi: indeed
    • dhīro: the wise
    • ghṛtam: clarified butter (ghee)
    • ene: has come
    • ajanan: born
    • nannamāne: not to be neglected
    • yadet: whatever
    • antā: within
    • adadṛhanta: they have held
    • pūrva: earlier
    • ād: thus
    • id: this
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: heaven and earth
    • aprathetām: not to be separated
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the connection between the physical and the mental realms.
    • The "father" symbolizes guidance and wisdom, while "eyes" represent perception and understanding.
    • The wise person (dhīro) uses their mind (manasā) to discern and appreciate the essence of life.
    • Clarified butter (ghṛtam) is often associated with nourishment and purity, suggesting that wisdom nourishes the soul.
    • The phrase "not to be neglected" emphasizes the importance of recognizing and valuing wisdom and knowledge.
    • The mantra concludes with the idea that heaven and earth are interconnected and should not be seen as separate entities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family, the father often plays a crucial role in guiding children, helping them to see the world with clarity and wisdom.
    • In education, a wise teacher uses their knowledge to enlighten students, fostering a connection between their minds and the subject matter.
    • In personal development, individuals who seek wisdom and understanding often find that it nourishes their spirit, much like how ghee is used in rituals for purification.
    • In environmental awareness, recognizing the interconnectedness of nature (heaven and earth) can lead to more sustainable living practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom and the interconnectedness of all aspects of life.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding, recognizing that these elements are essential for personal growth and harmony with the world.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to better relationships, enhanced learning experiences, and a deeper appreciation for the environment.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that wisdom is a guiding force that nourishes our lives and connects us to the greater universe.
