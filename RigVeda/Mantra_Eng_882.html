इन्द्रो वृतस्य डोधतः सानुं वज्रेण हीलितः |अभिक्रम्यावा जिघ्नतेऽपः शर्माय चोदयन्न आर्चन्न अनु स्वराज्यम् ||
indro vṛtrasya dodhataḥ sānuṁ vajreṇa hīḻitaḥ | abhikramyāva jighnate 'paḥ sarmāya codayann arcann anu svarājyam ||
  • Indro: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Vṛtrasya: Relates to Vritra, a serpent or dragon that represents obstacles or challenges in life.
  • Dodhataḥ: Means to strike or to overcome, indicating the act of confronting challenges.
  • Sānuṁ: Refers to the support or assistance, suggesting the importance of community and collaboration.
  • Vajreṇa: Translates to "with the thunderbolt," symbolizing strength and the ability to break through barriers.
  • Hīḻitaḥ: Means to be struck or to be affected, indicating the impact of divine intervention.
  • Abhikramyāva: Refers to the act of advancing or moving forward, emphasizing progress and determination.
  • Jighnate: Means to strike down or to defeat, highlighting the importance of overcoming adversities.
  • ‘Paḥ: Refers to the protective aspect, suggesting the need for safety and security.
  • Sarmāya: Means to provide shelter or comfort, indicating the importance of a safe environment.
  • Codayann: Refers to inspiring or motivating, emphasizing the role of encouragement in achieving goals.
  • Arcann: Means to worship or to honor, highlighting the significance of reverence in life.
  • Anu: Means following or in accordance with, suggesting alignment with higher principles.
  • Svarājyam: Refers to sovereignty or self-rule, indicating the importance of autonomy and self-governance.

The mantra speaks to the themes of overcoming obstacles, the importance of community support, and the divine strength that aids in achieving one's goals. It emphasizes the need for courage and determination in the face of challenges, while also highlighting the significance of safety, comfort, and motivation in our lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties in life, individuals can draw inspiration from Indra's strength to confront and overcome their own Vṛtras, or obstacles.
  • Community Support: The mantra underscores the importance of seeking help from others, reminding us that collaboration can lead to greater success.
  • Leadership: Leaders can embody the qualities of Indra by inspiring and motivating their teams to overcome challenges and achieve collective goals.
  • Spiritual Growth: The act of worship and reverence mentioned in the mantra encourages individuals to seek a deeper connection with the divine, fostering personal growth and understanding.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the strength within us to overcome challenges, the importance of community, and the divine support that guides us towards achieving our goals. It encourages us to embrace our sovereignty and strive for progress in all aspects of life.
