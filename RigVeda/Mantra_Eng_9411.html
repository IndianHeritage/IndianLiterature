मध्यā यत्कर्त्वम् अभवत् अभीके कामं कृत्वाने पितरि युवत्याम् |मनानग रेतो जहतुर वियन्ता सानौ निषिक्तं सुखृतस्य योनौ ||
madhyā yat kartvam abhavad abhīke kāmaṁ kṛṇvāne pitari yuvatyām | manānag reto jahatur viyantā sānau niṣiktaṁ sukṛtasya yonau ||
  • madhyā yat kartvam abhavad: This phrase can be translated as "In the middle, that which was done." The term "madhyā" refers to the center or the middle, suggesting a balance or a pivotal point in actions or decisions.
  • abhīke kāmaṁ kṛṇvāne: This translates to "In the act of fulfilling desires." Here, "abhīke" indicates a sense of urgency or immediacy, while "kāmaṁ" refers to desires or wishes. This part emphasizes the importance of taking action to fulfill one's desires.
  • pitari yuvatyām: This means "in the youth of the father." The term "pitari" refers to the father, and "yuvatyām" indicates youth. This suggests a generational aspect, where the actions and desires of the youth are influenced by their parents.
  • manānag reto jahatur viyantā: This can be interpreted as "the mind's energy is released." "Manānag" refers to the mind or intellect, while "reto" signifies energy or vitality. This phrase highlights the importance of mental energy in achieving one's goals.
  • sānau niṣiktaṁ sukṛtasya yonau: This translates to "in the union of the good deeds' source." "Sānau" refers to union or coming together, "niṣiktaṁ" means concentrated or focused, and "sukṛtasya yonau" indicates the source of good deeds. This emphasizes the significance of collective good actions and their origins.

Conclusion: The mantra reflects the interconnectedness of actions, desires, and generational influences. It emphasizes the importance of taking decisive actions to fulfill one's desires while being mindful of the wisdom and experiences passed down from previous generations. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in family dynamics where the aspirations of the youth are shaped by their parents' values. It also highlights the necessity of mental focus and collective good actions in achieving personal and communal goals. This mantra serves as a reminder to balance personal desires with the wisdom of the past and the importance of contributing positively to society.
