न वा उ एतन म्रियसे न ऋष्यसि देवाम् इद एषि पथिभिः सुगेभिः |हरि ते युञ्जा पृथती अभूताम् उपास्थाद् वाजी धुरी रासभस्य ||
na vā u etan mriyase na riṣyasi devām̐ id eṣi pathibhiḥ sugebhiḥ | harī te yuñjā pṛṣatī abhūtām upāsthād vājī dhuri rāsabhasya ||
  • na vā u etan mriyase: This phrase translates to "You do not die." It emphasizes the eternal nature of the soul, suggesting that while the physical body may perish, the essence of life continues. In real life, this can be related to the concept of legacy; for instance, the teachings and memories of influential figures like Mahatma Gandhi or Martin Luther King Jr. continue to inspire generations, demonstrating that their impact transcends their physical existence.
  • na riṣyasi devām̐: This means "You do not suffer, O divine one." It highlights the idea of divine protection and the absence of suffering for those who are aligned with the divine will. In practical terms, this can be seen in the lives of individuals who find strength in their faith during challenging times, such as people who turn to prayer or meditation to cope with personal loss or hardship.
  • id eṣi pathibhiḥ sugebhiḥ: This translates to "You are on the path with auspicious ways." It suggests that one is guided by positive and beneficial paths in life. For example, when individuals make choices that align with their values and ethics, they often find themselves on a path that leads to fulfillment and success, such as pursuing a career that resonates with their passions and skills.
  • harī te yuñjā: This means "The divine energy unites with you." It signifies a connection with the divine or higher consciousness. In real life, this can be observed in moments of inspiration or creativity, where individuals feel a surge of energy that propels them to create art, music, or literature, often attributing this experience to a higher power or muse.
  • pṛṣatī abhūtām: This translates to "The powerful one has become." It indicates transformation and the realization of potential. In everyday life, this can be seen in personal growth stories, such as someone overcoming adversity to achieve their dreams, like athletes who train rigorously to excel in their sport despite initial setbacks.
  • upāsthād vājī dhuri rāsabhasya: This means "May you be seated on the chariot of strength." It symbolizes empowerment and the ability to navigate life's challenges with vigor. In practical terms, this can relate to individuals who take charge of their lives, such as entrepreneurs who start their own businesses, harnessing their strengths to overcome obstacles and achieve their goals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound themes of immortality, divine protection, auspicious guidance, connection with higher energies, personal transformation, and empowerment. Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals to recognize their eternal essence, seek divine support, and pursue paths that align with their true selves. In real-life scenarios, these teachings can be applied to foster resilience, encourage personal growth, and promote a sense of purpose, ultimately guiding individuals toward a fulfilling and meaningful life.
