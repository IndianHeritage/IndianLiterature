आ हरयः ससृजिरे 'रुषीर अधि बरहिषि | यत्राभि संनवामहे ||
ā harayaḥ sasṛjrire 'ruṣīr adhi barhiṣi | yatrābhi saṁnavāmahe ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā: A preposition meaning "from" or "out of."
    • harayaḥ: Refers to "the ones who take away" or "the ones who remove."
    • sasṛjrire: Means "they have spread" or "they have expanded."
    • 'ruṣīr: Translates to "the seers" or "the wise ones."
    • adhi: A preposition meaning "upon" or "over."
    • barhiṣi: Refers to "the sacred grass" or "the place of worship."
    • yatrābhi: Means "where we come together" or "where we unite."
    • saṁnavāmahe: Translates to "we invoke" or "we call upon."
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine forces that remove obstacles and spread wisdom.
    • The "seers" or wise individuals are invoked in a sacred space, symbolizing a gathering of knowledge and spiritual energy.
    • The act of coming together signifies unity and collective strength in seeking divine guidance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, people come together to share knowledge and support each other, much like the invocation of the wise in the mantra.
    • In educational settings, teachers (the seers) guide students (the seekers) in a sacred space of learning, fostering wisdom and understanding.
    • During rituals or ceremonies, participants invoke divine blessings to remove obstacles in their lives, reflecting the essence of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, wisdom, and the removal of obstacles through collective invocation.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as community gatherings, educational institutions, and spiritual rituals, where individuals seek guidance and support.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the power of coming together in pursuit of knowledge and divine assistance.
