पर्वतश्चिन महि वृद्धो बिभाय दिवश्चित्सानु रेजत स्वने वः |यत्क्रीळथ मरुत ऋष्टिमन्त आप इव सध्रियञ्चो धवध्वे ||
parvataś cin mahi vṛddho bibhāya divaś cit sānu rejata svane vaḥ | yat krīḻatha maruta ṛṣṭimanta āpa iva sadhryañco dhavadhve ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • parvataś: mountain
    • cin: indeed
    • mahi: great
    • vṛddho: grown or increased
    • bibhāya: fear or awe
    • divaś: of the sky
    • cit: conscious or aware
    • sānu: with a sense of
    • rejata: shines or radiates
    • svane: sound or voice
    • vaḥ: of you
    • yat: that which
    • krīḻatha: you play or engage
    • maruta: winds or storm gods
    • ṛṣṭimanta: those who have achieved success
    • āpa: waters
    • iva: like or as
    • sadhryañco: with harmony
    • dhavadhve: may you be strong or powerful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the majestic mountains that evoke a sense of awe and reverence.
    • The mountains are described as great and powerful, symbolizing stability and strength in life.
    • The reference to the sky suggests a connection to the divine and the vastness of existence.
    • The winds (maruta) represent the forces of nature that can be both gentle and fierce, reminding us of the duality of life.
    • The mention of waters signifies nourishment and the flow of life, akin to how emotions and experiences shape our journey.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, mountains can be seen as symbols of endurance. For instance, the Himalayas stand tall, representing the strength and resilience needed to overcome life's challenges.
    • In personal life, when facing difficulties, one can draw inspiration from the mountains, reminding themselves to remain steadfast and grounded.
    • The winds can be likened to the challenges we face; they can either uplift us or test our limits, much like how we navigate through various life situations.
    • Water, as a symbol of adaptability, teaches us to flow with circumstances, embracing change while maintaining harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, harmony, and adaptability in life.
    • It encourages individuals to find stability in their lives, much like the mountains, while being aware of the ever-changing nature of existence.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can serve as a reminder to remain resilient in the face of adversity and to embrace the flow of life with grace.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us to cultivate a sense of awe and respect for the natural world and our place within it, fostering a deeper connection to both ourselves and the universe.
