उप शिक्षापतस्थुषो भियसम आ धेहि शत्रुषु |पवमान विदा रयिम ||
upa śikṣāpatasthuṣo bhiyasam ā dhehi śatruṣu | pavamāna vidā rayim ||
  • upa: This word translates to "near" or "approaching." It signifies the act of coming closer to something, whether it be knowledge, wisdom, or a divine presence.
  • śikṣā: Meaning "instruction" or "teaching," this term emphasizes the importance of learning and guidance in one's life.
  • patasthuṣo: This can be interpreted as "to the one who is established" or "to the one who is firm." It refers to a stable and secure foundation, which is essential for growth and development.
  • bhiyasam: This word means "fear" or "danger." It highlights the challenges and adversities that one may face in life.
  • ā dhehi: Translated as "please grant" or "bestow," this phrase is a plea for assistance or support in overcoming difficulties.
  • śatruṣu: This means "in the presence of enemies" or "against adversaries." It signifies the struggles one faces in life, whether they are external challenges or internal conflicts.
  • pavamāna: This term refers to "the purifying one" or "the one who brings clarity." It symbolizes the cleansing of negativity and the pursuit of truth.
  • vidā: Meaning "to know" or "to understand," this word emphasizes the importance of knowledge and awareness in navigating life's challenges.
  • rayim: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the abundance that comes from overcoming obstacles and achieving success.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for guidance and support in the face of challenges. It emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and instruction to overcome fears and adversities. The plea for assistance in the presence of enemies highlights the struggles we encounter in life, whether they are external conflicts or internal doubts.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Education: Students can invoke this mantra when facing academic challenges, seeking guidance from teachers and mentors to overcome difficulties in their studies.
  • Career: Professionals may use this mantra when dealing with competition or workplace conflicts, asking for clarity and support to navigate their careers successfully.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals facing personal struggles, such as mental health issues or relationship conflicts, can find solace in this mantra, seeking knowledge and understanding to overcome their fears.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of seeking guidance and knowledge in overcoming life's challenges. It encourages individuals to approach their struggles with a sense of purpose and clarity, ultimately leading to personal growth and prosperity.
