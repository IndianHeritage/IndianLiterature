अग्न इन्द्र वरुण मित्र देवाः शर्धः प्र यन्त मारुतोता विष्णो |उभा नासत्याः रुद्रो अध ग्नाः पूषा भगः सरस्वती जुषन्त ||
agna indra varuṇa mitra devāḥ śardhaḥ pra yanta mārutota viṣṇo | ubhā nāsatyā rudro adha gnāḥ pūṣā bhagaḥ sarasvatī juṣanta ||
  • Agna: Refers to the fire god, Agni, who is considered the mediator between humans and the divine. In real life, fire symbolizes transformation and purification, much like how we use fire in rituals or cooking to transform raw ingredients into nourishing meals.
  • Indra: The king of the gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms. Indra represents strength and power. In modern life, we can see Indra's qualities in leaders who bring about change and protect their communities, much like how a strong leader can rally people during a crisis.
  • Varuṇa: The god of cosmic order and water. Varuṇa embodies the principles of truth and moral order. In our lives, this can be likened to the importance of ethics and integrity in our actions, similar to how laws govern society.
  • Mitra: The god of friendship and contracts. Mitra signifies the importance of relationships and trust. In everyday life, this can be seen in the bonds we form with friends and colleagues, emphasizing the value of loyalty and cooperation.
  • Devāḥ: Refers to the divine beings or gods. This highlights the belief in higher powers that guide and protect us, akin to how people often seek guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders in their lives.
  • Śardhaḥ: Means 'together' or 'in unity.' This emphasizes the importance of community and collaboration. In real life, teamwork in workplaces or community service projects exemplifies this principle.
  • Pra yanta: Translates to 'may they come forth' or 'may they approach.' This can be seen as a call for divine blessings and support in our endeavors, similar to how people pray for success before starting a new venture.
  • Mārutota: Refers to the winds or the storm gods. This symbolizes change and the dynamic nature of life. In real life, we experience changes that can be both challenging and transformative, much like the winds that can bring both storms and gentle breezes.
  • Viṣṇo: The preserver and protector in the Hindu trinity. Viṣṇo represents stability and preservation. In our lives, this can be reflected in the efforts we make to maintain balance and harmony in our personal and professional lives.
  • Ubhā: Means 'both' or 'together.' This signifies the duality and balance in life, such as the balance between work and personal life, or between giving and receiving.
  • Nāsatyā: Refers to the twin gods, representing duality and balance. This can be related to the importance of having a balanced perspective in life, recognizing both sides of a situation before making decisions.
  • Rudro: The fierce aspect of the divine, often associated with transformation and healing. In real life, this can be seen in the challenges we face that lead to personal growth and resilience.
  • Adha: Means 'below' or 'beneath.' This can symbolize the foundation of our actions and beliefs, much like how our values shape our decisions and interactions.
  • Gnāḥ: Refers to knowledge or wisdom. This emphasizes the importance of learning and understanding in our lives, akin to how education shapes our future.
  • Pūṣā: The god of nourishment and growth. This can be related to the importance of nurturing relationships and personal development, similar to how we cultivate our skills and talents.
  • Bhagaḥ: Represents prosperity and good fortune. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of success and happiness, emphasizing the importance of striving for a fulfilling life.
  • Sarasvatī: The goddess of knowledge, music, and arts. This highlights the significance of creativity and learning in our lives, much like how artistic expression enriches our experiences.
  • Juṣanta: Means 'to enjoy' or 'to delight in.' This signifies the importance of finding joy in our lives, whether through relationships, work, or personal passions.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and blessings in various aspects of life, from personal growth to community harmony. It emphasizes the importance of unity, knowledge, and ethical living, which are applicable in our daily interactions and decisions. By invoking these divine qualities, we can strive for a balanced and fulfilling life,
