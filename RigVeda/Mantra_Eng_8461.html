राजा सिंधूनाम् पवते पतिर दिव ऋतस्य याति पथिभिः कनिक्रदत् |सहस्रधारः परि सिच्यते हरिः पुनानो वाचं जनयन्न उपावसुः ||
rājā sindhūnām pavate patir diva ṛtasya yāti pathibhiḥ kanikradat | sahasradhāraḥ pari ṣicyate hariḥ punāno vācaṁ janayann upāvasuḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "The king of rivers, the lord of the sky, moves along the paths of truth, like a swift current. The one with a thousand streams showers blessings, renewing the speech of those who dwell in the sacred space."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • rājā: king
    • sindhūnām: of rivers
    • pavate: moves or flows
    • patir: lord or master
    • diva: of the sky
    • ṛtasya: of truth or order
    • yāti: goes or travels
    • pathibhiḥ: by the paths
    • kanikradat: like a swift current
    • sahasradhāraḥ: one with a thousand streams
    • pari: all around
    • ṣicyate: showers or blesses
    • hariḥ: the divine or auspicious one
    • punāno: renewing
    • vācaṁ: speech or words
    • janayann: generating or creating
    • upāvasuḥ: those who dwell in the sacred space
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The "king of rivers" can be likened to major rivers like the Ganges or Nile, which are not only vital for the ecosystem but also hold cultural and spiritual significance for millions.
    • The concept of "truth" and "order" can be seen in the legal systems of modern societies, where laws are established to maintain harmony and justice.
    • The "thousand streams" can represent the various paths of knowledge and wisdom that flow into our lives, such as education, experiences, and cultural teachings.
    • The act of "renewing speech" can be related to the importance of communication in relationships, where open dialogue fosters understanding and connection.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its celebration of the interconnectedness of nature, truth, and human experience. It emphasizes the importance of following the paths of righteousness and truth, much like rivers that nourish the land. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek harmony in their actions, promote communication, and appreciate the natural world around them. It serves as a reminder that just as rivers flow and renew the earth, our words and actions can also bring about positive change in our communities and relationships.
