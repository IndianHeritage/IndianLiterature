इन्द्रं ईशानं ओजसाभि स्तोमा अनूषत |सहस्रं यस्य रातयु उता वा सन्ति भूयसीः ||
indram īśānam ojasābhi stomā anūṣata | sahasraṁ yasya rātaya uta vā santi bhūyasīḥ ||
  • Indram: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
  • īśānam: Means 'the lord' or 'the ruler', indicating authority and governance.
  • ojasābhi: Translates to 'with energy' or 'with vigor', emphasizing the importance of strength and vitality in leadership.
  • stomā: Refers to 'praises' or 'hymns', highlighting the role of devotion and acknowledgment in leadership.
  • anūṣata: Means 'to be invoked' or 'to be called upon', suggesting the need for divine support in leadership.
  • sahasraṁ: Translates to 'thousands', indicating abundance and the vastness of resources or followers.
  • yasya: Means 'of whom', referring to the leader or the divine being invoked.
  • rātaya: Refers to 'nights' or 'journeys', symbolizing the passage of time and the experiences that come with it.
  • uta: Means 'or', indicating a choice or alternative.
  • vā: Translates to 'and', connecting ideas or concepts.
  • santi: Means 'exist' or 'are', affirming the presence of something.
  • bhūyasīḥ: Refers to 'greater' or 'more', suggesting an increase in strength or resources.

This mantra emphasizes the qualities of effective leadership, invoking the divine support of Indra, the ruler of the gods. It highlights the importance of strength, energy, and the acknowledgment of divine forces in guiding and leading people. The mention of 'thousands' signifies the vastness of influence and the potential for greater achievements when one is aligned with divine energy.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various leadership contexts, such as:

  • Corporate Leadership: A CEO or manager can embody the qualities of Indra by leading with strength and energy, inspiring their team through recognition and praise.
  • Community Leadership: A community leader can invoke the support of their constituents, ensuring that their leadership is inclusive and acknowledges the contributions of all members.
  • Personal Development: Individuals can reflect on their own leadership qualities, seeking to cultivate strength and energy in their personal and professional lives.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strong, energetic, and divine-supported leadership. It serves as a reminder that effective leadership is not just about authority but also about invoking support, recognizing contributions, and striving for greater achievements. In any leadership role, whether in business, community, or personal life, embodying these qualities can lead to success and fulfillment.
