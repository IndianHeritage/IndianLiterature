अग्निर ईशे वसूनां शुचिर यो धार्णिर एषाम् |प्रियाम् अपिधीमृ वनिषीष्ट मेधिर आ वनिषीष्ट मेधिरः ||
agnir īśe vasūnāṁ śucir yo dharṇir eṣām | priyām̐ apidhīm̐r vaniṣīṣṭa medhira ā vaniṣīṣṭa medhiraḥ ||
  • Agnir: This word translates to "fire" and symbolizes energy, transformation, and purity. In real life, fire is essential for cooking, warmth, and light, representing the transformative power of knowledge and action.
  • īśe: Meaning "to the lord" or "to the divine," this term emphasizes the connection between the individual and a higher power. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way people seek guidance or inspiration from spiritual leaders or mentors.
  • vasūnāṁ: This refers to "the wealth" or "the good things." It signifies abundance and prosperity. In practical terms, this can relate to the pursuit of material and spiritual wealth, such as education, relationships, and personal growth.
  • śucir: Translated as "pure," this word highlights the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions. In real life, maintaining purity can be seen in ethical practices, honesty in relationships, and integrity in work.
  • yo: Meaning "who," this word introduces a subject, indicating a person or entity that embodies the qualities described.
  • dharṇir: This term means "the one who holds" or "the supporter." It can be related to the idea of responsibility and support in relationships, such as a parent supporting a child or a friend being there for another in times of need.
  • eṣām: Meaning "of these," it refers to the qualities or attributes being discussed, linking them back to the previous terms.
  • priyām̐: This translates to "beloved" or "dear," indicating the importance of love and affection in human relationships. In real life, this can be seen in the bonds we form with family, friends, and partners.
  • apidhīm̐r: This word means "to be invoked" or "to be called upon." It suggests the act of seeking blessings or guidance from the divine or from those we respect.
  • vaniṣīṣṭa: This term means "the best" or "the most excellent." It emphasizes the pursuit of excellence in all endeavors, whether in personal goals, professional achievements, or spiritual growth.
  • medhira: Meaning "intelligent" or "wise," this word highlights the value of wisdom and knowledge. In real life, wisdom is crucial for making informed decisions and navigating life's challenges.
  • ā: This preposition can mean "towards" or "to," indicating direction or purpose.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking divine guidance and embodying qualities such as purity, wisdom, and love in our lives. It encourages individuals to strive for excellence and support one another in their journeys. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal development, fostering healthy relationships, and pursuing ethical practices in professional settings. By invoking these qualities, individuals can create a harmonious and prosperous life, both for themselves and for those around them.
