एष सुवानः परि सोमः पवित्रे सर्गो न सृष्टो अदधावद अर्वा |तिग्मे शिशानो महिषो न शृङ्गे गा गव्यन्न अभि शूरो न सत्वा ||
eṣa suvānaḥ pari somaḥ pavitre sargo na sṛṣṭo adadhāvad arvā | tigme śiśāno mahiṣo na śṛṅge gā gavyann abhi śūro na satvā ||
  • eṣa suvānaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "this is the good one" or "this is the auspicious one." It signifies the presence of positivity and auspiciousness in life.
  • pari somaḥ: "Soma" refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. "Pari" means "around" or "surrounding," indicating a holistic approach to well-being.
  • pavitre sargo: "Pavitre" means "pure," and "sargo" refers to "creation" or "origin." Together, they emphasize the importance of purity in creation and the natural world.
  • na sṛṣṭo adadhāvad arvā: This can be interpreted as "not created, but established." It suggests that true essence or nature is not merely a product of creation but is inherent and eternal.
  • tigme śiśāno: "Tigme" means "sharp" or "keen," and "śiśāno" refers to "the one who is wise." This phrase highlights the value of wisdom and sharp intellect in navigating life.
  • mahiṣo na śṛṅge: "Mahiṣo" refers to a "great one" or "powerful being," while "na śṛṅge" means "without horns." This can symbolize strength without aggression, suggesting that true power lies in restraint and wisdom.
  • gā gavyann abhi: "Gā" means "to go" or "to move," and "gavyann" refers to "the one who is nourished." This indicates the importance of progress and nourishment in life.
  • śūro na satvā: "Śūro" means "brave" or "heroic," while "na satvā" refers to "without essence." This suggests that true bravery is not just about physical strength but also about having a strong moral and ethical foundation.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of living a life that is auspicious, pure, wise, and strong without aggression. It emphasizes the importance of inner strength, wisdom, and moral courage in our daily lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as leadership, personal development, and community building. For instance, a leader who embodies these qualities can inspire others to pursue a path of integrity and wisdom, fostering a positive environment. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate these virtues within ourselves and in our interactions with others, leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
