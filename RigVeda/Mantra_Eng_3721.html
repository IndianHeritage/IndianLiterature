त्वां अग्नि अतिथिम पूर्व्यं विशः शोचिष्केशं गृहपतिं नि षेदिरे | बृहत्केतुम पुरुरूपं धनस्पृतम् सुशर्माणं स्ववसं जरद्विषम् ||
tvām agne atithim pūrvyaṁ viśaḥ śociṣkeśaṁ gṛhapatiṁ ni ṣedire | bṛhatketum pururūpaṁ dhanaspṛtaṁ suśarmāṇaṁ svavasaṁ jaradviṣam ||
  • tvām - "You" or "to you": This refers to Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
  • agne - "O Agni": A direct address to Agni, emphasizing his importance in rituals and daily life.
  • atithim - "the guest": In Vedic culture, guests are treated with utmost respect and honor, symbolizing hospitality.
  • pūrvyaṁ - "from the past": This indicates the ancient traditions and the continuity of rituals that have been passed down through generations.
  • viśaḥ - "of the community": This signifies the collective aspect of worship and the importance of community in spiritual practices.
  • śociṣkeśaṁ - "radiant-haired": This describes Agni's divine and luminous nature, representing purity and enlightenment.
  • gṛhapatiṁ - "the lord of the house": This refers to the protector and provider of the household, highlighting the role of Agni in domestic life.
  • ni ṣedire - "they have seated": This indicates the act of inviting Agni into the home, symbolizing the welcoming of divine presence.
  • bṛhatketum - "the one with a great banner": This signifies Agni's power and might, representing strength and protection.
  • pururūpaṁ - "of many forms": This highlights Agni's multifaceted nature, showing how he can manifest in various ways in life.
  • dhanaspṛtaṁ - "the one who desires wealth": This reflects the aspiration for prosperity and abundance, which is often sought in life.
  • suśarmāṇaṁ - "the one who brings good fortune": This emphasizes the blessings and positive outcomes associated with Agni's presence.
  • svavasaṁ - "the one who is self-sufficient": This indicates independence and the ability to thrive through one's own efforts.
  • jaradviṣam - "the one who overcomes obstacles": This signifies resilience and the ability to face challenges in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the significance of Agni as a central figure in Vedic rituals and daily life. It emphasizes the importance of hospitality, community, and the divine presence in the home. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to the practice of welcoming guests with respect, fostering a sense of community, and seeking divine blessings for prosperity and protection. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of tradition, and the pursuit of well-being and resilience in the face of challenges.
