तद देवानां देवतमाया कर्त्वम् अश्रथ्नन दृढाव्रदन्त वीलिताः |उद गा आजद अभिनद ब्रह्मणा वलम् अगूहत तमो व्य अचक्षत स्वः ||
tad devānāṁ devatamāya kartvam aśrathnan dṛḻhāvradanta vīḻitā | ud gā ājad abhinad brahmaṇā valam agūhat tamo vy acakṣayat svaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • devānāṁ: of the gods
    • devatamāya: to the supreme deity
    • kartvam: action or duty
    • aśrathnan: not to be disturbed
    • dṛḻhāvradanta: with firm resolve
    • vīḻitā: having been accomplished
    • ud gā: to sing or chant
    • ājad: to be born or arise
    • abhinad: to be awakened
    • brahmaṇā: by the ultimate reality or Brahman
    • valam: strength or power
    • agūhat: to remove or dispel
    • tamo: darkness
    • vy: indeed
    • acakṣayat: to reveal or show
    • svaḥ: heaven or the celestial realm
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the relationship between the divine and the actions of humans.
    • It emphasizes the importance of performing one's duties with a firm resolve, undisturbed by external influences.
    • The act of singing or chanting is highlighted as a means to connect with the divine and awaken one's inner strength.
    • It suggests that through devotion and righteous action, one can dispel darkness and ignorance, revealing the truth of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an employee who remains focused and dedicated to their work, despite challenges, embodies the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal life, someone who practices mindfulness and meditation can relate to the idea of awakening one's inner strength and dispelling negativity.
    • Community service or volunteer work can be seen as a way to perform one's duty towards society, reflecting the mantra's call to action.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of duty, devotion, and inner strength in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to act with purpose and clarity, fostering a connection with the divine and the greater good.
    • In a world filled with distractions and challenges, the teachings of this mantra can guide us towards a more meaningful and enlightened existence.
