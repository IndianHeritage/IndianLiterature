शतं न इन्द ऊतिभिः सहस्रं वा शुचीनाम् |पवस्व मंहयद्रयिः ||
śataṁ na inda ūtibhiḥ sahasraṁ vā śucīnām | pavasva maṁhayadrayiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • śataṁ: hundred
    • na: not
    • inda: to be praised or worshipped
    • ūtibhiḥ: with efforts or endeavors
    • sahasraṁ: thousand
    • vā: or
    • śucīnām: of the pure or the virtuous
    • pavasva: may you purify or cleanse
    • maṁhayadrayiḥ: with great strength or power
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of purity and strength in one's endeavors.
    • The phrase "śataṁ na inda ūtibhiḥ" suggests that even a hundred efforts may not be sufficient without purity and virtue.
    • "Sahasraṁ vā śucīnām" emphasizes that the efforts of the pure and virtuous are far more valuable, even if they are fewer in number.
    • The call to "pavasva maṁhayadrayiḥ" indicates a desire for divine purification and empowerment, suggesting that true strength comes from purity of heart and intention.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a team may work hard (hundreds of efforts) to achieve a goal, but if their intentions are not pure or if they engage in unethical practices, their efforts may not yield the desired results.
    • In personal relationships, one may put in a lot of effort to maintain connections, but if those efforts are not rooted in genuine care and respect, the relationships may falter.
    • In education, students may study hard, but if they resort to dishonest means like cheating, their knowledge and skills will not be truly developed.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of purity and virtue in all endeavors.
    • It teaches that the quality of efforts is more important than the quantity; pure intentions lead to true strength and success.
    • In real life, applying this principle can lead to more meaningful achievements, whether in personal, professional, or spiritual pursuits.
