अग्निर जज्ञे जुह्वा रेजमानो महस् पुत्रां अरुषस्य प्रयक्षे |महान गर्भो मह्य आ जातम एषाम मही प्रवृद्ध ध्यर्यश्वस्य यज्ञैः ||

  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • agnir: Fire
    • jajñe: was born
    • juhvā: offering
    • rejamāno: being invoked
    • mahas: great
    • putrām̐: sons
    • aruṣasya: of the red one (often referring to the sun)
    • prayakṣe: in the presence
    • mahān: great
    • garbho: womb or embryo
    • mahy: for me
    • ā: from
    • jātam: born
    • eṣām: of these
    • mahī: earth
    • pravṛd: flourishing
    • dharyaśvasya: of the one who holds the earth
    • yajñaiḥ: through sacrifices
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine fire (Agni) as a vital force that is invoked during rituals.
    • Agni is seen as a source of life and energy, representing the connection between the earthly and the divine.
    • The mention of "sons of the red one" can symbolize the sun, which is essential for life on Earth.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of rituals (yajñas) in nurturing and sustaining life, as they invoke blessings from the divine.
    • The "great womb" signifies the potential for life and growth, highlighting the nurturing aspect of nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, fire rituals are often performed to invoke rain and fertility for crops, reflecting the connection to the earth and the divine.
    • Modern environmental movements can draw inspiration from this mantra, emphasizing the need to respect and nurture the earth for future generations.
    • In personal life, the concept of invoking positive energy through rituals can be seen in practices like meditation or prayer, which aim to connect individuals with a higher purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of Agni as a life-giving force and the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
    • It serves as a reminder of our responsibility to nurture the earth and respect the cycles of life.
    • In contemporary life, the principles of this mantra can be applied in environmental conservation, personal growth, and community building through shared rituals and values.
