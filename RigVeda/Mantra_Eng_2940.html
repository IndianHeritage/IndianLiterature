अयम् अस्मान् वनस्पतिर् मा च हा मा च रीरीषत् |स्वस्त्य आगृहिभ्य आवसाऽ आविमोचनात् ||
ayam asmān vanaspatir mā ca hā mā ca rīriṣat | svasty ā gṛhebhya āvasā ā vimocanāt ||
  • ayam asmān: This phrase translates to "this is us" or "this is our essence." It signifies a sense of identity and belonging.
  • vanaspatir: This word refers to "the tree" or "the plant." In a broader sense, it symbolizes nature and its nurturing qualities.
  • mā ca hā: This translates to "do not let go" or "do not abandon." It emphasizes the importance of holding onto something valuable.
  • mā ca rīriṣat: This means "do not desire harm" or "do not wish for negativity." It encourages a mindset of positivity and goodwill.
  • svasty: This word means "well-being" or "prosperity." It is a call for health and happiness.
  • ā gṛhebhya: This translates to "from the homes" or "from the households." It signifies the importance of family and community.
  • āvasā: This means "to dwell" or "to reside." It emphasizes the idea of stability and security in one's environment.
  • ā vimocanāt: This translates to "from liberation" or "from release." It signifies the importance of freedom and the removal of obstacles.

The mantra as a whole can be interpreted as a call for unity and well-being within a community, emphasizing the importance of nature, family, and positive intentions. It encourages individuals to hold onto their roots and values while fostering a sense of harmony and prosperity.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In community gatherings, it can serve as a reminder to cherish relationships and work towards collective well-being.
  • In environmental initiatives, it highlights the importance of preserving nature and recognizing its role in our lives.
  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to focus on positive thoughts and actions, avoiding negativity and harm.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of individuals, nature, and community. It serves as a guiding principle for fostering well-being, harmony, and positive intentions in everyday life.
