वयं त इन्द्र स्तोमेभिर विद्हेम तवम् अस्माकं शतक्रतो |महि स्थूरं शशयं राधो अह्रयम् प्रस्कण्वाय नितोषय ||
vayaṁ ta indra stomebhir vidhema tvam asmākaṁ śatakrato | mahi sthūraṁ śaśayaṁ rādho ahrayam praskaṇvāya ni tośaya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vayaṁ: We
    • ta: That
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • stomebhir: With hymns
    • vidhema: We invoke
    • tvam: You
    • asmākaṁ: Our
    • śatakrato: O hundred-minded one (another name for Indra)
    • mahi: Great
    • sthūraṁ: Strong
    • śaśayaṁ: Abundant
    • rādho: Wealth
    • ahrayam: We offer
    • praskaṇvāya: For the purpose of prosperity
    • ni: To
    • tośaya: Please
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the chief deity in the Rigveda, who represents strength, power, and prosperity.
    • The invocation is made with hymns, emphasizing the importance of devotion and the power of words in spiritual practices.
    • By calling Indra "śatakrato," the mantra acknowledges his multifaceted nature and ability to grant various blessings.
    • The terms "mahi" and "sthūraṁ" highlight the desire for great strength and abundance, indicating a wish for both physical and spiritual wealth.
    • The phrase "ahrayam praskaṇvāya" signifies the offering made for the purpose of prosperity, showing the connection between worship and the attainment of material and spiritual goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the practice of setting intentions and invoking positive energies through affirmations and prayers.
    • For instance, during a significant life event such as a wedding or a new business venture, individuals may recite similar invocations to seek blessings for strength and prosperity.
    • In corporate settings, leaders may use motivational speeches that echo the essence of this mantra, invoking collective strength and abundance for the team.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in achieving strength and prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of community and collective effort in invoking blessings, whether in personal or professional realms.
    • By understanding and applying the principles behind this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset that attracts abundance and fosters resilience in the face of challenges.
