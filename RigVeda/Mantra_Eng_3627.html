शुनासीराव इमां वाचं जुषेथां यद दिवि चक्रतुः पयः |तेनेमाम उप सिञ्चतम् ||
śunāsīrāv imāṁ vācaṁ juṣethāṁ yad divi cakrathuḥ payaḥ | tenemām upa siñcatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • śunāsīrāv: This term can be interpreted as "those who are wise" or "those who have understanding."
    • imāṁ: This means "this" or "these."
    • vācaṁ: This translates to "speech" or "words."
    • juṣethāṁ: This means "may you enjoy" or "may you take pleasure in."
    • yad: This means "that" or "which."
    • divi: This translates to "in the heavens" or "in the divine realm."
    • cakrathuḥ: This can be understood as "they who have created" or "those who have established."
    • payaḥ: This means "nourishment" or "milk," often symbolizing sustenance and purity.
    • tenemām: This means "may that" or "let that."
    • upa: This translates to "near" or "close to."
    • siñcatam: This means "may it be poured" or "may it be sprinkled."
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer or invocation that seeks the blessings of wisdom and nourishment.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wise speech and the divine creation of sustenance.
    • The mantra calls for the pouring of nourishment, both physical and spiritual, into the lives of those who are wise.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied during gatherings where wisdom is shared, such as in educational institutions or spiritual retreats.
    • In personal life, one might recite this mantra before meals, reflecting on the nourishment provided by nature and the importance of wise choices in life.
    • In leadership, this mantra can serve as a reminder for leaders to speak wisely and nurture their teams, ensuring that their words and actions promote growth and sustenance.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for wisdom and nourishment, both of which are essential for personal and communal growth.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal reflection to community gatherings, emphasizing the importance of wise speech and the sustenance that comes from the divine.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset that values wisdom and nourishment, leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
