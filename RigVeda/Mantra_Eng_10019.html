अव नो वृजिनā शिशीहि ऋचा वनेमानृचः |नाब्रह्मा यज्ञ ऋधग जोषति त्वे ||
ava no vṛjinā śiśīhy ṛcā vanemānṛcaḥ | nābrahmā yajña ṛdhag joṣati tve ||
  • ava no vṛjinā śiśīhy: This phrase can be translated to "May the obstacles be removed from us." The term "vṛjinā" refers to obstacles or hindrances, while "śiśīhy" implies the act of being removed or eliminated. In real life, this can be understood as a prayer for the removal of challenges that prevent us from achieving our goals.
  • ṛcā vanemānṛcaḥ: This part translates to "May we be blessed with the essence of the hymns." Here, "ṛcā" refers to hymns or praises, and "vanemānṛcaḥ" suggests a connection to the divine or the sacred. This can be related to the importance of spirituality and the power of positive affirmations in our daily lives. For instance, reciting positive mantras or affirmations can uplift our spirits and guide us towards a more fulfilling life.
  • nābrahmā yajña ṛdhag: This translates to "The divine essence of the sacrifice is not diminished." "Nābrahmā" indicates the divine or the ultimate reality, while "yajña" refers to sacrifice or offerings. This highlights the significance of selfless actions and contributions to the greater good. In practical terms, this can be seen in community service or charitable acts, where individuals contribute to society without expecting anything in return.
  • joṣati tve: This phrase means "You are the one who nourishes." It emphasizes the idea that the divine or the higher power is the source of nourishment and support in our lives. This can be related to the concept of gratitude, where acknowledging the support we receive from others, nature, or the universe can lead to a more harmonious existence.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about overcoming obstacles, embracing spirituality, recognizing the importance of selfless actions, and acknowledging the divine support in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can serve as a reminder to maintain a positive outlook, engage in community service, and express gratitude for the blessings we receive. By embodying these principles, we can lead a more meaningful and fulfilling life, fostering a sense of connection with ourselves, others, and the universe.
