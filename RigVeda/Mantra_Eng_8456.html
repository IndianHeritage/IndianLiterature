तवेमाहः प्रजा दिव्यस्य रेतसस् त्वं विश्वस्य भुवनस्य राजसि |अथेदं विश्वं पवमान ते वशे त्वं इन्दो प्रथमो धामधाऽसि ||
tavemāḥ prajā divyasya retasas tvaṁ viśvasya bhuvanasya rājasi | athedaṁ viśvam pavamāna te vaśe tvam indo prathamo dhāmadhā asi ||
  • tavemāḥ prajā divyasya retasas: This phrase can be translated to "You are the divine essence of creation." Here, "tavemāḥ" refers to "you," "prajā" means "creation" or "beings," and "divyasya retasas" signifies "the divine essence." In real life, this can be understood as recognizing the divine presence in all living beings, encouraging us to treat every individual with respect and reverence.
  • tvaṁ viśvasya bhuvanasya rājasi: This translates to "You reign over the entire universe." "Tvaṁ" means "you," "viśvasya" refers to "of the universe," "bhuvanasya" means "of the worlds," and "rājasi" signifies "you rule." This can be related to the idea of leadership and stewardship in our communities, where individuals are encouraged to take responsibility for the well-being of the world around them.
  • athedaṁ viśvam pavamāna te vaśe: This means "This entire universe is under your control." "Athedaṁ" translates to "this," "viśvam" means "universe," "pavamāna" refers to "purifying" or "sacred," and "te vaśe" signifies "under your command." In practical terms, this can be seen in how we manage our resources and environment, emphasizing the importance of sustainable practices that honor the interconnectedness of all life.
  • tvam indo prathamo dhāmadhā asi: This translates to "You, O Indra, are the first to bestow abundance." "Tvam" means "you," "indo" refers to "Indra," a deity associated with rain and thunder, "prathamo" means "first," and "dhāmadhā asi" signifies "you bestow abundance." This can be related to the idea of generosity and sharing resources, highlighting the importance of community support and collaboration in achieving prosperity.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the divine essence present in all beings, the responsibility of leadership, the importance of stewardship over the universe, and the value of generosity. In real life, these principles can guide us in our interactions with others, our approach to environmental sustainability, and our commitment to community welfare. By embodying these values, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous world for all.
