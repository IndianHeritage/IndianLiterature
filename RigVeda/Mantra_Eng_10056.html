तेऽवदन प्रथमा ब्रह्मकिल्बिषेऽकूपारः सलिलो मातरिश्वा |वीळुहरास तप उग्रो मयोभूः आपो देविः प्रथमजा ऋतेन ||
te 'vadan prathamā brahmakilbiṣe 'kūpāraḥ salilo mātariśvā | vīḻuharās tapa ugro mayobhūr āpo devīḥ prathamajā ṛtena ||
  • te 'vadan: This phrase translates to "they who speak" or "those who utter." It signifies the importance of communication and expression in the spiritual and material realms.
  • prathamā: Meaning "first" or "foremost," this word emphasizes the priority of certain actions or entities in the cosmic order.
  • brahmakilbiṣe: This term can be interpreted as "in the realm of Brahman" or "in the essence of the ultimate reality." It points to the connection between the material and spiritual worlds.
  • 'kūpāraḥ: This means "the one who is in the water" or "the one who resides in the depths." It symbolizes the hidden knowledge and wisdom that lies beneath the surface of our understanding.
  • salilo: Referring to "water," it represents purity, life, and the flow of energy. Water is often seen as a source of sustenance and renewal.
  • mātariśvā: This term refers to the "wind" or "air," which is essential for life. It symbolizes movement, change, and the unseen forces that govern existence.
  • vīḻuharās: This can be interpreted as "those who are powerful" or "those who are strong." It highlights the strength and resilience found in nature and within ourselves.
  • tapa: Meaning "austerity" or "penance," this word signifies the importance of discipline and self-control in achieving spiritual goals.
  • ugro: This translates to "fierce" or "intense," indicating the powerful forces at play in the universe and within our own lives.
  • mayobhūr: This phrase means "may I be filled with" or "may I become." It expresses a desire for transformation and growth.
  • āpo devīḥ: This translates to "the waters are divine" or "the waters are goddesses." It signifies the sacredness of water and its vital role in life.
  • prathamajā: Meaning "the firstborn" or "the primordial," this term emphasizes the original source of creation and existence.
  • ṛtena: This word means "by truth" or "in accordance with the cosmic order." It highlights the importance of living in harmony with universal laws.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the interconnectedness of various elements of existence—communication, nature, spirituality, and the pursuit of truth. It emphasizes the significance of understanding the deeper meanings behind our experiences and the forces that shape our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek knowledge, embrace change, and live in harmony with the natural world. By recognizing the divine in everyday elements like water and air, we can cultivate a sense of reverence and responsibility towards our environment and ourselves, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and enlightened existence.
