इन्द्रासोमा तपतं रक्ष उभजातं न्य अर्पयतं वृषणा तमोवृद्धः |परा श्रिणीतम अचितो न्य ओषतं हतम् नुदेथां नि शिशीतम अत्रिणः ||
indrāsomā tapataṁ rakṣa ubjataṁ ny arpayataṁ vṛṣaṇā tamovṛdhaḥ | parā śṛṇītam acito ny oṣataṁ hataṁ nudethāṁ ni śiśītam atriṇaḥ ||
  • Indrāsomā: This term refers to the divine entities Indra and Soma. Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength, power, and rain, while Soma symbolizes the sacred drink that grants immortality and is associated with the moon.
  • Tapataṁ: This word means "to shine" or "to radiate." It signifies the brilliance and energy of the divine forces.
  • Rakṣa: This translates to "protection" or "safeguarding." It emphasizes the need for divine protection in our lives.
  • Ubjataṁ: This means "to be awakened" or "to be energized." It suggests a call for awakening the divine energies within us.
  • Ny arpayataṁ: This phrase means "to offer" or "to dedicate." It indicates the importance of offering our actions and intentions to the divine.
  • Vṛṣaṇā: This term refers to "strength" or "power." It highlights the need for strength in overcoming challenges.
  • Tamovṛdhaḥ: This means "the one who increases darkness." It represents ignorance or obstacles that hinder our progress.
  • Parā: This translates to "beyond" or "transcendent." It signifies the higher state of consciousness or divine awareness.
  • Śṛṇītam: This means "to hear" or "to listen." It emphasizes the importance of being receptive to divine guidance.
  • Acito: This translates to "the one who is not disturbed" or "calm." It represents a state of inner peace amidst chaos.
  • Ny oṣataṁ: This means "to nourish" or "to sustain." It signifies the need for spiritual nourishment.
  • Hataṁ: This translates to "defeated" or "overcome." It indicates the triumph over obstacles and ignorance.
  • Nudethāṁ: This means "to drive away" or "to remove." It emphasizes the action of dispelling negativity.
  • Ni śiśītam: This translates to "to be cooled" or "to be calmed." It signifies the restoration of peace and balance.
  • Atriṇaḥ: This refers to "the seer" or "the sage." It represents wisdom and the pursuit of knowledge.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a profound invocation for divine protection, strength, and enlightenment. It calls upon the energies of Indra and Soma to awaken within us, urging us to offer our intentions to the divine while seeking to overcome ignorance and obstacles in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during times of struggle or uncertainty, serving as a reminder to seek divine guidance, maintain inner peace, and strive for personal growth. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate resilience and clarity, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
