मन्दस्व होत्राद् अनु जोषम् अन्धसोऽध्वर्यवः स पूर्णां वष्ट्य आसिचम् |तस्मा एतम् भारत तद्वशो ददिर होत्रात् सोमं द्रविणोदः पिब ऋतुभिः ||
mandasva hotrād anu joṣam andhaso 'dhvaryavaḥ sa pūrṇāṁ vaṣṭy āsicam | tasmā etam bharata tadvaśo dadir hotrāt somaṁ draviṇodaḥ piba ṛtubhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mandasva: Be strong or powerful.
    • hotrād: From the priest or the one who offers.
    • anu: Following or in accordance with.
    • joṣam: Enjoyment or delight.
    • andhaso: Of the blind or those who are unaware.
    • 'dhvaryavaḥ: The one who performs the sacrifice.
    • sa: He or that.
    • pūrṇāṁ: Full or complete.
    • vaṣṭy: Offering or oblation.
    • āsicam: To pour or to sprinkle.
    • tasmā: Therefore or for this reason.
    • etam: This.
    • bharata: O Bharata (a term often referring to the people of India).
    • tadvaśo: Under its influence or control.
    • dadir: To give or grant.
    • hotrāt: From the priest or the one who offers.
    • somaṁ: Soma (a sacred drink in Vedic rituals).
    • draviṇodaḥ: Wealth or prosperity.
    • piba: Drink.
    • ṛtubhiḥ: With the seasons or in accordance with the seasons.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength and power in the context of rituals and offerings.
    • The term "mandasva" suggests that one should cultivate strength, which can be interpreted as both physical and mental resilience.
    • The reference to "hotrād" indicates the role of the priest in performing rituals, highlighting the significance of guidance and leadership in spiritual practices.
    • "anu joṣam" implies that one should follow the path of enjoyment and delight, suggesting that spiritual practices should be fulfilling and joyous.
    • The mention of "andhaso" points to the need for awareness and enlightenment, as rituals should not be performed blindly.
    • "dhvaryavaḥ" refers to the importance of the sacrificial act, which can be seen as a metaphor for selfless giving in everyday life.
    • The phrase "pūrṇāṁ vaṣṭy āsicam" indicates the act of offering something complete and pure, which can be related to the idea of giving one's best in any endeavor.
    • "tasmā etam bharata" calls upon the people of India to recognize the importance of these values in their lives.
    • The mantra concludes with a call to drink the "soma," symbolizing the pursuit of prosperity and abundance in alignment with the natural rhythms of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of strength, awareness, and selfless giving in both spiritual and everyday life.
    • It encourages individuals to seek joy and fulfillment in their actions while being mindful of their impact on others.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as community service, leadership roles, and personal development, where one strives to contribute positively to society.
    • Ultimately, the mantra highlights the interconnectedness of individual actions and the collective well-being, urging us to act with intention and purpose.
