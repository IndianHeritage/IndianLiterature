आय पवस्व धारया यया सूर्यं अरोचयः |हिन्वानो मानवीर अपः ||
ayā pavasva dhārayā yayā sūryam arocayaḥ | hinvāno mānuṣīr apaḥ ||
  • ayā pavasva: This phrase can be translated as "O Wind, may you come forth." Here, the wind symbolizes the vital force or energy that sustains life. In real life, we can relate this to the importance of air and breath in our existence. Just as the wind is essential for nature, our breath is vital for our survival.
  • dhārayā: This word means "by which you support" or "sustain." It emphasizes the role of the wind in maintaining balance in the environment. For example, the wind helps in pollination, which is crucial for the growth of plants and crops, thereby supporting life on Earth.
  • yayā sūryam arocayaḥ: This translates to "by which you make the Sun shine." The Sun is a source of light and energy, essential for life. The wind plays a role in weather patterns that can affect sunlight, such as clouds and storms. In our daily lives, we often rely on sunlight for warmth and growth, both in nature and in our homes (e.g., solar energy).
  • hinvāno mānuṣīr apaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "removing the waters of humanity." Here, "waters" can symbolize emotions, challenges, or obstacles that humans face. The wind can be seen as a force that clears away these difficulties, allowing for clarity and progress. In real life, we often experience challenges that can feel overwhelming, and just as the wind can clear the skies, we can find ways to overcome our obstacles through resilience and support from others.

  • Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the interconnectedness of natural elements and their significance in sustaining life. The wind, as a vital force, supports life, influences the Sun's energy, and helps clear emotional or physical obstacles. In our daily lives, we can apply the teachings of this mantra by recognizing the importance of nature in our well-being and finding ways to harness our inner strength to overcome challenges. It serves as a reminder to appreciate the natural forces around us and to seek balance in our lives.
