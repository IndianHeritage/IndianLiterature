शृण्वन्तु नो वृषणः पर्वतासो ध्रुवक्षेमासा इळया मदन्तः |आदित्यैः नो अदितिः शृणोतु यच्छन्तु नो मरुतः शर्मा भद्रम् ||
śṛṇvantu no vṛṣaṇaḥ parvatāso dhruvakṣemāsa iḻayā madantaḥ | ādityair no aditiḥ śṛṇotu yacchantu no marutaḥ śarma bhadram ||
  • śṛṇvantu no vṛṣaṇaḥ: This phrase translates to "Let the strong ones listen to us." Here, "vṛṣaṇaḥ" refers to those who are powerful or strong, often symbolizing leaders or deities. In real life, this can be seen as a call for attention from those in positions of authority or influence, urging them to heed the voices of the people.
  • parvatāso dhruvakṣemāsa: This means "the mountains that are stable and secure." Mountains often symbolize strength and permanence. In a real-life context, this can represent the stability and support that we seek in our lives, whether it be in our relationships, careers, or communities.
  • iḻayā madantaḥ: This translates to "with the joy of the mind." It emphasizes the importance of mental well-being and happiness. In everyday life, this can relate to the pursuit of mental health, joy, and contentment, highlighting the need for a positive mindset.
  • ādityair no aditiḥ: This phrase means "Let the sun gods listen to us." "Āditya" refers to the sun, which is often associated with life, energy, and enlightenment. In practical terms, this can be seen as a reminder to seek guidance and illumination in our lives, drawing from the wisdom of nature and the universe.
  • śṛṇotu yacchantu no marutaḥ: This translates to "Let the winds hear us and grant us." "Marutaḥ" refers to the winds, which symbolize change and movement. In real life, this can signify the importance of adaptability and the need to embrace change, as well as the desire for support from the forces of nature.
  • śarma bhadram: This means "may we have auspicious protection." It is a plea for safety and well-being. In a contemporary context, this can relate to the need for security in various aspects of life, such as health, relationships, and personal safety.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for connection, stability, joy, guidance, adaptability, and protection. Its significance lies in the universal human desire for support from both the divine and natural realms. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as seeking guidance during challenging times, fostering mental well-being, and embracing change with resilience. It serves as a reminder that we are part of a larger universe, and by aligning ourselves with its forces, we can find strength and security in our lives.
