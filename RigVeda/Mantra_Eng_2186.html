सास्मा अरम् प्रथमं स द्वितीयम् उतो तृतीयम् मनुष्यः स होता |अन्यस्याः गर्भम् अन्य ū जनन्त सो अन्यभिः साचते जेन्यो वृषा ||
sāsmā aram prathamaṁ sa dvitīyam uto tṛtīyam manuṣaḥ sa hotā | anyasyā garbham anya ū jananta so anyebhiḥ sacate jenyo vṛṣā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sāsmā: From this
    • aram: The first
    • prathamaṁ: The foremost
    • sa: He
    • dvitīyam: The second
    • uto: And
    • tṛtīyam: The third
    • manuṣaḥ: The human being
    • sa: He
    • hotā: The priest or the one who offers
    • anyasyā: Of another
    • garbham: The womb or the embryo
    • anya: Another
    • ū: They
    • jananta: Know or understand
    • so: He
    • anyebhiḥ: By others
    • sacate: Is born or is produced
    • jenyo: The one who is born
    • vṛṣā: The strong or the powerful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the stages of human existence and the roles played by individuals in the cosmic order.
    • The first part emphasizes the importance of the initial stages of life, suggesting that every human being has a unique role to play.
    • The mention of "hotā" indicates the role of a priest or a guide, highlighting the significance of spiritual leadership in society.
    • The latter part of the mantra reflects on the interconnectedness of individuals, where one’s existence is influenced by others, and knowledge is shared among them.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family, the firstborn child often takes on a leadership role, guiding younger siblings, similar to the "hotā" in the mantra.
    • In a workplace, a mentor (the "hotā") plays a crucial role in shaping the careers of new employees, emphasizing the importance of guidance and knowledge sharing.
    • In communities, the collective effort of individuals leads to the success of projects, showcasing how interconnectedness and collaboration are vital for progress.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing our roles in the larger scheme of life, whether as leaders, guides, or learners.
    • It teaches us that every individual contributes to the collective knowledge and strength of society.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as family dynamics, workplace mentorship, and community collaboration, reminding us of our responsibilities towards one another.
