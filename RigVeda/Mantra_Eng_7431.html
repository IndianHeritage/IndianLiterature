कया ते अग्नि अंगिर उर्जो नपाद उपस्तुतिम् |वराय देव मन्यवे ||
kayā te agne aṅgira ūrjo napād upastutim | varāya deva manyave ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kayā: With what (or how)
    • te: Your
    • agne: O Agni (the Fire God)
    • aṅgira: O Angiras (a sage or divine being)
    • ūrjo: Energy or strength
    • napād: With the foot (or in the presence)
    • upastutim: Praise or worship
    • varāya: Bestow or grant
    • deva: O God
    • manyave: With thought or intention
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the God of Fire, and Angiras, a divine sage, seeking their blessings and strength.
    • The speaker is inquiring how to praise Agni and Angiras effectively, emphasizing the importance of intention and energy in worship.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek guidance and strength from higher powers or mentors when facing challenges. For instance, a student may pray for focus and energy before an important exam, similar to how the mantra seeks divine support.
    • In a corporate setting, a team may invoke the spirit of collaboration and innovation before starting a new project, reflecting the essence of seeking strength and intention in their endeavors.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of intention and energy in any form of worship or effort. It teaches that the way we approach our goals—whether through prayer, work, or personal relationships—should be filled with purpose and strength.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as setting intentions for personal growth, seeking blessings for new ventures, or fostering a positive mindset in challenging situations.
