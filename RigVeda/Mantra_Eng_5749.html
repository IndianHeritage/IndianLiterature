अश्वासो ये वाम उप दाशुषो गृहम् युवां दीयन्ति बिभ्रतः |मक्षूयुभिर नराः हयेभिः अश्विनाः देवाः यातम अस्मयु ||
aśvāso ye vām upa dāśuṣo gṛhaṁ yuvāṁ dīyanti bibhrataḥ | makṣūyubhir narā hayebhir aśvinā devā yātam asmayū ||
  • Mantra: "aśvāso ye vām upa dāśuṣo gṛhaṁ yuvāṁ dīyanti bibhrataḥ | makṣūyubhir narā hayebhir aśvinā devā yātam asmayū ||"
  • Translation: "Those who, like horses, bring you wealth and prosperity, may the divine Aśvins, who are like men, lead you to your home with abundance."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • aśvāso: Refers to horses, symbolizing strength, speed, and wealth.
    • ye: Those who.
    • vām: You (plural), addressing a group.
    • upa: Towards or near.
    • dāśuṣo: Bringing or providing.
    • gṛhaṁ: Home or dwelling.
    • yuvāṁ: You both (plural).
    • dīyanti: They give or bestow.
    • bibhrataḥ: Abundance or wealth.
    • makṣūyubhir: With the help of the divine.
    • narā: Men or people.
    • hayebhir: Like horses.
    • aśvinā: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin gods of medicine and health.
    • devā: Gods or divine beings.
    • yātam: May lead or guide.
    • asmayū: To prosperity or abundance.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine blessings in achieving prosperity and abundance in life.
    • The reference to horses symbolizes not just wealth but also the strength and speed required to achieve one's goals.
    • The Aśvins, as divine figures, represent health and well-being, suggesting that prosperity is not just material but also encompasses physical and mental health.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be invoked during significant life events such as starting a new business, moving into a new home, or embarking on a new journey.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek divine guidance and support in all endeavors, ensuring that one remains grounded and focused on both material and spiritual wealth.
    • Practicing gratitude and recognizing the abundance in life can lead to a more fulfilling existence, aligning with the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the idea that true prosperity comes from a combination of divine blessings, personal effort, and the support of the community.
    • By acknowledging the role of the divine and striving for balance in life, individuals can navigate their paths with confidence and purpose.
    • Ultimately, it encourages a holistic approach to wealth, encompassing not just material success but also health, happiness, and harmony.
