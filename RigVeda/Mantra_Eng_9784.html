इन्द्राय गीरो अनिशितसर्गा अपः प्रेयरयं सागरस्य बुद्धनात् |यो अक्षेणेव चक्रिया शचीभिर विश्वक तस्तम्भ पृथिवीं उता द्याम् ||
indrāya giro aniśitasargā apaḥ prerayaṁ sagarasya budhnāt | yo akṣeṇeva cakriyā śacībhir viṣvak tastambha pṛthivīm uta dyām ||
  • Indrāya: This refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Giro: This means "words" or "speech," indicating the importance of communication and expression.
  • Aniśitasargā: This can be interpreted as "the swift-moving" or "the one who is quick in action," emphasizing the need for promptness and decisiveness.
  • Apaḥ: This translates to "waters," which often symbolize life, nourishment, and purification.
  • Prerayaṁ: This means "to inspire" or "to motivate," highlighting the role of divine influence in human actions.
  • Sagarasya: Referring to "the ocean," it represents vastness, depth, and the source of life.
  • Budhnāt: This means "from the depths" or "from the ocean," suggesting wisdom and knowledge that can be drawn from profound sources.
  • Yo: This means "who," indicating a subject or a person of focus.
  • Akṣeṇeva: This translates to "like a wheel," symbolizing movement, progress, and the cyclical nature of life.
  • Cakriyā: This means "action" or "deed," emphasizing the importance of taking initiative.
  • Śacībhir: This refers to "with the powers" or "with the energies," indicating the support of divine forces.
  • Viṣvak: This means "all" or "universal," suggesting inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Tastambha: This translates to "he established" or "he set in place," indicating the act of creation or foundation.
  • Pṛthivīm: This means "the earth," symbolizing stability, grounding, and material existence.
  • Uta: This means "and" or "also," connecting ideas and concepts.
  • Dyām: This translates to "the sky" or "heaven," representing the spiritual realm and aspirations.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine inspiration and the interconnectedness of all elements of life. It emphasizes the importance of communication, decisive action, and the support of divine forces in establishing a harmonious existence on earth and in the heavens. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as leadership, teamwork, and personal growth. For instance, a leader who communicates effectively and inspires their team can create a productive environment, much like Indra, who brings order and strength to the cosmos. The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of words, actions, and the divine influence that guides us in our endeavors.
