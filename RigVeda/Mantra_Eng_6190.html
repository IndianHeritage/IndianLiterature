मन्त्र: सृजन्ति रश्मिम ओजसा पन्थां सूर्याय यातवे |ते भानुभिर विथस्थिरे ||
sṛjanti raśmim ojasā panthāṁ sūryāya yātave | te bhānubhir vi tasthire ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sṛjanti: They create or manifest
    • raśmim: rays or beams
    • ojasā: with energy or power
    • panthāṁ: the path
    • sūryāya: to the Sun
    • yātave: who is moving or traveling
    • te: they
    • bhānubhir: with the light of the Sun
    • vi: indeed
    • tasthire: they stand or exist
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra speaks of the creation of paths by the rays of the Sun, which are infused with energy. It suggests that the Sun, as a source of light and vitality, illuminates the paths that are traveled by those who seek knowledge and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Guidance in Life: Just as the Sun's rays illuminate the path, in life, we often seek guidance from mentors, teachers, or experiences that help us navigate our journey. For instance, a student may find clarity in their career path through the guidance of a teacher.
    • Energy and Vitality: The Sun is a symbol of energy. In real life, we can relate this to the importance of maintaining our physical and mental health. Engaging in outdoor activities under sunlight can boost our mood and energy levels, much like the rays of the Sun energizing the path.
    • Spiritual Enlightenment: Many spiritual practices emphasize the importance of light as a metaphor for knowledge and enlightenment. For example, meditation and yoga often take place in natural light, symbolizing the pursuit of inner peace and understanding.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its representation of the Sun as a source of energy, guidance, and enlightenment. It teaches us that just as the Sun creates paths with its rays, we too can find our way through life by seeking knowledge, maintaining our vitality, and embracing the light of understanding. This mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, from personal growth to spiritual journeys, reminding us to look for the light that guides us forward.
