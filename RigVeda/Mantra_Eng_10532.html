वात आ वातु भेषजं शम्भु मयोभु नो हृदे |प्र ण आयूंषि तारीषत ||
vāta ā vātu bheṣajaṁ śambhu mayobhu no hṛde | pra ṇa āyūṁṣi tāriṣat ||
  • vāta: This word refers to the wind or air, symbolizing the vital life force that sustains all living beings. In real life, we can relate this to the importance of fresh air and its role in our health and well-being.
  • ā vātu: This phrase can be interpreted as "may it protect" or "may it come to us." It signifies a plea for protection and support from the divine forces of nature.
  • bheṣajaṁ: This term means "medicine" or "healing." It emphasizes the need for health and wellness, which is crucial in our daily lives. For example, we often seek medical help or natural remedies to maintain our health.
  • śambhu: Referring to Lord Shiva, who is often associated with auspiciousness and well-being. In a broader sense, it represents the divine presence that brings peace and harmony into our lives.
  • mayobhu: This word can be translated as "may it be in our hearts." It suggests the importance of internalizing positive qualities and divine blessings within ourselves.
  • no: This is a possessive pronoun meaning "our," indicating a collective desire for well-being and protection.
  • hṛde: This word means "heart," symbolizing the emotional and spiritual center of a person. It highlights the significance of love, compassion, and connection in our lives.
  • pra ṇa: This term refers to "life force" or "breath," which is essential for existence. It reminds us of the importance of being mindful of our health and vitality.
  • āyūṁṣi: This word means "lifespan" or "longevity." It emphasizes the desire for a long and healthy life, which is a common aspiration for many individuals.
  • tāriṣat: This term can be interpreted as "may it be preserved" or "may it be sustained." It reflects the hope for continuity and stability in life.

Conclusion: The mantra "vāta ā vātu bheṣajaṁ śambhu mayobhu no hṛde | pra ṇa āyūṁṣi tāriṣat ||" encapsulates a profound prayer for health, protection, and longevity. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of the elements of nature, divine blessings, and human well-being. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during meditation, healing practices, or when seeking peace and harmony in our lives. By internalizing its message, we can cultivate a sense of gratitude for the life force within us and strive for a balanced and healthy existence.
