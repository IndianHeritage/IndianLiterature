उत न इम मतयोऽश्वयोगाः शिशुं न गावस तरुणं रिहन्ति |तम इं गिरो जनयो न पत्निः सुरभिष्टमं नरां नसन्त ||
uta na īm matayo 'śvayogāḥ śiśuṁ na gāvas taruṇaṁ rihanti | tam īṁ giro janayo na patnīḥ surabhiṣṭamaṁ narāṁ nasanta ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and
    • na: not
    • īm: we (or our)
    • matayo: thoughts or intentions
    • 'śvayogāḥ: those who are skilled or adept
    • śiśuṁ: child
    • na: not
    • gāvas: cows (symbolizing wealth and nourishment)
    • taruṇaṁ: young or youthful
    • rihanti: they harm or injure
    • tam: that
    • īṁ: we (or our)
    • giro: words or speech
    • janayo: may generate or create
    • na: not
    • patnīḥ: wives or partners
    • surabhiṣṭamaṁ: the best of fragrant or auspicious
    • narāṁ: of men or humans
    • nasanta: may we not be harmed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of positive thoughts and intentions.
    • It suggests that those who are skilled should not harm the innocent, represented by the child and young cows.
    • The mantra calls for the generation of words that are not harmful, promoting harmony and well-being.
    • It highlights the significance of nurturing relationships, particularly in the context of partnerships and community.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, this mantra can be applied by encouraging a culture of support and collaboration, where experienced employees mentor newcomers instead of undermining them.
    • In family dynamics, it serves as a reminder to protect and nurture children, ensuring they grow up in a safe and loving environment.
    • In community settings, it can inspire individuals to use their words wisely, fostering dialogue that builds rather than divides.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for compassion, responsibility, and the power of words.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, emphasizing the need for positive interactions.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and supportive society.
