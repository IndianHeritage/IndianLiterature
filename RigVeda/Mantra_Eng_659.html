अप्रक्षितं वसु बिभर्षि हस्तयोः अशाल्हं सहस तन्वी श्रुतो दधे |आवृतासो 'वतासो न कर्तृभिः तनूषु ते क्रतव इन्द्र भूयः ||
aprakṣitaṁ vasu bibharṣi hastayor aṣāḻhaṁ sahas tanvi śruto dadhe | āvṛtāso 'vatāso na kartṛbhis tanūṣu te kratava indra bhūrayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aprakṣitaṁ: Unseen or hidden
    • vasu: Wealth or prosperity
    • bibharṣi: You bear or carry
    • hastayor: Of the hands
    • aṣāḻhaṁ: Unbroken or undeterred
    • sahas: With strength
    • tanvi: Delicate or subtle
    • śruto: Heard or known
    • dadhe: You establish or create
    • āvṛtāso: Covered or enveloped
    • 'vatāso: Those who are protected
    • na: Not
    • kartṛbhis: By the doers
    • tanūṣu: In the bodies
    • te: Your
    • kratava: Energies or powers
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • bhūrayaḥ: Abundant or plentiful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the unseen wealth and prosperity that one carries within themselves.
    • It emphasizes the strength and subtlety of one's actions and intentions.
    • The reference to Indra suggests a call for divine support in manifesting these energies.
    • The phrase "not by the doers" implies that true success comes from a higher power rather than mere human effort.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to the idea that while hard work is essential, sometimes success comes from unexpected sources or divine intervention.
    • In personal development, it can remind individuals that their inner strengths and qualities may not always be visible but are crucial for their growth.
    • In community service, it highlights the importance of collective efforts and the unseen support that can come from the community or higher ideals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and harnessing the unseen strengths and resources within ourselves.
    • It teaches that while we may strive for success through our actions, there is often a greater force at play that aids in our journey.
    • In real life, this can be applicable in various scenarios, from personal growth to professional endeavors, reminding us to remain open to the unseen possibilities that life offers.
