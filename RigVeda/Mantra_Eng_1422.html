मन्त्र: चत्वारिंशद दशरथस्य शोणाः सहस्रस्याग्रे श्रेणिं नयन्ति | मदाच्युतः कृशनावतः अत्यान कक्षीवन्त उद अमृक्षन्त पज्राः ||
catvāriṁśad daśarathasya śoṇāḥ sahasrasyāgre śreṇiṁ nayanti | madacyutaḥ kṛśanāvato atyān kakṣīvanta ud amṛkṣanta pajrāḥ ||
  • catvāriṁśad: This term translates to "forty." In the context of the mantra, it signifies a large number or a significant quantity.
  • daśarathasya: Referring to "Dasaratha," a legendary king in Hindu mythology, particularly known as the father of Lord Rama. This evokes themes of leadership and responsibility.
  • śoṇāḥ: This word means "red" or "beautiful." It can symbolize vitality, strength, and auspiciousness.
  • sahasrasyāgre: This translates to "at the forefront of thousands," indicating prominence or leadership among many.
  • śreṇiṁ: Meaning "group" or "line," it suggests organization and unity among individuals.
  • nayanti: This means "they lead" or "they guide," emphasizing the role of leadership and direction.
  • madacyutaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "one who is intoxicated with joy" or "blissful," suggesting a state of happiness and fulfillment.
  • kṛśanāvato: This refers to "the one who is lean" or "slender," which can symbolize humility or simplicity.
  • atyān: Meaning "beyond" or "transcending," it indicates surpassing limitations or boundaries.
  • kakṣīvanta: This term means "those who are strong" or "powerful," highlighting strength and resilience.
  • ud: This translates to "up" or "above," suggesting elevation or ascension.
  • amṛkṣanta: Meaning "they attain immortality," it signifies the pursuit of eternal truths or spiritual enlightenment.
  • pajrāḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who are wise" or "knowledgeable," emphasizing the importance of wisdom in leadership and life.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates themes of leadership, strength, unity, and the pursuit of wisdom. It highlights the importance of guiding others while embodying qualities such as humility and joy. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in leadership roles within organizations, community service, or personal development. By embracing these qualities, individuals can inspire and lead others towards a common goal, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose. The mantra serves as a reminder of the virtues that contribute to effective leadership and the importance of striving for higher ideals in our lives.
