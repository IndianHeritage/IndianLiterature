सहस्रधारेऽव ते समस्वरन् दिवो नाके मधुजिव्हा असश्चतः |अस्य स्पशो न निमिषन्ति भूर्णयः पदे-पदे पाशिनः सन्ति सेतवः ||
sahasradhāre 'va te sam asvaran divo nāke madhujihvā asaścataḥ | asya spaśo na ni miṣanti bhūrṇayaḥ pade-pade pāśinaḥ santi setavaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahasradhāre: One who has a thousand streams or channels.
    • va: or.
    • te: your.
    • sam asvaran: have become harmonious or united.
    • divo: of the heavens.
    • nāke: in the sky.
    • madhujihvā: sweet-tongued or eloquent.
    • asaścataḥ: in all directions.
    • asya: of this.
    • spaśo: the one who is perceptible.
    • na: not.
    • ni miṣanti: do not blink or close (the eyes).
    • bhūrṇayaḥ: the great ones or the powerful.
    • pade-pade: at every step.
    • pāśinaḥ: those who are bound or tied.
    • santi: are.
    • setavaḥ: the means of liberation or release.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence that is omnipresent and powerful, symbolized by the "thousand streams" which can be interpreted as the various forms of energy or blessings that flow from the divine.
    • The phrase "sweet-tongued" suggests eloquence and the ability to communicate effectively, which is essential in both personal and professional relationships.
    • The idea that "the great ones do not blink" emphasizes the importance of vigilance and awareness in our lives, encouraging us to remain alert to opportunities and challenges.
    • The mention of "pāśinaḥ" indicates that there are bindings or limitations in life, but the "setavaḥ" signifies that there are also means to overcome these limitations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, the mantra can be applied to teamwork where diverse talents (the "thousand streams") come together harmoniously to achieve a common goal.
    • In personal relationships, effective communication (the "sweet-tongued") can help resolve conflicts and strengthen bonds.
    • In daily life, being vigilant (not blinking) can help individuals seize opportunities, such as a job opening or a chance to learn something new.
    • Recognizing personal limitations (the "bindings") and actively seeking ways to overcome them (the "means of liberation") can lead to personal growth and fulfillment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives, encouraging us to harness our collective strengths, communicate effectively, remain vigilant, and seek liberation from our limitations.
    • Its significance lies in its applicability across various aspects of life, from personal development to professional success, highlighting the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of unity and awareness.
