उद यद इन्द्रो महते दानवाय वधार यमिष्ट सहो अप्रतीतम् |यद ईं वज्रस्य प्रभृतौ ददाभ विś्वस्य जन्तोर अधमं चकार ||
ud yad indro mahate dānavāya vadhar yamiṣṭa saho apratītam | yad īṁ vajrasya prabhṛtau dadābha viśvasya jantor adhamaṁ cakāra ||
  • ud yad indro mahate dānavāya vadhar yamiṣṭa saho apratītam
    • ud: This word can be interpreted as "up" or "out," indicating a rise or elevation.
    • yad: Translates to "that" or "which," referring to something specific.
    • indro: Refers to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power and authority.
    • mahate: Means "great" or "mighty," emphasizing the strength of Indra.
    • dānavāya: Refers to "the demon" or "the adversary," representing challenges or obstacles.
    • vadhar: Means "to strike" or "to kill," indicating the action of overcoming challenges.
    • yamiṣṭa: Translates to "what is desired" or "what is wished for," referring to aspirations or goals.
    • saho: Means "with strength" or "with power," indicating the need for resilience.
    • apratītam: Refers to "unseen" or "unknown," suggesting the unpredictable nature of challenges.
  • yad īṁ vajrasya prabhṛtau dadābha viśvasya jantor adhamaṁ cakāra
    • yad: Again, "that" or "which," referring to a specific action or event.
    • īṁ: A term that can denote "the essence" or "the core," indicating the fundamental nature of something.
    • vajrasya: Refers to "the thunderbolt" or "the weapon of Indra," symbolizing strength and invincibility.
    • prabhṛtau: Means "in the manifestation" or "in the display," indicating the realization of power.
    • dadābha: Translates to "he gave" or "he bestowed," indicating the act of granting strength or power.
    • viśvasya: Means "of the universe" or "of all beings," indicating a universal application.
    • jantor: Refers to "creatures" or "living beings," emphasizing the relevance to all life.
    • adhamaṁ: Means "the lowest" or "the weakest," indicating the challenges faced by the vulnerable.
    • cakāra: Means "he made" or "he created," indicating the act of transformation or change.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of strength and resilience in overcoming challenges. It invokes the power of Indra, who symbolizes authority and the ability to conquer adversities. The mantra suggests that even in the face of unseen and unpredictable challenges, one can aspire to achieve their goals with strength and determination.

In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Facing personal challenges, like overcoming fears or obstacles in career paths.
  • In community settings, where collective strength is needed to address social issues.
  • In leadership, where the ability to inspire and empower others is crucial for success.

Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that with the right mindset and strength, one can navigate through life's adversities and emerge victorious.
