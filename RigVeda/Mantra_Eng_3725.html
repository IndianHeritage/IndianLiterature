त्वां अग्नि समिधानं यविष्ठ्य देवā दूतं चक्रिरे हव्यवाहनम् |उरुज्रयसं घृतयोनिमाहुतं त्वेषं चक्षुर दधिरे चोदयन्मति ||
tvām agne samidhānaṁ yaviṣṭhya devā dūtaṁ cakrire havyavāhanam | urujrayasaṁ ghṛtayonim āhutaṁ tveṣaṁ cakṣur dadhire codayanmati ||
  • tvām agne samidhānaṁ: This phrase translates to "O Agni, we kindle you." Agni, the fire god, represents energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, this can be likened to the act of igniting passion or motivation within oneself to pursue goals.
  • yaviṣṭhya devā dūtaṁ cakrire: This means "the gods have made you their messenger." Here, Agni is seen as a divine messenger, conveying offerings to the gods. In a modern context, this can be compared to the role of communication in our lives, where messages and intentions are conveyed through various means, such as technology or interpersonal relationships.
  • havyavāhanam: This term refers to "the one who carries offerings." It signifies the importance of giving and sharing in our lives. For example, in community service, individuals offer their time and resources to help others, embodying the spirit of 'havyavāhanam.'
  • urujrayasaṁ ghṛtayonim: This translates to "the one who is rich in strength and has a ghee-like essence." Ghee symbolizes purity and nourishment. In real life, this can be related to the idea of nurturing relationships and fostering strength within communities, much like how ghee is used in cooking to enhance flavors and provide sustenance.
  • āhutaṁ tveṣaṁ: This means "we invoke you." It reflects the act of calling upon divine energy or inspiration. In everyday life, this can be seen in moments of prayer, meditation, or even when seeking guidance from mentors or leaders.
  • cakṣur dadhire: This translates to "they have given you sight." It signifies the importance of vision and insight. In a practical sense, this can relate to having clarity in one’s goals and aspirations, akin to setting a clear path in one’s career or personal life.
  • codayanmati: This means "inspiring thought." It emphasizes the role of inspiration in decision-making and creativity. In real life, this can be observed in how art, literature, and innovation inspire individuals to think differently and pursue new ideas.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of invoking divine energy, communication, and the importance of offerings in our lives. It signifies the need for clarity, strength, and inspiration in our pursuits. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, community service, and creative endeavors. By kindling our inner fire (Agni), we can become messengers of positivity and inspiration, fostering a sense of purpose and connection in our lives and the lives of others.
