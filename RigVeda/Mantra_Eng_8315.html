अमृत्क्तेन रुशता वाससा हरिर अमर्त्यो निर्णिजानः परि व्यत || दिवस पृष्टम् बरहणा निर्णिजे कृतोपस्तरणं चाम्वोर नभस्मयम् ||
amṛktena ruśatā vāsasā harir amartyo nirṇijānaḥ pari vyata | divas pṛṣṭham barhaṇā nirṇije kṛtopastaraṇaṁ camvor nabhasmayam ||
  • amṛktena: This word translates to "with the immortal" or "by the nectar of immortality." In a real-life context, this can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom that transcends the temporary nature of life.
  • ruśatā: This means "shining" or "radiant." It signifies the brilliance of enlightenment or understanding that comes from knowledge.
  • vāsasā: This can be interpreted as "with the garment" or "with the covering." It suggests the idea of protection or the essence that envelops us, akin to the values and principles we live by.
  • harir: Referring to "Hari," a name often associated with the divine or God, representing the ultimate source of life and sustenance.
  • amartyo: This translates to "the immortal one," indicating a state of being beyond death, which can be seen as a metaphor for the legacy we leave behind through our actions and contributions.
  • nirṇijānaḥ: This means "one who knows the truth" or "the enlightened one." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in navigating life.
  • pari vyata: This phrase can be understood as "surrounding" or "encompassing," suggesting a holistic approach to life and existence.
  • divas: Meaning "day" or "heaven," it symbolizes clarity, enlightenment, and the pursuit of higher ideals.
  • pṛṣṭham: This translates to "the back" or "the support," indicating the foundation upon which we build our lives.
  • barhaṇā: This can be interpreted as "the one who is adorned" or "the one who is celebrated," representing the recognition of virtues and achievements.
  • nirṇije: Meaning "without doubt" or "certain," it emphasizes the importance of conviction in our beliefs and actions.
  • kṛtopastaraṇaṁ: This translates to "the one who has made the offering" or "the one who has prepared the way," indicating the significance of service and contribution to society.
  • camvor: This word can be understood as "of the sky" or "heavenly," suggesting a connection to the divine or higher consciousness.
  • nabhasmayam: This means "the one who is in the sky" or "the celestial," representing the ultimate goal of spiritual elevation and enlightenment.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking immortality through knowledge, wisdom, and virtuous living. It emphasizes the importance of enlightenment, the pursuit of truth, and the recognition of our divine connection. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in education, where the quest for knowledge leads to personal and societal growth, or in community service, where our contributions can create a lasting impact. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to live a life of purpose, integrity, and connection to the greater whole.
