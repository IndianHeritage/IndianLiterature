त्वेषस ते धूम ऋण्वति दिवि षañ चुक्र आततः |सूरो न हि द्युताऽ त्वं कृपा पावक रोचसे ||
tveṣas te dhūma ṛṇvati divi ṣañ chukra ātataḥ | sūro na hi dyutā tvaṁ kṛpā pāvaka rocase ||
  • tveṣas te - This phrase can be translated as "Your form" or "Your appearance." It refers to the divine manifestation or the essence of a deity.
  • dhūma ṛṇvati - This means "the smoke rises" or "the smoke is offering." In many cultures, smoke is associated with offerings to the divine, symbolizing the connection between the earthly and the spiritual realms.
  • divi - This word translates to "in the heavens" or "in the sky," indicating a celestial or divine context.
  • ṣañ chukra ātataḥ - This can be interpreted as "the six bright ones" or "the six luminous beings." It suggests a connection to celestial bodies or divine entities that illuminate the sky.
  • sūro na hi dyutā - This phrase means "the hero is not without brilliance." It emphasizes the idea that true valor and heroism are accompanied by a radiant quality, often associated with divine favor.
  • tvaṁ kṛpā - This translates to "You are compassion." It highlights the importance of compassion as a divine attribute, suggesting that the deity embodies mercy and kindness.
  • pāvaka rocase - This means "the fire that shines." Fire is often a symbol of purification and transformation, representing the divine energy that illuminates and purifies the soul.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine presence, emphasizing the connection between the earthly and the celestial. It highlights the importance of compassion, heroism, and the transformative power of fire. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during rituals, meditation, or moments of reflection, where individuals seek to connect with the divine, cultivate compassion, and embrace their inner strength. It serves as a reminder of the divine qualities that can guide us in our daily lives, encouraging us to embody compassion and brilliance in our actions.
