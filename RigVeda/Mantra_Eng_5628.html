ओ षु घृष्विराधसो यातनान्धांसी पीतये |इमा वो हव्या मरुतो ररे हि कम मो ष्व अन्यत्र गन्तना ||
o ṣu ghṛṣvirādhaso yātanāndhāṁsi pītaye | imā vo havyā maruto rare hi kam mo ṣv a1nyatra gantana ||
  • Mantra: "o ṣu ghṛṣvirādhaso yātanāndhāṁsi pītaye | imā vo havyā maruto rare hi kam mo ṣv a1nyatra gantana ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be interpreted as a call to the Maruts, the storm deities, to come and partake in the offerings made to them. It expresses a desire for their presence and blessings.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • o: An invocation or call.
    • ṣu: A term that can denote auspiciousness or goodness.
    • ghṛṣvirādhaso: Refers to the Maruts, often associated with strength and vigor.
    • yātanāndhāṁsi: Suggests a journey or movement, possibly indicating the Maruts' swift nature.
    • pītaye: Relates to drinking or consuming, indicating the act of receiving offerings.
    • imā: This means 'these' or 'this', referring to the offerings being made.
    • vo: Means 'to you', addressing the Maruts directly.
    • havyā: Refers to offerings that are suitable for the gods.
    • maruto: Again, a reference to the Maruts, emphasizing their divine nature.
    • rare: Indicates a call or a shout, urging the Maruts to come forth.
    • hi: A particle that can mean 'indeed' or 'certainly'.
    • kam: Means 'who' or 'what', questioning the nature of the offerings.
    • mo: A term that can mean 'to you' or 'for you'.
    • ṣv: A particle that can indicate a wish or desire.
    • anyatra: Means 'elsewhere' or 'in another place'.
    • gantana: Refers to going or moving, indicating the action of the Maruts.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, similar invocations are made during ceremonies to invite blessings from deities, such as during weddings or festivals.
    • In nature, the Maruts symbolize the forces of wind and storm, reminding us of the power and unpredictability of nature, which can be seen during thunderstorms.
    • In personal life, one might call upon divine forces during times of need, seeking guidance and support in challenging situations.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine forces to bless and accept offerings. It highlights the relationship between humans and the divine, emphasizing the importance of rituals in seeking favor and support. In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios where individuals seek strength, guidance, and blessings, whether in personal challenges, communal gatherings, or during significant life events. The essence of the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the divine, encouraging individuals to acknowledge and honor the forces that shape their existence.
