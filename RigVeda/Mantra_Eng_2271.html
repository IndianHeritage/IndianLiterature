सुगो हि वो आर्यमन् मित्र पन्था अनृत्क्षरः वरुण साधुर अस्ति |तेनादित्याः अधि वाचतā नो यच्छतā नो दुष्परिहन्तु शर्म ||
sugo hi vo aryaman mitra panthā anṛkṣaro varuṇa sādhur asti | tenādityā adhi vocatā no yacchatā no duṣparihantu śarma ||
  • Translation: "Indeed, Aryaman, the friend, is good for you; the path is unerring, Varuna is indeed righteous. He who is the sun, has spoken to us; may he grant us protection from whatever is difficult to overcome."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Sugo: Good or auspicious.
    • Hi: Indeed or certainly.
    • Vo: For you.
    • Aryaman: A deity associated with friendship and social order.
    • Mitra: Another deity representing friendship and harmony.
    • Panthā: Path or way.
    • Anṛkṣaro: Unerring or infallible.
    • Varuṇa: A deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority.
    • Sādhur: Righteous or virtuous.
    • Asti: Is or exists.
    • Tenādityā: He who is the sun.
    • Adhi: Above or over.
    • Vocata: Has spoken.
    • No: To us.
    • Yacchatā: May he grant.
    • Duṣparihantu: Difficult to overcome.
    • Śarma: Protection or shelter.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of friendship and support in our lives. For instance, in a workplace, having a supportive colleague can make challenging projects more manageable.
    • The idea of an unerring path can be related to making ethical decisions in business. Companies that adhere to moral principles often gain trust and loyalty from customers.
    • Seeking protection from difficulties can be seen in personal relationships where friends or family provide emotional support during tough times, such as during illness or loss.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for righteousness, friendship, and divine guidance. It teaches us to seek the right path in our actions and to rely on the support of others. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as fostering strong relationships, making ethical choices, and seeking help during challenging times. By embodying these principles, we can create a harmonious and supportive environment in our personal and professional lives.
