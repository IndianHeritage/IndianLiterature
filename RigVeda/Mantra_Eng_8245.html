प्र सोम याहि धारणाय सुत इन्द्राय मत्सरः | दधानो अक्षिति श्रवः ||
pra soma yāhi dhārayā suta indrāya matsaraḥ | dadhāno akṣiti śravaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: forward, towards
    • soma: a sacred drink, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration
    • yāhi: come, approach
    • dhārayā: by holding, by sustaining
    • suta: son, offspring, or something that is produced
    • indrāya: to Indra, the king of gods and god of rain and thunderstorms
    • matsaraḥ: jealousy, envy
    • dadhāno: producing, creating
    • akṣiti: strength, power
    • śravaḥ: fame, glory
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra calls upon the sacred drink, Soma, to come forth and be sustained.
    • It emphasizes the importance of offering this to Indra, who represents strength and power.
    • The mention of "matsaraḥ" suggests a desire to overcome jealousy and foster unity.
    • By invoking Indra, the mantra seeks to channel divine energy and strength for the benefit of all.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be seen as a call for collaboration and support among individuals, encouraging them to work together without jealousy.
    • In a corporate environment, it can symbolize the importance of teamwork and the need to set aside personal rivalries for the greater good of the organization.
    • In personal relationships, it serves as a reminder to foster love and understanding, rather than envy, to build stronger bonds.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and the overcoming of negative emotions like jealousy.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to community and corporate settings, where collaboration and support are essential for success.
    • By invoking the divine energy of Indra and the essence of Soma, individuals can aspire to create a harmonious environment that promotes growth and collective well-being.
