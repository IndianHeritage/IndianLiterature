दश ते कलशानां हिरण्यानाम् अधीमहि |भूरिदा असि वृत्रहन् ||
daśa te kalaśānāṁ hiraṇyānām adhīmahi | bhūridā asi vṛtrahan ||
  • Mantra: "daśa te kalaśānāṁ hiraṇyānām adhīmahi | bhūridā asi vṛtrahan ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • daśa: ten
    • te: of you
    • kalaśānāṁ: of the vessels or pots
    • hiraṇyānām: of gold
    • adhīmahi: we meditate or contemplate
    • bhūridā: you are the giver of abundance
    • asi: you are
    • vṛtrahan: the slayer of Vritra (the demon of drought)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for abundance and prosperity, invoking the divine to bless us with wealth and resources.
    • The mention of "ten vessels of gold" symbolizes the richness and the potential for prosperity that can be achieved through divine grace.
    • By meditating on this mantra, one seeks to connect with the divine energy that brings forth abundance and removes obstacles, represented by Vritra, who symbolizes drought and scarcity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, entrepreneurs may recite this mantra to attract financial success and overcome challenges in their ventures.
    • Individuals seeking personal growth and abundance in their lives can use this mantra as a daily affirmation to cultivate a mindset of prosperity.
    • During festivals or rituals, this mantra can be chanted to invoke blessings for wealth and well-being for families and communities.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire hope and a sense of abundance in the face of challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that through meditation and connection with the divine, one can attract prosperity and overcome obstacles.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various aspects, including personal development, business endeavors, and community blessings, making it a versatile tool for invoking abundance.
