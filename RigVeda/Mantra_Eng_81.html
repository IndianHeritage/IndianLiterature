इन्द्रेहि मच्छ्य अन्धसो विश्वेभिः सोमपर्वभिः |महाम् अभिष्टिर ओजसा ||
indrehi matsy andhaso viśvebhiḥ somaparvabhiḥ | mahām̐ abhiṣṭir ojasā ||
  • Indrehi: This term refers to the divine powers or deities associated with Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature. Indra is often seen as a symbol of strength and power.
  • Matsy: This word translates to "fish," which can symbolize adaptability and the ability to navigate through life's challenges, much like a fish swims through water.
  • Andhaso: This term means "blind" or "darkness," representing ignorance or lack of awareness. It can signify the challenges one faces when they are not enlightened or aware of their surroundings.
  • Viśvebhiḥ: This word means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity and the collective nature of existence. It suggests that the mantra is addressing all beings or aspects of life.
  • Somaparvabhiḥ: This term refers to the "moon" or "soma," which is often associated with nourishment, tranquility, and the cyclical nature of life. It symbolizes the importance of emotional and spiritual sustenance.
  • Mahām̐: This word means "great" or "mighty," emphasizing the significance of the blessings or powers being invoked.
  • Abhiṣṭir: This term translates to "blessing" or "desire," indicating the aspirations or wishes that one seeks to fulfill through divine intervention.
  • Ojasā: This word means "with strength" or "vigor," highlighting the importance of energy and vitality in achieving one's goals.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for divine blessings and strength to overcome ignorance and challenges in life. It emphasizes the need for awareness and adaptability, much like a fish navigating through murky waters. The invocation of Indra and the moon signifies a desire for both physical and spiritual nourishment, suggesting that one should seek balance in all aspects of life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to seek knowledge and awareness, overcoming ignorance in their personal and professional lives.
  • Adaptability: Just as a fish adapts to its environment, people can draw inspiration from this mantra to remain flexible and resilient in the face of challenges.
  • Spiritual Practices: The mantra can be incorporated into meditation or prayer, invoking the blessings of divine powers for strength and guidance.
  • Community and Inclusivity: The reference to "all" encourages individuals to foster a sense of community and support among diverse groups, promoting unity and collective growth.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of divine blessings, awareness, and adaptability in navigating life's complexities. It encourages individuals to seek strength and nourishment from both the physical and spiritual realms, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and enlightened existence.
