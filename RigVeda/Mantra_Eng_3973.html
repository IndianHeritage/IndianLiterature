मन्त्र: प्रतिमे स्तोमम् अदितिः जगृभ्यāt सूनुं न माताऽ हृदयं सुशेवम् |ब्रह्म प्रियं देवहितं यद अस्त्य अहम् मित्रे वरुणे यन् मयोभू ||
prati me stomam aditir jagṛbhyāt sūnuṁ na mātā hṛdyaṁ suśevam | brahma priyaṁ devahitaṁ yad asty aham mitre varuṇe yan mayobhu ||
  • Translation: "O Aditi, may my hymn reach you; may it be pleasing to the heart of my mother. What is dear to Brahman, what is beneficial to the gods, that I am, with Mitra and Varuna, who are my protectors."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Prati me stomam: "May my hymn reach" - This signifies the importance of devotion and the act of offering prayers or hymns to the divine.
    • Aditir: "O Aditi" - Aditi is the mother of the gods, representing the infinite and the nurturing aspect of the universe.
    • Jagṛbhyāt: "May it be received" - This indicates a desire for the divine to accept the offerings made.
    • Sūnuṁ: "Son" - This reflects the relationship between the devotee and the divine, akin to a child seeking blessings from a parent.
    • Na mātā hṛdyaṁ suśevam: "May it be pleasing to the heart of my mother" - This emphasizes the emotional connection and the wish for the divine to be pleased.
    • Brahma priyaṁ: "What is dear to Brahman" - This refers to the ultimate reality or consciousness that is cherished by the divine.
    • Devahitaṁ: "Beneficial to the gods" - This highlights the importance of actions and offerings that are favorable to the divine beings.
    • Yad asty aham: "That I am" - This asserts the identity of the devotee with the divine essence.
    • Mitre varuṇe: "With Mitra and Varuna" - Mitra represents friendship and harmony, while Varuna symbolizes cosmic order and truth.
    • Yan mayobhu: "Who are my protectors" - This indicates the reliance on divine forces for guidance and protection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek blessings from their parents or elders before embarking on new ventures, similar to how the mantra seeks blessings from Aditi.
    • In times of uncertainty, people may turn to spiritual practices, such as prayer or meditation, to connect with the divine and seek guidance, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • Community rituals that honor deities, like festivals, can be seen as collective hymns that aim to please the divine, akin to the personal hymn in the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its expression of devotion, the nurturing aspect of the divine, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It teaches us the importance of seeking blessings and guidance from higher powers, fostering a sense of community and harmony. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate gratitude, respect for the divine, and a sense of responsibility towards others, promoting a balanced and harmonious existence.
