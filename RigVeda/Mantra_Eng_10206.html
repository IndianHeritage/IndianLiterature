बीभत्सूनां सयुजं हंसम् आहुर अपां दिव्यानां सख्ये चरन्तम् |अनुष्टुभम् अनु चरचूर्यमाणम् इन्द्रं नि चिक्युः कवयो मनीषा ||
bībhatsūnāṁ sayujaṁ haṁsam āhur apāṁ divyānāṁ sakhye carantam | anuṣṭubham anu carcūryamāṇam indraṁ ni cikyuḥ kavayo manīṣā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bībhatsūnāṁ: Of those who are fearful or hesitant.
    • sayujaṁ: United or connected.
    • haṁsam: The swan, often symbolizing purity and wisdom.
    • āhur: They say or declare.
    • apāṁ: Of the waters.
    • divyānāṁ: Divine or celestial.
    • sakhye: In friendship.
    • carantam: Moving or wandering.
    • anuṣṭubham: A type of meter in poetry, often associated with completeness.
    • anu: Following or after.
    • carcūryamāṇam: Being discussed or deliberated upon.
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods, representing power and authority.
    • ni: Not or without.
    • cikyuḥ: They have perceived or understood.
    • kavayo: The wise or the poets.
    • manīṣā: Intellect or wisdom.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the connection between the fearful and the wise, suggesting that even those who are hesitant can find unity and wisdom.
    • The swan symbolizes purity and the ability to discern truth, indicating that wisdom can guide those who are uncertain.
    • It emphasizes the importance of friendship and collaboration, especially in the pursuit of knowledge and understanding.
    • Indra, as a powerful figure, represents the strength that comes from wisdom and unity, suggesting that true power lies in collective understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, team members who may feel insecure about their roles can find strength in collaboration, leading to innovative solutions.
    • In educational environments, students who are hesitant to participate can benefit from supportive friendships that encourage them to share their ideas.
    • In community initiatives, individuals with different fears and uncertainties can unite to address common challenges, drawing on each other's strengths.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity, wisdom, and friendship in overcoming fear and hesitation.
    • It teaches that through collaboration and mutual support, individuals can achieve greater understanding and strength.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more cohesive teams, supportive communities, and a deeper appreciation for collective wisdom.
