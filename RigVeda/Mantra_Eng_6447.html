न त्वा रासीयाभिशस्तये वसो न पापत्वाय सन्त्य। न मे स्तोतामतीव न दुरहितः स्याद अग्ने न पापया॥
na tvā rāsīyābhiśastaye vaso na pāpatvāya santya | na me stotāmatīvā na durhitaḥ syād agne na pāpayā ||
  • na tvā rāsīyābhiśastaye vaso: This phrase can be interpreted as "May I not be bound by your wealth or possessions." It suggests a desire for freedom from material attachments. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who prioritize experiences and relationships over accumulating wealth. For example, someone who chooses to travel and explore the world rather than invest in a luxury car embodies this sentiment.
  • na pāpatvāya santya: This translates to "May I not be subjected to sin or wrongdoing." It emphasizes the importance of living a virtuous life. In contemporary society, this can relate to ethical decision-making in business, where a leader chooses integrity over profit, thereby fostering a culture of honesty and accountability.
  • na me stotāmatīvā: This means "May I not be overly praised." It reflects humility and the understanding that excessive praise can lead to arrogance. In real life, this can be observed in leaders who remain grounded despite their achievements, such as a successful entrepreneur who credits their team for their success rather than seeking personal accolades.
  • na durhitaḥ syād agne: This phrase translates to "May I not be in a difficult situation, O Fire." Here, 'Fire' symbolizes transformation and purification. In practical terms, this can relate to individuals seeking guidance during challenging times, such as turning to mentors or spiritual practices to navigate life's difficulties.
  • na pāpayā: This means "May I not be tainted by sin." It reinforces the idea of striving for a life free from moral corruption. In everyday life, this can be seen in community leaders who advocate for social justice and work tirelessly to uplift marginalized voices, ensuring their actions align with ethical principles.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound desire for spiritual and moral integrity, urging individuals to seek freedom from materialism, avoid wrongdoing, maintain humility, and strive for a virtuous life. Its significance lies in its applicability to modern life, where individuals can reflect on their values and actions. By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can cultivate a life of purpose, integrity, and positive impact on society.
