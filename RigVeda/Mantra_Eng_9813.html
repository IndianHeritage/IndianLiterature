सप्ताश्यासन परिधयस् त्रिः सप्त समिधः कृताः |देवा यद यज्ञं तन्वाना अभध्नन पुरुषं पशुम् ||
saptāsyāsan paridhayas triḥ sapta samidhaḥ kṛtāḥ | devā yad yajñaṁ tanvānā abadhnan puruṣam paśum ||
  • Mantra: "saptāsyāsan paridhayas triḥ sapta samidhaḥ kṛtāḥ | devā yad yajñaṁ tanvānā abadhnan puruṣam paśum ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • saptāsyāsan: Seven (saptā) are the seats (āsan) or positions.
    • paridhayas: Surrounding or encompassing.
    • triḥ: Three times or thrice.
    • sapta: Seven.
    • samidhaḥ: Fuel or offerings (specifically wood for the fire).
    • kṛtāḥ: Created or made.
    • devā: The gods or divine beings.
    • yad: Whatever or that which.
    • yajñaṁ: Sacrifice or ritual offering.
    • tanvānā: Having taken form or manifested.
    • abadhnan: They bound or tied.
    • puruṣam: The person or being (often referring to the cosmic being).
    • paśum: The animal or sacrifice (often referring to the sacrificial animal).
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a ritualistic context where seven seats are prepared, symbolizing the importance of community and collective participation in spiritual practices.
    • The mention of "three times" indicates the cyclical nature of rituals, emphasizing repetition and consistency in spiritual endeavors.
    • The "seven fuels" represent the offerings made to the divine, which can be seen as a metaphor for the various efforts and resources we invest in our spiritual and personal growth.
    • The gods are depicted as active participants in the yajña (sacrifice), suggesting that divine forces are engaged in our efforts when we perform our duties with sincerity.
    • The binding of the "puruṣam" (cosmic being) and "paśum" (sacrificial animal) can be interpreted as the connection between the material and spiritual realms, highlighting the importance of sacrifice in achieving higher consciousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of "sacrifice" can be seen in the dedication of individuals who work hard for their families or communities, often putting aside personal desires for the greater good.
    • Community gatherings, such as festivals or religious ceremonies, reflect the idea of coming together (the seven seats) to celebrate and honor shared beliefs.
    • The practice of mindfulness and meditation can be viewed as a form of yajña, where individuals offer their thoughts and distractions to achieve inner peace and connection with the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of community, sacrifice, and the divine in our lives. It teaches us that our efforts, when aligned with a higher purpose, can lead to spiritual growth and fulfillment.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles of this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, personal development, and spiritual practices, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of our contributions to the collective.
