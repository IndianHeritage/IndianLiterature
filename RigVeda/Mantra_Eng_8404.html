पर्जन्यः पिता महिषस्य पर्णिनो नाभा पृथिव्या गिरिषु क्षयम् दधे |स्वसारा आपो अभि गा उतासरण सं ग्रावभिर नासते वीते अध्वरे ||
parjanyaḥ pitā mahiṣasya parṇino nābhā pṛthivyā giriṣu kṣayaṁ dadhe | svasāra āpo abhi gā utāsaran saṁ grāvabhir nasate vīte adhvare ||
  • Mantra: "parjanyaḥ pitā mahiṣasya parṇino nābhā pṛthivyā giriṣu kṣayaṁ dadhe | svasāra āpo abhi gā utāsaran saṁ grāvabhir nasate vīte adhvare ||"
  • Translation: This mantra speaks of Parjanya, the rain god, as the father of the earth and the source of nourishment. It emphasizes the connection between the heavens and the earth, highlighting the importance of rain for life and prosperity.
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Parjanyaḥ: Refers to the rain god, symbolizing fertility and abundance.
    • Pitā: Means 'father', indicating a nurturing and protective role.
    • Mahiṣasya: Relates to the earth or the great mother, symbolizing sustenance.
    • Parṇino: Refers to the leaves or vegetation, representing the bounty of nature.
    • Nābhā: Means 'navel' or 'center', indicating the core of the earth.
    • Pṛthivyā: Refers to the earth itself, emphasizing its importance in the cycle of life.
    • Giriṣu: Means 'in the mountains', highlighting the geographical aspect of rain and its sources.
    • Kṣayaṁ: Refers to 'destruction' or 'loss', indicating the consequences of lack of rain.
    • Dadhe: Means 'to give', emphasizing the act of providing sustenance.
    • Svasāra: Refers to 'sisters' or 'water bodies', indicating the interconnectedness of water sources.
    • Āpo: Means 'waters', symbolizing the essence of life.
    • Abhi: Means 'towards', indicating direction or purpose.
    • Gā: Refers to 'song' or 'praise', indicating reverence for nature.
    • Utāsaran: Means 'to rise' or 'to ascend', symbolizing growth and prosperity.
    • Saṁ: Means 'together', indicating unity in purpose.
    • Grāvabhir: Refers to 'communities' or 'groups', emphasizing collective effort.
    • Nasate: Means 'to nourish', indicating the role of rain in sustaining life.
    • Vīte: Refers to 'wealth' or 'prosperity', indicating the outcome of abundance.
    • Adhvare: Means 'sacrifice' or 'ritual', indicating the spiritual aspect of nature's bounty.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The importance of rain in agriculture: Farmers depend on timely rainfall for their crops to thrive, which directly impacts food supply and economic stability.
    • Water conservation efforts: Communities come together to manage water resources, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on collective action for sustenance.
    • Environmental rituals: Many cultures perform rituals to honor nature and seek blessings for rain, showcasing the spiritual connection to the earth and its resources.
  • Conclusion: This mantra highlights the vital relationship between the rain, the earth, and all living beings. It serves as a reminder of our dependence on nature for sustenance and prosperity. In real life, it encourages us to respect and protect our environment, engage in sustainable practices, and recognize the interconnectedness of all life forms. By acknowledging the significance of rain and water, we can foster a deeper appreciation for the natural world and work towards a harmonious existence with it.
