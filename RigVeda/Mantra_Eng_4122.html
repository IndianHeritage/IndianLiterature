तं नाकम् आर्यो अगृभीतशोचिषं रुशत पिप्पलम् मरुतो वि धूनुथा |सम आच्यन्त वृजनातित्विषन्त यत स्वरन्ति घोषं विततम ऋतायवः ||
taṁ nākam aryo agṛbhītaśociṣaṁ ruśat pippalam maruto vi dhūnutha | sam acyanta vṛjanātitviṣanta yat svaranti ghoṣaṁ vitatam ṛtāyavaḥ ||
  • taṁ nākam: This phrase can be translated as "that sky" or "that heaven." In a broader sense, it refers to the higher realms of existence or consciousness.
  • aryo: This term signifies the noble or the righteous. It emphasizes the importance of virtue and moral integrity in one's life.
  • agṛbhītaśociṣaṁ: This can be interpreted as "having not been grasped" or "unattainable brightness." It suggests the idea of enlightenment or knowledge that is beyond ordinary reach.
  • ruśat: This word means "to shine" or "to illuminate." It indicates the presence of light, knowledge, or wisdom.
  • pippalam: Referring to the "pepper" or "pippali," it symbolizes the essence of life and vitality, often associated with health and well-being.
  • maruto vi dhūnutha: This phrase translates to "O Maruts, shake or stir." The Maruts are storm deities, representing the forces of nature and the dynamic aspects of life.
  • sam acyanta: This means "together they have come" or "they have united." It signifies harmony and collective effort.
  • vṛjanātitviṣanta: This can be interpreted as "those who are beyond the reach of darkness." It suggests a state of enlightenment or liberation from ignorance.
  • yat svaranti ghoṣaṁ: This translates to "that which resonates with sound." It indicates the power of sound, speech, or communication in manifesting reality.
  • vitatam ṛtāyavaḥ: This phrase means "those who spread the truth." It emphasizes the importance of truthfulness and righteousness in life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates profound themes of enlightenment, virtue, and the interconnectedness of life. It encourages individuals to seek higher knowledge and to embody righteousness in their actions. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, ethical decision-making, and community building. By striving for truth and wisdom, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and enlightened society. The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of collective effort in achieving a higher state of consciousness and well-being.
