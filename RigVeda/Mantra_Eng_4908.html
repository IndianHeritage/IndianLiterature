मां व एनो अन्यकृतं भुजेम मां तत कर्म वसवो यच चयध्वे |विश्वस्य हि क्षयथ विश्वदेवाः स्वयं रिपुस तंवं रीरीषिष्ट ||
mā va eno anyakṛtam bhujema mā tat karma vasavo yac cayadhve | viśvasya hi kṣayatha viśvadevāḥ svayaṁ ripus tanvaṁ rīriṣīṣṭa ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mā: not
    • va: indeed
    • eno: this
    • anyakṛtam: other actions
    • bhujema: may we enjoy
    • mā: not
    • tat: that
    • karma: action
    • vasavo: the divine beings
    • yac: which
    • cayadhve: you may desire
    • viśvasya: of the universe
    • hi: indeed
    • kṣayatha: may you perish
    • viśvadevāḥ: the universal gods
    • svayaṁ: themselves
    • ripus: enemies
    • tanvaṁ: body
    • rīriṣīṣṭa: may they be pleased
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for protection and the desire to avoid negative actions that could lead to harm.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine beings in guiding human actions and ensuring that we do not engage in harmful deeds.
    • The phrase "may we enjoy" suggests a longing for positive experiences and blessings in life, while "may you perish" indicates a wish for the downfall of negativity or adversaries.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied when seeking harmony among colleagues. By focusing on positive actions and avoiding harmful competition, a more productive environment can be fostered.
    • In personal relationships, it serves as a reminder to avoid actions that could hurt others, promoting understanding and compassion instead.
    • During challenging times, such as conflicts or disputes, invoking this mantra can help individuals seek divine guidance to overcome adversities and maintain peace.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine protection and the pursuit of positive actions.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, encouraging individuals to reflect on their actions and strive for harmony in their interactions.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that seeking the blessings of the divine can lead to a more fulfilling and peaceful life.
