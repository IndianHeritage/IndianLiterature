दूरं किल प्रथमा जग्मुर आसाम इन्द्रस्य याः प्रसवे सस्रुर आपः |क्व स्विद अग्रं क्व बुद्धना आसाम आपो मध्यं क्व वो नूनम अन्तः ||
dūraṁ kila prathamā jagmur āsām indrasya yāḥ prasave sasrur āpaḥ | kva svid agraṁ kva budhna āsām āpo madhyaṁ kva vo nūnam antaḥ ||
  • Translation: "Indeed, they (the waters) have gone far away; they are the first to flow from Indra's birth. Where is the beginning, where is the end of these waters? Where, indeed, is their middle?"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • dūraṁ: far away, distant
    • kila: indeed, certainly
    • prathamā: first, foremost
    • jagmur: they have gone, they have flowed
    • āsām: of these, of them
    • indrasya: of Indra, the god of rain and thunder
    • yāḥ: who, which
    • prasave: from the birth, from the creation
    • sasrur: they have flowed, they have poured
    • āpaḥ: waters, rivers
    • kva: where
    • svid: indeed, where
    • agraṁ: beginning, source
    • kva: where
    • budhna: end, boundary
    • āsām: of these, of them
    • āpo: waters
    • madhyaṁ: middle, center
    • kva: where
    • vo: your, of you
    • nūnam: indeed, certainly
    • antaḥ: end, conclusion
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra reflects the profound relationship between nature and divinity, particularly focusing on water as a life-giving force.
    • It emphasizes the mystery of water's journey, from its source to its end, symbolizing the cycle of life and the interconnectedness of all elements in nature.
    • In real life, this can be related to the importance of water conservation and the understanding of water cycles in environmental science.
    • For instance, rivers that flow from mountains to oceans represent the journey of water, which is essential for agriculture, drinking, and sustaining ecosystems.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the sacredness of water and its vital role in sustaining life.
    • It encourages us to respect and protect our natural resources, recognizing that every drop of water has a journey and a purpose.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can inspire initiatives for sustainable water management and environmental stewardship.
