अग्निः स्तोमं मनामहे सिद्ध्रम अद्य दिविष्पृशः |देवस्य द्रविणस्यवः ||
agneḥ stomam manāmahe sidhram adya divispṛśaḥ | devasya draviṇasyavaḥ ||
  • Agneḥ: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Agni symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine presence in the form of fire.
  • Stomam: This term means praise or hymn. It signifies the act of offering reverence and acknowledgment to the divine.
  • Manāmahe: This translates to "we meditate" or "we contemplate." It indicates a deep, focused thought or reflection on the divine.
  • Sidhram: This word means "auspicious" or "beneficial." It suggests that the focus of the mantra is on attracting positive energies and blessings.
  • Adya: This means "today" or "now." It emphasizes the immediacy of the invocation, suggesting that the time for action and connection is present.
  • Divispṛśaḥ: This term means "touching the heavens" or "reaching for the divine." It signifies the aspiration to connect with higher realms of existence.
  • Devasya: This word means "of the divine" or "of the god." It refers to the higher powers and spiritual entities that govern the universe.
  • Draviṇasyavaḥ: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." It indicates a desire for abundance and the blessings of the divine in material and spiritual forms.

The mantra "agneḥ stomam manāmahe sidhram adya divispṛśaḥ | devasya draviṇasyavaḥ ||" can be understood as a heartfelt invocation to Agni, the fire god, expressing a desire for auspiciousness and divine blessings in the present moment. It emphasizes the importance of meditation and contemplation on the divine to attract positive energies and prosperity.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use this mantra during meditation to focus on their goals and aspirations, inviting positive energy into their lives.
  • Rituals and Ceremonies: It can be recited during rituals to invoke blessings for success and abundance, such as during housewarming ceremonies or new ventures.
  • Community Gatherings: The mantra can be used in community events to foster unity and collective prosperity, encouraging participants to reflect on shared goals.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the connection between the divine and our aspirations. By invoking Agni and focusing on auspiciousness, individuals can align themselves with positive energies, enhancing their personal and communal lives. Its significance lies in the ability to transform thoughts into reality through meditation and reverence, making it applicable in various aspects of life, from personal development to community well-being.
