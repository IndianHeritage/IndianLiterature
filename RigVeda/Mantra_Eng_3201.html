अनायतो अनिबद्धः कथायं न्यङ्ग उत्तानोऽव पद्यते न |कया याति स्वधया को ददर्श दिवः स्कम्भः समृतः पाति नाकम् ||
anāyato anibaddhaḥ kathāyaṁ nyaṅṅ uttāno 'va padyate na | kayā yāti svadhayā ko dadarśa divaḥ skambhaḥ samṛtaḥ pāti nākam ||
  • anāyato: This word can be interpreted as "without attachment" or "unbound." It suggests a state of freedom from worldly ties.
  • anibaddhaḥ: Meaning "unbound" or "not tied down," it emphasizes the idea of liberation and the ability to move freely.
  • kathāyaṁ: This translates to "in the story" or "in the narrative," indicating the importance of storytelling or the experiences we share.
  • nyaṅṅ: This word can be understood as "that" or "this," referring to the subject of the mantra.
  • uttāno: Meaning "elevated" or "uplifted," it signifies a rise in consciousness or spiritual elevation.
  • 'va: This is a conjunction meaning "or," suggesting alternatives or choices in life.
  • padyate: This translates to "is placed" or "is situated," indicating a state of being or existence.
  • na: Meaning "not," it negates the following statement, emphasizing what is absent or unattainable.
  • kayā: This word means "by what" or "with what," prompting inquiry into the means or methods of action.
  • yāti: Translated as "goes" or "moves," it indicates the act of progression or journey.
  • svadhayā: Meaning "by one's own nature" or "by one's own will," it emphasizes self-determination and personal agency.
  • ko: This translates to "who," inviting contemplation on identity and existence.
  • dadarśa: Meaning "saw" or "perceived," it indicates awareness or realization.
  • divaḥ: This word means "of the sky" or "heavenly," suggesting a connection to the divine or higher realms.
  • skambhaḥ: Translated as "pillar" or "support," it symbolizes stability and strength.
  • samṛtaḥ: Meaning "remembered" or "recalled," it emphasizes the importance of memory and legacy.
  • pāti: This translates to "protects" or "sustains," indicating a nurturing force.
  • nākam: Meaning "the sky" or "heaven," it represents the ultimate goal or aspiration.

The mantra speaks to the themes of liberation, self-awareness, and the pursuit of higher consciousness. It suggests that by freeing ourselves from attachments and recognizing our own nature, we can elevate our existence and connect with the divine. The imagery of the sky and pillars evokes a sense of stability and aspiration towards something greater.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can strive to let go of limiting beliefs and attachments that hinder their growth.
  • In relationships, fostering a sense of independence while maintaining connections can lead to healthier dynamics.
  • In spiritual practices, meditation and self-reflection can help individuals recognize their true nature and purpose.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of self-liberation and the pursuit of higher ideals. It encourages individuals to seek their own path, free from external constraints, and to recognize the divine support that sustains them on their journey towards enlightenment.
