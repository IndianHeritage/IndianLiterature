असुनीते पुनर asmāsu चक्षुः पुनः प्राणम इह नो धेहि भोगम् |ज्योक पश्येम सूर्यं उच्चरन्तम् अनुमते मृळयाऽनः स्वस्ति ||
asunīte punar asmāsu cakṣuḥ punaḥ prāṇam iha no dhehi bhogam | jyok paśyema sūryam uccarantam anumate mṛḻayā naḥ svasti ||
  • asunīte: This term can be interpreted as "may we be purified" or "may we be cleansed." It signifies the importance of mental and spiritual purity in our lives.
  • punar: Meaning "again" or "once more," this word emphasizes the idea of renewal and the cyclical nature of life.
  • asmāsu: This translates to "in us" or "within us," indicating that the focus is on the inner self and personal growth.
  • cakṣuḥ: This means "eyes" or "sight," symbolizing perception and awareness. It suggests the need for clarity in understanding our surroundings.
  • punaḥ: Again meaning "again," reinforcing the theme of renewal and the importance of revisiting our perspectives.
  • prāṇam: This term refers to "life force" or "breath," highlighting the significance of vitality and energy in our existence.
  • iha: Meaning "here" or "in this world," it emphasizes the importance of our current existence and experiences.
  • no: This translates to "our," indicating a collective aspect, suggesting that the mantra is not just for the individual but for the community.
  • dhehi: Meaning "bestow" or "grant," it expresses a plea for blessings or gifts from the divine.
  • bhogam: This term means "enjoyment" or "experience," indicating the desire for a fulfilling life filled with joy and abundance.
  • jyok: This translates to "light" or "radiance," symbolizing knowledge, enlightenment, and positivity.
  • paśyema: Meaning "may we see," it expresses a wish for vision and insight, both literally and metaphorically.
  • sūryam: This refers to the "sun," a symbol of life, energy, and illumination, representing the source of all vitality.
  • uccarantam: Meaning "rising" or "ascending," it signifies growth, progress, and the pursuit of higher ideals.
  • anumate: This translates to "with consent" or "with approval," indicating the importance of alignment with divine will.
  • mṛḻayā: This term means "with gentleness" or "with kindness," emphasizing the need for compassion in our actions.
  • naḥ: Meaning "our," reinforcing the collective aspect of the mantra.
  • svasti: This translates to "well-being" or "prosperity," signifying the ultimate goal of health, happiness, and success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound plea for purification, renewal, and the attainment of clarity and vitality in life. It emphasizes the importance of community and collective well-being, urging individuals to seek enlightenment and joy while being aligned with a higher purpose. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during meditation, yoga practices, or community gatherings, where individuals come together to seek personal and collective growth. It serves as a reminder to focus on inner purity, the pursuit of knowledge, and the importance of compassion in our interactions, ultimately leading to a harmonious and prosperous life.
