आयं गौः पृष्णिर अक्रमीद असदन मातरं पुरः |पितरं च प्रयाण स्वः ||
āyaṁ gauḥ pṛśnir akramīd asadan mātaram puraḥ | pitaraṁ ca prayan svaḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • āyaṁ: This word can be interpreted as "this" or "here." It signifies presence and immediacy.
    • gauḥ: Translates to "cow," which is a symbol of wealth, nourishment, and motherly care in Vedic literature.
    • pṛśnir: Refers to "the one who is strong" or "the powerful." It denotes strength and resilience.
    • akramīd: Means "I have not transgressed" or "I have not crossed." It implies adherence to dharma (righteousness).
    • asadan: Translates to "the abode" or "the dwelling." It signifies a place of stability and security.
    • mātaram: Means "mother," representing nurturing, care, and the source of life.
    • puraḥ: Translates to "in front" or "before," indicating direction or guidance.
    • pitaraṁ: Refers to "father," symbolizing authority, protection, and guidance.
    • ca: Means "and," connecting the ideas of mother and father.
    • prayan: Translates to "journey" or "path," indicating movement or progression.
    • svaḥ: Refers to "heaven" or "the celestial realm," symbolizing higher consciousness or spiritual elevation.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of life, emphasizing the roles of both mother and father in guiding and nurturing individuals.
    • The cow, as a symbol of wealth and nourishment, represents the sustenance provided by nature and the importance of respecting and protecting it.
    • The phrase "I have not transgressed" suggests a commitment to living a life of integrity and adherence to moral principles.
    • The mention of "journey" signifies the ongoing path of life, where guidance from parental figures is crucial for navigating challenges.
    • Overall, the mantra encapsulates the essence of familial bonds, respect for nature, and the pursuit of righteousness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the cow is revered and protected, reflecting the idea of nurturing and respect for life. For instance, in India, cows are often seen as sacred and are treated with great care.
    • Parents play a vital role in shaping the values and ethics of their children, guiding them through life's challenges, much like the guidance mentioned in the mantra.
    • Individuals who adhere to their moral principles and live a life of integrity often find themselves respected and trusted in their communities, illustrating the significance of "not transgressing."
    • The journey of life is filled with various paths, and having strong familial support can make a significant difference in overcoming obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of familial relationships, the respect for nature, and the commitment to living a righteous life.
    • It serves as a reminder to honor our parents and the nurturing aspects of life while also being mindful of our actions and their impact on the world around us.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate strong family bonds, respect for the environment, and a commitment to ethical living.
