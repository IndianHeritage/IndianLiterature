इन्द्र ब्रह्म क्रियमानā जुषस्व यā ते शविष्ठ नव्यā अकर्म |वस्त्रेवा भद्रā सुखृतā वसūयū रथं न धीरः स्वपā अतक्षम् ||
indra brahma kriyamāṇā juṣasva yā te śaviṣṭha navyā akarma | vastreva bhadrā sukṛtā vasūyū rathaṁ na dhīraḥ svapā atakṣam ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
  • Brahma: Represents the creative aspect of the divine, often associated with knowledge and wisdom.
  • Kriyamāṇā: Means "being performed" or "being done," indicating action or activity.
  • Juṣasva: Translates to "may you enjoy" or "may you take pleasure in," suggesting a call for blessings and enjoyment.
  • Yā te: Means "that which is yours," indicating possession or belonging.
  • Śaviṣṭha: Refers to the "most powerful" or "the best," emphasizing excellence.
  • Navyā: Means "new" or "fresh," indicating innovation or renewal.
  • Akarma: Refers to "actionless" or "without action," suggesting a state of being or existence beyond mere activity.
  • Vastreva: Means "like a garment," symbolizing protection or covering.
  • Bhadrā: Translates to "auspicious" or "good," indicating positivity and well-being.
  • Sukṛtā: Refers to "good deeds" or "virtuous actions," emphasizing moral integrity.
  • Vasūyū: Means "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating abundance.
  • Rathaṁ: Refers to a "chariot," symbolizing movement, progress, and the journey of life.
  • Na dhīraḥ: Means "not the wise" or "not the courageous," indicating a contrast to those who are strong and wise.
  • Svapā: Refers to "dream" or "vision," indicating aspirations or goals.
  • Atakṣam: Means "not to be restrained" or "unhindered," suggesting freedom and the ability to act without limitations.

The mantra essentially invokes the blessings of Indra and Brahma, encouraging the pursuit of virtuous actions and the enjoyment of life's blessings. It emphasizes the importance of strength, wisdom, and the ability to innovate while remaining grounded in good deeds and auspiciousness.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra represents strength and leadership, individuals in positions of authority can draw inspiration from this mantra to lead with wisdom and integrity.
  • Creativity: The reference to Brahma encourages individuals to embrace creativity and innovation in their personal and professional lives.
  • Personal Growth: The mantra's emphasis on good deeds and auspiciousness serves as a reminder to engage in actions that contribute positively to society and oneself.
  • Resilience: The idea of being unhindered encourages individuals to overcome obstacles and pursue their dreams without fear of failure.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the values of strength, wisdom, and virtuous action. It encourages individuals to seek blessings in their endeavors while remaining committed to good deeds and personal growth. By embodying these principles, one can navigate life's challenges with courage and purpose.
