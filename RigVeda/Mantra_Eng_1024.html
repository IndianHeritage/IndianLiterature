उषो अद्येह गोमती अश्वावति विभावरि |रेवद अस्मे व्य उच्छ सूनृतावति ||
uṣo adyeha gomaty aśvāvati vibhāvari | revad asme vy uccha sūnṛtāvati ||
  • uṣo: This word refers to the dawn or the early morning light. In many cultures, dawn symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the dispelling of darkness.
  • adyeha: This translates to "today" or "in this moment," emphasizing the importance of the present time.
  • gomaty: This term is associated with cows, which are often seen as symbols of wealth and nourishment in Vedic culture. Cows represent abundance and prosperity.
  • aśvāvati: This word relates to horses, which symbolize strength, speed, and vitality. In ancient times, horses were crucial for transportation and warfare.
  • vibhāvari: This term means "illuminating" or "radiant," suggesting the light that dawn brings, which can be interpreted as knowledge and enlightenment.
  • revad: This word can be understood as "to bring" or "to lead," indicating a sense of guidance or direction.
  • asme: This translates to "to us," indicating a collective or communal aspect.
  • vy: This is a prefix that can denote emphasis or intensity.
  • uccha: This means "high" or "elevated," suggesting aspirations or goals that are lofty.
  • sūnṛtāvati: This term can be interpreted as "truthful" or "righteous," emphasizing the importance of honesty and integrity in one's actions.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for the dawn to bring prosperity, strength, and enlightenment to the community. It highlights the significance of the present moment and the blessings that come with the new day. The imagery of cows and horses symbolizes abundance and vitality, suggesting that these qualities are essential for a thriving life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Starting a new venture or project: Just as dawn signifies a new beginning, individuals can invoke this mantra to seek prosperity and strength in their endeavors.
  • Community gatherings: The collective aspect of the mantra can be used in community prayers or rituals to foster unity and shared goals.
  • Personal growth: The emphasis on truthfulness and righteousness can serve as a guiding principle for individuals striving for personal development and ethical living.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of new beginnings, community, and the pursuit of truth and prosperity. It encourages individuals to embrace the present moment and seek enlightenment and strength in their lives, making it relevant in various aspects of daily living.
