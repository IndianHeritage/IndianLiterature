इति स्तुतासो असथा ऋशादसो ये स्थ त्रयश च त्रिंशच च |मनोर देवाः यज्ञियासः ||
iti stutāso asathā riśādaso ye stha trayaś ca triṁśac ca | manor devā yajñiyāsaḥ ||
  • iti stutāso: This phrase translates to "thus praised" or "therefore, those who are praised." It indicates the acknowledgment and reverence given to divine entities or forces.
  • asathā: This word means "not false" or "true." It emphasizes the importance of sincerity in devotion and the authenticity of the praise offered.
  • riśādaso: This term can be interpreted as "those who are in distress" or "those who are suffering." It highlights the need for divine assistance and the role of the divine in alleviating human suffering.
  • ye stha: This translates to "those who stand" or "those who are steadfast." It refers to individuals who remain firm in their beliefs and practices, regardless of challenges.
  • trayaś ca triṁśac ca: This phrase means "three and thirty." It can symbolize completeness or a multitude of divine forces or aspects that are being invoked.
  • manor devā: This translates to "the gods of the mind" or "the divine beings of thought." It signifies the connection between human consciousness and divine inspiration.
  • yajñiyāsaḥ: This term refers to "those who are engaged in sacrifice" or "those who perform rituals." It underscores the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of sincere praise and devotion to the divine, especially in times of distress. It highlights the importance of steadfastness in faith and the role of rituals in fostering a connection with the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of personal crisis or when seeking guidance. It encourages individuals to remain true to their beliefs, engage in acts of devotion, and seek divine support through sincere offerings and rituals. This approach can lead to inner peace, resilience, and a deeper understanding of one's purpose in life.
