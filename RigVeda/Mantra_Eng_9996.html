इन्द्रस्य वृष्णो वरुणस्य राज्ञ आदित्यनाम मरुतां शर्ध उग्रं |महामनसाम bhuvanacyavānां घोषो देवानां जयतामुद अस्तात् ||
indrasya vṛṣṇo varuṇasya rājña ādityānām marutāṁ śardha ugram | mahāmanasām bhuvanacyavānāṁ ghoṣo devānāṁ jayatām ud asthāt ||
  • Indrasya: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing power and authority.
  • Vṛṣṇo: Pertains to the Vrishni clan, often associated with Lord Krishna, representing strength and valor.
  • Varuṇasya: Refers to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water, symbolizing the moral and natural laws of the universe.
  • Rājña: Means 'of kings' or 'royalty', indicating leadership and governance.
  • Ādityānām: Refers to the Adityas, a group of solar deities, representing light, truth, and righteousness.
  • Marutāṁ: Refers to the Maruts, storm deities associated with wind and storms, symbolizing power and dynamism.
  • Śardha: Means 'together' or 'in unity', emphasizing the importance of collaboration.
  • Ugram: Translates to 'fierce' or 'powerful', indicating strength and intensity.
  • Mahāmanasām: Refers to those with great intellect or wisdom, highlighting the importance of knowledge.
  • Bhuvanacyavānāṁ: Means 'of those who move through the worlds', indicating the vastness of existence and the divine influence in it.
  • Ghoṣo: Refers to 'the sound' or 'the call', symbolizing communication and the spread of divine messages.
  • Devānāṁ: Means 'of the gods', indicating the divine nature of the subjects discussed.
  • Jayatām: Translates to 'of those who are victorious', emphasizing success and triumph.
  • Ud asthāt: Means 'may rise' or 'may ascend', indicating growth and elevation.

This mantra invokes the powers of various deities, emphasizing the importance of unity, strength, and wisdom in achieving success. It highlights the interconnectedness of divine forces and their influence on the world. The mention of Indra, Varuna, and the Adityas signifies the need for leadership, moral order, and righteousness in governance and life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Leadership: Leaders can draw inspiration from the qualities of Indra and Varuna, embodying strength and moral integrity in their decision-making.
  • Collaboration: The emphasis on unity (śardha) encourages teamwork and collective efforts in achieving common goals.
  • Wisdom in Action: The call for great intellect (mahāmanasām) reminds individuals to seek knowledge and apply it wisely in their endeavors.
  • Overcoming Challenges: The fierce nature (ugram) of the mantra serves as a reminder to face challenges with courage and determination.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the divine qualities that can guide individuals and communities towards success, emphasizing the importance of strength, unity, wisdom, and moral integrity in all aspects of life.
