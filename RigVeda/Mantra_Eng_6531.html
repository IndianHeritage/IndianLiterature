अग्नि तव त्ये अजरेन्द्रधानासो बृहद्भा: | अश्वा इव वृषणस तविषीयव: ||
agne tava tye ajarendhānāso bṛhad bhāḥ | aśvā iva vṛṣaṇas taviṣīyavaḥ ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • Tava: Meaning "your," it indicates possession or connection to Agni.
  • Tye: This translates to "in you" or "within you," suggesting a deep relationship with Agni.
  • Ajarendhānāso: This term can be broken down into "ajar" (immortal) and "endhānāso" (those who are kindled). It implies the eternal nature of the divine fire that is always present and active.
  • Bṛhad: Meaning "great" or "vast," it signifies the expansive and powerful nature of Agni.
  • Bhāḥ: This word means "light" or "radiance," emphasizing the illuminating aspect of fire.
  • Aśvā: Referring to "horses," it symbolizes strength, speed, and vitality, often associated with the sun and energy.
  • Iva: Meaning "like" or "as," it draws a comparison.
  • Vṛṣaṇas: This term refers to "powerful" or "strong," indicating the might of the horses and, by extension, the energy of Agni.
  • Taviṣīyavaḥ: This can be interpreted as "those who are powerful" or "those who are capable," suggesting the strength derived from Agni.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation to Agni, recognizing his eternal and powerful nature. It highlights the connection between the divine fire and the strength it imparts to those who revere it. The imagery of horses suggests speed and vitality, indicating that the energy of Agni can propel individuals forward in their endeavors.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Just as Agni represents transformation, individuals can invoke this energy to foster personal development and resilience in the face of challenges.
  • Leadership: The qualities of strength and illumination can inspire leaders to guide others with clarity and purpose, much like the guiding light of fire.
  • Rituals and Ceremonies: In Hindu rituals, Agni is often invoked for blessings, protection, and purification, emphasizing the importance of fire in spiritual practices.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with the divine energy of Agni, promoting strength, clarity, and transformation. By recognizing the power of Agni in our lives, we can harness its qualities to navigate challenges, inspire others, and engage in meaningful spiritual practices.
