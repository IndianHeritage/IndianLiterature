इन्द्र नेदीय एद इहि मितमेधाभिर ऊतिभिः |आ शान्तम शान्तमाभिर अभिष्टिभिर आ स्वापे स्वापिभिः ||
indra nedīya ed ihi mitamedhābhir ūtibhiḥ | ā śaṁtama śaṁtamābhir abhiṣṭibhir ā svāpe svāpibhiḥ ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Nedīya: This term can be interpreted as "to lead" or "to guide," indicating a call for direction and support.
  • Ed: Translates to "come" or "arrive," suggesting an invitation or a plea for presence.
  • Mitamedhābhir: This word can be broken down into "Mita" (measured) and "Medhā" (intellect or wisdom), implying a request for wisdom that is balanced and thoughtful.
  • Ūtibhiḥ: Refers to "strengths" or "powers," indicating the various forms of support and energy that can be drawn upon.
  • Ā: A prefix that can mean "towards" or "in the direction of," suggesting a movement or intention.
  • Śaṁtama: Means "peaceful" or "calm," emphasizing the importance of tranquility in one's life.
  • Śaṁtamābhir: This can be interpreted as "the most peaceful," reinforcing the desire for ultimate serenity.
  • Abhiṣṭibhir: Refers to "blessings" or "auspiciousness," indicating a call for divine favor and positive outcomes.
  • Ā: Again, a prefix indicating direction or intention.
  • Svāpe: Translates to "in sleep," suggesting a state of rest or unconsciousness.
  • Svāpibhiḥ: Refers to "dreams" or "visions," indicating the importance of aspirations and the subconscious mind.

This mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation to Indra, seeking guidance, wisdom, and strength. It emphasizes the need for peace and auspiciousness in one's life, particularly in times of uncertainty or challenge. The call for "measured wisdom" suggests that one should approach life with a balanced perspective, making decisions that are thoughtful and considerate.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra is a leader among the gods, individuals in positions of authority can invoke this mantra to seek guidance and strength in their decision-making processes.
  • Conflict Resolution: The emphasis on peace and calm can be particularly relevant in resolving disputes, whether in personal relationships or professional settings.
  • Personal Growth: The call for wisdom and measured intellect can inspire individuals to pursue knowledge and self-improvement, leading to a more fulfilling life.
  • Dreams and Aspirations: The reference to sleep and dreams highlights the importance of aspirations and the subconscious mind, encouraging individuals to pursue their goals and visions.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of seeking guidance, wisdom, and peace in our lives. It encourages us to approach challenges with a balanced mindset and to remain open to the blessings that come our way. By embodying these principles, we can navigate life's complexities with grace and purpose.
