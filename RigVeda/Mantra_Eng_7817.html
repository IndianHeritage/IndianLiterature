निरिणानो विघावति जहच्छर्याणि तान्वा |अत्रा सं जिघ्नते युजा ||
niriṇāno vi dhāvati jahac charyāṇi tānvā | atrā saṁ jighnate yujā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • niriṇāno: One who is free from desires or attachments.
    • vi: In this context, it can mean 'in a special way' or 'distinctly.'
    • dhāvati: Runs or moves swiftly.
    • jahac: To overcome or to conquer.
    • charyāṇi: Actions or deeds.
    • tānvā: Those who are engaged in such actions.
    • atrā: Here or in this context.
    • saṁ: Together or in unity.
    • jighnate: To strive or to aim.
    • yujā: To unite or to connect.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about individuals who are free from worldly desires and attachments.
    • It emphasizes the importance of overcoming obstacles through dedicated actions.
    • The phrase suggests that when individuals unite in their efforts, they can achieve their goals more effectively.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team that works together without personal agendas can achieve greater success than individuals working in isolation.
    • In community service, volunteers who come together to serve a common cause can create a significant impact, demonstrating the power of unity.
    • In personal development, individuals who let go of their attachments to material possessions often find greater peace and fulfillment in life.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and selflessness in actions.
    • It teaches that by overcoming personal desires and working together, individuals can achieve greater goals.
    • This principle is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, professional environments, and community initiatives.
