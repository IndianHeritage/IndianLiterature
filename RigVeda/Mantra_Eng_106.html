पुरां भिन्दुर युवा कविर अमितौजा अजायता | इन्द्रो विश्वस्य कर्मणो धार्ता वज्री पुरुष्टुतः ||
purām bhindur yuvā kavir amitaujā ajāyata | indro viśvasya karmaṇo dhartā vajrī puruṣṭutaḥ ||
  • Purām bhindur yuvā kavir amitaujā ajāyata:
    • Purām: This word translates to "the city" or "the dwelling place." It symbolizes the physical and metaphorical spaces we inhabit.
    • Bhindur: This means "to break" or "to destroy." It signifies the act of overcoming obstacles or challenges.
    • Yuvā: Translated as "young" or "youthful," it represents vitality, energy, and potential.
    • Kavir: This word means "poet" or "wise one." It indicates creativity, intelligence, and the ability to articulate thoughts and ideas.
    • Amitaujā: This translates to "having immense power" or "infinite energy." It signifies strength and capability beyond measure.
    • Ajāyata: This means "was born" or "came into being." It indicates the emergence of something new or the beginning of a journey.
  • Indro viśvasya karmaṇo dhartā vajrī puruṣṭutaḥ:
    • Indro: Refers to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing leadership and authority.
    • Viśvasya: This means "of the universe" or "of all." It signifies inclusiveness and universality.
    • Karmaṇo: Translated as "of actions" or "of deeds," it emphasizes the importance of actions in shaping reality.
    • Dhartā: This means "the supporter" or "the sustainer." It indicates the role of a protector or nurturer.
    • Vajrī: This translates to "one who wields the thunderbolt," symbolizing strength and the ability to overcome challenges.
    • Puruṣṭutaḥ: This means "praised by men" or "worthy of respect." It signifies the recognition and honor given to those who embody these qualities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of overcoming challenges through youthful energy, creativity, and immense power. It emphasizes the importance of actions and the role of leadership in sustaining the universe. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, where one must break through obstacles to achieve their goals, embodying the youthful spirit and creativity.
  • In leadership roles, where the qualities of strength, support, and recognition are essential for guiding others and fostering a positive environment.
  • In community building, where collective actions and the nurturing of relationships lead to a harmonious and thriving society.

This mantra serves as a reminder of the potential within each individual to create, support, and lead, ultimately contributing to the greater good of the universe.
