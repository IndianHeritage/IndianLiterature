नैतावद एनापरो अन्यद अस्त्य उक्षाऽ स द्यावापृथिवी बिभर्ति |त्वचं पवित्रं कृत्नुत स्वधावान यद ईं सूर्यं न हरितो वहन्ति ||
naitāvad enā paro anyad asty ukṣā sa dyāvāpṛthivī bibharti | tvacam pavitraṁ kṛṇuta svadhāvān yad īṁ sūryaṁ na harito vahanti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • naitāvad: not this much
    • enā: by this
    • paro: other
    • anyad: anything else
    • asty: exists
    • ukṣā: the rains or the waters
    • sa: that
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: heaven and earth
    • bibharti: sustains
    • tvacam: skin or covering
    • pavitraṁ: pure
    • kṛṇuta: make
    • svadhāvān: self-moving or self-acting
    • yad: that which
    • īṁ: the sun
    • sūryaṁ: the sun
    • na: not
    • harito: green or flourishing
    • vahanti: carry or bear
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects on the interconnectedness of all elements of nature.
    • It suggests that there is no other existence beyond what is manifested in the duality of heaven and earth.
    • The phrase "tvacam pavitraṁ" emphasizes the importance of purity, indicating that the essence of life is to maintain purity in our actions and thoughts.
    • The reference to the sun ("sūryaṁ") signifies the source of life and energy, which is essential for growth and sustenance.
    • It also implies that the natural world, represented by the rains and the earth, is a nurturing force that supports life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, understanding the balance of nature—how rain nourishes the earth and how the sun provides energy—is crucial for successful farming.
    • In personal life, maintaining purity in thoughts and actions can lead to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
    • Environmental conservation efforts reflect the mantra's essence, as they aim to protect the purity of nature and ensure that the earth continues to sustain life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the unity of existence and the importance of purity in our lives.
    • It encourages us to recognize our connection to nature and the vital role it plays in our survival and well-being.
    • By applying these principles, we can foster a more sustainable and harmonious relationship with the world around us.
