स राजसि पुरुष्टुतमं एको वृत्राणि जिघ्नसे |इन्द्र जैत्रा श्रवस्याः च यन्तवे ||
sa rājasi puruṣṭutam̐ eko vṛtrāṇi jighnase | indra jaitrā śravasyā ca yantave ||
  • sa rājasi puruṣṭutam̐: This phrase can be translated as "He who is praised by the kings." Here, "sa" refers to a divine entity, often Indra, who is revered and honored by rulers and leaders. In real life, this can be likened to how leaders seek the blessings of higher powers or deities for guidance and strength in their governance.
  • eko vṛtrāṇi jighnase: This translates to "You alone are the one who slays the Vritra." Vritra is a serpent or dragon-like figure representing chaos and obstacles. In contemporary terms, this can symbolize overcoming challenges or adversities in life. For instance, a student facing academic challenges may seek motivation and strength to conquer their fears and succeed.
  • indra jaitrā: This means "Indra, the victorious." Indra is often depicted as a warrior who triumphs over evil. This can be related to personal victories in life, such as achieving a long-term goal or overcoming personal struggles, where one feels a sense of victory akin to Indra's conquests.
  • śravasyā ca yantave: This translates to "and to those who are worthy of praise." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring those who contribute positively to society. In real life, this can be seen in how communities celebrate heroes, teachers, or leaders who have made significant contributions, fostering a culture of appreciation and respect.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of leadership, overcoming challenges, victory, and the importance of honoring those who contribute positively to society. In real life, it serves as a reminder for individuals to seek strength in adversity, celebrate their victories, and recognize the contributions of others. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as in personal development, community building, and leadership, encouraging individuals to strive for excellence and support one another in their journeys.
