तेन नासत्यागतम् रथेन सूर्यत्वचा |येन शाश्वदूहतुर दाशुषे वसु मध्वः सोमस्य पीतये ||
tena nāsatyā gataṁ rathena sūryatvacā | yena śaśvad ūhathur dāśuṣe vasu madhvaḥ somasya pītaye ||
  • Mantra: "tena nāsatyā gataṁ rathena sūryatvacā | yena śaśvad ūhathur dāśuṣe vasu madhvaḥ somasya pītaye ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "By that (divine) power, the Asvins (Nāsatyas) have reached (the goal) with their chariot, shining like the sun; by that (power), they have always brought forth wealth for the worshipper, for the enjoyment of the soma."
  • Explanation:
    • Nāsatyas: The Nāsatyas, or the Asvins, are twin deities in the Rigveda, often associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between opposites.
    • Rathena: The term "rathena" refers to a chariot, which is a common symbol of movement and progress. In real life, this can be likened to the journey we undertake in our personal and professional lives, where we strive to reach our goals.
    • Sūryatvacā: This word means "shining like the sun," representing brilliance, clarity, and enlightenment. In our lives, this can be seen in moments of inspiration or clarity when we gain insight into our challenges.
    • Vasu Madhvaḥ: "Vasu" refers to wealth or prosperity, while "Madhvaḥ" signifies sweetness or enjoyment. This indicates that the blessings we receive should not only be abundant but also enjoyable, akin to the sweetness of life.
    • Soma: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine ecstasy. In modern terms, it can represent the pursuit of happiness and fulfillment in our lives.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine assistance in achieving our goals and the need for balance in our lives.
    • It teaches us that with the right guidance and support, we can navigate through life's challenges and attain prosperity.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to seek help from others, whether through mentorship, community support, or spiritual guidance, to achieve our aspirations.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can inspire teamwork and collaboration, reminding us that we can achieve more together than alone.
    • In personal development, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom from various sources, enhancing their journey towards self-improvement.
    • In times of difficulty, it serves as a reminder to look for divine or external support, reinforcing the idea that we are not alone in our struggles.
