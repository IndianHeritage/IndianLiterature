महीषासो मायिनश्चित्रभानवो गिरयो न स्वतवसो रघुष्यदः |मृगा इव हस्तिनः खादथā वनā यद आरुणीषु तविषीर आयुग्ध्वम् ||
mahiṣāso māyinaś citrabhānavo girayo na svatavaso raghuṣyadaḥ | mṛgā iva hastinaḥ khādathā vanā yad āruṇīṣu taviṣīr ayugdhvam ||
  • mahiṣāso: This term refers to the "buffaloes" or "great beasts," symbolizing strength and resilience.
  • māyinaś: This word means "magical" or "illusionary," indicating the presence of something beyond the ordinary.
  • citrabhānavo: Translated as "those with colorful or radiant forms," it suggests diversity and beauty in nature.
  • girayo: This means "mountains," representing stability and permanence in the natural world.
  • na svatavaso: This phrase translates to "not belonging to oneself," indicating a sense of shared existence or community.
  • raghuṣyadaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "those who are swift or agile," often referring to the qualities of animals or beings that are quick and alert.
  • mṛgā iva: Meaning "like the deer," this phrase evokes the image of grace and agility in nature.
  • hastinaḥ: This translates to "of the elephants," symbolizing strength, wisdom, and memory.
  • khādathā: This means "you shall eat," suggesting sustenance and nourishment.
  • vanā: This word means "from the forest," indicating a connection to nature and the wild.
  • yad āruṇīṣu: Translated as "when the dawn breaks," it signifies new beginnings and opportunities.
  • taviṣīr: This term means "to be strong" or "to be powerful," emphasizing the importance of strength in overcoming challenges.
  • ayugdhvam: This can be interpreted as "do not be divided," suggesting unity and harmony among beings.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of strength, unity, and resilience in the face of challenges. It highlights the beauty of diversity in nature and the need for cooperation among different entities. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in organizations, community building, and environmental conservation efforts. By recognizing our shared existence and working together, we can overcome obstacles and create a harmonious society. The mantra serves as a reminder to embrace our strengths while fostering unity and collaboration in our daily lives.
