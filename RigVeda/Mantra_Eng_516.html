मां नः सोमपरिबाधो मारातयो जुहुरन्त |आ न इन्दो वाजे भज ||
mā naḥ somaparibādho mārātayo juhuranta | ā na indo vāje bhaja ||
  • mā naḥ somaparibādho mārātayo juhuranta
    • mā naḥ - "May we not" or "Let us not". This phrase expresses a desire for protection or avoidance of negativity.
    • somaparibādho - "the harm caused by the moon" or "the influence of the moon". In Vedic literature, the moon is often associated with emotions, mind, and the subtler aspects of life.
    • mārātayo - "the enemies" or "the harmful forces". This refers to any negative influences or adversities that one may face.
    • juhuranta - "may they not come near" or "may they be kept away". This is a plea for protection from these negative influences.
  • ā na indo vāje bhaja
    • ā na - "May we" or "Let us". This is an invocation for positive action or blessings.
    • indo - "Indra\ the king of gods, often associated with strength, power, and protection.
    • vāje - "in strength" or "in power". This signifies the importance of strength in overcoming challenges.
    • bhaja - "worship" or "serve". This indicates a call to action, to honor and seek the blessings of Indra.

Conclusion:

This mantra is a powerful invocation for protection against negative influences and a call for strength and support from divine forces. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • During challenging times, individuals can recite this mantra to seek protection from adversities and emotional turmoil.
  • In moments of fear or anxiety, it serves as a reminder to focus on inner strength and seek divine assistance.
  • In communal settings, such as during festivals or gatherings, this mantra can be chanted to foster a sense of unity and collective strength against negativity.

Overall, the significance of this mantra lies in its ability to empower individuals to confront challenges with courage while seeking divine protection and guidance.
