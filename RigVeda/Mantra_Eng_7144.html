दिवो मानं नोत सदन सोमपृष्ठासो अद्रयः |उक्ताः ब्रह्म च शंस्याः ||
divo mānaṁ not sadan somapṛṣṭhāso adrayaḥ | ukthā brahma ca śaṁsyā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Divo: Of the heavens or celestial.
    • Mānaṁ: Respect or honor.
    • Not: Not or without.
    • Sadan: Abode or dwelling.
    • Somapṛṣṭhāso: Those who are nourished by the essence of Soma (a sacred drink).
    • Adrayaḥ: The non-material or spiritual.
    • Ukthā: Chanted or recited.
    • Brahma: The ultimate reality or cosmic spirit.
    • Ca: And.
    • Śaṁsyā: To be praised or celebrated.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the celestial realm and the respect that should be given to it.
    • It emphasizes the importance of spiritual nourishment, akin to how one would nourish the body with food.
    • The mention of "Soma" refers to the divine essence that sustains life, suggesting that spiritual sustenance is as vital as physical sustenance.
    • The mantra encourages the recitation of sacred verses, which can be seen as a way to connect with the divine and elevate one's consciousness.
    • It highlights the significance of praising the ultimate reality, Brahma, which is essential for spiritual growth and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, one can practice respect for nature and the universe, recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • Engaging in meditation or spiritual practices can serve as a form of nourishment for the soul, similar to how one consumes food for the body.
    • Participating in community rituals or gatherings where sacred verses are recited can foster a sense of belonging and spiritual upliftment.
    • In moments of gratitude, acknowledging the greater forces at play in life can enhance one's appreciation for existence.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of honoring the spiritual aspects of life.
    • It encourages individuals to seek nourishment beyond the physical realm, fostering a holistic approach to well-being.
    • By reciting sacred verses and acknowledging the divine, one can cultivate a deeper connection with the universe and enhance their spiritual journey.
    • Ultimately, the mantra signifies the balance between the material and spiritual worlds, urging us to live in harmony with both.
