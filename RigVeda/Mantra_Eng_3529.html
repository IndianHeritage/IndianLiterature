युवं श्रीयम अश्विनाऽ देवता तां दिवो नपाताः वनतः शचीभिः |युवोर वपुर अभि पृक्षः साचन्ते वहन्ति यत ककुहासो रथे वाम् ||
yuvaṁ śriyam aśvinā devatā tāṁ divo napātā vanathaḥ śacībhiḥ | yuvor vapur abhi pṛkṣaḥ sacante vahanti yat kakuhāso rathe vām ||
  • Mantra: "yuvaṁ śriyam aśvinā devatā tāṁ divo napātā vanathaḥ śacībhiḥ | yuvor vapur abhi pṛkṣaḥ sacante vahanti yat kakuhāso rathe vām ||"
  • Translation: This mantra invokes the youthful energy and prosperity represented by the Aśvins, the twin deities of health and vitality. It calls upon them to bestow their blessings, emphasizing the importance of divine support in achieving success and well-being.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yuvaṁ: youthful, representing vigor and energy.
    • śriyam: prosperity or wealth, often associated with success and abundance.
    • aśvinā: referring to the Aśvins, divine twin brothers known for their healing and life-giving powers.
    • devatā: deities or divine beings, indicating the spiritual aspect of the invocation.
    • tāṁ: that, referring to the blessings or qualities being sought.
    • dīvo: of the sky or heaven, suggesting a connection to higher powers.
    • napātā: offspring or descendants, indicating a lineage of divine qualities.
    • vanathaḥ: to be invoked or called upon, emphasizing the act of seeking divine intervention.
    • śacībhiḥ: with strength or power, indicating the force behind the blessings.
    • yuvor: of the youthful, reinforcing the theme of vitality.
    • vapur: form or body, referring to the physical manifestation of energy.
    • abhi: towards or in the direction of, indicating intention.
    • pṛkṣaḥ: to be observed or perceived, suggesting awareness and mindfulness.
    • sacante: they who are strong or powerful, referring to the divine beings.
    • vahanti: they carry or bring forth, indicating the act of delivering blessings.
    • yat: that which, referring to the qualities or blessings being sought.
    • kakuhāso: in the womb or source, indicating the origin of life and potential.
    • rathe: in the chariot, symbolizing the journey of life.
    • vām: for you, indicating a personal appeal for blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive environment, such as a job interview, invoking the qualities of the Aśvins can symbolize the need for confidence and vitality to present oneself effectively.
    • During a challenging phase in life, such as illness or personal loss, this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek divine support and maintain hope for recovery and prosperity.
    • In educational pursuits, students can recite this mantra to invoke focus and energy, helping them to achieve their academic goals with the blessings of the divine.
  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of invoking divine support for achieving prosperity and vitality in life. It serves as a reminder that, like the Aśvins, we can seek strength and blessings from higher powers to navigate the challenges we face. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal struggles, professional endeavors, or academic pursuits, this mantra encourages individuals to remain connected to their spiritual roots and seek the energy and support necessary for success.
