इन्द्रासोमा समाघशंसम अभ्यघं तपुर ययस्तु चरुर अग्निवाम् इव |ब्रह्मद्विषे क्रव्यादे घोरचक्षसे द्वेषो धत्तम अनवायं किमीदिने ||
indrāsomā sam aghaśaṁsam abhy a1ghaṁ tapur yayastu carur agnivām̐ iva | brahmadviṣe kravyāde ghoracakṣase dveṣo dhattam anavāyaṁ kimīdine ||
  • Indrāsomā: This term refers to the divine entities Indra and Soma. Indra is the king of the gods and represents strength, power, and rain, while Soma symbolizes the sacred drink that grants immortality and is associated with the moon.
  • Sam aghaśaṁsam: This phrase can be interpreted as a call for the removal of obstacles or sins. It suggests a desire for purification and the elimination of negativity.
  • Abhy a1ghaṁ: This means "to remove sin" or "to purify." It emphasizes the importance of cleansing oneself from impurities, both physical and spiritual.
  • Tapur yayastu: This refers to the act of performing penance or austerity. It highlights the significance of self-discipline and dedication in achieving spiritual goals.
  • Carur agnivām̐ iva: This phrase likens the divine qualities to a swift and powerful fire. It symbolizes transformation, energy, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • Brahmadviṣe: This term refers to the enemies of the divine or those who oppose spiritual growth. It signifies the presence of negativity and obstacles in one's path.
  • Kravyāde: This can be interpreted as referring to the spirits or entities that may cause harm. It serves as a reminder of the darker aspects of existence that one must confront.
  • Ghoracakṣase: This phrase means "to the fierce-eyed one," which can symbolize a powerful force or deity that observes and judges actions. It emphasizes the need for awareness of one's deeds.
  • Dveṣo dhattam: This means "let hatred be cast away." It calls for the elimination of negative emotions and encourages love and compassion instead.
  • Anavāyaṁ: This term signifies the idea of protection or safeguarding. It suggests a plea for divine protection from harm and negativity.
  • Kimīdine: This phrase can be interpreted as a question about the nature of the day or the present moment. It encourages mindfulness and awareness of one's current state.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of purification, self-discipline, and the removal of negativity in one's life. It serves as a reminder to seek divine guidance and protection while striving for spiritual growth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during moments of introspection, meditation, or when facing challenges. By focusing on the principles of love, compassion, and self-improvement, individuals can navigate their lives with greater awareness and purpose, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
