उदुष्य वः सविता सुप्रणीतयोऽस्थादूर्ध्वो वरेंयः |निद्विपादश्चतुर्ष्पादो अर्थिनोऽविश्रान्तपतयिष्णवः ||
ud u ṣya vaḥ savitā supraṇītayo 'sthād ūrdhvo vareṇyaḥ | ni dvipādaś catuṣpādo arthino 'viśran patayiṣṇavaḥ ||
  • ud u ṣya vaḥ savitā supraṇītayo 'sthād ūrdhvo vareṇyaḥ
    • ud u ṣya: This phrase can be interpreted as "rise up" or "awaken." It signifies the call for enlightenment and awareness.
    • vaḥ: This means "to you" or "for you," indicating a direction towards the divine or higher consciousness.
    • savitā: Refers to the Sun God, Savitā, who represents illumination, life, and energy. The Sun is often seen as a source of vitality and inspiration.
    • supraṇītayo: This term means "well-directed" or "well-placed," suggesting that the energies or intentions are aligned properly.
    • 'sthād: This means "to stand" or "to be established," indicating stability and grounding in one's pursuits.
    • ūrdhvo: Translates to "upward" or "higher," symbolizing aspirations and the pursuit of higher goals.
    • vareṇyaḥ: This means "worthy of worship" or "most excellent," referring to the divine qualities that are to be revered.
  • ni dvipādaś catuṣpādo arthino 'viśran patayiṣṇavaḥ
    • ni: This can mean "not" or "without," indicating a negation or absence of something.
    • dvipādaś: Refers to "two-footed," which can symbolize humans or beings that walk on two legs, representing humanity.
    • catuṣpādo: Means "four-footed," which can refer to animals or creatures that walk on four legs, symbolizing the natural world.
    • arthino: This means "seeking" or "desiring," indicating a quest for meaning or purpose.
    • 'viśran: This term can be interpreted as "to be at ease" or "to be free from worry," suggesting a state of peace.
    • patayiṣṇavaḥ: This means "to be established" or "to be grounded," indicating a sense of stability and security in one's pursuits.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of awakening to higher consciousness and aligning one's intentions with divine energy. It calls for individuals to rise above mundane concerns and seek a deeper understanding of life, both in the human and natural realms. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Personal development: Encouraging individuals to pursue self-awareness and enlightenment through meditation and reflection.
  • Environmental consciousness: Recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of living in harmony with nature.
  • Community building: Fostering a sense of unity and purpose among individuals, encouraging collaboration and support.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to strive for higher ideals, seek knowledge, and cultivate a sense of peace and stability in our lives and communities.
