आपः प्रणीता भेषजं वरूथं तन्वे मम |ज्योत् च सूर्यं दृष्टे ||
āpaḥ pṛṇīta bheṣajaṁ varūthaṁ tanve3 mama | jyok ca sūryaṁ dṛśe ||
  • āpaḥ: This word translates to "waters" or "liquids." In the context of the RigVeda, it often symbolizes the life-giving and purifying aspects of water.
  • pṛṇīta: This means "to fill" or "to nourish." It indicates the act of providing sustenance or abundance.
  • bheṣajaṁ: This term refers to "healing" or "medicine." It signifies the restorative properties of certain elements, particularly in relation to health and well-being.
  • varūthaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the protective" or "the covering." It suggests a sense of safety and security, often associated with divine protection.
  • tanve3: This word means "my body" or "my being." It emphasizes the personal connection to the physical form and existence.
  • mama: This translates to "my," indicating possession or personal relevance.
  • jyok: This term means "light" or "radiance." It symbolizes knowledge, enlightenment, and the divine presence.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking the concepts together.
  • sūryaṁ: This word refers to the "sun." In many cultures, the sun is a symbol of life, energy, and vitality.
  • dṛśe: This means "to the sight" or "to be seen." It indicates visibility or perception, often relating to awareness and understanding.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for the nourishing and healing qualities of water, seeking protection for the body, and calling upon the light of the sun for guidance and enlightenment. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature and the divine, highlighting the importance of these elements in sustaining life and promoting well-being.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Health and Wellness: The invocation of healing waters can be a reminder to stay hydrated and to seek natural remedies for health issues, emphasizing the importance of nature in our healing processes.
  • Environmental Awareness: Recognizing the significance of water and sunlight in our ecosystem can inspire actions towards conservation and sustainable living.
  • Personal Growth: The call for light and enlightenment can motivate individuals to pursue knowledge and self-improvement, fostering a deeper understanding of oneself and the world.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the essential elements that sustain life—water, light, and the protective forces of nature. It encourages individuals to seek nourishment, healing, and enlightenment in their lives, fostering a holistic approach to well-being and personal development.
