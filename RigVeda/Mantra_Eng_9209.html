एतं वां स्तोमम् अश्विनाव अकर्मातक्षाम भृगवो न रथम् |न्य अमृक्षाम योषणां न मर्ये नित्यं न सूनुं तनयं दधानाः ||
etaṁ vāṁ stomam aśvināv akarmātakṣāma bhṛgavo na ratham | ny amṛkṣāma yoṣaṇāṁ na marye nityaṁ na sūnuṁ tanayaṁ dadhānāḥ ||
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "We invoke you, O Ashvins, who are free from all faults, who are the sons of Bhṛgu, who do not abandon the chariot. We seek your protection, O divine ones, who are ever present, who do not forsake the women, who are eternal and who bestow sons and progeny."
  • Explanation of Key Terms:
    • Ashvins: The Ashvins are twin deities in Vedic mythology, often associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of nature and the balance between opposites.
    • Akarmātakṣāma: This term refers to being free from faults or imperfections. It emphasizes the purity and perfection of the Ashvins.
    • Bhṛgu: Bhṛgu is one of the seven great sages (Saptarishi) in Vedic literature, representing wisdom and knowledge.
    • Ratham: This word means 'chariot,' symbolizing the journey of life and the support we seek in our endeavors.
    • Amṛkṣāma: This term indicates the desire for protection and support, especially in times of need.
    • Yoṣaṇāṁ: Referring to women, this highlights the importance of nurturing and protecting the feminine aspect in society.
    • Marye: This term signifies mortality, reminding us of the transient nature of life.
    • Nityaṁ: Meaning 'eternal,' it emphasizes the everlasting nature of divine support.
    • Tanayaṁ: This refers to progeny or offspring, symbolizing the continuation of life and legacy.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern healthcare, the invocation of the Ashvins can be likened to the pursuit of medical advancements and the quest for healing. Just as the Ashvins are associated with health, contemporary medicine seeks to provide remedies and support for the sick.
    • The concept of seeking protection and support can be seen in community initiatives that aim to safeguard vulnerable populations, such as women and children, ensuring their well-being and security.
    • The idea of nurturing relationships and family ties resonates in today's society, where the importance of family and community support is paramount for personal growth and stability.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine support and protection in our lives. It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking help from higher powers, whether in times of health crises, personal challenges, or societal issues. The Ashvins symbolize the balance of dualities and the need for harmony in our lives. By recognizing the eternal nature of support and the importance of nurturing relationships, we can apply the teachings of this mantra in our daily lives, fostering a sense of community, health, and well-being.
