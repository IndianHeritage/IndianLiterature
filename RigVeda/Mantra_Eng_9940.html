कं नश् चित्रम् इषण्यसि चिकीत्वान् पृथुग्मानं वाश्रं वावृद्धध्यै |कत तस्य दातु शवसो व्युष्टौ तक्षद वज्रं वृतृतुरम् अपिन्वत ||
kaṁ naś citram iṣaṇyasi cikitvān pṛthugmānaṁ vāśraṁ vāvṛdhadhyai | kat tasya dātu śavaso vyuṣṭau takṣad vajraṁ vṛtraturam apinvat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kaṁ: Who
    • naś: Our
    • citram: Wonderful
    • iṣaṇyasi: You are thinking
    • cikitvān: With consideration
    • pṛthugmānaṁ: Broadly manifest
    • vāśraṁ: The powerful
    • vāvṛdhadhyai: You have increased
    • kat: What
    • tasya: Of that
    • dātu: To give
    • śavaso: Of the dead
    • vyuṣṭau: In the dawn
    • takṣad: You have made
    • vajraṁ: Thunderbolt
    • vṛtraturam: Slayer of Vritra
    • apinvat: You have not withheld
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses the divine, asking who among the powerful beings is considering the wonderful aspects of our existence.
    • It reflects on the broad manifestations of power and how they have been increased by divine intervention.
    • The mantra also inquires about the gifts that can be bestowed upon the dead, particularly in the context of dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and hope.
    • It highlights the act of creation and destruction, referencing the thunderbolt, a symbol of divine power, and the slaying of Vritra, a demon representing chaos and obstacles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, this mantra can be related to moments of reflection when one is contemplating their life choices and the broader implications of those choices.
    • In a community setting, it can symbolize the collective efforts to overcome challenges, akin to how a community comes together to support one another during difficult times.
    • In a spiritual context, it can be seen as a reminder of the divine forces at play in our lives, encouraging individuals to seek guidance and strength in their endeavors.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for introspection and recognition of the divine forces that shape our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that while we face challenges (symbolized by Vritra), there is always the potential for renewal and strength (represented by the dawn and the thunderbolt).
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to seek wisdom, embrace change, and acknowledge the interconnectedness of all beings in the journey of life.
