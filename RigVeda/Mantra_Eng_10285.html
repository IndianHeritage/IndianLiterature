यं कुमार प्रावर्तयो रथं विप्रेभ्यस् परि |तं सामानु प्रावर्तत सम इतो नाव्य आहितम् ||
yaṁ kumāra prāvartayo rathaṁ viprebhyas pari | taṁ sāmānu prāvartata sam ito nāvy āhitam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: who
    • kumāra: the young one or prince
    • prāvartayo: who initiates or sets in motion
    • rathaṁ: the chariot
    • viprebhyas: for the learned or the wise
    • pari: around or in all directions
    • taṁ: that
    • sāmānu: the one who is in harmony or agreement
    • prāvartata: may it be initiated or set in motion
    • sam: together
    • ito: from here
    • nāvy: the ship or vessel
    • āhitam: that is set or established
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a young prince or leader who initiates the movement of a chariot, symbolizing the beginning of a journey or a new venture.
    • The reference to "viprebhyas" indicates that this action is for the wise or learned individuals, suggesting that knowledge and wisdom are essential for guiding the journey.
    • The phrase "sāmānu prāvartata" emphasizes the importance of harmony and collective effort in initiating actions, highlighting that success is often a result of collaboration.
    • The mention of "nāvy āhitam" suggests that the journey is not just on land but also involves navigating through challenges, akin to a ship setting sail on the ocean.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a young manager (the kumāra) may lead a new project (the rathaṁ) that requires the input and expertise of experienced team members (the viprebhyas). Their collaboration and shared vision (sāmānu) are crucial for the project's success.
    • In community initiatives, a youth leader may initiate a social campaign that requires the support and wisdom of elders and knowledgeable individuals, emphasizing the importance of collective action (sam) for achieving common goals.
    • In personal development, an individual embarking on a new life journey, such as starting a family or pursuing higher education, must seek guidance from mentors and wise individuals, ensuring that their path is well-planned and harmonious.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of leadership, wisdom, and collaboration in initiating new ventures and navigating life's challenges.
    • It teaches us that success is not solely dependent on individual effort but rather on the collective wisdom and harmony of those involved.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal, professional, or community contexts, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide us in making informed decisions and fostering cooperation for a successful journey.
