उषो वाजं हि वंस्व यश चित्रो मानवसे जनें |तेन वहा सुक्रतो अध्वराम् उप ये त्वा गृणन्ति वहनयः ||
uṣo vājaṁ hi vaṁsva yaś citro mānuṣe jane | tenā vaha sukṛto adhvarām̐ upa ye tvā gṛṇanti vahnayaḥ ||
  • uṣo: This word refers to the dawn or the early morning light, symbolizing new beginnings and the dispelling of darkness.
  • vājaṁ: This translates to "strength" or "power," often associated with the energy and vitality that comes with the dawn.
  • hi: A particle that emphasizes the preceding word, indicating certainty or truth.
  • vaṁsva: This can mean "belonging to the family" or "lineage," highlighting the importance of heritage and community.
  • yaś: This word means "fame" or "glory," suggesting the pursuit of honor and recognition in life.
  • citro: Meaning "brilliant" or "colorful," it signifies the beauty and vibrancy of life.
  • mānuṣe: This refers to "human" or "mankind," emphasizing the mantra's relevance to human experience.
  • jane: This means "people" or "individuals," reinforcing the collective aspect of humanity.
  • tenā: This word means "by that" or "through that," indicating a connection or means to an end.
  • vaha: This translates to "carry" or "bring forth," suggesting the act of delivering or manifesting something.
  • sukṛto: Meaning "good deeds" or "virtuous actions," it emphasizes the importance of morality and ethics.
  • adhvarām̐: This refers to "sacrificial rites" or "rituals," indicating the significance of spiritual practices in life.
  • upa: This means "near" or "towards," suggesting a movement towards something desirable.
  • ye: This translates to "those who," referring to individuals or groups.
  • tvā: This means "you," indicating a direct address to a divine or spiritual entity.
  • gṛṇanti: This translates to "sing" or "praise," highlighting the act of worship or acknowledgment.
  • vahnayaḥ: This refers to "the fires" or "the divine flames," symbolizing purification, transformation, and the divine presence.

Conclusion: The mantra speaks to the significance of dawn as a metaphor for new beginnings, the strength and vitality that comes with it, and the importance of community, good deeds, and spiritual practices. In real life, this can be applied to the idea of starting each day with intention, focusing on positive actions, and recognizing the beauty in our surroundings. It encourages individuals to engage in virtuous actions and to honor the divine through rituals and praise, fostering a sense of connection with both the community and the spiritual realm. This mantra serves as a reminder to embrace each day with hope and purpose, striving for personal and collective growth.
