अदब्द्हेभिः सवितः पायुभिष् त्वं शिवेभिर अद्य परि पाहि नो गयम् |हिरण्यजिह्वः सुविताया नव्यसे रक्षां माकिर नो अघशंस ईशत ||
adabdhebhiḥ savitaḥ pāyubhiṣ ṭvaṁ śivebhir adya pari pāhi no gayam | hiraṇyajihvaḥ suvitāya navyase rakṣā mākir no aghaśaṁsa īśata ||
  • Mantra: "adabdhebhiḥ savitaḥ pāyubhiṣ ṭvaṁ śivebhir adya pari pāhi no gayam | hiraṇyajihvaḥ suvitāya navyase rakṣā mākir no aghaśaṁsa īśata ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • adabdhebhiḥ: From the unbroken, or undisturbed.
    • savitaḥ: O Savita, the Sun God.
    • pāyubhiṣ: With your powers or energies.
    • ṭvaṁ: You.
    • śivebhir: With auspiciousness or blessings.
    • adya: Today.
    • pari: All around or completely.
    • pāhi: Protect.
    • no: Us.
    • gayam: Our song or chant.
    • hiraṇyajihvaḥ: With a golden tongue.
    • suvitāya: For good nourishment or prosperity.
    • navyase: New or fresh.
    • rakṣā: Protection.
    • mākir: May you not allow.
    • no: Us.
    • aghaśaṁsa: Sin or wrongdoing.
    • īśata: To rule or govern.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Savita, the Sun God, asking for protection and blessings.
    • The phrase "adabdhebhiḥ savitaḥ" signifies a call to the Sun to shine upon us without disturbance, representing clarity and purity in our lives.
    • When we say "pāyubhiṣ ṭvaṁ," we are invoking the energies of the Sun to empower and protect us.
    • The term "śivebhir" emphasizes the need for auspiciousness in our endeavors, suggesting that we seek divine blessings in our daily activities.
    • By asking Savita to "pāhi no gayam," we are requesting protection for our collective voice or community, highlighting the importance of unity and harmony.
    • "Hiraṇyajihvaḥ" refers to the Sun's golden tongue, symbolizing the power of speech and communication, which can be used for positive or negative purposes.
    • The mantra concludes with a plea for protection from sins or wrongdoings, indicating a desire for moral integrity and ethical living.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be recited during morning rituals to seek guidance and protection for the day ahead.
    • It can be applied in community gatherings where collective well-being is prioritized, reminding participants to act with integrity and seek blessings for their endeavors.
    • In moments of decision-making, invoking this mantra can help individuals align their actions with auspiciousness and moral values.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine guidance and protection in our lives.
    • It emphasizes the need for unity, auspiciousness, and ethical living, making it relevant in various aspects of life, from personal growth to community well-being.
    • By incorporating this mantra into our daily practices, we can cultivate a sense of peace, purpose, and moral clarity in our actions.
