इदां हि वो विधते रत्नमस्तीदां वीराय दाशुष उषासः |इदां विप्राय जरतें यदुक्ता निःष्म मावत वहता पुरा चित ||
idā hi vo vidhate ratnam astīdā vīrāya dāśuṣa uṣāsaḥ | idā viprāya jarate yad ukthā ni ṣma māvate vahathā purā cit ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • idā: Here, in this context.
    • hi: Indeed, certainly.
    • vo: For you, to you.
    • vidhate: Creates, establishes.
    • ratnam: Jewel, treasure, or valuable thing.
    • astī: Is, exists.
    • vīrāya: For the brave, for the hero.
    • dāśuṣa: For the one who is devoted, for the servant.
    • uṣāsaḥ: Of the dawn, of the new beginning.
  • Second Line Breakdown:
    • viprāya: For the wise, for the learned.
    • jarate: Grows, increases.
    • yad: That which.
    • ukthā: Spoken, uttered.
    • ni: Not.
    • ṣma: To be, to exist.
    • māvate: To the one who is worthy, to the deserving.
    • vahathā: You carry, you bear.
    • purā: In the past, previously.
    • cit: Remembered, known.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks about the creation of treasures and the importance of bravery and devotion.
    • It emphasizes that the wise and learned individuals grow and flourish through their spoken words and actions.
    • The mantra suggests that one should not forget the past and the knowledge that has been carried forward.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who encourages bravery and innovation can create a 'treasure' of ideas that benefit the organization.
    • Teachers who inspire their students to learn and grow embody the essence of the wise, nurturing the next generation.
    • In personal relationships, remembering and valuing past experiences can strengthen bonds and foster deeper connections.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call to recognize and cultivate the treasures of bravery, wisdom, and devotion in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various scenarios, from personal growth to professional development, reminding us of the importance of our words and actions.
    • By embracing these values, we can create a brighter future, much like the dawn that the mantra references.
