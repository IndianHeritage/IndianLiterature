प्र श्यावाश्व धृष्णुयार्चा मरुद्भिर ऋक्वभिः |ये अद्रोघम अनुष्वधं श्रवो मदन्ति यज्ञियाः ||
pra śyāvāśva dhṛṣṇuyārcā marudbhir ṛkvabhiḥ | ye adrogham anuṣvadhaṁ śravo madanti yajñiyāḥ ||
  • pra śyāvāśva: This phrase can be interpreted as "the swift horses" or "the powerful steeds." In ancient Vedic culture, horses were symbols of strength, speed, and vitality. They were often associated with the sun and were crucial in warfare and agriculture.
  • dhṛṣṇuyārcā: This term refers to "the one who is worthy of worship" or "the one who is revered." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring those who embody strength and virtue.
  • marudbhir ṛkvabhiḥ: "Marud" refers to the storm gods or winds, and "ṛkvabhiḥ" relates to the hymns or praises. This part of the mantra suggests invoking the forces of nature and the divine through hymns, highlighting the connection between the natural world and spiritual practices.
  • ye adrogham anuṣvadhaṁ: This translates to "those who do not harm" or "those who are free from wrongdoing." It emphasizes the importance of ethical conduct and integrity in one's actions.
  • śravo madanti yajñiyāḥ: This phrase means "those who are engaged in sacrifice" or "those who perform rituals." It signifies the value of selfless actions and contributions to the community and the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, reverence, ethical conduct, and the importance of rituals in Vedic culture. In real life, it serves as a reminder to honor those who embody virtues, to act with integrity, and to engage in selfless service. The invocation of natural forces and divine beings through hymns highlights the interconnectedness of humanity and nature. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership, where one must lead with strength and integrity, or in community service, where selfless actions contribute to the greater good. Ultimately, it encourages individuals to strive for a harmonious existence with both the divine and the natural world.
