मन्त्र: कदाचित् स्त्रीर् असि नेंद्र सश्चसि दाशुषे | उपोपेन नु मगवन् भूय इन् नु ते दानं देवस्य पृच्यते ||
kadā cana starīr asi nendra saścasi dāśuṣe | upopen nu maghavan bhūya in nu te dānaṁ devasya pṛcyate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kadā cana: at any time
    • starīr: you are a star
    • asi: you are
    • nendra: not a leader
    • saścasi: you are not strong
    • dāśuṣe: for the one who gives
    • upopen: to be approached
    • nu: indeed
    • maghavan: O bountiful one
    • bhūya: may you be
    • in nu: indeed
    • te: your
    • dānaṁ: gift
    • devasya: of the divine
    • pṛcyate: is sought
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects a plea for divine generosity and support.
    • The phrase "kadā cana starīr asi" suggests that at any time, one can aspire to be like a star, symbolizing guidance and brilliance.
    • However, it also acknowledges the limitations of human strength, as indicated by "nendra saścasi," which means one may not always be a leader or strong.
    • The call to "upopen nu maghavan" emphasizes the importance of approaching the divine for blessings and abundance.
    • The mantra culminates in the request for divine gifts, highlighting the significance of seeking help from higher powers.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal struggle, individuals often turn to prayer or meditation, seeking guidance and strength from a higher power, much like the plea in this mantra.
    • In community settings, leaders may invoke such mantras during gatherings to inspire collective strength and generosity, reminding everyone of the importance of supporting one another.
    • Philanthropic efforts can also be seen as a manifestation of the "dānaṁ devasya," where individuals or organizations give back to society, reflecting the divine quality of generosity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the human condition—our aspirations, limitations, and the need for divine support.
    • It emphasizes the significance of humility in seeking help and the importance of generosity in our actions.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to strive for greatness while recognizing their limitations and the value of community and divine assistance.
