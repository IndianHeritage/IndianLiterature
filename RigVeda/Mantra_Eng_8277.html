हिन्वन्ति सूरम् उषरयः पवमानम् मधुश्चुतम् |अभि गिरा सम अस्वरन् ||
hinvanti sūram usrayaḥ pavamānam madhuścutam | abhi girā sam asvaran ||
  • hinvanti sūram usrayaḥ: This phrase can be translated to "the strong ones (sūram) who are endowed with vigor (usrayaḥ)". Here, "sūram" refers to those who are powerful or courageous, while "usrayaḥ" signifies strength or energy. In real life, this can be likened to individuals or groups who exhibit bravery and resilience in the face of challenges, such as firefighters or soldiers who risk their lives to protect others.
  • pavamānam madhuścutam: This part translates to "the one who is purified (pavamānam) and brings sweetness (madhuścutam)". "Pavamānam" indicates something that is pure or sacred, while "madhuścutam" refers to something that is sweet or delightful. This can be related to the idea of achieving purity in thoughts and actions, leading to positive outcomes. For example, a person who practices mindfulness and compassion can create a harmonious environment around them, much like a teacher who inspires students through their positive attitude.
  • abhi girā sam asvaran: This translates to "they (the strong ones) are called upon (abhi girā) with a unified voice (sam asvaran)". "Abhi girā" suggests invoking or calling forth, while "sam asvaran" implies a collective or harmonious sound. This can be seen in real-life scenarios where communities come together to support a cause, such as during a protest or a charity event, where their unified voice amplifies their message and impact.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, purity, and unity. It signifies that when individuals or communities embody courage and purity of purpose, they can create a positive and impactful presence in the world. In real-life scenarios, this can be applicable in various contexts, such as teamwork in a corporate environment, community service initiatives, or even personal development journeys. By embracing these qualities, we can contribute to a more harmonious and supportive society.
