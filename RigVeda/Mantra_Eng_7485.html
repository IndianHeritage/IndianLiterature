इन्द्र इन नो महानां दाता वाजानां नृतुः |महाम् अभिज्ञ्वा यमत ||
indra in no mahānāṁ dātā vājānāṁ nṛtuḥ | mahām̐ abhijñv ā yamat ||
  • Indra: Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He symbolizes strength and power.
  • In no: This phrase can be interpreted as "in no" or "not in." It suggests a negation or absence of something.
  • Mahānāṁ: This word means "of the great" or "of the mighty." It refers to those who possess significant power or influence.
  • Dātā: This translates to "giver" or "donor." It signifies someone who provides or bestows something valuable.
  • Vājānāṁ: This term means "of the strong" or "of the powerful." It is often associated with strength and valor.
  • Nṛtuḥ: This word can be understood as "of the dance" or "of the rhythm." It implies movement, celebration, or a dynamic aspect of life.
  • Mahām̐: This is an emphatic form of "great" or "mighty," reinforcing the significance of the subject being discussed.
  • Abhijñv: This term means "knowledgeable" or "wise." It refers to someone who possesses deep understanding or insight.
  • Ā yamat: This phrase can be interpreted as "may he come" or "let him arrive." It expresses a desire for the presence or influence of the subject.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation to Indra, recognizing his role as a powerful giver and wise protector. It emphasizes the importance of strength, knowledge, and the dynamic nature of life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra is a leader among the gods, individuals in positions of authority can embody the qualities of strength and wisdom to guide others effectively.
  • Community Support: The idea of being a "giver" resonates in community service, where individuals contribute their resources and knowledge for the betterment of society.
  • Personal Growth: The pursuit of knowledge and strength is essential for personal development. Individuals can strive to be "abhijñv" by seeking wisdom and understanding in their lives.

In conclusion, this mantra highlights the significance of strength, wisdom, and the act of giving. It serves as a reminder to embody these qualities in our daily lives, fostering a sense of community and personal growth. By invoking the spirit of Indra, we can aspire to be powerful and knowledgeable individuals who contribute positively to the world around us.
