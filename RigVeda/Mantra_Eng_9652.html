न तं विदाथ य इमा जजानान्यद युष्माकम अन्तरम् बभूव |नीहारिणा प्रावृतā जल्प्या चासुतृप उक्तशासश्चरन्ति ||
na taṁ vidātha ya imā jajānānyad yuṣmākam antaram babhūva | nīhāreṇa prāvṛtā jalpyā cāsutṛpa ukthaśāsaś caranti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na: not
    • taṁ: him/that
    • vidātha: you know
    • ya: who
    • imā: these
    • jajānā: have been born
    • anyad: other
    • yuṣmākam: your
    • antaram: between
    • babhūva: has become
    • nīhāreṇa: with mist
    • prāvṛtā: having spread
    • jalpyā: with water
    • cā: and
    • asutṛpa: of great strength
    • ukthaśāsaś: of the utterance of mantras
    • caranti: they move
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the interconnectedness of existence and the divine.
    • It suggests that there is a knowledge that transcends individual understanding, emphasizing unity.
    • The imagery of mist and water symbolizes the nurturing aspects of nature, which are essential for life.
    • The reference to mantras indicates the power of sound and intention in shaping reality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, the collective knowledge and experiences of individuals create a stronger foundation than isolated knowledge.
    • Environmental awareness can be seen as a modern application of the nurturing aspect of nature, where water conservation efforts reflect the importance of water in sustaining life.
    • In spiritual practices, chanting mantras is believed to create vibrations that can influence one's surroundings and inner state, showcasing the power of sound.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity, the importance of nature, and the power of intention.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios such as community building, environmental conservation, and spiritual practices.
    • By recognizing our interconnectedness and the nurturing aspects of nature, we can foster a more harmonious existence.
