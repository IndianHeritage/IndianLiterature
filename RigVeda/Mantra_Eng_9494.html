इन्द्रप्रसूता वरुणप्रशिष्टा ये सूर्यस्य ज्योतिषो भागम् आनशुः |मरुद्गणे वृजने मनमा धीमहि माग्ने यज्ञं जनयन्त सूरयः ||
indraprasūtā varuṇapraśiṣṭā ye sūryasya jyotiṣo bhāgam ānaśuḥ | marudgaṇe vṛjane manma dhīmahi māghone yajñaṁ janayanta sūrayaḥ ||
  • Indraprasūtā: This term refers to that which is born of Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition. Indra symbolizes strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • Varuṇapraśiṣṭā: This means that which is bestowed by Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water. Varuna represents the moral and ethical aspects of the universe.
  • Ye sūryasya jyotiṣo bhāgam ānaśuḥ: This phrase translates to "those who partake in the light of the sun." The sun is a symbol of knowledge, enlightenment, and life-giving energy.
  • Marudgaṇe: Refers to the Maruts, the storm deities associated with wind and storms, representing the dynamic and ever-changing aspects of nature.
  • Vṛjane: This term means to remove or to dispel, indicating a desire to eliminate negativity or obstacles.
  • Manma dhīmahi: This translates to "let us meditate upon my mind," suggesting a focus on introspection and self-awareness.
  • Māghone: Refers to the month of Māgha, which is associated with auspiciousness and rituals.
  • Yajñaṁ janayanta sūrayaḥ: This means "the wise ones who create the sacrifice." Yajña refers to a ritual of offerings, symbolizing the importance of community and shared purpose.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of divine forces, nature, and human consciousness. It calls for meditation and introspection while recognizing the importance of community and ethical living. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their thoughts and actions, striving for a balance between strength (Indra) and moral integrity (Varuna).
  • In community settings, the mantra encourages collective efforts towards positive actions, akin to the yajña, fostering unity and shared goals.
  • In environmental consciousness, recognizing the role of natural forces (Maruts) can inspire sustainable practices and respect for the earth.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of aligning oneself with higher principles, fostering community, and engaging in self-reflection for a harmonious life.
