यत तुदत सूर एतशं वङ्कू वातस्य पर्णिना | वहत कुत्सम आरजुनेयं शतक्रतुः त्सरद गन्धर्वम् अस्तृतम् ||
yat tudat sūra etaśaṁ vaṅkū vātasya parṇinā | vahat kutsam ārjuneyaṁ śatakratuḥ tsarad gandharvam astṛtam ||
  • yat tudat sūra etaśaṁ vaṅkū vātasya parṇinā: This phrase can be translated to "What the brave ones have achieved, may the wind carry it like the wings of a bird." Here, "sūra" refers to the brave or the strong, while "vaṅkū" symbolizes the wings of a bird, representing freedom and the ability to soar high. The wind ("vāta") is a metaphor for the forces of nature that help in the journey of life.
  • vahat kutsam ārjuneyaṁ: This part means "may it carry away the obstacles." "Kutsam" refers to difficulties or hindrances, and "ārjuneyaṁ" can be interpreted as something that is to be overcome or removed. This suggests a plea for the removal of challenges that hinder progress.
  • śatakratuḥ tsarad gandharvam astṛtam: This translates to "the one with a hundred powers, may the celestial beings be pleased." "Śatakratuḥ" refers to someone with immense strength or capabilities, often associated with divine beings. "Gandharvam" refers to celestial musicians or beings, indicating a connection to the divine or spiritual realm. "Astṛtam" implies that this is a call for blessings or support from these higher powers.

  • Real-Life Example: In a real-life scenario, this mantra can be applied to individuals facing challenges in their careers or personal lives. For instance, a student preparing for exams may recite this mantra to seek strength and support in overcoming academic hurdles. The imagery of the wind carrying away obstacles can inspire them to let go of their fears and doubts.
  • Significance: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance and the empowerment of individuals to overcome challenges. It emphasizes the importance of courage and the belief that with the right support, one can rise above difficulties.
  • Application: This mantra can be applicable in various situations, such as during times of personal struggle, in professional settings when facing competition, or even in community efforts to overcome social issues. It serves as a reminder that with bravery and divine support, one can navigate through life's challenges successfully.

  • Conclusion: In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength and support from both the divine and the natural world. It encourages individuals to embrace their inner courage and trust in the forces that help them overcome obstacles. By invoking this mantra, one can find motivation and reassurance in their journey, making it a powerful tool for personal growth and resilience.
