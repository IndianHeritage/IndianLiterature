वसिष्ठं ह वरुणो नावी आढाद ऋषिं चकार स्वपां महोभिः |स्तोतारं विप्रः सुदिनत्वे अह्नां यान नु dyāvas तातनन याद उषासः ||
vasiṣṭhaṁ ha varuṇo nāvy ādhād ṛṣiṁ cakāra svapā mahobhiḥ | stotāraṁ vipraḥ sudinatve ahnāṁ yān nu dyāvas tatanan yād uṣāsaḥ ||
  • vasiṣṭhaṁ: This refers to the sage Vasistha, who is known for his wisdom and knowledge. In real life, we can relate this to individuals who are wise and serve as guides in our lives.
  • ha: This is an affirmation, similar to saying "indeed" or "certainly." It emphasizes the importance of what follows.
  • varuṇo: Varuna is the god of water and the celestial ocean, representing the moral order and cosmic law. In our lives, this can symbolize the importance of ethics and the natural laws that govern our existence.
  • nāvy: This term means "not" or "without," indicating a negation or absence. It can remind us of the importance of recognizing what is lacking in our lives.
  • ādhād: This means "to support" or "to uphold." In practical terms, it can refer to the support systems we have in our lives, such as family, friends, and community.
  • ṛṣiṁ: This refers to a sage or seer, someone who has attained a higher level of consciousness. In modern life, this can be likened to mentors or spiritual leaders who guide us on our paths.
  • cakāra: This means "to create" or "to make." It signifies the act of bringing something into existence, akin to how we create our realities through our actions and thoughts.
  • svapā: This term means "dream" or "vision." It highlights the importance of aspirations and goals in our lives, encouraging us to pursue our dreams.
  • mahobhiḥ: This means "with great strength" or "powerfully." It emphasizes the need for determination and resilience in achieving our goals.
  • stotāraṁ: This refers to a praiser or one who offers praise. In our lives, this can be seen in the importance of gratitude and recognition of the good in our lives.
  • vipraḥ: This means "the learned" or "the wise." It signifies the value of knowledge and education in our personal and professional growth.
  • sudinatve: This term means "in good fortune" or "auspiciousness." It reminds us to seek positivity and good fortune in our endeavors.
  • ahnāṁ: This means "of the day" or "daily." It emphasizes the importance of living in the present and making the most of each day.
  • yān: This means "who" or "that which." It connects the previous ideas to the subject being discussed.
  • nu: This is an interrogative particle, often used to introduce a question or inquiry. It encourages us to reflect on our circumstances and seek deeper understanding.
  • dyāvas: This refers to the heavens or the sky, symbolizing the vastness of possibilities and the connection to the divine.
  • tatanan: This means "to stretch" or "to expand." It signifies growth and the potential for development in our lives.
  • yād: This means "where" or "when," indicating a point of reference in our exploration of life.
  • uṣāsaḥ: This refers to the dawn or the new beginning, symbolizing hope and the opportunity for renewal each day brings.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, support, and the pursuit of dreams. It emphasizes the importance of ethical living, the guidance of wise individuals, and the power of resilience in achieving our goals. In real life, we can apply these teachings by seeking wisdom from mentors, supporting one another in our journeys, and remaining hopeful for new beginnings. The mantra serves as a reminder to live with intention, embrace our aspirations, and recognize the interconnectedness of all beings in the cosmic order.
