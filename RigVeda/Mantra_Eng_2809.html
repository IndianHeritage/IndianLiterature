इन्द्रियाणि शतक्रतो या ते जनेषु पञ्चसु |इन्द्र तानि त आ वृणे ||
indriyāṇi śatakrato yā te janeṣu pañcasu | indra tāni ta ā vṛṇe ||
  • Indriyāṇi: This term refers to the senses or faculties of perception. In a broader context, it signifies the various ways through which we experience the world around us.
  • Śatakrato: This is an epithet of Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles. The term can also imply someone who has a hundred powers or capabilities.
  • Yā te: This phrase translates to "that which is yours" or "that which belongs to you," indicating a connection or possession.
  • Janeṣu: This word means "among people" or "in the community," highlighting the social aspect of human existence.
  • Pañcasu: This refers to the five senses, which are sight, hearing, touch, taste, and smell. These senses are crucial for our interaction with the world.
  • Indra: Again, this refers to the deity Indra, symbolizing strength and the ability to harness the powers of the senses.
  • Tāni: This means "those" or "them," referring back to the senses mentioned earlier.
  • Ta ā vṛṇe: This translates to "I choose them" or "I select them," indicating a conscious decision to engage with the senses in a particular way.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Indra, asking for the ability to choose and utilize the five senses wisely among people. It emphasizes the importance of perception and awareness in our interactions with the world and others.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Mindfulness: In today's fast-paced world, being mindful of our senses can enhance our experiences. For example, when eating, focusing on the taste, texture, and aroma of food can lead to greater enjoyment and appreciation.
  • Interpersonal Relationships: Understanding and being aware of how we perceive others through our senses can improve communication and empathy. For instance, being attentive to body language (a visual sense) can help us better understand someone's feelings.
  • Decision Making: The mantra encourages us to consciously choose how we engage with our senses. In a situation where we are overwhelmed by information, such as during a debate, we can choose to listen carefully (auditory sense) rather than react impulsively.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for conscious engagement with our senses. By invoking the strength of Indra, we are reminded of the power we hold in shaping our experiences and interactions. In a world filled with distractions, this mantra serves as a guide to harness our senses effectively, leading to a more fulfilling and aware life.
