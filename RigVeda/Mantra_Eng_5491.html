उवोचिथ हि मगवन देश्णं महो अर्बस्य वसुनो विभागे |उभा ते पूर्णा वसुनाऽगभस्तीन न सूनृताऽनि यमते वसव्याः ||
uvocitha hi maghavan deṣṇam maho arbhasya vasuno vibhāge | ubhā te pūrṇā vasunā gabhastī na sūnṛtā ni yamate vasavyā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uvocitha: You have spoken or declared
    • hi: indeed
    • maghavan: O Maghavan (a name for Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms)
    • deṣṇam: the place or region
    • maho: of the great
    • arbhasya: of the strong or powerful
    • vasuno: of the wealth or riches
    • vibhāge: in the distribution or sharing
    • ubhā: both
    • te: your
    • pūrṇā: full or complete
    • vasunā: with wealth
    • gabhastī: the light or brilliance
    • na: not
    • sūnṛtā: false or untrue
    • ni: in
    • yamate: you control or govern
    • vasavyā: of the wealth or riches
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Maghavan (Indra), invoking his power and influence over wealth and prosperity.
    • The speaker acknowledges the importance of sharing and distributing wealth fairly and justly.
    • It emphasizes the idea that both the giver and the receiver should be fulfilled and enriched through this process.
    • The mention of light (gabhastī) suggests that truth and clarity should guide the distribution of wealth, ensuring that it is not tainted by falsehood.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to corporate social responsibility, where companies are encouraged to share their wealth with the community, ensuring that both the business and society benefit.
    • In personal relationships, it can be seen in the act of sharing resources, such as time and money, with family and friends, fostering a sense of community and support.
    • In governance, it reflects the importance of equitable distribution of resources, where leaders are called to ensure that wealth is shared among all citizens, promoting social justice.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wealth distribution and the moral responsibility that comes with it.
    • It serves as a reminder that true prosperity is achieved when wealth is shared, and that clarity and truth should guide our actions.
    • In today's world, where inequality is a pressing issue, the teachings of this mantra can inspire individuals and organizations to act with integrity and compassion in their dealings with wealth.
