उतो विहुत्मतीनां विश्वां ववर्जुशीणाम् |विश्वा इत ते धेनवो दुह्र आशीरं घृतं दुह्रत आशीरम् ||
uto vihutmatīnāṁ viśāṁ vavarjuṣīṇām | viśvā it te dhenavo duhra āśiraṁ ghṛtaṁ duhrata āśiram ||
  • uto - This word can be interpreted as "from above" or "from the heavens." It signifies a divine or higher source of inspiration and blessings.
  • vihutmatīnāṁ - This term refers to those who are wise or possess great intellect. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom in guiding actions and decisions.
  • viśāṁ - This word means "of the people" or "of the community." It highlights the collective aspect of society and the interconnectedness of individuals.
  • vavarjuṣīṇām - This term can be understood as "those who are protected" or "those who are shielded." It suggests a sense of safety and security provided by divine forces.
  • viśvā - This word translates to "all" or "universal." It indicates inclusivity and the idea that the blessings or resources are available to everyone.
  • it - This is a demonstrative pronoun meaning "this" or "that," pointing towards the blessings being discussed.
  • dhenavo - This term means "cows" or "nourishing resources." In the Vedic context, cows symbolize wealth, nourishment, and abundance.
  • duhra - This word means "double" or "twice," suggesting an abundance or a doubling of blessings.
  • āśiraṁ - This term refers to "blessings" or "auspiciousness." It signifies the positive outcomes that arise from divine favor.
  • ghṛtaṁ - This word means "ghee" or "clarified butter," which is considered a sacred and purifying substance in Vedic rituals.
  • duhrata - This term can be interpreted as "to produce" or "to generate," indicating the act of bringing forth blessings or resources.
  • āśiram - Similar to the earlier mention, this word again refers to "blessings" or "auspiciousness," reinforcing the theme of divine favor.

The mantra essentially invokes blessings from a higher source for the community, emphasizing the importance of wisdom and protection. It speaks of universal nourishment and abundance, symbolized by cows and ghee, which are vital for sustenance and rituals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Community Support: Just as the mantra emphasizes the collective well-being of the community, it encourages individuals to support one another, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.
  • Wisdom in Decision-Making: The reference to wisdom highlights the importance of informed choices in personal and professional life. Seeking guidance from knowledgeable individuals can lead to better outcomes.
  • Abundance Mindset: The imagery of nourishment and blessings encourages an abundance mindset, where individuals focus on the positive aspects of life and share their resources with others.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals within a community and the importance of seeking wisdom and divine blessings for collective prosperity and well-being. It encourages a mindset of abundance, support, and protection, applicable in various aspects of life, from personal relationships to community initiatives.
