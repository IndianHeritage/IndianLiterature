आ नः सोम स्वध्वर इयानो अत्यो न तोषते |यं ते स्वदावन स्वदन्ति गूर्तयः पौरे चन्दयसे हवम् ||
ā naḥ some svadhvara iyāno atyo na tośate | yaṁ te svadāvan svadanti gūrtayaḥ paure chandayase havam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā naḥ: "May it come to us" - a call for blessings or positive energy.
    • some: "from the moon" - often symbolizes nourishment, tranquility, and the mind.
    • svadhvara: "with our own strength" - emphasizes self-reliance and personal power.
    • iyāno: "may we be led" - a desire for guidance and direction.
    • atyō: "beyond" - signifies transcending limitations or obstacles.
    • na tośate: "not to be displeased" - a wish for harmony and satisfaction.
    • yaṁ: "whom" - refers to a divine entity or force.
    • te: "your" - indicating possession or connection to the divine.
    • svadāvan: "who is sweet" - represents the pleasant and benevolent nature of the divine.
    • svadanti: "who delights" - signifies joy and fulfillment.
    • gūrtayaḥ: "the wise ones" - refers to enlightened beings or sages.
    • paure: "in the city" - symbolizes community or society.
    • chandayase: "you inspire" - indicates the divine influence on human actions.
    • havam: "our offering" - represents devotion and the act of giving.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for guidance and blessings from a higher power, particularly associated with the moon, which is often seen as a source of nourishment and tranquility.
    • It emphasizes the importance of self-reliance and personal strength while seeking divine guidance to overcome challenges.
    • The mantra also highlights the desire for harmony and satisfaction in life, both personally and within the community.
    • It acknowledges the wisdom of enlightened beings and the influence of the divine in inspiring human actions and offerings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging situation, such as a job interview, one might recite this mantra to invoke confidence and seek guidance, reminding oneself of their own strengths while hoping for a positive outcome.
    • During community gatherings or festivals, this mantra can be chanted to foster a sense of unity and collective joy, encouraging everyone to contribute positively to the environment.
    • In personal development practices, individuals may reflect on this mantra to cultivate self-awareness and seek inspiration from wise mentors or spiritual guides.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of self, community, and the divine.
    • Its significance lies in the balance between self-reliance and seeking guidance, promoting a harmonious existence.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to embrace their strengths while remaining open to divine inspiration and the wisdom of others, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and enriched life.
