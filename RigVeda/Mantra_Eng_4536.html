अग्नि युक्ष्वा हि ये तवाश्वासो देव साधवः |अरं वहन्ति मन्यवे ||
agne yukṣvā hi ye tavāśvāso deva sādhavaḥ | araṁ vahanti manyave ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • Yukṣvā: This term means "to harness" or "to direct." It implies the act of channeling energy or intention towards a specific goal.
  • Hi: A particle that emphasizes the preceding statement, often translated as "indeed" or "certainly."
  • Ye: This means "those who" or "whoever," indicating a group of people or entities.
  • Tavāśvāso: This compound word can be broken down into "tava" (your) and "āśvāsa" (breath or life force). It signifies the vital energy or essence that comes from Agni.
  • Deva: This translates to "god" or "divine being," representing the higher powers or forces in the universe.
  • Sādhavaḥ: This term means "those who are virtuous" or "righteous." It refers to individuals who embody moral and ethical values.
  • Araṁ: This word means "to carry" or "to bring forth." It suggests the act of delivering or manifesting something.
  • Vahanti: This means "they carry" or "they convey," indicating the action of transporting or transmitting energy or intention.
  • Manyave: This term translates to "with thought" or "with intention." It emphasizes the importance of mental focus and clarity in actions.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to Agni, asking for the divine fire to be harnessed and directed by those who are virtuous, so that they may carry forth their intentions with clarity and purpose. It highlights the significance of aligning one's actions with higher moral values and the power of focused thought.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Development: Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to channel their inner energy and intentions towards personal growth and ethical living.
  • Leadership: Leaders can embody the principles of this mantra by guiding their teams with integrity and purpose, ensuring that their actions are aligned with a greater good.
  • Rituals and Ceremonies: In spiritual practices, invoking Agni can symbolize the purification of intentions and the commitment to righteous actions.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for harnessing divine energy through virtuous actions and focused thought. It serves as a reminder that our intentions and actions should be aligned with higher principles, ultimately leading to personal and collective transformation. By embodying these values, individuals can create a positive impact in their lives and the lives of others.
