पवमान सुवीर्यं रयिं सोम रिरिहि नः |इन्दव इन्द्रेण नो युजा ||
pavamāna suvīryaṁ rayiṁ soma rirīhi naḥ | indav indreṇa no yujā ||
  • pavamāna: This term refers to the "purifying" or "cleansing" aspect. In a real-life context, it can be likened to the process of self-reflection and personal growth, where one seeks to cleanse their mind and spirit of negativity.
  • suvīryaṁ: This means "good strength" or "noble vigor." It emphasizes the importance of having a strong character and moral integrity. For example, a leader who stands firm in their values during challenging times exemplifies suvīryaṁ.
  • rayiṁ: This word translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional abundance. In modern life, this can be seen in individuals who seek a balanced life filled with love, happiness, and fulfillment, rather than just financial success.
  • soma: Soma is often associated with the divine nectar or elixir that brings joy and immortality. In practical terms, it can represent the pursuit of happiness and well-being through healthy living, mindfulness, and positive relationships.
  • rirīhi: This term means "to desire" or "to wish for." It reflects the human tendency to aspire for betterment and fulfillment in life. For instance, someone striving for personal goals or dreams embodies this desire.
  • naḥ: This means "for us" or "to us." It indicates a collective aspect, suggesting that the blessings sought are not just for the individual but for the community or society as a whole.
  • indav: This term refers to "the divine" or "the gods." It highlights the connection between the human and the divine, suggesting that seeking guidance and support from higher powers is essential in our endeavors.
  • indreṇa: This means "with Indra," who is often seen as the king of the gods and a symbol of strength and power. In real life, this can be interpreted as aligning oneself with powerful forces or principles that guide us towards success.
  • no yujā: This phrase translates to "may we be united" or "may we be connected." It emphasizes the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving goals. For example, teamwork in a workplace or community efforts in social causes reflect this unity.

Conclusion: The mantra "pavamāna suvīryaṁ rayiṁ soma rirīhi naḥ | indav indreṇa no yujā ||" encapsulates the essence of seeking purification, strength, prosperity, and divine guidance while emphasizing the importance of unity and collective aspiration. In real life, it serves as a reminder to strive for personal and communal growth, to seek happiness and fulfillment, and to align ourselves with higher principles and values. This mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, leadership, community building, and spiritual practices, guiding individuals and societies towards a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
