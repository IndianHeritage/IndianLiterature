उपेदम् उपपरचनम् आसु गोषूप पृच्यताम् |उप ऋषभस्य रेतस्य उपेन्द्र तव वीर्ये ||
upedam upaparcanam āsu goṣūpa pṛcyatām | upa ṛṣabhasya retasy upendra tava vīrye ||
  • Mantra: "upedam upaparcanam āsu goṣūpa pṛcyatām | upa ṛṣabhasya retasy upendra tava vīrye ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • upedam: This can be interpreted as "to approach" or "to come near."
    • upaparcanam: This refers to "the act of worship" or "adoration."
    • āsu: Meaning "quickly" or "swiftly."
    • goṣūpa: This can be understood as "in the midst of the cows" or "among the herd."
    • pṛcyatām: This means "may it be asked" or "let it be inquired."
    • upa: Again, "near" or "approaching."
    • ṛṣabhasya: Referring to "the bull" or "the strong one," often symbolizing strength and virility.
    • retasy: This translates to "of the seed" or "of the essence."
    • upendra: A name for Indra, often associated with power and strength.
    • tava: Meaning "your."
    • vīrye: This means "in strength" or "in valor."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of approaching divinity with reverence and seeking blessings swiftly.
    • The mention of "cows" symbolizes nourishment and prosperity, indicating that the act of worship should be done in a manner that promotes abundance.
    • The reference to "the bull" signifies strength and virility, suggesting that one should embody these qualities in their pursuits.
    • By invoking "Indra," the mantra calls upon divine strength and valor, encouraging individuals to seek empowerment in their endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers may recite this mantra before the harvest to seek blessings for a bountiful yield, symbolizing the connection between worship and prosperity.
    • In personal development, individuals may reflect on the qualities of strength and valor in their lives, using the mantra as a reminder to approach challenges with confidence.
    • During community gatherings, this mantra can be recited to foster a sense of unity and shared purpose, invoking the strength of the collective.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of approaching life with reverence and seeking strength from the divine.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement to embody qualities of strength, valor, and abundance in our daily lives.
    • By integrating the teachings of this mantra into real-life scenarios, individuals can cultivate a mindset that promotes growth, resilience, and community.
