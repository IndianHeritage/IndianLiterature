त्वद विप्रो जायते वाज्य अग्ने त्वद वीरासो अभिमातिषाहः |वैश्वानर त्वं अस्मासु धेहि वसूनि राजन स्पृहयाय्याणि ||
tvad vipro jāyate vājy agne tvad vīrāso abhimātiṣāhaḥ | vaiśvānara tvam asmāsu dhehi vasūni rājan spṛhayāyyāṇi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvad: Your
    • vipro: Wise or learned one
    • jāyate: Is born or arises
    • vājy: Through the power of the sacrifice
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • tvad: Your
    • vīrāso: Heroes or strong ones
    • abhimātiṣāhaḥ: Who are endowed with strength and valor
    • vaiśvānara: Universal or all-pervading
    • tvam: You
    • asmāsu: In us
    • dhehi: Bestow or grant
    • vasūni: Wealth or good things
    • rājan: O King
    • spṛhayāyyāṇi: Desirable or worthy of aspiration
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals. It expresses a desire for the emergence of wisdom and strength through the divine fire, asking Agni to bestow wealth and desirable qualities upon us.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often invoke the spirit of Agni by fostering a culture of innovation and transformation, encouraging employees to embrace change and seek wisdom in their decisions.
    • In personal development, individuals may meditate on this mantra to inspire courage and strength in overcoming challenges, seeking to embody the qualities of the 'vīrāso' or heroes in their own lives.
    • During community gatherings or rituals, this mantra can be recited to invoke collective strength and prosperity, aiming to unite individuals towards a common goal of growth and abundance.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance and empowerment. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom, strength, and the aspiration for prosperity in both individual and collective endeavors. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in personal growth, leadership, and community building, reminding us to seek the divine presence in our actions and aspirations.
