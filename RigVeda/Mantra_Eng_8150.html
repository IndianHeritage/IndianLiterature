इषमूरजं च पिन्वस इन्द्राय मत्सरिण्तमः |चमूष्वा नि शीदसि ||
iṣam ūrjaṁ ca pinvasa indrāya matsarintamaḥ | camūṣv ā ni ṣīdasi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • iṣam: strength or power
    • ūrjaṁ: energy or vitality
    • ca: and
    • pinvasa: bestow or grant
    • indrāya: to Indra (the king of gods)
    • matsarintamaḥ: the one who is free from envy or jealousy
    • camūṣv: among the armies or groups
    • ā: towards
    • ni: down or in
    • ṣīdasi: you sit or reside
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra invokes the divine energy and strength, asking for the blessings of Indra, who is considered the god of rain, thunder, and war. It emphasizes the importance of vitality and power, free from jealousy, and seeks to channel these qualities into a collective force, represented by the armies or groups.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Leadership: In a leadership context, this mantra can be applied to inspire teams. A leader who embodies strength and energy, while remaining free from jealousy, can foster a positive environment where collaboration thrives.
    • Sports Teams: In sports, teams often invoke a sense of unity and strength. This mantra can symbolize the collective energy of players working together, free from individual envy, to achieve a common goal.
    • Community Building: In community initiatives, the mantra can represent the collective effort of individuals coming together, channeling their strengths and energies towards a shared purpose, while ensuring that personal rivalries do not hinder progress.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength and unity, free from negative emotions like jealousy. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder of the power of collective effort and the importance of fostering an environment where individuals can thrive together. Whether in leadership, sports, or community building, the principles encapsulated in this mantra can guide individuals and groups towards achieving their goals harmoniously.
