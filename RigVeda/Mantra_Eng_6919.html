यस्ते रेवाम् अदाशुरिः प्रममर्ष मघत्तये |तस्य नो वेदा आ भर ||
yas te revām̐ adāśuriḥ pramamarṣa maghattaye | tasya no veda ā bhara ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • revām̐: light or brilliance
    • adāśuriḥ: the one who is powerful or divine
    • pramamarṣa: has been pleased or satisfied
    • maghattaye: for the purpose of greatness or abundance
    • tasya: of that
    • no: our
    • veda: knowledge or wisdom
    • ā: towards
    • bhara: bring or bestow
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to a divine power, asking for the light and brilliance of that power to be bestowed upon us, particularly for the purpose of achieving greatness and abundance in our lives. It emphasizes the relationship between the divine and the seeker, highlighting the importance of divine favor in attaining knowledge and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, individuals often seek guidance and support from mentors or leaders who embody wisdom and success. This mantra reflects the idea that by aligning ourselves with those who possess knowledge and brilliance, we can enhance our own potential.
    • In personal development, people may engage in practices such as meditation or prayer to connect with a higher power, seeking clarity and abundance in their lives. This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of divine connection in achieving personal goals.
    • In community settings, leaders may invoke this mantra during gatherings to inspire collective growth and prosperity, emphasizing the need for divine support in their endeavors.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in the pursuit of knowledge and abundance. It teaches us that greatness is not solely achieved through individual effort but is also a result of divine grace and support. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal growth, professional development, and community leadership, reminding us to seek and acknowledge the higher powers that guide us towards our aspirations.
