शाक्मना शाको अरुणः सुपर्ण आ यो महः शूरः सनाद अनीलः |यच चिकेत सत्यम इति तन् न मोघं वसु स्पार्थम् उता जेतोत दाता ||
śākmanā śāko aruṇaḥ suparṇa ā yo mahaḥ śūraḥ sanād anīḻaḥ | yac ciketa satyam it tan na moghaṁ vasu spārham uta jetota dātā ||
  • śākmanā: This term can be interpreted as "with strength" or "powerful." It signifies the importance of inner strength and resilience in facing life's challenges.
  • śāko: This word refers to "branches" or "offshoots." It symbolizes growth, expansion, and the interconnectedness of life.
  • aruṇaḥ: Meaning "the reddish one" or "dawn," it represents new beginnings, hope, and the arrival of light after darkness.
  • suparṇa: This term translates to "well-winged" or "having good wings." It can symbolize freedom, elevation, and the ability to rise above difficulties.
  • ā yo: This phrase means "who is" or "that which is." It introduces a subject of significance, often referring to a divine or powerful entity.
  • mahaḥ: Meaning "great" or "mighty," it emphasizes the grandeur and importance of the subject being discussed.
  • śūraḥ: This word translates to "brave" or "heroic." It highlights the qualities of courage and valor in overcoming obstacles.
  • sanād: This term can mean "from the beginning" or "eternally." It suggests a timeless quality, indicating that the essence of the mantra is everlasting.
  • anīḻaḥ: Meaning "unmoving" or "stable," it represents steadfastness and reliability in the face of change.
  • yac: This word means "that which" or "whatever." It introduces a clause that elaborates on the qualities or actions of the subject.
  • ciketa: This term translates to "understood" or "perceived." It emphasizes the importance of awareness and comprehension in life.
  • satyam: Meaning "truth," it signifies the value of honesty and integrity in one's actions and thoughts.
  • it: This word means "that" or "thus," linking the previous concepts to the conclusion or outcome.
  • tan: This term translates to "that" or "it," referring back to the subject of the mantra.
  • na moghaṁ: Meaning "not in vain" or "not futile," it emphasizes that actions taken with purpose and intention yield meaningful results.
  • vasu: This word means "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the abundance that comes from living a life aligned with truth and strength.
  • spārham: Meaning "desirable" or "valuable," it highlights the importance of seeking what is truly beneficial in life.
  • uta: This term translates to "or" and serves to present an alternative or additional thought.
  • jetota: Meaning "victorious" or "conquering," it emphasizes the triumph that comes from perseverance and strength.
  • dātā: This word means "giver" or "donor," indicating the importance of generosity and sharing in achieving success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, truth, and resilience. It teaches us that by embodying these qualities, we can navigate life's challenges effectively. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership, and community building. For instance, a leader who demonstrates courage and integrity inspires their team to strive for excellence. Similarly, individuals who embrace their inner strength and seek truth can create meaningful relationships and contribute positively to society. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that our actions, rooted in strength and truth, lead to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
