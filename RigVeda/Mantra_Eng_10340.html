आ वां सुम्नैः शंयू इव मंहिष्ठा विश्ववेदसा |सम अस्मे भूषतं नरोत्सं न पिप्युषीर इषः ||
ā vāṁ sumnaiḥ śaṁyū iva maṁhiṣṭhā viśvavedasā | sam asme bhūṣataṁ narotsaṁ na pipyuṣīr iṣaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vāṁ: From us
    • sumnaiḥ: With auspiciousness
    • śaṁyū: Like a well-formed body
    • iva: Like
    • maṁhiṣṭhā: The best or most excellent
    • viśvavedasā: With universal knowledge
    • sam: Together
    • asme: We are
    • bhūṣataṁ: Adorned or decorated
    • narotsaṁ: Among the best of men
    • na: Not
    • pipyuṣīr: To be deprived
    • iṣaḥ: Of the essence or the best
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of auspiciousness and excellence in our lives.
    • It suggests that we should strive to be adorned with knowledge and virtues, akin to the best among men.
    • The phrase "not to be deprived of the essence" indicates the necessity of retaining our core values and wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, fostering a culture of collaboration and knowledge sharing can lead to a more productive environment, where everyone is adorned with the collective wisdom of the team.
    • In personal relationships, being supportive and uplifting towards one another can create a bond that reflects the essence of love and respect.
    • In education, students who engage in learning with enthusiasm and share their knowledge contribute to a vibrant academic community.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate auspiciousness and excellence in our lives.
    • It encourages us to seek knowledge and virtues, ensuring that we are not deprived of our core values.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, harmonious relationships, and a thriving community.
