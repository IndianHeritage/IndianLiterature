विओ ररप्श ऋषिभिर नवैभिर वृक्षो न पक्वः श्रिण्यो न जेताः |मर्यो न योषाम अभि मन्यमानो 'च्छा विवक्मि पुरुहूतम इन्द्रम् ||
vi yo rarapśa ṛṣibhir navebhir vṛkṣo na pakvaḥ sṛṇyo na jetā | maryo na yoṣām abhi manyamāno 'cchā vivakmi puruhūtam indram ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "He who is not ripe, who is not to be heard, who is not to be conquered, I do not consider him as a mortal, I speak of the many-formed Indra."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vi yo: He who is not.
    • rarapśa: Not ripe or mature.
    • ṛṣibhir: By the sages or seers.
    • navebhir: New or fresh.
    • vṛkṣo: Tree or being.
    • na pakvaḥ: Not ripe or developed.
    • sṛṇyo: Not to be heard.
    • na jetā: Not to be conquered.
    • maryo: Mortal or human.
    • na yoṣām: Not of women or feminine.
    • abhi manyamāno: Considering or regarding.
    • 'cchā: I speak.
    • vivakmi: I declare or express.
    • puruhūtam: Many-formed or diverse.
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods.
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra reflects the idea of recognizing the essence of maturity and development, both in nature and in human life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of discernment in understanding what is truly valuable and worthy of attention.
    • Indra, as a representation of strength and power, symbolizes the ultimate goal of achieving greatness and overcoming challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, this mantra can be applied to the journey of self-improvement. Just as a tree takes time to grow and bear fruit, individuals must cultivate their skills and knowledge before they can achieve their goals.
    • In a professional setting, recognizing the potential in new ideas or projects that may not yet be fully developed can lead to innovation and success.
    • In relationships, understanding that not everyone is ready to engage or contribute equally can help in managing expectations and fostering growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for awareness and discernment in our interactions with the world.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the process of growth and the importance of patience in achieving maturity.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages us to recognize the potential in ourselves and others, fostering an environment where growth and development can flourish.
