समुद्रेण सिन्धवो यादमानā इन्द्राय सोमं सुषुतं भरन्तः |अंशुं दूहन्ति हस्तिनो भरित्रैर मध्वः पुनन्ति धारया पवित्रैः ||
samudreṇa sindhavo yādamānā indrāya somaṁ suṣutam bharantaḥ | aṁśuṁ duhanti hastino bharitrair madhvaḥ punanti dhārayā pavitraiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "samudreṇa sindhavo yādamānā indrāya somaṁ suṣutam bharantaḥ | aṁśuṁ duhanti hastino bharitrair madhvaḥ punanti dhārayā pavitraiḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samudreṇa: with the ocean
    • sindhavo: of the rivers
    • yādamānā: flowing
    • indrāya: for Indra (the king of gods)
    • somaṁ: soma (a sacred drink)
    • suṣutam: well-prepared
    • bharantaḥ: carrying
    • aṁśuṁ: the light or energy
    • duhanti: they churn
    • hastino: of the elephants
    • bharitrair: with the strong
    • madhvaḥ: sweet (nectar)
    • punanti: purifying
    • dhārayā: by holding
    • pavitraiḥ: with the pure
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the rivers flowing into the ocean, symbolizing the interconnectedness of all life and the divine.
    • It highlights the importance of Indra, who represents strength and power, and the sacred soma, which is a symbol of spiritual nourishment.
    • The act of carrying and churning signifies effort and the process of transformation, much like how we must work to achieve our goals in life.
    • The mention of elephants suggests strength and wisdom, indicating that we should approach challenges with both power and intelligence.
    • The purification aspect emphasizes the need for clarity and purity in our actions and thoughts, akin to how we seek to cleanse our minds and spirits in daily life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, rivers flow into the ocean, demonstrating how individual efforts contribute to a greater whole, similar to how teamwork in a workplace leads to success.
    • The preparation of soma can be likened to the careful planning and execution of projects in our lives, where the outcome is a result of our dedication and hard work.
    • Just as elephants are revered for their strength and memory, we can draw on our own strengths and experiences to navigate life's challenges.
    • Purification rituals in various cultures remind us of the importance of mental and emotional clarity, which can be achieved through practices like meditation or mindfulness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of strength and wisdom, and the need for purity in our actions.
    • It encourages us to recognize our contributions to the greater whole and to approach our endeavors with dedication and clarity.
    • In real life, we can apply these teachings by working collaboratively, being mindful of our actions, and striving for personal and spiritual growth.
