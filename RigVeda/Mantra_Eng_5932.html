यो वर्धन ओषधीनां यो अपां यो विश्वस्य जगतो देव ईशे |स त्रिधातु शरणं शर्मा यंषत त्रिवर्तु ज्योतिः स्वभिष्ट्य अस्मे ||
yo vardhana oṣadhīnāṁ yo apāṁ yo viśvasya jagato deva īśe | sa tridhātu śaraṇaṁ śarma yaṁsat trivartu jyotiḥ svabhiṣṭy a1sme ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "He who nourishes the herbs, He who nourishes the waters, He who is the Lord of the entire universe, may He be our refuge, may He grant us protection, and may we attain the light of knowledge and well-being."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yo: He who
    • vardhana: nourishes or increases
    • oṣadhīnāṁ: of the herbs or plants
    • yo apāṁ: He who is of the waters
    • yo viśvasya jagato: He who is the Lord of the entire universe
    • deva īśe: divine lord
    • sa: may He
    • tridhātu: three elements (earth, water, fire)
    • śaraṇaṁ: refuge
    • śarma: protection or well-being
    • yaṁsat: may we attain
    • trivartu: threefold or triple
    • jyotiḥ: light or knowledge
    • svabhiṣṭy: well-being or prosperity
    • a1sme: for us
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of nature and its elements. For instance, in agriculture, farmers often pray for the nourishment of their crops (oṣadhīnāṁ) and the availability of water (apāṁ) to ensure a good harvest.
    • In a broader sense, this mantra can be applied to environmental conservation efforts. By recognizing the divine presence in nature, individuals and communities can be motivated to protect and preserve their surroundings.
    • In personal life, individuals can invoke this mantra during meditation or yoga practices to seek inner peace and clarity (trivartu jyotiḥ) that leads to overall well-being (svabhiṣṭy a1sme).
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of nature, the universe, and human existence. It serves as a reminder to seek refuge in the divine forces that govern the world and to strive for knowledge and well-being. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate a respectful relationship with nature, promote sustainability, and enhance personal growth through spiritual practices. By acknowledging the divine in all aspects of life, we can foster a sense of harmony and purpose.
