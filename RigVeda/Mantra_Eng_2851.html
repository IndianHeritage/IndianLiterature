इन्द्रं इत्त्था गीरो ममाच्छागुर इषिता इतः |आवृते सोमपीतये ||
indram itthā giro mamācchāgur iṣitā itaḥ | āvṛte somapītaye ||
  • Indram: This refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunder, and war. Indra symbolizes strength, power, and leadership.
  • Itthā: This word means "thus" or "in this manner," indicating a way of doing things or a method of approach.
  • Giro: This translates to "words" or "speech," emphasizing the importance of communication and expression in human interactions.
  • Mamācchāgur: This can be interpreted as "may I be protected" or "may I be shielded," suggesting a plea for safety and guidance.
  • Iṣitā: This means "desired" or "wished for," indicating aspirations or goals that one seeks to achieve.
  • Itaḥ: This translates to "from here" or "from this place," grounding the mantra in a specific context or situation.
  • Āvṛte: This means "enveloped" or "surrounded," suggesting a protective barrier or a nurturing environment.
  • Somapītaye: This refers to the "drinking of soma," a ritualistic drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight, symbolizing spiritual nourishment and enlightenment.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation to Indra, seeking protection and guidance through words and communication. It emphasizes the importance of expressing one's desires and aspirations while being enveloped in a nurturing and protective environment. The reference to soma highlights the need for spiritual sustenance in achieving one's goals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra is a symbol of strength and leadership, individuals in positions of authority can draw inspiration from this mantra to lead with wisdom and protect their teams.
  • Communication: The emphasis on words reminds us of the power of effective communication in personal and professional relationships. It encourages individuals to express their thoughts and feelings clearly.
  • Seeking Protection: In times of uncertainty or challenge, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek guidance and protection, whether through spiritual practices or support from loved ones.
  • Spiritual Growth: The reference to soma encourages individuals to seek spiritual nourishment through practices like meditation, yoga, or other forms of self-care that promote inner peace and clarity.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, protection, and spiritual nourishment through effective communication and expression of desires. It serves as a guiding principle for individuals navigating the complexities of life, reminding them of the importance of leadership, connection, and spiritual growth.
