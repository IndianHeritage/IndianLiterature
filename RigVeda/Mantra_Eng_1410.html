उत ते वयश्चिद्वसतेरपाप्तन् नरश्च ये पितुभाजो व्युष्टौ |अमा सते वहसि भूर्वामम् उषो देवी दाशुषे मार्त्याय ||
ut te vayaś cid vasater apaptan naraś ca ye pitubhājo vyuṣṭau | amā sate vahasi bhūri vāmam uṣo devi dāśuṣe martyāya ||
  • Mantra: "ut te vayaś cid vasater apaptan naraś ca ye pitubhājo vyuṣṭau | amā sate vahasi bhūri vāmam uṣo devi dāśuṣe martyāya ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ut: O!
    • te: your
    • vayaś: youth or vigor
    • cid: indeed
    • vasater: dwelling or residing
    • apaptan: those who are not deprived
    • naraś: men or humans
    • ca: and
    • ye: who
    • pitubhājo: partaking of the father
    • vyuṣṭau: in the light or dawn
    • amā: we
    • sate: being or existence
    • vahasi: you carry or bring
    • bhūri: abundant or plentiful
    • vāmam: your goodness or auspiciousness
    • uṣo: O Dawn
    • devi: goddess
    • dāśuṣe: for the mortals
    • martyāya: to the mortal beings
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the goddess of dawn, Uṣas, invoking her to bestow youth, vigor, and abundance upon those who are deserving.
    • The phrase "ut te vayaś cid vasater" emphasizes the importance of vitality and the essence of life that resides within us.
    • It acknowledges the connection between humans and their divine origins, as indicated by "naraś ca ye pitubhājo," suggesting that humans partake in the blessings of their forefathers.
    • The invocation of "uṣo devi" highlights the reverence for the dawn as a symbol of new beginnings, hope, and enlightenment.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be related to the daily rituals of waking up with gratitude, embracing the opportunities of a new day, and seeking blessings for health and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine blessings that enhance our physical and spiritual well-being.
    • It serves as a reminder to appreciate the gifts of life and to strive for a harmonious existence, both personally and in our communities.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during morning prayers, meditation, or any time one seeks to invoke positivity and abundance in their life.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to recognize their potential and the support they receive from the universe, fostering a sense of gratitude and purpose.
