इदाह्नः पीतिम उता वो मदं धुर न ऋते श्रान्तस्य सख्याय देवाः |ते नूनम अस्मे ऋभवो वसूनि तृतीये अस्मिन सवने दधात ||
idāhnaḥ pītim uta vo madaṁ dhur na ṛte śrāntasya sakhyāya devāḥ | te nūnam asme ṛbhavo vasūni tṛtīye asmin savane dadhāta ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • idāhnaḥ: This day
    • pītim: nourishment or satisfaction
    • uta: and
    • vo: your
    • madaṁ: joy or intoxication
    • dhuḥ: burden or weight
    • na: not
    • ṛte: without
    • śrāntasya: of the weary
    • sakhyāya: for friendship
    • devāḥ: the gods
    • te: they
    • nūnam: surely
    • asme: for us
    • ṛbhavo: the bountiful
    • vasūni: wealth or good things
    • tṛtīye: in the third
    • asmin: in this
    • savane: offering or sacrifice
    • dadhāta: may grant
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer for nourishment and joy from the divine.
    • It emphasizes the importance of friendship and support, especially for those who are weary.
    • The invocation of the gods suggests a reliance on higher powers for blessings and abundance.
    • The mention of "the third" could symbolize a specific time or phase in rituals, indicating a moment of significance in spiritual practices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be reflected in the way we seek support from friends and family during tough times.
    • It can also be seen in community gatherings where people come together to share resources and uplift each other.
    • In workplaces, fostering a supportive environment can lead to greater productivity and satisfaction, akin to the nourishment mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in achieving joy and abundance while emphasizing the value of companionship.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek and offer support to those around us, creating a cycle of positivity and strength.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages us to cultivate relationships and community bonds, ensuring that we do not face challenges alone.
