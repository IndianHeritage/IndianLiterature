पुरा यत सूरस तमसो अपीतस तम अद्रिवः फलिगं हेतिम अस्य | शुष्णस्य चित परिहितं यद ओजो दिवस परि सुग्रथितं तद आदः ||
purā yat sūras tamaso apītes tam adrivaḥ phaligaṁ hetim asya | śuṣṇasya cit parihitaṁ yad ojo divas pari sugrathitaṁ tad ādaḥ ||
  • Translation: "In the beginning, when the gods were born, they brought forth light from darkness. The source of this light is the energy that sustains life, and it is through this energy that the day is made bright and fruitful."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • purā: In the beginning, referring to the primordial state of existence.
    • yat: When, indicating a specific time or event.
    • sūras: Gods or divine beings, representing higher powers or forces.
    • tamaso: Darkness, symbolizing ignorance or chaos.
    • apītes: Brought forth, suggesting creation or emergence.
    • tam: That, referring back to darkness.
    • adrivaḥ: Light, representing knowledge, clarity, and enlightenment.
    • phaligaṁ: Source of fruitfulness, indicating the productive aspect of light.
    • hetim: Energy or cause, the driving force behind creation.
    • asya: Of this, referring to the light and energy.
    • śuṣṇasya: Of the day, symbolizing time and the cycle of life.
    • cit: Consciousness or awareness, the essence of being.
    • parihitaṁ: Established or set in place, indicating stability.
    • yad: That which, referring to the qualities of light.
    • ojo: Vital energy or strength, essential for life.
    • divas: Day, representing the cycle of time.
    • pari: All around, indicating completeness.
    • sugrathitaṁ: Made bright or fruitful, suggesting abundance.
    • tad: That, referring to the established light.
    • ādaḥ: Let it be, a call to action or affirmation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The transition from night to day can be seen as a metaphor for personal growth. Just as the sun rises and dispels darkness, individuals can overcome ignorance and challenges to achieve enlightenment and success.
    • In nature, the cycle of day and night is crucial for the growth of plants. Photosynthesis, which occurs during the day, is essential for producing food, symbolizing how light (knowledge) leads to sustenance (fruitfulness).
    • In a workplace, the collaboration of team members can be likened to the gods bringing forth light. When individuals work together, sharing knowledge and ideas, they create a productive environment that fosters innovation and success.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of light as a metaphor for knowledge, energy, and productivity. It teaches us that from darkness (ignorance) can emerge light (knowledge), leading to growth and abundance. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, teamwork, and the natural cycles of life. By embracing knowledge and collaboration, we can create a brighter, more fruitful existence.
