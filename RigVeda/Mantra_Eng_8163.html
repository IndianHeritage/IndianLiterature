मन्त्र: सुतā इन्द्राय वज्रीण सोमासो दध्याशिरः |पवित्रं अत्य अक्षरन् ||
sutā indrāya vajriṇe somāso dadhyāśiraḥ | pavitram aty akṣaran ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Sutā: Refers to the offerings or libations, often associated with the sacred drink, Soma.
    • Indrāya: Pertaining to Indra, the king of gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms.
    • Vajriṇe: The one who wields the thunderbolt, symbolizing strength and power.
    • Somāso: Related to Soma, which is both a deity and a sacred drink that represents immortality and divine inspiration.
    • Dadyaśiraḥ: To bestow or grant wisdom and intelligence.
    • Pavitram: Pure or sacred, indicating the divine nature of the offerings.
    • Aty: Beyond or transcending.
    • Akṣaran: Imperishable or eternal, signifying the everlasting nature of the divine.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra, asking for strength and wisdom through the sacred offerings of Soma.
    • It emphasizes the purity and eternal nature of the divine, suggesting that true wisdom and strength come from a connection with the sacred.
    • The mention of "vajriṇe" highlights the importance of power and protection, as Indra is often seen as a protector against chaos and evil.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a call for strength in facing challenges, much like how individuals seek inspiration and guidance during difficult times.
    • In a corporate setting, leaders may invoke the spirit of this mantra when making decisions that require wisdom and strength, ensuring that their actions are pure and beneficial for all stakeholders.
    • During personal struggles, individuals may reflect on the qualities of Indra, seeking to embody strength and resilience in their own lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and strength in our lives.
    • It teaches us that true power comes from purity of intention and connection to the sacred.
    • In various aspects of life, whether personal, professional, or spiritual, invoking the qualities represented in this mantra can lead to greater wisdom and resilience.
