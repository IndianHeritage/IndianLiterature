यस्य ते महिना महः परि ज्मायन्तम ईयतुः |हस्ताः वज्रं हिरण्ययम् ||
yasya te mahinā mahaḥ pari jmāyantam īyatuḥ | hastā vajraṁ hiraṇyayam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • te: your
    • mahinā: with great strength
    • mahaḥ: greatness or power
    • pari: all around
    • jmāyantam: who is born or created
    • īyatuḥ: may it come forth
    • hastā: hands
    • vajraṁ: thunderbolt or strength
    • hiraṇyayam: golden or precious
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine power and strength that is inherent in the universe.
    • It emphasizes the greatness that surrounds us and the potential for creation and manifestation.
    • The reference to "hands" and "thunderbolt" symbolizes the ability to act with strength and decisiveness.
    • The term "golden" suggests that this power is not only strong but also valuable and precious.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, this mantra can inspire individuals to harness their inner strength and capabilities to achieve their goals.
    • In leadership, it can serve as a reminder for leaders to act decisively and with integrity, using their power for the greater good.
    • In creative endeavors, it encourages artists and innovators to tap into their unique talents and bring forth their creations into the world.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call to recognize and utilize the inherent power within ourselves and the universe.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to leadership and creativity.
    • By embracing the strength and greatness that surrounds us, we can manifest our desires and contribute positively to the world.
