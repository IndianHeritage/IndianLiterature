अरण्योऽनिहितो जातवेदाः गर्भ इव सुदितो गर्भिणीषु |दिवे-दिवा ईड्यो जागृवद्भिर हविष्मद्भिर मनुष्येभिर अग्निः ||
araṇyor nihito jātavedā garbha iva sudhito garbhiṇīṣu | dive-diva īḍyo jāgṛvadbhir haviṣmadbhir manuṣyebhir agniḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • araṇyor: In the context of the mantra, it refers to the two worlds or realms.
    • nihito: Means 'placed' or 'situated.'
    • jātavedā: Refers to the 'one who knows all births' or the divine fire, Agni.
    • garbha: Means 'womb' or 'source,' symbolizing creation and potential.
    • iva: Translates to 'like' or 'as.'
    • sudhito: Means 'well-formed' or 'well-prepared.'
    • garbhiṇīṣu: Refers to 'in the wombs' or 'in the sources of creation.'
    • dive-diva: Means 'in the heavens' or 'in the day and night.'
    • īḍyo: Means 'worthy of worship' or 'adorable.'
    • jāgṛvadbhir: Refers to 'those who are awake' or 'those who are aware.'
    • haviṣmadbhir: Means 'with offerings' or 'with sacrifices.'
    • manuṣyebhir: Refers to 'by humans' or 'by mankind.'
    • agniḥ: Refers to 'Agni,' the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and divine presence.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of Agni, the divine fire, as a central figure in the cosmic order.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the physical and spiritual realms, suggesting that Agni resides in both.
    • The reference to 'wombs' signifies the nurturing aspect of creation, where potential is held before manifestation.
    • Agni is portrayed as a force that is worshipped by the awakened beings, highlighting the importance of awareness and intention in spiritual practices.
    • The mantra suggests that offerings made to Agni are not just physical but also represent the inner offerings of consciousness and awareness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, the concept of Agni can be seen in the transformative processes we undergo, such as personal growth and learning.
    • Just as Agni transforms offerings into energy, individuals can transform their experiences and challenges into wisdom and strength.
    • The idea of being 'awake' or 'aware' can be applied in mindfulness practices, where individuals cultivate awareness of their thoughts and actions.
    • In rituals and ceremonies, the act of making offerings symbolizes gratitude and recognition of the divine presence in everyday life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of Agni as a bridge between the material and spiritual worlds.
    • It teaches us the importance of awareness, intention, and transformation in our lives.
    • By recognizing the divine in our daily actions and being mindful of our offerings, we can cultivate a deeper connection with the universe.
    • The teachings of this mantra are applicable in various aspects of life, including personal development, spirituality, and community rituals.
