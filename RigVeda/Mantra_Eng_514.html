शं नः करत्यर्वते सुगं मेषाय मेष्ये |नृभ्यो नारिभ्यो गवे ||
śaṁ naḥ karaty arvate sugam meṣāya meṣye | nṛbhyo nāribhyo gave ||
  • śaṁ naḥ karaty: This phrase translates to "may peace be with us." The word "śaṁ" signifies peace, tranquility, and well-being. In real life, this can be understood as a wish for harmony in our lives, whether in personal relationships, work environments, or community interactions.
  • arvate: This term means "to be strong" or "to be powerful." It emphasizes the importance of strength, both physical and mental. For example, in a workplace, having a strong team that supports each other can lead to greater productivity and success.
  • sugam: This word means "easy" or "pleasant." It suggests that the path we take in life should be smooth and free from unnecessary obstacles. In practical terms, this can relate to making choices that lead to a fulfilling and enjoyable life, such as pursuing a career that aligns with one's passions.
  • meṣāya meṣye: "Meṣāya" refers to "the sheep" and "meṣye" can be interpreted as "to the sheep." In a broader sense, this can symbolize guidance and protection. Just as a shepherd cares for their flock, we should look out for one another in our communities, ensuring that everyone is safe and supported.
  • nṛbhyo nāribhyo gave: This part translates to "to men, to women, and to the cow." It highlights the importance of inclusivity and respect for all beings. In modern society, this can be seen in movements advocating for gender equality and animal rights, emphasizing that all lives deserve dignity and care.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic vision of peace, strength, ease, and care for all beings. Its significance lies in promoting harmony in our lives and communities. In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to foster supportive relationships, pursue fulfilling paths, and advocate for the well-being of all individuals and creatures. By embodying these principles, we contribute to a more peaceful and compassionate world.
