मन्त्र: येन चष्टे वरुणो मित्रो आर्यमा येन नासत्याः भागः |वयं तत्ते शवसां गातुवित्तमा इन्द्रत्वोता विद्हेमहि ||
yena caṣṭe varuṇo mitro aryamā yena nāsatyā bhagaḥ | vayaṁ tat te śavasā gātuvittamā indratvotā vidhemahi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yena: by whom
    • caṣṭe: moves or acts
    • varuṇo: Varuna (the god of cosmic order)
    • mitro: Mitra (the god of friendship and contracts)
    • aryamā: Aryama (a deity associated with the afterlife)
    • yena: by whom
    • nāsatyā: the Nasatyas (the twin gods, often associated with health and medicine)
    • bhagaḥ: fortune or prosperity
    • vayaṁ: we
    • tat: that
    • te: your
    • śavasā: with strength or vigor
    • gātuvittamā: the best of the givers
    • indratvotā: the power of Indra (the king of gods)
    • vidhemahi: we may attain or achieve
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine powers of various deities, emphasizing the interconnectedness of cosmic forces.
    • It highlights the importance of divine guidance in our actions and decisions, suggesting that we should seek the blessings of these gods for strength and prosperity.
    • For example, in a business context, one might seek the blessings of Varuna for ethical dealings, Mitra for partnerships, and the Nasatyas for health and well-being in the workplace.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be recited during important decisions or transitions, such as starting a new job, entering a partnership, or embarking on a new venture.
    • It serves as a reminder to align one's actions with higher principles and to seek support from the divine in achieving one's goals.
    • For instance, before a significant presentation or meeting, one might reflect on this mantra to invoke clarity, strength, and success.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance and the recognition of the interconnectedness of various forces in our lives.
    • By acknowledging the roles of different deities, we cultivate a sense of humility and gratitude, which can lead to greater success and fulfillment.
    • In essence, this mantra encourages us to act with integrity, seek support, and strive for prosperity in all our endeavors.
