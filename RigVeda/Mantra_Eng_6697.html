मन्त्र: उता नो गोमातस् कृधि हिरण्यवतः अश्विनः |इळाभिः सं रभेमहि ||
uta no gomatas kṛdhi hiraṇyavato aśvinaḥ | iḻābhiḥ saṁ rabhemahi ||
  • Mantra: "uta no gomatas kṛdhi hiraṇyavato aśvinaḥ | iḻābhiḥ saṁ rabhemahi ||"
  • Translation: "O Ashvins, may you, who are the givers of wealth, grant us the strength of the cows. With your blessings, may we be united with prosperity."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • uta: a call for attention or invocation.
    • no: our, indicating possession or belonging.
    • gomatas: related to cows, symbolizing wealth and nourishment in Vedic culture.
    • kṛdhi: to create or grant, a request for action.
    • hiraṇyavato: of gold or wealth, referring to prosperity.
    • aśvinaḥ: the Ashvins, twin deities associated with health, medicine, and prosperity.
    • iḻābhiḥ: with blessings or auspiciousness.
    • saṁ rabhemahi: may we be united or connected, expressing a desire for harmony and abundance.

  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra invokes the Ashvins, who are revered in Vedic literature as divine physicians and bringers of prosperity.
    • The mention of "gomatas" highlights the importance of cows in ancient Indian society, symbolizing wealth, sustenance, and nurturing.
    • The mantra reflects a desire for strength and prosperity, which can be interpreted as a holistic approach to well-being, encompassing physical, emotional, and material aspects of life.

  • Real-Life Applications:
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be recited during rituals or ceremonies aimed at invoking prosperity and health, such as housewarming or business openings.
    • It can serve as a reminder to appreciate the resources we have, like livestock or agricultural wealth, and to seek harmony in our relationships and endeavors.
    • In personal development, the mantra encourages individuals to strive for balance in their lives, seeking both material success and spiritual fulfillment.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for prosperity and strength, emphasizing the interconnectedness of wealth, health, and harmony.
    • Its significance lies in its timeless relevance, reminding us to cultivate gratitude and strive for balance in our pursuits.
    • By invoking the Ashvins, we align ourselves with the energies of abundance and well-being, applicable in various aspects of life, from personal growth to community welfare.
