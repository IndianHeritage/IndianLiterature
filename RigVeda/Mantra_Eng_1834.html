स्तुतासो नो मरुतो मृळयन्तूत स्तुतो मगवाः शम्भविष्टः |ऊर्ध्वा नः सन्तु कोम्याः वनानि अहानि विश्वा मरुतो जिगीषा ||
stutāso no maruto mṛḻayantūta stuto maghavā śambhaviṣṭhaḥ | ūrdhvā naḥ santu komyā vanāny ahāni viśvā maruto jigīṣā ||
  • stutāso no maruto mṛḻayantūta: This phrase can be translated as "May the Maruts, who are praised, bring us prosperity." The Maruts are storm deities in Vedic literature, often associated with strength and vitality. In real life, this can be seen as a call for the forces of nature to bless us with abundance and well-being.
  • stuto maghavā śambhaviṣṭhaḥ: This translates to "May the mighty one, who is praised, be favorable to us." Here, "maghavā" refers to the powerful and benevolent forces in the universe. In practical terms, this can be interpreted as seeking the support of influential and positive energies in our lives, such as mentors or leaders who can guide us towards success.
  • ūrdhvā naḥ santu komyā vanāny ahāni: This means "May the lofty trees and forests be ours." This line emphasizes the importance of nature and its resources. In a real-life context, it can be seen as a reminder to respect and protect our environment, as it provides us with essential resources for survival and well-being.
  • viśvā maruto jigīṣā: This translates to "May all the Maruts grant us victory." This line signifies the desire for triumph over challenges and obstacles. In everyday life, this can be related to striving for success in various endeavors, whether personal, professional, or spiritual.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep reverence for nature and the forces that govern our lives. It serves as a reminder to seek harmony with the environment and to acknowledge the powerful energies that can aid us in our pursuits. In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during times of struggle when we seek guidance and support, or when we engage in environmental conservation efforts. By invoking the blessings of the Maruts, we align ourselves with the forces of nature and the universe, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in our lives.
