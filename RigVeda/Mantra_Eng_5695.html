आ यातम् मित्रावरुणा जुषाणाव आहुतिं नराः |पातं सोमं ऋतावृद्धा ||
ā yātam mitrāvaruṇā juṣāṇāv āhutiṁ narā | pātaṁ somam ṛtāvṛdhā ||
  • Mantra: "ā yātam mitrāvaruṇā juṣāṇāv āhutiṁ narā | pātaṁ somam ṛtāvṛdhā ||"
  • Translation: "May the offerings made by men be accepted by Mitra and Varuna; may they drink the soma, which increases righteousness."

  • Key Terms:
    • ā yātam: This phrase can be interpreted as "may it come" or "may it be accepted."
    • mitrāvaruṇā: Refers to the deities Mitra and Varuna, who symbolize friendship, harmony, and cosmic order.
    • juṣāṇāv: Means "who are pleased" or "who enjoy," indicating the deities' acceptance of offerings.
    • āhutiṁ: Refers to the offerings or sacrifices made by individuals.
    • narā: Means "men" or "humans," emphasizing the role of humanity in the ritual.
    • pātaṁ: Translates to "may they drink," suggesting the act of receiving or accepting the offerings.
    • somam: Refers to soma, a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight.
    • ṛtāvṛdhā: Means "which increases righteousness" or "which promotes truth and order."

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with divine forces.
    • Mitra and Varuna represent the dual aspects of social order and cosmic law, highlighting the need for balance in human relationships and the universe.
    • The act of offering (āhutiṁ) is a way for individuals to express gratitude and seek blessings from the divine.
    • The reference to soma signifies the pursuit of higher consciousness and the quest for truth, which is essential for personal and societal growth.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, the principles of harmony and friendship represented by Mitra and Varuna can be seen in community-building efforts, where individuals come together to support one another.
    • Rituals, whether religious or secular, serve as a means of fostering connections and expressing shared values, similar to the offerings made in the mantra.
    • The pursuit of righteousness (ṛtāvṛdhā) can be reflected in ethical decision-making in business, where companies strive to operate with integrity and social responsibility.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine, emphasizing the importance of rituals in maintaining harmony and righteousness.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement of ethical living and the pursuit of truth, which are applicable in various aspects of life, from personal relationships to professional conduct.
    • By invoking the blessings of Mitra and Varuna, individuals can aspire to create a more just and harmonious society, reflecting the core values of the RigVeda in contemporary life.
