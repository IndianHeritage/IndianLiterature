ये वध्वश चन्द्रं वहतुः यक्ष्मा यान्ति जनाद् अनु |पुनस्तान यज्ञियाः देवाः नयन्तु यत आगताः ||
ye vadhvaś candraṁ vahatuṁ yakṣmā yanti janād anu | punas tān yajñiyā devā nayantu yata āgatāḥ ||
  • Translation: "May those who are endowed with strength and power, who are like the moon, lead us towards prosperity and well-being. May the divine beings guide us back to the path of righteousness and abundance."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: those who
    • vadhvaś: endowed with strength or power
    • candraṁ: moon, symbolizing beauty, calmness, and prosperity
    • vahatuṁ: may lead or guide
    • yakṣmā: prosperity or well-being
    • yanti: go or move towards
    • janād: people or community
    • anu: following or in accordance with
    • punas: again or once more
    • tān: those
    • yajñiyā: divine or sacred
    • devā: gods or divine beings
    • nayantu: may guide or lead
    • yata: because or as
    • āgatāḥ: those who have come or arrived
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, leaders who embody strength and wisdom can guide their people towards prosperity, much like the moon guiding travelers at night.
    • In personal life, individuals can seek guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders who help them navigate challenges and lead them towards a fulfilling life.
    • In times of crisis, communities often look to their leaders for direction, hoping they will lead them back to stability and abundance.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strong and wise leadership in guiding individuals and communities towards prosperity and well-being. It highlights the role of divine guidance in our lives, reminding us to seek support from higher powers and wise individuals. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community leadership, personal development, and spiritual guidance, reinforcing the idea that we are not alone in our journey and that support is available to help us thrive.
