उच छोचिषा सहसस् पुत्र स्तुतो बृहद् वयः शशमानेषु धेहि |रेवद् अग्नि विश्वामित्रेषु शं योर मर्मृज्मा ते तन्वं भूरी कृत्वः ||
uc chociṣā sahasas putra stuto bṛhad vayaḥ śaśamāneṣu dhehi | revad agne viśvāmitreṣu śaṁ yor marmṛjmā te tanva1m bhūri kṛtvaḥ ||
  • uc chociṣā: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we rise" or "may we be elevated." It signifies the aspiration for growth and enlightenment.
  • sahasas: This word means "with strength" or "with vigor." It emphasizes the importance of strength in achieving one's goals.
  • putra: Translated as "sons" or "children," it represents the next generation and the continuity of life and values.
  • stuto: This means "praised" or "worshipped," indicating the importance of recognition and reverence in our lives.
  • bṛhad: Meaning "great" or "vast," it refers to the expansive nature of existence and the universe.
  • vayaḥ: This translates to "age" or "time," highlighting the significance of time in our lives.
  • śaśamāneṣu: This term can be understood as "in the shining" or "in the bright," symbolizing clarity and enlightenment.
  • dhehi: Meaning "may you grant" or "may you bestow," it expresses a plea for blessings and support.
  • revad: This word can be interpreted as "to shine" or "to illuminate," suggesting the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom.
  • agne: Referring to "Agni," the fire god, it symbolizes transformation, energy, and the divine spark within us.
  • viśvāmitreṣu: This term refers to "in all beings" or "in all creatures," emphasizing the interconnectedness of life.
  • śaṁ: Meaning "peace" or "well-being," it signifies the ultimate goal of harmony in life.
  • yor: This translates to "of whom," indicating a connection to the divine or higher powers.
  • marmṛjmā: This can be understood as "may we attain" or "may we achieve," reflecting the desire for accomplishment.
  • te: Meaning "your," it denotes a relationship with the divine or the universe.
  • tanva1m: This word refers to "body" or "form," indicating the physical aspect of existence.
  • bhūri: Meaning "abundant" or "plentiful," it signifies the richness of life and experiences.
  • kṛtvaḥ: This translates to "having done" or "having achieved," reflecting the importance of action and effort.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound aspiration for growth, strength, and enlightenment in life. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring the divine within ourselves and in others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, family values, and community building. By striving for greatness and seeking blessings, we can foster a harmonious existence that benefits not only ourselves but also future generations. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, strength, and the pursuit of knowledge, which are essential for a fulfilling life.
