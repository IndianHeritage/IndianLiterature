अहम एतं गव्ययम् अश्व्यम् पशुम् पुरीषिणं सायकेन हिरण्ययम् |पुरू सहस्राणि शिशामि दाशुषे यन् मा सोमास उक्तिनो अमन्दिषुः ||
aham etaṁ gavyayam aśvyam paśum purīṣiṇaṁ sāyakenā hiraṇyayam | purū sahasrā ni śiśāmi dāśuṣe yan mā somāsa ukthino amandiṣuḥ ||
  • Translation: "I have this cow, the one that is a source of wealth, the one that is a protector of the herd, the one that is adorned with gold. I offer a thousand (gifts) to the one who is a servant, for the one who is a devotee of the Soma, the one who is in the midst of the offerings."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • aham: I
    • etaṁ: this
    • gavyayam: cow, a symbol of wealth and sustenance
    • aśvyam: the one that is a protector
    • paśum: animal or herd
    • purīṣiṇaṁ: adorned or decorated
    • sāyakenā: with arrows or offerings
    • hiraṇyayam: golden or valuable
    • purū: many or numerous
    • sahasrā: thousand
    • ni: not
    • śiśāmi: I offer
    • dāśuṣe: to the servant or devotee
    • yan: who
    • mā: not
    • somāsa: of the Soma, a sacred drink
    • ukthino: of the offerings
    • amandiṣuḥ: in the midst of the offerings
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wealth, protection, and devotion. The cow is often seen as a symbol of prosperity and nurturing in Vedic literature.
    • In real life, this can be related to the idea of valuing resources that provide sustenance and security, such as family, community, and nature.
    • The act of offering to the devotee signifies the importance of gratitude and recognition of those who serve and support us in our lives.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern society, this mantra can inspire individuals to appreciate the resources they have, whether it be their family, friends, or community, and to recognize the efforts of those who contribute to their well-being.
    • It encourages a sense of responsibility towards nurturing and protecting these resources, akin to how one would care for livestock.
    • Additionally, it highlights the importance of giving back to those who support us, fostering a culture of gratitude and reciprocity.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of wealth, protection, and devotion. It encourages individuals to recognize and appreciate the resources in their lives, to be grateful for the support they receive, and to actively contribute to the well-being of their community. By embodying these principles, one can cultivate a life of abundance and harmony.
