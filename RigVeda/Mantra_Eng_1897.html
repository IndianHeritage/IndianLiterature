मन्त्र: न यद् युवेते नीयुतः सुदानू उप स्वधाभिः सृजथः पुरंढिम् | प्रेषद् वेषद् वातो न सूरीर् आ महे ददे सुव्रतः न वाजम् ||
ni yad yuvethe niyutaḥ sudānū upa svadhābhiḥ sṛjathaḥ puraṁdhim | preṣad veṣad vāto na sūrir ā mahe dade suvrato na vājam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ni yad yuvethe: "When you are united" - This phrase emphasizes the importance of unity and collaboration.
    • niyutaḥ: "With great effort" - Suggests that achieving goals requires significant effort and dedication.
    • sudānū: "With good intentions" - Highlights the necessity of having positive motives in our actions.
    • upa svadhābhiḥ: "With offerings" - Refers to the act of giving or offering, which can be interpreted as sharing resources or knowledge.
    • sṛjathaḥ: "You create" - Indicates the act of creation, whether it be ideas, relationships, or physical structures.
    • puraṁdhim: "The great city" - Symbolizes a place of prosperity, community, and collective achievement.
    • preṣad: "As a messenger" - Implies the role of communication and the importance of conveying messages effectively.
    • veṣad: "In disguise" - Suggests that sometimes things are not as they appear, and one must look deeper.
    • vāto: "The wind" - Represents change and the unseen forces that influence our lives.
    • na sūrir: "Not a thief" - Emphasizes integrity and honesty in our actions.
    • ā mahe: "We are great" - A declaration of self-worth and potential.
    • dade: "Bestow" - The act of giving or sharing something valuable.
    • suvrato: "With good vows" - Refers to the importance of commitment to ethical principles.
    • na vājam: "Not the prize" - Suggests that the focus should not solely be on rewards but on the journey and values.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving common goals. In real life, this can be seen in team projects at work where individuals come together, each contributing their strengths to create something greater than themselves.
    • The idea of creating with good intentions and offerings can be applied in community service, where individuals come together to help those in need, fostering a sense of belonging and support.
    • The mention of integrity and honesty serves as a reminder in personal and professional relationships. For instance, in business, maintaining transparency builds trust and long-term partnerships.
    • The concept of not focusing solely on rewards but valuing the journey can be reflected in personal development. For example, individuals pursuing education should appreciate the learning process rather than just the degree.

Conclusion: This mantra from the RigVeda encapsulates the essence of collaboration, integrity, and the importance of good intentions in our actions. It serves as a guiding principle in various aspects of life, encouraging individuals to work together towards common goals while upholding ethical values. By applying these teachings, we can foster a more harmonious and productive society.
