इदं dyāvāpṛthivī सत्यं अस्तु पितर मातर यद इहोपब्रुवे वाम् |भूतं देवानाम् अवमे अवोभिर विद्यामेषं वृजनं जीरदानुम् ||
idaṁ dyāvāpṛthivī satyam astu pitar mātar yad ihopabruve vām | bhūtaṁ devānām avame avobhir vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum ||
  • Translation: "May this heaven and earth be true; O parents, whatever you have spoken to us, may it be so. Let us be aware of the essence of the beings, and let us be endowed with knowledge and wisdom."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • idaṁ: this
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: heaven and earth
    • satyam: truth
    • astu: may it be
    • pitar: parents
    • mātar: mother
    • yad: whatever
    • ihopabruve: you have spoken to us
    • vām: to us
    • bhūtaṁ: essence of beings
    • devānām: of the divine
    • avame: let us be aware
    • avobhir: endowed with
    • vidyāmeṣaṁ: knowledge and wisdom
    • vṛjanaṁ: let us be
    • jīradānum: endowed with life
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of truth and the connection between the divine and earthly realms.
    • It highlights the role of parents and the wisdom they impart, suggesting that their teachings should guide us in our lives.
    • The invocation of knowledge and awareness signifies the pursuit of understanding and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be a reminder to seek truth in our actions and relationships, fostering honesty and integrity.
    • It encourages individuals to value the teachings of their parents and elders, applying their wisdom to navigate challenges.
    • The call for knowledge and awareness can inspire lifelong learning and self-improvement, essential in both personal and professional growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a profound reminder of the interconnectedness of truth, wisdom, and the guidance of our ancestors.
    • By embodying its teachings, we can cultivate a life rooted in integrity, awareness, and respect for the wisdom passed down through generations.
