अपो यद अद्रिम पुरुहूत दर्दर आवीर भुवत सरमा पूर्व्यं ते |स नो नेतā वाजम् आ दर्षि भूरीं गोत्रा रुजन्न अंगिरोभिर गृणानः ||
apo yad adrim puruhūta dardar āvir bhuvat saramā pūrvyaṁ te | sa no netā vājam ā darṣi bhūriṁ gotrā rujann aṅgirobhir gṛṇānaḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O waters, you who are the source of all life, may you bring forth abundance and prosperity. May you, O divine waters, guide us towards our goals and grant us strength and nourishment."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apo: O waters
    • yad: who
    • adrim: the source of life
    • puruhūta: many offerings
    • dardar: may you bring forth
    • āvir: manifest
    • bhuvat: may you be
    • saramā: guide us
    • pūrvyaṁ: towards our goals
    • te: you
    • sa: may
    • no: us
    • netā: guide
    • vājam: strength
    • ā: towards
    • darṣi: grant
    • bhūriṁ: abundance
    • gotrā: prosperity
    • rujann: free from pain
    • aṅgirobhir: with blessings
    • gṛṇānaḥ: we sing
  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of water as a life-giving force. In many cultures, water is seen as sacred and essential for survival. The invocation of water in this mantra highlights its role in providing nourishment, abundance, and guidance in our lives.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In agriculture, water is crucial for crop growth. Farmers often pray for rain and healthy water sources to ensure a good harvest, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal development, individuals may seek guidance and strength in their endeavors, akin to how the mantra calls upon water to guide and nourish.
    • In community rituals, people may gather to celebrate and honor water sources, recognizing their vital role in sustaining life and promoting prosperity.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the essential role of natural elements like water. By acknowledging and respecting these forces, we can cultivate abundance, health, and guidance in our lives. It encourages us to seek harmony with nature and to appreciate the blessings that come from it.
