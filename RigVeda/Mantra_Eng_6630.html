अग्निरुक्ते पुरोहितो ग्रावाणो बर्हिरध्वरे |ऋचां यामि मरुतो ब्रह्मणसपतिं देवांऽवो वरेण्यम् ||
agnir ukthe purohito grāvāṇo barhir adhvare | ṛcā yāmi maruto brahmaṇas patiṁ devām̐ avo vareṇyam ||
  • Agnir: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying and transformative element in many cultures.
  • ukthe: This means "in the invocation" or "in the praise." It signifies the act of calling upon the divine.
  • purohito: This term translates to "the priest" or "the one who performs rituals." It emphasizes the role of a mediator between the divine and the human.
  • grāvāṇo: This means "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is called upon." It highlights the importance of invoking the right energies or deities.
  • barhir: This refers to "the sacred grass" or "the altar." It symbolizes the space where rituals are performed and where the divine is invited.
  • adhvare: This means "in the sacrifice" or "in the ritual." It indicates the context of the invocation, which is a sacred act.
  • ṛcā: This translates to "with hymns" or "with verses." It signifies the use of sacred words to connect with the divine.
  • yāmi: This means "I go" or "I approach." It indicates the speaker's intention to connect with the divine.
  • maruto: This refers to "the Maruts," the storm gods associated with strength and power. They represent the forces of nature.
  • brahmaṇas: This means "of the Brahman" or "of the ultimate reality." It signifies the connection to the universal consciousness.
  • patiṁ: This translates to "the lord" or "the master." It indicates reverence towards the divine authority.
  • devām̐: This means "the divine" or "the god." It emphasizes the sacred nature of the being being invoked.
  • avo: This means "we invoke" or "we call upon." It signifies the collective intention of the community.
  • vareṇyam: This translates to "worthy of worship" or "to be revered." It indicates the high status of the divine being invoked.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energies through rituals and hymns. It highlights the role of the priest as a mediator and the significance of fire as a purifying force. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during religious ceremonies, personal prayers, or community gatherings where individuals seek to connect with higher powers for guidance, strength, and blessings. The act of invocation serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of reverence in our spiritual practices.
