नक्षद धवम् अरुणीः पूर्व्यं राट तुरो विश्वाम अंगिरसाम अनु dyūn |तक्षद वज्रम नियुतं तस्तम्भद dyāṁ चतुष्पदे नार्याय द्विपादे ||
nakṣad dhavam aruṇīḥ pūrvyaṁ rāṭ turo viśām aṅgirasām anu dyūn | takṣad vajraṁ niyutaṁ tastambhad dyāṁ catuṣpade naryāya dvipāde ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Nakṣad: Stars or celestial bodies
    • Dhavam: To shine or illuminate
    • Aruṇīḥ: The reddish or dawn-like
    • Pūrvyaṁ: Eastern or from the east
    • Rāṭ: King or ruler
    • Turo: Swift or quick
    • Viśām: Of the people or community
    • Aṅgirasām: Of the Angirasas, a group of sages
    • anu: Following or after
    • Dyūn: The heavens or sky
    • Takṣad: To create or fashion
    • Vajraṁ: Thunderbolt or strength
    • Niyutaṁ: Bound or tied
    • Tastambhad: To support or uphold
    • Dyāṁ: The sky or heavens
    • Catuṣpade: Four-footed beings or animals
    • Naryāya: For the man or human
    • Dvipāde: Two-footed beings or humans
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the celestial bodies, particularly the stars, shining brightly in the eastern sky at dawn.
    • It emphasizes the role of a ruler or leader who is swift and attentive to the needs of the community.
    • The mention of the Angirasas suggests a connection to wisdom and knowledge, indicating that the guidance of wise individuals is essential for the prosperity of society.
    • The creation of strength (vajra) and support for all beings, both four-footed and two-footed, highlights the interconnectedness of all life and the responsibility of humans to care for the world around them.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern leadership, this mantra can be applied to the qualities of effective leaders who are responsive to their communities, much like how a good manager listens to their team and acts swiftly to resolve issues.
    • The idea of interconnectedness can be seen in environmental movements where humans are urged to protect wildlife and natural habitats, recognizing that all beings share the same ecosystem.
    • In personal development, the pursuit of wisdom and knowledge, akin to the Angirasas, can be reflected in lifelong learning and mentorship, where individuals seek guidance to improve themselves and their communities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of leadership, wisdom, and the interconnectedness of all life.
    • It encourages individuals to be mindful of their roles in society and to act with responsibility towards both people and nature.
    • In essence, it calls for a harmonious existence where the light of knowledge and the strength of community support guide our actions.
