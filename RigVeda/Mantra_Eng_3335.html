भूयसा वसनम् अचरत कनीयो 'विक्रीतो अकाणिषम् पुनर यन् |स भूयसा कनीयो नारीरेचिद दीनाऽदक्षाऽविधुहन्ति प्र वाणम् ||
bhūyasā vasnam acarat kanīyo 'vikrīto akāniṣam punar yan | sa bhūyasā kanīyo nārirecīd dīnā dakṣā vi duhanti pra vāṇam ||
  • bhūyasā: This word translates to "more" or "greater." It signifies the pursuit of higher ideals or aspirations.
  • vasnam: This refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." It can be interpreted as both material and spiritual wealth.
  • acarat: This means "to move" or "to act." It emphasizes the importance of action in achieving one's goals.
  • kanīyo: This translates to "smaller" or "lesser." It suggests humility or the idea of starting small.
  • vikrīto: This means "to be sold" or "to be exchanged." It can imply the idea of trade or the exchange of ideas and values.
  • akāniṣam: This refers to "not being deprived" or "not lacking." It emphasizes abundance and fulfillment.
  • punar: This means "again" or "once more." It signifies renewal or the cyclical nature of life.
  • yan: This translates to "that" or "which." It connects the previous ideas to a specific outcome or goal.
  • sa: This means "he" or "that one." It refers to the individual or entity that embodies the qualities discussed.
  • bhūyasā: Reiterating the idea of "more" or "greater," emphasizing the importance of striving for excellence.
  • kanīyo: Again, it refers to "smaller," reinforcing the notion of humility in one's pursuits.
  • nārirecīd: This means "not to be harmed" or "not to be injured." It emphasizes protection and safety.
  • dīnā: This translates to "poor" or "needy." It highlights the importance of compassion and support for those in need.
  • dakṣā: This means "skilled" or "capable." It signifies the value of competence and expertise.
  • vi: This is a preposition meaning "in" or "with," indicating the context of the following action.
  • duhanti: This means "to produce" or "to bring forth." It emphasizes the act of creation or contribution.
  • pra vāṇam: This translates to "the word" or "the speech." It signifies communication and the power of expression.

The mantra emphasizes the importance of striving for greater wealth and prosperity through action, while maintaining humility and compassion. It suggests that one should not be deprived of their needs and should work towards renewal and protection for themselves and others. The qualities of skill and capability are highlighted as essential for producing positive outcomes and effective communication.


Conclusion: This mantra is significant in real life as it encourages individuals to pursue their goals with a sense of humility and compassion. It reminds us that true wealth is not just material but also includes the well-being of others. In practical terms, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in business, where ethical practices and support for the community can lead to sustainable success. It also serves as a reminder to communicate effectively and to use our skills to uplift those around us, fostering a sense of community and shared prosperity.
