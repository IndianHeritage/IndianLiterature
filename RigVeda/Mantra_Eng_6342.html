वृषायम इन्द्र ते रथ उतो ते वृषणा हरि |वृषा त्वं शतक्रतो वृषा हवः ||
vṛṣāyam indra te ratha uto te vṛṣaṇā harī | vṛṣā tvaṁ śatakrato vṛṣā havaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vṛṣāyam: To the strong or powerful.
    • indra: O Indra, the king of gods.
    • te: Your.
    • ratha: Chariot or vehicle.
    • uto: And also.
    • vṛṣaṇā: Of the strong or powerful.
    • harī: The one who is swift or quick.
    • vṛṣā: Strong or powerful.
    • tvaṁ: You.
    • śatakrato: O hundred-fold knower (a title of Indra).
    • havaḥ: We invoke or call upon.
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a call to Indra, the chief deity in the Rigveda, who represents strength, power, and the forces of nature. The speaker is invoking Indra's powerful chariot and his swift nature, asking for his blessings and assistance. The repetition of the word "vṛṣā" emphasizes the strength and power associated with Indra, highlighting his role as a protector and a source of energy.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of struggle or adversity, individuals often seek strength and support from higher powers or deities, similar to how the mantra calls upon Indra for assistance.
    • In sports, athletes may invoke the spirit of strength and power, akin to the qualities of Indra, to enhance their performance and overcome challenges.
    • In leadership, invoking the qualities of strength and decisiveness can help guide teams through difficult situations, much like how the mantra seeks the guidance of Indra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of strength and power, qualities that are essential in various aspects of life. Whether in personal challenges, professional endeavors, or communal efforts, the essence of seeking strength from a higher power can inspire individuals to overcome obstacles and achieve their goals. This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of resilience and the support we can seek from the universe or divine forces in our journey through life.
