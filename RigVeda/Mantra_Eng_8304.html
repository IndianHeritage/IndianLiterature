स मातरā विचरन् वाजयन्न अपः प्र मेधिरः स्वधया पिन्वते पदम् | अंशुर यवेना पिपीषे यतो नृभिः सं जामिभिर नासते रक्षते शिरः ||
sa mātarā vicaran vājayann apaḥ pra medhiraḥ svadhayā pinvate padam | aṁśur yavena pipiśe yato nṛbhiḥ saṁ jāmibhir nasate rakṣate śiraḥ ||
  • sa mātarā vicaran: This phrase can be translated as "the one who moves like a mother." In this context, it refers to a nurturing and protective force, akin to a mother’s love and care.
  • vājayann apaḥ: This means "the waters that bring strength." Here, water symbolizes nourishment and vitality, essential for life and growth.
  • pra medhiraḥ: Translated as "the wise one," this term signifies intelligence and understanding, suggesting that wisdom is crucial in navigating life’s challenges.
  • svadhayā pinvate padam: This can be interpreted as "by their own nature, they attain their goal." It emphasizes the importance of self-determination and intrinsic qualities in achieving success.
  • aṁśur yavena pipiśe: This phrase means "the rays of the sun nourish." The sun is often associated with energy and growth, indicating that external forces can also contribute to our development.
  • yato nṛbhiḥ: Translated as "where men," this phrase highlights the human aspect, suggesting that the mantra is relevant to human experiences and interactions.
  • saṁ jāmibhir nasate: This means "they protect us." It indicates a sense of community and support, emphasizing the importance of relationships in our lives.
  • rakṣate śiraḥ: This translates to "protects the head." The head symbolizes intellect and decision-making, suggesting that wisdom and protection are intertwined.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of nurturing, wisdom, and community support. It signifies the importance of both internal qualities and external influences in achieving one's goals. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as parenting, education, and teamwork. For instance, a nurturing environment at home or in schools fosters growth and development, while wise decision-making and community support can lead to collective success. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, where individual efforts and communal bonds work together to create a harmonious existence.
