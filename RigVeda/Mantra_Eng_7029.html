यद ध नूनं परावती यद वा पृथिव्यां दिवि |युजाना इन्द्र हरिभिर महेमत ऋष्व ऋष्वेभिर आगहि ||
yad dha nūnam parāvati yad vā pṛthivyāṁ divi | yujāna indra haribhir mahemata ṛṣva ṛṣvebhir ā gahi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • dha: is placed or established
    • nūnam: indeed or certainly
    • parāvati: the highest or supreme
    • yad: that which
    • vā: or
    • pṛthivyāṁ: on the earth
    • divi: in the heavens
    • yujāna: connected or united
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • haribhir: with the horses (often symbolizing speed and power)
    • mahemata: with great strength or might
    • ṛṣva: the powerful or strong
    • ṛṣvebhir: with the seers or sages
    • ā gahi: come or approach
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the connection between the earthly and the divine realms.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and strength, invoking Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, who is often associated with power and leadership.
    • The mention of "haribhir" (with horses) symbolizes the swiftness and vigor needed to achieve one's goals.
    • The phrase "ṛṣvebhir" (with the seers) highlights the value of wisdom and guidance from enlightened beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied to teamwork, where individuals with different strengths (like the horses) come together to achieve a common goal under strong leadership (like Indra).
    • In personal development, it can signify the importance of seeking guidance from mentors (the seers) to navigate challenges effectively.
    • In community initiatives, it reflects the need for unity and collective strength to address social issues, drawing on the power of collaboration.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of all realms—earthly and divine—and the importance of strength, unity, and wisdom in achieving success.
    • It serves as a reminder that by coming together with purpose and seeking guidance, we can overcome challenges and reach our highest potential.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals and communities to harness their collective strengths and wisdom to create positive change.
