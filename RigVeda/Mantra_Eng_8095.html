एतं उ त्यं दश क्षिपो मृजन्ति सिंधुमातरम् |सम आदित्येभिर अख्यत ||
etam u tyaṁ daśa kṣipo mṛjanti sindhumātaram | sam ādityebhir akhyata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • etam: this
    • u: indeed
    • tyaṁ: ten
    • daśa: ten
    • kṣipo: they throw
    • mṛjanti: they cleanse
    • sindhumātaram: the mother river
    • sam: together
    • ādityebhir: with the sun
    • akhyata: is known
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the cleansing and nurturing qualities of the river, often personified as a mother.
    • The phrase "ten they throw" can be interpreted as the act of offering or dedicating something to the river, symbolizing respect and reverence.
    • The mention of "with the sun" suggests a connection between the river and the life-giving energy of the sun, highlighting the interdependence of natural elements.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rivers are considered sacred and are often the sites of rituals and offerings. For instance, the Ganges River in India is revered, and people perform rituals to cleanse themselves in its waters.
    • Environmental movements often emphasize the importance of rivers in sustaining ecosystems, drawing parallels to the nurturing aspect of the river as a mother.
    • In agriculture, rivers provide essential water for crops, illustrating the connection between the river (mother) and the sustenance of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of rivers in our lives, not just as physical entities but as symbols of nourishment and purity.
    • It teaches us to respect and protect our natural resources, recognizing their role in sustaining life.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages a harmonious relationship with nature, reminding us that our well-being is intertwined with the health of our environment.
