यज्ञसाहं दुव इषे 'ग्निम पूर्वस्य शेवस्य | अद्रेः सूनुम आयुम आहुः ||
yajñāsāhaṁ duva iṣe 'gnim pūrvasya śevasya | adreḥ sūnum āyum āhuḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yajñāsāhaṁ duva iṣe 'gnim pūrvasya śevasya | adreḥ sūnum āyum āhuḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yajñā: Sacrifice or offering
    • sāhaṁ: I am
    • duva: Two or dual
    • iṣe: To be desired or sought
    • 'gnim: Fire
    • pūrvasya: Of the east or the previous
    • śevasya: Of the west or the latter
    • adreḥ: Of the mountain or the high place
    • sūnum: Son or offspring
    • āyum: Life or longevity
    • āhuḥ: They say or declare
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the dual nature of sacrifice, emphasizing the importance of fire as a central element in rituals.
    • The mention of "pūrvasya" and "śevasya" suggests a connection between the past and the future, indicating that sacrifices made in the present have roots in ancient traditions and implications for future generations.
    • The reference to "adreḥ" signifies the elevation of spiritual practices, akin to reaching a higher state of consciousness or understanding.
    • By invoking the "sūnum," the mantra highlights the significance of life and longevity, suggesting that through these rituals, one seeks blessings for a prosperous and long life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is a symbol of purification and transformation. For instance, in Hindu weddings, the couple circles a sacred fire, symbolizing their commitment and the dual nature of their union.
    • In modern life, the concept of sacrifice can be seen in the dedication of individuals who work hard for their families, often putting their own desires aside for the sake of their loved ones.
    • Environmental rituals, such as planting trees, can be viewed as a form of yajña, where individuals contribute to the well-being of future generations, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on life and longevity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of sacrifice, the importance of fire in spiritual practices, and the connection between past, present, and future.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that our actions today, especially those that involve selflessness and dedication, have lasting impacts on our lives and the lives of those who come after us.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to engage in meaningful rituals and sacrifices that promote harmony, longevity, and a deeper understanding of their place in the universe.
