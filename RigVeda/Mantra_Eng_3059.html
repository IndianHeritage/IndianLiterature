त्रिर अस्य ता परमां सन्ति सत्यां स्पार्था देवस्य जनिमान्य अग्निः |अनन्ते अन्तः परिवीता आगाच्छुचिः शुक्रो आर्यो रोरुचानाḥ ||
trir asya tā paramā santi satyā spārhā devasya janimāny agneḥ | anante antaḥ parivīta āgāc chuciḥ śukro aryo rorucānaḥ ||
  • trir asya tā paramā santi satyā spārhā devasya janimāny agneḥ
    • trir - This word means "three" and signifies the triad of existence: creation, preservation, and destruction.
    • asya - Translates to "of this," indicating a connection to the divine or the universe.
    • tā - Means "those," referring to the qualities or aspects being discussed.
    • paramā - Means "supreme" or "highest," suggesting the ultimate truth or reality.
    • santyā - Refers to "truth" or "reality," emphasizing the importance of truth in existence.
    • spārhā - Means "desire" or "aspiration," indicating the human longing for connection with the divine.
    • devasya - Means "of the deity," referring to the divine presence, in this case, Agni.
    • janimāny - Translates to "born" or "origin," indicating the source of creation.
    • agneḥ - Refers to "Agni," the fire god, symbolizing transformation and energy.
  • anante antaḥ parivīta āgāc chuciḥ śukro aryo rorucānaḥ
    • anante - Means "infinite," suggesting the boundless nature of the universe.
    • antaḥ - Means "end," indicating the concept of boundaries or limits.
    • parivīta - Translates to "surrounded" or "enveloped," suggesting a protective or encompassing quality.
    • āgāc - Means "coming" or "arriving," indicating the presence of divine energy.
    • shuciḥ - Means "pure," emphasizing the importance of purity in thought and action.
    • śukro - Refers to "bright" or "radiant," symbolizing enlightenment and knowledge.
    • aryo - Means "noble" or "worthy," indicating the qualities of a virtuous person.
    • rorucānaḥ - Means "shining" or "radiant," suggesting the brilliance of divine presence.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates profound philosophical concepts about existence, truth, and the divine. It emphasizes the triad of creation, preservation, and destruction, highlighting the importance of truth and aspiration in our lives. The invocation of Agni symbolizes transformation and the energy that fuels our desires and actions.

In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth and self-improvement. Just as Agni transforms matter, individuals can transform their lives through knowledge, purity of intention, and noble actions. The mantra encourages us to seek truth and aspire for higher ideals, reminding us that our desires should align with the greater good.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a guide for living a life of integrity, purpose, and connection to the divine, fostering a sense of community and shared aspirations among individuals.
