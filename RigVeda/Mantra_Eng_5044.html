एषा स्याऽनो दुहिताऽदिवोजाः क्षितीरुचन्ती मानवशीर्ज अजिगः |या भानुना रुशता रम्याऽस्व अजñायि तिरस्तमसश्चिदक्तून् ||
eṣā syā no duhitā divojāḥ kṣitīr ucchantī mānuṣīr ajīgaḥ | yā bhānunā ruśatā rāmyāsv ajñāyi tiras tamasaś cid aktūn ||
  • Mantra: eṣā syā no duhitā divojāḥ kṣitīr ucchantī mānuṣīr ajīgaḥ | yā bhānunā ruśatā rāmyāsv ajñāyi tiras tamasaś cid aktūn ||
  • Translation: "This is our daughter, born of the heavens, who rises from the earth, a human being. She, illuminated by the sun, is beautiful and wise, guiding us through the darkness."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • eṣā: This (referring to a divine entity or concept)
    • syā: may be (indicating potential or possibility)
    • no: our (possessive pronoun)
    • duhitā: daughter (symbolizing nurturing and care)
    • divojāḥ: born of the heavens (suggesting a divine origin)
    • kṣitīr: earth (the physical realm)
    • ucchantī: rising (indicating growth or emergence)
    • mānuṣīr: human being (representing humanity)
    • ajīgaḥ: who is wise (implying intelligence and insight)
    • yā: who (referring to the subject)
    • bhānunā: illuminated by the sun (symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment)
    • ruśatā: beautiful (indicating aesthetic and moral beauty)
    • rāmyāsv: in a pleasant manner (suggesting harmony)
    • ajñāyi: guiding us (implying leadership and direction)
    • tiras: through (indicating a pathway)
    • tamasaś: darkness (representing ignorance or challenges)
    • cid: consciousness (implying awareness)
    • aktūn: of the light (symbolizing knowledge and truth)

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of a "daughter" can be seen in the nurturing role of women in society, where they often guide and support their families.
    • The idea of being "illuminated by the sun" can be related to education and knowledge, where learning enlightens individuals and helps them navigate life's challenges.
    • In a corporate setting, a wise leader can be seen as a "daughter" of the organization, guiding the team through difficult times with insight and clarity.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, guidance, and enlightenment in our lives.
    • It signifies the role of nurturing figures, often women, who provide support and direction in both personal and societal contexts.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as family dynamics, educational pursuits, and leadership roles, highlighting the need for wisdom and guidance to overcome challenges and achieve growth.
