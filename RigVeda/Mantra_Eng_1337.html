युवं भुज्युम् भुरमानं विभिर् गतं स्वयुक्तिभिर् निवहन्ता पितृभ्य आ |यासिष्टं वर्तिर् वृषणा विजेन्यं दिवोदासाय महि चेति वाम अवः ||
yuvam bhujyum bhuramāṇaṁ vibhir gataṁ svayuktibhir nivahantā pitṛbhya ā | yāsiṣṭaṁ vartir vṛṣaṇā vijenya1ṁ divodāsāya mahi ceti vām avaḥ ||
  • yuvam: This word translates to "you" in the plural form, indicating a collective or community aspect.
  • bhujyum: This means "to be strong" or "to be powerful," suggesting the importance of strength in unity.
  • bhuramāṇaṁ: This can be interpreted as "bearing burdens" or "supporting," emphasizing the responsibility that comes with strength.
  • vibhir: This word means "by the powers" or "by the forces," indicating that strength and support come from various sources.
  • gataṁ: This translates to "gone" or "departed," which may refer to the past or experiences that shape the present.
  • svayuktibhir: This means "by one's own arrangements" or "by one's own efforts," highlighting the importance of self-determination.
  • nivahantā: This translates to "those who carry" or "those who support," referring to individuals or groups that take on responsibilities.
  • pitṛbhya: This means "to the ancestors" or "to the forefathers," acknowledging the role of lineage and heritage.
  • yāsiṣṭaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the best" or "the most excellent," suggesting a pursuit of excellence.
  • vartir: This translates to "the way" or "the path," indicating a journey or direction in life.
  • vṛṣaṇā: This means "of the strong" or "of the powerful," reinforcing the theme of strength.
  • vijenyaṁ: This translates to "victorious" or "conquering," suggesting success through strength and unity.
  • divodāsāya: This refers to "to the divine" or "to the gods," indicating a spiritual or higher purpose.
  • mahi: This means "great" or "mighty," emphasizing the significance of greatness in actions.
  • ceti: This translates to "that" or "which," linking the previous concepts together.
  • vām: This means "you" in a plural sense, again emphasizing community.
  • avaḥ: This translates to "bring" or "lead," suggesting a call to action or a request for guidance.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of collective strength, responsibility, and the pursuit of excellence. It highlights the role of ancestors and the divine in guiding individuals and communities towards greatness. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork in professional settings, community service, and family responsibilities. By recognizing the strength in unity and the importance of heritage, individuals can strive for success while honoring their roots and supporting one another.
