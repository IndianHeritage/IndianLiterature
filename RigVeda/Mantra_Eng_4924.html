विष्वे देवास आ गत श्रिणुत मा इमं हवम् |एदं बर्हिर न शीदत ||
viśve devāsa ā gata śṛṇutā ma imaṁ havam | edam barhir ni ṣīdata ||
  • Translation: "O all gods, come hither; listen to this invocation of mine. This offering is spread out."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • viśve: All, universal, encompassing all.
    • devāsa: Gods, divine beings.
    • ā gata: Come hither, approach.
    • śṛṇutā: Listen, pay attention.
    • ma: My, mine.
    • imaṁ: This, referring to the invocation.
    • havam: Invocation, offering, sacrifice.
    • edam: This, indicating the offering.
    • barhir: Spread out, laid out, often referring to the sacred grass used in rituals.
    • ni ṣīdata: Sit down, settle, indicating a place for the gods to receive the offering.
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra is a call to all divine beings, inviting them to partake in the offerings made by the devotee.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and inclusivity in spiritual practices, recognizing the presence of all divine forces.
    • The act of spreading out the offering symbolizes preparation and respect for the divine, akin to setting a table for honored guests.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, such as weddings or festivals, people often prepare elaborate offerings or meals to honor their ancestors and deities, inviting blessings and goodwill.
    • In community gatherings, the act of sharing food can be seen as a way to invite harmony and connection among participants, similar to the invocation of the gods in this mantra.
    • In personal prayer or meditation, individuals may create a sacred space, laying out items that hold significance, inviting a sense of the divine into their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of inviting divine presence into our lives and recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It serves as a reminder to approach spiritual practices with openness and respect, acknowledging the multitude of forces that shape our existence.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages us to create spaces of inclusivity and reverence in our daily lives, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
