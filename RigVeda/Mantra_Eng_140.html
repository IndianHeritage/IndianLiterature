घृतपृष्ठा मनोयुजो ये त्वां वहन्ति वह्नयः |आ देवान सोमपीतये ||
ghṛtapṛṣṭhā manoyujo ye tvā vahanti vahnayaḥ | ā devān somapītaye ||
  • ghṛtapṛṣṭhā: This term can be broken down into two parts: "ghṛta," which means clarified butter or ghee, and "pṛṣṭhā," meaning back or support. Together, it signifies something that is nourished or supported by ghee, symbolizing richness and abundance.
  • manoyujo: This word translates to "those who are united in thought" or "those who have a common mental focus." It emphasizes the importance of collective intention and unity of purpose.
  • ye tvā vahanti vahnayaḥ: This phrase means "those who carry you, O Fire." Here, "vahnayaḥ" refers to fire, which is a significant element in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity, transformation, and energy. The phrase suggests that there are beings or forces that support and carry the essence of fire.
  • ā devān somapītaye: This translates to "for the sake of the gods, for the enjoyment of the soma." "Soma" is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, associated with immortality and divine inspiration. This line indicates a connection between the offerings made to the gods and the sustenance of divine energy.

  • Significance: This mantra highlights the importance of unity, intention, and the sacredness of offerings in spiritual practices. It reflects the interconnectedness of the material and spiritual worlds, where offerings made with a pure heart and collective intention can invoke divine blessings.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community gatherings, rituals, or even in collaborative work environments. For instance, when a team comes together with a shared goal, focusing their thoughts and intentions, they can achieve greater success, much like the collective energy invoked in the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity and intention in our actions. By recognizing the significance of our offerings—whether they are physical, emotional, or spiritual—we can foster a deeper connection with the divine and enhance our collective experiences. It encourages us to approach our endeavors with a sense of purpose and reverence, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
