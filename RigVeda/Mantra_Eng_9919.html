याः फलिनीः याः अपफलाः अपुष्पा याश्च पुष्पिणीः | बृहस्पतिप्रसूता ता नो मुचन्त्व अंहसः ||
yāḥ phalinīr yā aphalā apuṣpā yāś ca puṣpiṇīḥ | bṛhaspatiprasūtās tā no muñcantv aṁhasaḥ ||
  • yāḥ phalinīr: This phrase refers to those that bear fruit. In a metaphorical sense, it can represent actions or endeavors that yield positive results or benefits in life.
  • yā aphalā: This translates to those that do not bear fruit. It signifies efforts or actions that may not lead to tangible outcomes, reminding us of the importance of evaluating our pursuits.
  • apuṣpā: This means without flowers. In a broader context, it can symbolize endeavors that lack beauty or fulfillment, suggesting that not all efforts are rewarding or aesthetically pleasing.
  • yāś ca puṣpiṇīḥ: This refers to those that are blossoming or flourishing. It highlights the importance of growth and development in various aspects of life, encouraging us to nurture our potential.
  • bṛhaspatiprasūtās: This phrase can be interpreted as those born of the wisdom of Brihaspati, the deity of knowledge and eloquence. It emphasizes the value of wisdom and guidance in our actions and decisions.
  • tā no muñcantv aṁhasaḥ: This translates to "may they free us from darkness." It signifies a plea for liberation from ignorance and negativity, urging us to seek enlightenment and clarity in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the duality of existence—fruitful and unfruitful endeavors, beauty and lack thereof, and the pursuit of wisdom. In real life, it serves as a reminder to evaluate our actions and their outcomes, encouraging us to focus on pursuits that lead to growth and enlightenment. It is applicable in various scenarios, such as personal development, career choices, and relationships, guiding us to seek knowledge and clarity while striving for meaningful and fruitful experiences.
