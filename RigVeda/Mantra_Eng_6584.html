महान्ता मित्रावरुणा सम्राज देवाव असुराः |ऋतावानाव ऋतम आगोषतो बृहत् ||
mahāntā mitrāvaruṇā samrājā devāv asurā | ṛtāvānāv ṛtam ā ghoṣato bṛhat ||
  • Translation: "The great ones, Mitra and Varuna, are the sovereign deities; they are the divine and the non-divine. They are the upholders of truth, and they come forth from the great sound."
  • Explanation of Key Terms:
    • Mahāntā: Refers to the great or the mighty, indicating the importance and power of the deities mentioned.
    • Mitrāvaruṇā: This compound word combines Mitra (the god of friendship and alliances) and Varuna (the god of cosmic order and water), symbolizing the duality of nature and the balance between different forces.
    • Samrājā: Means sovereign or ruler, highlighting their authority over the universe.
    • Devāv asurā: Refers to the divine (devas) and the non-divine (asuras), suggesting the coexistence of good and evil in the world.
    • Ṛtāvānāv: Signifies those who uphold ṛta, the cosmic order or truth, emphasizing the importance of righteousness.
    • Ṛtam: Represents the ultimate truth or order in the universe.
    • Ā ghoṣato bṛhat: Implies the great sound or call, which can be interpreted as the universal resonance of truth and order.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of balance between good and evil can be seen in everyday life, where individuals must navigate moral dilemmas, making choices that reflect their values.
    • The importance of truth and righteousness is echoed in various cultures and religions, where honesty and integrity are upheld as virtues in personal and professional relationships.
    • The idea of cosmic order can be related to environmental sustainability, where respecting nature and maintaining balance is crucial for the well-being of future generations.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of truth, balance, and the coexistence of different forces in the universe. It serves as a reminder to uphold righteousness in our actions and decisions, fostering harmony in our lives and communities. In a world often filled with chaos and moral ambiguity, this mantra encourages individuals to seek truth and maintain balance, applicable in various aspects of life, from personal ethics to global issues like environmental conservation and social justice.
