सोमारुद्रā युवम् एतान्य अस्मे विश्वा तनूषु भेषजानी धत्तम् |अव स्यातम् मुञ्चतं यन् नो अस्ति तनूषु बद्धं कृतम् एनो अस्मत् ||
somārudrā yuvam etāny asme viśvā tanūṣu bheṣajāni dhattam | ava syatam muñcataṁ yan no asti tanūṣu baddhaṁ kṛtam eno asmat ||
  • Mantra: "somārudrā yuvam etāny asme viśvā tanūṣu bheṣajāni dhattam | ava syatam muñcataṁ yan no asti tanūṣu baddhaṁ kṛtam eno asmat ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • somārudrā: Referring to the divine energy associated with the moon and Rudra (a form of Shiva), symbolizing healing and rejuvenation.
    • yuvam: You (plural), addressing the divine forces or energies.
    • etāny: These (energies or forces).
    • asme: To us, indicating a plea or invocation for assistance.
    • viśvā: All or universal, suggesting inclusivity and wholeness.
    • tanūṣu: In the bodies or forms, referring to physical existence.
    • bheṣajāni: Healing or medicinal, indicating the need for health and wellness.
    • dhattam: May be bestowed or granted, a request for divine intervention.
    • ava syatam: Let it be released or freed, a call for liberation.
    • muñcataṁ: To be freed or liberated, emphasizing the desire for release from bondage.
    • yan: That which, referring to the ailments or issues at hand.
    • no: Our, indicating a personal connection to the plea.
    • asti: Is or exists, affirming the presence of challenges.
    • tanūṣu: In the bodies, reiterating the physical aspect of existence.
    • baddhaṁ: Bound or tied, referring to the constraints or afflictions faced.
    • kṛtam: Done or created, indicating actions that have led to the current state.
    • eno: This, referring to the current situation or affliction.
    • asmat: Our, emphasizing the collective experience of suffering.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer for healing and liberation from physical and spiritual ailments.
    • It invokes the divine energies associated with the moon and Rudra, seeking their blessings for health and well-being.
    • The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the universal need for healing.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In times of illness or distress, individuals can recite this mantra to seek divine support and healing.
    • It can be used in meditation practices to focus on releasing negative energies and promoting wellness.
    • Communities can come together to chant this mantra during health crises, fostering a sense of unity and collective healing.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of divine energies in our lives and the importance of seeking help in times of need.
    • It highlights the significance of health and well-being, both physically and spiritually, in our daily lives.
    • By invoking these energies, we can cultivate a sense of hope, healing, and liberation from the challenges we face.
