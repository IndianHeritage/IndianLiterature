एष क्षेति रथवीतिर मगवाऽगमतीर अनु |पर्वतेष्व अपश्रितः ||
eṣa kṣeti rathavītir maghavā gomatīr anu | parvateṣv apaśritaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣa: this
    • kṣeti: dwells or resides
    • rathavītir: the charioteer or the one who drives the chariot
    • maghavā: the powerful or the mighty one
    • gomatīr: the one who possesses wealth or abundance
    • anu: following or in accordance with
    • parvateṣv: in the mountains
    • apaśritaḥ: sheltered or protected
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful entity that resides in the mountains, symbolizing strength and protection.
    • The term "rathavītir" can be interpreted as a guide or leader, suggesting that this entity leads the way through challenges.
    • The mention of "gomatīr" indicates a connection to prosperity and abundance, highlighting the importance of wealth in a broader sense, not just material but also spiritual and emotional wealth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a strong leader (akin to "rathavītir") guides their team through challenges, ensuring that they remain focused on their goals (symbolizing "gomatīr").
    • In personal life, individuals often seek mentors or guides who provide shelter and support during difficult times, much like the protective nature of the mountains ("parvateṣv").
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, guidance, and protection in our lives.
    • It teaches us the value of seeking support from powerful entities or mentors during challenging times.
    • In a broader context, it reminds us that true wealth encompasses not just material possessions but also the strength of character and the support of our community.
