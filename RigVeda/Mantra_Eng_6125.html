तेन नो वाजिनीवासू परावतश्चिदागतम् | उपेमां सुश्रुतिम् मम ||
tena no vājinīvasū parāvataś cid ā gatam | upemāṁ suṣṭutim mama ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tena: by that
    • no: us
    • vājinīvasū: the best of the strong or powerful
    • parāvataś: from the highest or supreme
    • cid: consciousness or awareness
    • ā: to
    • gatam: come or arrive
    • upemāṁ: may I attain or achieve
    • suṣṭutim: good praise or commendation
    • mama: my
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer for strength and power that comes from a higher consciousness.
    • The speaker seeks to be blessed with the qualities of the strong and powerful, which are derived from a supreme source.
    • It emphasizes the importance of awareness and consciousness in achieving one's goals and aspirations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive environment, such as a workplace, individuals often seek guidance and strength from mentors or leaders who embody the qualities they aspire to achieve.
    • In sports, athletes often visualize their goals and draw inspiration from their coaches or past champions, reflecting the idea of seeking strength from a higher source.
    • In personal development, individuals may engage in mindfulness practices to enhance their awareness and consciousness, thereby empowering themselves to achieve their aspirations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the quest for strength and commendation that is rooted in higher consciousness.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, including personal growth, professional development, and spiritual practices.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals can align themselves with the qualities of strength and awareness, ultimately leading to greater achievements and fulfillment in life.
