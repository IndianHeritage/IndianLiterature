आ नूरजं वहतम अश्विनाऽ युवं मधुमत्याऽ नः कशयाऽ मिमिक्षतम् |प्रायुः तारीष्टं नी रपांसि मृक्षतं सेधतं द्वेषो भवतं सचाभुवाऽ ||
ā na ūrjaṁ vahatam aśvinā yuvam madhumatyā naḥ kaśayā mimikṣatam | prāyus tāriṣṭaṁ nī rapāṁsi mṛkṣataṁ sedhataṁ dveṣo bhavataṁ sacābhuvā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā - towards
    • na - us
    • ūrjaṁ - energy or strength
    • vahatam - may carry or bring
    • aśvinā - O Ashvins (the twin gods of medicine and health)
    • yuvam - you two
    • madhumatyā - with sweetness or pleasantness
    • naḥ - our
    • kaśayā - with care or attention
    • mimikṣatam - may you look upon
    • prāyus - may we be
    • tāriṣṭaṁ - strong or powerful
    • nī - lead or guide
    • rapāṁsi - our bodies
    • mṛkṣataṁ - may you protect
    • sedhataṁ - may you unite
    • dveṣo - enmity or hatred
    • bhavataṁ - may it not be
    • sacābhuvā - and may it be with us
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the Ashvins, seeking their blessings for strength and unity.
    • The invocation of the Ashvins highlights the importance of health and well-being in our lives.
    • The plea for energy and strength reflects a desire for vitality in both physical and mental aspects.
    • The mention of "madhumatyā" signifies the importance of sweetness in life, suggesting that pleasant experiences and relationships are essential.
    • The call for protection of our bodies and unity among us emphasizes the need for community and support in overcoming challenges.
    • The request to eliminate enmity indicates a longing for peace and harmony in relationships.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, this mantra can be applied by fostering a collaborative environment where team members support each other, enhancing productivity and morale.
    • In personal relationships, it encourages individuals to approach conflicts with understanding and compassion, promoting harmony.
    • In health practices, it can inspire individuals to seek holistic well-being, combining physical fitness with mental and emotional health.
    • In community settings, it can motivate collective efforts to address social issues, emphasizing unity and cooperation.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, health, and community.
    • Its significance lies in the holistic approach to life, advocating for balance between personal well-being and social harmony.
    • By invoking the blessings of the Ashvins, we are encouraged to cultivate positive relationships and foster a supportive environment.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that true strength comes not just from individual power, but from unity and collective well-being.
