मन्त्र: हिरण्ययी अरणी यं निर्मन्थतो अश्विनाः |तं ते गर्भं हवामहे दशमे मासि सूतवे ||
hiraṇyayī araṇī yaṁ nirmanthato aśvinā | taṁ te garbhaṁ havāmahe daśame māsi sūtave ||
  • hiraṇyayī: This word translates to "golden" or "radiant." In a broader sense, it symbolizes prosperity, wealth, and the divine light that guides us.
  • araṇī: Refers to the "two sticks" used in the ancient practice of fire-making. It represents the duality of existence and the effort required to create something valuable.
  • yaṁ: This is a pronoun meaning "which" or "that," indicating a connection to the subject being discussed.
  • nirmanthato: This term means "by churning" or "through effort." It signifies the process of creation and the hard work involved in bringing forth something new.
  • aśvinā: Refers to the Aśvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the divine assistance in our endeavors.
  • taṁ: Another pronoun meaning "that," pointing to the object of the mantra.
  • te: This means "your," indicating a personal connection or offering to the divine.
  • garbhaṁ: Translates to "womb" or "embryo," symbolizing potential, creation, and the nurturing aspect of life.
  • havāmahe: This means "we invoke" or "we offer," indicating a call to the divine for assistance or blessings.
  • daśame: This translates to "tenth," which can refer to a specific time or occasion, often linked to rituals or significant events.
  • māsi: Means "month," indicating a time frame for the invocation or ritual.
  • sūtave: This term means "to the one who brings forth" or "to the one who produces," referring to the divine force that aids in creation.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for the divine assistance of the Aśvins in the process of creation and nurturing. It emphasizes the importance of effort and collaboration with the divine to bring forth prosperity and potential in our lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra speaks of churning and effort, individuals can reflect on their own journeys of self-improvement, recognizing that growth often requires hard work and perseverance.
  • Entrepreneurship: The symbolism of creating something valuable from effort can inspire entrepreneurs to seek divine guidance and support in their ventures, understanding that success often comes from diligent work and collaboration.
  • Family and Nurturing: The reference to "garbhaṁ" can be related to the nurturing of children and family, highlighting the importance of creating a supportive environment for growth and development.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interplay between human effort and divine assistance in the process of creation. It encourages individuals to seek prosperity and potential through hard work, while also recognizing the importance of spiritual support in their endeavors.
