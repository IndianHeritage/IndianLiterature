तुभ्यं दक्ष कविक्रतो यानिमादेवा मर्तासो अध्वरे अकर्म |त्वं विश्वस्य सुरथस्य बोधि सर्वं तद अग्ने अमृत स्वदेहा ||
tubhyaṁ dakṣa kavikrato yānīmā deva martāso adhvare akarma | tvaṁ viśvasya surathasya bodhi sarvaṁ tad agne amṛta svadeha ||
  • tubhyaṁ - This word translates to "to you" or "for you," indicating a sense of devotion and reverence towards a divine entity.
  • dakṣa - Meaning "skilled" or "capable," this term highlights the importance of competence and expertise in various aspects of life.
  • kavikrato - This can be interpreted as "the wise one" or "the one with a thoughtful plan," suggesting the value of wisdom and foresight in decision-making.
  • yānīmā - Translates to "the one who leads" or "the guide," emphasizing the role of leadership and guidance in our lives.
  • deva - Meaning "god" or "divine," this term signifies the higher powers that govern the universe.
  • martāso - This word refers to "mortals" or "human beings," reminding us of our human condition and limitations.
  • adhvare - This translates to "in the sacrifice" or "in the offering," indicating the importance of rituals and offerings in spiritual practices.
  • akarma - Meaning "without action" or "inactive," this term can reflect the need for balance between action and contemplation.
  • tvaṁ - This means "you," reinforcing the personal connection to the divine.
  • viśvasya - Translates to "of the universe" or "of all," indicating the interconnectedness of all beings and the cosmos.
  • surathasya - Meaning "of the well-being" or "of the prosperous," this term emphasizes the pursuit of welfare and prosperity.
  • bodhi - This word means "wisdom" or "enlightenment," highlighting the importance of knowledge and understanding in life.
  • sarvaṁ - Translates to "all" or "everything," indicating inclusivity and the holistic nature of existence.
  • tad - Meaning "that," it refers to the divine or the ultimate reality.
  • agne - This term refers to "Agni," the fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • amṛta - Meaning "immortal" or "eternal," this term signifies the quest for immortality and the divine essence within.
  • svadeha - This translates to "in your body" or "in your form," indicating the physical manifestation of the divine in the material world.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of devotion, wisdom, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It emphasizes the importance of skilled leadership, the pursuit of knowledge, and the significance of rituals in our spiritual journey. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership roles where wisdom and guidance are crucial, in personal development through the pursuit of knowledge, and in community rituals that foster unity and well-being. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of our connection to the divine and the importance of striving for enlightenment and prosperity in our lives.
