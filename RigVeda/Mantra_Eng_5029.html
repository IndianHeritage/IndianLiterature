अकारि वाम अन्धसो वरिम्न्न अस्तारी बर्हिः सुप्रायणतमम् |उत्तानहस्तो युवयुर ववन्दां वां नक्षन्तो अद्रय आञ्जन ||
akāri vām andhaso varīmann astāri barhiḥ suprāyaṇatamam | uttānahasto yuvayur vavandā vāṁ nakṣanto adraya āñjan ||
  • akāri vām: This phrase can be interpreted as "you have created" or "you have made." It signifies the act of creation and the power of intention behind actions.
  • andhaso: This word means "of the blind" or "those who are in darkness." It represents ignorance or lack of awareness.
  • varīmann: This term can be understood as "the one who is powerful" or "the one who has strength." It refers to a source of strength or a powerful entity.
  • astāri: This means "to elevate" or "to raise up." It signifies the act of lifting oneself or others from a lower state to a higher one.
  • barhiḥ: This word refers to "the sacred grass" or "the seat of the divine." It symbolizes purity and the connection to the divine.
  • suprāyaṇatamam: This can be translated as "the best path" or "the most auspicious journey." It indicates a favorable direction or course in life.
  • uttānahasto: This phrase means "with uplifted hands." It symbolizes readiness to receive blessings or to offer gratitude.
  • yuvayur: This term refers to "youth" or "young ones." It signifies vitality, energy, and the potential for growth.
  • vavandā: This means "to honor" or "to worship." It indicates respect and reverence towards the divine or powerful forces.
  • vāṁ: This word means "you" or "your." It personalizes the mantra, addressing the listener or the divine directly.
  • nakṣanto: This term can be interpreted as "those who shine" or "the luminous ones." It refers to enlightened beings or those who have attained knowledge.
  • adraya: This means "the mountains" or "the firm." It symbolizes stability and strength in the face of challenges.
  • āñjan: This word refers to "the eye salve" or "the ointment." It signifies clarity of vision and the ability to see beyond the superficial.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the themes of creation, elevation from ignorance, and the pursuit of a higher path. It encourages individuals to honor their potential and the divine forces that guide them. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, spiritual growth, and community service. By recognizing our strengths and the importance of uplifting ourselves and others, we can create a more enlightened and harmonious society. The mantra serves as a reminder to seek knowledge, honor our journey, and strive for a higher purpose in life.
