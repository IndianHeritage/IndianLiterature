ये ते त्रिर अहन सवितः सवासो दिवे-दिवे सौभगम आसुवन्ति |इन्द्रो dyावापृथिवी सिन्धुर अद्भिर आदित्यैर नो अदितिः शर्मा यंषत ||
ye te trir ahan savitaḥ savāso dive-dive saubhagam āsuvanti | indro dyāvāpṛthivī sindhur adbhir ādityair no aditiḥ śarma yaṁsat ||
  • Translation: "Those who, O Savita, three times a day, bring forth auspiciousness, may Indra, the Sky and Earth, and the rivers with their waters, grant us protection and happiness."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye te: "Those who" - Refers to individuals or entities that perform certain actions.
    • trir ahan: "three times a day" - Indicates regularity and discipline in practice.
    • savitāḥ: "O Savita" - Savita is a deity representing the sun, symbolizing light, energy, and life.
    • savāso: "bring forth" - Suggests the act of generating or producing something beneficial.
    • dive-dive: "day by day" - Emphasizes the continuity and consistency of the actions.
    • saubhagam: "auspiciousness" - Refers to good fortune, prosperity, and well-being.
    • āsuvanti: "may they grant" - A plea for blessings and positive outcomes.
    • indro: "Indra" - The king of gods, representing strength and protection.
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: "Sky and Earth" - Symbolizes the duality of existence and the balance of nature.
    • sindhur: "rivers" - Represents the flow of life and resources.
    • adbhir: "with their waters" - Suggests nourishment and sustenance.
    • ādityair: "with the sun" - Refers to the life-giving energy of the sun.
    • no: "for us" - A personal plea for blessings.
    • aditiḥ: "protection" - Refers to safety and security.
    • śarma: "happiness" - Represents joy and contentment.
    • yaṁsat: "may it be" - A hopeful expression for the fulfillment of desires.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, the practice of gratitude and mindfulness can be seen as a way of invoking auspiciousness. For instance, taking a moment each morning to reflect on what one is thankful for can set a positive tone for the day.
    • Regular rituals, such as meditation or prayer, performed three times a day can help individuals cultivate a sense of peace and connection to the divine, similar to the invocation of Savita.
    • Community gatherings that focus on sharing resources and supporting one another can embody the essence of Indra, promoting strength and protection within the community.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of regular, disciplined actions that lead to auspiciousness and well-being. It highlights the interconnectedness of nature, the divine, and human efforts. In real life, this can be applied through consistent practices of gratitude, community support, and mindfulness, ultimately leading to a harmonious and prosperous existence. By invoking the blessings of the divine and nature, individuals can seek protection and happiness in their lives.
