असर्जि कलशाम् अभि मीळ्हे सप्तिर न वाजयुः |पुनानो वाचं जनयन्न असिष्यदत ||
asarji kalaśām̐ abhi mīḻhe saptir na vājayuḥ | punāno vācaṁ janayann asiṣyadat ||
  • asarji: This term can be interpreted as "to create" or "to bring forth." It signifies the act of manifestation or emergence.
  • kalaśām̐: Refers to "the pot" or "vessel," often symbolizing abundance and the nurturing aspect of creation.
  • abhi: This word means "towards" or "in the direction of," indicating a movement or intention towards something.
  • mīḻhe: Translates to "to be filled" or "to be satisfied," suggesting a state of fulfillment or completeness.
  • saptir: This term can be understood as "seven," which often symbolizes completeness or wholeness in various cultures.
  • na: A negation, meaning "not," indicating the absence of something.
  • vājayuḥ: This can be interpreted as "to be strong" or "to be powerful," often associated with vitality and energy.
  • punāno: Means "renewing" or "revitalizing," suggesting a process of regeneration or rebirth.
  • vācaṁ: Refers to "speech" or "words," highlighting the importance of communication and expression.
  • janayann: This means "to generate" or "to produce," indicating the act of creation or bringing forth something new.
  • asiṣyadat: Can be interpreted as "to bless" or "to bestow," suggesting a divine or positive influence.

The mantra can be understood as a call for the creation and manifestation of abundance and strength through the power of words and communication. It emphasizes the importance of intention and the act of bringing forth new ideas or energies into the world.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to manifest their goals and aspirations through positive thinking and effective communication.
  • Creative Endeavors: Artists, writers, and creators can invoke this mantra to inspire their work, emphasizing the importance of expressing their ideas and renewing their creative energies.
  • Community Building: In social or community settings, this mantra can serve as a reminder of the power of collective speech and action in creating a supportive and abundant environment.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the interconnectedness of creation, communication, and abundance. It serves as a powerful reminder that our words and intentions can shape our reality, encouraging us to actively participate in the process of creation and renewal in our lives and communities.
