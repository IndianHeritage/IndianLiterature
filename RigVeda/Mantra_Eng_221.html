महī dyauḥ pṛthivī ca na इमं यज्ञं मिमिक्षताम् |पिपृतां नो भरīmabhiḥ ||
mahī dyauḥ pṛthivī ca na imaṁ yajñam mimikṣatām | pipṛtāṁ no bharīmabhiḥ ||
  • Translation: "The Earth and the Sky observe this sacrifice; may they fill us with their abundance."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • mahī: Great, vast, or expansive.
    • dyauḥ: Sky or heaven.
    • pṛthivī: Earth or the physical world.
    • yajñam: Sacrifice or offering, often in the context of rituals.
    • mimikṣatām: To observe or witness.
    • pipṛtāṁ: To fill or provide.
    • no: Us or our.
    • bharīmabhiḥ: With abundance or wealth.
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra emphasizes the relationship between humanity and the natural world, represented by the Earth and the Sky.
    • It highlights the importance of rituals and sacrifices in seeking blessings and abundance from nature.
    • The invocation of the Earth and Sky suggests a holistic view of existence, where human actions are interconnected with cosmic forces.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often perform rituals to honor the Earth and Sky, seeking favorable weather and bountiful harvests.
    • Environmental movements emphasize the need to respect and protect nature, aligning with the idea of living in harmony with the Earth and Sky.
    • In personal life, individuals may engage in practices of gratitude, acknowledging the abundance they receive from nature, such as food, water, and shelter.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of our dependence on the natural world and the importance of respecting it.
    • It encourages us to engage in practices that honor the Earth and Sky, fostering a sense of gratitude and responsibility.
    • In a modern context, it can inspire sustainable living and environmental stewardship, ensuring that we maintain a balance with nature for future generations.
