यावः शर्मा शशमानाय संति त्रिधातूनि दाशुषे यच्छताधि |अस्मभ्यं तानि मरुतो वि यन्त रयिं नो धत्त वृषणः सुवीरम् ||
yā vaḥ śarma śaśamānāya santi tridhātūni dāśuṣe yacchatādhi | asmabhyaṁ tāni maruto vi yanta rayiṁ no dhatta vṛṣaṇaḥ suvīram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yā vaḥ: That which is yours
    • śarma: Protection, comfort, or happiness
    • śaśamānāya: For the one who is flourishing or thriving
    • santi: There are
    • tridhātūni: Three realms or dimensions
    • dāśuṣe: For the one who is devoted or dedicated
    • yacchatādhi: That which is given or bestowed
    • asmabhyaṁ: For us
    • tāni: Those
    • maruto: The winds or the forces of nature
    • vi yanta: Who move or flow
    • rayiṁ: Wealth or prosperity
    • no: For us
    • dhatta: Bestow or grant
    • vṛṣaṇaḥ: The strong or powerful
    • suvīram: Good heroes or valiant ones
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the forces of nature, particularly the Maruts, who are often associated with storms and winds.
    • It emphasizes the importance of protection and prosperity in our lives, suggesting that these elements are interconnected.
    • The mantra calls for the bestowal of wealth and strength upon us, highlighting the need for support from both the natural world and the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of difficulty, individuals often seek protection and support from their community, akin to the mantra's call for divine assistance.
    • In business, the pursuit of prosperity can be seen as a modern reflection of the mantra's request for wealth and strength.
    • Environmental awareness can be linked to the Maruts, as they represent the forces of nature that we must respect and protect for our own well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, nature, and the divine.
    • It encourages us to seek protection and prosperity while acknowledging the forces that govern our existence.
    • In real life, it is applicable in various scenarios, from personal challenges to broader societal issues, urging us to cultivate strength and resilience.
