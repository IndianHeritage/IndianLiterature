मित्रस्तन नो वरुणो देवो आर्यः प्र साधिष्ठेभिः पथिभिर् नयन्तु |ब्रवद् यथा न आद् अरिः सुदासा इषा मदेम सह देवगोपाः ||
mitras tan no varuṇo devo aryaḥ pra sādhiṣṭhebhiḥ pathibhir nayantu | bravad yathā na ād ariḥ sudāsa iṣā madema saha devagopāḥ ||
  • Mitras: This term refers to "friends" or "allies." In the context of the mantra, it signifies the importance of friendship and cooperation in achieving common goals.
  • Tan: This means "that" or "those," indicating a connection to the divine or higher powers.
  • Varuṇo: Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water, representing the moral and ethical aspects of life. He is often associated with the night sky and the celestial order.
  • Devo: This translates to "god" or "divine being," emphasizing the spiritual aspect of the mantra.
  • Aryaḥ: This term means "noble" or "honorable," referring to individuals who uphold righteousness and virtue.
  • Pra sādhiṣṭhebhiḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we be guided" or "may we be led," indicating a desire for divine guidance in our actions.
  • Pathibhir: This means "by the paths," suggesting the various ways or methods we can take in life.
  • Nayantu: This translates to "lead" or "guide," reinforcing the idea of seeking direction from higher powers.
  • Bravad: This means "speak" or "proclaim," indicating the importance of communication and expression in our lives.
  • Yathā: This translates to "as" or "like," drawing a comparison or analogy.
  • Na ād: This means "not" or "no," indicating a negation or rejection of something.
  • Ariḥ: This term means "enemy" or "adversary," highlighting the challenges we face in life.
  • Sudāsa: Referring to a historical figure, Sudāsa represents a person who overcame obstacles and achieved success.
  • Iṣā: This means "may we" or "let us," expressing a wish or desire.
  • Madema: This translates to "may we be" or "let us be," reinforcing the collective aspect of the mantra.
  • Saha: This means "together" or "with," emphasizing unity and collaboration.
  • Devagopāḥ: This term refers to "divine protectors" or "guardians," indicating the presence of higher powers watching over us.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and support in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of friendship, unity, and moral integrity while navigating the challenges we face. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork in professional settings, fostering community relationships, or even in personal development. By invoking the principles of cooperation and divine guidance, individuals can strive for success and harmony in their endeavors, much like Sudāsa, who overcame adversities with the support of his allies and divine forces.
