ओजस्तदस्य तित्विष उभे यत्समवर्तयत् |इन्द्रश्चर्मेव रोदसी ||
ojas tad asya titviṣa ubhe yat samavartayat | indraś carmeva rodasī ||
  • Ojas: This term refers to vitality, energy, or strength. In a real-life context, it can be related to physical health, mental resilience, and overall well-being. For example, a person who practices yoga and maintains a balanced diet may exhibit high ojas, reflecting their vitality.
  • Tad: This word means "that" or "it," indicating a reference to something previously mentioned or understood. In the context of the mantra, it refers to the energy or vitality being discussed.
  • Asya: Meaning "of this" or "of it," this word connects the vitality to the subject at hand, emphasizing its importance in the context of the mantra.
  • Titviṣa: This term can be interpreted as "radiant" or "brilliant." It signifies a state of illumination or enlightenment. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who inspire others through their actions and wisdom, much like a teacher or a leader.
  • Ubhe: Meaning "both," this word suggests a duality or a balance between two aspects. In life, this can refer to the balance between work and personal life, or between physical and mental health.
  • Yat: This translates to "that which" or "what." It introduces the action or state that follows, linking it to the vitality and radiance discussed earlier.
  • Samavartayat: This term means "to revolve" or "to turn." It can symbolize the cyclical nature of life, where energy and vitality are constantly in motion, much like the seasons or the cycles of day and night.
  • Indra: A significant deity in Vedic literature, Indra represents strength, power, and leadership. In a modern context, Indra can symbolize a strong leader or a person who takes charge in challenging situations, much like a project manager who guides a team through difficulties.
  • Carmeva: This term can be interpreted as "like a song" or "in a harmonious manner." It suggests that the actions taken should be done with grace and fluidity, akin to how a musician plays an instrument or how a dancer moves.
  • Rodasī: This word refers to the "two worlds" or "the sky and the earth." It symbolizes the connection between the spiritual and the material realms, reminding us of the importance of grounding our spiritual practices in everyday life.

Conclusion: The mantra "ojas tad asya titviṣa ubhe yat samavartayat | indraś carmeva rodasī ||" emphasizes the significance of vitality and energy in our lives, highlighting the balance between different aspects of existence. It encourages us to embrace our inner strength and radiance while navigating the cycles of life with grace and harmony. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to maintain their health, seek balance in their endeavors, and lead with confidence and compassion, much like a leader guiding their team through challenges. By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can cultivate a life filled with vitality, purpose, and connection to both the material and spiritual worlds.
