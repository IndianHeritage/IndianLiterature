इमं घा वीरो अमृतं दूतं कृत्वीत मर्त्यः |पावकं कृष्णवर्तिनं विहायसम् ||
imaṁ ghā vīro amṛtaṁ dūtaṁ kṛṇvīta martyaḥ | pāvakaṁ kṛṣṇavartaniṁ vihāyasam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imaṁ: this
    • ghā: song or hymn
    • vīro: hero or brave one
    • amṛtaṁ: immortal or eternal
    • dūtaṁ: messenger
    • kṛṇvīta: may create or establish
    • martyaḥ: mortal or human
    • pāvakaṁ: pure or sacred
    • kṛṣṇavartaniṁ: black or dark, often referring to the divine or cosmic aspect
    • vihāyasam: in the sky or atmosphere
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the creation of a sacred hymn or song by a mortal, invoking the qualities of a hero and the essence of immortality.
    • The term "dūtaṁ" suggests the role of a messenger, indicating the importance of communication and the transmission of divine knowledge.
    • The reference to "pāvakaṁ" emphasizes purity, suggesting that the message or hymn should be free from impurities and negativity.
    • "kṛṣṇavartaniṁ" can be interpreted as acknowledging the cosmic forces at play, indicating that the hymn is not just a personal endeavor but a universal one.
    • Finally, "vihāyasam" connects the message to the heavens, suggesting that the hymn has a divine purpose and reaches beyond the earthly realm.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a call for individuals to embrace their inner hero, to strive for greatness while remaining grounded in their humanity.
    • For instance, a leader in a community may embody the qualities of a "vīro" by inspiring others through their actions and words, creating a "ghā" that resonates with the people.
    • In the context of environmental activism, individuals can act as "dūtaṁ" by spreading awareness about sustainability, creating a "pāvakaṁ" message that promotes purity in our relationship with nature.
    • Artists and musicians often channel the essence of "kṛṣṇavartaniṁ" by drawing inspiration from the cosmos, creating works that reflect universal themes and connect with audiences on a deeper level.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of words and the importance of creating messages that are both heroic and pure.
    • It encourages individuals to act as messengers of positivity and inspiration, fostering a sense of community and connection.
    • In a world often filled with negativity, the call to create a sacred hymn can lead to transformative actions that uplift and unite people.
    • Ultimately, the significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to strive for greatness while remaining connected to the divine and the greater cosmos.
