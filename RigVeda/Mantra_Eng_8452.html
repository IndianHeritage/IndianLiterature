त्वां सोम पवमानं स्वाध्योऽनु विप्रासो अमदन्न अवस्यवः |त्वां सुपर्ण आभरद दिवस परिंदो विश्वाभिर मतीभिः परिष्कृतम् ||
tvāṁ soma pavamānaṁ svādhyo 'nu viprāso amadann avasyavaḥ | tvāṁ suparṇa ābharad divas parīndo viśvābhir matibhiḥ pariṣkṛtam ||
  • tvāṁ - This word translates to "you" and refers to the divine essence or the deity being addressed, often interpreted as Soma, the sacred drink in Vedic rituals.
  • soma - Soma is both a deity and a ritual drink that symbolizes immortality and divine inspiration. It is often associated with the moon and represents the nourishing aspects of nature.
  • pavamānaṁ - This means "purifying" or "that which is purified." It signifies the cleansing and uplifting qualities of Soma, which is believed to elevate the mind and spirit.
  • svādhyo - This term can be understood as "self-satisfied" or "self-pleasing," indicating a state of contentment and fulfillment that comes from within.
  • anu - This preposition means "after" or "following," suggesting a sequence or a result that follows the action of consuming Soma.
  • viprāso - This refers to "the wise" or "the learned," often denoting the priests or sages who perform rituals and seek knowledge.
  • amadann - This word means "they have consumed" or "they have partaken," indicating the act of drinking Soma and experiencing its effects.
  • avasyavaḥ - This translates to "they who are destined" or "those who are meant to," suggesting a sense of purpose or inevitability in the consumption of Soma.
  • suparṇa - This means "well-winged" or "beautifully winged," often associated with the divine bird Garuda, symbolizing speed, power, and the ability to transcend limitations.
  • ābharad - This translates to "brought" or "carried," indicating the act of bringing forth or delivering something significant.
  • divas - This means "day" or "heaven," representing the celestial realm and the light of consciousness.
  • parīndo - This term can be interpreted as "surrounding" or "encompassing," suggesting a holistic approach to understanding and experiencing life.
  • viśvābhir - This means "with all" or "by all," indicating inclusivity and the collective experience of wisdom and knowledge.
  • matibhiḥ - This translates to "with minds" or "with thoughts," emphasizing the importance of mental clarity and intention in the pursuit of knowledge.
  • pariṣkṛtam - This means "refined" or "perfected," suggesting the process of purification and enhancement of one's understanding and consciousness.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine inspiration and knowledge through the consumption of Soma, which symbolizes the pursuit of higher consciousness and self-realization. In real life, this can be applied to various practices such as meditation, mindfulness, and the pursuit of wisdom through study and reflection. The significance lies in the understanding that true fulfillment and enlightenment come from within, and by refining our thoughts and intentions, we can elevate our existence and connect with the divine. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek purity in our actions and thoughts, fostering a deeper connection with ourselves and the universe.
