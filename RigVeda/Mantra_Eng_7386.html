अर्थिनो यान्ति चेदर्थं गच्छानिद्ददुषो रातिम् |ववृज्युस्तृष्यतः कामम् ||
arthino yanti ced arthaṁ gacchān id daduṣo rātim | vavṛjyus tṛṣyataḥ kāmam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • arthino: Those who seek wealth or prosperity
    • yanti: Go or proceed
    • ced: If
    • arthaṁ: Wealth or resources
    • gacchān: Going or moving towards
    • id: This
    • daduṣo: Of those who are not righteous or who do wrong
    • rātim: Joy or happiness
    • vavṛjyus: They will be deprived or removed
    • tṛṣyataḥ: From thirst or desire
    • kāmam: Desire or longing
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra suggests that those who seek wealth and prosperity, if they pursue it through wrongful means, will ultimately be deprived of true joy and satisfaction.
    • It emphasizes the importance of righteousness in the pursuit of material wealth.
    • Real-life example: A person who engages in unethical business practices may initially gain wealth but may face legal issues, loss of reputation, and personal dissatisfaction, leading to a lack of true happiness.
  • Significance:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder that the means of acquiring wealth are as important as the wealth itself.
    • It encourages individuals to pursue their goals with integrity and ethical considerations.
    • In a broader context, it can be applied to various aspects of life, including career choices, relationships, and personal development.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the relationship between ethical behavior and the attainment of true happiness.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals must choose between right and wrong in their pursuit of success.
    • Ultimately, it teaches that true fulfillment comes from righteous actions rather than mere accumulation of wealth.
