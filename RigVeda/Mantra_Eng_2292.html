यो मे राजन् युज्यो वा सखा वा स्वप्ने भयम् भीरवे मह्यम् आह |स्टेनो वा यो दिप्सति नो वृको वा त्वं तस्माद वरुण पाहि अस्मान् ||
yo me rājan yujyo vā sakhā vā svapne bhayam bhīrave mahyam āha | steno vā yo dipsati no vṛko vā tvaṁ tasmād varuṇa pāhy asmān ||
  • Translation: "O Varuna, whether he is a king, a friend, or a companion, who brings fear to me in dreams, or who is a thief or a wolf, protect us from him."

  • Meaning of Key Words:
    • Varuna: The Vedic god associated with water, cosmic order, and moral authority.
    • Rājan: Refers to a king or ruler, symbolizing authority and power.
    • Sakhā: Means friend or companion, indicating trust and camaraderie.
    • Bhayam: Translates to fear, representing the anxieties and threats we face.
    • Steno: Refers to a thief, symbolizing deceit and betrayal.
    • Vṛko: Means wolf, often associated with danger and predation.

  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra reflects the human experience of fear and the need for protection from various threats, whether they come from authority figures, friends, or external dangers.
    • In real life, we often encounter situations where we feel vulnerable, such as in competitive environments like workplaces or social settings where trust can be tested.
    • The invocation of Varuna signifies a plea for divine protection against these fears and threats, emphasizing the importance of moral order and justice in our lives.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, an employee may feel threatened by a manipulative colleague (the "thief") who undermines their work. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek protection and support from higher authorities or mentors.
    • In personal relationships, one might experience fear of betrayal from a close friend. The mantra encourages individuals to seek guidance and protection from a higher moral authority, reinforcing the value of trust and integrity.
    • In times of societal unrest, communities may feel threatened by external forces (the "wolf"). This mantra can inspire collective action and reliance on shared values to overcome fear and restore safety.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the universal human experience of fear and the need for protection from various forms of threat, whether they are personal, social, or existential.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition of the divine as a source of strength and security, encouraging individuals to seek moral guidance and support in challenging times.
    • In practical terms, it serves as a reminder to cultivate trust, integrity, and community, fostering resilience against the fears that life presents.
