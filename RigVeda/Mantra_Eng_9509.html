ऋतं शंसन्त ऋजु दीद्याना दिवस पुत्रासो असुरस्य वीराः | विप्रम पदम अंगिरसो दधानā यज्ञस्य धाम प्रथमं मनन्त ||
ṛtaṁ śaṁsanta ṛju dīdhyānā divas putrāso asurasya vīrāḥ | vipram padam aṅgiraso dadhānā yajñasya dhāma prathamam mananta ||
  • ṛtaṁ: This term refers to the cosmic order or truth that governs the universe. It signifies the principle of righteousness and the natural law that sustains harmony in the world.
  • śaṁsanta: This word means to praise or to extol. It indicates the act of acknowledging and celebrating the virtues of the divine or the righteous.
  • ṛju: This translates to 'straight' or 'honest'. It emphasizes the importance of integrity and straightforwardness in one's actions and thoughts.
  • dīdhyānā: This term means 'illuminating' or 'shining'. It suggests the idea of enlightenment and the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom.
  • divas: This word means 'day' or 'heaven'. It can symbolize the divine realm or the higher state of consciousness.
  • putrāso: This translates to 'sons' or 'children'. It signifies the next generation and the continuity of values and teachings.
  • asurasya: This term refers to 'the divine' or 'the powerful'. It can denote the forces of good that protect and guide humanity.
  • vīrāḥ: This means 'heroes' or 'warriors'. It represents those who are courageous and stand up for righteousness and truth.
  • vipram: This word means 'the learned' or 'the wise'. It highlights the importance of knowledge and wisdom in guiding society.
  • padam: This translates to 'place' or 'position'. It signifies the importance of finding one's rightful place in the cosmic order.
  • aṅgiraso: This term refers to the sages or seers. It emphasizes the role of enlightened beings in imparting wisdom and guidance.
  • dadhānā: This means 'holding' or 'possessing'. It indicates the act of maintaining or upholding values and principles.
  • yajñasya: This word refers to 'sacrifice' or 'offering'. It signifies the importance of selflessness and the act of giving for the greater good.
  • dhāma: This translates to 'abode' or 'place of residence'. It can symbolize the ultimate goal or the divine realm.
  • prathamam: This means 'first' or 'foremost'. It indicates the priority of certain values or actions in life.
  • mananta: This term means 'infinite' or 'endless'. It signifies the eternal nature of truth and righteousness.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of living in accordance with the cosmic order (ṛtaṁ) and the importance of praising and upholding truth and righteousness. It highlights the role of enlightened beings and the next generation in maintaining these values. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in education, where the wisdom of the learned (vipram) guides the youth (putrāso) towards a righteous path. It also serves as a reminder for individuals to act with integrity (ṛju) and to contribute selflessly (yajñasya) to the well-being of society. Ultimately, it calls for a collective effort to uphold truth and righteousness in our daily lives, ensuring a harmonious existence for all.
