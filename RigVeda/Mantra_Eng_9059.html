यस्यानक्षा दुहिता जात्व आस कस्तां विद्वाम् अभि मन्याते अन्धाम् |कतरो मेनिम प्रति तं मुचाते य इं वहाते य इं वा वर्यत् ||
yasyānakṣā duhitā jātv āsa kas tāṁ vidvām̐ abhi manyāte andhām | kataro menim prati tam mucāte ya īṁ vahāte ya īṁ vā vareyāt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasyā: of whom
    • anākṣā: the daughter (or offspring) who is not blind
    • duhitā: daughter
    • jātv: having been born
    • āsa: is (or exists)
    • kas: who
    • tām: her
    • vidvām̐: wise (or knowledgeable)
    • abhi: towards
    • manyāte: considers
    • andhām: blind (or ignorant)
    • kataro: which
    • menim: thought (or intention)
    • prati: towards
    • tam: that
    • mucāte: releases (or frees)
    • ya: who
    • īṁ: that (or this)
    • vahāte: carries (or brings)
    • ya: who
    • īṁ: that (or this)
    • vā: or
    • vareyāt: is worthy (or desirable)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the relationship between knowledge, perception, and the essence of existence.
    • It suggests that a wise person recognizes the true nature of their offspring (or ideas) and does not consider them to be blind or ignorant.
    • The mantra poses a question about the nature of thought and intention, asking which thoughts lead to liberation and which do not.
    • In real life, this can be related to parenting, where a wise parent nurtures their child's understanding and perception of the world, guiding them away from ignorance.
    • It also reflects on the importance of self-awareness and the pursuit of knowledge, encouraging individuals to seek wisdom and clarity in their thoughts and actions.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in shaping our perceptions and actions.
    • In a real-life scenario, it can be applied in educational settings, where teachers and parents play a crucial role in guiding the younger generation towards enlightenment.
    • It encourages individuals to reflect on their thoughts and intentions, promoting a life of awareness and conscious decision-making.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that true understanding leads to liberation from ignorance, fostering a more enlightened society.
