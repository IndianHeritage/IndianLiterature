इहोपा याता शवसो नपातः साउधन्वना ऋभवो माप भूत |अस्मिन हि वः सवने रत्नधेयं गमन्त्व इन्द्रं अनु वो मदासः ||
ihopa yāta śavaso napātaḥ saudhanvanā ṛbhavo māpa bhūta | asmin hi vaḥ savane ratnadheyaṁ gamantv indram anu vo madāsaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ihopa: Here, indeed
    • yāta: Go forth
    • śavaso: Of the powerful
    • napātaḥ: Descendants
    • saudhanvanā: With wealth
    • ṛbhavo: The wise
    • māpa: May not
    • bhūta: Be lost
    • asmin: In this
    • hi: Indeed
    • vaḥ: Your
    • savane: Sacrifice
    • ratnadheyaṁ: Worthy of riches
    • gamantv: May go
    • indram: To Indra
    • anu: Following
    • vo: You
    • madāsaḥ: With intoxication
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the powerful and wise descendants who are rich and prosperous.
    • It emphasizes the importance of not losing one's wealth and prosperity in the pursuit of spiritual goals.
    • The reference to Indra, the king of gods, suggests a desire for divine blessings and support in achieving success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be seen as a reminder for entrepreneurs to seek wisdom and guidance while ensuring their ventures remain profitable.
    • In personal life, it encourages individuals to maintain their values and integrity while striving for success, ensuring that their achievements do not come at the cost of their principles.
    • In community settings, it can inspire collective efforts towards prosperity, urging members to support each other in achieving common goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for balance between material wealth and spiritual wisdom.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to community development, reminding us to seek divine guidance while pursuing our goals.
    • Ultimately, it teaches that true prosperity is achieved not just through wealth, but through wisdom, integrity, and community support.
