मन्त्र: विडुर्गा विद्विषः पुरो घ्नन्ति राजान एषाम् |नयन्ति दुरितातिरः ||
vi durgā vi dviṣaḥ puro ghnanti rājāna eṣām | nayanti duritā tiraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vi: away, apart
    • durgā: obstacles, difficulties
    • vi: away, apart
    • dviṣaḥ: enemies, those who wish harm
    • puro: in front, before
    • ghnanti: strike, destroy
    • rājāna: kings, rulers
    • eṣām: of these, of them
    • nayanti: lead, guide
    • duritā: misfortunes, troubles
    • tiraḥ: across, beyond
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks about overcoming obstacles and enemies.
    • It suggests that rulers or leaders can destroy their adversaries and lead their people beyond misfortunes.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a CEO may face competition (dviṣaḥ) from rival companies (enemies). By strategizing effectively (rājāna), they can overcome these challenges (durgā) and lead their organization to success (nayanti duritā tiraḥ).
    • In personal life, an individual may encounter various challenges such as financial difficulties or health issues. By seeking guidance and support (rājāna), they can navigate through these hardships (durgā) and emerge stronger (nayanti duritā tiraḥ).
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of leadership and resilience in the face of adversity.
    • It serves as a reminder that with determination and the right guidance, one can overcome obstacles and lead others to safety and success.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, from personal struggles to professional challenges, encouraging individuals to take charge and navigate through difficulties.
