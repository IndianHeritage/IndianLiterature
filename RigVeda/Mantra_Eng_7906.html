तं त्वा हिन्वन्ति वेधसः पवमान गिरावृधम् |इन्दव इन्द्राय मत्सरम् ||
taṁ tvā hinvanti vedhasaḥ pavamāna girāvṛdham | indav indrāya matsaram ||
  • taṁ tvā hinvanti vedhasaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "Those who are wise and knowledgeable (vedhasaḥ) invoke you (tvā)." Here, the term "vedhasaḥ" refers to the wise or learned individuals who possess deep understanding and insight.
  • pavamāna girāvṛdham: This part means "the one who is purified (pavamāna) and is exalted in speech (girāvṛdham)." The term "pavamāna" signifies purity, often associated with the divine or spiritual essence, while "girāvṛdham" refers to the elevation or enhancement of speech, indicating the importance of communication and expression in spiritual practices.
  • indav indrāya matsaram: This translates to "for the sake of Indra (indrāya), free from jealousy (matsaram)." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with strength, power, and leadership. The term "matsaram" implies the absence of jealousy, highlighting the importance of harmony and unity in relationships and endeavors.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, purity, and harmonious relationships. It suggests that those who are knowledgeable and pure in their intentions can invoke divine blessings and support.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:
    • Education: Students and educators can embody the qualities of wisdom and purity in their pursuit of knowledge, fostering an environment free from jealousy and competition.
    • Workplace: In professional settings, collaboration and teamwork can thrive when individuals focus on shared goals rather than personal rivalries, invoking the spirit of Indra for strength and unity.
    • Personal Relationships: In friendships and family dynamics, practicing open communication and understanding can help eliminate feelings of jealousy, leading to stronger bonds.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the values of wisdom, purity, and unity. By embodying these principles, individuals can create a more harmonious and supportive environment in various aspects of life, ultimately leading to personal and collective growth.
