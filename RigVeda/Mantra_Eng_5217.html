मन्त्र: असन्नित तवे आहवानानि भूरी भुवो विश्वेभिः सुमना अनीकैः | स्तुतश्चिद अग्ने श्रिण्विषे गृणानः स्वयं वर्धस्व तन्वं सुजात ||
asann it tve āhavanāni bhūri bhuvo viśvebhiḥ sumanā anīkaiḥ | stutaś cid agne śṛṇviṣe gṛṇānaḥ svayaṁ vardhasva tanvaṁ sujāta ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asann: not existing, absent
    • it: here, in this place
    • tve: you
    • āhavanāni: offerings, invocations
    • bhūri: abundant, plentiful
    • bhuvo: of the worlds, of existence
    • viśvebhiḥ: with all, by all
    • sumanā: with good thoughts, auspicious
    • anīkaiḥ: with groups, with multitudes
    • stutaś: praised, glorified
    • cid: consciousness, awareness
    • agne: O Agni, the fire god
    • śṛṇviṣe: listen, hear
    • gṛṇānaḥ: singing, chanting
    • svayaṁ: yourself
    • vardhasva: may you grow, may you prosper
    • tanvaṁ: body, form
    • sujāta: well-born, auspiciously born
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, emphasizing the importance of offerings and the collective participation of all beings.
    • The phrase "asann it tve āhavanāni" suggests that even in absence, the offerings are made with intention and purpose.
    • It highlights the significance of community and collective consciousness in spiritual practices, as indicated by "viśvebhiḥ sumanā anīkaiḥ."
    • The call to Agni to "listen" and "grow" signifies a desire for divine presence and blessings in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, such as weddings or festivals, the collective participation of family and friends mirrors the essence of this mantra, where everyone contributes to the sacredness of the event.
    • In community service, when people come together with good intentions ("sumanā") to help others, they embody the spirit of this mantra, invoking a sense of unity and purpose.
    • In personal growth, the idea of "vardhasva tanvaṁ sujāta" can be applied to self-improvement practices, where individuals seek to grow and prosper in their personal and professional lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of intention, community, and divine connection in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that our actions, whether in rituals or daily life, should be rooted in good thoughts and collective effort.
    • By invoking Agni, we seek not only personal growth but also the prosperity of our communities, making this mantra relevant in various aspects of life, from spiritual practices to social interactions.
