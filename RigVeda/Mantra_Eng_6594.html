मन्त्र: उता नः सिन्धुर अपां तन् मरुतस् तद अश्विनाः |इन्द्रो विष्णुर मीढ्वांसः सजोषसः ||
uta naḥ sindhur apāṁ tan marutas tad aśvinā | indro viṣṇur mīḍhvāṁsaḥ sajoṣasaḥ ||
  • uta naḥ - "May we" or "Let us" - This phrase signifies a collective aspiration or desire, emphasizing the importance of community and shared goals.
  • sindhur apāṁ - "the ocean of waters" - Here, "sindhu" refers to the vastness of water, symbolizing abundance, life, and the nurturing aspects of nature. In real life, this can be likened to the oceans and rivers that sustain ecosystems and human life.
  • tan marutas - "the Maruts" - The Maruts are storm deities, representing the forces of nature, particularly wind and storms. They embody strength and vitality, reminding us of the power of natural elements in our lives.
  • tad aśvinā - "that which is auspicious" - The Aśvins are twin deities associated with health, medicine, and well-being. This phrase suggests a connection to positive outcomes and the importance of seeking good fortune and health.
  • indro viṣṇur - "Indra and Vishnu" - Indra is the king of gods and represents power and authority, while Vishnu symbolizes preservation and protection. Together, they signify the balance of strength and sustenance in life.
  • mīḍhvāṁsaḥ - "may they be pleased" - This phrase expresses a desire for the deities' favor and blessings, highlighting the human need for divine support in endeavors.
  • sajoṣasaḥ - "with harmony" - This emphasizes the importance of unity and cooperation among people, suggesting that collective efforts lead to success and fulfillment.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a profound understanding of the interconnectedness of nature, divinity, and human aspirations. It calls for collective efforts towards well-being, harmony, and the pursuit of auspiciousness in life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in community initiatives, environmental conservation efforts, and health awareness campaigns, where the collaboration of individuals leads to greater outcomes. The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking blessings from higher powers while working together for a common good, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in our lives.
