ते वो हृदे मनसे सन्तु यज्ञा जुष्टासो अद्य घृतनिर्णिजो गुह् |प्र वः सुतासो हरयन्त पूर्णाः क्रत्वे दक्षाय हर्षयन्त पीताः ||
te vo hṛde manase santu yajñā juṣṭāso adya ghṛtanirṇijo guḥ | pra vaḥ sutāso harayanta pūrṇāḥ kratve dakṣāya harṣayanta pītāḥ ||
  • te vo hṛde manase santu: This phrase translates to "May they be in your heart and mind." It emphasizes the importance of intention and sincerity in one's actions. In real life, this can be seen in situations where individuals engage in community service or charitable acts, driven by genuine compassion and empathy.
  • yajñā juṣṭāso adya: This means "today, may the sacrifices be pleasing." It refers to the rituals and offerings made to deities or the divine. In modern contexts, this can relate to the idea of giving back to society, such as participating in environmental conservation efforts or supporting local businesses, which can be seen as a form of sacrifice for the greater good.
  • ghṛtanirṇijo guḥ: This translates to "the clarified butter (ghee) is the essence." Ghee is often used in Vedic rituals as a symbol of purity and nourishment. In everyday life, this can be likened to the importance of nurturing relationships and fostering a supportive environment, akin to how ghee enhances the flavor of food.
  • pra vaḥ sutāso harayanta: This means "may the sons (or offspring) bring joy." It highlights the significance of family and community. In real life, this can be observed in family gatherings or celebrations where the presence of loved ones brings happiness and strengthens bonds.
  • pūrṇāḥ kratve dakṣāya: This translates to "full of strength and skill." It signifies the importance of being capable and resourceful. In practical terms, this can be seen in professional settings where individuals strive for excellence and contribute effectively to their teams, showcasing their skills and abilities.
  • harṣayanta pītāḥ: This means "may they bring joy." It emphasizes the role of happiness and positivity in our lives. In real life, this can be reflected in acts of kindness, such as volunteering or simply being there for a friend in need, which can uplift spirits and foster a sense of community.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of intention, sacrifice, nourishment, joy, strength, and positivity. Its significance lies in the reminder to approach life with a sincere heart, to engage in acts that benefit others, and to cultivate joy and strength within ourselves and our communities. In today's world, this mantra can inspire individuals to contribute positively to society, nurture relationships, and strive for personal and collective growth, ultimately leading to a harmonious existence.
