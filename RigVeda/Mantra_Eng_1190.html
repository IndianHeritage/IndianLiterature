अश्रवं हि भूридावत्तरा वां विजामातुर उता वा घा स्यालात् |अथा सोमस्य प्रयती युवभ्यां इन्द्राग्नī स्तोमं जनयामि नव्यम् ||
aśravaṁ hi bhūridāvattarā vāṁ vijāmātur uta vā ghā syālāt | athā somasya prayatī yuvabhyām indrāgnī stomaṁ janayāmi navyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśravaṁ: flow, stream, or movement
    • hi: indeed, certainly
    • bhūridāvattarā: the one who is rich in offerings
    • vāṁ: your
    • vijāmātur: of the wise mother
    • uta: or
    • vā: or
    • ghā: to be, to exist
    • syālāt: may it be so, may it happen
    • athā: then, therefore
    • somasya: of the soma (sacred drink)
    • prayatī: effort, endeavor
    • yuvabhyām: with the young ones
    • indrāgnī: Indra and Agni (deities)
    • stomaṁ: hymn, praise
    • janayāmi: I generate, I create
    • navyam: new, fresh
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the flow of offerings and the richness of the divine connection.
    • The reference to "wise mother" suggests a nurturing aspect of the divine, emphasizing the importance of wisdom in our endeavors.
    • The invocation of Indra and Agni highlights the significance of these deities in Vedic rituals, representing strength and purification.
    • The act of generating a new hymn signifies the continuous evolution of spiritual practice and the importance of innovation in worship.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of "flow" can be related to the idea of being in the zone or experiencing a state of flow in creative endeavors, such as writing, painting, or sports.
    • The nurturing aspect of the "wise mother" can be seen in the role of mentors or educators who guide and support individuals in their personal and professional growth.
    • Indra and Agni can be likened to the forces of nature that we respect and seek to understand, such as the power of storms (Indra) and the transformative power of fire (Agni).
    • The creation of new hymns can be compared to the innovation in spiritual practices, where individuals or communities adapt ancient traditions to fit contemporary contexts.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings, wisdom, and the divine connection in our lives.
    • It encourages us to recognize the flow of energy and creativity in our endeavors and to honor the nurturing aspects of wisdom.
    • In a broader sense, it reminds us of the significance of tradition while also embracing innovation in our spiritual practices.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a call to engage with the divine in a meaningful way, fostering a sense of community and connection with the forces that shape our lives.
