मन्त्र: चित्तिर ā उपबरहनं चक्षुर ā अभ्यञ्जनम् | द्यौः भूमिः कोश āsīd यद आयात सूर्यā पतिम् ||
cittir ā upabarhaṇaṁ cakṣur ā abhyañjanam | dyaur bhūmiḥ kośa āsīd yad ayāt sūryā patim ||
  • cittir ā upabarhaṇaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the mind is the source of all creation." In real life, this emphasizes the power of thoughts and intentions. For example, when someone sets a goal and visualizes it, they are using their mind to create a path toward that goal.
  • cakṣur ā abhyañjanam: This translates to "the eye is the means of perception." It highlights the importance of perception in understanding the world around us. For instance, a photographer uses their eye to capture beauty, showing how perception can transform reality into art.
  • dyaur bhūmiḥ: This means "the sky and the earth." It signifies the duality of existence and the interconnectedness of all things. In real life, this can be seen in environmental awareness, where the health of the earth (nature) is directly linked to the well-being of humanity (the sky).
  • kośa āsīd: This phrase translates to "there was a sheath." In a metaphorical sense, it refers to layers of existence or consciousness. In practical terms, this can relate to personal development, where individuals peel back layers of their personality to discover their true selves.
  • yad ayāt sūryā patim: This means "when the sun was the lord." The sun symbolizes energy, life, and enlightenment. In real life, this can be seen in how sunlight is essential for growth, both in nature (photosynthesis) and in personal growth (inspiration and motivation).

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound truths about the mind, perception, existence, and the interconnectedness of life. Its significance lies in the understanding that our thoughts shape our reality, perception influences our experiences, and the natural world is a reflection of our inner selves. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages mindfulness, self-awareness, and a deeper appreciation for the environment, reminding us that we are all part of a larger cosmic order. By applying these principles, individuals can foster personal growth, enhance their creativity, and cultivate a harmonious relationship with the world around them.
