मन्त्र: दिव्य अन्यः सदनं चक्र उच् चापृथिव्यां अन्यो अध्य अन्तरिक्षे |ताव अस्मभ्यम् पूर्ववारम् पुरुक्षं रायस पोषं विश्यातां नाभिम् अस्मे ||
divy a1nyaḥ sadanaṁ cakra uccā pṛthivyām anyo adhy antarikṣe | tāv asmabhyam puruvāram purukṣuṁ rāyas poṣaṁ vi ṣyatāṁ nābhim asme ||
  • Divy a1nyaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "divine or celestial." It signifies the higher, spiritual aspects of existence that transcend the mundane.
  • Sadanaṁ: This means "abode" or "dwelling." It refers to a place of residence, which can be both physical and spiritual.
  • Cakra: Translated as "wheel" or "circle," it symbolizes the cyclical nature of life and the universe, representing continuity and the interconnectedness of all things.
  • Uccā: This word means "high" or "elevated," indicating a state of being that is above the ordinary, often associated with spiritual elevation.
  • Pṛthivyām: This refers to "the earth," grounding the divine and elevated concepts in the physical realm.
  • Anyo adhy antarikṣe: This can be understood as "other than in the atmosphere," suggesting a distinction between the earthly and the celestial realms.
  • Tāv asmabhyam: This phrase means "may they grant us," indicating a plea or request for blessings or assistance.
  • Puruvāram: This translates to "the ancient" or "the primordial," connecting the present with the wisdom of the past.
  • Purukṣuṁ: Meaning "many beings" or "various forms," it emphasizes the diversity of life and existence.
  • Rāyas: This word means "wealth" or "prosperity," highlighting the importance of abundance in various forms.
  • Poṣaṁ: Translated as "nourishment" or "sustenance," it signifies the need for support and sustenance in life.
  • Vi ṣyatāṁ: This means "may it be fulfilled" or "may it manifest," expressing a desire for the realization of aspirations.
  • Nābhim asme: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we have a center" or "may we have a foundation," indicating the importance of stability and grounding in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound understanding of the relationship between the divine, the earthly, and the interconnectedness of all beings. It serves as a reminder of the need for spiritual elevation while remaining grounded in reality. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as seeking guidance in times of uncertainty, fostering a sense of community and diversity, and striving for personal and collective prosperity. It encourages individuals to seek nourishment—both physical and spiritual—and to recognize the importance of a stable foundation in their lives. Ultimately, it emphasizes the significance of balance between the celestial and the earthly, urging us to aspire for higher ideals while being rooted in our reality.
