अस्य व्रते सजोषसो विश्वे देवासो अद्रुहः |स्पार्हा भवन्ति रन्तयो जुषन्त यत ||
asya vrate sajoṣaso viśve devāso adruhaḥ | spārhā bhavanti rantayo juṣanta yat ||
  • Mantra: "asya vrate sajoṣaso viśve devāso adruhaḥ | spārhā bhavanti rantayo juṣanta yat ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asya: of this
    • vrate: vow or commitment
    • sajoṣaso: with joy or enthusiasm
    • viśve: all or universal
    • devāso: the gods or divine beings
    • adruhaḥ: without enmity or harm
    • spārhā: desires or aspirations
    • bhavanti: become or manifest
    • rantayo: those who are joyful or happy
    • juṣanta: enjoying or delighting in
    • yat: that which
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about a commitment or vow that is made with joy and enthusiasm.
    • It emphasizes the importance of harmony and goodwill among all divine beings, suggesting that true divinity is free from enmity.
    • The mantra also highlights that when desires are aligned with joy and positivity, they manifest into reality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, when team members commit to a project with enthusiasm and goodwill, they are more likely to achieve their goals successfully.
    • In personal relationships, fostering an environment free from conflict and filled with mutual respect can lead to deeper connections and happiness.
    • In community service, when individuals come together with a shared joyful purpose, they can create significant positive change in society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the power of positive intentions and harmonious relationships in achieving collective goals.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, professional environments, and community interactions.
    • By embracing joy, goodwill, and a commitment to harmony, individuals can create a more fulfilling and prosperous life.
