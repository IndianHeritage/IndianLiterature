उद īर्ष्वातः पतिवती ह्य ए3षा विश्वावसुः नमसā गीरभिर īले |अन्यां इच्छ पितृषदं व्यक्तां स ते भागो जनुषा तस्य विद्धि ||
ud īrṣvātaḥ pativatī hy e3ṣā viśvāvasuṁ namasā gīrbhir īḻe | anyām iccha pitṛṣadaṁ vyaktāṁ sa te bhāgo januṣā tasya viddhi ||
  • ud īrṣvātaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "having risen up" or "having elevated oneself." It signifies the act of striving for higher goals or aspirations.
  • pativatī: This term refers to "the one who is devoted" or "the one who is faithful." It emphasizes loyalty and commitment, whether to a cause, a person, or a principle.
  • hy e3ṣā: This can be understood as "indeed, this is." It serves to affirm the importance of what follows in the mantra.
  • viśvāvasuṁ: This word translates to "all-encompassing wealth" or "universal abundance." It suggests a connection to prosperity and the richness of life.
  • namasā: This means "with reverence" or "with respect." It indicates an attitude of humility and acknowledgment towards the divine or the greater forces at play.
  • gīrbhir: This term refers to "with hymns" or "with words." It highlights the power of speech and expression in invoking blessings or divine favor.
  • īḻe: This can be interpreted as "I invoke" or "I praise." It is an active call to the divine, seeking connection and support.
  • anyām iccha: This phrase means "I desire another" or "I seek something else." It reflects the human tendency to aspire for more, to seek new experiences or knowledge.
  • pitṛṣadaṁ: This term refers to "the ancestors" or "the forefathers." It acknowledges the importance of lineage and the wisdom passed down through generations.
  • vyaktāṁ: This means "manifest" or "expressed." It signifies the realization of desires or aspirations in the physical world.
  • sa te bhāgo: This translates to "that portion of you" or "that share of yours." It suggests a connection to the divine or the universe, indicating that we are part of a larger whole.
  • januṣā: This term means "by birth" or "through life." It emphasizes the human experience and the journey we undertake in our lives.
  • tasya viddhi: This phrase means "know that" or "understand this." It serves as a directive to gain insight and awareness about the teachings of the mantra.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of striving for higher goals while remaining devoted and respectful. It emphasizes the importance of seeking abundance and wisdom, both from the divine and from our ancestors. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, where one aims to elevate oneself through education and self-improvement, while also honoring the teachings and sacrifices of those who came before. It encourages individuals to express their desires and aspirations with humility and reverence, recognizing their place within the larger tapestry of life. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking knowledge and growth throughout our lives.
