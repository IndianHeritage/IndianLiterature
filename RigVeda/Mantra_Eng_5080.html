प्र सम्राजे बृहत् मन्म नु प्रियं अर्च देवाय वरुणाय सप्रथः |अयं य उर्वी महिना महिव्रतः क्रत्वा विभात्य अजरो न शोचिषा ||
pra samrāje bṛhate manma nu priyam arca devāya varuṇāya saprathaḥ | ayaṁ ya urvī mahinā mahivrataḥ kratvā vibhāty ajaro na śociṣā ||
  • pra samrāje: This phrase can be translated as "in the great kingdom" or "in the vast realm." It signifies the importance of governance and the role of leadership in society.
  • bṛhate: Meaning "great" or "vast," this word emphasizes the magnitude of the subject being discussed, which can refer to the universe, nature, or the divine.
  • manma nu: This translates to "my mind" or "my thoughts." It indicates a personal connection and the importance of intention in one's actions.
  • priyam: Meaning "beloved" or "dear," this word highlights the value of love and affection in relationships, whether with people or the divine.
  • arca: This means "worship" or "adoration." It signifies the act of reverence towards the divine, which is a central theme in many spiritual practices.
  • devāya: Translated as "to the deity" or "to the divine," this word emphasizes the connection between humans and the divine forces that govern the universe.
  • varuṇāya: Referring specifically to Varuna, the Vedic god of water and cosmic order, this word signifies the importance of natural elements and their divine representation.
  • saprathaḥ: This means "together" or "in unity." It emphasizes the importance of community and collective action in achieving goals.
  • ayaṁ ya urvī: This translates to "this earth" or "this land," indicating a connection to the physical world and its significance in our lives.
  • mahinā: Meaning "great" or "powerful," this word signifies strength and the vastness of existence.
  • mahivrataḥ: This translates to "great vow" or "sacred promise," indicating the importance of commitment and dedication in one's life.
  • kratvā: Meaning "by the power of action," this word emphasizes the significance of deeds and their impact on the world.
  • vibhāty: This means "shines" or "illuminates," indicating the idea of enlightenment and the pursuit of knowledge.
  • ajaro: Translated as "immortal" or "eternal," this word signifies the timeless nature of the soul and the divine.
  • na śociṣā: This means "without sorrow" or "free from grief," indicating a state of peace and contentment that can be achieved through spiritual practice.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of the individual with the divine, nature, and the community. It highlights the importance of love, worship, and collective action in achieving a harmonious existence. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community service, environmental conservation, and personal relationships. By fostering unity and commitment to a greater cause, individuals can contribute to a more peaceful and prosperous society. The mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives and the importance of living with intention and purpose.
