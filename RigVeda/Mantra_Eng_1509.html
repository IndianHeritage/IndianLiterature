मन्त्र: उता वाम उषसो बुद्धि साकं सूर्यस्य रश्मिभिः |सुतो मित्राय वरुणाय पीतये चारुर ऋताय पीतये ||
uta vām uṣaso budhi sākaṁ sūryasya raśmibhiḥ | suto mitrāya varuṇāya pītaye cārur ṛtāya pītaye ||
  • uta vām uṣaso budhi sākaṁ sūryasya raśmibhiḥ: This phrase can be translated to "May we, with the dawn's wisdom, be united with the rays of the sun." Here, "uṣaso" refers to the dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and enlightenment. "Budhi" signifies wisdom or intellect, while "sūryasya raśmibhiḥ" refers to the rays of the sun, which are often associated with knowledge and clarity.
  • suto mitrāya varuṇāya: This part translates to "May we be the offspring of Mitra and Varuna." Mitra and Varuna are deities representing friendship, harmony, and cosmic order. This line emphasizes the importance of relationships and the need for unity and cooperation in society.
  • pītaye cārur ṛtāya pītaye: This can be interpreted as "For the sake of the beautiful truth." "Pītaye" suggests a sense of nourishment or sustenance, while "cārur ṛtāya" refers to the beautiful or pleasing truth. This line highlights the value of truth and its role in nurturing our lives and relationships.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking wisdom, fostering harmonious relationships, and valuing truth. It serves as a reminder to embrace new beginnings with clarity and understanding, to build connections based on trust and friendship, and to uphold truth as a guiding principle in our lives.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios. For instance, in a workplace setting, fostering a culture of collaboration and open communication can lead to better teamwork and productivity. In personal relationships, prioritizing honesty and understanding can strengthen bonds and create a supportive environment. Additionally, embracing new opportunities with a positive mindset can lead to personal growth and fulfillment.
