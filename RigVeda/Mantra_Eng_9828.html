यस्मिन्न अश्वास ऋषभास उक्षणो वशा मेषा अवसृष्टास आहुताः |कीलालपे सोमपृष्ठाय वेधसे हृदा मतीं जनये चारुम अग्नये ||
yasminn aśvāsa ṛṣabhāsa ukṣaṇo vaśā meṣā avasṛṣṭāsa āhutāḥ | kīlālape somapṛṣṭhāya vedhase hṛdā matiṁ janaye cārum agnaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasminn: in whom
    • aśvāsa: the horses
    • ṛṣabhāsa: the bulls
    • ukṣaṇo: the strong
    • vaśā: the controlled
    • meṣā: the sheep
    • avasṛṣṭāsa: the released
    • āhutāḥ: the offerings
    • kīlālape: in the sound of the bell
    • somapṛṣṭhāya: to the one who is the source of soma
    • vedhase: to the one who is the knower
    • hṛdā: with the heart
    • matiṁ: the intellect
    • janaye: may I generate
    • cārum: beautiful
    • agnaye: to the fire
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine connection between the offerings made to the fire and the various elements of nature, represented by horses, bulls, and sheep.
    • The mention of "aśvāsa" (horses) and "ṛṣabhāsa" (bulls) symbolizes strength and vitality, which are essential for life and prosperity.
    • The phrase "kīlālape somapṛṣṭhāya" indicates a call to the divine source of nourishment and vitality, represented by soma, which is often associated with immortality and divine bliss.
    • The invocation to "janaye cārum agnaye" emphasizes the desire to generate beautiful thoughts and intentions directed towards the divine fire, which is a symbol of transformation and purification.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the strength of horses and bulls is crucial for plowing fields, which can be seen as a metaphor for the hard work needed to cultivate one's life.
    • In modern life, the concept of "soma" can be related to the pursuit of health and well-being, where individuals seek nourishment for both body and mind.
    • The act of offering to the fire can be likened to the practice of meditation or mindfulness, where one seeks to purify thoughts and intentions, leading to personal growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of nature, strength, and the divine, highlighting the importance of intention and purity in our actions.
    • It serves as a reminder to cultivate beautiful thoughts and actions, aligning ourselves with the greater forces of life.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in practices that promote well-being, mindfulness, and a deeper connection with the world around them.
