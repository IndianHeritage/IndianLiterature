युवं चित्रं ददथुर भोजनो नराः चोदेथां सूनृतावतः |अर्वाग् रथं समनसा न यच्छतं पिबतं सोम्यम् मधु ||
yuvaṁ citraṁ dadathur bhojanaṁ narā codethāṁ sūnṛtāvate | arvāg rathaṁ samanasā ni yacchatam pibataṁ somyam madhu ||
  • yuvaṁ: This word translates to "young" or "youthful." It signifies vitality, energy, and the potential for growth and development.
  • citraṁ: Meaning "varied" or "colorful," this term suggests diversity and richness in experiences or offerings.
  • dadathur: This means "they give" or "they provide," indicating an act of generosity or sharing.
  • bhojanaṁ: Translated as "food" or "nourishment," it represents sustenance, both physical and spiritual.
  • narā: This word means "men" or "humans," referring to the community or society.
  • codethāṁ: This translates to "they inspire" or "they encourage," suggesting motivation and guidance.
  • sūnṛtāvate: Meaning "with good truth" or "with righteousness," it emphasizes the importance of honesty and virtue in actions.
  • arvāg: This word means "all" or "every," indicating inclusivity.
  • rathaṁ: Translated as "chariot," it symbolizes the journey of life and the means to achieve one's goals.
  • samanasā: This means "with harmony" or "in unity," highlighting the importance of cooperation and togetherness.
  • ni yacchatam: This translates to "may they bestow" or "may they grant," indicating a wish for blessings or gifts.
  • pibataṁ: Meaning "drink," it refers to the act of consuming or taking in, often associated with enjoyment or celebration.
  • somyam: This word means "pleasant" or "soma-like," referring to something that brings joy or bliss.
  • madhu: Translated as "honey," it symbolizes sweetness, pleasure, and the rewards of life.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of youth, diversity, nourishment, and community. It encourages individuals to inspire one another towards righteousness and to share their resources generously. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community gatherings, where people come together to share food and experiences, fostering a sense of unity and support. It also highlights the significance of living harmoniously and celebrating the sweetness of life, reminding us to appreciate the joys and blessings we receive. Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the importance of uplifting one another in our journeys.
