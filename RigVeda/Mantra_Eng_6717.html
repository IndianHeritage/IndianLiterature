इह त्या सदहमाद्याः हरि हिरण्यकेश्याः |वोळ्हाम अभि प्रयो हितम् ||
iha tyā sadhamādyā harī hiraṇyakeśyā | voḻhām abhi prayo hitam ||
  • iha - This word translates to "here" or "in this place." It signifies the importance of the present moment and the immediate environment.
  • tyā - This means "by that" or "through that," indicating a connection or a means to achieve something.
  • sadhamādyā - This term can be broken down into "sadhamā," which refers to "goodness" or "virtue," and "dyā," which implies "to shine" or "to illuminate." Together, it suggests the idea of virtuous actions leading to enlightenment.
  • harī - This word often refers to "the divine" or "the Lord," symbolizing a higher power or spiritual essence.
  • hiraṇyakeśyā - This term can be interpreted as "the one with golden hair," often associated with divinity and purity, representing the divine qualities of beauty and radiance.
  • voḻhām - This means "to bring" or "to lead," indicating an action of guiding or directing towards a goal.
  • abhi - This word translates to "towards" or "in the direction of," suggesting movement or progression.
  • prayo - This means "effort" or "endeavor," emphasizing the importance of action and striving towards a goal.
  • hitam - This translates to "beneficial" or "good," indicating the positive outcomes of actions taken.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to recognize the importance of virtuous actions in the present moment, guided by divine qualities, and leading towards beneficial outcomes. It emphasizes the connection between our actions and their consequences, urging us to strive for goodness and enlightenment.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to focus on their current actions and decisions, ensuring they align with virtuous principles.
  • In leadership, it serves as a reminder for leaders to guide their teams towards positive and beneficial goals, fostering an environment of integrity and enlightenment.
  • In community service, it highlights the importance of taking action that benefits others, reinforcing the idea that our efforts can lead to a greater good.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call to action, urging individuals to be mindful of their present actions and their alignment with virtue. It serves as a reminder that through our efforts, guided by divine qualities, we can achieve beneficial outcomes not only for ourselves but also for the community and the world at large.
