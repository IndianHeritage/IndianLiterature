मन्त्र: स्थिरं मनश्चक्रिषे जात इन्द्र वेषीद एको युध्यये भूयसश्चित् |अश्मानं चिच्छवसां दिद्युतो वि विदो गवां ऊर्वं उष्रियाणाम् ||
sthiram manaś cakṛṣe jāta indra veṣīd eko yudhaye bhūyasaś cit | aśmānaṁ cic chavasā didyuto vi vido gavām ūrvam usriyāṇām ||
  • sthiram manaś cakṛṣe: This phrase translates to "You have made my mind steady." Here, the term "sthiram" signifies stability or firmness, while "manaś" refers to the mind. In real life, a steady mind is crucial for making sound decisions, especially in stressful situations, such as during exams or important meetings.
  • jāta indra veṣīd: This means "O Indra, you who are born." Indra is often regarded as the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and power. In a contemporary context, this can be likened to seeking guidance from a strong leader or mentor when facing challenges.
  • eko yudhaye bhūyasaś cit: This translates to "You alone are the one who fights." The word "yudhaye" means battle or struggle, and "bhūyasaś" implies greatness. This can be related to personal struggles in life, where one must rely on their inner strength and determination to overcome obstacles, much like athletes preparing for a competition.
  • aśmānaṁ cic chavasā didyuto: This phrase means "With the strength of stones, you shine." "Aśmānaṁ" refers to stones, symbolizing resilience and durability. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who face hardships but emerge stronger, akin to a person who perseveres through adversity to achieve their goals.
  • vi vido gavām ūrvam usriyāṇām: This translates to "You know the strength of the cows and the fields." Cows represent nourishment and sustenance, while "ūrvam" refers to the earth or fields. This can be applied to the importance of agriculture and sustainable living in today's world, emphasizing the need to respect and understand our environment for future generations.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of mental stability, strength, and resilience in the face of challenges. It highlights the need for guidance from strong leaders and the significance of understanding our environment. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, leadership, and sustainable living. By embodying the qualities represented in this mantra, individuals can navigate life's battles with confidence and purpose.
