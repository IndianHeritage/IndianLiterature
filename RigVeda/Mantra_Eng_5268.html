स मन्द्रयाऽ च जिव्हया वह्निर् आसā विदुष्टारः |अग्नि रयिम् मघवद्भ्यो न ā वह हव्यदातिं च सूदय ||
sa mandrayā ca jihvayā vahnir āsā viduṣṭaraḥ | agne rayim maghavadbhyo na ā vaha havyadātiṁ ca sūdaya ||
  • sa mandrayā ca jihvayā vahnir āsā viduṣṭaraḥ: This line can be broken down into several components:
    • sa: This means "he" or "that," referring to a divine entity.
    • mandrayā: This term can be interpreted as "with the mind" or "with thought," indicating a connection to mental clarity or intention.
    • jihvayā: This translates to "with the tongue," symbolizing speech or communication.
    • vahnir: This means "fire," which is often associated with energy, transformation, and purification.
    • āsā: This can mean "hope" or "desire," suggesting a yearning for something greater.
    • viduṣṭaraḥ: This term refers to "knowledge" or "wisdom," indicating a pursuit of understanding.
  • agne rayim maghavadbhyo na ā vaha havyadātiṁ ca sūdaya: This line also has significant components:
    • agne: This is a direct address to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing the divine presence in rituals.
    • rayim: This means "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating a desire for abundance.
    • maghavadbhyo: This refers to "the generous ones," suggesting a connection to those who are benevolent or giving.
    • na ā vaha: This translates to "do not bring," indicating a request or plea.
    • havyadātiṁ: This term refers to "the offerings," which are often made in rituals to please the deities.
    • ca sūdaya: This means "and may you purify," suggesting a desire for cleansing or sanctification.

  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of intention and communication in the pursuit of knowledge and prosperity.
    • It highlights the role of Agni as a mediator between humans and the divine, representing the transformative power of fire.
    • The invocation of wealth and the plea for purification suggest a holistic approach to life, where material and spiritual aspects are intertwined.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied to the importance of clear communication and intention in achieving business goals. Just as Agni transforms offerings, effective communication can transform ideas into successful projects.
    • In personal relationships, the emphasis on speech and intention can be seen in the way we express our feelings and desires. Open and honest communication fosters understanding and connection.
    • In spiritual practices, the mantra can be used during rituals to invoke blessings for prosperity and purification, reminding practitioners of the need for both material and spiritual wealth.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of thought, speech, and action in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the understanding that our intentions and communications shape our reality, and that seeking knowledge and prosperity requires both mental clarity and divine support.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more meaningful relationships, successful endeavors, and a balanced approach to life.
