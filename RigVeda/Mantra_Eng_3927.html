यो रोहितौ वाजिनौ वाजिनिवान त्रिभिः शतैः सचमानाव अदिष्ट |यून सम अस्मै क्षितयो नमन्तां श्रुतरथाय मरुतो दुवोया ||
yo rohitau vājinau vājinīvān tribhiḥ śataiḥ sacamānāv adiṣṭa | yūne sam asmai kṣitayo namantāṁ śrutarathāya maruto duvoyā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • rohitau: red, often symbolizing vitality or life
    • vājinau: the strong, powerful ones
    • vājinīvān: the one who possesses strength
    • tribhiḥ: with three
    • śataiḥ: with hundreds
    • sacamānāv: those who are worthy of offerings
    • adiṣṭa: commanded or directed
    • yūne: in the union or together
    • sam: together
    • asmai: to us
    • kṣitayo: the earth or the ground
    • namantāṁ: bowing down or paying homage
    • śrutarathāya: to the one who hears or listens
    • maruto: the storm gods or winds
    • duvoyā: twofold or dual
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful entity or force that is vibrant and strong, symbolized by the term "rohitau." In real life, this can be likened to the energy and vitality found in nature, such as the sun rising or the flourishing of life in spring.
    • The mention of "vājinau" and "vājinīvān" emphasizes strength and power, which can be related to individuals or communities that exhibit resilience and determination in the face of challenges, much like athletes or leaders who inspire others.
    • The phrase "tribhiḥ śataiḥ" suggests abundance and prosperity, indicating that the blessings or offerings are plentiful. This can be seen in successful businesses or communities that thrive through collaboration and shared resources.
    • "Yūne sam asmai" reflects unity and togetherness, which is crucial in various aspects of life, such as family, teamwork in workplaces, or social movements that aim for collective goals.
    • The act of "namantāṁ" signifies respect and reverence, which is essential in maintaining relationships, whether personal or professional. It highlights the importance of humility and acknowledgment of others' contributions.
    • "Śrutarathāya" refers to the importance of listening and understanding, which is vital in communication. In real life, this can be applied in conflict resolution, negotiations, or any scenario where empathy plays a key role.
    • The "maruto" or storm gods symbolize the forces of nature, reminding us of the power and unpredictability of the environment, which can be seen in climate change discussions and the need for sustainable practices.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates themes of strength, unity, respect, and the acknowledgment of natural forces. Its significance lies in the reminder to harness our inner strength while respecting the interconnectedness of life.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to work together harmoniously, respect one another, and remain resilient in the face of challenges, much like how nature thrives through balance and cooperation.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a guiding principle for personal growth, community building, and environmental stewardship, urging us to recognize our place within the larger tapestry of life.
