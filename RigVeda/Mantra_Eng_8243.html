तव शुक्रासो अर्चयो दिवस पृष्ठे वि तन्मते |पवित्रं सोम धामभिः ||
tava śukrāso arcayo divas pṛṣṭhe vi tanvate | pavitraṁ soma dhāmabhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tava: Your
    • śukrāso: Brightness or purity
    • arcayo: Offerings or worship
    • divas: Of the heavens or sky
    • pṛṣṭhe: On the back or support
    • vi: In various ways
    • tanvate: They are made to shine or are illuminated
    • pavitraṁ: Pure or sacred
    • soma: The divine nectar or elixir
    • dhāmabhiḥ: With the realms or abodes
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine offerings made to the heavens, emphasizing the purity and brightness of these offerings.
    • The term "soma" refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing spiritual nourishment and enlightenment.
    • The phrase "they are made to shine" suggests that through worship and offerings, one can attain a state of purity and divine connection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be reflected in the practice of gratitude. For instance, when we express gratitude for the food we eat, we acknowledge the purity and effort that went into its creation.
    • In a corporate setting, when teams celebrate their achievements, they can be seen as making offerings to the collective effort, illuminating the path for future success.
    • During festivals, the act of preparing and offering food to deities can be viewed as a way to connect with the divine, ensuring that the offerings are pure and made with love.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of purity and intention in our actions and offerings.
    • It teaches us that through sincere worship and gratitude, we can elevate our consciousness and connect with higher realms.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, from personal rituals to communal celebrations, reminding us to approach our lives with purity of heart and intention.
