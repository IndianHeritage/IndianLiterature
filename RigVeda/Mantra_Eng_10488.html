इमं इन्द्रो अदीदहरद ध्रुवं ध्रुवेण हविषा |तस्मै सोमो अधि ब्रवत तस्मा उ ब्रह्मणस पतिḥ ||
imam indro adīdharad dhruvaṁ dhruveṇa haviṣā | tasmai somo adhi bravat tasmā u brahmaṇas patiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imam: this
    • indro: Indra (the king of gods)
    • adīdharad: has taken (or accepted)
    • dhruvaṁ: firm or stable
    • dhruveṇa: with stability
    • haviṣā: with offerings
    • tasmai: to him
    • somo: Soma (a sacred drink or deity)
    • adhi: above or in addition
    • bravat: speaks or proclaims
    • tasmā: therefore
    • u: and
    • brahmaṇas: of the Brahman (the ultimate reality)
    • patiḥ: lord or master
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the king of gods, who is seen as a powerful force in the universe.
    • It emphasizes the importance of stability and firm foundations in life, represented by the word "dhruva."
    • The mention of offerings ("haviṣā") signifies the need for devotion and gratitude towards the divine.
    • Soma, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration, is called upon to enhance the spiritual experience.
    • The mantra suggests a connection between the divine (Indra and Soma) and the ultimate reality (Brahman), indicating that through devotion and offerings, one can attain a higher understanding of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of stability can be applied to personal relationships, where a strong foundation of trust and communication is essential for lasting bonds.
    • In professional settings, the mantra can inspire individuals to seek stability in their careers by making thoughtful decisions and showing dedication to their work.
    • The act of making offerings can be seen in various cultures where people express gratitude through rituals, whether in religious ceremonies or personal practices of thankfulness.
    • Engaging with nature, such as through meditation or spending time outdoors, can be a way to connect with the divine and seek inspiration, akin to the essence of Soma.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of stability, devotion, and the connection between the divine and the ultimate reality.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate a strong foundation in their lives, whether in relationships, careers, or spiritual practices.
    • By recognizing the importance of offerings and gratitude, one can foster a deeper connection with the divine and gain insights into the nature of existence.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interplay between the material and spiritual realms, guiding individuals towards a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
