मरतो वीजुपाणिभिः चित्रा रोधस्वतीर अनु |यातेम अखिद्रयामभिः ||
maruto vīḻupāṇibhiś citrā rodhasvatīr anu | yātem akhidrayāmabhiḥ ||
  • Maruto: This term refers to the Maruts, the storm deities in Vedic literature. They are often associated with wind, storms, and the power of nature.
  • Vīḻupāṇibhiś: This word can be broken down into "vīḻu" meaning "to move" or "to flow" and "pāṇibhiś" meaning "with hands." Together, it suggests the Maruts moving with their powerful hands, symbolizing their control over the forces of nature.
  • Citrā: This word means "colorful" or "varied." It signifies the diversity and vibrancy of the natural world, as well as the dynamic nature of the Maruts.
  • Rodhasvatīr: This term refers to the "expansive" or "broad" nature of the sky or the atmosphere. It indicates the vastness of the space in which the Maruts operate.
  • Yātem: This word means "to go" or "to move towards." It implies a sense of direction or purpose in the actions of the Maruts.
  • Akidrayāmabhiḥ: This term can be interpreted as "with unbroken strength" or "with unwavering power." It emphasizes the relentless and powerful nature of the Maruts as they traverse the skies.

The mantra can be understood as a vivid depiction of the Maruts, the storm deities, as they move through the expansive sky with their powerful hands, embodying the colorful and dynamic forces of nature. It highlights their strength and purpose as they navigate the vastness of the atmosphere.


In real life, this mantra can be seen as a metaphor for the forces of nature that impact our daily lives. For example:

  • Weather Patterns: Just as the Maruts control storms, we experience the effects of weather changes in our lives, reminding us of nature's power.
  • Human Endeavors: The mantra can inspire individuals to harness their own inner strength and purpose, much like the Maruts, as they navigate challenges in life.
  • Environmental Awareness: The vibrant imagery of the mantra encourages us to appreciate and respect the natural world, promoting sustainability and harmony with our environment.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its celebration of the powerful forces of nature and the importance of recognizing our connection to the environment. It serves as a reminder to embrace our inner strength and navigate life's challenges with purpose, while also fostering a sense of respect and responsibility towards the natural world around us.
