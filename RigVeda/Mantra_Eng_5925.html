त्वं विष्णो सुमतिं विश्वजन्यां अप्रयुताम एवयावो मतीं दाह् |पर्चो यथा नः सुवितस्य भूरेर अश्वावतः पुरुषचन्द्रस्य रायः ||
tvaṁ viṣṇo sumatiṁ viśvajanyām aprayutām evayāvo matiṁ dāḥ | parco yathā naḥ suvitasya bhūrer aśvāvataḥ puruścandrasya rāyaḥ ||
  • tvaṁ viṣṇo: This phrase translates to "You, O Vishnu." Vishnu is one of the principal deities in Hinduism, often associated with preservation and protection. In real life, this can symbolize the need for a guiding force or protector in our lives, akin to a mentor or a leader who helps us navigate challenges.
  • sumatiṁ viśvajanyām: "Good understanding, born of all." This suggests the importance of wisdom and understanding that encompasses all aspects of life. In practical terms, this can relate to the value of education and learning from diverse experiences, which can lead to better decision-making in personal and professional contexts.
  • aprayutām evayāvo matiṁ dāḥ: "Bestow upon us unwavering intellect." This line emphasizes the desire for a stable and clear mind. In everyday life, this can be seen in the pursuit of mental health and clarity, where individuals seek practices like meditation or mindfulness to enhance their focus and emotional stability.
  • parco yathā naḥ: "As the divine beings (gods) do for us." This indicates a connection to the divine and the support we receive from higher powers. In real life, this can be interpreted as the support we find in community, family, or spiritual beliefs that guide us through difficult times.
  • suvitasya bhūrer: "Of the well-being of the earth." This phrase highlights the importance of the earth and our responsibility towards it. In contemporary society, this can relate to environmental sustainability and the need to care for our planet, reflecting the growing awareness of climate change and ecological preservation.
  • aśvāvataḥ puruścandrasya rāyaḥ: "The wealth of the horse and the moon." Horses symbolize strength and mobility, while the moon represents calmness and stability. This can be seen in real life as the balance between ambition (represented by the horse) and tranquility (represented by the moon), suggesting that true wealth comes from a harmonious blend of both qualities.

  • Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking wisdom, stability, and divine support in our lives. Its significance lies in the reminder to cultivate understanding, protect our environment, and strive for a balanced life. In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to seek guidance, embrace learning, and maintain a connection with both the material and spiritual aspects of existence. By embodying these principles, we can navigate life's challenges with grace and purpose.
