मन्त्र: सेद अग्निर यो वानुष्यतो निपाति समेद्धारम अंहसा उरुष्यāt |सुजातासः परि चरन्ति वीराः ||
sed agnir yo vanuṣyato nipāti sameddhāram aṁhasa uruṣyāt | sujātāsaḥ pari caranti vīrāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sed: but
    • agnir: fire
    • yo: who
    • vanuṣyato: from the human realm
    • nipāti: descends
    • sameddhāram: with great strength
    • aṁhasa: from the light
    • uruṣyāt: from the vastness
    • sujātāsaḥ: well-born
    • pari caranti: move around
    • vīrāḥ: the heroes
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine fire (Agni) that descends from the human realm with great strength and light.
    • The term "vīrāḥ" refers to heroes, suggesting that those who are well-born and virtuous move around in the world, guided by this divine fire.
    • The imagery of fire symbolizes transformation, energy, and the divine presence that inspires courage and valor in individuals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is seen as a purifying element. For instance, in a traditional Indian wedding, the couple circles around a sacred fire, symbolizing their commitment and the divine presence in their union.
    • In modern contexts, the concept of "fire" can be related to passion and motivation. For example, an entrepreneur may feel a strong "fire" within them that drives them to innovate and succeed in their business endeavors.
    • Heroes in society, such as activists or leaders, often embody the qualities of courage and strength, much like the "vīrāḥ" mentioned in the mantra. They inspire others to act for the greater good, reflecting the divine fire's influence.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine inspiration and the transformative power of fire in our lives.
    • It teaches us that strength and courage come from a higher source, encouraging us to embody these qualities in our daily actions.
    • In real life, we can apply the teachings of this mantra by seeking inspiration from our passions and values, striving to be heroes in our own right, and recognizing the divine presence in our endeavors.
