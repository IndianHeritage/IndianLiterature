व्युच्छा दूहितर दिवो मा चिरं तनुता अपः |नेत त्वा स्तेनं यथा रिपुं तपाति सूरो आर्चिषा सुजाते अश्वसूऋते ||
vy ucchā duhitar divo mā ciraṁ tanuthā apaḥ | net tvā stenaṁ yathā ripuṁ tapāti sūro arciṣā sujāte aśvasūnṛte ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vy ucchā: "We declare" or "We proclaim"
    • duhitar: "daughter"
    • divo: "of the sky" or "heavenly"
    • mā: "not"
    • ciraṁ: "for a long time"
    • tanuthā: "to be extended" or "to be prolonged"
    • apaḥ: "water" or "the waters"
    • net: "lead" or "guide"
    • tvā: "you"
    • stenam: "thief" or "robber"
    • yathā: "as" or "like"
    • ripuṁ: "enemy"
    • tapāti: "burns" or "torments"
    • sūro: "hero" or "brave one"
    • arciṣā: "with light" or "with radiance"
    • sujāte: "well-born" or "noble"
    • aśvasūnṛte: "in the truth of the horse" or "in the truth of the steed"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea or declaration for the protection and guidance of the divine, particularly in relation to the daughter of the heavens.
    • It emphasizes the importance of not allowing negative influences (symbolized by the "thief" or "enemy") to affect one's life.
    • The invocation of "water" signifies purity, nourishment, and life, suggesting a desire for these qualities to be abundant and untainted.
    • The mention of "hero" and "light" indicates a call for strength and clarity in overcoming challenges.
    • The phrase "in the truth of the horse" can symbolize speed, power, and nobility, suggesting that one should strive to embody these qualities in their actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, this mantra can be applied when facing adversities or challenges in life, such as dealing with difficult relationships or work situations. It serves as a reminder to seek guidance and protection from higher powers.
    • In a community setting, it can be invoked during rituals or gatherings to promote unity and strength against common challenges, ensuring that negative influences are kept at bay.
    • In environmental contexts, the reference to "water" can be a call to protect natural resources and ensure that they remain pure and abundant for future generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine protection, the importance of purity, and the strength to overcome adversities.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek guidance from higher powers and to embody noble qualities in our actions.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals and communities to remain vigilant against negative influences and to strive for a harmonious existence.
