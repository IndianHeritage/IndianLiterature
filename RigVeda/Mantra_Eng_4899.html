उत स्य देवः सविता भगो नो 'पां नपाद अवतु दानु पप्रिः |त्वष्टा देवैभिर जनिभिः सजोषा द्यौर देवैभिः पृथिवी समुद्रैः ||
uta sya devaḥ savitā bhago no 'pāṁ napād avatu dānu papriḥ | tvaṣṭā devebhir janibhiḥ sajoṣā dyaur devebhiḥ pṛthivī samudraiḥ ||
  • uta sya devaḥ savitā bhago: This phrase refers to the divine energy of Savitar, the Sun God, who is often associated with illumination and enlightenment. In real life, this can be likened to the way sunlight nourishes plants, enabling them to grow and thrive.
  • no 'pāṁ napād avatu: This part translates to a plea for protection. It signifies the desire for safety and guidance in our lives. For example, when embarking on a new journey or facing challenges, individuals often seek blessings for protection, much like a student praying for success before an exam.
  • dānu papriḥ: This can be interpreted as a request for the removal of obstacles. In everyday life, this resonates with the idea of overcoming difficulties, such as a person striving to achieve their goals despite facing numerous challenges.
  • tvaṣṭā devebhir janibhiḥ sajoṣā: Tvaṣṭā is the divine craftsman, symbolizing creativity and the ability to shape one's destiny. This reflects the importance of human effort and creativity in achieving success, akin to an artist meticulously crafting a masterpiece.
  • dyaur devebhiḥ pṛthivī samudraiḥ: This phrase highlights the connection between the heavens (dyaur) and the earth (pṛthivī), emphasizing the unity of all elements in the universe. In real life, this can be seen in the balance of nature, where every element plays a crucial role in maintaining ecological harmony.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, protection, and the removal of obstacles while emphasizing the importance of creativity and the interconnectedness of all elements in life. Its significance lies in its applicability to various real-life scenarios, such as personal growth, overcoming challenges, and fostering a harmonious relationship with nature. By invoking these principles, individuals can navigate their lives with a sense of purpose and connection to the divine.
