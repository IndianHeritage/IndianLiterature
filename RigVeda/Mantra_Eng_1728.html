मन्त्र: पञ्चारे चक्रे परिवर्तमाने तस्मिन्न ā तस्थुर भुवनानि विश्वा |तस्य नाक्षस तप्यते भूर्भारः सनाद एव न शीर्यते सनाभिः ||
pañcāre cakre parivartamāne tasminn ā tasthur bhuvanāni viśvā | tasya nākṣas tapyate bhūribhāraḥ sanād eva na śīryate sanābhiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pañcāre cakre parivartamāne tasminn ā tasthur bhuvanāni viśvā | tasya nākṣas tapyate bhūribhāraḥ sanād eva na śīryate sanābhiḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pañcāre: In the five-fold cycle
    • cakre: In the wheel or cycle
    • parivartamāne: Turning or revolving
    • tasminn: In that
    • ā: From
    • tasthur: They exist or are established
    • bhuvanāni: The worlds or realms
    • viśvā: All or the universe
    • tasya: Of that
    • nākṣas: The heavens or sky
    • tapyate: Is heated or shines
    • bhūribhāraḥ: The great burden or weight
    • sanād: From the beginning or eternally
    • eva: Indeed
    • na: Not
    • śīryate: Diminished or decayed
    • sanābhiḥ: With the eternal or everlasting
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the cyclical nature of existence, represented by the "five-fold cycle" and the "wheel." It suggests that all worlds and realms are established within this cycle.
    • The phrase "tasminn ā tasthur bhuvanāni viśvā" indicates that all beings and worlds exist within this cosmic order, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all life.
    • The line "tasya nākṣas tapyate bhūribhāraḥ" suggests that the heavens are illuminated or affected by the great burdens of existence, which can be interpreted as the collective experiences and responsibilities of all beings.
    • Finally, "sanād eva na śīryate sanābhiḥ" conveys the idea that despite the burdens and cycles of life, there is an eternal essence that does not diminish or decay, highlighting the concept of immortality or the eternal soul.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we observe cycles such as the seasons, life and death, and the water cycle, which reflect the mantra's theme of cyclical existence.
    • In human life, we experience various phases—childhood, adulthood, and old age—demonstrating the continuous cycle of life.
    • The concept of interconnectedness can be seen in social structures, where individual actions impact the community, echoing the idea that all beings are part of a larger whole.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of understanding the cyclical nature of life and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize their place within the larger cosmic order and to acknowledge the burdens and responsibilities that come with existence.
    • In real life, this understanding can lead to greater compassion, mindfulness, and a sense of purpose, as individuals strive to contribute positively to the world around them.
