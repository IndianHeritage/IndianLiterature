अपेंद्र द्विषतो मनोऽप जिज्यासतो वधम् |विमन्योः शर्म यच्छ वरियो यवया वधम् ||
apendra dviṣato mano 'pa jijyāsato vadham | vi manyoḥ śarma yaccha varīyo yavayā vadham ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apendra: O Indra, the king of gods
    • dviṣato: of the enemies
    • mano: mind
    • 'pa: protect
    • jijyāsato: who desires to live
    • vadham: destruction or harm
    • vi: from
    • manyoḥ: of the two (the enemies)
    • śarma: peace or protection
    • yaccha: grant
    • varīyo: the best or the most excellent
    • yavayā: by the means of
    • vadham: destruction or harm
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, seeking protection from enemies and harm.
    • It emphasizes the importance of a peaceful mind and the desire to live without fear.
    • The mantra acknowledges the presence of adversaries and calls for divine intervention to ensure safety and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive work environment, individuals often face challenges from colleagues. This mantra can be a reminder to seek inner peace and protection from negative influences.
    • In personal relationships, conflicts may arise. Reciting this mantra can help individuals focus on resolving issues peacefully rather than engaging in harmful confrontations.
    • During times of societal unrest or conflict, communities can invoke this mantra to seek divine protection and harmony among its members.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine protection and the pursuit of peace amidst adversities.
    • It serves as a reminder that while challenges and enemies may exist, one can seek strength and tranquility through faith and prayer.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, from personal struggles to broader societal issues, encouraging individuals to maintain a peaceful mindset and seek divine assistance in overcoming obstacles.
