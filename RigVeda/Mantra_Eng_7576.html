तं उष्टवाम य इमा जजाना विश्वा जातान्य अवराण्य अस्मात् | इन्द्रेण मित्रं दिधिषेम गीरभिर उपो नमोभिर वृषभं विशेम ||
tam u ṣṭavāma ya imā jajāna viśvā jātāny avarāṇy asmāt | indreṇa mitraṁ didhiṣema gīrbhir upo namobhir vṛṣabhaṁ viśema ||
  • tam u ṣṭavāma: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke that." It signifies the act of calling upon a higher power or divine presence for guidance and support.
  • ya imā jajāna: This means "who has created these beings." It refers to the creator or the divine force responsible for the existence of all living entities.
  • viśvā jātāny avarāṇy asmāt: This translates to "all beings that have been born from this." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all life and the idea that everything originates from a single source.
  • indreṇa mitraṁ didhiṣema: This means "may we be strengthened by Indra and Mitra." Indra is often associated with strength and power, while Mitra represents friendship and harmony. Together, they symbolize the balance of strength and cooperation.
  • gīrbhir upo namobhir: This translates to "with hymns and salutations." It highlights the importance of reverence and respect in our approach to the divine and the world around us.
  • vṛṣabhaṁ viśema: This means "may we attain the strength." It signifies the aspiration for strength, both physical and spiritual, to overcome challenges and achieve our goals.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of invoking divine support and recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings. It teaches us the importance of strength, cooperation, and reverence in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during challenging times when we seek guidance, in fostering harmonious relationships, or in moments of reflection and gratitude. By acknowledging the divine and the unity of all life, we can cultivate a sense of purpose and strength in our daily endeavors.
