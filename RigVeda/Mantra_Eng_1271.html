तन् मित्रस्य वरुणस्याभिचक्षे सूर्यो रूपं कृतुते द्योः उपस्थे |अनन्तम् अन्यद रुशद अस्य पाजः कृष्णम् अन्यद धरितः सम भरन्ति ||
tan mitrasya varuṇasyābhicakṣe sūryo rūpaṁ kṛṇute dyor upasthe | anantam anyad ruśad asya pājaḥ kṛṣṇam anyad dharitaḥ sam bharanti ||
  • tan mitrasya varuṇasyābhicakṣe: This phrase can be translated as "that which is the essence of Mitra and Varuna." Mitra and Varuna are deities representing friendship, harmony, and cosmic order. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of maintaining relationships and harmony in our communities and societies.
  • sūryo rūpaṁ kṛṇute dyor upasthe: This translates to "the Sun creates a form in the sky." The Sun is often seen as a source of life and energy. In our daily lives, we can relate this to the importance of the Sun in agriculture, health, and overall well-being. For example, sunlight is essential for photosynthesis in plants, which in turn supports the food chain.
  • anantam anyad ruśad asya pājaḥ: This means "the infinite and the other that is the source of this." It suggests the existence of a vast universe beyond our immediate perception. In real life, this can be likened to the exploration of the unknown, whether in science, philosophy, or personal growth. For instance, the quest for knowledge in fields like astronomy or psychology reflects our desire to understand the infinite complexities of existence.
  • kṛṣṇam anyad dharitaḥ sam bharanti: This translates to "the black (or dark) one carries another." This can symbolize the duality of existence, where light and dark coexist. In practical terms, this can be seen in the balance of good and evil in human nature. For example, every individual has the capacity for both kindness and malice, and understanding this duality can lead to personal growth and empathy towards others.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, the importance of harmony, and the duality of existence. It teaches us to appreciate the relationships we have, the energy that sustains us, and the vastness of the universe. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in fostering community relationships, understanding the importance of nature, and embracing the complexities of human emotions and actions. By reflecting on these themes, we can strive for a more harmonious and balanced life.
