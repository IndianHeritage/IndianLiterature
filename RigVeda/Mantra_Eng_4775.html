सखायो ब्रह्मवाहसेऽर्चत प्र च गायत। स हि नः प्रमतिर मही॥
sakhāyo brahmavāhase 'rcata pra ca gāyata | sa hi naḥ pramatir mahī ||
  • Mantra: "sakhāyo brahmavāhase 'rcata pra ca gāyata | sa hi naḥ pramatir mahī ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sakhāyo: friends or companions
    • brahmavāhase: those who carry or embody the divine knowledge
    • 'rcata: may they worship or praise
    • pra: indeed or truly
    • ca: and
    • gāyata: may they sing or chant
    • sa: he or that
    • hi: indeed
    • naḥ: our
    • pramatir: understanding or wisdom
    • mahī: great or vast
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of community and collective worship.
    • It suggests that friends and companions should come together to praise and celebrate the divine knowledge.
    • The phrase "sa hi naḥ pramatir mahī" indicates that this collective effort leads to a greater understanding and wisdom that is vast and profound.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, team-building activities can be seen as a modern-day interpretation of this mantra, where colleagues come together to foster a sense of community and shared purpose.
    • In educational settings, group studies or collaborative projects reflect the essence of this mantra, as students work together to enhance their understanding and knowledge.
    • Religious gatherings or community service events also embody this principle, where individuals unite to worship or serve a greater cause, leading to collective wisdom and strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity and collective effort in the pursuit of knowledge and understanding.
    • It teaches us that when individuals come together with a common purpose, they can achieve greater wisdom and insight.
    • In real life, this principle can be applied in various contexts, such as in families, communities, workplaces, and educational institutions, fostering collaboration and shared growth.
