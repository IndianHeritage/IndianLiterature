यज्ञस्य केतुम् प्रथमं पुरोहितं हविष्मन्त इळते सप्त वाजिनम् |श्रिण्वन्तम् अग्निं घृतपृष्ठम् उक्षणम् पृणन्तं देवम् पृणते सुवीर्यम् ||
yajñasya ketum prathamam purohitaṁ haviṣmanta īḻate sapta vājinam | śṛṇvantam agniṁ ghṛtapṛṣṭham ukṣaṇam pṛṇantaṁ devam pṛṇate suvīryam ||
  • yajñasya ketum: This phrase refers to the "flag" or "standard" of the sacrifice (yajña). In a real-life context, this can be likened to the guiding principles or values that lead a community or individual in their endeavors.
  • prathamam purohitaṁ: This means "the foremost priest" or "the chief officiant." In modern terms, this can be compared to a leader or mentor who guides others in their spiritual or professional journeys.
  • haviṣmanta īḻate: This translates to "the offerings that are made." In everyday life, this can represent the efforts and contributions we make towards a cause or community, emphasizing the importance of giving back.
  • sapta vājinam: This means "the seven steeds." In a metaphorical sense, this can symbolize the various strengths or resources one has at their disposal to achieve their goals, akin to teamwork or collaboration in a project.
  • śṛṇvantam agniṁ: This translates to "listening to Agni." Agni, the fire god, represents transformation and purification. In real life, this can be seen as being open to new ideas and experiences that lead to personal growth.
  • ghṛtapṛṣṭham ukṣaṇam: This means "the one who is nourished by ghee." Ghee symbolizes richness and abundance. In practical terms, this can refer to the nourishment we receive from our environment, relationships, and experiences that enrich our lives.
  • pṛṇantaṁ devam: This translates to "the divine that is being nourished." This can be interpreted as the spiritual aspect of our lives that requires attention and care, similar to how we nurture our mental and emotional well-being.
  • pṛṇate suvīryam: This means "the one who nourishes with strength." In a contemporary context, this can represent the idea of empowering others, whether through mentorship, support, or sharing knowledge.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of leadership, community, and the act of giving in both spiritual and practical realms. It highlights how our actions, guided by strong principles and collaboration, can lead to personal and communal growth. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as team projects, community service, or personal development initiatives, reminding us that our contributions and the strength we derive from each other are vital for success and fulfillment.
