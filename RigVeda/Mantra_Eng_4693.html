स नीयव्याभिर जरीतारम् अच्छा महो वाजेभिर महद्भिः च शुष्मैः |पुरुवीराभिर वृषभ क्षितीनाम आ गिरवणः सुविताय प्र याहि ||
sa nīvyābhir jaritāram acchā maho vājebhir mahadbhiś ca śuṣmaiḥ | puruvīrābhir vṛṣabha kṣitīnām ā girvaṇaḥ suvitāya pra yāhi ||
  • sa nīvyābhir: This phrase can be interpreted as "with the help of the boats" or "with the support of the vessels." In a real-life context, this can symbolize the importance of teamwork and collaboration in achieving goals. Just as boats work together to navigate waters, individuals must work together to overcome challenges.
  • jaritāram: This word refers to "the one who is strong" or "the powerful." In everyday life, this can represent leaders or mentors who guide and support others. For example, a strong leader in a community can inspire and uplift those around them, much like a powerful figure in mythology.
  • acchā maho: This translates to "may we attain greatness." It emphasizes the aspiration for success and excellence. In practical terms, this can relate to personal goals, such as pursuing education or career advancement, where individuals strive to achieve their highest potential.
  • vājebhir mahadbhiś ca: This phrase means "with great strength and power." It highlights the significance of having robust resources and support systems. In real life, this can be seen in organizations that leverage their strengths, such as a company utilizing its skilled workforce to innovate and grow.
  • śuṣmaiḥ: This word means "with those who are wise" or "with the knowledgeable." It underscores the value of wisdom and experience. In a real-world scenario, this can be likened to seeking advice from mentors or experts in a field, which can lead to better decision-making and success.
  • puruvīrābhir: This translates to "with the brave ones" or "with the courageous." It signifies the importance of courage in facing challenges. For instance, individuals who stand up for social justice or advocate for change demonstrate the bravery that this phrase embodies.
  • vṛṣabha kṣitīnām: This phrase can be interpreted as "the bull of the earth" or "the strong protector of the land." It symbolizes strength and protection. In a modern context, this can refer to environmental activists who work to protect the planet, embodying the spirit of guardianship over nature.
  • ā girvaṇaḥ: This means "to the one who is praised" or "to the one who is revered." It highlights the importance of respect and recognition. In real life, this can relate to honoring those who contribute positively to society, such as healthcare workers or educators, who deserve acknowledgment for their efforts.
  • suvitāya pra yāhi: This translates to "may you go towards prosperity." It is a blessing for success and abundance. In practical terms, this can be seen in the pursuit of financial stability, personal growth, or community development, where individuals and groups strive for a better future.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of collaboration, strength, wisdom, courage, and the pursuit of greatness. Its significance lies in the reminder that success is often a collective effort, supported by strong leaders, wise mentors, and brave individuals. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork in the workplace, community initiatives, and personal development. By embodying the values expressed in this mantra, individuals and communities can work towards a prosperous and harmonious existence.
