स नो विश्वाहā सुक्रतुर आदित्यः सुपथा करत् |प्र ण आयूषंसी तारीषत् ||
sa no viśvāhā sukratur ādityaḥ supathā karat | pra ṇa āyūṁṣi tāriṣat ||
  • sa no viśvāhā: This phrase can be translated as "may all beings" or "may all of us." It emphasizes inclusivity and the collective well-being of all living entities.
  • sukratur: This word means "good thought" or "noble intention." It signifies the importance of having positive and constructive thoughts in our actions and interactions.
  • ādityaḥ: Referring to the sun, this term symbolizes light, knowledge, and enlightenment. The sun is often seen as a source of life and energy, representing clarity and truth.
  • supathā karat: This translates to "may we walk on the right path." It suggests the importance of following a righteous and ethical path in life, making choices that lead to positive outcomes.
  • pra ṇa āyūṁṣi: This phrase means "may we attain long life." It reflects a desire for longevity and vitality, not just in terms of years lived but also in the quality of life experienced.
  • tāriṣat: This word can be interpreted as "may we be protected" or "may we be saved." It conveys a wish for safety and security in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra "sa no viśvāhā sukratur ādityaḥ supathā karat | pra ṇa āyūṁṣi tāriṣat ||" encapsulates a profound message of collective well-being, positive intentions, ethical living, and the pursuit of longevity and safety. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:


  • In community gatherings, where the focus is on collective growth and support.
  • In personal development, encouraging individuals to cultivate positive thoughts and intentions.
  • In decision-making processes, reminding individuals to choose paths that are ethical and beneficial for all.
  • In health and wellness practices, emphasizing the importance of a long and fulfilling life.

This mantra serves as a guiding principle for living harmoniously with oneself and others, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in our actions.
