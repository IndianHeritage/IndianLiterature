ते नो रायो द्यमतो वाजवतो दातारो भूता नृवतः पुरुक्षोः |दशस्यन्तो दिव्याः पार्थिवासो गोजाता अप्यां मृळता च देवाः ||
te no rāyo dyumato vājavato dātāro bhūta nṛvataḥ purukṣoḥ | daśasyanto divyāḥ pārthivāso gojātā apyā mṛḻatā ca devāḥ ||
  • te no rāyo dyumato vājavato dātāro bhūta nṛvataḥ purukṣoḥ
    • te no rāyo: "May we receive wealth" - This phrase emphasizes the desire for prosperity and abundance in life.
    • dyumato: "of the wise" - This suggests that the wealth we seek should come from wisdom and intelligence.
    • vājavato: "of the strong" - This indicates that strength, both physical and mental, is essential in acquiring and maintaining wealth.
    • dātāro: "the givers" - This refers to those who are generous and share their wealth, highlighting the importance of community and support.
    • bhūta nṛvataḥ purukṣoḥ: "of the beings and the great" - This phrase acknowledges the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of collective prosperity.
  • daśasyanto divyāḥ pārthivāso gojātā apyā mṛḻatā ca devāḥ
    • daśasyanto: "the ten" - This could symbolize completeness or a full cycle, suggesting that wealth and prosperity should be holistic.
    • divyāḥ: "divine" - This implies that the wealth we seek should have a higher purpose and be aligned with spiritual values.
    • pārthivāso: "earthly" - This indicates that while we seek divine wealth, we must also be grounded in reality and practical matters.
    • gojātā: "from the cattle" - Cattle are often a symbol of wealth in many cultures, representing sustenance and livelihood.
    • apyā mṛḻatā ca devāḥ: "and the gods who are gentle" - This suggests that divine blessings come from a place of kindness and compassion.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of seeking wealth that is not only material but also rooted in wisdom, strength, and community. It highlights the significance of generosity and the interconnectedness of all beings in the pursuit of prosperity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In business, fostering a culture of collaboration and support among colleagues can lead to greater success.
  • In personal finance, making wise investments that benefit not just oneself but also the community can create a more sustainable future.
  • In social initiatives, promoting generosity and kindness can lead to a more equitable society.

Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that true wealth encompasses not just material gain but also the well-being of all beings, urging us to pursue prosperity with wisdom and compassion.
