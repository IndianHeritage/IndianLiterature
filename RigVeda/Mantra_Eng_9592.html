प्र सु व आपो महिमानम् उत्तमं कारुर वोचाति सदने विवस्वतः |प्र सप्त-सप्त त्रेधा हि चक्रमुः प्र सृत्वरिणाम् अति सिन्धुर ओजसा ||
pra su va āpo mahimānam uttamaṁ kārur vocāti sadane vivasvataḥ | pra sapta-sapta tredhā hi cakramuḥ pra sṛtvarīṇām ati sindhur ojasā ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May the waters, with their great power, call us to the highest abode. The sevenfold wheel of energy moves in three ways, and the mighty waters flow with strength."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra su va: A call to the divine or higher powers.
    • āpo: Refers to waters, symbolizing purity and life.
    • mahimānam: Greatness or power, often associated with divine qualities.
    • uttamaṁ: The highest or supreme state.
    • kārur: A call or invocation.
    • vocāti: To speak or call forth.
    • sadane: Abode or dwelling place.
    • vivasvataḥ: Related to the sun or light, symbolizing enlightenment.
    • sapta-sapta: Sevenfold, indicating completeness or wholeness.
    • tredhā: In three ways, suggesting different paths or methods.
    • cakramuḥ: Wheel, representing cycles of life and energy.
    • sṛtvarīṇām: Flowing or moving, indicating dynamism.
    • ati sindhur: Mighty waters, symbolizing strength and abundance.
    • ojasā: With energy or vitality, emphasizing the power of the waters.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The invocation of water in rituals signifies the importance of purity and life. For instance, in many cultures, water is used in cleansing rituals before important events, symbolizing the removal of negativity.
    • The concept of the "sevenfold wheel" can be related to the cycles of life, such as the stages of human development or the seasons of nature, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all things.
    • The idea of flowing waters can be seen in the context of rivers that nourish lands and sustain life, such as the Ganges in India, which is revered for its spiritual and physical sustenance.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of the divine qualities of water and the cycles of energy in life. It teaches us to recognize the power of nature and the importance of purity, both in a physical and spiritual sense. In real life, this mantra can inspire us to seek higher states of consciousness, to appreciate the natural world, and to understand the interconnectedness of all beings. By acknowledging the strength and vitality of water, we can cultivate a deeper respect for our environment and strive for harmony in our lives.
