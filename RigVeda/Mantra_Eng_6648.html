यदाद्य सूर्य उद्यति प्रियकṣत्रा ऋतं दध |यन् निम्रुचि प्रबुधि विश्ववेदसो यद्वा मध्यंदिने दिवः ||
yad adya sūrya udyati priyakṣatrā ṛtaṁ dadha | yan nimruci prabudhi viśvavedaso yad vā madhyaṁdine divaḥ ||
  • yad adya sūrya udyati: This phrase translates to "As the sun rises today." The sun is often seen as a symbol of enlightenment, knowledge, and the dispelling of darkness. In real life, the rising sun can represent new beginnings and opportunities. For example, each day brings a chance to start fresh, much like how the sun rises anew every morning.
  • priyakṣatrā ṛtaṁ dadha: This means "may it establish the truth." Here, "ṛta" refers to cosmic order or truth. In our daily lives, establishing truth can be seen in the importance of honesty and integrity in our actions. For instance, in a workplace, maintaining transparency and ethical practices fosters trust among colleagues and clients.
  • yan nimruci prabudhi: This translates to "that which awakens knowledge." Knowledge is a powerful tool that can lead to personal and societal growth. In real life, education and learning can be seen as the means to awaken this knowledge. For example, attending workshops or pursuing higher education can enhance one's understanding and skills, leading to better opportunities.
  • viśvavedaso: This means "of all-knowing." It signifies the universal knowledge that encompasses all aspects of life. In practical terms, this can relate to the wisdom gained from diverse experiences and cultures. For instance, traveling to different countries can broaden one’s perspective and understanding of the world.
  • yad vā madhyaṁdine divaḥ: This translates to "or in the midday of the day." The midday symbolizes the peak of the day, a time when one is most active and productive. In real life, this can be related to the importance of utilizing our peak hours for important tasks. For example, many people find they are most productive in the morning or early afternoon, and scheduling challenging tasks during these times can lead to better outcomes.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of truth, knowledge, and the cyclical nature of time represented by the sun. It encourages individuals to seek enlightenment and establish truth in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied through personal growth, ethical practices, and the pursuit of knowledge. By recognizing the importance of these elements, one can lead a more fulfilling and meaningful life, contributing positively to society.
