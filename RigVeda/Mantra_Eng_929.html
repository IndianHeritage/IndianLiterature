मन्त्र: अत्राह गोर्म अन्वत नाम त्वष्टुर अपीच्यम् |इत्त्था चन्द्रमसो गृहे ||
atrāha gor amanvata nāma tvaṣṭur apīcyam | itthā candramaso gṛhe ||
  • atrāha: This word can be interpreted as "here" or "in this place." It signifies the importance of the present moment and the context in which the mantra is being recited.
  • gor: This term translates to "the cow" or "cattle," which in Vedic culture symbolizes wealth, nourishment, and prosperity. Cows are often revered and considered sacred in many Indian traditions.
  • amanvata: This word can mean "not to be disturbed" or "peaceful." It emphasizes the idea of tranquility and stability in one's life.
  • nāma: This translates to "name." In this context, it refers to the identity or essence of the divine or the subject being addressed.
  • tvaṣṭur: This term refers to "the divine craftsman" or "the creator." Tvaṣṭa is often associated with the act of creation and the shaping of the universe.
  • apīcyam: This can be interpreted as "that which is desired" or "that which is sought after." It indicates aspirations or goals that one wishes to achieve.
  • itthā: This word means "thus" or "in this manner." It connects the previous ideas to the outcome or the desired state.
  • candramaso: This translates to "of the moon." The moon is often associated with calmness, beauty, and the cyclical nature of life.
  • gṛhe: This means "in the home" or "in the household." It signifies the importance of the domestic space and the relationships within it.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking peace and prosperity in one's life, particularly within the home. It highlights the importance of stability and tranquility, which can be achieved through divine creation and the nurturing aspects of life.
  • Real-life Application: In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life. For instance, when one is facing challenges in their personal or professional life, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek peace and stability. It can also be used in rituals or prayers aimed at fostering harmony within the family and attracting prosperity.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of peace, prosperity, and the divine. By focusing on these elements, individuals can cultivate a harmonious environment in their homes and lives, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
