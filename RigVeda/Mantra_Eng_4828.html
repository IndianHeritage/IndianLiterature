इन्द्र मृळ मह्यं जीवातुम् इच्छ चोदय धियं अयसो न धाराम् |यत् किं चाहं त्वायुर इदं वदामि तज् जुषस्व कृधि मा देववन्तम् ||
indra mṛḻa mahyaṁ jīvātum iccha codaya dhiyam ayaso na dhārām | yat kiṁ cāhaṁ tvāyur idaṁ vadāmi taj juṣasva kṛdhi mā devavantam ||
  • Indra: Refers to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
  • Mṛḻa: Means to be gentle or to be kind, indicating a softer aspect of strength.
  • Mahyaṁ: Translates to "to me," suggesting a personal appeal or request.
  • Jīvātum: Means "to live" or "to thrive," emphasizing the importance of life and vitality.
  • Iccha: Means "desire" or "wish," indicating a longing for something.
  • Codaya: Means "to inspire" or "to motivate," suggesting a call for divine guidance.
  • Dhiyam: Refers to "intellect" or "understanding," highlighting the importance of wisdom.
  • Ayaso: Means "of strength" or "of power," reinforcing the theme of vitality.
  • Na: Means "not," indicating a negation or a desire to avoid something.
  • Dhārām: Refers to "support" or "sustenance," emphasizing the need for stability.
  • Yat: Means "whatever" or "that which," indicating a broad scope of inquiry.
  • Kiṁ: Means "what," suggesting a question or a quest for knowledge.
  • Cāhaṁ: Means "I," indicating a personal declaration or statement.
  • Tvāyur: Refers to "your life," indicating a focus on the recipient's existence.
  • Idaṁ: Means "this," pointing to the present moment or situation.
  • Vadāmi: Means "I speak" or "I say," indicating communication or expression.
  • Taj: Means "that," referring to the subject of the previous statement.
  • Juṣasva: Means "enjoy" or "accept," suggesting a welcoming of the offering.
  • Kṛdhi: Means "do" or "make," indicating action or creation.
  • Mā: Means "not," again indicating a negation.
  • Devavantam: Refers to "the divine" or "the godly," emphasizing the sacred nature of the request.

This mantra can be interpreted as a heartfelt plea to Indra, asking for the strength to live a fulfilling life, guided by wisdom and intellect. The speaker desires to be inspired and supported in their endeavors, while also expressing a wish for the divine to accept their offerings and actions.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In moments of personal struggle, individuals can recite this mantra to seek strength and guidance from a higher power.
  • In leadership roles, one can reflect on the qualities of Indra, striving to balance strength with kindness and wisdom.
  • In educational settings, students can use the mantra to inspire their pursuit of knowledge and understanding.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support and guidance in the pursuit of a meaningful life. It emphasizes the importance of strength, wisdom, and kindness, making it applicable in various aspects of daily life, from personal challenges to leadership and education. By invoking these qualities, individuals can strive to lead a life that is not only successful but also fulfilling and harmonious.
