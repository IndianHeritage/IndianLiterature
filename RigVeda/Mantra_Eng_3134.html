ऋतं वोच नमतसाऽ पृच्छ्यमानस् तवाशसाऽ जातवेदो यदीदम् |त्वम् अस्य क्षयसि यद धा विश्वं दिवि यद उ द्रविणं यत पृथिव्याम् ||
ṛtaṁ voce namasā pṛcchyamānas tavāśasā jātavedo yadīdam | tvam asya kṣayasi yad dha viśvaṁ divi yad u draviṇaṁ yat pṛthivyām ||
  • ṛtaṁ: This term refers to the cosmic order or truth that governs the universe. It signifies the principle of harmony and balance in nature.
  • voce: This means "to speak" or "to express." It indicates the act of communicating or invoking the divine.
  • namasā: This translates to "with reverence" or "with respect." It emphasizes the importance of humility and devotion in our approach to the divine.
  • pṛcchyamānas: This word means "inquired" or "asked." It suggests a quest for knowledge or understanding, often through prayer or meditation.
  • tavāśasā: This can be interpreted as "by your power" or "through your energy." It acknowledges the divine force that sustains and supports all existence.
  • jātavedo: This term refers to the "born-knower" or "the one who knows all." It is often associated with Agni, the fire god, symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
  • yadīdam: This means "whatever is here" or "that which exists." It encompasses all forms of life and matter in the universe.
  • tvam: This translates to "you," addressing the divine presence directly.
  • asya: This means "of this" or "of this world," indicating the connection between the divine and the earthly realm.
  • kṣayasi: This term means "you destroy" or "you remove." It signifies the transformative power of the divine to eliminate negativity or obstacles.
  • yad: This means "that" or "which," referring to the aspects of existence being discussed.
  • dha: This can be interpreted as "that which is," emphasizing the essence of being.
  • viśvaṁ: This term means "the universe" or "all that exists," highlighting the vastness of creation.
  • divi: This translates to "in the heavens" or "in the sky," representing the spiritual realm or higher consciousness.
  • yat: Similar to the previous "yat," it refers to "that" or "which," continuing the exploration of existence.
  • u: This means "and," connecting different elements of the mantra.
  • draviṇaṁ: This term refers to "wealth" or "resources," symbolizing abundance and prosperity.
  • yat: Again, this refers to "that" or "which," reinforcing the connection to the material world.
  • pṛthivyām: This means "on earth" or "in the physical realm," grounding the spiritual concepts in our tangible reality.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and understanding the interconnectedness of all existence. It emphasizes the importance of approaching the divine with reverence and humility while recognizing the cosmic order that governs our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of reflection, meditation, or prayer, reminding us to seek knowledge and clarity in our actions and decisions. It encourages us to acknowledge the divine presence in our lives, fostering a sense of gratitude and connection to the universe. By understanding and embodying the principles of this mantra, we can navigate life's challenges with wisdom and grace, ultimately contributing to a harmonious existence.
