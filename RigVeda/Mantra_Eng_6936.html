दभ्रं चिद्धि त्वावतः कृतं श्रिण्वे अधि क्षमि |जिगात्व इन्द्र ते मनः ||
dabhraṁ cid dhi tvāvataḥ kṛtaṁ śṛṇve adhi kṣami | jigātv indra te manaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dabhraṁ: A term that can be interpreted as "to be strong" or "to be powerful."
    • cid: This word means "indeed" or "certainly."
    • hii: A particle that emphasizes the preceding word, often translated as "certainly."
    • tvāvataḥ: Refers to "your" or "of you," indicating possession or connection.
    • kṛtaṁ: Means "done" or "created," suggesting an action that has been completed.
    • śṛṇve: Translates to "listen" or "hear," indicating the act of receiving knowledge or wisdom.
    • adhi: Means "above" or "over," often used to denote a higher state or level.
    • kṣami: Refers to "forgiveness" or "patience," indicating a quality of tolerance or understanding.
    • jigātv: Means "to conquer" or "to win," suggesting a sense of achievement or victory.
    • indra: Refers to the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power and authority.
    • te: Means "your," again indicating possession.
    • manaḥ: Translates to "mind" or "intellect," referring to the cognitive aspect of a person.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra can be interpreted as a call for strength and wisdom, urging the listener to be powerful and to listen to the higher wisdom that has been created.
    • It emphasizes the importance of conquering one's mind and thoughts, seeking forgiveness, and understanding the higher aspects of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied when facing challenges. For instance, a leader may need to listen to their team (śṛṇve) and be open to new ideas (kṣami) to conquer (jigātv) obstacles.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra encourages individuals to be strong (dabhraṁ) and to forgive (kṣami) misunderstandings, fostering a deeper connection.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for inner strength, wisdom, and the ability to listen and forgive.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as leadership, personal growth, and relationship management, reminding us of the importance of mental clarity and emotional resilience.
