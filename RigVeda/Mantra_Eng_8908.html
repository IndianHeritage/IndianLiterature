अङ्गिरसो नः पितरो नवग्वा अथर्वाणो भृगवः सोम्यासः |तेषां वयं सुमतौ यज्ञियानाम् अपि भद्रे सुमनसे स्याम ||
aṅgiraso naḥ pitaro navagvā atharvāṇo bhṛgavaḥ somyāsaḥ | teṣāṁ vayaṁ sumatau yajñiyānām api bhadre saumanase syāma ||
  • Mantra: "aṅgiraso naḥ pitaro navagvā atharvāṇo bhṛgavaḥ somyāsaḥ | teṣāṁ vayaṁ sumatau yajñiyānām api bhadre saumanase syāma ||"
  • Translation: "O Angirases, our fathers, the nine sacred ones, the Atharvans, the Bhṛgus, may we be of good mind, and may we be blessed with auspiciousness in our sacrifices."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • aṅgiraso: Refers to the Angiras, a group of sages known for their wisdom and knowledge.
    • naḥ: Means "our," indicating a collective identity.
    • pitaro: Means "fathers," symbolizing ancestors or forefathers.
    • navagvā: Refers to the "nine sacred ones," which can symbolize various divine or spiritual entities.
    • atharvāṇo: Refers to the Atharvans, who are associated with the Atharva Veda, known for its focus on rituals and practical aspects of life.
    • bhṛgavaḥ: Refers to the Bhṛgus, another group of sages known for their spiritual insights.
    • somyāsaḥ: Means "of good mind" or "auspicious," indicating a positive state of being.
    • teṣāṁ: Means "of them," referring to the divine entities mentioned earlier.
    • vayaṁ: Means "we," again emphasizing the collective.
    • sumatau: Means "good mind" or "auspiciousness," indicating a state of mental clarity and positivity.
    • yajñiyānām: Refers to "sacrifices," highlighting the importance of rituals in Vedic culture.
    • api: Means "also," indicating inclusion.
    • bhadre: Means "auspicious" or "good," emphasizing the desire for positive outcomes.
    • saumanase: Means "of good mind" or "pleasant," reinforcing the theme of positivity.
    • syāma: Means "may we be," indicating a wish or prayer.
  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking blessings from divine entities and ancestors for mental clarity and auspiciousness in life. It reflects the Vedic belief in the interconnectedness of individuals with their spiritual heritage and the divine.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In personal rituals, individuals may recite this mantra to invoke blessings for clarity and positivity in their endeavors, such as starting a new job or beginning a significant project.
    • During family gatherings or ceremonies, this mantra can be chanted to honor ancestors and seek their guidance, fostering a sense of unity and respect for one's heritage.
    • In community events, such as festivals or religious ceremonies, this mantra can be used to promote collective well-being and harmony among participants.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of mental clarity, auspiciousness, and the blessings of ancestors and divine entities in achieving success and harmony in life. It encourages individuals to cultivate a positive mindset and seek spiritual guidance in their daily lives.
