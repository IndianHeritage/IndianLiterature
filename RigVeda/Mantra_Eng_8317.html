मन्त्र: सिन्धोर इव प्रवणें निम्न आशवो वृषच्युताः मदासो गातुम आशत || शं नो निवेशे द्विपदे चतुष्पदेऽस्मे वाजाः सोम तिष्ठन्तु कृष्टयः ||
sindhor iva pravaṇe nimna āśavo vṛṣacyutā madāso gātum āśata | śaṁ no niveśe dvipade catuṣpade 'sme vājāḥ soma tiṣṭhantu kṛṣṭayaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "sindhor iva pravaṇe nimna āśavo vṛṣacyutā madāso gātum āśata | śaṁ no niveśe dvipade catuṣpade 'sme vājāḥ soma tiṣṭhantu kṛṣṭayaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sindhor: of the ocean
    • iva: like
    • pravaṇe: in the flow
    • nimna: low
    • āśavo: swift ones
    • vṛṣacyutā: those who are strong and powerful
    • madāso: intoxicated
    • gātum: to go
    • āśata: may they go
    • śaṁ: peace
    • no: to us
    • niveśe: in our dwelling
    • dvipade: two-legged (humans)
    • catuṣpade: four-legged (animals)
    • 'sme: may there be
    • vājāḥ: steeds or resources
    • soma: the sacred drink
    • tiṣṭhantu: may they remain
    • kṛṣṭayaḥ: cultivated fields
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra draws a parallel between the flow of the ocean and the swift, powerful forces of nature.
    • The reference to "nimna āśavo" suggests that even the lowly or humble can possess great strength and speed, akin to the currents of the ocean.
    • The invocation for peace ("śaṁ no niveśe") emphasizes the importance of harmony in our lives, both for humans and animals.
    • The mention of "vājāḥ soma" indicates a desire for abundance and sustenance, symbolizing the resources necessary for life and prosperity.
    • In real life, this mantra can be seen as a call for balance and harmony in our environment, encouraging us to respect both nature and the resources it provides.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the environment.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek peace and abundance in our lives while respecting the natural world.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can inspire sustainable living practices, encouraging individuals and communities to cultivate their resources wisely and harmoniously.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can contribute to a more balanced and prosperous world for all living beings.
