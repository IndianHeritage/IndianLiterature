यः स्मारुन्धानो गध्या समत्सु सन्तरश्चरति गोषु गच्छन् | आविर्ऋजिको विदथा निचिक्यत तिरो अरतिम पर्य आप आयोः ||
yaḥ smārundhāno gadhyā samatsu sanutaraś carati goṣu gacchan | āvirṛjīko vidathā nicikyat tiro aratim pary āpa āyoḥ ||
  • yaḥ - This word translates to "who" or "that one." It indicates a subject or a person being referred to.
  • smārundhāno - This term can be broken down into "smāra," which means "to remember," and "undhāna," which refers to "the one who brings forth." Together, it suggests someone who brings forth memories or knowledge.
  • gadhyā - This word means "with strength" or "powerfully." It implies a sense of vigor or forcefulness in action.
  • samatsu - This term means "in harmony" or "together." It emphasizes unity and collective action.
  • sanutaraś - This translates to "the best" or "the most excellent." It denotes superiority or high quality.
  • carati - This means "moves" or "travels." It indicates action or progression.
  • goṣu - This word means "among the cows" or "in the herd." It symbolizes nurturing, sustenance, and the importance of community.
  • gacchan - This translates to "going" or "moving forward." It signifies progress and the journey of life.
  • āvirṛjīko - This term can be interpreted as "the one who reveals" or "brings forth." It suggests enlightenment or the unveiling of truth.
  • vidathā - This means "the assembly" or "gathering." It refers to a collective group or community.
  • nicikyat - This translates to "having been established" or "set in place." It indicates stability and foundation.
  • tiro - This word means "beyond" or "across." It suggests transcending boundaries or limitations.
  • aratim - This translates to "joy" or "delight." It signifies happiness and contentment.
  • pary - This means "around" or "surrounding." It indicates inclusivity and the encompassing nature of existence.
  • āpa - This word means "water" or "flow." It symbolizes nourishment, life, and the essential nature of water.
  • āyoḥ - This translates to "of the two" or "of the pairs." It signifies duality and the balance between opposites.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of collective memory, strength, and harmony in the journey of life. It highlights the significance of community and the nurturing aspects of existence, akin to a herd of cows that support each other. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in a workplace, family support systems, or community initiatives where individuals come together to achieve a common goal. The mantra serves as a reminder that through unity and shared purpose, we can transcend challenges and find joy in our collective endeavors.
