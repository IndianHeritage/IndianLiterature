विवातजूतः अतसेषु तिष्ठते वृथा जुहूभिः श्रिण्याऽतुविष्वणिः |तृषु यद अग्ने वनिनो वृषायसे कृष्णं त एमा रुशदूर्मे अजरा ||
vi vātajūto ataseṣu tiṣṭhate vṛthā juhūbhiḥ sṛṇyā tuviṣvaṇiḥ | tṛṣu yad agne vanino vṛṣāyase kṛṣṇaṁ ta ema ruśadūrme ajara ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vi: apart, separately
    • vātajūto: connected with the wind
    • ataseṣu: in the vastness
    • tiṣṭhate: stands, remains
    • vṛthā: in vain, uselessly
    • juhūbhiḥ: with offerings, sacrifices
    • sṛṇyā: with the sound, with the call
    • tuviṣvaṇiḥ: the one who is all-pervading
    • tṛṣu: in the three realms (heaven, earth, and the atmosphere)
    • yad: that which
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • vanino: of the forest, of the wilderness
    • vṛṣāyase: who is powerful, strong
    • kṛṣṇaṁ: the dark one, often referring to Lord Krishna
    • ta: that
    • ema: may it be
    • ruśadūrme: the one who is unyielding, firm
    • ajara: immortal, eternal
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the connection between the divine and the natural world, emphasizing the presence of Agni, the fire god, in all realms.
    • The phrase "vi vātajūto ataseṣu tiṣṭhate" suggests that the divine energy exists separately yet is connected to the vastness of the universe.
    • It highlights the futility of offerings made without understanding the true nature of the divine, as indicated by "vṛthā juhūbhiḥ."
    • The mantra calls upon Agni, representing the transformative power of fire, to bless the offerings made in the three realms.
    • It also refers to the strength and resilience of the divine, as seen in "ruśadūrme ajara," indicating that the divine is eternal and unyielding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the importance of intention behind actions. For instance, in a corporate setting, a company may engage in charitable activities. However, if these actions are not genuine or are done merely for publicity, they may be seen as "vṛthā juhūbhiḥ."
    • In personal relationships, understanding and genuine connection are crucial. If one offers love and support without true understanding, it may lead to misunderstandings and conflicts.
    • The concept of Agni can be likened to the passion and drive one has in their career. Just as Agni transforms offerings, a person's dedication can transform their work and lead to success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of understanding the deeper connections in life and the importance of genuine intentions behind our actions.
    • It teaches us that true offerings, whether in the form of love, work, or service, must come from a place of authenticity to be meaningful.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages us to recognize the divine presence in all aspects of life, urging us to act with purpose and integrity.
