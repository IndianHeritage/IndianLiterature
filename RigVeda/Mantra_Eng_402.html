त्रिर वार्तिर यातं त्रिर अनुव्रते जनें त्रिः सुप्राव्ये त्रेदेव शिक्षतम् |त्रिर नन्द्यं वहतम् अश्विनां युवं त्रिः पृक्षो अस्मे अक्षरेव पिन्वतम् ||
trir vartir yātaṁ trir anuvrate jane triḥ suprāvye tredheva śikṣatam | trir nāndyaṁ vahatam aśvinā yuvaṁ triḥ pṛkṣo asme akṣareva pinvatam ||
  • Mantra Breakdown:
    • trir vartir yātaṁ: "Three times the path is traveled." This suggests the importance of repetition and consistency in one's actions and decisions.
    • trir anuvrate jane: "Three times the people follow." This emphasizes the collective journey and the significance of community in achieving goals.
    • triḥ suprāvye: "Three times well-versed." This indicates the value of knowledge and wisdom, suggesting that one should strive to be well-informed.
    • tredheva śikṣatam: "Three times the divine teaches." This highlights the role of divine guidance in learning and personal growth.
    • trir nāndyaṁ vahatam: "Three times the joy is carried." This reflects the importance of joy and positivity in life’s journey.
    • aśvinā yuvaṁ: "With the youthful Aśvins." The Aśvins are twin deities associated with health and vitality, symbolizing the importance of youthfulness and energy.
    • triḥ pṛkṣo asme: "Three times we are observed." This suggests that our actions are watched and evaluated, emphasizing accountability.
    • akṣareva pinvatam: "Like the imperishable." This refers to the eternal nature of truth and wisdom, which remains constant despite the changes in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of "three times" can be seen in various aspects of life, such as the three stages of life: childhood, adulthood, and old age. Each stage requires different approaches and learning.
    • In a workplace, teamwork is essential. The mantra emphasizes collective effort, where team members support each other to achieve common goals.
    • Education systems often stress the importance of revisiting concepts multiple times to ensure understanding, reflecting the idea of "three times well-versed."
    • In personal development, seeking guidance from mentors or spiritual leaders can be seen as the "divine teaching" that helps individuals grow.
    • Joy can be found in simple moments, such as spending time with loved ones or achieving personal milestones, which aligns with the idea of carrying joy in life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of learning, community, joy, and divine guidance. It serves as a reminder that our journey in life is enriched by knowledge, collective effort, and the pursuit of joy.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, stronger relationships, and a more fulfilling life. It encourages individuals to be mindful of their actions, seek knowledge, and cherish the joy in their experiences.
