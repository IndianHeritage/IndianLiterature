तं उ ज्येष्ठं नमसाऽहविर्भिः सुशेवम् ब्रह्मणस पतिं गृणीषे | इन्द्रं श्लोको महि दैव्यः सिषक्तु यो ब्रह्मणो देवकृतस्य राजा ||
tam u jyeṣṭhaṁ namasā havirbhiḥ suśevam brahmaṇas patiṁ gṛṇīṣe | indraṁ śloko mahi daivyaḥ siṣaktu yo brahmaṇo devakṛtasya rājā ||
  • tam u jyeṣṭhaṁ: This phrase refers to the supreme or elder one, often interpreted as a reference to a deity or a higher power. In real life, this can symbolize the respect and reverence we hold for our elders or leaders in society.
  • namasā: This means 'with reverence' or 'with humility'. It emphasizes the importance of approaching the divine or our elders with respect. For example, in many cultures, bowing or greeting elders signifies respect.
  • havirbhiḥ: This term relates to offerings or sacrifices. In a modern context, it can be seen as the act of giving or contributing to a cause, such as charity or community service, which is a way of honoring the greater good.
  • suśevam: This translates to 'well-formed' or 'auspicious'. It suggests that our actions should be positive and beneficial. For instance, engaging in sustainable practices can be seen as creating a 'well-formed' environment for future generations.
  • brahmaṇas patiṁ: This means 'the lord of creation' or 'the master of the universe'. It signifies the ultimate authority or power in the universe. In real life, this can be interpreted as recognizing the interconnectedness of all life and the responsibility we have towards it.
  • gṛṇīṣe: This means 'I sing' or 'I praise'. It reflects the act of expressing gratitude and appreciation. In everyday life, this can be seen in practices like gratitude journaling or simply acknowledging the efforts of others.
  • indraṁ: Referring to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength and leadership. In a contemporary context, this can represent the qualities of a good leader, such as courage and wisdom.
  • śloko mahi: This translates to 'great verse' or 'sacred hymn'. It highlights the importance of sacred texts and teachings in guiding our lives. For example, many people turn to religious or philosophical texts for guidance in difficult times.
  • daivyaḥ siṣaktu: This means 'may the divine empower'. It suggests a plea for divine support and strength. In real life, this can be seen in moments of prayer or meditation where individuals seek strength to overcome challenges.
  • yo brahmaṇo devakṛtasya rājā: This phrase refers to the one who is the king of the creation made by the divine. It signifies the idea of divine order and governance. In a practical sense, this can relate to the importance of ethical leadership in society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of reverence, respect for creation, and the importance of ethical leadership. It encourages individuals to approach life with humility and gratitude while recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, environmental stewardship, and ethical governance, fostering a society that values respect, responsibility, and harmony.
