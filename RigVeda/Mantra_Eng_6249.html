आ नूनं रघुवर्तनिं रथं तिष्ठाथो अश्विनाः |आ वां स्तोमा इमे मम नभो न चुच्यवीरता ||
ā nūnaṁ raghuvartaniṁ rathaṁ tiṣṭhātho aśvinā | ā vāṁ stomā ime mama nabho na cucyavīrata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā nūnaṁ: Indeed, certainly
    • raghuvartaniṁ: the one who follows the path of the Raghu dynasty
    • rathaṁ: chariot
    • tiṣṭhātho: stand firm, remain steady
    • aśvinā: O Ashvins (the twin deities associated with health and medicine)
    • ā vāṁ: we invoke you
    • stomā: hymns, praises
    • ime: these
    • mama: my
    • nabho: sky, heavens
    • na: not
    • cucyavīrata: let it fall away, let it not be lost
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep invocation to the divine, particularly to the Ashvins, who are revered for their healing and protective qualities.
    • The reference to "raghuvartaniṁ" signifies a connection to a noble lineage, suggesting that the speaker seeks strength and guidance from a lineage known for its valor and righteousness.
    • The chariot symbolizes the journey of life, and the call to "stand firm" indicates a desire for stability and support during this journey.
    • The invocation of hymns reflects the importance of expressing gratitude and reverence to the divine forces that guide and protect us.
    • The plea for the "sky" or "heavens" to not let anything fall away emphasizes the importance of maintaining one's aspirations and connections to the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often turn to prayer or meditation, invoking divine support to help them navigate their challenges, much like the invocation in this mantra.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, people may seek blessings from their ancestors or deities, paralleling the call for divine guidance in the mantra.
    • In the context of leadership, a leader may draw inspiration from noble lineages or historical figures, seeking to embody their virtues and strength, akin to the reference to the Raghu dynasty.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in our lives.
    • It emphasizes the need for stability and strength as we navigate our personal journeys, invoking the protective qualities of the divine.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to remain connected to their roots and values, drawing inspiration from noble lineages and the divine to overcome challenges.
