मूर्धानं दिवो अरतिम पृथिव्यां वैश्वानरम् ऋत आ जातम अग्निम् | कविं सम्राजं अतिथिं जनानां आसन्न आ पात्रं जनयन्त देवाः ||
mūrdhānaṁ divo aratim pṛthivyā vaiśvānaram ṛta ā jātam agnim | kaviṁ samrājam atithiṁ janānām āsann ā pātraṁ janayanta devāḥ ||
  • mūrdhānaṁ: This word translates to "the head" or "the top." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent the pinnacle of knowledge or consciousness.
  • divo: Meaning "of the sky" or "heaven," this term signifies the divine or celestial realm.
  • aratim: This translates to "joy" or "delight," indicating a state of happiness or contentment.
  • pṛthivyā: Referring to "the earth," it symbolizes the physical world and our earthly existence.
  • vaiśvānaram: This term can be understood as "the universal fire," representing the vital energy or life force that sustains all beings.
  • ṛta: Meaning "truth" or "cosmic order," it signifies the underlying principle of harmony and balance in the universe.
  • ā jātam: This phrase means "born from" or "arising from," indicating the origin or source of something.
  • agnim: This translates to "fire," which is often associated with transformation, purification, and energy.
  • kaviṁ: Meaning "the wise one" or "the poet," it represents creativity and insight.
  • samrājam: This term translates to "the ruler" or "king," symbolizing authority and governance.
  • atithiṁ: Meaning "the guest," it signifies hospitality and the importance of welcoming others.
  • janānām: This translates to "of the people," indicating a collective or community aspect.
  • āsann: Meaning "near" or "approaching," it suggests proximity or readiness.
  • ā pātraṁ: This phrase means "the vessel" or "the container," symbolizing receptivity and the ability to hold or nurture.
  • janayanta: This translates to "creating" or "generating," indicating the act of bringing forth or manifesting.
  • devāḥ: Meaning "the gods," it signifies divine beings or higher powers.

In summary, this mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of the divine, the earth, and humanity. It emphasizes the importance of joy, truth, and the creative forces that govern our lives. The invocation of fire symbolizes transformation and the vital energy that sustains existence. The mention of guests and community highlights the significance of hospitality and collective well-being.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for harmony between the celestial and earthly realms, urging individuals to embrace joy, truth, and creativity in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as community building, fostering creativity, and promoting a sense of belonging. By recognizing the divine presence in everyday life and nurturing relationships, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling existence.
