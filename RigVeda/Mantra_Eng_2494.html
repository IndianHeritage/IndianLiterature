वनस्पतेऽव सृजोऽप देवान अग्निः हविः शमितासूदयाति |सेदु होतासत्यतरो यजाती यथा देवानां जनिमानि वेद ||
vanaspate 'va sṛjopa devān agnir haviḥ śamitā sūdayāti | sed u hotā satyataro yajāti yathā devānāṁ janimāni veda ||
  • vanaspate: This term refers to the "lord of the plants" or "vegetation." In a real-life context, it can symbolize the importance of nature and the environment in sustaining life.
  • 'va sṛjopa: This phrase means "may you create" or "may you bring forth." It emphasizes the act of creation and the role of divine forces in the manifestation of life.
  • devān: This word translates to "gods" or "divine beings." It signifies the higher powers that govern the universe and influence human existence.
  • agnir: Meaning "fire," this element is often associated with energy, transformation, and purification. In daily life, fire can represent both physical fire and metaphorical fire, such as passion or ambition.
  • haviḥ: This term refers to offerings or sacrifices made to the gods. It highlights the importance of gratitude and the act of giving back to the universe.
  • śamitā: This means "calm" or "peaceful." It suggests the need for tranquility in life, which can be achieved through meditation, mindfulness, or connecting with nature.
  • sūdayāti: This translates to "he who nourishes" or "he who sustains." It reflects the idea of nurturing and supporting life, akin to how caregivers or mentors play a vital role in our lives.
  • sed u hotā: This phrase means "but the priest" or "the one who invokes." It signifies the role of individuals who guide and lead spiritual practices, akin to teachers or leaders in society.
  • satyataro: This translates to "the truest" or "the most truthful." It emphasizes the value of truth and integrity in our actions and relationships.
  • yajāti: Meaning "he sacrifices" or "he offers," this term reinforces the idea of selflessness and the importance of contributing to the greater good.
  • yathā devānāṁ: This phrase means "as the gods" or "like the divine beings." It suggests that human actions should aspire to the standards set by higher powers.
  • janimāni: This translates to "births" or "origins." It signifies the cycles of life and the continuous process of creation and rebirth.
  • veda: Meaning "knowledge" or "wisdom," this term highlights the importance of learning and understanding in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, divinity, and human existence. It calls for a harmonious relationship with the environment, the importance of truth and integrity, and the need for selflessness in our actions. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied through sustainable living practices, fostering honesty in relationships, and contributing positively to society. By embodying these principles, individuals can create a more balanced and fulfilling life, reflecting the wisdom of the RigVeda in contemporary contexts.
