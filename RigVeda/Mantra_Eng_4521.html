अग्निस् तिग्मेन शोचिषा यासद् विश्वं न्यातृणम् |अग्निर्नो वानते रयिम् ||
agnis tigmena śociṣā yāsad viśvaṁ ny a1triṇam | agnir no vanate rayim ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Agnis: Fire
    • Tigmena: With brilliance or radiance
    • Śociṣā: Shining or illuminating
    • Yāsad: May he (Agni) bring
    • Viśvaṁ: All or universal
    • Ny a1triṇam: To those who are devoted or worshipping
    • Agnir: Fire (again, referring to Agni)
    • No: For us
    • Vanate: May he bestow or grant
    • Rayim: Wealth or prosperity
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, asking him to shine brightly and bring prosperity to all who worship him. It emphasizes the importance of fire as a source of light, warmth, and sustenance, symbolizing knowledge and purity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Rituals: In many cultures, fire is central to rituals and ceremonies. For instance, in Hindu weddings, the sacred fire (Agni) is a witness to the vows taken by the couple, symbolizing purity and the divine presence.
    • In Daily Life: The use of fire in cooking not only provides nourishment but also brings families together, fostering relationships and community bonding over shared meals.
    • In Knowledge: Just as fire illuminates darkness, knowledge enlightens the mind. In educational settings, the pursuit of knowledge can be seen as a way to 'ignite' one's potential, leading to personal and professional growth.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of Agni as a vital force in life, representing both physical and spiritual nourishment. It teaches us to honor the elements that sustain us and to seek prosperity through devotion and respect. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate gratitude for the resources they have, to engage in community-building activities, and to pursue knowledge and enlightenment in their personal journeys.
