मन्त्र: विश्वस्मा इद इषुध्यते देवत्रा हव्यं ओहिषे |विश्वस्मा इत सुखृत वारण ऋण्वत्य अग्निर द्वारा व्य ऋण्वति ||
viśvasmā id iṣudhyate devatrā havyam ohiṣe | viśvasmā it sukṛte vāram ṛṇvaty agnir dvārā vy ṛṇvati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viśvasmā: From all directions or universally.
    • id: This.
    • iṣudhyate: Is purified or made pure.
    • devatrā: By the divine or deities.
    • havyam: Offerings or that which is offered.
    • ohiṣe: To be accepted or received.
    • sukṛte: In good deeds or virtuous actions.
    • vāram: A boon or blessing.
    • ṛṇvaty: Is freed from debt or obligation.
    • agnir: Fire, often symbolizing energy or the divine.
    • dvārā: Through the door or gateway.
    • vy ṛṇvati: Is made to flow or is offered.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the universal nature of divine offerings and the purification that comes from them.
    • It emphasizes the importance of good deeds and how they can lead to blessings and freedom from obligations.
    • The mention of fire symbolizes transformation and the divine energy that is invoked through offerings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to help those in need, they are performing good deeds (sukṛte) that can lead to blessings for the entire community.
    • When individuals engage in acts of kindness, such as volunteering or donating, they are purifying their intentions (iṣudhyate) and contributing to a greater good.
    • In rituals, offerings made to deities (devatrā) are believed to bring prosperity and peace, reflecting the idea of receiving blessings (vāram).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of universal offerings and the power of good deeds in our lives.
    • It teaches us that through virtuous actions, we can purify our intentions and receive blessings that help us navigate life's challenges.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as community service, personal relationships, and spiritual practices, reminding us of the interconnectedness of our actions and their impact on the world.
