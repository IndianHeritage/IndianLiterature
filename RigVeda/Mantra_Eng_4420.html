अध जिव्हा पापतिति प्र वृष्णो गोषुयुधो नाशनिः सृजानाऽ। शूरस्येव प्रसितिः क्षातिर अग्नेर दुर्वर्तुर भीमो दयते वनानि॥
adha jihvā pāpatīti pra vṛṣṇo goṣuyudho nāśaniḥ sṛjānā | śūrasyeva prasitiḥ kṣātir agner durvartur bhīmo dayate vanāni ||
  • Adha jihvā pāpatīti: This phrase can be translated as "the tongue that speaks ill." It signifies the importance of speech and the impact of words. In real life, this can be related to gossip or slander, which can harm relationships and reputations.
  • Pra vṛṣṇo: This refers to the "powerful one" or "the strong." It symbolizes strength and resilience. For example, in a workplace, a strong leader who inspires and motivates their team can lead to success.
  • Goṣuyudho nāśaniḥ sṛjānā: This can be interpreted as "the one who creates the destruction of the enemies." It emphasizes the idea of overcoming obstacles and adversaries. In a real-life scenario, this could relate to a person who faces challenges head-on, such as an athlete overcoming injuries to achieve their goals.
  • Śūrasyeva prasitiḥ: This means "like the valor of a hero." It highlights the qualities of bravery and courage. For instance, a firefighter risking their life to save others exemplifies this valor in action.
  • Kṣātir agner: This translates to "the power of fire." Fire symbolizes transformation and purification. In life, this can be seen in the process of personal growth, where challenges act as a fire that refines one's character.
  • Durvartur bhīmo: This phrase means "the terrifying one who is difficult to overcome." It represents formidable challenges. For example, a serious illness can be a durvartur bhīmo that tests a person's strength and resilience.
  • Dayate vanāni: This translates to "the one who shows compassion to the forests." It signifies the importance of compassion and care for nature. In real life, this can be related to environmental conservation efforts, where individuals or groups work to protect forests and wildlife.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of speech, strength, courage, and compassion. It teaches us to be mindful of our words, to face challenges with bravery, and to show care for the environment. In today's world, these values are applicable in various scenarios, such as in personal relationships, professional settings, and environmental activism. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute positively to society and foster a harmonious existence.
