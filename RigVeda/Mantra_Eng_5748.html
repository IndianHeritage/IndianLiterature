आ यातम उप भूषतम मध्वः पिबतम अश्विनाः |दुग्धं पयो वृषणा जेन्यावसू मा नो मर्दिष्टम आगतम ||
ā yātam upa bhūṣatam madhvaḥ pibatam aśvinā | dugdham payo vṛṣaṇā jenyāvasū mā no mardhiṣṭam ā gatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yātam: "Come to us" - a call for divine presence.
    • upa bhūṣatam: "Bestow your blessings" - an appeal for grace and support.
    • madhvaḥ: "Sweetness" - symbolizes joy and positivity.
    • pibatam: "Drink" - an invitation to partake in the blessings.
    • aśvinā: "O Ashvins" - referring to the twin deities associated with health and medicine.
    • dugdham: "Milk" - represents nourishment and purity.
    • payo: "Of the milk" - emphasizing the essence of sustenance.
    • vṛṣaṇā: "Of the strong" - indicating strength and vitality.
    • jenyāvasū: "May we be protected" - a plea for safety and well-being.
    • mā no: "May not" - a negation, expressing a desire for avoidance of harm.
    • mardhiṣṭam: "Be harmed" - a reference to protection from adversity.
    • ā gatam: "That which has come" - acknowledging the presence of blessings or challenges.
  • Context and Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the Ashvins, who are revered as divine physicians in Vedic literature.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking divine intervention for health, prosperity, and protection.
    • The imagery of milk signifies nourishment, both physical and spiritual, highlighting the need for sustenance in life.
    • The call for blessings and protection reflects a universal human desire for safety and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of illness, individuals often seek spiritual support through prayers, similar to invoking the Ashvins for healing.
    • During significant life events, such as childbirth or marriage, people may recite mantras to invoke blessings for health and happiness.
    • In communities, rituals may be performed to seek protection from natural disasters, echoing the plea for safety in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of health, nourishment, and divine support.
    • It highlights the significance of seeking blessings in daily life, whether through personal prayer or communal rituals.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages individuals to cultivate positivity and strength, fostering resilience against life's challenges.
