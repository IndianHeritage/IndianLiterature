अनिरेण वचसा फल्ग्वेन प्रतीत्येन कृधुनातृपाशः |अधā ते अग्ने किम इहā वदन्त्य अनायुधासाऽसतā सचन्ताम् ||
anireṇa vacasā phalgvena pratītyena kṛdhunātṛpāsaḥ | adhā te agne kim ihā vadanty anāyudhāsa āsatā sacantām ||
  • anireṇa: This word can be interpreted as "without any hindrance" or "unobstructed." It signifies the importance of clarity and openness in communication.
  • vacasā: This translates to "by speech" or "through words." It emphasizes the power of words and their ability to convey thoughts and intentions.
  • phalgvena: This term refers to "with a light or gentle touch." It suggests that communication should be gentle and considerate, rather than harsh or aggressive.
  • pratītyena: This means "by understanding" or "through realization." It highlights the necessity of comprehension in effective communication.
  • kṛdhunātṛpāsaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "those who are not burdened" or "free from distress." It indicates that effective communication occurs when individuals are at ease and free from emotional burdens.
  • adhā: This means "therefore" or "thus," indicating a conclusion or result based on the previous statements.
  • te: This translates to "you" or "to you," addressing the audience or the divine.
  • agne: This refers to "O Agni," the fire god, symbolizing purity, energy, and the divine spark within us.
  • kim ihā vadanty: This means "what do they say here?" It questions the nature of the discourse or the messages being conveyed.
  • anāyudhāsa: This can be interpreted as "without weapons" or "unarmed," suggesting a peaceful approach to communication.
  • āsatā: This means "those who are present" or "those who exist," referring to the audience or the community engaged in the dialogue.
  • sacantām: This translates to "may they be truthful" or "may they be sincere," emphasizing the importance of honesty in communication.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of clear, gentle, and understanding communication, free from emotional burdens. It highlights the role of honesty and sincerity in discourse, particularly in the context of seeking knowledge or truth. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various settings such as personal relationships, professional environments, and community discussions. By fostering open and honest communication, individuals can build trust, resolve conflicts, and create a harmonious atmosphere conducive to growth and understanding. The invocation of Agni symbolizes the divine aspect of communication, reminding us to approach conversations with respect and integrity.
