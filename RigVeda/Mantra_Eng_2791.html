इन्द्राय सोमाः प्रदिवो विदाना ऋभर येभिर वृषपर्वा विहायाः |प्रयम्यमानान प्रति षू गृभायन्द्र पिब वृषधूतस्य वृष्णः ||
indrāya somāḥ pradivo vidānā ṛbhur yebhir vṛṣaparvā vihāyāḥ | prayamyamānān prati ṣū gṛbhāyendra piba vṛṣadhūtasya vṛṣṇaḥ ||
  • Indrāya: This refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunder, and war. Indra symbolizes strength and power.
  • Somāḥ: This word refers to Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic rituals believed to confer immortality and divine inspiration. It represents the essence of life and vitality.
  • Pradivo vidānā: This phrase can be interpreted as "knowing the heavens" or "understanding the celestial realms." It signifies the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment.
  • Ṛbhur: This term means "the wise" or "the learned." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in life.
  • Yebhir: This word translates to "by whom" or "through which," indicating a connection or means to achieve something.
  • Vṛṣaparvā: This refers to the "bull" or "strength," often symbolizing fertility and abundance. It represents the power of nature and its bounties.
  • Vihāyāḥ: This means "having abandoned" or "having let go." It suggests the idea of relinquishing attachments or burdens.
  • Prayamyamānān: This term can be understood as "those who are being offered" or "those who are being invoked." It indicates a ritualistic offering or prayer.
  • Prati ṣū gṛbhāy: This phrase means "to receive" or "to grasp." It signifies the act of accepting blessings or divine gifts.
  • Indra piba: This translates to "Indra, drink" or "Indra, imbibe." It is a call to Indra to partake in the offerings made to him.
  • Vṛṣadhūtasya: This means "of the bull of strength" or "of the powerful." It emphasizes the potency of the offerings being made.
  • Vṛṣṇaḥ: This term refers to "the strong" or "the powerful one," often associated with prosperity and success.

Conclusion: This mantra is a prayer to Indra, invoking his strength and blessings through the sacred drink of Soma. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom, knowledge, and the relinquishing of burdens to receive divine blessings. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during rituals for prosperity, seeking strength in challenging times, or pursuing knowledge and enlightenment. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature, divinity, and human effort, encouraging individuals to seek wisdom and strength in their endeavors.
