यद अन्तरिक्षे यद दिवि यत् पञ्च मानवश्चानु | नृम्णं तद धत्तम अश्विनाः ||
yad antarikṣe yad divi yat pañca mānuṣām̐ anu | nṛmṇaṁ tad dhattam aśvinā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • antarikṣe: in the atmosphere or space
    • yad: whatever
    • divi: in the heavens
    • yat: whatever
    • pañca: five
    • mānuṣām̐: of human beings
    • anu: following or in accordance with
    • nṛmṇaṁ: the human body or form
    • tad: that
    • dhattam: may be held or supported
    • aśvinā: by the Aśvins (the twin deities)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of all realms: the atmosphere, the heavens, and the human experience.
    • The phrase "yad antarikṣe yad divi" suggests that everything in the universe, whether in the physical realm or the spiritual, is part of a greater whole.
    • The mention of "pañca mānuṣām̐" indicates a focus on humanity, emphasizing the importance of human existence and experience in the cosmic order.
    • The Aśvins, known as divine physicians, symbolize healing and the pursuit of knowledge, suggesting that the support and sustenance of life come from both divine and earthly sources.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern science, the concept of interconnectedness is reflected in ecology, where every organism plays a role in the ecosystem, similar to how the mantra connects different realms.
    • In personal health, the idea of holistic well-being aligns with the Aśvins' role as healers, reminding us to consider both physical and mental health.
    • In community living, the mantra encourages collaboration and support among individuals, akin to how the Aśvins assist humanity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity and support across different realms of existence.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions and well-being are interconnected with the universe and each other.
    • In real life, it encourages us to seek balance, support one another, and recognize the divine presence in our daily lives.
