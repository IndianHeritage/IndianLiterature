मो षु ब्रह्मेवं तन्द्रयुर भूवो वाजानां पते |मत्स्वा सुतस्य गोमता: ||
mo ṣu brahmeva tandrayur bhuvo vājānām pate | matsvā sutasya gomataḥ ||
  • mo ṣu: This phrase can be interpreted as "may I not be deprived" or "let me not be separated." It expresses a desire for connection and continuity.
  • brahmeva: This term refers to "Brahman," the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit in Hindu philosophy. It signifies the essence of the universe and the source of all creation.
  • tandrayur: This word can be understood as "long-lived" or "having longevity." It emphasizes the importance of a long and fulfilling life.
  • bhuvo: This translates to "of the earth" or "of the world," indicating a connection to the physical realm and the experiences within it.
  • vājānām: This term means "of the strong" or "of the powerful," often associated with strength, vitality, and energy.
  • pate: This word means "lord" or "master," suggesting a reverence for a higher power or authority.
  • matsvā: This can be interpreted as "my own" or "belonging to me," indicating a personal connection or ownership.
  • sutasya: This term means "of the son" or "of the offspring," highlighting the importance of lineage and family.
  • gomataḥ: This translates to "of the cow," which is a significant symbol in Hindu culture, representing nourishment, wealth, and motherhood.

In summary, this mantra expresses a deep yearning for connection with the divine essence (Brahman) while seeking longevity and strength in life. It emphasizes the importance of family and personal relationships, particularly in the context of nurturing and sustaining life, as symbolized by the cow. The mantra can be applied in real-life scenarios such as family gatherings, rituals, or personal reflections where one seeks blessings for health, strength, and a harmonious life. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing relationships, both with the divine and within one's family.
