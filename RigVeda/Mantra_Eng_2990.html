षड् भाराम् एको अचरन् बिभर्त्य ऋतं वर्षिष्ठम् उप गावा आगुः |तिस्रो महीर उपरास तस्थुर अत्य गुफा द्वे निहिते दर्श्य एका ||
ṣaḍ bhārām̐ eko acaran bibharty ṛtaṁ varṣiṣṭham upa gāva āguḥ | tisro mahīr uparās tasthur atyā guhā dve nihite darśy ekā ||
  • ṣaḍ bhārām̐: This phrase translates to "six burdens." In a real-life context, this can refer to the various responsibilities and challenges that individuals face in their daily lives, such as work, family, health, and social obligations.
  • eko acaran: Meaning "one who moves," this can symbolize the idea of unity and singularity of purpose. In life, this can be seen in individuals who focus on a single goal or mission, such as a student dedicated to their studies or an athlete training for a competition.
  • bibharty: This word means "carries" or "supports." It reflects the idea of sustaining or bearing the weight of responsibilities. For example, a parent who supports their family emotionally and financially embodies this concept.
  • ṛtaṁ varṣiṣṭham: Translated as "the best truth," this phrase emphasizes the importance of adhering to truth and righteousness. In real life, this can be seen in ethical business practices or individuals who stand up for justice and integrity.
  • upa gāva āguḥ: This means "the cows approach." Cows are often seen as symbols of nourishment and abundance in many cultures. This can relate to the idea of seeking sustenance, both physically and spiritually, as individuals strive for fulfillment in their lives.
  • tisro mahīr: This translates to "three great earths." It can represent the different dimensions of existence—physical, mental, and spiritual. In real life, this can be reflected in a balanced lifestyle that nurtures all aspects of being.
  • uparās tasthur: Meaning "the one who is above," this can signify a higher consciousness or awareness. In practical terms, this can be seen in leaders or mentors who guide others towards greater understanding and wisdom.
  • atyā guhā: This phrase means "the great cave," which can symbolize the depths of knowledge or the subconscious mind. In real life, this can relate to introspection and the exploration of one's inner self.
  • dve nihite darśy ekā: Translated as "two are hidden, one is visible," this can refer to the duality of existence—what is seen and what is unseen. In life, this can be understood as the balance between the material world and the spiritual realm.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life's journey, emphasizing the importance of carrying burdens with purpose, seeking truth, and nurturing all dimensions of existence. It serves as a reminder to maintain balance in our responsibilities while striving for higher understanding and fulfillment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to reflect on their roles, seek inner wisdom, and act with integrity in their personal and professional lives.
