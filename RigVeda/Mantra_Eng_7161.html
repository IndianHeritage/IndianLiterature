क्व स्य वृषभो युवा तुविग्रीवो अनानतः |ब्रह्मा कस्तं सपर्यति ||
kva1 sya vṛṣabho yuvā tuvigrīvo anānataḥ | brahmā kas taṁ saparyati ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • kva1: Where
    • sya: is
    • vṛṣabho: the bull (symbolizing strength and power)
    • yuvā: young
    • tuvigrīvo: with great strength
    • anānataḥ: unyielding or unbending
    • brahmā: the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit
    • kas: who
    • taṁ: him
    • saparyati: worships or honors
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra poses a question about the nature of strength and divinity.
    • It reflects on the young bull, a symbol of vigor and resilience, and questions who truly honors or worships this embodiment of power.
    • The reference to "brahmā" indicates a connection to the ultimate reality, suggesting that the strength of the bull is a manifestation of a higher cosmic order.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, a strong bull is essential for plowing fields, symbolizing the hard work and strength required for cultivation.
    • In sports, young athletes often embody the vigor and resilience of the "young bull," showcasing their strength and determination to succeed.
    • In leadership, a strong and unyielding leader can be compared to the "young bull," inspiring others through their unwavering commitment and strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring strength, both in the physical and spiritual realms.
    • It encourages individuals to reflect on who they honor and what qualities they value in themselves and others.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, personal development, and community building, where strength and resilience are essential for growth and success.
