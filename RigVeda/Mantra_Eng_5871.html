गीर्भिर् विप्रः प्रमतिम् इच्छमान ईट्टे रयिं यशसम् पूर्वभाजम् | इन्द्राग्नī वृत्रहणा सुवज्रā प्र नो नव्येभिः तिरतं देश्णैः ||
gīrbhir vipraḥ pramatim icchamāna īṭṭe rayiṁ yaśasam pūrvabhājam | indrāgnī vṛtrahaṇā suvajrā pra no navyebhis tirataṁ deṣṇaiḥ ||
  • gīrbhir vipraḥ: This phrase refers to the learned or wise individuals (vipraḥ) who are endowed with knowledge and wisdom (gīrbhir). In real life, this can be likened to scholars, teachers, or mentors who guide others through their understanding and insights.
  • pramatim icchamāna: This means desiring or seeking understanding (pramatim). It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and awareness in our lives. For example, in a corporate setting, employees who seek to understand their roles and the company's vision are more likely to contribute effectively.
  • īṭṭe rayiṁ yaśasam pūrvabhājam: Here, 'īṭṭe' signifies the act of invoking or calling upon, 'rayiṁ' refers to wealth or prosperity, and 'yaśasam pūrvabhājam' means the glory or fame that comes from past actions. This can be related to how individuals or organizations build their reputation and success based on their previous efforts and achievements. For instance, a business that has consistently delivered quality products will attract more customers due to its established reputation.
  • indrāgnī vṛtrahaṇā: This phrase invokes the deities Indra and Agni, who are associated with strength and fire, respectively. 'Vṛtrahaṇā' refers to the slayer of Vritra, a demon representing obstacles. This can be seen in real life as the need to overcome challenges and adversities, much like how athletes train to overcome physical and mental barriers to achieve their goals.
  • su vajrā: This translates to 'well-armed' or 'powerful', indicating readiness and strength. In a practical sense, this can be related to individuals or teams preparing thoroughly for challenges, such as students preparing for exams or athletes training for competitions.
  • pra no navyebhis tirataṁ deṣṇaiḥ: This line calls for guidance and support (tirataṁ) from new and fresh insights (navyebhis) to lead us forward (pra no). In everyday life, this can be interpreted as the importance of seeking new knowledge and perspectives to navigate through life's complexities, such as how businesses adapt to changing market trends by embracing innovation.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom, the pursuit of knowledge, and the importance of overcoming obstacles with strength and preparation. It highlights how past actions shape our present and future, urging individuals to seek guidance and new insights. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as education, personal development, and professional growth, reminding us that knowledge and resilience are key to success and prosperity.
