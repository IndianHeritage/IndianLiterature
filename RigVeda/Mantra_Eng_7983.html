आविवासन परावतो अथो अर्वावतः सुतः |इन्द्राय सिच्यते मधु ||
āvivāsan parāvato atho arvāvataḥ sutaḥ | indrāya sicyate madhu ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • āvivāsan: This term can be interpreted as "to bring forth" or "to manifest." It signifies the act of revealing or making something known.
    • parāvato: This word means "of the highest" or "supreme." It denotes something that is elevated or exalted.
    • atho: This translates to "then" or "therefore," indicating a transition or a consequence.
    • arvāvataḥ: This term refers to "the one who is vast" or "the expansive." It suggests a sense of boundlessness or abundance.
    • sutaḥ: This means "son" or "offspring," often used in a divine context to refer to a progeny of the gods.
    • indrāya: This is a reference to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power, strength, and leadership.
    • sicyate: This means "is poured" or "is sprinkled," indicating an act of offering or bestowing something.
    • madhu: This translates to "honey," symbolizing sweetness, nourishment, and abundance.
  • Meaning:
    • The mantra speaks of the act of bringing forth the supreme and expansive essence, which is then offered to Indra, the divine leader, in the form of sweetness and nourishment.
    • It suggests a connection between the divine and the earthly, where offerings are made to the gods to invoke their blessings and support.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, offerings to deities like Indra were common during harvest festivals, where farmers would present the first fruits of their labor as a way to express gratitude and seek blessings for future prosperity.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be seen as a metaphor for seeking guidance and support from higher powers or leaders in our lives, whether in personal endeavors or professional settings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring the divine forces that influence our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek abundance and nourishment, both spiritually and materially, by acknowledging the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to cultivate gratitude and to make offerings—be it in the form of kindness, respect, or tangible gifts—to those who guide and support them, fostering a sense of community and shared prosperity.
