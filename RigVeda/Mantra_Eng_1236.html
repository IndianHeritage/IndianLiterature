इदं श्रेष्ठं ज्योतिषां ज्योतिर्गाच्चित्रः प्रकेतो जनिष्ट विभ्वा |यथा प्रसूता सवितुः सवायं एव रात्री उषसे योनिम आरैक ||
idaṁ śreṣṭhaṁ jyotiṣāṁ jyotir āgāc citraḥ praketo ajaniṣṭa vibhvā | yathā prasūtā savituḥ savāyam̐ evā rātry uṣase yonim āraik ||
  • idaṁ: This word translates to "this" or "here." It signifies the present moment or the immediate experience.
  • śreṣṭhaṁ: Meaning "the best" or "the most excellent," it emphasizes the quality of what is being referred to.
  • jyotiṣāṁ: This refers to "of lights" or "of luminaries," indicating a connection to illumination, knowledge, or enlightenment.
  • jyotir: Translated as "light," it symbolizes clarity, wisdom, and the dispelling of darkness.
  • āgāc: This means "has come" or "arrived," suggesting the presence of something significant.
  • citraḥ: Meaning "variegated" or "multicolored," it represents diversity and richness in experience.
  • praketo: This translates to "of the creator" or "of the one who manifests," indicating a divine or creative force.
  • ajaniṣṭa: Meaning "has not been born" or "has not come into being," it suggests a state of potentiality or the unmanifested.
  • vibhvā: This word means "the one who is vast" or "the expansive," indicating a sense of infinity or boundlessness.
  • yathā: Translated as "as" or "like," it introduces a comparison or analogy.
  • prasūtā: Meaning "born" or "produced," it refers to the act of creation or manifestation.
  • savituḥ: This refers to "of the sun," symbolizing life, energy, and vitality.
  • savāyam̐: Meaning "with all," it indicates inclusiveness and unity.
  • evā: This translates to "indeed" or "certainly," reinforcing the statement that follows.
  • rātry: Meaning "night," it symbolizes darkness, rest, and the unknown.
  • uṣase: This refers to "dawn" or "daybreak," representing new beginnings, hope, and enlightenment.
  • yonim: Meaning "womb" or "source," it signifies the origin of creation and life.
  • āraik: This translates to "may we attain" or "may we reach," indicating a desire for realization or achievement.

The mantra speaks to the interplay between light and darkness, creation and potentiality. It emphasizes the arrival of the best light, which is diverse and expansive, and connects it to the cycles of night and dawn. The imagery of the sun and the womb suggests a continuous process of creation and rebirth.


In real life, this mantra can be applied to various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Just as dawn follows night, individuals can experience personal transformations, moving from ignorance to knowledge, from despair to hope.
  • Creativity: The diverse and expansive nature of light can inspire artists, writers, and creators to explore new ideas and perspectives.
  • Community and Unity: The mantra's emphasis on inclusiveness can encourage collaboration and harmony within communities, fostering a sense of belonging and shared purpose.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra serves as a reminder of the cyclical nature of life, the importance of enlightenment, and the potential for growth and unity. It encourages individuals to embrace change, seek knowledge, and recognize the interconnectedness of all beings.
