एतावतश्चिदेषां सुम्नं भिक्षेत मार्त्यः |अदाभ्यस्य मन्मभिः ||
etāvataś cid eṣāṁ sumnam bhikṣeta martyaḥ | adābhyasya manmabhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • etāvataś - to this extent
    • cid - indeed
    • eṣāṁ - of these
    • sumnam - auspiciousness or good fortune
    • bhikṣeta - may one seek or obtain
    • martyaḥ - the mortal or human being
    • adābhyasya - of the one who is not obstructed
    • manmabhiḥ - by my thoughts or intentions
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the extent to which a human being can seek auspiciousness or good fortune.
    • It emphasizes the importance of one's thoughts and intentions in achieving this auspiciousness.
    • The phrase "to this extent" suggests that there are limits or boundaries to what can be achieved, which can be interpreted as a reminder of the human condition.
    • The term "martyaḥ" refers to the mortal nature of human beings, highlighting the transient nature of life and the importance of making the most of our time.
    • The idea of seeking auspiciousness is universal; it can be related to various aspects of life, such as health, relationships, and success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual may seek auspiciousness by setting clear intentions and goals, thereby aligning their actions with their aspirations.
    • In personal relationships, one can cultivate positive thoughts and intentions towards others, which can lead to more harmonious interactions and deeper connections.
    • In health and wellness, focusing on positive outcomes and maintaining a healthy mindset can significantly impact one's physical and mental well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of intention and thought in shaping our experiences and outcomes in life.
    • It encourages individuals to be mindful of their desires and to seek auspiciousness through positive thinking and actions.
    • In a broader context, it highlights the interconnectedness of human beings and the importance of striving for goodness in our lives and the lives of others.
    • Ultimately, the mantra teaches that while we may be limited by our mortal nature, our thoughts and intentions can transcend these limitations and lead us towards a more fulfilling existence.
