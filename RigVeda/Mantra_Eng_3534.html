मन्त्र: इहेह यद वां समना पपृक्षे सेयम अस्मे सुमतिर वाजरत्ना | उरुष्यतं जरितारं युवं ह श्रितः कामो नासत्याऽ युवद्रिक् ||
iheha yad vāṁ samanā papṛkṣe seyam asme sumatir vājaratnā | uruṣyataṁ jaritāraṁ yuvaṁ ha śritaḥ kāmo nāsatyā yuvadrik ||
  • iheha: This word translates to "here" or "in this world." It emphasizes the importance of our current existence and the actions we take in the present.
  • yad: Meaning "that" or "which," it refers to the subject of the mantra, indicating a connection to a specific idea or concept.
  • vāṁ: This means "your" or "of you," suggesting a personal address or connection to the listener or the divine.
  • samanā: Translated as "together" or "united," it highlights the importance of harmony and collaboration.
  • papṛkṣe: This means "I ask" or "I inquire," indicating a quest for knowledge or understanding.
  • seyam: Meaning "this" or "that," it refers to something specific that is being discussed or sought after.
  • asme: This translates to "for us," indicating a collective aspect, emphasizing community and shared experiences.
  • sumatir: Meaning "good thought" or "wisdom," it refers to the pursuit of knowledge and understanding.
  • vājaratnā: This translates to "like a diamond" or "precious," symbolizing the value of wisdom and good thoughts.
  • uruṣyataṁ: Meaning "may it be vast" or "may it expand," it suggests a desire for growth and expansion of knowledge.
  • jaritāraṁ: This means "of the wise" or "of the learned," referring to those who possess knowledge and wisdom.
  • yuvaṁ: Translated as "young" or "youthful," it signifies freshness, energy, and potential.
  • ha: This is an affirmation, often used to emphasize a statement.
  • śritaḥ: Meaning "dependent on" or "relying on," it indicates a connection or reliance on something greater.
  • kāmo: This translates to "desire" or "wish," referring to the aspirations and goals we hold.
  • nāsatyā: This means "not false" or "truthful," emphasizing the importance of honesty and integrity.
  • yuvadrik: Meaning "young seer" or "youthful vision," it suggests a fresh perspective and insight.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking wisdom and understanding in our lives. It encourages unity, collaboration, and the pursuit of good thoughts, which are as precious as diamonds. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as education, teamwork, and personal development. For instance, in a workplace setting, fostering a culture of collaboration and open communication can lead to innovative solutions and a more harmonious environment. Similarly, in personal relationships, valuing honesty and shared aspirations can strengthen bonds and create a supportive community. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek knowledge, embrace unity, and pursue our desires with integrity and wisdom.
