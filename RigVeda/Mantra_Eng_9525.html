यदा वलस्य पीयतो जसुम भेद बृहस्पतिर अग्नितपोभिर अर्कैः |दद्भिर न जिव्हा परिविष्टम आदद आविर निधींर अकृणोद उष्रियाणाम् ||
yadā valasya pīyato jasum bhed bṛhaspatir agnitapobhir arkaiḥ | dadbhir na jihvā pariviṣṭam ādad āvir nidhīm̐r akṛṇod usriyāṇām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yadā: When
    • valasya: of the strong
    • pīyato: who is drinking
    • jasum: the essence or strength
    • bhed: separation or division
    • bṛhaspatir: the great teacher or guru (Bṛhaspati)
    • agnitapobhir: with the power of fire and energy
    • arkaiḥ: with the rays or light
    • dadbhir: with those who give
    • na: not
    • jihvā: the tongue
    • pariviṣṭam: surrounded or enveloped
    • ādad: to take or receive
    • āvir: manifest or appear
    • nidhīm̐r: the treasure or wealth
    • akṛṇod: did not create or establish
    • usriyāṇām: of the wise or learned
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the process of gaining wisdom and knowledge.
    • It highlights the importance of the guidance of a teacher (Bṛhaspati) in understanding the essence of strength and energy.
    • The metaphor of drinking the essence signifies the absorption of knowledge and wisdom.
    • The mention of the tongue suggests the importance of speech and communication in sharing knowledge.
    • It implies that without the guidance of a wise teacher, one may not be able to access the true treasures of knowledge.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In educational settings, students often rely on teachers to guide them through complex subjects, much like how Bṛhaspati guides seekers of knowledge.
    • In professional environments, mentors play a crucial role in helping individuals navigate their careers, providing insights that help them grow and succeed.
    • In personal development, individuals often seek guidance from life coaches or counselors to help them uncover their potential and achieve their goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking knowledge and wisdom through the guidance of a teacher or mentor.
    • It teaches us that true understanding comes from the right sources and that communication is key in sharing and receiving knowledge.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as education, mentorship, and personal growth, reminding us of the value of wisdom and the importance of learning from those who are knowledgeable.
