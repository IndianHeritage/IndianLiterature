मन्त्र: उता स्यां नः सरस्वती जुषाणोप श्रवत सुभगा यज्ञे अस्मिन् |मितज्ञुभिर नमस्यैः इयानां राया युजा चिद उत्तरा सखिभ्यः ||
uta syā naḥ sarasvatī juṣāṇopa śravat subhagā yajṇe asmin | mitajñubhir namasyair iyānā rāyā yujā cid uttarā sakhibhyaḥ ||
  • uta syā naḥ sarasvatī: This phrase can be translated as "May Sarasvati, the goddess of knowledge and wisdom, be with us." Sarasvati is often associated with learning, music, and art. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of education and the arts, where individuals seek her blessings for success and creativity.
  • juṣāṇopa śravat subhagā: This means "may she who is auspicious and brings forth abundance flow towards us." The idea of abundance can be related to prosperity in various aspects of life, such as financial stability, emotional well-being, and fulfilling relationships. For example, a person who practices gratitude and mindfulness may experience a flow of positive experiences in their life.
  • yajṇe asmin: This translates to "in this sacrifice." In ancient times, yajnas (sacrifices) were performed to please the deities and seek their blessings. In modern life, this can be interpreted as the sacrifices we make for our goals, such as dedicating time and effort to our careers or personal development.
  • mitajñubhir namasyair: This phrase means "with those who are wise and respectful." It emphasizes the importance of surrounding ourselves with knowledgeable and respectful individuals. In real life, this can be seen in the value of mentorship and collaboration, where learning from others enhances our own wisdom.
  • iyānā rāyā yujā: This translates to "may we be united with wealth." This can refer to both material wealth and the wealth of knowledge and relationships. In practical terms, this can be applied to networking and building connections that lead to opportunities for growth and success.
  • cid uttarā sakhibhyaḥ: This means "may we attain the higher knowledge and friendship." It highlights the significance of seeking higher truths and forming meaningful connections with others. In everyday life, this can be reflected in the pursuit of personal growth through education, self-reflection, and building supportive friendships.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge, wisdom, and abundance in our lives. It encourages us to surround ourselves with wise individuals, make sacrifices for our goals, and strive for higher understanding and meaningful relationships. In a real-life scenario, this mantra can serve as a reminder to pursue education, practice gratitude, and foster connections that enrich our lives, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and prosperous existence.
