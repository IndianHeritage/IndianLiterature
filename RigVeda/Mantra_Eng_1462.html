अतिथिग्वाय शम्बरं गिरेर उग्रो अवाभरत् |महो धनानि दयमान ओजसा विश्वा धनानि ओजसा ||
atithigvāya śambaraṁ girer ugro avābharat | maho dhanāni dayamāna ojasā viśvā dhanāny ojasā ||
  • atithigvāya: This term refers to the guest or the one who arrives. In many cultures, including Indian traditions, the guest is considered akin to God. The importance of hospitality is emphasized here.
  • śambaraṁ: This word can be interpreted as a reference to wealth or abundance. It signifies the richness that one can attain through proper conduct and hospitality.
  • girer: This translates to "of the mountain" or "of the hills." Mountains often symbolize stability, strength, and permanence in various cultures.
  • ugro: This means "mighty" or "powerful." It denotes strength and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • avābharat: This can be understood as "to bring forth" or "to carry." It suggests the act of bringing something into existence or making it available.
  • maho dhanāni: This phrase translates to "great riches" or "abundant wealth." It emphasizes the idea of prosperity and the blessings that come from good deeds.
  • dayamāna: This means "being generous" or "showing kindness." It highlights the importance of compassion and generosity in human interactions.
  • ojasā: This word signifies "energy" or "vitality." It represents the life force that drives individuals to achieve their goals.
  • viśvā dhanāny: This translates to "all wealth" or "universal riches." It suggests that true wealth encompasses more than just material possessions; it includes spiritual and emotional richness as well.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of hospitality, generosity, and the pursuit of true wealth. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways, such as:

  • Practicing hospitality by welcoming guests into our homes and treating them with respect and kindness.
  • Engaging in acts of generosity, whether through charitable donations or helping those in need, thereby creating a sense of community and support.
  • Recognizing that true wealth is not solely about material possessions but also includes relationships, experiences, and the well-being of others.

This mantra serves as a reminder that by embodying these values, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous society, where the richness of life is shared and celebrated by all.
