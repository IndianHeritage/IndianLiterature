आ स्वं आत्म युवमानो अजरस तृष्व अविष्यन्न अतसेषु तिष्ठति |अत्यो न पृष्ठं पुरुषितस्य रोचते दिवो न सानु स्तनयन्न अचिक्रदत ||
ā svam adma yuvamāno ajaras tṛṣv aviṣyann ataseṣu tiṣṭhati | atyo na pṛṣṭham pruṣitasya rocate divo na sānu stanayann acikradat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • svam: one's own
    • adma: self
    • yuvamāno: being youthful
    • ajaras: ageless
    • tṛṣv: thirst
    • aviṣyann: not being disturbed
    • ataseṣu: in the midst of
    • tiṣṭhati: stands
    • aty: beyond
    • na: not
    • pṛṣṭham: the back
    • pruṣitasya: of the one who is disturbed
    • rocate: shines
    • dīvo: of the sky
    • na: not
    • sānu: with a breast
    • stanayann: nurturing
    • acikradat: has cried out
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the eternal nature of the self, emphasizing the idea of being youthful and ageless.
    • The phrase "not being disturbed" suggests a state of inner peace and stability, even amidst challenges.
    • The reference to the sky and nurturing indicates a connection to the divine and the nurturing aspects of nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, individuals often face stress and challenges that can disturb their peace. This mantra encourages maintaining a calm and stable mind, akin to practices like meditation or mindfulness.
    • The concept of being ageless can be related to the idea of maintaining a youthful spirit and outlook on life, regardless of physical age. For instance, many people engage in lifelong learning or physical activities to keep their minds and bodies active.
    • The nurturing aspect can be seen in the way communities support each other, much like how nature provides for all living beings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of inner peace, the eternal nature of the self, and the nurturing qualities of the universe.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios where individuals seek to cultivate resilience, maintain a youthful spirit, and foster community support.
    • By embodying these principles, one can navigate life's challenges with grace and strength, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence.
