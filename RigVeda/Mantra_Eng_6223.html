आ नो यातम उपश्रुत्य अश्विनā सोमपीतये |स्वाहा स्तोमस्य वर्धना प्र कवि धीतिभिर नराः ||
ā no yātam upaśruty aśvinā somapītaye | svāhā stomasya vardhanā pra kavī dhītibhir narā ||
  • Mantra: "ā no yātam upaśruty aśvinā somapītaye | svāhā stomasya vardhanā pra kavī dhītibhir narā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā no yātam: "May we be led" or "May we go forth."
    • upaśruty: "With attention" or "With listening."
    • aśvinā: "By the Ashvins," who are the twin deities associated with health and medicine.
    • somapītaye: "For the drinking of Soma," referring to the sacred drink that grants immortality and divine inspiration.
    • svāhā: A term often used in Vedic rituals, signifying an offering or a salutation.
    • stomasya: "Of the hymn" or "Of the praise."
    • vardhanā: "Increase" or "Growth."
    • pra: "For" or "Towards."
    • kavī: "The poet" or "The seer."
    • dhītibhir: "With thoughts" or "With wisdom."
    • narā: "Men" or "People."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for guidance and inspiration from the divine, specifically invoking the Ashvins, who are known for their healing and benevolent qualities.
    • The phrase "ā no yātam" emphasizes the desire for collective progress and unity, suggesting that we should move forward together with purpose.
    • By invoking the Ashvins, the mantra seeks their blessings for health and well-being, which can be seen in real-life scenarios such as seeking medical help or support during difficult times.
    • The reference to "somapītaye" highlights the importance of spiritual nourishment and the quest for higher knowledge, akin to how individuals seek wisdom through education and personal growth.
    • The call for "vardhanā" signifies a wish for growth and prosperity, which can be applied in various aspects of life, including personal development, community growth, and even economic prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and collective progress. It serves as a reminder of the importance of unity, health, and wisdom in our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in community gatherings, health initiatives, and educational pursuits, where the focus is on collective well-being and growth.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to seek inspiration and support from the divine while fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
