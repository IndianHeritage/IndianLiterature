ऋतेन ऋतम अपिहितं ध्रुवं वां सूर्यस्य यत्र विमुचन्त्य अश्वान् |दश शतां सह तस्थुस तद एकं देवानां श्रेष्टं वपुषां अपश्यम् ||
ṛtena ṛtam apihitaṁ dhruvaṁ vāṁ sūryasya yatra vimucanty aśvān | daśa śatā saha tasthus tad ekaṁ devānāṁ śreṣṭhaṁ vapuṣām apaśyam ||
  • ṛtena: This word refers to the concept of "truth" or "cosmic order." In real life, it can be likened to the principles of honesty and integrity that govern our actions and relationships.
  • ṛtam: Similar to "ṛtena," it signifies the underlying order of the universe. For example, the laws of nature that dictate the cycles of seasons and life can be seen as manifestations of ṛtam.
  • apihitaṁ: This means "hidden" or "concealed." In our lives, it can represent the deeper truths or insights that are not immediately visible but can be discovered through introspection and wisdom.
  • dhruvaṁ: This translates to "constant" or "unchanging." An example in real life is the unwavering support of a true friend or the steadfastness of one's principles in the face of adversity.
  • vāṁ: This word means "your" or "of you." It emphasizes the personal connection and responsibility we have towards our own actions and their consequences.
  • sūryasya: Referring to the "sun," it symbolizes light, knowledge, and enlightenment. In practical terms, it can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and the illumination that comes from education and experience.
  • yatra: This means "where." It indicates a location or state of being, which can be interpreted as the journey we undertake in life.
  • vimucanty: This translates to "liberate" or "release." In a real-life context, it can refer to the process of freeing oneself from ignorance or negative influences.
  • aśvān: This word means "horses," which in Vedic literature often symbolizes strength and energy. In modern terms, it can represent the vigor and enthusiasm we bring to our endeavors.
  • daśa: Meaning "ten," it can symbolize completeness or a full cycle, akin to the ten fingers that allow us to grasp and interact with the world.
  • śatā: This means "hundred," often representing abundance or a multitude of possibilities in life.
  • saha: This translates to "together" or "with." It emphasizes the importance of community and collaboration in achieving goals.
  • tasthus: This word means "standing" or "existing." It can be related to the idea of being grounded and present in the moment.
  • tad: Meaning "that," it refers to a specific entity or concept, often pointing to the divine or higher truth.
  • ekaṁ: This translates to "one," symbolizing unity and the interconnectedness of all things.
  • devānāṁ: This means "of the gods," indicating a divine aspect or higher power that influences our lives.
  • śreṣṭhaṁ: This translates to "the best" or "the highest." It signifies the pursuit of excellence and the highest ideals in our actions.
  • vapuṣām: This means "of forms" or "of bodies," referring to the physical manifestation of existence.
  • apaśyam: This translates to "I have seen" or "I have perceived," indicating the importance of awareness and understanding in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking truth and understanding the cosmic order that governs our lives. It emphasizes the importance of integrity, knowledge, and unity in our actions. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with honesty and strength, while also recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings. By embodying these principles, one can achieve a harmonious existence and contribute positively to the world around them.
