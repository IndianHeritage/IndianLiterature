ककुहं चित् त्वा कवे मन्दन्तु धृष्णव इन्दवः |आ त्वा पणिं यद ईमहे ||
kakuhaṁ cit tvā kave mandantu dhṛṣṇav indavaḥ | ā tvā paṇiṁ yad īmahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kakuhaṁ: "What is the secret?" or "What is hidden?"
    • cit: "Indeed" or "certainly."
    • tvā: "You" (referring to a divine entity or inspiration).
    • kave: "O poet" or "O sage."
    • mandantu: "May they be favorable" or "may they bless."
    • dhṛṣṇav: "With strength" or "with courage."
    • indavaḥ: "The moon" or "the divine light."
    • ā: "Come" or "approach."
    • tvā: "You" (repeated for emphasis).
    • paṇiṁ: "We invoke" or "we call upon."
    • yad: "That" or "which."
    • īmahe: "We meditate" or "we contemplate."
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra expresses a call to the divine for guidance and inspiration.
    • It seeks to uncover hidden truths and gain strength through divine blessings.
    • The invocation of the moon symbolizes enlightenment and the illumination of the mind.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In moments of uncertainty, individuals often seek guidance through meditation or prayer, similar to invoking the divine in this mantra.
    • Artists and writers may find inspiration in nature or the cosmos, akin to calling upon the "moon" for creative strength.
    • In challenging situations, people may rely on their inner strength and seek blessings from a higher power to overcome obstacles.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and strength in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various scenarios, such as during personal challenges, creative endeavors, or moments of introspection.
    • By invoking the divine, we open ourselves to greater understanding and the courage to face life's uncertainties.
