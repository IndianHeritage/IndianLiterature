समिद्धो अद्य राजसी देवो देवैः सहस्रजित् |दूतः हव्याः कविः वह ||
samiddho adya rājasi devo devaiḥ sahasrajit | dūto havyā kavir vaha ||
  • Mantra: "samiddho adya rājasi devo devaiḥ sahasrajit | dūto havyā kavir vaha ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samiddho: kindled, ignited
    • adya: today, now
    • rājasi: in the royal, regal context
    • devo: the god, divine being
    • devaiḥ: with the gods, divine beings
    • sahasrajit: one who has conquered thousands, a powerful entity
    • dūto: messenger, envoy
    • havyā: offerings, that which is to be offered
    • kavir: the wise one, poet, seer
    • vaha: carry, bring forth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence that is currently active and powerful.
    • The term "samiddho" suggests that the divine energy is ignited and ready to manifest.
    • "Adya" emphasizes the immediacy of this divine action, indicating that it is relevant in the present moment.
    • The phrase "rājasi devo devaiḥ sahasrajit" highlights the idea of a god who is not only powerful but also supported by other divine beings.
    • The mention of a "dūto" signifies the role of a messenger, suggesting that communication between the divine and the earthly realm is taking place.
    • "Havyā" refers to offerings, indicating that there is a need for devotion and the act of giving to the divine.
    • The term "kavir" points to the wisdom and insight that can be gained through this divine connection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be related to moments of inspiration or creativity, where one feels a surge of energy to create or innovate.
    • For instance, an artist may feel "samiddho" when they are inspired to paint, as if a divine force is guiding their hand.
    • In leadership, a "rājasi" figure may embody the qualities of wisdom and strength, rallying others to achieve a common goal, much like a CEO inspiring their team.
    • During rituals or ceremonies, the act of making offerings ("havyā") can be seen as a way to connect with the divine, seeking blessings and guidance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing and harnessing divine energy in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to be aware of the present moment and to seek inspiration and guidance from higher powers.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in various aspects of life, such as creativity, leadership, and spirituality, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of offering our best selves to the world.
