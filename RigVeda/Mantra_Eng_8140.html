मन्त्र: एते सोमाः असृक्षत गृणानाः श्रवसे महे |मदन्तमस्य धारयā ||
ete somā asṛkṣata gṛṇānāḥ śravase mahe | madintamasya dhārayā ||
  • Translation: "These (soma) have poured forth, singing, for the great glory; they hold the essence of the dark (mad) and the light (tamas)."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ete: These
    • somā: Soma, often associated with a sacred drink or plant, symbolizes vitality and divine inspiration.
    • asṛkṣata: Have poured forth or flowed, indicating abundance and generosity.
    • gṛṇānāḥ: Singing or praising, suggesting a celebration or acknowledgment of the divine.
    • śravase: For the great glory, referring to the magnificence of the divine or the universe.
    • mahe: Great, emphasizing the vastness of the glory being referred to.
    • madintamasya: Of the dark (mad) and the light (tamas), representing duality in existence.
    • dhārayā: They hold or sustain, indicating a nurturing aspect.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the duality of existence, where both light and darkness coexist. In real life, this can be seen in the balance of joy and sorrow, success and failure.
    • The reference to "soma" can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, much like how people seek inspiration through art, music, or nature.
    • The act of singing or praising can be compared to how communities come together to celebrate achievements or milestones, reinforcing social bonds and shared values.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and embracing the dualities of life. It teaches us that both light and darkness are essential for growth and understanding.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various aspects of life, such as personal development, where one learns from failures (darkness) to achieve success (light).
    • It encourages a holistic view of existence, reminding us to appreciate the beauty in both the highs and lows of our experiences.
