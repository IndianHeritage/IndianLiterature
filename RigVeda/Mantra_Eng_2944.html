इमा इन्द्र भारतस्य पुत्रा अपापित्वं चिकीतुर्न प्रापित्वम् |हिन्वन्त्य अश्वम् अरणं न नित्यं ज्यावाजम् परि णयन्त्य आजौ ||
ima indra bharatasya putrā apapitvaṁ cikitur na prapitvam | hinvanty aśvam araṇaṁ na nityaṁ jyāvājam pari ṇayanty ājau ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ima: these
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • bharatasya: of the Bharata tribe
    • putrā: sons
    • apapitvaṁ: not falling short
    • cikitur: have achieved
    • na: not
    • prapitvam: the ultimate goal
    • hinvanty: they drive away
    • aśvam: the horse
    • araṇaṁ: the enemy
    • na: not
    • nityaṁ: always
    • jyāvājam: the strong one
    • pari: around
    • ṇayanty: they lead
    • ājau: to victory
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the sons of the Bharata tribe who, under the guidance of Indra, have achieved great feats.
    • It emphasizes the idea of not falling short of one's goals and the importance of striving for success.
    • The reference to driving away the enemy and leading to victory signifies the importance of courage and strength in overcoming challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive environment, such as a corporate setting, individuals or teams often strive to achieve their targets and not fall short of expectations, much like the sons of Bharata.
    • In sports, athletes train rigorously to overcome their opponents, embodying the spirit of driving away the enemy and achieving victory.
    • In personal development, individuals set goals for themselves and work diligently to achieve them, reflecting the mantra's message of perseverance and strength.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of determination, strength, and the pursuit of excellence.
    • It serves as a reminder that with the right mindset and effort, one can overcome obstacles and achieve their goals.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, from personal ambitions to collective efforts in communities, encouraging individuals to strive for success and lead with courage.
