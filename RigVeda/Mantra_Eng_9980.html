न्य अकृन्दयन्न उपयन्त एनम् अमेहायन वृषभं मध्य आजेः |तेन सुभर्वं शतवत सहस्रं गवाम् मुद्गलः प्रधानें जिगाय ||
ny akrandayann upayanta enam amehayan vṛṣabham madhya ājeḥ | tena sūbharvaṁ śatavat sahasraṁ gavām mudgalaḥ pradhane jigāya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ny akrandayann: Not crying out or lamenting
    • upayanta: Approaching or coming near
    • enam: This
    • amehayan: Not being disturbed or agitated
    • vṛṣabham: The bull or the strong one
    • madhya: In the middle or center
    • ājeḥ: Of the sacrifice or offering
    • tena: By that
    • sūbharvaṁ: The good or auspicious
    • śatavat: Hundredfold
    • sahasraṁ: Thousandfold
    • gavām: Of the cows
    • mudgalaḥ: The one who is strong or powerful
    • pradhane: In the principal or chief
    • jigāya: Achieved or attained
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a state of calmness and strength, where one does not lament or cry out in distress.
    • The reference to the "bull" symbolizes strength and stability, suggesting that one should remain centered and strong amidst challenges.
    • The phrase "by that" indicates that through maintaining this calmness and strength, one can achieve great abundance and prosperity, represented by "hundredfold" and "thousandfold" blessings.
    • The mention of "cows" signifies wealth and nourishment, as cows are traditionally seen as symbols of prosperity in many cultures.
    • The term "mudgalaḥ" emphasizes the idea of being powerful and capable of overcoming obstacles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In stressful situations, such as during exams or job interviews, maintaining a calm demeanor can lead to better performance and outcomes.
    • In business, a leader who remains composed during crises can inspire confidence in their team, leading to successful navigation through challenges.
    • Practices like meditation and mindfulness can help individuals cultivate the strength and calmness referred to in this mantra, allowing them to face life's difficulties with resilience.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of inner strength and calmness in achieving success and abundance in life.
    • It teaches that by not succumbing to distress and maintaining a centered approach, one can attract prosperity and overcome challenges.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and professional success.
