उतो स मह्यम् इन्दुभिः षड् युक्ताम् अनुसेषिधत् |गोभिः यवं न चर्कृषत् ||
uto sa mahyam indubhiḥ ṣaḍ yuktām̐ anuseṣidhat | gobhir yavaṁ na carkṛṣat ||
  • Mantra: "uto sa mahyam indubhiḥ ṣaḍ yuktām̐ anuseṣidhat | gobhir yavaṁ na carkṛṣat ||"
  • Translation: "May that (divine) being, who is endowed with six (qualities), bestow upon me (the blessings) of the moon; may he not be obstructed by the cows and the grains."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uto: May (it) come
    • sa: that (divine being)
    • mahyam: to me
    • indubhiḥ: with the blessings of the moon
    • ṣaḍ: six
    • yuktām̐: endowed with qualities
    • anuseṣidhat: bestow or grant
    • gobhir: by the cows
    • yavaṁ: grains or barley
    • na: not
    • carkṛṣat: obstructed or hindered
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes a divine being, asking for blessings associated with the moon, which symbolizes nourishment, fertility, and growth.
    • The "six qualities" refer to attributes such as wisdom, strength, compassion, and others that are essential for a fulfilling life.
    • The mention of "cows" and "grains" signifies the importance of agriculture and livestock in sustaining life, representing abundance and prosperity.
    • In real life, this mantra can be seen as a prayer for a balanced life where one seeks divine guidance while also acknowledging the importance of natural resources.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking divine blessings while being mindful of the resources that sustain us.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as during agricultural rituals, personal prayers for prosperity, or even in moments of reflection on the interconnectedness of life.
    • By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of gratitude and responsibility towards nature and the divine, fostering a harmonious existence.
