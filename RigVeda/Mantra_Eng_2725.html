सम्पश्यमानाम अमदन्न अभि स्वं पयः प्रत्नस्य रेतसो दुधानः |विरोदसी अतपद घोष एषां जाते निष्ठाम अदधुर गोषु वीरान् ||
sampaśyamānā amadann abhi svam payaḥ pratnasya retaso dughānāḥ | vi rodasī atapad ghoṣa eṣāṁ jāte niṣṭhām adadhur goṣu vīrān ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sampaśyamānā: Observing or witnessing
    • amadann: They have consumed or taken
    • abhi: Towards or for
    • svam: Their own
    • payaḥ: Milk or nourishment
    • pratnasya: Of the essence or the root
    • retaso: Of the vital energy or life force
    • dughānāḥ: Those who produce or give milk
    • vi: In the midst of
    • rodasī: The two worlds or realms
    • atapad: Have established or set
    • ghoṣa: The sound or call
    • eṣāṁ: Of these
    • jāte: Born or created
    • niṣṭhām: Stability or foundation
    • adadhur: They have placed or established
    • goṣu: In the cows or among the cattle
    • vīrān: The heroes or strong ones
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the act of witnessing and consuming the nourishing essence of life, symbolized by milk.
    • The reference to "dughānāḥ" highlights the importance of those who provide sustenance, akin to caregivers or nurturers in our lives.
    • The phrase "vi rodasī" suggests a connection between the earthly and the celestial, indicating that nourishment comes from both realms.
    • The establishment of "niṣṭhām" among the "goṣu" signifies the importance of a stable foundation in nurturing and supporting life, much like how communities support each other.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family, parents act as "dughānāḥ," providing emotional and physical nourishment to their children, ensuring their growth and stability.
    • In a community, local farmers can be seen as the "dughānāḥ," supplying food and resources that sustain the community, reflecting the interconnectedness of life.
    • In a workplace, mentors and leaders can be viewed as "dughānāḥ," guiding and nurturing the potential of their team members, fostering a supportive environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of nourishment—both physical and emotional—and the roles individuals play in providing that sustenance.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of life, where the act of nurturing is essential for stability and growth in various aspects of life.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire us to recognize and appreciate the caregivers, mentors, and community members who contribute to our well-being and success.
