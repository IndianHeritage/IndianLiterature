प्रति प्रियतमं रथं वृषणं वसुवाहनम् |स्तोता वाम अश्विनाव ऋषिः स्तोमेना प्रति भूषति माध्वी मम श्रुतं हवम् ||
prati priyatamaṁ rathaṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ vasuvāhanam | stotā vām aśvināv ṛṣiḥ stomena prati bhūṣati mādhvī mama śrutaṁ havam ||
  • Mantra: "prati priyatamaṁ rathaṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ vasuvāhanam | stotā vām aśvināv ṛṣiḥ stomena prati bhūṣati mādhvī mama śrutaṁ havam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prati: towards
    • priyatamaṁ: the most beloved
    • rathaṁ: chariot
    • vṛṣaṇaṁ: strong or powerful
    • vasuvāhanam: one who is carried by the wealth or prosperity
    • stotā: the one who praises
    • vām: you (plural)
    • aśvināv: O Ashvins (the twin deities)
    • ṛṣiḥ: sage or seer
    • stomena: through the hymn
    • prati: towards
    • bhūṣati: adorns or embellishes
    • mādhvī: sweet or delightful
    • mama: my
    • śrutaṁ: heard or known
    • havam: offering or invocation
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the Ashvins, who are considered the divine physicians and bringers of health and prosperity.
    • The phrase "prati priyatamaṁ rathaṁ" suggests a journey towards the most beloved or cherished goals in life, akin to pursuing one's dreams or aspirations.
    • "vṛṣaṇaṁ vasuvāhanam" symbolizes strength and the support of wealth, indicating that one should seek strength in their endeavors while being supported by prosperity.
    • The invocation of the Ashvins as "stotā vām" emphasizes the importance of praise and gratitude in our lives, recognizing the divine forces that aid us.
    • The mantra concludes with a call for the sweet offerings of one's heart and intentions to be heard, highlighting the significance of sincere intentions in our prayers and actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied when pursuing a career goal. One may visualize their "ratha" (chariot) as their career path, seeking strength and support from mentors and colleagues (the "vasuvāhanam").
    • In personal relationships, the mantra encourages individuals to cherish their loved ones and express gratitude, akin to the "mādhvī" offerings of love and appreciation.
    • During challenging times, invoking the Ashvins can serve as a reminder to seek help and healing, whether through medical assistance or emotional support from friends and family.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of pursuing one's goals with strength and support while maintaining a sense of gratitude and appreciation for the divine and those around us.
    • It teaches us that our intentions and offerings, when sincere, can lead to blessings and prosperity in our lives.
    • In essence, it serves as a reminder to align our actions with our highest aspirations, supported by the strength of community and divine grace.
