धियं वो अप्सु दधिषे स्वर्षां ययातरण दश मासो नवग्वाः |अया धियā स्याम देवगोपाऽ अया धियā तुतुर्व्यामात्य अंहः ||
dhiyaṁ vo apsu dadhiṣe svarṣāṁ yayātaran daśa māso navagvāḥ | ayā dhiyā syāma devagopā ayā dhiyā tuturyāmāty aṁhaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "dhiyaṁ vo apsu dadhiṣe svarṣāṁ yayātaran daśa māso navagvāḥ | ayā dhiyā syāma devagopā ayā dhiyā tuturyāmāty aṁhaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "May we obtain your thoughts, O waters, which are rich in nourishment, and which flow for ten months like the nine streams. With that thought, may we become the protectors of the divine, and with that thought, may we attain strength and prosperity."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • dhiyaṁ: thoughts or intellect
    • apsu: in the waters
    • dadhiṣe: may we obtain
    • svarṣāṁ: rich in nourishment
    • yayātaran: flowing
    • daśa: ten
    • māso: months
    • navagvāḥ: nine streams
    • ayā: with that
    • syāma: may we become
    • devagopā: protectors of the divine
    • tuturyāmāty: attain strength
    • aṁhaḥ: prosperity
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of thoughts and intellect, symbolized by the waters, which are essential for nourishment and growth.
    • The reference to "ten months" and "nine streams" can be seen as a metaphor for the continuous flow of knowledge and wisdom that nourishes the mind and spirit.
    • In real life, this can be related to the importance of education and learning, where knowledge acts as a source of sustenance for personal and professional growth.
    • The idea of becoming "protectors of the divine" suggests a responsibility to uphold values and ethics in our actions, akin to being guardians of our community and environment.
    • Attaining strength and prosperity through wisdom can be observed in various fields, such as business leaders who prioritize ethical practices and community welfare, leading to sustainable success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of nurturing our intellect and thoughts, as they are foundational to our growth and success.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom, not just for personal gain but also for the betterment of society.
    • In a world where ethical dilemmas are prevalent, this mantra inspires us to act as protectors of the divine, ensuring that our actions contribute positively to the world around us.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the interconnectedness of knowledge, responsibility, and prosperity, urging us to cultivate a mindset that values these principles in our daily lives.
