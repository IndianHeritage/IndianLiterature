तवे इन्द्राप्य अभूम विप्राः धियं वनेम ऋतया सपन्तः |अवस्यवो धीमहि प्रशस्तिं सद्यस ते रायो दावने स्याम ||
tve indrāpy abhūma viprā dhiyaṁ vanema ṛtayā sapantaḥ | avasyavo dhīmahi praśastiṁ sadyas te rāyo dāvane syāma ||
  • tve indrāpy abhūma viprā dhiyaṁ vanema ṛtayā sapantaḥ
    • tve - "to you" or "for you": This indicates a direct address to a divine entity, in this case, Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
    • indrāpy - "Indra": Represents strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • abhūma - "we have become": This suggests a transformation or a state of being that has been achieved through devotion or action.
    • viprā - "the wise ones" or "the learned": Refers to those who possess knowledge and wisdom, often the sages or seers in Vedic tradition.
    • dhiyaṁ - "intellect" or "thought": This emphasizes the importance of mental clarity and wisdom in achieving one's goals.
    • vanema - "we seek" or "we desire": Indicates a quest or aspiration towards something greater.
    • ṛtayā - "through righteousness" or "truth": Highlights the significance of following the path of truth and righteousness in one's endeavors.
    • sapantaḥ - "with the help of the wise": Suggests that the support and guidance of knowledgeable individuals are essential in this journey.
  • avasyavo dhīmahi praśastiṁ sadyas te rāyo dāvane syāma
    • avasyavo - "we shall attain" or "we shall achieve": This conveys a sense of certainty and determination in achieving one's goals.
    • dhīmahi - "we meditate" or "we contemplate": Indicates the importance of reflection and meditation in the pursuit of knowledge and success.
    • praśastiṁ - "praise" or "glory": Refers to the recognition and honor that comes from achieving one's goals.
    • sadyas - "immediately" or "quickly": Suggests a sense of urgency and the desire for prompt results.
    • te - "your": Refers back to Indra, indicating that the praise and glory are directed towards him.
    • rāyo - "wealth" or "prosperity": Represents the material and spiritual abundance that one seeks.
    • dāvane - "in the fire" or "in the sacrifice": Symbolizes the transformative power of sacrifice and dedication in achieving one's desires.
    • syāma - "may we become" or "let us be": A call to action, inviting the divine to bless the seekers with prosperity and success.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of seeking wisdom, righteousness, and divine guidance in our pursuits. It highlights the transformative power of intellect and the necessity of meditation and contemplation in achieving our goals. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In education, where students are encouraged to seek knowledge and wisdom from their teachers and mentors.
  • In professional settings, where teamwork and collaboration with knowledgeable colleagues can lead to success.
  • In personal development, where individuals are reminded to reflect on their actions and strive for righteousness in their decisions.

Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that with the right mindset, guidance, and dedication, we can achieve our aspirations and attain prosperity in both material and spiritual realms.
