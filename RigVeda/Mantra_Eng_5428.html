मन्त्र: एवेनु कं सिन्धुम एभिस तातारेवेनु कम भेदं एभिर जग्हान | एवेनु कं दाशराज्ञे सुदासं प्रावद इन्द्रो ब्रह्मणा वो वसिष्ठाः ||
even nu kaṁ sindhum ebhis tatāreven nu kam bhedam ebhir jaghāna | even nu kaṁ dāśarājñe sudāsam prāvad indro brahmaṇā vo vasiṣṭhāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "even nu kaṁ sindhum ebhis tatāreven nu kam bhedam ebhir jaghāna | even nu kaṁ dāśarājñe sudāsam prāvad indro brahmaṇā vo vasiṣṭhāḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine for guidance and support in overcoming obstacles and achieving unity. It speaks of the power of Indra, the king of gods, and his ability to help in overcoming divisions and conflicts.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • even nu: "now" or "indeed," indicating immediacy and urgency.
    • kaṁ: "who" or "what," questioning the nature of the obstacles.
    • sindhum: "ocean" or "sea," symbolizing vast challenges or the collective consciousness.
    • ebhis: "with these," referring to the means or tools available to overcome challenges.
    • tatāreven: "to cross over," suggesting the act of overcoming difficulties.
    • bhedam: "division" or "separation," highlighting conflicts or barriers.
    • jaghāna: "to strike down" or "to defeat," indicating the action of overcoming obstacles.
    • dāśarājñe: "in the reign of Dasaratha," referring to a historical or mythological context, possibly invoking the legacy of leadership.
    • sudāsam: "the good servant," symbolizing loyalty and righteousness.
    • prāvad: "he spoke," indicating communication or divine revelation.
    • indro: "Indra," the king of gods, representing strength and power.
    • brahmaṇā: "by the divine," suggesting a higher power or universal truth.
    • vasiṣṭhāḥ: "the best," indicating excellence or superiority.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek guidance from a higher power or community support, similar to invoking Indra for strength.
    • In a corporate setting, teams may face divisions or conflicts; leaders can draw on the principles of unity and collaboration to overcome these challenges.
    • During social movements, activists often call for unity and strength to overcome societal divisions, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in overcoming obstacles and achieving unity. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal struggles to collective challenges faced by communities. By invoking the strength of Indra and the principles of unity, individuals and groups can find the courage and means to navigate through difficulties, fostering a sense of togetherness and resilience.
