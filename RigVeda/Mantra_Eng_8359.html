दिवो यः स्कम्भो धरुणः स्वातत āपूर्णो अंशुः पर्येति विश्वतः |सेमे मही रोदसी यक्षद आवृतā समीचीन दाधार सम इषः कविः ||
divo yaḥ skambho dharuṇaḥ svātata āpūrṇo aṁśuḥ paryeti viśvataḥ | seme mahī rodasī yakṣad āvṛtā samīcīne dādhāra sam iṣaḥ kaviḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Divo: Of the heavens
    • Yaḥ: Who
    • Skambho: The support or pillar
    • Dharuṇaḥ: Firm or stable
    • Svātata: Self-sustaining
    • Āpūrṇo: Full or complete
    • Aṁśuḥ: Radiance or light
    • Paryeti: Encompasses or pervades
    • Viśvataḥ: Everywhere
    • Seme: In the earth
    • Mahī: The great or vast
    • Rodasī: The two realms (heaven and earth)
    • Yakṣad: Has established
    • Āvṛtā: Covered or enveloped
    • Samīcīne: In harmony or balance
    • Dādhāra: Holds or supports
    • Sam: Together
    • Iṣaḥ: The divine will or power
    • Kaviḥ: The seer or wise one
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine entity that is the pillar of the heavens, stable and self-sustaining.
    • The entity is described as full of radiance, pervading everywhere, indicating a universal presence.
    • It emphasizes the connection between the earth and the heavens, suggesting a balance and harmony in existence.
    • The mention of the wise seer indicates the importance of knowledge and insight in understanding the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we can observe the stability of mountains (the pillars of the earth) that have stood the test of time, symbolizing the support and strength described in the mantra.
    • The concept of balance can be seen in ecosystems where every element, from the smallest insect to the largest predator, plays a role in maintaining harmony.
    • In human relationships, the idea of being a support for one another reflects the essence of this mantra, where individuals contribute to the stability and well-being of the community.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of all existence, highlighting the importance of stability, balance, and wisdom.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios such as environmental conservation, community building, and personal development, where understanding our role in the larger scheme of things can lead to a more harmonious existence.
    • By recognizing the divine presence in all aspects of life, we can strive for a deeper connection with ourselves, others, and the universe.
