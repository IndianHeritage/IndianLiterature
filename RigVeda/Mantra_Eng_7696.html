पुनाति ते parisrutaṁ सोमं सूर्यस्य दुहितā |वारेण शाश्वता तनā ||
punāti te parisrutaṁ somaṁ sūryasya duhitā | vāreṇa śaśvatā tanā ||
  • punāti: This word means "purifies" or "cleanses." It signifies the act of making something pure or free from impurities.
  • te: This translates to "your," indicating possession or relation.
  • parisrutaṁ: This term refers to "flowing" or "spreading," often used in the context of something that is abundant or overflowing.
  • somaṁ: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. It is often associated with the moon and is considered a source of nourishment.
  • sūryasya: This means "of the sun," referring to the sun's energy and life-giving properties.
  • duhitā: This translates to "daughter," often used in a metaphorical sense to represent something that is born from or nurtured by another entity.
  • vāreṇa: This word means "with protection" or "by means of," indicating a sense of safeguarding or ensuring well-being.
  • śaśvatā: This means "eternal" or "everlasting," suggesting something that is timeless and enduring.
  • tanā: This translates to "body" or "form," often referring to the physical manifestation of life or existence.

  • Meaning of the Mantra: The mantra speaks of the purification and nourishment that comes from the divine, particularly through the blessings of the sun and the sacred soma. It emphasizes the importance of protection and the eternal nature of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, we often seek purification through various means, such as meditation, yoga, or even physical cleansing rituals. These practices help us to clear our minds and bodies, much like the purification mentioned in the mantra.
    • The concept of nourishment can be seen in our dietary choices. Just as soma is a source of divine nourishment, we can choose foods that are wholesome and beneficial for our health, promoting a sense of well-being.
    • Protection is a universal need. Whether it is through family, community, or spiritual practices, we seek to create a safe environment for ourselves and our loved ones, reflecting the idea of "vāreṇa" in the mantra.

  • Conclusion: This mantra highlights the significance of purification, nourishment, and protection in our lives. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the divine forces that sustain us. In real-life scenarios, we can apply the teachings of this mantra by engaging in practices that promote mental and physical well-being, fostering a sense of community, and seeking spiritual growth. By embodying these principles, we can lead a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
