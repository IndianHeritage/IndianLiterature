व्यन्त्विनु येषु मन्दसानस्तृपत सोमं पाहि द्रव्याद इन्द्र |अस्मान् सुपृत्स्वा तरुत्रावर्धयो द्यां बृहद्भिरर्कैः ||
vyantv in nu yeṣu mandasānas tṛpat somam pāhi drahyad indra | asmān su pṛtsv ā tarutrāvardhayo dyām bṛhadbhir arkaiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "vyantv in nu yeṣu mandasānas tṛpat somam pāhi drahyad indra | asmān su pṛtsv ā tarutrāvardhayo dyām bṛhadbhir arkaiḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vyantv: May it be fulfilled
    • in: in
    • nu: indeed
    • yeṣu: in whom
    • mandasānas: those who are slow or lethargic
    • tṛpat: satisfied
    • somam: the soma (a sacred drink)
    • pāhi: protect
    • drahyad: may it be strong
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • asmān: us
    • su: well
    • pṛtsv: in the fields
    • ā: towards
    • tarutrā: trees
    • vardhayo: may you increase
    • dyām: the sky
    • bṛhadbhir: with great
    • arkaiḥ: rays (of light)
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the god of rain and thunder, asking for protection and satisfaction.
    • The phrase "vyantv in nu yeṣu mandasānas" suggests a desire for fulfillment in those who may be lethargic or slow, indicating a need for motivation and energy.
    • "tṛpat somam pāhi drahyad indra" emphasizes the importance of being satisfied and protected, particularly through the sacred soma, which symbolizes nourishment and spiritual fulfillment.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "asmān su pṛtsv ā tarutrāvardhayo dyām bṛhadbhir arkaiḥ," calls for growth and abundance in our surroundings, particularly in nature, represented by trees and the sky.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied to encourage team members who may be feeling unmotivated. By invoking a sense of purpose and satisfaction, leaders can help their teams feel more engaged and productive.
    • In personal life, individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to seek spiritual nourishment and fulfillment, perhaps through meditation or community service, to combat feelings of lethargy.
    • In environmental contexts, the call for growth and abundance can inspire actions towards sustainability, such as planting trees or supporting local agriculture, to enhance the natural world around us.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking fulfillment, protection, and growth in our lives and surroundings.
    • It serves as a reminder to cultivate energy and satisfaction, not only within ourselves but also in our communities and the environment.
    • By applying the teachings of this mantra, individuals can foster a more harmonious and prosperous existence, both personally and collectively.
