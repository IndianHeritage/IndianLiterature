अनश्वो जातो अनभिषुर अर्वा कनिक्रदत पतयद ऊर्ध्वसानुः |अचित्तं ब्रह्म जुञ्जुषुर युवानः प्र मित्रे धाम वरुणे गृणन्तः ||
anaśvo jāto anabhīśur arvā kanikradat patayad ūrdhvasānuḥ | acittam brahma jujuṣur yuvānaḥ pra mitre dhāma varuṇe gṛṇantaḥ ||
  • anaśvo jāto: This phrase can be translated as "born without a horse." In a metaphorical sense, it suggests the idea of being unencumbered or free from worldly attachments. In real life, this can relate to individuals who pursue their goals without relying on external resources or support, much like entrepreneurs who start businesses with minimal resources.
  • anabhīśur: This term means "without fear." It emphasizes the importance of courage and confidence in one's actions. For example, a person facing a challenging situation, such as a job interview or a public speaking event, can draw strength from this idea, reminding themselves to act fearlessly.
  • arvā kanikradat: This can be interpreted as "the one who moves swiftly." It signifies agility and adaptability. In today's fast-paced world, being able to adapt quickly to changes—like a business pivoting its strategy in response to market demands—illustrates this concept.
  • patayad ūrdhvasānuḥ: This phrase translates to "one who flies high." It symbolizes ambition and the pursuit of lofty goals. For instance, an athlete training for the Olympics embodies this spirit, striving to reach the pinnacle of their sport.
  • acittam brahma: This means "the unconditioned absolute." It refers to the ultimate reality or truth that transcends human understanding. In practical terms, this can relate to philosophical pursuits or spiritual journeys where individuals seek deeper meaning in life beyond material existence.
  • jujuṣur yuvānaḥ: This translates to "the youthful ones who desire." It highlights the aspirations and dreams of the younger generation. For example, young activists fighting for climate change represent this desire for a better future, embodying hope and determination.
  • pra mitre dhāma varuṇe gṛṇantaḥ: This phrase means "singing praises to the friend, the abode of Varuna." Varuna is often associated with cosmic order and moral authority. In real life, this can be seen in communities coming together to celebrate shared values and ethics, fostering unity and harmony.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of freedom, courage, adaptability, ambition, the search for truth, youthful aspirations, and the importance of community. Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to pursue their goals fearlessly and with integrity, while also recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide personal development, community engagement, and the pursuit of a meaningful existence, reminding us to strive for higher ideals while remaining grounded in our shared humanity.
