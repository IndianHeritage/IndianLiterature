रथाय नावमुत नो गृहाय नित्यारित्रां पदवतीं रास्य अग्ने |अस्माकं वीरामुत नो मघोनो जनाम्श्च या पारयाच चरमा या च ||
rathāya nāvam uta no gṛhāya nityāritrām padvatīṁ rāsy agne | asmākaṁ vīrām̐ uta no maghono janām̐ś ca yā pārayāc charma yā ca ||
  • Mantra: "rathāya nāvam uta no gṛhāya nityāritrām padvatīṁ rāsy agne | asmākaṁ vīrām̐ uta no maghono janām̐ś ca yā pārayāc charma yā ca ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as a prayer to Agni, the fire god, seeking protection and guidance for our journey, whether it be in our vehicles (rathāya), boats (nāvam), or homes (gṛhāya). It emphasizes the importance of strength (vīrām̐) and prosperity (maghono) for our community (janām̐ś) and the blessings that come from the divine.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • rathāya: Refers to a chariot or vehicle, symbolizing the journey of life.
    • nāvam: Means boat, representing the navigation through life's challenges.
    • gṛhāya: Refers to home, indicating the importance of safety and stability.
    • nityāritrām: Suggests continuous protection and guidance.
    • padvatīṁ: Implies a path or journey, highlighting the importance of direction in life.
    • rāsy: Refers to the divine or sacred, emphasizing the spiritual aspect of our endeavors.
    • agne: The fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • asmākaṁ: Means "our," indicating a collective prayer for the community.
    • vīrām̐: Refers to strength or valor, essential for overcoming obstacles.
    • maghono: Means prosperity or abundance, crucial for a fulfilling life.
    • janām̐ś: Refers to people or community, highlighting the importance of social bonds.
    • pārayāc: Suggests the act of bestowing or granting, indicating a request for blessings.
    • charma: Refers to protection or covering, symbolizing safety.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of a rathāya can be seen in the vehicles we use daily, such as cars or bicycles, which take us on our journeys. Just as we seek safety on the road, this mantra reminds us to seek divine guidance in our travels.
    • The nāvam represents our ability to navigate through life's challenges, much like how we use boats to traverse rivers. This can be likened to overcoming personal struggles or professional hurdles.
    • gṛhāya emphasizes the importance of home as a sanctuary. In today's world, creating a safe and nurturing environment for our families is paramount, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • The call for vīrām̐ and maghono resonates in our pursuit of strength and prosperity in our careers and personal lives, reminding us to support one another in our community.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, encompassing the physical, emotional, and spiritual aspects of our existence. It serves as a reminder to seek divine guidance in our journeys, to foster strength and prosperity within our communities, and to create safe havens for ourselves and our loved ones. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in daily prayers, during significant life events such as weddings or new ventures, and as a guiding principle in our interactions with others, promoting unity and support.
