यास ते प्रजा अमृतस्य परस्मिन् धाम्न ऋतस्य |मूर्धा नाभा सोम वेन आभूषन्तीः सोम वेदः ||
yās te prajā amṛtasya parasmin dhāmann ṛtasya | mūrdhā nābhā soma vena ābhūṣantīḥ soma vedaḥ ||
  • yās te prajā amṛtasya: This phrase can be translated as "those who are your progeny, O Immortal." Here, "yās" refers to "those," "te" means "your," "prajā" signifies "progeny" or "offspring," and "amṛtasya" translates to "of the immortal" or "of the eternal." This highlights the connection between the divine and the lineage of beings, emphasizing the importance of spiritual heritage.
  • parasmin dhāmann ṛtasya: This part means "in the realm of truth." "Parasmin" translates to "in the other" or "in the higher," "dhāmann" refers to "the realm" or "the domain," and "ṛtasya" signifies "of truth" or "of cosmic order." This suggests that the progeny are connected to a higher truth or cosmic order, indicating that their existence is not just physical but also spiritual.
  • mūrdhā nābhā soma: This translates to "the head is the navel of Soma." "Mūrdhā" means "head," "nābhā" refers to "navel," and "soma" is often associated with the divine nectar or the moon. This metaphor suggests that the head (intellect) is central to the essence of life (navel), and it highlights the importance of wisdom and knowledge in nurturing life.
  • vena ābhūṣantīḥ soma vedaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the essence of Soma is adorned." "Vena" means "essence" or "sound," "ābhūṣantīḥ" translates to "adorned" or "decorated," and "soma vedaḥ" refers to "the knowledge of Soma." This indicates that the knowledge of the divine or the essence of life is something that should be cherished and adorned, suggesting a reverence for spiritual wisdom.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the connection between the divine, the truth, and the essence of life. It signifies the importance of spiritual heritage and the role of wisdom in nurturing life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to the way we view our lineage and the values we pass on to future generations. It encourages individuals to seek higher truths and to adorn their lives with knowledge and wisdom, much like how one would cherish and cultivate a garden. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of spiritual growth, and the reverence for knowledge that transcends the physical realm.
