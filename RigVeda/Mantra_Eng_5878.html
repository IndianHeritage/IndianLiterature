मां पापत्वाय नो नरेन्द्राग्नि मांभिषस्तये |मा नो रीर्धतं निदे ||
mā pāpatvāya no narendrāgnī mābhiśastaye | mā no rīradhataṁ nide ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mā: not
    • pāpatvāya: to evil or sin
    • no: our
    • narendrāgnī: O fire of the king (or leader)
    • mā: not
    • abhiśastaye: for the purpose of harming or punishing
    • mā: not
    • no: our
    • rīradhataṁ: may you take away or consume
    • nide: our wealth or prosperity
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a plea to the divine fire (Agni) to not lead us towards evil or sin, and to protect our wealth and prosperity from harm. It emphasizes the importance of maintaining righteousness and the need for divine protection against negative influences.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be interpreted as a call for ethical business practices. Leaders may invoke this sentiment to ensure that their decisions do not lead to unethical outcomes that could harm employees or stakeholders.
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during times of moral dilemma, seeking guidance to avoid actions that could lead to regret or harm to others.
    • In community gatherings, this mantra can serve as a reminder to uphold values of integrity and compassion, fostering a supportive environment that discourages wrongdoing.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for protection against negativity and the pursuit of righteousness. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal decision-making to leadership in organizations. By invoking this mantra, individuals and communities can strive to create a harmonious environment that values ethical behavior and collective well-being. It serves as a reminder that our actions should align with higher principles, ensuring that we do not stray into paths that could lead to harm or destruction.
