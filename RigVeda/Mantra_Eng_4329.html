अनागसः अदितये देवस्य सवितुः सवे |विश्वा वामानि धीमहि ||
anāgaso aditaye devasya savituḥ save | viśvā vāmāni dhīmahi ||
  • anāgaso: This term translates to "without sin" or "innocent." It signifies purity and the absence of wrongdoing.
  • aditaye: This word refers to "Aditi," the mother of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing the infinite and the boundless.
  • devasya: This means "of the divine" or "of the god," indicating a connection to the higher spiritual realm.
  • savituḥ: This translates to "of Savitar," the sun god, representing light, energy, and life-giving properties.
  • save: This word means "to save" or "to protect," emphasizing the nurturing aspect of the divine.
  • viśvā: This means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusiveness and the entirety of existence.
  • vāmāni: This translates to "beautiful" or "auspicious," suggesting positivity and harmony.
  • dhīmahi: This means "we meditate" or "we contemplate," indicating a focus on spiritual practice and reflection.

  • Meaning: The mantra can be interpreted as a call to meditate on the divine, pure, and protective aspects of the universe, represented by Aditi and Savitar. It emphasizes the importance of seeking wisdom and beauty in all aspects of life.
  • Real-life Example: In daily life, this mantra can inspire individuals to strive for purity in their thoughts and actions, to seek knowledge and wisdom, and to appreciate the beauty around them. For instance, a person may reflect on this mantra while engaging in meditation or yoga, aiming to connect with their inner self and the universe.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for purity, wisdom, and connection to the divine. It encourages individuals to meditate on the beauty and protection offered by the universe, fostering a sense of peace and harmony in their lives. This mantra can be applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as during meditation, in moments of reflection, or when seeking guidance in challenging situations. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a more meaningful and spiritually enriched life.
