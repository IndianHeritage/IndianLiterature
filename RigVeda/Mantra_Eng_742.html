घृषुं पावकं वनिनं विचर्षणिं रुद्रस्य सूनुं हवसा गृणीमसि |रजस्तुरं तवसम मारुतं गणम ऋजीषिणं वृषणं सश्चत श्रीये ||
ghṛṣum pāvakaṁ vaninaṁ vicarṣaṇiṁ rudrasya sūnuṁ havasā gṛṇīmasi | rajasturaṁ tavasam mārutaṁ gaṇam ṛjīṣiṇaṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ saścata śriye ||
  • ghṛṣum: This word refers to the "ghee" or "clarified butter," which is often used in Vedic rituals as an offering to the deities. In real life, ghee is considered a symbol of purity and is used in various culinary practices.
  • pāvakaṁ: Meaning "pure" or "sacred," this term emphasizes the importance of purity in both physical and spiritual contexts. For example, in many cultures, cleanliness is associated with health and well-being.
  • vaninaṁ: This word translates to "of the forest" or "wild," indicating a connection to nature. In modern life, this can relate to the importance of preserving natural habitats and biodiversity.
  • vicarṣaṇiṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who moves swiftly" or "the one who travels." It reflects the dynamic nature of life and the need for adaptability in various situations.
  • rudrasya: Referring to "Rudra," a deity associated with storms and the hunt, this word signifies strength and power. In real life, it can symbolize overcoming challenges and harnessing inner strength.
  • sūnuṁ: Meaning "son," this term can represent lineage and the passing of knowledge through generations. In contemporary society, this can relate to the importance of family and mentorship.
  • havasā: This word means "offering" or "sacrifice," highlighting the significance of giving and sharing in human relationships. In everyday life, acts of kindness and generosity are essential for building community.
  • gṛṇīmasi: This translates to "we sing" or "we praise," indicating the act of worship and reverence. In real life, this can be seen in various forms of expression, such as music, art, and cultural celebrations.
  • rajasturaṁ: This term refers to "the one who is active" or "the one who moves with energy." It emphasizes the importance of action and initiative in achieving goals.
  • tavasam: Meaning "of your power," this word signifies the divine strength that guides and supports individuals in their endeavors.
  • mārutaṁ: This refers to "the winds" or "the storm," symbolizing change and the forces of nature. In real life, it can represent the unpredictability of life and the need to be resilient.
  • gaṇam: Meaning "group" or "community," this term highlights the importance of collective effort and unity in achieving common goals.
  • ṛjīṣiṇaṁ: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is righteous" or "the one who follows the path of truth." It emphasizes the value of integrity and ethical behavior in personal and professional life.
  • vṛṣaṇaṁ: This term refers to "the strong" or "the powerful," symbolizing strength and resilience. In real life, it can relate to the importance of mental and physical strength in overcoming obstacles.
  • saścata: Meaning "with all," this word signifies inclusivity and the importance of community support in achieving success.
  • śriye: This translates to "prosperity" or "wealth," indicating the ultimate goal of well-being and abundance in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of purity, strength, community, and the dynamic nature of life. It emphasizes the importance of offering, praise, and collective effort in achieving prosperity and well-being. In real life, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as fostering community relationships, promoting environmental sustainability, and encouraging personal growth through resilience and integrity. By embodying these values, individuals can contribute to a harmonious and prosperous society.
