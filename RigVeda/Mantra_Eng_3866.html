भद्रम इदं रुशमा अग्ने अक्रन् गवां चत्वारी ददतः सहस्राः |ऋणं चयस्य प्रयताः मग्हानि प्रत्य अग्रभिष्म नृतमस्य नृणाम् ||
bhadram idaṁ ruśamā agne akran gavāṁ catvāri dadataḥ sahasrā | ṛṇaṁcayasya prayatā maghāni praty agrabhīṣma nṛtamasya nṛṇām ||
  • bhadram idaṁ: This phrase translates to "may this be auspicious." It sets a positive tone, indicating a desire for good fortune and well-being.
  • ruśamā: This word can be interpreted as "radiant" or "brilliant," often associated with the divine light or energy that guides and protects.
  • agne: Referring to Agni, the fire god, who symbolizes transformation, purification, and the divine presence in rituals. Fire is a central element in many cultures, representing both destruction and renewal.
  • akran: This means "to create" or "to make." It emphasizes the act of bringing something into existence, which can be related to creativity and innovation in our lives.
  • gavāṁ catvāri: This translates to "four of the cows." Cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and prosperity in many cultures, particularly in agrarian societies where livestock is a measure of wealth.
  • dadataḥ sahasrā: This means "giving thousands." It signifies abundance and generosity, suggesting that one should share their wealth and resources with others.
  • ṛṇaṁcayasya: This can be interpreted as "of the debts." It refers to the responsibilities and obligations we have in life, whether they are financial, social, or moral.
  • prayatā: This means "with effort" or "with diligence." It highlights the importance of hard work and perseverance in achieving our goals.
  • maghāni: This translates to "great blessings" or "riches." It indicates the rewards that come from our efforts and the blessings we receive in life.
  • praty agrabhīṣma: This phrase can be understood as "towards the foremost." It suggests striving for excellence and aiming for the highest achievements in our endeavors.
  • nṛtamasya nṛṇām: This means "of the best among men." It emphasizes the qualities of leadership, virtue, and righteousness that one should aspire to embody.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the importance of auspiciousness, effort, and the pursuit of excellence in life. It encourages individuals to seek blessings, share their wealth, and fulfill their responsibilities with diligence. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal development, community service, and professional endeavors. By embodying the principles of generosity, hard work, and striving for greatness, individuals can create a positive impact in their lives and the lives of others, fostering a sense of harmony and prosperity in society.
