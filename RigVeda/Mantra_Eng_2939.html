अभि व्यायस्व खदिरस्य सारम् ओजो धेहि स्पन्दने शिंशपायाम् |अक्ष वीलो वीलित वीलयस्व मा यामाद अस्माद अव जिहिपो नः ||
abhi vyayasva khadirasya sāram ojo dhehi spandane śiṁśapāyām | akṣa vīḻo vīḻita vīḻayasva mā yāmād asmād ava jīhipo naḥ ||
  • Mantra: "abhi vyayasva khadirasya sāram ojo dhehi spandane śiṁśapāyām | akṣa vīḻo vīḻita vīḻayasva mā yāmād asmād ava jīhipo naḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • vyayasva: may you become powerful or strong
    • khadirasya: of the Khadira tree (Acacia catechu)
    • sāram: essence, core, or substance
    • ojo: energy, vitality, or strength
    • dhehi: grant or bestow
    • spandane: in the vibration or resonance
    • śiṁśapāyām: in the Shirish tree (Albizia lebbeck)
    • akṣa: eye or vision
    • vīḻo: may it be fulfilled or realized
    • vīḻita: may it be made manifest
    • vīḻayasva: may you attain or achieve
    • mā: do not
    • yāmād: from this, from here
    • asmād: from us
    • ava: down, away, or apart
    • jīhipo: may you not abandon or forsake
    • naḥ: us, our
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence and strength of the Khadira and Shirish trees, which are known for their resilience and medicinal properties.
    • The phrase "may you become powerful" suggests a desire for personal empowerment and vitality, akin to how trees draw strength from the earth.
    • The mention of "energy" and "vibration" indicates a connection to the life force that flows through all living beings, emphasizing the importance of being in tune with one's surroundings.
    • In real life, this can be related to practices such as yoga or meditation, where individuals seek to harness their inner strength and connect with nature.
    • The call to "not abandon us" signifies a plea for support and guidance, reflecting the human need for community and connection.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of strength, vitality, and connection to nature, which are essential for personal growth and well-being.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek empowerment from the natural world and to remain grounded in our relationships with others.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in daily life through mindfulness practices, fostering community ties, and appreciating the resilience found in nature.
