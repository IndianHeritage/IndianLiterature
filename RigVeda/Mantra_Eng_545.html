हविषा जARO अपाम् पिपर्ति पपुरिर नराः |पिता कुटस्य चर्षणिः ||
haviṣā jāro apām piparti papurir narā | pitā kuṭasya carṣaṇiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "haviṣā jāro apām piparti papurir narā | pitā kuṭasya carṣaṇiḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "The one who is nourished by the offerings (haviṣā) is the one who brings forth (piparti) the essence of water (apām) and the one who is the father (pitā) of the household (kuṭasya) is the one who is the protector (carṣaṇiḥ) of the community (narā)."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • haviṣā: Refers to offerings or sacrifices made to the deities, symbolizing the act of giving and sharing.
    • jāro: Indicates the one who is nourished or sustained, often referring to a divine or spiritual being.
    • apām: Means 'of water', representing purity, life, and sustenance.
    • piparti: Translates to 'brings forth' or 'produces', indicating creation or manifestation.
    • narā: Refers to 'humans' or 'people', emphasizing the community aspect.
    • pitā: Means 'father', symbolizing authority, protection, and guidance.
    • kuṭasya: Refers to 'household' or 'home', indicating the importance of family and community.
    • carṣaṇiḥ: Means 'protector' or 'guardian', highlighting the role of a leader or elder in society.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The act of making offerings in various cultures, such as during religious ceremonies, reflects the idea of nourishing the divine and, in return, receiving blessings for the community.
    • In many families, the father figure often takes on the role of a protector and provider, ensuring the well-being of the household and guiding the younger generation.
    • Water is essential for life, and its significance in rituals and daily practices underscores the importance of purity and sustenance in both physical and spiritual realms.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of offerings, nourishment, and community. It highlights the roles individuals play within their families and society, particularly the responsibilities of leaders and protectors.
    • In real life, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of giving, nurturing relationships, and the responsibilities we hold towards our families and communities.
    • By embodying the principles of protection, nourishment, and community service, individuals can contribute to a harmonious and thriving society.
