धनं न स्यन्द्रम बहुलं यो अस्मै तीव्रान सोमां आसुनोति प्रयस्वान् |तस्मै शत्रून सुतुकान प्रातर अह्नो नि स्वष्ट्रान युवति हन्ति वृत्रम् ||
dhanaṁ na syandram bahulaṁ yo asmai tīvrān somām̐ āsunoti prayasvān | tasmai śatrūn sutukān prātar ahno ni svaṣṭrān yuvati hanti vṛtram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dhanaṁ: wealth
    • na: not
    • syandram: flowing or abundant
    • bahulaṁ: plentiful
    • yo: who
    • asmai: to him
    • tīvrān: intense or powerful
    • somām̐: soma (a sacred drink)
    • āsunoti: bestows or grants
    • prayasvān: energetic or vigorous
    • tasmai: to him
    • śatrūn: enemies
    • sutukān: well-prepared or strong
    • prātar: in the morning
    • ahno: of the day
    • ni: down or away
    • svaṣṭrān: well-armed or equipped
    • yuvati: youth or vigor
    • hanti: destroys
    • vṛtram: obstacles or barriers
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of wealth and the power of the divine to bestow it upon those who are vigorous and energetic.
    • The reference to "soma" indicates a connection to spiritual nourishment and the importance of rituals in achieving prosperity.
    • The mantra emphasizes that true wealth is not just material but also includes the strength to overcome obstacles and enemies.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to entrepreneurs who work energetically to build their enterprises. Their hard work and dedication can lead to financial success.
    • In personal life, individuals who actively seek knowledge and skills (the "soma") can overcome challenges and achieve their goals, reflecting the mantra's message of empowerment.
    • In community settings, leaders who inspire and energize their teams can effectively tackle social issues, representing the destruction of "vṛtram" or barriers to progress.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic view of wealth, emphasizing that true prosperity comes from a combination of material abundance and the inner strength to face challenges.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate both their physical and spiritual resources, suggesting that success is achievable through dedication, energy, and the right mindset.
    • This mantra can be applicable in various aspects of life, including personal development, professional growth, and community leadership, serving as a reminder of the interconnectedness of wealth, strength, and overcoming obstacles.
