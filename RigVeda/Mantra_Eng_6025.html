न घेम अन्यद ā पापन वज्रिन्न अपसो नविष्टौ |तवेद उ स्तोमं चिकेट ||
na ghem anyad ā papana vajrinn apaso naviṣṭau | taved u stomaṁ ciketa ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na: not
    • ghem: to be disturbed or shaken
    • anyad: anything else
    • ā: from
    • papana: to fall or to be cast down
    • vajrinn: like a thunderbolt or powerful
    • apas: waters or the flow
    • naviṣṭau: not to be obstructed or hindered
    • taved: you have made or created
    • u: and
    • stomaṁ: praise or hymn
    • ciketa: to understand or to know
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes resilience and strength in the face of challenges.
    • It suggests that one should not be disturbed by external forces or obstacles.
    • The reference to "vajrinn" indicates a powerful, unyielding nature, akin to a thunderbolt that cannot be easily swayed.
    • The phrase "taved u stomaṁ ciketa" implies that through understanding and creation, one can achieve a state of harmony and praise.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an employee may face criticism or setbacks. By embodying the essence of this mantra, they can remain steadfast and focused on their goals, much like a strong tree that withstands a storm.
    • In personal relationships, conflicts may arise. By not allowing external negativity to disturb one's peace, individuals can foster healthier interactions, akin to a calm lake that reflects the sky despite the winds.
    • In the face of societal challenges, activists and leaders can draw inspiration from this mantra to remain resolute and continue their efforts without being deterred by opposition.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for inner strength and resilience.
    • It teaches us to remain undisturbed by external circumstances and to focus on our inner wisdom and understanding.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various situations where one faces adversity, encouraging individuals to rise above challenges and maintain their integrity and purpose.
