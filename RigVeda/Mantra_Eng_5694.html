दिवो धामभिर वरुण मित्रश्च यातम अद्रुहा |पिबतं सोमम आतुजी ||
divo dhāmabhir varuṇa mitraś cā yātam adruhā | pibataṁ somam ātujī ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Divo: Of the heavens or celestial.
    • Dhāmabhir: By the abodes or realms.
    • Varuṇa: The god of cosmic order and water.
    • Mitra: The god of friendship and alliances.
    • Ścā: And.
    • Yātam: May we go or proceed.
    • Adruhā: Without enmity or harm.
    • Pibataṁ: Drink or imbibe.
    • Somam: Soma, a sacred drink or elixir.
    • Ātujī: For health or well-being.
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra invokes the celestial realms and the deities Varuṇa and Mitra, asking for a journey or progression that is free from enmity. It encourages the consumption of Soma, symbolizing health and vitality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Seeking Harmony: In personal relationships, this mantra can be a reminder to approach interactions with a spirit of friendship and without hostility. For instance, during a conflict resolution, one might reflect on this mantra to foster a peaceful dialogue.
    • Health and Well-being: The reference to Soma can be likened to modern practices of wellness, such as meditation or herbal remedies, which promote physical and mental health. For example, participating in a community wellness program can embody the essence of this mantra.
    • Community and Cooperation: In a workplace setting, this mantra can inspire teamwork and collaboration, encouraging colleagues to work together harmoniously towards common goals, much like the deities Mitra and Varuṇa symbolize unity and order.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for harmony, health, and the pursuit of collective well-being. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to community interactions, reminding us to foster peace and cooperation while nurturing our health and vitality.
