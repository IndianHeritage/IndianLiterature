तद् इन्व अस्य सवितुर नकीर मे हिरण्ययीं अमतिं याम् अशिश्रेत् |आ सुष्टुती रोडसी विश्वमिन्वे अपीव योषा जनिमानि वव्रे ||
tad in nv asya savitur nakir me hiraṇyayīm amatiṁ yām aśiśret | ā suṣṭutī rodasī viśvaminve apīva yoṣā janimāni vavre ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • in: in
    • nv: indeed
    • asya: of this
    • savituh: of the Sun
    • nakir: not
    • me: my
    • hiraṇyayīm: golden
    • amatiṁ: thought
    • yām: which
    • aśiśret: has chosen
    • ā: may
    • suṣṭutī: well-being
    • rodasī: the two worlds
    • viśvamin: all
    • ve: indeed
    • apīva: like
    • yoṣā: woman
    • janimāni: births
    • vavre: has chosen
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine light of the Sun (Savitṛ) and its role in guiding thoughts and choices.
    • The phrase "hiraṇyayīm amatiṁ" suggests a golden thought or wisdom that one aspires to attain.
    • The mention of "rodasī" refers to the duality of existence, encompassing both the physical and spiritual realms.
    • The term "yoṣā" symbolizes the nurturing aspect of creation, akin to a mother giving birth to new ideas or life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek guidance and clarity in their thoughts, much like how the Sun illuminates the world.
    • When making important decisions, one might reflect on their "golden thoughts," aiming for wisdom that leads to positive outcomes.
    • The balance of duality in life can be seen in the roles people play, such as being both a provider and a nurturer in a family.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and wisdom in our thoughts and actions.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of all aspects of life, urging individuals to embrace both the material and spiritual dimensions.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire people to pursue their goals with clarity and purpose, fostering a harmonious balance in their lives.
