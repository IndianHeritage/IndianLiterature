एहि अग्न इह होतā न षीदादब्धः सु पुरेतā भवā नः |अवतां त्वा रोदसी विश्वमिन्वे यजā महे सुमनसāय देवān ||
ehy agna iha hotā ni ṣīdādabdhaḥ su puraetā bhavā naḥ | avatāṁ tvā rodasī viśvaminve yajā mahe saumanasāya devān ||
  • ehy agna: This phrase translates to "Come, O Agni." Agni is the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, this can be likened to the way fire is used in rituals, cooking, and even in industrial processes to transform materials.
  • iha hotā: This means "here is the priest." The priest (hotā) is a crucial figure in Vedic rituals, representing the mediator between the divine and the human. In modern contexts, this can be seen in various roles where individuals act as intermediaries, such as teachers, leaders, or counselors who guide others in their spiritual or personal journeys.
  • ni ṣīdādabdhaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "do not be unsteady." It emphasizes the importance of stability and focus. In everyday life, this can relate to maintaining composure in stressful situations, such as during exams or important presentations, where steadiness can lead to better outcomes.
  • su puraetā bhavā naḥ: This translates to "may you be well-disposed towards us." It expresses a desire for goodwill and positive energy from the divine. In practical terms, this can be seen in how we seek support and kindness from others in our communities, fostering relationships based on mutual respect and understanding.
  • avatāṁ tvā rodasī: This means "we invoke you from the two worlds." It signifies the connection between the earthly and the celestial realms. In real life, this can be related to the idea of seeking balance between our material pursuits and spiritual growth, recognizing that both aspects are essential for a fulfilling life.
  • viśvaminve: This translates to "may you be the all-pervading one." It acknowledges the omnipresence of the divine. In contemporary terms, this can be likened to the idea of interconnectedness in nature and society, where every action has a ripple effect on the larger community and environment.
  • yajā mahe saumanasāya devān: This means "we perform this great sacrifice for the benevolent gods." It highlights the importance of rituals and offerings in seeking blessings. In modern life, this can be seen in acts of charity, community service, or any endeavor aimed at contributing positively to society, reflecting a spirit of giving and gratitude.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, stability, and goodwill in our lives. Its significance lies in the reminder to maintain focus, foster positive relationships, and recognize the interconnectedness of all beings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in self-reflection, community service, and spiritual practices that promote harmony and well-being, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and purposeful existence.
