आ ते दधामीन्त्रियं उक्था विश्वा शतक्रतो | स्तोत्रिभ्य इन्द्र मृळय ||
ā te dadhāmīndriyam ukthā viśvā śatakrato | stotṛbhya indra mṛḻaya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: to you
    • te: your
    • dadhāmi: I offer
    • indriyam: senses or faculties
    • ukthā: uttered or spoken
    • viśvā: all or universal
    • śatakrato: O Indra, the one with a hundred powers
    • stotṛbhya: to the worshippers
    • indra: O Indra
    • mṛḻaya: please grant or bestow
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, asking him to bestow upon the worshipper the ability to use their senses and faculties effectively. It emphasizes the importance of divine support in enhancing one's capabilities and understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, individuals often seek guidance and support from mentors or leaders to enhance their skills and decision-making abilities. This mantra reflects the same sentiment of seeking divine assistance in honing one's faculties.
    • Students preparing for exams may recite this mantra to invoke clarity of thought and focus, hoping to improve their cognitive abilities and performance.
    • In moments of stress or uncertainty, individuals may turn to spiritual practices, including mantras, to seek inner strength and clarity, similar to how this mantra seeks the blessing of Indra for enhanced faculties.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of the need for divine support in our endeavors. It teaches us that while we strive to improve ourselves, seeking help from a higher power can enhance our abilities and lead to greater success. In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as education, career development, and personal growth, reminding us to remain humble and open to receiving guidance from the universe.
