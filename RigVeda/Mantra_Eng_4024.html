अस्मा उक्ताया पर्वतस्य गर्भो महीनां जनुषे पूर्व्याय |वि पर्वतो जिहीत साधत द्यौर आविवासन्तो दासयन्त भूमा ||
asmā ukthāya parvatasya garbho mahīnāṁ januṣe pūrvyāya | vi parvato jihīta sādhata dyaur āvivāsanto dasayanta bhūma ||
  • asmā - This word translates to "us" or "we," indicating a collective identity or community.
  • ukthāya - This means "to the utterance" or "to the invocation," suggesting a call or prayer directed towards a higher power.
  • parvatasya - This refers to "of the mountain," symbolizing strength, stability, and the natural world.
  • garbho - This translates to "the womb" or "the source," indicating a place of origin or creation.
  • mahīnāṁ - This means "of the great" or "of the vast," often referring to the earth or the universe.
  • januṣe - This word means "for the beings" or "for the people," emphasizing the connection between the divine and humanity.
  • pūrvyāya - This translates to "to the ancient" or "to the previous," suggesting a link to tradition and history.
  • vi - This means "through" or "by," indicating a means of connection or action.
  • parvato - This is another reference to "the mountain," reinforcing the theme of strength and stability.
  • jihīta - This means "to strive" or "to seek," indicating an active pursuit of goals or ideals.
  • sādhata - This translates to "to accomplish" or "to achieve," emphasizing the importance of action and effort.
  • dyaur - This means "the sky" or "the heavens," representing the divine or spiritual realm.
  • āvivāsanto - This translates to "coming forth" or "emerging," suggesting a manifestation of ideas or energies.
  • dasayanta - This means "to create" or "to bring forth," indicating the act of bringing something into existence.
  • bhūma - This refers to "the earth" or "the ground," symbolizing the physical world and our connection to it.

The mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of humanity, nature, and the divine. It emphasizes the importance of invoking strength and stability (represented by the mountains) while recognizing our origins and the vastness of existence. The call to action is clear: we must strive to achieve our goals and bring forth new ideas, all while remaining grounded in our earthly existence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Community Building: Just as the mantra emphasizes collective identity, it can inspire communities to come together for a common purpose, fostering unity and collaboration.
  • Environmental Awareness: The references to mountains and the earth remind us of our responsibility to protect and preserve nature, encouraging sustainable practices.
  • Personal Growth: The call to strive and achieve can motivate individuals to pursue their goals, emphasizing the importance of effort and determination in personal development.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of our connection to each other, the natural world, and the divine. It encourages us to strive for greatness while remaining rooted in our origins, making it relevant in various aspects of life, from community engagement to personal aspirations.
