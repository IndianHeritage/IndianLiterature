आयम् अग्निः उरुष्यति अमृताद् इव जन्मनः |सहसश् चित् सहीयान् देवो जीवातवे कृतः ||
ayam agnir uruṣyaty amṛtād iva janmanaḥ | sahasaś cit sahīyān devo jīvātave kṛtaḥ ||
  • ayam agnir: This phrase translates to "this fire." In the context of the RigVeda, fire is often seen as a symbol of energy, transformation, and divine presence.
  • uruṣyaty: This means "is powerful" or "is strong." It signifies the potency and strength of the fire, which can be interpreted as a metaphor for knowledge or spiritual enlightenment.
  • amṛtād iva: This translates to "like immortality." Here, fire is compared to the concept of immortality, suggesting that the knowledge or energy derived from it is eternal and transcendent.
  • janmanaḥ: This means "of birth" or "of creation." It indicates that this fire or energy is fundamental to the process of creation and existence.
  • sahasaś cit: This phrase can be understood as "with great wisdom." It emphasizes the idea that the fire (or knowledge) is accompanied by profound understanding and insight.
  • sahīyān: This means "enduring" or "sustaining." It suggests that the wisdom and energy derived from this fire are not fleeting but rather lasting and supportive.
  • devo: This translates to "the divine" or "the god." It indicates that the fire is not just a physical element but also has a divine aspect, representing higher consciousness or spiritual truth.
  • jīvātave: This means "for the living." It signifies that the knowledge and energy from this fire are meant to sustain life and promote well-being.
  • kṛtaḥ: This translates to "created" or "made." It implies that this divine energy or wisdom is actively generated and can be harnessed for various purposes.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of fire as a symbol of strength, wisdom, and divine energy that is essential for creation and sustenance of life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment. Just as fire transforms materials, knowledge transforms individuals and societies. The mantra encourages us to seek wisdom and understanding, recognizing that these qualities are vital for personal growth and the betterment of the community. It serves as a reminder that the pursuit of knowledge is a divine endeavor that can lead to a more fulfilling and meaningful life.
