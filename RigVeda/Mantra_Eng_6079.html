प्र चक्रे सहसा सहो बभञ्ज मन्युम ओजसा |विश्वे त इन्द्र पृतनायवो यहो न वृक्षाऽ इव येमिरे ||
pra cakre sahasā saho babhañja manyum ojasā | viśve ta indra pṛtanāyavo yaho ni vṛkṣā iva yemire ||
  • pra cakre: This phrase can be translated as "he created" or "he established." It signifies the act of bringing something into existence or forming a structure.
  • sahasā: This means "with strength" or "with power." It emphasizes the forceful and vigorous nature of the action being described.
  • saho: This translates to "of strength" or "of courage." It highlights the importance of resilience and bravery in the face of challenges.
  • babhañja: This word means "he broke" or "he shattered." It indicates a decisive action that leads to the dismantling of obstacles or barriers.
  • manyum: This refers to "anger" or "wrath." It suggests the emotional state that can drive one to take action, often in a context of conflict or struggle.
  • ojasā: This translates to "with energy" or "with vitality." It signifies the dynamic force that propels actions forward.
  • viśve: This means "all" or "universal." It indicates inclusivity and the idea that the action or effect is widespread.
  • ta: This is a pronoun meaning "that" or "those," referring to the subjects of the mantra.
  • indra: Indra is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, rain, and thunder. He represents the force that overcomes challenges.
  • pṛtanāyavo: This can be interpreted as "those who are striving" or "those who are making efforts." It emphasizes the importance of perseverance.
  • yaho: This means "where" or "when," indicating a context or situation.
  • ni: This is a preposition meaning "down" or "towards," suggesting a direction or focus.
  • vṛkṣā: This translates to "trees." In a metaphorical sense, trees can represent stability, growth, and the interconnectedness of life.
  • iva: This means "like" or "as," drawing a comparison.
  • yemire: This means "they stood" or "they remained," indicating a state of being or presence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the themes of strength, resilience, and the power of collective effort in overcoming obstacles. It highlights the importance of courage and energy in the face of challenges, much like how a community comes together to support one another during difficult times. In real life, this can be seen in various scenarios, such as social movements, team sports, or even personal struggles where individuals draw upon their inner strength and the support of others to achieve their goals. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that through unity and determination, we can break through barriers and create a positive impact in our lives and the lives of those around us.
