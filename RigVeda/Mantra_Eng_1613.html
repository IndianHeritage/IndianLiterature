आ वाम ऋताया केशिनीर अनूषत मित्र यत्र वरुण गातुम आर्चथः |अव तमनā सृजतम पिन्वतं धियो युवं विप्रस्य मनमनाम इराज्यथः ||
ā vām ṛtāya keśinīr anūṣata mitra yatra varuṇa gātum arcathaḥ | ava tmanā sṛjatam pinvataṁ dhiyo yuvaṁ viprasya manmanām irajyathaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vām: From you both
    • ṛtāya: For the truth
    • keśinīr: With flowing hair (often referring to the divine or celestial beings)
    • anūṣata: Who are to be worshipped
    • mitra: Friend
    • yatra: Where
    • varuṇa: The cosmic order or the god of water
    • gātum: To sing or to praise
    • arcathaḥ: You are being worshipped
    • ava: Down
    • tmanā: With your own self
    • sṛjatam: Let it be created
    • pinvataṁ: Nourishing
    • dhiyo: Intellects
    • yuvaṁ: Young
    • viprasya: Of the wise
    • manmanām: Of the minds
    • irajyathaḥ: May you be inspired
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine energies represented by Mitra and Varuṇa, emphasizing the importance of truth and friendship.
    • The phrase "ā vām ṛtāya" suggests a call for unity and collaboration in the pursuit of truth.
    • By referring to "keśinīr," it acknowledges the divine presence that guides and protects.
    • The mantra encourages the worship of these divine forces, recognizing their role in maintaining cosmic order.
    • The line "ava tmanā sṛjatam pinvataṁ dhiyo" highlights the need for nurturing intellect and wisdom, particularly among the youth.
    • It suggests that through the inspiration of divine forces, individuals can cultivate their minds and contribute positively to society.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied during gatherings where individuals come together to discuss and promote truth and friendship, fostering a sense of unity.
    • In educational institutions, it can inspire students to seek knowledge and wisdom, encouraging them to work collaboratively for a common goal.
    • In personal relationships, the essence of this mantra can guide individuals to nurture their friendships and support each other in their pursuits of truth and understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of truth, friendship, and the nurturing of intellect.
    • It serves as a reminder of the divine guidance available to those who seek wisdom and understanding.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages collaboration, support, and the pursuit of knowledge, making it relevant in various aspects of life, from personal relationships to community engagement.
