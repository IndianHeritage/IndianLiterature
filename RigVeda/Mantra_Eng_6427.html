तस्येद arvanto रंहयन्त आशीवस तस्य द्युम्नितमं यशः |न तं अंहो देवकृतं कुतश्चन न मार्त्यकृतं नशत ||
tasyed arvanto raṁhayanta āśavas tasya dyumnitamaṁ yaśaḥ | na tam aṁho devakṛtaṁ kutaś cana na martyakṛtaṁ naśat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tasyed: of that
    • arvanto: powerful, strong
    • raṁhayanta: bringing joy, happiness
    • āśavas: swift, quick
    • tasya: of that
    • dyumnitamaṁ: most glorious, radiant
    • yaśaḥ: fame, glory
    • na: not
    • tam: that
    • aṁho: of the mortal
    • devakṛtaṁ: made by the gods
    • kutaś: from where
    • cana: at all
    • na: not
    • martyakṛtaṁ: made by mortals
    • naśat: of the hundred
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power and glory that comes from divine sources rather than from human efforts.
    • The phrase "tasyed arvanto raṁhayanta" suggests that those who are strong and powerful derive their strength from a higher, divine source.
    • The mention of "dyumnitamaṁ yaśaḥ" emphasizes that true glory and fame are not merely the result of human actions but are bestowed by the divine.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "na tam aṁho devakṛtaṁ kutaś cana," indicates that the achievements made by mortals are insignificant compared to those granted by the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the context of leadership, a successful leader often attributes their achievements to the support of their team and the guidance of mentors, reflecting the idea that true power comes from collaboration and divine inspiration.
    • In sports, athletes may speak of their victories as being a result of hard work, but they often also mention the role of faith, luck, or divine intervention in their success.
    • In personal life, individuals who practice gratitude often recognize that their happiness and achievements are not solely due to their efforts but also the blessings they receive from the universe or a higher power.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder that while human effort is important, it is the divine grace and support that ultimately lead to true strength and glory.
    • It encourages humility, urging individuals to recognize the limitations of human endeavors and the significance of divine influence in their lives.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in daily life by fostering a sense of gratitude and acknowledging the support we receive from others and the universe, leading to a more fulfilled and harmonious existence.
