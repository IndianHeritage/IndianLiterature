मो षू ण इन्द्रात्र पृत्सु देवैर अस्ति हि ष्मा ते शुष्मिन्न अवयाः |महश्चिद यस्य मीळ्हुषो यव्याः हविष्मतो मरुतो वन्दते गीः ||
mo ṣū ṇa indrātra pṛtsu devair asti hi ṣmā te śuṣminn avayāḥ | mahaś cid yasya mīḻhuṣo yavyā haviṣmato maruto vandate gīḥ ||
  • mo ṣū ṇa indrātra pṛtsu devair asti hi ṣmā te śuṣminn avayāḥ
    • mo ṣū ṇa: This phrase can be interpreted as a call for strength or support. In real life, this can be likened to seeking help from friends or family during challenging times.
    • indrātra: Referring to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power and authority. This can be compared to leaders or mentors in our lives who guide us.
    • pṛtsu devair: This means 'with the gods' or 'among the divine beings.' It signifies the importance of community and collaboration, much like teamwork in a workplace.
    • asti hi ṣmā te: Translates to 'there is indeed your presence.' This emphasizes the idea of divine support, akin to feeling the presence of loved ones during tough times.
    • śuṣminn avayāḥ: This can be understood as 'in the state of being dry or barren.' It reflects the struggles one faces, similar to experiencing a drought in life, whether emotionally or materially.
  • mahaś cid yasya mīḻhuṣo yavyā haviṣmato maruto vandate gīḥ
    • mahaś cid: This phrase suggests 'greatness' or 'vastness.' It can be related to the vast potential within each individual, encouraging self-belief.
    • yasya mīḻhuṣo: Meaning 'whose essence is sweet or pleasant.' This can be likened to the positive impact of kindness and compassion in our interactions.
    • yavyā haviṣmato: Refers to offerings or sacrifices made with intention. In real life, this can be seen in acts of service or charity, where one gives selflessly for the benefit of others.
    • maruto: This refers to the storm gods, symbolizing strength and energy. It can be compared to the challenges we face that test our resilience.
    • vandate gīḥ: This translates to 'the hymns that praise.' It signifies the importance of gratitude and recognition, similar to acknowledging the efforts of those who support us.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support and recognizing the interconnectedness of life. It teaches us the importance of community, resilience, and gratitude. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied when facing challenges, reminding us to seek help, appreciate our support systems, and remain hopeful in times of adversity. The mantra encourages us to acknowledge the divine presence in our lives and to act with kindness and intention, fostering a sense of unity and strength in our communities.
