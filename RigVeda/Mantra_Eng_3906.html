आ यः सोमेन जठरम् अपिप्रतामन्दत मगवाऽ मध्वोऽन्धसः |यद ईम मृगाय हन्तवे महावधः सहस्रभृष्टिम उशनā वधं यमत ||
ā yaḥ somena jaṭharam apipratāmandata maghavā madhvo andhasaḥ | yad īm mṛgāya hantave mahāvadhaḥ sahasrabhṛṣṭim uśanā vadhaṁ yamat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yaḥ: "He who" or "that which"
    • somena: "with the soma" (a sacred drink in Vedic rituals)
    • jaṭharam: "the belly" or "the stomach"
    • apipratāmandata: "has been filled" or "has been satisfied"
    • maghavā: "the powerful one" or "the great one" (often referring to Indra)
    • madhvo: "of sweetness" or "of delight"
    • andhasaḥ: "of the blind" or "of darkness"
    • yad: "that which"
    • īm: "we desire" or "we seek"
    • mṛgāya: "for the deer" or "for the creatures"
    • hantave: "to be killed" or "to be sacrificed"
    • mahāvadhaḥ: "the great sacrifice" or "the great offering"
    • sahasrabhṛṣṭim: "with a thousand powers" or "with great strength"
    • uśanā: "with the light" or "with brightness"
    • vadhaṁ: "the killing" or "the sacrifice"
    • yamat: "that which is controlled" or "that which is restrained"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power of the soma, a drink that symbolizes immortality and divine energy.
    • It highlights the relationship between the divine (Indra) and the natural world, emphasizing the need for balance and respect in the act of sacrifice.
    • The mention of "the great sacrifice" suggests that life involves giving and taking, and that sacrifices are necessary for sustenance and growth.
    • In real life, this can be seen in the way communities come together to support one another, often sacrificing individual desires for the greater good.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of sacrifice, and the pursuit of sweetness and joy in existence.
    • It teaches us that true power comes from understanding and respecting the balance of nature and the divine.
    • In modern life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as environmental conservation, community service, and personal relationships, where we must often sacrifice for the benefit of others.
