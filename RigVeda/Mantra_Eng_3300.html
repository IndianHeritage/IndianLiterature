सत्रा यद ईं भारवरस्य वृष्णः सिषक्ति शुष्मः स्तुवते भराय |गुहा यद ईं औशिजस्य गोहे प्र यद धिये प्रायसे मदाय ||
satrā yad īm bhārvarasya vṛṣṇaḥ siṣakti śuṣmaḥ stuvate bharāya | guhā yad īm auśijasya gohe pra yad dhiye prāyase madāya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • satrā: In the context of the mantra, it refers to the "sacrifice" or "offering."
    • yad: This means "that" or "which."
    • īm: This can be interpreted as "we" or "us."
    • bhārvarasya: This refers to "the one who is powerful" or "the mighty."
    • vṛṣṇaḥ: This is often associated with "strength" or "the strong one."
    • siṣakti: This means "to empower" or "to give strength."
    • śuṣmaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the subtle" or "the refined."
    • stuvate: This means "to praise" or "to worship."
    • bharāya: This refers to "to bear" or "to carry."
    • guhā: This means "cave" or "hidden place."
    • auśijasya: This refers to "the one who is born of the light" or "radiant."
    • gohe: This means "in the cow" or "in the essence."
    • pra: This means "before" or "in front of."
    • yad: Again, "that" or "which."
    • dhiyē: This refers to "intellect" or "understanding."
    • prāyase: This means "to strive" or "to endeavor."
    • madāya: This can be interpreted as "for the sake of joy" or "for happiness."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of sacrifice and offering in the pursuit of strength and empowerment.
    • It suggests that through praise and worship, one can connect with the divine power that resides in the subtle and refined aspects of existence.
    • The reference to "guhā" indicates that true understanding and strength may be found in hidden or less obvious places, much like the wisdom that can be gained from introspection.
    • The mantra also highlights the significance of striving for knowledge and understanding, which leads to joy and fulfillment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team that collaborates and sacrifices individual interests for the collective goal often achieves greater success, reflecting the essence of "satrā."
    • In personal development, individuals who engage in self-reflection and seek knowledge often find deeper insights about themselves, akin to the "guhā" mentioned in the mantra.
    • In spiritual practices, rituals and offerings are made to honor the divine, which can lead to a sense of empowerment and connection to a higher purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of sacrifice, praise, and the pursuit of knowledge in achieving personal and collective strength.
    • Its significance lies in the understanding that true empowerment comes from both external offerings and internal introspection.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to greater fulfillment, joy, and a deeper connection with oneself and the universe.
