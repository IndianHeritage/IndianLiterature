आर्यमणं वरुणं मित्रम् एषाम् इन्द्राविष्णू मरुतो अश्विनोता |स्वश्वो अग्ने सुरथः सुराधा एद उ वह सुहविषे जनाय ||
aryamaṇaṁ varuṇam mitram eṣām indrāviṣṇū maruto aśvinota | svaśvo agne surathaḥ surādhā ed u vaha suhaviṣe janāya ||
  • aryamaṇaṁ: This term refers to the concept of a noble or virtuous being. In real life, it can be associated with individuals who embody integrity and righteousness, serving as role models in society.
  • varuṇam: Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water. This can be related to the importance of maintaining balance in nature and respecting the environment, as water is essential for life.
  • mitram: Meaning 'friend', this word emphasizes the value of friendship and alliances in our lives. Real-life examples include the support systems we build with friends and family, which are crucial during challenging times.
  • eṣām: This word translates to 'of these', indicating a connection or relationship among the entities mentioned.
  • indrāviṣṇū: Referring to Indra and Vishnu, two significant deities in Hinduism. Indra represents strength and power, while Vishnu symbolizes preservation and protection. In life, this duality can be seen in the balance between assertiveness and compassion in leadership.
  • maruto: The Maruts are storm deities, representing the forces of nature. This can be likened to the unpredictable aspects of life, reminding us to be adaptable and resilient in the face of challenges.
  • aśvinota: This term refers to the Aśvins, twin gods associated with health and medicine. Their significance in real life can be seen in the importance of healthcare and well-being, highlighting the need for physical and mental health in our lives.
  • svaśvo: Meaning 'our own', this word emphasizes the importance of personal responsibility and ownership in our actions and decisions.
  • agne: Agni is the fire god, symbolizing energy and transformation. In real life, this can be related to the passion and drive we have in pursuing our goals and aspirations.
  • surathaḥ: This term means 'well-being' or 'prosperity', indicating the importance of striving for a fulfilling and prosperous life.
  • surādhā: Referring to 'good fortune' or 'blessings', this highlights the role of positivity and gratitude in our lives, encouraging us to appreciate the good things we have.
  • ed u vaha: This phrase can be interpreted as 'bring forth' or 'carry forth', suggesting the importance of action and initiative in achieving our goals.
  • suhaviṣe: Meaning 'for the good', this emphasizes the intention behind our actions, encouraging us to act with kindness and compassion.
  • janāya: This term means 'for the people', highlighting the importance of community and collective well-being in our endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of harmony, friendship, and the balance between strength and compassion. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of acting with integrity and purpose. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as fostering healthy relationships, promoting environmental sustainability, and striving for personal and communal well-being. By embodying the values represented in this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
