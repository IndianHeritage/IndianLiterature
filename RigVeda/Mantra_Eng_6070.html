मन्त्र: निर अग्नयो रुरुचुर निर उ सूर्यो निः सोम इन्द्रियो रसः |निर अन्तरिक्षाद अधमो महाम् अहिं कृषे तद इन्द्र पौंश्यम् ||
nir agnayo rurucur nir u sūryo niḥ soma indriyo rasaḥ | nir antarikṣād adhamo mahām ahiṁ kṛṣe tad indra pauṁsyam ||
  • nir agnayo rurucur: This phrase can be translated as "without the fire, they are not burning." Here, "agnayo" refers to fire, which symbolizes energy, passion, and transformation. In real life, this can be likened to the absence of motivation or drive in a person's life. For example, someone who is not pursuing their passions may feel stagnant, much like a fire that has gone out.
  • nir u sūryo: This translates to "without the sun." The sun represents light, knowledge, and life. In practical terms, this can be seen in the context of education and enlightenment. For instance, a society lacking access to education may struggle to progress, similar to a world without sunlight.
  • niḥ soma indriyo rasaḥ: This means "the essence of the moon is without the senses." Here, "soma" refers to the moon, which symbolizes calmness, intuition, and emotional depth. The phrase suggests that without sensory experiences, one cannot fully appreciate the essence of life. For example, a person who isolates themselves from social interactions may miss out on the richness of human experience.
  • nir antarikṣād adhamo: This translates to "without the space, the lowest." "Antarikṣa" refers to the space or the cosmos, indicating that without a broader perspective, one may feel limited or confined. In real life, this can be related to individuals who do not seek to expand their horizons, such as those who remain in their comfort zones and miss opportunities for growth.
  • mahām ahiṁ kṛṣe tad indra pauṁsyam: This phrase means "the great serpent, Indra, is the essence of masculinity." Here, "ahiṁ" refers to a serpent, which can symbolize wisdom and transformation. Indra represents strength and power. In a real-life context, this can be interpreted as the importance of harnessing one's inner strength and wisdom to navigate challenges, much like a leader who must balance power with responsibility.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of various elements in life—energy, knowledge, emotional depth, perspective, and strength. It signifies that to lead a fulfilling life, one must cultivate these aspects. In practical scenarios, this can be applied in personal development, education, and leadership. By recognizing the importance of motivation, knowledge, emotional intelligence, and a broader perspective, individuals can strive for a more balanced and enriched existence.
