इषं तोकाय नो दधद अस्मभ्यं सोम विश्वतः |आ पवस्व सहस्रिणम् ||
iṣaṁ tokāya no dadhad asmabhyaṁ soma viśvataḥ | ā pavasva sahasriṇam ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • iṣaṁ: This word can be interpreted as "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the abundance that one seeks in life.
    • tokāya: This translates to "for the offspring" or "for the progeny," indicating a desire for continuity and legacy.
    • no: This means "for us," suggesting a collective aspiration or need.
    • dadhad: This word means "may grant" or "may bestow," indicating a request or prayer for blessings.
    • asmabhyaṁ: This translates to "to us," reinforcing the personal and communal aspect of the prayer.
    • soma: Soma refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing nourishment, vitality, and divine blessings.
    • viśvataḥ: This means "from all directions" or "universally," indicating a desire for blessings that encompass all aspects of life.
    • ā: This is a preposition meaning "towards" or "to," indicating directionality in the prayer.
    • pavasva: This translates to "may you purify" or "may you cleanse," suggesting a desire for spiritual and physical purification.
    • sahasriṇam: This means "a thousand," symbolizing abundance and the vastness of blessings sought.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for prosperity and blessings, not just for oneself but for future generations.
    • It emphasizes the importance of community and shared aspirations, highlighting that the well-being of one is interconnected with the well-being of all.
    • The invocation of Soma signifies a request for divine nourishment and vitality, essential for both physical and spiritual growth.
    • The desire for blessings from all directions suggests an openness to receiving good fortune and support from various sources.
    • The call for purification indicates a need for clarity and cleansing, both in a spiritual sense and in the context of one's life circumstances.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, this mantra can be recited during significant events like weddings or childbirth, symbolizing the hope for prosperity and well-being for future generations.
    • In a community gathering, such as a festival or a religious ceremony, this mantra can be used to invoke collective blessings, emphasizing unity and shared aspirations.
    • In personal practice, individuals may recite this mantra during meditation or prayer to seek clarity and abundance in their lives, fostering a mindset of gratitude and openness to blessings.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra "iṣaṁ tokāya no dadhad asmabhyaṁ soma viśvataḥ ā pavasva sahasriṇam" serves as a profound reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of community, and the pursuit of prosperity.
    • Its significance lies in the holistic approach to well-being, encompassing physical, spiritual, and communal aspects.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, from personal aspirations to communal gatherings, reinforcing the idea that blessings and prosperity are best when shared.
