पिबा वर्धस्व तव घा सुतास इन्द्र सोमासः प्रथमā उतेमे |यथापिभः पूर्व्यां इन्द्र सोमां एव पाही पण्यो अद्यā नवीयान ||
pibā vardhasva tava ghā sutāsa indra somāsaḥ prathamā uteme | yathāpibaḥ pūrvyām̐ indra somām̐ evā pāhi panyo adyā navīyān ||
  • Mantra: "pibā vardhasva tava ghā sutāsa indra somāsaḥ prathamā uteme | yathāpibaḥ pūrvyām̐ indra somām̐ evā pāhi panyo adyā navīyān ||"
  • Translation: "Drink, grow strong, O Indra, the first of the Soma, as you have drunk before; protect us today with your new strength."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pibā: Drink
    • vardhasva: Grow strong
    • tava: Your
    • ghā: Strength
    • sutāsa: Of the Soma
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • somāsaḥ: Of the Soma
    • prathamā: First
    • uteme: We invoke
    • yathāpibaḥ: As you have drunk
    • pūrvyaṁ: Before
    • pāhi: Protect
    • panyo: Us
    • adyā: Today
    • navīyān: New strength
  • Context and Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who is often associated with strength and power.
    • The reference to "Soma" indicates a ritual drink that was believed to confer immortality and strength, symbolizing the divine energy that sustains life.
    • The invocation of Indra to "drink" and "grow strong" reflects a desire for divine support and empowerment in overcoming challenges.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a metaphor for seeking strength and resilience in the face of adversity.
    • For example, athletes often invoke a sense of inner strength and focus before competitions, akin to calling upon divine support.
    • In personal development, individuals may use this mantra as a reminder to draw upon their inner resources and seek guidance during difficult times.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking strength and protection from higher powers, which can be relevant in various aspects of life, including personal challenges, professional endeavors, and spiritual growth.
    • By invoking the qualities of Indra and the essence of Soma, individuals can cultivate a mindset of resilience and empowerment, making this mantra applicable in everyday life.
