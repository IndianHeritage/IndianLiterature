सख्ये त इन्द्र वाजिनो मा भीम शवसस पते |त्वाम अभि प्र णोनुमो जेतारम अपराजितम् ||
sakhye ta indra vājino mā bhema śavasas pate | tvām abhi pra ṇonumo jetāram aparājitam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sakhye: In friendship or alliance
    • ta: That
    • indra: O Indra, the king of gods
    • vājino: The one who bestows strength and power
    • mā: Do not
    • bhema: Fear or be afraid
    • śavasas: Of the dead or the weak
    • pate: O lord or master
    • tvām: You
    • abhi: Towards or for
    • pra ṇonumo: We bow down or offer our prayers
    • jetāram: The conqueror or victor
    • aparājitam: Unconquered or invincible
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the chief deity in the Rigveda, who represents strength, power, and victory. The speaker expresses a desire for friendship and alliance with Indra, seeking his protection and strength. The plea is to not be afraid of the weak or the dead, emphasizing the importance of courage and resilience. The speaker acknowledges Indra as the unconquered victor and offers prayers to him for support and guidance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal challenges, such as facing a difficult situation at work or in relationships, individuals can invoke the spirit of this mantra by seeking strength and courage, much like how one would turn to a mentor or a supportive friend for guidance.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied when groups come together to support each other in overcoming obstacles, fostering a sense of unity and collective strength against adversity.
    • In sports, athletes often seek the support of their teammates and coaches, embodying the essence of this mantra by relying on each other’s strengths to achieve victory.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, courage, and the importance of alliances in overcoming challenges. It teaches us that in the face of fear and adversity, we should seek support from those around us and draw upon our inner strength. This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal struggles to collective efforts in communities, reminding us that we are stronger together and that we can conquer our fears with the help of others.
