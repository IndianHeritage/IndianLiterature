विनः सहस्रं शुरुधो रदन्त्व ऋतावानो वरुणो मित्रो अग्निः |यच्छन्तु चन्द्रा उपमं नो अर्कम आ नः कामं पूपुरन्तु स्तवानाः ||
vi naḥ sahasraṁ śurudho radantv ṛtāvāno varuṇo mitro agniḥ | yacchantu candrā upamaṁ no arkam ā naḥ kāmam pūpurantu stavānāḥ ||
  • Translation: "May the thousand-eyed Varuna, the friend, and Agni, the one who knows the truth, grant us the desires that we seek, just as the moon grants its light."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vi naḥ: "May" - a plea for blessings.
    • sahasraṁ: "thousand" - signifies abundance and vastness.
    • śurudho: "eyed" - refers to the all-seeing nature of the divine.
    • radantv: "may they roar" - a call for strength and power.
    • ṛtāvāno: "truthful" - emphasizes the importance of truth in our lives.
    • varuṇo: "Varuna" - the god of cosmic order and water.
    • mitro: "friend" - signifies companionship and support.
    • agniḥ: "Agni" - the fire god, representing energy and transformation.
    • yacchantu: "may they grant" - a request for blessings.
    • candrā: "moon" - symbolizes calmness and serenity.
    • upamaṁ: "like" - a comparison to the moon's light.
    • no: "to us" - indicating the recipients of the blessings.
    • arkam: "light" - represents knowledge and enlightenment.
    • ā: "to" - a preposition indicating direction.
    • naḥ: "our" - indicating possession.
    • kāmam: "desires" - refers to our aspirations and needs.
    • pūpurantu: "may they fulfill" - a wish for the realization of desires.
    • stavānāḥ: "praises" - signifies the importance of gratitude and reverence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of uncertainty, individuals often seek guidance and support from friends and family, akin to invoking the divine friendship of Mitra.
    • When facing challenges, people may turn to their inner strength, represented by Agni, to transform their circumstances.
    • In moments of reflection, one might look to the moon for inspiration, reminding them of the calmness and clarity that can be found in nature.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking divine blessings for abundance, truth, and companionship. It highlights the interconnectedness of desires and the natural world, encouraging individuals to cultivate gratitude and reverence in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during personal challenges, communal gatherings, or moments of introspection, serving as a reminder of the support available from both the divine and the community.
