अश्वी रथी सुरूप इद गोमां इद इन्द्र ते सखा |श्वात्रभाजा वयसा सचते सदा चन्द्रो याती सभाम् उप ||
aśvī rathī surūpa id gomām̐ id indra te sakhā | śvātrabhājā vayasā sacate sadā candro yāti sabhām upa ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvī: horse, symbolizing strength and speed.
    • rathī: charioteer, representing guidance and control.
    • surūpa: beautiful or well-formed, indicating harmony and balance.
    • id: this, referring to the present moment or situation.
    • gomām̐: cow, a symbol of nourishment and wealth.
    • id: this.
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods, representing power and authority.
    • te: your, indicating possession or connection.
    • sakhā: friend, symbolizing companionship and support.
    • śvātrabhājā: one who is well-provided or prosperous.
    • vayasā: with age or maturity, indicating wisdom.
    • sacate: to sit or reside, suggesting stability.
    • sadā: always, indicating continuity.
    • candro: moon, symbolizing calmness and serenity.
    • yāti: goes or moves, indicating progress.
    • sabhām: assembly or gathering, representing community.
    • upa: towards, indicating direction or purpose.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the imagery of a strong horse and a skilled charioteer, symbolizing the importance of guidance and strength in life.
    • The mention of the cow signifies the importance of sustenance and wealth, which are essential for a prosperous life.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents the ultimate authority and power, suggesting that one should seek divine support in their endeavors.
    • The idea of friendship and companionship emphasizes the value of relationships in achieving success and stability.
    • The reference to the moon indicates the need for calmness and serenity in the journey of life, reminding us to maintain balance amidst challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, having a mentor (the charioteer) can guide you through challenges, much like a skilled driver navigating a chariot.
    • The importance of community support can be seen in teamwork, where collaboration leads to shared success.
    • In personal life, nurturing relationships (friends and family) can provide emotional sustenance, akin to the nourishment provided by cows.
    • Maintaining a calm demeanor (like the moon) during stressful situations can lead to better decision-making and outcomes.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, guidance, companionship, and serenity in navigating life's journey.
    • It teaches us to seek support from others and the divine, while also reminding us to maintain balance and calmness in our pursuits.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to a more fulfilling and successful existence, both personally and professionally.
