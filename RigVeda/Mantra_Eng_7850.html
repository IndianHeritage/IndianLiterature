या इमे रोधसी मही सम मातरेव दोहते |मादेषु सर्वधाऽसि ||
ya ime rodasī mahī sam mātareva dohate | madeṣu sarvadhā asi ||
  • Mantra: "ya ime rodasī mahī sam mātareva dohate | madeṣu sarvadhā asi ||"
  • Translation: "You, O Earth, who are like a mother, nourish all beings in every way."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ya: who
    • ime: these
    • rodasī: Earth or the expanse
    • mahī: great or vast
    • sam: together or alike
    • mātareva: like a mother
    • dohate: nourish or provide sustenance
    • madeṣu: in all beings
    • sarvadhā: in every way
    • asi: you are

  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the nurturing aspect of the Earth, likening it to a mother who provides for her children.
    • It reflects the interconnectedness of all life forms and the essential role of the Earth in sustaining them.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers rely on the Earth to provide nutrients for crops, showcasing the nurturing aspect of the land.
    • Environmental conservation efforts aim to protect the Earth, recognizing its role as a life-giving force for all beings.
    • In many cultures, rituals and practices are performed to honor the Earth, acknowledging its importance in sustaining life.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of our responsibility to care for the Earth, which, like a mother, provides for us in countless ways.
    • It encourages a sense of gratitude and respect towards nature, urging us to live in harmony with our environment.
    • In a world facing environmental challenges, this mantra is particularly relevant, highlighting the need for sustainable practices and a deeper connection to the Earth.
