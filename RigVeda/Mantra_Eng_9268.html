यस्ते अद्य कृतवद्भद्रशोचेपूपं देव घृतवंतमाग्ने |प्र तं नय प्रतरं वस्यो अच्छाभि सुम्नं देवभक्तं यविष्ट ||
yas te adya kṛṇavad bhadraśoce 'pūpaṁ deva ghṛtavantam agne | pra taṁ naya prataraṁ vasyo acchābhi sumnaṁ devabhaktaṁ yaviṣṭha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • adya: today
    • kṛṇavad: has made
    • bhadraśoce: auspicious and bright
    • apūpaṁ: without blemish
    • deva: O God
    • ghṛtavantam: full of ghee (clarified butter)
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • pra: forward
    • taṁ: that
    • naya: lead
    • prataraṁ: across
    • vasyo: to the one who is worthy
    • acchābhi: with good looks
    • sumnaṁ: auspicious
    • devabhaktaṁ: devoted to the gods
    • yaviṣṭha: the best
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for guidance and blessings.
    • The speaker acknowledges the auspiciousness and purity of the offerings made to Agni.
    • It emphasizes the importance of devotion and the desire for a prosperous and fulfilling life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a traditional Hindu wedding, offerings are made to Agni, symbolizing the divine witness to the union and seeking blessings for a harmonious life.
    • During festivals like Diwali, people light lamps and offer prayers to Agni, representing the removal of darkness and the welcoming of prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and blessings in our daily lives.
    • It teaches us to approach life with purity, devotion, and a desire for auspiciousness.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in rituals, personal prayers, and moments of seeking clarity and direction in challenging times.
