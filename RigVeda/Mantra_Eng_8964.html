इमं जीवेषु परिधिं दधामि मैषां नु गाद अपरो अर्थम् एतम् |शतम् जीवन्तु शरदः पुरूचीर अन्तर मृत्युः दधताम् पर्वतेन ||
imaṁ jīvebhyaḥ paridhiṁ dadhāmi maiṣāṁ nu gād aparo artham etam | śataṁ jīvantu śaradaḥ purūcīr antar mṛtyuṁ dadhatām parvatena ||
  • Translation: "I create a boundary for these living beings; may they not be harmed by any other force. Let them live a hundred years, and may they be protected from death like a mountain."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • imaṁ: this
    • jīvebhyaḥ: for the living beings
    • paridhiṁ: a boundary or protection
    • dadhāmi: I create or establish
    • maiṣāṁ: may they not be harmed
    • nu: indeed or certainly
    • gād: by any other force
    • aparo: another or different
    • artham: purpose or meaning
    • etam: this
    • śataṁ: a hundred
    • jīvantu: may they live
    • śaradaḥ: for many years
    • purūcīr: protected or shielded
    • antar: from within
    • mṛtyuṁ: death
    • dadhatām: may they hold or bear
    • parvatena: like a mountain
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of protection and longevity for all living beings.
    • It reflects a deep understanding of the interconnectedness of life and the need for safeguarding it from external threats.
    • The imagery of a mountain symbolizes strength and stability, suggesting that just as mountains withstand the test of time, so too should living beings be granted resilience against mortality.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in healthcare, where the focus is on ensuring the well-being and longevity of patients.
    • In environmental conservation, it serves as a reminder of the need to protect ecosystems and biodiversity, creating boundaries against harmful practices.
    • In personal life, it encourages individuals to create safe spaces for themselves and others, fostering a supportive community that promotes health and longevity.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of our responsibility to protect life and promote well-being.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can contribute to a more harmonious existence, ensuring that all living beings thrive and flourish.
