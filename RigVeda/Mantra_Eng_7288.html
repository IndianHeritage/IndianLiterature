दुहन्ति सप्तैकाम उप द्वा पञ्च सृजतः |तीर्थे सिन्धोर अधि स्वरे ||
duhanti saptaikām upa dvā pañca sṛjataḥ | tīrthe sindhor adhi svare ||
  • Mantra: "duhanti saptaikām upa dvā pañca sṛjataḥ | tīrthe sindhor adhi svare ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • duhanti: they churn or extract
    • saptaikām: seven (sapta) in one (aikām)
    • upa: near or towards
    • dvā: two (dvā)
    • pañca: five (pañca)
    • sṛjataḥ: from the creation or the act of creating
    • tīrthe: in the sacred place or at the shore
    • sindhor: of the ocean or river (sindhu)
    • adhi: above or over
    • svare: in the sound or in the voice
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a process of churning or extracting something valuable from a combination of elements.
    • The phrase "saptaikām" suggests the unity of seven elements into one, which can symbolize the integration of various aspects of life or nature.
    • The mention of "dvā pañca" indicates a duality that leads to a quintessence, perhaps referring to the balance between two forces that creates a greater whole.
    • The reference to "tīrthe sindhor" implies a sacred space, often associated with rivers or oceans, which are vital sources of life and nourishment.
    • "Adhi svare" suggests that this process or creation is elevated or exists in a higher realm, possibly indicating spiritual or divine significance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, the process of churning can be likened to the way rivers carve through landscapes, creating fertile plains and ecosystems, symbolizing the transformative power of water.
    • In personal development, the integration of various life experiences (the seven aspects) can lead to a more profound understanding of oneself, akin to the process of self-reflection and growth.
    • The balance of dualities, such as work and life, can lead to a harmonious existence, where both aspects contribute to overall well-being.
    • In spiritual practices, sacred spaces (like temples or natural sites) are often places where individuals seek to connect with the divine, reflecting the "tīrthe sindhor" aspect of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of integration, balance, and the sacredness of natural elements in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that through the process of churning our experiences and understanding the dualities of life, we can create a more unified and meaningful existence.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to seek harmony in their lives, recognize the sacredness in their surroundings, and strive for personal growth through reflection and integration.
