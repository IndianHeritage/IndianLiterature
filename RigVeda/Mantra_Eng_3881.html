आयं जनाऽभिचक्षे जगामेन्द्रः सखायं सुतसोममिच्छन् |वदन ग्रावाव वेदिम्भ्रियाते यस्य जीरम् अध्वर्यवश्चरन्ति ||
āyaṁ janā abhicakṣe jagāmendraḥ sakhāyaṁ sutasomam icchan | vadan grāvāva vedim bhriyāte yasya jīram adhvaryavaś caranti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āyaṁ: this, here
    • janā: people, beings
    • abhicakṣe: may they be blessed, may they be fulfilled
    • jagām: have come, arrived
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods
    • sakhāyaṁ: as a friend, companion
    • sutasomam: the son of Soma, referring to the moon or nectar
    • icchan: desiring, wishing
    • vadan: speaking, uttering
    • grāvāva: the sacred fire, the altar
    • vedim: the knowledge, the Vedic wisdom
    • bhriyāte: is being offered, is being given
    • yasya: of whom, whose
    • jīram: life, vitality
    • adhvaryavaḥ: the priests, the ritual performers
    • caranti: they perform, they act
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the arrival of people who are blessed and fulfilled.
    • It invokes Indra, the king of gods, as a friend and companion, highlighting the importance of divine support in human endeavors.
    • The reference to "sutasomam" suggests a connection to nourishment and vitality, akin to the moon's influence on life.
    • The mantra emphasizes the significance of speaking and sharing knowledge, particularly Vedic wisdom, in rituals and daily life.
    • It acknowledges the role of priests and ritual performers in sustaining life and vitality through their actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In community gatherings, the presence of supportive friends and family can create a nurturing environment, similar to the blessings invoked in the mantra.
    • In professional settings, having a mentor or guide (akin to Indra) can significantly impact one's success and fulfillment.
    • Rituals and traditions in various cultures often emphasize the sharing of knowledge and wisdom, reflecting the importance of communication highlighted in the mantra.
    • In personal development, seeking nourishment for the mind and spirit, much like the "sutasomam," can lead to a more fulfilling life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of community, divine support, and the sharing of knowledge in achieving fulfillment and vitality in life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, emphasizing the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine.
    • By invoking these principles, one can cultivate a more harmonious and prosperous existence, reflecting the essence of the mantra in daily life.
