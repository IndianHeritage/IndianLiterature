तवे धर्माण आसते जुहूभिः सिञ्चतीर इव |कृष्णा रूपाण्य अर्जुनā वि वो मदे विश्वा अधि श्रियो धिषे विवक्षसे ||
tve dharmāṇa āsate juhūbhiḥ siñcatīr iva | kṛṣṇā rūpāṇy arjunā vi vo made viśvā adhi śriyo dhiṣe vivakṣase ||
  • tve dharmāṇa āsate: This phrase can be translated as "You (O Divine) are the essence of righteousness." Here, "tve" refers to the divine or the ultimate reality, while "dharmāṇa" signifies the principles of dharma or righteousness. In real life, this can be related to the idea that ethical behavior and moral values are foundational to a harmonious society.
  • juhūbhiḥ siñcatīr iva: This part means "like those who pour offerings." The act of "juhūbhiḥ" refers to the ritual of offering, often seen in various religious practices. For example, in Hindu rituals, offerings are made to deities to seek blessings. This signifies the importance of devotion and the act of giving in our lives.
  • kṛṣṇā rūpāṇy arjunā: Translated, this means "the forms of the black (or dark) ones, O Arjuna." "Kṛṣṇā" can refer to the divine, often associated with Lord Krishna, who embodies love and compassion. "Arjunā" refers to Arjuna, a key figure in the Mahabharata. This highlights the relationship between the divine and the seeker, emphasizing that understanding and embodying divine qualities is essential for personal growth.
  • vi vo made: This translates to "in your joy." It suggests that the divine presence brings joy and fulfillment. In real life, this can be seen in moments of happiness and contentment that arise from spiritual practices, community gatherings, or acts of kindness.
  • viśvā adhi śriyo dhiṣe: This means "to the universal glory." "Viśvā" refers to the universe or all-encompassing nature, while "śriyo" signifies prosperity or beauty. This part emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the pursuit of collective well-being. In practical terms, it can relate to global efforts for peace, sustainability, and social justice.
  • vivakṣase: This translates to "you express" or "you desire." It indicates the divine will or intention. In our lives, this can be interpreted as the importance of aligning our desires with higher principles and values, striving for a purpose that transcends individual gain.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of righteousness, devotion, and the pursuit of universal well-being. It serves as a reminder of the importance of ethical living, the joy derived from spiritual practices, and the interconnectedness of all beings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in community service, uphold moral values, and seek a deeper connection with the divine, ultimately contributing to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
