आसु षमा णो मगवन्न इन्द्र पृत्स्व अस्मभ्यम् महि वरिवः सुगं कः |अपां तोकस्य तनयस्य जेष इन्द्र सूरीन कृणुहि स्मा नो अर्धम् ||
āsu ṣmā ṇo maghavann indra pṛtsv a1smabhyam mahi varivaḥ sugaṁ kaḥ | apāṁ tokasya tanayasya jeṣa indra sūrīn kṛṇuhi smā no ardham ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āsu: quickly, swiftly
    • ṣmā: may we have, may we attain
    • ṇo: our
    • maghavann: O mighty one (referring to Indra)
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • pṛtsv: in the world
    • a1smabhyam: for us
    • mahi: great
    • varivaḥ: bestowing, granting
    • sugaṁ: good fortune, prosperity
    • kaḥ: who
    • apāṁ: of waters
    • tokasya: of the offspring
    • tanayasya: of the son
    • jeṣa: victory
    • indra: Indra
    • sūrīn: of the heroes
    • kṛṇuhi: grant, bestow
    • smā: may we
    • no: our
    • ardham: half, part
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, asking for swift blessings and prosperity.
    • The speaker seeks Indra's favor for good fortune and victory, particularly in the context of offspring and their success.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine support in achieving goals and overcoming challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be likened to seeking the blessings of a mentor or a leader who can guide and support one's endeavors.
    • In personal life, it reflects the desire for parental support in achieving educational or career goals, akin to asking for guidance and blessings from family.
    • In community settings, it can represent collective prayers for prosperity and success in local initiatives or projects.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine intervention and support in human endeavors.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness between individuals and the divine, emphasizing the need for humility and gratitude.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek help and guidance from others, recognizing that success often comes through collaboration and support.
    • Ultimately, it teaches the value of striving for prosperity not just for oneself, but for the community and future generations.
