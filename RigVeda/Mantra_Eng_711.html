इन्द्रस्याङ्गिरसां चेष्टौ विदत्सरमा तनयाय धासिम् | बृहस्पतिर भिनद अद्रिं विदद गाः सम उष्रियाभिर वावशन्त नरः ||
indrasyāṅgirasāṁ ceṣṭau vidat saramā tanayāya dhāsim | bṛhaspatir bhinad adriṁ vidad gāḥ sam usriyābhir vāvaśanta naraḥ ||
  • Indrasyāṅgirasāṁ ceṣṭau: This phrase refers to the actions or movements associated with Indra and the Angirasas, who are considered powerful deities in Vedic literature. Indra is often seen as the king of the gods, representing strength and leadership.
  • Vidat saramā tanayāya dhāsim: Here, "vidat" means to know or to understand, while "saramā" refers to a female dog, often symbolizing loyalty and protection. "Tanayāya" means for the offspring, and "dhāsim" implies servitude or dedication. This part of the mantra emphasizes the importance of loyalty and understanding in relationships, particularly in the context of family and community.
  • Bṛhaspatir bhinad adriṁ: "Bṛhaspatir" refers to Brihaspati, the guru of the gods and a symbol of wisdom and knowledge. "Bhinad adriṁ" means to break or overcome the mountain, which can symbolize overcoming obstacles or challenges in life.
  • Vidad gāḥ sam usriyābhir vāvaśanta naraḥ: This part translates to "the man who knows the cows (gāḥ) and is surrounded by the auspicious (usriyābhir) will prosper." Cows in Vedic culture symbolize wealth and nourishment, while "naraḥ" refers to a man or human being. This emphasizes the importance of knowledge and auspiciousness in achieving prosperity.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, loyalty, wisdom, and prosperity. It highlights the interconnectedness of these qualities in achieving success and overcoming challenges in life.
  • Real-life Application: In modern contexts, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:
    • In leadership roles, understanding the importance of loyalty and dedication to one's team can lead to better collaboration and success.
    • In personal relationships, fostering loyalty and understanding can strengthen bonds within families and communities.
    • In business, overcoming challenges with wisdom and knowledge can lead to prosperity and growth.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the virtues that lead to a fulfilling and prosperous life. By embodying the qualities of leadership, loyalty, wisdom, and the pursuit of knowledge, individuals can navigate the complexities of life and achieve their goals.
