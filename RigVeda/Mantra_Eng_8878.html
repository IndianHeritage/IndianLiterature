मन्त्र: बतो बतासि यम नैव ते मनो हृदयं चाविदाम | अन्याऽकिल त्वां कक्ष्येव युक्तं परि स्वजाते लिबुजेव वृक्षम् ||
bato batāsi yama naiva te mano hṛdayaṁ cāvidāma | anyā kila tvāṁ kakṣyeva yuktam pari ṣvajāte libujeva vṛkṣam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bato: O friend
    • batāsi: you speak
    • yama: O restraint or control
    • naiva: not at all
    • te: your
    • mano: mind
    • hṛdayaṁ: heart
    • cāvidāma: we do not know
    • anyā: other
    • kila: indeed
    • tvāṁ: you
    • kakṣyeva: like a tree
    • yuktam: connected or joined
    • pari: all around
    • ṣvajāte: embraces
    • libujeva: like a flower
    • vṛkṣam: tree
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a deep connection between individuals, emphasizing the importance of understanding and emotional bonds.
    • The phrase "O friend, you speak" suggests a dialogue or communication that is essential in relationships.
    • The mention of "restraint" indicates the need for self-control and awareness in interactions.
    • By stating "we do not know your heart," it highlights the complexity of understanding another person's feelings and thoughts.
    • The imagery of a tree and a flower symbolizes growth, nurturing, and the beauty of relationships that require care and attention.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In friendships, open communication is vital. Just as the mantra suggests, we must strive to understand each other's feelings and thoughts to strengthen our bonds.
    • In a workplace, collaboration and restraint in discussions can lead to a more harmonious environment, where everyone's ideas are valued.
    • In family relationships, nurturing connections, much like caring for a tree, can lead to a flourishing family dynamic.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of communication, understanding, and emotional connection in all relationships.
    • It teaches us that while we may not fully understand another's heart, our efforts to connect and communicate can lead to deeper bonds.
    • In real life, applying these principles can enhance our relationships, whether with friends, family, or colleagues, fostering a sense of community and support.
