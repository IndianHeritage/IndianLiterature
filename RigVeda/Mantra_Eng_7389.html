मां नः सोम सं विविजो मां वि बीभीषथा राजन |मा नो हार्दि त्विषा वधिः ||
mā naḥ soma saṁ vīvijo mā vi bībhiṣathā rājan | mā no hārdi tviṣā vadhīḥ ||
  • mā naḥ soma saṁ vīvijo: This phrase can be translated as "May the Soma not separate us." Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing nourishment, vitality, and divine inspiration. In a real-life context, this can be understood as a plea for unity and harmony among individuals or communities, emphasizing the importance of shared experiences and connections.
  • mā vi bībhiṣathā rājan: This translates to "Do not let fear come upon us, O King." Here, "rājan" refers to a ruler or leader. This line highlights the need for protection and guidance from those in authority. In modern terms, it can be related to the role of leaders in alleviating fears and providing security to their people, whether in a political, social, or familial context.
  • mā no hārdi tviṣā vadhīḥ: This means "May we not be harmed by your brilliance." The term "tviṣā" refers to brilliance or radiance, which can symbolize power or authority. This line suggests a desire for the powerful to use their strength wisely and benevolently, ensuring that their actions do not cause harm. In everyday life, this can be seen in the expectation that leaders and influential figures act responsibly and ethically.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about unity, the role of leadership, and the responsible use of power. It emphasizes the importance of community and the need for leaders to foster an environment free from fear and harm. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in governance, community building, and interpersonal relationships, reminding us of the collective responsibility to nurture harmony and protect one another from fear and harm. It serves as a timeless reminder of the values of compassion, unity, and ethical leadership in our daily lives.
