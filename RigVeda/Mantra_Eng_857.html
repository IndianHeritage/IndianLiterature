यो अध्वरेषु शान्तम ऋतावा होता तं ऊ नमोभिर आ कृत्नुध्वम् |अग्निर यद वेर मर्ताय देवान स च बोधाति मनसा यजाती ||
yo adhvareṣu śaṁtama ṛtāvā hotā tam ū namobhir ā kṛṇudhvam | agnir yad ver martāya devān sa cā bodhāti manasā yajāti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo - who
    • adhvareṣu - in the sacrifices
    • śaṁtama - the peaceful
    • ṛtāvā - the one who is in accordance with truth
    • hotā - the priest or the one who invokes
    • tam - him
    • ū - indeed
    • namobhir - with reverence
    • ā - to
    • kṛṇudhvam - you should offer
    • agnir - fire
    • yat - which
    • ver - for the mortal
    • martāya - for the human beings
    • devān - to the gods
    • sa - he
    • cā - and
    • bodhāti - understands
    • manasā - with the mind
    • yajāti - offers
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of performing sacrifices with a peaceful and truthful mindset.
    • The invocation of fire (Agni) symbolizes the divine presence and the connection between the earthly and the spiritual realms.
    • The priest (hotā) is encouraged to offer reverence and devotion, which is essential in any spiritual practice.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, whether religious or personal, the intention behind the action is crucial. For instance, when lighting a candle during a prayer, the act should be accompanied by a peaceful and sincere mindset.
    • In community gatherings, such as weddings or festivals, the role of the officiant (similar to the hotā) is to invoke blessings with a focus on truth and harmony, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of intention and reverence in spiritual practices.
    • It teaches that true offerings are made not just through rituals but through the mindset and purity of heart.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as personal growth, community service, and any act of kindness, where the intention behind the action is what truly matters.
