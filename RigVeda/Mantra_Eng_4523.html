त्वं नः पाहि अम्हसो जातवेदो अग्हायतः |रक्षा नो ब्रह्मणस कवे ||
tvaṁ naḥ pāhy aṁhaso jātavedo aghāyataḥ | rakṣā ṇo brahmaṇas kave ||
  • tvaṁ naḥ - "You (O Agni) are ours": This phrase addresses Agni, the fire god, indicating a personal relationship and a plea for protection.
  • pāhy - "protect": A request for safeguarding, highlighting the importance of security and safety in life.
  • aṁhaso - "from our sins": This refers to the transgressions or wrongdoings that may lead to negative consequences. It emphasizes the need for purification and redemption.
  • jātavedo - "O Jātavedas": Another name for Agni, meaning "the one who knows all births." This signifies the omniscience of Agni, who is aware of all actions and their consequences.
  • aghāyataḥ - "from the evil": A plea to be saved from malevolent forces or harmful influences in life.
  • rakṣā - "protection": Reiterating the need for divine safeguarding in various aspects of life.
  • ṇo - "for us": This personalizes the request, making it clear that the protection is sought for the individual or community.
  • brahmaṇas - "of the wise": Referring to the learned or knowledgeable individuals, suggesting that wisdom is a source of strength and guidance.
  • kave - "O poet": Acknowledging the creative and expressive power of Agni, linking it to inspiration and enlightenment.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of seeking protection from divine forces, particularly from Agni, who symbolizes knowledge, purity, and transformation. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In times of personal crisis, individuals may seek spiritual guidance or protection through prayer or meditation, invoking the qualities of Agni.
  • In community settings, collective prayers for safety and wisdom can foster a sense of unity and shared purpose.
  • In the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, individuals can reflect on their actions and seek to rectify their mistakes, thus embodying the essence of the mantra.

This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, protection, and the need for moral integrity in our lives.
