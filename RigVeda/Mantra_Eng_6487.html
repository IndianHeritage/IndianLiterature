आ याहिम इन्दवो 'श्वपते गोपता उर्वरापते |सोमं सोमपते पिब ||
ā yāhīma indavo 'śvapate gopata urvarāpate | somaṁ somapate piba ||
  • Mantra: "ā yāhīma indavo 'śvapate gopata urvarāpate | somaṁ somapate piba ||"
  • Translation: "O Indra, the lord of the horses, the protector of the cows, come to us. Drink the soma, O lord of the soma."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yāhīma: "Come to us" - A call for divine presence and assistance.
    • indavo: "Indra" - The king of gods, associated with strength and power.
    • 'śvapate: "lord of the horses" - Symbolizing speed, strength, and nobility.
    • gopata: "protector of the cows" - Cows are sacred in Vedic culture, representing wealth and nourishment.
    • urvarāpate: "lord of the fields" - Acknowledging the importance of agriculture and fertility.
    • somaṁ: "soma" - A sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight.
    • somapate: "lord of the soma" - Referring to the divine essence and the spiritual benefits of soma.
    • piba: "drink" - A request for the divine to partake in the soma, symbolizing communion with the divine.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer for divine intervention and support in various aspects of life, including strength, protection, and prosperity.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents the power to overcome obstacles and challenges, much like how individuals seek strength in times of difficulty.
    • The reference to cows highlights the importance of sustenance and nurturing, akin to how communities rely on agriculture and livestock for survival.
    • The soma drink symbolizes the pursuit of higher knowledge and spiritual awakening, reflecting the human quest for enlightenment and understanding.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be invoked during challenging times, such as personal struggles or community crises, to seek strength and guidance.
    • It can also be relevant in agricultural practices, where farmers may seek blessings for a bountiful harvest and protection for their livestock.
    • On a spiritual level, individuals may use this mantra during meditation or rituals to connect with higher consciousness and seek wisdom.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support in various facets of life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of strength, sustenance, and spiritual growth.
    • Its relevance spans across different contexts, reminding us of the importance of community, agriculture, and the pursuit of knowledge in our daily lives.
