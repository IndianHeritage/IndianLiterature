महि शिविष्ट नस् कृधि संचक्षे भुजे अस्यै |महि स्तोत्रिभ्यः मगवन सुवीर्यम् मथीर उग्रो न शवसः ||
mahi śaviṣṭha nas kṛdhi saṁcakṣe bhuje asyai | mahi stotṛbhyo maghavan suvīryam mathīr ugro na śavasā ||
  • mahi: This word translates to "great" or "mighty." It signifies the vastness and power of the universe.
  • śaviṣṭha: This term means "the best" or "the most excellent." It refers to the highest qualities or attributes.
  • nas: This means "to us" or "for us," indicating a personal connection or appeal.
  • kṛdhi: This translates to "do" or "perform," suggesting an action or a request for action.
  • saṁcakṣe: This word means "to gather" or "to assemble," indicating a coming together of forces or energies.
  • bhuje: This translates to "in the arms" or "in the embrace," symbolizing support and protection.
  • asyai: This means "for this" or "for this purpose," indicating a specific intention or goal.
  • stotṛbhyo: This term refers to "those who praise" or "the worshippers," highlighting the importance of devotion and reverence.
  • maghavan: This translates to "the generous one" or "the bountiful," often referring to the divine or a deity that provides abundance.
  • suvīryam: This means "good strength" or "excellent power," indicating the qualities of resilience and capability.
  • mathīr: This translates to "intellect" or "wisdom," emphasizing the importance of knowledge and understanding.
  • ugro: This means "fierce" or "powerful," suggesting a strong and dynamic force.
  • na: This translates to "not," indicating a negation or a contrast.
  • śavasā: This term means "of the dead" or "of the lifeless," suggesting a connection to life and vitality.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for the divine to act with great strength and wisdom, gathering support and protection for those who praise and worship. It emphasizes the importance of community and collective strength, as well as the need for divine assistance in achieving one's goals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Community Support: When individuals come together for a common cause, they can invoke the spirit of this mantra to seek strength and unity.
  • Personal Challenges: In times of difficulty, one can recite this mantra to seek divine guidance and support, fostering resilience and wisdom.
  • Spiritual Practices: During rituals or prayers, this mantra can be used to enhance the connection with the divine and to express gratitude for the abundance in life.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for collective strength, wisdom, and divine support. It serves as a reminder of the power of community and the importance of seeking guidance in our endeavors. By incorporating this mantra into daily life, individuals can cultivate a sense of purpose, resilience, and connection to the greater universe.
