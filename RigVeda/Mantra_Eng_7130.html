प्रभङ्गी शूरो मगवाऽतुवीमघः सम्मिश्लो वीर्याय कम् |उभा ते बाहू वृषणा शतक्रतो न यां वज्रम मिमिक्षतुः ||
prabhaṅgī śūro maghavā tuvīmaghaḥ sammiślo viryāya kam | ubhā te bāhū vṛṣaṇā śatakrato ni yā vajram mimikṣatuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prabhaṅgī: Radiant or shining.
    • śūro: Brave or heroic.
    • maghavā: Mighty or powerful.
    • tuvīmaghaḥ: You who are endowed with strength.
    • sammiślo: United or harmonious.
    • viryāya: For strength or valor.
    • kam: To whom or for whom.
    • ubhā: Both or two.
    • te: Your.
    • bāhū: Arms.
    • vṛṣaṇā: Powerful or strong.
    • śatakrato: One who has a hundred powers (referring to Indra).
    • ni: May.
    • yā: Who.
    • vajram: Thunderbolt or weapon.
    • mimikṣatuḥ: May strike or may be directed.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the qualities of bravery, strength, and unity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of having powerful arms (both physical and metaphorical) to achieve one's goals.
    • The reference to "śatakrato" highlights the divine strength and capabilities of Indra, the king of gods, symbolizing the ultimate power and authority.
    • The mantra suggests that when united in purpose and strength, individuals can overcome challenges and adversities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, whether in sports or business, the collective strength and unity of the team can lead to success, much like the "ubhā te bāhū" aspect of the mantra.
    • In personal development, individuals often seek to cultivate their inner strength and bravery to face life's challenges, resonating with the qualities of "viryāya" and "maghavā."
    • In community efforts, when people come together with a shared purpose, they can achieve remarkable outcomes, reflecting the essence of "sammiślo" or harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity, strength, and bravery in overcoming obstacles.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal challenges to collective efforts in communities and organizations.
    • By embodying the qualities of the mantra, individuals and groups can harness their inner strength and work together towards achieving their goals.
