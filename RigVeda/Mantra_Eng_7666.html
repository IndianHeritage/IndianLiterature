तं arvantaṁ न सानसिं गृणीहि विप्र शुष्मिणम् |मित्रं न यातयजनम् ||
tam arvantaṁ na sānasiṁ gṛṇīhi vipra śuṣmiṇam | mitraṁ na yātayajjanam ||
  • tam arvantaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "that which is worthy" or "that which is to be revered." It signifies the importance of recognizing and honoring what is truly valuable in life.
  • na sānasiṁ: This means "do not be ungrateful" or "do not neglect." It serves as a reminder to appreciate the blessings and resources we have, rather than taking them for granted.
  • gṛṇīhi: This word translates to "sing" or "praise." It emphasizes the act of expressing gratitude and reverence through words and actions, highlighting the importance of vocalizing appreciation.
  • vipra: This term refers to a wise person or a learned individual, often a priest or sage. It suggests that wisdom and knowledge should be sought and respected in our lives.
  • śuṣmiṇam: This can be interpreted as "the one who is radiant" or "the one who shines." It symbolizes the qualities of enlightenment and positivity that one should aspire to embody.
  • mitraṁ: This word means "friend" or "ally." It highlights the importance of relationships and the support we receive from others in our journey through life.
  • na yātayajjanam: This phrase translates to "do not forsake the one who is devoted." It serves as a reminder to value and maintain connections with those who are loyal and supportive.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing and honoring what is valuable in life, expressing gratitude, and valuing wisdom and relationships. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied by practicing gratitude daily, nurturing friendships, and seeking knowledge from wise individuals. By embodying these principles, we can create a more fulfilling and harmonious life, fostering a sense of community and support around us.
