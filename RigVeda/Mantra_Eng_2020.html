तवे अग्नि विश्वे अमृतासो अद्रुहा आस देवाः हविः अदन्त्य आहुतम् |त्वया मर्तासः स्वदन्त आसुतिं त्वं गर्भो वीरुधां जजñeषे शुचिः ||
tve agne viśve amṛtāso adruha āsā devā havir adanty āhutam | tvayā martāsaḥ svadanta āsutiṁ tvaṁ garbho vīrudhāṁ jajñiṣe śuciḥ ||
  • tve agne - "O Agni," refers to the divine fire, symbolizing energy, purity, and the transformative power of fire in rituals and life.
  • viśve amṛtāso - "all beings who are immortal," signifies the universal connection of all life and the eternal nature of the soul.
  • adruha āsā - "without enmity," emphasizes the importance of harmony and peace among all beings.
  • devā havir adanty āhutam - "the gods, the offerings, are unending," highlights the continuous nature of divine blessings and the importance of offerings in spiritual practices.
  • tvayā martāsaḥ - "by you, the mortals," indicates the reliance of human beings on divine energy for sustenance and guidance.
  • svadanta āsutiṁ - "the sweet taste of life," suggests the joy and fulfillment that comes from living in accordance with divine principles.
  • tvaṁ garbho vīrudhāṁ - "you, the womb of all growth," symbolizes the nurturing aspect of the divine, akin to a mother providing for her children.
  • jajñiṣe śuciḥ - "you are born pure," signifies the inherent purity and sanctity of the divine essence.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of divine energy, the interconnectedness of all beings, and the importance of living harmoniously. In real life, it serves as a reminder to foster peace, engage in spiritual practices, and recognize the divine presence in all aspects of life. By acknowledging the purity and nurturing qualities of the divine, individuals can cultivate a sense of gratitude and responsibility towards themselves and others, leading to a more harmonious existence.
