सां मा सत्योक्तिः परि पातु विश्वतो dyāvā च यत्र ततान्न अहानि च |विश्वम् अन्यन् नि विश्वते यद एजति विश्वाहापो विश्वाहोद एति सूर्यः ||
sā mā satyoktiḥ pari pātu viśvato dyāvā ca yatra tatanann ahāni ca | viśvam anyan ni viśate yad ejati viśvāhāpo viśvāhod eti sūryaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sā: that
    • mā: not
    • satyoktiḥ: the truth or the statement of truth
    • pari: all around
    • pātu: may protect
    • viśvato: from all sides
    • dyāvā: the sky or heaven
    • ca: and
    • yatra: where
    • tatanann: it spreads or expands
    • ahāni: days
    • ca: and
    • viśvam: the universe or all
    • anyan: other
    • ni: not
    • viśate: enters
    • yad: that which
    • ejati: moves or vibrates
    • viśvāhāpo: all waters
    • viśvāhod: all that is bright
    • eti: goes
    • sūryaḥ: the sun
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the protective nature of truth and the expansive quality of the universe.
    • It emphasizes the importance of truth in our lives, suggesting that it surrounds us and protects us from all sides.
    • The reference to the sun symbolizes enlightenment, knowledge, and the life-giving force that truth provides.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, fostering a culture of honesty and transparency can lead to better teamwork and productivity.
    • In personal relationships, being truthful strengthens bonds and builds trust, allowing for deeper connections.
    • In education, the pursuit of knowledge and truth can empower individuals to make informed decisions and contribute positively to society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of truth as a protective and guiding force in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to seek truth and embrace it, as it leads to enlightenment and a harmonious existence.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of truthfulness can enhance personal growth, relationships, and societal well-being.
