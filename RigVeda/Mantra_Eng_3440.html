ये हरि मेधयोक्ताः मदन्त इन्द्राय चक्रुः सुयुजा ये अश्वा |ते रायस पोषं द्रविणान्य अस्मे धत्त ऋभवः क्षेमयन्तो न मित्रम् ||
ye harī medhayokthā madanta indrāya cakruḥ suyujā ye aśvā | te rāyas poṣaṁ draviṇāny asme dhatta ṛbhavaḥ kṣemayanto na mitram ||
  • Translation: "Those who, with their intellect, have invoked the divine Indra, who is the master of strength and wealth, and who are well-prepared, may they bestow upon us prosperity and wealth, bringing us safety and friendship."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: Those who
    • harī: Divine or auspicious
    • medhayokthā: With intellect or wisdom
    • madanta: Invoked or called upon
    • indrāya: To Indra, the god of rain and thunder
    • cakruḥ: Have made or created
    • suyujā: Well-prepared or well-connected
    • te: They
    • rāyas: Prosperity or wealth
    • poṣaṁ: Nourishment or sustenance
    • draviṇāny: Wealth or resources
    • asme: For us
    • dhatta: Bestow or grant
    • ṛbhavaḥ: Abundant or prosperous
    • kṣemayanto: Bringing safety or well-being
    • na: Not
    • mitram: Friendship or ally
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team that collaborates effectively and uses their collective intellect to solve problems can be seen as invoking the spirit of Indra, leading to prosperity for the organization.
    • In personal relationships, those who nurture their connections and seek mutual support can create a network of friendship and safety, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • Communities that come together to support each other during crises, such as natural disasters, embody the principles of invoking divine protection and prosperity.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, collaboration, and divine invocation in achieving prosperity and safety. It teaches us that by working together and invoking higher powers, we can create a supportive environment that fosters growth and well-being. This principle is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, community building, and professional endeavors, reminding us that collective effort and divine guidance can lead to a prosperous and harmonious existence.
