ते अस्मभ्यं शर्म यं सन्नं अमृताः मार्त्येभ्यः |बाधमानाः अप द्विषः ||
te asmabhyaṁ śarma yaṁsann amṛtā martyebhyaḥ | bādhamānā apa dviṣaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • te: to you
    • asmabhyaṁ: for us
    • śarma: protection, comfort, or shelter
    • yaṁsann: who is present or who is here
    • amṛtā: immortal, eternal
    • martyebhyaḥ: from mortals, or from the mortal realm
    • bādhamānā: being troubled or afflicted
    • apa: away from
    • dviṣaḥ: enemies or those who wish harm
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer for divine protection and comfort.
    • It seeks the presence of the eternal, immortal essence to shield us from the troubles and afflictions that arise from the mortal world.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking refuge from negativity and harmful influences.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, such as loss or grief, individuals often seek comfort through spiritual practices, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In a workplace setting, when facing competition or negativity from colleagues, one might invoke this mantra to seek protection and maintain a positive environment.
    • During challenging times, such as illness or financial difficulties, people may turn to meditation or prayer, embodying the essence of seeking shelter from afflictions.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking divine protection and comfort in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to turn towards the eternal and immortal aspects of existence when faced with challenges.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of inner peace and resilience against external negativity, making it applicable in various life scenarios, from personal struggles to professional challenges.
