मन्त्र: कृष्णप्रुतौ वेविजे अस्य सक्षिता उभा तरेते अभि मातरा शिशुम् | प्राचाजिह्वं ध्वसयन्तं तृषुच्युतम् आ साच्यं कुपयं वर्धनम् पितुः ||
kṛṣṇaprutau vevije asya sakṣitā ubhā tarete abhi mātarā śiśum | prācājihvaṁ dhvasayantaṁ tṛṣucyutam ā sācyaṁ kupayaṁ vardhanam pituḥ ||
  • kṛṣṇaprutau: This term can be broken down into "kṛṣṇa" meaning black or dark, and "prutau" which refers to the earth or ground. This can symbolize the nurturing aspect of the earth, which is essential for life.
  • vevije: This word means to vibrate or resonate. It signifies the dynamic nature of existence and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • asya: Translated as "of this," it indicates possession or belonging, emphasizing the relationship between the elements of nature and life.
  • sakṣitā: This term means "witness" or "observer." It suggests a higher consciousness or awareness that observes the unfolding of life.
  • ubhā: Meaning "both," it signifies duality, which is a fundamental aspect of existence, such as life and death, joy and sorrow.
  • tarete: This word means "to cross over" or "to transcend." It implies overcoming obstacles or challenges in life.
  • abhi: This term can mean "towards" or "in the direction of," indicating movement or progress.
  • mātarā: Meaning "mothers," it symbolizes nurturing, care, and the life-giving aspect of femininity.
  • śiśum: This word means "child" or "infant," representing new beginnings, innocence, and potential.
  • prācājihvaṁ: This term refers to "the ancient tongue" or "the primordial voice," suggesting the importance of communication and expression in human experience.
  • dhvasayantaṁ: Meaning "to destroy" or "to remove," it indicates the act of letting go of negativity or obstacles.
  • tṛṣucyutam: This term can be interpreted as "thirsting" or "yearning," representing the human desire for fulfillment and understanding.
  • ā sācyaṁ: Meaning "to the truth," it emphasizes the pursuit of truth and authenticity in life.
  • kupayaṁ: This word refers to "the well" or "source," symbolizing the depths of knowledge and wisdom that can be drawn upon.
  • vardhanam: Meaning "to nourish" or "to increase," it signifies growth, development, and the nurturing of potential.
  • pituḥ: This term means "of the father," representing authority, guidance, and the protective aspect of masculinity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing, both from the earth and from parental figures. It highlights the journey of life, where one must witness, transcend, and seek truth while being nourished by the wisdom of the past. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, parenting, and the pursuit of knowledge. It serves as a reminder to embrace both the nurturing aspects of life and the challenges that come with it, ultimately guiding individuals towards a deeper understanding of themselves and their place in the universe.
