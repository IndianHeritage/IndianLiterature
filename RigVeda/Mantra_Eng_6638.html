वि नो देवासो अद्रुहो 'च्छिद्रं शर्म यच्छत |न यद दूराद वसवो नू चिद अन्तितो वरूथम आदधर्षति ||
vi no devāso adruho 'cchidraṁ śarma yacchata | na yad dūrād vasavo nū cid antito varūtham ādadharṣati ||
  • Translation: "May the gods, who are not hostile, grant us protection and shelter. Let not the Vasus, from afar, bring any harm to us, nor let Varuna, the all-encompassing, cause us distress."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vi no: for us
    • devāso: gods
    • adruho: not hostile
    • 'cchidraṁ: protection or shelter
    • śarma: comfort or safety
    • yacchata: grant or bestow
    • na yad: let not
    • dūrād: from afar
    • vasavo: the Vasus (deities of abundance)
    • nū cid: nor let
    • antito: all-encompassing
    • varūtham: Varuna (deity of cosmic order)
    • ādadharṣati: cause distress or harm
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes the benevolence of the gods, emphasizing the importance of divine protection in our lives.
    • The mention of "Vasus" and "Varuna" highlights the need for safeguarding against both material and spiritual adversities.
    • In real life, this can be related to seeking protection from negative influences, whether they be from people, circumstances, or even our own thoughts.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of crisis, such as during a natural disaster, people often turn to prayer or rituals to seek divine protection, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal relationships, one might seek the support of friends and family to create a safe and nurturing environment, akin to the "śarma" or shelter mentioned in the mantra.
    • In a professional setting, fostering a positive work culture can be seen as a way to ensure that the "Vasus" do not bring harm, promoting collaboration and support among colleagues.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking protection and support from higher powers and the community around us.
    • Its significance lies in the universal human desire for safety and comfort, applicable in various aspects of life, from personal to communal.
    • By invoking the blessings of the gods, we acknowledge our vulnerabilities and the need for divine and social support in navigating life's challenges.
