त्वं ह त्यद ऋणया इन्द्र धीरः असिर न पर्व वृजिना शृणासि | प्र ये मित्रस्य वरुणस्य धाम युजं न जनाः मिनन्ति मित्रम् ||
tvaṁ ha tyad ṛṇayā indra dhīro 'sir na parva vṛjinā śṛṇāsi | pra ye mitrasya varuṇasya dhāma yujaṁ na janā minanti mitram ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • ha: indeed
    • tyad: that
    • ṛṇayā: by the debt
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • dhīro: wise
    • 'sir: you have cut
    • na: not
    • parva: the bond
    • vṛjinā: by the sin
    • śṛṇāsi: you hear
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, invoking his wisdom and power to cut through the bonds of sin and debt.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine intervention in overcoming obstacles and challenges in life.
    • The reference to "mitrasya varuṇasya dhāma" suggests the harmony between the forces of nature and the divine, highlighting the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal crisis, individuals often seek guidance and support from higher powers or spiritual practices, similar to invoking Indra for wisdom.
    • In a community facing challenges, collective prayers or rituals can be seen as a way to seek divine assistance, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • When individuals face moral dilemmas or debts, they may turn to ethical teachings or spiritual leaders for guidance, akin to the wisdom sought from Indra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine wisdom and support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It teaches us that through faith and connection with the divine, we can find the strength to cut through our bonds of sin and debt.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in moments of difficulty, encouraging individuals to seek higher guidance and maintain harmony in their lives.
