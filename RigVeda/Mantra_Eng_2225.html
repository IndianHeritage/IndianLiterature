मन्त्र: सुनीतिभिर नयसि त्रायसे जनं यस तुभ्यं दाशान न तं अंहो अश्नवत् | ब्रह्मद्विषस् तपनो मन्युमीर असि बृहस्पते महि तत् ते महित्वनम् ||
sunītibhir nayasi trāyase janaṁ yas tubhyaṁ dāśān na tam aṁho aśnavat | brahmadviṣas tapano manyumīr asi bṛhaspate mahi tat te mahitvanam ||
  • sunītibhir nayasi: This phrase translates to "You lead with good guidance." It emphasizes the importance of wise leadership and the ability to guide others towards a positive path. In real life, this can be seen in effective leaders who inspire their teams through clear vision and ethical decision-making.
  • trāyase janaṁ: This means "You protect the people." It highlights the role of a protector or guardian. For example, in a community, a local leader or a government official who ensures the safety and well-being of citizens embodies this aspect.
  • yas tubhyaṁ dāśān: This translates to "To whom we offer our devotion." It signifies the act of devotion and respect towards a higher power or authority. In everyday life, this can be reflected in the respect shown to mentors, teachers, or spiritual leaders who guide us.
  • na tam aṁho aśnavat: This phrase means "Do not let us be harmed." It expresses a plea for protection from harm or negativity. In practical terms, this can relate to seeking safety in various aspects of life, such as physical safety, emotional well-being, or protection from adverse situations.
  • brahmadviṣas tapano: This translates to "You are the one who overcomes the enemies of knowledge." It refers to the idea of overcoming ignorance and negativity. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of education and wisdom, where individuals strive to dispel ignorance in themselves and others.
  • manyumīr asi: This means "You are the one who calms anger." It emphasizes the importance of peace and tranquility. In daily life, this can be observed in conflict resolution, where individuals or mediators work to calm tensions and foster understanding among conflicting parties.
  • bṛhaspate mahi tat te mahitvanam: This translates to "O Bṛhaspati, may your greatness be magnified." Bṛhaspati is often associated with wisdom and knowledge. This line signifies the aspiration for wisdom to flourish. In real life, this can be seen in the reverence for knowledge and the pursuit of intellectual growth in various fields.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wise leadership, protection, devotion, and the pursuit of knowledge. Its significance lies in the reminder of the values we should uphold in our lives—guiding others with wisdom, protecting those in need, and striving for knowledge while maintaining peace. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in leadership roles, community service, education, and personal development, fostering a harmonious and enlightened society.
