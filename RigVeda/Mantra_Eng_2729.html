मह्य ā ते सख्यं वश्मि शक्तीर ā वृतघ्नि नियुतो यान्ति पूर्वीः |महि स्तोत्रम् अव āगन्म सूरेर अस्माकं सु मगवन बोधि गोपाः ||
mahy ā te sakhyaṁ vaśmi śaktīr ā vṛtraghne niyuto yanti pūrvīḥ | mahi stotram ava āganma sūrer asmākaṁ su maghavan bodhi gopāḥ ||
  • mahy ā te sakhyaṁ: This phrase translates to "I am your friend." It emphasizes the importance of friendship and camaraderie in human relationships. In real life, this can be seen in the bonds formed between colleagues, family members, or even in community groups where mutual support is essential.
  • vaśmi śaktīr: This means "I possess power." It signifies the inner strength and capabilities that individuals have. For example, a person overcoming personal challenges, such as health issues or financial difficulties, demonstrates their inherent power and resilience.
  • ā vṛtraghne: This translates to "O destroyer of obstacles." It refers to the divine force that helps in overcoming challenges. In everyday life, this can be likened to seeking help from mentors or guides who assist in navigating through tough situations, such as a teacher helping a student overcome academic hurdles.
  • niyuto yanti pūrvīḥ: This means "the past leads us." It suggests that our experiences shape our present and future. For instance, learning from past mistakes in business can lead to better decision-making and success in future ventures.
  • mahi stotram: This translates to "great praise." It highlights the importance of acknowledging and appreciating the good in life. In practice, this can be seen in gratitude practices, where individuals reflect on what they are thankful for, fostering a positive mindset.
  • ava āganma: This means "we come together." It signifies unity and collaboration. In real life, this can be observed in community service projects where people unite for a common cause, such as environmental clean-ups or charity events.
  • sūrer asmākaṁ: This translates to "O divine one, ours." It indicates a connection to the divine or higher power. Many people find solace and guidance in their faith or spirituality, which can provide comfort during difficult times.
  • su maghavan: This means "O bountiful one." It refers to abundance and prosperity. In real life, this can be related to the idea of abundance mindset, where individuals focus on opportunities and growth rather than scarcity.
  • bodhi gopāḥ: This translates to "guardians of wisdom." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in guiding our actions. In practice, this can be seen in the role of educators and mentors who impart knowledge to the younger generation, shaping their future.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of friendship, inner strength, overcoming obstacles, learning from the past, unity, divine connection, abundance, and the importance of wisdom. Its significance lies in the reminder that we are not alone in our struggles; we have the power to overcome challenges with the support of friends and the guidance of wisdom. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in personal development, community building, and fostering resilience in the face of adversity. By embodying these values, individuals can create a more harmonious and prosperous life for themselves and those around them.
