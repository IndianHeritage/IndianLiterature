भुवस् त्वं इन्द्र ब्रह्मणा महान भुवो विश्वेषु सवनेषु यज्ञियः |भुवो नृमिंश्च्यौत्नो विश्वस्मिन् भरे ज्येष्ठश्च मन्त्रो विश्वचर्षणे ||
bhuvas tvam indra brahmaṇā mahān bhuvo viśveṣu savaneṣu yajñiyaḥ | bhuvo nṝm̐ś cyautno viśvasmin bhare jyeṣṭhaś ca mantro viśvacarṣaṇe ||
  • Mantra: "bhuvas tvam indra brahmaṇā mahān bhuvo viśveṣu savaneṣu yajñiyaḥ | bhuvo nṝm̐ś cyautno viśvasmin bhare jyeṣṭhaś ca mantro viśvacarṣaṇe ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bhuvas: The realm of existence or the material world.
    • tvam: You (referring to Indra, the king of gods).
    • indra: Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, symbolizing power and strength.
    • brahmaṇā: By the divine or through the supreme consciousness.
    • mahān: Great or mighty.
    • bhuvo: Of the worlds or realms.
    • viśveṣu: In all or among all.
    • savanesu: In the offerings or sacrifices.
    • yajñiyaḥ: Worthy of worship or sacrifice.
    • nṝm̐ś: Men or humanity.
    • cyautno: The one who brings forth or manifests.
    • viśvasmin: In the universe or in all things.
    • bhare: To bear or to support.
    • jyeṣṭhaś: The eldest or the most significant.
    • ca: And.
    • mantro: The mantra or sacred utterance.
    • viśvacarṣaṇe: In the universal manifestation or creation.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, recognizing his greatness and power in the material world.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine consciousness in all realms and offerings, suggesting that all actions should be aligned with a higher purpose.
    • The mention of humanity indicates a collective responsibility to uphold values that resonate with the divine.
    • In real life, this can be seen in community efforts where individuals come together for a common cause, such as environmental conservation or social justice, reflecting the idea of collective action for a greater good.
    • The mantra also highlights the significance of rituals and offerings, which can be interpreted as acts of gratitude and acknowledgment of the forces that sustain life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of aligning our actions with a higher purpose.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize their role in the universe and to contribute positively to society.
    • In practical terms, it can inspire people to engage in community service, environmental stewardship, and other forms of altruism, fostering a sense of unity and shared responsibility.
    • Ultimately, the mantra signifies the power of collective intention and action in creating a harmonious and sustainable world.
