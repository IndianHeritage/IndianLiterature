मन्त्र: त्र्य आर्यमाण मनुषो देवताता त्रि रोचनā दिव्यā धारण्ति |अर्चन्ति त्वां मरुतः पूतदक्षास त्वम एषां ऋषिः इन्द्रासि धीरः ||
try aryamā manuṣo devatātā trī rocanā divyā dhārayanta | arcanti tvā marutaḥ pūtadakṣās tvam eṣām ṛṣir indrāsi dhīraḥ ||
  • try aryamā: This phrase can be translated as "three Aryamas." In Vedic literature, Aryama is often associated with the concept of nobility and righteousness. The number three may symbolize completeness or a triad of virtues.
  • manuṣo devatātā: This translates to "the divine nature of humanity." It emphasizes the connection between humans and the divine, suggesting that humans possess a divine essence or potential.
  • trī rocanā divyā dhārayanta: This means "three radiant divine beings hold." The term "rocanā" refers to brightness or radiance, indicating the presence of divine light or energy that sustains life.
  • arcanti tvā marutaḥ: This translates to "the Maruts worship you." The Maruts are storm deities in Vedic mythology, representing the forces of nature. Their worship signifies the acknowledgment of the divine in natural phenomena.
  • pūtadakṣās: This term means "pure and capable." It highlights the qualities of purity and competence, suggesting that those who are pure in heart and action are favored by the divine.
  • tvam eṣām ṛṣir indrāsi dhīraḥ: This translates to "you are the sage among them, O Indra, the wise one." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with strength and wisdom. This line emphasizes the importance of wisdom and guidance in leadership.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine connection, the importance of purity, and the role of wisdom in leadership. In real life, it serves as a reminder to cultivate noble qualities, recognize the divine in ourselves and nature, and strive for wisdom in our actions. Whether in personal development, community leadership, or environmental stewardship, the principles outlined in this mantra encourage individuals to embody righteousness, purity, and wisdom, fostering a harmonious relationship with the divine and the world around us.
