देवाश्चित्ते असुर्याय पूर्वेऽनु क्षत्राय ममिरे सहांसि। इन्द्रो मग्हानि दयते विषह्येन्द्रं वाजस्य जोहुवन्त सातौ॥
devāś cit te asuryāya pūrve 'nu kṣatrāya mamire sahāṁsi | indro maghāni dayate viṣahyendraṁ vājasya johuvanta sātau ||
  • devāś cit te asuryāya: This phrase translates to "O gods, indeed, you are not of the asura (demonic) nature." It emphasizes the divine qualities of the gods, contrasting them with the asuras, who are often associated with chaos and negativity. In real life, this can be seen in the distinction between positive and negative influences in our lives, where we seek to align ourselves with positive forces.
  • pūrve 'nu kṣatrāya mamire sahāṁsi: This means "the ancient ones (the gods) have come together to support the kṣatra (warrior class)." This highlights the importance of unity and support among those who protect and uphold righteousness. In modern contexts, this can be likened to teamwork in organizations or communities where individuals come together for a common cause, such as social justice or environmental protection.
  • indro maghāni dayate: This translates to "Indra bestows great wealth." Indra, the king of the gods, is often associated with rain and thunder, symbolizing abundance and prosperity. In real life, this can be related to the blessings of nature and the importance of gratitude for the resources we have, such as clean water and fertile land, which are essential for sustenance.
  • viṣahyendraṁ vājasya johuvanta sātau: This means "the strong Indra, who is invoked for strength." It signifies the importance of invoking divine strength and support in our endeavors. In practical terms, this can be seen in how individuals seek motivation and strength through prayer, meditation, or community support when facing challenges in life, such as health issues or personal struggles.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine support and the importance of unity among those who uphold righteousness. It serves as a reminder to align ourselves with positive forces, seek abundance, and invoke strength in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as community building, environmental stewardship, and personal growth, encouraging individuals to work together for a common good while remaining grateful for the blessings they receive.
