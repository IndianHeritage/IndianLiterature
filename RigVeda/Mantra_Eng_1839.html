आरे सावाḥ सुदानवो मरुत ऋञ्जती शरुः |आरे अश्मा यम् अस्यथ ||
āre sā vaḥ sudānavo maruta ṛñjatī śaruḥ | āre aśmā yam asyatha ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āre: O, call or invocation
    • sā: that, she, or it
    • vaḥ: of you, your
    • sudānavo: well-fed, nourished
    • maruta: the storm gods, winds
    • ṛñjatī: making noise, roaring
    • śaruḥ: the arrow, or the weapon
    • aśmā: stone, rock
    • yam: which, that
    • asyatha: you have, you possess
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is an invocation to the Maruts, the storm deities in Vedic literature, who are often associated with strength, power, and the forces of nature.
    • The phrase "āre sā vaḥ sudānavo" can be understood as a call to the well-nourished and powerful forces of nature, asking them to come forth and assist.
    • The mention of "ṛñjatī śaruḥ" suggests a request for the Maruts to bring forth their strength and energy, akin to the sound of thunder or the force of a storm.
    • The latter part, "āre aśmā yam asyatha," indicates a connection to the earth and stability, invoking the strength of stone or rock, which symbolizes endurance and resilience.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often invoke natural forces for favorable weather conditions. This mantra can be seen as a metaphor for seeking the blessings of nature to ensure a good harvest.
    • In personal development, the call to the Maruts can symbolize the need for inner strength and resilience in facing life's challenges, much like how one might seek motivation during tough times.
    • In environmental contexts, this mantra can remind us of the importance of respecting and nurturing the natural world, recognizing the power of the elements and their role in our lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of natural forces, emphasizing the relationship between humanity and the environment.
    • It teaches us to seek strength and nourishment from the world around us, reminding us of our interconnectedness with nature.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire us to cultivate resilience, respect for nature, and a deeper understanding of the forces that shape our existence.
