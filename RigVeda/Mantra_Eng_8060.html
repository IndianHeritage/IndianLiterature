तं हिन्वन्ति मदाच्युतं हरिं नदिषु वाजिनम् |इन्दुम इन्द्राय मत्सरम् ||
taṁ hinvanti madacyutaṁ hariṁ nadīṣu vājinam | indum indrāya matsaram ||
  • taṁ hinvanti: This phrase can be translated as "they invoke him" or "they call upon him." It signifies the act of seeking divine assistance or guidance.
  • madacyutaṁ: This term refers to "the one who is never fallen from bliss" or "the one who is eternally joyful." It highlights the idea of a divine being who embodies eternal happiness and stability.
  • hariṁ: This word is often associated with Lord Vishnu or a divine figure who brings about preservation and sustenance in the universe.
  • nadīṣu: This translates to "in the rivers" or "in the waters," symbolizing the flow of life, nourishment, and the essential nature of water in sustaining existence.
  • vājinam: This term means "the one who is swift" or "the one who moves quickly," often referring to a horse or a chariot, symbolizing strength, speed, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • indum: This word means "the moon," which is often associated with calmness, serenity, and the nurturing aspects of nature.
  • indrāya: This refers to "to Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, representing power, authority, and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • matsaram: This term translates to "jealousy" or "envy," indicating the negative emotions that can arise in human interactions and the importance of overcoming them.

In essence, this mantra speaks to the invocation of divine qualities and the importance of seeking guidance from higher powers. It emphasizes the need for joy, strength, and the nurturing aspects of nature while also acknowledging the challenges posed by negative emotions like jealousy.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in navigating life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in moments of stress or conflict, reminding individuals to seek inner peace and strength while fostering positive emotions. By invoking these qualities, one can strive for a harmonious existence, overcoming obstacles with grace and joy.
