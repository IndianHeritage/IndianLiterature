नū रोधसी अभिष्टुते वसिष्ठैर ऋतावानो वरुणो मित्रो अग्निः | यच्छन्तु चन्द्रा उपमं नो अर्कं यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
nū rodasī abhiṣṭute vasiṣṭhair ṛtāvāno varuṇo mitro agniḥ | yacchantu candrā upamaṁ no arkaṁ yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • nū rodasī abhiṣṭute: This phrase can be translated as "O heavens, we invoke you." Here, "nū" signifies a call or invocation, while "rodasī" refers to the heavens or the sky. This reflects the human desire to connect with the divine and the cosmos.
  • vasiṣṭhair ṛtāvāno: "Vasiṣṭha" refers to the sage Vasiṣṭha, known for his wisdom and knowledge. "Ṛtāvāno" means one who is established in truth or righteousness. This part emphasizes the importance of wisdom and truth in our lives, suggesting that we should seek guidance from those who embody these qualities.
  • varuṇo mitro agniḥ: "Varuṇa" is the god of cosmic order and water, "Mitro" refers to friendship and harmony, and "Agni" is the fire god, symbolizing energy and transformation. Together, they represent the essential elements of life: order, friendship, and transformation. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of maintaining harmony in relationships and the transformative power of knowledge and energy.
  • yacchantu candrā upamaṁ no arkaṁ: This translates to "May the moon grant us a likeness to the sun." The moon symbolizes reflection and calmness, while the sun represents vitality and energy. This line suggests a balance between these two forces in our lives, encouraging us to reflect on our actions and seek enlightenment.
  • yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: "Yūyam" means "you" (referring to the divine), "pāta" means "protect," and "svastibhiḥ" refers to well-being or auspiciousness. This line is a plea for protection and blessings from the divine, emphasizing the need for spiritual guidance and support in our daily lives.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, wisdom, and harmony in life. It highlights the importance of truth, friendship, and transformation, urging individuals to reflect on their actions and strive for balance. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal challenges, in relationships, or when seeking clarity in decision-making. By invoking these divine qualities, individuals can foster a sense of peace, purpose, and well-being in their lives.
