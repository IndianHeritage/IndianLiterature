यज्ञेन गातुम apturo vividrire धियो हिन्वाना उशिजो मनीषिणः |अभिस्वरā निषदा गा अवस्यव इन्द्रे हिन्वाना द्रविणान्य आशत ||
yajñena gātum apturo vividrire dhiyo hinvānā uśijo manīṣiṇaḥ | abhisvarā niṣadā gā avasyava indre hinvānā draviṇāny āśata ||
  • yajñena: This word refers to the concept of sacrifice or offering, which is central to Vedic rituals. In real life, this can be seen in various forms of selfless service or contributions to society, such as volunteering or charitable donations.
  • gātum: This means to go or to move forward. In a practical sense, it can symbolize progress or the journey of life, where individuals strive to achieve their goals and aspirations.
  • apturo: This term signifies the attainment of what is desired or the fulfillment of needs. In everyday life, this can relate to achieving personal or professional goals, such as completing a project or reaching a milestone.
  • vividrire: This word suggests diversity or variety. It highlights the importance of embracing different perspectives and experiences in life, akin to how diverse cultures enrich our understanding of the world.
  • dhiyo: This refers to intellect or understanding. In real life, it emphasizes the value of wisdom and knowledge in making informed decisions, whether in personal relationships or professional endeavors.
  • hinvānā: This term means to inspire or to lead. It can be related to leadership qualities in individuals who motivate others towards a common goal, such as a teacher guiding students or a manager leading a team.
  • uśijo: This word refers to the powerful or the strong. In a practical context, it can symbolize resilience and strength in overcoming challenges, much like athletes who train rigorously to achieve their best performance.
  • manīṣiṇaḥ: This means wise or knowledgeable individuals. In real life, it can refer to mentors or guides who provide valuable insights and support in our personal and professional journeys.
  • abhisvarā: This term signifies the call or the voice of the divine. It can be interpreted as the inner voice or intuition that guides individuals in making choices aligned with their true purpose.
  • niṣadā: This means to sit or to be present. In a practical sense, it emphasizes the importance of mindfulness and being present in the moment, which can enhance our experiences and relationships.
  • gā: This word means to sing or to express joy. In real life, it can symbolize the importance of celebrating achievements and expressing gratitude, whether through music, art, or simple acts of kindness.
  • avasyava: This term refers to the essential or the necessary. It highlights the importance of prioritizing what truly matters in life, such as health, relationships, and personal growth.
  • indre: This word refers to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing power and authority. In real life, it can represent the pursuit of leadership and influence in various spheres, such as community service or corporate leadership.
  • draviṇāny: This means wealth or resources. It emphasizes the importance of utilizing resources wisely and sharing them for the greater good, akin to sustainable practices in business or community development.
  • āśata: This term signifies abundance or prosperity. In practical terms, it can relate to the pursuit of a fulfilling life, where individuals seek not just material wealth but also emotional and spiritual richness.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of living a life of purpose, wisdom, and service. It emphasizes the importance of sacrifice, progress, diversity, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional lives, encouraging them to lead with integrity, embrace diversity, and strive for a balanced and fulfilling existence. By embodying these values, one can contribute positively to society and foster a sense of community and shared purpose.
