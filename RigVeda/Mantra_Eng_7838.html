प्र निम्नेनिव सिन्धवो घ्नन्तो वृत्राणि भूर्णयः |सोमा असृग्म आषवः ||
pra nimneneva sindhavo ghnanto vṛtrāṇi bhūrṇayaḥ | somā asṛgram āśavaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: forward, towards
    • nimneneva: like the flowing (of water)
    • sindhavo: rivers
    • ghnanto: striking, defeating
    • vṛtrāṇi: the obstacles or enemies
    • bhūrṇayaḥ: to make abundant, to increase
    • somā: the soma (a sacred drink)
    • asṛgram: to flow, to pour
    • āśavaḥ: with strength, vigor
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power of rivers, likening their flow to the overcoming of obstacles.
    • The rivers are depicted as forces that strike down the enemies (vṛtrāṇi), symbolizing challenges or negative forces in life.
    • The mention of soma suggests a connection to vitality and strength, indicating that overcoming obstacles leads to abundance and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, one might face challenges such as job loss or health issues. Just as rivers flow and overcome barriers, individuals can find ways to navigate through these difficulties, emerging stronger.
    • In a community setting, when a natural disaster strikes, the collective effort of people can be likened to the flowing rivers that work together to overcome the obstacles posed by nature.
    • In a business environment, companies often face competition (vṛtrāṇi). By innovating and adapting (like the flowing rivers), they can overcome these challenges and thrive.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of resilience and the ability to overcome obstacles in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that, like rivers, we can flow around challenges and emerge victorious.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals and communities to harness their inner strength and work together to achieve abundance and prosperity.
