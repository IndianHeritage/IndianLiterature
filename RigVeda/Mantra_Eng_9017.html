हरī nv asya यावने विदे वस्व इन्द्रो मगैर मगवाऽ वृत्रहā भुवत् |ऋभुर वाजा ऋभुक्षाः पत्यते शवोऽव क्ष्णुं दासस्य नाम चित् ||
harī nv asya yā vane vide vasv indro maghair maghavā vṛtrahā bhuvat | ṛbhur vāja ṛbhukṣāḥ patyate śavo 'va kṣṇaumi dāsasya nāma cit ||
  • Mantra: "harī nv asya yā vane vide vasv indro maghair maghavā vṛtrahā bhuvat | ṛbhur vāja ṛbhukṣāḥ patyate śavo 'va kṣṇaumi dāsasya nāma cit ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • harī: a term often associated with the divine or auspiciousness.
    • nv: indeed or certainly.
    • asya: of this.
    • yā: who or which.
    • vane: in the forest or wilderness.
    • vide: to spread or to expand.
    • vasv: all or universal.
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods, associated with rain and thunderstorms.
    • maghair: with great power or might.
    • maghavā: the powerful one, often referring to Indra.
    • vṛtrahā: the slayer of Vritra, a demon representing drought and obstacles.
    • bhuvat: has become or has manifested.
    • ṛbhur: the one who is rich or bountiful.
    • vāja: wealth or prosperity.
    • ṛbhukṣāḥ: those who are nourished or fed abundantly.
    • patyate: is being served or is being worshipped.
    • śavo: the dead or the lifeless.
    • 'va: or.
    • kṣṇaumi: I invoke or I call upon.
    • dāsasya: of the servant or devotee.
    • nāma: name.
    • cit: consciousness or awareness.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence, particularly that of Indra, who is revered for his strength and ability to overcome obstacles.
    • The reference to "vṛtrahā" signifies the removal of barriers and challenges, akin to how one might seek help in overcoming personal struggles in life.
    • The mention of "vane" and "vide" suggests a connection to nature and the universe, emphasizing the importance of harmony with the environment.
    • In real life, this can be seen in the way communities come together to support each other during difficult times, much like how Indra is called upon for assistance.
    • The idea of "ṛbhur vāja" reflects the abundance and prosperity that can come from collective efforts and divine blessings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine intervention in overcoming life's challenges and the importance of community and nature in achieving prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek help from higher powers and to work together for the common good, applicable in various real-life scenarios such as community service, environmental conservation, and personal growth.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to remain aware of their connection to the divine and to each other, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
