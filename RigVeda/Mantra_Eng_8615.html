त्वया वयम् पवमानेन सोम भरे कृतं वि चिनुयाम शाश्वत् |तन नो मित्रो वरुणो मामहन्ताम् अदितिः सिन्धुः पृथिवी उता द्यौः ||
tvayā vayam pavamānena soma bhare kṛtaṁ vi cinuyāma śaśvat | tan no mitro varuṇo māmahantām aditiḥ sindhuḥ pṛthivī uta dyauḥ ||
  • tvayā vayam pavamānena soma: This phrase can be translated as "By you, we are purified by the Soma." Here, "tvayā" means "by you," "vayam" means "we," "pavamānena" refers to "purified," and "soma" is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition believed to confer immortality and divine insight.
  • bhare kṛtaṁ vi cinuyāma śaśvat: This translates to "We seek the eternal essence that has been created." "Bhare" means "to seek," "kṛtaṁ" means "created," "vi" means "well," "cinuyāma" means "we may obtain," and "śaśvat" means "eternal." This line emphasizes the pursuit of eternal truths and wisdom.
  • tan no mitro varuṇo māmahantām: This means "May that (truth) be with us, O Mitra and Varuna." "Tan" means "that," "no" means "with us," "mitro" refers to "Mitra," the god of friendship and contracts, and "varuṇo" refers to "Varuna," the god of cosmic order and water. "Māmahantām" means "greatness." This line invokes the blessings of these deities for guidance and support.
  • aditiḥ sindhuḥ pṛthivī uta dyauḥ: This translates to "Aditi, the ocean, the earth, and the sky." "Aditiḥ" refers to the mother of gods, "sindhuḥ" means "ocean," "pṛthivī" means "earth," and "uta dyauḥ" means "and the sky." This line acknowledges the interconnectedness of all elements of the universe and the divine presence in nature.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of purification, the pursuit of eternal wisdom, and the invocation of divine support from the gods Mitra and Varuna. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as seeking clarity in decision-making, fostering friendships, and recognizing the interconnectedness of nature. It encourages individuals to strive for higher truths and maintain harmony with the universe, reminding us that our actions and intentions are guided by a greater cosmic order. This mantra serves as a reminder to seek purity in thoughts and actions, fostering a sense of unity with the world around us.
