बिभयाऽहि त्वावता उग्राद् अभिप्रभङ्गिणः |दस्माद् अहम् ऋतीषहः ||
bibhayā hi tvāvata ugrād abhiprabhaṅgiṇaḥ | dasmād aham ṛtīṣahaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bibhayā: Out of fear
    • hi: Indeed
    • tvā: You
    • avata: Protect
    • ugrād: From the fierce
    • abhiprabhaṅgiṇaḥ: Those who are powerful and destructive
    • dasmād: Therefore
    • aham: I
    • ṛtīṣahaḥ: Am the one who upholds truth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for protection from fierce and destructive forces.
    • The speaker acknowledges their fear and seeks refuge in a higher power, emphasizing the importance of truth and righteousness.
    • It highlights the duality of fear and strength, suggesting that acknowledging fear can lead to a greater understanding of one's own strength.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of crisis, such as natural disasters or personal challenges, individuals often seek protection and guidance from a higher power or their community.
    • In the workplace, employees may feel fear when facing challenges, but by upholding integrity and truth, they can navigate through difficulties effectively.
    • In personal relationships, acknowledging fears and seeking support can lead to stronger bonds and mutual understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking protection and guidance in the face of fear and adversity.
    • It emphasizes that by upholding truth and righteousness, one can find strength and resilience.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various situations where individuals face challenges, encouraging them to seek support and remain steadfast in their values.
