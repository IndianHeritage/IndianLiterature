विप्रासो न मन्मभिः स्वाध्यो देवाव्यो न यज्ञैः स्वप्नसः |राजानो न चित्राः सुसंदृशः क्षितीनां न मर्याऽरेपसः ||
viprāso na manmabhiḥ svādhyo devāvyo3 na yajñaiḥ svapnasaḥ | rājāno na citrāḥ susaṁdṛśaḥ kṣitīnāṁ na maryā arepasaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • viprāso: wise or learned individuals
    • na: not
    • manmabhiḥ: by my thoughts or intentions
    • svādhyo: self-controlled or disciplined
    • devāvyo: divine beings or deities
    • na: not
    • yajñaiḥ: by sacrifices or offerings
    • svapnasaḥ: those who are in a dream-like state
    • rājāno: kings or rulers
    • na: not
    • citrāḥ: colorful or diverse
    • susaṁdṛśaḥ: well-seen or well-perceived
    • kṣitīnāṁ: of the earth or land
    • na: not
    • maryā: boundaries or limits
    • arepasaḥ: those who are beyond
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and self-discipline among individuals.
    • It suggests that true understanding and connection with the divine cannot be achieved through mere thoughts or superficial actions.
    • It highlights that rulers and leaders should be perceptive and aware, rather than being distracted by the colorful and diverse aspects of life.
    • The mantra also implies that there are limits to what can be achieved without true awareness and discipline.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a wise leader who practices self-discipline can inspire their team to achieve greater results than one who relies solely on charisma or superficial charm.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who are self-aware and disciplined are often more successful in maintaining healthy connections than those who are easily swayed by emotions or external influences.
    • In education, students who cultivate self-discipline and critical thinking skills tend to excel, as opposed to those who rely on rote memorization or passive learning.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of wisdom, self-discipline, and awareness in all aspects of life.
    • It encourages individuals to look beyond superficial distractions and to cultivate a deeper understanding of themselves and their surroundings.
    • In a world filled with noise and distractions, the teachings of this mantra are applicable in personal growth, leadership, and community building.
