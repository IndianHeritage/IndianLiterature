अग्निं वः पूर्व्यं हुवे होतारं चर्षणीनाम् |तम आय वाचा गृणे तम उ वः स्तुषे ||
agniṁ vaḥ pūrvyaṁ huve hotāraṁ carṣaṇīnām | tam ayā vācā gṛṇe tam u vaḥ stuṣe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Agniṁ: Fire, representing energy and transformation.
    • Vaḥ: We invoke or we call upon.
    • Pūrvyaṁ: The ancient or the primordial.
    • Huve: I invoke or I call.
    • Hotāraṁ: The priest or the one who offers sacrifices.
    • Carṣaṇīnām: Of the seers or sages, those who have insight.
    • Tam: That (referring to Agni).
    • Ayā: By this (referring to the invocation).
    • Vācā: With words or speech.
    • Gṛṇe: I sing or I praise.
    • U: And.
    • Vaḥ: We invoke or we call upon.
    • Stuṣe: To praise or to extol.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • It emphasizes the importance of invoking Agni, the ancient force that brings transformation and purification.
    • The mention of "hotāraṁ" signifies the role of a priest or a guide who facilitates the connection between the material and spiritual realms.
    • By invoking Agni with words, the mantra highlights the power of speech and intention in spiritual practices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, lighting a fire during ceremonies symbolizes the presence of Agni, representing purification and the offering of intentions to the universe.
    • In personal development, the concept of transformation through inner fire can be seen in practices like meditation and yoga, where individuals seek to ignite their inner potential.
    • The role of a mentor or guide in one’s life can be likened to the "hotāraṁ," as they help navigate challenges and facilitate growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of invoking divine energies for guidance and transformation.
    • It teaches us the value of intention and the power of words in shaping our reality.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek transformation through spiritual practices and to recognize the role of mentors in their journey.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine forces that guide us.
