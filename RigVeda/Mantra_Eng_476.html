प्र वेपयन्ति पर्वतान् वि विञ्चन्ति वनस्पतिन् |प्रो आरत मारुतो दुर्मदाऽ इव देवासः सर्वयाऽविशा ||
pra vepayanti parvatān vi viñcanti vanaspatīn | pro ārata maruto durmadā iva devāsaḥ sarvayā viśā ||
  • Translation: "The mountains tremble, the trees sway; the Maruts, like intoxicated gods, move everywhere."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra vepayanti: "tremble" or "shake" - This indicates a powerful force causing movement.
    • parvatān: "mountains" - Symbolizes stability and strength in nature.
    • vi viñcanti: "sway" or "move" - Refers to the dynamic nature of the environment.
    • vanaspatīn: "trees" - Represents life and growth, integral to the ecosystem.
    • pro ārata: "like intoxicated" - Suggests a state of exuberance or overwhelming energy.
    • maruto: "Maruts" - The storm gods, embodying the forces of nature.
    • durmadā: "intoxicated" - Implies a loss of control due to overwhelming power.
    • iva: "like" - A simile that draws a comparison.
    • devāsaḥ: "gods" - Represents divine forces or energies.
    • sarvayā: "everywhere" - Indicates omnipresence.
    • viśā: "all beings" - Encompasses all forms of life and existence.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra illustrates the powerful and dynamic forces of nature, represented by the mountains and trees responding to the Maruts.
    • It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all elements in the environment, showcasing how divine forces influence the natural world.
    • The imagery of trembling mountains and swaying trees can be likened to real-life scenarios such as natural disasters (earthquakes, storms) that remind us of nature's power.
    • In a broader sense, it reflects the idea that life is in constant motion and change, urging us to adapt and respond to our surroundings.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In environmental science, understanding the forces of nature is crucial for disaster preparedness and ecological balance.
    • In personal development, the mantra serves as a reminder to embrace change and remain resilient in the face of life's challenges.
    • In spiritual practices, it encourages individuals to recognize the divine presence in nature and to cultivate a sense of reverence for the environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of nature's power and the divine forces that govern it.
    • It serves as a reminder of our place within the larger ecosystem and the importance of respecting and understanding the natural world.
    • By acknowledging these forces, we can foster a deeper connection with our environment and promote sustainability in our actions.
