शतं चक्षाणो अक्षभिर देवो वनेषु तुर्वणिः |सदो दधाना उपरेषु सानुष्व अग्निः परेषु सानुषु ||
śataṁ cakṣāṇo akṣabhir devo vaneṣu turvaṇiḥ | sado dadhāna upareṣu sānuṣv agniḥ pareṣu sānuṣu ||
  • śataṁ: This word translates to "hundred" or "many," indicating abundance or a multitude.
  • cakṣāṇo: This means "having eyes" or "perceptive," suggesting awareness and insight.
  • akṣabhir: This refers to "with eyes" or "by sight," emphasizing the importance of vision and perception.
  • devo: This translates to "god" or "divine being," representing a higher power or spiritual entity.
  • vaneṣu: This means "in the forests" or "in nature," indicating a connection to the natural world.
  • turvaṇiḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is strong" or "the powerful," referring to a forceful presence.
  • sado: This means "with truth" or "truthful," highlighting the importance of honesty and integrity.
  • dadhāna: This translates to "holding" or "maintaining," suggesting stability and support.
  • upareṣu: This means "above" or "in the higher realms," indicating a connection to the spiritual or divine.
  • sānuṣv: This refers to "with warmth" or "with affection," emphasizing compassion and care.
  • agniḥ: This translates to "fire," symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • pareṣu: This means "in the higher" or "in the transcendent," indicating a connection to the ultimate reality.
  • sānuṣu: This again refers to "with warmth" or "with affection," reinforcing the theme of compassion.

The mantra speaks to the divine presence that is perceptive and aware, existing in the natural world and embodying strength and truth. It emphasizes the importance of vision and insight, both in a literal and metaphorical sense, suggesting that one should strive to see beyond the surface and understand the deeper truths of existence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Environmental Awareness: Just as the mantra connects to nature, it encourages individuals to be aware of their surroundings and the impact of their actions on the environment. For example, engaging in sustainable practices and protecting natural habitats reflects the essence of this mantra.
  • Personal Integrity: The emphasis on truthfulness and compassion can guide individuals in their personal and professional lives. For instance, being honest in business dealings and treating others with kindness can lead to stronger relationships and a more harmonious community.
  • Spiritual Growth: The connection to the divine and the higher realms encourages individuals to seek spiritual enlightenment. Practices such as meditation, mindfulness, and self-reflection can help cultivate a deeper understanding of oneself and the universe.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of perception, truth, and compassion in our lives. It encourages us to connect with nature, uphold integrity, and seek spiritual growth, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
