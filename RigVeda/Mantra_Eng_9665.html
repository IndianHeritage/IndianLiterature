आभूत्या सहजा वज्र सायका सहो बिभर्ष्य अभिभूत उत्तराम् |क्रत्वा नो मन्यो सह मेद्य एधि महाधनस्य पुरुहूत संश्रिजि ||
ābhūtyā sahajā vajra sāyaka saho bibharṣy abhibhūta uttaram | kratvā no manyo saha medy edhi mahādhanasya puruhūta saṁsṛji ||
  • ābhūtyā: This term refers to prosperity or abundance. In real life, it can be related to financial stability or emotional well-being.
  • sahajā: Meaning 'born together' or 'natural', this word signifies the innate qualities or characteristics that one possesses. For example, a person may have a natural talent for music or art.
  • vajra: Translated as 'thunderbolt' or 'diamond', it symbolizes strength and indestructibility. In a practical sense, this can be likened to resilience in the face of challenges.
  • sāyaka: This means 'weapon' or 'tool'. In everyday life, it can represent the skills and resources we use to navigate our challenges.
  • saho: This term means 'together' or 'with'. It emphasizes the importance of collaboration and unity in achieving goals.
  • bibharṣy: This word means 'to bear' or 'to carry'. It can be related to the burdens we carry in life, whether they are emotional, financial, or social.
  • abhibhūta: This means 'overcome' or 'conquered'. It signifies triumph over obstacles, akin to overcoming personal fears or societal challenges.
  • uttaram: Translated as 'the higher' or 'the best', it refers to striving for excellence in all endeavors.
  • kratvā: This term means 'with effort' or 'by action'. It highlights the importance of taking initiative and working hard to achieve one's goals.
  • no: This means 'our', indicating a collective identity or shared purpose.
  • manyo: This translates to 'thought' or 'intention', emphasizing the power of mindset in shaping our reality.
  • saha: Again meaning 'together', it reinforces the theme of unity and collaboration.
  • medy: This term means 'worthy' or 'noble', suggesting that our actions should be guided by integrity and virtue.
  • edhi: Translated as 'rise' or 'elevate', it encourages personal growth and striving for higher ideals.
  • mahādhanasya: This means 'of great wealth', which can refer to both material and spiritual wealth. It signifies the importance of abundance in various forms.
  • puruhūta: This term refers to 'the one who is invoked', often associated with deities or higher powers. It suggests seeking guidance from a higher source.
  • saṁsṛji: This means 'to create' or 'to manifest', indicating the power of intention and action in bringing our desires into reality.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, effort, and the pursuit of excellence in achieving prosperity and overcoming challenges. It teaches us that by working together and harnessing our innate strengths, we can conquer obstacles and elevate ourselves to greater heights. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in a workplace, collaboration in community projects, or personal development efforts. The mantra serves as a reminder that our thoughts, intentions, and actions play a crucial role in shaping our reality and achieving our goals.
