त्वं जामिर जनानां अग्ने मित्रोऽसि प्रियः |सखा सकिभ्य इड्यः ||
tvaṁ jāmir janānām agne mitro asi priyaḥ | sakhā sakhibhya īḍyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • jāmir: The one who is born or the origin
    • janānām: Of the people or beings
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • mitro: You are a friend
    • asi: You are
    • priyaḥ: Beloved or dear
    • sakhā: Companion or friend
    • sakhibhya: To friends
    • īḍyaḥ: Worthy of worship or praise
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Agni, the fire god, acknowledging him as the origin of all beings.
    • It emphasizes the relationship between Agni and humanity, portraying him as a friend and a beloved companion.
    • The term "īḍyaḥ" suggests that Agni is worthy of worship, highlighting the importance of reverence in human relationships.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire symbolizes transformation and purification. For instance, in Hindu rituals, Agni is invoked during ceremonies to purify the surroundings and the participants.
    • The concept of friendship and companionship can be seen in modern relationships where individuals rely on each other for support, much like the bond expressed in this mantra.
    • In a workplace setting, a leader can be seen as a 'Agni' figure, guiding and supporting their team, fostering a sense of camaraderie and trust.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the divine presence in our lives and the relationships we cultivate.
    • It teaches us to value companionship and the bonds we share with others, encouraging us to treat our friends and loved ones with respect and reverence.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire us to foster deeper connections, appreciate the support we receive, and acknowledge the divine in our everyday interactions.
