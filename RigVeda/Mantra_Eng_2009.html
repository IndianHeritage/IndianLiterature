त्वं अग्न इन्द्रो वृषभः सताम् असि त्वं विष्णुर उरुगायो नमस्यः |त्वं ब्रह्मा रयिविद् ब्रह्मणस् पते त्वं विधर्तः सचसे पुरंढ्या ||
tvam agna indro vṛṣabhaḥ satām asi tvaṁ viṣṇur urugāyo namasyaḥ | tvam brahmā rayivid brahmaṇas pate tvaṁ vidhartaḥ sacase puraṁdhyā ||
  • tvam agna indro vṛṣabhaḥ satām asi: This phrase can be translated as "You are the fire, Indra, the bull among the strong." Here, "tvam" means "you," "agna" refers to "fire," "Indra" is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, and "vṛṣabhaḥ" means "bull," symbolizing strength and power. This highlights the importance of fire as a purifying and transformative force in life, much like how Indra represents strength and leadership.
  • tvaṁ viṣṇur urugāyo namasyaḥ: This translates to "You are Vishnu, the all-pervading one, to whom we bow." "Viṣṇur" refers to Lord Vishnu, who is known for his role as the preserver in the Hindu trinity. "Urugāya" means "the one who is everywhere," emphasizing the omnipresence of the divine. This can be related to the idea of finding divinity in all aspects of life, encouraging respect and reverence for the universe.
  • tvam brahmā rayivid brahmaṇas pate: This means "You are Brahma, the lord of wealth and the creator." "Brahmā" is the creator god in Hinduism, and "rayivid" refers to wealth or abundance. This line signifies the connection between creation and prosperity, suggesting that creativity and abundance are intertwined in our lives.
  • tvaṁ vidhartaḥ sacase puraṁdhyā: This translates to "You are the sustainer, the one who supports the universe." "Vidhartaḥ" means "sustainer," and "puraṁdhyā" refers to the one who upholds the world. This highlights the importance of support and sustenance in our lives, both materially and spiritually, reminding us of our responsibilities towards each other and the environment.

  • Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divinity, strength, creativity, and sustenance. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all aspects of life, urging us to recognize the divine in our daily experiences. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to embrace their inner strength, foster creativity, and support one another in their communities. By acknowledging the divine presence in everything, we can cultivate a sense of gratitude and responsibility towards the world around us.
