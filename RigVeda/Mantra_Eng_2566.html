अग्नि विश्वानि वारीय वाजेषु सनीषामहे |त्वे देवासा एरिरे ||
agne viśvāni vāryā vājeṣu saniṣāmahe | tve devāsa erire ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying force and a source of energy.
  • Viśvāni: This term means "all" or "universal." It signifies inclusiveness and the idea of encompassing everything.
  • Vāryā: This word translates to "worthy" or "powerful." It indicates strength and capability.
  • Vājeṣu: This term means "in the offerings" or "in the sacrifices." It relates to the rituals performed to honor the deities.
  • Saniṣāmahe: This phrase means "we meditate" or "we contemplate." It reflects the act of focusing one's thoughts and intentions.
  • Tve: This word means "to you," indicating a direct address to the divine.
  • Devāsa: This term refers to "gods" or "divine beings." It emphasizes the spiritual aspect of the mantra.
  • Erire: This word means "to be invoked" or "to be called upon." It signifies the act of seeking the presence and blessings of the divine.

This mantra can be understood as a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for his blessings and strength in all endeavors. It emphasizes the importance of universal power and the act of meditation in connecting with the divine. The invocation of Agni signifies the need for purification and energy in our lives.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: During religious ceremonies, invoking Agni can help purify the space and intentions, making the rituals more effective.
  • Personal Growth: Meditating on this mantra can inspire individuals to seek strength and capability in their personal and professional lives.
  • Community Gatherings: In communal settings, reciting this mantra can foster a sense of unity and collective strength among participants.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with the divine energy of Agni, promoting purification, strength, and unity. It serves as a reminder of the power of meditation and the importance of invoking divine blessings in all aspects of life.
