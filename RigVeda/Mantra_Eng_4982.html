इन्द्राग्नि अपाद इयं पूर्वागात पदवतीभ्यः |हित्वि शिरो जिह्वया वावदच चरत त्रिंशत पदा न् यक्रमीत ||
indrāgnī apād iyam pūrvāgāt padvatībhyaḥ | hitvī śiro jihvayā vāvadac carat triṁśat padā ny akramīt ||
  • Indrāgnī: This term refers to the combined powers of Indra, the king of gods, and Agni, the fire god. In a real-life context, this can symbolize the strength and energy derived from leadership and creativity.
  • Apād: Meaning 'without feet' or 'unstoppable', this suggests a force that moves forward without hindrance. In life, this can represent determination and resilience in overcoming obstacles.
  • Iyām: This word translates to 'this' or 'here', indicating a focus on the present moment or the current situation. It emphasizes the importance of being aware of our surroundings and circumstances.
  • Pūrvāgāt: Meaning 'having gone before', this can refer to the wisdom gained from past experiences. In practical terms, it encourages learning from history to make better decisions in the present.
  • Padvatībhyaḥ: This term relates to 'the mountains' or 'the elevated places', symbolizing stability and strength. In real life, it can represent the support systems we rely on, such as family and community.
  • Hitvī: Meaning 'having abandoned' or 'letting go', this suggests the importance of releasing burdens or negative influences. In everyday life, it can be about moving on from past grievances to achieve personal growth.
  • Śiro: This translates to 'head', symbolizing intellect and leadership. It highlights the significance of using wisdom and knowledge to guide our actions.
  • Jihvayā: Meaning 'with the tongue', this emphasizes the power of speech and communication. In real life, it can remind us of the impact our words have on others and the importance of speaking truthfully.
  • Vāvadac: This term means 'to speak' or 'to declare', reinforcing the idea of expressing thoughts and intentions clearly. It encourages open dialogue in relationships and communities.
  • Carat: Meaning 'moving' or 'acting', this signifies the importance of taking action. In life, it can be a reminder that thoughts must be followed by deeds to create change.
  • Triṁśat: This translates to 'thirty', which can symbolize completeness or a full cycle. In a broader sense, it can represent the various stages of life and the experiences that shape us.
  • Padā: Meaning 'foot' or 'step', this emphasizes the journey we undertake in life. It can symbolize progress and the importance of each step we take towards our goals.
  • Ny akramīt: This means 'did not transgress' or 'did not overstep', suggesting the importance of boundaries and respect for oneself and others. In real life, it can be about maintaining integrity in our actions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, wisdom, and the importance of communication in our lives. It teaches us to learn from the past, act with intention, and respect the boundaries of ourselves and others. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in leadership roles, personal relationships, and community interactions, where the balance of power, knowledge, and respect is crucial for harmony and progress.
