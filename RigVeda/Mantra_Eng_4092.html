नū मन्वाना एषां देवाम् acchā न वक्षाणā |दानā सचेता सूरिभिर यामश्रुतेभिर अञ्जिभिः ||
nū manvāna eṣāṁ devām̐ acchā na vakṣaṇā | dānā saceta sūribhir yāmaśrutebhir añjibhiḥ ||
  • nū manvāna eṣāṁ devām̐: This phrase can be translated as "O mindful ones, these deities." Here, "nū" signifies a call or invocation, while "manvāna" refers to being mindful or aware. The term "devām̐" denotes the divine or deities. This suggests a plea for attention and reverence towards the divine forces that govern the universe.
  • acchā na vakṣaṇā: This translates to "may we not be deprived." The word "acchā" implies a sense of protection or preservation, while "vakṣaṇā" refers to being deprived or taken away. This line expresses a desire for safeguarding against loss or misfortune, emphasizing the importance of divine protection in our lives.
  • dānā saceta sūribhir: This means "with offerings, O wise ones." "Dānā" refers to offerings or gifts, and "sūribhir" indicates wise or knowledgeable beings. This line highlights the significance of making offerings to the divine, suggesting that wisdom and knowledge are essential in our interactions with the divine.
  • yāmaśrutebhir añjibhiḥ: This translates to "let us be guided by the well-heard." "Yāma" means to guide or lead, "śrutebhir" refers to what is heard or learned, and "añjibhiḥ" implies being adorned or enriched. This line emphasizes the importance of guidance from knowledge and wisdom that has been passed down through generations.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the relationship between humans and the divine. It emphasizes mindfulness, the importance of offerings, and the need for guidance from wisdom. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, prayers, or moments of reflection. It serves as a reminder to remain aware of the divine presence, to seek protection from misfortunes, and to value the wisdom that comes from tradition and experience. By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a deeper connection with the divine and navigate life's challenges with greater awareness and understanding.
