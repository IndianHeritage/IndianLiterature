युगे-युगे विदथ्यं गृणद्भ्योऽग्ने रयिं यशसं धेहि नव्यसीं |पव्येव राजन् अघशंसम अजरा नीचां न वृष्च वनिनं न तेजसा ||
yuge-yuge vidathyaṁ gṛṇadbhyo 'gne rayiṁ yaśasaṁ dhehi navyasīm | pavyeva rājann aghaśaṁsam ajara nīcā ni vṛśca vaninaṁ na tejasā ||
  • yuge-yuge: This phrase translates to "in every age" or "throughout the ages." It signifies the timeless nature of the message being conveyed.
  • vidathyaṁ: This word means "to bestow" or "to grant." It implies a sense of giving or providing something valuable.
  • gṛṇadbhyo: This term refers to "to those who sing" or "to the singers." It highlights the importance of expression and communication, particularly through music or hymns.
  • agne: This is a reference to Agni, the fire god in Vedic tradition, symbolizing energy, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • rayiṁ: This word means "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the material and spiritual abundance one seeks in life.
  • yaśasaṁ: This translates to "fame" or "glory." It represents the desire for recognition and honor in society.
  • dhehi: This means "please grant" or "bestow upon us." It is a plea for divine intervention and support.
  • navyasīm: This term can be interpreted as "new" or "fresh." It suggests the importance of renewal and innovation in life.
  • pavyeva: This means "like a pure one" or "as if pure." It emphasizes purity of intention and action.
  • rājann: This translates to "O King" or "O ruler." It signifies respect and acknowledgment of authority.
  • aghaśaṁsam: This term means "without sin" or "pure." It denotes the importance of righteousness and ethical conduct.
  • ajara: This translates to "immortal" or "everlasting." It signifies the eternal nature of the soul or spirit.
  • nīcā: This means "low" or "humble." It reflects the value of humility in one's character.
  • ni vṛśca: This phrase can be interpreted as "and do not" or "and not." It indicates a negation or avoidance of certain qualities.
  • vaninaṁ: This term refers to "the forest" or "nature." It symbolizes the connection to the natural world and its importance in life.
  • na tejasā: This translates to "without brilliance" or "without radiance." It suggests the need for inner strength and vitality.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine blessings for prosperity, fame, and purity in every age. It highlights the need for humility and righteousness while acknowledging the eternal nature of the soul. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during personal endeavors, community initiatives, or spiritual practices. It serves as a reminder to maintain ethical conduct, seek renewal, and connect with the divine for guidance and support in our pursuits. By embodying these principles, individuals can strive for a balanced and fulfilling life, contributing positively to society and the environment.
