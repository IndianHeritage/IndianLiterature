सत्यमुगरस्य बृहतः सं श्रवन्ति संश्रवाः |सं यन्ति रसिनो रसाः पुनानो ब्रह्मणा हर इन्द्रायन्दो परि श्रव ||
satyamugrasya bṛhataḥ saṁ sravanti saṁsravāḥ | saṁ yanti rasino rasāḥ punāno brahmaṇā hara indrāyendo pari srava ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • satyamugrasya: Of the powerful truth
    • bṛhataḥ: Of the great
    • saṁ sravanti: They flow together
    • saṁsravāḥ: They are connected
    • saṁ yanti: They unite
    • rasino: Of the essence
    • rasāḥ: The flavors or tastes
    • punāno: Purifying
    • brahmaṇā: By the divine or the ultimate reality
    • hara: To remove or to take away
    • indrāyendo: To Indra, the king of gods
    • pari srava: Flowing all around
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the powerful and great truth that flows and connects all things.
    • It emphasizes the unity of essence and the purifying nature of this truth.
    • The invocation to Indra suggests a plea for divine assistance in realizing and embodying this truth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, the mantra can be applied to the importance of transparency and honesty in business practices. Just as the truth flows and connects, so does trust among colleagues and clients.
    • In personal relationships, the essence of communication and understanding can purify and strengthen bonds, much like the mantra suggests.
    • In environmental contexts, recognizing the interconnectedness of all life can lead to more sustainable practices, reflecting the unity and flow described in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for recognizing and embodying the powerful truth that connects all aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek purity in our actions and thoughts, aligning ourselves with the divine essence.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of unity, truth, and purification can lead to more harmonious relationships and a deeper understanding of our place in the world.
