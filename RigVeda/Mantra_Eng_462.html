मो षु णः परा-परा निरृतिर दुर्हणा वधीत् |पदीष्ट तृष्णया सह ||
mo ṣu ṇaḥ parā-parā nirṛtir durhaṇā vadhīt | padīṣṭa tṛṣṇayā saha ||
  • mo ṣu ṇaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "may we not be harmed" or "let us not be afflicted." It expresses a desire for protection and safety.
  • parā-parā: This term refers to "the highest" or "beyond the beyond." It signifies a state of transcendence or reaching the ultimate truth.
  • nirṛtir: This word means "destruction" or "calamity." It represents the challenges and adversities that one may face in life.
  • durhaṇā: This translates to "difficult" or "hard to overcome." It emphasizes the severity of the challenges mentioned earlier.
  • vadhīt: This term means "to kill" or "to eliminate." It suggests the act of overcoming or vanquishing difficulties.
  • padīṣṭa: This word can be understood as "established" or "set in place." It indicates a foundation or a state of being grounded.
  • tṛṣṇayā: This translates to "thirst" or "desire." It represents the human condition of longing and aspiration.
  • saha: This means "together" or "with." It signifies the importance of companionship and support in overcoming challenges.

  • Meaning and Interpretation: The mantra expresses a plea for protection from harm and adversity while striving for a higher state of existence. It acknowledges the presence of difficulties in life but emphasizes the importance of overcoming them with the support of others. The desire for safety and the aspiration for transcendence are central themes.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, someone facing a serious illness may recite this mantra to seek protection and strength to overcome their health challenges.
    • In a community setting, during times of natural disasters, people may come together, invoking this mantra to seek safety and support from one another.
    • In a professional environment, employees facing a challenging project may use this mantra to foster teamwork and collaboration, emphasizing the need to overcome difficulties together.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its universal message of seeking protection from adversity while aspiring for higher truths. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, reminding individuals and communities of the importance of resilience, support, and the collective effort to overcome challenges. By invoking this mantra, one can cultivate a mindset of strength and unity in the face of difficulties.
