गच्छतां दाशुषो गृहं इत्यस्तुवतो अश्विनाः |मध्वः सोमस्य पीतये ||
gacchataṁ dāśuṣo gṛham itthā stuvato aśvinā | madhvaḥ somasya pītaye ||
  • gacchataṁ: This word translates to "go" or "move towards." It signifies the act of progression or journeying towards a goal.
  • dāśuṣo: This term refers to "the house of the Dāśus," where Dāśus can be interpreted as those who are deceitful or obstructive. In a broader sense, it can symbolize challenges or obstacles in life.
  • gṛham: This means "home" or "abode." It represents a place of safety, comfort, and belonging.
  • itthā: This word means "thus" or "in this manner," indicating a way of doing things or a method.
  • stuvato: This translates to "of those who praise" or "of the worshippers." It emphasizes the importance of reverence and acknowledgment of the divine.
  • aśvinā: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing and benevolent qualities. They symbolize health, vitality, and the duality of life.
  • madhvaḥ: This word means "sweet" or "pleasant," often associated with the essence of life and joy.
  • somasya: Referring to "of Soma," which is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
  • pītaye: This translates to "for drinking" or "to be consumed," indicating the act of partaking in something nourishing or beneficial.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to move towards a place of safety and comfort, away from deceit and obstacles, while praising the divine Aśvins for their blessings. It emphasizes the importance of seeking joy and nourishment in life, both physically and spiritually.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • When facing challenges or obstacles, one can reflect on the need to move towards a supportive environment, whether it be family, friends, or community.
  • In moments of stress or confusion, invoking the qualities of the Aśvins can serve as a reminder to seek healing and balance in life.
  • The reference to "Soma" can inspire individuals to seek out experiences that bring joy and nourishment, whether through nature, relationships, or creative pursuits.

Conclusion: This mantra serves as a reminder of the journey towards a fulfilling and harmonious life. It encourages individuals to seek refuge in supportive spaces, to honor the divine, and to embrace the sweetness of life. By integrating these principles into daily life, one can cultivate a sense of peace, purpose, and connection with the world around them.
