अग्नि पवस्व स्वपाऽस्मे वर्चः सुवीर्यम् |दधद् रयिम् मयि पोषम् ||
agne pavasva svapā asme varcaḥ suvīryam | dadhad rayim mayi poṣam ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire symbolizes purity, energy, and transformation.
  • Pavasva: This means "to purify" or "to cleanse." It indicates the act of making something pure or sacred.
  • Svapā: This term can be interpreted as "to awaken" or "to inspire." It suggests a call to action or a plea for enlightenment.
  • Asme: This means "for us" or "to us," indicating a personal connection or a request directed towards the divine.
  • Varcaḥ: This translates to "brilliance" or "radiance." It signifies the light of knowledge and wisdom.
  • Suvīryam: This means "great strength" or "power." It refers to the vigor and vitality that one seeks in life.
  • Dadha: This means "to give" or "to bestow." It indicates a request for the divine to grant something valuable.
  • Rayim: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies abundance in various forms, including material and spiritual wealth.
  • Mayi: This means "in me" or "within me," indicating a personal plea for the divine presence in one's life.
  • Poṣam: This refers to "nourishment" or "sustenance." It signifies the need for support and growth in life.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer to Agni, asking for purification, inspiration, and the bestowal of strength and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of divine guidance in achieving personal and spiritual growth.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or yoga to seek clarity and strength in their personal endeavors.
  • Career Aspirations: Professionals may use this mantra to invoke the energy of Agni for success and prosperity in their careers.
  • Spiritual Practices: It can be incorporated into rituals or prayers to enhance one's spiritual journey and connection with the divine.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in achieving purity, strength, and prosperity. It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking higher guidance in our lives, encouraging individuals to strive for personal and spiritual excellence while remaining connected to the divine source of energy and wisdom.
