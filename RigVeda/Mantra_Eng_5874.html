सो अग्न एना नमसā समिद्धोऽच्छा मित्रं वरुणं इन्द्रं वोचः |यत सīm आगश चक्रमा तद सु मृळ तद आर्यमादितिḥ शिश्रथन्तु ||
so agna enā namasā samiddho 'cchā mitraṁ varuṇam indraṁ voceḥ | yat sīm āgaś cakṛmā tat su mṛḻa tad aryamāditiḥ śiśrathantu ||
  • so agna enā namasā: This phrase can be translated as "O Agni, we bow to you with reverence." Agni, the fire god, represents energy, transformation, and the divine spark within us. In real life, this can be likened to the respect we show towards our passions and the drive that fuels our ambitions.
  • samiddho 'cchā: This means "kindled and glowing." It signifies the importance of being prepared and ready to act. For example, a student who diligently studies and prepares for exams is akin to a fire that is always ready to burn brightly when the time comes.
  • mitraṁ varuṇam indraṁ voceḥ: This translates to "I call upon Mitra, Varuna, and Indra." These deities represent friendship, cosmic order, and strength, respectively. In our lives, we often seek the support of friends (Mitra), adhere to moral values (Varuna), and draw upon our inner strength (Indra) to overcome challenges.
  • yat sīm āgaś cakṛmā: This means "whatever we have done." It reflects on our actions and their consequences. In a practical sense, this can relate to the idea that our daily actions, whether in our careers or personal lives, shape our future and the world around us.
  • tat su mṛḻa: This translates to "that is good." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and striving for goodness in our actions and intentions. For instance, choosing to help others in need or making ethical decisions in business reflects this principle.
  • tad aryamāditiḥ: This means "that which is noble." It encourages us to aspire towards nobility in our character and actions. In real life, this can be seen in leaders who inspire others through their integrity and commitment to the greater good.
  • śiśrathantu: This translates to "may they be nourished." It signifies the importance of nurturing our relationships, values, and aspirations. For example, investing time in family and friendships is essential for personal growth and happiness.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing reverence for the divine, the importance of preparation, the value of friendship and strength, and the need for noble actions. In real life, it serves as a reminder to align our actions with our values, nurture our relationships, and strive for goodness in all endeavors. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling existence, both personally and within our communities.
