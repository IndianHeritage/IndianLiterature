इमं मे गंगे यमुनें सरस्वती शुतुद्रि स्तोमं साचतां पुरुष्ण्याः |असिक्न्या मरुद्वृधे वितस्तयार्जीकिये श्रिणुहि आ सुशोमया ||
imam me gaṅge yamune sarasvati śutudri stomaṁ sacatā paruṣṇy ā | asiknyā marudvṛdhe vitastayārjīkīye śṛṇuhy ā suṣomayā ||
  • Translation: This mantra invokes the sacred rivers of India: Ganga, Yamuna, and Sarasvati, along with the other rivers and natural elements. It is a call for blessings and prosperity.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • imam: This means "this" or "here," indicating a focus on the present moment.
    • me: Translates to "my," suggesting a personal connection to the divine or the elements being invoked.
    • gaṅge: Refers to the Ganges River, considered sacred and a symbol of purity.
    • yamune: Refers to the Yamuna River, another sacred river in India.
    • sarasvati: Refers to the Sarasvati River, often associated with knowledge and wisdom.
    • śutudri: Refers to the river Sutudri, which is often identified with the Beas River.
    • stomaṁ: Means "praise" or "hymn," indicating the act of worship.
    • sacatā: Means "to be fulfilled" or "to be satisfied."
    • paruṣṇy: Refers to the fierce or powerful aspects of nature.
    • ā: A prefix that can mean "towards" or "in the direction of."
    • asiknyā: Refers to the river Asikni, often identified with the Chenab River.
    • marudvṛdhe: Refers to the Maruts, the storm deities, indicating the power of nature.
    • vitastayārjīkīye: Refers to the river Vitasta, often identified with the Jhelum River.
    • śṛṇuhy: Means "to hear" or "to listen," indicating a call for attention from the divine.
    • ā: Again, a prefix indicating direction.
    • suṣomayā: Refers to the auspicious or pleasant aspects of nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The Ganges River is not only a physical entity but also a cultural symbol for millions of people who perform rituals and seek spiritual cleansing in its waters.
    • The Yamuna River is celebrated in various festivals, such as Yamuna Jayanti, where devotees honor the river and seek her blessings.
    • In modern times, environmental movements are inspired by the reverence for these rivers, advocating for their protection and restoration.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of the sacred rivers and natural elements, symbolizing purity, knowledge, and the power of nature. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the environment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to respect and protect natural resources, engage in spiritual practices that promote well-being, and foster a sense of community through shared cultural rituals. By acknowledging the divine in nature, we can cultivate a deeper appreciation for our surroundings and work towards a sustainable future.
