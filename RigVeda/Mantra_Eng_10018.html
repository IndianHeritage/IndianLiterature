वज्रं यश चक्रे सुहनाय दस्यवे हिरीमशो हिरीमान् |अरुतहनुर अद्भुतं न रजः ||
vajraṁ yaś cakre suhanāya dasyave hirīmaśo hirīmān | arutahanur adbhutaṁ na rajaḥ ||
  • vajraṁ: This word translates to "thunderbolt" or "diamond," symbolizing strength, power, and indestructibility. In real life, this can be likened to the resilience of individuals who overcome challenges, much like a diamond that withstands pressure.
  • yaś: Meaning "fame" or "glory," this term emphasizes the importance of reputation and recognition. For example, a scientist whose groundbreaking research earns them accolades embodies this concept.
  • cakre: This translates to "created" or "established," indicating the act of bringing something into existence. In a practical sense, this can refer to entrepreneurs who create businesses that contribute to society.
  • suhanāya: Meaning "for the good" or "beneficial," this word highlights the intention behind actions. A community project aimed at improving local education reflects this idea.
  • dasyave: This term means "to the one who is worthy" or "to the deserving." It suggests that rewards and recognition should be given to those who truly merit them, similar to how promotions are awarded based on merit in the workplace.
  • hirīmaśo: This word can be interpreted as "of great wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies abundance, which can be seen in individuals who achieve financial success through hard work and dedication.
  • hirīmān: This term means "wealthy" or "prosperous," reinforcing the idea of abundance. For instance, a philanthropist who uses their wealth to support charitable causes exemplifies this concept.
  • arutahanur: This translates to "the one who brings forth" or "the one who leads." It can be associated with leaders who inspire and guide others, such as a coach motivating their team to victory.
  • adbhutaṁ: Meaning "wonderful" or "extraordinary," this word emphasizes the remarkable nature of certain achievements. An athlete breaking a world record is a real-life example of something extraordinary.
  • na rajaḥ: This phrase means "not of the dust" or "not of the mundane," suggesting a distinction from ordinary matters. It can be related to individuals who strive for higher ideals, such as activists fighting for social justice.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, glory, creation for the good, and the pursuit of prosperity. It serves as a reminder that true success is not just about personal gain but also about contributing positively to society. In real life, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as in leadership, entrepreneurship, and community service. By embodying the values expressed in this mantra, individuals can strive to achieve greatness while uplifting those around them.
