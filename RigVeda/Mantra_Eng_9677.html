ऋक्सामाभ्यां अभिहितौ गावौ ते सामनाव इतः |श्रौतं ते चक्रे आस्तां दिवि पन्थाश चराचरः ||
ṛksāmābhyām abhihitau gāvau te sāmanāv itaḥ | śrotraṁ te cakre āstāṁ divi panthāś carācāraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ṛksāmābhyām: With the two hymns (or verses)
    • abhihitau: Having been uttered or spoken
    • gāvau: The two cows (or the two forms of nourishment)
    • te: Your
    • sāmanāv: In the form of melodies or chants
    • itaḥ: From here
    • śrotraṁ: The ear or the sense of hearing
    • te: Your
    • cakre: Have made or created
    • āstāṁ: May it be established
    • divi: In the heavens
    • panthāś: Paths or ways
    • carācāraḥ: Moving and non-moving beings
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the power of hymns and chants, suggesting that they can create a connection between the divine and the earthly realms.
    • The "two cows" symbolize nourishment and abundance, which can be interpreted as the spiritual and material sustenance that hymns provide.
    • The reference to the ear signifies the importance of listening to these hymns, as they carry wisdom and guidance.
    • The mantra emphasizes the establishment of paths in the heavens, indicating that through the recitation of these hymns, one can find direction and purpose in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, chanting or singing hymns is a way to connect with the divine. For instance, in Christianity, hymns are sung during worship to uplift the spirit and create a sense of community.
    • In yoga and meditation practices, the recitation of mantras is believed to enhance focus and bring about a sense of peace, similar to the way the RigVeda suggests that hymns can create paths in the heavens.
    • In daily life, people often turn to music or poetry for comfort and inspiration, reflecting the idea that words and melodies can nourish the soul.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of the power of sound and words in shaping our experiences and connecting us to higher realms.
    • It teaches us that through the practice of listening and reciting sacred hymns, we can find nourishment for both our spiritual and material lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to seek out and engage with uplifting and meaningful expressions of art, music, and spirituality to navigate their paths in life.
