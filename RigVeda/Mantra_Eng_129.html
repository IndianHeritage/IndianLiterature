नक्तोषासाऽ सुपेशसास्मिन यज्ञ उप हवये |इदं नो बर्हिर आसद ||
naktoṣāsā supeśasāsmin yajña upa hvaye | idaṁ no barhir āsade ||
  • Mantra: "naktoṣāsā supeśasāsmin yajña upa hvaye | idaṁ no barhir āsade ||"
  • Translation: "O Agni, we invoke you, the one who is well adorned, to this sacrifice; may this offering of ours be accepted."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • naktoṣāsā: Refers to the well-adorned or beautifully decorated, often associated with the divine.
    • supeśasāsmin: Indicates the presence of auspiciousness and beauty, often linked to the divine qualities of Agni.
    • yajña: Means sacrifice or offering, a central theme in Vedic rituals, symbolizing the connection between humans and the divine.
    • upa hvaye: Translates to "we invoke" or "we call upon," expressing a plea for divine attention and acceptance.
    • idaṁ: Means "this," referring to the offering being made.
    • no: Means "our," indicating possession and personal connection to the offering.
    • barhir: Refers to the sacred grass or the altar, symbolizing the space where the divine and human meet.
    • āsade: Means "may it be accepted," a request for the divine to receive the offering favorably.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings and rituals in connecting with the divine.
    • It highlights the qualities of beauty and auspiciousness in the divine, suggesting that our offerings should also be made with care and intention.
    • The invocation of Agni, the fire god, symbolizes transformation and purification, essential elements in any spiritual practice.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various rituals and ceremonies, such as weddings, housewarming, or any auspicious event where offerings are made.
    • It serves as a reminder to approach our actions with intention and beauty, whether in personal relationships, work, or community service.
    • The mantra encourages us to create a sacred space in our lives, where we can connect with our higher selves and the divine, fostering a sense of peace and purpose.
  • Conclusion: The mantra "naktoṣāsā supeśasāsmin yajña upa hvaye | idaṁ no barhir āsade ||" serves as a profound reminder of the significance of offerings and the beauty of intention in our spiritual practices. By invoking the divine with care and respect, we not only honor the sacred but also enrich our own lives and the lives of those around us. This mantra encourages us to cultivate a deeper connection with the divine through our actions, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose in our everyday lives.
