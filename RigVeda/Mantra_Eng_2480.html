अग्नि जारस्व स्वपत्या आयुंय ऊर्जां पिन्वस्व सम इषो दिदीहि नः |वयांसी जिन्व बृहतः च जागृव उषिग देवानाम् असि सुक्रतुर विपाम् ||
agne jarasva svapatya āyuny ūrjā pinvasva sam iṣo didīhi naḥ | vayāṁsi jinva bṛhataś ca jāgṛva uśig devānām asi sukratur vipām ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, light, and purity. In real life, fire is essential for cooking, warmth, and rituals.
  • Jarasva: This term means "to grow old" or "to age." It signifies the passage of time and the importance of wisdom that comes with age.
  • Svapatya: This translates to "may you grant us offspring" or "progeny." It highlights the desire for continuity and the importance of family in human life.
  • Āyuny: Meaning "long life," it emphasizes the wish for longevity and health, which is a universal aspiration.
  • Ūrjā: This word means "strength" or "vigor." In everyday life, strength can refer to physical health, mental resilience, or emotional stability.
  • Pinvasva: This term means "to nourish" or "to provide sustenance." It reflects the need for nourishment, both physically and spiritually.
  • Sam: This means "together" or "in harmony." It signifies the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving goals.
  • Iṣo: This translates to "desire" or "wish." It represents human aspirations and the drive to achieve one's goals.
  • Didīhi: Meaning "to grant" or "to bestow," it emphasizes the importance of blessings and support from higher powers.
  • Naḥ: This means "us," indicating a collective plea or request for blessings.
  • Vayāṁsi: This term refers to "youth" or "vigor," highlighting the importance of vitality in life.
  • Jinva: Meaning "to conquer" or "to overcome," it signifies the human spirit's ability to triumph over challenges.
  • Bṛhataś: This translates to "greatness" or "vastness," reflecting the desire for greatness in one's endeavors.
  • Jāgṛva: Meaning "to awaken," it signifies the importance of awareness and consciousness in life.
  • Uśig: This term refers to "the wise" or "the learned," emphasizing the value of knowledge and wisdom.
  • Devānām: Meaning "of the gods," it signifies the divine aspect of life and the connection to higher powers.
  • Asi: This translates to "you are," indicating a direct relationship with the divine.
  • Sukratur: Meaning "good deeds" or "virtuous actions," it emphasizes the importance of morality and ethics in life.
  • Vipām: This term means "to us," indicating a collective request for blessings and support.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates a deep yearning for blessings, strength, and wisdom in life. It emphasizes the importance of family, health, and unity while recognizing the divine's role in human affairs. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during family gatherings, rituals for health and prosperity, or moments of reflection on personal growth and aspirations. It serves as a reminder to seek strength, wisdom, and support from both the divine and the community, fostering a sense of interconnectedness and purpose in our lives.
