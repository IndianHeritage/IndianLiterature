yajñasya vo rathyaṁ viśpatiṁ viśāṁ hotāram aktor atithiṁ vibhāvasum | śocañ chuṣkāsu hariṇīṣu jarbhurad vṛṣā ketur yajato dyām aśāyata ||
  • yajñasya: This term refers to the concept of sacrifice or offering, which is central to Vedic rituals. In real life, this can be seen in various cultures where offerings are made to deities or ancestors, symbolizing gratitude and respect.
  • vo: This is a pronoun meaning "your," indicating a connection to the audience or the community involved in the ritual.
  • rathyaṁ: This word translates to "the chariot," symbolizing movement and progress. In modern life, this can be likened to the journey of life, where individuals strive to achieve their goals.
  • viśpatiṁ: Meaning "lord of the people," this term signifies leadership and guidance. In contemporary society, this can relate to leaders or mentors who guide others towards success.
  • viśāṁ: This means "of the community" or "of the people," emphasizing the importance of collective effort and unity in achieving common goals.
  • hotāram: Refers to the priest or the one who performs the rituals. In everyday life, this can be compared to individuals who facilitate important events, such as teachers or community leaders.
  • aktor: This term means "the one who is invoked," highlighting the importance of calling upon divine forces or energies in rituals. In real life, this can be seen in prayer or meditation practices where individuals seek guidance or support.
  • atithiṁ: This translates to "the guest," symbolizing hospitality and the importance of welcoming others. In modern contexts, this can relate to the value of community and the significance of treating others with kindness.
  • vibhāvasum: This word means "the one who shines" or "the radiant one," often referring to the divine or the sacred. In real life, this can be associated with individuals who inspire others through their actions and character.
  • śocañ: This term means "to lament" or "to grieve," indicating the human experience of sorrow. It reminds us of the importance of empathy and support in times of distress.
  • chuṣkāsu: This translates to "in the dry" or "in barren places," symbolizing challenges or hardships. In life, this can represent difficult times that require resilience and strength.
  • hariṇīṣu: Meaning "in the deer," this term can symbolize grace and agility. In real life, it can be related to the need for adaptability in various situations.
  • jarbhurad: This word refers to "the one who brings forth," indicating creation and manifestation. In modern contexts, this can relate to innovation and the birth of new ideas.
  • vṛṣā: This term means "the strong" or "the powerful," symbolizing strength and determination. In life, this can be seen in individuals who overcome obstacles through perseverance.
  • ketur: This translates to "the banner" or "the sign," representing identity and purpose. In real life, this can relate to personal values and the causes individuals stand for.
  • yajato: This means "those who perform the sacrifice," emphasizing the importance of action and commitment. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who dedicate themselves to their work or community service.
  • dyām: This term means "the sky" or "the heavens," symbolizing aspiration and higher goals. In modern contexts, this can relate to dreams and ambitions that drive individuals forward.
  • aśāyata: This translates to "may it be fulfilled," indicating a wish or prayer for success and prosperity. In life, this reflects the hopes and aspirations we hold for ourselves and our communities.
