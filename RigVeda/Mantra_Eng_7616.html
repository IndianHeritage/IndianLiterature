त्वं न इन्द्रा भरम् ओजो नृम्णं शतक्रतो विचर्षणे |आ वीरम् पृतनाशहम् ||
tvaṁ na indrā bharam̐ ojo nṛmṇaṁ śatakrato vicarṣaṇe | ā vīram pṛtanāṣaham ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity, often interpreted as Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
  • na - This means "not," suggesting a negation or a request for something to be avoided.
  • indrā - Refers to Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, symbolizing strength and power.
  • bharam̐ - This word means "to bear" or "to carry," indicating a request for support or strength.
  • ojo - Translates to "energy" or "vigor," representing the life force or strength that one seeks.
  • nṛmṇaṁ - This means "of men" or "human beings," indicating the context of human experience and existence.
  • śatakrato - This is an epithet of Indra, meaning "the one with a hundred powers," emphasizing his omnipotence and capability.
  • vicarṣaṇe - This translates to "in the act of wandering" or "in the pursuit," suggesting a journey or quest for something significant.
  • ā - This is a preposition meaning "towards" or "to," indicating direction or intention.
  • vīram - This means "hero" or "brave one," referring to the qualities of courage and valor.
  • pṛtanāṣaham - This word can be interpreted as "the one who destroys obstacles," indicating a plea for the removal of hindrances in life.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation to Indra, asking for strength and support in overcoming challenges faced by humanity. It emphasizes the need for divine assistance in our endeavors, particularly when we are on a quest or journey that requires courage and resilience.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties in life, such as health issues or personal crises, individuals can recite this mantra to seek strength and support.
  • Professional Endeavors: In the workplace, when embarking on new projects or facing competition, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek the energy and courage needed to succeed.
  • Spiritual Journeys: For those on a spiritual path, this mantra can be a source of inspiration, encouraging them to overcome obstacles and remain steadfast in their pursuit of truth and enlightenment.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine support in our lives. It reminds us that while we may face challenges, we are not alone; we can call upon higher powers for strength and guidance. By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate resilience and courage, enabling them to navigate the complexities of life with confidence.
