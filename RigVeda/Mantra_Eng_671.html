त्वं तं इन्द्र पर्वतम महाम उरुं वज्रेण वज्रिन पर्वशश चकार्थिथ | अवासृजो निवृताः सर्तवाः अपः सत्रां विश्वं दधिषे केवलं सहः ||
tvaṁ tam indra parvatam mahām uruṁ vajreṇa vajrin parvaśaś cakartitha | avāsṛjo nivṛtāḥ sartavā apaḥ satrā viśvaṁ dadhiṣe kevalaṁ sahaḥ ||
  • tvaṁ tam indra parvatam: This phrase addresses Indra, the king of the gods, and refers to a great mountain. In a metaphorical sense, mountains represent strength and stability in life.
  • mahām uruṁ: This translates to "the great and vast." It signifies the immense power and presence of Indra, which can be likened to the vastness of the universe or the challenges we face in life.
  • vajreṇa vajrin: "With the thunderbolt, the wielder of the thunderbolt." The thunderbolt symbolizes strength and the ability to overcome obstacles. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who face challenges with resilience and determination.
  • parvaśaś cakartitha: This means "you have made the mountains tremble." It reflects the idea of causing significant change or impact, similar to how a leader can inspire change in a community or organization.
  • avāsṛjo nivṛtāḥ sartavā: This phrase can be interpreted as "you have released the waters that were restrained." It symbolizes the idea of liberation and the flow of life, akin to overcoming obstacles that prevent progress.
  • apaḥ satrā: "The waters of the sacrifice." This refers to the importance of offerings and rituals in life, which can be compared to the efforts we put into our relationships and endeavors.
  • viśvaṁ dadhiṣe kevalaṁ sahaḥ: This translates to "you have established the universe in unity." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of harmony in society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, resilience, and unity. It highlights the significance of overcoming challenges and the importance of community and harmony in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal growth, leadership, and the collective efforts of individuals working together for a common goal. The mantra serves as a reminder that, like Indra, we have the power to create change and foster unity in our lives and communities.
