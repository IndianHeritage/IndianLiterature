इन्द्राग्नī रोचनā दिवः परि वाजेषु भूषथः |तद वां चिति प्र वीर्यम् ||
indrāgnī rocanā divaḥ pari vājeṣu bhūṣathaḥ | tad vāṁ ceti pra vīryam ||
  • Indrāgnī: This term refers to the combined powers of Indra, the king of gods, and Agni, the fire god. Indra symbolizes strength, power, and rain, while Agni represents energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Rocanā: This word means "brightness" or "radiance." It signifies the illuminating qualities of both Indra and Agni, suggesting that their combined forces bring clarity and enlightenment.
  • Divaḥ: This translates to "of the heavens" or "divine." It indicates a connection to the celestial realm, emphasizing the divine nature of the energies being invoked.
  • Pari vājeṣu bhūṣathaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "may you adorn the offerings" or "may you bless the sacrifices." It suggests a request for divine blessings on the offerings made to the gods, enhancing their potency and effectiveness.
  • Tad vāṁ ceti pra vīryam: This part means "that which is your strength." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and harnessing the divine strength and energy that Indra and Agni provide.

Conclusion: The mantra "indrāgnī rocanā divaḥ pari vājeṣu bhūṣathaḥ | tad vāṁ ceti pra vīryam ||" encapsulates the essence of invoking divine energies for strength, clarity, and blessings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals, ceremonies, or personal meditative practices aimed at seeking guidance and empowerment. By recognizing the divine forces of Indra and Agni, individuals can cultivate a sense of purpose and strength in their endeavors, whether in personal growth, community service, or professional pursuits. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect the practitioner with higher energies, fostering a sense of unity with the cosmos and enhancing the effectiveness of their actions and intentions.
