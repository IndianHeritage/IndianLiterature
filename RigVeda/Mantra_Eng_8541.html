स मत्सरः पृथ्सु वन्बन्न अवातः सहस्ररेता अभि वाजम् अर्षा | इन्द्रायन्दो पवमानो मनिष्य अंषोर ऊर्मिम ईरय गा इषण्यन ||
sa matsaraḥ pṛtsu vanvann avātaḥ sahasraretā abhi vājam arṣa | indrāyendo pavamāno manīṣy a1ṁśor ūrmim īraya gā iṣaṇyan ||
  • sa matsaraḥ: This phrase translates to "he who is free from envy." In real life, this can be related to the importance of fostering a spirit of generosity and kindness in our interactions with others. For example, in a workplace, a team member who celebrates the success of others rather than feeling envious contributes to a positive environment.
  • pṛtsu vanvann avātaḥ: This means "like a wind that blows through the trees." It symbolizes the idea of movement and change. In life, we often face changes that can be likened to the wind. For instance, when we adapt to new circumstances, such as moving to a new city or starting a new job, we embody this principle of flowing with change.
  • sahasraretā: This term refers to "the one with a thousand rays." It signifies abundance and the multifaceted nature of existence. In practical terms, this can be seen in the diversity of talents and skills people possess. For example, a community thrives when individuals contribute their unique abilities, much like the rays of light that come together to create a vibrant atmosphere.
  • abhi vājam arṣa: This translates to "may we attain strength." It emphasizes the pursuit of strength and resilience. In everyday life, this can be reflected in personal development, such as overcoming challenges or pursuing fitness goals. For instance, someone training for a marathon demonstrates the essence of striving for strength and endurance.
  • indrāyendo pavamāno: This phrase means "the wind that moves towards Indra." Indra is often associated with power and leadership. This can be interpreted as seeking guidance from higher ideals or leaders in our lives. For example, a student may look up to a mentor who inspires them to achieve their goals, embodying the pursuit of wisdom and strength.
  • manīṣy a1ṁśor ūrmim: This translates to "the waves of thought." It signifies the importance of contemplation and reflection. In real life, this can be seen in practices like meditation or journaling, where individuals take time to process their thoughts and emotions, leading to greater self-awareness and clarity.
  • īraya gā iṣaṇyan: This means "may we reach our goals." It emphasizes the aspiration to achieve our desires and objectives. In practical terms, this can be related to setting personal or professional goals and working diligently towards them. For instance, a person pursuing higher education exemplifies this drive to reach their aspirations.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of envy-free living, adaptability, abundance, strength, guidance, contemplation, and goal attainment. Its significance lies in the holistic approach to life it advocates, encouraging individuals to cultivate positive qualities and strive for personal growth. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as personal relationships, professional environments, and self-development practices, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
