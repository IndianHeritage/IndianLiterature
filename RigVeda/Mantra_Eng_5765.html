प्रति त्वा स्तोमैर ईळते वसिष्ठा उषर्बुधः सुभगे तुष्टुवांसाḥ |गवां नेत्री वाजपत्नी न उचोषः सुजाते प्रथमā जरस्व ||
prati tvā stomair īḻate vasiṣṭhā uṣarbudhaḥ subhage tuṣṭuvāṁsaḥ | gavāṁ netrī vājapatnī na ucchoṣaḥ sujāte prathamā jarasva ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prati: towards
    • tvā: you
    • stomair: with hymns
    • īḻate: are invoked
    • vasiṣṭhā: the best
    • uṣarbudhaḥ: the one who awakens
    • subhage: O auspicious one
    • tuṣṭuvāṁsaḥ: those who praise
    • gavāṁ: of the cows
    • netrī: the leader
    • vājapatnī: the one who brings strength
    • na: not
    • ucchoṣaḥ: to be deprived
    • sujāte: well-born
    • prathamā: first
    • jarasva: in old age
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence, asking for blessings and strength.
    • The term "vasiṣṭhā" refers to the best or the most excellent, indicating a call for the highest form of support.
    • "uṣarbudhaḥ" signifies awakening, suggesting a need for enlightenment or realization.
    • The mention of "subhage" emphasizes auspiciousness, indicating that the blessings sought are for positive outcomes.
    • The reference to "gavāṁ" (cows) symbolizes nourishment and wealth, as cows were considered valuable in ancient society.
    • "vājapatnī" represents strength and vitality, indicating the importance of these qualities in life.
    • The phrase "na ucchoṣaḥ" suggests a plea to not be deprived of these blessings, highlighting the human desire for security and abundance.
    • "sujāte" and "jarasva" reflect the importance of being well-born and the inevitability of aging, reminding us of the cycle of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be seen as a call for ethical practices and the pursuit of excellence in service and products.
    • In personal life, it can be interpreted as a reminder to seek wisdom and strength in relationships and personal growth.
    • In community settings, it emphasizes the importance of supporting one another to ensure collective well-being and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of seeking blessings, and the pursuit of excellence.
    • Its significance lies in the acknowledgment of divine support in our endeavors, whether in personal, professional, or communal aspects of life.
    • By invoking these qualities, individuals can strive for a balanced and fulfilling life, ensuring that they remain grounded and connected to their roots.
