यस्माद् ऋते न सिद्ध्यति यज्ञो विपश्चितश्चन |स धीनां योगम इन्बति ||
yasmād ṛte na sidhyati yajño vipaścitaś cana | sa dhīnāṁ yogam invati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasmād: From whom/which
    • ṛte: Without
    • na: Not
    • sidhyati: Succeeds
    • yajño: Sacrifice or offering
    • vipaścitaś: Of the wise or learned
    • cana: At all
    • sa: He/that
    • dhīnāṁ: Of the wise or intelligent
    • yogam: Union or practice
    • invati: Invokes or brings forth
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in achieving success in any endeavor, particularly in the context of rituals or sacrifices.
    • It suggests that without the guidance of wisdom, no offering or sacrifice can truly succeed.
    • The phrase "yajño vipaścitaś cana" indicates that the offerings made by the wise are the ones that truly matter and yield results.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a project may fail if it is not backed by thorough research and understanding. For instance, a marketing campaign without proper market analysis may not yield the desired results.
    • In personal relationships, communication and understanding are crucial. A relationship built on assumptions without wisdom may lead to misunderstandings and conflicts.
    • In education, students who approach their studies with curiosity and a desire to understand (wisdom) are more likely to succeed than those who merely memorize information without comprehension.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom in all aspects of life, particularly in actions that require intention and purpose, such as rituals, projects, and personal relationships.
    • It serves as a reminder that success is not merely about the actions we take but the knowledge and understanding we bring to those actions.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom in their pursuits, ensuring that their efforts are meaningful and effective.
