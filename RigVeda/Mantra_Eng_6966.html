सनीतः सुसनितर उग्र चित्र चितिष्ठ सूनृत || प्रासहाः सम्राट् सहुरिं सहन्तं भुज्युं वाजेषु पूर्व्यम् ||
sanitaḥ susanitar ugra citra cetiṣṭha sūnṛta | prāsahā samrāṭ sahuriṁ sahantam bhujyuṁ vājeṣu pūrvyam ||
  • Sanitaḥ: This word can be interpreted as "well-being" or "health." In real life, this emphasizes the importance of maintaining physical and mental health, akin to how individuals prioritize their wellness through exercise, nutrition, and mindfulness practices.
  • Susanitar: This term suggests "well-ordered" or "harmonious." It reflects the idea of living in a balanced environment, similar to how communities strive for harmony through cooperation and understanding among their members.
  • Ugra: Translated as "powerful" or "intense," this word signifies strength. In practical terms, it can relate to the strength found in nature, such as the force of a river or the resilience of a tree, reminding us of the power that exists around us.
  • Citra: Meaning "varied" or "colorful," this word highlights diversity. In real life, this can be seen in the richness of cultures, ideas, and experiences that contribute to a vibrant society, much like a garden filled with different flowers.
  • Cetiṣṭha: This term can be understood as "to be aware" or "to be conscious." It encourages mindfulness and awareness in our actions and thoughts, similar to how meditation practices help individuals become more present and aware of their surroundings.
  • Sūnṛta: This word means "truth" or "righteousness." It underscores the importance of honesty and integrity in our lives, akin to how ethical business practices build trust within communities and foster long-term relationships.
  • Prāsahā: This can be interpreted as "to be strong" or "to endure." It reflects the resilience required to face life's challenges, much like how athletes train to overcome obstacles and achieve their goals.
  • Samrāṭ: Meaning "emperor" or "sovereign," this term signifies leadership and authority. In a real-world context, it can relate to effective leadership in organizations, where leaders inspire and guide their teams towards a common vision.
  • Sahuriṁ: This word suggests "together" or "in unity." It emphasizes the importance of collaboration and teamwork, similar to how successful projects often rely on the collective efforts of diverse individuals working towards a shared objective.
  • Sahantam: Translated as "to bear" or "to support," this term highlights the significance of support systems in our lives, such as family and friends who help us navigate difficult times.
  • Bhujyuṁ: This word can mean "to be strong" or "to have strength." It reflects the idea of inner strength, akin to how individuals draw on their resilience during challenging life events.
  • Vājeṣu: Meaning "in the offerings" or "in the sacrifices," this term signifies the importance of giving and sharing, much like how charitable acts contribute to the well-being of society.
  • Pūrvyam: This word can be interpreted as "from the east" or "from the beginning." It symbolizes new beginnings and the potential for growth, similar to how each sunrise brings a fresh start and new opportunities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing well-being, harmony, strength, diversity, awareness, truth, resilience, leadership, unity, support, inner strength, generosity, and new beginnings. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, from personal health and community relationships to leadership and resilience in the face of challenges. By embodying these principles, individuals can foster a more balanced and fulfilling life, contributing positively to their communities and the world at large.
