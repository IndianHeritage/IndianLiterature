अर्वाचीनं सु ते मन उता चक्षुः शतक्रतो |इन्द्र कृण्वन्तु वाघतः ||
arvācīnaṁ su te mana uta cakṣuḥ śatakrato | indra kṛṇvantu vāghataḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • arvācīnaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is ancient" or "the one who is eternal." It signifies something that transcends time.
    • su: This means "good" or "well," indicating positivity or auspiciousness.
    • te: This translates to "your," referring to a divine entity, in this case, Indra.
    • mana: This word means "mind" or "intellect," suggesting the importance of mental clarity and wisdom.
    • uta: This can be understood as "and" or "also," connecting thoughts or ideas.
    • cakṣuḥ: This means "eye" or "sight," symbolizing perception and understanding.
    • śatakrato: This refers to "the one with a hundred powers," a title often associated with Indra, representing strength and capability.
    • indra: This is the name of the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power, leadership, and protection.
    • kṛṇvantu: This means "may they create" or "may they bring forth," indicating a call to action.
    • vāghataḥ: This term can be interpreted as "speech" or "words," emphasizing the power of communication and expression.
  • Overall Meaning: The mantra invokes Indra, asking for the blessings of wisdom, clarity of mind, and effective communication. It emphasizes the importance of ancient wisdom and the power of words in shaping reality. The mantra suggests that with the right mindset and perception, one can harness the strength and capabilities associated with Indra to create positive outcomes in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, effective communication is crucial. Just as the mantra calls for clarity of mind and speech, a leader who communicates well can inspire and motivate their team, leading to successful project outcomes.
    • In personal relationships, understanding and empathy are vital. The mantra's emphasis on wisdom and perception can be applied to improve interpersonal dynamics, fostering deeper connections and resolving conflicts.
    • In education, the pursuit of knowledge and clarity of thought is essential. Students who embody the principles of this mantra may approach their studies with a mindset geared towards understanding and effective expression, leading to academic success.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for wisdom, clarity, and the power of words. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to cultivate a positive mindset, communicate effectively, and harness one's inner strength to create meaningful change. By invoking the qualities associated with Indra, individuals can strive for excellence in various aspects of life, whether in leadership, relationships, or personal growth.
