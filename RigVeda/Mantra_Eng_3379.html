मन्त्र: उता सिन्धुं विभाल्यं वितस्थानाम् अधि क्षमि |परि ष्ठा इन्द्र मायया ||
uta sindhuṁ vibālyaṁ vitasthānām adhi kṣami | pari ṣṭhā indra māyayā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and, also
    • sindhuṁ: ocean, sea
    • vibālyaṁ: spreading, expanding
    • vitasthānām: of those who are established, settled
    • adhi: above, over
    • kṣami: to forgive, to tolerate
    • pari: all around, completely
    • ṣṭhā: to stand, to be firm
    • indra: the king of gods, a powerful deity
    • māyayā: by illusion, through magic
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the vastness of the ocean and the power of Indra, the king of gods, who stands above and can forgive or tolerate the illusions of life.
    • The ocean symbolizes the depth of knowledge and the vast experiences of life, while Indra represents strength and authority.
    • The phrase "vibālyaṁ vitasthānām" suggests the idea of expansion and establishment, indicating that knowledge and strength can lead to a stable and expansive life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, a leader who embodies the qualities of Indra can guide their team through challenges, forgiving mistakes and encouraging growth, much like how the ocean nurtures life.
    • In personal relationships, understanding and tolerance can help individuals navigate misunderstandings, allowing for deeper connections, akin to the vastness of the ocean that holds countless secrets.
    • In education, the pursuit of knowledge can be likened to exploring the ocean; the more one learns, the more expansive their understanding becomes, leading to a stable foundation for future endeavors.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, forgiveness, and the pursuit of knowledge in our lives.
    • It teaches us that, like the ocean, we should be expansive in our understanding and tolerant of the illusions and challenges we face.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to personal growth, stronger relationships, and effective leadership, making this mantra relevant in various aspects of life.
