देवं-देवं वोऽवस इन्द्रं-इन्द्रं गृणीषाणि |अध यज्ञाय तुर्वणे व्यानशुः ||
devaṁ-devaṁ vo 'vasa indram-indraṁ gṛṇīṣaṇi | adhā yajñāya turvaṇe vy ānaśuḥ ||
  • Devaṁ-devaṁ: This phrase translates to "the divine among the divine." It emphasizes the supreme nature of divinity, suggesting that there are multiple forms of the divine, yet one stands out as the most significant.
  • Vo 'vasa: This means "may you dwell with us." It is an invocation for the divine presence to be among us, highlighting the importance of connection between the divine and humanity.
  • Indram-indraṁ: Referring to "Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, this phrase acknowledges his power and authority. Indra is often associated with rain, storms, and the warrior spirit.
  • Gṛṇīṣaṇi: This translates to "we sing" or "we praise." It indicates an act of devotion and reverence towards the divine, showcasing the importance of worship and acknowledgment of divine forces.
  • Adhā: Meaning "below" or "in this world," it signifies the earthly realm where humans reside and interact with the divine.
  • Yajñāya: This term refers to "sacrifice" or "offering," which is a central theme in Vedic rituals. It underscores the idea of giving back to the divine as a form of gratitude and respect.
  • Turvaṇe: This word can be interpreted as "to the strong" or "to the powerful," indicating the strength and might of the divine forces we are addressing.
  • Vy ānaśuḥ: This phrase suggests "may we be nourished" or "may we be fulfilled," indicating a desire for blessings and sustenance from the divine.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound relationship between humanity and the divine, emphasizing the importance of worship, connection, and the acknowledgment of divine power. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts such as rituals, prayers, and moments of reflection where individuals seek guidance, strength, and blessings from higher powers. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of gratitude and reverence in our daily lives.
