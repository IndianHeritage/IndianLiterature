अनु द्वा जहिता नयोऽन्धं श्रोणं च वृत्रहन् |न तत्ते सुम्नमष्टवे ||
anu dvā jahitā nayo 'ndhaṁ śroṇaṁ ca vṛtrahan | na tat te sumnam aṣṭave ||
  • anu dvā jahitā nayo 'ndhaṁ śroṇaṁ ca vṛtrahan
    • anu - This word means "following" or "after." It signifies the idea of continuity or progression.
    • dvā - Translated as "two," it can represent duality or choices in life.
    • jahitā - This means "to abandon" or "to let go." It suggests the act of releasing or moving away from something.
    • nayo - This word means "guidance" or "leadership." It refers to the direction one takes in life.
    • 'ndhaṁ - This translates to "blindness" or "ignorance." It symbolizes a lack of awareness or understanding.
    • śroṇaṁ - Meaning "hearing" or "listening," it emphasizes the importance of being receptive to knowledge and wisdom.
    • ca - This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking the concepts together.
    • vṛtrahan - This term means "slayer of Vritra," referring to the overcoming of obstacles or challenges, often associated with the deity Indra.
  • na tat te sumnam aṣṭave
    • na - This means "not," indicating negation or the absence of something.
    • tat - Translated as "that," it refers to something previously mentioned.
    • te - This means "to you," indicating a personal connection or address.
    • sumnam - This word means "auspiciousness" or "well-being," representing positive outcomes.
    • aṣṭave - This translates to "eight," which can symbolize completeness or a full cycle.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to abandon ignorance and to seek guidance in overcoming obstacles. It emphasizes the importance of listening and being aware of one's surroundings to achieve auspiciousness and well-being.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can reflect on their lives, identifying areas where they may be blind to opportunities or challenges. By seeking guidance and being open to learning, they can overcome personal obstacles.
  • Leadership: Leaders can use this mantra to remind themselves of the importance of guiding others away from ignorance and towards knowledge, fostering an environment of growth and understanding.
  • Conflict Resolution: In situations of conflict, this mantra encourages individuals to listen actively and abandon preconceived notions, leading to more effective communication and resolution.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for awareness, guidance, and the overcoming of ignorance. By applying its teachings in daily life, individuals can foster personal growth, effective leadership, and harmonious relationships, ultimately leading to a more auspicious and fulfilling existence.
