प्रातर अग्निः पुरुप्रियो विशः स्तवे तातिथिः |विश्वानि यो अमर्त्यो हव्याः Marteṣu रण्यति ||
prātar agniḥ purupriyo viśaḥ stavetātithiḥ | viśvāni yo amartyo havyā marteṣu raṇyati ||
  • prātar: This word means "in the morning." It signifies the beginning of a new day, a time for renewal and fresh opportunities.
  • agniḥ: Refers to "fire," which is a sacred element in Vedic rituals. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence.
  • purupriyo: This translates to "beloved by all." It indicates something that is universally cherished and respected.
  • viśaḥ: Means "the community" or "the people." It emphasizes the collective aspect of society and the importance of unity.
  • stavetātithiḥ: This can be interpreted as "the guest who is praised." It highlights the significance of hospitality and honoring guests in Vedic culture.
  • viśvāni: This word means "all" or "universal." It suggests inclusiveness and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • yo: Translates to "who." It introduces a subject, often referring to a divine or noble being.
  • amartyo: Means "immortal." This term signifies the eternal nature of the soul or the divine essence.
  • havyā: Refers to "offering" or "sacrifice." It denotes the act of giving and the importance of offerings in rituals.
  • marteṣu: This translates to "among mortals." It indicates the human condition and the challenges faced in life.
  • raṇyati: Means "to strive" or "to fight." It suggests the effort and struggle involved in achieving one's goals.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of morning rituals, emphasizing the importance of fire as a purifying force and the value of community and hospitality. It reminds us to honor our guests and recognize the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Practicing hospitality by welcoming guests with warmth and respect.
  • Engaging in community service to strengthen bonds within society.
  • Embracing new beginnings each day, using the morning as a time for reflection and setting intentions.
  • Recognizing the struggles of life and striving to overcome challenges with resilience and determination.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the sacredness of life, the importance of community, and the eternal nature of the soul, encouraging us to live with purpose and connection.
