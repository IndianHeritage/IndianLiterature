तं त्वा विप्रā विपण्यवो जागृवांसः सम इन्दते | हव्यवाहम् अमर्त्यं सहोवृद्धम् ||
taṁ tvā viprā vipanyavo jāgṛvāṁsaḥ sam indhate | havyavāham amartyaṁ sahovṛdham ||
  • taṁ tvā: This phrase translates to "that you" or "to you." It signifies a direction of reverence and intention towards a higher power or divine entity.
  • viprā: This term refers to the wise or learned individuals, often associated with the priests or sages in Vedic tradition. It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in spiritual practices.
  • vipanyavo: This word can be interpreted as "those who are engaged in the sacred knowledge" or "the knowledgeable ones." It highlights the role of those who seek and disseminate wisdom.
  • jāgṛvāṁsaḥ: This term means "those who are awake" or "those who are vigilant." It suggests the importance of being aware and conscious in one's spiritual journey.
  • sam indhate: This phrase translates to "come together" or "unite." It signifies the collective effort of individuals in their spiritual pursuits.
  • havyavāham: This term refers to "the one who carries offerings" or "the one who brings forth sacrifices." It emphasizes the act of giving and the importance of offerings in spiritual practices.
  • amartyaṁ: This word means "immortal" or "divine." It signifies the eternal nature of the soul and the ultimate goal of spiritual realization.
  • sahovṛdham: This term translates to "together with growth" or "in unity with abundance." It emphasizes the idea of collective growth and prosperity in spiritual endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of collective spiritual pursuit, emphasizing the importance of wisdom, awareness, and unity in the journey towards the divine. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as community gatherings, spiritual retreats, or educational settings where individuals come together to share knowledge and grow collectively. The significance lies in recognizing that spiritual growth is not a solitary endeavor but a shared journey that thrives on collaboration, wisdom, and the offerings we make to the greater good. This mantra serves as a reminder to engage with others in our quest for knowledge and spiritual enlightenment, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
