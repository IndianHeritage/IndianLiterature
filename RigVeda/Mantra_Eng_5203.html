यो देह्योऽनमयाद्वधस्नैर्यो आर्यपत्नीः उषसश्चकार। स निरुध्य नहुषो यह्वो अग्निर्विशश्चक्रे बलिहृतः सहोभिः॥
yo dehyo3 anamayad vadhasnair yo aryapatnīr uṣasaś cakāra | sa nirudhyā nahuṣo yahvo agnir viśaś cakre balihṛtaḥ sahobhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • dehyo: of the body
    • anamayad: free from ailments
    • vadhasnair: with the strength of speech
    • yo: who
    • aryapatnīr: of noble wives
    • uṣasaś: of the dawn
    • cakāra: created
    • sa: he
    • nirudhyā: having been restrained
    • nahuṣo: of Nahusha (a king)
    • yahvo: who
    • agnir: fire
    • viśaś: of the universe
    • cakre: created
    • balihṛtaḥ: having strength
    • sahobhiḥ: with companions
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine being who is free from ailments and possesses the strength of speech.
    • The reference to "noble wives" and "dawn" suggests a connection to purity, new beginnings, and the importance of relationships.
    • The mention of Nahusha, a king who was transformed into a serpent due to his pride, serves as a reminder of the consequences of one's actions.
    • The invocation of fire symbolizes transformation, energy, and the essence of life, indicating that this divine being has the power to create and sustain the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In our daily lives, we often seek strength and resilience, much like the divine being described in the mantra. For instance, individuals overcoming health challenges embody the essence of being "anamayad."
    • The concept of "noble wives" can be related to the importance of supportive relationships in our lives, such as family and friends who uplift us during difficult times.
    • The transformation associated with fire can be seen in personal growth experiences, where individuals emerge stronger after facing adversity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, resilience, and the importance of relationships in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to cultivate inner strength and to be mindful of our actions, as they can lead to transformation, both positive and negative.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek healing, foster supportive relationships, and embrace personal growth through challenges.
