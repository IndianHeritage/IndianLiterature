आयं स यो दिवस परि रघुयामā पवित्र ā |सिन्धोर ऊर्मा व्य अक्षरत ||
ayaṁ sa yo divas pari raghuyāmā pavitra ā | sindhor ūrmā vy akṣarat ||
  • ayaṁ sa yo divas: This phrase can be translated to "this is he who is the day." It signifies the presence of a divine entity or force that governs the day, representing light, knowledge, and clarity.
  • pari raghuyāmā pavitra ā: This part means "surrounding the pure one." It suggests the idea of purity and sanctity, indicating that the divine presence is enveloped in purity and righteousness.
  • sindhor ūrmā vy akṣarat: This translates to "the waves of the ocean do not diminish." Here, the ocean symbolizes the vastness of existence and the waves represent the continuous flow of life and experiences. The phrase implies that the essence of life remains constant and unchanging despite the fluctuations.

The mantra as a whole emphasizes the importance of divine presence in our lives, the purity of intentions, and the constancy of existence. It reflects the idea that while life may present challenges and changes (the waves), there is a divine force that remains steadfast and pure (the day and the ocean).


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation to invoke a sense of peace and connection with the divine, helping them to focus on purity and clarity in their thoughts and actions.
  • Personal Growth: The mantra serves as a reminder to maintain purity in intentions and actions, encouraging individuals to strive for righteousness in their daily lives.
  • Resilience in Challenges: Just as the waves of the ocean do not diminish, this mantra can inspire individuals to remain resilient in the face of life's challenges, understanding that change is a part of existence but does not affect the core essence of life.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra encapsulates profound wisdom about the divine, purity, and the constancy of life. It encourages individuals to seek clarity, maintain purity in their actions, and embrace resilience, making it highly applicable in various aspects of life, from spiritual practices to personal development.
