विभक्तारं हवामहे वसोश्चित्रस्य राधसः |सवितारं नृचक्षसम् ||
vibhaktāraṁ havāmahe vasoś citrasya rādhasaḥ | savitāraṁ nṛcakṣasam ||
  • Vibhaktāraṁ: This term can be translated as "the one who is divided" or "the one who manifests in various forms." It signifies the diversity and multiplicity of existence.
  • Havāmahe: This means "we invoke" or "we call upon." It indicates a prayer or a request for divine attention and assistance.
  • Vasoś: This word refers to "the essence" or "the nature of being." It emphasizes the fundamental qualities that define existence.
  • Citrasya: Translated as "of the colorful" or "of the diverse," it highlights the richness and variety in the universe.
  • Rādhasaḥ: This term can be understood as "of the radiant" or "of the luminous," suggesting a connection to light and enlightenment.
  • Savitāraṁ: This refers to "the sun" or "the solar energy," symbolizing life, vitality, and the source of illumination.
  • Nṛcakṣasam: This can be interpreted as "of the human eye" or "of human perception," indicating the importance of awareness and understanding in our lives.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer invoking the divine essence that manifests in various forms, celebrating the diversity and richness of existence. It calls upon the radiant energy of the sun, which is essential for life and enlightenment, while also emphasizing the importance of human perception and awareness.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Embracing Diversity: Just as the mantra acknowledges the multiplicity of existence, we can apply this understanding in our daily lives by embracing diversity in cultures, ideas, and perspectives. For example, in a workplace, fostering an inclusive environment where different viewpoints are valued can lead to greater creativity and innovation.
  • Seeking Enlightenment: The invocation of the sun symbolizes the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment. In our personal lives, this can translate to a commitment to lifelong learning, whether through formal education, reading, or engaging in meaningful conversations.
  • Awareness and Perception: The emphasis on human perception reminds us to be mindful and aware of our surroundings and experiences. Practicing mindfulness can enhance our understanding of ourselves and others, leading to more compassionate interactions.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of recognizing and celebrating diversity. It encourages us to seek knowledge, embrace different perspectives, and cultivate awareness in our lives, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
