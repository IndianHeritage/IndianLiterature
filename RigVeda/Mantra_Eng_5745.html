आ पश्चातान नासत्यā पुरस्ताद आश्विनā यातम अधरād उदक्तāt |आ विश्वतः पाञ्चजन्येन राया यūयम पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
ā paścātān nāsatyā purastād āśvinā yātam adharād udaktāt | ā viśvataḥ pāñcajanyena rāyā yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: from
    • paścātān: behind
    • nāsatyā: O Nasatyas (the twin deities)
    • purastāt: in front
    • āśvinā: O Ashvins (the divine horsemen)
    • yātam: you go
    • adharād: from below
    • udaktāt: from above
    • ā: from
    • viśvataḥ: from all sides
    • pāñcajanyena: with the conch (Pāñcajanya)
    • rāyā: with wealth
    • yūyam: you all
    • pāta: protect
    • svastibhiḥ: with auspiciousness
    • sadā: always
    • naḥ: us
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine twin deities, the Nasatyas and the Ashvins, asking for their protection and blessings.
    • The phrase "from behind and in front" signifies a desire for comprehensive protection, suggesting that the divine forces are requested to guard the devotee from all directions.
    • The mention of "wealth" and "auspiciousness" indicates a prayer for prosperity and well-being in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek protection and guidance during challenging times, such as during a job search or when facing personal difficulties. This mantra can be a source of inspiration, reminding them to seek divine support.
    • In ceremonies or rituals, this mantra can be recited to invoke blessings for a new venture, such as starting a business or moving to a new home, ensuring that the path is clear and auspicious.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine protection and blessings from all directions, emphasizing the importance of spiritual support in achieving success and well-being.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as during transitions, challenges, or new beginnings, serving as a reminder of the divine presence that guides and protects us.
