मन्त्र: उता स्वस्याऽरात्या अरिर हि षा उतान्यस्याऽरात्या वृको हि षः | धन्वन् न तृष्णा सम अरीत तांऽभि सोम जहि पवमान दुराध्यः ||
uta svasyā arātyā arir hi ṣa utānyasyā arātyā vṛko hi ṣaḥ | dhanvan na tṛṣṇā sam arīta tām̐ abhi soma jahi pavamāna durādhyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and
    • svasyā: of one's own
    • arātyā: without obstacles
    • arir: the enemy
    • hi: indeed
    • ṣa: six
    • utānyasyā: of the other
    • vṛko: wolf
    • dhanvan: with wealth
    • na: not
    • tṛṣṇā: thirst
    • sam: together
    • arīta: to be overcome
    • tām̐: that
    • abhi: towards
    • soma: the divine nectar
    • jahi: conquer
    • pavamāna: the purifying one
    • durādhyaḥ: difficult to attain
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of overcoming obstacles and enemies in the pursuit of wealth and prosperity.
    • The phrase "uta svasyā arātyā" suggests that one should strive for their own goals without hindrance.
    • The mention of "arir hi" indicates that there are adversaries or challenges that one must face.
    • The reference to "vṛko" (wolf) symbolizes cunning and the need to be vigilant against threats.
    • "Dhanvan na tṛṣṇā" implies that wealth should not lead to insatiable desires, but rather be pursued wisely.
    • The call to "jahi pavamāna durādhyaḥ" emphasizes the need to conquer the difficult paths to attain the divine essence or ultimate truth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to entrepreneurs who face competition (the "enemy") and must navigate challenges to achieve success.
    • In personal life, individuals may encounter various obstacles (like financial difficulties or health issues) that require resilience and determination to overcome.
    • In a broader societal context, communities striving for development must confront adversities such as poverty or injustice, emphasizing the need for collective effort and wisdom.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of perseverance and strategic thinking in the face of challenges.
    • It highlights that while the pursuit of wealth and success is essential, it should be balanced with wisdom and ethical considerations.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to conquer their inner and outer adversities to attain a higher state of being, represented by the divine essence of "soma."
