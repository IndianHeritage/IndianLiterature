मिम्याक्ष येषु सुदिता घृताचि हिरण्यनिर्णिग उपरा न ऋष्टिः |गुहा चरन्ती मनुषो न योषा सभावती विदथ्येव सं वाक् ||
mimyakṣa yeṣu sudhitā ghṛtācī hiraṇyanirṇig uparā na ṛṣṭiḥ | guhā carantī manuṣo na yoṣā sabhāvatī vidathyeva saṁ vāk ||
  • mimyakṣa: This term can be interpreted as "to be aware" or "to perceive." It emphasizes the importance of awareness in our lives.
  • yeṣu: This means "in whom" or "in which." It indicates a connection or relationship with the subject being discussed.
  • sudhitā: Translated as "well-thought-out" or "intelligent," this word highlights the value of wisdom and careful consideration in decision-making.
  • ghṛtācī: This term refers to "clarity" or "purity," often associated with ghee (clarified butter) in Vedic literature, symbolizing nourishment and enlightenment.
  • hiraṇyanirṇig: This can be understood as "golden" or "radiant," representing prosperity and abundance in life.
  • uparā: Meaning "above" or "beyond," it suggests a higher state of being or consciousness.
  • na ṛṣṭiḥ: This phrase translates to "there is no obstacle" or "there is no hindrance," indicating a state of freedom and ease.
  • guhā: This means "cave" or "hidden place," symbolizing the depths of the mind or the subconscious.
  • carantī: Translated as "moving" or "wandering," it refers to the journey of life and the exploration of one's inner self.
  • manuṣo: This means "of a human" or "human being," emphasizing the human experience and its complexities.
  • na yoṣā: This translates to "not a woman," which can be interpreted as a reference to the masculine aspect of existence or the balance between genders.
  • sabhāvatī: This term means "gathering" or "assembly," indicating the importance of community and collective wisdom.
  • vidathyeva: This can be understood as "in the assembly" or "in the gathering," emphasizing the significance of collaboration and shared knowledge.
  • saṁ vāk: This phrase translates to "with speech" or "with words," highlighting the power of communication and expression in human interactions.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of awareness, wisdom, and clarity in our lives. It suggests that by being mindful and thoughtful, we can navigate the complexities of existence without obstacles. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in decision-making processes, personal growth, and community interactions. For instance, in a workplace setting, fostering a culture of open communication and collaboration can lead to better outcomes and a more harmonious environment. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to seek clarity and wisdom in our thoughts and actions, allowing us to thrive in both personal and communal aspects of life.
