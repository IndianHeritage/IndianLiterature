रथं युञ्जते मरुतः शुभे सुखं शूरो न मित्रावरुणा गविष्टिषु |राजांसि चित्रा वि चरन्ति तान्यवो दिवः सम्राजाऽ पयसा न उक्षतम् ||
rathaṁ yuñjate marutaḥ śubhe sukhaṁ śūro na mitrāvaruṇā gaviṣṭiṣu | rajāṁsi citrā vi caranti tanyavo divaḥ samrājā payasā na ukṣatam ||
  • Mantra: "rathaṁ yuñjate marutaḥ śubhe sukhaṁ śūro na mitrāvaruṇā gaviṣṭiṣu | rajāṁsi citrā vi caranti tanyavo divaḥ samrājā payasā na ukṣatam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • rathaṁ: chariot
    • yuñjate: they join or unite
    • marutaḥ: the winds or storm gods
    • śubhe: auspicious or good
    • sukhaṁ: happiness or joy
    • śūro: the brave or heroic
    • na: not
    • mitrāvaruṇā: the deities Mitra and Varuna
    • gaviṣṭiṣu: in the cows or in the wealth
    • rajāṁsi: kings or rulers
    • citrā: colorful or diverse
    • vi: indeed
    • caranti: they move or travel
    • tanyavo: the strong or powerful
    • divaḥ: of the heavens
    • samrājā: the great ruler or emperor
    • payasā: with nourishment or sustenance
    • na: not
    • ukṣatam: to be neglected or overlooked
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the unity and collaboration of the forces of nature, represented by the winds (Maruts), in creating a harmonious and prosperous environment.
    • The "chariot" symbolizes the journey of life, where the winds (Maruts) are seen as guiding forces that help individuals navigate through challenges.
    • The mention of "sukhaṁ" (happiness) and "śūro" (bravery) emphasizes the importance of courage and joy in overcoming obstacles.
    • The reference to "mitrāvaruṇā" suggests the divine protection and support from the gods, which can be likened to the moral and ethical guidance we receive in our lives.
    • The "rajāṁsi citrā" indicates the diverse paths and choices available to us, akin to the various roles and responsibilities we hold in society.
    • The phrase "samrājā payasā" highlights the significance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual, in achieving success and fulfillment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, teamwork can be seen as the "chariot" where different departments (the winds) come together to achieve a common goal, leading to a successful project.
    • In personal life, facing challenges with bravery and a positive attitude can lead to happiness and fulfillment, much like the mantra suggests.
    • Community support during difficult times reflects the divine protection and guidance mentioned in the mantra, where friends and family act as the "mitrāvaruṇā."
    • The diverse choices we make in our careers and personal lives can be compared to the "rajāṁsi citrā," where each decision shapes our journey.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of unity, courage, and divine support in our lives. It teaches us that by working together and embracing our diverse paths, we can achieve happiness and success.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide us in personal development, teamwork, and community building, reminding us that we are not alone in our journeys.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to seek nourishment—both physical and spiritual—as we navigate the complexities of life, ensuring that we do not overlook the essential aspects that contribute to our well-being.
