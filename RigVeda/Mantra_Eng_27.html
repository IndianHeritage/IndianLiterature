विष्वे देवासो अस्रिधा एहीमायासो अद्रुहः |मेधं जुषन्त वहनयः ||
viśve devāso asridha ehimāyāso adruhaḥ | medhaṁ juṣanta vahnayaḥ ||
  • viśve devāso: This phrase translates to "all the gods" or "universal deities." It signifies the collective presence of divine forces in the universe.
  • asridha: This term means "to be invoked" or "to be called upon." It indicates the act of seeking the blessings or assistance of the divine.
  • ehimāyāso: This can be interpreted as "those who are powerful" or "those who have strength." It emphasizes the might and influence of the divine beings.
  • adruhaḥ: This word means "without enmity" or "harmonious." It suggests that the divine forces are benevolent and free from malice.
  • medhaṁ: This translates to "intellect" or "wisdom." It highlights the importance of mental clarity and understanding.
  • juṣanta: This term means "to enjoy" or "to take pleasure in." It indicates the act of cherishing or valuing something.
  • vahnayaḥ: This refers to "the fire" or "the divine fire." In many cultures, fire symbolizes transformation, purification, and energy.

The mantra as a whole can be understood as a prayer or invocation to the universal deities, asking for their blessings of wisdom and harmony, while acknowledging their powerful and benevolent nature. It emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance in our pursuit of knowledge and understanding.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Seeking Guidance: When faced with difficult decisions or challenges, individuals can recite this mantra to invoke the support of divine forces, seeking clarity and wisdom.
  • Promoting Harmony: In interpersonal relationships, the essence of "adruhaḥ" can be a reminder to approach conflicts with a spirit of non-enmity and understanding, fostering peace and cooperation.
  • Embracing Knowledge: The emphasis on "medhaṁ" encourages individuals to value education and wisdom, motivating them to pursue learning and personal growth.
  • Transformation: The reference to "vahnayaḥ" can inspire individuals to embrace change and transformation in their lives, recognizing the potential for growth and renewal.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of divine forces, the importance of wisdom, and the need for harmony in our lives. By invoking these principles, individuals can navigate their lives with greater clarity, purpose, and peace.
