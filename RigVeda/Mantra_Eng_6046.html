गाथश्रवसं सत्पतिं श्रवस्कामम पुरुत्मानम् |कण्वासो गात वाजिनम् ||
gāthaśravasaṁ satpatiṁ śravaskāmam purutmānam | kaṇvāso gāta vājinam ||
  • gāthaśravasaṁ: This term can be broken down into two parts: "gātha" meaning song or hymn, and "śravasaṁ" meaning one who is renowned or famous. Together, it refers to the one who is celebrated through hymns or songs.
  • satpatiṁ: This word translates to "the true lord" or "the righteous ruler." It signifies a leader who embodies truth and righteousness, guiding others with wisdom.
  • śravaskāmam: This term means "desiring fame" or "seeking renown." It reflects the human aspiration for recognition and respect in society.
  • purutmānam: This word can be understood as "the most excellent" or "the supreme." It denotes something or someone of the highest quality or status.
  • kaṇvāso: This term refers to "the wise" or "the learned." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in leadership and decision-making.
  • gāta: This word means "to sing" or "to chant." It highlights the significance of vocal expression and communication in sharing knowledge and values.
  • vājinam: This term translates to "the one who brings victory" or "the victorious." It signifies success and triumph, often in the context of battles or challenges.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to recognize and honor the true leader who is celebrated through hymns, embodies righteousness, seeks fame through noble deeds, and possesses supreme wisdom. It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and communication in achieving victory and success.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In leadership roles, individuals can strive to be "satpatiṁ," guiding their teams with integrity and wisdom.
  • In educational settings, teachers can embody the qualities of "kaṇvāso," imparting knowledge and inspiring students to seek excellence.
  • In personal endeavors, individuals can aspire to achieve their goals with the understanding that true success comes from righteous actions and the pursuit of knowledge.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the qualities of a true leader and the importance of wisdom, communication, and righteousness in achieving success. It serves as a reminder that fame and recognition should be pursued through noble actions and the sharing of knowledge, ultimately leading to victory in various aspects of life.
