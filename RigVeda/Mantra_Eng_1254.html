माता देवानाम् अदितेर अनीकं यज्ञस्य केतुर्वृहति वि भाहि |प्रशस्तिकृत् ब्रह्मणे नो व्युच्छा नो जन जनय विश्ववारे ||
mātā devānām aditer anīkaṁ yajñasya ketur bṛhatī vi bhāhi | praśastikṛd brahmaṇe no vy u1cchā no jane janaya viśvavāre ||
  • mātā devānām: This phrase translates to "the mother of the gods." It signifies the nurturing and protective aspect of divinity, suggesting that the divine is a source of sustenance and care.
  • aditer anīkaṁ: "Aditi" refers to the infinite or boundless, often associated with the mother of the Adityas (solar deities). "Anīkaṁ" means "without a host" or "unconquered," indicating a state of purity and completeness.
  • yajñasya ketur: "Yajña" refers to a sacred ritual or sacrifice. "Ketur" means "the banner" or "the guiding principle." This suggests that the essence of rituals is to be guided by a higher purpose or principle.
  • bṛhatī vi bhāhi: "Bṛhatī" means "great" or "vast," and "bhāhi" implies "to shine" or "to manifest." This indicates that the greatness of the divine manifests in the world through rituals and devotion.
  • praśastikṛd: This term means "the one who is praised" or "the one who performs good deeds." It emphasizes the importance of virtuous actions and their recognition in the divine realm.
  • brahmaṇe no vy u1cchā: "Brahmaṇe" refers to the ultimate reality or the cosmic spirit. "No vy u1cchā" can be interpreted as "may we not be separated from." This highlights the desire for unity with the divine.
  • no jane: This translates to "not among people," suggesting a distinction between the divine and the mundane, emphasizing the need for spiritual elevation.
  • janaya viśvavāre: "Janaya" means "to create" or "to generate," and "viśvavāre" refers to "the protector of the universe." This indicates the role of the divine in creation and protection of all beings.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine motherhood, the significance of rituals, and the pursuit of unity with the ultimate reality. In real life, it serves as a reminder of the nurturing aspects of the divine in our lives, encouraging us to engage in virtuous actions and rituals that connect us to a higher purpose. It emphasizes the importance of community and the role of the divine in the protection and sustenance of the universe. By recognizing the divine in our daily actions and rituals, we can foster a sense of unity and purpose in our lives, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
