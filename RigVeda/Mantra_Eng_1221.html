याभिः सुदानु औशिजाय वणिजे दीर्घश्रवसे मधु कोशो अक्षरत् |कक्षीवन्तं स्तोतारं याभिर आवतं ताभिर ऊषु ऊतिभिर अश्विनाऽ गतम् ||
yābhiḥ sudānū auśijāya vaṇije dīrghaśravase madhu kośo akṣarat | kakṣīvantaṁ stotāraṁ yābhir āvataṁ tābhir ū ṣu ūtibhir aśvinā gatam ||
  • yābhiḥ - This word can be interpreted as "by whom" or "through whom." It signifies the agents or forces that facilitate certain actions or outcomes.
  • sudānū - This term refers to "good nourishment" or "wholesome food." It emphasizes the importance of sustenance that is beneficial for health and well-being.
  • auśijāya - This word relates to "strength" or "vigor." It highlights the need for vitality and energy in life.
  • vaṇije - This means "in trade" or "in commerce." It points to the economic activities that sustain society.
  • dīrghaśravase - This term translates to "long-lasting fame" or "enduring reputation." It signifies the value of being remembered positively over time.
  • madhu - Meaning "honey," it symbolizes sweetness, pleasure, and the rewards of life.
  • kośo - This word means "treasure" or "wealth." It refers to the resources and riches that one accumulates.
  • akṣarat - This translates to "imperishable" or "eternal." It signifies the everlasting nature of certain truths or values.
  • kakṣīvantaṁ - This term refers to "the one who is strong" or "the powerful." It emphasizes strength and capability.
  • stotāraṁ - This means "the one who praises" or "the worshipper." It highlights the importance of gratitude and recognition.
  • yābhir - Similar to "yābhiḥ," it means "by whom" or "through whom," reinforcing the idea of agency.
  • āvataṁ - This translates to "protected" or "sheltered." It signifies the need for safety and security in life.
  • tābhir - This means "by those" or "through those," again emphasizing the role of agents in our lives.
  • ū - This word can be interpreted as "you" or "your," indicating a personal connection.
  • ṣu - This means "good" or "well," emphasizing positive outcomes.
  • ūtibhir - This translates to "by the powers" or "through the energies," indicating the forces that influence our lives.
  • aśvinā - Referring to the twin deities associated with health and medicine, it symbolizes healing and well-being.
  • gatam - This means "gone" or "departed," indicating a transition or change.

The mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of nourishment, strength, commerce, and the pursuit of lasting fame and wealth. It emphasizes the importance of being protected and supported by various forces in life, including divine energies represented by the Aśvins.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In the context of health, it reminds us to seek wholesome food and maintain our vitality.
  • In business, it highlights the importance of ethical trade practices that contribute to long-lasting success.
  • In personal relationships, it emphasizes the need for gratitude and recognition of those who support us.
  • In spiritual practices, it encourages seeking protection and guidance from higher powers.

Conclusion: This mantra serves as a reminder of the essential elements that contribute to a fulfilling life: nourishment, strength, ethical commerce, and the pursuit of lasting values. It encourages us to recognize the forces that support us and to strive for a life that is rich in both material and spiritual wealth.
