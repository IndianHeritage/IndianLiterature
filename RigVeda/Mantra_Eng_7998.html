एष प्रात्नेन मनमना देवो देवभ्यस् परि |धारयापवते सुतः ||
eṣa pratnena manmanā devo devebhyas pari | dhārayā pavate sutaḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • eṣa: This means "this" or "this one," indicating a specific focus or subject.
    • pratnena: Translates to "by the effort" or "through the endeavor," suggesting the importance of action and intention.
    • manmanā: This can be interpreted as "with the mind" or "with thought," emphasizing the role of mental focus and contemplation.
    • devo: Refers to "the divine" or "gods," indicating a connection to higher powers or spiritual entities.
    • devebhyas: This means "to the gods," suggesting offerings or reverence directed towards divine beings.
    • pari: Translates to "around" or "in all directions," indicating a sense of completeness or universality.
    • dhārayā: Means "by sustaining" or "by holding," which implies support and stability.
    • pavate: This translates to "the wind" or "the air," often symbolizing movement, life force, or energy.
    • sutaḥ: Refers to "the offspring" or "the child," which can symbolize new beginnings or creations.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of focused effort and intention in connecting with the divine.
    • It suggests that through our mental endeavors and actions, we can reach out to the divine forces and seek their blessings.
    • The reference to "the wind" symbolizes the life force that sustains all beings, indicating that our thoughts and actions can influence this energy.
    • The mention of "offspring" signifies the results of our efforts, which can manifest as new ideas, projects, or spiritual growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when a team works together with a clear focus and intention, they can achieve remarkable results, akin to invoking divine support.
    • In personal development, individuals who meditate and concentrate their thoughts on positive outcomes often find that their lives improve, reflecting the sustaining power of their focused efforts.
    • In parenting, nurturing a child with love and intention can lead to the development of a well-rounded individual, symbolizing the "offspring" of our efforts.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of intention and focused effort in our lives.
    • It highlights the significance of connecting with the divine through our thoughts and actions, suggesting that we can draw upon higher energies to support our endeavors.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal growth, professional achievements, or nurturing relationships, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide us towards success and fulfillment.
