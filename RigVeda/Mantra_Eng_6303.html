यद इन्द्र पृतनाज्ये देवास् त्वा दधिरे पुरः |आद इद् ते हर्यतां हरि ववक्षतुः ||
yad indra pṛtanājye devās tvā dadhire puraḥ | ād it te haryatā harī vavakṣatuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • pṛtanājye: in the context of praise or worship
    • devās: the gods
    • tvā: you
    • dadhire: have established or placed
    • puraḥ: in front or before
    • ād: may
    • it: this
    • te: for you
    • haryatā: with joy or happiness
    • harī: the one who brings joy
    • vavakṣatuḥ: may it be revealed or manifest
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the chief deity associated with rain, thunder, and war, emphasizing the importance of divine support in human endeavors.
    • The phrase "yad indra pṛtanājye" suggests that whatever praise or worship is directed towards Indra, it is acknowledged and established by the gods.
    • The mantra expresses a desire for the blessings of Indra to manifest in a joyful manner, indicating that divine favor brings happiness and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, invoking Indra through this mantra can be seen as a way to seek favorable weather conditions for crops, highlighting the reliance on divine forces for sustenance.
    • During festivals, people often recite such mantras to invite blessings and joy into their lives, reflecting the cultural significance of divine connection in celebrations.
    • In personal endeavors, individuals may recite this mantra before starting new projects, seeking the support of higher powers to ensure success and happiness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine acknowledgment and support in human activities.
    • It teaches that joy and prosperity are often linked to the blessings of higher powers, encouraging individuals to seek a connection with the divine.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to appreciate the forces beyond our control and to cultivate a sense of gratitude and reverence in our daily lives.
