उत नः सूत्रात्रो देवगोपाः सूरिभ्य इन्द्रावरुणा रयिः श्यāt |येṣां शुष्मः पृतनासु साह्वान प्र सद्यो द्युम्ना तिरते तातुरीः ||
uta naḥ sutrātro devagopāḥ sūribhya indrāvaruṇā rayiḥ ṣyāt | yeṣāṁ śuṣmaḥ pṛtanāsu sāhvān pra sadyo dyumnā tirate taturiḥ ||
  • uta naḥ sutrātro devagopāḥ: This phrase can be translated as "May the divine protectors, the guardians of the sacred threads, be with us." Here, "sutra" refers to a thread or connection, symbolizing the ties that bind us to the divine and to each other.
  • sūribhya indrāvaruṇā rayiḥ ṣyāt: This part means "May we receive wealth and prosperity from Indra and Varuna." Indra and Varuna are deities associated with rain, thunder, and the cosmic order, representing the balance of nature and the abundance it provides.
  • yeṣāṁ śuṣmaḥ pṛtanāsu sāhvān: This translates to "Those who are strong and steadfast in their endeavors." It emphasizes the importance of resilience and determination in achieving one's goals.
  • pra sadyo dyumnā tirate taturiḥ: This means "May we swiftly attain glory and success." It highlights the aspiration for immediate success and recognition in our pursuits.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine protection, prosperity, and success through resilience and determination. It reflects the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine support.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be invoked during challenging times, such as starting a new venture or facing obstacles. For instance, an entrepreneur might recite this mantra before a crucial business meeting to seek guidance and success.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of divine support in our endeavors and the need for personal strength and perseverance. It encourages individuals to seek harmony with the universe while striving for their goals, reinforcing the belief that success is a combination of personal effort and divine grace.
