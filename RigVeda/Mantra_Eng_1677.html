उद्वत्स्व अस्मा अकृत्नोतना तृणं निवत्स्व अपः स्वपस्यया नरः |अगोह्यस्य यद असस्तना गृहे तद अद्येदम ऋभवो नानु गच्छथा ||
udvatsv asmā akṛṇotanā tṛṇaṁ nivatsv apaḥ svapasyayā naraḥ | agohyasya yad asastanā gṛhe tad adyedam ṛbhavo nānu gacchatha ||
  • udvatsv: This word can be interpreted as "rise" or "ascend." It signifies the act of elevating oneself or one's consciousness.
  • asmā: Meaning "from us" or "from our side," it indicates a collective or communal aspect.
  • akṛṇotanā: This term refers to "not being obstructed" or "not being hindered," suggesting a state of freedom or openness.
  • tṛṇaṁ: Translated as "grass," it symbolizes the basic sustenance and the natural world, representing simplicity and humility.
  • nivatsv: This means "to dwell" or "to reside," indicating a sense of belonging or stability.
  • apaḥ: Meaning "water," it represents life, nourishment, and purity.
  • svapasyayā: This can be interpreted as "with vision" or "with insight," emphasizing the importance of awareness and perception.
  • naraḥ: This word means "human" or "man," highlighting the focus on humanity and human experience.
  • agohyasya: This term refers to "the unapproachable" or "the incomprehensible," suggesting a higher power or divine aspect.
  • yad: Meaning "that" or "which," it connects the previous concepts to what follows.
  • asastanā: This can be interpreted as "the abode of the unworthy," indicating a place or state that is not conducive to growth or enlightenment.
  • gṛhe: Meaning "in the home," it emphasizes the importance of the domestic sphere and personal space.
  • tad: This means "that," reinforcing the connection to the previous ideas.
  • adyedam: Translated as "this day" or "today," it signifies immediacy and relevance to the present moment.
  • ṛbhavo: This term refers to "the wise" or "the enlightened," indicating those who possess knowledge and understanding.
  • nānu: Meaning "not," it negates the following action.
  • gacchatha: This means "to go" or "to move," suggesting a journey or progression.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to rise and elevate oneself, to dwell in a state of openness and awareness, and to seek nourishment from the natural world. It emphasizes the importance of community and the human experience while acknowledging the presence of a higher power that is beyond comprehension. The mantra warns against dwelling in unworthy places or states of being, urging individuals to focus on the present and strive for wisdom and enlightenment.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios. For instance, in personal development, it encourages individuals to rise above challenges and seek growth through awareness and insight. In community settings, it highlights the importance of collective well-being and the need to create environments that foster growth and understanding. Additionally, in spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder to connect with the divine and seek wisdom while being mindful of one's surroundings and choices.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for elevation, awareness, and community. It serves as a guide for individuals to navigate their lives with purpose, seeking nourishment from both the natural world and the wisdom of the enlightened. By applying its teachings, one can cultivate a life of meaning, connection, and growth.
