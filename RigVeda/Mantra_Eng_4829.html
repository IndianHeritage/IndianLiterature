मन्त्र: त्रातारं इन्द्रं अवितारं इन्द्रं हव-हवे सुहवं शूरं इन्द्रम् |हवयामि शक्रम पुरुहूतं इन्द्रं स्वस्ति नो मगवाऽ धात्व इन्द्रः ||
trātāram indram avitāram indraṁ have-have suhavaṁ śūram indram | hvayāmi śakram puruhūtam indraṁ svasti no maghavā dhātv indraḥ ||
  • trātāram indram: This phrase translates to "O Indra, the protector." Indra is often seen as a guardian and a deity who provides safety and security. In real life, this can be likened to the role of a leader or a guardian in a community who ensures the well-being of its members.
  • avitāram indraṁ: This means "O Indra, the one who does not abandon." It emphasizes the reliability and steadfastness of Indra. In everyday life, this can be compared to a loyal friend or family member who stands by you in times of need, providing unwavering support.
  • have-have suhavaṁ śūram indram: This can be interpreted as "I invoke you, O Indra, the one who is noble and brave." It highlights the qualities of courage and nobility. In practical terms, this can relate to individuals who take brave actions for the greater good, such as activists or leaders who fight for justice.
  • hvayāmi śakram puruhūtam indraṁ: This translates to "I call upon you, O Indra, the powerful and widely invoked." It signifies the importance of calling upon strength and power in challenging situations. In real life, this can be seen in moments when people seek motivation or strength to overcome obstacles, such as athletes preparing for a competition.
  • svasti no maghavā dhātv indraḥ: This means "May Indra, the great one, bestow prosperity upon us." It reflects a desire for blessings and prosperity. In a modern context, this can be related to the aspirations of individuals and communities for success, health, and well-being, often seen in prayers or positive affirmations for a better future.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking protection, support, courage, and prosperity from the divine figure of Indra. Its significance lies in the universal human desire for safety, strength, and success. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as during challenging times when individuals seek guidance and support, or in communal gatherings where collective well-being is invoked. It serves as a reminder of the importance of community, loyalty, and the pursuit of noble actions in our lives.
