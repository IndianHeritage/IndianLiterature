सप्त स्वसारो अभि मातरः शिशुं नवं जज्ञानां जेन्यं विपश्चितम् |अपां गन्धर्वं दिव्यं नृचक्षसं सोमं विश्वस्य भुवनस्य राजसे ||
sapta svasāro abhi mātaraḥ śiśuṁ navaṁ jajñānaṁ jenyaṁ vipaścitam | apāṁ gandharvaṁ divyaṁ nṛcakṣasaṁ somaṁ viśvasya bhuvanasya rājase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Sapta: Seven
    • Svasāro: Breaths or vital energies
    • Abhi: Towards or in the direction of
    • Mātaraḥ: Mothers or nurturing forces
    • Śiśuṁ: Child or offspring
    • Navaṁ: New
    • Jajñānaṁ: Born or created
    • Jenyaṁ: Worthy of being born or produced
    • Vipaścitam: Wise or knowledgeable
    • Apāṁ: Of waters
    • Gandharvaṁ: Celestial beings or divine musicians
    • Divyaṁ: Divine or heavenly
    • Nṛcakṣasaṁ: Human-like or anthropomorphic
    • Somaṁ: The sacred drink or elixir
    • Viśvasya: Of the universe or all
    • Bhuvanasya: Of the worlds or realms
    • Rājase: Royal or kingly
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the seven vital energies or breaths that are directed towards the nurturing forces (mothers) that give birth to a new, wise child.
    • It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in the process of creation and nurturing.
    • The reference to waters and celestial beings suggests a connection to the divine and the natural world, highlighting the interplay between the earthly and the heavenly.
    • The mention of Soma indicates the significance of sacred substances that promote health, vitality, and spiritual awakening.
    • Overall, the mantra reflects a holistic view of life, where the physical, mental, and spiritual aspects are interconnected.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In parenting, the nurturing aspect can be seen in how parents provide love, support, and wisdom to their children, helping them grow into knowledgeable and capable individuals.
    • In education, teachers embody the nurturing force by guiding students towards wisdom and understanding, much like the mothers mentioned in the mantra.
    • In health and wellness, the concept of Soma can be related to the importance of nutrition and holistic practices that promote overall well-being.
    • In community building, the idea of seven vital energies can be reflected in the diverse contributions of individuals working together towards a common goal, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of life, emphasizing the roles of nurturing, wisdom, and the divine in the process of creation and growth.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from parenting and education to health and community building, reminding us of the importance of supporting one another in our journeys.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to recognize the divine presence in our lives and the significance of nurturing wisdom in ourselves and others.
