यं ते श्येनः पदाभरात तिरो राजांस्य अस्पृतम् |पिबेद अस्य त्वम् ईशिषे ||
yaṁ te śyenaḥ padābharat tiro rajāṁsy aspṛtam | pibed asya tvam īśiṣe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: who
    • te: your
    • śyenaḥ: eagle or falcon, symbolizing strength and power
    • padābharat: carrying or bearing the path
    • tiro: beyond or away
    • rajāṁsy: kings or rulers
    • aspṛtam: untouched or untainted
    • pibed: may drink or consume
    • asya: of this
    • tvam: you
    • īśiṣe: O Lord or Master
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes a powerful imagery of an eagle, representing strength and the ability to rise above challenges.
    • The phrase "who your eagle carries" suggests a connection to divine guidance and protection, as the eagle is often seen as a messenger between the earthly and the divine.
    • The reference to "kings untouched" implies a state of purity and nobility, suggesting that the blessings or wisdom being sought are free from corruption and negativity.
    • The act of drinking from this source symbolizes the absorption of divine knowledge, strength, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, one can draw parallels to the qualities of an eagle—vision, strength, and the ability to navigate through challenges. Leaders who embody these traits can inspire their teams to achieve greatness.
    • In personal development, individuals may seek to "drink" from sources of wisdom, such as mentors or spiritual teachings, to cultivate their own strength and resilience.
    • In times of adversity, invoking the imagery of the eagle can serve as a reminder to rise above difficulties and maintain a noble spirit, much like how leaders and visionaries have done throughout history.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine guidance and the pursuit of strength and purity in one's actions.
    • It serves as a reminder that by seeking wisdom and embodying the qualities of strength and nobility, individuals can navigate life's challenges effectively.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in leadership, personal growth, and overcoming adversity, encouraging individuals to rise above and remain untainted by negativity.
