विषां कविं विष्पतिम मानवशीनां शुचिम पावकं घृतपृष्ठम अग्निम् |निहोतारं विश्वविदं दधिध्वे स देवेषु वनते वार्याणि ||
viśāṁ kaviṁ viśpatim mānuṣīṇāṁ śucim pāvakaṁ ghṛtapṛṣṭham agnim | ni hotāraṁ viśvavidaṁ dadhidhve sa deveṣu vanate vāryāṇi ||
  • viśāṁ kaviṁ: This phrase refers to the "poet of the people" or "the one who knows all." In a real-life context, this can be likened to a leader or a visionary who understands the needs and aspirations of the community.
  • viśpatim: This term means "lord of the universe" or "the one who governs." It signifies authority and control, similar to how a wise leader or a government official should guide and protect their people.
  • mānuṣīṇāṁ: This translates to "of humans" or "pertaining to mankind." It emphasizes the human aspect of existence, reminding us of our responsibilities towards each other in society.
  • śucim: Meaning "pure" or "clean," this word highlights the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions. In everyday life, this can relate to ethical behavior and integrity in personal and professional relationships.
  • pāvakaṁ: This means "sacred" or "fire." Fire is often seen as a purifying element, symbolizing transformation and renewal. In real life, this can be associated with the idea of personal growth and the cleansing of negative influences.
  • ghṛtapṛṣṭham: This term refers to "the one who is covered with ghee (clarified butter)." Ghee is a symbol of nourishment and abundance, representing prosperity and the richness of life.
  • agnim: This simply means "fire." Fire is a central element in many cultures, representing energy, passion, and the drive to achieve goals. It can also symbolize the warmth of community and family.
  • ni hotāraṁ: This translates to "the one who is invoked" or "the priest." It signifies the role of a mediator between the divine and the earthly realm, akin to spiritual leaders or guides in our lives.
  • viśvavidaṁ: Meaning "the one who knows everything," this term refers to omniscience. In a practical sense, it can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom in our daily lives.
  • dadhidhve: This means "may we offer" or "let us give." It emphasizes the importance of generosity and sharing, which are vital in building strong communities and relationships.
  • sa deveṣu: This translates to "to the gods" or "to the divine." It signifies the connection between the material and spiritual worlds, reminding us of the importance of spirituality in our lives.
  • vanate: This means "to flourish" or "to grow." It symbolizes the potential for growth and development in various aspects of life, including personal, professional, and spiritual growth.
  • vāryāṇi: This term refers to "the best" or "the most excellent." It encourages us to strive for excellence in our endeavors and to seek the highest good for ourselves and others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, purity, knowledge, and the divine connection in human life. It serves as a reminder of our responsibilities towards ourselves and our communities. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in leadership roles, community service, personal development, and spiritual practices. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
