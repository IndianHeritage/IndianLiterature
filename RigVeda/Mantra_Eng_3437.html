मन्त्र: द्वादश द्यून यद अगोऽह्यस्यातिथ्ये रणन्न ऋभवः ससन्तः | सुखक्षेत्राकृत्वान्न अनयन्त सिन्धून धन्वातिष्ठन्न ओषधीर निम्नम आपः ||
dvādaśa dyūn yad agohyasyātithye raṇann ṛbhavaḥ sasantaḥ | sukṣetrākṛṇvann anayanta sindhūn dhanvātiṣṭhann oṣadhīr nimnam āpaḥ ||
  • dvādaśa dyūn: This phrase translates to "twelve heavens." In Vedic cosmology, the number twelve often represents completeness and the full cycle of time. It can be related to the twelve zodiac signs in astrology, which influence human behavior and events in life.
  • yad agohyasyātithye: This means "when the guest arrives." In many cultures, hospitality is a significant virtue. For example, in Indian culture, the saying "Atithi Devo Bhava" emphasizes treating guests as divine. This mantra highlights the importance of welcoming and honoring guests.
  • raṇann ṛbhavaḥ sasantaḥ: This translates to "the brave ones who fight." It signifies courage and valor in facing challenges. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who stand up for justice or fight against adversity, such as activists or soldiers.
  • sukṣetrākṛṇvann: This means "creating a good field." It refers to the cultivation of positive environments, whether in agriculture or personal relationships. For instance, a supportive community can be seen as a "good field" for growth and development.
  • anayanta sindhūn: This translates to "leading the rivers." Rivers are often symbols of life and prosperity. In a real-life context, this can relate to environmental conservation efforts aimed at protecting rivers and ensuring they continue to provide resources for future generations.
  • dhanvātiṣṭhann: This means "standing with wealth." It signifies the importance of prosperity and abundance. In modern life, this can be interpreted as striving for financial stability and success, which allows individuals to contribute positively to society.
  • oṣadhīr nimnam āpaḥ: This translates to "the herbs and the waters." Herbs symbolize healing and nourishment, while water represents life. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of natural resources in maintaining health and well-being, such as the use of herbal medicine and the necessity of clean water.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of various elements of life, such as hospitality, courage, prosperity, and the nurturing of the environment. Its significance lies in the reminder to cultivate positive relationships, honor guests, and strive for a harmonious existence with nature. In real-life scenarios, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and communal lives, promoting a balanced and fulfilling existence. By embodying these values, one can contribute to a more compassionate and sustainable world.
