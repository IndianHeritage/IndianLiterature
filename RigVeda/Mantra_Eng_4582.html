मरत्वन्तं वृषभं वावृद्धानम् अकवारिं दिव्यं शासम् इन्द्रम् |विश्वासाहम् अवसे नूतनायोग्रं सहोदाम् इह तं हुवेम ||
marutvantaṁ vṛṣabhaṁ vāvṛdhānam akavāriṁ divyaṁ śāsam indram | viśvāsāham avase nūtanāyograṁ sahodām iha taṁ huvema ||
  • marutvantaṁ: This term refers to the Maruts, the storm deities in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and power. In real life, this can be likened to the forces of nature that can bring both destruction and renewal, such as thunderstorms that nourish the earth.
  • vṛṣabhaṁ: This word means "bull," which is often a symbol of strength, fertility, and virility. In agriculture, a bull is essential for plowing fields, representing hard work and productivity in human life.
  • vāvṛdhānam: This term means "the one who increases" or "the one who grows." It signifies growth and prosperity, akin to how a business expands or how a community thrives through cooperation and innovation.
  • akavāriṁ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is unrestrained" or "the one who is free." In a modern context, this could refer to the spirit of entrepreneurship, where individuals pursue their passions without limitations.
  • divyaṁ: Meaning "divine" or "heavenly," this word suggests a connection to higher ideals or spiritual aspirations. In everyday life, this can be seen in acts of kindness and compassion that elevate human experience.
  • śāsam: This term translates to "command" or "rule." It can be related to leadership and governance, where effective leaders guide their communities towards progress and harmony.
  • indram: Referring to Indra, the king of the gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms, this symbolizes authority and power. In real life, this can be compared to influential leaders or figures who shape society.
  • viśvāsāham: This means "I have faith" or "I trust." Trust is fundamental in relationships, whether personal or professional, and is essential for collaboration and success.
  • avase: This term can mean "to invoke" or "to call upon." In practical terms, this can relate to the act of seeking help or guidance from others, whether through mentorship or community support.
  • nūtanāyograṁ: This translates to "the new and powerful." It signifies innovation and the emergence of new ideas or technologies that can transform lives, such as renewable energy sources that are reshaping our approach to sustainability.
  • sahodām: Meaning "brother" or "sibling," this term emphasizes the importance of unity and cooperation among individuals. In real life, this can be seen in teamwork and collaboration in various fields, from family to business.
  • iha: This word means "here" or "in this place," grounding the mantra in the present moment and emphasizing the importance of being mindful and aware of our surroundings.
  • taṁ: This refers to "that" or "him," indicating a focus on the subject of the mantra, which is the divine or the higher power being invoked.
  • huvema: This means "we invoke" or "we call upon." It signifies the act of prayer or supplication, where individuals seek blessings or guidance from the divine.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, growth, divinity, and trust, emphasizing the interconnectedness of nature, community, and spirituality. Its significance lies in the reminder to invoke higher powers for guidance and support in our endeavors. In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during times of challenge when we seek strength and resilience, or when embarking on new ventures that require faith and collaboration. By embodying the principles expressed in this mantra, individuals can foster a sense of unity and purpose in their lives and communities.
