अस्मे इन्द्राबृहस्पति रयिं धत्तं शतग्विनम् |अश्वावन्तं सहस्रिणम् ||
asme indrābṛhaspatī rayiṁ dhattaṁ śatagvinam | aśvāvantaṁ sahasriṇam ||
  • asme: This word translates to "for us" or "to us," indicating a personal or collective appeal.
  • indrābṛhaspatī: This is a compound of two significant deities, Indra and Brihaspati. Indra is often regarded as the king of the gods and a god of rain and thunderstorms, while Brihaspati is the god of wisdom and eloquence. Together, they symbolize strength and wisdom.
  • rayiṁ: This term means "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies not just material wealth but also spiritual and intellectual abundance.
  • dhattaṁ: This means "may you grant" or "may you bestow," indicating a request or prayer for blessings.
  • śatagvinam: This word refers to "a hundredfold" or "many," suggesting abundance and multiplicity in blessings.
  • aśvāvantaṁ: This term means "possessing horses," which is a metaphor for strength, speed, and capability. In ancient times, horses were symbols of wealth and power.
  • sahasriṇam: This translates to "thousands," further emphasizing the idea of vastness and abundance.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for the bestowal of wealth and prosperity, guided by the strength and wisdom of the divine. It reflects a desire for not just material wealth but also the wisdom to use it effectively and the strength to achieve one's goals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra when seeking guidance in their personal or professional lives, asking for wisdom and strength to navigate challenges.
  • Business Ventures: Entrepreneurs may use this mantra to invoke prosperity and success in their business endeavors, hoping for abundant opportunities and wise decisions.
  • Community Well-being: Communities can collectively chant this mantra during gatherings or rituals to seek blessings for prosperity and harmony among members.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to wealth and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and strength in achieving one's goals. By invoking the blessings of Indra and Brihaspati, individuals and communities can aspire for a life filled with abundance, both materially and spiritually. This mantra serves as a reminder that true wealth encompasses not just possessions but also the wisdom to use them wisely and the strength to pursue one's aspirations.
