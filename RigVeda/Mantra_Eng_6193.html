मरतः यद धा वो दिवः सुम्नायन्तो हवामहे |आ तु न उप गन्तना ||
maruto yad dha vo divaḥ sumnāyanto havāmahe | ā tū na upa gantana ||
  • Mantra: "maruto yad dha vo divaḥ sumnāyanto havāmahe | ā tū na upa gantana ||"
  • Translation: "O Maruts, we invoke you who are auspicious and who move in the sky; may you come to us, and may we not be obstructed."
  • Explanation:
    • Maruts: The Maruts are a group of storm deities in Vedic literature, often associated with wind, storms, and the forces of nature. They symbolize strength, vitality, and the dynamic aspects of life.
    • Yad dha vo divaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "who are in the heavens" or "who dwell in the sky," indicating the divine nature of the Maruts and their connection to the celestial realm.
    • Sumnāyanto: This term means "auspicious" or "bringing good fortune." It reflects the hope that the Maruts will bring positive energy and blessings to those who invoke them.
    • Havāmahe: This is a call to action, meaning "we invoke" or "we call upon." It signifies the act of seeking the presence and assistance of the Maruts.
    • Ā tū na upa gantana: This phrase translates to "may we not be obstructed." It expresses a desire for protection and the removal of obstacles in one's path.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of uncertainty or challenges, individuals often seek blessings or guidance from higher powers, similar to how the mantra invokes the Maruts for support.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new ventures, people may perform rituals to invite auspicious energies, paralleling the invocation of the Maruts for positive outcomes.
    • In nature, the Maruts can be seen as a metaphor for the forces that bring change and renewal, such as the winds that clear the air and bring rain, essential for agriculture and life.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine forces to bring auspiciousness and remove obstacles. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature and the divine, encouraging individuals to seek support in their endeavors. In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various situations where one seeks guidance, protection, and positive energy, such as during personal challenges, important life transitions, or communal gatherings. By acknowledging the forces that influence our lives, we can cultivate a sense of harmony and purpose in our actions.
