उत त्या मे रौद्राव् आर्चिमन्ता नासत्याव् इन्द्र गूर्तये यजध्यै |मनुष्वद् वृक्तबरिषे रराणां मन्दू हितप्रयसा विक्षु यज्युः ||
uta tyā me raudrāv arcimantā nāsatyāv indra gūrtaye yajadhyai | manuṣvad vṛktabarhiṣe rarāṇā mandū hitaprayasā vikṣu yajyū ||
  • uta - This word can be translated as "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition to what has been previously mentioned.
  • tyā - This means "by you" or "through you," suggesting a reliance on a higher power or divine influence.
  • me - Translates to "my," indicating a personal connection or ownership.
  • raudrāv - This term refers to the fierce or powerful aspect of the divine, often associated with Rudra, a form of Shiva, representing destruction and transformation.
  • arcimantā - This means "radiant" or "brilliant," often used to describe the divine light or energy that guides and protects.
  • nāsatyāv - Refers to the Asvins, twin deities known for their healing and protective qualities, symbolizing duality and balance.
  • indra - The king of the gods, associated with strength, rain, and thunder, representing power and authority.
  • gūrtaye - This means "for the sake of" or "to achieve," indicating a purpose or intention behind the invocation.
  • yajadhyai - Refers to "sacrifice" or "worship," highlighting the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine.
  • manuṣvad - This translates to "human" or "mortal," emphasizing the human experience and the need for divine assistance.
  • vṛktabarhiṣe - Refers to "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is worshipped," indicating the importance of recognizing and honoring the divine.
  • rarāṇā - This means "to be nourished" or "to be sustained," suggesting a need for support and sustenance from the divine.
  • mandū - This term can mean "pleasant" or "agreeable," indicating a positive state or outcome.
  • hitaprayasā - Refers to "with good intentions" or "for beneficial purposes," emphasizing the importance of purity in thought and action.
  • vikṣu - This means "to be invoked" or "to be called upon," highlighting the act of reaching out to the divine.
  • yajyū - Refers to "sacred offerings" or "rituals," reinforcing the significance of worship and devotion.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and support in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of rituals, intentions, and the connection between the human and the divine. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as during personal challenges, seeking clarity in decision-making, or when embarking on new ventures. By invoking the divine with sincerity and good intentions, individuals can find strength, guidance, and nourishment in their journeys. The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of faith and the importance of maintaining a harmonious relationship with the divine forces that influence our lives.
