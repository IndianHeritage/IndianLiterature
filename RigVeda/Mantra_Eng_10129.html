इदं हविः मघवन तुभ्यं रातं प्रति सम्राज् अहृणानो गृभाय | तुभ्यं सुतो मघवन तुभ्यम् पक्वोऽद्धीन्द्र पिब च प्रस्थितस्य ||
idaṁ havir maghavan tubhyaṁ rātam prati samrāḻ ahṛṇāno gṛbhāya | tubhyaṁ suto maghavan tubhyam pakvo3 'ddhīndra piba ca prasthitasya ||
  • Mantra: "idaṁ havir maghavan tubhyaṁ rātam prati samrāḻ ahṛṇāno gṛbhāya | tubhyaṁ suto maghavan tubhyam pakvo3 'ddhīndra piba ca prasthitasya ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "O Maghavan (Indra), this offering is for you; may it reach you as we perform our rituals. We offer you this prepared food; drink it, O Indra, as you set forth."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • idaṁ: This
    • havir: Offering or oblation
    • maghavan: O Maghavan, a name for Indra, the king of gods
    • tubhyaṁ: For you
    • rātam: Ritual or sacrifice
    • prati: Towards
    • samrāḻ: Prosperity or abundance
    • ahṛṇāno: Prepared or made ready
    • gṛbhāya: For consumption or acceptance
    • suto: Offspring or product
    • pakvo: Cooked or prepared
    • piba: Drink
    • ca: And
    • prasthitasya: Of the one who is setting forth
  • Context and Significance:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, who is often associated with strength and power.
    • It emphasizes the importance of offerings in Vedic rituals, where devotees present food and drink to deities as a form of respect and gratitude.
    • The phrase "may it reach you" signifies the desire for divine acceptance of the offerings, which is crucial in Vedic practices.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of gratitude and respect towards nature and the forces that sustain us.
    • For example, when we celebrate festivals like harvest festivals, we often offer food to deities, symbolizing our appreciation for the abundance provided by nature.
    • Additionally, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in acts of charity and sharing, reflecting the spirit of giving and community.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, gratitude, and the significance of offerings in spiritual practices.
    • It serves as a reminder to acknowledge and appreciate the abundance in our lives, fostering a sense of connection with the divine and the community.
    • By incorporating such values into our daily lives, we can cultivate a more harmonious and respectful relationship with the world around us.
