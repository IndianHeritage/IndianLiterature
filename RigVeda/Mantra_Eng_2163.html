स विद्वां अपगोहं कनीनाम आविर्भवन्न उद अतिष्ठत परावृक् |प्रतिः श्रोणः स्थाद व्य अ1नग अचष्ट सोमस्य ता मद इन्द्रश्चकार ||
sa vidvām̐ apagohaṁ kanīnām āvir bhavann ud atiṣṭhat parāvṛk | prati śroṇaḥ sthād vy a1nag acaṣṭa somasya tā mada indraś cakāra ||
  • sa vidvām̐: This phrase can be translated as "he who knows" or "the wise one." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in life.
  • apagohaṁ: This term refers to "the one who is free from sin" or "the pure." It signifies the value of purity in thoughts and actions.
  • kanīnām: This word means "of the wise" or "of the learned." It highlights the significance of learning and understanding in achieving a higher state of being.
  • āvir bhavann: This translates to "may you manifest" or "may you come forth." It is a call for the emergence of wisdom and purity in our lives.
  • ud atiṣṭhat: This means "rising above" or "transcending." It suggests the idea of overcoming challenges and limitations through knowledge and purity.
  • parāvṛk: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is all-encompassing" or "the universal." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of a holistic perspective.
  • prati śroṇaḥ: This phrase means "to the one who listens" or "to the attentive." It emphasizes the importance of being receptive and open to learning.
  • sthād vy a1nag: This can be translated as "standing firm" or "being steadfast." It highlights the need for resilience and determination in the pursuit of knowledge and purity.
  • acaṣṭa: This term means "to see" or "to perceive." It signifies the importance of awareness and insight in understanding the world around us.
  • somasya: This refers to "of the soma," which is often associated with divine inspiration or enlightenment. It symbolizes the pursuit of higher consciousness.
  • tā mada: This phrase can be interpreted as "the intoxication of that" or "the bliss of that." It signifies the joy and fulfillment that comes from wisdom and purity.
  • indraś: This term refers to "Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power and authority. It represents the strength that comes from knowledge and purity.
  • cakāra: This means "he made" or "he created." It signifies the active role of wisdom and purity in shaping our lives and experiences.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of knowledge, purity, and wisdom in our lives. It encourages individuals to rise above challenges, remain steadfast in their pursuits, and be receptive to learning. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as education, personal development, and spiritual growth. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a deeper understanding of themselves and the world, leading to a more fulfilling and enlightened existence.
