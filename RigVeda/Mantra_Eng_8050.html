त्वं हि सोम वर्धयन सुतो मदाय भूर्णये |वृषन् स्तोतारम् ऊतये ||
tvaṁ hi soma vardhayan suto madāya bhūrṇaye | vṛṣan stotāram ūtaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • hi: indeed
    • soma: Soma (a sacred drink, often associated with immortality)
    • vardhayan: increasing or enhancing
    • suto: the one who is born or produced
    • madāya: for the sake of joy or intoxication
    • bhūrṇaye: for the purpose of abundance or prosperity
    • vṛṣan: the strong or powerful
    • stotāram: the one who is praised or worshipped
    • ūtaye: for the purpose of elevation or upliftment
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of Soma, a symbol of vitality and divine inspiration.
    • It emphasizes the importance of enhancing one's life and experiences through joy and abundance.
    • The reference to "the strong" suggests the need for power and strength in achieving one's goals.
    • It highlights the act of praising and worshipping as a means to elevate oneself and others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied to team-building exercises where the focus is on enhancing collaboration and joy among team members to achieve greater productivity.
    • In personal life, it can be reflected in practices such as gratitude journaling, where one acknowledges and praises the positive aspects of life, leading to a more abundant mindset.
    • In community service, uplifting others through acts of kindness can be seen as a way to embody the principles of this mantra, promoting collective strength and prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call to enhance life through joy, strength, and praise.
    • It serves as a reminder that by uplifting ourselves and others, we can create a more prosperous and harmonious existence.
    • In various aspects of life, from personal growth to community engagement, the principles encapsulated in this mantra can guide individuals towards a fulfilling and enriched life.
