यस् ते अनु स्वधाम् असत् सुते न यच्छ तन्वम् |स त्वा ममतु सोम्यम् ||
yas te anu svadhām asat sute ni yaccha tanvam | sa tvā mamattu somyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • anu: following
    • svadhām: self-knowledge or self-awareness
    • asat: non-existent or false
    • sute: in the offering or sacrifice
    • ni: not
    • yaccha: grant or give
    • tanvam: body or form
    • sa: he or that
    • tvā: you
    • mamattu: may I be blessed or may I attain
    • somyam: the essence of Soma, often associated with immortality or divine nectar
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for guidance and clarity. It seeks the divine's assistance in distinguishing between what is real and what is false.
    • The phrase "yas te anu svadhām" emphasizes the importance of following one's true self or inner wisdom.
    • By asking "ni yaccha tanvam," the speaker is requesting not to be granted a false or illusory form, but rather a true essence.
    • The concluding part "sa tvā mamattu somyam" expresses a desire for divine blessings that lead to a state of purity and enlightenment, akin to the qualities of Soma.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a world filled with distractions and misinformation, this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek authenticity in our lives. For instance, when making career choices, one should strive to follow their true passion rather than societal expectations.
    • In relationships, this mantra encourages individuals to seek genuine connections rather than superficial ones, promoting deeper understanding and love.
    • In the context of personal growth, it can inspire individuals to engage in self-reflection and mindfulness practices, helping them to discern their true desires and values.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for self-awareness and authenticity. It encourages individuals to seek clarity in their thoughts and actions, ensuring that they align with their true selves.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, including decision-making, personal relationships, and spiritual practices. By embodying its teachings, one can cultivate a life that is not only fulfilling but also aligned with higher truths.
