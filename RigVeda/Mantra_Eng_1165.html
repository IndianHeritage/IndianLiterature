एनाङ्गूषेण वयं इन्द्रवन्तोऽभि ष्यामा वृजन सर्ववीराः |तन नो मित्रो वरुणो मामहन्ताम् अदितिः सिन्धुः पृथिवी उता द्यौः ||
enāṅgūṣeṇa vayam indravanto 'bhi ṣyāma vṛjane sarvavīrāḥ | tan no mitro varuṇo māmahantām aditiḥ sindhuḥ pṛthivī uta dyauḥ ||
  • Mantra: "enāṅgūṣeṇa vayam indravanto 'bhi ṣyāma vṛjane sarvavīrāḥ | tan no mitro varuṇo māmahantām aditiḥ sindhuḥ pṛthivī uta dyauḥ ||"
  • Translation: "With the strength of our unity, may we become like Indra, the powerful one, in overcoming all obstacles. May the great deities Mitra and Varuna bless us, along with Aditi, the mother of all, and the vastness of the oceans, the earth, and the sky."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • enāṅgūṣeṇa: With strength or unity.
    • vayam: We.
    • indravanto: Like Indra, the king of gods, known for his strength and valor.
    • 'bhi: In overcoming or conquering.
    • ṣyāma: To become or to be.
    • vṛjane: In overcoming obstacles or challenges.
    • sarvavīrāḥ: All heroes or those who are strong.
    • tan: That or may that.
    • no: Us.
    • mitro: Mitra, the deity of friendship and harmony.
    • varuṇo: Varuna, the deity of cosmic order and water.
    • māmahantām: May bless us greatly.
    • aditiḥ: Aditi, the mother of all gods and the infinite.
    • sindhuḥ: The ocean or water.
    • pṛthivī: The earth.
    • uta: And.
    • dyauḥ: The sky or heavens.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and collective strength. For instance, in a team project at work, when all members collaborate effectively, they can overcome challenges and achieve their goals, much like the strength of Indra.
    • The invocation of Mitra and Varuna highlights the significance of harmony and balance in relationships. In personal life, maintaining friendships and family bonds requires mutual respect and understanding, akin to the blessings of these deities.
    • Aditi represents the nurturing aspect of the universe. In real life, this can be seen in the role of a mother or caregiver who provides unconditional support and love, fostering growth and development.
    • The mention of the earth, ocean, and sky symbolizes the interconnectedness of all elements in nature. This can be related to environmental conservation efforts, where protecting our planet is essential for sustaining life.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity, the importance of divine blessings, and the interconnectedness of all life. It encourages individuals to work together, seek harmony in relationships, and respect the natural world.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in various aspects of life, from personal relationships to community efforts and environmental stewardship. By embodying the principles of strength, cooperation, and respect for nature, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and balanced existence.
