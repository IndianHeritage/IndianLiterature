विश्वा धामानि विश्वचक्षः ऋभ्वसः प्रभोस ते सतः परि यन्ति केतवः |व्याणशिः पवसे सोम धर्मभिः पतिर विश्वस्य भुवनस्य राजसि ||
viśvā dhāmāni viśvacakṣa ṛbhvasaḥ prabhos te sataḥ pari yanti ketavaḥ | vyānaśiḥ pavase soma dharmabhiḥ patir viśvasya bhuvanasya rājasi ||
  • viśvā dhāmāni: This phrase translates to "all places" or "universal abodes." It signifies the interconnectedness of all existence and the idea that everything is part of a larger whole.
  • viśvacakṣa: Meaning "all-seeing" or "omniscient," this term refers to a divine perspective that encompasses all knowledge and understanding.
  • ṛbhvasaḥ: This can be interpreted as "those who are righteous" or "the virtuous." It emphasizes the importance of righteousness in one's actions and thoughts.
  • prabhos te sataḥ: Translated as "O Lord of the truthful," this phrase addresses a higher power, acknowledging the divine authority that governs truth and morality.
  • pari yanti ketavaḥ: This means "the wise ones who seek the truth." It highlights the pursuit of wisdom and understanding as a noble endeavor.
  • vyānaśiḥ: This term refers to "the one who brings forth" or "the creator." It signifies the creative force in the universe that brings life and order.
  • pavase soma: "Soma" is often associated with a divine drink that grants immortality and enlightenment. "Pavase" means "to purify," indicating the cleansing aspect of this divine essence.
  • dharmabhiḥ: This translates to "by the principles of righteousness." It emphasizes the importance of living in accordance with dharma, or moral law.
  • patir viśvasya bhuvanasya: Meaning "the lord of the entire universe," this phrase acknowledges the divine authority over all creation.
  • rājasi: This term means "to rule" or "to govern," indicating the power and sovereignty of the divine in the universe.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of universal interconnectedness, the pursuit of truth, and the importance of righteousness. It serves as a reminder that all beings are part of a greater whole, governed by divine principles. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek wisdom, act virtuously, and recognize the interconnectedness of all life. It encourages us to live in harmony with others and the universe, promoting a sense of responsibility towards our actions and their impact on the world around us.
