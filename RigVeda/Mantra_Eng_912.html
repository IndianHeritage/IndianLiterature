आद अङ्गिराः प्रथमं दधिरे वय इद्धाग्नयः शम्या ये सुखृत्यया |सर्वं पणेः सम अविन्दन्त भोजनेम अश्वावन्तं गोमन्तं आ पशुं नरः ||
ād aṅgirāḥ prathamaṁ dadhire vaya iddhāgnayaḥ śamyā ye sukṛtyayā | sarvam paṇeḥ sam avindanta bhojanam aśvāvantaṁ gomantam ā paśuṁ naraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ād: first
    • aṅgirāḥ: the Angirasas (a group of sages)
    • prathamaṁ: the first
    • dadhire: they established
    • vaya: we
    • iddhāgnayaḥ: with the fire that is invoked
    • śamyā: with the offerings
    • ye: who
    • sukṛtyayā: with good deeds
    • sarvam: all
    • paṇeḥ: of the wealth
    • sam: together
    • avindanta: they found
    • bhojanam: food
    • aśvāvantaṁ: with horses
    • gomantam: with cattle
    • ā: to
    • paśuṁ: the animals
    • naraḥ: the humans
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the Angirasas, who are revered sages in Vedic literature, establishing the first rituals and invoking the sacred fire.
    • It emphasizes the importance of good deeds (sukṛtya) in achieving prosperity and abundance.
    • The mantra suggests that through collective efforts and virtuous actions, humans can attain all forms of wealth, including food, livestock, and other resources.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, community service and charitable actions can be seen as a form of sukṛtya, where individuals come together to help those in need, leading to a more prosperous community.
    • In agriculture, farmers often work together to share resources and knowledge, which can lead to better yields and a more sustainable way of living, reflecting the collective effort mentioned in the mantra.
    • In business, ethical practices and corporate social responsibility can be viewed as modern interpretations of good deeds that contribute to overall success and community well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and virtuous action as a means to achieve prosperity.
    • It teaches that through collaboration and good deeds, individuals can attain not only material wealth but also spiritual fulfillment.
    • This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from community building to ethical business practices, highlighting the timeless relevance of Vedic wisdom in contemporary life.
