अभी षु णस त्वं रयिम मन्दसानः सहस्रिणम् |प्रयन्ता बोधि दाशुषे ||
abhī ṣu ṇas tvaṁ rayim mandasānaḥ sahasriṇam | prayantā bodhi dāśuṣe ||
  • abhī: This word can be interpreted as "towards" or "in the direction of." It signifies a movement or an intention towards something desirable.
  • ṣuṇas: This term means "to hear" or "to listen." It emphasizes the importance of being receptive and attentive to knowledge and wisdom.
  • tvaṁ: This translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • rayim: This word means "wealth" or "prosperity." It represents not just material wealth but also spiritual and intellectual abundance.
  • mandasānaḥ: This term can be broken down into "manda," meaning "gentle" or "slow," and "sānaḥ," which refers to "sitting" or "being." Together, it suggests a state of calmness and tranquility.
  • sahasriṇam: This word means "thousandfold" or "multiplied." It indicates abundance and the idea of wealth being vast and plentiful.
  • prayantā: This translates to "those who strive" or "those who move forward." It signifies action and the pursuit of goals.
  • bodhi: This term means "awakening" or "enlightenment." It represents the attainment of knowledge and understanding.
  • dāśuṣe: This word can be interpreted as "to the one who gives" or "to the benefactor." It emphasizes the importance of generosity and sharing knowledge or resources.

The mantra can be understood as a call to the divine for guidance and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of being receptive to wisdom, striving for enlightenment, and recognizing the abundance that comes from a calm and gentle approach to life. The invocation of wealth is not limited to material possessions but extends to spiritual and intellectual growth.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to remain open to learning and to seek knowledge actively. For example, a student striving for academic excellence can meditate on this mantra to cultivate focus and receptivity.
  • Business and Prosperity: Entrepreneurs can recite this mantra to attract success and abundance in their ventures. It serves as a reminder to approach business with a calm and thoughtful mindset, fostering an environment of collaboration and generosity.
  • Community and Relationships: In social settings, this mantra encourages individuals to listen actively and engage with others, promoting understanding and harmony. It can be particularly useful in conflict resolution, where calmness and receptivity are essential.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking wealth and enlightenment through calmness, receptivity, and action. Its significance lies in its ability to guide individuals towards a balanced and prosperous life, fostering both personal and communal growth.
