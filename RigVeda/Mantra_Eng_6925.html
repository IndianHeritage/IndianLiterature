मन्त्र: स्तोत्रं इन्द्राय गायत पुरुंऋम्णाय सत्वने |नकिर यं वृण्वते युधि ||
stotram indrāya gāyata purunṛmṇāya satvane | nakir yaṁ vṛṇvate yudhi ||
  • Stotram: This term refers to a hymn or praise, often directed towards a deity. In this context, it is a call to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition.
  • Indrāya: This word signifies "to Indra." Indra is associated with rain, thunderstorms, and is often seen as a warrior god who protects and provides for his people.
  • Gāyata: This means "to sing" or "to chant." It indicates the act of devotion and reverence through vocal expression.
  • Purunṛmṇāya: This term can be broken down into "puru" meaning "many" or "abundant," and "nṛmṇāya" which relates to "human" or "mankind." It suggests a connection to the collective human experience and the abundance of life.
  • Satvane: This word refers to "truth" or "virtue." It emphasizes the importance of righteousness and moral integrity in one's actions.
  • Nakir: This term translates to "who" or "which," indicating a questioning or inquisitive nature.
  • Yaṁ: This means "that" or "whom," referring to the subject of the inquiry.
  • Vṛṇvate: This word means "to choose" or "to select." It implies an active decision-making process.
  • Yudhi: This translates to "in battle" or "in conflict." It signifies the challenges and struggles one faces in life.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to Indra, asking for his blessings and guidance in the face of life's battles. It emphasizes the importance of truth and virtue in our choices, especially when confronted with challenges.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties, individuals can invoke the spirit of Indra by seeking strength and clarity in their decisions, ensuring they remain true to their values.
  • Community Support: Just as Indra is a protector, communities can come together to support one another in times of need, embodying the principles of abundance and righteousness.
  • Leadership: Leaders can draw inspiration from this mantra to make choices that benefit the greater good, prioritizing truth and virtue in their actions.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in overcoming life's battles while adhering to truth and virtue. It serves as a reminder that in our struggles, we should seek guidance, make righteous choices, and support one another, fostering a sense of community and shared strength.
