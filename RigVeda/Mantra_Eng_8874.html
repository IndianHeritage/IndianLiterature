रात्रिभिर अस्मा अहभिर दशस्येत सूर्यस्य चक्षुर मुहुर उन्मिमीयात् |दिवा पृथिव्या मिथुनā सबन्धू यमीर यमस्य बिभृत्याद अजामि ||
rātrībhir asmā ahabhir daśasyet sūryasya cakṣur muhur un mimīyāt | divā pṛthivyā mithunā sabandhū yamīr yamasya bibhṛyād ajāmi ||
  • Translation: "May the night, with its many forms, open our eyes to the light of the sun, and may the earth, in its duality, support us in our endeavors, as we navigate the paths of life."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • rātrībhir: By the nights
    • asmā: Us
    • ahabhir: By the days
    • daśasyet: Of the ten (often referring to the ten directions or aspects of life)
    • sūryasya: Of the sun
    • cakṣur: Eyes
    • muhur: Again
    • un: To us
    • mimīyāt: May open
    • divā: By day
    • pṛthivyā: Of the earth
    • mithunā: In duality
    • sabandhū: With connections
    • yamīr: May support
    • yamasya: Of Yama (the god of death and the moral order)
    • bibhṛyād: May carry
    • ajāmi: I have come
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the balance between night and day, symbolizing the duality of existence.
    • It highlights the importance of awareness and perception, urging us to open our eyes to the truths of life.
    • The reference to the earth and its support signifies the grounding nature of our experiences and relationships.
    • Yama's mention connects to the moral and ethical dimensions of life, reminding us of the consequences of our actions.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be a reminder to seek balance between work (day) and rest (night), ensuring we do not neglect either aspect.
    • It encourages mindfulness, prompting us to be aware of our surroundings and the lessons they offer.
    • In relationships, it serves as a reminder to nurture connections and support one another through life's challenges.
    • In ethical decision-making, it prompts reflection on the impact of our choices, aligning with the moral teachings of Yama.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of balance, awareness, and moral responsibility in our lives.
    • By integrating its teachings, we can navigate the complexities of existence with greater clarity and purpose.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a guide for living harmoniously with ourselves, others, and the world around us.
