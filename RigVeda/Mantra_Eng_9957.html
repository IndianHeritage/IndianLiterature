इन्द्रस्य नु सुखृतं दैव्यम् सहो 'ग्निर गृहे जारिता मेधिरः कविः |यज्ञश च भूद विदथे चारुर अन्तमा आ सर्वतातिम अदितिं वृणीमहे ||
indrasya nu sukṛtaṁ daivyaṁ saho 'gnir gṛhe jaritā medhiraḥ kaviḥ | yajñaś ca bhūd vidathe cārur antama ā sarvatātim aditiṁ vṛṇīmahe ||
  • Indrasya nu sukṛtaṁ: This phrase refers to the auspicious deeds or actions of Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature. It emphasizes the importance of good actions and their divine nature.
  • Daivyaṁ saho 'gnir: Here, 'daivyaṁ' means divine, and 'saho 'gnir' refers to the fire that is sacred and powerful. This signifies the role of fire in rituals and its connection to divine energy.
  • Gṛhe jaritā medhiraḥ kaviḥ: This part speaks of the wise and knowledgeable seer (kavi) who resides in the home (gṛhe) and possesses intelligence (medhiraḥ). It highlights the importance of wisdom and knowledge in domestic life.
  • Yajñaś ca bhūd vidathe: 'Yajña' refers to the sacred ritual or sacrifice, and 'vidathe' indicates the gathering or assembly. This suggests that rituals are essential for community bonding and spiritual growth.
  • Cārur antama ā: This phrase can be interpreted as seeking the beautiful and ultimate truth or reality. It emphasizes the pursuit of beauty and truth in life.
  • Sarvatātim aditiṁ vṛṇīmahe: 'Aditi' is the mother of the gods, representing boundless and infinite nature. This line expresses the desire to embrace the infinite and the divine in our lives.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance through good deeds, wisdom, and rituals. It emphasizes the importance of community, knowledge, and the pursuit of truth and beauty in life.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied by engaging in community service, participating in rituals that promote unity, and striving for personal growth through knowledge and wisdom. It encourages individuals to act with integrity and seek a deeper understanding of their existence.
