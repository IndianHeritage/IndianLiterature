यजमानाय सुन्वत आग्ने सुवीर्यं वह | देवैर आ सत्सि बर्हिषि ||
yajamānāya sunvata āgne suvīryaṁ vaha | devair ā satsi barhiṣi ||
  • yajamānāya: This term refers to the "yajamāna," the person who performs the yajna (sacred ritual). In a broader sense, it signifies the individual or community that seeks blessings and prosperity through their actions.
  • sunvata: This word means "to be invoked" or "to be called upon." It emphasizes the act of inviting divine presence and blessings into one's life.
  • āgne: This term refers to "Agni," the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine. Fire is a symbol of purity and transformation.
  • suvīryaṁ: This word translates to "good strength" or "excellent vigor." It signifies the qualities of strength, vitality, and energy that one seeks to cultivate.
  • vaha: This term means "to carry" or "to bring forth." It indicates the act of bringing forth or manifesting the desired qualities or blessings.
  • devair: This word means "by the gods." It highlights the divine aspect of the blessings being sought.
  • ā satsi: This phrase can be interpreted as "to be present" or "to be established." It signifies the establishment of divine presence in the ritual.
  • barhiṣi: This term refers to "the sacred grass" used in rituals, symbolizing purity and the connection between the earthly and the divine.

In essence, this mantra is a prayer for invoking the divine presence of Agni to bless the yajamāna with strength and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine and seeking blessings for personal and communal well-being.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in achieving strength and prosperity. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal challenges, community gatherings, or significant life events where blessings and positive energy are sought. By invoking the divine through rituals, individuals and communities can foster a sense of connection, purpose, and support in their endeavors.
