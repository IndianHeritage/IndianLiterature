आयं वो यज्ञ ऋभवो 'कारि यम आ मनुष्यवत प्रदिवो दधिध्वे |प्र वो 'च्छा जुजुषाणासो अस्तुर अभूत विश्वे अग्रियोत वाजाः ||
ayaṁ vo yajña ṛbhavo 'kāri yam ā manuṣvat pradivo dadhidhve | pra vo 'cchā jujuṣāṇāso asthur abhūta viśve agriyota vājāḥ ||
  • ayaṁ vo yajña ṛbhavo 'kāri: This phrase can be translated as "This is your sacrifice, O noble ones." Here, "yajña" refers to a ritual or sacrifice, which is a central theme in Vedic literature. It signifies the importance of selfless actions and contributions to the community.
  • yam ā manuṣvat pradivo dadhidhve: This translates to "which you, O humans, have established in the heavens." It emphasizes the connection between human actions and divine acknowledgment. It suggests that our deeds can create a bridge to the divine, reinforcing the idea that our actions have cosmic significance.
  • pra vo 'cchā jujuṣāṇāso: This means "may we be worthy of your blessings." It reflects a humble request for divine grace and support, highlighting the importance of seeking guidance and favor from higher powers in our endeavors.
  • asthur abhūta viśve agriyota vājāḥ: This can be interpreted as "may we be strong and powerful, O all-encompassing ones." It speaks to the aspiration for strength and unity among people, suggesting that collective power can lead to greater achievements.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of community, sacrifice, and the pursuit of divine blessings. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as community service, teamwork, and collaborative efforts where individuals come together for a common cause. It reminds us that our actions, when performed selflessly and with the intention of benefiting others, can lead to greater harmony and success. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and the recognition of the interconnectedness of human actions and divine will, encouraging us to strive for a higher purpose in our daily lives.
