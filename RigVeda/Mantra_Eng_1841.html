गायत सामा नभन्यं यथा वेर आर्चाम तद वावृद्धानं स्वरवत् |गावो धेनवो बरहिष्य अदब्धा आ यत सद्मानं दिव्यं विवासान् ||
gāyat sāma nabhanya1ṁ yathā ver arcāma tad vāvṛdhānaṁ svarvat | gāvo dhenavo barhiṣy adabdhā ā yat sadmānaṁ divyaṁ vivāsān ||
  • Mantra: "gāyat sāma nabhanyaṁ yathā ver arcāma tad vāvṛdhānaṁ svarvat | gāvo dhenavo barhiṣy adabdhā ā yat sadmānaṁ divyaṁ vivāsān ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine for the enhancement of our spiritual and material well-being. It emphasizes the importance of harmony and abundance in our lives.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • gāyat: To sing or chant, indicating the importance of vocal expression in spiritual practices.
    • sāma: A melody or hymn, suggesting that music and sound play a crucial role in connecting with the divine.
    • nabhanyaṁ: Referring to the sky or heavens, symbolizing the higher realms of existence.
    • yathā: As or like, indicating a comparison or a way of doing things.
    • ver: A term that can imply strength or power, highlighting the need for vigor in our pursuits.
    • arcāma: To worship or offer, emphasizing the act of devotion.
    • tad: That, referring to the divine or the object of worship.
    • vāvṛdhānaṁ: To increase or enhance, indicating growth and prosperity.
    • svarvat: Celestial or divine, relating to the higher aspects of existence.
    • gāvo: Cows, symbolizing nourishment and abundance.
    • dhenavo: Milking cows, representing the sustenance and wealth derived from nature.
    • barhiṣy: Sacred grass, often used in rituals, symbolizing purity and sanctity.
    • adabdhā: Unharmed or untouched, indicating protection and safety.
    • ā: From or out of, suggesting a source or origin.
    • yat: That which, referring to the divine blessings we seek.
    • sadmānaṁ: Abode or dwelling, indicating a place of comfort and safety.
    • divyaṁ: Divine, highlighting the sacred nature of our aspirations.
    • vivāsān: To dwell or reside, suggesting a state of being in harmony with the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied during rituals or prayers where individuals seek blessings for prosperity and protection for their families.
    • In a community setting, it can be recited during festivals to invoke abundance and harmony among the members, fostering a sense of unity.
    • In personal development, the mantra encourages individuals to cultivate a mindset of growth and positivity, akin to nurturing a garden for it to flourish.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine blessings that enhance both spiritual and material aspects of life. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature, community, and the divine. By invoking this mantra, individuals can align themselves with higher energies, promoting well-being, abundance, and harmony in their lives. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal prayers to community gatherings, emphasizing the importance of devotion and gratitude in our daily existence.
