अश्वो न क्रन्दञ जनिभिः सम इध्यते वैश्वानरः कुशिकेभिर युगे-युगे | स नो अग्निः सुवीर्यं स्वव्यं दधातु रत्नम् अमृतेषु जागृविः ||
aśvo na krandañ janibhiḥ sam idhyate vaiśvānaraḥ kuśikebhir yuge-yuge | sa no agniḥ suvīryaṁ svaśvyaṁ dadhātu ratnam amṛteṣu jāgṛviḥ ||
  • Mantra: "aśvo na krandañ janibhiḥ sam idhyate vaiśvānaraḥ kuśikebhir yuge-yuge | sa no agniḥ suvīryaṁ svaśvyaṁ dadhātu ratnam amṛteṣu jāgṛviḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśvo: horse, symbolizing strength and vitality.
    • na: not.
    • krandañ: crying or lamenting.
    • janibhiḥ: by the beings or people.
    • sam: together or in harmony.
    • idhyate: is invoked or arises.
    • vaiśvānaraḥ: the universal fire or the divine energy.
    • kuśikebhir: by the wise or learned ones.
    • yuge-yuge: in every age or era.
    • sa: may he.
    • no: our.
    • agniḥ: fire, representing knowledge and purity.
    • suvīryaṁ: great strength or valor.
    • svaśvyaṁ: our own or personal.
    • dadhātu: may he bestow or grant.
    • ratnam: treasure or wealth.
    • amṛteṣu: in the immortal or eternal.
    • jāgṛviḥ: those who are awake or enlightened.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the strength and vitality represented by the horse, emphasizing that true power does not come from lamentation or weakness.
    • The invocation of the universal fire (vaiśvānaraḥ) suggests a connection to divine energy that is accessible to all beings, especially when they come together in harmony.
    • The phrase "kuśikebhir yuge-yuge" indicates that this wisdom and strength are timeless, applicable in every era, reminding us that the pursuit of knowledge and unity is a continuous journey.
    • The request for "suvīryaṁ svaśvyaṁ" highlights the importance of personal strength and valor, encouraging individuals to cultivate their own inner resources.
    • Finally, the mantra concludes with a plea for the bestowal of treasures (ratnam) that are eternal, signifying that true wealth lies in enlightenment and spiritual awakening.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied in leadership contexts where collaboration and unity are essential for success. For instance, a team working together harmoniously can achieve greater results than individuals working in isolation.
    • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their strengths and seek to cultivate them, much like athletes training to enhance their physical capabilities.
    • In community building, the mantra serves as a reminder that collective wisdom and shared goals can lead to a more prosperous and enlightened society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, unity, and the pursuit of knowledge across all ages. It encourages individuals to seek their inner power and work together for a common good.
    • Its application in real life can be seen in various domains, including personal growth, teamwork, and community engagement, making it a timeless guide for living a fulfilling and purposeful life.
