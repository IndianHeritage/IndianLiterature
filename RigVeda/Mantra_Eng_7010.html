त्वं सोम पितृभिः संविदानोऽनु dyāvāpṛthivī ā ततान्थ |तस्मै त इन्दो हविषा विद्हेम वयं स्याम पतयो रयीणाम ||
tvaṁ soma pitṛbhiḥ saṁvidāno 'nu dyāvāpṛthivī ā tatantha | tasmai ta indo haviṣā vidhema vayaṁ syāma patayo rayīṇām ||
  • tvaṁ soma pitṛbhiḥ saṁvidāno: This phrase can be translated as "You, O Soma, who are known by the ancestors." Here, Soma refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. The mention of "pitṛbhiḥ" (ancestors) emphasizes the connection between the divine and the lineage of humanity.
  • anu dyāvāpṛthivī ā tatantha: This part means "You spread across the sky and the earth." It signifies the omnipresence of the divine, suggesting that the essence of Soma permeates all aspects of existence, both celestial and terrestrial.
  • tasmai ta indo haviṣā vidhema: Translated, this means "To that Indra, we offer our oblation." Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic texts, often associated with strength and the power of storms. This line indicates a ritualistic offering, highlighting the importance of devotion and gratitude towards the divine forces that govern nature.
  • vayaṁ syāma patayo rayīṇām: This final phrase translates to "May we be strong, O Lord, in wealth." It expresses a desire for prosperity and strength, not just in material wealth but also in spiritual and communal well-being.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of Vedic spirituality, emphasizing the interconnectedness of the divine, nature, and humanity. It reflects the importance of honoring ancestors and the divine forces that sustain life.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be applied in various ways:
    • Rituals and Offerings: Just as the mantra suggests offerings to the divine, individuals can engage in rituals that express gratitude for the blessings in their lives, fostering a sense of community and connection.
    • Environmental Awareness: The acknowledgment of the earth and sky encourages a respect for nature. This can translate into sustainable practices and a commitment to protecting the environment.
    • Family and Ancestry: Recognizing the role of ancestors can inspire individuals to honor their heritage, fostering a sense of identity and belonging.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in all aspects of life and the importance of gratitude, connection, and respect for both the past and the natural world. By integrating these principles into daily life, individuals can cultivate a deeper sense of purpose and community.
