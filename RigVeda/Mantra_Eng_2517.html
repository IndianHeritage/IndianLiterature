इळाम अग्नि पुरुदंसं सनिं गोः शश्वत्तमं हवमानाय साधा |स्यान नः सूनुस तनयो विजावाग्नि सा ते सुमतिर भूत्व अस्मे ||
iḻām agne purudaṁsaṁ saniṁ goḥ śaśvattamaṁ havamānāya sādha | syān naḥ sūnus tanayo vijāvāgne sā te sumatir bhūtv asme ||
  • iḻām agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni," referring to the Vedic fire god, Agni, who symbolizes energy, light, and the divine messenger between humans and the gods.
  • purudaṁsaṁ: This means "of great glory" or "of great fame," highlighting Agni's esteemed position in Vedic rituals and his role as a purifying force.
  • saniṁ goḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who is the protector of the cattle," indicating Agni's role in safeguarding wealth and prosperity, particularly in agrarian societies where cattle were vital.
  • śaśvattamaṁ: This means "the eternal" or "the most enduring," emphasizing the timeless nature of Agni and the fire he represents, which is a constant in human life.
  • havamānāya: This translates to "to the one who is invoked," indicating the act of calling upon Agni during rituals, which is a common practice in Vedic traditions.
  • sādha: This means "to fulfill" or "to accomplish," suggesting the desire for Agni to help achieve one's goals and aspirations.
  • syān naḥ sūnus: This phrase means "may he be our son," indicating a wish for Agni to bless the family with progeny and prosperity.
  • tanayo vijāvāgne: This translates to "the son of wisdom, O Agni," suggesting that Agni is not just a physical entity but also embodies wisdom and knowledge.
  • sā te sumatir bhūtv asme: This means "may we have good thoughts from you," expressing a desire for Agni to inspire positive and wise thoughts in the devotees.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of Agni as a divine force that connects humans with the divine, representing purity, wisdom, and prosperity. It reflects the Vedic belief in invoking deities for blessings in various aspects of life.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a call for guidance and support in personal and communal endeavors. For instance, during significant life events such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies, people often light a fire and recite mantras to invoke blessings for a prosperous and harmonious life.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of seeking wisdom, and the need for divine support in achieving one's goals. It encourages individuals to cultivate positive thoughts and actions, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
