एष वः स्तोमो मरुत इयं गीर मांदार्यस्य मान्यस्य कारोः |एषा यासिष्ट तंवे वयां विद्यामेषं वृजनं जीरदानुम् ||
eṣa vaḥ stomo maruta iyaṁ gīr māndāryasya mānyasya kāroḥ | eṣā yāsīṣṭa tanve vayāṁ vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum ||
  • eṣa vaḥ stomo maruta iyaṁ gīr māndāryasya mānyasya kāroḥ
    • eṣa - This word translates to "this" or "this is," indicating a focus on the subject at hand.
    • vaḥ - Meaning "of you," it signifies a connection or relationship with the audience or the divine.
    • stomo - Refers to "praise" or "hymn," suggesting the act of offering reverence or acknowledgment.
    • maruta - This term is associated with the "Maruts," the storm deities in Vedic literature, representing power and energy.
    • iyaṁ - Translates to "this," reinforcing the focus on the present moment or offering.
    • gīr - Meaning "song" or "chant," it emphasizes the importance of vocal expression in worship.
    • māndāryasya - Refers to "of the Mānḍāra tree," which symbolizes nourishment and sustenance.
    • mānyasya - Meaning "of the honored" or "respected," indicating reverence towards the subject of the hymn.
    • kāroḥ - Refers to "the creator" or "the doer," emphasizing the active role of the divine in creation.
  • eṣā yāsīṣṭa tanve vayāṁ vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum
    • eṣā - Again, "this," reinforcing the immediacy of the message.
    • yāsīṣṭa - Meaning "that which is desired" or "that which is sought after," indicating aspirations or goals.
    • tanve - Refers to "in the body" or "in the form," suggesting a physical manifestation of desires.
    • vayāṁ - Meaning "our," indicating a collective identity or shared experience.
    • vidyāmeṣaṁ - Translates to "this knowledge," emphasizing the importance of wisdom and understanding.
    • vṛjanaṁ - Refers to "the gathering" or "the community," highlighting the significance of collective effort.
    • jīradānum - Meaning "the vital energy" or "life force," indicating the essence of existence and vitality.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of praise, community, and the pursuit of knowledge. It highlights the relationship between the divine and humanity, suggesting that through reverence and collective aspiration, one can attain wisdom and vitality. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • In community gatherings or spiritual practices, where collective praise and acknowledgment of the divine can foster unity and strength.
  • In educational settings, where the pursuit of knowledge is celebrated as a vital force for personal and communal growth.
  • In personal development, where recognizing and honoring one's aspirations can lead to a more fulfilling and energized life.

Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of honoring the divine through our actions and intentions.
