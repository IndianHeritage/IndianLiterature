गर्भे योषाम् अदधुर् वत्सम् आसन्य अपीच्येन मनसोता जिह्वया |स विś्वाहाः सुमना योग्याः अभि सिषासनिर् वनते कार इज् जितिम् ||
garbhe yoṣām adadhur vatsam āsany apīcyena manasota jihvayā | sa viśvāhā sumanā yogyā abhi siṣāsanir vanate kāra ij jitim ||
  • Garbhe yoṣām adadhur: This phrase translates to "In the womb of women, they have placed the calf." Here, "garbhe" refers to the womb, and "yoṣām" refers to women. The imagery evokes the nurturing aspect of motherhood, emphasizing the importance of women in the creation and sustenance of life.
  • Vatsam āsany apīcyena: This means "They have placed the calf, even in the womb." The "calf" symbolizes new life or potential. This part of the mantra highlights the idea that life begins in the womb, and the nurturing environment provided by women is crucial for the development of new beings.
  • Manasota jihvayā: This translates to "With the tongue of the mind." Here, "manasota" refers to the mind, and "jihvayā" refers to the tongue. This suggests that our thoughts and intentions (the tongue of the mind) play a significant role in shaping our reality and experiences.
  • Sa viśvāhā sumanā yogyā: This means "He who is all-encompassing, with a good mind, is fit." "Viśvāhā" indicates universality, while "sumanā" refers to a good or pure mind. This part emphasizes the importance of having a positive mindset and being open to the universe's possibilities.
  • Abhi siṣāsanir vanate: This translates to "He who is established in the world." This phrase suggests that one must be grounded and aware of their surroundings while striving for higher ideals.
  • Kāra ij jitim: This means "The one who creates the world." "Kāra" refers to the creator, and "jitim" refers to the world. This part of the mantra highlights the role of creation and the importance of actively participating in the world around us.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of motherhood, the nurturing of life, and the power of the mind in shaping our reality. It teaches us that the environment we create, both physically and mentally, is crucial for the development of new life and ideas. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to parenting, education, and personal development. By fostering a positive mindset and nurturing environment, we can contribute to the growth and well-being of future generations. The mantra serves as a reminder of our interconnectedness and the responsibilities we hold in creating a better world.
