तेन नो वाजिनीवासू पश्वे तोकाय शं गवे | वहतम पीवरीर इषः ||
tena no vājinīvasū paśve tokāya śaṁ gave | vahatam pīvarīr iṣaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "tena no vājinīvasū paśve tokāya śaṁ gave | vahatam pīvarīr iṣaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tena: by that
    • no: to us
    • vājinīvasū: the one who is strong and powerful (often interpreted as a metaphor for wealth or prosperity)
    • paśve: in cattle or livestock
    • tokāya: for offspring or progeny
    • śaṁ: auspiciousness or well-being
    • gave: in the cow or in the context of cattle
    • vahatam: may carry or bring
    • pīvarīr: the one who is abundant or rich
    • iṣaḥ: strength or power
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for prosperity and well-being, particularly in the context of livestock and wealth.
    • The invocation of "vājinīvasū" suggests a desire for strength and abundance, which can be related to both material wealth and spiritual richness.
    • The mention of "paśve" and "gave" emphasizes the importance of cattle in Vedic society, symbolizing wealth, sustenance, and the nurturing of life.
    • The phrase "vahatam pīvarīr iṣaḥ" indicates a wish for the blessings of strength and abundance to be brought to us, enhancing our lives and ensuring our prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, cattle are often seen as a measure of wealth. This mantra can be recited by farmers seeking blessings for a fruitful harvest and healthy livestock.
    • In modern contexts, the principles of this mantra can be applied to business and entrepreneurship, where individuals seek strength and prosperity in their ventures.
    • Families may use this mantra during rituals to invoke blessings for their children, hoping for their well-being and success in life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of prosperity, strength, and well-being in both personal and communal life.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of material wealth and spiritual richness, encouraging individuals to seek balance in their pursuits.
    • In contemporary life, the essence of this mantra can inspire individuals to strive for success while nurturing their relationships and responsibilities towards family and community.
