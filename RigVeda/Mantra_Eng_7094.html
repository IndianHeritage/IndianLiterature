अच्छा हि त्वा सहसः सूनो अंगिरः श्रुचश्चरन्त्यध्वरे |ऊर्जो नपातं घृतकेशम इमहे 'ग्निं यज्ञेषु पूर्व्यम् ||
acchā hi tvā sahasaḥ sūno aṅgiraḥ srucaś caranty adhvare | ūrjo napātaṁ ghṛtakeśam īmahe 'gniṁ yajñeṣu pūrvyam ||
  • acchā hi tvā sahasaḥ sūno aṅgiraḥ srucaś caranty adhvare
    • acchā hi tvā: This phrase can be interpreted as "Indeed, we invoke you." It signifies a call to a divine presence, emphasizing the importance of connection with the divine in rituals.
    • sahasaḥ sūno: This translates to "the son of the mighty." It refers to a powerful deity, often associated with strength and protection.
    • aṅgiraḥ: A reference to the Angiras, a group of sages known for their wisdom and knowledge. This highlights the importance of wisdom in spiritual practices.
    • srucaś caranty adhvare: This means "who moves in the rituals." It indicates the active participation of the divine in the rituals performed by humans.
  • ūrjo napātaṁ ghṛtakeśam īmahe 'gniṁ yajñeṣu pūrvyam
    • ūrjo napātaṁ: This phrase translates to "the offspring of energy." It signifies the vital force or energy that sustains life and the universe.
    • ghṛtakeśam: This means "the one with golden hair," often referring to Agni, the fire god. Fire is a symbol of transformation and purification.
    • īmahe: This translates to "we invoke." It emphasizes the act of calling upon the divine for assistance and blessings.
    • 'gniṁ yajñeṣu pūrvyam: This means "Agni, the first in sacrifices." It highlights the significance of Agni in Vedic rituals, where fire is central to offerings and prayers.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energies and the role of Agni in rituals. In real life, it signifies the need for spiritual connection and the pursuit of wisdom and strength in our endeavors. Just as the sages called upon Agni for guidance and support, individuals today can seek divine assistance in their personal and professional lives through prayer, meditation, and rituals. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, energy, and the divine, encouraging us to engage in practices that foster spiritual growth and harmony.
