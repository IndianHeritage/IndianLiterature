तं त्वा हस्तिनो मधुमन्तम अद्रिभिर दूहन्त्य अप्सु वृषभं दश क्षिपः | इन्द्रं सोम मादयन दैव्यम जनं सिन्धोर इवोर्मिः पवमानो अर्षसि ||
taṁ tvā hastino madhumantam adribhir duhanty apsu vṛṣabhaṁ daśa kṣipaḥ | indraṁ soma mādayan daivyaṁ janaṁ sindhor ivormiḥ pavamāno arṣasi ||
  • taṁ tvā hastino madhumantam: This phrase can be translated as "to you, O strong one, who is full of sweetness." Here, "hastino" refers to strength, often associated with elephants, symbolizing power and might. "Madhumantam" signifies sweetness or richness, indicating a benevolent and nurturing aspect.
  • adribhir duhanty apsu vṛṣabhaṁ: This part means "the mountains are giving forth the waters, like a bull." It illustrates the idea of nature's bounty, where mountains (symbolizing stability and strength) provide water (a source of life), akin to a bull that is strong and nurturing.
  • daśa kṣipaḥ: Translated as "ten swift ones," this could refer to the swift-moving rivers or forces of nature that bring life and sustenance. The number ten often symbolizes completeness in Vedic literature.
  • indraṁ soma mādayan: This phrase means "Indra, who is made joyful by Soma." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength and power, while Soma is a sacred drink that symbolizes immortality and divine inspiration. This line emphasizes the importance of divine blessings and joy in life.
  • daivyaṁ janaṁ sindhor ivormiḥ: This translates to "the divine people, like the waves of the ocean." It suggests a connection between humanity and the divine, indicating that people can embody divine qualities, much like the waves that are part of the ocean yet distinct in their forms.
  • pavamāno arṣasi: This means "flowing, you rise." It signifies the idea of elevation and progress, suggesting that through divine blessings and the nurturing aspects of nature, one can rise and achieve greatness.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the interconnectedness of strength, nature, and divinity. It highlights the importance of nurturing and the blessings of the divine in achieving success and joy in life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to the way we seek strength and support from nature and the community around us. It encourages us to recognize the divine in ourselves and others, promoting a sense of unity and purpose. By acknowledging the sweetness of life and the nurturing forces around us, we can strive for personal growth and collective well-being.
