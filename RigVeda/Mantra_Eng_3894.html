एव हि त्वां ऋतुथा यातयन्तं मघा विप्रेभ्यो ददतं श्रिणोमि |किं ते ब्रह्माणो गृहते सखायो ये त्वायां निदधुः कामं इन्द्र ||
evā hi tvām ṛtuthā yātayantam maghā viprebhyo dadataṁ śṛṇomi | kiṁ te brahmāṇo gṛhate sakhāyo ye tvāyā nidadhuḥ kāmam indra ||
  • evā hi tvām - This phrase translates to "Indeed, I call upon you." It signifies a direct invocation, suggesting a deep connection and respect towards the entity being addressed, often a deity or a cosmic principle.
  • ṛtuthā yātayantam - This can be interpreted as "who moves in accordance with truth." Here, "ṛtu" refers to the natural order or truth, indicating that the subject operates within the framework of cosmic law.
  • maghā viprebhyo dadataṁ - This means "who grants wealth to the wise." It highlights the importance of wisdom and knowledge, suggesting that true prosperity comes from understanding and enlightenment.
  • śṛṇomi - This translates to "I hear." It indicates receptiveness and the importance of listening, whether to divine guidance or the wisdom of others.
  • kiṁ te brahmāṇo - This phrase means "What do the wise ones (Brahmanas) say to you?" It emphasizes the role of learned individuals in society and their insights into the divine or cosmic order.
  • gṛhate sakhāyo - This translates to "friends who dwell in the home." It suggests the importance of community and relationships, indicating that support and companionship are vital in life.
  • ye tvāyā nidadhuḥ - This means "those who have placed their desires upon you." It reflects the idea of devotion and reliance on a higher power for fulfillment of desires.
  • kāmam indra - This translates to "O Indra, fulfill our desires." Indra, often seen as a king of gods, symbolizes strength and the ability to grant wishes, representing the hope for divine intervention in human affairs.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and support in the pursuit of truth and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom, community, and the role of the divine in fulfilling human desires.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:
    • In times of decision-making, invoking the principles of truth and wisdom can lead to better outcomes.
    • Building strong relationships and communities can provide support and guidance, much like the friends mentioned in the mantra.
    • Seeking divine assistance in personal goals or desires can foster a sense of hope and purpose.
