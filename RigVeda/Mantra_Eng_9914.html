अति विश्वाः परिष्टाहः स्तेन इव व्रजम् अक्रमुः |ओषधिः प्राचुच्यवुर यत किं च तन्भोऽरपः ||
ati viśvāḥ pariṣṭhāḥ stena iva vrajam akramuḥ | oṣadhīḥ prācucyavur yat kiṁ ca tanvo3 rapaḥ ||
  • ati viśvāḥ pariṣṭhāḥ: This phrase can be translated as "all beings are established" or "all creatures are firmly placed." It signifies the interconnectedness of all life forms and the importance of harmony in the universe.
  • stenā iva vrajam akramuḥ: This part translates to "like thieves, they do not transgress." It suggests that despite the potential for chaos or wrongdoing, there exists a natural order that is respected by all beings.
  • oṣadhīḥ prācucyavur: This means "the plants have flourished." It highlights the significance of nature and its role in sustaining life, emphasizing the importance of vegetation in our ecosystem.
  • yat kiṁ ca tanvo3 rapaḥ: This translates to "whatever is in the bodies." It refers to the essence or life force present in all living beings, indicating that every entity has its own unique vitality.

In conclusion, this mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the unity and interdependence of all life forms. It teaches us that while there may be challenges and potential for disorder, there is an inherent respect for the natural order. The flourishing of plants symbolizes the sustenance of life, reminding us of our reliance on nature. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as environmental conservation, promoting biodiversity, and fostering a sense of community among diverse beings. By recognizing our interconnectedness, we can work towards a more harmonious existence, respecting both nature and each other.
