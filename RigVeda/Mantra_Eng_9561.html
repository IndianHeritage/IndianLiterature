अक्षण्वन्तः कर्णवन्तः सखायो मनोजवेष्व असमा बभूवुः |आदघ्नासा उपकक्षासा उ त्वे ह्रदाऽइव स्नात्वा उ त्वे ददृश्रे ||
akṣaṇvantaḥ karṇavantaḥ sakhāyo manojaveṣv asamā babhūvuḥ | ādaghnāsa upakakṣāsa u tve hradā iva snātvā u tve dadṛśre ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • akṣaṇvantaḥ: Those who have eyes or vision.
    • karṇavantaḥ: Those who have ears or are able to hear.
    • sakhāyo: Friends or companions.
    • manojaveṣv: In the realm of the mind or with mental agility.
    • asamā: Unequaled or unparalleled.
    • babhūvuḥ: They became or they were.
    • ādaghnāsa: Those who are not harmed or are invulnerable.
    • upakakṣāsa: Those who are supportive or helpful.
    • u tve: And you (plural).
    • hradā: Like a lake or water body.
    • iva: Like or as.
    • snātvā: After bathing or cleansing.
    • u tve: And you (plural).
    • dadṛśre: Have seen or have perceived.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of individuals who are perceptive and aware, both in terms of sight and hearing.
    • It emphasizes the importance of companionship and mental agility, suggesting that true strength lies in unity and shared understanding.
    • The phrase "like a lake" symbolizes tranquility and depth, indicating that those who are united and perceptive can achieve a state of peace and clarity.
    • The idea of being "not harmed" suggests resilience and the ability to support one another through challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, individuals who communicate effectively and listen to each other can achieve greater success than those who work in isolation.
    • Friendship and support networks can provide emotional resilience during tough times, much like the invulnerability mentioned in the mantra.
    • In meditation or mindfulness practices, achieving a state of mental clarity can be likened to the tranquility of a lake, allowing for deeper insights and understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of awareness, companionship, and mental strength in navigating life's challenges.
    • It teaches us that by being perceptive and supportive, we can create a harmonious environment that fosters growth and resilience.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to stronger relationships, effective teamwork, and a more profound sense of peace and clarity.
