प्र वः पान्तमन्धसो धियायते महे शूराय विष्णवे चाऽर्चत |या सानुनी पर्वतानाम अदाभ्यां महस् तस्थतुर अर्वतेव साधुना ||
pra vaḥ pāntam andhaso dhiyāyate mahe śūrāya viṣṇave cārcata | yā sānuni parvatānām adābhyā mahas tasthatur arvateva sādhunā ||
  • pra vaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "let us go forth" or "let us move forward." It signifies a call to action, encouraging individuals to take initiative and progress in their endeavors.
  • pāntam: This word means "the path" or "the way." It emphasizes the importance of following a righteous or correct path in life, akin to making ethical choices in our daily actions.
  • andhaso: Translated as "from darkness," this term symbolizes ignorance or lack of knowledge. It highlights the need to seek enlightenment and understanding, moving away from ignorance.
  • dhiyāyate: This word means "to think" or "to contemplate." It suggests the importance of reflection and thoughtful consideration in decision-making processes.
  • mahe: Meaning "great" or "mighty," this term refers to the strength and power that can be harnessed through collective effort and wisdom.
  • śūrāya: This translates to "to the brave" or "to the courageous." It acknowledges the valor required to face challenges and pursue one's goals.
  • viṣṇave: Referring to Lord Vishnu, this term signifies the divine aspect of preservation and protection. It suggests seeking guidance from a higher power in our endeavors.
  • cārcata: This means "to worship" or "to honor." It emphasizes the importance of reverence and gratitude in our lives, recognizing the forces that support us.
  • yā: This word means "that" or "which," indicating a connection to the following concepts.
  • sānuni: Translated as "with strength" or "with power," it emphasizes the need for resilience and determination in our pursuits.
  • parvatānām: This means "of the mountains," symbolizing stability, permanence, and the challenges that one must overcome in life.
  • adābhyā: This term means "from the unyielding" or "from the firm," suggesting the importance of steadfastness in our beliefs and actions.
  • mahas: Meaning "greatness" or "magnitude," it refers to the vast potential that exists within us and the universe.
  • tasthatur: This word means "they stood" or "they remained," indicating resilience and the ability to endure through challenges.
  • arvateva: Translated as "like the strong," it emphasizes the qualities of strength and determination in overcoming obstacles.
  • sādhunā: This means "by the righteous" or "by the virtuous," highlighting the importance of ethical conduct and integrity in our actions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the journey of life, urging individuals to seek knowledge, act courageously, and remain steadfast in their pursuits. It emphasizes the importance of ethical conduct and the need for divine guidance. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership, and community building. By embodying the principles of courage, wisdom, and righteousness, individuals can navigate the complexities of life and contribute positively to society.
