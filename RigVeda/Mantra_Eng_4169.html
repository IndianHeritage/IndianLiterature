ईळे अग्निं स्ववसं नमोभिर इह प्रसत्तो वि चयत कृतं नः | रथैर इव प्र भरे वाजयद्भिः प्रदक्षिणिन मरुतां स्तोमं ऋध्याम् ||
īḻe agniṁ svavasaṁ namobhir iha prasatto vi cayat kṛtaṁ naḥ | rathair iva pra bhare vājayadbhiḥ pradakṣiṇin marutāṁ stomam ṛdhyām ||
  • īḻe agniṁ: This phrase translates to "I invoke Agni," where Agni represents the fire god in Vedic tradition. Fire is often seen as a purifying force and a medium for offerings to the divine.
  • svavasaṁ: This means "with our own strength" or "with our own resources." It emphasizes self-reliance and the importance of personal effort in achieving goals.
  • namobhir: This translates to "with reverence" or "with salutations." It indicates the importance of humility and respect in one's approach to the divine and to life.
  • iha prasatto: This means "here, pleased" or "here, satisfied." It suggests that the divine presence is accessible and responsive to sincere offerings and prayers.
  • vi cayat kṛtaṁ naḥ: This translates to "may he bestow upon us what we have done." It reflects the idea of seeking blessings for our actions and efforts, highlighting the connection between deeds and divine favor.
  • rathair iva: This means "like chariots." Chariots symbolize strength, movement, and progress, indicating the need for support and resources in our endeavors.
  • pra bhare: This translates to "may he carry forth" or "may he bring forth." It signifies the desire for divine assistance in our pursuits.
  • vājayadbhiḥ: This means "with the forces of strength." It emphasizes the importance of power and energy in achieving success.
  • pradakṣiṇin: This translates to "in a circular manner" or "in a respectful manner." It indicates the importance of tradition and ritual in our approach to the divine.
  • marutāṁ: This refers to the Maruts, the storm deities associated with strength and vitality. They symbolize the dynamic forces of nature that can aid in our endeavors.
  • stomam ṛdhyām: This means "the hymn of praise." It reflects the importance of expressing gratitude and reverence for the divine and the forces that support us.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of invoking divine support through self-effort, humility, and respect. It teaches us that our actions are interconnected with the divine, and by acknowledging this relationship, we can seek blessings for our endeavors. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as starting a new project, seeking guidance in difficult times, or expressing gratitude for the resources and support we receive. By embodying the principles of self-reliance, respect, and gratitude, we can navigate life's challenges with a sense of purpose and connection to the greater forces at play.
