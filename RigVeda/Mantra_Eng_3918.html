त्वं तं इन्द्र मार्त्यम अमित्रयन्तम अद्रिवः | सर्वरथा शतक्रतो न याहि शवसस पते ||
tvaṁ tam indra martyam amitrayantam adrivaḥ | sarvarathā śatakrato ni yāhi śavasas pate ||
  • tvaṁ tam indra: This phrase translates to "You, Indra." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • martyam amitrayantam: Here, "martyam" means "mortal" and "amitrayantam" can be interpreted as "one who does not have enemies" or "one who is free from adversaries." This suggests a state of being that transcends the usual struggles of life.
  • adrivaḥ: This term can be understood as "without obstacles" or "unhindered." It implies a sense of freedom and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • sarvarathā: This word means "all chariots" or "all vehicles." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent all means of progress or movement in life.
  • śatakrato: This term refers to "the one with a hundred powers" or "the one who has achieved many victories." It emphasizes the multifaceted nature of strength and capability.
  • ni yāhi: This phrase translates to "go" or "move towards." It is an imperative, urging action and progress.
  • śavasas pate: "śavasas" means "of the dead" or "of the departed," and "pate" means "lord" or "master." This can refer to the lord of the departed souls, indicating a connection to the cycle of life and death.

In summary, this mantra invokes Indra, the powerful deity, and calls upon him to guide the individual towards a state of being that is free from enemies and obstacles. It emphasizes the importance of strength, progress, and the mastery over life's challenges.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be seen as a call for personal empowerment. In real life, individuals often face various challenges and adversities. By invoking the qualities of Indra, one can seek the strength to overcome these obstacles.
  • In a professional context, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with determination and resilience, much like a warrior facing challenges on the battlefield.
  • In personal relationships, it encourages the idea of resolving conflicts and fostering harmony, promoting a life free from enmity.
  • Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder of the inner strength and capabilities that each person possesses, urging them to move forward in life with confidence and purpose.
