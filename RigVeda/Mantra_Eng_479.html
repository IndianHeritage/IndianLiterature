युष्मेषितो मरुतो मार्त्येषित आ यो नो अभ्वा ईषते |वितं युयोत शवसा व्य ओजसा वि युष्माकाभिर ऊतिभिः ||
yuṣmeṣito maruto martyeṣita ā yo no abhva īṣate | vi taṁ yuyota śavasā vy ojasā vi yuṣmākābhir ūtibhiḥ ||
  • yuṣmeṣito maruto: This phrase can be translated as "O Maruts, who are invoked by us." The Maruts are a group of storm deities in the Rigveda, representing the forces of nature, particularly storms and winds. They symbolize the dynamic and powerful aspects of nature.
  • martyeṣita: This word refers to "the mortal beings." It highlights the connection between the divine and human realms, emphasizing that mortals seek the blessings and protection of the divine forces.
  • ā yo no abhva īṣate: This translates to "may they not harm us." It expresses a plea for safety and protection from the potentially destructive forces of nature, which the Maruts embody.
  • vi taṁ yuyota: This phrase means "may they unite with us." It signifies a desire for harmony and collaboration between humans and divine forces, suggesting that humans should work in tandem with nature rather than against it.
  • śavasā vy ojasā: This can be interpreted as "with strength and vigor." It emphasizes the importance of vitality and energy in both the divine and human realms, suggesting that strength is essential for overcoming challenges.
  • vi yuṣmākābhir ūtibhiḥ: This translates to "with your blessings." It indicates a recognition of the need for divine support and guidance in achieving success and well-being in life.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates a profound relationship between humans and the divine forces of nature. It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking protection and blessings from the deities, particularly in times of uncertainty or danger. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as during natural disasters, when individuals seek safety and strength, or in moments of personal struggle, where one may call upon higher powers for support. Ultimately, it emphasizes the need for harmony with nature and the acknowledgment of our dependence on divine forces for guidance and protection.
