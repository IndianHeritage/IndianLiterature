त्वष्टारं वायुम ऋभवो य ओहते दैव्याः होताराः उषसं स्वस्तये | बृहस्पतिं वृत्रखादं सुमेधसम इन्द्रियं सोमं धनसा उ ईमहे ||
tvaṣṭāraṁ vāyum ṛbhavo ya ohate daivyā hotārā uṣasaṁ svastaye | bṛhaspatiṁ vṛtrakhādaṁ sumedhasam indriyaṁ somaṁ dhanasā u īmahe ||
  • tvaṣṭāraṁ: This term refers to the divine craftsman or the creator, often associated with the act of shaping and forming. In real life, this can be likened to artists or engineers who create and innovate, shaping the world around us.
  • vāyum: This means 'the wind' or 'air', symbolizing movement and the vital force of life. In our daily lives, we can relate to this as the breath of life, essential for survival and representing change and freedom.
  • ṛbhavo: This word signifies 'the wise' or 'the enlightened ones'. It can be associated with individuals who possess knowledge and wisdom, such as teachers or mentors who guide others in their journeys.
  • ya ohate: This phrase translates to 'who is invoked' or 'who is called upon'. In practical terms, it reflects the act of seeking help or guidance from higher powers or wise individuals in times of need.
  • daivyā: Meaning 'divine', this term emphasizes the connection to the spiritual or higher realms. It can be seen in the context of seeking blessings or divine intervention in our lives.
  • hotārā: This refers to the 'priest' or 'one who offers', symbolizing the act of giving and sharing. In real life, this can be related to community leaders or volunteers who contribute to the welfare of society.
  • uṣasaṁ: This means 'dawn' or 'the new day', representing new beginnings and hope. It can be compared to the start of a new project or phase in life, filled with potential and opportunities.
  • svastaye: This term signifies 'well-being' or 'prosperity'. In everyday life, it can be associated with health, happiness, and success in various endeavors.
  • bṛhaspatiṁ: Referring to the deity of wisdom and knowledge, this can be likened to the pursuit of education and understanding in our lives, emphasizing the importance of learning.
  • vṛtrakhādaṁ: This term means 'the one who has slain Vritra', symbolizing the overcoming of obstacles and challenges. In real life, it can represent personal triumphs over difficulties, such as achieving goals despite setbacks.
  • sumedhasam: This translates to 'of good intellect', highlighting the value of intelligence and wisdom in decision-making. It can be seen in the context of making informed choices in our careers or personal lives.
  • indriyaṁ: Meaning 'senses', this term emphasizes the importance of perception and awareness in our experiences. It can relate to mindfulness practices that enhance our understanding of the world.
  • somaṁ: This refers to the sacred drink that symbolizes immortality and divine inspiration. In modern terms, it can be associated with the pursuit of spiritual fulfillment and enlightenment.
  • dhanasā: This means 'wealth' or 'prosperity', reflecting the importance of material and spiritual abundance in our lives. It can be related to financial stability and the pursuit of a fulfilling life.
  • u īmahe: This phrase translates to 'we invoke' or 'we call upon', emphasizing the act of seeking blessings and support from the divine or wise individuals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of creation, wisdom, and the pursuit of well-being. It highlights the importance of invoking divine support and wisdom in our lives, encouraging us to overcome challenges and seek prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can serve as a reminder to embrace new beginnings, seek knowledge, and contribute positively to our communities, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
