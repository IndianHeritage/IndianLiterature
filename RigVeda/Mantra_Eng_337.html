अनु प्रात्नस्यौकसो हुवे तुविप्रतिं नरम् |यं ते पूर्वम् पिता हुवे ||
anu pratnasyaukaso huve tuvipratiṁ naram | yaṁ te pūrvam pitā huve ||
  • anu pratnasyaukaso huve tuvipratiṁ naram
    • anu - This word means "following" or "after." It signifies continuity and the idea of progression.
    • pratnasyaukaso - This can be broken down into "pratna" (knowledge or wisdom) and "saukasa" (comfort or ease). Together, it suggests the comfort that comes from wisdom.
    • huve - This term translates to "I invoke" or "I call upon." It indicates a plea or a request for divine assistance.
    • tuvipratiṁ - This word can be understood as "to you, O divine one," indicating a direct address to a higher power or deity.
    • naram - This means "man" or "human being," referring to the individual or humanity in general.
  • yaṁ te pūrvam pitā huve
    • yaṁ - This means "whom" or "that which," referring to a specific entity or being.
    • te - This translates to "your," indicating possession or connection to the divine.
    • pūrvam - This means "previous" or "before," suggesting a historical or ancestral connection.
    • pitā - This word means "father," symbolizing authority, guidance, and protection.
    • huve - Again, this indicates "I invoke" or "I call upon," reinforcing the act of seeking divine intervention.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for wisdom and guidance from a higher power, particularly in the context of human existence and the ancestral lineage. It emphasizes the importance of invoking divine support in our lives, especially when seeking knowledge and comfort.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various situations:

  • Seeking Guidance: When faced with difficult decisions, individuals can recite this mantra to seek wisdom and clarity.
  • Family and Ancestry: It can be used in family gatherings or rituals to honor ancestors and seek their blessings for the present and future generations.
  • Education and Learning: Students can invoke this mantra before exams or learning sessions to gain comfort and wisdom in their studies.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with divine wisdom and ancestral guidance. It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking knowledge and comfort in our lives, reinforcing the idea that we are part of a larger continuum that includes our ancestors and the divine. By invoking this mantra, individuals can find strength and clarity in their personal journeys.
