मन्त्र: वोचेमेद इन्द्रं मगवाणं एनं महो रायो राधसो यद ददन नः |यो अर्चतो ब्रह्मकृतिम अविष्टो यूम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
vocemed indram maghavānam enam maho rāyo rādhaso yad dadan naḥ | yo arcato brahmakṛtim aviṣṭho yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Vocemed Indram: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke Indra." Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength, power, and the forces of nature. In real life, invoking Indra can symbolize seeking strength and guidance in challenging situations.
  • Maghavānam enam: This means "the powerful one." It refers to Indra's might and ability to overcome obstacles. In our daily lives, we often face challenges that require us to tap into our inner strength or seek help from powerful allies.
  • Maho rāyo rādhaso: This translates to "the great wealth of the prosperous." It emphasizes the importance of abundance and prosperity. In practical terms, this can relate to financial stability, emotional wealth, or the richness of relationships.
  • Yad dadan naḥ: This means "that which you give us." It signifies a request for blessings and support. In life, we often seek help from others, whether it be from friends, family, or a higher power, to achieve our goals.
  • Yo arcato: This translates to "who is worshipped." It highlights the importance of reverence and respect towards those who provide guidance and support. In real life, this can be seen in how we honor mentors, leaders, or even spiritual figures.
  • Brahmakṛtim: This means "the creation of the divine." It refers to the idea that everything in existence is a manifestation of a higher power. In our lives, recognizing the divine in all things can lead to a greater appreciation for the world around us.
  • Aviṣṭho: This translates to "unobstructed." It signifies the removal of barriers and obstacles. In practical terms, this can relate to overcoming challenges in our personal or professional lives, allowing us to move forward freely.
  • Yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ: This means "may you protect us with auspiciousness." It is a plea for safety and well-being. In everyday life, we often seek protection and positive energy, whether through community support, family, or spiritual practices.
  • Sadā naḥ: This translates to "always for us." It emphasizes the desire for continuous support and blessings. In real life, this can reflect our longing for enduring relationships and consistent guidance throughout our journeys.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for strength, prosperity, and divine protection. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, where invoking higher powers and seeking support from others can lead to personal growth and fulfillment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in moments of challenge, during rituals for prosperity, or when seeking guidance in decision-making. By embracing the essence of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a mindset of gratitude, reverence, and resilience in their daily lives.
