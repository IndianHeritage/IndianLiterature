अस्य स्तोमे मघोनः सख्ये वृद्धशोचिषः |विश्वा यस्मिन तुविष्वणि सम आर्ये शुष्मम् आदधुः ||
asya stome maghonaḥ sakhye vṛddhaśociṣaḥ | viśvā yasmin tuviṣvaṇi sam arye śuṣmam ādadhuḥ ||
  • Asya stome maghonaḥ sakhye vṛddhaśociṣaḥ: This line can be translated to "In this hymn, we invoke the powerful and radiant friendship of the great ones." Here, "asya" refers to "this," "stome" means "hymn" or "praise," "maghonaḥ" signifies "of the powerful," "sakhye" translates to "in friendship," and "vṛddhaśociṣaḥ" means "of the great and radiant ones." This emphasizes the importance of camaraderie and the strength derived from collective efforts.
  • Viśvā yasmin tuviṣvaṇi: This part translates to "In whom all beings are united." "Viśvā" means "all" or "universal," "yasmin" translates to "in whom," and "tuviṣvaṇi" refers to "all beings." This highlights the interconnectedness of all life and the idea that unity among diverse entities leads to strength and harmony.
  • Sam arye śuṣmam ādadhuḥ: This can be interpreted as "They have gathered together the essence of all." "Sam" means "together," "arye" refers to "noble ones," "śuṣmam" translates to "essence" or "substance," and "ādadhuḥ" means "they have gathered." This signifies the importance of collaboration and the pooling of resources and wisdom for a common goal.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, friendship, and collaboration among individuals and communities. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork in workplaces, community service projects, or even in family settings where collective efforts lead to greater achievements. The essence of this mantra serves as a reminder that when we come together, respecting each other's strengths and contributions, we can achieve remarkable outcomes. It encourages us to foster relationships based on mutual respect and support, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and prosperous society.
