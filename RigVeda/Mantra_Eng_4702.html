न यं हिंसन्ति धीतयो न वाणीर इन्द्रं नक्षन्तीद अभि वर्धयन्तीः |यदि स्तोतारः शतं यत सहस्रं गृणन्ति गिरवणसं शं तद अस्मै ||
na yaṁ hiṁsanti dhītayo na vāṇīr indraṁ nakṣantīd abhi vardhayantīḥ | yadi stotāraḥ śataṁ yat sahasraṁ gṛṇanti girvaṇasaṁ śaṁ tad asmai ||
  • Mantra: "na yaṁ hiṁsanti dhītayo na vāṇīr indraṁ nakṣantīd abhi vardhayantīḥ | yadi stotāraḥ śataṁ yat sahasraṁ gṛṇanti girvaṇasaṁ śaṁ tad asmai ||"
  • Translation: "Those who do not harm the wise, and those who do not speak ill of Indra, they are the ones who increase in strength. If a hundred or a thousand singers praise him, may that peace come to him."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na: not
    • yaṁ: whom
    • hiṁsanti: harm
    • dhītayo: wise or learned
    • na: not
    • vāṇīr: speakers or words
    • indraṁ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • nakṣantīd: do not speak ill
    • abhi: towards
    • vardhayantīḥ: increase or enhance
    • yadi: if
    • stotāraḥ: singers or praisers
    • śataṁ: hundred
    • yat: that
    • sahasraṁ: thousand
    • gṛṇanti: sing or praise
    • girvaṇasaṁ: of the one who is praised
    • śaṁ: peace or well-being
    • tad: that
    • asmai: to him
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of non-violence and respect towards the wise and knowledgeable individuals in society.
    • It suggests that those who refrain from harming others and who do not speak ill of powerful figures (like Indra) will find strength and growth in their own lives.
    • The mention of singers praising someone indicates the power of positive words and affirmations in uplifting individuals and communities.
    • In real life, this can be seen in various contexts, such as in workplaces where supportive and respectful communication leads to a more productive environment.
    • For example, in a team setting, when members encourage each other and refrain from negative criticism, the overall morale and performance improve.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its advocacy for harmony, respect, and the power of positive reinforcement.
    • It teaches us that by fostering a culture of support and non-violence, we can enhance our own lives and the lives of those around us.
    • This principle is applicable in various real-life scenarios, including personal relationships, educational settings, and professional environments.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder that our words and actions have the power to uplift or harm, and choosing the former leads to collective well-being.
