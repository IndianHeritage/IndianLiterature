उक्षां मिमाति प्रति यान्ति धेनवो देवस्य देवीर उप यान्ति निष्कृतम् |अत्य अक्रमीद अर्जुनं वारम् अव्ययम् अत्कं न निक्तम् परि सोमो अव्यत ||
ukṣā mimāti prati yanti dhenavo devasya devīr upa yanti niṣkṛtam | aty akramīd arjunaṁ vāram avyayam atkaṁ na niktam pari somo avyata ||
  • ukṣā mimāti: This phrase can be translated as "the dawn rises" or "the light of dawn approaches." In a real-life context, this can symbolize new beginnings, hope, and the start of a new day. Just as dawn brings light, it signifies opportunities and the potential for growth.
  • prati yanti dhenavo: This means "the cows (or resources) move towards." Cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and nourishment in Vedic literature. In modern life, this can represent the pursuit of resources or the journey towards prosperity and sustenance.
  • devasya devīr upa yanti niṣkṛtam: This translates to "the divine beings approach the divine." It signifies the connection between the earthly and the divine, suggesting that when we seek purity and righteousness (niṣkṛtam), we align ourselves with higher spiritual truths. In real life, this can be seen in practices like meditation or prayer, where individuals seek to connect with a higher power or their inner selves.
  • aty akramīd arjunaṁ vāram avyayam: This phrase can be interpreted as "the invincible Arjuna (a hero) does not falter." Arjuna, a central character in the Mahabharata, represents courage and determination. In everyday life, this can inspire individuals to face challenges head-on, embodying resilience and strength in the face of adversity.
  • atkaṁ na niktam pari somo avyata: This translates to "the moon does not hide its light." The moon symbolizes calmness and serenity, and its consistent presence reminds us that even in darkness, there is light. In practical terms, this can encourage individuals to remain steadfast and true to themselves, even during difficult times.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of new beginnings, the pursuit of resources, the connection to the divine, resilience in adversity, and the unwavering presence of light. Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals to embrace hope, seek spiritual connection, and face life's challenges with courage. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of transition, during personal struggles, or when seeking guidance and strength, reminding us that every dawn brings new opportunities and that we are never alone in our journey.
