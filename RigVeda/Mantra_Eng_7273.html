उरुष्याणो मा परा दा aghāyate जातवेदः |दुराध्ये मर्ताय ||
uruṣyā ṇo mā parā dā aghāyate jātavedaḥ | durādhye3 martāya ||
  • uruṣyā: This word can be interpreted as "of the man" or "of the human." It signifies the essence of humanity and the collective experience of human beings.
  • ṇo: This is a form of the pronoun "we," indicating a collective identity or a shared experience among people.
  • mā: This word translates to "not" or "do not," serving as a negation in the mantra.
  • parā: Meaning "beyond" or "further," it suggests a sense of transcendence or something that is beyond the ordinary.
  • dā: This translates to "give" or "bestow," indicating a request or a plea for something to be granted.
  • aghāyate: This word means "to harm" or "to injure," implying a desire for protection from harm.
  • jātavedaḥ: This term refers to the "born of knowledge" or "the one who knows," often associated with Agni, the fire god, symbolizing wisdom and enlightenment.
  • durādhye: This means "difficult to overcome" or "hard to achieve," indicating challenges or obstacles in life.
  • martāya: This translates to "for the mortal" or "for human beings," emphasizing the human condition and the struggles faced by individuals.

The mantra can be interpreted as a plea for protection and guidance in the face of life's challenges. It expresses a desire for the divine to bestow wisdom and shield humanity from harm, particularly in difficult situations. The invocation of Agni, as the embodiment of knowledge, suggests that enlightenment and understanding are essential for overcoming obstacles.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties in personal life, such as health issues or emotional struggles, individuals can recite this mantra to seek strength and guidance.
  • Professional Obstacles: In the workplace, when encountering challenges in projects or relationships, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek wisdom and support from others.
  • Community Support: In times of crisis, such as natural disasters or social unrest, communities can come together, invoking this mantra to foster unity and resilience.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine protection and wisdom in overcoming life's challenges. It emphasizes the importance of collective strength and the pursuit of knowledge as a means to navigate difficulties. By invoking this mantra, individuals and communities can find solace and empowerment in their struggles, fostering a sense of hope and resilience.
