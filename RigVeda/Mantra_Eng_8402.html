मन्त्र: असावी सोमो अरुषो वृषा हरि राजेव दसमो अभि गा अचिक्रदत् | पुनानो वारम् पर्य एत्य अव्ययं श्येनो न योनिं घृतवंतम् आसदम् ||
asāvi somo aruṣo vṛṣā harī rājeva dasmo abhi gā acikradat | punāno vāram pary ety avyayaṁ śyeno na yoniṁ ghṛtavantam āsadam ||
  • asāvi somo: This phrase refers to the "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. In real life, it can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment that nourishes the mind and spirit.
  • aruṣo vṛṣā: "Aruṣa" means red or radiant, and "vṛṣā" refers to strength or power. Together, they symbolize the vibrant energy and strength that can be harnessed for positive action. For example, this can be seen in individuals who channel their passion into creative endeavors or social causes.
  • harī rājeva: "Harī" signifies the divine or the auspicious, while "rājeva" suggests a king or ruler. This can be interpreted as the divine guidance that leads one to success and prosperity. In everyday life, this could relate to leaders who inspire their teams through vision and integrity.
  • dasmo abhi gā acikradat: This phrase can be understood as a call to action, urging one to sing or express joy and gratitude. In practical terms, it emphasizes the importance of celebrating achievements and sharing happiness with others, much like community gatherings or festivals.
  • punāno vāram: "Punāno" refers to renewal or rejuvenation, while "vāram" means a boon or blessing. This suggests the cyclical nature of life and the importance of seeking renewal. In real life, this can be seen in practices like meditation or self-care that help individuals refresh their minds and spirits.
  • pary ety avyayaṁ: "Pary ety" means to go around or encompass, and "avyayaṁ" refers to the eternal or unchanging. This can symbolize the journey of life and the pursuit of eternal truths. For instance, it reflects the idea of lifelong learning and the quest for wisdom.
  • śyeno na yoniṁ: "Śyena" refers to a falcon or bird of prey, symbolizing speed and agility, while "yoniṁ" means womb or source. This can represent the idea of harnessing one's potential and rising above challenges, akin to athletes or entrepreneurs who overcome obstacles to achieve their goals.
  • ghṛtavantam āsadam: "Ghṛtavantam" means possessing ghee or richness, and "āsadam" refers to a place or abode. This can symbolize a state of abundance and prosperity. In real life, it can be related to creating a nurturing environment, whether at home or in the workplace, that fosters growth and success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of divine inspiration, strength, renewal, and the pursuit of knowledge. Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to seek enlightenment, celebrate life, and create a nurturing environment for themselves and others. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in personal development, leadership, community building, and the pursuit of one's passions, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
