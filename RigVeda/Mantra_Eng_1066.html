धन्वन् स्रोतः कृतुते गातुम ऊर्मिं शुक्रैर ऊर्मिभिर अभि नक्षति क्षाम् |विश्वा सनानि जठरेषु धत्ते 'न्तर नवासु चरति प्रसूषु ||
dhanvan srotaḥ kṛṇute gātum ūrmiṁ śukrair ūrmibhir abhi nakṣati kṣām | viśvā sanāni jaṭhareṣu dhatte 'ntar navāsu carati prasūṣu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dhanvan: One who possesses wealth or prosperity.
    • srotaḥ: Streams or channels.
    • kṛṇute: Creates or brings forth.
    • gātum: To sing or to express.
    • ūrmiṁ: Waves or movements.
    • śukrair: With brightness or purity.
    • ūrmibhir: By the waves.
    • abhi: Towards or in the direction of.
    • nakṣati: To shine or to illuminate.
    • kṣām: Endurance or stability.
    • viśvā: All or universal.
    • sanāni: Eternal or everlasting.
    • jaṭhareṣu: In the wombs or within.
    • dhatte: Holds or contains.
    • 'ntar: Within or inside.
    • navāsu: New or fresh.
    • carati: Moves or travels.
    • prasūṣu: In the births or creations.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine forces that create and sustain life, symbolized by the waves and streams of prosperity.
    • The imagery of waves suggests the dynamic nature of existence, where wealth and abundance flow like water, nourishing all forms of life.
    • The reference to brightness and purity indicates that true wealth is not just material but also encompasses spiritual and moral values.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of endurance and stability in life, suggesting that true prosperity is rooted in resilience and the ability to adapt to change.
    • It also highlights the interconnectedness of all beings, as it speaks of universal existence and the eternal nature of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to the idea of sustainable growth, where companies focus not only on profits but also on ethical practices and community welfare.
    • In personal life, it can inspire individuals to seek balance between material success and spiritual fulfillment, encouraging them to invest in relationships and personal development.
    • In environmental discussions, the mantra can serve as a reminder of the importance of nurturing our planet, recognizing that our well-being is tied to the health of the earth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of prosperity, resilience, and interconnectedness.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us that true wealth is multifaceted, encompassing not just material gain but also spiritual and ethical dimensions.
    • In real life, it encourages us to cultivate a holistic approach to success, where we honor our responsibilities to ourselves, others, and the environment.
