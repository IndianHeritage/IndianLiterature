स इज जनेन स विशा स जनमना स पुत्रैर वाजम् भरते धनान नृभिः |देवानां यः पितरम् आविवासति श्रद्धामना हविषा ब्रह्मणस पतिम ||
sa ij janena sa viśā sa janmanā sa putrair vājam bharate dhanā nṛbhiḥ | devānāṁ yaḥ pitaram āvivāsati śraddhāmanā haviṣā brahmaṇas patim ||
  • sa ij janena: This phrase can be translated as "may he be born with the people." It emphasizes the importance of community and social connections in an individual's life. In real life, this can be seen in how people thrive in supportive environments, such as families, friendships, and communities that foster growth and development.
  • sa viśā: This means "may he be with the tribes." It highlights the significance of belonging to a group or society. For example, in modern society, being part of a team or organization can provide individuals with a sense of identity and purpose, much like how tribes functioned in ancient times.
  • sa janmanā: Translated as "may he be born with good qualities." This suggests the aspiration for individuals to possess virtues and positive traits. In real life, this can relate to the values instilled in children through education and upbringing, encouraging them to develop qualities like kindness, honesty, and resilience.
  • sa putrair vājam bharate dhanā nṛbhiḥ: This phrase means "may he be enriched with wealth and strength by sons." It reflects the traditional view of prosperity being linked to family and offspring. In contemporary contexts, this can be interpreted as the importance of nurturing future generations to ensure the continuation of values, knowledge, and resources within families and communities.
  • devānāṁ yaḥ pitaram āvivāsati: This translates to "he who worships the divine and the ancestors." This highlights the significance of honoring both the divine and familial lineage. In real life, this can be seen in various cultures where rituals and traditions are performed to pay respect to ancestors, fostering a sense of gratitude and continuity.
  • śraddhāmanā haviṣā brahmaṇas patim: This means "with faith, he offers oblations to the Lord of creation." It emphasizes the importance of faith and devotion in one's life. In practical terms, this can be related to how individuals engage in spiritual practices or community service, reflecting their beliefs and values through actions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of community, virtue, prosperity, respect for ancestors, and faith. Its significance lies in the interconnectedness of individuals within society and the values that bind them together. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate strong relationships, uphold moral values, honor their heritage, and engage in spiritual practices, ultimately contributing to a harmonious and prosperous life. By embodying these principles, one can navigate the complexities of modern life while remaining grounded in tradition and community.
