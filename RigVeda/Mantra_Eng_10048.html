नाहं तं वेद दभ्यं दभत स यस्येदं दूतीर असरम् पराकात् |न तं गूहन्ति श्रवतोगभीराः हता इन्द्रेण पाणयः शयध्वे ||
nāhaṁ taṁ veda dabhyaṁ dabhat sa yasyedaṁ dūtīr asaram parākāt | na taṁ gūhanti sravato gabhīrā hatā indreṇa paṇayaḥ śayadhve ||
  • nāhaṁ taṁ veda dabhyaṁ: This phrase translates to "I do not know that which is to be known." It signifies the limits of human knowledge and understanding. In real life, this can be related to the concept of humility in the face of the vastness of the universe. For example, scientists often acknowledge that there is much they do not know about the cosmos, which drives them to explore further.
  • dabhat sa yasyedaṁ dūtīr asaram parākāt: This can be interpreted as "that which is beyond the reach of the senses." It suggests that there are truths and realities that are not accessible through ordinary perception. In everyday life, this can be seen in the realm of emotions and thoughts, which often cannot be fully expressed or understood by others. For instance, a person may feel deep sorrow that they cannot articulate, highlighting the limitations of communication.
  • na taṁ gūhanti sravato gabhīrā: This means "the deep and hidden do not conceal it." It implies that even the most profound truths will eventually come to light. In practical terms, this can be observed in situations where secrets or hidden truths are revealed over time, such as in personal relationships or historical events. For example, the uncovering of hidden documents can change the understanding of a historical narrative.
  • hatā indreṇa paṇayaḥ śayadhve: This translates to "those who are struck down by Indra lie down." Indra, in Vedic literature, is often associated with power and authority. This line can be interpreted as a reminder of the consequences of actions and the inevitability of facing the results of one's deeds. In real life, this can be seen in the context of justice, where individuals must face the repercussions of their actions, whether in legal terms or moral consequences.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound philosophical insights about knowledge, perception, truth, and consequences. It emphasizes the importance of humility in understanding the limits of our knowledge, the recognition of deeper truths beyond our immediate perception, and the inevitability of facing the outcomes of our actions. In real life, these teachings can guide individuals in their personal growth, relationships, and ethical decision-making, encouraging a deeper exploration of both the self and the world around them.
