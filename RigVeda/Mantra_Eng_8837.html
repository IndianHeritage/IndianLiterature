शूषेभिर वृधो जुषाणो अर्कैर देवाम् acchā रघुपत्वा जिगाति |मन्द्रो होतā स जुह्वा यजिष्ठः सम्मिश्लो अग्निर ā जिघर्ति देवान् ||
śūṣebhir vṛdho juṣāṇo arkair devām̐ acchā raghupatvā jigāti | mandro hotā sa juhvā3 yajiṣṭhaḥ sammiślo agnir ā jigharti devān ||
  • śūṣebhir vṛdho juṣāṇo arkair: This phrase can be translated as "the one who is nourished by the offerings and is pleased with the hymns." In real life, this can be likened to how individuals thrive on appreciation and recognition. For example, a teacher who receives positive feedback from students feels motivated and continues to give their best.
  • devām̐ acchā raghupatvā jigāti: This means "the divine beings are pleased with the offerings." It signifies the importance of devotion and sincerity in our actions. In a practical sense, this can be seen in community service, where selfless acts of kindness towards others can lead to a sense of fulfillment and connection with the divine.
  • mandro hotā sa juhvā3 yajiṣṭhaḥ: This translates to "the one who is the best among the priests and offers the oblation." This highlights the role of leadership and guidance in spiritual practices. In everyday life, this can be compared to a mentor or a leader who inspires others through their actions and wisdom, much like a coach who motivates their team to achieve success.
  • sammiślo agnir ā jigharti devān: This phrase means "the fire that is mixed and reaches the divine." Fire in this context symbolizes transformation and purification. In real life, this can be related to personal growth, where challenges and hardships act as a fire that refines our character and leads us to greater understanding and enlightenment.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of devotion, leadership, and the transformative power of challenges. It teaches us that through sincere offerings and actions, we can connect with the divine and inspire others. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community service, mentorship, and personal growth, reminding us that our actions have the power to uplift not only ourselves but also those around us.
