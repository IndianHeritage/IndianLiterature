अदर्दर उत्सम् असृजो विखानि त्वम् अर्णवान बद्बधानाम् अरम्णाः |महान्तम् इन्द्र पर्वतं वियद वः सृजो विधारा अव दानवं हन् ||
adardar utsam asṛjo vi khāni tvam arṇavān badbadhānām̐ aramṇāḥ | mahāntam indra parvataṁ vi yad vaḥ sṛjo vi dhārā ava dānavaṁ han ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • adardar: to be seen or perceived
    • utsam: the best or the highest
    • asṛjo: the one who creates or brings forth
    • vi: indeed or truly
    • khāni: you (plural) or your
    • tvam: you
    • arṇavān: oceanic or vast
    • badbadhānām̐: of those who are bound or tied
    • aramṇāḥ: those who are free or liberated
    • mahāntam: the great or the mighty
    • indra: the king of gods or the powerful
    • parvataṁ: mountain or peak
    • vi: indeed or truly
    • yad: that which
    • vaḥ: of you (plural)
    • sṛjo: you create or bring forth
    • vi: indeed or truly
    • dhārā: streams or flows
    • ava: down or below
    • dānavaṁ: the demon or the adversary
    • han: to kill or to destroy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine power that perceives and creates the highest forms of existence.
    • It acknowledges the vastness of the universe, represented by the ocean, and the liberation from the bonds that tie us down.
    • It invokes Indra, the king of gods, symbolizing strength and authority, to help overcome obstacles and adversities.
    • The reference to mountains signifies stability and strength, while the streams represent the flow of life and energy.
    • Ultimately, it calls for the destruction of negative forces or demons that hinder progress and well-being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, this mantra can be seen as a call to recognize and harness one's inner strength to overcome challenges.
    • In a corporate setting, it can symbolize the need for leadership (like Indra) to guide teams through turbulent times.
    • In environmental contexts, the ocean and mountains can represent the need to respect and protect nature, while also drawing strength from it.
    • In mental health, the liberation from bonds can relate to overcoming anxiety and depression, seeking help to break free from these constraints.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing one's potential and the divine support available to overcome life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that strength, both internal and external, can help us navigate through difficulties and achieve liberation from negativity.
    • In essence, it encourages individuals to seek empowerment and to act against the forces that hinder their growth and well-being.
