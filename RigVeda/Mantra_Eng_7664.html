मन्त्र: विश्वेषाम् इह स्तुहि होत्रिणां यशस्तमम् | अग्निं यज्ञेषु पूर्व्यम् ||
viśveṣām iha stuhi hotṝṇāṁ yaśastamam | agniṁ yajñeṣu pūrvyam ||
  • viśveṣām: This word translates to "of all" or "universal." It signifies inclusiveness and the idea that the mantra addresses all beings and entities.
  • iha: Meaning "here" or "in this place," it emphasizes the immediacy and relevance of the invocation in the present context.
  • stuhi: This means "praise" or "worship." It indicates an act of reverence and acknowledgment of the divine or the forces at play.
  • hotṝṇāṁ: Referring to "the priests" or "those who invoke," this term highlights the role of individuals who perform rituals and sacrifices, acting as intermediaries between the divine and the earthly realm.
  • yaśastamam: This translates to "the most glorious" or "the most renowned." It signifies the highest form of fame or honor, suggesting that the subject of the praise is worthy of the utmost respect.
  • agniṁ: This word means "fire," which in Vedic tradition symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence. Fire is often seen as a medium through which offerings are made to the gods.
  • yajñeṣu: This translates to "in sacrifices" or "in rituals." It indicates the context in which the praise and invocation are being made, emphasizing the importance of ritualistic practices in connecting with the divine.
  • pūrvyam: Meaning "the first" or "the foremost," this word suggests that Agni holds a primary position in the rituals and is often the first deity invoked in Vedic ceremonies.

In summary, this mantra is a call to praise the universal essence of all beings, particularly focusing on the role of Agni in rituals. It acknowledges the priests who perform these sacred acts and emphasizes the significance of honoring the divine through fire in sacrificial practices. The mantra encapsulates the idea that through collective worship and recognition of the divine, one can attain glory and honor.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in modern life by recognizing the importance of community and collective efforts in achieving goals. Just as the priests come together to perform rituals, individuals can collaborate to create positive change in society.
  • The invocation of Agni symbolizes the transformative power of fire, which can be seen in real-life scenarios such as personal growth, where challenges (like fire) can lead to purification and renewal.
  • In a broader sense, the mantra encourages individuals to engage in practices that honor the environment and the interconnectedness of all beings, promoting sustainability and respect for nature.
