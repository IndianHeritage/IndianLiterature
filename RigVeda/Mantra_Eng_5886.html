मन्त्र: उक्तेभिर वृत्रहन्तमाऽ यां मन्दानां चिद् आ गिरा |आङ्गूषैर आविवासतः ||
ukthebhir vṛtrahantamā yā mandānā cid ā girā | āṅgūṣair āvivāsataḥ ||
  • ukthebhir: This word can be broken down into "uktha," which refers to a hymn or a chant, and "bhir," which implies through or by means of. Thus, "ukthebhir" signifies the act of invoking or calling upon through hymns.
  • vṛtrahantamā: This term is derived from "vṛtra," which means obstacle or enemy, and "hantamā," which translates to the one who destroys or overcomes. Therefore, "vṛtrahantamā" refers to the one who vanquishes obstacles or adversaries.
  • yā: This word means "who" or "that," serving as a connector in the mantra.
  • mandānā: This term can be interpreted as "with a gentle or soft voice," indicating a calm and composed manner of expression.
  • cid: This word means "consciousness" or "awareness," suggesting a state of heightened perception or understanding.
  • ā: A preposition meaning "from" or "out of," indicating a source or origin.
  • girā: This term translates to "speech" or "sound," emphasizing the importance of verbal expression in the context of the mantra.
  • āṅgūṣair: This word refers to "with the fingers," suggesting a physical action or gesture that accompanies the invocation.
  • āvivāsataḥ: This term can be understood as "having been revealed" or "having been made manifest," indicating the outcome of the invocation or the realization of the desired state.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to overcome obstacles through the power of speech and gentle expression. It emphasizes the importance of using hymns and chants to invoke divine assistance in overcoming challenges. The act of using one's voice and gestures signifies the active participation of the individual in the process of overcoming adversities.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Public Speaking: When facing the fear of speaking in front of an audience, one can recite this mantra to invoke confidence and clarity in expression.
  • Conflict Resolution: In situations of disagreement, using gentle words and a calm demeanor can help in resolving conflicts and overcoming obstacles in communication.
  • Personal Challenges: When dealing with personal struggles, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to approach problems with awareness and a positive mindset.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the power of words and gentle expression in overcoming obstacles. It teaches us that through conscious speech and actions, we can manifest our intentions and navigate through life's challenges effectively. By invoking this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of awareness and empowerment in their daily lives.
