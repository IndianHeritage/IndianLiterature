आद्याऽनो देव सविता प्रजावत सावीः सौभगम् |परा दुष्ष्वप्न्यं सुव ||
adyā no deva savitaḥ prajāvat sāvīḥ saubhagam | parā duṣṣvapnyaṁ suva ||
  • adyā - Today
  • no - for us
  • deva - O divine one
  • savitaḥ - Savita, the Sun God
  • prajāvat - who gives birth to all beings
  • sāvīḥ - may you bestow
  • saubhagam - auspiciousness, prosperity
  • parā - supreme
  • duṣṣvapnyaṁ - dispeller of bad dreams
  • suva - good, auspicious

This mantra can be translated as: "O divine Savita, who gives birth to all beings, may you bestow upon us today auspiciousness and dispel all bad dreams."
The invocation of Savita, the Sun God, signifies the importance of light, knowledge, and life. The Sun is often seen as a source of energy and vitality, essential for growth and prosperity.
In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Morning Rituals: Many people recite this mantra during their morning prayers to seek blessings for a fruitful day ahead. Just as the Sun rises and dispels darkness, individuals hope to start their day with positivity and clarity.
  • Overcoming Challenges: The reference to dispelling bad dreams can be interpreted as a desire to overcome fears and anxieties. For instance, a student preparing for exams may recite this mantra to seek confidence and clarity, hoping to eliminate doubts and negative thoughts.
  • Community Gatherings: In communal settings, such as festivals or family gatherings, this mantra can be chanted to invoke collective prosperity and well-being, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.

In conclusion, this mantra emphasizes the significance of divine blessings in our daily lives. It serves as a reminder to seek positivity, dispel negativity, and embrace the auspiciousness that life has to offer. By invoking the Sun God, individuals can cultivate a mindset that is open to growth, prosperity, and the dispelling of fears, making it applicable in various real-life scenarios.
