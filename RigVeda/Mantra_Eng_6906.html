बृहन्निदिध्म ईषां भूरी शस्तं पृथुः स्वरुः |येषां इन्द्रो युवा सखा ||
bṛhann id idhma eṣām bhūri śastam pṛthuḥ svaruḥ | yeṣām indro yuvā sakhā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bṛhann: Great, vast, or expansive.
    • id: This.
    • idhma: Fire or sacrifice.
    • eṣām: Of them.
    • bhūri: Abundant or plentiful.
    • śastam: Best or most excellent.
    • pṛthuḥ: Broad or wide.
    • svaruḥ: Nature or essence.
    • yeṣām: Of whom.
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods.
    • yuvā: Young or youthful.
    • sakhā: Friend or companion.
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra speaks of the greatness and abundance of the offerings made to the divine, particularly to Indra, who is depicted as a youthful and powerful friend to those who worship him. It emphasizes the importance of sacrifice and the nature of the divine as being vast and generous.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals and sacrifices are performed to honor deities, similar to how offerings are made in the Vedic tradition. For instance, in Hinduism, the practice of 'Homa' involves making offerings into a sacred fire, symbolizing the connection between the material and spiritual worlds.
    • The concept of friendship and support from a higher power can be seen in various religious practices where individuals seek guidance and strength from their faith during challenging times, akin to how Indra is portrayed as a youthful companion to his devotees.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the vastness of divine blessings and the importance of sincere offerings. It teaches us that through our actions and sacrifices, we can cultivate a relationship with the divine that is nurturing and supportive. In real life, this can be applied by engaging in acts of kindness, community service, and spiritual practices that foster a sense of connection with something greater than ourselves, ultimately leading to personal growth and fulfillment.
