तस्माद् अश्वा अजायन्त ये के चोभयादतः |गावो ह जज्निरे तस्मात् तस्माद् जाताः अजावयः ||
tasmād aśvā ajāyanta ye ke cobhayādataḥ | gāvo ha jajñire tasmāt tasmāj jātā ajāvayaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tasmād: Therefore
    • aśvā: Horses
    • ajāyanta: Were born
    • ye: Those who
    • ke: Who
    • cobhayādataḥ: From both sides
    • gāvo: Cows
    • ha: Indeed
    • jajñire: Were born
    • tasmāt: Therefore
    • tasmāj: From that
    • jātā: Born
    • ajāvayaḥ: The progeny of the horses
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the origin of horses and cows, suggesting a divine or cosmic process of creation.
    • The phrase "tasmād aśvā ajāyanta" indicates that horses were born from a certain source, which can be interpreted as a metaphor for strength and nobility.
    • The mention of cows, "gāvo ha jajñire," highlights their importance in Vedic culture, symbolizing nourishment and wealth.
    • The term "ajāvayaḥ" refers to the offspring of horses, which can be seen as a representation of vitality and energy in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, horses are often used for labor and transportation, symbolizing strength and hard work.
    • Cows are revered in many cultures for their role in providing milk and other dairy products, representing sustenance and prosperity.
    • The relationship between horses and cows can be seen in farming practices where both animals contribute to the ecosystem, promoting balance and harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life and the importance of both strength (horses) and sustenance (cows) in human existence.
    • It serves as a reminder of the respect and reverence that should be given to nature and its creatures, as they play vital roles in our lives.
    • In modern contexts, this can be applied to sustainable farming practices, where the balance between different species is crucial for ecological health.
