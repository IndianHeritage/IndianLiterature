मन्त्र: एते सोमाः अभि गव्याः सहस्राः महे वाजायामृताय श्रवांसि |पवित्रैः पवमानाः असृग्गृण च्रवस्यवो न पृतनाजो अत्याः ||
ete somā abhi gavyā sahasrā mahe vājāyāmṛtāya śravāṁsi | pavitrebhiḥ pavamānā asṛgrañ chravasyavo na pṛtanājo atyāḥ ||
  • Translation: "These (soma) are the thousand (soma) that are offered to the great (deities) for the purpose of immortality and glory. Purified by the purifying (soma), they flow forth, and the hearers are not to be disturbed by the unworthy."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ete: these
    • somā: soma, a sacred drink in Vedic rituals
    • abhi: towards, for
    • gavyā: related to cows, symbolizing wealth and nourishment
    • sahasrā: thousand, representing abundance
    • mahe: great, significant
    • vājāyāmṛtāya: for the purpose of immortality and glory
    • śravāṁsi: fame, glory
    • pavitrebhiḥ: by the purified
    • pavamānā: flowing, moving
    • asṛgrañ: not to be disturbed
    • chravasyavo: hearers, listeners
    • na: not
    • pṛtanājo: unworthy, impure
    • atyāḥ: disturbed, troubled
  • Contextual Significance:
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of purity and the sacredness of offerings in Vedic rituals.
    • Soma, as a drink, symbolizes not just physical nourishment but also spiritual sustenance, representing the connection between the material and spiritual worlds.
    • The mention of "thousand" signifies abundance and the idea that true wealth comes from spiritual practices and community offerings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the practice of mindfulness and purity in our actions and thoughts. Just as the soma is purified, individuals can strive to purify their intentions and actions.
    • Community gatherings, such as festivals or rituals, can be seen as modern-day equivalents of the offerings made in the Vedic times, where people come together to celebrate and share their resources for collective well-being.
    • The idea of not being disturbed by the unworthy can be applied to personal boundaries in life, where one learns to filter out negativity and focus on positive influences.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of purity, community, and the pursuit of spiritual wealth over material wealth.
    • It encourages individuals to engage in practices that promote inner peace and collective harmony, making it relevant in today's fast-paced and often chaotic world.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious society, fostering connections that are based on mutual respect and spiritual growth.
