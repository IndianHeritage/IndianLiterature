युवं ह गर्भं जगतीषु धत्तो युवं विश्वेषु भुवनेष्व अन्तः |युवं अग्निं च वृषाणाव अपश च वनस्पतींर अश्विनाव ऐरयेताम् ||
yuvaṁ ha garbhaṁ jagatīṣu dhattho yuvaṁ viśveṣu bhuvaneṣv antaḥ | yuvam agniṁ ca vṛṣaṇāv apaś ca vanaspatīm̐r aśvināv airayethām ||
  • Mantra: "yuvaṁ ha garbhaṁ jagatīṣu dhattho yuvaṁ viśveṣu bhuvaneṣv antaḥ | yuvam agniṁ ca vṛṣaṇāv apaś ca vanaspatīm̐r aśvināv airayethām ||"
  • Translation: "You, O Youth, are the womb of the world; you are the essence within all beings. You are the fire, the strength, and the plants; may the Ashvins (divine twin horsemen) bring you forth."

  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yuvaṁ: Youth, representing vitality and potential.
    • ha: Indeed, emphasizing the statement.
    • garbhaṁ: Womb or source, indicating creation and nurturing.
    • jagatīṣu: In the worlds or in existence, referring to the universe.
    • dhattho: You hold or support, indicating strength and stability.
    • viśveṣu: In all, encompassing everything.
    • bhuvaneṣv: In the realms or domains, referring to various aspects of life.
    • antaḥ: Within, indicating the inner essence.
    • agniṁ: Fire, symbolizing energy and transformation.
    • vṛṣaṇāv: Strength or power, indicating capability.
    • apaś: Plants, representing growth and sustenance.
    • vanaspatīm̐r: Vegetation or trees, symbolizing life and nature.
    • aśvināv: The Ashvins, divine beings associated with health and well-being.
    • airayethām: May they bring forth, a request for manifestation.

  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of youth and vitality as the source of creation and sustenance in the universe.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of all beings and the essential role of energy (Agni) and nature (plants) in our lives.
    • The invocation of the Ashvins suggests a plea for health, prosperity, and the nurturing of life.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In personal development, this mantra can inspire individuals to harness their youthful energy and creativity to contribute positively to society.
    • In environmental contexts, it serves as a reminder of the importance of nature and the need to protect our ecosystems for future generations.
    • In health and wellness, invoking the qualities of the Ashvins can encourage practices that promote physical and mental well-being.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of youth, creation, and the vital forces of nature, urging us to recognize our role in the universe.
    • By understanding and applying its teachings, we can foster a deeper connection with ourselves, others, and the environment, leading to a more harmonious existence.
