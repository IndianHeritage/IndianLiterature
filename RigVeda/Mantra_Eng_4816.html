यत्र शूरासस तन्भो वितन्वते प्रियā शर्मा पितृणाम् |अध स्मा यच्छ तन्बे तने च छर्दिर अचित्तं यावय द्वेषः ||
yatra śūrāsas tanvo vitanvate priyā śarma pitṝṇām | adha smā yaccha tanve3 tane ca chardir acittaṁ yāvaya dveṣaḥ ||
  • yatra - where
  • śūrāsas - the brave or the valiant
  • tanvo - bodies or forms
  • vitanvate - spread or extend
  • priyā - dear or beloved
  • śarma - comfort or happiness
  • pitṝṇām - of the ancestors
  • adha - below or beneath
  • smā - may we
  • yaccha - grant or give
  • tanve - to the body
  • tane - to the form
  • ca - and
  • chardir - prosperity or wealth
  • acittaṁ - without anxiety or worry
  • yāvaya - as long as
  • dveṣaḥ - hatred or enmity

This mantra from the Rigveda speaks to the themes of bravery, comfort, and the blessings of ancestors. It emphasizes the importance of spreading happiness and prosperity while minimizing negativity and hatred.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Community Building: Just as the mantra calls for the spread of comfort and happiness, communities can thrive when individuals work together to support one another, fostering an environment free from hatred.
  • Family Relationships: The reference to ancestors highlights the importance of family ties. By honoring our ancestors and maintaining strong family bonds, we can create a nurturing environment for future generations.
  • Personal Growth: The mantra encourages individuals to seek prosperity and happiness while letting go of negative emotions. This can be seen in practices like mindfulness and emotional intelligence, which promote mental well-being.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, positivity, and the honoring of our roots. In a world often filled with conflict and division, it serves as a reminder to cultivate love and support, both within ourselves and in our communities. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
