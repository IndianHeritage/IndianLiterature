वाचो दीर्घप्रसद्मनीशे वाजस्य गोमतः |ईशे हि पित्वो 'विषस्य दावने ||
vaco dīrghaprasadmanīśe vājasya gomataḥ | īśe hi pitvo 'viṣasya dāvane ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • vaco: Refers to "speech" or "words". In a broader sense, it signifies communication and expression.
    • dīrghaprasadmanīśe: This can be interpreted as "long-lasting and beneficial". It suggests the importance of words that have a lasting impact.
    • vājasya: Means "of strength" or "of power". It indicates the potency of the words spoken.
    • gomataḥ: Translates to "of the cow". In Vedic culture, the cow is a symbol of nourishment and wealth.
    • īśe: Means "to the Lord" or "to the divine". It signifies a connection to a higher power.
    • hi: A particle that emphasizes the preceding statement.
    • pitvo: Refers to "drinking" or "consuming". It can symbolize the act of taking in knowledge or wisdom.
    • 'viṣasya: Means "of poison". This can be interpreted as negative influences or harmful words.
    • dāvane: Translates to "in the fire". It symbolizes purification or transformation through trials.
  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the power of words and their ability to create lasting effects.
    • It suggests that words should be beneficial and nourishing, much like the cow in Vedic culture.
    • The connection to the divine indicates that our speech should be aligned with higher principles and values.
    • It warns against the consumption of harmful words or negative influences, which can be likened to poison.
    • The imagery of fire suggests that through challenges and trials, one can purify their speech and intentions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal relationships, using kind and constructive words can strengthen bonds, while harsh words can cause lasting damage.
    • In leadership, effective communication can inspire and motivate teams, whereas negative speech can lead to discord.
    • In education, teachers who use encouraging language can foster a positive learning environment, while criticism can hinder student growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of our words and their impact on ourselves and others.
    • It encourages us to speak with intention, ensuring that our words are beneficial and aligned with our values.
    • In a world where communication is key, this mantra highlights the importance of mindful speech in fostering positive relationships and environments.
