न रेवता पणिना सख्यम इन्द्रो 'सुन्वता सुतपाः सं गृणीते |आस्य वेदः खिदति हन्ति नग्नं वि सुष्वये पक्टये केवलो भूत ||
na revatā paṇinā sakhyam indro 'sunvatā sutapāḥ saṁ gṛṇīte | āsya vedaḥ khidati hanti nagnaṁ vi suṣvaye paktaye kevalo bhūt ||
  • na revatā paṇinā sakhyam: This phrase can be translated as "not by the hand of the one who is unworthy." It suggests that true friendship and alliances are formed not through superficial means but through genuine worthiness and mutual respect.
  • indro 'sunvatā sutapāḥ: Here, "Indra" refers to the king of gods, symbolizing strength and power. "Sunvatā" means "of the wise" and "sutapāḥ" refers to "those who are well-prepared." This part emphasizes that true strength and wisdom come from preparation and understanding.
  • saṁ gṛṇīte: This means "he sings together" or "he praises." It indicates the importance of collective effort and harmony in achieving goals.
  • āsya vedaḥ khidati: Translated as "this knowledge is hidden," it suggests that wisdom and understanding are often not immediately visible and require effort to uncover.
  • hanti nagnaṁ: This phrase means "it destroys the naked." It can be interpreted as the idea that ignorance or lack of preparation can lead to vulnerability and downfall.
  • vi suṣvaye paktaye: "Vi" means "well," "suṣvaye" refers to "well-being," and "paktaye" means "to the one who cooks." This part highlights the importance of nurturing and care in achieving success and well-being.
  • kevalo bhūt: This translates to "only the essence exists." It signifies that the core truth or essence of existence is what truly matters, beyond superficial appearances.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of genuine relationships, wisdom, and preparation in life. It teaches that true strength comes from understanding and collaboration, while ignorance can lead to vulnerability. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal relationships, teamwork in professional settings, and the pursuit of knowledge. By fostering genuine connections and preparing ourselves wisely, we can navigate life's challenges more effectively and achieve a deeper sense of fulfillment and success.
