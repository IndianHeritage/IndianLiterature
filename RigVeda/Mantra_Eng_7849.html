आ यो विश्वानि वार्याः वासूनि हस्तयोः दधे |मदेषु सर्वधाऽसि ||
ā yo viśvāni vāryā vasūni hastayor dadhe | madeṣu sarvadhā asi ||
  • Mantra: "ā yo viśvāni vāryā vasūni hastayor dadhe | madeṣu sarvadhā asi ||"
  • Translation: "He who has placed all the powers and riches in our hands, may He grant us all kinds of enjoyment."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā: A prefix meaning "from" or "out of."
    • yo: "who" or "that which."
    • viśvāni: "all" or "universal," referring to all powers or energies.
    • vāryā: "powers" or "strengths," indicating capabilities or resources.
    • vasūni: "riches" or "wealth," referring to material and spiritual abundance.
    • hastayor: "in our hands," symbolizing possession or control.
    • dadhe: "may He grant," a request for bestowal or blessing.
    • madeṣu: "in enjoyments," referring to pleasures or experiences.
    • sarvadhā: "in all ways," indicating a comprehensive approach.
    • asi: "you are," affirming existence or presence.
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine blessings in acquiring both material and spiritual wealth.
    • It reflects a universal desire for abundance and the acknowledgment that such blessings come from a higher power.
    • In real life, this can be seen in various contexts, such as:
      • In business, entrepreneurs often seek divine guidance and blessings for success and prosperity.
      • Individuals may pray for health and happiness, recognizing that these are forms of wealth.
      • Communities may come together in rituals or prayers to invoke blessings for collective well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all aspects of life—material, spiritual, and communal.
    • It highlights the significance of seeking blessings and recognizing the source of our strengths and resources.
    • In practical terms, it encourages gratitude and mindfulness in our pursuits, fostering a holistic approach to life.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that true wealth encompasses not just material possessions but also joy, health, and spiritual fulfillment.
