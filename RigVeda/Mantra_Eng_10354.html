वृषारवाय वदते यद उपावति चिच्चिकः |आघाटिभिर इव धावयन्न अरण्यानिर महीयते ||
vṛṣāravāya vadate yad upāvati ciccikaḥ | āghāṭibhir iva dhāvayann araṇyānir mahīyate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vṛṣāravāya: Referring to the one who is powerful or strong, often associated with the bull, symbolizing strength and vitality.
    • vadate: Speaks or expresses.
    • yad: That which.
    • upāvati: Approaches or comes near.
    • ciccikaḥ: A term that can imply a sense of awareness or consciousness.
    • āghāṭibhir: By those who strike or hit.
    • iva: Like or as.
    • dhāvayan: Running or moving swiftly.
    • araṇyānir: In the wilderness or forest.
    • mahīyate: Becomes great or is exalted.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful entity that expresses itself and approaches with awareness.
    • It draws a parallel to the swift movement in the wilderness, suggesting a sense of freedom and strength.
    • The imagery of running in the forest evokes a sense of untamed nature, where one can find true power and greatness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the context of personal growth, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to embrace one's inner strength and express oneself authentically.
    • In nature, the powerful movement of animals in the wild can symbolize the importance of being in tune with one's instincts and surroundings.
    • In leadership, the idea of approaching challenges with awareness and strength can inspire individuals to take decisive actions in their careers.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call to recognize and harness one's inner strength and awareness.
    • It encourages individuals to move boldly through life's challenges, much like a powerful creature in the wilderness.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal development, leadership, and the pursuit of one's passions, reminding us that true greatness comes from within.
