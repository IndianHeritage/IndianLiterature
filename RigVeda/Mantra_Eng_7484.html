पुरुहूतं पुरुष्टुतं गाथान्यं सनाश्रुतम् |इन्द्र इति ब्रवीतान ||
puruhūtam puruṣṭutaṁ gāthānya1ṁ sanaśrutam | indra iti bravītana ||
  • Mantra: "puruhūtam puruṣṭutaṁ gāthānyaṁ sanaśrutam | indra iti bravītana ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Puruhūtam: One who is invoked frequently; a term often associated with deities who are called upon in rituals.
    • Puruṣṭutaṁ: The one who is praised or extolled; signifies the importance of recognition and reverence.
    • Gāthānyaṁ: Related to hymns or verses; indicates the power of words and chants in spiritual practices.
    • Sanaśrutam: That which is heard or known; emphasizes the significance of knowledge and wisdom.
    • Indra: The king of gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength, power, and leadership.
    • Bravītana: To declare or proclaim; signifies the act of announcing or affirming something important.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of Indra, highlighting the importance of calling upon divine forces in our lives.
    • The repeated invocation of "puruhūtam" suggests that in our daily lives, we often seek guidance and support from higher powers, whether through prayer, meditation, or reflection.
    • "Puruṣṭutaṁ" reminds us of the value of appreciation and acknowledgment, both in spiritual contexts and in our relationships with others.
    • The reference to "gāthānyaṁ" illustrates the power of words; just as hymns can uplift and inspire, our spoken words can have a profound impact on ourselves and those around us.
    • "Sanaśrutam" emphasizes the importance of learning and wisdom, encouraging us to seek knowledge and understanding in our pursuits.
    • Indra, as a symbol of strength and leadership, serves as a reminder of the qualities we should aspire to embody in our own lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of difficulty, individuals often turn to prayer or meditation, invoking a higher power for strength and guidance, similar to the invocation of "puruhūtam."
    • In professional settings, recognizing and praising the contributions of team members can foster a positive environment, reflecting the essence of "puruṣṭutaṁ."
    • Public speaking or motivational speaking often relies on the power of words, akin to "gāthānyaṁ," to inspire and motivate audiences.
    • Continuous learning and seeking wisdom, as highlighted by "sanaśrutam," is crucial in personal and professional development, encouraging lifelong education.
    • Leadership qualities, represented by Indra, can be seen in effective leaders who inspire and guide their teams towards success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of invoking divine support, recognizing the importance of praise, the power of words, and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • Its significance lies in its applicability to everyday life, reminding us to seek guidance, appreciate others, communicate effectively, and strive for wisdom.
    • By embodying the qualities represented in this mantra, we can enhance our personal growth and contribute positively to our communities.
