अग्नेर वयं प्रथमस्यामृतानाम् मनामहे चारु देवस्य नाम। स नो मह्या अदितये पुनर दात पितरं च दृष्टेयं मातरं च॥
agner vayam prathamasyāmṛtānām manāmahe cāru devasya nāma | sa no mahyā aditaye punar dāt pitaraṁ ca dṛśeyam mātaraṁ ca ||
  • Agner: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification.
  • Vayam: This means "we" or "us," indicating a collective identity or community.
  • Prathamasyāmṛtānām: "Prathama" means "first," and "amṛtānām" refers to "immortal beings." Together, it signifies the first among the immortal beings, possibly referring to the divine or celestial entities.
  • Manāmahe: This translates to "we meditate" or "we contemplate," suggesting a focus on spiritual practice and devotion.
  • Cāru: Meaning "beautiful" or "auspicious," it denotes the positive qualities of the divine.
  • Devasya: This means "of the deity," referring to the divine presence or godliness.
  • Nāma: This translates to "name," indicating the importance of the divine name in spiritual practices.
  • Sa no: This phrase means "may he" or "let him," expressing a wish or prayer.
  • Mahyā: Meaning "to us," it emphasizes the collective aspect of the prayer.
  • Aditaye: Referring to "Aditi," the mother of gods and a symbol of boundless space and potential.
  • Punar: This means "again" or "once more," indicating a cycle or repetition.
  • Dāt: Meaning "to give," it expresses a desire for blessings or gifts from the divine.
  • Pitaraṁ: This translates to "father," symbolizing paternal guidance and protection.
  • Dṛśeyam: Meaning "to be seen," it indicates a wish for visibility or recognition of the divine presence.
  • Mātaraṁ: This means "mother," representing maternal care and nurturing.

Conclusion: This mantra is a prayer invoking the divine presence of Agni, seeking blessings for the community. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and meditating on the divine qualities of beauty and auspiciousness. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during rituals, family gatherings, or personal meditation practices, where individuals seek guidance, protection, and blessings from both paternal and maternal figures. The significance lies in fostering a sense of community, reverence for the divine, and the pursuit of spiritual growth and harmony in everyday life.
