त्वं वृधा इन्द्र पूर्व्यो भूर्वरिवस्यन्न उषणे काव्याय |परा नववास्त्वम अनुदेयम महे पितरे ददाथ स्वं नपातम् ||
tvaṁ vṛdha indra pūrvyo bhūr varivasyann uśane kāvyāya | parā navavāstvam anudeyam mahe pitre dadātha svaṁ napātam ||
  • tvaṁ vṛdha indra pūrvyo: This phrase addresses Indra, the king of the gods, emphasizing his greatness and strength. "Vṛdha" means great or powerful, and "pūrvyo" refers to the ancient or primordial aspect of Indra. In real life, this can be likened to leaders or figures of authority who are respected for their wisdom and experience.
  • bhūr varivasyann uśane kāvyāya: Here, "bhūr" signifies abundance or wealth, while "varivasyann" suggests the act of bestowing or providing. "Uśane" refers to the sun, symbolizing light and clarity, and "kāvyāya" relates to poetry or creativity. This can be interpreted as a call for the blessings of abundance and creativity in our lives, akin to how artists and innovators seek inspiration from nature and the universe.
  • parā navavāstvam anudeyam: "Parā" means supreme or beyond, while "navavāstvam" refers to newness or freshness. "Anudeyam" implies something that is to be attained or achieved. This part of the mantra encourages the pursuit of new and higher goals, much like how individuals strive for personal growth and new experiences in their lives.
  • mahe pitre dadātha svaṁ napātam: "Mahe" means great, "pitre" refers to the father or progenitor, and "dadātha" means to give or bestow. "Svaṁ napātam" translates to one's own offspring or legacy. This line highlights the importance of heritage and the blessings we receive from our ancestors, reminding us of our roots and the values passed down through generations.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates themes of strength, abundance, creativity, personal growth, and the significance of heritage. In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek guidance from wise leaders, embrace creativity, strive for new achievements, and honor our ancestors. By reflecting on these principles, individuals can cultivate a fulfilling and meaningful life, drawing inspiration from both the divine and their own lineage.
