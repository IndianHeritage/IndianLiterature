प्रत्नवज जनयाऽ गिरः श्रिणुधी जारितुर हवम् |मदेमद ववक्षिता सुखृत्वने ||
pratnavaj janayā giraḥ śṛṇudhī jaritur havam | made-made vavakṣithā sukṛtvane ||
  • Mantra: "pratnavaj janayā giraḥ śṛṇudhī jaritur havam | made-made vavakṣithā sukṛtvane ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pratnavaj: One who is born from the essence of knowledge or wisdom.
    • janayā: By giving birth or generating.
    • giraḥ: Words or speech.
    • śṛṇudhī: Listen or pay attention.
    • jaritur: The one who is old or wise.
    • havam: The offering or invocation.
    • made-made: With joy or enthusiasm.
    • vavakṣithā: You have declared or expressed.
    • sukṛtvane: Those who perform good deeds or actions.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in our lives.
    • It calls upon individuals to listen carefully to the words of the wise, suggesting that true understanding comes from heeding the experiences and teachings of those who have lived longer and learned more.
    • The invocation of "havam" indicates a call to action, urging individuals to participate in the process of learning and sharing knowledge.
    • The phrase "made-made" signifies the joy that comes from engaging in this process, highlighting that learning and sharing wisdom should be a joyful experience.
    • Finally, the mantra encourages individuals to perform good deeds, suggesting that wisdom should lead to positive actions in the world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In educational settings, teachers embody the "jaritur" by sharing their knowledge with students, who are encouraged to listen and learn.
    • In community gatherings, elders often share stories and wisdom, reminding younger generations to pay attention and learn from their experiences.
    • In workplaces, mentorship programs allow experienced employees to guide newcomers, fostering a culture of knowledge sharing and collaboration.
    • Volunteering for community service can be seen as a "sukṛtvane," where individuals perform good deeds inspired by the wisdom they have gained.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of wisdom and the importance of listening to those who have more experience.
    • It highlights the joy that can be found in learning and sharing knowledge, encouraging a culture of positivity and good deeds.
    • In real life, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to personal growth, stronger communities, and a more harmonious society.
