मन्त्र: कद ū महीर अधृष्टा अस्य तविषीः कद उ वृतघ्नो अस्तृतम् |इन्द्रो विश्वान बेकनाटाम् अहर्दृष्ट उ त क्रत्वा पणींर अभि ||
kad ū mahīr adhṛṣṭā asya taviṣīḥ kad u vṛtraghno astṛtam | indro viśvān bekanāṭām̐ ahardṛśa uta kratvā paṇīm̐r abhi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kad: when
    • ū: indeed
    • mahīr: of the great
    • adhṛṣṭā: unseen or unperceived
    • asya: of this
    • taviṣīḥ: strength or power
    • kad: when
    • u: or
    • vṛtraghno: the slayer of Vritra (a demon)
    • astṛtam: has been established
    • indro: Indra (the king of gods)
    • viśvān: all
    • bekanāṭām̐: the vastness or expanse
    • ahardṛśa: day by day
    • uta: or
    • kratvā: with power or strength
    • paṇīm̐r: with hands
    • abhi: towards
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects a deep inquiry into the nature of strength and power.
    • It questions when the unseen forces of strength will manifest and when the slayer of obstacles (Vritra) will establish order.
    • Indra, as a symbol of divine power, is invoked to bring forth the vastness of existence and to act with strength day by day.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal challenges, individuals often seek strength to overcome obstacles, much like Indra overcoming Vritra.
    • In leadership, the mantra can be seen as a call for leaders to harness their power responsibly and to act decisively for the greater good.
    • In community efforts, the invocation of collective strength can lead to significant changes, reflecting the vastness of potential when united.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and harnessing unseen strengths in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that challenges can be overcome with the right mindset and collective effort.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to seek strength and act with purpose, fostering resilience and unity.
