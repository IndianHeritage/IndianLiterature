एव बभ्रो वृषभ चेकिताना यथा देव न हृणीषे न हंसि |हवनश्रुं नो रुद्रहे बोधि बृहद् वदेम विदथे सुवीराः ||
evā babhro vṛṣabha cekitāna yathā deva na hṛṇīṣe na haṁsi | havanaśrun no rudreha bodhi bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ ||
  • evā babhro: This phrase can be interpreted as "thus, we have come" or "in this manner, we have arrived." It signifies a sense of presence and readiness.
  • vṛṣabha: This word translates to "the bull," which is often a symbol of strength, power, and fertility in Vedic literature. The bull represents the force of nature and the vigor of life.
  • cekitāna: This term refers to "the one who is aware" or "the one who knows." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and awareness in our actions and decisions.
  • yathā deva: Meaning "as the gods," this phrase suggests that our actions should align with divine principles or the way of the gods, indicating a higher moral standard.
  • na hṛṇīṣe na haṁsi: This translates to "do not take away, do not harm." It is a plea for protection and non-violence, urging us to refrain from causing harm to others.
  • havanaśrun: This word means "the sound of the offering" or "the call of the sacrifice." It signifies the importance of rituals and offerings in connecting with the divine.
  • no rudreha: This phrase translates to "to us, O Rudra," invoking the deity Rudra, who represents storm and destruction but also healing and transformation.
  • bodhi: Meaning "wisdom" or "enlightenment," this term emphasizes the pursuit of knowledge and understanding in our lives.
  • bṛhad: This word means "great" or "vast," indicating the importance of expansive thinking and broad perspectives.
  • vadema: This translates to "we speak" or "we declare," highlighting the power of words and communication in shaping our reality.
  • vidathe: This term refers to "the assembly" or "the gathering," emphasizing the significance of community and collective action.
  • suvīrāḥ: Meaning "the brave" or "the valiant," this word calls for courage and strength in facing challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the importance of strength, knowledge, and non-violence in our lives. It encourages us to act with awareness and align our actions with divine principles, fostering a sense of community and collective responsibility. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as promoting peace in conflict situations, encouraging ethical leadership in organizations, and inspiring individuals to pursue wisdom and courage in their personal and professional lives. By embodying these values, we contribute to a more harmonious and just society.
