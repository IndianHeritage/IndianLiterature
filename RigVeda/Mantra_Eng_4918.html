न तद् दिवा न पृथिव्याऽनु मन्ये न यज्ञेन नोत शमीभिराभिः |उभ्जन्तु तं सुभवः पर्वतासो न हीयताम् अतियाजस्य यष्टा ||
na tad divā na pṛthivyānu manye na yajñena nota śamībhir ābhiḥ | ubjantu taṁ subhva1ḥ parvatāso ni hīyatām atiyājasya yaṣṭā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na - not
    • tad - that
    • divā - in the heavens
    • na - not
    • pṛthivyā - on the earth
    • anu - after
    • manye - I think
    • na - not
    • yajñena - by sacrifice
    • na - not
    • uta - or
    • śamībhir - by the trees
    • ābhiḥ - with their branches
    • ubjantu - may they arise
    • taṁ - that
    • subhvaḥ - auspicious
    • parvatāso - from the mountains
    • ni - not
    • hīyatām - may they be diminished
    • atiyājasya - of the one who performs great sacrifices
    • yaṣṭā - the one who offers
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a profound thought about the nature of existence and the importance of sacrifice.
    • The speaker reflects on the idea that true value and essence cannot be confined to the heavens or the earth.
    • It emphasizes that neither rituals (yajñas) nor the natural world (represented by trees) can fully encapsulate the divine or the ultimate reality.
    • The invocation for auspiciousness from the mountains suggests a connection to nature and the higher realms, indicating that true blessings come from a higher source.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the pursuit of deeper meaning beyond material success. For instance, individuals often seek fulfillment through relationships, personal growth, and spiritual practices rather than just through wealth or status.
    • In environmental discussions, the reference to trees and mountains can symbolize the importance of nature in our lives. It reminds us that our well-being is interconnected with the health of our planet.
    • In the context of community service, the idea of sacrifice can be seen in acts of kindness and charity, where individuals contribute to the welfare of others, reflecting the essence of true giving.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of looking beyond the superficial aspects of life and recognizing the deeper connections that bind us to the universe.
    • It encourages individuals to engage in meaningful actions that contribute to the greater good, whether through personal sacrifice, environmental stewardship, or community involvement.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that true fulfillment comes from aligning our actions with higher principles and values, fostering a sense of unity with all of existence.
