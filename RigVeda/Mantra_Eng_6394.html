दीर्घस् ते अस्त्व अङ्कुशो येन वसु प्रयच्छसि |यजमानाय सुन्वते ||
dīrghas te astv aṅkuśo yenā vasu prayacchasi | yajamānāya sunvate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dīrghas: Long, enduring, or lasting.
    • te: Your.
    • astv: May there be.
    • aṅkuśo: A goad or a guiding force.
    • yenā: By which.
    • vasu: Wealth or prosperity.
    • prayacchasi: You bestow or grant.
    • yajamānāya: To the one who is performing the sacrifice or offering.
    • sunvate: May they be well spoken of or praised.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer for long-lasting guidance and prosperity.
    • The "goad" symbolizes a force that directs and motivates individuals towards their goals.
    • It emphasizes the importance of the giver (the divine or the universe) in bestowing wealth and prosperity upon those who are engaged in righteous actions.
    • The phrase "may they be well spoken of" highlights the significance of reputation and the impact of one's actions on how they are perceived by others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, a leader who guides their team with a clear vision (the goad) can lead to long-term success and prosperity for the organization.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who support and uplift each other contribute to a lasting bond and mutual respect.
    • In community service, those who dedicate their time to help others often receive recognition and gratitude, enhancing their reputation and sense of fulfillment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of enduring guidance and the role of divine blessings in achieving prosperity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as leadership, relationships, and community involvement, where the focus is on positive actions and their long-term impact.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can strive for a life that is not only prosperous but also respected and admired by others.
