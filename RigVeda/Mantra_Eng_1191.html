माच्छेद्मा रश्मींरिति नाधमानाः pitṝṇां शक्तीर अनुयच्छमानाः | इन्द्राग्निभ्यां कं वृषणो मदन्ति ता ह्य अद्री धिषणायाः उपस्थे ||
mā cchedma raśmīm̐r iti nādhamānāḥ pitṝṇāṁ śaktīr anuyacchamānāḥ | indrāgnibhyāṁ kaṁ vṛṣaṇo madanti tā hy adrī dhiṣaṇāyā upasthe ||
  • mā cchedma raśmīm̐r iti - This phrase can be translated as "Do not cut off the rays." Here, "raśmīm̐r" refers to the rays of light or energy, symbolizing knowledge, wisdom, and divine guidance. In real life, this can be related to the importance of maintaining a connection with our sources of inspiration and enlightenment, such as mentors, teachers, or spiritual guides.
  • nādhamānāḥ pitṝṇāṁ - This translates to "those who are not diminished, the ancestors." It emphasizes the idea of continuity and the strength of ancestral wisdom. In practical terms, this can be seen in how families and communities honor their ancestors through traditions, rituals, and storytelling, ensuring that their values and teachings are passed down through generations.
  • śaktīr anuyacchamānāḥ - This means "the power that is being transmitted." It highlights the concept of energy and strength that flows from one generation to another. In everyday life, this can be observed in how individuals draw strength from their heritage, cultural practices, and the support of their community, which empowers them to face challenges.
  • indrāgnibhyāṁ kaṁ vṛṣaṇo madanti - This phrase can be interpreted as "who are delighted by Indra and Agni." Indra and Agni are deities representing rain and fire, respectively, symbolizing the forces of nature that sustain life. In a real-world context, this can relate to the joy and gratitude people feel when they receive the blessings of nature, such as rain for crops or fire for warmth and cooking.
  • tā hy adrī dhiṣaṇāyā upasthe - This translates to "they are indeed the support of the mountains." Mountains often symbolize stability, strength, and endurance. In life, this can be seen in how individuals and communities rely on their foundational values and principles, much like mountains provide a solid base for the earth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of connection, continuity, and the transmission of strength and wisdom from ancestors to descendants. It emphasizes the importance of maintaining our ties to knowledge and the natural world, as well as the support systems that help us navigate life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as family traditions, community support, and the appreciation of nature's gifts, reminding us to honor our past while building a resilient future.
