सोमस्य राजा वरुणस्य धर्मणि बृहस्पतेर अनुमत्याऽउ शर्मणि |तवाहम अद्य मगवन्न उपस्तुतौ धातार विधाता: कलशां अभक्षयम् ||
somasya rājño varuṇasya dharmaṇi bṛhaspater anumatyā u śarmaṇi | tavāham adya maghavann upastutau dhātar vidhātaḥ kalaśām̐ abhakṣayam ||
  • Mantra: "somasya rājño varuṇasya dharmaṇi bṛhaspater anumatyā u śarmaṇi | tavāham adya maghavann upastutau dhātar vidhātaḥ kalaśām̐ abhakṣayam ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • somasya: of Soma (the divine nectar or drink)
    • rājño: of the king
    • varuṇasya: of Varuna (the god of cosmic order)
    • dharmaṇi: in righteousness or duty
    • bṛhaspater: of Brihaspati (the lord of speech and wisdom)
    • anumatyā: with permission or approval
    • u: and
    • śarmaṇi: in protection or shelter
    • tavāham: I, today, for you
    • adya: today
    • maghavann: O mighty one
    • upastutau: in praise
    • dhātar: the creator
    • vidhātaḥ: the ordainer
    • kalaśām̐: of the pot (often symbolizing abundance)
    • abhakṣayam: unconsumed or undiminished
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine qualities of Soma, the king, and Varuna, emphasizing the importance of righteousness and cosmic order.
    • The reference to Brihaspati highlights the need for wisdom and guidance in our actions.
    • The speaker expresses a desire to offer praise and seek protection, indicating a relationship of devotion and respect towards the divine.
    • The mention of the pot symbolizes abundance and the idea that divine blessings should remain undiminished, reflecting the need for sustenance and prosperity in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be related to seeking guidance and wisdom from mentors (akin to Brihaspati) while ensuring that actions taken are in line with ethical practices (dharma).
    • In personal life, individuals may recite this mantra during times of uncertainty, seeking protection and abundance in their endeavors, much like a student preparing for exams seeks divine support for success.
    • Communities may come together to chant this mantra during festivals, invoking blessings for prosperity and harmony, reflecting the collective spirit of seeking divine favor.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of aligning oneself with divine principles of righteousness, wisdom, and abundance.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek guidance and protection in our daily lives, ensuring that our actions contribute positively to the cosmic order.
    • In essence, it encourages individuals and communities to cultivate a relationship with the divine, fostering a sense of gratitude and responsibility towards the greater good.
