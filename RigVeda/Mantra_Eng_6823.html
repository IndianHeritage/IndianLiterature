तं शिशीतां सुवृक्तिभिः तवेषं सत्वानाम ऋग्मियम् |उतो नु चिद य ओजसा शुष्णस्याण्डानि भेदति जेषत स्वरवतीर अपो नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
taṁ śiśītā suvṛktibhis tveṣaṁ satvānam ṛgmiyam | uto nu cid ya ojasā śuṣṇasyāṇḍāni bhedati jeṣat svarvatīr apo nabhantām anyake same ||
  • taṁ - This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity or force.
  • śiśītā - This means "who is cool" or "who brings coolness," often associated with calmness and tranquility.
  • suvṛktibhis - This can be interpreted as "with good words" or "with auspicious speech," emphasizing the importance of positive communication.
  • tveṣaṁ - This means "your" or "of you," indicating a personal connection to the divine.
  • sattvānam - This refers to "of the beings" or "of the living," highlighting the interconnectedness of all life.
  • ṛgmiyam - This word relates to "the hymns" or "the verses," suggesting the power of sacred texts and their recitation.
  • uto - This translates to "or," indicating a choice or alternative.
  • nu - This means "now," emphasizing the present moment.
  • cid - This word means "if" or "whether," introducing a conditional aspect.
  • ya - This translates to "who," referring to a person or entity.
  • ojasā - This means "with energy" or "with strength," highlighting the importance of vitality.
  • śuṣṇasyāṇḍāni - This can be interpreted as "the heat" or "the warmth," often associated with the sun or fire.
  • bhedati - This means "to separate" or "to divide," indicating a process of differentiation.
  • jeṣat - This translates to "to conquer" or "to overcome," emphasizing victory.
  • svarvatīr - This refers to "the waters" or "the divine waters," symbolizing purity and life.
  • apo - This means "from the waters," indicating a source of nourishment.
  • nabhantām - This translates to "the sky" or "the heavens," representing the vastness of existence.
  • anyake - This means "other" or "different," suggesting diversity.
  • same - This translates to "in the same way" or "similarly," indicating a connection or similarity among different entities.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine connection, positive communication, and the interdependence of all living beings. It highlights the importance of strength, purity, and the power of sacred words. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as fostering harmonious relationships, promoting positive dialogue, and recognizing the interconnectedness of all life forms. By embodying these principles, individuals can cultivate a sense of peace and unity in their communities, ultimately leading to a more compassionate and understanding world.
