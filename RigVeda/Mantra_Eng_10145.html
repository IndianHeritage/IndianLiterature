जरामाणः सम इध्यसे देवभ्यः हव्यवाहन |तं त्वा हवन्त मर्त्याः ||
jaramāṇaḥ sam idhyase devebhyo havyavāhana | taṁ tvā havanta martyāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • jaramāṇaḥ: The one who is aging or growing old.
    • sam idhyase: You are being invoked or called upon.
    • devebhyo: To the gods or divine beings.
    • havyavāhana: The one who carries offerings or sacrifices.
    • taṁ: That (referring to the divine being).
    • tvā: You (referring to the divine being).
    • havanta: Those who offer or invoke.
    • martyāḥ: Mortals or human beings.
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra expresses a call to the divine, acknowledging the inevitability of aging and the need for divine support. It emphasizes the relationship between mortals and the divine, where humans invoke the gods for assistance and blessings as they navigate the challenges of life, including the process of aging.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals are performed to honor the elderly, recognizing their wisdom and the challenges they face as they age. This mantra can be seen as a reminder to seek divine guidance and support during such transitions.
    • In modern life, people often turn to spirituality or prayer when facing difficulties related to aging, such as health issues or loss of loved ones. This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of invoking divine help in these times.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra highlights the connection between humans and the divine, emphasizing the importance of seeking help and guidance from higher powers.
    • It is applicable in various life scenarios, such as during personal crises, health challenges, or when facing the inevitability of aging and mortality.
    • By invoking the divine, individuals can find strength, comfort, and wisdom to navigate the complexities of life.
