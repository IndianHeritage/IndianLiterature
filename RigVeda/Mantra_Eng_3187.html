त्वद अग्ने काव्याऽ तव मनिषास् त्वद उक्ताऽ जायन्ते राध्याणि |त्वद एति द्रविणं वीरपेक्षाऽ इत्ताधिये दाशुषे मार्त्याय ||
tvad agne kāvyā tvan manīṣās tvad ukthā jāyante rādhyāni | tvad eti draviṇaṁ vīrapeśā itthādhiye dāśuṣe martyāya ||
  • tvad agne - "O Agni" or "O Fire": This phrase addresses Agni, the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, fire represents not just physical fire but also the inner fire of passion and creativity.
  • kāvyā - "poetic" or "creative": This word signifies the creative aspect of Agni, suggesting that the energy of fire is also linked to inspiration and artistic expression. For example, a writer or artist may draw inspiration from their inner fire to create meaningful works.
  • tvan manīṣās - "your thoughts" or "your wisdom": This indicates the wisdom associated with Agni, suggesting that the fire god embodies knowledge and insight. In practical terms, this can relate to seeking wisdom in decision-making or problem-solving in everyday life.
  • tvan ukthā - "your hymns" or "your praises": This refers to the hymns and praises offered to Agni, emphasizing the importance of gratitude and recognition of divine forces in our lives. For instance, expressing gratitude for the blessings we receive can enhance our well-being.
  • jāyante rādhyāni - "the beneficial things arise": This phrase suggests that from the energy and wisdom of Agni, beneficial outcomes manifest. In real life, this can be seen in how positive actions and intentions lead to favorable results, such as community service leading to a stronger community.
  • tvad eti draviṇaṁ - "from you comes wealth": This indicates that prosperity and abundance are derived from the divine energy of Agni. In practical terms, this can relate to the idea that hard work and dedication, fueled by passion, lead to financial and personal success.
  • vīrapeśā - "the mighty one": This term highlights the strength and power of Agni, symbolizing the force that drives change and transformation. In real life, this can be likened to the determination and resilience individuals show in overcoming challenges.
  • itthādhiye - "thus, in this way": This phrase suggests a method or approach to harnessing the energy of Agni in our lives. It encourages individuals to adopt practices that align with their inner fire, such as meditation or creative pursuits.
  • dāśuṣe - "for the servant": This indicates the importance of service and humility in the presence of divine energy. In real life, this can be seen in acts of kindness and support for others, which enrich our lives and communities.
  • martyāya - "for the mortal": This emphasizes the human experience and the connection between the divine and the mortal realm. It reminds us that while we are mortal, we can tap into divine energies to enhance our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of Agni as a source of inspiration, wisdom, and prosperity. It teaches us that by recognizing and honoring the divine energies in our lives, we can cultivate creativity, seek knowledge, and manifest beneficial outcomes. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied through practices such as gratitude, service to others, and pursuing our passions with dedication. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine and human experience, encouraging us to harness our inner fire for personal and communal growth.
