यस्य वायोर इव द्रवद भद्रā रातिः सहस्रिणी | सद्यो दानाय मंहत े ||
yasya vāyor iva dravad bhadrā rātiḥ sahasriṇī | sadyo dānāya maṁhate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • vāyor: of the wind
    • iva: like
    • dravad: flowing
    • bhadrā: auspicious
    • rātiḥ: delight or joy
    • sahasriṇī: a thousandfold
    • sadyo: immediately
    • dānāya: for giving
    • maṁhate: to the one who is worthy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine quality that is as powerful and flowing as the wind.
    • The term "bhadrā rātiḥ" suggests a state of auspicious joy or delight that is abundant and fulfilling.
    • The phrase "sahasriṇī" indicates that this joy is not limited but is available in a thousandfold, emphasizing its vastness.
    • The concluding part "sadyo dānāya maṁhate" implies that this joy is immediately available for those who are deserving or worthy of it.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Consider a person who practices kindness and generosity. They often find themselves surrounded by joy and positive energy, akin to the flowing wind that brings life and movement.
    • In a community where people support each other, the collective joy and prosperity can be seen as a manifestation of "bhadrā rātiḥ," where the happiness is abundant and shared among many.
    • In a workplace, when employees feel valued and appreciated, their morale and productivity can soar, reflecting the "sahasriṇī" aspect of joy that is available to all who contribute positively.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of cultivating joy and positivity in our lives and communities.
    • It serves as a reminder that the flow of joy and auspiciousness is available to those who are deserving, encouraging us to act with kindness and generosity.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, community interactions, or professional environments, fostering a spirit of support and appreciation can lead to a more joyful and fulfilling existence.
