यत्रा वदेत एवराḥ परश्च यज्ञन्योः कतरो नउ विबेद |आ शेकुर इति सदहमादं सखायो नक्षन्त यज्ञं का इदं विबोचत ||
yatrā vadete avaraḥ paraś ca yajñanyoḥ kataro nau vi veda | ā śekur it sadhamādaṁ sakhāyo nakṣanta yajñaṁ ka idaṁ vi vocat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yatrā: where
    • vadete: they speak
    • avaraḥ: the inferior
    • paraś: the superior
    • ca: and
    • yajñanyoḥ: of the sacrifices
    • kataro: which one
    • nau: we
    • vi: indeed
    • veda: know
    • ā: to
    • śekur: they have achieved
    • it: this
    • sadhamādaṁ: good fellowship
    • sakhāyo: friends
    • nakṣanta: they have gathered
    • yajñaṁ: the sacrifice
    • ka: who
    • idaṁ: this
    • vi: indeed
    • vocat: has spoken
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects on the nature of knowledge and understanding in the context of sacrifices (yajñas).
    • It poses a question about who truly knows the essence of sacrifices, whether it is the inferior or the superior.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of fellowship and collaboration among friends in achieving a common goal.
    • It suggests that true understanding comes from collective wisdom and shared experiences.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, team projects often require collaboration. The success of a project can depend on how well team members communicate and share their knowledge.
    • In educational environments, group studies can lead to a deeper understanding of subjects, as students learn from each other’s perspectives.
    • In community service, working together towards a common cause can foster a sense of unity and shared purpose, leading to greater impact.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the value of collective knowledge and the importance of collaboration.
    • It teaches us that understanding and wisdom are not solely the domain of the superior but can be enriched through the contributions of all.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages us to seek knowledge through dialogue, cooperation, and shared experiences, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and effective community.
