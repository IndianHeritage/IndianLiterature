परी द्युक्षं सहसः पर्वतावृधं मध्वः सिञ्चन्ति हरम्यस्य सक्षणिम् |आ यस्मिन गावाḥ सुहुताद ūधनि मूर्धञ् छ्रीणन्त्य् अग्रियम् वरिमभिः ||
pari dyukṣaṁ sahasaḥ parvatāvṛdham madhvaḥ siñcanti harmyasya sakṣaṇim | ā yasmin gāvaḥ suhutāda ūdhani mūrdhañ chrīṇanty agriyaṁ varīmabhiḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pari dyukṣaṁ sahasaḥ parvatāvṛdham madhvaḥ siñcanti harmyasya sakṣaṇim | ā yasmin gāvaḥ suhutāda ūdhani mūrdhañ chrīṇanty agriyaṁ varīmabhiḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pari: around, in all directions
    • dyukṣaṁ: shining, illuminating
    • sahasaḥ: with great force or power
    • parvatāvṛdham: surrounded by mountains
    • madhvaḥ: sweet, pleasant
    • siñcanti: they pour, they bestow
    • harmyasya: of the abode, of the home
    • sakṣaṇim: the one who is capable, the one who has strength
    • ā: in, at
    • yasmin: in which
    • gāvaḥ: cows, representing wealth and prosperity
    • suhutāda: well-nourished, healthy
    • ūdhani: in the water, in the flow
    • mūrdhañ: on the head, at the top
    • chrīṇanty: they rejoice, they celebrate
    • agriyaṁ: the best, the foremost
    • varīmabhiḥ: with great abundance, with plenty
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence that illuminates the surroundings, akin to the sun shining brightly in a clear sky.
    • The phrase "pari dyukṣaṁ" suggests a light that radiates in all directions, similar to how a lighthouse guides ships safely to shore.
    • The mention of "sahasaḥ parvatāvṛdham" evokes imagery of majestic mountains, which can be compared to the challenges we face in life that are often surrounded by beauty and strength.
    • "Madhvaḥ siñcanti" indicates the pouring of sweetness, reminiscent of how kindness and love can enrich our lives and the lives of those around us.
    • The reference to "gāvaḥ suhutāda" symbolizes prosperity and abundance, much like a thriving farm where livestock flourish, representing the fruits of hard work and care.
    • Finally, "agriyaṁ varīmabhiḥ" emphasizes the importance of celebrating the best aspects of life, akin to community gatherings where people come together to share joy and abundance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of nature, prosperity, and divine presence in our lives.
    • It teaches us to appreciate the beauty around us, to nurture our relationships, and to celebrate the abundance we have.
    • In real-life scenarios, this can be applied by fostering a sense of community, engaging in acts of kindness, and recognizing the blessings in our lives, much like how festivals bring people together to celebrate shared values and prosperity.
