यं वै सूर्यं स्वर्भानुस तमसाविध्यद आसुरः |अत्रयस् तं अन्व अविन्दन् न ह्य अन्ये अशक्नुवन् ||
yaṁ vai sūryaṁ svarbhānus tamasāvidhyad āsuraḥ | atrayas tam anv avindan nahy a1nye aśaknuvan ||
  • Mantra: "yaṁ vai sūryaṁ svarbhānus tamasāvidhyad āsuraḥ | atrayas tam anv avindan nahy a1nye aśaknuvan ||"
  • Translation: "The Asuras, who were enveloped in darkness, could not reach the radiant Sun, but the Atris were able to find Him."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • yaṁ: who/that
    • vai: indeed
    • sūryaṁ: the Sun
    • svarbhānus: radiant or luminous
    • tamasā: by darkness
    • avidhyat: was enveloped
    • āsuraḥ: the Asuras (demonic beings)
    • atrayas: the Atris (a group of sages)
    • tam: Him (referring to the Sun)
    • anv avindan: found
    • nahy: not
    • a1nye: others
    • aśaknuvan: could reach
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the contrast between the Asuras, who represent ignorance and darkness, and the Atris, who symbolize knowledge and enlightenment.
    • The Sun is often seen as a symbol of truth, knowledge, and spiritual illumination. The Asuras, being enveloped in darkness, are unable to perceive or reach this truth.
    • The Atris, through their wisdom and efforts, are able to find and connect with the Sun, representing the attainment of knowledge and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In our daily lives, this mantra can be applied to the pursuit of knowledge and truth. Just as the Atris sought the Sun, individuals should strive to seek knowledge and enlightenment in their own lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that ignorance (symbolized by the Asuras) can prevent us from achieving our goals and understanding the world around us.
    • In a broader context, this mantra can be seen as a call to overcome ignorance and darkness in society, encouraging individuals to seek education, wisdom, and understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and enlightenment while warning against the perils of ignorance.
    • It encourages individuals to strive for personal growth and understanding, ultimately leading to a more enlightened society.
    • By recognizing the value of knowledge, we can navigate through life's challenges and illuminate the path for ourselves and others.
