तद अस्यानीकम उता चारु नामापीच्यं वर्धते नप्तुर अपाम |यम् इन्दते युवतयः सम इत्त्था हिरण्यवर्णं घृतम् अन्नम् अस्य ||
tad asyānīkam uta cāru nāmāpīcyaṁ vardhate naptur apām | yam indhate yuvatayaḥ sam itthā hiraṇyavarṇaṁ ghṛtam annam asya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • asyānīkam: of this (or this world)
    • uta: or
    • cāru: beautiful
    • nāmāpīcyaṁ: name (or fame) also
    • vardhate: increases
    • naptur: of the descendant
    • apām: of the waters
    • yam: whom
    • indhate: they invoke
    • yuvatayaḥ: young women
    • sam: together
    • itthā: thus
    • hiraṇyavarṇaṁ: golden-colored
    • ghṛtam: clarified butter
    • annam: food
    • asya: of this
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the beauty and prosperity that arises from the blessings of the divine.
    • The phrase "tad asyānīkam" suggests a connection to the earthly realm, indicating that the blessings are tangible and present in our lives.
    • "cāru nāmāpīcyaṁ" emphasizes that not only does beauty exist, but it also grows in name and fame, suggesting that good deeds and virtues lead to recognition.
    • The mention of "naptur apām" refers to the descendants and the waters, symbolizing the nurturing aspect of life, where prosperity flows like water.
    • "yam indhate yuvatayaḥ" indicates that young women invoke this blessing, highlighting the role of femininity and nurturing in the growth of prosperity.
    • The imagery of "hiraṇyavarṇaṁ ghṛtam annam" evokes a sense of richness and abundance, where clarified butter and food symbolize nourishment and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, this mantra can be reflected in the way parents nurture their children, ensuring they grow up in a loving and prosperous environment.
    • In a community, the collective efforts of individuals to support one another can lead to a flourishing society, much like the invocation of blessings by young women for the well-being of all.
    • In a business context, the mantra can be seen in how companies that prioritize ethical practices and community welfare often gain recognition and success, akin to the "cāru nāmāpīcyaṁ" aspect of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of nurturing relationships, both in personal and communal contexts, to foster growth and prosperity.
    • It teaches us that beauty and abundance are not just material but also spiritual, arising from our connections with others and the divine.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious existence, where individuals and communities thrive together.
