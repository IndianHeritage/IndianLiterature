सो अग्न इजे शशमे च मर्तो यस् त आनाṭ समिधा हव्यदातिम् |य आहुतिम् परि वेदाः नमोभिर् विश्वेत स वामाः दधते त्वोतः ||
so agna īje śaśame ca marto yas ta ānaṭ samidhā havyadātim | ya āhutim pari vedā namobhir viśvet sa vāmā dadhate tvotaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • so: that
    • agna: O Fire
    • īje: I invoke
    • śaśame: and I praise
    • ca: and
    • marto: the mortal
    • yas: who
    • ta: that
    • ānaṭ: who is eternal
    • samidhā: kindled
    • havyadātim: the one who offers oblations
    • ya: who
    • āhutim: the offering
    • pari: all around
    • vedā: the Vedas
    • namobhir: with salutations
    • viśvet: all
    • sa: he
    • vāmā: auspicious
    • dadhate: bestows
    • tvotaḥ: O Invoker
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the sacred fire, symbolizing the divine presence and the energy that sustains life.
    • The speaker invokes Agni, acknowledging the connection between the mortal and the eternal, emphasizing the importance of offerings and rituals.
    • It highlights the significance of Vedic knowledge and the collective nature of worship, where all beings are included in the offerings made to the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, Agni is often invoked during ceremonies such as weddings and homas (fire sacrifices), where offerings are made to the fire as a witness and a medium to the divine.
    • The concept of offering and gratitude can be seen in modern practices, such as expressing thanks for food before meals, which reflects the same spirit of acknowledging the source of sustenance.
    • In a broader sense, the mantra encourages individuals to recognize the interconnectedness of life and the importance of community in spiritual practices.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the sacredness of fire and the divine energy that permeates existence.
    • It emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings in maintaining a connection with the divine and the community.
    • In contemporary life, the principles of gratitude, interconnectedness, and respect for the divine can be applied in various contexts, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
