मन्त्र: चत्तो इतश्चत्तामुतः सर्वा भ्रूणान्य आरुषी |अराय्यम् ब्रह्मणस पते तीक्ष्णशृङ्गोदृषन्न इहि ||
catto itaś cattāmutaḥ sarvā bhrūṇāny āruṣī | arāyyam brahmaṇas pate tīkṣṇaśṛṇgodṛṣann ihi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • catto: from here
    • itaś: from this place
    • cattāmutaḥ: all beings
    • sarvā: all
    • bhrūṇāny: the embryos or beings
    • āruṣī: red or bright
    • arāyyam: not to be harmed
    • brahmaṇas: of the divine or the creator
    • pate: lord
    • tīkṣṇaśṛṇgodṛṣann: sharp-eyed or keen observer
    • ihi: here or now
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the sanctity of all forms of life, particularly the unborn or embryos.
    • It calls upon the divine to protect all beings, highlighting the importance of compassion and non-violence.
    • The term "sharp-eyed" suggests the need for vigilance and awareness in our actions towards others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the context of environmental conservation, this mantra can be seen as a call to protect all living beings, including endangered species.
    • In discussions about reproductive rights, it serves as a reminder of the value of life and the ethical considerations surrounding it.
    • In daily interactions, it encourages individuals to practice kindness and empathy towards others, recognizing the shared experience of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its universal message of protection and respect for all forms of life.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from environmental activism to personal relationships, urging us to act with awareness and compassion.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and respectful world.
