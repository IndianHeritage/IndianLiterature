आयाः ह त्यम् मायया वावृद्धानम् मनोजुवाः स्वतवः पर्वतेन |अच्युताः चिद् वीळिताः स्वजो रुजो विदृढाः धृताः धृतशताः विरप्शिन् ||
ayā ha tyam māyayā vāvṛdhānam manojuvā svatavaḥ parvatena | acyutā cid vīḻitā svojo rujo vi dṛḻhā dhṛṣatā virapśin ||
  • ayā ha tyam māyayā: This phrase can be interpreted as "this is indeed the manifestation of illusion." It suggests that the world we perceive is often a product of our own perceptions and illusions.
  • vāvṛdhānam manojuvā svatavaḥ parvatena: This translates to "the mind, which is elevated by the mountain of its own nature." Here, the 'mountain' symbolizes strength and stability, indicating that a strong mind can overcome challenges.
  • acyutā cid vīḻitā: This means "the unchanging consciousness is realized." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing the eternal aspect of our being, which remains unaffected by the transient nature of the world.
  • sujo rujo vi dṛḻhā: This can be understood as "the good and the painful are both seen clearly." It highlights the necessity of understanding both joy and suffering in life, as they are integral to the human experience.
  • dhṛṣatā virapśin: This phrase translates to "with courage, one can overcome obstacles." It underscores the significance of bravery and determination in facing life's challenges.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates profound philosophical insights about the nature of reality, the mind, and the human experience. It teaches us that our perceptions can often be misleading, and that true understanding comes from recognizing the eternal consciousness within us. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, where one learns to navigate through illusions and challenges with a strong and courageous mind. It encourages individuals to embrace both joy and suffering as essential parts of life, fostering resilience and wisdom. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate inner strength and clarity in the face of life's complexities.
