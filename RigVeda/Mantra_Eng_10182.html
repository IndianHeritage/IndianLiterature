वसुं न चित्रमहसं गृणीषे वामं शेवम अतिथिम अद्विषेण्यम् |स रासते शुरुधो विश्वधायसो 'ग्निर होतā गृपतिḥ सुवीर्यम् ||
vasuṁ na citramahasaṁ gṛṇīṣe vāmaṁ śevam atithim adviṣeṇyam | sa rāsate śurudho viśvadhāyaso 'gnir hotā gṛhapatiḥ suvīryam ||
  • Vasuṁ: This word refers to wealth or prosperity. In a real-life context, it can be associated with the abundance of resources, both material and spiritual, that contribute to a fulfilling life.
  • Na citramahasaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "not extraordinary" or "not unusual." It suggests that true wealth and prosperity are often found in the ordinary aspects of life, such as relationships and community.
  • Gṛṇīṣe: This means "I sing" or "I praise." It emphasizes the importance of expressing gratitude and appreciation for the blessings one has, which can enhance one's overall well-being.
  • Vāmaṁ: This translates to "pleasant" or "beautiful." It signifies the importance of beauty and harmony in life, whether in nature, relationships, or personal achievements.
  • Śevam: This word means "the best" or "the most excellent." It encourages individuals to strive for excellence in their endeavors, whether in their careers, personal growth, or contributions to society.
  • Atithim: This refers to "the guest." In many cultures, hospitality is a virtue, and this word highlights the significance of treating guests with respect and kindness, fostering community and connection.
  • Adviṣeṇyam: This means "without enmity" or "without hostility." It promotes the idea of peace and harmony in relationships, urging individuals to resolve conflicts amicably and cultivate understanding.
  • Sa rāsate: This phrase can be interpreted as "he who is established." It suggests stability and strength, which are essential for personal and communal growth.
  • Śurudho: This means "the one who is strong" or "the powerful." It emphasizes the importance of inner strength and resilience in facing life's challenges.
  • Viśvadhāyaso: This translates to "the one who supports all." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the responsibility to support one another in a community.
  • 'Agnir: This word means "fire," which symbolizes transformation, energy, and purification. In real life, it can represent the passion and drive that fuels one's ambitions.
  • Hotā: This means "the one who offers." It highlights the importance of giving and sharing, whether through resources, knowledge, or love.
  • Gṛhapatiḥ: This translates to "the lord of the house." It signifies the role of a leader or caretaker in a family or community, emphasizing responsibility and stewardship.
  • Suvīryam: This means "good strength" or "noble power." It encourages individuals to harness their strengths for positive purposes, contributing to the welfare of society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of living a life filled with gratitude, harmony, and responsibility. It teaches us to appreciate the ordinary, strive for excellence, and foster connections with others. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as in family dynamics, community service, and personal development. By embodying the values expressed in this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more peaceful and prosperous society, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and meaningful life.
