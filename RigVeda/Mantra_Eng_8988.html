स हि क्षेमो हविः यज्ञः श्रुष्टीद अस्य गातुर एति |अग्निं देवाः वाशीमनतम् ||
sa hi kṣemo havir yajñaḥ śruṣṭīd asya gātur eti | agniṁ devā vāśīmantam ||
  • sa hi kṣemo havir yajñaḥ: This phrase can be translated to "Indeed, the offering (havi) of the sacrifice (yajña) brings safety (kṣema)." In this context, it emphasizes the importance of offerings made during rituals and sacrifices, suggesting that they lead to well-being and protection.
  • śruṣṭīd asya gātur eti: This part means "the one who is the creator (śruṣṭi) of this (asya) world (gātur) comes forth." It indicates that the divine presence or energy that creates and sustains the universe is invoked through these rituals.
  • agniṁ devā vāśīmantam: This translates to "the divine (devā) Agni (the fire god) who is the master (vāśīmantam)." Agni is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine, representing purity, transformation, and the energy of life.

  • Real-Life Example: In many cultures, rituals and offerings are made to seek blessings and protection. For instance, during Diwali in India, people light lamps and offer sweets to invoke prosperity and safety in their homes. This aligns with the idea that offerings bring safety and well-being.
  • Significance: The mantra highlights the interconnectedness of human actions (through rituals) and divine responses (through blessings). It suggests that by engaging in acts of devotion and sacrifice, individuals can foster a sense of security and harmony in their lives.
  • Application in Real Life: This mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as in personal rituals, community gatherings, or even in corporate settings where team-building activities are conducted to foster unity and well-being. The essence of making offerings—whether in the form of time, effort, or resources—can lead to positive outcomes and a sense of collective safety.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the belief that through sincere offerings and rituals, individuals can connect with the divine, ensuring their safety and well-being. It serves as a reminder of the importance of gratitude and the role of spirituality in everyday life, encouraging people to engage in practices that promote harmony and protection in their communities.
