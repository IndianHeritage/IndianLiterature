उत न इम मारुतो वृद्धसेनाः स्मद rodasī समनसः सदन्तु |पृषदश्वासो 'वनयो न रथाः ऋशादसो मित्रयुजो न देवाः ||
uta na īm maruto vṛddhasenāḥ smad rodasī samanasaḥ sadantu | pṛṣadaśvāso 'vanayo na rathā riśādaso mitrayujo na devāḥ ||
  • uta - This word can be translated as "and" or "also," indicating a connection or addition.
  • na - This means "not," serving as a negation in the mantra.
  • īm - This can be interpreted as "to us" or "for us," suggesting a plea or request.
  • maruto - Refers to the "Maruts," the storm deities in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and power.
  • vṛddhasenāḥ - This translates to "the great army," indicating a collective force or strength.
  • smad - Meaning "our," it denotes possession or belonging.
  • rodasī - Refers to "the two worlds" or "the sky and the earth," representing the duality of existence.
  • samanasaḥ - This means "in harmony," suggesting unity and cooperation.
  • sadantu - Translates to "may they be," indicating a wish or hope for something to occur.
  • pṛṣadaśvāso - Refers to "the swift horses," symbolizing speed and agility.
  • 'avanayo - This means "the protectors," indicating guardianship or safety.
  • na - Again, this serves as a negation.
  • rathā - Refers to "the chariots," symbolizing movement and progress.
  • riśādaso - This can be interpreted as "those who are weak," indicating vulnerability.
  • mitrayujo - Refers to "the friends," symbolizing camaraderie and support.
  • na - Another negation.
  • devāḥ - This means "the gods," representing divine forces or higher powers.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation for strength, unity, and protection. It expresses a desire for the Maruts, as powerful deities, to come together in harmony and safeguard the two worlds (the sky and the earth). The mention of swift horses and chariots symbolizes the need for speed and progress, while the reference to weakness highlights the importance of support and protection from divine forces.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Teamwork: Just as the Maruts are called upon to unite, individuals in a team can work together harmoniously to achieve common goals.
  • Community Support: The mantra emphasizes the importance of protecting the vulnerable, reminding us to support those in need within our communities.
  • Personal Growth: The call for strength and protection can inspire individuals to seek guidance and support from higher powers or mentors in their personal journeys.

Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of unity, strength, and protection in our lives. It serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our struggles and that divine forces, as well as our communities, can provide the support we need to overcome challenges and progress forward.
