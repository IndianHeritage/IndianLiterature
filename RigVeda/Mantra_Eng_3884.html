त्वं उत्सां ऋतुभिर बद्बधानां अरंह ऊधः पर्वतस्य वज्रिन | अहिं चिद उग्र प्रयुतं शयानं जग्हन्वां इन्द्र तविषीम अधत्ताह ||
tvam utsām̐ ṛtubhir badbadhānām̐ araṁha ūdhaḥ parvatasya vajrin | ahiṁ cid ugra prayutaṁ śayānaṁ jaghanvām̐ indra taviṣīm adhatthāḥ ||
  • tvam utsām̐: This phrase can be translated as "You are the source of strength." In real life, this can be related to individuals who inspire and empower others, such as leaders or mentors who provide guidance and support.
  • ṛtubhir badbadhānām̐: This means "with the seasons of life." It signifies the cyclical nature of existence, akin to how seasons change and affect our lives. For example, just as spring brings new beginnings, challenges in life can also lead to growth and transformation.
  • araṁha ūdhaḥ: This translates to "the one who rises above." It refers to overcoming obstacles and challenges. In a real-life context, this can be seen in individuals who face adversity but rise above it, such as athletes who overcome injuries to achieve their goals.
  • parvatasya vajrin: This means "the one who is like a mountain." Mountains symbolize stability and strength. In life, this can represent individuals who provide a solid foundation for others, such as parents or community leaders who offer unwavering support.
  • ahiṁ cid ugra: This phrase translates to "the fierce one." It can refer to the inner strength and determination that individuals possess when facing challenges. For instance, a person fighting for social justice embodies this fierce spirit.
  • prayutaṁ śayānaṁ: This means "lying in wait." It suggests the idea of being prepared and vigilant. In real life, this can be likened to a student preparing for exams, always ready to seize opportunities when they arise.
  • jaghanvām̐ indra: This translates to "Indra, the mighty." Indra is often seen as a powerful deity in Vedic literature. In a contemporary context, this can symbolize leaders or influential figures who wield significant power and responsibility in society.
  • taviṣīm adhatthāḥ: This means "you have established strength." It signifies the importance of building resilience and fortitude. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who cultivate mental strength through practices like meditation or therapy.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the themes of strength, resilience, and the cyclical nature of life. It serves as a reminder that we all possess the ability to rise above challenges and support one another, much like the changing seasons. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to embrace their inner strength, be vigilant in their pursuits, and provide support to others, fostering a community of empowerment and growth.
