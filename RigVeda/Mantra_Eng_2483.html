वैश्वानर तव धामान्य आ चके येभिः स्वरविद अभवो विचक्षण |जात आऩृणो भुवनानि रोधसी अग्ने ता विश्वा परिभूर असि तमनाः ||
vaiśvānara tava dhāmāny ā cake yebhiḥ svarvid abhavo vicakṣaṇa | jāta āpṛṇo bhuvanāni rodasī agne tā viśvā paribhūr asi tmanā ||
  • Vaiśvānara: This term refers to the universal or cosmic aspect of fire, symbolizing the divine presence that pervades all existence.
  • Tava dhāmāni: This translates to "your abodes" or "your realms," indicating the various forms and manifestations of the divine fire.
  • Ā cake: This phrase means "we have come" or "we have arrived," suggesting a call to the divine for guidance and presence.
  • Yebhiḥ: This means "by whom" or "through which," referring to the means or methods of connection to the divine.
  • Svarvid: This term relates to the celestial or heavenly realms, emphasizing the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • Abhavo: This translates to "become" or "manifest," indicating the process of transformation and realization of the divine within the material world.
  • Vicakṣaṇa: This means "wise" or "perceptive," highlighting the importance of wisdom in understanding and connecting with the divine.
  • Jāta: This term means "born" or "created," referring to the emergence of life and existence from the divine source.
  • Āpṛṇo: This translates to "fills" or "nourishes," indicating the sustaining power of the divine in the universe.
  • Bhuvanāni: This means "worlds" or "realms," referring to the various dimensions of existence that are nourished by the divine.
  • Rodasī: This term refers to the "two worlds" or "heavens and earth," symbolizing the connection between the material and spiritual realms.
  • Agne: This is a direct address to Agni, the fire god, representing the divine energy that sustains life.
  • Tā viśvā: This translates to "all" or "the entirety," emphasizing the all-encompassing nature of the divine presence.
  • Paribhūr asi tmanā: This means "you are the all-encompassing one," indicating the omnipresence and omnipotence of the divine in all aspects of life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of the divine fire, Agni, as a universal force that nourishes and sustains all realms of existence. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and perception in recognizing the divine presence in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to foster a sense of connection with the universe, encouraging individuals to seek wisdom and understanding in their daily lives. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine energy that flows through everything, inspiring us to live harmoniously and with purpose.
