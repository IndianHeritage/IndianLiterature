उप नो देवाः अवसागमन्त्वङ्गिरसां सामभिः स्तूयमानाः |इन्द्र इन्द्रियैः मरुतो मरुद्भिः आदित्यैः नो अदितिः शर्म यंषत ||
upa no devā avasā gamantv aṅgirasāṁ sāmabhiḥ stūyamānāḥ | indra indriyair maruto marudbhir ādityair no aditiḥ śarma yaṁsat ||
  • upa no devā avasā gamantv: This phrase can be translated as "May the gods come near us." It expresses a desire for divine presence and guidance in our lives. In real life, this can be seen in rituals or prayers where individuals seek blessings and support from higher powers, such as during significant life events like weddings or the birth of a child.
  • aṅgirasāṁ sāmabhiḥ stūyamānāḥ: This part means "being praised by the Angirasas." The Angirasas are considered sages or seers in Vedic literature. This suggests that the divine beings are not only present but are also being honored and revered. In contemporary life, this can be likened to moments when we acknowledge and appreciate the contributions of mentors or leaders in our communities.
  • indra indriyair maruto marudbhir: Here, "Indra" refers to the king of gods, and "maruto" refers to the storm gods. This line emphasizes the power and strength of these deities. In practical terms, this can relate to how we harness our inner strength and resilience in the face of challenges, much like how communities come together during natural disasters to support one another.
  • ādityair no aditiḥ śarma yaṁsat: This translates to "may the Adityas grant us protection." The Adityas are solar deities associated with health and prosperity. This line highlights the importance of seeking protection and well-being. In everyday life, this can be reflected in our pursuit of safety and security, whether through community support systems or personal health practices.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound yearning for divine presence, guidance, and protection. It emphasizes the importance of community, reverence for wisdom, and the strength derived from divine forces. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek support from their communities, honor their mentors, and cultivate resilience in the face of adversity. It serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our journeys and that seeking divine guidance can lead to a more fulfilling and protected life.
