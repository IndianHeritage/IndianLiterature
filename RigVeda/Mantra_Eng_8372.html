धर्ता दिवः पवते कृत्व्यो रसो दक्षो देवानाम् अनुमाद्यो नृभिः |हरिः सृजानो अत्यो न सत्वभिर वृथा पाजांसी कृतुते नदिष्वा ||
dhartā divaḥ pavate kṛtvyo raso dakṣo devānām anumādyo nṛbhiḥ | hariḥ sṛjāno atyo na satvabhir vṛthā pājāṁsi kṛṇute nadīṣv ā ||
  • dhartā divaḥ: This phrase translates to "the supporter of the heavens." It signifies a divine force that upholds the universe, much like how gravity keeps planets in orbit.
  • pavate: Meaning "to blow" or "to purify," this word refers to the wind or air, which is essential for life. In real life, we see how fresh air revitalizes our environment and health.
  • kṛtvyo raso: This can be interpreted as "the essence of actions." It emphasizes the importance of intention behind actions, similar to how a chef's passion influences the taste of a dish.
  • dakṣo devānām: Translated as "skilled among the gods," this phrase highlights the idea of expertise and wisdom. In modern life, we often seek skilled professionals, such as doctors or engineers, to guide us.
  • anumādyo nṛbhiḥ: This means "approved by humans." It suggests that divine actions must resonate with human values, akin to how ethical practices in business are crucial for success.
  • hariḥ sṛjāno: This translates to "the creator of all." It reflects the idea of creation and innovation, similar to how artists and inventors bring new ideas to life.
  • atyona satvabhir: Meaning "beyond the qualities," this phrase indicates a transcendence of ordinary attributes, much like how a great leader inspires beyond their personal traits.
  • vṛthā pājāṁsi: This translates to "the rituals that are in vain." It serves as a reminder that actions without purpose or sincerity are ineffective, similar to how empty gestures in relationships can lead to misunderstandings.
  • kṛṇute nadīṣv ā: This means "they create in the rivers." It symbolizes the flow of life and the interconnectedness of all beings, much like how rivers nourish ecosystems and communities.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of divine support, the importance of intention in actions, and the interconnectedness of life. It teaches us that our actions should align with higher values and that true creation comes from a place of skill and purpose. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership, where ethical decision-making and genuine intentions lead to sustainable success, or in personal relationships, where sincerity and understanding foster deeper connections. Ultimately, the mantra encourages us to live with awareness and purpose, recognizing the divine in our everyday actions.
