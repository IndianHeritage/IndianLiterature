प्र पीपय वृषभ जिन्व वाजान अग्ने त्वं रोधसी नः सुदोगहे |देवेभिर देव सुरुचाऽ रुचानो मा नो मर्तस्य दुरमतीः परि स्थात् ||
pra pīpaya vṛṣabha jinva vājān agne tvaṁ rodasī naḥ sudoghe | devebhir deva surucā rucāno mā no martasya durmatiḥ pari ṣṭhāt ||
  • pra pīpaya: This phrase can be interpreted as "to fill or to nourish." It signifies the act of providing sustenance or support.
  • vṛṣabha: This word means "bull" or "strong one," often symbolizing strength, power, and virility. In a real-life context, it can represent a leader or a strong figure in a community.
  • jinva: This term translates to "conquer" or "overcome." It emphasizes the importance of triumphing over challenges and adversities.
  • vājān: This word means "wealth" or "prosperity." It highlights the significance of abundance and success in life.
  • agne: Referring to "Agni," the fire god, this term symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine spark within us.
  • tvaṁ: This means "you," addressing the divine or a higher power directly.
  • rodasī: This word translates to "the two worlds" or "the heavens and the earth," indicating the connection between the spiritual and the material realms.
  • naḥ: Meaning "our," it signifies a collective identity or community.
  • sudoghe: This term can be interpreted as "well-being" or "prosperity," emphasizing the desire for a flourishing life.
  • devebhir: This means "by the gods," indicating divine influence or support.
  • deva: Referring to "god" or "divine," it emphasizes the spiritual aspect of existence.
  • surucā: This translates to "well-formed" or "beautiful," suggesting the importance of aesthetics and harmony in life.
  • rucāno: Meaning "radiant" or "brilliant," it signifies the pursuit of enlightenment and knowledge.
  • mā no: This phrase means "may not," indicating a plea or a request.
  • martasya: This word translates to "of mortals," referring to human beings and their experiences.
  • durmatiḥ: This term means "bad thoughts" or "ill intentions," highlighting the need to overcome negativity.
  • pari ṣṭhāt: This translates to "to stand around" or "to protect," indicating a desire for safeguarding and support.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of divine support in overcoming challenges and achieving prosperity. It calls upon Agni, the fire god, to fill our lives with strength, wealth, and well-being while protecting us from negative thoughts and influences. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of struggle, encouraging individuals to seek strength and guidance from higher powers, fostering a sense of community, and promoting positive intentions. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the spiritual and material worlds, urging us to strive for balance and harmony in our lives.
