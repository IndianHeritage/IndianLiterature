त्वं अग्नि राजा वरुणो धृतव्रतस् त्वं मित्रो भवसि दसम ईड्यः |त्वं आर्यमा सत्पतिर यस्य संभुजं त्वं अंशो विदथे देव भागयुः ||
tvam agne rājā varuṇo dhṛtavratas tvam mitro bhavasi dasma īḍyaḥ | tvam aryamā satpatir yasya sambhujaṁ tvam aṁśo vidathe deva bhājayuḥ ||
  • tvam agne - "You, O Agni" - This refers to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a divine messenger and a vital force in Vedic rituals. In real life, fire symbolizes transformation and purification, much like how challenges can lead to personal growth.
  • rājā varuṇo - "King Varuna" - Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water. He represents the moral and ethical aspects of life. In our daily lives, this can be seen in the importance of integrity and justice in leadership roles.
  • dhṛtavratas - "One who holds firm vows" - This emphasizes the importance of commitment and discipline. For example, a person who adheres to a fitness regimen demonstrates dhṛtavratas by maintaining their vows to health and well-being.
  • tvam mitro bhavasi - "You become a friend" - This highlights the significance of friendship and support in human relationships. In real life, being a reliable friend can foster strong bonds and community support.
  • dasma īḍyaḥ - "Therefore, you are worthy of praise" - This suggests that those who embody these qualities deserve recognition. In a workplace, employees who demonstrate dedication and integrity are often celebrated for their contributions.
  • tvam aryamā - "You, O Aryaman" - Aryaman is associated with social order and relationships. This can be related to the importance of social connections and networks in achieving personal and professional goals.
  • satpatir - "The one who leads on the right path" - This refers to guidance and mentorship. In real life, mentors in various fields help individuals navigate their careers and personal lives, ensuring they stay on the right path.
  • yasya sambhujaṁ - "Whose sustenance is shared" - This emphasizes the importance of sharing and community. For instance, communal meals or shared resources in a neighborhood foster a sense of belonging and cooperation.
  • tvam aṁśo vidathe - "You are a part of the assembly" - This signifies participation and contribution to a group. In real life, being an active member of a team or community enhances collaboration and collective success.
  • deva bhājayuḥ - "Those who partake in the divine" - This suggests that engaging with the divine or higher principles leads to fulfillment. In practice, this can be seen in individuals who find purpose through spirituality or altruistic endeavors.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, friendship, commitment, and community. It serves as a reminder of the values that guide us in our personal and professional lives. By embodying these principles, individuals can foster a harmonious and supportive environment, leading to collective growth and well-being. In today's world, where individualism often prevails, this mantra encourages us to recognize the importance of our connections and responsibilities towards one another, ultimately contributing to a more balanced and fulfilling life.
