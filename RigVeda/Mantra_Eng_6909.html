प्रति त्वा शवसी वदद गिराव अप्सो न योधिषत् |यस् ते शत्रुत्वम् आचके ||
prati tvā śavasī vadad girāv apso na yodhiṣat | yas te śatrutvam ācake ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prati: towards
    • tvā: you
    • śavasī: the one who is powerful or strong
    • vadad: speak or utter
    • girāv: words or speech
    • apso: of the waters or the divine
    • na: not
    • yodhiṣat: to fight or engage in battle
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • śatrutvam: enmity or hostility
    • ācake: has declared or revealed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra can be interpreted as a call to the powerful entity (possibly a deity) to speak words that will guide and protect against adversaries.
    • It emphasizes the importance of communication and the power of words in overcoming challenges and hostility.
    • The phrase "not to fight" suggests a preference for resolution through dialogue rather than conflict.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, when conflicts arise, it is often more effective to engage in open communication rather than resorting to confrontational tactics. This mantra encourages dialogue to resolve issues.
    • In personal relationships, addressing misunderstandings through conversation can prevent escalation into arguments, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In community disputes, leaders who advocate for peaceful discussions rather than aggressive actions embody the spirit of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its advocacy for the power of words and communication in resolving conflicts.
    • It teaches that strength is not only in physical prowess but also in the ability to articulate thoughts and feelings effectively.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more harmonious relationships, effective conflict resolution, and a deeper understanding among individuals and communities.
