अभि कण्वा अनूषतापो न प्रवता यतीः |इन्द्रं वनन्वती मतिḥ ||
abhi kaṇvā anūṣatāpo na pravatā yatīḥ | indraṁ vananvatī matiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • kaṇvā: the wise or learned
    • anūṣatāpo: the one who is not disturbed or agitated
    • na: not
    • pravatā: flowing or moving
    • yatīḥ: the ascetics or those who practice self-discipline
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods
    • vananvatī: possessing wisdom or knowledge
    • matiḥ: intellect or understanding
  • Detailed Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the qualities of wise individuals who remain undisturbed and focused.
    • The term abhi kaṇvā suggests that wisdom is directed towards a higher purpose or goal.
    • Anūṣatāpo indicates a state of calmness and composure, essential for achieving clarity in thought.
    • Na pravatā emphasizes the importance of not being swayed by external circumstances.
    • Yatīḥ refers to those who practice self-discipline, highlighting the need for inner strength.
    • Indraṁ symbolizes the ultimate goal or aspiration, representing power and authority.
    • Vananvatī matiḥ suggests that true wisdom is rooted in understanding and knowledge.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who remains calm under pressure and makes decisions based on wisdom rather than emotions exemplifies the qualities described in this mantra.
    • A student preparing for exams who practices self-discipline and focuses on their studies without being distracted by social media or peer pressure embodies the essence of yatīḥ.
    • In personal relationships, someone who approaches conflicts with a calm and wise mindset, seeking understanding rather than reacting impulsively, reflects the teachings of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom, self-discipline, and composure in achieving one's goals.
    • It teaches us that true strength lies in maintaining our intellect and understanding, even in challenging situations.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to better decision-making, improved relationships, and personal growth.
    • Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate inner peace and wisdom as we navigate through life's complexities.
