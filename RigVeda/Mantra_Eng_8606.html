अभि वायुं वीत्य अर्षा गृणानोऽभि मित्रावरुणा पूयमानः |अभी नरं धीजनं रथेष्टाम अभिंद्रं वृषणं वज्रबाहुम् ||
abhi vāyuṁ vīty arṣā gṛṇāno3 'bhi mitrāvaruṇā pūyamānaḥ | abhī naraṁ dhījavanaṁ ratheṣṭhām abhīndraṁ vṛṣaṇaṁ vajrabāhum ||
  • abhi vāyuṁ: This phrase can be translated as "towards the wind" or "to the vital air." In a real-life context, this can symbolize the importance of breath and life force, akin to how we rely on air for survival.
  • vīty arṣā: This can be interpreted as "wealth and prosperity." In modern terms, it reflects the pursuit of success and abundance in life, whether it be financial, emotional, or spiritual.
  • gṛṇāno: This means "singing" or "chanting." It emphasizes the power of sound and expression, similar to how music and poetry can uplift our spirits and connect us with others.
  • mitrāvaruṇā: This refers to "Mitra and Varuna," deities representing friendship and cosmic order. In everyday life, it highlights the importance of relationships and harmony in our interactions.
  • pūyamānaḥ: This translates to "being filled" or "being nourished." It signifies the need for sustenance, both physical and emotional, akin to how we seek nourishment from food, love, and support.
  • abhī naraṁ: This means "towards the man" or "to the human." It emphasizes the significance of humanity and our collective journey, reminding us of our shared experiences and responsibilities.
  • dhījavanaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the wise one" or "the intelligent." It underscores the value of wisdom and knowledge in navigating life's challenges, similar to how we seek guidance from mentors and educators.
  • ratheṣṭhām: This translates to "on the chariot." In a metaphorical sense, it represents the journey of life, where we are all passengers on our own chariots, steering our destinies.
  • abhīndraṁ: This means "towards the powerful" or "to the strong." It signifies the aspiration for strength and resilience, akin to how we admire and strive to embody qualities of courage and determination.
  • vṛṣaṇaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the one who brings prosperity." It reflects the desire for abundance and success, similar to how we work hard to achieve our goals and dreams.
  • vajrabāhum: This translates to "the one with a thunderbolt arm." It symbolizes strength and protection, akin to how we seek safety and security in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic view of life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of breath, prosperity, relationships, wisdom, and strength. It serves as a reminder to seek balance and harmony in our pursuits, whether in personal relationships, professional endeavors, or spiritual growth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate resilience, nurture their connections, and strive for wisdom, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
