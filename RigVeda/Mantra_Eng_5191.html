पृष्टो दिवि धाय्य अग्निः पृथिव्यां नेताऽसिन्धूनां वृषभः स्तियानाम् |स मानुषीर अभि विश्वो वि भाति वैश्वानरो वावृद्धानो वरेंन ||
pṛṣṭo divi dhāyy agniḥ pṛthivyāṁ netā sindhūnāṁ vṛṣabhaḥ stiyānām | sa mānuṣīr abhi viśo vi bhāti vaiśvānaro vāvṛdhāno vareṇa ||
  • pṛṣṭo divi dhāyy agniḥ: This phrase translates to "the fire that is invoked in the heavens." Here, "Agni" represents the divine fire, a symbol of purity and transformation. In real life, fire is often associated with warmth, light, and energy, much like the inspiration we seek in our lives.
  • pṛthivyāṁ netā sindhūnāṁ: This means "the leader of the rivers on the earth." Rivers are vital for sustenance and life, symbolizing the flow of knowledge and resources. For example, just as rivers nourish the land, knowledge and wisdom nourish our minds and communities.
  • vṛṣabhaḥ stiyānām: This translates to "the bull of strength." The bull is often seen as a symbol of power and fertility. In a real-life context, this can be likened to the strength of character and resilience that individuals exhibit in overcoming challenges.
  • sa mānuṣīr abhi viśo vi bhāti: This means "he shines among men." It emphasizes the idea of enlightenment and the pursuit of knowledge. In today's world, this can be seen in leaders and thinkers who inspire others through their actions and wisdom.
  • vaiśvānaro vāvṛdhāno vareṇa: This translates to "the universal fire that grows with blessings." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing our relationships and communities. In practical terms, this can be reflected in acts of kindness and cooperation that foster unity and growth.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine energy, leadership, strength, enlightenment, and universal connection. Its significance lies in the reminder that we are all part of a larger whole, and our actions can either contribute to or detract from the collective well-being. In real life, this mantra encourages us to seek knowledge, act with integrity, and foster connections that uplift ourselves and others. By embodying these principles, we can create a harmonious and thriving community, much like the rivers that nourish the earth.
