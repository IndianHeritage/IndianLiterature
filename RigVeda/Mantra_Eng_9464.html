कथा कविस तुवीरवान कया गिरा बृहस्पतिर वावृद्धते सुवृक्तिभिः |अज एकपात सुहवेभिर ऋक्वभिर अहिः श्रिणोतु बुध्न्योऽ हविमनि ||
kathā kavis tuvīravān kayā girā bṛhaspatir vāvṛdhate suvṛktibhiḥ | aja ekapāt suhavebhir ṛkvabhir ahiḥ śṛṇotu budhnyo3 havīmani ||
  • kathā kavis tuvīravān: This phrase can be translated as "how the poet, the mighty one," indicating the power of creativity and expression in poetry. In real life, this can be seen in the works of great poets like Rumi or Shakespeare, whose words have the ability to inspire and evoke deep emotions.
  • kayā girā bṛhaspatir vāvṛdhate: This translates to "by what speech does the great sage Brihaspati grow." Brihaspati is often associated with wisdom and knowledge. This can be related to how effective communication and wisdom can lead to personal and communal growth, much like how leaders or mentors guide others through their words.
  • suvṛktibhiḥ: Meaning "with good utterances," this emphasizes the importance of positive and constructive speech. In everyday life, this can be reflected in how encouraging words can uplift someone's spirit, similar to how a teacher's praise can motivate a student.
  • aja ekapāt suhavebhir: This can be interpreted as "the one who is unborn, with a single path." This suggests a focus on a singular, true path in life. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who pursue their passions relentlessly, like an athlete training for the Olympics, dedicated to their goal.
  • ṛkvabhir ahiḥ: This translates to "the serpent with the hymns." The serpent can symbolize wisdom and transformation. In real life, this can be likened to how challenges (the serpent) can lead to personal growth and enlightenment when faced with the right mindset (the hymns).
  • śṛṇotu budhnyo3 havīmani: This means "may the wise hear the offerings." It emphasizes the importance of listening and understanding. In a practical sense, this can be seen in how active listening in conversations can lead to better relationships and understanding among people.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, creativity, and the power of words. It signifies that through positive speech and wisdom, individuals can grow and transform. In real life, this mantra is applicable in various scenarios such as education, leadership, and personal development, where the right words and understanding can lead to significant change and growth. It encourages us to be mindful of our speech and to seek wisdom in our interactions, ultimately fostering a more harmonious and enlightened society.
