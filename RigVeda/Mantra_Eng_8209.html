हिन्वन्ति सूरं उषरयः स्वसारो जामयस् पतिम् |महाम इन्दुम महीयुवः ||
hinvanti sūram usrayaḥ svasāro jāmayas patim | mahām indum mahīyuvaḥ ||
  • hinvanti sūram usrayaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "the brave ones invoke the strength." Here, "hinvanti" means to invoke or call upon, "sūram" refers to strength or valor, and "usrayaḥ" signifies the brave or courageous. In real life, this can be seen in situations where individuals or groups come together to face challenges, such as a community rallying to support a cause or a team uniting to overcome obstacles in sports.
  • svasāro jāmayas patim: This part translates to "the one who is the lord of the family." "Svasāro" refers to family or household, "jāmayas" means to be born or to arise, and "patim" signifies lord or master. This can be related to the role of a leader or a caretaker in a family or community, emphasizing the importance of responsibility and guidance in nurturing relationships. For example, a parent or guardian who provides for and protects their family embodies this concept.
  • mahām indum mahīyuvaḥ: This phrase translates to "the great moon, the one who is powerful." "Mahām" means great, "indum" refers to the moon, and "mahīyuvaḥ" signifies someone who is powerful or influential. The moon is often associated with calmness, reflection, and guidance. In real life, this can be seen in leaders or mentors who provide wisdom and direction, much like how the moon guides travelers at night.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of courage, leadership, and guidance in both personal and communal contexts. It highlights the need for individuals to invoke their inner strength and take on the role of a leader within their families and communities.
  • Real-life Application: The principles outlined in this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in workplaces where teamwork and leadership are crucial for success, or in social movements where collective strength is needed to bring about change. It serves as a reminder that invoking courage and taking responsibility can lead to positive outcomes.
