तं त्वा सहस्रचक्षसम अथो सहस्रभर्णसम |अति वारम् अपाविषुः ||
taṁ tvā sahasracakṣasam atho sahasrabharṇasam | ati vāram apāviṣuḥ ||
  • taṁ tvā: This phrase translates to "to you" or "that you." It signifies a direct address to a divine entity or a higher power, indicating a relationship of devotion and reverence.
  • sahasracakṣasam: This term means "of the thousand-eyed one." It refers to a deity or divine being who possesses immense perception and awareness, symbolizing the ability to see and understand everything in the universe.
  • atho: This word means "then" or "therefore," indicating a transition or a conclusion drawn from the previous statements.
  • sahasrabharṇasam: This translates to "of the thousand-bright ones." It suggests a being or force that radiates brilliance and enlightenment, representing knowledge, wisdom, and clarity.
  • ati vāram: This phrase means "beyond the limits" or "exceeding boundaries." It implies transcending ordinary experiences or limitations, reaching for something greater.
  • apāviṣuḥ: This term can be interpreted as "we have been protected" or "we have been saved." It conveys a sense of safety and divine intervention, suggesting that the speaker seeks refuge in the divine presence.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the relationship between the devotee and the divine, highlighting the attributes of the divine being as all-seeing and all-knowing. It reflects a deep sense of trust and reliance on the divine for protection and guidance.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be invoked during times of uncertainty or fear. For instance, when facing challenges at work or in personal relationships, one might recite this mantra to seek clarity and protection. It serves as a reminder that there is a higher power watching over and guiding us through difficult times.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and protection. It encourages individuals to look beyond their immediate circumstances and trust in a greater wisdom. By acknowledging the presence of a higher power, one can find solace and strength in the face of adversity, making it a powerful tool for spiritual growth and resilience.
