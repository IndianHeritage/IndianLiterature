इमा रुद्राय तवसे कपर्दिने क्षयद्वीराय प्र भरामहे मतीः |यथा शम असद द्विपदे चतुष्पदे विश्वं पुष्टं ग्रामे अस्मिन्न अनातुरम् ||
imā rudrāya tavase kapardine kṣayadvīrāya pra bharāmahe matīḥ | yathā śam asad dvipade catuṣpade viśvam puṣṭaṁ grāme asminn anāturam ||
  • imā rudrāya: This phrase refers to invoking Rudra, a significant deity in the Rigveda associated with storms, wind, and the hunt. Rudra represents both destruction and healing, embodying the duality of nature.
  • tavase kapardine: "Tavase" means "to you" and "kapardine" refers to the one with matted hair, a common depiction of Rudra. This highlights the ascetic nature of the deity, emphasizing the importance of discipline and control over one's desires.
  • kṣayadvīrāya: This term translates to "to the one who destroys the brave." It suggests that Rudra has the power to challenge and overcome even the strongest warriors, reminding us of the transient nature of strength and valor.
  • pra bharāmahe matīḥ: This means "we offer our thoughts." It signifies the act of dedicating our intentions and mental energies to Rudra, seeking guidance and wisdom in our endeavors.
  • yathā śam asad: "Yathā" means "as," and "śam asad" refers to peace and tranquility. This part of the mantra expresses a desire for peace in our lives, akin to the calm after a storm.
  • dvipade catuṣpade: This phrase translates to "among the two-legged and four-legged." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all living beings, highlighting the importance of harmony between humans and animals.
  • viśvam puṣṭaṁ: "Viśvam" means "the whole" and "puṣṭaṁ" refers to nourishment or abundance. This suggests a vision of a thriving community where all beings are well-fed and cared for.
  • grāme asminn anāturam: This translates to "in this village, without suffering." It emphasizes the aspiration for a community free from pain and hardship, where everyone can live in peace and prosperity.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound prayer for peace, strength, and harmony within a community. It acknowledges the dual nature of existence, recognizing both the destructive and nurturing aspects of life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in community-building efforts, conflict resolution, and fostering a sense of unity among diverse groups. By invoking the essence of Rudra, individuals can seek guidance in overcoming challenges and promoting well-being for all, ultimately striving for a balanced and prosperous society.
