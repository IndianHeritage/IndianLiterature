पवित्रवन्तः परि वाचं आसते पितैषाम् प्रत्नो अभि रक्षति व्रतम् |महः समुद्रं वरुणस तिरो दधे धीराः इच्छे कुरु धरणेषु आरभम् ||
pavitravantaḥ pari vācam āsate pitaiṣām pratno abhi rakṣati vratam | mahaḥ samudraṁ varuṇas tiro dadhe dhīrā ic chekur dharuṇeṣv ārabham ||
  • pavitravantaḥ: This term refers to those who are pure or sanctified. In a real-life context, this can be related to individuals who maintain moral integrity and ethical standards in their actions.
  • pari vācam āsate: This phrase means "they speak in a complete manner." It emphasizes the importance of communication that is truthful and comprehensive. For example, in a workplace, clear and honest communication can lead to better teamwork and understanding among colleagues.
  • pitaiṣām: This word translates to "of the fathers" or "ancestors." It signifies respect for one's lineage and heritage. In modern life, this can be seen in the way families honor their traditions and values passed down through generations.
  • pratno abhi rakṣati vratam: This means "protects the vow." It highlights the significance of commitment and dedication to one's promises. For instance, a person who commits to a cause, such as environmental conservation, embodies this principle by actively working towards it.
  • mahaḥ samudraṁ: This translates to "the great ocean." The ocean symbolizes vastness and depth, representing the challenges and opportunities in life. Just as the ocean is essential for life, so too are the challenges we face that help us grow.
  • varuṇas tiro dadhe: This phrase means "Varuna (the deity of water) places (or creates) a barrier." It suggests the idea of protection and boundaries. In real life, this can relate to setting personal boundaries to maintain mental health and well-being.
  • dhīrā ic chekur: This means "the wise ones desire." It emphasizes the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom. In today's world, lifelong learning and the quest for knowledge are crucial for personal and professional development.
  • dharuṇeṣv ārabham: This translates to "beginning in the firm." It signifies the importance of a strong foundation in any endeavor. For example, starting a business requires a solid plan and groundwork to ensure success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of purity, communication, respect for heritage, commitment, the vastness of life's challenges, the importance of boundaries, the pursuit of wisdom, and the necessity of a strong foundation. In real life, these principles can guide individuals in their personal and professional lives, encouraging them to act with integrity, communicate effectively, honor their roots, and strive for continuous growth. By embodying these values, one can navigate the complexities of life with purpose and clarity.
