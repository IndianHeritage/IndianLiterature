यो अग्निः क्रव्यात् प्रविवेश वो गृहम इमं पश्यन्न इतरं जातवेदसम् |तं हरामि पितृयज्ञाय देवं स घर्मम् इन्बात् परमेषु सधस्थे ||
yo agniḥ kravyāt praviveśa vo gṛham imam paśyann itaraṁ jātavedasam | taṁ harāmi pitṛyajñāya devaṁ sa gharmam invāt parame sadhasthe ||
  • Mantra: "yo agniḥ kravyāt praviveśa vo gṛham imam paśyann itaraṁ jātavedasam | taṁ harāmi pitṛyajñāya devaṁ sa gharmam invāt parame sadhasthe ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • agniḥ: fire
    • kravyāt: from the offerings
    • praviveśa: has entered
    • vo: your
    • gṛham: home
    • imam: this
    • paśyann: seeing
    • itaraṁ: other
    • jātavedasam: the one who knows all births (a name for Agni)
    • taṁ: him
    • harāmi: I take away
    • pitṛyajñāya: for the ancestral offerings
    • devaṁ: the divine
    • sa: he
    • gharmam: warmth
    • invāt: may he come
    • parame: to the highest
    • sadhasthe: in the place of well-being
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the sacred fire, which is central to Vedic rituals and symbolizes purity and transformation.
    • The phrase "yo agniḥ kravyāt" suggests that Agni has entered the home, indicating the presence of divine energy and blessings.
    • By saying "paśyann itaraṁ jātavedasam," it acknowledges Agni as the one who perceives all aspects of existence, emphasizing his omniscience.
    • The act of "harāmi pitṛyajñāya" signifies the importance of honoring ancestors through offerings, which is a common practice in many cultures, such as the Day of the Dead in Mexico or the Obon festival in Japan.
    • The concluding part "sa gharmam invāt parame sadhasthe" expresses a desire for Agni to bring warmth and well-being, reflecting a universal human need for comfort and security.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of fire as a purifying and transformative force in both spiritual and everyday life.
    • It serves as a reminder of the connection between the living and the ancestors, emphasizing the importance of remembrance and gratitude.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable during rituals, family gatherings, or moments of reflection, where one seeks blessings for warmth, safety, and connection with loved ones.
    • Overall, it encapsulates the essence of reverence for the divine and the importance of community and familial bonds.
