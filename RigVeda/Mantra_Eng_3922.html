सा आगमद इन्द्रो यो वसूनां चिकेटद दातुं दामनो रयीणाम् | धन्वाचरो न वंसगस तृषाणश चकमानः पिबतु दुग्धम् अंशुम् ||
sa ā gamad indro yo vasūnāṁ ciketad dātuṁ dāmano rayīṇām | dhanvacaro na vaṁsagas tṛṣāṇaś cakamānaḥ pibatu dugdham aṁśum ||
  • sa ā gamad indro: This phrase refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature, who is often associated with strength and power. "sa ā gamad" translates to "he has come" or "he has arrived," indicating the presence of divine energy or assistance.
  • yo vasūnāṁ ciketad: "yo" means "who," and "vasūnāṁ" refers to the "Vasu," which are the deities of abundance and prosperity. "ciketad" means "to think" or "to consider." This part suggests that Indra is contemplating the needs of the Vasus, emphasizing the importance of divine consideration for prosperity.
  • dātuṁ dāmano rayīṇām: "dātuṁ" means "to give," and "dāmano" refers to "the giver." "rayīṇām" means "of wealth" or "of riches." This indicates that Indra is ready to bestow wealth and abundance upon those who are deserving.
  • dhanvacaro na vaṁsagas: "dhanvacaro" refers to "the one who wields the bow," symbolizing a warrior or protector. "na vaṁsagas" means "not of the lineage" or "not of the family." This suggests that true strength and protection come from one's actions rather than lineage or birth.
  • tṛṣāṇaś cakamānaḥ: "tṛṣāṇaś" means "thirst" or "desire," and "cakamānaḥ" means "having desired." This indicates a yearning for fulfillment, whether it be spiritual, material, or emotional.
  • pibatu dugdham aṁśum: "pibatu" means "may he drink," "dugdham" refers to "milk," and "aṁśum" means "the sun" or "light." This symbolizes nourishment and enlightenment, suggesting that one should seek sustenance from the divine light.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine intervention, the importance of wealth, and the pursuit of fulfillment. It signifies that true strength comes from one's actions and intentions rather than mere lineage. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership roles where one must act for the greater good, or in personal endeavors where one seeks to achieve goals through hard work and determination. The mantra encourages individuals to seek divine guidance and nourishment, both materially and spiritually, fostering a sense of abundance and prosperity in their lives.
