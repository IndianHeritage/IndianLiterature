अपूर्व्या पुरुतमान्य अस्मै महे वीराय तवसे तुराय |विरप्शिने वज्रीणि शान्तमानी वचांस्य आसā स्थविराय तक्षम् ||
apūrvyā purutamāny asmai mahe vīrāya tavase turāya | virapśine vajriṇe śaṁtamāni vacāṁsy āsā sthavirāya takṣam ||
  • apūrvyā: This word can be translated as "unprecedented" or "unique." It signifies something that has never been seen or experienced before.
  • purutamāny: This term means "most excellent" or "supreme." It refers to the highest quality or the best among all.
  • asmai: This word translates to "to him" or "for him," indicating a direction of reverence or offering.
  • mahe: Meaning "great" or "mighty," it emphasizes the power and significance of the subject being addressed.
  • vīrāya: This translates to "to the hero" or "to the brave one," highlighting qualities of courage and valor.
  • tavase: This word means "to your strength" or "to your power," acknowledging the strength of the subject.
  • turāya: This can be interpreted as "to the swift" or "to the one who moves quickly," suggesting agility and responsiveness.
  • virapśine: This term means "to the one who is powerful" or "to the strong one," reinforcing the theme of strength.
  • vajriṇe: This translates to "to the one who wields the thunderbolt," symbolizing immense power and authority.
  • śaṁtamāni: This word means "peaceful" or "calm," indicating a state of tranquility and harmony.
  • vacāṁsy: This translates to "words" or "speech," suggesting the importance of communication and expression.
  • āsā: This word means "hope" or "expectation," indicating a sense of aspiration or desire.
  • sthavirāya: This translates to "to the elder" or "to the wise one," acknowledging wisdom and experience.
  • takṣam: This word means "capable" or "skilled," emphasizing the ability to achieve or create.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to a powerful and unique entity, acknowledging its strength, wisdom, and the peaceful nature of its presence. It emphasizes the importance of courage, agility, and effective communication in achieving one's goals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In leadership, where a leader must embody the qualities of strength, wisdom, and agility to guide their team effectively.
  • In personal development, where individuals strive to cultivate their unique strengths and capabilities while maintaining a peaceful and hopeful outlook.
  • In conflict resolution, where effective communication and a calm demeanor can lead to peaceful outcomes.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for recognizing and harnessing one's unique strengths and capabilities while fostering peace and effective communication. It serves as a reminder that true power comes from a combination of courage, wisdom, and the ability to adapt swiftly to changing circumstances. In everyday life, embodying these qualities can lead to personal growth, effective leadership, and harmonious relationships.
