मक्षू न वह्निः प्रजायाः उपब्धिर अग्निं न नग्न उप सीदद ऊधः |सन्तिध्मं सन्तोत वाजं स धर्ता जजñe सहसा यवीयुत् ||
makṣū na vahniḥ prajāyā upabdir agniṁ na nagna upa sīdad ūdhaḥ | sanitedhmaṁ sanitota vājaṁ sa dhartā jajñe sahasā yavīyut ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • makṣū: not to be harmed
    • na: not
    • vahniḥ: fire
    • prajāyā: of progeny or creation
    • upabdir: to be approached or invoked
    • agniṁ: the fire or divine energy
    • na: not
    • nagna: naked or exposed
    • upa: towards
    • sīdad: to sit or settle
    • ūdah: to rise or elevate
    • sanitedhmaṁ: well-prepared or well-cooked
    • sanitota: well-placed or well-structured
    • vājaṁ: strength or vigor
    • sa: he or that
    • dhartā: the supporter or sustainer
    • jajñe: was born or came into being
    • sahasā: with great force or power
    • yavīyut: the best or most excellent
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine fire (Agni) as a protector and sustainer of life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of fire in creation and sustenance, suggesting that it should not be harmed or left exposed.
    • The mantra also highlights the need for preparation and structure in life, symbolized by the well-cooked offerings to Agni.
    • It suggests that through proper invocation and respect for the divine, one can attain strength and excellence in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In cooking, the process of preparing food with fire symbolizes the transformation of raw ingredients into nourishing meals, reflecting the idea of 'sanitedhmaṁ' (well-prepared).
    • In personal development, the mantra can be seen as a reminder to cultivate one's inner strength and character, ensuring that one is not left 'nagna' (exposed) in the face of challenges.
    • In community building, the invocation of Agni can represent the collective efforts of individuals coming together to support and uplift one another, creating a strong foundation for growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the vital role of fire (Agni) as a source of energy, transformation, and sustenance in life.
    • It teaches us the importance of preparation, respect for the divine, and the pursuit of strength and excellence.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to cultivate their inner resources, support their communities, and approach challenges with resilience and vigor.
