सोमापूषणा जनना रयीणां जनना दिवो जनना पृथिव्याः |जातौ विश्वस्य bhuvanasya गोपौ देवाः अकृण्वन् अमृतस्य नाभिम् ||
somāpūṣaṇā jananā rayīṇāṁ jananā divo jananā pṛthivyāḥ | jātau viśvasya bhuvanasya gopau devā akṛṇvann amṛtasya nābhim ||
  • Mantra: "somāpūṣaṇā jananā rayīṇāṁ jananā divo jananā pṛthivyāḥ | jātau viśvasya bhuvanasya gopau devā akṛṇvann amṛtasya nābhim ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • somāpūṣaṇā: Related to the nourishing aspect of Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic rituals.
    • jananā: Creation or birth.
    • rayīṇāṁ: Wealth or prosperity.
    • divo: Of the heavens or celestial realms.
    • pṛthivyāḥ: Of the earth.
    • jātau: In the birth or origin.
    • viśvasya: Of the universe or all beings.
    • bhuvanasya: Of the world or realms.
    • gopau: The protectors or guardians.
    • devā: The gods or divine beings.
    • akṛṇvann: They have created or established.
    • amṛtasya: Of immortality or eternal life.
    • nābhim: The navel or center.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the nourishing qualities of Soma, emphasizing the importance of sustenance and prosperity in life.
    • The repetition of "jananā" highlights the theme of creation, suggesting that wealth and abundance are born from both the earth and the heavens.
    • The phrase "gopau devā" refers to the divine protectors who oversee the universe, indicating that there is a higher power ensuring the balance and prosperity of all beings.
    • The mention of "amṛtasya nābhim" symbolizes the central essence of immortality, suggesting that true wealth is not just material but also spiritual and eternal.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the nurturing of crops can be seen as a reflection of the "somāpūṣaṇā" aspect, where farmers rely on the earth's resources and divine blessings for a bountiful harvest.
    • In personal finance, the mantra can inspire individuals to seek not just material wealth but also to cultivate relationships and experiences that enrich their lives.
    • In community building, the idea of "gopau devā" can be applied to leaders who protect and nurture their communities, ensuring that everyone thrives together.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of creation, prosperity, and divine protection.
    • It teaches us that true wealth encompasses both material and spiritual dimensions, urging us to seek balance in our lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to nurture our surroundings, relationships, and inner selves, fostering a holistic approach to prosperity and well-being.
