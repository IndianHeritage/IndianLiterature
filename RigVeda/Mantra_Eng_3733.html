तं नो अग्नि अभि नरो रयिं सहस्व आ भर |स क्षेपयत स पोषयद भूवद वाजस्य सातय उतैधि पृथ्सु नो वृधे ||
taṁ no agne abhī naro rayiṁ sahasva ā bhara | sa kṣepayat sa poṣayad bhuvad vājasya sātaya utaidhi pṛtsu no vṛdhe ||
  • taṁ no agne: This phrase translates to "that (Agni) may be ours." Agni, the fire god, symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine spark within us. In real life, this can be seen as a call to harness our inner strength and potential.
  • abhī naro rayiṁ: This means "may he (Agni) bestow upon us wealth." Here, "naro" refers to humans, and "rayiṁ" signifies wealth or prosperity. This can be related to the pursuit of success and abundance in our lives, whether it be material wealth or spiritual richness.
  • sahasva ā bhara: This translates to "may he fill us with strength." The word "sahasva" implies courage and vigor. In practical terms, this can be interpreted as a desire for resilience and the ability to face challenges head-on, much like athletes training for a competition.
  • sa kṣepayat: This means "may he direct." It suggests guidance and leadership. In everyday life, this can be likened to seeking mentorship or guidance from experienced individuals to navigate our paths effectively.
  • sa poṣayad: This translates to "may he nourish." This emphasizes the importance of sustenance, both physically and emotionally. For instance, just as plants need water and sunlight to grow, humans require love, support, and resources to thrive.
  • bhuvad vājasya: This means "of the one who is powerful." "Vāja" refers to strength or power. This can be seen in leaders or influential figures in society who inspire and empower others, such as community leaders or activists.
  • sātaya utaidhi: This translates to "may he grant us stability." Stability is crucial in life, whether in our careers, relationships, or mental health. It reflects the need for a solid foundation to build upon, akin to a well-structured home.
  • pṛtsu no vṛdhe: This means "may he increase our prosperity." This is a prayer for growth and abundance in all aspects of life. In real life, this can be related to personal development, financial growth, or the flourishing of relationships.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a prayer for guidance, strength, nourishment, and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of seeking divine support in our endeavors and the need for stability and growth in our lives. In a real-life scenario, this mantra can be applied during times of uncertainty or when embarking on new ventures, serving as a reminder to harness our inner strength, seek guidance, and strive for abundance in all forms.
