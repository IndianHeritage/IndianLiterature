एष स्य ते तन्भो नृम्णवर्धनः सह ओजः प्रदिवि बाह्वोर हितः |तुभ्यं सुतो मघवन तुभ्यम आभृतस् त्वम् अस्य ब्राह्मणाद आ तृपत पिब ||
eṣa sya te tanvo nṛmṇavardhanaḥ saha ojaḥ pradivi bāhvor hitaḥ | tubhyaṁ suto maghavan tubhyam ābhṛtas tvam asya brāhmaṇād ā tṛpat piba ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣa: this
    • sya: of you
    • te: your
    • tanvo: body
    • nṛmṇavardhanaḥ: the one who increases the strength of men
    • saha: together
    • ojaḥ: vitality or energy
    • pradivi: in the sky or heaven
    • bāhvor: of the arms
    • hitaḥ: beneficial
    • tubhyaṁ: to you
    • suto: a son or offspring
    • maghavan: O bountiful one (referring to the deity)
    • tubhyam: to you
    • ābhṛtas: endowed with
    • tvam: you
    • asya: of this
    • brāhmaṇād: from the Brahman (spiritual essence)
    • ā: from
    • tṛpat: satisfaction or fulfillment
    • piba: drink
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine energy that enhances the strength and vitality of individuals.
    • It emphasizes the importance of spiritual nourishment and fulfillment, suggesting that true strength comes from a higher source.
    • The reference to "arms" symbolizes action and effort, indicating that one should engage in righteous deeds to receive divine blessings.
    • The phrase "O bountiful one" acknowledges the abundance of blessings that can be received through devotion and spiritual practice.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, individuals can draw strength from teamwork and collaboration, enhancing their collective productivity.
    • In personal relationships, nurturing bonds with family and friends can lead to emotional fulfillment and support, akin to the blessings sought in the mantra.
    • Practicing mindfulness and meditation can help individuals connect with their inner strength and vitality, reflecting the spiritual essence mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine support for personal growth and strength.
    • It teaches that true vitality comes from a connection with the spiritual realm and through righteous actions.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to enhanced well-being, fulfillment, and a sense of purpose.
