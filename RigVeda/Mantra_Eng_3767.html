अङ्होयुवस तंवस तंवते वि वयो महद दुष्टारं पूर्व्याय |स संवतोनवजातस तुतुर्यāt सिंहं न क्रुद्धम् अभितः परि ष्ठुः ||
aṅhoyuvas tanvas tanvate vi vayo mahad duṣṭaram pūrvyāya | sa saṁvato navajātas tuturyāt siṅhaṁ na kruddham abhitaḥ pari ṣṭhuḥ ||
  • Mantra: "aṅhoyuvas tanvas tanvate vi vayo mahad duṣṭaram pūrvyāya | sa saṁvato navajātas tuturyāt siṅhaṁ na kruddham abhitaḥ pari ṣṭhuḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aṅho: of the light or brightness
    • yuvas: youthful or energetic
    • tanvas: of the body or physical form
    • tanvate: to be manifested or to take form
    • vi: indeed or truly
    • vayo: of age or time
    • mahad: great or significant
    • duṣṭaram: difficult or challenging
    • pūrvyāya: towards the past or ancient
    • sa: he or that
    • saṁvato: of the gathering or assembly
    • navajātas: newly born or fresh
    • tuturyāt: may he protect or guide
    • siṅhaṁ: the lion
    • na: not
    • kruddham: angry or wrathful
    • abhitaḥ: around or nearby
    • pariṣṭhuḥ: in all directions or everywhere
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the interplay between youth, strength, and the challenges of life.
    • The reference to "light" and "youth" suggests a vibrant energy that is essential for overcoming difficulties.
    • The phrase "mahad duṣṭaram" indicates that while challenges may be significant, they can be faced with courage and strength.
    • The mention of the "lion" symbolizes bravery and leadership, suggesting that one should approach challenges with a fearless heart.
    • The mantra encourages a collective approach ("saṁvato"), emphasizing the importance of community and support in overcoming obstacles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, a team facing a challenging project can draw on their collective strengths and youthful energy to innovate and succeed.
    • In personal life, individuals may face difficulties such as health issues or financial struggles, but by harnessing their inner strength and seeking support from loved ones, they can navigate these challenges.
    • In educational environments, students can collaborate to tackle difficult subjects, embodying the spirit of the mantra by working together to achieve success.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of youth, community, and courage in the face of adversity.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement to embrace challenges with a positive and collective spirit, much like a lion facing its surroundings with confidence.
    • In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios, from personal struggles to professional challenges, reinforcing the idea that together, we can overcome even the most daunting obstacles.
