प्र यो नानक्षे अभ्य ओजसा कृविं वधैः शुष्णं निघोषयन् |यदेद अस्तम्भीत प्रथयन्न अमूम् दिवम् आद इज जनिष्ट पार्थिवः ||
pra yo nanakṣe abhy ojasā kriviṁ vadhaiḥ śuṣṇaṁ nighoṣayan | yaded astambhīt prathayann amūṁ divam ād ij janiṣṭa pārthivaḥ ||
  • pra yo nanakṣe: This phrase can be interpreted as "whoever does not falter" or "whoever does not waver." It emphasizes the importance of steadfastness and determination in one's actions.
  • abhy ojasā: This means "with great energy" or "with vigor." It highlights the necessity of putting forth one's full strength and effort in endeavors.
  • kriviṁ vadhaiḥ: This translates to "by the means of actions" or "through deeds." It suggests that actions are the primary way to achieve results and that one must engage actively in the world.
  • śuṣṇaṁ nighoṣayan: This can be understood as "overcoming obstacles" or "defeating challenges." It signifies the importance of resilience and the ability to face difficulties head-on.
  • yaded astambhīt: This phrase means "that which is unshaken" or "that which remains steady." It refers to the stability and reliability of certain principles or truths in life.
  • prathayann amūṁ divam: This translates to "illuminating the heavens" or "bringing forth the divine." It suggests a connection to higher ideals or spiritual aspirations.
  • ād ij janiṣṭa: This means "from which the earth was born" or "from which creation emerged." It indicates the source of life and existence, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • pārthivaḥ: This term refers to "earthly" or "mortal." It reminds us of our human condition and the importance of grounding ourselves in reality.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of perseverance, action, and the pursuit of higher truths. It serves as a reminder that steadfastness and energy are crucial in overcoming life's challenges.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can inspire individuals to remain committed to their goals, whether in personal development, career pursuits, or spiritual growth. For example, an athlete training for a competition embodies the qualities of determination and vigor, facing obstacles with resilience.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encourages us to act with purpose and strength, illuminating our path while remaining grounded in our earthly existence. It teaches that through our actions, we can achieve not only personal success but also contribute to the greater good, ultimately connecting us to the divine and the universe.
