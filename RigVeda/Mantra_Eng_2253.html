उताशिष्ठा अनु श्रिण्वन्ति वह्नयः सभेयो विप्रो भरते मती धनाः |वीळुद्वेषा अनु वश ऋणम आददिः स ह वाजी समिथे ब्रह्मणस पतिḥ ||
utāśiṣṭhā anu śṛṇvanti vahnayaḥ sabheyo vipro bharate matī dhanā | vīḻudveṣā anu vaśa ṛṇam ādadiḥ sa ha vājī samithe brahmaṇas patiḥ ||
  • utāśiṣṭhā: This term can be interpreted as "those who are endowed with strength" or "those who are powerful." In real life, this can refer to individuals or groups who possess the ability to influence or lead, such as community leaders or influential figures in society.
  • anu śṛṇvanti: This phrase means "they listen attentively" or "they are receptive." In a modern context, this can relate to the importance of active listening in communication, whether in personal relationships, workplaces, or educational settings.
  • vahnayaḥ: This word translates to "the fires" or "the flames." Symbolically, fire can represent knowledge, transformation, and purification. For example, in many cultures, fire is used in rituals to signify the burning away of negativity and the welcoming of positive energy.
  • sabheyo: This term means "in the assembly" or "in the gathering." It highlights the significance of community and collective decision-making. In real life, this can be seen in town hall meetings, corporate board meetings, or any scenario where a group comes together to discuss important matters.
  • vipro: This word refers to "the wise" or "the learned." It emphasizes the value of knowledge and wisdom in guiding actions and decisions. In contemporary society, this can be related to educators, scholars, and mentors who play a crucial role in shaping the minds of future generations.
  • bharate: This term can be interpreted as "to carry" or "to bear." It signifies responsibility and the act of taking on duties. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who take on leadership roles in their families, workplaces, or communities.
  • matī dhanā: This phrase translates to "wealth of thought" or "richness of intellect." It underscores the importance of intellectual wealth over material wealth. In real life, this can be exemplified by innovators and thinkers who create value through ideas rather than just financial means.
  • vīḻudveṣā: This term can be understood as "those who are free from enmity" or "without hatred." It promotes the idea of harmony and peace among individuals. In practice, this can be seen in conflict resolution efforts, peace-building initiatives, and community service projects that foster understanding and cooperation.
  • anu vaśa: This phrase means "under control" or "in command." It suggests the importance of self-discipline and governance. In real life, this can relate to personal development practices, such as mindfulness and self-regulation, that help individuals manage their emotions and actions effectively.
  • ṛṇam ādadiḥ: This translates to "the debt is taken" or "the obligation is accepted." It signifies the acceptance of responsibilities and duties. In modern contexts, this can be seen in social contracts, where individuals agree to uphold certain values and responsibilities within their communities.
  • sa ha vājī: This term can be interpreted as "he who is a victor" or "the one who triumphs." It emphasizes success and achievement. In real life, this can be related to individuals who overcome challenges and achieve their goals, serving as inspirations to others.
  • samithe: This word means "in the assembly" or "in the gathering." It reinforces the idea of collective action and unity. In practice, this can be observed in collaborative projects, team sports, and community initiatives where people come together for a common purpose.
  • brahmaṇas patiḥ: This phrase translates to "the lord of the learned" or "the master of knowledge." It signifies the ultimate authority in wisdom and learning. In contemporary society, this can be represented by thought leaders, philosophers, and educators who guide others in their pursuit of knowledge.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, wisdom, community, and responsibility in achieving success and harmony in life. It highlights the value of knowledge and the need for individuals to listen, collaborate, and act with integrity. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, education, conflict resolution, and personal development. By embodying these values, individuals and communities can work towards a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
