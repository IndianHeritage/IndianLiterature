मात्स्य अपायि ते महः पात्रस्येव हरिवो मत्सरो मदः |वृषा ते वृष्ण इन्दुर वाजी सहस्रसातमः ||
matsy apāyi te mahaḥ pātrasyeva harivo matsaro madaḥ | vṛṣā te vṛṣṇa indur vājī sahasrasātamaḥ ||
  • matsy apāyi te mahaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "the great one who has departed like a fish." Here, "matsy" refers to fish, which are often seen as symbols of abundance and life in water. The idea of something great departing can symbolize loss or transformation.
  • pātrasyeva harivo matsaro madaḥ: This translates to "like the vessel of the divine, the intoxication of the fish." "Pātra" means vessel, and "harivo" refers to the divine or the gods. This part of the mantra suggests that the divine essence is contained within a vessel, and the intoxication represents the joy or ecstasy that comes from divine connection.
  • vṛṣā te vṛṣṇa indur: This can be interpreted as "the bull, you are the moon." "Vṛṣā" refers to a bull, which is often a symbol of strength and fertility, while "indur" means moon, representing calmness and serenity. This juxtaposition highlights the balance between strength and tranquility.
  • vājī sahasrasātamaḥ: This translates to "the one who is the best among thousands." "Vājī" refers to a horse or chariot, often symbolizing speed and power, while "sahasrasātamaḥ" means the best among thousands, indicating excellence and superiority.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates themes of transformation, divine connection, strength, and excellence. It reflects the journey of life where one experiences loss, seeks divine intoxication, and strives for balance between strength and serenity.
  • Real-life Application: In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:
    • Personal Growth: Just as the fish symbolizes transformation, individuals can embrace change and growth in their lives, learning to let go of the past to embrace new opportunities.
    • Spiritual Connection: The idea of divine intoxication encourages individuals to seek a deeper connection with the divine, whether through meditation, prayer, or other spiritual practices.
    • Balance in Life: The contrast between the bull and the moon serves as a reminder to maintain balance in our lives, combining strength with calmness in our actions and decisions.
    • Striving for Excellence: The reference to being the best among thousands inspires individuals to pursue excellence in their endeavors, whether in their careers, relationships, or personal goals.
