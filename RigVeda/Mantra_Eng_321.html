उच छिष्टं चंम्वोर भर सोमं पवित्र आ सृजा |नि धेहि गोर अधि त्वचि ||
uc chiṣṭaṁ camvor bhara somam pavitra ā sṛja | ni dhehi gor adhi tvaci ||
  • uc chiṣṭaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "the remnants" or "the leftover." In a broader sense, it signifies the importance of what remains after the essential has been taken. In life, this can relate to the idea of valuing the lessons learned from experiences, even if they are not the primary focus.
  • camvor bhara: This means "bring forth the essence" or "fill with the essence." It emphasizes the need to draw out the core qualities or the most important aspects of something. For example, in a work environment, focusing on the essential skills and qualities of team members can lead to better collaboration and productivity.
  • somam pavitra ā sṛja: This translates to "create the pure soma." Soma is often associated with a divine drink that grants immortality and enlightenment. In a real-life context, this can be interpreted as the pursuit of purity and clarity in our thoughts and actions, striving for a higher state of being or consciousness.
  • ni dhehi: This means "place" or "establish." It suggests the act of grounding or setting something firmly in place. In practical terms, this can relate to establishing strong foundations in relationships, careers, or personal goals.
  • gor adhi tvaci: This phrase can be understood as "on the back of the cow." The cow is a symbol of nourishment and abundance in many cultures, particularly in India. This can signify the importance of nurturing and supporting our resources, whether they are material, emotional, or spiritual.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of valuing what remains, drawing forth the essential qualities, and striving for purity in our pursuits. It teaches us to establish strong foundations and to nurture our resources. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, professional growth, and fostering meaningful relationships. By focusing on these principles, we can lead a more fulfilling and enriched life, ultimately contributing to our own well-being and that of those around us.
