यद वा उ विश्वपतिḥ शितः सुप्रीतो मनुषो विशि |विश्वेद अग्निः प्रति रक्षांसी सेधति ||
yad vā u viśpatiḥ śitaḥ suprīto manuṣo viśi | viśved agniḥ prati rakṣāṁsi sedhati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • vā: or
    • u: indeed
    • viśpatiḥ: the lord of all beings
    • śitaḥ: pure or white
    • suprīto: well-pleased
    • manuṣo: human
    • viśi: distinguished or special
    • viśved: all
    • agniḥ: fire
    • prati: towards
    • rakṣāṁsi: protections or safeguards
    • sedhati: establishes or strengthens
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the universal lord, who is pure and well-pleased with humanity.
    • It emphasizes the role of fire (Agni) as a divine force that provides protection and strength.
    • The invocation suggests that when humans align with the divine will, they receive blessings and safeguards.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together with a pure heart and good intentions, they often experience a sense of unity and protection, akin to the blessings of Agni.
    • In personal endeavors, when individuals act with integrity and seek the greater good, they often find that their efforts are supported by unforeseen circumstances, reflecting the establishment of protections mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of aligning oneself with higher principles and the divine.
    • It teaches that purity of intention and action can lead to divine favor and protection in various aspects of life.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and communities to act with integrity, fostering a sense of safety and well-being through collective goodwill.
