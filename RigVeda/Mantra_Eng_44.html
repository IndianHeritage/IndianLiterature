यस्य संस्थे न वृण्वते हरि समत्सु शत्रवः |तस्मा इन्द्राय गायत ||
yasya saṁsthe na vṛṇvate harī samatsu śatravaḥ | tasmā indrāya gāyata ||
  • Mantra: "yasya saṁsthe na vṛṇvate harī samatsu śatravaḥ | tasmā indrāya gāyata ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • saṁsthe: in the assembly or gathering
    • na: not
    • vṛṇvate: are chosen or selected
    • harī: the divine or the powerful
    • samatsu: in the midst of the enemies
    • śatravaḥ: the foes or adversaries
    • tasmā: therefore
    • indrāya: to Indra (the king of gods)
    • gāyata: sing or chant
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of unity and the strength that comes from collective action.
    • It emphasizes that when a group is united and does not allow division among themselves, they can overcome their adversaries.
    • The reference to Indra suggests invoking divine support and strength in times of conflict.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team that works cohesively without internal conflicts is more likely to succeed in achieving its goals, especially when facing competition.
    • In community initiatives, when members come together for a common cause, they can effectively address social issues, demonstrating the power of collective effort.
    • In sports, a team that plays together and supports each other can often outperform a group of individually talented players who do not cooperate.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity and collective strength in overcoming challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that invoking higher powers, like Indra, can provide additional support in our endeavors.
    • In real life, whether in personal relationships, professional environments, or community efforts, the principles of collaboration and unity are essential for success and overcoming adversities.
