ये त्वां इन्द्र न तुष्टुवुर ऋषयो ये च तुष्टुवुः |ममेद वर्धस्व सुष्टुतः ||
ye tvām indra na tuṣṭuvur ṛṣayo ye ca tuṣṭuvuḥ | mamed vardhasva suṣṭutaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ye tvām indra na tuṣṭuvur ṛṣayo ye ca tuṣṭuvuḥ | mamed vardhasva suṣṭutaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Those sages who do not praise you, O Indra, and those who do praise you, may you increase my wealth."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • ye: Those who
    • tvām: You
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • na: Not
    • tuṣṭuvur: Praise
    • ṛṣayo: Sages or seers
    • ye: Those who
    • ca: And
    • tuṣṭuvuḥ: Praise
    • mamed: My
    • vardhasva: Increase
    • suṣṭutaḥ: Well-praised or well-acknowledged

  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, the deity associated with rain, thunder, and strength, highlighting the importance of both praise and acknowledgment in spiritual and material success.
    • The first part of the mantra reflects on the duality of human behavior—some may not recognize or praise the divine, while others do. This can be likened to real-life scenarios where individuals may overlook the contributions of others or fail to express gratitude.
    • The second part of the mantra is a plea for the increase of wealth, which can be interpreted not just in material terms but also in spiritual and emotional wealth. For example, in a workplace, recognizing and appreciating the efforts of colleagues can lead to a more harmonious and productive environment.

  • Real-Life Applications:
    • In personal relationships, acknowledging the efforts of loved ones can strengthen bonds and foster a supportive atmosphere.
    • In community settings, recognizing the contributions of volunteers or leaders can enhance collective morale and encourage further participation.
    • In professional environments, leaders who praise their teams often see increased motivation and productivity, illustrating the mantra's relevance in fostering a culture of appreciation.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of recognition and gratitude in all aspects of life. By acknowledging both the divine and the contributions of others, we open ourselves to greater abundance and prosperity.
    • It encourages individuals to reflect on their own practices of praise and acknowledgment, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and enriched life.
