नहī नु वो मरुतो अन्त्य अस्मे आरात्ताच चिच्छवसो अन्तम आपुः |ते धृष्णुना शवसा शूशुवांसो 'र्णो न द्वेषो धृतता परि ष्ठुः ||
nahī nu vo maruto anty asme ārāttāc cic chavaso antam āpuḥ | te dhṛṣṇunā śavasā śūśuvāṁso 'rṇo na dveṣo dhṛṣatā pari ṣṭhuḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "Indeed, O Maruts, you are not far from us; you are the ultimate source of strength and vitality. With your courage, you have brought forth the essence of life, and there is no enmity in your strength."
  • Meaning of Key Terms:
    • Maruts: The Maruts are storm deities in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, power, and the forces of nature.
    • Anty: This term refers to the ultimate or final aspect, indicating a source or culmination.
    • Ārāttāc: This implies a sense of closeness or presence, suggesting that the Maruts are always near.
    • Cic: This word denotes consciousness or awareness, emphasizing the vitality and life force.
    • Dhṛṣṇunā: This term refers to courage or bravery, highlighting the strength of the Maruts.
    • Śavasā: This relates to the essence of life or vitality, indicating the life-giving force.
    • Dveṣo: This means enmity or hostility, suggesting that the Maruts embody harmony rather than conflict.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The Maruts can be likened to the forces of nature that bring rain and storms, essential for agriculture. Farmers often pray for the Maruts' blessings to ensure a good harvest, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on the life-giving essence of nature.
    • In modern contexts, the mantra can be interpreted as a call for unity and strength in communities. For instance, during natural disasters, people come together, embodying the courage and vitality of the Maruts to support one another.
    • In personal development, the mantra serves as a reminder to harness one's inner strength and courage, much like the Maruts, to overcome challenges without harboring negativity or enmity towards others.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its celebration of strength, unity, and the life force that connects all beings. It teaches us to recognize the power of nature and the importance of courage in our lives. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal struggles, community support, or environmental awareness, the essence of this mantra encourages us to embrace our inner strength and work harmoniously with others, fostering a spirit of cooperation and vitality. By invoking the Maruts, we are reminded of the interconnectedness of life and the importance of nurturing both our physical and spiritual well-being.
