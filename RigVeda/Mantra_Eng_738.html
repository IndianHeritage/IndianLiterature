सिंहा इव नानदति प्रचेतसः पीशा इव सुपिशो विश्ववेदसः |क्षपो जिन्वन्तः पृथतीभिर ऋष्टिभिः सम इत सबाधः शवसाहिमन्यवः ||
siṁhā iva nānadati pracetasaḥ piśā iva supiśo viśvavedasaḥ | kṣapo jinvantaḥ pṛṣatībhir ṛṣṭibhiḥ sam it sabādhaḥ śavasāhimanyavaḥ ||
  • siṁhā iva nānadati: This phrase translates to "like lions, they roar." Here, the lion symbolizes strength, courage, and leadership. In real life, this can be seen in leaders who inspire their followers with their powerful presence and decisive actions.
  • pracetasaḥ: This word means "with intelligence" or "with wisdom." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in achieving one's goals. For example, a successful entrepreneur often relies on their intelligence to navigate challenges in the business world.
  • piśā iva supiśo: This translates to "like the well-fed." It suggests abundance and prosperity. In a real-life context, this can refer to individuals or communities that thrive due to good resources and opportunities, such as a well-nourished family or a prosperous society.
  • viśvavedasaḥ: This means "those who know everything" or "all-knowing." It highlights the value of wisdom and knowledge in various fields. For instance, scientists and scholars who contribute to human understanding can be seen as embodying this quality.
  • kṣapo jinvantaḥ: This phrase means "those who are victorious." It signifies triumph and success. In everyday life, this can be observed in athletes who win competitions or individuals who overcome personal challenges to achieve their dreams.
  • pṛṣatībhir ṛṣṭibhiḥ: This translates to "with the support of the wise." It emphasizes the importance of guidance and mentorship. For example, students often succeed in their studies with the help of knowledgeable teachers or mentors who provide support and direction.
  • sam it sabādhaḥ: This means "together with unity." It signifies the strength found in collaboration and teamwork. In real life, this can be seen in successful organizations where teamwork leads to innovative solutions and achievements.
  • śavasāhimanyavaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "those who are strong in their resolve." It highlights the importance of determination and resilience. For instance, individuals who persist in their efforts despite setbacks exemplify this quality.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, wisdom, unity, and resilience. It serves as a reminder that success in life is often achieved through a combination of courage, intelligence, support from others, and a strong resolve. In various real-life scenarios, whether in leadership, education, or personal growth, these qualities are essential for overcoming challenges and achieving one's goals. The significance of this mantra lies in its applicability to everyday life, encouraging individuals to embody these virtues in their pursuits.
