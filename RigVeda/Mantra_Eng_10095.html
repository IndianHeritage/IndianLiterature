तं अस्य विष्णुर महिमानम् ओजसांषुं दधन्वान मधुनो वि रप्शते |देवेभिर इन्द्रो मगवाऽयावभिर वृतं जगहन्वाम् अभवद् वरेण्यः ||
tam asya viṣṇur mahimānam ojasāṁśuṁ dadhanvān madhuno vi rapśate | devebhir indro maghavā sayāvabhir vṛtraṁ jaghanvām̐ abhavad vareṇyaḥ ||
  • tam asya viṣṇur: This phrase refers to "that Vishnu," indicating the divine presence and power of Lord Vishnu, who is often associated with preservation and protection in Hinduism.
  • mahimānam: This word means "greatness" or "glory." It signifies the vast and unparalleled nature of Vishnu's attributes and deeds.
  • ojasāṁśuṁ: This term translates to "radiant energy" or "brilliance." It highlights the illuminating and life-giving qualities of Vishnu.
  • dadhanvān: This means "one who wields a bow." It symbolizes strength and the ability to protect and defend against evil.
  • madhuno vi rapśate: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who brings forth sweetness." It suggests that Vishnu not only protects but also provides abundance and prosperity.
  • devebhir indro: "Indra among the gods" refers to Indra, the king of the gods, who represents power and authority.
  • maghavā: This term means "the mighty one," emphasizing the strength and capability of Indra.
  • sayāvabhir: This word means "with the help of," indicating collaboration and support among divine beings.
  • vṛtraṁ jaghanvām̐: This phrase translates to "defeated Vritra," where Vritra symbolizes chaos and obstacles. It signifies the triumph of good over evil.
  • abhavad vareṇyaḥ: This means "he became the most excellent," indicating the ultimate victory and recognition of divine power.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of divine protection, strength, and the triumph of good over evil. It emphasizes the importance of seeking the blessings of Vishnu and Indra for overcoming challenges in life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be invoked during times of difficulty or uncertainty, reminding individuals of the divine support available to them. It encourages faith in the protective and nurturing aspects of the universe, promoting a sense of security and hope. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to strive for greatness while relying on divine guidance and support in their endeavors.
