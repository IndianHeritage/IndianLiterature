मन्त्र: सूर्याय वहतुः प्रागात् सविता यम आवसृजत् |अघासु हन्यन्ते गावोऽर्जुन्योः पर्युह्यते ||
sūryāyā vahatuḥ prāgāt savitā yam avāsṛjat | aghāsu hanyante gāvo 'rjunyoḥ pary uhyate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sūryāyā: To the Sun
    • vahatuḥ: May it carry or bring
    • prāgāt: Forth or forward
    • savitā: The creator or the Sun God
    • yam: Whom
    • avāsṛjat: Has produced or manifested
    • aghāsu: In the realms of sin or evil
    • hanyante: Are destroyed
    • gāvo: Cows
    • 'rjunyoḥ: Of the two (or the dual)
    • pary: All around
    • uhyate: Are protected or surrounded
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Sun God, Savitā, asking for his blessings and protection.
    • The phrase "sūryāyā vahatuḥ prāgāt" suggests a desire for the Sun to bring forth light and energy, which is essential for life.
    • The mention of "aghāsu hanyante" indicates that the presence of the Sun can dispel darkness and negativity, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil.
    • The reference to "gāvo" (cows) signifies nourishment and prosperity, as cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and sustenance in Vedic culture.
    • The concluding part "pary uhyate" emphasizes the protective nature of the Sun, surrounding and safeguarding those who seek its light.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the Sun is vital for crop growth. Farmers often pray for sunlight to ensure a good harvest, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In daily life, people seek positivity and protection from negativity, akin to how the Sun dispels darkness. This mantra can be a reminder to focus on positivity and seek guidance in challenging times.
    • In spiritual practices, meditation under the sun can symbolize enlightenment and clarity, aligning with the mantra's invocation of the Sun's energy.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of the Sun as a source of life, energy, and protection.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking light in our lives, both literally and metaphorically, to overcome challenges and negativity.
    • In various aspects of life, from agriculture to personal well-being, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide individuals towards a more prosperous and positive existence.
