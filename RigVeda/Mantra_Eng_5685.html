ते स्याम देव वरुण ते मित्र सूरिभिः सह |इषं स्वश्च धीमहि ||
te syāma deva varuṇa te mitra sūribhiḥ saha | iṣaṁ svaś ca dhīmahi ||
  • Translation: "O Varuna, may we meditate upon you along with Mitra and the strong ones."
  • Deities Mentioned:
    • Varuna: The god of cosmic order and water, representing the moral and natural laws of the universe.
    • Mitra: The god of friendship and contracts, symbolizing harmony and cooperation among people.
    • Sūribhiḥ: Refers to the strong or powerful beings, often associated with warriors or those who uphold righteousness.
  • Key Concepts:
    • Dhīmahi: This term means "we meditate" or "we contemplate," indicating a call for reflection and understanding.
    • Connection to Nature: The invocation of Varuna emphasizes the importance of water and natural order, which are vital for life.
    • Social Harmony: The mention of Mitra highlights the need for friendship and cooperation in society, essential for peaceful coexistence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Environmental Awareness: Just as Varuna represents water and cosmic order, modern society must recognize the importance of preserving natural resources. For instance, community clean-up drives can reflect this mantra's essence by promoting environmental stewardship.
    • Building Relationships: The invocation of Mitra can be seen in initiatives that foster teamwork and collaboration in workplaces. Team-building exercises can enhance cooperation, mirroring the spirit of this mantra.
    • Conflict Resolution: In situations of conflict, invoking the principles of Varuna and Mitra can guide individuals towards dialogue and understanding, promoting peace and harmony.
  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of meditating upon divine principles that govern both nature and human relationships. By invoking Varuna and Mitra, we are reminded of our responsibilities towards the environment and each other. In real life, this can manifest in various ways, such as environmental conservation efforts, fostering friendships, and resolving conflicts amicably. The mantra serves as a timeless reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of upholding moral and ethical values in our daily lives.
