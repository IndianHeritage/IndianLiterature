इन्द्रं वाणीर अनुत्तमन्युम एव सत्रा राजानं दधिरे सहध्यै | हर्यश्वाय बरहयाऽसम आपीन ||
indraṁ vāṇīr anuttamanyum eva satrā rājānaṁ dadhire sahadhyai | haryaśvāya barhayā sam āpīn ||
  • Indraṁ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • vāṇīr: This term can be interpreted as "the voice" or "speech," indicating communication and expression.
  • anuttamanyum: Means "the unparalleled or supreme being," emphasizing the uniqueness and superiority of Indra.
  • eva: Translates to "indeed" or "certainly," reinforcing the certainty of the statement.
  • satrā: Refers to "sacrifices" or "rituals," which were significant in Vedic culture for invoking divine blessings.
  • rājānaṁ: Means "kings" or "rulers," indicating those in positions of authority and governance.
  • dadhire: Translates to "they hold" or "they possess," suggesting the act of receiving or embracing something.
  • sahadhyai: Refers to "together" or "in unity," highlighting the importance of collaboration and collective effort.
  • haryaśvāya: This term can be interpreted as "to the one who rides the horse," symbolizing speed, agility, and strength.
  • barhayā: Means "with the support" or "with the help," indicating reliance on divine assistance.
  • sam āpīn: Translates to "they have come together" or "they have united," emphasizing the theme of unity and cooperation.

The mantra speaks to the significance of Indra as a powerful leader who is invoked through rituals and sacrifices. It highlights the importance of communication and unity among rulers and leaders, suggesting that they should work together to achieve common goals. The imagery of Indra riding a horse symbolizes the strength and agility required in leadership.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Leadership in Organizations: Just as Indra is a leader among gods, effective leaders in organizations must communicate clearly and work collaboratively with their teams to achieve success.
  • Community Building: The emphasis on unity and collective effort can be seen in community initiatives where individuals come together to support a common cause, such as environmental conservation or social justice.
  • Political Governance: Rulers and politicians can draw inspiration from this mantra to foster cooperation and dialogue among different factions to create a harmonious society.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the qualities of effective leadership—strength, communication, and unity. It encourages individuals in positions of authority to embrace collaboration and work together for the greater good, reflecting the timeless values found in the RigVeda.
