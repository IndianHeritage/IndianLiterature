नमस्यत हव्यदातिं स्वध्वरं दुवस्यत दाम्यं जातवेदसम् | रथीर ऋतस्य बृहतो विचर्षणिर अग्निर देवानाम् अभवत् पुरोहितः ||
namasyata havyadātiṁ svadhvaraṁ duvasyata damyaṁ jātavedasam | rathīr ṛtasya bṛhato vicarṣaṇir agnir devānām abhavat purohitaḥ ||
  • Namasyata: This word signifies respect and reverence. It indicates the act of bowing or offering salutations.
  • Havyadātiṁ: Refers to the one who is worthy of offerings, often associated with deities or divine entities that receive sacrifices.
  • Svadhvaraṁ: This term can be interpreted as the one who has a self-formed or self-created nature, emphasizing individuality and uniqueness.
  • Duvasyata: This word suggests the act of being invoked or called upon, indicating a connection between the divine and the devotee.
  • Damyaṁ: This refers to the one who is to be controlled or restrained, often implying the need for discipline and order.
  • Jātavedasam: This term is often associated with Agni, the fire god, who is seen as the mediator between humans and the divine, representing knowledge and enlightenment.
  • Rathīr: This word means 'charioteer' and symbolizes guidance and direction in life.
  • Ṛtasya: Refers to 'truth' or 'cosmic order,' emphasizing the importance of living in harmony with the universe.
  • Bṛhato: This means 'great' or 'vast,' indicating the expansive nature of the divine and the universe.
  • Vicarṣaṇir: This term can be interpreted as 'one who moves swiftly,' symbolizing the dynamic nature of life and the universe.
  • Agnir: Refers to Agni, the fire god, who plays a crucial role in rituals and is a symbol of transformation and purification.
  • Devānām: This means 'of the gods,' indicating the divine aspect of the mantra.
  • Abhavat: This word means 'became' or 'manifested,' suggesting the realization of divine presence in the material world.
  • Purohitaḥ: Refers to the priest or the one who performs rituals, symbolizing the connection between the divine and the earthly realm.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of reverence, discipline, and the connection between the divine and the human experience. It highlights the role of Agni as a mediator and the significance of living in accordance with cosmic order (ṛta). In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as rituals, personal discipline, and the pursuit of truth. By acknowledging the divine and maintaining respect for the forces that guide us, individuals can find direction and purpose in their lives, much like a charioteer guiding a chariot through the vastness of existence.
