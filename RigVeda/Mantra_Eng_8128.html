अयं विचर्षणिर हितः पवमानः स चेतति |हिन्वान आप्यम् बृहत् ||
ayaṁ vicarṣaṇir hitaḥ pavamānaḥ sa cetati | hinvāna āpyam bṛhat ||
  • ayaṁ - This word translates to "this" or "this one," indicating a specific focus or subject.
  • vicarṣaṇir - This term can be understood as "the one who moves or travels," often associated with the idea of exploration or journey.
  • hitaḥ - Meaning "beneficial" or "good," this word emphasizes the positive aspects of the subject being discussed.
  • pavamānaḥ - This refers to "the purifying one" or "the one that brings purity," often associated with the wind or air, symbolizing cleansing and renewal.
  • sa - This means "he" or "that," referring back to the subject of the mantra.
  • cetati - This translates to "thinks" or "contemplates," indicating a state of awareness or mindfulness.
  • hinvāna - This word means "removing" or "dispelling," suggesting the act of eliminating negativity or obstacles.
  • āpyam - This can be interpreted as "the ultimate" or "the highest," pointing towards a goal or aspiration.
  • bṛhat - Meaning "great" or "vast," this word signifies something expansive, often related to the universe or higher consciousness.

The mantra can be understood as a call to recognize and embrace the journey of life, focusing on the beneficial aspects of our experiences. It emphasizes the importance of purification and mindfulness, encouraging individuals to think deeply and remove obstacles in their path towards achieving greatness.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra suggests contemplation and purification, individuals can reflect on their life choices, seeking to eliminate negative habits and thoughts that hinder their progress.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to "pavamānaḥ" can inspire people to appreciate and protect nature, recognizing the purifying qualities of the air and the importance of a clean environment.
  • Mindfulness Practices: The idea of "cetati" encourages practices such as meditation and mindfulness, which help individuals stay present and aware of their thoughts and actions.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the journey of life, urging individuals to focus on the beneficial aspects, engage in self-reflection, and strive for greatness while purifying their thoughts and actions. Its significance lies in its applicability to personal development, environmental consciousness, and mindfulness, making it relevant in various aspects of modern life.
