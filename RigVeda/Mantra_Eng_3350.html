भरद यदि वीर अतो वेविजानः पथोरुणा मनोजवा असर्जि |तूयं ययौ मधुना सोम्येनोता श्रवो विविदे श्येनो अत्र ||
bharad yadi vir ato vevijānaḥ pathoruṇā manojavā asarji | tūyaṁ yayau madhunā somyenota śravo vivide śyeno atra ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bharad: Carrying or bearing
    • yadi: If
    • vir: Strong or powerful
    • ato: From here
    • vevijānaḥ: Those who know or understand
    • pathoruṇā: By the path of light or knowledge
    • manojavā: Swift as the mind
    • asarji: Not to be hindered or obstructed
    • tūyaṁ: You (the divine)
    • yayau: Went or traveled
    • madhunā: With sweetness or nectar
    • somyenota: With the essence of the moon
    • śravo: Fame or glory
    • vivide: Diverse or various
    • śyeno: Like a falcon or eagle
    • atra: Here
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of knowledge and understanding.
    • It emphasizes the importance of being strong and swift in thought and action.
    • The reference to "the path of light" suggests a journey towards enlightenment and wisdom.
    • The metaphor of "swift as the mind" indicates the speed at which one can achieve understanding and insight.
    • The mention of "nectar" and "moon" symbolizes the sweetness and beauty of knowledge.
    • It also highlights the diversity of experiences and the glory that comes from pursuing knowledge.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, students who are curious and eager to learn often excel, embodying the qualities of being "swift as the mind."
    • In professional settings, individuals who embrace knowledge and adapt quickly to changes can navigate challenges effectively, much like the "strong" and "powerful."
    • In personal development, seeking wisdom and understanding can lead to a fulfilling life, akin to traveling the "path of light."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of knowledge, strength, and swiftness in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to pursue wisdom and understanding, which can lead to personal and collective growth.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can enhance decision-making, foster creativity, and promote a deeper understanding of the world around us.
