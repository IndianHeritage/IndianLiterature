कुविन नो अग्निर उचथस्य वीर असद वसुष कुविद वसुभिः कामम् आवरत् |चोदः कुवित तुतुज्यात सातये धियः शुचिप्रतीकं तम अया धियā गृणे ||
kuvin no agnir ucathasya vīr asad vasuṣ kuvid vasubhiḥ kāmam āvarat | codaḥ kuvit tutujyāt sātaye dhiyaḥ śucipratīkaṁ tam ayā dhiyā gṛṇe ||
  • kuvin no agnir: This phrase can be translated as "May the fire (Agnir) be favorable to us." In many cultures, fire symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. For example, in a household, lighting a fire for cooking or warmth can be seen as a source of sustenance and comfort.
  • ucathasya vīr: This means "the hero of the invocation." It suggests that the fire is not just a physical element but also embodies strength and valor. In real life, we often invoke courage in challenging situations, much like a hero stepping forward to face adversity.
  • asad vasuṣ: This translates to "may it bring us wealth." Wealth here can be interpreted not only in terms of material possessions but also in terms of knowledge, relationships, and experiences. For instance, a successful business venture can be seen as a source of wealth that enriches lives.
  • kuvid vasubhiḥ kāmam āvarat: This phrase means "may it fulfill our desires with the help of the good." It emphasizes the importance of positive influences in achieving our goals. In real life, having supportive friends and mentors can significantly impact our ability to reach our aspirations.
  • codaḥ kuvit tutujyāt: This can be interpreted as "may the good thoughts be realized." It highlights the power of positive thinking and intention. For example, setting a clear goal and visualizing success can often lead to achieving that goal.
  • sātaye dhiyaḥ: This means "for the sake of wisdom." Wisdom is crucial in making informed decisions. In everyday life, seeking advice from experienced individuals can lead to better outcomes in various situations, such as career choices or personal relationships.
  • śucipratīkaṁ: This translates to "the symbol of purity." Purity can refer to intentions, actions, and thoughts. In practice, maintaining integrity in our actions, such as being honest in our dealings, reflects this purity.
  • tam ayā dhiyā gṛṇe: This means "I sing of that with my intellect." It suggests a deep appreciation and acknowledgment of the divine or the higher principles guiding us. In real life, expressing gratitude for the positive aspects of our lives can enhance our overall well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking positive energies, wisdom, and purity in our lives. It serves as a reminder that our thoughts and intentions can shape our reality. In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as setting personal goals, fostering supportive relationships, and maintaining integrity in our actions. By embodying these principles, we can create a more fulfilling and prosperous life.
