मन्त्र: ऐभिर अग्ने दुवो गिरो विश्वेभिः सोमपीतये |देवेभिर याहि यक्षि च ||
aibhir agne duvo giro viśvebhiḥ somapītaye | devebhir yāhi yakṣi ca ||
  • Mantra: "aibhir agne duvo giro viśvebhiḥ somapītaye | devebhir yāhi yakṣi ca ||"
  • Translation: "O Agni, with your two divine voices, come to us with all the gods for the sake of the Soma drink."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • aibhir: This term refers to "with" or "by means of," indicating a connection or accompaniment.
    • agne: This is the name of the fire god, Agni, who is a central figure in Vedic rituals and represents the divine messenger.
    • duvo: Meaning "two," this signifies duality, possibly referring to the two aspects of Agni or the dual nature of existence.
    • giro: This translates to "voices" or "sounds," suggesting the importance of speech and communication in invoking the divine.
    • viśvebhiḥ: This means "with all" or "universal," indicating the inclusion of all deities and forces in the invocation.
    • somapītaye: This term refers to the "drinking of Soma," a sacred ritual drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight.
    • devebhir: Meaning "with the gods," this emphasizes the collective nature of the invocation, calling upon divine beings.
    • yāhi: This translates to "come" or "approach," a direct invitation to the divine presence.
    • yakṣi: This term can mean "to bestow" or "to grant," indicating a request for blessings or gifts from the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, people often invoke divine energies for guidance and support, similar to how the mantra calls upon Agni and the gods.
    • During significant life events, such as weddings or new beginnings, individuals may seek blessings from higher powers, paralleling the request for divine presence in this mantra.
    • In various cultures, the act of sharing a drink, like wine or tea, symbolizes unity and connection, akin to the communal aspect of consuming Soma in Vedic traditions.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energies and the interconnectedness of all beings. It highlights the role of Agni as a mediator between humans and the divine, showcasing the significance of communication and collective worship. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek guidance, foster unity, and recognize the divine presence in their daily lives, reminding us of the importance of rituals and the sacredness of shared experiences.
