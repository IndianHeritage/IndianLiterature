इन्द्र क्रतुविदं सुतं सोमं हर्य पुरुष्टुत |पिबा वृषस्व तातृपिम ||
indra kratuvidaṁ sutaṁ somaṁ harya puruṣṭuta | pibā vṛṣasva tātṛpim ||
  • Indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. He represents strength and power.
  • Kratuvidaṁ: This term refers to the one who knows the power of sacrifice and ritual. It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in performing actions.
  • Sutaṁ: Suta means 'son' or 'offspring.' In this context, it can refer to the divine qualities or blessings that are passed down through generations.
  • Somaṁ: Soma is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. It is often associated with the elixir of life.
  • Harya: This term can be interpreted as 'the one who is worthy' or 'the one who is to be praised.' It signifies the qualities that deserve recognition and honor.
  • Puruṣṭuta: This word means 'the one who is praised' or 'the one who is celebrated.' It highlights the importance of acknowledging and honoring those who contribute positively to society.
  • Pibā: This means 'drink' or 'consume.' It suggests the act of taking in or absorbing something beneficial.
  • Vṛṣasva: This term can be translated as 'the one who is strong' or 'the one who is powerful.' It emphasizes the qualities of strength and resilience.
  • Tātṛpim: This word refers to 'the one who brings joy' or 'the one who provides happiness.' It signifies the importance of joy and fulfillment in life.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to Indra, the powerful deity, to bestow blessings of strength, wisdom, and joy upon us. It emphasizes the significance of knowledge and the importance of recognizing and honoring those who contribute positively to our lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Leadership: Just as Indra is a leader among the gods, effective leaders in our communities and workplaces should embody strength, wisdom, and the ability to inspire joy in others.
  • Education: The emphasis on knowledge (kratuvidaṁ) highlights the importance of education and learning in our lives. It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and understanding.
  • Celebration of Achievements: Recognizing and honoring those who contribute positively (puruṣṭuta) fosters a culture of appreciation and motivation in society.
  • Personal Growth: The act of 'drinking' (pibā) from the well of knowledge and experience can lead to personal development and fulfillment (tātṛpim).

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the values of strength, wisdom, and joy. It encourages us to seek knowledge, honor those who uplift us, and strive for personal and communal growth. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
