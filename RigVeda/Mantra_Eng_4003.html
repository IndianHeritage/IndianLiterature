मातुष् पदे परमे शुक्र आयोर विपण्यवो रास्पिरासो अग्मन् |सुशेव्यं नमसां रातहव्याः शिशुम मृजन्त्य आयवो न वासे ||
mātuṣ pade parame śukra āyor vipanyavo rāspirāso agman | suśevyaṁ namasā rātahavyāḥ śiśum mṛjanty āyavo na vāse ||
  • mātuṣ pade: This phrase translates to "at the mother's feet." In many cultures, the mother is revered as a source of nurturing and wisdom. For example, in a family setting, children often seek guidance and comfort from their mothers, symbolizing the importance of maternal influence in one's life.
  • parame śukra: This means "the supreme brightness" or "the highest purity." It can be associated with enlightenment or divine knowledge. In real life, this can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and truth, where individuals strive to attain clarity and understanding in their lives, much like a student seeking wisdom through education.
  • āyor vipanyavo: This translates to "the blessings of longevity." It emphasizes the importance of health and long life. In contemporary society, this can be seen in the growing focus on wellness, fitness, and healthy living, where people engage in activities like yoga and balanced diets to enhance their longevity.
  • rāspirāso agman: This phrase can be interpreted as "the desires that come from the heart." It reflects the idea that true fulfillment comes from pursuing one's passions and desires. In real life, this can be observed in individuals who follow their dreams, such as artists or entrepreneurs, who find joy and satisfaction in their work.
  • suśevyaṁ namasā: This means "with reverence and respect." It highlights the importance of humility and gratitude in our interactions. For instance, in professional settings, showing respect to colleagues and mentors can foster a positive work environment and lead to better collaboration.
  • rātahavyāḥ: This translates to "the offerings made during the night." It signifies the importance of rituals and traditions in maintaining a connection with the divine. In modern life, this can be seen in various cultural practices where people engage in rituals to honor their heritage and spirituality.
  • śiśum mṛjanty: This means "they cleanse the child." It symbolizes the nurturing and protective aspects of motherhood. In real life, this can be related to the role of parents in guiding and educating their children, ensuring they grow up with strong values and morals.
  • āyavo na vāse: This translates to "may they not be deprived." It emphasizes the importance of abundance and prosperity. In today's world, this can be reflected in efforts to ensure that everyone has access to basic needs such as food, shelter, and education, promoting social justice and equality.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of nurturing, wisdom, and the pursuit of a fulfilling life. It emphasizes the significance of maternal influence, the quest for knowledge, and the importance of health and respect in our lives. In real-life scenarios, these themes resonate through our relationships, personal aspirations, and societal values. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can strive for a balanced and meaningful existence, fostering a sense of community and well-being.
