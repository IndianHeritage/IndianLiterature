आपो ह यद् बृहतीर विश्वम् आयन गर्भं दधाना जनयन्तीर अग्निम् |ततो देवानां सम अवर्ततासुर एकः कसमै देवाय हविषा विद्हेम ||
āpo ha yad bṛhatīr viśvam āyan garbhaṁ dadhānā janayantīr agnim | tato devānāṁ sam avartatāsur ekaḥ kasmai devāya haviṣā vidhema ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āpo: Waters
    • ha: Indeed
    • yad: Which
    • bṛhatīr: Great
    • viśvam: All
    • āyan: Abode
    • garbhaṁ: Womb
    • dadhānā: Holding
    • janayantīr: Giving birth
    • agnim: Fire
    • tato: From that
    • devānāṁ: Of the gods
    • sam: Together
    • avartatā: Revolved
    • asur: Demons
    • ekaḥ: One
    • kasmai: To whom
    • devāya: To the god
    • haviṣā: With offerings
    • vidhema: We should invoke
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the waters as a source of life and creation, emphasizing their great and universal nature.
    • The waters are depicted as holding the womb of creation, symbolizing fertility and the nurturing aspect of nature.
    • It highlights the connection between the waters and fire, suggesting a balance between different elements of nature.
    • The invocation to the gods signifies a plea for blessings and guidance in the process of creation and sustenance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the importance of water for crop growth can be seen as a reflection of the waters' nurturing role in the mantra.
    • In environmental conservation, the balance between water and fire (e.g., controlled burns for forest management) illustrates the interconnectedness of natural elements.
    • In spiritual practices, rituals involving water (like purification) and fire (like offerings) are common, echoing the themes of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the essential role of water in creation and life, urging us to recognize and respect the natural elements.
    • Its application in real life can be seen in agriculture, environmental practices, and spiritual rituals, reminding us of our connection to nature.
    • By invoking the blessings of the gods, we acknowledge the need for harmony and balance in our lives and the world around us.
