त्वं पाहिंद्र सहीयसो नृन् भवā मरुद्भिर अवयातहेळाः |सुप्रकेतिभिः सासहिर दधानो विद्यामेषं वृजनं जीरदानुम् ||
tvam pāhīndra sahīyaso nṝn bhavā marudbhir avayātaheḻāḥ | supraketebhiḥ sāsahir dadhāno vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum ||
  • tvam pāhīndra: This phrase translates to "You, O Indra," where Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and power. In real life, this can be likened to a leader or a guiding force in our lives, such as a mentor or a teacher who protects and guides us.
  • sahīyaso nṝn: This means "supporting the men." It emphasizes the importance of community and support among individuals. In modern society, this can be seen in teamwork and collaboration in workplaces or community service, where individuals come together to uplift one another.
  • bhavā marudbhir avayātaheḻāḥ: This translates to "may you be protected by the winds." The winds here symbolize the forces of nature and the universe that provide protection and guidance. In real life, this can be related to the idea of being in harmony with nature and seeking protection from the elements, such as through sustainable living practices.
  • supraketebhiḥ: This means "with good intentions." It highlights the significance of having positive thoughts and actions. In everyday life, this can be applied in our interactions with others, where kindness and good intentions can lead to better relationships and a more harmonious society.
  • sāsahir dadhāno: This translates to "holding the strength." It signifies the importance of inner strength and resilience. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who overcome challenges and adversities, demonstrating courage and determination in their pursuits.
  • vidyāmeṣaṁ: This means "this knowledge." It emphasizes the value of knowledge and wisdom. In contemporary life, this can be related to the pursuit of education and lifelong learning, which empowers individuals to make informed decisions and contribute positively to society.
  • vṛjanaṁ jīradānum: This translates to "the essence of life." It signifies the fundamental aspects that sustain life. In real life, this can be connected to the importance of health, nutrition, and well-being, which are essential for a fulfilling life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of protection, support, knowledge, and strength. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals within a community and the importance of positive intentions and actions. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as fostering teamwork, promoting sustainable living, and valuing education and personal growth. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and supportive society.
