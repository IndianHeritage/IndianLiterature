अस्य क्रत्वा विचेतसो दसमस्य वसु रथ आ |अधा विश्वासु हव्यो 'ग्निर विक्षु प्र शस्यते ||
asya kratvā vicetaso dasmasya vasu ratha ā | adhā viśvāsu havyo 'gnir vikṣu pra śasyate ||
  • Mantra: "asya kratvā vicetaso dasmasya vasu ratha ā | adhā viśvāsu havyo 'gnir vikṣu pra śasyate ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "By this power of thought, may the fire, which is worthy of all offerings, be invoked to come to us from all directions."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • asya: of this
    • kratvā: by the power of thought or action
    • vicetaso: of the wise or the intelligent
    • dasmasya: of the stone or the solid foundation
    • vasu: wealth or abundance
    • ratha: chariot or vehicle
    • adhā: down or below
    • viśvāsu: in all directions
    • havyo: worthy of offerings
    • agnir: fire
    • vikṣu: to be invoked or called
    • pra: forth or forward
    • śasyate: is praised or is invoked
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of intention and thought in invoking positive energies and resources in life.
    • The reference to "fire" symbolizes transformation, purification, and the energy needed to achieve one's goals.
    • The phrase "worthy of offerings" suggests that the energies we seek must be approached with respect and gratitude.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team may come together to brainstorm ideas (the power of thought) to drive a project forward (invoking the fire of creativity).
    • During a personal challenge, an individual may meditate (the power of thought) to attract positive outcomes and support from their community (invoking the fire of connection).
    • In rituals, offerings are made to deities or natural elements, symbolizing respect and the desire to receive blessings in return.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the power of intention and the importance of invoking positive energies in our lives.
    • It teaches us that by aligning our thoughts and actions, we can attract abundance and transformation.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal growth, professional endeavors, or spiritual practices, this mantra serves as a reminder to approach our goals with clarity, respect, and a sense of purpose.
