अग्नि यत् ते दिवि वर्चः पृथिव्यां यद् ओषधिष्व अप्स्व आ यजत्र |येनान्तरिक्षं उर्व आततन्थ त्वेषः स भानुर अर्णवो नृचक्षाः ||
agne yat te divi varcaḥ pṛthivyāṁ yad oṣadhīṣv apsv ā yajatra | yenāntarikṣam urv ātatantha tveṣaḥ sa bhānur arṇavo nṛcakṣāḥ ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic tradition, symbolizing energy, light, and purity.
  • yat te: This translates to "what is yours," indicating a connection to the divine and the offerings made to Agni.
  • divi varcaḥ: "divi" means "in the heavens," and "varcaḥ" means "radiance" or "brilliance." Together, they signify the celestial light and energy that Agni embodies.
  • pṛthivyāṁ: This means "on earth," representing the physical realm and the connection between the divine and the material world.
  • yad oṣadhīṣv: "yad" means "that which," and "oṣadhīṣv" refers to "in the plants" or "herbs," highlighting the importance of nature and its offerings.
  • apsv ā yajatra: "apsv" means "in the waters," and "yajatra" refers to "the one who is worshipped." This emphasizes the sacredness of water and its role in rituals.
  • yenāntarikṣam: "ye" means "by which," and "antarikṣam" refers to "the atmosphere" or "the space between." This indicates the connection between the earth and the heavens.
  • urv ātatantha: "urv" means "the wide expanse," and "ātatantha" means "spreads out." This suggests the vastness of creation and the divine presence in all aspects of life.
  • tveṣaḥ: This translates to "you," referring to Agni as the focal point of worship and reverence.
  • sa bhānur: "sa" means "he," and "bhānur" means "the sun." This connects Agni to the sun, symbolizing life, energy, and illumination.
  • arṇavo: This means "the ocean," representing the vastness of existence and the depths of knowledge.
  • nṛcakṣāḥ: "nṛ" means "human" and "cakṣāḥ" means "sight." This signifies the human ability to perceive and understand the divine through the elements of nature.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the interconnectedness of the divine, nature, and humanity. It highlights the importance of fire, water, and earth in rituals and daily life, reminding us of the sacredness of our environment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire us to respect and honor nature, recognizing the divine presence in all elements of our world. It encourages us to engage in rituals that celebrate the beauty and energy of life, fostering a deeper connection with the universe and promoting harmony in our lives.
