मन्त्र: वातेवाजुर्य नद्येव रीतिर्मक्षी इव चक्षुषा यातमर्वाक् | हस्ताव इव तंवे3 शम्भविष्टा पादेव नो नयतं वस्यो अच्छ ||
vātevājuryā nadyeva rītir akṣī iva cakṣuṣā yātam arvāk | hastāv iva tanve3 śambhaviṣṭhā pādeva no nayataṁ vasyo accha ||
  • vātevājuryā: This phrase can be broken down into two parts: "vāte" meaning "of the wind" and "vājura" meaning "swift" or "powerful". This suggests the idea of a force that is both dynamic and essential, much like the wind that carries seeds and facilitates growth.
  • nadyeva: "Nadya" refers to "river". The comparison to a river indicates a flow of energy or resources, symbolizing nourishment and life. Just as rivers sustain ecosystems, this mantra suggests a connection to the vital forces of nature.
  • rītir akṣī iva: "Rītir" means "order" or "truth\ while "akṣī" translates to "eye". The phrase can be interpreted as "the order of truth is like an eye\ emphasizing the importance of clarity and perception in understanding the world around us.
  • cakṣuṣā yātam arvāk: "Cakṣuṣā" means "with the eye" and "yātam" means "to go". This suggests that one should navigate life with awareness and insight, akin to how one uses their eyes to perceive their surroundings.
  • hastāv iva: "Hastāv" translates to "like hands". This implies action and the ability to grasp or hold onto what is important, reinforcing the idea of taking initiative in life.
  • tanve3 śambhaviṣṭhā: "Tanve" means "body" and "śambhaviṣṭhā" can be interpreted as "established in auspiciousness". This suggests that our physical existence is rooted in positive energy and auspiciousness, highlighting the importance of maintaining a healthy and balanced life.
  • pādeva no nayataṁ: "Pādeva" means "like feet\ indicating movement and direction. "Nayataṁ" translates to "lead us". This part of the mantra calls for guidance in our journey, akin to how feet guide our path.
  • vasyo accha: "Vasyo" means "controlled" or "subdued\ and "accha" means "good". This suggests that the forces we encounter in life should be harnessed positively, leading to beneficial outcomes.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, perception, action, and guidance. It teaches us to navigate life with awareness, to take initiative, and to seek auspiciousness in our physical existence. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways, such as making conscious decisions that align with our values, being mindful of our environment, and taking proactive steps towards our goals. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for a harmonious existence, where we are aware of the forces around us and use them to lead a fulfilling life.
