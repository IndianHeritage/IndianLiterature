यो हव्याण्य एयरयता मनुर्हितो देव आसā सुगन्धिना |विवासते वार्याणि स्वध्वरो होतā देवो अमर्त्यः ||
yo havyāny airayatā manurhito deva āsā sugandhinā | vivāsate vāryāṇi svadhvaro hotā devo amartyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • havyāny: the offerings
    • airayatā: has brought forth
    • manurhito: the mind directed
    • deva: the divine
    • āsā: with hope
    • sugandhinā: with fragrant offerings
    • vivāsate: they are invoked
    • vāryāṇi: the powers
    • svadhvaro: the self-chosen
    • hotā: the priest
    • devo: the divine
    • amartyaḥ: the immortal
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine connection between the offerings made by humans and the response from the divine realm.
    • The phrase "who has brought forth the offerings" emphasizes the importance of intention and mindfulness in our actions.
    • The term "sugandhinā" suggests that the quality of our offerings, both material and spiritual, should be pure and fragrant, symbolizing positivity and goodwill.
    • The invocation of "the powers" indicates that our prayers and offerings are not just rituals but are meant to connect us with higher energies and divine forces.
    • The mention of "the immortal" highlights the eternal nature of the divine and the belief that our spiritual practices can lead us to a higher state of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be reflected in the way we approach our work and relationships. For instance, when we perform our duties with dedication and a positive mindset, we are essentially making offerings to the universe.
    • In a community setting, when people come together to help others, such as organizing a charity event, they are embodying the essence of this mantra by bringing forth their resources and goodwill.
    • In personal development, practicing mindfulness and gratitude can be seen as a way of making fragrant offerings to the divine, enhancing our connection with the universe.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of intention, quality, and connection in our spiritual practices.
    • It teaches us that our actions, when performed with mindfulness and purity, can invoke divine energies and lead to a more fulfilling life.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal growth, community service, or professional endeavors, embodying the principles of this mantra can enhance our experiences and foster a deeper connection with the divine.
