यस्य ते पीत्वा वृषभो वृषायतेऽस्य पीता स्वरविदः |स सुप्रकेतो अभ्यक्रमीद् इषोऽच्छा वाजं नैतशः ||
yasya te pītvā vṛṣabho vṛṣāyate 'sya pītā svarvidaḥ | sa supraketo abhy akramīd iṣo 'cchā vājaṁ naitaśaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasya: of whom
    • te: you
    • pītvā: having drunk
    • vṛṣabho: the bull (symbolizing strength)
    • vṛṣāyate: becomes strong
    • 'sya: of him
    • pītā: the father
    • svarvidaḥ: of those who know the sound (or knowledge)
    • sa: he
    • supraketo: having a good name or reputation
    • abhy akramīd: has approached
    • iṣo: of the bow (symbolizing power)
    • 'cchā: not
    • vājaṁ: the prize or reward
    • naitaśaḥ: not from here
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the strength and power derived from knowledge and reputation.
    • The "bull" symbolizes strength and vitality, suggesting that true power comes from wisdom and understanding.
    • The "father" represents guidance and support, indicating that knowledge is passed down through generations.
    • The phrase "having drunk" implies that one must actively engage with knowledge to gain strength.
    • The "bow" symbolizes the ability to achieve goals, and the mantra suggests that one must not seek rewards from superficial sources.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who possesses deep knowledge and a good reputation can inspire their team to achieve great results, much like the "bull" that becomes strong through wisdom.
    • In education, a student who actively engages with their studies (drinking from the well of knowledge) will find themselves empowered to tackle challenges, akin to the strength derived from the mantra.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who communicate openly and supportively (like a father) foster a strong bond, leading to mutual growth and understanding.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of knowledge, reputation, and strength in achieving one's goals.
    • It teaches that true power comes from within, cultivated through learning and the support of others.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal and professional success, as it encourages individuals to seek wisdom and build a strong foundation based on integrity and knowledge.
