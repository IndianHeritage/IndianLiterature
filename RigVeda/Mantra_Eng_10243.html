कामस तद अग्रे सम अवर्तताधि मनसो रेतः प्रथमं यद आसीत् |सतो बन्धुम असति निर अविन्दन हृदि प्रतिष्याऽ कवयो मनीषा ||
kāmas tad agre sam avartatādhi manaso retaḥ prathamaṁ yad āsīt | sato bandhum asati nir avindan hṛdi pratīṣyā kavayo manīṣā ||
  • kāmas tad agre sam avartatādhi manaso retaḥ prathamaṁ yad āsīt
    • kāmas: This word translates to "desire" or "wish." It signifies the fundamental human emotions and aspirations that drive individuals.
    • tad agre: Meaning "that in the beginning," it refers to the primordial state or the initial condition of existence.
    • sam avartat: This phrase means "turned around" or "revolved," indicating a cyclical nature of existence and the unfolding of desires.
    • ādhi manaso: Translated as "of the mind," it emphasizes the role of consciousness and thought in shaping desires.
    • retaḥ: This term refers to "semen" or "creative energy," symbolizing the potential for creation and manifestation.
    • prathamaṁ: Meaning "first," it highlights the primary importance of this creative energy in the process of existence.
    • yad āsīt: Translates to "that which existed," indicating the foundational aspect of this creative force.
  • sato bandhum asati nir avindan hṛdi pratīṣyā kavayo manīṣā
    • sato: This word means "of the existent" or "truth," referring to the reality that is tangible and observable.
    • bandhum: Translates to "relation" or "connection," emphasizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • asati: Meaning "in the non-existent," it refers to the void or the absence of form and structure.
    • nir avindan: This phrase means "did not find," indicating a search for understanding or truth.
    • hṛdi: Translated as "in the heart," it signifies the emotional and spiritual center of a person.
    • pratīṣyā: Meaning "the wise" or "the seers," it refers to those who possess insight and understanding.
    • kavayo: This term means "poets" or "sages," indicating those who express profound truths through creativity.
    • manīṣā: Translates to "thought" or "intellect," emphasizing the role of wisdom in understanding existence.

Conclusion:

The mantra reflects the intricate relationship between desire, consciousness, and the creative forces of the universe. It suggests that the initial desires and thoughts of the mind are fundamental to the process of creation and existence. In real life, this can be seen in how our aspirations and intentions shape our reality. For instance, when individuals set goals and work towards them, they are manifesting their desires into tangible outcomes. The mantra also highlights the importance of wisdom and connection in understanding the deeper truths of life. In a world where individuals often feel disconnected, this teaching encourages us to seek relationships and insights that foster a sense of unity and purpose.
