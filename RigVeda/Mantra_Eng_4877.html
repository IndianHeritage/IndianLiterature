पर्जन्यवाताऽवृषभा पृथिव्याः पुरीषाणि जिन्वतम अप्यानी |सत्यश्रुतः कवयो यस्य गीरभिर जगतः स्थातर जगद आ कृतुंध्वम् ||
parjanyavātā vṛṣabhā pṛthivyāḥ purīṣāṇi jinvatam apyāni | satyaśrutaḥ kavayo yasya gīrbhir jagataḥ sthātar jagad ā kṛṇudhvam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • parjanyavātā: The winds that bring rain.
    • vṛṣabhā: The bull, symbolizing strength and fertility.
    • pṛthivyāḥ: Of the earth.
    • purīṣāṇi: The excretions or waste, often referring to the byproducts of life.
    • jinvatam: Victorious or conquering.
    • apyāni: That which is beneficial or auspicious.
    • satyaśrutaḥ: One who is known for truth or has a reputation for truthfulness.
    • kavayo: The wise or the poets.
    • yasya: Whose.
    • gīrbhir: By words or hymns.
    • jagataḥ: Of the universe or the world.
    • sthātar: Establishing or stabilizing.
    • jagad: The world.
    • ā kṛṇudhvam: May you create or manifest.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the forces of nature, particularly the rain-bearing winds, to bring forth abundance and fertility to the earth.
    • The bull symbolizes strength and the ability to cultivate the land, which is essential for agriculture and sustenance.
    • The reference to waste products highlights the cycle of life, where even what is discarded can lead to new growth and prosperity.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of truth and wisdom, suggesting that those who speak with integrity can influence the world positively.
    • It calls upon the wise to use their words to stabilize and nurture the universe, indicating the power of language and thought in shaping reality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the arrival of monsoon rains is crucial for crop growth. Farmers often pray for rain, reflecting the invocation of parjanyavātā.
    • The bull is a common symbol in farming communities, representing hard work and the fertility of the land, as seen in rural festivals celebrating harvests.
    • In environmental science, the concept of waste leading to new life can be seen in composting, where organic waste is transformed into nutrient-rich soil.
    • In leadership and community building, the importance of truthfulness and wisdom is evident in how effective leaders inspire trust and stability among their followers.
    • In literature and art, poets and writers use their words to influence society, echoing the call for kavayo to use their wisdom for the betterment of the world.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of nature, agriculture, and human values.
    • It teaches us the importance of nurturing the earth and recognizing the cycles of life, where every element has a role to play.
    • In real life, it encourages us to speak truthfully and wisely, using our words to create a positive impact on the world around us.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of our responsibility to care for the environment and to use our knowledge and creativity to foster growth and stability in our communities.
