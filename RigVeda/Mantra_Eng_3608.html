प्र पस्त्यां अदितिं सिन्धुम अर्कैः स्वस्तिम ईळे सख्याय देविम् | उभे यथा नो अहनी निपात उषासनक्तां करतातम् अदब्दे ||
pra pastyā3m aditiṁ sindhum arkaiḥ svastim īḻe sakhyāya devīm | ubhe yathā no ahanī nipāta uṣāsānaktā karatām adabdhe ||
  • Translation: "I invoke Aditi, the mother of all, with hymns and prayers for prosperity and well-being. May she, who is the goddess of the dawn, grant us both the blessings of the day and the night, and may she protect us from all harm."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pra: A prefix meaning "forth" or "outward," indicating a call or invocation.
    • pastyā: Refers to "Aditi," the mother of the gods, symbolizing the infinite and the boundless.
    • aditiṁ: Directly translates to "Aditi," emphasizing her importance in the mantra.
    • sindhum: Means "ocean" or "river," symbolizing abundance and the flow of life.
    • arkaiḥ: Refers to "hymns" or "light," indicating the invocation of divine energy.
    • svastim: Translates to "well-being" or "prosperity," a key aspect of the prayer.
    • īḻe: Means "to worship" or "to praise," indicating the act of devotion.
    • sakhyāya: Refers to "friendship" or "companionship," highlighting the importance of community.
    • devīm: Means "goddess," reinforcing the divine aspect of the invocation.
    • ubhe: Means "both," indicating a duality in blessings.
    • yathā: Translates to "as" or "in the way that," introducing a conditional aspect.
    • no: Means "us," indicating the collective nature of the prayer.
    • ahanī: Refers to "day" or "light," symbolizing clarity and knowledge.
    • nipāta: Means "fall" or "descend," indicating the arrival of blessings.
    • uṣāsānaktā: Refers to "the goddess of dawn," symbolizing new beginnings.
    • karatām: Means "to do" or "to act," indicating the importance of action in receiving blessings.
    • adabdhe: Translates to "without harm," emphasizing protection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek blessings for prosperity and well-being through various rituals and prayers, similar to the invocation of Aditi in this mantra.
    • Communities come together to celebrate festivals, invoking divine blessings for harmony and friendship, reflecting the essence of "sakhyāya" in the mantra.
    • In times of uncertainty or challenges, people may turn to spiritual practices for protection and guidance, akin to the plea for safety from harm in the mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of Aditi, representing the infinite and nurturing aspects of the universe. It emphasizes the importance of community, well-being, and protection. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal prayers, community gatherings, and rituals aimed at fostering harmony and prosperity. By invoking divine blessings, individuals and communities can cultivate a sense of security and abundance in their lives.
