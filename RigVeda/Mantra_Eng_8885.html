उद īरय पितरā जार ā भागम् इयक्षति हर्यतो हृत्त इष्यति |विवक्ति वह्निः स्वपस्यते मखस् तविष्यते असुरो वेपते मती ||
ud īraya pitarā jāra ā bhagam iyakṣati haryato hṛtta iṣyati | vivakti vahniḥ svapasyate makhas taviṣyate asuro vepate matī ||
  • ud īraya: This phrase can be translated as "rise up" or "elevate." It signifies the call for awakening or uplifting oneself.
  • pitarā: This word means "ancestors" or "forefathers." It emphasizes the connection to lineage and the wisdom passed down through generations.
  • jāra: This term refers to "old age" or "decay." It highlights the inevitability of aging and the importance of wisdom that comes with it.
  • ā bhagam: This translates to "may we attain prosperity." It is a prayer for abundance and well-being in life.
  • iyakṣati: This word means "to see" or "to perceive." It suggests the importance of awareness and understanding in our lives.
  • haryato: This term can be interpreted as "from the horse" or "from the swift." It symbolizes speed, strength, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • hṛtta: This means "to be taken away" or "to be removed." It can refer to the challenges and burdens we face in life.
  • iṣyati: This word means "to desire" or "to wish." It emphasizes the importance of intention and aspiration in achieving our goals.
  • vivakti: This translates to "to express" or "to reveal." It signifies the importance of communication and sharing one's thoughts and feelings.
  • vahniḥ: This term means "fire." Fire symbolizes transformation, energy, and the power of creation.
  • svapasyate: This means "to dream" or "to envision." It highlights the significance of imagination and vision in shaping our future.
  • makhas: This word refers to "sacrifice" or "offering." It signifies the importance of giving and contributing to the greater good.
  • taviṣyate: This translates to "to be strong" or "to be powerful." It emphasizes the need for strength and resilience in facing life's challenges.
  • asuro: This term means "demonic" or "negative." It represents the obstacles and negative forces we encounter in life.
  • vepate: This means "to tremble" or "to shake." It signifies the impact of fear or uncertainty in our lives.
  • matī: This word translates to "intellect" or "wisdom." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in navigating life's complexities.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the journey of life, emphasizing the importance of rising above challenges, seeking prosperity, and maintaining awareness. It teaches us to honor our ancestors while recognizing the inevitability of aging and decay. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, resilience in the face of adversity, and the pursuit of wisdom. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can strive for a balanced and fulfilling life, contributing positively to their communities and honoring the legacy of those who came before them.
