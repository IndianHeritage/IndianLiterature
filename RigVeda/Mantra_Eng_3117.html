इह त्वां भूय आ चरेद उप तमन दोषावस्तर दीदिवांसम् अनु dyūn |कीर्णन्तस त्वां सुमनसः सपेमाभि द्युम्ना तस्थिवांसो जनानाम् ||
iha tvā bhūry ā cared upa tman doṣāvastar dīdivāṁsam anu dyūn | krīḻantas tvā sumanasaḥ sapemābhi dyumnā tasthivāṁso janānām ||
  • iha tvā bhūry ā cared: This phrase can be translated as "Here, may we worship you abundantly." It signifies the importance of devotion and reverence in our lives. In real life, this can be seen in various forms of worship, whether in temples, homes, or during personal meditation.
  • upa tman doṣāvastar dīdivāṁsam anu dyūn: This part means "May we be free from faults and may we attain the divine light." It emphasizes the aspiration to overcome personal shortcomings and seek enlightenment. For example, in a workplace, striving for self-improvement and learning from mistakes can lead to personal and professional growth.
  • krīḻantas tvā sumanasaḥ: This translates to "Those who are joyful and have good intentions towards you." It highlights the significance of positive relationships and community support. In everyday life, this can be reflected in friendships and family bonds where mutual respect and joy are present.
  • sapemābhi dyumnā tasthivāṁso janānām: This means "May we be endowed with glory and may we stand firm among the people." It speaks to the desire for recognition and stability within society. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who strive for excellence in their fields, gaining respect and admiration from their peers.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of devotion, self-improvement, positive relationships, and the pursuit of glory. Its significance lies in its call for individuals to engage in worship, seek enlightenment, foster joy and goodwill, and strive for recognition in their communities. In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as personal development, building strong relationships, and contributing positively to society. By embodying these principles, individuals can lead fulfilling lives that resonate with the values expressed in the RigVeda.
