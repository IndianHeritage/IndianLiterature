सहस्रā ते शतā वयं गवām ā च्यावयामसि |अस्मत्रā राध एतु ते ||
sahasrā te śatā vayaṁ gavām ā cyāvayāmasi | asmatrā rādha etu te ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sahasrā: thousand
    • te: of you
    • śatā: hundred
    • vayaṁ: we
    • gavām: of cows
    • ā: indeed
    • cyāvayāmasi: we are seeking
    • asmatrā: from us
    • rādha: prosperity or wealth
    • etu: may come
    • te: to you
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra expresses a prayer for abundance and prosperity. The speaker is invoking the divine to grant them a thousandfold wealth, particularly in the form of cows, which are symbolic of nourishment and prosperity in Vedic culture. The mantra emphasizes a collective desire for abundance and the hope that this wealth will come to the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, cows are often seen as a source of wealth and sustenance. This mantra can be recited during harvest festivals to invoke blessings for a bountiful yield.
    • In modern business contexts, entrepreneurs may use this mantra as a form of affirmation, seeking prosperity and success in their ventures, reflecting the desire for growth and abundance.
    • During family gatherings or rituals, this mantra can be recited to bless the household with prosperity, symbolizing the collective hopes of family members for a prosperous future.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of abundance and prosperity. It serves as a reminder of the importance of community and collective aspirations for wealth and nourishment. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts, from agricultural practices to personal affirmations in business and family settings. By reciting this mantra, individuals and communities can align their intentions towards attracting prosperity and fostering a sense of abundance in their lives.
