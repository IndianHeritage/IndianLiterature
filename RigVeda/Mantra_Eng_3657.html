क्षेत्राद अपश्यं सनुतश्चरन्तं सुमद् यूथं न पुरुषोभमानम् |न ता अगृभ्रन्न् अजनिष्ट हि षः पलिक्नीर इद् युवतयो भवन्ति ||
kṣetrād apaśyaṁ sanutaś carantaṁ sumad yūthaṁ na puru śobhamānam | na tā agṛbhrann ajaniṣṭa hi ṣaḥ paliknīr id yuvatayo bhavanti ||
  • kṣetrād: This word translates to "from the field" or "from the area." In a broader sense, it can refer to the environment or the context in which one operates.
  • apaśyaṁ: Meaning "not seeing" or "not perceiving," this term highlights a lack of awareness or understanding.
  • sanutaś: This means "with praise" or "with respect," indicating a positive acknowledgment or recognition.
  • carantaṁ: Translated as "moving" or "wandering," it suggests a sense of journey or exploration.
  • sumad: This word means "good thought" or "well thought out," emphasizing the importance of wisdom and intelligence.
  • yūthaṁ: Referring to "a group" or "a collective," it signifies the strength found in unity and collaboration.
  • na puru: This translates to "not a man" or "not a person," which can imply a broader, more universal perspective rather than focusing on individualism.
  • śobhamānam: Meaning "shining" or "glorious," this term conveys a sense of beauty and excellence.
  • na tā: This means "not that," indicating a negation or rejection of something.
  • agṛbhrann: Translated as "not grasping" or "not holding," it suggests a sense of detachment or non-attachment.
  • ajaniṣṭa: This means "did not create" or "did not produce," indicating a lack of manifestation or realization.
  • hi: A particle that can mean "indeed" or "certainly," reinforcing the statement that follows.
  • ṣaḥ: This word means "six," which can symbolize completeness or a cycle.
  • paliknīr: Referring to "the waters" or "the streams," it can symbolize the flow of life and experiences.
  • id: Meaning "this," it points to the present moment or current situation.
  • yuvatayo: This translates to "of the young" or "of the youth," indicating vitality and potential.
  • bhavanti: Meaning "become" or "come into being," it signifies the process of transformation or emergence.

Conclusion: This mantra reflects the importance of awareness and understanding in our environment. It emphasizes the value of collective wisdom and the beauty that arises from unity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as teamwork in a workplace, community building, or even in personal relationships where mutual respect and collaboration lead to greater achievements. The mantra serves as a reminder that true success and glory come not from individual pursuits but from the collective efforts and good thoughts of a united group. It encourages us to be mindful of our surroundings and to appreciate the contributions of others, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
