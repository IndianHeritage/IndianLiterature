अभि हि सत्य सोमपा उभे बभूथा रोडसी |इन्द्रासि सुन्वतो वृद्धः पतिर्दिवः ||
abhi hi satya somapā ubhe babhūtha rodasī | indrāsi sunvato vṛdhaḥ patir divaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "abhi hi satya somapā ubhe babhūtha rodasī | indrāsi sunvato vṛdhaḥ patir divaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, in the direction of
    • hi: indeed, certainly
    • satya: truth, reality
    • somapā: those who drink the soma (a sacred drink)
    • ubhe: both, two
    • babhūtha: have become, have manifested
    • rodasī: the two worlds, the heavens and the earth
    • indrāsi: O Indra, the king of gods
    • sunvato: of those who listen, of the hearers
    • vṛdhaḥ: great, powerful
    • patir: lord, master
    • dvaḥ: of the sky, of heaven
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the essence of truth and the divine connection between the heavens and the earth.
    • The phrase "abhi hi satya somapā" emphasizes the importance of truth as a guiding principle for those who partake in sacred rituals, symbolized by the soma drink.
    • "Ubhe babhūtha rodasī" suggests that both realms, the earthly and the celestial, are interconnected and have manifested through truth.
    • By addressing Indra, the mantra acknowledges the power of the divine and the importance of listening to the wisdom of the universe.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the pursuit of truth can be seen in various fields such as science, journalism, and personal relationships. For instance, a journalist who adheres to ethical standards seeks to report the truth, thereby fostering trust within the community.
    • In personal development, individuals who practice honesty and integrity in their actions often find themselves more connected to others and to their own sense of purpose.
    • The concept of interconnectedness can be observed in environmental movements, where the health of the earth (rodasī) is seen as directly linked to human well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of truth in our lives and the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • It encourages individuals to seek truth and wisdom, fostering a deeper understanding of their place in the universe.
    • In practical terms, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more harmonious relationships, ethical practices, and a greater sense of responsibility towards the environment and society.
