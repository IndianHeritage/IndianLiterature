यदा ते हर्यतां हरि वावृधाते दिवे-दिवे |आदित ते विश्वा भुवनानि येमिरे ||
yadā te haryatā harī vāvṛdhāte dive-dive | ād it te viśvā bhuvanāni yemire ||
  • Mantra: "yadā te haryatā harī vāvṛdhāte dive-dive | ād it te viśvā bhuvanāni yemire ||"
  • Translation: "Whenever your energy, O Hari, increases day by day, all the worlds are filled with your presence."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • yadā: Whenever
    • te: Your
    • haryatā: Energy or vitality
    • harī: O Hari (a name for the divine, often associated with Vishnu)
    • vāvṛdhāte: Increases or grows
    • dive-dive: Day by day
    • ād: All
    • it: Here
    • te: Your
    • viśvā: All or universal
    • bhuvanāni: Worlds or realms
    • yemire: Are filled or are pervaded

  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of divine energy and its impact on the universe.
    • The term "Hari" refers to a divine presence that embodies sustenance and protection.
    • The phrase "day by day" emphasizes the continuous and gradual increase of this divine energy.
    • As this energy grows, it fills all realms, suggesting a universal influence that transcends individual existence.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often experience a gradual increase in motivation and energy, leading to greater achievements and fulfillment.
    • In nature, the changing seasons can be seen as a manifestation of this energy, where spring brings new life and vitality to the earth.
    • In community settings, when a leader inspires and uplifts others, the collective energy of the group can lead to significant positive changes.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine energy in our lives and the world around us.
    • It teaches us that as we cultivate our inner vitality and connect with the divine, we can positively influence our surroundings.
    • In real-life scenarios, recognizing and harnessing this energy can lead to personal growth, community development, and a deeper connection with the universe.
