ये पायवो मातेत्यं ते अग्ने पश्यन्तो अन्धं दुरिताद् अरक्षन् | ररक्ष तान सुखृतो विश्ववेदाः दिप्सन्त इद् रिपवो नाह देभुः ||
ye pāyavo māmateyaṁ te agne paśyanto andhaṁ duritād arakṣan | rarakṣa tān sukṛto viśvavedā dipsanta id ripavo nāha debhuḥ ||
  • ye pāyavo māmateyaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "those who protect my interests." It emphasizes the importance of guardianship and support in our lives. In real life, this can relate to friends, family, or mentors who look out for our well-being.
  • te agne paśyanto: Here, "Agni" refers to the divine fire or energy. "Paśyanto" means "those who see." This suggests that those who are aware of the divine energy or truth are blessed. In practical terms, this can relate to individuals who are spiritually aware and seek enlightenment, such as philosophers or spiritual leaders.
  • andhaṁ duritād arakṣan: "Andhaṁ" means "blindness" and "duritād" refers to "from misfortune." This part of the mantra speaks to the idea of being protected from ignorance and adversity. In everyday life, this can be seen in the context of education and awareness, where knowledge helps individuals avoid pitfalls.
  • rarakṣa tān sukṛto viśvavedā: "Rarakṣa" means "to protect," "sukṛto" refers to "good deeds," and "viśvavedā" means "all-knowing." This suggests that those who engage in righteous actions are safeguarded by universal knowledge. In real life, this can be exemplified by individuals who act ethically and are rewarded with positive outcomes, such as community leaders or activists.
  • dipsanta id ripavo nāha debhuḥ: "Dipsanta" means "desiring," "ripavo" refers to "enemies," and "nāha debhuḥ" translates to "may not harm." This part of the mantra expresses a desire for protection from adversaries. In practical terms, this can relate to the need for security in various aspects of life, such as personal safety, financial stability, or emotional well-being.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of protection, awareness, and righteous living. It highlights the importance of having supportive relationships and being mindful of the divine energy that guides us. In real-life scenarios, the teachings of this mantra can be applied to foster a sense of community, promote ethical behavior, and seek knowledge to navigate life's challenges. By embodying these principles, individuals can create a more harmonious and secure environment for themselves and others.
