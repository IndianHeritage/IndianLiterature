न यः सम्पृच्छे न पुनर् हवीतवे न संवादाय रमते |तस्मान् नो अद्य समृतेर उरुष्यतम् बाहुभ्यां न उरुष्यतम् ||
na yaḥ sampṛcche na punar havītave na saṁvādāya ramate | tasmān no adya samṛter uruṣyatam bāhubhyāṁ na uruṣyatam ||
  • na yaḥ sampṛcche: This phrase translates to "he who does not ask." It emphasizes the importance of inquiry and seeking knowledge. In real life, this can be related to students who do not ask questions in class, potentially missing out on valuable information.
  • na punar havītave: This means "he who does not offer again." It suggests the significance of continuous effort and contribution. For example, in a workplace, an employee who does not contribute ideas or feedback may hinder team progress.
  • na saṁvādāya ramate: This translates to "he who does not engage in dialogue." It highlights the necessity of communication and interaction. In personal relationships, lack of communication can lead to misunderstandings and conflicts.
  • tasmān no adya samṛter uruṣyatam: This phrase means "therefore, today, may the great wealth of knowledge not be withheld from us." It signifies the desire for wisdom and understanding. In a practical sense, this can be seen in the pursuit of education and lifelong learning.
  • bāhubhyāṁ na uruṣyatam: This translates to "may it not be withheld from our arms." It implies that the knowledge and wealth we seek should be accessible and within our reach. In real life, this can relate to the importance of resources and opportunities in achieving one's goals.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of inquiry, continuous contribution, and communication in the pursuit of knowledge and wealth. It serves as a reminder that to gain wisdom and resources, one must actively seek, engage, and not shy away from asking questions. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in educational settings, workplaces, and personal relationships, where open dialogue and proactive efforts lead to growth and success.
