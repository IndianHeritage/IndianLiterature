मरतु यस्य हि क्षये पाथा दिवो विमहसः |स सुगोपातमो जनः ||
maruto yasya hi kṣaye pāthā divo vimahasaḥ | sa sugopātamo janaḥ ||
  • Maruto: This term refers to the Maruts, the storm deities in Vedic literature, often associated with wind, storms, and the forces of nature.
  • Yasya: This means "whose" or "of whom," indicating possession or association.
  • Hi: A particle that emphasizes the statement, often translated as "indeed" or "certainly."
  • Kṣaye: This word means "in destruction" or "in decay," referring to the end or dissolution of something.
  • Pāthā: This term can be interpreted as "the path" or "the way," suggesting a journey or direction.
  • Divo: Meaning "of the sky" or "heaven," it indicates a celestial or divine aspect.
  • Vimahasaḥ: This can be translated as "of the vastness" or "of the expansive," referring to the boundless nature of the sky or universe.
  • Sa: This means "he" or "that one," referring to a person or entity.
  • Sugopātamo: This term means "well-protected" or "safeguarded," indicating a sense of security or guardianship.
  • Janaḥ: This translates to "people" or "individuals," referring to humanity or a community.

The mantra can be interpreted as follows: "Indeed, whose destruction is like the vastness of the sky, that person is well-protected among the people." This suggests that those who are connected to the divine forces of nature, represented by the Maruts, possess a profound strength and resilience that protects them from the adversities of life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to individuals who embrace the natural world and its elements, recognizing the power of nature in their lives. For example, a farmer who respects the cycles of nature and works in harmony with the environment may find that they are better protected from the uncertainties of weather and crop failure. Similarly, individuals who cultivate a deep spiritual connection with the universe may experience a sense of inner peace and security, even in challenging times.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the protective power of nature and the divine. It encourages individuals to seek harmony with the natural world and to recognize the strength that comes from such a connection. In a world filled with chaos and uncertainty, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for finding security and resilience through a deeper understanding of our relationship with the universe.
