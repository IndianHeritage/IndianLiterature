विद्युद्रथा मरुत ऋष्टिमन्तो दिवो मर्यā ऋतजाता अयासः |सरस्वती श्रिणवन् यज्ञियासो धाता रयिं सहवीरं तुरासः ||
vidyudrathā maruta ṛṣṭimanto divo maryā ṛtajātā ayāsaḥ | sarasvatī śṛṇavan yajñiyāso dhātā rayiṁ sahavīraṁ turāsaḥ ||
  • Vidyudrathā: This term can be broken down into "vidyut" meaning lightning and "rathā" meaning chariot. It symbolizes the swift and powerful nature of knowledge and enlightenment.
  • Maruta: Refers to the storm gods or winds, representing the dynamic and uncontrollable forces of nature. In real life, this can be likened to the unpredictable challenges we face.
  • Ṛṣṭimanto: This word suggests those who are endowed with wisdom or insight. It emphasizes the importance of having a clear vision and understanding in life.
  • Divo: Meaning "of the sky" or "heaven," it signifies the higher realms of existence and aspirations that humans strive for.
  • Maryā: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is worthy" or "noble." It highlights the importance of virtue and integrity in our actions.
  • Ṛtajātā: Refers to the concept of truth and order. It emphasizes the significance of living in harmony with the natural laws and principles of the universe.
  • Ayāsaḥ: This word means "without fatigue" or "effortless." It suggests that true knowledge and wisdom come with ease when aligned with the right principles.
  • Sarasvatī: The goddess of knowledge, music, art, and wisdom. She represents the pursuit of learning and creativity in our lives.
  • Śṛṇavan: Meaning "to listen" or "to hear," it emphasizes the importance of being receptive to knowledge and wisdom from various sources.
  • Yajñiyāso: Refers to those who are engaged in sacrifice or offerings, symbolizing the importance of selflessness and contribution to the greater good.
  • Dhātā: Meaning "the creator" or "the sustainer," it signifies the divine force that supports and nurtures life.
  • Rayiṁ: This term means "wealth" or "prosperity," highlighting the importance of abundance in various forms, not just material.
  • Sahavīraṁ: Refers to "with heroes" or "companions," emphasizing the value of community and collaboration in achieving goals.
  • Turāsaḥ: This word can be interpreted as "swift" or "quick," suggesting the need for agility and responsiveness in our actions.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of knowledge, virtue, and the interconnectedness of life. It teaches us that true wisdom comes from understanding the natural order and being receptive to learning. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various fields such as education, leadership, and community building. By embodying the principles of knowledge, integrity, and collaboration, individuals can navigate the challenges of life with grace and purpose, ultimately contributing to a harmonious society.
