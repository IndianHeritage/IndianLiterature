धृतव्रतो धनदाः सोमवृद्धः स हि वामस्य वसुनः पुरुक्षुः |सं जग्मिरे पथ्याः रयो अस्मिन समुद्रे न सिन्धवो यादमानाः ||
dhṛtavrato dhanadāḥ somavṛddhaḥ sa hi vāmasya vasunaḥ purukṣuḥ | saṁ jagmire pathyā3 rāyo asmin samudre na sindhavo yādamānāḥ ||
  • dhṛtavrato: This term can be broken down into two parts: "dhṛta," meaning steadfast or firm, and "vrata," which refers to a vow or commitment. Together, it signifies a person who is resolute in their commitments and principles.
  • dhanadāḥ: This word translates to "those who give wealth." It implies generosity and the act of sharing resources with others, which is a valued trait in many cultures.
  • somavṛddhaḥ: "Soma" refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. "Vṛddha" means increased or enhanced. Thus, this term suggests the enhancement of spiritual or divine qualities through sacred practices.
  • sa hi vāmasya vasunaḥ purukṣuḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "he is indeed the great one of the left (or the auspicious one) who possesses wealth." It highlights the importance of wealth not just in material terms but also in terms of spiritual and moral richness.
  • saṁ jagmire pathyā3 rāyo asmin: This translates to "they have gone along the path of prosperity." It emphasizes the journey towards success and abundance, suggesting that there is a righteous path to achieving wealth and prosperity.
  • samudre na sindhavo yādamānāḥ: This part means "in the ocean, not the rivers." It signifies the vastness of opportunities and resources available, contrasting the limited nature of rivers with the boundless potential of the ocean.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of steadfastness in one's commitments, the importance of generosity, and the pursuit of spiritual and material wealth through righteous means. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as business ethics, community service, and personal development. For instance, a business leader who remains committed to ethical practices while generously sharing profits with the community embodies the essence of this mantra. It teaches us that true wealth is not just about accumulation but also about the positive impact we have on others and the world around us.
