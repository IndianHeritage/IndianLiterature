मां नो वधि रुद्र मां परा दा मां ते भूमा प्रसितौ हीळितस्य |आं नो भज बर्हिषि जीवशंसे यूम पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
mā no vadhī rudra mā parā dā mā te bhūma prasitau hīḻitasya | ā no bhaja barhiṣi jīvaśaṁse yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • mā no vadhī rudra: This phrase translates to "O Rudra, do not harm us." Rudra is a deity associated with storms and the hunt, often seen as a fierce protector. In real life, this can be understood as a plea for protection from the forces of nature or any adversities we may face.
  • mā parā dā: This means "do not give us any great pain." It reflects a universal human desire to avoid suffering and hardship. For example, in times of personal loss or tragedy, this mantra can resonate with individuals seeking solace and relief from their emotional turmoil.
  • mā te bhūma prasitau hīḻitasya: This can be interpreted as "may we not be overwhelmed by your might." It acknowledges the power of Rudra while asking for mercy. In everyday life, this can relate to situations where we feel overpowered by challenges, such as work stress or personal conflicts, and seek balance and peace.
  • ā no bhaja barhiṣi: This translates to "may we be nourished by your blessings." It signifies the importance of divine grace in our lives. For instance, in a community setting, this can be seen when people come together to support one another, fostering a sense of unity and shared blessings.
  • jīvaśaṁse: This means "in the praise of life." It emphasizes the value of life and existence. In real life, this can be reflected in our appreciation for nature, relationships, and the simple joys of living, encouraging us to celebrate life.
  • yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: This translates to "may you always protect us with auspiciousness." It is a call for ongoing protection and well-being. In practical terms, this can be related to the importance of community support, where individuals look out for one another, ensuring a safe and nurturing environment.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for protection, relief from suffering, and the blessings of life. Its significance lies in its universal appeal to the divine for guidance and support in navigating life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in moments of distress, during communal gatherings, or when seeking a deeper connection with nature and the universe. By invoking this mantra, individuals can foster a sense of peace, resilience, and gratitude in their lives.
