पृच्छे तद एनो वरुण दिदृक्षूपो एमी चिकीतुषो विपृच्छम् |समानम् इन मे कवयश्चिद आहुर अयं ह तुभ्यं वरुणो हृणीते ||
pṛcche tad eno varuṇa didṛkṣūpo emi cikituṣo vipṛccham | samānam in me kavayaś cid āhur ayaṁ ha tubhyaṁ varuṇo hṛṇīte ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pṛcche: I ask
    • tad: that
    • eno: this
    • varuṇa: O Varuna (the god of cosmic order)
    • didṛkṣū: desiring to see
    • upo: I approach
    • emi: I come
    • cikituṣo: of the wise
    • vipṛccham: I inquire
    • samānam: similar
    • in me: in me
    • kavayaś: the poets
    • cid: indeed
    • āhur: have said
    • ayaṁ: this
    • ha: indeed
    • tubhyaṁ: to you
    • varuṇo: O Varuna
    • hṛṇīte: may he grant
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards Varuna, the deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority.
    • The speaker expresses a desire to understand the deeper truths of existence and seeks guidance from Varuna.
    • It reflects a quest for knowledge and wisdom, emphasizing the importance of inquiry and understanding in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the pursuit of knowledge in various fields such as science, philosophy, and spirituality.
    • For instance, students and researchers often seek guidance from mentors or divine inspiration when tackling complex problems.
    • Professionals in any field may invoke a sense of moral responsibility akin to Varuna's cosmic order when making ethical decisions.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for wisdom and moral clarity in our actions and thoughts.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding, fostering a sense of responsibility towards oneself and society.
    • In a world filled with uncertainties, invoking the essence of Varuna can guide us towards making informed and ethical choices.
