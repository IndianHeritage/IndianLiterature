अतः सहस्रनिर्णिजा रथेनायातम अश्विना |वत्सो वाम मधुमद वचोऽशंसीत काव्यः कविः ||
ataḥ sahasranirṇijā rathenā yātam aśvinā | vatso vām madhumad vaco 'śaṁsīt kāvyaḥ kaviḥ ||
  • ataḥ - This word translates to "therefore" or "hence," indicating a conclusion or a result derived from previous statements or actions.
  • sahasranirṇijā - This term can be broken down into "sahasra," meaning "thousand," and "nirṇijā," which refers to "decisions" or "resolutions." Together, it suggests a multitude of decisions or a thousand paths to choose from.
  • rathenā - This means "by the chariot," symbolizing movement, progress, or the journey of life. The chariot is often seen as a vehicle that carries one through various experiences.
  • yātam - This translates to "may we go" or "let us proceed," indicating a desire for action or movement towards a goal.
  • aśvinā - Referring to the Aśvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing and benevolent qualities. They symbolize duality and partnership.
  • vatso - This word means "calf" or "young one," often representing innocence, potential, and the nurturing aspect of life.
  • vām - This translates to "your," indicating possession or connection, often used to address the divine or a higher power.
  • madhumad - Meaning "sweet" or "honeyed," this word symbolizes the sweetness of life, joy, and the rewards of hard work.
  • vaco - This means "speech" or "words," highlighting the importance of communication and expression in human relationships.
  • 'śaṁsīt - This translates to "may be praised" or "may be celebrated," indicating a desire for recognition and appreciation.
  • kāvyaḥ - Referring to "poetry" or "literature," this word emphasizes the beauty of artistic expression and the power of words.
  • kaviḥ - This means "poet" or "sage," representing wisdom, creativity, and the ability to convey profound truths through art.

In summary, this mantra speaks to the journey of life, filled with numerous choices and the importance of moving forward with the guidance of divine forces (the Aśvins). It emphasizes the nurturing aspects of life (represented by the calf) and the sweetness that comes from meaningful communication and artistic expression. The invocation of the Aśvins suggests that we seek their blessings as we navigate our paths.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as:

  • Decision-Making: In moments of uncertainty, one can reflect on the multitude of choices available and seek guidance from higher powers or mentors, akin to the Aśvins.
  • Personal Growth: Embracing the nurturing aspect of life, like a calf, encourages individuals to recognize their potential and seek opportunities for development.
  • Communication: The emphasis on speech and expression highlights the importance of clear communication in relationships, fostering understanding and connection.
  • Creativity: Engaging in artistic pursuits can lead to personal fulfillment and a deeper understanding of oneself and the world, echoing the role of the poet in society.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to embrace the journey of life with awareness, creativity, and a sense of connection to the divine, allowing us to navigate our paths with wisdom and joy.
