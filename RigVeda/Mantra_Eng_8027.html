अयā सोमः सुखृत्यया महश्चिदभ्यवर्धत | मन्दान उद वृषायते ||
ayā somaḥ sukṛtyayā mahaś cid abhy avardhata | mandāna ud vṛṣāyate ||
  • ayā: This word can be translated as "this" or "here." It indicates a presence or a specific focus on something significant.
  • somaḥ: Soma refers to a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration. It symbolizes the essence of life and spiritual awakening.
  • sukṛtyayā: This term means "by good deeds" or "through virtuous actions." It emphasizes the importance of righteousness and ethical conduct in life.
  • mahaś: Translated as "great" or "mighty," this word signifies something of immense power or significance.
  • cid: This word means "consciousness" or "awareness." It represents the ultimate reality or the essence of being.
  • abhy avardhata: This phrase can be understood as "has grown" or "has expanded." It indicates a process of development or enhancement.
  • mandāna: This term refers to "the one who is gentle" or "the one who is pleasing." It suggests a quality of softness or kindness.
  • ud: Meaning "up" or "out," this word indicates elevation or rising.
  • vṛṣāyate: This term can be translated as "to be nourished" or "to be strengthened." It implies growth and sustenance.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine essence (Soma) to grow and flourish through virtuous actions and consciousness. It emphasizes the importance of good deeds and the nurturing of one's inner self to achieve greatness and awareness.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Development: By focusing on virtuous actions and self-awareness, individuals can cultivate a more fulfilling and meaningful life.
  • Community Service: Engaging in good deeds within the community can lead to collective growth and harmony, reflecting the essence of the mantra.
  • Spiritual Practices: Meditative practices that enhance consciousness and promote kindness can help individuals connect with their inner selves and the divine.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for personal and collective growth through virtuous actions and heightened awareness. It serves as a reminder that by nurturing our inner selves and engaging in good deeds, we can elevate our consciousness and contribute positively to the world around us.
