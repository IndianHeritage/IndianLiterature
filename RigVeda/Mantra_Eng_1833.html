एष वः स्तोमो मरुतो नमस्वान हृदा तष्टो मनसा धायि देवाः |उपेम आ याता मनसा जुषाणा यूयं हि ष्ठा नमसा इद वृधासः ||
eṣa vaḥ stomo maruto namasvān hṛdā taṣṭo manasā dhāyi devāḥ | upem ā yāta manasā juṣāṇā yūyaṁ hi ṣṭhā namasa id vṛdhāsaḥ ||
  • eṣa vaḥ stomo maruto namasvān: This phrase can be translated as "This hymn is for you, O Maruts, who are worthy of our reverence." The Maruts are storm deities in Vedic literature, representing the forces of nature. In real life, we can relate this to the respect we give to natural phenomena, such as storms and winds, acknowledging their power and influence on our lives.
  • hṛdā taṣṭo manasā dhāyi devāḥ: This translates to "May the gods, who are established in our hearts and minds, grant us their blessings." This emphasizes the connection between the divine and human consciousness. In everyday life, this can be seen in how people seek guidance and inspiration from their beliefs or spiritual practices, often turning to meditation or prayer to connect with a higher power.
  • upem ā yāta manasā juṣāṇā: This means "May you come to us, O divine beings, who are pleasing to our minds." It reflects the desire for divine presence and support in our lives. In practical terms, this can be likened to seeking positive influences, whether through relationships, mentors, or inspirational figures who uplift our spirits and guide us toward our goals.
  • yūyaṁ hi ṣṭhā namasa id vṛdhāsaḥ: This translates to "You indeed stand firm, worthy of our reverence." This line acknowledges the strength and stability of the divine forces. In real life, this can be interpreted as recognizing the importance of resilience and steadfastness in our own lives, whether in personal challenges or societal issues, and the need to honor those who embody these qualities.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the relationship between humans and the divine, highlighting the importance of reverence, connection, and the influence of natural forces. In real life, it serves as a reminder to respect nature, seek divine guidance, and cultivate resilience. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during times of uncertainty or when facing challenges, encouraging individuals to find strength in their beliefs and the support of the community around them.
