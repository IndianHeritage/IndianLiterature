मन्त्र: द्वाव इमौ वातौ वाता आ सिन्धोर आ परावतः |दक्षं ते अन्य आ वातु पराण्यो वातु यद रपः ||
dvāv imau vātau vāta ā sindhor ā parāvataḥ | dakṣaṁ te anya ā vātu parānyo vātu yad rapaḥ ||
  • dvāv imau vātau: This phrase translates to "These two winds." In the context of the mantra, it refers to the dual forces of nature, symbolizing the balance between opposing elements.
  • vāta ā sindhor ā parāvataḥ: This means "the wind that comes from the ocean and the wind that comes from the mountains." Here, the ocean represents vastness and depth, while the mountains symbolize stability and strength. Together, they illustrate the interconnectedness of different natural forces.
  • dakṣaṁ te anya ā vātu: This translates to "May you be skilled and may you protect." It emphasizes the importance of skillfulness and protection in our endeavors, suggesting that we should strive for excellence while also safeguarding our surroundings.
  • parānyo vātu yad rapaḥ: This means "May the winds bring us what is beneficial." It highlights the desire for positive outcomes and the importance of being open to the blessings that come our way through nature's forces.

  • Real-Life Example: Consider the duality of life, such as work and rest. Just as the winds from the ocean and mountains balance each other, we must balance our professional and personal lives to achieve harmony.
  • Application: In a corporate setting, leaders can apply the principles of this mantra by fostering a culture that values both productivity (the winds of the ocean) and employee well-being (the winds of the mountains). This balance can lead to a more engaged and motivated workforce.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for balance, skillfulness, and receptiveness to positive influences. In our daily lives, we can apply these teachings by striving for equilibrium in our actions, being skilled in our pursuits, and remaining open to the opportunities that nature and life present to us. By doing so, we can navigate the complexities of existence with grace and wisdom.
