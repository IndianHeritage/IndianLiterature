धूनुथ द्यां पर्वतान दाशुषे वसु नि वो वनां जिहते यामनो भियाः |कोपयथा पृथिवीं पृष्णिमातरः शुभे यद उग्राः पृषतीर अयुग्ध्वम् ||
dhūnutha dyām parvatān dāśuṣe vasu ni vo vanā jihate yāmano bhiyā | kopayatha pṛthivīm pṛśnimātaraḥ śubhe yad ugrāḥ pṛṣatīr ayugdhvam ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "O you who dwell in the heavens, shake the mountains and bring forth the wealth of the earth. May the fierce ones, who are the protectors of the earth, not be angry with us, and may we not be disturbed by their wrath."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dhūnutha: shake or move
    • dyām: heavens or sky
    • parvatān: mountains
    • dāśuṣe: for the wealth or prosperity
    • vasu: wealth or good things
    • ni: to or for
    • vo: you (plural)
    • vanā: forest or wilderness
    • jihate: may it not be disturbed
    • yāmano: fierce or powerful
    • bhiyā: with fear or wrath
    • kopayatha: do not be angry
    • pṛthivīm: earth
    • pṛśnimātaraḥ: protectors or guardians
    • śubhe: auspicious or good
    • yad: that
    • ugrāḥ: fierce or terrible
    • pṛṣatīr: protectors or guardians
    • ayugdhvam: may not be disturbed
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra can be seen as a call for harmony between nature and humanity. For instance, in modern environmental movements, there is a strong emphasis on respecting and protecting the earth's resources, akin to the plea for the mountains and earth to provide wealth without anger.
    • In community gatherings, such as festivals or rituals, invoking the blessings of nature and the earth can be a way to ensure prosperity and peace, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal life, when facing challenges, one might recite this mantra to seek strength and protection from the forces that seem overwhelming, much like asking for the fierce protectors of the earth to be benevolent.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of the natural forces and the plea for harmony and prosperity. It emphasizes the relationship between humanity and nature, urging respect and balance. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in environmental conservation efforts, community rituals, and personal challenges, reminding us of the importance of maintaining a respectful and harmonious relationship with the earth and its resources. By acknowledging the fierce protectors of nature, we cultivate a sense of responsibility and reverence towards the environment, which is crucial in today's world.
