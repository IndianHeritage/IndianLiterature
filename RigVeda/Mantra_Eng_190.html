ये शुभ्राः घोरवर्पसः सुख्षत्रासो रिषादसः | मरुद्भिः अग्न आ गहि ||
ye śubhrā ghoravarpasaḥ sukṣatrāso riśādasaḥ | marudbhir agna ā gahi ||
  • Translation: "Those who are pure, terrifying, and powerful, who are the protectors of the good, come to us, O Agni, with the Maruts."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ye: Those who
    • śubhrā: Pure or auspicious
    • ghoravarpasaḥ: Terrifying or formidable
    • sukṣatrāso: Protectors of the good
    • riśādasaḥ: Powerful or strong
    • marudbhir: With the Maruts (the storm deities)
    • agna: O Agni (the fire god)
    • ā gahi: Come to us
  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The term "śubhrā" signifies purity, which can be related to the idea of moral integrity in our lives. For example, a person who acts with honesty and kindness embodies this quality.
    • "Ghoravarpasaḥ" refers to the formidable nature of certain forces in life, such as challenges or adversities. For instance, facing a serious illness can be seen as a "ghoravarpasaḥ" experience.
    • "Sukṣatrāso" emphasizes the importance of protection and support. In real life, this can be likened to the role of family and friends who stand by us during tough times.
    • The Maruts represent the storm deities, symbolizing the dynamic and sometimes chaotic forces of nature. They remind us that life is full of ups and downs, much like the changing weather.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra highlights the duality of life—purity and terror, strength and vulnerability. It teaches us to seek the divine for guidance and support in navigating life's challenges.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to cultivate purity in thoughts and actions while being prepared to face formidable challenges with courage.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can inspire collective strength, urging people to come together to support one another in times of need, much like how communities rally during natural disasters.
