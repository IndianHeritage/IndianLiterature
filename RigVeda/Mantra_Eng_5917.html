मन्त्र: परो मातृया तन्मा वृधाना न ते महित्वम् अन्व अशन्वन्ति |उभे ते विद्म रजसी पृथिव्या विष्णो देव त्वं परमस्य विटसे ||
paro mātrayā tanvā vṛdhāna na te mahitvam anv aśnuvanti | ubhe te vidma rajasī pṛthivyā viṣṇo deva tvam paramasya vitse ||
  • paro mātrayā: This phrase can be translated as "by the supreme measure" or "by the highest standard." It suggests a sense of transcendence and the idea of something that is beyond ordinary comprehension.
  • tanvā vṛdhāna: This means "growing in body" or "expanding in form." It indicates the idea of growth, both physically and spiritually, suggesting that the divine or the supreme essence is ever-expanding.
  • na te mahitvam anv aśnuvanti: This translates to "they do not attain your greatness." It implies that the true essence of divinity is beyond what can be fully grasped or attained by mortals.
  • ubhe te vidma: This means "we know both." It suggests a duality of understanding, recognizing both the material and spiritual aspects of existence.
  • rajasī pṛthivyā: This can be interpreted as "the energy of the earth." It signifies the connection between the divine and the earthly realm, emphasizing the importance of grounding spiritual knowledge in the physical world.
  • viṣṇo deva: This refers to "O Lord Vishnu," a principal deity in Hinduism representing preservation and protection. It highlights the divine aspect of the mantra.
  • tvam paramasya vitse: This translates to "you are the essence of the supreme." It acknowledges the divine as the ultimate reality, the source of all existence.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of understanding the divine as both transcendent and immanent. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing the duality of existence—both the spiritual and the material. In real life, this can be applied in various ways, such as in personal growth, where one seeks to expand their understanding and consciousness while remaining grounded in reality. It also serves as a reminder of the limitations of human understanding in grasping the full magnitude of the divine. This mantra encourages individuals to seek a balance between their spiritual aspirations and their earthly responsibilities, fostering a holistic approach to life.
