वियद् आहेर अध तविषो विश्वे देवासो अक्रमुः |विदन् मृगस्य तां अमः ||
vi yad aher adha tviṣo viśve devāso akramuḥ | vidan mṛgasya tām̐ amaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vi - indeed
    • yad - whatever
    • aher - is born
    • adha - below
    • tviṣo - of the light
    • viśve - all
    • devāso - the divine beings
    • akramuḥ - are unbroken
    • vidan - knowing
    • mṛgasya - of the creature
    • tām̐ - that
    • amaḥ - I seek
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine light that permeates existence.
    • The phrase "vi yad aher adha tviṣo" suggests that whatever is born from the light, whether above or below, is part of a greater whole.
    • The mention of "viśve devāso akramuḥ" indicates that all divine beings are unbroken and interconnected, emphasizing unity in diversity.
    • Finally, "vidan mṛgasya tām̐ amaḥ" reflects a personal quest for understanding and connection with this divine essence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In nature, we see how all creatures, from the smallest insects to the largest mammals, are part of an ecosystem. Each plays a role in maintaining balance, much like the divine beings mentioned in the mantra.
    • In human relationships, the mantra can be applied to the idea of empathy and understanding. Recognizing that we are all connected can lead to more compassionate interactions.
    • In spiritual practices, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek a deeper connection with the universe, encouraging meditation and reflection on one's place within the larger tapestry of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the unity of all existence and the divine light that connects us.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding of their relationship with the world around them.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can guide us towards greater harmony with nature, deeper connections with others, and a more profound spiritual journey.
