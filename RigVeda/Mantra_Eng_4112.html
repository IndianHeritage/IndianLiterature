प्र वो मरुतस्तविषा उदन्यवो वायोवृद्धो अश्वयुजः परिज्रयः |सं विद्युतां दधति वाशति तृतः स्वरन्त्य आपो 'वनां परिज्रयः ||
pra vo marutas taviṣā udanyavo vayovṛdho aśvayujaḥ parijrayaḥ | saṁ vidyutā dadhati vāśati tritaḥ svaranty āpo 'vanā parijrayaḥ ||
  • pra vo marutas: This phrase can be translated as "to you, O Maruts," where "Maruts" refers to the storm deities or the winds in Vedic literature. They symbolize strength and power.
  • taviṣā udanyavo: This means "with great strength and energy." It emphasizes the vigor and dynamism of the Maruts, suggesting that they are not just powerful but also active and lively.
  • vayovṛdho aśvayujaḥ: This translates to "the one who increases the winds and is connected with the horses." In Vedic culture, horses symbolize speed, power, and nobility, indicating that the Maruts are associated with both the elements and the forces of nature.
  • parijrayaḥ: This term means "those who are surrounded" or "those who are encircled." It suggests a sense of community or collective strength among the Maruts.
  • saṁ vidyutā dadhati: This can be interpreted as "they hold the lightning." Lightning is often associated with divine power and energy, indicating that the Maruts possess not only physical strength but also spiritual potency.
  • vāśati tritaḥ: This phrase means "they dwell in the third realm." The "third realm" can be understood as a spiritual or higher plane of existence, suggesting that the Maruts have a connection to the divine.
  • svaranty āpo 'vanā: This translates to "the waters flow and the winds blow." It signifies the interconnectedness of natural elements, highlighting the importance of harmony in nature.
  • parijrayaḥ: Reiterating the idea of being surrounded, it emphasizes the collective nature of the Maruts and their role in the cosmic order.

Conclusion: The mantra reflects the power and dynamism of the Maruts, emphasizing their role as forces of nature that bring energy, strength, and harmony. In real life, this can be applied to the understanding of natural phenomena and the importance of respecting and living in harmony with nature. The Maruts symbolize the balance between strength and community, reminding us that true power lies not just in individual might but in collective unity and respect for the environment. This mantra can inspire individuals to appreciate the forces of nature and to strive for balance in their own lives, fostering a sense of community and interconnectedness with the world around them.
