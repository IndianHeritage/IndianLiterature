अभिख्यā नो मघवन नादहमानान् सखे बोधि वसुपते सखीनाम् |रणं कृत्हि रणकृत सत्यशुष्माभक्ते चिद् आ भजाऽ राये अस्मान् ||
abhikhyā no maghavan nādhamānān sakhe bodhi vasupate sakhīnām | raṇaṁ kṛdhi raṇakṛt satyaśuṣmābhakte cid ā bhajā rāye asmān ||
  • abhikhyā no maghavan: This phrase can be interpreted as a call for recognition or acknowledgment from the divine. "Maghavan" refers to a deity associated with wealth and abundance, often identified with the god of prosperity, Indra.
  • nādhamānān sakhe: This part translates to "to those who are not deprived, O friend." It emphasizes the importance of community and support among friends and allies, suggesting that those who are prosperous should help those who are not.
  • bodhi vasupate sakhīnām: "Bodhi" means wisdom or enlightenment, while "vasupate" refers to the lord of wealth. This line suggests that true wealth comes from wisdom and understanding, and that friends should share this wisdom with one another.
  • raṇaṁ kṛdhi raṇakṛt: This phrase means "engage in battle, O warrior." It signifies the importance of taking action and being proactive in the face of challenges. It encourages individuals to fight for their rights and well-being.
  • satyaśuṣmābhakte: This can be interpreted as "with the devotion of truth." It highlights the significance of sincerity and truthfulness in one's actions and intentions.
  • cid ā bhajā rāye asmān: This final part translates to "may we be blessed with wealth." It is a prayer for prosperity and abundance, emphasizing the desire for material and spiritual wealth.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates the essence of community, wisdom, and proactive engagement in life. It teaches us that true wealth is not just material but also comes from knowledge and mutual support among friends. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In business, fostering a culture of collaboration and support among colleagues can lead to greater success and innovation.
  • In personal relationships, being there for friends and sharing wisdom can strengthen bonds and create a supportive network.
  • In community service, helping those who are less fortunate aligns with the mantra's call for recognizing and uplifting others.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek wisdom, support one another, and actively engage in the pursuit of both material and spiritual wealth.
