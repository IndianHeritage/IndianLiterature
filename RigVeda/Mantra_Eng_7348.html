विध्मा हि ते पुरा वयम् अग्ने पितुर यथावसः |अधाऽ ते सुम्नम ईमहे ||
vidmā hi te purā vayam agne pitur yathāvasaḥ | adhā te sumnam īmahe ||
  • vidmā hi te purā: This phrase translates to "We know you, O Agni, from ancient times." Here, "vidmā" means "we know," and "purā" refers to "ancient" or "from the past." Agni, the fire god, is revered in Vedic literature as a symbol of knowledge and enlightenment.
  • vayam agne pitur yathāvasaḥ: This part means "We are your children, O Agni, as per your wish." "Vayam" means "we," "agne" refers to "O Agni," and "pitur" means "of the father." This line emphasizes the relationship between humans and Agni, portraying him as a guiding force or protector.
  • adhā te sumnam īmahe: This translates to "We invoke your auspiciousness." "Adhā" means "here," "te" means "your," "sumnam" refers to "auspiciousness," and "īmahe" means "we invoke." This line expresses a desire for blessings and positive outcomes from Agni.

  • Significance: This mantra highlights the importance of Agni as a divine entity that connects humans to the spiritual realm. It reflects the reverence for fire as a purifying and transformative force in life.
  • Real-life Application: In modern contexts, this mantra can be applied to rituals and ceremonies where fire plays a central role, such as weddings, homas (fire sacrifices), and other auspicious events. It serves as a reminder of the need for guidance, protection, and blessings in our endeavors.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking knowledge, guidance, and auspiciousness from a higher power. It encourages individuals to acknowledge their roots and the divine forces that influence their lives, fostering a sense of connection and gratitude.
