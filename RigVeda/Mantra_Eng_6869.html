यद अग्ने दिविजा अस्य अप्सुजा वा सहस्कृत |तं त्वा गीरभिर हवामहे ||
yad agne divijā asy apsujā vā sahaskṛta | taṁ tvā gīrbhir havāmahe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • divijā: born in the heavens
    • asy: of this
    • apsujā: of the waters
    • vā: or
    • sahaskṛta: made powerful
    • taṁ: him
    • tvā: you
    • gīrbhir: with hymns
    • havāmahe: we invoke
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a divine intermediary between humans and the gods.
    • The phrase "yad agne divijā" refers to the divine nature of Agni, emphasizing his celestial origin.
    • "apsujā vā sahaskṛta" suggests the connection between Agni and the waters, indicating the importance of both elements in sustaining life.
    • The invocation "taṁ tvā gīrbhir havāmahe" expresses the desire to call upon Agni through hymns, highlighting the significance of ritual and devotion in Vedic culture.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern rituals, such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies, fire is often used as a sacred element, symbolizing purity and the presence of divine energy.
    • Agni's role as a mediator can be seen in various cultural practices where offerings are made to fire, representing the connection between the material and spiritual worlds.
    • The importance of water in agriculture and sustenance reflects the idea of balance between fire (energy) and water (life), which is crucial for human survival.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the reverence for Agni as a vital force in the universe, embodying the connection between the divine and the earthly realms.
    • It teaches us the importance of invoking higher powers through sincere devotion and the acknowledgment of natural elements that sustain life.
    • In contemporary life, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate a sense of gratitude and respect for nature, recognizing the interconnectedness of all elements in our existence.
