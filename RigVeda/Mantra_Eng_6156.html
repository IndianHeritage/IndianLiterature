तवेद इन्द्र प्रणीतीषूत प्रशस्तिर अद्रिवः |यज्ञो वितन्तसाय्यः ||
taved indra praṇītiṣūta praśastir adrivaḥ | yajño vitantasāyyaḥ ||
  • taved: This word can be interpreted as "your" or "of you," indicating possession or connection.
  • indra: Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and the king of gods. He symbolizes strength and power.
  • praṇītiṣūta: This term can be broken down into "praṇīti," meaning "to lead" or "to guide," and "sūta," which refers to "born" or "produced." Together, it suggests the idea of being guided or led by a powerful force.
  • praśastir: This word means "praise" or "commendation," indicating recognition of excellence or virtue.
  • adrivaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "without obstacles" or "unhindered," suggesting a state of freedom or ease.
  • yajño: Referring to a "sacrifice" or "offering," this term is central to Vedic rituals, symbolizing the act of giving and the importance of community and connection with the divine.
  • vitantasāyyaḥ: This can be understood as "expanding" or "growing," indicating a sense of development or increase, particularly in the context of offerings or sacrifices.

The mantra can be translated as: "Your guidance, O Indra, is praiseworthy and unhindered; the sacrifice expands." This reflects a deep reverence for Indra as a guiding force in life, emphasizing the importance of divine support in our endeavors.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership and Guidance: Just as Indra is seen as a leader, individuals in positions of authority can draw inspiration from this mantra to lead with wisdom and strength, ensuring their actions are praiseworthy and beneficial to their community.
  • Overcoming Obstacles: The idea of being "without obstacles" can motivate individuals to pursue their goals with determination, knowing that divine support is available to help them navigate challenges.
  • Community and Sacrifice: The emphasis on yajña (sacrifice) highlights the importance of community and selflessness. In modern contexts, this can translate to charitable actions, volunteering, or any form of contribution that benefits others.

Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of divine guidance and the value of selfless actions in achieving personal and communal growth. It encourages individuals to seek strength in leadership, overcome challenges, and engage in acts of service, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in their lives.
