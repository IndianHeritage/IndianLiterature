हंसः शुचिषद वसुर अन्तरिक्षसद धोतā वेदिषद अतिथिर दुरोणसत् | नृषद वरसद ऋतसद व्योमसद अभ्जा गोजा ऋतजा अद्रिजा ऋतम् ||
haṁsaḥ śuciṣad vasur antarikṣasad dhotā vediṣad atithir duroṇasat | nṛṣad varasad ṛtasad vyomasad abjā gojā ṛtajā adrijā ṛtam ||
  • haṁsaḥ: The term "haṁsa" refers to a swan or a bird, often symbolizing purity and spiritual wisdom. In real life, swans are known for their grace and beauty, representing the pursuit of higher knowledge and enlightenment.
  • śuciṣad: This word means "pure" or "clean." In our daily lives, purity can be seen in actions, thoughts, and intentions. For example, maintaining a clean environment or practicing honesty in our dealings reflects the essence of purity.
  • vasur: "Vasur" translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." In contemporary terms, this can relate to financial stability or abundance in life. For instance, a successful business or a fulfilling career can be seen as manifestations of "vasur."
  • antarikṣasad: This word means "of the sky" or "celestial." It signifies the vastness and the infinite possibilities that lie beyond our immediate perception. In real life, this can be likened to aspirations and dreams that reach beyond our current circumstances.
  • dhotā: "Dhotā" refers to a "mover" or "shaker," someone who initiates change. In a practical sense, this could be a leader or an innovator who inspires others to take action, such as a community organizer or a social activist.
  • vediṣad: This term means "one who knows" or "wise." In our lives, wisdom can be found in mentors, teachers, or even through personal experiences that guide us in making informed decisions.
  • atithir: "Atithir" means "guest" or "stranger." This highlights the importance of hospitality and kindness towards others, reflecting how we treat those who come into our lives, whether they are friends or strangers.
  • duroṇasat: This word can be interpreted as "strong" or "resilient." In real life, resilience is crucial in overcoming challenges, such as facing personal hardships or societal issues with strength and determination.
  • nṛṣad: "Nṛṣad" refers to "human" or "mankind." This emphasizes our shared humanity and the importance of community and connection among people.
  • varasad: This term means "blessing" or "grace." In everyday life, we often seek blessings in various forms, such as support from family, friends, or even good fortune in our endeavors.
  • ṛtasad: "Ṛtasad" signifies "truth" or "order." This can be related to the principles of justice and fairness in society, where truth prevails in our actions and interactions.
  • vyomasad: This word means "of the heavens" or "cosmic." It reflects the interconnectedness of all beings and the universe, reminding us of our place in the larger scheme of existence.
  • abjā: "Abjā" translates to "born of water," symbolizing life and nourishment. In real life, water is essential for survival, representing the sustenance we need to thrive.
  • gojā: This term means "born of the earth" or "earthly." It signifies our connection to nature and the importance of caring for our environment, as it provides us with resources and sustenance.
  • ṛtajā: "Ṛtajā" refers to "born of truth." This emphasizes the significance of living authentically and aligning our actions with our values and beliefs.
  • adrijā: "Adrijā" means "born of the mountains," symbolizing strength and stability. In real life, mountains can represent challenges we must overcome, and the strength we gain from facing them.
  • ṛtam: This final term signifies "the ultimate truth" or "cosmic order." It serves as a reminder of the importance of living in harmony with the universe and understanding the deeper truths of existence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound concepts of purity, wisdom, resilience, and interconnectedness. It encourages us to strive for higher knowledge, maintain purity in our actions, and recognize the importance of community and truth in our lives.
