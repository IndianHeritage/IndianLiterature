उद īरयन्त वायुभिर वाश्रासः पृष्णिमातरः |धुक्षन्त पिप्युषीम इषम् ||
ud īrayanta vāyubhir vāśrāsaḥ pṛśnimātaraḥ | dhukṣanta pipyuṣīm iṣam ||
  • ud īrayanta: This phrase can be translated as "they who are uplifting" or "those who elevate." It signifies the act of raising or promoting something to a higher state.
  • vāyubhir: This word means "by the winds" or "with the help of the winds." In a broader sense, it can represent the forces of nature or the divine energies that facilitate movement and change.
  • vāśrāsaḥ: This term refers to "the ones who are powerful" or "the strong." It denotes strength and capability, often associated with deities or forces that govern the universe.
  • pṛśnimātaraḥ: This can be interpreted as "the ones who are connected to the earth" or "those who are grounded." It emphasizes the importance of being rooted and stable while also being elevated.
  • dhukṣanta: This word means "they who churn" or "those who agitate." It suggests a process of transformation, where something is stirred or mixed to create a new outcome.
  • pipyuṣīm: This term translates to "the sweet nectar" or "the essence." It symbolizes the rewards or benefits that come from the process of churning or transformation.
  • iṣam: This word means "the arrow" or "the goal." It represents direction, purpose, and the aim of one's efforts.

The mantra as a whole can be understood as a call to harness the uplifting and transformative powers of nature and the divine. It emphasizes the importance of strength, grounding, and the process of churning to achieve one's goals and attain the sweet rewards of life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the winds elevate and the earth grounds, individuals can seek to uplift themselves through education, self-improvement, and resilience while remaining connected to their roots and values.
  • Teamwork: In a collaborative environment, the collective strength of a team can uplift each member. The process of working together (churning) can lead to innovative ideas (nectar) that help achieve common goals (the arrow).
  • Environmental Awareness: Recognizing the power of nature (winds) and the importance of being grounded (earth) can inspire actions that promote sustainability and respect for the environment.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, transformation, and purpose. It encourages individuals to harness the uplifting forces of nature and their own inner strength to achieve their goals while remaining grounded in their values. The significance of this mantra lies in its applicability to personal development, teamwork, and environmental consciousness, making it relevant in various aspects of life.
