उषो यद अद्य भानुना विं द्वाराव ऋणवो दिवः | प्र नो यच्छताद अवृकम पृथु छर्दिः प्र देवी गोमतीर इषः ||
uṣo yad adya bhānunā vi dvārāv ṛṇavo divaḥ | pra no yacchatād avṛkam pṛthu cchardiḥ pra devi gomatīr iṣaḥ ||
  • uṣo: This word refers to the dawn or the early morning light, symbolizing new beginnings and the dispelling of darkness.
  • yad: This translates to "that" or "which," indicating a connection to what follows.
  • adya: Meaning "today," it emphasizes the present moment and the importance of the current day.
  • bhānunā: This means "by the sun" or "with the light," highlighting the role of the sun in bringing light and clarity.
  • vi dvārāv: This phrase translates to "at the two doors," which can symbolize choices or opportunities that lie ahead.
  • ṛṇavo: This word means "debt" or "obligation," suggesting a sense of responsibility or the consequences of actions.
  • divaḥ: Meaning "of the heavens," it connects the earthly experience with the divine or spiritual realm.
  • pra no: This translates to "may it come to us," indicating a desire for blessings or positive outcomes.
  • yacchatād: Meaning "may it grant," it expresses a wish for the fulfillment of desires or needs.
  • avṛkam: This word means "the wolf," which can symbolize challenges or adversities that one may face.
  • pṛthu: Meaning "broad" or "vast," it signifies abundance and the richness of life.
  • cchardiḥ: This translates to "the expulsion" or "the release," indicating the removal of negativity or obstacles.
  • pra devi: Meaning "to the goddess," it invokes divine feminine energy and protection.
  • gomatīr: This word means "the one who possesses wealth," symbolizing prosperity and abundance.
  • iṣaḥ: This translates to "the power" or "the strength," indicating the ability to overcome challenges.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of new beginnings, the importance of light, and the duality of choices we face in life. It emphasizes the need for divine blessings to navigate through challenges and to seek abundance and prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during moments of decision-making, such as starting a new venture, entering a new phase in life, or seeking guidance in overcoming obstacles. By invoking the energies of dawn and the divine, individuals can find clarity, strength, and the courage to face their challenges, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and prosperous life.
