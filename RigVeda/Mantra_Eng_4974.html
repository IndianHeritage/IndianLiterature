अजाश्वः पशुपा वाजपस्त्यो धियं जिन्वो भुवने विश्वे अर्पितः |अष्ट्राम पूषा शिथिराम उद्वरिवृजत संचक्षाणो भुवना देव ईयते ||
ajāśvaḥ paśupā vājapastyo dhiyaṁjinvo bhuvane viśve arpitaḥ | aṣṭrām pūṣā śithirām udvarīvṛjat saṁcakṣāṇo bhuvanā deva īyate ||
  • ajāśvaḥ: This term can be broken down into "aja" meaning "unborn" or "eternal" and "aśva" meaning "horse." In Vedic literature, the horse symbolizes strength, speed, and vitality. Thus, "ajāśvaḥ" refers to an eternal source of energy or life force.
  • paśupā: This word translates to "the protector of animals" or "the lord of all creatures." It signifies a divine presence that nurtures and safeguards all living beings, emphasizing the interconnectedness of life.
  • vājapastyo: "Vāja" means "strength" or "power," and "pasty" refers to "the one who possesses." This term can be interpreted as the one who possesses great strength or power, often associated with the divine forces that govern the universe.
  • dhiyaṁjinvo: This word can be understood as "the one who inspires thoughts" or "the one who enlightens." It highlights the importance of wisdom and intellect in guiding human actions and decisions.
  • bhuvane viśve arpitaḥ: "Bhuvane" means "in the world," "viśve" refers to "all," and "arpitaḥ" means "offered." This phrase suggests that all beings in the world are offered to the divine, indicating a sense of surrender and devotion.
  • aṣṭrām: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is powerful" or "the one who wields weapons." It signifies strength and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • pūṣā: "Pūṣā" is a deity associated with nourishment and sustenance. This term emphasizes the importance of nurturing and providing for all living beings.
  • śithirām: This word means "the one who is steady" or "the one who is stable." It represents the idea of stability and balance in life.
  • udvarīvṛjat: This term can be understood as "the one who expands" or "the one who elevates." It signifies growth and the potential for development.
  • saṁcakṣāṇo: This word means "the one who sees all" or "the all-knowing." It highlights the omniscience of the divine and the ability to perceive the truth in all situations.
  • bhuvanā deva: "Bhuvanā" refers to "the world" or "the universe," and "deva" means "god" or "divine." This phrase emphasizes the divine presence that permeates the universe.
  • īyate: This term means "is invoked" or "is worshipped." It signifies the act of devotion and the importance of connecting with the divine.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine protection, strength, wisdom, and nourishment. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of surrendering to a higher power. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of uncertainty or challenge, reminding individuals to seek strength and guidance from the divine. It encourages a sense of community and responsibility towards all living beings, promoting harmony and balance in the world. By invoking this mantra, one can cultivate a deeper connection with the universe and foster a sense of peace and stability in their life.
