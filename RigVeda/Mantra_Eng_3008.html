पुराणम् ओकः सख्यं शिवं वां युवोर नराः द्रविणं जह्नाव्यां |पुनः कृत्वानाः सख्याः शिवानि मध्वा मदेम सह नू समानाः ||
purāṇam okaḥ sakhyaṁ śivaṁ vāṁ yuvor narā draviṇaṁ jahnāvyām | punaḥ kṛṇvānāḥ sakhyā śivāni madhvā madema saha nū samānāḥ ||
  • Purāṇam: This word translates to "ancient" or "eternal." It signifies the timeless nature of certain truths and values that have been passed down through generations.
  • Okaḥ: This means "home" or "shelter." It represents a place of safety and comfort, where individuals can find peace and support.
  • Sakhyaṁ: This translates to "friendship" or "companionship." It emphasizes the importance of relationships and bonds between individuals.
  • Śivaṁ: This word means "auspicious" or "good." It is often associated with the divine and signifies positivity and well-being.
  • Vāṁ: This means "your" or "of you," indicating a personal connection and the importance of individual relationships.
  • Yuvor: This translates to "of the young" or "youthful." It highlights the vitality and energy of youth, as well as the potential for growth and development.
  • Narā: This means "men" or "humans." It signifies the collective human experience and the shared journey of life.
  • Draviṇaṁ: This translates to "wealth" or "resources." It emphasizes the importance of material and spiritual abundance in life.
  • Jahnāvyām: This word can be interpreted as "through the flow" or "in the current." It suggests the dynamic nature of life and the need to adapt to changing circumstances.
  • Punaḥ: This means "again" or "once more." It signifies renewal and the cyclical nature of existence.
  • Kṛṇvānāḥ: This translates to "creating" or "making." It emphasizes the active role individuals play in shaping their lives and environments.
  • Sakhyā: This is a reiteration of "friendship," reinforcing the theme of companionship and support.
  • Śivāni: This word means "auspicious" or "good," similar to "śivaṁ," further emphasizing positivity.
  • Madhvā: This translates to "sweetness" or "honey." It symbolizes the sweetness of life and the joy found in relationships and experiences.
  • Madema: This means "let us enjoy" or "may we partake." It encourages a collective experience of joy and fulfillment.
  • Saha: This translates to "together" or "with." It emphasizes unity and collaboration among individuals.
  • Nū: This means "now" or "at this moment," highlighting the importance of the present.
  • Samānāḥ: This translates to "equal" or "similar." It signifies the idea of equality and shared experiences among individuals.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of friendship, unity, and the pursuit of auspiciousness in life. It highlights the importance of relationships and the collective journey of humanity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to foster strong bonds among friends, family, and communities. It encourages individuals to create a supportive environment where everyone can thrive together, celebrating the sweetness of life and the joy of shared experiences. By embracing these values, we can cultivate a harmonious and prosperous society, where each individual contributes to the collective well-being.
