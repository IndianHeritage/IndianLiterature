या ओजिष्टस तं आ भर पवमान श्रवाय्यम् |यः पञ्च चर्षणीर अभि रयिं येन वनामहै ||
ya ojiṣṭhas tam ā bhara pavamāna śravāyyam | yaḥ pañca carṣaṇīr abhi rayiṁ yena vanāmahai ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ya: who
    • ojiṣṭhas: the most powerful or the strongest
    • tam: that
    • ā: to
    • bhara: bring or carry
    • pavamāna: the purifying or the wind
    • śravāyyam: fame or glory
    • yaḥ: who
    • pañca: five
    • carṣaṇīr: the realms or the regions
    • abhi: towards
    • rayiṁ: wealth or prosperity
    • yena: by whom
    • vanāmahai: we invoke or we call upon
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer invoking the most powerful forces of nature, represented by the wind (pavamāna), to bring forth prosperity and glory.
    • The reference to "five realms" can symbolize the five elements (earth, water, fire, air, and space) that are essential for life and harmony.
    • It emphasizes the importance of invoking these natural forces to achieve success and well-being in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for favorable weather conditions (wind, rain) to ensure a good harvest, reflecting the invocation of natural forces for prosperity.
    • In personal development, individuals may seek to harness their inner strength (ojiṣṭhas) to overcome challenges, similar to invoking powerful energies for success.
    • Communities often come together to celebrate festivals that honor nature, invoking blessings for wealth and harmony, akin to the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of human life with nature and the importance of invoking its powers for prosperity and success.
    • It teaches us to respect and harness the natural forces around us, reminding us that our well-being is tied to the environment.
    • In modern life, this mantra can inspire practices such as sustainability, mindfulness, and community cooperation to achieve collective prosperity.
