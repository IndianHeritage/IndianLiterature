ऊर्ध्वो गन्धर्वो अधि नाके अस्ताद विश्वा रूपा प्रातिचक्षाणो अस्य |भानुः शुक्रेण शोचिषा व्य अद्यौत प्रारूरुचद रोदसी मातरा शुचिः ||
ūrdhvo gandharvo adhi nāke asthād viśvā rūpā praticakṣāṇo asya | bhānuḥ śukreṇa śociṣā vy adyaut prārūrucad rodasī mātarā śuciḥ ||
  • ūrdhvo: This word translates to "upward" or "above." It signifies elevation, both in a physical and spiritual sense.
  • gandharvo: Refers to "Gandharvas," celestial musicians and beings associated with the heavens. They symbolize creativity and the arts.
  • adhi: Means "above" or "over," indicating a position of superiority or transcendence.
  • nāke: Translates to "in the sky" or "in the heavens," suggesting a divine or elevated realm.
  • asthād: Means "he stood" or "he established," indicating a sense of permanence or stability.
  • viśvā: This word means "all" or "universal," representing inclusivity and the entirety of existence.
  • rūpā: Translates to "forms" or "shapes," indicating the diversity of creation.
  • praticakṣāṇo: Means "perceiving" or "seeing," suggesting awareness and understanding.
  • asya: Refers to "of this" or "of it," indicating possession or belonging.
  • bhānuḥ: Translates to "the sun" or "light," symbolizing knowledge, clarity, and enlightenment.
  • śukreṇa: Means "with brightness" or "with purity," indicating a state of being clear and untainted.
  • śociṣā: Refers to "radiance" or "brilliance," emphasizing the illuminating quality of knowledge.
  • vy adyaut: Translates to "shone forth" or "illuminated," indicating the emergence of light or knowledge.
  • prārūrucad: Means "it shone" or "it became bright," suggesting a transformation or awakening.
  • rodasī: Refers to "the two worlds" or "the horizons," symbolizing the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • mātarā: Means "mothers" or "nurturers," indicating the nurturing aspect of creation.
  • śuciḥ: Translates to "pure" or "sacred," emphasizing the importance of purity in thought and action.

This mantra from the Rigveda speaks to the interconnectedness of the universe, the elevation of consciousness, and the importance of purity and creativity. It highlights the role of divine beings and the nurturing aspects of existence, suggesting that through awareness and understanding, one can achieve enlightenment and harmony.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In creative pursuits, such as music, art, or writing, one can draw inspiration from the idea of Gandharvas, aiming to elevate their work to a higher level of expression.
  • In personal development, the emphasis on purity and clarity can guide individuals to cultivate a mindset that fosters growth and enlightenment.
  • In relationships, recognizing the nurturing aspect of "mothers" can inspire individuals to create supportive and loving environments for others.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the potential for elevation and enlightenment in our lives. It encourages us to seek knowledge, embrace creativity, and maintain purity in our thoughts and actions, ultimately leading to a harmonious existence that connects the earthly with the divine.
