अस्माकं इन्द्रावरुणा भरे-भरे पुरोयोधा भवतं कृष्ट्योजसा |यद वां हवन्त उभये अध स्पृधि नरस् तोकस्य तनयस्य सातिषु ||
asmākam indrāvaruṇā bhare-bhare puroyodhā bhavataṁ kṛṣṭyojasā | yad vāṁ havanta ubhaye adha spṛdhi naras tokasya tanayasya sātiṣu ||
  • Translation: "May Indra and Varuna, the powerful deities, bestow upon us strength and valor. Whatever offerings we make, may they be accepted by both, for the benefit of our progeny and their descendants."
  • Meaning of Key Terms:
    • Indra: The king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
    • Varuna: The god of cosmic order and water, representing truth and moral authority.
    • Bhare-bhare: A call for abundance and strength, indicating a desire for support and resources.
    • Puroyodhā: Refers to the warriors or champions, emphasizing the importance of courage and valor in life.
    • Havanta: The offerings made to the deities, signifying devotion and the act of seeking blessings.
    • Naras: Refers to humans or mankind, highlighting the connection between the divine and human existence.
    • Tokasya: Meaning progeny or descendants, indicating the importance of family and lineage.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family gathering, when blessings are sought for the younger generation, this mantra can be recited to invoke divine support for their success and well-being.
    • During a community event, leaders may use this mantra to inspire courage and unity among participants, emphasizing the importance of collective strength.
    • In personal challenges, individuals can reflect on the essence of this mantra to seek inner strength and moral guidance, drawing inspiration from the qualities of Indra and Varuna.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine support in achieving strength and valor in life.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of human efforts and divine blessings, reminding us that our actions and intentions matter.
    • By invoking the blessings of Indra and Varuna, individuals and communities can foster a sense of responsibility towards their progeny and future generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra serves as a reminder of the values of strength, courage, and moral integrity in our lives.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal challenges to community endeavors, reinforcing the idea that seeking divine guidance can lead to positive outcomes.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to act with purpose and integrity, ensuring that our actions benefit not only ourselves but also our descendants.
