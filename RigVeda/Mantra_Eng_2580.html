दीदिवांसम अपूर्व्यं वस्वीभिर अस्य धीतिभिः |ऋक्वाणो अग्निम इन्दते होतारं विश्वतीं विश्वाम् ||
dīdivāṁsam apūrvyaṁ vasvībhir asya dhītibhiḥ | ṛkvāṇo agnim indhate hotāraṁ viśpatiṁ viśām ||
  • Mantra: "dīdivāṁsam apūrvyaṁ vasvībhir asya dhītibhiḥ | ṛkvāṇo agnim indhate hotāraṁ viśpatiṁ viśām ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "With the brilliance of the heavens, the unique and extraordinary, through the wisdom of the wise, the seers invoke the fire, the lord of offerings, the master of the community."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • dīdivāṁsam: Refers to the brilliance or radiance of the heavens, symbolizing divine light and knowledge.
    • apūrvyaṁ: Means something unique or extraordinary, indicating the special nature of the divine or spiritual experiences.
    • vasvībhir: Relates to the wise or learned individuals, those who possess knowledge and understanding.
    • asya: Means 'of this' or 'of this world', indicating the connection to our earthly existence.
    • dhītibhiḥ: Refers to wisdom or intellect, emphasizing the importance of knowledge in invoking the divine.
    • ṛkvāṇo: Refers to the seers or sages who are knowledgeable in the Vedic hymns.
    • agnim: Means 'fire', which is a symbol of purity, transformation, and divine presence.
    • indhate: Means 'to invoke' or 'to kindle', indicating the act of calling upon the divine.
    • hotāraṁ: Refers to the priest or the one who offers sacrifices, symbolizing the act of devotion.
    • viśpatiṁ: Means 'lord of the community', indicating a leader or protector of the people.
    • viśām: Refers to 'the community' or 'the people', emphasizing the collective aspect of society.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of invoking divine fire can be seen in various cultures where fire rituals are performed to seek blessings, such as in Hindu yajnas or in Native American ceremonies.
    • The importance of wisdom and knowledge is reflected in modern education systems, where learning is valued as a means to invoke progress and enlightenment in society.
    • Community leaders today, much like the 'viśpatiṁ', play a crucial role in guiding and protecting their communities, whether in local governance or social initiatives.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the interplay between divine wisdom, community, and the act of invocation. It teaches us that through knowledge and collective effort, we can connect with the divine and seek guidance for the betterment of society. In real life, this mantra encourages individuals to value wisdom, engage in community service, and participate in rituals that foster a sense of unity and purpose. It serves as a reminder that the pursuit of knowledge and the invocation of higher powers can lead to transformative experiences for both individuals and communities.
