यश्चिद्धित्थाभगः शशमानः पुरा निदः |अद्वेषो हस्तयोर्दधे ||
yaś cid dhi ta itthā bhagaḥ śaśamānaḥ purā nidaḥ | adveṣo hastayor dadhe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaś: fame, glory
    • cid: who, that
    • dhi: indeed, certainly
    • ta: this, that
    • itthā: thus, in this manner
    • bhagaḥ: fortune, prosperity
    • śaśamānaḥ: shining, radiant
    • purā: in the past, formerly
    • nidaḥ: hidden, concealed
    • adveṣo: non-enmity, absence of hatred
    • hastayor: of the hands
    • dadhe: to place, to establish
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the essence of fame and prosperity that is inherent in a person who embodies non-enmity.
    • The phrase "yaś cid dhi ta itthā bhagaḥ" suggests that true glory and fortune are achieved through virtuous actions and a peaceful disposition.
    • The mention of "adveṣo hastayor dadhe" emphasizes the importance of placing non-enmity in our actions, particularly in our dealings with others.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a team member who promotes collaboration and avoids conflict often earns respect and recognition, embodying the essence of this mantra.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who practice kindness and understanding, rather than harboring grudges, tend to build stronger, more fulfilling connections.
    • Communities that foster peace and cooperation, rather than division and hostility, often thrive and prosper, reflecting the mantra's teachings.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of non-enmity and virtuous conduct as the foundation for achieving true fame and prosperity.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions and attitudes towards others can shape our destiny and the quality of our relationships.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious existence, both personally and collectively.
