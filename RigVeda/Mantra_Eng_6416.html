ते नो भद्रेण शर्मणा युष्माकं नाव वसवः |अति विश्वानि दुरिता पिपर्तन ||
te no bhadreṇa śarmaṇā yuṣmākaṁ nāvā vasavaḥ | ati viśvāni duritā pipartana ||
  • te no bhadreṇa: This phrase translates to "may we be blessed with auspiciousness." The term "bhadreṇa" signifies something that is beneficial or good. In real life, this can be related to seeking positive outcomes in various aspects of life, such as health, relationships, and career.
  • śarmaṇā: This word means "with protection" or "with comfort." It emphasizes the importance of safety and security in our lives. For example, in a workplace, having a supportive environment can lead to better productivity and job satisfaction.
  • yuṣmākaṁ: This translates to "your" or "yours," indicating a collective aspect. It suggests that the blessings and protection are not just for an individual but for a community or group. In real life, this can be seen in community efforts where people come together for a common cause, such as disaster relief or social welfare.
  • nāvā vasavaḥ: This phrase means "may the forces of prosperity and abundance be with us." "Nāvā" refers to a boat or vessel, symbolizing a means of navigating through life's challenges. "Vasavaḥ" refers to the divine forces that bring wealth and prosperity. This can be related to how businesses seek to thrive and grow, navigating through market challenges with the help of good fortune and strategic planning.
  • ati viśvāni duritā: This translates to "may we overcome all obstacles." "Ati" means "beyond" or "over," while "duritā" refers to difficulties or misfortunes. In real life, this can be applied to personal challenges, such as overcoming health issues or financial difficulties, where resilience and determination play a crucial role.
  • pipartana: This word means "to remove" or "to dispel." It signifies the act of clearing away negativity or obstacles. In practical terms, this can be seen in mental health practices where individuals work to dispel negative thoughts and emotions through mindfulness and therapy.

Conclusion: The mantra "te no bhadreṇa śarmaṇā yuṣmākaṁ nāvā vasavaḥ | ati viśvāni duritā pipartana ||" encapsulates a profound wish for collective well-being, protection, and the ability to overcome challenges. Its significance lies in the universal desire for auspiciousness and prosperity, not just for oneself but for the community as a whole. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals and groups to seek positive outcomes, support one another, and navigate through life's difficulties with resilience and hope. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our lives and the importance of working together towards common goals.
