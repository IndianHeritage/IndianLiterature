बृहस्पते प्रतिमे देवतामिहि मित्रो वा यद वरुणो वासि पूषा |आदित्यैर्वा यद वसुभिर मरुत्वान स पर्जन्यं शान्तनवे वृषाय ||
bṛhaspate prati me devatām ihi mitro vā yad varuṇo vāsi pūṣā | ādityair vā yad vasubhir marutvān sa parjanyaṁ śaṁtanave vṛṣāya ||
  • Mantra: "bṛhaspate prati me devatām ihi mitro vā yad varuṇo vāsi pūṣā | ādityair vā yad vasubhir marutvān sa parjanyaṁ śaṁtanave vṛṣāya ||"
  • Translation: "O Bṛhaspati, may you come to me with the divine beings; whether you are Mitra or Varuṇa, or Pūṣa, or the Adityas, or the Vasus, or the Maruts, may you bring forth rain for the flourishing of the earth."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Bṛhaspati: The deity of wisdom and eloquence, often associated with the planet Jupiter.
    • Prati: Towards or in the direction of.
    • Me: To me.
    • Devatām: Divine beings or deities.
    • Ihi: Here or come here.
    • Mitra: The god of friendship and alliances.
    • Varuṇa: The god of cosmic order and water.
    • Pūṣā: The deity associated with nourishment and prosperity.
    • Ādityair: Referring to the Adityas, a group of solar deities.
    • Vasubhir: Referring to the Vasus, deities of abundance and material wealth.
    • Marutvān: Referring to the Maruts, storm deities associated with wind and rain.
    • Sa: He or that.
    • Parjanyaṁ: The rain god or the one who brings rain.
    • Śaṁtanave: For the flourishing or prosperity.
    • Vṛṣāya: For the earth or for the crops.
  • Significance: This mantra is a prayer for divine assistance in bringing rain and prosperity to the earth. It acknowledges various deities associated with nature and abundance, emphasizing the interconnectedness of divine forces in sustaining life.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In agriculture, farmers often pray for rain during dry seasons, recognizing the importance of water for crop growth. This mantra can be recited during rituals to invoke the blessings of rain deities.
    • In personal life, individuals may seek wisdom and guidance from Bṛhaspati when making important decisions, reflecting the need for divine insight in everyday matters.
    • Communities may come together to perform rituals during festivals to honor these deities, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose in seeking prosperity and well-being.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the vital role that nature and divine forces play in our lives. By acknowledging and honoring these deities, we cultivate a sense of gratitude and responsibility towards the environment, promoting harmony and sustainability in our actions.
