तं सानाव् अधि जामयो हरिं हिन्वन्त्य अद्रिभिः |हर्यतम् भूरिकक्षसम् ||
taṁ sānāv adhi jāmayo hariṁ hinvanty adribhiḥ | haryatam bhūricakṣasam ||
  • taṁ - This word refers to "that" or "him," indicating a focus on a specific entity or divine presence.
  • sānāv - This term means "together" or "in harmony," suggesting unity and collective effort.
  • adhi - Translated as "above" or "over," it implies a higher state or elevated position.
  • jāmayo - This word means "born of" or "produced by," indicating creation or emergence.
  • hariṁ - Referring to "the divine" or "the Lord," it signifies a higher power or deity.
  • hinvanty - This term means "to remove" or "to drive away," suggesting the act of dispelling negativity or obstacles.
  • adribhiḥ - Meaning "by the mountains," it symbolizes strength, stability, and the natural world.
  • haryatam - This word translates to "most joyful" or "the best," indicating a state of happiness or fulfillment.
  • bhūricakṣasam - Meaning "of great vision" or "with abundant sight," it refers to wisdom, insight, and understanding.

  • Meaning: The mantra speaks of a divine presence that is born from unity and harmony, elevated above all, and capable of removing obstacles. It emphasizes the importance of collective effort in achieving a higher state of joy and wisdom.
  • Real-life Example: In a team setting, when individuals come together with a common goal, they can overcome challenges and achieve success. For instance, in a community project aimed at environmental conservation, the collective efforts of individuals can lead to significant positive changes, reflecting the essence of this mantra.

  • Conclusion: This mantra signifies the power of unity and the divine's role in guiding and uplifting humanity. It teaches us that by working together and seeking higher wisdom, we can overcome obstacles and attain joy. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork in professional settings, community initiatives, and personal relationships, where collaboration and shared vision lead to greater achievements.
