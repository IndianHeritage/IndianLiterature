यत् पार्जन्य कणिक्रदात् स्तनयन हंसि दुष्कृतः |प्रतीदं विश्वं मोदते यत् किं च पृथिव्याम् अधि ||
yat parjanya kanikradat stanayan haṁsi duṣkṛtaḥ | pratīdaṁ viśvam modate yat kiṁ ca pṛthivyām adhi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yat - that which
    • parjanya - the rain or the clouds
    • kanikradat - when it thunders
    • stanayan - producing sound
    • haṁsi - you (the rain) destroy
    • duṣkṛtaḥ - the evil deeds
    • pratīdaṁ - indeed
    • viśvam - the whole universe
    • modate - rejoices
    • yat - that which
    • kiṁ ca - and whatever
    • pṛthivyām - on the earth
    • adhi - above
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of rain and its ability to cleanse the world of negativity.
    • The thunder of the clouds signifies a call to attention, reminding us of the natural forces that govern our lives.
    • When the rain falls, it not only nourishes the earth but also symbolizes the removal of evil deeds, suggesting a purification process.
    • The phrase "the whole universe rejoices" indicates that the effects of rain are felt universally, impacting all living beings.
    • It emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature and humanity, where the well-being of one affects the other.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the arrival of rain is celebrated as it brings life to crops, ensuring food security for communities.
    • During monsoon seasons, festivals are held in many cultures to honor the rain gods, reflecting gratitude for the sustenance provided.
    • In urban areas, rain can symbolize a fresh start, washing away pollution and revitalizing the environment.
    • On a personal level, individuals often find solace in the sound of rain, associating it with peace and reflection, allowing for emotional cleansing.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of rain as a life-giving force that not only nourishes the earth but also purifies it from negativity.
    • It serves as a reminder of the cyclical nature of life, where destruction and creation coexist, and the importance of respecting natural elements.
    • In real life, it encourages us to appreciate the environment and recognize our role in maintaining its balance.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us that just as rain brings joy and sustenance, we too can contribute positively to the world around us.
