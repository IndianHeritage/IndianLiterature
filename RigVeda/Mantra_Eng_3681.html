जुषस्वाग्न इळया सजोषा यतमानो रश्मिभिः सूर्यस्य |जुषस्व नः समिधं जातवेद आ च देवान हविरद्याय वक्षि ||
juṣasvāgna iḻayā sajoṣā yatamāno raśmibhiḥ sūryasya | juṣasva naḥ samidhaṁ jātaveda ā ca devān haviradyāya vakṣi ||
  • juṣasvāgna: This term can be broken down into "juṣasva," which means "to enjoy" or "to take pleasure in," and "agna," which refers to "fire" or "the divine fire." In a real-life context, this can be likened to the warmth and comfort we derive from a fire, symbolizing the nurturing aspect of life.
  • iḻayā: This word translates to "with the offering" or "with the oblation." It signifies the act of giving or offering something valuable, akin to how we might offer our time or resources to help others in our community.
  • sajoṣā: This term means "with joy" or "with happiness." It emphasizes the importance of joy in our actions, suggesting that when we engage in activities, especially those involving others, we should do so with a positive and joyful spirit.
  • yatamāno: This translates to "striving" or "making an effort." It reflects the human condition of working hard towards our goals, similar to how students study diligently for exams or how athletes train for competitions.
  • raśmibhiḥ: This word means "with rays" or "with light." It symbolizes enlightenment and knowledge, akin to how we seek guidance and wisdom in our lives, whether through education, mentorship, or personal experiences.
  • sūryasya: This translates to "of the sun." The sun is often seen as a source of life and energy, representing vitality and growth. In real life, we can relate this to the importance of sunlight for agriculture and our overall well-being.
  • naḥ: This means "to us." It indicates a collective aspect, emphasizing community and togetherness, much like how families and friends come together to support one another.
  • samidhaṁ: This term refers to "the fuel" or "the offering." It signifies the resources we provide for growth and sustenance, similar to how we invest in our education or relationships to foster development.
  • jātaveda: This translates to "the one who knows all births" or "the all-knowing." It represents wisdom and understanding, akin to how we seek knowledge from various sources to navigate life's challenges.
  • ā ca: This means "and also," indicating an addition or continuation of thought, much like how we build upon our experiences and learnings in life.
  • devān: This translates to "the gods" or "divine beings." It signifies the higher powers or forces that guide us, similar to how we might look to role models or mentors for inspiration.
  • haviradyāya: This term means "for the offering" or "for the sacrifice." It emphasizes the importance of giving back, akin to how individuals contribute to charitable causes or community service.
  • vakṣi: This translates to "may you speak" or "may you express." It highlights the importance of communication and expression in our lives, similar to how we share our thoughts and feelings with others to foster understanding.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of joy, effort, and community in our lives. It encourages us to engage in our actions with a sense of purpose and positivity, while also recognizing the importance of knowledge and divine guidance. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in our personal relationships, professional endeavors, and community involvement. By embodying the principles of joy, effort, and giving, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling existence, both for ourselves and those around us.
