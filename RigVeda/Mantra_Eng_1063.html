उद यंयमीति सवितेव बाहू उभे सिचौ यतते भीमा ऋञ्जन |उच शुक्रम् अटकम् अजते सिमस्मान नवामातृभ्यो वसनां जहाति ||
ud yaṁyamīti saviteva bāhū ubhe sicau yatate bhīma ṛñjan | uc chukram atkam ajate simasmān navā mātṛbhyo vasanā jahāti ||
  • ud yaṁyamīti saviteva bāhū: This phrase can be translated as "As the Sun (Savita) rises, it stretches its arms." Here, "Savita" refers to the Sun, symbolizing energy, life, and illumination. The act of stretching arms signifies the spreading of light and warmth, which is essential for life on Earth.
  • ubhe sicau yatate bhīma ṛñjan: This part means "Both the powerful and the radiant strive for greatness." It emphasizes the duality of strength and purity, suggesting that both qualities are necessary for achieving success and overcoming challenges in life.
  • uc chukram atkam ajate simasmān: This translates to "The bright and pure one (Chukra) does not perish." It signifies the eternal nature of purity and truth, suggesting that while material things may fade, the essence of purity remains everlasting.
  • navā mātṛbhyo vasanā jahāti: This means "The new (Navā) leaves behind the old (Mātṛbhyo)." It indicates the cycle of renewal and transformation, where new ideas and generations emerge, leaving behind outdated beliefs and practices.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of life, energy, and the importance of both strength and purity. It teaches us that as we strive for greatness, we must embrace both our powerful and radiant qualities. In real life, this can be seen in various scenarios, such as in personal development, where individuals work on their strengths while also nurturing their inner purity and truth. The mantra also highlights the importance of renewal, encouraging us to let go of outdated beliefs and embrace new ideas for growth. Overall, it serves as a reminder of the balance between strength and purity, and the continuous cycle of renewal in our lives.
