यं त्वं अग्ने समदहस तं उ निर वापयाऽ पुनः |कियाम्भव् अत्र रोहतु पाकदूर्वा व्यलकशा ||
yaṁ tvam agne samadahas tam u nir vāpayā punaḥ | kiyāmbv atra rohatu pākadūrvā vyalkaśā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: whom
    • tvam: you
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • samadahas: who burns evenly
    • tam: that
    • u: and
    • nir: without
    • vāpayā: with the offering
    • punaḥ: again
    • kiyāmbv: how much
    • atra: here
    • rohatu: may it grow
    • pākadūrvā: the sacred grass
    • vyalkaśā: with abundance
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The phrase "whom you, O Agni, burn evenly" suggests a request for Agni's consistent and purifying presence in our lives.
    • The term "without offering" indicates a desire for Agni's blessings even in the absence of material offerings, emphasizing the importance of intention over materialism.
    • "How much here may it grow" reflects a yearning for growth and prosperity in our lives, particularly in spiritual and material aspects.
    • The mention of "sacred grass" symbolizes purity and the natural world, suggesting that our growth should be rooted in nature and spirituality.
    • "With abundance" indicates a wish for plentiful blessings and a fruitful life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied during rituals or ceremonies where fire is used, symbolizing the purification of intentions and actions.
    • In a modern context, it can be interpreted as a reminder to seek balance and consistency in our endeavors, whether in personal relationships, work, or spiritual practices.
    • For instance, when starting a new project, one might recite this mantra to invoke the spirit of Agni for guidance and support, ensuring that the project is rooted in good intentions and a desire for growth.
    • In environmental practices, the reference to "sacred grass" can inspire individuals to engage in sustainable living, promoting growth that respects nature.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of intention, purity, and the desire for growth in both spiritual and material realms.
    • It serves as a reminder that true abundance comes from a balanced approach to life, where our actions are aligned with our values and respect for nature.
    • In essence, it encourages individuals to cultivate a life that is rich in purpose, connection, and harmony with the world around them.
