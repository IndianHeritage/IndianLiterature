तं वश्चराथा वयं वसत्यास्तं न गावो नक्षन्त इद्धम् ||
taṁ vaś carāthā vayaṁ vasatyāstaṁ na gāvo nakṣanta iddham ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • taṁ: that
    • vaś: under control
    • carāthā: moving or wandering
    • vayaṁ: we
    • vasatyā: dwelling or residing
    • astaṁ: that which is
    • na: not
    • gāvo: cows (symbolizing wealth and prosperity)
    • nakṣanta: to be lost or to stray
    • iddham: indeed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the idea of control and stability in life.
    • It emphasizes that we, as individuals, should strive to maintain control over our actions and surroundings.
    • The reference to "cows" symbolizes wealth, prosperity, and nourishment, indicating that we should not let our resources stray or be lost.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to the management of resources. For instance, a company should ensure that its assets (like finances and human resources) are well-managed and not wasted.
    • In personal life, it can relate to maintaining control over one's emotions and actions. For example, during stressful situations, one should strive to remain calm and not let anger or frustration lead to poor decisions.
    • In relationships, it signifies the importance of nurturing connections and not allowing misunderstandings to cause rifts.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for mindfulness and control in various aspects of life.
    • It teaches us to be aware of our actions and their consequences, ensuring that we do not lose sight of our goals and values.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages a harmonious existence where we manage our resources wisely, maintain our relationships, and navigate life's challenges with composure.
