कथा दाशेमाग्नये कास्मै देवजुष्टोच्यते भामिने गीः |यो मर्त्येष्व अमृत ऋतावा होतā यजिष्ठ इति कृत्नोति देवान् ||
kathā dāśemāgnaye kāsmai devajuṣṭocyate bhāmine gīḥ | yo martyeṣv amṛta ṛtāvā hotā yajiṣṭha it kṛṇoti devān ||
  • kathā dāśemāgnaye: This phrase can be translated as "How shall we offer to Agni?" Here, Agni represents the divine fire, a significant element in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and the divine presence.
  • kāsmai devajuṣṭocyate: This means "To whom is the offering made?" It emphasizes the importance of intention and the recipient of the offerings, highlighting the relationship between humans and the divine.
  • bhāmine gīḥ: Translated as "the radiant hymn," this refers to the praises or hymns sung in honor of the deities, particularly Agni, who is often invoked for blessings and guidance.
  • yo martyeṣv amṛta ṛtāvā: This part means "He who is immortal among mortals." It signifies the divine aspect that exists within the human realm, suggesting that there is a connection between the mortal and the immortal.
  • hotā yajiṣṭha it kṛṇoti devān: This translates to "The one who offers the best sacrifices to the gods." It highlights the importance of devotion and the act of giving in spiritual practices, suggesting that true offerings come from the heart.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of intention, devotion, and the connection between the mortal and the divine. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to various aspects of life, such as:

  • Spiritual Practices: When engaging in rituals or prayers, one should focus on the intention behind the offerings, ensuring they are made with sincerity and respect.
  • Community and Relationships: Just as offerings are made to the divine, we can apply this principle to our relationships, where genuine acts of kindness and support strengthen bonds.
  • Personal Growth: Recognizing the divine within ourselves encourages self-reflection and the pursuit of higher ideals, motivating individuals to strive for personal excellence.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the sacredness of our actions and the importance of connecting with the divine through our intentions and offerings.
