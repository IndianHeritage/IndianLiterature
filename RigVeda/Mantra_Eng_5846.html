मन्त्र: क्रत्वः समः दीनता प्रतिपं जगमा शुचि |मृळा सुख्षत्र मृळय ||
kratvaḥ samaha dīnatā pratīpaṁ jagamā śuce | mṛḻā sukṣatra mṛḻaya ||
  • Kratvaḥ: This term refers to the concept of action or effort. It emphasizes the importance of taking initiative and being proactive in life.
  • Samaha: This word signifies balance or harmony. It suggests that actions should be taken with a sense of equilibrium, ensuring that one does not go to extremes.
  • Dīnatā: This translates to humility or modesty. It highlights the value of being humble in one's actions and interactions with others.
  • Pratīpaṁ: This term means opposition or resistance. It indicates that challenges and obstacles are a natural part of life that one must face.
  • Jagamā: This word means to attain or achieve. It signifies the goal of reaching a desired state or outcome through one's efforts.
  • Śuce: This translates to purity or cleanliness. It emphasizes the importance of maintaining purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions.
  • Mṛḻā: This term refers to gentleness or softness. It suggests that one should approach situations with kindness and compassion.
  • Sukṣatra: This means auspicious or favorable. It indicates that actions taken with the right intentions can lead to positive outcomes.
  • Mṛḻaya: This word means to soothe or calm. It implies the importance of finding peace and tranquility amidst life's challenges.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of living a balanced and humble life while facing challenges with a pure heart and gentle approach. It teaches us that through our actions, we can achieve our goals while maintaining harmony and kindness. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal relationships, professional environments, and community interactions. By embodying these principles, individuals can foster a more peaceful and productive existence, ultimately contributing to the well-being of themselves and those around them.
