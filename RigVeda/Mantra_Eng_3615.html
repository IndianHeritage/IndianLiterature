तत् सु नः सविता भगो वरुणो मित्रो आर्यमाः | इन्द्रो नो राधसात् गमत् ||
tat su naḥ savitā bhago varuṇo mitro aryamā | indro no rādhasā gamat ||
  • tat: This word translates to "that" or "that which." It signifies a connection to a higher reality or divine essence.
  • su: Meaning "good" or "well," it emphasizes positivity and auspiciousness.
  • naḥ: This means "our," indicating a sense of community and collective identity.
  • savitā: Referring to the Sun God, Savitā represents illumination, life, and energy. In real life, the sun is essential for growth, both in nature and in human endeavors.
  • bhago: This word means "wealth" or "prosperity." It suggests the abundance that comes from divine blessings.
  • varuṇo: Varuṇa is the god of cosmic order and water. He symbolizes the moral and ethical aspects of life, reminding us of the importance of righteousness.
  • mitro: Meaning "friend," it signifies companionship and support, highlighting the importance of relationships in our lives.
  • aryamā: This refers to a deity associated with the sky and the heavens, representing higher aspirations and spiritual goals.
  • indro: Indra is the king of gods and represents strength, power, and leadership. In real life, this can be seen in the qualities of effective leaders who inspire and protect their communities.
  • no: This means "us," reinforcing the collective aspect of the mantra.
  • rādhasā: This word can be interpreted as "with abundance" or "with prosperity," indicating a desire for a fruitful life.
  • gamat: Meaning "may come" or "may arrive," it expresses a wish or prayer for the arrival of blessings.

Conclusion: This mantra is a prayer for divine blessings and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of community, ethical living, and the pursuit of higher goals. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as seeking guidance in leadership roles, fostering friendships, and striving for personal and collective growth. By invoking these divine qualities, individuals can cultivate a life filled with abundance, righteousness, and meaningful connections. The mantra serves as a reminder to align oneself with higher principles and to seek the support of the divine in all endeavors.
