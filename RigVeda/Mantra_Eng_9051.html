नाहं तं वेद य इति ब्रवीत्य अदेवयून समरणे जगहन्वान् |यदावाख्यात समरणम् ऋघावद आद इद ध मे वृषभा प्र ब्रुवन्ति ||
nāhaṁ taṁ veda ya iti bravīty adevayūn samaraṇe jaghanvān | yadāvākhyat samaraṇam ṛghāvad ād id dha me vṛṣabhā pra bruvanti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nāhaṁ: I do not
    • taṁ: that
    • veda: know
    • ya: who
    • iti: thus
    • bravīty: speaks
    • adevayūn: of the divine
    • samaraṇe: in the battle
    • jaghanvān: the one who is strong
    • yad: what
    • āvākhyat: has been declared
    • samaraṇam: the battle
    • ṛghāvad: of the powerful
    • ād: and
    • id: this
    • dha: indeed
    • me: to me
    • vṛṣabhā: the strong one
    • pra: indeed
    • bruvanti: they speak
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a sense of humility and recognition of one's limitations in understanding the divine or the greater truths of existence.
    • The speaker acknowledges that they do not know who truly speaks of the divine in the context of battle, suggesting a deeper philosophical inquiry into the nature of knowledge and power.
    • The reference to "the strong one" can be interpreted as a metaphor for inner strength or resilience that one must cultivate in the face of challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive environment, such as a workplace, individuals may often feel overwhelmed by the knowledge and skills of others. This mantra reminds us to focus on our own strengths and contributions rather than comparing ourselves to others.
    • In personal relationships, understanding that we may not fully grasp another person's perspective can lead to greater empathy and communication, fostering stronger connections.
    • In times of conflict or struggle, such as during a health crisis or personal loss, this mantra encourages individuals to seek inner strength and resilience, recognizing that true power comes from within.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for humility, self-awareness, and the pursuit of inner strength.
    • It teaches us that while we may not have all the answers or understand the divine fully, we can still find power and resilience within ourselves.
    • This mantra is applicable in various real-life scenarios, including personal growth, conflict resolution, and the pursuit of knowledge, reminding us to embrace our journey with humility and strength.
