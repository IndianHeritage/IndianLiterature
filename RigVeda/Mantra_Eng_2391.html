याद्राध्यं वरुणो योनिम अप्यम अनिशितं निमिषि जर्भुराणः |विश्वो मार्ताण्डो व्रजम् आ पशुर गात स्थशो जन्मानि सविता व्य आकः ||
yādrādhya1ṁ varuṇo yonim apyam aniśitaṁ nimiṣi jarbhurāṇaḥ | viśvo mārtāṇḍo vrajam ā paśur gāt sthaśo janmāni savitā vy ākaḥ ||
  • yādrādhyaṁ: This term can be understood as "whatever is desired" or "whatever is sought after." It signifies the aspirations and goals that individuals pursue in life.
  • varuṇo: Referring to Varuna, the Vedic deity associated with water, cosmic order, and truth. Varuna represents the moral and ethical aspects of life, emphasizing the importance of righteousness.
  • yonim: This word means "womb" or "source." It symbolizes the origin of life and the nurturing aspect of nature, akin to how a mother nurtures her child.
  • apyam: Translated as "the waters" or "the essence," it refers to the vital elements that sustain life, highlighting the importance of water and nourishment.
  • aniśitaṁ: This term means "unwavering" or "constant." It suggests stability and reliability, qualities that are essential in both nature and human relationships.
  • nimiṣi: Meaning "to blink" or "to close the eyes," it can symbolize the transient nature of life and the importance of being aware of the present moment.
  • jarbhurāṇaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who brings forth" or "the creator." It signifies the creative forces in the universe that lead to the manifestation of life.
  • viśvo: Meaning "all" or "universal," it emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the universe.
  • mārtāṇḍo: Referring to the sun, this term symbolizes light, knowledge, and enlightenment. The sun is a source of energy and life, representing the pursuit of knowledge and truth.
  • vrajam: This means "to the flock" or "to the community." It highlights the importance of social bonds and collective well-being.
  • ā paśur: Translated as "the animal" or "the living being," it signifies the essence of life and the importance of all creatures in the ecosystem.
  • gāt: Meaning "to go" or "to move," it represents progress and the journey of life.
  • sthaśo: This term means "standing" or "stable," indicating the need for a solid foundation in life.
  • janmāni: Referring to "births" or "lives," it signifies the cycles of life and the importance of each existence.
  • savitā: This term refers to the sun as a life-giver, emphasizing the nurturing aspect of the sun in sustaining life.
  • vy ākaḥ: Meaning "to shine" or "to illuminate," it symbolizes the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of ethical living, nurturing, and the pursuit of knowledge. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to foster a sense of community, promote environmental sustainability, and encourage individuals to seek truth and righteousness in their actions. It serves as a reminder of the cyclical nature of life and the importance of nurturing relationships, both with nature and within society.
