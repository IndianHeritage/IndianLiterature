विष्वे देवाः नो अद्य स्वस्तये वैश्वानरो वसुर अग्निः स्वस्तये |देवाः अवन्तु ऋभवः स्वस्तये स्वस्ति नो रुद्रः पातु अम्हसः ||
viśve devā no adyā svastaye vaiśvānaro vasur agniḥ svastaye | devā avantv ṛbhavaḥ svastaye svasti no rudraḥ pātv aṁhasaḥ ||
  • Translation: "May all the gods, today, bring us well-being; may the universal god Agni, the benefactor, bring us well-being. May the gods protect us; may Rudra grant us well-being and protect us from harm."
  • Meaning of Key Terms:
    • Viśve Devā: Refers to all gods, symbolizing the collective divine forces that govern the universe.
    • Svastaye: Means well-being or auspiciousness, indicating a desire for health, prosperity, and harmony.
    • Vaiśvānaro: A form of Agni, representing the universal fire that sustains life and purifies.
    • Avantv: To protect or safeguard, emphasizing the need for divine protection.
    • Rudra: A fierce aspect of the divine, often associated with storms and transformation, but also with healing and protection.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, people often invoke blessings from various deities for the success of their endeavors, similar to how this mantra seeks blessings from all gods.
    • During a fire ceremony (Homa), Agni is worshipped as the divine witness and purifier, reflecting the significance of Vaiśvānaro Agni in this mantra.
    • In times of crisis, individuals often turn to prayer or meditation, seeking protection and well-being, akin to the invocation of Rudra for safeguarding against harm.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine blessings for well-being and protection. It highlights the interconnectedness of all divine forces and their role in ensuring harmony in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during rituals, personal prayers, or community events where collective well-being is desired. By invoking the blessings of the gods, individuals and communities can foster a sense of unity, purpose, and protection against adversities.
