स नो मन्द्राभिर अध्वरे जिह्वाभिर यज महः |आ देवान वक्षि यक्षि च ||
sa no mandrābhir adhvare jihvābhir yajā mahaḥ | ā devān vakṣi yakṣi ca ||
  • sa no mandrābhir adhvare jihvābhir yajā mahaḥ
    • sa no: "May that (which is) ours" - This phrase indicates a collective aspiration or desire for something beneficial.
    • mandrābhir: "by the hymns" - Refers to the sacred verses or mantras that are recited during rituals.
    • adhvare: "in the sacrifice" - This signifies the act of offering or performing a ritual, which is central to Vedic practices.
    • jihvābhir: "with our tongues" - This emphasizes the importance of speech and communication in invoking divine blessings.
    • yajā: "we worship" - A call to engage in worship or reverence towards the divine.
    • mahaḥ: "the great" - Refers to the greatness of the divine or the significance of the act of worship.
  • ā devān vakṣi yakṣi ca
    • ā: "to" - Indicates direction or intention towards the divine.
    • devān: "the gods" - Refers to the various deities worshipped in the Vedic tradition.
    • vakṣi: "may you speak" - A request for the divine to communicate or respond to the worshippers.
    • yakṣi: "may you grant" - A plea for the gods to bestow their blessings or fulfill the desires of the devotees.
    • ca: "and" - Connects the two actions of speaking and granting, indicating a holistic approach to worship.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of collective worship and the power of spoken words in rituals. It highlights the aspiration for divine blessings through hymns and the act of sacrifice. In real life, this can be seen in various religious practices where communities come together to pray, chant, or perform rituals, seeking guidance and support from a higher power. The significance lies in the belief that through sincere worship and communication with the divine, individuals and communities can attain peace, prosperity, and fulfillment of their desires. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine, encouraging individuals to engage in spiritual practices that foster unity and reverence.
