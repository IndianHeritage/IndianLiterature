नाभा नाभिं न आ ददेद चक्षुश्चित सूर्ये सचा |कवेर अपत्यं आ दूहे ||
nābhā nābhiṁ na ā dade cakṣuś cit sūrye sacā | kaver apatyam ā duhe ||
  • nābhā nābhiṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the navel" or "the center." In many cultures, the navel is seen as a source of life and connection to the mother. It symbolizes the core of existence and the point from which life emanates.
  • na ā dade: This translates to "does not give" or "does not bestow." It suggests a sense of withholding or the idea that certain things are not freely given but must be earned or cultivated.
  • cakṣuś cit: "cakṣuś" means "eye" or "sight," and "cit" refers to consciousness or awareness. Together, this can imply the idea of enlightened vision or the ability to perceive deeper truths beyond the surface.
  • sūrye sacā: "sūrye" refers to the sun, a symbol of illumination, vitality, and energy. "sacā" can be interpreted as "with truth" or "in reality." This phrase emphasizes the connection between the sun and the essence of truth and life.
  • kaver apatyam: "kaver" means "of the poet" or "of the seer," while "apatyam" refers to "offspring" or "creation." This suggests that the creative force of the universe is linked to the insights of the wise or the poets.
  • ā duhe: This translates to "I milk" or "I draw forth." It implies the act of extracting or bringing forth something valuable, akin to milking a cow for nourishment.

In summary, this mantra reflects profound themes of existence, perception, and creativity. It suggests that the core of life (nābhā) does not simply give away its essence (na ā dade) but requires conscious awareness (cakṣuś cit) to truly understand and appreciate the truth (sūrye sacā). The creative insights of the wise (kaver apatyam) are essential for drawing forth (ā duhe) the richness of life.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra encourages individuals to seek deeper understanding and awareness in their lives. Just as the sun illuminates the world, one must strive to illuminate their own consciousness.
  • In practical terms, this can be applied in various fields such as education, where the pursuit of knowledge requires effort and insight rather than passive reception.
  • In creative endeavors, it highlights the importance of drawing inspiration from the depths of one's experiences and insights, much like a poet or artist who channels their understanding into their work.
  • Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder that true wisdom and creativity come from a place of deep awareness and connection to the core of existence.
