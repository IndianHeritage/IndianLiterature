यद् इन nv इन्द्र पृथिवी दशभुजिर अहानि विश्वा ततानन्त कृष्टयः |अत्राह ते मगवन विश्वुतं सहो द्याम अनु शवसां बरहणा भुवत ||
yad in nv indra pṛthivī daśabhujir ahāni viśvā tatananta kṛṣṭayaḥ | atrāha te maghavan viśrutaṁ saho dyām anu śavasā barhaṇā bhuvat ||
  • Mantra: "yad in nv indra pṛthivī daśabhujir ahāni viśvā tatananta kṛṣṭayaḥ | atrāha te maghavan viśrutaṁ saho dyām anu śavasā barhaṇā bhuvat ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "O Indra, who holds the earth with ten arms, may you bestow upon us the strength and abundance that pervades all realms. Here, O Maghavan, may your renowned power, along with the heavens, bring forth prosperity and nourishment."
  • Key Terms:
    • Indra: The king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing strength and power.
    • Pṛthivī: Refers to the earth, representing stability and sustenance.
    • Daśabhujir: Literally means "ten-armed," symbolizing the ability to perform multiple tasks and provide protection.
    • Maghavan: Another name for Indra, emphasizing his role as a giver of wealth and abundance.
    • Dyām: Refers to the heavens, indicating a connection between the earth and the sky.
    • Barhaṇā: Signifies nourishment and prosperity.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the deity associated with rain, storms, and strength, highlighting the importance of divine support in achieving prosperity.
    • The mention of "ten arms" symbolizes the multifaceted nature of challenges in life and the need for strength to overcome them.
    • By addressing both the earth and the heavens, the mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all elements of existence, suggesting that true prosperity comes from harmony between the material and spiritual realms.
    • In real life, this can be seen in the way communities come together to support each other during difficult times, much like how Indra is called upon for strength and abundance.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in achieving balance and prosperity in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that strength, whether physical or spiritual, is essential in navigating life's challenges.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in various situations, such as during community gatherings, agricultural rituals, or personal challenges, where invoking strength and abundance is necessary.
    • Ultimately, it teaches us to seek support from both the divine and our community, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.
