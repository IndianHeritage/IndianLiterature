उत दासस्य वर्चिनः सहस्राणि शतावधिः |अधि पञ्च प्रधींर इव ||
uta dāsasya varcinaḥ sahasrāṇi śatāvadhīḥ | adhi pañca pradhīm̐r iva ||
  • Mantra: "uta dāsasya varcinaḥ sahasrāṇi śatāvadhīḥ | adhi pañca pradhīm̐r iva ||"
  • Translation: "May the thousandfold brilliance of the servant (or devotee) shine forth, like the five great intellects."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • uta: a particle often used to denote 'and' or 'also'.
    • dāsasya: of the servant or devotee, indicating humility and service.
    • varcinaḥ: brilliance or radiance, referring to the light of knowledge or wisdom.
    • sahasrāṇi: thousands, emphasizing abundance.
    • śatāvadhīḥ: hundreds, further stressing the vastness of brilliance.
    • adhi: above or over, suggesting elevation.
    • pañca: five, which can symbolize completeness or the five elements.
    • pradhīm̐r: intellects or wisdom, referring to the higher faculties of understanding.
    • iva: like or as, indicating a comparison.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of humility and service. The term "dāsasya" suggests that true brilliance comes from serving others.
    • The phrase "sahasrāṇi śatāvadhīḥ" indicates that the radiance of a devoted servant can be vast and multifaceted, akin to a thousand lights shining together.
    • The comparison to "pañca pradhīm̐r" suggests that just as there are five great intellects, the brilliance of a devoted servant can also be profound and impactful.
    • In real life, this can be seen in the actions of individuals who serve their communities, such as volunteers, social workers, or caregivers. Their selfless acts often illuminate the lives of others, creating a ripple effect of positivity and inspiration.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra teaches us that true brilliance and wisdom are not just inherent traits but can be cultivated through service and humility.
    • In a world that often values individual achievement, this mantra reminds us of the power of collective effort and the importance of uplifting others.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as community service, teamwork in workplaces, and even in family dynamics, where supporting one another leads to greater overall success and happiness.
