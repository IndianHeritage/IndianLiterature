अभ्यर्ष महानां देवानां वीतिं अन्धसा |अभि वाजं उता श्रवः ||
abhy arṣa mahānāṁ devānāṁ vītim andhasā | abhi vājam uta śravaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhy: towards, for
    • arṣa: the seer, sage, or wise one
    • mahānāṁ: of the great ones
    • devānāṁ: of the gods
    • vītim: protection, shelter, or guidance
    • andhasā: from darkness or ignorance
    • abhi: towards, for
    • vājam: strength, power, or energy
    • uta: and, also
    • śravaḥ: fame, glory, or renown
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for guidance and protection from the great seers and gods, seeking their strength and fame while being shielded from ignorance and darkness. It emphasizes the importance of divine support in overcoming challenges and achieving greatness.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, individuals often seek mentorship from experienced leaders (the "great ones") to navigate their careers effectively. This aligns with the idea of seeking guidance from the wise.
    • Students may pray for divine assistance before exams, hoping to gain clarity and knowledge, thus seeking protection from ignorance.
    • In times of personal crisis, people may turn to spiritual practices or rituals to invoke strength and support from higher powers, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance and strength in the face of challenges. It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking wisdom and support from those who have achieved greatness. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts, such as education, career development, and personal growth, encouraging individuals to look beyond themselves for strength and enlightenment while striving for success and recognition.
