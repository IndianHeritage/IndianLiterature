सनेमि सख्यं स्वपश्यमानः सूनुर दाधार शवसा सुदंसाः |आमासु चिद दधिषे पक्वम अन्तः पयः कृष्णासु रुशद रोहिणीषु ||
sanemi sakhyaṁ svapasyamānaḥ sūnur dādhāra śavasā sudaṁsāḥ | āmāsu cid dadhiṣe pakvam antaḥ payaḥ kṛṣṇāsu ruśad rohiṇīṣu ||
  • Sanemi: This word can be interpreted as "we are united" or "we come together." It signifies the importance of unity and collaboration among individuals.
  • Sakhyaṁ: This translates to "friendship" or "companionship." It emphasizes the value of relationships and bonds that strengthen our social fabric.
  • Svapasyamānaḥ: Meaning "seeing in dreams" or "visionary," this term suggests the importance of aspirations and the ability to envision a better future.
  • Sūnur: This word means "son" or "offspring," symbolizing continuity, legacy, and the responsibilities that come with nurturing the next generation.
  • Dādhāra: Translated as "held" or "supported," it indicates the idea of providing strength and stability to one another in a community.
  • Śavasā: This term refers to "life" or "vitality," highlighting the essence of living and the energy that drives us forward.
  • Sudaṁsāḥ: Meaning "goodly" or "well-formed," it suggests the importance of quality and excellence in our endeavors.
  • Āmāsu: This word can be interpreted as "in the waters" or "in the depths," symbolizing the subconscious and the deeper aspects of our existence.
  • Cid: Meaning "consciousness" or "awareness," it emphasizes the significance of being mindful and aware of our surroundings and actions.
  • Dadhiṣe: This translates to "you have given" or "you have provided," indicating the act of sharing and generosity within a community.
  • Pakvam: Meaning "ripe" or "mature," it signifies the importance of growth and development in our personal and communal lives.
  • Antaḥ: This word means "inside" or "within," suggesting introspection and the importance of understanding our inner selves.
  • Payaḥ: Translated as "milk," it symbolizes nourishment and sustenance, both physically and spiritually.
  • Kṛṣṇāsu: This term refers to "dark" or "black," which can symbolize the unknown or the challenges we face in life.
  • Ruśad: Meaning "to shine" or "to illuminate," it emphasizes the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment.
  • Rohiṇīṣu: This word refers to "the stars" or "the celestial bodies," symbolizing guidance and the aspirations we look up to.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of community, friendship, and the journey of life. It highlights the importance of unity, support, and shared aspirations in achieving personal and collective growth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as fostering teamwork in workplaces, nurturing relationships in families, and building supportive communities. By embracing the values expressed in this mantra, individuals can contribute to a harmonious society where everyone thrives together.
