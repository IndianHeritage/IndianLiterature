यस्मिन् देवाः विदथे मादयन्ते विवस्वतः सदने धारणन्ते |सूर्ये ज्योतिर अदधुर मास्य अक्तून परि द्योतनीं चरतो अजस्राः ||
yasmin devā vidathe mādayante vivasvataḥ sadane dhārayante | sūrye jyotir adadhur māsy a1ktūn pari dyotaniṁ carato ajasrā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasmin: in whom
    • devā: the gods
    • vidathe: in the assembly or gathering
    • mādayante: delight or rejoice
    • vivasvataḥ: of the sun
    • sadane: in the abode or dwelling
    • dhārayante: sustain or uphold
    • sūrye: in the sun
    • jyotir: light or radiance
    • adadhur: they have placed
    • māsy: of the month
    • aktūn: the phases or cycles
    • pari: around
    • dyotaniṁ: illuminating
    • carato: moving or wandering
    • ajasrā: ever-present or eternal
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence that brings joy and sustenance to the assembly of gods.
    • It highlights the connection between the sun and the cycles of time, suggesting that the sun's light is a source of energy and life.
    • The phrase "ever-present" indicates the constant nature of divine energy in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate, they often feel a sense of joy and unity, similar to the divine assembly mentioned in the mantra.
    • The sun's role in agriculture is crucial; it sustains crops and provides energy, reflecting the sustenance mentioned in the mantra.
    • In daily life, the cycles of the moon and sun can be seen in our routines, such as the changing of seasons, which affects our mood and activities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine energy in our lives, particularly through the sun, which is a symbol of life and sustenance.
    • It reminds us to appreciate the cycles of nature and the joy that comes from community and connection.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to seek harmony with the natural world and recognize the divine presence in our daily experiences.
