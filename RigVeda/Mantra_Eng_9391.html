शं रोडसी सुभन्दवे यह्वी ऋतस्य मातरः |भरताम् अप यद् रपो द्यौः पृथिवी क्षमा रपो मोषु ते किं चनामतम् ||
śaṁ rodasī subandhave yahvī ṛtasya mātarā | bharatām apa yad rapo dyauḥ pṛthivi kṣamā rapo mo ṣu te kiṁ canāmamat ||
  • śaṁ rodasī: This phrase can be interpreted as "peaceful and harmonious in the universe." It signifies the importance of maintaining balance and tranquility in our surroundings.
  • subandhave: This word means "well-connected" or "in harmony with others." It emphasizes the value of relationships and community in achieving a fulfilling life.
  • yahvī ṛtasya mātarā: This translates to "the mother of truth." It highlights the significance of truth and righteousness as foundational principles in life.
  • bharatām: Referring to "the land of Bharata," it symbolizes the cultural and spiritual heritage of India, reminding us of our roots and responsibilities towards our nation.
  • apa yad rapo: This phrase can be understood as "from where the waters flow." It signifies the importance of natural resources and the need to respect and protect our environment.
  • dyauḥ pṛthivi: Meaning "heaven and earth," this phrase represents the duality of existence and the interconnectedness of all life forms.
  • kṣamā rapo: This can be interpreted as "the waters of forgiveness." It suggests that forgiveness is essential for personal and communal harmony.
  • mo ṣu te kiṁ canāmamat: This translates to "what is it that you desire?" It encourages introspection and understanding of one's true desires and aspirations.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound wisdom about harmony, truth, and interconnectedness. In real life, it serves as a reminder to foster peaceful relationships, uphold truth, and respect our environment. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious society. This mantra is applicable in various scenarios, such as conflict resolution, community building, and environmental conservation, guiding us towards a balanced and fulfilling life.
