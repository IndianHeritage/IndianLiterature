न पर्वताः न नद्यो वरन्त वो यत्राचिध्वं मरुतो गच्छतेदु तात् |उत द्यावापृथिवी याथना परि शुभं याताम् अनु रथा अवृत्तसत ||
na parvatā na nadyo varanta vo yatrācidhvam maruto gacchathed u tat | uta dyāvāpṛthivī yāthanā pari śubhaṁ yātām anu rathā avṛtsata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na: not
    • parvatā: mountains
    • na: not
    • nadyo: rivers
    • varanta: obstructing
    • vo: you
    • yatrā: where
    • cid: at all
    • hvam: you (plural)
    • maruto: O winds
    • gacchathed: may go
    • u: and
    • tat: that
    • uta: or
    • dyāvāpṛthivī: heaven and earth
    • yāthanā: may be established
    • pari: all around
    • śubhaṁ: auspicious
    • yātām: may go
    • anu: following
    • rathā: chariots
    • avṛtsata: may be protected
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of freedom and the removal of obstacles.
    • The phrase "na parvatā na nadyo varanta vo" suggests that neither mountains nor rivers should obstruct you.
    • It emphasizes the importance of overcoming barriers in life, whether they are physical, emotional, or spiritual.
    • The invocation of "maruto" (winds) symbolizes the forces of nature that can aid in this journey.
    • The latter part of the mantra, "uta dyāvāpṛthivī yāthanā," calls for the establishment of auspiciousness in the realms of heaven and earth.
    • This can be interpreted as a desire for harmony and balance in one's life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual may face challenges such as competition or lack of resources. This mantra encourages them to push through these obstacles and seek support from their environment, much like the winds that carry a chariot forward.
    • In personal relationships, misunderstandings can act as mountains or rivers that create distance. The mantra serves as a reminder to communicate openly and work towards resolving conflicts.
    • In a spiritual context, the mantra can inspire individuals to seek inner peace and clarity, overcoming the distractions and disturbances of daily life.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its universal message of overcoming obstacles and seeking harmony.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal growth, professional challenges, and spiritual journeys.
    • By invoking the energies of nature and the universe, individuals can find strength and guidance in their pursuits.
    • This mantra serves as a powerful reminder that with determination and support, one can navigate through life's challenges and emerge victorious.
