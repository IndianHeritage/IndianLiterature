आ तं भज सौश्रवसेष्व अग्न उक्थ-उक्थ आ भज शस्यमाने |प्रियः सूर्ये प्रिय आग्ना भवात्य उज जातेन भिनदद उज जनितवैः ||
ā tam bhaja sauśravaseṣv agna uktha-uktha ā bhaja śasyamāne | priyaḥ sūrye priyo agnā bhavāty uj jātena bhinadad uj janitvaiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā tam: to that
    • bhaja: worship or honor
    • sauśravaseṣv: in the auspicious or glorious
    • agna: O Agni (the fire god)
    • uktha-uktha: through the hymns or praises
    • ā bhaja: worship or honor
    • śasyamāne: when the crops are flourishing
    • priyaḥ: dear or beloved
    • sūrye: to the Sun
    • priyo: dear or beloved
    • agnā: O Agni
    • bhavāty: may become
    • uj: bright or radiant
    • jātena: by the birth or generation
    • bhinadad: may separate or distinguish
    • uj: bright or radiant
    • janitvaiḥ: by the progeny or offspring
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, and the Sun, symbolizing the importance of light, energy, and sustenance in life.
    • The invocation of Agni signifies the need for purification and the transformative power of fire, which is essential in rituals and daily life.
    • The mention of flourishing crops indicates the connection between divine blessings and agricultural prosperity, emphasizing the importance of nature in human sustenance.
    • The reference to the Sun highlights its role as a source of life and energy, essential for growth and vitality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, rituals are often performed to honor Agni and the Sun to ensure a good harvest, reflecting the reliance on divine forces for sustenance.
    • Modern practices of environmental conservation can be seen as a way to honor nature, akin to the reverence shown in this mantra towards the elements that sustain life.
    • In personal development, the mantra can inspire individuals to seek inner light and clarity, akin to the brightness associated with Agni and the Sun.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of divine forces, nature, and human life, emphasizing the need for respect and gratitude towards the elements that sustain us.
    • It serves as a reminder to honor the sources of energy and nourishment in our lives, whether through spiritual practices, environmental stewardship, or personal growth.
    • In a broader context, it encourages a holistic approach to life, recognizing the importance of balance between the material and spiritual realms.
