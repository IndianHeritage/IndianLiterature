त्री रोचनā वरुण त्रिम् उता द्यून त्रिणि मित्र धारणाथो राजांसि | वावृद्धानाव अमतिं क्षत्रियस्यानु व्रतं रक्षमाणाव अजुर्म् ||
trī rocanā varuṇa trīm̐r uta dyūn trīṇi mitra dhārayatho rajāṁsi | vāvṛdhānāv amatiṁ kṣatriyasyānu vrataṁ rakṣamāṇāv ajuryam ||
  • trī rocanā varuṇa: This phrase can be translated as "three luminous ones, O Varuna." Here, Varuna is a deity associated with the cosmic order and the waters. The "three luminous ones" may refer to the sun, moon, and stars, which are essential for life and symbolize guidance and clarity.
  • trīm̐r uta dyūn: This translates to "three realms, indeed the heavens." This line emphasizes the importance of the three realms: the earth, the atmosphere, and the heavens. It signifies the interconnectedness of all existence and the divine presence in each realm.
  • trīṇi mitra dhārayatho rajāṁsi: This means "three friends, support the kingdoms." The term "mitra" refers to friendship and alliance. In a real-life context, this can be seen in the importance of alliances in governance and community building, where mutual support leads to stability and prosperity.
  • vāvṛdhānāv amatiṁ kṣatriyasyānu: This translates to "those who increase the strength of the warrior." The Kshatriya class in ancient India was responsible for protection and governance. This line highlights the importance of strength and valor in leadership, which can be applied in modern contexts such as leadership in business or community service.
  • vrataṁ rakṣamāṇāv ajuryam: This means "those who uphold the vow of immortality." This can be interpreted as a call to maintain one's commitments and values, which is crucial in personal and professional life. Upholding one's vows leads to trust and respect in relationships.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of harmony among the three realms of existence, the importance of alliances and support in governance, the valor of leadership, and the commitment to one's values. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as fostering teamwork in organizations, building strong communities, and leading with integrity. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the responsibilities we hold towards each other and the world around us.
