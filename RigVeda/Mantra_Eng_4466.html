यस् ते सूनो सहसो गीरभिर उक्थैर यज्ञैर मर्तो निशितिं वेद्यानट् |विश्वं स देव प्रति वारम् अग्ने धत्ते धान्यं पत्यते वसव्यैः ||
yas te sūno sahaso gīrbhir ukthair yajñair marto niśitiṁ vedyānaṭ | viśvaṁ sa deva prati vāram agne dhatte dhānya1m patyate vasavyaiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yas: who
    • te: your
    • sūno: sons
    • sahaso: with strength
    • gīrbhir: with hymns
    • ukthair: with utterances
    • yajñair: with sacrifices
    • marto: mortal
    • niśitiṁ: night
    • vedyānaṭ: knowing
    • viśvaṁ: all
    • sa: he
    • deva: god
    • prati: towards
    • vāram: protection
    • agne: O Agni (Fire)
    • dhatte: bestows
    • dhānyaṁ: wealth
    • patyate: is governed
    • vasavyaiḥ: by the auspicious
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence of Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
    • The phrase "who your sons with strength" suggests the importance of family and lineage, emphasizing that strength and virtue are passed down through generations.
    • By using hymns and sacrifices, the mantra highlights the significance of rituals in connecting with the divine and seeking blessings.
    • The mention of "mortal night" indicates the transient nature of human life and the need for divine guidance during challenging times.
    • The mantra concludes with a plea for Agni to bestow wealth and protection, symbolizing the desire for prosperity and safety in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, families often gather for rituals and ceremonies, such as weddings or festivals, to invoke blessings for strength and prosperity, similar to the sacrifices mentioned in the mantra.
    • In times of difficulty, people may turn to spiritual practices, such as prayer or meditation, seeking guidance and protection, reflecting the essence of invoking Agni for support.
    • The importance of passing down values and virtues from parents to children can be seen in various cultures, where teachings about strength, resilience, and morality are emphasized.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of family, spirituality, and the pursuit of prosperity.
    • It highlights the significance of rituals in fostering a connection with the divine and seeking blessings for strength and protection.
    • In real life, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide individuals in their personal and familial endeavors, encouraging them to seek divine support and uphold values that lead to a prosperous and harmonious life.
