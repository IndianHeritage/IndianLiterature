अवीन नो अग्निर हव्यान् नमोभिः प्रेष्ठो अस्मा अधायि स्तोमः ||
avīn no agnir havyān namobhiḥ preṣṭho asmā adhāyi stomaḥ ||
  • avīn: This word can be interpreted as "may we be protected" or "may we be guided." It signifies a plea for divine guidance and protection in our endeavors.
  • no: This is a possessive pronoun meaning "our," indicating a collective identity or community.
  • agnir: Referring to "Agni," the fire god, who symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals. Fire is often seen as a purifying force.
  • havyān: This term means "offerings" or "sacrifices," which are made to the deities, particularly Agni, in Vedic rituals. It emphasizes the importance of devotion and the act of giving.
  • namobhiḥ: This translates to "with salutations" or "with reverence," indicating a respectful approach towards the divine.
  • preṣṭho: Meaning "the most beloved" or "the dearest," this word highlights the intimate relationship between the devotee and the divine.
  • asmā: This means "us" or "to us," reinforcing the communal aspect of the mantra.
  • adhāyi: This term can be understood as "may he bestow" or "may he grant," indicating a request for blessings or gifts from the divine.
  • stomaḥ: Referring to "praise" or "hymn," it signifies the act of expressing gratitude and reverence through words.

In essence, this mantra is a prayer for divine guidance and blessings from Agni, the fire god, emphasizing the importance of offerings and reverence in spiritual practices. It reflects a deep connection between the devotee and the divine, highlighting the significance of community and collective worship.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for protection, guidance, and blessings in our lives. In real-life scenarios, it can be applicable in various contexts such as during rituals, ceremonies, or even in personal prayers where individuals seek strength and support in their endeavors. By invoking Agni, the mantra encourages individuals to engage in acts of devotion and gratitude, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose. This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of reverence and the transformative power of divine energy in our lives.
