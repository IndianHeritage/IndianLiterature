आ वातस्य ध्रजतो रन्त इत्यापीपयन्त धेनवो न सूदाः |महो दिवः सदने जायमानो 'चिक्रदद वृषभः सस्मिन्न ऊधन ||
ā vātasya dhrajato ranta ityā apīpayanta dhenavo na sūdāḥ | maho divaḥ sadane jāyamāno 'cikradad vṛṣabhaḥ sasminn ūdhan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vātasya: of the wind
    • dhrajato: from the one who is strong
    • ranta: the one who is joyful
    • ityā: thus
    • apīpayanta: nourishing
    • dhenavo: the cows
    • na sūdāḥ: do not suffer
    • maho: of the great
    • dvaḥ: of the sky
    • sadane: in the abode
    • jāyamāno: being born
    • cikradad: has made a sound
    • vṛṣabhaḥ: the bull
    • sasminn: in that
    • ūdhan: in the waters
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the wind as a powerful and joyful force that nourishes life, represented by the cows.
    • The cows symbolize abundance and sustenance, indicating that nature provides for all living beings without suffering.
    • The mention of the "great sky" suggests a divine presence that oversees and nurtures creation.
    • The "bull" represents strength and vitality, emphasizing the importance of these qualities in the cycle of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the wind plays a crucial role in pollination and the dispersal of seeds, which is essential for crop growth.
    • The nurturing aspect of nature can be seen in how ecosystems support various species, ensuring that none suffer unnecessarily.
    • In many cultures, the bull is revered as a symbol of strength and fertility, often used in farming and as a representation of prosperity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the interconnectedness of nature, emphasizing the importance of harmony and balance in our environment.
    • It serves as a reminder to respect and nurture the natural world, recognizing that our well-being is tied to the health of our ecosystems.
    • In modern life, this can be applied through sustainable practices, conservation efforts, and a deeper appreciation for the resources that nature provides.
