बृहस्पतिर्मत हि त्यदासां नाम स्वरिणां सदने गुहा यत् |आṇḍेवा भित्त्वा शकुनस्य गर्भमुदुश्रियाः पर्वतस्य तमनाजत ||
bṛhaspatir amata hi tyad āsāṁ nāma svarīṇāṁ sadane guhā yat | āṇḍeva bhittvā śakunasya garbham ud usriyāḥ parvatasya tmanājat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bṛhaspatir: Refers to Brihaspati, the deity of wisdom and eloquence.
    • amata: Immortal or eternal.
    • hi: Indeed or surely.
    • tyad: That or those.
    • āsāṁ: Of the desires or aspirations.
    • nāma: Name or identity.
    • svarīṇāṁ: Of the celestial beings or divine entities.
    • sadane: In the abode or dwelling.
    • guhā: Cave or hidden place.
    • yat: Which or that.
    • āṇḍeva: Like an egg or in the form of an egg.
    • bhittvā: Breaking or piercing through.
    • śakunasya: Of the bird or omen.
    • garbham: Embryo or essence.
    • ud: Up or out.
    • usriyāḥ: By the force or power.
    • parvatasya: Of the mountain.
    • tmanājat: By the self or through the self.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the wisdom of Brihaspati, emphasizing the eternal nature of knowledge and aspirations.
    • The reference to breaking through the egg symbolizes the emergence of new ideas or consciousness from the hidden depths of the mind.
    • The cave (guhā) represents the subconscious, where desires and aspirations reside, waiting to be realized.
    • The act of piercing through signifies overcoming obstacles to reach enlightenment or understanding.
    • The mention of the mountain suggests stability and strength, indicating that true wisdom is grounded in solid foundations.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, students often face challenges that require them to break through their limitations (bhittvā) to achieve their goals (āṇḍeva).
    • In personal development, individuals may need to confront their fears (guhā) to realize their true potential (garbham).
    • In leadership, wise leaders (bṛhaspatir) guide their teams through challenges, helping them to aspire towards greater achievements (nāma).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom and the pursuit of knowledge as a means to transcend limitations and realize one's true potential.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including education, personal growth, and leadership, encouraging individuals to seek enlightenment and overcome obstacles.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that true wisdom is eternal and can guide us through the complexities of life.
