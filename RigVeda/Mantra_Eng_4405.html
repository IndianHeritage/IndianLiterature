नितिक्ति यो वारणम् अन्नम् अत्ति वायुर न राष्ट्र्य अत्य एत्य अक्तून् |तुर्यामा यस त आदिशाम अरातीर अत्यो न हृतः पततः परिहृत ||
nitikti yo vāraṇam annam atti vāyur na rāṣṭry aty ety aktūn | turyāma yas ta ādiśām arātīr atyo na hrutaḥ patataḥ parihrut ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • nitikti: (to be careful, to be wise)
    • yo: (who)
    • vāraṇam: (the protector, the guardian)
    • annam: (food, nourishment)
    • atti: (eats, consumes)
    • vāyur: (the wind, the life force)
    • na: (not)
    • rāṣṭry: (the state, the realm)
    • aty: (beyond)
    • ety: (goes, moves)
    • aktūn: (the offerings, the sacrifices)
    • turyāma: (the one who is beyond, the supreme)
    • yas: (who)
    • ta: (that)
    • ādiśām: (of the beginning, the origin)
    • arātīr: (the enemies, the obstacles)
    • aty: (beyond)
    • na: (not)
    • hrutaḥ: (taken away, removed)
    • patataḥ: (falling, descending)
    • parihrut: (protected, safeguarded)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and protection in the consumption of nourishment.
    • It suggests that one who is wise and careful in their actions will be safeguarded from negative influences and obstacles.
    • The reference to "vāyur" indicates the vital life force that sustains us, linking our physical nourishment to our spiritual well-being.
    • It highlights the idea that true nourishment goes beyond mere physical food; it encompasses mental and spiritual sustenance as well.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a practical sense, this mantra can be applied in our daily lives by being mindful of what we consume, both physically and mentally. For instance, choosing healthy foods and avoiding toxic relationships or negative environments.
    • In the workplace, a wise leader who protects their team from unnecessary stress and distractions can foster a more productive and harmonious environment.
    • In personal development, individuals who seek knowledge and wisdom are better equipped to navigate life's challenges and protect themselves from harmful influences.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for mindfulness and wisdom in all aspects of life.
    • It teaches us that true nourishment is holistic, encompassing physical, mental, and spiritual dimensions.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, we can cultivate a life that is not only fulfilling but also protected from negativity and obstacles.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder to be vigilant and wise in our choices, ensuring that we are nourished in every sense of the word.
