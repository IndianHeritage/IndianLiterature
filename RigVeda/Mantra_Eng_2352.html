तान् वो महो मरुत एवयाव्नो विष्णोर एषस्य प्रभृत हे हवामहे |हिरण्यवर्णान् ककुहान यतस्रुचो ब्रह्मण्यन्तः शंस्यं राध इमहे ||
tān vo maho maruta evayāvno viṣṇor eṣasya prabhṛthe havāmahe | hiraṇyavarṇān kakuhān yatasruco brahmaṇyantaḥ śaṁsyaṁ rādha īmahe ||
  • tān vo maho maruta evayāvno viṣṇor eṣasya prabhṛthe havāmahe
    • tān - "those" refers to the Maruts, the storm deities.
    • vo - "to you" indicates a reverence towards these deities.
    • maho - "great" signifies the immense power and significance of the Maruts.
    • maruta - "the Maruts" are associated with storms, winds, and the forces of nature.
    • evayāvno - "who are present" suggests their omnipresence in nature.
    • viṣṇor - "of Vishnu" connects the Maruts to the divine order and protection.
    • eṣasya - "of this" refers to the world or the universe.
    • prabhṛthe - "in the greatness" emphasizes the vastness of their influence.
    • havāmahe - "we invoke" is a call to the Maruts for their blessings.
  • hiraṇyavarṇān kakuhān yatasruco brahmaṇyantaḥ śaṁsyaṁ rādha īmahe
    • hiraṇyavarṇān - "golden-colored" symbolizes prosperity and abundance.
    • kakuhān - "from the heavens" indicates a divine source of blessings.
    • yatasruco - "from whom the light flows" refers to the illumination and knowledge bestowed by the Maruts.
    • brahmaṇyantaḥ - "within the essence of the universe" signifies the connection to the cosmic order.
    • śaṁsyaṁ - "peace" or "well-being" is the ultimate goal of invoking these deities.
    • rādha - "to be fulfilled" indicates the desire for blessings and fulfillment in life.
    • īmahe - "we invoke" is a reiteration of the call for divine assistance.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda is a powerful invocation to the Maruts, the storm deities, and Vishnu, representing the forces of nature and divine protection. It emphasizes the importance of seeking blessings for prosperity, peace, and well-being. In real life, this mantra can be applicable in various scenarios such as:

  • During rituals for rain and agricultural prosperity, invoking the Maruts for favorable weather conditions.
  • In personal prayers for guidance and protection in times of uncertainty or turmoil.
  • In community gatherings where collective well-being and harmony are sought.

By understanding and reciting this mantra, individuals can connect with the natural forces and divine energies, fostering a sense of peace and abundance in their lives.
