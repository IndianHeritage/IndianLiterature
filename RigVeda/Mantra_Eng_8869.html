न यत पुरा चक्रमा कद ध नूनम ऋता वदन्तो अनृतं रपेमा |गन्धर्वो अप्स्व अप्याऽ च योषा सा नो नाभिः परमं ज्ञामि तन् नौ ||
na yat purā cakṛmā kad dha nūnam ṛtā vadanto anṛtaṁ rapema | gandharvo apsv apyā ca yoṣā sā no nābhiḥ paramaṁ jāmi tan nau ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na yat: not what
    • purā: in the past
    • cakṛmā: we have done
    • kad: when
    • dha: indeed
    • nūnam: surely
    • ṛtā: truth
    • vadanto: those who speak
    • anṛtaṁ: falsehood
    • rapema: may we attain
    • gandharvo: the celestial musician
    • apsv: in the waters
    • apyā: and also
    • ca: and
    • yoṣā: the woman
    • sā: she
    • no: for us
    • nābhiḥ: with our hearts
    • paramaṁ: the highest
    • jāmi: I know
    • tan: that
    • nau: for us
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects a quest for truth and the rejection of falsehood. It emphasizes the importance of sincerity in our actions and thoughts.
    • The mention of "gandharvo" and "yoṣā" symbolizes the beauty and harmony in life, suggesting that truth leads to a more fulfilling existence.
    • The phrase "nābhiḥ paramaṁ" indicates a deep connection with the heart, suggesting that true knowledge and understanding come from within.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied to ethical business practices. Companies that prioritize honesty and transparency tend to build stronger relationships with clients and employees.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra encourages open communication and trust. By speaking the truth and avoiding deceit, individuals can foster deeper connections with others.
    • In the context of self-reflection, the mantra serves as a reminder to seek inner truth and authenticity, guiding individuals to align their actions with their values.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the significance of truth in our lives. It teaches us that by embracing honesty and rejecting falsehood, we can achieve a higher understanding and a more harmonious existence.
    • Its application is relevant in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, professional ethics, and self-awareness, making it a timeless guide for living a meaningful life.
