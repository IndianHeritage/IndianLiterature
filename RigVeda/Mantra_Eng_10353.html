अरण्याण्य अरण्याण्य असौ या प्रेवा नश्यसि |कथा ग्रामं न पृच्छसि न त्वा भीर इव विन्दतीं ||
araṇyāny araṇyāny asau yā preva naśyasi | kathā grāmaṁ na pṛcchasi na tvā bhīr iva vindatī3m̐ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • araṇyāny: wilderness, forest, or uninhabited places
    • asau: that (referring to a person or entity)
    • yā: who
    • preva: to perish or to be lost
    • naśyasi: you are lost or you perish
    • kathā: how
    • grāmaṁ: village or community
    • na: not
    • pṛcchasi: you ask
    • na: not
    • tvā: you
    • bhīr: fear
    • iva: like or as
    • vindatī: finds or discovers
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects on the nature of existence and the human condition.
    • It suggests that one who is lost in the wilderness (both literally and metaphorically) does not seek help or guidance from the community.
    • The phrase "na pṛcchasi" indicates a reluctance or inability to ask for assistance, which can be seen in real-life scenarios where individuals may feel isolated or ashamed to reach out for help.
    • The comparison to fear ("bhīr iva vindatī") highlights how fear can prevent individuals from seeking support, much like a frightened animal that hides away.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, many people struggle with mental health issues but may not reach out for help due to stigma or fear of judgment.
    • In a workplace setting, an employee may feel overwhelmed by tasks but hesitate to ask for assistance, fearing it may reflect poorly on their capabilities.
    • In personal relationships, individuals may avoid discussing their problems with friends or family, leading to feelings of isolation.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of community and the necessity of seeking help when in distress.
    • It emphasizes that isolation can lead to a sense of being lost, and that asking for help is a strength rather than a weakness.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to overcome their fears and reach out to others, fostering connections and support systems that are vital for well-being.
