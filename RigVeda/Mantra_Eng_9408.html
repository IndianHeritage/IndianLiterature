मन्त्र: मनो न येषु हवनेषु तिग्मं विपः शच्यā वनुथो द्रवन्तā |आ यः शर्याभिस तुविनृम्णो अस्याश्रीणीतादिशं गभस्तौ ||
mano na yeṣu havaneṣu tigmaṁ vipaḥ śacyā vanutho dravantā | ā yaḥ śaryābhis tuvinṛmṇo asyāśrīṇītādiśaṁ gabhastau ||
  • Mantra: "mano na yeṣu havaneṣu tigmaṁ vipaḥ śacyā vanutho dravantā | ā yaḥ śaryābhis tuvinṛmṇo asyāśrīṇītādiśaṁ gabhastau ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mano: mind
    • na: not
    • yeṣu: in whom
    • havaneṣu: in the offerings
    • tigmaṁ: sharp or intense
    • vipaḥ: the wise or learned
    • śacyā: with power or strength
    • vanutho: those who are in the forest or nature
    • dravantā: moving or flowing
    • ā: towards
    • yaḥ: who
    • śaryābhis: with the blessings
    • tuvinṛmṇo: of the one who is pure
    • asyāśrīṇītādiśaṁ: the one who gives auspiciousness
    • gabhastau: the light or radiance
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the mind and its relationship with offerings and wisdom.
    • The phrase "mano na yeṣu havaneṣu" suggests that the mind should not be attached to the offerings made, indicating a need for detachment in spiritual practices.
    • "tigmaṁ vipaḥ" refers to the wise individuals who possess sharp intellect and understanding, emphasizing the importance of wisdom in spiritual pursuits.
    • The mention of "śacyā vanutho dravantā" highlights the connection between nature and the flow of life, suggesting that wisdom and strength can be found in the natural world.
    • "ā yaḥ śaryābhis tuvinṛmṇo" indicates that those who are pure and blessed can attain auspiciousness and radiance in their lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied to the practice of mindfulness and meditation, where individuals are encouraged to let go of attachments and focus on the present moment.
    • In the context of environmental awareness, recognizing the wisdom of nature and its importance in our lives can lead to more sustainable living practices.
    • In personal development, seeking knowledge and wisdom, rather than material gains, can lead to a more fulfilling and meaningful life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of the mind in spiritual practices and the importance of wisdom and purity in achieving a radiant and auspicious life.
    • It encourages individuals to cultivate a mindset of detachment from material offerings and to seek deeper understanding and connection with nature.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to personal growth, environmental consciousness, and a more profound sense of purpose.
