षळ अश्वां अतीथिग्व इन्द्रोते वधूमतः |सचā पूतक्रतौ सनम् ||
ṣaḻ aśvām̐ ātithigva indrote vadhūmataḥ | sacā pūtakratau sanam ||
  • ṣaḻ aśvām̐: This phrase translates to "six horses." In Vedic literature, horses symbolize strength, speed, and prosperity. They are often associated with the sun and the divine, representing the power of the cosmos.
  • ātithigva: This term refers to "the guest" or "the one who is welcomed." In many cultures, hospitality is a sacred duty, and this word emphasizes the importance of treating guests with respect and honor.
  • indrote: This word can be interpreted as "to Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic mythology. Indra is often associated with rain, storms, and the warrior spirit, symbolizing strength and protection.
  • vadhūmataḥ: This translates to "from the bride" or "of the bride." It signifies the importance of the bride in a marriage, highlighting her role in family and society.
  • sacā: This word means "with truth" or "with sincerity." It emphasizes the value of honesty and integrity in relationships and interactions.
  • pūtakratau: This term can be understood as "pure intentions" or "sacred vows." It reflects the importance of purity in thoughts and actions, especially in the context of commitments and relationships.
  • sanam: This word means "to be well" or "to prosper." It signifies well-being, health, and success in life.

Conclusion: The mantra "ṣaḻ aśvām̐ ātithigva indrote vadhūmataḥ | sacā pūtakratau sanam" encapsulates themes of strength, hospitality, respect for relationships, and the importance of purity in intentions. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In weddings, where the bride and groom are honored, and their commitment to each other is celebrated with sincerity and pure intentions.
  • In everyday interactions, where treating guests with respect and kindness reflects the values of hospitality and community.
  • In personal growth, where individuals strive for well-being and success through honest and pure actions.

This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, respect, and purity in our lives, encouraging us to foster these values in our relationships and communities.
