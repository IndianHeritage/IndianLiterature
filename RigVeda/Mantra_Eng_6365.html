तदाद्याऽचित्त त उक्तिनोऽनु ष्टुवन्ति पूर्वथा |वृषपत्नीः अपो जयाऽदिवेदिवे ||
tad adyā cit ta ukthino 'nu ṣṭuvanti pūrvathā | vṛṣapatnīr apo jayā dive-dive ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • adyā: today
    • cit: indeed
    • ta: that
    • ukthino: those who recite (mantras)
    • 'nu: indeed
    • ṣṭuvanti: praise
    • pūrvathā: as before
    • vṛṣapatnīr: the wife of the bull (often referring to the Earth or a deity)
    • apo: waters
    • jayā: victory
    • dive-dive: day by day
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the continuity of praise and the importance of reciting mantras.
    • It emphasizes the idea that those who recite these sacred verses are engaging in a timeless tradition.
    • The reference to "vṛṣapatnīr" can symbolize the nurturing aspect of nature, often associated with the Earth, which provides sustenance and support.
    • The phrase "apo jayā" suggests a victory or triumph that is linked to the waters, which can be interpreted as the life-giving properties of water and its essential role in existence.
    • Overall, the mantra encourages a daily practice of reverence and gratitude towards the forces of nature and the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can inspire individuals to engage in practices that honor nature, such as planting trees or conserving water.
    • It can also encourage people to express gratitude for the resources they have, fostering a sense of community and responsibility towards the environment.
    • In spiritual practices, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to connect with the divine and acknowledge the blessings received each day.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for continuous praise and recognition of the divine forces that sustain life.
    • It is applicable in real-life scenarios as a reminder to cultivate gratitude, respect for nature, and a sense of community.
    • By integrating the essence of this mantra into daily life, individuals can foster a deeper connection with the world around them and contribute positively to their environment.
