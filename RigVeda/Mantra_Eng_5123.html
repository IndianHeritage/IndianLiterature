स्वादुषंसदः पितरो वयोधाः कृत्श्रेश्रितः शक्तिवन्तो गभीराः | चित्रसेना इषुबला अमृतध्राः सतवीराः उरवो व्रातसाहाः ||
svāduṣaṁsadaḥ pitaro vayodhāḥ kṛcchreśritaḥ śaktīvanto gabhīrāḥ | citrasenā iṣubalā amṛdhrāḥ satovīrā uravo vrātasāhāḥ ||
  • svāduṣaṁsadaḥ: This term can be broken down into "svādhu" meaning sweet or pleasant, and "ṣaṁsadaḥ" which refers to those who are worthy of praise. Together, it signifies the idea of praising those who are sweet or pleasant in nature.
  • pitaro: This word translates to "fathers" or "ancestors." It emphasizes the importance of honoring and remembering our forebears, who have laid the foundation for our existence.
  • vayodhāḥ: This term means "those who are strong in their youth." It highlights the vigor and vitality of youth, suggesting that strength and energy are essential during this phase of life.
  • kṛcchreśritaḥ: This can be interpreted as "those who are in difficulty" or "those who are struggling." It acknowledges the challenges that individuals may face in life.
  • śaktīvanto: This word means "powerful" or "full of energy." It suggests that despite difficulties, there is an inherent strength within individuals.
  • gabhīrāḥ: This translates to "deep" or "profound." It can refer to deep wisdom or understanding, indicating that true strength often comes from profound insights.
  • citrasenā: This term can be understood as "having a colorful army" or "varied forces." It symbolizes diversity and the strength that comes from unity in diversity.
  • iṣubalā: This means "those who are strong" or "powerful." It reinforces the idea of strength and capability.
  • amṛdhrāḥ: This term translates to "immortal" or "eternal." It signifies the enduring nature of certain qualities or virtues.
  • satovīrā: This can be interpreted as "those who are true heroes" or "virtuous warriors." It emphasizes the importance of integrity and righteousness in strength.
  • uravo: This word means "broad" or "expansive." It can refer to the vastness of one's capabilities or the breadth of one's influence.
  • vrātasāhāḥ: This term can be understood as "those who are united in purpose." It highlights the significance of community and collective strength.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of honoring our ancestors, recognizing the strength of youth, and acknowledging the struggles that individuals face. It teaches us that true power comes from deep understanding, unity in diversity, and virtuous living. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as community building, leadership, and personal development. By fostering a sense of community, honoring our roots, and striving for integrity, we can navigate challenges and emerge stronger, embodying the qualities of true heroes in our lives.
