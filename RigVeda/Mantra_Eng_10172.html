मन्त्र: हिरण्यगर्भः सम अवर्तताग्रे भूतस्य जातः पतिरेक आसीत् |स दाधार पृथिवीं द्यामुतेमां कस्यै देवाय हविषा विधेम ||
hiraṇyagarbhaḥ sam avartatāgre bhūtasya jātaḥ patir eka āsīt | sa dādhāra pṛthivīṁ dyām utemāṁ kasmai devāya haviṣā vidhema ||
  • hiraṇyagarbhaḥ: This term translates to "golden womb" or "golden egg." It symbolizes the source of creation, representing the cosmic principle from which all existence emerges. In real life, this can be likened to the idea of potential and possibilities, much like a seed that holds the potential for a great tree.
  • sam avartatāgre: This phrase means "was revolving at the beginning." It indicates the cyclical nature of time and existence, suggesting that creation is not linear but rather a continuous cycle. For example, the seasons change in a cycle, reflecting the idea of renewal and rebirth.
  • bhūtasya jātaḥ: This translates to "born of the elements." It emphasizes that all beings and matter originate from the fundamental elements of nature. In our daily lives, we can see this in the way we are all interconnected with nature, as we are made of the same elements that compose the earth, air, water, and fire.
  • patir eka āsīt: This means "there was one lord." It suggests the idea of unity and singularity in the divine or cosmic order. In a real-life context, this can be related to the concept of leadership or a guiding principle that unites people towards a common goal, such as a community leader or a shared vision in a team.
  • sa dādhāra pṛthivīṁ dyām utemāṁ: This phrase translates to "He supported the earth and the sky." It signifies the foundational role of the divine in maintaining balance in the universe. In practical terms, this can be seen in environmental conservation efforts, where humans strive to maintain the balance of nature and protect the earth and its resources.
  • kasmai devāya haviṣā vidhema: This means "to which deity shall we offer our oblation?" It reflects the human quest for connection with the divine and the search for guidance. In everyday life, this can be interpreted as seeking inspiration or support from higher ideals, whether through spirituality, philosophy, or community values.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound philosophical concepts about creation, unity, and the interconnectedness of all beings. Its significance lies in the reminder of our origins and the cyclical nature of existence. In real life, it encourages us to recognize our potential, seek unity in diversity, and strive for balance in our environment. By understanding these principles, we can foster a deeper connection with ourselves, others, and the world around us, leading to a more harmonious existence.
