आ वां सहस्रं हरय इन्द्रवायू अभि प्रयः |वहन्तु सोमपीतये ||
ā vāṁ sahasraṁ haraya indravāyū abhi prayaḥ | vahantu somapītaye ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā vāṁ: "May we" - a call for collective well-being and unity.
    • sahasraṁ: "thousands" - signifies abundance and prosperity.
    • haraya: "the forces of nature" - refers to the divine energies that govern the universe.
    • indravāyū: "Indra and Vayu" - Indra is the king of gods, associated with rain and thunderstorms, while Vayu is the god of wind, symbolizing strength and movement.
    • abhi prayaḥ: "may they come forth" - a request for the manifestation of these divine forces.
    • vahantu: "may they carry" - an invocation for support and guidance.
    • somapītaye: "for the sake of the soma drink" - soma is a sacred drink that symbolizes immortality and divine inspiration.
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer for the divine forces of nature, represented by Indra and Vayu, to come forth and support us.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and collective effort in seeking abundance and prosperity.
    • The reference to soma highlights the pursuit of spiritual nourishment and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied during festivals or gatherings where people come together to celebrate and seek blessings for prosperity and harmony.
    • In personal life, individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or yoga practices to invoke strength and support from the universe in their endeavors.
    • In environmental contexts, it can serve as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature and the importance of respecting and nurturing the environment for future generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, abundance, and divine support.
    • It encourages individuals and communities to seek strength from the natural forces and to work together towards common goals.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder of the importance of collaboration, respect for nature, and the pursuit of spiritual growth.
