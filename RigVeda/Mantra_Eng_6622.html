मन्त्र: उता स्या श्वेतयावरī वाहिष्ठा वां नदीनाम् |सिन्धुर हिरण्यवर्तिनिः ||
uta syā śvetayāvarī vāhiṣṭhā vāṁ nadīnām | sindhur hiraṇyavartaniḥ ||
  • uta syā: This phrase can be translated as "may there be" or "let there be." It expresses a desire or intention for something to occur.
  • śvetayāvarī: This term refers to "the white (or pure) waters." In many cultures, water symbolizes purity, life, and sustenance. The color white often represents clarity and peace.
  • vāhiṣṭhā: This word means "the best" or "the most excellent." It signifies a high standard or quality, suggesting that the waters being referred to are of the highest purity and value.
  • vāṁ nadīnām: This translates to "of your rivers." It indicates a connection to the rivers, which are often seen as life-giving forces in many cultures.
  • sindhur: This word means "ocean" or "river." It represents vastness and abundance, often associated with the flow of life and resources.
  • hiraṇyavartaniḥ: This term can be interpreted as "golden flowing" or "that which flows like gold." It suggests richness, prosperity, and the idea that the waters bring wealth and nourishment.

The mantra as a whole can be understood as a prayer or invocation for the pure and excellent waters of the rivers to flow abundantly, bringing prosperity and life. It reflects a deep reverence for nature and the essential role that water plays in sustaining life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Environmental Awareness: The mantra emphasizes the importance of clean and pure water sources. In today's world, where pollution and water scarcity are pressing issues, this invocation serves as a reminder to protect our rivers and oceans.
  • Spiritual Cleansing: Just as water is seen as a purifying element, individuals can use this mantra during meditation or rituals to seek mental and spiritual clarity, promoting a sense of peace and well-being.
  • Community and Cooperation: The reference to rivers can symbolize the interconnectedness of communities. It encourages collaboration and sharing of resources for the common good, much like rivers nourish the lands they flow through.

In conclusion, this mantra highlights the significance of water as a source of life, purity, and prosperity. It calls for the preservation of natural resources and serves as a reminder of our responsibility to care for the environment. By invoking the qualities of purity and abundance, it encourages individuals and communities to strive for a harmonious relationship with nature, ensuring that future generations can also benefit from the blessings of clean and life-giving waters.
