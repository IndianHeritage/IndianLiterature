वृषासि दिवो वृषभः पृथिव्या वृषा सिंधूनां वृषभः स्तियानाम् |वृष्णे त इन्दुर्वृषभ पीपाय स्वादू रसो मधुपेयो वराय ||
vṛṣāsi divo vṛṣabhaḥ pṛthivyā vṛṣā sindhūnāṁ vṛṣabhaḥ stiyānām | vṛṣṇe ta indur vṛṣabha pīpāya svādū raso madhupeyo varāya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vṛṣāsi: You are strong or powerful.
    • divo: Of the heavens or sky.
    • vṛṣabhaḥ: The bull or the best among.
    • pṛthivyā: Of the earth.
    • vṛṣā: Strong or powerful.
    • sindhūnāṁ: Of the rivers.
    • stiyānām: Of the streams or flowing waters.
    • vṛṣṇe: To Vishnu or the divine.
    • ta: That.
    • indur: The moon.
    • pīpāya: May it nourish or provide sustenance.
    • svādū: Sweet or pleasant.
    • raso: Essence or taste.
    • madhupeyo: Like honey or sweet drink.
    • varāya: May it be chosen or selected.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the strength and power found in nature, particularly in the elements of the earth, sky, and rivers.
    • The reference to "vṛṣabhaḥ" (the bull) symbolizes strength and vitality, often associated with agricultural prosperity and the sustenance of life.
    • The mention of "indur" (the moon) signifies nourishment and the cyclical nature of life, as the moon influences tides and agricultural cycles.
    • The phrase "svādū raso madhupeyo" emphasizes the sweetness of life and the importance of enjoying the essence of existence, akin to savoring honey.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the strength of the earth and the nourishing qualities of water are vital for crop growth, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on the power of nature.
    • In personal life, the idea of savoring the sweetness of experiences can be seen in how people celebrate milestones, such as weddings or festivals, where food and joy are central.
    • The cyclical nature of the moon can be observed in how communities plan agricultural activities, festivals, and rituals around lunar phases, highlighting the interconnectedness of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, nourishment, and the sweetness of life, reminding us of our connection to nature and the divine.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition of the vital forces that sustain us, encouraging a harmonious relationship with the environment.
    • In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to appreciate the resources we have, to celebrate life's sweetness, and to acknowledge the cycles of nature that influence our existence.
