द्यम्नी वां स्तोमो अश्विनाः कृविर न सेका आ गतम् |मध्वः सुतस्य स दिवि प्रियो नराः पातं गौराव इवेरीन ||
dyumnī vāṁ stomo aśvinā krivir na seka ā gatam | madhvaḥ sutasya sa divi priyo narā pātaṁ gaurāv iveriṇe ||
  • dyumnī vāṁ stomo aśvinā krivir na seka ā gatam
    • dyumnī: This word can be interpreted as "glory" or "brightness." It signifies the radiance and brilliance associated with success and achievement.
    • vāṁ: This means "your" or "of you," indicating a personal connection or address to the divine or a higher power.
    • stomo: This refers to "praise" or "hymn," suggesting the act of offering reverence or acknowledgment to the divine forces.
    • aśvinā: The Aśvins are twin deities in Vedic mythology, associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between opposites.
    • krivir: This term can be understood as "to bring forth" or "to create," indicating the act of manifestation or realization of desires.
    • na seka ā gatam: This phrase translates to "do not let it be lost" or "do not let it fade away," emphasizing the importance of preserving and nurturing what is achieved.
  • madhvaḥ sutasya sa divi priyo narā pātaṁ gaurāv iveriṇe
    • madhvaḥ: This word means "sweet" or "pleasant," often referring to the sweetness of life or the joy that comes from fulfilling experiences.
    • sutasya: This translates to "of the son" or "of the offspring," indicating a connection to lineage, heritage, and the continuation of values through generations.
    • sa divi: This means "in the heavens" or "in the divine realm," suggesting a connection to higher spiritual truths or divine blessings.
    • priyo: This means "beloved" or "dear," indicating the affection and importance of relationships, whether with the divine or among people.
    • narā: This translates to "men" or "humans," emphasizing the human experience and the collective journey of individuals.
    • pātaṁ: This means "protection" or "safeguarding," highlighting the need for security and support in life.
    • gaurāv iveriṇe: This phrase can be interpreted as "like the shining light," symbolizing hope, guidance, and the pursuit of enlightenment.

Conclusion:

The mantra encapsulates themes of glory, divine connection, and the importance of nurturing achievements. It serves as a reminder to appreciate the sweetness of life and the relationships that enrich our existence. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to recognize their achievements and maintain their focus on growth.
  • In relationships, it emphasizes the importance of cherishing loved ones and fostering connections that bring joy and support.
  • In spiritual practices, it serves as a reminder to seek divine guidance and protection in one's journey.

Overall, this mantra highlights the significance of balance, appreciation, and the pursuit of a fulfilling life, encouraging individuals to embrace their journey with gratitude and awareness.
