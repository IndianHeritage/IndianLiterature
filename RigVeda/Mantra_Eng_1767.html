दिव्यं सुपर्णं वायसम बृहन्तम अपां गर्भं दर्शतम ओषधीनाम् |अभिपतो वृष्टिभिः तर्पयन्तं सरस्वन्तं अवसे जोहवीमि ||
divyaṁ suparṇaṁ vāyasam bṛhantam apāṁ garbhaṁ darśatam oṣadhīnām | abhīpato vṛṣṭibhis tarpayantaṁ sarasvantam avase johavīmi ||
  • Divyaṁ suparṇaṁ vāyasam bṛhantam: This phrase refers to a divine, magnificent bird, often interpreted as the mythical Garuda. In real life, this can symbolize the pursuit of higher knowledge and enlightenment, akin to how people strive for personal growth and understanding in their lives.
  • Apāṁ garbhaṁ darśatam oṣadhīnām: This translates to the essence or the womb of waters, revealing the hidden treasures of plants. In practical terms, it can be related to the importance of nature and the environment, emphasizing how water is essential for life and growth, much like how we rely on natural resources for sustenance.
  • Abhīpato vṛṣṭibhis tarpayantaṁ: This part speaks to the fulfillment of desires through rains. In a contemporary context, it can be linked to agriculture and the significance of rain in ensuring food security, highlighting the interdependence between nature and human needs.
  • Sarasvantam avase johavīmi: This phrase indicates the act of invoking or calling upon the divine for abundance and prosperity. In everyday life, this can be seen in rituals or prayers for success in endeavors, whether in business, education, or personal aspirations.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for prosperity, growth, and fulfillment in life. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, spirituality, and human existence. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as environmental conservation, agricultural practices, and personal development. By recognizing the significance of nature and invoking divine support, individuals can strive for a balanced and prosperous life.
