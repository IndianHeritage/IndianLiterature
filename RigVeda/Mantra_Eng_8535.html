सम अस्य हरिं हरयो मृजन्त्य अश्वहयैर अनिशितं नमोभिः |आ तिष्ठति रथम इन्द्रस्य सखा विद्वांं एना सुमतिं यात्य अच्छ ||
sam asya hariṁ harayo mṛjanty aśvahayair aniśitaṁ namobhiḥ | ā tiṣṭhati ratham indrasya sakhā vidvām̐ enā sumatiṁ yāty accha ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "The horses of the chariot of Indra, the great god, are being wiped clean by the haris (the swift ones). With unwavering devotion, I bow to them. The chariot of Indra stands firm, and the wise one who knows this attains good understanding."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • sam asya: "of this" or "of the chariot" - indicating the focus on the chariot of Indra.
    • hariṁ: "the swift ones" or "the horses" - symbolizing speed and power.
    • harayo: "the haris" - referring to the divine beings or forces that assist in the journey.
    • mṛjanty: "are being wiped clean" - signifies purification and readiness for the journey.
    • aśvahayair: "by the horses" - emphasizing the role of the horses in the chariot.
    • aniśitaṁ: "unwavering" or "steadfast" - representing the stability and strength of the chariot.
    • namobhiḥ: "with devotion" - an expression of reverence and humility.
    • ā tiṣṭhati: "stands firm" - indicating the strength and reliability of Indra's chariot.
    • ratham: "the chariot" - a symbol of the journey of life and the pursuit of goals.
    • indrasya: "of Indra" - referring to the king of gods, representing power and authority.
    • sakhā: "friend" - indicating companionship and support in the journey.
    • vidvām̐: "the wise one" - someone who possesses knowledge and understanding.
    • enā: "this" - referring to the wisdom or understanding being sought.
    • sumatiṁ: "good understanding" - representing clarity of thought and wisdom.
    • yāty: "attains" - signifies the achievement of goals through wisdom.
    • accha: "good" - indicating positive outcomes and success.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, the mantra can be likened to a team preparing for a project. Just as the horses are readied for the chariot, team members must be prepared and aligned to achieve success.
    • In personal development, the idea of purification and readiness can be applied to self-reflection and growth. One must cleanse their thoughts and intentions to move forward effectively.
    • In relationships, the concept of companionship and support is crucial. Just as Indra's chariot is supported by his friends, individuals must rely on their support systems to navigate life's challenges.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on preparation, support, and wisdom. It teaches us that in our journeys—whether personal, professional, or spiritual—we must be ready, seek companionship, and strive for understanding. By embodying these principles, we can navigate life's challenges with strength and clarity, ultimately leading to success and fulfillment.
