तं प्रात्नथा पूर्वथा विश्वतेमथा ज्येष्ठतातिम बर्हिषदं स्वरविदम् |प्रतीचीनं वृजनं दोहसे गिराशुं जयन्तं अनु यासु वर्धसे ||
tam pratnathā pūrvathā viśvathemathā jyeṣṭhatātim barhiṣadaṁ svarvidam | pratīcīnaṁ vṛjanaṁ dohase girāśuṁ jayantam anu yāsu vardhase ||
  • tam pratnathā: This phrase can be interpreted as "that which is the source of all." It signifies the ultimate reality or the divine essence that underlies everything in existence.
  • pūrvathā: Meaning "from the east" or "the origin," this word emphasizes the importance of beginnings and the source of light and knowledge.
  • viśvathemathā: This term can be understood as "with universal understanding." It suggests a holistic perspective that encompasses all aspects of life and existence.
  • jyeṣṭhatātim: Translated as "the greatest," this word highlights the supremacy of the divine or the ultimate truth over all other entities.
  • barhiṣadaṁ: This refers to "the sacred grass," which is often used in rituals and symbolizes purity and the connection between the earthly and the divine.
  • svarvidam: Meaning "knowledge of the heavens," this term points to the understanding of cosmic principles and the divine order of the universe.
  • pratīcīnaṁ: This word means "the past" or "ancient," indicating the wisdom and knowledge that has been passed down through generations.
  • vṛjanaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the gathering" or "the community," emphasizing the importance of collective wisdom and shared experiences.
  • dohase: Meaning "to nourish" or "to support," this term highlights the nurturing aspect of the divine and the importance of sustenance in life.
  • girāśuṁ: This refers to "the voice" or "the sound," symbolizing communication and the power of words in shaping reality.
  • jayantam: Translated as "victorious," this word signifies triumph and success, particularly in the pursuit of knowledge and truth.
  • anu yāsu: This phrase means "in accordance with" or "following," indicating the importance of aligning oneself with the divine will or cosmic order.
  • vardhase: Meaning "to grow" or "to flourish," this term emphasizes the potential for growth and development in life when one is in harmony with the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking knowledge, understanding the divine, and recognizing the interconnectedness of all existence. It encourages individuals to draw from ancient wisdom, nurture their communities, and strive for personal and collective growth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in educational settings where holistic learning is emphasized, in community-building efforts that focus on shared values and experiences, and in personal development practices that align with one's higher purpose. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the importance of connecting with the divine source and fostering a sense of unity and growth in all aspects of life.
