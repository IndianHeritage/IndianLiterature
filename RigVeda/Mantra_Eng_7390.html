अव यत स्वे साधस्ते देवानां दुरमतीर ईक्षे |राजन् अप द्विषः सेध मीड्ह्वो अप श्रिधः सेध ||
ava yat sve sadhasthe devānāṁ durmatīr īkṣe | rājann apa dviṣaḥ sedha mīḍhvo apa sridhaḥ sedha ||
  • Mantra: "ava yat sve sadhasthe devānāṁ durmatīr īkṣe | rājann apa dviṣaḥ sedha mīḍhvo apa sridhaḥ sedha ||"
  • Translation: "O King, may the evil thoughts of those who are envious of us be removed; may the divine beings look upon us with favor."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ava: away, remove
    • yat: that which
    • sve: in our own
    • sadhasthe: in the good place, in the right position
    • devānāṁ: of the divine beings
    • durmatīr: evil thoughts, ill intentions
    • īkṣe: look upon, regard
    • rājann: O King
    • apa: away from
    • dviṣaḥ: of the envious
    • sedha: remove, dispel
    • mīḍhvo: of the good
    • sridhaḥ: of prosperity
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for protection against negative influences and ill intentions from others.
    • The invocation of divine beings signifies a call for higher powers to intervene and safeguard the individual or community.
    • The term "O King" can be interpreted as addressing a leader or authority figure, emphasizing the importance of wise and benevolent leadership in society.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in situations where one feels threatened by jealousy or negativity from others, such as in competitive environments like workplaces or schools.
    • For example, a student facing bullying or a professional dealing with workplace rivalry can recite this mantra to seek divine protection and support.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the power of positive thinking and divine intervention in overcoming adversity.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek protection from negative energies and to cultivate a mindset focused on goodness and prosperity.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to foster a supportive community and to rise above envy and malice, promoting harmony and well-being.
    • Ultimately, this mantra can be a source of strength and reassurance, guiding individuals through challenging times while invoking the support of higher powers.
