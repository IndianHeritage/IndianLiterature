मन्त्र: उता नो गोमतीर इषा आ वहा दूहितर दिवः |साकं सूर्यस्य रश्मिभिः शुक्रैः शोचद्भिर आर्चिभिः सुजाते अश्वसूऋते ||
uta no gomatīr iṣa ā vahā duhitar divaḥ | sākaṁ sūryasya raśmibhiḥ śukraiḥ śocadbhir arcibhiḥ sujāte aśvasūnṛte ||
  • Translation: "May the cows, the daughters of the sky, bring us wealth; together with the rays of the sun, may they shine brightly with their pure light, and may they bless us with good fortune and prosperity."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Uta: A term often used to invoke or call upon something.
    • No: Refers to "us" or "our," indicating a collective desire.
    • Gomatīr: Derived from "gomati," meaning "cows" or "wealth," symbolizing prosperity and nourishment.
    • Iṣa: Means "wealth" or "prosperity," often associated with abundance.
    • Ā vahā: Translates to "may bring" or "may carry," indicating a request for blessings.
    • Duhitar: Means "daughter," symbolizing the nurturing aspect of wealth.
    • Divaḥ: Refers to "of the sky," indicating a divine or celestial origin.
    • Sākaṁ: Means "together," emphasizing unity in the pursuit of prosperity.
    • Sūryasya: Refers to "of the sun," symbolizing light, energy, and life.
    • Raśmibhiḥ: Means "rays," indicating the illuminating power of the sun.
    • Śukraiḥ: Translates to "pure" or "bright," emphasizing clarity and positivity.
    • Śocadbhir: Means "shining" or "illuminating," indicating the blessings of light.
    • Arcibhiḥ: Refers to "worship" or "offerings," indicating reverence and gratitude.
    • Su-jāte: Means "well-born" or "noble," indicating the quality of the blessings sought.
    • Aśvasūnṛte: Refers to "good fortune" or "prosperity," often associated with success and abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the mantra can be seen as a prayer for a bountiful harvest, where the "cows" symbolize the livestock that provide sustenance and wealth to farmers.
    • In business, invoking this mantra can represent the collective effort of a team working together to achieve success, akin to the "rays of the sun" illuminating their path.
    • In personal life, it can be a reminder to seek purity and positivity in one's actions, ensuring that the blessings received are well-deserved and noble.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of prosperity, unity, and divine blessings. It emphasizes the importance of collective effort and purity in the pursuit of wealth and success. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts, such as agriculture, business, and personal growth, serving as a reminder to seek abundance while maintaining integrity and gratitude. By embracing the principles encapsulated in this mantra, individuals and communities can foster an environment conducive to prosperity and well-being.
