मन्त्र: दिवो न यस्य विधातो नवीनोद वृषा रुक्ष ओषधिषु नूनोत् |घृणा न यो ध्रजसा पत्मना यन्न आरोदसी वसुनां दं सुपत्नि ||
divo na yasya vidhato navīnod vṛṣā rukṣa oṣadhīṣu nūnot | ghṛṇā na yo dhrajasā patmanā yann ā rodasī vasunā daṁ supatnī ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Divo: Of the heavens
    • Na: Not
    • Yasya: Whose
    • Vidhato: Creator or maker
    • Navīnod: New or fresh
    • Vṛṣā: Strong or powerful
    • Rukṣa: Dry or harsh
    • Oṣadhīṣu: Among the plants or herbs
    • Nūnot: To be nourished or sustained
    • Ghṛṇā: Compassion or understanding
    • Na: Not
    • Yo: Who
    • Dhrajasā: With strength or might
    • Patmanā: With a flower or blossom
    • Yann: That which
    • Ā: To
    • Rodasī: The earth or the world
    • Vasunā: With wealth or abundance
    • Daṁ: To give or grant
    • Supatnī: Good or auspicious
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine qualities of creation and the sustenance of life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of compassion and strength in nurturing the earth and its resources.
    • The reference to "new" and "strong" suggests a continuous cycle of renewal and vitality in nature.
    • It highlights the relationship between the heavens and the earth, indicating that the divine presence is essential for the flourishing of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, understanding the balance of nature and using sustainable practices can be seen as a way to honor the divine qualities mentioned in the mantra.
    • In personal relationships, showing compassion and strength can lead to nurturing and supportive environments, akin to how the earth is nourished.
    • Environmental conservation efforts reflect the essence of this mantra, as they aim to protect and sustain the earth's resources for future generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of all life and the divine forces that sustain it.
    • It serves as a reminder to cultivate compassion and strength in our actions, whether in personal relationships, community involvement, or environmental stewardship.
    • By embodying these qualities, we contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous world, reflecting the essence of the divine in our daily lives.
