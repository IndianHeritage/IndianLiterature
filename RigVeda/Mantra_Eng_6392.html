तुविग्रीवो वपोदरः सुभाहुरन्दसो मदे |इन्द्रो वृत्राणि जिघ्नते ||
tuvigrīvo vapodaraḥ subāhur andhaso made | indro vṛtrāṇi jighnate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tuvigrīvo: One who is strong and powerful.
    • vapodaraḥ: The one who has a great body or form.
    • subāhur: One who has beautiful arms.
    • andhaso: Of the blind or darkness.
    • made: In intoxication or joy.
    • indro: Indra, the king of gods.
    • vṛtrāṇi: The obstacles or enemies.
    • jighnate: To slay or destroy.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the powerful deity known for his strength and ability to overcome obstacles.
    • It describes Indra as having a strong and beautiful form, capable of bringing joy and light where there is darkness.
    • The mantra emphasizes the idea of overcoming challenges and adversities, symbolized by the term "vṛtrāṇi," which refers to enemies or obstacles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, this mantra can be related to overcoming challenges in life, such as facing fears or obstacles in career or relationships. For instance, someone might invoke this strength when preparing for a job interview or a significant life change.
    • In a broader societal context, it can symbolize the fight against social injustices or challenges faced by communities. Activists often embody the spirit of Indra when they confront systemic issues, striving to bring about positive change.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the inner strength and resilience that individuals possess to overcome difficulties.
    • It encourages the belief that with determination and courage, one can face and conquer the challenges that life presents.
    • In daily life, this mantra can be a source of inspiration, motivating individuals to seek strength in themselves and to act against the darkness or obstacles they encounter.
