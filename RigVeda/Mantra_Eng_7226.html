मन्त्र: उरु णस तन्बे तना उरु क्षयाय नस् कृधि | उरु णो यन्द्धि जीवसे ||
uru ṇas tanve3 tana uru kṣayāya nas kṛdhi | uru ṇo yandhi jīvase ||
  • uru: This word translates to "great" or "vast." It signifies the expansive nature of existence and the universe.
  • ṇa: This is a particle that can denote negation or absence, often used to emphasize the importance of what follows.
  • tanve: This means "body" or "form." It refers to the physical manifestation of life.
  • tana: Similar to "tanve," it also refers to the body or physical form.
  • kṣayāya: This word means "for destruction" or "to perish." It indicates the transient nature of life and existence.
  • nas: This means "us" or "to us," indicating a collective or communal aspect.
  • kṛdhi: This translates to "do" or "make," suggesting an action or a request for creation.
  • yandhi: This means "to live" or "to thrive," emphasizing the importance of life and vitality.
  • jīvase: This is derived from "jīva," meaning "life" or "living being," reinforcing the focus on existence and vitality.

  • Meaning of the Mantra: The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for the enhancement of life and vitality. It acknowledges the vastness of existence and the physical form while recognizing the inevitability of destruction. The speaker is asking for the creation of a life that thrives and flourishes despite the transient nature of existence.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, this mantra can inspire individuals to embrace their physical and spiritual forms while acknowledging the challenges and impermanence of life. For instance, someone facing a health crisis may find strength in the idea of thriving despite adversity.
    • In environmental conservation, the mantra can serve as a reminder of the vastness of nature and the importance of protecting it. Communities can come together to create sustainable practices that enhance life on Earth, recognizing that while nature may face destruction, collective action can lead to rejuvenation.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic view of life, urging individuals and communities to recognize the beauty and fragility of existence. It encourages proactive engagement with life, fostering resilience and vitality. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in personal growth, community building, and environmental stewardship, reminding us to thrive and create meaning in the face of life's impermanence.
