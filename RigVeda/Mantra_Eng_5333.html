सखायस त इन्द्र विश्वहा स्याम नमोवृद्धासो महिना तरुत्र |वन्वन्तु स्मा ते 'वसा समीके3 'भीतिम आर्यो वनुषां शवांसि ||
sakhāyas ta indra viśvaha syāma namovṛdhāso mahinā tarutra | vanvantu smā te 'vasā samīke3 'bhītim aryo vanuṣāṁ śavāṁsi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sakhāyas: Friends or companions
    • ta: Those
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • viśvaha: All-encompassing or universal
    • syāma: May we be
    • namovṛdhāso: With reverence to the elders
    • mahinā: With greatness or strength
    • tarutra: In the trees or in the forest
    • vanvantu: May they flourish
    • smā: May we remember
    • te: You
    • 'avasā: In your presence
    • samīke: In harmony
    • abhītim: Fearlessness
    • aryo: Noble or honorable
    • vanuṣāṁ: Of the beings in the forest
    • śavāṁsi: Bodies or forms
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a desire for unity and strength among friends and companions.
    • It invokes the blessings of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, symbolizing power and protection.
    • The phrase "may we be in harmony" emphasizes the importance of community and collaboration.
    • It encourages fearlessness and nobility among beings, suggesting that strength comes from unity and respect for one another.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, this mantra can be applied to foster teamwork and collaboration, where employees support each other and work towards common goals.
    • In community service, it can inspire individuals to come together to help those in need, promoting a sense of belonging and mutual respect.
    • In personal relationships, it encourages friends and family to stand by each other, creating a supportive environment that nurtures growth and fearlessness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of friendship, unity, and strength in overcoming challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder that by working together and respecting one another, we can create a harmonious and fearless community.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to stronger relationships, effective teamwork, and a supportive environment that fosters growth and resilience.
