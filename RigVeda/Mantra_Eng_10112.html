कश्चन्दसां योगमावेद धीरः को धिष्ण्यां प्रति वाचं पपाद |कम ऋत्विजां अष्टमं शूरमाहुर हरि इन्द्रस्य न चिकाय कः स्वित ||
kaś chandasāṁ yogam ā veda dhīraḥ ko dhiṣṇyām prati vācam papāda | kam ṛtvijām aṣṭamaṁ śūram āhur harī indrasya ni cikāya kaḥ svit ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kaś: Who
    • chandasāṁ: Of the hymns or verses
    • yogam: The union or connection
    • ā veda: Knows or understands
    • dhīraḥ: The wise or the intelligent
    • ko: Who
    • dhiṣṇyām: In the place of worship or the altar
    • prati: Towards
    • vācam: The speech or utterance
    • papāda: Has come forth or manifested
    • kam: Who
    • ṛtvijām: Of the priests or the officiants
    • aṣṭamaṁ: The eighth
    • śūram: The brave or the valiant
    • āhur: They say or declare
    • harī: The divine or the auspicious
    • indrasya: Of Indra (the king of gods)
    • ni: In or within
    • cikāya: Has been established or set
    • kaḥ: Who
    • svit: Indeed or truly
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a series of questions about knowledge, wisdom, and the divine.
    • It asks who among the wise understands the connection of the hymns and who has manifested the speech at the altar.
    • It also inquires about the eighth priest who is considered brave and has been established in the divine realm of Indra.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader may ask who among the team understands the vision (the hymns) and can articulate it effectively (the speech) to inspire others.
    • In education, a teacher may seek to identify the student who can connect various concepts (the union) and express them clearly in discussions.
    • In community service, volunteers may look for the brave individuals (the eighth priest) who take initiative and lead efforts to help others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, understanding, and effective communication in various aspects of life.
    • It highlights the need for brave individuals who can lead and inspire others in their pursuits.
    • In real life, the principles of this mantra can be applied in leadership, education, and community engagement, reminding us to seek knowledge and articulate our thoughts clearly for the benefit of all.
