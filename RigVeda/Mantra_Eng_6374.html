यस्मिन्नुक्तानि रण्यन्ति विश्वानि च श्रवस्याः |अपाम् अवो न समुद्रे ||
yasminn ukthāni raṇyanti viśvāni ca śravasyā | apām avo na samudre ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasminn: In which
    • ukthāni: utterances or hymns
    • raṇyanti: resonate or vibrate
    • viśvāni: all or universal
    • ca: and
    • śravasyā: of fame or glory
    • apām: of waters
    • avo: protect or guard
    • na: not
    • samudre: in the ocean
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of words and hymns that resonate universally.
    • It emphasizes the significance of utterances that carry fame and glory.
    • The latter part suggests a protective aspect of water, indicating that not all waters (or perhaps not all forms of expression) are to be found in the vast ocean.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, the stories and teachings passed down through generations can resonate with individuals, creating a shared sense of identity and purpose.
    • In modern times, social media platforms serve as a medium where powerful messages can resonate globally, influencing public opinion and fostering movements.
    • In environmental discussions, the metaphor of water can represent the need to protect our natural resources, emphasizing that not all expressions of nature are beneficial or pure.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of words and their impact on society and the environment.
    • It serves as a reminder that while some expressions can resonate positively, others may not contribute to the greater good.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to be mindful of their words and actions, understanding their potential to influence and protect the world around them.
