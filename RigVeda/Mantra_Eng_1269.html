मन्त्र: भद्राऽश्वा हरितः सूर्यस्य चित्रा एतग्वा अनुमाद्याशः | नमस्यन्तो दिव आ पृष्ठम् अस्तुः परि द्यावापृथिवी यन्ति सद्यः ||
bhadrā aśvā haritaḥ sūryasya citrā etagvā anumādyāsaḥ | namasyanto diva ā pṛṣṭham asthuḥ pari dyāvāpṛthivī yanti sadyaḥ ||
  • bhadrā: This word translates to "auspicious" or "beneficial." It signifies the importance of positive and favorable conditions in life.
  • aśvā: Meaning "horses," this term symbolizes strength, speed, and the ability to overcome obstacles. In ancient times, horses were vital for transportation and warfare, representing power and mobility.
  • haritaḥ: This word means "green" or "prosperous," often associated with nature, fertility, and abundance. It reflects the importance of a healthy environment for growth and sustenance.
  • sūryasya: Translating to "of the sun," this term emphasizes the sun's role as a source of light, energy, and life. The sun is often seen as a divine entity that nurtures all living beings.
  • citrā: This word means "variegated" or "colorful," suggesting diversity and richness in life experiences. It highlights the beauty found in variety and the importance of embracing differences.
  • etagvā: This term can be interpreted as "these" or "this," indicating a connection to the preceding elements and their significance in the mantra.
  • anumādyāsaḥ: This word means "those who are worthy of praise" or "who are honored." It reflects the idea of recognizing and valuing those who contribute positively to society.
  • namasyanto: Meaning "those who bow down" or "who show respect," this term emphasizes the importance of humility and reverence in relationships and interactions.
  • diva: This word translates to "heaven" or "sky," representing the spiritual realm and the connection between the earthly and divine.
  • ā: A preposition meaning "towards" or "to," indicating direction or movement.
  • pṛṣṭham: This term means "back" or "behind," suggesting a sense of support or foundation that is essential for progress.
  • asthuḥ: This word translates to "may it be" or "let it be," expressing a wish or intention for positive outcomes.
  • pari: Meaning "around" or "surrounding," this term indicates the encompassing nature of the elements mentioned in the mantra.
  • dyāvāpṛthivī: This compound word refers to "heaven and earth," symbolizing the duality of existence and the interconnectedness of all things.
  • yanti: This word means "they go" or "they move," indicating progress, journey, or transformation.
  • sadyaḥ: Meaning "immediately" or "right away," this term emphasizes the urgency and importance of the actions described in the mantra.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the significance of auspiciousness, strength, respect, and the interconnectedness of life. It encourages individuals to recognize the beauty in diversity, honor those who contribute positively to society, and maintain a respectful relationship with both the divine and the natural world. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as fostering a positive work environment, promoting environmental sustainability, and encouraging respectful interactions among individuals. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, one can contribute to a harmonious and prosperous society.
