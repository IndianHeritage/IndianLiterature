स इधाना उषसो रम्या अनु स्वर्ण न दीदेद अरुसेण भानुना |होत्राभिर अग्निर मनुष्यः स्वध्वरो राजा विश्वाम अतिथिश चारुर आयवे ||
sa idhāna uṣaso rāmyā anu sva1r ṇa dīded aruṣeṇa bhānunā | hotrābhir agnir manuṣaḥ svadhvaro rājā viśām atithiś cārur āyave ||
  • sa idhāna - This phrase can be interpreted as "he who is present" or "he who is here." It signifies the presence of a divine or powerful entity.
  • uṣaso rāmyā - "uṣaso" refers to the dawn, and "rāmyā" means beautiful. Together, they evoke the image of a beautiful dawn, symbolizing new beginnings and hope.
  • anu sva1r - This can be understood as "following the sound" or "in harmony with the sound," suggesting a connection to the cosmic order and the natural rhythms of life.
  • ṇa dīded - This phrase means "may it shine" or "may it illuminate," indicating a desire for enlightenment and clarity.
  • aruṣeṇa bhānunā - "aruṣeṇa" means "with the red one," referring to the sun, and "bhānunā" means "by the light." This emphasizes the importance of sunlight in bringing life and energy to the world.
  • hotrābhir agnir - "hotrābhir" refers to the priests or those who invoke, and "agnir" means fire. This highlights the role of fire in rituals and its significance in connecting the earthly and divine realms.
  • manuṣaḥ svadhvaro - "manuṣaḥ" means "human" and "svadhvaro" can be interpreted as "the one who is self-chosen" or "the one who has authority." This suggests the empowerment of individuals to take charge of their destinies.
  • rājā viśām - "rājā" means "king" and "viśām" refers to "of the people" or "of the community." This indicates the importance of leadership that serves the community.
  • atithiś cārur āyave - "atithiś" means "guest" or "stranger," "cārur" means "beautiful," and "āyave" refers to "in abundance." This emphasizes the value of hospitality and the beauty of welcoming others into our lives.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates themes of presence, beauty, enlightenment, empowerment, leadership, and hospitality. In real life, it serves as a reminder to embrace new beginnings with hope, to seek clarity and understanding, and to take responsibility for our actions. It encourages us to lead with compassion and to welcome others into our lives, fostering a sense of community and connection. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to live harmoniously with the world around them, promoting a balanced and fulfilling life.
