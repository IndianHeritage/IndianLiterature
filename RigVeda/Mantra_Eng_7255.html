अषाल्हम् उग्रं पृथनासु सासहिं यस्मिन महीर उरुज्रयः |सं धेनवो जायमानें अनोनवुर dyāvaḥ क्षामो अनोनवुः ||
aṣāḻham ugram pṛtanāsu sāsahiṁ yasmin mahīr urujrayaḥ | saṁ dhenavo jāyamāne anonavur dyāvaḥ kṣāmo anonavuḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aṣāḻham: Unyielding, strong
    • ugram: Fierce, powerful
    • pṛtanāsu: In the realms of existence
    • sāsahiṁ: With strength, with support
    • yasmin: In which
    • mahīr: Of the earth, of the great
    • urujrayaḥ: Expansive, vast
    • saṁ: Together, in unity
    • dhenavo: Cows, symbols of nourishment
    • jāyamāne: Being born, emerging
    • anonavur: Not lacking, abundant
    • dyāvaḥ: The heavens
    • kṣāmo: Earthly, grounded
    • anonavuḥ: Not lacking, plentiful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the strength and power inherent in the universe.
    • It emphasizes the unity of the earth and heavens, suggesting that they work together in harmony.
    • The mention of "cows" symbolizes nourishment and abundance, indicating that life flourishes when there is support and strength.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the relationship between the earth (soil) and the heavens (weather) is crucial for a successful harvest. Just as the mantra suggests unity, farmers rely on both elements to produce food.
    • In community living, the strength of individuals coming together can create a supportive environment, much like the "cows" that symbolize nourishment. For instance, community gardens thrive when people collaborate and share resources.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of strength, unity, and abundance in life.
    • It teaches us that when we work together, drawing on the strengths of both the earth and the heavens, we can create a nurturing environment that fosters growth and prosperity.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, community efforts, or environmental stewardship, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide us toward a more harmonious existence.
