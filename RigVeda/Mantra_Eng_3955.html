प्र साक्षणो दिव्यः कण्वहोतā तृतो दिवः सजोषा वातो अग्निः |पुषा भगः प्रभरथे विश्वभोजा आजिं न जग्मुर आश्वश्वतमाः ||
pra sakṣaṇo divyaḥ kaṇvahotā trito divaḥ sajoṣā vāto agniḥ | pūṣā bhagaḥ prabhṛthe viśvabhojā ājiṁ na jagmur āśvaśvatamāḥ ||
  • pra sakṣaṇo: This phrase can be interpreted as "with the divine vision" or "with the ability to see clearly." It emphasizes the importance of perception and awareness in our lives.
  • divyaḥ: Meaning "divine" or "heavenly," this word suggests a connection to higher powers or spiritual realms.
  • kaṇvahotā: This term refers to the "invoker" or "one who calls upon," indicating the act of invoking divine energies or blessings.
  • trito divaḥ: Translated as "the third heaven," this phrase signifies a higher state of consciousness or spiritual elevation.
  • sajoṣā: Meaning "with joy" or "with happiness," it highlights the importance of joy in spiritual practices and life.
  • vāto agniḥ: This translates to "the wind and fire," symbolizing the elements that are essential for life and transformation.
  • pūṣā bhagaḥ: "Pūṣā" refers to nourishment or sustenance, while "bhagaḥ" means "prosperity" or "wealth." Together, they signify the abundance that comes from divine blessings.
  • prabhṛthe: This word means "with great power" or "with strength," indicating the force of divine energy in our lives.
  • viśvabhojā: Meaning "the one who enjoys all," it suggests a universal connection and the idea of sharing and abundance.
  • ājiṁ na jagmur: This phrase can be interpreted as "they do not go away," indicating the permanence of divine presence and blessings in our lives.
  • āśvaśvatamāḥ: Translated as "the most eternal," it emphasizes the timeless nature of divine truths and principles.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of divine connection, perception, and the importance of joy and abundance in life. It teaches us to invoke higher energies and to recognize the divine presence in our everyday experiences. In real life, this can be applied through practices such as meditation, gratitude, and mindfulness, which help us cultivate awareness and joy. By acknowledging the divine in our lives, we can foster a sense of abundance and connection with the universe, leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
