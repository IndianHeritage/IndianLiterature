सङ्गच्छमाने युवती समन्ते स्वसारा जामी पितरोर उपस्थे |अभिजिघ्रन्ती भुवनस्य नाभिं dyावा रक्षतं पृथिवी नो अभ्वात ||
saṁgacchamāne yuvatī samante svasārā jāmī pitror upasthe | abhijighrantī bhuvanasya nābhiṁ dyāvā rakṣatam pṛthivī no abhvāt ||
  • saṁgacchamāne: This word translates to "coming together" or "unifying." It emphasizes the importance of unity and collaboration among individuals.
  • yuvatī: Refers to "young women" or "youth." This highlights the role of the younger generation in society.
  • samante: Means "from all sides" or "in all directions," indicating inclusivity and the collective effort of the community.
  • svasārā: Translates to "sisters," symbolizing familial bonds and the importance of relationships among women.
  • jāmī: Refers to "the one who binds" or "the one who connects," suggesting the role of relationships in creating unity.
  • pitror: Means "of the parents," emphasizing respect and reverence for parental figures.
  • upasthe: Translates to "in the presence" or "nearby," indicating the importance of being present for one another.
  • abhijighrantī: Means "to desire" or "to aspire," suggesting the pursuit of goals and aspirations.
  • bhuvanasya: Refers to "of the world" or "of the universe," indicating a broader perspective beyond individual desires.
  • nābhiṁ: Translates to "the navel," symbolizing the center or source of life and energy.
  • dyāvā: Means "heaven" or "sky," representing the spiritual or divine aspect of existence.
  • rakṣatam: Translates to "may protect" or "may safeguard," indicating a plea for protection and safety.
  • pṛthivī: Refers to "earth," symbolizing the physical world and our connection to it.
  • no abhvāt: Means "may it not be absent," expressing a desire for continuity and presence in relationships and life.

This mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, familial bonds, and the collective aspirations of individuals, particularly young women, in society. It calls for a harmonious coexistence where relationships are nurtured, and the community comes together for a common purpose. The plea for protection from the heavens and the earth signifies a deep connection to both the spiritual and physical realms.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Community Building: Encouraging collaboration among community members to achieve common goals, such as organizing events or supporting local initiatives.
  • Family Relationships: Fostering strong bonds among family members, particularly among siblings, to create a supportive environment.
  • Empowerment of Women: Promoting the aspirations and ambitions of young women, ensuring they have the support and resources to pursue their dreams.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of unity, respect for relationships, and the collective strength of individuals working together. It highlights the need for protection and support from both the spiritual and physical worlds, making it relevant in various aspects of life, from personal relationships to community engagement.
