मां नस्तोके तनये मां न आयौ मां नो गोषु मां नो अश्वेषु रीरिषः |वीरान मां नो रुद्र भामितो वधीः हविष्मन्तः सदमित त्वा हवामहे ||
mā nas toke tanaye mā na āyau mā no goṣu mā no aśveṣu rīriṣaḥ | vīrān mā no rudra bhāmito vadhīr haviṣmantaḥ sadam it tvā havāmahe ||
  • mā nas toke tanaye: This phrase translates to "May we not be deprived of our children." It emphasizes the importance of progeny and the continuity of family lineage. In real life, this can be related to the universal desire for families to thrive and for future generations to carry on traditions and values.
  • mā na āyau: This means "May we not be harmed." It reflects a plea for protection and safety. In contemporary life, this can be seen in the context of seeking security in various aspects, such as physical safety, emotional well-being, and financial stability.
  • mā no goṣu: This translates to "May we not be deprived of our cattle." Cattle were a symbol of wealth and prosperity in ancient times. Today, this can be interpreted as a desire for abundance and resources, whether it be in agriculture, business, or personal life.
  • mā no aśveṣu rīriṣaḥ: This means "May we not be deprived of our horses." Horses were vital for transportation and warfare. In modern terms, this can symbolize the need for mobility and progress in life, whether through education, career advancement, or personal growth.
  • vīrān mā no rudra bhāmito vadhīr: This phrase translates to "May we not be harmed by the fierce ones." It is a call for protection from those who may wish to do harm. In real life, this can relate to the need for safeguarding oneself from negative influences, whether they be people, situations, or even internal struggles.
  • haviṣmantaḥ sadam it tvā havāmahe: This means "We invoke you, O Agni, who is the lord of offerings." Agni represents the divine fire and is a symbol of transformation and purification. In everyday life, this can be seen as a call to seek guidance and support from higher powers or inner strength during challenging times.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for protection, prosperity, and the well-being of family and community. Its significance lies in the universal human desire for safety, abundance, and continuity. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios, such as family prayers for health and safety, community gatherings seeking collective well-being, or personal reflections on the importance of resilience and support in the face of challenges. By invoking these sentiments, individuals can foster a sense of unity and purpose in their lives and communities.
