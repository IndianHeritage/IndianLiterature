त्वं तु न इन्द्र तं रयिं दा ओजिष्ठया दक्षिणायेव रातिम् |स्तुतश्च यास ते चकनन्त वायोः स्तनं न मध्वः पीपयन्त वाजैः ||
tvaṁ tū na indra taṁ rayiṁ dā ojiṣṭhayā dakṣiṇayeva rātim | stutaś ca yās te cakananta vāyoḥ stanaṁ na madhvaḥ pīpayanta vājaiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • tū: indeed
    • na: not
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • taṁ: that
    • rayiṁ: wealth or prosperity
    • dā: give
    • ojiṣṭhayā: with strength or vigor
    • dakṣiṇayeva: like the south (often associated with abundance)
    • rātim: joy or delight
    • stutaś: praised
    • ca: and
    • yās: those who
    • te: your
    • cakananta: who are ever-growing
    • vāyoḥ: of the wind
    • stanaṁ: breast or heart
    • na: not
    • madhvaḥ: sweet
    • pīpayanta: drinking
    • vājaiḥ: with strength or vigor
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the god of rain and thunder, asking for prosperity and strength.
    • The speaker acknowledges Indra's power and seeks his blessings for wealth and joy.
    • The reference to "dakṣiṇayeva" suggests a connection to the south, which is often seen as a direction of abundance and fertility.
    • The phrase "stutaś ca yās te cakananta" indicates that those who praise Indra will receive his blessings, emphasizing the importance of devotion.
    • The imagery of the wind and the heart suggests a connection between nature and human emotions, highlighting the need for harmony with the environment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural societies, invoking Indra for rain is crucial for crop growth, symbolizing the dependence on divine forces for prosperity.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of gratitude and recognition of the forces that contribute to our well-being.
    • In personal development, the idea of seeking strength and joy can inspire individuals to pursue their goals with vigor and positivity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine blessings in achieving prosperity and joy.
    • It teaches the value of devotion and gratitude, encouraging individuals to recognize and appreciate the sources of their strength and happiness.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to seek harmony with nature and to cultivate a positive mindset, which can lead to personal and communal growth.
