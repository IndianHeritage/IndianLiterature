उप ते 'धां सहमानाम् अभि त्वाधां सहीयसा |माम् अनु प्र ते मनो वत्सं गौर इव धावतु पथा वार इव धावतु ||
upa te 'dhāṁ sahamānām abhi tvādhāṁ sahīyasā | mām anu pra te mano vatsaṁ gaur iva dhāvatu pathā vār iva dhāvatu ||
  • upa te 'dhāṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "I approach you" or "I come to you." It signifies a sense of seeking guidance or support from a higher power or divine entity.
  • sahamānām: This word means "those who are enduring" or "those who are patient." It reflects the quality of resilience and the ability to withstand challenges.
  • abhi tvādhāṁ: This translates to "I offer my devotion to you." It indicates a sense of surrender and reverence towards the divine.
  • sahīyasā: This term means "with strength" or "with power." It emphasizes the importance of inner strength and the ability to face adversities.
  • mām anu pra te mano: This phrase can be understood as "let my mind follow you." It expresses the desire for alignment of thoughts and intentions with the divine will.
  • vatsaṁ: This word means "like a calf" or "young one." It symbolizes innocence, purity, and the nurturing aspect of life.
  • gaur iva: This translates to "like a cow." Cows are often seen as symbols of nourishment and abundance in many cultures, particularly in Indian tradition.
  • dhāvatu: This means "may it run" or "may it move." It signifies the action of pursuing a path or goal.
  • pathā vār iva dhāvatu: This phrase can be interpreted as "may it run on the path." It emphasizes the importance of following the right path in life, guided by wisdom and virtue.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message of seeking divine guidance and strength while navigating life's challenges. It encourages individuals to approach the divine with humility and devotion, fostering resilience and alignment of thoughts with higher principles. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during times of difficulty or uncertainty, reminding individuals to remain patient, seek guidance, and pursue their goals with determination and integrity. It serves as a reminder that, like a calf following its mother, we too should follow the path of righteousness and nourishment, both spiritually and materially.
