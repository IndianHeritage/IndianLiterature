महान्तं महिना वयं स्तोमेभिर हवनश्रुतम् |अर्कैर अभि प्र णोनुमः सम ओजसे ||
mahāntam mahinā vayaṁ stomebhir havanaśrutam | arkair abhi pra ṇonumaḥ sam ojase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mahāntam: The great one, the vast, the supreme.
    • mahinā: By the power, by the strength.
    • vayaṁ: We, us.
    • stomebhir: With hymns, with praises.
    • havanaśrutam: Heard in the offerings, known through the sacrifices.
    • arkair: With the rays, with the light.
    • abhi: Towards, in the direction of.
    • pra ṇonumaḥ: We invoke, we praise.
    • sam: Together, in unity.
    • ojase: With strength, with energy.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of invoking the great power and strength through collective praise and offerings.
    • It emphasizes the importance of unity and collective effort in spiritual practices.
    • The reference to "heard in the offerings" suggests that the divine is responsive to sincere devotion and rituals.
    • The use of "light" symbolizes knowledge, enlightenment, and the divine presence that guides and protects.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate a festival, they invoke blessings through collective prayers, similar to the essence of this mantra.
    • In workplaces, team-building activities that emphasize collaboration and shared goals can be seen as a modern application of invoking strength through unity.
    • In personal life, when individuals face challenges, seeking support from family and friends can be a way of harnessing collective strength and energy.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the power of collective invocation and the importance of unity in spiritual and everyday life.
    • It teaches that through shared efforts and sincere devotion, individuals can connect with the divine and harness greater strength.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in community, workplace, or personal challenges, the principles of collaboration and collective strength can lead to success and fulfillment.
