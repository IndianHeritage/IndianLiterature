उग्रं न वीरं नमसोपा सेदिमा विभूतिम अक्षितावसुम् |उद्रीव वज्रिन्न अवतो न सिञ्चते क्षरन्तीन्द्र धीतयः ||
ugraṁ na vīraṁ namasopa sedima vibhūtim akṣitāvasum | udrīva vajrinn avato na siñcate kṣarantīndra dhītayaḥ ||
  • ugraṁ: This word translates to "fierce" or "powerful." It signifies strength and intensity, often associated with deities or forces of nature.
  • na vīraṁ: "na" means "not," and "vīraṁ" refers to "hero" or "brave one." Together, it suggests a call to the powerful forces to not overlook the brave or heroic.
  • namasopa: This can be interpreted as "salutations" or "obeisance." It indicates respect and reverence towards the divine or powerful forces.
  • sedima: This word means "to grant" or "to bestow." It implies a request for blessings or gifts from the divine.
  • vibhūtim: This translates to "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies abundance and the richness of life.
  • akṣitāvasum: "akṣita" means "unfading" or "eternal," and "avasum" refers to "wealth" or "resources." Together, it suggests a plea for everlasting prosperity.
  • udrīva: This word means "to elevate" or "to uplift." It indicates a desire for growth and ascension.
  • vajrinn: This translates to "like a thunderbolt" or "indestructible." It symbolizes strength and resilience.
  • avato: This means "to protect" or "to guard." It signifies the need for safeguarding and support from divine forces.
  • na siñcate: "na" means "not," and "siñcate" refers to "to sprinkle" or "to pour." This suggests a request for the divine not to withhold blessings.
  • kṣarantīndra: "kṣarantī" means "flowing" or "pouring," and "Indra" refers to the king of gods, often associated with rain and storms. This indicates a plea for the flow of divine blessings.
  • dhītayaḥ: This translates to "wisdom" or "intellect." It signifies the importance of knowledge and understanding in life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a deep reverence for powerful forces and a plea for their blessings. It emphasizes the importance of strength, wisdom, and the need for protection and prosperity in life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in various contexts such as seeking guidance during challenging times, invoking strength in personal endeavors, or fostering a sense of community and support. It serves as a reminder to respect the forces that govern our lives and to seek their blessings for a prosperous and fulfilling existence.
