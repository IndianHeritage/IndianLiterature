प्र विष्णवे शूषम् एतु मन्म गिरीक्षित उरुगायाय वृष्णे |य इदं दीर्घम् प्रयतं सदस्थम् एको विममे त्रिभिर इत पदेभिः ||
pra viṣṇave śūṣam etu manma girikṣita urugāyāya vṛṣṇe | ya idaṁ dīrgham prayataṁ sadhastham eko vimame tribhir it padebhiḥ ||
  • pra viṣṇave: This phrase can be translated as "to Vishnu," who is often regarded as the preserver and protector in Hindu mythology. Vishnu represents the sustaining force of the universe.
  • śūṣam etu: This means "may it come to us," indicating a desire for blessings or positive energy to flow into one's life.
  • manma girikṣita: This translates to "my thoughts are protected," suggesting a plea for divine guidance and safeguarding of one's mental and spiritual well-being.
  • urugāyāya: This term refers to "the one who is widely praised," highlighting the importance of reverence and acknowledgment of divine qualities.
  • vṛṣṇe: This is another name for Vishnu, emphasizing his role in the mantra.
  • ya idaṁ dīrgham: This means "whoever this long (life) is," which can be interpreted as a wish for longevity and prosperity.
  • prayataṁ: This translates to "with effort," indicating that one must actively seek and strive for their desires.
  • sadhastham: This means "in the assembly," which can refer to the gathering of people or community, emphasizing the importance of collective effort and support.
  • eko vimame: This translates to "one who contemplates," suggesting the importance of introspection and self-reflection in achieving one's goals.
  • tribhir it padebhiḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "with three steps," which may symbolize the journey of life and the stages one must navigate to reach their aspirations.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates a deep yearning for divine protection, guidance, and the pursuit of a meaningful life. It emphasizes the importance of community, effort, and introspection in achieving one's goals. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied when seeking clarity in decision-making, during challenging times when one needs support, or when embarking on new ventures. By invoking the qualities of Vishnu, individuals can find strength and resilience, reminding them that they are not alone in their journey. The mantra serves as a reminder to remain focused, work diligently, and seek the blessings of the divine in all endeavors.
