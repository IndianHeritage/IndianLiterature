ओषधयः सं वदन्ते सोमेन सह राज्ञा |यस्मै कृत्नोति ब्राह्मणस तं राजन पारयामसि ||
oṣadhayaḥ saṁ vadante somena saha rājñā | yasmai kṛṇoti brāhmaṇas taṁ rājan pārayāmasi ||
  • oṣadhayaḥ: This term refers to herbs or plants, often symbolizing the healing and nourishing aspects of nature.
  • saṁ vadante: This means "speak together" or "speak in unison," indicating a collective voice or agreement.
  • somena: Refers to 'Soma,' a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing vitality, immortality, and divine inspiration.
  • saha: This means "together" or "with," emphasizing unity and collaboration.
  • rājñā: This translates to "king" or "ruler," representing authority and governance.
  • yasmai: This means "to whom" or "for whom," indicating a recipient or beneficiary.
  • kṛṇoti: This means "makes" or "creates," suggesting an act of creation or offering.
  • brāhmaṇas: Refers to the priestly class or scholars, symbolizing knowledge and wisdom.
  • taṁ: This means "that" or "him," referring back to the subject of the mantra.
  • rājan: Again refers to "king," reinforcing the idea of leadership.
  • pārayāmasi: This means "we praise" or "we honor," indicating reverence and respect.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call for unity among the natural elements (represented by herbs), the divine (Soma), and the rulers (kings). It emphasizes the importance of collaboration between nature, knowledge, and leadership for the well-being of society.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Environmental Conservation: Just as the mantra speaks of herbs and nature, it reminds us of the importance of preserving our environment. For example, community efforts to plant trees or restore natural habitats reflect this unity with nature.
  • Leadership and Governance: The role of leaders in society is crucial. This mantra suggests that wise leaders (brāhmaṇas) should work together with the community (oṣadhayaḥ) to create a harmonious society. For instance, local governments collaborating with citizens to address community issues exemplifies this principle.
  • Collective Action: The idea of speaking together (saṁ vadante) can be seen in social movements where individuals unite for a common cause, such as climate action or social justice.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity among nature, knowledge, and leadership. It serves as a reminder that collaboration and respect for the environment are essential for the prosperity of society. By applying these principles in our daily lives, we can foster a more harmonious and sustainable world.
