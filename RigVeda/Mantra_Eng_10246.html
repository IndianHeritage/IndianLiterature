इयं विसृष्टिर यत आबभूव यदि वा दधे यदि वा न |यो अस्याध्यक्षः परम व्योमन सो अंग वेद यदि वा न वेद ||
iyaṁ visṛṣṭir yata ābabhūva yadi vā dadhe yadi vā na | yo asyādhyakṣaḥ parame vyoman so aṅga veda yadi vā na veda ||
  • Mantra: "iyaṁ visṛṣṭir yata ābabhūva yadi vā dadhe yadi vā na | yo asyādhyakṣaḥ parame vyoman so aṅga veda yadi vā na veda ||"
  • Translation: "This creation, from where it has arisen, whether it is established or not; the one who oversees it in the highest realm, knows it, or does not know it."

  • Explanation:
    • iyaṁ visṛṣṭir: This refers to the creation or manifestation of the universe. It signifies the physical and metaphysical aspects of existence.
    • yata ābabhūva: This phrase means "from where it has arisen," indicating the source or origin of creation. It prompts us to ponder the beginnings of life and the universe.
    • yadi vā dadhe yadi vā na: This translates to "whether it is established or not," suggesting that creation may be transient or permanent, and raises questions about the nature of reality.
    • yo asyādhyakṣaḥ: This refers to the overseer or the divine entity that governs creation. It implies a higher power or consciousness that watches over the universe.
    • parame vyoman: This means "in the highest realm," indicating a transcendent space beyond ordinary perception, often associated with the divine or spiritual dimensions.
    • so aṅga veda yadi vā na veda: This suggests that the overseer knows the creation or does not know it, raising philosophical questions about knowledge, awareness, and the nature of existence.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In scientific exploration, the quest to understand the origins of the universe mirrors the inquiry posed in this mantra. Just as scientists seek to uncover the Big Bang and the laws of physics, this mantra invites us to reflect on the source of all existence.
    • In personal life, individuals often grapple with the meaning of their existence and the forces that govern their lives. This mantra encourages introspection about one's purpose and the unseen forces that shape our destinies.
    • In spiritual practices, many traditions emphasize the importance of connecting with a higher power or consciousness. This mantra resonates with those seeking to understand their relationship with the divine and the universe.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates profound philosophical inquiries about creation, existence, and the nature of knowledge.
    • Its significance lies in prompting individuals to explore their understanding of the universe and their place within it.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages a deeper contemplation of existence, the search for meaning, and the acknowledgment of a higher consciousness that may govern our lives.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the mysteries of life and the importance of seeking knowledge and understanding in our journey through existence.
