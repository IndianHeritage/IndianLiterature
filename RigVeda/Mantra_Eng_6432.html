यस्याग्निर वपुर गृहे स्तोमं चनो दधीत विश्ववार्यः | हव्यā वā वेविषद विषः ||
yasyāgnir vapur gṛhe stomaṁ cano dadhīta viśvavāryaḥ | havyā vā veviṣad viṣaḥ ||
  • yasyāgnir: This word refers to "whose fire" or "whose energy." In a broader sense, it symbolizes the divine energy or the life force that sustains all beings.
  • vapur: This translates to "form" or "body." It signifies the physical manifestation of energy or spirit in the material world.
  • gṛhe: Meaning "in the home," this word emphasizes the importance of the domestic space as a center of spiritual and material life.
  • stomaṁ: This refers to "praise" or "hymn." It indicates the act of worship or reverence, often through chanting or singing.
  • cano: This means "may he sing" or "may he chant." It suggests an invocation or a call to the divine to bless the home and its inhabitants.
  • dadhīta: This translates to "may he establish" or "may he create." It implies the act of bringing something into existence, particularly in a spiritual context.
  • viśvavāryaḥ: This means "all-protecting" or "universal protector." It signifies the divine presence that safeguards and nurtures all beings.
  • havyā: This word refers to "offerings" or "sacrifices." It highlights the importance of giving and sharing in the spiritual practice.
  • vā: This means "or," indicating a choice or alternative.
  • veviṣad: This translates to "may he enter" or "may he come forth." It suggests the idea of divine presence entering the home or the heart of the devotee.
  • viṣaḥ: This means "poison" or "harm." It symbolizes negativity or obstacles that can affect one's life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of divine energy and protection within the home. It highlights the importance of worship and offerings as a means to invite positive energy and safeguard against negativity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during rituals, family gatherings, or personal meditation practices to create a harmonious and protective environment. By invoking the divine presence, individuals can foster a sense of peace, security, and spiritual growth in their lives and homes.
