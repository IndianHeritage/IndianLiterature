ऋतं दिवे तद अवोचं पृथिव्या अभिश्रावाया प्रथमं सुमेधा: | पाताम अवद्याद दुरिताद अभीक पिताऽ माता च रक्षताम अवोभिः ||
ṛtaṁ dive tad avocam pṛthivyā abhiśrāvāya prathamaṁ sumedhāḥ | pātām avadyād duritād abhīke pitā mātā ca rakṣatām avobhiḥ ||
  • ṛtaṁ: This term refers to the cosmic order or truth that governs the universe. It signifies the principle of harmony and balance in nature.
  • dive: Meaning "in the heavens," this word connects the mantra to the celestial realm, suggesting a divine influence or guidance.
  • tad avocam: Translated as "I speak of that," indicating a declaration or invocation of the cosmic order.
  • pṛthivyā: This means "on the earth," grounding the mantra in the physical world and emphasizing the connection between the heavens and the earth.
  • abhiśrāvāya: This term can be understood as "for the well-being" or "for the prosperity," indicating a desire for abundance and positive outcomes.
  • prathamaṁ: Meaning "first," this word highlights the importance of prioritizing the cosmic order and truth in our lives.
  • sumedhāḥ: This translates to "wise" or "intelligent," suggesting that wisdom is essential for understanding and aligning with the cosmic order.
  • pātām: Meaning "may they protect," this word expresses a plea for safeguarding and support from higher powers.
  • avadyād: This term means "from sin" or "from wrongdoing," indicating a desire to be shielded from negative influences.
  • duritād: Translated as "from difficulties" or "from misfortunes," it emphasizes the need for protection from life's challenges.
  • abhīke: This word can be interpreted as "may they be present," invoking the presence of divine guardianship.
  • pitā: Meaning "father," it symbolizes paternal protection and guidance.
  • mātā: This translates to "mother," representing maternal care and nurturing.
  • ca: Meaning "and," it connects the roles of both parents in providing protection.
  • rakṣatām: This term means "may they protect," reinforcing the plea for safeguarding from both parents.
  • avobhiḥ: This can be understood as "by them," indicating that the protection sought is through the divine and parental figures.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of aligning oneself with the cosmic order (ṛta) and seeking protection from both divine and parental figures. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as during challenging times when one seeks guidance and support. For instance, individuals facing difficulties in their personal or professional lives can recite this mantra to invoke wisdom and protection, fostering a sense of security and balance. The significance lies in recognizing the interconnectedness of the universe and the role of wisdom in navigating life's challenges, ultimately leading to a harmonious existence.
