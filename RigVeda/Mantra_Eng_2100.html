गुहा हितं गुह्यं गूढम् अप्स्व अपीवृतम् मायिनं क्षियन्तम् |उतो अपो द्यां तस्तभ्वांसम् अहन्न अहिं शूर वीर्येण ||
guhā hitaṁ guhyaṁ gūḻham apsv apīvṛtam māyinaṁ kṣiyantam | uto apo dyāṁ tastabhvāṁsam ahann ahiṁ śūra vīryeṇa ||
  • guhā hitaṁ: This phrase translates to "hidden in the cave." In a metaphorical sense, it can refer to the deeper truths or knowledge that are often concealed within our subconscious or the universe.
  • guhyaṁ: Meaning "secret" or "mysterious," this word emphasizes the idea that there are aspects of existence that are not immediately visible or understandable.
  • gūḻham: This term can be interpreted as "covered" or "shrouded," suggesting that there are layers of reality that require exploration to uncover.
  • apsv: Referring to "water," this element symbolizes life, purity, and the flow of knowledge. Water is essential for survival and can represent the nurturing aspects of wisdom.
  • apīvṛtam: This means "not covered" or "unobstructed," indicating the importance of clarity and openness in understanding the hidden truths of life.
  • māyinaṁ: Translated as "the one who creates illusions," this word points to the deceptive nature of reality and how perceptions can be misleading.
  • kṣiyantam: Meaning "diminishing" or "fading," this term suggests that ignorance or falsehoods can erode our understanding over time.
  • uto apo: This phrase can be interpreted as "and the waters," linking back to the idea of knowledge and wisdom flowing like water.
  • dyāṁ: Meaning "heaven" or "sky," this word represents the higher realms of consciousness and spiritual understanding.
  • tastabhvāṁsam: This term can be understood as "having taken form," indicating the manifestation of knowledge or truth in the physical world.
  • ahann: Translated as "I have struck" or "I have conquered," this word signifies the triumph of knowledge over ignorance.
  • ahiṁ: Meaning "non-violence" or "peace," this term emphasizes the importance of harmony in the pursuit of truth.
  • śūra: This word means "brave" or "valiant," highlighting the courage required to seek out and confront hidden truths.
  • vīryeṇa: Translated as "with strength" or "power," this term underscores the necessity of inner strength in the quest for understanding.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the journey of seeking hidden knowledge and understanding the deeper truths of existence. It emphasizes the importance of courage, clarity, and non-violence in this pursuit. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, spiritual exploration, and the quest for wisdom in relationships and decision-making. By recognizing the layers of reality and striving to uncover them, individuals can lead more fulfilling and enlightened lives.
