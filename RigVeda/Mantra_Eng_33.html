अथा ते अन्तमानां विद्यामा सुमतिनाम् |मा नो अति ख्य आ गहि ||
athā te antamānāṁ vidyāma sumatīnām | mā no ati khya ā gahi ||
  • athā: This word signifies "now" or "therefore," indicating a transition or a moment of realization.
  • te: This translates to "your," referring to the divine or higher power being addressed.
  • antamānāṁ: This term means "the ultimate" or "the end," suggesting a quest for the highest knowledge or truth.
  • vidyāma: This means "we know" or "we seek knowledge," emphasizing the importance of wisdom and understanding.
  • sumatīnām: This translates to "of the wise" or "of those with good thoughts," highlighting the value of positive and constructive thinking.
  • mā: This word means "not," indicating a negation or a plea for avoidance of negativity.
  • no: This translates to "our," indicating a collective identity or shared experience.
  • ati: This means "excess" or "too much," suggesting a caution against extremes.
  • khya: This term can be interpreted as "fame" or "reputation," relating to how one is perceived in society.
  • ā gahi: This phrase means "may you take" or "may you grasp," indicating a request for divine intervention or guidance.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for wisdom and guidance, asking the divine to help us attain the ultimate knowledge while avoiding the pitfalls of excessive pride or negative thoughts. It emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge with a clear and positive mind, and the need for divine support in this journey.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Education: Students can recite this mantra before exams or important learning sessions, seeking clarity and wisdom in their studies.
  • Decision Making: Individuals facing critical life choices can use this mantra to invoke a sense of calm and clarity, ensuring their decisions are guided by wisdom rather than impulsiveness.
  • Personal Growth: In moments of self-reflection, this mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate positive thoughts and avoid negativity, fostering a healthier mindset.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for wisdom and positive thinking while seeking knowledge. It serves as a reminder that the pursuit of knowledge should be accompanied by humility and a clear mind. In various aspects of life, from education to personal development, this mantra encourages individuals to seek guidance and maintain a balanced perspective, ultimately leading to a more enlightened and fulfilling existence.
