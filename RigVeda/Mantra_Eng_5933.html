मन्त्र: स्तरीरु त्वद्भवति सूत उ त्वद् यथावशं तंवं चक्र एषः |पितुः पयः प्रति गृभ्णाति माता तेन पिता वर्धते तेन पुत्रः ||
starīr u tvad bhavati sūta u tvad yathāvaśaṁ tanvaṁ cakra eṣaḥ | pituḥ payaḥ prati gṛbhṇāti mātā tena pitā vardhate tena putraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • starīr: a term often associated with the concept of strength or support.
    • u: a conjunction meaning "and".
    • tvad: means "your" or "of you".
    • bhavati: translates to "becomes" or "is".
    • sūta: refers to "the one who brings forth" or "the progenitor".
    • yathāvaśaṁ: means "as per one's own will" or "according to one's desire".
    • tanvaṁ: refers to "body" or "form".
    • cakra: means "wheel" or "circle\ often symbolizing cycles of life.
    • eṣaḥ: means "this".
    • pituḥ: means "of the father".
    • payaḥ: translates to "milk\ symbolizing nourishment.
    • prati: means "towards" or "in relation to".
    • gṛbhṇāti: means "receives" or "takes".
    • mātā: means "mother".
    • tena: means "by that" or "through that".
    • pitā: means "father".
    • vardhate: means "grows" or "increases".
    • putraḥ: means "son" or "child".
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the interconnectedness of family and the roles of parents in nurturing their children.
    • The phrase "starīr u tvad bhavati" suggests that strength and support come from the divine or higher power.
    • "Sūta u tvad yathāvaśaṁ tanvaṁ" indicates that the progenitor (father) shapes the body and destiny of the child according to their will.
    • The mention of "pituḥ payaḥ" symbolizes the nourishment provided by the father, akin to how a mother nourishes her child with milk.
    • The mantra emphasizes that through the nurturing of both parents, the child grows and flourishes.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, the support and guidance from parents can be seen in how children develop their values and skills.
    • For instance, a father teaching his son the importance of hard work and integrity can shape the son's character and future.
    • Similarly, a mother providing emotional support and care can significantly impact a child's mental well-being and confidence.
    • In a broader context, communities that emphasize family values often see stronger social bonds and collective growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of parental roles in a child's life, emphasizing that both parents contribute to the child's growth and development.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of nurturing relationships within families and the impact they have on future generations.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to appreciate their parents' efforts and to strive to provide similar support and guidance to their own children.
    • Ultimately, it underscores the cyclical nature of life, where the values and strengths passed down from parents to children shape the fabric of society.
