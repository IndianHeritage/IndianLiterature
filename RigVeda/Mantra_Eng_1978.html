पाहि नो अग्नि पायुभिर अजस्रैर उता प्रिय सदना आ शुशुक्वान् |मा ते भयम् जरितारं यविष्ट नूनं विदन मापरं सहस्वः ||
pāhi no agne pāyubhir ajasrair uta priye sadana ā śuśukvān | mā te bhayaṁ jaritāraṁ yaviṣṭha nūnaṁ vidan māparaṁ sahasvaḥ ||
  • pāhi no agne: This phrase translates to "O Agni, protect us." Agni, the fire god, symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, this can be likened to the protective forces in our lives, such as family, friends, or even our own inner strength that helps us navigate challenges.
  • pāyubhir ajasrair: This means "with your constant support." It emphasizes the need for continuous guidance and support in our lives. For example, consider a mentor or a teacher who provides unwavering support to their students, helping them grow and succeed.
  • uta priye sadana: This translates to "and in the beloved abode." It refers to a place of comfort and safety, akin to a home or a community where one feels secure. In modern life, this could represent the importance of having a supportive environment, whether it be at home, work, or among friends.
  • ā śuśukvān: This means "may we be joyful." It highlights the aspiration for happiness and contentment in life. For instance, engaging in activities that bring joy, such as hobbies or spending time with loved ones, is essential for mental well-being.
  • mā te bhayaṁ: This translates to "may there be no fear for you." It signifies the desire to be free from fear and anxiety. In real life, this can relate to overcoming fears, such as public speaking or facing new challenges, and finding courage in difficult situations.
  • jaritāraṁ yaviṣṭha: This means "the one who is strong and powerful." It refers to the strength that comes from within or from divine sources. For example, athletes often draw on their inner strength to push through physical and mental barriers during competitions.
  • nūnaṁ vidan māparaṁ: This translates to "surely knowing no other." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom in making informed decisions. In today's world, continuous learning and adapting to new information is crucial for personal and professional growth.
  • sahasvaḥ: This means "be strong." It is a call to embrace strength and resilience in the face of adversity. For instance, individuals facing health challenges often exhibit remarkable strength and determination to overcome their circumstances.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound plea for protection, support, joy, and strength. It highlights the significance of community, knowledge, and resilience in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can serve as a reminder to seek support from loved ones, embrace joy in our daily activities, and cultivate inner strength to face challenges. By embodying these principles, we can navigate life's complexities with grace and confidence.
