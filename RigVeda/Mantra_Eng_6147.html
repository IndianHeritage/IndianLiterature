यद अस्य मन्युर अध्वनीद विगृत्रं पर्वशो रुजन् |अपः समुद्रम ऐरयत ||
yad asya manyur adhvanīd vi vṛtram parvaśo rujan | apaḥ samudram airayat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: that which
    • asya: of this
    • manyur: anger or wrath
    • adhvanīd: has been directed or has moved
    • vi: apart or away
    • vṛtram: the obstacle or enemy
    • parvaśo: in the context of the seasons or cycles
    • rujan: pain or suffering
    • apaḥ: the waters
    • samudram: the ocean
    • airayat: has brought forth or has released
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of overcoming obstacles and the release of anger or wrath.
    • The phrase "yad asya manyur adhvanīd" suggests that the anger or wrath that has been directed can be transformed or redirected.
    • "Vi vṛtram parvaśo rujan" indicates the removal of obstacles and suffering, akin to how seasons change and bring new life.
    • The mention of "apaḥ samudram airayat" symbolizes the vastness of the ocean, representing the potential for renewal and the flow of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal conflicts, redirecting anger into constructive dialogue can lead to resolution and healing.
    • In nature, the changing seasons remind us that challenges (like winter) are temporary and can lead to growth (like spring).
    • In community efforts, collective action can overcome societal obstacles, much like how rivers flow into the ocean, creating a larger impact.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of transforming negative emotions into positive actions.
    • It teaches us that obstacles can be overcome through understanding and collective effort.
    • In real life, it encourages us to embrace change and find strength in unity, reminding us that just as the ocean is vast and powerful, so too is our potential to create positive change.
