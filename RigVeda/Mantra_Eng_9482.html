मित्राय शिक्ष वरुणाय दाशुषे या सम्राजा मनसा न प्रयुच्छतः |ययोर्धाम धर्मणा रोचते बृहद् ययोः उभे रोधसी नाधसी वृत्तौ ||
mitrāya śikṣa varuṇāya dāśuṣe yā samrājā manasā na prayucchataḥ | yayor dhāma dharmaṇā rocate bṛhad yayor ubhe rodasī nādhasī vṛtau ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "To Mitra, to Varuna, to the one who is a servant, may the ruler not withhold his mind. Whose abode is pleasing to the righteous, and whose two realms are not separated by the law."
  • Explanation of Key Terms:
    • Mitra: Represents friendship, harmony, and the sun. In real life, this can be seen in the importance of building relationships and fostering goodwill among people.
    • Varuna: Symbolizes cosmic order and moral authority. This can be related to the concept of justice and the rule of law in society, where fairness and righteousness govern actions.
    • Dāśuṣe: Refers to a servant or one who serves. This highlights the value of service in our lives, whether in personal relationships or professional settings.
    • Samrājā: Means ruler or king. This can be likened to leadership roles in various contexts, emphasizing the responsibility that comes with power.
    • Dhāma: Refers to a dwelling or abode, which can symbolize a place of peace and righteousness, akin to a harmonious home or community.
    • Dharma: Represents duty, righteousness, and moral law. In everyday life, this can be seen in ethical decision-making and the importance of adhering to one's principles.
    • Rodasī: Refers to the two realms or worlds, which can be interpreted as the physical and spiritual dimensions of existence.
  • Real-Life Applications:
    • This mantra encourages individuals to cultivate friendships and harmonious relationships, which can lead to a more supportive community.
    • It emphasizes the importance of justice and moral authority, reminding leaders and individuals alike to act with integrity and fairness.
    • The concept of service is vital in various professions, such as healthcare, education, and community service, where helping others is paramount.
    • In leadership roles, the mantra serves as a reminder of the responsibilities that come with power, urging leaders to act in the best interest of their followers.
    • It highlights the significance of creating a peaceful and righteous environment, whether at home or in society, fostering a sense of belonging and security.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of harmony, justice, and service. Its significance lies in its call for individuals to embody these values in their daily lives. By fostering friendships, upholding justice, and serving others, we contribute to a more balanced and righteous society. This mantra serves as a guiding principle for personal conduct and leadership, reminding us of our interconnectedness and the impact of our actions on the world around us.
