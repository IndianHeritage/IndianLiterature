इन्द्रं इवेद उभये वि हवयन्त उदीराणां यज्ञं उपप्रयन्तः |दधिक्रामु सुदानं मार्त्याय ददथुर मित्रावरुणा नो अश्वम् ||
indram ived ubhaye vi hvayanta udīrāṇā yajñam upaprayantaḥ | dadhikrām u sūdanam martyāya dadathur mitrāvaruṇā no aśvam ||
  • Indram: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • ived: Means "to know" or "to understand," indicating the importance of knowledge and wisdom.
  • ubhaye: Translates to "both," suggesting a duality or balance in aspects of life.
  • vi hvayanta: Means "calling forth" or "invoking," emphasizing the act of seeking divine assistance or guidance.
  • udīrāṇā: Refers to "those who are elevated" or "those who rise," indicating aspiration and the pursuit of higher goals.
  • yajñam: Represents "sacrifice" or "offering," a central theme in Vedic rituals that signifies devotion and selflessness.
  • upaprayantaḥ: Means "those who are engaged in," highlighting active participation in rituals or actions.
  • dadhikrām: Translates to "he who moves" or "he who advances," symbolizing progress and forward movement.
  • u sūdanam: Refers to "the one who brings happiness" or "the benefactor," indicating the importance of benevolence and support.
  • martyāya: Means "to mortals" or "to human beings," emphasizing the connection between the divine and human realms.
  • dadathur: Translates to "they give" or "they bestow," indicating generosity and the act of sharing blessings.
  • mitrāvaruṇā: Refers to the deities Mitra and Varuna, symbolizing friendship, harmony, and cosmic order.
  • no: Means "to us," indicating a personal appeal or request for blessings.
  • aśvam: Translates to "the horse," which in Vedic culture symbolizes strength, vitality, and the ability to overcome obstacles.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of invoking divine support and wisdom in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of knowledge, sacrifice, and the pursuit of higher goals while fostering a sense of community and harmony. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during personal challenges, community gatherings, or rituals where individuals seek guidance and strength. By understanding and embodying the principles of this mantra, one can cultivate a life of purpose, generosity, and connection with the divine and the community.
