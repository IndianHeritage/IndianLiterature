तवेदाṁ विश्वम् अभितः पशव्यं यत पश्यसि चक्षसा सूर्यस्य |गवाम् असि गोपतिरेका इन्द्र भक्षीमहि ते प्रयतस्य वस्वः ||
tavedaṁ viśvam abhitaḥ paśavya1ṁ yat paśyasi cakṣasā sūryasya | gavām asi gopatir eka indra bhakṣīmahi te prayatasya vasvaḥ ||
  • tavedaṁ viśvam abhitaḥ paśavyaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "This entire universe is under your observation." It emphasizes the omnipresence and omniscience of a higher power, suggesting that everything in the universe is interconnected and observed by a divine entity.
  • yat paśyasi cakṣasā sūryasya: This translates to "What you see with the eyes of the sun." Here, the sun symbolizes enlightenment, knowledge, and clarity. It suggests that true understanding comes from a higher perspective, much like how the sun illuminates the world.
  • gavām asi gopatir eka: This means "You are the sole protector of the cows." In Vedic culture, cows are considered sacred and symbolize wealth, nourishment, and the sustenance of life. This line highlights the importance of protection and care for all living beings.
  • indra bhakṣīmahi te prayatasya vasvaḥ: This can be interpreted as "We seek the blessings of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, for your abundant grace." Indra represents strength, power, and the life-giving force of rain, which is essential for agriculture and sustenance.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of divine observation, protection, and the pursuit of knowledge. It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of seeking blessings for prosperity and sustenance. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:


  • Environmental Awareness: Recognizing the importance of protecting nature and all living beings, much like the protection of cows in Vedic culture.
  • Seeking Knowledge: Encouraging individuals to seek higher knowledge and understanding, akin to the illumination provided by the sun.
  • Community Support: Emphasizing the need for collective efforts in seeking blessings for prosperity, especially in agricultural communities reliant on rain and natural resources.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of our responsibilities towards nature, the pursuit of knowledge, and the importance of community in achieving abundance and harmony in life.
