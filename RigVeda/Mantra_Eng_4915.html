ग्रावाणः सोम नो हि कं सखित्वनाया वावशुः |जही न्य अत्रिणं पाणिं वृको हि षः ||
grāvāṇaḥ soma no hi kaṁ sakhitvanāya vāvaśuḥ | jahī ny a1triṇam paṇiṁ vṛko hi ṣaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "grāvāṇaḥ soma no hi kaṁ sakhitvanāya vāvaśuḥ | jahī ny a1triṇam paṇiṁ vṛko hi ṣaḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine Soma, seeking friendship and support, while also expressing a desire to overcome obstacles and adversaries.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • grāvāṇaḥ: Refers to the one who is invoked or called upon, often associated with divine entities.
    • soma: A sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
    • no: Means "our," indicating a collective appeal.
    • hi: A particle used for emphasis, often translated as "indeed" or "certainly."
    • kaṁ: Refers to "who" or "what," questioning the identity of the adversary.
    • sakhitvanāya: Relates to friendship or companionship, suggesting a desire for unity and support.
    • vāvaśuḥ: Implies a call for assistance or help.
    • jahī: Means "to abandon" or "to let go," indicating a desire to overcome challenges.
    • ny a1triṇam: Refers to "the adversary" or "the enemy," highlighting the presence of obstacles.
    • paṇiṁ: Means "the thief" or "the one who takes away," symbolizing loss or hindrance.
    • vṛko: Translates to "wolf," often representing cunning or predatory forces.
    • hi: Again, used for emphasis.
    • ṣaḥ: Refers to "six," which can symbolize completeness or a cycle.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, one might invoke this mantra when facing competition or challenges at work, seeking support from colleagues and allies to overcome obstacles.
    • In personal relationships, it can be a reminder to seek unity and friendship, especially when dealing with misunderstandings or conflicts.
    • During times of crisis, individuals may find solace in the idea of divine support, calling upon their faith to help them navigate through difficulties.
  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine assistance and fostering friendships in the face of adversity. It serves as a reminder that while challenges may arise, the support of allies and the invocation of higher powers can lead to overcoming obstacles. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, from personal struggles to professional challenges, highlighting the significance of community and divine guidance in achieving success and harmony.
