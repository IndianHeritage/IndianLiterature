तं त्वा गीरभिर उरुक्षयाऽ हव्यवाहं सम ईधिरे | यजिष्टम् मानवसे जन ||
taṁ tvā gīrbhir urukṣayā havyavāhaṁ sam īdhire | yajiṣṭham mānuṣe jane ||
  • taṁ tvā - This phrase translates to "to you" or "that you." It signifies a direct address to a divine entity or a higher power, indicating a relationship of devotion and reverence.
  • gīrbhir - This word means "with hymns" or "with praises." It emphasizes the importance of vocalizing one's devotion and gratitude through sacred chants or mantras.
  • urukṣayā - This term can be interpreted as "with great strength" or "with vast resources." It suggests the idea of abundance and the power that comes from the divine.
  • havyavāhaṁ - This word refers to "the one who carries offerings." It indicates the act of presenting offerings to the divine, which is a common practice in many spiritual traditions, symbolizing respect and gratitude.
  • sam īdhire - This phrase means "may they come together" or "may they unite." It reflects the desire for harmony and collective worship, emphasizing the importance of community in spiritual practices.
  • yajiṣṭham - This translates to "the most worthy of worship." It highlights the significance of recognizing and honoring the divine presence in our lives.
  • mānuṣe jane - This phrase means "among human beings." It indicates the context of human existence and the need for divine guidance and support in our daily lives.

In summary, this mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of devotion, community, and the recognition of divine strength and abundance. It calls upon the divine to unite the community through hymns and offerings, emphasizing the importance of collective worship and gratitude. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during communal prayers, rituals, or gatherings where individuals come together to express their reverence and seek blessings. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine, encouraging individuals to foster a sense of unity and shared purpose in their spiritual journeys.
