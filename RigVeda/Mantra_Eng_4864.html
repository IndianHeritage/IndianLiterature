तवेषं शर्धो न मारुतं तुविष्वण्य अनर्वाणं पूषणं सं यथा शतां | सं सहस्रां कारीषच चर्षणिभ्य आम् आविर्गूढा वसू करत सुवेदा नो वसू करत ||
tveṣaṁ śardho na mārutaṁ tuviṣvaṇy anarvāṇam pūṣaṇaṁ saṁ yathā śatā | saṁ sahasrā kāriṣac carṣaṇibhya ām̐ āvir gūḻhā vasū karat suvedā no vasū karat ||
  • tveṣaṁ: This word refers to the divine form or appearance. It signifies the manifestation of the divine in various forms.
  • śardho: This term can be interpreted as strength or vigor, often associated with the energy of life and nature.
  • na mārutaṁ: "Māruta" refers to the winds or the forces of nature. The phrase suggests a relationship or contrast with these natural elements.
  • tuviṣvaṇy: This word can be understood as the universal or all-encompassing aspect of the divine, indicating a connection to the cosmos.
  • anarvāṇam: This term means 'unfailing' or 'everlasting', suggesting the eternal nature of the divine presence.
  • pūṣaṇaṁ: Referring to the deity Pūṣan, who is associated with nourishment and prosperity, this word emphasizes the importance of sustenance in life.
  • saṁ yathā: This phrase means 'as' or 'in the way that', indicating a comparison or a method of understanding.
  • śatā: This word means 'hundreds', often used to denote abundance or a multitude of blessings.
  • saṁ sahasrā: This translates to 'thousands', further emphasizing the idea of vastness and infinite blessings.
  • kāriṣac: This term refers to the act of creation or doing, highlighting the active role of the divine in the world.
  • carṣaṇibhya: This word can be interpreted as 'from the earth' or 'from the ground', connecting the divine to the physical world.
  • ām̐: A sacred sound or syllable, often used in mantras to invoke spiritual energy.
  • āvir: This means 'to manifest' or 'to appear', indicating the desire for the divine presence to be revealed.
  • gūḻhā: This term means 'hidden' or 'concealed', suggesting that the divine may be present but not always visible.
  • vasū: Referring to the 'wealth' or 'prosperity', this word emphasizes the importance of abundance in life.
  • karat: This means 'to do' or 'to make', indicating action and the manifestation of intentions.
  • suvedā: This term can be interpreted as 'well-knowing' or 'wise', suggesting the importance of knowledge and understanding in achieving prosperity.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the relationship between the divine and the natural world, highlighting the importance of nourishment, abundance, and the manifestation of divine energy in our lives. It teaches us that the divine is both hidden and revealed, and that through knowledge and action, we can access the blessings of prosperity and strength. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as seeking guidance in times of uncertainty, invoking strength during challenges, or fostering a sense of gratitude for the abundance in our lives. It serves as a reminder to recognize the divine presence in our everyday experiences and to act with wisdom and intention to manifest our desires.
