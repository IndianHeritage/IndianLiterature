मन्त्र: कद ध नूनं कधप्रियः पिता पुत्रं न हस्तयोः |दधिध्वे वृक्तबरिषः ||
kad dha nūnaṁ kadhapriyaḥ pitā putraṁ na hastayoḥ | dadhidhve vṛktabarhiṣaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kad: when
    • dha: indeed
    • nūnaṁ: certainly
    • kadhapriyaḥ: dear to whom
    • pitā: father
    • putraṁ: son
    • na: not
    • hastayoḥ: of the hands
    • dadhidhve: may you give
    • vṛktabarhiṣaḥ: with the sacred grass
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects a deep inquiry into the relationship between a father and son.
    • It questions the nature of love and attachment, asking when a father does not cherish his son.
    • The phrase "may you give with the sacred grass" suggests a blessing or offering, indicating the importance of rituals and the sacred in familial relationships.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, the bond between a father and son is celebrated through various rites of passage, such as bar mitzvahs or coming-of-age ceremonies.
    • In modern society, this mantra can be reflected in the support a father provides to his son in education, career choices, and personal development.
    • It can also be seen in the emotional support and guidance that fathers offer, which is crucial for the son’s growth and self-esteem.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of paternal love and the responsibilities that come with it.
    • It serves as a reminder of the sacred bond that exists within families and the importance of nurturing these relationships.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to reflect on their familial ties and the ways they can strengthen them through love, support, and shared rituals.
