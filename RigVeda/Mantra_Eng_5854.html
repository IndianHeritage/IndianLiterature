ईशानासो ये दधते स्वर णो गोभिर अश्वेभिर वसुभिर हिरण्यैः | इन्द्रवायू सूरयो विश्वम आयुर अर्वद्भिर वीरैः पृतनासु सह्युः ||
īśānāso ye dadhate svar ṇo gobhir aśvebhir vasubhir hiraṇyaiḥ | indravāyū sūrayo viśvam āyur arvadbhir vīraiḥ pṛtanāsu sahyuḥ ||
  • īśānāso: This term refers to those who are in control or possess authority. In a real-life context, this can be likened to leaders or individuals in positions of power who guide and influence others.
  • ye dadhate: This means "who establish" or "who create." It signifies the act of building or forming something significant, much like entrepreneurs who create businesses or innovators who develop new technologies.
  • svar ṇo: This translates to "of the heavens" or "of the divine." It can be associated with aspirations and goals that are lofty and noble, similar to individuals striving for excellence in their fields.
  • gobhir: This word means "with the cows," which in Vedic culture symbolizes wealth and prosperity. In modern terms, it can represent the importance of nurturing resources, whether they be financial, human, or natural.
  • aśvebhir: This refers to "with horses," symbolizing strength and speed. In contemporary life, this can be related to the importance of having strong support systems or teams that help achieve goals efficiently.
  • vasubhir: This means "with the wealth" or "with the good things." It emphasizes the value of abundance and the blessings one receives, akin to appreciating the good fortune in life.
  • hiraṇyaiḥ: This translates to "with gold" or "with riches," representing material wealth and prosperity. In real life, it can be seen in the pursuit of financial stability and success.
  • indravāyū: This term combines the names of Indra, the king of gods, and Vayu, the god of wind. It signifies the power of divine forces working together, much like collaboration in teams or communities to achieve common goals.
  • sūrayo: This means "the brave" or "the strong." It highlights the importance of courage and resilience in facing challenges, similar to individuals who overcome obstacles in their personal or professional lives.
  • viśvam āyur: This translates to "universal life" or "life for all." It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of community and support, akin to social networks that foster well-being.
  • arvadbhir: This means "with the best" or "with the excellent." It signifies striving for quality and excellence in endeavors, much like professionals who aim for high standards in their work.
  • vīraiḥ: This translates to "with heroes" or "with valorous ones." It represents the idea of standing together with those who are courageous and noble, similar to teamwork in sports or collaborative projects.
  • pṛtanāsu: This means "in the struggles" or "in the battles." It acknowledges the challenges faced in life, akin to the perseverance required in personal or professional journeys.
  • sahyuḥ: This translates to "together" or "in unity." It emphasizes the importance of collaboration and support among individuals, much like communities coming together for a common cause.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, collaboration, and the pursuit of prosperity and excellence. It highlights the significance of working together, supporting one another, and striving for higher goals in life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork in organizations, community building, and personal development. The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of unity and the importance of nurturing both material and spiritual wealth in our lives.
