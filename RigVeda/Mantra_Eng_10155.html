मन्त्र: न हि मे अक्षिपच चनाच्छान्त्सुः पञ्च कृष्टयः | कुवित सोमस्यापाम इति ||
nahi me akṣipac canācchāntsuḥ pañca kṛṣṭayaḥ | kuvit somasyāpām iti ||
  • Translation: The mantra translates to "I do not cast my eyes upon the five kinds of crops; I do not desire the water of the moon." This reflects a sense of detachment from material desires and the natural world.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • nahi: not
    • me: my
    • akṣipac: cast (as in to cast one's gaze)
    • canācchāntsuḥ: upon the five kinds of crops
    • pañca: five
    • kṛṣṭayaḥ: kinds of crops (referring to agricultural produce)
    • kuvit: I do not desire
    • somasyāpām: the water of the moon (symbolizing nectar or divine essence)
    • iti: thus
  • Contextual Meaning: This mantra emphasizes the importance of renunciation and the idea that true fulfillment does not come from material possessions or desires. The "five kinds of crops" can symbolize various worldly pursuits, while "the water of the moon" represents spiritual or divine nourishment that is often sought after.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be applied to the practice of minimalism, where individuals choose to live with less and focus on what truly matters, such as relationships and personal growth.
    • In a corporate setting, it can be seen in leaders who prioritize ethical practices over profit maximization, understanding that true success is not just about financial gain but also about contributing positively to society.
    • In personal development, it encourages individuals to let go of superficial desires and focus on inner peace and self-awareness, much like mindfulness practices that promote living in the moment.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for introspection and the pursuit of higher values over materialistic ones. It serves as a reminder that true contentment comes from within and that one should strive for spiritual growth rather than being entangled in the transient nature of worldly possessions. This principle is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal relationships, career choices, and overall life satisfaction, encouraging individuals to seek deeper meaning and purpose.
