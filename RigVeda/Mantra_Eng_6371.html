यद इन्द्र मनमशस त्वा नानां हवन्त ऊतये |अस्माकेबhir नृभिर अत्रा स्वर जय ||
yad indra manmaśas tvā nānā havanta ūtaye | asmākebhir nṛbhir atrā svar jaya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • manmaśas: of the mind or thoughts
    • tvā: you
    • nānā: various
    • havanta: offerings or invocations
    • ūtaye: for the purpose of
    • asmākebhir: by us
    • nṛbhir: by men
    • atrā: here
    • svar: the celestial or heavenly
    • jaya: victory or success
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to Indra, the god of rain and thunder, to accept the various offerings made by humans.
    • It emphasizes the importance of collective effort and intention in seeking divine blessings.
    • The phrase "by us, men, here" signifies unity and the shared purpose of the community in invoking divine support.
    • The ultimate goal is to achieve success and victory in endeavors, symbolized by "svar jaya," which can be interpreted as attaining a higher state of existence or fulfillment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, such as during a festival or a harvest celebration, people come together to offer prayers and rituals to seek blessings for prosperity and success.
    • In a corporate environment, teams often gather to brainstorm and align their goals, invoking a sense of unity and shared purpose to achieve success in projects.
    • During challenging times, such as natural disasters or crises, communities often unite in prayer or collective action, seeking divine intervention and support.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of collective intention and the power of community in seeking divine blessings.
    • It serves as a reminder that success is often a result of collaboration and shared efforts, whether in spiritual, social, or professional contexts.
    • By invoking higher powers with sincerity and unity, individuals and communities can aspire to achieve their goals and overcome challenges.
