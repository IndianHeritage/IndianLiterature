मात्र पूषन्नाघृण इरस्यो वरूत्री यद रातिषाचश च रासन |मयोभुवो नो अर्वन्तो नि पान्तु वृष्टिम परिज्मा वातो ददातु ||
mātra pūṣann āghṛṇa irasyo varūtrī yad rātiṣācaś ca rāsan | mayobhuvo no arvanto ni pāntu vṛṣṭim parijmā vāto dadātu ||
  • mātra pūṣann: This phrase can be translated as "O Nourisher, in measure." Here, "mātra" refers to measure or moderation, and "pūṣann" signifies nourishment or sustenance. In real life, this can be related to the importance of balanced nutrition and the idea that one should consume food in moderation for optimal health.
  • āghṛṇa: This term means "one who is aware" or "one who understands." It emphasizes the need for awareness and understanding in our actions and decisions. For example, being aware of the consequences of our choices in daily life, such as environmental impacts or social responsibilities.
  • irasyo varūtrī: "irasyo" refers to the essence or the core, while "varūtrī" can be interpreted as a protector or a guardian. This suggests the idea of safeguarding the essential aspects of life, such as values and relationships. In practice, this could mean protecting one’s family and community from harm.
  • yad rātiṣācaś ca rāsan: This phrase can be understood as "whatever is beneficial and sweet." It highlights the importance of seeking out what is good and pleasant in life. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of happiness and positive experiences, such as engaging in hobbies or spending time with loved ones.
  • mayobhuvo: This term translates to "may we be blessed." It reflects a desire for blessings and positive outcomes in life. For instance, people often seek blessings before starting new ventures, such as a business or a journey, hoping for success and prosperity.
  • no arvanto: This means "may they not be obstructed." It signifies the wish for unobstructed paths in life, whether in personal goals or professional endeavors. In a real-life context, this could relate to overcoming obstacles in education or career advancement.
  • ni pāntu: This phrase translates to "may they protect." It emphasizes the need for protection and support from higher powers or the universe. For example, individuals often seek protection through prayers or rituals during challenging times.
  • vṛṣṭim parijmā: "vṛṣṭim" refers to rain or abundance, while "parijmā" means to encompass or surround. This can be interpreted as a desire for abundance in life, whether it be in terms of wealth, health, or happiness. In real life, this can be seen in the efforts people make to create a prosperous environment for themselves and their families.
  • vāto dadātu: This translates to "may the winds give." It symbolizes the idea of receiving blessings and gifts from nature. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of nature in our lives, such as clean air and a healthy environment, which are essential for well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing nourishment, awareness, protection, and the pursuit of abundance. Its significance lies in the reminder to seek balance and positivity in our actions and decisions. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate a mindful lifestyle, prioritize health and relationships, and strive for personal and communal growth. By embodying these principles, one can navigate life's challenges with grace and purpose.
