मन्त्र: वज्रेण हि वृत्रहā वृत्रम अस्तर अदेवस्य शूशुवानस्य मायाः |विधृष्णो अत्र धृतता जगन्थाथाभवो मगवन् बाह्वोजाः ||
vajreṇa hi vṛtrahā vṛtram astar adevasya śūśuvānasya māyāḥ | vi dhṛṣṇo atra dhṛṣatā jaghanthāthābhavo maghavan bāhvojāḥ ||
  • vajreṇa hi vṛtrahā: This phrase translates to "with the thunderbolt, the slayer of Vritra." In this context, "vajra" refers to a thunderbolt, symbolizing power and strength, while "Vritra" represents a demon or obstacle that obstructs the flow of life and prosperity. In real life, this can be likened to overcoming challenges or adversities that hinder progress.
  • vṛtram astar adevasya: This means "the Vritra is laid low by the divine." Here, "astar" signifies the act of laying low or defeating, and "adevasya" refers to the divine or godly forces. This can be seen in situations where individuals or communities rely on higher principles or moral values to overcome difficulties, much like how a community might come together to support one another in times of crisis.
  • śūśuvānasya māyāḥ: This translates to "the illusions of the one who is powerful." "Māyā" refers to illusion or deception, suggesting that even the strongest can be misled. In everyday life, this can be related to how people may sometimes be blinded by their ambitions or desires, leading them to make poor decisions.
  • vi dhṛṣṇo atra dhṛṣatā: This phrase means "with courage, here is strength." "Dhṛṣṇo" signifies courage, while "dhṛṣatā" refers to strength or resilience. This emphasizes the importance of bravery in facing challenges. For example, a person standing up for their beliefs in the face of opposition exemplifies this courage.
  • jaghanthāthābhavo maghavan bāhvojāḥ: This translates to "the one who is strong, the bounteous one, with powerful arms." "Maghavan" refers to a deity associated with abundance and prosperity, while "bāhvojāḥ" emphasizes strength. This can be related to individuals or leaders who use their strength and resources to uplift others, such as philanthropists who contribute to social causes.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of overcoming obstacles through divine strength, courage, and the recognition of illusions. It signifies that while challenges (Vritra) may arise, they can be defeated with the right mindset and support from higher principles. In real life, this mantra serves as a reminder to face adversities with bravery, to seek divine guidance, and to use one's strength for the greater good. It is applicable in various scenarios, such as personal struggles, community challenges, and even in leadership roles where one must navigate through difficulties while uplifting others.
