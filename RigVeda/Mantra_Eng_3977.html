मरत्वतो अप्रतीतस्य जीष्णोर अजूर्यतः प्र ब्रवामā कृतानि |न ते पूर्वे मघवन नापरासो न वीर्यं नूतनः कश्चनाप ||
marutvato apratītasya jiṣṇor ajūryataḥ pra bravāmā kṛtāni | na te pūrve maghavan nāparāso na vīrya1ṁ nūtanaḥ kaś canāpa ||
  • Mantra: "marutvato apratītasya jiṣṇor ajūryataḥ pra bravāmā kṛtāni | na te pūrve maghavan nāparāso na vīryaṁ nūtanaḥ kaś canāpa ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Marutvato: Pertaining to the Maruts, the storm deities.
    • Apratītasya: Of the one who is not easily perceived or understood.
    • Jiṣṇor: Of the victorious or the one who conquers.
    • Ajūryataḥ: From the one who is not diminished or weakened.
    • Pra bravāmā: We proclaim or declare.
    • Kṛtāni: The deeds or actions.
    • Na te: Not yours.
    • Pūrve: The former or previous ones.
    • Maghavan: The one who is rich or bountiful.
    • Nāparāso: Not the latter or the subsequent ones.
    • Na vīryaṁ: Not the strength or valor.
    • Nūtanaḥ: The new or the fresh.
    • Kaś canāpa: Anyone at all.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the Maruts, who are often associated with storms and winds, symbolizing power and energy.
    • The phrase "apratītasya jiṣṇor" suggests a connection to the idea of victory that is not easily grasped, indicating that true strength may not always be visible.
    • When we say "na te pūrve maghavan," it implies that the previous forms of abundance or strength are not the same as what is being proclaimed now, suggesting a new understanding or realization of power.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing new strengths and actions that may not be immediately apparent, akin to how a storm can bring both destruction and renewal.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often face challenges that may not seem surmountable at first. However, through perseverance and the realization of inner strength, they can achieve victory over their circumstances.
    • In a business context, companies may face competition that seems overwhelming. By innovating and adapting, they can find new ways to succeed that were not previously considered.
    • In nature, storms can devastate landscapes, but they also bring necessary rain that fosters new growth, illustrating the duality of destruction and renewal.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder that true strength and victory often come from within and may not always be visible at first glance.
    • It encourages individuals to look beyond the surface and recognize the potential for new beginnings and strengths in their lives.
    • In a broader sense, it highlights the cyclical nature of life, where challenges can lead to growth and transformation, making it applicable in various real-life scenarios.
