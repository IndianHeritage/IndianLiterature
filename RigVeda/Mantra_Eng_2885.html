मरत्वन्तं वृषभं वावृद्धानम् अकवारीं दिव्यं शासम् इन्द्रम् |विश्वासाहम् अवसे नूतनायोग्रं सहोदाम् इह तं हुवेम ||
marutvantaṁ vṛṣabhaṁ vāvṛdhānam akavāriṁ divyaṁ śāsam indram | viśvāsāham avase nūtanāyograṁ sahodām iha taṁ huvema ||
  • marutvantaṁ: This term refers to the Maruts, the storm deities in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and power. In real life, this can be likened to the forces of nature that can bring both destruction and renewal, such as thunderstorms that nourish the earth.
  • vṛṣabhaṁ: This word means "bull," which is often associated with strength, virility, and fertility. In agriculture, a bull is essential for plowing fields, representing hard work and productivity in human endeavors.
  • vāvṛdhānam: This term signifies "growth" or "expansion." In a modern context, it can relate to personal development or the growth of a community or organization, emphasizing the importance of nurturing and fostering progress.
  • akavāriṁ: This word can be interpreted as "the one who is unrestrained" or "free." In life, this can represent the idea of breaking free from limitations, whether they are societal, personal, or psychological, to achieve one's true potential.
  • divyaṁ: Meaning "divine" or "heavenly," this term suggests a connection to higher ideals or spiritual aspirations. In everyday life, this can be seen in the pursuit of moral values, compassion, and altruism.
  • śāsam: This word translates to "command" or "rule." It can be related to leadership and governance, where effective leadership is crucial for guiding individuals and communities towards a common goal.
  • indram: Referring to Indra, the king of the gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms, this term embodies authority and power. In a real-life scenario, it can symbolize the importance of strong leadership in overcoming challenges.
  • viśvāsāham: This phrase means "I have faith" or "I trust." In daily life, trust is fundamental in relationships, whether personal or professional, as it fosters cooperation and understanding.
  • avase: This term can be interpreted as "to be present" or "to exist." It emphasizes the importance of being grounded and aware of one's surroundings, which is essential for making informed decisions.
  • nūtanāyograṁ: This phrase signifies "new strength" or "fresh vigor." In a practical sense, it can relate to the idea of rejuvenation, whether through rest, education, or new experiences that invigorate one's life.
  • sahodām: Meaning "brother" or "sibling," this term highlights the importance of community and support systems in our lives. It reflects the idea that we are stronger together, much like a family or a team working towards a common goal.
  • iha: This word means "here" or "in this place," emphasizing the significance of the present moment. It reminds us to focus on the now and make the most of our current circumstances.
  • taṁ huvema: This phrase translates to "we invoke that." It signifies the act of calling upon divine forces or energies to assist us in our endeavors, reflecting the human desire for guidance and support in life's journey.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, growth, community, and divine guidance. Its significance lies in the reminder that we can harness the forces of nature and the universe to foster personal and communal development. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in leadership roles, community building, personal growth, and the pursuit of higher ideals. By invoking these qualities, individuals can strive for a balanced and fulfilling life, grounded in trust, support, and a connection to the divine.
