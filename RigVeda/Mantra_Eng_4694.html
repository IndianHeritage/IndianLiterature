स सर्गेण शवसात्क्तो अत्यैर्व अप इन्द्रो दक्षिणतस् तुराशाट् |इत्त्था सृजानान् अनपावृदर्थं दिवे-दिवे विविषुर अप्रमृष्यम् ||
sa sargeṇa śavasā takto atyair apa indro dakṣiṇatas turāṣāṭ | itthā sṛjānā anapāvṛd arthaṁ dive-dive viviṣur apramṛṣyam ||
  • sa sargeṇa: This phrase can be translated as "with the creation" or "with the act of creation." It signifies the beginning of existence and the process of bringing forth life.
  • śavasā: This word means "with the dead" or "with the lifeless." It can refer to the state of being before life is infused into matter, highlighting the contrast between life and death.
  • takto: This term can be interpreted as "having been established" or "having taken form." It indicates the transition from potentiality to actuality, where something comes into being.
  • atyair: This word means "beyond" or "transcending." It suggests a movement beyond the ordinary, reaching towards the divine or the ultimate reality.
  • apa indro: This phrase translates to "O Indra," referring to the king of the gods in Vedic tradition. Indra symbolizes strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • dakṣiṇatas: This word means "from the south" or "towards the south." In many cultures, the south is associated with warmth, fertility, and abundance.
  • turāṣāṭ: This term can be understood as "with great speed" or "swiftly." It emphasizes the dynamic nature of creation and the rapid unfolding of existence.
  • itthā: This word means "thus" or "in this way." It indicates a manner of proceeding or a method of creation.
  • sṛjānā: This term translates to "creating" or "bringing forth." It emphasizes the active role of the divine in the process of creation.
  • anapāvṛd: This word means "unobstructed" or "without hindrance." It suggests a state of flow and freedom in the creative process.
  • arthaṁ: This term translates to "meaning" or "purpose." It signifies the intention behind creation, the underlying reason for existence.
  • dive-dive: This phrase means "day by day" or "each day." It emphasizes the continuous nature of creation and the unfolding of life over time.
  • viviṣur: This word means "they have entered" or "they have pervaded." It indicates the presence of the divine in all aspects of life.
  • apramṛṣyam: This term translates to "unattainable" or "beyond reach." It suggests that the ultimate truth or reality is something that transcends ordinary understanding.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, the interplay between life and death, and the divine forces that govern existence. It highlights the significance of purpose and intention in the act of creation, reminding us that life unfolds continuously and dynamically. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to recognize the importance of their actions and intentions in shaping their lives and the world around them. It encourages a deeper understanding of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine presence in everyday life, urging us to seek meaning and purpose in our endeavors.
