शृण्वे वीर उग्रं-उग्रं दामायन्न अन्यं-अन्यं अतिनेनीयमानः |एधमानद्विष् उभयस्य राजा चोष्कूयते विश इन्द्रो मनुष्याण् ||
śṛṇve vīra ugram-ugraṁ damāyann anyam-anyam atinenīyamānaḥ | edhamānadviḻ ubhayasya rājā coṣkūyate viśa indro manuṣyān ||
  • śṛṇve: This word translates to "I listen" or "I hear." It emphasizes the importance of being attentive and receptive to knowledge and wisdom.
  • vīra: Meaning "hero" or "brave one," it signifies strength and valor, qualities that are essential for overcoming challenges in life.
  • ugram-ugraṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "fierce" or "intense." It suggests a powerful force or energy that can be harnessed for positive outcomes.
  • damāyann: This means "controlling" or "restraining." It highlights the need for self-discipline and the ability to manage one's impulses and desires.
  • anyam-anyam: This translates to "each other" or "one another," indicating the interconnectedness of individuals and the importance of community.
  • atinenīyamānaḥ: This word refers to "being guided" or "being led." It suggests the necessity of guidance in our journeys, whether from mentors, traditions, or inner wisdom.
  • edhamānadviḻ: This can be understood as "kindled" or "ignited." It symbolizes the awakening of potential and the spark of inspiration that drives action.
  • ubhayasya: Meaning "of both," it signifies balance and harmony between different forces or aspects of life.
  • rājā: This translates to "king" or "ruler," representing authority and leadership, which can be applied in various contexts, from personal leadership to societal roles.
  • coṣkūyate: This means "is stirred" or "is agitated," indicating a dynamic process of change and movement in life.
  • viśa: This word means "of the people" or "community," emphasizing the collective aspect of human existence.
  • indro: Referring to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic tradition, symbolizing power, protection, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • manuṣyān: This translates to "humans" or "mankind," reminding us of our shared humanity and the responsibilities we have towards one another.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of listening, strength, control, and community. It emphasizes the importance of being guided and igniting one's potential while maintaining balance and leadership. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership roles, and community building. By embodying the qualities of a hero and being receptive to guidance, individuals can navigate challenges effectively and contribute positively to society. The significance of this mantra lies in its call for self-awareness, discipline, and the recognition of our interconnectedness, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and empowered existence.
