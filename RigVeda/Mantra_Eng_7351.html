वावृधानो मरुत्सखेंद्रो वि वृतम् ऐरयत् |सृजन समुद्रियाऽपः ||
vāvṛdhāno marutsakhendro vi vṛtram airayat | sṛjan samudriyā apaḥ ||
  • vāvṛdhāno: This term can be interpreted as "the one who increases" or "the one who expands." It signifies growth and enhancement, which can be related to personal development or the expansion of knowledge.
  • marutsakhendro: This refers to "the lord of the Maruts," where Maruts are storm deities associated with wind and storms. This term symbolizes power and the ability to bring about change, akin to how storms can transform landscapes.
  • vi vṛtram airayat: This phrase translates to "he who removes the obstacle." In real life, this can be seen as a metaphor for overcoming challenges or barriers that hinder progress, much like how a strong leader can guide a team through difficulties.
  • sṛjan: This word means "to create" or "to bring forth." It emphasizes the act of creation, which can be related to innovation in various fields, such as technology or art.
  • samudriyā apaḥ: This translates to "the waters of the ocean." The ocean represents vastness and depth, symbolizing the abundance of resources and opportunities available in life.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates themes of growth, power, overcoming obstacles, creation, and the abundance of resources. Its significance lies in the encouragement it provides to individuals to embrace change, seek personal development, and harness their inner strength to overcome challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in leadership roles, personal growth journeys, or creative endeavors, reminding us that with determination and the right mindset, we can navigate through life's storms and create a fulfilling existence.
