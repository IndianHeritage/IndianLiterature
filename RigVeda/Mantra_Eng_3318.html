कथा श्रिणोति हूयमानं इन्द्रः कथा श्रिण्वन्न अवसाम अस्य वेदः |का अस्य पूर्वीर उपमातयो ह कथेयं आहुः पपुरिं जरित्रे ||
kathā śṛṇoti hūyamānam indraḥ kathā śṛṇvann avasām asya veda | kā asya pūrvīr upamātayo ha kathainam āhuḥ papuriṁ jaritre ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Kathā: How
    • Śṛṇoti: Listens
    • Hūyamānam: Being invoked
    • Indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • Kathā: How
    • Śṛṇvann: Listening
    • Avasām: To this
    • Asya: Of this
    • Veda: Knows
    • Kā: What
    • Asya: Of this
    • Pūrvīr: Previous
    • Upamātayo: Comparisons
    • Ha: Indeed
    • Kathainam: How they
    • Āhuḥ: Say
    • Papurim: The great city
    • Jaritre: In the context of strength or power
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra addresses Indra, the chief deity in Vedic literature, who is often associated with strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • The phrase "kathā śṛṇoti" suggests a call to listen, indicating the importance of being attentive to divine guidance.
    • It reflects on how Indra, while being invoked, listens to the prayers and needs of the devotees.
    • The mantra also questions the nature of knowledge and understanding, asking how previous sages and wise individuals have perceived and articulated the divine.
    • The reference to "papurim jaritre" can be interpreted as a metaphor for the strength and resilience found in a community or society, akin to a great city that thrives on collective power.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be related to leadership and governance. Just as Indra listens to the needs of his devotees, effective leaders must listen to the concerns and aspirations of their people.
    • In personal life, it emphasizes the importance of being attentive to the voices around us, whether in family, friendships, or community, fostering a sense of connection and understanding.
    • In a corporate setting, the mantra can inspire leaders to create an environment where employees feel heard and valued, leading to a more productive and harmonious workplace.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of listening, understanding, and the collective strength that arises from unity and shared knowledge.
    • It teaches us that divine guidance is accessible when we are open to listening and learning from the wisdom of those who came before us.
    • In real life, it encourages us to cultivate a culture of attentiveness and respect, whether in personal relationships, community interactions, or professional environments, ultimately leading to a more harmonious existence.
