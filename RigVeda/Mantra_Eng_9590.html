शचीव इन्द्रं अवसे कृणुध्वम् अनानतं दमयन्तं पृथन्यून् |ऋभुक्षणं मगवानं सुवृक्तिम भार्ता यो वज्रं नार्यम् पुरुक्षुः ||
śacīva indram avase kṛṇudhvam anānataṁ damayantam pṛtanyūn | ṛbhukṣaṇam maghavānaṁ suvṛktim bhartā yo vajraṁ naryam purukṣuḥ ||
  • śacīva indram avase kṛṇudhvam: This phrase can be translated as "O Indra, the powerful one, we invoke you." Here, "Indra" represents the king of the gods, symbolizing strength and leadership. In real life, this can be likened to seeking guidance from a strong leader or mentor in challenging situations.
  • anānataṁ damayantam pṛtanyūn: This translates to "who is unyielding and brings prosperity." The term "damayantam" refers to the ability to control or manage, suggesting that true strength lies in the ability to govern oneself and one's circumstances. For example, a successful entrepreneur often demonstrates this quality by managing resources effectively to ensure growth.
  • ṛbhukṣaṇam maghavānaṁ: This means "the one who is generous and bountiful." It highlights the importance of generosity in leadership. In a community setting, a leader who shares resources and knowledge fosters a sense of unity and progress among members.
  • suvṛktim bhartā yo vajraṁ naryam purukṣuḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who possesses excellent qualities and wields the thunderbolt." The "thunderbolt" symbolizes power and the ability to overcome obstacles. In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who face challenges head-on, using their skills and qualities to achieve their goals.

  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the qualities of strength, leadership, generosity, and resilience. In real life, these attributes are essential for personal and communal success. By invoking the spirit of Indra, individuals can aspire to embody these qualities, whether in their professional lives, community involvement, or personal growth. The mantra serves as a reminder that true power comes from within and is best expressed through positive actions and leadership.
