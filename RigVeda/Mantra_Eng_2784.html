तवायं सोमस त्वम एष्य अरवाङ् चश्वत्तमं सुमना अस्य पाहि |अस्मिन यज्ञे बरहिष्य आ निषद्यां दधिष्वेमं जठर इन्दुम इन्द्र ||
tavāyaṁ somas tvam ehy arvāṅ chaśvattamaṁ sumanā asya pāhi | asmin yajñe barhiṣy ā niṣadyā dadhiṣvemaṁ jaṭhara indum indra ||
  • tavāyaṁ somas: This phrase can be translated as "O Soma, this is for you." Soma is a significant deity in Vedic literature, often associated with the moon and the elixir of immortality. In real life, this can symbolize the importance of nourishment and sustenance, both physical and spiritual.
  • tvam ehy: This means "come to us." It is an invitation for the divine presence to be with us. In everyday life, this can be seen as a call for guidance and support in our endeavors, whether in personal challenges or communal efforts.
  • arvāṅ chaśvattamaṁ: This translates to "the best of the best." It signifies the highest quality or the ultimate form of something. In practical terms, this can relate to striving for excellence in our actions, relationships, and pursuits.
  • sumanā asya pāhi: This means "protect this good thought." It emphasizes the importance of positive thinking and intentions. In real life, this can be applied by fostering a mindset that encourages growth, compassion, and understanding in our interactions with others.
  • asmin yajñe barhiṣy ā: This translates to "in this sacrifice, let us offer." Sacrifice here refers to the act of giving up something for a greater purpose. In modern contexts, this can be seen in charitable acts, community service, or personal sacrifices made for family and friends.
  • niṣadyā dadhiṣvemaṁ: This means "let us establish this." It signifies the act of creating or manifesting something. In real life, this can relate to setting goals and working towards them, whether in career, education, or personal development.
  • jaṭhara indum indra: This translates to "the moon in the belly, O Indra." Here, the moon symbolizes nourishment and the divine essence within us. Indra, as a powerful deity, represents strength and leadership. This can be interpreted as the inner strength we possess and the potential for growth and enlightenment that resides within us.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the importance of divine presence, positive intentions, and the pursuit of excellence. It encourages individuals to seek nourishment—both physical and spiritual—and to protect and cultivate good thoughts. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, community service, and fostering positive relationships. By inviting divine guidance and striving for the best in our actions, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
