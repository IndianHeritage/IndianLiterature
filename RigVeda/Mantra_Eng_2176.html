तद अस्मै नव्यम् अङ्गिरस्वद् आर्चत शुष्मा यद अस्य प्रत्नथोदीरते |विश्वा यद गोत्रा सहसा परिवृता मादे सोमस्य दृष्मितान्य ऐरयत ||
tad asmai navyam aṅgirasvad arcata śuṣmā yad asya pratnathodīrate | viśvā yad gotrā sahasā parīvṛtā made somasya dṛṁhitāny airayat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad: that
    • asmai: to him
    • navyam: new
    • aṅgirasvad: of the Angiras (a group of sages)
    • arcata: worshipped
    • śuṣmā: the beautiful
    • yad: which
    • asya: of this
    • pratnathodīrate: when it rises
    • viśvā: all
    • yad: which
    • gotrā: by the clans
    • sahasā: suddenly
    • parīvṛtā: surrounded
    • made: in the joy
    • somasya: of the Soma (a sacred drink)
    • dṛṁhitāny: the offerings
    • airayat: may they bring
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the worship of a new and beautiful aspect of divinity, represented by the Angiras sages.
    • It emphasizes the importance of collective worship and the joy that arises from it, particularly in the context of the sacred Soma drink, which symbolizes divine inspiration and ecstasy.
    • The phrase "when it rises" suggests a moment of awakening or enlightenment, indicating that spiritual growth often comes suddenly and unexpectedly.
    • The mention of clans being surrounded by joy highlights the communal aspect of spirituality, where collective experiences enhance individual growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, this mantra can be related to community gatherings, such as festivals or religious ceremonies, where people come together to celebrate and worship, creating a sense of unity and shared joy.
    • In workplaces, team-building activities can be seen as a form of collective worship, where individuals come together to foster collaboration and creativity, leading to unexpected breakthroughs.
    • Personal moments of enlightenment, such as a sudden realization or inspiration, can be likened to the "rising" mentioned in the mantra, reminding us that growth can happen in an instant.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of collective worship and the joy that arises from shared spiritual experiences.
    • It teaches us that enlightenment and growth can occur unexpectedly and that communal support enhances individual journeys.
    • In real life, it encourages us to seek out community, celebrate together, and remain open to moments of inspiration that can lead to profound personal transformation.
