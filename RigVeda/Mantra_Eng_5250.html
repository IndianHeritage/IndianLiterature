सेमां वेतु वषट्कृतिम अग्निर जुषत नो गिरः | यजिष्टो हव्यवाहनः ||
semāṁ vetu vaṣaṭkṛtim agnir juṣata no giraḥ | yajiṣṭho havyavāhanaḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May the fire, which is the bestower of offerings, accept our invocations and grant us the wealth of speech."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • semāṁ: This word refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the abundance that one seeks in life.
    • vetu: This means "to know" or "to understand." It implies a quest for knowledge and wisdom.
    • vaṣaṭkṛtim: This term relates to the act of invocation or calling upon the divine. It is often used in the context of rituals.
    • agnir: This refers to "fire," which is a sacred element in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and transformation.
    • juṣata: This means "to enjoy" or "to accept." It indicates a desire for the divine to accept our offerings.
    • no: This translates to "our," indicating a personal connection to the invocation.
    • giraḥ: This word means "speech" or "words," emphasizing the importance of communication and expression.
    • yajiṣṭho: This means "the best of the worshippers," highlighting the significance of devotion in rituals.
    • havyavāhanaḥ: This term refers to "the one who carries offerings," often associated with deities who accept sacrifices.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be applied during significant life events such as weddings or housewarming ceremonies, where individuals seek blessings for prosperity and harmonious communication.
    • In professional settings, invoking this mantra can serve as a reminder to communicate effectively and seek knowledge, fostering a collaborative environment.
    • In personal development, the quest for wisdom and understanding, as suggested by the mantra, can inspire individuals to pursue education and self-improvement.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine acceptance of our offerings and the pursuit of prosperity through knowledge and effective communication. It emphasizes the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine and the role of speech in manifesting our desires. In real life, this mantra encourages individuals to seek wisdom, communicate clearly, and engage in practices that foster abundance and harmony in their lives.
