त्वां चित्रश्रवस्तम हवन्ते विक्षु जन्तवः | शोचिष्केशं पुरुप्रियाग्ने हव्याय वोऽलभते ||
tvāṁ citraśravastama havante vikṣu jantavaḥ | śociṣkeśam purupriyāgne havyāya voḻhave ||
  • tvāṁ - This word translates to "you" and signifies a direct address to a divine entity or force.
  • citraśravastama - This term means "of varied fame" or "most renowned," indicating the multifaceted nature of the divine being's glory.
  • havante - This word means "those who offer" or "the worshippers," referring to the individuals who are performing the ritual or offering.
  • vikṣu - This translates to "the seekers" or "those who are in search," highlighting the quest for spiritual knowledge or fulfillment.
  • jantavaḥ - This term means "beings" or "creatures," encompassing all living entities that are part of the universe.
  • śociṣkeśam - This phrase can be interpreted as "the one with radiant hair," symbolizing purity and brilliance, often associated with divine beings.
  • purupriyāgne - This means "the one who is dear to the people," indicating a connection between the divine and humanity, emphasizing the importance of the divine in everyday life.
  • havyāya - This translates to "for the offering," referring to the act of giving or dedicating something to the divine.
  • voḻhave - This word means "to you," reinforcing the personal connection and devotion of the worshippers to the divine entity.

The mantra can be understood as a prayer or invocation directed towards a divine being, acknowledging their multifaceted glory and the relationship between the divine and all living beings. It emphasizes the importance of offerings and the quest for spiritual fulfillment.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or prayer to connect with the divine and seek guidance in their lives.
  • Community Gatherings: It can be used in communal rituals or festivals, where people come together to offer their prayers and celebrate their shared beliefs.
  • Personal Reflection: The mantra encourages individuals to reflect on their actions and their relationship with the divine, fostering a sense of responsibility towards themselves and others.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with the divine, emphasizing the importance of offerings and the quest for spiritual knowledge. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the role of the divine in guiding and supporting humanity. By incorporating this mantra into daily life, individuals can cultivate a deeper sense of spirituality and community.
