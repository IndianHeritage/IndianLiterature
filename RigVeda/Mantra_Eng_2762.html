एतद वाचो जारितर मापि मृष्ठा आ यत ते घोषान उत्तरā युगानि |उक्तेषु कारो प्रति नो जुषस्व मा नो नि कः पुरुषत्रा नमस ते ||
etad vaco jaritar māpi mṛṣṭhā ā yat te ghoṣān uttarā yugāni | uktheṣu kāro prati no juṣasva mā no ni kaḥ puruṣatrā namas te ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • etad: this
    • vaco: speech or words
    • jaritar: older or ancient
    • māpi: even
    • mṛṣṭhā: made or formed
    • ā: from
    • yat: which
    • te: your
    • ghoṣān: sounds or voices
    • uttarā: higher or superior
    • yugāni: ages or epochs
    • uktheṣu: in the utterances
    • kāro: action or deed
    • prati: towards
    • no: us
    • juṣasva: may you delight
    • mā: not
    • no: us
    • ni: down or below
    • kaḥ: who
    • puruṣatrā: to the man or person
    • namas: salutation or respect
    • te: to you
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the power of words and the importance of respecting the ancient wisdom that has been passed down through generations.
    • It emphasizes the need for us to engage with and appreciate the higher truths and sounds that resonate through time.
    • The phrase "may you delight us" suggests a call for positive engagement and the nurturing of wisdom in our lives.
    • It also reflects a sense of humility, acknowledging that we are part of a larger continuum of knowledge and existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern education, the respect for ancient texts and teachings can be seen in how we study classical literature, philosophy, and science. For instance, the teachings of Socrates or the writings of the RigVeda itself are still relevant and respected today.
    • In personal development, individuals often seek wisdom from past generations, whether through literature, mentorship, or spiritual practices, recognizing that these insights can guide their current lives.
    • In community settings, honoring the voices of elders and their experiences can lead to a richer understanding of cultural heritage and values, fostering a sense of belonging and continuity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of words and the wisdom they carry across ages.
    • It encourages us to engage with the teachings of the past, to respect the voices that have shaped our understanding, and to seek higher truths in our lives.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in various aspects of life, from education to personal growth, and in fostering community connections, ultimately leading to a more enlightened and harmonious existence.
