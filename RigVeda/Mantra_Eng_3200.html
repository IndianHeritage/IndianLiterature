वहिष्ठेभिर विहरन् यासि तन्तुम अवव्ययन्न असितं देव वस्म |दविद्भतो रश्मयः सूर्यस्य चर्मेवावाधुस तमो अप्स्व अन्तः ||
vahiṣṭhebhir viharan yāsi tantum avavyayann asitaṁ deva vasma | davidhvato raśmayaḥ sūryasya carmevāvādhus tamo apsv a1ntaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vahiṣṭhebhir: With the best or the most excellent.
    • viharan: Moving or traveling.
    • yāsi: You go or you move.
    • tantum: The thread or the fabric.
    • avavyayann: Unbroken or undiminished.
    • asitaṁ: The dark or the black.
    • deva: God or divine.
    • vasma: The essence or the substance.
    • davidhvato: Of the one who is moving or traveling.
    • raśmayaḥ: Rays or beams.
    • sūryasya: Of the sun.
    • carmev: Like a garment.
    • āvādhus: To remove or to dispel.
    • tamo: Darkness.
    • apsv: In the waters.
    • antaḥ: Within or inside.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine journey through the best paths, emphasizing the importance of moving towards enlightenment and truth.
    • The reference to "the dark" signifies ignorance or obstacles that one must overcome to reach a higher state of being.
    • The "rays of the sun" symbolize knowledge, clarity, and illumination that dispel darkness, akin to how sunlight brightens a room.
    • In real life, this can be related to personal growth, where individuals strive to overcome challenges and ignorance in their lives, seeking knowledge and wisdom.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the journey of life, where one must navigate through challenges (darkness) to reach a state of enlightenment (light).
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and truth, much like how one would seek guidance in difficult times.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various scenarios such as education, personal development, and spiritual growth, where the pursuit of knowledge leads to overcoming ignorance and achieving one's goals.
