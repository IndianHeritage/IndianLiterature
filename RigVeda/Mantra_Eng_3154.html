तं īं होतारं आनुṣक चिकीत्वांसं नि षेदिरे |रण्वं पावकशोचिषं यजिष्ठं सप्त धामभिः ||
tam īṁ hotāram ānuṣak cikitvāṁsaṁ ni ṣedire | raṇvam pāvakaśociṣaṁ yajiṣṭhaṁ sapta dhāmabhiḥ ||
  • tam - This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity or force.
  • īṁ - This is a sound that signifies invocation or calling upon a deity.
  • hotāram - This means "the one who offers," typically referring to a priest or a divine being who performs rituals.
  • ānuṣak - This can be interpreted as "the one who is aware" or "the one who understands," indicating a level of consciousness or mindfulness.
  • cikitvāṁsaṁ - This word suggests "the one who is wise" or "the one who has knowledge," emphasizing the importance of wisdom in actions.
  • ni ṣedire - This translates to "they have restrained" or "they have controlled," indicating the power of discipline and control over one's actions.
  • raṇvam - This means "the one who is radiant" or "the one who shines," often associated with purity and brightness.
  • pāvakaśociṣaṁ - This can be interpreted as "the one who is pure and bright," referring to a divine light or energy that purifies.
  • yajiṣṭhaṁ - This means "the most worshipped" or "the one who is revered," indicating the importance of devotion and reverence in spiritual practices.
  • sapta - This translates to "seven," which often symbolizes completeness or wholeness in Vedic literature.
  • dhāmabhiḥ - This means "with realms" or "with abodes," referring to different planes of existence or states of being.

The mantra speaks to the invocation of a divine force that embodies wisdom, purity, and control. It emphasizes the importance of understanding and mindfulness in one's actions, as well as the significance of worship and reverence towards the divine. The mention of "seven realms" suggests a holistic approach to spirituality, encompassing various aspects of existence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Mindfulness in Daily Life: Just as the mantra emphasizes awareness and understanding, individuals can practice mindfulness in their daily activities, leading to better decision-making and emotional regulation.
  • Discipline and Control: The idea of restraint can be applied in personal development, where individuals learn to control their impulses and focus on long-term goals.
  • Spiritual Practices: The reverence for the divine can be reflected in various spiritual practices, such as meditation, prayer, or rituals that promote a sense of connection to something greater than oneself.
  • Community and Wholeness: The concept of "seven realms" can be seen in the importance of community and interconnectedness, where individuals recognize their role in a larger societal context.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of wisdom, purity, and discipline in our lives. It encourages individuals to cultivate mindfulness, control their actions, and engage in spiritual practices that foster a deeper connection to the divine and to each other. By embodying these principles, one can lead a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
