मन्त्र: मन्द्रस्य रूपं विविधुर मनीषिणः श्येनो यदन्धो अभरत परावतः |तम मार्जयन्त सुर्वृद्धं नदिष्व आमुशन्तम् अंशुम पर्यन्तम् ऋग्मियम् ||
mandrasya rūpaṁ vividur manīṣiṇaḥ śyeno yad andho abharat parāvataḥ | tam marjayanta suvṛdhaṁ nadīṣv ām̐ uśantam aṁśum pariyantam ṛgmiyam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mandrasya: of the mantra or hymn
    • rūpaṁ: form or essence
    • vividur: the wise or knowledgeable
    • manīṣiṇaḥ: the thinkers or philosophers
    • śyena: the eagle or bird of prey
    • yad: that which
    • andho: the blind or ignorant
    • abharat: carried or brought
    • parāvataḥ: the higher or elevated
    • tam: that
    • marjayanta: purifying or cleansing
    • suvṛdhaṁ: well-nourished or prosperous
    • nadīṣv: in the rivers
    • ām̐: indeed
    • uśantam: shining or radiant
    • aṁśum: the light or sun
    • pariyantam: encompassing or surrounding
    • ṛgmiyam: the hymns or verses
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the essence of a hymn that is understood by the wise.
    • The eagle symbolizes vision and clarity, suggesting that true understanding comes from a higher perspective.
    • The reference to the blind indicates ignorance, which can be overcome by seeking knowledge and wisdom.
    • The act of purification signifies the importance of cleansing one's thoughts and actions to attain prosperity.
    • The rivers represent the flow of life and knowledge, where the radiant light symbolizes enlightenment and truth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, students who seek knowledge with an open mind and a desire to learn can be compared to the "wise" who understand the essence of learning.
    • Professionals who take a step back to gain a broader perspective on their work can be likened to the eagle, allowing them to make better decisions.
    • Individuals who engage in self-reflection and strive to purify their intentions can find prosperity in their personal and professional lives.
    • Communities that come together to share knowledge and support each other can create a flow of wisdom akin to the rivers mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom, purification, and the pursuit of knowledge.
    • It teaches us that by overcoming ignorance and seeking a higher understanding, we can achieve prosperity and enlightenment.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, better decision-making, and a more harmonious community.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the importance of continuous learning and the transformative power of knowledge.
