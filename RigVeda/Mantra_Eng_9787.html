जघान वृतं स्वधितिर वनेव रुरोज पुरो अरदन् न सिन्धून् |बिभेद गिरीं नवमिन् न कुम्भम् आ गा इन्द्रो अकृत्नुत स्वयुग्भिः ||
jaghāna vṛtraṁ svadhitir vaneva ruroja puro aradan na sindhūn | bibheda giriṁ navam in na kumbham ā gā indro akṛṇuta svayugbhiḥ ||
  • jaghāna vṛtraṁ: This phrase translates to "he struck down Vritra." Vritra is often depicted as a serpent or dragon that represents chaos and obstruction. In real life, this can symbolize overcoming obstacles or challenges that hinder progress.
  • svadhitir vaneva ruroja: This means "like a river that flows." Here, "svadhitir" refers to self-determination or inner strength, while "van" means "to flow." This can be likened to how a river carves its path through the landscape, representing resilience and the ability to adapt to circumstances.
  • puro aradan na sindhūn: This translates to "he did not let the rivers be obstructed." This signifies the importance of maintaining the flow of life and resources, akin to ensuring that opportunities and pathways remain open in our lives.
  • bibheda giriṁ: This means "he split the mountain." Mountains often symbolize great challenges or barriers. The act of splitting a mountain can represent breaking through significant challenges in life, much like achieving a major goal or overcoming a personal struggle.
  • navam in na kumbham: This translates to "new and fresh." It signifies renewal and the idea of starting anew, which is essential in various aspects of life, such as personal growth, career changes, or new beginnings in relationships.
  • ā gā indro akṛṇuta svayugbhiḥ: This means "Indra, with his own strength, created." Indra is a deity associated with power and leadership. This phrase emphasizes the importance of self-reliance and the ability to create one's destiny through personal effort and strength.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the themes of overcoming obstacles, resilience, and self-determination. In real life, it serves as a reminder that challenges can be faced and conquered, much like how rivers flow and mountains can be split. It encourages individuals to harness their inner strength and creativity to navigate life's difficulties and to embrace new beginnings. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as personal development, professional challenges, and even in collective efforts to bring about social change. By embodying the spirit of this mantra, one can strive for progress and transformation in their life and the lives of others.
