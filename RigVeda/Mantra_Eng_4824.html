धृतश् पिब कलशे सोमं इन्द्र वृत्रहा शूर समरे वसूनाम् |माध्यं दिने सवन आ वृषस्व रयिष्ठानो रयिम अस्मासु धेहि ||
dhṛṣat piba kalaśe somam indra vṛtrahā śūra samare vasūnām | mādhyaṁdine savana ā vṛṣasva rayisthāno rayim asmāsu dhehi ||
  • Translation: "O Indra, the slayer of Vritra, drink the soma from the vessel; you are the brave one in battle, grant us the wealth of the Vasus. In the midday offering, be present and bestow upon us the riches that reside with us."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dhṛṣat: O brave one
    • piba: drink
    • kalaśe: from the vessel
    • somam: soma (a sacred drink)
    • indra: Indra (the king of gods)
    • vṛtrahā: slayer of Vritra (a demon)
    • śūra: brave
    • samare: in battle
    • vasūnām: of the Vasus (deities of wealth)
    • mādhyaṁdine: at midday
    • savana: offering
    • ā: come
    • vṛṣasva: bestow
    • rayisthāno: the one who resides in wealth
    • rayim: riches
    • asmāsu: with us
    • dhehi: grant
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, a central figure in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, courage, and the power to overcome obstacles.
    • The reference to drinking soma signifies the pursuit of divine inspiration and vitality, akin to seeking knowledge and enlightenment in modern life.
    • The mention of the Vasus highlights the importance of wealth and prosperity, which can be interpreted as a call for abundance in various aspects of life, including health, relationships, and material success.
    • The midday offering symbolizes the importance of rituals and gratitude, reminding us to acknowledge and appreciate the blessings we receive throughout the day.
  • Real-Life Applications:
    • In personal development, this mantra can inspire individuals to cultivate bravery and resilience in the face of challenges, much like Indra's role in overcoming Vritra.
    • In business, the call for wealth can motivate entrepreneurs to strive for success while maintaining ethical practices, ensuring that their endeavors benefit not just themselves but also their communities.
    • In daily life, the practice of gratitude, as suggested by the midday offering, can enhance mental well-being and foster a positive outlook, encouraging individuals to recognize the abundance around them.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the virtues of courage, the pursuit of wealth, and the importance of gratitude. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate their lives with strength and purpose, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling existence. Its significance transcends time, making it relevant in various aspects of modern life, from personal growth to professional success.
