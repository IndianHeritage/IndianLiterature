अभि त्वा वृषभा सुत सुतं सृजामि पीतये |तृम्पा व्य अशनुही मदम् ||
abhi tvā vṛṣabhā sute sutaṁ sṛjāmi pītaye | tṛmpā vy aśnuhī madam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • abhi: towards, for
    • tvā: you
    • vṛṣabhā: the strong, the bull (often symbolizes strength and power)
    • sute: offspring, progeny
    • sutaṁ: son, child
    • sṛjāmi: I create, I produce
    • pītaye: for the one who is to be nourished
    • tṛmpā: satisfaction, contentment
    • vy: indeed
    • aśnuhī: may consume, may partake
    • madam: intoxication, bliss
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra expresses a prayer or invocation to a powerful entity (often interpreted as a deity) to bless the speaker with strength and progeny. The speaker is creating or producing a child for the purpose of nourishment and satisfaction, invoking a sense of bliss and fulfillment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, parents often pray for the health and strength of their children, hoping they will grow up to be strong and capable individuals, much like the "vṛṣabhā" or bull, symbolizing strength.
    • In agricultural communities, the birth of strong livestock is crucial for the prosperity of the farm, paralleling the idea of producing strong offspring for nourishment.
    • In a corporate environment, leaders may invoke this mantra metaphorically when they seek to nurture talent within their teams, aiming to create strong leaders who will contribute to the organization's success.
  • Conclusion and Significance: The mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, nourishment, and the creation of future generations. It highlights the interconnectedness of life, where the strength of the progeny is vital for the sustenance of families, communities, and organizations. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as parenting, leadership, and community building, reminding us of our responsibilities to nurture and empower the next generation for a prosperous future.
