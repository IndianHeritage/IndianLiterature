एषा स्यावो मरुतोऽनुभर्त्री प्रतिष्ठोभति वाघतो न वाणी |अस्तोभयद वृथासाम् अनु स्वधां गभस्त्योः ||
eṣā syā vo maruto 'nubhartrī prati ṣṭobhati vāghato na vāṇī | astobhayad vṛthāsām anu svadhāṁ gabhastyoḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣā: this
    • syā: may be
    • vo: for you
    • maruto: O Maruts (the storm deities)
    • anubhartrī: the one who supports or nourishes
    • prati: towards
    • ṣṭobhati: may it be established
    • vāghato: of speech
    • na: not
    • vāṇī: the voice or sound
    • astobhayad: may it not be disturbed
    • vṛthāsām: of the unworthy
    • anu: following
    • svadhāṁ: of the self or essence
    • gabhastyoḥ: of the luminous or radiant
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the Maruts, the storm deities, asking for their blessings and support.
    • The phrase "may it be established" indicates a desire for stability and strength in communication and expression.
    • The mention of "not disturbed" suggests a plea for clarity and focus, especially in the face of distractions or negativity.
    • The reference to "the unworthy" implies a desire to rise above trivial matters and engage in meaningful dialogue.
    • The term "luminous" signifies enlightenment and wisdom, suggesting that the speaker seeks guidance from higher knowledge.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied when preparing for a presentation or important meeting, invoking clarity and confidence in communication.
    • In personal relationships, it can serve as a reminder to engage in meaningful conversations, avoiding misunderstandings and focusing on genuine connection.
    • During challenging times, such as conflicts or crises, reciting this mantra can help individuals seek inner strength and clarity to navigate through difficulties.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in communication and expression.
    • It emphasizes the importance of clarity, focus, and meaningful dialogue in various aspects of life.
    • By invoking the blessings of the Maruts, individuals can aspire to rise above distractions and engage in enlightened conversations, fostering better relationships and understanding.
