त्वं दूतो अमर्त्य आ वहा दैव्यं जनम् |शृण्वन विप्रस्य सुश्रुतिम ||
tvaṁ dūto amartya ā vahā daivyaṁ janam | śṛṇvan viprasya suṣṭutim ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a divine or celestial being.
  • dūto - Meaning "messenger" or "envoy," this term signifies the role of a divine intermediary.
  • amartya - This translates to "immortal," suggesting a connection to the eternal or the divine nature of the messenger.
  • ā vahā - This phrase means "you carry" or "you bring forth," indicating the act of delivering or conveying something important.
  • daivyaṁ - Meaning "divine," this word emphasizes the sacred or heavenly quality of the subject being addressed.
  • janam - This translates to "people" or "humanity," referring to the collective of individuals who are receiving the message.
  • śṛṇvan - Meaning "hearing" or "listening," this term highlights the importance of attentiveness to the message being conveyed.
  • viprasya - This translates to "of the wise" or "of the learned," indicating that the message is intended for those who possess knowledge and wisdom.
  • suṣṭutim - Meaning "good praise" or "excellent commendation," this term signifies the positive acknowledgment or reverence that is to be expressed.

  • Meaning and Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to a divine messenger who is immortal and carries a significant message for humanity.
    • The emphasis on listening suggests that the message is crucial and should be received with attention and respect.
    • The reference to the wise indicates that the teachings or insights being shared are meant for those who seek knowledge and understanding.
    • The call for "good praise" implies that the message is not only to be heard but also to be appreciated and honored.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be likened to the role of educators or mentors who serve as messengers of knowledge and wisdom.
    • For instance, a teacher delivering an important lesson embodies the essence of the "dūto," guiding students towards understanding and enlightenment.
    • In spiritual practices, individuals often seek guidance from spiritual leaders or texts, reflecting the idea of listening to the wise and honoring their teachings.
    • In community settings, leaders who advocate for positive change can be seen as divine messengers, carrying messages of hope and progress for the people.

  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call to recognize and honor the divine messages that come through various channels in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to be attentive listeners, especially to those who possess wisdom and knowledge.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of communication, respect for knowledge, and the appreciation of those who guide us.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine, urging us to embrace the messages that lead to growth and understanding.
