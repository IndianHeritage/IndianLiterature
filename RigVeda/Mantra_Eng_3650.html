प्र सद्यो अग्ने अत्य एष्य अन्यान आविर्यस्मै चारुतमो बभूथा |ईḻेन्यो वपुष्यो विभावा प्रियो विश्वाम अतिथिर मानवशीलाम ||
pra sadyo agne aty eṣy anyān āvir yasmai cārutamo babhūtha | īḻenyo vapuṣyo vibhāvā priyo viśām atithir mānuṣīṇām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: forward, towards
    • sadyo: immediately, right now
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • aty: very, exceedingly
    • eṣy: may come, may arrive
    • anyān: others, different
    • āvir: manifest, appear
    • yasmai: to whom, for whom
    • cārutamo: the most beautiful, the most charming
    • babhūtha: has become, has manifested
    • īḻenyo: with offerings, with worship
    • vapuṣyo: of forms, of bodies
    • vibhāvā: of various kinds, of different types
    • priyo: dear, beloved
    • viśām: of the people, of the community
    • atithir: guest, visitor
    • mānuṣīṇām: of humans, of mankind
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, asking for his immediate presence and blessings.
    • It emphasizes the beauty and charm of Agni, suggesting that he is the most appealing among the deities.
    • The mantra also highlights the importance of offerings and worship, indicating that various forms of devotion are appreciated.
    • It recognizes the significance of guests and the community, suggesting that hospitality and kindness are valued traits among humans.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a traditional Indian household, lighting a fire or a lamp during rituals symbolizes inviting divine presence, akin to invoking Agni.
    • When hosting guests, the emphasis on hospitality reflects the idea of treating visitors as sacred, resonating with the concept of 'atithir' in the mantra.
    • Community gatherings often involve offerings and shared meals, embodying the spirit of togetherness and appreciation for diverse forms of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine presence in our lives, urging us to seek blessings and beauty in our actions.
    • It teaches us the value of hospitality, community, and the various forms of devotion we can express.
    • In real life, it encourages us to foster connections with others, appreciate beauty, and honor the divine through our actions and relationships.
