त्वां अग्नि अङ्गिरसो गुहा हितं अन्व अविन्दञ् चिष्रियाणं वने-वने |स जायतसे मथ्यमानः सहो महत त्वां आहुः सहसस् पुत्रम् अङ्गिरः ||
tvām agne aṅgiraso guhā hitam anv avindañ chiśriyāṇaṁ vane-vane | sa jāyase mathyamānaḥ saho mahat tvām āhuḥ sahasas putram aṅgiraḥ ||
  • tvām agne: This phrase translates to "to you, O Agni." Agni is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, light, and transformation. In real life, fire represents both physical and metaphorical transformation, such as the process of cooking food or the burning away of old habits to make way for new growth.
  • aṅgiraso: Referring to the Angirasas, a group of sages known for their wisdom and knowledge. This highlights the importance of seeking guidance from wise individuals in our lives, such as mentors or teachers who can help us navigate challenges.
  • guhā hitam: This means "hidden in the cave." It symbolizes the deeper truths and knowledge that are often concealed within us or in the world around us. In life, this can relate to introspection and the journey of self-discovery, where we uncover our true potential.
  • anv avindañ chiśriyāṇaṁ: This translates to "we have found the most excellent." It signifies the pursuit of excellence and the importance of striving for the best in our endeavors, whether in our careers, relationships, or personal growth.
  • vane-vane: Meaning "in the forest," this phrase evokes the idea of nature and the wilderness. It reminds us of the importance of connecting with nature and finding peace and inspiration in the natural world, which can be a source of rejuvenation and clarity.
  • sa jāyase mathyamānaḥ: This means "you are born from the great." It emphasizes the idea of being part of something larger than ourselves, such as community, culture, or the universe. In real life, this can inspire us to contribute positively to society and recognize our interconnectedness.
  • saho mahat: This translates to "with great strength." It signifies the power of resilience and determination in overcoming obstacles. In our daily lives, this can be seen in individuals who face challenges with courage and perseverance, inspiring others around them.
  • tvām āhuḥ sahasas putram aṅgiraḥ: This means "the Angirasas call you their son." It highlights the relationship between knowledge, wisdom, and the divine. In practical terms, it suggests that by seeking knowledge and wisdom, we can cultivate a deeper connection with the universe and our own spiritual growth.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of seeking knowledge, wisdom, and transformation through the divine energy of Agni. It encourages us to explore the hidden depths within ourselves, strive for excellence, and connect with nature. In real life, these principles can guide us in our personal and professional journeys, reminding us of the importance of resilience, community, and the pursuit of higher truths. By embodying these values, we can lead more fulfilling and meaningful lives.
