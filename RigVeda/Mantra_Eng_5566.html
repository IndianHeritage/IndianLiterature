आदित्यानाम् अवसाऽनूतनेन सक्षीमहि शर्मणा शान्तमेना |अनागास्त्वे अदितित्वे तुरास इमं यज्ञं दधतु श्रोषमाणाः ||
ādityānām avasā nūtanena sakṣīmahi śarmaṇā śaṁtamena | anāgāstve adititve turāsa imaṁ yajñaṁ dadhatu śroṣamāṇāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ādityānām: Of the Adityas (solar deities)
    • avasā: We take refuge
    • nūtanena: With the new (or fresh)
    • sakṣīmahi: We invoke or call upon
    • śarmaṇā: With protection or shelter
    • śaṁtamena: With peace or tranquility
    • anāgāstve: You are free from sin
    • adititve: O Aditi (the infinite or boundless)
    • turāsa: Swift or quick
    • imaṁ: This
    • yajñaṁ: Sacrifice or offering
    • dadhatu: May they establish or create
    • śroṣamāṇāḥ: Those who are listening or attentive
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the Adityas, who are considered the solar deities in Vedic literature. They symbolize light, truth, and the life-giving force of the sun.
    • The phrase "we take refuge with the new" suggests a desire for renewal and fresh beginnings, which can be related to personal growth or starting new ventures in life.
    • By calling upon the Adityas for protection and peace, the mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in our endeavors.
    • The reference to Aditi, the mother of the Adityas, signifies the boundless nature of the universe and the infinite possibilities available to us.
    • The term "yajña" refers to a sacred offering or sacrifice, which can be interpreted as the dedication of our actions and intentions to a higher purpose.
    • The concluding part, "may they establish for those who are listening," highlights the importance of being attentive and receptive to divine wisdom and guidance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, someone starting a new job might recite this mantra to seek guidance and protection as they embark on this new journey.
    • A community coming together for a festival or celebration may chant this mantra to invoke blessings for peace and harmony among its members.
    • In a spiritual practice, individuals may use this mantra during meditation to focus on renewal and the pursuit of higher consciousness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine support in our lives, especially during times of change or uncertainty.
    • It encourages us to be mindful of our actions and intentions, dedicating them to a greater purpose.
    • By invoking the Adityas and Aditi, we align ourselves with the forces of light and renewal, fostering a sense of peace and protection in our lives.
