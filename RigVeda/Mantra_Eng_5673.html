तā हि देवानाम् असुरā ताव् आर्यā ता नः क्षितीः करतम् ऊर्जयन्तीः |अश्याम् मित्रावरुणा वयं वां द्यावā च यत्र पीपयन्न अहा च ||
tā hi devānām asurā tāv aryā tā naḥ kṣitīḥ karatam ūrjayantīḥ | aśyāma mitrāvaruṇā vayaṁ vāṁ dyāvā ca yatra pīpayann ahā ca ||
  • tā hi devānām asurā: This phrase translates to "they are indeed the Asuras of the gods." In Vedic literature, Asuras are often seen as powerful beings, sometimes in opposition to the Devas (gods). This can be interpreted as a reminder that strength and power can exist in various forms, and one must discern the nature of that power.
  • tāv aryā: This means "they are indeed noble." The term "aryā" refers to those who are honorable and virtuous. This suggests that true nobility comes from the qualities of character and actions rather than mere status or power.
  • tā naḥ kṣitīḥ karatam ūrjayantīḥ: This translates to "may they strengthen our earth." Here, "kṣitīḥ" refers to the earth, and "ūrjayantīḥ" implies the act of providing strength or vitality. This can be seen as a call for the divine to bless the earth with strength, fertility, and abundance, which is essential for human survival and prosperity.
  • aśyāma mitrāvaruṇā: This phrase means "may we be protected by Mitra and Varuna." Mitra and Varuna are deities associated with friendship, order, and cosmic law. This highlights the importance of harmony and balance in our lives, suggesting that we seek protection and guidance from higher powers.
  • vayaṁ vāṁ dyāvā ca yatra pīpayann ahā ca: This translates to "we are here, and the heavens are where we are nourished." This emphasizes the interconnectedness of life and the universe, suggesting that sustenance comes from both the earth and the heavens. It reflects the idea that our well-being is supported by both material and spiritual nourishment.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, nobility, and protection from divine forces while recognizing the interconnectedness of life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways, such as:

  • In personal development, striving for nobility in character and actions can lead to a more fulfilling life.
  • In environmental efforts, the call to strengthen the earth can inspire actions towards sustainability and conservation.
  • In community building, seeking harmony and protection from higher ideals can foster a sense of unity and cooperation among individuals.

This mantra serves as a reminder of the balance between earthly existence and spiritual guidance, encouraging individuals to seek strength and nobility in their lives while being mindful of their connection to the universe.
