समाना ऊर्वे अधि संगतासः सं जानते न यतन्ते मिथस ते |ते देवानां न मिनन्ति व्रतान्य अमर्धन्तो वसुभिर यादमानाः ||
samāna ūrve adhi saṁgatāsaḥ saṁ jānate na yatante mithas te | te devānāṁ na minanti vratāny amardhanto vasubhir yādamānāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samāna: similar, equal
    • ūrve: earth, ground
    • adhi: upon, over
    • saṁgatāsaḥ: those who are united, connected
    • saṁ: together
    • jānate: know, understand
    • na: not
    • yatante: strive, exert
    • mithas: among themselves
    • te: they
    • devānāṁ: of the gods
    • na: not
    • minanti: diminish, reduce
    • vratāny: vows, commitments
    • amardhanto: unbroken, undiminished
    • vasubhir: with the wealth, resources
    • yādamānāḥ: those who are striving, working
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the unity and collective strength of individuals who are connected to the earth and to each other.
    • It emphasizes the importance of understanding and knowledge among those who are united, suggesting that true strength comes from collaboration rather than individual effort.
    • The phrase "na yatante mithas te" indicates that when individuals work together harmoniously, they do not need to struggle against each other.
    • The reference to the gods and their unbroken vows signifies that divine support is available to those who maintain their commitments and work together.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, when team members collaborate effectively, they achieve greater results than when they work in isolation. For instance, a project team that communicates openly and shares responsibilities is more likely to succeed.
    • In community service, groups that come together to address social issues, such as poverty or education, can create a more significant impact than individuals acting alone. For example, a neighborhood clean-up initiative can foster a sense of unity and shared purpose.
    • In sports, teams that function cohesively, understanding each other's strengths and weaknesses, often outperform those with individual star players who do not cooperate.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of unity, collaboration, and shared knowledge in achieving common goals.
    • It serves as a reminder that when individuals come together with a shared purpose, they can overcome challenges and achieve greatness.
    • In real life, this principle can be applied in various contexts, including workplaces, communities, and personal relationships, emphasizing the importance of teamwork and mutual support.
