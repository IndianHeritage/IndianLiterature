अध्वर्यो द्रावयाऽत्वं सोमं इन्द्रः पिपासति |उप नूनं युयुजे वृषणा हरिः आ च जगाम वृत्रहा ||
adhvaryo drāvayā tvaṁ somam indraḥ pipāsati | upa nūnaṁ yuyuje vṛṣaṇā harī ā ca jagāma vṛtrahā ||
  • adhvaryo: This term refers to the priest or the officiant in the Vedic rituals, particularly in the context of the Soma sacrifice. The adhvaryu plays a crucial role in performing the rituals and invoking the deities.
  • drāvayā: This word means "to pour" or "to flow." It signifies the act of offering, particularly the pouring of the Soma juice during the ritual.
  • tvaṁ: This translates to "you," indicating the direct address to the deity or the essence of the Soma.
  • somam: Soma is both a deity and a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. It is often associated with the moon and is believed to bestow health and vitality.
  • indraḥ: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, known for his strength and valor. He is often invoked for protection and victory in battles.
  • pipāsati: This term means "thirsts" or "desires." It indicates a longing or yearning, particularly for the Soma, which is essential for the gods.
  • upa: This means "towards" or "near," suggesting an approach or a call to the deity.
  • nūnaṁ: This word can be translated as "indeed" or "certainly," emphasizing the certainty of the action or the invocation.
  • yuyuje: This means "to unite" or "to join," indicating the act of bringing together the offerings and the divine.
  • vṛṣaṇā: This term refers to the "strong" or "powerful," often associated with the might of Indra.
  • harī: This word means "the one who is yellow" or "the one who is bright," often referring to the sun or the divine light.
  • ā: This is a preposition meaning "to" or "towards," indicating direction or movement.
  • ca: This means "and," connecting the ideas or actions in the mantra.
  • jagāma: This translates to "went" or "arrived," indicating the action of the deity responding to the invocation.
  • vṛtrahā: This term means "the slayer of Vritra," referring to Indra's victory over the serpent Vritra, symbolizing the triumph of order over chaos.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of Vedic rituals, emphasizing the importance of the Soma offering and the invocation of Indra. It highlights the relationship between the divine and the earthly, where the adhvaryu plays a pivotal role in connecting the two realms. In real life, this mantra can be seen as a metaphor for seeking divine guidance and strength in overcoming challenges. Just as Indra thirsts for Soma, individuals can seek inspiration and vitality in their pursuits, whether in personal growth, professional endeavors, or spiritual journeys. The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of rituals in fostering a sense of community, and the pursuit of higher ideals through divine connection.
