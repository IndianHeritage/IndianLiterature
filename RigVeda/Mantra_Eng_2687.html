आयं ते योनिर ऋत्वियो यतो जातो अरोचथाḥ |तं जानन्न अग्न आ सीदाथा नो वर्धयाऽगिरः ||
ayaṁ te yonir ṛtviyo yato jāto arocathāḥ | taṁ jānann agna ā sīdāthā no vardhayā giraḥ ||
  • ayaṁ te yonir ṛtviyo: This phrase can be translated as "This is your source of life, O Agni, the divine fire." Here, "yonir" refers to the source or origin, and "ṛtviyo" signifies the sacred or divine aspects associated with life.
  • yato jāto arocathāḥ: This means "from which you were born and have arisen." It emphasizes the idea of creation and the emergence of life from a divine source.
  • taṁ jānann agna: This translates to "knowing that, O Agni." It indicates a recognition or understanding of the divine fire's significance in life.
  • ā sīdāthā no vardhayā giraḥ: This can be interpreted as "may you sit with us and increase our words." It suggests a desire for Agni's presence to enhance communication, wisdom, and understanding among people.

The mantra as a whole invokes Agni, the fire god, symbolizing the divine spark of life and knowledge. It acknowledges Agni as the source of life and seeks his blessings for wisdom and communication.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or rituals to connect with the divine and seek guidance in their lives.
  • Community Gatherings: In gatherings, invoking Agni can symbolize unity and the sharing of knowledge, fostering a sense of togetherness and understanding among participants.
  • Personal Growth: The mantra encourages individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom, reminding them of the importance of learning and communication in their personal and professional lives.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of Agni as a source of life and wisdom. It highlights the importance of understanding our origins and the divine aspects of existence. By seeking Agni's blessings, individuals can enhance their communication and understanding, fostering personal growth and community harmony. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, knowledge, and the divine, applicable in various aspects of daily living.
