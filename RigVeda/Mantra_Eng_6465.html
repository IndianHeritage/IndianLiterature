स्वधाम् अनु श्रीयं नरो महि तेषा अमवन्तो वृषप्सवः |वाहन्ते अहृतप्सवः ||
svadhām anu śriyaṁ naro mahi tveṣā amavanto vṛṣapsavaḥ | vahante ahrutapsavaḥ ||
  • svadhām anu: This phrase can be interpreted as "following one's own path" or "in accordance with one's own nature." It emphasizes the importance of individuality and self-awareness in one's actions.
  • śriyaṁ: This word translates to "prosperity" or "wealth." It signifies not just material wealth but also spiritual and emotional richness.
  • naro: This term means "man" or "human." It highlights the human experience and the responsibilities that come with it.
  • mahi: This word means "great" or "mighty." It suggests the importance of strength and resilience in the face of challenges.
  • tveṣā: This can be understood as "your essence" or "your nature." It calls for individuals to connect with their true selves.
  • amavanto: This term means "those who are full of life" or "those who are vibrant." It encourages a life filled with energy and enthusiasm.
  • vṛṣapsavaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "those who are powerful" or "those who possess strength." It emphasizes the importance of inner strength and capability.
  • vahante: This means "they carry" or "they bear." It suggests the act of taking responsibility for one's actions and their consequences.
  • ahrutapsavaḥ: This term can be understood as "those who are wise" or "those who possess knowledge." It highlights the value of wisdom in guiding one's life.

In conclusion, this mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of self-awareness, individuality, and the pursuit of prosperity in all aspects of life. It encourages individuals to embrace their true nature, harness their inner strength, and take responsibility for their actions. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership, and community building. For instance, a leader who follows their own path while being aware of their strengths and weaknesses can inspire others to do the same, fostering a culture of empowerment and growth. Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder to live authentically and to strive for a life filled with purpose and vitality.
