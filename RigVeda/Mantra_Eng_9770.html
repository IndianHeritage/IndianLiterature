यं देवासो जनयन्ताग्निं यस्मिन्न आजुहवुर भुवनानि विश्वा |सो अरचिषा पृथिवीं द्यां उतेमां ऋजुयमानो अतपन् महित्वा ||
yaṁ devāso 'janayantāgniṁ yasminn ājuhavur bhuvanāni viśvā | so arciṣā pṛthivīṁ dyām utemām ṛjūyamāno atapan mahitvā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: whom
    • devāso: the gods
    • ajanayant: have created
    • āgniṁ: fire
    • yasmin: in whom
    • ājuhavur: have invoked
    • bhuvanāni: the worlds
    • viśvā: all
    • so: he
    • arciṣā: with light
    • pṛthivīṁ: the earth
    • dyām: the sky
    • utemām: this
    • ṛjūyamāno: straightened
    • atapan: shining
    • mahitvā: with greatness
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the creation of fire by the gods, which is a fundamental element in many cultures.
    • It emphasizes the importance of fire as a source of light and energy, essential for life and civilization.
    • The invocation of fire symbolizes the connection between the divine and the earthly realms, highlighting the role of fire in rituals and daily life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is used in rituals to symbolize purification and transformation, such as in Hindu ceremonies where fire is central to the rituals.
    • Fire is also a source of warmth and cooking, essential for survival, demonstrating its practical significance in daily life.
    • In modern times, fire is harnessed for energy, showcasing its importance in technological advancements and industrial processes.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the divine origin of fire and its vital role in sustaining life and civilization.
    • It reminds us of the interconnectedness of the natural and spiritual worlds, urging us to respect and honor the elements that support our existence.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire us to appreciate the resources we have, use them wisely, and recognize their significance in our lives.
