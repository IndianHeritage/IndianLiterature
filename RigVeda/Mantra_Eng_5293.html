इन्द्रेणैते तृ्त्सवो वेविषाणां आपो न सृष्टा अधवन्त निचीः |दुर्मित्रासः प्रकलविन मिमानां जहुर विश्वानि भोजना सुदासे ||
indreṇaite tṛtsavo veviṣāṇā āpo na sṛṣṭā adhavanta nīcīḥ | durmitrāsaḥ prakalavin mimānā jahur viśvāni bhojanā sudāse ||
  • Indreṇaite: This term refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Tṛtsavo: This word can be interpreted as those who are thirsty or desirous, often representing the human condition of longing for fulfillment.
  • Veviṣāṇā: This term suggests the act of being drawn or attracted, indicating a connection or bond.
  • Āpo: This word means 'waters' and symbolizes nourishment, life, and the essential elements for survival.
  • Na sṛṣṭā: This phrase translates to 'not created,' implying a sense of permanence or the eternal nature of certain truths.
  • Adhavanta nīcīḥ: This can be interpreted as 'those who are lowly or humble,' suggesting a contrast between the powerful and the meek.
  • Durmitrāsaḥ: This term refers to 'difficult friends' or adversaries, highlighting the challenges one may face in relationships.
  • Prakalavin: This word means 'to be wise or knowledgeable,' indicating the importance of wisdom in navigating life's challenges.
  • Mimānā: This term suggests 'to ponder or reflect,' emphasizing the need for contemplation in decision-making.
  • Jahur: This word means 'to give or offer,' indicating the act of sharing or providing for others.
  • Viśvāni: This term translates to 'all' or 'universal,' suggesting inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Bhojanā: This word means 'food' or 'nourishment,' symbolizing the sustenance necessary for life.
  • Sudāse: This term can be interpreted as 'well-fed' or 'prosperous,' indicating abundance and well-being.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of strength, wisdom, and the interconnectedness of all beings in the pursuit of fulfillment and nourishment. It highlights the duality of power and humility, suggesting that true prosperity comes from understanding and reflecting on our relationships with others. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as leadership roles where one must balance authority with empathy, or in community settings where sharing resources and wisdom leads to collective well-being. The mantra serves as a reminder that in our quest for success, we must remain grounded, reflective, and inclusive, fostering a sense of unity and support among all individuals.
