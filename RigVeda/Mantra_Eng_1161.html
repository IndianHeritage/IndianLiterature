ब्रह्मा कृणोति वरुणो गातुविदं तं इमहे |व्य ऊर्णोति हृदा मतीं नव्यो जाताम् ऋतं वित्तं मे अस्य रोदसी ||
brahmā kṛṇoti varuṇo gātuvidaṁ tam īmahe | vy ūrṇoti hṛdā matiṁ navyo jāyatām ṛtaṁ vittam me asya rodasī ||
  • Translation: "Brahma (the creator) creates; Varuna (the god of cosmic order) knows the way. We meditate upon that (Brahman). May the mind be filled with new thoughts, and may the truth and wealth of this world be born in me."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • Brahma: Refers to the ultimate reality or the creator, symbolizing the source of all existence.
    • Kṛṇoti: Means "creates" or "makes," indicating the active role of Brahma in the universe.
    • Varuṇa: Represents the god of cosmic order and moral authority, emphasizing the importance of righteousness.
    • Gātuvidaṁ: Translates to "knows the way," suggesting that Varuna has the knowledge of the path of truth and order.
    • Tam: Means "that," referring to the ultimate reality or Brahman.
    • Īmahe: Means "we meditate," indicating a collective focus on spiritual contemplation.
    • Vy: A prefix that can mean "may" or "let," expressing a wish or desire.
    • Ūrṇoti: Means "to fill" or "to enrich," suggesting the act of filling the mind with positive thoughts.
    • Hṛdā: Means "with the heart," indicating that the meditation should be heartfelt and sincere.
    • Matiṁ: Refers to "thought" or "intellect," emphasizing the importance of mental clarity.
    • Navyo: Means "new," suggesting the generation of fresh ideas and perspectives.
    • Jāyatām: Means "may be born," indicating a desire for new thoughts to emerge.
    • Ṛtaṁ: Refers to "truth" or "cosmic order," highlighting the significance of living in accordance with universal laws.
    • Vittam: Means "wealth," which can refer to both material and spiritual abundance.
    • Me: Means "my," indicating a personal connection to the desires expressed in the mantra.
    • Asya: Means "of this," referring to the world or the universe.
    • Rodasī: Refers to "the heavens" or "the sky," symbolizing the vastness of existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders can embody the principles of Brahma and Varuna by creating a vision (Brahma) and ensuring that their actions align with ethical standards (Varuna). This can lead to a harmonious workplace where creativity and integrity thrive.
    • In personal life, individuals can meditate on this mantra to cultivate new ideas and perspectives, especially when facing challenges. For instance, a student preparing for exams can use this mantra to focus their mind and invite clarity and understanding.
    • In community service, the principles of truth and wealth can be applied by engaging in activities that promote social justice and support for the underprivileged, thereby enriching both the giver and the receiver.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of creation, knowledge, and ethical living. It serves as a reminder to seek truth and wisdom in our thoughts and actions. By meditating on this mantra, individuals can aspire to enrich their minds and lives with new ideas while contributing positively to the world around them. Its significance lies in its applicability across various aspects of life, from personal growth to social responsibility, encouraging a holistic approach to existence.
