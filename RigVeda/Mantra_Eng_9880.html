सम अस्मिञ्जायमान आसत ग्ना उतेम अवर्धन नद्यः स्वगूर्ताः |मह्य यत त्वा पुरूरवो रणायावर्धयन दस्युहत्याय देवाः ||
sam asmiñ jāyamāna āsata gnā utem avardhan nadya1ḥ svagūrtāḥ | mahe yat tvā purūravo raṇāyāvardhayan dasyuhatyāya devāḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "In this birth, we are born together; may we be endowed with knowledge and strength. The rivers, flowing with their own essence, enhance the great one, who is the protector in battles, and the gods support him against the enemies."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • sam asmiñ: "together" or "in this birth" - signifies unity and collective existence.
    • jāyamāna: "born" - refers to the act of birth or coming into existence.
    • āsata: "may we be endowed" - a wish or prayer for blessings.
    • gnā: "knowledge" - represents wisdom and understanding.
    • utem: "strength" - signifies power and resilience.
    • avardhan: "enhance" - to increase or elevate.
    • nadyaḥ: "rivers" - symbolizes the flow of life and resources.
    • svagūrtāḥ: "own essence" - refers to the intrinsic qualities of the rivers.
    • mahe: "great one" - denotes a powerful entity or individual.
    • yat: "who" - refers to the subject of the mantra.
    • tvā: "you" - addressing the great one directly.
    • purūravo: "protector in battles" - signifies a warrior or guardian.
    • raṇāyā: "in battles" - refers to the context of conflict or struggle.
    • avardhayan: "support" - to provide assistance or strength.
    • dasyuhatyāya: "against the enemies" - signifies the opposition faced.
    • devāḥ: "gods" - represents divine forces or higher powers.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, the mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and collective effort. For instance, in a sports team, players must work together, combining their strengths and knowledge to achieve victory.
    • In educational settings, students can relate to the idea of being "born together" in a learning environment, where collaboration enhances their understanding and knowledge.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra can be applied to friendships and partnerships, where mutual support and shared experiences strengthen bonds against challenges.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on unity, knowledge, and strength in the face of challenges. It teaches us that collective effort and support from higher powers can lead to success in various aspects of life, whether in personal relationships, teamwork, or facing adversities. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate their journeys with resilience and purpose, drawing strength from their connections with others and the divine.
