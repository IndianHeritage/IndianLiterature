कथा महे रुद्रियाय ब्रवाम कद राये चिकीतुषे भगाय |आप ओषधीर उता नो 'वन्तु द्यौर वनाः गिरयो वृक्षकेशाः ||
kathā mahe rudriyāya bravāma kad rāye cikituṣe bhagāya | āpa oṣadhīr uta no 'vantu dyaur vanā girayo vṛkṣakeśāḥ ||
  • kathā mahe rudriyāya bravāma: This phrase can be translated as "We speak of the great Rudra." Rudra is a significant deity in the Vedic tradition, often associated with storms, wind, and the hunt. In real life, this can be seen as a call to acknowledge the powerful forces of nature and the divine, much like how we respect and honor the elements in our environment.
  • kad rāye cikituṣe bhagāya: This translates to "When shall we attain the blessings of prosperity?" This line reflects a universal human desire for abundance and well-being. In modern life, this can be related to the pursuit of success in various fields, whether it be in career, relationships, or personal growth.
  • āpa oṣadhīr uta no 'vantu: This means "May the waters and herbs be favorable to us." Here, the emphasis is on the importance of nature and its resources for human survival and prosperity. In contemporary terms, this can be linked to environmental sustainability and the need to protect our natural resources for future generations.
  • dyaur vanā girayo vṛkṣakeśāḥ: This translates to "May the heavens, forests, and mountains protect us." This line highlights the interconnectedness of humans with nature. In real life, it can be seen in the context of conservation efforts, where we strive to protect our environment and the ecosystems that support life on Earth.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing and respecting the forces of nature, seeking prosperity, and fostering a harmonious relationship with the environment. In today's world, it serves as a reminder of our responsibility towards nature and the importance of sustainable living. By acknowledging these elements, we can work towards a balanced existence that honors both our needs and the health of our planet.
