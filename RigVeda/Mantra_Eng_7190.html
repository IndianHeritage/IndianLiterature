पूर्वीश चिद्धि त्वे तुविकूर्मिन्न आशसो हवन्त इन्द्रोतयः |तिरश्चिद आर्यः सवना वसो गहि शविष्ट श्रुधि मे हवम् ||
pūrvīś cid dhi tve tuvikūrminn āśaso havanta indrotayaḥ | tiraś cid aryaḥ savanā vaso gahi śaviṣṭha śrudhi me havam ||
  • pūrvīś cid dhi tve tuvikūrminn āśaso havanta indrotayaḥ
    • pūrvīś: Refers to the "ancient" or "previous," indicating a connection to the past or ancestral wisdom.
    • cid: Means "indeed" or "certainly," emphasizing the truth of the statement.
    • dhi: Translates to "intellect" or "understanding," highlighting the importance of wisdom.
    • tve: Means "you," addressing a divine entity or force.
    • tuvikūrminn: Suggests "in the midst of," indicating a presence or involvement in a situation.
    • āśaso: Refers to "blessings" or "prosperity," indicating a desire for abundance.
    • havanta: Means "those who offer," referring to the act of giving or sacrifice.
    • indrotayaḥ: Refers to "the powers of Indra," invoking the strength and might of the deity Indra.
  • tiraś cid aryaḥ savanā vaso gahi śaviṣṭha śrudhi me havam
    • tiraś: Means "across" or "beyond," suggesting a reach or extension beyond the ordinary.
    • cid: Again emphasizes certainty or truth.
    • aryaḥ: Refers to "noble" or "honorable," indicating a call to those of high moral standing.
    • savanā: Means "sacrifice" or "offering," highlighting the importance of giving in rituals.
    • vaso: Refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating a desire for abundance.
    • gahi: Means "to grasp" or "to hold," suggesting an active engagement with the divine.
    • śaviṣṭha: Refers to "the best" or "the most excellent," indicating a pursuit of the highest quality.
    • śrudhi: Means "listen" or "hear," indicating a call for attention from the divine.
    • me: Means "my," indicating a personal connection to the invocation.
    • havam: Refers to "offering" or "sacrifice," emphasizing the act of giving.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, sacrifice, and the invocation of divine powers for prosperity and abundance. It calls upon the ancient wisdom and the strength of Indra, urging individuals to engage in noble actions and offerings. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, seeking wisdom from past experiences and ancestors to guide current decisions.
  • In community service, engaging in acts of giving and sacrifice for the betterment of society.
  • In spiritual practices, invoking divine energies through rituals and offerings to seek blessings and prosperity.

Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of wisdom, sacrifice, and divine support in achieving a fulfilling and prosperous life.
