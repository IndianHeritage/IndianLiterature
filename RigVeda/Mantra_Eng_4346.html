प्र सम्राजे बृहद् आर्चा गभीरम् ब्रह्म प्रियं वरुणाय श्रुताय |विओ जगहान शमितेव चर्मोपस्तिरे पृथिवीं सूर्याय ||
pra samrāje bṛhad arcā gabhīram brahma priyaṁ varuṇāya śrutāya | vi yo jaghāna śamiteva carmopastire pṛthivīṁ sūryāya ||
  • pra samrāje: This phrase can be interpreted as "in the great governance" or "in the vast rule." It signifies the importance of leadership and authority in maintaining order and harmony in society.
  • bṛhad arcā: Translated as "great worship" or "vast reverence," this emphasizes the significance of devotion and respect towards the divine and the natural order.
  • gabhīram: Meaning "deep" or "profound," this word suggests the depth of understanding and wisdom that is required in governance and spirituality.
  • brahma priyaṁ: This translates to "beloved of the ultimate reality" or "dear to Brahman," indicating the connection between the divine and the principles of governance.
  • varuṇāya: Referring to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water, this highlights the importance of natural elements and their role in sustaining life and order.
  • śrutāya: Meaning "to the one who hears," this suggests the importance of listening and understanding in leadership and spiritual practices.
  • vi yo jaghāna: This can be interpreted as "who has conquered" or "who has overcome," indicating the triumph over challenges and adversities.
  • śamiteva: Translated as "like a calm" or "peaceful," this emphasizes the need for tranquility and peace in governance and personal life.
  • carmopastire: Meaning "to the one who is covered," this suggests the idea of protection and support, akin to how a mother nurtures her child.
  • pṛthivīṁ: This word means "the earth," symbolizing the physical realm and the importance of caring for our environment.
  • sūryāya: Referring to the sun, this signifies light, knowledge, and the source of life, emphasizing the need for enlightenment in our actions and thoughts.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, spirituality, and the interconnectedness of nature and humanity. It teaches us that effective governance requires deep understanding, respect for the divine, and a commitment to peace and protection. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as community leadership, environmental stewardship, and personal development. By embodying these principles, individuals can contribute to a harmonious society that honors both the divine and the natural world.
