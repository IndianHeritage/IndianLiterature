श्रोनाम एक उदकम् गाम अवाजति मांसम् एकः पिंशति सूनयाभृतम् |आ निम्रुचः शकृत एको अपाभरत किम् स्वित पुत्रेभ्यः पितराः उपावतुः ||
śroṇām eka udakaṁ gām avājati māṁsam ekaḥ piṁśati sūnayābhṛtam | ā nimrucaḥ śakṛd eko apābharat kiṁ svit putrebhyaḥ pitarā upāvatuḥ ||
  • śroṇām eka udakaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "one water from the streams." It signifies the unity and interconnectedness of all living beings, much like how water flows and nourishes various forms of life.
  • gām avājati: This means "the earth is nourished." It reflects the idea that the earth provides sustenance and support to all creatures, emphasizing the importance of nature in our lives.
  • māṁsam ekaḥ piṁśati: Translated as "one flesh is molded," this suggests the idea of creation and transformation. It can be seen as a metaphor for how individuals contribute to the larger community, shaping and being shaped by their experiences.
  • sūnayābhṛtam: This phrase means "with the strength of the sun." It symbolizes vitality, energy, and the life-giving force of the sun, which is essential for growth and sustenance.
  • ā nimrucaḥ: This translates to "the one who is not diminished." It signifies resilience and the enduring nature of life, suggesting that despite challenges, life continues to thrive.
  • śakṛd eko apābharat: This means "once, one has taken." It can be interpreted as the importance of making choices and taking actions that impact our lives and the lives of others.
  • kiṁ svit putrebhyaḥ: Translated as "what indeed for the sons," this phrase raises questions about legacy and the responsibilities we hold towards future generations.
  • pitarā upāvatuḥ: This means "may the ancestors protect." It emphasizes the connection to our roots and the guidance we receive from those who came before us, highlighting the importance of honoring our heritage.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates profound themes of unity, nourishment, resilience, and legacy. In real life, it serves as a reminder of our interconnectedness with nature and each other. It encourages us to make conscious choices that not only benefit ourselves but also contribute positively to our communities and the environment. By honoring our ancestors and recognizing our responsibilities towards future generations, we can create a more harmonious and sustainable world. This mantra is applicable in various scenarios, such as environmental conservation efforts, community building, and personal growth, reminding us of the importance of nurturing both our physical and spiritual well-being.
