अध प्रियम इषिराय षष्टिं सहस्रासनम् |अश्वानाम् इन् न वृष्णाम् ||
adha priyam iṣirāya ṣaṣṭiṁ sahasrāsanam | aśvānām in na vṛṣṇām ||
  • Adha: This word translates to "down" or "below," indicating a sense of humility or grounding.
  • Priyam: Meaning "beloved" or "dear," it signifies something that is cherished or valued.
  • Iṣirāya: This term refers to "the one who is skilled" or "the one who has power," often associated with a person of authority or capability.
  • Ṣaṣṭiṁ: This word means "sixty," which can symbolize completeness or a significant number in various contexts.
  • Sahasrāsanam: This translates to "thousand seats" or "thousand thrones," suggesting abundance, authority, or a place of honor.
  • Aśvānām: Meaning "of the horses," it can symbolize strength, speed, and vitality, often associated with warriors or noble beings.
  • In: This word is a conjunction that connects ideas, often translated as "and."
  • Na: This means "not," indicating negation or absence.
  • Vṛṣṇām: Referring to "of the Vṛṣṇis," a clan or lineage known for their valor and strength, often associated with Lord Krishna.

  • Meaning of the Mantra: The mantra can be interpreted as a call for humility and respect towards those who are skilled and powerful, while also acknowledging the abundance and strength represented by the horses and the Vṛṣṇi lineage. It emphasizes the importance of valuing relationships and the qualities of leadership.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, a leader who values their team and acknowledges their skills fosters a positive environment. This aligns with the idea of respecting those who are capable and skilled.
    • In sports, a coach who recognizes the strengths of each player and encourages teamwork embodies the essence of this mantra, promoting unity and respect.
    • In personal relationships, valuing and cherishing loved ones, while also recognizing their strengths, can lead to deeper connections and mutual respect.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for humility, respect, and recognition of strength and capability in others. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as leadership, teamwork, and personal relationships, where valuing and acknowledging the contributions of others can lead to harmony and success. By embodying these principles, individuals can create a more supportive and thriving environment in their personal and professional lives.
