उपस्थाय मातरम् अन्नम् ऐट्ट तिग्मम् अपश्यद अभि सोमम् ऊधः |प्रयावयन् अचरद गृत्सो अन्यान महानि चक्रे पुरुधप्रतीकः ||
upasthāya mātaram annam aiṭṭa tigmam apaśyad abhi somam ūdhaḥ | prayāvayann acarad gṛtso anyān mahāni cakre purudhapratīkaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • upasthāya: Approaching or coming near.
    • mātaram: Mother.
    • annam: Food or nourishment.
    • aiṭṭa: To be made or prepared.
    • tigmam: Sharp or intense.
    • apaśyad: Saw or perceived.
    • abhi: Towards or in the direction of.
    • somam: Soma, a sacred drink or essence.
    • ūdhaḥ: Uplifted or raised.
    • prayāvayann: Causing to flow or to move.
    • acarad: Moved or acted.
    • gṛtso: Ghee or clarified butter.
    • anyān: Other or different.
    • mahāni: Great or significant.
    • cakre: Created or established.
    • purudhapratīkaḥ: Many forms or manifestations.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of approaching the mother, which symbolizes the Earth or nature, and recognizing the importance of food as nourishment.
    • The mention of "sharp" food could refer to the intensity of flavors or the vitality that food brings to life.
    • The act of perceiving the sacred drink, Soma, signifies the importance of spiritual nourishment alongside physical sustenance.
    • It emphasizes the interconnectedness of actions, where the movement of ghee (a symbol of purity and richness) signifies the flow of life and energy.
    • The creation of "many forms" suggests the diversity of life and experiences that arise from the nourishment provided by nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern society, the mantra can be related to the importance of sustainable agriculture, where we honor the Earth (mother) by consuming locally sourced and organic foods.
    • The concept of "Soma" can be likened to the importance of mindfulness and spiritual practices that nourish our inner selves, such as meditation or yoga.
    • In culinary practices, the use of spices and herbs can be seen as a way to enhance the "sharpness" and vitality of food, promoting health and well-being.
    • The idea of creating "many forms" can be reflected in the diversity of cuisines around the world, showcasing how different cultures interpret nourishment.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of recognizing and honoring the sources of our nourishment, both physical and spiritual.
    • It encourages a holistic approach to life, where we appreciate the interconnectedness of all beings and the environment.
    • In real life, this can manifest in our dietary choices, our respect for nature, and our pursuit of spiritual growth, ultimately leading to a more balanced and fulfilling existence.
