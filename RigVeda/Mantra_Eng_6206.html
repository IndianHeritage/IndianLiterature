अनु तृतस्य युध्यतः शुष्मं आवन्न उता क्रतुम् |अन्व इन्द्रं वृततूर्ये ||
anu tritasya yudhyataḥ śuṣmam āvann uta kratum | anv indraṁ vṛtratūrye ||
  • anu tritasya: This phrase can be translated as "following the third" or "after the third." In a broader context, it may refer to the idea of progression or moving forward after a certain point.
  • yudhyataḥ: This means "in battle" or "in conflict." It signifies the act of engaging in a struggle or contest, which can be both physical and metaphorical.
  • śuṣmam: This word translates to "dryness" or "drought." It can symbolize a lack of vitality or energy, representing challenges or obstacles that one may face.
  • āvann: This term means "having come" or "arrived." It indicates the presence or manifestation of something, often in a significant or impactful way.
  • uta: This word can be understood as "or" or "and," serving as a conjunction that connects ideas or concepts.
  • kratum: This translates to "intention" or "resolve." It reflects the mental determination and purpose behind actions, emphasizing the importance of willpower.
  • anv indraṁ: This phrase means "following Indra," where Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength, power, and leadership.
  • vṛtratūrye: This term refers to "the slayer of Vritra," where Vritra is a dragon or serpent symbolizing chaos and obstacles. Indra's victory over Vritra represents the triumph of order over disorder.

The mantra encapsulates a journey of overcoming challenges through determination and strength. It emphasizes the importance of intention and the need to engage in struggles to achieve victory. The reference to Indra highlights the qualities of leadership and courage required to face adversities.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties in life, such as health issues or personal loss, one can draw inspiration from this mantra to confront and overcome these challenges with resolve.
  • Professional Struggles: In the workplace, individuals may encounter competition or obstacles in their career paths. The mantra encourages them to engage in their "battles" with determination and a clear intention to succeed.
  • Social Issues: On a broader scale, communities facing social injustices can find motivation in this mantra to unite and fight against oppression, embodying the spirit of Indra in their quest for justice.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of courage, intention, and the willingness to engage in struggles to achieve success. It encourages individuals to confront their challenges head-on, drawing strength from the qualities exemplified by Indra, ultimately leading to personal and collective victories.
