मरत्स्तोत्रस्य वृजनस्य गोपा वयं इन्द्रेण सनुयाम वाजम् |तन नो मित्रो वरुणो मामहन्ताम् अदितिः सिन्धुः पृथिवी उता द्यौः ||
marutstotrasya vṛjanasya gopā vayam indreṇa sanuyāma vājam | tan no mitro varuṇo māmahantām aditiḥ sindhuḥ pṛthivī uta dyauḥ ||
  • Mantra Breakdown:
    • Marutstotrasya: Refers to the praise or hymns dedicated to the Maruts, the storm deities associated with wind and storms.
    • Vṛjanasya: This term can be interpreted as "of the one who removes obstacles" or "the one who is free from sin."
    • Gopā: Means "the protector" or "the one who looks after," often associated with the divine guardianship of nature.
    • Vayam: Translates to "we" or "us," indicating a collective identity or community.
    • Indreṇa: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods, known for his strength and power, especially in battles.
    • Sanuyāma: Means "with control" or "with restraint," suggesting the importance of discipline.
    • Vājam: Refers to "the strength" or "the power," often associated with resources or wealth.
    • Tan: Means "that" or "this," indicating a focus on the subject of the mantra.
    • No: Translates to "our," indicating possession or belonging.
    • Mitro: Refers to Mitra, the god of friendship and harmony.
    • Varuṇo: Refers to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water.
    • Māmahantām: Means "the great one" or "the supreme," indicating reverence.
    • Aditiḥ: Refers to Aditi, the mother of the gods, symbolizing infinity and the cosmos.
    • Sindhuḥ: Means "ocean" or "river," representing the flow of life and resources.
    • Pṛthivī: Refers to the earth, symbolizing stability and nourishment.
    • Uta: Means "and" or "also," connecting different elements.
    • Dyauḥ: Refers to the sky or heaven, representing the divine realm.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, the divine, and humanity.
    • It calls upon various deities for protection, strength, and harmony, highlighting the importance of community and cooperation.
    • The invocation of natural elements like the ocean, earth, and sky signifies the need for balance and respect for the environment.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can inspire individuals and communities to work together for environmental conservation, recognizing the divine in nature.
    • It encourages the practice of discipline and control in personal and communal endeavors, fostering a sense of responsibility.
    • The invocation of harmony and friendship can be applied in conflict resolution and building strong relationships in society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of unity, respect for nature, and the divine forces that guide our lives.
    • By embodying the principles of cooperation, strength, and harmony, individuals can contribute to a more balanced and peaceful world.
