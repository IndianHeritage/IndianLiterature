स्मदभीशू कशावन्ता विप्रā नविष्टहया मती |महो वाजिनाव अर्वन्ता सच्चासनम् ||
smadabhīśū kaśāvantā viprā naviṣṭhayā matī | maho vājināv arvantā sacāsanam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • smadabhīśū: Those who are endowed with wisdom and knowledge.
    • kaśāvantā: Those who are rich or prosperous.
    • viprā: The learned or the wise ones, often referring to the priests or scholars.
    • naviṣṭhayā: With a focused or determined mind.
    • matī: Intellect or understanding.
    • maho: Great or powerful.
    • vājināv: Those who are swift or strong, often referring to horses or chariots.
    • arvantā: Those who are capable or able.
    • sacāsanam: With a firm or stable seat or position.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in achieving prosperity and strength.
    • It suggests that those who are learned and focused in their thoughts are the ones who attain greatness.
    • The reference to "vājināv" indicates that strength and capability are essential for success, much like a strong horse is vital for a chariot's speed and efficiency.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team that is knowledgeable and focused on their goals is more likely to succeed than one that lacks direction.
    • In education, students who apply themselves and seek knowledge tend to achieve better results and opportunities in life.
    • In sports, athletes who train diligently and maintain a strong mindset often outperform their competitors.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom, focus, and strength in achieving success and prosperity.
    • It is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal development, education, and professional endeavors.
    • By embodying the qualities of the wise and capable, individuals can navigate challenges and reach their goals effectively.
