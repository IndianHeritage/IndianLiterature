समिध्यमानो अमृतस्य राजसी हविष कृत्वन्तं सचसे स्वस्तये |विश्वं स धत्ते द्रविणं यं इन्द्वस्य आतिथ्यम अग्नि नि च धत्त इत पुरः ||
samidhyamāno amṛtasya rājasi haviṣ kṛṇvantaṁ sacase svastaye | viśvaṁ sa dhatte draviṇaṁ yam invasy ātithyam agne ni ca dhatta it puraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samidhyamāno: kindled or ignited
    • amṛtasya: of immortality or eternal
    • rājasi: in the royal or noble
    • haviṣ: offerings or oblations
    • kṛṇvantaṁ: making or creating
    • sacase: with strength or power
    • svastaye: for well-being or prosperity
    • viśvaṁ: the whole or universe
    • sa: he or that
    • dhatte: supports or holds
    • draviṇaṁ: wealth or resources
    • yam: whom or which
    • invasy: inviting or welcoming
    • ātithyam: hospitality or guest-friendship
    • agne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • ni: and
    • ca: also
    • dhatta: bestow or grant
    • it: here or in this place
    • puraḥ: in front or before
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Agni, the fire god, symbolizing the divine presence in rituals and offerings.
    • It emphasizes the importance of creating offerings that lead to prosperity and well-being.
    • The phrase "amṛtasya rājasi" suggests that these offerings are not just material but are connected to the eternal and the divine.
    • It highlights the significance of hospitality and the welcoming of guests, which is a vital aspect of many cultures.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, lighting a fire during a ceremony symbolizes the presence of the divine and the connection between the earthly and the spiritual.
    • In business, creating a welcoming environment for clients and partners can lead to fruitful relationships, akin to the hospitality mentioned in the mantra.
    • Communities often come together to celebrate festivals with offerings, reflecting the idea of collective prosperity and well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of offerings, hospitality, and the pursuit of prosperity through divine connection.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal rituals to business practices, emphasizing the need for respect, generosity, and the nurturing of relationships.
    • By embodying these principles, individuals and communities can foster a sense of unity and abundance, aligning with the eternal values represented in the mantra.
