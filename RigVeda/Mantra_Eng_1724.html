युक्ता मातासीद धुरी दक्षिणायाऽतिष्ठद गर्भो वृजनिष्व अन्तः |अमीमेद वत्सो अनु गाम अपश्यद विश्वरूप्यं त्रिषु योजनेषु ||
yuktā mātāsīd dhuri dakṣiṇāyā atiṣṭhad garbho vṛjanīṣv antaḥ | amīmed vatso anu gām apaśyad viśvarūpyaṁ triṣu yojaneṣu ||
  • yuktā mātāsīd: This phrase translates to "the mother was wise" or "the mother was intelligent." In real life, this can be seen in the nurturing and protective roles that mothers play in families, making decisions that benefit their children and guiding them through life.
  • dhuri dakṣiṇāyā atiṣṭhad: This means "she stood firmly in the southern direction." The south is often associated with stability and grounding in various cultures. For example, in many traditions, the south is considered a direction of strength and support, akin to the role of a mother in providing a stable environment for her children.
  • garbho vṛjanīṣv antaḥ: This translates to "the embryo was in the womb." This highlights the importance of the gestation period and the nurturing that occurs before birth. In real life, this can be related to prenatal care and the significance of a healthy environment for the development of a child.
  • amīmed vatso anu gām apaśyad: This means "the calves (or young ones) followed the path." This can be interpreted as the younger generation following the guidance of their elders. In modern society, this is evident in how children learn from their parents and mentors, adopting values and behaviors that shape their future.
  • viśvarūpyaṁ triṣu yojaneṣu: This translates to "the universal form in three realms." This can be understood as the interconnectedness of all beings and the universe. In real life, this is reflected in the idea of community and the importance of recognizing our place within a larger system, whether it be social, environmental, or spiritual.

  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the wisdom and nurturing qualities of motherhood, the importance of stability, and the interconnectedness of life. It serves as a reminder of the roles we play in guiding the younger generation and the significance of a supportive environment for growth. In real life, this mantra can be applied in family dynamics, educational settings, and community building, encouraging us to foster wisdom, stability, and connection in our relationships and society.
