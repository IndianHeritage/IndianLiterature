आ नो यज्ञं दिविष्पृशं वायो याहि सुमन्मभिः |अन्तः पवित्र उपरी श्रीणानोऽयं शुक्रो आयामि ते ||
ā no yajñaṁ divispṛśaṁ vāyo yāhi sumanmabhiḥ | antaḥ pavitra upari śrīṇāno3 'yaṁ śukro ayāmi te ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: O (a call or invocation)
    • no: our
    • yajñaṁ: sacrifice or offering
    • divispṛśaṁ: that which touches the heavens
    • vāyo: O Wind
    • yāhi: come
    • sumanmabhiḥ: with good thoughts or auspicious intentions
    • antaḥ: within
    • pavitra: pure
    • upari: above
    • śrīṇāno: we are adorned or blessed
    • yaṁ: that
    • śukro: bright or pure
    • ayāmi: I approach or I come to
    • te: you
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer invoking the divine forces, particularly the Wind, to come and bless our offerings.
    • The term "yajña" signifies not just a ritual sacrifice but also the spirit of giving and selflessness in our actions.
    • By calling upon the Wind, which is often seen as a messenger of the gods, the mantra emphasizes the connection between the earthly and the divine.
    • The phrase "with good thoughts" highlights the importance of intention behind our actions, suggesting that purity of mind is essential for spiritual progress.
    • The concluding lines express a desire to approach the divine with purity and blessings, reinforcing the idea of striving for a higher state of being.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied during rituals or ceremonies where offerings are made, such as weddings or festivals, to invoke blessings for the participants.
    • In a corporate setting, the principles of "yajña" can be reflected in ethical business practices, where companies prioritize social responsibility and community welfare.
    • On a personal level, individuals can embody the essence of this mantra by engaging in acts of kindness and service, ensuring that their intentions are pure and selfless.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for purity, intention, and connection with the divine.
    • It serves as a reminder that our actions, when performed with good thoughts and a pure heart, can lead to spiritual elevation and harmony in our lives.
    • In essence, it encourages us to approach life with a sense of reverence and responsibility, fostering a deeper connection with ourselves, others, and the universe.
