रेवां इद रेवतः स्तोतā स्यात् त्वावतो मघोनः |प्रेदु हरिवः श्रुतस्य ||
revām̐ id revataḥ stotā syāt tvāvato maghonaḥ | pred u harivaḥ śrutasya ||
  • Mantra: "revām̐ id revataḥ stotā syāt tvāvato maghonaḥ | pred u harivaḥ śrutasya ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "May the one who is praised (stotā) by the powerful (revataḥ) be the source of our strength (maghonaḥ) and may we be blessed with the knowledge (śrutasya) that leads us to prosperity (harivaḥ)."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • Revām̐: Refers to the act of praising or honoring.
    • Id: This word signifies 'this' or 'here'.
    • Revataḥ: Denotes the powerful or the strong, often referring to divine forces or deities.
    • Stotā: Means 'the one who is praised' or 'the praiser'.
    • Syāt: Translates to 'may be' or 'let it be'.
    • Tvāvato: Refers to 'your' or 'of you', indicating a personal connection.
    • Maghonaḥ: Means 'of strength' or 'of power'.
    • Pred: Can be interpreted as 'blessed' or 'granted'.
    • U: Means 'and'.
    • Harivaḥ: Refers to 'prosperity' or 'wealth'.
    • Śrutasya: Means 'of knowledge' or 'of hearing', often associated with wisdom.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied when a team acknowledges the contributions of a leader (the one who is praised) who empowers them (the powerful) to achieve their goals (strength).
    • In personal life, one might recite this mantra during a family gathering to express gratitude towards a family member who provides wisdom and support, thus fostering a sense of unity and prosperity.
    • In educational contexts, teachers can embody the essence of this mantra by recognizing and praising students (the praisers) who contribute positively to the learning environment, thereby encouraging a culture of respect and knowledge sharing.

  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on the power of praise and recognition. It highlights the importance of acknowledging those who contribute to our strength and prosperity. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal relationships, workplaces, or educational institutions, the act of praising and recognizing others fosters a positive environment that encourages growth, collaboration, and success. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a culture of appreciation that leads to collective well-being and prosperity.
