नū न एहि वारीयम् अग्ने गृणान आ भर |ये वयं ये च सूरयः स्वस्ति धामहे सचोतैधि पृथ्सु नो वृधे ||
nū na ehi vāryam agne gṛṇāna ā bhara | ye vayaṁ ye ca sūrayaḥ svasti dhāmahe sacotaidhi pṛtsu no vṛdhe ||
  • nū na ehi vāryam agne gṛṇāna ā bhara: This line can be translated to "O Agni, do not come to us with anger; we invoke you with our praises." Here, Agni represents the divine fire, a symbol of purity and transformation. In real life, this can be likened to how we approach challenges or conflicts. Instead of confronting them with hostility, we should approach them with respect and positivity.
  • ye vayaṁ ye ca sūrayaḥ: This translates to "We, and those who are strong." This line emphasizes the importance of community and strength in unity. In a real-life context, this can be seen in teamwork, where individuals come together, each contributing their strengths to achieve a common goal, much like a sports team working together to win a match.
  • svasti dhāmahe sacotaidhi pṛtsu no vṛdhe: This can be interpreted as "May we attain well-being and prosperity; may we grow in strength." This line highlights the aspiration for growth and well-being, both individually and collectively. In everyday life, this can be reflected in personal development, such as pursuing education or skills that enhance one's life and contribute positively to society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of approaching life with respect, unity, and a desire for growth. Its significance lies in the reminder to invoke positivity in our actions and relationships, fostering a sense of community and shared strength. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as in workplaces, families, and social groups, where collaboration and mutual respect lead to collective success and well-being.
