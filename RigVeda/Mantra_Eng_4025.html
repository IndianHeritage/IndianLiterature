सूक्तैर्भो वाचोभिर देवजुष्टैर इन्द्रा nv अग्नि अवसे हुवध्यै | उक्तैर्भि ष्मा कवयः सुयज्ञा आविवासन्तो मरुतो यजन्ति ||
sūktebhir vo vacobhir devajuṣṭair indrā nv a1gnī avase huvadhyai | ukthebhir hi ṣmā kavayaḥ suyajñā āvivāsanto maruto yajanti ||
  • Mantra: "sūktebhir vo vacobhir devajuṣṭair indrā nv a1gnī avase huvadhyai | ukthebhir hi ṣmā kavayaḥ suyajñā āvivāsanto maruto yajanti ||"
  • Translation: "With hymns and words, we invoke you, O Indra, and Agni, who are nourished by the divine. The wise poets, through their utterances, call upon you, O Maruts, to come forth and partake in the offerings."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • sūktebhir: With hymns or praises
    • vo: To you
    • vacobhir: With words
    • devajuṣṭair: Nourished by the divine
    • indrā: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • nv: Indeed
    • agnī: O Agni (the fire god)
    • avase: We invoke
    • huvadhyai: To partake or accept
    • ukthebhir: Through utterances
    • hi: Indeed
    • ṣmā: The wise
    • kavayaḥ: Poets or seers
    • suyajñā: Well-performed sacrifices
    • āvivāsanto: Coming forth
    • maruto: O Maruts (storm deities)
    • yajanti: They worship or offer
  • Context and Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine forces through hymns and words, highlighting the role of language and expression in spiritual practices.
    • Indra and Agni are central figures in Vedic rituals, representing strength and purity, respectively. Their invocation signifies the need for divine support in human endeavors.
    • The mention of the Maruts indicates the connection between natural forces and divine blessings, suggesting that harmony with nature is essential for successful offerings.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the power of words and intentions. Just as the Vedic seers invoked the gods, individuals today can use positive affirmations and prayers to manifest their desires.
    • The idea of community and collective effort in worship can be applied to teamwork in various fields, where collaboration and shared goals lead to greater success.
    • In environmental contexts, recognizing the divine in nature and invoking its protection can inspire sustainable practices and respect for the earth.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a timeless reminder of the significance of invoking higher powers through sincere expression and collective effort. It encourages individuals to recognize the interconnectedness of their actions with the divine and the natural world, fostering a sense of responsibility and reverence in everyday life.
