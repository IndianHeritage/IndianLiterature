मन्त्र: स्तुहि श्रुतं गर्तसदं युवानं मृगं न भीमम् उपहत्नुम उग्रं |मृळा जरीत्रे रुद्र स्तवाणोऽन्यं ते अस्मन् नि वपन्तु सेनाः ||
stuhi śrutaṁ gartasadaṁ yuvānam mṛgaṁ na bhīmam upahatnum ugram | mṛḻā jaritre rudra stavāno 'nyaṁ te asman ni vapantu senāḥ ||
  • stuhi: This word translates to "praise" or "worship." It signifies the act of honoring or acknowledging the divine.
  • śrutaṁ: Meaning "heard" or "known," this term emphasizes the importance of knowledge and wisdom that comes from listening and understanding.
  • gartasadaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the one who resides in the depths" or "the one who is hidden." It suggests a connection to the deeper, often unseen aspects of existence.
  • yuvānam: Translated as "young" or "youthful," this word symbolizes vitality, energy, and the potential for growth and renewal.
  • mṛgaṁ: This means "deer" or "animal," often representing grace, agility, and the natural world.
  • na bhīmam: This phrase means "not fearful" or "without fear," indicating a state of courage and confidence.
  • upahatnum: This translates to "to overcome" or "to conquer," suggesting the ability to rise above challenges.
  • ugram: Meaning "fierce" or "powerful," this word denotes strength and intensity.
  • mṛḻā: This term can mean "gentle" or "calm," indicating a balance between strength and gentleness.
  • jaritre: This refers to "the one who brings forth" or "the creator," highlighting the role of creation and nurturing in life.
  • rudra: A name for a deity often associated with storms and the hunt, representing both destruction and regeneration.
  • stavāno: This means "one who is praised" or "the praiseworthy," emphasizing the importance of reverence in spiritual practice.
  • 'nyaṁ: This translates to "other" or "different," suggesting diversity and the multiplicity of existence.
  • te: Meaning "you," this word addresses the divine or the higher power being invoked.
  • asman: This means "us" or "our," indicating a collective identity or community.
  • ni vapantu: This phrase translates to "may they protect" or "may they guard," invoking a sense of safety and support.
  • senāḥ: Meaning "armies" or "forces," this term symbolizes strength in numbers and the power of unity.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of praise, knowledge, and the balance between strength and gentleness. It encourages individuals to overcome fear and challenges while recognizing the divine presence in their lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to personal growth, community support, and the pursuit of wisdom. It serves as a reminder to honor both the fierce and gentle aspects of existence, fostering resilience and unity in the face of adversity.
