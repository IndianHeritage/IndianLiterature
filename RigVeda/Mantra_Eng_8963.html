इमे जीवाः विमृतैः आववृत्रन्न अभूद् भद्राः देवहूतिः नो अद्य | प्राञ्चो अगाम नृतये हसाया द्राघीय आयुः प्रतरं दधानाः ||
ime jīvā vi mṛtair āvavṛtrann abhūd bhadrā devahūtir no adya | prāñco agāma nṛtaye hasāya drāghīya āyuḥ prataraṁ dadhānāḥ ||
  • ime jīvā vi mṛtair āvavṛtrann abhūd bhadrā devahūtir no adya
    • ime jīvā - "These living beings" refers to all forms of life, emphasizing the interconnectedness of existence.
    • vi mṛtair - "from the dead" suggests a transition or transformation, indicating that life emerges from death, a cycle seen in nature.
    • āvavṛtrann - "have come together" signifies unity and harmony among living beings.
    • abhūd bhadrā - "may there be auspiciousness" expresses a desire for well-being and positive outcomes.
    • devahūtir no adya - "may the divine blessing be upon us today" invokes divine support and guidance in our endeavors.
  • prāñco agāma nṛtaye hasāya
    • prāñco - "with life" emphasizes the importance of vitality and energy in our actions.
    • agāma - "that has come" indicates the arrival of new opportunities or experiences.
    • nṛtaye - "for the dance" symbolizes the rhythm of life, where each individual plays a role in the larger cosmic dance.
    • hasāya - "with joy" highlights the importance of joy and positivity in our lives.
  • drāghīya āyuḥ prataraṁ dadhānāḥ
    • drāghīya - "longer" refers to the desire for longevity and a fulfilling life.
    • āyuḥ - "life" signifies the essence of existence and the importance of nurturing it.
    • prataraṁ - "to cross over" suggests overcoming challenges and obstacles.
    • dadhānāḥ - "holding" indicates the responsibility we have in shaping our lives and destinies.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of life, transformation, and the pursuit of auspiciousness. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of joy and vitality in our actions. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, recognizing that challenges (death) can lead to growth (life).
  • In community building, fostering unity and support among individuals for collective well-being.
  • In health and wellness, focusing on joy and vitality to enhance the quality of life.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to embrace life with positivity, seek divine guidance, and strive for longevity and fulfillment in our journeys.
