शृण्वन्तु स्तोमं मरुतः सुदानवो 'ग्निजिह्वा ऋतावृद्धः |पिबतु सोमं वरुणो धृतव्रतो 'श्विभ्यां उषसां सजूः ||
śṛṇvantu stomam marutaḥ sudānavo 'gnijihvā ṛtāvṛdhaḥ | pibatu somaṁ varuṇo dhṛtavrato 'śvibhyām uṣasā sajūḥ ||
  • śṛṇvantu stomam marutaḥ: This phrase translates to "Let the Maruts (the storm deities) listen to the hymn." The Maruts are often associated with the forces of nature, particularly storms and winds. In real life, this can be seen as a call to the natural elements to pay attention to human prayers and offerings.
  • sudānavo 'gnijihvā: This means "the well-fed, with fiery tongues." Here, 'sudānavo' refers to those who are nourished or well-fed, while 'agnijihvā' suggests a connection to fire or energy. In a practical sense, this can symbolize the importance of sustenance and vitality in our lives, emphasizing that nourishment fuels our actions and intentions.
  • ṛtāvṛdhaḥ: This term means "those who increase the order of truth." 'Rta' refers to cosmic order or truth, and 'vṛdha' means to increase or enhance. This can be related to the idea of living in harmony with the universe and promoting truth and righteousness in our daily lives.
  • pibatu somaṁ varuṇo: This translates to "Let Varuna drink the soma." Varuna is the god of cosmic order and water, and soma is a sacred drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight. In real life, this can be interpreted as a desire for divine blessings and wisdom, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and spiritual nourishment.
  • dhṛtavrato 'śvibhyām: This means "with steadfast vows, for the horses." 'Dhṛtavrato' refers to those who are committed to their vows or promises, while 'śvibhyām' relates to horses, which symbolize strength and speed. This can be seen in the context of dedication and commitment in our pursuits, whether in personal goals or professional endeavors.
  • uṣasā sajūḥ: This translates to "may the dawns be auspicious." 'Uṣasā' refers to the dawn or new beginnings, and 'sajūḥ' implies auspiciousness or positivity. This can be related to the idea of new opportunities and the hope that each day brings, encouraging individuals to embrace new beginnings with optimism.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, nourishment, truth, divine blessings, commitment, and new beginnings. Its significance lies in the recognition of the forces that govern our lives and the importance of aligning ourselves with these forces through prayer, dedication, and a commitment to truth. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek harmony with nature, pursue knowledge, uphold their commitments, and embrace each new day with hope and positivity. It serves as a reminder of the spiritual and practical aspects of life, encouraging a balanced approach to existence.
