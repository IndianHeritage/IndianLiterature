स्तविष्यामि त्वां अहं विश्वस्यामृत भोजना | अग्ने त्रातारम अमृतं मियेद्ध्य यजिष्ठं हव्यवाहन ||
staviṣyāmi tvām ahaṁ viśvasyāmṛta bhojana | agne trātāram amṛtam miyedhya yajiṣṭhaṁ havyavāhana ||
  • staviṣyāmi: This word translates to "I will praise" or "I will extol." It signifies the act of offering reverence and admiration.
  • tvām: This means "you," referring to the deity being addressed, in this case, Agni, the fire god.
  • ahaṁ: This translates to "I," indicating the speaker's personal connection and commitment.
  • viśvasyāmṛta: This compound word can be broken down into "viśva" (universal) and "amṛta" (immortal). Together, it suggests the idea of universal immortality or the essence of life that transcends death.
  • bhojana: This means "food" or "nourishment," indicating the sustenance that is essential for life.
  • agne: This is a direct address to Agni, the fire god, who is often seen as a mediator between humans and the divine.
  • trātāram: This translates to "protector" or "savior," emphasizing Agni's role in safeguarding and guiding humanity.
  • amṛtam: Again, this refers to "immortal" or "eternal," reinforcing the theme of life beyond physical existence.
  • miyedhya: This can be interpreted as "may I attain" or "may I reach," expressing a desire for connection or realization.
  • yajiṣṭhaṁ: This means "most worthy of worship," highlighting the importance of Agni in rituals and spiritual practices.
  • havyavāhana: This term refers to "one who carries offerings," indicating Agni's role in accepting and transporting offerings to the divine.

The mantra can be understood as a heartfelt invocation to Agni, expressing the speaker's intention to praise and seek the blessings of the fire god. It emphasizes the importance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual, and the desire for protection and immortality. The speaker acknowledges Agni as a vital force in the universe, one who facilitates the connection between the mortal and the divine.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Spiritual Practices: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or rituals to invoke the protective and purifying qualities of fire, seeking guidance and nourishment for their spiritual journey.
  • Life Challenges: In times of difficulty, one might reflect on the themes of protection and immortality, finding strength in the belief that challenges are temporary and that there is a greater, eternal essence to life.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to Agni can also inspire a respect for fire and its role in nature, encouraging sustainable practices and a deeper understanding of our relationship with the elements.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of nourishment, and the protective forces that guide us. It encourages individuals to seek a deeper understanding of their existence and to honor the divine presence in their lives.
