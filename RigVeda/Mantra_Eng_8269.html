त्वं सोमासि धारणुर मन्द्र ओजिष्ठो अध्वरे |पवस्व मंहयद्रविः ||
tvaṁ somāsi dhārayur mandra ojiṣṭho adhvare | pavasva maṁhayadrayiḥ ||
  • tvaṁ somāsi: This phrase translates to "You are the Soma." In Vedic literature, Soma is often associated with a divine drink that grants immortality and is a symbol of the moon. It represents nourishment, vitality, and the essence of life.
  • dhārayur: This word means "you sustain" or "you support." It emphasizes the role of Soma as a life-giving force that sustains all living beings.
  • mandra: This term can be interpreted as "sweet" or "pleasant." It signifies the enjoyable and uplifting qualities of Soma, which can be likened to the joy and happiness that come from fulfilling experiences in life.
  • ojiṣṭho: This word means "the most powerful" or "the strongest." It highlights the potency of Soma, suggesting that it has the ability to enhance strength and vitality in individuals.
  • adhvare: This term refers to "sacrifice" or "ritual." In the context of the mantra, it indicates that the essence of Soma is invoked during sacred rituals, emphasizing its importance in spiritual practices.
  • pavasva: This word translates to "may you flow" or "may you purify." It suggests a desire for the divine essence of Soma to flow into the lives of individuals, purifying and enriching their existence.
  • maṁhayadrayiḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "with great strength." It conveys the idea that the blessings of Soma should empower individuals, granting them the strength to overcome challenges and obstacles in life.

Conclusion: The mantra "tvaṁ somāsi dhārayur mandra ojiṣṭho adhvare | pavasva maṁhayadrayiḥ ||" encapsulates the essence of life, vitality, and spiritual nourishment. It signifies the importance of divine support and strength in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during moments of struggle or when seeking inspiration and empowerment. For instance, individuals may recite this mantra during meditation or yoga practices to invoke a sense of peace and strength, or during significant life events such as weddings or new beginnings to seek blessings for a prosperous journey ahead. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life and the divine forces that sustain and uplift us.
