आग्ने वह हविरद्याय देवान इन्द्रज्येष्ठास इह मादयन्ताम् |इमं यज्ञं दिवि देवेषु धेहि यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
āgne vaha haviradyāya devān indrajyeṣṭhāsa iha mādayantām | imaṁ yajñaṁ divi deveṣu dhehi yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Āgne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god, who is considered a vital force in Vedic rituals. Fire symbolizes purity and is a medium for offerings to the deities.
  • Vaha: This means "to carry" or "to bring forth." It signifies the act of invoking or inviting the divine presence.
  • Haviradyāya: "Havi" refers to offerings, particularly those made in the fire. "Adyāya" can be interpreted as "today" or "in this moment," emphasizing the immediacy of the offerings.
  • Devān: This translates to "the gods," indicating the divine beings to whom the offerings are made.
  • Indrajyeṣṭhāsa: This term refers to the "most excellent" or "supreme" among the gods, particularly Indra, who is often regarded as the king of the gods in Vedic literature.
  • Iha: Meaning "here" or "in this place," it emphasizes the physical presence of the ritual and the offerings being made.
  • Mādayantām: This means "may they be pleased" or "may they be delighted," expressing the hope that the gods will accept the offerings and be satisfied.
  • Imam yajñam: "Imam" means "this," and "yajñam" refers to the sacrifice or ritual. Together, it signifies the specific ritual being performed.
  • Divi: This translates to "in the heavens," indicating the spiritual realm where the gods reside.
  • Deveṣu: This means "among the gods," reinforcing the connection between the offerings and the divine beings.
  • Dhehi: This means "may you place" or "may you establish," indicating a request for the gods to accept and bless the offerings.
  • Yūyam: This translates to "you all," referring to the collective group of gods being addressed.
  • Pāta: Meaning "may you protect" or "may you safeguard," it expresses a desire for divine protection.
  • Svastibhiḥ: This word means "with auspiciousness" or "with well-being," indicating a wish for prosperity and good fortune.
  • Sadā: This means "always" or "forever," emphasizing the desire for continuous blessings.
  • Naḥ: This translates to "us," indicating the community or individuals making the offerings.

Conclusion: The mantra is a prayer invoking Agni and the gods to accept the offerings made in a ritual. It emphasizes the importance of divine presence and blessings in our lives. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various rituals and ceremonies, such as weddings, housewarming, or any significant life events where blessings and auspiciousness are sought. The significance lies in the belief that by performing rituals with sincerity and devotion, individuals can invite divine grace, protection, and prosperity into their lives, fostering a sense of community and connection with the divine.
