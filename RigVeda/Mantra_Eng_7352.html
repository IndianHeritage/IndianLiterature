आयं ह येन वा इदं स्वर मरुत्वता जितम् |इन्द्रेण सोमपीतये ||
ayaṁ ha yena vā idaṁ svar marutvatā jitam | indreṇa somapītaye ||
  • ayaṁ - This word translates to "this" or "this one," indicating a specific focus or subject.
  • ha - A particle often used for emphasis, similar to "indeed" or "certainly."
  • yena - Meaning "by whom" or "by which," it suggests a connection or agency.
  • vā - Translates to "or," presenting an alternative or choice.
  • idaṁ - Meaning "this" or "this thing," reinforcing the focus on the subject.
  • svar - Refers to "heaven" or "the celestial realm," often associated with divine or spiritual aspects.
  • marutvatā - This term relates to the "Maruts," the storm deities, symbolizing power, energy, and movement.
  • jitam - Meaning "conquered" or "overcome," indicating victory or achievement.
  • indreṇa - Refers to "Indra," the king of the gods, associated with strength, rain, and thunder.
  • somapītaye - This term relates to "the drinking of Soma," a ritualistic drink believed to confer immortality and divine insight.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to recognize the power and agency of the divine, particularly through the influence of Indra and the Maruts. It emphasizes the connection between the earthly and celestial realms, suggesting that through divine intervention and the consumption of Soma, one can achieve victory and overcome challenges.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties, one can invoke the strength of Indra and the Maruts, seeking divine support to overcome obstacles.
  • Community and Leadership: Leaders can draw inspiration from this mantra, recognizing the importance of collective strength and divine guidance in achieving communal goals.
  • Spiritual Practices: The reference to Soma can be seen in modern spiritual practices where individuals seek enlightenment and connection with the divine through meditation and rituals.

Conclusion: This mantra signifies the importance of divine support in overcoming life's challenges. It serves as a reminder that through faith and connection with the celestial, individuals can find strength and achieve their goals. In various aspects of life, whether personal, communal, or spiritual, invoking this mantra can inspire resilience and a sense of purpose.
