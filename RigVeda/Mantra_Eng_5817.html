पुनिषे वाम अरक्षसम मनीषां सोमं इन्द्राय वरुणाय जुह्वत् |घृतप्रतīkām उषसं न देवीं ता नो यामन्न उरुष्यताम अभीक ||
punīṣe vām arakṣasam manīṣāṁ somam indrāya varuṇāya juhvat | ghṛtapratīkām uṣasaṁ na devīṁ tā no yāmann uruṣyatām abhīke ||
  • punīṣe: This word translates to "purifier" or "one who cleanses." It signifies the divine aspect of purification, both physically and spiritually.
  • vām: Meaning "your," it indicates a personal connection to the divine or the sacred.
  • arakṣasam: This term refers to "the unprotected" or "those who are vulnerable." It highlights the need for protection and support from higher powers.
  • manīṣāṁ: This word means "intellect" or "wisdom." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and understanding in our lives.
  • somam: Referring to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, it symbolizes nourishment and spiritual enlightenment.
  • indrāya: This means "to Indra," the king of gods, representing strength, power, and leadership.
  • varuṇāya: Referring to Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water, it signifies the importance of balance and harmony in life.
  • juhvat: This term means "to offer" or "to sacrifice," indicating the act of giving and devotion in rituals.
  • ghṛtapratīkām: This translates to "with the essence of ghee," symbolizing purity, richness, and the essence of life.
  • uṣasaṁ: Meaning "dawn," it represents new beginnings, hope, and the arrival of light after darkness.
  • na devīṁ: This means "not to the goddess," indicating a specific focus of devotion or offering.
  • tā: This word means "that," referring to the object of worship or the divine presence.
  • no: Meaning "to us," it emphasizes the collective aspect of the prayer or offering.
  • yāmann: This translates to "whoever," indicating inclusivity in the divine connection.
  • uruṣyatām: Meaning "may it be vast" or "may it expand," it signifies the desire for growth and abundance.
  • abhīke: This term means "to be invoked" or "to be called upon," indicating the act of seeking divine presence and assistance.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of purification, protection, wisdom, and the offering of devotion to the divine. It highlights the interconnectedness of human experience with the divine forces of nature and the cosmos. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during rituals for personal growth, seeking guidance in difficult times, or fostering a sense of community through collective offerings. It serves as a reminder of the need for spiritual nourishment and the importance of invoking divine support in our daily lives.
