त्वं उत्तमास्योषधेतव वृक्षाः उपस्तयः |उपस्तिर अस्तु सोऽस्माकं यो अस्मां अभिदासति ||
tvam uttamāsy oṣadhe tava vṛkṣā upastayaḥ | upastir astu so3 'smākaṁ yo asmām̐ abhidāsati ||
  • tvam uttamāsy oṣadhe: This phrase translates to "You are the best of herbs." Here, "tvam" means "you," "uttamāsy" means "of the best," and "oṣadhe" refers to "herbs" or "plants." This can be understood as a recognition of the importance of nature and its resources in sustaining life.
  • tava vṛkṣā upastayaḥ: This means "your trees are our support." "Tava" means "your," "vṛkṣā" means "trees," and "upastayaḥ" refers to "support" or "sustenance." This highlights the vital role that trees play in providing shelter, food, and ecological balance.
  • upastir astu so3 'smākaṁ: This translates to "may there be support for us." "Upastir" means "support," "astu" means "may it be," and "so3 'smākaṁ" means "for us." This is a prayer for the sustenance and support that nature provides to humanity.
  • yo asmām̐ abhidāsati: This means "whoever addresses us." "Yo" means "who," "asmām̐" means "to us," and "abhidāsati" means "addresses" or "speaks to." This can be interpreted as a call for communication and connection with the natural world, emphasizing the importance of understanding and respecting nature.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of nature, particularly herbs and trees, as essential sources of support and sustenance for humanity. In real life, this can be applied in various ways, such as promoting environmental conservation, sustainable agriculture, and the importance of biodiversity. By recognizing the value of plants and trees, we can foster a deeper connection with nature, leading to a more harmonious existence. This mantra serves as a reminder of our responsibility to protect and cherish the natural world, ensuring that it continues to provide for future generations.
