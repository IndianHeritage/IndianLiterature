सनिर्मित्रस्य पाप्रथ इन्द्रः सोमस्य पीतये |प्राची वाशिव सून्वते मिमीत इति ||
sanir mitrasya papratha indraḥ somasya pītaye | prācī vāśīva sunvate mimīta it ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sanir: Together, united
    • mitrasya: of friendship
    • papratha: spread, extend
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods
    • somasya: of the soma, a sacred drink
    • pītaye: for drinking
    • prācī: from the east
    • vāśīva: like a ruler
    • sunvate: who brings prosperity
    • mimīta: may it be fulfilled
    • it: thus, in this way
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and friendship.
    • It invokes Indra, the god of rain and thunderstorms, symbolizing strength and power.
    • The reference to soma indicates the significance of rituals and offerings in fostering divine connections.
    • The phrase "from the east" suggests new beginnings and the arrival of light and knowledge.
    • It calls for prosperity and fulfillment of desires through collective efforts and divine blessings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, teamwork and collaboration can lead to greater success, akin to the unity mentioned in the mantra.
    • Community gatherings, where people come together to celebrate and support each other, reflect the spirit of friendship and shared prosperity.
    • Rituals and traditions in various cultures, such as family gatherings during festivals, embody the essence of invoking blessings for collective well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the power of unity, friendship, and collective efforts in achieving prosperity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments, highlighting the importance of collaboration.
    • By invoking divine blessings through rituals and fostering a spirit of togetherness, individuals and communities can work towards fulfilling their aspirations and enhancing their lives.
