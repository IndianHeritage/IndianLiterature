सूर उपाके तन्वं दधानो वि यत ते चेत्य अमृतस्य वरपः |मृगो न हस्ती तविषीम उषाणः सिंहो न भीमा आयुधानि बिभ्रत् ||
sūra upāke tanva1ṁ dadhāno vi yat te cety amṛtasya varpaḥ | mṛgo na hastī taviṣīm uṣāṇaḥ siṁho na bhīma āyudhāni bibhrat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sūra: Brave, valiant, or heroic.
    • upāke: One who is engaged in a task or action.
    • tanvaṁ: Body or form.
    • dadhāno: Holding or possessing.
    • vi: In this context, it can mean 'in' or 'within.'
    • yat: That which.
    • te: Your.
    • cety: In the sense of 'to know' or 'to understand.'
    • amṛtasya: Of immortality or eternal life.
    • varpaḥ: A term that can refer to a covering or a protective layer.
    • mṛgo: Animal or creature.
    • na: Not.
    • hastī: Elephant.
    • taviṣīm: Powerful or strong.
    • uṣāṇaḥ: Referring to the dawn or the light of day.
    • siṁho: Lion.
    • na: Not.
    • bhīma: Fearsome or formidable.
    • āyudhāni: Weapons or tools.
    • bibhrat: Bearing or carrying.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the valor and strength of a brave individual who possesses a powerful form.
    • It emphasizes the importance of understanding the essence of immortality and the protective layers that surround it.
    • The mention of animals like the elephant and lion symbolizes strength and fearlessness, suggesting that true power does not rely on weapons or brute force.
    • In real life, this can be interpreted as the idea that inner strength, wisdom, and courage are more significant than physical might or material possessions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, a true leader embodies the qualities of bravery and wisdom, guiding their team through challenges without resorting to fear or intimidation.
    • In personal development, individuals who cultivate inner strength and resilience often find themselves better equipped to handle life's adversities, much like the lion that does not need to show its claws to assert its dominance.
    • In community service, those who work selflessly for the betterment of society demonstrate the essence of this mantra by prioritizing the welfare of others over their own interests.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call to recognize and cultivate inner strength, wisdom, and courage.
    • It teaches that true power comes from understanding and embodying these qualities rather than relying on external tools or fear.
    • In various aspects of life, whether in personal growth, leadership, or community engagement, the principles highlighted in this mantra can guide individuals toward a more meaningful and impactful existence.
