उत स्म सद्म हर्यतस्य पष्ट्योऽरत्यो न वाजं हरिवाम् अचिक्रदत् |महī चिद् हि धिषणा हर्यद् ओजसा बृहद् वयो दधिषे हर्यतश् चिद् आ ||
uta sma sadma haryatasya pastyo3r atyo na vājaṁ harivām̐ acikradat | mahī cid dhi dhiṣaṇāharyad ojasā bṛhad vayo dadhiṣe haryataś cid ā ||
  • Mantra: "uta sma sadma haryatasya pastyo3r atyo na vājaṁ harivām̐ acikradat | mahī cid dhi dhiṣaṇāharyad ojasā bṛhad vayo dadhiṣe haryataś cid ā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: indeed, or thus
    • sma: in the past, or previously
    • sadma: dwelling, or home
    • haryatasya: of the one who is swift or moving
    • pastyo3r: of the one who is powerful or strong
    • atyo: beyond, or surpassing
    • na: not
    • vājaṁ: wealth, or prosperity
    • harivām̐: of the one who is like a horse, or swift
    • acikradat: has spoken, or has declared
    • mahī: great, or vast
    • cid: indeed, or surely
    • dhi: intellect, or understanding
    • dhiṣaṇā: the one who is the ruler or controller
    • haryad: of the swift one
    • ojasā: with energy, or strength
    • bṛhad: great, or vast
    • vayo: age, or life
    • dadhiṣe: you have given
    • haryataś: of the swift one
    • cid: indeed
    • ā: to, or towards
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the greatness and power of a divine entity, often interpreted as a representation of the universal spirit or consciousness.
    • The phrase "uta sma sadma" suggests a connection to the past and the idea of a dwelling or home that is both physical and spiritual.
    • The mention of "haryatasya" and "pastyo3r" emphasizes the qualities of swiftness and strength, which can be seen in natural phenomena like the flow of a river or the movement of the wind.
    • In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, where one must be swift and strong in their endeavors to achieve success.
    • The line "mahī cid dhi dhiṣaṇā" indicates that true understanding and control come from a vast and profound intellect, which is essential in leadership and decision-making.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of strength, swiftness, and wisdom in both personal and communal life.
    • It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding, to be strong in their pursuits, and to create a harmonious dwelling, both materially and spiritually.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various aspects of life, such as in leadership roles, personal development, and community building, where the qualities of strength and wisdom are essential for success and harmony.
