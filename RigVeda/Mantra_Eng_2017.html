त्वं अग्नि अदितिर्देव दाशुषे त्वं होत्रा भारती वर्धसे गिरा |त्वं इला शतहिमासी दक्षसे त्वं वृत्रहा वसुपते सरस्वती ||
tvam agne aditir deva dāśuṣe tvaṁ hotrā bhāratī vardhase girā | tvam iḻā śatahimāsi dakṣase tvaṁ vṛtrahā vasupate sarasvatī ||
  • tvam agne: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni." Agni is the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, fire is essential for cooking, warmth, and rituals, representing the transformative power of energy in our lives.
  • aditir deva: "Aditi, the divine." Aditi is often considered the mother of the gods and represents the infinite and the boundless. In our lives, this can be seen as the nurturing aspect of nature and the universe, reminding us of the importance of care and compassion.
  • dāśuṣe: This means "to the worshipper." It emphasizes the relationship between the divine and the devotee, highlighting the importance of devotion and faith in our daily lives. For example, people often turn to prayer or meditation in times of need, seeking guidance and support.
  • tvaṁ hotrā bhāratī: "You, the one who is invoked, O Bharati." Bharati refers to the goddess of speech and knowledge, symbolizing the power of communication and wisdom. In real life, effective communication is crucial in personal and professional relationships, enabling understanding and collaboration.
  • vardhase girā: "You increase the voice." This signifies the enhancement of expression and creativity. In practical terms, this can relate to how art, literature, and music enrich our lives, allowing us to express emotions and ideas.
  • tvam iḻā: "You, O Iḻā." Iḻā is associated with the earth and fertility, representing abundance and sustenance. In real life, this can be seen in agriculture and the importance of nurturing the land to provide for future generations.
  • śatahimāsi dakṣase: "You who are capable of a hundred." This phrase suggests strength and capability. In our lives, it can be interpreted as the potential within each individual to achieve greatness through hard work and perseverance.
  • tvaṁ vṛtrahā: "You, the slayer of Vritra." Vritra is a serpent or dragon that represents obstacles and challenges. This signifies overcoming difficulties in life. For instance, facing personal challenges or societal issues requires courage and determination, much like the battle against Vritra.
  • vasupate sarasvatī: "O Lord of wealth, Sarasvatī." This refers to the goddess of knowledge, music, and arts, emphasizing the importance of wisdom and prosperity. In real life, education and knowledge are vital for personal and societal growth, leading to a more prosperous future.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine energy, knowledge, and the importance of overcoming obstacles. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the significance of devotion, and the pursuit of wisdom. In real-life scenarios, these themes are applicable in personal growth, community building, and the quest for knowledge and prosperity. By invoking these qualities, individuals can strive for a balanced and fulfilling life, contributing positively to society.
