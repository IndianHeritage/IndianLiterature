विष्वेद एते जनिमाऽसं विविक्तो महो देवान् बिभ्रती न व्यथेत् |एजद ध्रुवं पत्यते विष्वं एकं चरत् पतत्रि विषुणं वि जातम् ||
viśved ete janimā saṁ vivikto maho devān bibhratī na vyathete | ejad dhruvam patyate viśvam ekaṁ carat patatri viṣuṇaṁ vi jātam ||
  • viśved ete janimā: This phrase can be translated as "all beings are born." It signifies the universality of life and the interconnectedness of all living entities. In real life, this can be seen in the way ecosystems function, where every organism, from the smallest microbe to the largest mammal, plays a role in maintaining balance.
  • saṁ vivikto maho devān: This means "the great gods are distinct." It highlights the diversity of divine forces in the universe. In our daily lives, we can observe this in the various cultures and religions that celebrate different deities, each representing different aspects of life, such as love, wisdom, and strength.
  • bibhratī na vyathete: This translates to "they do not fear." It suggests a state of fearlessness and confidence. In practical terms, this can be related to individuals who face challenges in life with courage, such as activists fighting for social justice or individuals overcoming personal struggles.
  • ejad dhruvam patyate: This phrase means "what is stable is established." It emphasizes the importance of stability and permanence in life. For example, in a business context, a company that has a solid foundation and ethical practices is more likely to succeed in the long run compared to one that is unstable or unethical.
  • viśvam ekaṁ carat: This translates to "the whole universe moves as one." It reflects the idea of unity in diversity. In real life, this can be seen in global movements for climate change, where people from different backgrounds come together for a common cause, demonstrating collective action for the greater good.
  • patatri viṣuṇaṁ vi jātam: This means "like a bird that flies." It symbolizes freedom and the ability to transcend limitations. In everyday life, this can be illustrated by individuals pursuing their dreams and aspirations, breaking free from societal constraints, much like an artist or an entrepreneur who follows their passion.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound philosophical concepts about life, divinity, fearlessness, stability, unity, and freedom. Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals to recognize their interconnectedness with all beings, embrace diversity, and face life's challenges with courage. In real-life scenarios, these teachings can be applied in various contexts, such as environmental activism, personal growth, and community building, encouraging a holistic approach to living harmoniously in a diverse world.
