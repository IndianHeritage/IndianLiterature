तं शिशीतां स्वध्वरं सत्यं सत्वानाम ऋत्वियम् |उतो नु चिद य ओहात आण्डा शुष्णस्य भेदत्य अजैः स्वरवतीर अपो नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
taṁ śiśītā svadhvaraṁ satyaṁ satvānam ṛtviyam | uto nu cid ya ohata āṇḍā śuṣṇasya bhedaty ajaiḥ svarvatīr apo nabhantām anyake same ||
  • taṁ - This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity or force.
  • śiśītā - This means "who is cool" or "who brings calmness," often associated with peace and tranquility.
  • svadhvaraṁ - This can be interpreted as "the one who is self-possessed" or "the one who is in control of oneself."
  • satyaṁ - This word means "truth," emphasizing the importance of honesty and integrity.
  • satvānam - This refers to "the essence of beings" or "the nature of living entities," highlighting the interconnectedness of all life.
  • ṛtviyam - This translates to "the one who is righteous" or "the one who follows the right path," indicating moral and ethical conduct.
  • uto - This means "and" or "also," connecting ideas within the mantra.
  • nu - This can be interpreted as "indeed" or "certainly," adding emphasis to the statement.
  • cid - This word means "consciousness" or "awareness," pointing to the importance of being aware and mindful.
  • ya - This translates to "who" or "that which," referring to the divine or higher power.
  • ohata - This means "who is invoked" or "who is called upon," indicating a plea for divine assistance.
  • āṇḍā - This refers to "the egg" or "the universe," symbolizing creation and the cosmos.
  • šuṣṇasya - This means "of the one who is powerful" or "of the one who is strong," referring to a divine force.
  • bhedaty - This translates to "who separates" or "who distinguishes," indicating the ability to discern.
  • ajaiḥ - This means "by the unbroken" or "by the eternal," referring to the everlasting nature of the divine.
  • svarvatīr - This translates to "the one who is everywhere" or "the omnipresent," emphasizing the all-encompassing nature of the divine.
  • apo - This means "water," symbolizing purity and life.
  • nabhantām - This refers to "the sky" or "the heavens," indicating a connection to the divine realm.
  • anyake - This translates to "other" or "different," suggesting diversity in existence.
  • same - This means "in harmony" or "together," emphasizing unity and balance.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking truth, peace, and righteousness in life. It emphasizes the importance of self-control, awareness, and the interconnectedness of all beings. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal development, ethical decision-making, and fostering harmony in relationships. By invoking these principles, individuals can strive for a balanced and meaningful life, contributing positively to their communities and the world at large.
