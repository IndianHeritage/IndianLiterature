मन्त्र: अन्तरैश् चक्रैस् तनयाय वर्तिर द्युमता यातं नृवतां रथेन | सन्त्येन त्यजसा मार्त्यस्य वनुष्यताम् अपि शीरषा ववृक्तम् ||
antaraiś cakrais tanayāya vartir dyumatā yātaṁ nṛvatā rathena | sanutyena tyajasā martyasya vanuṣyatām api śīrṣā vavṛktam ||
  • antaraiś: This word can be interpreted as "with the help of" or "through the means of." It suggests a connection or a method of achieving something.
  • cakrais: Refers to "wheels" or "circles." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent the cycles of life, time, or the universe.
  • tanayāya: This means "for the offspring" or "for the children." It emphasizes the importance of future generations and the legacy we leave behind.
  • vartir: Translates to "to exist" or "to be present." It indicates a state of being or existence.
  • dyumatā: This word means "brilliant" or "illuminated." It signifies knowledge, wisdom, or enlightenment.
  • yātaṁ: Means "having gone" or "having traveled." It implies a journey or a process of moving forward.
  • nṛvatā: Refers to "like a man" or "human-like." It emphasizes the human experience and qualities.
  • rathena: Translates to "by the chariot." The chariot can symbolize the journey of life, driven by our actions and decisions.
  • sanutyena: Means "with great care" or "with attention." It highlights the importance of mindfulness in our actions.
  • tyajasā: This word means "by abandoning" or "by letting go." It suggests the need to release attachments or negative aspects of life.
  • martyasya: Refers to "of mortals" or "of human beings." It emphasizes the human condition and our shared experiences.
  • vanuṣyatām: Means "to the state of being human" or "to humanity." It signifies the essence of what it means to be human.
  • api: Translates to "even" or "also." It adds an inclusive aspect to the statement.
  • śīrṣā: Refers to "with the head" or "from the top." It can symbolize leadership, guidance, or the source of wisdom.
  • vavṛktam: Means "has been covered" or "has been enveloped." It suggests a protective or nurturing aspect.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of life, the importance of wisdom and mindfulness, and the legacy we leave for future generations. It encourages us to navigate our journeys with care, to embrace our humanity, and to let go of what no longer serves us. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in parenting, leadership, and personal growth, reminding us to act thoughtfully and to prioritize the well-being of ourselves and others. The mantra serves as a guiding principle for living a meaningful and impactful life, fostering a sense of responsibility towards our actions and their effects on the world around us.
