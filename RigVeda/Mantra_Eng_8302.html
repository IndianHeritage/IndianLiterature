स रोरुवद अभि पूर्वा अचिक्रदद उपारुहः श्रथयन स्वादते हरिः |तिरः पवित्रं पर्यन्न उरु ज्रयो नि शर्याणि दधते देव आ वरम् ||
sa roruvad abhi pūrvā acikradad upāruhaḥ śrathayan svādate hariḥ | tiraḥ pavitram pariyann uru jrayo ni śaryāṇi dadhate deva ā varam ||
  • sa roruvad: This phrase can be interpreted as "he who cries out" or "he who is in distress." It signifies a call for help or a plea for assistance in times of trouble.
  • abhi pūrvā: This means "towards the east" or "from the past." It suggests a connection to the origins or the beginning of something, possibly indicating a return to foundational values or principles.
  • acikradad: This term can be understood as "he who has made a sound" or "he who has called out." It emphasizes the act of communication or expression, particularly in a spiritual or divine context.
  • upāruhaḥ: This word translates to "he who ascends" or "he who rises up." It symbolizes growth, elevation, and the pursuit of higher knowledge or enlightenment.
  • śrathayan: This can be interpreted as "he who is steadfast" or "he who is firm." It denotes stability and resilience in the face of challenges.
  • svādate: This means "he who enjoys" or "he who takes pleasure." It reflects the idea of finding joy and satisfaction in life, particularly in spiritual pursuits.
  • hariḥ: This term refers to "the divine" or "the remover of sins." It is often associated with Lord Vishnu, symbolizing protection and preservation.
  • tiraḥ pavitram: This phrase translates to "the pure beyond" or "the sacred beyond." It signifies a realm of purity and divinity, suggesting a connection to higher spiritual truths.
  • pariyann: This means "surrounding" or "encompassing." It indicates a protective or nurturing presence that envelops and supports.
  • uru jrayo: This can be interpreted as "great strength" or "vast power." It emphasizes the importance of inner strength and resilience in overcoming obstacles.
  • ni śaryāṇi: This phrase means "in the realms of the divine" or "in the sacred spaces." It suggests a connection to spiritual realms and the divine presence.
  • dadhate: This translates to "they hold" or "they bear." It indicates the act of carrying or supporting something significant, possibly referring to the weight of responsibilities or spiritual duties.
  • deva ā varam: This means "the divine grants blessings" or "the gods bestow favors." It signifies the importance of divine grace and blessings in human life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound spiritual message about seeking divine assistance, embracing purity, and striving for higher knowledge and strength. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied during times of distress or uncertainty, reminding individuals to call upon their inner strength and seek divine guidance. It emphasizes the importance of resilience, joy in spiritual pursuits, and the blessings that come from a connection with the divine. This mantra serves as a reminder to remain steadfast in one's journey, to seek purity in thoughts and actions, and to embrace the support of the divine in all endeavors.
