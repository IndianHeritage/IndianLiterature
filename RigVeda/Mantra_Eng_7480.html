इमानी त्रिणि विष्टपा तानिन्द्र विह रोहय |शिरस्ततस्योर्वराम आद इदं मा उपोदरे ||
imāni trīṇi viṣṭapā tānīndra vi rohaya | śiras tatasyorvarām ād idam ma upodare ||
  • imāni: This word translates to "these" or "this" in English, indicating a focus on specific elements or aspects.
  • trīṇi: Meaning "three," it signifies a triad or a set of three important components.
  • viṣṭapā: This term can be interpreted as "the one who spreads" or "the one who expands," often referring to a divine force or energy that encompasses and nurtures.
  • tānīndra: This word combines "tā" (that) and "Indra," referring to the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power, strength, and authority.
  • vi rohaya: This phrase translates to "may you grow" or "may you flourish," indicating a desire for growth and prosperity.
  • śiras: Meaning "head" or "top," it often symbolizes leadership, wisdom, or the pinnacle of achievement.
  • tatasyorvarām: This can be interpreted as "the best of that," suggesting a selection of the finest qualities or attributes.
  • ād: This word means "from" or "out of," indicating a source or origin.
  • idam: Meaning "this," it emphasizes the importance of the present moment or the current situation.
  • ma: This translates to "not" or "do not," often used to express a negation or a caution.
  • upodare: This term can be understood as "to be elevated" or "to rise up," suggesting an aspiration for higher states of being or consciousness.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for growth, strength, and the attainment of the best qualities in life. It emphasizes the importance of nurturing and expanding one's potential, guided by divine strength and wisdom.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Development: Individuals can use this mantra as a daily affirmation to encourage their growth and to strive for excellence in their personal and professional lives.
  • Leadership: Leaders can reflect on the qualities of Indra, seeking to embody strength and wisdom while fostering an environment where their team can flourish.
  • Education: Educators can invoke this mantra to inspire students to reach their full potential, emphasizing the importance of nurturing their talents and abilities.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the interconnectedness of growth, strength, and wisdom. It encourages individuals to seek the best within themselves and to rise above challenges, fostering a sense of purpose and aspiration in their lives.
