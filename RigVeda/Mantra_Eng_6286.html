यदि प्रवृद्ध सत्पते सहस्रं महिषांऽघः |आदित त इन्द्रियम महि प्र वावृधे ||
yadi pravṛddha satpate sahasram mahiṣām̐ aghaḥ | ād it ta indriyam mahi pra vāvṛdhe ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yadi: if
    • pravṛddha: increased or grown
    • satpate: of the good or virtuous
    • sahasram: a thousand
    • mahiṣām̐: of the great or powerful
    • aghaḥ: sin or wrongdoing
    • ād: from
    • it: this
    • ta: that
    • indriyam: sense or perception
    • mahi: great
    • pra: forth
    • vāvṛdhe: may it increase or grow
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the potential for growth and the overcoming of negativity or sin.
    • It suggests that if the virtuous or good qualities are increased, they can overpower a thousand wrongdoings.
    • The emphasis is on the power of positive attributes and how they can enhance our perception and understanding of the world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, if a group of individuals focuses on kindness and cooperation, their positive actions can overshadow any negative behaviors present.
    • In personal development, cultivating virtues such as patience, empathy, and integrity can help an individual overcome past mistakes or negative habits.
    • In a workplace, fostering a culture of respect and collaboration can lead to a more productive environment, diminishing the impact of conflicts or misunderstandings.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its encouragement to cultivate positive qualities in ourselves and our surroundings.
    • It teaches that by focusing on the good, we can effectively diminish the influence of negativity and wrongdoing.
    • This principle is applicable in various aspects of life, including personal growth, community building, and professional environments, promoting a more harmonious existence.
