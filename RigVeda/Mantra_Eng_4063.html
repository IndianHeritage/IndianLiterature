अग्नि सुतस्य पीतये विश्वैर ऊमेभिर आगहि |देवेभिर हव्यदातये ||
agne sutasya pītaye viśvair ūmebhir ā gahi | devebhir havyadātaye ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire is often seen as a purifying and transformative force.
  • Sutasya: This translates to "of the son" or "of the offspring." It signifies the connection between generations and the importance of lineage.
  • Pītaye: This means "for the drink" or "for the nourishment." It indicates the act of offering something valuable, often in the context of rituals.
  • Viśvair: This word means "with all" or "with the universe." It emphasizes inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Ūmebhir: This refers to "with the women" or "with the divine feminine." It highlights the role of women in spiritual and social contexts.
  • Ā gahi: This means "come" or "approach." It is an invitation to the divine or to the forces of nature.
  • Devebhir: This translates to "by the gods." It signifies divine intervention or support in human endeavors.
  • Havyadātaye: This means "for the offering" or "for the sacrifice." It underscores the importance of giving and sharing in spiritual practices.

This mantra can be understood as a call to Agni, inviting him to partake in the offerings made by the community, emphasizing the importance of connection with the divine and the collective. It reflects the values of nourishment, inclusivity, and the sacredness of offerings.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: During family gatherings or community events, invoking Agni can symbolize the unity and warmth of the gathering.
  • Environmental Awareness: The reference to fire can also be seen as a reminder of the importance of nature and the need to respect and protect our environment.
  • Gender Equality: The mention of women highlights the significance of their roles in society, encouraging respect and equality in all aspects of life.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity, respect for the divine, and the importance of offerings in our lives. It serves as a reminder to acknowledge our connections with each other, the environment, and the divine forces that guide us. By incorporating these values into our daily lives, we can foster a sense of community, respect, and spiritual awareness.
