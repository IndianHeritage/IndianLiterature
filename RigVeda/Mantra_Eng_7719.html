एष प्रात्नेन जन्मना देवो देवभ्यः सुतः |हरिः पवित्रे अर्षति ||
eṣa pratnena janmanā devo devebhyaḥ sutaḥ | hariḥ pavitre arṣati ||
  • eṣa pratnena janmanā: This phrase can be translated as "this (being) is born through effort." It emphasizes the importance of effort and intention in the process of creation and existence. In real life, this can be seen in various contexts such as education, where students must put in effort to achieve their goals, or in sports, where athletes train rigorously to excel.
  • devo devebhyaḥ sutaḥ: This translates to "the divine is the offspring of the divine." It suggests a connection between the divine and the earthly, indicating that all beings are interconnected through a divine essence. For example, in nature, we see how ecosystems are interdependent, with each species playing a role in the health of the environment, reflecting a divine order.
  • hariḥ pavitre arṣati: This can be interpreted as "the divine (Hari) shines in purity." It signifies the idea that purity and divinity are intertwined. In practical terms, this can relate to the importance of maintaining purity in thoughts, actions, and surroundings. For instance, practicing mindfulness and ethical living can lead to a more harmonious life, reflecting the purity of the divine.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of effort, divine connection, and purity. Its significance lies in the reminder that our existence is shaped by our efforts, that we are part of a larger divine framework, and that purity in our lives leads to a deeper connection with the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to strive for their goals, recognize their interconnectedness with others, and maintain purity in their thoughts and actions, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
