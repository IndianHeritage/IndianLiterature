प्र स क्षयं तिरते वि महीर इषो यो वो वराय दाशति |प्र प्रजाभिर जायते धर्मणस पर्य अरिष्टः सर्व एधते ||
pra sa kṣayaṁ tirate vi mahīr iṣo yo vo varāya dāśati | pra prajābhir jāyate dharmaṇas pary ariṣṭaḥ sarva edhate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: forward, towards
    • sa: that, he, she, it
    • kṣayaṁ: destruction, decay
    • tirate: crosses, transcends
    • vi: apart, separately
    • mahīr: of the great, the vast
    • iṣo: of the ruler, the lord
    • yo: who
    • vo: your
    • varāya: to protect, to save
    • dāśati: gives, bestows
    • pra: forward, towards
    • prajābhir: with progeny, with beings
    • jāyate: is born, arises
    • dharmaṇas: of righteousness, of duty
    • pary: around, in all directions
    • ariṣṭaḥ: free from obstacles, unharmed
    • sarva: all, every
    • edhate: thrives, flourishes
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about transcending destruction and decay.
    • It emphasizes the importance of protection and the role of a ruler or leader in safeguarding the community.
    • The mantra suggests that through righteousness and duty, beings are born and thrive without obstacles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community, a leader who promotes sustainable practices helps the environment thrive, thus transcending decay.
    • In personal life, individuals who adhere to ethical principles often find themselves flourishing in their careers and relationships.
    • Organizations that prioritize corporate social responsibility tend to thrive, as they protect their stakeholders and the environment.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for leadership that prioritizes protection and righteousness.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal ethics to community leadership and corporate governance.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and leaders can create a thriving environment free from obstacles and decay.
