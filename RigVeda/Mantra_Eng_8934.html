शृतं यदा करोṣi जातवेदोऽथेम एनं परि दत्तात् पितृभ्यः |यदा गच्छत्य असुनीतिम एताम् अथ देवानां वशनीर भवाति ||
śṛtaṁ yadā karasi jātavedo 'them enam pari dattāt pitṛbhyaḥ | yadā gacchāty asunītim etām athā devānāṁ vaśanīr bhavāti ||
  • śṛtaṁ: This word translates to "that which is heard" or "that which is known." It signifies the importance of knowledge and understanding in our actions.
  • yadā: Meaning "when," it indicates a specific time or condition under which something occurs.
  • karasi: This means "you do" or "you perform," emphasizing the role of individual action.
  • jātavedo: Referring to "the one who knows the birth," this term is often associated with Agni, the fire god, symbolizing transformation and the divine spark within us.
  • 'them: This can be interpreted as "to them" or "to those," indicating a direction or focus of action.
  • enam: Meaning "this," it points to a specific object or concept that is being addressed.
  • pari dattāt: This phrase translates to "having been given completely," suggesting the idea of total surrender or offering.
  • pitṛbhyaḥ: Meaning "to the ancestors," it highlights the importance of honoring and remembering our forebears.
  • gacchāty: This means "goes" or "moves," indicating a transition or journey.
  • asunītim: Translated as "the path of the unworthy," it refers to a negative or undesirable direction in life.
  • etām: Meaning "this," it emphasizes the specific path being discussed.
  • athā: This translates to "then" or "therefore," linking the previous ideas to a conclusion or result.
  • devānāṁ: Meaning "of the gods," it signifies divine influence or guidance.
  • vaśanīr: This term means "one who is controlled" or "subdued," indicating a state of surrender to divine will.
  • bhavāti: Meaning "becomes," it signifies transformation or change as a result of the preceding actions.

In summary, this mantra speaks to the importance of knowledge, action, and the honoring of our ancestors. It suggests that when we act with awareness and intention, surrendering our actions to a higher power (represented by Agni), we can avoid negative paths and align ourselves with divine guidance. This is applicable in real life as it encourages individuals to reflect on their actions, seek knowledge, and honor their heritage, ultimately leading to a more meaningful and purposeful existence.


In conclusion, the significance of this mantra lies in its call for conscious living and respect for the past. By integrating these principles into our daily lives, we can navigate our journeys with wisdom and grace, ensuring that our actions contribute positively to our lives and the lives of those around us.
