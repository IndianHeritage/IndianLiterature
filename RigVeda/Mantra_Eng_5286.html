दुराध्यॊऽअदितिं श्रेव्यन्तोऽचेतसो वि जगृभ्रे परुष्णीम् |मह्नाविव्यक पृथिवीं पत्यमानः पशुः कविः अशयच चायमानः ||
durādhyo3 aditiṁ srevayanto 'cetaso vi jagṛbhre paruṣṇīm | mahnāvivyak pṛthivīm patyamānaḥ paśuṣ kavir aśayac cāyamānaḥ ||
  • Durādhyo Aditiṁ: The term "Durādhyo" refers to something that is difficult to attain or comprehend. "Aditi" is often associated with the infinite or the boundless, representing the cosmic mother or the source of all creation. In real life, this can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge or enlightenment, which can be challenging yet rewarding.
  • Srevayanto 'cetaso: This phrase suggests the act of seeking or striving with one's mind or consciousness. It emphasizes the importance of intention and focus in our endeavors. For example, in a professional setting, a person who is dedicated and focused on their goals is more likely to achieve success.
  • Vi Jagṛbhre Paruṣṇīm: "Vi Jagṛbhre" means to grasp or understand something deeply. "Paruṣṇīm" can refer to the harsh or challenging aspects of life. This can be interpreted as the necessity to confront and understand the difficulties we face. For instance, overcoming personal challenges, such as health issues or financial struggles, requires a deep understanding and acceptance of the situation.
  • Mahnāvivyak Pṛthivīm: "Mahnāvivyak" implies a vast or expansive understanding, while "Pṛthivīm" refers to the Earth or the material world. This suggests that a broad perspective is essential for navigating the complexities of life. In practical terms, this can relate to environmental awareness and the need to understand our impact on the planet.
  • Patyamānaḥ Paśuṣ: "Patyamānaḥ" indicates being supported or sustained, and "Paśuṣ" refers to living beings or creatures. This highlights the interconnectedness of all life forms and the importance of nurturing and supporting one another. For example, community support systems play a crucial role in helping individuals during tough times.
  • Kavir Aśayac Cāyamānaḥ: "Kavir" refers to a wise or knowledgeable person, while "Aśayac" suggests the essence or nature of something. This part of the mantra emphasizes the value of wisdom and understanding in shaping our actions and decisions. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way mentors or leaders guide others through their experiences and insights.

  • Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the journey of seeking knowledge and understanding in the face of life's challenges. It emphasizes the importance of intention, wisdom, and interconnectedness in our lives. In real-life scenarios, it serves as a reminder to approach difficulties with a focused mind, to support one another, and to strive for a deeper understanding of our existence and the world around us. The significance of this mantra lies in its universal applicability, encouraging individuals to embrace challenges as opportunities for growth and connection.
