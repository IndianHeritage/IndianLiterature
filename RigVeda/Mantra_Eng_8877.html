न वा उ ते तन्बा तन्बवम् सम पप्रच्याम पापम् आहुर यः स्वसारं निगच्छात् |अन्येन मत प्रमुदः कल्पयस्व न ते भ्राता सुभगे वष्ट्य एतत् ||
na vā u te tanvā tanva1ṁ sam papṛcyām pāpam āhur yaḥ svasāraṁ nigacchāt | anyena mat pramudaḥ kalpayasva na te bhrātā subhage vaṣṭy etat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na vā: not indeed
    • u te: you
    • tanvā: body
    • tanvaṁ: form
    • sam: together
    • papṛcyām: I ask
    • pāpam: sin
    • āhur: they say
    • yaḥ: who
    • svasāraṁ: own sister
    • nigacchāt: goes away
    • anyena: by another
    • mat: my
    • pramudaḥ: joyful
    • kalpayasva: create
    • na te: not your
    • bhrātā: brother
    • subhage: auspicious one
    • vaṣṭy: this
    • etat: this
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects a dialogue about the nature of sin and relationships.
    • The speaker questions the idea of sin associated with the body and form, suggesting that one should not be judged solely based on physical attributes.
    • It emphasizes the importance of inner qualities and the joy of creating relationships based on understanding rather than superficial judgments.
    • The mention of "own sister" signifies the deep bonds of familial relationships and the need to protect and cherish them.
    • It encourages the listener to create joy and positivity in their relationships, rather than allowing negativity or sin to dictate their actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied to foster a culture of inclusivity where employees are valued for their skills and contributions rather than their appearance.
    • In personal relationships, it serves as a reminder to appreciate loved ones for their character and actions, rather than judging them based on external factors.
    • In community interactions, it encourages individuals to build connections that are based on mutual respect and understanding, promoting harmony and reducing conflict.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra teaches us the significance of looking beyond the surface and recognizing the deeper connections we share with others.
    • It highlights the importance of creating a positive environment in our relationships, free from the burdens of judgment and negativity.
    • By applying the wisdom of this mantra in our daily lives, we can cultivate a more compassionate and understanding society, where joy and love prevail over sin and discord.
