जनूष चिद वो मरुतस् त्वेष्येण भीमासस् तुविमन्यवो 'यासः | प्र ये महोभिर ओजसोता सन्ति विश्वो वो यामन् भयते स्वर्दृक ||
janūś cid vo marutas tveṣyeṇa bhīmāsas tuvimanyavo 'yāsaḥ | pra ye mahobhir ojasota santi viśvo vo yāman bhayate svardṛk ||
  • janūś cid vo marutas tveṣyeṇa bhīmāsas tuvimanyavo 'yāsaḥ
    • janūś: Refers to the "joints" or "limbs," symbolizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • cid: Means "if" or "whether," indicating a conditional aspect.
    • vo: Translates to "your," addressing the Maruts, the storm deities.
    • marutas: The Maruts are the personifications of storms and winds, representing power and energy.
    • tveṣyeṇa: Means "with your strength," emphasizing the might of the Maruts.
    • bhīmāsas: Refers to "terrifying" or "fearsome," highlighting the awe-inspiring nature of the Maruts.
    • tuvimanyavo: Suggests "those who are not to be disregarded," indicating their importance and reverence.
    • 'yāsaḥ: Means "to come," indicating a call for the Maruts to arrive or manifest.
  • pra ye mahobhir ojasota santi viśvo vo yāman bhayate svardṛk
    • pra: Means "for" or "towards," indicating direction or purpose.
    • ye: Translates to "those who," referring to the Maruts or the forces of nature.
    • mahobhir: Means "with great power," emphasizing the strength of the Maruts.
    • ojasota: Refers to "with energy" or "vigor," highlighting the dynamic nature of the Maruts.
    • santi: Means "exist" or "are," indicating presence.
    • viśvo: Translates to "all" or "universal," suggesting inclusivity.
    • vo: Again means "your," addressing the Maruts.
    • yāman: Refers to "fear" or "danger," indicating the protective aspect of the Maruts.
    • bhayate: Means "to be afraid" or "to fear," emphasizing the power of the Maruts to instill awe.
    • svardṛk: Refers to "the one who sees," indicating awareness or insight.

Conclusion:

This mantra invokes the Maruts, the storm deities, emphasizing their power, energy, and protective qualities. In real life, this can be seen as a metaphor for the forces of nature that can be both awe-inspiring and terrifying. Just as storms can bring destruction, they also bring renewal and life through rain. The mantra serves as a reminder of the balance of nature and the importance of respecting its power. It encourages individuals to seek strength and protection from these forces, recognizing their role in the cycle of life. In contemporary scenarios, this can be applied to understanding environmental changes and the need for harmony with nature, urging us to respect and protect our environment while acknowledging its formidable power.
