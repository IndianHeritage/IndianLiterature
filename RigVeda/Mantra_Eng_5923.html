वषट् ते विष्णव आस आ कृत्नोमि तन् मे जुषस्व शिपिविष्ट हव्यं |वर्धन्तु त्वा सुश्रुतयो गीरः मे युयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
vaṣaṭ te viṣṇav āsa ā kṛṇomi tan me juṣasva śipiviṣṭa havyam | vardhantu tvā suṣṭutayo giro me yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • vaṣaṭ: This is an invocation, often used in Vedic rituals, calling upon the divine. It signifies the act of offering or calling forth the presence of a deity.
  • te: This means "to you," indicating the direction of the invocation towards the deity, in this case, Vishnu.
  • viṣṇav: Refers to Lord Vishnu, the preserver and protector in Hindu mythology, symbolizing stability and sustenance.
  • āsa: This translates to "may you come" or "may you be present," expressing a desire for the divine presence.
  • ā: A prefix that can mean "towards" or "in the direction of."
  • kṛṇomi: This means "I offer" or "I create," indicating the act of making an offering to the divine.
  • tan: This translates to "that," referring to the offering being made.
  • me: Means "my," indicating personal ownership or connection to the offering.
  • juṣasva: This means "may you enjoy" or "may you accept," expressing a wish for the deity to accept the offering.
  • śipiviṣṭa: This term refers to the "one who is seated in the heart," often associated with the divine presence within.
  • havyam: This means "the offering," typically referring to food or other items offered in a ritual.
  • vardhantu: This translates to "may they grow" or "may they increase," indicating a desire for prosperity and growth.
  • tvā: Means "you," again referring to the deity being invoked.
  • suṣṭutayo: This means "well praised," indicating the importance of good words and praises in the invocation.
  • giro: Refers to "words" or "speech," emphasizing the power of spoken words in rituals.
  • me: Again means "my," indicating personal connection.
  • yūyam: This means "you all," referring to the collective presence of the divine.
  • pāta: This translates to "protect" or "guard," expressing a desire for divine protection.
  • svastibhiḥ: This means "with auspiciousness," indicating a wish for well-being and prosperity.
  • sadā: This translates to "always," indicating a continuous desire for divine presence and protection.
  • naḥ: This means "us," indicating the collective group seeking the blessings of the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra is a powerful invocation to Lord Vishnu, seeking his presence, blessings, and protection. It emphasizes the importance of offerings, praises, and the power of words in connecting with the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during rituals, prayers, or moments of seeking guidance and support in challenging times. It serves as a reminder of the need for divine assistance in our lives, encouraging us to cultivate a relationship with the divine through sincere offerings and prayers. The significance lies in its ability to foster a sense of community and shared purpose, as it calls upon the collective strength of divine protection and auspiciousness for all.
