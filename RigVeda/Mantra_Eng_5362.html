न सोम इन्द्रं असुतो ममाद नाब्रह्माणो मगवानं सुतासः |तस्मा उक्थं जनये यज जुजोषन् नृवन नवीयः श्रिणवद यथा नः ||
na soma indram asuto mamāda nābrahmāṇo maghavānaṁ sutāsaḥ | tasmā ukthaṁ janaye yaj jujoṣan nṛvan navīyaḥ śṛṇavad yathā naḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na: not
    • soma: the moon or a sacred drink
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods
    • asuto: without delay or quickly
    • mamāda: may I be filled or may I attain
    • nā: not
    • brahmāṇo: the Brahman or the ultimate reality
    • maghavānaṁ: the powerful one, often referring to Indra
    • sutāsaḥ: the sons or offspring
    • tasmā: therefore
    • ukthaṁ: the hymn or praise
    • janaye: may I generate or create
    • yaj: sacrifice or worship
    • jujoṣan: who is engaged in or who enjoys
    • nṛvan: of men or humanity
    • navīyaḥ: new or fresh
    • śṛṇavad: listening or hearing
    • yathā: as or in the way
    • naḥ: of us or our
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a desire for divine blessings and the attainment of spiritual fulfillment.
    • The speaker seeks the favor of Indra, the god of rain and thunder, who is often associated with strength and power.
    • The reference to "soma" indicates a longing for spiritual nourishment and enlightenment.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of engaging in rituals and sacrifices to connect with the divine.
    • It highlights the need for community and collective worship, as indicated by the mention of "nṛvan" (humanity).
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the practice of meditation and mindfulness, where individuals seek inner peace and connection with a higher power.
    • Community gatherings, such as festivals or religious ceremonies, reflect the collective aspect of worship mentioned in the mantra.
    • Engaging in acts of kindness and service to others can be seen as a form of sacrifice that aligns with the themes of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance and support in our lives.
    • It encourages individuals to engage in spiritual practices and community worship to foster a sense of connection and purpose.
    • In a broader sense, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine, urging us to listen and respond to the needs of ourselves and others.
