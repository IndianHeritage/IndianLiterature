इन्द्रो न यो महा कर्माणि चक्रिर हन्ता वृत्राणाम् असि सोम पूर्वित् |पैद्वो न हि त्वम् अहिनाम्नां हन्ता विश्वस्यासि सोम दस्योः ||
indro na yo mahā karmāṇi cakrir hantā vṛtrāṇām asi soma pūrbhit | paidvo na hi tvam ahināmnāṁ hantā viśvasyāsi soma dasyoḥ ||
  • Indro: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Na yo: Translates to "who does not," indicating a negation or absence of a certain quality or action.
  • Mahā: Means "great" or "mighty," emphasizing the significance of the actions being discussed.
  • Karmāṇi: Refers to "actions" or "deeds," highlighting the importance of one's actions in the world.
  • Cakrir: Means "performed" or "executed," indicating the act of carrying out actions.
  • Hantā: Translates to "destroyer" or "slayer," referring to the power to overcome obstacles or enemies.
  • Vṛtrāṇām: Refers to "Vritra," a serpent or dragon symbolizing chaos and obstacles that need to be overcome.
  • Asi: Means "you are," affirming the identity or role of the subject.
  • Soma: A sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing nourishment, vitality, and divine inspiration.
  • Pūrbhit: Means "filled" or "saturated," indicating abundance or completeness.
  • Paidvo: Refers to "the one who is powerful" or "the strong one," emphasizing strength.
  • Na hi: Translates to "not indeed," reinforcing the negation of a certain quality.
  • Tvam: Means "you," addressing the subject directly.
  • Ahināmnāṁ: Refers to "the serpents" or "the enemies," indicating those who oppose or challenge.
  • Viśvasyāsi: Means "you are of the universe," indicating a universal presence or significance.
  • Soma: Repeated here, reinforcing its importance as a source of strength and vitality.
  • Dasyoḥ: Refers to "the enemies" or "the foes," emphasizing the adversarial forces in life.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the power of Indra as a destroyer of obstacles and enemies, highlighting the significance of strong actions in overcoming challenges. In real life, this can be applied to situations where individuals face difficulties or adversities, encouraging them to harness their inner strength and take decisive actions to overcome these challenges. The invocation of Soma symbolizes the need for nourishment and vitality in this journey, reminding us that strength comes not only from within but also from the support and inspiration we receive from our surroundings. This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of courage, resilience, and the pursuit of greatness in the face of adversity.
