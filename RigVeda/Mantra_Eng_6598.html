मन्त्र: परि यो रश्मिना दिवो अन्तान ममे पृथिव्याः | उभे आ पप्रौ रोदसी महित्वा ||
pari yo raśminā divo 'ntān mame pṛthivyāḥ | ubhe ā paprau rodasī mahitvā ||
  • Mantra: "pari yo raśminā divo 'ntān mame pṛthivyāḥ | ubhe ā paprau rodasī mahitvā ||"
  • Translation: "He who has spread out the rays of the sky, may he bring to me the greatness of both the heavens and the earth."

  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • pari: around, encompassing
    • yo: who
    • raśminā: with rays, light
    • divo: of the sky, heaven
    • 'ntān: the ends, boundaries
    • mame: to me
    • pṛthivyāḥ: of the earth
    • ubhe: both
    • ā: towards
    • paprau: may he bring
    • rodasī: the two realms, heavens and earth
    • mahitvā: greatness, magnificence

  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra reflects a deep connection between the individual and the cosmos, emphasizing the importance of both the spiritual and material worlds.
    • It acknowledges the divine light that permeates the universe and seeks to harness that energy for personal growth and understanding.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be applied when seeking clarity and guidance in decision-making. For instance, when faced with a significant life choice, one might meditate on this mantra to invite wisdom from both the earthly and heavenly realms.
    • In moments of stress or confusion, reciting this mantra can serve as a reminder to look for balance and greatness in both personal and professional aspects of life.

  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra encapsulates the aspiration for a harmonious existence that draws from both the spiritual and material aspects of life.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that we are part of a larger universe, and by seeking the greatness of both the heavens and the earth, we can achieve a more fulfilling and enlightened life.
