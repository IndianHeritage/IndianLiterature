ओ चित् सखायं सख्याऽववृत्यां तिरः पुरू चिदर्णवं जगन्मान् | पितुर् नपातम् आ दधीत वेधाऽधि क्षमि प्रतरं दीध्यानाḥ ||
o cit sakhāyaṁ sakhyā vavṛtyāṁ tiraḥ purū cid arṇavaṁ jaganvān | pitur napātam ā dadhīta vedhā adhi kṣami prataraṁ dīdhyānaḥ ||
  • O Cit Sakhāyaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "O Consciousness, O Friend." Here, 'Cit' refers to consciousness or awareness, and 'Sakhāyaṁ' means friend or companion. This suggests a deep connection between the individual and the universal consciousness.
  • Sakhyā Vavṛtyāṁ: This translates to "may friendship grow." It emphasizes the importance of relationships and the nurturing of bonds among individuals, highlighting the value of companionship in life.
  • Tiraḥ Purū: This means "beyond the vastness." It indicates the idea of transcending limitations and exploring the infinite possibilities that lie beyond our immediate perception.
  • Cid Arṇavaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the ocean of consciousness." It symbolizes the depth and vastness of awareness, suggesting that consciousness is as expansive as the ocean, filled with potential and knowledge.
  • Jaganvān: This term means "the one who pervades the universe." It refers to the omnipresence of consciousness, indicating that the same consciousness exists in all beings and the universe itself.
  • Pitur Napātam: This translates to "the offspring of the father." It signifies the connection to lineage and heritage, emphasizing the importance of familial bonds and the wisdom passed down through generations.
  • Ā Dadhīta: This means "may he establish." It suggests a call to action, encouraging individuals to create and nurture their relationships and connections.
  • Vedhā Adhi Kṣami: This can be interpreted as "may we attain knowledge." It emphasizes the pursuit of wisdom and understanding, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and enlightenment.
  • Prataraṁ Dīdhyānaḥ: This translates to "with focused attention." It highlights the importance of mindfulness and concentration in achieving one's goals and understanding the deeper truths of existence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of consciousness, friendship, and the pursuit of knowledge. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing relationships. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as fostering friendships, building strong family ties, and pursuing personal growth through knowledge and mindfulness. By recognizing the vastness of consciousness and our connection to it, we can cultivate a deeper understanding of ourselves and the world around us, leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
