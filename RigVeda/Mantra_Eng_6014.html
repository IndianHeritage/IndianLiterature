गोभिर यद ईम अन्ये अस्मन् मृगं न व्रां मृगयन्ते |अभित्सरन्ति धेनुभिः ||
gobhir yad īm anye asman mṛgaṁ na vrā mṛgayante | abhitsaranti dhenubhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • gobhir: by the cows
    • yad: which
    • īm: we desire
    • anye: others
    • asman: us
    • mṛgaṁ: the deer
    • na: not
    • vrā: to hunt
    • mṛgayante: they hunt
    • abhitsaranti: they approach
    • dhenubhiḥ: with the cows
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the relationship between humans and nature, particularly focusing on the gentle and nurturing aspects of life.
    • The phrase "by the cows" signifies abundance and nourishment, as cows are often seen as symbols of wealth and sustenance in Vedic culture.
    • The desire for "the deer" can be interpreted as a longing for peace and harmony, as deer are often associated with gentleness and tranquility.
    • The line "they do not hunt" emphasizes a non-violent approach to life, advocating for coexistence rather than aggression.
    • The mention of "approaching with the cows" suggests that nurturing and caring for life leads to a more fulfilling existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern agriculture, sustainable farming practices that prioritize animal welfare and environmental health reflect the essence of this mantra.
    • Community initiatives that focus on wildlife conservation and protecting natural habitats resonate with the idea of not hunting or harming other beings.
    • In personal relationships, fostering compassion and understanding rather than competition aligns with the mantra's message of nurturing and peaceful coexistence.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of living in harmony with nature and each other, promoting values of compassion, sustainability, and non-violence.
    • Its significance is applicable in various aspects of life, from environmental conservation to personal relationships, encouraging a lifestyle that respects and nurtures all forms of life.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and communities can contribute to a more peaceful and sustainable world.
