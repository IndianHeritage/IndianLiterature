अभागः सन्न अप पaretो अस्मि तव क्रत्वा तविषस्य प्रचेतः |तं त्वा मन्यो अक्रतुर जिहीळाहं स्वा तनूर बलदेयाय मेहि ||
abhāgaḥ sann apa pareto asmi tava kratvā taviṣasya pracetaḥ | taṁ tvā manyo akratur jihīḻāhaṁ svā tanūr baladeyāya mehi ||
  • abhāgaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "without division" or "unbroken." It signifies unity and wholeness.
  • sann: Meaning "being" or "existing," it emphasizes the state of existence or presence.
  • apa: This word translates to "from" or "away from," indicating a separation or a source.
  • pareto: This can be understood as "beyond" or "further," suggesting a transcendence or going beyond limits.
  • asmi: Meaning "I am," it denotes self-identity and existence.
  • tava: This translates to "your," indicating possession or relation.
  • kratvā: Referring to "action" or "sacrifice," it highlights the importance of deeds and efforts.
  • taviṣasya: This term means "of strength" or "powerful," emphasizing the significance of strength in actions.
  • pracetaḥ: Meaning "intelligent" or "wise," it points to the necessity of wisdom in decision-making.
  • taṁ: This translates to "that," referring to a specific entity or concept.
  • tvā: Meaning "you," it indicates a direct address to a higher power or deity.
  • manyo: This term means "thoughtful" or "considerate," suggesting a reflective approach.
  • akratur: Referring to "not acting" or "without action," it highlights the importance of purposeful action.
  • jihīḻāhaṁ: This can be interpreted as "I wish" or "I desire," indicating a longing or aspiration.
  • svā: Meaning "my own" or "self," it emphasizes personal connection and ownership.
  • tanūr: This translates to "body" or "form," referring to the physical aspect of existence.
  • baladeyāya: Meaning "to strength" or "to power," it signifies a connection to strength and vitality.
  • mehi: This term means "may I have" or "let me attain," indicating a desire for attainment or achievement.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of unity, strength, and purposeful action. It emphasizes the importance of being whole and connected, while also recognizing the need for wisdom and strength in our actions. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork, where unity and collaboration lead to success, or in personal development, where one seeks to align their actions with their values and aspirations. The mantra serves as a reminder to act thoughtfully and with intention, striving for personal and collective growth.
