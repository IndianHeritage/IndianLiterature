पत्तो जगारा प्रत्यञ्चम् अत्ति शीरष्णा शिरः प्रति दधौ वरूथम् |आसीन ऊर्ध्वाम् उपसि क्षिणाति न्यङ्ग उत्तानाम् अन्व एति भूमिम् ||
patto jagāra pratyañcam atti śīrṣṇā śiraḥ prati dadhau varūtham | āsīna ūrdhvām upasi kṣiṇāti nyaṅṅ uttānām anv eti bhūmim ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • patto: The one who is connected or related.
    • jagāra: Awakened or enlightened.
    • pratyañcam: Towards the east or the direction of the rising sun.
    • atti: To attain or reach.
    • śīrṣṇā: With the head or the top.
    • śiraḥ: Head or consciousness.
    • prati: Towards or in relation to.
    • dadhau: Supported or upheld.
    • varūtham: The great or the vast.
    • āsīna: Sitting or being in a position.
    • ūrdhvām: Upwards or elevated.
    • upasi: Near or close to.
    • kṣiṇāti: Diminishes or reduces.
    • nyaṅṅ: Those who are upright or standing.
    • uttānām: Of the elevated or the exalted.
    • anv: Following or pursuing.
    • eti: Goes or moves towards.
    • bhūmim: The earth or the ground.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the connection between the enlightened individual and the universe.
    • The reference to the "east" symbolizes new beginnings, much like the rising sun heralds a new day.
    • It emphasizes the importance of consciousness and awareness, suggesting that true understanding comes from being grounded and connected to the earth.
    • The idea of "sitting upwards" can be interpreted as maintaining a positive and elevated state of mind, which is crucial for personal growth.
    • The mantra also highlights the importance of supporting one another in our journeys, as we all pursue our paths on this earth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, an enlightened leader who supports their team fosters a positive environment, leading to collective success.
    • In personal relationships, being aware and present with loved ones can strengthen bonds and create a supportive atmosphere.
    • Practicing mindfulness and meditation can help individuals connect with their inner selves, leading to personal enlightenment and growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of awareness, support, and connection in our lives.
    • It teaches us to be grounded while pursuing our aspirations and to uplift those around us.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal and communal harmony, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
