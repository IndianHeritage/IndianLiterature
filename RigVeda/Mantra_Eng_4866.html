मां काकाम्बीरमुद्वृहो वनस्पतिमशस्तीरविहिनीनशः |मोता सूरो अह एव चन ग्रिवा आदधते वेः ||
mā kākambīram ud vṛho vanaspatim aśastīr vi hi nīnaśaḥ | mota sūro aha evā cana grīvā ādadhate veḥ ||
  • mā kākambīram ud vṛho vanaspatim: This phrase can be interpreted as a plea to not let the "kākambīra" (often associated with a type of bird or a metaphor for something that causes disturbance) rise up and disturb the "vanaspatim" (the trees or nature). In real life, this can symbolize the importance of maintaining harmony in our environment and protecting nature from disruptive forces.
  • aśastīr vi hi nīnaśaḥ: This part suggests that those who are "aśastīr" (unworthy or harmful) should not be allowed to prevail. It emphasizes the need to keep negativity and harmful influences at bay. For example, in a workplace, this could relate to fostering a positive environment by addressing toxic behaviors or attitudes that can hinder productivity and morale.
  • mota sūro aha evā cana grīvā: Here, "mota sūro" can be interpreted as a call for strength and courage. "Grīvā" refers to the neck, which can symbolize the ability to hold one's head high. This can be related to personal empowerment and resilience in the face of challenges. For instance, when facing adversity, maintaining one's dignity and strength is crucial for overcoming obstacles.
  • ādadhate veḥ: This phrase can be understood as a call to action, urging individuals to take charge and uphold their values and principles. In a real-life scenario, this could relate to standing up for justice and integrity in various situations, whether in personal relationships or societal issues.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of protecting nature, fostering positive environments, maintaining personal strength, and taking action for justice. In today's world, where environmental issues, workplace toxicity, and social injustices are prevalent, the teachings of this mantra can guide individuals to create a more harmonious and just society. By embodying these principles, we can contribute to a better world for ourselves and future generations.
