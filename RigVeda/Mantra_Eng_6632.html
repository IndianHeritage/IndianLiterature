प्र सू न एत्व अध्वरॊऽग्ना देवेषु पूर्व्यः |आदित्येषु प्र वरुणे धृतव्रते मरुत्सु विश्वभानुषु ||
pra sū na etv adhvaro3 'gnā deveṣu pūrvyaḥ | ādityeṣu pra varuṇe dhṛtavrate marutsu viśvabhānuṣu ||
  • pra sū na etv adhvaro3 'gnā deveṣu pūrvyaḥ
    • pra sū: This can be interpreted as "to bring forth" or "to manifest." It signifies the act of creation or initiation.
    • na etv: This phrase can mean "not to be obstructed" or "not to be hindered." It emphasizes the importance of unobstructed progress.
    • adhvaro3 'gnā: "adhvara" refers to the ritual or sacrifice, and "agnā" relates to the divine fire or energy. Together, they highlight the significance of sacred rituals in connecting with the divine.
    • deveṣu pūrvyaḥ: This translates to "in the deities of the past." It suggests reverence for the ancient deities and the wisdom they impart.
  • ādityeṣu pra varuṇe dhṛtavrate marutsu viśvabhānuṣu
    • ādityeṣu: Refers to the Adityas, a group of solar deities. This signifies the importance of light, knowledge, and truth.
    • pra varuṇe: "Varuna" is the god of cosmic order and water. This indicates the need for balance and harmony in life.
    • dhṛtavrate: This means "with steadfast vows" or "with firm resolve." It emphasizes the importance of commitment and dedication in one's actions.
    • marutsu: Refers to the Maruts, the storm deities, symbolizing strength and power. This highlights the dynamic forces of nature and their influence on life.
    • viśvabhānuṣu: This translates to "in all beings" or "in the universal light." It signifies the interconnectedness of all life and the divine presence within it.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of rituals, commitment, and reverence for the divine forces that govern the universe. It teaches us to approach life with a sense of purpose and dedication, recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways:

  • In personal development, one can strive to manifest their goals with unwavering commitment, much like the steadfast vows mentioned in the mantra.
  • In community and environmental efforts, recognizing the balance and harmony represented by Varuna can inspire actions that promote sustainability and respect for nature.
  • In spiritual practices, engaging in rituals with sincerity can foster a deeper connection with the divine and enhance one's understanding of life's purpose.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the sacredness of life and the importance of living with intention and respect for the forces that shape our existence.
