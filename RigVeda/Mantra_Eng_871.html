क्षपो राजन् उता तमनाग्ने वस्तोर उतोषसः |स तिग्मजम्भ रक्षसो दह प्रति ||
kṣapo rājann uta tmanāgne vastor utoṣasaḥ | sa tigmajambha rakṣaso daha prati ||
  • kṣapo rājann: This phrase can be translated as "O King of the Dawn." Here, "kṣapo" refers to the dawn or the light of the morning, while "rājann" signifies a ruler or king. This invokes the idea of a powerful force that brings light and clarity, much like the dawn dispels darkness.
  • uta tmanāgne: This translates to "and with the self of Agni." "Agni" is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. The phrase suggests a connection to the divine energy that fuels life and creativity.
  • vastor utoṣasaḥ: This means "of the vastness and the light." "Vastor" refers to the vastness of the universe, while "utoṣasaḥ" relates to the light or brightness. Together, they emphasize the expansive nature of existence and the illumination that comes with understanding it.
  • sa tigmajambha rakṣaso: This translates to "he who fiercely protects from the demons." "Tigmajambha" refers to a fierce or powerful force, while "rakṣaso" means demons or negative energies. This part of the mantra calls upon the protective qualities of the divine to shield us from harmful influences.
  • daha prati: This means "burn away." It signifies the act of purification and transformation, suggesting that the divine fire (Agni) can burn away negativity and obstacles in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra "kṣapo rājann uta tmanāgne vastor utoṣasaḥ | sa tigmajambha rakṣaso daha prati ||" encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and protection. It emphasizes the importance of light, energy, and purification in overcoming darkness and negativity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during times of uncertainty or fear, invoking the protective energies of the universe to help navigate challenges. It serves as a reminder that, like the dawn, there is always hope and renewal, and that we can call upon divine forces to aid us in our journey towards clarity and strength.
