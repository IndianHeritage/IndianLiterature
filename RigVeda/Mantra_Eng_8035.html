विष्वस्मा इति स्वर्वृशे साधारणं रजस्तुरम् | गोपां ऋतस्य विर भरत ||
viśvasmā it svar dṛśe sādhāraṇaṁ rajasturam | gopām ṛtasya vir bharat ||
  • viśvasmā: This term can be translated as "from all" or "from the universe." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and elements in the cosmos.
  • it: This word means "here" or "thus," indicating a presence or a state of being.
  • svar: Translated as "light" or "heaven," it represents the divine or spiritual realm, often associated with knowledge and enlightenment.
  • dṛśe: This means "to see" or "to perceive," emphasizing the importance of awareness and understanding in our lives.
  • sādhāraṇaṁ: This term refers to "common" or "shared," suggesting a collective experience or understanding among all beings.
  • rajasturam: This can be interpreted as "the essence of activity" or "the principle of action," highlighting the dynamic nature of existence.
  • gopām: This word means "protector" or "guardian," often associated with the divine or higher powers that safeguard the universe.
  • ṛtasya: Translated as "truth" or "cosmic order," it signifies the fundamental principles that govern the universe and our lives.
  • vir bharat: This phrase means "may you be filled with strength" or "may you be empowered," calling for the infusion of vitality and energy into our actions and endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of awareness and action in our lives. It calls for a collective understanding of the cosmic order and encourages individuals to seek enlightenment and strength. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as community building, environmental conservation, and personal growth. By recognizing our shared existence and striving for truth and action, we can create a harmonious and empowered society.
