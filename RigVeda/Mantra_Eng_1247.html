यावयद्वेषा ऋतपाऽ ऋतेजाः सुम्नावरī सूनृताऽ इरयन्ती |सुमङ्गलीर बिभ्रती देववीतिम इहाद्योषः श्रेष्टतमā व्य उच् छ ||
yāvayaddveṣā ṛtapā ṛtejāḥ sumnāvarī sūnṛtā īrayantī | sumaṅgalīr bibhratī devavītim ihādyoṣaḥ śreṣṭhatamā vy uccha ||
  • yāvayaddveṣā: This term can be broken down into "yāva" meaning "as long as" and "dveṣā" meaning "hatred" or "enmity". It suggests that as long as there is hatred, certain conditions prevail.
  • ṛtapā: This word refers to "truth" or "righteousness". It signifies the importance of living in accordance with universal laws and principles.
  • ṛtejāḥ: This can be interpreted as "radiance" or "brilliance\ often associated with the divine or spiritual enlightenment.
  • sumnāvarī: This term can be understood as "auspiciousness" or "good fortune\ indicating a state of positivity and well-being.
  • sūnṛtā: This word means "truthful" or "honest\ emphasizing the value of integrity in one's actions and words.
  • īrayantī: This can be translated as "they who inspire" or "they who lead\ referring to those who guide others towards righteousness and truth.
  • sumaṅgalīr: This term means "the one who brings good fortune" or "auspicious\ highlighting the importance of positive influences in life.
  • bibhratī: This means "holding" or "bearing\ suggesting the responsibility of maintaining auspiciousness and truthfulness.
  • devavītim: This can be interpreted as "divine qualities" or "attributes of the gods\ indicating the aspiration to embody divine virtues.
  • ihādyoṣaḥ: This phrase means "in this world" or "in this life\ emphasizing the relevance of these qualities in our current existence.
  • śreṣṭhatamā: This term means "the best" or "the highest\ indicating the ultimate goal of striving for excellence in character and conduct.
  • vy uccha: This can be understood as "may it be elevated" or "may it rise\ suggesting a prayer or wish for the elevation of these qualities in oneself and society.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of overcoming hatred and embracing truth, righteousness, and auspiciousness in our lives. It serves as a reminder that by embodying these divine qualities, we can inspire others and create a positive environment. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal relationships, community interactions, and even in professional settings. By fostering an atmosphere of truthfulness and positivity, we contribute to a more harmonious and prosperous society. The significance of this mantra lies in its call to action for individuals to strive for excellence in character and to uplift those around them, ultimately leading to a better world.
