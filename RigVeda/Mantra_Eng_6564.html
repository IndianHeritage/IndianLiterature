उपो हरिणाम् पतिं दक्षं पृñचन्तम् अब्रवम् |नूनं श्रुधि स्तुवतो अश्व्यस्य ||
upo harīṇām patiṁ dakṣam pṛñcantam abravam | nūnaṁ śrudhi stuvato aśvyasya ||
  • Upo harīṇām: This phrase can be translated as "to the lord of the horses" or "to the master of the steeds." In Vedic literature, horses symbolize strength, speed, and vitality. They are often associated with the sun and the divine.
  • Patiṁ dakṣam: "Pati" means "lord" or "master," and "dakṣam" means "skilled" or "capable." Together, this phrase refers to a capable and skilled master, highlighting the importance of leadership qualities in guiding others.
  • Pṛñcantam abravam: "Pṛñcantam" can be interpreted as "one who is being praised" or "the one who is being invoked." "Abravam" means "I spoke" or "I declared." This suggests a call to acknowledge and praise the capable leader or master.
  • Nūnaṁ śrudhi: "Nūnaṁ" translates to "indeed" or "certainly," while "śrudhi" means "listen" or "hear." This phrase emphasizes the importance of paying attention to the words of praise and acknowledgment being offered.
  • Stuvato aśvyasya: "Stuvato" means "of the one who is being praised," and "aśvyasya" refers to "of the horses" or "of the steed." This indicates that the praise is directed towards the master of the horses, reinforcing the connection between leadership and strength.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of recognizing and praising capable leadership, particularly in contexts that require strength and guidance. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various fields such as business, sports, and community leadership. A skilled leader, much like a master of horses, can guide their team towards success, harnessing the strengths of each individual. The mantra serves as a reminder to acknowledge and support those who lead with skill and capability, fostering an environment of respect and collaboration.
