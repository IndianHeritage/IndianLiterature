अध्वर्यवो यः शतं शम्बरस्य पुरो बिभेदाश्मनेव पूर्वीः |यो वर्चिनः शतम इन्द्रः सहस्रम अपावपद भरतā सोमम अस्मै ||
adhvaryavo yaḥ śataṁ śambarasya puro bibhedāśmaneva pūrvīḥ | yo varcinaḥ śatam indraḥ sahasram apāvapad bharatā somam asmai ||
  • adhvaryavo: This term refers to the priests who perform the rituals in the Vedic tradition. They are responsible for the meticulous execution of the sacrificial rites.
  • yaḥ: This means "who" and introduces the subject of the mantra.
  • śataṁ: This translates to "hundred," indicating a large quantity or multitude.
  • śambarasya: Refers to "of the Shambara," which can symbolize a powerful entity or force, often associated with challenges or obstacles.
  • puro: This means "in front" or "before," suggesting a position of prominence or priority.
  • bibheda: This means "he broke" or "he pierced," indicating an action of overcoming or conquering.
  • āśmaneva: This translates to "like the stone," suggesting strength and resilience.
  • pūrvīḥ: This means "the earlier ones" or "the predecessors," indicating a connection to tradition or history.
  • yo: This means "who," again referring to the subject of the mantra.
  • varcinaḥ: This translates to "of the radiant" or "of the brilliant," often associated with divine qualities.
  • śatam: Again, this means "hundred," reinforcing the idea of abundance.
  • indraḥ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing power and authority.
  • sahasram: This means "thousand," indicating an even greater abundance or strength.
  • apāvapad: This translates to "he who removes obstacles," emphasizing the role of divine intervention in overcoming challenges.
  • bharatā: This term can refer to the people of Bharat (India) or the concept of unity and strength among them.
  • somam: Refers to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing nourishment and divine bliss.
  • asmai: This means "to him" or "to that one," indicating a dedication or offering to the divine.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the power of ritual and the role of divine forces in overcoming obstacles. It highlights the importance of tradition, community, and the pursuit of strength and abundance. In real life, this can be applied to various situations where individuals or communities face challenges, reminding them to seek strength through unity, tradition, and divine support. Whether in personal struggles, community efforts, or larger societal challenges, the essence of this mantra encourages resilience and the pursuit of collective well-being.
