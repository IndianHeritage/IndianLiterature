उत ते बृहन्तो अर्चयः समिधानस्य दीदिवः |अग्नि शुक्रास इरते ||
ut te bṛhanto arcayaḥ samidhānasya dīdivaḥ | agne śukrāsa īrate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ut: O
    • te: your
    • bṛhanto: great, powerful
    • arcayaḥ: we worship, we invoke
    • samidhānasya: of the one who is kindled, of the one who is offered
    • dīdivaḥ: shining, radiant
    • agne: O Agni, the fire god
    • śukrāsa: pure, bright
    • īrate: may you be pleased, may you accept
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, invoking his greatness and purity. It expresses a desire for Agni to accept the offerings made to him and to shine brightly in response to the worship.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, Agni is often invoked during ceremonies such as weddings and yajnas (sacrificial offerings). The fire symbolizes purity and the divine presence, and offerings are made to seek blessings.
    • In a modern context, the concept of 'fire' can be seen in the passion and dedication people have towards their work or goals. Just as Agni is invoked for blessings, individuals often seek inspiration and motivation to achieve their aspirations.
  • Significance and Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine energies in our lives. It teaches us to recognize and honor the forces that guide and support us.
    • In daily life, this can be applied by acknowledging the efforts of those who help us, whether it be family, friends, or mentors, and expressing gratitude for their contributions.
    • Furthermore, it encourages individuals to cultivate purity in their thoughts and actions, aligning with the qualities of Agni, which can lead to personal growth and fulfillment.
