तनूनापात पथ ऋतस्य यानान मध्वा समञ्जन स्वदया सुजिह्वा |मन्मानि धीभिर उता यज्ञं ऋन्धन देवत्रा च कृतुहि अध्वरं नः ||
tanūnapāt patha ṛtasya yānān madhvā samañjan svadayā sujihva | manmāni dhībhir uta yajñam ṛndhan devatrā ca kṛṇuhy adhvaraṁ naḥ ||
  • tanūnapāt: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is born from the body" or "the one who is connected to the physical form." It signifies the importance of the physical body in spiritual practices and rituals.
  • patha: This word means "path" or "way." It represents the journey one takes in life, emphasizing the significance of choosing the right path in both spiritual and worldly matters.
  • ṛtasya: This term refers to "truth" or "cosmic order." It highlights the importance of living in harmony with the universal laws and principles that govern existence.
  • yānān: This word means "vehicles" or "means of transport." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent the various methods or practices one employs to achieve spiritual goals.
  • madhvā: This term translates to "sweetness" or "honey." It symbolizes the sweetness of life and the rewards of following the righteous path.
  • samañjan: This word can be interpreted as "to bring together" or "to unite." It signifies the importance of unity and collaboration in achieving common goals.
  • svadayā: This term means "with one's own effort." It emphasizes the necessity of personal effort and dedication in spiritual pursuits.
  • sujihva: This word translates to "well-wishing" or "auspicious." It indicates the importance of positive intentions and goodwill in one's actions.
  • manmāni: This term means "my mind" or "my thoughts." It signifies the role of the mind in shaping one's reality and experiences.
  • dhībhir: This word translates to "with wisdom" or "with understanding." It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and discernment in decision-making.
  • uta: This term means "and" or "also," indicating a connection between ideas or concepts.
  • yajñam: This word refers to "sacrifice" or "offering." It highlights the significance of selflessness and giving in spiritual practices.
  • ṛndhan: This term means "to kindle" or "to ignite." It symbolizes the act of awakening one's spiritual potential.
  • devatrā: This word translates to "with the gods" or "divine." It signifies the connection between human efforts and divine support.
  • kṛṇuhy: This term means "to create" or "to establish." It emphasizes the act of bringing forth new possibilities through one's actions.
  • adhvaraṁ: This word refers to "the ritual" or "the sacred act." It highlights the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
  • naḥ: This term means "for us" or "to us," indicating a plea for divine assistance and blessings.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of aligning one's physical existence with the cosmic order through conscious choices and actions. It encourages individuals to embark on a righteous path, utilizing their own efforts and wisdom while fostering unity and goodwill. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, community building, and spiritual practices. By embodying the principles of truth, sacrifice, and divine connection, individuals can create a harmonious and fulfilling life, ultimately contributing to the greater good of society.
