त्वं अग्नि यज्यवे पायुर अन्तरोऽनिषङ्गाय चतुरक्ष इध्यसे |यो रातहव्योऽवृकाय धायसे कीरेश चिन् मन्त्रं मनसा वनोषि तं ||
tvam agne yajyave pāyur antaro 'niṣaṅgāya caturakṣa idhyase | yo rātahavyo 'vṛkāya dhāyase kīreś cin mantram manasā vanoṣi tam ||
  • tvam agne: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni." Agni is the fire god in Vedic literature, symbolizing energy, transformation, and purification. In real life, fire is essential for cooking, warmth, and various industrial processes, representing the transformative power of energy.
  • yajyave: This means "worthy of worship." It emphasizes the importance of reverence and respect towards divine forces. In everyday life, this can be seen in how people honor their traditions and values, whether through rituals, community gatherings, or personal practices that foster a sense of connection to something greater.
  • pāyur antaro 'niṣaṅgāya: This translates to "may you protect us from within." It signifies the need for inner strength and protection. In a real-life context, this can relate to mental and emotional resilience, where individuals seek inner peace and strength to navigate life's challenges.
  • caturakṣa: Meaning "four-eyed," this term symbolizes vigilance and awareness. In practical terms, it can refer to being observant and mindful in our daily lives, ensuring we are aware of our surroundings and the impact of our actions on others.
  • idhyase: This means "you are invoked." It highlights the act of calling upon divine energy or support. In real life, this can be seen in moments of prayer, meditation, or seeking guidance during difficult times, where individuals look for a higher power to assist them.
  • yo rātahavyo 'vṛkāya dhāyase: This translates to "who moves swiftly like the wind." It signifies agility and adaptability. In today's fast-paced world, being able to adapt to change and move quickly in response to new situations is crucial for success in both personal and professional realms.
  • kīreś: This means "the lord of glory." It represents the pursuit of excellence and honor. In real life, this can be reflected in striving for personal achievements, recognition in one's field, or contributing positively to society.
  • cin mantram manasā vanoṣi tam: This translates to "may you inspire us with your mantra." It emphasizes the importance of inspiration and guidance. In practical terms, this can relate to the influence of mentors, leaders, or even literature that inspires individuals to pursue their goals and dreams.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, protection, and inspiration in our lives. It highlights the importance of inner strength, awareness, and adaptability in navigating the complexities of life. By invoking the qualities of Agni, individuals can strive for transformation, honor their values, and seek inspiration to achieve their goals. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine and the human experience, encouraging individuals to cultivate a sense of reverence and purpose in their daily lives.
