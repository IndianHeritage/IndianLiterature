आयम् उष्य प्र देवयुर होतā यज्ञाय नीयते |रथो न योर अभिवृतो घृणीवान् चेतति त्मना ||
ayam u ṣya pra devayur hotā yajñāya nīyate | ratho na yor abhīvṛto ghṛṇīvāñ cetati tmanā ||
  • ayam uṣya pra devayur hotā yajñāya nīyate
    • ayam - This means "this" or "this one," indicating a specific focus or subject.
    • uṣya - Refers to "dawn" or "morning," symbolizing new beginnings and enlightenment.
    • pra - Means "before" or "in front of," suggesting a sense of priority or importance.
    • devayur - Translates to "divine life" or "life of the gods," indicating a connection to the divine or spiritual realm.
    • hotā - Refers to the "priest" or "one who invokes," emphasizing the role of a mediator between the divine and the earthly.
    • yajñāya - Means "for the sacrifice" or "for the offering," highlighting the importance of rituals and offerings in spiritual practices.
    • nīyate - Translates to "is led" or "is directed," indicating guidance or a path being taken.
  • ratho na yor abhīvṛto ghṛṇīvāñ cetati tmanā
    • ratho - Means "chariot," symbolizing movement, progress, and the journey of life.
    • na - Translates to "not," indicating negation or absence.
    • yor - Refers to "of those," indicating a connection to a group or community.
    • abhīvṛto - Means "surrounded" or "enveloped," suggesting protection or being encompassed by something.
    • ghṛṇīvāñ - Refers to "one who is compassionate" or "kind-hearted," emphasizing the importance of empathy and kindness.
    • cetati - Means "thinks" or "reflects," indicating contemplation or awareness.
    • tmnā - Refers to "the self" or "soul," emphasizing the inner self and personal identity.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of spiritual guidance and the role of rituals in connecting with the divine. It highlights the journey of life, the need for compassion, and the significance of self-reflection. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways:

  • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their actions and intentions, seeking to align them with their higher purpose.
  • In community settings, the mantra encourages compassion and kindness towards others, fostering a supportive environment.
  • In spiritual practices, it underscores the importance of rituals and offerings as a means to connect with the divine and seek guidance.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of compassion, and the pursuit of spiritual growth.
