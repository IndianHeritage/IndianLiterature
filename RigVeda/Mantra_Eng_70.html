इन्द्रं वो विश्वतस् परि हवामहे जनैभ्यः |अस्माकं अस्तु केवलः ||
indraṁ vo viśvatas pari havāmahe janebhyaḥ | asmākam astu kevalaḥ ||
  • Indraṁ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • vo: Means "to you," indicating a direction of reverence and devotion towards Indra.
  • viśvatas: Translates to "from all sides" or "universally," suggesting an all-encompassing approach.
  • pari: Means "around" or "surrounding," indicating a holistic perspective.
  • havāmahe: Translates to "we invoke" or "we offer," signifying a call to action or a plea for assistance.
  • janebhyaḥ: Means "from the people" or "to the people," emphasizing community and collective effort.
  • asmākam: Means "our," indicating possession or belonging.
  • astu: Translates to "let it be" or "may it be," expressing a wish or desire.
  • kevalaḥ: Means "only" or "exclusive," suggesting a singular focus or intention.

The mantra can be interpreted as a collective invocation to Indra, asking for his blessings and support from all directions, emphasizing the importance of community and unity in seeking strength and guidance. It reflects a desire for Indra's exclusive favor upon the people, highlighting the significance of divine assistance in achieving collective goals.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Community Gatherings: When people come together for a common purpose, such as festivals or social events, invoking a higher power can foster unity and strength among participants.
  • Leadership and Governance: Leaders can draw inspiration from this mantra to seek guidance and support from the community they serve, ensuring that their actions benefit the collective.
  • Teamwork in Organizations: In a corporate setting, teams can use this mantra to emphasize collaboration and collective effort, invoking a spirit of unity to achieve shared objectives.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on collective strength, community, and the importance of seeking divine guidance. It serves as a reminder that in our endeavors, whether personal or communal, invoking support from a higher power can lead to greater success and harmony. By fostering unity and collaboration, we can achieve our goals more effectively, making this mantra relevant in various aspects of life.
