न स राजा व्यथते यस्मिन्न इन्द्रस तीव्रं सोमं पिबति गोसखायम् |आ सत्वनैर अजति हन्ति वृत्रं क्षेति क्षितीः सुभागो नाम पुष्यन् ||
na sa rājā vyathate yasminn indras tīvraṁ somam pibati gosakhāyam | ā satvanair ajati hanti vṛtraṁ kṣeti kṣitīḥ subhago nāma puṣyan ||
  • Translation: "The king does not suffer who drinks the potent soma, the one who is a friend to the cows. He, with his strong powers, slays Vritra and establishes the earth; he is known by the name of the auspicious Pushan."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • na sa rājā vyathate: "the king does not suffer" - This implies that a wise and just ruler remains unaffected by troubles.
    • yasminn indras tīvraṁ somam pibati: "who drinks the potent soma" - Soma is a sacred drink that symbolizes divine inspiration and strength, associated with Indra, the king of gods.
    • gosakhāyam: "the one who is a friend to the cows" - Cows are revered in Vedic culture, symbolizing wealth and nourishment.
    • ā satvanair ajati: "with his strong powers" - Refers to the strength and capabilities of the ruler.
    • hanti vṛtraṁ: "slays Vritra" - Vritra represents obstacles or adversities; overcoming them is essential for prosperity.
    • kṣeti kṣitīḥ: "establishes the earth" - This signifies the ruler's role in maintaining order and stability in society.
    • subhago nāma puṣyan: "known by the name of the auspicious Pushan" - Pushan is a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity, indicating the ruler's benevolent nature.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • A leader who prioritizes the welfare of their community, ensuring that resources are shared equitably, can be seen as embodying the qualities of the king described in the mantra.
    • In modern governance, policies that promote agricultural sustainability and protect livestock reflect the idea of being a "friend to the cows."
    • Business leaders who navigate challenges and adversities, akin to slaying Vritra, can inspire their teams and foster a resilient organizational culture.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the qualities of a just and effective leader who draws strength from divine inspiration and acts for the welfare of all. In real life, it serves as a reminder for leaders to remain steadfast in the face of challenges, prioritize the well-being of their community, and strive for prosperity and harmony. This mantra can be applied in various contexts, from political leadership to corporate governance, highlighting the timeless relevance of these principles in fostering a just and prosperous society.
