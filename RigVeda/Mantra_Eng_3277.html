त्वं महीम अवनिं विश्वधेनां तुर्वीतये वय्याय क्षरन्तीम् |अरमयो नमसैजद अर्णः सुतराणाम् अकृत्नोर इन्द्र सिंधून् ||
tvam mahīm avaniṁ viśvadhenāṁ turvītaye vayyāya kṣarantīm | aramayo namasaijad arṇaḥ sutaraṇām̐ akṛṇor indra sindhūn ||
  • tvam mahīm avaniṁ viśvadhenāṁ turvītaye vayyāya kṣarantīm
    • tvam - "You" or "Thou": This refers to a divine entity or force, often interpreted as a deity or the universal spirit.
    • mahīm - "greatness" or "earth": This signifies the vastness and the nurturing aspect of the earth.
    • avaniṁ - "earth" or "ground": It emphasizes the physical realm we inhabit.
    • viśvadhenāṁ - "all-encompassing" or "universal knowledge": This suggests a connection to the wisdom that transcends individual understanding.
    • turvītaye - "for the purpose of strength" or "to empower": This indicates a call for strength and support in endeavors.
    • vayyāya - "to the one who is worthy" or "to the deserving": This highlights the importance of merit and righteousness.
    • kṣarantīm - "flowing" or "moving": This can symbolize the dynamic nature of life and the universe.
  • aramayo namasaijad arṇaḥ sutaraṇām̐ akṛṇor indra sindhūn
    • aramayo - "to be revered" or "worthy of worship": This indicates the divine nature of the subject being addressed.
    • namasaijad - "with reverence" or "with devotion": This emphasizes the importance of humility and respect in spiritual practices.
    • arṇaḥ - "ocean" or "vastness": This symbolizes the infinite and boundless nature of existence.
    • sutaraṇām̐ - "of the well-constructed" or "of the strong": This refers to the strength and stability found in the divine or in nature.
    • akṛṇor - "who has not created" or "who is unbound": This suggests a sense of freedom from limitations.
    • indra - "Indra": The king of gods in Vedic tradition, representing power, strength, and leadership.
    • sindhūn - "rivers" or "streams": This symbolizes the flow of life, knowledge, and resources.

Conclusion:

This mantra encapsulates the essence of reverence towards the divine and the interconnectedness of all life. It emphasizes the importance of strength, wisdom, and humility in our pursuits. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, recognizing the need for strength and wisdom in overcoming challenges.
  • In environmental consciousness, understanding our connection to the earth and the importance of nurturing it.
  • In community building, fostering respect and reverence for each individual's contributions and strengths.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives and the importance of living with purpose, respect, and interconnectedness.
