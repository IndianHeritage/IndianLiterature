प्रति ष्टोभन्ति सिन्धवः पविभ्यो यद अभ्रियां वाचम उदीरयन्ति |अव स्मयन्त विद्युताः पृथिव्यां यदी घृतं मरुतः पृथ्नुवन्ति ||
prati ṣṭobhanti sindhavaḥ pavibhyo yad abhriyāṁ vācam udīrayanti | ava smayanta vidyutaḥ pṛthivyāṁ yadī ghṛtam marutaḥ pruṣṇuvanti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prati: towards
    • ṣṭobhanti: they make to resound
    • sindhavaḥ: the rivers
    • pavibhyo: from the winds
    • yad: that which
    • abhriyāṁ: in the beautiful
    • vācam: voice
    • udīrayanti: they raise up
    • ava: down
    • smayanta: they smile
    • vidyutaḥ: the lightning
    • pṛthivyāṁ: on the earth
    • yadī: if
    • ghṛtam: the clarified butter
    • marutaḥ: the winds
    • pruṣṇuvanti: they produce
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the interconnectedness of nature, where rivers resonate with the winds, and the beauty of sound is raised up.
    • The rivers, symbolizing life and nourishment, are influenced by the winds, which can be seen as the forces of change and movement in our lives.
    • The mention of lightning and earth signifies the powerful forces of nature that bring about transformation and renewal.
    • The reference to clarified butter (ghṛtam) can be interpreted as the essence of purity and nourishment, which the winds (marutaḥ) help to manifest.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the relationship between rivers and winds is crucial. Rivers provide water, while winds help in pollination and seed dispersal, demonstrating the harmony of nature.
    • In personal life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of how external influences (like friends or mentors) can uplift our voices and aspirations, much like the winds uplift the sounds of the rivers.
    • During storms, the interplay of lightning and earth can be a metaphor for challenges in life that lead to growth and renewal, akin to how rain nourishes the earth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of harmony in nature and life, illustrating how different elements work together to create beauty and sustenance.
    • It serves as a reminder to appreciate the interconnectedness of all things and the importance of external influences in our personal growth.
    • In a broader context, it encourages us to recognize the forces that shape our lives and to embrace the changes they bring, much like the rivers and winds that shape the landscape.
