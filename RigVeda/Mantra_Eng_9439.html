इन्द्रेण युजा निः सृजन्त वाघतो व्रजं गोमन्तम् अश्विनम् |सहस्रं मे ददतो अष्टकर्ण्यः श्रवो देवेष्व अकृत ||
indreṇa yujā niḥ sṛjanta vāghato vrajaṁ gomantam aśvinam | sahasram me dadato aṣṭakarṇya1ḥ śravo deveṣv akrata ||
  • Indreṇa: This refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength.
  • Yujā: This means to join or unite, indicating a connection or collaboration.
  • Niḥ sṛjanta: This translates to "they who release" or "they who let go," suggesting a sense of liberation or creation.
  • Vāghato: This term can be interpreted as "the one who speaks" or "the voice," often associated with communication or expression.
  • Vrajaṁ: This means "the herd" or "the group," often referring to a community or collective.
  • Gomantam: This translates to "the one who possesses cows," symbolizing wealth, prosperity, and sustenance.
  • Aśvinam: Referring to the Aśvins, the twin gods of medicine and health, representing healing and well-being.
  • Sahasram: This means "thousand," indicating abundance or a large quantity.
  • Me: This translates to "my," indicating a personal connection or ownership.
  • Dadato: This means "giving" or "bestowing," suggesting generosity or the act of sharing.
  • Aṣṭakarṇyaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "having eight ears," symbolizing the ability to listen and understand deeply.
  • Śravo: This translates to "fame" or "glory," indicating recognition and respect.
  • Deveṣv: This means "among the gods," suggesting a divine context or connection.
  • Akrata: This translates to "not done" or "not created," indicating a state of potential or unfulfilled action.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, communication, and the sharing of resources within a community. It highlights the role of divine forces in bestowing abundance and prosperity, as well as the significance of listening and understanding in achieving collective goals. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as teamwork in a workplace, community service initiatives, or family gatherings where collaboration and mutual support lead to greater success and harmony. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine, encouraging us to work together for the common good while being receptive to the needs and voices of others.
