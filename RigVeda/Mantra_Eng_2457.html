इमं यज्ञं सहसावन त्वं नो देवत्रा धेहि सुक्रतो रराणः |प्र यंसी होतर बृहतीर इषो नो 'अग्नि महि द्रविणम आ यजस्व ||
imaṁ yajñaṁ sahasāvan tvaṁ no devatrā dhehi sukrato rarāṇaḥ | pra yaṁsi hotar bṛhatīr iṣo no 'gne mahi draviṇam ā yajasva ||
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "O Divine One, we offer this sacrifice to you with great devotion. May you, the deity, grant us the strength to perform our duties and may our offerings reach you, O Agni, the great priest of the sacrifice, who brings us wealth."
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imaṁ yajñaṁ: this sacrifice
    • sahasāvan: with great devotion
    • tvaṁ: you
    • no: to us
    • devatrā: O deity
    • dhehi: grant
    • sukrato: strength to perform good deeds
    • rarāṇaḥ: to perform
    • pra: may
    • yaṁsi: you, the priest
    • hotar: the one who invokes
    • bṛhatīr: great
    • iṣo: of the sacrifice
    • no: to us
    • agne: O Agni
    • mahi: great
    • draviṇam: wealth
    • ā: to
    • yajasva: may you accept
  • Context and Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of devotion and the act of offering. In real life, this can be seen in various cultures where people perform rituals or sacrifices to express gratitude or seek blessings. For example, during festivals like Diwali in India, people perform pujas (rituals) to invite prosperity and well-being into their homes.
    • The invocation of Agni as the great priest signifies the role of intermediaries in our lives. Just as Agni is believed to carry offerings to the divine, in modern contexts, we often rely on mentors, leaders, or spiritual guides to help us navigate our paths and achieve our goals.
    • The call for strength to perform good deeds resonates with the idea of personal responsibility. In everyday life, this can be reflected in community service, where individuals come together to help those in need, embodying the spirit of selflessness and duty.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for devotion, the importance of performing good deeds, and the acknowledgment of divine support in our endeavors. It teaches us that through sincere offerings and actions, we can seek blessings and prosperity in our lives. In a broader sense, it encourages us to cultivate a sense of responsibility towards our community and the world, reminding us that our actions can have a profound impact on the collective well-being.
