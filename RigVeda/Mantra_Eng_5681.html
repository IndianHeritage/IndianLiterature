सुप्रवीर अस्तु स क्षयः प्र नु यामन सुदानवः |ये नो अंहो 'तिपिप्रति ||
suprāvīr astu sa kṣayaḥ pra nu yāman sudānavaḥ | ye no aṁho 'tipiprati ||
  • Mantra: "suprāvīr astu sa kṣayaḥ pra nu yāman sudānavaḥ | ye no aṁho 'tipiprati ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • suprāvīr: May there be great strength or valor.
    • astu: Let it be or may it exist.
    • sa: That or he.
    • kṣayaḥ: Destruction or loss.
    • pra: Forth or forward.
    • nu: Indeed or surely.
    • yāman: Those who are strong or powerful.
    • sudānavaḥ: Those who are well-versed or knowledgeable.
    • ye: Who.
    • no: Our.
    • aṁho: Of the heavens or celestial.
    • 'tipiprati: May they not be obstructed or hindered.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the idea of strength and valor, suggesting that one should possess great courage and resilience in the face of challenges.
    • The phrase "may there be great strength" emphasizes the importance of inner strength and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • It also highlights the significance of knowledge and wisdom, as indicated by "those who are well-versed," suggesting that true power comes from understanding and insight.
    • The mantra calls for the removal of obstacles, indicating a desire for clarity and success in endeavors.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual facing a challenging project may recite this mantra to invoke the strength and knowledge needed to succeed.
    • A student preparing for exams might reflect on the mantra to cultivate resilience and focus, ensuring they are well-prepared and confident.
    • In personal relationships, this mantra can serve as a reminder to approach conflicts with courage and wisdom, fostering understanding and resolution.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for strength, knowledge, and the removal of obstacles, making it applicable in various aspects of life.
    • It encourages individuals to harness their inner power and wisdom to navigate challenges effectively.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can cultivate resilience and clarity, leading to personal and professional growth.
