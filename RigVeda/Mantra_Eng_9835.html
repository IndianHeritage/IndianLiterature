क्राणā रुद्रā मरुतो विश्वकृष्टयो दिवः श्येनासो असुरस्य नीलयः |तेभिś चषṭे वरुणो मित्रो आर्यमेन्द्रो देवैभिर अर्वशैभिर अर्वशः ||
krāṇā rudrā maruto viśvakṛṣṭayo divaḥ śyenāso asurasya nīḻayaḥ | tebhiś caṣṭe varuṇo mitro aryamendro devebhir arvaśebhir arvaśaḥ ||
  • Translation: This mantra from the Rigveda invokes various deities, particularly Rudra and the Maruts, and speaks of their divine qualities and powers. It highlights the interconnectedness of nature and the divine, emphasizing the importance of harmony and balance.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • krāṇā: To create or to manifest.
    • rudrā: Referring to Rudra, a fierce form of Shiva, associated with storms and the hunt.
    • maruto: The Maruts, storm deities, representing the forces of nature.
    • viśvakṛṣṭayo: All-encompassing or universal.
    • divaḥ: Of the heavens or celestial.
    • śyenāso: Referring to the eagle, symbolizing strength and vision.
    • asurasya: Of the asuras, often interpreted as powerful beings or deities.
    • nīḻayaḥ: Abode or dwelling place.
    • tebhiś: With them or by them.
    • caṣṭe: To move or to act.
    • varuṇo: Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water.
    • mitro: Mitra, the god of friendship and contracts.
    • aryamendro: Aryaman, a deity associated with social order and hospitality.
    • devebhir: By the gods.
    • arvaśebhir: With all beings or creatures.
    • arvaśaḥ: All-encompassing or universal.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The invocation of Rudra and the Maruts can be seen in modern rituals where people seek protection and blessings from natural forces, such as during monsoon seasons when storms are prevalent.
    • The concept of harmony with nature is applicable in environmental movements today, where individuals and communities strive to live sustainably and respect the natural world.
    • In personal relationships, the qualities of Mitra (friendship) and Aryaman (hospitality) can be reflected in how we treat others, fostering a sense of community and support.
  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine forces in maintaining balance and harmony in the universe. It serves as a reminder of our connection to nature and the importance of respecting and honoring the elements around us. In real life, it encourages us to cultivate relationships based on friendship, hospitality, and mutual respect, while also advocating for a sustainable and harmonious existence with the environment.
