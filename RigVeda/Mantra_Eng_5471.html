शं नः सोमो भवतु ब्रह्म शं नः शं नो ग्रावाणः शमु सन्तु यज्ञाः |शं नः स्वरुणाम् मित्रयो भवन्तु शं नः प्रस्वः शम वस्तु वेदिः ||
śaṁ naḥ somo bhavatu brahma śaṁ naḥ śaṁ no grāvāṇaḥ śam u santu yajñāḥ | śaṁ naḥ svarūṇām mitayo bhavantu śaṁ naḥ prasva1ḥ śam v astu vediḥ ||
  • śaṁ naḥ somo bhavatu brahma: This phrase translates to "May the Soma (the divine nectar) be auspicious for us, may it be Brahman (the ultimate reality)." Soma is often associated with immortality and divine inspiration in Vedic literature. In real life, this can be interpreted as a desire for spiritual nourishment and enlightenment.
  • śaṁ naḥ śaṁ no grāvāṇaḥ: This means "May auspiciousness be ours, may it be our protector." Here, the emphasis is on seeking protection and well-being. In everyday life, this can relate to the importance of community and support systems that help individuals thrive.
  • śam u santu yajñāḥ: This translates to "May the sacrifices (yajñas) be peaceful." Yajñas are rituals performed for the welfare of society. This can be seen in modern contexts where community service and charitable acts are performed to promote harmony and peace.
  • śaṁ naḥ svarūṇām mitayo bhavantu: This means "May auspiciousness come to our true friends." It highlights the value of friendship and relationships that are genuine and supportive. In real life, this can be reflected in the importance of surrounding oneself with positive influences and trustworthy companions.
  • śaṁ naḥ prasvaḥ: This translates to "May auspiciousness be our strength." This can be interpreted as a call for inner strength and resilience. In practical terms, this can relate to personal development and the pursuit of goals despite challenges.
  • śam v astu vediḥ: This means "May there be peace in our knowledge." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in achieving peace. In real life, this can be applied to the pursuit of education and the sharing of knowledge to foster a more harmonious society.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a holistic approach to life, emphasizing the importance of spiritual nourishment, community support, peaceful actions, genuine relationships, inner strength, and the pursuit of knowledge. Its significance lies in its call for collective well-being and harmony, applicable in various real-life scenarios such as community service, personal development, and the cultivation of meaningful relationships. By embodying these principles, individuals and communities can work towards a more peaceful and prosperous existence.
