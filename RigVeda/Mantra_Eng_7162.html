कस्य स्वित सवनाṁ वृषा जुजुष्वाम् अव गच्छति |इन्द्रं का उ स्विद आ चके ||
kasya svit savanaṁ vṛṣā jujuṣvām̐ ava gacchati | indraṁ ka u svid ā cake ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kasya: Whose
    • svit: indeed
    • savanaṁ: the offering or sacrifice
    • vṛṣā: the strong or powerful
    • jujuṣvām̐: may we desire or may we seek
    • ava: down or away
    • gacchati: goes or moves
    • indraṁ: Indra, the king of gods
    • ka: who
    • u: or
    • svid: where
    • ā: to
    • cake: has spoken or has declared
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a question about the source of strength and offerings.
    • It reflects a quest for understanding who is the recipient of our sacrifices and offerings.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, symbolizes power, strength, and the fulfillment of desires.
    • The mantra encourages individuals to seek clarity in their intentions and the purpose behind their actions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, when a team works on a project, they often reflect on who will benefit from their efforts and how their contributions will be recognized.
    • In personal relationships, individuals may ask themselves what they truly desire from their interactions and who they are ultimately serving.
    • During community service, volunteers might contemplate the impact of their actions and who will receive the benefits of their work.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of intention behind actions and the recognition of the divine or higher power in our endeavors.
    • It serves as a reminder to be mindful of our contributions and the recipients of our efforts, fostering a sense of purpose and connection.
    • In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, encouraging individuals to reflect on their motivations and the broader impact of their actions.
