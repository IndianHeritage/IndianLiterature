उत न एनā पवयā पवस्वाधि श्रुते श्रवाय्यस्य तीर्थे |षष्टिं सहस्रā नैगुतो वसूनि वृक्षं न पक्वं धूनवद रणाय ||
uta na enā pavayā pavasvādhi śrute śravāyyasya tīrthe | ṣaṣṭiṁ sahasrā naiguto vasūni vṛkṣaṁ na pakvaṁ dhūnavad raṇāya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and
    • na: not
    • enā: these
    • pavayā: by the wind
    • pavasvādhi: may you be purified
    • śrute: by hearing
    • śravāyyasya: of the one who is worthy of praise
    • tīrthe: at the sacred place
    • ṣaṣṭiṁ: sixty
    • sahasrā: thousand
    • naiguto: of the one who is not bound
    • vasūni: the treasures
    • vṛkṣaṁ: the tree
    • na: not
    • pakvaṁ: ripe
    • dhūnavad: like smoke
    • raṇāya: for battle
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the purification and the importance of sacredness in life.
    • The phrase "may you be purified by the wind" suggests a cleansing of negative energies or thoughts, akin to how a fresh breeze can clear the air.
    • The reference to "sacred place" indicates the significance of spiritual locations in our lives, where one can connect with the divine.
    • The mention of "sixty thousand treasures" symbolizes the abundance of blessings and virtues one can attain through spiritual practice.
    • The idea of "not bound" reflects the freedom that comes from spiritual enlightenment, where one is liberated from worldly attachments.
    • The metaphor of "the tree not ripe like smoke" can be interpreted as the potential that has not yet been realized, emphasizing the importance of nurturing one's growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, one can practice purification through meditation or yoga, which helps in clearing the mind and spirit, similar to the wind cleansing the atmosphere.
    • Visiting sacred places, such as temples or nature spots, can provide a sense of peace and connection, reinforcing the idea of spiritual grounding.
    • Recognizing and appreciating the "treasures" in life, such as relationships, knowledge, and experiences, can lead to a more fulfilling existence.
    • Striving for personal growth and freedom from material attachments can lead to a more meaningful life, akin to the liberation mentioned in the mantra.
    • Understanding that not all potential is realized immediately encourages patience and perseverance in personal and professional endeavors.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of purification, spiritual connection, and the realization of one's potential.
    • Its significance lies in guiding individuals towards a path of self-discovery and enlightenment, encouraging them to seek the sacred in everyday life.
    • By applying the teachings of this mantra, one can cultivate a deeper understanding of themselves and their place in the universe, leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling life.
