सूरश्चिदाहरतः अस्य रीरामद इन्द्राद् आ कश्चिद् भयते तवीयसः |भीमस्य वृष्णो जठराद् अभिश्वसो दिवेदिवे सहुरिः स्तन्न अबाधितः ||
sūraś cid ā harito asya rīramad indrād ā kaś cid bhayate tavīyasaḥ | bhīmasya vṛṣṇo jaṭharād abhiśvaso dive-dive sahuriḥ stann abādhitaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sūraś: strong, powerful
    • cid: indeed, certainly
    • ā: from
    • harito: the one who is green or prosperous
    • asya: of this
    • rīramad: who brings joy or happiness
    • indrād: from Indra, the king of gods
    • ā: from
    • kaś: who
    • cid: indeed
    • bhayate: fears
    • tavīyasaḥ: of your greatness
    • bhīmasya: of the mighty
    • vṛṣṇo: of the Vṛṣṇi clan (often associated with Krishna)
    • jaṭharād: from the belly or core
    • abhiśvaso: who is fearless
    • dive-dive: day by day
    • sahuriḥ: the one who is strong
    • stann: the one who is nourished
    • abādhitaḥ: untroubled or unharmed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the powerful and prosperous nature of a divine being, likely referring to Indra, who is known for his strength and ability to bring joy.
    • The phrase "who fears your greatness" suggests that those who recognize the power of the divine are in awe and perhaps intimidated by it.
    • The mention of "the mighty Vṛṣṇi" indicates a connection to strength and resilience, emphasizing that true power comes from within and is nurtured over time.
    • The idea of being "untroubled" signifies a state of peace and security that comes from understanding one's own strength and the support of the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who embodies strength and resilience can inspire their team to overcome challenges, much like the divine strength described in the mantra.
    • In personal life, individuals who cultivate inner strength and confidence often find themselves untroubled by external fears, similar to the "unharmed" state mentioned.
    • In community settings, those who support and uplift others can create an environment where everyone feels empowered and joyful, reflecting the essence of bringing happiness as described in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of inner strength, resilience, and the power of divine support in overcoming fears and challenges.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal development to leadership and community building, reminding us that true power lies within and is nurtured through connection with the divine.
    • By embodying the qualities of strength and joy, individuals can create a positive impact in their lives and the lives of others, fostering a sense of security and happiness.
