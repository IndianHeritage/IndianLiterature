स सूर्यः पर्य उरू वरांस्य इन्द्रो ववृत्याद रथ्येव चक्रा |अतिष्ठन्तम अपस्यं न सर्गं कृष्णा तमांसी तिविष्या जग्हान ||
sa sūryaḥ pary urū varāṁsy endro vavṛtyād rathyeva cakrā | atiṣṭhantam apasya1ṁ na sargaṁ kṛṣṇā tamāṁsi tviṣyā jaghāna ||
  • sa sūryaḥ: This phrase translates to "that Sun." The Sun is often seen as a symbol of light, knowledge, and enlightenment in various cultures. In real life, the Sun provides energy and sustains life on Earth.
  • pary urū varāṁsy: This means "spreading wide its rays." It signifies the vastness and reach of the Sun's light, which can be compared to the way knowledge and wisdom can spread across societies, illuminating minds and fostering growth.
  • endro vavṛtyād: Here, "Indra" refers to the king of the gods, representing power and authority. "Vavṛtyād" suggests the act of encompassing or surrounding. This can be likened to a leader who protects and guides their people, much like a strong leader in a community or organization.
  • rathyeva cakrā: This translates to "like a wheel on a chariot." The wheel symbolizes movement and progress. In real life, this can be seen in the way technology and innovation drive society forward, much like a chariot moves with its wheels.
  • atiṣṭhantam apasyaṁ: This means "standing firm, unseen." It suggests a presence that is powerful yet not always visible. This can relate to the underlying principles of justice or morality that guide our actions, even when they are not immediately apparent.
  • na sargaṁ kṛṣṇā: "Na sargaṁ" means "not creation," and "kṛṣṇā" refers to darkness or ignorance. This implies a rejection of ignorance and a call for enlightenment. In real life, this can be seen in the pursuit of education and understanding, where individuals strive to overcome ignorance.
  • tamāṁsi tviṣyā jaghāna: This translates to "he struck down the darkness with light." It signifies the triumph of knowledge and enlightenment over ignorance. In practical terms, this can be observed in various movements that aim to eradicate poverty, illiteracy, and social injustice.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of light, knowledge, and leadership in overcoming ignorance and darkness. It serves as a reminder of the importance of enlightenment in our lives, encouraging individuals to seek knowledge and wisdom. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in educational settings, leadership roles, and social movements aimed at fostering understanding and progress. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and communities can work towards a brighter, more enlightened future.
