उद्वेति प्रसवीता जनानां महान केतुर्वर्णवः सूर्यस्य |समानं चक्रं पर्याविवृत्त्सन यद एतशो वहति धूर्षु युक्तः ||
ud v eti prasavītā janānām mahān ketur arṇavaḥ sūryasya | samānaṁ cakram paryāvivṛtsan yad etaśo vahati dhūrṣu yuktaḥ ||
  • ud v eti prasavītā janānām: This phrase can be translated as "the one who rises, brings forth the people." Here, "ud" signifies rising or ascending, while "v eti" means to go or to lead. "Prasavītā" refers to the act of bringing forth or creating, and "janānām" means of the people. This can be likened to a leader or a guiding force in society, such as a teacher or a mentor who inspires and nurtures the community.
  • mahān ketur arṇavaḥ sūryasya: This translates to "the great banner of the ocean of the sun." "Mahān" means great, "ketur" refers to a banner or a flag, "arṇavaḥ" means ocean, and "sūryasya" refers to the sun. This can symbolize the vastness of knowledge and enlightenment that the sun represents, akin to how the ocean holds countless treasures. For example, a scientist or a philosopher can be seen as a "banner" of knowledge, guiding others through the vast ocean of information.
  • samānaṁ cakram paryāvivṛtsan: This means "the wheel that revolves uniformly." "Samānaṁ" indicates uniformity, "cakram" refers to a wheel, and "paryāvivṛtsan" means to revolve or turn around. This can be related to the cycles of life, such as the seasons or the cycles of time, which are consistent and predictable. In real life, this can be seen in the way businesses operate in cycles, adapting to market changes while maintaining a steady course.
  • yad etaśo vahati dhūrṣu yuktaḥ: This translates to "that which carries the essence of all things." "Yad" means that, "etaśo" refers to essence or core, "vahati" means to carry, and "dhūrṣu" means in all things, while "yuktaḥ" implies being connected or united. This can be interpreted as the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of unity in diversity. For instance, in a community, every individual contributes to the whole, and their collective efforts lead to progress and harmony.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of leadership, knowledge, unity, and the cyclical nature of life. It signifies that true leaders rise to guide and nurture their communities, much like the sun that illuminates the vast ocean of knowledge. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various fields such as education, community service, and environmental sustainability, where individuals and groups work together to create a better future. The mantra serves as a reminder of our interconnectedness and the collective responsibility we hold in fostering growth and harmony in society.
