महाम् अमात्रो वृजनें विरप्श्य उग्रं शवः पत्यते धृष्ण्व ओजः |नाह विव्याच पृथिवी चनैणं यत सोमासो हर्यश्वं अमन्दन ||
mahām̐ amatro vṛjane virapśy u1graṁ śavaḥ patyate dhṛṣṇv ojaḥ | nāha vivyāca pṛthivī canainaṁ yat somāso haryaśvam amandan ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mahām̐: Great or supreme
    • amatro: Without death or mortality
    • vṛjane: In the act of overcoming or defeating
    • virapśy: With strength or power
    • u1graṁ: Fierce or intense
    • śavaḥ: The dead or lifeless
    • patyate: Is controlled or governed
    • dhṛṣṇv: With courage or boldness
    • ojaḥ: Vital energy or strength
    • nāha: Not
    • vivyāca: To reveal or disclose
    • pṛthivī: The earth or the world
    • canainaṁ: This or that
    • yat: That which
    • somāso: The moon or the nectar
    • haryaśvam: The swift or powerful
    • amandan: Without sorrow or blissful
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the supreme power that transcends mortality and the physical realm.
    • It emphasizes the strength and courage needed to overcome challenges and adversities in life.
    • The reference to the earth and the moon suggests a connection between the material and spiritual worlds.
    • It highlights the importance of inner strength and vitality in facing life's struggles.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In moments of personal crisis, such as the loss of a loved one, individuals often draw upon their inner strength to cope and move forward.
    • In professional settings, leaders who exhibit courage and decisiveness can inspire their teams to overcome obstacles and achieve success.
    • In sports, athletes often tap into their vital energy and mental fortitude to push through physical limits and achieve greatness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of resilience and the importance of harnessing one's inner strength.
    • It is applicable in various life scenarios, encouraging individuals to confront challenges with courage and determination.
    • Ultimately, it teaches that true strength lies not just in physical prowess but in the ability to rise above adversity and maintain a positive spirit.
