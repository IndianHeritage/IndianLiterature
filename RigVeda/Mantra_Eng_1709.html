मन्त्र: अत्रा ते रूपम् उत्तमम् अपश्यं जिगीषमाणम् इषा आ पदे गोः |यदा ते मर्तो अनु भोगम् आनल् आद इद् ग्रसिष्ठ ओषधीर अजिगः ||
atrā te rūpam uttamam apaśyaṁ jigīṣamāṇam iṣa ā pade goḥ | yadā te marto anu bhogam ānaḻ ād id grasiṣṭha oṣadhīr ajīgaḥ ||
  • atrā te rūpam uttamam: This phrase translates to "Here, I see your supreme form." It signifies the recognition of the divine or the ultimate reality in its most exalted state. In real life, this can be likened to moments of profound realization or enlightenment, where one perceives beauty and truth in their purest form, such as witnessing a breathtaking sunset or experiencing a deep connection with nature.
  • apaśyaṁ jigīṣamāṇam: This means "I have not seen you, yet I desire to." It reflects the human longing for understanding and connection with the divine or the unknown. In everyday life, this can be compared to the pursuit of knowledge or truth, such as a student striving to grasp complex concepts in science or philosophy, driven by curiosity and a desire for deeper understanding.
  • iṣa ā pade goḥ: This translates to "the power that resides in the feet of the cow." The cow is often seen as a sacred symbol in Hinduism, representing nourishment and abundance. This phrase can be interpreted as recognizing the divine energy present in all living beings and the importance of respecting and nurturing them. In practical terms, this can relate to sustainable living and the ethical treatment of animals, emphasizing the interconnectedness of life.
  • yadā te marto anu bhogam ānaḻ: This means "When a mortal approaches the enjoyment of your essence." It suggests the idea of seeking fulfillment and joy through connection with the divine. In real life, this can be seen in practices such as meditation, yoga, or any spiritual pursuit that aims to bring peace and happiness, illustrating the journey towards self-realization and inner contentment.
  • ād id grasiṣṭha oṣadhīr ajīgaḥ: This translates to "the one who consumes the best of the herbs." It implies the importance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual. In a modern context, this can relate to the significance of healthy eating and holistic wellness, where individuals seek to nourish their bodies with wholesome foods and their minds with positive thoughts and experiences.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound spiritual journey, emphasizing the recognition of the divine, the pursuit of knowledge, the respect for all living beings, and the quest for fulfillment. Its significance lies in its applicability to everyday life, encouraging individuals to seek deeper connections, practice sustainability, and nourish both body and spirit. By embodying these principles, one can lead a more harmonious and meaningful life, fostering a sense of unity with the universe and all its inhabitants.
