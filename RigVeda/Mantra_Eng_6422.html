तं गूर्धया स्वर्णरं देवासो देवम् अरतिं दधन्विरे |देवत्रा हव्यं ओहिरे ||
taṁ gūrdhayā svarṇaraṁ devāso devam aratiṁ dadhanvire | devatrā havyam ohire ||
  • taṁ - This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • gūrdhayā - This term can be interpreted as "with strength" or "with vigor," suggesting the power and might of the divine.
  • svarṇaraṁ - This means "golden" or "radiant," symbolizing purity, value, and the divine nature of the entity being addressed.
  • devāso - This translates to "gods" or "divine beings," indicating the presence of multiple deities or a collective divine force.
  • devam - This word means "the divine" or "the god," emphasizing the singular focus on a particular deity.
  • aratiṁ - This can be understood as "displeasure" or "aversion," suggesting a desire to overcome negativity or obstacles.
  • dadhanvire - This term means "who wields" or "who carries," indicating action and agency, particularly in the context of divine intervention.
  • devatrā - This translates to "by the gods," reinforcing the connection to divine forces.
  • havyam - This word means "offering" or "sacrifice," which is a common theme in Vedic rituals, symbolizing devotion and the act of giving.
  • ohire - This can be interpreted as "they have accepted" or "they have received," indicating the response of the divine to the offerings made.

This mantra speaks to the relationship between humans and the divine, emphasizing the importance of strength, purity, and devotion in seeking divine favor. It suggests that by offering our best to the gods, we can overcome negativity and receive blessings in return.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra emphasizes strength and purity, individuals can strive to cultivate these qualities in their lives, overcoming personal challenges and negativity.
  • Community Rituals: In communal settings, this mantra can be recited during rituals to invoke divine blessings for the collective well-being, reinforcing the idea of unity and shared purpose.
  • Mindfulness and Offerings: The concept of offering can be translated into acts of kindness and service in daily life, where individuals offer their time, resources, or support to others, thereby creating a positive impact.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of divine connection and the importance of approaching life with strength, purity, and a spirit of giving. By embodying these principles, individuals can navigate life's challenges and foster a deeper relationship with the divine.
