मन्त्र: हनामिनाम् इति त्वष्टा यद अब्रवीच् चमसं ये देवपानम् अनिन्दिषुः |अन्याः नामानि कृत्वते सुते सच्चाम् अन्यैः एनान् कन्याः नामभिः स्परत् ||
hanāmainām̐ iti tvaṣṭā yad abravīc camasaṁ ye devapānam anindiṣuḥ | anyā nāmāni kṛṇvate sute sacām̐ anyair enān kanyā3 nāmabhiḥ sparat ||
  • Mantra: "hanāmainām̐ iti tvaṣṭā yad abravīc camasaṁ ye devapānam anindiṣuḥ | anyā nāmāni kṛṇvate sute sacām̐ anyair enān kanyā3 nāmabhiḥ sparat ||"
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • hanāmainām̐: This can be interpreted as "to those who are to be sacrificed" or "to those who are to be offered."
    • iti: This means "thus" or "so."
    • tvaṣṭā: Refers to the divine craftsman or creator, often associated with the act of creation and shaping.
    • yad: This translates to "that" or "which."
    • abravīc: Means "spoke" or "declared."
    • camasaṁ: Refers to a "sacrificial cup" or "vessel," often used in rituals.
    • ye: This means "who" or "those."
    • devapānam: Refers to "the drink of the gods," symbolizing divine nourishment or blessings.
    • anindiṣuḥ: Means "without blame" or "pure," indicating something that is faultless.
    • anyā: This means "other" or "different."
    • nāmāni: Refers to "names" or "identities."
    • kṛṇvate: Means "to create" or "to make."
    • sute: Refers to "the one who is born" or "the offspring."
    • sacām̐: This can be interpreted as "with truth" or "with goodness."
    • anyair: Means "by others" or "different."
    • enān: Refers to "these" or "them."
    • kanyā3: This can be interpreted as "the maiden" or "the daughter."
    • nāmabhiḥ: Refers to "by names" or "through identities."
    • sparat: Means "to touch" or "to influence."
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra speaks to the act of creation and the divine influence in the process of offering and sacrifice.
    • It emphasizes the importance of purity and intention in rituals, suggesting that offerings made with a pure heart are blessed by the divine.
    • In real life, this can be applied to various situations where intention and purity of action are crucial, such as in charitable acts, community service, or personal relationships.
    • For example, when one donates to a cause, doing so with a pure intention can lead to greater blessings and positive outcomes, akin to the divine nourishment mentioned in the mantra.
    • Furthermore, the idea of names and identities suggests that our actions and how we present ourselves to the world can influence our relationships and the impact we have on others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of our actions, intentions, and the divine.
    • It encourages individuals to act with purity and intention, recognizing that our offerings—whether material or emotional—can have profound effects on ourselves and others.
    • In essence, it teaches that the quality of our actions and the purity of our intentions can lead to divine blessings and a harmonious existence.
