यā ओषधिः सोमराज्ञीर बह्वीः शतविचक्षणाः |तासां त्वम् अस्य उत्तमारं कामाय शं हृदे ||
yā oṣadhīḥ somarājñīr bahvīḥ śatavicakṣaṇāḥ | tāsāṁ tvam asy uttamāraṁ kāmāya śaṁ hṛde ||
  • yā oṣadhīḥ - This phrase refers to the "herbs" or "plants" that are considered sacred and beneficial. In real life, this can be related to the use of medicinal plants in Ayurveda, which emphasizes the healing properties of various herbs.
  • somarājñīr - This term translates to "the queen of the soma," where "soma" is a sacred drink in Vedic tradition believed to confer immortality and divine insight. In modern terms, this can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment through various means, such as education and self-discovery.
  • bahvīḥ - Meaning "many" or "various," this word signifies the abundance of resources or blessings available to us. In everyday life, this can be seen in the diversity of opportunities and experiences that one can encounter.
  • śatavicakṣaṇāḥ - This translates to "those who have a hundred eyes" or "those who are very perceptive." It emphasizes the importance of awareness and insight in making informed decisions. For example, in business, having a keen understanding of market trends can lead to success.
  • tāsāṁ - This word means "of them," referring back to the herbs and the blessings mentioned earlier. It indicates a connection to the resources we seek.
  • tvam - This translates to "you," addressing a divine or higher power, suggesting a plea or invocation for assistance.
  • asy - Meaning "of this," it connects the previous elements to the speaker's intention or desire.
  • uttamāraṁ - This term means "the best" or "the highest," indicating a pursuit of excellence or the ultimate goal in life.
  • kāmāya - This translates to "for desire" or "for the purpose of fulfilling wishes," highlighting the human inclination towards seeking fulfillment and happiness.
  • śaṁ hṛde - This phrase means "peace in the heart," suggesting that the ultimate goal of all desires and pursuits should be inner peace and contentment.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking abundance, wisdom, and fulfillment through the blessings of nature and divine guidance. It emphasizes the importance of being perceptive and aware in our pursuits, ultimately leading to inner peace. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal growth, professional development, and holistic well-being, reminding us to seek not just material success but also spiritual and emotional fulfillment.
