यद्वासि रोचने दिवः समुद्रस्याधि विष्टपि |यत्पार्थिवे सदने वृत्रहन्तम यदन्तरिक्ष आगहि ||
yad vāsi rocane divaḥ samudrasyādhi viṣṭapi | yat pārthive sadane vṛtrahantama yad antarikṣa ā gahi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • vāsi: you dwell
    • rocane: in brightness
    • divaḥ: of the heavens
    • samudrasyādhi: above the ocean
    • viṣṭapi: in the vastness
    • yat: whatever
    • pārthive: in the earthly
    • sadane: dwelling
    • vṛtrahantama: the slayer of Vritra
    • yat: whatever
    • antarikṣa: in the atmosphere
    • ā gahi: you take
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the omnipresence of the divine, suggesting that the divine dwells in various realms: the heavens, the ocean, the earth, and the atmosphere.
    • The mention of "Vritra," a demon in Vedic mythology, symbolizes obstacles or challenges. The mantra implies that the divine presence helps in overcoming these challenges.
    • It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all elements of existence, from the celestial to the terrestrial.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, one can find the divine in nature, such as the beauty of a sunrise (rocane divaḥ) or the vastness of the ocean (samudrasyādhi viṣṭapi).
    • When facing challenges, such as personal or professional obstacles (vṛtrahantama), individuals can seek strength and guidance from their spiritual beliefs, recognizing that they are not alone in their struggles.
    • The mantra can also be reflected in community gatherings where people come together to celebrate life, acknowledging the divine presence in their collective experiences.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the divine's omnipresence and the support available to us in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It encourages individuals to recognize the interconnectedness of all aspects of life and to seek strength from the divine in their daily experiences.
    • In a broader sense, it highlights the importance of spirituality in fostering resilience and unity within communities.
