मन्तर: नृभिर धूतः सुतो अष्नैर अव्यो वारैः परिपूतः | अश्वो न निक्तो नदिषु ||
nṛbhir dhūtaḥ suto aśnair avyo vāraiḥ paripūtaḥ | aśvo na nikto nadīṣu ||
  • nṛbhir dhūtaḥ: This phrase can be translated as "cleansed by men" or "purified by humans." It signifies the importance of human effort in achieving purity and clarity in life.
  • suto aśnair: This means "born of the food" or "nourished by sustenance." It emphasizes the connection between nourishment and life, highlighting how our physical and spiritual well-being is intertwined with what we consume.
  • avyo vāraiḥ paripūtaḥ: This translates to "surrounded by the unblemished" or "enveloped by purity." It suggests the idea of being in an environment that is free from negativity and impurities, which is essential for growth and prosperity.
  • aśvo na nikto nadīṣu: This can be interpreted as "the horse is not captured in the rivers." The horse symbolizes strength, freedom, and vitality, indicating that true power cannot be confined or restricted by obstacles, much like a river that flows freely.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of human effort, nourishment, purity, and freedom. It serves as a reminder that our actions, choices, and environment significantly impact our lives.
  • Real-life Application: In daily life, this mantra can inspire individuals to focus on personal growth through healthy living, maintaining a positive environment, and striving for freedom from limitations. For example, in a workplace, fostering a culture of support and positivity can lead to enhanced productivity and creativity.

  • Conclusion: The mantra teaches us that by cleansing ourselves through our actions, nourishing our bodies and minds, surrounding ourselves with positivity, and embracing our freedom, we can achieve a fulfilling and prosperous life. It encourages us to be proactive in our personal and communal environments, leading to holistic well-being.
