तं वो महो महाय्यं इन्द्रं दानाय सक्षणिम् |यो गाधेषु य आरणेषु हव्यो वाजेष्व अस्ति हव्यः ||
taṁ vo maho mahāyyam indraṁ dānāya sakṣaṇim | yo gādheṣu ya āraṇeṣu havyo vājeṣv asti havyaḥ ||
  • taṁ vo maho mahāyyam indraṁ: This phrase can be translated as "to that great and powerful Indra." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunder, and strength. In real life, this can symbolize the pursuit of strength and power in our endeavors, whether in personal growth, career, or community leadership.
  • dānāya sakṣaṇim: This translates to "for the purpose of giving." It emphasizes the importance of generosity and sharing. In modern life, this can be seen in acts of charity, community service, or simply helping a friend in need. It highlights the value of altruism in building strong relationships and communities.
  • yo gādheṣu ya āraṇeṣu: This means "who is in the battles and in the struggles." It acknowledges the challenges and conflicts we face in life. For example, in a professional setting, this could refer to the competitive nature of the job market or the struggles one faces while pursuing a goal. It reminds us that challenges are a part of life and that perseverance is key.
  • havyo vājeṣv asti havyaḥ: This translates to "the one who is worthy of offerings in the sacrifices." It signifies the importance of recognizing and honoring those who contribute positively to our lives. In real life, this could relate to acknowledging mentors, leaders, or anyone who has made a significant impact on our journey. It teaches us to appreciate and celebrate the contributions of others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, generosity, perseverance, and recognition of contributions. Its significance lies in the reminder that while we strive for power and success (Indra), we must also be generous (dānāya), face our challenges (gādheṣu ya āraṇeṣu), and honor those who support us (havyo vājeṣv). In real life, this mantra can guide us in our personal and professional lives, encouraging us to be strong leaders, compassionate individuals, and grateful community members. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and supportive environment for ourselves and others.
