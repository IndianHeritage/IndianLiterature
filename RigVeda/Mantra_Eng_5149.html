आयं सो अग्निराहुतः पुरुत्रा यं ईशानः समिदिन्दे हविष्मान् |परि यं एत्य अध्वरेषु होताः ||
ayaṁ so agnir āhutaḥ purutrā yam īśānaḥ sam id indhe haviṣmān | pari yam ety adhvareṣu hotā ||
  • ayaṁ: This word translates to "this" or "here." It signifies the presence of something significant in the context of the mantra.
  • so: Meaning "that," it connects the subject to the action or state being described.
  • agnir: Refers to "fire," which is a central element in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity, transformation, and the divine.
  • āhutaḥ: This means "invoked" or "called upon." It indicates the act of summoning the divine presence through rituals.
  • purutrā: Translates to "from all directions" or "everywhere," suggesting the omnipresence of the divine energy.
  • yam: Meaning "whom," it refers to the divine being or energy that is being addressed.
  • īśānaḥ: This term means "lord" or "master," indicating a higher power or authority.
  • sam: Translates to "together" or "completely," emphasizing unity in the invocation.
  • id: Meaning "now," it signifies the immediacy of the invocation.
  • indhe: This means "ignite" or "kindle," referring to the act of lighting the fire, both literally and metaphorically.
  • haviṣmān: Refers to "the one who possesses offerings," indicating the divine being who receives the offerings made during rituals.
  • pari: Meaning "around" or "about," it suggests the encompassing nature of the divine presence.
  • yam ety: Translates to "whom he approaches," indicating the relationship between the devotee and the divine.
  • adhvareṣu: This means "in the rituals," referring to the sacred acts performed to honor the divine.
  • hotā: Refers to "the priest" or "the one who invokes," indicating the role of the individual performing the ritual.

The mantra essentially speaks about invoking the divine fire (Agni) from all directions, recognizing its omnipresence and the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine. It emphasizes the act of calling upon Agni, who is seen as a mediator between humans and the divine, to ignite the offerings made during the rituals.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: The mantra can be recited during religious ceremonies to invoke divine blessings and ensure the presence of positive energy.
  • Personal Transformation: Just as fire transforms materials, individuals can use the essence of this mantra to symbolize personal growth and transformation in their lives.
  • Community Gatherings: In communal settings, invoking Agni can foster unity and collective purpose, reminding participants of their shared goals and values.

In conclusion, this mantra highlights the significance of fire as a divine element that connects the material and spiritual worlds. It serves as a reminder of the importance of rituals in our lives, encouraging individuals to seek transformation and unity through their spiritual practices.
