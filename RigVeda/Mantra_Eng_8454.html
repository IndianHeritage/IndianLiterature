इन्दुः पुनानो अति गाहते मृधो विश्वानि कृत्वान सुपथानि यज्ञवे |गाहः कृत्वानो निर्णिजं हर्यतः कविर अत्यो न क्रीळन् परि वारम् अर्षति ||
induḥ punāno ati gāhate mṛdho viśvāni kṛṇvan supathāni yajyave | gāḥ kṛṇvāno nirṇijaṁ haryataḥ kavir atyo na krīḻan pari vāram arṣati ||
  • induḥ: This word refers to the moon or the soma, which is a significant element in Vedic rituals. The moon symbolizes nourishment and the cyclical nature of life.
  • punāno: This means 'purifying' or 'refreshing'. It indicates a process of renewal or cleansing, both physically and spiritually.
  • ati gāhate: This phrase translates to 'goes beyond' or 'transcends'. It suggests an elevation or a journey towards a higher state of being.
  • mṛdho: This word means 'from the earth' or 'earthly'. It connects the mantra to the physical world and our earthly existence.
  • viśvāni: This means 'all' or 'universal'. It signifies inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings and elements.
  • kṛṇvan: This translates to 'creating' or 'making'. It emphasizes the act of bringing forth or manifesting something new.
  • supathāni: This means 'good paths' or 'auspicious ways'. It suggests the importance of choosing the right direction in life.
  • yajyave: This refers to 'sacrifice' or 'worship'. It highlights the significance of devotion and the act of giving in spiritual practices.
  • gāḥ: This word means 'songs' or 'hymns'. It indicates the power of sound and expression in connecting with the divine.
  • kṛṇvāno: Similar to 'kṛṇvan', it means 'creating' or 'making', reinforcing the theme of manifestation.
  • nirṇijaṁ: This translates to 'without obstacles' or 'unhindered'. It signifies the removal of barriers in the pursuit of goals.
  • haryataḥ: This means 'from the horse' or 'swiftly'. It symbolizes speed and the dynamic nature of life.
  • kavir: This word means 'poet' or 'wise one'. It represents wisdom and the ability to articulate profound truths.
  • atyō: This translates to 'beyond' or 'exceeding'. It suggests going beyond ordinary limits.
  • na krīḻan: This means 'not playing' or 'not engaging in triviality'. It emphasizes seriousness and focus.
  • pari vāram: This translates to 'all around' or 'surrounding'. It indicates a holistic view of existence.
  • arṣati: This means 'shines' or 'radiates'. It signifies the manifestation of light and knowledge.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of spiritual and earthly existence. It emphasizes the importance of purification, creation, and the pursuit of auspicious paths in life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal growth, decision-making, and the importance of community and interconnectedness. By choosing the right paths and engaging in meaningful actions, individuals can transcend their limitations and contribute positively to the world around them. The mantra serves as a reminder of the power of intention, wisdom, and the collective journey towards enlightenment.
