सुशंसो बोधि गृणते यविष्ठ्य मधुजिव्हः स्वाहुतः |प्रस्कणवस्य प्रतिरन्न आयुर जीवसे नमस्यादैव्यम् जनम् ||
suśaṁso bodhi gṛṇate yaviṣṭhya madhujihvaḥ svāhutaḥ | praskaṇvasya pratirann āyur jīvase namasyā daivyaṁ janam ||
  • suśaṁso: This term can be translated as "well-spoken" or "auspicious." It signifies the importance of positive speech and communication in our lives. For example, when we encourage others with kind words, we create a supportive environment that fosters growth and happiness.
  • bodhi: This word means "wisdom" or "enlightenment." It emphasizes the value of knowledge and understanding in our daily lives. In real life, seeking wisdom can be seen in the pursuit of education, self-improvement, and learning from experiences.
  • gṛṇate: This translates to "to sing" or "to praise." It highlights the significance of expressing gratitude and appreciation. For instance, when we acknowledge the efforts of our friends or family, we strengthen our relationships and create a sense of community.
  • yaviṣṭhya: This term refers to "the one who is well-established" or "the one who is grounded." It suggests the importance of stability and security in life. In practical terms, having a stable job or a secure home environment allows individuals to thrive and focus on their personal growth.
  • madhujihvaḥ: This word means "sweet-tongued" or "one who speaks sweetly." It underscores the power of gentle and kind communication. For example, using a calm and soothing tone during conflicts can lead to better resolutions and understanding.
  • svāhutaḥ: This term can be interpreted as "offered" or "sacrificed." It signifies the act of giving and sharing. In real life, acts of charity and selflessness, such as volunteering or helping those in need, embody this principle.
  • praskaṇvasya: This translates to "of the one who is prosperous" or "of the flourishing." It emphasizes the importance of prosperity and abundance in life. For instance, a thriving business or a successful project can bring joy and fulfillment to individuals and communities.
  • pratirann: This word means "to receive" or "to accept." It highlights the importance of openness to new experiences and opportunities. In real life, being receptive to feedback and learning from others can lead to personal and professional growth.
  • āyur: This term translates to "life" or "longevity." It signifies the value of health and well-being. For example, maintaining a balanced diet and regular exercise contributes to a longer, healthier life.
  • jīvase: This word means "to live" or "to exist." It emphasizes the essence of life and the importance of making the most of our time. Engaging in meaningful activities and pursuing passions can enhance our overall quality of life.
  • namasyā: This term refers to "to bow" or "to show respect." It signifies the importance of humility and reverence towards others. In real life, showing respect to elders or mentors fosters a culture of appreciation and learning.
  • daivyaṁ: This word means "divine" or "heavenly." It emphasizes the connection to the spiritual or higher aspects of life. Engaging in spiritual practices, such as meditation or prayer, can provide a sense of peace and purpose.
  • janam: This term translates to "people" or "community." It highlights the significance of social connections and relationships. Building a strong community through collaboration and support can lead to collective well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of positive communication, wisdom, gratitude, stability, kindness, generosity, prosperity, receptiveness, health, respect, spirituality, and community. Its significance lies in the reminder to cultivate these qualities in our lives, fostering a harmonious existence. In real-life scenarios, applying these principles can lead to personal growth, stronger relationships, and a more fulfilling life. By embodying the teachings of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more compassionate and prosperous society.
