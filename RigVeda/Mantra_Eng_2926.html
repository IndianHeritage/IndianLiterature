अपाः सोमम अस्तम इन्द्र प्र याहि कल्याणीर जाया सुरणं गृहे ते |यत्रा रथस्य बृहतो निधाणं विमोचनं वाजिनो दक्षिणावत ||
apāḥ somam astam indra pra yāhi kalyāṇīr jāyā suraṇaṁ gṛhe te | yatrā rathasya bṛhato nidhānaṁ vimocanaṁ vājino dakṣiṇāvat ||
  • apāḥ: This word translates to "water" or "the waters." In many cultures, water is seen as a source of life and purity.
  • somam: Refers to "soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration.
  • astam: Means "to the west" or "to the setting." This can symbolize the end of a cycle or the transition from one state to another.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, representing strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • pra yāhi: Translates to "come forth" or "approach," indicating a call to action or a request for divine presence.
  • kalyāṇīr: Means "auspicious" or "beneficial," suggesting a desire for positive outcomes and well-being.
  • jāyā: Refers to "wife" or "spouse," symbolizing partnership and familial bonds.
  • suraṇaṁ: Means "of the divine" or "of the gods," indicating a connection to higher powers.
  • gṛhe te: Translates to "in your home," emphasizing the importance of the household as a sacred space.
  • yatrā: Means "where," indicating a location or a state of being.
  • rathasya: Refers to "of the chariot," symbolizing movement, progress, and the journey of life.
  • bṛhato: Means "great" or "vast," suggesting something of significant importance or magnitude.
  • nidhānaṁ: Translates to "treasure" or "store," indicating the presence of valuable resources or wisdom.
  • vimocanaṁ: Means "liberation" or "release," suggesting the idea of freeing oneself from constraints.
  • vājino: Refers to "the swift" or "the powerful," symbolizing strength and agility.
  • dakṣiṇāvat: Means "to the south," which can symbolize prosperity and abundance.

This mantra invokes the divine presence of Indra, asking for auspiciousness and blessings in the household. It emphasizes the importance of water and soma as sources of life and inspiration, while also highlighting the significance of familial bonds and the home as a sacred space. The imagery of the chariot suggests a journey, where the divine aids in overcoming obstacles and achieving liberation.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • During family gatherings or rituals, invoking blessings for harmony and prosperity.
  • In times of transition or change, seeking divine guidance to navigate challenges.
  • In personal development, using the concept of liberation to free oneself from limiting beliefs.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in achieving a harmonious and prosperous life. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine, nature, and human relationships, encouraging individuals to seek blessings for themselves and their families in their daily lives.
