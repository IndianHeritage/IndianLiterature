इन्द्राग्नि युवं सु नः सहन्ता दासथो रयिम् |येन दृढा समत्स्वा वीळु चित साहीषीमह्य अग्निर वनेव वात इन नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
indrāgnī yuvaṁ su naḥ sahantā dāsatho rayim | yena dṛḻhā samatsv ā vīḻu cit sāhiṣīmahy agnir vaneva vāta in nabhantām anyake same ||
  • Indrāgnī: This term refers to the combined powers of Indra, the king of gods, and Agni, the fire god. In a real-life context, this can symbolize the strength and energy derived from both natural and divine sources.
  • yuvaṁ: Meaning "young" or "youthful," this word emphasizes vitality and the potential for growth and change. In modern life, this can relate to the energy and innovation found in youth, driving progress in society.
  • su naḥ: Translated as "good for us," this phrase indicates a desire for positive outcomes and blessings in life. It reflects the human aspiration for well-being and prosperity.
  • sahantā: This word means "to endure" or "to withstand." In practical terms, it can relate to resilience in the face of challenges, such as overcoming personal or professional obstacles.
  • dāsatho: This term can be interpreted as "servants" or "those who serve." It highlights the importance of community and support systems in achieving goals, akin to teamwork in a workplace.
  • rayim: Meaning "wealth" or "prosperity," this word signifies the abundance that one seeks in life, whether it be material wealth, knowledge, or spiritual richness.
  • yena: Translated as "by which," this word connects the previous ideas to the means or methods of achieving them.
  • dṛḻhā: This term means "firm" or "strong," indicating the need for stability and reliability in one's pursuits, much like the foundation of a successful business or relationship.
  • samatsv: Meaning "to be equal" or "to be balanced," this word emphasizes the importance of harmony in life, akin to maintaining work-life balance.
  • ā vīḻu: This phrase can be interpreted as "to be victorious" or "to conquer." It reflects the human desire to overcome challenges and achieve success.
  • cit: Meaning "consciousness" or "awareness," this word highlights the importance of mindfulness and being present in one's actions and decisions.
  • sāhiṣīmahy: This term means "we are strong" or "we are powerful," reinforcing the idea of collective strength and unity in achieving goals.
  • agnir: Referring to "fire," this symbolizes transformation and purification, much like how challenges can lead to personal growth.
  • vaneva: Meaning "like a forest," this phrase suggests the richness and diversity of life experiences, akin to the variety found in nature.
  • vāta: This term means "wind," symbolizing change and movement, reflecting the dynamic nature of life.
  • in nabhantām: Translated as "in the heavens," this phrase connects earthly experiences with divine or higher aspirations.
  • anyake: Meaning "others," this word emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of community.
  • same: This term means "together" or "in unity," highlighting the significance of collaboration and collective effort.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking strength, prosperity, and unity through divine and natural forces. It emphasizes resilience, community support, and the pursuit of balance in life. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied to personal development, teamwork, and the quest for a fulfilling life. By invoking the energies of Indra and Agni, individuals can aspire to overcome challenges and achieve their goals, fostering a sense of interconnectedness and shared purpose in their communities.
