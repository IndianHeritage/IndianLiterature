मन्त्र: बभ्राणः सूनो सहसो व्य अद्यौद दधानाḥ शुक्रा रभसā वपूंषि | श्चोतन्ति धारā मधुनो घृतस्य वृषा यत्र वावृधे काव्येन ||
babhrāṇaḥ sūno sahaso vy adyaud dadhānaḥ śukrā rabhasā vapūṁṣi | ścotanti dhārā madhuno ghṛtasya vṛṣā yatra vāvṛdhe kāvyena ||
  • babhrāṇaḥ: This term refers to the one who possesses or holds. In a broader sense, it can signify abundance or richness.
  • sūno: This word means 'of the son' or 'descendant.' It emphasizes lineage and the importance of familial connections.
  • sahaso: This translates to 'with strength' or 'powerfully.' It denotes vigor and the ability to overcome challenges.
  • vy adyaud: This phrase can be interpreted as 'today' or 'in the present moment,' highlighting the significance of the current time.
  • dadhānaḥ: Meaning 'holding' or 'possessing,' it reinforces the idea of having control or ownership over something valuable.
  • śukrā: This word means 'pure' or 'bright,' often associated with auspiciousness and positivity.
  • rabhasā: This term refers to 'with speed' or 'quickly,' suggesting a sense of urgency or dynamic action.
  • vapūṁṣi: This translates to 'bodies' or 'forms,' indicating the physical manifestation of energy or vitality.
  • ścotanti: Meaning 'they shine' or 'they illuminate,' it signifies the radiance that comes from purity and strength.
  • dhārā: This word means 'streams' or 'flows,' often used to describe the flow of energy or resources.
  • madhuno: This translates to 'of honey,' symbolizing sweetness, nourishment, and the rewards of hard work.
  • ghṛtasya: Meaning 'of ghee' or 'clarified butter,' it represents richness and the essence of sustenance.
  • vṛṣā: This term can mean 'bull' or 'strength,' often associated with power and fertility.
  • yatra: This word means 'where' or 'in which place,' indicating a specific context or location.
  • vāvṛdhe: This translates to 'has increased' or 'has grown,' suggesting prosperity and abundance.
  • kāvyena: Meaning 'by poetry' or 'through art,' it emphasizes the role of creativity and expression in life.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates themes of strength, abundance, and the importance of lineage and creativity. It highlights the idea that through purity and strength, one can achieve prosperity and nourishment in life.
  • Real-life Application: In modern contexts, this mantra can be applied to personal development, family values, and the pursuit of creative endeavors. For instance, a person striving for success in their career can draw inspiration from the mantra's emphasis on strength and purity, reminding them to maintain integrity and focus on their goals.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, creativity, and abundance. It encourages individuals to embrace their lineage, act with vigor, and pursue their passions, ultimately leading to a fulfilling and prosperous life.
