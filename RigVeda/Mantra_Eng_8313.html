अव्ये वधूयुः पवते परि त्वचि श्रथ्नीत नप्तीर अदितेर ऋतं यते |हरिर अक्रान यजतः संयतो मदो नृम्णा शिशानो महिषो न शोभते ||
avye vadhūyuḥ pavate pari tvaci śrathnīte naptīr aditer ṛtaṁ yate | harir akrān yajataḥ saṁyato mado nṛmṇā śiśāno mahiṣo na śobhate ||
  • Mantra: "avye vadhūyuḥ pavate pari tvaci śrathnīte naptīr aditer ṛtaṁ yate | harir akrān yajataḥ saṁyato mado nṛmṇā śiśāno mahiṣo na śobhate ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • avye: unblemished, pure
    • vadhūyuḥ: the bride, the feminine energy
    • pavate: flows, moves
    • pari: around, in all directions
    • tvaci: in your body, in your essence
    • śrathnīte: in the sacredness, in the purity
    • naptīr: descendants, progeny
    • aditer: of Aditi, the mother of gods
    • ṛtaṁ: truth, order, cosmic law
    • yate: strives, works towards
    • harir: the divine, often associated with Vishnu
    • akrān: has created, has made
    • yajataḥ: worshipping, performing rituals
    • saṁyato: controlled, disciplined
    • mado: intoxication, ecstasy
    • nṛmṇā: among men, in humanity
    • śiśāno: the one who is wise, knowledgeable
    • mahiṣo: the great one, often referring to a bull or a powerful being
    • na: not
    • śobhate: shines, is beautiful
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the purity and sacredness of the feminine energy, represented by the bride, and how it flows in all directions.
    • The mention of Aditi signifies the nurturing aspect of the universe, emphasizing the importance of truth and cosmic order.
    • It highlights the significance of worship and discipline in achieving a higher state of being, where one can experience true ecstasy without losing control.
    • The mantra warns against the false allure of power and intoxication that does not lead to true beauty or wisdom.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In relationships, the mantra can be applied to the importance of nurturing and respecting the feminine energy, which can lead to harmony and balance.
    • In personal development, striving for truth and order in one’s life can lead to a more fulfilling existence, where one is in tune with the universe.
    • In spiritual practices, the mantra encourages disciplined worship and meditation, which can help individuals attain higher states of consciousness.
    • In leadership, it serves as a reminder that true power comes from wisdom and compassion, rather than mere authority or control.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of purity, truth, and the importance of disciplined worship in achieving a higher state of being.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to guide individuals towards a life of harmony, wisdom, and true beauty, both in personal and communal contexts.
    • By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, one can navigate the complexities of life with grace and integrity.
