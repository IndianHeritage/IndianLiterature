देवीर द्वारो वि श्रयध्वं सुप्रायणा न ऊतये |प्र-प्र यज्ञं पृणीतन ||
devīr dvāro vi śrayadhvaṁ suprāyaṇā na ūtaye | pra-pra yajñam pṛṇītana ||
  • devīr: This word translates to "goddesses" or "divine beings." In the context of the mantra, it signifies the presence of divine energy or feminine power that guides and protects.
  • dvāro: Meaning "at the door" or "gate," this term symbolizes the threshold between the material and spiritual worlds, representing opportunities and transitions in life.
  • vi śrayadhvaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "may you find shelter" or "may you take refuge." It emphasizes the importance of seeking protection and guidance from the divine.
  • suprāyaṇā: This word means "well-being" or "auspiciousness." It suggests that the divine presence brings positive energy and blessings into our lives.
  • na ūtaye: This translates to "not for destruction" or "not for harm." It indicates that the divine energy is meant to foster growth and prosperity rather than negativity.
  • pra-pra: This term can be understood as "to offer" or "to present." It signifies the act of giving and sharing, particularly in the context of rituals and offerings to the divine.
  • yajñam: Meaning "sacrifice" or "ritual," this word refers to the sacred acts performed to honor the divine and seek blessings.
  • pṛṇītana: This word translates to "fulfillment" or "completion." It signifies the idea that through devotion and offerings, one can achieve their desires and goals.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and protection in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of approaching the divine with respect and devotion, recognizing that the blessings we receive are meant for our well-being and growth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during significant life transitions, such as starting a new job, moving to a new home, or embarking on a new journey. By invoking the divine presence, individuals can find the strength and support needed to navigate challenges and achieve their aspirations. The mantra serves as a reminder to honor the divine through our actions and intentions, fostering a sense of connection and purpose in our lives.
