ब्रह्म गाम् अश्वं जनयन्त ओषधीर वनस्पतिन् पृथिवीं पर्वताम् अपः |सूर्यं दिवि रोहयन्तः सुदानव आर्याः व्रता विसृजन्तो अधि क्षमि ||
brahma gām aśvaṁ janayanta oṣadhīr vanaspatīn pṛthivīm parvatām̐ apaḥ | sūryaṁ divi rohayantaḥ sudānava āryā vratā visṛjanto adhi kṣami ||
  • Brahma gām aśvaṁ janayanta: This phrase can be translated as "the divine (Brahma) brings forth (janayanta) the horse (aśvaṁ)." In Vedic culture, the horse symbolizes strength, speed, and nobility. It can also represent the vital energy or life force that drives us forward in our endeavors. For example, in modern life, this can be seen in the way we harness technology (the 'horse' of our time) to propel our ambitions.
  • Oṣadhīr vanaspatīn: This translates to "the herbs (oṣadhīr) and plants (vanaspatīn)." Herbs and plants are essential for sustenance and healing. In real life, this can be related to the growing trend of herbal medicine and organic farming, where people are increasingly turning to nature for health and wellness solutions.
  • Pṛthivīm parvatām̐ apaḥ: This means "the earth (pṛthivīm), mountains (parvatām̐), and waters (apaḥ)." It emphasizes the importance of nature and the elements in sustaining life. In contemporary society, this can be reflected in environmental conservation efforts, where we strive to protect our planet's resources for future generations.
  • Sūryaṁ divi rohayantaḥ: This translates to "the sun (sūryaṁ) in the sky (divi) rises (rohayantaḥ)." The sun is often seen as a source of life and energy. In our daily lives, this can be likened to the importance of optimism and positivity, as the sun symbolizes hope and new beginnings.
  • Sudānava āryā vratā: This phrase can be interpreted as "the noble (āryā) who are steadfast (vratā) and virtuous (sudānava)." It highlights the significance of integrity and commitment in our actions. In real life, this can be seen in leaders and individuals who uphold ethical standards and inspire others through their dedication.
  • Visṛjanto adhi kṣami: This means "those who release (visṛjanto) and are forgiving (adhi kṣami)." It speaks to the importance of letting go of grudges and practicing forgiveness. In everyday life, this can be applied in personal relationships, where forgiveness can lead to healing and stronger bonds.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the interconnectedness of life, nature, and the divine. It emphasizes the importance of harnessing our inner strength, respecting nature, and embodying virtues such as integrity and forgiveness. In real life, these principles can guide us in our personal and professional lives, encouraging us to live harmoniously with ourselves, others, and the environment. By embracing these values, we can contribute to a more balanced and fulfilling existence.
