पुनर एता निवर्तन्ताम् अस्मिन् पुष्यन्तु गोपतौ |इहैवाग्ने न धारयेह तिष्ठतु या रयिः ||
punar etā ni vartantām asmin puṣyantu gopatau | ihaivāgne ni dhārayeha tiṣṭhatu yā rayiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • punar: again, back
    • etā: these
    • ni: not
    • vartantām: may they return
    • asmin: in this
    • puṣyantu: may they prosper
    • gopatau: in the presence of the Lord of cattle (a reference to prosperity and abundance)
    • ihaivāgne: here, O Agni (the fire god)
    • ni: not
    • dhārayeha: may I hold
    • tiṣṭhatu: may it stand
    • yā: which
    • rayiḥ: wealth, prosperity
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a desire for the return of prosperity and abundance.
    • It invokes Agni, the fire god, symbolizing purification and the sustenance of life.
    • The phrase "may they return" suggests a longing for the return of good fortune that may have been lost.
    • The reference to "Lord of cattle" signifies wealth and prosperity, as cattle were a primary measure of wealth in ancient times.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of maintaining wealth and prosperity in one's life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, farmers may recite this mantra during the planting season, hoping for a bountiful harvest.
    • In business, entrepreneurs might use this mantra to invoke success and the return of lost opportunities.
    • During personal challenges, individuals may reflect on this mantra to regain their focus and attract positive energy back into their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the cyclical nature of prosperity and the importance of invoking positive energies.
    • It highlights the significance of Agni as a purifying force that can help restore balance and abundance in life.
    • In modern contexts, the mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, including personal growth, financial stability, and community well-being.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to seek out and nurture the wealth and prosperity that surrounds them.
