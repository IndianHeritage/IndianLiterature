इमे हि ते ब्रह्मकृतः सुते सचा मधौ न मक्ष आसते |इन्द्रे कामं जरितारो वसूयवो रथे न पादम् आ दधुः ||
ime hi te brahmakṛtaḥ sute sacā madhau na makṣa āsate | indre kāmaṁ jaritāro vasūyavo rathe na pādam ā dadhuḥ ||
  • ime hi te brahmakṛtaḥ: This phrase translates to "These are indeed your creations, O Brahman." Here, "Brahman" refers to the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit in Hindu philosophy. It signifies the divine source of all creation.
  • sute sacā madhau na makṣa āsate: This means "In the sweet nectar, they do not fall short." The "nectar" symbolizes the essence of life and fulfillment. It suggests that the creations of Brahman are abundant and provide sustenance and joy.
  • indre kāmaṁ jaritāro: This translates to "Indra, the one who fulfills desires." Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and the fulfillment of desires. This line emphasizes the role of divine intervention in achieving one's aspirations.
  • vasūyavo rathe na pādam ā dadhuḥ: This means "The powerful ones do not place a foot on the chariot." This can be interpreted as a metaphor for the strength and stability of those who are blessed by divine grace. It suggests that true power comes from spiritual alignment rather than mere physical strength.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of divine creation, fulfillment of desires, and the importance of spiritual strength. In real life, it serves as a reminder that our aspirations can be realized through alignment with the divine and that true power lies in our connection to a higher reality. It encourages individuals to seek spiritual fulfillment and recognize the abundance in their lives, fostering a sense of gratitude and purpose. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during personal challenges, decision-making processes, or when seeking inspiration and motivation in life.
