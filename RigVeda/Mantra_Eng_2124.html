यं क्रन्दसी संयती विह्वयते परे 'वर उभया अमित्राः | समानं चिद रथम आतस्थिवांसां नाना हवेत स जनासा इन्द्रः ||
yaṁ krandasī saṁyatī vihvayete pare 'vara ubhayā amitrāḥ | samānaṁ cid ratham ātasthivāṁsā nānā havete sa janāsa indraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: who
    • krandasī: the one who is powerful or strong
    • saṁyatī: controls or restrains
    • vihvayete: brings forth or manifests
    • pare: in the higher realms or beyond
    • 'vara: the best or the most excellent
    • ubhayā: both
    • amitrāḥ: enemies or adversaries
    • samānaṁ: equal or similar
    • cid: consciousness or awareness
    • ratham: chariot or vehicle
    • ātasthivāṁsā: those who are established or grounded
    • nānā: various or diverse
    • havete: invoke or call upon
    • sa: that
    • janāsa: of the people or community
    • indraḥ: Indra, the king of gods
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a powerful entity (possibly Indra) who controls and manifests strength in both the higher realms and in the material world.
    • The phrase "ubhayā amitrāḥ" suggests that this power can overcome both internal and external adversities.
    • The mention of "samānaṁ cid ratham" indicates a balance of consciousness and action, suggesting that awareness is essential for effective action.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of community ("janāsa") and the collective strength that can be invoked through unity and shared purpose.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In leadership, a strong leader embodies the qualities described in this mantra by controlling their emotions and manifesting strength to guide their team through challenges.
    • In a community setting, when individuals come together to support a cause, they invoke a collective strength that can overcome adversities, much like the mantra suggests.
    • In personal development, the idea of balancing awareness and action can be seen in practices like mindfulness, where being conscious of one's thoughts leads to more effective decision-making.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of inner strength, control, and community in overcoming challenges.
    • It teaches that true power lies in the ability to balance awareness with action, and that collective efforts can lead to greater achievements.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to personal growth, effective leadership, and stronger communities.
