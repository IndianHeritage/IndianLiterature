ब्रह्मा देवानाम् पदवीः कवीनाम् ऋषिर् विप्राणाम् महिषो मृगाणाम् |श्येनो गृध्राणां स्वधितिः वनानां सोमः पवित्रम् अत्येति रेब्हन् ||
brahmā devānām padavīḥ kavīnām ṛṣir viprāṇām mahiṣo mṛgāṇām | śyeno gṛdhrāṇāṁ svadhitir vanānāṁ somaḥ pavitram aty eti rebhan ||
  • Brahmā: Refers to the creator or the ultimate reality in Hindu philosophy. It signifies the source of all knowledge and existence.
  • Devānām: Means 'of the gods'. This indicates a connection to divine beings and higher powers.
  • Padavīḥ: Translates to 'paths' or 'ways'. It suggests the various routes or methods to attain knowledge and enlightenment.
  • Kavīnām: Refers to 'poets' or 'sages'. This highlights the importance of wisdom and creativity in understanding the universe.
  • Ṛṣir: Means 'sage' or 'seer'. It emphasizes the role of enlightened individuals who perceive deeper truths.
  • Viprāṇām: Refers to 'the learned' or 'intellectuals'. This signifies the value of knowledge and scholarship in society.
  • Mahiṣo: Translates to 'the great one' or 'the powerful'. It can refer to a strong entity or force in nature.
  • Mṛgāṇām: Means 'of the animals'. This indicates a connection to the natural world and its inhabitants.
  • Śyeno: Refers to 'the eagle' or 'the bird of prey'. It symbolizes vision, strength, and the ability to rise above challenges.
  • Gṛdhrāṇāṁ: Means 'of the vultures'. This can represent the cycle of life and death, as vultures play a role in the ecosystem.
  • Svadhitir: Translates to 'self-rule' or 'self-determination'. It emphasizes the importance of autonomy and personal agency.
  • Vanānāṁ: Means 'of the forests'. This signifies the connection to nature and the wisdom it holds.
  • Somaḥ: Refers to the sacred drink or elixir that grants immortality and enlightenment. It symbolizes spiritual awakening.
  • Pavitram: Means 'pure' or 'sacred'. This highlights the importance of purity in thought and action.
  • Aty eti: Translates to 'transcends' or 'goes beyond'. It signifies the journey towards higher consciousness.
  • Rebhan: Refers to 'the sound' or 'the call'. This can symbolize the resonance of truth and knowledge in the universe.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking knowledge, wisdom, and enlightenment through the divine and natural world. It emphasizes the importance of sages, poets, and learned individuals in guiding society towards higher truths. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as education, environmental conservation, and personal development. By recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the pursuit of purity and self-determination, individuals can strive for a more enlightened and harmonious existence. The mantra serves as a reminder to transcend ordinary experiences and seek deeper understanding in our lives.
