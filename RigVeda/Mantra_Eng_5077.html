यं युवं दाश्वध्वराय देवā रयिं धत्तो वसुमन्तं पुरुक्षुम् |अस्मे स इन्द्रावरुणाव अपि ष्यāt प्र यो भणक्ति वनुषाम् अशस्तिः ||
yaṁ yuvaṁ dāśvadhvarāya devā rayiṁ dhattho vasumantam purukṣum | asme sa indrāvaruṇāv api ṣyāt pra yo bhanakti vanuṣām aśastīḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaṁ: who
    • yuvaṁ: young
    • dāśvadhvarāya: to the one who performs the sacrifice
    • devā: the gods
    • rayiṁ: wealth
    • dhattho: you bestow
    • vasumantam: the prosperous
    • purukṣum: the one with many qualities
    • asme: for us
    • sa: may he
    • indrāvaruṇāv: Indra and Varuna
    • api: also
    • ṣyāt: may be
    • pra: who
    • yo: who
    • bhanakti: speaks
    • vanuṣām: of men
    • aśastīḥ: the unworthy
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the young and dynamic energy represented by the gods, particularly Indra and Varuna, who are associated with strength and cosmic order.
    • The phrase "who performs the sacrifice" emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings in gaining divine favor and prosperity.
    • The mantra suggests that the gods bestow wealth and prosperity upon those who are virtuous and possess many qualities, highlighting the connection between moral character and material success.
    • The concluding lines indicate a desire for protection and support from the divine against those who may speak ill or act unworthily.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the practice of ethical business. Just as the mantra emphasizes virtue and qualities, businesses that operate with integrity often find greater success and loyalty from customers.
    • In personal relationships, the idea of being prosperous and virtuous can be seen in how individuals who are kind and supportive tend to attract positive relationships and community support.
    • In community service, those who contribute selflessly to society often receive recognition and support, reflecting the blessings of the divine as mentioned in the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of virtue, sacrifice, and the divine connection in achieving prosperity and protection.
    • It teaches that true wealth comes not just from material possessions but from moral integrity and the qualities one embodies.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more fulfilling and prosperous existence, both personally and in the community.
