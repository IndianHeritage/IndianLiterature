त्रीणि शतां त्रि सहस्राण्य अग्निं त्रिंशच च देवā नव चासपर्यन् | aukṣan घृतैर अस्तृणन बर्हिर अस्मा आद इद धोतारं न्य असादयन्त ||
trīṇi śatā trī sahasrāṇy agniṁ triṁśac ca devā nava cāsaparyan | aukṣan ghṛtair astṛṇan barhir asmā ād id dhotāraṁ ny asādayanta ||
  • Mantra: "trīṇi śatā trī sahasrāṇy agniṁ triṁśac ca devā nava cāsaparyan | aukṣan ghṛtair astṛṇan barhir asmā ād id dhotāraṁ ny asādayanta ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • trīṇi: three
    • śatā: hundreds
    • trī: three
    • sahasrāṇy: thousands
    • agniṁ: fire
    • triṁśac: thirty
    • ca: and
    • devā: gods
    • nava: nine
    • cāsaparyan: who serve
    • aukṣan: having increased
    • ghṛtair: with ghee
    • astṛṇan: having spread
    • barhir: the grass
    • asmā: here
    • ād: thus
    • id: this
    • dhotāraṁ: the one who is invoked
    • ny: indeed
    • asādayanta: they have satisfied
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the invocation of Agni, the fire god, who is central to Vedic rituals.
    • The phrase "trīṇi śatā trī sahasrāṇy" suggests a vast number of offerings, indicating the importance of abundance in rituals.
    • Agni is described as being served by "nava cāsaparyan," which refers to the nine deities who assist in the offerings.
    • The use of "ghṛtair" (ghee) signifies the purity and sanctity of the offerings, as ghee is considered a sacred substance in Hindu rituals.
    • The act of spreading "barhir" (grass) is a traditional practice in Vedic rituals, symbolizing the creation of a sacred space for the divine.
    • Finally, the mantra concludes with the idea that through these offerings, the deities are satisfied, highlighting the reciprocal relationship between humans and the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern Hindu rituals, offerings of ghee and grains are still made to Agni during ceremonies like weddings and homas (fire sacrifices).
    • The concept of abundance can be seen in community feasts where food is shared, reflecting the idea of offering to the divine and sharing blessings.
    • Creating a sacred space in homes for daily prayers mirrors the practice of spreading grass and preparing an altar for worship.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of rituals in connecting with the divine, showcasing the importance of offerings and the role of Agni as a mediator.
    • It teaches us about the values of abundance, community, and the sacredness of our actions, which are applicable in various aspects of life, from personal spirituality to communal gatherings.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of honoring the divine through our actions and offerings.
