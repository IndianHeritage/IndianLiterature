पुषन्न अनु प्र गा इहि यजमानस्य सुन्वतः |अस्माकं स्तुवताम उता ||
pūṣann anu pra gā ihi yajamānasya sunvataḥ | asmākaṁ stuvatām uta ||
  • Mantra: "pūṣann anu pra gā ihi yajamānasya sunvataḥ | asmākaṁ stuvatām uta ||"
  • Translation: "O Pūṣan, come here quickly to the worshipper who is invoking you; may you be pleased with our offerings."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • Pūṣan: A deity associated with nourishment and prosperity, often invoked for blessings in agriculture and sustenance.
    • anu: Meaning "after" or "following," indicating a sense of urgency or immediacy.
    • pra gā: Translates to "come forth" or "arrive," a call for the deity to manifest.
    • ihi: Meaning "here," emphasizing the physical presence of the deity in the ritual space.
    • yajamānasya: Refers to the "worshipper" or "one who performs the sacrifice," highlighting the role of the devotee in the ritual.
    • sunvataḥ: Meaning "who is invoking" or "who is calling," indicating the act of prayer or offering.
    • asmākaṁ: Translates to "our," signifying a collective identity among the worshippers.
    • stuvatām: Meaning "may be praised" or "may be honored," expressing the desire for the deity's acknowledgment.
    • uta: A particle that can mean "or" or "indeed," reinforcing the sincerity of the invocation.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agricultural communities, farmers often invoke deities like Pūṣan during planting and harvest seasons, seeking blessings for a bountiful crop.
    • During family rituals or festivals, individuals may recite this mantra to invite prosperity and well-being into their homes, reflecting the collective hope for abundance.
    • In modern contexts, this mantra can be adapted for corporate settings where teams come together to seek success and growth, invoking a sense of unity and purpose.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine presence in our endeavors, particularly in seeking nourishment and prosperity.
    • Its significance lies in the recognition of a higher power that supports human efforts, reminding us of the interconnectedness between the divine and our daily lives.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether in agriculture, family gatherings, or corporate settings, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek blessings and acknowledge the contributions of the divine in our pursuits.
