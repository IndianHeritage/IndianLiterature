या एक इच च्यावयति प्र भूमा राजा कृष्णीनाम पुरुहूत इन्द्रः |सत्यं एनम अनु विश्वे मदन्ति रातिं देवस्य गृणतो मघोनः ||
ya eka ic cyāvayati pra bhūmā rājā kṛṣṭīnām puruhūta indraḥ | satyam enam anu viśve madanti rātiṁ devasya gṛṇato maghonaḥ ||
  • ya eka ic cyāvayati: This phrase can be translated as "He who alone brings forth." It signifies the power of a singular entity or force that has the ability to create or manifest.
  • pra bhūmā rājā: This means "the great king of the earth." It refers to a supreme authority or a ruler who governs with wisdom and strength.
  • kṛṣṭīnām puruhūta indraḥ: This translates to "Indra, the lord of many, who is invoked." Indra is often seen as a deity of rain and thunderstorms, symbolizing strength and power in various aspects of life.
  • satyam enam anu viśve madanti: This means "the truth that all beings celebrate." It emphasizes the universal acknowledgment of truth and its importance in life.
  • rātiṁ devasya gṛṇato maghonaḥ: This translates to "the joy of the divine, sung by the mighty." It highlights the celebration of divine joy and the strength that comes from recognizing and honoring it.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of leadership, truth, and divine joy. It emphasizes the importance of a singular powerful force (like a leader or a deity) that brings forth creation and governs with wisdom.
  • Real-life Application: In a real-world context, this mantra can be applied to leadership in various fields, such as politics, business, or community service. A leader who embodies the qualities of strength, wisdom, and truth can inspire others and create a positive impact.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of truth and the role of strong leadership in society. It encourages individuals to seek and celebrate truth, and to recognize the divine joy that comes from living in alignment with these principles.
