तुभ्यं वाता अभिप्रियास तुभ्यम अर्षन्ति सिन्धवः |सोमा वर्धन्ति ते महः ||
tubhyaṁ vātā abhipriyas tubhyam arṣanti sindhavaḥ | soma vardhanti te mahaḥ ||
  • tubhyaṁ - This word translates to "to you" or "for you," indicating a sense of devotion or offering directed towards a divine entity.
  • vātā - This means "winds" or "air," often symbolizing the life force or vital energy that sustains all living beings.
  • abhipriyas - This can be interpreted as "pleasing" or "beloved," suggesting that the winds are not just forces of nature but are also cherished and revered.
  • tubhyam - Reiterating the devotion, this word emphasizes the personal connection to the divine.
  • arṣanti - This means "they flow" or "they move," referring to the rivers or waters that are often seen as life-giving and nurturing.
  • sindhavaḥ - This translates to "rivers," which are vital sources of water, symbolizing abundance and sustenance.
  • soma - This refers to the sacred drink or elixir, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
  • vardhanti - This means "they increase" or "they grow," indicating the flourishing of life and prosperity.
  • te - This translates to "your," reinforcing the connection to the divine.
  • mahaḥ - This means "greatness" or "majesty," signifying the vastness and power of the divine forces at play.

  • Meaning: The mantra expresses a deep reverence for the natural elements, particularly the winds and rivers, as well as the divine essence represented by Soma. It acknowledges the interconnectedness of life and the divine, emphasizing that these elements are not just physical entities but are also imbued with spiritual significance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the importance of wind and water is paramount. Farmers often pray for favorable winds and sufficient rainfall to ensure a good harvest, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In many cultures, rivers are considered sacred. For instance, the Ganges in India is revered as a goddess, and people perform rituals to honor the river, similar to the reverence expressed in this mantra.
    • The concept of Soma can be likened to modern-day practices of seeking inspiration and rejuvenation through meditation or consuming nourishing foods that promote well-being.

  • Conclusion: This mantra highlights the significance of nature and the divine in our lives. It serves as a reminder to respect and honor the elements that sustain us. In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to cultivate a sense of gratitude for the natural world and to recognize the spiritual dimensions of our existence. By acknowledging the winds, rivers, and the essence of life, we can foster a deeper connection with the universe and promote harmony in our lives.
