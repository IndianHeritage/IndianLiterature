समिधाग्निं दुवस्यत घृतैर बोधयतातिथिम् |आस्मिन हव्याः जुहोतना ||
samidhāgniṁ duvasyata ghṛtair bodhayatātithim | āsmin havyā juhotana ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samidhāgniṁ: The fire that is kindled or ignited.
    • duvasyata: To be nourished or fed.
    • ghṛtair: With ghee (clarified butter).
    • bodhayatātithim: Awakening or rousing the guest.
    • āsmin: In this (context).
    • havyā: Offerings or oblations.
    • juhotana: To be offered or sacrificed.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of fire in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and the divine.
    • The act of nourishing the fire with ghee signifies the offering of one's best resources to the divine.
    • Rousing the guest (ātithi) represents the hospitality and respect shown to visitors, which is a significant cultural value.
    • The phrase "in this offering" indicates the context of the ritual, where offerings are made to the divine through the medium of fire.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu weddings, the sacred fire (Agni) is central to the ceremony, where couples make vows and offerings, symbolizing their commitment.
    • During festivals like Diwali, people light lamps and offer sweets to deities, nourishing the divine presence in their homes.
    • In many cultures, hospitality is a virtue; welcoming guests with food and warmth reflects the essence of this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the sacredness of fire and the importance of offerings in spiritual practices.
    • It teaches us about the values of hospitality, respect for guests, and the significance of sharing our resources with the divine and others.
    • In modern life, the principles of this mantra can be applied in fostering community, showing gratitude, and maintaining spiritual practices that honor tradition and connection.
