अग्निं वर्धन्तु नो गिरो यतो जाताय उक्त्यः |महें वाजाय द्रविणाय दर्शतः ||
agniṁ vardhantu no giro yato jāyata ukthyaḥ | mahe vājāya draviṇāya darśataḥ ||
  • Agniṁ: This word refers to "Agni," the fire god in Vedic literature. Fire symbolizes purity, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals.
  • Vardhantu: This means "may it grow" or "may it increase." It signifies a desire for growth and prosperity.
  • No: This translates to "our" or "for us," indicating a collective aspiration.
  • Giro: This word means "words" or "songs." It represents the power of speech and the importance of communication in rituals and life.
  • Yato: This means "from where" or "from which," indicating a source or origin.
  • Jāyata: This translates to "arise" or "be born," suggesting the emergence of something new.
  • Ukthyaḥ: This refers to "sacrificial hymns" or "invocations," emphasizing the role of sacred chants in invoking divine blessings.
  • Mahe: This means "great" or "mighty," indicating the significance of what is being invoked.
  • Vājāya: This translates to "for strength" or "for power," highlighting the pursuit of strength and vitality.
  • Draviṇāya: This means "for wealth" or "for resources," indicating a desire for material abundance.
  • Darśataḥ: This translates to "to be seen" or "to be manifested," suggesting the importance of visibility and realization of desires.

The mantra "agniṁ vardhantu no giro yato jāyata ukthyaḥ | mahe vājāya draviṇāya darśataḥ ||" can be interpreted as a prayer for the growth of our words and invocations, originating from the divine fire, Agni. It expresses a collective wish for strength and wealth to manifest in our lives through the power of sacred chants.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as Agni represents transformation, individuals can use this mantra to inspire personal development and growth in their lives.
  • Communication: The emphasis on "giro" highlights the importance of effective communication. In professional settings, clear and powerful communication can lead to better teamwork and success.
  • Wealth and Prosperity: The desire for "draviṇāya" can be seen in business ventures where individuals seek to create wealth and resources through hard work and dedication.
  • Spiritual Practices: In rituals and prayers, this mantra can be chanted to invoke blessings for strength and abundance, reinforcing the connection between the divine and the material world.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its holistic approach to life, encompassing personal growth, effective communication, and the pursuit of wealth and strength. By invoking Agni, individuals can seek transformation and abundance in their lives, making it applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal development to professional success.
