दिव्यः सुपर्णोऽव चक्षि सोम पिन्वन् धाराः कर्मणा देववीतौ |एन्दो विष कलेशं सोमधानं क्रन्दन्न इहि सूर्यस्योप रश्मिम ||
divyaḥ suparṇo 'va cakṣi soma pinvan dhārāḥ karmaṇā devavītau | endo viśa kalaśaṁ somadhānaṁ krandann ihi sūryasyopa raśmim ||
  • Divyaḥ suparṇo 'va cakṣi: This phrase translates to "the divine, beautiful bird (or eagle) opens its eyes." Here, "divyaḥ" signifies something celestial or divine, while "suparṇo" refers to a bird, often interpreted as a symbol of higher consciousness or spiritual awakening.
  • Soma pinvan dhārāḥ karmaṇā devavītau: "Soma" refers to the sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing immortality and divine inspiration. "Pinvan" means to drink or consume, and "dhārāḥ" refers to streams or flows. This part of the mantra suggests that by consuming the essence of life (Soma), one can engage in righteous actions ("karmaṇā") that lead to divine realization or connection with the gods ("devavītau").
  • Endo viśa kalaśaṁ somadhānaṁ: "Endo" refers to the moon or the essence of life, while "viśa" means to spread or expand. "Kalaśaṁ" is a pot or vessel, and "somadhānaṁ" refers to the offering of Soma. This line emphasizes the importance of sharing and spreading the essence of life, akin to how the moon reflects light and nourishes life on Earth.
  • Krandann ihi sūryasyopa raśmim: "Krandann" means to cry out or call, "ihi" means here or now, "sūryasya" refers to the sun, and "opa raśmim" means the rays or beams of the sun. This suggests a call to the sun's rays, invoking their energy and light, which is essential for life and growth.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine inspiration and enlightenment through the consumption of life’s essence (Soma) and the invocation of celestial energies (the sun and moon). It highlights the interconnectedness of nature, spirituality, and human actions.
  • Real-life Application: In practical terms, this mantra can be applied in daily life by encouraging individuals to seek higher knowledge and consciousness through meditation, mindfulness, and righteous actions. For example, engaging in community service can be seen as a way to embody the principles of "karmaṇā devavītau," where one's actions contribute to the greater good and connect with the divine.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of spiritual awakening, the pursuit of knowledge, and the impact of our actions on the world around us. By aligning ourselves with these divine energies and principles, we can lead more fulfilling and purposeful lives.
