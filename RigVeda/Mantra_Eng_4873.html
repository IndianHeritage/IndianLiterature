विषो-विष इड्यम अध्वरेष्व अदृप्तकृतुम अरतिं युवत्योः |दिवः शिशुं सहसः सूनुम अग्निं यज्ञस्य केतुम अरुषं यजधै ||
viśo-viśa īḍyam adhvareṣv adṛptakratum aratiṁ yuvatyoḥ | divaḥ śiśuṁ sahasaḥ sūnum agniṁ yajñasya ketum aruṣaṁ yajadhyai ||
  • viśo-viśa: This phrase can be interpreted as "the essence of the universe" or "the collective consciousness." It signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of unity.
  • īḍyam: This term refers to something that is worthy of worship or reverence. It emphasizes the need to recognize and honor the divine in all aspects of life.
  • adhvareṣv: This word relates to rituals or sacrifices. It highlights the significance of performing actions with intention and devotion.
  • adṛptakratum: This can be translated as "one who is steadfast in purpose." It suggests the importance of determination and focus in achieving one's goals.
  • aratiṁ yuvatyoḥ: This phrase refers to the "youthful energy" or "vitality" that drives individuals. It signifies the importance of harnessing youthful enthusiasm for constructive purposes.
  • divaḥ śiśuṁ: This translates to "the child of the heavens," symbolizing purity, innocence, and potential. It represents the aspirations and dreams that arise from a higher consciousness.
  • sahasaḥ sūnum: This means "the son of strength," indicating the power and resilience that comes from a strong foundation. It emphasizes the importance of inner strength in overcoming challenges.
  • agniṁ: Agni, or fire, is a central symbol in Vedic literature, representing transformation, energy, and the divine spark within. It signifies the need for purification and the transformative power of knowledge.
  • yajñasya ketum: This phrase refers to the "light" or "guiding principle" of sacrifice. It highlights the importance of selflessness and the greater good in our actions.
  • aruṣaṁ: This term means "the red one," often associated with vitality and life force. It symbolizes the dynamic energy that fuels creation and progress.
  • yajadhyai: This word means "to be worshipped" or "to be offered." It emphasizes the importance of devotion and the act of giving in our spiritual practices.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of unity, purpose, and the transformative power of devotion. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of harnessing our inner strength and youthful energy for constructive actions. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as community service, teamwork, and personal development. By recognizing the divine in ourselves and others, we can foster a sense of belonging and work towards a common goal, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and fulfilling existence.
