स हि क्रतुः स मर्यः स साधुर मित्रो न भूद अद्भुतस्य रथीः |तम मेधेṣu प्रथमं देवयन्तीर विश उप ब्रुवते दसमं आरिः ||
sa hi kratuḥ sa maryaḥ sa sādhur mitro na bhūd adbhutasya rathīḥ | tam medheṣu prathamaṁ devayantīr viśa upa bruvate dasmam ārīḥ ||
  • sa hi kratuḥ: This phrase translates to "Indeed, he is the will" or "He is the intention." In real life, this can be related to the power of determination and focus in achieving one's goals. For example, a student who is determined to excel in their studies embodies this concept by channeling their willpower towards their academic success.
  • sa maryaḥ: This means "he is the noble one" or "he is the brave one." In contemporary terms, this can refer to individuals who display courage and integrity, such as activists fighting for social justice. Their bravery inspires others to stand up for what is right.
  • sa sādhur: This translates to "he is the righteous one." In everyday life, this can be seen in individuals who act ethically and uphold moral values, such as a whistleblower who exposes corruption despite personal risks. Their actions reflect righteousness and integrity.
  • mitro na bhūd adbhutasya rathīḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the friend of the wondrous charioteer." It signifies the importance of companionship and support in achieving extraordinary feats. For instance, a sports team relies on teamwork and camaraderie to win championships, highlighting the value of friendship in overcoming challenges.
  • tam medheṣu prathamaṁ: This means "that one is the first among the wise." It emphasizes the significance of wisdom and knowledge. In real life, this can be related to leaders or mentors who guide others with their insights, such as a teacher who inspires students to think critically and creatively.
  • devayantīr viśa upa bruvate: This translates to "the divine ones speak of him." This suggests that those who are virtuous and wise are recognized and revered by society. For example, historical figures like Mahatma Gandhi are celebrated for their contributions to peace and justice, illustrating how virtuous individuals are honored.
  • dasmam ārīḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the tenth enemy." It symbolizes the challenges and adversities one must face. In real life, this can refer to personal struggles, such as overcoming addiction or mental health issues, which require resilience and strength to conquer.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of willpower, bravery, righteousness, friendship, wisdom, and the recognition of virtuous individuals. Its significance lies in the reminder that these qualities are essential for personal growth and societal progress. In real life, we can apply these teachings by embodying determination in our pursuits, standing up for what is right, supporting one another, seeking wisdom, and recognizing the challenges we face. By doing so, we contribute to a more just and compassionate world.
