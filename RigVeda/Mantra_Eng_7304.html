यद अद्य कर्हि कर्हि चिच्छुश्रूयातम इमं हवम् |अन्ति षड्भूत वाम अवः ||
yad adya karhi karhi cic chuśrūyātam imaṁ havam | anti ṣad bhūtu vām avaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • adya: today
    • karhi: whenever
    • cic: indeed
    • chuśrūyātam: may be heard
    • imaṁ: this
    • havam: offering
    • anti: may they come
    • ṣad: six
    • bhūtu: beings
    • vām: to you
    • avaḥ: may they bring
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a desire for the offerings made today to be heard and acknowledged by the divine.
    • The phrase "may they come" signifies an invitation for the presence of spiritual beings or energies.
    • The mention of "six beings" can be interpreted as a reference to various aspects of existence or different forms of consciousness that are invited to partake in the offering.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be likened to the act of making a heartfelt request or prayer during significant life events, such as weddings or ceremonies, where individuals seek blessings and acknowledgment from higher powers.
    • For instance, during a graduation ceremony, students may express gratitude and hope for future success, akin to making an offering to the universe for recognition and support.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on communication with the divine and the importance of being heard in our spiritual practices.
    • It serves as a reminder that our intentions and offerings, whether in prayer, meditation, or daily actions, are vital in connecting with the greater forces of existence.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages individuals to be mindful of their intentions and to seek a deeper connection with the spiritual realm, fostering a sense of community and shared existence.
