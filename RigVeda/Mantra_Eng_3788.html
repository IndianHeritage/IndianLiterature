प्रियं दुग्धं न काम्यम् अजामि जाम्योः सचा |घर्मो न वाजजठरो 'दब्धः शाश्वतो दभः ||
priyaṁ dugdhaṁ na kāmyam ajāmi jāmyoḥ sacā | gharmo na vājajaṭharo 'dabdhaḥ śaśvato dabhaḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "The beloved milk is not desired; I am born from the unyielding, eternal warmth, and I am not bound by the desires of the world."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • priyaṁ: Beloved or dear.
    • dugdhaṁ: Milk, often symbolizing nourishment and purity.
    • na kāmyam: Not desired or not sought after.
    • ajāmi: I am born or I come into being.
    • jāmyoḥ: From the unyielding or eternal.
    • sacā: And I am (or I exist).
    • gharmo: Warmth or heat, often representing passion or energy.
    • na vājajaṭharo: Not bound by worldly desires.
    • 'dabdhaḥ: Unyielding or eternal.
    • śaśvato: Everlasting or permanent.
    • dabhaḥ: A state of being or existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra reflects the idea of transcending material desires. For instance, a person who chooses to live a minimalist lifestyle may find that they are not attached to material possessions, similar to the sentiment expressed in the mantra.
    • In a professional context, an individual who prioritizes passion over monetary gain in their career embodies the essence of this mantra, focusing on what truly matters to them rather than societal expectations.
    • In relationships, someone who values emotional connection over superficial attractions aligns with the idea of not desiring what is merely beloved but rather seeking deeper, more meaningful bonds.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call to rise above transient desires and to seek a deeper understanding of existence. It encourages individuals to reflect on what truly nourishes their spirit and to embrace the eternal aspects of life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various ways, such as in personal growth, career choices, and relationships, guiding individuals to prioritize what is truly valuable and lasting over fleeting pleasures. By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can cultivate a more fulfilling and meaningful life.
