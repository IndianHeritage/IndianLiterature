वियस्य ते पृथिव्याम् पाजो अश्रेत तृषु यदन्ना समवृक्त जम्बैः |सेनेव सृष्टा प्रसितिष्ठा एति यवम् न दसम जुघ्वा विवेक्षि ||
vi yasya te pṛthivyām pājo aśret tṛṣu yad annā samavṛkta jambhaiḥ | seneva sṛṣṭā prasitiṣ ṭa eti yavaṁ na dasma juhvā vivekṣi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vi - in this context, it can mean "in" or "within".
    • yasya - "of whom" or "whose".
    • te - "your".
    • pṛthivyām - "on the earth" or "in the world".
    • pājo - "the one who is revered" or "the one who is worshipped".
    • aśret - "may reside" or "may dwell".
    • tṛṣu - "in three" or "in the three realms".
    • yad - "that which".
    • annā - "food" or "nourishment".
    • samavṛkta - "surrounded" or "enveloped".
    • jambhaiḥ - "by the fruits" or "by the results".
    • seneva - "like an army".
    • sṛṣṭā - "created" or "formed".
    • prasitiṣṭa - "established" or "set in motion".
    • eti - "goes" or "moves".
    • yavaṁ - "barley" or "grain".
    • na - "not".
    • dasma - "from this" or "from here".
    • juhvā - "offering" or "sacrifice".
    • vivekṣi - "with discernment" or "with wisdom".
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the reverence and connection between the divine and the earthly realm.
    • It emphasizes the importance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual, and how it is surrounded by the fruits of our actions.
    • The mention of "three realms" can be interpreted as the physical, mental, and spiritual dimensions of existence.
    • It suggests that just as an army is organized and established, so too should our lives be structured around the principles of wisdom and discernment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, this mantra can remind us to be mindful of the food we consume, understanding that it nourishes not just the body but also the mind and spirit.
    • In a workplace setting, the idea of being "like an army" can be applied to teamwork, where each member plays a crucial role in achieving a common goal.
    • In personal relationships, the concept of discernment can guide us in making wise choices about whom we surround ourselves with and how we interact with others.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all aspects of life—physical, mental, and spiritual.
    • Its significance lies in promoting a balanced approach to living, where nourishment, wisdom, and community are valued.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to a more harmonious existence, fostering growth and understanding in various aspects of life.
