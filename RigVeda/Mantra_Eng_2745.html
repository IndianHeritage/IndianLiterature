इन्द्रस्य कर्म सुकृतानि पुरूणि व्रतानि देवाः न मिनन्ति विश्वे | दाधार यः पृथिवीं द्यां उतेमां जजाना सूर्यं उषसं सुदंसाः ||
indrasya karma sukṛtā purūṇi vratāni devā na minanti viśve | dādhāra yaḥ pṛthivīṁ dyām utemāṁ jajāna sūryam uṣasaṁ sudaṁsāḥ ||
  • Indrasya karma: This phrase refers to the actions or deeds of Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology. Indra is often associated with strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • Sukṛtā purūṇi: "Sukṛtā" means good or virtuous, while "purūṇi" refers to many or numerous. Together, this suggests that the actions of Indra are numerous and virtuous, highlighting the importance of righteous deeds.
  • Vratāni devā: "Vratāni" means vows or commitments, and "devā" refers to the gods. This indicates that the gods uphold certain vows or principles that guide their actions and interactions with the world.
  • Na minanti viśve: "Na minanti" means do not diminish or do not fail, and "viśve" refers to all or the universe. This implies that the virtuous actions of Indra and the gods do not diminish the overall harmony of the universe.
  • Dādhāra: This term means to support or uphold. It signifies the role of the gods in maintaining the balance and order of the cosmos.
  • Yaḥ pṛthivīṁ dyām utemāṁ: "Yaḥ" means who, "pṛthivīṁ" refers to the earth, and "dyām" refers to the sky or heavens. This part emphasizes the connection between the earth and the heavens, suggesting that the actions of the gods influence both realms.
  • Jajāna sūryam uṣasaṁ sudaṁsāḥ: "Jajāna" means to give birth or create, "sūryam" refers to the sun, "uṣasaṁ" means dawn, and "sudaṁsāḥ" refers to the beautiful or auspicious. This indicates that the gods are responsible for the creation of light, dawn, and the sun, which are essential for life.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of virtuous actions and the role of divine beings in maintaining cosmic order. It highlights the interconnectedness of the earth and heavens, suggesting that our actions, guided by virtue and commitment, contribute to the overall harmony of the universe. In real life, this can be applied by encouraging individuals to engage in righteous deeds, uphold their commitments, and recognize the impact of their actions on the world around them. By embodying these principles, we can contribute to a more harmonious and balanced existence, much like the gods in the mantra.
