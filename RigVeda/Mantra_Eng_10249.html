मन्त्र: कासीत प्रमā प्रतिमा किम निदानम् आज्यं किम आसीत परिधिः का आसीत | चन्दः किम आसीत प्रउगं किम उक्तं यद देवाः देवम् अयजन्त विश्वे ||
kāsīt pramā pratimā kiṁ nidānam ājyaṁ kim āsīt paridhiḥ ka āsīt | chandaḥ kim āsīt praügaṁ kim ukthaṁ yad devā devam ayajanta viśve ||
  • kāsīt pramā pratimā: This phrase can be interpreted as "What was the measure, what was the form?" It raises questions about the essence and nature of existence. In real life, this can relate to our quest for understanding the universe and our place within it. For example, scientists seek to measure the universe through various means, such as physics and astronomy.
  • kiṁ nidānam ājyaṁ: Translated, this means "What is the source of the clarified butter?" In Vedic rituals, clarified butter (ghee) is a significant offering. This can symbolize nourishment and sustenance in our lives. In a modern context, it can relate to the sources of our sustenance, whether they be physical, emotional, or spiritual.
  • kim āsīt paridhiḥ: This translates to "What was the boundary?" It prompts us to consider the limits and boundaries we encounter in life. In practical terms, this can refer to personal boundaries in relationships or societal boundaries that define our interactions. For instance, understanding and respecting personal space in social situations is crucial for healthy relationships.
  • ka āsīt: This phrase means "What was?" It invites reflection on the past and the events that have shaped our current reality. In everyday life, this can be seen in how we learn from history to make informed decisions for the future, such as studying past economic trends to predict future market behavior.
  • chandaḥ kim āsīt: This translates to "What was the rhythm?" It suggests a search for harmony and order in the universe. In real life, this can be related to the rhythms we find in nature, such as the changing seasons, or in our daily routines, like the balance between work and leisure.
  • praügaṁ kim ukthaṁ: This means "What was the utterance?" It emphasizes the power of words and communication. In contemporary society, this can be reflected in the impact of social media and how our words can influence others, for better or worse. For example, a single tweet can spark a global movement.
  • yad devā devam ayajanta viśve: This translates to "What did the gods worship, the all-encompassing?" It highlights the universal search for the divine or the higher power. In real life, this can be seen in the various forms of worship and spirituality across cultures, where people seek connection with something greater than themselves, whether through religion, nature, or community.

Conclusion: The mantra from the Rigveda invites us to ponder fundamental questions about existence, boundaries, communication, and the divine. Its significance lies in its ability to inspire introspection and a deeper understanding of our lives and the world around us. In real-life scenarios, these themes are applicable in various contexts, such as personal growth, relationships, and our quest for knowledge and spirituality. By reflecting on these questions, we can cultivate a more meaningful and connected existence.
