युवानो रुद्राऽजराऽभोग्घनो ववक्षुर अध्रिगावः पर्वताऽ इव |दृढा चिद् विश्वा भुवनानि पार्थिवा प्र च्यावयन्ति दिव्यानी मजनना ||
yuvāno rudrā ajarā abhogghano vavakṣur adhrigāvaḥ parvatā iva | dṛḻhā cid viśvā bhuvanāni pārthivā pra cyāvayanti divyāni majmanā ||
  • yuvāno rudrā: This phrase refers to the youthful and dynamic aspect of Rudra, a deity associated with storms and the hunt. In real life, this can symbolize the energy and vigor of youth, representing the potential for growth and change.
  • ajarā: Meaning "immortal" or "everlasting," this term highlights the eternal nature of the divine. In a practical sense, it can remind us of the enduring qualities of wisdom and knowledge that transcend time.
  • abhogghano: This word can be interpreted as "one who is full of strength" or "powerful." In everyday life, it signifies the importance of resilience and strength in overcoming challenges.
  • vavakṣur: This term means "they have spoken" or "they have declared." It emphasizes the power of words and communication, which can be seen in how leaders and influencers shape society through their speech.
  • adhri-gāvaḥ: This phrase translates to "like the mountains." Mountains symbolize stability and permanence, reminding us of the importance of having a strong foundation in our lives.
  • parvatā iva: This reinforces the previous idea, comparing the strength of Rudra to that of mountains, which can be seen in the way people draw inspiration from nature's resilience.
  • dṛḻhā cid: This means "firm and stable." In real life, it can refer to the importance of having a solid character and principles that guide our actions.
  • viśvā bhuvanāni: This translates to "all worlds" or "all realms." It signifies the interconnectedness of all existence, reminding us that our actions have far-reaching consequences.
  • pārthivā: Meaning "earthly" or "material," this term emphasizes the physical world and our responsibilities within it, such as caring for the environment and our communities.
  • pra cyāvayanti: This means "they bring forth" or "they manifest." It highlights the creative power inherent in all beings, encouraging us to harness our potential to create positive change.
  • divyāni majmanā: This phrase translates to "divine manifestations." It suggests that through our actions and intentions, we can bring forth divine qualities in our lives and the lives of others.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of youthful energy, strength, and the eternal nature of the divine. It serves as a reminder of the importance of resilience, communication, and the interconnectedness of all existence. In real life, we can apply these teachings by striving for personal growth, fostering strong relationships, and being mindful of our impact on the world around us. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous society.
