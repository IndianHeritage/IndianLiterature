उदप्रुतो न वयो रक्षमाṇā वावदतो अभ्रियस्येव घोषाः |गिरिभ्रजो नर्मयो मदन्तो बृहस्पतिम अभ्य अर्का अनावन ||
udapruto na vayo rakṣamāṇā vāvadato abhriyasyeva ghoṣāḥ | giribhrajo normayo madanto bṛhaspatim abhy a1rkā anāvan ||
  • udapruto: This term can be interpreted as "having been brought forth from the waters" or "emerging from the depths." In a real-life context, this can symbolize new beginnings or the emergence of ideas and creativity from the subconscious, much like how a seed grows into a plant after being nourished by water.
  • na vayo: This phrase translates to "not old" or "not aged." It signifies freshness, youthfulness, and vitality. In everyday life, this can relate to the idea of maintaining a youthful spirit and approach to life, regardless of physical age.
  • rakṣamāṇā: This means "being protected" or "guarded." It emphasizes the importance of safety and security in our lives. For instance, in a community, the protection of its members can be seen in the way people look out for one another, ensuring a safe environment for all.
  • vāvadato: This can be understood as "speaking" or "expressing." It highlights the significance of communication and expression in human relationships. In real life, effective communication is crucial in resolving conflicts and building strong connections with others.
  • abhriyasyeva ghoṣāḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "like the sound of the rich." It suggests abundance and prosperity. In practical terms, this can relate to the joy and celebration that comes with achieving success, whether in personal endeavors or community achievements.
  • giribhrajo: This term refers to "the gathering of mountains" or "the assembly of great beings." It symbolizes strength and unity. In real life, this can be seen in teamwork and collaboration, where individuals come together to achieve a common goal, much like mountains standing strong together.
  • normayo: This translates to "not ordinary" or "extraordinary." It signifies the pursuit of excellence and the aspiration to rise above mediocrity. In everyday life, this can inspire individuals to strive for greatness in their personal and professional lives.
  • madanto: This means "joyful" or "delighted." It reflects the importance of happiness and contentment in life. For example, celebrating small victories and finding joy in everyday moments can enhance overall well-being.
  • bṛhaspatim: This refers to "Brihaspati," the deity of wisdom and knowledge. It emphasizes the value of learning and wisdom in guiding our actions. In real life, seeking knowledge and wisdom can lead to better decision-making and personal growth.
  • abhy a1rkā anāvan: This phrase can be interpreted as "approaching the light" or "moving towards enlightenment." It signifies the journey towards understanding and awareness. In practical terms, this can relate to personal development and the pursuit of truth in one’s life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of emergence, protection, communication, abundance, unity, excellence, joy, wisdom, and enlightenment. Its significance lies in the encouragement to embrace new beginnings, maintain a youthful spirit, protect one another, communicate effectively, celebrate abundance, work together towards common goals, strive for excellence, seek wisdom, and pursue enlightenment. In real life, these principles can guide individuals and communities towards a harmonious and fulfilling existence, fostering a sense of purpose and connection in an ever-changing world.
