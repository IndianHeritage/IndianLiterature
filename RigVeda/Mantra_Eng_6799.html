इमां गायत्रवर्तनीं जुषेथां सुश्रुतिम मम |इन्द्राग्नि आ गतं नराः ||
imāṁ gāyatravartaniṁ juṣethāṁ suṣṭutim mama | indrāgnī ā gataṁ narā ||
  • imāṁ - This word translates to "this" or "these," indicating a focus on the subject at hand.
  • gāyatravartaniṁ - This refers to the "Gāyatrī," a sacred meter in Vedic literature, symbolizing the essence of knowledge and enlightenment.
  • juṣethāṁ - This means "may you enjoy" or "may you appreciate," suggesting a desire for the recipient to find joy or value in what is being offered.
  • suṣṭutim - This translates to "good praise" or "excellent commendation," indicating the importance of recognizing and honoring virtues.
  • mama - Meaning "my," it personalizes the offering or sentiment being expressed.
  • indrāgnī - This compound word refers to "Indra" and "Agni," two significant deities in Vedic tradition; Indra represents strength and power, while Agni symbolizes fire and transformation.
  • ā gataṁ - This means "has come" or "has arrived," indicating a sense of presence or manifestation.
  • narā - This translates to "men" or "humans," emphasizing the human experience and the connection to the divine.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to appreciate and honor the divine qualities represented by Indra and Agni, while also recognizing the importance of knowledge and praise in our lives. It suggests that by embracing these virtues, we can enhance our own existence and the lives of those around us.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the Gāyatrī represents enlightenment, individuals can seek knowledge and wisdom in their daily lives, striving for personal development.
  • Community Building: By praising and recognizing the strengths of others (like Indra's power), we foster a supportive environment that encourages collaboration and unity.
  • Transformation: Agni symbolizes transformation; individuals can embrace change in their lives, using challenges as opportunities for growth.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of knowledge, praise, and the divine qualities embodied by Indra and Agni. It serves as a reminder to appreciate the virtues in ourselves and others, fostering a sense of community and personal transformation. By integrating these principles into our daily lives, we can create a more harmonious and enlightened existence.
