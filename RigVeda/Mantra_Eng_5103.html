इन्द्रासोमा महि तद्वाम महित्वं युवं महानी प्रथमानी चक्रतुः |युवं सूर्यं विविधथुर युवं स्वार विश्वा तमांस्य अहतं निदश च ||
indrāsomā mahi tad vām mahitvaṁ yuvam mahāni prathamāni cakrathuḥ | yuvaṁ sūryaṁ vividathur yuvaṁ sva1r viśvā tamāṁsy ahataṁ nidaś ca ||
  • Indrāsomā: This term refers to the combined powers of Indra, the king of gods, and Soma, the divine nectar. Indra represents strength and power, while Soma symbolizes vitality and immortality.
  • Mahi tad vām mahitvaṁ: This phrase translates to "great is your glory." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring the greatness of these divine forces.
  • Yuvam mahāni prathamāni cakrathuḥ: This means "you two, the great ones, are the first to be invoked." It highlights the significance of calling upon these deities for blessings and support in our endeavors.
  • Yuvaṁ sūryaṁ: This refers to the youthful sun, symbolizing energy, life, and enlightenment. The sun is often seen as a source of inspiration and guidance.
  • Vividathur: This word means "varied" or "diverse," indicating the multifaceted nature of existence and the different paths one can take in life.
  • Sva1r viśvā tamāṁsy ahataṁ nidaś ca: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who brings light to all darkness." It signifies the role of divine forces in dispelling ignorance and guiding individuals towards knowledge and truth.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of invoking divine energies for strength, vitality, and enlightenment. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts such as:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or prayer to seek guidance and strength in their personal journeys.
  • Community and Leadership: Leaders can invoke the qualities of Indra and Soma to inspire and uplift their communities, promoting unity and collective strength.
  • Education and Knowledge: The reference to dispelling darkness can be applied in educational settings, encouraging the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine support available to us in our endeavors and the importance of recognizing and honoring these forces in our lives.
