मंहिष्ठं वो मघोनां राजानं चर्षणीनाम् | इन्द्रं उप प्रशस्तये पूर्वीभिर जुजुषे गिरः ||
maṁhiṣṭhaṁ vo maghonāṁ rājānaṁ carṣaṇīnām | indram upa praśastaye pūrvībhir jujuṣe giraḥ ||
  • maṁhiṣṭhaṁ: This word translates to "the best" or "the most excellent." It signifies the highest quality or the supreme aspect of something.
  • vo: This means "to you" or "for you," indicating a direction towards the audience or the deities being addressed.
  • maghonāṁ: This refers to "of the wealthy" or "of the prosperous." It highlights abundance and richness, both materially and spiritually.
  • rājānaṁ: This translates to "of kings" or "of rulers." It signifies authority, leadership, and governance.
  • carṣaṇīnām: This word means "of the seers" or "of the wise." It refers to those who possess knowledge and insight.
  • indram: This is a reference to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power, strength, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • upa: This means "towards" or "near," indicating a movement or approach towards something.
  • praśastaye: This translates to "for praise" or "for commendation," suggesting an intention to honor or glorify.
  • pūrvībhir: This means "by the ancients" or "by those who came before," indicating a connection to tradition and the wisdom of past generations.
  • jujuṣe: This translates to "you have desired" or "you have wished," indicating a longing or aspiration.
  • giraḥ: This means "words" or "speech," emphasizing the power of communication and expression.

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to the divine, specifically addressing Indra, the king of the gods, to bestow excellence, prosperity, and wisdom upon the rulers and seers. It emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance from the wise and honoring the traditions of the past.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: Leaders can reflect on the qualities of excellence and wisdom, striving to embody these traits in their governance.
  • Community Prosperity: Communities can come together to seek abundance and prosperity, invoking the spirit of cooperation and shared goals.
  • Respect for Tradition: Individuals can honor the wisdom of their ancestors, integrating traditional knowledge into modern practices.
  • Effective Communication: The significance of words and their impact can be recognized, encouraging thoughtful and respectful dialogue.

Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of leadership, wisdom, and tradition. It encourages individuals and communities to aspire for excellence and prosperity while respecting the knowledge passed down through generations. By invoking the qualities of Indra, it inspires a collective effort towards achieving greatness in various aspects of life.
