त्वं पिप्रम् मृगयं शूशुवांसम् ऋजिष्वने वैदथिनाय रन्धीः |पञ्चाशत कृष्णा न वपः सहस्रात्कं न पुरो जरीमा विदर्दः ||
tvam piprum mṛgayaṁ śūśuvāṁsam ṛjiśvane vaidathināya randhīḥ | pañcāśat kṛṣṇā ni vapaḥ sahasrātkaṁ na puro jarimā vi dardaḥ ||
  • tvam piprum: This phrase can be interpreted as "You are the one who is sought after" or "You are the essence of desire." In real life, this can relate to the pursuit of goals or aspirations that individuals strive for, such as career achievements or personal growth.
  • mṛgayaṁ: This word means "the hunt" or "the pursuit." It signifies the journey one undertakes in search of something valuable. For example, a student may go through rigorous studies in pursuit of knowledge and success.
  • śūśuvāṁsam: This term can be translated as "the one who is adorned" or "the one who is rich in qualities." It reflects the idea of possessing virtues or attributes that are admirable. In a real-life context, this could refer to individuals who are respected for their integrity and character.
  • ṛjiśvane: This can mean "the one who is straight" or "the one who is honest." Honesty is a fundamental value in personal and professional relationships, and it is essential for building trust.
  • vaidathināya: This word refers to "the one who is wise" or "the one who possesses knowledge." Wisdom is often sought after in leaders and mentors, guiding others through their experiences and insights.
  • randhīḥ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who creates" or "the one who brings forth." It signifies the act of creation, whether it be in art, innovation, or nurturing relationships.
  • pañcāśat: This means "fifty" and can symbolize abundance or a large quantity. In life, this could represent the multitude of opportunities available to individuals if they are willing to seek them out.
  • kṛṣṇā: This word translates to "black" or "dark," often associated with depth and mystery. It can represent the unknown aspects of life that one must navigate, such as challenges or uncertainties.
  • ni vapaḥ: This phrase can mean "the one who is not to be ignored" or "the one who is significant." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and valuing what truly matters in life.
  • sahasrātkaṁ: This term translates to "thousands" or "many." It signifies the vastness of experiences and lessons one can encounter throughout life.
  • na puro: This means "not in front" or "not visible." It can symbolize the hidden aspects of life that require introspection and self-discovery.
  • jarimā: This word refers to "old age" or "decay." It serves as a reminder of the transient nature of life and the importance of making the most of the present moment.
  • vi dardaḥ: This can be interpreted as "the one who does not falter" or "the one who remains steadfast." It highlights the value of resilience and perseverance in the face of challenges.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking wisdom, honesty, and resilience in life's pursuits. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing one's goals and the qualities that lead to success. In real-life scenarios, this mantra serves as a guiding principle for individuals striving for personal and professional growth, encouraging them to embrace their journey with integrity and determination. By understanding and applying these values, one can navigate the complexities of life with purpose and clarity.
