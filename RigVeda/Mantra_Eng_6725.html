क ईं वेद सुत सचा पिबन्तं कद वयो दधे |अयं यः पुरो विभिनत्त्य ओजसा मंदानः शिप्र्य अन्धसः ||
ka īṁ veda sute sacā pibantaṁ kad vayo dadhe | ayaṁ yaḥ puro vibhinatty ojasā mandānaḥ śipry andhasaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ka: who
    • īṁ: indeed
    • veda: knowledge or wisdom
    • sute: in the drink or essence
    • sacā: and truth
    • pibantaṁ: drinking
    • kad: when
    • vayo: age or life
    • dadhe: bestows or gives
    • ayaṁ: this
    • yaḥ: who
    • puro: in the front or before
    • vibhinatty: separates or distinguishes
    • ojasā: with energy or strength
    • mandānaḥ: slow or gentle
    • śipry: in the darkness
    • andhasaḥ: of the blind or ignorance
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a question about the nature of knowledge and truth.
    • It suggests that true wisdom is like a drink that nourishes life.
    • The phrase "who indeed drinks the essence of knowledge" implies that only those who seek truth and wisdom can truly benefit from it.
    • The mantra also reflects on the idea of age and the gifts it bestows, hinting at the wisdom that comes with experience.
    • Furthermore, it emphasizes the importance of distinguishing between what is beneficial and what is not, especially in the context of ignorance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, students who actively seek knowledge and engage with their studies are more likely to succeed, akin to "drinking" the essence of learning.
    • In personal development, individuals who reflect on their experiences and learn from them can gain wisdom over time, illustrating the idea of age bestowing knowledge.
    • In decision-making, distinguishing between beneficial choices and those that lead to ignorance is crucial for personal and professional growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking knowledge and truth in life.
    • It serves as a reminder that wisdom is not just about age but about the conscious effort to learn and grow.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to better decision-making, personal growth, and a deeper understanding of oneself and the world.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to be proactive in their pursuit of knowledge, ensuring they do not remain in the darkness of ignorance.
