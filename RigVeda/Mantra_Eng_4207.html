सम्राजाग उग्रा वृषभा दिवस पति पृथिव्या मित्रावरुणा विचर्षणी |चित्रैर्भिर अभ्रैर उप तिष्ठथो रवं द्यां वर्षयथो असुरस्य मायया ||
samrājā ugrā vṛṣabhā divas patī pṛthivyā mitrāvaruṇā vicarṣaṇī | citrebhir abhrair upa tiṣṭhatho ravaṁ dyāṁ varṣayatho asurasya māyayā ||
  • Mantra: "samrājā ugrā vṛṣabhā divas patī pṛthivyā mitrāvaruṇā vicarṣaṇī | citrebhir abhrair upa tiṣṭhatho ravaṁ dyāṁ varṣayatho asurasya māyayā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • samrājā: the great king or ruler
    • ugrā: fierce or powerful
    • vṛṣabhā: the bull, symbolizing strength and virility
    • divas: of the day or heavenly
    • patī: lord or master
    • pṛthivyā: of the earth
    • mitrāvaruṇā: the divine pair of Mitra and Varuna, representing friendship and cosmic order
    • vicarṣaṇī: the one who moves or travels
    • citrebhir: with colorful or varied
    • abhrair: clouds
    • upa tiṣṭhatho: standing near or approaching
    • ravaṁ: the roar or sound
    • dyāṁ: the sky or heavens
    • varṣayatho: may you rain
    • asurasya: of the demon or negative forces
    • māyayā: by illusion or magic
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes the powerful ruler of the heavens and the earth, symbolizing the strength and authority of nature.
    • The mention of "Mitra and Varuna" highlights the importance of harmony and balance in the universe, akin to the relationship between friends or allies in real life.
    • The imagery of colorful clouds and the act of raining signifies abundance and nourishment, much like how rain is essential for agriculture and sustenance.
    • The reference to "asurasya māyayā" serves as a reminder of the challenges and illusions we face in life, urging us to seek clarity and truth amidst confusion.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the arrival of rain is crucial for crop growth, symbolizing the blessings of nature that this mantra seeks to invoke.
    • In personal relationships, the principles of friendship and cooperation (Mitra and Varuna) can be applied to foster better communication and understanding among individuals.
    • In facing life's challenges, recognizing the "asuras" or negative influences can help individuals make better choices and seek positive paths.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of nature, the importance of balance, and the need to overcome illusions in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals to seek strength, harmony, and clarity in their personal and communal lives.
    • By reflecting on these themes, one can cultivate a deeper understanding of their place in the world and the forces that shape their experiences.
