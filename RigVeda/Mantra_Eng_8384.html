समुद्रियाऽप्सरसो मनीषिणमासीनान्तरभि सोममक्षरन्। ताऽईं हिन्वन्ति हरम्यस्य सक्षणिं याचन्ते सुम्नं पवमानमक्षितम्॥
samudriyā apsaraso manīṣiṇam āsīnā antar abhi somam akṣaran | tā īṁ hinvanti harmyasya sakṣaṇiṁ yācante sumnam pavamānam akṣitam ||
  • Translation: The mantra speaks of the divine waters (apsarasa) and the wise beings (manīṣiṇam) who are seated within them, invoking the immortal essence (somam akṣaran). They are the ones who bring forth the blessings (sumnam) from the celestial abode (harmyasya) and seek the pure, unblemished (akṣitam) essence of life.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • samudriyā: Pertaining to the ocean or waters, symbolizing depth and vastness.
    • apsaraso: Refers to celestial nymphs or divine waters, often associated with beauty and purity.
    • manīṣiṇam: Wise beings or sages, representing knowledge and enlightenment.
    • āsīnā: Seated or established, indicating a state of being grounded or centered.
    • antar: Within or inside, suggesting introspection or inner exploration.
    • abhi: Towards or in the direction of, indicating intention or focus.
    • somam: The essence of life or immortality, often linked to the moon and its nourishing qualities.
    • akṣaran: Imperishable or eternal, signifying the everlasting nature of the essence.
    • tā: They, referring to the divine beings or energies.
    • īṁ: A particle that can denote direction or intention.
    • hinvanti: They bring forth or invoke, indicating action and manifestation.
    • harmyasya: Of the celestial abode or heavenly realm, representing higher consciousness.
    • sakṣaṇiṁ: The essence or core, often related to the heart or center of being.
    • yācante: They seek or request, indicating a desire for connection or communion.
    • sumnam: Blessings or auspiciousness, representing positive outcomes.
    • pavamānam: Pure or purified, indicating clarity and sanctity.
    • akṣitam: Unblemished or intact, signifying wholeness and integrity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In meditation practices, individuals often seek to connect with their inner wisdom (manīṣiṇam) and tap into the vastness of their consciousness (samudriyā) to gain insights and clarity.
    • In environmental conservation, the idea of protecting our waters (apsaraso) reflects the need to preserve the purity of nature, which is essential for sustaining life (somam akṣaran).
    • In personal development, individuals may strive to cultivate their inner essence (akṣitam) by engaging in practices that promote mental and emotional well-being, seeking blessings (sumnam) for a fulfilling life.
  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of connecting with the divine essence within and around us. It encourages individuals to seek wisdom, purity, and blessings in their lives. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in practices of meditation, environmental stewardship, and personal growth, reminding us of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of nurturing our inner and outer worlds.
