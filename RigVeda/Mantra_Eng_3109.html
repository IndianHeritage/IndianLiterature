कृणुष्व पाजः प्रसितिं न पृथ्वीं याहि राजेवामवाम् इभेन |तृष्विम् अनु प्रसितिं द्रुणानोऽस्तासी विध्य रक्षसस् तपिष्ठैः ||
kṛṇuṣva pājaḥ prasitiṁ na pṛthvīṁ yāhi rājevāmavām̐ ibhena | tṛṣvīm anu prasitiṁ drūṇāno 'stāsi vidhya rakṣasas tapiṣṭhaiḥ ||
  • kṛṇuṣva: This word translates to "make" or "create." It signifies the act of bringing something into existence or manifesting a desire.
  • pājaḥ: This refers to "worship" or "offering." It emphasizes the importance of devotion and the act of giving in spiritual practices.
  • prasitiṁ: This means "prosperity" or "well-being." It indicates a state of abundance and success that one seeks to achieve.
  • na pṛthvīṁ: This translates to "not the earth." It suggests a focus beyond the material world, hinting at spiritual or higher aspirations.
  • yāhi: This means "go" or "move towards." It is an invitation to pursue a path or direction.
  • rājevāmavām̐: This can be interpreted as "to the king" or "to the ruler." It signifies authority and leadership, possibly referring to a higher power or divine entity.
  • ibhena: This word means "with strength" or "with power." It emphasizes the need for vigor and determination in one's pursuits.
  • tṛṣvīm: This translates to "thirst" or "desire." It represents the longing for something greater, whether it be knowledge, success, or spiritual fulfillment.
  • anu: This means "following" or "after." It indicates a journey or progression towards a goal.
  • drūṇāno: This can be interpreted as "with determination" or "with resolve." It emphasizes the importance of steadfastness in one's efforts.
  • 'stāsi: This means "you will stand" or "you will remain." It signifies stability and endurance in the face of challenges.
  • vidhya: This translates to "knowledge" or "wisdom." It highlights the importance of understanding and insight in achieving one's goals.
  • rakṣasas: This refers to "demons" or "obstacles." It symbolizes the challenges and adversities one may face in life.
  • tapiṣṭhaiḥ: This means "with strength" or "with intensity." It emphasizes the need for powerful resolve to overcome difficulties.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about the pursuit of prosperity and spiritual fulfillment. It encourages individuals to create their own path through devotion and determination, while also acknowledging the challenges that may arise. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as striving for personal goals, overcoming obstacles in one's career, or seeking spiritual growth. The mantra serves as a reminder that with strength, knowledge, and unwavering resolve, one can navigate the complexities of life and achieve their aspirations.
