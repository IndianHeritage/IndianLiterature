उभोभयाविन्न उप दहेहि दंष्ट्रा हिंस्रः शिशानोऽवरं परं च |उतान्तरिक्षे परि याहि राजाñ जम्बैः सं दहेय अभि यातुधानान् ||
ubhobhayāvinn upa dhehi daṁṣṭrā hiṁsraḥ śiśāno 'varam paraṁ ca | utāntarikṣe pari yāhi rājañ jambhaiḥ saṁ dhehy abhi yātudhānān ||
  • Mantra: "ubhobhayāvinn upa dhehi daṁṣṭrā hiṁsraḥ śiśāno 'varam paraṁ ca | utāntarikṣe pari yāhi rājañ jambhaiḥ saṁ dhehy abhi yātudhānān ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ubhobhayāvinn: Both sides or both aspects
    • upa dhehi: Please bring near or approach
    • daṁṣṭrā: With fangs or teeth
    • hiṁsraḥ: Fierce or harmful
    • śiśāno: The one who is born or created
    • 'varam: The best or the most excellent
    • paraṁ: The ultimate or supreme
    • ca: And
    • utāntarikṣe: In the sky or in the atmosphere
    • pari: All around or everywhere
    • yāhi: Go or move
    • rājañ: O king or ruler
    • jambhaiḥ: With the help of the powerful
    • saṁ dhehy: Please bring together or unite
    • abhi: Towards or against
    • yātudhānān: The evil spirits or malevolent beings
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra invokes a sense of duality, suggesting the need to balance both sides of a situation.
    • The phrase "upa dhehi" indicates a call to bring forth or approach something that may be harmful, represented by "daṁṣṭrā hiṁsraḥ," which refers to fierce or dangerous aspects of life.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking the best or supreme outcome ("'varam paraṁ") in the face of challenges.
    • The mention of "utāntarikṣe" suggests a connection to the divine or higher powers, indicating that one should look beyond the immediate and seek guidance from the universe.
    • The call to "yāhi rājañ" signifies the importance of leadership and guidance in overcoming obstacles, while "jambhaiḥ" implies the need for strength and support from powerful allies.
    • Finally, the mantra addresses the need to confront and unite against malevolent forces ("yātudhānān"), suggesting a proactive approach to dealing with negativity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace setting, this mantra can be applied when facing competition or challenges. A leader may need to unite their team ("saṁ dhehy") to confront external pressures ("yātudhānān") and strive for the best outcomes ("'varam paraṁ").
    • In personal relationships, it can serve as a reminder to address conflicts directly and seek resolution, balancing both perspectives ("ubhobhayāvinn") to achieve harmony.
    • In times of crisis, such as natural disasters, communities can come together ("yāhi rājañ") to support each other and confront the challenges posed by the situation, drawing strength from collective efforts ("jambhaiḥ").
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of balance, unity, and proactive engagement in the face of adversity.
    • It serves as a reminder that while challenges may be fierce and daunting, seeking the best outcomes and drawing on collective strength can lead to overcoming obstacles.
    • In essence, it encourages individuals and communities to confront negativity with courage and collaboration, ultimately leading to growth and resilience.
