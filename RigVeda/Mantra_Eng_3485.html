उत स्य वाजी सहुरिर ऋतावा शुश्रूषमाणस तन्वा समर्ये |तुरं यतीषु तुरयन्न ऋजिप्योऽधि भ्रुवोः किरते रेणुम ऋञ्जन ||
uta sya vājī sahurir ṛtāvā śuśrūṣamāṇas tanvā samarye | turaṁ yatīṣu turayann ṛjipyo 'dhi bhruvoḥ kirate reṇum ṛñjan ||
  • uta sya vājī: This phrase can be translated as "may we be endowed with strength." The term "vājī" refers to a horse, symbolizing power and vitality. In real life, this can be related to the pursuit of physical fitness and mental strength, where individuals strive to enhance their capabilities.
  • sahurir ṛtāvā: This means "the one who is strong and righteous." It emphasizes the importance of strength that is aligned with truth and righteousness. In contemporary life, this can be seen in leaders or individuals who use their power for just causes, advocating for integrity in their actions.
  • śuśrūṣamāṇas tanvā: This translates to "with a body that is attentive and ready to serve." It highlights the significance of being prepared and willing to assist others. In everyday scenarios, this can be exemplified by community service or volunteering, where individuals contribute their time and effort for the benefit of others.
  • samarye: This word means "in the battle." It signifies the challenges and struggles one faces in life. In a real-life context, this can relate to personal battles, such as overcoming obstacles in career or health, where resilience is key.
  • turaṁ yatīṣu: This phrase can be interpreted as "swiftly moving among the strong." It suggests the idea of agility and adaptability in the face of challenges. In modern life, this can be seen in how businesses adapt to market changes or how individuals pivot in their careers to seize new opportunities.
  • turayann ṛjipyo: This means "overcoming the obstacles." It emphasizes the importance of perseverance and determination. In real life, this can be illustrated by stories of individuals who have faced significant hardships but have managed to rise above them through sheer willpower.
  • 'dhi bhruvoḥ: This translates to "with the wisdom of the wise." It underscores the value of learning from those who are knowledgeable. In practical terms, this can be applied in mentorship relationships, where experienced individuals guide others in their personal or professional journeys.
  • kirate reṇum ṛñjan: This phrase means "to create a path through the dust." It symbolizes the act of forging one's own way in life. In contemporary scenarios, this can be seen in entrepreneurs who innovate and create new markets, paving the way for future generations.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, righteousness, readiness to serve, and the wisdom to navigate life's challenges. Its significance lies in the encouragement it provides to individuals to cultivate inner strength, act with integrity, and remain adaptable in the face of adversity. In real-life applications, it serves as a guiding principle for personal development, leadership, and community engagement, inspiring individuals to strive for excellence while contributing positively to society.
