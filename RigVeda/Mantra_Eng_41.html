आ त्व एता नि शीदतेन्द्रम् अभि प्र गायत |सखायः स्तोमवाहसः ||
ā tv etā ni ṣīdatendram abhi pra gāyata | sakhāyaḥ stomavāhasaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: From, away
    • tv: You
    • etā: These
    • ni: Down, in place
    • ṣīdat: Sit, settle
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods
    • abhi: Towards, in the direction of
    • pra: Forth, forward
    • gāyata: Sing, chant
    • sakhāyaḥ: Friends, companions
    • stomavāhasaḥ: Those who carry praises, hymns
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra can be understood as a call to the divine, specifically to Indra, to come and settle among us.
    • The phrase "ā tv etā ni ṣīdatendram" suggests an invitation for Indra to take his place among us, indicating a desire for divine presence and guidance.
    • "abhi pra gāyata" emphasizes the act of singing or chanting, which is a form of devotion and connection to the divine.
    • The mention of "sakhāyaḥ stomavāhasaḥ" highlights the importance of community and friendship in the act of worship, suggesting that collective praise is powerful.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, people gather to sing hymns or chants during religious ceremonies, creating a sense of unity and inviting divine blessings.
    • In modern contexts, community gatherings, such as festivals or spiritual retreats, often involve collective singing, which fosters a sense of belonging and shared purpose.
    • In personal life, individuals may find solace in singing or chanting as a form of meditation or prayer, inviting peace and clarity into their lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of inviting divine presence into our lives and the power of collective worship.
    • It teaches us that spirituality is not just an individual journey but a communal experience that strengthens bonds and enhances our connection to the divine.
    • In real-life scenarios, whether through community gatherings, personal practices, or collective celebrations, the essence of this mantra encourages us to seek divine guidance and support through unity and shared devotion.
