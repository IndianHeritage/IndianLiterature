कन्या वār अवायतī सोमं अपि श्रुताविदत् |अस्तं भरन्त्य अब्रवीद इन्द्राय सुनवै त्वा शक्राय सुनवै त्वा ||
kanyā3 vār avāyatī somam api srutāvidat | astam bharanty abravīd indrāya sunavai tvā śakrāya sunavai tvā ||
  • kanyā3 vār avāyatī somam api srutāvidat: This line can be interpreted as "The maiden (kanyā) brings forth the essence of the moon (somam) and is aware of the sacred knowledge (srutāvidat)." Here, the term "kanyā" symbolizes purity and potential, while "somam" represents nourishment and the divine essence. In real life, this can be likened to a young woman who embodies hope and potential, nurturing her dreams and aspirations.
  • astam bharanty abravīd: This translates to "They (the maidens) carry forth the offerings." This line emphasizes the act of giving and sharing, which is a fundamental aspect of human relationships. For example, in a community, individuals come together to support one another, sharing resources and knowledge to uplift each other.
  • indrāya sunavai tvā: This means "To Indra, we offer our praises." Indra is a deity associated with strength, rain, and the heavens. In a modern context, this can be seen as acknowledging and respecting the forces of nature and the importance of gratitude in our lives. For instance, when we appreciate the rain for nourishing our crops, we are recognizing the divine forces at play.
  • śakrāya sunavai tvā: This translates to "To Shakra (another name for Indra), we offer our praises." This reinforces the idea of reverence towards powerful forces that govern our lives. In everyday life, this can be reflected in our respect for leaders, mentors, or anyone who plays a significant role in guiding us towards success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of purity, nurturing, community support, and reverence for divine forces. Its significance lies in the reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of gratitude, and the role of individuals in uplifting one another. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages us to recognize our potential, support our communities, and appreciate the forces that shape our existence, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in our lives.
