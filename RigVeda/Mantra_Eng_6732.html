वहन्तु त्वा रथेष्टाम् ā हरयो रथयुजः |तिरश्चिद आर्यं सवनाानि वृत्रहन् अन्येषां या शतक्रतो ||
vahantu tvā ratheṣṭhām ā harayo rathayujaḥ | tiraś cid aryaṁ savanāni vṛtrahann anyeṣāṁ yā śatakrato ||
  • Vahantu: This word translates to "may they carry" or "may they bring." It signifies a wish for support and assistance.
  • Tvā: This means "you," referring to a divine entity or a higher power, often interpreted as Indra in the context of the Rigveda.
  • Ratheṣṭhām: This term means "on the chariot" or "in the chariot," symbolizing movement, progress, and the journey of life.
  • Ā: This is a preposition that can mean "towards" or "to," indicating direction or purpose.
  • Harayo: This refers to "the horses," which are often seen as symbols of strength and speed, essential for the journey.
  • Rathayujaḥ: This means "those who are yoked to the chariot," representing unity and teamwork in achieving goals.
  • Tiraś: This word translates to "across" or "beyond," suggesting overcoming obstacles or challenges.
  • Cid: This means "if" or "when," indicating a conditional aspect of the mantra.
  • Aryaṁ: This term means "noble" or "honorable," referring to the qualities of righteousness and virtue.
  • Savanāni: This translates to "offerings" or "sacrifices," which are essential in Vedic rituals, symbolizing devotion and gratitude.
  • Vṛtrahann: This means "the slayer of Vritra," referring to Indra's victory over the serpent Vritra, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil.
  • Anyesāṁ: This translates to "of others," indicating a collective aspect, emphasizing community and shared experiences.
  • Yā: This means "who" or "that," referring to the qualities or actions of the subjects in the mantra.
  • Śatakrato: This term means "the one with a hundred powers," often used to describe Indra, highlighting his strength and capabilities.

The mantra expresses a prayer for divine assistance and guidance in the journey of life. It emphasizes the importance of teamwork, strength, and noble qualities in overcoming challenges. The imagery of a chariot and horses symbolizes the journey we all undertake, with the divine as a guiding force.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Teamwork in Professional Settings: Just as the horses are yoked to the chariot, individuals in a team must work together harmoniously to achieve common goals.
  • Overcoming Challenges: The reference to overcoming obstacles can inspire individuals facing difficulties in their personal or professional lives, encouraging them to seek support and remain resilient.
  • Spiritual Growth: The noble qualities mentioned in the mantra can serve as a reminder to cultivate virtues such as honesty, integrity, and compassion in daily life.

In conclusion, this mantra from the Rigveda serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of divine support, teamwork, and the pursuit of noble qualities in our lives. It encourages us to seek guidance in our journeys and to work together to overcome challenges, ultimately leading to personal and collective growth.
