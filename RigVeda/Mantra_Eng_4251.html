उप नः सुतं आगतम् वरुण मित्र दाशुषः |अस्य सोमस्य पीतये ||
upa naḥ sutam ā gataṁ varuṇa mitra dāśuṣaḥ | asya somasya pītaye ||
  • upa naḥ sutam ā gataṁ varuṇa mitra dāśuṣaḥ
    • upa: This word means "towards" or "near." It signifies a direction or an approach.
    • naḥ: This translates to "our," indicating possession or belonging.
    • sutam: Meaning "son" or "offspring," it represents the idea of lineage or continuation.
    • ā gataṁ: This means "having come" or "arrived," suggesting a sense of arrival or attainment.
    • varuṇa: Varuṇa is a deity associated with cosmic order and water, symbolizing the moral and natural laws of the universe.
    • mitra: This translates to "friend," indicating companionship, support, and harmony.
    • dāśuṣaḥ: This word can be interpreted as "of the servants" or "of the devotees," referring to those who serve or are devoted to the divine.
  • asya somasya pītaye:
    • asya: This means "of this," indicating a specific reference or connection.
    • somasya: Referring to "soma," which is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals, symbolizing nourishment and divine bliss.
    • pītaye: This translates to "for drinking" or "to drink," suggesting consumption or partaking in something beneficial.

Meaning and Explanation:

This mantra invokes the blessings of Varuṇa and Mitra, two deities representing cosmic order and friendship, respectively. It emphasizes the importance of lineage and the arrival of blessings or prosperity. The mention of "soma" highlights the significance of spiritual nourishment and the divine connection that sustains life.


Real-Life Examples:

  • In a family setting, the mantra can be seen as a prayer for the well-being of children, emphasizing the importance of nurturing and guiding the next generation.
  • In friendships, it reflects the value of support and harmony, encouraging individuals to foster strong, positive relationships.
  • In spiritual practices, the reference to soma can be likened to the importance of rituals and practices that nourish the soul, such as meditation or community gatherings.

Conclusion:

The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine blessings in personal and communal life. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of relationships, the importance of spiritual nourishment, and the pursuit of harmony in all aspects of life. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in family prayers, community gatherings, and personal reflections, guiding individuals towards a balanced and fulfilling existence.
