यद वा यज्ञं मनवे सम्मिमिक्षथुर एवेत काणवस्य बोधतम् | बृहस्पतिं विश्वान देवां अहं हुव इन्द्राविष्णू अश्विनाव् आशुहेषसः ||
yad vā yajñam manave sammimikṣathur evet kāṇvasya bodhatam | bṛhaspatiṁ viśvān devām̐ ahaṁ huva indrāviṣṇū aśvināv āśuheṣasā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad vā: whatever or that which
    • yajñam: sacrifice or offering
    • manave: to Manu (the progenitor of mankind)
    • sammimikṣathur: you have perceived or understood
    • evet: indeed
    • kāṇvasya: of the Kāṇva (a lineage or family)
    • bodhatam: may you be enlightened or awakened
    • bṛhaspatiṁ: to Brihaspati (the deity of wisdom and knowledge)
    • viśvān: all or universal
    • devām̐: to the gods
    • ahaṁ: I
    • huva: invoke or call upon
    • indra: Indra (the king of gods)
    • aviṣṇū: Vishnu (the preserver)
    • aśvināv: the Ashvins (twin gods of medicine and health)
    • āśuheṣasā: with swift actions or quickly
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of understanding and perceiving the essence of sacrifices and offerings.
    • It invokes the blessings of various deities, including Brihaspati, Indra, Vishnu, and the Ashvins, highlighting the interconnectedness of knowledge, power, and health.
    • The reference to Manu suggests a connection to humanity and the moral and ethical responsibilities that come with it.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the practice of mindfulness and awareness in our actions, particularly in rituals or significant life events.
    • For instance, during a wedding ceremony, the couple may reflect on the deeper meanings of their vows, akin to the understanding of yajña (sacrifice) in this mantra.
    • In a corporate setting, invoking the blessings of wisdom (Brihaspati) can be seen in team meetings where collective decision-making is emphasized, ensuring that all voices are heard and valued.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of understanding our actions and their implications in the broader context of life.
    • It encourages us to seek wisdom and guidance from higher powers and to act with intention and awareness.
    • In essence, it highlights the significance of community, knowledge, and the divine in our everyday lives, urging us to be mindful of our contributions to the world.
