उपो रुरुचे युवतिर न योषा विश्वं जीवम् प्रसुवन्ती चरायै |अभूद अग्निः समिधे मानवशाणाम अकर ज्योतिर बादमानā तमांसि ||
upo ruruce yuvatir na yoṣā viśvaṁ jīvam prasuvantī carāyai | abhūd agniḥ samidhe mānuṣāṇām akar jyotir bādhamānā tamāṁsi ||
  • Upo ruruce yuvatir na yoṣā viśvaṁ jīvam prasuvantī carāyai:
    • Upo ruruce: This phrase can be interpreted as "may she shine forth" or "may she emerge." It signifies the emergence of energy or vitality.
    • Yuvatir: Refers to a young woman or maiden, symbolizing youth, beauty, and potential.
    • Na yoṣā: This translates to "not a woman," indicating a broader context beyond just femininity.
    • Viśvaṁ jīvam: Means "all living beings," suggesting inclusivity and the interconnectedness of life.
    • Prasuvantī: This term means "giving birth" or "producing," emphasizing creation and nurturing.
    • Carāyai: Refers to "moving" or "active," indicating the dynamic nature of life and existence.
  • Abhūd agniḥ samidhe mānuṣāṇām:
    • Abhūd: Translates to "has become" or "has arisen," indicating a transformation or emergence.
    • Agniḥ: Refers to "fire," which symbolizes energy, passion, and purification.
    • Samidhe: Means "kindled" or "lit," suggesting the activation of energy or spirit.
    • Mānuṣāṇām: Refers to "of humans," indicating the relevance of this energy to human existence.
  • Akar jyotir bādhamānā tamāṁsi:
    • Akar: Means "created" or "formed," indicating the manifestation of light or energy.
    • Jyotir: Refers to "light," symbolizing knowledge, clarity, and enlightenment.
    • Bādhamānā: Means "overcoming" or "dispelling," indicating the act of removing darkness or ignorance.
    • Tamāṁsi: Refers to "darkness," symbolizing ignorance, confusion, or negativity.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the themes of emergence, creation, and the transformative power of light over darkness. It emphasizes the role of energy and vitality in the lives of all beings, highlighting the importance of nurturing and active participation in life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to personal growth, where individuals strive to overcome ignorance and negativity, seeking enlightenment and clarity in their lives. It also reflects the significance of community and interconnectedness, as the vitality of one contributes to the well-being of all. The mantra serves as a reminder of the potential within each individual to shine forth and contribute positively to the world around them.
