शृणुतं जारितुर हवम् इन्द्राग्नī वनतं गिरः |ईशानā पिप्यतं धियः ||
śṛṇutaṁ jaritur havam indrāgnī vanataṁ giraḥ | īśānā pipyataṁ dhiyaḥ ||
  • śṛṇutaṁ: This word translates to "listen" or "hear." It emphasizes the importance of being attentive and receptive to knowledge and wisdom.
  • jaritur: This term refers to "the one who is old" or "the elder." It signifies the wisdom that comes with age and experience.
  • havam: This means "sacrifice" or "offering." In the context of the mantra, it suggests the act of giving or dedicating something valuable.
  • indrāgnī: This compound word refers to "Indra" and "Agni," two significant deities in Vedic literature. Indra is the king of gods and represents strength and power, while Agni is the fire god, symbolizing transformation and purification.
  • vanataṁ: This word means "to the one who is in the forest" or "the one who is wandering." It can symbolize the journey of life and the search for truth.
  • giraḥ: This translates to "words" or "speech." It highlights the power of communication and the importance of sharing knowledge.
  • īśānā: This term means "the lord" or "the ruler." It signifies authority and control, often associated with divine power.
  • pipyataṁ: This word means "to be filled" or "to be nourished." It suggests the idea of receiving abundance and sustenance.
  • dhiyaḥ: This translates to "intellect" or "understanding." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and discernment in life.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to listen to the wisdom of the elders and the divine, to offer sacrifices that nourish our intellect and understanding. It encourages us to be receptive to the teachings of those who have walked the path before us, and to seek knowledge that can guide us in our own journeys.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In educational settings, students are encouraged to listen to their teachers and mentors, who provide valuable insights based on their experiences.
  • In family dynamics, respecting the wisdom of elders can lead to stronger relationships and a deeper understanding of cultural values.
  • In personal development, being open to new ideas and perspectives can enhance one's intellectual growth and emotional well-being.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of listening, learning, and growing through the wisdom of others. It serves as a reminder that knowledge is a collective journey, and by being receptive and respectful, we can enrich our lives and the lives of those around us.
