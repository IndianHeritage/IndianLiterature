नू मित्रो वरुणो आर्यमा नस् त्मने तोकाय वरिवो दधन्तु |सुगा नो विश्वा सुपथानि सन्तु यूयम् पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
nū mitro varuṇo aryamā nas tmane tokāya varivo dadhantu | sugā no viśvā supathāni santu yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • nū mitro varuṇo aryamā: This phrase invokes the deities Mitra, Varuna, and Aryama.
    • Mitra: Represents friendship and harmony, often associated with the sun and light.
    • Varuna: Symbolizes cosmic order and moral authority, governing the waters and the night sky.
    • Aryama: Associated with the ancestors and the concept of righteousness.
  • nas tmane tokāya varivo dadhantu: This line expresses a desire for these deities to grant us strength and support.
    • tmane: Refers to strength or power.
    • tokāya: Implies the act of granting or bestowing.
    • varivo dadhantu: Means to provide or give us abundance and prosperity.
  • sugā no viśvā supathāni santu: This part wishes for all paths to be auspicious and beneficial.
    • sugā: Signifies goodness or auspiciousness.
    • viśvā: Refers to all or universal.
    • supathāni: Means good paths or directions.
  • yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ: This line calls for the protection and well-being from the deities.
    • yūyam: Refers to the deities being addressed.
    • pāta: Means to protect or guard.
    • svastibhiḥ: Refers to well-being or auspiciousness.
    • sadā naḥ: Implies always or forever for us.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of divine support, harmony, and auspiciousness in our lives. It reflects a universal desire for strength, protection, and guidance from higher powers. In real-life scenarios, this can be applicable in various contexts such as during significant life events (like weddings or new ventures), where individuals seek blessings for success and well-being. The invocation of these deities serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine, encouraging us to foster relationships based on trust, righteousness, and mutual support.
