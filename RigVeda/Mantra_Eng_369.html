इन्द्रस्य नु वीर्याणि प्र वोचं यानि चकार प्रथमानी वज्री |अहन्न अहिम अन्व अपस ततरद प्र वक्षाणाऽभिनत पर्वतानाम् ||
indrasya nu vīryāṇi pra vocaṁ yāni cakāra prathamāni vajrī | ahann ahim anv apas tatarda pra vakṣaṇā abhinat parvatānām ||
  • Indrasya nu vīryāṇi: This phrase refers to the "strengths or powers of Indra," who is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with rain, thunderstorms, and war. Indra symbolizes courage and valor.
  • Pra vocaṁ yāni cakāra prathamāni vajrī: This translates to "I speak of the deeds that he (Indra) performed first with his thunderbolt." The term "vajrī" refers to the thunderbolt, which is a symbol of power and strength.
  • Ahann ahim anv apas tatarda: This means "I have struck down the serpent (or enemy) and have overcome obstacles." The serpent often symbolizes chaos or evil, and overcoming it signifies triumph over challenges.
  • Pra vakṣaṇā abhinat parvatānām: This translates to "I declare the victories that have split the mountains." This can be interpreted as achieving great feats that change the landscape, both literally and metaphorically.

  • Real-Life Example: In modern contexts, the mantra can be related to overcoming personal challenges or adversities. For instance, a person facing a significant life challenge, such as a health issue or a career setback, can draw inspiration from Indra's strength to confront and overcome their "serpents" or obstacles.
  • Significance: The mantra emphasizes the importance of courage, strength, and the ability to overcome challenges. It serves as a reminder that, like Indra, individuals can harness their inner strength to face adversities and achieve their goals.
  • Application: This mantra can be applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as in motivational speeches, personal development workshops, or even in sports, where athletes draw on their inner strength to overcome competition and personal limits.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, courage, and the ability to overcome challenges. It serves as a powerful reminder that, like the deity Indra, we all possess the potential to face our fears and emerge victorious. By invoking this mantra, individuals can inspire themselves and others to strive for greatness and resilience in the face of adversity.
