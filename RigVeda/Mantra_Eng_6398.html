वास्तोष पते ध्रुवां स्थूणांसत्रं सोम्यानाम् |द्रप्सो भेत्ता पुरां शाश्वतीनाम् इन्द्रो मुनीनां सखा ||
vāstoṣ pate dhruvā sthūṇāṁsatraṁ somyānām | drapso bhettā purāṁ śaśvatīnām indro munīnāṁ sakhā ||
  • vāstoṣ pate: This phrase can be translated as "O Lord of the dwelling" or "O Lord of the house." It signifies a divine presence that governs and protects our homes and living spaces.
  • dhruvā sthūṇāṁsatraṁ: "dhruvā" means "fixed" or "stable," while "sthūṇāṁsatraṁ" refers to "the great assembly" or "the gathering of the strong." This part emphasizes the importance of stability and strength in our communities and relationships.
  • somyānām: This word translates to "of the pleasant" or "of the agreeable." It suggests a sense of harmony and positivity that should prevail in our interactions and environments.
  • drapso bhettā: "drapso" means "of the grass" or "of the earth," and "bhettā" means "the one who breaks" or "the destroyer." This phrase can be interpreted as a call to remove obstacles or negativity that hinder growth and prosperity.
  • purāṁ: This word means "the city" or "the place." It signifies the importance of our surroundings and the communities we build.
  • śaśvatīnām: This translates to "of the eternal" or "of the everlasting." It highlights the idea of permanence and continuity in our lives and relationships.
  • indro munīnāṁ sakhā: "Indro" refers to the deity Indra, who is often associated with strength and leadership. "Munīnāṁ" means "of the sages" or "of the wise," and "sakhā" means "friend." This part emphasizes the importance of wisdom and friendship in leadership and community building.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of stability, harmony, and the importance of community. It serves as a reminder to cultivate a positive environment in our homes and relationships, encouraging us to remove negativity and obstacles. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as fostering a supportive family atmosphere, building strong friendships, and creating a harmonious workplace. By invoking the qualities of strength, wisdom, and pleasantness, we can enhance our lives and the lives of those around us, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and prosperous existence.
