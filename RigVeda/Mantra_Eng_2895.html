शुनं हुवेम मगवाणं इन्द्रं अस्मिन भरे नृतमं वाजसातौ | श्रिण्वन्तं उग्रं ऊतये समत्सु घ्नन्तं वृत्राणि संजितं धनानाम् ||
śunaṁ huvema maghavānam indram asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau | śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi saṁjitaṁ dhanānām ||
  • śunaṁ huvema: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke the powerful." The term "śunaṁ" refers to something that is auspicious or beneficial, while "huvema" indicates the act of invoking or calling upon.
  • maghavānam indram: Here, "maghavānam" refers to the mighty or powerful, and "indram" is a reference to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic literature, known for his strength and ability to overcome obstacles.
  • asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau: This part can be interpreted as "in this place, we offer our best." "Nṛtamaṁ" signifies the best or the most excellent, while "vājasātau" refers to the offerings or sacrifices made to the divine.
  • śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye: "śṛṇvantam" means "listening" or "hearing," and "ugram" refers to something fierce or powerful. "ūtaye" indicates the purpose of invoking this power, which is for protection or assistance.
  • samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi: "samatsu" means "in the assembly" or "among the people," while "ghnantaṁ" refers to the act of striking down or defeating. "vṛtrāṇi" refers to the obstacles or enemies that one faces.
  • saṁjitaṁ dhanānām: This phrase translates to "the gathered wealth." "Saṁjitaṁ" means collected or assembled, and "dhanānām" refers to wealth or resources.

In summary, this mantra is a powerful invocation to Indra, seeking his assistance in overcoming obstacles and achieving prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of invoking divine strength and protection in our endeavors. The mantra can be applied in real-life scenarios such as:

  • During challenging times, individuals can recite this mantra to seek strength and guidance in overcoming difficulties.
  • In communal gatherings or ceremonies, it can be used to invoke collective strength and unity among participants.
  • In business or financial ventures, the mantra serves as a reminder to seek divine blessings for success and prosperity.

In conclusion, the significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with divine power, encouraging them to face challenges with courage and to strive for excellence in their pursuits. It serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking help from higher powers in times of need, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.
