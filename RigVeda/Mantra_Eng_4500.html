त्वां अग्नि स्वाध्योऽर्तासो देववीतये |यज्ञेषु देवम् ईळते ||
tvām agne svādhyo3 martāso devavītaye | yajñeṣu devam īḻate ||
  • tvām - This word translates to "you," referring to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature.
  • agne - This is the vocative form of Agni, emphasizing the invocation of the fire deity.
  • svādhyo - This term can be interpreted as "worthy of worship" or "desirable," indicating the reverence for Agni.
  • martāso - This word means "of mortals" or "of human beings," suggesting a connection between the divine and humanity.
  • devavītaye - This phrase can be understood as "for the sake of the divine," indicating the purpose of the invocation.
  • yajñeṣu - This term refers to "sacrifices" or "rituals," highlighting the importance of rituals in Vedic culture.
  • devam - This means "the divine" or "the god," reinforcing the focus on the divine presence.
  • īḻate - This word translates to "is worshipped" or "is honored," indicating the act of reverence towards Agni.

The mantra can be translated as: "O Agni, you who are worthy of worship, for the sake of mortals, are honored in sacrifices." This invocation emphasizes the significance of Agni as a mediator between humans and the divine, highlighting the role of fire in rituals and sacrifices.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: In Hindu weddings and other significant ceremonies, fire is often invoked as a witness and a purifying element. The mantra serves as a reminder of the sacredness of these rituals.
  • Personal Growth: Just as Agni represents transformation and purification, individuals can reflect on their own lives, seeking to burn away negativity and emerge renewed, much like the phoenix rising from the ashes.
  • Community and Unity: The invocation of Agni in communal rituals fosters a sense of togetherness and shared purpose, reminding participants of their connection to each other and the divine.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its recognition of Agni as a vital force in both the physical and spiritual realms. It underscores the importance of rituals in connecting mortals with the divine, promoting personal transformation, and fostering community. In contemporary life, the principles embodied in this mantra can guide individuals in their spiritual practices, personal development, and communal engagements.
