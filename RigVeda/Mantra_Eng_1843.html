नक्षद धोतā परि सद्म मितā यन् भरद गर्भम ā शरदः पृथिव्याः |क्रन्दद अश्वो नयमानो रुवद गौर अन्तर दूतो न रोदसी चरद वाक् ||
nakṣad dhotā pari sadma mitā yan bharad garbham ā śaradaḥ pṛthivyāḥ | krandad aśvo nayamāno ruvad gaur antar dūto na rodasī carad vāk ||
  • nakṣad dhotā: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who brings forth the stars." In a real-life context, this can symbolize individuals who inspire or lead others to achieve greatness, much like a mentor or a leader guiding their team towards success.
  • pari sadma mitā: This translates to "surrounding the abode with harmony." It emphasizes the importance of creating a peaceful and harmonious environment, whether at home, in the workplace, or within a community. For example, a family that practices open communication and mutual respect fosters a nurturing atmosphere.
  • yan bharad garbham ā śaradaḥ pṛthivyāḥ: This can be understood as "the one who carries the essence of the earth and the seasons." It signifies the connection between humanity and nature, reminding us of our responsibility to care for the environment. In real life, this could relate to sustainable practices, such as recycling and conservation efforts.
  • krandad aśvo nayamāno: This phrase means "the horse that cries out while being led." It can represent the struggles and challenges faced by individuals when they are guided or directed by others. For instance, a student may feel overwhelmed by academic pressures, yet they are being guided by teachers and parents towards their future.
  • ruvad gaur antar dūto: This translates to "the messenger who roams within." It suggests the idea of introspection and self-discovery. In real life, this can be seen in practices like meditation or journaling, where individuals seek to understand their inner thoughts and emotions.
  • na rodasī carad vāk: This means "the words that traverse the heavens." It highlights the power of communication and expression. In today's world, effective communication is crucial in various aspects of life, from personal relationships to professional settings, where clear dialogue can lead to better understanding and collaboration.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of guidance, harmony, connection with nature, introspection, and the power of communication. Its significance lies in the reminder that we are all interconnected and that our actions, words, and thoughts have the potential to influence not only our lives but also the lives of those around us. In real-life scenarios, applying these principles can lead to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence, whether through leadership, environmental stewardship, or personal growth.
