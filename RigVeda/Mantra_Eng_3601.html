देवेभ्यो हि प्रथमं यज्ञियेब्यो 'मृतत्वं सुवसि भागम् उत्तमम् |आद इद दामानं सवितर व्यूर्णुषे 'अनूचीनां जीविता मानविषेभ्यः ||
devebhyo hi prathamaṁ yajñiyebhyo 'mṛtatvaṁ suvasi bhāgam uttamam | ād id dāmānaṁ savitar vy ūrṇuṣe 'nūcīnā jīvitā mānuṣebhyaḥ ||
  • Translation: "Indeed, from the gods, the first offerings bring immortality, the best share of prosperity. Therefore, O Savitar, grant us the strength to live, and may we be nourished by your grace."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • devebhyo: From the gods
    • hi: Indeed
    • prathamaṁ: First
    • yajñiyebhyo: From the offerings
    • 'mṛtatvaṁ: Immortality
    • suvasi: Prosperity
    • bhāgam: Share
    • uttamam: Best
    • ād: Therefore
    • id: This
    • dāmānaṁ: Grant us strength
    • savitā: O Savitar (the Sun)
    • vy: To us
    • ūrṇuṣe: Nourish
    • 'nūcīnā: With grace
    • jīvitā: Life
    • mānuṣebhyaḥ: For humanity
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings and gratitude towards divine forces. In modern life, this can be seen in various cultures where people express gratitude through rituals, prayers, or charitable acts.
    • The concept of seeking prosperity and strength can be related to personal development practices, such as setting goals, meditation, and community service, which aim to enhance one's life and the lives of others.
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can inspire ethical business practices where companies contribute to society, ensuring that their success also benefits the community, akin to corporate social responsibility initiatives.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine blessings and the recognition of the interconnectedness of life. It teaches us that true prosperity and strength come from a harmonious relationship with the divine and the community. In real life, this mantra encourages us to be grateful, to give back, and to seek a life that is not only successful but also meaningful and beneficial to others. By embodying these principles, we can create a more compassionate and prosperous society.
