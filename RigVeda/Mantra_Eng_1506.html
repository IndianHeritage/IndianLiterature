इन्द्रं अग्निमुप स्तुहि द्युक्शम् आर्यमणं भागम् |योग जीवन्तः प्रजया सचेमहि सोमस्योती सचेमहि ||
indram agnim upa stuhi dyukṣam aryamaṇam bhagam | jyog jīvantaḥ prajayā sacemahi somasyotī sacemahi ||
  • Indram: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Agnim: Represents Agni, the fire god, who is a vital force in rituals and symbolizes transformation and purification.
  • Upa stuhi: Means to praise or invoke, indicating a call to the divine for blessings and support.
  • Dyukṣam: Signifies the sky or heavens, representing the vastness and the divine realm.
  • Aryamaṇam: Refers to the noble or virtuous, emphasizing the importance of righteousness and moral values.
  • Bhagam: Means fortune or wealth, indicating the blessings of prosperity and abundance.
  • Jyog: Represents the concept of union or connection, often associated with spiritual practices and harmony.
  • Jīvantaḥ: Means living or life-giving, highlighting the essence of vitality and existence.
  • Prajayā: Refers to progeny or creation, emphasizing the importance of growth and continuity in life.
  • Sacemahi: Means "we aspire" or "we seek," indicating a desire for divine guidance and support.
  • Somasyotī: Refers to the light of the moon (Soma), symbolizing nourishment, tranquility, and the cyclical nature of life.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer invoking the divine forces of Indra and Agni, seeking their blessings for strength, prosperity, and virtuous living. It emphasizes the importance of connection with the divine and the pursuit of a righteous life filled with abundance and vitality.


Conclusion: This mantra holds significant relevance in real-life scenarios, particularly in the context of seeking guidance and blessings in various aspects of life, such as personal growth, family prosperity, and spiritual development. It encourages individuals to cultivate virtues, seek harmony, and aspire for a fulfilling life, making it applicable in daily prayers, rituals, and moments of reflection.
