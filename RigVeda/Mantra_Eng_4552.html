वर्धान यं विश्वे मरुतः सजोषाः पचच छतम महिषाम् इन्द्र तुभ्यम् |पुषा विष्णुस् त्रिणि सरांसि धावन् वृत्रहणम् मदिराम् अंशुम् अस्मै ||
vardhān yaṁ viśve marutaḥ sajoṣāḥ pacac chatam mahiṣām̐ indra tubhyam | pūṣā viṣṇus trīṇi sarāṁsi dhāvan vṛtrahaṇam madiram aṁśum asmai ||
  • Vardhān yaṁ viśve marutaḥ sajoṣāḥ pacac chatam mahiṣām̐ indra tubhyam:
    • Vardhān: This word means "to increase" or "to promote growth." It signifies the idea of enhancement and prosperity.
    • Viśve: Refers to "all" or "universal," indicating inclusivity.
    • Marutaḥ: This term refers to the "Maruts," the storm deities associated with wind and storms, symbolizing power and energy.
    • Sajoṣāḥ: Means "with joy" or "with enthusiasm," suggesting a positive and vibrant energy.
    • Pacac: Translates to "cooking" or "preparing," which can be interpreted as the process of transformation or creation.
    • Chatam: Means "hundred," often symbolizing abundance or completeness.
    • Mahiṣām̐: Refers to "the great ones" or "the powerful," often associated with strength and might.
    • Indra: The king of the gods, representing authority, strength, and leadership.
    • Tubhyam: Means "to you," indicating a direct address to Indra, seeking his blessings.
  • Pūṣā viṣṇus trīṇi sarāṁsi dhāvan:
    • Pūṣā: The deity associated with nourishment and growth, symbolizing sustenance and support.
    • Viṣṇus: Refers to Lord Vishnu, the preserver and protector in Hindu mythology, representing stability and balance.
    • Trīṇi: Means "three," which can symbolize various triads in life, such as past, present, and future.
    • Sarāṁsi: Refers to "streams" or "flowing waters," symbolizing the flow of life and abundance.
    • Dhāvan: Means "running" or "moving," indicating action and dynamism.
  • Vṛtrahaṇam madiram aṁśum asmai:
    • Vṛtrahaṇam: Refers to the "slayer of Vritra," a serpent or dragon representing obstacles and challenges.
    • Madiram: Means "intoxicating" or "delightful," suggesting joy and ecstasy.
    • Aṁśum: Refers to "light" or "radiance," symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
    • Asmai: Means "to him," indicating a dedication or offering to the divine.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of growth, strength, and divine support. It calls upon the energies of Indra, Pūṣā, and Viṣṇu to enhance life, overcome obstacles, and promote abundance.
    • In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as seeking blessings for personal growth, overcoming challenges in professional life, or fostering a supportive community.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine assistance in achieving success and prosperity, reminding us to remain connected to higher energies while striving for our goals.
