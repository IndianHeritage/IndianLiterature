मन्त्र: उता त्वः पश्यन् न ददर्श वाचं उता त्वः श्रिण्वन् न श्रिणोति एनाम् |उतो त्वस्मै तन्वं वि सस्रे जाया एव पत्या उषती सुवासाः ||
uta tvaḥ paśyan na dadarśa vācam uta tvaḥ śṛṇvan na śṛṇoty enām | uto tvasmai tanva1ṁ vi sasre jāyeva patya uśatī suvāsāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • uta: and
    • tvaḥ: you
    • paśyan: seeing
    • na: not
    • dadarśa: perceived
    • vācam: speech
    • uta: and
    • tvaḥ: you
    • śṛṇvan: hearing
    • na: not
    • śṛṇoty: listen
    • enām: to it
    • uto: and
    • tvasmai: to you
    • tanvaṁ: body
    • vi: indeed
    • sasre: has spread
    • jāyeva: like a wife
    • patya: husband
    • uśatī: auspicious
    • suvāsāḥ: well-clad
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the relationship between perception and understanding.
    • It suggests that merely seeing or hearing does not equate to true understanding or awareness.
    • The imagery of a body spreading like a wife to a husband indicates a deep connection and unity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of active engagement in communication rather than passive observation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a classroom setting, a student may see the teacher and hear the lecture but may not grasp the concepts unless they actively participate and engage with the material.
    • In relationships, partners may live together and communicate daily, yet true understanding and connection require deeper emotional engagement and empathy.
    • In the workplace, employees may attend meetings and hear discussions, but without active participation and feedback, they may miss critical insights and collaborative opportunities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of active listening and engagement in all forms of communication.
    • It serves as a reminder that true understanding comes from more than just sensory perception; it requires emotional and intellectual involvement.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying this principle can lead to stronger relationships, better learning outcomes, and more effective teamwork.
