मन्त्र: सोमः प्रथमो विविदे गन्धर्वो विविद उत्तरः | तृतीयो अग्निषṭे पतिस तुरीयस् ते मनुष्यजाः ||
somaḥ prathamo vivide gandharvo vivida uttaraḥ | tṛtīyo agniṣ ṭe patis turīyas te manuṣyajāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • somaḥ: Soma, a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, often associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • prathamo: First or foremost.
    • vivide: To perceive or to understand.
    • gandharvo: Gandharva, celestial musicians and beings associated with the arts and beauty.
    • vivida: Diverse or varied.
    • uttaraḥ: Superior or higher.
    • tṛtīyo: Third.
    • agniṣ: Of fire, representing energy and transformation.
    • ṭe: Your or of you.
    • patis: Lord or master.
    • turīyas: The fourth state of consciousness, beyond waking, dreaming, and deep sleep.
    • te: You or your.
    • manuṣyajāḥ: Born of humans or pertaining to humanity.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine aspects of existence, highlighting the importance of understanding and perceiving the various layers of reality.
    • The mention of Soma suggests the pursuit of higher knowledge and spiritual enlightenment, akin to how individuals seek inspiration in their lives through art, music, or philosophy.
    • The reference to Gandharvas indicates the beauty and creativity that enrich human experience, reminding us of the importance of art and culture in our lives.
    • Agni, representing fire, symbolizes transformation and energy, which can be seen in real-life scenarios such as personal growth, change, and the pursuit of goals.
    • The concept of Turīyas, or the fourth state of consciousness, encourages individuals to seek deeper understanding and awareness beyond the mundane aspects of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of seeking higher knowledge and understanding the diverse aspects of existence.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to appreciate art, culture, and the transformative power of experiences, leading to personal growth and enlightenment.
    • By recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine, one can cultivate a more profound sense of purpose and fulfillment in life.
    • Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to strive for a higher state of consciousness and to embrace the beauty and diversity of life.
