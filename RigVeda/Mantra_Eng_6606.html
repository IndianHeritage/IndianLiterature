युवं वरो सुशाम्णे महे तने नासत्याः |अवोभिर यातो वृषणा वृषणवसू ||
yuvaṁ varo suṣāmṇe mahe tane nāsatyā | avobhir yātho vṛṣaṇā vṛṣaṇvasū ||
  • Yuvaṁ: This word translates to "young" or "youthful." It signifies vitality, energy, and the potential for growth and development.
  • Vara: Meaning "best" or "excellent," this term denotes something of high quality or value, often referring to a person or a thing that is chosen or preferred.
  • Suṣāmṇe: This can be interpreted as "well-formed" or "beautifully arranged." It suggests harmony and balance, both in physical form and in character.
  • Mahe: This word means "great" or "mighty," indicating strength and significance.
  • Tane: Translated as "body" or "form," it refers to the physical aspect of existence.
  • Nāsatyā: This term is often associated with the Aśvins, the twin deities of health and medicine, symbolizing truth and reliability.
  • Avobhir: Meaning "with these," it suggests a collective or communal aspect, emphasizing the importance of togetherness.
  • Yātho: This word can be interpreted as "to go" or "to move," indicating action and progress.
  • Vṛṣaṇā: This term refers to "strength" or "power," often associated with the ability to overcome challenges.
  • Vṛṣaṇvasū: This can be understood as "the one who brings strength" or "the powerful one," reinforcing the theme of empowerment.

The mantra as a whole can be understood as a call for the youth to embody excellence, strength, and beauty in their actions and character. It emphasizes the importance of truth and reliability, encouraging individuals to move forward with vigor and purpose.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Development: Young individuals can take inspiration from this mantra to strive for excellence in their studies, careers, and personal lives, focusing on building a strong and reliable character.
  • Community Engagement: The emphasis on togetherness can inspire collective action in communities, encouraging youth to work together for common goals, such as social justice or environmental sustainability.
  • Health and Wellness: The association with the Aśvins highlights the importance of health. Young people can be motivated to prioritize their physical and mental well-being, seeking balance and harmony in their lives.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the potential and responsibility of youth. It encourages individuals to embrace their strengths, pursue excellence, and contribute positively to society, making it highly relevant in today's world.
