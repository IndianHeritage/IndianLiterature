प्राण्यच चक्रम् अवृहः सूर्यस्य कुत्सायान्यद वरिवो यातवे 'कः |अनासो दस्यूम् अमृतो वधेन न दुर्ज्योण आवृणङ मृध्रवाचः ||
prānyac cakram avṛhaḥ sūryasya kutsāyānyad varivo yātave 'kaḥ | anāso dasyūm̐r amṛṇo vadhena ni duryoṇa āvṛṇaṅ mṛdhravācaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • prānyac: to be alive or to have life
    • cakram: wheel or cycle
    • avṛhaḥ: unbroken or undisturbed
    • sūryasya: of the sun
    • kutsāyā: in the midst of troubles or difficulties
    • anyad: other or different
    • varivo: great or powerful
    • yātave: to the one who travels or moves
    • 'kaḥ: who or what
    • anāso: without a burden or free
    • dasyūm̐r: the wicked or the evil-doers
    • amṛṇo: of the immortal or eternal
    • vadhena: by killing or destruction
    • ni: not
    • duryoṇa: difficult to conquer or hard to overcome
    • āvṛṇaṅ: to cover or envelop
    • mṛdhravācaḥ: with gentle speech or kind words
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the cycle of life and the challenges that come with it.
    • The "wheel" symbolizes the continuous nature of existence, where life and death are interconnected.
    • The reference to the "sun" indicates the source of life and energy, which remains constant despite the troubles faced.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of overcoming evil and negativity without resorting to violence, advocating for peaceful resolutions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal relationships, when conflicts arise, instead of resorting to harsh words or actions, one can choose to communicate gently and resolve issues amicably.
    • In a workplace setting, when faced with challenges or difficult colleagues, maintaining a positive attitude and addressing issues with kindness can lead to a more harmonious environment.
    • In broader societal contexts, promoting peace and understanding over violence can lead to more sustainable solutions to conflicts.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the cyclical nature of life and the importance of maintaining a peaceful approach in the face of adversity.
    • Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to seek non-violent solutions and to remain resilient amidst challenges.
    • In real life, applying the teachings of this mantra can lead to healthier relationships, better conflict resolution, and a more compassionate society.
