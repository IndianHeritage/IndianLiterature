न तं जिनन्ति बहवो न दभ्राऽ उर्व अस्मा अदितिः शर्म यंषत् |प्रियः सुक्र्त प्रिय इन्द्रे मनायुः प्रियः सुप्राविः प्रियः अस्य सोमि ||
na taṁ jinanti bahavo na dabhrā urv asmā aditiḥ śarma yaṁsat | priyaḥ sukṛt priya indre manāyuḥ priyaḥ suprāvīḥ priyo asya somī ||
  • na taṁ jinanti bahavo: This phrase translates to "many do not conquer him." It suggests the idea of resilience and strength in the face of adversity. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who overcome challenges, such as athletes who face tough competition but remain steadfast in their pursuit of victory.
  • na dabhrā urv asmā: This means "the earth does not shake." It signifies stability and grounding. For example, in a business context, a company that remains stable during economic downturns exemplifies this principle, maintaining its core values and operations despite external pressures.
  • aditiḥ śarma yaṁsat: Here, "Aditi" refers to the mother of gods and signifies protection and nurturing. "Śarma" means comfort or shelter. This can be related to the role of a family or community that provides support and safety to its members, much like a nurturing environment that fosters growth and well-being.
  • priyaḥ sukṛt: This translates to "the one who is dear is virtuous." It emphasizes the importance of virtue and moral integrity in relationships. In everyday life, this can be seen in friendships where loyalty and honesty are valued, leading to deeper connections and trust.
  • prīya indre manāyuḥ: This phrase means "the beloved is dear to Indra." Indra is often associated with power and authority. This can be interpreted as the idea that those who are virtuous and beloved will receive blessings and support from higher powers, akin to how leaders in a community support those who contribute positively.
  • priyaḥ suprāvīḥ priyo asya somī: This translates to "the beloved is well-versed and dear to the moon." The moon symbolizes calmness and serenity. This can relate to individuals who are knowledgeable and wise, earning respect and admiration in their communities, much like a mentor who guides others with wisdom.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of resilience, stability, nurturing, virtue, and the importance of wisdom in relationships. Its significance lies in the reminder that strength and integrity are essential in navigating life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in personal relationships, community dynamics, and professional environments, encouraging individuals to embody these values for a harmonious and fulfilling life.
