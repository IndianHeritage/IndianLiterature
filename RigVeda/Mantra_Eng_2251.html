यो 'वरे वृजने विश्वथा विभुर महाम् उ रण्वः शवसां ववक्षिथ |स देवो देवान प्रति पप्रथे पृथु विश्वेदु ता परिभूर ब्रह्मणस पतिḥ ||
yo 'vare vṛjane viśvathā vibhur mahām u raṇvaḥ śavasā vavakṣitha | sa devo devān prati paprathe pṛthu viśved u tā paribhūr brahmaṇas patiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo - who
    • vare - the best, the most excellent
    • vṛjane - in the act of choosing or selecting
    • viśvathā - everywhere, in all directions
    • vibhur - all-pervading, omnipresent
    • mahām - great, mighty
    • u - and
    • raṇvaḥ - the one who is powerful
    • śavasā - with strength, with vigor
    • vavakṣitha - has spoken, has declared
    • sa - he, that one
    • devo - god, divine being
    • devān - to the gods
    • prati - towards, in relation to
    • paprathe - has approached, has sought
    • pṛthu - vast, extensive
    • viśved - all, every
    • u - and
    • tā - that, those
    • paribhūr - encompassing, surrounding
    • brahmaṇas - of the ultimate reality, of Brahman
    • patiḥ - lord, master
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine being who is the best among all, omnipresent, and powerful.
    • It emphasizes the idea of seeking the divine in all directions and recognizing the vastness of the universe.
    • The mantra suggests that the divine is not only a master of the ultimate reality but also a protector and guide for all beings.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek guidance and strength from a higher power during challenging times, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In moments of decision-making, people may pray or meditate, hoping to connect with the divine for clarity and wisdom.
    • The concept of omnipresence can be seen in nature, where people find spirituality in the vastness of the universe, such as during a starlit night or while standing on a mountain.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the divine presence in all aspects of life.
    • It encourages individuals to seek strength and guidance from a higher power, fostering a sense of connection with the universe.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of reflection, prayer, and meditation, helping individuals navigate their paths with a sense of purpose and support.
