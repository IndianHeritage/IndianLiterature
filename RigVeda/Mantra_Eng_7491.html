शिक्षा ण इन्द्र राया आ पुरु विद्वाम् ऋचिषमा |अव नः पार्ये धनें ||
śikṣā ṇa indra rāya ā puru vidvām̐ ṛcīṣama | avā naḥ pārye dhane ||
  • śikṣā: This word translates to "instruction" or "teaching." It emphasizes the importance of learning and acquiring knowledge.
  • ṇa: This is a particle that can mean "not" or "no," often used to negate a statement.
  • indra: Indra is the king of the gods in Vedic literature, representing strength, power, and leadership. He is often associated with rain and thunderstorms, symbolizing abundance and prosperity.
  • rāya: This term means "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies the material and spiritual abundance one seeks in life.
  • ā: A preposition that can mean "towards" or "to," indicating direction or purpose.
  • puru: This word means "man" or "person," often referring to humanity or individuals.
  • vidvām̐: This term translates to "wise" or "knowledgeable," highlighting the value of wisdom and understanding.
  • ṛcīṣama: This word can be interpreted as "we have recited" or "we have sung," indicating the act of expressing or invoking through words.
  • avā: This means "may" or "let," suggesting a desire or wish for something to happen.
  • naḥ: This translates to "our," indicating possession or belonging.
  • pārye: This term means "to protect" or "to guard," emphasizing the need for safeguarding what is valuable.
  • dhane: This word means "wealth" or "riches," reinforcing the theme of prosperity and abundance.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for wisdom and protection in the pursuit of wealth and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of learning and knowledge as foundational elements in achieving success. The reference to Indra suggests that divine support and strength are essential in this journey.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • In education, it highlights the significance of acquiring knowledge and skills to succeed in one's career.
  • In business, it serves as a reminder to seek wisdom and guidance while striving for financial success.
  • In personal development, it encourages individuals to cultivate their inner strength and wisdom to navigate life's challenges.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for wisdom and divine support in the pursuit of prosperity. It teaches that knowledge and strength are essential in achieving one's goals, and it encourages individuals to seek protection and guidance in their endeavors. By embodying these principles, one can navigate the complexities of life with confidence and purpose.
