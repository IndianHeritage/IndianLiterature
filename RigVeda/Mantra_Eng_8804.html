स तु वस्त्राण्य अध पेषणानि वसानो अग्निर नाभा पृथिव्याः |अरुषो जातः पद इळायाः पुरोहितो राजन यक्षीह देवान ||
sa tu vastrāṇy adha peśanāni vasāno agnir nābhā pṛthivyāḥ | aruṣo jātaḥ pada iḻāyāḥ purohito rājan yakṣīha devān ||
  • sa tu vastrāṇy adha peśanāni vasāno: This phrase can be translated to "He, who is clothed in the garments of the world." Here, "He" refers to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing the divine presence that envelops and sustains the universe. In real life, this can be likened to how we are surrounded by nature and the elements that provide for us, much like how Agni is essential for life.
  • agnir nābhā pṛthivyāḥ: This translates to "Agni is the navel of the earth." The navel symbolizes the center or the source of nourishment. In a practical sense, this can be seen in how fire is central to many cultures for cooking, warmth, and rituals, representing the core of sustenance and life.
  • aruṣo jātaḥ pada iḻāyāḥ: This means "the red one born in the abode of the earth." The term "aruṣa" refers to the reddish hue of fire, which can be associated with dawn or new beginnings. This can be related to the idea of renewal and the cyclical nature of life, where every day brings a new opportunity, much like the rising sun.
  • purohito rājan yakṣīha devān: This translates to "the priest of the king, who invokes the gods." This highlights the role of Agni as a mediator between humans and the divine. In modern life, this can be seen in the way leaders often seek guidance and blessings from spiritual leaders or practices to ensure prosperity and peace in their governance.

  • Significance: This mantra emphasizes the importance of Agni as a central figure in both the physical and spiritual realms. It illustrates the interconnectedness of life, nature, and the divine.
  • Real-life Application: The teachings of this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as:
    • Recognizing the importance of nature and the elements in our daily lives.
    • Understanding the significance of rituals and spirituality in leadership and community well-being.
    • Embracing the concept of renewal and new beginnings in personal growth and development.
