अस्य श्रीये समिधानस्य वृष्णो वासोर अनीकं दाम आ रुरोच |रुशद वसानः सुदृशीकृतः क्षितिर न राया पूर्वारो अद्यौत ||
asya śriye samidhānasya vṛṣṇo vasor anīkaṁ dama ā ruroca | ruśad vasānaḥ sudṛśīkarūpaḥ kṣitir na rāyā puruvāro adyaut ||
  • Mantra: "asya śriye samidhānasya vṛṣṇo vasor anīkaṁ dama ā ruroca | ruśad vasānaḥ sudṛśīkarūpaḥ kṣitir na rāyā puruvāro adyaut ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • asya: of this
    • śriye: prosperity or wealth
    • samidhānasya: of the one who is well-established
    • vṛṣṇo: of the Vṛṣhṇi (a clan or lineage, often associated with Lord Krishna)
    • vasor: of the divine or the celestial
    • anīkaṁ: the host or army
    • dama: control or restraint
    • ā ruroca: shone forth or became radiant
    • ruśad: shining or bright
    • vasānaḥ: the one who is adorned
    • sudṛśīkarūpaḥ: having a beautiful form
    • kṣitir: the earth
    • na: not
    • rāyā: wealth or abundance
    • puruvāro: the one who is the first or foremost
    • adyaut: has shone or has become bright
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks of the prosperity and radiance that comes from being well-established in one's purpose and lineage.
    • The reference to "Vṛṣhṇi" connects to the idea of divine heritage, suggesting that true wealth comes from a noble lineage and purpose.
    • The term "dama" emphasizes the importance of self-control and restraint in achieving success and prosperity.
    • The imagery of brightness and beauty ("sudṛśīkarūpaḥ") signifies that true prosperity is not just material but also encompasses spiritual and aesthetic dimensions.
    • The earth ("kṣitir") is mentioned, indicating that this prosperity is grounded and connected to the physical world.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a company that values ethical practices and self-discipline often thrives, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on restraint and noble lineage.
    • In personal life, individuals who cultivate self-control and a strong sense of purpose often find greater fulfillment and success, embodying the principles of this mantra.
    • Communities that focus on sustainable practices and respect for the earth can create a prosperous environment, aligning with the mantra's connection to the earth and abundance.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of self-discipline, noble heritage, and the beauty of prosperity that is both material and spiritual.
    • It serves as a reminder that true wealth is achieved through ethical living, self-control, and a connection to one's roots.
    • In real life, applying these principles can lead to a more fulfilling and prosperous existence, both individually and collectively.
