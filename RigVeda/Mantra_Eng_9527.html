अश्नापिनद्धं मधु पर्य अपश्यन् मत्स्यं न दीन उदनि क्षीयन्तम् |निषṭज जभारे चमसं न वृक्षाद बृहस्पतिर विरवेण विकृत्य ||
aśnāpinaddham madhu pary apaśyan matsyaṁ na dīna udani kṣiyantam | niṣ ṭaj jabhāra camasaṁ na vṛkṣād bṛhaspatir viraveṇā vikṛtya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśnāpinaddham: Not being consumed or devoured.
    • madhu: Honey, often symbolizing sweetness or prosperity.
    • pary: Around or surrounding.
    • apaśyan: Not seeing or perceiving.
    • matsyaṁ: Fish, which can symbolize adaptability and survival.
    • na: Not.
    • dīna: Poor or weak.
    • udani: In the water or aquatic.
    • kṣiyantam: Diminishing or decreasing.
    • niṣ ṭaj: Firm or stable.
    • jabhāra: To bear or carry.
    • camasaṁ: A vessel or container.
    • na: Not.
    • vṛkṣād: From the tree.
    • bṛhaspatir: Referring to the deity Brihaspati, the lord of wisdom and eloquence.
    • viraveṇā: With a roar or sound.
    • vikṛtya: Distorted or changed.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the idea of resilience and stability in the face of adversity.
    • The imagery of honey suggests that even in difficult situations, there can be sweetness and prosperity if one remains steadfast.
    • The mention of fish indicates the importance of adaptability; just as fish thrive in water, we must learn to navigate our environments effectively.
    • The reference to Brihaspati emphasizes the need for wisdom and guidance in our actions and decisions.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, a company facing financial difficulties may need to adapt its strategies (like the fish) while maintaining a focus on its core values (the honey).
    • In personal life, individuals may encounter challenges such as health issues or relationship problems. By staying resilient and seeking wisdom, they can find solutions and maintain their well-being.
    • In environmental contexts, communities affected by natural disasters must adapt and rebuild, drawing on their collective wisdom and resources to restore stability.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of resilience, adaptability, and wisdom in overcoming life's challenges.
    • Its significance lies in encouraging individuals to remain steadfast and seek guidance, ensuring that they can navigate through difficulties while still finding moments of sweetness and prosperity.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, successful problem-solving, and a deeper understanding of one's environment and relationships.
