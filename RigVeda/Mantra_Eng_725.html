त्वं ह त्यद इन्द्र कोदिः सखा वृत्रं यद वज्रिन वृषकर्मन्न उभ्नाः |यद ध शूर वृषमणः पराचैर विदस्यूम् योनाव अकृतो वृत्थाषाट ||
tvaṁ ha tyad indra codīḥ sakhā vṛtraṁ yad vajrin vṛṣakarmann ubhnāḥ | yad dha śūra vṛṣamaṇaḥ parācair vi dasyūm̐r yonāv akṛto vṛthāṣāṭ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tvaṁ: You
    • ha: indeed
    • tyad: that
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • codīḥ: inspire or encourage
    • sakhā: friend or ally
    • vṛtraṁ: Vritra (the serpent or dragon, symbolizing obstacles)
    • yad: which
    • vajrin: the one who wields the thunderbolt
    • vṛṣakarmann: in the act of doing good deeds
    • ubhnāḥ: having overcome
    • yad: which
    • dha: indeed
    • śūra: the brave or the strong
    • vṛṣamaṇaḥ: the one who brings forth strength
    • parācair: from the enemies
    • vi: indeed
    • dasyūm̐r: the wicked or the evil-doers
    • yonāv: from the wombs
    • akṛto: not done
    • vṛthāṣāṭ: in vain or without purpose
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a call to Indra, the chief deity in the Rigveda, who represents strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles.
    • The speaker seeks inspiration and encouragement from Indra, asking him to act as a friend in overcoming the challenges represented by Vritra, a metaphor for obstacles and adversities in life.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of courage and strength in facing adversities, suggesting that true bravery comes from overcoming challenges and not succumbing to fear.
    • It also highlights the idea that the efforts to overcome evil (represented by the wicked) should not be in vain, and that divine support is essential in this struggle.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, when facing a significant challenge such as a tough project or a difficult colleague, one might invoke the spirit of this mantra by seeking support from mentors or colleagues, akin to asking for Indra's encouragement.
    • In personal life, when dealing with adversities such as health issues or family problems, individuals can draw strength from their support systems, much like the strength derived from divine intervention in the mantra.
    • In social justice movements, activists often face significant opposition (the 'Vritra' of their cause). This mantra serves as a reminder to remain courageous and united in the face of adversity, ensuring that their efforts are not in vain.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine support and inner strength in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It teaches that one should not face adversities alone and that seeking help from allies, whether divine or human, is crucial.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to be brave, to act against evil, and to ensure that their efforts are purposeful and not in vain.
