आ नो बरही ऋषादसो वरुणो मित्रो आर्यमा |सीदन्तु मनुषो यथा ||
ā no barhī riśādaso varuṇo mitro aryamā | sīdantu manuṣo yathā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: May (we) approach
    • no: us
    • barhī: the sacred grass (often used in rituals)
    • riśādaso: of the wise or learned
    • varuṇo: Varuna (the god of cosmic order and water)
    • mitro: Mitra (the god of friendship and contracts)
    • aryamā: Aryama (a deity associated with the ancestors)
    • sīdantu: may they sit or dwell
    • manuṣo: among humans
    • yathā: as
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the presence of divine beings, specifically Varuna, Mitra, and Aryama, asking them to be present among humans.
    • The term "barhī" refers to the sacred grass used in Vedic rituals, symbolizing purity and sanctity.
    • The phrase "sīdantu manuṣo yathā" suggests a desire for these divine entities to dwell among humans, guiding and protecting them.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a wedding ceremony, the couple may invoke divine blessings for their union, similar to how this mantra seeks the presence of deities for guidance.
    • During community gatherings or festivals, people often pray for harmony and the presence of positive energies, akin to the invocation in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine guidance and the need for a harmonious relationship between humans and the divine.
    • It is applicable in various life scenarios, such as rituals, ceremonies, and personal prayers, where individuals seek blessings and support from higher powers.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of humanity and the divine, encouraging individuals to foster a sense of community and spirituality in their lives.
