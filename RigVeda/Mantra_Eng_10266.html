युवोः हि मातादितिः विचेतसां द्यौः न भूमिः पयसा पूपूतनि |अव प्रिया दिदिष्टना सूरो निनिक्त रश्मिभिः ||
yuvor hi mātāditir vicetasā dyaur na bhūmiḥ payasā pupūtani | ava priyā didiṣṭana sūro ninikta raśmibhiḥ ||
  • yuvor hi mātā: This phrase translates to "the young ones, indeed, are like a mother." It signifies the nurturing aspect of youth, suggesting that the young have the potential to care for and support others, much like a mother does.
  • aditir vicetasā: "Aditi" refers to the infinite or the boundless, often associated with the mother of gods in Vedic literature. "Vicetasā" means with consciousness or awareness. Together, this implies that the boundless nature of existence is rooted in conscious awareness.
  • dyaur na bhūmiḥ: This translates to "the sky is not the earth." It highlights the distinction between the celestial and the terrestrial, suggesting a balance between the spiritual and the material realms.
  • payasā pupūtani: "Payasā" means with nourishment or sustenance, and "pupūtani" refers to being filled or satisfied. This indicates that true fulfillment comes from nurturing and sustaining life.
  • ava priyā didiṣṭana: "Ava" means down or away, "priyā" means dear or beloved, and "didiṣṭana" refers to the act of desiring or wishing. This suggests a longing for what is cherished, emphasizing the importance of love and connection.
  • sūro ninikta raśmibhiḥ: "Sūro" means the hero or the divine, "ninikta" refers to being established or grounded, and "raśmibhiḥ" means with rays or light. This implies that the divine or heroic qualities are grounded in the light of awareness and consciousness.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of nurturing, awareness, and the balance between the spiritual and material worlds. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as parenting, where the nurturing aspect of youth is crucial. It also emphasizes the importance of conscious living, where individuals are aware of their actions and their impact on others. The distinction between the sky and the earth serves as a reminder to seek balance in our lives, appreciating both our spiritual aspirations and our material responsibilities. Ultimately, this mantra encourages us to cultivate love, awareness, and a sense of connection with the world around us, fostering a harmonious existence.
