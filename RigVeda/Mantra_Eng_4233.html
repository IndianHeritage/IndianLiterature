विष्वे हि विष्ववेदसो वरुणो मित्रो आर्यमाः |व्रता पदेव सश्चिरे पान्ति मृत्यं ऋषः ||
viśve hi viśvavedaso varuṇo mitro aryamā | vratā padeva saścire pānti martyaṁ riṣaḥ ||
  • viśve hi viśvavedaso: This phrase translates to "All beings, indeed, are knowledgeable." It emphasizes the idea that every entity in the universe possesses some form of wisdom or understanding. In real life, this can be seen in how different cultures and communities have their own unique knowledge systems, contributing to a collective understanding of the world.
  • varuṇo mitro aryamā: Here, "Varuna" represents the cosmic order and moral authority, "Mitro" signifies friendship and harmony, and "Aryamā" refers to the noble or the honorable. Together, they symbolize the interconnectedness of moral values, friendship, and nobility in maintaining societal order. For example, in a workplace, fostering a culture of respect and collaboration can lead to a more productive and harmonious environment.
  • vratā padeva saścire: This translates to "The vows (or commitments) are like a path." It suggests that commitments guide us in our journey through life. In practical terms, this can be related to personal goals or professional ethics. For instance, a student committing to their studies is following a path that leads to academic success.
  • pānti martyaṁ riṣaḥ: This means "The wise protect the mortal beings." It highlights the role of wisdom in safeguarding life. In real life, this can be seen in the actions of leaders or mentors who use their knowledge to guide and protect those who are less experienced. For example, a teacher who nurtures and supports their students is embodying this principle.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of interconnectedness, wisdom, and moral responsibility. It teaches us that every being has knowledge to share, and that through friendship, commitment, and wisdom, we can create a harmonious society. In real-life scenarios, this mantra is applicable in various contexts such as education, leadership, and community building, reminding us of our duty to protect and uplift one another.
