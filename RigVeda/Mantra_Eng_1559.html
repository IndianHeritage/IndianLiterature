मन्द्रजिह्वा जुगुर्वणी होतारा दैव्याः कविः |यज्ञं नो यक्षताम इमं सिद्ध्रम अद्य दिविष्पृशम् ||
mandrajihvā jugurvaṇī hotārā daivyā kavī | yajñaṁ no yakṣatām imaṁ sidhram adya divispṛśam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mandrajihvā: Refers to the "one with a sweet tongue" or "the one who speaks sweetly." This can symbolize a person who communicates effectively and harmoniously.
    • jugurvaṇī: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is eloquent" or "the one who has a beautiful voice." It emphasizes the importance of articulate expression.
    • hotārā: This means "the priest" or "the one who invokes." It signifies the role of a mediator between the divine and the human realms.
    • daivyā: Translates to "divine" or "heavenly," indicating a connection to higher powers or spiritual entities.
    • kavī: This word means "poet" or "sage," representing wisdom and the ability to convey profound truths through art.
    • yajñaṁ: Refers to "sacrifice" or "offering," a central concept in Vedic rituals that signifies devotion and the act of giving.
    • no: Means "our," indicating a collective identity or community.
    • yakṣatām: This can be interpreted as "may it be fulfilled" or "may it be granted," expressing a desire for blessings or outcomes.
    • imaṁ: Means "this," referring to the specific offering or sacrifice being made.
    • sidhram: Translates to "successful" or "auspicious," indicating the desired outcome of the yajña.
    • adya: Means "today," emphasizing the immediacy of the request or action.
    • divispṛśam: Refers to "the one who touches the heavens," symbolizing aspirations that reach for the divine or the highest ideals.
  • Significance and Real-Life Application:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of eloquence and effective communication in our lives. Just as the "sweet-tongued" and "eloquent" individuals can inspire and lead, we too can strive to communicate positively and constructively in our daily interactions.
    • The role of the "hotārā" or priest signifies the importance of guidance and mentorship. In real life, we often look to mentors or leaders who can help us navigate challenges and invoke our potential.
    • The concept of "yajña" or sacrifice is relevant in our lives as it encourages us to contribute to our communities and make sacrifices for the greater good. This can be seen in acts of charity, volunteering, or simply being there for others in times of need.
    • The aspiration to achieve "sidhram" or success is universal. This mantra reminds us to set our goals and work towards them with dedication, seeking divine blessings or support in our endeavors.
    • Finally, the phrase "divispṛśam" encourages us to aim high and reach for our dreams, reminding us that our aspirations can connect us to something greater than ourselves.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of effective communication, guidance, sacrifice, and aspiration. In our daily lives, we can apply these principles by striving to communicate positively, seeking mentorship, contributing to our communities, pursuing our goals with dedication, and aiming high in our aspirations. By embodying these values, we can create a more harmonious and fulfilling life, both for ourselves and for those around us.
