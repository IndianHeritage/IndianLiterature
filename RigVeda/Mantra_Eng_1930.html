अतारीष्म तमसस् पारम् अस्य प्रति वां स्तोमो अश्विनाव् अधायि |एह यातम् पथिभिर देवयानैर विद्यामेषं वृजनं जीरदानुम् ||
atāriṣma tamasas pāram asya prati vāṁ stomo aśvināv adhāyi | eha yātam pathibhir devayānair vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum ||
  • atāriṣma: This word can be interpreted as "to transcend" or "to overcome." It signifies the act of rising above challenges or obstacles.
  • tamasas: This translates to "darkness" or "ignorance." It represents the state of being lost or unaware, often associated with confusion or lack of knowledge.
  • pāram: Meaning "beyond" or "across," it indicates a journey or transition from one state to another, often implying enlightenment or clarity.
  • asya: This word means "of this" or "of this world," referring to the current existence or reality we inhabit.
  • prati: Translated as "towards" or "in the direction of," it suggests movement or intention towards a goal.
  • vāṁ: This means "your" or "of you," indicating a personal connection or address to the listener or the divine.
  • stomo: This can be understood as "praise" or "hymn," reflecting the act of expressing reverence or gratitude.
  • aśvināv: Referring to the "Ashvins," the twin deities of health and medicine in Vedic tradition, symbolizing healing and well-being.
  • adhāyi: This means "to bestow" or "to grant," indicating the act of giving or providing something valuable.
  • eha: Translated as "here" or "in this place," it emphasizes the present moment and the importance of being grounded in reality.
  • yātam: This word means "to go" or "to move," suggesting action and progress.
  • pathibhir: Meaning "by the paths," it refers to the various ways or methods one can take in life.
  • devayānair: This translates to "by the divine beings," indicating the influence or guidance of higher powers or spiritual entities.
  • vidyāmeṣaṁ: Meaning "this knowledge," it emphasizes the importance of wisdom and understanding in one's journey.
  • vṛjanaṁ: This can be interpreted as "the group" or "the community," highlighting the significance of collective effort and support.
  • jīradānum: This translates to "the essence of life," referring to the vital energy or spirit that sustains existence.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound journey from ignorance to enlightenment, emphasizing the importance of transcending darkness through knowledge and divine guidance. It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and support from the community while acknowledging the role of divine beings in their journey. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal growth, education, and community building, where individuals strive to overcome challenges and seek a deeper understanding of themselves and the world around them. It serves as a reminder that with the right guidance and collective effort, one can navigate through life's complexities and achieve a state of clarity and purpose.
