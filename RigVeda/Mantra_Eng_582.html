सं नो रायाऽ बृहतā विश्वपेक्षसā मिमिक्ष्वा सम इळाभिर ā |सं द्युम्नेन विश्वतुरोषो महि सं वाजैर वाजिनिवति ||
saṁ no rāyā bṛhatā viśvapeśasā mimikṣvā sam iḻābhir ā | saṁ dyumnena viśvaturoṣo mahi saṁ vājair vājinīvati ||
  • saṁ no rāyā: This phrase translates to "may we be blessed with prosperity." The term "rāyā" refers to wealth or abundance, indicating a desire for material and spiritual richness in life.
  • bṛhatā: This word means "great" or "vast." It signifies the importance of seeking not just any wealth, but a profound and expansive form of prosperity that encompasses all aspects of life.
  • viśvapeśasā: This term can be interpreted as "with the power of the universe." It suggests that the prosperity sought is not just personal but is connected to the greater cosmic order.
  • mimikṣvā: This word means "may we see" or "may we perceive." It emphasizes the importance of awareness and understanding in recognizing and appreciating the blessings of life.
  • sam iḻābhir ā: This phrase translates to "with all our efforts." It indicates that achieving prosperity requires collective action and collaboration.
  • saṁ dyumnena: This means "with glory" or "with brilliance." It suggests that the prosperity we seek should be accompanied by honor and respect.
  • viśvaturoṣo: This term can be understood as "the one who encompasses all." It refers to a universal force or deity that supports and nurtures all beings.
  • mahi: This word means "great" or "mighty," reinforcing the idea of seeking something substantial and powerful.
  • saṁ vājair vājinīvati: This phrase translates to "may we be endowed with strength and vigor." It emphasizes the need for energy and determination to achieve our goals.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound desire for collective prosperity, awareness, and strength. It highlights the interconnectedness of individual efforts with the universal forces that govern existence. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in community development projects, where collaboration and shared vision lead to greater outcomes. It also serves as a reminder to seek not just material wealth but a holistic form of prosperity that includes mental, emotional, and spiritual well-being. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and communities can strive for a more abundant and harmonious life.
