प्रातः रथो नवो योऽजि सस्निष चतुर्व्युगस त्रिकशः सप्तर्षिमः |दशारित्रो मनुष्यः स्वर्षाः स इष्टिभिः मतिभिः रम्ह्यो भूत ||
prātā ratho navo yoji sasniś caturyugas trikaśaḥ saptaraśmiḥ | daśāritro manuṣyaḥ svarṣāḥ sa iṣṭibhir matibhī raṁhyo bhūt ||
  • prātā: This word translates to "morning" or "dawn," symbolizing new beginnings and the arrival of light after darkness.
  • ratho: Meaning "chariot," it represents the vehicle of progress and movement in life.
  • nava: This means "new," indicating freshness and innovation.
  • yoji: Translates to "one who is skilled" or "expert," suggesting the importance of knowledge and expertise in our endeavors.
  • sasniś: This word can be interpreted as "to shine" or "to illuminate," emphasizing the role of enlightenment and clarity.
  • caturyugas: Refers to the "four ages" or "yugas," which are significant periods in Hindu cosmology, representing the cyclical nature of time.
  • trikaśaḥ: This means "threefold" or "triple," which can symbolize various triads in life, such as mind, body, and spirit.
  • saptaraśmiḥ: Translates to "seven rays," often associated with the sun and its life-giving energy, representing vitality and growth.
  • daśāritro: This word can be broken down to mean "ten" (daśa) and "arrows" (aritra), which may symbolize focus and direction in life.
  • manuṣyaḥ: Meaning "human" or "mankind," it emphasizes the human experience and our journey through life.
  • svarṣāḥ: This can be interpreted as "divine" or "heavenly," indicating a connection to higher consciousness or spirituality.
  • sa: Meaning "he" or "that," referring to the essence or the subject of the mantra.
  • iṣṭibhir: This word means "with offerings" or "sacrifices," highlighting the importance of giving and gratitude.
  • matibhī: Translates to "with thoughts" or "with intentions," emphasizing the power of our mindset and beliefs.
  • raṁhyo: This means "prosperous" or "auspicious," indicating success and well-being.
  • bhūt: Refers to "existence" or "being," connecting to the essence of life and the universe.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of life, emphasizing the importance of new beginnings, expertise, and enlightenment. It reflects the cyclical nature of existence and the interconnectedness of humanity with the divine. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to embrace change, seek knowledge, and cultivate a positive mindset. It serves as a reminder that our thoughts and intentions shape our reality, and through gratitude and offerings, we can attract prosperity and well-being into our lives. This mantra is applicable in various contexts, such as personal development, spiritual growth, and community building, encouraging us to strive for a harmonious existence.
