मित्रा तना न रथ्या वरुणो यश च सुक्रतुः |सनात सुजातā तनया धृतव्रता ||
mitrā tanā na rathyā3 varuṇo yaś ca sukratuḥ | sanāt sujātā tanayā dhṛtavratā ||
  • mitrā: This word translates to "friend" or "allies." In a broader sense, it signifies the importance of friendship and cooperation in life.
  • tanā: This means "body" or "form." It emphasizes the physical aspect of existence and the connection between the body and the spirit.
  • na rathyā: This phrase can be interpreted as "not wandering" or "not straying." It suggests the importance of staying on the right path and maintaining focus in life.
  • varuṇo: Referring to "Varuna," the deity associated with water, cosmic order, and truth. Varuna represents the moral and ethical order of the universe.
  • yaś: This means "fame" or "glory." It highlights the significance of achieving recognition through righteous actions.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking the ideas presented in the mantra.
  • sukratuḥ: This translates to "good intention" or "noble thought." It emphasizes the importance of having a pure and positive mindset.
  • sanāt: Meaning "eternal" or "everlasting," it signifies the timeless nature of certain truths and values.
  • sujātā: This means "well-born" or "noble." It refers to the idea of being born into a good lineage or having good qualities.
  • tanayā: This translates to "offspring" or "children," indicating the importance of family and the continuation of values through generations.
  • dhṛtavratā: This means "steadfast in vows" or "committed to one's promises." It emphasizes the importance of dedication and integrity in one's actions.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of living a life grounded in friendship, moral integrity, and good intentions. It teaches us the significance of staying true to our values and commitments while fostering positive relationships with others. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as in personal relationships, professional environments, and community interactions. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a harmonious society, promote ethical behavior, and ensure the well-being of future generations.
