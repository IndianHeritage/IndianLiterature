तदन्नाय तदपसे तं भागम् उपसेदुषे |तृताय च द्विताय चोषो दुष्ष्वप्न्यं वहानेहसो वा ऊतयः सूतयः वा ऊतयः ||
tadannāya tadapase tam bhāgam upaseduṣe | tritāya ca dvitāya coṣo duṣṣvapnyaṁ vahānehaso va ūtayaḥ suūtayo va ūtayaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "tadannāya tadapase tam bhāgam upaseduṣe | tritāya ca dvitāya coṣo duṣṣvapnyaṁ vahānehaso va ūtayaḥ suūtayo va ūtayaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tadannāya: That which is to be attained
    • tadapase: That which is to be worshipped or revered
    • tam bhāgam: That portion or aspect
    • upaseduṣe: To be approached or sought after
    • tritāya: The third
    • dvitāya: The second
    • coṣo: The one who is knowledgeable or wise
    • duṣṣvapnyaṁ: Difficult to attain or elusive
    • vahānehaso: One who carries or bears
    • va ūtayaḥ: And those who are enlightened
    • suūtayo: Those who are well-versed or skilled
    • va ūtayaḥ: And those who are enlightened
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the pursuit of knowledge and the reverence for wisdom.
    • It emphasizes the importance of seeking out that which is beneficial and worthy of worship.
    • The mention of "tritāya" and "dvitāya" suggests a hierarchy or levels of understanding, indicating that wisdom can be approached in stages.
    • The phrase "duṣṣvapnyaṁ" highlights the challenges in attaining true knowledge, which can often feel elusive.
    • In real life, this can be likened to the journey of education and personal growth, where one must navigate through various levels of understanding and face challenges along the way.
    • For example, a student may start with basic concepts (dvitāya) and gradually move to more complex ideas (tritāya), facing difficulties (duṣṣvapnyaṁ) that require perseverance and dedication.
    • The idea of "vahānehaso" and "suūtayo" can be seen in mentors or teachers who guide students, carrying the burden of knowledge and helping others to become enlightened.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking knowledge and wisdom in life.
    • It teaches us to respect and revere those who possess knowledge and to strive for understanding despite the challenges we may face.
    • In practical terms, it encourages continuous learning and the pursuit of personal growth, reminding us that wisdom is a journey that requires effort and dedication.
    • Ultimately, this mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, including education, personal development, and professional growth, as we seek to attain higher levels of understanding and enlightenment.
