मन्त्र: वरिष्टे न इन्द्र वन्धुरे धा वहिष्टयोः शतावन्न अश्वयोः आ |इषम आ वक्षीषां वरिष्ष्ठाम मा नस् तारीन मघवन रायो आर्यः ||
variṣṭhe na indra vandhure dhā vahiṣṭhayoḥ śatāvann aśvayor ā | iṣam ā vakṣīṣāṁ varṣiṣṭhām mā nas tārīn maghavan rāyo aryaḥ ||
  • Variṣṭhe: This word translates to "the best" or "the most excellent." It signifies the highest quality or the utmost excellence in a given context.
  • Na: This is a negation, meaning "not." It indicates the absence of something.
  • Indra: Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with rain, storms, and the king of gods. He represents strength and power.
  • Vandhure: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is bound" or "the one who is connected." It suggests a relationship or a bond.
  • Dhā: This means "to hold" or "to support." It implies stability and strength.
  • Vahiṣṭhayoḥ: This word refers to "the best of the best" or "the most excellent among the excellent." It emphasizes superiority.
  • Śatāvann: This translates to "hundreds" or "many." It indicates abundance or a large quantity.
  • Aśvayor: This means "of the horses." In Vedic culture, horses symbolize strength, speed, and wealth.
  • Iṣam: This word means "the arrow" or "the weapon." It symbolizes precision and focus.
  • Ā: This is a preposition meaning "towards" or "to." It indicates direction or intention.
  • Vakṣīṣāṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the best of the best" or "the most excellent." It reinforces the idea of superiority.
  • Varṣiṣṭhām: This means "the most excellent" or "the best." It emphasizes the highest quality.
  • Mā: This is a negation, meaning "do not." It indicates a request or a plea.
  • Nas: This means "us" or "our." It signifies a collective identity.
  • Tārīn: This translates to "to save" or "to protect." It implies a sense of security and safety.
  • Maghavan: This refers to "the generous one" or "the one who bestows wealth." It is often associated with prosperity.
  • Rāyo: This means "wealth" or "prosperity." It signifies abundance and success.
  • Aryaḥ: This term means "noble" or "honorable." It represents virtue and integrity.

In summary, this mantra invokes the blessings of Indra, asking for the best qualities and abundance in life. It emphasizes the importance of strength, protection, and prosperity while also highlighting the need for noble intentions. The plea for protection and wealth reflects a universal human desire for security and success.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

  • This mantra can be applied in various aspects of life, such as:
    • Personal Growth: Striving for excellence in personal and professional endeavors, seeking to be the best version of oneself.
    • Community Support: Encouraging collective efforts to protect and uplift the community, fostering a sense of unity and strength.
    • Wealth and Prosperity: Acknowledging the importance of generosity and sharing wealth, promoting a culture of giving and support.
    • Spiritual Practice: Using this mantra in meditation or prayer to invoke strength, protection, and abundance in one's life.
