यातं चर्दिष्पा उता नः परस्पा भूतं जगत्पा उता नस् तनूपा |वर्तिस तोकाय तनयाय यातम् ||
yātaṁ chardiṣpā uta naḥ paraspā bhūtaṁ jagatpā uta nas tanūpā | vartis tokāya tanayāya yātam ||
  • yātaṁ - This word can be interpreted as "that which has been achieved" or "that which has come to pass." It signifies the results of actions or efforts.
  • chardiṣpā - This term refers to "the one who is in a state of bliss" or "the one who is fulfilled." It indicates a state of contentment and satisfaction.
  • uta - This word means "and" or "also," serving as a conjunction to connect thoughts or ideas.
  • naḥ - This translates to "our" or "of us," indicating possession or belonging.
  • paraspā - This can be understood as "the one who is beyond" or "the one who transcends," suggesting a higher state of being or consciousness.
  • bhūtaṁ - This word means "the created" or "the manifested," referring to the physical world and all that exists within it.
  • jagatpā - This term translates to "the protector of the world," indicating a divine or higher power that safeguards the universe.
  • uta - Again, this word serves as a conjunction, reinforcing the connection between ideas.
  • nas - This means "us" or "to us," emphasizing the collective aspect of the mantra.
  • tanūpā - This can be interpreted as "the body" or "the physical form," referring to the material existence of individuals.
  • vartis - This word means "to dwell" or "to reside," indicating a state of being or existence.
  • tokāya - This term refers to "for the offspring" or "for the descendants," highlighting the importance of future generations.
  • tanayāya - This translates to "for the child" or "for the progeny," further emphasizing the focus on lineage and continuity.
  • yātam - This word signifies "that which is to be achieved" or "the goal," indicating aspirations and objectives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of striving for fulfillment and protection in both the material and spiritual realms. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of individual existence with the greater universe and the importance of safeguarding future generations. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as parenting, where the focus is on nurturing and protecting children, or in community efforts aimed at ensuring a sustainable and harmonious environment for all. It serves as a reminder of our responsibilities towards ourselves, our families, and the world at large, urging us to seek balance and fulfillment in our lives while being mindful of the legacy we leave behind.
