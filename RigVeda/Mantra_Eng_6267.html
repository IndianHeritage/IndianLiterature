यद अद्याश्विनाव अपाग यत प्राक स्थो वाजिनीवासू |यद द्रुह्यव्य अनवितुर्वशे यदौ हुवे वाम अथ मा गतम् ||
yad adyāśvināv apāg yat prāk stho vājinīvasū | yad druhyavy anavi turvaśe yadau huve vām atha mā gatam ||
  • Mantra: "yad adyāśvināv apāg yat prāk stho vājinīvasū | yad druhyavy anavi turvaśe yadau huve vām atha mā gatam ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be interpreted as a call to the Ashvins, the divine twin horsemen, to come forth and assist in times of need. It acknowledges their past actions and seeks their presence and blessings.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • yad: that which
    • adyā: today
    • āśvināv: to the Ashvins
    • apāg: have come
    • yat: that which
    • prāk: before
    • stho: standing
    • vājinīvasū: those who bring wealth or prosperity
    • druhya: those who are friendly or benevolent
    • anavi: without harm
    • turvaśe: to the one who is powerful
    • yadau: when I call
    • huve: I invoke
    • vām: you
    • atha: then
    • mā: not
    • gatam: gone or departed
  • Contextual Significance:
    • The Ashvins are often associated with healing, health, and the dawn. They symbolize the arrival of new opportunities and the dispelling of darkness.
    • This mantra can be recited in times of distress or when one seeks guidance and support in overcoming challenges.
    • In real life, this can be likened to seeking help from mentors, friends, or divine forces when facing difficulties, much like calling upon a trusted ally in times of need.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking assistance and recognizing the benevolent forces in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that we are not alone in our struggles and that help is available if we reach out for it.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to foster relationships and build a support system, whether through community, family, or spiritual practices.
