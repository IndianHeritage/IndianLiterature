अप्सु धूतस्य हरिवः पिभेह नृभिः सुतस्य जठरं पृणस्व |मिमिक्षुर यम अद्रय इन्द्र तुभ्यं तेभिर वर्धस्व मदं उक्थवाहः ||
apsu dhūtasya harivaḥ pibeha nṛbhiḥ sutasya jaṭharam pṛṇasva | mimikṣur yam adraya indra tubhyaṁ tebhir vardhasva madam ukthavāhaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apsu: in the waters
    • dhūtasya: of the purified
    • harivaḥ: O Indra, the one who brings joy
    • pibeha: drink
    • nṛbhiḥ: with the men
    • sutasya: of the son
    • jaṭharam: the belly
    • pṛṇasva: fill
    • mimikṣur: the one who is eager
    • yam: whom
    • adraya: the mountains
    • indra: O Indra
    • tubhyaṁ: for you
    • tebhir: with these
    • vardhasva: may you grow
    • madam: the intoxication
    • ukthavāhaḥ: the one who brings forth the hymn
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic tradition, asking him to partake in the offerings made by the devotees.
    • The phrase "in the waters of the purified" suggests a connection to the cleansing and rejuvenating properties of water, which is often seen as a source of life and sustenance.
    • By inviting Indra to "drink with the men," the mantra emphasizes the communal aspect of worship, where the divine is invited to partake in human experiences.
    • The request to "fill the belly of the son" symbolizes the nurturing aspect of the divine, ensuring that the next generation is provided for and supported.
    • The mention of "the one who is eager" and "the mountains" indicates a desire for growth and strength, both in the physical and spiritual realms.
    • Overall, the mantra reflects a deep connection between the divine and humanity, highlighting the importance of community, sustenance, and spiritual growth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to community gatherings where people come together to share food and drink, symbolizing unity and support.
    • It can also be seen in family traditions where blessings are sought for the well-being of children, ensuring they are nurtured and cared for.
    • In a broader sense, the mantra encourages individuals to seek growth and strength in their personal and communal lives, fostering a sense of belonging and purpose.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine presence in human affairs, emphasizing the importance of community, nourishment, and spiritual growth.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from family gatherings to community events, where the essence of sharing and support is celebrated.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, urging individuals to seek blessings for themselves and their communities while fostering a spirit of gratitude and reverence for the divine.
