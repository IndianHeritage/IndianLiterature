एव ता विश्वा चक्रवांसम इन्द्रं महाम उग्रं अजुर्म्यं सहोदाम् |सुवीरं त्वा स्वायुधं सुवज्रम आ ब्रह्म नव्यम अवसे ववृत्यात् ||
evā tā viśvā cakṛvāṁsam indram mahām ugram ajuryaṁ sahodām | suvīraṁ tvā svāyudhaṁ suvajram ā brahma navyam avase vavṛtyāt ||
  • evā tā viśvā cakṛvāṁsam: This phrase can be interpreted as "Thus, all the powers and energies are directed towards you." It signifies the collective strength and unity of various forces coming together.
  • indram mahām ugram ajuryaṁ sahodām: Here, "Indra" refers to the king of gods, representing power and authority. "Mahām ugram" translates to "great and fierce," indicating the formidable nature of Indra. "Ajuryaṁ" suggests immortality or eternal strength, while "sahodām" implies brotherhood or companionship, emphasizing the importance of unity in strength.
  • suvīraṁ tvā svāyudhaṁ suvajram: "Suvīraṁ" means "good hero," and "tvā" refers to "you." "Svāyudhaṁ" translates to "with your own weapons," indicating self-reliance and preparedness. "Suvajram" means "good thunderbolt," symbolizing power and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  • ā brahma navyam avase vavṛtyāt: "Ā brahma" refers to the ultimate reality or the cosmic spirit. "Navyam" means "new" or "fresh," suggesting innovation and renewal. "Avase vavṛtyāt" implies the act of manifesting or bringing forth, indicating the importance of creation and progress.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of unity, strength, and the power of collective energies. It highlights the importance of self-reliance and preparedness in facing challenges. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as teamwork in organizations, where individuals come together to achieve a common goal, or in personal development, where one harnesses their inner strength and resources to overcome obstacles. The mantra serves as a reminder that true power lies in collaboration and the ability to innovate and adapt in the face of adversity.
