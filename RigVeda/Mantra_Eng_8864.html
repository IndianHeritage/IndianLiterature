इदं आपः प्र वहत यत किं च दुरितं मयि |यद वाहम् अभिदुद्रोह यद वा शेप उतानृतम् ||
idam āpaḥ pra vahata yat kiṁ ca duritam mayi | yad vāham abhidudroha yad vā śepa utānṛtam ||
  • Translation: "This water, may it carry away whatever sin is in me; whatever I have done that is wrong, whatever falsehood I have uttered."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • idam: This
    • āpaḥ: Water
    • pra vahata: May it carry away
    • yat: Whatever
    • kiṁ ca: And also
    • duritam: Sin or wrongdoing
    • mayi: In me
    • yad: Whatever
    • vāham: I have done
    • abhidudroha: That is wrong
    • yad: Whatever
    • vā: Or
    • śepa: Falsehood
    • utānṛtam: I have uttered

  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra is a plea for purification and forgiveness. It acknowledges human fallibility and the need for cleansing from sins and falsehoods.
    • In real life, this can be related to moments of regret or guilt where individuals seek redemption or a fresh start. For example, someone who has wronged a friend may reflect on their actions and seek to make amends.
    • The use of water symbolizes purity and the cleansing of the soul, akin to how people often use water in rituals for purification, such as taking a bath before a significant event or during religious ceremonies.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of self-reflection and the desire for moral and spiritual cleanliness. It serves as a reminder that everyone makes mistakes, but acknowledging them and seeking forgiveness is crucial for personal growth.
    • In daily life, this mantra can inspire individuals to practice honesty, seek forgiveness, and strive for betterment, whether in personal relationships, professional settings, or spiritual journeys.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the universal human experience of seeking redemption and the transformative power of acknowledging one's faults.
