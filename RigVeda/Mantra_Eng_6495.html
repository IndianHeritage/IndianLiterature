त्वया ह स्विद् युजा वयम् प्रति श्वसन्तं वृषभ ब्रुवीमहि |संस्थे जनस्य गोमताḥ ||
tvayā ha svid yujā vayam prati śvasantaṁ vṛṣabha bruvīmahi | saṁsthe janasya gomataḥ ||
  • tvayā ha svid yujā vayam prati śvasantaṁ vṛṣabha bruvīmahi
    • tvayā - "by you" or "through you": This indicates a connection or reliance on a higher power or divine force.
    • ha - "indeed": This word emphasizes the certainty of the statement being made.
    • svid - "where": This word suggests a quest for understanding or a search for a place or state of being.
    • yujā - "to unite" or "to join": This implies a coming together, whether of people, ideas, or energies.
    • vayam - "we": This signifies a collective identity, emphasizing community and togetherness.
    • prati - "towards": This indicates direction or intention in the action being described.
    • śvasantaṁ - "breathing" or "inhaling": This can symbolize life, vitality, and the essence of existence.
    • vṛṣabha - "bull" or "strong one": In Vedic literature, the bull often represents strength, power, and fertility.
    • bruvīmahi - "we speak" or "we declare": This indicates an act of communication or expression of intent.
  • saṁsthe janasya gomataḥ
    • saṁsthe - "in the assembly" or "in the gathering": This refers to a collective space where people come together.
    • janasya - "of the people": This emphasizes the importance of community and the collective will of society.
    • gomataḥ - "of the cow" or "of the cattle": Cows are revered in Vedic culture, symbolizing nourishment, wealth, and the sustenance of life.

Conclusion:

This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, strength, and community in the pursuit of life and sustenance. It reflects a deep connection to the divine and the natural world, suggesting that through collective effort and communication, we can achieve harmony and prosperity. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • Community gatherings where people come together to support one another.
  • Collaborative projects that require teamwork and shared goals.
  • Environmental initiatives that emphasize the importance of caring for nature and animals, particularly in agricultural societies.

Ultimately, the mantra serves as a reminder of our interconnectedness and the strength we derive from unity, urging us to work together for the greater good.
