अस्मे धेहि द्युमतीं वाचं आसन बृहस्पते अनमीवाम इषिराम् |यया वृष्टिं शान्तनवे वनाव दिवो द्रप्सो मधुमाम् आविवेश ||
asme dhehi dyumatīṁ vācam āsan bṛhaspate anamīvām iṣirām | yayā vṛṣṭiṁ śaṁtanave vanāva divo drapso madhumām̐ ā viveśa ||
  • asme: This word translates to "to us" or "for us." It indicates a plea or request directed towards a higher power.
  • dhehi: Meaning "give" or "bestow," this word expresses a desire for something valuable or beneficial.
  • dyumatīṁ: This term refers to "brilliant" or "radiant," often associated with wisdom or enlightenment.
  • vācam: Translated as "speech" or "word," it signifies the importance of communication and expression.
  • āsan: Meaning "to sit" or "to be present," it suggests a state of readiness or receptivity.
  • bṛhaspate: Referring to "Bṛhaspati," the deity of wisdom and eloquence, this invokes divine guidance in matters of intellect and speech.
  • anamīvām: This word can be interpreted as "unfailing" or "everlasting," indicating a desire for enduring qualities.
  • iṣirām: Meaning "powerful" or "effective," it emphasizes the strength of the words or intentions being sought.
  • yayā: Translated as "by which," it connects the previous requests to the outcomes desired.
  • vṛṣṭiṁ: This term means "rain" or "abundance," symbolizing prosperity and nourishment.
  • śaṁtanave: Referring to "the one who brings peace," it highlights the importance of harmony and tranquility.
  • vanāva: Meaning "in the forest" or "in nature," it signifies a connection to the natural world and its resources.
  • divo: This word translates to "of the sky" or "heavenly," indicating a divine or celestial aspect.
  • drapso: Meaning "of the earth" or "earthly," it connects the divine with the material world.
  • madhumām̐: This term means "sweet" or "pleasant," often associated with joy and fulfillment.
  • ā viveśa: Translated as "enter" or "come forth," it signifies the manifestation of the desired qualities or blessings.

  • Significance: This mantra is a prayer for wisdom, eloquence, and abundance. It emphasizes the importance of clear communication and the desire for divine guidance in achieving prosperity and peace.
  • Real-life Application: In everyday life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:
    • In professional settings, individuals can invoke the essence of this mantra when preparing for presentations or negotiations, seeking clarity and confidence in their speech.
    • In personal relationships, it can serve as a reminder to communicate effectively and harmoniously, fostering understanding and connection.
    • During times of uncertainty or challenge, individuals can recite this mantra to attract abundance and positive outcomes, reinforcing their faith in divine support.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound request for wisdom, effective communication, and the blessings of abundance. Its significance lies in its ability to inspire individuals to seek clarity and harmony in their lives, making it applicable in various real-life situations where guidance and prosperity are desired.
