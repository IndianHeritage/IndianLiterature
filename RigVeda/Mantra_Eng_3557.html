वायो शतं हरिणां युवस्व पोष्याणाम् |उत वा ते सहस्रिणो रथ आ यातु पाजसाः ||
vāyo śataṁ harīṇāṁ yuvasva poṣyāṇām | uta vā te sahasriṇo ratha ā yātu pājasā ||
  • vāyo: This word translates to "O Wind" or "O Air". In the context of the Rigveda, it often symbolizes the vital force or energy that sustains life.
  • śataṁ: Meaning "hundred\ this term signifies abundance or a multitude, often used to express a wish for prosperity.
  • harīṇāṁ: This refers to "the swift ones" or "the horses". In Vedic literature, horses are often associated with strength, speed, and vitality.
  • yuvasva: This word means "may you be youthful" or "may you be vigorous". It is a blessing for vitality and energy.
  • poṣyāṇām: This translates to "of those who nourish" or "of those who sustain". It indicates a nurturing aspect, possibly referring to the forces that support life.
  • uta: This means "or" and is used to present an alternative or additional thought.
  • vā: This is another form of "or\ emphasizing choice or possibility.
  • te: This means "your\ indicating possession or connection.
  • sahasriṇo: This translates to "of the thousand\ again indicating abundance and strength.
  • ratha: Meaning "chariot\ it symbolizes movement, progress, and the journey of life.
  • ā: This is a preposition meaning "towards" or "to\ indicating direction.
  • yātu: This means "may it go" or "may it proceed\ expressing a wish for movement or progress.
  • pājasā: This translates to "with reverence" or "with honor\ indicating the importance of respect and devotion in the journey.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to the Wind, asking for the blessings of vitality, abundance, and progress. It emphasizes the importance of nurturing forces in life and the desire for a journey filled with honor and respect.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Health and Vitality: Just as the mantra invokes youthfulness, individuals can focus on maintaining their health through exercise, nutrition, and mental well-being.
  • Abundance in Life: The wish for "hundreds" can be seen in the pursuit of prosperity, whether in personal finances, relationships, or career growth.
  • Respectful Journey: The emphasis on reverence can be applied in daily interactions, promoting respect and honor in relationships and endeavors.

Conclusion: This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life forces, the importance of nurturing energy, and the pursuit of a respectful and abundant journey. It encourages individuals to seek vitality and progress while honoring the paths they take in life.
