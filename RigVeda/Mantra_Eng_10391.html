त्वं इन्द्रासि वृत्रहा व्य अन्तरिक्षम् अतिरः |उद् द्यां अस्तभ्ना ओजसा ||
tvam indrāsi vṛtrahā vy a1ntarikṣam atiraḥ | ud dyām astabhnā ojasā ||
  • tvam - This word translates to "you" or "thou," indicating a direct address to a divine entity.
  • indrāsi - This refers to "Indra," the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • vṛtrahā - This means "the slayer of Vritra," where Vritra represents a serpent or dragon that embodies chaos and obstacles. Indra is known for defeating Vritra, which signifies overcoming challenges.
  • vy - This is a particle that can mean "indeed" or "certainly," emphasizing the statement.
  • antarikṣam - This translates to "the space" or "the atmosphere," representing the realm between the earth and the heavens.
  • atiraḥ - This means "beyond" or "transcending," suggesting a sense of elevation or surpassing limitations.
  • ud - This word means "up" or "above," indicating a movement towards higher realms.
  • dyām - This refers to "the sky" or "heaven," symbolizing the divine or spiritual realm.
  • astabhnā - This means "to establish" or "to stabilize," indicating the act of creating a firm foundation.
  • ojasā - This translates to "with energy" or "with strength," emphasizing the power behind the actions being described.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to Indra, the powerful deity, to help establish strength and stability in the world, transcending obstacles and chaos. It reflects the human desire for divine assistance in overcoming challenges and achieving a higher state of being.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties, individuals can invoke the spirit of Indra to find the strength to overcome their personal Vritras, such as fears, doubts, or external obstacles.
  • Leadership: Leaders can embody the qualities of Indra by striving to be strong and decisive, guiding their teams through turbulent times and establishing a stable environment.
  • Spiritual Growth: The mantra encourages individuals to seek higher consciousness and transcend their limitations, promoting personal development and spiritual awakening.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of divine strength and guidance in overcoming life's challenges. It serves as a reminder that, with the right mindset and support, one can transcend obstacles and establish a stable and fulfilling life. By invoking the qualities of Indra, individuals can find the courage and energy needed to navigate their journeys effectively.
