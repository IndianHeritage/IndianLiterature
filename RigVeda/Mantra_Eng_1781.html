आ यद दुवस्याद दुवसे न कारुर अस्माण चक्रे मान्यस्य मेधा |ओ षु वर्त्त मरुतो विप्रम अच्छेमा ब्रह्माणि जरिता वो अर्चत ||
ā yad duvasyād duvase na kārur asmāñ cakre mānyasya medhā | o ṣu vartta maruto vipram acchemā brahmāṇi jaritā vo arcat ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yad: Whatever
    • duvasyād: is beneficial
    • duvase: for the one who is wise
    • na kārur: not harmful
    • asmāñ: to us
    • cakre: may create
    • mānyasya: of the respected
    • medhā: intelligence or wisdom
    • o: O
    • ṣu: auspicious
    • vartta: may flow
    • maruto: O Winds
    • vipram: the learned or wise
    • acchemā: may not be obstructed
    • brahmāṇi: in the divine or sacred
    • jaritā: the one who brings forth
    • vo: for you
    • arcat: may be worshipped
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom and intelligence in our lives.
    • It suggests that whatever is beneficial and wise should not be harmful to us.
    • The invocation to the winds (Maruts) symbolizes the flow of positive energy and knowledge.
    • It highlights the need for respect towards wisdom and the learned individuals in society.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, fostering an environment where ideas flow freely without fear of obstruction can lead to innovation.
    • In education, respecting teachers and valuing their wisdom can enhance the learning experience for students.
    • In personal relationships, open communication and respect for each other's thoughts can strengthen bonds.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of wisdom and respect in our lives.
    • It encourages us to seek beneficial knowledge and to create an environment where wisdom can thrive.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, harmonious relationships, and a more enlightened society.
