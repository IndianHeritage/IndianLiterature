देवेभ्यः कम अवृणीत मृत्युम प्रजायै कम अमृतं नावृणीत |बृहस्पतिं यज्ञं अकृण्वत ऋषिम प्रियां यमस्तन्वं प्रारिरेचीत ||
devebhyaḥ kam avṛṇīta mṛtyum prajāyai kam amṛtaṁ nāvṛṇīta | bṛhaspatiṁ yajñam akṛṇvata ṛṣim priyāṁ yamas tanva1m prārirecīt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • devebhyaḥ: From the gods
    • kam: Whom
    • avṛṇīta: Should not choose
    • mṛtyum: Death
    • prajāyai: For progeny
    • kam: Whom
    • amṛtaṁ: Immortality
    • nāvṛṇīta: Should not choose
    • bṛhaspatiṁ: To Brihaspati (the deity of wisdom)
    • yajñam: Sacrifice
    • akṛṇvata: They performed
    • ṛṣim: The sage
    • priyāṁ: Beloved
    • yamas: Of Yama (the god of death)
    • tanva1m: Body
    • prārirecīt: He should be invoked
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra reflects a profound philosophical inquiry into the nature of life and death.
    • It emphasizes the importance of choosing life (immortality) over death, particularly in the context of progeny and future generations.
    • The invocation of Brihaspati signifies the pursuit of wisdom and knowledge, suggesting that through understanding, one can navigate the complexities of life and death.
    • The mention of Yama, the god of death, indicates a recognition of mortality and the need to honor it while striving for a life of purpose and meaning.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the choices we make regarding health and well-being. For instance, choosing a healthy lifestyle can be seen as a way to embrace life and longevity.
    • In the context of education, the pursuit of knowledge (like Brihaspati) can lead to a more fulfilling life, where individuals contribute positively to society and future generations.
    • In family dynamics, the mantra encourages parents to focus on nurturing their children, ensuring they inherit wisdom and values that promote life and growth rather than fear of death.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the delicate balance between life and death, urging individuals to make conscious choices that favor life and wisdom.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement to seek knowledge and understanding, which can lead to a more meaningful existence.
    • In practical terms, it can inspire individuals to engage in practices that promote health, education, and the well-being of future generations, ultimately contributing to a more enlightened society.
