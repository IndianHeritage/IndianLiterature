तं नेमिम ऋभवो यथा नमस्व सहूतिभिः |नेदीयो यज्ञं अंगिरः ||
taṁ nemim ṛbhavo yathā namasva sahūtibhiḥ | nedīyo yajñam aṅgiraḥ ||
  • taṁ nemim ṛbhavo: This phrase can be translated as "to that wheel of the Ṛbhus." The Ṛbhus are divine beings associated with skill and craftsmanship. The "wheel" symbolizes the cycle of life and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • yathā namasva sahūtibhiḥ: This translates to "as we bow down with reverence." It emphasizes the importance of humility and respect in our interactions with the divine and the universe.
  • nedīyo yajñam aṅgiraḥ: This can be interpreted as "may we not be deprived of the sacrifice." The term "yajña" refers to a sacred ritual or offering, highlighting the significance of selfless actions and contributions to the greater good.

The mantra as a whole encourages individuals to recognize the divine craftsmanship in the universe, to approach it with humility, and to engage in selfless acts that contribute to the collective well-being. It serves as a reminder of the importance of reverence and respect in our lives.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for humility, respect, and selfless service. In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Community Service: Engaging in acts of kindness and service to others, recognizing the interconnectedness of all individuals.
  • Environmental Stewardship: Approaching nature with reverence and taking actions that protect and preserve the environment.
  • Personal Growth: Cultivating humility and respect in personal relationships, fostering a sense of community and collaboration.

By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more harmonious and respectful world, aligning their actions with the greater cosmic order.
