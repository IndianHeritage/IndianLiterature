वैश्वानरं विश्वहां दिदिवांसं मन्त्रैर अग्निं कविम अच्छा वदामः |यो महिम्ना परिभभूवोर्वी उतावस्ताद उता देवः परस्तात् ||
vaiśvānaraṁ viśvahā dīdivāṁsam mantrair agniṁ kavim acchā vadāmaḥ | yo mahimnā paribabhūvorvī utāvastād uta devaḥ parastāt ||
  • vaiśvānaraṁ: This term refers to the universal or cosmic aspect of fire, symbolizing the divine presence in all living beings. In real life, it can be related to the warmth and energy that sustains life, much like the sun's rays that nourish plants and provide energy to the ecosystem.
  • viśvahā: This word means "all-encompassing" or "universal." It signifies the idea that the divine fire is present everywhere, connecting all aspects of existence. For example, consider how technology connects people globally, creating a network of communication and understanding.
  • dīdivāṁsam: This term translates to "brilliant" or "radiant." It highlights the illuminating quality of fire, which can be likened to knowledge and enlightenment in our lives. For instance, education acts as a light that dispels ignorance, guiding individuals toward a better future.
  • mantrair: This word means "with mantras" or "through hymns." It emphasizes the power of words and intentions in shaping our reality. In everyday life, affirmations and positive self-talk can significantly influence our mindset and outcomes.
  • agniṁ: This refers to "fire," which is not just a physical element but also a symbol of transformation and purification. In practical terms, fire can represent the challenges we face that ultimately lead to personal growth, much like how a phoenix rises from its ashes.
  • kavim: This term means "the wise one" or "the poet." It suggests that the divine fire possesses wisdom and creativity. In real life, this can be seen in artists and thinkers who inspire others through their creative expressions, igniting passion and innovation.
  • acchā vadāmaḥ: This phrase translates to "we speak well" or "we declare." It signifies the importance of positive communication and intention in our lives. For example, encouraging words can uplift others and foster a supportive community.
  • yo mahimnā: This means "who with greatness" or "who is magnificent." It refers to the divine presence that embodies greatness. In real life, we can relate this to leaders or role models who inspire others through their actions and values.
  • paribabhūvorvī: This term means "who encompasses the earth." It signifies the all-pervading nature of the divine. In practical terms, it can be related to environmental consciousness, where we recognize our responsibility to care for the planet.
  • utāvastād: This translates to "and from there." It suggests a connection to higher realms or spiritual dimensions. In real life, this can be seen in practices like meditation, which help individuals connect with their inner selves and the universe.
  • uta devaḥ parastāt: This phrase means "and the divine is beyond." It emphasizes the transcendental nature of the divine. In everyday life, this can be reflected in the pursuit of higher ideals and values that guide our actions and decisions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of the divine fire, representing universal energy, wisdom, and the interconnectedness of all beings. Its significance lies in the reminder to recognize the divine presence in our lives and the world around us. By embracing the qualities of illumination, transformation, and positive communication, we can foster a more harmonious existence. This mantra encourages us to seek knowledge, inspire creativity, and care for our environment, ultimately leading to personal and collective growth.
