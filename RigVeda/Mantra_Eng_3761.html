तं हि शश्वन्त īḻते श्रुचā देवं घृतश्चुतā |अग्निं हव्या य वोल्हवे ||
taṁ hi śaśvanta īḻate srucā devaṁ ghṛtaścutā | agniṁ havyāya voḻhave ||
  • taṁ hi śaśvanta: This phrase can be translated as "that indeed is eternal." The term "śaśvanta" refers to something that is everlasting or perpetual. In real life, this can be related to concepts such as love, truth, or nature, which endure despite the changes around them.
  • īḻate: This word means "to shine" or "to illuminate." It signifies the presence of light or knowledge. For example, in our daily lives, education and wisdom can be seen as sources of illumination that guide our decisions and actions.
  • srucā: This term refers to "with offerings" or "with oblations." It highlights the importance of giving and sharing in our lives. In a practical sense, this can be seen in charitable acts or community service, where individuals contribute to the welfare of others.
  • devaṁ: This word means "divine" or "god." It represents the higher powers or forces that govern the universe. In real life, people often seek guidance from spiritual beliefs or practices, which can provide comfort and direction during challenging times.
  • ghṛtaścutā: This term can be interpreted as "the clarified butter that flows." Ghee (ghṛta) is often used in rituals and symbolizes purity and nourishment. In everyday life, this can relate to the idea of nurturing oneself and others, whether through food, care, or emotional support.
  • agniṁ: This word means "fire." Fire is a powerful symbol in many cultures, representing transformation, energy, and purification. In practical terms, it can be seen in the way we use fire for cooking, warmth, and even as a metaphor for passion and motivation in our pursuits.
  • havyāya: This term refers to "for the offerings" or "for the divine." It emphasizes the act of giving back to the divine or the universe. In real life, this can be reflected in the practice of gratitude, where individuals acknowledge and appreciate the blessings they receive.
  • voḻhave: This word means "to be invoked" or "to be called upon." It signifies the act of reaching out to the divine or seeking assistance. In our lives, this can be seen in prayer, meditation, or moments of reflection where we seek guidance or support from a higher power.

Conclusion: The mantra "taṁ hi śaśvanta īḻate srucā devaṁ ghṛtaścutā | agniṁ havyāya voḻhave ||" encapsulates the essence of seeking the eternal truth and divine guidance through offerings and illumination. Its significance lies in the reminder to recognize the enduring aspects of life, to give back to the community and the divine, and to seek knowledge and support in our journeys. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of gratitude, engage in acts of kindness, and pursue wisdom, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and harmonious existence.
