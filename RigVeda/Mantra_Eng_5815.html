अस्मे इन्द्रावरुणा विश्ववारं रयिं धत्तं वसुमन्तं पुरुक्षुम् |प्र य आदित्यो अनृतā मिनात्य अमितā शूरो दयते वसूनि ||
asme indrāvaruṇā viśvavāraṁ rayiṁ dhattaṁ vasumantam purukṣum | pra ya ādityo anṛtā mināty amitā śūro dayate vasūni ||
  • asme - This word translates to "to us" or "for us," indicating a plea or request directed towards the divine.
  • indrāvaruṇā - This refers to the combined powers of Indra, the king of gods, and Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water. Together, they symbolize strength and the upholding of natural laws.
  • viśvavāraṁ - This means "all-protecting" or "universal protector," suggesting a divine force that safeguards all beings.
  • rayiṁ - This word signifies "wealth" or "prosperity," indicating a desire for abundance in life.
  • dhattaṁ - Translated as "may grant" or "may bestow," it expresses a wish for the divine to provide.
  • vasumantam - This means "possessor of wealth," referring to the divine as a source of material and spiritual riches.
  • purukṣum - This translates to "many forms" or "varied," indicating the diverse manifestations of the divine in the world.
  • pra - A prefix meaning "forth" or "towards," suggesting movement or direction.
  • yādityo - Referring to "the sun" or "Aditya," symbolizing light, knowledge, and enlightenment.
  • anṛtā - This means "falsehood" or "deceit," indicating a desire for truth and integrity.
  • mināty - Translated as "may not diminish," expressing a wish for the preservation of truth and righteousness.
  • amitā - This means "infinite" or "immeasurable," suggesting boundless qualities of the divine.
  • śūro - This translates to "brave" or "heroic," indicating strength and valor.
  • dayate - Meaning "to give" or "to bestow," it expresses the act of generosity.
  • vasūni - This refers to "wealth" or "prosperities," indicating the blessings of abundance.

In summary, this mantra is a prayer for divine blessings from Indra and Varuna, seeking protection, prosperity, and truth. It emphasizes the importance of integrity and the desire for abundance in various forms. The invocation of the sun symbolizes the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, while the plea for bravery and generosity highlights the values of courage and compassion.


Significance and Real-Life Application:

This mantra can be applied in various real-life scenarios, such as:

  • In personal life, individuals can recite this mantra to seek protection and guidance in their endeavors, fostering a sense of security and abundance.
  • In business, it can be used to invoke prosperity and ethical practices, ensuring that success is achieved with integrity.
  • In community settings, the mantra encourages collective strength and support, promoting values of bravery and generosity among members.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of truth, and the pursuit of prosperity through righteous means.
