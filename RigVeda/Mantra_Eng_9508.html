इमां धियं सप्तशीर्ष्णीम् पिता न ऋत्प्रजाताम् बृहतीं अविन्दत् |तुरीयं स्विज जनयद् विश्वजन्योऽयास्य उक्तम् इन्द्राय शंसन् ||
imāṁ dhiyaṁ saptaśīrṣṇīm pitā na ṛtaprajātām bṛhatīm avindat | turīyaṁ svij janayad viśvajanyo 'yāsya uktham indrāya śaṁsan ||
  • imāṁ dhiyaṁ: This phrase translates to "this thought" or "this understanding." It emphasizes the importance of focused thought and intention in our lives.
  • saptaśīrṣṇīm: This means "seven-headed," which can symbolize the seven aspects of knowledge or the seven chakras in the human body, representing a holistic approach to understanding and wisdom.
  • pitā: This word means "father," indicating a source of guidance, support, and nurturing. In real life, this can relate to parental figures or mentors who provide wisdom and direction.
  • na ṛtaprajātām: This translates to "not born of truth," suggesting the importance of aligning our thoughts and actions with truth and righteousness. In everyday life, this can be seen in the importance of honesty in relationships and decision-making.
  • bṛhatīm: Meaning "great" or "vast," this word signifies the expansive nature of knowledge and understanding. It encourages individuals to seek greater wisdom beyond superficial understanding.
  • avindat: This means "found" or "discovered," indicating the process of seeking and uncovering deeper truths in life.
  • turīyaṁ: This translates to "the fourth," which can refer to a higher state of consciousness or awareness, beyond the ordinary experiences of life.
  • sviṣ: This word means "to create" or "to generate," emphasizing the active role we play in shaping our reality through our thoughts and actions.
  • janayad: This means "to give birth" or "to produce," highlighting the creative power inherent in our thoughts and intentions.
  • viśvajanyo: This translates to "universal" or "all-encompassing," suggesting that the knowledge and wisdom we seek are interconnected with the universe.
  • 'yāsya uktham: This means "whose utterance" or "whose words," indicating the power of speech and communication in manifesting our thoughts into reality.
  • indrāya: Referring to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic tradition, this signifies the importance of divine guidance and support in our endeavors.
  • śaṁsan: This translates to "to be praised" or "to be honored," emphasizing the value of recognizing and honoring the wisdom and guidance we receive.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of focused thought, the pursuit of truth, and the creative power of our intentions. It encourages individuals to seek wisdom and guidance from both earthly and divine sources, recognizing the interconnectedness of all knowledge. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as personal development, decision-making, and fostering meaningful relationships. By aligning our thoughts and actions with truth and seeking greater understanding, we can create a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
