मनोजवाऽयमान आयसीं अतरत पुरम् |दिवं सुपर्णो गत्वाय सोमं वज्रीण आभारत ||
manojavā ayamāna āyasīm atarat puram | divaṁ suparṇo gatvāya somaṁ vajriṇa ābharat ||
  • Manojavā: This word translates to "swift of mind" or "quick-witted." It signifies the importance of mental agility and the ability to think and act quickly in various situations.
  • Ayamāna: This term can be interpreted as "the one who is moving" or "the one who is in motion." It emphasizes the dynamic nature of life and the necessity of progress and movement in our endeavors.
  • Āyasīm: This word refers to "strength" or "power." It highlights the significance of inner strength and resilience in overcoming challenges.
  • Atarat puram: This phrase means "crossing the city" or "overcoming obstacles." It symbolizes the journey through life's challenges and the ability to navigate through difficulties.
  • Divaṁ: This translates to "heaven" or "the divine." It represents the higher aspirations and goals that one aims to achieve in life.
  • Suparṇo: This word means "well-winged" or "having good wings." It can be seen as a metaphor for having the ability to soar high and reach one's potential.
  • Gatvāya: This term means "having gone" or "having reached." It signifies the achievement of goals and the journey towards success.
  • Somaṁ: Soma is often associated with a divine nectar or elixir that grants immortality and wisdom. It symbolizes the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment.
  • Vajriṇa: This word translates to "the one who wields the thunderbolt" or "powerful." It represents strength, authority, and the ability to overcome obstacles with force.
  • Ābharat: This means "to bring" or "to carry." It signifies the act of bringing forth strength, wisdom, and power into one's life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of mental agility, strength, and the journey of overcoming obstacles to reach higher aspirations. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal development, career advancement, and overcoming challenges in relationships. For instance, a student preparing for exams can embody the qualities of "manojavā" by being quick-witted in problem-solving, while "atarat puram" reflects their journey through the challenges of academic life. Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder to harness our inner strength and wisdom to navigate life's complexities and strive for our highest potential.
