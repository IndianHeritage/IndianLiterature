मन्त्र: अश्वेव चित्रारुशी माता गावाम ऋतावरी | सखाभूद अश्विनोर उषाḥ ||
aśveva citrāruṣī mātā gavām ṛtāvarī | sakhābhūd aśvinor uṣāḥ ||
  • Mantra: "aśveva citrāruṣī mātā gavām ṛtāvarī | sakhābhūd aśvinor uṣāḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • aśveva: like the horses
    • citrāruṣī: bright and radiant
    • mātā: mother
    • gavām: of the cows
    • ṛtāvarī: the one who brings truth or righteousness
    • sakhābhūd: became a friend
    • aśvinor: of the Ashvins (the twin deities)
    • uṣāḥ: dawn
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the qualities of the dawn, personified as a mother who is bright and radiant like horses.
    • The mention of "mātā gavām ṛtāvarī" signifies the nurturing aspect of nature, particularly in relation to cows, which are revered in Vedic culture for their role in agriculture and sustenance.
    • The phrase "sakhābhūd aśvinor uṣāḥ" suggests a friendship or companionship that the dawn brings, akin to the benevolence of the Ashvins, who are known for their healing and protective qualities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the arrival of dawn is crucial for farmers as it marks the beginning of a new day for work, symbolizing hope and renewal.
    • The nurturing aspect of the mantra can be seen in the way communities care for livestock, ensuring their well-being, which in turn supports human life.
    • The concept of friendship and companionship can be reflected in human relationships, where the dawn symbolizes new beginnings and the support we offer each other.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of nature, nurturing, and companionship in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to appreciate the cycles of life and the support systems we have, both in nature and in our relationships.
    • In a broader sense, it encourages us to embrace new beginnings and the positive energy that comes with each dawn, fostering a sense of community and care for one another.
