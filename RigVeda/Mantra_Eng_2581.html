मन्त्र: उता नो ब्रह्मन् अविष उक्तेषु देवहूतमः |शं नः शोचाऽ मरुद्वृधोऽग्ने सहस्रसातमः ||
uta no brahmann aviṣa uktheṣu devahūtamaḥ | śaṁ naḥ śocā marudvṛdho 'gne sahasrasātamaḥ ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "May the supreme divine, the best of the gods, protect us with his words; may the powerful Maruts, the mighty ones, grant us peace and prosperity."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • uta: May (a word of invocation or prayer)
    • no: us (referring to the collective community or individuals)
    • brahmann: supreme divine or the ultimate reality
    • aviṣa: protect or safeguard
    • uktheṣu: with words or through speech
    • devahūtamaḥ: the best of the gods or the supreme deity
    • śaṁ: peace or well-being
    • naḥ: our (indicating possession or belonging)
    • śocā: may it shine or may it be auspicious
    • marudvṛdho: the powerful Maruts, the storm deities
    • 'gne: O Agni (the fire god)
    • sahasrasātamaḥ: the one with a thousand forms or manifestations
  • Contextual Significance:
    • This mantra invokes the protection and blessings of the divine, emphasizing the importance of words and communication in spiritual practice.
    • The Maruts, representing the forces of nature, are called upon for their strength and power, highlighting the connection between humanity and the natural world.
    • Agni, the fire god, symbolizes transformation and purification, suggesting that through divine intervention, one can achieve peace and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In daily life, this mantra can be recited during rituals or prayers to seek guidance and protection from higher powers, especially during challenging times.
    • It can be used in community gatherings to foster unity and collective well-being, reminding individuals of their interconnectedness.
    • In moments of conflict or uncertainty, invoking this mantra can serve as a reminder to focus on peace and constructive communication.
  • Conclusion:
    • The mantra emphasizes the significance of divine protection, the power of words, and the importance of harmony with nature.
    • It serves as a reminder that seeking blessings from the divine can lead to peace and prosperity in both personal and communal aspects of life.
    • By incorporating this mantra into daily practices, individuals can cultivate a sense of spiritual awareness and connection to the greater universe.
