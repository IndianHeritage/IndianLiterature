प्रवतवतीयम् पृथिवी मरुद्भ्यः प्रवतवती द्यौर भवति प्रयद्भ्यः |प्रवतवतिः पथ्या अन्तरिक्ष्याः प्रवत्वन्तः पर्वताः जीरदानवः ||
pravatvatīyam pṛthivī marudbhyaḥ pravatvatī dyaur bhavati prayadbhyaḥ | pravatvatīḥ pathyā antarikṣyāḥ pravatvantaḥ parvatā jīradānavaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "pravatvatīyam pṛthivī marudbhyaḥ pravatvatī dyaur bhavati prayadbhyaḥ | pravatvatīḥ pathyā antarikṣyāḥ pravatvantaḥ parvatā jīradānavaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pravatvatīyam: That which is moving or flowing.
    • pṛthivī: The Earth.
    • marudbhyaḥ: From the winds or the forces of nature.
    • dyaur: The sky or heaven.
    • bhavati: Becomes or manifests.
    • prayadbhyaḥ: From the energies or powers.
    • pathyā: The path or way.
    • antarikṣyāḥ: The space or atmosphere.
    • pravatvantaḥ: Those that are flowing or moving.
    • parvatā: The mountains.
    • jīradānavaḥ: The life-giving or nourishing.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the interconnectedness of the Earth, sky, and the forces of nature.
    • The term "pravatvatīyam" suggests a dynamic quality, indicating that all elements of nature are in constant motion and interaction.
    • The Earth ("pṛthivī") is influenced by the winds ("marudbhyaḥ"), which can be seen in real life through weather patterns and climate changes.
    • The sky ("dyaur") becomes a manifestation of these forces, showing how the atmosphere is shaped by earthly conditions.
    • The mention of "pathyā" and "antarikṣyāḥ" emphasizes the importance of the paths and spaces that connect these elements, akin to how rivers flow through landscapes, creating routes for life.
    • The "parvatā" or mountains symbolize stability and permanence, while "jīradānavaḥ" highlights their role in providing sustenance and life, as mountains often house ecosystems that support diverse life forms.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The relationship between the Earth and the atmosphere can be observed in agriculture, where farmers depend on weather patterns to determine planting and harvesting times.
    • Natural disasters, such as floods or droughts, illustrate the impact of the dynamic forces of nature on human life and the environment.
    • Mountain ranges, like the Himalayas, not only provide a physical barrier but also influence climate and water sources for millions of people.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the harmony and interdependence of natural elements, reminding us of our connection to the environment.
    • It teaches us to respect and understand the forces of nature, as they play a crucial role in sustaining life.
    • In real life, this understanding can lead to better environmental practices, conservation efforts, and a deeper appreciation for the natural world.
