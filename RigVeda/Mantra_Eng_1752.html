न वि जानामि यद इवेदम अस्मि निण्यः संनद्धो मनसा चरामि |यदा मागन प्रथमजा ऋतस्याद इद वाचो अष्णुवे भागम अस्याः ||
na vi jānāmi yad ivedam asmi niṇyaḥ saṁnaddho manasā carāmi | yadā māgan prathamajā ṛtasyād id vāco aśnuve bhāgam asyāḥ ||
  • Translation: "I do not know what this is; I am bound by my mind and I move. When I attain the first part of truth, then I will grasp a portion of this."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • na vi jānāmi: "I do not know" - This reflects a state of ignorance or unawareness.
    • yad ivedam: "what this is" - Refers to the nature of existence or reality.
    • asmi niṇyaḥ: "I am bound" - Indicates a sense of being tied or restricted, possibly by thoughts or perceptions.
    • saṁnaddho manasā: "bound by my mind" - Suggests that the mind is a limiting factor in understanding the true nature of reality.
    • carāmi: "I move" - Implies action or the journey of life.
    • yadā māgan: "when I attain" - Refers to the moment of realization or enlightenment.
    • prathamajā ṛtasyād: "the first part of truth" - Indicates the initial understanding or insight into reality.
    • id vāco: "then I will grasp" - Suggests the act of understanding or comprehending.
    • aśnuve bhāgam asyāḥ: "a portion of this" - Refers to the idea that one can only grasp a part of the greater truth.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In the journey of education, students often feel overwhelmed by the vastness of knowledge. Initially, they may say, "I do not know what this is," but as they study and engage with the material, they begin to understand and grasp the concepts, reflecting the mantra's essence of moving from ignorance to knowledge.
    • In personal development, individuals may feel "bound by their mind" due to limiting beliefs. Through practices like mindfulness and meditation, they can achieve moments of clarity, akin to "attaining the first part of truth," leading to personal growth and a better understanding of themselves.
  • Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the journey from ignorance to knowledge, highlighting the limitations imposed by our own minds. It signifies the importance of seeking truth and understanding, suggesting that while we may not grasp the entirety of existence, each step towards enlightenment is valuable. In real life, this can be applied in various contexts, such as education, personal growth, and spiritual exploration, encouraging individuals to embrace the journey of learning and self-discovery.
