आ नो नियुद्भिः शतिनीभिर अध्वरं सहस्रिणीभिर उप याहि यज्ञम् |वायो अस्मिन सवने मदयस्व यूम पात स्वस्तिभिः सदा नः ||
ā no niyudbhiḥ śatinībhir adhvaraṁ sahasriṇībhir upa yāhi yajñam | vāyo asmin savane mādayasva yūyam pāta svastibhiḥ sadā naḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā no: "May we" or "Let us"
    • niyudbhiḥ: "With the help of the strong"
    • śatinībhir: "With the hundredfold"
    • adhvaraṁ: "The sacred ritual" or "the sacrifice"
    • sahasriṇībhir: "With the thousandfold"
    • upa yāhi: "Come near" or "approach"
    • yajñam: "The sacrifice" or "the offering"
    • vāyo: "O Wind" or "O Vayu"
    • asmin: "In this" or "in this place"
    • savane: "In the offering" or "in the sacrifice"
    • mādayasva: "Be pleased" or "delight us"
    • yūyam: "You" (referring to the deities)
    • pāta: "Protect" or "guard"
    • svastibhiḥ: "With auspiciousness" or "with well-being"
    • sadā: "Always" or "forever"
    • naḥ: "Us" or "to us"
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer for divine assistance and protection during rituals and sacrifices.
    • The invocation of "Vayu," the wind god, signifies the importance of the natural elements in spiritual practices.
    • The phrase "may we approach the sacrifice" emphasizes the collective effort and intention of the community in spiritual endeavors.
    • The call for "auspiciousness" reflects a desire for well-being and harmony in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern ceremonies, such as weddings or community festivals, participants often seek blessings from nature and the divine, similar to the invocation of Vayu.
    • In team projects or community service, the collective effort and intention to achieve a common goal resonate with the mantra's emphasis on unity and purpose.
    • Practices like meditation and yoga often incorporate elements of nature, reflecting the connection to the divine and the importance of auspiciousness in personal well-being.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of community, nature, and divine blessings in achieving success and well-being.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of collective intention in spiritual and everyday practices.
    • In real life, the principles of this mantra can be applied in various contexts, from personal growth to community initiatives, emphasizing the need for harmony, protection, and auspiciousness in our endeavors.
