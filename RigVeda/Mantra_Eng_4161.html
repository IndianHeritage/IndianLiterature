प्र वः स्पष् अक्रन् सुविताय दावने 'र्चा दिवे प्र पृथिव्या ऋतम् भरे | उक्षन्ते अश्वान तरुषन्त आ रजो 'नु स्वं भानुं श्रथयन्ते अर्णवैः ||
pra vaḥ spaḻ akran suvitāya dāvane 'rcā dive pra pṛthivyā ṛtam bhare | ukṣante aśvān taruṣanta ā rajo 'nu svam bhānuṁ śrathayante arṇavaiḥ ||
  • pra vaḥ spaḻ akran suvitāya dāvane 'rcā dive pra pṛthivyā ṛtam bhare
    • pra vaḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "let us go forth" or "let us move forward." It signifies a call to action, encouraging individuals to take initiative in their lives.
    • spaḻ akran: This can be understood as "to shine brightly" or "to illuminate." It suggests the importance of clarity and enlightenment in our endeavors.
    • suvitāya: Meaning "for the well-being" or "for the good," this word emphasizes the intention behind actions, highlighting the need for positive outcomes.
    • dāvane 'rcā: This refers to "the hymns of praise" or "the offerings." It indicates the significance of expressing gratitude and reverence in our lives.
    • dive pra pṛthivyā: Translated as "in the sky and on the earth," this phrase reflects the interconnectedness of all realms of existence.
    • ṛtam bhare: This means "let us fill with truth" or "let us uphold righteousness." It underscores the importance of truth and moral integrity in our actions.
  • ukṣante aśvān taruṣanta ā rajo 'nu svam bhānuṁ śrathayante arṇavaiḥ
    • ukṣante: This word means "they are being stirred" or "they are being awakened." It suggests a call to awaken one's potential and energy.
    • aśvān: Referring to "horses," this symbolizes strength, speed, and vitality. Horses are often associated with progress and movement in life.
    • taruṣanta: This can be interpreted as "they who are striving" or "those who are working hard." It emphasizes the value of effort and perseverance.
    • ā rajo 'nu: Meaning "towards the light" or "towards the brightness," this phrase encourages individuals to seek enlightenment and positivity.
    • svam bhānuṁ: This translates to "one's own light" or "one's own radiance," highlighting the importance of self-awareness and personal growth.
    • śrathayante arṇavaiḥ: This can be understood as "they who are guided by the waves" or "they who are led by the currents." It signifies the need to adapt and flow with life's changes.

Conclusion:

The mantra emphasizes the importance of taking initiative, striving for well-being, and upholding truth in our lives. It encourages individuals to awaken their potential, work hard, and seek enlightenment while remaining adaptable to life's challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal development, leadership, and community service. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute positively to their surroundings and foster a sense of unity and purpose.
