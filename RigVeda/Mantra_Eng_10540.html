प्र नूनं जातवेदसम अश्वं हिनोत वाजिनम् |इदं नो बर्हिर आसदे ||
pra nūnaṁ jātavedasam aśvaṁ hinota vājinam | idaṁ no barhir āsade ||
  • pra nūnaṁ: This phrase can be translated as "indeed, surely" or "truly." It emphasizes certainty and conviction in the following statements.
  • jātavedasam: This term refers to "the one who knows all births" or "the divine fire." In the context of the Rigveda, it often symbolizes Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
  • aśvaṁ: This word means "horse." In Vedic culture, the horse is a symbol of strength, speed, and vitality, often associated with power and nobility.
  • hinota: This verb means "to drive away" or "to remove." It indicates an action of dispelling negativity or obstacles.
  • vājinam: This term translates to "the one who possesses strength" or "the powerful." It is often used to describe a warrior or a strong individual.
  • idaṁ: This word means "this" or "here." It indicates something present or immediate.
  • no: This is a possessive pronoun meaning "our." It signifies a sense of belonging or ownership.
  • barhir: This term refers to "the sacred grass" or "the altar." In Vedic rituals, grass is often used as a symbol of purity and sanctity.
  • āsade: This verb means "to sit" or "to dwell." It indicates a state of being or presence.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation to Agni, asking for the removal of obstacles and the blessing of strength and vitality. It reflects a deep reverence for the divine and the natural elements that sustain life.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Overcoming Challenges: Just as the mantra calls for the removal of obstacles, individuals can use this invocation during difficult times, seeking strength and clarity to overcome personal challenges.
  • Spiritual Growth: The reference to Agni symbolizes the inner fire of knowledge and wisdom. Practicing meditation or self-reflection can help individuals connect with their inner strength and pursue spiritual growth.
  • Community and Leadership: The horse symbolizes power and nobility. Leaders can embody the qualities of strength and decisiveness, driving their communities toward progress and unity.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of divine guidance, strength, and the removal of obstacles in our lives. It serves as a reminder to seek inner strength and clarity, whether in personal struggles, spiritual journeys, or leadership roles. By invoking these qualities, individuals can navigate life's challenges with confidence and purpose.
