प्र दानुदो दिव्यो दानुपिन्व ऋतम ऋताया पवते सुमेधा: |धर्मा भूवद वृजन्यस्य राजा प्र रश्मिभिर दशभिर भारी भूम ||
pra dānudo divyo dānupinva ṛtam ṛtāya pavate sumedhāḥ | dharmā bhuvad vṛjanyasya rājā pra raśmibhir daśabhir bhāri bhūma ||
  • pra dānudo divyo dānupinva: This phrase can be interpreted as "the divine giver of gifts." It emphasizes the importance of generosity and the divine nature of giving. In real life, this can be seen in acts of charity, where individuals or organizations provide support to those in need, reflecting the divine quality of selflessness.
  • ṛtam ṛtāya pavate sumedhāḥ: Here, "ṛtam" refers to the cosmic order or truth, while "sumedhāḥ" signifies those with good intellect or wisdom. This part of the mantra suggests that those who align with truth and righteousness will be blessed with wisdom. For example, in a workplace, ethical practices and integrity can lead to a harmonious environment and successful outcomes.
  • dharmā bhuvad vṛjanyasya rājā: "Dharmā" refers to righteousness or duty, and "rājā" means king or ruler. This line indicates that a ruler who upholds dharma will prosper. In modern governance, leaders who prioritize justice and ethical governance tend to gain the trust and loyalty of their citizens, leading to a stable society.
  • pra raśmibhir daśabhir bhāri bhūma: This phrase can be interpreted as "with ten rays of light, the heavy earth is illuminated." The "ten rays" symbolize various virtues or qualities that bring enlightenment. In everyday life, this can relate to the idea that diverse talents and perspectives contribute to a well-rounded community, enhancing collective understanding and progress.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of generosity, truth, righteousness, and the illumination of wisdom. Its significance lies in the reminder that by embodying these virtues, individuals and leaders can create a positive impact in their communities. In real-life scenarios, whether through charitable actions, ethical leadership, or collaborative efforts, the principles outlined in this mantra encourage a harmonious and prosperous society. By striving to live by these ideals, we can contribute to a more just and enlightened world.
