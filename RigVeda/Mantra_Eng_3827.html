एदं मरुतो अश्विनाः मित्रः सिदन्तु वरुणः |देवाशः सर्वयाः विश्वा ||
edam maruto aśvinā mitraḥ sīdantu varuṇaḥ | devāsaḥ sarvayā viśā ||
  • edam: This word translates to "this" or "here." It signifies the presence of something important in the immediate context.
  • maruto: Refers to the "Maruts," the storm deities in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength, power, and the forces of nature.
  • aśvinā: This refers to the "Aśvins," the twin gods associated with health, medicine, and the dawn, representing duality and balance.
  • mitraḥ: Translates to "friend" or "ally," indicating the importance of companionship and support in life.
  • sīdantu: Means "may they sit" or "may they dwell," suggesting a desire for these divine forces to be present and active.
  • varuṇaḥ: Refers to "Varuna," the god of cosmic order and water, symbolizing the moral and ethical aspects of life.
  • devāsaḥ: This means "the gods," indicating the divine presence and their role in human affairs.
  • sarvayā: Translates to "all" or "every," emphasizing inclusivity and universality.
  • viśā: Refers to "communities" or "groups," highlighting the importance of social structures and collective existence.

The mantra can be interpreted as a prayer or invocation for the presence and blessings of various divine forces in our lives. It calls upon the Maruts, Aśvins, Mitra, and Varuna to dwell among us, signifying a desire for strength, health, friendship, and moral order.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Community Support: Just as the mantra invokes divine presence for communities, it reminds us of the importance of support systems in our lives. For example, during challenging times, having friends and family around can provide the strength needed to overcome difficulties.
  • Health and Well-being: The Aśvins symbolize health and medicine. In modern life, this can relate to the importance of maintaining physical and mental health through regular check-ups, healthy living, and seeking help when needed.
  • Environmental Awareness: The Maruts represent the forces of nature. This can inspire individuals to respect and protect the environment, recognizing the power of nature in our lives.
  • Moral Integrity: Varuna symbolizes cosmic order and ethics. This serves as a reminder to uphold moral values in our actions and decisions, fostering a just and harmonious society.

In conclusion, this mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and support in various aspects of life. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of individuals within communities and the importance of divine forces in maintaining balance, health, and moral integrity. By invoking these deities, we are reminded to cultivate these qualities in our daily lives, fostering a harmonious existence.
