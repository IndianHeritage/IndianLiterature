को अद्धा वेद क इह प्र वोचद देवां अच् छा पथ्या क सम एति |ददृश्र एषां अवमा सदांसी परेषु या गुह्येषु व्रतेषु ||
ko addhā veda ka iha pra vocad devām̐ acchā pathyā3 kā sam eti | dadṛśra eṣām avamā sadāṁsi pareṣu yā guhyeṣu vrateṣu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ko: Who
    • addhā: Indeed
    • veda: Knows
    • ka: Who
    • iha: Here
    • pra vocad: Can speak
    • devām̐: To the gods
    • acchā: Good
    • pathyā: Path
    • kā: Which
    • sam eti: Leads to
    • dadṛśra: They have seen
    • eṣām: Of them
    • avamā: Lower
    • sadāṁsi: Eternal
    • pareṣu: In others
    • yā: Who
    • guhyeṣu: In the hidden
    • vrateṣu: In the vows
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra poses a profound question about knowledge and understanding.
    • It asks who truly knows the divine and can articulate the path that leads to the gods.
    • The reference to "hidden" and "vows" suggests that true knowledge is often concealed and requires commitment to uncover.
    • In real life, this can be likened to the pursuit of wisdom in various fields, such as science, philosophy, or spirituality, where deeper truths are often hidden beneath surface-level understanding.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, students often seek knowledge that is not immediately apparent, requiring dedication and effort to understand complex subjects.
    • In personal development, individuals may engage in practices like meditation or self-reflection to uncover deeper truths about themselves and their purpose.
    • In relationships, understanding the motivations and feelings of others often requires patience and a willingness to look beyond the obvious.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge and understanding the divine, which is applicable in various aspects of life.
    • It encourages individuals to pursue deeper truths, whether in their personal lives, professional endeavors, or spiritual journeys.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder that the path to wisdom often involves dedication, introspection, and a willingness to explore the hidden aspects of existence.
