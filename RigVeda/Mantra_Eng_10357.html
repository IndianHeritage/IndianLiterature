न वा अरण्यानीर हन्त्य अन्यश्चेन नाभिगच्छति |स्वादोः फलस्य जग्ध्वाय यथाकामं निपद्यते ||
na vā araṇyānir hanty anyaś cen nābhigacchati | svādoḥ phalasya jagdhvāya yathākāmaṁ ni padyate ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • na: not
    • vā: or
    • araṇyā: of the forest
    • anir: without
    • hanty: kills
    • anyaś: another
    • cen: if
    • nābhigacchati: does not approach
    • svādoḥ: of the taste
    • phalasya: of the fruit
    • jagdhvā: having eaten
    • yathākāmaṁ: as desired
    • ni padyate: is satisfied
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra suggests that if one does not approach or engage with the essence of life (symbolized by the forest), they cannot experience the richness and satisfaction that comes from it.
    • The phrase "svādoḥ phalasya jagdhvāya" indicates that true satisfaction comes from experiencing the fruits of one's labor or the rewards of one's actions.
    • It emphasizes the importance of actively participating in life and seeking out experiences that bring joy and fulfillment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional context, an employee who merely goes through the motions of their job without engaging with their work or seeking to improve will find little satisfaction. In contrast, someone who actively seeks challenges and opportunities for growth will likely feel more fulfilled.
    • In personal relationships, if one does not invest time and effort into nurturing connections with others, they may find themselves feeling isolated and unfulfilled. Engaging deeply with friends and family can lead to richer, more satisfying relationships.
    • In the pursuit of knowledge, a student who only studies to pass exams without a genuine interest in learning will miss out on the joy and satisfaction that comes from understanding and mastering a subject.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its reminder that engagement and active participation in life are essential for true satisfaction and fulfillment.
    • It encourages individuals to seek out experiences that resonate with them, to approach life with curiosity, and to savor the fruits of their efforts.
    • In various aspects of life—be it work, relationships, or personal growth—this mantra serves as a guiding principle to ensure that one does not merely exist but thrives by embracing the richness of experiences available to them.
