पश्चेदम अन्यद अभवत यजत्रम् अमर्त्यस्य भुवनस्य भूना |सुपर्णो अङ्ग सवितुर गरुत्मान पूर्वो जातः स उ अस्यानु धर्म ||
paścedam anyad abhavad yajatram amartyasya bhuvanasya bhūnā | suparṇo aṅga savitur garutmān pūrvo jātaḥ sa u asyānu dharma ||
  • paścedam anyad abhavad: This phrase can be translated to "This is different from that." It emphasizes the distinction between various forms of existence and the importance of recognizing diversity in the universe.
  • yajatram amartyasya bhuvanasya bhūnā: Here, "yajatram" refers to the act of worship or sacrifice, while "amartyasya" signifies immortality. This part suggests that through worship, one can connect with the eternal essence of the universe.
  • suparṇo aṅga: "Suparṇo" means "well-winged" or "having good wings," often symbolizing the ability to rise above challenges. "Aṅga" refers to a part or aspect, indicating that this quality is inherent in all beings.
  • savituh garutmān: "Savituh" refers to the Sun or the divine source of light, while "garutmān" means "the one who is powerful." This highlights the significance of the Sun as a source of energy and life.
  • pūrvo jātaḥ sa u asyānu dharma: This translates to "the one who was born first, he is the one who upholds the law." It signifies the primordial nature of the divine and its role in maintaining cosmic order.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of existence, the importance of worship, and the recognition of the divine in the natural world. It teaches us to appreciate the diversity of life and the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • Real-life Application: In our daily lives, we can apply the teachings of this mantra by recognizing the uniqueness of individuals and cultures. Engaging in acts of kindness and respect towards others can be seen as a form of worship, fostering a sense of community and harmony.
  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives and the importance of maintaining balance and order through our actions. By embracing diversity and practicing reverence for life, we contribute to the greater cosmic harmony.
