त्वं सूरो हरितो रामयो नृन् भरच चक्रम् एतशो नायम् इन्द्र |प्रास्य पारं नवतिं नाव्याणाम् अपि कर्तम् अवर्तयो यज्यून् ||
tvaṁ sūro harito rāmayo nṝn bharac cakram etaśo nāyam indra | prāsya pāraṁ navatiṁ nāvyānām api kartam avartayo 'yajyūn ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or force.
  • sūro - Meaning "hero" or "brave one," this term signifies strength and valor.
  • harito - This can be interpreted as "green" or "prosperous," often associated with growth and vitality.
  • rāmayo - This word relates to "delight" or "joy," suggesting a connection to happiness and fulfillment.
  • nṝn - Meaning "men" or "humans," it emphasizes the connection between the divine and humanity.
  • bharac - This translates to "bear" or "carry," indicating support or sustenance.
  • cakram - Referring to a "wheel," it symbolizes the cycle of life and the universe.
  • etaśo - Meaning "from here," it suggests a point of origin or a starting place.
  • nāyam - This translates to "not," indicating negation or absence.
  • indra - Referring to the king of gods, Indra represents power, authority, and protection.
  • prāsya - Meaning "of the offering," it relates to rituals and sacrifices.
  • pāraṁ - This translates to "beyond," indicating transcendence or something that surpasses ordinary limits.
  • navatiṁ - Meaning "ninety," it can symbolize completeness or a large number.
  • nāvyānām - Referring to "ships," it symbolizes journeys and exploration.
  • api - Meaning "even," it suggests inclusivity or addition.
  • kartam - This translates to "to do" or "to act," emphasizing action and agency.
  • avartayo - Meaning "to turn" or "to revolve," it relates to cycles and the nature of existence.
  • 'yajyūn - Referring to "sacrifices" or "offerings," it highlights the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.

The mantra speaks to the relationship between the divine and humanity, emphasizing the qualities of strength, prosperity, and joy. It acknowledges the support provided by divine forces in the journey of life, symbolized by the wheel, which represents the cyclical nature of existence. The invocation of Indra suggests a plea for protection and guidance in our endeavors.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Personal Growth: Just as the mantra invokes strength and prosperity, individuals can draw inspiration from it to pursue their goals with courage and resilience.
  • Community Support: The emphasis on bearing burdens and supporting one another can be reflected in community initiatives where people come together to help those in need.
  • Rituals and Offerings: The mention of sacrifices highlights the importance of gratitude and acknowledgment of the forces that support us, which can be practiced through personal rituals or community celebrations.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, the importance of strength and support, and the cyclical nature of existence. It encourages individuals to act with purpose and to seek divine guidance in their journeys, fostering a sense of community and shared responsibility.
