तनूनापात पवमानः श्रिङ्गे शिशानो अर्षति |अन्तरिक्षेण रारजत ||
tanūnapāt pavamānaḥ śṛṅge śiśāno arṣati | antarikṣeṇa rārajat ||
  • tanūnapāt: This term can be broken down into two parts: "tanū" meaning body or form, and "napāt" meaning descendant or offspring. Together, it refers to the essence or the vital energy that is present in all living beings.
  • pavamānaḥ: This word translates to "the purifying one" or "the one that moves swiftly." It signifies the divine energy or force that cleanses and elevates the spirit.
  • śṛṅge: This term means "horn" or "peak." In a metaphorical sense, it can represent strength, power, or the heights of spiritual attainment.
  • śiśāno: This word means "the one who is born" or "the one who is created." It signifies the manifestation of life and the continuous cycle of birth and rebirth.
  • arṣati: This term translates to "to rise" or "to ascend." It indicates the upward movement towards higher consciousness or spiritual awakening.
  • antarikṣeṇa: This word means "in the atmosphere" or "in the space between." It signifies the connection between the earthly realm and the divine, highlighting the importance of the spiritual dimension in our lives.
  • rārajat: This term translates to "shone" or "radiated." It indicates the manifestation of divine light or energy that illuminates the path of life.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to recognize and honor the vital energy that exists within all forms of life. It emphasizes the importance of purification and spiritual elevation, encouraging individuals to rise to their highest potential. The imagery of horns and peaks suggests strength and the pursuit of higher consciousness, while the reference to the atmosphere highlights the interconnectedness of all beings.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios. For instance, in personal development, individuals can reflect on their inner energy and strive for purification through practices like meditation, yoga, or mindfulness. In a broader context, it can inspire communities to work together towards a common goal, recognizing the divine energy that connects them all.


In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the vital energy that flows through all living beings and the importance of striving for spiritual elevation. It encourages individuals to purify their thoughts and actions, rise to their highest potential, and recognize the interconnectedness of life. By embodying these principles, one can lead a more fulfilling and meaningful life, contributing positively to the world around them.
