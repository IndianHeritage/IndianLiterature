महश्चित्त्वमिन्द्र यत एतान महश्चिदसि त्यजसो वरूताः |स नो वेधो मरुतां चिकीत्वान सुम्ना वनुष्व तव हि प्रेष्ठा ||
mahaś cit tvam indra yata etān mahaś cid asi tyajaso varūtā | sa no vedho marutāṁ cikitvān sumnā vanuṣva tava hi preṣṭhā ||
  • mahaś cit tvam indra yata etān mahaś cid asi tyajaso varūtā
    • mahaś - Great or vast; signifies the immense power and presence of the divine.
    • cit - Consciousness or awareness; refers to the ultimate reality or truth.
    • tvam - You; addressing the divine, in this case, Indra, the king of gods.
    • indra - The deity representing strength, power, and leadership.
    • yata - Where; indicates a location or state of being.
    • etān - These; refers to the various aspects or manifestations of existence.
    • asi - You are; affirming the identity of the divine.
    • tyajaso - Of those who abandon; refers to those who renounce worldly attachments.
    • varūtā - The protective forces or divine guardians; signifies the support from the divine in overcoming challenges.
  • sa no vedho marutāṁ cikitvān sumnā vanuṣva tava hi preṣṭhā
    • sa - He; referring to the divine presence.
    • no - Us; indicating a collective appeal for guidance and support.
    • vedho - Knowledge or wisdom; signifies the importance of understanding and insight.
    • marutāṁ - Of the Maruts; the storm deities representing strength and vitality.
    • cikitvān - Having understood; emphasizes the need for awareness and comprehension.
    • sumnā - With auspiciousness; refers to the positive and beneficial aspects of life.
    • vanuṣva - May you dwell; a request for the divine to reside among us.
    • tava - Your; indicating possession or belonging to the divine.
    • hi - Indeed; reinforcing the statement.
    • preṣṭhā - Most beloved; referring to the cherished nature of the divine in our lives.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the connection between the divine and the individual, highlighting the importance of consciousness, strength, and the protective forces that guide us through life. It serves as a reminder that by renouncing worldly attachments and seeking divine wisdom, we can navigate challenges with the support of higher powers. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in moments of difficulty or decision-making, where invoking the divine presence can provide clarity and strength. It encourages individuals to seek knowledge and understanding, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose in the pursuit of a meaningful life.
