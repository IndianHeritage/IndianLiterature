तं अग्निम् अस्ते वसवो न ऋण्वन् सुप्रतिचक्षम् अवसे कुतश्चित् |दक्षाय्यो यो दाम् आस नित्यः ||
tam agnim aste vasavo ny ṛṇvan supraticakṣam avase kutaś cit | dakṣāyyo yo dama āsa nityaḥ ||
  • tam agnim: This phrase translates to "that fire" or "that divine light." In the context of the Rigveda, fire symbolizes knowledge, purity, and the divine presence. It represents the transformative power of enlightenment.
  • aste vasavo: "Aste" means "exists" and "vasavo" refers to the "Vasu" deities, who are associated with the elements and prosperity. This part emphasizes the existence of divine forces that govern the material world.
  • ny ṛṇvan: This term can be interpreted as "one who is free from debts" or "one who is liberated." It signifies the importance of living a life of integrity and fulfilling one's responsibilities.
  • supraticakṣam: This means "well-seeing" or "having good vision." It suggests the ability to perceive truth and reality clearly, which is essential for making wise decisions in life.
  • avase kutaś cit: This phrase translates to "from where does it come?" It reflects a quest for understanding the source of knowledge and existence, encouraging individuals to seek deeper truths.
  • dakṣāyyo: This term refers to "skillful" or "capable." It highlights the importance of competence and expertise in one's endeavors, whether in personal or professional life.
  • yo dama āsa: "Yo" means "who," "dama" refers to "self-control," and "āsa" means "to possess." This part emphasizes the value of self-discipline and control over one's desires and actions.
  • nityaḥ: This translates to "eternal" or "everlasting." It signifies the timeless nature of truth and the enduring principles that guide human behavior.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates profound wisdom about the nature of existence, the pursuit of knowledge, and the importance of integrity and self-discipline. In real life, it serves as a guiding principle for individuals seeking to lead a meaningful and responsible life. By embodying the qualities of clarity, skillfulness, and self-control, one can navigate the complexities of life with wisdom and purpose. This mantra is applicable in various scenarios, such as personal development, leadership, and ethical decision-making, reminding us to seek truth and act with integrity in all our endeavors.
