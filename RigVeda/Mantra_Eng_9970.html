प्रīणीताश्वान हितं जयाथ स्वस्तिवाहं रथम इति कृणुध्वम् |द्रोणाहावम अवतं अश्मचक्रं अंशत्रकोशं सिञ्चतां नृपाणम् ||
prīṇītāśvān hitaṁ jayātha svastivāhaṁ ratham it kṛṇudhvam | droṇāhāvam avatam aśmacakram aṁsatrakośaṁ siñcatā nṛpāṇam ||
  • prīṇītāśvān: This term can be broken down into "prīṇī" meaning to please or satisfy, and "aśvān" referring to horses. In ancient times, horses were symbols of strength and power, often associated with warriors and kings. Thus, this phrase can be interpreted as a call to please the horses, which in a broader sense signifies the importance of nurturing strength and vitality in one's life.
  • hitaṁ: This word means "well-being" or "goodness." It emphasizes the importance of ensuring that all actions lead to positive outcomes and the welfare of individuals and society as a whole.
  • jayātha: This term translates to "may you conquer" or "may you be victorious." It is a call for success and triumph, encouraging individuals to strive for victory in their endeavors.
  • svastivāhaṁ: "Svastivāhaṁ" can be understood as "bringing auspiciousness" or "carrying well-being." It signifies the importance of fostering a positive and prosperous environment.
  • ratham: This word means "chariot." In the context of the mantra, it symbolizes the journey of life and the means by which one navigates through challenges and opportunities.
  • it kṛṇudhvam: This phrase translates to "make it happen" or "bring it into being." It is an encouragement to take action and manifest one's desires and goals.
  • dronāhāvam: This term refers to "the great vessel" or "the container." It symbolizes the capacity to hold and nurture one's aspirations and achievements.
  • avatam: This word means "to protect" or "to guard." It emphasizes the need for safeguarding one's goals and the well-being of oneself and others.
  • aśmacakram: This term can be interpreted as "the wheel of stone," symbolizing stability and strength. It represents the foundation upon which one builds their life and endeavors.
  • aṁsatrakośaṁ: This phrase refers to "the treasure of strength." It signifies the importance of harnessing inner strength and resources to achieve one's goals.
  • siñcatā: This word means "to pour" or "to sprinkle." It symbolizes the act of nurturing and fostering growth, whether in personal endeavors or in the community.
  • nṛpāṇam: This term translates to "of the kings" or "of the rulers." It highlights the responsibility of leaders to ensure the well-being and prosperity of their subjects.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of nurturing strength, well-being, and auspiciousness in life. It calls for action and the protection of one's goals while fostering a supportive environment for growth. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, personal development, and community building. Leaders can take inspiration from this mantra to create a positive impact on their followers, while individuals can use its teachings to pursue their aspirations with determination and care. Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, well-being, and success in our journeys through life.
