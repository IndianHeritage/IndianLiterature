त्वं अग्नि सुहवो रण्वसंदृक सुदीती सूनो सहसो दिदीहि |मा त्वे सचा तनये नित्य आ धङ् मा वीरो अस्मन् नार्यो विदासीत ||
tvam agne suhavo raṇvasaṁdṛk sudītī sūno sahaso didīhi | mā tve sacā tanaye nitya ā dhaṅ mā vīro asman naryo vi dāsīt ||
  • tvam agne: This phrase translates to "You, O Agni." Agni is the Vedic fire god, symbolizing energy, light, and purity. In real life, fire represents transformation and the energy that fuels our actions and intentions.
  • suhavo raṇvasaṁdṛk: This means "pleasant and powerful." It emphasizes the dual nature of Agni as both nurturing and fierce. In our lives, we often encounter situations that require us to balance kindness with strength, such as in leadership roles.
  • sudītī sūno: This translates to "the one who brings good fortune." It highlights the importance of seeking positive outcomes in our endeavors. For example, in business, a leader who fosters a positive environment can lead to greater success.
  • sahaso didīhi: This means "bestow strength." It is a call for empowerment, urging Agni to grant us the strength to overcome challenges. In everyday life, this can be seen in athletes who draw on their inner strength to achieve their goals.
  • mā tve sacā tanaye nitya: This translates to "may we not be deprived of your presence." It signifies the need for constant support and guidance. In relationships, this can relate to the importance of having a supportive network of friends and family.
  • ā dhaṅ mā vīro asman naryo vi dāsīt: This means "may we not be defeated by our enemies." It reflects the desire for protection and victory over adversities. In real life, this can be applied to personal struggles, where resilience and determination help us overcome obstacles.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of Agni as a source of strength, guidance, and positive energy in our lives. It teaches us the importance of balancing kindness with power, seeking good fortune, and relying on a supportive community. In real-life scenarios, these principles can be applied in various contexts, such as leadership, personal development, and overcoming challenges. By invoking the qualities of Agni, we can strive for empowerment and resilience in our daily lives.
