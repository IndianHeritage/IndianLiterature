यद इन्द्राग्नि दिवि ष्ठो यत पृथिव्यां यत पर्वतेष्व ओषधिष्व अप्सु |अतः परि वृषाणाव आ हि यातम अथ सोमस्य पिबतं सुतस्य ||
yad indrāgnī divi ṣṭho yat pṛthivyāṁ yat parvateṣv oṣadhīṣv apsu | ataḥ pari vṛṣaṇāv ā hi yātam athā somasya pibataṁ sutasya ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • indrāgnī: Indra and Agni (the deities representing rain and fire)
    • divi: in the heavens
    • ṣṭho: residing
    • yat: whatever
    • pṛthivyāṁ: on the earth
    • yat: whatever
    • parvateṣv: in the mountains
    • oṣadhīṣv: in the plants
    • apsu: in the waters
    • ataḥ: therefore
    • pari: all around
    • vṛṣaṇāv: the powerful ones (referring to the deities)
    • ā hi: indeed
    • yātam: we seek
    • athā: and then
    • somasya: of Soma (the divine nectar)
    • pibataṁ: drink
    • sutasya: of the brewed (referring to the offerings)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the blessings of Indra and Agni, the deities associated with rain and fire, respectively. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all elements of nature.
    • The mention of various elements like the heavens, earth, mountains, plants, and waters signifies the holistic approach to life and the environment.
    • The phrase "we seek" indicates a desire for divine blessings and nourishment, both physically and spiritually, through the consumption of Soma, which symbolizes the essence of life and vitality.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In agriculture, the reliance on rain (Indra) and fire (Agni) is crucial for crop growth. Farmers often pray for favorable weather conditions, reflecting the essence of this mantra.
    • In modern environmentalism, the mantra's emphasis on the interconnectedness of nature can be seen in sustainable practices that respect and preserve the earth's resources.
    • In personal well-being, the pursuit of spiritual nourishment through meditation or rituals can be likened to the consumption of Soma, promoting mental and emotional health.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of seeking harmony with nature and the divine. It teaches us to appreciate the elements that sustain life and encourages a respectful relationship with the environment.
    • In real-life scenarios, the principles derived from this mantra can guide individuals and communities towards sustainable living, spiritual growth, and a deeper understanding of the interconnectedness of all life.
    • Ultimately, it serves as a reminder of the importance of gratitude and reverence for the natural world and the divine forces that govern it.
