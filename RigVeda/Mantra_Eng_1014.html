अधि पेशांसी वपत नृतूर इवापोर्णुते वक्ष उश्रेव बरजहम् |ज्योतिर विश्वस्मै भुवनाया कृण्वती गावो न व्रजं व्य उषा आवर तमः ||
adhi peśāṁsi vapate nṛtūr ivāporṇute vakṣa usreva barjaham | jyotir viśvasmai bhuvanāya kṛṇvatī gāvo na vrajaṁ vy u1ṣā āvar tamaḥ ||
  • adhi peśāṁsi vapate: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who is the master of all beings." It signifies a higher power or divine force that governs the universe.
  • nṛtūr ivāporṇute: This translates to "like a dancer, it brings forth." This suggests the idea of grace and fluidity in creation, akin to how a dancer moves effortlessly, creating beauty and harmony.
  • vakṣa usreva barjaham: This can be understood as "the one who protects and nurtures." It emphasizes the protective aspect of the divine, akin to a mother caring for her child.
  • jyotir viśvasmai bhuvanāya kṛṇvatī: This means "the light that creates the universe." It symbolizes knowledge, enlightenment, and the creative force that brings the world into existence.
  • gāvo na vrajaṁ vy u1ṣā āvar tamaḥ: This translates to "the cows do not stray; the dawn dispels darkness." It signifies guidance and the arrival of knowledge that dispels ignorance and confusion.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of divine guidance, protection, and the creative force of the universe. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In times of uncertainty, individuals can seek the divine for guidance, much like how the dawn brings clarity after a dark night.
  • In nurturing relationships, the protective aspect of the divine can inspire individuals to care for and support one another, similar to a parent-child relationship.
  • The mantra encourages the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, reminding us that just as light dispels darkness, knowledge dispels ignorance.

Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the divine presence that nurtures and guides us through life's journey.
