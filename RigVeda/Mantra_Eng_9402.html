यतेयं पृथिवी मही दाधारेमान वनस्पतīn |एवा दाधारा ते मनो जीवातवे न मृत्युवे 'थो अरिष्टतातये ||
yatheyam pṛthivī mahī dādhāremān vanaspatīn | evā dādhāra te mano jīvātave na mṛtyave 'tho ariṣṭatātaye ||
  • yatheyam: This word translates to "as this" or "in the same way." It sets the context for the following statements, indicating a comparison or a parallel.
  • pṛthivī: Refers to the "earth" or "world." It symbolizes the physical realm and the foundation of life.
  • mahī: This means "great" or "vast," emphasizing the enormity and significance of the earth.
  • dādhāremān: Translates to "supports" or "holds." It indicates the nurturing aspect of the earth, which sustains all forms of life.
  • vanaspatīn: Refers to "plants" or "vegetation." This highlights the importance of flora in sustaining life and maintaining ecological balance.
  • evā: This means "thus" or "in this way," reinforcing the connection between the previous statements and the following thought.
  • dādhāra: Again means "supports" or "holds," reiterating the nurturing role of the earth.
  • te: This translates to "your," indicating a personal connection or relationship with the subject being addressed.
  • mano: Refers to "mind" or "thought." It signifies the mental and emotional aspects of existence.
  • jīvātave: This means "for the sake of life" or "for living beings." It emphasizes the purpose of the support provided by the earth and nature.
  • na: Translates to "not," indicating negation or absence.
  • mṛtyave: Refers to "death." This suggests a focus on life and vitality rather than mortality.
  • 'tho: This is a conjunction meaning "or," presenting an alternative.
  • ariṣṭatātaye: This translates to "for the sake of well-being" or "for the avoidance of calamity." It emphasizes the importance of maintaining harmony and balance in life.

The mantra essentially conveys the idea that just as the earth supports and nurtures all living beings through its vast resources, so too should our minds and thoughts be directed towards life, well-being, and the avoidance of negativity or calamity. It highlights the interconnectedness of nature and human consciousness, suggesting that a harmonious relationship with the earth leads to a flourishing life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various ways:

  • Environmental Awareness: It encourages individuals to respect and protect the environment, recognizing that the earth sustains us.
  • Mental Well-being: It serves as a reminder to cultivate positive thoughts and mental health, focusing on life and well-being rather than negativity.
  • Community Support: It emphasizes the importance of supporting one another in a community, akin to how the earth supports all life.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the profound relationship between the earth, life, and consciousness. It teaches us to appreciate the nurturing aspects of nature and encourages us to foster positive thoughts and actions for the well-being of ourselves and the world around us. By embodying these principles, we can lead more fulfilling and harmonious lives.
