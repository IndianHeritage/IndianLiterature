वृषा शोणो अभिकनिक्रदद गा नदयन्यति पृथिवीं उता द्यां | इन्द्रस्येव वाग्नुर आ श्रिण्वा आजौ प्रचेतयन्न अर्षति वाचमेमाम् ||
vṛṣā śoṇo abhikanikradad gā nadayann eti pṛthivīm uta dyām | indrasyeva vagnur ā śṛṇva ājau pracetayann arṣati vācam emām ||
  • vṛṣā: This word translates to "the strong" or "the powerful." It signifies strength and vigor, often associated with the divine or heroic qualities.
  • śoṇo: This term means "of the red" or "of the beautiful." It can refer to the color red, which is often symbolic of life, energy, and passion.
  • abhikanikradad: This word can be broken down into "abhi" (towards) and "kanikradad" (to roar or to make a sound). It suggests a powerful, resonant sound or call, indicating a forceful presence.
  • gā: This means "to sing" or "to call." It emphasizes the act of invoking or celebrating through sound.
  • nadayann: This translates to "flowing" or "moving." It can refer to the movement of rivers or the flow of energy.
  • eti: This means "to go" or "to move towards." It indicates a direction or purpose in movement.
  • pṛthivīm: This word means "the earth." It represents the physical world and our connection to it.
  • uta: This translates to "and" or "also," linking ideas together.
  • dyām: This means "the sky" or "heaven." It signifies the spiritual or celestial realm.
  • indrasyeva: This phrase means "like Indra," referring to the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing power and authority.
  • vagnur: This translates to "the fiery one" or "the one who burns." It can symbolize transformation, energy, and purification.
  • ā: This is a preposition meaning "towards" or "to." It indicates directionality.
  • śṛṇva: This means "hearing" or "listening." It emphasizes the importance of being receptive to sounds and messages.
  • ājau: This translates to "in the assembly" or "in the gathering." It signifies community and collective action.
  • pracetayann: This word means "inspiring" or "awakening." It suggests a call to action or awareness.
  • arṣati: This translates to "to rise" or "to ascend." It indicates growth, elevation, or improvement.
  • vācam: This means "speech" or "voice." It emphasizes communication and expression.
  • emām: This translates to "this" or "here." It indicates a specific focus or subject matter.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of strength, beauty, and the interconnectedness of the earth and sky. It emphasizes the importance of powerful communication and the collective spirit of community. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as leadership, where one must inspire and uplift others, or in environmental movements that call for the protection of our planet. The mantra serves as a reminder of the divine energy that flows through all aspects of life, urging us to listen, act, and rise together in harmony.
