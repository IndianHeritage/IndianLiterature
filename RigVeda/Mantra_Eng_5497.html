उदु ष्य देवः सविता ययाम हिरण्ययीं अमतिं याम अषिश्रेत् |नूनं भगो हव्यो मानुषेभिर् वि यो रत्ना पुरूवसुर् दधाति ||
ud u ṣya devaḥ savitā yayāma hiraṇyayīm amatiṁ yām aśiśret | nūnam bhago havyo mānuṣebhir vi yo ratnā purūvasur dadhāti ||
  • ud u ṣya devaḥ savitā: This phrase refers to the divine entity Savitā, often associated with the sun. The term "ud u ṣya" can be interpreted as "rising" or "emerging," symbolizing the dawn or the beginning of a new day.
  • yayāma hiraṇyayīm amatiṁ: Here, "yayāma" means "by which" and "hiraṇyayīm" translates to "golden" or "radiant." "Amatiṁ" refers to "knowledge" or "wisdom." This part of the mantra emphasizes the pursuit of enlightenment and the illumination that comes from divine knowledge.
  • yām aśiśret: This phrase can be understood as "which has been established" or "which has been created." It signifies the foundation of existence and the importance of divine creation in the universe.
  • nūnam bhago havyo mānuṣebhir: "Nūnam" means "indeed," while "bhago" refers to "wealth" or "prosperity." "Havyo" indicates something that is worthy of being offered, and "mānuṣebhir" means "by humans." This part highlights the relationship between divine blessings and human efforts in achieving prosperity.
  • vi yo ratnā purūvasur dadhāti: "Vi yo" translates to "who" or "that which," while "ratnā" means "gems" or "treasures." "Purūvasur" refers to a powerful being or deity, and "dadhāti" means "gives" or "bestows." This emphasizes the idea that divine forces provide treasures and blessings to those who seek them.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of seeking divine wisdom and prosperity through the blessings of Savitā, the sun god. It signifies the importance of enlightenment and the pursuit of knowledge as a means to achieve a prosperous life. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects such as personal growth, education, and the pursuit of goals. By recognizing the divine influence in our endeavors and striving for wisdom, individuals can cultivate a life filled with abundance and fulfillment. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness between the divine and human efforts, encouraging us to seek enlightenment and prosperity in our daily lives.
