तं शुभ्रम अग्निम अवसे हवामहे वैश्वानरम् मातरिश्वानम् उक्त्यम् |बृहस्पतिम मनुषो देवतातये विप्रं श्रोतारम् अतिथिं रघुश्यदम् ||
taṁ śubhram agnim avase havāmahe vaiśvānaram mātariśvānam ukthyam | bṛhaspatim manuṣo devatātaye vipraṁ śrotāram atithiṁ raghuṣyadam ||
  • taṁ - This word translates to "that" or "him," referring to the divine fire or Agni.
  • śubhram - Meaning "bright" or "pure," it signifies the auspicious nature of Agni.
  • agnim - This is the noun form of Agni, the fire god, who is considered a mediator between humans and the divine.
  • avase - This means "we invoke" or "we call upon," indicating a prayerful request.
  • havāmahe - Translates to "we offer" or "we worship," emphasizing the act of devotion.
  • vaiśvānaram - Refers to the universal or all-pervading aspect of Agni, representing the fire that exists everywhere.
  • mātariśvānam - This term refers to the wind or the atmospheric deity, symbolizing the connection between fire and air.
  • ukthyam - Meaning "the hymn" or "the chant," it signifies the importance of sacred words in rituals.
  • bṛhaspatim - Refers to Brihaspati, the guru of the gods, symbolizing wisdom and knowledge.
  • manuṣo - Meaning "of humans," it emphasizes the human aspect of the divine connection.
  • devatātaye - This means "for the divine purpose," indicating the spiritual significance of the invocation.
  • vipraṁ - Refers to the learned or wise person, often a priest or sage, who performs the rituals.
  • śrotāram - Meaning "the listener," it signifies the importance of being receptive to divine wisdom.
  • atithiṁ - This translates to "the guest," symbolizing hospitality and the importance of welcoming others.
  • raghuṣyadam - Refers to the lineage of Raghu, a royal lineage in Hindu mythology, symbolizing nobility and honor.

The mantra invokes Agni, the divine fire, as a symbol of purity and brightness. It emphasizes the importance of worship and the connection between the divine and human realms. The mention of Brihaspati highlights the pursuit of wisdom, while the reference to hospitality signifies the importance of welcoming guests and sharing knowledge.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Rituals and Worship: It can be recited during rituals to invoke blessings and purity in one's life.
  • Seeking Knowledge: The invocation of Brihaspati encourages individuals to seek wisdom and guidance in their personal and professional lives.
  • Hospitality: The emphasis on welcoming guests can be a reminder to practice kindness and generosity in everyday interactions.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of the divine and human realms. It encourages individuals to seek purity, wisdom, and hospitality in their lives, fostering a sense of community and spiritual growth.
