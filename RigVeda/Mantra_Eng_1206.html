तक्षन् रथं सुवृतं विद्मनापसस तक्षन् हरि इन्द्रवाहा वृषण्वसू |तक्षन् पितृभ्यां ऋभवो युवद वयस् तक्षन् वत्साय मातरं सचाभुवम् ||
takṣan rathaṁ suvṛtaṁ vidmanāpasas takṣan harī indravāhā vṛṣaṇvasū | takṣan pitṛbhyām ṛbhavo yuvad vayas takṣan vatsāya mātaraṁ sacābhuvam ||
  • takṣan: This word means "to build" or "to create." It signifies the act of construction or formation, which can be related to various aspects of life, such as building relationships, careers, or communities.
  • rathaṁ: This translates to "chariot." In a metaphorical sense, a chariot can represent the journey of life, where one navigates through different experiences and challenges.
  • suvṛtaṁ: Meaning "well-formed" or "well-constructed," this term emphasizes the importance of quality and integrity in whatever we create or build.
  • vidmanāpasas: This can be interpreted as "with knowledge" or "with wisdom." It highlights the necessity of understanding and insight in the process of creation.
  • harī: This word refers to "the divine" or "the god." It signifies the presence of a higher power or guidance in our endeavors.
  • indravāhā: This term can be understood as "carrying the essence of Indra," who is a deity associated with strength and power. It suggests that our creations should embody strength and resilience.
  • vṛṣaṇvasū: This translates to "the best of the best" or "the most excellent." It encourages striving for excellence in all our pursuits.
  • pitṛbhyām: Meaning "to the ancestors," this term acknowledges the importance of honoring our roots and the wisdom passed down through generations.
  • ṛbhavo: This can be interpreted as "the wise" or "the knowledgeable." It emphasizes the value of wisdom in our actions and decisions.
  • yuvad vayas: This phrase means "youthful age" or "the vigor of youth." It signifies the energy and potential that comes with youth, encouraging us to harness this vitality in our endeavors.
  • vatsāya: This translates to "for the calf" or "for the young." It symbolizes nurturing and caring for the younger generation, ensuring their growth and development.
  • mātaraṁ: Meaning "mother," this term highlights the nurturing aspect of creation, emphasizing the importance of love and care in our actions.
  • sacābhuvam: This can be interpreted as "with truth" or "with sincerity." It underscores the significance of honesty and integrity in all our endeavors.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of building and creating with wisdom, strength, and integrity. It encourages us to honor our roots and the wisdom of our ancestors while nurturing the younger generation. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in family dynamics, community building, and personal development. By embodying these principles, we can create a more harmonious and prosperous society, ensuring that our actions are guided by truth and care for others.
