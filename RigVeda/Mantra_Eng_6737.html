अधः पश्यस्व मोपरी संतराम पादकौ हर |मा ते कशप्लकौ दृष्टं स्त्री हि ब्रह्मा बभूविथ ||
adhaḥ paśyasva mopari saṁtarām pādakau hara | mā te kaśaplakau dṛśan strī hi brahmā babhūvitha ||
  • Adhaḥ paśyasva: This phrase translates to "Look down" or "See below." It suggests the importance of humility and awareness of one's surroundings. In real life, this can be related to being grounded and understanding the realities of life rather than being overly ambitious or detached from the present.
  • Mopari saṁtarām pādakau hara: This can be interpreted as "Do not let your feet stray." It emphasizes the need for focus and direction in life. For example, in a professional setting, staying committed to one's goals and not getting distracted by temptations or diversions is crucial for success.
  • Mā te kaśaplakau: This phrase means "May you not be blinded." It serves as a reminder to maintain clarity of vision and purpose. In everyday life, this can relate to making informed decisions, whether in personal relationships or career choices, ensuring that one is not misled by superficial appearances.
  • Dṛśan strī hi brahmā babhūvitha: This translates to "The woman is indeed the embodiment of the divine." It highlights the significance of femininity and the divine feminine energy in creation and existence. In contemporary society, this can be seen in the empowerment of women and the recognition of their vital roles in various spheres, from family to leadership positions.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates essential life lessons about humility, focus, clarity, and the reverence for the feminine divine. Its significance lies in its applicability to everyday life, encouraging individuals to remain grounded, stay true to their paths, and honor the contributions of women in society. By embodying these principles, one can navigate life's challenges with wisdom and grace, fostering a more balanced and respectful world.
