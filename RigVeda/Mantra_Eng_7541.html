आ ते दक्षं वि रोचनां दधद् रत्नां वि दाशुषे |स्तोत्रिभ्य इन्द्रं अर्चत ||
ā te dakṣaṁ vi rocanā dadhad ratnā vi dāśuṣe | stotṛbhya indram arcata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā te: To you
    • dakṣaṁ: Skillful or capable
    • vi: In various ways
    • rocanā: Radiance or brilliance
    • dadhad: Bestowing or giving
    • ratnā: Jewels or valuable things
    • vi: In various ways
    • dāśuṣe: For the one who serves or worships
    • stotṛbhya: To the worshippers or those who praise
    • indram: Indra, the king of gods
    • arcata: May be worshipped or honored
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of the gods, asking him to bestow his skills, radiance, and valuable blessings upon those who serve and worship him. It emphasizes the importance of devotion and the rewards that come from honoring the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, individuals often seek guidance and support from mentors or leaders. Just as this mantra calls upon Indra for skill and brilliance, employees may look to their managers for wisdom and direction to excel in their roles.
    • In personal relationships, showing appreciation and respect to those who support us can lead to stronger bonds. This mantra reflects the idea that honoring those who help us can bring about positive outcomes in our lives.
    • In spiritual practices, individuals may recite prayers or mantras to seek blessings and guidance. This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of devotion and the potential rewards of such practices.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance and the recognition of the importance of devotion. In real-life scenarios, it teaches us to seek guidance from those who are more experienced and to honor those who support us. By doing so, we can cultivate our skills and radiance, ultimately leading to personal and professional growth.
