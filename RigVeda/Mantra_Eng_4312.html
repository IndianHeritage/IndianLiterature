एतावद् वेद उषस् त्वं भूयो वा दातुम् अर्हसि |या स्तोत्रिभ्यो विभावरी उच्छन्ती न प्रमीयसे सुजाते अश्वसूत्रे ||
etāvad ved uṣas tvam bhūyo vā dātum arhasi | yā stotṛbhyo vibhāvary ucchantī na pramīyase sujāte aśvasūnṛte ||
  • etāvad: This word translates to "thus far" or "to this extent." It indicates a limit or a boundary that has been reached.
  • ved: This means "knowledge" or "wisdom." In the context of the RigVeda, it refers to the sacred knowledge that is revered in Vedic traditions.
  • uṣas: This word means "dawn" or "the light of dawn." It symbolizes new beginnings, hope, and the dispelling of darkness.
  • tvam: This translates to "you," referring to the divine or the personified dawn in this mantra.
  • bhūyo: This means "again" or "more." It suggests a request for a repeated action or blessing.
  • vā: This translates to "or," indicating an alternative or choice.
  • dātum: This means "to give" or "to bestow." It implies a request for a gift or blessing.
  • arhasi: This translates to "you are worthy" or "you are capable." It acknowledges the divine's ability to grant blessings.
  • yā: This means "who" or "that which," referring to the dawn or the divine presence.
  • stotṛbhyo: This translates to "to the worshippers" or "to those who praise." It acknowledges the devotees who seek the divine's grace.
  • vibhāvary: This means "the one who brings forth" or "the one who manifests." It highlights the creative aspect of the divine.
  • ucchantī: This translates to "who is praised" or "who is exalted." It signifies the glory and reverence associated with the divine.
  • na: This means "not," indicating a negation or absence.
  • pramīyase: This translates to "you are measured" or "you are limited." It suggests that the divine cannot be confined or quantified.
  • sujāte: This means "well-born" or "noble." It refers to the purity and high status of the divine.
  • aśvasūnṛte: This translates to "in truth" or "in righteousness." It emphasizes the importance of truth and integrity in the divine's actions.

Conclusion: This mantra from the RigVeda is a profound invocation to the divine dawn, symbolizing the arrival of light, knowledge, and new beginnings. It expresses a desire for the divine to bestow blessings upon the worshippers, acknowledging the divine's worthiness and capability to grant such gifts. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during personal challenges, transitions, or new ventures where hope and guidance are sought. It serves as a reminder to embrace truth and righteousness in our actions, fostering a sense of integrity and purpose in our lives. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire individuals to seek enlightenment and blessings while recognizing the divine's omnipresence and boundless nature.
