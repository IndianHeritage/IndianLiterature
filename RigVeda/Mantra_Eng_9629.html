यो अस्मान् अन्नं तृष्वा अधधात्य आञ्जैः घृतैः जुहोति पुष्यति | तस्मै सहस्रं अक्षभिः वि चक्षे अग्ने विश्वतः प्रत्यङ्ग् असि त्वम् ||
yo asmā annaṁ tṛṣv ā3dadhāty ājyair ghṛtair juhoti puṣyati | tasmai sahasram akṣabhir vi cakṣe 'gne viśvataḥ pratyaṅṅ asi tvam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • asmā: to us
    • annaṁ: food
    • tṛṣv: with thirst
    • ādadhāty: offers
    • ājyair: with clarified butter
    • ghṛtair: with ghee
    • juhoti: sacrifices
    • puṣyati: nourishes
  • Significance of the Mantra:
    • This mantra speaks to the act of offering and sacrifice, emphasizing the importance of food and nourishment in spiritual practices.
    • The mention of "clarified butter" (ājyair) and "ghee" (ghṛtair) signifies the purity and richness of offerings made to the divine.
    • The phrase "sahasram akṣabhir" suggests a multitude of blessings or eyes, indicating the omnipresence and watchfulness of the divine.
    • The term "agne" refers to Agni, the fire god, who is central to Vedic rituals and symbolizes transformation and purification.
  • Real-Life Application:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be seen as a reminder of the importance of gratitude for the food we receive. Just as offerings are made in rituals, we can express gratitude for our meals, recognizing the effort that goes into producing and preparing food.
    • The act of sharing food with others can be viewed as a form of sacrifice and nourishment, fostering community and connection, much like the offerings made in Vedic rituals.
    • In a broader sense, the mantra encourages us to be mindful of our consumption and to appreciate the resources we have, promoting sustainability and respect for nature.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of offering, nourishment, and the divine connection through food. It serves as a reminder of the sacredness of what we consume and the importance of gratitude.
    • In our daily lives, we can apply the teachings of this mantra by being conscious of our food choices, sharing with others, and recognizing the interconnectedness of all beings through the act of nourishment.
    • Ultimately, it highlights the significance of rituals and offerings in fostering a deeper relationship with the divine and the world around us.
