एवेन्द्रः सुत एस्तावी सोमे भरद्वाजेषु क्षयद इन मग्होनः |असद यथा जरीत्र उता सूरीर इन्द्रो रायो विश्ववारस्य दाता ||
eved indraḥ sute astāvi some bharadvājeṣu kṣayad in maghonaḥ | asad yathā jaritra uta sūrir indro rāyo viśvavārasya dātā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eved: Indeed
    • indraḥ: Indra (the king of gods)
    • sute: in the son
    • astāvi: may he be strong
    • some: in the soma (a sacred drink)
    • bharadvājeṣu: in the Bharadvaja (a clan or lineage)
    • kṣayad: may he destroy
    • in: in
    • maghonaḥ: of the mighty
    • asad: as if
    • yathā: as
    • jaritra: the one who is old
    • uta: or
    • sūrir: the wise
    • indro: Indra
    • rāyo: of wealth
    • viśvavārasya: of the all-protecting
    • dātā: the giver
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes Indra, the king of gods, asking for strength and protection.
    • The reference to "soma" highlights the importance of this sacred drink in rituals, symbolizing divine inspiration and vitality.
    • The mention of "Bharadvaja" connects the mantra to a specific lineage, emphasizing the importance of heritage and community in spiritual practices.
    • The phrase "may he destroy" suggests a plea for the removal of obstacles and negativity, akin to seeking help in overcoming challenges in life.
    • The comparison to the wise and the old indicates the value of wisdom and experience in navigating life's complexities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, invoking the spirit of Indra can be likened to seeking leadership qualities and strength in decision-making.
    • During a family gathering, sharing a soma-like drink can symbolize unity and the sharing of blessings among members.
    • In personal challenges, one might reflect on the wisdom of elders, drawing parallels to the mantra's emphasis on the wise and experienced.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine strength, wisdom, and community support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal struggles to communal gatherings, reminding us of the interconnectedness of strength, wisdom, and heritage.
    • By invoking these qualities, individuals can seek guidance and support in their endeavors, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
