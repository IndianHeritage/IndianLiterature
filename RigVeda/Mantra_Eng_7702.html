आ वच्यस्व महि प्सरो वृषेन्दो द्युम्नवत् तमः |आ योनिं धर्णसिः सदा ||
ā vacyasva mahi psaro vṛṣendo dyumnavattamaḥ | ā yoniṁ dharṇasiḥ sadaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "ā vacyasva mahi psaro vṛṣendo dyumnavattamaḥ | ā yoniṁ dharṇasiḥ sadaḥ ||"
  • Translation: This mantra can be translated as: "O great one, may you speak forth, you who are endowed with the best of glory, and you who hold the essence of all beings." The essence of this mantra is a call for divine communication and the recognition of the inherent greatness in all beings.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • ā vacyasva: "Speak forth" - a request for expression and communication.
    • mahi psaro: "Great one" - refers to a powerful entity or divine being.
    • vṛṣendo: "Endowed with glory" - signifies someone who possesses exceptional qualities or virtues.
    • dyumnavattamaḥ: "Best of glory" - highlights the supreme nature of the being being addressed.
    • ā yoniṁ: "You who hold the essence" - refers to the fundamental nature or source of existence.
    • dharṇasiḥ: "You who sustain" - indicates the nurturing aspect of the divine.
    • sadaḥ: "Always" - emphasizes the eternal nature of the divine presence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often need to communicate effectively to inspire their teams. This mantra can be seen as a reminder for leaders to express their vision clearly and to recognize the potential in their team members.
    • In personal relationships, open communication is vital. This mantra encourages individuals to speak their truths and acknowledge the greatness in one another, fostering deeper connections.
    • In spiritual practices, chanting this mantra can serve as a meditation on the divine qualities within oneself and others, promoting self-awareness and compassion.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine communication and recognition of the inherent greatness in all beings. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as leadership, personal relationships, and spiritual practices. By embodying the essence of this mantra, individuals can foster better communication, enhance relationships, and cultivate a deeper understanding of the divine presence in themselves and others.
