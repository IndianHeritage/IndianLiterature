इन्द्रो मह्ना महतो अर्णवस्य वि मूर्धानम अभिनद अर्बुदस्य |अहन्न अहिम अरिणात सप्त सिन्धून देवैर द्यावापृथिवी प्रावतम नः ||
indro mahnā mahato arṇavasya vi mūrdhānam abhinad arbudasya | ahann ahim ariṇāt sapta sindhūn devair dyāvāpṛthivī prāvataṁ naḥ ||
  • Indro: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Mahnā: Means 'great' or 'mighty', emphasizing the vastness of Indra's power.
  • Mahato: Translates to 'of the great', indicating the importance of the subject being discussed.
  • Arṇavasya: Refers to the ocean or vast waters, symbolizing depth and the source of life.
  • Vi mūrdhānam: Means 'in the head' or 'at the top', suggesting a position of authority or prominence.
  • Abhinad: Translates to 'to break' or 'to split', indicating a transformative action.
  • Arbudasya: Refers to 'the great multitude' or 'the vastness', often associated with challenges or obstacles.
  • Ahann: Means 'I have', indicating possession or realization.
  • Ahim: Refers to 'the serpent' or 'the enemy', symbolizing challenges or adversities.
  • Ariṇāt: Means 'to overcome' or 'to conquer', suggesting victory over obstacles.
  • Sapta sindhūn: Refers to 'the seven rivers', symbolizing abundance and prosperity.
  • Devair: Means 'by the gods', indicating divine support or intervention.
  • Dyāvāpṛthivī: Refers to 'heaven and earth', symbolizing the entire universe.
  • Prāvataṁ: Means 'to bestow' or 'to grant', indicating a request for blessings.
  • Naḥ: Means 'for us', indicating a collective need or desire.

This mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for strength and guidance from the divine, particularly from Indra, to overcome challenges and achieve prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of divine support in navigating life's obstacles, much like how individuals seek guidance from mentors or leaders in their personal and professional lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied when facing significant challenges, such as starting a new business, overcoming personal struggles, or embarking on a new journey. Just as the rivers symbolize abundance, individuals can seek to harness their inner strength and the support of their community or divine forces to navigate through difficulties.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine assistance in overcoming challenges and achieving success. It serves as a reminder that, like the mighty rivers that flow through the land, we too can find strength and support in our journey through life. By invoking the blessings of the divine, we can aspire to rise above our obstacles and attain our goals.
