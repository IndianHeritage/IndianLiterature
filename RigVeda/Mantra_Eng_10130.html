अद्धीद इन्द्र प्रस्थितेमाऽ हविंषि चनो दधिष्व पचतो सोमम् |प्रयस्वन्तः प्रति हर्यामसि त्वा सत्याः सन्तु यजमानस्य कामाः ||
addhīd indra prasthitemā havīṁṣi cano dadhiṣva pacatota somam | prayasvantaḥ prati haryāmasi tvā satyāḥ santu yajamānasya kāmāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • addhīd: May we attain
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • prasthitemā: who is established
    • havīṁṣi: the offerings
    • cano: we invoke
    • dadhiṣva: may you accept
    • pacatota: the one who cooks
    • somam: the soma (sacred drink)
    • prayasvantaḥ: those who strive
    • prati: towards
    • haryāmasi: we are moving
    • tvā: to you
    • satyāḥ: the truthful
    • santu: may they be
    • yajamānasya: of the worshipper
    • kāmāḥ: desires
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of gods, asking him to accept the offerings made by the worshipper. It emphasizes the importance of striving towards truth and the fulfillment of desires through sincere worship and effort.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, this mantra can be likened to a team working diligently towards a common goal, seeking the approval and support of their leaders (akin to Indra) to achieve success.
    • In personal life, it reflects the idea of setting intentions and working hard to manifest one's desires, such as pursuing a career or personal growth, while remaining truthful and sincere in one's efforts.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine support in our endeavors. It teaches us that through dedication, truthfulness, and sincere offerings, we can achieve our goals and fulfill our desires. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal aspirations, professional ambitions, and spiritual practices, reminding us to remain committed and truthful in our pursuits.
