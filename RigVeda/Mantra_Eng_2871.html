आ मन्द्रैर इन्द्र हरिभिर याहि मयूररोमभिः |मा त्वा के चिन् न यमन विं न पाशिनो 'ति धन्वेव तां इहि ||
ā mandrair indra haribhir yāhi mayūraromabhiḥ | mā tvā ke cin ni yaman viṁ na pāśino 'ti dhanveva tām̐ ihi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā: From
    • mandrair: Sweet or pleasant
    • indra: Indra, the king of gods
    • haribhir: With the yellow or golden ones (often referring to the sun or light)
    • yāhi: Come
    • mayūraromabhiḥ: With the feathers of the peacock (symbolizing beauty and grace)
    • mā: Do not
    • tvā: You
    • ke cin: Anyone
    • ni yaman: Bind or restrain
    • viṁ: In any way
    • na: Not
    • pāśino: Those who bind (often referring to obstacles or negativity)
    • 'ti: Like
    • dhanveva: Like a bow
    • tām̐: That
    • ihi: Here
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence of Indra, asking him to come with pleasantness and grace.
    • The reference to "mayūraromabhiḥ" symbolizes beauty and elegance, suggesting that one should approach life with grace.
    • The plea to not be bound by anyone or anything emphasizes the importance of freedom and liberation from negativity or obstacles.
    • The comparison to a bow indicates strength and the ability to overcome challenges, suggesting that one should remain resilient.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a challenging work environment, invoking this mantra can serve as a reminder to maintain a positive attitude and approach tasks with grace.
    • During personal struggles, the mantra can inspire individuals to seek freedom from negative thoughts and external pressures, encouraging resilience.
    • In creative pursuits, the beauty of the peacock's feathers can symbolize the importance of expressing oneself with elegance and originality.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of invoking divine support to navigate life's challenges with grace and strength.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek freedom from negativity and to embrace beauty in our actions and thoughts.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as personal growth, professional challenges, and creative endeavors, encouraging individuals to remain resilient and graceful.
