अध प्र जज्ञे तरणिर ममत्तु प्र रोच्य अस्याऽ उषसो न सूरः |इन्दुर येभिर आष्ठ स्वेदुहव्यैः स्रुवेण सिञ्चञ जरणाभि धाम ||
adha pra jajñe taraṇir mamattu pra rocy asyā uṣaso na sūraḥ | indur yebhir āṣṭa sveduhavyaiḥ sruveṇa siñcañ jaraṇābhi dhāma ||
  • Adha pra jajñe taraṇir mamattu pra rocy asyā uṣaso na sūraḥ
    • Adha: This word translates to "down" or "below," indicating a grounding or foundational aspect.
    • Pra jajñe: Means "was born" or "came into being," suggesting the emergence of something new.
    • Taraṇir: Refers to "the one who crosses" or "the one who helps in crossing," often associated with overcoming obstacles.
    • Mamattu: Translates to "my" or "to me," indicating a personal connection or ownership.
    • Pra rocy: Means "to shine forth" or "to illuminate," suggesting enlightenment or clarity.
    • Asyā: Refers to "this," pointing to something specific in the context.
    • Uṣaso: Means "of dawn," symbolizing new beginnings and hope.
    • Na sūraḥ: Translates to "not the hero," indicating a contrast between the ordinary and the extraordinary.
  • Indur yebhir āṣṭa sveduhavyaiḥ sruveṇa siñcañ jaraṇābhi dhāma
    • Indur: Refers to "the moon," often symbolizing calmness and serenity.
    • Yebhir: Means "by whom," indicating agency or the means through which something is done.
    • Āṣṭa: Translates to "eight," which can symbolize completeness or wholeness.
    • Sveduhavyaiḥ: Refers to "the offerings of sweat," symbolizing hard work and effort.
    • Sruveṇa: Means "with a ladle," indicating the act of offering or sharing.
    • Siñcañ: Translates to "pouring," suggesting the act of giving or bestowing.
    • Jaraṇābhi: Refers to "the old," indicating wisdom that comes with age.
    • Dhāma: Means "abode" or "place," suggesting a sacred or significant space.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates themes of emergence, personal connection, and the interplay between the ordinary and the extraordinary. It emphasizes the importance of hard work and the wisdom that comes with experience. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as:

  • In personal development, where one must overcome obstacles to achieve new beginnings.
  • In community efforts, where collective hard work leads to shared success and enlightenment.
  • In spiritual practices, where the dawn symbolizes hope and renewal, encouraging individuals to seek clarity and purpose.

Overall, the mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of effort, the beauty of new beginnings, and the wisdom gained through experience, making it relevant in various aspects of life.
