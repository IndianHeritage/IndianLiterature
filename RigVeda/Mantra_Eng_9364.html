द्विधा सूनवोऽसुरं स्वरविदम् आस्थापयन्त तृतीयेन कर्मणा |स्वाम् प्रजाम् पितरः पितृयं सह आवरेष्व् अदधुस् तन्तुम् आततम् ||
dvidhā sūnavo 'suraṁ svarvidam āsthāpayanta tṛtīyena karmaṇā | svām prajām pitaraḥ pitryaṁ saha āvareṣv adadhus tantum ātatam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • dvidhā: in two ways
    • sūnavo: sons or offspring
    • 'suraṁ: divine or celestial
    • svarvidam: pertaining to the heavenly realm
    • āsthāpayanta: establishing or placing
    • tṛtīyena: by the third
    • karmaṇā: through action or deed
    • svām: their own
    • prajām: progeny or offspring
    • pitaraḥ: fathers or ancestors
    • pitryaṁ: pertaining to the ancestors
    • saha: together
    • āvareṣv: in the realms of the departed
    • adadhus: they placed or established
    • tantum: the thread or connection
    • ātatatam: that which is eternal or everlasting
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the dual nature of existence and the role of offspring in establishing a connection between the divine and the earthly realms.
    • The phrase "dvidhā sūnavo" suggests that there are two types of offspring: those who are aligned with the divine and those who are not.
    • The term "suraṁ svarvidam" indicates a relationship with the celestial or divine, emphasizing the importance of spiritual lineage.
    • By "āsthāpayanta tṛtīyena karmaṇā," it implies that through righteous actions (karma), one can establish a connection with both the divine and ancestral realms.
    • The mention of "svām prajām pitaraḥ" highlights the responsibility of parents to nurture their children in a way that honors their ancestors.
    • Finally, "tantum ātatam" signifies the eternal bond that exists through these connections, suggesting that our actions have lasting impacts beyond our immediate lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, rituals are performed to honor ancestors, reflecting the idea of maintaining a connection with the past and ensuring that the values and teachings of previous generations are passed down.
    • In modern parenting, the mantra can be seen in the way parents strive to instill values in their children, ensuring that they grow up with a sense of identity and respect for their heritage.
    • Community service and charitable actions can be viewed as a way of establishing a connection with the divine, as individuals contribute positively to society, reflecting the "karmaṇā" aspect of the mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of understanding our dual nature as both earthly beings and spiritual entities.
    • It highlights the importance of our actions in shaping our legacy and maintaining connections with both our ancestors and the divine.
    • In real life, it serves as a reminder to honor our roots, act with integrity, and recognize the impact of our deeds on future generations.
