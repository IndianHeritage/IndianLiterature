मन्त्र: सोमः पुनानो अर्षति सहस्रधारो अत्यविः | वायोर इन्द्रस्य निष्कृतम् ||
somaḥ punāno arṣati sahasradhāro atyaviḥ | vāyor indrasya niṣkṛtam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Somaḥ: Refers to the sacred drink, Soma, which is associated with immortality and divine inspiration.
    • Punāno: Means 'purifying' or 'refreshing'.
    • Arṣati: Translates to 'shines' or 'radiates'.
    • Sahasradhāro: Means 'one who has a thousand streams' or 'abundant'.
    • Atyaviḥ: Refers to 'beyond' or 'transcendent'.
    • Vāyor: Means 'of the wind' or 'air'.
    • Indrasya: Refers to 'of Indra', the king of gods and god of rain and thunderstorms.
    • Niṣkṛtam: Means 'liberated' or 'freed'.
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks of the purifying and radiant qualities of Soma, which is abundant and transcendent, and its connection to the divine, particularly to Indra.
    • It emphasizes the idea of liberation and the refreshing nature of divine inspiration that comes from the sacred drink.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, the concept of 'Soma' can be likened to experiences or practices that rejuvenate the spirit, such as meditation, yoga, or engaging in nature.
    • The idea of 'purification' can be seen in various wellness practices, such as detox diets or mindfulness, which aim to cleanse the body and mind.
    • Indra's association with rain can be related to the importance of water in agriculture, symbolizing abundance and prosperity in our lives.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of divine inspiration and the refreshing qualities of life that lead to liberation and abundance.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to seek out experiences that purify and rejuvenate the spirit, fostering a connection with the divine and the natural world.
    • By embracing these qualities, one can cultivate a life filled with vitality, creativity, and a sense of freedom.
