गम्भीराम् उदधीरिव क्रतुम् पुष्यसि गा इव |प्र सुगोपाऽयवसां धेनवो यथा ह्रदं कुल्या इवाशत ||
gambhīrām̐ udadhīm̐r iva kratum puṣyasi gā iva | pra sugopā yavasaṁ dhenavo yathā hradaṁ kulyā ivāśata ||
  • gambhīrām̐ udadhīm̐r iva: This phrase translates to "like the deep ocean." The ocean is often seen as a symbol of depth, mystery, and vastness. In real life, this can be related to the depth of knowledge or wisdom that one can attain through experience and learning.
  • kratum puṣyasi gā iva: This means "you nourish the intellect like a cow." Cows are revered in many cultures for their nurturing qualities, providing milk and sustenance. This can be likened to how education and knowledge nourish our minds and help us grow intellectually.
  • pra sugopā yavasaṁ: This translates to "the good protector of the grains." Grains are essential for sustenance and survival. This can be seen in the context of agriculture and food security, where protecting crops is vital for the well-being of society.
  • dhenavo yathā hradaṁ: This means "like the cows by the lake." Lakes are often seen as sources of life and nourishment. This can symbolize the importance of community and resources that support life, much like how cows provide milk and lakes provide water.
  • kulyā ivāśata: This translates to "like the streams that flow." Streams represent the flow of life, continuity, and connection. In real life, this can be related to the importance of relationships and networks that support our growth and well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of nourishment—both intellectual and physical. It highlights the importance of deep knowledge, protection of resources, and the nurturing aspects of life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to education, agriculture, and community building. By fostering a deep understanding and protecting our resources, we can ensure a sustainable and nurturing environment for future generations. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of knowledge, sustenance, and community in our lives.
