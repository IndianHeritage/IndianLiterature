इन्द्रे विश्वानि वीर्याणि कृतानि कर्त्वानि च |यम् अर्का अध्वरं विदुः ||
indre viśvāni vīryā kṛtāni kartvāni ca | yam arkā adhvaraṁ viduḥ ||
  • Indre: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Viśvāni: Means "all" or "universal," indicating inclusiveness and the entirety of existence.
  • Vīryā: Translates to "strength" or "heroic deeds," emphasizing valor and courage.
  • Kṛtāni: Means "performed" or "done," suggesting actions that have been taken or accomplishments achieved.
  • Kartvāni: Refers to "those who act" or "doers," highlighting the importance of action in achieving results.
  • Yam: Means "whom," indicating a connection or relationship with the subject being discussed.
  • Arkā: Refers to "the sun" or "light," symbolizing knowledge, enlightenment, and truth.
  • Adhvaraṁ: Means "sacrifice" or "ritual," representing the importance of spiritual practices and offerings.
  • Viduḥ: Translates to "know" or "understand," emphasizing the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom.

This mantra can be interpreted as a call to Indra to bestow strength and valor upon those who act with purpose and integrity. It highlights the significance of collective efforts and the importance of knowledge and enlightenment in achieving success. The invocation of the sun symbolizes the pursuit of truth and clarity in one's actions.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Leadership: Leaders can draw inspiration from Indra's strength to guide their teams effectively, fostering a sense of unity and purpose.
  • Teamwork: The emphasis on collective action encourages collaboration among individuals to achieve common goals, much like a sports team working together to win a game.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can reflect on their own actions and strive for personal development, seeking knowledge and wisdom to enhance their capabilities.
  • Spiritual Practices: Engaging in rituals and spiritual practices can help individuals connect with higher truths and foster a sense of community.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of collective strength, the importance of knowledge, and the value of purposeful action. It encourages individuals and communities to strive for excellence and to seek enlightenment in their endeavors, ultimately leading to a more harmonious and successful existence.
