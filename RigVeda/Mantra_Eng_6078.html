मन्दन्तु त्वा मगवन्न इन्द्रेन्दवो राधोदयाय सुन्वते |आमुष्यā सोमम् अपिबश् चमू सुतं ज्येष्ठं तद् दधिषे सहः ||
mandantu tvā maghavann indrendavo rādhodeyāya sunvate | āmuṣyā somam apibaś camū sutaṁ jyeṣṭhaṁ tad dadhiṣe sahaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • mandantu: May they be pleased or may they be satisfied.
    • tvā: You (referring to Indra).
    • maghavann: O powerful one, or O mighty one.
    • indrendavo: Indra, the king of gods.
    • rādhodeyāya: For the sake of prosperity or wealth.
    • sunvate: Who are praised or who are worshipped.
    • āmuṣyā: From this (or from here).
    • somam: The soma (a sacred drink).
    • apibaś: May we drink.
    • camū: The army or the host.
    • sutaṁ: The son or offspring.
    • jyeṣṭhaṁ: The elder or the most excellent.
    • tad: That.
    • dadhiṣe: You may bestow or grant.
    • sahaḥ: Together or with.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer to Indra, the king of gods, asking for his blessings and satisfaction.
    • It emphasizes the importance of prosperity and wealth, suggesting that the divine powers should be pleased for the well-being of the community.
    • The reference to soma indicates a ritualistic aspect, where the sacred drink is offered to the gods, symbolizing the connection between the divine and the earthly realms.
    • The mention of the army and the elder son signifies the importance of strength and leadership in achieving success and prosperity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community gathering or festival, people often invoke blessings from deities for prosperity and success in their endeavors, similar to the invocation in this mantra.
    • In business, leaders may seek the blessings of higher powers or mentors (akin to Indra) to ensure the success of their ventures.
    • During family rituals, the eldest son may be given special respect and responsibilities, reflecting the significance of the 'jyeṣṭhaṁ' or elder in this mantra.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of divine blessings in achieving prosperity and success.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness between the divine, the community, and individual efforts.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals and communities to seek blessings, honor their leaders, and strive for collective well-being.
