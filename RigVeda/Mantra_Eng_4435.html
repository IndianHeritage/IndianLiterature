अस्माकं अग्नि मगवत्सु धारयाणामि क्षत्रं अजरोऽसुवीर्यम् |वयं जयेम शतिनं सहस्रिणं वैश्वानर वाजम् अग्ने तवोतििभिः ||
asmākam agne maghavatsu dhārayānāmi kṣatram ajaraṁ suvīryam | vayaṁ jayema śatinaṁ sahasriṇaṁ vaiśvānara vājam agne tavotibhiḥ ||
  • asmākam: This word translates to "our" or "of us," indicating a collective identity or community.
  • agne: Refers to "Agni," the fire god, symbolizing energy, light, and transformation.
  • maghavatsu: This means "the powerful" or "the mighty," often associated with strength and capability.
  • dhārayānāmi: Translates to "I hold" or "I support," suggesting a sense of responsibility and nurturing.
  • kṣatram: Refers to "power" or "strength," often in the context of leadership and protection.
  • ajaraṁ: Means "immortal" or "everlasting," indicating something that transcends time.
  • suvīryam: Translates to "good strength" or "excellent valor," emphasizing the importance of moral and physical strength.
  • vayaṁ: Means "we," reinforcing the collective aspect of the mantra.
  • jayema: Translates to "we may conquer" or "we may triumph," indicating a desire for victory and success.
  • śatinaṁ: Refers to "wealth" or "prosperity," suggesting a holistic view of success that includes material and spiritual wealth.
  • sahasriṇaṁ: Means "thousandfold," indicating abundance and multiplicity.
  • vaiśvānara: Refers to the universal or cosmic aspect of fire, symbolizing unity and interconnectedness.
  • vājam: Translates to "food" or "nourishment," indicating sustenance and support.
  • agne: Reiterating the invocation to Agni, emphasizing the importance of this divine energy.
  • tavotibhiḥ: Means "by your powers" or "through your strength," indicating reliance on divine support.

The mantra essentially invokes Agni, the fire god, to bestow strength, prosperity, and victory upon the community. It emphasizes the importance of collective strength and the nurturing of power that is both moral and physical. The repetition of themes such as immortality, abundance, and divine support highlights the interconnectedness of life and the reliance on higher powers for success.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Leadership: Leaders can draw inspiration from this mantra to foster a sense of community and collective strength, ensuring that their actions benefit the group as a whole.
  • Teamwork: In workplaces or sports teams, the mantra can serve as a reminder of the importance of collaboration and shared goals, encouraging individuals to support one another.
  • Personal Growth: Individuals can reflect on the qualities of strength, resilience, and moral integrity that the mantra embodies, using it as a guide for personal development.
  • Spiritual Practices: The invocation of Agni can be a part of rituals or meditative practices aimed at seeking guidance and strength from the divine.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of community, strength, and divine support in achieving success and prosperity. It encourages individuals and groups to work together harmoniously, drawing on both their inner strength and the blessings of higher powers.
