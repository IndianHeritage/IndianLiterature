कुतस्त्वं इन्द्र माहिनः सन्न एको यासि सत्पते किं त इत्तथा |सम्पृच्छसे समराणः शुभानैर वोचेस तन्नो हरिवो यत ते अस्मे ||
kutas tvam indra māhinaḥ sann eko yāsi satpate kiṁ ta itthā | sam pṛcchase samarāṇaḥ śubhānair voces tan no harivo yat te asme ||
  • kutas tvam indra māhinaḥ - This phrase can be translated as "Where are you, Indra, the mighty one?" Here, "Indra" refers to the king of the gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing strength and power. In real life, this can be likened to seeking guidance or strength from a higher power or authority when faced with challenges.
  • sann eko yāsi satpate - This translates to "You alone go towards the path of truth." It emphasizes the importance of individual responsibility and the pursuit of truth. In everyday life, this can be seen in the actions of individuals who choose to stand by their principles, even when it is difficult to do so.
  • kiṁ ta itthā - This means "What is this?" It reflects a sense of inquiry and curiosity. In a real-world context, this can be related to the scientific method, where questioning and seeking answers is fundamental to progress and understanding.
  • sam pṛcchase samarāṇaḥ - This translates to "You ask the assembled ones." It signifies the importance of community and collective wisdom. In modern society, this can be seen in collaborative efforts, such as team projects or community discussions, where diverse perspectives lead to better solutions.
  • śubhānair voces - This means "Speak with auspicious words." It highlights the power of positive communication. In real life, this can be exemplified by leaders who inspire and motivate others through uplifting language, fostering a positive environment.
  • tan no harivo yat te asme - This translates to "May we be blessed by your grace." It expresses a desire for divine blessings and support. In practical terms, this can be related to the human need for hope and encouragement, often sought through prayer, meditation, or community support during tough times.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates themes of strength, truth, inquiry, community, positive communication, and the quest for divine blessings. Its significance lies in its timeless relevance, encouraging individuals to seek strength from higher powers, uphold truth, engage with their communities, communicate positively, and seek blessings in their endeavors. In real life, these principles can guide personal development, enhance relationships, and foster a sense of purpose and belonging in a rapidly changing world.
