स मह्ना विश्वा दुरितानि साह्वान अग्निः ष्टवे दमा आ जातवेदाḥ |स नो रक्षिषद दुरिताद अवद्याद अस्मान गृणत उता नो मग्नोः ||
sa mahnā viśvā duritāni sāhvān agniḥ ṣṭave dama ā jātavedāḥ | sa no rakṣiṣad duritād avadyād asmān gṛṇata uta no maghonaḥ ||
  • sa mahnā viśvā duritāni sāhvān agniḥ ṣṭave dama ā jātavedāḥ
    • sa mahnā: This phrase can be interpreted as "with great strength" or "with great power." It emphasizes the importance of strength in overcoming challenges.
    • viśvā duritāni: "All obstacles" or "all difficulties." This signifies the various challenges one faces in life.
    • sāhvān agniḥ: "The fire of strength" or "the powerful fire." Fire here symbolizes energy, transformation, and purification.
    • ṣṭave dama: "We praise" or "we invoke." This indicates a call for divine assistance or recognition of the power of the divine.
    • ā jātavedāḥ: "O Jātavedas," referring to the divine being who knows all births and is associated with knowledge and wisdom.
  • sa no rakṣiṣad duritād avadyād asmān gṛṇata uta no maghonaḥ
    • sa no rakṣiṣad: "May he protect us." This is a plea for divine protection and guidance.
    • duritād avadyād: "From all difficulties and sins." This highlights the desire to be shielded from negative influences and hardships.
    • asmān gṛṇata: "May he sing for us." This suggests a wish for blessings and positive energy to be bestowed upon us.
    • uta no maghonaḥ: "And may he grant us strength." This is a request for empowerment and support in our endeavors.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of seeking divine strength and protection in the face of life's challenges. It serves as a reminder that, despite the obstacles we encounter, we can invoke higher powers for guidance and support. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied during difficult times, such as facing personal struggles, health issues, or professional challenges. By reciting this mantra, individuals can cultivate a sense of resilience and hope, reinforcing the belief that they are not alone in their struggles and that divine assistance is always available. The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to inspire courage and foster a connection with the divine, encouraging individuals to overcome their difficulties with strength and grace.
