वṁसगेव पूषर्यā शिम्बातā मित्रेव ऋतā शतरā शातपन्तā |वाजेवोच्चā वयसा घर्म्येष्ठā मेषेवेषā सपर्यā3 पुरीषā ||
vaṁsageva pūṣaryā śimbātā mitreva ṛtā śatarā śātapantā | vājevoccā vayasā gharmyeṣṭhā meṣeveṣā saparyā3 purīṣā ||
  • vaṁsageva pūṣaryā: This phrase can be interpreted as "like the one who is nourished by the earth." It signifies the importance of being grounded and connected to nature. In real life, this can be seen in practices like organic farming, where individuals work in harmony with the earth to produce food sustainably.
  • śimbātā mitreva: This translates to "like a friend who is supportive." It emphasizes the value of friendship and support in our lives. For example, in a workplace, having colleagues who support each other can lead to a more productive and positive environment.
  • ṛtā śatarā: Here, "ṛtā" refers to truth or order, and "śatarā" means a hundredfold. This suggests that living in accordance with truth brings abundant rewards. In everyday life, this can be seen in the importance of honesty in relationships, where trust leads to deeper connections.
  • śātapantā: This term can be understood as "the one who is steadfast." It highlights the significance of perseverance and resilience. For instance, athletes who train rigorously and remain committed to their goals exemplify this quality.
  • vājevoccā: This phrase can be interpreted as "like the wind that rises." It symbolizes freedom and the ability to adapt. In real life, this can be related to individuals who embrace change and are flexible in their careers, allowing them to thrive in dynamic environments.
  • vayasā gharmyeṣṭhā: This translates to "in the warmth of youth." It signifies the vitality and energy of youth. For example, young entrepreneurs often bring fresh ideas and innovation to the business world, showcasing the power of youthful enthusiasm.
  • meṣeveṣā saparyā: This phrase can be understood as "the one who is devoted to service." It emphasizes the importance of serving others. In real life, volunteers who dedicate their time to help those in need exemplify this principle, contributing to the betterment of society.
  • purīṣā: This term refers to "the essence of life." It signifies the core values that guide our actions. In everyday life, individuals who prioritize integrity and compassion reflect this essence, influencing those around them positively.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates essential values such as connection to nature, the importance of friendship, living in truth, perseverance, adaptability, youthful energy, devotion to service, and core life values. These principles are applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal relationships to professional environments. By embodying these qualities, individuals can lead fulfilling lives and contribute positively to their communities, fostering a harmonious and supportive society.
