स रोरुवद वृषभस तिग्मशृङ्गो वर्ष्मन् तस्थौ वरिमन् आ पृथिव्याः | विश्वेष्व एनं वृजनेषु पामि यो मे कुक्षी सुतसोमः पृणाति ||
sa roruvad vṛṣabhas tigmaśṛṅgo varṣman tasthau varimann ā pṛthivyāḥ | viśveṣv enaṁ vṛjaneṣu pāmi yo me kukṣī sutasomaḥ pṛṇāti ||
  • sa roruvad: This phrase can be translated as "he who roars" or "he who cries out." It signifies a powerful presence, often associated with strength and authority.
  • vṛṣabhas: This word means "the bull," which is a symbol of strength, fertility, and virility in many cultures. In real life, the bull can represent hard work and determination, as seen in agricultural societies where bulls are used for plowing fields.
  • tigmaśṛṅgo: This translates to "having sharp horns." The sharpness of the horns symbolizes readiness to defend and protect, akin to how individuals or communities stand up for their rights and values in challenging situations.
  • varṣman tasthau: This means "he stands firm in the rain." It represents resilience and the ability to withstand adversity, much like how a person might face challenges in life, such as economic hardships or personal struggles, yet remains steadfast.
  • varimann ā pṛthivyāḥ: This phrase can be interpreted as "the one who is great on the earth." It signifies a person of great stature or importance, akin to leaders or influential figures in society who make significant contributions to their communities.
  • viśveṣv enaṁ vṛjaneṣu pāmi: This translates to "I protect him in all endeavors." It emphasizes the importance of support and protection in various aspects of life, such as family, friendships, and community bonds, where individuals look out for one another.
  • yo me kukṣī sutasomaḥ pṛṇāti: This means "who nourishes me from the womb." It highlights the nurturing aspect of relationships, particularly the bond between a mother and child, and can extend to the care provided by society to its members.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of strength, resilience, protection, and nurturing. It signifies the importance of standing firm in the face of challenges while being supported by a community. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as in family dynamics, community support systems, and personal growth. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the strength that comes from unity and support in overcoming life's adversities.
