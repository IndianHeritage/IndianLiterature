सनā च सोम जेषि च पवमान महि श्रवः | अथā नो वस्यसस कृधि ||
sanā ca soma jeṣi ca pavamāna mahi śravaḥ | athā no vasyasas kṛdhi ||
  • Mantra: "sanā ca soma jeṣi ca pavamāna mahi śravaḥ | athā no vasyasas kṛdhi ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sanā: eternal, everlasting
    • ca: and
    • soma: the moon, often associated with nectar or divine elixir
    • jeṣi: victorious, conqueror
    • pavamāna: the purifying, the one that brings purity
    • mahi: great, vast
    • śravaḥ: fame, glory, or renown
    • athā: therefore, hence
    • no: our
    • vasyasas: of those who are subdued or controlled
    • kṛdhi: make, create, or establish
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the eternal and victorious nature of the divine, represented by Soma, which symbolizes nourishment and immortality.
    • The term "pavamāna" indicates a purifying force, suggesting that the divine has the power to cleanse and elevate the human spirit.
    • The phrase "mahi śravaḥ" emphasizes the greatness and renown of this divine essence, highlighting its importance in the cosmic order.
    • Finally, the mantra calls for the establishment of control or harmony in our lives, suggesting that by invoking this divine presence, we can achieve balance and peace.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often seek moments of clarity and purity, akin to the "pavamāna" aspect of the mantra. For instance, meditation or mindfulness practices can serve as a means to purify the mind and spirit.
    • The concept of "soma" can be related to the nourishment we seek in our lives, whether through healthy relationships, spiritual practices, or physical health, all contributing to our overall well-being.
    • Victory over personal challenges, represented by "jeṣi," can be seen in stories of individuals overcoming adversity, such as athletes who triumph over injuries or personal struggles to achieve their goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and purity in our lives. It serves as a reminder of the eternal nature of the spirit and the importance of striving for victory over our challenges.
    • In practical terms, it encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of harmony and balance, drawing on the divine qualities of nourishment, purity, and greatness in their daily lives.
    • By invoking this mantra, one can aspire to create a life that reflects these values, leading to personal growth and a deeper connection with the universe.
