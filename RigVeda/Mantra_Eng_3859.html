मन्त्र: परो यत् त्वं परम आजनिष्ठाः परावती श्रुत्यं नाम बिभ्रत् |अतश्चिद इन्द्राद अभयन्त देवाः विश्वा अपो अजयद दासपत्नीः ||
paro yat tvam parama ājaniṣṭhāḥ parāvati śrutyaṁ nāma bibhrat | ataś cid indrād abhayanta devā viśvā apo ajayad dāsapatnīḥ ||
  • paro yat tvam parama ājaniṣṭhāḥ: This phrase can be translated as "That which is supreme, you who are the ultimate source." Here, "paro" signifies the highest or supreme, while "tvam" refers to the divine or the ultimate reality. "Paramā" indicates the highest state, and "ājaniṣṭhāḥ" suggests a foundation or origin.
  • parāvati śrutyaṁ nāma bibhrat: This part means "You who hold the name of the supreme knowledge." "Parāvati" refers to the highest knowledge or wisdom, while "śrutyaṁ" indicates that which is heard or learned. "Nāma bibhrat" means to carry or embody a name, suggesting the importance of knowledge in one's identity.
  • ataś cid indrād abhayanta devā: This translates to "From this, the gods, like Indra, grant fearlessness." "Ataś" means from this, "cid" refers to consciousness or awareness, and "indrād" relates to Indra, the king of gods. "Abhayanta" means to grant fearlessness, indicating that divine knowledge leads to liberation from fear.
  • viśvā apo ajayad dāsapatnīḥ: This can be interpreted as "The universal waters have conquered the servant's wife." "Viśvā" means universal or all-encompassing, "apo" refers to waters, and "ajayad" means to conquer. "Dāsapatnīḥ" refers to the wife of a servant, which can symbolize the mundane or earthly attachments that are transcended through divine knowledge.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of supreme knowledge and divine consciousness in overcoming fear and earthly attachments. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as personal growth, spiritual awakening, and the pursuit of wisdom. For instance, individuals facing challenges or fears in their lives can find solace and strength through the understanding of higher truths and knowledge. The mantra serves as a reminder that by seeking wisdom and embracing the divine, one can achieve a state of fearlessness and transcend the limitations of the material world.
