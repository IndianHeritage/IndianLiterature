अध यच चारिते गणे शतम उष्ट्रां अचिक्रदत् |अध श्वित्नेषु विंशतिं शताः ||
adha yac cārathe gaṇe śatam uṣṭrām̐ acikradat | adha śvitneṣu viṁśatiṁ śatā ||
  • Mantra: "adha yac cārathe gaṇe śatam uṣṭrām̐ acikradat | adha śvitneṣu viṁśatiṁ śatā ||"
  • Translation: "Whatever you have done in the assembly, O hundred camels, may you accomplish it. In the white (sacred) places, may you achieve twenty (goals) out of a hundred."

  • Word Breakdown:
    • adha: below, or whatever
    • yac: that which
    • cārathe: you have done or performed
    • gaṇe: in the assembly or group
    • śatam: hundred
    • uṣṭrām̐: camels, symbolizing wealth and strength
    • acikradat: may you accomplish or achieve
    • śvitneṣu: in the white or sacred places
    • viṁśatiṁ: twenty
    • śatā: out of a hundred

  • Contextual Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of collective effort and the achievements that can arise from it.
    • The mention of "hundred camels" symbolizes abundance and prosperity, suggesting that through unity and collaboration, one can achieve great success.
    • The "white places" can be interpreted as sacred or pure environments where positive actions and intentions are nurtured.
    • In real life, this can be seen in team projects where the combined efforts of individuals lead to significant accomplishments, much like a group of people working together to achieve a common goal.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of collaboration and the potential for success when individuals come together with a shared purpose.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as in workplaces, community projects, or any situation where teamwork is essential.
    • By recognizing the value of each member's contribution, we can strive to achieve our goals more effectively, just as the mantra suggests achieving twenty out of a hundred through collective effort.
