मन्त्र: चक्रात्थे हि साध्रियङ् नाम भद्रं साध्रीचीनाऽ वृत्रहणा उता स्थः |ताव इन्द्राग्नī साध्रियञ्चा निषद्यā वृष्णः सोमस्य वृषणा वृषेथाम् ||
cakrāthe hi sadhrya1ṅ nāma bhadraṁ sadhrīcīnā vṛtrahaṇā uta sthaḥ | tāv indrāgnī sadhryañcā niṣadyā vṛṣṇaḥ somasya vṛṣaṇā vṛṣethām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • cakrāthe: In the context of the wheel or cycle, often symbolizing the cycle of life or the universe.
    • hi: Indeed or certainly, emphasizing the truth of the statement.
    • sadhryaṅ: Together or in harmony, indicating unity.
    • nāma: Name or identity, referring to the essence of something.
    • bhadraṁ: Auspicious or beneficial, suggesting positivity.
    • sadhrīcīnā: Those who are in harmony or aligned, indicating a collective effort.
    • vṛtrahaṇā: Slayer of Vritra, referring to Indra, the deity who overcomes obstacles.
    • uta: And or also, connecting ideas.
    • sthaḥ: Firm or stable, indicating strength.
    • tāv: Those, referring to the deities Indra and Agni.
    • indrāgnī: Indra and Agni, representing power and energy.
    • sadhryañcā: And in harmony, reinforcing the idea of unity.
    • niṣadyā: Sitting or residing, indicating a state of being.
    • vṛṣṇaḥ: Strong or powerful, often associated with strength and vitality.
    • somasya: Of Soma, a sacred drink symbolizing immortality and divine energy.
    • vṛṣaṇā: Of the strong or powerful, indicating robustness.
    • vṛṣethām: May we be strong or empowered, a wish for strength.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and harmony among individuals and deities.
    • It invokes the strength of Indra and Agni, who symbolize power and energy, to overcome obstacles (Vritra).
    • The mantra suggests that when people come together in harmony, they can achieve auspicious outcomes.
    • Real-life example: In a team setting, when members work together harmoniously, they can overcome challenges and achieve their goals, much like how Indra overcomes Vritra.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for unity and collective strength in facing challenges.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as teamwork in professional settings, community building, and even family dynamics.
    • By invoking the energies of Indra and Agni, individuals can find the strength to overcome personal and collective obstacles.
    • Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder that together, we can achieve greater things and create a positive impact in our lives and communities.
