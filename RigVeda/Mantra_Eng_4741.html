मन्त्र: एम् एनम् प्रत्येतन सोमभिः सोमपातमम् | अमात्रेभिर ऋजीषिणम् इन्द्रं सुतैभिर इन्दुभिः ||
em enam pratyetana somebhiḥ somapātamam | amatrebhir ṛjīṣiṇam indraṁ sutebhir indubhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • em enam: This phrase can be interpreted as "to him" or "to that one."
    • pratyetana: This means "to be invoked" or "to be called upon."
    • somebhiḥ: Refers to "with the soma," which is a sacred drink in Vedic rituals.
    • somapātamam: This translates to "the bestower of soma" or "the one who grants soma."
    • amatrebhir: This can be understood as "with the mothers" or "with the nurturing forces."
    • ṛjīṣiṇam: This means "the one who is righteous" or "the one who upholds truth."
    • indraṁ: Refers to "Indra," the king of gods and the god of rain and thunderstorms.
    • sutebhir: This means "with the offerings" or "with the libations."
    • indubhiḥ: Refers to "the moon" or "the lunar essence," often associated with nourishment and fertility.
  • Contextual Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a prayer invoking Indra, asking for his blessings and the nourishment that comes from the sacred soma.
    • The mention of "mothers" signifies the nurturing aspect of nature and the importance of feminine energy in creation and sustenance.
    • Indra, as a deity, represents strength, power, and the ability to overcome obstacles, making this mantra a call for support in challenging times.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of personal struggle, individuals may seek strength and guidance from a higher power, similar to how this mantra calls upon Indra for support.
    • During rituals or ceremonies, offerings made to deities can be seen as a way to connect with the divine, much like the offerings of soma in this mantra.
    • The nurturing aspect represented by "mothers" can be reflected in the care and support provided by family and community during difficult times.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of invoking divine support and the nurturing forces of nature in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of strength, nourishment, and the divine, encouraging individuals to seek help and support when needed.
    • In a broader sense, it highlights the significance of rituals and prayers in fostering a sense of community and connection to the divine, which can be applicable in various cultural and spiritual practices today.
