वाहिष्ठो वां हवानाṁ स्तोमो दूतो हुवन नराः |युवाभ्यां भूत्व अश्विनाः ||
vāhiṣṭho vāṁ havānāṁ stomo dūto huvan narā | yuvābhyām bhūtv aśvinā ||
  • vāhiṣṭho: This word translates to "the best" or "the most excellent." It signifies the highest quality or the most favorable aspect of something.
  • vāṁ: This is a pronoun meaning "you" in the plural form, indicating a collective or group.
  • havānāṁ: This term refers to "offerings" or "sacrifices," particularly in the context of rituals where offerings are made to deities.
  • stomo: This word means "praise" or "song," often used in the context of hymns or mantras that are recited to honor the divine.
  • dūto: This translates to "messenger," indicating a role of communication or delivery of messages, often between humans and the divine.
  • huvan: This term means "to invoke" or "to call upon," suggesting an act of reaching out to the divine or higher powers.
  • narā: This word means "men" or "humans," emphasizing the human aspect of the invocation.
  • yuvābhyām: This translates to "by the two young ones," referring to the divine twins, the Aśvins, who are often associated with health, vitality, and well-being.
  • bhūtv: This means "becoming" or "manifesting," indicating a transformation or realization of potential.
  • aśvinā: This refers to the Aśvins, the twin deities in Vedic mythology known for their healing powers and ability to bring prosperity and joy.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to the divine, specifically addressing the Aśvins, to bring forth the best offerings and praises from the collective. It emphasizes the role of humans as messengers who invoke the divine for blessings and well-being.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • Rituals and Ceremonies: During religious ceremonies, individuals can recite this mantra to seek blessings for health and prosperity.
  • Community Gatherings: In gatherings where collective efforts are made for a common cause, this mantra can serve as a reminder of unity and the importance of invoking higher powers for support.
  • Personal Reflection: Individuals can use this mantra in meditation or personal reflection to focus on their aspirations and the qualities they wish to embody.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its ability to connect individuals with the divine, fostering a sense of community and shared purpose. It serves as a reminder of the importance of invoking higher powers for guidance and support in our endeavors, whether in personal growth, community efforts, or spiritual practices.
