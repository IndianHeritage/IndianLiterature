अदिते मित्र वरुणोता मृळ यद वो वयं चक्रमा कच चिद आगः |उर्व अश्यां अभयं ज्योतिर इन्द्र मा नो दीर्घा अभि नशन तामिस्राः ||
adite mitra varuṇota mṛḻa yad vo vayaṁ cakṛmā kac cid āgaḥ | urv aśyām abhayaṁ jyotir indra mā no dīrghā abhi naśan tamisrāḥ ||
  • Adite: This term refers to the divine mother, often associated with the concept of the infinite or the unbounded. In real life, this can symbolize the nurturing aspect of nature or the universe that provides for all beings.
  • Mitra: Mitra is the deity of friendship and harmony. In our daily lives, this can be seen in the importance of building and maintaining relationships, whether in personal or professional settings.
  • Varuṇa: Varuṇa is the god of cosmic order and water. This can be related to the significance of maintaining balance in our lives, akin to how water sustains life and is essential for growth.
  • Mṛḻa: This word means to soothe or calm. In a real-life context, it can refer to the need for peace and tranquility in our often chaotic lives, emphasizing the importance of mental health and well-being.
  • Yad vo vayaṁ cakṛmā: This phrase translates to "what we have done for you." It highlights the idea of service and contribution to the community, reminding us of our responsibilities towards others.
  • Kac cid āgaḥ: This means "may we not be harmed." It reflects a universal desire for safety and protection, which is relevant in today's world where security is a major concern.
  • Urva aśyām: This translates to "the vast and beautiful." It can be interpreted as a call to appreciate the beauty in our surroundings and the importance of environmental conservation.
  • Abhayaṁ: Meaning "fearless," this word encourages us to face challenges with courage and resilience, a quality that is essential in overcoming obstacles in life.
  • Jyotir: This means "light" or "illumination." In a practical sense, it can symbolize knowledge and wisdom, urging us to seek enlightenment in our personal and professional journeys.
  • Indra: Indra is the king of gods and represents strength and power. This can be related to the importance of leadership and the ability to inspire others in various aspects of life.
  • Mā no dīrghā abhi naśan tamisrāḥ: This phrase translates to "may we not be consumed by darkness." It serves as a reminder to strive for positivity and hope, especially in difficult times.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message of seeking harmony, protection, and enlightenment in our lives. It emphasizes the importance of nurturing relationships, maintaining balance, and facing challenges with courage. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as fostering community spirit, promoting mental well-being, and encouraging environmental stewardship. Ultimately, it serves as a guiding principle for living a fulfilling and meaningful life, urging us to seek light and positivity amidst the darkness.
