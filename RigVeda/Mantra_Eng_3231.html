इन्द्रं कामा वसूयन्तो अग्मन स्वर्मील्हे न सवने चकानाḥ |श्रवस्यवः शशमानासा उक्थैर ओको न रण्वा सुदृशíva पुष्टिः ||
indraṁ kāmā vasūyanto agman svarmīḻhe na savane cakānāḥ | śravasyavaḥ śaśamānāsa ukthair oko na raṇvā sudṛśīva puṣṭiḥ ||
  • Indraṁ: Refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Kāmā: Means desires or wishes, indicating the aspirations and goals of individuals.
  • Vasūyanto: Refers to those who are generous or benevolent, highlighting the importance of kindness and giving.
  • Agman: Means to come or arrive, suggesting the arrival of blessings or good fortune.
  • Svarmīḻhe: Refers to the heavenly or divine realm, indicating a connection to the spiritual or higher consciousness.
  • Na savane: Means not in the act of sacrifice or offering, suggesting a different approach to receiving blessings.
  • Cakānāḥ: Refers to those who are wise or knowledgeable, emphasizing the value of wisdom in achieving desires.
  • Śravasyavaḥ: Means those who are renowned or famous, indicating the importance of reputation and recognition.
  • Śaśamānāsa: Refers to those who are peaceful or calm, highlighting the significance of tranquility in life.
  • Ukthair: Means through the power of words or speech, indicating the influence of communication.
  • Oko: Refers to the abode or home, suggesting a sense of belonging and stability.
  • Na raṇvā: Means not in conflict or strife, emphasizing the importance of harmony.
  • Sudṛśīva: Refers to the one who is auspicious or favorable, indicating the presence of good fortune.
  • Puṣṭiḥ: Means nourishment or prosperity, highlighting the importance of sustenance in life.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of desires, wisdom, and benevolence in achieving prosperity and peace in life. It suggests that by embodying qualities such as kindness, knowledge, and tranquility, individuals can attract blessings and create a harmonious environment. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in personal relationships, community interactions, and professional settings where collaboration and understanding lead to collective success and well-being. The mantra serves as a reminder that true strength lies not just in power, but in the ability to uplift others and foster a supportive community.
