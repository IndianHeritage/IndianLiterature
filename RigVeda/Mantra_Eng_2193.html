नूनं सा ते प्रति वरं जरीत्रे दूहीयद इन्द्र दक्षिणा मघोनि |शिक्षा स्तोत्रिभ्यो माती धग भगो नो बृहद वदेम विदथे सुवीराः ||
nūnaṁ sā te prati varaṁ jaritre duhīyad indra dakṣiṇā maghonī | śikṣā stotṛbhyo māti dhag bhago no bṛhad vadema vidathe suvīrāḥ ||
  • nūnaṁ sā te prati varaṁ: This phrase can be interpreted as "Indeed, that is your excellent gift." It suggests a recognition of the divine blessings that one receives.
  • jaritre duhīyad indra: Here, "Indra" refers to the king of gods in Vedic literature, and "jaritre" implies the act of nurturing or sustaining. This part emphasizes the importance of divine support in our endeavors.
  • dakṣiṇā maghonī: "Dakṣiṇā" means a gift or offering, often given to priests or sages, while "maghonī" refers to wealth or abundance. This indicates that offerings made with sincerity can lead to prosperity.
  • śikṣā stotṛbhyo: "Śikṣā" means instruction or learning, and "stotṛbhyo" refers to those who praise or chant. This highlights the value of education and the power of words in uplifting oneself and others.
  • māti dhag bhago: "Māti" signifies wisdom or intellect, while "bhago" refers to wealth or fortune. This suggests that true wealth comes from wisdom and understanding.
  • no bṛhad vadema: "No" means "we," and "bṛhad" translates to "great." This part expresses a collective aspiration for greatness and the pursuit of higher ideals.
  • vidathe suvīrāḥ: "Vidathe" refers to a gathering or assembly, and "suvīrāḥ" means "brave" or "valiant." This emphasizes the importance of community and collaboration in achieving noble goals.

  • Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings, the importance of wisdom, and the value of community. In real life, it can be applied in various scenarios such as:
    • In personal development, recognizing the need for guidance and support from mentors or spiritual leaders.
    • In professional settings, understanding that collaboration and teamwork can lead to greater achievements.
    • In community service, emphasizing the importance of giving back and sharing resources for collective prosperity.
  • Overall, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek wisdom, offer gratitude, and work together towards common goals, fostering a sense of unity and purpose in our lives.
