त्यं चिदात्रिम ऋतजुरम् अर्थम् अश्वं न यातवे |कक्षीवन्तं यदī पुनः रथं न कृतुथो नवम् ||
tyaṁ cid atrim ṛtajuram artham aśvaṁ na yātave | kakṣīvantaṁ yadī punā rathaṁ na kṛṇutho navam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tyaṁ: that
    • cid: indeed
    • atram: here
    • ṛtajuram: the one who is truthful
    • artham: for the purpose
    • aśvaṁ: horse
    • na: not
    • yātave: to be sacrificed
    • kakṣīvantaṁ: the one who is strong
    • yadī: if
    • punā: again
    • rathaṁ: chariot
    • na: not
    • kṛṇutho: you should make
    • navam: new
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the importance of truth and strength in our actions.
    • The phrase "ṛtajuram artham" emphasizes that truthfulness is essential for achieving one's goals.
    • The mention of "aśvaṁ" (horse) and "rathaṁ" (chariot) symbolizes the tools and resources we use in our journey of life.
    • It suggests that one should not sacrifice their integrity ("na yātave") for the sake of material gains.
    • The idea of not creating a "new chariot" if the existing one is strong implies the value of utilizing what we have effectively rather than seeking new things unnecessarily.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a manager may face pressure to manipulate data for better results. This mantra reminds them to uphold honesty and integrity instead of compromising their values.
    • In personal relationships, being truthful and strong in character fosters trust and respect, which are essential for lasting connections.
    • In education, students are encouraged to value knowledge and truth over grades, understanding that true learning is more important than merely achieving high scores.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of truth and strength in our endeavors.
    • It serves as a reminder to maintain integrity and utilize our existing resources wisely rather than seeking new paths that may lead us away from our core values.
    • In a world where shortcuts and dishonesty can seem tempting, this mantra encourages individuals to stay true to themselves and their principles, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and meaningful life.
