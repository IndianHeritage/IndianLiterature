युवां ह घोषा पर्य अश्विना यती राजा ऊचे दुषिता पृच्छे वां नराः |भूतं मे अह्न उता भूतं अक्तवे अश्वावते रथिने शक्तं अर्वते ||
yuvāṁ ha ghoṣā pary aśvinā yatī rājña ūce duhitā pṛcche vāṁ narā | bhūtam me ahna uta bhūtam aktave 'śvāvate rathine śaktam arvate ||
  • yuvāṁ ha ghoṣā pary aśvinā: This phrase can be translated as "You two, O Ashvins, are the ones who are called upon." The Ashvins are twin deities in Vedic mythology, often associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize the duality of life and the balance between opposites.
  • yatī rājña ūce duhitā: This translates to "The daughter of the king speaks." Here, the 'daughter of the king' can represent someone of high status or authority, indicating that the message or prayer is significant and worthy of attention.
  • pṛcche vāṁ narā: This means "I ask you, O men." It signifies a plea or inquiry directed towards the Ashvins, emphasizing the importance of communication and seeking guidance from higher powers.
  • bhūtam me ahna uta bhūtam: This can be interpreted as "What has been, I call upon you." It reflects a connection to the past and the acknowledgment of ancestral wisdom, suggesting that understanding history is crucial for present and future actions.
  • aktave 'śvāvate rathine: This phrase translates to "For the one who is active and powerful." It highlights the importance of action and strength in achieving one's goals, resonating with the idea that effort and determination lead to success.
  • śaktam arvate: This means "The one who is capable and strong." It reinforces the theme of empowerment and the belief that individuals possess the strength to overcome challenges and achieve their desires.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking guidance from divine forces, acknowledging the wisdom of the past, and recognizing one's own strength and capabilities. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as leadership, personal development, and health. For instance, a leader may invoke the wisdom of their predecessors while making decisions, or an individual may draw upon their inner strength to overcome personal challenges. The mantra serves as a reminder that by connecting with higher powers and understanding our own potential, we can navigate life's complexities with confidence and purpose.
