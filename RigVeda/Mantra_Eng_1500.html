धन्वञ्चिद्ये अनाशवो जीराश्चिदगिरौकसः |सूर्यस्येव रश्मयो दुरनियन्तवो हस्तयोर्दुरनियन्तवः ||
dhanvañ cid ye anāśavo jīrāś cid agiraukasaḥ | sūryasyeva raśmayo durniyantavo hastayor durniyantavaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "dhanvañ cid ye anāśavo jīrāś cid agiraukasaḥ | sūryasyeva raśmayo durniyantavo hastayor durniyantavaḥ ||"
  • Translation: "Those who are like the arrows of the sun, unyielding and powerful, are indeed the ones who are not subject to decay or destruction."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • dhanvañ: Refers to arrows or weapons, symbolizing strength and precision.
    • cid: Indicates a state of being or existence.
    • ye: Those who.
    • anāśavo: Unyielding or indestructible.
    • jīrāś: Refers to decay or deterioration.
    • agiraukasaḥ: Powerful or strong, often associated with divine energy.
    • sūryasyeva: Like the sun.
    • raśmayo: Rays or beams, symbolizing light and energy.
    • durniyantavo: Difficult to control or restrain.
    • hastayor: Of the hands.
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the qualities of strength, resilience, and the ability to overcome challenges.
    • The comparison to arrows and the sun illustrates the idea of focused energy and unstoppable force.
    • In real life, this can be seen in individuals who face adversity but remain steadfast, much like a warrior who does not falter in battle.
    • For example, consider a student who struggles academically but continues to work hard and eventually excels. Their determination mirrors the unyielding nature of the arrows described in the mantra.
  • Significance:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of resilience and the importance of maintaining focus in the face of challenges.
    • It encourages individuals to harness their inner strength and to be like the rays of the sun—radiant and unstoppable.
    • In practical terms, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, professional challenges, or even in social movements where perseverance is key to success.
