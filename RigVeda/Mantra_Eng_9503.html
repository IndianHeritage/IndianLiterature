समुद्रः सिन्धू रजो अन्तरिक्षम् अज एकपात तनयित्नुर अर्णवः |अहिर बुद्धनः श्रिणवद वचांसि मे विश्वे देवासा उता सूरयो मम ||
samudraḥ sindhū rajo antarikṣam aja ekapāt tanayitnur arṇavaḥ | ahir budhnyaḥ śṛṇavad vacāṁsi me viśve devāsa uta sūrayo mama ||
  • samudraḥ sindhū rajo antarikṣam: This phrase refers to the vastness of the ocean (samudra) and rivers (sindhu), as well as the space (antarikṣa) that exists between them. It symbolizes the interconnectedness of all elements in nature.
  • aja ekapāt tanayitnur arṇavaḥ: Here, "aja" means unborn or eternal, "ekapāt" refers to the single path or direction, and "tanayitnur arṇavaḥ" can be interpreted as the offspring or creation of the ocean. This suggests a divine source that is both singular and eternal, emphasizing unity in diversity.
  • ahir budhnyaḥ: This term can be translated as "the serpent of the deep" or "the great cosmic serpent," which is often associated with the primordial waters and the cycle of creation and destruction.
  • śṛṇavad vacāṁsi me: This means "listen to my words" or "pay attention to my speech." It indicates a call for attention and understanding, suggesting that the speaker has important insights to share.
  • viśve devāsa uta sūrayo mama: This phrase translates to "all the gods and divine beings are with me." It signifies a connection to the divine and the support of higher powers in one's endeavors.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of unity, creation, and divine support. It highlights the importance of recognizing the interconnectedness of all elements in the universe, from the vast oceans to the cosmic forces. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as environmental conservation, where understanding the relationship between water bodies and ecosystems is crucial. It also serves as a reminder to seek guidance from higher powers in our pursuits, fostering a sense of community and support. Ultimately, this mantra encourages us to listen to the wisdom of the universe and act in harmony with it.
