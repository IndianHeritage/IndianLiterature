बृहस्पतिः प्रथमं जायमानो महो ज्योतिषः परम व्योमन् |सप्ताश्यस तुविजातो रवेण वि सप्तरश्मिर अधमत तमांसि ||
bṛhaspatiḥ prathamaṁ jāyamāno maho jyotiṣaḥ parame vyoman | saptāsyas tuvijāto raveṇa vi saptaraśmir adhamat tamāṁsi ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • bṛhaspatiḥ: Refers to the deity Brihaspati, the god of wisdom and eloquence.
    • prathamaṁ: First or foremost.
    • jāyamāno: Being born or emerging.
    • maho: Great or vast.
    • jyotiṣaḥ: Light or illumination.
    • parame: Supreme or highest.
    • vyoman: In the sky or the celestial realm.
    • saptāsyas: Of the seven (often refers to the seven sages or stars).
    • tuvijāto: Born or generated from you.
    • raveṇa: By the sun or sunlight.
    • vi: With or through.
    • saptaraśmir: The seven rays (often associated with the sun).
    • adhamat: Overcoming or dispelling.
    • tamāṁsi: Darkness or ignorance.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the emergence of Brihaspati, the embodiment of wisdom and knowledge, as the first light in the vast celestial space.
    • It highlights the importance of illumination, both literal and metaphorical, in dispelling darkness, which can be interpreted as ignorance or confusion.
    • The reference to the "seven rays" can symbolize the various aspects of knowledge and enlightenment that come from the divine source.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In education, the pursuit of knowledge can be seen as a quest for light, where teachers (like Brihaspati) guide students out of ignorance.
    • In personal development, individuals often seek wisdom through experiences, books, and mentors, striving to overcome their own darkness or challenges.
    • In a broader societal context, movements that promote awareness and education aim to dispel ignorance and bring about enlightenment, akin to the rays of the sun illuminating the world.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of wisdom and knowledge in overcoming ignorance and darkness in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek enlightenment and understanding, whether through education, personal growth, or spiritual practices.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals and societies to strive for clarity and insight, fostering a more informed and enlightened world.
