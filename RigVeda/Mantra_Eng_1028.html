एह देवाः मयोभुवा दसराः हिरण्यवर्तनी |उषर्बुधो वहन्तु सोमपीतये ||
eha devā mayobhuvā dasrā hiraṇyavartanī | uṣarbudho vahantu somapītaye ||
  • Mantra: "eha devā mayobhuvā dasrā hiraṇyavartanī | uṣarbudho vahantu somapītaye ||"
  • Translation: "O divine beings, may you who are endowed with brilliance and who travel on the golden path, bring us the essence of the soma drink."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • eha: Here, in this world.
    • devā: Divine beings or gods.
    • mayobhuvā: Endowed with brilliance or radiance.
    • dasrā: Those who travel or move.
    • hiraṇyavartanī: Golden path or the path of prosperity.
    • uṣarbudho: Those who are awakened or enlightened.
    • vahantu: May they bring or carry.
    • somapītaye: For the essence of the soma drink, which symbolizes nourishment and vitality.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The invocation of divine beings can be seen in various cultures where people seek blessings for prosperity and well-being, such as during festivals or rituals.
    • The concept of a "golden path" can be related to the pursuit of ethical living and making choices that lead to personal and communal growth, similar to the idea of following a moral compass in everyday decisions.
    • The soma drink symbolizes nourishment, akin to how people today seek healthy food and drink to enhance their vitality and well-being, reflecting a modern understanding of health and wellness.

  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of seeking divine guidance and blessings in our lives, particularly in our pursuits of prosperity and well-being.
    • It highlights the significance of enlightenment and ethical living, encouraging individuals to follow a path that leads to personal and collective growth.
    • In contemporary life, the mantra serves as a reminder to seek nourishment not just physically, but also spiritually and ethically, fostering a holistic approach to well-being.
