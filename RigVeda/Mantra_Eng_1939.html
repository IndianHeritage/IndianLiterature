उभा शंसा नार्यां माम अविष्टाम उभे माम ऊती अवसा सचेताम् |भूरी चिद आर्यः सुदास्तरायेषा मदन्त इषयेम देवाः ||
ubhā śaṁsā naryā mām aviṣṭām ubhe mām ūtī avasā sacetām | bhūri cid aryaḥ sudāstarāyeṣā madanta iṣayema devāḥ ||
  • Ubhā: This word translates to "both" or "together." It signifies unity and the duality of existence.
  • Śaṁsā: This means "praise" or "glory." It reflects the importance of acknowledging and celebrating virtues.
  • Naryā: This refers to "woman" or "female." It highlights the role of women in society and their contributions.
  • Mām: This means "me" or "myself," indicating a personal connection or appeal.
  • Aviṣṭām: This translates to "not disturbed" or "unshaken," suggesting stability and resilience.
  • Ubhē: Again, "both," reinforcing the theme of duality.
  • Mām ūtī: This means "my strength" or "my support," emphasizing the need for assistance and empowerment.
  • Avasā: This translates to "in the presence of" or "with," indicating a sense of community and togetherness.
  • Sacetām: This means "of the mind" or "intellect," pointing to the importance of wisdom and awareness.
  • Bhūri: This translates to "abundant" or "plentiful," suggesting richness in experience or resources.
  • Cid: This means "consciousness" or "awareness," highlighting the significance of being aware and mindful.
  • Aryaḥ: This refers to "noble" or "honorable," indicating the value of integrity and virtue.
  • Sudāstarāyeṣā: This can be interpreted as "to the one who is well-versed" or "to the knowledgeable," emphasizing the importance of wisdom.
  • Madanta: This means "to be joyful" or "to rejoice," reflecting the importance of happiness and positivity.
  • Iṣayema: This translates to "we may attain" or "we may achieve," indicating aspiration and ambition.
  • Devāḥ: This means "gods" or "divine beings," suggesting a connection to the spiritual or higher powers.

The mantra emphasizes the importance of unity, strength, and wisdom in both personal and communal contexts. It acknowledges the role of women and the need for support and resilience in the face of challenges. The call for joy and aspiration reflects a desire for a fulfilling and meaningful life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Empowerment of Women: Recognizing and celebrating the contributions of women in society, promoting gender equality, and supporting women's rights.
  • Community Support: Encouraging individuals to come together to support one another, fostering a sense of belonging and collective strength.
  • Mindfulness and Awareness: Promoting the importance of being aware of one's thoughts and actions, leading to better decision-making and personal growth.
  • Joy and Positivity: Cultivating a positive outlook on life, encouraging individuals to find joy in their experiences and share that joy with others.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the importance of supporting one another in the pursuit of wisdom, strength, and joy. It encourages a holistic approach to life, where personal growth and community well-being go hand in hand.
