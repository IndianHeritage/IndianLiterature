त्वं राजेवा सुव्रतो गिरः सोमाऽविवेशिथ || पुनानो वह्ने अद्भुत ||
tvaṁ rājeva suvrato giraḥ somā viveśitha | punāno vahne adbhuta ||
  • tvaṁ - This word translates to "you," indicating a direct address to a divine entity or a higher power.
  • rājeva - This means "like a king" or "as a ruler," suggesting authority, power, and governance.
  • suvrato - This translates to "one who has good vows" or "with good intentions," emphasizing the importance of righteous conduct and moral integrity.
  • giraḥ - This means "words" or "speech," indicating the significance of communication and expression in the context of divine interaction.
  • somā - Referring to "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic tradition, symbolizing nourishment, vitality, and spiritual enlightenment.
  • viveśitha - This translates to "you have entered," suggesting the presence of the divine in the physical or spiritual realm.
  • punāno - This means "renewing" or "revitalizing," indicating a process of rejuvenation or transformation.
  • vahne - This translates to "fire," symbolizing purification, energy, and the transformative power of the divine.
  • adbhuta - This means "wonderful" or "extraordinary," highlighting the miraculous nature of divine actions and presence.

The mantra can be interpreted as a call to a divine presence that embodies the qualities of a ruler with good intentions, emphasizing the importance of righteous speech and the transformative power of divine energy. It suggests that the divine has entered the realm of existence, bringing renewal and extraordinary qualities akin to fire.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Leadership: Just as a king should lead with integrity and good intentions, individuals in positions of authority can reflect on their responsibilities to govern wisely and justly.
  • Communication: The emphasis on words reminds us of the power of speech in our daily interactions. Practicing mindful communication can lead to better relationships and understanding.
  • Spiritual Growth: The reference to Soma and renewal encourages individuals to seek spiritual nourishment and transformation in their lives, whether through meditation, prayer, or other practices.
  • Personal Development: The idea of rejuvenation can inspire people to embrace change and seek personal growth, much like the transformative power of fire that purifies and revitalizes.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the qualities of good leadership, the importance of righteous speech, and the potential for personal and spiritual renewal. It encourages individuals to embody these principles in their lives, fostering a sense of responsibility, integrity, and transformation.
