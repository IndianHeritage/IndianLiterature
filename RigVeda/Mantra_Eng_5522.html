उतेदानीं भगवन्तः स्यामोता प्रपित्वा उता मध्य अह्नाम् |उतोदिता मगवन् सूर्यस्य वयं देवानां सुमतौ स्याम ||
utedānīm bhagavantaḥ syāmota prapitva uta madhye ahnām | utoditā maghavan sūryasya vayaṁ devānāṁ sumatau syāma ||
  • Utedānīm: This word can be interpreted as "now" or "at this moment." It signifies the present time, emphasizing the importance of the current moment in spiritual practices.
  • Bhagavantaḥ: This term refers to the "divine" or "blessed one," often used to denote deities or revered figures in Hinduism. It highlights the connection to the divine presence.
  • Syāmota: This can be translated as "may we be" or "let us become," indicating a desire for transformation or attainment of a certain state.
  • Prapitva: This word means "to attain" or "to achieve." It suggests a goal or aspiration that one seeks to reach.
  • Madhye: Meaning "in the midst" or "among," this word emphasizes the importance of community and collective experience.
  • Ahnām: This refers to "of the day" or "of the light," symbolizing clarity, knowledge, and enlightenment.
  • Utoditā: This means "having risen" or "having emerged," often associated with the rising sun, symbolizing new beginnings and hope.
  • Maghavan: This term refers to "the bountiful one" or "the generous one," often associated with the deity of wealth and abundance.
  • Sūryasya: This means "of the sun," representing vitality, energy, and the source of life.
  • Vayaṁ: This translates to "we" or "us," indicating a collective identity and shared purpose.
  • Devānāṁ: This means "of the gods," suggesting a connection to the divine and the higher powers that guide and protect.
  • Sumatau: This can be interpreted as "with good thoughts" or "with auspicious intentions," emphasizing the importance of positive thinking and intentions in one's actions.
  • Syāma: This means "may we be" or "let us become," reinforcing the desire for transformation and alignment with divine qualities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound aspiration for spiritual growth and connection with the divine. It emphasizes the importance of the present moment, collective identity, and positive intentions. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during meditation, group prayers, or community gatherings, where individuals come together to seek enlightenment, abundance, and divine blessings. By focusing on these principles, one can cultivate a harmonious and fulfilling life, aligning with the energies of the universe and fostering a sense of unity with others.
