ऋधग इत्त्था स मार्त्यः शशमे देवतातये |यो नूनं मित्रावरुणाव अभिष्टय आचक्रे हव्यदातये ||
ṛdhag itthā sa martyaḥ śaśame devatātaye | yo nūnam mitrāvaruṇāv abhiṣṭaya ācakre havyadātaye ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ṛdhag: Prosperous, flourishing
    • itthā: Thus, in this manner
    • sa: He, that person
    • martyaḥ: Mortal, human being
    • śaśame: May attain, may achieve
    • devatātaye: To the divine, to the gods
    • yo: Who
    • nūnam: Certainly, indeed
    • mitrāvaruṇāv: To Mitra and Varuna (the deities of friendship and cosmic order)
    • abhiṣṭaya: Offerings, prayers
    • ācakre: Has made, has performed
    • havyadātaye: For the purpose of offering, for the sake of sacrifice
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about a mortal who, through their actions and offerings, seeks to achieve prosperity and divine favor.
    • The reference to Mitra and Varuna highlights the importance of maintaining harmony and order in one's life and relationships.
    • The act of making offerings signifies the importance of gratitude and recognition of the divine forces that govern existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a leader who acknowledges the contributions of their team and fosters a collaborative environment can be seen as embodying the principles of this mantra.
    • In personal relationships, individuals who practice gratitude and make efforts to maintain harmony with friends and family reflect the essence of this mantra.
    • During festivals or rituals, making offerings to deities can be a way for individuals to express their hopes for prosperity and well-being, aligning with the mantra's message.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of human actions in achieving prosperity and divine blessings.
    • It teaches the importance of maintaining harmonious relationships and recognizing the divine forces in our lives.
    • In real life, applying the principles of gratitude, harmony, and acknowledgment can lead to a more fulfilling and prosperous existence.
