पुरु हि वाम पुरुभुजां देश्णं धेनुं न इषम् पिन्वतम असक्राम् |स्तुतश्च वाम माध्वी सुष्टुतिश्च रसाश्च ये वाम अनु रातिम अग्मन ||
puru hi vām purubhujā deṣṇaṁ dhenuṁ na iṣam pinvatam asakrām | stutaś ca vām mādhvī suṣṭutiś ca rasāś ca ye vām anu rātim agman ||
  • Meaning of the Mantra:
    • Puru hi vām: "Indeed, you are the many." This phrase acknowledges the multiplicity and diversity of existence, suggesting that the divine or the ultimate reality encompasses all forms and beings.
    • Purubhujā: "You who enjoy many forms." This indicates the ability of the divine to manifest in various forms and experiences, highlighting the richness of life.
    • Deṣṇaṁ dhenuṁ: "The cow of plenty." In Vedic culture, the cow symbolizes abundance and nourishment, representing the sustenance provided by nature.
    • Na iṣam pinvatam asakrām: "May we not be deprived of your blessings." This line expresses a prayer for the continuous flow of divine grace and abundance in our lives.
    • Stutaś ca vām: "And praised are you." This acknowledges the importance of reverence and gratitude towards the divine.
    • Mādhvī suṣṭutiś ca: "Sweet praise and good words." This emphasizes the power of positive speech and the impact of kind words in our lives.
    • Rasāś ca ye vām anu rātim agman: "And those who follow you with joy." This suggests that those who align themselves with the divine will experience joy and fulfillment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The concept of "Puru hi vām" can be seen in the diversity of cultures and communities around the world. Each culture contributes to the richness of human experience, reminding us that unity in diversity is essential.
    • The "cow of plenty" can be related to sustainable farming practices today, where respect for nature and its resources leads to abundance and nourishment for all.
    • The prayer for blessings ("Na iṣam pinvatam asakrām") resonates with individuals seeking guidance and support in challenging times, such as during economic hardships or personal crises.
    • In modern contexts, "Mādhvī suṣṭutiś ca" highlights the importance of positive communication in relationships, workplaces, and communities, fostering an environment of support and encouragement.
    • The idea of following the divine with joy can be reflected in the pursuit of passions and vocations that align with one's values, leading to a fulfilling and joyful life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of interconnectedness, abundance, and the power of positive expression. It serves as a reminder to appreciate the diversity of life, seek blessings, and communicate with kindness.
    • In real-life scenarios, the teachings of this mantra can be applied in personal development, community building, and fostering a harmonious relationship with nature and each other.
    • Ultimately, it encourages individuals to recognize the divine presence in all aspects of life and to cultivate joy and gratitude in their daily experiences.
