यस्मिन विश्वानि भुवनानि तस्थुः तिस्रो द्यावस त्रेधा सस्रुर आपः |त्रयः कोशास उपसेचनासो मध्वः श्चोतन्त्य अभितो विरप्शम् ||
yasmin viśvāni bhuvanāni tasthus tisro dyāvas tredhā sasrur āpaḥ | trayaḥ kośāsa upasecanāso madhvaḥ ścotanty abhito virapśam ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yasmin: in which
    • viśvāni: all
    • bhuvanāni: worlds
    • tasthus: are established
    • tisro: three
    • dyāvas: heavens
    • tredhā: in three ways
    • sasrur: have flowed
    • āpaḥ: waters
    • trayaḥ: three
    • kośāsa: sheaths
    • upasecanāso: nourishing
    • madhvaḥ: sweet
    • ścotanty: flow
    • abhito: around
    • virapśam: the vastness
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a cosmic unity where all worlds are established within a singular essence.
    • The mention of "three heavens" can be interpreted as the physical, mental, and spiritual realms that coexist.
    • The flowing waters symbolize the life force and nourishment that sustains all beings.
    • The "three sheaths" refer to the layers of existence—physical, mental, and spiritual—that envelop the soul.
    • The sweetness of life and experiences is highlighted, suggesting that despite challenges, there is beauty and nourishment in existence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family, the three realms can be seen as the physical home, the emotional bonds, and the spiritual values that hold the family together.
    • In a community, the interconnectedness of individuals can be likened to the flowing waters that nourish relationships and foster growth.
    • In personal development, recognizing the three sheaths can help individuals understand their physical needs, emotional health, and spiritual aspirations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the interconnectedness of all existence and the importance of nurturing all aspects of life.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek balance between the physical, mental, and spiritual realms in our daily lives.
    • By acknowledging the sweetness and nourishment in our experiences, we can cultivate a deeper appreciation for life and its myriad forms.
    • In practical terms, this mantra can inspire individuals to create harmony in their relationships, communities, and personal journeys.
