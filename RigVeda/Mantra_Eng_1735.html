द्वा सुपर्णा सयुजा सखायाः समानं वृक्षं परिषस्वजाते |तयोः अन्यः पिप्पलं स्वाद्वत्त्य अनशनन्न अन्यो अभि चाकाशीति ||
dvā suparṇā sayujā sakhāyā samānaṁ vṛkṣam pari ṣasvajāte | tayor anyaḥ pippalaṁ svādv atty anaśnann anyo abhi cākaśīti ||
  • dvā suparṇā: This phrase translates to "two beautiful birds." In a metaphorical sense, these birds can represent the duality of existence, such as the physical and spiritual realms or the mind and body.
  • sayujā sakhāyā: This means "joined together as friends." It signifies the interconnectedness of these two aspects of life, suggesting that they coexist and support each other.
  • samānaṁ vṛkṣam pari ṣasvajāte: This translates to "they are perched on the same tree." The tree symbolizes life or the universe, indicating that both aspects of existence are part of a larger whole.
  • tayor anyaḥ pippalaṁ svādv atty: This means "one of them eats the sweet fruit of the fig tree." This can be interpreted as the soul or the higher self enjoying the fruits of life, which represents knowledge, wisdom, and spiritual fulfillment.
  • anaśnann anyo abhi cākaśīti: This translates to "the other does not eat, but watches." This suggests that the other aspect, possibly the material or ego-driven self, remains detached and observant, indicating a state of awareness.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of duality in life, emphasizing the relationship between the material and spiritual aspects of existence. It encourages individuals to recognize the interplay between their physical desires and spiritual aspirations.
  • Real-life Application: In daily life, this mantra can be applied to the practice of mindfulness and self-awareness. For instance, when faced with choices, one can reflect on whether they are acting out of material desire or seeking deeper fulfillment. This reflection can lead to more conscious decision-making and a balanced life.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of balancing the material and spiritual aspects of life. By understanding the relationship between the two, individuals can strive for a harmonious existence, leading to greater peace and fulfillment. It encourages a journey of self-discovery, where one learns to enjoy the fruits of life while remaining aware of the deeper truths that govern existence.
