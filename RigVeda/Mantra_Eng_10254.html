अप प्राच इन्द्र विश्वाम् अमित्रान अपापाचो अभिभूते नुदस्व |अपोदिचो अप शूराधराच उरौ यथा तव शरमन मदेम ||
apa prāca indra viśvām̐ amitrān apāpāco abhibhūte nudasva | apodīco apa śūrādharāca urau yathā tava śarman madema ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • apa: away from
    • prāca: the east
    • indra: O Indra (the king of gods)
    • viśvām̐: all
    • amitrān: from enemies
    • apāpāco: from the unworthy
    • abhibhūte: having overcome
    • nudasva: drive away
    • apodīco: from the waters
    • apa: away from
    • śūrādharāca: from the brave
    • urau: in the heights
    • yathā: as
    • tava: your
    • śarman: protection
    • madema: may we attain
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Indra, seeking protection and the removal of obstacles and enemies. It emphasizes the desire to be free from negativity and to attain safety and security.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, individuals may face competition or challenges from colleagues. This mantra can be invoked to seek strength and clarity to overcome such challenges.
    • In personal life, one may encounter toxic relationships or negative influences. The essence of this mantra encourages individuals to distance themselves from such negativity and seek a supportive environment.
    • During times of crisis, such as natural disasters or personal hardships, this mantra serves as a reminder to seek divine protection and guidance to navigate through difficulties.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine intervention to remove obstacles and provide protection. It is applicable in various real-life scenarios where individuals seek strength against adversities, whether in personal relationships, professional challenges, or during crises. By invoking this mantra, one can cultivate a mindset of resilience and hope, fostering a sense of security and well-being in their lives.
