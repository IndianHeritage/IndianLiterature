गोवित पवस्व वसुविद धीरण्यविद रेतोधा इन्दो भुवनेष्व अर्पितः |त्वं सुवीरोऽसि सोम विश्ववित तं त्वा विप्राः उप गिरेम आसते ||
govit pavasva vasuvid dhiraṇyavid retodhā indo bhuvaneṣv arpitaḥ | tvaṁ suvīro asi soma viśvavit taṁ tvā viprā upa girema āsate ||
  • govit pavasva: This phrase can be interpreted as a call to the divine for guidance and support. "Govit" refers to the protector or guardian, while "pavasva" means to purify or cleanse. In real life, this can be seen as seeking help from a mentor or a leader who can guide us through challenges.
  • vasuvid dhiraṇyavid: "Vasuvid" refers to the one who knows the essence of wealth or prosperity, and "dhiraṇyavid" means the one who understands the nature of gold or valuable things. This can relate to individuals who are wise in financial matters, such as financial advisors or successful entrepreneurs who understand how to create and manage wealth.
  • retodhā indo: "Retodhā" signifies the one who brings forth energy or vitality, while "indo" refers to the moon or a source of nourishment. This can be likened to the importance of maintaining our physical and mental health, drawing parallels to how the moon influences tides and cycles in nature, reminding us of the need for balance in our lives.
  • bhuvaneṣv arpitaḥ: This phrase means "offered in the worlds." It signifies the act of dedicating one's efforts and achievements to a higher purpose or the greater good. In a real-life context, this can be seen in charitable acts or community service, where individuals contribute their time and resources for the benefit of others.
  • tvaṁ suvīro asi: "Tvaṁ" means "you," and "suvīro" translates to "you are the brave one." This can be interpreted as recognizing the strength and courage within oneself or in others. In everyday life, this can be applied to situations where individuals face fears or challenges, such as public speaking or taking on new responsibilities.
  • soma viśvavit: "Soma" refers to the divine nectar or essence, while "viśvavit" means the one who knows everything. This can symbolize the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom, akin to lifelong learning and the quest for understanding in various fields, such as science, philosophy, or spirituality.
  • taṁ tvā viprā upa girema āsate: This phrase translates to "we, the wise ones, sing your praises." It emphasizes the importance of acknowledging and honoring those who possess wisdom and knowledge. In real life, this can be seen in the respect given to teachers, leaders, and mentors who guide us on our paths.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking guidance, understanding wealth, maintaining vitality, dedicating efforts for the greater good, recognizing inner strength, pursuing knowledge, and honoring wisdom. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, encouraging individuals to strive for personal growth, contribute positively to society, and respect the wisdom of others. By embodying these principles, one can lead a fulfilling and purposeful life.
