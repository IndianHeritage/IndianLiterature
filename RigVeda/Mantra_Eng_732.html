ते जज्ञिरे दिव ऋष्वासा उक्षणो रुद्रस्य मर्याऽ असुराऽरेपसः |पावकासः शुचयः सूर्याऽ इव सत्त्वानो न द्रप्सिनो घोरवर्पसः ||
te jajñire diva ṛṣvāsa ukṣaṇo rudrasya maryā asurā arepasaḥ | pāvakāsaḥ śucayaḥ sūryā iva satvāno na drapsino ghoravarpasaḥ ||
  • te jajñire: This phrase translates to "they were born" or "they came into existence." It signifies the emergence of beings or entities.
  • diva: Meaning "in the heavens" or "of the sky," this word connects the mantra to celestial or divine realms.
  • ṛṣvāsa: This term can be interpreted as "with strength" or "powerful," indicating the vigor and vitality of the beings mentioned.
  • ukṣaṇo: This word means "of the Rudra," referring to a form of the divine associated with storms and transformation.
  • rudrasya: This is a possessive form meaning "of Rudra," emphasizing the connection to this powerful deity.
  • maryā: This term can mean "the mortal" or "the human," suggesting a link between the divine and human realms.
  • asurā: Referring to "the non-divine" or "the demonic," this word contrasts with the divine beings, highlighting the duality in existence.
  • arepasaḥ: This can be interpreted as "those who are far away," indicating a distance or separation from the divine.
  • pāvakāsaḥ: Meaning "those who are pure" or "the purifiers," this term emphasizes the importance of purity in spiritual practice.
  • śucayaḥ: This translates to "the clean" or "the sacred," reinforcing the theme of purity and sanctity.
  • sūryā iva: This phrase means "like the sun," drawing a parallel between the beings and the sun, a symbol of light, energy, and life.
  • satvāno: This term refers to "those who possess essence" or "the vital," indicating a deep connection to life force.
  • na drapsino: Meaning "not of the weak," this phrase emphasizes strength and resilience.
  • ghoravarpasaḥ: This can be interpreted as "those with terrible forms," suggesting a powerful and awe-inspiring presence.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the essence of creation, the interplay between the divine and the mortal, and the importance of purity and strength. It highlights the emergence of powerful beings in the celestial realm, drawing a connection to the sun as a source of life and energy. In real life, this mantra can be applied to the understanding of our own existence, the importance of maintaining purity in thoughts and actions, and recognizing the strength within ourselves to overcome challenges. It serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives and the potential for transformation and growth.
