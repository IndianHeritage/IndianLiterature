तस्य वयं सुमतौ यज्ञियस्यापि भद्रे सुमनसे स्याम |स सुत्रामां स्ववाम् इन्द्रो अस्मे आराच चिद् द्वेषः सन्तर युयोतु ||
tasya vayaṁ sumatau yajñiyasyāpi bhadre saumanase syāma | sa sutrāmā svavām̐ indro asme ārāc cid dveṣaḥ sanutar yuyotu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tasya: of that
    • vayaṁ: we
    • sumatau: with good thoughts
    • yajñiyasyāpi: of the one who is worthy of worship
    • bhadre: auspicious
    • saumanase: with a pleasant mind
    • syāma: may we be
    • sa: he
    • sutramā: with good connections
    • svavām̐: our own
    • indro: Indra (the king of gods)
    • asme: to us
    • ārāc: may he come
    • cid: indeed
    • dveṣaḥ: enmity
    • sanutar: may he be far away
    • yuyotu: may he fight
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra expresses a prayer for unity and harmony among individuals.
    • It emphasizes the importance of good thoughts and auspiciousness in our actions and relationships.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies the desire for strength and protection against negativity and enmity.
    • In real life, this can be related to teamwork in a workplace where collaboration and positive thinking lead to success.
    • For example, in a project team, fostering a pleasant and cooperative environment can help achieve common goals.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for collective well-being and the pursuit of harmony.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as family gatherings, community events, and professional settings.
    • By promoting good thoughts and distancing ourselves from negativity, we can create a more peaceful and productive environment.
    • Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the power of positive intentions and the importance of unity in overcoming challenges.
