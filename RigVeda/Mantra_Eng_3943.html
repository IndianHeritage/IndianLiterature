आ याह्य अद्रिभिः सुतं सोमं सोमपते पिब |वृषन्न इन्द्र वृषभिर वृत्रहन्तम ||
ā yāhy adribhiḥ sutaṁ somaṁ somapate piba | vṛṣann indra vṛṣabhir vṛtrahantama ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā yāhy: "Come here" or "Let us approach."
    • adribhiḥ: "With the mountains" or "from the hills."
    • sutaṁ: "The son" or "the drink."
    • somaṁ: "Soma," a sacred drink in Vedic rituals.
    • somapate: "O Lord of Soma," referring to Indra, the king of gods.
    • piba: "Drink."
    • vṛṣann: "O mighty one" or "the strong."
    • indra: "Indra," the god of rain and thunderstorms.
    • vṛṣabhir: "With the strong ones" or "with the bulls."
    • vṛtrahantama: "The slayer of Vritra," referring to Indra's victory over the serpent Vritra, symbolizing chaos and drought.
  • Context and Interpretation:
    • This mantra is a call to Indra, inviting him to partake in the sacred Soma drink, which is believed to bestow strength, vitality, and immortality.
    • The reference to "adribhiḥ" signifies the mountains, which are often seen as sources of strength and stability in nature.
    • By invoking Indra as "vṛtrahantama," the mantra acknowledges his role as a protector and provider, emphasizing the importance of overcoming obstacles and chaos in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, this mantra can be seen as a reminder to seek strength and support from our surroundings, much like how communities come together to support one another in times of need.
    • In a broader societal context, it reflects the importance of leadership and guidance, akin to how leaders are called upon to address challenges and bring about positive change.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine strength and support in overcoming life's challenges.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek help from both the divine and our community, emphasizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
    • In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to face their struggles with courage and to rely on the strength of their relationships and environment.
