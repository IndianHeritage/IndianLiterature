अग्नि नय सुपथां राये अस्मान् विश्वानि देव वयुनानि विद्वान् |युयोध्य अस्मज जुहुराणम् एनो भूयिष्ठां ते नमुक्तिं विदेम ||
agne naya supathā rāye asmān viśvāni deva vayunāni vidvān | yuyodhy a1smaj juhurāṇam eno bhūyiṣṭhāṁ te namaüktiṁ vidhema ||
  • Agne: This word refers to Agni, the fire god, symbolizing energy, light, and purity. In real life, fire is essential for warmth, cooking, and energy, representing transformation and sustenance.
  • Naya: This means "lead" or "guide." In our lives, we often seek guidance from mentors, leaders, or even our inner wisdom to navigate challenges.
  • Supathā: Translated as "the good path," it signifies the righteous or virtuous way of living. In modern terms, this can relate to ethical decision-making in personal and professional life.
  • Rāye: This word means "wealth" or "prosperity." It emphasizes the importance of abundance in various forms, such as financial stability, health, and happiness.
  • Asmān: This translates to "us" or "our," indicating a collective identity. It highlights the importance of community and collaboration in achieving common goals.
  • Viśvāni: Meaning "all" or "universal," it suggests inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings. In real life, this can be seen in global movements for social justice and environmental sustainability.
  • Deva: Referring to "gods" or divine beings, it represents higher powers or ideals that inspire us. In everyday life, this can relate to the values and principles we aspire to uphold.
  • Vayunāni: This means "powers" or "forces," indicating the various energies at play in our lives. It can be likened to the different skills and talents we harness to achieve our goals.
  • Vidvān: Translated as "wise" or "knowledgeable," it emphasizes the importance of wisdom in decision-making. In real life, this can be seen in the value placed on education and experience.
  • Yuyodhy: This means "to fight" or "to strive." It signifies the effort and determination required to overcome obstacles. In our lives, this can relate to personal struggles, such as pursuing a career or overcoming adversity.
  • A1smaj: This word means "our offerings" or "sacrifices." It highlights the importance of giving and sharing in our communities, whether through time, resources, or support.
  • Juhurāṇam: This translates to "the offerings" or "sacrifices made." It signifies the act of devotion and gratitude, which can be seen in various cultural practices and rituals.
  • Eno: Meaning "to us," it emphasizes the personal connection to the divine or higher ideals. It reflects our desire for guidance and support in our endeavors.
  • Bhūyiṣṭhāṁ: This means "the best" or "the highest." It signifies the aspiration for excellence in all aspects of life, whether in personal achievements or contributions to society.
  • Te: This translates to "to you," indicating a relationship with the divine or higher powers. It reflects our acknowledgment of forces greater than ourselves.
  • Namuktiṁ: Meaning "salutation" or "offering of respect," it signifies reverence and gratitude. In real life, this can be seen in acts of kindness and appreciation towards others.
  • Vidhema: This means "we may know" or "we may understand," emphasizing the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom in our lives.

Conclusion: The mantra "agne naya supathā rāye asmān viśvāni deva vayunāni vidvān | yuyodhy a1smaj juhurāṇam eno bhūyiṣṭhāṁ te namaüktiṁ vidhema ||" encapsulates a profound message about seeking guidance, striving for righteousness, and aspiring for prosperity and wisdom. It encourages us to recognize our interconnectedness and the importance of community in achieving our goals. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as in personal development, community service, and ethical leadership, reminding us to honor our values and strive for the greater good.
