यद अश्विनाऽ पृच्छमानाव् अयातं त्रिचक्रेण वहतुः सूर्यायाः |विश्वे देवाऽ अनु तद् वाम् अजानन् पुत्रः पितराव् अवृणीत पूषा ||
yad aśvinā pṛcchamānāv ayātaṁ tricakreṇa vahatuṁ sūryāyāḥ | viśve devā anu tad vām ajānan putraḥ pitarāv avṛṇīta pūṣā ||
  • yad aśvinā pṛcchamānāv: This phrase refers to the Aśvins, the twin deities associated with health and medicine in Vedic literature. The term "pṛcchamānāv" means "inquiring" or "asking." This suggests a quest for knowledge or understanding.
  • ayātaṁ tricakreṇa: "Ayātaṁ" means "not coming" or "not arriving," while "tricakreṇa" refers to the three wheels or cycles, symbolizing the interconnectedness of life, time, and the cosmos. This can be interpreted as a call for the divine to bring forth knowledge or assistance that is currently absent.
  • vahatuṁ sūryāyāḥ: "Vahatuṁ" means "to carry" or "to bring," and "sūryāyāḥ" refers to the Sun. This line can be understood as a plea for the Aśvins to bring forth the light and energy of the Sun, which is essential for life and growth.
  • viśve devā anu tad: "Viśve devā" translates to "all the gods," and "anu tad" means "following that." This indicates a collective acknowledgment of the divine presence and the interconnectedness of all deities in the pursuit of knowledge and well-being.
  • vām ajānan: "Vām" means "you" (plural), and "ajānan" translates to "not knowing." This suggests that the divine beings are being called upon to reveal knowledge that is currently unknown to humanity.
  • putraḥ pitarāv avṛṇīta pūṣā: "Putraḥ" means "sons," "pitarāv" refers to "fathers," and "avṛṇīta" means "to choose" or "to select." "Pūṣā" is a deity associated with nourishment and prosperity. This line emphasizes the importance of familial bonds and the role of divine nourishment in human life.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the significance of seeking divine knowledge and assistance in our lives. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of divine guidance in understanding the mysteries of existence. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied to various aspects such as:

  • In healthcare, invoking the Aśvins for healing and well-being.
  • In education, seeking knowledge and wisdom from teachers and mentors.
  • In family dynamics, recognizing the importance of nurturing relationships and the role of parental guidance.

Ultimately, this mantra serves as a reminder of the divine support available to us in our quest for knowledge, health, and harmony in our lives.
