प्र पवमान धन्वसि सोमेंद्राय पातवे |नृभिर यतो विनीयसे ||
pra pavamāna dhanvasi somendrāya pātave | nṛbhir yato vi nīyase ||
  • Mantra: "pra pavamāna dhanvasi somendrāya pātave | nṛbhir yato vi nīyase ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: forward, towards
    • pavamāna: the purifying one, often associated with the essence of life or the divine
    • dhanvasi: in the bow, symbolizing strength and readiness
    • somendrāya: to the lord of Soma, a divine drink that represents immortality and bliss
    • pātave: to the one who is powerful or strong
    • nṛbhir: by men, referring to humanity
    • yato: from where
    • vi: indeed, truly
    • nīyase: you are led or guided
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the purifying essence that leads us towards strength and power.
    • The reference to "Soma" highlights the importance of spiritual nourishment and the quest for immortality.
    • The phrase "by men" suggests that this journey or guidance is a collective human endeavor.
    • Overall, it emphasizes the need for divine guidance in our pursuit of strength and purity in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often seek guidance and inspiration to lead their teams effectively, akin to invoking divine strength.
    • In personal development, individuals may turn to meditation or spiritual practices to purify their thoughts and enhance their inner strength.
    • Communities often come together in rituals or gatherings to seek collective strength and guidance, reflecting the mantra's emphasis on unity.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of seeking divine guidance in our lives, especially in our quest for strength and purity.
    • It is applicable in various real-life scenarios, from personal growth to leadership and community building.
    • By invoking this mantra, individuals and communities can aspire to achieve greater heights with the support of divine energy.
