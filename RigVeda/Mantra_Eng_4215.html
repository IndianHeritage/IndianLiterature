युवाभ्यां मित्रावरुणोपमं धेयाम ऋचा |यद ध क्षये मगोनां स्तोत्रिणां च स्पूर्धसे ||
yuvābhyām mitrāvaruṇopamaṁ dheyām ṛcā | yad dha kṣaye maghonāṁ stotṝṇāṁ ca spūrdhase ||
  • yuvābhyām: This word translates to "by the two young ones" or "by the two youthful beings." In the context of the Rigveda, it often refers to the duality of forces or deities, symbolizing balance and harmony.
  • mitrāvaruṇopamaṁ: This phrase can be broken down into "Mitra" and "Varuna," who are deities representing friendship and cosmic order, respectively. "Upamaṁ" means "like" or "similar to." Thus, this part of the mantra suggests a relationship or connection akin to that of these two divine figures.
  • dheyām: This word means "may I be granted" or "may I receive." It indicates a desire or a prayer for blessings or guidance.
  • ṛcā: This term refers to "hymns" or "verses," particularly those that are recited in praise or worship. It emphasizes the importance of sacred words and their power.
  • yad dha: This translates to "when" or "if," introducing a conditional aspect to the mantra.
  • kṣaye: This word means "in the decline" or "in the destruction." It suggests a state of loss or a need for restoration.
  • maghonāṁ: This term refers to "of the wealthy" or "of the prosperous." It highlights the importance of abundance and prosperity in life.
  • stotṝṇāṁ: This means "of the worshippers" or "of those who praise." It indicates a community or group engaged in devotion.
  • ca: This is a conjunction meaning "and," linking the ideas presented in the mantra.
  • spūrdhase: This word can be interpreted as "to be stirred" or "to be awakened." It suggests a call to action or a plea for revitalization.

Conclusion: The mantra "yuvābhyām mitrāvaruṇopamaṁ dheyām ṛcā | yad dha kṣaye maghonāṁ stotṝṇāṁ ca spūrdhase ||" encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance and blessings in times of need. It emphasizes the importance of harmony, community, and the power of sacred words. In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as during challenging times when one seeks support from friends or the divine, or when a community comes together to uplift each other through shared prayers and hymns. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of individuals and the divine, encouraging us to seek balance and prosperity in our lives.
