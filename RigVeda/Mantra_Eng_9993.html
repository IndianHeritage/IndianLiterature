गोत्रभिदं गोविदं वज्रबाहुं जयन्तमज्म प्रमृणन्तमोजसा |इमं सजाता अनु वीर्यध्वम् इन्द्रं सखायो अनु सं रभध्वम् ||
gotrabhidaṁ govidaṁ vajrabāhuṁ jayantam ajma pramṛṇantam ojasā | imaṁ sajātā anu vīrayadhvam indraṁ sakhāyo anu saṁ rabhadhvam ||
  • Gotrabhidaṁ: This term can be interpreted as "the one who breaks the lineage" or "the one who transcends family ties." In real life, this can refer to individuals who rise above their familial backgrounds to achieve greatness, such as leaders or innovators who change societal norms.
  • Govidaṁ: This translates to "the one who is knowledgeable about the essence of life" or "the one who understands the nature of existence." An example could be a philosopher or a scientist who seeks to understand the fundamental truths of life and the universe.
  • Vajrabāhuṁ: Meaning "the one with arms like thunder," this symbolizes strength and power. In a real-life context, this could represent a person who is a protector or a warrior, someone who stands up for justice and defends the weak.
  • Jayantam: This word means "the victorious one." It can be related to individuals who achieve success in their endeavors, such as athletes who win championships or entrepreneurs who build successful businesses.
  • Ajma: This term refers to "the one who is invoked" or "the one who is called upon." In everyday life, this could be likened to leaders or mentors whom people look up to for guidance and support.
  • Pramṛṇantam: This means "the one who is full of energy" or "the one who is dynamic." This can be seen in individuals who inspire others through their passion and enthusiasm, such as motivational speakers or community leaders.
  • Ojasā: This translates to "with vigor" or "with strength." It signifies the importance of energy and vitality in achieving goals, much like athletes who train rigorously to enhance their performance.
  • Imam Sajātā: This phrase means "together with those of the same kind." It emphasizes the importance of community and collaboration, akin to teamwork in a corporate setting where collective effort leads to success.
  • Vīrayadhvam: This means "become courageous" or "become strong." It encourages individuals to embrace bravery in the face of challenges, similar to activists who fight for social justice despite facing opposition.
  • Indraṁ: Referring to the king of gods, Indra symbolizes leadership and authority. In real life, this can represent influential leaders in various fields who guide and inspire others.
  • Sakhāyo: This translates to "friends" or "companions." It highlights the importance of friendship and support systems in achieving success, much like how friends encourage each other in personal and professional endeavors.
  • Saṁ Rabhadhvam: This means "come together" or "unite." It signifies the power of unity and collaboration, as seen in movements where people come together for a common cause, such as environmental activism.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of strength, knowledge, and unity in overcoming challenges and achieving success. It encourages individuals to rise above their backgrounds, seek wisdom, and work together with others to create a positive impact in the world. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork in organizations, community building, and personal development, highlighting the importance of collaboration and courage in the pursuit of goals.
