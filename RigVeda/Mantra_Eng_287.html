इमं मे वरुण श्रुधी हवम अद्या च मृळय |त्वां अवस्युर आ चके ||
imam me varuṇa śrudhī havam adyā ca mṛḻaya | tvām avasyur ā cake ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • imam: this
    • me: my
    • varuṇa: Varuna (the god of water and celestial ocean)
    • śrudhī: listen or pay attention
    • havam: invocation or offering
    • adyā: today
    • ca: and
    • mṛḻaya: grant or bestow
    • tvām: you
    • avasyur: surely or certainly
    • ā: from
    • cake: I have made
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer directed towards Varuna, asking him to listen to the invocation being made today and to bestow blessings upon the devotee.
    • It emphasizes the importance of divine attention and the need for spiritual connection in daily life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a modern context, this mantra can be likened to moments when individuals seek guidance or support from a higher power during challenging times, such as during a personal crisis or when making significant life decisions.
    • For instance, a student preparing for an important exam may recite this mantra to invoke focus and clarity, hoping for divine assistance in their efforts.
  • Significance and Application:
    • The mantra highlights the relationship between humans and the divine, illustrating the need for communication and connection with higher forces.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek help and guidance in our daily lives, reinforcing the belief that we are not alone in our struggles.
    • This invocation can be applied in various situations, such as during rituals, personal prayers, or moments of reflection, encouraging individuals to reach out for support and to remain open to receiving blessings.
