मन्त्र: तिस्रो मातृः त्रीन् पितृन् बिभ्रद एक ऊर्ध्वस तस्थौ नेम अव ग्लापयन्ति | मन्त्रयन्ते दिवो अमुष्य पीठे विश्वविदं वाचं अविश्वमिन्वाम ||
tisro mātṝs trīn pitṝn bibhrad eka ūrdhvas tasthau nem ava glāpayanti | mantrayante divo amuṣya pṛṣṭhe viśvavidaṁ vācam aviśvaminvām ||
  • tisro mātṝs: This phrase translates to "three mothers." In the context of the Rigveda, the term "mātṝs" often refers to the nurturing and protective aspects of nature, symbolizing the Earth, Water, and Sky as maternal figures that provide sustenance and support to all living beings.
  • trīn pitṝn: This means "three fathers." The "pitṝn" can be interpreted as the guiding forces or ancestors that provide wisdom and direction. In a real-life context, this could represent the influence of our forefathers, teachers, or mentors who shape our values and beliefs.
  • bibhrad eka: This translates to "holding one." It signifies unity and the importance of coming together as one entity, despite the diversity represented by the three mothers and three fathers. In modern life, this can be seen in communities that unite for a common cause, emphasizing collaboration and harmony.
  • ūrdhvas tasthau: This means "standing above." It suggests a higher state of being or consciousness. In practical terms, this can relate to striving for personal growth and enlightenment, aiming to rise above challenges and limitations in life.
  • nem ava glāpayanti: This phrase can be interpreted as "they do not let go." It emphasizes the idea of holding onto values, traditions, and relationships that are essential for stability and growth. In everyday life, this can be seen in families that maintain strong bonds and cultural practices across generations.
  • mantrayante divo amuṣya pṛṣṭhe: This translates to "they chant the words of the heavens." It signifies the importance of communication and connection with the divine or the universe. In real life, this can be reflected in practices such as meditation, prayer, or any form of spiritual expression that seeks to connect with a higher power.
  • viśvavidaṁ vācam: This means "the all-knowing speech." It represents the wisdom that comes from understanding the universe and our place within it. In practical terms, this can be related to the pursuit of knowledge and education, encouraging individuals to seek truth and understanding in their lives.
  • aviśvaminvām: This phrase can be interpreted as "we are all-encompassing." It suggests inclusivity and the interconnectedness of all beings. In a real-life context, this can be seen in movements that promote unity, diversity, and acceptance among different cultures and communities.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of unity, guidance, and the nurturing aspects of nature and ancestry. It emphasizes the importance of coming together as one, holding onto our values, and striving for higher consciousness. In real life, this mantra is applicable in various scenarios, such as community building, family relationships, spiritual practices, and the pursuit of knowledge. It serves as a reminder of our interconnectedness and the collective wisdom that can be harnessed when we unite for a common purpose.
