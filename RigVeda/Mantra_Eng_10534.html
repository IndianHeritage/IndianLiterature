यद् ado वाता ते गृहेऽमृतस्य निधिः हितः |ततो नो देहि जीवसे ||
yad ado vāta te gṛhe3 'mṛtasya nidhir hitaḥ | tato no dehi jīvase ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yad: whatever
    • ado: is below
    • vāta: O Wind (or Breath)
    • te: your
    • gṛhe: in the house
    • mṛtasya: of the dead
    • nidhir: treasure
    • hitaḥ: beneficial
    • tato: therefore
    • no: to us
    • dehi: grant
    • jīvase: for living
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the Wind or Breath, symbolizing life and vitality.
    • It speaks of a treasure that lies beneath, which can be interpreted as the hidden potential or resources within us.
    • The plea is for the Wind to grant us this treasure so that we may live fully and meaningfully.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often seek to uncover their hidden talents and abilities, akin to discovering the "treasure" mentioned in the mantra.
    • In a community context, this can relate to harnessing the collective strengths of its members to foster growth and well-being.
    • In environmental discussions, the "Wind" can symbolize the natural forces that sustain life, reminding us of the importance of preserving our environment for future generations.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of recognizing and accessing the hidden resources within ourselves and our surroundings.
    • It serves as a reminder that life is enriched by the treasures we uncover, whether they are personal strengths, community resources, or natural elements.
    • In practical terms, it encourages us to seek out and utilize these treasures to enhance our lives and the lives of those around us.
