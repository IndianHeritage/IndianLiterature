ते हि पुत्रासो अदितेर विदुर द्वेषांसी योतवे |अंहोश्चिद उरुचक्रयोऽनेहसः ||
te hi putrāso aditer vidur dveṣāṁsi yotave | aṁhoś cid urucakrayo 'nehasaḥ ||
  • Translation: "They, the sons of Aditi, know the two enmities that are in the world; they are the ones who are strong and powerful."
  • Word Breakdown:
    • te: They
    • hi: indeed
    • putrāso: sons
    • aditer: of Aditi (Aditi is often considered the mother of the gods)
    • vidur: know
    • dveṣāṁsi: enmities
    • yotave: that are in the world
    • aṁhoś: of the strong
    • cid: indeed
    • urucakrayo: powerful
    • 'nehasaḥ: those who are strong
  • Contextual Meaning:
    • This mantra speaks about the sons of Aditi, who are often associated with divine qualities and wisdom.
    • The "two enmities" can be interpreted as the dualities present in life, such as good and evil, light and darkness, or love and hate.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of understanding these dualities to navigate through life effectively.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal relationships, understanding the enmities of jealousy and trust can help individuals foster healthier connections.
    • In a workplace setting, recognizing the dualities of competition and collaboration can lead to a more harmonious and productive environment.
    • On a broader scale, societies that acknowledge and address the enmities of conflict and peace can work towards sustainable development and coexistence.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of awareness and understanding of the dualities in life.
    • By recognizing these enmities, individuals can cultivate wisdom and strength, leading to personal growth and societal harmony.
    • It serves as a reminder that knowledge and awareness are crucial in overcoming challenges and fostering positive relationships.
