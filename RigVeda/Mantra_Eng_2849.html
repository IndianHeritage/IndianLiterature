उप नः सुतम् आ गहि सोमं इन्द्र गवाशिरम् |हरिभ्यां यस ते अस्मयुः ||
upa naḥ sutam ā gahi somam indra gavāśiram | haribhyāṁ yas te asmayuḥ ||
  • upa naḥ sutam ā gahi: This phrase translates to "Come to us, O Indra, with your son." Here, "upa" means "come," "naḥ" means "to us," "sutam" refers to "son" or "offspring," and "ā gahi" means "take" or "hold." This invocation suggests a plea for divine assistance and connection.
  • somam indra gavāśiram: This part translates to "the soma, O Indra, that is the head of the cows." "Somam" refers to the sacred drink, "indra" is the deity being addressed, and "gavāśiram" means "the head of the cows." In Vedic culture, soma was considered a divine elixir that provided strength and vitality, while cows symbolized wealth and nourishment.
  • haribhyāṁ yas te asmayuḥ: This translates to "by the grace of the divine, may we attain strength." "Haribhyāṁ" refers to the divine or auspicious, "yas" means "who" or "that," and "te asmayuḥ" means "may we attain strength." This line emphasizes the importance of divine blessings in achieving strength and prosperity.

  • Significance: This mantra is a call for divine support and strength, highlighting the relationship between humans and the divine. It reflects the Vedic belief in the importance of seeking blessings from deities for success and well-being.
  • Real-life Application: In modern life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during challenging times when individuals seek strength and guidance. For example, during exams, students may recite this mantra to invoke focus and clarity. Similarly, in business, entrepreneurs may use it to seek divine support for their ventures.
  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine intervention for strength and prosperity. It serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness between the divine and human efforts, encouraging individuals to remain humble and seek support in their endeavors.
