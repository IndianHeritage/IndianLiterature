आ वो यक्ष्य अमृतत्वं सुवीरं यथा वो देवाः वरिवः कराणि |आ बाह्वोर वज्रम इन्द्रस्य धेयाम अतहेमा विश्वाः पृतना जयाति ||
ā vo yakṣy amṛtatvaṁ suvīraṁ yathā vo devā varivaḥ karāṇi | ā bāhvor vajram indrasya dheyām athemā viśvāḥ pṛtanā jayāti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vo: To you all
    • yakṣy: May we attain
    • amṛtatvaṁ: Immortality
    • suvīraṁ: Strong and valiant
    • yathā: As
    • vo: To you
    • devā: The gods
    • varivaḥ: Bestow
    • karāṇi: Actions
  • Second Line Breakdown:
    • ā: To
    • bāhvor: The arms
    • vajram: Thunderbolt or strength
    • indrasya: Of Indra (the king of gods)
    • dheyām: May we meditate upon
    • athemā: These
    • viśvāḥ: All
    • pṛtanā: Struggles or endeavors
    • jayāti: Triumphs or succeeds
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer for strength, immortality, and success in endeavors.
    • It invokes the blessings of the gods, asking them to bestow their divine strength and guidance.
    • The reference to Indra signifies the desire for the power and valor associated with him, which can be applied to overcoming challenges in life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive environment, such as a workplace or academic setting, individuals may recite this mantra to seek the strength and wisdom to excel.
    • During challenging times, such as illness or personal loss, people may find solace in the idea of seeking divine support for resilience and recovery.
    • In sports, athletes often invoke strength and courage, similar to the essence of this mantra, to perform at their best.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its universal appeal for strength, immortality, and success.
    • It serves as a reminder that seeking divine guidance can empower individuals to face life's challenges with courage and determination.
    • In various aspects of life, from personal struggles to professional endeavors, this mantra can inspire individuals to strive for greatness and resilience.
