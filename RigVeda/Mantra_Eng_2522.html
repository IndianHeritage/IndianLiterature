जानन्ति वृष्णो अरुषस्य शेवम् उता ब्रध्नस्य शासने रणन्ति |दिवोरुचः सुरुचो रोचमानाऽ इला येषां गण्या माहिना गीः ||
jānanti vṛṣṇo aruṣasya śevam uta bradhnasya śāsane raṇanti | divorucaḥ suruco rocamānā iḻā yeṣāṁ gaṇyā māhinā gīḥ ||
  • jānanti: This word translates to "they know" or "they are aware." It signifies knowledge and understanding.
  • vṛṣṇo: Refers to "Vṛṣṇa," which is often associated with Lord Vishnu, representing preservation and protection in the universe.
  • aruṣasya: This means "of the red" or "of the dawn," symbolizing new beginnings, hope, and enlightenment.
  • śevam: Translates to "the best" or "the ultimate," indicating the highest form of something.
  • uta: This word means "or" and is used to present an alternative or additional thought.
  • bradhnasya: Refers to "of the strong" or "of the powerful," indicating strength and authority.
  • śāsane: This means "in the command" or "in the governance," suggesting leadership and control.
  • raṇanti: Translates to "they fight" or "they engage in battle," symbolizing action and struggle.
  • divorucaḥ: This means "of the divine light" or "of the heavenly radiance," representing purity and divinity.
  • suruco: Refers to "beautiful" or "pleasing," indicating aesthetic appeal and harmony.
  • rocamānā: This translates to "shining" or "radiant," symbolizing brilliance and positivity.
  • iḻā: Refers to "the goddess" or "the divine," often associated with creativity and inspiration.
  • yeṣāṁ: This means "of whom" or "whose," indicating possession or belonging.
  • gaṇyā: Translates to "to be counted" or "to be valued," suggesting importance and significance.
  • māhinā: Refers to "with strength" or "with power," indicating forcefulness and impact.
  • gīḥ: This means "songs" or "hymns," representing expression and communication through art.

This mantra speaks to the essence of knowledge, strength, and divine beauty. It emphasizes the importance of understanding the higher truths of existence, the power of leadership, and the beauty of creativity. In real life, this can be seen in various scenarios:


  • In leadership roles, individuals who embody the qualities of strength and wisdom can inspire others, much like a leader who guides their team through challenges with knowledge and integrity.
  • In personal development, embracing new beginnings (aruṣasya) can lead to transformative experiences, such as starting a new career or pursuing a passion that brings joy and fulfillment.
  • In artistic endeavors, the pursuit of beauty (suruco) and creativity (iḻā) can lead to impactful works that resonate with others, such as a powerful painting or a moving piece of music.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call to recognize and embrace the interplay of knowledge, strength, and beauty in our lives. It encourages individuals to strive for excellence, to lead with wisdom, and to create with passion. In a world that often challenges us, these principles can guide us toward a more meaningful and impactful existence.
