यया रध्रम पारयथात्य अंहो यया निदो मुंचथा वन्दितारम् |अर्वाची सा मरुतो या व उतीर ओ षु वाश्रेवा सुमतिर जिगातु ||
yayā radhram pārayathāty aṁho yayā nido muñcatha vanditāram | arvācī sā maruto yā va ūtir o ṣu vāśreva sumatir jigātu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yayā: by which
    • radhram: the swift (or quick)
    • pārayathāty: you may cross (or overcome)
    • aṁho: the obstacles (or difficulties)
    • yayā: by which
    • nido: the bonds (or ties)
    • muñcatha: you may release (or free)
    • vanditāram: the one who is to be worshipped (or revered)
    • arvācī: the one who is forward (or progressive)
    • sā: she (or it)
    • maruto: the winds (or forces)
    • yā: who
    • va: or
    • ūtir: the crossing (or passage)
    • o: oh
    • ṣu: in the (or among)
    • vāśreva: like a (or as a)
    • sumatir: good thought (or wisdom)
    • jigātu: may it thrive (or prosper)
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about overcoming obstacles and releasing oneself from bonds through the power of worship and reverence.
    • The "swift" and "obstacles" can be seen as the challenges we face in life, while "worship" signifies the importance of seeking guidance and strength from a higher power.
    • The mention of "winds" symbolizes the forces of nature or life that can aid in our journey, suggesting that we are not alone in our struggles.
    • The phrase "good thought" emphasizes the significance of positive thinking and wisdom in navigating life's challenges.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, an individual may face challenges such as project deadlines or team conflicts. By seeking guidance from mentors (the "worshipped") and maintaining a positive mindset (the "good thought"), they can navigate these obstacles effectively.
    • In personal life, someone dealing with emotional struggles may find solace in spiritual practices or community support, which helps them release their burdens and move forward.
    • In environmental contexts, the "winds" can represent the natural forces that can be harnessed for renewable energy, symbolizing progress and innovation in overcoming ecological challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the importance of faith, positive thinking, and the support of higher powers or community in overcoming life's challenges.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that we can transcend our difficulties through reverence, wisdom, and the forces around us.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to personal growth, resilience, and a deeper connection with the world around us.
