इन्द्राय साम गायत विप्राय बृहत बृहत् |धर्मकृत्य विपश्चिते पनस्यवे ||
indrāya sāma gāyata viprāya bṛhate bṛhat | dharmakṛte vipaścite panasyave ||
  • Indrāya: This word refers to Indra, the king of the gods in Vedic mythology, symbolizing strength, power, and leadership.
  • Sāma: This term means song or melody, often associated with the chanting of hymns or mantras, indicating harmony and unity.
  • Gāyata: This means to sing or chant, emphasizing the act of vocal expression and devotion.
  • Viprāya: This refers to the wise or learned individuals, often the priests or sages who possess knowledge and insight.
  • Bṛhate: This word means great or vast, indicating something of significant importance or magnitude.
  • Bṛhat: Similar to bṛhate, it also signifies greatness or vastness, reinforcing the idea of something profound.
  • Dharmakṛte: This term translates to "for the sake of righteousness" or "in the pursuit of duty," highlighting the importance of moral and ethical actions.
  • Vipaścite: This means "to the wise" or "to the knowledgeable," indicating a focus on wisdom and understanding.
  • Panasyave: This term can be interpreted as "to the one who brings prosperity" or "to the benefactor," suggesting a connection to abundance and well-being.

  • Meaning: The mantra can be understood as a call to Indra to sing praises and to the wise individuals who act righteously, seeking greatness and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of wisdom, ethical conduct, and the pursuit of greatness in life.
  • Real-life Example: In a corporate setting, this mantra can be applied to leaders who inspire their teams through ethical practices and wisdom. For instance, a CEO who prioritizes corporate social responsibility and fosters a culture of integrity can be seen as embodying the principles of this mantra.
  • Significance: The mantra highlights the importance of leadership, wisdom, and righteousness in achieving greatness and prosperity. It serves as a reminder that true success is not just about personal gain but also about uplifting others and contributing positively to society.
  • Application: In daily life, individuals can apply the teachings of this mantra by striving to act ethically, seeking knowledge, and supporting others in their pursuits. Whether in personal relationships, community involvement, or professional endeavors, embodying these values can lead to a more harmonious and prosperous existence.
