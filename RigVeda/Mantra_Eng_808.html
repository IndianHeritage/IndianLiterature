वीलु चिद दृढा पितरो न उक्थैर अद्रिं रुजन्न अंगिरसो रवेण |चक्रुर दिवो बृहतो गातुम अस्मे अहः स्वर विविदुः केतुम उष्राः ||
vīḻu cid dṛḻhā pitaro na ukthair adriṁ rujann aṅgiraso raveṇa | cakrur divo bṛhato gātum asme ahaḥ svar vividuḥ ketum usrāḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • vīḻu: to be strong or powerful
    • cid: indeed or certainly
    • dṛḻhā: firm or steadfast
    • pitaro: fathers or ancestors
    • na: not
    • ukthair: by words or speech
    • adriṁ: the mountain or the firm ground
    • rujann: causing pain or distress
    • aṅgiraso: the Angiras, a group of sages or seers
    • raveṇa: by the sun or light
    • cakrur: they have made or created
    • divo: of the sky or heaven
    • bṛhato: of the great or vast
    • gātum: to go or to move
    • asme: for us
    • ahaḥ: day or light
    • svar: sound or voice
    • vividuḥ: they have distinguished or separated
    • ketum: the banner or the sign
    • usrāḥ: the dawn or the early light
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the strength and steadfastness of our ancestors, emphasizing that true power does not come merely from words.
    • It highlights the importance of action and the creation of a path illuminated by the sun, symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
    • The reference to the Angiras suggests a connection to wisdom and the pursuit of truth, indicating that we should strive to follow the path laid out by those who came before us.
    • The imagery of the sky and dawn signifies new beginnings and the potential for growth and understanding in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied to leadership. A true leader inspires through actions rather than mere words, creating a strong foundation for their team.
    • In personal relationships, the mantra reminds us to honor our ancestors and learn from their experiences, ensuring that we carry forward their wisdom into our lives.
    • In education, the pursuit of knowledge is akin to following the path illuminated by the sun, where students are encouraged to seek understanding actively rather than passively receiving information.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of strength, wisdom, and action in our lives.
    • It encourages us to honor our roots while forging our own paths, illuminated by the light of knowledge and experience.
    • In essence, it teaches us that true power lies not in what we say, but in what we do and how we choose to live our lives.
