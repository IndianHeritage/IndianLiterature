परि णो अश्वम् अश्वविद गोमद इन्दो हिरण्यवत् |क्षराः सहस्रिणीर इषः ||
pari ṇo aśvam aśvavid gomad indo hiraṇyavat | kṣarā sahasriṇīr iṣaḥ ||
  • Word Breakdown:
    • pari: around, in all directions
    • ṇa: to us, for us
    • aśvam: horse, symbolizing strength and energy
    • aśvavid: one who knows the horse, a metaphor for wisdom or knowledge
    • gomad: possessing cows, representing wealth and prosperity
    • indo: O Indra, the god of rain and thunder, symbolizing power and authority
    • hiraṇyavat: golden, representing purity and value
    • kṣarā: perishable, transient
    • sahasriṇīr: thousandfold, representing abundance
    • iṣaḥ: desires, aspirations
  • Meaning:
    • This mantra invokes the divine forces to surround us with strength and wisdom.
    • The horse symbolizes not just physical strength but also the energy needed to achieve our goals.
    • Indra, as a powerful deity, represents the authority and support we seek in our endeavors.
    • The mention of wealth (cows) signifies the importance of prosperity in our lives.
    • The mantra emphasizes the transient nature of material possessions while aspiring for abundance.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, invoking this mantra can be seen as a call for the strength and wisdom needed to navigate challenges.
    • In personal life, it can represent the desire for prosperity and the support of powerful allies in achieving one's goals.
    • In a community setting, it can symbolize the collective strength and wisdom needed to overcome societal challenges.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of strength, wisdom, and prosperity in our lives.
    • It encourages us to seek support from higher powers and to recognize the transient nature of material wealth.
    • In practical terms, it can be applied in various aspects of life, from personal aspirations to community goals, emphasizing the need for collective strength and wisdom.
