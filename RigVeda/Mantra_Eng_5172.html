उद यस्य ते नवजातस्य वृष्णो 'ग्ने चरन्त्य अजरा इधाना: |अच्छा द्यां अरुषो धूम एति सं दूतो अग्न ईयसे हि देवान ||
ud yasya te navajātasya vṛṣṇo 'gne caranty ajarā idhānāḥ | acchā dyām aruṣo dhūma eti saṁ dūto agna īyase hi devān ||
  • ud yasya te navajātasya vṛṣṇo 'gne caranty ajarā idhānāḥ
    • ud - This word can be interpreted as "up" or "out," indicating a rising or elevating force.
    • yasya - Meaning "of whom," it refers to a divine entity or force.
    • te - Translates to "your," indicating possession or connection.
    • navajātasya - This means "newly born" or "newly arisen," suggesting freshness or renewal.
    • vṛṣṇo - Refers to "Vṛṣṇa," a name associated with the divine, often linked to Lord Vishnu.
    • agne - This means "O Agni," addressing the fire god, symbolizing energy and transformation.
    • caranty - Means "moving" or "wandering," indicating a dynamic presence.
    • ajarā - Translates to "immortal" or "everlasting," signifying eternal qualities.
    • idhānāḥ - Means "here," indicating presence in the current moment or space.
  • acchā dyām aruṣo dhūma eti saṁ dūto agna īyase hi devān
    • acchā - This means "good" or "auspicious," indicating positive qualities.
    • dyām - Refers to "the sky" or "heaven," representing the vastness and the divine realm.
    • aruṣo - Translates to "red" or "brilliant," often associated with the rising sun or energy.
    • dhūma - Means "smoke," which can symbolize transformation or the process of change.
    • eti - Means "comes" or "approaches," indicating movement towards something.
    • saṁ - This is a prefix meaning "together" or "with," indicating unity.
    • dūto - Means "messenger," suggesting communication or the transfer of messages.
    • agna - Again refers to "Agni," the fire god, symbolizing energy and transformation.
    • īyase - Means "you move" or "you go," indicating action or dynamism.
    • hi - This is an emphatic particle, often translated as "indeed" or "certainly."
    • devān - Refers to "the gods," indicating the divine or spiritual beings.

Conclusion:

This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the dynamic and transformative nature of divine energy, particularly through the symbolism of Agni, the fire god. It highlights themes of renewal, immortality, and the connection between the divine and the earthly realms. In real life, this can be applied to various situations where transformation and renewal are necessary, such as personal growth, overcoming challenges, or seeking spiritual enlightenment. The invocation of Agni serves as a reminder of the energy and vitality that can be harnessed in our lives, encouraging us to embrace change and seek the divine in our everyday experiences.
