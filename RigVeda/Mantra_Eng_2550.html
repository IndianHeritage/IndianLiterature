त्वां यज्ञेषु ऋत्विजं अग्ने होतारं ईळते | गोपा ऋतस्य दीदिहि स्वे दामे ||
tvāṁ yajñeṣv ṛtvijam agne hotāram īḻate | gopā ṛtasya dīdihi sve dame ||
  • tvāṁ - This word translates to "you," referring to Agni, the fire god in Vedic literature.
  • yajñeṣv - This means "in the sacrifices," indicating the context of rituals and offerings made to the deities.
  • ṛtvijam - This refers to "the priest," specifically the one who performs the rituals and mediates between the divine and the human.
  • agne - This is the vocative form of Agni, directly addressing the fire god.
  • hotāram - This means "the one who invokes," referring to the role of Agni in the rituals as the one who is called upon to accept the offerings.
  • īḻate - This translates to "shines" or "is invoked," indicating the presence and importance of Agni in the rituals.
  • gopā - This means "the protector" or "the guardian," suggesting Agni's role in safeguarding the rituals and the participants.
  • ṛtasya - This translates to "of truth" or "of order," emphasizing the importance of righteousness and cosmic order in the rituals.
  • dīdihi - This means "shine forth" or "illuminate," calling for Agni to bring light and clarity.
  • sve - This means "in your own," referring to the personal connection and the sacred space of the ritual.
  • dame - This translates to "home" or "abode," indicating the domestic aspect of the rituals and the importance of the household in Vedic culture.

The mantra essentially invokes Agni, the fire god, as a central figure in Vedic rituals. It highlights the role of the priest in performing sacrifices and calls upon Agni to shine forth in the home, ensuring the presence of truth and order. The mantra emphasizes the connection between the divine and the domestic, illustrating how spiritual practices are intertwined with everyday life.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various aspects of life, such as:

  • Rituals and Celebrations: During festivals or family gatherings, invoking Agni can symbolize the importance of unity and the sacredness of the occasion.
  • Personal Growth: The call for Agni to illuminate one's home can be seen as a metaphor for seeking knowledge and clarity in personal endeavors.
  • Community and Order: The emphasis on truth and order can inspire individuals to contribute positively to their communities, fostering a sense of responsibility and harmony.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of rituals in maintaining the connection between the divine and the mundane. It encourages individuals to seek truth, order, and illumination in their lives, reinforcing the idea that spirituality is not separate from daily existence but rather an integral part of it.
