शश्वन्तो हि शत्रवो रारधुष्टे भेदस्य चिच्छर्धतो विन्द रन्धिम् |मार्तां एनः स्तुवतो यः कृत्नोति तिग्मं तस्मिन् न जहि वज्रम इन्द्र ||
śaśvanto hi śatravo rāradhuṣ ṭe bhedasya cic chardhato vinda randhim | martām̐ enaḥ stuvato yaḥ kṛṇoti tigmaṁ tasmin ni jahi vajram indra ||
  • śaśvanto hi śatravo: This phrase can be translated as "the enemies are indeed peaceful." It suggests that even those who may seem like adversaries can have a peaceful aspect. In real life, this can be seen in situations where competition in business or sports leads to mutual respect and collaboration.
  • rāradhuṣ ṭe bhedasya: This means "they are separated by differences." It highlights the idea that differences can create barriers. For example, in a multicultural society, people may have different beliefs and practices, which can lead to misunderstandings. Recognizing and respecting these differences is crucial for harmony.
  • cic chardhato vinda randhim: This translates to "from the one who is aware, find the connection." It emphasizes the importance of awareness and understanding in bridging gaps. In real life, this can be applied in conflict resolution, where understanding each party's perspective can lead to a solution.
  • martām̐ enaḥ stuvato: This means "the one who praises the mortal." It suggests the significance of acknowledging and appreciating human efforts and achievements. In everyday life, this can be seen in workplaces where recognition of employees' hard work boosts morale and productivity.
  • yaḥ kṛṇoti tigmaṁ: This translates to "who creates the sharpness." It refers to the ability to sharpen one's skills or intellect. In practical terms, this can be related to continuous learning and self-improvement, such as taking courses or seeking mentorship to enhance one's capabilities.
  • tasmin ni jahi vajram indra: This means "in that, do not let go of the thunderbolt, O Indra." It signifies the importance of holding onto strength and power in the face of challenges. In real life, this can be interpreted as resilience in overcoming obstacles, whether in personal life or professional endeavors.

  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of understanding, respect, and resilience in human interactions. It teaches us that even adversaries can have peaceful aspects, and recognizing differences can lead to better connections. In real life, applying these principles can foster collaboration, enhance personal growth, and build resilience in the face of challenges. The mantra serves as a reminder to appreciate human efforts and to maintain strength in adversity, making it relevant in various aspects of life, from personal relationships to professional environments.
