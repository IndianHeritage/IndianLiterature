कनिक्रान्ति हरिरासृज्यमानः सीदन वनस्य जठरे पुनानः |नृभिर यतः कृतुते निर्णिजं गा अतो मतीर जनयता स्वधाभिः ||
kanikranti harir ā sṛjyamānaḥ sīdan vanasya jaṭhare punānaḥ | nṛbhir yataḥ kṛṇute nirṇijaṁ gā ato matīr janayata svadhābhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • kanikranti: The act of transformation or change.
    • harir: Referring to the divine or the Lord.
    • ā sṛjyamānaḥ: Being created or manifested.
    • sīdan: To sit or reside.
    • vanasya: Of the forest or nature.
    • jaṭhare: In the womb or within.
    • punānaḥ: Purifying or cleansing.
    • nṛbhir: By humans or mankind.
    • yataḥ: From where or from which.
    • kṛṇute: Creates or establishes.
    • nirṇijaṁ: The unblemished or pure.
    • gā: The song or hymn.
    • ato: Therefore or thus.
    • matīr: Intellect or understanding.
    • janayata: Generates or brings forth.
    • svadhābhiḥ: By one's own nature or essence.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine transformation and creation that occurs within nature and humanity.
    • The reference to "harir" indicates a connection to the divine, suggesting that the process of creation is sacred.
    • The phrase "sīdan vanasya jaṭhare" implies that this transformation is rooted in the natural world, emphasizing the importance of nature in the cycle of life.
    • The idea of purification ("punānaḥ") suggests that through our actions and intentions, we can cleanse ourselves and our surroundings.
    • The mantra highlights the role of humanity ("nṛbhir") in this process, indicating that our thoughts and actions can create a pure and unblemished reality ("nirṇijaṁ").
    • It emphasizes the importance of understanding and intellect ("matīr") in generating positive outcomes in life.
    • Finally, "svadhābhiḥ" suggests that this process is intrinsic to our nature, indicating that we have the power to shape our reality through our essence.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In environmental conservation, the mantra can be seen as a call to purify and protect nature, recognizing that our actions can lead to a healthier planet.
    • In personal development, individuals can reflect on their thoughts and actions, understanding that they have the power to create a positive change in their lives and communities.
    • In social movements, the mantra can inspire collective action towards justice and equality, emphasizing the role of humanity in creating a better society.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates the profound relationship between the divine, nature, and humanity.
    • Its significance lies in the reminder that we are co-creators of our reality, and through our intentions and actions, we can purify and transform our surroundings.
    • In real life, it encourages us to engage with nature, reflect on our actions, and strive for a harmonious existence, ultimately leading to a more enlightened and fulfilling life.
