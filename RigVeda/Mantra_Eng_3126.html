साम द्विभरहा महि तिग्मभृष्टिः सहस्ररेता वृषभस्तुविष्मान् |पदं न गोर अपगूढं विविध्वान अग्निर मह्यम् प्रेदु वाचन मनिषाम् ||
sāma dvibarhā mahi tigmabhṛṣṭiḥ sahasraretā vṛṣabhas tuviṣmān | padaṁ na gor apagūḻhaṁ vividvān agnir mahyam pred u vocan manīṣām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sāma: A hymn or song, often associated with harmony and balance.
    • dvibarhā: Two-fold or dual, indicating a dual nature or aspect.
    • mahi: Great or vast, often referring to the universe or the earth.
    • tigmabhṛṣṭiḥ: Sharp or intense, suggesting a powerful force or energy.
    • sahasraretā: Thousand-fold, indicating abundance or multiplicity.
    • vṛṣabhas: Bull, symbolizing strength and vitality.
    • tuviṣmān: One who is knowledgeable or wise.
    • padaṁ: Footstep or path, representing a journey or direction.
    • na: Not, indicating negation.
    • gor: Cow, often symbolizing nourishment and sustenance.
    • apagūḻhaṁ: Hidden or concealed, suggesting something that is not easily seen or understood.
    • vividvān: One who knows or understands, a wise person.
    • agnir: Fire, representing energy, transformation, and purification.
    • mahyam: To me or for me, indicating a personal connection.
    • pred: Spirit or soul, often referring to the essence of life.
    • u: And, a conjunction linking ideas.
    • vocan: To speak or declare, indicating communication.
    • manīṣām: Intellect or wisdom, referring to the capacity for thought and understanding.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the duality of existence, emphasizing the balance between different forces in the universe.
    • The mention of "sāma" suggests the importance of harmony in our lives, akin to how music can bring people together.
    • The "tigmabhṛṣṭiḥ" and "sahasraretā" highlight the intensity and abundance of life experiences, much like the challenges and opportunities we face daily.
    • The "vṛṣabhas" symbolizes strength, reminding us to harness our inner power to overcome obstacles.
    • The reference to "agnir" signifies the transformative power of fire, akin to how challenges can lead to personal growth and renewal.
    • The phrase "padaṁ na gor apagūḻhaṁ" suggests that the path to understanding may be obscured, yet it is essential to seek knowledge and clarity.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a workplace, the balance between teamwork (sāma) and individual contributions (dvibarhā) can lead to greater success.
    • During personal challenges, embracing the intensity of emotions (tigmabhṛṣṭiḥ) can lead to profound personal insights and growth.
    • In relationships, understanding the strength (vṛṣabhas) and wisdom (tuviṣmān) of each partner can foster deeper connections.
    • In times of crisis, the transformative power of adversity (agnir) can lead to new beginnings and opportunities.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of balance, strength, and the pursuit of knowledge in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in reminding us to embrace both the challenges and the harmony present in our experiences.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the wisdom of this mantra can lead to personal growth, improved relationships, and a deeper understanding of our paths.
    • Ultimately, it encourages us to seek knowledge and clarity, transforming our lives through the power of understanding and resilience.
