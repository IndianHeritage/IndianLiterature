अस्तावी मनमा पूर्व्यम् ब्रह्मेन्द्राय वोचत | पूर्वीर ऋतस्य बृहतीर अनूषत स्तोतुर मेधा असृक्षत ||
astāvi manma pūrvyam brahmendrāya vocata | pūrvīr ṛtasya bṛhatīr anūṣata stotur medhā asṛkṣata ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • astāvi: Eight
    • manma: Minds or thoughts
    • pūrvyam: From the east or the beginning
    • brahmendrāya: To the great lord of creation (Brahma)
    • vocata: They spoke or called
    • pūrvīr: By the ancients or the wise
    • ṛtasya: Of truth or cosmic order
    • bṛhatīr: The great or vast
    • anūṣata: They followed or adhered
    • stotur: To praise or worship
    • medhā: Intelligence or wisdom
    • asṛkṣata: They protected or preserved
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the invocation of wisdom and intelligence, represented by the eight minds, directed towards the great creator, Brahma.
    • The phrase "from the east" symbolizes new beginnings, enlightenment, and the dawn of knowledge.
    • The ancients, or wise ones, are recognized for their adherence to truth and cosmic order, suggesting that wisdom is rooted in tradition and the understanding of universal laws.
    • The act of praising or worshiping is a call to recognize and honor the intelligence that guides us, emphasizing the importance of wisdom in our lives.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern education, the pursuit of knowledge can be seen as a reflection of the "eight minds," where diverse perspectives and disciplines come together to foster a holistic understanding.
    • In leadership, the invocation of wisdom and adherence to truth can guide decision-making processes, ensuring that actions are aligned with ethical and moral standards.
    • In personal development, individuals often seek enlightenment and new beginnings, akin to the "pūrvyam" aspect, as they strive to grow and evolve in their understanding of life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of wisdom, truth, and the pursuit of knowledge in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to honor the teachings of the ancients and to seek intelligence in our actions and decisions.
    • In a world filled with distractions and misinformation, this mantra encourages us to return to the core values of wisdom and truth, guiding us towards a more enlightened existence.
