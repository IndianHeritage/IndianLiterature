मन्त्र: नि शत्रोः सोम वृष्ण्यं नि शुष्मं नि वयस् तिर | दूरै वा सतो अन्ति वा ||
ni śatroḥ soma vṛṣṇyaṁ ni śuṣmaṁ ni vayas tira | dūre vā sato anti vā ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ni: not
    • śatroḥ: of enemies
    • soma: soma (a sacred drink)
    • vṛṣṇyaṁ: of the Vṛṣṇis (a clan or tribe)
    • ni: not
    • śuṣmaṁ: of the weak
    • ni: not
    • vayas: of age
    • tira: of the young
    • dūre: far away
    • vā: or
    • sato: of the good
    • anti: they exist
    • vā: or
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a desire for protection and the absence of enemies.
    • It emphasizes the importance of strength and vitality, distancing oneself from weakness and age.
    • The mention of "soma" signifies a connection to divine energy and nourishment, suggesting that one should seek strength from sacred sources.
    • The phrase "dūre vā sato anti vā" indicates a longing for the presence of goodness, whether near or far.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a competitive work environment, individuals may seek to distance themselves from negativity and conflict (enemies) while striving for personal and professional growth (strength).
    • In health and wellness, people often turn to nutritious foods and practices (soma) to enhance their vitality and ward off illness (weakness).
    • In relationships, fostering positive connections and distancing from toxic influences can lead to a more fulfilling life.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder to seek strength and goodness in life while avoiding negativity and weakness.
    • Its significance lies in the pursuit of personal growth, health, and positive relationships.
    • In real-life scenarios, it encourages individuals to cultivate resilience and surround themselves with supportive and uplifting influences.
