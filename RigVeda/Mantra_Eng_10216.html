तद् हि वयं वृणीमहे वरुण मित्रार्यमन् |येना निर अम्हसो युयम् पाथ नेथा च मार्त्यम् अति द्विषः ||
tad dhi vayaṁ vṛṇīmahe varuṇa mitrāryaman | yenā nir aṁhaso yūyam pātha nethā ca martyam ati dviṣaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • tad - that
    • dhī - intellect or understanding
    • vayaṁ - we
    • vṛṇīmahe - we choose or we invoke
    • varuṇa - Varuna, the god of cosmic order and water
    • mitra - friend or ally
    • aryaman - a deity associated with hospitality and social order
    • yenā - by whom
    • nir - free from
    • aṁhaso - darkness or ignorance
    • yūyam - you (plural)
    • pātha - path or way
    • nethā - guide or leader
    • ca - and
    • martyam - mortal or human
    • ati - beyond
    • dviṣaḥ - enmity or hatred
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer invoking the divine forces of Varuna, Mitra, and Aryaman.
    • It expresses a desire for guidance and protection from darkness and ignorance.
    • The mantra emphasizes the importance of friendship, harmony, and the pursuit of a righteous path.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a community setting, this mantra can be applied when seeking unity and cooperation among members, promoting a sense of friendship and collective purpose.
    • In personal relationships, invoking the qualities of Varuna and Mitra can help individuals navigate conflicts and foster understanding, leading to stronger bonds.
    • In educational environments, this mantra can inspire students to seek knowledge and wisdom, guiding them away from ignorance and towards enlightenment.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance and the cultivation of positive relationships.
    • It serves as a reminder of the importance of harmony, understanding, and the pursuit of knowledge in overcoming challenges.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be a source of inspiration for individuals and communities striving for peace, wisdom, and unity.
