स नः पप्रिः पारयाति स्वस्ति नावाः पुरुहूतः | इन्द्रो विश्वा अतिद्विषः ||
sa naḥ papriḥ pārayāti svasti nāvā puruhūtaḥ | indro viśvā ati dviṣaḥ ||
  • sa naḥ - "May he" or "Let him" - This phrase invokes a higher power or deity, suggesting a plea or request for assistance.
  • papriḥ - "Bestow" or "Grant" - This word indicates the act of giving or providing something valuable, often associated with blessings or prosperity.
  • pārayāti - "Lead" or "Guide" - This term emphasizes the importance of direction and support in one's journey, whether it be in life, work, or spiritual endeavors.
  • svasti - "Well-being" or "Welfare" - This word signifies health, prosperity, and overall good fortune, highlighting the desire for a positive state of existence.
  • nāvā - "Boat" or "Vessel" - Symbolically, a boat represents a means of navigation through life's challenges, suggesting the need for guidance and support in turbulent times.
  • puruhūtaḥ - "Many offerings" or "One who is invoked" - This term refers to a deity or divine figure who is called upon for assistance, often associated with abundance and generosity.
  • indro - "Indra" - The king of gods in Vedic tradition, representing strength, power, and protection, often invoked for overcoming obstacles.
  • viśvā - "All" or "Universal" - This word signifies inclusivity and the idea that the blessings sought are for everyone, not just the individual.
  • ati dviṣaḥ - "Overcome adversaries" - This phrase emphasizes the need for strength and support in facing challenges and enemies, both external and internal.

Conclusion: The mantra "sa naḥ papriḥ pārayāti svasti nāvā puruhūtaḥ | indro viśvā ati dviṣaḥ ||" is a powerful invocation for guidance, well-being, and protection. It emphasizes the importance of divine assistance in navigating life's challenges and achieving prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during difficult times when one seeks support, in business endeavors where success and well-being are desired, or in personal struggles where overcoming obstacles is essential. By invoking the blessings of higher powers, individuals can find strength and direction, fostering a sense of community and shared well-being. This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of all beings and the universal quest for peace and prosperity.
