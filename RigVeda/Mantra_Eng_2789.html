शुनं हुवेम मगवाणं इन्द्रं अस्मिन भरे नृतमं वाजसातौ | श्रिण्वन्तं उग्रं ऊतये समत्सु घ्नन्तं वृत्राणि संजितं धनानाम् ||
śunaṁ huvema maghavānam indram asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau | śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi saṁjitaṁ dhanānām ||
  • śunaṁ huvema: This phrase can be translated as "We invoke the powerful." Here, "śunaṁ" refers to the act of invoking or calling upon, while "huvema" signifies the act of offering or addressing a deity.
  • maghavānam indram: This refers to "Indra, the mighty one." Indra is a prominent deity in the Rigveda, often associated with strength, rain, and thunder. "Maghavānam" emphasizes his greatness and power.
  • asmin bhare nṛtamaṁ vājasātau: This can be interpreted as "In this place, we offer our best." "Asmin" means "in this," "bhare" refers to "place," "nṛtamaṁ" signifies "the best," and "vājasātau" relates to "the best of offerings." This line emphasizes the importance of making the best offerings to the divine.
  • śṛṇvantam ugram ūtaye: This translates to "Listening to the fierce one for our benefit." "Śṛṇvantam" means "listening," "ugram" refers to "fierce" or "powerful," and "ūtaye" signifies "for our benefit." This suggests that by listening to the divine, one can gain strength and benefits.
  • samatsu ghnantaṁ vṛtrāṇi: This means "In the assembly, striking down the obstacles." "Samatsu" refers to "in the assembly," "ghnantaṁ" means "striking down," and "vṛtrāṇi" signifies "obstacles" or "enemies." This line highlights the importance of overcoming challenges in a collective manner.
  • saṁjitaṁ dhanānām: This translates to "Gathering wealth." "Saṁjitaṁ" means "gathering" or "collecting," and "dhanānām" refers to "wealth." This emphasizes the pursuit of prosperity and abundance.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of invoking divine strength and guidance in overcoming obstacles and achieving prosperity. It highlights the importance of making sincere offerings, listening to divine wisdom, and working together to strike down challenges. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as during rituals for success in business, community gatherings for collective goals, or personal endeavors where one seeks strength and guidance. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals and communities can foster resilience, unity, and prosperity in their lives.
