मां नो अग्नेऽमतये मावीरतायै रीरधः |मागोतायै सहसस् पुत्र मां निदेऽप द्वेषांस्याकृधि ||
mā no agne 'mataye māvīratāyai rīradhaḥ | māgotāyai sahasas putra mā nide 'pa dveṣāṁsy ā kṛdhi ||
  • mā no agne 'mataye: This phrase can be translated as "O Agni, may you not lead us to wrong thoughts." Here, Agni represents the divine fire and is often seen as a symbol of knowledge and enlightenment. In real life, this can be understood as a plea for guidance in our thoughts and decisions, ensuring that we remain on the right path.
  • māvīratāyai rīradhaḥ: This translates to "may we not be led to cowardice." This part emphasizes the importance of courage and bravery in facing challenges. For example, in a workplace scenario, this could relate to taking bold decisions or standing up for one's beliefs despite opposition.
  • māgotāyai sahasas putra: This means "may we not be deprived of strength and vitality." It highlights the need for physical and mental strength. In everyday life, this can be seen in the context of maintaining good health through exercise and a balanced diet, which empowers individuals to face life's challenges effectively.
  • mā nide 'pa dveṣāṁsy ā kṛdhi: This translates to "may you not create enmity or hatred." This is a call for harmony and peace in relationships. In real life, this can be applied in various social settings, such as promoting understanding and tolerance among diverse groups in a community.

Conclusion: The mantra serves as a profound reminder of the values of wisdom, courage, strength, and harmony. In a world often filled with conflict and confusion, invoking these principles can guide individuals and communities towards a more enlightened and peaceful existence. By applying the teachings of this mantra in our daily lives—whether in personal relationships, professional environments, or community interactions—we can foster a more compassionate and understanding society.
