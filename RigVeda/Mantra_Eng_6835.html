यः श्वेताम् अधिनिर्णिजश चक्रे कृष्णाम् अनु व्रता |स धाम पूर्व्यम् ममे यः स्कम्भेन विडोदसी अजो न द्याम् अधारयन् नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
yaḥ śvetām̐ adhinirṇijaś cakre kṛṣṇām̐ anu vratā | sa dhāma pūrvyam mame yaḥ skambhena vi rodasī ajo na dyām adhārayan nabhantām anyake same ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yaḥ: who
    • śvetām̐: white, pure
    • adhinirṇijaś: having established, determined
    • cakre: wheel, cycle
    • kṛṣṇām̐: black, dark
    • anu: following
    • vratā: vow, commitment
    • sa: he
    • dhāma: abode, place
    • pūrvyam: ancient, prior
    • mame: my
    • yaḥ: who
    • skambhena: by the pillar, support
    • vi: in, within
    • rodasī: the two worlds, realms
    • ajo: unborn, eternal
    • na: not
    • dyām: sky, heaven
    • adhārayan: supporting, holding
    • nabhantām: the atmosphere, space
    • anyake: others
    • same: equal, alike
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine entity who has established a cycle (or wheel) that encompasses both purity (represented by the white) and darkness (represented by the black).
    • The reference to "vow" suggests a commitment to a path or a way of life that is guided by this divine presence.
    • The "pillar" symbolizes stability and support, indicating that this divine force holds up the two realms of existence—spiritual and material.
    • The mantra emphasizes the eternal nature of this divine presence, which is not bound by the physical world but supports it nonetheless.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In daily life, individuals often face choices that reflect the duality of purity and darkness. For instance, a person may choose to follow a path of honesty (purity) or succumb to deceit (darkness). This mantra encourages individuals to commit to the path of truth.
    • The concept of a "pillar" can be seen in the support systems we create in our lives, such as family, friends, or mentors, who help us navigate challenges and maintain balance.
    • In a broader sense, the mantra can be applied to societal structures where the balance between different ideologies (purity and darkness) is essential for harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the importance of recognizing the dualities in life and the commitment to a path that aligns with higher principles.
    • It serves as a reminder that there is a divine support system that upholds the universe, encouraging individuals to seek balance and stability in their lives.
    • In practical terms, it can inspire people to reflect on their choices, seek guidance, and build supportive relationships that foster growth and understanding.
