स पूर्व्यः पवते यं दिवस परि श्येनो मथायद इषितस तिरो रजः |स मध्वा आ युवते वेविजान इत कृशानोर अस्तुर मनसाहा बिभ्युषा ||
sa pūrvyaḥ pavate yaṁ divas pari śyeno mathāyad iṣitas tiro rajaḥ | sa madhva ā yuvate vevijāna it kṛśānor astur manasāha bibhyuṣā ||
  • sa pūrvyaḥ pavate: This phrase can be translated as "He who blows from the east." In many cultures, the east is associated with new beginnings, enlightenment, and the rising sun. For example, in real life, the dawn of a new day brings hope and opportunities.
  • yaṁ divas pari: This means "who encompasses the day." It signifies the all-encompassing nature of time and existence. In our daily lives, we often find ourselves caught up in the hustle and bustle of the day, yet we must remember to take a step back and appreciate the larger picture of life.
  • śyeno mathāyad: Translated as "like a hawk," this phrase evokes the image of a bird of prey, symbolizing sharp vision and focus. In real life, this can be related to the importance of having a clear vision and purpose in our endeavors, much like a hawk that hunts with precision.
  • iṣitas tiro rajaḥ: This translates to "the one who moves through the dust." It suggests the idea of navigating through challenges and obstacles. In our lives, we often face difficulties, and this phrase reminds us to persevere and keep moving forward despite the dust and chaos around us.
  • sa madhva ā yuvate: This means "he who is sweet and youthful." It symbolizes vitality and the joy of life. In real life, this can be seen in the enthusiasm of youth, where energy and passion drive individuals to pursue their dreams and aspirations.
  • vevijāna it kṛśānor: Translated as "he who knows the essence of the weak," this phrase highlights the importance of empathy and understanding. In our interactions, recognizing the struggles of others can lead to compassion and support, fostering a sense of community.
  • astur manasāha bibhyuṣā: This means "may he protect us with his mind." It emphasizes the power of thought and intention. In real life, our thoughts shape our reality, and maintaining a positive mindset can lead to better outcomes in various aspects of life.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates themes of guidance, vision, resilience, empathy, and the power of thought. Its significance lies in the reminder to embrace new beginnings, maintain focus, navigate challenges, and foster compassion. In real life, these principles can be applied in personal growth, relationships, and professional endeavors, encouraging individuals to strive for a balanced and meaningful existence.
