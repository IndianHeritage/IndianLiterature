सनायते गोतम इन्द्र नव्यम अतक्षद ब्रह्म हरियोजनाय | सुनीताया नः शवसाना नोधाः प्रातर मक्षू धियावसुर जगम्याट ||
sanāyate gotama indra navyam atakṣad brahma hariyojanāya | sunīthāya naḥ śavasāna nodhāḥ prātar makṣū dhiyāvasur jagamyāt ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sanāyate: to be invoked or called upon
    • gotama: referring to the sage or seer, often associated with wisdom
    • indra: the king of gods, representing strength and power
    • navyam: new or fresh
    • atakṣad: to be established or made firm
    • brahma: the ultimate reality or cosmic spirit
    • hariyojanāya: for the purpose of achieving or reaching
    • sunīthāya: for the well-being or good fortune
    • naḥ: our
    • śavasāna: the essence or vitality
    • nodhāḥ: may not be obstructed
    • prātar: in the morning
    • makṣū: with clarity or insight
    • dhiyāvasur: with intelligence or wisdom
    • jagamyāt: may we attain or reach
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine presence of Indra, the king of gods, to bless the seeker with new strength and wisdom.
    • It emphasizes the importance of establishing a firm foundation in spiritual and worldly pursuits, akin to how a strong tree needs deep roots to grow tall.
    • The reference to "sunīthāya" suggests a focus on well-being, indicating that the pursuit of knowledge and strength should also consider the welfare of oneself and others.
    • The phrase "may not be obstructed" highlights the desire for clarity and unobstructed paths in life, similar to how a clear road allows for smooth travel.
    • Finally, the mantra concludes with a plea for wisdom and insight in the morning, symbolizing new beginnings and the potential for growth each day.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a professional setting, this mantra can be applied when starting a new project. Invoking the qualities of Indra can inspire team members to work together with strength and clarity.
    • In personal development, individuals can recite this mantra during morning meditation to set intentions for the day, seeking wisdom and unobstructed paths in their endeavors.
    • In community gatherings, this mantra can serve as a reminder to prioritize the well-being of all members, fostering an environment of support and growth.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra from the Rigveda serves as a powerful invocation for strength, wisdom, and clarity in life.
    • Its significance lies in the holistic approach it promotes—encouraging individuals to seek not only personal success but also the well-being of others.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts, from personal growth to community building, making it a timeless guide for navigating life's challenges.
