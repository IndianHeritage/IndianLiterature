अग्निनाग्निः सम इध्यते कविर गृहपतिर युवा |हव्यवाड् जुह्वास्यः ||
agnināgniḥ sam idhyate kavir gṛhapatir yuvā | havyavāḍ juhvāsyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Agninā: By the fire
    • Agnīḥ: The fire itself
    • Sam: Together, in harmony
    • Idhyate: Is established, is created
    • Kavir: The wise one, the poet, the creator
    • Gṛhapatir: The lord of the house, the protector
    • Yuvā: Young, youthful
    • Havyavāḍ: The one who is worthy of offerings
    • Juhvāsyaḥ: The one who is invoked, the one who is called upon
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the sacred fire (Agni) and its significance in Vedic rituals.
    • It emphasizes the idea of creation and harmony, suggesting that the fire is a medium through which offerings are made to the divine.
    • The term "Kavir" refers to the wise creator, indicating that the fire is not just a physical entity but also a symbol of knowledge and creativity.
    • "Gṛhapatir" signifies the protective aspect of the fire, representing the household and its sanctity.
    • The youthful aspect ("Yuvā") suggests vitality and the potential for growth and renewal.
    • "Havyavāḍ" indicates the fire's role in receiving offerings, highlighting the importance of rituals in connecting with the divine.
    • "Juhvāsyaḥ" points to the act of invocation, showing that the fire is a channel for communication with higher powers.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern times, the concept of fire can be seen in various cultural rituals, such as lighting candles during festivals, which symbolizes the dispelling of darkness and ignorance.
    • The idea of a "home" as a sacred space can be related to the importance of family gatherings around a fireplace, where stories and wisdom are shared, fostering a sense of community.
    • In creative fields, the metaphor of fire as a source of inspiration can be observed in artists and writers who describe their creative process as ignited by passion and knowledge.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra encapsulates the essence of fire as a vital force in both physical and spiritual realms.
    • Its significance lies in the understanding that fire is not merely a source of heat and light but also a symbol of transformation, protection, and divine connection.
    • In real life, the principles derived from this mantra can guide individuals in their personal and communal lives, emphasizing the importance of harmony, creativity, and the sacredness of home and relationships.
