सदा कविः सुमतिम् आ चके वां विश्वा धियः अश्विनाः प्रावतम् मे |अस्मे रयिं नासत्याः बृहन्तम् अपत्यसाचं श्रुत्यं रराथाम् ||
sadā kavī sumatim ā cake vāṁ viśvā dhiyo aśvinā prāvatam me | asme rayiṁ nāsatyā bṛhantam apatyasācaṁ śrutyaṁ rarāthām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sadā - always
    • kavī - O wise one, poet, or seer
    • sumatim - good understanding or wisdom
    • ā cake - may you grant
    • vāṁ - to you
    • viśvā - all
    • dhiyo - intellects or thoughts
    • aśvinā - O Ashvins (the twin deities)
    • prāvatam - may you inspire
    • me - for me
    • asme - for us
    • rayiṁ - wealth or prosperity
    • nāsatyā - O Nasatyas (another name for the Ashvins)
    • bṛhantam - great or abundant
    • apatyasācaṁ - with offspring or progeny
    • śrutyaṁ - with knowledge or hearing
    • rarāthām - may you bestow
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer to the Ashvins, the divine twin horsemen in Vedic mythology, who are often associated with health, medicine, and prosperity.
    • The speaker seeks wisdom and understanding from the Ashvins, asking them to inspire all thoughts and intellects.
    • It emphasizes the importance of collective wisdom and prosperity, not just for the individual but for the community.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, leaders often seek collective wisdom from their teams to make informed decisions. This reflects the essence of the mantra, where the speaker asks for the inspiration of all intellects.
    • In educational institutions, students and teachers collaborate to enhance learning experiences, embodying the idea of seeking wisdom together.
    • In family settings, parents often pray for the well-being and prosperity of their children, aligning with the desire for abundant offspring and knowledge.
  • Conclusion and Significance:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of wisdom, collective thought, and prosperity in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder to seek guidance from higher powers or collective wisdom in our endeavors.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals and communities to work together towards common goals, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.
