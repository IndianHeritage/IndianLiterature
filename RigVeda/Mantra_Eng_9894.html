सोऽस्य वज्रो हरितो य आयसो हरिर निकामो हरिर आ गभस्त्योः |द्युन्मी सुशिप्रो हरिमन्युसायक इन्द्रे नि रूपा हरिता मिमिक्षिरे ||
so asya vajro harito ya āyaso harir nikāmo harir ā gabhastyoḥ | dyumnī suśipro harimanyusāyaka indre ni rūpā haritā mimikṣire ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • so asya: this (is) of him
    • vajro: the thunderbolt or strength
    • harito: green or flourishing
    • ya: who
    • āyaso: of iron or strength
    • harir: the one who removes or takes away
    • nikāmo: the one who is free or liberated
    • harir: again, the one who removes
    • ā: from
    • gabhastyoḥ: of the depths or the hidden
    • dyumnī: glorious or radiant
    • suśipro: well-formed or auspicious
    • harimanyusāyaka: the one who is powerful and fierce
    • indre: in Indra (the king of gods)
    • ni: down or below
    • rūpā: forms or shapes
    • haritā: green or flourishing
    • mimikṣire: they have looked or observed
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine strength and vitality represented by Indra, the king of gods, who embodies the qualities of power, protection, and prosperity.
    • The mention of "vajro" (thunderbolt) symbolizes the unyielding strength and the ability to overcome obstacles, much like how a thunderstorm clears the air and brings new life.
    • The term "harito" (green) signifies growth and flourishing, akin to how nature rejuvenates after rain, representing hope and renewal in our lives.
    • The phrase "dyumnī suśipro" emphasizes the importance of auspiciousness and well-formed intentions, which can be related to setting clear goals in our personal and professional lives.
    • In a real-life context, this mantra can be seen as a call to harness our inner strength and resilience, much like athletes who prepare for competitions, drawing on their mental and physical fortitude.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its invocation of divine strength and the encouragement to cultivate resilience and prosperity in our lives.
    • It serves as a reminder that, like the forces of nature, we too can overcome challenges and emerge stronger, provided we align our intentions with positive and constructive actions.
    • This mantra can be applicable in various real-life scenarios, such as during times of personal struggle, in professional challenges, or when seeking to foster growth and positivity in our communities.
