मन्त्र: दिवो न सानु स्तनयन्न अचिक्रदद द्यौश्च यस्य पृथिवी च धर्मभिः | इन्द्रस्य सख्यम् पवते विवेदित सोमः पुनानः कलशेषु सीदति ||
divo na sānu stanayann acikradad dyauś ca yasya pṛthivī ca dharmabhiḥ | indrasya sakhyam pavate vivevidat somaḥ punānaḥ kalaśeṣu sīdati ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Divo: Of the heavens
    • Na: Not
    • Sānu: The one who is near
    • Stanayann: Who nourishes
    • Acikradad: Who has made a sound
    • Dyauś: The sky
    • Ca: And
    • Yasya: Whose
    • Pṛthivī: The earth
    • Ca: And
    • Dharmabhiḥ: By righteousness
    • Indrasya: Of Indra
    • Sakhyam: Friendship
    • Pavate: Blows (or moves)
    • Vivevidat: Distinguishing
    • Somaḥ: The soma (a sacred drink)
    • Punānaḥ: Purifying
    • Kalaśeṣu: In the vessels
    • Sīdati: Sits or resides
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine connection between the heavens and the earth, emphasizing the nurturing aspect of the divine.
    • It highlights the importance of righteousness (Dharma) in maintaining harmony between the celestial and terrestrial realms.
    • Indra, a significant deity in Vedic literature, symbolizes strength and friendship, suggesting that divine support is essential for human endeavors.
    • The mention of Soma indicates the importance of rituals and offerings in purifying and connecting with the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In modern life, this mantra can be related to the importance of environmental conservation, where the nurturing of the earth (Pṛthivī) is essential for sustaining life.
    • In personal relationships, the concept of friendship (Sakhyam) can be applied to foster strong bonds based on mutual respect and support, akin to the relationship between humans and the divine.
    • Rituals and traditions in various cultures can be seen as a way to connect with the divine, similar to the offerings made to Soma, which can be paralleled with modern practices of gratitude and mindfulness.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra signifies the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of righteousness in our actions.
    • It serves as a reminder to nurture our relationships with nature and each other, promoting harmony and balance in our lives.
    • By understanding and applying the principles of this mantra, individuals can cultivate a more meaningful and purposeful existence, aligning themselves with the divine order of the universe.
