आयम् अद्य सुखृतम् प्रातरिच्छन्न इष्टेः पुत्रं वसुमतां रथेन |अंशोः सुतं पायय मात्सरस्य क्षयवीरं वर्धय सूनृताभिः ||
āyam adya sukṛtam prātar icchann iṣṭeḥ putraṁ vasumatā rathena | aṁśoḥ sutam pāyaya matsarasya kṣayadvīraṁ vardhaya sūnṛtābhiḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • āyam: This refers to "this" or "this day."
    • adya: Means "today."
    • sukṛtam: Translates to "good deeds" or "virtuous actions."
    • prātar: Means "in the morning."
    • icchann: Refers to "desiring" or "wishing."
    • iṣṭeḥ: Means "of the desired" or "of the worshipped."
    • putraṁ: Translates to "son" or "child."
    • vasumatā: Refers to "the earth" or "the prosperous."
    • rathena: Means "by the chariot."
    • aṁśoḥ: Refers to "of the part" or "of the portion."
    • sutam: Means "son" or "offspring."
    • pāyaya: Translates to "protect" or "nurture."
    • matsarasya: Refers to "of envy" or "of jealousy."
    • kṣayadvīraṁ: Means "the one who destroys the brave" or "the one who diminishes strength."
    • vardhaya: Means "increase" or "enhance."
    • sūnṛtābhiḥ: Refers to "with good truths" or "with virtuous words."
  • Overall Meaning:
    • This mantra is a prayer for the blessings of prosperity and virtue.
    • It expresses a desire for the well-being and protection of one's offspring.
    • The invocation of the earth and the chariot symbolizes the journey of life and the support of nature.
    • It emphasizes the importance of nurturing children away from envy and negativity, promoting growth through positive truths.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • Parents often pray for their children's success and well-being, hoping to guide them towards virtuous paths.
    • In educational settings, teachers encourage students to support each other, fostering an environment free from jealousy.
    • Communities come together to nurture the younger generation, providing resources and mentorship to help them thrive.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra highlights the significance of good deeds and the nurturing of future generations.
    • It serves as a reminder to cultivate positive values and protect our loved ones from negative influences.
    • In real life, it encourages individuals to take responsibility for the growth and well-being of their families and communities.
