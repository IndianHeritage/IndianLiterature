सस्निम अविन्दच चरणे नदीनाम अपावृणोद दुरो अश्मव्रजानाम् |प्रासां गन्धर्वो अमृतानि वोचद इन्द्रो दक्षं परि जानाद अहीनाम् ||
sasnim avindac caraṇe nadīnām apāvṛṇod duro aśmavrajānām | prāsāṁ gandharvo amṛtāni vocad indro dakṣam pari jānād ahīnām ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • Sasnim: With strength or power
    • Avindac: He found or discovered
    • Caraṇe: In the feet or in the path
    • Nadīnām: Of the rivers
    • Apāvṛṇod: He uncovered or revealed
    • Duro: Difficult or hard
    • Aśmavrajānām: Of the stone-like beings or obstacles
    • Prāsāṁ: The essence or the best part
    • Gandharvo: The celestial musicians or divine beings
    • Amṛtāni: Immortal or eternal
    • Vocad: He spoke or declared
    • Indro: Indra, the king of gods
    • Dakṣam: Skillful or capable
    • Pari: All around or completely
    • Jānād: He knows or understands
    • Ahīnām: Of the serpents or those who are deceptive
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the discovery of strength and power in the paths of rivers, symbolizing the flow of life and the challenges that come with it.
    • The mention of uncovering difficult obstacles suggests the importance of perseverance and resilience in overcoming life's challenges.
    • The reference to celestial beings (Gandharvas) and immortality indicates the pursuit of higher knowledge and the eternal truths that guide us.
    • Indra, as the king of gods, represents authority and mastery, emphasizing the need for skill and understanding in navigating life's complexities.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In personal development, individuals often face obstacles that seem insurmountable. This mantra encourages one to find strength in their journey, much like a river that carves its path through rock.
    • In professional settings, the need for skill and understanding is paramount. Just as Indra is depicted as capable, professionals must cultivate their skills to navigate challenges effectively.
    • In relationships, understanding and overcoming deceptive behaviors (symbolized by serpents) is crucial for building trust and harmony.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the strength found in life's journey, the importance of overcoming obstacles, and the pursuit of higher knowledge.
    • Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, encouraging individuals to embrace challenges, develop skills, and seek deeper understanding.
    • By embodying the principles of this mantra, one can navigate the complexities of life with resilience and wisdom.
