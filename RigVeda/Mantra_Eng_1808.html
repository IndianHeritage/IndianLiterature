एष वः स्तोमो मरुत इयं गीर मांदार्यस्य मान्यस्य कारोः |एषा यासिष्ट तंवे वयां विद्यामेषं वृजनं जीरदानुम् ||
eṣa vaḥ stomo maruta iyaṁ gīr māndāryasya mānyasya kāroḥ | eṣā yāsīṣṭa tanve vayāṁ vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum ||
  • eṣa vaḥ stomo maruta: This phrase can be translated as "This hymn is for you, O Maruts." The Maruts are a group of storm deities in Vedic literature, often associated with the power of wind and storms. They symbolize strength and the dynamic forces of nature.
  • iyaṁ gīr māndāryasya mānyasya kāroḥ: This translates to "This song is from the revered and honorable Mānḍārya." Here, "gīr" refers to a song or hymn, indicating the importance of music and poetry in spiritual practices. Mānḍārya could represent a sage or a revered figure whose wisdom is being invoked.
  • eṣā yāsīṣṭa tanve vayāṁ: This means "This is the one that has been desired for our bodies." It suggests a longing for physical and spiritual well-being, emphasizing the connection between the body and the divine.
  • vidyāmeṣaṁ vṛjanaṁ jīradānum: This can be interpreted as "We seek knowledge and the essence of life." "Vidyā" refers to knowledge, while "jīradānum" implies the vital energy or life force. This line highlights the importance of wisdom and vitality in achieving a fulfilling life.

  • Significance: This mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine blessings for strength, wisdom, and vitality. It acknowledges the forces of nature and the importance of revered figures in guiding one's spiritual journey.
  • Real-life Application: In contemporary life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios such as:
    • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra during meditation or yoga to invoke strength and clarity in their pursuits.
    • Community Gatherings: It can be used in group settings to foster a sense of unity and collective strength, especially during challenging times.
    • Health and Well-being: The emphasis on vitality can inspire people to focus on their physical health and mental well-being through balanced living.

  • Conclusion: The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of nature, knowledge, and the human experience. By invoking the Maruts and seeking wisdom, individuals can cultivate strength and vitality in their lives, making it relevant in both personal and communal contexts.
