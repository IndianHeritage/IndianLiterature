शिवा नः सख्यां सन्तु भ्रात्राग्ने देवेषु युष्मे |सां नो नाभिः सदने सस्मिन्न ऊधन ||
śivā naḥ sakhyā santu bhrātrāgne deveṣu yuṣme | sā no nābhiḥ sadane sasminn ūdhan ||
  • śivā naḥ sakhyā santu: This phrase translates to "May auspiciousness be our friend." The term "śivā" refers to auspiciousness or well-being, while "sakhyā" means friendship. In real life, this can be understood as a wish for positive relationships and harmony in our interactions with others.
  • bhrātrāgne deveṣu yuṣme: This part means "O divine ones, may we be united with our brothers." Here, "bhrātrāgne" refers to brothers or kin, and "deveṣu" signifies the divine or celestial beings. This can be related to the importance of family and community bonds, emphasizing the need for unity and support among loved ones.
  • sā no nābhiḥ sadane: This translates to "May that (auspiciousness) be our support in the abode." "Nābhiḥ" refers to the center or source, while "sadane" means dwelling or abode. This suggests that auspiciousness should be the foundation of our homes and lives, providing stability and security.
  • sasminn ūdhan: This phrase means "In that, may we thrive." It implies that by embracing auspiciousness and unity, we can flourish in our endeavors. This can be seen in real-life scenarios where communities come together to support each other, leading to collective success and well-being.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of auspiciousness, unity, and support in our lives. It highlights the importance of fostering positive relationships and creating a harmonious environment, both at home and in the community. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts, such as family gatherings, community events, or even in workplaces where collaboration and mutual support lead to greater achievements. By embodying the principles of this mantra, individuals can contribute to a more positive and prosperous society.
