पुनाने तंवा मिथः स्वेन दक्षेण राजथः |ऊह्याथे सनाद ऋतम् ||
punāne tanvā mithaḥ svena dakṣeṇa rājathaḥ | ūhyāthe sanād ṛtam ||
  • punāne: This word translates to "purifying" or "renewing." It signifies the act of cleansing or revitalizing.
  • tanvā: This means "bodies" or "forms." It refers to the physical existence or the material aspect of beings.
  • mithaḥ: This translates to "together" or "in unity." It emphasizes the importance of collaboration and collective effort.
  • svena: This means "by one's own" or "self." It indicates the personal responsibility and individual contribution in the process.
  • dakṣeṇa: This word means "skillfully" or "with dexterity." It highlights the need for skill and expertise in actions.
  • rājathaḥ: This translates to "as kings" or "like rulers." It suggests a sense of authority, leadership, and governance.
  • ūhyāthe: This means "you are invoked" or "you are called upon." It indicates a plea or request for divine assistance or guidance.
  • sanād: This translates to "from the beginning" or "eternally." It signifies timelessness and the continuity of existence.
  • ṛtam: This means "truth" or "cosmic order." It represents the fundamental principles that govern the universe and moral righteousness.

The mantra "punāne tanvā mithaḥ svena dakṣeṇa rājathaḥ | ūhyāthe sanād ṛtam ||" encapsulates the essence of unity, personal responsibility, and the pursuit of truth. It emphasizes the importance of coming together in a purifying manner, utilizing our individual skills and strengths, much like rulers who govern wisely and justly.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Community Service: When individuals come together to serve their community, they purify their intentions and actions, working skillfully to create a positive impact.
  • Teamwork in Organizations: In a workplace, employees can embody this mantra by collaborating effectively, using their unique skills to achieve common goals, much like a well-governed kingdom.
  • Personal Growth: On an individual level, one can reflect on their actions and strive for self-improvement, ensuring that their contributions are meaningful and aligned with the greater good.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the significance of unity, skillful action, and the pursuit of truth in our lives. It encourages us to work together harmoniously, take personal responsibility, and strive for a higher moral order, ultimately leading to a more just and prosperous society.
