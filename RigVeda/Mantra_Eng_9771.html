स्तोमेना हि दिवि देवासो अग्निमजीजनञ्च शक्तिभिः रोडसिप्राम् |तमू अकृण्वन् त्रेधा भूवे कं स ओषधिः पचति विश्वरूपाः ||
stomena hi divi devāso agnim ajījanañ chaktibhī rodasiprām | tam ū akṛṇvan tredhā bhuve kaṁ sa oṣadhīḥ pacati viśvarūpāḥ ||
  • Stomena hi divi devāso: This phrase can be translated as "The gods, through their hymns, have invoked." It signifies the power of divine invocation through sound and prayer. In real life, this can be likened to how people use music or chants in various cultures to invoke feelings of peace, joy, or connection to the divine.
  • Agnim ajījanañ chaktibhī rodasiprām: This translates to "They have generated the fire with their powers in the sky." Here, 'Agnim' refers to fire, which is often seen as a purifying and transformative force. In practical terms, fire can symbolize knowledge and enlightenment, much like how education and learning can illuminate our lives.
  • Tam ū akṛṇvan tredhā bhuve: This means "They have created it in three ways on the earth." The number three often symbolizes completeness or wholeness in various cultures. This can be related to the three stages of life: birth, life, and death, or the three aspects of time: past, present, and future. In everyday life, this can remind us to consider our actions and their consequences across different phases of our existence.
  • Kaṁ sa oṣadhīḥ pacati viśvarūpāḥ: This translates to "What is it that the herbs, in their universal forms, cook?" This line emphasizes the nurturing aspect of nature and how it provides sustenance. In real life, this can be seen in agriculture and the importance of plants in our diet, as well as the healing properties of herbs in traditional medicine.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the interconnectedness of the divine, nature, and human existence. It highlights the significance of invoking the divine through sound, the transformative power of fire (knowledge), the completeness of life stages, and the nurturing role of nature. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek harmony with the environment, appreciate the cycles of life, and recognize the importance of spiritual practices in fostering a deeper connection with the universe.
