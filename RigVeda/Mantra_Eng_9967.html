सीराऽयुञ्जन्ति कवयो युगाऽवितन्वते पृथक् |धीरा देवेषु सुम्नया ||
sīrā yuñjanti kavayo yugā vi tanvate pṛthak | dhīrā deveṣu sumnayā ||
  • Mantra: "sīrā yuñjanti kavayo yugā vi tanvate pṛthak | dhīrā deveṣu sumnayā ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • sīrā: threads or strands
    • yuñjanti: they unite or connect
    • kavayo: the wise or poets
    • yugā: ages or epochs
    • vi: separately or distinctly
    • tanvate: they manifest or express
    • pṛthak: apart or individually
    • dhīrā: the wise or prudent
    • deveṣu: in the divine or among the gods
    • sumnayā: with auspiciousness or good fortune
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks about the connection and unity among wise individuals (kavayo) across different ages (yugā).
    • The "threads" (sīrā) symbolize the ideas, thoughts, and wisdom that bind these individuals together, even when they express themselves separately (pṛthak).
    • The wise (dhīrā) are encouraged to seek auspiciousness (sumnayā) in their endeavors, particularly in their relationship with the divine (deveṣu).
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a collaborative work environment, individuals from different backgrounds and expertise come together to achieve a common goal. Their unique contributions (pṛthak) create a stronger outcome, much like the threads woven together to form a fabric.
    • In literature, poets and writers from various eras have influenced each other, creating a rich tapestry of ideas that transcend time. For instance, the works of Shakespeare continue to inspire modern playwrights, demonstrating the timeless nature of wisdom.
    • In spiritual practices, individuals often seek guidance from divine sources, aiming for auspicious outcomes in their lives. This reflects the essence of the mantra, where the wise connect with the divine for greater understanding and success.
  • Conclusion:
    • The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on unity and collaboration among the wise across different times and spaces.
    • It teaches us that while we may express our thoughts and ideas separately, there is a greater connection that binds us together, much like the threads of a tapestry.
    • In real life, this mantra encourages us to seek wisdom, collaborate with others, and maintain a connection with the divine for auspicious outcomes in our endeavors.
