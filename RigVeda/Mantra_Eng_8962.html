मृत्योः पदं योपयन्तो यद ऐत द्राघीय आयुः प्रतरं दधानाः |आप्यायमानाः प्रजया धनेन शुद्धाः पूताः भवत यज्ञियासः ||
mṛtyoḥ padaṁ yopayanto yad aita drāghīya āyuḥ prataraṁ dadhānāḥ | āpyāyamānāḥ prajayā dhanena śuddhāḥ pūtā bhavata yajñiyāsaḥ ||
  • mṛtyoḥ padaṁ: This phrase translates to "the path of death." It signifies the inevitable journey every being must take towards mortality.
  • yopayanto: This means "those who approach" or "those who seek." It indicates the active pursuit of understanding or transcending death.
  • yad aita drāghīya āyuḥ: This translates to "that which grants a long life." It suggests that there are ways or practices that can lead to longevity.
  • prataraṁ dadhānāḥ: This means "holding the path." It implies that one must maintain a course or a set of principles to achieve the desired outcome.
  • āpyāyamānāḥ: This translates to "those who are nourished" or "those who are fulfilled." It indicates a state of abundance and well-being.
  • prajayā: This means "by progeny" or "through offspring." It highlights the importance of family and lineage in achieving fulfillment.
  • dhanena: This translates to "by wealth." It signifies the role of material prosperity in life.
  • śuddhāḥ: This means "pure" or "clean." It emphasizes the importance of purity in thoughts, actions, and intentions.
  • pūtā: This translates to "sacred" or "holy." It suggests that purity leads to a higher state of existence.
  • bhavata: This means "may you become" or "may you be." It is a blessing or a wish for the listener.
  • yajñiyāsaḥ: This translates to "those who perform sacrifices." It indicates the importance of rituals and offerings in spiritual practice.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda emphasizes the significance of understanding the nature of life and death, the pursuit of longevity, and the importance of purity in one's life. It suggests that by following a righteous path, nurturing relationships, and engaging in spiritual practices, one can achieve a fulfilling and prosperous life. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied through healthy living, maintaining strong family ties, and participating in community or spiritual activities that promote well-being and harmony. The mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, wealth, and spirituality, encouraging individuals to strive for a balanced and meaningful existence.
