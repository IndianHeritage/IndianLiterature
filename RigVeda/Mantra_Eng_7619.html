त्वां इदाह्योनरोऽपिप्यन् वज्रिन् भूर्णयः | स इन्द्र स्तोमवाहसाम् इह श्रुध्य उप स्वसारम् आगहि ||
tvām idā hyo naro 'pīpyan vajrin bhūrṇayaḥ | sa indra stomavāhasām iha śrudhy upa svasaram ā gahi ||
  • tvām - This word translates to "you" and signifies a direct address to a divine entity, often referring to Indra, the king of gods in Vedic literature.
  • idā - Meaning "here" or "now," it indicates the immediacy of the invocation, suggesting that the speaker is calling upon the divine presence in the current moment.
  • hyo - This term can be interpreted as "today" or "in this time," reinforcing the urgency and relevance of the prayer.
  • naro - Translated as "man" or "human," it emphasizes the connection between the divine and humanity, highlighting the need for divine assistance in human affairs.
  • 'pīpyan - This word means "to be satisfied" or "to be fulfilled," indicating a desire for abundance and prosperity.
  • vajrin - Referring to "the one who wields the thunderbolt," this term is often associated with Indra, symbolizing strength and power.
  • bhūrṇayaḥ - This can be understood as "to bestow" or "to grant," suggesting a request for blessings or gifts from the divine.
  • sa - Meaning "he" or "that," it refers back to Indra, reinforcing the focus on this divine figure.
  • indra - The name of the god, representing authority, power, and the protector of humanity.
  • stomavāhasām - This term can be broken down into "stoma" (praise) and "vāhasām" (of those who carry), indicating the act of carrying forth praises or hymns to the divine.
  • iha - Meaning "here" or "in this place," it emphasizes the physical and spiritual presence of the divine in the current context.
  • śrudhy - This word means "to hear" or "to listen," suggesting a plea for the divine to pay attention to the prayers being offered.
  • upa - Translated as "near" or "close," it indicates a desire for the divine presence to be felt closely by the worshipper.
  • svasaram - Meaning "one's own voice" or "sound," it emphasizes the personal nature of the prayer, as it is a direct communication from the devotee.
  • ā gahi - This phrase means "come" or "approach," indicating a call for the divine to manifest in the lives of the worshippers.

Conclusion: This mantra is a heartfelt invocation to Indra, seeking his blessings and presence in the lives of humans. It emphasizes the relationship between the divine and humanity, highlighting the need for divine support in achieving fulfillment and prosperity. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable in moments of need, such as during challenges in personal or professional life, where individuals seek strength, guidance, and abundance. By reciting this mantra, one can cultivate a sense of connection with the divine, inviting positive energies and blessings into their life.
