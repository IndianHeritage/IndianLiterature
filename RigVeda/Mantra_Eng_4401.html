यथा होतर मनुषो देवताता यज्ञेभिः सूनो सहसो यजासि |एवा नो अद्य समना समानान उषन्न अग्न उषतो यक्षि देवान ||
yathā hotar manuṣo devatātā yajñebhiḥ sūno sahaso yajāsi | evā no adya samanā samānān uśann agna uśato yakṣi devān ||
  • Mantra: "yathā hotar manuṣo devatātā yajñebhiḥ sūno sahaso yajāsi | evā no adya samanā samānān uśann agna uśato yakṣi devān ||"
  • Translation: "As the priest (hotar) invokes the divine (devatātā) through the offerings (yajñebhiḥ), may you, O Agni, grant us the strength (sūno sahaso) to perform our duties. Today, may we be united (samanā) in our intentions, and may you, O Agni, bestow your blessings upon us and the deities (yakṣi devān)."

  • Explanation of Key Terms:
    • Hotar: The priest or the one who performs the rituals, symbolizing the human effort to connect with the divine.
    • Devata: The divine beings or deities that are invoked during rituals, representing higher powers that govern the universe.
    • Yajña: A sacrificial ritual that involves offerings to the deities, symbolizing the importance of gratitude and reciprocity in life.
    • Sūno Sahaso: Refers to strength and vigor, highlighting the need for energy and determination in fulfilling one's responsibilities.
    • Agni: The fire god, representing transformation, purification, and the divine presence in rituals.
    • Samanā: Unity or harmony, emphasizing the importance of collective intention and action.
    • Yakṣi: To bestow or grant, indicating the blessings and support from the divine.

  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a corporate setting, a team may come together to work on a project, invoking the spirit of collaboration (similar to the invocation of Agni) to achieve a common goal. Just as the mantra emphasizes unity and strength, successful teams often rely on collective effort and shared intentions.
    • During a community event, individuals may perform rituals or activities that honor their cultural heritage, akin to the yajña. This fosters a sense of belonging and gratitude, reinforcing the idea that we are all connected and supported by a greater force.
    • In personal development, individuals may seek to invoke their inner strength (sūno sahaso) to overcome challenges, much like the invocation of divine support in the mantra. This can be seen in practices such as meditation or affirmations that encourage unity of purpose and clarity of intention.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of invoking divine support through collective effort and intention. It highlights the significance of rituals in fostering unity, strength, and gratitude in our lives. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal growth, teamwork, or community engagement, the principles of this mantra remind us of the importance of connecting with the divine and each other to achieve our goals and fulfill our responsibilities. By embodying these values, we can create a harmonious and supportive environment that benefits all.
