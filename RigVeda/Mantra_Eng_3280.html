वम्रीभिः पुत्रम् अग्रुवो दानं निवेशनाद धरीव आ जभर्था |व्य अ1न्धो अख्यद अहिम आददानो निर्भूद उखच्छित सम अरन्त पर्व ||
vamrībhiḥ putram agruvo adānaṁ niveśanād dhariva ā jabhartha | vy a1ndho akhyad ahim ādadāno nir bhūd ukhacchit sam aranta parva ||
  • Vamrībhiḥ: This term can be interpreted as "with the help of the wise" or "through the guidance of the learned." It emphasizes the importance of wisdom and knowledge in our lives.
  • Putram: This word means "son" or "child." It signifies the continuation of lineage and the responsibilities that come with parenthood.
  • Agruvo: This can be understood as "the one who is foremost" or "the leader." It highlights the qualities of leadership and the importance of being a guiding force in one's community.
  • Adānaṁ: This term means "giving" or "offering." It reflects the value of generosity and the act of sharing resources with others.
  • Niveśanād: This can be translated as "from the establishment" or "from the foundation." It suggests the importance of building a strong base for future endeavors.
  • Dhariva: This word means "to hold" or "to support." It indicates the need for stability and support in relationships and communities.
  • Ā Jabhartha: This phrase can be interpreted as "he who has taken" or "he who has received." It signifies the act of receiving blessings or gifts from the divine.
  • Vy A1ndho: This term can be understood as "the blind" or "those who are unaware." It serves as a reminder of the importance of awareness and enlightenment in our lives.
  • Akhyad: This means "to declare" or "to announce." It emphasizes the significance of communication and sharing knowledge with others.
  • Ahim: This term refers to "non-violence" or "peace." It highlights the importance of harmony and compassion in our interactions.
  • Ādadāno: This can be interpreted as "the giver" or "the one who provides." It reinforces the value of generosity and selflessness.
  • Nir Bhūd: This phrase means "without fear" or "free from anxiety." It suggests the importance of courage and confidence in facing life's challenges.
  • Ukhacchit: This term can be understood as "to remove obstacles" or "to clear the path." It signifies the need for perseverance and determination in overcoming difficulties.
  • Sam Aranta: This means "together with strength" or "united in power." It emphasizes the importance of community and collective effort.
  • Parva: This word refers to "a moment" or "a time." It signifies the significance of seizing opportunities and making the most of the present.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of wisdom, leadership, generosity, and community. It serves as a reminder of the values that are essential for personal growth and societal harmony. In real life, these principles can be applied in various scenarios, such as parenting, community service, and leadership roles. By embodying these qualities, individuals can contribute positively to their families and communities, fostering an environment of support, awareness, and peace. The mantra encourages us to be mindful of our actions and their impact on others, ultimately guiding us towards a more fulfilling and harmonious life.
