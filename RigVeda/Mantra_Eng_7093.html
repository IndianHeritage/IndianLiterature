अग्न आ याह्य अग्निभिर होतारं त्वा वृणीमहे |आ त्वाम अनक्तु प्रयतā हविष्मती यजिष्ठम् बर्हिर आसदे ||
agna ā yāhy agnibhir hotāraṁ tvā vṛṇīmahe | ā tvām anaktu prayatā haviṣmatī yajiṣṭham barhir āsade ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • agna: O Fire
    • ā yāhy: may we approach
    • agnibhir: with the fires
    • hotāram: the one who is invoked
    • tvā: you
    • vṛṇīmahe: we choose
    • ā: may
    • tvām: you
    • anaktu: to be nourished
    • prayatā: with effort
    • haviṣmatī: with offerings
    • yajiṣṭham: the most worthy of worship
    • barhir: on the altar
    • āsade: may it sit
  • Overall Meaning: This mantra is a prayer to Agni, the fire god, asking for his presence and blessings. It expresses the desire to approach Agni with reverence and to choose him as the one to be invoked. The mantra emphasizes the importance of offerings and efforts in worship, highlighting Agni as the most worthy of worship who should be nourished and honored on the altar.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In Hindu rituals, Agni is often invoked during ceremonies such as weddings and yajnas (sacrificial offerings). The fire symbolizes purity and the divine presence, making it a central element in many spiritual practices.
    • In modern life, the concept of 'Agni' can be seen in the way people approach their goals with dedication and effort. Just as offerings are made to Agni, individuals invest time and resources into their aspirations, seeking to achieve success and fulfillment.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine guidance and support in our endeavors. It teaches us the value of approaching our goals with respect, effort, and dedication. In real life, this mantra can inspire individuals to honor their commitments, work diligently towards their aspirations, and recognize the importance of spiritual and moral values in their pursuits. By invoking the spirit of Agni, we are reminded to keep our intentions pure and our actions aligned with our higher purpose.
