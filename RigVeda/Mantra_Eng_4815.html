अध स्मा नो वृद्धे भवेंद्र नायम अवा युधि |यद अन्तरिक्षे पतयन्ति पर्णिनो दिद्यवस तिग्ममूर्धानः ||
adha smā no vṛdhe bhavendra nāyam avā yudhi | yad antarikṣe patayanti parṇino didyavas tigmamūrdhānaḥ ||
  • Adha smā no vṛdhe bhavendra: This phrase can be translated as "May we be blessed with strength and prosperity, O Lord." Here, "adha" means "down" or "here," "smā" refers to "us," "no" means "our," "vṛdhe" signifies "growth" or "prosperity," and "bhavendra" refers to "O Lord" or "God." This part of the mantra emphasizes the desire for divine blessings in our endeavors.
  • Nāyam avā yudhi: This translates to "May we not be defeated in battle." "Nāyam" means "not," "avā" refers to "be," and "yudhi" means "in battle." This indicates a plea for protection and strength in facing challenges, whether they are physical, emotional, or spiritual.
  • Yad antarikṣe patayanti: This means "As the birds fly in the sky." "Yad" translates to "that," "antarikṣe" means "in the sky" or "in the atmosphere," and "patayanti" refers to "they fly." This imagery evokes the freedom and grace of birds, symbolizing the aspiration to rise above challenges and attain higher goals.
  • Parṇino didyavas tigmamūrdhānaḥ: This can be interpreted as "Those who are sharp-minded and have a keen intellect." "Parṇino" refers to "those who are sharp," "didyavas" means "who possess," and "tigmamūrdhānaḥ" translates to "sharp intellect." This part highlights the importance of wisdom and intelligence in overcoming obstacles and achieving success.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound message about seeking divine blessings for strength and prosperity while emphasizing the importance of resilience and wisdom in the face of challenges. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios, such as during difficult times in personal or professional life, where one seeks guidance and support. It encourages individuals to strive for growth, maintain a positive mindset, and utilize their intellect to navigate through adversities. The mantra serves as a reminder that with divine support and a sharp mind, one can overcome any battle in life.
