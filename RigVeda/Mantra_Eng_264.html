तत् त्वा यामि ब्रह्मणा वन्दमानस तद् आ शास्ते यजमानो हविर्भिः |अहेळमानो वरुणेह बोध्य उरुशंस मा न आयुः प्र मोषीः ||
tat tvā yāmi brahmaṇā vandamānas tad ā śāste yajamāno havirbhiḥ | aheḻamāno varuṇeha bodhy uruśaṁsa mā na āyuḥ pra moṣīḥ ||
  • tat tvā yāmi: This phrase translates to "I go to you." It signifies a journey or a quest towards a higher truth or understanding. In real life, this can be likened to individuals seeking knowledge or enlightenment, such as students pursuing education or professionals striving for personal growth.
  • brahmaṇā: This word refers to the ultimate reality or the cosmic spirit, often associated with the concept of Brahman in Hindu philosophy. It represents the source of all existence. For example, in a modern context, one might see this as the pursuit of universal truths in science or philosophy.
  • vandamānas: This term means "worshipping" or "paying homage." It reflects the importance of respect and reverence in various aspects of life. In everyday scenarios, this can be seen in how people honor their parents, teachers, or mentors, acknowledging their contributions to their lives.
  • tad ā śāste: This translates to "that which commands." It implies a sense of authority or guidance. In practical terms, this can relate to leaders or figures of authority in society who guide others, such as political leaders, spiritual guides, or even parents.
  • yajamāno: This word refers to the one who performs a sacrifice or offering. It symbolizes the act of giving and the importance of selflessness. In real life, this can be seen in charitable acts, where individuals or organizations contribute to the welfare of others, such as donating to a cause or volunteering time.
  • havirbhiḥ: This means "with offerings." It emphasizes the significance of contributions made in various forms, whether material or spiritual. For instance, in a community, people may come together to support a local initiative, showcasing the power of collective effort.
  • aheḻamāno: This term can be interpreted as "not being disturbed" or "unwavering." It reflects the idea of maintaining composure and focus amidst challenges. In real life, this can be seen in individuals who remain calm under pressure, such as emergency responders or leaders during crises.
  • varuṇeha: This refers to Varuna, the deity associated with cosmic order and moral authority. It signifies the importance of ethics and righteousness in life. In contemporary society, this can be related to the legal system and the moral compass that guides individuals' actions.
  • bodhy: This means "to be aware" or "to understand." It highlights the importance of awareness and consciousness in decision-making. In real life, this can be seen in mindfulness practices, where individuals strive to be present and aware of their thoughts and actions.
  • uruśaṁsa: This term translates to "great praise" or "extensive glory." It signifies the recognition of greatness in others. In everyday life, this can be observed in how people celebrate achievements, whether in sports, arts, or personal milestones.
  • mā na āyuḥ pra moṣīḥ: This phrase means "may you not lose your life." It reflects a prayer for longevity and well-being. In practical terms, this can be related to health and wellness practices, where individuals seek to maintain a healthy lifestyle to prolong their lives.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound journey towards understanding the ultimate reality while emphasizing the importance of respect, guidance, selflessness, awareness, and ethical living. Its significance lies in its applicability to various aspects of life, from personal growth and community service to maintaining composure in adversity and celebrating the achievements of others. By embodying the principles outlined in this mantra, individuals can lead more meaningful and fulfilling lives, contributing positively to society and fostering a sense of interconnectedness with the universe.
