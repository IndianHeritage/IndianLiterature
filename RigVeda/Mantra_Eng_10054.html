नाहं वेद भ्रातृत्वं नो स्वसृत्वम् इन्द्रो विदुर अंगिरसः च घोरा: |गोकामाः मे अच्छदयन यद आयम् अपात इता पाणयो वरियः ||
nāhaṁ veda bhrātṛtvaṁ no svasṛtvam indro vidur aṅgirasaś ca ghorāḥ | gokāmā me acchadayan yad āyam apāta ita paṇayo varīyaḥ ||
  • nāhaṁ veda bhrātṛtvaṁ: This phrase translates to "I do not know brotherhood." It suggests a state of ignorance or lack of awareness regarding familial or brotherly bonds. In real life, this can be seen in situations where individuals feel disconnected from their family or community, leading to feelings of isolation.
  • no svasṛtvam: This means "nor do I know sisterhood." Similar to the previous line, it indicates a lack of understanding or recognition of sisterly relationships. This can be reflected in modern society where sibling rivalry or estrangement can occur, causing emotional distress.
  • indro vidur aṅgirasaś ca ghorāḥ: This part refers to "Indra and the fierce Aṅgirasa." Indra is a prominent deity in Vedic literature, often associated with strength and power. The mention of Aṅgirasa, a group of sages, emphasizes the importance of wisdom and strength in overcoming challenges. In real life, this can be likened to the need for both physical and intellectual strength to navigate life's difficulties.
  • gokāmā me acchadayan: This translates to "may my desires be fulfilled." It reflects a yearning for the fulfillment of one's aspirations. In everyday life, this can be seen in the pursuit of personal goals, whether they are related to career, relationships, or personal growth.
  • yad āyam apāta ita paṇayo varīyaḥ: This phrase means "wherever the obstacles may arise." It acknowledges the presence of challenges in life. In practical terms, this can be related to the various hurdles one faces in achieving their dreams, such as financial difficulties, societal pressures, or personal limitations.

  • Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates a profound understanding of human relationships and the challenges faced in life. It highlights the importance of recognizing our connections with others, whether as siblings or as part of a larger community. The invocation of Indra and the Aṅgirasa serves as a reminder that strength and wisdom are essential in overcoming life's obstacles. In real life, this mantra encourages individuals to seek fulfillment in their desires while remaining aware of the challenges that may arise. It is applicable in various scenarios, such as personal development, family dynamics, and the pursuit of goals, reminding us to cultivate both inner strength and meaningful relationships.
