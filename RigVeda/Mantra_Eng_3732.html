तवाहम् अग्न ūtिभिर मित्रस्य च प्रशस्तिभिः |द्वेषोयुतो न दुरितā तुर्यामा मार्त्यānām ||
tavāham agna ūtibhir mitrasya ca praśastibhiḥ | dveṣoyuto na duritā turyāma martyānām ||
  • tavāham: This word translates to "I am yours" or "I belong to you." It signifies a sense of devotion and surrender to a higher power or divine entity.
  • agna: Referring to "Agni," the fire god, who symbolizes energy, transformation, and the divine presence in rituals. Fire is often seen as a purifying force in many cultures.
  • ūtibhir: This means "with the powers" or "with the energies." It indicates the various forces or blessings that come from the divine.
  • mitrasya: This translates to "of Mitra," who represents friendship, harmony, and the moral order in the universe. Mitra is often associated with the sun and light.
  • praśastibhiḥ: Meaning "with praises" or "with commendations," this word emphasizes the importance of acknowledging and honoring the divine forces.
  • dveṣoyuto: This means "attached to enmity" or "full of hatred." It highlights the negative emotions that can bind individuals and lead to suffering.
  • na: Simply means "not," indicating a negation or rejection of something.
  • duritā: This translates to "misdeeds" or "evil actions." It refers to the negative consequences of one's actions that can lead to suffering.
  • turyāma: This means "let us transcend" or "let us overcome." It suggests a call to rise above negativity and challenges.
  • martyānām: Referring to "of mortals" or "of human beings," it emphasizes the human condition and the struggles faced in life.

The mantra essentially conveys a message of devotion to the divine, invoking the energies of Agni and Mitra to help transcend negativity and misdeeds. It emphasizes the importance of surrendering to a higher power while recognizing the need to overcome hatred and evil actions in our lives.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can use this mantra as a reminder to let go of negative emotions and focus on positive energies, fostering personal development and emotional well-being.
  • Conflict Resolution: In situations of conflict, invoking the principles of friendship and harmony (as represented by Mitra) can help in resolving disputes and promoting peace.
  • Spiritual Practices: This mantra can be incorporated into daily spiritual practices, such as meditation or prayer, to cultivate a sense of connection with the divine and seek guidance in overcoming life's challenges.

In conclusion, this RigVeda mantra serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of devotion, the need to transcend negativity, and the role of divine energies in guiding human actions. By embracing these principles, individuals can lead more harmonious and fulfilling lives.
