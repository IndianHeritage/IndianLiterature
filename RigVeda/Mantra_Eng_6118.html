युवं कण्वाय नासत्याः ऋपिरिप्ताय हार्म्ये |शाश्वद ऊतीर दशस्यथ ||
yuvaṁ kaṇvāya nāsatyā ṛpiriptāya harmye | śaśvad ūtīr daśasyathaḥ ||
  • yuvaṁ - This word translates to "young" or "youthful." It signifies vitality, energy, and the potential for growth and development.
  • kaṇvāya - Referring to "the sage Kanva," this term connects the mantra to wisdom and knowledge. Kanva is often associated with enlightenment and spiritual insight.
  • nāsatyā - This word means "the two Asvins," who are divine twin horsemen in Vedic mythology, symbolizing health, medicine, and the duality of existence.
  • ṛpiriptāya - This can be interpreted as "to the one who is worthy of praise" or "to the one who is deserving of offerings." It emphasizes the importance of recognizing and honoring those who contribute positively to society.
  • harmye - Meaning "in the abode" or "in the home," this word signifies a place of safety, comfort, and community.
  • śaśvad - This translates to "everlasting" or "eternal," indicating something that is timeless and enduring.
  • ūtīr - This word means "to be praised" or "to be celebrated," suggesting the importance of recognition and appreciation in life.
  • daśasyathaḥ - This can be interpreted as "of the ten," which may refer to the ten directions or aspects of life, symbolizing completeness and wholeness.

The mantra can be understood as a call to honor youth and wisdom, recognizing the contributions of those who are deserving of praise, and celebrating the eternal aspects of life within a safe and nurturing community. It emphasizes the importance of vitality, knowledge, and the duality of existence, as represented by the Asvins.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios:

  • Education and Mentorship: The emphasis on youth and wisdom can inspire educational systems to foster environments where young people are encouraged to learn from experienced mentors, creating a cycle of knowledge transfer.
  • Healthcare and Well-being: The reference to the Asvins highlights the importance of health and wellness. This can be seen in modern healthcare practices that prioritize holistic approaches to well-being.
  • Community Building: The idea of a safe and nurturing home resonates with the need for strong community ties. Initiatives that promote community support and recognition of individual contributions can lead to a more harmonious society.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of youth, wisdom, health, and community. It encourages us to honor those who contribute positively to our lives and to create environments where everyone can thrive. By applying these principles in our daily lives, we can foster a more supportive and enriching society.
