प्र होत्रे पूर्व्यं वचो 'ग्नये भरता बृहत् | विपां ज्योतिंषि बिभ्रते न वेधसे ||
pra hotre pūrvyaṁ vaco 'gnaye bharatā bṛhat | vipāṁ jyotīṁṣi bibhrate na vedhase ||
  • pra hotre: This term refers to the invocation or calling upon a deity or divine force. In a real-life context, this can be likened to how individuals seek guidance or blessings in various situations, such as starting a new venture or during significant life events.
  • pūrvyaṁ vaco: This phrase translates to "the words of the ancients" or "the wisdom of the past." It emphasizes the importance of learning from history and tradition. For example, in modern education, we often study historical texts and philosophies to gain insights that can guide our current decisions.
  • 'gnaye: This word refers to the divine fire or energy, symbolizing transformation and purification. In everyday life, this can be seen in the way people undergo personal growth or change, often through challenges that ignite their inner strength.
  • bharatā bṛhat: This can be interpreted as "the great expanse of Bharat (India)." It signifies the vastness of culture, knowledge, and spirituality in India. In a contemporary context, this can relate to the diverse cultural heritage of India, which continues to influence global perspectives.
  • vipāṁ jyotīṁṣi bibhrate: This phrase means "those who hold the light of knowledge." It highlights the value of wisdom and enlightenment. In real life, this can be seen in leaders, educators, and thinkers who illuminate paths for others through their knowledge and insights.
  • na vedhase: This translates to "not for the one who is ignorant." It suggests that knowledge and wisdom are not meant for those who do not seek to understand or learn. In practical terms, this can be observed in how individuals who are open to learning and growth often achieve greater success and fulfillment in life.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of invoking divine guidance, learning from the wisdom of the past, and the transformative power of knowledge. It serves as a reminder that enlightenment is available to those who seek it, and that cultural heritage plays a crucial role in shaping our understanding of the world. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to pursue knowledge, honor their traditions, and seek guidance in their personal and professional lives, ultimately leading to a more enlightened and fulfilling existence.
