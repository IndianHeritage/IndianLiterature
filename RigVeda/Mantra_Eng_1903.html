आ वाम अश्वासः शुचयः पयस्पा वातरंहसो दिव्यासो अत्याः |मनोजुवो वृषाणो वीटपृष्ठा एहा स्वराजो अश्विनाः वहन्तु ||
ā vām aśvāsaḥ śucayaḥ payaspā vātaraṁhaso divyāso atyāḥ | manojuvo vṛṣaṇo vītapṛṣṭhā eha svarājo aśvinā vahantu ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā vām: From you both
    • aśvāsaḥ: The horses (or energy)
    • śucayaḥ: Pure or auspicious
    • payaspā: The nourishing or life-giving
    • vātaraṁhaso: Of the powerful winds
    • divyāso: Divine or celestial
    • atyāḥ: Beyond or transcendent
    • manojuvo: Born of the mind
    • vṛṣaṇo: The strong or powerful
    • vītapṛṣṭhā: With the back of the tree (symbolizing support)
    • eha: Here
    • svarājo: The lord of sound or voice
    • aśvinā: By the Ashvins (the twin deities)
    • vahantu: May they carry or bring
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra invokes the divine energies represented by the Ashvins, who are known for their healing and life-giving qualities.
    • The mention of "pure" and "nourishing" signifies the importance of maintaining purity in thoughts and actions to receive divine blessings.
    • The reference to "powerful winds" can be seen as a metaphor for the dynamic forces of nature that can either create or destroy, emphasizing the need for balance.
    • The phrase "born of the mind" suggests that our thoughts and intentions shape our reality, highlighting the significance of positive thinking.
    • The "strong" and "support" elements indicate the importance of having a solid foundation in life, whether it be through relationships, community, or personal strength.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a personal context, one might reflect on this mantra during meditation, focusing on purifying their thoughts and intentions to attract positive outcomes.
    • In a community setting, this mantra can inspire collective efforts towards health and well-being, encouraging individuals to support one another in achieving common goals.
    • In environmental discussions, the reference to "powerful winds" can remind us of the need to respect and protect nature, acknowledging its dual capacity for creation and destruction.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of mind, body, and spirit, urging us to cultivate purity and strength in our lives.
    • Its significance lies in the encouragement to harness divine energies for personal and communal growth, emphasizing the power of positive thought and action.
    • In real-life scenarios, it can be applied in various contexts, from personal development to community health initiatives, reinforcing the idea that we are all part of a larger cosmic order.
