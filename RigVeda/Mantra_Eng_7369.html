एता च्यौत्नानि ते कृतā वर्षिष्ठानि परीनसā | हृदा वीढ्व अधारयः ||
etā cyautnāni te kṛtā varṣiṣṭhāni parīṇasā | hṛdā vīḍv adhārayaḥ ||
  • etā: This word translates to "these" or "this," indicating a focus on specific elements or aspects.
  • cyautnāni: This term can be understood as "the offerings" or "the gifts," referring to the things that are given or presented.
  • te: Meaning "your," this word denotes possession or belonging, indicating that the offerings are directed towards a specific entity.
  • kṛtā: This translates to "made" or "created," suggesting that the offerings have been prepared with intention.
  • varṣiṣṭhāni: This word means "the best" or "the most excellent," highlighting the quality of the offerings being presented.
  • parīṇasā: This can be interpreted as "with fullness" or "in abundance," indicating that the offerings are not just sufficient but plentiful.
  • hṛdā: Meaning "with the heart," this word emphasizes the emotional and spiritual aspect of the offerings, suggesting sincerity and devotion.
  • vīḍv: This term can be understood as "wise" or "knowledgeable," indicating that the act of offering is done with understanding and awareness.
  • adhārayaḥ: This translates to "support" or "uphold," suggesting that the offerings are meant to sustain or support the divine or the greater good.

The mantra can be interpreted as a heartfelt offering made with wisdom and intention, directed towards a higher power or the universe. It emphasizes the importance of quality and abundance in what is given, as well as the sincerity behind the act of giving.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as:

  • Charity and Philanthropy: When donating to those in need, one should do so with a full heart, ensuring that the contributions are meaningful and abundant.
  • Personal Relationships: In relationships, offering love, support, and understanding should be done sincerely and with the intention of nurturing the bond.
  • Spiritual Practices: In rituals or prayers, the offerings made to deities or the universe should be done with mindfulness and respect, reflecting the quality and intention behind them.

In conclusion, this mantra signifies the importance of intentional and heartfelt offerings in various aspects of life. It teaches us that what we give, whether material or emotional, should come from a place of sincerity and abundance, ultimately supporting the greater good and fostering connections with others and the divine.
