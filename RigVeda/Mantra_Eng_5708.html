प्र वां रथो मनोजवाऽ इयर्ति तिरो राजांस्य अश्विनाऽ शतोतिः |अस्मभ्यं सूर्यावसू इयानः ||
pra vāṁ ratho manojavā iyarti tiro rajāṁsy aśvinā śatotiḥ | asmabhyaṁ sūryāvasū iyānaḥ ||
  • pra vāṁ ratho manojavā: This phrase can be translated as "May the swift chariot of thought (or mind) come to us." Here, "ratho" refers to a chariot, symbolizing the journey of thoughts and ideas. The term "manojavā" indicates speed and agility, suggesting that our thoughts should be quick and effective.
  • iyarti tiro rajāṁsy: This part means "may it (the chariot) carry us beyond the obstacles." "Iyarti" implies movement or progress, while "tiro rajāṁsy" refers to overcoming barriers or challenges. This can be likened to how we navigate through life's difficulties, striving to achieve our goals despite the hurdles we face.
  • aśvinā śatotiḥ: This translates to "with the help of the Aśvins, the divine twin horsemen." The Aśvins are deities associated with health, medicine, and the dawn. They symbolize assistance and guidance in our endeavors. In real life, this can be seen as seeking help from mentors, friends, or divine forces when we are in need.
  • asmabhyaṁ sūryāvasū iyānaḥ: This phrase means "may the radiant ones (the Sun) guide us." "Asmabhyaṁ" refers to us, while "sūryāvasū" signifies the sun's brilliance and energy. This can be interpreted as a call for enlightenment and clarity in our lives, akin to how sunlight illuminates our surroundings, helping us see the path ahead.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the importance of swift and clear thinking, overcoming obstacles, seeking divine assistance, and being guided by enlightenment. In real life, it serves as a reminder to harness our mental agility, seek help when needed, and strive for clarity in our pursuits. This mantra can be applicable in various scenarios, such as during challenging times in our careers, personal relationships, or health issues, encouraging us to remain focused and resilient while seeking guidance and support from others.
