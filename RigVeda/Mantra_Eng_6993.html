यच च गोषु दुष्स्वप्न्यं यच चास्मे दूहितर दिवः |तृताय तद विभावरी आप्त्याय परा वहानेहसो वा ऊतयः सुतयः वा ऊतयः ||
yac ca goṣu duṣṣvapnyaṁ yac cāsme duhitar divaḥ | tritāya tad vibhāvary āptyāya parā vahānehaso va ūtayaḥ suūtayo va ūtayaḥ ||
  • Mantra: "yac ca goṣu duṣṣvapnyaṁ yac cāsme duhitar divaḥ | tritāya tad vibhāvary āptyāya parā vahānehaso va ūtayaḥ suūtayo va ūtayaḥ ||"
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yac: whatever
    • ca: and
    • goṣu: in the cows (or in the wealth)
    • duṣṣvapnyaṁ: difficult to attain
    • yac: whatever
    • cāsme: we are
    • duhitar: daughter
    • divaḥ: of the heavens
    • tritāya: the third
    • tad: that
    • vibhāvary: which is manifest
    • āptyāya: for the purpose of fulfillment
    • parā: supreme
    • vahānehaso: of the one who carries
    • va: or
    • ūtayaḥ: those who are uplifted
    • suūtayo: those who are well uplifted
    • va: or
    • ūtayaḥ: those who are uplifted
  • Explanation:
    • This mantra speaks about the various forms of wealth and fulfillment, both material and spiritual.
    • The phrase "yac ca goṣu duṣṣvapnyaṁ" refers to the wealth that is difficult to attain, symbolized by cows, which in ancient times represented prosperity and abundance.
    • "yac cāsme duhitar divaḥ" indicates the connection to the divine or heavenly aspects, suggesting that our endeavors are not just for earthly gains but also for spiritual elevation.
    • The term "tritāya" signifies the third aspect, which can be interpreted as the balance between material wealth, spiritual wealth, and the relationships we cultivate.
    • "tad vibhāvary āptyāya" emphasizes the importance of manifesting our desires and fulfilling our goals, whether they are related to personal growth or community welfare.
    • The concluding part "vahānehaso va ūtayaḥ suūtayo va ūtayaḥ" suggests that those who carry these aspirations, whether they are uplifted or well uplifted, are on a path of growth and enlightenment.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a business context, this mantra can be applied to the pursuit of success. Entrepreneurs often face challenges (duṣṣvapnyaṁ) in achieving their goals (goṣu), but they must also remember the importance of ethical practices and community support (duhitar divaḥ).
    • In personal relationships, the balance between giving and receiving (tritāya) is crucial. One must strive for fulfillment in both personal and spiritual realms, ensuring that relationships are nurtured and valued.
    • In community service, individuals who uplift others (vahānehaso) contribute to a greater good, embodying the essence of this mantra by manifesting their intentions for the benefit of all.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of material and spiritual wealth.
    • It encourages individuals to pursue their goals while maintaining ethical standards and fostering relationships that uplift themselves and others.
    • In essence, it highlights the importance of balance in life, urging us to seek fulfillment not just for ourselves but for the greater community.
