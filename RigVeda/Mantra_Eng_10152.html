उं मापीताऽयंषत रथम अश्वा इवाशवः |कुवित सोमस्यापामिति ||
un mā pītā ayaṁsata ratham aśvā ivāśavaḥ | kuvit somasyāpām iti ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • un: This can be interpreted as "not" or "without."
    • mā: This means "mother" or "nurturer."
    • pītā: This translates to "father" or "protector."
    • ayaṁsata: This can mean "to be strong" or "to be powerful."
    • ratham: This refers to a "chariot," symbolizing movement or progress.
    • aśvā: This means "horses," often representing speed and strength.
    • ivāśavaḥ: This can be interpreted as "like the swift" or "like the powerful."
    • kuvit: This means "to be wise" or "to have good judgment."
    • somasyāpām: This refers to "the essence of the moon" or "nectar," symbolizing nourishment and vitality.
    • iti: This is a concluding particle, often used to indicate the end of a statement.
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks to the importance of both maternal and paternal figures in providing strength and guidance.
    • The "chariot" symbolizes the journey of life, where the "horses" represent the energy and drive needed to move forward.
    • The phrase "like the swift" suggests the need for agility and quick thinking in navigating life's challenges.
    • The reference to "the essence of the moon" indicates the importance of nourishment, both physical and spiritual, in achieving one's goals.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a family setting, the nurturing role of a mother and the protective role of a father can be seen as foundational for a child's development, providing both emotional support and guidance.
    • In a professional context, a team leader (akin to a parent) guides their team (the chariot) towards achieving goals, with each team member contributing their unique strengths (the horses).
    • In sports, a coach (parental figure) helps athletes (the chariot) harness their skills (the horses) to achieve victory, emphasizing the need for quick decision-making (like the swift).
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of familial and supportive relationships in fostering strength and wisdom.
    • It highlights the importance of guidance and nourishment in both personal and professional journeys.
    • In real life, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to better decision-making, stronger relationships, and a more fulfilling life journey.
