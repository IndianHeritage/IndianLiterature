यो धारणया पावकया परिप्रस्यन्दते सुतः |इन्दुर अश्वो न कृतव्यः ||
yo dhārayā pāvakayā pariprasyandate sutaḥ | indur aśvo na kṛtvyaḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • yo: who
    • dhārayā: by holding or supporting
    • pāvakayā: by the purifying or sacred (often referring to fire)
    • pariprasyandate: flows or is poured forth
    • sutaḥ: the offspring or the son
    • indur: the moon
    • aśvo: the horse
    • na: not
    • kṛtvyaḥ: to be made or created
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of the divine connection between the sacred fire (pāvak) and the offspring (suta). It suggests that through the purifying power of fire, blessings and prosperity flow.
    • The mention of the moon (indur) and the horse (aśvo) symbolizes the celestial and earthly realms, indicating a balance between spiritual and material aspects of life.
    • The phrase "not to be made" implies that certain divine qualities or blessings are inherent and do not require human intervention to manifest.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In many cultures, fire is seen as a purifying element. For instance, in Hindu rituals, the sacred fire (yajna) is central to ceremonies, symbolizing the connection between the earthly and divine.
    • The moon is often associated with emotions and intuition. In real life, people may find that their emotional states are influenced by lunar phases, reflecting the mantra's connection to the celestial.
    • The horse symbolizes strength and freedom. In modern contexts, horses are often used in therapy (equine therapy) to help individuals connect with their emotions and find healing.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of purity, divine blessings, and the interconnectedness of all elements in life.
    • Its significance lies in the understanding that spiritual practices can lead to material and emotional well-being, highlighting the balance between the sacred and the mundane.
    • In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek purification through various means—be it through meditation, rituals, or connecting with nature—ultimately leading to a harmonious existence.
