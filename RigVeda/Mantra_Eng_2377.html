जुषेथां यज्ञं बोधतं हवस्य मे सत्तो होतā निविदः पूर्व्याऽनु |अच्छा राजानां नम एत्य आवृतं प्रशास्त्राद आपिबतं सोम्यम् मधु ||
juṣethāṁ yajñam bodhataṁ havasya me satto hotā nividaḥ pūrvyā anu | acchā rājānā nama ety āvṛtam praśāstrād ā pibataṁ somyam madhu ||
  • juṣethāṁ: This word translates to "may you enjoy" or "may you take pleasure in." It signifies the importance of enjoyment and fulfillment in life.
  • yajñam: Refers to "sacrifice" or "offering." In the Vedic context, yajña is a ritual of offerings to the deities, symbolizing the interconnectedness of all beings.
  • bodhataṁ: This means "to be enlightened" or "to gain knowledge." It emphasizes the pursuit of wisdom and understanding in our actions.
  • havasya: Translates to "of the offering" or "of the sacrifice." It highlights the significance of the offerings made in rituals and their impact on the community.
  • me: Means "my," indicating a personal connection to the offerings and the rituals performed.
  • satto: Refers to "truth" or "reality." It underscores the importance of sincerity and authenticity in our actions.
  • hotā: This word means "the priest" or "the one who invokes." It represents the role of the mediator between the divine and the human realms.
  • nividaḥ: Translates to "the wise" or "the knowledgeable." It signifies the value of wisdom in guiding our actions and decisions.
  • pūrvyā: Means "from the past" or "ancient." It connects the present actions to the wisdom and traditions of our ancestors.
  • anu: This word means "following" or "after." It indicates continuity and the importance of following the right path.
  • acchā: Translates to "good" or "auspicious." It emphasizes the positive outcomes of righteous actions.
  • rājānā: Refers to "kings" or "rulers." It signifies leadership and the responsibility of those in power to act for the welfare of all.
  • nama: Means "salutation" or "obeisance." It reflects humility and respect towards the divine and the community.
  • ety: Translates to "coming" or "arriving." It signifies the arrival of blessings or positive energies.
  • āvṛtam: Means "enveloped" or "surrounded." It indicates being surrounded by positive influences and energies.
  • praśāstrād: Refers to "from the teachings" or "from the scriptures." It emphasizes the importance of learning and guidance from sacred texts.
  • ā: Means "from" or "out of." It indicates the source of the blessings or knowledge.
  • pibataṁ: Translates to "drink" or "consume." It signifies the act of taking in knowledge or blessings.
  • somyam: Refers to "the divine nectar" or "soma," which symbolizes immortality and bliss.
  • madhu: Means "honey," representing sweetness, joy, and the rewards of righteous actions.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of living a life filled with joy, wisdom, and righteousness. It emphasizes the importance of rituals and offerings, not just as acts of devotion but as means to connect with the divine and the community. In real life, this can be applied by engaging in acts of kindness, participating in community service, and seeking knowledge and truth in our daily actions. The mantra serves as a reminder that our actions should be guided by wisdom and a sense of responsibility towards others, ultimately leading to a harmonious and fulfilling life.
