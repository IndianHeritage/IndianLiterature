मन्त्र: उता गावा इवादन्त्य उता वेष्मेव दृष्ट्यते |उतो अरण्यानि: सायं शकटीर इव सरजति ||
uta gāva ivādanty uta veśmeva dṛśyate | uto araṇyāniḥ sāyaṁ śakaṭīr iva sarjati ||
  • Translation: The mantra can be translated as: "Like the cows that are called, like the home that is seen, like the evening that brings forth the chariot, so does the world manifest."
  • Word-by-Word Explanation:
    • uta: This word can mean "also" or "furthermore," indicating a continuation or addition to a thought.
    • gāva: Refers to "cows," which are often symbolic of nourishment and abundance in Vedic literature.
    • ivādanty: Means "they are called" or "they speak," suggesting communication or a call to action.
    • veśmeva: Translates to "like a home," symbolizing safety, comfort, and belonging.
    • dṛśyate: Means "is seen" or "is perceived," indicating visibility or recognition.
    • araṇyāniḥ: Refers to "the evening" or "the wilderness," which can symbolize transition and the unknown.
    • sāyaṁ: Means "evening," a time of change and reflection.
    • śakaṭīr: Translates to "the chariot," representing movement, progress, and the journey of life.
    • iva: Means "like" or "as," used for comparison.
    • sarjati: Means "brings forth" or "creates," indicating the act of manifestation.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • The calling of cows can be likened to the way we call our loved ones or friends, emphasizing the importance of connection and communication in our lives.
    • The concept of a home can be related to the feeling of safety and belonging we seek in our communities and relationships.
    • The evening symbolizes the transitions we face in life, such as moving from one phase to another, like graduating from school or changing jobs.
    • The chariot represents our ambitions and goals, illustrating how we must navigate through life's challenges to reach our destinations.
  • Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its portrayal of the interconnectedness of life and the various elements that contribute to our existence. It teaches us to recognize the calls of our surroundings, the importance of home and community, and the inevitability of change. In real life, this mantra can be applied to our daily experiences, reminding us to appreciate the journey, embrace transitions, and maintain connections with those around us. It encourages us to be aware of the manifestations in our lives and to navigate them with intention and purpose.
