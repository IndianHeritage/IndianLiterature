मन्त्र: श्येनाव इव पतथो हव्यदातये सोमं सुतं महिषेवावा गच्छतः | सजोषसां उषसा सूर्येण च त्रिर वर्तिर यातम अश्विनाः ||
śyenāv iva patatho havyadātaye somaṁ sutam mahiṣevāva gacchathaḥ | sajoṣasā uṣasā sūryeṇa ca trir vartir yātam aśvinā ||
  • śyenāv iva patatho: This phrase translates to "like the hawks in their flight." Here, the hawk symbolizes speed, agility, and precision. In real life, this can be related to individuals or teams that are focused and determined in achieving their goals, much like athletes or professionals who train rigorously to excel in their fields.
  • havyadātaye: This means "for the offering." It signifies the importance of giving and sharing, which is a fundamental aspect of many cultures. For example, in community service, individuals come together to offer their time and resources to help those in need, reflecting the spirit of this mantra.
  • somaṁ sutam: "the soma, the pressed drink." Soma is often associated with vitality and divine inspiration. In modern terms, this can be likened to the pursuit of knowledge and enlightenment, such as through education or spiritual practices that enhance one's understanding of life.
  • mahiṣevāva gacchathaḥ: This translates to "like the powerful moving forward." It emphasizes strength and progress. In a real-life context, this can be seen in leaders who inspire their teams to overcome challenges and move towards success, embodying resilience and determination.
  • sajoṣasā uṣasā: "with the dawn." Dawn symbolizes new beginnings and opportunities. This can be related to personal growth, where individuals embrace change and start anew, much like how each day offers a fresh start to pursue one's dreams.
  • sūryeṇa ca: "and with the sun." The sun represents energy, life, and clarity. In practical terms, this can be associated with the importance of maintaining a positive outlook and energy in life, akin to how sunlight is essential for growth in nature.
  • trir vartir yātam aśvinā: "may we go thrice with the Ashvins." The Ashvins are twin deities associated with health and medicine. This part of the mantra can be interpreted as a prayer for health and well-being, reflecting the importance of taking care of one's physical and mental health in everyday life.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda encapsulates themes of agility, offering, vitality, strength, new beginnings, positivity, and health. Its significance lies in its call for individuals to embody these qualities in their lives. In real-life scenarios, it encourages us to pursue our goals with determination, contribute to our communities, seek knowledge, embrace change, maintain a positive outlook, and prioritize our well-being. By integrating these principles, we can lead more fulfilling and purposeful lives.
