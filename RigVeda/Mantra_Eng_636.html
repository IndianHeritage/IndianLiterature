युधा युधम् उप गहेद एषि धृष्णुया पुरा पुरं सम इदं हंस्य ओजसा |नम्या यद इन्द्र सख्या परावती निबर्हयो नमुचिं नाम मायिनम् ||
yudhā yudham upa ghed eṣi dhṛṣṇuyā purā puraṁ sam idaṁ haṁsy ojasā | namyā yad indra sakhyā parāvati nibarhayo namuciṁ nāma māyinam ||
  • yudhā yudham: This phrase can be translated as "in battle, the battle." It emphasizes the idea of engaging in conflict or struggle, which can be interpreted both literally and metaphorically. In real life, this can relate to personal challenges, professional competitions, or even internal conflicts.
  • upa ghed eṣi: This means "approaching with determination." It signifies the importance of being resolute and focused when facing challenges. For example, a student preparing for an important exam must approach their studies with dedication and a clear goal in mind.
  • dhṛṣṇuyā purā puraṁ: This translates to "with courage, the ancient city." Here, "courage" is highlighted as a vital quality needed to overcome obstacles. In a real-life scenario, a person might need to summon their courage to confront a difficult situation, such as speaking in public or standing up for a cause they believe in.
  • sam idaṁ haṁsy ojasā: This phrase means "this is the strength of the united." It suggests that collective strength and unity are essential in overcoming challenges. For instance, a team working together on a project can achieve greater success than individuals working in isolation.
  • namyā yad indra sakhyā: This translates to "bowing to the friendship of Indra." Indra, in Vedic literature, is often seen as a symbol of power and leadership. This line emphasizes the importance of alliances and friendships in achieving success. In real life, networking and building relationships can open doors to new opportunities.
  • parāvati nibarhayo: This means "to overcome the obstacles." It highlights the need to confront and surmount challenges that may arise. For example, an entrepreneur may face numerous hurdles while starting a business, but perseverance and strategic planning can help them navigate these difficulties.
  • namuciṁ nāma māyinam: This translates to "the one who binds with illusion." It refers to the deceptive nature of challenges that may seem insurmountable. Recognizing and understanding these illusions can help individuals make better decisions. For instance, a person might feel overwhelmed by societal expectations, but by questioning these norms, they can find their true path.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of facing challenges with courage, unity, and strategic thinking. It teaches us that while struggles are a part of life, approaching them with determination and the support of others can lead to success. In real-life scenarios, whether in personal endeavors, professional settings, or community efforts, the principles outlined in this mantra can guide individuals to navigate their battles effectively and emerge victorious.
