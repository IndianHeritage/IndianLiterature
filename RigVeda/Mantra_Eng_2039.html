देव बर्हिर वर्धमानं सुवीरं स्थीर्णं राये सुभरं वेद्यमस्याम् |घृतनक्तं वसवः सीदतेदं विश्वे देवाः आदित्याः यज्ञियासः ||
deva barhir vardhamānaṁ suvīraṁ stīrṇaṁ rāye subharaṁ vedy asyām | ghṛtenāktaṁ vasavaḥ sīdatedaṁ viśve devā ādityā yajñiyāsaḥ ||
  • Deva: This word translates to "gods" or "divine beings." In the context of the mantra, it refers to the celestial powers that govern the universe.
  • Barhir: Meaning "outside" or "external," it signifies the external manifestations of divine energy.
  • Vardhamānaṁ: This term means "growing" or "increasing," indicating the expansion of prosperity and abundance.
  • Suvīraṁ: Translated as "good strength" or "valiant," it refers to the strength that is beneficial and righteous.
  • Stīrṇaṁ: This means "steady" or "firm," suggesting stability and resilience in the face of challenges.
  • Rāye: This word means "wealth" or "prosperity," emphasizing the importance of material and spiritual abundance.
  • Subharaṁ: Meaning "well-off" or "prosperous," it highlights the blessings of abundance and well-being.
  • Vedy: This term refers to "knowledge" or "wisdom," indicating the importance of understanding and enlightenment.
  • Ghr̥tenāktaṁ: This means "smeared with ghee," symbolizing purity and the sacredness of offerings in rituals.
  • Vasavaḥ: Referring to the "Vasus," a group of deities associated with abundance and prosperity.
  • Sīdatedaṁ: This term means "to be established" or "to be set," indicating the establishment of divine blessings in one's life.
  • Viśve: Meaning "all" or "universal," it signifies the inclusiveness of divine blessings.
  • Devā: Again referring to "gods," emphasizing the role of divine forces in human affairs.
  • Ādityā: This term refers to the "Adityas," solar deities representing light, truth, and cosmic order.
  • Yajñiyāsaḥ: Meaning "sacrificial offerings," it highlights the importance of rituals and offerings in seeking divine favor.

The mantra essentially invokes the divine forces to bless the individual with growth, strength, stability, and prosperity. It emphasizes the importance of knowledge and the sacredness of offerings in rituals, suggesting that through sincere devotion and righteous actions, one can attract divine blessings.


Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for divine intervention in the pursuit of prosperity and well-being. In real-life scenarios, it can be applicable in various contexts such as:

  • Personal Growth: Individuals can recite this mantra during times of personal development, seeking strength and stability in their endeavors.
  • Business Ventures: Entrepreneurs may use this mantra to invoke blessings for success and prosperity in their business pursuits.
  • Rituals and Offerings: It can be incorporated into rituals to enhance the sacredness of offerings made to deities, ensuring a connection with divine energies.
  • Community Well-being: Communities can chant this mantra collectively to seek blessings for overall prosperity and harmony.

In essence, this mantra serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of divine forces and human efforts in achieving a prosperous and fulfilling life.
