मन्त्र: आयुद्ध इद युधा वृत्तं शूर आजाति सत्वभिः |येषाम इन्द्रो युवा सखा ||
ayuddha id yudhā vṛtaṁ śūra ājati satvabhiḥ | yeṣām indro yuvā sakhā ||
  • ayuddha: This term translates to "without battle" or "peaceful." It signifies a state of harmony and the absence of conflict.
  • id: This word can be interpreted as "this" or "here," indicating a focus on the present moment or situation.
  • yudhā: Meaning "in battle" or "war," it represents the challenges and struggles one faces in life.
  • vṛtaṁ: This word means "surrounded" or "enclosed," suggesting being enveloped by circumstances or situations.
  • śūra: Translated as "brave" or "valiant," it refers to those who possess courage and strength in the face of adversity.
  • ājati: This term means "arises" or "comes forth," indicating the emergence of qualities or situations.
  • satvabhiḥ: This word translates to "with strength" or "with valor," emphasizing the importance of inner strength and resilience.
  • yeṣām: Meaning "of whom," it refers to a group or community.
  • indro: This term refers to "Indra," the king of gods in Vedic literature, symbolizing power and authority.
  • yuvā: Translated as "young" or "youthful," it signifies vitality, energy, and potential.
  • sakhā: This word means "friend" or "ally," indicating the importance of companionship and support in overcoming challenges.

The mantra speaks to the duality of life, highlighting the contrast between conflict and peace. It emphasizes the qualities of bravery and strength that arise in individuals, particularly in challenging situations. The reference to Indra as a youthful ally suggests that divine support and friendship are crucial in navigating life's battles.


In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied to various situations, such as:

  • Conflict Resolution: In personal or professional conflicts, the mantra encourages individuals to seek peaceful resolutions rather than engaging in battles. For example, in a workplace disagreement, approaching the situation with a mindset of collaboration and understanding can lead to a more harmonious outcome.
  • Personal Challenges: When facing difficulties, such as health issues or financial struggles, the mantra reminds individuals to draw upon their inner strength and courage. For instance, someone battling a chronic illness may find empowerment in their resilience and the support of friends and family.
  • Community Support: The emphasis on friendship and alliances highlights the importance of community. In times of crisis, such as natural disasters, communities often come together to support one another, embodying the spirit of the mantra.

In conclusion, this mantra serves as a reminder of the balance between conflict and peace, the importance of inner strength, and the value of companionship. It encourages individuals to face challenges with bravery and to seek harmony in their interactions, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and resilient life.
