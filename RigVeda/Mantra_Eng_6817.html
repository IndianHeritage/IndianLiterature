अभ्यर्च नाभाकवद इन्द्राग्नीय यजसā गिरा |ययोर्विश्वम इदं जगद इयं द्यौः पृथिवी मह्य उपस्तहे बिभृतो वसु नभन्ताम् अन्यके समे ||
abhy arca nabhākavad indrāgnī yajasā girā | yayor viśvam idaṁ jagad iyaṁ dyauḥ pṛthivī mahy u1pasthe bibhṛto vasu nabhantām anyake same ||
  • Mantra: "abhy arca nabhākavad indrāgnī yajasā girā | yayor viśvam idaṁ jagad iyaṁ dyauḥ pṛthivī mahy u1pasthe bibhṛto vasu nabhantām anyake same ||"
  • Translation: This mantra invokes the divine energies of Indra and Agni, asking for their blessings and support in the creation and sustenance of the universe. It acknowledges the interconnectedness of the sky (dyauḥ) and the earth (pṛthivī), and the abundance (vasu) that they provide.
  • Word Breakdown:
    • abhy: towards, in the direction of
    • arca: worship, adoration
    • nabhākavad: like the sky, celestial
    • indrāgnī: Indra (the king of gods) and Agni (the fire god)
    • yajasā: through worship, by sacrifice
    • girā: through speech, with words
    • yayor: of whom, whose
    • viśvam: the whole, all
    • idaṁ: this
    • jagad: world, universe
    • iyaṁ: this
    • dyauḥ: sky
    • pṛthivī: earth
    • mahy: for me, to me
    • u1pasthe: in the lap, in the embrace
    • bibhṛto: holding, sustaining
    • vasu: wealth, abundance
    • nabhantām: of the heavens
    • anyake: other, different
    • same: in harmony, together
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • This mantra can be related to the practice of environmental conservation, where we recognize the importance of both the earth and sky in sustaining life. For instance, planting trees helps in maintaining the balance between the earth and the atmosphere.
    • In a community gathering, when people come together to celebrate a festival, they often invoke the blessings of nature and the divine, similar to the worship of Indra and Agni in this mantra.
    • In personal life, one can reflect on the interconnectedness of all beings and the universe, fostering a sense of gratitude and responsibility towards nature.
  • Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the significance of worship and gratitude towards the divine forces that sustain the universe. It teaches us about the interconnectedness of all elements of nature and encourages us to live in harmony with our surroundings. In real life, this can be applied through practices that promote environmental sustainability, community bonding, and personal reflection on our relationship with nature.
