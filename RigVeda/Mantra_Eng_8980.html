आ निवर्त निवार्तय पुनर न इन्द्र गा देहि |जीवाभिर भुनजामहि ||
ā nivarta ni vartaya punar na indra gā dehi | jīvābhir bhunajāmahai ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • ā nivarta: "Come back" or "Return."
    • ni vartaya: "Do not turn away" or "Do not depart."
    • punar: "Again" or "Once more."
    • na: "Not."
    • indra: "O Indra," referring to the king of gods, symbolizing strength and power.
    • gā: "Give" or "Bestow."
    • dehi: "Grant" or "Provide."
    • jīvābhir: "With life" or "By living beings."
    • bhunajāmahai: "We enjoy" or "We partake."
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra expresses a plea for the return of blessings and prosperity.
    • It emphasizes the importance of not turning away from the divine or the source of strength.
    • The invocation of Indra signifies a call for divine intervention and support in our lives.
    • It highlights the interconnectedness of life and the need for sustenance and support from the divine.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In times of hardship, individuals often seek spiritual guidance or support, similar to invoking Indra for strength.
    • Communities coming together to support one another during crises reflect the essence of "jīvābhir bhunajāmahai," where collective strength is sought.
    • In personal development, the mantra can inspire individuals to seek renewal and not shy away from challenges, embodying the spirit of "ā nivarta ni vartaya."
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the importance of seeking divine support and not turning away from challenges.
    • It encourages individuals to embrace life and its struggles, knowing that they can call upon higher powers for assistance.
    • In a broader context, it signifies the need for community and support systems in overcoming life's obstacles.
    • Overall, it is applicable in various life scenarios, from personal struggles to communal efforts, emphasizing resilience and divine connection.
