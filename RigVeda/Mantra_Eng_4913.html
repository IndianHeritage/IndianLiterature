नū सद्मानं दिव्यं नंशि देवाः भारद्वाजः सुमतिं याति होताः |आसानेभिर यजमानो मियेदैः देवानां जन्म वसूयुर ववन्द ||
nū sadmānaṁ divyaṁ naṁśi devā bhāradvājaḥ sumatiṁ yāti hotā | āsānebhir yajamāno miyedhair devānāṁ janma vasūyur vavanda ||
  • nū sadmānaṁ divyaṁ: This phrase translates to "O divine abode." It refers to a sacred or heavenly place, often associated with spiritual enlightenment or divine presence. In real life, this can be likened to a peaceful sanctuary or a place of worship where individuals seek solace and connection with the divine.
  • naṁśi devā bhāradvājaḥ: This means "the gods, O Bhāradvāja." Bhāradvāja is a sage or a revered figure in Vedic literature. This part emphasizes the connection between the divine and the sage, suggesting that the sage is invoking the presence of the gods. In modern terms, this can be seen as a call for guidance and wisdom from higher powers, similar to how individuals seek advice from mentors or spiritual leaders.
  • sumatiṁ yāti hotā: This translates to "the priest (hotā) attains good understanding." The hotā is a priest who performs rituals and sacrifices. This line highlights the importance of wisdom and understanding in spiritual practices. In everyday life, this can be compared to the idea that those who engage in thoughtful reflection and seek knowledge will achieve clarity and insight in their lives.
  • āsānebhir yajamāno: This means "the one who is performing the sacrifice." It refers to the individual or community engaging in rituals. In contemporary society, this can be related to the act of participating in community service or charitable activities, where individuals come together for a common cause.
  • miyedhair devānāṁ: This translates to "with the help of the gods." It emphasizes the belief that divine assistance is crucial in achieving one's goals. In real life, this can be seen in the way people often pray or seek blessings before embarking on important endeavors, such as starting a new job or beginning a family.
  • janma vasūyur vavanda: This means "may the birth of prosperity be honored." It signifies the aspiration for abundance and well-being. In practical terms, this can be related to the pursuit of success and happiness in life, where individuals strive to create a prosperous environment for themselves and their families.

Conclusion: The mantra encapsulates the essence of seeking divine guidance, wisdom, and prosperity through rituals and community engagement. Its significance lies in the recognition of the interconnectedness between the divine, the sage, and the individual. In real-life scenarios, this mantra encourages individuals to seek knowledge, engage in meaningful actions, and honor the blessings of prosperity. It serves as a reminder that spiritual practices and community involvement can lead to a fulfilling and enlightened life.
