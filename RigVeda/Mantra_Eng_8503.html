शूरग्रामः सर्ववीरः सहावाण् जेत पवस्व सनीता धनानि |तिग्मायुधः क्षिप्रधन्वा समत्स्व अशाल्हः साह्वान पृथनासु शत्रून् ||
śūragrāmaḥ sarvavīraḥ sahāvāñ jetā pavasva sanitā dhanāni | tigmāyudhaḥ kṣipradhanvā samatsv aṣāḻhaḥ sāhvān pṛtanāsu śatrūn ||
  • śūragrāmaḥ: This term can be broken down into two parts: "śūra," meaning brave or valiant, and "grāma," meaning village or community. Together, it signifies a community of brave individuals.
  • sarvavīraḥ: This translates to "all heroes" or "all warriors," indicating a collective strength and valor among people.
  • sahāvāñ: This word means "together" or "united," emphasizing the importance of unity in achieving goals.
  • jetā: This means "victorious" or "conqueror," suggesting the outcome of their united efforts.
  • pavasva: This term can be interpreted as "to purify" or "to cleanse," indicating a call for spiritual or moral purification.
  • sanitā dhanāni: "Sanitā" means "to be well" or "to be healthy," and "dhanāni" refers to "wealth" or "resources." Together, they imply a state of well-being and abundance.
  • tigmāyudhaḥ: This translates to "sharp weapons," symbolizing readiness and the ability to defend oneself.
  • kṣipradhanvā: This means "swift bow," indicating agility and quickness in action.
  • samatsv: This term means "in the midst of," suggesting being present in the situation or environment.
  • aṣāḻhaḥ: This translates to "unwavering" or "steadfast," indicating resilience in the face of challenges.
  • sāhvān: This means "with strength" or "with power," emphasizing the importance of strength in overcoming obstacles.
  • pṛtanāsu: This term refers to "in battles" or "in conflicts," indicating the context of struggle.
  • śatrūn: This means "enemies," referring to the challenges or adversaries one faces.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the significance of bravery, unity, and strength in overcoming challenges and achieving victory. It highlights the importance of moral and spiritual purification, as well as the readiness to defend oneself. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applied in various contexts, such as teamwork in professional settings, community efforts in social causes, or personal challenges where resilience and unity are required. It serves as a reminder that collective strength and a courageous spirit can lead to success and well-being, both individually and as a community.
