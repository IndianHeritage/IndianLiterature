आतिष्ठन्तं परि विश्वे अभूषाण् श्रियो वसानश् चरति स्वरोजिः |महत तद वृष्णो असुरस्य नामा विश्वरूपो अमृतानि तस्थौ ||
ātiṣṭhantam pari viśve abhūṣañ chriyo vasānaś carati svarociḥ | mahat tad vṛṣṇo asurasya nāmā viśvarūpo amṛtāni tasthau ||
  • ātiṣṭhantam: This word can be interpreted as "standing firm" or "established." It signifies stability and strength in one's position or beliefs.
  • pari viśve: This phrase means "all around" or "in all directions." It suggests a sense of universality and inclusiveness.
  • abhūṣañ: This term refers to "ornaments" or "adornments." It symbolizes beauty and the embellishments that enhance one's existence.
  • chriyo: This word can be translated as "glory" or "radiance." It represents the brilliance and honor associated with a person or entity.
  • vasānaś: This means "dwelling" or "residing." It indicates a place of comfort or a state of being.
  • carati: This term means "to move" or "to act." It signifies the dynamic nature of existence and the importance of action.
  • svarociḥ: This word translates to "radiant" or "luminous." It represents enlightenment and the pursuit of knowledge.
  • mahat: This means "great" or "mighty." It signifies the importance of greatness in character or deeds.
  • tad: This word means "that" or "it." It refers to the subject being discussed.
  • vṛṣṇo: This term refers to "Vṛṣṇa," often associated with the divine or a powerful being. It symbolizes strength and divinity.
  • asurasya: This means "of the demons" or "of the adversaries." It represents challenges or obstacles in life.
  • nāmā: This translates to "name." It signifies identity and recognition.
  • viśvarūpo: This term means "of universal form." It indicates the all-encompassing nature of existence.
  • amṛtāni: This word means "immortal" or "eternal." It signifies the everlasting aspects of life and existence.
  • tasthau: This means "stood" or "remained." It indicates stability and permanence.

The mantra speaks to the essence of existence, emphasizing the importance of strength, beauty, and action in life. It suggests that one should stand firm in their beliefs while being inclusive and radiant in their actions. The reference to Vṛṣṇa and the adversaries highlights the challenges one faces, yet it also points to the greatness that can be achieved through overcoming these obstacles.


In real life, this mantra can be applied in various scenarios, such as:

  • In personal development, standing firm in one's values while embracing diversity and beauty in others can lead to a more fulfilling life.
  • In leadership, embodying the qualities of strength and radiance can inspire others to act and overcome challenges.
  • In community building, fostering an environment that values inclusivity and action can lead to collective growth and resilience.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its call for individuals to embody strength, beauty, and action while navigating the complexities of life. It encourages us to recognize the greatness within ourselves and others, to stand firm against challenges, and to strive for a radiant existence that contributes positively to the world around us.
