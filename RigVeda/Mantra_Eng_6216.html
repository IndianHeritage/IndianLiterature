गिरयश्चिन्निजिहते पर्वतानसो मन्यमानाः |पर्वताश्चिन्नियमिरे ||
girayaś cin ni jihate parśānāso manyamānāḥ | parvatāś cin ni yemire ||
  • Girayaś: This word refers to mountains or hills. In a broader sense, it symbolizes stability, strength, and permanence in nature.
  • Cin: This term means "to know" or "to perceive." It indicates awareness or consciousness.
  • Ni jihate: This phrase translates to "does not move" or "does not shake." It signifies steadfastness and resilience.
  • Parśānāso: This word can be interpreted as "those who are strong" or "the powerful." It refers to individuals or entities that possess strength and authority.
  • Manyamānāḥ: This term means "considering" or "thinking." It reflects the process of contemplation or reflection.
  • Parvatāś: This is the plural form of "parvata," meaning mountains. It emphasizes the collective strength and presence of mountains.
  • Yemire: This word translates to "they have settled" or "they have established." It indicates a sense of permanence and stability.

The mantra can be interpreted as a reflection on the strength and permanence of mountains, which do not waver despite external forces. It suggests that those who are strong and powerful, when they contemplate their position, find stability and assurance in their existence.


In real life, this mantra can be applied to various scenarios:

  • Personal Resilience: Just as mountains stand firm against storms, individuals can draw strength from their inner resilience during challenging times. For example, someone facing a personal crisis may find solace in the idea of being steadfast like a mountain.
  • Leadership and Authority: Leaders who embody the qualities of strength and stability can inspire confidence in their teams. A manager who remains calm and composed during a crisis can help their team navigate challenges effectively.
  • Environmental Awareness: The mountains symbolize the importance of nature and the need to protect our environment. This mantra can inspire individuals to appreciate and preserve the natural world, recognizing its enduring strength.

Conclusion: The significance of this mantra lies in its emphasis on strength, stability, and resilience. It serves as a reminder that, like mountains, we can find our footing and remain steadfast in the face of adversity. By contemplating our strengths and the permanence of our values, we can navigate life's challenges with confidence and grace.
