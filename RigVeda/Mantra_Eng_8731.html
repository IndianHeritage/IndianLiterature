एष स्य धारणया सुतोऽव्यो वारेभिः पवते मदिन्तमः |क्रीळन्न ऊर्मिर अपामिव ||
eṣa sya dhārayā suto 'vyo vārebhiḥ pavate madintamaḥ | krīḻann ūrmir apām iva ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • eṣa: this
    • sya: of him
    • dhārayā: by holding or supporting
    • suto: son or offspring
    • 'vyo: of the heavens
    • vārebhiḥ: with the best or the most excellent
    • pavate: blows or moves
    • madintamaḥ: the most delightful or the best
    • krīḻann: playing or sporting
    • ūrmir: wave
    • apām: of the waters
    • iva: like
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra speaks of a divine presence that supports and nurtures life.
    • The phrase "eṣa sya dhārayā" suggests that this divine force holds everything together, much like a parent supports their child.
    • The reference to "suto 'vyo" indicates that this nurturing is not just for the earthly realm but extends to the heavens, symbolizing a universal connection.
    • "Vārebhiḥ pavate" implies that this divine energy moves with grace and excellence, akin to how the wind flows effortlessly through nature.
    • The imagery of "madintamaḥ" evokes feelings of joy and delight, suggesting that this divine presence brings happiness and fulfillment.
    • The comparison of "ūrmir apām iva" likens this divine energy to the waves of water, which are both powerful and soothing, representing the dual nature of life.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In parenting, the support and nurturing provided by a parent can be seen as a reflection of the divine energy described in this mantra.
    • In nature, the way the wind moves through trees or how waves crash on the shore can remind us of the divine presence that sustains life.
    • In moments of joy, such as celebrating a festival or a family gathering, we can feel the essence of "madintamaḥ" as we experience happiness and connection.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra emphasizes the significance of divine support and nurturing in our lives.
    • It reminds us of the interconnectedness of all beings and the importance of recognizing the divine presence in our everyday experiences.
    • In practical terms, we can apply this understanding by fostering supportive relationships, appreciating the beauty of nature, and celebrating moments of joy in our lives.
