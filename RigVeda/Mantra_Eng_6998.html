स्वादोर अभक्षि वयसः सुमेधाः स्वाध्यो वरिवोवित्तरस्य |विश्वे यं देवाऽउत मार्त्यासो मधु ब्रुवन्तो अभि संचरन्ति ||
svādor abhakṣi vayasaḥ sumedhāḥ svādhyo varivovittarasya | viśve yaṁ devā uta martyāso madhu bruvanto abhi saṁcaranti ||
  • svādor abhakṣi vayasaḥ sumedhāḥ: This phrase can be translated as "those who are wise and have good understanding, who are nourished by the essence of life." Here, "svādor" refers to the essence or taste of life, "abhakṣi" implies nourishment, "vayasaḥ" means age or maturity, and "sumedhāḥ" signifies wisdom or good understanding.
  • svādhyo varivovittarasya: This part translates to "those who are self-sufficient and possess great wealth." "Svādhyo" indicates self-sufficiency or independence, while "varivovittarasya" refers to the abundance of wealth or resources.
  • viśve yaṁ devā uta martyāso: This translates to "all the gods and mortals who are aware of this." "Viśve" means all, "devā" refers to the divine or gods, and "martyāso" signifies mortals or human beings.
  • madhu bruvanto abhi saṁcaranti: This can be interpreted as "those who speak of sweetness and move forward." "Madhu" means sweetness, "bruvanto" refers to speaking or expressing, and "abhi saṁcaranti" implies moving forward or progressing.

Conclusion: This mantra emphasizes the importance of wisdom, self-sufficiency, and the pursuit of sweetness or positivity in life. It suggests that both divine beings and humans should strive for understanding and abundance. In real-life scenarios, this can be applied in various contexts such as personal development, where individuals seek knowledge and wisdom to navigate life's challenges. It also highlights the significance of community and collaboration, as both gods and mortals are encouraged to work together towards a common goal of prosperity and positivity. This mantra serves as a reminder to cultivate wisdom and share resources, fostering a harmonious existence.
