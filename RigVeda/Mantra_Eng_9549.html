देवी दिवो दुहितरा सुशिल्पे उषासानक्तā सदतां नि योनौ |आ वां देवास उषती उषन्त उरौ सीदन्तु सुभगे उपस्थे ||
devī divo duhitarā suśilpe uṣāsānaktā sadatāṁ ni yonau | ā vāṁ devāsa uśatī uśanta urau sīdantu subhage upasthe ||
  • devī divo duhitarā: This phrase translates to "the goddess, the daughter of the sky." In many cultures, the sky is often associated with divinity and higher powers. For example, in Greek mythology, the sky god Zeus had daughters who were considered goddesses. This highlights the connection between the divine and the celestial.
  • suśilpe uṣāsānaktā: This means "well-formed, the dawn that awakens." The dawn symbolizes new beginnings and opportunities. In real life, every sunrise can be seen as a chance to start anew, much like how people often set resolutions at the beginning of a new year.
  • sadatāṁ ni yonau: This translates to "always in the womb." This can be interpreted as the idea of potential and creation. For instance, in nature, seeds lie dormant in the soil, holding the potential for new life, which can be likened to the creative potential within each individual.
  • ā vāṁ devāsa uśatī uśanta: This means "may the gods who are auspicious and benevolent come to us." This reflects the human desire for divine blessings and support. In everyday life, people often seek guidance through prayer or meditation, hoping for positive outcomes in their endeavors.
  • urau sīdantu subhage upasthe: This translates to "may they sit in the auspicious place." This can be understood as a call for divine presence in our lives, particularly in moments of importance. For example, during significant events like weddings or ceremonies, people often invoke blessings to ensure a positive atmosphere.

Conclusion: The mantra emphasizes the connection between the divine, the potential for new beginnings, and the importance of seeking blessings in our lives. It serves as a reminder that every day is an opportunity for renewal and that we can invite positive energies into our lives through intention and prayer. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can be applicable during personal milestones, rituals, or even daily affirmations, encouraging individuals to embrace the auspiciousness of life and the support of the divine.
