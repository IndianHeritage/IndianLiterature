विश्वासां गृहपतिर विश्वाम् असि त्वम् अग्ने मानवशीलाम् | शतम् पूर्वभिर यविष्ट पाहि अंहसः समेद्धारं शतम् हिमाः स्तोत्रिभ्यो ये च ददति ||
viśvāsāṁ gṛhapatir viśām asi tvam agne mānuṣīṇām | śatam pūrbhir yaviṣṭha pāhy aṁhasaḥ sameddhāraṁ śataṁ himāḥ stotṛbhyo ye ca dadati ||
  • viśvāsāṁ gṛhapatir viśām asi tvam agne mānuṣīṇām
    • viśvāsāṁ: This word translates to "of trust" or "of faith." It signifies the importance of trust in relationships and communities.
    • gṛhapatir: This means "lord of the house" or "householder," referring to the protector and provider of the family.
    • viśām: This translates to "of the people" or "of the community," emphasizing the collective aspect of society.
    • asi: This means "you are," indicating the presence and role of the divine in human affairs.
    • tvam: This means "you," referring to Agni, the fire god, who is central to many Vedic rituals.
    • agne: This is the name of the fire god, symbolizing purity, energy, and transformation.
    • mānuṣīṇām: This translates to "of humans," indicating the connection between the divine and human beings.
  • śatam pūrbhir yaviṣṭha
    • śatam: This means "hundred," often symbolizing abundance and prosperity.
    • pūrbhir: This translates to "from the past" or "from the earlier times," indicating a connection to tradition and heritage.
    • yaviṣṭha: This means "the best" or "the most excellent," suggesting a pursuit of the highest ideals.
  • pāhy aṁhasaḥ sameddhāraṁ
    • pāhy: This means "protect" or "guard," emphasizing the need for safeguarding what is valuable.
    • aṁhasaḥ: This translates to "of the light" or "of the radiance," symbolizing knowledge and enlightenment.
    • sameddhāraṁ: This means "the one who brings together" or "the unifier," indicating the importance of unity and harmony.
  • śataṁ himāḥ stotṛbhyo ye ca dadati
    • śataṁ: Again, this means "hundred," reinforcing the theme of abundance.
    • himāḥ: This translates to "the cold" or "the frost," which can symbolize challenges or hardships.
    • stotṛbhyo: This means "to the worshippers" or "to those who praise," indicating the importance of devotion and gratitude.
    • ye ca dadati: This translates to "who give," emphasizing the act of generosity and sharing.

Conclusion: This mantra from the Rigveda highlights the significance of trust, protection, and community in human life. It calls upon Agni, the fire god, to be the protector and provider for humanity, emphasizing the importance of unity and the pursuit of excellence. In real life, this can be applied in various scenarios such as family dynamics, community building, and personal relationships. Trust and protection are foundational to any relationship, and by fostering these qualities, individuals can create a harmonious and prosperous environment. The mantra serves as a reminder of the divine presence in our lives and the importance of gratitude and generosity towards others.
