प्र युजो वाचो अग्रियो वृषावा चक्रदद वने |सद्माभि सत्यो अध्वरः ||
pra yujo vāco agriyo vṛṣāva cakradad vane | sadmābhi satyo adhvaraḥ ||
  • Word-by-Word Meaning:
    • pra: forward, towards
    • yujo: to unite, to join
    • vāco: speech, words
    • agriyo: the foremost, the best
    • vṛṣāva: like a bull, strong
    • cakradad: one who wields the wheel (symbol of power)
    • vane: in the forest, in nature
    • sadmābhi: with homes, with shelters
    • satyo: true, real
    • adhvaraḥ: the sacred ritual, the right path
  • Interpretation:
    • This mantra emphasizes the importance of unity and strength in communication.
    • The phrase "pra yujo vāco" suggests that we should strive to unite our words and intentions.
    • By referring to "agriyo" and "vṛṣāva," it highlights the need for powerful and impactful speech.
    • The mention of "cakradad" symbolizes the authority and responsibility that comes with effective communication.
    • Being in "vane" indicates that this communication should be grounded in nature and reality.
    • The term "sadmābhi" implies that our words should create a sense of belonging and shelter for others.
    • Finally, "satyo adhvaraḥ" reminds us that our communication should be truthful and aligned with righteous conduct.
  • Real-Life Examples:
    • In a team setting, effective communication can lead to better collaboration and project success.
    • In personal relationships, honest and strong communication fosters trust and understanding.
    • In leadership, the ability to convey powerful messages can inspire and motivate others.
    • In community building, words that create a sense of belonging can strengthen social ties.
  • Conclusion:
    • This mantra serves as a reminder of the power of words and the importance of using them wisely.
    • It encourages individuals to unite their intentions with their speech, ensuring that communication is both strong and truthful.
    • In real-life scenarios, applying the principles of this mantra can lead to more meaningful interactions and a harmonious environment.
    • Ultimately, it highlights that effective communication is not just about speaking, but about creating connections and fostering understanding.
