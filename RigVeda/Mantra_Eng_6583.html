तां माता विश्ववेदसासूर्याय प्रमहसाः |महि जजानादितिर ऋतावरि ||
tā mātā viśvavedasāsuryāya pramahasā | mahī jajānāditir ṛtāvarī ||
  • tā mātā: This phrase translates to "that mother." In the context of the mantra, it refers to the nurturing and protective aspect of the divine, akin to how a mother cares for her children.
  • viśvavedasā: This means "of all knowledge" or "universal knowledge." It signifies the all-encompassing wisdom that is present in the universe, suggesting that the divine mother embodies this knowledge.
  • sūryāya: This translates to "to the Sun." The Sun is often seen as a source of life, energy, and illumination. In this mantra, it indicates a connection to the life-giving and sustaining power of the Sun.
  • pramahasā: This term can be interpreted as "the great one" or "the supreme." It emphasizes the grandeur and significance of the divine mother, highlighting her exalted status in the cosmic order.
  • mahī: This word means "the Earth." It represents the physical realm and the nurturing aspect of nature, which is often associated with the feminine principle.
  • jajānā: This translates to "has given birth" or "has created." It signifies the act of creation and the generative power of the divine mother, emphasizing her role in bringing forth life.
  • aditir: Aditi is often referred to as the "mother of the gods" in Vedic literature. This term signifies the boundless and infinite nature of the divine, representing the source of all creation.
  • ṛtāvarī: This can be interpreted as "the one who upholds truth" or "the embodiment of righteousness." It emphasizes the importance of truth and moral order in the universe, suggesting that the divine mother is a guardian of these principles.

Conclusion: This mantra encapsulates the essence of the divine feminine as a nurturing force that embodies universal knowledge, creation, and truth. It highlights the interconnectedness of the Earth, the Sun, and the divine mother, suggesting that all life is sustained through her grace. In real-life scenarios, this mantra can inspire individuals to seek wisdom, embrace nurturing qualities, and uphold truth in their actions. It serves as a reminder of the importance of respecting nature and recognizing the divine presence in all aspects of life, encouraging a harmonious relationship with the environment and each other.
